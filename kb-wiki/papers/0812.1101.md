# Infrared stability of quarkyonic matter with the 1/p^4 confinement

> We demonstrate an exact cancellation of the infrared divergences in the color-singlet quarkyonic matter with the 1/p^4 Coulomb-like confining interaction both in the chiral symmetry broken and restored regimes.

# Infrared stability of quarkyonic matter with the 1/p^4 confinement

## Key claim
The infrared divergences that arise in a many-body fermion system with a 1/p^4 confining interaction are exactly canceled in color-singlet quarkyonic matter by the infrared divergences from single-quark self-energies, both in the chiral symmetry broken and restored regimes. This cancellation relies on the color-dependence of the interaction and the fact that the quadratic Casimir operator vanishes for any color-singlet many-quark system.

## Method
The author uses a model with an instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential that is linear in configuration space (sigma r) and has a 1/p^4 behavior in momentum space, regularized with an infrared cutoff mu_IR. The Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy is solved in the rainbow approximation (valid in the large N_c limit), and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons and variational equations for baryons are considered. The cancellation of infrared divergences is demonstrated by computing the color-dependent Casimir factors for the self-energy and interquark interaction contributions in color-singlet systems, and then extending the argument to many-quark systems using the property that the quadratic Casimir operator vanishes for any color-singlet state.

## Key numbers
- The regularized confining potential is given by V(p) = 8 pi sigma / (p^2 + mu_IR^2)^2, where sigma is the string tension and mu_IR is the infrared cutoff.
- The single-quark energy diverges as omega(p) = sigma/(2 mu_IR) + omega_f(p), where omega_f(p) is infrared-finite.
- The color factor for the quark self-energy contribution is 4/3, while the quark-antiquark interaction color factor is -4/3, leading to exact cancellation: 2*(sigma/(2 mu_IR)) - sigma/mu_IR = 0.
- The interquark interaction color factor for three quarks in a baryon is -2, which cancels the self-energy factor 4/3 multiplied by the number of quarks (with an additional factor of 1/2 from the self-energy equation).

## Context
This work addresses an objection raised by Shuryak that quarkyonic matter with a 1/p^4 confining interaction should collapse due to infrared divergences. The author extends the model of Glozman and Wagenbrunn (2008) for confining but chirally symmetric hadrons, which itself builds on earlier studies of the linear confining Coulomb-like potential observed in Coulomb-gauge QCD studies and lattice simulations. The result shows that quarkyonic matter can exist as a color-singlet system, in contrast to a many-fermion system without color where such an interaction would lead to divergence. This enables further study of the confined but chirally symmetric phase that may appear in high-density heavy ion collisions.
