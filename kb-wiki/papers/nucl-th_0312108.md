# Nonextensive statistical effects on the relativistic nuclear equation of state

> Following the basic prescriptions of the Tsallis' nonextensive thermodynamics, we study the relativistic nonextensive thermodynamics and the equation of state for a perfect gas at the equilibrium. The obtained results are used to study the relativistic nuclear equation of state in the hadronic and in the quark-gluon plasma phase. We show that small deviations from the standard extensive statistics imply remarkable effects into the shape of the equation of state.

## Key claim
Small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (quantified by the Tsallis parameter \(q\) near 1) produce significant modifications in the shape of both the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma equations of state, but the analysis is limited to \(q>1\) based on prior phenomenological studies of heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors generalized relativistic kinetic theory to Tsallis nonextensive thermodynamics, deriving equilibrium distribution functions and thermodynamic variables (particle density, pressure, energy density) for a relativistic perfect gas. They then applied these relations to a relativistic nonlinear mean-field model for hadronic matter (with nucleons and \(\sigma,\omega,\rho\) mesons) and to a free-quark bag model for the quark-gluon plasma phase, evaluating the equations of state numerically for \(q>1\) at fixed temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Hadronic equation of state plotted at temperature \(T = 100\) MeV and fixed \(Z/A = 0.4\), with baryon density in units of nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Quark-gluon plasma equation of state plotted at temperature \(T = 100\) MeV and fixed \(Z/A = 0.4\), with bag parameter \(B^{1/4} = 170\) MeV.
- Quark degeneracy factor \(\gamma_f = 6\) for each flavor (u, d); gluon degeneracy factor \(\gamma_g = 16\).
- The range of baryon density and parameters chosen correspond to conditions realizable in high-energy heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Au-Au collisions).

## Context
This work extends the Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics (Tsallis, 1988) to the relativistic regime for nuclear matter, building on prior applications of nonextensive statistics to nuclear and high-energy physics (Alberico et al., 2000; Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000; Bediaga et al., 2000). It generalizes earlier calculations by Miller and Suhonen (2002) for the quark-gluon phase transition diagram. The results enable future studies of how nonextensive effects modify the deconfinement phase transition density, symmetry energy, and nuclear compressibility, which are connected to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions at facilities like GSI.
