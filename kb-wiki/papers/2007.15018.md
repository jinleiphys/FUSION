# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: nearly complete results for QCD

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We continue study of the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap (which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD), avoiding soft-emission approximations. Previous work has computed overlap effects for double splitting $g \to gg \to ggg$. To make use of those results, one also needs calculations of related virtual loop corrections to single splitting $g \to gg$ in order to cancel severe (power-law) infrared (IR) divergences. This paper provides calculations of nearly all such processes involving gluons and discusses how to organize the results to demonstrate the cancellation. In the soft emission limit, our results reproduce the known double-log behavior of earlier authors who worked in leading-log approximation. We also present a first (albeit numerical and not yet analytic) investigation of sub-leading, single IR logarithms. Ultraviolet divergences appearing in our calculations correctly renormalize the coupling $α_{\rm s}$ in the usual LPM result for leading-order $g \to gg$.

## Key claim

This paper provides nearly complete calculations of virtual loop corrections to single gluon splitting ($g \to gg$) needed to cancel power-law infrared divergences from overlapping formation-time effects in double gluon splitting ($g \to gg \to ggg$), but leaves out contributions from instantaneous interactions and 4-gluon vertices for future work.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop virtual corrections to single gluon splitting in QCD using Light-Cone Perturbation Theory (LCPT), relating most virtual diagrams to previously computed real double-splitting diagrams via back-end and front-end transformations. One remaining virtual diagram is adapted from a previously computed QED result by converting group factors, splitting functions, and medium propagation parameters to QCD. The calculations are performed in the large-$\Nc$ limit, assuming a thick medium and using the multiple-scattering ($\hat q$) approximation.

## Key numbers

* The real double-splitting overlap effect scales as $\left[ \Delta \frac{d\Gamma}{dx\,dy} \right]_{g\to ggg} \sim \frac{\CA^2 \alphas^2}{x y^{3/2}} \left( \frac{\hat q}{E} \right)^{1/2}$ for $y \ll x < z$, showing power-law $y^{-3/2}$ infrared behavior.
* In the soft emission limit, the combination $\V(x,y) + \tfrac12 \R(x,y)$ approaches $-\frac{\CA\alphas}{8\pi} \left[ \frac{d\Gamma}{dx} \right]^{\rm LO} \frac{\ln y}{y}$, producing a double-log infrared divergence when integrated over $y$.
* Numerical results for the ratio $\frac{ \V(x,y) + \tfrac12 \R(x,y) }{ \frac{\CA\alphas}{8\pi} \left[ \frac{d\Gamma}{dx} \right]^{\rm LO} \frac{1}{y} }$ plotted against $\ln y$ for $x=0.3$ confirm the leading-log slope of $-1$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of overlap effects for real double gluon splitting $g \to gg \to ggg$ (Arnold, Gorda, Iqbal, 2018-2019) by providing the necessary virtual corrections to single splitting $g \to gg$ that cancel power-law infrared divergences. The results reproduce the leading-log double-log behavior derived earlier by Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2013) in the soft emission limit. The paper enables future investigation of whether overlap effects are small enough to justify a picture of independent parton showers inside a quark-gluon plasma, or whether high-energy partons lose their individual identity as suggested by gauge-gravity duality studies (Gubser, 2006; Chesler, 2014).
