# Nuclear Ground State Observables and QCD Scaling in a Refined Relativistic Point Coupling Model

> We present results obtained in the calculation of nuclear ground state properties in relativistic Hartree approximation using a Lagrangian whose QCD-scaled coupling constants are all natural (dimensionless and of order 1). Our model consists of four-, six-, and eight-fermion point couplings (contact interactions) together with derivative terms representing, respectively, two-, three-, and four-body forces and the finite ranges of the corresponding mesonic interactions. The coupling constants have been determined in a self-consistent procedure that solves the model equations for representative nuclei simultaneously in a generalized nonlinear least-squares adjustment algorithm. The extracted coupling constants allow us to predict ground state properties of a much larger set of even-even nuclei to good accuracy. The fact that the extracted coupling constants are all natural leads to the conclusion that QCD scaling and chiral symmetry apply to finite nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a relativistic point-coupling model with eleven coupling constants, all of which are "natural" (dimensionless and of order 1) after QCD scaling, can reproduce nuclear ground state observables to good accuracy, leading to the conclusion that QCD scaling and chiral symmetry apply to finite nuclei. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the specific choice of Lorentz invariants included and the assumption that higher-order terms not yet tested (such as tensor couplings and mixed third/fourth-order couplings) will not alter the naturalness of the extracted constants.

## Method
The authors constructed a relativistic point-coupling (RMF-PC) Lagrangian with four-, six-, and eight-fermion contact interactions plus derivative terms, containing eleven coupling constants. They solved the relativistic Hartree equations with BCS pairing and a no-sea approximation, fitting the coupling constants simultaneously to ground state observables of representative even-even nuclei using a combined fitting procedure: first a simulated annealing algorithm with a population of walkers to explore parameter space, then a Bevington Curved Step (Levenberg-Marquardt) method for final minimization. The fit included binding energies, charge radii, diffraction radii, and surface thicknesses, with pairing strengths for protons and neutrons fitted simultaneously.

## Key numbers
- The extracted coupling constant α_S = -4.2252 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻² (from the NL-Z2 RMF-FR model for comparison), with the actual PC-F1 set having α_S = -3.86 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻² and α_V = 2.77 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻² (both with units of MeV⁻²)
- The derivative coupling δ_S = -1.7374 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻⁴ (from NL-Z2 comparison), with the PC-F1 set having δ_S = -1.64 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻⁴ and δ_V = 1.02 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻⁴ (both with units of MeV⁻⁴)
- The model uses 11 coupling constants: α_S, α_V, α_TS, α_TV, β_S, γ_S, γ_V, δ_S, δ_V, δ_TS, δ_TV, plus two pairing strength parameters V_P and V_N
- The fit used two sets of observables: set 1 (smaller, from Ref. NHM92) for simulated annealing exploration, and set 2 (larger, same as used for NL-Z2) for final minimization

## Context
This work extends the relativistic point-coupling approach of Nikolaus et al. (1992) and Hirsch et al. (1994) by including a complete set of four-fermion scalar and vector currents (including the isovector-scalar channel, δ-meson exchange, which had not been simultaneously included with its derivative term in previous RMF-PC work), by introducing BCS pairing fitted simultaneously with the coupling constants, and by using a combined simulated annealing and Levenberg-Marquardt fitting algorithm to avoid local minima. The results enable comparison between RMF-PC, RMF-FR (finite-range meson exchange), and nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approaches, and provide a starting point for future inclusion of tensor couplings and mixed third/fourth-order couplings. The work also tests the naturalness hypothesis of Weinberg (1990) regarding QCD scaling and chiral symmetry in finite nuclei.
