# Analyticity and the phase diagram of QCD

> Some consequences of the analyticity of the free energy (pressure) of QCD at finite chemical potential are deduced. These include a method for numerical exploration of the full phase diagram by a novel use of simulations at imaginary chemical potential to extract Yang-Lee zeroes of the grand-canonical partition function. We make use of, and comment on, CPT symmetries, positivity of non-linear susceptibilities and the finiteness of screening lengths. We also comment on the structure of zeroes expected for the usual picture of the phases of QCD, following a discussion of the physics of imaginary chemical potential.

## Key claim

The free energy of QCD is an analytic function of the chemical potential due to CP symmetry, which allows a numerical method for exploring the full phase diagram by using simulations at imaginary chemical potential to extract Yang-Lee zeroes of the grand-canonical partition function, going beyond Taylor series extrapolations that are limited to the phase connected to μ=0.

## Method

The paper develops a method to construct the grand-canonical partition function as a finite Laurent series in the fugacity z=exp(μ/T) by Fourier transforming the partition function computed at imaginary chemical potential μ=iφ. The coefficients of this series are the canonical partition functions for fixed quark number Q, which are real and non-negative. By locating the zeroes of this polynomial in z and identifying where they pinch the positive real z axis as the volume increases, one can determine phase transition lines in the (μ,T) plane, including those to conjectured colour-superconducting phases.

## Key numbers

- The partition function has a periodicity of 2πT in the imaginary part of the chemical potential: Z(T,μ) = Z(T,μ+2πikT) for any integer k.
- For an ideal gas, the free energy is F(T,μ) = N_cN_fV[ (7π²/180)T⁴ + (1/6)μ²T² + (1/(12π²))μ⁴ ].
- In the massless limit of the Gibbs model extended to SU(N_c), the zeroes of the partition function lie at z_n = exp[i(2n+1)π/(2N_c)] for 0 ≤ n < N_c.
- The RMS error in determining zeroes from simulations scales as σ z_n/N, where σ is the statistical error width and N is proportional to the system volume.

## Context

This work extends the Yang-Lee theory of phase transitions (Yang and Lee, 1952) to QCD at finite chemical potential, providing a method that goes beyond Taylor expansion approaches used by Allton et al. (2002) and others. The technique enables numerical exploration of the QCD phase diagram including the conjectured colour-superconducting phases (Alford et al., 2001) that lie beyond the reach of extrapolations from μ=0. The paper also discusses the subtle differences between phase transitions at real versus imaginary chemical potential, warning against misinterpreting non-thermodynamical singularities that appear at imaginary μ as genuine critical points.
