# Semisuper Efimov effect induced by resonant pair exchange in mixed dimensions

> We introduce a new member to the class of semisuper Efimov effects, where an infinite number of bound states emerge with their binding energies obeying the universal scaling law $E_n\sim e^{-2(πn/γ)^2}$ for sufficiently high excitation $n\in\mathbb{N}$. Our system consists of a pair of two-component fermions in three dimensions at infinite scattering length, which furthermore interact with a boson confined in two dimensions so as to form a three-body bound state at zero energy. When another boson is added, the exchange of the resonant pair of fermions between two bosons leads to the semisuper Efimov effect of four such particles with the scaling exponent $γ$ determined by the mass ratio of bosons to fermions. If bosons exist in three dimensions, an infinite number of bound states do not emerge, but some of them may survive for a large mass ratio, making our findings potentially relevant to two-neutron halo nuclei as well as ultracold atoms.

# Semisuper Efimov effect induced by resonant pair exchange in mixed dimensions

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new semisuper Efimov effect in a system of two bosons and two fermions in mixed dimensions, where an infinite number of bound states emerge with binding energies scaling as \(E_n \sim e^{-2(\pi n/\gamma)^2}\), but only if the bosons are confined to two dimensions; if bosons exist in three dimensions, an infinite number of bound states do not appear, though some may survive for large boson-to-fermion mass ratios.

## Method

The author constructs an effective field theory describing two-component fermions in three dimensions at infinite scattering length (via a dimer field) interacting with bosons confined to \(d\) dimensions (with \(d=1,2,3\) analyzed) through a trimer field tuned to zero binding energy. A Born-Oppenheimer analysis for \(M \gg m\) derives the effective potential between two bosons from resonant pair exchange, revealing a nearly scale-invariant attraction with a logarithmic correction. A renormalization-group analysis for the \(d=2\) case computes the running of the two-body and four-body couplings, whose divergences in the infrared limit signal the binding energies of the four-particle system.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent for the semisuper Efimov effect is \(\gamma = \sqrt{\frac{2(M+2m)^2}{(M+m)m}}\), where \(M\) is the boson mass and \(m\) the fermion mass.
- In the limit \(M \gg m\), the exponent approaches \(\gamma \to \sqrt{2M/m}\); in the opposite limit \(M \ll m\), it reduces to \(\gamma \to 2\sqrt{2}\).
- For bosons in \(d=3\) with large mass ratio, bound states with excitation numbers up to \(n \sim (2M/m)/\pi\) are expected to survive.
- For finite scattering length \(a\) or nonzero trimer binding energy \(\mathcal{E}\), only a finite number of bound states up to \(n \sim \gamma \sqrt{\ln(R_0/r_0)}/\pi\) emerge, where \(R_0 \sim |a|\) or \(1/\sqrt{m|\mathcal{E}|}\).

## Context

This work extends the classification of few-body universality classes (Nishida, 2017) by adding a new member to the semisuper Efimov class, which previously included four bosons in two dimensions at a three-body resonance. The analysis is motivated by an effective field theory developed by Hongo (2022) for two-neutron halo nuclei, where a core nucleus binds a neutron pair. The findings are potentially relevant to two-neutron halo nuclei (identifying bosons as core nuclei and fermions as neutrons) and ultracold-atom experiments, where simultaneous fine-tuning of two-body and three-body interactions could be implemented using schemes from Daley (2014) and Petrov (2014).
