# Parton energy loss effect on Z+jet production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We give a report of medium modification of Z+jet correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using Sherpa to generate initial Z+jet at next-leading-order matrix element matched parton shower, and the Linear Boltzmann Transport Model for jet propagation in the expanding quark-gluon-plasma. Our numerical calculations show excellent agreement with all available observables of Z+jet simultaneously in both proton + proton and Pb+Pb collisions. Our results can well explain the shift of momentum asymmetry $x_{jZ}=p_T^{jet}/p_T^Z$ as well as its mean values, the suppression of the jet yields per Z trigger $R_{jZ}$ and the modification of azimuthal angle correlation $Δϕ_{jZ}$. We also demonstrate that it is the energy loss effect on multi-jets from high-order corrections that leads to the suppression of the Z+jet correlation at small azimuthal angle difference $Δϕ_{jZ}$ and at small $x_{jZ}$. The jet shape reflecting transverse momentum distribution inside the jet is also calculated, which indicates that large fraction of jet energy is carried away from the jet axis in Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the suppression of Z+jet correlations at small azimuthal angle difference and small momentum asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions is caused specifically by energy loss effects on multi-jets from high-order corrections, not by transverse momentum broadening of single jets.

## Method

The authors used Sherpa to generate initial Z+jet events at next-to-leading order matrix element matched to parton shower (NLO+PS) for the p+p baseline at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. They then applied the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate jet propagation, energy loss, and medium response in an expanding quark-gluon plasma described by (3+1)D hydrodynamics, with the strong coupling constant alpha_s set to 0.2 as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Z boson mass M_Z = 91.18 GeV
- Jet transverse momentum threshold p_T^jet = 30 GeV for event selection
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.2 used to fix jet-medium interaction strength
- Collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions
- Centrality selection 0-30% for central Pb+Pb collisions
- Jet shape normalized to unity over radius r < 0.3, with depletion observed in region 0.05 < r < 0.1 and enhancement at r > 0.1 in Pb+Pb relative to p+p

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order matched parton shower calculations (Zhang 2018, Dai 2018) that underestimated azimuthal angle correlations at small angle differences due to missing additional hard radiation from high-order matrix elements. The calculations use the LBT model (He 2015) for jet-medium interactions and are compared to CMS experimental data (Sirunyan 2017) on Z+jet correlations at 5.02 TeV. The results enable simultaneous description of all available Z+jet observables and demonstrate that multi-jet energy loss, not single-jet broadening, drives the observed modifications in heavy-ion collisions.
