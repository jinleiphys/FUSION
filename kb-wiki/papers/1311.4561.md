# Pionic and Hidden-Color, Six-Quark Contributions to the Deuteron b1 Structure Function

> The b1 structure function is an observable feature of a spin-1 system sensitive to non-nucleonic components of the target nuclear wave function. The contributions of exchanged pions in the deuteron are estimated and found to be of measurable size for small values of x. A simple model for a hidden-color, six-quark configurations (with~ 0.15% probability to exist in the deuteron) is proposed and found to give substantial contributions for values of x>0.2. Good agreement with Hermes data is obtained. Predictions are made for an upcoming JLab experiment. The Close & Kumano sum rule is investigated and found to be a useful guide to understanding various possible effects that may contribute.

## Key claim
The paper estimates that pionic contributions to the deuteron b1 structure function are measurable at low x, while hidden-color six-quark configurations (with approximately 0.15% probability in the deuteron) give substantial contributions at x > 0.2; the combination reproduces Hermes data, but the Close and Kumano sum rule is violated by pionic, nucleonic, and double-scattering mechanisms while holding for the hidden-color valence-quark mechanism.

## Method
The pionic contribution is computed using the Sullivan mechanism, where the pion-nucleon form factor is related to the axial form factor via the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation, and the deuteron wave function is taken from the Argonne V18 potential. The hidden-color six-quark contribution is modeled using non-relativistic harmonic oscillator wave functions for s-state and d-state six-quark configurations, with the d-state obtained by promoting one quark to a d3/2 state. The probability amplitude P6q is fixed to 0.0015 to reproduce the Hermes data point at x = 0.452.

## Key numbers
- The hidden-color six-quark configuration probability in the deuteron is set to approximately 0.15% (P6q = 0.0015) to reproduce the measured b1 value at x = 0.452.
- The measured b1 at x = 0.452 is -0.38 × 10^{-2} with statistical uncertainty ±0.16 × 10^{-2} and systematic uncertainty ±0.03 × 10^{-2} at ⟨Q²⟩ = 4.69 GeV².
- The pionic contribution at x = 0.012 is 10.5 × 10^{-2} using the Aicher (2010) pion structure function, compared to the measured value of 11.20 × 10^{-2} ± 5.51 (stat) ± 2.77 (sys) at ⟨Q²⟩ = 0.51 GeV².
- The axial mass MA is taken as 1.03 ± 0.04 GeV.
- The harmonic oscillator radius parameter R is 1.2 fm and the quark mass is 338 MeV.
- The pionic contribution at x = 0.452 is -0.02 × 10^{-2} using the Aicher (2010) structure function.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier estimate of pionic effects (Miller, 1989) and addresses the puzzle from the Hermes experiment (Airapetian, 2005) which observed a significant negative b1 at x = 0.45 where nucleonic contributions (Jaffe, 1989; Khan, 1991; Umnikov, 1996) are negligible. The hidden-color model builds on the six-quark state decomposition of Harvey (1988) and earlier work on six-quark states in the EMC effect (Carlson, 1983). The paper provides predictions for an upcoming Jefferson Lab experiment (JLab) and shows that the Close and Kumano sum rule (Close, 1990) is violated by pionic, nucleonic (Khan, 1991), and double-scattering (Bora, 1997) mechanisms, but holds for the hidden-color valence-quark contribution, making a measured up-down pattern in b1 a potential signature of hidden-color components.
