# Isospin-breaking effects in the two-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization

> Isospin-breaking (IB) effects in the two-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) can be resonantly enhanced, if related to the interference of the $ρ(770)$ and $ω(782)$ resonances. This particular IB contribution to the pion vector form factor and thus the line shape in $e^+e^-\to π^+π^-$ can be described by the residue at the $ω$ pole - the $ρ$-$ω$ mixing parameter $ε_ω$. Here, we argue that while in general analyticity requires this parameter to be real, the radiative channels $π^0γ$, $ππγ$, $ηγ$ can induce a small phase, whose size we estimate as $δ_ε=3.5(1.0)^\circ$ by using a narrow-width approximation for the intermediate-state vector mesons. We then perform fits to the $e^+e^-\to π^+π^-$ data base and study the consequences for the two-pion HVP contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, its IB part due to $ρ$-$ω$ mixing, and the mass of the $ω$ resonance. We find that the global fit does prefer a non-vanishing value of $δ_ε=4.5(1.2)^\circ$, close to the narrow-resonance expectation, but with a large spread among the data sets, indicating systematic differences in the $ρ$-$ω$ region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while analyticity generally requires the ρ–ω mixing parameter ε_ω to be real, radiative channels (π⁰γ, ππγ, ηγ) can induce a small phase δ_ε in this parameter, estimated at 3.5(1.0)° using a narrow-width approximation; the global fit to e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data prefers a non-vanishing value of δ_ε = 4.5(1.2)°, close to this expectation, but with a large spread among data sets indicating systematic differences in the ρ–ω region.

## Method

The authors generalize a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor from Colangelo (2018) by introducing a complex ρ–ω mixing parameter ε_ω, implementing the imaginary part via a modified Omnès function that accounts for radiative intermediate states. They perform fits to individual e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data sets (SND, CMD-2, BaBar, KLOE, BESIII, SND20) and their combinations, allowing the phase δ_ε to vary freely, and study the consequences for the two-pion HVP contribution to a_μ, the isospin-breaking part due to ρ–ω mixing, and the ω resonance mass.

## Key numbers

- Estimated phase from radiative channels: δ_ε = 3.5(1.0)° from narrow-width approximation
- Global fit result (excluding SND20): δ_ε = 4.5(1.2)°, Re ε_ω = 1.97(3) × 10⁻³
- Two-pion HVP contribution to a_μ up to 1 GeV from global fit: a_μ^{ππ}|_{≤1 GeV} = 494.6(2.3) × 10⁻¹⁰
- ω mass from global fit with phase: M_ω = 782.09(12) MeV
- Isospin-breaking contribution to a_μ from ρ–ω mixing with δ_ε = 4.5(1.2)°: a_μ^{ρ–ω} = 3.68(14)(10) × 10⁻¹⁰
- Individual data set phases: BaBar δ_ε = 1.3(1.9)°, KLOE'' δ_ε = 6.1(1.7)°, CMD-2 δ_ε = 11.4(3.1)°, SND06 δ_ε = 8.5(2.3)°, SND20 δ_ε = 10.1(2.4)°
- Strong IB contribution from leading-order chiral prediction: a_μ[m_u–m_d, LO] = 3.68(17) × 10⁻¹⁰

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for the pion vector form factor from Colangelo (2018) by incorporating a complex phase in the ρ–ω mixing parameter, motivated by radiative decay channels of the ρ and ω mesons. The results enable improved quantification of isospin-breaking effects in the two-pion HVP contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, which is critical given the 4.2σ discrepancy between experiment and the Standard Model prediction. The study also provides a consistency check on e⁺e⁻ data sets in the ρ–ω interference region, revealing systematic differences that need to be resolved by future precision measurements from CMD-3, BaBar, BESIII, and Belle II.
