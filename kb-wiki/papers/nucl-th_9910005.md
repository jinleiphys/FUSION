# Low Energy Behavior of the \protect(^{7}\protect) Be \protect( (p,γ)^{8}\protect) B reaction

> Three related features of the astrophysical S-factor, (S_{17}), for low energies are an upturn as the energy of the proton goes to zero, a pole at the proton separation energy and a long ranged radial integral peaking at approximately 40 fm. These features, particularly the last, mean (S_{17}) at threshold is largely determined by the asymptotic normalization of the proton bound-state wave function. In this paper we show that by taking the pole explicitly into account, (S_{17}) can be calculated in terms of purely short range integrals involving the two-body potential. Much of the discusion is relevant to other radiative capture processes especially those wth weakly bound final states.

## Key claim

The low-energy astrophysical S-factor \(S_{17}\) for the \(^{7}\text{Be}(p,\gamma)^{8}\text{B}\) reaction can be calculated using only short-range integrals over the nuclear interior, provided the pole at the proton separation energy is explicitly taken into account; this avoids explicit reliance on the asymptotic behavior of the bound-state wave function, but the derivation assumes a local two-body potential and may fail for non-local or charge-exchange potentials.

## Method

The author starts from the t-matrix expansion in eigenstates of the full Hamiltonian and uses the bound-state Lippmann-Schwinger equation to relate the residue of the pole in the t-matrix to the asymptotic normalization constant \(A_n\). For the dipole matrix element of the capture reaction, the commutator with the Hamiltonian is applied twice to expose a second-order pole, yielding an expression where the integrand is restricted to the range of the nuclear potential. For a two-body interaction, Newton's third law cancels the Coulomb contributions, leaving only neutron-proton potential terms in the matrix element.

- The S-factor at threshold is given by \(S_{17}(0) = 38 A_n\) (where \(A_n\) is the asymptotic normalization), accurate to about 1%.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic normalization method of Mukhamedzhanov and collaborators (1990s) by showing that the asymptotic normalization, and hence \(S_{17}\), can be obtained from integrals over the nuclear interior rather than from the tail of the wave function. It also refines earlier work by Jennings (1990s) on the pole structure of the S-factor. The result enables shell-model calculations (which are accurate in the nuclear interior but poor in the tail) to determine the asymptotic normalization and thus \(S_{17}\) at solar energies, though the extreme sensitivity to the potential radius suggests that experimental determination of the asymptotic normalization may ultimately be necessary.
