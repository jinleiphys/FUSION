# Evidence for Tetrahedral Structure of Calcium-40

> More than 100 excited states of the Calcium-40 nucleus, with isospin 0, are classified into rotational-vibrational bands of an intrinsic tetrahedral structure. Almost all observed states below 8 MeV can be accommodated, as well as many high-spin states above 8 MeV. The bands have some similarity to those classifying states of Oxygen-16, but the A-mode vibrational frequency is lower relative to the E-mode and F-mode frequencies than in Oxygen-16. Previously identified rotational bands up to spin 16 and energy above 20 MeV are unified here into a smaller number of tetrahedral bands.

## Key claim

The paper claims that more than 100 excited states of Calcium-40 with isospin 0 can be classified into rotational-vibrational bands of an intrinsic tetrahedral structure, but the classification relies on making choices among experimentally allowed spin/parity values for about 20 states between 7 and 10 MeV, and the author notes these choices cannot be regarded as firm predictions.

## Method

The author assigns 104 experimentally observed states of Calcium-40 from the ENSDF compilation to rovibrational bands of a tetrahedron, using the group theory of the tetrahedral group T_d to determine allowed spin/parities for each vibrational phonon configuration. The model treats A-, E-, and F-mode vibrations (with approximate frequencies 3.4, 2.5, and 5 MeV respectively) combined with rotations, following the same approach previously applied to Oxygen-16 in Halcrow, King and Manton (2017, 2018). The assignments are tabulated and plotted, with particular attention to the E-band (one E-phonon) which shows jumps in state multiplicity at spin 8 and spin 14 as predicted by tetrahedral symmetry.

## Key numbers

* 104 observed states of Calcium-40 are assigned to tetrahedral rovibrational bands
* The E-phonon frequency is approximately 2.5 MeV
* The A-phonon frequency is approximately 3.4 MeV (first excited 0+ state at 3.353 MeV)
* The F-phonon frequency is approximately 5 MeV (from 1- state at 5.903 MeV and 3+ state at 6.030 MeV)
* The yrast 5- state at 4.491 MeV is interpreted as belonging to the E-band but shifted down by more than 2 MeV due to 1p1h character
* The highest spin state classified is a 16+ state at 22.106 MeV
* The 3- yrast state at 3.737 MeV is assigned to the ground state band
* The second excited 0+ state at 5.212 MeV is identified as the E2-bandhead

## Context

This work extends the tetrahedral cluster model previously applied to Oxygen-16 by Halcrow, King and Manton (2017, 2018) to the larger doubly magic nucleus Calcium-40, building on earlier suggestions by Lezuo (1970s) that Calcium-40 and Lead-208 show tetrahedral features. The paper unifies rotational bands previously identified by Ide (2019) for positive parity states and Tor (2019) for negative parity states into a smaller number of tetrahedral bands, and it enables future theoretical work on the Skyrmion with baryon number 40 found by Lau (2019) and its low-lying vibrational modes.
