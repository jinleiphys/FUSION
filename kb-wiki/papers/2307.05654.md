# Practical Dirac Majorana confusion theorem: Issues and Applicability

> We inspect the model-independent study of practical Dirac Majorana confusion theorem (pDMCT) -- a wide spread belief that the difference between Dirac and Majorana neutrinos via any kinematical observable would be practically impossible to determine because of the difference only being proportional to the square of neutrino mass -- in context of processes that have at least a neutrino antineutrino pair in their final state. We scrutinize the domain of applicability of pDMCT and also highlight those aspects that are often misunderstood. We try to clarify some of the frequently used concepts that are used to assert pDMCT as a generic feature irrespective of the process, or observable, such as the existence of any analytic continuity between Dirac and Majorana neutrinos in the limit mass(neutrino) -> 0. In summary, we illustrate that pDMCT is not any fundamental property of neutrinos, instead, it is a phenomenological feature of neutrino non-observation, depending on models and processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "practical Dirac Majorana confusion theorem" (pDMCT) is not a fundamental property of neutrinos but rather a phenomenological feature of neutrino non-observation that depends on models and processes; the theorem holds only under specific conditions such as neutral current processes within the Standard Model or when full phase space integration over invisible neutrinos is required, and can be avoided in special kinematic configurations or with new physics contributions.

## Method

The authors perform a model-independent analysis of processes containing a neutrino-antineutrino pair in the final state, comparing Dirac and Majorana transition amplitudes through anti-symmetrization of the amplitude for Majorana neutrinos. They examine the squared amplitude difference between Dirac and Majorana cases, identifying two sources of possible difference: unequal direct and exchange terms, and non-zero interference terms. The analysis is applied to Standard Model neutral current processes (e.g., Z boson decay) and extended to include new physics contributions parameterized by effective couplings, as well as to doubly weak charged current four-body decays such as B^0 → μ⁺ μ⁻ ν_μ \barν_μ with special back-to-back kinematic configurations.

## Key numbers

- For the Standard Model Z → ν \barν decay, the squared amplitude difference |M^D|² - |M^M|² = (g_Z²/2) m_ν², showing proportionality to the square of neutrino mass
- For Z → ν \barν with new physics contributions parameterized by ε_V^ℓ and ε_A^ℓ, the difference becomes (g_Z²/3)(ε_V^ℓ - ε_A^ℓ) m_Z² when neglecting m_ν-dependent terms, where m_Z is the Z boson mass
- For the back-to-back muon configuration in B^0 → μ⁺ μ⁻ ν_μ \barν_μ, the energy relation is E_μ + E_ν = m_B/2, where m_B is the B^0 meson mass
- The differential decay rate difference between Majorana and Dirac cases for B^0 → μ⁺ μ⁻ ν_μ \barν_μ after full phase space integration is proportional to m_ν²

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the practical Dirac Majorana confusion theorem originally formulated by Kayser (1982) and precursors by Schechter and Valle (1980, 1981), which was previously verified for neutral current processes such as γ* → ν \barν, Z → ν \barν, e⁺ e⁻ → ν \barν, K⁺ → π⁺ ν \barν, and others. The paper builds on the author's own earlier model-independent studies (Kim 2021, 2022) to clarify the domain of applicability of pDMCT and demonstrate that it can be overcome within the Standard Model using special kinematic configurations in four-body decays like B^0 → μ⁺ μ⁻ ν_μ \barν_μ, or through new physics contributions that modify the vector and axial-vector couplings. This enables future experimental searches for the Dirac versus Majorana nature of neutrinos using kinematic observables that avoid full phase space integration over invisible neutrinos.
