# Re-investigation of heat capacity and paring phase transition in hot $^{93-98}$Mo nuclei

> The empirical heat capacities of $^{93-98}$Mo nuclei are re-investigated by using the latest updated and recommended nuclear level density (NLD) data below the neutron binding energy $B_n$ combined with the back-shifted Fermi-gas (BSFG) model for the energy region above $B_n$. For the latter, the BSFG formula with energy-dependent level density parameter is used and the new parameterization has been carried out in order to obtain the best fit to the new NLD data in the whole data range. The results obtained show that the S-shaped heat capacity, a fingerprint of the pairing phase transition, is more pronounced in even $^{94,96,98}$Mo nuclei than that in odd $^{93,95,97}$Mo isotopes. This result is different with those obtained in two previous studies by R. Chankova et al., [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 73}, 034311 (2006)] and K. Kaneko et al., [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 74}, 024325 (2006)], in which the old NLD data and the BSFG model with energy-independent level density parameter were used. Moreover, the present work suggests that the very strong S-shape observed in the heat capacities of both even and odd Molybdenum isotopes by K. Kaneko et al., [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 74}, 024325 (2006)] should be re-investigated. The present work also suggests that obtain the correct heat capacity and associated pairing phase transition in excited nuclei, one should use the correct NLD data and the best fitted BSFG NLD in the entire region where the experimental data are available.

## Key claim

The S-shaped heat capacity, a signature of the pairing phase transition, is more pronounced in even-even \(^{94,96,98}\)Mo nuclei than in odd-A \(^{93,95,97}\)Mo isotopes, contradicting earlier studies by Chankova (2006) and Kaneko (2006) which used older nuclear level density data and an energy-independent level density parameter; the present work suggests that the very strong S-shape reported by Kaneko (2006) for both even and odd isotopes may be due to a numerical error.

## Method

The authors used the latest recommended experimental nuclear level density (NLD) data for \(^{93-98}\)Mo below the neutron binding energy \(B_n\) (from the 2013 re-analysis by the Oslo group) and combined them with the back-shifted Fermi-gas (BSFG) model using an energy-dependent level density parameter \(a(E)\) to extend the NLD up to 120 MeV. They performed a new parameterization of the BSFG formula to fit the updated NLD data, then constructed the canonical ensemble partition function from the combined experimental-plus-BSFG NLD to extract the heat capacity as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{94}\)Mo, the recommended neutron resonance spacing \(D_0^{\rm rec}\) is 81 eV; the new BSFG parameters are \(\tilde{a} = 10.495\) MeV\(^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 0.081\) MeV\(^{-1}\), and \(E_1 = 0.801\) MeV.
* For \(^{96}\)Mo, the recommended \(D_0^{\rm rec}\) is 66 eV; the new BSFG parameters are \(\tilde{a} = 10.509\) MeV\(^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 0.080\) MeV\(^{-1}\), and \(E_1 = 0.879\) MeV.
* For \(^{98}\)Mo, the recommended \(D_0^{\rm rec}\) is 47 eV; the new BSFG parameters are \(\tilde{a} = 11.071\) MeV\(^{-1}\), \(\gamma = 0.036\) MeV\(^{-1}\), and \(E_1 = 0.764\) MeV.
* The experimental quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) for the even-even isotopes are: \(^{94}\)Mo = 0.1511 \(\pm\) 0.0016, \(^{96}\)Mo = 0.1715 \(\pm\) 0.0014, and \(^{98}\)Mo = 0.1691 \(\pm\) 0.0017.

## Context

This work extends the line of investigation into nuclear pairing phase transitions via heat capacity, previously studied by Chankova (2006) and Kaneko (2006) for the same molybdenum isotopes. It directly contradicts Kaneko (2006) by finding that the S-shaped heat capacity is weak or absent in odd-A isotopes, and it suggests that the strong S-shape reported by Kaneko (2006) for both even and odd nuclei may stem from a numerical error. The use of the updated recommended NLD data from the 2013 re-analysis (exp13) and the energy-dependent BSFG parameterization enables a more reliable extraction of thermodynamic properties, providing a benchmark for future theoretical calculations of pairing correlations in medium-mass nuclei.
