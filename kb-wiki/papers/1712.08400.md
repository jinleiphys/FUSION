# Heavy light tetraquarks from Lattice QCD

> We present preliminary results from a lattice calculation of tetraquark states in the charm and bottom sector of the type $ud\bar{b}\bar{b}$, $us\bar{b}\bar{b}$, $ud\bar{c}\bar{c}$ and $sc\bar{b}\bar{b}$. These calculations are performed on $N_f = 2 + 1 + 1$ MILC ensembles with lattice spacing of $a = 0.12~\mathrm{fm} $ and $a=0.06~\mathrm{fm} $. A relativistic action with overlap fermions is employed for the light and charm quarks while a non-relativistic action with non-perturbatively improved coefficients is used in the bottom sector. Preliminary results provide a clear indication of presence of energy levels below the relevant thresholds of different tetraquark states. While in double charm sector we find shallow bound levels, our results suggest deeply bound levels with double bottom tetraquarks.

## Key claim

Preliminary lattice QCD results provide a clear indication of energy levels below the relevant thresholds for several tetraquark channels, with shallow bound levels in the double charm sector and deeply bound levels suggested for double bottom tetraquarks, though the authors note these results are preliminary and may change with added statistics.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on MILC ensembles with N_f = 2+1+1 flavors at lattice spacings of a = 0.12 fm and a = 0.06 fm. They employed a relativistic overlap action for light and charm quarks and a non-relativistic NRQCD action with non-perturbatively improved coefficients for bottom quarks. A 2x2 correlation matrix was constructed using tetraquark operators (diquark-antidiquark type) and two-meson operators, then solved via a generalized eigenvalue problem to extract ground state energies.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for udbb tetraquark at a = 0.1207 fm and a = 0.0583 fm: results consistent between the two lattice spacings, with binding deepening as pion mass is lowered
- Binding energy for usbb tetraquark at m_pi = 497 MeV: clear indication of a level below the B_s B* threshold, with a less pronounced slope in binding energy versus pion mass compared to udbb
- For udcc tetraquark at a = 0.0583 fm: results lie below but close to the D D* threshold, indicating a shallow bound level
- For scbb tetraquark: calculation performed with all quark masses at their physical values, with the effective mass close to the B_c B*_s threshold

## Context

This work extends recent lattice studies by Bicudo et al. (2016, 2017) and Francis et al. (2016) that identified promising tetraquark channels in the bottom sector, particularly confirming the presence of deeply bound levels in the udbb channel found by Francis et al. (2016). The results enable future systematic studies with improved statistics, lower pion masses, and additional lattice spacings to determine binding energies at the physical point and explore finite volume effects, especially for the scbb channel where binding appears shallow.
