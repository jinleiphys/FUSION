# Light element ($Z=1,2$) production from spontaneous ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf

> The yields of light elements ($Z=1,2$) obtained from spontaneous ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf are treated within a nonequilibrium approach, and the contribution of unstable nuclei and excited bound states is taken into account. These light cluster yields may be used to probe dense matter, and to infer in-medium corrections. Continuum correlations are calculated from scattering phase shifts using the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula, and the effect of medium modification is estimated. The relevant distribution is reconstructed from the measured yields of isotopes. This describes the state of the nucleon system at scission and cluster formation, using only three Lagrange parameters which are the nonequilibrium counterparts of the temperature and chemical potentials, as defined in thermodynamic equilibrium. We concluded that a simple nuclear statistical equilibrium model neglecting continuum correlations and medium effects is not able to describe the measured distribution of H and He isotopes. Moreover, the freeze-out concept may serve as an important ingredient to the nonequilibrium approach using the relevant statistical operator concept.

## Key claim

The measured yields of hydrogen and helium isotopes from spontaneous ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf can be described by a nonequilibrium approach using only three Lagrange parameters, but only if continuum correlations (via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula) and in-medium effects such as Pauli blocking are included; a simple nuclear statistical equilibrium model neglecting these effects fails to reproduce the observed distribution.

## Method

The authors reconstructed the relevant distribution of cluster yields at freeze-out from experimental data using the nonequilibrium statistical operator method, with three Lagrange parameters (the nonequilibrium counterparts of temperature, neutron chemical potential, and proton chemical potential) determined by minimizing the relative square deviation between calculated and measured yields. Continuum correlations were calculated from scattering phase shifts using the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula, and medium modifications (Pauli blocking) were estimated for weakly bound states such as $^6$He, $^5$He, and $^7$He. The final observed yields were related to primary yields by accounting for feeding from unstable nuclei (e.g., $^4$H feeding $^3$H$^f$, $^5$He and $^8$Be feeding $^4$He$^f$).

## Key numbers

- The experimental yield of $^4$He$^f$ is fixed to 10000 (relative scale); $^1$H yield is 160(20), $^2$H yield is 63(3), $^3$H$^f$ yield is 950(90), $^6$He$^f$ yield is 270(30), $^8$He$^f$ yield is 25(5) (all relative to $^4$He$^f$ = 10000).
- The Lagrange parameters for the final distribution (ideal gas approximation) are $\lambda_T^{\rm final} = 0.806219$ MeV, $\lambda_n^{\rm final} = -2.19217$ MeV, $\lambda_p^{\rm final} = -17.7023$ MeV.
- Including excited states and continuum correlations (virial expansion) gives $\lambda_T^{\rm rel,\gamma} = 1.28018$ MeV, $\lambda_n^{\rm rel,\gamma} = -3.18741$ MeV, $\lambda_p^{\rm rel,\gamma} = -16.5882$ MeV.
- The reduction factor for continuum correlations at $\lambda_T = 1.3$ MeV is $R_{d}^{\rm vir}(1.3) = 0.98$ for $^2$H, $R_{4,1}^{\rm vir}(1.3) = 0.06064$ for $^4$H, $R_{5,2}^{\rm vir}(1.3) = 0.70441$ for $^5$He.
- The ratio of primary yields $Y_{^5{\rm He}}/Y_{^4{\rm He}} = 0.21(5)$ and $Y_{^7{\rm He}}/Y_{^6{\rm He}} = 0.21(5)$ were reported from energy spectra.
- The multiplicity of protons emitted in ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf is $6.086 \times 10^{-5}$, corresponding to $Y^{\rm exp}_{1,1} \approx 190.2$ relative to $\alpha$ yield.
- The total neutron multiplicity in $^{252}$Cf fission is $3.81 \pm 0.05$, giving a scission neutron multiplicity of $0.290 \pm 0.008$ and an experimental yield $Y^{\rm exp}_{1,0} \approx 906250$ relative to $\alpha$ yield.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of ternary fission yields beyond the interpolation formula of Valski (Valskii2) and the Boltzmann-distribution approach used by Koester (Koester) and others, by applying the nonequilibrium statistical operator method of Zubarev (NSO) to the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf. It builds on the Albergo method (Albergo) for determining temperature and chemical potentials from heavy-ion collision yields, but explicitly accounts for continuum correlations via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula and in-medium effects such as Pauli blocking, which were previously treated for specific channels by Ropke (R20). The results enable inference of nucleon densities at scission and constrain the equation of state of low-density, low-temperature nuclear matter, while also motivating further experimental studies of ternary-particle-unstable nuclei as noted by Mutterer (Mutterer08).
