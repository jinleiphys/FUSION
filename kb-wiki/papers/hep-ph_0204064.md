# Superconducting K strings in high density QCD

> Recently it has been argued that the ground state of high density QCD is likely be a combination of the CFL-phase along with condensation of the K^0 field. This state spontaneously breaks a global U(1)_Y symmetry, therefore one would expect the formation of U(1)_Y global strings. We discuss the core structure of these strings and demonstrate that under some conditions the global U(1)_Y symmetry may not be restored inside the string, in contrast with the standard expectations. Instead, K^+ condensation occurs inside the core of the string if a relevant parameter \cosθ_K = \mkzero^2/μ_eff^2 is larger than some critical value θ_K > θ_crit. If this phenomenon happens, the U(1)_Y strings become superconducting and may considerably influence the magnetic properties of dense quark matter, in particular in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Under certain conditions, the global U(1)_Y symmetry may not be restored inside the core of K^0 strings in the CFL+K^0 phase of high density QCD; instead, K^+ condensation occurs inside the core if the parameter cosθ_K ≡ m_{K^0}^2/μ_eff^2 is larger than some critical value θ_K > θ_crit, making the strings superconducting.

## Method

The authors analyze the core structure of global K^0 strings using an effective field theory for the kaon doublet Φ = (K^+, K^0) with a Mexican hat potential, including explicit isospin symmetry breaking via a term δm^2 Φ^† τ_3 Φ. They expand the energy in the K^0 string background to quadratic order in the K^+ field, reduce the problem to a two-dimensional Schrodinger equation for the K^+ mode, and solve numerically for the critical condensation angle θ_crit where the lowest eigenvalue becomes negative. They also compare results using the exponential representation of Goldstone fields from the original effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The critical condensation angle is numerically estimated as θ_crit ≈ 53° for the parameters m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 8 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, μ = 500 MeV, and Δ = 100 MeV, which gives ε ≡ a m_s (m_d - m_u)/(2 μ_eff^2) ≈ 0.1.
- A second estimate using the exponential representation of Goldstone fields gives θ_crit ≈ 70°.
- The parametric expression for the critical angle is sin(θ_crit/2) ≈ constant × (Δ/m_s) √[(m_d - m_u)/m_s], with Δ ≈ 100 MeV being the superconducting gap.
- The string core radius is parametrically given by r_core ∼ v_π / μ_eff (sin θ_K/2), where v_π^2 ≈ 1/3 and f_π ∼ μ.
- The string tension α ∼ 2π v_π^2 f_π^2 ln R, where f_π ∼ μ is the decay constant in dense QCD.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of global U(1)_Y strings in the CFL+K^0 phase of QCD, previously discussed by the same authors and collaborators (FZstrings, KRstrings), by demonstrating that the core can undergo a transition to a superconducting state with K^+ condensation, analogous to core transitions observed in superfluid ^3He-B vortices (He review). The phenomenon is related to the general theory of superconducting cosmic strings (Witten, Turner, Core). The results suggest that if the CFL+K^0 phase occurs in neutron stars or in the early universe during the QCD phase transition (ARZ), these superconducting strings could influence magnetic properties and possibly contribute to dark matter.
