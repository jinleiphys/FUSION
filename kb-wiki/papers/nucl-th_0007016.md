# Extra dimensions, SN1987a, and nucleon-nucleon scattering data

> One of the strongest constraints on the existence of large, compact, "gravity-only" dimensions comes from SN1987a. If the rate of energy loss into these putative extra dimensions is too high, then the neutrino pulse from the supernova will differ from that actually seen. The dominant mechanism for the production of Kaluza-Klein gravitons and dilatons in the supernova is via gravistrahlung and dilastrahlung from the nucleon-nucleon system. In this paper we compute the rates for these processes in a model-independent way using low-energy theorems which relate the emissivities to the measured nucleon-nucleon cross section. This is possible because for soft gravitons and dilatons the leading contribution to the energy-loss rate is from graphs in which the gravitational radiation is produced from external nucleon legs. Previous calculations neglected these mechanisms. We re-evaluate the bounds on toroidally-compactified "gravity-only" dimensions (GODs), and find that consistency with the observed SN1987a neutrino signal requires that if there are two such dimensions then their radius must be less than 1 micron.

## Key claim

The paper derives model-independent bounds on the size of large, compact "gravity-only" dimensions (GODs) from SN1987a by computing the energy loss due to Kaluza-Klein graviton and dilaton emission from nucleon-nucleon collisions, using low-energy theorems that relate the emissivity to measured nucleon-nucleon cross sections rather than relying on the one-pion-exchange approximation used in prior work.

## Method

The authors derived low-energy theorems for the emission of soft Kaluza-Klein gravitons and dilatons from the nucleon-nucleon system, analogous to theorems for photon bremsstrahlung, which relate the bremsstrahlung amplitude to the on-shell nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude. They computed the energy-loss rates at leading and next-to-leading order in the soft expansion, finding that the next-to-leading order correction vanishes, and then used these rates together with the Raffelt criterion (emissivity less than 10^19 ergs/g/s) to constrain the radius of toroidally-compactified extra dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For two extra dimensions, the radius must be less than 1 micron to be consistent with the SN1987a neutrino signal.
- The Raffelt criterion used is an emissivity threshold of 10^19 ergs/g/s, above which energy loss would invalidate the current understanding of Type-II supernovae neutrino signals.
- The inverse radii of extra dimensions are constrained to be larger than 1 TeV from collider experiments.

## Context

This work extends the earlier bounds on extra dimensions from SN1987a by Arkani-Hamed et al. (1998) and the calculations by Cullen and Perelstein (1999) and Barger et al. (1999), which used the uncontrolled one-pion-exchange approximation for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The model-independent approach using low-energy theorems, previously applied to axion and neutrino bremsstrahlung by Hanhart, Phillips, and Reddy (2000), eliminates the two-body dynamics as a source of uncertainty. The results provide a firm foundation for future calculations of many-body corrections to these processes.
