# Finite-volume analysis and universal scaling signatures near the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> For quantifying the universal properties of the chiral phase transition in QCD through numerical calculations on a discrete space-time lattice, one needs to perform controlled extrapolations to the continuum and infinite-volume limits followed by an extrapolation to the limit of massless light quarks. We discuss here, the results on the latter two limits at still finite lattice spacings. We use here for chiral symmetry breaking, an improved order parameter free of additive and multiplicative divergences and we analyse its volume and quark mass dependence. Comparing to the expected universal behavior in the chiral limit, we quantify deviations from the universal finite-size scaling behavior as function of the light to strange quark mass ratio.

# Finite-volume analysis and universal scaling signatures near the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that systematic finite-volume analysis with controlled infinite-volume extrapolations at small quark masses near the chiral transition temperature reveals universal scaling signatures consistent with the 3-d O(2) universality class, but only for light-to-strange quark mass ratios H ≤ 1/80; at larger H values (H ≥ 1/40), significant deviations from universal finite-volume scaling persist even on large lattices.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge and HISQ actions implemented in SIMULATeQCD, with fixed temporal extent Nτ = 8 and varying spatial extent Nσ. They used a subtracted chiral order parameter M that eliminates additive ultraviolet divergences and linear contributions in the quark mass ratio H = mℓ/ms, then performed infinite-volume extrapolations using an ansatz inspired by the finite-volume scaling function of the 3-d O(2) universality class, comparing the scaled order parameter M/H^(1/δ) against the finite-size scaling variable zL.

## Key numbers
- Light quark masses were varied corresponding to pion masses mπ ∈ [45:140] MeV
- Quark mass ratios H = mℓ/ms were studied in the range H ∈ [1/240 : 1/27], extending existing data sets for H ∈ [1/160 : 1/27] and adding new data at H = 1/240
- The new preliminary estimate for the chiral transition temperature on Nτ = 8 lattices is Tc = 145.1 MeV
- The preliminary slope m0 = 28 for the scaling relation M = m0 H^(1/δ) at Tc is approximately 10% smaller than the value obtained in Karsch (2023)
- The aspect ratio Nσ/Nτ needed to reach 2% accuracy in infinite-volume extrapolation increases from approximately 5 at H = 1/27 to approximately 7 at H = 1/160
- Data at H = 1/160 and H = 1/240 show agreement with universal finite-volume scaling even for zL ∼ 0.6, corresponding to smaller lattice volumes
- Temperature range studied was 142.8 MeV ≤ T ≤ 147.4 MeV

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume scaling analysis of pseudo-critical temperatures in (2+1)-flavor QCD performed by HotQCD (2019) using the 3-d O(4) universality class scaling functions, which covered mass ratios down to H = 1/160. The authors push to smaller quark masses (H = 1/240) and perform a more systematic analysis of finite-volume corrections to identify universal contributions. The results enable a more precise determination of Tc and the underlying universality class, potentially distinguishing between O(4) and U(2)×U(2) universality classes in the continuum limit, and provide a more stringent bound on the QCD critical point location as discussed in Karsch (2019).
