# Two-gluon correlations and initial conditions for small-x evolution

> We derive the effective action of the hard large-x valence charges up to fourth order in their density. Such non-Gaussian weight functionals contribute at leading order in N_c to the connected two-gluon production diagrams which determine di-hadron correlations. The corresponding diagrams are not necessarily (highly) suppressed by the density of valence charges since their infrared divergences differ from those obtained in a Gaussian theory. Therefore, it appears prudent to include such higher dimensional operators when determining initial ensembles for JIMWLK evolution of higher n-point functions of Wilson lines.

## Key claim

The effective action for hard valence color charges in a finite nucleus or high-multiplicity proton-proton collision includes non-Gaussian terms up to quartic order in the charge density, which contribute to connected two-gluon production at leading order in \(N_c\) and are not necessarily suppressed by the density of valence charges because their infrared divergences differ from those of a Gaussian theory.

## Method

The authors derive the effective action for a system of \(k\) quarks in SU(3) by expanding the probability distribution for the representation labeled by Dynkin labels \((m,n)\) in powers of \(1/k\) up to order \(1/k^3\), expressing the result in terms of the quadratic, cubic, and quartic Casimir operators. They then convert the action to a continuum form in terms of the color charge density \(\rho^a(x)\), obtaining couplings \(\mu^2\), \(\kappa_3\), and \(\kappa_4\) that scale with the mass number \(A\) and the coupling \(g\). The two-point and four-point functions of the color charge density are computed perturbatively in \(1/\kappa_4\) using lattice regularization, and the resulting expressions are used to evaluate the color factor and momentum dependence of correlated two-gluon production diagrams in the ridge kinematics.

## Key numbers

* The quartic coupling in the effective action is \(\kappa_4 \sim g^4 (A/\pi R^2)^3 \sim O(g^4 A)\), where \(A\) is the mass number and \(R\) the nuclear radius.
* The cubic (odderon) coupling is \(\kappa_3 \sim 3 g^3 A^2/(\pi R^2)^2 \sim O(g^3 A^{2/3})\).
* The Gaussian variance is \(\mu^2 \equiv g^2 A/(2\pi R^2) \sim O(g^2 A^{1/3})\).
* The uncorrelated (single-inclusive) squared cross section scales as \(\sim (g A^{1/3})^8 N_c^2 (N_c^2-1)^2\).
* The connected contribution from the Gaussian action (Fig. 5) scales as \(\sim N_c^2 (N_c^2-1) \; \pi R^2\; \tilde\mu^8/(p^2 q^2) \int dk^2/k^4 \cdot 1/(p-k)^2 \cdot 1/(q-k)^2\) and is suppressed by \(1/(N_c^2-1)\) relative to uncorrelated production.
* The connected contribution from the quartic term (Fig. 6) scales as \(\sim g^8 A^{5/3} \; N_c^2 (N_c^2-1)^2\), which is of the same order in \(g^2\) and \(N_c\) as the uncorrelated contribution but suppressed by one power of \(A\).
* The odderon contribution at order \(1/\kappa_3^2\) scales as \(\sim g^8 A^{5/3} \; N_c^2 (N_c^2-1) (N_c - 4/N_c)\), of the same order in \(A\) as the quartic contribution but suppressed by one power of \(N_c\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) by deriving non-Gaussian corrections to the effective action for finite nuclei or high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions, building on the odderon operator derived by Jeon and Venugopalan (2005). The results provide initial conditions for JIMWLK evolution (Jalilian-Marian et al., 1997) of higher \(n\)-point functions of Wilson lines, which are needed to describe the ridge correlation observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions at the LHC (CMS Collaboration, 2010) and in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The analysis enables future non-perturbative calculations of two-particle correlations at leading order in \(N_c\) and suggests that numerical solutions of JIMWLK evolution should include the quartic and cubic operators in the initial action.
