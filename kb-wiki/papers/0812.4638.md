# The Flavours of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Quarks of other flavours than up and down, i.e. $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks, have been long recognized as effective probes of the structure of hot QCD matter. In this talk, I review some of the motivations for their investigation and discuss the salient results obtained so far, with a focus on the results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Some ideas for future studies are also mentioned.

## Key claim

Strange, charm, and bottom quarks serve as effective probes of hot QCD matter, with the existing data from RHIC best explained by the hypothesis that these quarks are deconfined during the early phase of the reaction and that most multi-strange hadrons and many heavy quark bound states form near the hadronization boundary, suffering only rare interactions after production.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical and experimental results on strange, charm, and bottom quark production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, focusing on strangeness enhancement, chemical equilibration, and the role of deconfinement. It discusses calculations using the three-flavour PNJL model to compute thermal rates of strange quark pair production, including the effects of the Polyakov loop on gluon and quark spectra near the critical temperature. The review also examines quark recombination models for hadron formation and the use of heavy quark bound states as probes of the medium.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass is approximately \(m_s \approx 104 \pm 30\) MeV.
- The transition between confined and deconfined matter occurs between \(0.9\,T_c\) and \(1.1\,T_c\).
- The \(\Omega\) and \(\overline{\Omega}\) yield in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS is enhanced by a factor of about 20 relative to p+p collisions.
- At RHIC, the enhancement for \(\Omega(\overline{\Omega})\) in central Au+Au collisions reaches up to a factor of 10.
- The gluonic reaction rate for \(gg\to s\bar{s}\) falls below the light quark rate at \(T\approx 230\) MeV.
- Strangeness equilibration requires initial conditions in the range \(T > 300\) MeV.
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature for the hadron gas is \(T_f^{(kin)} \approx 100\) MeV.
- The low-transverse mass spectrum of \(J/\psi\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV is very close to that measured for the \(\Omega\) hyperon.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work by Rafelski (1982) on strangeness enhancement in quark-gluon plasma and the subsequent systematic study by Koch (1986) on strange quark equilibration in hadron yields. It discusses results from the SPS and RHIC experiments, including the observation of valence quark scaling of elliptic flow predicted by recombination models (Fries 2003, Greco 2003). The paper enables future studies of heavy quark transport and hadronization at the LHC and with upgraded RHIC detectors, and identifies open questions about model-independent medium properties such as susceptibilities, chemical relaxation times, and diffusion coefficients.
