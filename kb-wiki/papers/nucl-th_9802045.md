# Onset of T=0 Pairing and Deformations in High Spin States of the N=Z Nucleus 48Cr

> The yrast line of the N=Z nucleus 48Cr is studied up to high spins by means of the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method including the T=0 and T=1 isospin pairing channels. A Skyrme force is used in the mean-field channel together with a zero-range density-dependent interaction in the pairing channels. The extensions of the method needed to incorporate the neutron-proton pairing are summarized. The T=0 pairing correlations are found to play a decisive role for deformation properties and excitation energies above 16hbar which is the maximum spin that can be obtained in the f7/2 subshell.

# Onset of T=0 Pairing and Deformations in High Spin States of the N=Z Nucleus 48Cr

## Key claim

T=0 neutron-proton pairing correlations play a decisive role for deformation properties and excitation energies of 48Cr above 16ℏ, which is the maximum spin obtainable within the f7/2 subshell; however, the authors qualify that solutions in the spin region I=18-30ℏ are obtained *only* in the presence of T=0 pairing correlations, and if this mode is switched off, neither CHFB nor pure CHF solutions could be found.

## Method

The authors performed cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations using the Skyrme force SIII for the mean-field channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force for both the T=0 and T=1 isospin pairing channels. The Bogoliubov transformation was generalized to mix proton and neutron quasiparticle states, with parity as the only remaining symmetry, allowing real Bogoliubov transformations and a real pairing tensor. The pairing strength for T=0 was scaled to 1.3 times the T=1 strength, based on proton-neutron scattering data showing the spin-triplet interaction is stronger than the spin-singlet.

## Key numbers

- The T=1 pairing strength G_{T=1} was fixed to 1000 MeV·fm³, with the T=0 strength set to 1.3 × G_{T=1} (i.e., 1300 MeV·fm³)
- The density cutoff parameter ρ_c in the pairing force was taken as 0.16 fm⁻³
- The T=0 pairing correlations reach a maximum at I = 24ℏ
- At I = 24ℏ, the deformation minimum occurs at β = 0.11, γ = 60°
- At I = 32ℏ, the CHFB solution with T=0 pairing gives total quadrupole moment Q₀ = 205 fm² and β = 0.26, compared to the pure CHF solution with Q₀ = 123 fm² and β = 0.16
- The charge quadrupole moment at I = 32ℏ is Q_c = 104 e·fm² with T=0 pairing versus 63 e·fm² for CHF
- The g-factor is nearly constant along the yrast line at approximately 0.52
- Above 16ℏ, γ transition energies become almost constant around 5 MeV, with slight shifts at 24 and 32ℏ
- The occupation of g9/2 (d3/2) grows (decreases) from 0 (1) to 1 (0.4) in the spin range 16ℏ to 32ℏ, reaching 0.6 (0.73) at I = 24ℏ

## Context

This work extends previous cranked Hartree-Fock calculations on 48Cr by Caurier et al. (1995) that used the Gogny force and reproduced the backbending at low spins but did not address states above 16ℏ. The study also builds on earlier schematic-force treatments of neutron-proton pairing by Goodman (1979) and others. The results enable experimental searches for fingerprints of T=0 pairing in high-spin states of N=Z nuclei, suggesting that the smooth increase of quadrupole moment and nearly constant transition energies above 16ℏ are characteristic signatures. The finding that T=0 pairing becomes active precisely where pairing would not normally be expected opens questions about the role of spin-orbit partner pairs (like f7/2 and f5/2) at high angular momenta, and about the connection to the reduction of Wigner energy in the fp-shell noted by Satula et al. (1997).
