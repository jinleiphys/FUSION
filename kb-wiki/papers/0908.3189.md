# R-evolution: Improving perturbative QCD

> Perturbative QCD results in the MSbar scheme can be dramatically improved by switching to a scheme that accounts for the dominant power law dependence on the factorization scale in the operator product expansion. We introduce the ``MSR scheme'' which achieves this in a Lorentz and gauge invariant way. The MSR scheme has a very simple relation to MSbar, and can be easily used to reanalyze MSbar results. Results in MSR depend on a cutoff parameter R, in addition to the mu of MSbar. R variations can be used to independently estimate i) the size of power corrections, and ii) higher order perturbative corrections (much like mu in MSbar). We give two examples at three-loop order, the ratio of mass splittings in the B*-B and D*-D systems, and the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule as a function of momentum transfer Q in deep inelastic scattering. Comparing to data, the perturbative MSR results work well even for Q ~ 1 GeV, and the size of power corrections is reduced compared to those in MSbar.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the MSR (MSbar-R) scheme, which modifies the standard MSbar scheme by subtracting a perturbative series that captures the dominant power-law dependence on the factorization scale, thereby removing infrared renormalons and improving perturbative convergence. The authors claim this works well even for momentum transfers Q as low as 1 GeV, with reduced power corrections compared to MSbar.

## Method

The authors define the MSR scheme by taking the logarithm of the Wilson coefficient and subtracting a term proportional to (R/Q)^p times the same coefficient evaluated at scale R, where R is a new cutoff parameter. They formulate an R-evolution renormalization group equation in the R variable to sum large logarithms between the hard scale Q and the low scale R0 near Lambda_QCD. The method is applied to two observables at three-loop order: the ratio of mass splittings in the B*-B and D*-D systems (p=1 renormalon), and the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule in deep inelastic scattering (p=2 renormalon).

## Key numbers

- For the ratio r of B*-B to D*-D mass splittings at N3LL(MSR): r = 0.860 ± 0.065 (power correction) ± 0.008 (perturbative), compared to experimental values of 0.886 (D_u,d, B_u,d) and 0.854 (D_s, B_s)
- The MSR perturbative uncertainty from varying R1 is Δr ≈ ±0.008, compared to Δr ≈ ±0.068 from μ variation in MSbar at N3LO
- For the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule, the MSR scheme gives power corrections θ1(R0,R0) in the range -0.01 GeV^2 to 0.01 GeV^2, compared to the naive dimensional analysis estimate of ~0.1 GeV^2 in MSbar
- The MSR scheme uses R0 values in the range 0.7 GeV to 1.2 GeV for both examples

## Context

This work extends the concept of renormalon subtraction schemes, previously implemented in the RS scheme by Campanario et al. (2005), by providing a simpler definition directly from MSbar coefficients without needing to compute Borel pole residues. The MSR scheme enables improved perturbative predictions for observables like the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (originally formulated by Ellis and Jaffe in 1973) and heavy meson mass splittings (with three-loop computations by Grozin in 2007), allowing reliable predictions at lower momentum transfers than possible in MSbar. The method opens the way for systematic treatment of power corrections in OPE analyses across many QCD observables.
