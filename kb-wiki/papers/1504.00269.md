# Electric dipole moment of $^{225}$Ra due to P- and T-violating weak interactions

> We report rigorous calculations of electric dipole moment (EDM) in $^{225}$Ra due to parity and time-reversal violating tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) and nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interactions between the electrons and nucleus by employing the relativistic all order coupled-cluster (RCC) methods at various levels of approximation. The most accurate EDM ($d_A$) results are obtained as $d_A=-10.04\times 10^{-20} C_T <σ_n>|e|cm$ and $d_A^{NSM}=-6.79 \times 10^{-17} S (|e|fm^3)^{-1} |e|cm$ with $C_T$ and $S$ are the T-PT coupling constant and NSM respectively. Due to exhaustive treatment of the electron correlation effects in these calculations, the EDM results for the corresponding T-PT and NSM interactions reduce by about 45\% and 23\%, respectively, from the previously known values. Nonetheless they are still found to be 2-3 times larger than the $^{199}$Hg results. Validity of the RCC results are countenanced by comparing our calculations at the zeroth order Dirac-Fock method and all order random-phase approximation with the other groups. In view of the current experimental progress in the $^{225}$Ra EDM measurement, our high accuracy calculations are very useful to yield limits on $C_T$ and $S$ in this atom. Thusly, it is possible to realize more accurate limits on the electron-quark T-PT interaction and $θ_{QCD}$ parameter of particle physics in future.

# Electric dipole moment of $^{225}$Ra due to P- and T-violating weak interactions

## Key claim

The relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) calculations yield atomic EDM values for $^{225}$Ra that are reduced by about 45% (for T-PT interaction) and 23% (for NSM interaction) from previously known values, yet remain 2-3 times larger than the corresponding $^{199}$Hg results, making $^{225}$Ra a promising candidate for improved limits on CP-violating parameters once experimental sensitivity improves.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic all-order coupled-cluster method with single and double excitations (CCSD method) in the Dirac-Coulomb framework to compute the ground state wave function and first-order perturbed wave functions for $^{225}$Ra. The atomic EDM was evaluated using the expectation value approach with the T-PT and NSM interaction Hamiltonians, including contributions from the non-truncative series of cluster operators through an iterative procedure. Intermediate calculations at the Dirac-Fock (DF) and random-phase approximation (RPA) levels were performed for comparison, and corrections from Breit interaction, QED effects, and basis truncation were estimated using RPA.

## Key numbers

- Final atomic EDM due to T-PT interaction: $d_A = -10.04 \times 10^{-20} C_T \langle \sigma_n \rangle |e|$ cm
- Final atomic EDM due to NSM interaction: $d_A^{NSM} = -6.79 \times 10^{-17} S (|e| \text{fm}^3)^{-1} |e|$ cm
- Dipole polarizability from CCSD method: $\alpha_d = 247.76$ $e a_0^3$
- RPA results for comparison: $\alpha_d = 296.85$ $e a_0^3$, $d_A^{TPT} = -16.66 \times 10^{-20} C_T \langle \sigma_n \rangle |e|$ cm, $d_A^{NSM} = -8.12 \times 10^{-17} S (|e| \text{fm}^3)^{-1} |e|$ cm
- Upper bound on NSM from combining with experimental limit: $S < 7.4 \times 10^{-6} |e|$ fm$^3$
- Extracted upper bounds on pion-nucleon couplings: $|\bar{g}_0| < 3.6 \times 10^{-7}$ and $|\bar{g}_1| < 9.1 \times 10^{-8}$
- Extracted upper bound on QCD theta parameter: $|\theta_{QCD}| < 2.0 \times 10^{-5}$
- Extracted upper bound on quark chromo-EDM difference: $|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 4.6 \times 10^{-22} |e|$ cm

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of $^{225}$Ra EDM by Dzuba and Coworkers (2002, 2009) using CI+MBPT methods and by Latha and Amjith using RPA, demonstrating that higher-order correlation effects (non-RPA contributions) substantially reduce the predicted EDM values. The calculations are motivated by the first experimental measurement of the $^{225}$Ra EDM at Argonne National Laboratory (Lu, 2015), which currently gives a less stringent limit than the $^{199}$Hg experiment (Griffith, 2009) but is expected to improve rapidly using cold-atom techniques and the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams. The results enable extraction of improved limits on the electron-quark T-PT coupling constant, the QCD theta parameter, and quark chromo-EDMs once more sensitive $^{225}$Ra measurements become available.
