# Matter and charge radius of 6He in the hyperspherical-harmonics approach

> We present ab-initio calculations of the binding energy and radii of the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He using two-body low-momentum interactions based on chiral effective field theory potentials. Calculations are performed via a hyperspherical harmonics expansion where the convergence is sped up introducing an effective interaction for non-local potentials. The latter is essential to reach a satisfactory convergence of the extended matter radius and of the point-proton radius. The dependence of the results on the resolution scale is studied. A correlation is found between the radii and the two-neutron separation energy. The importance of three-nucleon forces is pointed out comparing our results and previous calculations to experiment.

## Key claim

The paper presents ab-initio calculations of the binding energy and radii of the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He using only two-body low-momentum interactions based on chiral effective field theory potentials, finding that the results for the point-proton radius lie at too low values compared to experiment, which highlights the importance of including three-nucleon forces that are not yet incorporated in this study.

## Method

The authors combined renormalization group evolution of chiral EFT potentials to low-momentum interactions (Vlowk) with the ab-initio hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method for 6He, using an effective interaction of the Lee-Suzuki type (EIHH) to speed up convergence for non-local potentials. The Entem and Machleidt N3LO chiral NN potential with cutoff 500 MeV was taken as a starting point and evolved to low-momentum interactions with cutoffs ranging from 1.8 to 2.4 fm^-1. The wave function was expanded in hyperspherical harmonics with grandangular momentum up to Kmax=14, corresponding to about 3 million basis states.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground-state energy with Vlowk potential at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1: -28.65(5) MeV (agreement with Faddeev-Yakubovsky result from Nogga)
- 4He ground-state energy varies from 29.30 to 27.40 MeV for cutoff range 1.8 to 2.4 fm^-1
- 6He two-neutron separation energy S2n: 1.036(7) MeV at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, 0.82(4) MeV at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, 0.48(9) MeV at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- 6He point-proton radius: 1.78(1) fm at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, 1.804(9) fm at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, 1.853(9) fm at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- 6He matter radius: 2.30(6) fm at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, 2.33(5) fm at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, 2.39(3) fm at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- 6He point-neutron radius: 2.51(6) fm at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, 2.54(6) fm at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, 2.60(4) fm at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- 4He point-proton radius: 1.427(7) fm at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, 1.435(7) fm at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, 1.464(7) fm at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- Experimental 6He charge radius: 2.060(8) fm (from Maxime), converted to point-proton radius of 1.938(23) fm or 1.953(22) fm depending on proton radius used
- Experimental matter radius from ion scattering: 2.33(4) fm (Tan92), 2.30(7) fm (Alk97), 2.37(5) fm (Kis05)
- 6He spin-orbit correction to point-proton radius: -0.0828 fm^2 at cutoff 1.8 fm^-1, -0.0822 fm^2 at cutoff 2.0 fm^-1, -0.0808 fm^2 at cutoff 2.4 fm^-1
- Cutoff variation over 1.8-2.4 fm^-1 leads to about 8% running in energy and 4% in radii

## Context

This work extends previous ab-initio calculations of 6He using the hyperspherical harmonics method (Sonia_EPJ) by achieving converged results for radii through the effective interaction approach, and goes beyond coupled-cluster theory investigations (Hag06) by studying cutoff variation for both binding energy and radii. The results are compared to other approaches including No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) with CD-Bonn and INOY potentials (NCSMHe), Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) with UCOM potential (FMD), Microscopic Cluster Model (MCM) with Minnesota potential (Brida), and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) with three-nucleon forces (GFMC_Enrico_Fermi_School). The correlation found between radii and two-neutron separation energy is analogous to the Phillips and Tjon lines in few-body systems, where three-body physics manifests as a breaking from the NN-only band. This work enables future inclusion of evolved three-nucleon forces using similarity renormalization group evolution in momentum space (Hebeler) to reduce the cutoff dependence and improve agreement with experiment.
