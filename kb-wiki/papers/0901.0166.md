# Charmonium suppression at RHIC and SPS: a hadronic baseline

> A kinetic equation approach is applied to model anomalous J/psi suppression at RHIC and SPS by absorption in a hadron resonance gas which successfully describes statistical hadron production in both experiments. The puzzling rapidity dependence of the PHENIX data is reproduced as a geometric effect due to a longer absorption path for J/psi production at forward rapidity.

## Key claim
The puzzling rapidity dependence of PHENIX data (stronger J/psi suppression at forward rapidity than at midrapidity) can be reproduced by a purely hadronic model without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, as a geometric effect from a longer absorption path for J/psi production at forward rapidity.

## Method
A kinetic equation approach was applied to model J/psi suppression by absorption in a hadron resonance gas (HRG) in thermal equilibrium, expanding longitudinally and transversally according to relativistic hydrodynamics. The model simultaneously accounts for absorption in cold nuclear matter (CNM) with a constant cross section sigma_{J/psi N}=4 mb and in the hadron gas via inelastic scattering on hadrons, using a universal cross section for baryons and sigma_m=2 sigma_{J/psi N}/3 for mesons. The survival probability was computed by averaging over trajectories of J/psi particles through the expanding medium, with the freeze-out temperature set to T_f.o.=150 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for most central Au-Au collisions: T_0 = 222.6 MeV (epsilon_0 = 5.4 GeV/fm^3)
- Initial temperature for most central Cu-Cu collisions: T_0 = 201.8 MeV (epsilon_0 = 2.5 GeV/fm^3)
- Absorption cross section for J/psi on nucleons: sigma_{J/psi N}=4 mb, consistent with sigma_abs=4.2 +/- 0.5 mb from NA50 p-A measurements
- Power a in temperature evolution T(tau)=T_0*tau^{-a} varies in range a=0.148-0.156 for epsilon_0 in range 5.5-0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f.o.=150 MeV

## Context
This work extends the model of Blaizot (1989) and Prorok (2000) to include non-zero rapidity, providing a unified hadronic description of J/psi suppression data from NA38, NA50, NA60 at SPS and PHENIX at RHIC. The authors note that the hadronic absorption cross sections employed (4 mb) cannot be reconciled with microscopic quark model calculations by Martins (1994), Barnes (2003), or Ivanov (2003) which peak below 2 mb, suggesting strong medium dependence is required. The model enables a baseline for future unified descriptions of charmonium suppression kinetics within a quantum statistical approach to strongly coupled QGP.
