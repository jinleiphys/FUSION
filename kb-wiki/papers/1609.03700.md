# Collisions of Small Nuclei in the Thermal Model

## Key claim

The thermal model predicts that the maxima observed in strange particle-to-pion ratios as a function of beam energy in heavy-ion collisions are reduced when considering smaller nuclei, but the Λ/π⁺ ratio shows the strongest maximum which survives even in collisions of small nuclei. The results are presented as qualitative trends, with the caveat that variations of temperature and baryon chemical potential with system size have not been taken into account.

## Method

The authors apply a thermal-statistical model with hadronic resonances in chemical equilibrium, characterized by freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB. For small systems, exact strangeness conservation is implemented via a canonical ensemble with a strangeness correlation volume Vc, parameterized by a radius RC that is varied from 3.0 fm down to 1.0 fm to simulate decreasing system size. Particle ratios are calculated along the chemical freeze-out line as a function of center-of-mass energy √sNN, using freeze-out parameters from heavy-ion collisions (Cleymans 2005).

## Key numbers

- The maxima in K⁺/π⁺ and Λ/π⁺ ratios occur at a center-of-mass energy of around 10 GeV.
- The strangeness correlation radius RC is varied from 3.0 fm (top curve) to 2.5, 2.0, 1.5, and finally 1.0 fm (bottom curve) to simulate decreasing system size.
- For pp collisions, the correlation radius RC is about 1.5 fm (Kraus 2007).
- The overall volume parameter R is kept fixed at 10 fm, corresponding to central Pb-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the thermal-statistical model description of particle production, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Cleymans 2005, Andronic 2005), to collisions of small nuclei such as p-p systems. The maxima in particle ratios have been conjectured to indicate a phase change to deconfined matter (Gazdzicki 1998) and coincide with the energy regime of maximum baryon density (Randrup 2006). The study enables systematic investigation of how the strangeness suppression mechanism, implemented via canonical ensemble with a correlation volume (Braun-Munzinger 2001, Braun-Munzinger 2003), evolves from large to small collision systems, with the Λ/π⁺ ratio identified as the most robust observable for detecting the maximum even in small systems.
