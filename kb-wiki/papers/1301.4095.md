# J/Psi production in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We compute cross sections for incoherent and coherent diffractive J/Psi production in ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions using two different dipole models fitted to HERA data. We obtain a reasonably good description of the available ALICE data for coherent J/Ψproduction and present our prediction for the incoherent cross section. We also find that while the normalization of the cross section depends quite strongly on the dipole model and vector meson wave function used, the rapidity dependence is very well constrained.

## Key claim

The paper computes cross sections for coherent and incoherent diffractive J/Psi production in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions using two dipole models fitted to HERA data, finding that while the absolute normalization depends strongly on the choice of dipole model and vector meson wave function, the rapidity dependence is very well constrained and agrees with ALICE data.

## Method

The authors use the dipole picture of photon-nucleus scattering, where a photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters off the target. They employ two phenomenological dipole cross section parametrizations with realistic impact parameter dependence: the IIM model (a parametrization including features of BK evolution) and the fIPsat model (a factorized approximation of the IPsat model with DGLAP-evolved gluon distribution). The diffractive vector meson production cross section in ultraperipheral collisions is obtained by folding the equivalent photon flux from one nucleus with the photon-nucleus cross section, with coherent and incoherent contributions separated by averaging the scattering amplitude over nucleon positions in the nucleus using a Woods-Saxon distribution.

## Key numbers

- For coherent J/Psi production in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the fIPsat model with Gaus-LC wavefunction overshoots ALICE data points by a factor of approximately 1.4
- The ratio of coherent cross sections at midrapidity to y=2 is 1.41-1.46 across all models and wavefunctions
- The ratio of incoherent cross sections at midrapidity to y=2 is 1.35-1.43 across all models and wavefunctions
- For PHENIX data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and y=0, the fIPsat model with gaus-LC wavefunction gives 109 microbarns, compared to the measured 76 +/- 34 microbarns
- The gluon Bjorken x probed at midrapidity for J/Psi production at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV is approximately 0.001
- The IIM model uses B_p=5.59 GeV^{-2} for the proton impact parameter profile; the fIPsat model uses B_p=4.0 GeV^{-2}
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear radius is R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}) fm with surface thickness d=0.54 fm

## Context

This work extends the dipole model calculations of Lappi and Mantysaari (2010) for photon-nucleus scattering to ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions at LHC energies. It provides predictions that can be tested against ALICE data (Abelev 2012) for coherent J/Psi production and presents new predictions for incoherent production. The results enable future measurements of the transverse spatial distribution of gluons in nuclei through the t-dependence of incoherent scattering, and the nuclear transparency ratio can probe dipole absorption in nuclear matter.
