# Isotopic analysis of 295 MeV proton scattering off $^{204,206,208}$Pb for improvement of neutron densities and radii

> A new method of reaction analysis for proton elastic scattering is proposed, combining systematic analyses of nuclear structure and reactions in a series of isotopes. This method is applied to $^{204}$Pb$(p,p)$, $^{206}$Pb$(p,p)$, and $^{208}$Pb$(p,p)$ at $E_p=295$ MeV to obtain improved neutron densities and radii from experimental cross section data. The reaction calculation is performed according to the relativistic impulse approximation using the modified Murdock and Horowitz model with density-dependent effective $NN$ interaction. Analysis of Pb isotopes is performed using theoretical densities given by the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations and the experimental density extracted from the $(p,p)$ data at 295 MeV. The isotopic ratios ($R(σ)$) of the $^{A}$Pb$(p,p)$-to-$^{206}$Pb$(p,p)$ cross sections are analyzed in connection with the isotopic differences ($D(ρ_n)$ and $D(r_n)$) in neutron densities $(ρ_n(r))$ and radii $(r_n)$ between $^A$Pb and $^{208}$Pb. A hole-model analysis is performed, assuming $^{204}$Pb and $^{206}$Pb as a $^{208}$Pb core and neutron holes to clarify the one-to-one correspondence between isotopic structure properties such as $D(ρ_n)$ and $D(r_n)$ and the isotopic cross-section ratio. By fitting $R(σ)$, the values of $D(ρ_n)$ and $D(r_n)$ are extracted from the experimental $(p,p)$ cross section data with less uncertainty of the structure and reaction models. The isotopic neutron radius difference between $^{206}$Pb and $^{208}$Pb was obtained as $D(r_n)=-0.012$ fm with an acceptable range of $D(r_n)=-0.03 \sim -0.006$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that isotopic analysis of proton elastic scattering cross-section ratios between neighboring Pb isotopes can extract neutron density and radius differences with reduced systematic uncertainty compared to independent analyses, but the extracted values depend on the choice of reaction model (ddMH vs. MH) and the assumed core-hole structure, with the best-fit isotopic neutron radius difference between 206Pb and 208Pb being D(r_n) = -0.012 fm within an acceptable range of -0.03 to -0.006 fm.

## Method

The author performed relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) calculations using the density-dependent Murdock and Horowitz (ddMH) model for 295 MeV proton elastic scattering off 204Pb, 206Pb, and 208Pb, using theoretical neutron densities from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) with DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 interactions and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) with SKM* and SLy4 interactions, plus experimental SOG-fit densities from prior work. The isotopic cross-section ratios R(sigma) of 204Pb(p,p) and 206Pb(p,p) to 208Pb(p,p) were analyzed, and a hole model was introduced assuming 204Pb and 206Pb as a 208Pb core with neutron holes in the 3p1/2 orbit, using two parameters (effective hole number nhole and core-size scaling factor delta_s) to fit the experimental R(sigma) and extract isotopic neutron density differences D(rho_n) and radius differences D(r_n).

## Key numbers

- Isotopic neutron radius difference between 206Pb and 208Pb: D(r_n) = -0.012 fm, with acceptable range D(r_n) = -0.03 to -0.006 fm
- Theoretical isotopic neutron radius differences from RHB and SHFB calculations: D(204Pb; r_n) approximately -0.04 fm and D(206Pb; r_n) approximately -0.02 fm
- Experimental SOG-fit best-fit values: D(r_n) = -0.055 fm for 204Pb and D(r_n) = -0.040 fm for 206Pb
- Theoretical isotopic proton radius differences: D(r_p) = -0.02 fm for 204Pb and D(r_p) = -0.01 fm for 206Pb, consistent with experimental charge radii data
- Proton energy: E_p = 295 MeV for all three Pb isotopes

## Context

This work extends the reaction analysis method of Zenihiro (2010) for extracting neutron densities from 295 MeV proton scattering on Pb isotopes, but introduces a new isotopic ratio approach to reduce systematic errors from internal density uncertainties. The method contrasts with independent analyses of individual isotopes and with the density functional theory (DFT) approach of PhysRevC.102.064307 that compared surface-neutron-density profiles to symmetry-energy parameters. The hole-model analysis enables extraction of improved neutron radii with quantified uncertainty, and the results can be compared with prior determinations from 800 MeV proton scattering (Ray 1979), 650 MeV scattering (Starodubsky 1994), antiprotonic atom x-ray cascades (Klos 2007), and parity-violating electron scattering (Abrahamyan 2012).
