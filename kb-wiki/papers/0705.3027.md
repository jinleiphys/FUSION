# The s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential

> We calculate the s-wave part of the pion-nucleus optical potential using a unitarized chiral approach that has been previously used to simultaneously describe pionic hydrogen and deuterium data as well as low energy pi N scattering in the vacuum. This energy dependent model allows for additional isoscalar parts in the potential from multiple rescattering. We consider Pauli blocking and pion polarization in an asymmetric nuclear matter environment. Also, higher order corrections of the pi N amplitude are included. The model can accommodate the repulsion required by phenomenological fits, though the theoretical uncertainties are bigger than previously thought. At the same time, we also find an enhancement of the isovector part compatible with empirical determinations.

# The s-wave pion-nucleus optical potential

## Key claim

The unitarized chiral approach can accommodate the repulsion required by phenomenological fits to pionic atom data, but the theoretical uncertainties are larger than previously thought; at the same time, the model finds an enhancement of the isovector part compatible with empirical determinations, though the problem of missing repulsion persists.

## Method

The authors calculate the s-wave part of the pion-nucleus optical potential using a unitarized chiral approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation, previously applied to pionic hydrogen and deuterium data and low-energy πN scattering in vacuum. They incorporate Pauli blocking and pion polarization in asymmetric nuclear matter, including particle-hole and Δ-hole excitations with short-range correlations parametrized by a Migdal parameter g' = 0.7, and also include higher-order vertex corrections and contributions from the N*(1440) Roper resonance decaying into Nππ.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum isoscalar term b0 = (-28 ± 40) × 10⁻⁴ mπ⁻¹ and isovector term b1 = (-881 ± 48) × 10⁻⁴ mπ⁻¹ from the dimensional regularization fit
- The vacuum isoscalar term b0 = -29 × 10⁻⁴ mπ⁻¹ and isovector term b1 = -883 × 10⁻⁴ mπ⁻¹ from the cut-off scheme fit
- The reduced chi-squared for the cut-off scheme fit is χr² = 48/(2·10+3) ≈ 2.1
- For asymmetric matter corresponding to ²⁰⁸Pb, the neutron Fermi momentum kFⁿ = 1.154 kFᵖ, with kFᵖ = 247 MeV and kFⁿ = 286 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.483 mπ³
- The Migdal parameter for short-range correlations is chosen as g' = 0.7, with form factor cutoff Λ = 0.9 GeV
- The imaginary part of the s-wave selfenergy from the present model shows a 30% discrepancy compared to phenomenological values from Tauscher (1974) and Stricker (1980)

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Doring (2004) for vacuum πN scattering to the nuclear medium, addressing the long-standing problem of missing repulsion in pionic atoms that was highlighted by Garcia-Recio (1987) and motivated by deeply bound pionic atoms discovered at GSI by Yamazaki (1996, 1997). The model enables improved calculations of s-levels in pionic atoms, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei such as ²⁰⁸Pb, and provides a framework for testing the density dependence of the optical potential against phenomenological fits from Tauscher (1974), Stricker (1980), and Nieves (1993).
