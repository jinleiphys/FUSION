# A Resummed Hydrodynamic Description of Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> We introduce a resummed hydrodynamic scheme for evolving the viscous stress tensors in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, based on which the necessary non-linear causality conditions can be imposed. When the magnitudes of the shear and bulk viscous stress tensors are small relative to the ideal part energy-momentum tensor, this new resummed scheme reduces to the standard second-order relativistic hydrodynamic theories. Nontrivial nonlinear corrections from high-order gradient terms retain the sizes of shear and bulk viscous stress tensors within tunable maximum allowed values. We perform event-by-event simulations for Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV to quantify the theoretical uncertainties from this resummed scheme on final-state flow observables.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a resummed hydrodynamic scheme that modifies transport coefficients as functions of local inverse Reynolds numbers, which reduces to standard second-order DNMR theory near equilibrium but imposes tunable maximum allowed values for viscous stress tensors to enforce non-linear causality conditions. The scheme ensures necessary causality conditions are satisfied during evolution once constraints are imposed on initial conditions, with the parameter α = 1.5 effectively enforcing these conditions for the relevant speed of sound range.

## Method
The authors promote transport coefficients in the DNMR hydrodynamic equations to functions of local inverse Reynolds numbers for shear (R̃_π) and bulk (R̃_Π) viscous stress tensors, using a renormalization factor f = 1/(1 + arctanh²(α(R̃_Π + R̃_shear))). They implement this scheme in the MUSIC hydrodynamic framework within the IP-Glasma + MUSIC + UrQMD simulation chain, performing event-by-event simulations for Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV with three different initialization conditions (equilibrium, near-equilibrium with half viscous stresses, and far-from-equilibrium with full IP-Glasma stresses).

## Key numbers
- The resummed scheme with α = 1.5 constrains the maximum allowed values for R̃_shear and R̃_bulk to be 2/3, ensuring necessary causality conditions are valid for speed of sound squared in the range 0.13 < c_s² < 1/3
- Hydrodynamic simulations are initialized at τ_hydro = 0.4 fm/c with average inverse Reynolds number for shear stress tensor around R̃_shear ∼ 0.6
- In p+Pb collisions initialized at equilibrium, a 10% difference in charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients is observed between numerical schemes
- In central and semi-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions (centrality less than 50%), final-state observables are robust against different numerical schemes

## Context
This work extends the non-linear causality conditions derived by Bemfica et al. (2020) for second-order DNMR hydrodynamics theory (Denicol, Niemi, Molnár, Rischke, 2012), building on earlier constraints for maximum-allowed inverse Reynolds numbers by Chiu et al. (2021) and Plumberg et al. (2021). Similar resummation approaches were previously proposed for the bulk viscosity sector only by Gavassino (2023) and Yang (2023). The scheme enables quantification of theoretical uncertainties from causality conditions in precision comparisons with experimental flow measurements, particularly for small collision systems (p+Pb, p+p) where strong pressure gradients drive systems far from equilibrium.
