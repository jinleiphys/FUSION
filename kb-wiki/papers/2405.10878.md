# Modelling stochastic fluctuations in relativistic kinetic theory

> Using the information current, we develop a Lorentz-covariant framework for modeling equilibrium fluctuations in relativistic kinetic theory in the grand-canonical ensemble. The resulting stochastic theory is proven to be causal and covariantly stable, and its predictions do not depend on the choice of spacetime foliation used to define the grand-canonical probabilities. As expected, in a box containing $N{>}5$ particles, Boltzmann's molecular chaos postulate is broken with (almost exact) probability $N^{-1/2}$, leading to a breakdown of the Boltzmann equation in small systems. We also verify that, in ultrarelativistic gases, transient hydrodynamics already accounts for at least 80% of the equilibrium fluctuations of the stress-energy tensor at a given time. Finally, we compute the correlators at non-equal times for two selected collision kernels: That of a chemically active diluted solution, and that of ultrarelativistic scalar particles self-interacting via a quartic potential. For the former, we compute the density-density correlators analytically in real space, and dehydrodynamization of the stochastic theory is proven to occur whenever the mean free path diverges at high energy.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Lorentz-covariant stochastic framework for equilibrium fluctuations in relativistic kinetic theory that is proven to be causal and covariantly stable, with predictions independent of the spacetime foliation used to define grand-canonical probabilities; however, the framework applies only to linearized perturbations around global equilibrium, and the proof of causality and stability relies on the positivity of the information current and the entropy production.

## Method

The authors construct a fluctuating Boltzmann equation by adding a stochastic source term to the linearized Boltzmann equation, with the noise correlator chosen to match the entropy production kernel so that the system relaxes to the correct equilibrium probability distribution. They compute equal-time and non-equal-time correlators of the deviation function phi for two specific collision kernels: the relaxation time approximation (RTA) and a quartic self-interaction (phi^4) theory for ultrarelativistic scalar particles. For the RTA case, they analytically evaluate the two-point correlator in real space, while for the phi^4 theory they expand in orthogonal Laguerre polynomial moments and derive the coupled mode equations.

## Key numbers

- In a box containing N > 5 particles, Boltzmann's molecular chaos postulate is broken with probability approximately N^{-1/2}, leading to a breakdown of the Boltzmann equation in small systems.
- In ultrarelativistic gases, transient hydrodynamics accounts for at least 80% of the equilibrium fluctuations of the stress-energy tensor at a given time.
- For the RTA kernel, the two-point correlator of phi is given by (1/(2 f_eq,p)) exp(-|x^0 - y^0|/tau_R) delta^{(3)}(p-k) delta^{(3)}(x - y + (x^0 - y^0) p/E_p), where tau_R is the relaxation time.

## Context

This work extends the information current formalism of Gavassino (2021) to relativistic kinetic theory, providing a rigorous foundation for stochastic fluctuations that respects causality and covariance. It builds on the stochastic relativistic hydrodynamics framework of Mullins (2023) and applies it to the Boltzmann equation, enabling the study of fluctuations in small systems where the Boltzmann equation breaks down. The results enable future work on dehydrodynamization when the mean free path diverges at high energy, and provide explicit correlators for testing against microscopic simulations.
