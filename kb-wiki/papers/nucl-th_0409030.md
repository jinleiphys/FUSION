# Nuclear phenomena derived from quark-gluon strings

> We propose a QCD based many-body model for the nucleus where the strong coupling regime is controlled by a three body string force and the weak coupling regime is dominated by a pairing force. This model operates effectively with a quark-gluon Lagrangian containing a pairing force from instantons and a baryonic string term which contains a confining potential. The unified model for weak and strong coupling regimes, is, however, only consistent at the border of perturbative QCD. The baryonic string force is necessary, as a {stability and} compressibility analysis shows, for the occurrence of the phases of nuclear matter. The model exhibits a quark deconfinement transition and chiral restoration which are suggested by QCD and give qualitatively correct numerics. The effective model is shown to be isomorphic to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and exhibits the correct chirality provided that the chiral fields are identified with the 2-particle strings, which are natural in a QCD framework

# Nuclear phenomena derived from quark-gluon strings

## Key claim

The paper proposes a QCD-based many-body model for the nucleus that unifies a strong-coupling regime (controlled by a three-body string force) with a weak-coupling regime (dominated by a pairing force from instantons), but the authors explicitly state that this unified model is **only consistent at the border of perturbative QCD**, where the strong coupling constant α_QCD is around 1.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for nuclear matter starting from quark-gluon degrees of freedom, incorporating a two-body pairing force derived from instanton contributions and a three-body baryonic string force proportional to the minimal string length L (with potential V_st = κ L, where κ = √σ is the square root of the string tension). They then map this quark-level description onto an extended sigma model (ESM) where nucleons are treated as composite particles with a density-dependent coupling g_s(σ,π) that depends on the local values of the sigma and pion fields, and they solve the resulting Dirac equations and gap equations in a mean-field approximation to obtain the equation of state of nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon bag size in vacuum is approximately 0.8 fm, derived from the asymptotic solution of the Dirac equation with the string potential (κ = √σ).
- For the ESMI parametrization: binding energy E_B = -15.75 MeV, equilibrium density ρ_0 = 0.15 fm⁻³, effective mass ratio M/M_0 = 0.7, incompressibility K = 225 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 150 MeV, chiral transition density ρ_χ = 0.46 fm⁻³, deconfinement density ρ_de = 0.687 fm⁻³.
- For the ESMII parametrization: binding energy E_B = -15.75 MeV, equilibrium density ρ_0 = 0.148 fm⁻³, effective mass ratio M/M_0 = 0.58, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 108 MeV, chiral transition density ρ_χ = 0.3 fm⁻³, deconfinement density ρ_de = 0.36 fm⁻³.
- For ESMI, chiral symmetry restoration occurs at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 3.1 and deconfinement at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 4.5; for ESMII, chiral restoration at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 2.0 and deconfinement at ρ/ρ_0 ≈ 2.4.
- The quark matter coupling constant G_S = 2.14/Λ² with Λ = 654 MeV reproduces f_π = 93 MeV and quark mass m_q = 313 MeV in vacuum.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (Ref. [0]) by replacing the MIT bag confining potential with a chiral-invariant string force, and it shows that the effective model is isomorphic to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Klevansky, 1992) at the mean-field level for bulk properties. The model enables the study of two distinct phase transitions in nuclear matter: chiral symmetry restoration (driven by the instanton pairing force) and quark deconfinement (driven by the diminishing baryonic string force), and it predicts a possible superfluid exotic phase of hadronic matter between these critical points, as well as consistency with the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase above the second critical point.
