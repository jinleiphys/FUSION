# Minkowski Space Dynamics and Light-Front Projection

> We explore the connection between the four-dimensional Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter equation and its light-front projection, emphasizing the implications for bound-state dynamics. Our approach incorporates dressed particles, such as quarks, via the integral representation of the corresponding propagator. We analyze the light-front dynamics of the valence component of the physical state using a hierarchical set of Green's functions, which reveals its coupling to higher Fock components when dressed particles are considered. We also present the light-front Faddeev-Bethe-Salpeter equations for three-body systems with dressed constituents. Furthermore, we discuss formal developments that are central to connecting the three-dimensional light-front dynamics onto the null-plane and the four-dimensional Minkowski-space framework, based on the Nakanishi integral representation. Selected applications to hadron structure are also reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space can be connected to its light-front projection through the Nakanishi integral representation and a quasi-potential reduction, enabling access to the valence component of the light-front wave function while implicitly accounting for higher Fock components when dressed particles (such as quarks with self-energy corrections) are included.

## Method

The authors employ the Nakanishi integral representation to transform the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation into a two-dimensional integral equation for a weight function, avoiding Wick rotation to Euclidean space. They then perform a light-front projection by integrating out the relative light-front energy variable \(k^-\), using the Källen-Lehmann spectral representation to incorporate dressed propagators for both bosonic and fermionic constituents. The quasi-potential method introduces an auxiliary interaction \(W(K)\) and a three-dimensional Green's function \(g_0(K)\) to derive a light-front T-matrix equation that couples the valence component to higher Fock components through the effective interaction \(w(K)\).

## Key numbers

- The free-global LF propagator for two bosons is given by \(g_0(K)= i\,\frac{\theta (K^{+}-\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})\theta (\widehat{k}_{1}^{+})}{ \widehat k^+_1 (K^+-\widehat k^+_1)} \int_0^\infty ds_1^2\int_0^\infty ds_2^2 \,\frac{\rho(s_1^2)\,\rho(s_2^2)}{K^{-}-\widehat{k}_{1on}^{-}-\widehat{k}_{2on}^{-}+i\varepsilon}\), where the spectral densities \(\rho(s_i^2)\) are integrated over the full range \(0\) to \(\infty\).
- For the fermionic case, the sum rules \(\int_0^\infty ds^2 \,\rho_V(s^2)=1\) and \(\int_0^\infty ds^2 \,\rho_S(s^2)=m_0\) (where \(m_0\) is the current quark mass) ensure that the instantaneous term in the Dirac propagator cannot be ignored due to asymptotic freedom.
- In the phenomenological fixed-constituent-mass limit, the global LF two-fermion propagator reduces to \(g_0(K)= { (\rlap\slash\widehat{k}_{1on} +m) \otimes (\rlap\slash\widehat{k}_{2on} +m) \over \widehat k^+_1 (K^+-\widehat k^+_1)(K^--\widehat k_{1on}^--\widehat k_{2on}^-+i\varepsilon)}\), with constituent mass \(m\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-potential approach developed by Sales (1999, 2001) and the Nakanishi integral representation framework pioneered by Nakanishi (1969, 1971) and later advanced by Carbonell (2010) and Frederico (2011, 2013). It provides a formal bridge between four-dimensional Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter dynamics and three-dimensional light-front quantization, enabling future calculations of hadron structure observables such as electromagnetic form factors, generalized parton distributions, and distribution amplitudes that are directly relevant to experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
