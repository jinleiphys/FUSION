# The energy-momentum tensor at the earliest stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Nuclear collisions at high energies produce a gluon field that can be described using the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory at proper times $τ\lesssim 1$ fm/c. The theory can be used to calculate the gluon energy-momentum tensor, which provides information about the early time evolution of the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields, energy density, longitudinal and transverse pressures, and other quantities. We obtain an analytic expression for the energy-momentum tensor using an expansion in the proper time, and working to sixth order. The calculation is technically difficult, in part because the number of terms involved grows rapidly with the order of the $τ$ expansion, but also because of several subtle issues related to the definition of event-averaged correlators, the method chosen to regulate these correlators, and the dependence of results on the parameters introduced by the regularization and nuclear density profile functions. All of these issues are crucially related to the important question of the extent to which we expect a CGC approach to be able to accurately describe the early stages of a heavy ion collision. We present some results for the evolution of the energy density and the longitudinal and transverse pressures. We show that our calculation gives physically meaningful results up to values of the proper time which are close to the regime at which hydrodynamic simulations are initialized. In a companion paper [1] we give a detailed analysis of several other experimentally relevant quantities that can be calculated from the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The authors obtain an analytic expression for the gluon energy-momentum tensor in the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory using a proper-time expansion to sixth order, but they emphasize that the calculation involves subtle issues regarding event-averaged correlators, regularization, and dependence on nuclear density profile parameters that crucially affect whether the CGC approach can accurately describe the earliest stage of a heavy-ion collision.

## Method

The authors solve the sourceless Yang-Mills equation in Milne coordinates using a power-series expansion in proper time τ to sixth order, obtaining the gauge potentials α(τ, x⊥) and α⃗⊥(τ, x⊥) in terms of their initial values at τ=0. They then compute the energy-momentum tensor from the field-strength tensor, apply boundary conditions connecting the post-collision potentials to pre-collision potentials from each nucleus, and average over Gaussian-distributed colour charge sources using the Glasma Graph approximation to express results in terms of two-point correlators of pre-collision potentials.

## Key numbers

- The sixth-order expansion gives physically meaningful results up to τ ∼ 0.05 fm.
- The proper-time expansion is valid for τ ≲ 1 fm/c, with convergence determined by the time scale τ0 ∼ 1/Qs where Qs is the saturation scale.
- The infra-red regulator m is chosen such that m ∼ ΛQCD, giving L = 2e−γE/m where γE ≈ 0.577 is Euler's constant.
- The initial longitudinal electric field at τ=0 is E ≡ Ez(0, x⊥) = −2α(0, x⊥), and the initial longitudinal magnetic field is B ≡ Bz(0, x⊥) = ∂yαx⊥(0, x⊥) − ∂xαy⊥(0, x⊥) − ig[αy⊥(0, x⊥), αx⊥(0, x⊥)].

## Context

This work extends the near-field expansion approach proposed by Fries (2005) and developed by Fukushima (2007), Fujii (2008), Chen (2015), and Fries (2017), providing the first systematic study of the convergence of the proper-time expansion. The results provide analytic initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations at times close to where such simulations are initialized, and the method is being used to calculate heavy quark transport properties in glasma as in Mrowczynski (2017) and Carrington (2020). A companion paper (Carrington, 2021) gives a detailed analysis of experimentally relevant quantities derived from the energy-momentum tensor.
