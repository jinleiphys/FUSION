# Asymptotic behavior and critical coupling in the scalar Yukawa model from Schwinger-Dyson equations

> A sequence of $n$-particle approximations for the system of Schwinger-Dyson equations is investigated in the model of a complex scalar field $ϕ$ and a real scalar field $χ$ with the interaction $gϕ^*ϕχ$. In the first non-trivial two-particle approximation, the system is reduced to a system of two nonlinear integral equations for propagators. The study of this system shows that for equal masses a critical coupling constant $g^2_c$ exists, which separates the weak- and strong-coupling regions with the different asymptotic behavior for deep Euclidean momenta. In the weak-coupling region ($g^2<g^2_c$), the propagators are asymptotically free, which corresponds to the wide-spread opinion about the dominance of perturbation theory for this model. At the critical point the asymptotics of propagators are $\sim 1/p$. In the strong coupling region ($g^2>g^2_c$), the propagators are asymptotically constant, which corresponds to the ultra-local limit. For unequal masses, the critical point transforms into a segment of values, in which there are no solutions with a self-consistent ultraviolet behavior without Landau singularities.

## Key claim

For the scalar Yukawa model in the two-particle Schwinger-Dyson approximation, a critical coupling constant \(g^2_c\) exists that separates weak-coupling (asymptotically free propagators) from strong-coupling (asymptotically constant propagators) regions; at the critical point the propagators scale as \(1/p\), and for unequal masses the critical point expands into a segment of values where no self-consistent ultraviolet behavior without Landau singularities exists.

## Method

The author constructs a sequence of \(n\)-particle approximations for the Schwinger-Dyson equations of a complex scalar field \(\phi\) and a real scalar field \(\chi\) with interaction \(g\phi^*\phi\chi\). In the first non-trivial two-particle approximation, the system reduces to two coupled nonlinear integral equations for the phion and chion propagators, which are then studied analytically in the deep Euclidean region using a massless integration approximation. The asymptotic behavior is analyzed by converting the integral equations into differential equations and solving them in the weak-coupling, critical, and strong-coupling regimes.

## Key numbers

* For equal masses, the critical coupling is \(\lambda_c = 1\) in dimensionless units, corresponding to \(g^2_c = 32\pi^2 \mu m\) (with \(\mu = m\)).
* In the weak-coupling region (\(\lambda < 1\)), propagators behave as \(\Delta \sim 1/p^2\), \(D \sim 1/p^2\).
* At the critical point (\(\lambda = 1\)), propagators behave as \(\Delta \sim 1/\sqrt{p^2}\), \(D \sim 1/\sqrt{p^2}\).
* In the strong-coupling region (\(\lambda > 1\)), the asymptotic solution for equal masses is \(u_0 = \lambda/(\lambda-1)\), giving constant propagators.
* For unequal masses (\(\mu^2 > m^2\)), the weak-coupling region is \(\lambda < m/\mu\) and the strong-coupling region is \(\lambda > \mu/m\); the intermediate region \(m/\mu < \lambda < \mu/m\) has no self-consistent solutions.
* In the mean-field expansion, the critical coupling is \(g^2_c = 96\pi^2 m_r^2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of \(n\)-particle approximations for the Schwinger-Dyson equations in the \(\lambda(\phi^*\phi)^2\) model (Rochev 2011) and the Yukawa model (Rochev 2012). The existence of a critical coupling in the scalar Yukawa model has been noted by many authors using different methods (Nieuwenhuizen 1995, Rosenfelder 1995, Barro 1998, Sauli 2002), but here the author shows that self-consistent solutions exist also for strong coupling, suggesting the critical coupling represents a phase transition rather than a fundamental limit. The results enable further study of the three-particle approximation, which restores crossing symmetry missing in the two-particle approximation.
