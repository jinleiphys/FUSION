# Alpha-decay half-lives, alpha-capture and alpha-nucleus potential

> The alpha-decay half-lives and the alpha-capture cross-sections are evaluated in the framework of unified model for alpha-decay and alpha-capture. In the framework of this model the alpha-decay and alpha-capture are considered as penetration of the alpha-particle through the potential barrier formed by nuclear, Coulomb and centrifugal interactions between alpha-particle and nucleus. The spins and the parities of parent and daughter nuclei as well as the quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations of daughter nuclei are taken into account for evaluation of the alpha-decay half-lives. The alpha-decay half-lives for 344 nuclei and the alpha-capture cross-sections of 40Ca, 44Ca, 59Co, 208Pb and 209Bi agree well with the experimental data. The evaluated alpha-decay half-lives within the range 10^{-9} <T_{1/2} < 10^38 s for 1246 alpha-emitters are tabulated.

## Key claim

The authors present a unified model for alpha-decay and alpha-capture (UMADAC) that simultaneously describes both alpha-decay half-lives and alpha-capture cross-sections using a single alpha-nucleus potential, with the hedge that the model's accuracy depends on careful selection of well-defined ground-state-to-ground-state transitions and that the description is most accurate for even-even nuclei where angular momentum uncertainty is absent.

## Method

The authors model alpha-decay and alpha-capture as barrier penetration using the WKB approximation, with the alpha-nucleus potential consisting of Coulomb, nuclear, and centrifugal parts. They include quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations of daughter nuclei, account for spin-parity selection rules to determine the minimal angular momentum of alpha-transitions, and parametrize the nuclear potential strength, radius, diffuseness, and assault frequency with 22 parameters fitted simultaneously to 344 experimental alpha-decay half-lives and alpha-capture cross-sections for 40Ca, 44Ca, 59Co, 208Pb and 209Bi.

## Key numbers

- 344 ground-state-to-ground-state alpha-transitions used for fitting, comprising 136 even-even, 84 even-odd, 76 odd-even and 48 odd-odd alpha-emitters
- Mass range of selected alpha-emitters: 106 ≤ A ≤ 261; charge range: 52 ≤ Z ≤ 107
- Alpha-decay half-lives evaluated for 1246 alpha-emitters within range 10^{-9} s ≤ T_{1/2} ≤ 10^{38} s
- Longest observed alpha-decay half-life: T_{1/2} = (1.9 ± 0.2) × 10^{19} yr = (6.0 ± 0.6) × 10^{26} s for 209Bi
- Energy range of alpha-nucleus potential evaluation: 1.915 MeV ≤ Q_α ≤ 25 MeV
- 22 parameters in the model, with values given in Table I (e.g., v_1 = -40.1031 MeV, r_2 = 1.2915 fm, d_1 = 0.6870 fm)
- Root-mean-square errors of log10(T_{1/2}) are smallest in UMADAC compared to other models for all nuclear types (even-even, even-odd, odd-even, odd-odd)

## Context

This work extends the unified model approach of Denisov (2005) by using an updated dataset of 344 alpha-emitters (larger than the 367 nuclei used in that prior work) and incorporating spin-parity selection rules for even-odd, odd-even and odd-odd nuclei, which were neglected in earlier cluster model approaches such as Buck et al. (1991-1993) and Xu et al. (2005). The model enables prediction of alpha-decay half-lives for 902 nuclei with unknown experimental values and provides a parametrized alpha-nucleus potential applicable to nuclear physics and astrophysics reactions across wide mass (40 ≤ A ≤ 293) and charge (50 ≤ Z ≤ 118) ranges.
