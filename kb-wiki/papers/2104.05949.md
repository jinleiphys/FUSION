# Non-extensive Boltzmann Transport Equation: the Relaxation Time Approximation and Beyond

> We derive approximate iterative analytical solutions of the non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation. The approximate solutions almost overlap with the exact solution for a considerably wide range of the parameter values found in describing particle spectra originated in high-energy collisions. We also discuss the Landau kinetic approximation of the non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation and the emergence of the non-extensive Fokker-Planck equation, and use it to estimate the drag and diffusion coefficients of highly energetic light quarks passing through a gluonic plasma.

## Key claim

The paper derives approximate iterative analytical solutions of the non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation (NEBTE) in the relaxation time approximation, which almost overlap with the exact numerical solution for a wide range of parameter values relevant to high-energy collision phenomenology, though the approximation works better for higher-mass particles and shows slight deviation for low-mass particles at very low transverse momentum (below about 0.6 GeV).

## Method

The authors start from the non-extensive Boltzmann transport equation with a power-law index \(q\) on the distribution function in the left-hand side, and apply the relaxation time approximation to obtain a non-linear differential equation. They expand the integrand in a negative binomial series and express the exact solution in terms of a hypergeometric function, then propose a first-order iterative solution as a small increment over the zeroth-order solution. Separately, they impose the Landau kinetic approximation (soft momentum transfer) on the NEBTE to derive the non-extensive Fokker-Planck equation in Ito form, and use it to compute drag and diffusion coefficients for energetic light quarks traversing a gluonic plasma.

## Key numbers

* For neutral pions in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV: fitted parameters \(q = 1.005 \pm 0.003\), \(T = 0.066 \pm 0.024\) GeV, \(t/\tau = 1.047 \pm 0.096\), with \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} = 18.64/15\).
* For all charged hadrons at 130 GeV: fitted parameters \(q = 1.024 \pm 0.011\), \(T = 0.187 \pm 0.050\) GeV, \(t/\tau = 1.183 \pm 0.0104\), with \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} = 19.09/27\).
* For charged pions in Au+Au at 200 GeV: fitted parameters \(q = 1.049 \pm 0.020\), \(T = 0.156 \pm 0.036\) GeV, \(t/\tau = 0.394 \pm 0.065\), with \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} = 23.38/25\).
* For the Fokker-Planck transport coefficients, calculations used \(q = 1.01\), gluonic plasma temperature \(T_g = 350\) MeV (for momentum variation plots), and light quark momentum \(P = 5\) GeV (for temperature variation plots).
* The ratio of freeze-out time to relaxation time \(t/\tau\) is found to be of order 1, compared to a value of order 1.5 from conventional BTE calculations by Maciej (2021).

## Context

This work extends the Tsallis-statistics-inspired modification of the Boltzmann transport equation originally formulated by Lavagno (2002) and Biro (2012), which replaces the standard molecular chaos hypothesis with a generalized form that yields power-law stationary solutions. The approximate analytical solutions enable phenomenological description of the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{\mathrm{AA}}\) in heavy-ion collisions, similar to the approach using the conventional BTE by Tripathy (2017) but now incorporating non-extensive effects. The derived non-extensive Fokker-Planck equation and its transport coefficients provide a framework for studying energy loss of energetic partons in strongly interacting QCD media, particularly relevant for small collision systems where the generalized molecular chaos hypothesis may be important, as indicated by ALICE (2019) observations of QGP-like signatures in small systems.
