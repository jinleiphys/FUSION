# Role of the tensor interaction in He isotopes with a tensor-optimized shell model

> We studied the role of the tensor interaction in He isotopes systematically on the basis of the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM). We use a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction AV8 obtained from nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The short-range correlation is treated in the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Using the TOSM+UCOM approach, we investigate the role of tensor interaction on each spectrum in He isotopes. It is found that the tensor interaction enhances the LS splitting energy observed in 5He, in which the p1/2 and p3/2 orbits play different roles on the tensor correlation. In {6,7,8}He, the low-lying states containing extra neutrons in the p3/2 orbit gain the tensor contribution. On the other hand, the excited states containing extra neutrons in the p1/2 orbit lose the tensor contribution due to the Pauli-blocking effect with the 2p2h states in the 4He core configuration.

## Key claim

The tensor interaction systematically enhances the LS splitting energy in 5He and provides additional binding to low-lying states in 6,7,8He that contain extra neutrons in the p3/2 orbit, while excited states with extra neutrons in the p1/2 orbit lose tensor contribution due to Pauli blocking with the 2p2h states of the 4He core configuration.

## Method

The authors combined the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) using the bare AV8' nucleon-nucleon interaction. TOSM describes tensor correlations by including up to 2p2h excitations with fully optimized radial wave functions for particle states using Gaussian basis functions, while UCOM treats short-range central correlations via a unitary transformation. The method was applied to calculate the ground and excited states of 4He, 5He, 6He, 7He, and 8He, with the maximum orbital angular momentum Lmax taken as 10 for convergence.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground state energy: -20.46 MeV (TOSM+UCOM), -22.30 MeV (TOSM+S-UCOM), compared to -25.92 MeV from rigorous stochastic variational method (SVM) calculations
- 4He matter radius: 1.52 fm
- 4He 0p0h configuration probability: 84.14%; dominant 2p2h state (0s)⁻²(0p1/2)² probability: 2.32%
- 6He matter radius: 2.27 fm (present), 2.37 fm (cluster orbital shell model), experimental values 2.33(4) fm, 2.45(10) fm, 2.37(5) fm
- 8He matter radius: 2.44 fm (present), 2.52 fm (cluster orbital shell model), experimental values 2.49(4) fm, 2.53(8) fm, 2.49(4) fm
- 4He tensor energy contribution: -51.06 MeV (TOSM+UCOM), -54.55 MeV (TOSM+S-UCOM), compared to -68.32 MeV from SVM
- 4He kinetic energy: 86.95 MeV (TOSM+UCOM), 90.50 MeV (TOSM+S-UCOM), compared to 102.35 MeV from SVM

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOSM studies of 4He and 5He (Myo 2005, 2006, 2007, 2009) to systematically cover neutron-rich He isotopes up to 8He, using a fully microscopic approach with a bare NN interaction rather than semi-microscopic potentials. The results are compared with rigorous stochastic variational method calculations by Kamada (2001) and with cluster orbital shell model calculations by Myo (2010). The approach enables future studies of how tensor correlations affect nuclear structure in heavier nuclei, though the authors note that including 4p4h configurations and improving the UCOM treatment would be needed to match rigorous binding energies.
