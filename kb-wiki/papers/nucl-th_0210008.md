# Liquid-gas phase transition in a two-components isospin lattice gas model for asymmetric nuclear matter

> A two-components isospin lattice gas model has been employed to study the liquid-gas phase transition for asymmetric nuclear matter. An additional degree of freedom, namely, the asymmetry parameter alpha has been considered carefully for studying the phase transition. We have shown that under the mean field approximation, the liquid-gas phase transition given by this model is of second order. The entropy continues at the phase transition point. The binodal surface is addressed.

## Key claim

Under the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation, the liquid-gas phase transition in a two-component isospin lattice gas model for asymmetric nuclear matter is of second order (entropy continuous) for asymmetry parameters between 0 and 1, contrary to the first-order transition found in the one-component symmetric case. The transition becomes first order only at the endpoints alpha=0 (symmetric nuclear matter) and alpha=1 (neutron matter), where the system reduces to one component.

## Method

The authors employed a two-component isospin lattice gas model on a lattice where each site can be vacant, occupied by a proton, or occupied by a neutron, with nearest-neighbor interactions J_s for like-nucleon pairs and J_d for proton-neutron pairs. Using the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation, they derived the grand partition function with two independent order parameters: the relative emptiness r and the asymmetry parameter s (related to alpha). The phase coexistence conditions were solved by requiring equal chemical potentials for protons and neutrons and equal pressure between liquid and gas phases, with the interaction parameters C1=3.2 MeV and C2=16.0 MeV chosen to reproduce the binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation at T=0 K: -16 MeV (used to fix interaction parameters)
- Interaction parameters: C1 = 3.2 MeV, C2 = 16.0 MeV
- Critical temperature for asymmetric nuclear matter with alpha=0.50: T_c = 8.58 MeV
- Isotherms calculated at temperatures T = 6.00, 6.90, 7.95, 8.58, and 8.70 MeV for fixed alpha=0.50
- Chemical isobars calculated at fixed temperature T=7.50 MeV and pressure p=1.12 MeV fm^{-3}
- Binodal surface section shown at T=7.50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of liquid-gas phase transitions from one-component lattice gas models (Lee and Yang, 1952) and symmetric isospin lattice gas models (Shamanna et al., 1994; Kuo et al., 1996; Pan and Das Gupta, 1995) to asymmetric nuclear matter with unequal proton and neutron numbers. It contradicts the earlier result of Ray et al. (1997) who found a first-order transition for the same two-component lattice gas model, by properly including the asymmetry parameter as an additional degree of freedom following the Gibbs phase rule. The results are consistent with continuous-space models of asymmetric nuclear matter (Muller and Serot, 1995; Qian et al., 2000, 2001, 2002) that also find second-order transitions, and the approach enables future studies of the binodal surface structure and phase coexistence in asymmetric nuclear systems.
