# Fermion-Boson Interactions and Quantum Algebras

> Quantum Algebras (q-algebras) are used to describe interactions between fermions and bosons. Particularly, the concept of a su_q(2) dynamical symmetry is invoked in order to reproduce the ground state properties of systems of fermions and bosons interacting via schematic forces. The structure of the proposed su_q(2) Hamiltonians, and the meaning of the corresponding deformation parameters, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ground-state properties and spectra of interacting fermion-boson systems (specifically the Da Providencia-Schütte and extended Lipkin models) can be accurately reproduced by purely fermionic effective Hamiltonians built on the quantum algebra su_q(2), provided the deformation parameter q is fitted as a function of physical constraints. The authors stress that this is not a transformation of the original Hamiltonian onto a q-deformed space, but rather a new effective Hamiltonian that absorbs the bosonic degrees of freedom into the q-deformation.

## Method

The authors start with two schematic fermion-boson Hamiltonians: the Da Providencia-Schütte (DPS) model and two variants of an extended Lipkin (LE) model, each defined on a su(2) ⊕ h₃ Lie algebra. For each model, they construct an effective Hamiltonian defined solely on the su_q(2) algebra, with the same finite-dimensional block structure, where the boson degrees of freedom are replaced by a q-dependent coupling strength χ(q). The deformation parameter q (or z = ln q) is then determined numerically by requiring that the ground-state energies of the original and effective Hamiltonians agree within 1% for each fixed eigenvalue L of the conserved symmetry operator P.

## Context

This work extends the use of quantum algebras (q-algebras) from quantum optics (where the Dicke model interaction was described via su_q(2) by the same group) to nuclear-physics schematic models. It builds on the Da Providencia-Schütte model (Da Providencia and Schütte, 1977) and the extended Lipkin model (Ballesteros et al., 2000), which are solvable fermion-boson toy models for condensation phenomena and hadron physics. The results suggest that q-deformed fermionic quasiparticles can replace explicit boson degrees of freedom in such systems, potentially enabling simplified treatments of non-perturbative QCD and nuclear collective motion.
