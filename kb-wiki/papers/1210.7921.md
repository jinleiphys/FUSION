# Electrical conductivity of quark matter at finite T

> In this talk, I present the recent theoretical results on the electrical conductivity (EC) sigma of quark matter, using the Kubo formula at finite temperature and zero quark density (T>0, mu=0) in the presence of an external strong magnetic field. The dilute instanton-liquid model with the caloron distribution is taken into account. It turns out that sigma=(0.02~0.15)/fm for T=(0~400) MeV with the relaxation time tau=(0.3~0.9) fm. EC is parameterized as sigma/T (0.46,0.77,1.08,1.39)C_EM for tau=(0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9) fm, respectively. These results are well compatible with other theoretical estimations and show almost negligible effects from the magnetic field. The soft photon emission rate from the quark-gluon plasma is discussed as well.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of quark matter at finite temperature and zero quark density is computed using the dilute instanton-liquid model with caloron distribution, yielding values of sigma approximately (0.02 to 0.15)/fm for temperatures from 0 to 400 MeV, with the caveat that these results depend on the chosen relaxation time tau of (0.3 to 0.9) fm and show almost negligible effects from an external magnetic field.

## Method

The author computes the electrical conductivity using the Kubo formula in Euclidean space at finite temperature and zero quark chemical potential, incorporating the dilute instanton-liquid model with a temperature-dependent caloron distribution. The effective chiral action is used to derive the light-quark propagator under an external electromagnetic field via the Schwinger method, and the fermionic Matsubara sum is performed to obtain the conductivity integrals. A UV regulator function is inserted to tame divergences, and the conductivity is evaluated numerically for different relaxation times and temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the study of transport coefficients in hot QCD matter using the instanton-liquid model, building on prior lattice QCD simulations by Gupta (2003) and Aarts (2007) that gave sigma/T values differing by an order of magnitude. The results are compared with quenched SU(3) lattice QCD from Gupta (2003) and Aarts (2007), SU(2) lattice QCD from Buividovich (2010), and the Green-function method of Kerbikov (2012), showing good agreement for relaxation times tau approximately 0.3 to 0.7 fm. The parameterization enables future applications to soft photon emission rates from quark-gluon plasma and provides a framework for computing other transport coefficients such as shear and bulk viscosities.
