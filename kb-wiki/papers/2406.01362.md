# Low energy alpha-nucleus optical potential studied via (a,n) cross section measurements on Te isotopes

> In several processes of stellar nucleosynthesis, like the astrophysical gamma-process, nuclear reactions involving alpha particles play an important role. The description of these reactions necessitates the knowledge of the alpha-nucleus optical model potential (AOMP) which is highly ambiguous at low, astrophysical energies. This ambiguity introduces a substantial uncertainty in the stellar models for predicting elemental and isotopic abundances. The experimental study of the AOMP is thus necessary which can be implemented by measuring the cross section of alpha-induced nuclear reactions. At low energies, (a,n) reactions are suitable for such a purpose. Therefore, in the present work, the (a,n) cross sections of four Te isotopes have been measured, mostly for the first time, and compared with theoretical predictions. The (a,n) cross sections of 120,122,124,130Te have been measured in the energy range between about 10 and 17 MeV using the activation method. The detection of the gamma radiation following the decay of the radioactive reaction products were used to determine the cross sections. The measured cross sections are compared with statistical model calculations obtained from the widely used TALYS nuclear reaction simulation code. Predictions using various available AOMPs are investigated. It is found that the recently developed Atomki-V2 AOMP provides the best description for all studied reactions and this potential also reproduces well the total reaction cross sections from elastic scattering experiments, when they are available in literature. We recommend therefore to use the astrophysical reaction rates based on this potential for nucleosynthesis models of heavy elements.

## Key claim
The recently developed Atomki-V2 alpha-nucleus optical model potential (AOMP) provides the best description of (alpha,n) cross sections on four Te isotopes across the measured energy range, and the authors recommend using astrophysical reaction rates based on this potential for nucleosynthesis models of heavy elements, though the recommendation is conditional on the potential also reproducing total reaction cross sections from elastic scattering experiments where available.

## Method
The (alpha,n) cross sections of 120Te, 122Te, 124Te, and 130Te were measured between approximately 10 and 17 MeV using the activation method, where natural isotopic composition Te targets were irradiated with alpha beams from the Atomki cyclotron. After irradiation, the gamma radiation following the decay of the radioactive Xe reaction products (123Xe, 125Xe, 127Xe, and 133Xe) was detected using planar and coaxial HPGe detectors to determine cross sections. The measured cross sections were compared with statistical model calculations from the TALYS nuclear reaction simulation code using various available AOMPs, including the McFadden-Satchler (MCF), Demetriou et al. (DEM), Avrigeanu et al. (AVR), and Atomki-V2 potentials.

## Key numbers
- 120Te(alpha,n)123Xe cross section at 11.0 MeV: 0.336 +/- 0.134 mbarn; at 17.0 MeV: 345 +/- 27 mbarn
- 122Te(alpha,n)125Xe cross section at 10.0 MeV: 0.0172 +/- 0.0033 mbarn; at 16.0 MeV: 271 +/- 21 mbarn
- 124Te(alpha,n)127Xe cross section at 10.0 MeV: 0.0300 +/- 0.0063 mbarn; at 17.0 MeV: 619 +/- 50 mbarn (includes contribution from 125Te(alpha,2n)127Xe)
- 130Te(alpha,n)133Xe total cross section at 10.0 MeV: 0.0390 +/- 0.0042 mbarn; at 17.0 MeV: 78.0 +/- 6.0 mbarn
- Target thicknesses were typically around 600 microgram/cm2, with Te surface densities in the range of (2.0 - 2.5) x 10^18 atoms/cm2
- Te:O atomic ratios in targets were typically 0.7:0.3

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the alpha-nucleus optical model potential (AOMP) which has been historically studied via elastic scattering (McFadden and Satchler, 1966) and later through alpha-induced reactions including radiative capture and (alpha,n) reactions (Demetriou et al., 2002; Avrigeanu et al., 2003, 2014). The measurements provide new experimental data for Te isotopes where no (alpha,n) cross sections existed in this energy range, enabling improved constraints on AOMPs used in Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations for the astrophysical gamma-process and weak r-process nucleosynthesis. The results enable more reliable astrophysical reaction rates for nucleosynthesis models of heavy elements, particularly for p-nuclei production.
