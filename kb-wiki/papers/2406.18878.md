# Gluonic contributions to the pion parton distribution functions

> We investigate the role of a dynamical gluon in the pion within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework and compare it with the solution of the Minkowski space Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE), focusing on contributions beyond the valence sector. We develop a framework to compute the quark-antiquark-gluon component of the pion, starting from its valence light-front wave function. Additionally, the quark and gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) are derived by considering this higher Fock component in the pion state. The proposed operator, which acts on the valence state to produce the quark-antiquark-gluon component of the pion, is tested against results from BLFQ for its contribution to the quark and gluon PDFs, as well as with results from the BSE. In the BLFQ framework, we identify the effect of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking on the pion structure through the enhancement of the spin-flip matrix element. This enhancement is captured in the $|q\bar{q}g\rangle$ component of the light-front wave function and the associated gluon PDF. We explicitly demonstrate an enhancement of the low-$x$ contribution in the quark PDF, which is associated with the large spin-flip matrix element, which also drives the $π-ρ$ mass splitting. The proposed framework also enables an exploration of the effect of the dressed gluon mass for a given valence state at the pion scale. We show that when the gluon mass vanishes, departing from the constituent gluon picture, it has a significant impact on the gluon PDF.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the enhancement of the spin-flip matrix element, which drives the pion-rho mass splitting in the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, also produces a distinctive low-\(x\) enhancement in the quark parton distribution function (PDF) from the \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) Fock component; however, this enhancement depends on the specific treatment of gluonic contributions and the choice of gauge, as the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) results differ significantly from the BLFQ results.

## Method

The authors compute the quark-antiquark-gluon component of the pion light-front wave function by applying an operator to the valence wave function, using the relation \(\psi_{q\bar{q}g} = (M^2_\pi - M^2_{0,q\bar{q}g})^{-1} V \psi_{q\bar{q}}\) where \(V\) is the interaction connecting the \(q\bar{q}\) and \(q\bar{q}g\) sectors in the light-cone gauge. They then derive the quark and gluon PDFs from this higher Fock component using a power-law parametrization of the valence wave function fitted to either BLFQ or BSE results, and compare the PDF contributions across three parameter sets that vary the quark mass in the kinetic part (\(m_q\)), the effective quark mass in the vertex interaction (\(m_f\)), and the gluon mass (\(m_g\)).

## Key numbers

* Model II uses \(m_f = 5.69\) GeV (the large value that reproduces the \(\pi\)-\(\rho\) mass splitting) and yields a \(q\bar{q}g\) component probability of \(1-P_{val} = 0.508\).
* Model III uses masses from the BSE pion model (\(m_q = 0.255\) GeV, \(m_g = 0.638\) GeV) and yields a \(q\bar{q}g\) component probability of \(0.300\).
* The first moments of the PDFs from the \(q\bar{q}g\) sector in Model III are \(\langle x^{q\bar qg}_q \rangle = \langle x^{q\bar qg}_{\bar q} \rangle \approx 0.1\) and \(\langle x^{q\bar qg}_g \rangle \approx 0.1\), compared to BSE values of \(\langle x^{q\bar qg}_q \rangle = \langle x^{q\bar qg}_{\bar q} \rangle = 0.12\) and \(\langle x^{q\bar qg}_g \rangle = 0.06\).
* The BLFQ numerical calculations use basis parameters \(N_{\text{max}} = 14\), \(K = 15\), and HO scale \(b = 0.29\) GeV, with model parameters \(\{m_q, m_g, \kappa, m_f, g_s\} = \{0.39, 0.60, 0.65, 5.69, 1.92\}\) (GeV except \(g_s\)).

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach of Lan (2021) for light mesons, where the valence and \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) sectors each carry about 50% of the total normalization, and compares with the Minkowski-space BSE solution of de Paula (2020) which finds the valence component carrying 70% of the normalization. The framework enables exploration of the dressed gluon mass effect on PDFs and provides a practical method to generate the \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) contribution from the leading spin-antialigned valence wave function, which can be used in future studies applying the BSE in light-cone gauge.
