# Directed flow at midrapidity in sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> We analyze published data from the ALICE Collaboration in order to obtain the first extraction of the recently-proposed rapidity-even directed flow observable v1. An accounting of the correlation due to the conservation of transverse momentum restores the factorization seen by ALICE in all other Fourier harmonics and thus indicates that the remaining correlation gives a reliable measurement of directed flow. We then carry out the first viscous hydrodynamic calculation of directed flow, and show that it is less sensitive to viscosity than higher harmonics. This allows for a direct extraction of the dipole asymmetry of the initial state, providing a strict constraint on the non-equilibrium dynamics of the early-time system. A prediction is then made for v1 in Au-Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

After accounting for the correlation due to global transverse momentum conservation, the first Fourier harmonic of two-particle azimuthal correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV can be explained by collective flow, yielding the first reliable extraction of the rapidity-even directed flow observable \(v_1\) at midrapidity; this extraction is conditional on the assumption that momentum conservation is the only significant non-flow contribution affecting the first harmonic.

## Method

The authors analyzed published ALICE two-particle correlation data \(V_{1\Delta}\) and performed a factorization fit including an additional term \(-k p_T^t p_T^a\) to account for transverse momentum conservation, extracting \(v_1(p_T)\) as a function of centrality. They then carried out 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations using a deformed optical Glauber initial condition with a controlled dipole asymmetry \(\varepsilon_1\), computing \(v_1/\varepsilon_1\) for various values of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s\). By comparing the hydrodynamic results to the extracted \(v_1\) data, they determined allowed ranges for the root-mean-square dipole asymmetry \(\varepsilon_1\) in each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

- Fit parameter \(k\) from Eq. (corrv1) for 0–10% centrality: \(k = 2.5^{+1.1}_{-0.3} \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\); estimated from momentum conservation: \(6.2 \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\)
- Fit parameter \(k\) for 40–50% centrality: \(k = 41.5^{+4.7}_{-3.0} \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\); estimated from momentum conservation: \(35 \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\)
- \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the fit using Eq. (corrflow) (without momentum conservation) for 0–10% centrality: 6; using Eq. (corrv1) (with momentum conservation): 2.0
- \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for 40–50% centrality: 126 (without momentum conservation) vs. 2.4 (with momentum conservation)
- The hydrodynamic calculation used \(\eta/s\) values in the range \(0 < \eta/s < 0.24\), with a narrower range \(0.08 < \eta/s < 0.16\) representing common values from \(v_2\) analyses
- Predictions for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV were made using \(\eta/s = 0.16\)

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (Alver 2010) from higher harmonics (\(n=2\)–6) to the first Fourier harmonic \(v_1\), which had been neglected due to contamination from momentum conservation (Borghini 2000, Borghini 2002). It builds on earlier ideal hydrodynamic calculations of directed flow (Gardim 2011) and a previous extraction attempt at RHIC (Luzum 2010). The results provide the first direct constraint on the initial-state dipole asymmetry \(\varepsilon_1\) from experimental data, ruling out the DIPSY model (Flensburg 2011) above 10% centrality and the PHOBOS Glauber model (Alver 2008) as well as a recent improved MC-KLT model with KNO fluctuations (Dumitru 2012) over the entire centrality range. The extracted \(\varepsilon_1\) values enable predictions for directed flow at RHIC energies that are more reliable than those from any single initial-condition model.
