# A new empirical formula for α-decay half-life and decay chains of Z=120 isotopes

> Experimental $α$-decay half-life, spin, and parity of 398 nuclei in the range 50$\leq$Z$\leq$118 are utilized to propose a new formula (QF) with only 4 coefficients as well as to modify the Tagepera-Nurmia formula with just 3 coefficients (MTNF) by employing nonlinear regressions. These formulas, based on reduced mass ($μ$) and angular momentum taken away by the $α$-particle, are ascertained very effective for both favoured and unfavoured $α$-decay in addition to their excellent match with all (Z, N) combinations of experimental $α$-decay half-lives. After comparing with similar other empirical formulas of $α$-decay half-life, QF and MTNF formulas are purported with accuracy, minimum uncertainty and deviation, dependency on least number of fitted coefficients together with less sensitivity to the uncertainties of $Q$-values. The QF formula is applied to predict $α$-decay half-lives for 724 favoured and 635 unfavoured transitions having experimentally known $Q$-values. Moreover, these available $Q$-values are also employed to test various theoretical approaches viz. RMF, FRDM, WS4, RCHB, etc. along with machine learning method XGBoost for determining theoretical $Q$-values, incisively. Thereafter, using $Q$-values from the most precise theoretical treatment mentioned above along with the proposed formulas, probable $α$-decay chains for Z$=$120 isotopes are identified.

## Key claim

The authors propose two new empirical formulas for α-decay half-lives (QF with 4 coefficients and MTNF with 3 coefficients) that achieve lower standard deviation and uncertainty than other recent formulas, but the paper's predictions for Z=120 isotopes depend on using theoretical Q-values from the WS4 mass model, which the authors identify as the most precise among the tested theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors performed nonlinear regressions using the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm on experimental α-decay half-lives, spins, and parities of 398 nuclei in the range 50≤Z≤118 to fit two formulas: a new quadratic formula (QF) and a modified Tagepera-Nurmia formula (MTNF), both incorporating reduced mass (μ) and the minimum angular momentum (l_min) taken away by the α-particle. They then compared statistical parameters (standard deviation σ, uncertainty u, average deviation factor x̄, and mean deviation δ̄) against seven other recent empirical formulas. Finally, they used Q-values from the WS4 mass model (which gave the lowest σ of 0.3107 for 1580 Q-values) together with their QF and MTNF formulas to predict α-decay chains for even and odd isotopes of Z=120 with neutron numbers 168≤N≤184, comparing α-decay half-lives with spontaneous fission half-lives from a modified Bao formula.

## Key numbers

* Standard deviation (σ) for QF formula: 1.3018; for MTNF formula: 1.5091 (calculated for 398 nuclei in range 50≤Z≤118)
* Number of fitted coefficients: 4 for QF, 3 for MTNF (compared to 8–28 coefficients for other formulas listed)
* ROC (rate of change of half-life with 0.05 MeV Q-value uncertainty): 55.30% for QF, 53.16% for MTNF (lowest among all compared formulas)
* Standard deviation of WS4 theoretical Q-values: 0.3107 (best among RMF at 1.0097, FRDM at 0.4456, RCHB at 2.014, and XGBoost at 1.7021, for 1580 Q-values)
* Predicted log₁₀T₁/₂ for ²⁸⁸120: -6.38 seconds (QF) and -6.54 seconds (MTNF), with spontaneous fission half-life 9.53 seconds, indicating α-decay dominance

## Context

This work extends the line of empirical α-decay half-life formulas that incorporate centrifugal potential terms, such as those by Royer (2020), Akrawy (2018), and the modified RenB (2019) and MYQZR (2019) formulas, but achieves comparable or better accuracy with far fewer fitted coefficients (4 for QF versus 8–28 for others). The paper also tests theoretical mass models (RMF, FRDM, WS4, RCHB) and a machine learning method (XGBoost) for Q-value prediction, finding WS4 most reliable. The predicted decay chains for Z=120 isotopes, using WS4 Q-values, provide specific candidates for future experimental synthesis efforts toward the island of enhanced stability near N=184.
