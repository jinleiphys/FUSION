# Numerical simulations of non-relativistic stochastic fluids via the Metropolis algorithm

> Stochastic hydrodynamics provides a dynamical framework for the evolution of fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, but poses significant challenges in numerical simulations. We present an algorithm for the simulation of non-relativistic stochastic fluids in two spatial dimensions in a box. We use the robust Metropolis algorithm, handling fluctuations and dissipation at once by systematically replacing dissipative terms in the hydrodynamic equations by random forces. The algorithm can easily be modified for numerical simulations of other hydrodynamic theories. We present test cases as well as numerical calculations of the renormalization of shear viscosity.

## Key claim

The authors present a Metropolis-based algorithm for simulating non-relativistic stochastic fluids in two spatial dimensions that handles fluctuations and dissipation simultaneously by replacing dissipative terms with random forces, but the method is demonstrated only for an ideal gas equation of state and requires mesh refinement to control numerical dissipation from the ideal hydrodynamics solver.

## Method

The algorithm splits each time step into two substeps: first evolving the ideal (non-dissipative) advection dynamics using a Kurganov-Tadmor scheme with adaptive step-size integration, then performing a stochastic step where dissipative fluxes are sampled via the Metropolis algorithm. The stochastic step uses local L-shaped updates that correlate noise on adjacent cell faces to respect angular momentum conservation, with acceptance probabilities determined by the total entropy change. Mesh refinement via zero-padding Fourier interpolation is applied before the ideal step to smooth configurations and reduce numerical dissipation.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity renormalization extracted from shear-shear correlators on a lattice with L=80, Δx=1, showing a linear relation between bare L_η and renormalized L_η,ren with slope approximately 1 and a non-zero offset.
* Metropolis noise variances tuned to reproduce continuum fluctuation-dissipation relations: σ_κ² = 2L_κ/((Δx)² Δt), σ_ζ² = 2L_ζ/((Δx)² Δt), σ_η² = 2L_η/((Δx)² Δt).
* Shear mode decay rate in linearized theory given by Γ_k = (L_η)/(2m u_0) k², where u_0 is the equilibrium internal energy density.
* Simulations performed with parameters β=0.4, μ=10.0 in grand-canonical ensemble for shear viscosity extraction.

## Context

This work extends the recent application of the Metropolis algorithm to hydrodynamic simulations by Chattopadhyay (2025), Chattopadhyay (2024), Basar (2024), and Bhambure (2024), but focuses on non-relativistic fluids as a first step toward relativistic stochastic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions. The approach contrasts with earlier Langevin-based stochastic hydrodynamic simulations by Murase (2016), Hirano (2018), Nahrgang (2017), Bluhm (2018), and Singh (2018), which often required smoothing stochastic fluxes to avoid numerical instabilities. The algorithm enables systematic numerical studies of transport coefficient renormalization from hydrodynamic fluctuations, as discussed theoretically by Kovtun (2011), and can be extended to study critical dynamics with a critical equation of state.
