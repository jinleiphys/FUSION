# Rapidity dependence of large-p_t hadron production at RHIC

> We study the dependence of parton energy loss (quenching) on rapidity in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC. This can provides invaluable information on the density of the medium, which should be more dilute going away from mid-rapidity, thereby reducing the effect of quenching. We predict a clear effect at moderate transverse momenta $\sim$ 3 GeV.

# Rapidity dependence of large-$p_t$ hadron production at RHIC

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC should decrease with increasing rapidity, because the medium density is lower away from mid-rapidity, and that a concavity in the rapidity-dependent ratio of nuclear to proton-proton spectra at fixed $p_T$ is a distinctive signal of jet energy loss.

## Method
The authors compute charged hadron spectra in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV using a leading-order pQCD framework with parton distribution functions (GRV94), fragmentation functions (KKP), intrinsic $k_T$ broadening, nuclear shadowing (EKS98 parameterization with impact-parameter dependence), and a parameterized parton energy loss $\Delta E_c(y,E_c) = q_c E_c \, dn/dy$, where $dn/dy$ is the medium rapidity density normalized to PHOBOS data. The energy loss coefficient $q_c = 0.27$ is fixed by reproducing the charged hadron suppression observed at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV, and the rapidity dependence enters through the assumed proportionality of medium density to the measured charged hadron rapidity density.

## Key numbers
- The energy loss coefficient used is $q_c = 0.27$ for gluons, with quark energy loss taken as $4/9$ of the gluon value.
- The intrinsic $k_T$ broadening parameter $\Delta_p^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV for proton-proton collisions.
- The Cronin broadening coefficient $\lambda = 0.1$ GeV$^2$, obtained by fitting charged hadron spectra from various $pA$ data.
- The overall $K$-factor for the pQCD calculation is $K=1.5$.
- The study focuses on hadrons with transverse momenta $p_T \sim 2-6$ GeV, where preliminary RHIC data show suppression by a factor of $2-5$ relative to pQCD expectations.
- The rapidity density of initially produced gluons in the medium is in the range $dN^g/dy \sim 500-1000$ based on earlier $v_2$ analyses.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet quenching at mid-rapidity (Gyulassy-Wang 1994, Baier et al., Zakharov, Wiedemann, GLV, Wang) by proposing rapidity as an independent variable to probe the medium density profile. It builds on an analogous idea applied to $J/\psi$ suppression as a function of $x_F$ (Hüfner et al. 2000). The predicted rapidity-dependent concavity in the nuclear modification factor provides a testable observable for future RHIC data, and the authors suggest extending the approach to study the rapidity dependence of $v_2$ and to compare with direct photon production as a control measurement.
