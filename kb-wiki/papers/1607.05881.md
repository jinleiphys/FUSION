# Effect of isoscalar spin-triplet pairings on spin-isospin responses in $sd-$ shell nuclei

> The spin magnetic dipole transitions and the neutron-proton spin-spin correlations in $sd-$shell even-even nuclei with $N=Z$ are investigated using shell model wave functions. The isoscalar spin-triplet pairing correlation provides a substantial quenching effect on the spin magnetic dipole transitions, especially the isovector (IV) ones. Consequently, an enhanced isoscalar spin-triplet pairing interaction influences the proton-neutron spin-spin correlation deduced from the difference between the isoscalar (IS) and the IV sum rule strengths. The effect of the $Δ$ ($Δ_{33}$ resonance)-hole coupling is examined in the IV spin transition and the spin-spin correlations of the ground states.

## Key claim

The isoscalar spin-triplet pairing correlation provides a substantial quenching effect on spin magnetic dipole transitions, especially the isovector ones, and this effect can explain the observed quenching without relying solely on effective g-factor operators; the Delta-hole coupling contributes at most a 20% quenching effect on the isovector spin transitions, which is smaller than the 30-40% suggested by quark model calculations.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in the full sd-shell model space using the USDB effective interaction for even-even N=Z nuclei (20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar). The isoscalar spin-triplet pairing interaction was enhanced by multiplying the relevant J^π=1^+, T=0 matrix elements by a factor of 1.2 (USDB*), and an additional quenching factor q=0.9 was applied to the isovector spin operator to simulate Delta-hole coupling (USDB**). The calculated IS and IV spin-M1 transition strengths and accumulated sums were compared with experimental data from proton inelastic scattering at Ep=295 MeV from Matsubara (2015).

## Key numbers

- In 28Si, the enhanced IS pairing produces about a 20% quenching effect on the IS transition strength: B(sigma)=6.82 in USDB versus 5.63 in USDB*
- The IS spin quenching corresponds to g_s^eff(IS)/g_s(IS)=0.91, consistent with the factor introduced in USDB(4) by Richter (2008)
- The enhanced IS pairing reduces the IV spin transition strength corresponding to g_s^eff(IV)/g_s(IV)=0.87
- With the additional quenching factor q=0.9, the total IV quenching becomes g_s^eff(IV)/g_s(IV)=0.87x0.9=0.79, close to the value 0.764 found for GT transitions in USDB by Richter (2008)
- Two IV 1+ states in 28Si have strong spin strengths B(sigma tau)=2.05 and 0.92 at Ex=11.45 and 14.01 MeV, respectively
- The quenching factor q^2=(0.9)^2=0.81 for the IV transition gives the upper limit of the Delta-hole coupling effect, considerably smaller than the 30-40% suggested by the quark model of Bohr and Mottelson

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-isospin responses in sd-shell nuclei, following experimental measurements by Matsubara (2015) that showed IS quenching is substantially smaller than IV quenching in several N=Z sd-shell nuclei, contradicting earlier theoretical expectations from Richter (2008) that both quenchings should be similar. The paper builds on previous work by Bai, Fujita, Tanimura, and Sagawa-Colo showing that isoscalar spin-triplet pairing correlations enhance GT strength near ground states while quenching total sum rules (Zamick, 2002). The results enable future experimental and theoretical studies to validate the lower Delta-hole coupling effect and to examine meson exchange current effects and higher configuration mixings together with the IS pairing effect.
