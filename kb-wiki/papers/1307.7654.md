# Subthreshold pion production within a transport description of central Au+Au collisions

> Mapping out the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter is a long standing problem in nuclear physics. Recent emphasis is onto the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with experiments needing dedicated symmetry-energy observables. Towards the latter goal, we employ pBUU transport model to simulate pion production in heavy ion collision (HIC). We find that the net pion yield tests the momentum dependence of nuclear mean field (MF). In exploring the sensitivity of pion observables to the symmetry energy at higher than normal densities, we find that our calculations of pion ratios contradict, at some level, predictions from both IBUU and ImIQMD models. We propose to employ the pion ratio in the high-energy tail of spectra in future experiments, to distinguish between different variants of high-density symmetry energy.

# Subthreshold pion production within a transport description of central Au+Au collisions

## Key claim

The net pion yield in heavy ion collisions tests the momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field, but the charged pion ratio is largely insensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy in the pBUU transport model, contradicting predictions from both IBUU and ImIQMD models. The authors propose that the pion ratio in the high-energy tail of spectra can distinguish between different variants of high-density symmetry energy, rather than the net yield ratio.

## Method

The authors employed the pBUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 400A MeV, 800A MeV, and 1.5A GeV. They adjusted the parameters governing the momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field, specifically the parameters C and λ in the velocity parameterization, to reproduce measured pion multiplicities from the FOPI Collaboration. They then calculated charged pion ratios using different power-law parameterizations of the symmetry energy (varying the stiffness parameter γ) and compared results to FOPI data and to predictions from IBUU and ImIQMD models.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Nuclear incompressibility K = 240 MeV ± 20 MeV (from Giant Monopole Resonance studies)
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: -16 MeV per nucleon
- New mean-field parameters: C = 0.300, λ = 0.400 1/c² (compared to previous C = 0.643, λ = 0.948 1/c²)
- New incompressibility K = 230 MeV, nucleon effective mass m*/m = 0.75 (previous: K = 210 MeV, m*/m = 0.7)
- Kinetic symmetry energy coefficient S_kin0 ≈ 12.3 MeV
- Interaction symmetry energy coefficient S_int0 ≈ 20 MeV
- At 400A MeV, the previous momentum-dependent MF predicted only about half the measured pion yield; the new parameterization brings calculations into agreement with FOPI data at 400A MeV, 800A MeV, and 1.5A GeV
- Pion potentials in pBUU for γ from 1 to 2 are within 30% of the Toki pion-nucleus optical potential in the nuclear interior

## Context

This work extends the pBUU transport model developed by Danielewicz et al. (2002) to subthreshold pion production, testing the model against FOPI Collaboration measurements (Reisdorf et al., 2007) for the first time at energies near the NN pion production threshold. The paper addresses the long-standing problem of constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities, following the proposal by Li (2002) that charged pion ratios are sensitive observables. The authors find that their pBUU calculations contradict predictions from both the IBUU model (Li, 2005) and the ImIQMD model (Feng and Jin, 2010), which show opposing sensitivities of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio to symmetry energy stiffness. This work enables future experiments to use differential pion ratios in the high-energy tail of spectra, rather than net yield ratios, to distinguish between stiff and soft symmetry energy at high density.
