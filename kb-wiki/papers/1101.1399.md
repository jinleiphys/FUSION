# The relativistic mean-field equations of the atomic nucleus

> In nuclear physics, the relativistic mean-field theory describes the nucleus as a system of Dirac nucleons which interact via meson fields. In a static case and without nonlinear self-coupling of the $σ$ meson, the relativistic mean-field equations become a system of Dirac equations where the potential is given by the meson and photon fields. The aim of this work is to prove the existence of solutions of these equations. We consider a minimization problem with constraints that involve negative spectral projectors and we apply the concentration-compactness lemma to find a minimizer of this problem. We show that this minimizer is a solution of the relativistic mean-field equations considered.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first mathematically rigorous proof of the existence of solutions to the relativistic mean-field equations of the atomic nucleus, but only under the condition that the coupling constants \(g_\sigma, g_\omega, g_\rho\) and the elementary charge \(e\) are sufficiently small (the "weakly relativistic regime"), and without nonlinear self-coupling of the \(\sigma\) meson.

## Method

The authors consider a static, no-sea-approximation relativistic mean-field model with fixed occupation weights and no nonlinear \(\sigma\) self-coupling, reducing the system to nonlinear Dirac equations with Yukawa and Coulomb potentials. They formulate a minimization problem for the energy functional under orthonormality constraints and the additional constraint that each single-particle wavefunction lies in the positive spectral subspace of its corresponding Dirac Hamiltonian (i.e., \(\Lambda^-_{\mu,\Psi}\Psi_\mu = 0\) for protons and neutrons). Using the concentration-compactness lemma of Lions (1984), they analyze minimizing sequences and prove that a minimizer exists if a strict inequality involving the infimum of the extended minimization problem holds, and that this minimizer satisfies the original relativistic mean-field equations.

## Key numbers

* The condition for the self-adjoint isomorphism property of \(H_{\mu,\Psi}\) requires \(\frac{g_\sigma^2 A + g_\rho^2 \max(Z,N)}{4\pi} < \frac{2}{\pi/2 + 2/\pi}\) and similar inequalities for other combinations of coupling constants and nucleon numbers.
* The boundedness of minimizing sequences in \((H^{1/2})^A\) requires \(d_\mu < \frac{4}{5}\) for \(\mu = p,n\), where \(d_p = \frac{(g^2_\sigma + g^2_\omega + g^2_\rho)A + e^2 Z}{2\pi}\) and \(d_n = \frac{(g^2_\sigma + g^2_\omega + g^2_\rho)A}{2\pi}\).
* The potential \(V_{p,\Psi}\) belongs to \(L^r(\mathbb{R}^3)\) for \(3 < r \le r_c\) with \(r_c = \frac{9-3\varepsilon}{\varepsilon}\) for any \(\varepsilon > 0\), while \(V_{n,\Psi}\) belongs to \(L^r(\mathbb{R}^3)\) for \(1 \le r \le r_c\).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous mathematical analysis of nuclear models from nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock theory, studied by Gogny and Lions (1986), to the relativistic mean-field framework, which had previously lacked rigorous existence proofs despite its widespread use in nuclear physics. The approach adapts the variational method of Esteban and Sere (1999, 2001) for the Dirac-Fock equations, but differs because the present energy functional is translation-invariant and contains an attractive nonlinear interaction, requiring the concentration-compactness argument rather than relying on a confining nuclear potential. The result provides a condition for the existence of a ground state solution and opens the way to rigorous studies of relativistic nuclear models.
