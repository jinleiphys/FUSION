# QGP Formation and Strange Antibaryons

> We analyze current experimental results and explore, as function of the collision energy and stopping in relativistic nuclear collisions, the production yields of strange antibaryons, assuming formation of a deconfined thermal QGP-fireball which undergoes a sudden hadronisation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed yields of strange antibaryons in relativistic nuclear collisions can be explained by the formation of a deconfined thermal quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fireball that undergoes sudden hadronisation, but only if the fireball is baryon-rich at currently accessible energies (√s ≤ 10A GeV) rather than baryon-free as in the ultra-relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors model the collision as a thermal QGP fireball with initial conditions constrained by the specific energy E/B and dynamical pressure balance, using quark fugacities λ_i and an off-equilibrium strangeness saturation parameter γ_s. They compute the fireball properties at chemical equilibration temperature T_ch and at the later hadronisation temperature T_0, then derive particle ratios and yields as functions of collision energy and stopping fraction η. The theoretical results are compared to experimental data from the NA35 and WA85 collaborations for S–Ag/W/Pb and S–S collisions at 200A GeV and Au–Au collisions at AGS energies.

## Key numbers

- For S–W/Pb collisions at E/B = 8.8 GeV: T_0 = 233 MeV, which agrees with the experimental inverse slope T_⊥ = 232 ± 5 MeV from WA85 (1995)
- For S–W/Pb collisions at E/B = 8.8 GeV: λ_q = 1.49, in agreement with previous data analysis
- WA85 measured Ξ⁻/Λ̅ = 0.21 ± 0.02 for p_⊥ > 1.2 GeV, which determines γ_s = 0.70 and η_p = 1/2
- WA85 measured (Ω + Ω̅)/(Ξ⁻ + Ξ̅⁻) = 0.8 ± 0.4 for p_⊥ > 1.6 GeV
- NA35 measured Λ̅/p̅ ≈ 0.8 ± 0.25 for S–Au at 200A GeV
- Initial γ_s ≈ 1/7 at t_ch ≈ 1.5 fm/c, corresponding to a 7 times slower strange quark relaxation time
- Specific fireball energies range from E/B = 2.6 GeV (Au–Au at AGS) to 8.8 GeV (S–Pb at 200A GeV)
- For Au–Au at E/B = 2.6 GeV: T_ch = 0.212 GeV, λ_q = 4.14, S/B = 12.3
- For Pb–Pb at 158A GeV with η = 1: T_ch = 0.324 GeV, λ_q = 1.61, S/B = 34.9

## Context

This work extends the QGP-fireball model developed by Rafelski (1982) and collaborators, building on earlier predictions of enhanced strange antibaryon production in A–A reactions (StrRaf, 1982) and the sudden hadronisation scenario (gammas, 1991). The analysis uses experimental data from NA35 (1994) on Λ and Λ̅ rapidity distributions and from WA85 (1995) on cascade and omega yields. The paper enables identification of the threshold energy for QGP formation by looking for discontinuities in the Ξ̅/Λ̅ ratio as a function of collision energy, along with the onset of strangeness saturation (γ_s → 1), λ_s = 1, and entropy enhancement.
