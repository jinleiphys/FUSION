# The Color Glass Condensate at RHIC

> The Color Glass Condensate formalism and its application to high energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC are discussed. We argue that the RHIC data supports the view that the Color Glass Condensate provides the initial conditions for gold-gold collisions at RHIC while final state (Quark Gluon Plasma) effects are responsible for the high $p_t$ suppression in mid rapidity. At forward rapidities in deuteron-gold collisions, however, Color Glass Condensate is the underlying physics of the observed suppression of the particle spectra and their centrality dependence.

## Key claim

The paper argues that RHIC data supports the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) as the correct description of initial conditions for gold-gold collisions, but acknowledges that final-state Quark Gluon Plasma effects are responsible for the high transverse momentum suppression at mid rapidity; the CGC is the underlying physics for the observed suppression of particle spectra and their centrality dependence only at forward rapidities in deuteron-gold collisions.

## Method

The author applies the CGC effective field theory formalism to particle production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, using a k_t factorized gluon production cross section for nucleus-nucleus collisions and a dipole scattering cross section for quark-nucleus scattering in deuteron-gold collisions. The calculations are compared to experimental data on hadron multiplicities and nuclear modification factors, particularly the BRAHMS data at forward rapidity y=3.2.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at forward rapidity y=3.2: Q_s^A(y) = sqrt(2) e^(lambda y/2) ~ 2.2 GeV
- More than 95% of produced particles have transverse momentum less than 1 GeV in nucleus-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV
- The quark-nucleus scattering mechanism describes particle production up to ~1.8 GeV (assuming average fragmentation fraction <z> ~ 0.8)
- Hadron multiplicities in gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s)=130 GeV show good agreement with CGC calculations

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism from earlier theoretical developments by Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (GLR) and Mueller and Qiu (MQ) on gluon recombination, and the JIMWLK and BK evolution equations. It directly contradicts conventional Glauber-based multiple scattering models (which predicted Cronin enhancement at forward rapidity) and instead supports the CGC predictions of suppression. The paper enables future measurements of photons and dileptons to further test CGC, and calls for another deuteron-gold run at RHIC to establish CGC properties.
