# On the near-threshold $\bar pp$ invariant mass spectrum measured in $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ decays

> A systematic analysis of the near-threshold enhancement in the $\bar pp$ invariant mass spectrum seen in the decay reactions $J/ψ\to x \bar pp$ and $ψ(3686) \to x \bar pp$ $(x = γ,\, ω,\, ρ,\, π,\, η)$ is presented. The enhancement is assumed to be due to the $\bar NN$ final-state interaction (FSI) and the pertinent FSI effects are evaluated in an approach that is based on the distorted-wave Born approximation. For the $\bar NN$ interaction a recent potential derived within chiral effective field theory and fitted to results of a partial-wave analysis of $\bar pp$ scattering data is considered and, in addition, an older phenomenological model constructed by the Jülich group. It is shown that the near-threshold spectrum observed in various decay reactions can be reproduced simultaneously and consistently by our treatment of the $\bar pp$ FSI. It turns out that the interaction in the isospin-1 $^1S_0$ channel required for the description of the $J/ψ\to γ\bar pp$ decay predicts a $\bar NN$ bound state.

# On the near-threshold \(\bar pp\) invariant mass spectrum measured in \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi'\) decays

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the \(\bar pp\) invariant mass spectrum observed across multiple \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(3686)\) decay channels can be reproduced simultaneously and consistently by \(\bar NN\) final-state interaction (FSI) effects alone, provided the FSI is treated via the distorted-wave Born approximation rather than the simpler Migdal-Watson approach. The interaction in the isospin-1 \(^1S_0\) channel required to describe the \(J/\psi \to \gamma \bar pp\) decay predicts a \(\bar NN\) bound state, though its exact binding energy depends on the assumed isospin mixture of the production amplitude.

## Method

The authors analyzed the \(\bar pp\) invariant mass spectra from \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(3686)\) decays to \(x \bar pp\) with \(x = \gamma, \omega, \rho, \pi, \eta\) using a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) formalism. FSI effects were incorporated by multiplying the elementary decay amplitude with the inverse Jost function, calculated explicitly from realistic \(\bar NN\) potentials rather than from the effective-range approximation. Two \(\bar NN\) interactions were employed: the phenomenological Juelich model A(OBE) and a chiral effective field theory (EFT) potential fitted to a partial-wave analysis of \(\bar pp\) scattering data. For the radiative decay \(J/\psi \to \gamma \bar pp\), the chiral EFT potential was refitted to simultaneously reproduce the \(\bar pp\) invariant mass spectrum and the \(^1S_0\) partial-wave cross sections from the partial-wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar pp\) scattering lengths from antiprotonic hydrogen atoms are in the order of 1 to 2 fm, in contrast to the \(np\) \(^1S_0\) scattering length of approximately -24 fm.
- The analysis considered excess energies up to \(M(\bar pp)-2m_p \approx 100\) MeV for the \(\bar pp\) system.
- For the refitted chiral EFT potential with cutoff combination \(\{\Lambda, \tilde\Lambda\} = \{450\ \text{MeV}, 500\ \text{MeV}\}\), the bound state in the \(^{31}S_0\) channel is located at \(E_B = (-36.9 - i\,47.20)\ \text{MeV}\) relative to the \(\bar NN\) threshold.
- For the alternative isospin mixture \(T = 0.7 T^0 + 0.3 T^1\), the bound state position is around \(E_B = (-14.8 - i\,39.7)\ \text{MeV}\).
- The fit to the \(J/\psi \to \gamma \bar pp\) spectrum was performed up to excess energies of 67.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the Juelich-Bonn group (Sibirtsev et al., 2005; Haidenbauer et al., 2008) and others (Kerbikov, 2003; Bugg, 2003; Zou, 2003; Loiseau, 2005; Entem, 2006) that demonstrated FSI effects could explain the near-threshold enhancement in \(J/\psi \to \gamma \bar pp\), but which relied on the simpler Migdal-Watson approximation or effective-range approach. The present analysis shows that the Migdal-Watson approximation is inadequate for \(\bar NN\) systems due to their small scattering lengths (1-2 fm versus -24 fm for \(np\)), and that the DWBA treatment is required. The refitted chiral EFT potential produces a bound state in the isospin-1 \(^1S_0\) channel, which may be related to the \(X(1835)\) resonance observed by BES in \(J/\psi \to \gamma \pi^+ \pi^- \eta'\) (Ablikim, 2005), though the bound state found here is in \(I=1\) rather than \(I=0\) as previously suggested. The same formalism and interactions were previously applied successfully to the reaction \(e^+ e^- \leftrightarrow \bar pp\) by the same authors (Haidenbauer, 2014).
