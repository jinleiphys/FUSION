# gamma-vibrational states in superheavy nuclei

> Recent experimental advances have made it possible to study excited structure in superheavy nuclei. The observed states have often been interpreted as quasi-particle excitations. We show that in superheavy nuclei collective vibrations systematically appear as low-energy excitation modes. By using the microscopic Triaxial Projected Shell Model, we make a detailed prediction on gamma-vibrational states and their E2 transition probabilities to the ground state band in Fermium and Nobelium isotopes where active structure research is going on, and in 270Ds, the heaviest isotope where decay data have been obtained for the ground-state and for an isomeric state.

## Key claim
Collective gamma-vibrational states systematically appear as low-energy excitation modes below 1 MeV in superheavy nuclei, contrary to the prevailing interpretation that observed excited states in this mass region are exclusively quasi-particle excitations. The authors qualify that their predictions depend on the triaxial deformation parameter epsilon-prime, which is adjusted to reproduce the one candidate gamma-band in 256Fm and then applied uniformly across the region.

## Method
The authors used the Triaxial Projected Shell Model, which diagonalizes a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian in a basis of three-dimensionally angular-momentum-projected triaxially deformed Nilsson plus BCS states. The basis includes three major harmonic-oscillator shells for each nucleon type (N=5,6,7 for neutrons; N=4,5,6 for protons). The triaxial deformation parameter epsilon-prime is treated as a free parameter, set to 0.105 for 256Fm to match the observed 2+ bandhead and to 0.100 for all other nuclei studied.

## Key numbers
- Gamma-band head energies in Fm and No isotopes lie at 0.6-0.8 MeV above the ground state (for nuclei with N=150-156)
- For 270Ds, predicted ground-state band energies: E(2_0)=0.048 MeV, E(4_0)=0.159 MeV, E(6_0)=0.332 MeV, E(8_0)=0.568 MeV, E(10_0)=0.863 MeV
- For 270Ds, predicted gamma-band head energy E(2_2)=0.565 MeV
- Pairing gaps from BCS for 256Fm: Delta_n ≈ 0.5 MeV (neutrons), Delta_p ≈ 0.8 MeV (protons)
- 2-gamma phonon bandhead predicted at about 1.8 MeV in Fm and No isotopes, lower than the 2.029 MeV in 166Er and 2.056 MeV in 168Er
- 3-gamma phonon states predicted at about 3 MeV in Fm and No isotopes
- In-band B(E2) values for g-bands: e.g., 256Fm B(E2, 2_0→0_0)=297 W.u., B(E2, 4_0→2_0)=425 W.u., B(E2, 6_0→4_0)=470 W.u., B(E2, 8_0→6_0)=496 W.u.
- Inter-band B(E2, 2_2→0_0) values: 3.97 W.u. for 256Fm, 4.19 W.u. for 270Ds
- Inter-band B(E2, 2_2→2_0) values: 5.83 W.u. for 256Fm, 6.60 W.u. for 270Ds

## Context
This work extends the Triaxial Projected Shell Model approach of Sun (2000) to the superheavy mass region, where previous structure studies focused on quasi-particle excitations (Rodinger 2007, Rodinger 2006, Tandel 2006, Greenlees 2007). The calculations support the interpretation by Hall et al. of the 2+ band in 256Fm as a gamma-vibrational band, and predict that such collective states should be systematically present below 1 MeV excitation energy in Fm, No, and Ds isotopes. The results call for experimental attention to collective excitations in the low-energy region where active structure research is currently carried out, and provide predicted B(E2) values that can help distinguish collective from quasi-particle states in future experiments.
