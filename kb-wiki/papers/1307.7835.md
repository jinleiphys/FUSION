# Landau's statistical mechanics for quasiparticle models

> Landau's formalism of statistical mechanics \cite{la.1} is applied to the quasiparticle model of quark gluon plasma. It is a general formalism and consistent with our earlier studies \cite{ba.1} based on Pathria's formalism \cite{pa.1}. Both the formalism are consistent with thermodynamics and statistical mechanics. Under certain conditions, which are wrongly called thermodynamic consistent relation, we recover other formalism of quasiparticle system \cite{go.1}, widely studied in quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Landau's formalism of statistical mechanics, when correctly applied to quasiparticle models of quark-gluon plasma with temperature-dependent mass treated as an external parameter, yields thermodynamic expressions where only the energy density retains the ideal-gas form, while pressure and entropy acquire extra temperature-dependent terms, contradicting the widespread assumption (model-I) that both pressure and energy density should have the ideal-gas form.

## Method

The author applies Landau's statistical mechanics formalism for systems with external conditions (Landau 1980, pages 109–110) to a quasiparticle model of quark-gluon plasma at zero chemical potential, treating the thermal mass m(T) and optionally a bag pressure B(T) as temperature-dependent external parameters. Starting from the Gibbs distribution function, the author derives expressions for entropy, Helmholtz free energy, and pressure by comparing the variation of the normalization factor with the thermodynamic relation δU + PδV = TδS, then extends the calculation to include a temperature-dependent vacuum energy term B(T).

## Key numbers

- The formalism is shown to be consistent with lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budafest group (Fodor 2003) over the range from 1.5 T_c to 1000 T_c, using a single free parameter in model-II.
- Perturbative QCD requires expansion up to order g^6 to fit lattice data even at high temperatures like 1000 T_c, and still fails near T_c up to approximately 3 T_c.
- Model-I (Goloviznin and Satz 1993) has more than two free parameters, while model-II (Bannur 2007) has only one free parameter.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier quasiparticle model (Bannur 2007) developed using Pathria's formalism of statistical mechanics, and directly addresses the thermodynamic consistency debate between two classes of quasiparticle models: model-I (Goloviznin and Satz 1993; Gorenstein and Yang 1995) which starts from an ideal-gas expression for pressure, and model-II (Bannur 2007; various authors 2007–2013) which starts from energy density. The paper clarifies that the so-called "thermodynamic consistency relation" of model-I is an unnecessary extra constraint, and that Landau's formalism naturally reproduces model-II without such conditions. This enables a simpler, single-parameter description of quark-gluon plasma that fits lattice data from 1.5 T_c to 1000 T_c, while also showing that near T_c (below 1.5 T_c) plasma-based models may need replacement by strongly coupled plasma models (Shuryak 2004) or monopole models (Chernodub 2005).
