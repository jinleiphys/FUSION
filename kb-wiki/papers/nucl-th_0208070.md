# Effective Theory of the Color-Flavor-Locked Phase

> We explain how an effective theory of the CFL phase can be used to study the effect of the strange quark mass on the ground state and the excitation spectrum. We also apply the effective theory to the problem of neutrino emission from a CFL quark core inside a neutron star.

# Effective Theory of the Color-Flavor-Locked Phase

## Key claim

The effective chiral theory of the CFL phase can describe the effect of the strange quark mass on the ground state and excitation spectrum, but the effective theory is only reliable for chemical potentials smaller than the gap, and near the unlocking transition where the strange quark mass squared divided by twice the Fermi momentum approaches the gap, a more microscopic calculation is necessary.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian for the CFL phase, where the only relevant degrees of freedom at energies below the gap are the Goldstone modes associated with chiral symmetry and baryon number breaking. The coefficients of the effective Lagrangian (pion decay constant, linear and quadratic mass terms) are determined using perturbative QCD at high baryon density, and the effective potential is minimized to study kaon condensation as a function of the strange quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the CFL phase: f_pi^2 = (21 - 8 log(2))/18 * (p_F^2/(2 pi^2)), where p_F is the Fermi momentum
- Quadratic mass coefficients: A_1 = -A_2 = 3 Delta^2/(4 pi^2), A_3 = 0, where Delta is the gap
- Kaon mass at O(M^2) in the limit of exact isospin symmetry: (m_K^0)^2 = (4A/f_pi^2) * m_u,d * (m_u,d + m_s)
- Critical strange quark mass for kaon condensation: m_s|_crit approximately equals 3 (m_u,d Delta^2)^(1/3)
- Hypercharge density in the kaon condensed phase: n_Y = f_pi^2 mu_s (1 - (m_K^0)^4/mu_s^4), where mu_s = m_s^2/(2p_F)
- The hypercharge density satisfies n_Y < Delta p_F^2/(2 pi^2) within the range of validity of the effective theory

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral theory of the CFL phase developed by Casalbuoni (1999) and the perturbative calculations of the pion decay constant by Son (1999) and the quadratic mass coefficients by Son (1999) and Schafer (2001). It addresses the problem of flavor symmetry breaking due to quark masses first studied by Alford (1999), Schafer (1999), and others. The results enable calculations of neutrino emission and cooling properties of neutron stars with CFL quark cores, as pursued in subsequent work by Reddy (2002) and Jaikumar (2002).
