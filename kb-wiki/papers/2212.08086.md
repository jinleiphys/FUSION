# Are gluon showers inside a quark-gluon plasma strongly coupled? a theorist's test

> We study whether in-medium showers of high-energy gluons can be treated as a sequence of individual splitting processes $g{\to}gg$, or whether there is significant quantum overlap between where one splitting ends and the next begins. Accounting for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, we calculate such overlap effects to leading order in high-energy $α_{\rm s}(μ)$ for the simplest theoretical situation. We investigate a measure of overlap effects that is independent of physics that can be absorbed into an effective value $\hat q_{\rm eff}$ of the jet-quenching parameter $\hat q$.

## Key claim

Overlap effects from consecutive gluon splittings in a quark-gluon plasma produce only a tiny correction (a few percent) to the shape of the energy deposition distribution, even when the running coupling is as large as \(N_c \alpha_s(\mu) = 1\), provided that double-logarithmic effects from soft bremsstrahlung are absorbed into an effective jet-quenching parameter \(\hat q_{\rm eff}\). This contradicts naive expectations that such overlap corrections would be large when the coupling is strong.

## Method

The authors calculate next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections to the LPM/BDMPS-Z splitting rates for \(g \to gg\) and \(g \to ggg\) in a static, homogeneous quark-gluon plasma, using the multiple-scattering (\(\hat q\)) approximation and taking the large-\(N_c\) limit. They define a factorized net splitting rate that absorbs all double and subleading single logarithms from soft bremsstrahlung (with energy below a factorization scale \(\Lambda_{\rm fac}\)) into an effective \(\hat q_{\rm eff}(\Lambda_{\rm fac})\). They then solve the shower evolution equation for the energy deposition distribution \(\epsilon(z)\) and compute the shape function \(S(Z)\) (the distribution rescaled to unit mean stopping distance), isolating overlap corrections that cannot be absorbed into \(\hat q_{\rm eff}\).

## Key numbers

- The relative correction to the width \(\sigma / \langle z \rangle\) of the energy deposition shape is \(\chi \alpha_s = (-0.019 \pm 0.001 \ln \kappa) \, C_A \alpha_s(\mu)\) for \(\Lambda_{\rm fac} = \kappa x(1-x)E\) with canonical \(\kappa=1\), where \(\mu = (\hat q_A \Lambda_{\rm fac})^{1/4}\).
- Even for \(N_c \alpha_s(\mu) = 1\), the correction is a tiny few-percent effect.
- In contrast, the analogous correction for electron-initiated showers in large-\(N_f\) QED is \(\chi \alpha_{\rm EM} = -0.87 \, N_f \alpha_{\rm EM}(\mu)\), which is large for \(N_f \alpha_{\rm EM} = 1\).
- The relative difference between the factorized net rate \([d\Gamma/dx]_{\rm net}^{\rm fac}\) and the LO rate \([d\Gamma/dx]^{\rm LO}\) can be of order \(N_c \alpha_s \times 100\%\) for democratic splittings.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of overlapping formation-time effects in medium-induced showers, previously studied in QED by Arnold (2022) for charge deposition, to gluon showers in QCD. It builds on the LPM/BDMPS-Z formalism for single splittings (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff 1995-1997; Zakharov 1996-1997) and on the demonstration by Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2013-2014) that double-logarithmic overlap effects from soft bremsstrahlung can be absorbed into \(\hat q\). The results enable future studies of whether adding quarks or comparing energy vs. charge deposition would qualitatively change the conclusion that gluon showers remain weakly coupled even for moderately large coupling.
