# Relativistic field-theoretical formulation of the three-dimensional equations for the three fermion system

> A new kind of the relativistic three-body equations for the three fermion systems are suggested. These equations are derived in the framework of the standard field-theoretical $S$-matrix approach in the time-ordered three dimensional form. Therefore corresponding relativistic covariant equations are three-dimensional from the beginning. The solutions of the considered equations satisfy automatically the unitarity condition and for the leptons these equations are exactly gauge invariant even after the truncation over the multiparticle ($n>3$) intermediate states. Moreover, the form of these three-body equations does not depend on the choice of the model Lagrangian and it is the same for the formulations with and without quark degrees of freedom. The effective potential of the suggested equations is defined by the vertex functions with two on-mass shell particles. It is emphasized that these INPUT vertex functions can be constructed from experimental data. Special attention is given to the comparison with the three-body Faddeev equations. Unlike to these equations, the suggested three-body equation have the form of the Lippmann-Schwinger-type equations with the connected potential. In addition, the microscopical potential of the suggested equations contains the contributions from the three-body forces and from the particle creation (annihilation) mechanism on the one external particles. The structure of the three-body forces, appearing in the considered field-theoretical formulation, is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new kind of relativistic three-body equations for three-fermion systems that are three-dimensional from the outset, avoiding the ambiguities that arise from reducing four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations, and claims these equations automatically satisfy unitarity and, for leptons, exact gauge invariance even after truncation of multiparticle intermediate states.

## Method

The author derives the equations using the standard field-theoretical S-matrix approach in time-ordered three-dimensional form, starting from spectral decomposition formulas (off-shell unitarity conditions) for three-body amplitudes. After separating connected and disconnected parts of the amplitudes and potentials, the equations are linearized into Lippmann-Schwinger-type equations with a connected potential that includes contributions from three-body forces and particle creation/annihilation mechanisms. The effective potential is defined by vertex functions with two on-mass-shell particles, which can be constructed from experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The linearized potential has the explicit form \(U_{\alpha\beta}(E)=A_{\alpha\beta}+E B_{\alpha\beta}\) with hermitian matrices \(A\) and \(B\) satisfying \(A_{\alpha\beta}=A_{\beta\alpha}^{*}\) and \(B_{\alpha\beta}=B_{\beta\alpha}^{*}\).
* The cluster decomposition of the s-channel term in the effective potential generates \(2\times 4+6+2=16\) connected terms, and the u-channel term produces another 16 connected terms, yielding 32 independent skeleton diagrams.
* For the \(1d\to 1'd'\) scattering reaction, the cluster decomposition of the effective potential yields only 6 terms.
* The author notes that for intermediate energies up to 2 GeV of the incoming proton for the \(Nd-3N\) systems, one can neglect diagrams with \(2\overline{N}\) and \(3\overline{N}\) intermediate states.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical treatment of three-body systems beyond the traditional Faddeev equations, which require splitting the potential into four pieces to handle disconnected parts and suffer from overcounting problems. The author compares the approach to the three-body generalization of Bethe-Salpeter equations (Kvin, Afnan) and notes that the new equations are free of ambiguities in choosing three-dimensional Green functions and effective potentials. The formulation enables practical calculations with fewer approximations than the Bethe-Salpeter approach, and the equal-time anticommutator part of the potential can reproduce the One Boson Exchange Bonn model of the NN potential (Machavariani, 2001).
