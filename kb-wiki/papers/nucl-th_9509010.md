# Higher excitations of $ω$ and $ϕ$ in dilepton spectra

> We consider lepton pair production via two-hadron annihilation through various isoscalar vector mesons within hot, baryon-free matter. This is tantamount to constructing effective form factors which we model using a vector-meson-dominance approach and compare with experiment. In particular, we consider the reactions $πρ\to e^+e^-$ and $\bar K K^{*}(892)$ + c.c. $\to e^+e^-$. We find that $ω(1390)$ and $ϕ(1680)$ are visible in the mass spectrum for the thermal production rate above the $π^{+}π^{-} \to e^+e^-$ tail and even above the $πa_{1}\to e^+e^-$ results---both of which were considered important in their respective mass regions.

# Higher excitations of ω and φ in dilepton spectra

## Key claim

The higher-lying isoscalar vector meson resonances ω(1390) and φ(1680) produce visible peaks in the thermal dilepton production rate above the previously considered π+π- annihilation tail and even above the πa1 reaction, provided that the effective form factors are constructed using vector-meson dominance and constrained by experimental data on the time-reversed processes e+e- → πρ and e+e- → KK*.

## Method

The authors model lepton pair production from hot, baryon-free hadronic matter via the reactions πρ → e+e- and KK*(892) + c.c. → e+e-, where the intermediate state is an isoscalar vector meson that couples to a virtual photon through vector-meson dominance. They construct effective form factors by summing over the contributing resonances (φ(1020), ω(1390), ω(1600) for the πρ channel; φ(1680) for the KK* channel) and fix the coupling constant ratios and relative phases by fitting to experimental cross sections for the time-reversed processes e+e- → πρ and e+e- → K0S K± π∓. Thermal production rates are then computed assuming a thermal distribution of the initial hadrons, using both Bose-Einstein and classical (Boltzmann) distributions, with the latter found to be adequate.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant ratio for the φ(1020) is fixed as gφπρ/gφ = 0.15 GeV-1, derived from the φ → ρπ partial decay rate (12.9% of 4.43 MeV full width) and the φ → e+e- branching fraction (3.09 × 10-4) yielding gφ = 12.9.
- For the ω(1390) resonance, the fitted coupling ratio is gωπρ/gω = 0.29 GeV-1 with a phase angle φω = 5π/4.
- For the ω(1600) resonance, the fitted coupling ratio is gω'πρ/gω' = 0.05 GeV-1 with a phase angle φω' = π.
- For the φ(1680) resonance in the KK* channel, the coupling ratio is gφ'KK*/gφ' = 0.19 GeV-1.
- Thermal production rates are computed at temperatures of T = 150 MeV and T = 200 MeV, with the ω(1390) and φ(1680) peaks visible above the π+π- annihilation tail and the πa1 results at both temperatures.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of thermal dilepton production from a pion gas by Gale and Lichard (1994) and from πa1 reactions by Song, Ko, and Gale (1994), which had been considered dominant in their respective mass regions. The authors demonstrate that higher-lying vector meson excitations must be included to obtain a complete picture of dielectron production from hot hadronic matter for masses between mφ and mJ/ψ. The study enables more accurate modeling of dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the mass range 1-2.5 GeV, and emphasizes the importance of constraining hadronic form factors with experimental data from e+e- annihilation into hadrons.
