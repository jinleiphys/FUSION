# Phase transition on superfluid vortices in Higgs-Confinement crossover

> We propose a novel method to distinguish states of matter by identifying spontaneous symmetry breaking on extended objects, such as vortices, even in the absence of a bulk phase transition. As a specific example, we investigate the phase transition on superfluid vortices in the Higgs-confinement crossover using a $\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{gauge} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{global}$ model. This model exhibits superfluidity of $\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{global}$ symmetry and allows for a crossover between the Higgs and confinement regimes by varying the gauge coupling constant from weak to strong. We demonstrate that, on vortices, spontaneous breaking of the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ flavor symmetry occurs in the weak coupling (Higgs) regime, while it does not in the strong coupling (confinement) regime. We also confirm that those regimes are separated by a second-order phase transition through Monte Carlo simulations, whose universality class corresponds to the two-dimensional Ising model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spontaneous breaking of the \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) flavor symmetry occurs on superfluid vortices in the weak-coupling (Higgs) regime of a \(\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{gauge} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{global}\) model, but not in the strong-coupling (confinement) regime, even though the bulk exhibits no phase transition between these regimes. The authors confirm that these two regimes on the vortex are separated by a second-order phase transition whose universality class matches the two-dimensional Ising model.

## Method

The authors study a \((3+1)\)-dimensional lattice gauge theory with two \(\mathrm{U}(1)\)-valued Higgs fields and a \(\mathrm{U}(1)\) gauge field, using an action with hopping parameter \(\beta_H\) and gauge coupling \(\beta_g\) (where small \(\beta_g\) is strong coupling and large \(\beta_g\) is weak coupling). They insert a \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) flavor symmetry defect on a plane perpendicular to the vortex axis and analytically evaluate the ratio of partition functions with and without the defect in both the strong-coupling limit (\(\beta_g = 0\)) and weak-coupling regime (\(\beta_g \gg 1\)) to determine whether the symmetry is spontaneously broken. They then perform Monte Carlo simulations on lattices up to \(32^4\) with an external field that fixes a vortex and an anti-vortex, computing the correlation function of the magnetic flux \(\Gamma\) (the logarithm of a Wilson loop encircling the vortex) to detect long-range order and extract the susceptibility.

## Key numbers

* The lattice volumes used in Monte Carlo simulations are \(V = 16^4, 20^4, 24^4, 28^4,\) and \(32^4\).
* The hopping parameter is fixed at \(\beta_H = 2.0\) throughout the simulations.
* The external field \(a_\mu\) induces a flux of \(\pi\) at \((x_1,x_2) = (N_1/2 - 1, N_2/2 - 1)\) and a flux of \(-\pi\) at \((x_1,x_2) = (N_1 - 1, N_2/2 - 1)\) to create the vortex and anti-vortex.
* The correlation function \(C(l)\) of the magnetic flux \(\Gamma\) approaches nonzero values at long distance above a critical coupling constant, indicating long-range order.

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Motrunich and Senthil (2005) that a phase transition on vortices in this model should occur with the two-dimensional Ising universality class, providing both analytic and numerical confirmation. The results enable a new method for distinguishing states of matter based on spontaneous symmetry breaking on extended objects (vortices) even when the bulk exhibits no phase transition, which is relevant to the quark-hadron continuity scenario in dense QCD where the color-flavor locked phase and hadronic superfluid phase are connected by a crossover. The approach contrasts with earlier studies by Cherman (2020), Hidaka (2022), and Hayashi (2023) that used generalized global symmetries and topological phases to study Higgs-confinement continuity.
