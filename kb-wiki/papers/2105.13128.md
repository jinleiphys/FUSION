# Effect of non-uniform efficiency on higher-order cumulants in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform a systematic study on the effect of non-uniform track efficiency correction in higher-order cumulant analysis in heavy-ion collisions. Through analytical derivation, we find that the true values of cumulants can be successfully reproduced by the efficiency correction with an average of the realistic detector efficiency for particles with the same charges within each single phase space. The theoretical conclusions are supported by a toy model simulation by tuning the non-uniformity of the efficiency employed in the track-by-track efficiency correction method. The valid averaged efficiency is found to suppress the statistical uncertainties of the reproduced cumulants dramatically. Thus, usage of the averaged efficiency requires a careful study of phase space dependence. This study is important for carrying out precision measurements of higher-order cumulants in heavy-ion collision experiments at present and in future.

## Key claim

The true values of higher-order cumulants can be successfully reproduced by efficiency correction using an average of the realistic detector efficiency taken within each single phase space (internally averaged efficiency), but only when the average is taken for particles with the same charges; externally averaged efficiency across multiple phase spaces or non-averaged efficiency with the same mean value cannot reproduce cumulants beyond first order.

## Method

The authors performed an analytical derivation using generating functions and factorial cumulants to relate true and measured cumulants under binomial efficiency losses, then tested the validity of three types of efficiency correction (internally averaged, externally averaged, and non-averaged) by substituting the efficiency parameters in the correction formulas. They supported the analytical results with a toy Monte Carlo simulation of 10^7 events producing positively and negatively charged particles from independent Poisson distributions, with track efficiency modeled as a product of a transverse-momentum-dependent function and an azimuthal-angle-dependent function with tunable non-uniformity, and compared cumulants up to fourth order corrected using six different efficiency sets.

## Key numbers

- Toy model uses 10^7 events with positively charged particles following Poisson(λ1=12) and negatively charged particles following Poisson(λ2=8)
- Theoretical cumulants for net-charge: C1 = C3 = λ1 - λ2 = 4, C2 = C4 = λ1 + λ2 = 20
- Six efficiency sets defined: ε0 through ε5, all with the same mean efficiency μ[ε_i] = μ[u(p_T)] for i = 0,...,5
- Efficiency function u(p_T) uses parameters (p0,p1,p2) = (0.7, 0.4, 4.8) for positive and (0.6, 0.4, 4.2) for negative particles
- Azimuthal efficiency v(φ) uses parameters (p3,p4) = (0.4,3), (0.4,2), (0.2,3), (0.2,2) for ε0 through ε3 respectively, with 12 dips in φ corresponding to TPC sector boundaries

## Context

This work extends the track-by-track efficiency correction method developed by Luo (2018) for higher-order cumulant analysis, and provides theoretical justification for the numerical observations reported by Nonaka (2017) on averaged efficiency corrections. The study is directly relevant to the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (BES-I and BES-II), where precision measurements of fourth-order net-proton fluctuations (κσ²) showed a 3.1σ non-monotonic signal, and enables proper handling of non-uniform detector efficiency in future heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and other facilities.
