# MSW effect with quark matter: Neutron Star as a case study

> With the recent findings from various astrophysical results hinting towards possible existence of strange quark matters with the baryonic resonances such as $Λ^0, Σ^0, Ξ, Ω$ in the core of neutron stars, we investigate the MSW effect, in general, in quark matter. We find that the resonance condition for the complete conversion of down-quark to strange quark requires estremely large matter density ($ρ_u \simeq 10^{5}\,\mbox{fm}^{-3} $). Nonetheless the neutron stars provide a best condition for the conversion to be statistically significant which is of the same order as is expected from imposing charge neutrality condition. This has a possibility of resolving the hyperon puzzle as well as the equation of state for dense baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect, previously applied to neutrinos, can also cause flavor conversion of down quarks to strange quarks in dense quark matter; however, the resonance condition for complete conversion requires an extremely large up-quark number density of about \(10^{5}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\), which is not reached in neutron stars, so only a statistically significant but partial conversion (about 2 percent) is predicted.

## Method

The authors model two-generation quark flavor oscillation between down and strange quarks using a Schrodinger equation, with a vacuum Hamiltonian defined by the Cabibbo mixing angle \(\theta_C\) and the mass-squared difference \(\Delta m^2_{ds}\). They introduce a matter potential \(V^u_{cc} = \sqrt{2} G_F \rho_u\) arising from charged-current interactions with an up-quark background, which modifies the effective Hamiltonian and the in-medium mixing angle \(\theta^M_C\). The resonance condition and conversion probability are then evaluated for parameters typical of neutron star cores, using the up-quark number density \(\rho_u\) and down-quark energy \(E_d\) expressed in terms of nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0\) and Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- The resonance condition for down-to-strange quark conversion requires an up-quark number density \(\rho_u \simeq 10^{5}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- For a neutron star with representative parameters \(X \simeq 0.5\) and \(Y \simeq 5\) (where \(\rho_u = Y \rho_0\) and \(\rho_0 = 0.16\,\text{fm}^{-3}\)), the conversion probability is \(P^M(d \rightarrow s) \simeq 0.02\) (about 2 percent).
- The beta-equilibrium condition in neutron star matter gives \(\rho_d / \rho_s = 1 + 0.05\), implying about 5 percent strange quark number density relative to down-quark number density, using current quark masses \(m_d \simeq 5\) MeV, \(m_s \simeq 95\) MeV, and \(\mu_q^2 \simeq 500\,\text{MeV}^2\).
- In vacuum, for a down quark energy of 100 MeV, the oscillation length \(L_{\text{osc}}/2\) is about \(4\pi\) fermi, giving a maximal conversion probability of \(\sin^2(2\theta_C) \simeq 0.184\).

## Context

This work extends the MSW mechanism, originally developed for neutrino flavor conversion in matter by Wolfenstein (1978) and Mikheyev and Smirnov (1985), to the quark sector for the first time. It is motivated by recent astrophysical calculations suggesting the possible existence of strange quark matter or hyperons in neutron star cores, as discussed by Annala (2019) and Vidana (2018). The predicted 2 percent conversion of down quarks to strange quarks via MSW resonance is comparable to the strange quark abundance expected from beta-equilibrium conditions, and the authors suggest this could provide an additional source of strange quarks, potentially influencing the equation of state at high densities and offering a resolution to the hyperon puzzle for high-mass neutron stars.
