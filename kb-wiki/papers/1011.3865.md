# Nuclear symmetry energy at subnormal densities from measured nuclear masses

> The symmetry energy coefficients for nuclei with mass number A=20~250 are extracted from more than 2000 measured nuclear masses. With the semi-empirical connection between the symmetry energy coefficients of finite nuclei and the nuclear symmetry energy at reference densities, we investigate the density dependence of symmetry energy of nuclear matter at subnormal densities. The obtained results are compared with those extracted from other methods.

# Nuclear symmetry energy at subnormal densities from measured nuclear masses

## Key claim

The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at subnormal densities (0.42 ρ₀ to 0.64 ρ₀) is constrained by extracting symmetry energy coefficients from over 2000 measured nuclear masses and applying a semi-empirical relation linking finite-nuclei coefficients to nuclear matter symmetry energy at a reference density; the extracted range γ = 0.7 ± 0.1 for the power-law exponent is independent of the volume symmetry energy coefficient S₀.

## Method

The symmetry energy coefficients a_sym(A) for nuclei with mass number A = 20–250 were extracted by performing two-parameter parabola fits to the isospin-asymmetry dependence of the nuclear binding energy per particle (after subtracting Coulomb and Wigner terms) using the AME2003 mass table. The resulting a_sym(A) values were fitted to the form a_sym(A) = S₀(1 + κ A^{-1/3})⁻¹ to obtain the volume symmetry energy coefficient S₀ and the surface-to-volume ratio κ. Using the relation e_sym(ρ_A) = a_sym(A) proposed by Centelles et al. (2009) and the known reference density for ²⁰⁸Pb (ρ₂₀₈ ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³), the exponent γ in e_sym(ρ) = S₀(ρ/ρ₀)^γ was determined from κ and the reference density.

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry energy coefficient: S₀ = 31.1 ± 1.7 MeV (95% confidence interval), from fitting a_sym(A) for A = 20–250.
- Surface-to-volume ratio: κ = 2.31 ± 0.38 (95% confidence interval).
- Power-law exponent for symmetry energy density dependence: γ = 0.7 ± 0.1, derived from κ and the ²⁰⁸Pb reference density.
- Reference density for ²⁰⁸Pb: ρ₂₀₈ ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³, taken from Centelles et al. (2009) and Tripathi et al. (2008).
- Density range covered by nuclei with A = 20–250: 0.42 ρ₀ ≤ ρ ≤ 0.64 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Slope parameter L at normal density: 53 ≲ L ≲ 79 MeV, based on S₀ = 31.1 ± 1.7 MeV and γ = 0.7 ± 0.1.
- For S₀ = 31.1 MeV specifically: L = 65.3 ± 9.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Centelles et al. (2009), who proposed the relation e_sym(ρ_A) = a_sym(A) and demonstrated it for ²⁰⁸Pb, by applying it systematically across a wide mass range using experimental masses. The extracted symmetry energy at subnormal densities is consistent with constraints from isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions (Tsang et al., 2009; Li et al., 2005), the pygmy dipole resonance (Klimkiewicz et al., 2007; Carbone et al., 2010), neutron skin thickness (Warda et al., 2009), and giant dipole resonance data (Tripathi et al., 2008). The results enable further refinement of the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the slope parameter L, which are critical inputs for modeling neutron stars and nuclear reactions.
