# Hybrid Hadronization

> We discuss Hybrid Hadronization, a hadronization model which interpolates between string fragmentation in dilute parton systems and quark recombination in dense parton systems. We lay out the basic principles, discuss some details of the implementation, and show some prelimiary results. Hybrid Hadronization is realized as a software package which works with PYTHIA 8 and will be released publicly in the near future.

## Key claim

Hybrid Hadronization is a hadronization model that interpolates between Lund string fragmentation in dilute parton systems and quark recombination in dense parton systems, with the goal of describing both vacuum and in-medium hadronization consistently, though the authors note that no tuning to data has been carried out and results are preliminary.

## Method

The model processes partons from Monte Carlo generators (e.g., PYTHIA 8, MATTER) and optionally adds thermal partons from fluid dynamic simulations. It evaluates all quark-antiquark pairs and quark triplets for recombination probability using a Wigner formalism with Gaussian wave packets, where the probability depends on phase-space separation and hadron wave function widths fitted to measured charge radii. Partons that fail to recombine form QCD strings, which are repaired for holes left by recombined partons and then handed to PYTHIA 8 for fragmentation; gluons are always split into quark-antiquark pairs before processing.

## Key numbers

- In p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, deviations between Hybrid Hadronization and pure PYTHIA 8 string fragmentation for charged hadron yields are generally less than 2% across rapidity y, for initial momentum transfers hat p_T of 25 GeV, 100 GeV, 500 GeV, and 1 TeV.
- The proton/pion ratio in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV shows enhanced baryon production from thermal-shower recombination, compared to p+p collisions at the same energy; results below P_T = 2 GeV/c are not shown due to missing thermal hadrons and underlying event.

## Context

This work extends the instantaneous recombination model of Fries (2008) and the Lund string fragmentation model of Andersson (1983), which are the two most successful hadronization approaches for dilute and dense systems respectively. The Hybrid Hadronization code is part of the JETSCAPE collaboration effort (Kauder, 2018) and builds on the recombination probability formalism first proposed by Han (2016). It enables consistent hadronization modeling across p+p, p+A, and A+A collision systems, and will be publicly released in a future JETSCAPE version.
