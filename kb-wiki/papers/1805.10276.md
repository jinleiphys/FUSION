# Quenching of nuclear matrix elements for $0νββ$ decay by chiral two-body currents

> We examine the leading effects of two-body weak currents from chiral effective field theory on the matrix elements governing neutrinoless double-beta decay. In the closure approximation these effects are generated by the product of a one-body current with a two-body current, yielding both two- and three-body operators. When the three-body operators are considered without approximation, they quench matrix elements by about 10%, less than suggested by prior work, which neglected portions of the operators. The two-body operators, when treated in the standard way, can produce much larger quenching. In a consistent effective field theory, however, these large effects become divergent and must be renormalized by a contact operator, the coefficient of which we cannot determine at present.

## Key claim

The leading three-body operators from chiral two-body currents quench neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements by about 10%, less than suggested by prior work that neglected portions of the operators; however, the two-body operators produce much larger quenching that is divergent and must be renormalized by a contact operator with an unknown coefficient, so the net effect within consistent effective field theory remains uncertain.

## Method

The authors constructed the explicit product of one-body and two-body chiral weak currents in the closure approximation, yielding both two- and three-body operators for neutrinoless double-beta decay. They evaluated these operators in symmetric nuclear matter and then in realistic shell-model-like calculations using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to approximate wave functions for ⁷⁶Ge/⁷⁶Se and ⁴⁸Ca/⁴⁸Ti, computing three-body matrix elements in a Jacobi basis and three-body transition densities from GCM states.

## Key numbers

- Three-body operators quench matrix elements by about 10% (5-10% range) for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁴⁸Ca when all contractions are included, with the exact value depending on the choice of low-energy constants c₃, c₄, and c_D
- The leading one-body-only matrix element M⁰ for ⁷⁶Ge is 3.47 (compared to an exact result of 2.81 from Javier (2009))
- Two-body operators can quench the matrix element by up to more than 90% in ⁷⁶Ge and 60% in ⁴⁸Ca, but the quenching is very sensitive to c₃, c₄, and c_D, and can be as small as 10% in ⁷⁶Ge
- In nuclear matter, the net quenching from two-body operators is about 5/2 times the quenching from the diagrams considered in Javier (2011), using a cutoff Λ = 3k_F

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Javier (2011) and Engel (2014), who used normal-ordering with respect to symmetric nuclear matter to obtain effective density-dependent one-body currents that quenched matrix elements by roughly 30%. The present paper shows that the three-body operators produce less quenching than those prior effective-operator treatments suggested, but that the two-body operators introduce divergences requiring renormalization by contact operators with unknown coefficients, as also noted in the chiral EFT framework of Cirigliano (2017, 2018). The results enable future calculations that can discard three-body operators in favor of normal-ordered two-body pieces, but the overall quenching from two-body currents within consistent EFT remains to be determined, potentially requiring lattice QCD input.
