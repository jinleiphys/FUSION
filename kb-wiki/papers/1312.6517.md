# Multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock calculations of atomic electric dipole moments of 225^Ra, 199^Hg, and 171^Yb

> The multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method has been employed to calculate atomic electric dipole moments (EDM) of 225^Ra, 199^Hg, and 171^Yb. For the calculations of the matrix elements we extended the relativistic atomic structure package GRASP2K. The extension includes programs to evaluate matrix elements of (P, T)-odd e-N tensor-pseudotensor and pseudoscalar-scalar interactions, the atomic electric dipole interaction, the nuclear Schiff moment, and the interaction of the electron electric dipole moment with nuclear magnetic moments. The interelectronic interactions were accounted for through valence and core-valence electron correlation effects. The electron shell relaxation was included with separately optimised wave functions of opposite parities.

## Key claim

The multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method, extended within the GRASP2K package, can calculate atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) for diamagnetic atoms with closed outer s shells, but the accuracy depends on the choice of variational energy functional (Extended Optimal Level versus Extended Average Level), the use of orthogonal versus non-orthogonal orbital sets, and the number of virtual orbital layers included for electron correlation.

## Method

The authors extended the GRASP2K relativistic atomic structure package to evaluate matrix elements of (P,T)-odd electron-nucleon tensor-pseudotensor and pseudoscalar-scalar interactions, the atomic electric dipole interaction, the nuclear Schiff moment, and the interaction of the electron electric dipole moment with nuclear magnetic moments. They generated atomic wave functions for 225Ra, 199Hg, and 171Yb using the MCDHF method with separately optimised wave functions of opposite parities, including valence and core-valence electron correlation effects through single and restricted double electron substitutions from core-valence to virtual orbitals, with three or four layers of virtual orbitals of s, p, d, f, g symmetries.

## Key numbers

- For 225Ra with non-orthogonal orbital sets and three virtual orbital layers: TPT contribution to atomic EDM = -20.68 (arbitrary units), PSS contribution = -75.83, SM contribution = -8.59, eEDM contribution = -60.13
- For 225Ra with orthogonal orbital sets and three virtual orbital layers: TPT contribution = -19.9, PSS contribution = -70.3, SM contribution = -8.27, eEDM contribution = -66.9
- The difference between orthogonal and non-orthogonal calculations for 225Ra eEDM contribution reached up to 11% (from -66.9 to -60.13)
- For 225Ra with Dirac-Fock (uncorrelated) wave functions: TPT = -15.81, PSS = -57.87, SM = -6.32, eEDM = -46.67 (non-orthogonal)
- The innermost core orbitals 1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 3d of radium were kept closed for electron substitutions; for Hg and Yb, innermost core orbitals 1s, 2s, 2p were kept closed

## Context

This work extends the MCDHF methodology previously applied only to the Schiff moment in radium (Bieron 2009) to now include tensor-pseudotensor, pseudoscalar-scalar, and electron EDM interactions for three diamagnetic atoms. The calculations provide independent atomic EDM results for 225Ra, 199Hg, and 171Yb that can be compared with other theoretical approaches such as the Dirac-Hartree-Fock and random-phase approximation methods of Dzuba and Flambaum (2009) and Martensson (1987). The extended GRASP2K package enables future calculations for closed-p-valence-shell atoms and arbitrary open-shell systems, including lanthanides and actinides, which is relevant for interpreting experimental EDM searches that constrain extensions to the standard model of particle physics.
