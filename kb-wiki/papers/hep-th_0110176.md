# Renormalized Path Integral for the Two-Dimensional Delta-Function Interaction

> A path-integral approach for delta-function potentials is presented. Particular attention is paid to the two-dimensional case, which illustrates the realization of a quantum anomaly for a scale invariant problem in quantum mechanics. Our treatment is based on an infinite summation of perturbation theory that captures the nonperturbative nature of the delta-function bound state. The well-known singular character of the two-dimensional delta-function potential is dealt with by considering the renormalized path integral resulting from a variety of schemes: dimensional, momentum-cutoff, and real-space regularization. Moreover, compatibility of the bound-state and scattering sectors is shown.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a path-integral approach using infinite summations of perturbation theory, combined with renormalization, can reproduce the known bound-state and scattering properties of the two-dimensional delta-function potential, including the quantum anomaly that breaks the classical scale invariance of the problem.

## Method

The authors start from the path-integral representation of the quantum-mechanical propagator and derive an infinite perturbative series for the energy Green's function of a delta-function potential. They sum this geometric series exactly to obtain a closed-form expression for the full Green's function, from which bound-state poles and scattering observables are extracted. The singular two-dimensional case is regularized using three distinct schemes: dimensional regularization (extending the spatial dimension to D = 2 - epsilon), momentum-cutoff regularization (introducing a cutoff Lambda in momentum-space integrals), and real-space regularization (replacing the point delta function with a circular delta function at radius a).

## Key numbers

* The one-dimensional delta-function bound-state energy is E = - (hbar lambda/2)^2 / (2M), with ground-state wave function Psi(x) = sqrt(kappa) exp(-kappa |x|) and kappa = lambda/2.
* In dimensional regularization, the renormalized running coupling is lambda(epsilon) = 2 pi epsilon {1 + (epsilon/2)[g^(0) - (ln 4 pi - gamma)]}, where gamma is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
* The ground-state energy from dimensional regularization is E_(gs) = - (hbar^2 mu^2)/(2M) exp(g^(0)), where mu is the renormalization scale and g^(0) is a finite subtraction constant.
* The momentum-cutoff regularization yields the bound-state condition (lambda/4 pi) ln( (hbar^2 Lambda^2)/(2M|E|) + 1 ) = 1, leading to E_(gs) ~ - (hbar^2 Lambda^2)/(2M) exp(-4 pi/lambda).
* The Callan-Symanzik beta function from momentum-cutoff regularization is beta(lambda) = Lambda d lambda/d Lambda ~ - lambda^2/(2 pi).
* In real-space regularization, the s-wave (l=0) bound-state energy is E_(gs) = - (hbar^2/(2M)) (4 exp(-2 gamma)/a^2) exp(-4 pi/lambda), where a is the real-space cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous path-integral renormalization program for singular potentials (Camblong and Ordonez, 2001), which had already been applied to the inverse square potential. The two-dimensional delta-function potential provides a simpler example of a quantum anomaly than the inverse square potential, magnetic monopole, or magnetic vortex cases. The paper unifies several earlier renormalization approaches to this problem, including momentum-space regularization (Thorn, 1979; Huang, 1992; Jackiw, 1991), real-space regularization (Mandelzweig, 1994; Gosdzinsky and Tarrach, 1991), and dimensional regularization (Camblong and Ordonez, 1999), while demonstrating compatibility between the bound-state and scattering sectors.
