# Electron versus muon neutrino induced cross sections in charged current quasi-elastic processes

> Differences between $ν_e$ and $ν_μ$ quasielastic cross sections are essential in neutrino oscillation analyses and CP violation searches for experiments such as DUNE and T2HK. The ratio of these is however poorly known experimentally and for certain kinematic regions theoretical models give contradictory answers. We use two independent mean-field based models to investigate this ratio using $^{40}$Ar and $^{12}$C targets. We demonstrate that a proper treatment of the final nucleon's wave function confirms the dominance of $ν_μ$ over $ν_e$ induced cross sections at forward lepton scattering.

# Electron versus muon neutrino induced cross sections in charged current quasi-elastic processes

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that muon neutrino induced charged-current quasi-elastic cross sections can be larger than electron neutrino induced cross sections at forward lepton scattering angles, contrary to the expectation that the lighter lepton would produce larger cross sections. This result holds only when the final-state nucleon wave function is treated with proper distortion from the nuclear potential rather than approximated as a plane wave.

## Method
Two independent mean-field based models were used: a non-relativistic Hartree-Fock continuum random phase approximation (HF-CRPA) model with Skyrme interactions, and a relativistic mean field (RMF) model solving the Dirac equation with meson-field interactions. Both models compute the outgoing nucleon wave function in the same nuclear potential as the bound states, ensuring orthogonality of initial and final states. The calculations were performed for 12C and 40Ar targets, and compared with plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) approaches to isolate the effect of final-state distortion.

## Key numbers
- For 12C at an incoming neutrino energy of 200 MeV and lepton scattering angle of 5 degrees, the CRPA and RMF models both show muon neutrino cross sections larger than electron neutrino cross sections (Fig. 1)
- The momentum transfer q for forward scattering is given by q = sqrt(E_nu^2 + P_l^2 - 2 cos(theta_l) E_nu P_l), which increases with lepton mass; for fixed energy transfer omega, muon neutrinos produce larger q than electron neutrinos
- The oscillated nu_mu signal at T2K peaks around 300 MeV
- The HF-PWIA cross sections at forward angles were scaled down in some panels of Fig. 2 to be visible alongside the full HF results, indicating the PWIA overestimates cross sections by a large factor at low momentum transfers
- The ratio sigma_nu_e / sigma_nu_mu is smaller than 1 over a large part of the forward scattering region for a broad range of incoming energies (Fig. 5)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous finding in Martini and Jachowicz (2018) that muon neutrino cross sections can dominate at forward angles, which had been questioned by Ankowski (2016) as a possible artifact of incomplete phase space treatment. The paper resolves this debate by showing that proper treatment of final-state nucleon distortion, including Pauli blocking through orthogonal wave functions, confirms the muon neutrino dominance. The result is relevant for interpreting the low-energy electron-like excess in MiniBooNE and for CP violation searches in DUNE and T2HK, where forward scattering events dominate near detector rates. The paper also shows that plane wave approaches (RPWIA, HF-PWIA) incorrectly predict electron neutrino dominance because they miss the strong reduction of cross sections for slow nucleons that occurs when orthogonality between initial and final states is enforced.
