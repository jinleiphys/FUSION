# Double Delta Production in the gamma d ---> p n pi+ pi- Reaction

> We have studied the gamma d ---> Delta++ Delta- reaction which requires the collaboration of the two nucleons in deuteron. By means of a model previously developed for the gamma p ----> p pi+ pi- reaction, the two body exchange currents leading to double delta creation are derived. A fair agreement is obtained with a recent experiment, but more precise measurements and the extension to higher photon energies look advisable in order to see the limits of the present theoretical approach

# Double Delta Production in the γ d → p n π⁺ π⁻ Reaction

## Key claim
The reaction γ d → Δ⁺⁺ Δ⁻ is a genuine two-body process that can be modeled using meson exchange currents derived from the γ p → p π⁺ π⁻ reaction, and the model reproduces experimental cross sections fairly well at low photon energies but shows discrepancies at higher energies, suggesting the need for more precise measurements to determine the limits of the theoretical approach.

## Method
The authors derived two-body meson exchange current mechanisms for the γ d → Δ⁺⁺ Δ⁻ reaction by selecting dominant diagrams from a previously developed model of γ p → p π⁺ π⁻ where the final π N system comes from Δ decay, then allowing the second pion to be absorbed by the second nucleon to excite another Δ. The model includes contributions from the N*(1520) resonance via substitution in the Kroll-Ruderman terms, and uses the deuteron s-wave function with a monopole form factor (Λ = 1.3 GeV) for the π N Δ vertex. The cross section was calculated by integrating over the four-momenta of the two Δ particles with their finite widths included through the imaginary part of their propagators.

## Key numbers
- The cross section changes by less than 5% when using Λ = 1000 GeV instead of Λ = 1.3 GeV for the monopole form factor, across the entire energy range studied.
- Diagram 1b changes the cross section at the level of 10% when added to the dominant terms 1a + 1c (decreasing σ below E_γ = 740 MeV and increasing it above this energy).
- The N*(1520) resonance produces constructive interference below E_γ ≈ 820 MeV and destructive interference above this energy.
- The cross section stops increasing around E_γ = 1060 MeV and begins decreasing smoothly at higher energies.
- Coupling constants used: f̃_{N'* Δ π} = 0.677; for N* → γ p: g̃_γ = 0.108, g̃_σ = -0.049; for N* → γ n: g̃_γ = -0.129, g̃_σ = 0.00731.

## Context
This work extends the model of Gomez Tejedor and Oset (1994) for γ p → p π⁺ π⁻ to the deuteron system, where two-nucleon mechanisms are the leading term rather than corrections to impulse approximation. The reaction provides a test of meson exchange current ideas that have been applied to deuteron photodisintegration and electron scattering. The results support current microscopic approaches for photon absorption in nuclei and suggest that more accurate data and extension to higher energies would help determine the limits of the present theoretical method, particularly as the model for γ p → π⁺ π⁻ p begins showing discrepancies with data above E_γ > 800 MeV.
