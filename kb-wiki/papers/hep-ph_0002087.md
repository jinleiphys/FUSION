# Finite pion width effects on the rho-meson and di-lepton spectra

> Within a field theoretical model where all damping width effects are treated self-consistently we study the changes of the spectral properties of rho-mesons due to the finite damping width of the pions in dense hadronic matter at finite temperature. The corresponding effects in the di-lepton yields are presented. Some problems concerning the self consistent treatment of vector or gauge bosons are discussed.

## Key claim

The finite damping width of pions in dense hadronic matter at finite temperature adds significant strength to the di-lepton mass spectrum below 500 MeV, essentially eliminating the dip in that low-mass region even for a moderate pion width of 50 MeV, contrary to treatments using vacuum spectral functions.

## Method

The authors use a field-theoretical model with a mesonic Lagrangian (pions, rho-mesons, and a1-mesons) and apply the self-consistent Phi-functional method of Baym, truncating to two-point diagrams. They solve the coupled Dyson equations for the pion, rho-meson, and a1-meson propagators at a temperature of 110 MeV, dropping real parts of self-energies and setting temporal components of the rho polarization tensor to zero to circumvent gauge-invariance problems. Three cases are compared: perturbative (vacuum pions), and two self-consistent cases with moderate (50 MeV) and strong (125 MeV) pion damping widths tuned via a four-pion interaction.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: T = 110 MeV, chosen as appropriate for CERES data.
- Vacuum rho-meson mass: 770 MeV, with vacuum width Gamma_rho = 150 MeV.
- Vacuum a1-meson mass: 1200 MeV, with vacuum width Gamma_a1 = 400 MeV.
- Pion damping widths studied: moderate case Gamma_pi = 50 MeV, strong case Gamma_pi = 125 MeV around the peak in the spectral function.
- Pion momentum for displayed spectral functions: 150 MeV/c.
- The di-lepton mass region below 500 MeV shows significant enhancement from finite pion width, with the dip essentially disappearing already for the moderate 50 MeV pion width.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium vector meson properties relevant to di-lepton measurements by the CERES and DLS collaborations, which show interesting features in the low lepton pair mass spectrum between 300 and 600 MeV. Previous theoretical investigations had mostly ignored the damping width of asymptotically stable particles in dense matter. The self-consistent treatment here consistently accounts for both pi-pi annihilation and bremsstrahlung contributions, including Landau-Pomeranchuk suppression as discussed in Knoll (1996), and includes the in-medium pion electromagnetic form-factor for the bremsstrahlung part. The paper also discusses fundamental problems with maintaining gauge invariance in self-consistent treatments of vector bosons, noting that a fully satisfactory scheme remains an open problem, with numerical implementation of vertex corrections in progress.
