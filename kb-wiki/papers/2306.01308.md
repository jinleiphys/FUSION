# Self-consistent many-body approach to the electroproduction of hypernuclei

> The electroproduction of selected $p$- and $sd$-shell hypernuclei was studied within a many-body approach using realistic interactions between the constituent baryons. The cross sections were computed in distorted-wave impulse approximation using two elementary amplitudes for the electroproduction of the $Λ$ hyperon. The structure of the hypernuclei was investigated within the framework of the self-consistent $Λ$-nucleon Tamm-Dancoff approach and its extension known as the $Λ$-nucleon equation of motion phonon method. Use was made of the NNLOsat chiral potential plus the effective Nijmegen-F YN interaction. The method was first implemented on light nuclei for studying the available experimental data and establishing a relation to other approaches. After this proof test, it was adopted for predicting the electroproduction cross section of the hypernuclei $^{40}_{~Λ}$K and $^{48}_{~Λ}$K in view of the E12-15-008 experiment in preparation at JLab. On the ground of these predictions, appreciable effects on the spectra are expected to be induced by the YN interaction.

## Key claim

The self-consistent many-body approach, using the NNLOsat chiral potential plus the Nijmegen-F YN interaction, predicts appreciable effects on hypernuclear spectra induced by the YN interaction, but the agreement with experimental data is only partial and depends sensitively on the Fermi momentum parameter kF in the YN potential; the method requires further elaboration, such as including two-phonon configurations, to fully reproduce observed strengths and energy distributions.

## Method

The cross sections for electroproduction of hypernuclei were computed in the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) using two elementary amplitudes (BS3 and SLA) in the optimum on-shell approximation. The hypernuclear structure was described within the self-consistent Λ-nucleon Tamm-Dancoff approach (TDΛ) and its extension, the Λ-nucleon equation of motion phonon method (EMPMΛ), which couples Λ-nucleon particle-hole states to more complex nuclear core excitations. A Hartree-Fock basis was generated from the NNLOsat chiral potential (including NN and NNN interactions) plus the effective Nijmegen-F YN interaction (NF YNG) with various Fermi momenta kF.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of Λ in 12ΛB: TDΛ gives BΛ(TD) = 10.37 MeV, EMPMΛ gives BΛ(EM) = 12.88 MeV, compared to experimental BΛ(exp) = 11.37 ± 0.06 MeV.
- Binding energy of Λ in 16ΛN: TDΛ gives BΛ(TD) = 12.93 MeV, EMPMΛ gives BΛ(EM) = 17.19 MeV, compared to experimental BΛ(exp) = 13.76 ± 0.16 MeV.
- Binding energy of Λ in 28ΛAl: TDΛ gives BΛ(TD) = 15.58 MeV, EMPMΛ gives BΛ(EM) = 16.62 MeV, compared to empirical value for 28ΛSi of BΛ(exp) ≈ 17.2 MeV.
- Binding energy of Λ in 40ΛK: TDΛ gives BΛ(TD) = 18.62 MeV, EMPMΛ gives BΛ(EM) = 19.97 MeV, compared to a value of 18.70 MeV from a Λ-nucleus optical potential.
- Fermi momentum kF for 28ΛAl estimated via Thomas-Fermi approximation: kF = 1.24 fm−1.
- For 40ΛK, the first excited state 2+ is shifted up to 0.269 MeV in EMPMΛ relative to TDΛ; strongly populated states at 3.42 and 8.57 MeV in TDΛ are shifted by approximately 0.6 MeV in EMPMΛ.
- The FWHM used for smoothing peaks in 12ΛB was 820 keV; for 16ΛN it was 1177 keV; for 40ΛK predictions it was 800 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hypernuclear electroproduction using phenomenological shell models (Bydzovsky et al., 2018, 2019; Fermi, 2020) by introducing a self-consistent many-body approach with realistic chiral and YN interactions. It builds on the Tamm-Dancoff method for hypernuclei developed by Vesely et al. (2020) and the equation of motion phonon method (EMPM) applied to nuclear structure by Bianco et al. (2012). The predictions for 40ΛK and 48ΛK are made in view of the planned E12-15-008 experiment at JLab, and the results are compared with shell-model calculations by Motoba et al. (2012) and Cohen (1980). The approach enables parameter-free predictions but reveals the need for including two-phonon configurations to improve agreement with experimental data for light hypernuclei.
