# Fluctuating relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics as a gauge theory

> We argue that different formulations of hydrodynamics are related to uncertainties in the definitions of local thermodynamic and hydrodynamic variables. We show that this ambiguity can be resolved by viewing different formulations of hydrodynamics as particular "gauge choices" which lead to the same physical behavior of the system. Using the example of bulk viscosity, we show that Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) and Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics are particular "gauge choices" of this type, related by a well-defined transformation of thermodynamic and hydrodynamic variables. We argue that this gauge ambiguity is necessary to ascertain the causality of stochastic hydrodynamic evolution and conjecture that it could explain the applicability of hydrodynamics outside its expected regime of validity since far from equilibrium and close to equilibrium may be related through transformations of this type.

## Key claim
The paper argues that different formulations of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, such as Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) and Israel-Stewart theories, are related by a "gauge symmetry" corresponding to ambiguities in defining local thermodynamic and hydrodynamic variables; this gauge ambiguity is conjectured to be necessary for ensuring causality of stochastic hydrodynamic evolution and could explain why hydrodynamics remains applicable outside its expected regime of validity.

## Method
The authors develop a reparametrization symmetry framework where the fluid four-velocity, energy density, pressure, heat current, and shear tensor are treated as unobservable gauge choices, with only the full energy-momentum tensor being physically meaningful. They demonstrate this explicitly for bulk viscous fluids by showing that BDNK and Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics can be related through a well-defined transformation of thermodynamic and hydrodynamic variables, using Zubarev hydrodynamics as the underlying statistical mechanical foundation. The analysis connects the gauge ambiguity to the choice of spacetime foliation in the Zubarev formalism and to the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers
- The paper contains no explicit numerical results; it is a theoretical framework paper establishing a gauge interpretation of hydrodynamic formulations.

## Context
This work extends the recent development of BDNK first-order hydrodynamics (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, Kovtun, 2017-2020) and contrasts it with traditional Israel-Stewart second-order hydrodynamics (Israel and Stewart, 1970s). The authors build on Zubarev's statistical mechanical approach to nonequilibrium systems (Zubarev, 1970s) and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem (Kadanoff and Martin, 1963). The paper enables a unified understanding of different hydrodynamic formulations and suggests that the gauge symmetry could explain the "unreasonable effectiveness of hydrodynamics" in small systems such as proton-proton collisions, where traditional Knudsen number arguments would predict failure.
