# The 0+-spectrum in rare earth nuclei within the pseudo-SU(3) shell model

> The study of the structure of the 0+ spectrum in heavy nuclei has drawn much attention in the last two decades. In this contribution we study their properties from a microscopic point of view. The pseudo-SU(3) model (\tilde{SU}(3)) is applied to some rare earth nuclei, namely to Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Yb and Hf isotopes. It is shown that the 0+ spectrum, and the accumulation of states at certain energies, can be well understood using this microscopic model, which takes into account the Pauli Exclusion Principle (PES). Intentionally, a very simple model Hamiltonian is applied and only the valence shell is taken into account, in order to high-lighten certain cross features. It is demonstrated that the microscopic Hilbert space is essential in understanding the accumulation of 0+-states. A discussion to other models is provided. Also the dominance of B(E2)-transitions from the γ-band over those from the \b{eta}-band turns out to be trivial, in contrast to within some collective models.

## Key claim

The pseudo-SU(3) shell model, using only a simple Hamiltonian with no symmetry breaking and only the valence shell, can reproduce the observed dense accumulation of 0+ states in rare earth nuclei because the degeneracy of SU(3) irreducible representations in the microscopic Hilbert space naturally produces groups of near-degenerate 0+ states, a feature that collective models cannot capture.

## Method

The authors applied the pseudo-SU(3) (tilde-SU(3)) model to Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Yb, and Hf isotopes, constructing separate proton and neutron model spaces from Nilsson diagrams and coupling them via a linear alignment prescription. A simple Hamiltonian was used containing only Casimir operators of tilde-SU(3) plus L-squared and K-squared terms, with parameters adjusted to reproduce the first 2+ and 6+ ground band states and the first 2+ and 4+ gamma-band states for each nucleus. The algebraic quadrupole operator was multiplied by an effective charge adjusted to the 2g+ to 0g+ transition.

## Key numbers

* For 150Sm, the (24,6) irrep is nine-fold degenerate, corresponding to the observed near-degeneracy of the 0+5-8 states in experiment.
* For 158Gd, the (30,4) irrep is two-fold degenerate (associated with 0+2 and 0+3) and the (24,10) irrep is eight-fold degenerate (associated with the accumulation of higher 0+ states).
* For 162Dy, the ground state and gamma-band belong to the (30,8) irrep while the beta-band belongs to the (32,4) irrep, with the degeneracy structure reproducing the observed 0+ state accumulation.
* All effective charges obtained from fitting the 2g+ to 0g+ transition were near the value 0.5.
* The model reproduces the 0+ spectrum less well near the neutron shell closure at N=82, as seen in 146Sm84.

## Context

This work extends the critique of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) by K.T. Hecht (1990s), who showed that the IBA ignores the Pauli Exclusion Principle by treating bosons as structureless, and by Draayer (book), who called this the "fatal flaw" of the IBA. The paper demonstrates that the pseudo-SU(3) model naturally explains the dominance of B(E2) transitions from the gamma-band over those from the beta-band, which the IBA can only achieve through symmetry breaking (as done by Ani et al., 2025). The results enable future work using the proxy-SU(3) model, which includes part of the intruder levels, and suggest that collective model interpretations of beta-bands and gamma-bands lose geometric meaning at higher energies.
