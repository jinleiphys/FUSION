# Scattering of decuplet baryons in chiral effective field theory

> A formalism for treating the scattering of decuplet baryons in chiral effective field theory is developed. The minimal Lagrangian and potentials in leading-order SU(3) chiral effective field theory for the interactions of octet baryons ($B$) and decuplet baryons ($D$) for the transitions $BB\to BB$, $BB\leftrightarrow DB$, $DB\to DB$, $BB\leftrightarrow DD$, $DB\leftrightarrow DD$, and $DD\to DD$ are provided. As an application of the formalism we compare with results from lattice QCD simulations for $ΩΩ$ and $NΩ$ scattering. Implications of our results pertinent to the quest for dibaryons are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors develop a leading-order SU(3) chiral effective field theory formalism for scattering involving decuplet baryons, but they restrict the analysis to leading order in the Weinberg counting and assume approximate SU(3) flavor symmetry for coupling strengths while incorporating SU(3) breaking through physical baryon and meson masses, meaning the results are qualitative rather than precise predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed the minimal Lagrangian and potentials at leading order in SU(3) chiral effective field theory for all transitions among octet baryons (B) and decuplet baryons (D): BB→BB, BB↔DB, DB→DB, BB↔DD, DB↔DD, and DD→DD. They derived the meson-exchange potentials from pseudoscalar meson (π, η, K) exchange and contact terms without derivatives, then solved a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain scattering amplitudes and bound states. The low-energy constants were fitted to lattice QCD results using the lattice simulation's baryon and meson masses, and physical-point results were obtained by substituting physical masses in the potential and propagators.

## Key numbers

- The weak pseudoscalar-meson decay constant used is f₀ = 93 MeV, with axial-vector strength g_A = 1.26 and α = F/(F+D) = 0.4.
- The coupling constant C for the octet-decuplet-meson Lagrangian is fixed from Δ→πN decay as C = 3g_A/4, yielding f_BD = √2 C/f₀.
- The NΔπ coupling constant h_A, commonly used in πN scattering studies, coincides with f_BD and takes the large-N_c value h_A = 3g_A/(2√2).
- For the decuplet-decuplet-meson Lagrangian, the coupling constant relation gives f_DD = H/(3f₀), with SU(3) relations such as f_ΔΔπ = f_Ξ*Ξ*π = f_DD and f_Σ*ΔK = √6 f_DD.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of octet baryon interactions by Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2011, 2013) to include decuplet baryons, enabling analysis of lattice QCD results for ΩΩ and NΩ scattering from Buchoff (2012), Yamada (2015), Etminan (2014), and Sasaki (2016). The formalism allows investigation of possible dibaryon states including the d*(2380) in the ΔΔ system observed by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Adlarson 2011, 2014), and provides a framework for extrapolating lattice results at unphysical quark masses toward the physical point.
