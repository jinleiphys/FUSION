# Finite Volume Corrections to the Electromagnetic Mass of Composite Particles

> The long-range electromagnetic interaction presents a challenge for numerical computations in QCD + QED. In addition to power-law finite volume effects, the standard lattice gauge theory approach introduces non-locality through removal of photon zero-momentum modes. The resulting finite volume effects must be quantitatively understood; and, to this end, non-relativistic effective field theories are an efficient tool, especially in the case of composite particles. Recently an oddity related to non-locality of the standard lattice approach was uncovered by the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration. Explicit contributions from antiparticles appear to be required so that finite volume QED results for a point-like fermion can be reproduced in the effective field theory description. We provide transparency for this argument by considering point-like scalars and spinors in finite volume QED using the method of regions. For the more germane case of composite particles, we determine that antiparticle modes contribute to the finite-volume electromagnetic mass of composite spinors through terms proportional to the squares of time-like form factors evaluated at threshold. We extend existing finite volume calculations to one order higher, which is particularly relevant for the electromagnetic mass of light nuclei. Additionally, we verify that the analogous finite volume contributions to the nucleon mass in chiral perturbation theory vanish in accordance with locality.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that antiparticle modes contribute to the finite-volume electromagnetic mass of composite spinors through terms proportional to the squares of time-like form factors evaluated at threshold, but only when the standard non-local lattice QED formulation (with photon zero-momentum modes removed) is used; for composite scalars, no such antiparticle contributions appear at leading order in the fine-structure constant.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic effective field theories (NRQED and scalar NRQED) combined with the method of regions to compute finite-volume corrections to the electromagnetic self-energy of point-like and composite particles in QED defined on a three-dimensional spatial torus. They evaluate one-loop Feynman diagrams (sunset and tadpole topologies) in the particle rest frame, with photon zero-momentum modes removed, and match the relativistic results to effective field theory descriptions that include particle-antiparticle contact interactions. For composite particles, the matching uses time-like electromagnetic form factors evaluated at the pair-production threshold.

## Key numbers

* The universal power-law finite volume correction for all particles is given by \(\Delta M^{(1,2)}/M = (Q^2 \alpha)/(2\pi M L) c_2 + (Q^2 \alpha)/(M L)^2 c_1\), where \(c_j\) are shape coefficients defined by sums over non-zero lattice momenta.
* For a point-like scalar, the finite-volume electromagnetic mass shift includes an exponential term \(-6\pi Q^2 \alpha/(2\pi ML)^{3/2} e^{-ML}\) beyond the universal power-law terms.
* For a point-like spinor, there is an additional power-law term \(+3\pi Q^2 \alpha/(ML)^3\) beyond the universal correction, arising from antiparticle contributions.
* The antiparticle contribution to the spinor's finite-volume mass shift is \(-3\pi Q^2 \alpha/[2(ML)^3] c_0\), which when added to the non-relativistic result reproduces the full QED result.
* For composite scalars, the finite-volume mass shift to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha L^{-5})\) includes terms \(\Delta M_\phi = \Delta M^{(1,2)} + (2\pi\alpha)/(3L^3) Q\langle r^2\rangle - (4\pi^2/L^4)(\alpha_E + \beta_M)c_{-1} + (8\pi^3\alpha)/(3ML^4) Q\langle r^2\rangle c_{-1}\).
* The contact operator for composite scalars has coefficient proportional to \(|\mathcal{F}|^2/(16M^4)\), where \(\mathcal{F} \equiv F_\Phi(s=4M^2)\) is the time-like form factor at threshold.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume QED calculations of Davoudi (2014) to one order higher (\(\mathcal{O}(\alpha L^{-5})\)), which is particularly relevant for electromagnetic mass calculations of light nuclei. It provides transparency for the argument by Fodor (2015) that antiparticle contributions are required in NRQED to reproduce finite-volume QED results for point-like fermions, by using the method of regions. The paper also verifies that analogous finite-volume contributions to the nucleon mass in chiral perturbation theory vanish due to locality of finite-volume QCD, and notes that the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration (2014) had previously uncovered the oddity related to non-locality of the standard lattice approach.
