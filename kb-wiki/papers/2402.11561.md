# Transversity generalized parton distributions in spin-3/2 particles

> The definitions of the quark and gluon transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in spin-3/2 particles are obtained in the light-cone gauge. It is found that they contain 16 independent components for each parton. Their even or odd property is found in terms of skewness variable, and the odd transversity GPDs vanish in the forward limit. There are 16 amplitudes with helicity flip of quark or gluon. We conclude that all the amplitudes, unpolarized, polarized, and transversity amplitudes, have a common factor $F(ζ)$ carrying all the complex part. Here, the variable $ζ$ is the helicity difference $ζ=(λ' -λ)-(μ' - μ)$ for the amplitude $\mathcal{A}_{λ' μ',λμ}$. This kinematical factor is associated with the transfer of the orbital angular momentum in the quark- and gluon-spin-3/2 particle scattering amplitudes. We also derive another main physical quantity, transversity distribution, from the transversity GPDs in the forward limit for the spin-3/2 particles.

## Key claim

The paper defines the quark and gluon transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for spin-3/2 particles in the light-cone gauge, finding that they contain 16 independent components for each parton, with the odd transversity GPDs vanishing in the forward limit; all helicity amplitudes share a common kinematical factor \(F(\zeta)\) that carries the complex phase and is associated with orbital angular momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors decompose the quark and gluon transversity GPDs for spin-3/2 particles using non-local correlators with \(i\sigma^{ni}\) operators for quarks and \(G^{ni}G^{jn}\) operators for gluons, imposing parity, time-reversal, and hermiticity constraints to reduce the tensor structures to 16 independent GPDs per parton. They derive the helicity amplitudes for all 16 helicity-flip configurations and extract the common factor \(F(\zeta)\) from the amplitudes, where \(\zeta = (\lambda' - \lambda) - (\mu' - \mu)\). The forward limit of the transversity GPDs yields the transversity parton distributions, and sum rules connect the GPDs to tensor form factors.

## Key numbers

- There are **16 independent transversity GPDs** for each parton (quark or gluon) in a spin-3/2 hadron.
- The helicity amplitudes contain a common factor \(F(\zeta) = C^\zeta \theta(\zeta) + C^{*-\zeta} \theta(-\zeta)\), where \(\zeta = (\lambda' - \lambda) - (\mu' - \mu)\) and \(F(0) = 1\).
- There are **7 tensor form factors** for the spin-3/2 system (compared to 3 for spin-1/2 and 5 for spin-1).
- The quark tensor charge is given by the combination \(G^{qT}_1(0) - G^{qT}_2(0)\) in the forward limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on unpolarized and polarized GPDs for spin-3/2 particles (Fu, Dong, and Kumano, 2022, 2023) to the transversity sector, completing the leading-twist GPD decomposition for spin-3/2 hadrons. It generalizes the transversity GPD formalism previously developed for spin-1/2 hadrons (Diehl, 2001; Cosyn, 2018) and spin-1 hadrons (Cosyn, 2018) to the spin-3/2 case, providing a theoretical foundation for future experimental measurements of \(\Delta\) isobar and \(\Omega\) baryon structure at facilities such as EIC, EicC, BESIII, KEK-B, and J-PARC.
