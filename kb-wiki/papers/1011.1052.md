# Calculating Kaon Fragmentation Functions from NJL-Jet Model

> The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) - Jet model provides a sound framework for calculating the fragmentation functions in an effective chiral quark theory, where the momentum and isospin sum rules are satisfied without the introduction of ad hoc parameters. Earlier studies of the pion fragmentation functions using the NJL model within this framework showed qualitative agreement with the empirical parameterizations. Here we extend the NJL-Jet model by including the strange quark. The corrections to the pion fragmentation functions and corresponding kaon fragmentation functions are calculated using the elementary quark to quark-meson fragmentation functions from NJL. The results for the kaon fragmentation functions exhibit a qualitative agreement with the empirical parameterizations, while the unfavored strange quark fragmentation to pions is shown to be of the same order of magnitude as the unfavored light quark's. The results of these studies are expected to provide important guidance for the analysis of a large variety of semi-inclusive data.

## Key claim

The NJL-Jet model, extended to include strange quarks, produces kaon fragmentation functions that show qualitative agreement with empirical parameterizations, but the agreement is only within the large uncertainties of those parameterizations; the unfavored strange quark fragmentation to pions is shown to be of the same order of magnitude as the unfavored light quark's, contradicting the common phenomenological assumption that they are equal.

## Method

The authors extended the NJL-Jet model by introducing the strange quark with constituent mass \(M_s = 0.537\) GeV, determined by fitting the model-calculated kaon mass to the experimental value \(m_K = 0.495\) GeV. They calculated elementary quark-to-quark-meson fragmentation functions using the SU(3) NJL model with Lepage-Brodsky invariant mass cutoff regularization (\(\Lambda_3 = 0.67\) GeV), then solved the coupled integral equations for the total fragmentation functions of \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks to pions and kaons. The results were evolved at next-to-leading order from the model scale \(Q_0^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) to \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) using the DGLAP evolution software of Botje (2010).

## Key numbers

* Strange constituent quark mass: \(M_s = 0.537\) GeV, determined by fitting the model kaon mass to the experimental value \(m_K = 0.495\) GeV.
* Quark-kaon coupling constant: \(g_{KqQ} = 3.39\).
* Light constituent quark mass: \(M = 0.3\) GeV, taken from Bentz (1999).
* Three-momentum cutoff: \(\Lambda_3 = 0.67\) GeV, fixed by reproducing the experimental pion decay constant.
* Model scale: \(Q_0^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\); comparison scale after NLO evolution: \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).
* Momentum sum rule satisfied within numerical precision of less than 1%; for the \(u\) quark jet, 86% of isospin is transferred to pions and 14% to kaons.
* Fragmentation to \(K^+\) is about half as likely as fragmentation to \(\pi^+\) from a \(u\) quark.

## Context

This work extends the NJL-Jet model of Ito (2009), which previously calculated only pion fragmentation functions, by including strange quarks and kaon production. The results provide a first-principles calculation of kaon fragmentation functions within an effective chiral quark theory, offering an alternative to phenomenological parameterizations such as those of Hirai (2007). The finding that unfavored strange and light quark fragmentation to pions are of the same order of magnitude challenges the common assumption in phenomenological analyses (e.g., Miyama 1995) that \(D_d^{\pi^+} = D_s^{\pi^+}\). The framework can be extended to include vector meson and nucleon-antinucleon channels, and Monte Carlo simulations are suggested for more accurate descriptions of finite-momentum effects and transverse momentum distributions.
