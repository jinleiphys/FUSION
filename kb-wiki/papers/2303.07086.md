# Non-radial oscillations and gravitational wave emission of hybrid neutron stars

> We investigate non-radial oscillations of pure and hybrid neutron stars, employing equations of state of nuclear matter from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, and of quark matter from the Dyson-Schwinger quark model, performing a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase in hybrid stars. Characteristic differences between neutron-star and hybrid-star $g_1$-mode oscillation frequencies, damping times, and gravitational wave strains are pointed out. Prospects of observations are also discussed.

# Non-radial oscillations and gravitational wave emission of hybrid neutron stars

## Key claim

The g1-mode oscillation frequencies, damping times, and gravitational wave strains show characteristic differences between pure neutron stars and hybrid stars (those with a mixed hadron-quark phase in their core), but these differences are small enough that distinguishing hybrid stars from pure neutron stars using mass-radius observations alone remains difficult, even though the g-mode frequencies themselves differ significantly between the two types.

## Method

The authors compute non-radial quadrupole (l=2) oscillations of non-rotating neutron stars and hybrid stars using the relativistic Cowling approximation, which neglects metric perturbations. They employ Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with Argonne V18 and Bonn-B potentials plus three-body forces for nuclear matter, and the Dyson-Schwinger quark model with a Gaussian-type effective interaction for quark matter, using a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase in hybrid stars. The oscillation equations are solved with boundary conditions at the center and surface to obtain eigenfrequencies, damping times via gravitational wave emission, and gravitational wave strains.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses: V18 EOS gives M_max ≈ 2.36 solar masses; BOB EOS gives M_max ≈ 2.51 solar masses
- Radius constraints from NICER+GW170817: R_{1.4} = 12.45 ± 0.65 km (Miller21), 11.94^{+0.76}_{-0.87} km (Pang21), 12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81} km or 12.18^{+0.56}_{-0.79} km (Raaijmakers21)
- Radius constraint for PSR J0740+6620: R_{2.08} = 12.35 ± 0.75 km (Miller21)
- The bag constant for the Dyson-Schwinger model is set to B_DS = 90 MeV fm^{-3}
- The muon onset in nuclear matter produces a spike in the inverse sound speed difference around ρ_B ≈ 0.15 fm^{-3}
- The Cowling approximation yields about 10-30% accuracy for f-mode frequencies and about 20% for p-mode frequencies

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of radial oscillations (Sun 2021) to non-radial oscillations, which can directly produce gravitational wave signals. It builds on the theoretical framework for non-radial oscillations developed by Thorne (1967), Detweiler and Lindblom (1983, 1985), and Chandrasekhar and Ferrari (1991). The study uses the same nuclear and quark matter models as earlier work by Chen et al. (2011, 2015) and Luo et al. (2019), and follows the approach of Jaikumar et al. (2021) for treating the adiabatic speed of sound in the mixed phase. The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave observations from isolated neutron stars, potentially revealing the presence of quark matter in neutron star interiors.
