# Fully coupled-channel study of $K^-pp$ resonance in a chiral SU(3)-based $K^{bar}N$ potential

> Nuclear system with antikaons, so-called kaonic nuclei, has been a longstanding issue in strange nuclear physics and hadron physics, because they might have exotic nature; In particular, they could be a doorway to the dense matter due to the strong attraction between antikaon and nucleon. Among kaonic nuclei, the three-body system composed of two protons and a single $K^-$ meson, $K^-pp$, is the most essential. In this article, we will report on the recent situation of $K^-pp$ studies in both theoretical and experimental sides. Afterwards, we will explain our latest study of the $K^-pp$ with a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (Full ccCSM) using a chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$(-$πY$) potential. In Full ccCSM, the $K^-pp$ is completely treated as a resonant state of a $\bar{K}NN$-$πΣN$-$πΛN$ coupled-channel system. The energy dependence involved in the chiral potential is handled with a self-consistent calculation in which two extreme ansatzes are examined: field picture and particle picture. With our chiral SU(3)-based potential constrained by the precise data of kaonic hydrogen atom (SIDDHARTA experiment), the $K^-pp$ resonance is obtained as a shallowly bound state measured from the $\bar{K}NN$ threshold with a narrow width, if the field picture is employed in the calculation: the binding energy is $14-28$ MeV and half value of the mesonic decay width are $8-15$ MeV. On the other hand, if the particle picture is employed, it is found that the binding energy could be as large as about 50 MeV, even though such a chiral potential is used. Based on these results of Full ccCSM calculation, we have discussed on the possibility for kaonic nuclei to form a dense matter and on the latest experimental result reported by J-PARC E15 collaboration.

## Key claim
The $K^-pp$ resonance is obtained as a shallowly bound state with a narrow width (binding energy 14-28 MeV, half mesonic decay width 8-15 MeV) only when the "field picture" ansatz is employed for the self-consistent treatment of the energy-dependent chiral potential; the "particle picture" yields a binding energy as large as about 50 MeV even with the same potential constrained by SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data.

## Method
The authors performed a fully coupled-channel complex scaling method (Full ccCSM) calculation treating the $K^-pp$ system as a resonant state of a $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi\Sigma N$-$\pi\Lambda N$ coupled-channel system with spin-parity $J^\pi=0^-$ and isospin $T=1/2$. They used a chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$(-$\pi Y$) potential derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, with the Argonne v18 $NN$ potential, and handled the energy dependence of the chiral potential through a self-consistent calculation examining two extreme ansatzes: the field picture and the particle picture.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ with the chiral-latest potential and field picture: 14-28 MeV
- Half mesonic decay width of $K^-pp$ with the chiral-latest potential and field picture: 8-15 MeV
- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ with the chiral-latest potential and particle picture: 21-50 MeV
- Half mesonic decay width of $K^-pp$ with the chiral-latest potential and particle picture: 13-19 MeV
- For the typical case with $f_\pi=110$ MeV and field picture: binding energy 17.4 MeV, half decay width 9.8 MeV, mean $NN$ distance $2.14-i0.16$ fm
- For the typical case with $f_\pi=110$ MeV and particle picture: binding energy 27.3 MeV, half decay width 15.9 MeV, mean $NN$ distance $1.80-i0.06$ fm
- Two-body $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance with chiral-latest potential ($f_\pi=110$ MeV): $(B_{\bar{K}N}, \Gamma/2) = (8.0, 13.4)$ MeV
- Two-body $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance with chiral-old potential ($f_\pi=110$ MeV): $(B_{\bar{K}N}, \Gamma/2) = (17.2, 16.6)$ MeV
- J-PARC E15 second-run experimental result: binding energy $47 \pm 3 (stat.)\; ^{+3}_{-6} (sys.)$ MeV, decay width $115 \pm 7 (stat.)\; ^{+10}_{-9} (sys.)$ MeV

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of $K^-pp$ using variational methods and Faddeev-AGS approaches, which gave binding energies scattered from about 20 MeV with chiral SU(3) potentials (e.g., DHW, BGL, IKS) to 50-90 MeV with phenomenological potentials (e.g., AY, Shevchenko). The present Full ccCSM calculation is the first to fully treat all coupled channels ($\bar{K}NN$, $\pi\Sigma N$, $\pi\Lambda N$) with a chiral SU(3)-based potential constrained by the precise SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data. The results enable comparison with the J-PARC E15 second-run experiment and inform the question of whether kaonic nuclei can form dense matter, with the field picture suggesting normal nuclear density ($R_{NN} \approx 2.1$ fm, close to the 2.2 fm of normal nuclear matter) while the particle picture and phenomenological potentials suggest denser states.
