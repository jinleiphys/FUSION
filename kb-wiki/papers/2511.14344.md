# Plasma oscillations within Israel-Stewart theory

> It is well known that, at zero wavenumber, the non-hydrodynamic frequencies of uncharged kinetic theory are purely imaginary. On the other hand, it was recently shown that, in resistive magnetohydrodynamics, the interplay between the Israel-Stewart relaxation equation and the Ampère-Maxwell law can give rise to a pair of oscillating non-hydrodynamic modes. In this work, we analyze this phenomenon in detail. We first demonstrate that these oscillatory modes are exact solutions of the Drude model, corresponding to ordinary plasma oscillations. We then invoke the Onsager-Casimir principle to explain that their oscillatory nature reflects the distinct PT-transformation properties of the degrees of freedom: the distribution function is even, while the electric field is odd. Finally, we establish that, in a kinetic theory of charged particles, there can be at most one such pair of oscillatory modes per spatial dimension, while all other modes still must sit on the imaginary axis.

## Key claim

The oscillating non-hydrodynamic modes previously identified in Israel-Stewart resistive magnetohydrodynamics are not artifacts of the approximation but exact solutions of the Boltzmann-Vlasov-Maxwell system, corresponding to ordinary plasma oscillations (plasmons) in the Drude model, provided that the condition \(\omega_p \tau > 1/2\) is satisfied.

## Method

The author derives the equation of motion for the electric current by combining the Ampere-Maxwell law with the Israel-Stewart relaxation equation obtained from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for a non-relativistic degenerate electron gas with elastic isotropic scattering. The Onsager-Casimir principle is then applied to explain why coupling between PT-even moments of the distribution function and the PT-odd electric field enables oscillatory dynamics. Finally, a linear-algebra theorem is proved showing that the block matrix governing the coupled system of \(N\) non-equilibrium degrees of freedom and the electric field can have at most one complex-conjugate pair of eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

* Plasma frequency \(\omega_p \equiv \sqrt{e^2 n_e/m}\) appears as the natural frequency of the damped harmonic oscillator equation \(\partial^2_t J^j + (1/\tau) \partial_t J^j + \omega_p^2 J^j = 0\).
* Oscillatory modes exist when \(\omega_p \tau > 1/2\); in typical metals at room temperature \(\omega_p \tau \sim 100\), in white dwarfs and neutron stars \(\omega_p \tau \sim 10^3\), in the solar corona \(\omega_p \tau \sim 10^7\), and in accretion disks \(\omega_p \tau \sim 10^{15}\).
* The dispersion relation for the non-hydrodynamic modes is \(2\omega\tau = -i \pm i\sqrt{1 - (2\omega_p \tau)^2}\), yielding oscillating solutions in the underdamped case.

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Dash (2022) that Israel-Stewart-type relaxation in resistive magnetohydrodynamics produces oscillating non-hydrodynamic modes, which contradicted the conventional wisdom that all non-hydrodynamic frequencies of kinetic theory at zero wavenumber are purely imaginary (as seen in the work of Moore (2018) and others). The analysis shows that these oscillations are physical plasma oscillations, and the Onsager-Casimir framework explains why uncharged kinetic theory (studied by Gavassino (2022, 2024)) is purely relaxational while coupling to the electric field enables oscillatory dynamics. The theorem limiting oscillatory modes to at most one pair per spatial dimension provides a rigorous constraint for future hydrodynamic modeling of charged plasmas and quark-gluon matter.
