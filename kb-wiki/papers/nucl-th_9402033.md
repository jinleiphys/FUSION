# Comment on: "Transverse-Mass $M_\perp$ Dependence of Dilepton Emission from Preequilibrium and Quark-Gluon Plasma in High Energy Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions"

> In a recent Letter, Geiger presents calculations of the dilepton emission from the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions using the parton cascade model (PCM). He shows that the $M_\perp$ scaling is not observed. In this Comment, we point out that this is largely due to a defect in the PCM.

## Key claim

The large breaking of the $M_\perp$ scaling reported by Geiger is not a physical effect of the preequilibrium state or transverse expansion, but is largely due to a defect in the parton cascade model (PCM): the use of an unphysical lower cutoff on parton virtuality ($\mu_0 = 1$ GeV) that artificially suppresses $\bar{q}q$ annihilation at low invariant mass.

## Method

Asakawa identifies that the PCM imposes a lower limit on timelike parton virtuality, $\mu_0 = 1$ GeV, which gives each parton an effective mass larger than 1 GeV. Once a parton's virtuality reaches $\mu_0$ (typically by proper time $\tau \simeq 0.2$ fm), it is kept unchanged, whereas in reality virtuality should continue to decrease. This unphysical large effective quark mass causes a phase-space suppression of $\bar{q}q$ annihilation contributions to dilepton production for invariant masses $M$ below about 2.8 GeV, which is the main source of the claimed $M_\perp$ scaling violation.

## Key numbers

- The PCM cutoff on timelike virtuality is $\mu_0 = 1$ GeV, giving each parton an effective mass larger than 1 GeV.
- The cutoff time when virtuality is frozen is $\tau_{cut} \simeq 1/\mu_0 = 0.2$ fm.
- The $\bar{q}q$ annihilation contribution is strongly suppressed below $M \simeq 2.8$ GeV, with no $\bar{q}q$ contribution below $M = 2$ GeV.
- Local thermalization in the PCM is achieved as early as $\tau \simeq 0.3$ fm.
- Most $M_\perp$ scaling-breaking dileptons are created after $\tau = 0.5$ fm.

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the numerical results of Geiger (1994), who used the PCM to claim that $M_\perp$ scaling is not observed in dilepton emission from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Asakawa argues that the scaling violation is an artifact of the PCM's infrared cutoff, not a genuine non-equilibrium effect, and that earlier schematic calculations with on-shell quarks (M. Asakawa, 1991) showed enhancement of $\bar{q}q$ dilepton production at small $M$. The work implies that if the virtuality treatment is corrected, the $M_\perp$ scaling predicted by McLerran and Toimela (1985) should be restored, and the PCM in its present form is not suitable for calculating dilepton yields in the $M_\perp \sim 2-3$ GeV region.
