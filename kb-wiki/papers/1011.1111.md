# Phase diagram of dense quark matter in QCD-like theories

> I report the results of a series of works on the phase diagram of theories with a different number of colors and/or quarks in a different representation than in QCD. Similarities as well as differences as compared to the real world are pointed out, focusing in particular on the interplay of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking. It will be argued that recent lattice data may provide us with a clue to understand deconfinement in cold dense quark matter.

# Phase diagram of dense quark matter in QCD-like theories

## Key claim
QCD-like theories with quarks in (pseudo)real representations of the color group avoid the sign problem at nonzero baryon chemical potential, enabling lattice simulations at high density, but their phase diagrams differ from real QCD because baryons are bosons and the global flavor symmetry is enlarged to SU(2N_f). The paper argues that recent lattice data may provide a clue to understand deconfinement in cold dense quark matter, though the deconfinement temperature in two-color QCD appears essentially insensitive to chemical potential in model calculations.

## Method
The paper reviews analytic and model calculations for two-color QCD with fundamental quarks (pseudoreal representation) and adjoint QCD with two or three colors (real representation), using chiral perturbation theory at low density and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models augmented with the Polyakov loop (PNJL) at high density. For adjoint QCD, an improved PNJL model with a spin-model gauge sector is used to evaluate Polyakov loop expectation values in different representations and test Casimir scaling. The phase diagrams are constructed by tracking the chiral condensate, diquark condensate, and Polyakov loop expectation value as functions of temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- In two-color QCD with two light quark flavors, the ground state chiral symmetry breaking pattern is SU(4) → Sp(4), yielding 5 Nambu–Goldstone bosons (3 pions, 1 diquark, 1 antidiquark).
- In adjoint three-color QCD, the ratio of chiral restoration temperature to deconfinement temperature is about 7.8.
- For two-color adjoint QCD, the deconfinement transition is second-order, leading to a tetracritical point where deconfinement and BEC transition lines intersect.
- For three-color adjoint QCD, deconfinement is first-order, producing two nearby tricritical points where the second-order BEC line meets the first-order deconfinement line.
- In the high-temperature expansion for two-color adjoint QCD, Casimir scaling for Polyakov loops is violated only at fourth order: ⟨ℓ_j⟩^{1/j(j+1)} = 1 − 1/α + 1/(4α²) + [8j(j+1)−1]/(96α³) + ⋯.

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD-like theories initiated by Kogut (2000) and builds on lattice simulations by Hands (2010) that show anomalous thermodynamic behavior around the BEC transition in two-color QCD. The paper contrasts type-I theories (real quark representations, e.g., adjoint QCD) classified by Zhang (2010) with type-II theories (pseudoreal representations, e.g., two-color QCD), and uses the PNJL model framework of Ratti (2004) and Sun (2007). The results enable future lattice studies of deconfinement at finite density and provide analytic benchmarks for Casimir scaling in non-Abelian gauge theories at nonzero chemical potential.
