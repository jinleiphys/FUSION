# Dynamically Generated $Ξ(1690)$

> We show that the $Ξ(1690)$ resonance can be dynamically generated in the $s$-wave $\bar{K} Σ$-$\bar{K} Λ$-$πΞ$-$ηΞ$ coupled-channels chiral unitary approach. In our model, the $Ξ(1690)$ resonance appears near the $\bar{K} Σ$ threshold as a $\bar{K} Σ$ molecular state and the experimental data are reproduced well. We discuss properties of the dynamically generated $Ξ(1690)$.

## Key claim

The $\Xi(1690)$ resonance can be dynamically generated as a $\bar{K} \Sigma$ molecular state in the $s$-wave $\bar{K} \Sigma$-$\bar{K} \Lambda$-$\pi \Xi$-$\eta \Xi$ coupled-channels chiral unitary approach, but strictly speaking the peak seen in the mass spectra is a cusp at the $K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$ threshold rather than a usual Breit-Wigner resonance peak, as the pole exists above the $K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$ threshold in the first Riemann sheet.

## Method

The meson-baryon scattering amplitude was calculated using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in algebraic form with the Weinberg-Tomozawa term from chiral perturbation theory as the interaction kernel, and a covariant loop function evaluated with dimensional regularization. The model parameters (subtraction constants) were fixed by fitting the $\bar{K}^{0} \Lambda$ and $K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$ mass spectra from the decay $\Lambda_{c}^{+} \to \Xi(1690)^{0} K^{+}$ measured by Abe (2001). The resonance pole position, coupling constants, and compositeness were extracted from the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of $\Xi(1690)^{0}$: $w_{\rm pole} = 1684.3 - 0.5 i$ MeV
- Ratio $R \equiv \mathcal{B} [ \Lambda _{c}^{+} \to \Xi (1690)^{0} K^{+} \to ( K^{-} \Sigma ^{+} ) K^{+} ] / \mathcal{B} [\Lambda _{c} \to \Xi (1690)^{0} K^{+} \to ( \bar{K}^{0} \Lambda ) K^{+} ] = 1.06$, within $2 \sigma$ of the experimental value $0.62 \pm 0.33$
- Decay width of $\Xi(1690)^{0}$ in the model: $\sim 1$ MeV
- Compositeness sum for $K^{-} \Sigma^{+}$ and $\bar{K}^{0} \Sigma^{0}$ channels is almost unity, indicating a $\bar{K} \Sigma$ molecular state
- Scattering length $a ( \bar{K} \Sigma ( I = 1/2 ) ) \approx 4$ fm at $f_{K} = 105$ MeV
- Previous studies found $\Xi(1690)$ pole positions at $1663 - 2 i$ MeV (GarciaRecio, 2003) and $1651 - 2 i$ MeV (Gamermann, 2011)

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach studies of multi-strangeness baryons by Ramos (2002), GarciaRecio (2003), and Gamermann (2011), but finds a $\Xi(1690)$ pole much closer to the $\bar{K} \Sigma$ threshold than those earlier works. The difference arises from using the physical kaon decay constant $f_{K} = 1.2 f_{\pi} = 110.64$ MeV rather than $f_{K} = 90$ MeV used by GarciaRecio (2003), which makes the $\bar{K} \Sigma$ interaction about 1.5 times weaker. The result enables future studies of hadronic molecules in multi-strangeness baryon spectroscopy, particularly the role of the relatively heavy kaon mass compared to the pion mass in forming such molecular states.
