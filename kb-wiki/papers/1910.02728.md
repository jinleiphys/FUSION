# Rotation induced charged pion condensation in a strong magnetic field: A Nambu--Jona-Lasino model study

> We investigate the possibility of charged pion condensation in the presence of parallel rotation and magnetic field within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with quarks as the fundamental degrees of freedom. Previous study based on non-interacting Klein-Gordon theory for pions showed that the charged pions will undergo Bose-Einstein condensation under this circumstance [Y. Liu and I. Zahed, Phys. Rev. Lett. ${\bf 120}$, 032001 (2018)]. In this work, we take into account the internal quark structures of charged pions self-consistently through quark polarization loops in an interacting theory, i.e., the Nambu--Jona-Lasino model. The stability of the system is explored against the formation of a nonzero expectation value of the composite charged pion field $\bar{u}iγ_5d$. We find that two competing effects are induced by the rotation: the isospin enhancement which favors charged pion condensation and the spin breaking which disfavors the condensation. For a strong magnetic field ($\sqrt{eB}\sim1{\rm GeV}$) and system size of a few fermi, the isospin enhancement effect is stronger than the spin breaking one, and the charged pion condensation becomes energetically favored beyond a critical angular velocity of a few MeV.

## Key claim

The authors find that charged pion condensation can become energetically favored in a strong magnetic field under parallel rotation, but only when the isospin enhancement effect from rotation outweighs the spin breaking effect from the magnetic field; the abstract's claim of condensation "beyond a critical angular velocity of a few MeV" depends on the system size being a few fermi and the magnetic field strength being around √(eB) ~ 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with quarks as fundamental degrees of freedom, incorporating a constant parallel magnetic field and global rotation via a curved spacetime metric. They first solve the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass in the absence of pion condensate, then study the stability against charged pion condensation through a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the effective potential, computing the quadratic coefficient A from quark polarization loops summed over Landau levels up to n=10.

## Key numbers

- Critical angular velocity for charged pion condensation: Ω_PC of order 1 MeV for magnetic field eB = 0.5 GeV² and eB = 1 GeV², with system sizes R₁ = 20/√(2eB) and R₂ = 20/√(eB) (a few fermi)
- Dynamical quark mass starts to decrease at a critical angular velocity Ω_m of order O(MeV) for the chosen magnetic fields and system sizes
- Magnetic field strengths studied: eB = 0.5 GeV² and eB = 1.0 GeV² (√(eB) ~ 1 GeV)
- Model parameters: current quark mass m₀ = 5 MeV, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 653 MeV, coupling constant G = 4.93 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the study of Liu and Zahed (2018), who predicted charged pion condensation under parallel rotation and magnetic field using non-interacting Klein-Gordon theory for pions, by incorporating the internal quark structure of pions self-consistently through the NJL model. The results contradict the naive expectation from the spin breaking picture that charged pion condensation is not favored under parallel rotation and magnetic field, similar to the pure magnetic field case studied by Cao (2015). The findings enable future investigations of the interplay among dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, charged pion condensation, and the magnetovorticity effect in QCD matter under extreme conditions.
