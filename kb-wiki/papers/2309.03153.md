# Magnetized Baryonic layer and a novel BPS bound in the gauged-Non-Linear-Sigma-Model-Maxwell theory in (3+1)-dimensions through Hamilton-Jacobi equation

> It is show that one can derive a novel BPS bound for the gauged Non-Linear-Sigma-Model (NLSM) Maxwell theory in (3+1) dimensions which can actually be saturated. Such novel bound is constructed using Hamilton-Jacobi equation from classical mechanics. The configurations saturating the bound represent Hadronic layers possessing both Baryonic charge and magnetic flux. However, unlike what happens in the more common situations, the topological charge which appears naturally in the BPS bound is a non-linear function of the Baryonic charge. This BPS bound can be saturated when the surface area of the layer is quantized. The far-reaching implications of these results are discussed. In particular, we determine the exact relation between the magnetic flux and the Baryonic charge as well as the critical value of the Baryonic chemical potential beyond which these configurations become thermodynamically unstable.

## Key claim

A novel BPS bound for the gauged Non-Linear-Sigma-Model-Maxwell theory in (3+1) dimensions can be derived using the Hamilton-Jacobi equation from classical mechanics, and this bound can be saturated when the surface area of the hadronic layer is quantized in terms of the pion decay constant. The topological charge appearing naturally in the BPS bound is a non-linear function of the baryonic charge, unlike the more common linear relation.

## Method

The author considers a static ansatz for the SU(2)-valued scalar field and the U(1) gauge field that depends non-trivially on all three spatial coordinates, reducing the full system of seven coupled non-linear field equations to two coupled ordinary differential equations for the profiles H(r) and u(r). The Hamilton-Jacobi equation method is then applied to the energy density functional to derive a BPS bound, which requires solving a first-order partial differential equation for a superpotential W(H,u). When the layer area satisfies a specific quantization condition, the Hamilton-Jacobi equation is solved analytically, yielding first-order BPS equations that imply the second-order field equations.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant is f_pi ≈ 130 MeV, which enters the quantization condition for the layer area.
* The layer area A is quantized as A = (2π p / (e f_pi))^2, where p is a positive integer and e is the electric charge.
* The baryonic charge density is given by ρ_B = -12p [u(1 + cos(2H))]', with the total baryonic charge obtained by integration over the three-dimensional hypersurface.
* The magnetic field intensity is proportional to u', and the BPS equations give u' = p K^{1/2} L_r sqrt(1 - exp(-4u^2/p^2 + 2I_0)), where K = e^2 f_pi^2/4 and L_r, L are metric parameters defining the box volume.

## Context

This work extends the search for BPS bounds in the gauged non-linear sigma model, which had previously not yielded saturable bounds in (3+1) dimensions without modifying the theory (as done by gaugsol5, gaugsol6, gaugsol7 and others). The approach contrasts with earlier analytic constructions by Canfora and collaborators (Canfora 2018, crystal1, and others) that required electric fields of the same order as magnetic fields; here only magnetic fields are generated self-consistently. The results enable analytic determination of the exact relation between magnetic flux and baryonic charge, as well as the critical baryonic chemical potential for thermodynamic stability, which is relevant for neutron star physics and heavy ion collisions where nuclear pasta phases are expected (pasta1, pasta2, pasta3, pasta4).
