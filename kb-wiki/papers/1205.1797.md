# Holographic RG flow of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in strongly coupled anisotropic plasma

> We study holographic RG flow of the shear viscosity tensor of anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma by using its type IIB supergravity dual in anisotropic bulk spacetime. We find that the shear viscosity tensor has three independent components in the anisotropic bulk spacetime away from the boundary, and one of the components has a non-trivial RG flow while the other two have a trivial one. For the component of the shear viscosity tensor with non-trivial RG flow, we derive its RG flow equation, and solve the equation analytically to second order in the anisotropy parameter 'a'. We derive the RG equation using the equation of motion, holographic Wilsonian RG method, and Kubo's formula. All methods give the same result. Solving the equation, we find that the ratio of the component of the shear viscosity tensor to entropy density 'eta/s' flows from above '1/4pi' at the horizon (IR) to below '1/4pi' at the boundary (UV) where it violates the holographic shear viscosity (Kovtun-Son-Starinets) bound and where it agrees with the other longitudinal component.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s for one component of the shear viscosity tensor in anisotropic N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma flows from above 1/4π at the horizon (IR) to below 1/4π at the boundary (UV), violating the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound at the boundary, but only when the anisotropy parameter a is nonzero and the calculation is carried out to second order in a.

## Method

The authors study holographic RG flow of the shear viscosity tensor using the type IIB supergravity dual of anisotropic N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma, with anisotropy introduced via a Chern-Simons deformation with θ(z)=2πaz. They derive the RG flow equation for the shear viscosity component η^i_z using three independent methods: the equation of motion for gravitational shear mode fluctuations, the holographic Wilsonian RG method, and Kubo's formula. The flow equation is solved analytically to second order in the anisotropy parameter a, with initial conditions at the horizon determined by requiring regularity.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at the horizon (IR) for the component η^i_z: η/s = (1/4π)(1 + (log2)/(4π²)(a/T)² + O(a⁴)) > 1/4π for a ≠ 0
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at the boundary (UV) for the same component: η/s = (1/4π)(1 - (log2)/(4π²)(a/T)² + O(a⁴)) < 1/4π for a ≠ 0
- The entropy density of the anisotropic plasma: s = (π²N_c²T³)/2 + (N_c²T/16)a² + O(a⁴)
- The horizon location: u_h = 1/(πT) + (5log2 - 2)/(48π³T³)a² + O(a⁴)
- The trace of the energy-momentum tensor: ⟨T^b_b⟩ = (N_c²a⁴)/(48π⁴)

## Context

This work extends the membrane paradigm calculations of shear viscosity in anisotropic plasma by Rebhan (2011) to include a non-trivial RG flow for the component η^i_z, which was not captured by the membrane paradigm approach alone. The paper shows that while two components of the shear viscosity tensor (η^j_i and η^z_i) have trivial RG flow and can be computed via the membrane paradigm, the third independent component η^i_z exhibits non-trivial flow. This enables a complete understanding of shear viscosity in anisotropic strongly coupled plasmas relevant to heavy ion collision physics, where anisotropy is present as noted by Florkowski (2008). The work also confirms that at the boundary, η^i_z equals η^z_i, reducing the number of independent components to two, consistent with the symmetric energy-momentum tensor at the boundary as discussed by Erdmenger (2010).
