# Current conservation in electrodisintegration of a bound system in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> Using our solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with OBE kernel in Minkowski space both for the bound and scattering states, we calculate the transition form factors for electrodisintegration of the bound system which determine the electromagnetic current J of this process. Special emphasis is put on verifying the gauge invariance which should manifest itself in the current conservation. We find that for any value of the momentum transfer q the contributions of the plane wave and the final state interaction to the quantity J.q cancel each other thus providing J.q=0. However, this cancellation is obtained only if the initial Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (bound state), the final one (scattering state) and the current operator are strictly consistent with each other. A reliable result for the transition form factor can be found only in this case.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic current in electrodisintegration of a bound system is conserved only when the initial Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (bound state), the final Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (scattering state), and the current operator are strictly consistent with each other within the same dynamical framework; if this consistency is broken, the resulting transition form factor is unreliable even if one attempts to restore gauge invariance by projecting out the non-conserved part.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an one-boson-exchange (OBE) kernel in Minkowski space for both bound and scattering states of spinless particles. They calculated the transition electromagnetic current from the sum of two Feynman graphs: a plane-wave (PW) contribution and a final-state interaction (FSI) triangle graph. The form factors were extracted in a reference frame where \(q_0=0\) and the initial and final momenta are collinear, using numerical integration with a subtraction technique to handle pole singularities in the propagators and vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the initial bound state: \(B = 0.01\,m\) (bound state mass \(M = 1.99\,m\))
- Relative momentum of the final scattering state: \(p_s = 0.1\,m\) (final state invariant mass \(M' = 2.00998\,m\))
- Coupling constant in the OBE kernel: \(\alpha = 1.437\)
- The sum of FSI and PW contributions to the non-conserved form factor \(F'(Q^2)\) is smaller than each individual contribution by two to three orders of magnitude, indicating current conservation within numerical precision
- For the "phenomenological" test with parameters \(a^2 = 1.5\), \(b^2 = 1\), the form factor \(F'(Q^2)\) becomes non-zero, demonstrating violation of current conservation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of bound and scattering state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Minkowski space (Karmanov and Carbonell, 2013) to the electrodisintegration transition form factor. It addresses the gauge invariance problem extensively discussed by Adam, Van Orden, and Gross (1990s) in the framework of Bethe-Salpeter and Gross spectator equations. The results demonstrate that using phenomenological or inconsistent final-state interactions (as is common in practical calculations such as deuteron electrodisintegration) produces unreliable form factors even if one attempts to restore current conservation by projection, making internal consistency mandatory for reliable predictions.
