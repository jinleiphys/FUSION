# Interaction energy and itinerant ferromagnetism in a strongly interacting Fermi gas in the absence of molecule formation

> We investigate the interaction energy and the possibility of itinerant ferromagnetism in a strongly interacting Fermi gas at zero temperature in the absence of molecule formation. The interaction energy is obtained by summing the perturbative contributions of Galitskii-Feynman type to all orders in the gas parameter. It can be expressed by a simple phase space integral of an in-medium scattering phase shift. In both three and two dimensions (3D and 2D), the interaction energy shows a maximum before reaching the resonance from the Bose-Einstein condensate side, which provides a possible explanation of the experimental measurements of the interaction energy. This phenomenon can be theoretically explained by the qualitative change of the nature of the binary interaction in the medium. The appearance of an energy maximum has significant effects on the itinerant ferromagnetism. In 3D, the ferromagnetic transition is reentrant and itinerant ferromagnetism exists in a narrow window around the energy maximum. In 2D, the present theoretical approach suggests that itinerant ferromagnetism does not exist, which reflects the fact that the energy maximum becomes much lower than the energy of the fully polarized state.

## Key claim

The interaction energy of a strongly interacting Fermi gas in the absence of molecule formation shows a maximum before reaching the resonance from the BEC side in both 3D and 2D, but this maximum is much lower than the fully polarized state energy in 2D, leading to the prediction that itinerant ferromagnetism does not exist in 2D while in 3D it exists only in a narrow window around the energy maximum.

## Method

The author sums perturbative contributions of the Galitskii-Feynman type to all orders in the gas parameter, resumming particle-particle ladders, hole-hole ladders, and mixed ladders self-consistently. The interaction energy is expressed as a phase space integral of an in-medium scattering phase shift derived from the many-body T matrix. The approach is applied to both three-dimensional and two-dimensional Fermi gases at zero temperature, using a contact interaction renormalized by the scattering length.

## Key numbers

- In 3D for the balanced case, the interaction energy reaches a maximum of 0.62 times the noninteracting energy density at gas parameter g = 0.88 (where g = k_F a).
- In 3D, the second-order ferromagnetic transition occurs at g = 0.79, and a first-order transition back to paramagnetism occurs at g = 0.96.
- In 3D, the pairing decay rate (pair formation rate) arises at g = 0.93 and rapidly reaches a maximum at g = 1.8.
- In 3D, the maximum critical temperature for ferromagnetism is estimated as T_c^max approximately 0.2 T_F.
- In 2D for the balanced case, the interaction energy reaches a maximum of 0.47 times the noninteracting energy density at g = 0.71 (where g = -1/ln(k_F a_2)).
- In 2D, the fully polarized state energy is 2 times the noninteracting energy density, much higher than the interaction energy maximum of 0.47.
- In 3D at unitarity, the Bertsch parameter for the normal phase is xi = 0.507, which agrees with the experimental result xi = 0.51(2).

## Context

This work extends the explanation by Shenoy and Ho (2011) that the interaction energy of the repulsive branch shows a maximum before reaching the resonance, but overcomes the difficulty of negative compressibility that limited the generalized Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach at low temperature. The results provide a theoretical framework for understanding experimental measurements of interaction energy in 6Li Fermi gases by Ketterle and co-workers (2008) and rf spectroscopy measurements in 2D Fermi gases. The approach enables quantitative predictions for itinerant ferromagnetism and suggests that equilibrium studies of the repulsive Fermi gas are possible only for gas parameter g less than 0.25 at temperatures around 0.3 T_F, consistent with recent experiments.
