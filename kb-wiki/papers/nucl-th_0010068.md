# Limits on the low-energy antinucleon annihilations from the Heisenberg principle

> Here a short synthesis is presented of the work, developed in the last two years by the Brescia Collaboration, on the phenomenology of antinucleon-nucleon and antinucleon-nucleus annihilation at small momenta (below 300 MeV/c in the laboratory), with special stress on the role of general principles.

# Limits on the low-energy antinucleon annihilations from the Heisenberg principle

## Key claim
The strong shadowing observed in low-energy antinucleon-nucleus annihilation cross sections (below 300 MeV/c laboratory momentum) can be explained as a consequence of the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, rather than requiring detailed nuclear-structure models. Specifically, when the annihilation reaction region has a thickness Δ of about 1 fm or less, the uncertainty principle forbids relative momenta much below 200 MeV/c, causing the cross section to drop below its unitarity limit at low momenta regardless of the intrinsic reaction strength.

## Method
The author calculates plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) estimates of antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections using measured antiproton-proton and antineutron-proton annihilation data, after subtracting Coulomb effects and renormalizing to data at approximately 350 MeV/c to remove the geometric eclipse effect. Optical potential fits are performed for antiproton-proton and antineutron-proton data using a Woods-Saxon shape, and the resulting scattering lengths are derived from the requirement that the projectile wavefunction is completely damped within a range Δ at the nuclear surface, matching logarithmic derivatives at the matching radius.

## Key numbers
- The shadowing effect is evident for antiproton momenta in the laboratory below 70 MeV/c, where antiproton-proton total annihilation cross sections are larger than the corresponding antiproton-deuteron and antiproton-⁴He ones.
- For antiproton-proton, the optical potential parameters are: imaginary depth V_I = −8000 MeV, real depth V_R = −46 MeV, imaginary radius R_I = 0.52 fm, real radius R_R = 1.89 fm, diffuseness a_I = a_R = 0.15 fm, and Coulomb radius R_c = 1.25 fm.
- For antineutron-proton, the fit is obtained either by increasing V_I to 14000 MeV or by increasing R_I to 0.75 fm (with Coulomb effects removed).
- The annihilation region thickness Δ is approximately 1 fm or smaller, and the condition k << 1/Δ defines the regime where the unitarity limit cannot be attained.
- The scattering length is estimated as Im(α) ≈ −Δ and Re(α) ≈ +R_nucleus in the limit k << 1/Δ.

## Context
This work extends the Brescia Collaboration's studies of strong shadowing in antiproton annihilations on light nuclei, previously reported in Bianconi (1998). The shadowing phenomenon was also studied in antiprotonic atoms by Wycech (various years), Batty (1990), and in pionic atoms by Friedman and Soff (1985). The paper contradicts the naive expectation that stronger absorption leads to larger cross sections at low energies, showing instead that increasing the imaginary optical potential strength can decrease the reaction rate. This enables a unified understanding of antineutron-nucleus and antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections, where the former should show no systematic increase with target mass number A, and the latter should depend on the target only through its electric charge.
