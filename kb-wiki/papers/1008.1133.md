# Light-Front Densities for Transversely Polarized Hadrons

> We discuss the recent interpretation of quark distribution functions in the plane transverse to the light-cone direction. Such a mapping is model independent and allows one to build multidimensional pictures of the hadron and to develop a semi-classical intuition of the quark dynamics. We comment briefly the results obtained from the Form Factors of the nucleon. A generalization to a target with arbitrary spin led to a set of preferred values for the electromagnetic coupling characterizing structureless particles. Generalized polarizabilities can also be interpreted in that frame as the distortion of the charge densities due to an external electromagnetic field. Finally, we present preliminary results for the Generalized Transverse-Momentum dependent Distributions which encode in principle the most complete information about quark distributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a model-independent probabilistic interpretation of quark distribution functions is available only in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) with the Drell-Yan-West condition \(q^+=0\), not in the Breit frame; this interpretation reveals that transverse polarization of a hadron produces multipole patterns in the transverse charge density which are directly connected to quark orbital angular momentum and the composite nature of the target.

## Method

The authors derive transverse charge densities from the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the \(J^+\) matrix element in the IMF, using phenomenological parameterizations of nucleon form factors. They extend the formalism to generalized polarizabilities (GPs) via virtual Compton scattering in the low-energy limit, and to generalized transverse-momentum dependent distributions (GTMDs) using the Light-Cone Constituent Quark Model and the Chiral Quark-Soliton Model restricted to the valence sector.

## Key numbers

- The dipole moment in natural units (\(e/2M\)) is given by \(F_2(0)\), i.e. the anomalous magnetic moment.
- For a spin-\(1/2\) hadron, the charge density for transverse polarization receives a dipole contribution proportional to \(\frac{Q^2}{2M} J_1(bQ) F_2(Q^2)\).
- The natural EM moments for a spin-\(j\) particle with charge \(Z=+1\) are tabulated up to spin 3; for spin-\(1/2\) the natural values are \(G_{E0}(0)=1\), \(G_{M1}(0)=1\), and all higher moments zero.
- The equi-amplitude curve for an unpolarized quark in an unpolarized nucleon is shown at \(|\vec k_\perp| = 0.45\) GeV.
- The radial distributions for up and down quarks are compared at \(|\vec k_\perp| = 0.55\) GeV, with the up quark distribution divided by two for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of form factors in impact parameter space pioneered by Soper (1976) and Burkardt (2000, 2002), and the transverse charge density formalism for polarized targets developed by Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2007). It also builds on the derivation of natural electromagnetic moments for arbitrary-spin particles by Lorcé (2009). The results enable a unified framework connecting form factors, generalized polarizabilities, and Wigner distributions, with the GTMDs providing the most complete information about quark distributions; the authors note that only three of the six independent GPs can be mapped to spatial distributions in the IMF.
