# GT strengths and electron-capture rates for pf-shell nuclei of relevance for late stellar evolution

> This paper presents a systematic evaluation of the ability of theoretical models to reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller transition strength distributions measured via (n,p)-type charge-exchange reactions at intermediate beam energies. The focus is on transitions from stable nuclei in the pf shell (45<A<64). The impact of deviations between experimental and theoretical Gamow-Teller strength distributions on derived stellar electron-capture rates at densities and temperatures of relevance for Type Ia and Type II supernovae is investigated. The theoretical models included in the study are based on the shell-model, using the KB3G and GXPF1a interactions, and quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) using ground-state deformation parameters and masses from the finite-range droplet model.

## Key claim

The paper systematically evaluates how well shell-model (KB3G and GXPF1a interactions) and QRPA calculations reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions for 13 stable pf-shell nuclei (45 ≤ A ≤ 64), finding that shell-model calculations qualitatively reproduce the data with average deviations in derived electron-capture rates below 47% (KB3G) or 31% (GXPF1a) at high densities, while QRPA calculations exhibit much larger deviations and overestimate total strengths.

## Method

Experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions from (n,p), (d,2He), (t,3He), and (p,n) charge-exchange reactions at intermediate beam energies, supplemented with beta-decay data where available, were compiled for 13 nuclei. These were compared with strength distributions calculated using shell-model codes with the KB3G and GXPF1a effective interactions (with a quenching factor of (0.74)^2 applied) and with QRPA calculations using ground-state deformation parameters and masses from the finite-range droplet model. Electron-capture rates at stellar densities of ρYe = 10^7 and 10^9 g/cm^3 and temperatures from 10^7 K to 10^11 K were derived from each distribution using the Fuller-Fowler-Newman formalism.

## Key numbers

- For stellar densities above 10^7 g/cm^3, ground-state electron-capture rates from shell-model calculations using KB3G (GXPF1a) deviate on average less than 47% (31%) from those derived from experimental data where low-lying daughter states are well established.
- The quenching factor applied to shell-model GT strengths was (0.74)^2 for nuclei in the pf shell.
- For 45Sc, the beta-decay B(GT) for the ground-state to ground-state transition was 4.9 × 10^-3; shell-model calculations predicted B(GT) of 0.00023 (GXPF1a) and 0.00019 (KB3G), about a factor of 10 smaller.
- For 45Sc, the QRPA calculation overestimated total GT strength by a factor of 3 and the ground-state transition was about thirty times stronger than the beta-decay measurement.
- For 48Ti, the QRPA strength distribution was divided by a factor of 6 for visualization; the calculation was dominated by a single transition at 5 MeV in clear contradiction to the data.
- The electron Fermi energy at T=0 is approximately 1.2 MeV at ρYe = 10^7 g/cm^3 and 5.2 MeV at ρYe = 10^9 g/cm^3.
- The Q-value for electron capture on 45Sc is -0.7677 MeV; for 48Ti it is -4.505 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of individual nuclei by providing a systematic evaluation across the pf shell, testing the shell-model calculations that underpin the weak-reaction rate libraries of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2001) against experimental charge-exchange data. The study enables quantitative uncertainty estimates for electron-capture rates used in simulations of Type Ia and Type II supernovae, which previously relied on rates from Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980-1985) based on independent particle models or from shell-model calculations without systematic validation. The results provide guidance for sensitivity studies in stellar evolution models and identify specific cases (such as 45Sc) where configuration mixing beyond the pf shell may be needed.
