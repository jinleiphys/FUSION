# Hard-Loop Dynamics of Non-Abelian Plasma Instabilities

> Non-Abelian plasma instabilities may be responsible for the fast apparent quark-gluon thermalization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions if their exponential growth is not hindered by nonlinearities. We study the real-time evolution of instabilities in an anisotropic non-Abelian plasma with an SU(2) gauge group in the hard-loop approximation. We find exponential growth of non-Abelian plasma instabilities both in the linear and in the strongly nonlinear regime, with only a brief phase of subexponential behavior in between.

## Key claim
Non-Abelian plasma instabilities in an anisotropic SU(2) plasma continue to grow exponentially even in the strongly nonlinear regime, with only a brief subexponential phase in between, contradicting the possibility that intrinsic nonlinearities could halt growth before the instabilities become large enough to affect hard particle trajectories.

## Method
The authors discretize the hard-loop effective action for anisotropic plasmas using a finite set of auxiliary fields representing momentum directions on the unit sphere, then solve the resulting gauge-covariant Boltzmann-Vlasov equations on a 1+1-dimensional lattice with temporal axial gauge. They evolve small random seed chromoelectric fields and track the growth of induced currents, field commutators, and energy densities, using a leapfrog algorithm with 10,000 spatial sites and 100 auxiliary velocity directions.

## Key numbers
- For anisotropy parameter ξ=10, the maximum growth rate γ_* occurs at nonvanishing wave number k_*, with the growth rate vanishing at k=0; the toy model (Arnold, 2004) instead has maximum growth at k=0 with γ=μ.
- With N=100 velocity directions (20×5 in z and φ), the asymptotic mass m_∞ for stable modes with |k|≫μ is reproduced with an error of only 0.017% for ξ=10.
- The simulation uses lattice spacing am_∞=0.028, physical size L≈280 m_∞^{-1}, time steps ε/a=1/100, and initial seed chromoelectric field root-mean-square amplitude 0.012 m_∞^2/g.
- Total energy is conserved within less than 1% even when soft-field energy grows by more than a factor of 10^6.
- The Abelianization correlation length ξ_A remains comparable to the scale of maximal growth λ_*=2π/k_*≈7 m_∞^{-1}, rather than growing to lattice size as in the toy model of Arnold (2004).
- The dominant energy contribution is in transverse magnetic fields, growing roughly at the maximum rate γ_* both in linear and nonlinear regimes, with a transitory slowdown of about Δt∼γ_*^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends the static linear approximation of Arnold (2004) by incorporating the full frequency dependence and dynamical nonlinearity of the hard-loop effective action derived by Mrowczynski (2004) for anisotropic momentum distributions. The results confirm that non-Abelian plasma instabilities do not saturate before reaching amplitudes that can affect hard particle trajectories, supporting the scenario where such instabilities drive fast thermalization in heavy-ion collisions faster than perturbative scattering processes (Baier, 2000; Molnar, 2001; Heinz, 2004; Shuryak, 2004). The next step would be to extend simulations to full 3+1 dimensions and to study the breakdown of the hard-loop approximation when energy transfer from hard to soft modes becomes comparable to the initial hard-particle energy.
