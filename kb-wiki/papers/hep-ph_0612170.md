# Cherenkov gluons at RHIC and LHC

> The coherent hadron production analogous to Cherenkov radiation of photons gives rise to the ring-like events. Being projected on the ring diameter they produce the two-bump structure recently observed for the away-side jets at RHIC. The position of the peaks and their height determine such properties of the hadronic medium as its nuclear index of refraction, the parton density, the free path length and the energy loss of Cherenkov gluons. Beside comparatively low energy gluons observed at RHIC, there could be high energy gluons at LHC, related to the high energy region of positive real part of the forward scattering amplitude and possessing different characteristics.

# Cherenkov gluons at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The two-bump structure observed in azimuthal angle distributions of away-side jets at RHIC can be explained as a one-dimensional projection of ring-like events produced by coherent Cherenkov-like gluon emission, provided the nuclear index of refraction exceeds 1; the interpretation depends on the specific energy region where the real part of the forward scattering amplitude is positive, which occurs within the left wings of resonances at low energies and above a threshold of 70–100 GeV in the target rest system at high energies.

## Method

The author derives the nuclear index of refraction from the forward scattering amplitude using the optical theorem and Breit-Wigner resonance shapes, relating it to the cone angle of Cherenkov gluon emission via cos θ = 1/(βn). The index of refraction is extracted from the observed angular separation between peaks in RHIC data, and the parton density, energy loss, and free path length are estimated from the index using both gas-phase (Clausius-Mossotti-like) and liquid-phase formulas. Predictions are made for ring-like pseudorapidity distributions and shifted resonance masses in the ring, as well as for high-energy gluon emission at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear index of refraction: n = 3, extracted from the distance between peaks in RHIC away-side jet data.
- Parton density: approximately 20 partons within the volume of a single nucleon, estimated from the index of refraction using the Breit-Wigner resonance formula.
- Energy loss of Cherenkov gluons: dE/dx ≈ 1 GeV/Fm.
- Free path length of Cherenkov gluons: R_f ≈ 7 Fm.
- Threshold energy for positive real part of forward scattering amplitude: E_th = 70–100 GeV in the target rest system.
- Parameter a in the high-energy refractivity formula: a ≈ 2 × 10⁻³ GeV.
- Predicted cone angles in LHC c.m.s.: 60°–70°.
- Shifted ρ-meson mass peak: slightly below m_ρ − Γ_ρ/2 = 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of Cherenkov gluon emission in hadronic collisions (Dremin, 1980s) to explain the two-bump structure observed by PHENIX and STAR at RHIC. It contradicts interpretations that do not invoke coherent collective effects, and it connects to cosmic-ray observations of ring-like events at 10¹⁶ eV. The results enable experimental searches at LHC for high-energy Cherenkov gluons using pseudorapidity distributions in nontrigger events, and suggest measurements of shifted resonance masses in the ring to confirm the mechanism. The finite-size effects of the nuclear medium were considered in earlier work (Dremin, 2005), and the influence of medium motion on cone angles was treated by Sinyukov and Shapoval (2005).
