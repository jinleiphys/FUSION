# Cumulants and Correlation Functions of Net-proton, Proton and Antiproton Multiplicity Distributions in Au+Au Collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We report a systematic measurement of cumulants, $C_{n}$, for net-proton, proton and antiproton multiplicity distributions, and correlation functions, $κ_n$, for proton and antiproton multiplicity distributions up to the fourth order in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4 and 200 GeV. The $C_{n}$ and $κ_n$ are presented as a function of collision energy, centrality and kinematic acceptance in rapidity, $y$, and transverse momentum, $p_{T}$. The data were taken during the first phase of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program (2010 -- 2017) at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) facility. The measurements are carried out at midrapidity ($|y| <$ 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 $<$ $p_{\rm T}$ $<$ 2.0 GeV/$c$, using the STAR detector at RHIC. We observe a non-monotonic energy dependence ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 7.7 -- 62.4 GeV) of the net-proton $C_{4}$/$C_{2}$ with the significance of 3.1$σ$ for the 0-5\% central Au+Au collisions. This is consistent with the expectations of critical fluctuations in a QCD-inspired model. Thermal and transport model calculations show a monotonic variation with $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$. For the multiparticle correlation functions, we observe significant negative values for a two-particle correlation function, $κ_2$, of protons and antiprotons, which are mainly due to the effects of baryon number conservation. Furthermore, it is found that the four-particle correlation function, $κ_4$, of protons plays a role in determining the energy dependence of proton $C_4/C_1$ below 19.6 GeV, which cannot be understood by the effect of baryon number conservation.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports a non-monotonic energy dependence of the net-proton cumulant ratio C4/C2 in central Au+Au collisions, with a significance of 3.1 sigma, which is consistent with expectations from a QCD-inspired model of critical fluctuations; however, the authors note that thermal and transport model calculations show a monotonic variation with collision energy, meaning the observed non-monotonic behavior is not uniquely explained by critical phenomena and could be influenced by other physics.

## Method

The STAR detector at RHIC measured cumulants C_n (up to fourth order) of net-proton, proton, and antiproton multiplicity distributions, as well as correlation functions kappa_n for protons and antiprotons, in Au+Au collisions at nine beam energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV. The analysis used data from the first phase of the Beam Energy Scan program (2010-2017), with particles selected at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c. The cumulants and correlation functions were studied as functions of collision energy, centrality, and kinematic acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Net-proton C4/C2 shows a non-monotonic energy dependence across sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 62.4 GeV with a significance of 3.1 sigma for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions.
- Measurements were performed at nine collision energies: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV.
- The kinematic acceptance was |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c.
- The two-particle correlation function kappa_2 for protons and antiprotons shows significant negative values, attributed mainly to baryon number conservation.
- The four-particle correlation function kappa_4 of protons influences the energy dependence of proton C4/C1 below 19.6 GeV, an effect not explained by baryon number conservation alone.

## Context

This work extends the STAR Collaboration's earlier measurements of net-proton cumulants from the Beam Energy Scan program, providing a systematic study across a wider range of energies and including correlation functions for the first time. The observed non-monotonic behavior of net-proton C4/C2 is compared to predictions from a QCD-inspired model (which can produce such a signal near a critical point) and to thermal and transport model calculations (which show monotonic behavior). The results motivate further theoretical work to disentangle critical fluctuation signals from conservation-law effects, and provide input for the design of the second phase of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES-II) to search for the QCD critical point.
