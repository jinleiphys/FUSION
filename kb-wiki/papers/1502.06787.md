# Nuclear Lattice Simulations using Symmetry-Sign Extrapolation

> Projection Monte Carlo calculations of lattice Chiral Effective Field Theory suffer from sign oscillations to a varying degree dependent on the number of protons and neutrons. Hence, such studies have hitherto been concentrated on nuclei with equal numbers of protons and neutrons, and especially on the alpha nuclei where the sign oscillations are smallest. Here, we introduce the "symmetry-sign extrapolation" method, which allows us to use the approximate Wigner SU(4) symmetry of the nuclear interaction to systematically extend the Projection Monte Carlo calculations to nuclear systems where the sign problem is severe. We benchmark this method by calculating the ground-state energies of the $^{12}$C, $^6$He and $^6$Be nuclei, and discuss its potential for studies of neutron-rich halo nuclei and asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The symmetry-sign extrapolation method uses the approximate Wigner SU(4) symmetry of the nuclear interaction to systematically extend Projection Monte Carlo calculations to nuclear systems where the sign problem is severe, with the caveat that the extrapolation remains robust only as long as PMC calculations can be carried out for the dial parameter \(d_h > 0.75\).

## Method
The authors define an interpolating Hamiltonian \(H = d_h H_{LO} + (1-d_h) H_4\), where \(H_{LO}\) is the full leading-order lattice Chiral Effective Field Theory Hamiltonian and \(H_4\) is an SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonian free of sign oscillations. Projection Monte Carlo calculations are performed for values \(0 \leq d_h \leq 1\), and the physical results at \(d_h = 1\) are obtained by a global fit of the form \(X(d_h, C_4, n) = X_0 + X_0^{SU(4)}(1-d_h) + \sum_{j=1}^n X_j^{SU(4)} \sin(j\pi d_h)\), where \(C_4\) is the coupling constant of the SU(4) Hamiltonian and the parameter \(X_0\) is constrained to be independent of \(C_4\). For \(^{12}\)C, the extrapolation in \(d_h\) is performed before the Euclidean time extrapolation; for \(^6\)He and \(^6\)Be, the Euclidean time extrapolation is performed first for each pair of \(C_4\) and \(d_h\), followed by the \(d_h \to 1\) extrapolation.

## Key numbers
- \(^{12}\)C LO ground state energy contribution: \(-96.85(14)\) MeV (with SSE data included), compared to \(-96.92(16)\) MeV from previous work (Lahde 2013).
- \(^{12}\)C NLO contribution: \(10.47(3)\) MeV; EMIB contribution: \(7.76(1)\) MeV; 3NF contribution: \(-14.56(4)\) MeV (with SSE data included).
- \(^6\)He LO ground state energy contribution at \(d_h = 1\): \(-27.49(7)\) MeV; with SSE extrapolation using \(d_h \leq 0.95\): \(-27.54(4)\) MeV.
- \(^6\)He NLO contribution at \(d_h = 1\): \(2.61(4)\) MeV; EMIB contribution for \(^6\)He at \(d_h = 1\): \(1.021(6)\) MeV; EMIB contribution for \(^6\)Be at \(d_h = 1\): \(2.65(1)\) MeV; 3NF contribution at \(d_h = 1\): \(-3.77(3)\) MeV.
- SU(4) coupling constants used for \(^{12}\)C: \(C_4 = -3.2 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-3.4 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-3.8 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-4.2 \times 10^{-5}\), and \(-4.8 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).
- SU(4) coupling constants used for \(A=6\) system: \(C_4 = -3.6 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-4.2 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-4.4 \times 10^{-5}\), \(-4.8 \times 10^{-5}\), and \(-5.4 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context
This work extends lattice Chiral Effective Field Theory calculations beyond the "alpha nuclei" (nuclei with equal proton and neutron numbers and A a multiple of 4) that had been the focus of prior studies by Epelbaum (2012) and Lahde (2013, 2014). The method is analogous to extrapolation techniques used in Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations by Alhassid (1993) and Koonin (1996), but here the sign problem is mitigated by exploiting the approximate Wigner SU(4) symmetry of nuclear interactions, which was previously noted in the context of large-\(N_C\) QCD by Kaplan (1995) and large scattering lengths by Mehen (1999). The results enable future studies of neutron-rich halo nuclei and asymmetric nuclear matter where sign oscillations are more severe.
