# Studying of final states in p-Au and p-Pb collisions

> In the framework of PYTHIA8.2 program we considered p-Pb and p-Au heavy ion collisions at the energy of 5.02 TeV and 8 TeV. The advantage of this program is in the combining of several nucleon-nucleon collisions into one heavy ion collision, based on phenomenological treatment of a hadron as a vortex line in a color superconducting medium, the consistent treatment of the central rapidity region with improvements of Glauber-like model where diffractive excitation processes are taken into account. We have considered the influence of impact parameter correlations on the production cross sections of p-Pb and p-Au processes to estimate the influence of hard and soft subprocesses on basic hadronic final-state properties in proton-ion collisions. Using these characteristics based on semi-hard multiparton interaction model we received the transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of K-meson and Lambda baryon at the energy of 5.02 TeV and 8.14 TeV.

## Key claim

Using the PYTHIA8.3 program with the Angantyr model for heavy ions, the authors show that the transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of K-mesons and Lambda baryons in p-Pb and p-Au collisions at 5.02 TeV and 8.14 TeV exhibit the same qualitative behavior regardless of collision energy, with the number of events being the main difference; the study also finds that including initial-state radiation (ISR) and final-state radiation (FSR) increases the production cross section for p-Pb collisions.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA8.3 event generator with the Angantyr model for heavy-ion collisions, which combines multiple nucleon-nucleon collisions into a single heavy-ion collision using a Glauber-like model with Gribov diffractive corrections. They simulated p-Pb and p-Au collisions at center-of-mass energies of 5.02 TeV and 8.14 TeV, incorporating multiparton interactions (MPI), initial-state radiation (ISR), final-state radiation (FSR), and the Lund string model for hadronization. The transverse momentum and rapidity distributions for K-mesons and Lambda baryons were calculated for different impact parameters (b = 0 and b = 0.5), and production cross sections for various partonic subprocesses were tabulated for p-Pb and pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- p-Pb collisions at 8 TeV with ISR and FSR included: total production cross section = 7.206e+01 mb (estimated uncertainty 1.724e-02 mb)
- p-Pb collisions at 8 TeV without ISR and FSR: total production cross section = 7.147e+01 mb (estimated uncertainty 1.725e-02 mb)
- pp collisions at 8 TeV: total production cross section = 9.336e+01 mb (estimated uncertainty 3.086e-02 mb), dominated by non-diffractive processes at 5.194e+01 mb
- pp collisions at 13 TeV: total production cross section = 1.011e+02 mb (estimated uncertainty 4.977e-02 mb), with non-diffractive processes at 5.642e+01 mb
- The pp total cross section formula: sigma_TOT^pp(s) = (21.70 s^0.0808 + 56.08 s^-0.4525) mb, with s in units of GeV^2
- The elastic slope parameter: B_EL^pp(s) = 5 + 4 s^0.0808
- The central-diffractive cross section is defined at a reference energy sqrt(s_ref) = 2 TeV with sqrt(s_0) = 1 TeV
- The PYTHIA strong coupling constant: alpha_s(M_Z) ~ 0.139

## Context

This work extends the application of the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model (introduced by Bierlich et al.) to p-Au collisions, complementing existing studies of p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The calculations incorporate the Lund string model (Andersson et al., 1983) for hadronization and the Glauber model with Gribov corrections for nuclear interactions, building on the framework of multiparton interactions (Sjostrand and van Zijl, 1987). The results enable comparisons between p-Pb and p-Au systems and provide baseline predictions for future experimental measurements of strangeness production and collective effects in small collision systems at the LHC.
