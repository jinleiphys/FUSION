# AdS/CFT applications to relativistic heavy ion collisions: a brief review

> We review some of the recent progress in our understanding of the physics of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions due to applications of AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

AdS/CFT correspondence provides a powerful tool for studying strongly-coupled aspects of heavy ion collisions, but the results should be taken as qualitative at best due to the many differences between QCD and the N=4 super Yang-Mills theory used in the duality, including the fact that QCD is confining while N=4 SYM is not, and QCD coupling runs while it is constant in the conformal N=4 SYM theory.

## Method

The paper reviews how the AdS/CFT correspondence maps strongly-coupled N=4 SYM plasma to classical supergravity on AdS5 x S5 space, where a thermal medium in the gauge theory is dual to an AdS Schwarzschild black hole in the bulk. For heavy ion collisions, the colliding nuclei are modeled as Lorentz-contracted thin sheets of matter represented by gravity shock waves in AdS5, and the resulting metric in the forward light-cone is obtained either perturbatively or numerically by solving Einstein equations. The holographic renormalization prescription extracts the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor from the near-boundary expansion of the bulk metric.

## Key numbers

- Entropy of strongly-coupled N=4 SYM plasma: S = (pi^2/2) N_c^2 T^3 V_3d, which is 3/4 of the Stefan-Boltzmann ideal gas entropy S_SB = (2 pi^2/3) N_c^2 T^3 V_3d
- Energy-momentum tensor of static supersymmetric plasma: <T_mu_nu> = (pi^2/8) N_c^2 T^4 diag{3,1,1,1}
- Thermalization proper time from trapped surface analysis: tau_th ~ 1/(mu_1 mu_2)^(1/6), which for RHIC kinematics gives approximately 0.1 fm/c
- Numerical simulations give 0.35 fm/c as the time interval from when Gaussian shocks start to overlap to the onset of viscous hydrodynamics in RHIC kinematics, or about 0.17 fm/c when measured from tau=0
- Produced entropy per unit transverse area for delta-function shock waves: S/A_perp = (N_c^2/(2 pi^2)) (2 mu_1 mu_2)^(1/3)
- Hadron multiplicity prediction from AdS/CFT: dN/d_eta ~ s^(1/3), while experimental data gives dN/d_eta ~ s^(0.15) (Aamodt 2010)
- Lattice QCD entropy density above T_c: S/S_SB = 80-85% (Aoki 2005, Bazavov 2009)

## Context

This review extends the application of the AdS/CFT correspondence, originally formulated by Maldacena (1997), Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998), and Witten (1998), to the phenomenology of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The work builds on the success of ideal hydrodynamics in describing the produced medium (Kolb and Heinz 2003, Teaney 2001) and the need to understand transport coefficients like shear viscosity. The trapped surface analysis follows the work of Penrose and Eardley and Giddings (2002), with specific applications to AdS5 by Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom (2008) and Kovchegov, Lin, and others (2009). The Bjorken hydrodynamics solution derived from AdS/CFT by Janik and Peschanski (2005) enables further studies of viscous corrections and thermalization timescales, though the short thermalization times predicted (0.1 fm/c) disagree with phenomenological hydrodynamic simulations that use 1-2 fm/c (Teaney 2003, Romatschke 2007, Song 2010).
