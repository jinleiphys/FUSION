# Breakdown of N = 8 magic number near the neutron drip line from parallel momentum distribution analyses

> Using two theoretical models (the post form finite range distorted wave Born approximation and the adiabatic model) we calculate the parallel momentum distribution of the charged core in the Coulomb breakup of Be isotopes on a heavy target at 100 MeV/u. We show that the full width at half maxima of the parallel momentum distribution can be used to study the breakdown of N = 8 magic number away from the valley of stability.

## Key claim

The full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the parallel momentum distribution of the charged core in Coulomb breakup can be used to study the breakdown of the N = 8 magic number near the neutron drip line, but the authors show that for Be isotopes the largest FWHM is found for N = 6 (¹⁰Be) rather than for N = 8 (¹²Be), suggesting that N = 6 may be a magic number instead.

## Method

The authors used two theoretical models, the post form finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) and the adiabatic model (AD), to calculate the parallel momentum distribution of the charged core in the Coulomb breakup of ⁹Be, ¹⁰Be, ¹¹Be, and ¹²Be projectiles on a gold target at a beam energy of 100 MeV/u. Both models are fully quantum mechanical and include initial and final state Coulomb interactions to all orders. The ground state wave functions of the projectiles were obtained by solving the radial Schrödinger equation with a Woods-Saxon potential, with depth adjusted to reproduce the one-neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

- FWHM for ¹¹Be (N = 7) breakup: 43.23 MeV/c (FRDWBA) and 43.71 MeV/c (AD), comparing well with the experimental value of 43.6 ± 1.1 MeV/c from Orr (1995) at 63 MeV/u.
- FWHM for ¹²Be (N = 8) breakup: 88.93 MeV/c (FRDWBA) and 89.73 MeV/c (AD), comparing well with the experimental value of 89 MeV/c from Navin (2000) at 78 MeV/u on a light target.
- FWHM for ¹⁰Be (N = 6) breakup: 191.13 MeV/c (FRDWBA) and 170.30 MeV/c (AD), the highest among the isotopes studied.
- FWHM for ⁹Be (N = 5) breakup: 112.27 MeV/c (FRDWBA) and 113.87 MeV/c (AD).
- When mixing s-state and p-state configurations in ¹²Be with spectroscopic factors 0.42 and 0.37 respectively, the FWHM becomes 100.50 MeV/c (FRDWBA) and 101.94 MeV/c (AD).

## Context

This work extends the use of parallel momentum distribution FWHM, previously established as a probe for halo nuclei (Orr 1995, Orr 1992), to study shell magicity in neutron-rich Be isotopes. The finding that ¹²Be (N = 8) does not show the largest FWHM contradicts the expectation that a magic-number nucleus should be more tightly bound and thus have a broader distribution than its neighbors. The result supports earlier suggestions of N = 8 shell melting from studies of ¹¹Be (Talmi 1993) and ¹²Be (Iwasaki 2000, Navin 2000, Shimoura 2000, Pain 2006, Meharchand 2006, Krieger 2012), and aligns with the proposal by Otsuka (2001) and Sorlin (2008) that N = 6 could be a new magic number. The authors argue that since PMD width is independent of target mass and nearly constant over a wide range of beam energies (50 MeV/u to 2 GeV/u), this method can be applied to predict breakdown and emergence of new magic numbers in exotic nuclei.
