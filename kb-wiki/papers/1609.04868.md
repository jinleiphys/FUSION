# Psi(2S) Production at the LHC

> We calculate the production of $ψ(2S)$ and the pertinent double ratio of its nuclear modification factor ($R_{\rm AA}$) over that of the $J/ψ$ in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Based on a transport model with temperature dependent reaction rates, a sequential regeneration pattern emerges: the larger $ψ(2S)$ width, relative to the $J/ψ$, around and below the critical temperature, implies that most of the $ψ(2S)$ states are regenerated later in the evolution of the fireball. This has noticeable consequences for the transverse-momentum ($p_T$) spectra of the regenerated charmonia. While the total yield of $ψ(2S)$ meson remains smaller than those of $J/ψ$'s, their harder $p_T$ spectra can produce a double ratio above unity for a $p_T>3$GeV cut, as applied by the CMS collaboration. A significant uncertainty in our calculations is associated with the values of the temperature where most of the $ψ(2S)$ regeneration occurs, the quantitative temperature dependence of its inelastic width.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a "sequential regeneration" mechanism, where ψ(2S) states are regenerated later in the fireball evolution than J/ψ mesons due to their larger inelastic width, can explain the CMS observation of a ψ(2S)/J/ψ double ratio above unity for pT > 3 GeV in central Pb-Pb collisions, though the maximal enhancement in central collisions is not fully reproduced and significant uncertainty remains in the temperature dependence of the ψ(2S) inelastic width.

## Method
The authors use a kinetic rate equation approach with temperature-dependent reaction rates to evolve charmonium yields (J/ψ, ψ(2S), χc(1P)) through an expanding fireball in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The inelastic reaction rates in the quark-gluon plasma are computed using quasifree dissociation, while hadronic dissociation rates use a meson exchange model with SUf(4) flavor symmetry. The pT spectra are decomposed into a primordial component calculated via a Boltzmann equation with escape effects, and a regeneration component evaluated from a blastwave description assuming thermal equilibrium, normalized to the yield from the rate equation.

## Key numbers
- The double ratio RAA(ψ(2S))/RAA(J/ψ) exceeds unity for pT > 3 GeV and forward rapidities (1.6 < |y| < 2.4) in central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, while it is below unity for pT > 6.5 GeV and mid-rapidity (|y| < 1.6)
- The average regeneration temperature for ψ(2S) is estimated at T̄reg ≈ 160–165 MeV, while for J/ψ it is T̄reg ≈ 180–200 MeV at 2.76 TeV
- For predictions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the charged particle multiplicity is increased by 22.5%, the charm cross section in pp is increased by ~40% (from 0.65 mb to 0.92 mb at mid-rapidity and 0.59 mb to 0.84 mb at forward rapidity), and the J/ψ blast-wave temperature range is T = 179–181 MeV
- A 10% shadowing is assumed in the pT-spectra according to EPS09 NLO

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous schematic-model study (Du and Rapp, 2015) using a more quantitative rate equation approach that intimately couples suppression and regeneration yields. It builds on prior transport model frameworks (Grandchamp et al., 2003; Zhao et al., 2010, 2011) and addresses the puzzling CMS observation (Khachatryan et al., 2014) of ψ(2S)/J/ψ enhancement in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The results enable predictions for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and provide a framework for understanding how sequential regeneration affects the pT dependence of charmonium nuclear modification factors.
