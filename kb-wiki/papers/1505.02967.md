# Scattering cluster wave functions on the lattice using the adiabatic projection method

> The adiabatic projection method is a general framework for studying scattering and reactions on the lattice. It provides a low-energy effective theory for clusters which becomes exact in the limit of large Euclidean projection time. Previous studies have used the adiabatic projection method to extract scattering phase shifts from finite periodic-box energy levels using Lüschers method. In this paper we demonstrate that scattering observables can be computed directly from asymptotic cluster wave functions. For a variety of examples in one and three spatial dimensions, we extract elastic phase shifts from asymptotic cluster standing waves corresponding to spherical wall boundary conditions. We find that this approach of extracting scattering wave functions from the adiabatic Hamiltonian to be less sensitive to small stochastic and systematic errors as compared with using periodic-box energy levels.

## Key claim

The adiabatic projection method can extract elastic scattering phase shifts directly from asymptotic cluster wave functions using spherical wall boundary conditions, and this wave-function-based approach is less sensitive to small stochastic and systematic errors than the standard approach of using periodic-box energy levels with Lüscher's method.

## Method

The authors construct dressed two-cluster states by Euclidean time evolution of initial cluster states using the microscopic Hamiltonian, then form an adiabatic Hamiltonian matrix via the inverse square root of the norm matrix. For the asymptotic region where clusters are widely separated, the adiabatic Hamiltonian coincides with an effective cluster Hamiltonian, and scattering phase shifts are extracted by fitting the asymptotic cluster wave functions to Bessel function expansions (one dimension) or spherical Bessel function expansions (three dimensions) with spherical hard wall boundary conditions imposed at a large radius. The method is tested on a one-dimensional particle-dimer system and a three-dimensional fermion-dimer system (analogous to neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin-quartet channel at leading order of pionless effective field theory).

## Key numbers

* Dimer energy set to -2.2246 MeV for the three-dimensional fermion-dimer system, with lattice spacing 1.97 fm (0.0100 MeV^-1)
* For the one-dimensional particle-dimer system, the dimer binding energy is -2.0000 MeV
* For the three-dimensional fermion-dimer system using Lüscher's method, finite-volume energy accuracy is 40-130 keV, but relative errors in resulting phase shifts become as large as 50%
* Spherical hard wall imposed at R_wall = 13.0 lattice units; the first zero of the 1s wave function is at 13.32, the second zero of the 2s wave function at 13.32, and the third zero of the 3s wave function at 13.31 lattice units
* For the first three lowest p-states, the zeros are all at 13.32 lattice units

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic projection method introduced by Rupak (2013), Pine (2013), and Elhatisari (2014), which previously used Lüscher's finite-volume method (Lüscher, 1986; Lüscher, 1990) to extract phase shifts from periodic-box energy levels. The authors demonstrate that the wave-function approach avoids the large errors that arise when small finite-volume energy shifts (as small as a few keV) must be extracted from binding energies of tens to hundreds of MeV, a problem that also plagues lattice QCD calculations. This enables more reliable extraction of low-energy nuclear scattering observables from lattice simulations using chiral effective field theory.
