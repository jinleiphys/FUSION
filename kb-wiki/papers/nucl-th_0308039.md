# DSEs, the pion, and related matters

> We recapitulate on aspects of Dyson-Schwinger equation studies relevant to pseudoscalar mesons: lattice confirmation of the DSE prediction that propagators are nonperturbatively dressed in the infrared; and exact results, e.g., in the chiral limit the leptonic decay constant vanishes for every pseudoscalar meson except the pion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) studies, combined with lattice-QCD results, provide a nonperturbative framework for understanding pseudoscalar mesons, with the key exact result that in the chiral limit the leptonic decay constant vanishes for every radial excitation of the pion except the ground-state pion itself.

## Method

The authors solve QCD's gap equation (the DSE for the dressed-quark self-energy) to obtain the nonperturbative running quark mass, then use a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to compute the masses and decay constants of the ground-state pion and its first radial excitation. The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved via an iterative eigenvalue method, projecting out the ground-state eigenvector to isolate the first excited state.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state pion: \( f_{\pi_0} = 0.092 \) GeV, \( m_{\pi_0} = 0.14 \) GeV, \( \rho_{\pi_0} = (0.81 \text{ GeV})^2 \), obtained at current-quark masses \( m_u(1\text{ GeV}) = m_d(1\text{ GeV}) = 5.5 \) MeV.
- First radial excitation mass estimate: \( m_{\pi_{n=1}} \approx 1.1 \) GeV.
- Upper bound on the first excited state's leptonic decay constant: \( f_{\pi_{n=1}} \leq 0.016 \, f_{\pi_{n=0}} = 1.5 \) MeV.
- Lattice-QCD current-quark masses used for comparison: \( m(\zeta) = 0.030, 0.055, 0.110 \) GeV at \( \zeta = 19 \) GeV, corresponding to lattice bare masses \( a m_{\text{lattice}} = 0.018, 0.036, 0.072 \).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach to pseudoscalar mesons reviewed in earlier studies (Roberts and collaborators, late 1990s–early 2000s) and confirms the nonperturbative dressing of propagators predicted by DSEs, now corroborated by quenched lattice-QCD results from Bowman et al. (2002). The exact result that the leptonic decay constant of excited pions vanishes in the chiral limit is a model-independent consequence of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, and the framework enables future ab initio calculations of pion form factors, transition form factors, and valence-quark distribution functions, as well as extension to baryon observables.
