# Observation of the Symmetry-Protected Signature of 3-body Interactions

> Identifying and characterizing multi-body interactions in quantum processes remains a significant challenge. This is partly because 2-body interactions can produce an arbitrary time evolution, a fundamental fact often called the universality of 2-local gates in the context of quantum computing. However, when an unknown Hamiltonian respects a U(1) symmetry such as charge or particle number conservation, N-body interactions exhibit a distinct symmetry-protected signature known as the N-body phase, which fewer-body interactions cannot mimic. We develop and demonstrate an efficient technique for the detection of 3-body interactions despite the presence of unknown 2-body interactions. This technique, which takes advantage of GHZ states for phase estimation, requires probing the unitary evolution and measuring its determinant in a small subspace that scales linearly with the system size, making it an efficient approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that 3-body interactions in U(1)-symmetric Hamiltonians produce a distinct, measurable phase shift that cannot be mimicked by any combination of 2-body interactions, but this detection is only possible when the Hamiltonian respects the U(1) symmetry; without this symmetry, the no-go theorem based on the universality of 2-qubit gates applies and 3-body interactions become undetectable.

## Method

The authors implement a trapped-ion quantum simulator with 5 qubits from a 7-ion chain of 171Yb+ hyperfine qubits, using single-qubit gates with 99.6% fidelity and two-qubit Mølmer-Sørensen gates with 98.7-99.3% fidelity. They measure the symmetry-protected phase Δ₃ by probing only the 0, 1, n-1, and n excitation subspaces (a total dimension of 2n+2) using GHZ states for phase estimation between the vacuum and fully occupied states, and Hadamard-based interferometry for the single-excitation and single-hole sectors, requiring O(n²) measurement settings.

## Key numbers

- Single-qubit gate fidelity: 99.6(2)% (not corrected for SPAM errors of about 0.3%)
- Two-qubit gate fidelity range: 98.7(2)% to 99.3(1)% for the qubit pairs used (not corrected for SPAM)
- The phase Δ₃ oscillates eight times faster than the 3-body interaction strength α₃, with Δ₃ = -8α₃ (mod 2π) for a single 3-body term
- For the additivity experiment with two 3-body gates, Δ₃ = -8(ζ₁ + ζ₂) (mod 2π)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Marvian (2022) on symmetry-protected signatures of multi-body interactions, which showed that U(1) symmetry allows detection of 3-body interactions despite the universality of 2-local gates established by Lloyd (1995) and DiVincenzo (1995). The method improves upon the original proposal by requiring only O(n²) measurements in a subspace growing linearly with system size, rather than probing the exponentially large full Hilbert space. This enables efficient Hamiltonian learning and quantum sensing of multi-body interactions in systems such as quantum chromodynamics or effective theories where such interactions emerge.
