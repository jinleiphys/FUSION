# Correlation effects on the static structure factor of a Bose gas

> A theoretical treatment of the static structure factor $S(k)$ of a Bose gas is attempted. The low order expansion theory is implemented for the construction of the two body density distribution, while various trial functions for the radial distribution function $g(r)$ are used. $g(r)$ introduces the atomic correlations and describes the departure from the noninteracting gas. The Bose gas is studied as inhomogeneous one, trapped in harmonic oscillator well, as well as homogeneous. A suitable parametrization of the various trial functions $g(r)$ exists which leads to satisfactory reproduction of the experimental values of $S(k)$, both in inhomogeneous case as well as in homogeneous one. The phonon range behavior of the calculated $S(k)$ is also addressed and discussed both in finite and infinite Bose gas.

# Correlation effects on the static structure factor of a Bose gas

## Key claim

The static structure factor $S(k)$ of a Bose gas can be reproduced satisfactorily by using simple trial forms for the radial distribution function $g(r)$ within the low-order expansion theory, but only if the trial function is chosen under certain restrictions, hard-sphere and soft-repulsive forms either give negligible correlations or produce unphysical negative and fluctuating values for $S(k)$.

## Method

The low-order expansion theory (LOA) was used to construct the two-body density distribution from the product of single-particle densities and a Jastrow correlation function $f(r)$, with $g(r)=f^2(r)$. For the inhomogeneous (trapped) case, the ground-state wave function of a harmonic oscillator was used; for the homogeneous case, a constant density $\rho$ was assumed. Various trial forms for $g(r)$ (Gaussian, $\sin^4(ar)/(ar)^4$, and a damped sine form) were fitted to experimental Bragg spectroscopy data from two experiments (Steinhauer et al. and Stamper-Kurn et al.) by adjusting the correlation parameters to minimize $\chi^2$.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit correlation parameter for the Gaussian $g(r)$ in the inhomogeneous case: $\beta = 5.3\ \mu\mathrm{m}^{-2}$ (fit to EXP-1 data).
- Best-fit correlation parameter for the $\sin^4(ar)/(ar)^4$ form in the inhomogeneous case: $a = 1.34\ \mu\mathrm{m}^{-1}$ (fit to EXP-1 data).
- In the homogeneous case, the Gaussian $g(r)$ reproduces experimental data when the density is approximately $\rho \approx 1\ \mu\mathrm{m}^{-3} = 10^{12}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-3}$.
- In the homogeneous case with the $\sin^4(ar)/(ar)^4$ form, the best-fit density is also $\rho \sim 1\ \mu\mathrm{m}^{-3}$.
- For the homogeneous case, the Lee et al. hard-sphere result $S(k)=k/\sqrt{k^2+16\pi\alpha\rho}$ is compared with the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the static structure factor in Bose gases beyond the ideal (noninteracting) gas model, which fails to reproduce experimental data. It builds on the low-order cluster expansion approach used by Mazzanti et al. and Jastrow, and on the local density approximation of Zambelli et al. The results show that a simple Gaussian $g(r)$ can fit the experimental data of Steinhauer (2002) and Stamper-Kurn (1999) for both inhomogeneous and homogeneous treatments, but that the phonon-regime behavior ($S(k) \sim k$ vs. $S(k) \sim k^2$) depends critically on the long-range tail of $g(r)$. The work enables systematic fitting of correlation parameters to experimental Bragg spectroscopy data and clarifies which trial forms for $g(r)$ are physically admissible.
