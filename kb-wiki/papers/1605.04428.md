# Hadronic resonances enhanced by thresholds

> We present a neat example of a meson--baryon system where the vicinity of two different thresholds enhances the binding of a hadronic resonance, a pentaquark. As a consequence the pattern of states may change when moving among different flavor sectors, what poses a warning on naive extrapolations to heavy flavor sectors based on systematic expansions. For this purpose we simultaneously analyze the $N\bar D$ and $NB$ two-hadron systems looking for possible bound states or resonances. When a resonance is controlled by a coupled-channel effect, going to a different flavor sector may enhance or diminish the binding. This effect may, for example, generate significant differences between the charmonium and bottomonium spectra above open-flavor thresholds or pentaquark states in the open-charm and open-bottom sectors.

# Hadronic resonances enhanced by thresholds

## Key claim

The binding of a hadronic resonance (pentaquark) in a meson–baryon system can be strongly enhanced when two different two-hadron thresholds lie close together, even when the diagonal interactions in each channel are not attractive; this threshold-vicinity effect means that the number and ordering of bound states can change unpredictably between the charm and bottom flavor sectors, contradicting naive extrapolations based on heavy quark symmetry or systematic expansions.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann–Schwinger equation for negative energies in the $N\bar D$ and $NB$ two-hadron systems, using the chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) to obtain the baryon–meson interactions. They search for bound states by evaluating the Fredholm determinant at zero energy; a vanishing determinant signals a bound state. The calculation includes coupled channels in the isospin–spin $(T,J)$ basis, with relative $S$ and $D$ waves coupled by the tensor force, and accounts for quark Pauli effects between identical light quarks in the two hadrons.

## Key numbers

- $(T,J)=(1,5/2)$ state in the $N\bar D$ system (unique channel $\Delta \bar D^*$): binding energy of **3.9 MeV**.
- $(T,J)=(1,5/2)$ state in the $NB$ system (unique channel $\Delta B^*$): binding energy of **5.3 MeV**.
- $(T,J)=(2,3/2)$ state in the $NB$ system (coupled channels $\Delta B - \Delta B^*$): binding energy of **42 MeV**.
- Mass difference $M(\Delta \bar D^*) - M(\Delta \bar D) =$ **141 MeV** in the charm sector.
- Mass difference $M(\Delta B^*) - M(\Delta B) =$ **45 MeV** in the bottom sector.
- For the $(T,J)=(2,3/2)$ $N\bar D$ system, a bound state appears when the artificial mass difference between $\bar D^*$ and $\bar D$ is reduced below approximately **120 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of the $N\bar D$ system (Caramés and Valcarce, 2012), which was motivated by the $\bar{\rm P}$ANDA experiment at FAIR. It also builds on a general mechanism proposed by Vijande et al. (2015) for exotic mesonic states, where two nearby thresholds and a strong coupling between them can produce binding even when diagonal interactions are weak. The paper provides a concrete baryon–meson example of this mechanism and warns that predictions for pentaquark states in the open-bottom sector cannot be naively extrapolated from the open-charm sector, in contrast to the expectations of models based on heavy quark symmetry (Tornow et al., 1992; Sun et al., 2011). The results are relevant for interpreting the LHCb pentaquark states $P_c(4380)^+$ and $P_c(4450)^+$ (Aaij et al., 2015) and for guiding searches at future facilities.
