# Cosmic rays, anti-helium, and an old navy spotlight

> Cosmic-ray anti-deuterium and anti-helium have long been suggested as probes of dark matter, as their secondary astrophysical production was thought extremely scarce. But how does one actually predict the secondary flux? Anti-nuclei are dominantly produced in pp collisions, where laboratory cross section data is lacking. We make a new attempt at tackling this problem by appealing to a scaling law of nuclear coalescence with the physical volume of the hadronic emission region. The same volume is probed by Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) two-particle correlations. We demonstrate the consistency of the scaling law with systems ranging from central and off-axis AA collisions to pA collisions, spanning 3 orders of magnitude in coalescence yield. Extending the volume scaling to the pp system, HBT data allows us to make a new estimate of coalescence, that we test against preliminary ALICE pp data. For anti-helium the resulting cross section is 1-2 orders of magnitude higher than earlier estimates. The astrophysical secondary flux of anti-helium could be within reach of a five-year exposure of AMS02.

# Cosmic rays, anti-helium, and an old navy spotlight

## Key claim

Secondary astrophysical anti-helium production in pp collisions may be 1-2 orders of magnitude higher than most earlier estimates, potentially making it detectable by AMS02 within a five-year exposure, but this result depends on a new scaling law relating nuclear coalescence to Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation volumes rather than direct cross-section measurements.

## Method

The authors derive anti-nucleus production cross sections by applying a scaling law that relates the coalescence factor B_A to the hadronic emission volume measured via HBT two-particle correlations, validated against data from AA, pA, and pp collisions spanning three orders of magnitude in coalescence yield. They then use this HBT-based coalescence estimate together with measured proton-proton antiproton production cross sections and cosmic-ray propagation calibrated from boron-to-carbon ratios to compute the secondary anti-helium and anti-deuterium fluxes.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence factor for pp → anti-helium: B_3^(pp) = (2-20) × 10^(-4) GeV^4, derived from HBT radius R = 0.5-1.2 fm
- Coalescence factor for pp → anti-deuterium: B_2^(pp) = (0.75-2.4) × 10^(-2) GeV^2 from direct ISR measurements
- HBT radius for pp collisions: R = 0.5-1.2 fm
- Anti-helium nucleus size: b_3 ≈ 1.75 fm; deuterium nucleus size: b_2 = 3.2 fm
- ALICE pp data at √s = 7 TeV: 20 anti-helium and 20 anti-triton events observed with luminosity L ≈ 2.2 nb^(-1) and pseudo-rapidity cut |η| < 0.9
- The predicted anti-helium cross section is 1-2 orders of magnitude higher than earlier estimates by Chardonnet (1997), Duperray (2005), Ibarra (2013), Cirelli (2014), and Herms (2016)
- Analysis restricted to rigidity R ≥ 5 GV, with solar modulation parameter Φ = 450 MV

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model of Scheibl and Heinz (1998) that connects nuclear coalescence to HBT correlation volumes, and tests it against data from AA and pA collisions. It contradicts earlier secondary anti-helium flux estimates by Chardonnet (1997), Duperray (2005), Ibarra (2013), Cirelli (2014), and Herms (2016), which assumed that coalescence parameters from pp → anti-deuterium could be directly translated to anti-helium and that the same coalescence momentum described both pA and pp collisions. The result enables a testable prediction for AMS02 and motivates dedicated analysis of the coalescence factor B_3^(pp) by the ALICE collaboration.
