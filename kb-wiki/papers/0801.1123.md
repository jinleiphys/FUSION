# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.II

> This report concerns the energy of a zero-temperature many-body system of spin 1/2 fermions in the unitary limit. In a previous report (arXiv:0705.0944) this energy was determined to be xi~0.24 in units of the free gas kinetic energy, appreciably lower than most reports giving ~0.45$. In our calculation the 2-body interaction satisfied exactly the unitary limit i.e. infinite scattering length and effective range r_0=0. In the present report results with r_0>0are shown. A strong dependence on the effective range is found. It is for example found that an increase to r_0=1 fm increases xi to ~ 0.4 close to other reports of xi in the unitary limit. It is concluded that because of the singular character of the unitary limit it is necessary to verify that the interaction actually satisfies unitarity. The calculations done here in a pp-ladder approximation show a resonance in the in-medium interaction close to (and in) the unitary limit. This was already found in the previous work.

# Spin 1/2 Fermions in the Unitary Limit.II

## Key claim

The energy of a zero-temperature many-body system of spin 1/2 fermions in the unitary limit depends strongly on the effective range of the interaction; the author's previous result of xi = 0.24 (in units of the free gas kinetic energy) applies only when the effective range is exactly zero, and increasing the effective range to 1 fm raises xi to approximately 0.4, close to other reports.

## Method

The author constructs separable interactions by inverse scattering from phase-shifts with various scattering lengths and effective ranges, including the unitary limit (infinite scattering length, effective range r_0 = 0). The in-medium effective interaction is obtained from a particle-particle ladder summation (Brueckner approximation) with kinetic energies only in the propagators (effective mass m* = 1), and the total energy per particle is calculated at zero temperature for a range of Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- xi = 0.24 for the unitary limit with effective range r_0 = 0 fm (analytic result, independent of density and cutoff)
- xi increases to approximately 0.4 when effective range r_0 = 1 fm (with infinite scattering length)
- xi changes from approximately 0.4 to 0.5 for r_0 = 2.8 fm (with infinite scattering length)
- The analytic unitary-limit result xi = 0.24 is independent of Fermi momentum k_f and of the cutoff Lambda
- The experimental ^1S_0 phase-shifts (case A) give results that agree practically exactly with the corresponding curve of Sinner et al. (2007)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation (Kohler, 2007) which found xi = 0.24 in the exact unitary limit, a value appreciably lower than most reports giving xi approximately 0.45. The paper shows that the discrepancy arises because other calculations use interactions with finite effective range rather than the exact unitary limit. The results are compared with Steele (2000), who used effective field theory power counting and obtained the same expression for the effective interaction but approximated it to yield xi = 4/9, and with Sinner et al. (2007), who included ring diagrams and used effective ranges r_0 greater than or equal to 2.54 fm. The author notes that Schafer et al. (2005) independently obtained the same value xi = 0.24 and also found a strong dependence on effective range. The work enables future finite-temperature Green's function calculations including hole-hole ladders and spectral broadening.
