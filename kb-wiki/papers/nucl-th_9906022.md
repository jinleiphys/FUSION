# Imaging proton sources and space-momentum correlations

> The reliable extraction of information from two-proton correlation functions is a long-standing problem in two-particle interferometry. Recently introduced imaging techniques give one the ability to reconstruct source functions from the correlation data in a model independent way. We explore the applicability of two-proton imaging to realistic sources with transverse space-momentum correlations. We find that the proton images are very sensitive to these correlations. We show that one can reliably reconstruct the source functions from the two-proton correlation functions, regardless of the degree of the space-momentum correlations.

# Imaging proton sources and space-momentum correlations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that proton imaging can reliably reconstruct source functions from two-proton correlation functions even in the presence of extreme transverse space-momentum correlations, but only under the condition that the source function's weak dependence on relative momentum q for q below the cutoff (approximately 60 MeV/c) can be neglected.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) model to generate freeze-out phase-space distributions for 4 GeV/A Au-Au collisions with impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm, selecting pairs in the central rapidity region |y| ≤ 0.3 with no transverse momentum cut. They artificially altered the alignment between transverse position r_T and transverse momentum p_T of each proton to create three cases (random, unmodified, and aligned), then constructed correlation functions using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz method and applied the imaging technique (optimized discretization) to reconstruct source functions, comparing them to sources computed directly from RQMD.

## Key numbers

- Peak height of correlation function: approximately 1.45 for unmodified RQMD, 2.2 for the aligned case, and 1.2 for the randomized case
- Cutoff momentum q_cut = 60 MeV/c used for selecting low relative momentum pairs in source construction
- Approximately 4000 simulated events of 4 GeV/A Au-Au reactions with impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm
- Central rapidity selection: |y| ≤ 0.3

## Context

This work extends the imaging techniques developed by Brown and Danielewicz (1997-1998) from static Gaussian and non-Gaussian sources to realistic sources with transverse space-momentum correlations, addressing limitations of earlier model-dependent approaches that assumed simple chaotic Gaussian sources (Koonin 1977; Lednicky 1982; Gong 1991). The results enable reliable extraction of source functions from experimental proton correlation data at SIS, AGS, SPS, and upcoming RHIC experiments, and open the door to three-dimensional proton imaging and studies of phase-space density and entropy changes with varying space-momentum correlations.
