# Manifestation of deformation and nonlocality in $α$ and cluster decay

> It is common to study the strong decay of a heavy nucleus as a tunneling phenomenon where the $α$ ($^4$He) or a light nuclear cluster tunnels through the Coulomb barrier formed by its interaction with the heavier daughter nucleus. The position and width of the Coulomb barrier are determined by the total interaction potential between the two daughter nuclei. We examine the effects of including nonlocality and deformation in the interaction potential by calculating the half-lives, $t_{1/2}$, and thereby phenomenological preformation factors of several nuclei which have the possibility of decaying by emitting either an $α$ or a light nuclear cluster. The effect of deformation manifests itself by a decrease in $t_{1/2}$ for all the decays studied. The effect of nonlocality is studied within two different models of the nuclear potential: the energy-independent but angular momentum ($l$) dependent Mumbai (M) potential and the energy-dependent Perey-Buck (PB) potential. The nonlocal nuclear interaction leads to a decrease in all half-lives studied. Though the decrease is larger due to the Perey-Buck potential, half-lives evaluated using the Mumbai potential show a strong sensitivity to the $l$ value in the decay. This feature of the $α$ and cluster decay half-lives can provide a complementary tool in addition to scattering data which are more commonly used to fix the parameters of a nonlocal potential in literature.

## Key claim

The inclusion of nonlocality and deformation in the interaction potential for alpha and cluster decay calculations systematically decreases the calculated half-lives, with the Perey-Buck nonlocal potential producing a larger decrease than the Mumbai potential, though the Mumbai potential shows a strong sensitivity to the angular momentum quantum number \(l\) in the decay.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha and cluster decay half-lives using the JWKB approximation for the decay width, with the nuclear potential obtained from a double-folding model using M3Y-Reid-type NN interactions. Nonlocality was incorporated via two models: the energy-dependent Perey-Buck (PB) potential and the energy-independent but angular-momentum-dependent Mumbai (M) potential, both applied to spherical nuclei; deformation was included by considering one nucleus as axially deformed with quadrupole and hexadecapole parameters, averaging the decay width over orientation angles.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{222}\)Ra alpha decay (\(Q = 6.678\) MeV), the nonlocality parameter \(b = 0.2160\) fm; for \(^{14}\)C cluster decay (\(Q = 33.049\) MeV), \(b = 0.0648\) fm.
- For \(^{228}\)Th alpha decay (\(Q = 5.520\) MeV), the daughter \(^{224}\)Ra has deformation parameters \(\beta_2^{(d)} = 0.164\) and \(\beta_4^{(d)} = 0.112\); for \(^{20}\)O cluster decay (\(Q = 44.723\) MeV), the cluster has \(\beta_2^{(c)} = 0.003\) and \(\beta_4^{(c)} = -0.108\).
- For \(^{232}\)U alpha decay (\(Q = 5.413\) MeV), the daughter \(^{228}\)Th has \(\beta_2^{(d)} = 0.182\) and \(\beta_4^{(d)} = 0.112\); for \(^{24}\)Ne cluster decay (\(Q = 62.310\) MeV), the cluster has \(\beta_2^{(c)} = -0.215\) and \(\beta_4^{(c)} = 0.155\).
- The nonlocality range parameter for nucleus-nucleus interactions is given by \(b = b_0 m_0 / \mu\) with \(b_0 = 0.85\) fm for the nucleon-nucleus interaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Johan 2019) on nonlocality in alpha decay of spherical nuclei to include both alpha and cluster decay modes, and adds the effect of deformation. The results provide a complementary tool to scattering data (commonly used to fix nonlocal potential parameters, e.g., Perey and Buck 1962, Chamon 2003) by using half-life measurements to constrain nonlocal interactions, and build on prior cluster decay studies (e.g., Ni and Ren 2010, Xu and Ren 2006) that treated deformation in the JWKB framework.
