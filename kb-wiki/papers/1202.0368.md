# Ab initio study of the photoabsorption of $^4$He

> There are some discrepancies in the low energy data on the photoabsorption cross section of $^4$He. We calculate the cross section with realistic nuclear forces and explicitly correlated Gaussian functions. Final state interactions and two- and three-body decay channels are taken into account. The cross section is evaluated in two methods: With the complex scaling method the total absorption cross section is obtained up to the rest energy of a pion, and with the microscopic $R$-matrix method both cross sections $^4$He($γ, p$)$^3$H and $^4$He($γ, n$)$^3$He are calculated below 40\,MeV. Both methods give virtually the same result. The cross section rises sharply from the $^3$H+$p$ threshold, reaching a giant resonance peak at 26--27\,MeV. Our calculation reproduces almost all the data above 30\,MeV. We stress the importance of $^3$H+$p$ and $^3$He+$n$ cluster configurations on the cross section as well as the effect of the one-pion exchange potential on the photonuclear sum rule.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the photoabsorption cross section of \(^4\)He using two independent methods (complex scaling and microscopic \(R\)-matrix) and find that both give virtually the same result, but the calculation reproduces "almost all the data above 30 MeV," implying that discrepancies with low-energy experimental data remain unresolved.

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross section was computed via the complex scaling method (CSM) up to the pion rest energy, while the partial cross sections for \(^4\)He(\(\gamma, p\))\(^3\)H and \(^4\)He(\(\gamma, n\))\(^3\)He were computed below 40 MeV using the microscopic \(R\)-matrix method (MRM). Both calculations used realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne \(v8'\) or G3RS) plus a phenomenological three-nucleon force, with wave functions expanded in explicitly correlated Gaussian basis functions that included \(^3\)H+\(p\), \(^3\)He+\(n\), and \(d\)+\(p\)+\(n\) cluster configurations to account for final-state interactions and decay channels.

## Key numbers

* The giant resonance peak of the photoabsorption cross section is found at 26–27 MeV.
* The ground-state energy of \(^4\)He calculated with AV8'+3NF is \(-28.43\) MeV (experimental: \(-28.296\) MeV); with G3RS+3NF it is \(-28.56\) MeV.
* The point-proton root-mean-square radius of \(^4\)He is 1.45 fm (AV8'+3NF) and 1.47 fm (G3RS+3NF), compared to the experimental value of 1.457(14) fm.
* The \(D\)-state probability \(P(2,2)\) in \(^4\)He is 14.07% (AV8'+3NF) and 11.42% (G3RS+3NF).
* The \(^3\)H+\(p\) threshold energy is used as the reference for the rise of the cross section; the cross section rises sharply from this threshold.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the \(^4\)He photoabsorption cross section that used the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with the Argonne \(v18\) potential (Gazit et al., Bacca et al.) and with chiral effective field theory interactions (Quaglioni et al.), all of which disagreed with low-energy data from Shima et al. (2005). The present study uses bare realistic interactions without effective-force transformations and provides a cross-check between CSM and MRM, aiming to resolve the experimental discrepancies. The results enable future studies of the role of the tensor force and the one-pion exchange potential in the photonuclear sum rule, and they support the importance of \(^3\)H+\(p\) and \(^3\)He+\(n\) cluster configurations for the cross section.
