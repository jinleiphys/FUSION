# Correlations between azimuthal asymmetries and multiplicity and mean transverse momentum in small collisions systems in the CGC

> Considering a dilute-dense situation suitable for pA collisions, we compute in the Color Glass Condensate the correlation between azimuthal asymmetries, specifically the squared second Fourier coefficient $v_2^2$, and the total multiplicity in the event. We also analyse the correlation between $v_2^2$ and the mean squared transverse momentum of particles in the event. In both cases, we find that the correlations are generally very small, consistent with the observations. We also note an interesting sharp change in the value of $v_2^2$ and its correlations as a function of the width of the transverse momentum bin, related with a change of the dominance of Bose and HBT quantum correlations.

## Key claim

The correlations between the squared second Fourier coefficient \(v_2^2\) and both total multiplicity and mean squared transverse momentum are generally very small in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework for pA collisions, consistent with experimental observations; however, these correlations exhibit a sharp change as a function of the transverse momentum bin width, related to a transition between dominance of Bose enhancement and HBT quantum correlations.

## Method

The authors compute single, double, and triple inclusive gluon production in a dilute-dense CGC setup suitable for proton-nucleus collisions, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the projectile ensemble with translational invariance and the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GW) model for target Wilson line averages. They work at leading order in the number of colors and at leading power of \(Q_s^2/k_i^2\) (valid for \(Q_s^2/k_i^2 \ll 1\)), taking only the leading correlated pieces in the two- and three-gluon inclusive cross sections. The correlators \(\langle v_2^2 N \rangle\) and \(\langle v_2^2 \overline{k^2} \rangle\) are computed analytically with integrals over transverse momentum bins of width \(\Delta\), using an infrared cutoff \(\lambda \sim 1/(Q_s^2 S_\perp) \sim 1/25\).

## Key numbers

* The HBT contribution to \(v_2^2\) dominates over the Bose contribution by a factor of approximately 50 when the two transverse momentum bins overlap.
* The bin width used for the calculation of \(v_2^2\) as a function of transverse momentum is \(\Delta = Q_s/2\).
* For the study of \(v_2^2\) as a function of bin width, the centers of the two bins are chosen at \(k = 4.5 Q_s\) and \(k' = 5 Q_s\).
* The results are multiplied by \((N_c^2 - 1) S_\perp Q_s^2\) (approximately 200 for pPb collisions) to exhibit universal features applicable to any target and projectile.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based formalism for multi-gluon correlations in pA collisions developed by Altinoluk et al. (2018) to compute correlations between azimuthal asymmetries and event-wide observables. It addresses the "small system problem" observed at the LHC and RHIC, where small collision systems (pp and pA) show ridge-like structures similar to those in heavy-ion collisions. The results are consistent with experimental measurements from ATLAS (2017) showing that the ridge in small systems is almost independent of multiplicity, and the correlation with mean transverse momentum has been proposed as a probe of initial geometry by Bozek (2016), Giacalone (2020), and Lim (2021). The work enables further theoretical investigation of whether initial-state CGC effects or final-state hydrodynamic explanations are responsible for the observed correlations.
