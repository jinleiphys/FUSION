# Lattice QCD constraints on the parton distribution functions of ${}^3\text{He}$

> The fraction of the longitudinal momentum of ${}^3\text{He}$ that is carried by the isovector combination of $u$ and $d$ quarks is determined using lattice QCD for the first time. The ratio of this combination to that in the constituent nucleons is found to be consistent with unity at the few-percent level from calculations with quark masses corresponding to $m_π\sim 800$ MeV, extrapolated to the physical quark masses. This constraint is consistent with, and significantly more precise than, determinations from global nuclear parton distribution function fits. Including the lattice QCD determination of the momentum fraction in the nNNPDF global fitting framework results in the uncertainty on the isovector momentum fraction ratio being reduced by a factor of 2.5, and thereby enables a more precise extraction of the $u$ and $d$ parton distributions in ${}^3\text{He}$.

## Key claim
The isovector quark momentum fraction ratio in ${}^3\text{He}$ relative to the proton is consistent with unity at the few-percent level, but this result is obtained at unphysical quark masses corresponding to $m_\pi \sim 800$ MeV and then extrapolated to the physical point under the assumption that the two-body low-energy constant is relatively insensitive to quark mass variation.

## Method
The isovector quark momentum fractions (first Mellin moments of the unpolarized isovector parton distribution functions) for the proton, diproton, and ${}^3\text{He}$ were computed using lattice QCD on a single ensemble of gauge configurations with $N_f = 3$ degenerate clover-improved Wilson fermions at $m_\pi = 806$ MeV on a $32^3 \times 48$ lattice with spacing $a \sim 0.145$ fm. Matrix elements of the twist-two operator $\mathcal{T} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(\mathcal{T}_{33} - \mathcal{T}_{44})$ were extracted using a background-field method with compound propagators, and the bare results were renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI'MOM scheme and matched to $\overline{\text{MS}}$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV.

## Key numbers
- Renormalized isovector momentum fraction for the proton at $m_\pi = 806$ MeV: $\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(p)} = 0.191(1)(9)$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV in $\overline{\text{MS}}$, where the first uncertainty is statistical plus systematic and the second is from renormalization.
- Renormalized isovector momentum fraction for ${}^3\text{He}$ at $m_\pi = 806$ MeV: $\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(^3\text{He})} = 0.066(1)(3)$ at $\mu = 2$ GeV.
- Ratio of isovector momentum fraction in ${}^3\text{He}$ to that in the proton at $m_\pi = 806$ MeV: $3 \langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(^3\text{He})}/\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(p)} = 1.028(15)$, with renormalization uncertainties canceling.
- Extrapolated ratio at physical quark masses: $3 \langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(^3\text{He})}/\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(p)}|_{\text{LQCD}} = 1.035(26)$.
- Ratio from nNNPDF2.0 global analysis alone: $3 \langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(^3\text{He})}/\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(p)}|_{\text{nNNPDF2.0}} = 1.007(63)$ at 68% confidence.
- Combined nNNPDF2.0 plus LQCD constraint: $3 \langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(^3\text{He})}/\langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{(p)}|_{\text{nNNPDF2.0+LQCD}} = 1.028(25)$ at 68% confidence, reducing the uncertainty by a factor of 2.5.

## Context
This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of partonic structure in a light nucleus, extending prior lattice studies of nucleon Mellin moments (Lin 2017) and gluon momentum fractions in nuclei (Winter 2017) to the isovector quark sector of ${}^3\text{He}$. The results provide a direct QCD-based constraint on the flavor dependence of the EMC effect, complementing experimental constraints from deep-inelastic scattering and the nNNPDF2.0 global analysis (AbdulKhalek 2020). This enables more precise extraction of the $u$ and $d$ parton distributions in ${}^3\text{He}$ and opens the door to future lattice calculations at physical quark masses and for a wider range of nuclei.
