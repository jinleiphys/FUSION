# Non-Equilibrium Production of Photons via π^0\to 2γin DCC's

> We study production of photons via the non-equilibrium relaxation of a Disoriented Chiral Condensate with the chiral order parameter having a large initial amplitude along the π^0 direction. Assuming the validity of the low energy coupling of the neutral pion to photons via the U_A(1) anomalous vertex, we find that for large initial amplitudes along the π^0 direction, photon production is enhanced by parametric amplification. These processes are non-perturbative with a large contribution during the non-equilibrium stages of the evolution and result in a distinct distribution of the produced photons and a polarization asymmetry. For initial amplitudes of the π^0 component of the order parameter between 200-400 MeV, corresponding to energy densities between 1-12 GeV/fm^3 we find a peak in the photon distribution at energies between \approx 300 -600 MeV. We also find polarization asymmetries typically between 5-10%. We discuss the potential experimental impact of these results as well as the problems associated with its detection.

## Key claim

Photon production via parametric amplification from the non-equilibrium relaxation of a Disoriented Chiral Condensate (DCC) is enhanced when the chiral order parameter has a large initial amplitude along the π⁰ direction, assuming the validity of the low-energy anomalous vertex for π⁰ → 2γ in strongly out-of-equilibrium situations, an assumption the authors note may be modified above the critical temperature or in non-equilibrium settings.

## Method

The authors studied the linear sigma model with an O(4) symmetry, introducing the anomalous π⁰γγ interaction via the Wess-Zumino-Witten vertex. They used the large-N expansion to treat the non-equilibrium dynamics self-consistently, solving coupled equations for the order parameters φ(t) and ζ(t) (the σ and π⁰ expectation values), the pion mode functions, and the photon mode functions for circular polarizations. The system was evolved from a "quench" approximation with initial conditions corresponding either to local thermodynamic equilibrium above Tc or to a zero-temperature non-equilibrium state, with the π⁰ zero mode acting as a time-dependent "squeezing" term for the photon field.

## Key numbers

- Initial π⁰ component amplitudes between 200–400 MeV, corresponding to energy densities between 1–12 GeV/fm³.
- Peak in the photon distribution at energies between approximately 300–600 MeV.
- Polarization asymmetries typically between 5–10%.
- Linear sigma model parameters: Mσ ≈ 600 MeV, fπ ≈ 93 MeV, λ ≈ 4.5, h ≈ (120 MeV)³, Tc ≈ 200 MeV.
- Ultraviolet cutoff Λ ≈ 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of direct photon production from DCC relaxation by Boyanovsky et al. (1997) that focused on electromagnetic interactions of charged pions, and complements the suggestion by Minakata and Muller (1997) that strong electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions could induce a rotation of the chiral order parameter in the π⁰ direction. The mechanism of parametric amplification of pions during hadronization was conjectured by Mrowczynski (1993). The results provide a potential electromagnetic signature for DCC formation that could be searched for at RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX) and at CERN (WA98), though the authors note that the validity of the anomalous vertex in strongly out-of-equilibrium situations remains an open question requiring further study within non-equilibrium field theory.
