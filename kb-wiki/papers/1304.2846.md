# Semi-classical, microscopic approach to the liquid drop model - a possible way of the description of heavy ion reaction

> An isospin and spin dependent form of the equation of state for nuclear matter is presented. This form is used for the description of nucleon interaction in a new dynamic model. Preliminary calculations show that the new approach makes possible predicting the alpha structures appearing in the case of the ground state even-even nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a semi-classical, microscopic version of the liquid drop model (MLDM) in which the dynamics is governed by an isospin- and spin-dependent equation of state (EOS), and show that preliminary calculations can predict alpha cluster structures in ground-state even-even nuclei, though the results are described as "very preliminary" and based on a "roughly chosen" EOS.

## Method

The model represents each nucleon by a Gaussian wave packet with time-dependent position, momentum, and width parameters, and uses a third-degree polynomial form of the EOS that includes volume energy (from symmetric nuclear matter), symmetry energy (isospin-dependent), and spin symmetry energies (for neutron and proton spin polarization and mutual spin interaction). The total Hamiltonian is constructed as a sum of kinetic energy, volume energy (given by the integral of the EOS over the density distribution), surface energy (proportional to the variance of the energy density around each nucleon), and Coulomb energy, with the ground state found by minimizing the energy with respect to the wave packet positions and widths.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density assumed: ρ₀ = 0.159 nucl/fm³
- Incompressibility coefficient: K₀ = 300 MeV
- Volume energy coefficient (from fitting ⁴He and ¹²C): e₀₀ = -12.9 MeV
- Surface energy coefficient: s₀ = 0.09 fm²
- Isospin symmetry energy parameters: e_I0 = 30 MeV, L_I = 123 MeV, K_I = 500 MeV
- Spin symmetry energy for neutron (or proton) gas: e_ii0 = 65 MeV, L_ii = 300 MeV, K_ii = 300 MeV
- Mutual spin interaction parameters: e_ij0 = -1 MeV, L_ij = 20 MeV, K_ij = 300 MeV
- MLDM binding energy for ⁴He: -7.062 MeV (experimental: -7.074 MeV); RMS charge radius: 1.727 fm (experimental: 1.676 fm)
- MLDM binding energy for ¹²C: -7.719 MeV per nucleon (experimental: -7.68 MeV per nucleon); RMS charge radius: 2.466 fm (experimental: 2.47 fm)
- MLDM binding energy for ¹⁶O: -7.84 MeV per nucleon (experimental: -7.976 MeV per nucleon); RMS charge radius: 2.613 fm (experimental: 2.701 fm)
- MLDM binding energy for ⁴⁰Ca: -7.85 MeV per nucleon (experimental: -8.551 MeV per nucleon); RMS charge radius: 3.336 fm (experimental: 3.476 fm)
- Alpha-alpha bond distances centered around 3.23 fm up to 3.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop model approach of Blocki (1978) by incorporating a microscopic, density-functional description where the EOS replaces the usual potential parametrization used in Quantum Molecular Dynamics (Aich, 1991), Antisymmetric Molecular Dynamics (Ono, 1992), and Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (Feld, 1990). The model enables the prediction of alpha cluster structures in light even-even nuclei, reproducing the alpha-alpha bond counting scheme proposed by Bethe (1936) and Hafstad and Teller (1938), and provides a framework for future heavy-ion reaction studies and EOS parameter determination from a larger nuclear database.
