# Constraints on the Path-Length Dependence of Jet Quenching in Nuclear Collisions at RHIC and LHC

> Recent data on the high-pT pion nuclear modification factor, $R_{AA}(p_T)$, and its elliptic azimuthal asymmetry, $v_2(p_T)$, from RHIC/BNL and LHC/CERN are analyzed in terms of a wide class of jet-energy loss models coupled to different (2+1)d transverse plus Bjorken expanding hydrodynamic fields. We test the consistency of each model by demanding a simultaneous account of the azimuthal, the transverse momentum, and the centrality dependence of the data at both 0.2 and 2.76 ATeV energies. We find a rather broad class of jet-energy independent energy-loss models $dE/dx= κ(T) x^z T^{2+z} ζ_q$ that, when coupled to bulk constrained temperature fields T(x,t), can account for the current data at the $χ^2<2$ level with different temperature-dependent jet-medium couplings and path-length dependence exponents $0\le z \le 2$. We test the sensitivity of predictions to different skewed energy-loss fluctuations via a convenient scaling factor distributed in a finite range $0< ζ_q < 2+q$ with unit mean. While a previously proposed AdS/CFT jet-energy loss model with a temperature-independent jet-medium coupling as well as a near-$T_c$ dominated, pQCD-inspired energy-loss scenario are shown to be inconsistent with the LHC data, once the parameters are constrained by fitting to RHIC results, we find several new solutions with a temperature-dependent jet-medium coupling. We conclude that the current level of statistical and systematic uncertainties of the measured data does not allow a constraint on the path-length exponent z to a range narrower than [0-2].

# Constraints on the Path-Length Dependence of Jet Quenching in Nuclear Collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

A broad class of jet-energy independent energy-loss models with path-length dependence exponents in the range 0 ≤ z ≤ 2 can simultaneously describe RHIC and LHC data on the high-pT pion nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow at the χ²/d.o.f. < 2 level, provided the jet-medium coupling is allowed to depend on temperature. However, the current statistical and systematic uncertainties of the measured data do not allow constraining the path-length exponent z to a range narrower than [0-2].

## Method

The authors analyzed high-pT pion nuclear modification factor RAA(pT) and elliptic azimuthal asymmetry v2(pT) data from RHIC (200 AGeV) and LHC (2.76 ATeV) using a parametric class of jet-energy loss models dE/dx = κ(T) x^z T^(2+z) ζq coupled to three different (2+1)d transverse plus Bjorken expanding hydrodynamic temperature fields: ideal VISH2+1, viscous RL Hydro with η/s=0.08, and a v⊥=0.6 transverse blast wave model. They tested models with path-length exponents z = 0 (elastic pQCD), z = 1 (radiative pQCD), and z = 2 (AdS/CFT), including energy-loss fluctuations via a skewed distribution parameterized by q = -1, 0, 1, and requiring simultaneous description of azimuthal, transverse momentum, and centrality dependence at both collision energies.

## Key numbers

- The jet-medium coupling at LHC energies was reduced to approximately 40-50% of the RHIC value, with κLHC ≈ 0.5 κRHIC, to account for running-coupling effects
- For the QCDrad scenario at RHIC, the q=0 fluctuation case reproduced the nuclear modification factor in- and out-of-plane with χ²/d.o.f. ≤ 1.5 for a pT-range of 8.5 < pT < 13 GeV
- At RHIC, the reference point for fitting the jet-medium coupling was RAA(pT=7.5 GeV) = 0.2 in most central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV
- For LHC comparisons, two normalization choices were tested: RAA(pT=10 GeV) = 0.155 and RAA(pT=10 GeV) = 0.186, both within experimental error bars
- A modest azimuthal variation of 10-15% in the jet-medium coupling was sufficient to address the high-pT v2 problem at LHC energies
- The path-length exponent z could not be constrained to a range narrower than [0-2] given current data uncertainties

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Betz and Gyulassy (2012) and the JET Collaboration by including energy-loss fluctuations and testing temperature-dependent jet-medium couplings against both RHIC and LHC data simultaneously. The authors confirm earlier findings by Buzzatti (2012), Zakharov (2012), and Xu (2014) that the jet-medium coupling must be reduced from RHIC to LHC energies. They rule out conformal AdS/CFT models with temperature-independent coupling and near-Tc dominated pQCD-inspired models (Liao, 2008) when parameters are fixed at RHIC, while finding new solutions with temperature-dependent coupling. The work enables future discrimination of jet-energy loss mechanisms once experimental uncertainties are reduced, particularly through improved measurements of high-pT elliptic flow.
