# Analysis of clustering phenomena in ab initio approaches

> An approach for explicit consideration of cluster effects in nuclear systems and accurate ab initio calculations of cluster characteristics of nuclei is devised. The essential block of the approach is a construction of a basis which incorporates both conventional No-Core Shell Model wave functions and translationally-invariant wave functions of various cluster channels. The results of computations of the total binding energies of 8Be nucleus as well as the spectroscopic factors of cluster channels (amount of clustering) are presented. An unexpected fresh result of the rigorous study is that the contribution of "non-clustered" components of the basis to the total binding energy is great even in the typical cluster systems such as the discussed nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that even in a strongly clustered nucleus like 8Be, the contribution of "non-clustered" basis components to the total binding energy is large and dominates at higher truncation levels, despite the clustered components having a larger statistical weight in the wave function.

## Method

The authors construct a unified basis combining conventional No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) wave functions with translationally-invariant wave functions of various two-fragment cluster channels, using the technique of cluster coefficients to transform cluster wave functions into superpositions of Slater determinants. They then orthonormalize this combined basis and compute total binding energies and spectroscopic factors (amount of clustering) for the 8Be nucleus using the JISP16 and Daejeon16 NN-potentials, comparing pure NCSM, pure cluster, and hybrid bases at various truncation levels.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy of 8Be with Daejeon16 potential at N=12: 55.72 MeV (mod1, NCSM basis)
- Total binding energy of 8Be with Daejeon16 potential at N=12: not computed for mod4/mod5, but at N=10: 52.34 MeV (mod4) and 52.22 MeV (mod5)
- One-channel amount of clustering (ground-ground alpha channel) for 8Be with Daejeon16 at N=12: 0.875
- Three-channel amount of clustering for 8Be with Daejeon16 at N=12: 0.873
- One-channel amount of clustering at N=4: 0.068 (artefact of basis choice)
- Oscillator parameter used: hbar-omega = 22.5 MeV for JISP16, hbar-omega = 15.0 MeV for Daejeon16

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM and NCSMC approaches developed by Quaglioni and Navratil (2008-2009) and Baroni et al. (2013-2014) by focusing on bound and resonance states rather than reaction dynamics, and by using the cluster coefficient technique from Stetcu et al. (2007) and Tchuvil'sky (2012) for transforming cluster wave functions. The results contradict the expectation that a pure cluster basis would dominate the binding energy in a clustered system like 8Be, and they support the use of effective NN-potentials in cluster models such as AMD (Kanada-En'yo, 1995) and THSR (Tohsaki et al., 2001) to compensate for the missing non-clustered contributions.
