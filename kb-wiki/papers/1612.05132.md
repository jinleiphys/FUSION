# A shoving model for collectivity in hadronic collisions

> An extension of the rope hadronization model, which has previously provided good descriptions of hadrochemistry in high multiplicity pp collisions, is presented. The extension includes a dynamically generated transverse pressure, produced by the excess energy from overlapping strings. We find that this model can qualitatively reproduce soft features of Quark Gluon Plasma in small systems, such as higher $\langle p_\perp \rangle$ for heavier particles and long range azimuthal correlations forming a ridge. The effects are similar to those obtained from a hydrodynamic expansion, but without assuming a thermalized medium.

## Key claim

The authors show that a "shoving" mechanism, where overlapping strings in high-multiplicity pp collisions generate a transverse pressure from excess energy, can qualitatively reproduce flow-like features (higher mean transverse momentum for heavier particles and long-range azimuthal correlations) without assuming a thermalized medium or hydrodynamic expansion.

## Method

The authors extend the rope hadronization model in the DIPSY event generator by adding a spatio-temporal evolution where overlapping string segments in transverse space exert repulsive forces on each other. The repulsion is implemented by adding small transverse momentum kicks (via soft gluon insertions) to string segments in rapidity slices over multiple time steps, with the force per unit length derived from either a cylindrical overlap area (type I superconductor picture) or a Gaussian profile (type II superconductor picture). The resulting transverse boosts are then passed to the Pythia8 hadronization routine.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of enhanced rope tension to triplet string tension is given by: $\tilde{\kappa}/\kappa = \frac{1}{4}(p^2 + q^2 + pq + 3(p+q))$, where $\{p,q\}$ are SU(3) multiplet quantum numbers.
- The transverse momentum kick per time per string length is modeled as $dp_\perp/(dt\, dl) = (g\kappa)/(R\sqrt{2\pi})\exp(-d_\perp^2(t)/(2R^2))$, where $g$ is a free parameter of order unity and $R$ is a characteristic transverse radius.
- The width of the Gaussian $p_\perp$ distribution for string breaks scales as $\tilde{\sigma}_\perp = \sigma_\perp\sqrt{\tilde{\kappa}/\kappa}$.
- Results are shown for pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, with event bias selecting strings spanning $\Delta y > 8$ and no partons above $p_\perp=3$ GeV for correlation studies.
- Two-particle correlations are shown for particles with transverse momentum between 0.5 and 3 GeV, in the range $2<\Delta\eta<4$.

## Context

This work extends the rope hadronization model (Bierlich 2014), which previously described hadrochemistry in high-multiplicity pp collisions, by adding a shoving mechanism that generates flow-like effects. It builds on earlier analytic studies by Abramovsky (1988) and a recent Monte Carlo toy model by Altsybeev (2015) that used a type I superconductor picture of strings. The model provides a microscopic alternative to hydrodynamic descriptions (e.g., the core-corona model in EPOS by Werner 2007 and Pierog 2013) and to attempts to incorporate thermal exponential spectra into the Lund hadronization model (Fischer 2016). If successfully tuned, the model can be applied to pA and AA collisions in DIPSY and implemented in the developing heavy ion event generator based on Pythia8 (Bierlich 2016).
