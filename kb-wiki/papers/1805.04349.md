# Tetraneutron: Rigorous continuum calculation

> The four-neutron system is studied using exact continuum equations for transition operators and solving them in the momentum-space framework. A resonant behavior is found for strongly enhanced interaction but not a the physical strength, indicating the absence of an observable tetraneutron resonance, in contrast to a number of earlier works. As the transition operators acquire large values at low energies, it is conjectured that this behavior may explain peaks in many-body reactions even without a resonance.

# Tetraneutron: Rigorous continuum calculation

## Key claim

The four-neutron system shows a resonant behavior only when the neutron-neutron interaction is strongly enhanced beyond physical strength; at the physical interaction strength there is no observable tetraneutron resonance, though the transition operators acquire large values at low energies that may explain observed peaks in many-body reactions even without a true resonance.

## Method

The author solved exact Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using the complex-energy method with special integration weights to treat kernel singularities. Calculations were performed for the total angular momentum and parity J^Π = 0^+ state using three different neutron-neutron potential models: a similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved Argonne V18 potential, a chiral effective field theory potential at next-to-leading order (NLO), and the CD Bonn potential. To study the resonance trajectory, the higher-wave neutron-neutron potential was enhanced by a factor f > 1 while keeping the physical strength in the ¹S₀ wave to avoid bound dineutrons.

## Key numbers

- For the SRG potential with enhancement factor f = 5, the extracted resonance pole is at E_r - iΓ/2 ≈ (5.9 - 0.6i) MeV
- The four-neutron system becomes bound at f = 5.29
- For f < 4.3, the pole of the transition operators becomes too far from the real axis to be discernible from the nonresonant continuum
- The experimentally reported tetraneutron resonance from the double charge-exchange reaction ⁴He(⁸He,⁸Be) had energy E_r = 0.83 ± 0.65(stat) ± 1.25(syst) MeV and width Γ ≤ 2.6 MeV
- The imaginary part of the energy used in the complex-energy method ranged from 0.1 MeV to 1 MeV, requiring around 80 grid points for discretization of Jacobi momenta
- The squared matrix element multiplied by the phase-space factor k_z exhibits a sharp peak around 0.25 MeV and a broad peak around 4.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the rigorous continuum treatment previously applied to the three-neutron system by Deltuva (2018) and to four-nucleon reactions above the complete breakup threshold. It contradicts predictions of a tetraneutron resonance by Shirokov et al. (2016), Gandolfi et al. (2017), and Li et al. (2017), while agreeing with Lazauskas (2015) and Lazauskas (2018) who found no observable tetraneutron resonance. The author identifies a serious shortcoming in the earlier works: they applied the same enhancement factor to all neutron-neutron partial waves including the ¹S₀ wave, generating bound dineutrons and making the calculated "bound tetraneutron" states actually lie above the two-dineutron threshold, rendering extrapolation to physical strength meaningless. The calculation of half-shell matrix elements of four-neutron transition operators provides a first step toward understanding reactions such as ⁴He(⁸He,⁸Be) where a four-neutron subsystem appears in the final state.
