# QCD Phase Diagram: Phase Transition, Critical Point and Fluctuations

> Summary of the discussions on QCD Phase Diagram at 21st International Conference on Ultrarelativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions (QM2009), March 30 - 4 April, 2009, Knoxville, Tennessee, USA. New references added

## Key claim

The quark-hadron transition at zero baryon chemical potential is established as a cross-over, but the existence and location of a QCD critical point at finite baryon density remains ambiguous, with lattice calculations disagreeing on whether a critical point exists at all depending on the sign of the first Taylor expansion coefficient of the chiral critical surface.

## Method

The paper summarizes theoretical lattice QCD results (using staggered and Wilson fermion actions, canonical ensemble methods, and reweighting techniques) and experimental heavy-ion collision results (from RHIC and SPS) presented at the Quark Matter 2009 conference. It reviews calculations of the transition temperature, equation of state, order of the phase transition, and signatures of the QCD critical point including event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities and charge correlations.

## Key numbers

- Chiral phase transition temperature from HotQCD/RBC: 192 (4)(7) MeV; from Budapest/Wuppertal: 152 (3)(3) MeV
- Deconfinement transition temperature from HotQCD/RBC: 192 (4)(7) MeV; from Budapest/Wuppertal: 170 (4)(3) MeV
- First order phase transition region from lattice: for T/T_c less than 0.83 and mu_q/T greater than 2.3, using m_pi = 700 MeV and 2 quark flavors
- Lattice calculation of QCD critical point location: T_E approximately 160 MeV and mu_E approximately 600 MeV, using canonical ensemble with m_pi = 700 MeV
- Energy density uncertainty at T_c: approximately 60 percent due to the 40 MeV spread in transition temperature
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit approached at very high T approximately 10^5 to 10^7 MeV
- Correlation length estimate from model calculations: 2-3 fm; from NA49 data analysis: approximately 6 fm
- NA49 system size dependence shows non-monotonic behavior around mu_B approximately 250 MeV and T approximately 178 MeV
- RHIC net-proton moment study at mu_B less than 30 MeV shows behavior consistent with central limit theorem expectations

## Context

This conference summary reviews the status of the QCD phase diagram as of 2009, extending lattice results from the HotQCD/RBC and Budapest/Wuppertal collaborations and experimental results from RHIC and SPS. It highlights the unresolved discrepancy in transition temperature between lattice groups (HotQCD/RBC versus Budapest/Wuppertal) and the intense debate over whether lattice calculations support or rule out a QCD critical point, with the work of de Forcrand and Philipsen suggesting a negative c1 coefficient that would indicate no critical point (later revised with smaller lattice spacing). The paper enables future critical point searches at RHIC beam energy scan (sqrt(s_NN) approximately 39-5 GeV), SPS SHINE, FAIR CBM, and NICA facilities, and introduces the concept of a quarkyonic phase at high baryon density proposed by McLerran and Pisarski.
