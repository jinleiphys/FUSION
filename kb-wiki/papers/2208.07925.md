# Infinite conformal symmetry and emergent chiral (super)fields of topologically non-trivial configurations: From Yang-Mills-Higgs to the Skyrme model

> The present manuscript discusses a remarkable phenomenon concerning non-linear and non-integrable field theories in $(3+1)$-dimensions, living at finite density and possessing non-trivial topological charges and non-Abelian internal symmetries (both local and global). With suitable types of ansätze, one can construct infinite-dimensional families of analytic solutions with non-vanishing topological charges (representing the Baryonic number) labelled by both two integers numbers and by free scalar fields in $(1+1)$-dimensions. These exact configurations represent $(3+1)$-dimensional topological solitons hosting $(1+1)$-dimensional chiral modes localized at the energy density peaks. First, we analyze the Yang-Mills-Higgs model, in which the fields depend on all the space-time coordinates (to keep alive the topological Chern-Simons charge), but in such a way to reduce the equations system to the field equations of two-dimensional free massless chiral scalar fields. Then, we move to the non-linear sigma model, showing that a suitable ansatz reduces the field equations to the one of a two-dimensional free massless scalar field. Then, we discuss the Skyrme model concluding that the inclusion of the Skyrme term gives rise to a chiral two-dimensional free massless scalar field (instead of a free massless field in two dimensions as in the non-linear sigma model) describing analytically spatially modulated Hadronic layers and tubes. The comparison of the present approach both with the instantons-dyons liquid approach and with Lattice QCD is shortly outlined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in Yang-Mills-Higgs theory, the nonlinear sigma model, and the Skyrme model in (3+1) dimensions, suitable ansatze reduce the full field equations to those of two-dimensional free massless scalar fields, with the Skyrme and Yang-Mills-Higgs cases yielding chiral conformal field theories rather than ordinary ones. This reduction holds only for configurations confined to a finite spatial box and requires specific boundary conditions on the scalar function F(t,phi) to maintain a non-vanishing topological charge.

## Method

The authors construct ansatze based on Euler angle parameterizations of SU(2)-valued fields, using a flat metric with Cartesian dimensionless coordinates r, theta, phi and constant length scales L_r, L_theta, L_phi that fix the box volume. For Yang-Mills theory, the gauge potential is expressed in terms of a function F(t,phi) that satisfies the two-dimensional wave equation, with the topological Chern-Simons charge determined by boundary conditions on F at phi=0 and phi=pi. For the Skyrme model, a similar ansatz reduces the field equations to a chiral two-dimensional free massless scalar field, while the nonlinear sigma model without the Skyrme term reduces to a non-chiral free massless scalar field.

## Key numbers

- The topological Chern-Simons charge Q_CS equals the product pq, where p and q are non-vanishing integers appearing in the Euler angle parameterization, with the requirement that pq be integer.
- The energy density T_tt = (p^2 / (e^2 L_theta^2)) exp(5eta) cosh(eta) [ (partial F/partial t)^2 + (1/L_phi^2)(partial F/partial phi)^2 ], where e is the Yang-Mills coupling constant and eta is a real parameter.
- The topological charge density rho_CS = (pq exp(3eta) / (16 pi^2 (1 - exp(4eta) F^2)^(3/2))) (partial F/partial phi), which is well-defined only when |exp(4eta) F(t,phi)^2| <= 1.
- The coordinates r, theta, phi have ranges 0 <= theta <= 2pi, 0 <= phi <= pi, 0 <= r <= 4pi, related to Euler angle parameterization of SU(2).

## Context

This work extends previous constructions of non-spherical hedgehog ansatze for the Skyrme model (Canfora et al., 2014-2021) and for Einstein-Yang-Mills systems (Canfora et al., 2013-2018), generalizing them to reveal chiral conformal degrees of freedom representing modulations of hadronic tubes and layers. The approach enables analytic estimation of contributions to the partition function at low temperatures and baryon chemical potential, complementing lattice QCD methods that suffer from the sign problem, and provides tools to study nuclear pasta states whose numerical treatment is challenging.
