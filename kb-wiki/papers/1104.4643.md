# Similarity renormalization group evolution of $NN$ interactions within a subtractive renormalization scheme

> We apply the similarity renormalization group (SRG) approach to evolve a nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction in leading-order (LO) chiral effective field theory (ChEFT), renormalized within the framework of the subtracted kernel method (SKM). We derive a fixed-point interaction and show the renormalization group (RG) invariance in the SKM approach. We also compare the evolution of $NN$ potentials with the subtraction scale through a SKM RG equation in the form of a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik (NRCS) equation and the evolution with the similarity cutoff through the SRG transformation.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the similarity renormalization group (SRG) can be applied to evolve a leading-order chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction that has been renormalized via the subtracted kernel method (SKM), producing a fixed-point interaction that is renormalization group invariant. The key hedge is that this demonstration is performed only for the simple case of the \(^1S_0\) channel at leading order, with higher-order chiral interactions and other partial waves deferred to future work.

## Method
The authors first derive a fixed-point renormalized interaction \(V_{\cal R}\) within the SKM framework by solving a one-subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the leading-order chiral \(NN\) potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel, fitting the contact interaction strength to the experimental scattering length. They then evolve this fixed-point interaction using Wegner's flow equation with the generator \(\eta_s=[T_{\rm rel},H_s]\), solving the resulting system of \(N^2\) non-linear coupled differential equations numerically on a 200-point momentum grid with an adaptive fifth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm. The SRG evolution is compared with the evolution of the driving term through the non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik (NRCS) equation, and decoupling of low- from high-momentum components is tested by applying an exponential cutoff to the evolved potential.

## Key numbers
- The experimental scattering length in the \(^1S_0\) channel used to fix the contact interaction strength: \(a_s = -23.7\ \text{fm}\).
- The diagonal matrix element \(V^{(1)}(k,k)\) is shown for \(k \equiv \sqrt{E_k} \simeq 1.55\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Phase-shift relative differences between \(\mu = 10\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and \(\mu \rightarrow \infty\) are smaller than \(10^{-9}\) when the driving term is evolved through the NRCS equation.
- The SRG evolution uses a momentum grid of 200 gaussian integration points.
- The decoupling test uses a similarity cutoff \(\lambda = 2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and a momentum cutoff \(k_{\rm max} = 3.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) with exponent \(n=8\).

## Context
This work extends the application of the SRG approach, previously used to evolve phenomenological and chiral effective field theory \(NN\) interactions (Bogner et al., 2007; Bogner et al., 2010), to interactions renormalized via the SKM scheme. The SKM approach itself was developed in a series of papers (Timoteo et al., 1999; 2000; 2001; 2005; 2007; 2007) as an alternative to cutoff-based regularization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The paper enables future studies of SRG evolution for higher-order chiral interactions and multiple partial-wave channels, as well as comparisons between the SKM and other renormalization schemes for nuclear forces.
