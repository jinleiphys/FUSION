# SU(6)-breaking symmetry and the ratio of proton momentum distributions

> The ratio between the anomalous magnetic moments of proton and neutron has recently been suggested to be connected to the ratio of proton momentum fractions carried by valence quarks. This relation has been obtained within a parametrization of the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPD) \cite{gpv}, but it is completely independent of such a parametrization.\\ It will be shown that using different CQMs this relation holds within a few percent accuracy. This agreement is based on what all the CQMs have in common: the effective degrees of freedom of the three constituent quarks and the underlying SU(6) symmetry.\\ On the other hand, the experimental value of the ratio is not reproduced by CQMs. This means that the SU(6)-breaking mechanism contained in the phenomenological partonic distributions does not correspond to the SU(6) breaking mechanism implemented in the CQMs we have analyzed \cite{noi03}.\\ We will also show how this relation can be used in order to understand in which way to implement an $SU(6)$-breaking mechanism and to test models.

## Key claim

The relation between the ratio of proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments and the ratio of proton momentum fractions carried by valence quarks holds to within a few percent accuracy across different Constituent Quark Models (CQMs), but the experimental value of the magnetic moment ratio is not reproduced by these CQMs, indicating that the SU(6)-breaking mechanism in phenomenological partonic distributions differs from that implemented in the CQMs analyzed.

## Method

The authors calculated parton distributions at the hadronic scale using three different Constituent Quark Models: the Isgur-Karl (IK) model, a hypercentral Coulomb-like plus linear confinement potential model (hCQM), and an algebraic model (U(7)). They evaluated the ratio of proton momentum fractions carried by valence quarks (M₂^{q_val}) from the quark momentum density distributions, then performed NLO evolution up to Q² = 10 GeV² and compared the resulting R-ratio (the right-hand side of the proposed relation) with the experimental κ-ratio (κ^p/κ^n). They also performed a free-parameter fit within the Isgur-Karl model to test whether adjusting SU(6)-breaking amplitudes could simultaneously reproduce both ratios.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of κ^p/κ^n is approximately -0.937.
- In the SU(6)-invariant limit, both κ^p/κ^n and the R-ratio equal -1.
- For the hCQM, the R-ratio equals the κ-ratio within 0.2%, although the κ-ratio differs from experiment by about 7%.
- For the Isgur-Karl model, the R-ratio at Q² = 0.5, 5.0, and 10.0 GeV² is -1.0098, while the model prediction for κ^p/κ^n is -1.0.
- For the hCQM with One-Gluon-Exchange, the R-ratio is -1.0030 at all three Q² values, with κ^p/κ^n = -1.0.
- For the hCQM with isospin dependence, the R-ratio is -0.9983 at all three Q² values, with κ^p/κ^n = -1.0.
- For the U(7) model, the R-ratio is -0.9881 at all three Q² values, while κ^p/κ^n = -0.9372 (matching experiment by construction).
- In the D-model fit, using a_S = 0.955 and a_D = 0.295 with α = 2.1 fm⁻¹, the best R-ratio obtained is 0.9988, differing from the κ-ratio by about 7%.

## Context

This work extends the relation proposed by Goeke, Polyakov, and Vanderhaeghen (2001) between anomalous magnetic moments and valence quark momentum fractions, which was originally derived within a Generalized Parton Distributions parametrization. The authors test this relation across multiple CQMs including the Isgur-Karl model, the hypercentral model of Ferraris et al., and the algebraic model of Bijker et al., finding that all models satisfy the relation to within a few percent but fail to reproduce the experimental κ-ratio. This suggests that current CQMs are too constrained by three-constituent-quark degrees of freedom, and that additional degrees of freedom such as quark-antiquark pairs or gluons (pion cloud effects) are needed; the proposed relation provides a tool for testing such new models.
