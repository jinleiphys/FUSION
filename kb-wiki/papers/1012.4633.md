# The few body problem of the quark-model hadron structure

> We discuss recent results of hadron spectroscopy and hadron-hadron interaction within a quark model framework. New experimental data could point to the important role played by higher order Fock space components on low-energy observables. Our aim is to obtain a coherent description of the low-energy hadron phenomenology to constrain QCD phenomenological models and try to learn about low-energy realizations of the theory. This long-range effort is based in adapting few-body techniques to study many-quark systems.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a coherent description of low-energy hadron phenomenology requires extending the Hilbert space beyond simple quark-antiquark (meson) and three-quark (baryon) valence configurations to include higher-order Fock components such as four-quark and five-quark states, and that this same framework must simultaneously describe hadron spectroscopy and hadron-hadron scattering.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-D-meson scattering to search for attractive channels that may contain meson-meson molecules, coupling charmonium-light two-meson systems to the D-meson channels. For baryon anomalies, they parametrize the transition matrix element between three-quark states and meson-baryon channels and diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrix. For strangeness -1 and -2 baryon systems, they derive baryon-baryon interactions from the chiral constituent quark model using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation and solve the resulting few-body equations.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton binding energy: \(B_{0,1/2}=0.13\pm 0.05\) MeV (experimental error bars)
- \(\Lambda N\) spin-triplet scattering length constrained to \(1.41\le a_{1/2,1} \le 1.58\) fm based on the absence of a bound \((I,J)=(0,3/2)\) state
- \(\Lambda N\) spin-singlet scattering length constrained to \(2.33\le a_{1/2,0}\le 2.48\) fm to reproduce hypertriton binding within experimental error
- \(\Xi^- p\) elastic cross section: \(\sigma_{\Xi^- p}=30\pm 6.7^{+3.7}_{-3.6}\) mb at \(p^\Xi_{\rm lab}\approx 550\) MeV/c (in-medium experimental data)
- \(\Xi^- p \to \Lambda\Lambda\) cross section: \(\sigma=4.3^{+6.3}_{-2.7}\) mb at \(p^\Xi_{\rm lab}=500\) MeV/c
- Upper limit for \(H\)-dibaryon mass: 2223.7 MeV/\(c^2\) (lower limit) from the Nagara event at 90% confidence level
- \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}=7.25\pm 0.19\) MeV upper limit from the Nagara event
- \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}=11.90\pm 0.13\) MeV from the Demachi-Yanagi event (\({}^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)Be)
- \(^1S_0\) \(\Lambda\Lambda\) scattering length: \(-2.54\) fm (this work), yielding a rough estimate of \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\approx 5.41\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model framework of Valcarce et al. (2005) to simultaneously address hadron spectroscopy and hadron-hadron scattering, building on earlier quark-model studies of charmonium by Isgur (1983) and Eichten et al. (1980). The unquenching approach follows the ideas of Close (2005) for incorporating \((c\bar c)(n\bar n)\) components. The analysis of baryon anomalies builds on the comparative study of constituent quark model predictions by G. V. V. et al. (2008) and the identification of resonances overpredicted by three-quark models. The strangeness -2 baryon-baryon results provide predictions for upcoming J-PARC experiments and are compared with the Nijmegen-D model and the quark-model results of Fujiwara et al. (2007). The work enables future experimental searches for four-quark hidden-charm molecules, charged charmonium states, and doubly strange hypernuclei.
