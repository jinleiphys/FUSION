# Variational procedure for nuclear shell-model calculations and energy-variance extrapolation

> We discuss a variational calculation for nuclear shell-model calculations and propose a new procedure for the energy-variance extrapolation (EVE) method using a sequence of the approximated wave functions obtained by the variational calculation. The wave functions are described as linear combinations of the parity, angular-momentum projected Slater determinants, the energy of which is minimized by the conjugate gradient method obeying the variational principle. The EVE generally works well using the wave functions, but we found some difficult cases where the EVE gives a poor estimation. We discuss the origin of the poor estimation concerning shape coexistence. We found that the appropriate reordering of the Slater determinants allows us to overcome this difficulty and to reduce the uncertainty of the extrapolation.

## Key claim

The energy-variance extrapolation (EVE) method can give poor estimations for nuclear ground-state energies when shape coexistence causes the sequence of approximated wave functions to be initially dominated by an excited state rather than the true ground state; this difficulty can be overcome by reordering the basis states to produce a monotonic energy-variance plot, enabling a stable linear extrapolation.

## Method

The authors generate a sequence of approximated wave functions as linear combinations of parity- and angular-momentum-projected Slater determinants, with the determinant coefficients optimized sequentially by the conjugate gradient method (Sequential Conjugate Gradient, SCG) to minimize the energy at each step. For each set of N basis states, the energy and energy variance are computed after projection. When the standard EVE plot shows an anomalous kink (non-monotonic behavior), the basis states are reordered by a greedy algorithm that minimizes the slope of the energy-variance curve at each step, producing a new sequence that yields a nearly linear EV plot suitable for first-order polynomial extrapolation.

## Key numbers

* For the 0⁺₁ state of ⁷²Ge in the f₅pg₉-shell (JUN45 interaction, m-scheme dimension 140,050,484), the simple EVE without reordering gives a 50 keV overestimation of the exact ground-state energy.
* For the 0⁺₂ state of ⁷²Ge, the reordering technique reduces the extrapolation error from 20 keV (without reordering) to within 6 keV of the exact value.
* For ⁶⁴Ge in the pfg9-shell (PFG9B3 interaction, m-scheme dimension 1.7 × 10¹⁴), the extrapolated energies with reordering converge within a few keV for Nₘ > 40 basis states.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) approach of Shimizu et al. (2010) and the energy-variance extrapolation method introduced in condensed matter physics by Imada (2005) and applied to nuclear shell models by Mizusaki et al. (2008). The paper addresses a specific failure case reported by Shimizu et al. (2010) for ⁷²Ge, where shape coexistence (N=40 magicity, JUN45 interaction) causes the variational wave function to initially favor the 0⁺₂ state over the 0⁺₁ ground state. The reordering technique enables precise extrapolation for such difficult cases and is expected to be useful for large-scale shell-model and no-core shell-model calculations.
