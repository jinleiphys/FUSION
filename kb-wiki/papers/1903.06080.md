# Photon induced processes in semi-central nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We calculate total and differential cross sections for $J/ψ$ photoproduction in ultrarelativistic lead-lead collisions at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. We use a simple model based on vector dominance picture and multiple scattering of the hadronic ($c \bar c$) state in a cold nucleus. In our analysis we use Glauber formulae for calculating $σ_{tot,J/ψPb}$ which is a building block of our model. For semi-central collisions a modification of the photon flux is necessary. We discuss how to effectively correct photon fluxes for geometry effects. We try to estimate the cross sections for different centrality bins and for $J/ψ$ mesons emitted in forward rapidity range ($2.5<y<4$) corresponding to the ALICE experimental results. We discuss similar analysis for dilepton production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at very low pair transverse momenta, $P_T\leq 0.15$\,GeV. We investigate the interplay of thermal radiation with photon annihilation processes, $γγ\to l^+ l^-$, due to the coherent electromagnetic fields of the colliding nuclei. For the thermal radiation, we employ the emission from the QGP and hadronic phases with in-medium vector spectral functions. We first verify that the combination of photon fusion, thermal radiation and final-state hadron decays gives a fair description of the low-$P_T$ invariant-mass as well as $P_T$ distributions as measured recently by the STAR collaboration in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200\,GeV Au+Au collisions for different centralities. The coherent contribution dominates in peripheral collisions, while thermal radiation shows a significantly stronger increase with centrality. We also provide predictions for the ALICE experiment at the LHC. The resulting excitation function reveals a nontrivial interplay of photoproduction and thermal radiation.

# Photon induced processes in semi-central nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The standard equivalent-photon approximation used for ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) overestimates the cross section when naively applied to semi-central collisions; the author proposes two modified photon flux prescriptions that impose geometrical conditions on impact parameters to account for nuclear overlap and possible absorption, yielding upper and lower limits for the cross section.

## Method

For J/ψ photoproduction, the author uses a vector-dominance model with Glauber multiple scattering of the hadronic (c cbar) state in cold nuclear matter, modifying the photon flux by integrating over the nuclear geometry with step-function constraints on impact parameters. For dilepton production via γγ fusion, the author convolves transverse-momentum-dependent photon fluxes (calculated from Fourier transforms of nuclear charge densities) with the elementary γγ → l+l- cross section, and combines this with thermal radiation from QGP and hadronic phases using in-medium vector spectral functions and a hadronic decay cocktail.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ photoproduction in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, forward rapidity range 2.5 < y < 4, for centrality bins above 30%
- Dielectron invariant-mass spectra for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, pair P_T < 0.15 GeV, for three centrality classes: peripheral (60-80%), semi-peripheral (40-60%), and semi-central (10-40%)
- Predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for the same centrality classes with single-lepton acceptance cuts p_t > 0.2 GeV, |η_e| < 1, and |y_{e+e-}| < 1
- Excitation function of low-P_T (< 0.15 GeV) dilepton yields from γγ fusion and thermal radiation for heavy nuclei (A ≈ 200) around midrapidity, covering three orders of magnitude in collision energy from SPS to LHC

## Context

This work extends the analysis of J/ψ photoproduction in semi-central collisions first discussed by the ALICE collaboration (ALICE 2016) and interpreted by Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2016). For dilepton production, it builds on the STAR collaboration observation of enhanced dielectron pairs with small transverse momenta (Adam 2018) and the author's recent work with collaborators (Klusek-Gawenda, Rapp, Schafer 2019). The results enable predictions for the ALICE experiment at the LHC, particularly in view of plans to lower single-electron p_t cuts and measure very-low mass spectra, and show that RHIC energy √s_NN = 200 GeV is the most favorable for observing photon-photon fusion.
