# Heavy-flavour observables in relativistic nuclear collisions: theory overview

> Transport calculations represent the major tool to simulate the modifications induced by the presence of a hot-deconfined medium on the production of heavy-flavour particles in high-energy nuclear collisions. After a brief description of the approach and of the major achievements in its phenomenological applications we discuss some recent developments. From the theory side we focus on the evaluation of transport coefficients and on recent formulations of the problem of heavy-flavour in-medium propagation in the language of open quantum systems. From a more phenomenological perspective we give an overview of the attempts to extend theoretical models to reproduce recent experimental data arising from event-by-event fluctuations (odd flow harmonics, event-shape-engineering) or from medium-modifications of hadronization ($D_s$ and $Λ_c$ production)

## Key claim

Transport calculations are the major tool for simulating heavy-flavour particle modifications in hot deconfined medium, but a precise estimate of heavy-quark transport coefficients still requires measurements of beauty at low $p_T$ where non-relativistic dynamics allows more solid comparison with theoretical results.

## Method

The paper reviews the relativistic Boltzmann equation approximated as a Langevin equation for heavy-quark propagation, where momentum updates combine a deterministic friction force with a random noise term characterized by transverse and longitudinal momentum broadening coefficients. Transport calculations are applied to simulate heavy-flavour hadron production, with recent extensions to open quantum system formulations, event-by-event fluctuations, event-shape-engineering, and in-medium hadronization via coalescence or resonance formation mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficient from lattice-QCD: $\kappa = (1.8-3.4)T^3$, with large systematic uncertainty from extracting real-time information from Euclidean space-time simulations
- Momentum diffusion coefficient from quarkonium width: $0.24 \lesssim \kappa/T^3 \lesssim 4.2$, extracted from lattice-QCD in-medium spectral function
- Orbital angular momentum in non-central collisions: of order $1000\hbar$
- Triangular flow $v_3$ of D-mesons computed for 0-10% most central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV
- D-meson elliptic flow studied in 30-50% centrality class for 0-20% highest and 0-60% lowest-eccentricity subsamples

## Context

This review extends prior transport calculations for heavy-flavour production (Beraudo 2014, He 2014, Cao 2017, Nahrgang 2013) and compares them to ALICE experimental data (Acharya 2018). It enables future work on extracting transport coefficients from beauty measurements at low $p_T$, and connects to open quantum system approaches (Blaizot 2018, Brambilla 2019, Akamatsu 2014, Yao 2018) for rigorous derivation of Boltzmann equations and first-principle estimates of heavy-quark transport coefficients.
