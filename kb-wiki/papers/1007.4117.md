# Operator product expansion and the short distance behavior of 3-flavor baryon potentials

> The short distance behavior of baryon-baryon potentials defined through Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions is investigated using the operator product expansion. In a previous analysis of the nucleon-nucleon case, corresponding to the SU(3) channels $27_s$ and $\overline{10}_a$, we argued that the potentials have a repulsive core. A new feature occurs for the case of baryons made up of three flavors: manifestly asymptotically attractive potentials appear in the singlet and octet channels. Attraction in the singlet channel was first indicated by quark model considerations, and recently been found in numerical lattice simulations. The latter have however not yet revealed asymptotic attraction in the octet channels; we give a speculative explanation for this apparent discrepancy.

# Operator product expansion and the short distance behavior of 3-flavor baryon potentials

## Key claim

The operator product expansion analysis predicts manifestly attractive short-distance potentials in the SU(3) singlet and both octet channels for 3-flavor baryon-baryon systems, but the octet channel attraction may not be observable in lattice simulations because the relevant 6-quark operators vanish in the non-relativistic limit and subleading contributions can dominate in the distance range accessible to lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors extended their previous operator product expansion (OPE) analysis of nucleon-nucleon potentials to three quark flavors, computing the 1-loop anomalous dimensions of all local gauge-invariant 6-quark operators with flavor structure uuddss. They identified operators with anomalous dimensions larger than 24 (in units of 1/48π²) as "attractive" and determined which SU(3) flavor channels contain such operators at tree level in the OPE. The short-distance behavior of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions and corresponding potentials was then derived from the renormalization group analysis of the leading operators.

## Key numbers

- The 1-loop coefficient of the nucleon operator anomalous dimension is γ_N^(1) = 12/48π²
- Attractive operators were found with anomalous dimensions 42 (SU(3) singlet), 36 (singlet and octet), and 32 (singlet and octet)
- The RG power ν_k is given by ν_k = (48π² γ_k^(1) - 24)/(66 - 4N_f), where N_f = 3 for three flavors
- The model wave function used ν_1 = 2/9 and ν_2 = -0.10 to illustrate the octet channel behavior
- The 2-loop running coupling parameter κ = β_1/(2β_0²) = 32/81

## Context

This work extends the OPE analysis of Aoki, Balog, and Weisz (2009) from the 2-flavor nucleon-nucleon case (SU(3) channels 27_s and 10̅_a) to the full 3-flavor baryon octet. The singlet channel attraction agrees with quark model predictions by Oka, Shimizu, and Yazaki (1980s) and with lattice QCD simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration (2009-2010). The predicted octet channel attraction contradicts current lattice results, which the authors explain through the vanishing of octet 6-quark operators in the non-relativistic limit and the possible dominance of subleading repulsive terms in the 0.1-0.3 fm range. The analysis enables future lattice QCD checks of whether the SU(3) singlet attraction leads to a bound H-dibaryon state.
