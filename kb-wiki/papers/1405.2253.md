# Exclusive photoproduction of charmonia in $γp \to V p$ and $p p \to p V p$ reactions within $k_t$-factorization approach

> The amplitude for $γp \to J/ψp$ ($γp \to ψ' p$) is calculated in a pQCD $k_{T}$-factorization approach. The total cross section for this process is calculated for different unintegrated gluon distributions and compared with the HERA data and the data extracted recently by the LHCb collaboration. The amplitude for $γp \to J/ψp$ ($γp \to ψ' p$) is used to predict the cross section for exclusive photoproduction of $J/ψ$ ($ψ'$) meson in proton-proton collisions. Compared to earlier calculations we include both Dirac and Pauli electromagnetic form factors. The effect of Pauli form factor is quantified. Absorption effects are taken into account and their role is discussed in detail. Different differential distributions e.g. in $J/ψ$ ($ψ'$) rapidity and transverse momentum are presented and compared with existing experimental data. The UGDF with nonlinear effects built in better describe recent experimental data of the LHCb collaboration but no definite conclusion on onset of saturation can be drawn. We present our results also for the Tevatron. A good agreement with the CDF experimental data points at the midrapidity for both $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper calculates exclusive charmonia photoproduction using a pQCD \(k_t\)-factorization approach and finds that unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDFs) with nonlinear (saturation) effects built in better describe recent LHCb data, but no definite conclusion on the onset of saturation can be drawn because a simple parametrization of the HERA cross section also describes the data reasonably well.

## Method

The imaginary part of the forward amplitude for \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) and \(\gamma p \to \psi' p\) was computed using a \(k_t\)-factorization formalism with light-cone wave functions for the charmonia (Gaussian for 1S, node-type for 2S) and three different unintegrated gluon distributions (Ivanov-Nikolaev, Kutak-Stasto linear, Kutak-Stasto nonlinear). The full amplitude at finite momentum transfer was obtained by multiplying the forward amplitude by an exponential slope and restoring the real part via analyticity. For the \(pp \to pVp\) reaction, the Born amplitude included both Dirac (\(F_1\)) and Pauli (\(F_2\)) electromagnetic form factors, and eikonal absorption corrections (elastic rescattering) were applied to the amplitude.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\): calculations compared with HERA data and LHCb pseudodata over a range of center-of-mass energies \(W\).
* Slope parameter for the \(t\)-dependence: \(B(W) = b_0 + 2\alpha'_{\text{eff}} \log(W^2/W_0^2)\) with \(b_0 = 4.88\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\alpha'_{\text{eff}} = 0.164\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and \(W_0 = 90\) GeV.
* Absorption parameters for LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV): \(B_{el} = 19.89\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\sigma^{pp}_{\text{tot}} = 98.6\) mb.
* Absorption parameters for Tevatron (\(\sqrt{s}=1.96\) TeV): \(B_{el} = 17\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\sigma^{pp}_{\text{tot}} = 76\) mb.
* Effect of Pauli form factor: at \(p_t \sim 1\) GeV, the enhancement factor of the cross section is of order 10.
* Eikonal absorption damps the rapidity distribution of \(J/\psi\) by about 30% at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV.
* Good agreement with the CDF data point at midrapidity for both \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi'\) at \(\sqrt{s}=1.96\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier \(k_t\)-factorization calculations of Schafer and Szczurek (2007) for exclusive \(J/\psi\) production at the Tevatron by including both Dirac and Pauli electromagnetic form factors and a detailed treatment of absorption effects. It also extends the collinear formalism of Jones, Martin, Ryskin, and Teubner (2013) by using explicit light-cone wave functions that allow predictions for \(\psi'\) production and differential distributions beyond rapidity. The results enable future phenomenological extraction of UGDFs by simultaneously describing deep-inelastic structure function data and LHCb semi-exclusive charmonia data, including photonic-inelastic contributions.
