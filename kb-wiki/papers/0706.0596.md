# Cherenkov gluons (predictions and proposals)

> The coherent hadron production analogous to Cherenkov radiation of photons gives rise to the ring-like events. Being projected on the ring diameter they produce the two-bump structure recently observed for the away-side jets at RHIC. The position of the peaks and their height determine such properties of the hadronic medium as its nuclear refractive index, the parton density, the free path length and the energy loss of Cherenkov gluons. Cherenkov gluons may be responsible for the asymmetry of dilepton mass spectra near rho-meson observed in experiment. Beside comparatively low energy gluons observed at RHIC, there could be high energy gluons at LHC, related to the high energy region of positive real part of the forward scattering amplitude and possessing different characteristics. This would allow to scan (x, Q^2)-plane determining the parton densities in its various regions.

# Cherenkov gluons (predictions and proposals)

## Key claim

The two-bump structure observed in azimuthal angle distributions of away-side jets at RHIC can be explained by coherent hadron production from Cherenkov gluons, provided the nuclear refractive index exceeds unity; this interpretation requires that the gluon energies lie within the low-mass wings of hadronic resonances where the real part of the forward scattering amplitude is positive.

## Method

The author applies the optical relation between refractive index and forward scattering amplitude to gluons in nuclear matter, using Breit-Wigner resonance amplitudes to compute the refractive index at low energies. The Cherenkov cone angle is calculated from the refractive index via the standard formula cos θ_c = 1/(βn), and the resulting ring-like hadron distributions are projected onto one-dimensional azimuthal or pseudorapidity distributions to compare with experimental data from RHIC, cosmic ray events, and heavy-ion collisions at 158 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear refractive index n = 3, extracted from the distance between peaks in the PHENIX azimuthal distribution at √s = 200 GeV
- Parton density ν ≈ 20 partons within a single nucleon volume (4π/3 μ³, with μ the pion mass), derived from n = 3 using the Breit-Wigner formula
- Energy loss of Cherenkov gluons dE/dx ≈ 1 GeV/fm, calculated from the standard formula integrated over the ρ-meson resonance region
- Free path length of Cherenkov gluons R_f > 7 fm (or R_f > 20/E_R ≈ 4.5/μ ≈ 7 × 10⁻¹³ cm), estimated from the ring width δ ≈ 0.1
- Ring width δ ≈ 0.1 (in angular units), obtained from the peak height h_max ≈ 0.4 and internal radius r_i = 1.2 in the PHENIX data
- In-medium ρ-meson width Γ = 0.354 GeV (compared to in-vacuum width 150 MeV), fitted from dilepton mass spectra
- Cherenkov contribution parameter w = 0.19, fitted to the low-mass excess in dilepton spectra from 0.4 GeV to 0.75 GeV
- For the cosmic ray event at energy 10¹⁶ eV: ring radii r₁ = 1.75 cm and r₂ = 5 cm, with c.m.s. cone angle θ₁c ≈ 61°, Lorentz factor γ ≈ 2.3 × 10³, and interaction height H ≈ 68 m
- High-energy refractive index parameter a ≈ 2 × 10⁻³ GeV, with Δn_R(E_t) ≈ a ν_h / E_t for E_t above threshold

## Context

This work extends the concept of Cherenkov radiation from electrodynamics to QCD, building on earlier proposals by Dremin (2000s) for Cherenkov gluons in high-energy hadronic collisions. The interpretation of RHIC two-bump data as Cherenkov rings provides an alternative to other in-medium explanations, and the formalism connects to the Breit-Wigner resonance treatment used in optics (Feynman, 1963). The paper proposes that Cherenkov gluons can be used to scan the (x, Q²)-plane of parton distribution functions, with low-x, low-Q² regions (RHIC) giving high parton density and n ≈ 3, while high-x, high-Q² regions (cosmic rays, LHC) give low density and n ≈ 1. The universal asymmetry of in-medium resonances at low masses is predicted as a signature of Cherenkov gluons, supported by data from KEK (Muto, 2000s), CBELSA/TAPS, and PHENIX.
