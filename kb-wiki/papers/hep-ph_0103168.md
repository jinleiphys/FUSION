# The Glueball sector of two-flavor Color Superconductivity

> We construct the effective Lagrangian describing the light glueballs associated with the unbroken and confining SU(2) color subgroup for the 2 flavor superconductive phase of QCD. This Lagrangian constitutes a key ingredient for understanding the non perturbative physics of 2 flavor color superconductivity. We estimate the two photon decay process of the light glueballs using the saturation of the electromagnetic trace anomaly at the effective Lagrangian level. The present results are particularly relevant to our model of Gamma Ray Bursts based on color superconductivity in Quark Stars (R. Ouyed and F. Sannino astro-ph/0103022).

## Key claim
The light glueballs of the unbroken SU(2) color subgroup in two-flavor color superconductivity are light (MeV scale) relative to the superconducting gap and decay rapidly into two photons, providing a mechanism for converting glue into electromagnetic radiation. The authors estimate this decay using the saturation of the electromagnetic trace anomaly at the effective Lagrangian level, but note that the perturbative results for the dielectric constant are only quantitatively valid for chemical potentials much greater than 10^8 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for the light scalar glueball field associated with the confining SU(2) color subgroup in the 2SC phase, using the trace anomaly of the rescaled Yang-Mills theory in a medium with large dielectric constant. They estimate the in-medium glueball mass and two-photon decay width by coupling the glueball to the electromagnetic trace anomaly, using the modified photon field and charges that arise from the mixing of the ordinary photon with the eighth gluon in the 2SC medium.

## Key numbers
- Dielectric constant of the 2SC medium: \(\epsilon = 1 + \frac{g_s^2 \mu^2}{18 \pi^2 \Delta^2}\), with magnetic permeability \(\lambda = 1\) at asymptotically high densities
- Estimated glueball scale \(\hat{\Lambda} \simeq 1\) MeV, using \(\Lambda_{QCD} \simeq 300\) MeV, \(\mu \simeq 500\) MeV, and gap \(\Delta \simeq 30\) MeV
- Glueball mass \(M_h\) is of order \(\hat{\Lambda}\) (MeV range)
- Decay width \(\Gamma[h \rightarrow \gamma\gamma] \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-2} \cos\theta_Q^4 \left[\frac{M_h}{1~\text{MeV}}\right]^5\) eV
- For \(M_h \sim 1\) MeV, decay time \(\tau \sim 5.5 \times 10^{-14}\) s
- Mixing angle \(\theta_Q \sim 2.5^\circ\) (so \(\cos\theta_Q \sim 1\))
- Trace of squared new electric charge over quarks: \(\sum \widetilde{Q}^2_{\text{quarks}} = 2\)

## Context
This work extends the effective Lagrangian for the 2SC phase constructed by Casalbuoni, Duan, and Sannino (2000) by adding the confined SU(2) glueball degrees of freedom, following the observation by Rischke, Son, and Stephanov (2000) that the confining scale of the unbroken SU(2) subgroup is lighter than the superconducting gap due to the large dielectric constant. The glueball two-photon decay is applied to the model of Gamma Ray Bursts from Quark Stars by Ouyed and Sannino (2001), establishing a link between high-density QCD and astrophysical observables. The work enables further studies of the 2SC equation of state and non-perturbative physics in color superconducting quark matter.
