# Impossibility of the Efimov effect for p-wave interactions

> Whether the Efimov effect is possible, in principle, for p-wave or higher partial-wave interactions is a fundamental question. Recently, there has been a claim that three nonrelativistic particles with resonant p-wave interactions exhibit the Efimov effect. We point out that the assumed p-wave scattering amplitude inevitably causes a negative probability. This indicates that the Efimov states found there cannot be realized in physical situations. We also restate our previous argument that the Efimov effect, defined as an infinite tower of universal bound states characterized by discrete scale invariance, is impossible for p-wave or higher partial-wave interactions.

# Impossibility of the Efimov effect for p-wave interactions

## Key claim
The Efimov effect, defined as an infinite tower of universal bound states with discrete scale invariance, is impossible for p-wave or higher partial-wave interactions because the assumed p-wave scattering amplitude inevitably causes a negative probability or non-normalizable wave functions, meaning the claimed Efimov states cannot be realized in physical situations.

## Method
The author analyzes the p-wave scattering amplitude in the effective range approximation and demonstrates that extending this amplitude to arbitrarily high momenta leads to wave functions that are too singular to be normalized. By regularizing the short-distance behavior with a finite-range potential, the author shows that the probability outside the potential exceeds 1, requiring a negative probability inside the potential to maintain normalization. The argument is extended using an effective field theory approach where the scaling dimension of the two-particle operator violates the unitarity bound, confirming the existence of negative-norm states.

## Key numbers
- The probability outside the potential near resonance is given by \(\int_{r>R} d\mathbf{r} |\Psi(\mathbf{r})|^2 \simeq -2/(r_p R) \leq 1\), where \(r_p\) is the effective range parameter and \(R\) is the potential range.
- The lower bound on the effective range parameter near resonance is \(|r_p| \geq O(1)/R\) when \(|a_p|^{1/3} \gg R\).
- The scaling dimension of the two-particle operator \(\boldsymbol{\phi} \sim \psi \tensor \nabla \psi\) is \(\Delta = 1\), which violates the unitarity bound \(\Delta \geq 3/2\).

## Context
This paper directly contradicts the claim by Braaten (2011) that three nonrelativistic particles with resonant p-wave interactions exhibit the Efimov effect, and also addresses earlier work by Macek (2006). The author restates the argument from Nishida (2011) that the Efimov effect is impossible for p-wave or higher partial-wave interactions, which led to the idea of mixed dimensions in searching for new systems exhibiting Efimov physics. The analysis builds on constraints from Pricoupenko (2006), Jona-Lasinio (2008), and Hammer (2009) regarding the effective range parameter near resonance.
