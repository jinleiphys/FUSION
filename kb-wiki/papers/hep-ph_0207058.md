# Saturation and Wilson Line Distributions

> We introduce a Wilson line distribution function bar{W}_tau(v) to study gluon saturation at small Feynman x_F, or large tau=ln(1/x_F). This new distribution can be obtained from the distribution W_tau(alpha) of the Color Glass Condensate model and the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. bar{W}_tau(v) is physically more relevant, and mathematically simpler to deal with because of unitarity of the Wilson line v. A JIMWLK equation is derived for bar{W}_tau(v); its properties are studied. These properties are used to complete Mueller's derivation of the JIMWLK equation, though for bar{W}_tau(v) and not W_tau(alpha). They are used to derive a generalized Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for higher multipole amplitudes. They are also used to compute the unintegrated gluon distribution at x_F=0, yielding a completely flat spectrum in transverse momentum squared k^2, with a known height. This is similar but not identical to the mean field result at small k^2.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a Wilson line distribution function $\overline{W}_\tau(v)$ that is derived from the Color Glass Condensate model's distribution $W_\tau(\alpha)$ and satisfies the JIMWLK equation, offering mathematical advantages because the Wilson line $v$ is unitary and lives on a compact group manifold, unlike the non-compact linear space of the gauge potential $\alpha$.

## Method

The authors define the Wilson line distribution $\overline{W}_\tau(v)$ by folding the Yang-Mills potential distribution $W_\tau(\alpha)$ through a functional integral transformation that maps $\alpha$ to the Wilson line $v$. They derive the JIMWLK evolution equation for $\overline{W}_\tau(v)$ by transforming the functional derivatives with respect to $\alpha$ into differential operators on the unitary group manifold, using the Haar measure and representation theory of $U(n)$. The properties of this new distribution are then used to complete Mueller's derivation of the JIMWLK equation, derive a generalized Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for higher multipole amplitudes, and compute the unintegrated gluon distribution at $x_F=0$.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum $Q_s$ is defined by the relation $Q_s^2 = c \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{N_c^2-1} \frac{x_F G(x_F, Q_s^2)}{\pi R_A^2}$, with the constant $c$ determined to be $c = 8\pi^3$ from the flat spectrum at $x_F=0$
- The unintegrated gluon distribution at $x_F=0$ yields a completely flat spectrum in transverse momentum squared $k^2$ with a known height given by equation (gluespec), in contrast to the mean field result $\frac{d(x_F G)}{d^2 k} \simeq c_2 \frac{N_c^2-1}{N_c} \frac{\pi R_A^2}{\alpha_s} \ln\frac{Q_s^2(\tau)}{k^2}$ for $k^2 \ll Q_s^2(\tau)$
- The dipole amplitude in the saturation region for $|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|^2 \gg 1/Q_s^2$ behaves as $s_\tau(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}) = \exp\left[-\frac{c}{2}\left(\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{\pi}\right)^2(\tau-\tau_0)^2\right] s_{\tau_0}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})$ when $Q_s^2(y) = \exp[c\alpha_s N_c(y-\tau_0)/\pi] Q_s^2(\tau_0)$
- The inner product of two defining representation matrix elements is $\langle v_{ij}|v_{kl}\rangle = \frac{1}{N_c}\delta_{ik}\delta_{jl}$

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the JIMWLK renormalization group equation derived by Jalilian-Marian, Iancu, McLerran, Weigert, Leonidov, and Kovner (1997-1998). The Wilson line distribution approach provides a mathematically simpler formulation than the original $W_\tau(\alpha)$ distribution because the compact group manifold allows harmonic analysis using irreducible representations, whereas $W_\tau(\alpha)$ lives on a non-compact linear space where only Gaussian functionals can be integrated in practice. The results enable the derivation of a generalized Balitsky-Kovchegov equation where once the non-linear BK equation for the dipole amplitude is solved, all higher multipole amplitudes can be obtained by solving only linear equations, meaning saturation of the dipole amplitude automatically leads to saturation of higher multipole amplitudes.
