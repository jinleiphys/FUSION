# Nucleon Mass Sum Rule Violation in QCD and Confinement

> In this paper we show that for a nucleon at rest if the quarks and/or antiquarks are in motion inside the nucleon producing chromo-magnetic field then the mass sum rule in QCD is violated when the confinement potential energy at large distance $r$ rises linearly with $r$ (or faster). Hence we find that the mass of the nucleon at rest is not equal to the mass-energy of all the quarks plus antiquarks plus gluons inside the nucleon if there exists chromo-magnetic field inside the nucleon and the confinement potential energy at large distance $r$ rises linearly with $r$ (or faster).

## Key claim

For a nucleon at rest, the standard QCD mass sum rule (nucleon mass equals the sum of mass-energies of all quarks, antiquarks, and gluons inside it) is violated if the quarks and/or antiquarks are in motion producing a chromo-magnetic field *and* the confinement potential energy at large distance rises linearly with r (or faster); in that case, a non-zero energy flux term must be added to the sum rule.

## Method

The author derives the gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor continuity equation in classical Yang-Mills theory and shows that the boundary surface term at infinity does not vanish when the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields fall off no faster than 1/r, which is required for a linearly rising (or faster) confinement potential. This non-vanishing boundary term yields a non-zero energy flux, which is then carried over to the operator formalism in renormalized QCD to modify the nucleon mass sum rule.

## Key numbers

- The confinement potential energy at large distance r is required to rise linearly with r (or faster) for the violation to occur; no specific numerical coefficient for the potential is given.
- The chromo-electric field (and chromo-magnetic field) must not fall faster than 1/r at large r for the boundary term to be non-zero; no explicit numerical bound is provided beyond the functional form.
- The paper contains no numerical results for nucleon masses, energy fractions, or coupling constants; the argument is formal and analytic.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Nayak, 2017) of boundary surface terms in classical Yang-Mills theory and QCD, and it contradicts the standard nucleon mass sum rule as formulated by Jaffe (1989) and Ji (1995). The result implies that lattice QCD calculations or phenomenological models that assume the standard sum rule may need to include an energy flux contribution if confinement is linear and quarks are moving inside the nucleon. The paper does not provide a numerical estimate of the missing mass-energy, leaving that as an open question for future work.
