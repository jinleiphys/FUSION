# Precise measurement of alpha_K and alpha_T for the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te: Test of internal-conversion theory

> We have measured the K-shell and total internal conversion coefficients (ICCs), alpha_K and alpha_T, for the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te to be 185.0(40) and 350.0(38), respectively. Previously this transition's ICCs were considered anomalous, with alpha values lying below calculated values. When compared with Dirac-Fock calculations, our new results show good agreement. The alpha_K result agrees well with the version of the theory that takes account of the K-shell atomic vacancy and disagrees with the one that does not. This is consistent with our conclusion drawn from a series of high multipolarity transitions.

# Precise measurement of alpha_K and alpha_T for the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te: Test of internal-conversion theory

## Key claim

The previously reported anomaly in the internal conversion coefficients of the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te is resolved: the new measured values agree with Dirac-Fock calculations, and the alpha_K result shows a statistical preference (at approximately 1.4 sigma) for the version of theory that includes the K-shell atomic vacancy over the version that ignores it.

## Method

The authors activated an enriched 124Te target via neutron capture to produce the 57.4-day isomer 125mTe, then recorded gamma-ray and K x-ray spectra with a precisely calibrated HPGe detector whose relative efficiency is known to ±0.15%. Because the decay scheme involves a cascade of two transitions (35.5-keV and 109.3-keV), both of which convert in the K shell, the K-shell ICC for the 109.3-keV transition was extracted using a modified equation that subtracts the K x-ray contribution from the 35.5-keV transition, which required adopting a calculated value for alpha_K of the 35.5-keV transition. The total ICC for the 109.3-keV transition was derived from the gamma-ray intensity ratio of the two transitions using a relation that depends on the calculated total ICC of the 35.5-keV transition.

## Key numbers

- Measured alpha_K for the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te: 185.0(40)
- Measured alpha_T for the 109.3-keV M4 transition in 125Te: 350.0(38)
- Dirac-Fock calculation without vacancy: alpha_K = 179.5(1), alpha_T = 348.7(3)
- Dirac-Fock calculation with vacancy (frozen-orbital): alpha_K = 185.2(1), alpha_T = 355.6(3)
- Dirac-Fock calculation with vacancy (SCF): alpha_K = 184.2(1), alpha_T = 354.2(3)
- The difference between vacancy-included and no-vacancy calculations for alpha_K is 3.4%
- Fluorescence yield used: omega_K = 0.875(4) from Schoetzig (1996)
- Detector efficiency known to ±0.15% relative precision over the relevant energy range
- Source activity at measurement time: approximately 60 kBq

## Context

This work is the eighth in a series of precise alpha_K measurements by Nica, Hardy, and collaborators (2004-2017) testing internal conversion theory for E3 and M4 transitions across a range of Z values (48 to 78). Previous measurements of this transition by Bowe (1952), Reidy (1977), Muramatsu (1982), and Sato (1998) had consistently produced alpha values lower than both model calculations, leading to the characterization of these ICCs as anomalous. The new results resolve this discrepancy and support the Dirac-Fock theory with atomic vacancy included, consistent with the authors' earlier findings for transitions in 111Cd, 119Sn, 127Te, 134Cs, 137Ba, 193Ir, and 197Pt. This work influenced the 2008 reevaluation by Kibédi et al., who developed the BrIcc database (adopted by the National Nuclear Data Center) using the frozen-orbital vacancy-inclusive version of the code by Band et al. (2002).
