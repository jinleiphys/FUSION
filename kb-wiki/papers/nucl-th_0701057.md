# Electromagnetic KY production from the proton in a Regge-plus-resonance approach

> A Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) description of the p(γ,K)Y and p(e,e'K)Y processes (Y = Λ, Σ^{0,+}) is presented. The proposed reaction amplitude consists of Regge-trajectory exchanges in the t channel, supplemented with a limited selection of s-channel resonance diagrams. The RPR framework contains a considerably smaller number of free parameters than a typical effective-Lagrangian model. Nevertheless, it provides an acceptable overall description of the photo- and electroproduction observables over an extensive photon energy range. It is shown that the electroproduction response functions and polarization observables are particularly useful for fine-tuning both the background and resonance parameters.

## Key claim

The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework provides an acceptable overall description of kaon photo- and electroproduction observables over an extensive photon energy range, but the electroproduction response functions and polarization observables are particularly useful for fine-tuning both the background and resonance parameters that the photoproduction data alone cannot determine unambiguously.

## Method

The authors constructed a reaction amplitude for p(γ,K)Y and p(e,e'K)Y (Y = Λ, Σ⁰,⁺) by combining Regge-trajectory exchanges in the t-channel (specifically K(494) and K*(892) trajectories) with a limited selection of s-channel resonance diagrams using standard Feynman propagators. The Regge propagators were obtained by replacing Feynman propagators with Regge ones, and the resonance contributions included Gaussian hadronic form factors with a single cutoff mass Λ_res, while electromagnetic N* and Δ* form factors were calculated using a Lorentz-covariant constituent-quark model.

## Key numbers

- Trajectory slopes: α_K(t) = 0.70 (t - m_K²) and α_K*(t) = 1 + 0.85 (t - m_K*²)
- Monopole electromagnetic form factor cutoff for K⁺(494) and K*⁺(892) trajectories: Λ_K⁺ = Λ_K*⁺ = 1300 MeV
- χ² values for K⁺Λ RPR variants range from 2.7 to 3.2 when compared with low- and high-energy data
- χ² for K⁺Σ⁰ RPR 4' variant: 2.0
- Electroproduction data taken at W ≈ 2.15 GeV and θ_K* ≈ 0° for unseparated cross sections, and at W ≈ 1.84 GeV and θ_K* ≈ 8° for separated σ_L and σ_T
- Electroproduction structure functions measured at W = 1.875 GeV and Q² = 0.65 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the RPR approach previously developed by Corthals et al. (2006) for kaon photoproduction to electroproduction processes p(e,e'K⁺)Λ and p(e,e'K⁺)Σ⁰, building on the Regge-model framework pioneered by Guidal and Vanderhaeghen (2003). The RPR strategy contrasts with traditional isobar models (such as those by Adelseck and Wright (1988) or Mart and Bennhold (1995)) and coupled-channels approaches (e.g., Usov and Scholten (2005)) by using fewer free parameters and avoiding gauge-invariance issues from strong form factors. The results enable discrimination between competing model variants and missing-resonance candidates (specifically D₁₃(1900) versus P₁₁(1900)) that photoproduction data alone could not resolve, and the authors expect new CLAS data will provide further stringent tests.
