# Pion - Nucleon Bremsstrahlung beyond the Soft-Photon approximation

> A dynamical model based on effective Lagrangians is proposed to describe the bremsstrahlung reaction $ πN \to πN γ$ at low energies. The $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom are incorporated in a way consistent with both, electromagnetic gauge invariance and invariance under contact transformations. The model also includes the initial and final state rescattering of hadrons via a T-matrix with off the momentum-shell effects. The double differential distribution of photons is computed for three different T-matrix models and the results are compared with the soft photon approximation, and with experimental data. The aim of this analysis is to test the off-shell behaviour of the different T-matrices under consideration. Finally an alternative simpler dynamical model that incorporates the unstable character of the isobar-$Δ(1232)$ through a complex mass, is presented. As we will see it is suitable for the study of the magnetic moment of the resonance.

# Pion-Nucleon Bremsstrahlung beyond the Soft-Photon Approximation

## Key claim

The paper proposes a dynamical model for the reaction πN → πNγ that goes beyond the Soft Photon Approximation (SPA) by explicitly constructing gauge-invariant electromagnetic currents from effective Lagrangians, including Δ(1232) degrees of freedom and off-shell T-matrix effects. The model tests the off-shell behavior of three different πN T-matrices (OBQA, SEP, NEW) and finds that the SEP interaction gives the smallest off-shell effects and closest agreement with experimental data, while the OBQA model shows the strongest off-shell effects.

## Method

The gauge-invariant bremsstrahlung amplitude is constructed from effective Lagrangians including nucleon, Δ-isobar, π, ρ, and σ mesons, with electromagnetic currents obtained through minimal coupling. The amplitude is decomposed into Born, pre-scattering, post-scattering, and double-scattering terms, where the T-matrix for πN rescattering is obtained from three different models (OBQA, SEP, NEW) and implemented via a three-dimensional Thompson-reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation. A common monopole form factor f(q'') = Λ²/(Λ² + q''²) is used to regularize the integrals while preserving gauge invariance, with cutoff parameters Λ adjusted at each incident energy to match the SPA at low photon energies. An alternative simpler model (DMA II) treats only the Δ-pole contribution with a complex mass M_Δ² - iM_ΔΓ to incorporate the unstable character of the resonance.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion kinetic energies studied: T_lab = 269, 298, and 324 MeV
- Photon energy range considered: ω_γ = 0 - 150 MeV
- Cutoff parameters Λ (in MeV) for DMA I at T_lab = 269, 298, 324 MeV respectively: Λ_OBQA = 750, 700, 600; Λ_SEP = 700, 600, 500; Λ_NEW = 550, 500, 450
- For DMA II: M_Δ = 1232 MeV and Γ = 110 MeV fixed from previous experimental work (Pedroni)
- Coupling constant f_{NΔπ} = 0.24 determined by fitting low-energy photon emission region
- Pion detection angles: θ_π = 50.5°, φ_π = 180°
- Photon detection configurations: G14 (θ_γ = 103°, φ_γ = 180°), G11 (θ_γ = 160°, φ_γ = 180°), G7 (θ_γ = 120°, φ_γ = 0°)

## Context

This work extends the Soft Photon Approximation framework of Low (1958) and Liou (1985) for radiative πN scattering by constructing an explicit dynamical model that preserves gauge invariance while incorporating off-shell T-matrix effects. The three T-matrix models tested are: OBQA from Schutz (based on correlated 2π exchange), SEP from Nozawa (phenomenological separable potential), and NEW from a π and sharp ρ exchange model. The approach enables testing off-shell behavior that cannot be studied within SPA because off-shell contributions cancel in that approximation. The simpler DMA II model, using a complex-mass Δ propagator, is presented as suitable for extracting the unknown anomalous magnetic moment κ_Δ of the Δ(1232) resonance, which is self-gauge invariant and not fixed by the SPA procedure. The work is motivated by the need to evaluate background contributions in γN → πN photoproduction for extracting resonant parameters of nucleon resonances.
