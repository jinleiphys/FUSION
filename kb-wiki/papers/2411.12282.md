# Nuclear Pairing Energy vs Mean Field Energy: Do They Talk To Each Other For Searching The Energy Minimum?

> We study the evolution of the total binding energy (TBE) and pairing energy of Pb, Hg and Ar isotopes, as a function of the nuclear deformation. As for the nuclear model, we exploit a deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in the continuum (DRHBc), and a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS model. It is found that the dependence of pairing energy on the deformation is strongly correlated to that of the mean field energy, which is obtained by subtracting the pairing energy from the TBE; in other words, the energy minimum characterized by a large negative mean field energy has a smaller negative pairing energy or, equivalently, a smaller positive pairing gap, while a stronger pairing energy is found in the region away from the minimum of the total energy. Consequently, the two energies show an anti-symmetric feature in their deformation dependence, although the energy scales are very different. Moreover, since the pairing energy has a negative sign with respect to to the pairing gap, the evolution of mean field energy follows closely that of the pairing gap. This implies that the pairing energy (or pairing gap) and the mean field energy talk to each other and work together along the potential energy curve to determine the energy minimum and/or the local minimum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pairing energy and the mean field energy (total binding energy minus pairing energy) exhibit an anti-symmetric dependence on nuclear deformation: at the deformation where the mean field energy is most negative (strongest binding), the pairing energy is least negative (weakest pairing), and vice versa. This anti-correlation is presented as a general, robust phenomenon observed across spherical, oblate, and prolate nuclei, but the authors hedge that quantitative details depend on the specific pairing force approximation used.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in the continuum (DRHBc) calculations using the PC-PK1 density functional with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force (strength V0 = -325.0 MeV fm^3, saturation density 0.152 fm^-3, pairing window 100 MeV). They computed the total binding energy, the pairing energy from Eq. (4), and the mean field energy (total minus pairing) as functions of the quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 for Pb, Hg, and Ar isotopes. To test model dependence, they repeated the analysis for selected nuclei (186Pb and 36Ar) using a non-relativistic deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS (DSHF+BCS) model with the SLy4 functional and a constant-gap approximation (Delta = 12/A^1/2 MeV).

## Key numbers

* For 208Pb, the total binding energy and mean field energy show a local minimum at the spherical point (beta_2 = 0), where the pairing energy is zero; the pairing energy increases negatively with deformation.
* The pairing gaps of neutrons and protons are zero at spherical deformation for 208Pb but deviate from zero with increasing deformation.
* The pairing strength used is V0 = -325.0 MeV fm^3, with saturation density rho_sat = 0.152 fm^-3 and a pairing window of 100 MeV.
* The energy cutoff E_cut^+ = 300 MeV and angular momentum cutoff J_max = (23/2) hbar were used for the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis.
* For the DSHF+BCS calculations, the constant pairing gap was taken as Delta = 12/A^1/2 MeV, with smooth cutoffs at +/- 2.5 MeV around the Fermi energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in deformed nuclei beyond the constant-gap approximation, using the state-of-the-art DRHBc framework that has been applied to mass table calculations by Kaiyuan (2020, 2021) and Cong (2021). The finding of anti-symmetric evolution between mean field and pairing energies is consistent with the schematic gap-equation result that pairing energy is proportional to -1/2 * (average level density) * (Delta^2), as discussed in Ring (1980) and Nilsson (1995). The results for 186Pb, a well-known shape-coexistence nucleus studied by Andreyev (2000) and Ojala (2022), show larger pairing energy in the prolate region than the oblate region, which the authors connect to a smaller moment of inertia and larger rotational band spacing consistent with experimental data. The work enables future studies of how pairing correlations affect moments of inertia and rotational band structures in deformed and shape-coexisting nuclei.
