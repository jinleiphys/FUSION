# Fudan Multi-purpose Active TArget Time Projection Chamber (fMeta-TPC) for Photonnuclear Reaction Experiments

> Active Target Time Projection Chambers (AT-TPCs) are state-of-the-art tools in the field of low-energy nuclear physics, particularly suitable for experiments using low-intensity radioactive ion beams or gamma rays. The Fudan Multi-purpose Active Target Time Projection Chamber (fMeta-TPC) with 2048 channels has been developed to study $α$-clustering nuclei. {\fcb In this work, the focus is on the study of the photonuclear reaction with the Laser Compton Scattering (LCS) gamma source, especially for the decay of the highly excited $α$-cluster state.} The design of fMeta-TPC is described and a comprehensive evaluation of its offline performance is performed by ultraviolet (UV) laser and $^{241}$Am $α$ source. The result shows that the intrinsic angular resolution of the detector is within 0.30$^{\circ}$ and has an energy resolution of 6.85\% for 3.0 MeV $α$ particles. The gain uniformity of the detector is about 10\% (RMS/Mean), tested by the $^{55}$Fe X-ray source.

## Key claim

The fMeta-TPC detector achieves an intrinsic angular resolution within 0.30° and an energy resolution of 6.85% for 3.0 MeV alpha particles in offline tests, but these performance figures are demonstrated specifically for the photonuclear reaction program at the Laser Compton Scattering gamma source, with the detector optimized for studying decay of highly excited alpha-cluster states.

## Method

The fMeta-TPC uses a resistive Micromegas readout with 2048 channels segmented into 32×64 rectangular pixels of varying size (2 mm to 6 mm in x-direction, constant 4.5 mm in z-direction) to balance angular resolution and sensitive area coverage. Offline performance was characterized using a 266 nm UV laser for drift velocity, field homogeneity, and angular resolution measurements, a 241Am alpha source for energy resolution, and a 55Fe X-ray source for gain uniformity mapping via multiple linear regression. Tests were performed in Ar+CH4 (9:1, P10), Ar+iC4H10 (93:7), and He+CO2 (96:4) gas mixtures at various pressures.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic angular resolution within 0.30° (σθ) for the worst case when the laser traverses 6×4.5 mm² pixels; best angular resolution of 0.06° (σθ) at 5.6° incidence angle
- Energy resolution of 6.85% for 3.0 MeV alpha particles from 241Am source in 600 mbar P10 gas
- Gain uniformity of about 10% (RMS/Mean) over 2048 channels measured with 55Fe X-ray source
- Electronic gain variation across 2048 channels within 4% (σ/μ)
- Position resolution derived from angular resolution: optimal 0.71 mm (FWHM), worst within 3.4 mm (FWHM) over 288 mm sensitive length
- Drift velocity measurements agree with theoretical calculations within 5% uncertainty
- Electric field distortion within 3% for double wire-plane field cage and 1% for PCB field cage in sensitive volume
- Electronic system parameters: 40 MSPS sampling rate, 12-bit quantization, 25 μs dead time, dynamic range 2 fC to 3 pC

## Context

This work extends the development of Active Target Time Projection Chambers for low-energy nuclear physics, following designs such as ACTAR TPC at GANIL for shell evolution studies, O-TPC at University of Warsaw for cluster structure, MAIKo at Kyoto University, and TexAT at Texas A&M University. The fMeta-TPC is specifically designed for photonuclear reaction experiments at the Shanghai Laser Electron Gamma Source (SLEGS) beamline, enabling studies of alpha-clustering in light nuclei including the Hoyle state in 12C and analogous cluster states in 16O, 20Ne, and other nuclei. The detector's variable pixel size design and dual field cage options (wire-plane for radioactive ion beams, PCB for photonuclear reactions) enable future experiments on both low-intensity gamma beams and rare isotope beams.
