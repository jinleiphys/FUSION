# Isospin mixing effects in low-energy $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ interaction

> New strong coupled-channel $\bar{K}N - πΣ$ potential, reproducing all existing experimental data and suitable for using in an accurate few-body calculations, is constructed. Isospin breaking effects of direct inclusion of the Coulomb interaction and using of physical masses in calculations are investigated. The $1 s$ level shift and width of kaonic hydrogen, consistent with the scattering data, was obtained and the corresponding exact strong $K^- p$ scattering length was calculated. One- and two-pole form of $Λ(1405)$ resonance was considered.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new phenomenological coupled-channel $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ potential that simultaneously reproduces all existing experimental data, but the authors find that the DEAR kaonic hydrogen data are inconsistent with the existing scattering data, so their potential preferentially reproduces the KEK results rather than the DEAR results.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic separable coupled-channel potential in momentum space with Yamaguchi form factors, directly including the Coulomb interaction in the $K^- p$ subsystem and using physical masses for $\bar{K}N$ channels while using isospin-averaged masses for $\pi \Sigma$ channels. The strong interaction was treated via a Coulomb-modified separable $T$-matrix formalism, with parameters fitted to reproduce $K^- p$ elastic and inelastic cross-sections, threshold branching ratios, kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift and width, and the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance in both one-pole and two-pole versions.

## Key numbers

* One-pole $\Lambda(1405)$: pole position $z_1 = 1409 - i 32$ MeV; two-pole $\Lambda(1405)$: $z_1 = 1412 - i 32$ MeV and $z_2 = 1380 - i 105$ MeV
* One-pole potential: kaonic hydrogen $1s$ level shift $\Delta E_{1s} = -396$ eV, width $\Gamma_{1s} = 370$ eV; two-pole potential: $\Delta E_{1s} = -407$ eV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 476$ eV
* One-pole potential: exact strong $K^- p$ scattering length $a_{K^- p} = -1.07 + i 0.59$ fm; two-pole potential: $a_{K^- p} = -1.08 + i 0.76$ fm
* One-pole potential: isospin non-conserving part of scattering length $a^{nonc}_{K^- p} = 0.0139 + i 0.1077$ fm (real part is 1% of total, imaginary part is 18% of total)
* Two-pole potential: $a^{nonc}_{K^- p} = 0.0851 + i 0.1048$ fm (real part is 8% of total, imaginary part is 14% of total)
* One-pole potential range parameters: $\beta_{\bar{K}N} = 3.4$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta_{\pi \Sigma} = 1.9$ fm$^{-1}$; two-pole: $\beta_{\bar{K}N} = 3.2$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta_{\pi \Sigma} = 1.0$ fm$^{-1}$, $s = -0.87$

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological coupled-channel approach to $\bar{K}N - \pi \Sigma$ interaction by directly including Coulomb effects and physical masses, unlike earlier chiral-based potentials such as Borasoy (2005, 2006) which could not be used in few-body calculations, and unlike the simpler one-channel optical potentials used in approximate few-body calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Weise (2007). The potential is designed for use in accurate three-body coupled-channel Faddeev calculations of $\bar{K}NN - \pi \Sigma N$ systems, and the authors note that the upcoming SIDDHARTA experiment may help distinguish between the one-pole and two-pole $\Lambda(1405)$ interpretations.
