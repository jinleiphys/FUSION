# Effect of continuum couplings in fusion of halo $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb around the Coulomb barrier

> The effect of continuum couplings in the fusion of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb around the Coulomb barrier is studied using a three-body model within a coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism. We investigate in particular the role of continuum-continuum couplings. These are found to hinder total, complete and incomplete fusion processes. Couplings to the projectile $1p_{1/2}$ bound excited state redistribute the complete and incomplete fusion cross sections, but the total fusion cross section remains nearly constant. Results show that continuum-continuum couplings enhance the irreversibility of breakup and reduce the flux that penetrates the Coulomb barrier. Converged total fusion cross sections agree with the experimental ones for energies around the Coulomb barrier, but underestimate those for energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

# Effect of continuum couplings in fusion of halo $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb around the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

Continuum-continuum couplings hinder total, complete, and incomplete fusion processes for the $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb system, contrary to earlier suggestions that breakup couplings might enhance fusion. The converged total fusion cross sections agree with experimental data for energies around the Coulomb barrier but underestimate those for energies well above the Coulomb barrier by about 41%.

## Method

The authors used a three-body model (core + valence neutron + target) within the coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) formalism, implemented with the code FRESCO. They included the projectile's halo structure, transitions to its bound excited state and continuum states for multiple partial waves ($s_{1/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, $d_{5/2}$ and higher), and all couplings between bound and continuum states. Fusion cross sections were defined using a short-ranged imaginary potential inside the Coulomb barrier, with complete fusion defined as absorption from projectile bound channels and incomplete fusion as absorption from breakup channels.

## Key numbers

- The $^{11}$Be ground state energy is -0.50 MeV and the bound excited state ($1p_{1/2}$) energy is -0.18 MeV.
- A maximum continuum energy beyond 9 MeV was needed for converged results; fusion cross sections changed by ~10% around the barrier and ~1.5% well above the barrier when increasing from 9 MeV to 10 MeV.
- A continuum discretisation density greater than 1.67 bins/MeV was needed for convergence; changing from 1.67 to 2 bins/MeV changed fusion cross sections by ~6.5% around the barrier and ~1.6% well above.
- Including continuum partial waves up to $g$-waves and potential multipoles $K \leq 4$ changed fusion cross sections by ~8% (total), ~100% (complete), and ~3% (incomplete) around the barrier, and ~13% well above the barrier, compared to calculations up to $f$-waves and $K \leq 3$.
- Converged total fusion cross sections underestimated experimental data for $^{11}$Be + $^{209}$Bi by ~41% for energies well above the Coulomb barrier.
- The Coulomb barrier for the elastic channel is approximately 36 MeV.
- Couplings to the bound excited state $1p_{1/2}$ changed fusion cross sections by ~10%.
- The contribution to complete fusion from the elastic channel was 1.7-3.4 times larger than from the bound excited state without continuum-continuum couplings, and 1.7-8 times smaller for energies above the barrier when continuum-continuum couplings were included.
- Target excitations increased fusion cross sections by a factor of ~1.28 around the barrier and negligibly well above.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled channels calculations by Hagino et al. (2000) for the same system, which used the isocentrifugal approximation and did not include the projectile's halo structure, continuum-continuum couplings, or bound excited state couplings. The results contradict predictions by Dasso and Vitturi (1994) that breakup couplings might enhance complete fusion. The study shows that continuum-continuum couplings, previously shown to be crucial for breakup of $^{8}$B on $^{58}$Ni by Nunes and Thompson (1999) and Tostevin et al. (2001), also strongly affect fusion dynamics. The disagreement with experimental data for $^{11}$Be + $^{209}$Bi from Signorini et al. (1998, 1999) at above-barrier energies suggests that either the experimental fission cross section was overestimated or the 3n channel (unmeasured) is significant, motivating new experiments.
