# Measures of azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Based on the initial state geometrical symmetry for collisions between two identical heavy ions at high energy, the general form for the one- and two-particle azimuthal distributions is deduced. Relation between these distributions and the usual flow parameters is discussed. New measures for the azimuthal correlations are suggested. Some numerical results on the values of the measures are shown from an event generator for Au+Au collisions with different colliding centralities at 200 GeV.

## Key claim

Based on the initial-state geometrical symmetry of collisions between identical heavy ions, the general forms of one- and two-particle azimuthal distributions are deduced, and new measures for azimuthal correlations are suggested that are independent of the specific interaction dynamics during the evolution of the produced partons and hadrons. The paper argues that the factorization of the two-particle distribution into a product of one-particle distributions, which is often assumed experimentally, is invalidated by the presence of odd-harmonic terms in the azimuthal difference distribution.

## Method

The authors derive the most general Fourier expansions for event-averaged one- and two-particle azimuthal distributions, imposing symmetry conditions from the almond-shaped overlap region of identical nuclei. They then define new correlation measures as the coefficients \(\Delta^\pm_{k,l}\) in the two-particle expansion, which are related to \(\langle\cos(k\Phi_1 \pm l\Phi_2)\rangle\). Numerical results for these coefficients are obtained from the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) event generator for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV across multiple centralities, using charged hadrons with \(|\eta|<1.0\) and separating soft (\(p_T<1\) GeV/\(c\)) and hard (\(p_T>2\) GeV/\(c\)) particles.

## Key numbers

* For soft particles (\(p_T<1\) GeV/\(c\)) at centrality 30-40%: \(v_2=0.039\), \(v_4=0.0019\), \(v_6=1.02\times 10^{-4}\), while \(v_1\simeq v_3\simeq 0\).
* For hard particles (\(p_T>2\) GeV/\(c\)) at centrality 30-40%: \(v_2=0.115\), \(v_4=0.0107\), \(v_6=1.37\times 10^{-3}\).
* For soft-soft pairs at centrality 30-40%: \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1-2\Phi_2)\rangle = 2.36\times 10^{-3}\) and \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1+2\Phi_2)\rangle = 1.51\times 10^{-3}\).
* For hard-hard pairs at centrality 30-40%: \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1-2\Phi_2)\rangle = 2.38\times 10^{-2}\) and \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1+2\Phi_2)\rangle = 1.32\times 10^{-2}\).
* For soft-hard pairs at centrality 30-40%: \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1-2\Phi_2)\rangle = 7.38\times 10^{-3}\) and \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_1+2\Phi_2)\rangle = 4.47\times 10^{-3}\).
* The \(\Delta\Phi\) distribution for hard-hard pairs shows a peak at \(\Delta\Phi=\pi\), while distributions for soft-soft and soft-hard pairs are quite flat.

## Context

This work extends the standard flow formalism by introducing new two-particle correlation coefficients \(\Delta^\pm_{k,l}\) that contain more information than the usual flow parameters \(v_{2k}\). It directly contradicts the factorization assumption used by the RHIC experiments (e.g., Adler et al. 2001) to extract elliptic flow from two-particle correlations, showing that the presence of odd-harmonic terms in the \(\Delta\Phi\) distribution invalidates that factorization. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted \(\cos((2k+1)\Delta\Phi)\) terms in the azimuthal difference distribution, which would provide tighter constraints on theoretical models of heavy ion collisions.
