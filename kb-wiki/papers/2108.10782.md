# An improved spectral approach for solving the nonclassical neutron particle transport equation

> An improvement modification of the Spectral Approach (SA) used for approximating the nonclassical neutral particle transport equation is described in this work. The main focus of the modified SA lies on a slight modification of the nonclassical angular flux representation as a function of truncated Laguerre series. This leads, in some cases, to a considerable decrease of the Laguerre truncation order required to generate accurate solutions. Numerical results are given to illustrate this proposed improvement.

# An improved spectral approach for solving the nonclassical neutron particle transport equation

## Key claim

The modified Spectral Approach (SA) can reduce the required Laguerre truncation order for accurate nonclassical flux solutions by introducing an arbitrary constant alpha into the exponential factor of the angular flux representation, but the optimal choice of alpha depends on the free-path distribution and may introduce numerical concerns from the exponential term.

## Method

The authors modify the standard Spectral Approach by representing the nonclassical angular flux as Psi = psi_hat * exp(-integral(alpha + Sigma_t ds')), where alpha is an arbitrary constant, and expanding psi_hat in a truncated Laguerre series. This changes the kernel functions L_k(Omega) to include an exponential damping factor exp(-alpha s), causing them to decay faster with increasing polynomial order k. The resulting system of equations is solved using the Analytical Discrete Ordinates (ADO) method in slab geometry with Gauss-Legendre angular quadrature.

## Key numbers

- For the standard SA (alpha = 0) with truncation order M = 2, the relative deviation at x* = 10.0 was 1.83E+01 (1830%), and negative flux values were generated at x* = 8.0 and x* = 10.0.
- For the modified SA with alpha = -0.732 and M = 2, the maximum relative deviation across all spatial positions was 2.80E-04 (0.028%).
- The standard SA required M = 20 to achieve comparable accuracy (maximum relative deviation 1.53E-04 at x* = 10.0).
- The problem used a slab of length 20 mean free paths (sigma_t = 1), scattering ratio c = 0.5, and an isotropic source spanning x in [9.5, 10.5].

## Context

This work extends the Spectral Approach first described by Moraes (2020) for solving the nonclassical transport equation originally derived by Larsen (2001). The modification addresses numerical difficulties reported by Moraes (2021) where increasing the Laguerre truncation order could degrade accuracy due to finite precision arithmetic when using analytic nodal methods. The approach enables more efficient deterministic solutions of nonclassical transport problems relevant to nuclear engineering (Vasques, 2015) and computer graphics (Bitterli, 2018), though further convergence analysis of both the standard and modified SA remains as future work.
