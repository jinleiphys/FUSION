# D-mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate the in-medium $D$ and $\bar D$-meson masses in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in an effective chiral model. The $D$ and $\bar D$ - mass modifications arising from their interactions with the nucleons and the scalar mesons in the effective hadronic model are seen to be appreciable at high densities and have a strong isospin dependence. These mass modifications can open the channels of the decay of the charmonium states ($Ψ^\prime$, $χ_c$, $J/Ψ$) to $D \bar D$ pairs in the dense hadronic matter. The isospin asymmetry in the doublet $D=(D^0,D^+)$ is seen to be particularly appreciable at high densities and should show in observables like their production and flow in asymmetric heavy ion collisions in the compressed baryonic matter experiments in the future facility of FAIR, GSI. The results of the present work are compared to calculations of the $D(\bar D$) in-medium masses in the literature using the QCD sum rule approach, quark meson coupling model, coupled channel approach as well as from the studies of quarkonium dissociation using heavy quark potentials from lattice QCD at finite temperatures.

## Key claim

In isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, the in-medium masses of D and anti-D mesons show appreciable modifications at high densities with strong isospin dependence, particularly for the D=(D0, D+) doublet, though the exact magnitude depends on the specific interaction terms included (Weinberg-Tomozawa, scalar exchange, and range terms) and the asymmetry parameter.

## Method

The authors generalize a chiral SU(3) effective hadronic model to SU(4) flavor to derive interactions of D and anti-D mesons with nucleons and scalar mesons (sigma, zeta, delta) in asymmetric nuclear matter. They solve the dispersion relations for D and anti-D mesons in the mean-field approximation, computing self-energies from the Weinberg-Tomozawa vectorial interaction, scalar meson exchange terms, and range terms, with parameters fixed from kaon-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density (rho_0) for isospin symmetric matter (eta=0), the D+ mass drops by about 81 MeV from its vacuum value of 1869 MeV
- At rho_0 for eta=0, the D- mass drops by about 30 MeV, giving a mass splitting between D+ and D- of about 51 MeV
- For asymmetry parameter eta=0.5 at rho_0, the D0 mass rises by about 23 MeV from its eta=0 value of 1783 MeV
- For eta=0.5 at 5 rho_0, the D0 mass rises by about 105 MeV from its eta=0 value of 1441 MeV
- For eta=0.5 at rho_0, the D+ mass drops by about 18 MeV from its eta=0 value
- For eta=0.5 at 5 rho_0, the D- mass is 1690 MeV, higher by about 31 MeV from the isospin symmetric case
- For eta=0.5 at 5 rho_0, the anti-D0 mass is 1698 MeV, higher by about 43 MeV from the isospin symmetric case
- The D-decay constant is taken as 135 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) model previously used for kaon and antikaon modifications in asymmetric nuclear matter by Mishra and collaborators (2004-2007) to the charmed meson sector. The results are compared to QCD sum rule calculations by Arata (1993) and Weise (2008), the quark meson coupling model by Tsushima (1997), coupled channel approaches by Lutz (2004) and Mizutani (2006), and lattice QCD studies of heavy quark potentials. The mass modifications can open decay channels of charmonium states (Psi', chi_c, J/Psi) to D anti-D pairs in dense matter, which should be observable in asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the future FAIR facility at GSI.
