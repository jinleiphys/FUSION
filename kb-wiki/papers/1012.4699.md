# From many body wee parton dynamics to perfect fluid: a standard model for heavy ion collisions

> We discuss a standard model of heavy ion collisions that has emerged both from the experimental results of the RHIC program and associated theoretical developments. We comment briefly on the impact of early results of the LHC program on this picture. We consider how this standard model of heavy ion collisions could be solidified or falsified in future experiments at RHIC, the LHC and a future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim
A "standard model" of heavy ion collisions has emerged from RHIC experimental results and theoretical developments, but this picture could be solidified or falsified by future experiments at RHIC, the LHC, and a future Electron-Ion Collider. The author acknowledges that thermalization remains poorly understood even in weak coupling, and that the standard model may have "weak chinks" in its armor.

## Method
The paper reviews the chronology of heavy ion collisions as described by the standard model: Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial states, Glasma formation from the collision, thermalization to a strongly correlated Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP), hydrodynamic expansion as a nearly perfect fluid, and hadronization. The author evaluates evidence from RHIC and early LHC data for each stage, comparing weak coupling QCD calculations, AdS/CFT correspondence results, and phenomenological models against experimental measurements of multiplicities, flow coefficients, jet quenching, and ridge correlations.

## Key numbers
- The sQGP lasts for approximately 3 x 10^{-23} seconds in heavy ion collisions
- The conjectured lower bound on shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is eta/s = 1/(4 pi), derived for N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory
- Empirical extraction gives eta/s less than or equal to 0.4 from viscous hydrodynamic models
- R_AA for pi^0's at RHIC is suppressed by a factor of 5 relative to binary nucleon-nucleon collisions
- LHC Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV/nucleon show integrated v_2 about 30% higher than at RHIC
- Saturation model predictions for LHC multiplicities were low by about 20% compared to ALICE data for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV/nucleon
- A 20% discrepancy in multiplicity corresponds to a 10% discrepancy in the saturation scale Q_S
- The saturation scale in the adjoint representation is obtained by multiplying the fundamental representation Q_S^2 by 9/4
- For n-particle correlations in the Glasma flux tube picture, correlations are proportional to (S_perp / (1/Q_S^2))^{1-n} for n greater than or equal to 2

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework developed in works on Color Glass Condensates (GLR, Iancu-Mueller, MV), JIMWLK equations, and the Glasma (Lappi-M, KMW) to synthesize a comprehensive picture of heavy ion collisions. It builds on the experimental program at RHIC and early LHC results from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and STAR collaborations. The paper enables future tests of the standard model through precision measurements of flow, jet quenching, and ridge correlations at RHIC, LHC, and a future Electron-Ion Collider, and identifies open questions including the mechanism of thermalization and the applicability of weak coupling descriptions.
