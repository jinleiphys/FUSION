# Magnetic properties in the inhomogeneous chiral phase

> We investigate the magnetic properties of quark matter in the inhomogeneous chiral phase, where both scalar and pseudoscalar condensates spatially modulate. The energy spectrum of the lowest Landau level becomes asymmetric about zero in the external magnetic field, and gives rise to the remarkably magnetic properties: quark matter has a spontaneous magnetization, while the magnetic susceptibility does not diverge on the critical point.

## Key claim
Quark matter in the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase exhibits spontaneous magnetization, but the magnetic susceptibility does not diverge at the second-order critical point, unlike in familiar ferromagnetic systems. This behavior is unique to the inhomogeneous chiral phase transition.

## Method
The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit with the DCDW ansatz in the mean-field approximation. The thermodynamic potential is expanded in powers of the external magnetic field \(eB\), and the expansion coefficients are computed analytically, with careful treatment of the spectral asymmetry and the \(\eta\)-invariant for the lowest Landau level. The order parameters \(m\) (dynamical mass) and \(q\) (wavevector) are determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential at each chemical potential \(\mu\) and temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers
- Spontaneous magnetization \(M_0\) is nonzero only in the DCDW phase; at \(\mu = 340\) MeV and \(T=0\), the magnetic field on the surface of a uniform magnetized sphere is estimated as \(B_{\text{mag}} = \frac{8\pi}{3}M_0 \sim 10^{16}\) G.
- The DCDW phase exists in a \(\mu\) region bounded by first-order and second-order phase transition points; at \(T=0\) and \(T=30\) MeV, the range of \(\mu\) narrows and the magnitude of \(M_0\) decreases with increasing temperature.
- The normalized magnetic susceptibility \(\chi^{\text{nom}}\) shows a cusp at the point where valence quarks begin to appear and is discontinuous at the first-order phase transition, but shows no singularity at the second-order critical point.

## Context
This work extends the study of the inhomogeneous chiral phase in QCD, previously analyzed using chiral effective models by Nakano (2004) and Nickel (2009) and the Schwinger-Dyson approach by Mueller (2013). The DCDW configuration was introduced by Nakano (2004) and was shown to be favored in external magnetic fields by Nishiyama (2015). The paper builds on the authors' prior work (Yoshiike, 2015) on spontaneous magnetization in the DCDW phase and uses the formalism of spectral asymmetry and the \(\eta\)-invariant from Niemi (1983) and the Wess-Zumino-Witten term from Son (2004). The results suggest that the strong magnetic fields observed in magnetars (\(B > 10^{12-15}\) G) could be explained by microscopic magnetization of quark matter in neutron stars.
