# Nucleon-nucleon cross sections in neutron-rich matter and isospin transport in heavy-ion reactions at intermediate energies

> Nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections are evaluated in neutron-rich matter using a scaling model according to nucleon effective masses. It is found that the in-medium NN cross sections are not only reduced but also have a different isospin dependence compared with the free-space ones. Because of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting the difference between nn and pp scattering cross sections increases with the increasing isospin asymmetry of the medium. Within the transport model IBUU04, the in-medium NN cross sections are found to influence significantly the isospin transport in heavy-ion reactions. With the in-medium NN cross sections, a symmetry energy of $E_{sym}(ρ)\approx 31.6(ρ/ρ_{0})^{0.69}$ was found most acceptable compared with both the MSU isospin diffusion data and the presently acceptable neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb. The isospin dependent part $K_{asy}(ρ_{0})$ of isobaric nuclear incompressibility was further narrowed down to $-500\pm 50$ MeV. The possibility of determining simultaneously the in-medium NN cross sections and the symmetry energy was also studied. The proton transverse flow, or even better the combined transverse flow of neutrons and protons, can be used as a probe of the in-medium NN cross sections without much hindrance from the uncertainties of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, evaluated using a scaling model based on nucleon effective masses, are not only reduced compared to free-space values but also exhibit a splitting between nn and pp cross sections that grows with increasing isospin asymmetry of the medium; this alters the density dependence of the symmetry energy extracted from isospin transport data, and the authors find that a symmetry energy of \(E_{sym}(\rho)\approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_{0})^{0.69}\) is most acceptable when both the MSU isospin diffusion data and the neutron-skin thickness in \({}^{208}\)Pb are considered together with the in-medium cross sections.

## Method

The authors evaluated in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections using a scaling model where the reduction factor \(R_{medium} = (\mu_{NN}^{*}/\mu_{NN})^{2}\) depends on the reduced masses of the colliding nucleon pairs in the medium, which are derived from the nucleon effective masses given by the momentum-dependent single-particle potential of the modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI) used in the IBUU04 transport model. They then performed transport model simulations of \({}^{132}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn and related reactions at 50 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of about 6 fm, calculating the isospin transport observable \(R_i\) from the average isospin asymmetry of projectile-like residues, and compared results obtained with free-space and in-medium cross sections for four different symmetry energy parametrizations (x = 1, 0, -1, -2). The transverse flow of neutrons and protons was also calculated at beam energies of 50 and 400 MeV/A to assess its sensitivity to the in-medium cross sections versus the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy found most acceptable is \(E_{sym}(\rho)\approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_{0})^{0.69}\), with the exponent \(\gamma\) constrained between 0.69 and 1.05 when using in-medium cross sections.
* The isospin-dependent part of the isobaric nuclear incompressibility was narrowed down to \(K_{asy}(\rho_{0}) = -500 \pm 50\) MeV using the in-medium cross sections, compared to \(-550 \pm 100\) MeV with free-space cross sections.
* In the reaction \({}^{132}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn at 50 MeV/A and impact parameter 5 fm, the maximum density reached at 10 fm/c was about \(1.4\rho/\rho_{0}\).
* The reduction factor \(R_{medium}\) for in-medium NN cross sections showed on average as much as 50% reduction in the early stage of the reaction (at 10 fm/c) for \({}^{132}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn at 50 MeV/A and impact parameter 5 fm.
* The neutron-skin thickness in \({}^{208}\)Pb consistent with the favored symmetry energy is about 0.25 fm.

## Context

This work extends the effective mass scaling model for in-medium NN cross sections, previously applied to isospin symmetric matter by Neg (1981), Pan (1982), and Gale (1987), to isospin asymmetric neutron-rich matter. It directly builds on the IBUU04 transport model studies of Chen (2005) that extracted a symmetry energy of \(E_{sym}(\rho)\approx 32(\rho/\rho_{0})^{1.1}\) using free-space cross sections from the MSU isospin diffusion data of Betty (2004). The results enable simultaneous determination of the in-medium NN cross sections and the symmetry energy from heavy-ion reactions, and suggest that the combined transverse flow of neutrons and protons can serve as a probe of the in-medium cross sections largely independent of symmetry energy uncertainties.
