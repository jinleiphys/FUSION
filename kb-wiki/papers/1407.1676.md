# Proton root-mean-square radii and electron scattering

> The standard procedure of extracting the proton root-mean-square radii from models for the Sachs form factors $G_e (q)$ and $G_m (q)$ fitted to elastic electron-proton scattering data %has a serious flaw. is more uncertain than traditionally assumed. The extrapolation of $G(q)$, from the region $q_{min} < q < q_{max}$ covered by data to momentum transfer $q=0$ where the $rms$-radius is obtained, often depends on uncontrolled properties of the parameterization used. Only when ensuring that the corresponding densities have a physical behavior at large radii $r$ can reliable $rms$-radii be determined.

## Key claim

The standard procedure of extracting proton root-mean-square radii from fits of Sachs form factors to elastic electron-proton scattering data is more uncertain than traditionally assumed, because the extrapolation from the measured momentum-transfer region to q=0 depends on uncontrolled properties of the parameterization; only by ensuring that the corresponding densities have a physical behavior at large radii can reliable rms-radii be determined.

## Method

The authors analyze several recent form-factor fits to elastic electron-proton scattering data, including Inverse Polynomial (IP) fits by Bernauer et al. (2010), Continued Fraction (CF) fits by Lorenz et al. (2012), and a Pade fit of their own, examining the behavior of the corresponding charge and magnetic densities at large radii. They demonstrate that parameterizations which produce poles at q above the data range, or otherwise uncontrolled behavior at large q, generate unphysical density tails that falsify the extrapolation to q=0. The authors advocate instead for fits that constrain the large-radius density using a physical model based on the least-bound Fock component (the n+pi+ configuration), as implemented in Sick (2012).

## Key numbers

- The muonic Hydrogen Lamb shift gives a charge rms-radius of 0.8409 +/- 0.0004 fm (Pohl et al., 2010), which disagrees with the value 0.8775 +/- 0.005 fm from world electron scattering data (Mohr et al., 2010) and with 0.8758 +/- 0.0077 fm from electronic Hydrogen transition energies (Parthey et al., 2011).
- For the magnetic rms-radius from IP fits to Bernauer et al. (2010) data, the value jumped from 0.76 fm (order N=7) to 0.96 fm (order N=10), with the N=10 fit having the best chi-squared per degree of freedom.
- A 4-parameter Pade fit to the same data restricted to q_max = 2 fm^-1 gave an excellent chi-squared of 1.06 per degree of freedom but yielded a charge rms-radius of 1.48 fm.
- A fit of the world data using the constrained-tail procedure described in Sick (2012) yielded an rms-radius of 0.886 +/- 0.008 fm.

## Context

This work extends the critical scrutiny of proton radius determinations that followed the muonic Hydrogen Lamb shift result of Pohl et al. (2010), which revealed a large discrepancy with earlier values from electronic Hydrogen and electron scattering. The authors show that standard parameterizations such as the Inverse Polynomial fits of Bernauer et al. (2010) and the Continued Fraction fits of Lorenz et al. (2012) can produce unphysical density tails, and they contrast this with the approach used for heavier nuclei (A>2) where densities are parameterized directly in r-space with an implicit assumption that density falls to zero at large r. The proposed solution, using a physical model for the large-radius tail based on the n+pi+ Fock component as in Sick (2012), enables more reliable radius extraction and can help resolve the proton radius puzzle.
