# Determining Fundamental Properties of Matter Created in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Posterior distributions for physical parameters describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions, such as the viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, are extracted through a comparison of hydrodynamic-based transport models to experimental results from 100$A$ GeV + 100$A$ GeV Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). By simultaneously varying six parameters and by evaluating several classes of observables, we are able to explore the complex intertwined dependencies of observables to model parameters. We obtain a full multi-dimensional posterior distribution for the model output given a large set of experimental observations, the methods developed here provide a range of acceptable values for each parameter, and reveal correlations between them. The breadth of observables and the number of parameters considered here go far beyond previous studies in this field. The statistical tools, which are based upon Gaussian Process emulators, are tested in detail and should be extendable to larger data sets and a higher number of parameters.

## Key claim
The paper extracts posterior distributions for six physical parameters describing relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, by comparing a hybrid hydrodynamic+transport model to experimental data from 100A GeV + 100A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the results are conditional on assuming a fixed lattice-QCD equation of state and zero bulk viscosity, and the analysis is limited to soft observables (spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic radii).

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid model combining viscous hydrodynamics (for the quark-gluon plasma phase, approximately 1-7 fm/c) with a microscopic hadronic cascade (for the hadronic phase, approximately 7-20 fm/c). They varied six parameters simultaneously: four describing the initial energy-density and flow profiles fed into the hydrodynamic module, and two describing the shear viscosity and its energy dependence above the transition temperature. A Gaussian Process emulator was trained on 729 full model runs at carefully chosen points in the six-dimensional parameter space, then used as a surrogate for the full model in a Markov-Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) exploration to map the posterior distribution.

## Key numbers
- The Gaussian Process emulator was trained on 729 full model runs and tested against 32 withheld model runs; the emulator error summed over six principal components was less than unity for 31 of the 32 test points.
- The MCMC trace stored 10^6 points after a 100,000-step burn-in phase, with steps in each dimension of size 0.1 (in scaled parameter space where the flat prior had unit variance, ranging from -√3 to +√3).
- For the likelihood calculation, the emulator error per degree of freedom was set to σ_e = 0.1, which increased the width of the posterior region by only a few percent.
- Model calculations for kaon and pion spectra required normalization scaling factors of 0.85 and 0.6 respectively, due to missing chemical reactions.

## Context
This work extends earlier phenomenological studies that varied only two or three parameters (e.g., Romatschke and Romatschke (2007); Drescher et al. (2007); Soltz et al. (2013); Heinz, Shen, and Song (2012)) by simultaneously varying six parameters and considering multiple classes of observables (spectra, elliptic flow v2, and femtoscopic radii). The Gaussian Process emulator approach was adapted from cosmological parameter extraction (Schneider et al. (2008); Habib et al. (2007)) and galaxy formation studies (Gomez et al. (2012)). The methods are designed to scale to larger data sets and more parameters, enabling future inclusion of observables such as dilepton emission, higher flow harmonics, and species-dependent flow, as well as relaxation of assumptions about the equation of state and chemical equilibration.
