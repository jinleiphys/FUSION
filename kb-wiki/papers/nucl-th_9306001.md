# From Skyrmions to NN phaseshifts

> We study the $NN$ phaseshifts in a Hamiltonian obtained from quantization of the collective modes in the Skyrme model. We show that a combination of an adiabatic and diabatic approximation gives a good $NN$ force, with sufficient attraction to produce a bound deuteron. The description of the repulsive core appears to be the main cause for the remaining discrepancies between the Skyrme model force and phenomenology. Finally we discuss the possibility of finding non-strange dibaryon resonances in the $J^π=3^+$ channel.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model, when quantized using a combination of adiabatic and diabatic approximations, produces a nucleon-nucleon force that yields phase shifts surprisingly close to phenomenological ones, but the repulsive core remains the main source of discrepancy; a simple 6% rescaling of the potential parameters (excluding the most repulsive term) is sufficient to obtain realistic deuteron binding and singlet attraction.

## Method

The author starts from the Skyrme model Lagrangian and constructs a collective Hamiltonian for the B=2 system using a product Ansatz and numerically determined distorted Skyrmion configurations constrained by reflection symmetries. The classical Hamiltonian is requantized using an SU(4) interacting boson model with boson number equal to N_c=3, yielding a coupled N-Δ channels problem. The NN potential is then extracted via a Born-Oppenheimer (adiabatic) approximation for large separations, supplemented by a diabatic prescription that follows unmixed potential curves through avoided crossings at short distances. Phase shifts are computed by integrating the Schrödinger equation from a hard-core radius of 0.3 fm to a matching radius where solutions are matched to spherical Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron is bound by 0.053 MeV in the unadjusted calculation, reflecting too weak attraction.
- After a 6% rescaling of all potential parameters except b_4, the deuteron binding energy becomes 1.77 MeV.
- The hard-core radius of 0.7 fm gives wrong phase shifts; a radius of 0.3 fm is used throughout.
- In the J^π=3^+, T=0 channel, the Δ-Δ potential supports a bound state with binding energy of about 5 MeV.
- A sharp resonance is found in the NN D-wave, 5 MeV below the Δ-Δ threshold (at 600 MeV in the calculation).
- A Δ width of only 1 MeV is enough to destroy the resonant behavior.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Walet and Amado, 1992; Walet and Amado, 1993) that showed the Skyrme model gives a good qualitative description of intermediate and long range nuclear forces. It directly computes phase shifts from the model and compares them to the phenomenological VPI/SAID analysis of Arndt and Roper (1987). The paper also addresses the question of non-strange dibaryon resonances in the J^π=3^+ channel, previously studied by Wang et al. using a quark delocalization scheme, and finds much weaker attraction than their reported 400 MeV deep potential. The results enable future work on improving the repulsive core by including vector mesons (ρ, ω, A_1) and on understanding the behavior of inertial parameters using the Atiyah-Manton Ansatz.
