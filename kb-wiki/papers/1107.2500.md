# Thermal radiation from an expanding viscous medium

> The effects of viscosity on the space time evolution of QGP produced in nuclear collisions at RHIC energies have been studied. The entropy generated due to the viscous motion of the fluid has been taken into account in constraining the initial temperature by the final multiplicity (measured at the freeze-out point). The viscous effects on the photon spectra has been introduced consistently through the evolution dynamics and phase space factors of the particles participating in the production process. We notice a stronger effect on the photon spectra originating from QGP than hadronic matter. A detectable shift is observed in the space-time integrated p_T distribution of photons due to dissipative effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that viscous effects produce a detectable shift in the transverse momentum distribution of thermal photons from quark-gluon plasma produced in nuclear collisions at RHIC energies, but this shift is partially compensated when the initial temperature is consistently constrained by the final hadron multiplicity after accounting for entropy generated during viscous evolution.

## Method

The authors model the space-time evolution of the QGP using (1+1)-dimensional boost-invariant relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with shear viscosity, incorporating a first-order phase transition from QGP to hadronic matter. They compute thermal photon production from QGP via Compton and annihilation processes and from hadronic matter through reactions involving pions, rho mesons, omega mesons, and eta mesons, including viscous corrections to the phase-space distribution functions of the participating particles. The initial temperature is determined by matching the calculated final charged-particle multiplicity to experimental data, subtracting the entropy generated by viscous dissipation during the evolution.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures for Au+Au collisions at RHIC: 328 MeV for ideal fluid (eta/s = 0), 315 MeV for eta/s = 1/(4 pi), and 302 MeV for eta/s = 2/(4 pi), all constrained by the same final multiplicity.
- Initial thermalization time assumed: tau_i = 0.6 fm/c.
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f (not given numerically in the text, but used as the endpoint of evolution).
- The photon production rate from Compton and annihilation processes is a factor of 3-4 lower than the complete order-alpha_s calculation of Arnold (2001).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of viscous effects on photon spectra by JPG (2008) and Akc97 (1997) by consistently including viscous corrections to both the phase-space factors and the hydrodynamic evolution, and by considering contributions from both QGP and hadronic phases. It also accounts for entropy generation during viscous evolution when constraining the initial temperature, which was neglected in prior approaches such as those by Akc (2008), Bhatt (2010), and Dusling (2010). The results suggest that future high-precision measurements at LHC and RHIC may enable estimation of eta/s from thermal photon spectra.
