# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter View of Goldstone-Type Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Describing the lightest pseudoscalar mesons as bound states of quark and antiquark within the framework of an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism constructed such as to retain (in contrast to Salpeter's equation) as much information on the relativistic effects provided by the full quark propagator as conceivable allows for a surprisingly simple implementation of their near masslessness mandatory for their interpretability as pseudo-Goldstone bosons related to the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetries of quantum chromodynamics.

# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter View of Goldstone-Type Pseudoscalar Mesons

## Key claim

The instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism developed by the authors, which retains more relativistic effects from the full quark propagator than Salpeter's equation, can accommodate nearly massless pseudoscalar mesons as pseudo-Goldstone bosons, but only when the interaction kernel takes a specific form: a configuration-space potential that closely resembles an infinite square well, with no singularities or nonconfinement issues that plagued earlier Salpeter-equation analyses.

## Method

The authors start from a three-dimensional reduction of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation that retains the full quark propagator (mass function and wave-function renormalization) rather than approximating it by free propagators as in Salpeter's equation. They impose flavour symmetry, Fierz symmetry (using the linear combination of tensor products gamma_mu ⊗ gamma^mu + gamma_5 ⊗ gamma_5 - 1 ⊗ 1), and spherical symmetry to reduce the bound-state problem to a system of two coupled radial equations for the spin-singlet Salpeter amplitude components. By setting the bound-state mass to zero (the Goldstone limit), they decouple these equations and derive an integral equation for the surviving Salpeter component, which they then invert via Fourier-Bessel transforms to extract the configuration-space potential V(r) that would produce a massless pseudoscalar bound state. The required input for the quark propagator functions M(k^2) and Z(k^2) is taken from numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger equation in the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Maris-Tandy model (1999), with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude related to the quark propagator via the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- The configuration-space potential at the spatial origin: V(0) = -1.91772 GeV.
- The single zero of the potential occurs at r = 15.6978 GeV^{-1}.
- The average interquark distance for the massless meson ground state: ⟨r⟩ = 0.483 fm.
- The root-mean-square radius: √⟨r²⟩ = 0.535 fm.
- For comparison, the experimental pion charge radius: √⟨r_π²⟩ = (0.672 ± 0.008) fm.
- Parameters for the quark mass function ansatz: a = 0.112918 GeV, b = 0.870237 GeV², γ = 1.53153, c = 0.371719 GeV, d = 1.39261 GeV^{-2}.
- Parameters for the wave-function renormalization ansatz: u = 1.01234, v = 0.374334, x = 0.229377 GeV^{-1}, y = 0.258882 GeV^{-2}, z = 0.592377 GeV^{-4}, δ = 0.313777.
- Parameters for the Salpeter amplitude parametrization: s = 1.16176 GeV, η = 4.43353.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous inversion analyses of the Salpeter equation for Goldstone bosons (Lucha and Schöberl, 2015; 2016), which encountered parameter-dependent singularities or nonconfinement, by using a more sophisticated instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism (Lucha and Schöberl, 2005) that retains full quark propagator information. The key advance is that the resulting potential exhibits a smooth, confining square-well shape without the pathologies seen in Salpeter-equation treatments, and the analysis now includes the wave-function renormalization factor Z(k²) that was neglected in earlier work. The approach enables a direct connection between Dyson-Schwinger solutions for the quark propagator and the effective interquark potential for pseudoscalar mesons, and the resulting hadronic size (≈0.5 fm) is reasonably close to the experimental pion charge radius, lending credibility to the formalism for further studies of light meson structure.
