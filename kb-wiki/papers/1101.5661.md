# Non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy ion collisions at low SIS energies

> The Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, a microscopic transport model, is used to study the directed and elliptic collective flows and the nuclear stopping in Au+Au collisions at incident energies covered by INDRA and lower-energy FOPI experiments. It is seen clearly that these observables are sensitive to both, the potential terms (including iso-scalar and iso-vector parts as well as the momentum dependent term) in the equation of state (EoS) and the collision term (including the Pauli-blocking and the medium-modified nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section (NNECS)). The momentum modifications of both, the mean-field potentials and the density dependent NNECS, are found to be sensitive to the collectivity of heavy-ion collisions. At INDRA energies ($\le 150$~MeV/nucleon), the dynamic transport with a soft EoS with momentum dependence and with the momentum-modified density-dependent NNECS describes the directed flow exhibited by hydrogen isotopes ($Z=1$) emitted at mid-rapidity fairly well.

## Key claim

The directed and elliptic collective flows and nuclear stopping in Au+Au collisions at INDRA and lower-energy FOPI energies are sensitive to both the potential terms (including isoscalar, isovector, and momentum-dependent parts) in the equation of state and the collision term (including Pauli-blocking and medium-modified nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections), but the momentum modifications of both the mean-field potentials and the density-dependent NNECS are found to be sensitive to the collectivity of heavy-ion collisions, with the caveat that at INDRA energies (≤150 MeV/nucleon) only a soft EoS with momentum dependence combined with momentum-modified density-dependent NNECS describes the directed flow of hydrogen isotopes fairly well.

## Method

The Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model was used to simulate approximately 120,000 events of 197Au+197Au collisions at beam energies of 40, 50, 60, 80, 100, 150, and 400 MeV/nucleon within an impact parameter region of 0-7.5 fm. Fourteen different parameterizations of the UrQMD model were compared, varying the Pauli-blocking treatment (default versus new phase-space criteria), the equation of state (soft with momentum dependence SM, soft S, or cascade), the density-dependent medium correction factor Fu for nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections (parameter sets FU1, FU2, FU3), and the momentum modification Fp of Fu (parameter sets FP1, FP2, FP3, or no pNN limit). Clusters were constructed using a phase-space coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff P0=0.2 GeV/c and relative distance cutoff R0=2.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow slope of Z=1 particles from semi-central (b=2-5.5 fm) Au+Au collisions at 40 MeV/nucleon is negative (experimental value), while at 150 MeV/nucleon it is positive; the UrQMD model with soft EoS with momentum dependence and momentum-modified density-dependent NNECS describes these data fairly well.
- Nuclear stopping observable vartl for free protons from central (b=0-2 fm) Au+Au collisions at 80 MeV/nucleon: calculations with new Pauli-blocking treatment yield values smaller than unity, approaching the experimental value 0.80 ± 0.03 measured with INDRA.
- Elliptic flow v2 for free protons from semi-peripheral (b=5.5-7.5 fm) Au+Au collisions at 40 MeV/nucleon is positive with values near v2 = 0.08 experimentally; the new Pauli-blocking treatment gives results much closer to this value than the default treatment.
- At reduced density u=2, the density-dependent correction factor Fu values are 0.35 (FU1), 0.27 (FU2), and 0.17 (FU3) for the three parameter sets.
- The incompressibility K=200 MeV for the soft EoS with momentum dependence (SM-EoS).

## Context

This work extends previous investigations using the UrQMD model (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999) and earlier QMD-based studies (Aichelin 1986, 1991) by incorporating self-consistent medium modifications of both mean-field potentials and nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections, addressing discrepancies found in comparisons with INDRA and FOPI data (Lukasik 2004, Andronic 2006, Reisdorf 2006, 2010). The results contradict earlier treatments that considered medium effects only in the mean-field part but not in the collision part, which the authors argue led to unreliable conclusions. This work enables systematic studies of the momentum dependence of in-medium cross sections and its role in collective flow excitation functions at low SIS energies, and suggests that further investigation is still needed at these energies.
