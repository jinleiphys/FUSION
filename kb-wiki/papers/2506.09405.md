# Spin alignment of Quarkonia: A Possible Probe of a Deconfined QCD matter in Heavy-ion Collisions at TeV Energies

> In this study, we investigate the influence of deconfined QCD matter on quarkonium spin alignment in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We estimate the spin alignment of charmonium ($J/ψ$, and $ψ$(2S)) and bottomonium ($Υ$(1S), and $Υ$(2S)) states by calculating the energy eigenvalues in a thermal rotating medium. We solve the Schrödinger equation with a medium-modified color-singlet potential considering the coupling of spin with vorticity and magnetic field. Furthermore, we evaluate the effect of medium temperature, vorticity, magnetic field, and momentum-space anisotropy on the elements of the spin density matrix. Our findings reveal that vorticity increases the spin alignment, while the magnetic fields and anisotropy modify the observables in a state-dependent manner. These findings deepen our understanding of quarkonium spin alignment in an anisotropic magneto-vortical thermal medium, shedding light on spin transport phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Vorticity increases quarkonium spin alignment (deviation of ρ₀₀ from 1/3), while magnetic fields and momentum-space anisotropy modify the observables in a state-dependent manner; the effect of medium vorticity was found to be marginal for ω ≤ 0.16 fm⁻¹ (corresponding to C = 1 fm and r = 1 fm).

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation numerically for charmonium (J/ψ, ψ(2S)) and bottomonium (Υ(1S), Υ(2S)) states using a medium-modified color-singlet potential that includes spin-vorticity and spin-magnetic field couplings in a thermal rotating medium. The energy eigenvalues are obtained on a 10⁴-point logarithmically spaced finite spatial grid, with the Debye mass modified separately for strong magnetic fields and for momentum-space anisotropy. The spin density matrix elements are then computed from the energy eigenvalues using a thermal density operator transformed into a rotating frame.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological approaches to vector meson spin alignment that began with Liang (2004) and includes the quark coalescence model of Yang (2017) and Sheng (2020), as well as local spin alignment studies by Xia (2020). The authors incorporate simultaneous effects of vorticity, magnetic field, and momentum-space anisotropy on quarkonium spin alignment, building on prior work that treated these effects separately. The results enable experimental discrimination between global and local spin alignment mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions at TeV energies, particularly through the off-diagonal elements of the spin density matrix which encode quantum coherence and local spin structure.
