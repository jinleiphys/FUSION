# Pairing effects on neutron matter equation of state and symmetry energy at subsaturation densities

> Within the framework of BCS theory and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, we employ various microscopic pairing gaps and effective pairing interactions to study pairing effects on the equation of state (EOS) of neutron matter and the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. We find pairing effects may have considerable contributions to the EOS of neutron matter at very low densities ($\lesssim 0.02~\rm{fm}^{-3}$), while only have a small impact on the symmetry energy at subsatruation densities. In addition, the reliability of the parabolic approximation for the isospin asymmetry dependence of nuclear matter EOS with pairing correlations included is also discussed.

# Pairing effects on neutron matter equation of state and symmetry energy at subsaturation densities

## Key claim
Pairing correlations can reduce the neutron matter equation of state by about 20% at very low densities around 0.005 fm⁻³, but this effect becomes negligible (below 0.25 MeV) above approximately 0.02 fm⁻³; pairing effects on the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities are small (maximum about 0.9 MeV, or 4.5% of the symmetry energy) and can usually be safely neglected, though the magnitude depends on the isospin dependence of the nucleon effective mass.

## Method
Within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory combined with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model, the authors employed several microscopic pairing gaps (from Argonne V18, chiral N3LO, Brueckner theory, and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo) and effective contact pairing interactions (SLy4+IS, SLy4+IV, LNS+IV, SLy4+MSH) to compute the pairing energy density and its contribution to the equation of state of pure neutron matter and the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities. The pairing gap equation and density equation were solved self-consistently for each pairing interaction, and the symmetry energy was evaluated both exactly from the second derivative of the energy with respect to isospin asymmetry and via the parabolic approximation.

## Key numbers
- Neutron matter EOS is reduced by about 20% at densities around ρ = 0.005 fm⁻³ when using N3LO Chiral (BCS) or AV18 (BCS) pairing gaps.
- Pairing contribution to neutron matter EOS becomes less than 0.25 MeV above about ρ = 0.02 fm⁻³.
- For the LNS+IV effective pairing interaction, the pairing contribution to the symmetry energy reaches a maximum of about 0.9 MeV at density ρ = 0.08 fm⁻³.
- The maximum pairing contribution to the symmetry energy is about 4.5% of the symmetry energy itself (which is about 20 MeV).
- For SLy4-based effective pairing interactions (SLy4+IS, SLy4+IV, SLy4+MSH), the magnitude of the pairing contribution to the symmetry energy is less than about 0.5 MeV.
- The cutoff energy for effective contact pairing interactions is taken to be 60 MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density is ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological constraints on the symmetry energy and neutron matter EOS at subsaturation densities from analyses of nuclear masses (Zha13, Bro13), isobaric analog states and neutron skin data (Dan14), and electric dipole polarizability in ²⁰⁸Pb (Zha15), which were typically obtained without including nucleon pairing correlations. It also complements ab initio calculations using chiral effective field theory (Mac11, Tew13), self-consistent Green function method (Car14), coupled-cluster method (Hag14), and Quantum Monte Carlo (Car15), which do include pairing. The results enable more precise comparisons between phenomenological models and microscopic calculations, and suggest that future studies should simultaneously investigate pairing correlations and cluster formation (Nat10, Typ10, ZhangChen17) in a self-consistent framework.
