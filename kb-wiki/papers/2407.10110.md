# Strangeness Production in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=3$ GeV Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> We report multi-differential measurements of strange hadron production ranging from mid- to target-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at a center-of-momentum energy per nucleon pair of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=3$ GeV with the STAR experiment at RHIC. $K^0_S$ meson and $Λ$ hyperon yields are measured via their weak decay channels. Collision centrality and rapidity dependences of the transverse momentum spectra and particle ratios are presented. Particle mass and centrality dependence of the average transverse momenta of $Λ$ and $K^0_S$ are compared with other strange particles, providing evidence of the development of hadronic rescattering in such collisions. The 4$π$ yields of each of these strange hadrons show a consistent centrality dependence. Discussions on radial flow, the strange hadron production mechanism, and properties of the medium created in such collisions are presented together with results from hadronic transport and thermal model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that hadronic rescattering, rather than quark-gluon plasma formation, dominates the dynamics of strange hadron production in Au+Au collisions at the lowest RHIC beam energy of √s_NN = 3 GeV, with the caveat that the observed mass ordering of mean transverse momenta and centrality dependence are consistent with collective flow from a hadronic transport model rather than requiring a deconfined phase.

## Method

The STAR experiment used the fixed-target (FXT) setup with a 3.85 GeV/nucleon gold beam incident on a 0.25 mm gold target, collecting approximately 2.6×10^8 minimum bias events. Λ and K^0_S hadrons were reconstructed via their weak decay channels (Λ → p + π^- and K^0_S → π^+ + π^-) using the Time Projection Chamber for tracking and particle identification, with the KFParticle package based on Kalman Filter for decay topology reconstruction. The raw yields were corrected for acceptance, efficiency, and Λ feed-down from Ξ^- and Ξ^0 decays using Monte Carlo simulations with GEANT3, and the p_T spectra were fitted with the blast-wave model to extract integrated yields and mean transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- Λ and K^0_S yields measured at √s_NN = 3 GeV in Au+Au collisions with centrality intervals from 0-10% to 60-80%
- The power-law scaling parameter α_S for S=1 hadrons (Λ, K^0_S, K^-) from a simultaneous fit: α_S = 1.348 ± 0.028 with χ^2/NDF = 7/11
- Effective temperature of protons in 0-10% collisions at mid-rapidity: 0.1629 ± 0.0011 GeV, larger than that of Λs: 0.1469 ± 0.0005 GeV
- Systematic uncertainties for Λ dN/dy measurements in 0-10% collisions: total 10.8-15.3% across rapidity bins
- Systematic uncertainties for K^0_S dN/dy measurements in 0-10% collisions: total 10.2-11.6% across rapidity bins
- Λ feed-down contributions from Ξ decays: less than 4% in 0-10% collisions and less than 1% in 60-80% collisions
- UrQMD overpredicts K^0_S mean transverse momentum by approximately 20%
- UrQMD calculations for K^0_S rapidity distributions require scaling by factor 0.6 to match data

## Context

This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program to the lowest RHIC energy of √s_NN = 3 GeV, complementing previous measurements of φ and Ξ^- production at the same energy (STAR 2021). The results are compared with the hadronic transport model UrQMD (Bleicher 1999) and the thermal model THERMUS (Wheaton 2009), showing that canonical ensemble calculations with strangeness correlation radius r_c = 2.9-3.9 fm describe the particle ratios better than grand canonical ensemble. The energy dependence of the power-law scaling parameter α_S is compared with HADES results at √s_NN = 2.4 GeV and STAR results at higher BES energies (7.7-39 GeV), showing a decreasing trend with collision energy that saturates above 10 GeV. The measurements enable further studies of the nuclear equation of state and hypernuclei production mechanisms through the strangeness population factor S_3.
