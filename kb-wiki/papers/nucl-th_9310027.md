# Multinucleon Mechanisms in ($γ$,N) and ($γ$,NN) Reactions

> The similarities in the experimental indications for multinucleon mechanisms in $(γ,p)$ and $(e,e'p)$ processes are pointed out. For both types of reactions, the substantial role of two-nucleon emission processes for transitions to high excitation energies in the residual nucleus is stressed. A microscopic model for the calculation of the two-body knockout contributions to the inclusive $(γ,N)$ reaction is presented. It is based on an unfactorized formalism for the calculation of electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission cross sections. The model is shown to yield a reasonable description of the overall behaviour of the $^{12}$C$(γ,p)$ and $^{12}$C$(γ,n)$ data at high excitation energies in the residual nucleus. In the calculations effects from non-resonant and resonant pion exchange currents are included. Photoabsorption on these currents are predicted to produce the major contributions to the exclusive $^{16}$O$(γ,n_0)^{15}$O process at photonenergies above the pion threshold. Double differential cross sections for photon induced $pp$ and $pn$ emission from $^{16}$O are calculated and compared with the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multinucleon mechanisms, particularly two-nucleon emission following photoabsorption on pion-exchange and Delta-resonance currents, dominate inclusive (γ,N) reactions at high excitation energies in the residual nucleus, and that these mechanisms also largely explain exclusive (γ,n₀) cross sections above the pion threshold, though the model systematically underestimates measured cross sections, especially at forward angles and high missing energies.

## Method

The authors developed a non-relativistic microscopic model for electromagnetically induced two-nucleon emission, using an unfactorized formalism with partial-wave expansions for the outgoing nucleons and a shell-model treatment of final-state interactions. They calculated (γ,NN) cross sections for ¹²C and ¹⁶O targets, including one-pion exchange currents (seagull and pion-in-flight terms) and resonant Δ₃₃ currents, then integrated over one nucleon to obtain inclusive (γ,N) cross sections above the two-particle emission threshold. One-nucleon knockout contributions were computed using a continuum RPA technique with hole spectral functions folded to account for spreading of deep-lying hole strength.

## Key numbers

- For exclusive ¹⁶O(γ,n₀)¹⁵O at Eγ = 250 MeV, the Δ₃₃ contribution produces the major part of the cross section, while at Eγ = 150 and 200 MeV a fair description is obtained with one-body and nonresonant MEC contributions alone.
- The Δ₃₃ propagator uses MΔ = 1232 MeV and an energy-dependent decay width ΓΔ(Eγ) approximated as ΓΔ(Eγ) ≈ (8f²πNN)/(12π) × ((Eγ² − mπ²)^(3/2)/mπ²) × ((MΔ − MN)/Eγ).
- The one-pion exchange potential uses f²πNN/(4π) = 0.079 and a monopole cutoff Λπ = 1.2 GeV.
- For ¹²C(γ,n) at Eγ = 75 MeV, the 1s-shell knockout strength peaks at missing energy ≈ 39 MeV (Sn = 18.7 MeV, Spn = 27.4 MeV), and is still substantial compared to pn strength; at Eγ ≥ 100 MeV, one-body knockout from deep-lying holes becomes negligible.
- For ¹²C(γ,p) at Eγ = 123 and 150 MeV, the (γ,pn) calculations give a reasonable description of photoproton spectra, particularly at backward angles, but systematically underestimate the data; renormalizing the (γ,pn) contribution by a factor of two improves agreement.
- For ¹²C(γ,p) at Eγ = 150 MeV, plane-wave calculations show that distortion effects are not dramatic except just above threshold (Tp = 100 MeV), where one outgoing nucleon is slow and strongly interacts.
- The two-hole spectral functions for ¹⁶O use quasiparticle energies of 6 MeV for 1p₃/₂ and 30 MeV for 1s₁/₂ hole states, with a parametrized imaginary optical potential W(E) = 9E⁴/(E⁴ + (13.27)⁴) (MeV).

## Context

This work extends the quasideuteron model of Levinger (1951) by providing a microscopic, unfactorized calculation of two-nucleon knockout contributions to photonuclear reactions, including both nonresonant pion-exchange currents and resonant Δ₃₃ excitation. It builds on experimental evidence from Glasgow-Edinburgh-Mainz tagged-photon measurements (McG 1986) and MIT-BATES exclusive (γ,n₀) data (Bei 1989), and parallels similar findings in (e,e'p) reactions (Ulm 1987, Wei 1990, Lou 1986, Bag 1989). The model enables quantitative comparison of (γ,N) and (γ,NN) cross sections with data, and points to the need for including three-nucleon emission mechanisms to fully account for measured strength at forward angles and high missing energies.
