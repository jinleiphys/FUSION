# The Explicit and Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking in an Effective Quark-quark Interaction Model

> A method for obtaining the small current quark mass effect on the dressed quark propagator from an effective quark-quark interaction model is developed. Within this approach both explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking has been analyzed. a comparision with previous results is given.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to compute the small current quark mass effect on the dressed quark propagator within the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) at the mean-field level, allowing separate analysis of explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; the approach is valid only for low values of the current quark mass \(m\).

## Method

The authors start from the Euclidean action of the GCM with a small finite current quark mass \(m\) and apply a standard bosonization procedure to rewrite the partition function in terms of bilocal fields. At the mean-field level, they derive the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the dressed quark propagator in the chiral limit and the inhomogeneous ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the scalar vertex \(\Gamma\), which encodes the first-order dependence of the inverse propagator on \(m\). They then solve these coupled integral equations analytically using a simple infrared-dominant model for the dressed gluon propagator (Munczek-Nemirovsky model) to obtain explicit forms for the scalar and vector self-energy functions in both the "Nambu-Goldstone" and "Wigner" phases.

## Key numbers

* Chiral quark condensate in the chiral limit: \(\langle\tilde{0}|:\bar{q}q:|\tilde{0}\rangle_{m=0} = -\eta^3/(80\pi^2)\), where \(\eta\) is the scale parameter of the Munczek-Nemirovsky model gluon propagator.
* The model gluon propagator parameter is set to \(\eta^2 = 1.14~\text{GeV}^2\) for the numerical calculations shown in Figures 1 and 2.
* In the "Nambu-Goldstone" phase for \(p^2 < \eta^2/4\), the scalar vertex functions are \(D(p^2) = \frac{1}{2}(\eta^2+8p^2)/(\eta^2-4p^2)\) and \(C(p^2) = -2(\eta^2-4p^2)^{1/2}/(\eta^2-4p^2)\).
* In the "Wigner" phase, \(C'(p^2) \equiv 0\) and \(D'(p^2) = [p^2+\eta^2+p^2(1+2\eta^2/p^2)^{1/2}]/[p^2-\eta^2+p^2(1+2\eta^2/p^2)^{1/2}]\).

## Context

This work extends the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) framework developed by Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Tandy (1997) to include finite current quark mass effects beyond the chiral limit, where previous studies often set the current quark mass to zero. The approach is analogous to earlier treatments of nonperturbative vector and axial vector vertices by Frank (1995) and Meissner and Kisslinger (1999), and to studies of chemical potential dependence of the dressed quark propagator by Zong et al. (2002). The results enable calculation of vacuum condensates and susceptibilities at the mean-field level with explicit chiral symmetry breaking, though the authors note that the infrared-dominant gluon propagator model used does not represent the correct large-momentum behavior of QCD, and a more sophisticated ansatz is needed for future work.
