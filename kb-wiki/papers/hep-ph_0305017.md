# Locality of quark-hadron duality and deviations from quark counting rules above resonance region

> We show how deviations from the dimensional scaling laws for exclusive processes may be related to a breakdown in the locality of quark-hadron duality. The essential principles are illustrated in a pedagogic model of a composite system with two spinless charged constituents, for which a dual picture for the low-energy resonance phenomena and high-energy scaling behavior can be established. We introduce the concept of "restricted locality" of quark-hadron duality and show how this results in deviations from the pQCD quark counting rules above the resonance region. In particular it can be a possible source for oscillations about the smooth quark counting rule, as seen e.g. in the 90-degree differential cross sections for $γp\to π^+ n$.

## Key claim

Deviations from the dimensional scaling laws (quark counting rules) for exclusive processes may arise from a breakdown in the "restricted locality" of quark-hadron duality, where non-degenerate higher-mass resonances above the resonance region cause oscillations around the smooth scaling behavior predicted by perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors develop a pedagogic model of a composite system with two spinless charged constituents, using harmonic oscillator wave functions to sum over intermediate resonance states in Compton scattering. They then generalize to physical exclusive processes like meson photoproduction using effective Lagrangians for constituent-quark-meson and quark-photon couplings, introducing mass-degeneracy breaking (L-dependence) into the n=3 and n=4 terms of the transition amplitude to estimate deviations at 90 degrees in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- The 90-degree differential cross sections for γp → π⁺ n at W ∼ 3 GeV exhibit oscillations around the scaling curves predicted by the counting rules.
- For s ≲ 20 GeV² at 90 degrees, the empirical form factor is R(t) ≈ (0.22 − t)/(0.025 − t).
- The model applies for 2 ≲ √s ≲ 3.5 GeV, above the third resonance region.
- The effective Lagrangian calculations agree with experimental data up to E_γ ≈ 500 MeV for the low-energy regime.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron duality ideas of Bloom and Gilman (1970) and later developments by Melnitchouk, Isgur, Jaffe, and others, applying them to exclusive processes and the dimensional scaling laws of Brodsky and Farrar (1975) and Matveev et al. (1975). It offers an alternative to explanations for counting-rule deviations such as the opening of new flavor channels (Brodsky and de Teramond, 1988) or interference between pQCD and long-distance components (Brodsky, Carlson, and Lipkin). The model predicts that the oscillation pattern from resonance degeneracy breaking should have no simple periodicity and a non-trivial Q² dependence, which can be tested by experiments at Jefferson Lab measuring large-angle cross sections for ω, ρ⁰, and φ photoproduction.
