# Constraints on Axial Two-Body Currents from Solar Neutrino Data

> We briefly review recent calculations of neutrino deuteron cross sections within the effective field theory and traditional potential model approaches. We summarize recent efforts to determine the counter term describing axial two-body currents, L_{1A}, in the effective field theory approach. We determine the counter term directly from the solar neutrino data and find several, slightly different, ranges of L_{1A} under different sets of assumptions. Our most conservative fit value with the largest uncertainty is L_{1A} = 4.5 +18/-12 fm^3. We show that the contribution of the uncertainty of L_{1A} to the analysis and interpretation of the solar neutrino data measured at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory is significantly less than the uncertainty coming from the lack of having a better knowledge of θ_{13}.

## Key claim

The counter term describing axial two-body currents, \(L_{1A}\), can be determined directly from solar neutrino data, and its uncertainty contributes significantly less to the analysis of Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) data than the uncertainty from the lack of better knowledge of the mixing angle \(\theta_{13}\). The most conservative fit value with the largest uncertainty is \(L_{1A} = 4.5^{+18}_{-12} \, \mathrm{fm}^3\).

## Method

The authors used neutrino cross sections from effective field theory calculations (Butler 1999, Butler 2000) with radiative corrections from Kurylov (2001). They performed a covariance analysis fitting 34 data points from the SNO day-night spectrum, using the MSW survival probabilities with the Standard Solar Model of Bahcall (2000) for neutrino spectra and solar electron density, to extract constraints on \(L_{1A}\) while varying \(\theta_{12}\), \(\delta m_{12}^2\), and the \({}^{8}\mathrm{B}\) flux multiplier \(f_B\).

## Key numbers

*   Most conservative fit value: \(L_{1A} = 4.5^{+18}_{-12} \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) from solar neutrino data.
*   With the \({}^{8}\mathrm{B}\) flux fixed to reproduce the SNO total count rate, \(L_{1A}\) is constrained between \(-2 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(13 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) at \(1\sigma\).
*   With all parameters except \(L_{1A}\) fixed to their best-fit values from a global fit (including KamLAND), \(L_{1A}\) is constrained between \(2 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(8 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) at \(1\sigma\).
*   Dimensional analysis estimate: \(\vert L_{1A} \vert \sim 6 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) (renormalization scale set to muon mass).
*   Reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup constraint: \(L_{1A} = 3.6 \pm 5.5 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) (Butler 2002).
*   Helioseismology limit (fifth order): \(L_{1A} = 7.0 \pm 5.9 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) (Brown 2002), which gives \(L_{1A} = 4.8 \pm 6.7 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\) in third order.
*   Tritium \(\beta\)-decay adjustment (Schiavilla 1998) gives \(L_{1A} = 4.2 \pm 2.4 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   Chen, Heeger, and Robertson (CHR) result: \(L_{1A} = 4.0 \pm 6.3 \, \mathrm{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine the axial two-body current counter term \(L_{1A}\) in effective field theory, which had been constrained by reactor antineutrino data (Butler 2002), helioseismology (Brown 2002), and tritium \(\beta\)-decay (Schiavilla 1998). The authors directly use SNO and SuperKamiokande solar neutrino data to provide a new determination, finding a best-fit value slightly larger than that of Chen, Heeger, and Robertson (2002). The result enables more precise interpretation of SNO data by showing that the \(L_{1A}\) uncertainty is subdominant to the \(\theta_{13}\) uncertainty, and it motivates further experimental and theoretical work to reduce the dominant uncertainties in neutrino oscillation analyses.
