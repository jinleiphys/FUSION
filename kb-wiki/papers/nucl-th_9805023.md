# Omega meson as a chronometer and thermometer in hot-dense hadronic matter

> Changes in the properties of the vector mesons in hot and dense hadronic matter, as produced in heavy ion collisions, lead to the intriguing possibility of the opening of the decay channel $ω\ra ρπ$, for the omega meson, which is impossible in free space. This along with the channel $ωπ\ra ππ$ would result in a decrease in its effective life-time enabling it to decay within the hot zone and act as a chronometer in contradiction to the commonly held opinion and would have implications vis a vis determination of the size of the region through pion interferometry. A new peak and a radically altered shape of the low invariant mass dilepton spectra appears due to different shift in the masses of $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. The Walecka model is used for the underlying calculation for the sake of illustration.

# Omega meson as a chronometer and thermometer in hot-dense hadronic matter

## Key claim

Under specific conditions of temperature and baryon density, the omega meson mass can exceed the sum of the rho meson and pion masses, opening the decay channel ω → ρπ which is kinematically forbidden in free space; this channel, together with ωπ → ππ, reduces the omega lifetime to values comparable to the rho meson, enabling the omega to decay within the hot zone and serve as a chronometer and thermometer of the hadronic fireball, contrary to the commonly held view that the omega is too long-lived for this purpose.

## Method

The authors employ the Walecka model (Relativistic Hartree Approximation with Mean Field Theory for internal lines) to compute the in-medium masses of the rho and omega mesons as functions of temperature and baryon density, using coupling constants that reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties. The in-medium decay width for ω → ρπ is calculated using the Gell-Mann-Sharp-Wagner Lagrangian with finite-temperature cutting rules including Bose-Einstein factors, while the three-pion decay width is estimated from a phenomenological effective Lagrangian. The dilepton emission rate from vector meson decays is evaluated using a generalized Breit-Wigner formula at nonzero temperature and density, and the yield from an expanding hadronic system is obtained by convoluting the static emission rate with a Bjorken-like boost-invariant longitudinal expansion model.

## Key numbers

- Free omega meson mass: 782 MeV; free rho meson mass: 770 MeV; free omega full width: 8.4 MeV; free rho full width: 151 MeV
- Free omega lifetime: ~23 fm/c; free rho lifetime: ~1.3 fm/c
- The decay ω → ρπ becomes possible at zero baryon density for temperature T ≥ 185 MeV
- The decay ω → ρπ becomes possible at zero temperature for baryon density n_B/n_B^0 ≥ 0.3 (where n_B^0 is normal nuclear matter density)
- In-medium omega lifetime reduces to ~2.3 fm/c, comparable to the in-medium rho lifetime of ~2.1 fm/c under the same conditions
- A kink in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum appears at M = 680 MeV for the expanding system with initial baryon density n_B = n_B^0

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of temperature-dependent hadronic masses by Hatsuda, Brown, and Pisarski (various years) using different models, and specifically contrasts with the Walecka model approach where no universal scaling law is observed. The authors note that Pisarski (various years) argued for omega width increase due to chiral symmetry restoration, while Wolf (various years) proposed broadening from omega motion relative to the medium; the present mechanism works even for omega at rest. The results have direct implications for dilepton experiments at CERES and the upcoming HADES spectrometer at GSI, and for Hanbury-Brown Twiss interferometry where the omega's long lifetime previously complicated extraction of source radii.
