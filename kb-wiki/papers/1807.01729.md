# On the effect of high order empirical parameters on the nuclear equation of state

> A quantitative knowledge of the nuclear Equation of State (EoS) requires an accurate estimation of the uncertainties on the EoS parameters and their mutual correlations. Such correlations are empirically observed in a large set of EoS models by different authors, but they are not always fully understood. We show that some of these correlations can be interpreted from basic physical constraints imposed on a simple Taylor expansion of the binding energy around saturation density. In particular, we investigate the correlations among the following empirical parameters: the symmetry energy $E_{sym}$, the slope and curvature of the symmetry energy $L_{sym}$ and $K_{sym}$, and the curvature and skewness of the binding energy in symmetric matter $K_{sat}$ and $Q_{sat}$. The uncertainties on these correlations is estimated through analytical modelling as well as a meta-modelling analysis of the EoS subject to physical constraints. We show that a huge dispersion of the correlations among low order empirical parameters is induced by the unknown higher order empirical parameters, such as $K_{sym}$ (second order) and $Q_{sat}$ and $Q_{sym}$ (third order). We also propose an explanation of the reason why $Q_{sat}$ is weakly constrained by present experimental data. We conclude that selective observables on high order parameters, such as $K_{sym}$ and $Q_{sat}$, should be better determined before the present uncertainties on the EoS can be further reduced.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observed correlations among low-order empirical parameters of the nuclear equation of state (EoS), such as the well-known correlation between symmetry energy \(E_{sym}\) and its slope \(L_{sym}\), are significantly blurred by the unknown values of higher-order empirical parameters (specifically \(K_{sym}\), \(Q_{sat}\), and \(Q_{sym}\)), and that reducing uncertainties on these higher-order parameters is necessary before the present uncertainties on the EoS can be further reduced.

## Method

The authors employ a simple Taylor expansion of the nuclear binding energy per particle around saturation density \(n_{sat}\) up to fourth order in both the isoscalar and isovector sectors, deriving analytical expressions for correlations among empirical parameters. They then compare these analytical predictions against a set of approximately 50 realistic nuclear functionals (including Skyrme, Relativistic Mean Field, Relativistic Hartree-Fock, and chiral EFT interactions) and against a meta-modeling analysis that generates hundreds of thousands of functionals filtered by physical constraints such as stability, causality, and ab-initio predictions from chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

* The correlation between \(E_{sym}\) and \(L_{sym}\) is derived from the condition \(e_{sym}(n_a = 0.1\ \text{fm}^{-3}) = 24.1 \pm 0.8\ \text{MeV}\) (from Trippa 2008), yielding \(\beta_{EL} = 9\) and \(\alpha_{EL,2} = -221.5 \pm 17.5\ \text{MeV}\) for the 50-model set with \(28 < E_{sym} < 36\ \text{MeV}\).
* The correlation coefficient between \(L_{sym}\) and \(E_{sym}\) is approximately 0.80 for the full 50-model set, dropping to approximately 0.55 when restricting to models with \(28 < E_{sym} < 36\ \text{MeV}\).
* The correlation between \(K_{sym}\) and \(3E_{sym} - L_{sym}\) is derived from the condition \(e_{NM}(n=0) = 0\ \text{MeV}\), giving \(\beta_{K_{sym}} = -6\) and \(\alpha_{K_{sym},2} = 8 \pm 83\ \text{MeV}\) from the 50-model set.
* The correlation coefficient between \(K_{sym}\) and \(3E_{sym} - L_{sym}\) is \(-0.91\) for the full 50-model set, decreasing to \(-0.48\) when restricting to \(\chi\)EFT-inspired ranges (\(28 \leq E_{sym} \leq 36\ \text{MeV}\), \(40 \leq L_{sym} \leq 60\ \text{MeV}\)).
* The correlation between \(K_{sat}\) and \(Q_{sat}\) is derived from the GMR crossing condition \(K_{v,c} = 37 \pm 8\ \text{MeV}\) at \(n_c = (0.71 \pm 0.01) n_{sat}\), giving \(\beta_{KQ} = 29 \pm 4\) and \(\alpha_{KQ,3} = -6300 \pm 1200\ \text{MeV}\).
* The correlation coefficient between \(K_{sat}\) and \(Q_{sat}\) is 0.52 for the full 50-model set, dropping to 0.23 when restricting to models with \(210 < K_{sat} < 250\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of empirical EoS parameters by Margueron et al. (2018a, 2018b) and provides a physical interpretation for correlations previously observed empirically, such as the \(K_{sym}\) vs. \(3E_{sym}-L_{sym}\) correlation reported by Mondal et al. (2017). The paper also explains why the \(K_{sat}\)-\(Q_{sat}\) correlation is weak, building on the GMR analysis of Khan et al. (2012, 2013) and Blaizot (1980). The results enable a more systematic approach to reducing EoS uncertainties by identifying which higher-order parameters (\(K_{sym}\), \(Q_{sat}\)) most urgently need better experimental constraints.
