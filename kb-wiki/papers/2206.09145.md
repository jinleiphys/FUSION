# Analytical calculations of the Quantum Tsallis thermodynamic variables

> In this article, we provide an account of analytical results related to the Tsallis thermodynamics that have been the subject matter of a lot of studies in the field of high-energy collisions. After reviewing the results for the classical case in the massless limit and for arbitrarily massive classical particles, we compute the quantum thermodynamic variables. For the first time, the analytical formula for the pressure of a Tsallis-like gas of massive bosons has been obtained. Hence, this article serves both as a brief review of the knowledge gathered in this area, and as an original research that forwards the existing scholarship. The results of the present paper will be important in a plethora of studies in the field of high-energy collisions including the propagation of non-linear waves generated by the traversal of high-energy particles inside the quark-gluon plasma medium showing the features of non-extensivity.

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide, for the first time, an analytical formula for the pressure of a Tsallis-like gas of massive bosons, extending previous analytical results that were limited to classical particles or massless quantum particles. The results are expressed in terms of infinite series that converge rapidly for massive particles, with the convergence condition splitting into two regimes depending on whether the Tsallis parameter q is greater than or less than 1+T/m.

## Method

The authors express the Tsallis quantum single-particle distribution as an infinite sum of Tsallis classical (Maxwell-Boltzmann) distributions, then apply a Mellin-Barnes contour integral representation to the power-law function in the integrand. After performing the momentum integration analytically, they evaluate the contour integral by wrapping clockwise and summing residues, which yields closed-form expressions involving hypergeometric functions for two separate regions: q > 1+T/m (upper region) and q < 1+T/m (lower region). The infinite sum over the classical distribution index s is truncated at a finite s_0 determined by the desired numerical accuracy.

## Key numbers

* For massless bosons with q=1.2, T=0.08 GeV, m=10^{-7} GeV, the pressure from the analytical formula (s_0=20) agrees with numerical integration to eleven significant digits at 2.20098×10^{-5} GeV^4.
* For positively-charged pions (mass 0.140 GeV) at LHC parameters (q=1.154, T=0.0682 GeV), the pressure is 5.4318×10^{-6} GeV^4 with s_0=5.
* For protons (mass 0.938 GeV) at LHC parameters (q=1.107, T=0.073 GeV), the pressure is 3.5597×10^{-7} GeV^4 with s_0=2.
* For positively-charged pions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au-Au, q=1.090, T=0.117 GeV), the pressure is 3.0089×10^{-5} GeV^4.
* For all charged particles at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2760 GeV Pb-Pb, q=1.135, T=0.096 GeV), the pressure is 6.5733×10^{-5} GeV^4.
* The first pole in the thermodynamic variables appears at q=4/3, setting an upper bound q<4/3 for finite values.

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach to Tsallis thermodynamics developed by Cleymans and collaborators (2012) and the contour integral method of Bhattacharyya, Cleymans, and Parvan (2020) from the classical domain to quantum statistics. Previous analytical results for massive particles were limited to Taylor series expansions truncated at O(q-1)^2 (Cleymans et al., 2015) or expressed in terms of q-modified Bessel functions with poorly known properties (Lavagno, 2002). The results enable faster computation of equations of state for quark-gluon plasma studies and can be applied to the propagation of non-linear waves in non-extensive media.
