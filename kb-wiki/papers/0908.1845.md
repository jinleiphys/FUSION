# A Separable Pairing Force for Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation

> We have introduced a separable pairing force, which was adjusted to reproduce the pairing properties of the Gogny force in nuclear matter. This separable pairing force is able to describe in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations the pairing properties in the ground state of finite nuclei on almost the same footing as the original Gogny interaction. In this work we investigate excited states using the Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) with the same separable pairing force. For consistency the Goldstone modes and the convergence with various cutoff parameters in this version of RQRPA are studied. The first excited $2^+$ states for the chain of Sn-isotopes with Z=50 and the chain of isotones with N=82 isotones are calculated in RQRPA together with the $3^-$ states of Sn-isotopes. Comparing with experimental data and with the results of original Gogny force we find that this simple separable pairing interaction is very successful in depicting the pairing properties of vibrational excitations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple separable pairing force, adjusted to reproduce the pairing properties of the Gogny force in nuclear matter, can describe the pairing properties of vibrational excitations in finite nuclei on almost the same footing as the original Gogny force, but with the caveat that the separable force yields slightly larger pairing gaps and therefore slightly stronger pairing fields in both ground and excited states.

## Method

The authors implemented a separable pairing force with a Gaussian ansatz in momentum space, with parameters G = 728 MeV·fm³ and a = 0.644 fm fitted to the density dependence of the pairing gap from the Gogny D1S force in symmetric nuclear matter. They used the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model for ground states and the Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) for excited states, with the NL3 effective interaction in the particle-hole channel. The two-body matrix elements in the particle-particle channel were evaluated using Talmi-Moshinsky brackets to transform from laboratory to center-of-mass coordinates, yielding a sum over separable terms that converges with N₀ = 5 to 8 terms.

## Key numbers

* Pairing force parameters: G = 728 MeV·fm³ and a = 0.644 fm, fitted to the Gogny D1S force in nuclear matter.
* For the nucleus ¹²⁰Sn, the Nambu-Goldstone 0⁺ mode converges to ≤ 0.1 MeV with cutoff parameter E_Cp = 180 MeV and E_Ca = 1800 MeV.
* The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) peak energy in ¹²⁰Sn saturates for E_Ca ≥ 1300 MeV.
* The dimension of the RQRPA matrix is on the order of 3500 two-quasiparticle pairs for the chosen cutoff parameters.
* For nuclei around β-stability (¹²⁰Sn), N₀ = 5 separable terms is sufficient to bring the spurious 1⁻ state to zero excitation energy; N₀ = 8 is needed for ground state properties.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous introduction of a separable pairing force for RHB ground state calculations (Tian, Ma, and Ring, 2009) to the description of excited states via RQRPA. It builds on earlier relativistic QRPA work by Ring et al. (2001) and Paar et al. (2003) that used the full Gogny force D1S in the pairing channel. The separable force enables applications where the full Gogny force is computationally prohibitive, such as deformed nuclei, particle-vibrational coupling, generator coordinate methods, and projection techniques, which previously had to rely on simpler monopole or zero-range pairing forces.
