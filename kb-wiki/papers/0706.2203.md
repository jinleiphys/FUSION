# Entropy production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions and the correlation of shear viscosity and thermalization time

> We study entropy production in the early stage of high-energy heavy-ion collisions due to shear viscosity. We employ the second-order theory of Israel-Stewart with two different stress relaxation times, as appropriate for strong coupling or for a Boltzmann gas, respectively, and compare the hydrodynamic evolution. Based on present knowledge of initial particle production, we argue that entropy production is tightly constrained. We derive new limits on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$, independent from elliptic flow effects, and determine the corresponding Reynolds number. Furthermore, we show that for a given entropy production bound, that the initial time $τ_0$ for hydrodynamics is correlated to the viscosity. The conjectured lower bound for $η/s$ provides a lower limit for $τ_0$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that entropy production in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions is tightly constrained by initial particle production, which provides an independent upper bound on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s that does not rely on elliptic flow measurements; this bound correlates η/s with the initial time τ0 for hydrodynamics, and the conjectured lower bound η/s ≥ 1/(4π) provides a lower limit for τ0.

## Method

The authors employ the second-order Israel-Stewart theory of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for 1+1D boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, using two different stress relaxation times appropriate for a Boltzmann gas and for strong coupling (AdS/CFT), respectively. They determine initial conditions from the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) k⊥-factorization approach incorporating perturbative gluon saturation, fixing the initial parton density from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. They then solve the coupled evolution equations for energy density and shear stress, computing entropy production from the initial time τ0 to τfin = 5 fm/c and requiring ΔS/Sini = 10% to extract the maximal allowed η/s for each τ0.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, the average saturation momentum Qs ≈ 1.4 GeV at midrapidity, giving τs ≈ 0.14 fm/c and an initial parton density ≈ 40 fm⁻³ at that time
- The conjectured lower bound for η/s is 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- For a Boltzmann gas with τ0 = 1 fm/c and the natural initial stress condition Φ0 = Φ0*, the maximal η/s consistent with 10% entropy production is approximately 0.2
- For τ0 = 0.5 fm/c, the 10% entropy production bound cannot be satisfied with η/s ≥ 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- The sound attenuation length bound gives τ0 > (4/(3T0)) × (η/s) ≡ Γs(τ0)
- The centrality dependence of dN/dη per participant grows by only about 50% from most peripheral Cu+Cu to most central Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entropy production in heavy-ion collisions due to viscous effects (EntroHIC, Muronga) by incorporating recent progress in understanding initial gluon production from the Color Glass Condensate framework. The authors derive an upper bound on η/s independent from elliptic flow constraints (Teaney, Lacey (2006), DDGO, RR07) and from transverse momentum correlations (GavinAziz). The results provide guidance for initial conditions in large-scale numerical studies of relativistic second-order dissipative fluid dynamics, and the correlation between η/s and τ0 enables testing of the conjectured lower bound from Kovtun (2005) against RHIC multiplicity data.
