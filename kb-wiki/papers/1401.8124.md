# Centrality and rapidity dependence of inclusive pion and prompt photon production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC with EPS09s nPDFs

> The centrality dependencies of the inclusive neutral pion and prompt photon nuclear modification factors for p+Pb collisions at the LHC are studied using a spatially dependent set of nuclear PDFs, EPS09s. The calculations are performed at mid- and forward rapidities searching for an observable which would optimally probe the spatial dependence of the nuclear PDFs. In addition, we discuss to which $x$ values of the nucleus the different observables are sensitive.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of nuclear modification factors for pions and prompt photons in p+Pb collisions is predicted to be at most on the order of 10%, even at forward rapidities, making it a challenging observable to measure despite the expectation of stronger nuclear effects in central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the EPS09s spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), which are constructed by fitting a power series of the nuclear thickness function to reproduce the minimum-bias EPS09 nPDFs upon spatial averaging. Inclusive neutral pion and prompt photon yields were computed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in pQCD using the INCNLO code, with centrality classes defined via impact parameter intervals from the optical Glauber model for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV and 8.8 TeV. The nuclear modification factor R_pPb was then calculated for four centrality classes (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-80%) at mid-rapidity (y=0) and forward rapidity (y=4 for pions, y=4.5 for photons).

## Key numbers

- For p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV, the impact parameter intervals for centrality classes are: 0-20% (b = 0.0-3.471 fm, <N_bin> = 14.24), 20-40% (3.471-4.908 fm, <N_bin> = 11.41), 40-60% (4.908-6.012 fm, <N_bin> = 7.663), 60-80% (6.012-6.986 fm, <N_bin> = 3.680).
- For p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV, the impact parameter intervals are: 0-20% (b = 0.0-3.494 fm, <N_bin> = 15.44), 20-40% (3.494-4.941 fm, <N_bin> = 12.31), 40-60% (4.941-6.052 fm, <N_bin> = 8.165), 60-80% (6.052-7.029 fm, <N_bin> = 3.810).
- The maximal suppression due to nPDFs for prompt photons at forward rapidity (y=4.5) and sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV is about 20%, except at very small p_T values.
- The centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor is at most on the order of 10%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier minimum-bias nPDF analyses of Eskola (2009) (EPS09) by introducing a spatial dependence (EPS09s) developed in Helenius (2012). It provides predictions for p+Pb collisions at the LHC that can be compared to measurements from the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations. The results show that inclusive hadron and photon production have limited sensitivity to the spatial dependence of nPDFs due to smearing from fragmentation functions, suggesting that future studies should consider isolated prompt photons or heavy quark production as proposed by Stavreva (2010) to better constrain the impact-parameter dependence of nuclear PDFs.
