# Formation of superconducting pair correlations in spherical even-even nuclei

> The appearance of like nucleon pair correlations in the ground state of spherical even-even nuclei is considered within a special Bogoliubov transformation. It is confirmed that in closed subshell nuclei superconducting pair correlations start to form if the coupling constant $G$ exceeds a certain threshold value. Rough upper and lower estimates are obtained for the threshold value. It is shown, that superconducting correlations exist in open subshell nuclei at any positive $G$. In this case, nucleon pairs are distributed over all subshells participating in the pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that in closed subshell nuclei, superconducting pair correlations appear only when the coupling constant \(G\) exceeds a threshold value, while in open subshell nuclei such correlations exist at any positive \(G\); however, the threshold condition derived from the gap equation is a useful hint rather than a rigorous proof, because the chemical potential can approach a single-particle level closely enough to increase the sum in the inequality as \(G\) decreases.

## Method

The authors use a special Bogoliubov transformation with real coefficients to introduce quasiparticle operators and approximate the ground state of spherical even-even nuclei by the quasiparticle vacuum. They minimize the expectation value of the Hamiltonian with a particle-number constraint, expressing normal and abnormal densities in terms of trigonometric functions, and solve the resulting extremum equations analytically for one- and two-subshell systems to determine the conditions for non-zero abnormal density (superconducting correlations).

## Key numbers

- For a two-subshell system with equal degeneracies \(\degen_1 = \degen_2\) and particle number \(\npart = 2\degen_1\), the critical coupling constant is \(\GCRIT = (E_2 - E_1)/(2\degen_1 - 1)\); for \(G > \GCRIT\) the correlation function behaves as \(\corrf = G \degen_1 \sqrt{1 - (\GCRIT/G)^2}\).
- For a two-subshell system with \(\degen_1 \neq \degen_2\) and \(\npart = 2\degen_1\), the dimensionless critical coupling is \(\gcrit(\degen_1, \degen_2) = 1 / \left[ \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{\degen_1} + \sqrt{\degen_2})^2 - 1 \right]\).
- For a single open subshell with \(\npart = 2\degen_0 - 2P_0\), the correlation function is \(\corrf = (G/2) \sqrt{\npart (2\degen_0 - \npart)}\), linear in \(G\).
- For a two-subshell system with \(\npart < 2\degen_1\) (open lower subshell), the abnormal density \(t_2\) in the upper subshell is non-zero for any positive \(g\), with \(t_2 \approx (g/2) \degen_1 \sqrt{p_1(1-p_1)} / [1 + g(1 + p_1(\degen_1 - 1) - (\degen_1 + \degen_2)/2)]\).

## Context

This work extends the foundational analysis of pairing in nuclei by Bel (1959) and the textbook treatment by Varshalovich, Grinberg, and Shirokov (1971), who noted that a threshold coupling distinguishes finite nuclei from infinite superconducting systems. The paper provides explicit analytic threshold formulas for two-subshell systems and demonstrates that in open subshell nuclei superconducting correlations exist at arbitrarily small coupling, with pairs distributed across all participating subshells. These results enable more precise modeling of pairing in spherical nuclei and clarify the conditions under which the standard gap equation's threshold inequality is or is not reliable.
