# Near-Far Description of Elastic and Breakup Reactions of Halo Nuclei

> The angular distributions for elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei are analysed using a near-side/far-side decomposition within the framework of the dynamical eikonal approximation. This analysis is performed for 11Be impinging on Pb at 69AMeV. These distributions exhibit very similar features. In particular they are both near-side dominated, as expected from Coulomb-dominated reactions. The general shape of these distributions is sensitive mostly to the projectile-target interactions, but is also affected by the extension of the halo. This suggests that the link between elastic scattering and a possible loss of flux towards the breakup channel is not obvious.

# Near-Far Description of Elastic and Breakup Reactions of Halo Nuclei

## Key claim
The angular distributions for elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei exhibit very similar features when analysed via near-side/far-side decomposition within the dynamical eikonal approximation, suggesting that the projectile is scattered by the target in a similar way whether it remains bound or is broken up. However, the authors explicitly state that there is no obvious link between the drop observed in the elastic-scattering cross section and a possible transfer of probability flux towards the breakup channel, contrary to what had been postulated in earlier experimental work.

## Method
The authors performed a near-side/far-side decomposition of angular distributions computed within the Dynamical Eikonal Approximation (DEA) for the reaction of 11Be on Pb at 69A MeV. The DEA solves a three-body Schrödinger equation for a two-body projectile (core + valence neutron) impinging on a structureless target, using eikonal approximation without the usual adiabatic approximation, thus including dynamical effects. The Bessel functions in the cross-section expressions were decomposed into Hankel functions to separate the near-side (repulsive, positive deflection) and far-side (attractive, negative deflection) contributions.

## Key numbers
- The elastic-scattering cross section for 11Be on Pb at 69A MeV exhibits a Coulomb rainbow at about 2 degrees, followed by an exponential drop and oscillations at larger angles.
- The near and far sides of the elastic-scattering angular distribution cross at about 8 degrees, explaining the oscillatory pattern of the total cross section at larger angles.
- The total breakup cross section for the full Coulomb + nuclear potential is 1.70 b, for the point-sphere Coulomb potential is 2.10 b, and for the point-point Coulomb potential is 2.58 b (all at binding energy |E0| = 0.5 MeV).
- When the one-neutron separation energy is reduced to 0.05 MeV (expanding the halo), the total breakup cross section increases to 23.57 b; when increased to 5 MeV (shrinking the halo), it drops to 0.07 b.
- The breakup angular distribution at 10Be-n relative energy E = 0.5 MeV shows a Coulomb rainbow at about 2 degrees, identical to the elastic-scattering case.

## Context
This work extends the near-side/far-side decomposition technique, previously applied to elastic scattering (Fuller 1975; Hussein and McVoy 1984), to breakup reactions of halo nuclei. It directly addresses the experimental observation by Di Pietro (2010) that elastic scattering of 11Be on Zn near the Coulomb barrier shows a significant reduction at large angles compared to non-halo Be isotopes, which had been interpreted as flux loss to breakup. The authors show that at intermediate energy (69A MeV), this interpretation is not supported. The results are semi-quantitatively explained within the Recoil Excitation and Breakup (REB) model of Johnson (1997). The work enables future studies at lower energies using CDCC (Descouvemont 2010) and extensions to two-neutron halo (Borromean) systems.
