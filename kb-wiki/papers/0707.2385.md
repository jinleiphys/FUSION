# Ridge Structure associated with the Near-Side Jet in the (Delta phi)-(Delta eta) Correlation

> In the (Delta phi)-(Delta eta) correlation associated with a near-side jet observed by the STAR Collaboration in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC [Ref. 1-6], the ridge structure can be explained by the momentum kick model in which the ridge particles are identified as medium partons which suffer a collision with the jet and acquire a momentum kick along the jet direction. If this is indeed the correct mechanism, the ridge structure associated with the near-side jet may be used to probe the parton momentum distribution at the moment of the jet-parton collision, leading to the result that at that instant the parton temperature is slightly higher and the rapidity width substantially greater than corresponding quantities of their evolution product inclusive particles at the end point of the nucleus-nucleus collision.

## Key claim

The ridge structure observed in the near-side jet correlation can be explained by the momentum kick model, in which ridge particles are medium partons that suffer a collision with the jet and acquire a momentum kick along the jet direction. If this mechanism is correct, the ridge structure may be used to probe the parton momentum distribution at the moment of the jet-parton collision, revealing that at that instant the parton temperature is slightly higher and the rapidity width substantially greater than the corresponding quantities of their evolution product inclusive particles at the end point of the nucleus-nucleus collision.

## Method

The author proposes a momentum kick model where a near-side jet collides with medium partons near the surface, imparting a momentum kick q along the jet direction to each collided parton. The initial parton momentum distribution is parametrized as a Gaussian in rapidity with width sigma_y, a thermal transverse momentum distribution with temperature T, and a uniform azimuthal distribution. Using hadron-parton duality, the final momentum distribution of collided partons after the kick is compared with experimental ridge particle data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Momentum kick parameter q = 0.8 GeV gives the best qualitative description of the ridge data
- Initial parton rapidity width sigma_y = 5.5 reproduces the ridge structure, substantially larger than the inclusive rapidity width of sigma_y = 2.3
- Initial parton temperature T = 0.47 GeV, compared to the inclusive particle temperature T_incl = 0.40 GeV
- For q = 0.4 GeV, the full width at half maximum in Delta phi is about 2.4, much greater than the experimental full width at half maximum of about 1.2
- For sigma_y = 2.3, the ridge yield increases by a factor of about 1.7 when Delta eta increases from -1.7 to -0.7, whereas experimental data shows an increase of only a factor of about 1.2
- The theoretical ratio <R_Lambda/Ks0>_ridge = 0.71 within the kinematic domain of p_tjet,min = 2 GeV, p_tjet,max = 3 GeV, and identified ridge particles with p_tmin = 1.5 GeV < p_t < p_tjet

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model to explain the ridge phenomenon observed by the STAR Collaboration (Adams 2005, Adams 2006, Putschke 2007, Bielcikova 2007) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where a ridge structure appears in the Delta phi-Delta eta correlation associated with a near-side jet. The model contrasts with other theoretical approaches such as those by Hwa (2003), Chiu (2005), and Romatschke (2007), and provides a framework for using the ridge structure to probe the parton momentum distribution at the moment of jet-parton collision. The results enable future measurements of the ridge structure at greater pseudorapidity values to determine the rapidity width parameter more accurately and test whether the initial rapidity distribution may be non-Gaussian.
