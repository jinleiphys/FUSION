# Scaling of the $^{19}$B two-neutron halo properties close to unitarity

> We explore the description of the bound $^{19}$B isotope in terms of a $^{17}$B+n+n three-body system where the two-body subsystems $^{17}$B+n and neutron-neutron (nn) have virtual states close to the continuum. Dimensionless scaling functions for the root-mean-square (rms) radii are defined and studied for different parameters of the neutron-core potential and considering three different models for neutron-neutron interaction. The scaling functions for the radii are rooted on the universal behavior of three-body systems close to the Efimov limit and depend only on dimensionless quantities formed by the two-neutron separation energies and scattering lengths. Our results show in practice the model independence of these scaling functions close to unitarity. We provide an estimation of the different rms relative separation distances between the constituents, as well as of the proton and matter radii.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the structural properties of the two-neutron halo nucleus $^{19}$B, described as a $^{17}$B+n+n three-body system, follow universal scaling functions that are model-independent near the unitarity limit, provided the core size is substantially smaller than the halo size; however, the scaling functions depend on the specific neutron-neutron interaction model at the 10% level due to differences in the nn scattering lengths and effective ranges.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body problem for $^{17}$B+n+n using both the Gaussian Expansion Method and the Faddeev equation formalism in configuration space, employing a parameterized n-$^{17}$B potential with a hard-core radius R=3 fm and a Yukawa form, tuned to reproduce different n-$^{17}$B scattering lengths $a_s$. Three different neutron-neutron interaction models were used: Bonn-A (a_nn=-23.75 fm), AV18 (a_nn=-18.8 fm), and a charge-dependent version of MT13 (a_nn=-18.59 fm), and the dimensionless scaling functions for rms radii were computed as functions of the inverse scaled scattering length $1/(a_s \kappa_{nc})$.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ ranges from 0.087 MeV (for $a_s=-50$ fm with Bonn-A) to 0.176 MeV (for $a_s=-1000$ fm with Bonn-A)
- For $a_s=-50$ fm with Bonn-A: $\langle r^2_{nn}\rangle^{1/2}=16.15$ fm, $\langle r^2_{c-2n}\rangle^{1/2}=10.89$ fm, $\langle r^2_{nc}\rangle^{1/2}=13.56$ fm
- For $a_s=-1000$ fm with Bonn-A: $\langle r^2_{nn}\rangle^{1/2}=13.15$ fm, $\langle r^2_{c-2n}\rangle^{1/2}=9.18$ fm, $\langle r^2_{nc}\rangle^{1/2}=11.29$ fm
- The zero-range limit at unitarity gives $(\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle S_{2n})^{1/2}|_{zr}=0.8$ and $(\langle r_{nc}^2\rangle S_{2n})^{1/2}|_{zr}=0.7$
- From the experimental extraction of Cook et al. (2020): $\sqrt{\langle r^2_{c-2n}\rangle}=5.75\pm 0.11$ (stat) $\pm 0.21$ (sys) fm, giving $(\langle r^2_{c-2n}\rangle S_{2n})^{1/2}=0.632\pm 0.026$
- Using the scaling function fit for AV18, the authors estimate $S_{2n}\simeq 0.384\pm 0.036$ MeV from the experimental radius and $a_s=-150$ fm with 50% error

## Context

This work extends the universal scaling approach proposed by Yamashita et al. (2004) for three-body systems with s-wave zero-range potentials to the specific case of $^{19}$B, building on the three-body model calculations of Hiyama et al. (2019) that predicted the binding energy of $^{19}$B. The results are compared with the experimental radius extraction from Cook et al. (2020) and with the three-body calculations of Casal and Garrido (2020) using a Woods-Saxon potential. The scaling functions enable model-independent predictions of matter and proton radii for $^{19}$B based on minimal physical inputs (two-neutron separation energy and scattering lengths), and the approach can be applied to other two-neutron halo nuclei near the unitarity limit.
