# Low-energy p-d Scattering: High Precision Data, Comparisons with Theory, and Phase-Shift Analyses

> Angular distributions of sigma(theta), A_y, iT_11, T_20, T_21, and T_22 have been measured for d-p scattering at E_c.m.=667 keV. This set of high-precision data is compared to variational calculations with the nucleon-nucleon potential alone and also to calculations including a three-nucleon (3N) potential. Agreement with cross-section and tensor analyzing power data is excellent when a 3N potential is used. However, a comparison between the vector analyzing powers reveals differences of approximately 40% in the maxima of the angular distributions which is larger than reported at higher energies for both p-d and n-d scattering. Single-energy phase-shift analyses were performed on this data set and a similar data set at E_c.m.=431.3 keV. The role of the different phase-shift parameters in fitting these data is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that at E_c.m. = 667 keV, the discrepancy between measured vector analyzing powers (A_y and iT_11) and variational calculations using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone is approximately 40% in the maxima of the angular distributions, and that including a three-nucleon potential (UR-IX, TM, or TM') reduces this discrepancy only marginally, leaving a residual disagreement of 25-36% depending on the observable and the 3N potential model used.

## Method

Angular distributions of the differential cross section sigma(theta), the vector analyzing powers A_y and iT_11, and the tensor analyzing powers T_20, T_21, and T_22 were measured for deuteron-proton elastic scattering at E_c.m. = 667 +/- 1 keV using the FN tandem accelerator at Triangle Universities Nuclear Laboratory. The data were compared to variational calculations using the Pair-Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis and the Kohn variational principle, employing the AV18 potential alone and with the UR-IX, TM, and TM' three-nucleon potentials. Single-energy phase-shift analyses were performed at both 667 keV and 431.3 keV, allowing selected S- and P-wave phase shifts and mixing parameters to vary from the AV18+UR-IX starting values.

## Key numbers

- E_c.m. = 667 +/- 1 keV for the new measurements; also analyzed data at E_c.m. = 431.3 keV from prior work
- Absolute cross-section uncertainties: 0.5% from counting statistics, 0.5% from normalization to p-p scattering, 0.6% from beam-current integration
- Beam polarizations for T_20 and T_22 measurements: p_zz approximately +/- 0.8 with 3% error; for T_21: p_zz approximately +/- 0.8 with 2% error; for iT_11: p_z approximately +/- 0.55; for A_y: p_z approximately +/- 0.65 with 2% error
- Difference between A_y data and AV18-only calculation: approximately 40% at the maximum of the angular distribution; with UR-IX: approximately 36%; with TM: approximately 39%; with TM': approximately 32%
- Difference between iT_11 data and AV18-only calculation: approximately 37%; with UR-IX: approximately 29%; with TM: approximately 34%; with TM': approximately 25%
- Reduced chi-squared (chi^2_N) for AV18+UR-IX calculation at 667 keV: sigma(theta) = 1.2, A_y = 190.5, iT_11 = 61.4, T_20 = 1.0, T_21 = 2.5, T_22 = 0.7
- Best two-parameter fit (trial 3 at 667 keV, varying ^4P_1/2 and epsilon_3/2-): chi^2_N for A_y = 0.6, for iT_11 = 1.8
- Full 9-parameter S- and P-wave fit at 667 keV: chi^2_N approximately 0.9 for each observable
- Calculated ^4P_J splitting at 667 keV: Delta P = 0.87 degrees; fit value: 0.96 degrees; at 431.3 keV: calculated 0.48 degrees, fit 0.63 degrees
- Epsilon_3/2- increased in absolute value by approximately 19% from the 667 keV PSA and approximately 28% from the 431.3 keV PSA over the AV18+UR-IX predicted value

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the "A_y puzzle" in nucleon-deuteron scattering to lower energy (E_c.m. = 667 keV), where the discrepancy between measured vector analyzing powers and calculations using only nucleon-nucleon potentials (such as AV18, CD Bonn, Nijmegen III) is larger (approximately 40%) than at higher energies. The results confirm and extend the findings of Knutson (1993), Shimizu (1995), and the authors' own prior work at 431.3 keV (Brune 2000), showing that including three-nucleon potentials (UR-IX, TM, TM') provides only marginal improvement. The phase-shift analyses demonstrate that the discrepancy originates primarily in the ^4P_J phase shifts and the epsilon_3/2- mixing parameter, and the paper points toward a phenomenological 3N spin-orbit force proposed by Kievsky (1999) as a promising direction for resolving the puzzle. The results enable future investigations of p-^3He scattering, where the A_y observable is an order of magnitude larger than in p-d scattering.
