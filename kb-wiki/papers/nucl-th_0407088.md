# Inequalities for low-energy symmetric nuclear matter

> Using effective field theory we prove inequalities for the correlations of two-nucleon operators in low-energy symmetric nuclear matter. For physical values of operator coefficients in the effective Lagrangian, the S = 1, I = 0 channel correlations must have the lowest energy and longest correlation length in the two-nucleon sector. This result is valid at nonzero density and temperature.

## Key claim

Using effective field theory, the paper proves that in low-energy symmetric nuclear matter at nonzero density and temperature, the spin S=1, isospin I=0 two-nucleon channel must have the lowest energy and longest correlation length among all two-nucleon channels, provided the operator coefficients in the effective Lagrangian take their physical values.

## Method

The author starts from a pionless effective Lagrangian for non-relativistic nucleons with contact interactions, expressed in two equivalent forms using spin-isospin operator bases. The grand canonical partition function is rewritten via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, and the nucleon matrix determinant is shown to be real and non-negative at nonzero chemical potential due to a conjugate similarity relation analogous to the gamma5 relation in QCD. Using the positive measure and the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality, the two-point correlation function for a general two-nucleon operator B is bounded by that of the S=1, I=0 operator A, leading to inequalities for energies, scattering lengths, and correlation lengths.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalue of the operator (1/2):N-bar sigma N dot N-bar sigma N: is 1 in the S=1, I=0 channel and -3 in the S=0, I=1 channel.
- The eigenvalue of the operator (1/2):N-bar tau N dot N-bar tau N: is -3 in the S=1, I=0 channel and 1 in the S=0, I=1 channel.
- The inequality for the two-nucleon scattering length in the unbound case is a_A ≤ a_B, where a_A is the scattering length in the S=1, I=0 channel and a_B is the scattering length in any other two-nucleon channel.
- The energy of the lowest two-particle scattering state relative to threshold in a periodic box of length L is E_0 = 4π a / (m_N L^3) [1 + O(a L^{-1})], where a is the scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the tradition of QCD inequalities pioneered by Vafa and Witten (1984) and Witten (1983) for the QCD vacuum and hadronic spectrum, applying similar functional integral methods to low-energy effective field theory for nuclear matter. The results are motivated by recent lattice simulations of nuclear matter using effective field theory by Muller (1999), Chen (2003), and Lee (2004). The inequalities enable rigorous comparisons of two-nucleon correlations in strongly coupled regimes where the average nucleon separation is less than the scattering length, and can be checked in lattice simulations using chiral effective field theory.
