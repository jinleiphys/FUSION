# Prediction for a four-neutron resonance

> We utilize various {\em ab initio} approaches to search for a low-lying resonance in the four-neutron ($4n$) system using the JISP16 realistic $NN$ interaction. Our most accurate prediction is obtained using a $J$-matrix extension of the No-Core Shell Model and suggests a $4n$ resonant state at an energy near $E_r = 0.8$ MeV with a width of approximately $Γ= 1.4$ MeV.

## Key claim

Using the JISP16 realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction and a J-matrix extension of the No-Core Shell Model (the SS-HORSE method), the authors predict a four-neutron resonant state at an energy near 0.8 MeV with a width of approximately 1.4 MeV, but note that this result depends on including an additional low-energy false pole in the S-matrix parametrization to obtain a consistent description of the phase shifts.

## Method

The authors employed three ab initio many-body methods with the JISP16 realistic NN interaction: the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) with artificially scaled interactions to track bound states, the No-Core Gamow Shell Model (NCGSM) using Berggren basis states in the complex energy plane, and the Single-State Harmonic Oscillator Representation of Scattering Equations (SS-HORSE) formalism which extends the finite NCSM Hamiltonian matrix into the continuum using a democratic decay approximation with hyperspherical harmonics. The SS-HORSE approach calculated 4-to-4 S-matrix phase shifts from NCSM eigenenergies obtained with basis spaces up to Nmax=18 and oscillator frequencies from 1 to 40 MeV, then parametrized these phase shifts to extract resonance pole positions.

## Key numbers

- SS-HORSE prediction: resonance energy E_r = 0.844 MeV with width Γ = 1.378 MeV (from parametrization including a false pole at E_f = -54.9 keV)
- Alternative SS-HORSE fit without false pole gave E_r = 0.186 MeV and Γ = 0.815 MeV, inconsistent with direct phase shift inspection
- NCGSM results indicated a broad resonance in the range E_r ~ 2.5 to 3 MeV with width Γ ~ 2.5 to 6 MeV (2p-2h approximation)
- Experimental comparison: candidate 4n resonance at 0.83 ± 0.65 (stat) ± 1.25 (syst) MeV with upper limit Γ < 2.6 MeV
- RMS deviation of SS-HORSE fit to selected NCSM eigenenergies: 5.8 keV (single pole) and 6.2 keV (with false pole)

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies that ruled out a bound tetraneutron (Pieper, 2003) and suggested a possible resonance near 2 MeV, while also addressing earlier Complex Scaling Method calculations (Sofianos, Lazauskas, Carbonell) that predicted very large widths (~15 MeV). The prediction is compatible with a recent experiment (2016) that found a candidate 4n resonant structure, and the results enable future comparisons with forthcoming experiments at GSI and other facilities. The authors note that their NCGSM results agree only marginally with experiment due to large error bars in both theory and experiment.
