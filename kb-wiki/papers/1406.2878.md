# Dipole amplitude with uncertainty estimate from HERA data and applications in Color Glass Condensate phenomenology

> We determine the initial condition for the small-$x$ evolution equation (BK) from the HERA deep inelastic scattering data using a new parametrization that also keeps the unintegrated gluon distribution positive. The obtained dipole amplitude and its uncertainty estimate can be used to compute single inclusive particle production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions. We argue that one has to use consistently the proton transverse area measured in DIS and the total inelastic cross section when calculating the single inclusive cross section. This leads to a midrapidity nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ that approaches unity at large transverse momentum, independently of the center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim

The authors determine the initial condition for the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) small-x evolution equation from HERA deep inelastic scattering data using a new parametrization (MV\(^e\)) that keeps the unintegrated gluon distribution positive, and they argue that consistently using the proton transverse area measured in DIS together with the total inelastic cross section leads to a midrapidity nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) that approaches unity at large transverse momentum independently of center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The dipole-proton amplitude at initial \(x_0 = 0.01\) is parametrized using a modified McLerran-Venugopalan model with three variants (MV, MV\(^\gamma\), MV\(^e\)) and fitted to the combined H1 and ZEUS reduced cross section data for \(x < 10^{-2}\) and \(Q^2 < 50\ \textrm{GeV}^2\). The dipole-nucleus amplitude is obtained via the optical Glauber model, and experimental uncertainties are propagated to the dipole amplitude using the Hessian method with \(\Delta\chi^2 \approx 36\). Single inclusive particle production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions is computed using \(k_T\)-factorized and hybrid formalism formulas.

## Key numbers

- Best fit \(\chi^2/N = 1.15\) for the MV\(^e\) model, compared to 2.76 for the standard MV model and 1.17 for the MV\(^\gamma\) model.
- Initial saturation scale \(Q_{s,0}^2 = 0.060\ \textrm{GeV}^2\) for MV\(^e\), 0.104 GeV\(^2\) for MV, and 0.165 GeV\(^2\) for MV\(^\gamma\).
- Saturation scale \(Q_s^2 = 0.238\ \textrm{GeV}^2\) for MV\(^e\), 0.139 GeV\(^2\) for MV, and 0.245 GeV\(^2\) for MV\(^\gamma\).
- Proton DIS area \(\sigma_0/2 = 16.36\ \textrm{mb}\) for MV\(^e\), 18.81 mb for MV, and 16.45 mb for MV\(^\gamma\).
- Anomalous dimension \(\gamma = 1.135\) for MV\(^\gamma\); infrared cutoff parameter \(e_c = 18.9\) for MV\(^e\).
- Running coupling scale parameter \(C^2 = 7.2\) for MV\(^e\), 14.5 for MV, and 6.35 for MV\(^\gamma\).
- A normalization factor \(K = 2.5\) is needed to match leading-order hybrid formalism calculations to RHIC data.

## Context

This work extends the AAMQS fits (Albacete 2010) by introducing a new parametrization (MV\(^e\)) that ensures positivity of the unintegrated gluon distribution, and provides the first uncertainty estimate for the dipole amplitude from HERA data using the Hessian method. The resulting dipole amplitude enables consistent calculations of single inclusive particle production in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, and the finding that \(R_{pA} \to 1\) at large \(p_T\) independently of energy provides a robust CGC prediction testable at RHIC and LHC.
