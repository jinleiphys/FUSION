# Linear-response calculation in the time-dependent density functional theory

> Linear response calculations based on the time-dependent density-functional theory are presented. Especially, we report results of the finite amplitude method which we have recently proposed as an alternative and feasible approach to the (quasiparticle-)random-phase approximation. Calculated properties of the giant resonances and low-energy E1 modes are discussed. We found a universal linear correlation between the low-energy E1 strength and the neutron skin thickness.

## Key claim

The authors report a universal linear correlation between the low-energy E1 strength (pygmy dipole resonance fraction) and the neutron skin thickness, but this correlation holds only within specific neutron-number regions (N=28-34 and N≥50) and only for each isotopic chain, not across all nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the finite amplitude method (FAM), which avoids explicit calculation of residual interaction matrix elements by numerically differentiating the static density-functional Hamiltonian. For spherical and deformed nuclei across a wide mass region (A≤100), they computed photoabsorption cross sections using a three-dimensional grid representation with no symmetry restrictions, employing a complex energy ω=E+iΓ/2 with Γ=1 MeV for smoothing.

## Key numbers

- GDR peak energies approximately follow the empirical law 21 A^(-1/3) + 31 A^(-1/6) MeV across the mass region from oxygen to nickel
- The PDR strength fraction f_PDR is defined using an energy cutoff ω_c = 10 MeV
- The universal linear correlation slope between PDR fraction and neutron skin thickness is 0.18-0.20 fm^(-1) for all isotopes studied
- The linear correlation remains valid for neutron numbers 50 ≤ N < 76
- Cusps in GDR peak energies appear at N=14 and N=28, corresponding to subshell closures of 1d_(5/2) and 1f_(7/2) orbitals
- PDR fractions show sudden increases at N=28→30 and beyond N=50

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (FAM) previously proposed by Nakatsukasa et al. (2007) from RPA to QRPA for superfluid nuclei, and applies it systematically to calculate giant dipole resonances and low-energy E1 modes. The results contradict the notion of a universal correlation between low-energy E1 strength and neutron skin thickness across all nuclei, showing instead that the correlation is restricted to specific isotopic chains. The findings enable future constraints on the nuclear equation of state by linking pygmy dipole resonance measurements to neutron skin thickness, which is correlated with the slope of the neutron-matter equation of state as discussed by Brown (2000).
