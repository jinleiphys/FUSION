# Patterns of gauge symmetry in the background field method

> The correlation functions of Yang-Mills theories formulated in the background field method satisfy linear Slavnov-Taylor identities, which are naive generalizations of simple tree level relations, with no deformations originating from the ghost sector of the theory. In recent years, a stronger version of these identities has been found to hold at the level of the background gluon self-energy, whose transversality is enforced separately for each special block of diagrams contributing to the gluon Schwinger-Dyson equation. In the present work we demonstrate by means of explicit calculations that the same distinct realization of the Slavnov-Taylor identity persists in the case of the background three-gluon vertex. The analysis is carried out at the level of the exact Schwinger-Dyson equation for this vertex, with no truncations or simplifying assumptions. The demonstration entails the contraction of individual vertex diagrams by the relevant momentum, which activates Slavnov-Taylor identities of vertices and multi-particle kernels nested inside these graphs; the final result emerges by virtue of a multitude of extensive cancellations, without the need of performing explicit integrations. In addition, we point out that background Ward identities amount to replacing derivatives of propagators by zero-momentum background-gluon insertions, in exact analogy to standard properties of Abelian gauge theories. Finally, certain potential applications of these results are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Slavnov-Taylor identity for the background three-gluon vertex is realized "block-wise," meaning that each of four distinct subsets of diagrams in its Schwinger-Dyson equation independently satisfies the identity, mirroring a previously known property of the background gluon self-energy; this result holds for any value of the quantum gauge-fixing parameter and without any truncations or simplifying assumptions.

## Method

The authors analyze the exact Schwinger-Dyson equation for the background three-gluon vertex, classifying its diagrams into four blocks (one-loop gluonic, one-loop ghost, two-loop gluonic, and two-loop ghost) that correspond to the same blocks previously identified for the background gluon self-energy. They contract each block of vertex diagrams by the relevant momentum, which activates the Abelian Slavnov-Taylor identities of the vertices and multi-particle kernels nested inside the graphs, and show through algebraic cancellations that each block yields the difference of the corresponding self-energy blocks, without performing explicit integrations.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level three-gluon vertex is given by $\gh^{(0)}_{\alpha\mu\nu}(q,r,p) = (q-r)_{\nu}g_{\alpha\mu} + (r-p)_{\alpha} g_{\mu\nu} + (p-q)_{\mu}g_{\alpha\nu}$, matching the conventional QQQ vertex.
- The coupling constant combination $\lambda := g^2 C_{\rm A}$ appears throughout, where $C_{\rm A}$ is the Casimir eigenvalue of the adjoint representation (N for SU(N)).
- The one-loop ghost self-energy diagrams $(a_3)_{\mu\nu}(q)$ and $(a_4)_{\mu\nu}(q)$ are shown to cancel when contracted by $q^\nu$, yielding $q^\nu[(a_3)+(a_4)]_{\mu\nu}(q)=0$ for the second block.
- The one-loop gluonic vertex diagram $(b_3)$ contracts to $p^\nu(b_3)^{abc}_{\alpha\mu\nu}=f^{cae}(a_1)^{be}_{\alpha\mu}(r)-f^{bce}(a_1)^{ae}_{\alpha\mu}(q)$, establishing the block-wise identity for the first block.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of "block-wise" transversality for the background gluon self-energy (Aguilar 2006, Aguilar 2008, Aguilar 2015) to the three-point function, showing that the pattern is not unique to two-point functions. The results rely on the Abelian Slavnov-Taylor identities of the background field method (DeWitt 1967, Abbott 1981, Abbott 1983), which are ghost-free generalizations of tree-level relations, in contrast to the standard Slavnov-Taylor identities of the $R_\xi$ gauges (Taylor 1971, Slavnov 1972) where ghost contributions modify the identities non-trivially. The demonstration enables future applications to the nonperturbative study of Yang-Mills correlation functions within the background field method, particularly for the three-gluon vertex and its Ward identities.
