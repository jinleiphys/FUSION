# Theoretical Study of the ^3He(mu^-,nu_mu)^3H Capture

> The ^3He(mu^-,nu_mu)^3H weak capture is studied using correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics wave functions, obtained from realistic Hamiltonians consisting of the Argonne $v_{14}$ or Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon, and Tucson-Melbourne or Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions. The nuclear weak charge and current operators have vector and axial-vector components that include one- and two-body contributions. The strength of the leading two-body operator in the axial-vector current is adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium $β$-decay. The calculated total capture rate is in excellent agreement with the most recent experimental determination $1496\pm 4$ sec$^{-1}$, when the PCAC value is adopted for the induced pseudo-scalar coupling constant $g_{PS}$. The predictions for the capture rate and angular correlation parameters $A_v$, $A_t$, and $A_Δ$ are found to be only very weakly dependent on the model input Hamiltonian. The variation of these observables with $g_{PS}$ and the theoretical uncertainties deriving from the model-dependent procedure used to constrain the axial current are investigated.

## Key claim

The calculated total capture rate for the \(^3\text{He}(\mu^-,\nu_\mu)^3\text{H}\) process agrees with the most recent experimental value of \(1496 \pm 4\) sec\(^{-1}\) to within approximately 0.5%, but only when the PCAC value is adopted for the induced pseudo-scalar coupling constant \(g_{PS}\); the agreement is contingent on this specific choice and on the model-dependent procedure used to constrain the axial current.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body bound-state problem using the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) method with realistic Hamiltonians that include either the Argonne \(v_{14}\) or Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon interactions combined with the Tucson-Melbourne or Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions. The nuclear weak charge and current operators included one- and two-body vector and axial-vector components, with the strength of the leading two-body axial-vector operator (the \(N\Delta\)-transition axial coupling constant \(g_A^*\)) adjusted to reproduce the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium \(\beta\)-decay. The induced pseudo-scalar contributions were included for both the nucleon and the \(\Delta\)-isobar, with the coupling constants derived from pion-pole dominance and the partially-conserved-axial-current (PCAC) hypothesis.

## Key numbers

- Total capture rate \(\Gamma_0\) calculated with the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian model: \(1496 \pm 7\) sec\(^{-1}\) (central value with uncertainty from \(g_A^*\) determination), compared to the experimental value \(1496 \pm 4\) sec\(^{-1}\).
- Total capture rate calculated with the AV14/TM Hamiltonian model: within 1% of the experimental value \(1496 \pm 4\) sec\(^{-1}\).
- Total capture rate predicted by the AV18 and AV14 Hamiltonian models (without three-nucleon interactions): about 4% smaller than the experimental value.
- Total capture rate calculated with a modified AV14/TM Hamiltonian that gives triton binding energy 9.042 MeV: \(1509 \pm 7\) sec\(^{-1}\).
- Momentum transfer in the process: \(q \simeq 103\) MeV/\(c\).
- The nucleon induced pseudo-scalar term reduces the value of \(\Gamma_0\) by about 16%.
- The pseudo-scalar \(\Delta\)-current reduces the value of \(\Gamma_0\) by less than 1%.
- The parameter \(R_A\) (where \(g_A^* = R_A g_A\)) ranges from values corresponding to \((f_{\pi N\Delta}/f_{\pi NN})^2\) in the range 1.08–1.56 across the different Hamiltonian models.
- The experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium \(\beta\)-decay: GT\(^{\text{EXP}} = 0.957 \pm 0.003\).
- The axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) taken as \(1.2654 \pm 0.0042\).
- The cutoff mass \(\Lambda_A\) approximately 1 GeV/\(c^2\).
- The Bohr radius of the muonic atom in the ground state: about 130 fm.
- The factor \(\mathcal{R}\) accounting for finite nuclear charge distribution: 0.98.
- The hyperfine energy splitting between \(f=0\) and \(f=1\) states: 1.5 eV.

## Context

This work sharpens and updates the earlier microscopic calculation by Congleton and Truhlík (1995), which used the Argonne \(v_{14}\) and Tucson-Melbourne interactions and suffered from a 2% theoretical uncertainty in the capture rate. The present study uses more modern Hamiltonians (Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Urbana-IX) and includes additional induced pseudo-scalar contributions in both the nucleon and \(\Delta\)-isobar axial currents that were neglected in the earlier work. The results enable more precise predictions for the angular correlation parameters \(A_v\), \(A_t\), and \(A_\Delta\), which are more sensitive than the capture rate to the value of the nucleon pseudo-scalar axial coupling constant \(g_{PS}\), and provide a benchmark for future experimental measurements of these parameters with improved accuracy.
