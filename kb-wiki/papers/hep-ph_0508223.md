# The impact of QCD plasma instabilities on bottom-up thermalization

> QCD plasma instabilities, caused by an anisotropic momentum distributions of the particles in the plasma, are likely to play an important role in thermalization in heavy ion collisions. We consider plasmas with two different components of particles, one strongly anisotropic and one isotropic or nearly isotropic. The isotropic component does not eliminate instabilities but it decreases their growth rates. We investigate the impact of plasma instabilities on the first stage of the ``bottom-up'' thermalization scenario in which such a two-component plasma emerges, and find that even in the case of non-abelian saturation instabilities qualitatively change the bottom-up picture.

## Key claim

QCD plasma instabilities qualitatively change the bottom-up thermalization picture even when they are saturated by non-abelian interactions at amplitudes much smaller than the abelian saturation bound, contrary to earlier suggestions that abelianization would allow instabilities to isotropize the plasma quickly.

## Method

The author analyzes the linearized non-abelian Vlasov equations for a two-component plasma consisting of strongly anisotropic hard gluons and nearly isotropic soft gluons, computing the polarization tensor and solving the dispersion relation for unstable modes. The growth rates of instabilities are derived for different regimes of the soft-to-hard mass ratio, and the impact on the first stage of the bottom-up thermalization scenario is estimated by considering 2-to-2 scattering of hard gluons with the saturated unstable modes.

## Key numbers

- For hard and soft contributions of the same order (m_s ~ m_h), the instability growth rate is gamma ~ m_h/sqrt(2) for wave numbers m_h << |k| << k_max, with the soft gluon effect negligible in this regime.
- When m_s >> m_h, the growth rate for small |k| scales as gamma ~ (m_h^2 / (pi m_s^2))^(1/3) |k|, while for larger |k| (m_h^(1/3) m_s^(2/3) << |k| << k_max) it returns to gamma ~ m_h/sqrt(2).
- With non-abelian saturation, the hard gluon longitudinal momentum evolves as p_h^z ~ Q (Q tau)^(-1/4), which is larger than the bottom-up scenario estimate p_h^z ~ Q (Q tau)^(-1/3).
- The maximum wave number for instabilities is k_max ~ Q (Q tau)^(-1/4), which coincides with the estimated p_h^z.

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier et al. (2001) by incorporating QCD plasma instabilities, which were previously argued by Arnold et al. (2005) to potentially cause rapid isotropization if abelianization occurs. The paper addresses a concern raised by Mueller (2005) that soft gluons might suppress instabilities, showing they only reduce growth rates without eliminating them. The results are relevant to understanding whether plasma instabilities can explain the early onset of hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, as suggested by Arnold et al. (2005), but the author finds that with non-abelian saturation (as seen in lattice simulations by Arnold and Moore (2006) and Romatschke and Venugopalan (2006)) the isotropization is far from complete.
