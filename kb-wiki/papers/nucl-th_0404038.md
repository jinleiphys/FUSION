# Study of deuteron-proton charge exchange reaction at small transfer momentum

> The charge-exchange reaction pd->npp at 1 GeV projectile proton energy is studied in the multiple-scattering expansion technique. This reaction is considered in a special kinematics, when the transfer momentum from the beam proton to fast neutron is close to zero. The differential cross section and a set of polarization observables are calculated. It was shown that contribution of the final state interaction between two protons is very significant.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the deuteron-proton charge exchange reaction at 1 GeV projectile energy with near-zero transfer momentum, the final state interaction between the two slow protons is very significant for the differential cross section even at very small proton momenta, but for polarization observables the D-wave and FSI effects are negligible for proton momenta up to 10-15 MeV/c, allowing extraction of nucleon-nucleon charge exchange amplitude ratios in that region.

## Method

The reaction pd → npp at 1 GeV projectile proton energy is studied using the multiple-scattering expansion technique based on the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formulation of three-body theory. The matrix inversion method is applied to account for final state interaction contributions, and the Love and Franey parametrization with parameters from the SP00 phase shift analysis is employed for the nucleon-nucleon amplitudes. Calculations are performed in collinear geometry where the transfer momentum from the beam proton to the fast neutron is approximately zero (q ≈ 1.8 MeV/c, approximated as q=0), and the Paris deuteron wave function is used including both S- and D-wave components.

## Key numbers

- Projectile proton energy: 1 GeV
- Transfer momentum q ≈ 1.8 MeV/c (approximated as q=0 in calculations)
- The FSI contribution to the differential cross section is significant even at very small proton momentum; for polarization observables the difference between PWIA and PWIA+FSI is visible only for p₂ ≥ 10-15 MeV/c
- The vector-tensor spin correlation C_{y,xz} has a very small value, |C_{y,xz}| ≈ 0.06
- The magnitude of C_{y,y} is close to the theoretical limit -2/3
- The difference for the differential cross section between two NN-parametrizations (SP00 vs Love and Franey) is about 1.5-2 times
- The absolute value for the tensor analyzing power C_{0,yy} with the SP00 parametrization is about 2.5 times smaller than with the Love and Franey parametrization
- The vector-vector spin correlation C_{y,y} with the SP00 parametrization is 1.5 times larger compared to the Love and Franey parametrization

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the pd breakup process at 1 GeV (Ladygina and Shebeko, 2003) using the same theoretical approach. The reaction is motivated by the planned experiment at the ANKE setup at COSY, which aims to extract information about spin-dependent np-elastic scattering amplitudes in an energy region where phase-shift analysis data are absent, following the idea originally suggested by Pomeranchuk (1951) and developed by Dean, Wilkin, and Car (1970s). The paper shows that in the small momentum interval (p₂ ≤ 10-15 MeV/c), the ratio of nucleon-nucleon charge exchange amplitudes and their relative phase can be extracted from experimental data in a relatively model-independent way, while at higher momenta the procedure becomes more complicated and model dependent due to increasing FSI and D-wave contributions.
