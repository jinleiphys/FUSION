# Hexaquark picture for $d^*(2380)$

> Hexaquark wave function with the quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$, which might be relevant for $d^*(2380)$, is constructed under an assumption that this is composed only of $u,d$ quarks in an $S$-wave. By combining three diquarks of either type, ($\bm{\bar{3}}_c, I=1$) or ($\bm{6}_c, I=0$), we demonstrate that there are five possible configurations for the six-quark state. The fully antisymmetric wave function is constructed by linearly combining the five configurations on an equal footing. We then take this wave function as well as the five configurations to calculate the hexaquark mass using the contact type effective potential consisting of the color-spin, color electric and constant shift. The mass is found to be the same regardless of the configurations being used including the fully antisymmetric one. This result can be traced to the fact that the hexaquark system has a freedom in choosing three diquarks in the construction of its wave function. The calculated hexaquark mass using the empirical parameters independently fixed from the baryon spectroscopy is found to be around $2342$ MeV, which is indeed very close to the experimental mass of $d^*(2380)$. Therefore, the hexaquark picture is promising for $d^*(2380)$ as far as the mass is concerned.

## Key claim

The hexaquark mass calculated using a contact-type effective potential with parameters independently fixed from baryon spectroscopy is found to be around 2342 MeV, which is about 40 MeV below the experimental mass of d*(2380); the authors hedge that this agreement holds only "as far as the mass is concerned" and that the hexaquark picture is "promising" but not conclusively established.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully antisymmetric six-quark wave function with quantum numbers I(J^P)=0(3^+) by linearly combining five possible color-isospin configurations built from three diquarks of either the (3̅_c, I=1) or (6_c, I=0) type, assuming all u,d quarks are in an S-wave with spin-up. They calculated the hexaquark mass using a contact-type effective potential consisting of color-spin, color-electric, and constant shift terms, with parameters v0 = (-199.6 MeV)^3, v1 = (71.2 MeV)^3, and v2 = 122.5 MeV fixed from fitting the N, Δ, and Λ baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- The calculated hexaquark mass is 2341.5 MeV, which is about 40 MeV below the experimental d*(2380) mass of approximately 2380 MeV.
- The expectation value of the effective potential for the hexaquark is ⟨V_eff⟩ = 361.5 MeV, using constituent quark mass m = 330 MeV.
- The color-color expectation value ⟨∑_{j<k} λ_j·λ_k⟩ is -16 for all five color configurations.
- The effective potential parameters are v0 = (-199.6 MeV)^3, v1 = (71.2 MeV)^3, and v2 = 122.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hexaquark approach to d*(2380) previously investigated by Park (2015), who obtained a much larger mass (2630 or 2809 MeV) and concluded the hexaquark picture was not realistic, and by Shi (2019), who used only the spin-1 3̅_c diquark configuration. The authors argue that their contact-type effective potential, which freezes spatial dependence by fitting parameters from baryon spectroscopy, yields a mass close to the experimental value, in contrast to the variational calculation of Park (2015). The result also agrees closely with the 2350 MeV prediction from the two-baryon molecular picture of Dyson and Xuong (1964), suggesting that the hexaquark picture can accommodate the molecular picture as a component.
