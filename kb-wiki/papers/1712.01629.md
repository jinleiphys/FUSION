# Effects of Renormalizing the chiral SU(2) Quark-Meson-Model

> We investigate the restoration of chiral symmetry at finite temperature in the SU(2) quark meson model where the mean field approximation is compared to the renormalized version for quarks and mesons. In a combined approach at finite temperature all the renormalized versions show a crossover transition. The inclusion of different renormalization scales leave the order parameter and the mass spectra nearly untouched, but strongly influence the thermodynamics at low temperatures and around the phase transition. We find unphysical results for the renormalized version of mesons and the combined one.

## Key claim

The authors find that while the renormalization scale cancels in the SU(2) quark-meson model for the quark fields alone, the combined model for quarks and mesons is dependent on the renormalization scales, leading to unphysical results for the renormalized version of mesons and the combined approach; consequently, a combined model for quarks and mesons is only acceptable in the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors investigate the SU(2) quark-meson model using the path integral formalism for quarks and the 2PI (Cornwall-Jackiw-Toumboulis) formalism for mesons, comparing the mean field approximation to renormalized versions that include vacuum fluctuations via dimensional regularization. For the quark sector, vacuum contributions are renormalized using the minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme, while for the meson sector, a mass correction term is added to handle divergences. The gap equations are solved self-consistently to determine the order parameter (sigma condensate) and the masses of the sigma and pion mesons at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

* The constituent quark mass is chosen to be 300 MeV, with the pion decay constant set to 92.4 MeV.
* The sigma meson vacuum mass is varied in the range 500 ≤ m_σ^vac ≤ 900 MeV.
* For the standard value of m_σ = 550 MeV, a smooth chiral crossover phase transition is found around the critical temperature T_c ≃ 155 MeV.
* The Yukawa coupling is defined as g = m_q,vac / f_π, where m_q,vac = 300 MeV and f_π = 92.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of the quark-meson model that included vacuum terms via renormalization group flow equations, particularly those focusing on critical points by Bailin (1984), Berges (1997), Schaefer (2004), and Mocsy (2004). The authors compare their quark field results with works by Gupta (2011), Herbst (2013), and Stiele (2013), their mesonic field results with works by Lenaghan (1999), Lenaghan (2000), Grahl (2011), and Seel (2011), and their combined approach with the work of Mocsy (2004). The finding that the combined renormalized model yields unphysical results suggests that future work should restrict combined quark-meson treatments to the mean field approximation.
