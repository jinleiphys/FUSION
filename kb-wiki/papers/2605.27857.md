# Multiplicity distributions in DIS for heavy nucleus

> We found solutions to the linear but with complicated kernel and non-homogeneous evolution equations for the cross sections of productions of $n$-cut Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) Pomerons in the final states of high energy DIS on a nucleus, resumming all multiple rescatterings in the leading logarithmic approximation. For the model leading-twist BFKL kernel, we calculate analytical solutions of these equations by developing the homotopy approach. We also calculate the solution in the large $z=\ln\left(x_{01}^2\,Q_s^2(Y,\mathbf{b})\right)$ and large $n\gtrsim\langle n(z) \rangle$ limits, where $x_{01}$ is the dipole size, $Q_s$ the saturation scale and $\langle n(z) \rangle$ is the average multiplicity of the produced gluons. Having these cross sections we calculate the multiplicity distributions of the produced gluons and describe how the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can test our theoretical formalism.

## Key claim

The authors solve the linear but non-homogeneous evolution equations for the cross sections of n-cut BFKL Pomeron production in DIS on a heavy nucleus, but only for the model leading-twist BFKL kernel and in the leading logarithmic approximation; the solutions are obtained analytically using a homotopy approach for small n and in the large-z and large-n limits, with no matching between the two solution regimes.

## Method

The authors start from the evolution equations for the cross sections of n-cut BFKL Pomerons in dipole-nucleus scattering, which resum multiple rescatterings. They simplify these equations for the model leading-twist BFKL kernel in the kinematic regions where one dipole size dominates, introducing the geometric scaling variable z = ln(x_01^2 Q_s^2(Y,b)). They then solve the resulting equations using two approaches: a homotopy method that treats the linear part analytically and the nonlinear part perturbatively, and a large-z and large-n asymptotic analysis that yields a Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling form for the multiplicity distribution.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale evolves as Q_s^2(Y,b) = Q_s^2(Y=Y_A,b) exp[bar_alpha_S kappa Y - (3/(2(1-gamma_cr))) ln(bar_alpha_S Y)], where kappa and gamma_cr are determined by the BFKL eigenvalue function chi(gamma).
* The average multiplicity of produced gluons at large z is given by <n(z)> = 2 n_0 z exp(z^2/(2 kappa)), where n_0 is a constant fixed by matching to the initial condition.
* The multiplicity distribution P_n(z) at large z and large n takes the KNO scaling form P_n(z) = (2/kappa) (z^2/<n(z)>) Psi(n/<n(z)>) with Psi(xi) = xi e^{-xi}.
* The initial condition for sigma_n at Y=Y_A is sigma_n = ((1/2) x_01^2 Q_s^2(Y=Y_A,b))^n / n! exp(-(1/2) x_01^2 Q_s^2(Y=Y_A,b)).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of evolution equations for n-cut BFKL Pomeron cross sections by Kovchegov, Levin, and collaborators (KLP, Levin 2023) and applies them to DIS on a heavy nucleus. It builds on the Abramovsky-Gribov-Kancheli (AGK) cutting rules and the equivalence between BFKL Pomeron calculus and the dipole picture at leading logarithmic accuracy. The results enable predictions for multiplicity distributions that can be tested at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and compared with existing HERA data from the H1 collaboration.
