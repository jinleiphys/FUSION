# A Multi-Configuration Mixing Approach with Symmetry-Projected Complex Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Determinants

> A multi-configuration mixing approach built on essentially complex, symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean fields is introduced. The mean fields are obtained by variation after projection. The configuration space consists out of the symmetry-projected HFB vacuum and the symmetry-projected two-quasiparticle excitations for even, and the symmetry-projected one-quasiparticle excitations for odd A systems. The underlying complex HFB transformations are assumed to be time-reversal invariant and axially symmetric. The model allows nuclear structure calculations in large model spaces with arbitrary two-body interactions. The approach has been applied to $^{20}$Ne and $^{22}$Ne. Good agreement with the exact shell model results and considerable improvement with respect to older calculations, in which only real HFB transformations were admitted, is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a multi-configuration mixing approach (MONSTER) built on essentially complex, symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean fields obtained by variation after projection, claiming good agreement with exact shell model results for 20Ne and 22Ne and considerable improvement over older calculations that admitted only real HFB transformations, though the approach is restricted to time-reversal invariant and axially symmetric HFB transformations.

## Method

The method combines the VAMPIR approach, which provides a symmetry-projected HFB vacuum determined by variation after projection, with the MONSTER approach, which diagonalizes the Hamiltonian in a space consisting of that symmetry-projected vacuum plus symmetry-projected two-quasiparticle excitations (for even-A systems) or one-quasiparticle excitations (for odd-A systems). The underlying HFB transformations are restricted to be essentially complex, time-reversal invariant, and axially symmetric, which simplifies the projection operators by allowing two of the five integrations to be performed analytically. The configuration mixing degrees of freedom and the HFB transformation are determined self-consistently through variational equations including a generalized Brillouin theorem.

## Key numbers

- For 20Ne and 22Ne, calculations were performed in the 1s0d shell model space, allowing comparison with complete shell model diagonalizations.
- The angular momentum projection operator involves an integral over the Euler angle ϑ from 0 to π with factor (2I+1)/2 and the d-function d^I_{00}(ϑ).
- The number projection operator involves an integral over φ from -π to π with factor 1/(2π)^2.
- The isospin projection operator involves an integral over χ from 0 to π with factor 1/(2π)^2.

## Context

This work extends the VAMPIR and MONSTER approaches previously developed by Schmid et al. (1984, 1987, 1989) and Petrovici et al. (1991), which had been limited to real HFB transformations with axial symmetry and time-reversal invariance but without parity or proton-neutron mixing. The new complex VAMPIR transformations, which include parity and proton-neutron mixing while keeping axial symmetry and time-reversal invariance, were introduced by Schmid et al. (1987) and Zheng et al. (1989); the present paper applies MONSTER calculations on top of such complex VAMPIR solutions for the first time. This enables improved description of states with arbitrary spin and parity in even-even and doubly-odd nuclei, and enables future applications to larger model spaces and heavier nuclei beyond the 1s0d shell.
