# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: 4-gluon vertices

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. In this paper, we continue study of the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap (which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD), avoiding soft-gluon approximations. In particular, this paper completes the calculation of the rate for real double gluon bremsstrahlung from an initial gluon with various simplifying assumptions (thick media; $\hat q$ approximation; and large $N_c$) by now including processes involving 4-gluon vertices.

## Key claim

The paper completes the calculation of the double gluon bremsstrahlung rate from an initial gluon including processes with 4-gluon vertices, but the authors find that these new contributions have only a small effect on the total rate compared to the previously calculated contributions from 3-gluon vertices alone.

## Method

The authors evaluate interference diagrams involving 4-gluon vertices (figures 4subset2 and 44subset2) using the same formalism developed in prior work (Arnold and Iqbal 2015; Arnold, Chang, and Iqbal 2016) that reduces the problem to effective two-dimensional quantum mechanics. They work under the simplifying assumptions of a static, uniform, infinite medium; the large-Nc limit of QCD; and the multiple-scattering (harmonic oscillator or q-hat) approximation. The 4-gluon vertex matrix elements are derived and then combined with previously computed 3-gluon vertex contributions to obtain the total correction to the double bremsstrahlung rate due to overlapping formation times.

## Key numbers

* At the apex (x=y=1/3) of the triangular region, the total correction is pi^2 x y^{3/2} Delta dGamma/dx dy = 1.12 CA^2 alphas^2 sqrt(qhat_A/E).
* The contribution from diagrams with a single 4-gluon vertex at the apex is pi^2 x y^{3/2} [dGamma/dx dy]_(4) = 0.00012 CA^2 alphas^2 sqrt(qhat_A/E), which is numerically negligible compared to the total.
* The contribution from diagrams with two 4-gluon vertices at the apex is pi^2 x y^{3/2} [dGamma/dx dy]_(44) = 0.072 CA^2 alphas^2 sqrt(qhat_A/E).
* The 4-gluon vertex contributions scale as 1/y^{1/2} in the limit y << x << 1, while the crossed and sequential diagrams scale as 1/x y^{3/2} in the same limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of double bremsstrahlung with overlapping formation times (Arnold and Iqbal 2015; Arnold, Chang, and Iqbal 2016) by including the previously missing 4-gluon vertex contributions. The problem of overlapping formation times was analyzed at leading logarithm order in the limit y << x << 1 by Blaizot, Iancu, and collaborators (2013-2014) in the context of parton energy loss in QCD media. The complete calculation enables future work on the related virtual corrections to single-bremsstrahlung rates and their connection to anomalous scaling of the effective medium parameter q-hat with energy.
