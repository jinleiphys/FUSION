# Equation of state and composition of proto-neutron stars and merger remnants with hyperons

> Finite-temperature equation of state (EoS) and the composition of dense nuclear and hypernuclear matter under conditions characteristic of neutron star binary merger remnants and supernovas are discussed. We consider both neutrino free-streaming and trapped regimes which are separated by a temperature of a few MeV. The formalism is based on covariant density functional (CDF) theory for the full baryon octet with density-dependent couplings, suitably adjusted in the hypernuclear sector. The softening of the EoS with the introduction of the hyperons is quantified under various conditions of lepton fractions and temperatures. We find that $Λ$, $Ξ^-$, and $Ξ^0$ hyperons appear in the given order with a sharp density increase at zero temperature at the threshold being replaced by an extended increment over a wide density range at high temperatures. The $Λ$ hyperon survives in the deep subnuclear regime. The triplet of $Σ$s is suppressed in cold hypernuclear matter up to around seven times the nuclear saturation density, but appears in significant fractions at higher temperatures, $T\geq 20$ MeV, in both supernova and merger remnant matter. We point out that a special isospin degeneracy point exists where the baryon abundances within each of the three isospin multiplets are equal to each other as a result of (approximate) isospin symmetry. At that point, the charge chemical potential of the system vanishes. We find that under the merger remnant conditions, the fractions of electron and $μ$-on neutrinos are close and are about 1\%, whereas in the supernova case, we only find a significant fraction ($\sim$10\%) of electron neutrinos, given that in this case, the $μ$-on lepton number is zero.

## Key claim
The paper quantifies the softening of the equation of state due to hyperon appearance in proto-neutron stars and merger remnants, but this softening is strongly moderated by temperature: at high temperatures (T >= 20 MeV) the sharp threshold densities for hyperon appearance at zero temperature become broadened over a wide density range, and the triplet of Sigma hyperons, suppressed in cold matter up to about seven times nuclear saturation density, appears in significant fractions.

## Method
The authors employ covariant density functional (CDF) theory for the full baryon octet with density-dependent couplings, adjusted in the hypernuclear sector, to compute the finite-temperature equation of state and composition of dense nuclear and hypernuclear matter. They consider both neutrino free-streaming and trapped regimes, separated by a temperature of a few MeV, under conditions characteristic of neutron star binary merger remnants and supernovas. The formalism solves for the equilibrium composition including all baryon species and leptons with specified lepton fractions.

## Key numbers
- The transition between neutrino free-streaming and trapped regimes occurs at a temperature of a few MeV.
- The triplet of Sigma hyperons is suppressed in cold hypernuclear matter up to around seven times the nuclear saturation density.
- Sigma hyperons appear in significant fractions at temperatures T >= 20 MeV in both supernova and merger remnant matter.
- Under merger remnant conditions, the fractions of electron and muon neutrinos are close and are about 1%.
- In the supernova case, the electron neutrino fraction is about 10%, while the muon lepton number is zero.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hypernuclear matter in compact objects (e.g., earlier work by Sedrakian and collaborators) by systematically including finite-temperature effects and both neutrino regimes relevant to supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. The results enable more realistic simulations of proto-neutron star cooling and merger remnant evolution by providing the equation of state and composition needed as input for hydrodynamical models, and they identify an isospin degeneracy point where the charge chemical potential vanishes, which may have implications for the transport properties of the matter.
