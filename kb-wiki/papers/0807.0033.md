# Electromagnetic Baryon Form Factors from Holographic QCD

> In the holographic model of QCD suggested by Sakai and Sugimoto, baryons are chiral solitons sourced by D4 instantons in bulk of size 1/\sqrtλ with λ=g^2N_c. We quantize the D4 instanton semiclassically using \hbar=1/(N_cλ) and non-rigid constraints on the vector mesons. The holographic baryon is a small chiral bag in the holographic direction with a Cheshire cat smile. The vector-baryon interactions occur at the core boundary of the instanton in D4. They are strong and of order 1/\sqrt{\hbar}. To order \hbar^0 the electromagnetic current is entirely encoded on the core boundary and vector-meson dominated. To this order, the electromagnetic charge radius is of order λ^0. The meson contribution to the baryon magnetic moments sums identically to the core contribution. The proton and neutron magnetic moment are tied by a model independent relation similar to the one observed in the Skyrme model.

## Key claim

In the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, baryons are chiral solitons sourced by D4 instantons in bulk; to leading order in the semiclassical expansion (order $\hbar^0$ with $\hbar = 1/(N_c \lambda)$), the electromagnetic current is entirely encoded on the core boundary of the instanton and is vector-meson dominated, with the electromagnetic charge radius being of order $\lambda^0$ despite the instanton size being of order $1/\sqrt{\lambda}$.

## Method

The authors quantize the D4 instanton semiclassically using $\hbar = 1/(N_c \lambda)$ and non-rigid constraints on the vector mesons, which preserve causality by enforcing constraints only at the instanton location rather than throughout its body. They expand the 5D Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons gauge fields around the instanton configuration using collective coordinates for isorotations, translations, and the size and Z-direction soft modes, then derive the effective action for the $U(1)_V$ source to order $\hbar^0$ by integrating over the fifth dimension $Z$ with mode functions for the vector mesons.

## Key numbers

* The instanton size in bulk is of order $1/\sqrt{\lambda}$ with $\lambda = g^2 N_c$; the explicit expression is $\rho^2 = \frac{1}{8\pi^2 a \lambda} \sqrt{\frac{6}{5}}$, where $a = 1/(216\pi^3)$.
* The semiclassical expansion parameter is $\hbar = 1/(N_c \lambda)$, with $\kappa = \lambda N_c a \equiv \lambda N_c / (216\pi^3)$.
* The vector-baryon interactions at the core boundary are of order $1/\sqrt{\hbar}$.
* The electromagnetic charge radius is of order $\lambda^0$ (i.e., independent of $\lambda$ at leading order).

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2007) to baryon electromagnetic form factors, building on earlier holographic baryon constructions by Brodsky and collaborators and by Rho and collaborators. The analysis clarifies and systematizes a recent analysis by Hata and collaborators, and provides a holographic realization of the Cheshire cat principle where the core boundary $Z_c$ plays the role of the Cheshire cat smile. The results enable systematic semiclassical calculations of baryon properties in holographic QCD, with the proton and neutron magnetic moments tied by a model-independent relation similar to that observed in the Skyrme model.
