# Time-varying coupling strengths, nuclear forces and unification

> We investigate the dependence of the nucleon-nucleon force in the deuteron system on the values of coupling strengths at high energy, which will in general depend on the geometry of extra dimensions. The stability of deuterium at all times after nucleosynthesis sets a bound on the time variation of the ratio of the QCD confinement scale to light quark masses. We discuss the relation between this ratio, which is exponentially sensitive to high-energy couplings, and fundamental parameters, in various classes of unified theory. The binding energy of the deuteron is also expected to have an important effect on nucleosynthesis and we quantify this effect.

## Key claim
The deuteron stability after nucleosynthesis constrains the time variation of the ratio of the QCD confinement scale to light quark masses, but the resulting bounds on high-energy coupling variations are highly model-dependent because unknown effects in the electroweak, SUSY, and Yukawa sectors may dominate over the strong dependence of the QCD scale.

## Method
The authors calculate the deuteron binding energy using a meson-exchange model where the pion force dominates, expressing the relevant parameters as functions of the QCD scale Λ and the light quark masses m_u + m_d. They relate Λ and quark masses to high-energy coupling strengths using renormalization group evolution, considering both supersymmetric GUT models and nonsupersymmetric low-scale models. The stability criterion for the deuteron is derived from a square-well approximation, while similar calculations are performed for the dineutron and diproton systems using a Yukawa potential with a trial wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron becomes unstable when the parameter c ≡ √(m̂_q/Λ) decreases by 13% relative to its current value (c_unstable/c_0 = 0.77)
- The dineutron becomes bound when c increases by a factor of 2.6 relative to its current value (c/c_0 ≥ 2.6)
- The diproton becomes bound when c increases by a factor of 3.2 relative to its current value (c/c_0 ≥ 3.2)
- The current value of the dimensionless coupling f²/4π is 7.95 × 10⁻², derived from present-day measurements
- The deuteron binding energy is currently 2.226 MeV, with a square-well depth of V_sq = -66.15 MeV assuming a width a = m_π⁻¹
- The unification scale is M_U ≈ 10¹⁶ GeV with unified coupling α_U⁻¹ ≈ 25, derived from observed values of sin²θ_W, α₃(M_Z), and α(M_Z)

## Context
This work extends the approach of Langacker et al. (2001) and Calmet and Fritszch (2002) by including the full dependence on thresholds in the running of couplings and by quantifying the effect of deuteron binding energy changes on nucleosynthesis. The authors note that previous calculations by Sis1 assumed static quantities were directly proportional to Λ in the chiral limit, while Beane and Savage (2002) later argued that the dependence on the quark mass parameter could be erased by four-nucleon operator coefficients. The paper enables bounds on cosmological evolution of extra-dimensional models since nucleosynthesis, complementing constraints from light element abundances.
