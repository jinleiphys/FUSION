# Classification of resonances and pairing effects on $NA$-scattering within the HFB framework

> We analyze the properties of the scattering solutions obtained as the pole of the S- and K-matrix with the help of the Jost function framework and the Strum-Liouville theory within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov(HFB) framework, and clarify the scattering solutions which can be defined as the physical state. We found that there are three types of the resonances; "{\it shape resonance}", "{\it particle-type}" and "{\it hole-type quasiparticle resonances}", and another two types of solutions are given as the independent S-matrix and K-matrix poles. The shape resonance is formed by the Hartree-Fock(HF) mean field potential, is not affected by the pairing correlation so much. The particle-type and hole-type quasiparticle resonances originate from the particle and hole states by the configuration mixing effect by pairing. All of resonance are represented by the S-matrix pole which has the corresponding K-matrix pole. Two other types of solutions are given by the independent S-matrix and K-matrix poles. These poles are formed by the HF mean field potential. The effect of pairing for the independent S-matrix pole is small, but the one for the independent K-matrix pole has the remarkable effect. The independent K-matrix pole destroys the quasiparticle resonance as it approaches to the resonance by the pairing effect. The wave function of all resonances have the characteristic structure of the metastable property. However, the metastable structure of the wave function of the quasiparticle resonance can be broken by the independent standing wave solution or the Fano effect.

## Key claim

The paper classifies scattering solutions within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework into three types of resonances (shape resonance, particle-type and hole-type quasiparticle resonances) and two additional types of solutions (independent S-matrix and K-matrix poles), clarifying that a resonance requires both an S-matrix pole and a corresponding K-matrix pole nearby, while the independent poles lack this correspondence.

## Method

The authors analyzed the properties of S-matrix and K-matrix poles using the Jost function formalism and Sturm-Liouville theory within the HFB framework, extending the single-channel Jost function to a two-channel formalism that accounts for pairing correlations. They computed the S-matrix, T-matrix, and K-matrix from the HFB Jost function determinant, and studied the pairing dependence of each pole type by varying the mean pairing gap from 0 to 10 MeV for both stable (chemical potential lambda = -8.0 MeV) and unstable (lambda = -1.0 MeV) target nuclei. The wave functions of the scattering solutions were also examined to characterize their metastable properties.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB Jost function formalism (Mizuyama et al., 2020) and their analysis of the Fano effect in quasiparticle resonances (Mizuyama et al., 2020), providing a complete classification of scattering solutions that goes beyond the earlier particle-type and hole-type classification. The paper builds on the Sturm-Liouville theory of resonances (Sasakawa, 1970s) and the R-matrix theory (Lane and Thomas, 1958) by clarifying the distinct roles of S-matrix and K-matrix poles. This classification enables future studies of how pairing correlations affect resonance structures in neutron elastic scattering off open-shell nuclei, particularly for understanding quasiparticle resonances that may appear as sharp peaks in cross sections.
