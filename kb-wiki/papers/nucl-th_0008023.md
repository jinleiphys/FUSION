# Nucleon Resonances in Meson Nucleon Scattering with Strangeness Production

> An effective Lagrangian model in a coupled channels framework is applied to extract nucleon resonance parameters. In the K-matrix approximation, we simultaneously analyze all the available data for the transitions from pi N to five possible meson-baryon final states, pi N, pipi N, eta N, K Lambda, and KSigma, in the energy range from pi N threshold up to W = 2 GeV. In this work, we focus our efforts on the K Sigma channel. In particular, we include a set of Delta resonances around 1900 MeV: the S31(1900), P31(1910), and P33(1920) states. In most cases the parameters of nucleon resonances determined by the fits are consistent with the commonly accepted values in the literature. We find that in the low-energy region, the S11(1650), P11(1710), and P13(1720) states are important for the p(pi-,K0)Sigma0 and p(pi-,K+)Sigma- processes while the P33(1600) and D33(1700) states are significant for the pure I=3/2 reaction p(pi+,K+)Sigma+. In the high energy region, we find a D13 state at 1945 MeV which is important for the p(pi-,K0)Sigma0 reaction while the Delta resonances around 1900 MeV show up significantly in the p(pi-,K+)Sigma- and p(pi+,K+)Sigma+ processes.

# Nucleon Resonances in Meson Nucleon Scattering with Strangeness Production

## Key claim

The paper claims that the structure of differential cross sections for $\pi N \rightarrow K\Sigma$ reactions is dominated purely by resonance contributions, but this conclusion is qualified by the finding that in the high-energy region of the pure I=3/2 reaction $p(\pi^+,K^+)\Sigma^+$, the model cannot reproduce the measured differential cross section structure, suggesting missing high-spin resonances or background mechanisms.

## Method

An effective Lagrangian model in a coupled channels framework with a K-matrix approximation was applied to simultaneously analyze data for five meson-baryon final states ($\pi N$, $\pi\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$) from $\pi N$ threshold up to center-of-mass energy $W = 2$ GeV. The model included Born terms, $t$-channel exchanges via $\rho$, $a_0$, and $K^\ast$ mesons, and resonance contributions in the $s$- and $u$-channels, with all $2\pi$ final states parametrized through coupling to a scalar, isovector $\zeta$-meson of mass $m_\zeta = 2m_\pi$. The fit focused on the $K\Sigma$ channel and included three $\Delta$ resonances around 1900 MeV: $S_{31}(1900)$, $P_{31}(1910)$, and $P_{33}(1920)$.

## Key numbers

- The $D_{13}$ state at 1945 MeV has a total width of 583 MeV, with partial widths: $\Gamma_{\pi N} = 108$ MeV (19%), $\Gamma_{\zeta N} = 439$ MeV (75%), $\Gamma_{\eta N} = 28$ MeV (5%), $\Gamma_{K\Lambda} = 8$ MeV (1%), $\Gamma_{K\Sigma} = 0.08$ MeV (0%)
- The $P_{11}(1710)$ resonance has mass 1.716 GeV, total width 235 MeV, and partial width into $K\Sigma$ of 32 MeV (14%)
- The $P_{13}(1720)$ resonance has mass 1.736 GeV, total width 137 MeV, and partial width into $K\Sigma$ of 7 MeV (5%)
- The $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance has mass 1.689 GeV, total width 341 MeV, and coupling constant to $K\Sigma$ of 0.98
- The $P_{33}(1600)$ resonance has mass 1.677 GeV, total width 296 MeV; the $D_{33}(1700)$ has mass 1.672 GeV, total width 583 MeV
- The $S_{31}(1900)$ has mass 1.938 GeV, total width 334 MeV, $\Gamma_{K\Sigma} = 47$ MeV (14%)
- The $P_{31}(1910)$ has mass 1.967 GeV, total width 605 MeV, $\Gamma_{K\Sigma} = 34$ MeV (6%)
- The $P_{33}(1920)$ has mass 2.077 GeV, total width 689 MeV, $\Gamma_{K\Sigma} = 0.60$ MeV (0%)
- The $g_{K N \Lambda}$ coupling constant is -13.98; $g_{K N \Sigma}$ is 1.41

## Context

This work extends the model of Feuster and Mosel (1998) by including the previously neglected $K\Sigma$ final state and extending the energy range to $\sqrt{s} \le 2.0$ GeV, thereby including additional resonances between 1.9 and 2.0 GeV. The $g_{K\Lambda N}$ and $g_{K\Sigma N}$ couplings are found to be in accordance with approximate SU(3), in contrast to Feuster and Mosel (1998) which found a much smaller $g_{K\Lambda N}$ coupling constant. The role of the $P_{11}(1710)$ is consistent with recent studies of kaon photoproduction by Bennhold and collaborators (1999) and of $K\Sigma$ production in $NN$-scattering by Sibirtsev and collaborators (1998). The next step identified is to study the photoproduction reaction $\gamma N \rightarrow K\Sigma$ along with the hadronic production reactions discussed here.
