# Possible evidence of extended objects inside the proton

> Recent experimental determinations of the Nachtmann moments of the inelastic structure function of the proton F2p(x, Q**2), obtained at Jefferson Lab, are analyzed for values of the squared four-momentum transfer Q**2 ranging from ~ 0.1 to ~ 2 (GeV/c)**2. It is shown that such inelastic proton data exhibit a new type of scaling behavior and that the resulting scaling function can be interpreted as a constituent form factor consistent with the elastic nucleon data. These findings suggest that at low momentum transfer the inclusive proton structure function originates mainly from the elastic coupling with extended objects inside the proton. We obtain a constituent size of ~ 0.2 - 0.3 fm.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inelastic proton structure function data from Jefferson Lab, for \(Q^2\) between about 0.1 and 2 \((GeV/c)^2\), exhibit a new scaling behavior that can be interpreted as a constituent quark form factor, yielding a constituent size of about 0.2–0.3 fm. The hedge is that this interpretation holds only in a dual sense (averaging over resonance bumps) and for low moments (\(n>2\)), and that the scaling deteriorates at the lowest \(Q^2\) values due to unaccounted long-range higher-twist effects.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental Nachtmann moments of the proton inelastic structure function \(F_2^p(x, Q^2)\) for \(Q^2\) from \(\approx 0.1\) to \(\approx 2\) \((GeV/c)^2\), extracted from CLAS data at Jefferson Lab. They extended a two-stage model in which the proton structure function is a convolution of a constituent quark (CQ) light-front momentum distribution and a CQ structure function, including an elastic CQ contribution at low \(Q^2\). They calculated theoretical moments using a Gaussian ansatz for the proton wave function (with parameter \(\beta = 0.3\) GeV and CQ mass \(m = 0.25\) GeV) and applied target-mass corrections plus a phase-space threshold factor to account for finite-\(Q^2\) effects, then formed the ratio \(R_n^p(Q^2) = M_n^p(Q^2) / \overline{M}_n^p(Q^2)\) to test scaling.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark size extracted from the scaling function: \(r_Q = 0.21\) fm for \(\beta/m = 1.2\) (with \(\beta = 0.3\) GeV, \(m = 0.25\) GeV).
* Constituent quark size range when \(\beta/m\) varies from 1.2 to 1.8: \(r_Q\) changes from 0.21 fm to 0.27 fm.
* \(Q^2\) range of the analysis: \(0.1 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 2\) \((GeV/c)^2\).
* The scaling property holds for \(n > 2\) and for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.3\) \((GeV/c)^2\); at \(Q^2 \approx 0.1\) \((GeV/c)^2\) the scaling is satisfied within approximately 30%.
* The squared monopole form factor used to fit the scaling function: \([F(Q^2)]^2 = 1 / (1 + r_Q^2 Q^2 / 6)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the two-stage model of Altarelli et al. (1970s) and the earlier application by Petronzio, Simula, and Ricco (APR) to predict pion structure functions, now generalizing it to low \(Q^2\) (below the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV). It builds on the Bloom-Gilman duality concept (1970) and uses the Nachtmann moment formalism to handle target-mass corrections. The results enable a consistent interpretation of both inelastic proton data and elastic nucleon form factor data within a constituent quark picture, and they suggest that future work should account for long-range higher-twist effects (correlations among different CQs) to improve the scaling at the very lowest \(Q^2\).
