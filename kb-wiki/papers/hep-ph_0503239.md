# Production of multiply heavy flavoured baryons from Quark Gluon Plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> It is argued that in heavy ion collisions at LHC there could be a sizeable production of baryons containing two or three heavy quarks from statistical coalescence. This production mechanism is peculiar of Quark Gluon Plasma and the predicted rates, in heavy ion collisions at LHC energy, exceed those from a purely hadronic scenario, particularly for Xi_bc and Omega_ccc. Thus, besides the interest in the discovery of these new states, enhanced ratios of these baryons over singly heavy flavoured hadrons, like B or D, in heavy ion collisions with respect to pp at the same energy, would be a clear indication of kinetic equilibration of heavy quarks in the Quark Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in heavy ion collisions at LHC, statistical coalescence of uncorrelated heavy quarks from the Quark Gluon Plasma could produce multiply heavy flavoured baryons at rates exceeding those from purely hadronic scenarios, provided the heavy quarks have reached kinetic (not chemical) equilibrium within the plasma.

## Method

The author calculates mean multiplicities of heavy flavoured hadrons using the Statistical Coalescence Model (SCM), which is the statistical hadronization model supplemented with fixed numbers of charm and beauty quarks and antiquarks. The partition function is factorized for charm and beauty sectors, and an approximate analytic formula is derived for primary average multiplicities under conditions where the ratios of doubly- to singly-heavy hadron partition function terms are small. The final formula is applied to central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (5.5 TeV) using extrapolated volumes and heavy quark cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (Pb-Pb, 5.5 TeV, 5.1% centrality): average multiplicity of ccbar pairs ranges from 95 to 256; for bbbar pairs from 2.4 to 7.2.
- At RHIC (Au-Au, 200 GeV, 5.5% centrality): average multiplicity of ccbar pairs ranges from 2.8 to 17; for bbbar pairs from 0.03 to 0.07.
- Volume at LHC: approximately 4.5 x 10^4 fm^3; at RHIC: approximately 10^4 fm^3.
- Predicted primary yields for doubly charmed baryons (Xi_cc, Omega_cc) at LHC: between 0.019 and 0.38 per central event.
- Predicted primary yields for Omega_ccc at LHC: between 10^-3 and 0.03 per central event.
- Predicted primary yields for mixed charmed-beautiful hadrons (Xi_bc, Omega_bc, B_c) at LHC: between 3 x 10^-4 and 0.022 per central event.
- Ratio of Omega_ccc to charm quark multiplicity from coalescence at LHC: (0.1-1) x 10^-4, compared to O(10^-7) from hard scattering in pp at 14 TeV (Iran, year not given).
- Ratio of Xi_bc to bottom quark multiplicity from coalescence at LHC: g_eff (3-9) x 10^-4, compared to ~10^-5 from hard scattering in ppbar at 1.8 TeV (Kiselev, year not given).

## Context

This work extends the statistical coalescence idea previously applied to B_c and J/psi mesons at RHIC (Thews, 2001; Braun-Munzinger, 2002) to multiply heavy flavoured baryons, particularly Xi_bc and Omega_ccc. It improves upon earlier quark recombination models (Levai, 1995; Bielich, 1998) by providing definite predictions without free parameters and by explicitly linking the enhancement to heavy quark kinetic equilibration in the QGP. The predicted enhancement of Xi_bc/B and Omega_ccc/D ratios in heavy ion collisions relative to pp collisions would serve as a clear signal of QGP formation, and the large predicted yields at LHC make heavy ion collisions a suitable environment to discover these yet-unobserved states.
