# Bayesian model selection for electromagnetic kaon production on the nucleon

> We present the results of a Bayesian analysis of a Regge model to describe the background contribution for K+ Lambda and K+ Sigma0 photoproduction. The model is based on the exchange of K+(494) and K*+(892) trajectories in the t-channel. We utilise the Bayesian evidence Z to determine the best model variant for each channel. The Bayesian evidence integrals were calculated using the Nested Sampling algorithm. For different prior widths, we find decisive Bayesian evidence (Δln Z ~ 24) for a K+ Lambda photoproduction Regge model with a positive vector coupling and a negative tensor coupling constant for the K*+(892) trajectory, and a rotating phase factor for both trajectories. Using the chi^2 minimisation method, one could not draw this conclusion from the same dataset. For the K+ Sigma0 photoproduction Regge model, on the other hand, the difference between the evidence integrals is insufficient to pinpoint one model variant.

## Key claim

Bayesian evidence computed via Nested Sampling can resolve sign and phase ambiguities in the Regge-model background for K+Λ photoproduction that the χ² minimisation method could not distinguish using the same high-energy dataset; for K+Σ0 photoproduction, the evidence differences are too small to single out one model variant.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian model selection analysis of a Regge model for K+Λ and K+Σ0 photoproduction, where the background is described by t-channel exchange of K+(494) and K*+(892) trajectories. They computed the Bayesian evidence Z for twelve model variants arising from three choices of phase factors (rotating/rotating, rotating/constant, constant/rotating) and two possible signs for the vector and tensor coupling constants of the K*+ trajectory, using the Nested Sampling algorithm to evaluate the multidimensional evidence integrals. The models were constrained by high-energy photoproduction data: 72 data points for K+Λ (differential cross sections, photon asymmetries, recoil asymmetries) and 57 data points for K+Σ0 (differential cross sections and photon asymmetries), with uniform priors on the coupling constants and SU(3)-inspired priors on the K+Yp couplings.

## Key numbers

- For K+Λ photoproduction, the best model (positive vector coupling, negative tensor coupling, rotating phase for both trajectories) has Δln Z ≈ 24 relative to the second-best model, exceeding the Jeffreys scale threshold of 5 for "decisive" evidence.
- The best-model parameter estimates for K+Λ are: g_{K+Λp}/√(4π) = -3.22 ± 0.04, G_{K*+}^v = 12.47 ± 0.14, G_{K*+}^t = -32.19 ± 0.50.
- For K+Σ0 photoproduction, the evidence differences between model variants are all below |Δln Z| ≈ 2, with the best model (negative vector, negative tensor, rotating/constant phases) having Δln Z = 0 and the next-best (positive vector, negative tensor, rotating/constant) at Δln Z = -0.50 ± 0.42.
- The high-energy K+Λ dataset comprised 72 data points: 56 differential cross sections, 9 photon asymmetries, and 7 recoil asymmetries.
- The high-energy K+Σ0 dataset comprised 57 data points: 48 differential cross sections and 9 photon asymmetries.

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) approach of Corthals et al. (2006, 2007a, 2007b) by applying Bayesian model selection to resolve ambiguities that the traditional χ² method could not settle from high-energy data alone. The Bayesian evidence method, previously demonstrated in hadronic physics by Ireland et al. (2008) for pentaquark data and by Schindler et al. (2008) for effective field theories, enables the authors to identify the correct K+Λ background model without requiring additional resonance-region data. For K+Σ0, the ambiguity remains and requires supplementary data such as the neutron-target reaction n(γ,K+)Σ− analysed by Vancraeyveld et al. (2009). The results enable more reliable extraction of resonance contributions in the RPR framework, addressing the missing-resonance problem in nucleon spectroscopy.
