# Gluon radiation from a classical point particle: Recoil effects

> We propose a formula for gluon radiation in the fragmentation region. By comparing tree-level bremsstrahlung of a spin-less quark to the known result for gluon radiation from a classical particle struck by a sheet of colored glass arXiv:1903.01381, arXiv:1911.12738 we modify the classical formula to take into account the recoil of the struck particle. The new formula produces the correct perturbative behaviour at high momentum of the radiated gluon.

## Key claim

By comparing tree-level bremsstrahlung of a spin-less quark to the classical result for gluon radiation from a particle struck by a sheet of colored glass, the authors propose an interpolating formula that modifies the classical result to include recoil effects, producing the correct perturbative behavior at high gluon momentum. The proposed ansatz reduces to the classical result in the no-recoil limit (ξ ≪ 1) and falls off as ~1/k_T^4 at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors first compute tree-level gluon bremsstrahlung in scalar QCD with classical (commuting) color charge, removing quark spin and treating color classically to match the classical point particle studied by Kajantie et al. (2019). They then compare this perturbative scalar result to the classical result of Kajantie et al. (2019) and modify the classical formula by inserting a factor of 1/(1+ξ) and a factor of (1+ξ) in the bremsstrahlung term, where ξ = k^-/p'^- measures quark recoil. The resulting ansatz replaces the classical bremsstrahlung amplitude with a form that matches the scalar QCD result at lowest order in the background field.

## Key numbers

- The classical result falls off as ~1/k_T^2 at high transverse momentum, while the corrected ansatz falls off as ~1/k_T^4
- The ansatz contains a factor of 1/(1+ξ) multiplying the entire expression, where ξ = k^-/p'^- is the ratio of radiated gluon momentum to final quark momentum
- In the no-recoil limit ξ ≪ 1, the ansatz reduces exactly to the classical result of Kajantie et al. (2019)
- The bremsstrahlung contribution in the ansatz scales as ~1/k_T^4 for k_T ≫ x q_T, where x = k^-/p^-

## Context

This work extends the classical gluon radiation calculation by Kajantie et al. (2019) for a particle struck by a sheet of colored glass, which was derived in the no-recoil approximation and showed incorrect ~1/k_T^2 behavior at high momentum. The authors build on the perturbative QCD calculation by Lushozi (2019) to construct an interpolating formula that recovers the correct perturbative limit while preserving the non-perturbative physics of the Color Glass Condensate framework. The result enables phenomenological studies of energy and baryon density distributions in the fragmentation region of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding baryon stopping power in terms of the saturation scale Q_sat.
