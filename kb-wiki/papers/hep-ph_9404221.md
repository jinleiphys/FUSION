# H dibaryon in the QCD sum rule

> The QCD sum rule is applied to the H dibaryon and is compared to the flavor non-singlet di-nucleon. We find that the H dibaryon is almost degenerate to the di-nucleon in the $SU(3)_{flavor}$ limit and therefore is not deeply bound as far as th\ e threshold parameter is adjusted not to have a di-nucleon bound state. After introducing the $SU(3)_{f}$ breaking effects, the H dibaryon is found to be bound by $40 MeV$ below the $ΛΛ$ threshold.

## Key claim

The H dibaryon is found to be bound by 40 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold only after introducing SU(3) flavor breaking effects; in the SU(3) flavor limit, the H dibaryon is almost degenerate with the di-nucleon (within about 2 MeV) and therefore not deeply bound, provided the threshold parameter is adjusted to avoid a di-nucleon bound state.

## Method

The authors apply the QCD sum rule to the H dibaryon (a six-quark flavor-singlet state with I=0, J=0, strangeness -2) by constructing an interpolating current as a product of two baryonic currents. They compute the operator product expansion (OPE) for the current correlation function, keeping only the quark condensate terms up to ⟨q̅q⟩⁴ under the vacuum saturation assumption, and apply the Borel sum rule to extract the mass. The threshold parameter s₀ is fixed by requiring the di-nucleon (flavor non-singlet six-quark state) mass to equal twice the nucleon mass, rather than by Borel stability which is not achieved. SU(3) flavor breaking is introduced via the strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV and the ratio ⟨s̅s⟩/⟨u̅u⟩ = 0.8.

## Key numbers

- In the SU(3) flavor limit with s₀ = 5.694 GeV², the di-nucleon mass is 1.880 GeV (twice the nucleon mass) and the H dibaryon mass is 1.878 GeV, giving m_di-nucleon - m_H ≈ 2 MeV.
- The allowed Borel mass interval is M² = 1.97 GeV² to 2.54 GeV² in the SU(3) flavor limit, with the central point at M² = 2.26 GeV².
- With SU(3) breaking (m_s = 150 MeV, ⟨s̅s⟩/⟨u̅u⟩ = 0.8), the H dibaryon mass is estimated at m_H ≈ 2.19 GeV, which is about 40 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold of 2.23 GeV.
- The Borel mass interval for the SU(3) breaking case is M² = 2 - 3.4 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach, previously applied to single baryons by Ioffe and others, to the six-quark H dibaryon predicted by Jaffe (1977) in the MIT bag model with about 80 MeV binding. The result contrasts with the SU(6) quark model with gluon exchange, which predicts a distinct flavor-singlet bound state with binding energy of order 100 MeV in the SU(3) flavor limit. The paper also notes that instanton-induced interactions, studied by other authors, may reduce the H dibaryon binding, and that experimental upper limits from double hypernuclei give E_B ≤ 28 MeV. The present work enables further QCD sum rule studies of multi-quark states and suggests that the H dibaryon binding is weaker than quark model predictions.
