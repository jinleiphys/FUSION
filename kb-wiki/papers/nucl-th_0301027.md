# Dynamic study on fusion reactions for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr around Coulomb barrier

> By using the updated improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model in which a surface-symmetry potential term has been introduced for the first time, the excitation functions for fusion reactions of $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr at energies around the Coulomb barrier have been studied. The experimental data of the fusion cross sections for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr have been reproduced remarkably well without introducing any new parameters. The fusion cross sections for the neutron-rich fusion reactions of $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr around the Coulomb barrier are predicted to be enhanced compared with a non-neutron-rich fusion reaction. In order to clarify the mechanism of the enhancement of the fusion cross sections for neutron-rich nuclear fusions, we pay a great attention to study the dynamic lowering of the Coulomb barrier during a neck formation. The isospin effect on the barrier lowering is investigated. It is interesting that the effect of the projectile and target nuclear structure on fusion dynamics can be revealed to a certain extent in our approach. The time evolution of the N/Z ratio at the neck region has been firstly illustrated. A large enhancement of the N/Z ratio at neck region for neutron-rich nuclear fusion reactions is found.

# Dynamic study on fusion reactions for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr around Coulomb barrier

## Key claim

The improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model, with a newly introduced surface-symmetry potential term, reproduces experimental fusion cross sections for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr without new parameters and predicts enhanced fusion cross sections for neutron-rich $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr reactions, but the enhancement for $^{48}$Ca-induced reactions relative to $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr is less strong than the enhancement of $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr relative to $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr, because the isospin effect and the nuclear structure effect (octupole vibration rigidity of $^{48}$Ca) act in opposition.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD model, in which each nucleon is represented by a Gaussian wave packet and the time evolution is governed by Hamiltonian equations of motion with a Skyrme-type effective interaction. A surface-symmetry potential term was added to the volume symmetry term based on the finite-range Liquid-Drop Model, and a system-size-dependent wave packet width was adopted. Fusion cross sections were calculated by counting fusion events (defined as events with 6 or fewer escaped nucleons) over 100 bombarding events per energy and impact parameter, and the dynamic Coulomb barrier was computed microscopically from the time-dependent density distributions of projectile and target.

## Key numbers

- Fusion cross sections for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr were reproduced "remarkably well" without introducing new parameters (experimental data from Timmers et al. (1998)).
- At 5 MeV below the static Coulomb barrier, the maximum impact parameter leading to fusion was about 9 fm for $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr, about 8.5 fm for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr, and only 6.5 fm for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr.
- The elongation at touching configuration for the lower energy case (5 MeV below barrier) was about 10% larger than for the higher energy case (10 MeV above barrier).
- At the early stage of neck formation, the N/Z ratio at the neck region for $^{48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr reached a value of twice the average N/Z ratio of the whole system.
- After about 100 fm/c, the N/Z ratio at the neck region gradually approached the average N/Z ratio of the whole system.

## Context

This work extends the ImQMD model previously developed by Wang, Li, and Wu (2002) to study neutron-rich fusion reactions, motivated by the synthesis of superheavy elements near Z=114 or 120 and N=184. The study builds on earlier work showing that neck formation and dynamical deformation lower the fusion barrier (Kondratyev, Bonasera, and Iwamoto (2000) for symmetric oxygen and nickel isotopes; Beckerman (1985); Vandenbosch (1992); Reisdorf (1994); Balantekin and Takigawa (1998)). The paper enables future experimental measurements of fusion cross sections and barrier distributions for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr to disentangle the interplay between isospin effects and nuclear structure effects (specifically octupole vibration properties of $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{96}$Zr as discussed by Trotta et al. (2001)).
