# Circumstantial evidence for a soft nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities

> Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model it is shown that the recent FOPI data on the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in central heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies (Willy Reisdorf {\it et al.}, NPA {\bf 781}, 459 (2007)) provide circumstantial evidence suggesting a rather soft nuclear symmetry energy \esym at $ρ\geq 2ρ_0$ compared to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall prediction. Some astrophysical implications and the need for further experimental confirmations are discussed.

# Circumstantial evidence for a soft nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities

## Key claim
The FOPI data on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies provide circumstantial evidence that the nuclear symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) is rather soft at densities ρ ≥ 2ρ₀ compared to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) prediction, based on transport model calculations that do not perfectly reproduce the data even with the softest symmetry energy tested (x=1).

## Method
The authors used the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model, which incorporates a mean-field potential with a variable parameter x that mimics different forms of the symmetry energy predicted by various many-body theories. They simulated central heavy-ion collisions for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au reactions at beam energies from 0.25 to 1.2 AGeV, and compared the calculated π⁻/π⁺ ratios with FOPI detector data from Reisdorf et al. (2007).

## Key numbers
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central collisions at 0.4 AGeV for ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au reactions favors the calculation with x=1, which corresponds to a symmetry energy that decreases at supra-saturation densities.
- The maximum central density reached in Au+Au collisions varies from about 2.2ρ₀ to 3.5ρ₀ for beam energies from 0.25 to 1.2 AGeV.
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio shows very weak dependence on the reduced impact parameter b₀ for b₀ ≤ 0.15 in both data and calculations for the ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru reaction at 0.4 AGeV.
- The pion multiplicity per participant M_π/A_part calculated with IBUU04 is in reasonably good agreement with FOPI data over the energy range studied.

## Context
This work extends the effort to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, where theoretical predictions are extremely diverse: many microscopic approaches (Relativistic Mean Field, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) predict the symmetry energy increases continuously, while Variational Many-Body theory (Pan 1972, Friedman 1981, Wiringa 1988), Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Krastev 2006), and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (Brown 2000, Chabanat 1997, Stone 2003) predict it may decrease at high densities. The authors previously used NSCL/MSU isospin diffusion data (Tsang 2004) to limit the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities to between x=0 and x=-1 (Chen 2005, Li 2005). The present results suggest the symmetry energy reaches a maximum between ρ₀ and 2ρ₀ before decreasing at higher densities, which has implications for neutron star cooling, isospin separation instability (Kutschera 1994, Szmaglinski 2006), and possible formation of quark droplets (Kutschera 2000) or hyperons (Bao 2000) in neutron star cores. Further experimental confirmation is needed from TPC measurements at NSCL/MSU (Abi 2007) and RIKEN (Murakami 2008), as well as neutron-proton differential flow measurements at SIS/GSI (Roussel-Chomaz 2008) and future FAIR/GSI.
