# Fission of actinides and superheavy nuclei: covariant density functional theory perspective

> The current status of the application of covariant density functional theory to the description of fission barriers in actinides and superheavy nuclei is reviewed. The achievements and open problems are discussed.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) can describe inner fission barriers of actinides with accuracy comparable to other theoretical frameworks, but the predictions for fission barriers of superheavy nuclei (SHE) vary wildly between models, by up to 6 MeV in some nuclei, despite comparable accuracy in the actinide region, indicating that current parametrizations are insufficient for reliable extrapolation.

## Method

The author reviews systematic triaxial calculations of inner fission barriers in even-even Th, U, Pu, Cm, and Cf nuclei using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS approach with the NL3*, PC-PK1, and DD-PC1 parametrizations. For superheavy nuclei, inner fission barrier heights from CDFT are compared with results from two microscopic+macroscopic (MM) models (Möller et al. 2009 and Kowal et al. 2010). A separate cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) study with Lipkin-Nogami (LN) particle number projection uses a scaled Gogny D1S pairing force to examine pairing evolution with deformation in actinides.

## Key numbers

- The average deviation per inner fission barrier in actinides, defined as δ = Σ|B_f(th) – B_f(exp)|/N, is comparable across CDFT, MM, and Gogny DFT calculations (figure shows δ values of approximately 0.5–0.7 MeV for each framework).
- In superheavy nuclei, the difference in inner fission barrier heights between different models reaches 6 MeV in some nuclei.
- For the CRHB+LN calculations, the pairing strength scaling factor f is 0.899 for the NL3* parametrization and 0.9147 for the NL1 parametrization.
- The CRHB+LN(NL3*) calculations reproduce experimental kinematic moments of inertia of superdeformed rotational bands in 236,238U and 240Pu with deviation not exceeding 3.4%.
- The CRHB+LN(NL1) calculations overestimate experimental moments of inertia in the superdeformed minimum by 11.3% in 240Pu.
- Experimental charge quadrupole moments Q for superdeformed fission isomers: 32±5 eb (236U), 29±3 eb (238U), 37±10 eb (236Pu), 36±4 eb (239Pu), and 35.5±1.0_st±1.2_mod eb (242Am); CRHB+LN(NL3*) gives 33.9 eb (236U), 33.7 eb (238U), 34.8 eb (236Pu), and 34.9 eb (240Pu).

## Context

This review extends the systematic triaxial CDFT calculations of Afanasjev et al. (2010) and the later studies by Long et al. (2012) and Peña et al. (2012) to compare with non-relativistic approaches such as Skyrme DFT (SkM*, UNEDF1 parametrizations) and Gogny DFT (D1S force), as well as the microscopic+macroscopic models of Möller et al. (2009) and Kowal et al. (2010). The work identifies the need for improved pairing force parametrizations to enable reliable predictions for superheavy nuclei, and presents initial CRHB+LN results using a scaled Gogny D1S pairing force that accurately describes both normal-deformed and superdeformed rotational properties in actinides.
