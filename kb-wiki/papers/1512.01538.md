# Moving forward to constrain the shear viscosity of QCD matter

> We demonstrate that measurements of rapidity differential anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions can constrain the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of QCD matter. Comparing results from hydrodynamic calculations with experimental data from RHIC, we find evidence for a small η/s $\approx$ 0.04 in the QCD cross-over region and a strong temperature dependence in the hadronic phase. A temperature independent η/s is disfavored by the data. We further show that measurements of the event-by-event flow as a function of rapidity can be used to independently constrain the initial state fluctuations in three dimensions and the temperature dependent transport properties of QCD matter.

## Key claim

Measurements of rapidity-differential anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions can constrain the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of QCD matter, with evidence that η/s cannot be constant and must have a minimum value around 0.04 in the QCD cross-over region, though this conclusion depends on the assumption that the initial state model is not dramatically different from the one used.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the Music code with a Monte Carlo Glauber-based initial state model that includes three-dimensional fluctuating baryon and entropy densities, extended with longitudinal fluctuations via the Lexus model. They employed a parametrization of the temperature-dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (ηT/(ε+P)) with a minimum at the critical temperature Tc(μB) and linear slopes above and below that minimum, studying four scenarios: constant ηT/(ε+P)=0.12, large hadronic viscosity, large QGP viscosity, and large hadronic with moderate QGP viscosity. The hydrodynamic evolution started at τ0=0.38 fm/c and kinetic freeze-out occurred at an energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm³, using an equation of state interpolated between hadronic resonance gas and lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- Minimum value of η/s ≈ 0.04 at zero baryon chemical potential, almost one half of the lower bound conjectured using the AdS/CFT duality
- Constant ηT/(ε+P)=0.12 scenario is disfavored by PHOBOS data for v2(ηp) in √s=200 GeV Au+Au collisions
- Large QGP viscosity scenario with (ηT/(ε+P))(400 MeV)≈2.4 is excluded by experimental data
- Moderate QGP viscosity scenario with (ηT/(ε+P))(400 MeV)≈0.5 cannot be excluded
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ0=0.38 fm/c
- Quark-quark cross section σqq=9 mb for √s=200 GeV collisions, reproducing nucleon-nucleon cross section of 42 mb
- Parameter λ=0.22 in the Lexus model reproduces experimental net-baryon distribution at √s=200 GeV
- Gaussian smearing width in transverse plane: σT=0.2 fm

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic simulations that assumed a temperature-independent η/s (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) and builds on the approach of Niemi (2015) for extracting temperature-dependent transport coefficients. It contradicts earlier 3+1D hydrodynamic calculations (Nonaka 2006, Bozek 2011, Molnar 2014) that could not describe the pseudo-rapidity dependence of v2, showing this was due to the choice of transport parameters and their temperature dependence. The results enable future measurements of event-by-event vn distributions as functions of rapidity to independently constrain three-dimensional initial state fluctuations and temperature-dependent transport properties of QCD matter.
