# Search for singly charmed dibaryons in baryon-baryon scattering

> We perform a systematical investigation of the singly charmed dibaryon system with strangeness numbers $S=-1$, $-3$ and $-5$ in the framework of the chiral quark model. Two resonance states with strangeness numbers $S=-1$ are obtained in the baryon-baryon scattering process. In the $ΛΛ_{c}$ scattering phase shifts, the $ΣΣ_{c}$ appears as a resonance state with the mass and width 3591 MeV and 11.1 MeV, respectively. In the $NΞ_{c}$ and $NΞ^{\prime}_{c}$ scattering phase shifts, the $ΣΣ^{\ast}_{c}$ exhibits as a resonance state with the mass and width 3621-3624 MeV and 14.9 MeV, respectively. All these heavy-flavor dibaryons are worth searching for in experiments. Besides, we would like to emphasize that the coupling calculation between the bound channels and open channels is indispensable. The study of the scattering process maybe an effective way to look for the genuine resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two resonance states with strangeness \(S=-1\) exist in the singly charmed dibaryon system, but only when the coupling between bound channels and open channels is included in the scattering calculation; without such coupling, the states appear as bound states rather than resonances.

## Method

The authors used the chiral quark model (ChQM) extended to SU(4) flavor symmetry, incorporating one-gluon exchange, confinement, sigma meson exchange, and Goldstone boson exchange (including \(D\), \(D_s\), and \(\eta_c\) mesons). They solved the six-body problem as a two-body problem using the resonance group method (RGM), expanding the relative motion wave function in Gaussian bases. Scattering phase shifts were computed via the Kohn-Hulthén-Kato variational method to identify resonance states from rapid phase-shift changes.

## Key numbers

- In \(\Lambda\Lambda_{c}\) scattering, the \(\Sigma\Sigma_{c}\) resonance has mass 3591 MeV and width 11.1 MeV.
- In \(N\Xi_{c}\) and \(N\Xi^{\prime}_{c}\) scattering, the \(\Sigma\Sigma^{\ast}_{c}\) resonance has mass in the range 3621–3624 MeV and width 14.9 MeV.
- Single-channel bound states for \(S=-1\): \(\Sigma\Sigma_{c}\) binding energy \(-22.57\) MeV; \(\Sigma\Sigma^{*}_{c}\) binding energy \(-25.45\) MeV; \(\Sigma^{*}\Sigma_{c}\) binding energy \(-31.83\) MeV; \(\Sigma^{*}\Sigma^{*}_{c}\) binding energy \(-25.02\) MeV.
- No bound or resonance states were found for the \(S=-3\) or \(S=-5\) systems; all channels were unbound.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dibaryons using the chiral quark model, which had successfully described nucleon-nucleon interactions (nn), the \(N\Omega\) system (nomega5), and fully heavy dibaryons (full). It also builds on lattice QCD results by Junnarkar and Mathur (ccc1) that predicted stable heavy-flavor dibaryons. The findings suggest that scattering calculations with coupled channels are essential for identifying genuine resonances, and the predicted \(\Sigma\Sigma_{c}\) and \(\Sigma\Sigma^{\ast}_{c}\) states are candidates for experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb.
