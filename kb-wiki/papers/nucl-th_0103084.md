# Event-by-event fluctuations and the QGP

> We discuss the physics underlying event-by-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We will emphasize how the fluctuations of particle ratios can be utilized to explore the properties of the matter created in these collisions. In particular, we will argue that the fluctutions of the ratio of positively over negatively charged particles may serve as a unique signature for the Quark Gluon Plasma.

# Event-by-event fluctuations and the QGP

## Key claim

The fluctuations of the ratio of positively over negatively charged particles may serve as a unique signature for the Quark Gluon Plasma, provided that the measurement is performed over a sufficiently large rapidity window (Δy > 3) so that the reduced charge fluctuations from the QGP phase survive hadronization and the subsequent hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors derive the observable D = ⟨Nch⟩⟨δR²⟩, which measures charge fluctuations per unit entropy, and compare its expected value in a pion gas (D ≈ 4) versus a QGP (D ≈ 3/4 from free quarks and gluons, or D ≈ 1–1.5 from lattice QCD). They apply corrections for charge conservation (Cy) and finite net charge (Cμ) to obtain the corrected observable D̃, and test the formalism using the URQMD hadronic transport model.

## Key numbers

- For a pion gas without correlations, D ≈ 4.
- For a thermal system of free quarks and gluons, D ≈ 3/4.
- From lattice QCD results, D ≈ 1–1.5 for the QGP.
- Hadronic resonances reduce the pion gas value by about 30%, with about 50% of the correlations coming from ρ0 and ω meson decays.
- In URQMD simulations, the corrected observable D̃ ≈ 3 for a hadron gas, while D̃ ≈ 1 would indicate QGP formation.
- The charge conservation correction factor is Cy = 1 − ⟨Nch⟩Δy / ⟨Nch⟩total.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for event-by-event fluctuations developed by Mrowczynski (1998), Voloshin (1999), and Jeon & Koch (1999), and builds on the observation by Bialas (1999) that event-by-event fluctuations contain information equivalent to two-particle densities. The paper directly addresses the NA49 collaboration's experimental results on mean transverse momentum and K/π ratio fluctuations, which showed only statistical fluctuations. The proposed charge fluctuation observable enables a new experimental test for QGP formation, complementary to dilepton measurements, and predicts enhanced production of isoscalar resonances (η, ω, φ) that could be verified via electromagnetic decay channels.
