# Neutron-proton mass difference in nuclear matter

> Isospin-breaking effects in nuclear matter are studied in the framework of a medium-modified Skyrme model. The proposed effective Lagrangian incorporates both the medium influence of the surrounding nuclear environment on the single nucleon properties and an explicit isospin-breaking effect in the mesonic sector. The approach predicts that the neutron-proton mass difference decreases in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter but by a very small amount only.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton mass difference decreases in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, but only by a very small amount, approximately 4% at normal nuclear matter density, contrary to earlier suggestions that a rapid decrease could resolve the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly.

## Method

The authors construct a medium-modified Skyrme model Lagrangian that incorporates explicit isospin breaking in the mesonic sector via a generalized pion-mass term, and parameterizes the influence of the nuclear environment through S-wave and P-wave pion-nucleon scattering lengths and volumes. The classical soliton solutions are obtained under a hedgehog ansatz for isospin-symmetric homogeneous nuclear matter, and the strong part of the neutron-proton mass difference emerges from a constrained iso-rotation that cancels the isospin-breaking shift at the classical level. The electromagnetic part is computed from the in-medium electromagnetic form factors using a Cottingham-type formula.

## Key numbers

- Free-space neutron-proton mass difference: Δm_np^(Exp) = 1.29 MeV
- Electromagnetic part in free space: Δm_np^(EM) = -0.68 MeV (consistent with the range (-0.76 ± 0.30) MeV from Gasser (1982))
- Strong part in free space: Δm_np^(strong) = 1.97 MeV, fitted by adjusting the strong pion-mass difference Δm_π^(strong) ≈ 0.04 MeV
- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.5 m_π⁰³), the total neutron-proton mass difference decreases by approximately 4% relative to its free-space value
- Proton effective mass at ρ₀: m_p* = 645.2 MeV; neutron effective mass at ρ₀: m_n* = 646.4 MeV
- Proton magnetic moment at ρ₀: μ_p* = 1.48 n.m.; neutron magnetic moment at ρ₀: μ_n* = -0.75 n.m.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Rakhimov (1996) and Yakhshiev (2001) to include explicit isospin-breaking effects in the mesonic sector, following the formalism of Rathske (1988). It addresses the long-standing Nolen-Schiffer anomaly raised by Nolen and Schiffer (1969), for which Henley and Krein (1989) argued that a rapid decrease of the neutron-proton mass difference in medium could provide a resolution. The result, a very small decrease of only ~4% at saturation density, contradicts earlier expectations of a large medium effect and suggests that the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly cannot be explained by this mechanism alone. The approach enables future studies of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, where the effect may be more pronounced.
