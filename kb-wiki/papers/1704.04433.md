# Phenomenological QCD equations of state for neutron star mergers

> Thermal QCD equations of state at high baryon density are sensitive to the phase structure and the resulting excitation modes. The leading contribution at low temperature can be either ~p_F^2 T^2 (pF: Fermi momentum, T: temperature) for phases with gapless quarks, or ~T^4 for phases with gapped quarks. In the latter the thermal pressure is dominated by collective modes. Starting with a schematic quark model developed for neutron star structure, we estimate the thermal contributions and zero point energy from the Nambu-Goldstone modes by building them upon the mean field background for the color-flavor-locked quark matter. Applying the phase shift representation for thermodynamic potentials, we include not only the bound state pairs but also resonating pairs. According to the Levinson's theorem, the high energy contributions tend to cancel the pole contributions to the thermodynamics, tempering the UV behaviors in the zero point energy. Our primary target in this talk is the domain with baryon density nB as large as ~ 5-10n_0 (n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}: nuclear saturation density), and the temperature T of the order ~30-100 MeV. The insights into this domain may be obtained through the future detection of gravitational waves from neutron star merging events.

## Key claim

The thermal pressure in color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter is dominated by collective modes with a \(\sim T^4\) scaling, which is much smaller than the \(\sim p_F^2 T^2\) scaling from gapless quarks; however, the actual impact on neutron star merger simulations requires computing the prefactor, not just the parametric form, because pressure variations of 10-30% have deep impacts on neutron star structure.

## Method

The author starts with a schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark model developed for neutron star structure and builds collective Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes upon the mean-field background for CFL quark matter. The thermodynamic potentials are computed using a phase shift representation that includes both bound state pairs and resonating pairs, with the Levinson theorem ensuring cancellation of high-energy contributions against pole contributions. The NG mode spectra are calculated in the RPA approximation using vertices consistent with the mean-field calculations, including \(3\times 3\) coupled channels for charged modes and \(9\times 9\) coupled channels for neutral modes.

## Key numbers

* The target density domain is baryon density \(n_B \sim 5-10 n_0\), where \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density.
* The target temperature domain is \(T \sim 30-100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for neutron star merger events.
* The masses of NG modes (except \(K^+\) and \(K^0\)) lie in the range \(50-200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and decrease with increasing density.
* Light kaons have masses of \(5-20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at strong coupling and do not condense at \(n_B \gtrsim 5 n_0\).
* The zero point energy from pseudo-scalar NG channels reduces the mean-field pressure at given chemical potential by \(10-20\%\).
* Thermal corrections from the QCD sector are negligible up to temperature \(T \sim 40\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The momentum cutoff variation tested for the zero point energy is \(|\vec{p}|_{\mathrm{max}} = 600-1200\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with no sizable cutoff dependence found.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous zero-temperature studies of neutron star structure (Kojo 2015) to include thermal effects relevant for neutron star mergers, building on the hadron-quark continuity picture originally proposed by Schafer (1998) and later used in the neutron star context by Masuda (2012) and Kojo (2014). The phase shift representation for thermodynamics follows the approach of Beth (1937), Dashen (1969), and Zhuang (1994). The results enable future gravitational wave detections from neutron star mergers to constrain the QCD phase structure at high baryon density, and call for more systematic studies of two-particle correlations beyond the NG channels.
