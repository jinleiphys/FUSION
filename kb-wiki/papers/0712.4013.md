# How the Pauli principle governs the decay of three-cluster systems

> New approach to the problem of multichannel continuum spectrum of three-cluster systems composed of an s-cluster and two neutrons is suggested based on the discrete representation of a complete basis of allowed states of the multiparticle harmonic oscillator. The structure of the eigenfunctions and behavior of the eigenvalues of the three-cluster norm kernel are analyzed. Classification of the eigenvalues of the three-cluster systems with the help of eigenvalues of the two-body subsystem is suggested. Asymptotic boundary conditions for a three-cluster wave function in the continuum consistent with the requirements of the Pauli principle are established. Such asymptotic behavior corresponds rather to subsequent decay of the three-cluster system than to the so-called "democratic decay" associated with the hyperspherical harmonics. The 3H+n+n configuration of the 5H nucleus is considered in detail.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle forces the asymptotic decay of three-cluster systems composed of an s-cluster and two neutrons to proceed via sequential decay (a two-cluster subsystem decaying first, followed by the remaining neutron) rather than via "democratic decay" described by hyperspherical harmonics, provided the number of oscillator quanta is sufficiently large relative to the quantum number labeling the family of states.

## Method

The authors employed the Algebraic Version of the Resonating-Group Method (AVRGM) in the Fock-Bargmann space, constructing a complete basis of Pauli-allowed harmonic oscillator states classified by SU(3) symmetry indices. They solved the eigenvalue problem for the three-cluster norm kernel (the overlap integral of antisymmetrized Slater determinants) for systems of the form \(^{A}\mathrm{X} = ^{A-2}\mathrm{X} + n + n\) with \(A \leq 6\), analyzing the structure of eigenfunctions and the behavior of eigenvalues as the number of oscillator quanta increases.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(t\) (square of tangent of rotation angle between Jacobi trees) takes values: \(t = 3/2\) for \(^6\)He, \(t = 5/3\) for \(^5\)H, \(t = 2\) for tetraneutron, and \(t = 3\) for three-neutron system.
- For the \(^5\)H ground state, experimental resonance energies range from \(E_R = 1.8 \pm 0.1\) MeV (with \(\Gamma \leq 0.5\) MeV) to \(E_R = 5.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV (with \(\Gamma = 5.4 \pm 0.5\) MeV).
- The asymptotic form of the Pauli-allowed basis functions holds when \(\nu \geq k + 5\), where \(\nu\) is half the total number of oscillator quanta and \(k\) is the degree of the Kravchuk polynomial labeling the family.
- The \(^3\)H cluster is bound by 8.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic treatment of three-cluster systems beyond prior macroscopic approaches (Shul'gina, Fedorov) that used repulsive cores or orthogonalizing pseudopotentials (Kukulin) to simulate Pauli effects, which risked excluding allowed states or including spurious ones. It also contrasts with the hyperspherical harmonics method used by Vasilevsky (2004) for \(^5\)H, which mixes Pauli-allowed states with different hypermomentum and requires large hypermomenta for accurate asymptotics (Baye). The classification of three-cluster states by SU(3) symmetry and Kravchuk polynomials enables identification of the most important decay channels and provides a foundation for calculating resonance energies and widths in neutron-rich nuclei such as \(^5\)H.
