# Initial-conditions problem for a Chiral Gross-Neveu system

> A time-dependent projection technique is used to treat the initial-value problem for self-interacting fermionic fields. On the basis of the general dynamics of the fields, we derive formal equations of kinetic type for the set of one-body dynamical variables. A nonperturbative mean-field expansion can be written for these equations. We treat this expansion in lowest order, which corresponds to the Gaussian mean-field approximation, for a uniform system described by the Chiral Gross-Neveu Hamiltonian. Standard stationary features of the model, such as dynamical mass generation due to chiral symmetry breaking and a phenomenon analogous to dimensional transmutation, are reobtained in this context. The mean-field time evolution of non-equilibrium initial states is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed kinetic equations for the one-body density of a self-interacting fermionic field in the Gaussian mean-field (isoentropic) approximation, but explicitly defers the inclusion of higher-order collisional (correlation) corrections to future work, meaning the results presented are limited to the lowest-order mean-field truncation.

## Method

The authors apply a time-dependent projection technique, previously developed for nonrelativistic nuclear many-body dynamics, to the Chiral Gross-Neveu model in (1+1) dimensions. They expand the Dirac field operators in a momentum-space basis, construct the extended one-body density matrix (including pairing terms), and diagonalize it via a Bogolyubov transformation. The Gaussian mean-field approximation is implemented by replacing the full density operator with a Gaussian ansatz that factorizes higher-order correlations, yielding closed equations of motion for the Bogolyubov parameters and occupation numbers. The resulting equations are renormalized using a momentum cut-off procedure.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent projection technique, previously applied to the nonrelativistic nuclear many-body problem by Nemes and de Toledo Piza (1990) and to the bosonic \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory in (1+1) dimensions by Lin and de Toledo Piza (1994), to the fermionic case using the Chiral Gross-Neveu model. The approach recovers standard static results of the model, such as dynamical mass generation from chiral symmetry breaking and dimensional transmutation, which were originally obtained by Gross and Neveu (1974) using the \(1/N\) expansion. The paper sets the stage for future inclusion of collisional (correlation) corrections beyond the Gaussian mean-field approximation, analogous to the bosonic treatment of Lin and de Toledo Piza (1994).
