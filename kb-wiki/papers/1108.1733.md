# Neutron star properties and the symmetry energy

> The effect of the symmetry energy on the properties of compact stars is discussed. It is shown that, for stars with masses above 1 $M_\odot$, the radius of the star varies linearly with the symmetry energy slope $L$. The dependence of the hyperon content and onset density of the direct Urca process on the symmetry energy and meson coupling parametrization are also analyzed.

# Neutron star properties and the symmetry energy

## Key claim

For neutron stars with masses above 1 solar mass, the radius varies linearly with the symmetry energy slope L, but this linear relationship holds strictly only when the isoscalar channel is kept fixed; when different relativistic mean field models with varying isoscalar properties are compared, the linear trend is present but the behavior is not strictly linear.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) to generate equations of state for hadronic matter, including nucleons and the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰). They considered six existing parametrizations (NL3, GM1, GM3, NLρ, FSU, IU-FSU) and also generated seven modified versions of IU-FSU by varying the ρ-meson coupling g_ρ and the ρ-ω mixing term Λ_v while keeping the isoscalar channel fixed, producing a range of symmetry energy slopes L from 47.2 to 99.17 MeV. Two different hyperon-meson coupling schemes were employed: set A based on hypernuclear potentials with experimentally constrained binding energies, and set B with x_σ=0.8 for all hyperons as proposed in GM1.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy slope L values for the six standard models: NL3 (118 MeV), GM1 (94 MeV), GM3 (90 MeV), NLρ (85 MeV), FSU (61 MeV), IU-FSU (47.2 MeV)
- Symmetry energy at saturation for the six standard models: NL3 (37.4 MeV), GM1 (32.5 MeV), GM3 (32.5 MeV), NLρ (30.5 MeV), FSU (32.6 MeV), IU-FSU (31.3 MeV)
- For the modified IU-FSU models, L ranges from 47.20 MeV (Set 1) to 99.17 MeV (Set 7), with corresponding symmetry energies at saturation from 31.34 MeV to 35.41 MeV
- FSU predicts a maximum star mass of 1.72 solar masses, which is 0.25 solar masses below the measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97±0.04 solar masses
- For stars without hyperons, the radius reduction for modified IU-FSU models over the range 45 < L < 100 MeV is more than 1 km for 1.0 solar mass stars and approximately 0.5 km for maximum mass configurations
- Hyperon binding potentials used: V_Λ = -28 MeV, V_Σ = 30 MeV, V_Ξ = -18 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of how the symmetry energy affects neutron star properties, building on the FSU parametrization from FSU (2005) which was too soft to reach 1.97 solar masses, and the IU-FSU parametrization from IU-FSU (2010) which incorporated constraints from the empirical equation of state of Steiner et al. (2010). The study systematically varies the isovector channel while keeping isoscalar properties fixed, complementing earlier work by Piekarewicz (2002) on constraining the nuclear equation of state from giant resonances. The results enable better constraints on the high-density symmetry energy from neutron star radius measurements and direct Urca process thresholds, particularly relevant for future facilities like FAIR.
