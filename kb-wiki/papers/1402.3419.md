# Azimuthal Jet Tomography at RHIC and LHC

> A generic jet-energy loss model that is coupled to state-of-the-art hydrodynamic fields and interpolates between a wide class of running coupling pQCD-based and AdS/CFT-inspired models is compared to recent data on the azimuthal and transverse momentum dependence of high-pT pion nuclear modification factors and high-pT elliptic flow measured at RHIC and LHC. We find that RHIC data are surprisingly consistent with various scenarios considered. However, extrapolations to LHC energies favor running coupling pQCD-based models of jet-energy loss. While conformal holographic models are shown to be inconsistent with data, recent non-conformal generalizations of AdS holography may provide an alternative description.

## Key claim

While RHIC data are surprisingly consistent with various jet-energy loss scenarios, extrapolations to LHC energies favor running coupling pQCD-based models; conformal holographic models are ruled out by LHC data, but non-conformal generalizations of AdS holography may provide an alternative description.

## Method

The authors couple a generic jet-energy loss model, characterized by three exponents (a,b,c) in the energy-loss formula dE/dx ~ P^a τ^b T^c, to three different bulk QGP collective fields: ideal VISH2+1 hydrodynamics, viscous RL Hydro with η/s=0.08, and a simple v⊥=0.6 transverse blast wave model. They compare four specific energy-loss scenarios (QCDrad with a=0,b=1,c=3; QCDel with a=0,b=0,c=2; conformal AdS falling string with a=0,b=2,c=4; and SLTc with enhanced coupling at Tc≈170 MeV) to measured nuclear modification factors and high-pT elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, all scenarios based on a (viscous) hydrodynamic background describe measured pT>8 GeV data within present error bars; only the v⊥=0.6 transverse blast wave background leads to an in/out asymmetry a factor of two below PHENIX data.
- For pQCD-based scenarios at LHC, the jet-medium coupling κLHC is reduced by approximately 30-40% compared to κRHIC to simulate running coupling effects.
- The SLTc model uses an enhanced jet-medium coupling with κ(Tc)=3κ(∞) around a critical temperature of Tc≈170 MeV.
- The conformal AdS scenario with fixed coupling (κLHC=κRHIC) leads to overquenching of the nuclear modification factor at LHC, ruling out that model.

## Context

This work broadens the PHENIX analysis (Adare 2012) by considering a wider class of dE/dx models and extending to simultaneous RHIC and LHC description. It extends prior studies by Molnar (2013) and Jiechen (2014) which showed that pQCD (D)GLV energy loss coupled to 3D MPC or VISH2+1 gave high-pT elliptic flow a factor of ~2 below data. The results enable discrimination between conformal AdS/CFT (ruled out) and non-conformal AdS generalizations (Ficnar 2013) that may provide an alternative description at LHC.
