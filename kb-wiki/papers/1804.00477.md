# $^9$Be+$^{120}$Sn scattering at near-barrier energies within a four body model

> Cross sections for elastic and inelastic scattering of the weakly-bound $^9$Be nucleus on a $^{120}$Sn target have been measured at seven bombarding energies around and above the Coulomb barrier. The elastic angular distributions are analyzed with a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculation, which considers $^9$Be as a three-body projectile ($α$ + $α$ + n). An optical model analysis using the São Paulo potential is also shown for comparison. The CDCC analysis shows that the coupling to the continuum part of the spectrum is important for the agreement with experimental data even at energies around the Coulomb barrier, suggesting that breakup is an important process at low energies. At the highest incident energies, two inelastic peaks are observed at 1.19(5) and 2.41(5) MeV. Coupled-channels (CC) calculations using a rotational model confirm that the first inelastic peak corresponds to the excitation of the 2$_1^+$ state in $^{120}$Sn, while the second one likely corresponds to the excitation of the 3$_1^-$ state.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations show that coupling to the continuum part of the spectrum is important for agreement with experimental elastic scattering data even at energies around the Coulomb barrier, suggesting that breakup is an important process at low energies, but the calculations underestimate the data in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region at 29.5 and 28 MeV, which may indicate either unaccounted systematic errors or limitations in the theoretical models.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions for the weakly-bound projectile 9Be on a 120Sn target were measured at seven bombarding energies from 26 to 50 MeV using an array of eight surface-barrier detectors at the TANDAR laboratory. The elastic angular distributions were analyzed with four-body CDCC calculations treating 9Be as a three-body projectile (alpha + alpha + n) using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, and compared with optical model calculations using the Sao Paulo potential. Inelastic peaks at 1.19(5) and 2.41(5) MeV were analyzed with coupled-channels calculations using a rotational model for the target.

## Key numbers

- Seven bombarding energies measured: 26, 27, 28, 29.5, 31, 42, and 50 MeV (laboratory frame)
- 9Be binding energy: 1.5736 MeV below the alpha+alpha+n threshold
- Calculated ground-state energy from the three-body model: -1.574 MeV; calculated rms matter radius: 2.466 fm (experimental: 2.53 fm)
- Two inelastic peaks observed at excitation energies of 1.19(5) and 2.41(5) MeV
- Total reaction cross sections from CDCC calculations range from 191 mb at 26 MeV to 1832 mb at 50 MeV
- Total breakup cross sections from CDCC calculations range from 38.4 mb at 26 MeV to 140.4 mb at 50 MeV
- Optical model normalization factors: real part N_r from 0.95(9) to 1.59(4); imaginary part N_i from 1.12(6) to 1.66(15)
- Detector energy resolution: 0.5% to 1.0% FWHM; angular acceptance smaller than 0.5 degrees

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 9Be-induced reactions on 208Pb (Woollard 2004, Yu 2010) and 27Al (Gomes 2004) to the intermediate-mass target 120Sn, and follows the four-body CDCC approach applied to 9Be+208Pb and 9Be+27Al by Casal (2015). The results confirm that Coulomb breakup is important at low energies for 9Be, consistent with findings for other weakly-bound nuclei such as 6He, 11Li, and 11Be on heavy targets. The discrepancies in the nuclear-Coulomb interference region motivate future experiments measuring breakup angular distributions at the TANDAR Laboratory.
