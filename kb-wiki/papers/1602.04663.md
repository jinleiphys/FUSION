# Quantum-classical hybrids in a simplified model of QED and geometric phase induced by charged particle trajectory

> We derive a model of quantum-classical hybrids for a simplified model of quantum electrodynamics in the framework of the stochastic variational method. In this model, charged particle trajectories are affected by the interaction with quantized electromagnetic fields, and this quantum-classical interaction induces a displacement current. We further investigate a geometric phase in the wave functional of the gauge field configuration, which is induced by adiabatic motions of the charged particles. This phase contains the quantum-classical backreaction effect and usual Berry's phase is reproduced in the vanishing limit of the fluctuation of the charged particle trajectories.

## Key claim

In a simplified model of QED where charged particles are treated as classical degrees of freedom interacting with quantized electromagnetic fields, the geometric phase induced by adiabatic charged particle motions contains a quantum-classical backreaction effect, and the usual Berry's phase is reproduced only in the vanishing limit of the fluctuation of the charged particle trajectories.

## Method

The author derives a quantum-classical hybrid (QCH) model using the stochastic variational method (SVM), where quantization is treated as optimization of actions written with stochastic variables. Classical charged particle trajectories are described by a deterministic differential equation (removing the Wiener process from the particle stochastic differential equation), while the electromagnetic fields are quantized via a forward stochastic differential equation for the transverse gauge field components in the Coulomb gauge. The resulting coupled system yields a functional Schrödinger equation for the gauge field wave functional and a modified equation of motion for the classical particle trajectories, with consistency conditions enforced through the Fokker-Planck equation for the probability distribution.

## Key numbers

* The forward stochastic differential equation for the charged particle (Eq. (sder)) contains the term $\sqrt{\hbar/M} d{\bf W}^i(t)$, which is removed in the classicalization procedure to obtain the QCH model.
* The forward stochastic differential equation for the transverse gauge field (Eq. (sdea)) contains the noise amplitude $\sqrt{\hbar c^2/(\Delta x)^3}$ per lattice cell, where $\Delta x$ is the grid interval.
* The geometric phase $\gamma_n(t)$ is given by $i \sum_\alpha \langle n,t | \int_{C_\alpha(\{{\bf a}\})} d {\bf f}_\alpha \cdot \nabla_{{\bf f}_\alpha} |n, t\rangle_0$, where the path integration depends on the gauge field configuration $\{{\bf a}\}$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCH model for particle systems (Koide 2015) to a field-theoretical system with electromagnetic interactions, using the stochastic variational method originally proposed by Yasue (1981) and developed from Nelson's stochastic quantization. The geometric phase result differs from that found in another QCH model by Zhang (2014), which claimed the usual Berry's phase is still reproduced, because the present model includes a different backreaction mechanism between classical and quantum degrees of freedom. The model enables further study of gauge-invariant formulations, the Aharonov-Bohm effect in a QCH context, and the description of spin degrees of freedom through stochastic rotation.
