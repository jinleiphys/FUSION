# Chirality in $(\vec{p},2p)$ reactions induced by proton helicity

> It is shown that longitudinally polarized protons can be used to induce chirality in the final states of the $(\vec{p},pN)$ reaction at intermediate energies, when there exist three final-state particles with non-coplanar momentum vectors. The analyzing power $A_z$ is proposed as a measure of this effect. Theoretical descriptions to obtain $A_z$ based on an intuitive picture as well as a distorted wave impulse approximation are presented, showing that the helicity of incident protons is coupled to the chirality of the orbital motion of a single-particle wave function, resulting in the chirality of the final states and a large $A_z$ value.

## Key claim

Longitudinally polarized protons can induce chirality in the final states of the \((\vec{p},pN)\) reaction at intermediate energies when three final-state particles have non-coplanar momentum vectors, with the analyzing power \(A_z\) serving as a measure of this effect; the helicity of incident protons couples to the chirality of the orbital motion of a single-particle wave function, producing a large \(A_z\) value.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical description based on the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) to calculate the longitudinal vector analyzing power \(A_z\) for the \((\vec{p},2p)\) reaction. They applied this framework to the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}(\vec{p},2p)^{15}\mathrm{N}\) reaction at 250 MeV incident proton energy, using the computer program pikoe with the Franey-Love parametrization for the NN effective interaction and the EDAD1 Dirac optical potential for distorted waves. The triple differential cross section and \(A_z\) were computed as functions of the azimuthal angle difference \(\phi_{12}\) between the two emitted protons, with the kinematics fixed at \(T_1 = 158\) MeV, \(\theta_1 = 27^\circ\), and \(\theta_2 = 56^\circ\).

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 250 MeV for the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}(\vec{p},2p)^{15}\mathrm{N}\) reaction
- Particle-1 kinetic energy fixed at \(T_1 = 158\) MeV
- Particle-1 emission direction: \(\theta_1 = 27^\circ\), \(\phi_1 = 0^\circ\)
- Particle-2 polar angle: \(\theta_2 = 56^\circ\)
- \(A_z = 0\) at \(\phi_{12} = 180^\circ\) (coplanar condition)
- \(A_z\) peaks at around \(\phi_{12} = 140^\circ\)–\(150^\circ\) and \(210^\circ\)–\(220^\circ\)
- \(A_z\) has symmetry: \(A_z(\phi_{12}) = -A_z(360^\circ - \phi_{12})\)
- \(A_z\) has opposite sign between the \(0p_{1/2}\) and \(0p_{3/2}\) orbits

## Context

This work extends the study of spin observables in knockout reactions beyond the traditional transverse analyzing power \(A_y\) (measured in prior work by Jacob and Maris (1966, 1973), Wakasa (2017), and Noro (2020, 2023)) by introducing longitudinal polarization and the chirality-based observable \(A_z\). The Maris effect, which produces large \(A_y\) values through the combination of the spin correlation coefficient \(C_{yy}\) and nuclear absorption, is here generalized to the longitudinal case using \(C_{zz} \approx 1\). The approach enables future studies of single-particle structure in unstable nuclei using radioactive isotope beams with the \((p,pN)\) reaction in inverse kinematics, as discussed by Kawase (2018), and may also be sensitive to axial vector components of nuclear interactions such as rank-1 parts of three-nucleon forces.
