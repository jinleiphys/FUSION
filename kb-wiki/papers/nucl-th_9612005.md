# Dilepton Yield in Heavy-Ion Collisions with Bose Enhancement of Decay Width

> The excess of low invariant mass dilepton yield in heavy ion collisions arising from reduction in the rho meson mass at finite temperatures is partially suppressed because of the effect on the width of the rho meson induced by Bose enhancement, essentially due to emission of pions in a medium of the pion gas in the central rapidity region. The sensitivity of the effect on the initial temperature of the hadronic phase is also examined.

## Key claim

The excess low-invariant-mass dilepton yield arising from the reduction of the rho meson mass at finite temperatures is partially suppressed (by approximately 30%) when the Bose enhancement of the rho decay width due to induced pion emission in a hot pion gas is included, but an overall excess yield still remains.

## Method

The authors calculate the dilepton production rate from pion-pion annihilation via vector meson dominance (VMD) using a thermal nucleon loop in the imaginary-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory. The in-medium rho meson mass and decay width are computed from the polarization tensor, with the effective nucleon mass determined self-consistently from a tadpole diagram. The static emission rate is then convoluted with a Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion model to obtain the invariant mass distribution of dileptons at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of the total dilepton yield by approximately 30% when Bose enhancement of the decay width is included compared to results without Bose enhancement, for initial temperature T_i = 185 MeV, final temperature T_F = 130 MeV, R_A = 4.6 fm, and dN/dy = 225.
- The rho meson decay width remains in the region of approximately 155 to 161 MeV up to a temperature of 160 MeV, due to the offset between mass reduction and Bose enhancement.
- The rho-pi-pi coupling constant is estimated from the free-space decay width as g^2_{rho-pi-pi}/4pi approximately 2.9.
- The effective reduction factor at the rho peak is given by (1 + e^{-m_rho/(2kT_eff)})^{-4}, where T_eff is the temperature at which the vector meson mass equals the peak invariant mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Li, Ko and Brown (1995) that considered density-dependent vector meson masses but did not include Bose enhancement effects on the decay width. It contrasts with the results of Gale and Kapusta (1991), who found that medium corrections from pion loops were modest up to 150 MeV, whereas here the nucleon loop at finite temperature produces substantial effects because the nucleon suffers considerable mass reduction. The study enables more complete dynamical calculations of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions and suggests that similar Bose enhancement effects should be investigated for other hadronic channels contributing to lepton production.
