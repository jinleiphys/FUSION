# Equation of state of nuclear matter and neutron stars: Quark mean-field model versus relativistic mean-field model

> The equation of state of neutron-rich nuclear matter is of interest to both nuclear physics and astrophysics. We have demonstrated the consistency between laboratory and astrophysical nuclear matter in neutron stars by considering low-density nuclear physics constraints (from $^{208}$Pb neutron-skin thickness) and high-density astrophysical constraints (from neutron star global properties). We have used both quark-level and hadron-level models, taking the quark mean-field (QMF) model and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model as examples, respectively. We have constrained the equation of states of neutron stars and some key nuclear matter parameters within the Bayesian statistical approach, using the first multi-messenger event GW170817/AT 2017gfo, as well as the mass-radius simultaneous measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER, and the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from both PREX-II measurement and ab initio calculations. Our results show that, compared with the RMF model, QMF model's direct coupling of quarks with mesons and gluons leads to the evolution of the in-medium nucleon mass with the quark mass correction. This feature enables QMF model a wider range of model applicability, as shown by a slow drop of the nucleon mass with density and a large value at saturation that is jointly constrained by nuclear physics and astronomy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quark mean-field (QMF) model, which directly couples quarks with mesons and gluons, yields a wider range of model applicability than the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model when both are constrained by the same nuclear and astrophysical data, specifically because the QMF model predicts a slower drop of nucleon mass with density and a larger value at saturation.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic comparison of the RMF and QMF models by adopting the same functional form of the many-body Lagrangian and maintaining identical saturation properties for both models, with coupling constants fitted against empirical data at saturation density. They then carried out Bayesian inference for both models using likelihood functions from the multi-messenger event GW170817/AT 2017gfo, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb from both PREX-II measurement and ab initio calculations. The resulting equations of state were used to solve the TOV equations and compute neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm−3 and binding energy E/A = −16 MeV were used as fixed constants for both models
- Uniform prior ranges were adopted for: incompressibility K0 = U(220, 340) MeV, symmetry energy J0 = U(28, 45) MeV, symmetry energy slope L0 = U(20, 150) MeV, and effective mass ratio M*N/MN = U(0.55, 0.80)
- The free nucleon mass was taken as MN = 939 MeV and the free nucleon radius as rN = 0.87 fm
- Meson masses used: mσ = 510 MeV, mω = 783 MeV, mρ = 770 MeV
- The constituent quark mass was set to mq = 300 MeV
- The energy density at saturation was determined as 147.68 MeV/fm3

## Context

This work extends the comparison between quark-level and hadron-level nuclear models initiated by Guichon (1988) with the quark-meson coupling model and the Walecka (1974) RMF model, building on prior Bayesian analyses of neutron star EoS constraints from GW170817 and NICER observations. The results enable future studies to incorporate non-nucleon degrees of freedom such as deconfined quarks, hyperons, and Delta isobars, which were excluded from this analysis to avoid additional uncertainties.
