# Extraction of the Ratio of the N^*(1535) Electromagnetic Helicity Amplitudes from Eta Photoproduction off Neutrons and Protons

> Using the recent precise measurements of eta photopro- duction in proton and deuteron targets, we extract the ratio of the helicity amplitudes A^n_1/2 / A^p_1/2, for the excitation of N^*(1535), in the effective Lagrangian approach, It is fairly model-independent, free from the final-state interaction effects, and negative as predicted by the quark models. We stress the importance of polarization observables in further elucidation of the N^*(1520) photoexcitation amplitudes.

# Extraction of the Ratio of the N*(1535) Electromagnetic Helicity Amplitudes from Eta Photoproduction off Neutrons and Protons

## Key claim
The ratio of neutron to proton transverse helicity amplitudes for exciting the N*(1535) resonance is extracted as negative and approximately -0.84, in agreement with quark model predictions, but this extraction is only fairly model-independent because it relies on the effective Lagrangian approach to account for non-resonant contributions and other resonances such as N*(1520), which differ between proton and neutron targets.

## Method
The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level, including nucleon Born terms, rho and omega vector meson exchange in the t-channel, and contributions from the N*(1535) and N*(1520) resonances. They fit the strong interaction parameters (including the eta-nucleon pseudoscalar coupling constant g_eta) to Mainz proton data, then use the experimentally inferred ratio of neutron to proton cross sections (sigma_n/sigma_p = 0.66) from deuteron data to extract the neutron helicity amplitude for N*(1535) excitation. The ratio of helicity amplitudes is obtained by combining the extracted parameters xi_n and xi_p, which causes the strong interaction decay properties of the resonance to cancel out.

## Key numbers
- Ratio of neutron to proton cross sections inferred from deuteron data: sigma_n/sigma_p = 0.66 ± 0.07, for lab photon energies from eta production threshold up to 792 MeV
- Extracted parameter for neutron: xi_n = (-1.86 ± 0.20) × 10^(-4) MeV^(-1)
- Extracted parameter for proton: xi_p = (2.20 ± 0.15) × 10^(-4) MeV^(-1)
- Ratio of neutron to proton helicity amplitudes: A^n_1/2 / A^p_1/2 = -0.84 ± 0.15, using chi_p/chi_n ≈ 0.987
- Quark model estimate (Capstick) for the same ratio: approximately -0.83
- Pseudoscalar eta-nucleon coupling constant g_eta: approximately 2.3, from fit to Mainz proton angular distribution data
- Neutron eta photoproduction threshold energy: E_gamma^n(th) = 706.94 MeV

## Context
This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Mukhopadhyay, Zhang, and Benmerrouche (1995) for proton eta photoproduction to the neutron case, using Mainz data from Krusche et al. (1995) on both proton and deuteron targets. The extracted negative ratio confirms the flavor asymmetry prediction of quark models (Capstick, 1992) and contradicts any naive assumption that the cross section ratio directly implies the helicity amplitude ratio without accounting for different background contributions. The result enables tests via polarization observables (recoil nucleon polarization, polarized target asymmetry, polarized photon asymmetry) and has implications for understanding coherent eta photoproduction in nuclei, particularly the near cancellation of proton and neutron helicity amplitudes that explains the Mainz experiment seeing no coherent contribution in nuclei.
