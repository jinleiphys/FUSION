# Latest results from lattice QCD for the Roper resonance

> The present status of the Roper resonance in lattice QCD is reviewed. Some of the latest lattice results are discussed with particular emphasis on a large systematic error stemming from the finite size effect. These results suggest that the Roper resonance can be described by the simple three quark excitation of sizable extent.

## Key claim

After correcting for finite-size effects and higher-lying state contributions using the maximum entropy method (MEM), lattice QCD results suggest that the Roper resonance can be described by a simple three-quark excitation of sizable extent, and that the level ordering between the positive-parity Roper state and the negative-parity nucleon N*(1535) reverses near the chiral limit.

## Method

The author performed quenched lattice QCD simulations at beta = 6.0 (a^{-1} ≈ 1.9 GeV) using Wilson fermions on three lattice sizes (16^3 × 32, 24^3 × 32, and 32^3 × 32) to study finite-size effects. The maximum entropy method (MEM) was used to reconstruct spectral functions from two-point nucleon correlators, replacing the conventional matrix-correlator method to better account for higher-lying state contributions. Masses of the ground state and first excited positive-parity nucleon were extracted from peak positions in the spectral functions and extrapolated to infinite volume using a power-law behavior.

## Key numbers

- The lattice spacing is a^{-1} ≈ 1.9 GeV at beta = 6.0.
- Three lattice sizes were used: La ≈ 1.5 fm (16^3 × 32), La ≈ 2.2 fm (24^3 × 32), and La ≈ 3.0 fm (32^3 × 32).
- The pion mass range covered is M_π ≈ 0.6 to 1.0 GeV.
- The mass splitting between the ground state nucleon and the Roper state is approximately 0.5 GeV, consistent with the experimental value.
- The level switching between the negative-parity nucleon and the Roper state occurs near M_π ∼ 0.6 GeV in this work, in contrast to the results of Lee (2002) where it occurs at M_π ∼ 0.3 to 0.4 GeV.
- The infinite volume extrapolation of masses follows M_L - M_∞ ∝ c/L^3.

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice study by Sasaki (2001) which first observed the wrong level ordering between the Roper resonance N'(1440) and the negative-parity nucleon N*(1535) in the heavy-quark mass region, a problem also seen in quark confining models such as the harmonic oscillator quark model and the MIT bag model. The present results contradict the findings of Lee (2002) who observed strong quark mass dependence on the mass splitting and a different level-switching point using overlap fermions. This work enables future lattice studies to properly account for finite-size effects when studying excited baryon states, and suggests that the Roper resonance may be understood as a conventional three-quark state rather than requiring exotic interpretations.
