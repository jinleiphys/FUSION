# Phase diagram of alpha matter with Skyrme-like scalar interaction

> The equation of state and phase diagram of strongly interacting matter composed of $α$ particles are studied in the mean-field approximation. The particle interactions are included via a Skyrme-like mean field, containing both attractive and repulsive terms. The model parameters are found by fitting known values of binding energy and baryon density in the ground state of $α$ matter, obtained from microscopic calculations by Clark and Wang. Thermodynamic quantities of $α$ matter are calculated in the broad domains of temperature and baryon density, which can be reached in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The model predicts both first-order liquid-gas phase transition and Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ particles. We present the profiles of scaled variance, sound velocity and isochoric heat capacity along the isentropic trajectories of $α$ matter. Strong density fluctuations are predicted in the vicinity of the critical point at temperature $T_c\approx 14~\textrm{MeV}$ and density $n_c\approx 0.012~\textrm{fm}^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that pure α matter exhibits both a first-order liquid-gas phase transition and Bose-Einstein condensation, with the phase diagram containing a triple point where the BEC boundary meets the mixed-phase region; however, this scenario assumes a metastable branch of the phase diagram where α particles are more abundant, ignoring the admixture of nucleons.

## Method

The authors model a system of scalar bosons (α particles) using a mean-field approximation with a Skyrme-like scalar interaction Lagrangian containing attractive (φ⁴) and repulsive (φ⁶) terms. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the binding energy per α particle (19.7 MeV) and equilibrium baryon density (0.036 fm⁻³) of cold α matter from microscopic calculations by Clark and Wang (1966). Thermodynamic quantities are computed self-consistently via a gap equation for the scalar density, and the phase boundaries for the liquid-gas transition and BEC are determined from Gibbs equilibrium conditions and the condition μ = M(σ), respectively.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per α particle in the ground state: W₀ = 19.7 MeV, at equilibrium density n₀ = 0.036 fm⁻³ (baryon density n_B = 4n).
- Critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition: T_c ≈ 13.8 MeV, density n_c ≈ 0.0124 fm⁻³, chemical potential μ − m ≈ −48.9 MeV.
- Triple point temperature where BEC boundary meets the mixed-phase region: T_TP ≈ 3.67 MeV.
- Incompressibility modulus of cold α matter: K₀ ≈ 354 MeV.
- Model interaction parameters: a ≈ 7853, b ≈ 78.94 MeV⁻².
- Specific entropy at the BEC boundary (z = 1): S/N ≈ 1.284 (dimensionless).
- Specific entropy at the critical point: S/N ≈ 4.54.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field models of α matter by Satarov et al. (2017, 2019, 2020) that used vector potentials, introducing instead a scalar field with an effective mass that depends on density, which avoids superluminal sound velocities at high densities. The phase diagram qualitatively agrees with that observed for atomic ⁴He (Buckingham 1990) and with earlier calculations for α matter with vector interactions (Satarov 2017). The results enable predictions of density fluctuations and isentropic trajectories relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data at intermediate energies, particularly for systems where enhanced α-particle yields are observed.
