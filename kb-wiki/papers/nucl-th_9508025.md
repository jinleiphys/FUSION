# Improvement of the nucleon emission process and the statistical property in molecular dynamics

> We propose to introduce a new stochastic process in molecular dynamics in order to improve the description of the nucleon emission process from a hot nucleus. We give momentum fluctuations originating from the momentum width of the nucleon wave packet to the nucleon stochastically when it is being emitted from the nucleus. We show by calculating the liquid gas phase equilibrium in the case of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, that with this improvement, we can recover the quantum mechanical statistical property of the nucleus for the particle emission process.

## Key claim

The authors propose that adding a stochastic momentum fluctuation to nucleons as they are being emitted from a hot nucleus in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) recovers the quantum mechanical statistical property for particle emission, whereas the original AMD only reproduces the classical statistical property for that process. The improvement is presented as a minor branching process that does not require totally modifying the AMD equation of motion.

## Method

The authors introduce a new stochastic process (AMD-MF) into the existing antisymmetrized molecular dynamics framework. At each time step, a representative point is randomly generated for each nucleon according to a Gaussian distribution; if the nucleon was inside a nucleus at the previous time step and is now isolated in free space (density below 0.1 times normal nuclear density), a momentum fluctuation is applied. The relative momentum between the emitted nucleon and the residual nucleus is sampled stochastically from Gaussian distributions representing the momentum width of the nucleon wave packet and the center-of-mass momentum distribution of the nucleus, with total momentum and energy conserved by adjusting the state of the nucleus via a frictional cooling/heating method.

## Key numbers

- Width parameter of the Gaussian wave packets: nu = 0.16 fm^-2
- Normal nuclear density used as threshold: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3; nucleons with local density below 0.1 rho_0 are considered isolated
- For the liquid-gas equilibrium with the usual AMD (Volkov force, A_tot = 15, E_tot = -50 MeV): temperature T = 1.48 MeV and average internal energy per nucleon of the liquid phase <E_liq>/A_liq = -3.65 MeV for A_liq = 14
- For the liquid-gas equilibrium with AMD-MF (Volkov force, A_tot = 28, E_tot = 60 MeV): temperature T = 6.12 MeV and average internal energy per nucleon of the liquid phase <E_liq>/A_liq = -5.52 MeV for A_liq = 14
- Energy fluctuation in the AMD-MF calculation: sqrt(<Delta E_liq^2>)/A_liq = 1.2 MeV, corresponding to a temperature fluctuation sqrt(<Delta tau^2>) = 0.8 MeV
- The quantum mechanical Fermi gas formula reproduced by AMD-MF: <E_liq>/A_liq = T^2/(12 MeV) - B.E.

## Context

This work extends the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) approach developed by Ono, Horiuchi, Maruyama, and Ohnishi (1992-1995) for heavy ion collisions at 30-100 MeV/nucleon, where the authors observed that intermediate mass fragments were seldom produced and that the decay of excited nuclei was too slow compared to statistical decay model predictions. The improvement addresses a limitation noted by Ohnishi and Randrup (1993) regarding the statistical property of molecular dynamics, and the resulting AMD-MF now reproduces the quantum mechanical statistical behavior expected from the Fermi gas model, enabling more reliable simulations of multifragmentation reactions without assuming thermal equilibrium.
