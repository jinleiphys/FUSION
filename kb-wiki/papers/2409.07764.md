# Spin-hydrodynamics of electrons in graphene and magnetization due to thermal vorticity

> We examine the framework of relativistic spin-hydrodynamics in the context of electron hydrodynamics in graphene. We develop a spin-hydrodynamic model for a (2 + 1)-dimensional system of fermions under the condition of small spin polarization. Our analysis confirms that thermal vorticity, which satisfies the global equilibrium condition, is also a solution to the spin-hydrodynamic equations. Additionally, we calculate the magnetization of the system in global equilibrium and introduce a novel phenomenon - thermovortical magnetization - resulting from thermal vorticity, which can be experimentally observed in graphene.

# Spin-hydrodynamics of electrons in graphene and magnetization due to thermal vorticity

## Key claim

Thermal vorticity, which satisfies the global equilibrium condition in (2+1)-dimensional relativistic spin-hydrodynamics, is confirmed to be a solution of the spin-hydrodynamic equations for electrons in graphene under the condition of small spin polarization; this leads to a novel thermovortical magnetization that can in principle be experimentally observed in graphene, although the magnetization may be small.

## Method

The author develops a relativistic spin-hydrodynamic model for a (2+1)-dimensional system of massless fermions by generalizing single-particle phase-space distribution functions to spin density matrices, then Taylor-expanding the distribution functions to linear order in the polarization tensor under the assumption of small spin polarization. The charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor are computed from these distribution functions, and the conservation equations for net particle number, energy-momentum, and angular momentum yield hydrodynamic equations of motion for temperature, chemical potential, fluid velocity, and the polarization tensor. The magnetization tensor is also computed from the distribution functions using the magnetic dipole moment operator proportional to the spin operator.

## Key numbers

- Net number density: \( n = \frac{T^2}{\pi} \left[ \text{Li}_2(-e^{-\alpha}) - \text{Li}_2(-e^{\alpha}) \right] \), where \(\alpha = \mu/T\)
- Energy density: \( \varepsilon = \frac{2T^3}{\pi} \left[ -\text{Li}_3(-e^{-\alpha}) - \text{Li}_3(-e^{\alpha}) \right] \)
- Pressure: \( P = \varepsilon/2 \)
- Spin density: \( w = \frac{T^2}{4\pi} \ln(2 + 2\cosh\alpha) \)
- Magnetization tensor in global equilibrium: \( M^{\mu\nu} = \frac{g e}{8\pi} T \tanh(\alpha/2) \, \omega^{\mu\nu} \), where \(g\) is the electron g-factor, \(e\) is the elementary charge, and \(\omega^{\mu\nu}\) is the polarization tensor
- For rigid vortical motion with rotation speed \(\Omega\), the thermal vorticity matrix has constant off-diagonal components \(\pm \Omega/T_0\)

## Context

This work extends the framework of relativistic spin-hydrodynamics, previously developed for (3+1)-dimensional systems in the context of heavy-ion collisions (Florkowski 2017, Becattini 2013), to the (2+1)-dimensional setting of electron hydrodynamics in graphene. The author notes that attempts to apply the thermal vorticity solution to explain longitudinal spin-polarization in heavy-ion collisions yield the opposite sign compared to experimental results (Becattini 2017, Florkowski 2019), possibly because the short-lived fireball does not reach global equilibrium for spin dynamics; graphene avoids this limitation. The proposed thermovortical magnetization is reminiscent of the Barnett effect observed in solids (Barnett 1935). This work enables experimental tests of relativistic spin-hydrodynamics predictions using graphene, and opens the door to future studies of dissipative effects such as rotational viscosity and boost heat conductivity, as well as spin magnetohydrodynamics with external magnetic fields.
