# Proper construction of the continuum in light-cone QCD sum rules

> A proper way of subtracting the continuum contributions in light-cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR) is demonstrated. Specifically, we calculate the continuum corresponding to a typical OPE appearing in LCQSR by properly combining the double dispersion relation with QCD duality. We demonstrate how the subtraction terms can spuriously contribute to the sum rules. In the limit of zero external momentum, taking out the spurious continuum is found to yield the sum rules using the single-variable dispersion relation. The continuum factor constructed in this way differs from the one appearing in usual LCQSR. The difference substantially affects the extraction of hadronic parameters from the correlation function involving baryon currents.

## Key claim

The standard method of subtracting continuum contributions in light-cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR) inadvertently includes spurious terms from subtraction terms in the double dispersion relation; the correct continuum factor for a given operator product expansion (OPE) term of the form \(A^{n-2} \ln(-A)\) is \(E_{n-2}(S_0/M^2)\) rather than the commonly used \(E_{n-1}(S_0/M^2)\), and this difference substantially alters extracted hadronic parameters.

## Method

The authors start from a typical OPE term appearing in LCQSR, \(\int^1_0 dv\, v^k A^{n-2} \ln(-A)\) with \(A = v p_1^2 + (1-v) p_2^2\), and derive its spectral density via the double dispersion relation. They then isolate the spurious contributions that arise when QCD duality is naively applied by restricting the double spectral integral to \([0, S_0]\), showing that these spurious terms originate from subtraction terms that do not vanish after Borel transformation when the integration limit is finite. The correct OPE side after continuum subtraction is obtained by keeping only the term that reproduces the logarithmic part of the OPE, leading to a continuum factor \(E_{n-2}(S_0/M^2)\) instead of the usual \(E_{n-1}(S_0/M^2)\).

## Key numbers

* For a leading OPE term proportional to \(M^6\) (i.e., \(n=3\)) at a typical Borel mass \(M^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) and continuum threshold \(S_0 \sim 2\) GeV\(^2\), the correct continuum factor is \(E_1 \sim 0.6\), while the usual factor \(E_2 \sim 0.32\) is only about half as large.
* For an OPE term proportional to \(M^4\) (i.e., \(n=2\)) under the same conditions, the correct factor is \(E_0 \sim 0.86\), while the usual factor \(E_1 \sim 0.6\) is about 30% lower.
* The \(D^* D \pi\) coupling predicted by the usual LCQSR is around 12.5, substantially lower than the experimental value of 17.9 from the CLEO measurement (Anastassov 2001); the LCQSR with the continuum modification yields values comparable to experiment.

## Context

This work extends the earlier resolution of the spurious-continuum problem in conventional QCD sum rules with an external field (Kim 1999) to the light-cone QCD sum rule framework, where the double-variable dispersion relation is mandatory. The authors specifically address the discrepancy between the LCQSR calculation of the \(D^* D \pi\) coupling (Belyaev 1995) and the CLEO measurement (Anastassov 2001), and show that the same spurious-continuum issue affects other baryon sum rules, such as the nucleon magnetic moment calculation (Braun 1988) and the pion-nucleon coupling analysis (Zhu 1998, Braun 1988). The corrected continuum factors enable future LCQSR applications to yield more reliable hadronic parameters, particularly for baryon properties where the continuum threshold is around 2 GeV\(^2\).
