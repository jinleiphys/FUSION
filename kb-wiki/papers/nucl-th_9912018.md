# Positivity restrictions in polarized coincidence electronuclear scattering

> We make a systematic examination of the role played by the restriction that the cross section in polarized coincidence electronuclear processes must be positive. The necessary formalism for unpolarized scattering structure functions is developed within two frameworks: (i) the response tensor method; and (ii) the Jacob-Wick method. Equivalence of the two methods is demonstrated for unpolarized scattering, and then the simpler Jacob-Wick method is applied to the polarized pseudoscalar electroproduction off a nucleon. We derive three known and eight new inequalities among the polarized target structure functions, as well as eleven new polarized ejectile structure functions inequalities. We also provide rules for this method to be used in deuteron two-body electrodisintegration in conjunction with the results published in Phys. Rev. C 40, 2479 (1989).

## Key claim

The paper derives model-independent inequalities among polarized coincidence electronuclear structure functions based solely on the requirement that the cross section be positive semi-definite, but the derivation is restricted to the one-photon-exchange approximation and assumes a completely polarized electron beam (pure helicity state). The author notes that these constraints are not widely known in nuclear physics and have seen few applications.

## Method

The author develops the necessary formalism within two frameworks: the response tensor method and the Jacob-Wick helicity method, demonstrating their equivalence for unpolarized scattering. The simpler Jacob-Wick method is then applied to polarized pseudoscalar electroproduction off a nucleon, using the virtual photon density matrix expressed in terms of helicity transition amplitudes, with positivity constraints enforced by requiring all principal minors of the density matrix to be non-negative.

## Key numbers

- Three known and eight new inequalities are derived among the polarized target structure functions
- Eleven new polarized ejectile structure function inequalities are derived
- The unpolarized coincidence cross section contains five real linearly independent response functions (W1 through W5)
- The virtual photon density matrix in the unpolarized case reduces to five independent real parameters: w00, w++, w+-, Re w0+, Im w0+
- The electron helicity h takes only two eigenvalues: ±1/2

## Context

This work extends the positivity constraint analysis of Doncel and de Rafael (1972) from inclusive electron scattering to coincidence electronuclear scattering, and extends the earlier polarized target inequalities of De Rujula, Doncel and de Rafael (1973) and Raskin and Donnelly (1989) to include polarized ejectile structure functions. The results enable model-independent consistency checks for experimental extraction of coincidence observables and can provide upper bounds on structure functions such as R^(I)_LT' in terms of the other four unpolarized structure functions. The author provides rules for extending the method to deuteron two-body electrodisintegration using results from Phys. Rev. C 40, 2479 (1989).
