# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Anomalous Magnetic Moment

> The anomalous part of the magnetic moment of nucleon is studied. The electromagnetic vertex of nucleon is calculated using the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon interaction. The self-energy of nucleon suggested in the previous study is applied to the internal line.

# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Anomalous Magnetic Moment

## Key claim
The anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon can be calculated using pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon interaction, and the result is free from divergences within the lowest-order approximation; however, inclusion of the nucleon self-energy is necessary to approach experimental values, and even with a self-consistent effective mass of approximately 530 MeV, the calculated ratios only reach about 1.5 times the experimentally determined coefficients, not full agreement.

## Method
The electromagnetic vertex function of the nucleon was calculated using the pseudovector coupling pion-nucleon interaction, with the vertex divided into a pion current contribution and a vertex correction from the nucleon current. Dimensional regularization was applied to perform the momentum integrals, and the self-energy of the nucleon propagator was incorporated by replacing the internal off-shell nucleon propagator with one having an effective mass \( M' \) determined from a self-consistent equation.

## Key numbers
- At \( Q^2 = 0 \), the pion current contribution to the form factor \( F_2(0) = a \tau_3 \) with \( a = 0.831 \), using parameters \( M = 939 \) MeV, \( m_\pi = 139.6 \) MeV, and \( f_\pi = 1.0 \).
- The vertex correction contribution at \( Q^2 = 0 \) gives \( F_2(0) = b (1+\tau_{-}) \) with \( b = -1.086 \).
- The self-consistent solution for the self-energy yields \( \Sigma = 0.56 M \approx 530 \) MeV, leading to revised coefficients \( a = 0.758 \) and \( b = -1.077 \).
- Experimental values for comparison are \( \kappa_p = 1.79 \) and \( \kappa_n = -1.91 \) in units of the nuclear magneton.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Kinpara, no year given) on the self-energy of the nucleon propagator under pseudovector coupling, and addresses the electromagnetic form factor problem originally studied by Chew (no year given) using pion degrees of freedom. The calculation shows that lowest-order perturbative results differ substantially from experimental data, and that incorporating the self-energy through an effective mass improves but does not fully resolve the discrepancy, pointing to the need for higher-order corrections or a modified coupling constant.
