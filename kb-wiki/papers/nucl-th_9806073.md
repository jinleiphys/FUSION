# Description of even-even triaxial Nuclei within the Coherent State and the Triaxial Rotation-Vibration Models

> The coherent state model (CSM) and the triaxial rotation-vibration model (TRVM) are alternatively used to describe the ground, gamma and beta bands of 228Th. CSM is also applied to the nuclei 126Xe and 130Ba, which were recently considered in TRVM. The two models are compared with respect to both their underlying assumptions and to their predicted results for energy levels and E2 branching ratios. Both models describe energies and quadrupole transitions of 228Th equally well and in good agreement with experiment, if the 0$_3^+$ level at 1120 keV is interpreted as the head of the beta band. The other two 0$^+$ levels at 832 and 939 keV are most likely not of a pure quadrupole vibration nature as has already been pointed out in the literature.

## Key claim

Both the Coherent State Model (CSM) and the Triaxial Rotation-Vibration Model (TRVM) describe the energies and quadrupole transitions of 228Th equally well and in good agreement with experiment, but only if the 0₃⁺ level at 1120 keV is interpreted as the head of the beta band; the other two 0⁺ levels at 832 and 939 keV are most likely not of a pure quadrupole vibration nature.

## Method

The TRVM describes small oscillations of the nuclear surface around a triaxial equilibrium shape, expanding the Hamiltonian to second order in the deviations from static deformations and diagonalizing in a basis labeled by angular momentum I, its projection K, and phonon numbers n₀ (beta) and n₂ (gamma). The CSM projects angular-momentum components from three orthogonal deformed states (a coherent ground state and two excited states for gamma and beta bands) and uses a sixth-order boson Hamiltonian that is diagonal in the beta-band basis. Both models were fitted to experimental excitation energies and used to compute E2 branching ratios for 228Th, 126Xe, and 130Ba.

## Key numbers

- For 228Th, the 0₃⁺ level at 1120 keV is interpreted as the head of the beta band; the other two 0⁺ levels lie at 832 and 939 keV.
- The static gamma deformation extracted from the TRVM a₂/β₀ ratio is about 25° for 126Xe and 130Ba, and about 13° for 228Th.
- The sum of energies of the first two 2⁺ states minus the energy of the first 3⁺ state yields differences of about 2 keV for 228Th, 47 keV for 126Xe, and 95 keV for 130Ba.
- For 130Ba, the TRVM predictions for the normalized transitions 4⁺₃→2⁺₂ and 4⁺₃→2⁺₁ exceed experimental data by factors of about 231 and 132 respectively, while CSM results underestimate the data by factors of 5 and 4.3 respectively.
- The first 3⁻ state in 228Th lies at 396 keV.

## Context

This work extends the TRVM, previously applied to 126Xe and 130Ba by Meyer, Faessler, and Khadkikar (1997), to the newly measured nucleus 228Th from data by Gareev et al. (1998). The CSM, developed by Raduta and collaborators in the early 1980s and previously applied to O(6)-type nuclei in the platinum region, is here compared directly with the TRVM for triaxial nuclei. The paper suggests that the TRVM is the classical counterpart of the CSM in the harmonic limit, and that including anharmonic terms in the TRVM could resolve discrepancies in branching ratios for 126Xe and 130Ba. The results enable future work on whether the first excited 0⁺ band in these nuclei should be interpreted as a gamma band (TRVM) or a beta band (CSM).
