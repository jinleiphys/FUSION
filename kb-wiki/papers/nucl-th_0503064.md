# Relativistic Harmonic Oscillator with Spin Symmetry

> The eigenfunctions and eigenenergies for a Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials are derived. Equal scalar and vector potentials may be applicable to the spectrum of an antinucleion imbedded in a nucleus. Triaxial, axially deformed, and spherical oscillator potentials are considered. The spectrum has a spin symmetry for all cases and, for the spherical harmonic oscillator potential, a higher symmetry analogous to the SU(3) symmetry of the non-relativistic harmonic oscillator is discussed.

# Relativistic Harmonic Oscillator with Spin Symmetry

## Key claim

The Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials (triaxial, axially deformed, and spherical) is exactly solvable and exhibits spin symmetry for all shapes, but the claim that this applies to antinucleon spectra is hedged: the author states that equal scalar and vector potentials *may* be applicable to the spectrum of an antinucleon imbedded in a nucleus, and that reliable estimates require inclusion of the antinucleon annihilation potential, which is not treated here.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector potentials by converting the first-order Dirac equations into a second-order differential equation for the upper Dirac component, which reduces to an energy-dependent Schrodinger equation without spin dependence. For triaxial, axially deformed, and spherical harmonic oscillator potentials, the equation separates and yields analytic eigenfunctions in terms of Hermite polynomials (Cartesian) or Laguerre polynomials (cylindrical and spherical coordinates), with the lower components obtained via gradient operations on the upper component. The eigenenergies are obtained by solving a cubic equation derived from the separation conditions, with the normalization factor computed from the full four-component wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical harmonic oscillator fit to an antiproton outside 16O: C = 1.33, M-tilde = 252 MeV, V_V^0 = -677 MeV (parameters from relativistic mean field approximation, Thomas reference)
- The ratio of lower-component probability to upper-component probability for the spherical oscillator is R_k = (E-tilde_k - M-tilde) / [2 (E-tilde_k + M-tilde)]; for the most bound antinucleon states this probability is about 20%, compared to about 1% for nucleons inside a nucleus
- For small oscillator strength (A small), the binding energy approximates E_n1,n2,n3 - M-tilde ≈ sum_i omega_i (n_i + 1/2) + V_V^0 (non-relativistic limit)
- For large oscillator strength, the spectrum scales as E ~ M-tilde * A^{2/3} (relativistic asymptotic regime)
- The spherical oscillator degeneracy is (N+2)(N+1) per level; the axially deformed oscillator degeneracy is 2(N-n_3)+1 for even (N-n_3) and 2(N-n_3+1) for odd (N-n_3)
- The deformation parameter used for the axially deformed example is delta = 0.33, giving C_perp = 1.49, C_3 = 1.05

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the spherical relativistic harmonic oscillator with spin symmetry (Ravndal 1982, Bahdri 1985, Tegen 1985, Centelles 2000, Kukulin 2001, Chao 1993) and pseudospin symmetry (Chen 2003, Manuel 2003) by deriving analytic eigenfunctions and eigenenergies for triaxial and axially deformed harmonic oscillators as well. The results are motivated by theoretical suggestions (Thomas 2005) that antinucleon lifetimes in nuclei could be enhanced, and by self-consistent Hartree calculations (Dirk 2003, Rein 2004) indicating that scalar and vector potentials for antinucleons are approximately equal. The analytic solutions enable future studies of antinucleon spectra in deformed nuclei and provide a testbed for relativistic mean-field calculations, though the author notes that the annihilation potential must be included for reliable observability estimates.
