# Inference of Parameters for Back-shifted Fermi Gas Model using Feedback Neural Network

> The back-shifted Fermi gas model is widely employed for calculating nuclear level density (NLD) as it can effectively reproduce experimental data by adjusting parameters. However, selecting parameters for nuclei lacking experimental data poses a challenge. In this study, the feedforward neural network (FNN) was utilized to learn the level density parameters at neutron separation energy $a(S_{n})$ and the energy shift $\varDelta$ for 289 nuclei. Simultaneously, parameters for nearly 3000 nuclei are provided through the FNN. Using these parameters, calculations were performed for neutron resonance spacing in $s$ and $p$ waves, cumulative number of levels, and NLD. The FNN results were also compared with the calculated outcomes of the parameters from fitting experimental data (local parameters) and those obtained from systematic studies (global parameters), as well as the experimental data. The results indicate that parameters from the FNN achieve performance comparable to local parameters in reproducing experimental data. Moreover, for extrapolated nuclei, parameters from the FNN still offer a robust description of experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a feedforward neural network (FNN) can learn and extrapolate the level density parameter at neutron separation energy \(a(S_n)\) and the energy shift \(\Delta\) for the back-shifted Fermi gas model, achieving performance comparable to parameters fitted directly to experimental data (local parameters) and superior to systematic global parameters, but only for nuclei near the training set; the FNN's extrapolation quality is not guaranteed for nuclei far from the training data.

## Method

The authors trained separate FNNs on the level density parameter \(a(S_n)\) and the energy shift \(\Delta\) for 289 nuclei, using proton number \(Z\), neutron number \(N\), distances to nearest magic numbers, and (for \(a(S_n)\) only) neutron separation energy \(S_n\) as inputs. The \(\Delta\) dataset was split into three classes (even-even, odd-odd, odd-A) and each was trained independently. After 1000 training repetitions, the 100 sets with smallest mean square error were averaged to produce final parameter predictions, which were then used in the back-shifted Fermi gas model to compute neutron resonance spacings, cumulative level counts, and nuclear level densities.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation for \(a(S_n)\): FNN gives 0.67, global parameters give 1.18 (289 nuclei).
- Root mean square deviation factor \(f_{\text{rms}}\) for \(s\)-wave neutron resonance spacing \(D_0\) over all 288 nuclei: FNN = 1.87, local parameters = 1.76, global parameters = 2.22.
- For odd-A nuclei specifically, \(f_{\text{rms}}\) for \(D_0\): FNN = 2.04, local parameters = 2.05, global parameters = 2.59.
- Root mean square deviation factor \(f_{\text{rms}}\) for \(p\)-wave neutron resonance spacing \(D_1\) over 116 nuclei: FNN = 1.76, local parameters = 1.79, global parameters = 1.92.
- For 12 nuclei outside the training set with \(D_0\) data from RIPL-3, FNN and global parameters both yield \(f_{\text{rms}} = 1.98\).
- FNN provides parameters for 2882 nuclei beyond the training set, covering \(Z\) from 2 to 118 and \(N\) from 1 to 177.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of Koning et al. (2008), who provided local parameters for 289 nuclei and global parameters for general use in the back-shifted Fermi gas model. It also builds on the machine-learning approach of Ozdogan (2021), who used Bayesian neural networks to train the level density parameter \(a\) for the constant temperature model, back-shifted Fermi gas model, and generalized superfluid model but did not include energy shifts or perform extrapolation. The present FNN method enables parameter prediction for thousands of nuclei lacking experimental data, which can be used in Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations for nuclear reactions relevant to nucleosynthesis and fission yield assessment.
