# Low-energy neutron-12C analyzing powers: Results from a multichannel algebraic scattering theory

> Analyzing powers in low-energy neutron scattering from 12C are calculated in an algebraic momentum-space coupled-channel formalism (MCAS). The results are compared with recently obtained experimental data. The channel-coupling potentials have been defined previously to reproduce the total cross section and sub-threshold bound states of the compound system. Without further adjustment, good agreement with data for the analyzing powers is obtained.

## Key claim

The multichannel algebraic scattering theory (MCAS) reproduces recently measured analyzing powers for neutron-12C scattering without any parameter adjustment, but only for neutron laboratory energies up to 4 MeV; beyond that energy, higher-lying states of the target nucleus beyond the three included may be necessary.

## Method

The MCAS formalism solves the coupled-channel scattering problem in momentum space, using a rotational model potential with quadrupole deformation β2 = -0.52 to couple the incoming neutron to three states of 12C (ground 0+, first excited 2+ at 4.389 MeV, and second excited 0+ at 7.6542 MeV). Coupling is taken to second order, and the potential includes central, spin-orbit, orbit-orbit, and spin-spin terms, with closed-shell Pauli blocking effects incorporated via orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials with 1000 MeV couplings. The complete S-matrix is obtained, from which differential cross sections and analyzing powers are extracted using standard formulas.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = -0.52 used in the rotational model coupling potential.
- Target excited states included: 2+ at 4.389 MeV and 0₂+ at 7.6542 MeV.
- Neutron laboratory energy range of the experimental data: 2.2 MeV to 8.5 MeV; MCAS results limited to a maximum of 4 MeV.
- Experimental spread in neutron laboratory energy: 0.2 to 0.4 MeV.
- Near the sharp (5/2)+ resonance at 2.1 MeV, theoretical curves at 2.10, 2.12, and 2.14 MeV differ by 20 keV from each other but show very different shapes.
- Data shown at two center-of-mass scattering angles: 43.36° and 147.15°.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach previously applied to neutron-12C total cross sections by Svenne, Amos, and collaborators (2003) and to proton-12C scattering by Pisent et al. (2005), now testing the model against new analyzing power measurements by Roper et al. (2005). The good agreement without parameter adjustment demonstrates predictive power and supports using the model as a starting point for phase shift analysis, with ongoing work extending the method to mass-seven and mass-fifteen systems.
