# Longitudinal Wobbling Motion in $^{187}$Au

> The rare phenomenon of nuclear wobbling motion has been investigated for the nucleus $^{187}$Au. A longitudinal wobbling-bands pair has been identified and clearly distinguished from the associated signature-partner band on the basis of angular distribution measurements. Theoretical calculations in the framework of the Particle Rotor Model (PRM) are found to agree well with the experimental observations. This is the first experimental evidence for longitudinal wobbling bands where the expected signature partner band has also been identified, and establishes this exotic collective mode as a general phenomenon over the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first experimental observation of longitudinal wobbling motion in a nucleus, clearly distinguished from the associated signature-partner band, in $^{187}$Au. The claim is hedged by the fact that the identification relies on angular distribution measurements showing the connecting transitions have 87%–93% E2 component, and that no higher-phonon-number wobbling bands (n$_{\omega}$ = 2 and above) were identified.

## Method

High-spin states in $^{187}$Au were populated via a $^{19}$F beam on an enriched $^{174}$Yb target at beam energies of 105 MeV and 115 MeV using the Gammasphere array at ATLAS (Argonne National Laboratory). Triple-coincidence $\gamma$-ray data (1.08 × 10$^{9}$ events) were analyzed with the RADWARE suite, and angular distribution measurements using Gammasphere's 17 angular rings were performed to extract mixing ratios and distinguish E2-dominated wobbling transitions from M1-dominated signature-partner transitions. Particle Rotor Model (PRM) calculations with deformation parameters β=0.23, γ=23° and pairing gap Δ=0.88 MeV were carried out for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Combined total of three- and higher-fold $\gamma$-ray coincidence events: 1.08 × 10$^{9}$
- Beam energies: 105 MeV (first run, 57 detectors) and 115 MeV (second run, 73 detectors)
- Mixing ratios for stretched E2 transitions in Yrast band: δ = -0.04(1) for 333.8-keV transition and δ = -0.03(1) for 413.7-keV transition
- LW → Yrast connecting transitions have 87%–93% E2 component
- SP → Yrast connecting transitions have ≈0.4% and 1.0% E2 admixture (265.3-keV and 436.5-keV transitions)
- PRM deformation parameters: β=0.23, γ=23°; pairing gap Δ=0.88 MeV; chemical potential λ=-1.32 MeV
- Moments of inertia of irrotational-flow type: $\mathcal{J}_0=38.0~\hbar^2/\textrm{MeV}$
- B(E2,13/2$^-$→9/2$^-$) = 1.49 (eb)$^2$ for $^{187}$Au; B(E2,2$^+$→0$^+$) = 0.84 (eb)$^2$ for $^{186}$Pt
- Measured B(E2)$_\text{out}$/B(E2)$_\text{in}$ ratios for LW → Yrast transitions up to ~0.7

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear wobbling motion, previously observed only as transverse wobbling (TW) in odd-A nuclei such as $^{105}$Pd (Timar), $^{135}$Pr (Matta), $^{133}$La (Sayani), $^{161}$Lu (Bringel), $^{163}$Lu (Odegard, Jensen), $^{165}$Lu (Schonwasser), $^{167}$Lu (Amro), and $^{167}$Ta (Hartley). The theoretical classification of longitudinal versus transverse wobbling was introduced by Frauendorf and Donau (transverse). This result opens the A~190 mass region for wobbling studies and establishes longitudinal wobbling as a general phenomenon across the nuclear chart, enabled by the identification of both the wobbling band and its signature partner band.
