# Baryon-Baryon Interactions in the Flavor SU(3) Limit from Full QCD Simulations on the Lattice

> We investigate baryon-baryon (BB) interactions in the 3-flavor full QCD simulations with degenerate quark masses for all flavors. The BB potentials in the orbital S-wave are extracted from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions measured on the lattice. We observe strong flavor-spin dependences of the BB potentials at short distances. In particular, a strong repulsive core exists in the flavor-octet and spin-singlet channel (the 8_s representation), while an attractive core appears in the flavor singlet channel (the 1 representation). We discuss a relation of such flavor-spin dependence with the Pauli exclusion principle in the quark level. Possible existence of an H-dibaryon resonance above the Lambda-Lambda threshold is also discussed.

## Key claim

In the flavor SU(3) limit with degenerate quark masses, the authors extract all six independent baryon-baryon S-wave potentials from lattice QCD and find that the flavor-octet spin-singlet (8_s) channel exhibits a very strong repulsive core, while the flavor-singlet (1) channel shows attraction at all distances; however, the results are obtained at heavy quark masses (pion masses of 835 and 1014 MeV) on a small lattice volume (approximately 2 fm), so the quantitative features may change at lighter quark masses and larger volumes.

## Method

The authors performed 3-flavor full QCD simulations on a 16^3 × 32 lattice with two sets of degenerate quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 835 MeV and 1014 MeV. They extracted the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for two-baryon systems in the flavor SU(3) limit from four-point correlation functions, then derived effective central potentials at leading order of the derivative expansion by solving the Schrodinger equation. The six independent potentials for irreducible flavor representations (27, 8_s, 1, 10^*, 10, 8_a) were obtained, and potentials in the baryon basis were reconstructed using SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 835(1) MeV and 1014(1) MeV, with corresponding octet baryon masses of 1752(3) MeV and 2026(3) MeV
- Lattice spacing: 0.121(2) fm, lattice size: 1.93(3) fm (16^3 × 32)
- Number of gauge configurations: 800 for m_pi = 835 MeV, 700 for m_pi = 1014 MeV
- Energies extracted from the condition that potentials approach zero at large r: E^(27) ≈ -5 MeV, E^(8_s) ≈ 25 MeV, E^(1) ≈ -30 MeV, E^(10^*) ≈ -10 MeV, E^(10) ≈ 0 MeV, E^(8_a) ≈ -15 MeV
- Solving the Schrodinger equation with V^(1)(r) and E^(1) = -30 MeV yields a shallow bound state in the flavor-singlet channel

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's method for extracting baryon-baryon potentials from lattice QCD, previously applied to NN systems (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2010), Xi N and Lambda N systems (Nemura 2008, 2009), to a systematic study of all octet baryon interactions in the flavor SU(3) limit. The results are qualitatively consistent with phenomenological quark models (Oka 2000) and support the quark Pauli blocking mechanism for the repulsive core originally proposed by Otsuki (1965). The reconstructed potentials in the baryon basis, including off-diagonal coupled-channel terms, provide input for future studies of hypernuclei and neutron stars, and the attractive flavor-singlet potential suggests a possible H-dibaryon resonance above the Lambda-Lambda threshold that may relate to the enhancement observed in the KEK experiment (Yoon 2007).
