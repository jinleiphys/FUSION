# Modern Shell-Model Calculations

> We have performed shell-model calculations starting from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon NN potential and making use of a new approach for the calculation of the effective interaction. This consists in deriving from the NN potential a low-momentum effective potential V-low-k, defined within a given cut-off momentum Lambda. Being a smooth potential it can be used directly in nuclear structure calculations. To assess the practical value of this approach, we have also performed calculations within the framework of the usual Brueckner G-matrix formalism. Comparison of the calculated results between them and with the experimental data shows that the V-low-k approach represents an advantageous alternative to the use of the G-matrix.

# Modern Shell-Model Calculations

## Key claim

The low-momentum effective potential \(V_{low-k}\) derived from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential provides an advantageous alternative to the traditional Brueckner \(G\)-matrix formalism for realistic shell-model calculations, yielding results that are slightly better than those from the \(G\)-matrix when compared to experimental data for nuclei near \(^{132}\)Sn.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for the \(N=82\) isotones \(^{134}\)Te, \(^{135}\)I, and \(^{136}\)Xe, assuming \(^{132}\)Sn as a closed core with valence protons occupying the five single-particle orbits \(0g_{7/2}\), \(1d_{5/2}\), \(1d_{3/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), and \(h_{11/2}\). They derived the effective interaction using both the Brueckner \(G\)-matrix formalism (with a Tsai-Kuo matrix inversion method and folded-diagram series summed via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method) and the \(V_{low-k}\) approach (with a cut-off momentum \(\Lambda = 2.05\) fm\(^{-1}\)), both starting from the CD-Bonn free \(NN\) potential. The shell-model calculations were carried out using the OXBASH code.

## Key numbers

* The rms deviation \(\sigma\) between calculated and experimental energy levels for the \(V_{low-k}\) approach is 110 keV for \(^{134}\)Te, 73 keV for \(^{135}\)I, and 111 keV for \(^{136}\)Xe.
* The rms deviation \(\sigma\) for the \(G\)-matrix calculations is 163 keV for \(^{134}\)Te, 101 keV for \(^{135}\)I, and 154 keV for \(^{136}\)Xe.
* The cut-off momentum used for \(V_{low-k}\) is \(\Lambda = 2.05\) fm\(^{-1}\), with results practically insensitive to changes within the range 1.9–2.2 fm\(^{-1}\).
* The shell-model oscillator parameter is \(\hbar \omega = 7.88\) MeV, obtained from the expression \(\hbar \omega = 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}\) for \(A = 132\).
* The CD-Bonn potential fits the world \(NN\) data below 350 MeV with a \(\chi^2\)/datum of 1.02.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous realistic shell-model studies of nuclei around doubly magic \(^{100}\)Sn, \(^{132}\)Sn, and \(^{208}\)Pb (Covello et al., 2001), which had used the Bonn-A potential and the \(G\)-matrix formalism. The new \(V_{low-k}\) approach, proposed by Kuo et al. (2000), avoids the model-space dependence and complexity of the \(G\)-matrix while producing comparable or better agreement with experiment. The results enable further nuclear structure studies using the \(V_{low-k}\) method, which is also being applied in other fields such as Fermi liquid theory for nuclear matter (Brown, 2001).
