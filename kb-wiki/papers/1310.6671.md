# Probing eigenfunction nonorthogonality by parametric shifts of resonance widths

> Recently, it has been shown that the change of resonance widths in an open system under a perturbation of its interior is a sensitive indicator of the nonorthogonality of resonance states. We apply this measure to quantify parametric motion of the resonances. In particular, a strong redistribution of the widths is linked with the maximal degree of nonorthogonality. Then for weakly open chaotic systems we discuss the effect of spectral rigidity on the statistical properties of the parametric width shifts, and derive the distribution of the latter in a picket-fence model with equidistant spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that parametric shifts of resonance widths under internal perturbations serve as a sensitive measure of eigenfunction nonorthogonality, with the key hedge being that this measure specifically probes the *off-diagonal* elements of the Bell-Steinberger nonorthogonality matrix, not the diagonal Petermann factor.

## Method

The authors model open quantum systems using an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian and analyze the first-order perturbation theory result for resonance shifts under a Hermitian perturbation of the interior. For the two-level case, they provide an exact analytic solution parameterized by a complex mixing parameter f, deriving explicit expressions for parametric width velocities. For weakly open chaotic systems with preserved time-reversal symmetry, they perform statistical modeling using random matrix theory (GOE) and a picket-fence model with equidistant spectrum, averaging over Gaussian-distributed wavefunctions and coupling amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For the two-level system, the parametric width velocity is given by \(\dot\Gamma_1 = 4\mathrm{Re}f\frac{v(1-|f|^2)-2d\,\mathrm{Im}f}{(1+|f|^2)^2-4\mathrm{Re}f^2} = -\dot\Gamma_2\), where f is the complex mixing parameter
- For the picket-fence model with equidistant spectrum, the variance of the rescaled width velocity y is \(\mathrm{var}(y) = M/3\), where M is the number of open channels
- The distribution function for the picket-fence model is \(\phi^{\mathrm{(pf)}}(y) = \frac{\pi}{2[1+ \cosh(\pi y)]}\)
- For the GOE case, the distribution function is \(\phi^{\mathrm{(goe)}}(y) = \frac{4 + y^2}{6(1+y^2)^{5/2}}\), yielding a power-law tail \(\mathcal{P}^{\mathrm{(goe)}}_M(y) \propto |y|^{-3}\)
- The Porter-Thomas distribution for widths in units of mean partial width is \(P_M(\kappa) = \frac{1}{2^{M/2}\Gamma(M/2)}\kappa^{M/2-1}e^{-\kappa/2}\)

## Context

This work extends the recent connection established by Fyodorov and Savin (2012) between parametric width shifts and nonorthogonality, providing explicit analytic results for the two-level system and statistical analysis for chaotic systems. The results complement earlier studies by Poli et al. (2009) on diagonal elements of the nonorthogonality matrix (Petermann factors), now providing a complete description including off-diagonal elements. The approach enables probing spatial characteristics of resonance states through purely spectroscopic measurements, applicable to microwave cavities, optical microstructures, and elastic plates.
