# Color transparency in hard $pd$ collisions

> As one of the predictions of perturbative QCD, the effect of color transparency has been the focus of attention in the community studying modifications of hadrons in nuclear medium for several decades. The search for this effect in reactions involving heavy nuclei can be complicated by uncertainties in nuclear characteristics (nucleon density distributions and wave functions), which can affect the interpretation of experiments. In this work, we consider the reaction $d(p,pp)n$ at $p_{\rm lab}=15$ GeV/c caused by hard elastic $pp$ scattering, in which these uncertainties are actually reduced to the behavior of the deuteron wave function at large momenta. It is shown that for transverse momenta of the spectator neutron $\leq 0.4$ GeV/c the choice of the deuteron wave function cannot affect the identification of the color transparency effect. A simple method for studying color transparency in $dd$ collisions is also suggested based on the identification of quasi-free $pd$ interactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the reaction \(d(p,pp)n\) at \(p_{\rm lab}=15\) GeV/c, the uncertainty in the deuteron wave function does not affect the identification of color transparency for spectator neutron transverse momenta \(\leq 0.4\) GeV/c, but at higher transverse momenta the wave-function uncertainty becomes comparable to the color transparency effect.

## Method

The author uses the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to compute the invariant amplitude for the \(pd \to ppn\) reaction, including impulse approximation (IA) diagrams and coherently adding amplitudes with single and double rescattering on the spectator neutron. Color transparency effects are introduced via the quantum diffusion model, where the interaction cross section of fast hadrons with nucleons grows linearly from a small value proportional to \(|t|^{-1}\) to the normal hadronic cross section over a coherence length \(l_c = 2p/\Delta M^2\). The calculations are performed in the deuteron rest frame with the proton beam momentum along the \(z\)-axis, using the Paris potential (D-wave fraction \(P_D=5.8\%\)) and the CD-Bonn potential (\(P_D=4.9\%\)) for the deuteron wave function, with \(\Delta M^2 = 0.7\) and \(3\) GeV\(^2\) to bracket the color transparency uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam momentum: \(p_{\rm lab}=15\) GeV/c, corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.47\) GeV.
- Transverse momenta of the spectator neutron: \(p_{st} \leq 0.4\) GeV/c is the region where the deuteron wave function choice does not affect color transparency identification.
- Coherence length parameter values used: \(\Delta M^2 = 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) and \(3\) GeV\(^2\).
- D-wave fractions: Paris potential \(P_D=5.8\%\); CD-Bonn potential \(P_D=4.9\%\).
- The hard elastic \(pp\) scattering is at center-of-mass angle \(\Theta_{c.m.}=90^\circ\), with \(t=(4m^2-s)/2\).

## Context

This work extends the GEA model of Frankfurt (1996) and the author's own previous calculations (Larionov 2022) for the \(d(p,2p)n\) reaction, which were performed at \(p_{\rm lab}=6-75\) GeV/c and included interference of quark configurations following Ralston (1988). The present study addresses the sensitivity of observables to the deuteron wave function choice, a concern raised by the earlier work where sensitivity to color transparency effects was largest at \(p_{st}=0.2-0.4\) GeV/c. The paper also generalizes the model to \(dd\) collisions by identifying quasi-free \(pd\) interactions when one spectator neutron has small transverse momentum (\(\lesssim 0.1\) GeV/c), enabling color transparency studies at the NICA SPD experiment.
