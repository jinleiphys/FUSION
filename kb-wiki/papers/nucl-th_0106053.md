# pi^0 -> gamma gamma*, omega -> pi^0 gamma*, and rho -> pi gamma* decays in nuclear medium

> We calculate the medium modification of the pi-omega-rho vertex and analyze its significance for the pi^0 -> gamma gamma*, omega -> pi^0 gamma*, and rho -> pi gamma* decays in nuclear matter. We use a relativistic hadronic approach at zero temperature and in the leading-density limit, and consider decays of particles at rest with respect to the medium. It is shown that for the pi^0 -> gamma gamma* the effects of the Delta isobar cancel almost exactly the effects of the nucleon ph excitations, such that the net medium effect is small. On the contrary, for the decays omega -> pi^0 gamma* and rho -> pi gamma* we find a sizeable increase of the partial widths at virtualities of the photon in the range 0.3-0.6 GeV. The effect has direct significance for the calculation of dilepton yields from the Dalitz decays in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the Dalitz decays of vector mesons (ω → π⁰γ* and ρ → πγ*) in nuclear matter at saturation density, the partial widths increase sizeably (by a factor of 2 for ω and 3 for ρ) at photon virtualities around 0.4 GeV, whereas for π⁰ → γγ* the net medium effect is small (at most a few percent) due to an almost exact cancellation between nucleon particle-hole and Δ-isobar contributions.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic hadronic model with meson, nucleon, and Δ(1232)-isobar degrees of freedom, working at zero temperature and in the leading-density limit. They evaluate the in-medium πωρ vertex from the diagrams of Fig. 1 (nucleon loop, Δ intermediate states) using the density part of the in-medium nucleon propagator, with the decaying particle at rest with respect to the medium. The calculation is performed at nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, and the effective coupling g_eff is defined as g_eff = g_{πρω} + ρ₀ B, where B is the medium contribution.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³), the partial width for ω → π⁰γ* is enhanced by a factor of 2 at photon virtualities around 0.4 GeV.
- At nuclear saturation density, the partial width for ρ → πγ* is enhanced by a factor of 3 at photon virtualities around 0.4 GeV.
- For π⁰ → γγ* at nuclear saturation density, |g_eff/g_vac|² = 0.99 (with Δ width Γ_Δ = 120 MeV), indicating a net medium effect of only about 1%.
- The individual contributions to the medium constant B for π⁰ → γγ* are: B_(a) = 97 GeV⁻³ (nucleons only), B_(b) = -(2.6 + 1.5i) GeV⁻³, B_(c) = -(87 + 42i) GeV⁻³ (with Γ_Δ = 120 MeV).
- The vacuum πωρ coupling constant used for vector meson decays is g_{ρωπ} = -1.13, inferred from the experimental partial width of ω → π⁰γ.
- The Δ width used is Γ_Δ = 120 MeV; the pole in the Δ amplitudes for Γ_Δ = 0 occurs at M ≈ 0.34 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium hadronic three-point functions, following prior investigations of the ρππ coupling which was found to be largely increased in nuclear matter (Song 1998, Urban 2000). The results have direct significance for dilepton production calculations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the low-mass dilepton enhancement seen in the CERES and HELIOS experiments. The enhancement of vector meson Dalitz decays (by factors of 2-3) in the 0.4-0.5 GeV region may help resolve the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical calculations using vacuum vertex values and experimental data, while the negligible modification of π⁰ Dalitz decays preserves the good agreement in the low-mass (up to 0.2 GeV) region.
