# Roles of chiral three-nucleon forces in nucleon-nucleus scattering

> We investigate the roles of chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) in nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering, using the standard framework based on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method for nuclear matter and the $g$-matrix folding model for the nucleon-nucleus scattering. In nuclear matter, chiral 3NF at NNLO level (mainly the 2$π$-exchange diagram) makes the single particle potential less attractive for the singlet-even channel and more absorptive for the triplet channels. The single-particle potential calculated from chiral two-nucleon force (2NF) at N$^{3}$LO level is found to be close to that from Bonn-B 2NF. The Melbourne $g$-matrix interaction is a practical effective interaction constructed by localizing the $g$-matrices calculated from Bonn-B 2NF. We then introduce the chiral-3NF effects to the local Melbourne $g$-matrix interaction. For nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering on various targets at 65 MeV, chiral 3NF makes the folding potential less attractive and more absorptive. The novel property for the imaginary part is originated in the enhancement of tensor correlations due to chiral 3NF. The two effects are small for differential cross sections and vector analyzing powers at the forward and middle angles where the experimental data are available. If backward measurements are done, the data will reveal the effects of chiral 3NF.

## Key claim

Chiral three-nucleon forces (3NF) at NNLO level make the folding potential for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering less attractive and more absorptive, but these effects are small for differential cross sections and vector analyzing powers at forward and middle angles where experimental data exist; the effects would only become clearly visible if backward-angle measurements were performed.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using chiral N³LO two-nucleon force (2NF) plus NNLO 3NF with a cutoff of 550 MeV, deriving an effective 2NF from the 3NF by averaging over the third nucleon in the Fermi sea. They then introduced chiral-3NF effects into the local Melbourne g-matrix interaction by multiplying each spin-isospin channel of the g-matrix by the ratio of the single-particle potential calculated from chiral 2NF+3NF to that from chiral 2NF alone. The modified g-matrix was folded with target densities to produce optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering at 65 MeV on targets from ¹²C to ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-3NF effects become significant in the density region k_F ≳ 1.2 fm⁻¹, corresponding to ρ ≳ 0.7ρ₀ where ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ (normal density, k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹)
- At E = 65 MeV and ρ = ρ₀, the imaginary part of the single-particle potential differs by about a factor of 2 compared to the second-order perturbation results of Holt et al.
- The chiral 3NF enhances reaction cross sections σ_R by only a few percent for proton scattering at 65 MeV on targets from ¹²C to ²⁰⁸Pb
- The spin-orbit part of the g-matrix is enhanced by chiral 3NF at most by a factor of 4/3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral 3NF effects in nuclear matter (Kohno 2012, Kohno 2013) to nucleon-nucleus scattering for the first time, using the Melbourne g-matrix folding model that was previously successful in reproducing experimental data without phenomenological adjustments (Amos). The approach differs from earlier 3NF studies using the CEG07 g-matrix or AV18 2NF plus phenomenological 3NFs (Rafael 2013), which adjusted potentials to fit measured cross sections, whereas this work introduces chiral-3NF effects through a parameter-free ratio method. The results enable future experimental tests through backward-angle measurements and provide a foundation for incorporating chiral 3NF consistently in reaction calculations.
