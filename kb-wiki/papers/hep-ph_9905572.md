# First-order chiral phase transition may naturally lead to the ``quenched'' initial condition and strong soft-pion fields

> We propose a novel mechanism for DCC formation in a first-order chiral phase transition. In this case the effective potential for the chiral order parameter has a local minimum at $Φ\sim 0$ in which the chiral field can be ``trapped''. If the expansion is sufficiently fast a bubble of disoriented chiral field can emerge and decouple from the rest of the fireball. The bubble may overshoot the mixed phase and subsequently supercool until the barrier disappears, when the potential resembles that at T=0. This situation corresponds to the initial condition realized in a ``quench''. Thus, the subsequent alignment in the vacuum direction leads to strong amplification of low momentum modes of the pion field. We propose that these DCCs could accompany the previously suggested baryon rapidity fluctuations.

# First-order chiral phase transition may naturally lead to the "quenched" initial condition and strong soft-pion fields

## Key claim

If the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions is first-order rather than second-order or a smooth crossover, a decoupled bubble of disoriented chiral field can supercool until the effective potential resembles that at T=0, naturally realizing the "quenched" initial condition that previous dynamical simulations had shown does not emerge naturally for second-order or crossover transitions.

## Method

The authors solve the Euler-Lagrange equations of motion for the chiral fields (sigma and pions) in the linear sigma model coupled to a heat-bath of quarks, using a second-order leap-frog algorithm for the fields and the RHLLE algorithm for the ideal fluid energy-momentum tensor. The coupling constant g=5.5 is chosen to produce a first-order chiral phase transition, and the evolution is followed from an initial condition near the local minimum at Phi~0 with an initial expansion scalar of about 1/(2 fm/c).

## Key numbers

- The bubble supercools by about 15% before the barrier to the global minimum almost disappears (temperature drops from T=130 MeV to T=100 MeV)
- The pion field is amplified by more than a factor of 100, producing about 18 pi0 mesons and 3 sigma mesons for an initial bubble radius of 4 fm
- The time-scale to reach the spinodal region is approximately 2 fm/c
- The local expansion rate in heavy-ion collisions is roughly 10^20 times larger than that of the universe at spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking
- The quenched initial condition yields similar results: 21 pi0's and 4 sigma's for the same initial radius

## Context

This work extends the DCC formation mechanism proposed by Heiselberg and Jackson (1999) and Mishustin (1999), who studied bubble decoupling and supercooling in first-order phase transitions. It contrasts with earlier dynamical simulations by Randrup (1996) and Mishustin and Scavenius (1997) which showed that the "quench" does not emerge naturally for second-order or crossover transitions. The mechanism enables a natural connection between DCC formation and the baryon rapidity fluctuations previously suggested by Heiselberg and Jackson (1999) and Mishustin (1999), with the advantage that DCC pions decay at much lower temperature and density of the heat-bath, potentially making them more detectable.
