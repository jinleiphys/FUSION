# Low Energy Probes of Physics Beyond the Standard Model

> Low-energy tests of fundamental symmetries and studies of neutrino properties provide a powerful window on physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM). In this article, we provide a basic theoretical framework for a subsequent set of articles that review the progress and opportunities in various aspects of the low-energy program. We illustrate the physics reach of different low-energy probes in terms of an effective BSM mass scale and illustrate how this reach matches and, in some cases, even exceeds that accessible at the high energy frontier.

## Key claim

Low-energy tests of fundamental symmetries and neutrino properties can probe physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM) at effective mass scales that match or exceed those accessible at the high-energy frontier, provided that Standard Model predictions are sufficiently reliable and experimental precision is adequate.

## Method

The paper develops a theoretical framework based on effective field theory, where BSM effects are parameterized by operators of mass dimension six or lower containing Standard Model fields, with Wilson coefficients encoding new dynamics at a BSM scale Λ taken to be well above the weak scale v = 246 GeV. The authors review the Standard Model Lagrangian and its renormalization, then analyze how electroweak radiative corrections impact precision observables in charged-current processes (such as muon decay and beta decay) and neutral-current processes, using the modified minimal subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- The weak scale is v = 246 GeV, taken as the scale below which BSM dynamics are integrated out
- Electroweak precision observables agree with Standard Model predictions generally at the 10^{-3} level or better
- The muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy from the E821 Collaboration is at the greater-than-3-sigma level
- The GUT scale is approximately 10^{16} GeV, where Standard Model running couplings show a "near miss" for unification
- Dirac Yukawa couplings for neutrinos must have magnitude less than about 10^{-12} to be consistent with cosmological upper bounds on neutrino masses
- Electroweak radiative corrections generically arise at the O(alpha/pi) level

## Context

This paper provides the introductory theoretical framework for a subsequent set of articles reviewing progress and opportunities in the low-energy fundamental symmetries program, following the workshop "Beyond the Standard Model in Nuclear Physics" held at the University of Wisconsin-Madison in October 2011. It extends earlier reviews including Erler (2004), Ramsey-Musolf (2006) on supersymmetry applications, Pospelov (2005) on EDMs, and Avignone (2007) on neutrinoless double beta decay, while providing an updated and broader treatment that connects low-energy probes to the high-energy frontier. The framework enables systematic interpretation of rare and forbidden processes, precision tests, and cosmological/astrophysical probes in terms of an effective BSM mass scale.
