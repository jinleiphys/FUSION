# Inclusive Photoproduction of $η$ Mesons on Nuclei and the in-medium properties of the S$_{11}$ Resonance

> A relativistic non-local model for the inclusive photoproduction of $η$ mesons from complex nuclei is introduced. The model is based on the dominance of the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance. We compare the results of our calculations with the available data on inclusive cross sections for the nuclei C, Al and Cu. Assuming the resonance propagates freely in the nuclear medium, we find that the calculated angular distribution and energy dependence of the cross sections reproduce the data in a reasonable fashion. The present non-local model allows the inclusion of density dependent mass and width in the calculations. Including these in the calculations reveals that the presently available data do not show clear preference for the inclusion of such modifications of the properties of the S$_{11}$(1535) in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the available inclusive photoproduction data on C, Al, and Cu do not show clear evidence for medium modifications of the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance mass and width, despite the model being able to incorporate such density-dependent effects; the data can accommodate only much weaker changes than those suggested for the nucleon mass reduction.

## Method

A relativistic non-local model for inclusive $\eta$ photoproduction on finite nuclei was developed, based on the dominance of the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance in the second resonance region. The model uses an effective Lagrangian approach and relativistic mean-field nuclear dynamics, with the s-channel treated non-locally to allow inclusion of density-dependent mass and width parameters for the resonance, while the u-channel is treated in a local approximation. The inclusive cross section is obtained by numerically integrating the exclusive cross section over the phase space of the final nucleon and summing over all energy levels of the target nucleus, with final state interactions of the $\eta$ meson included via an optical potential.

## Key numbers

- Free S$_{11}$ resonance mass and width used: 1535 MeV and 160 MeV, respectively.
- Density-dependent mass reduction parameter b = 0.14 (from quark-meson coupling model) leads to cross sections much below the data; a much smaller value b = 0.014 gives relatively small effects.
- Density-dependent width broadening parameter c = 50 MeV (from Lehr and Mosel (2003)) leads to suppression of cross sections away from the data; a smaller value c = 25 MeV also lowers cross sections.
- For carbon at E$_\gamma$ = 820 MeV, the S$_{11}$ contribution dominates, with contributions from D$_{13}$, nucleon pole terms, and meson exchange diagrams at the few percent level.
- Calculations cover photon energies from near threshold to 1.1 GeV for targets C, Al, and Cu.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic non-local model for exclusive quasifree $\eta$ photoproduction (Hedayati-Poor and Sherif (2003, 2004)) to inclusive reactions, and improves upon their earlier local model (Hedayati-Poor and Sherif (1997)). It compares with data from three experimental groups: Roebig-Landau et al. (1996) at MAMI (Mainz), Yorita et al. (2000) at KEK, and Kinoshita et al. (2005) at LNS. The analysis uses density-dependent forms for mass suggested by Saito and Thomas (1995) and for width by Lehr, Post, and Mosel (2003). The results are consistent with the general conclusions of Krusche (2004), Kinoshita et al. (2005), Lehr and Mosel (2003), and Maruyama and Chiba (2004) that medium modifications of the S$_{11}$ resonance, if they exist, are likely rather small. The paper identifies uncertainties in the $\eta$-nucleus optical potential as a limiting factor for drawing firm conclusions.
