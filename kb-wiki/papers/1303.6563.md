# Combining Total Monte Carlo and Benchmarks for nuclear data uncertainty propagation on an LFRs safety parameters

> Analyses are carried out to assess the impact of nuclear data uncertainties on keff for the European Lead Cooled Training Reactor (ELECTRA) using the Total Monte Carlo method. A large number of Pu-239 random ENDF-formated libraries generated using the TALYS based system were processed into ACE format with NJOY99.336 code and used as input into the Serpent Monte Carlo neutron transport code to obtain distribution in keff. The keff distribution obtained was compared with the latest major nuclear data libraries - JEFF-3.1.2, ENDF/B-VII.1 and JENDL-4.0. A method is proposed for the selection of benchmarks for specific applications using the Total Monte Carlo approach. Finally, an accept/reject criterion was investigated based on chi square values obtained using the Pu-239 Jezebel criticality benchmark. It was observed that nuclear data uncertainties in keff were reduced considerably from 748 to 443 pcm by applying a more rigid acceptance criteria for accepting random files.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that applying an accept/reject criterion based on chi-square values from the Pu-239 Jezebel criticality benchmark can reduce nuclear data uncertainties in keff for the ELECTRA lead-cooled fast reactor from 748 to 443 pcm, but this reduction is conditional on the specific benchmark and the rigidness of the acceptance threshold chosen.

## Method

The authors used the Total Monte Carlo method with 740 random Pu-239 ENDF-formatted libraries generated by the TALYS-based system, processed into ACE format with NJOY99.336 at 1200K, and input into the Serpent Monte Carlo neutron transport code (version 1.1.17) to obtain a distribution in keff for the ELECTRA reactor. They compared the mean keff distribution with results from JEFF-3.1.2, ENDF/B-VII.1, and JENDL-4.0 libraries, and investigated a correlation-based method for selecting benchmarks by plotting keff values for ELECTRA against those for the Pu-239 Jezebel benchmark. An accept/reject criterion was applied by setting limiting chi-square values based on the Jezebel benchmark results and recalculating the nuclear data uncertainty for the accepted random files.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear data uncertainty in keff for ELECTRA was reduced from 748 pcm to 443 pcm by applying a more rigid acceptance criterion with a minimum limiting chi-square of 5.31e-06
- The mean keff from the random files was 1.001540 ± 0.00021, compared with ENDF/B-VII.1 at 1.00067 ± 0.00021, JEFF-3.1.1 at 1.00023 ± 0.00022, and JENDL-4.0 at 1.00043 ± 0.00021
- The nuclear data uncertainty was determined to be 745 ± 19 pcm from the full set of 740 random files
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between keff for ELECTRA and the Pu-239 Jezebel benchmark was R = 0.84
- Criticality calculations used 20 inactive and 500 active cycles with 50,000 neutrons per cycle (25 million histories), yielding a statistical uncertainty in keff of 21.5 pcm

## Context

This work extends the Total Monte Carlo methodology developed by Koning and Rochman (2006) and tested on various systems by Rochman (2007) to the specific case of the European Lead Cooled Training Reactor (ELECTRA), a 0.5 MW lead-cooled fast reactor proposed within the GENIUS framework. The paper introduces a novel approach for benchmark selection based on correlation analysis rather than the traditional "by eye" judgment, and demonstrates that integral benchmark constraints can significantly reduce nuclear data uncertainties in reactor safety parameters. This enables more rigorous, automated benchmark selection for specific reactor applications and opens possibilities for assigning weighted factors to benchmarks based on correlation coefficients, as subsequently applied to Am-241 in burnup evaluation of ELECTRA by Sjostrand (unpublished).
