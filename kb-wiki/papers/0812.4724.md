# Hadronization via Recombination

> The recombination model as a model for hadronization from a quark-gluon plasma has been recently revived since it has advantages in explaining several important features of the final state produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, such as the constituent quark number scaling of the elliptic coefficient versus the transverse energy of identified hadrons, the bending shape of the $p_T$ spectrum of hadrons near 5 GeV/c, and the measured large value of baryon to meson ratio(of the order of unity) in the same $p_T$ range. We have developed a dynamic simulation model of heavy-ion collisions in which a quark-gluon plasma, starting from a certain initial condition, evolves hydrodynamically until it reaches the phase boundary, and then hadronizes by valence quark recombination. Rescattering after hadronization is described by UrQMD. We discuss some details of the model and report first, preliminary results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamic simulation model for heavy-ion collisions where a quark-gluon plasma evolves hydrodynamically until it reaches the phase boundary, then hadronizes via valence quark recombination, with the caveat that the results shown are only first, preliminary results from 20 events and require many more events to reduce systematic error.

## Method

The authors use a three-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic program with a Lagrangian (comoving) grid to evolve the quark-gluon plasma from an initial condition until it reaches the critical temperature \(T_c\). At the phase boundary, hadronization is simulated via a sudden recombination model using Monte Carlo methods, where hadrons are created from fluid cells based on quark distributions and wave functions, with energy conservation enforced by summing cell energies within small rapidity intervals. After hadronization, the produced hadrons undergo rescattering described by the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) model.

## Key numbers

* Transverse momentum spectra \(dN/p_t dp_t\) vs. \(p_t\) for pions and protons are shown for central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon at RHIC, obtained from 20 events.
* The hadronization occurs at the critical temperature \(T_c\) of the quark-gluon plasma phase boundary.
* The baryon-to-meson ratio \(R(p/\pi) \sim 1\) near \(p_T \sim 2\) GeV is cited as experimental support for the recombination model.
* The transverse momentum spectrum shows bending behavior near \(p_T \sim 5\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the recombination model approach of Ko et al., who used a parton cascade for the initial partonic matter evolution, by instead using ideal hydrodynamics to evolve the quark-gluon plasma up to the phase boundary. The model enables future calculation of elliptic flow coefficients for various hadrons to study how flow anisotropy relates to initial momentum anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma, and comparison with the statistical model is noted as an interesting next step.
