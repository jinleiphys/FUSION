# Pion off-shell electromagnetic form factors: data extraction and model analysis

> We investigate the pion electromagnetic half off-shell form factors, which parametrize the matrix element of the charged pion electromagnetic current with one leg off-mass-shell and the other leg on-mass-shell, using an exactly solvable manifestly covariant model of a $(3+1)$ dimensional fermion field theory. The model provides a 3D imaging of the two off-shell pion form factors $F_1$ and $F_2$ as a function of $(Q^2,t)$, which are related to each other satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity. The normalization of the renormalized charge form factor $F_1$ is fixed by $F_1(Q^2=0, t=m^2_π)=1$ while the other form factor $F_2$ vanishes, i.e. $F_2(Q^2, t=m^2_π)=0$ for any value of $Q^2$ due to the time-reversal invariance of the strong interaction. We define the new form factor $g(Q^2,t)=F_2(Q^2,t)/(t-m^2_π)$ and find that $g(Q^2,t)$ can be measurable in the on-mass-shell limit. In particular, $g(Q^2=0, t=m^2_π)$ is related with the pion charge radius. We also compare our form factors with the data extracted from the pion electroproduction reaction for both the off-shell region ($t<0$) and the on-shell limit ($t \rightarrow m_π^2$).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the off-shell pion electromagnetic form factors \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity in an exactly solvable covariant model, and that the new form factor \(g(Q^2,t)=F_2(Q^2,t)/(t-m_\pi^2)\) remains nonzero in the on-shell limit \(t \to m_\pi^2\) and is related to the pion charge radius, suggesting it could be a measurable observable in pion electroproduction experiments despite the fact that \(F_2\) itself vanishes on-shell.

## Method

The authors compute the half-off-shell pion electromagnetic vertex using a tree-level triangle diagram in a \((3+1)\)-dimensional fermion field theory with constituent quarks, employing Feynman parametrization and dimensional regularization to obtain analytic expressions for the two form factors \(F_1(Q^2,t)\) and \(F_2(Q^2,t)\). They then extract experimental values of \(F_1\) and \(g\) from the longitudinal cross section data of the \(^1\mathrm{H}(e,e'\pi^+)n\) reaction (Blok 2008, Huber 2008) using the Chew-Low Born-term model with a monopole parametrization of the \(\pi NN\) form factor, and compare these data with their model predictions for both the off-shell region (\(t<0\)) and the on-shell limit (\(t \to m_\pi^2\)).

## Key numbers

- The model uses constituent quark masses in the range \(0.12 \leq m_q \leq 0.16\) GeV, with best-fit coupling constants \(g_{\pi q\bar q} = (1.32, 1.20, 1.11) \times (2 m_q / f_\pi^{\mathrm{Exp}})\) for \(m_q = (0.12, 0.14, 0.16)\) GeV respectively, where \(f_\pi^{\mathrm{Exp}} = 130\) MeV.
- The on-shell normalization is fixed by \(F_1(Q^2=0, t=m_\pi^2)=1\) and \(F_2(Q^2, t=m_\pi^2)=0\) for any \(Q^2\).
- The extracted experimental values of \(F_1\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 0.60\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W=1.95\) GeV range from \(F_1^{\mathrm{Exp}} = 0.502 \pm 0.013\) at \(-t=0.026\) GeV\(^2\) to \(0.340 \pm 0.022\) at \(-t=0.074\) GeV\(^2\); the corresponding model values \(F_1^{\mathrm{Cov}}\) range from \(0.487^{+0.032}_{-0.039}\) to \(0.419^{+0.030}_{-0.036}\).
- The extracted \(g^{\mathrm{Exp}}(Q^2,t)\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 0.60\) GeV\(^2\) ranges from \(0.740^{+0.060}_{-0.082}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) at \(-t=0.026\) GeV\(^2\) to \(0.734^{+0.076}_{-0.095}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) at \(-t=0.074\) GeV\(^2\); the model \(g^{\mathrm{Cov}}\) ranges from \(0.768^{-0.024}_{+0.018}\) to \(0.611^{-0.004}_{-0.005}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The threshold for the timelike region where imaginary parts develop is at \(q^2 = 4 m_q^2\).
- The relation \(g(Q^2=0, t=m_\pi^2) = \frac{1}{6} \langle r_\pi^2 \rangle\) connects the new form factor to the pion charge radius.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of off-shell pion electromagnetic form factors using chiral perturbation theory (Rudy 1998), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Weiss 1999), and continuum methods for the strong-interaction bound-state problem (Craig 2013, Craig 2014), by providing an exactly solvable manifestly covariant model that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity and allows direct comparison with experimental data from the Jefferson Lab pion electroproduction experiments (Blok 2008, Huber 2008). The results enable future extraction of both off-shell form factors \(F_1\) and \(g\) from electroproduction data, and the master equation derived from the Ward-Takahashi identity provides a sum rule that can be used to check consistency in experimental analyses.
