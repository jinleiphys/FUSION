# Predictions for squeezed back-to-back correlations of $ϕϕ$ and $K^+K^-$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions by event-by-event hydrodynamics

> We calculate the squeezed back-to-back correlation (BBC) functions of $ϕϕ$ and $K^+K^-$ for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, using ($2+1$)-dimensional hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions. The BBC functions averaged over event-by-event calculations for many events for the hydrodynamic sources are smoothed as a function of the particle momentum. For heavy-ion collisions of Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the BBC functions are larger than those for collisions of Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. The BBC of $ϕϕ$ may possibly be observed in peripheral collisions at the RHIC and LHC energies. It is large for the smaller sources of Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Key claim

The squeezed back-to-back correlation (BBC) functions for \(\phi\phi\) and \(K^+K^-\) in heavy-ion collisions, calculated using (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, are smoothed as a function of particle momentum and do not exhibit the oscillations seen in earlier work with Gaussian initial-energy distributions (Zhang 2015a). The BBC of \(\phi\phi\) may possibly be observed in peripheral collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, but this is a prediction based on model calculations and not a confirmed experimental observation.

## Method

The authors used (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and the s95p-PCE equation of state. Initial energy-density profiles were generated using the HIJING event generator via the AMPT code, with fluctuating initial conditions for each event. The freeze-out hypersurface was calculated using the AZHYDRO technique at fixed freeze-out temperatures (140 MeV for \(\phi\) mesons, 160 MeV for \(K\) mesons), and the BBC functions were averaged over 2000 events per collision system to smooth event-by-event fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- BBC functions for \(\phi\phi\) in Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV are larger than those for Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV (no explicit numerical values given for the functions themselves; the comparison is qualitative).
- For Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the peak BBC function for \(\phi\phi\) reaches about 1.3 for central collisions (impact parameter \(b=0-4\) fm) and about 2.5 for peripheral collisions (\(b=4-8\) fm).
- The freeze-out temperature for \(\phi\) mesons is taken as \(T_f = 140\) MeV; for \(K\) mesons, \(T_f = 160\) MeV.
- The initial proper time \(\tau_0\) is 0.6 fm/\(c\) for RHIC collisions and 0.4 fm/\(c\) for LHC collisions.
- The transverse width parameter \(\sigma_0 = 0.6\) fm is used for all calculations.
- The average BBC function \(\langle C(\mathbf{k},-\mathbf{k})\rangle_{|\mathbf{k}|}\) for \(\phi\phi\) is evaluated over the momentum region 0–1 GeV/\(c\) with \(m_* = 1.05\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of squeezed back-to-back correlations originally developed by Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1996, 1999) and later applied to hydrodynamic sources by Padula (2006, 2010) and Zhang (2015a). It contrasts with the earlier study by Zhang (2015a) that used Gaussian initial-energy distributions and found oscillatory BBC functions; the present work uses fluctuating initial conditions from HIJING and finds smooth BBC functions after event averaging. The results enable experimental searches for \(\phi\phi\) BBC in peripheral collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for smaller collision systems such as Cu+Cu, and suggest that the BBC signal is sensitive to the temporal distribution of freeze-out points and the source size.
