# Momentum distribution of relativistic nuclei with Hartree-Fock mesonic correlations

> The impact of Hartree-Fock correlations on the nuclear momentum distribution is studied in a fully relativistic one boson exchange model. Hartree-Fock equations are exactly solved to first order in the coupling constants. The renormalization of the Dirac spinors in the medium is shown to affect the momentum distribution, as opposed to what happens in the non-relativistic case. The unitarity of the model is shown to be preserved by the present renormalization procedure.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, in a relativistic Hartree-Fock (HF) framework with one-boson-exchange (OBE) interactions, the nuclear momentum distribution is modified by the renormalization of Dirac spinors in the medium, unlike the non-relativistic case where the HF field leaves the momentum distribution unchanged. However, this modification is quantified to first order in the coupling constants and is found to be a small effect (1-3%), with the net result essentially coinciding with the pionic contribution alone due to a cancellation among heavier mesons.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Fock equations exactly to first order in the squared meson-nucleon coupling constants for symmetric nuclear matter, using a one-boson-exchange model that includes pi, rho, omega, and sigma mesons. The nucleon self-energy is decomposed into scalar, time-like, and space-like components, and the renormalized Dirac spinors and field-strength renormalization constant Z_2 are computed analytically to first order. The momentum distribution is then obtained from the renormalized wave functions, and the unitarity condition (particle number conservation) is used to determine the renormalized Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

* The Dirac mass of the nucleon is reduced by about 30% due to the sigma meson, with a total reduction by about a factor of 0.6 for k_F = 250 MeV/c.
* The net effect of the full interaction on the momentum distribution is an increase of about 1% for p approximately 0 and a decrease of almost the same amount for p approximately k_F, for k_F = 250 MeV/c.
* The effect of the mesonic HF field on both the Dirac mass and the momentum distribution increases with density and is roughly proportional to k_F^3.
* The momentum distribution coincides with the free one (n(p)=2) for a value of p/k_F approximately sqrt(3/5), independent of the specific meson and the value of k_F.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Amaro et al., 2001; Amaro et al., 2002) on the impact of mesonic correlations and meson-exchange currents on electroweak response functions, where consistency required including Hartree-Fock self-energy insertions. It contrasts with the non-relativistic case, where the HF field does not affect the momentum distribution (Fetter and Walecka, 1971), and with non-relativistic treatments of short-range correlations (Muther et al., 2000) which produce much larger effects. The results enable a consistent relativistic treatment of nuclear response functions and show that such correlations are perturbatively small under typical circumstances.
