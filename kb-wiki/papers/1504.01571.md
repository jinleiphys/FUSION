# Equation of State in a Generalized Relativistic Density Functional Approach

> The basic concepts of a generalized relativistic density functional approach to the equation of state of dense matter are presented. The model is an extension of relativistic mean-field models with density-dependent couplings. It includes explicit cluster degrees of freedom. The formation and dissolution of nuclei is described with the help of mass shifts. The model can be adapted to the description of finite nuclei in order to study the effect of $α$-particle correlations at the nuclear surface on the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei. Further extensions of the model to include quark degrees of freedom or an energy dependence of the nucleon self-energies are outlined.

## Key claim
The generalized relativistic density functional (gRDF) model extends conventional relativistic mean-field models with density-dependent couplings by including explicit cluster degrees of freedom and mass shifts to describe the formation and dissolution of nuclei, but the present version is limited to hadronic and leptonic degrees of freedom and does not yet incorporate quark degrees of freedom or full energy-dependent nucleon self-energies.

## Method
The model is based on a grand canonical potential density that depends on temperature and chemical potentials for all particle species, including baryons (nucleons and hyperons), a full table of heavy nuclei (A>4, N,Z ≤ 184), light nuclei, charged leptons, and photons. All massive particles are treated as quasiparticles with scalar and vector potentials from meson exchange (σ, ω, ρ) using density-dependent couplings (DD2 parametrization), with composite particles receiving an additional mass shift to account for Pauli blocking and cluster dissolution. The model can be applied to finite nuclei at zero temperature using an extended Thomas-Fermi approximation in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells to study α-particle correlations at nuclear surfaces.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: n_sat = 0.149 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = 16.02 MeV
- Compressibility at saturation: K = 242.7 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 31.67 MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L = 55.04 MeV
- Heavy nuclei table includes species with A > 4 and N, Z ≤ 184

## Context
This work extends conventional relativistic mean-field models with density-dependent couplings (Typel 1999) by incorporating explicit cluster degrees of freedom and mass shifts, building on earlier approaches to the Mott effect (Roepke et al. 1982, 1983) and the virial equation of state (Horowitz 2005, O'Connor 2007). The model enables improved descriptions of the equation of state for core-collapse supernovae (Janka 2006, 2012) and neutron star mergers (Bauswein 2014), and allows investigation of whether α-particle correlations at nuclear surfaces affect the correlation between neutron skin thickness and the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy, which is relevant for interpreting measurements of neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei like 208Pb.
