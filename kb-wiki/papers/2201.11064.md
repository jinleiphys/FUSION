# The heavy ions are "preheated" prior to hight energy collisions

> The so called "isobar run" or RHIC was designed to compare a number of observables for collisions of $^{96}_{40}Zr$ with those of $^{96}_{44}Ru$, aimed at identification of $Z$-dependent effects. However, as the STAR data have shown with unprecedented accuracy, these two nuclides differ stronger than expected, producing effects larger than those depending on charge. So far, multiple studies tried to quantify their shape differences, in relation to various observables. General consensus is that these differences somehow should be related to nuclear structure, in particularly properties of the lowest excited states. Yet the precise connection between these fields -- low and high energy nuclear physics -- is still missing. In this paper I propose such a connection, via a concept of thermal density matrices of a "preheated" nuclei. The effective temperature should parameterize which set of excited states should be included in the calculations. I also suggest semiclassical "flucton" method at finite temperatures to be used to calculate thermal density matrices.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that heavy ions at the moment of high-energy collision should be described not by their ground state wave functions but by thermal density matrices corresponding to a "preheated" state with an effective transverse temperature of about 20 MeV, parameterizing which set of excited nuclear states contribute to the initial shape distribution.

## Method
The author derives an effective transverse temperature T_perp ~ 20 MeV from the uncertainty relation applied to nucleon localization in the transverse plane, using the typical NN cross section to estimate Delta x_perp ~ 1 fm and the resulting transverse momentum kick. The paper then suggests using semiclassical "flucton" path integral methods at finite temperature to calculate thermal density matrices over collective deformation variables (beta_2, gamma), and applies this framework to the specific isobar nuclei 96Zr and 96Ru using their known excitation spectra from nuclear structure calculations.

## Key numbers
- The estimated transverse temperature is T_perp ~ 20 MeV, derived from Delta p_perp ~ 0.2 GeV and nucleon kinetic energy Delta E_perp ~ 20 MeV
- The accuracy of STAR measurements in the isobar run reached approximately 0.4 percent, smaller than the differences between Ru and Zr
- For noncentral collisions, deviations between Ru and Zr observables are at the 2 percent level, but for ultra-central collisions they reach O(10 percent) or more
- The moment of inertia for the rotational band in 96Ru ranges from I_J=3603.17 GeV^-1 at J=1-2 to 21112.8 GeV^-1 at J=17-18, compared to a solid sphere value of approximately 32400 GeV^-1
- The phonon energy for 96Zr configuration B is approximately 0.644 MeV (difference between 2.226 and 1.582 MeV states)

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Shuryak 1999) that colliding deformed nuclei should be described by wave packets of many excited states rather than the ground state, which was controversial at the time. The paper connects low-energy nuclear structure calculations of deformation potentials and excitation trees (such as the interacting boson model calculations by Gavrielov 2021) to high-energy heavy ion collision initial conditions, addressing the puzzle from the STAR isobar run (STAR 2021) where 96Zr and 96Ru showed unexpectedly large differences. The proposed preheating framework enables quantitative calculation of nuclear shape distributions at the collision moment using either classical Boltzmann factors or semiclassical flucton methods, potentially explaining the observed centrality-dependent differences between the two isobar systems.
