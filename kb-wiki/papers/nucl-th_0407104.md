# Polarization observables of the gamma d --> PiNN reaction in the Delta(1232)-resonance region

> Polarization observables of the three charge states of the pion for the $γd\toπNN$ reaction with polarized photon beam and/or oriented deuteron target are evaluated over the whole $Δ$(1232)-resonance region adopting a nonrelativistic model based on time-ordered perturbation theory. Results for the $π$-meson spectra, linear photon asymmetry, vector and tensor target asymmetries are presented. Particular attention is given, for the first time, to double polarization asymmetries for which we present results for $T_{20}^{\ell}$ and $T_{2\pm 2}^{\ell}$. We found that all other double polarization asymmetries of photon and deuteron target are vanished.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical predictions for double polarization asymmetries \(T_{20}^{\ell}\) and \(T_{2\pm 2}^{\ell}\) in the \(\gamma d \to \pi NN\) reaction, but the author explicitly notes that all circular double polarization asymmetries vanish in the \(\Delta(1232)\)-resonance region and that the non-vanishing longitudinal asymmetries would be difficult to measure experimentally.

## Method

A nonrelativistic model based on time-ordered perturbation theory was used to evaluate the \(\gamma d \to \pi NN\) amplitude, with the elementary \(\gamma N \to \pi N\) operator taken from the effective Lagrangian model of Schmidt et al. (1996). The deuteron wave function was taken from the Paris potential. The fully exclusive differential cross section and all polarization observables were expressed in terms of the \(t\)-matrix elements using a density matrix formalism, and calculations were performed for photon lab-energy \(\omega_{\gamma}^{\rm lab} = 330\) MeV (and also 285 and 450 MeV for double asymmetries) at fixed pion emission angles.

## Key numbers

- Unpolarized differential cross section \(d^3\sigma/(d\Omega_\pi dq)\) was evaluated at \(\omega_{\gamma}^{\rm lab} = 330\) MeV for pion angles \(\theta_\pi = 10^\circ\) and \(120^\circ\) for all three isospin channels (\(\pi^- pp\), \(\pi^+ nn\), \(\pi^0 np\)).
- Linear photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\) was found to have always negative values at forward and backward pion angles for all channels at \(\omega_{\gamma}^{\rm lab} = 330\) MeV.
- Vector target asymmetry \(T_{11}\) for charged pion channels has always negative values at \(\omega_{\gamma}^{\rm lab} = 330\) MeV; for the \(\pi^0 np\) channel it shows very small negative values at smaller pion momentum and relatively large positive values at higher pion momentum.
- The double polarization asymmetry \(T_{20}^{\ell}\) for neutral pion production has always positive values for all photon lab-energies (285, 330, and 450 MeV) and emission pion angles.
- The double polarization asymmetry \(T_{2+2}^{\ell}\) has negative values at all photon energies (285, 330, and 450 MeV) and pion angles for all channels.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations of single-spin asymmetries for the \(\gamma d \to \pi NN\) reaction (Darwish, 2004) and earlier studies of differential and total cross sections (Darwish, 2003). It provides the first predictions for double polarization observables in this reaction, which can serve as a basis for planning new polarization experiments at facilities such as MAMI in Mainz, ELSA in Bonn, and JLab in Newport News. The author notes that no experimental data exist for comparison and that future improvements should include final state interactions and a more sophisticated elementary production operator.
