# Multi-reference many-body perturbation theory for nuclei III -- Ab initio calculations at second order in PGCM-PT

> In spite of missing dynamical correlations, the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) was recently shown to be a suitable method to tackle the low-lying spectroscopy of complex nuclei. Still, describing absolute binding energies and reaching high accuracy eventually requires the inclusion of dynamical correlations on top of the PGCM. In this context, the present work discusses the first realistic results of a novel multi-reference perturbation theory (PGCM-PT) that can do so within a symmetry-conserving scheme for both ground and low-lying excited states. First, proof-of-principle calculations in a small ($e_{\mathrm{max}}=4$) model space demonstrate that exact binding energies of closed- (\nucl{O}{16}) and open-shell (\nucl{O}{18}, \nucl{Ne}{20}) nuclei are reproduced within $0.5-1.5\%$ at second order, i.e. through PGCM-PT(2). Moreover, profiting from the pre-processing of the Hamiltonian via multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group transformations, PGCM-PT(2) can reach converged values within smaller model spaces than with an unevolved Hamiltonian. Doing so, dynamical correlations captured by PGCM-PT(2) are shown to bring essential corrections to low-lying excitation energies that become too dilated at leading order, i.e., at the strict PGCM level. The present work is laying the foundations for a better understanding of the optimal way to grasp static and dynamical correlations in a consistent fashion, with the aim of accurately describing ground and excited states of complex nuclei via ab initio many-body methods.

## Key claim
PGCM-PT(2) reproduces exact binding energies of closed- and open-shell nuclei within 0.5–1.5% in a small model space, but the abstract's claim of agreement with exact results is qualified by the fact that these are proof-of-principle calculations using only the two-body part of the Hamiltonian in a truncated (e_max=4) space, and that the actual disagreement with full configuration interaction (FCI) results is partially masked by a bias from the complex shift used to solve the linear system.

## Method
The authors performed second-order multi-reference perturbation theory on top of projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) states, using axially-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states as seeds with constraints on the rms radius (¹⁶O), pairing gap (¹⁸O), or axial quadrupole moment (²⁰Ne). The Hamiltonian was a N³LO chiral effective field theory interaction evolved via vacuum similarity renormalization group (VSRG) to λ_vsrg = 1.88 fm⁻¹, retaining only the two-body part, and calculations used a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with ℏω = 20 MeV and e_max = 4. Results were benchmarked against FCI calculations in the same model space, with additional comparisons to single-reference Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) and projected HFB (PHFB) approaches.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of ¹⁶O, ¹⁸O, and ²⁰Ne reproduced within 0.5–1.5% at PGCM-PT(2) order in the e_max = 4 model space
- ¹⁶O: GCM-PT(2) binding energy differs by 0.8% from FCI result; GCM underbound by ~20 MeV (12%) relative to FCI
- ¹⁸O: PGCM underbound by ~25 MeV (~13%) relative to FCI; PGCM-PT(2) lies 1.5 MeV (0.8%) above FCI, with ~1 MeV of that discrepancy attributed to complex shift bias
- ²⁰Ne: PGCM energy 21.7 MeV (10%) away from FCI; PGCM-PT(2) overshoots FCI by 1.7%; canonical BMBPT(2) underbound by 2.6 MeV (1.2%) relative to FCI
- ²⁰Ne: PGCM 2⁺₁ excitation energy differs from FCI by 300 keV (27%); 4⁺₁ differs by 560 keV (13%)
- ¹⁶O: GCM adds only 165 keV correlation energy to HF minimum; ¹⁸O: PHFB-PT(2) shift upward from HFB-PT(2) is ~1.5 MeV at canonical point; ²⁰Ne: angular momentum projection provides 5.5 MeV energy gain

## Context
This work extends the PGCM framework from Paper II (Frosini et al., 2021) by adding dynamical correlations via perturbation theory, addressing the limitation that PGCM alone underbinds absolute energies despite capturing static correlations. It builds on the multi-reference perturbation theory formalism developed in Paper I and compares to single-reference BMBPT (Duguet, 2015; Tichai, 2018) and PHFB approaches. The results demonstrate that PGCM-PT(2) corrects the over-dilated excitation energies seen at the PGCM level in ²⁰Ne, and the work establishes a foundation for systematically combining static and dynamical correlations through the three levers of Hamiltonian pre-processing, unperturbed state choice, and expansion order.
