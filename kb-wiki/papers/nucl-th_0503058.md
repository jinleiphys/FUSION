# Signals in Single-Event Pion Interferometry for Granular Sources of Quark-Gluon Plasma Droplets

> We investigate two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations of quark-gluon plasma droplet sources in single-event measurements. We find that the distribution of the fluctuation between correlation functions of the single- and mixed-events provide useful signals to detect the granular structure of the source.

## Key claim

The distribution of the fluctuation between single-event and mixed-event two-pion correlation functions can serve as a measurable signal to detect the granular droplet structure of a quark-gluon plasma source, but the method is most feasible when the number of droplets is small (less than or equal to 16) and the number of identical pion pairs per event is of order 5×10⁴ or more.

## Method

The authors simulate static and hydrodynamically evolving granular sources of quark-gluon plasma droplets, generating space-time coordinates of droplet centers and emitting pions with a Bose-Einstein thermal distribution at freeze-out temperature 104 MeV. For each simulated event, they compute the single-event correlation function C_s(q) and the mixed-event correlation function C_m(q) as a function of relative momentum q (or the side-component q_s), then define the fluctuation f(q) as the absolute difference between C_s and C_m divided by the statistical error. They collect the distribution of f over many events and compute its root-mean-square value f_rms for sources with different numbers of droplets N_d, different droplet sizes, and with or without collective radial expansion.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of pions: 0.65 T_c = 104 MeV (with T_c = 160 MeV)
- Static granular source parameters: droplet center distribution standard deviation σ_R = 5.0 fm, droplet density standard deviation σ_d = 1.5 fm
- For static sources with N_ππ = 5×10⁴ pion pairs per event, the distribution width allows distinguishing granular sources with N_d = 16 from a single Gaussian source
- For static sources with N_ππ = 10⁵ pion pairs per event, granular sources up to N_d = 32 can be distinguished from a single Gaussian source
- For dynamical granular sources with v_d = 0.5 and N_ππ = 5×10⁴, the constraint q_out ≤ 20 MeV restricts to about 3.5 percent of pion pairs within q_s ≤ 250 MeV
- Root-mean-square f for granular sources increases with N_ππ and decreases as σ_d and σ_R increase

## Context

This work extends the single-event intensity interferometry method proposed by Wong (2004) for detecting granular droplet structure, and builds on earlier HBT interferometry studies of granular droplets by Pratt et al. (1992) which averaged over many events. The authors address the practical limitation that at RHIC, the number of identical pions detected per event is only a few hundred (as reported by the STAR Collaboration in 2003), making detailed mapping of correlation function oscillations difficult. The fluctuation distribution method is intended as an initial-stage tool to detect granular structure, with future refinements needed to account for pion absorption, multiple scattering, and fluctuations in overall source size.
