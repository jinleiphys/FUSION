# Implications of PREX--2 data on the electron--neutrino opacity in dense matter

> Motivated by the recent measurement of the neutron distribution radius of ${}^{208}$Pb from the PREX--2 data, I study the effects of the new G3(M) parameter set constrained by PREX--2 data on the electron--neutrino scattering in dense matter using the extended relativistic mean field (E--RMF) model. I employ the G3(M) parameter set to describe the nuclear matter. The obtained equation of state for the G3(M) parameter set has an excellent agreement with experimental data and the chiral effective field theory calculation with N$^3$LO 3N forces. I analyze both differential cross section of electron--neutrino and electron--neutrino mean free path to observe their sensitivity to the G3(M) parameter set. One finds that the differential cross sections of electron--neutrino for different baryon densities have higher values compared with that obtained for the TM1e and FSU Garnet parameter sets. The higher cross section decreases the electron--neutrino mean free path.

## Key claim

The G3(M) parameter set, constrained by the PREX-2 measurement of the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, yields higher electron-neutrino differential cross sections and consequently shorter neutrino mean free paths in dense matter compared to the TM1e and FSU Garnet parameter sets, specifically at fixed transferred momentum |q| = 2.5 MeV and initial neutrino energy Eν = 5 MeV.

## Method

The author employed the extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model with the G3(M) parameter set, which was fine-tuned to reproduce the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from PREX-2 data. The equation of state for pure neutron matter was calculated and compared with chiral effective field theory calculations at N3LO with 3N forces. The differential cross section and mean free path for electron-neutrino scattering were computed using the standard model weak interaction Lagrangian, with the polarization tensor evaluated for neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in β-stable matter.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from combined PREX-2 and PREX data: ΔRskin = Rn - Rp = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm
- The G3(M) parameter set yields higher differential cross sections for electron-neutrino scattering at baryon densities ρB = 1.0 ρ0, 2.0 ρ0, and 3.0 ρ0 compared to TM1e and FSU Garnet sets, at fixed |q| = 2.5 MeV and Eν = 5 MeV
- The neutrino mean free path for the G3(M) parameter set is lower than that obtained for the TM1e and FSU Garnet parameter sets across all baryon densities studied
- All three parameter sets (G3(M), TM1e, FSU Garnet) predict the same sound velocity at around saturation density (ρB/ρ0 ≈ 1) and at ρB/ρ0 ≈ 3.7

## Context

This work extends the application of the G3(M) parameter set developed by PPBP (2021) to neutrino scattering, which had previously been used only for neutron star properties and validation against the GW170817 binary neutron star merger constraint. The results contrast with those from the TM1e parameter set of Bao (2014) and the FSU Garnet parameter set of Chen and Piekarewicz (2014), showing that the PREX-2 constrained EOS leads to systematically higher neutrino opacities. This enables more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling rates and supernova evolution, where neutrino transport is sensitive to the nuclear equation of state.
