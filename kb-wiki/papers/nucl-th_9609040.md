# Finite Nuclei in a Relativistic Mean-Field Model with Derivative Couplings

> We study finite nuclei, at the mean-field level, using the Zimanyi-Moskowski model and one of its variations (the ZM3 model). We calculate energy levels and ground-state properties in nuclei where the mean-field approach is reliable. The role played by the spin-orbit potential in sorting out mean-field model descriptions is emphasized.

# Finite Nuclei in a Relativistic Mean-Field Model with Derivative Couplings

## Key claim
The ZM3 variation of the Zimanyi-Moskowski model improves upon the standard ZM model for finite nuclei by giving a reasonable description of nuclear spectra and better spin-orbit splittings, while retaining the advantage of only two free parameters fitted to nuclear matter saturation. However, the improvement is still partial: the ZM3 model's spin-orbit splitting remains weaker than that of the non-linear sigma-omega model, and the overall agreement with experiment is described as "reasonable" rather than excellent.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations in the mean-field approximation for spherical nuclei, using the ZM and ZM3 models which differ in how the effective scalar factor m* = (1 + g_sigma sigma / M)^{-1} multiplies the nucleon derivative and coupling terms. For each nucleus (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, 208Pb), they iterate between solving the Dirac equation for nucleon wave functions and solving the meson field equations for sigma, omega, rho, and Coulomb fields until self-consistency is achieved. The coupling constants g_sigma and g_omega are fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3} and binding energy E/A = -15.75 MeV, while g_rho is fitted to the bulk symmetry energy a_4 = 32.5 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -15.75 MeV
- ZM model: compression modulus kappa = 224 MeV, effective mass M* = 797 MeV, vector-scalar potential difference lambda = 223 MeV
- ZM3 model: compression modulus kappa = 156 MeV, effective mass m* = 0.72 (M*/M), vector-scalar potential difference lambda = 471 MeV
- Ratio of spin-orbit potentials: V_ls^{ZM3} / V_ls^{ZM} approximately 2.5, derived from (V-S)^{ZM3} * m*_{ZM} / ((V-S)^{ZM} * m*_{ZM3})
- Bulk symmetry energy used for rho coupling: a_4 = 32.5 MeV at rho = rho_0
- Shift in single-particle energies in 208Pb due to rho-meson coupling can be as large as 5 MeV depending on choice of a_4

## Context
This work extends the nuclear matter calculations of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (1990) and Heide and Rudaz to finite nuclei, directly addressing the spin-orbit problem identified by Koepf, Sharma and Ring (KSR) for the standard ZM model. The authors compare their results against the linear sigma-omega (Walecka) model and non-linear scalar-coupling models (Boguta, Waldhauser; Feldmeier and Lindner), noting that the ZM3 model achieves a lower compressibility (156 MeV) than the ZM model (224 MeV) while improving the spin-orbit splitting, unlike the phenomenological non-linear model of Feldmeier and Lindner where reduced effective mass (m* = 0.71) leads to enhanced compressibility (kappa = 410 MeV). The work enables further exploration of derivative-coupling models as alternatives to four-parameter non-linear models for describing both nuclear matter and finite nuclei.
