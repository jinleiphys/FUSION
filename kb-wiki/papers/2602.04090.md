# Charmonium, exotic hadrons and hadron structure

> To celebrate the 50th anniversary of the discovery of the J/ψ, the first charmonium state observed, I start with a brief review of major progresses on the QCD inspired quark potential model originated from charmonium spectrum.Then I show the importance of unquenching dynamics, multiquark components and exotic multiquark states for understanding hadron structure and hadron spectrscopy. The J/ψ and charmonium-like states have played an important role in this aspect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a unified description of hadron structure, including both conventional and exotic states, requires going beyond traditional quenched quark models to incorporate unquenching dynamics, multiquark components, and hadronic molecule configurations, with the \(J/\psi\) and charmonium-like states serving as crucial probes for this extended framework.

## Method

The author reviews the historical development of QCD-inspired quark potential models, starting from the Cornell potential for heavy quarkonia and extending to chiral quark models that incorporate Goldstone boson exchange and vector meson exchange for light quarks. The paper then presents evidence for penta-quark components in the proton from deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan experiments, and discusses the observation of exotic hadrons including tetraquark states (\(X(3872)\), \(Z_c(3900)\)) and pentaquark states (\(P_c\)) from Belle, BESIII, and LHCb experiments. Systematic calculations of spectra and hadronic decays for bottomonium and \(D_s\) systems are performed using a coupled-channel framework with the \(^3P_0\) model for quark pair creation.

## Key numbers

- The proton requires about 30% penta-quark components to consistently explain the observed \(\bar d/\bar u\) asymmetry, strangeness content, and spin crisis.
- A surprisingly large asymmetry between \(\bar u\) and \(\bar d\) sea quark distributions in the proton with \(\bar d - \bar u \sim 0.12\) was observed from deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan experiments.
- For the ground state \(D_s\) meson, there is 17% tetra-quark components in the unquenched quark model.
- A \(D^*K^*\) dominant molecule with mass 2894 MeV is predicted, only 5 MeV below the \(D^*K^*\) threshold.
- 229 heavy-antiheavy hadronic molecular states and about the same amount of heavy-heavy hadronic molecular states were predicted within the hadronic molecule framework.

## Context

This work extends the traditional Cornell potential model (Eichten et al., 1970s) and chiral quark models (Manohar and Georgi, 1984; Glozman and Riska, 1995) by incorporating unquenching dynamics and coupled-channel effects. The paper builds on the observation of exotic states such as \(X(3872)\) by Belle (2003), \(Z_c(3900)\) by BESIII (2013), and \(P_c\) states by LHCb (2015, 2019), which were predicted by hadronic molecule models (Tornqvist, 1993; Wu et al., 2010-2012). The framework enables future experimental searches at facilities including Belle II, BESIII, PANDA, JLab, JPARC, and LHC to map the full landscape of multiquark matter and distinguish between compact tetraquark and extended molecular interpretations.
