# Causality, universality, and effective field theory for van der Waals interactions

> We analyze low-energy scattering for arbitrary short-range interactions plus an attractive 1/r^6 tail. We derive the constraints of causality and unitarity and find that the van der Waals length scale dominates over parameters characterizing the short-distance physics of the interaction. This separation of scales suggests a separate universality class for physics characterizing interactions with an attractive 1/r^6 tail. We argue that a similar universality class exists for any attractive potential 1/r^{alpha} for alpha >= 2. We also discuss the extension to multi-channel systems near a magnetic Feshbach resonance. We discuss the implications for effective field theory with attractive singular power law tails.

## Key claim

For low-energy scattering with an attractive $1/r^{6}$ tail, the van der Waals length scale $\beta_6$ dominates over parameters characterizing short-distance physics, creating a separate universality class; this universality extends to any attractive potential $1/r^{\alpha}$ for $\alpha \geq 2$, and the causality bound does not prevent taking the cutoff momentum arbitrarily large for any angular momentum $L$, unlike the case for finite-range or Coulomb-tail interactions.

## Method

The authors analyze low-energy two-body scattering for arbitrary short-range interactions plus an attractive $-C_6/r^6$ tail by deriving causality bounds for the short-range $K$ matrix $\hat{K}_L$ using Wronskian integral relations. They expand the van der Waals wave functions $F_L$ and $G_L$ in powers of $k^2$, compute the coefficient $K_{L,2}$ from the causality bound, and relate it to generalized scattering length $A_L$ and effective range $R_L$ via quantum-defect theory. They then extract single-channel $s$-wave scattering parameters for several alkali-metal atom pairs and compute the causal range $R^b$ for each.

## Key numbers

- Van der Waals length scale defined as $\beta_6 = (2\mu C_6)^{1/4}$, where $\mu$ is reduced mass and $C_6$ is the van der Waals coupling constant.
- For $^7$Li$_2$: reduced mass $\mu = 6394.7$ a.u., $C_6 = 1388$ a.u.
- For $^{23}$Na$_2$: reduced mass $\mu = 20954$ a.u., $C_6 = 1472$ a.u.
- For $^{133}$Cs$_2$: reduced mass $\mu = 121100$ a.u., $C_6 = 7020$ a.u.
- For all alkali-metal pairs studied, $K_{L,2}$ is at least one order of magnitude smaller than $\beta_6^2$.
- The causal range $R^b$ is less than $\beta_6$ in each case studied (exact numerical values for $R^b$ are not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the causality and unitarity bounds previously derived for finite-range interactions (Wigner 1955; Phillips and Cohen 1996; Hammer et al. 2009, 2010) and for interactions with Coulomb tails (Koenig et al. 2012) to the case of attractive van der Waals $1/r^6$ tails. It provides theoretical foundations for the approximate universal relationship between effective range and inverse scattering length noticed by Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola (2010, 2011) for alkali-metal atoms, and it enables the development of a van der Waals effective field theory with contact interactions that can be applied to study the Efimov effect and other low-energy phenomena in a model-independent way.
