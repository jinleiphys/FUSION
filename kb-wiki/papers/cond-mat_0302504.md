# Quantum dots in high magnetic fields: Rotating-Wigner-molecule versus composite-fermion approach

> Exact diagonalization results are reported for the lowest rotational band of N=6 electrons in strong magnetic fields in the range of high angular momenta 70 <= L <= 140 (covering the corresponding range of fractional filling factors 1/5 >= nu >= 1/9). A detailed comparison of energetic, spectral, and transport properties (specifically, magic angular momenta, radial electron densities, occupation number distributions, overlaps and total energies, and exponents of current-voltage power law) shows that the recently discovered rotating-electron-molecule wave functions [Phys. Rev. B 66, 115315 (2002)] provide a superior description compared to the composite-fermion/Jastrow-Laughlin ones.

## Key claim

The rotating-electron-molecule (REM) wave functions provide a superior description of the lowest rotational band of N=6 electrons in strong magnetic fields compared to composite-fermion/Jastrow-Laughlin (CF/JL) wave functions, specifically for high angular momenta 70 ≤ L ≤ 140 (covering fractional filling factors 1/5 ≥ ν ≥ 1/9), where the CF/JL approach fails to capture correlations arising from the long-range character of the Coulomb force.

## Method

The authors performed exact diagonalization (EXD) calculations restricted to the lowest Landau level for N=6 electrons covering angular momenta 15 ≤ L ≤ 140 (filling factors 1 ≥ ν ≥ 1/9), using Tsiper's analytic formula for two-body Coulomb matrix elements and the ARPACK code for the eigenvalue problem. They compared five properties, magic angular momenta, radial electron densities, occupation number distributions, overlaps and total energies, and current-voltage power law exponents, between EXD results, REM wave functions (constructed via symmetry-restored unrestricted Hartree-Fock with displaced Gaussian orbitals), and CF/JL wave functions (with compact Slater determinants and Jastrow factors).

## Key numbers

- EXD calculations covered angular momenta 15 ≤ L ≤ 140 for N=6 electrons, corresponding to filling factors 1 ≥ ν ≥ 1/9
- For L=105, the Hilbert space dimension was 56115 with l_max=40; for L=135, dimension was 187597 with l_max=50
- CF theory predicts nine magic numbers in any interval 15(2m-1) ≤ L ≤ 15(2m+1), but EXD shows 10 cusps in interval I₁, 10 in I₂, 7 in I₃, and 7 in I₄
- For N=3 electrons, JL overlaps with exact states were ≥0.99468 for L=15 to L=39; REM overlaps were ≥0.98347 for the same range
- For N=6 electrons at L=75 (ν=1/5): JL overlap = 0.837, REM overlap = 0.817
- For N=6 at L=105 (ν=1/7): JL overlap = 0.710, REM overlap = 0.850
- For N=6 at L=135 (ν=1/9): JL overlap = 0.665, REM overlap = 0.860

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of rotating-electron-molecule wave functions (Yannouleas and Landman, 2002) and provides systematic EXD results for N=6 at higher angular momenta than earlier studies, which were restricted to filling factors 1 ≥ ν ≥ 1/3 for N ≥ 5. The paper contradicts the prevailing CF/JL picture (Laughlin, 1983; Jain, 1989) by showing that CF/JL wave functions fail to capture the oscillatory radial densities and occupation numbers that reflect Wigner molecule formation, while REM wave functions succeed. This enables future work using REM wave functions as accurate trial states for quantum dots in high magnetic fields, particularly at low filling factors where CF/JL approaches deteriorate.
