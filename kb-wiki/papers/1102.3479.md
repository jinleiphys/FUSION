# Recent progress on nuclear parton distribution functions

> We report current status of global analyses on nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs). The optimum NPDFs are determined by analyzing high-energy nuclear reaction data. Due to limited experimental measurements, antiquark modifications have large uncertainties at x>0.2 and gluon modifications cannot be determined. A nuclear modification difference between u and d quark distributions could be an origin of the long-standing NuTeV sin^2 theta_w anomaly. There is also an issue of nuclear modification differences between the structure functions of charged-lepton and neutrino reactions. Next, nuclear clustering effects are discussed in structure functions F_2^A as a possible explanation for an anomalous result in the Be-9 nucleus at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab). Last, tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distribution functions are extracted from HERMES data on the polarized structure function b_1 of the deuteron, and they could be used for testing theoretical models and for proposing future experiments, for example, the one at JLab. Such measurements could open a new field of spin physics in spin-one hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper reports the current status of global analyses of nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs), noting that while optimum NPDFs can be determined from high-energy nuclear reaction data, antiquark modifications have large uncertainties at x>0.2 and gluon modifications cannot be determined at all due to limited experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors performed global χ² analyses of world data on nuclear structure function F₂ ratios, Drell-Yan cross-section ratios, RHIC dA data, and neutrino-DIS structure functions to determine nuclear modification factors w_i for valence quarks, sea quarks, and gluons. They used a parametrization where nuclear PDFs are expressed as nucleonic PDFs multiplied by x-dependent nuclear modification factors, with parameters determined by minimizing χ². For the clustering analysis, they used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and harmonic-oscillator shell model calculations to compute nucleon momentum distributions, then applied a convolution formalism to estimate nuclear structure functions. For the tensor structure analysis, they extracted tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distributions from HERMES b₁ data using a χ² fit with a parametrized weight function.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modifications of the structure function F₂ are typically 10-20% for medium size nuclei
- The NuTeV measurement of sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst) differs from other measurements giving sin²θ_W = 0.2227 ± 0.0004
- For the tensor structure analysis, χ²/d.o.f. = 2.83 for set-1 (no antiquark tensor polarization) and χ²/d.o.f. = 1.57 for set-2 (with antiquark tensor polarization)
- The analysis obtained a finite sum ∫dx b₁(x) = 0.0058 suggesting finite tensor polarization for antiquarks
- The initial Q² scale for the HKNS parametrization is Q₀² = 1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends previous global NPDF analyses by groups including HKN (Hirai, Kumano, Saito), de Florian and Sassot (2004), Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado (EPS), Schienbein et al., Kulagin and Petti (KP), and Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (FGS). The paper addresses the NuTeV sin²θ_W anomaly as a possible consequence of nuclear modification differences between u and d quarks, following theoretical work by Cloët, Bentz, and Thomas. The clustering analysis provides a possible explanation for anomalous JLab data on ⁹Be nuclear modification slopes, suggesting that nuclear clustering structure could be observable in high-energy deep inelastic scattering. The tensor structure analysis opens a new field of spin physics in spin-one hadrons and enables future experiments at JLab and J-PARC to test tensor-polarized antiquark distributions through Drell-Yan processes.
