# Wave functions in the f_{7/2} shell, for educational purposes and ideas

> We use the spectrum of {42}Sc as input to calculate wave functions and energy levels of all nuclei in the single-j-shell model with j equal to f_{7/2}. The value of these results for educational purposes is pointed out. Also new insights emerge. For example, it is noted that for the (Z=4,N=4) nucleus {48}Cr, a good quantum number comes out: (-1)^{(v_p+v_n)/2}, where v_p and v_n are the seniority quantum numbers of the protons and neutrons, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors use the experimental spectrum of \(^{42}\)Sc to calculate wave functions and energy levels for all nuclei in the single-\(j\)-shell model with \(j = f_{7/2}\), noting that while full \(fp\)-shell calculations are needed for quantitative agreement, the truncated space still yields some properties fairly well and provides valuable educational insights. A specific new result is that for the self-conjugate nucleus \(^{48}\)Cr, the quantity \((-1)^{(v_p+v_n)/2}\) (where \(v_p\) and \(v_n\) are the seniorities of protons and neutrons) emerges as a good quantum number.

## Method

The two-body matrix elements are taken directly from the experimental excitation energies of the lowest states in \(^{42}\)Sc, with even-\(J\) states assigned isospin \(T=1\) and odd-\(J\) states \(T=0\). Wave functions for systems with up to four protons and any number of neutrons are computed using coefficients of fractional parentage (cfp’s) via the Bayman–Lande method; for neutron numbers greater than four, neutron holes are used and the proton–neutron-hole interaction is obtained via a Pandya transformation. Results for mirror nuclei and cross-conjugate nuclei are related to the tabulated wave functions by simple phase formulas.

## Key numbers

* The experimental excitation energies (in MeV) used as two-body matrix elements for \(^{42}\)Sc: \(T=1\) states: \(J=0\) at 0.0000, \(J=2\) at 1.5863, \(J=4\) at 2.8153, \(J=6\) at 3.2420; \(T=0\) states: \(J=1\) at 0.6111, \(J=3\) at 1.4904, \(J=5\) at 1.5101, \(J=7\) at 0.6163.
* The proton–neutron-hole matrix elements (in MeV) for \(^{48}\)Sc, with the lowest state (\(J=6^+\)) set to zero, are: 7.03763 (\(J=0\)), 2.39516 (\(J=1\)), 0.32755 (\(J=2\)), 0.39826 (\(J=3\)), 0.12027 (\(J=4\)), 0.08821 (\(J=5\)), 0.00000 (\(J=6\)), 1.18812 (\(J=7\)).
* In \(^{43}\)Sc, the ground state (\(I=7/2\)) has excitation energy 0.00000 MeV and the first excited \(I=7/2\) state lies at 4.14201 MeV (assigned \(T=3/2\)).
* The lowest \(I=1/2\) state in \(^{47}\)Cr (obtained via mirror symmetry from \(^{47}\)V) has excitation energy 2.68294 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier single-\(j\)-shell calculations of McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964), which covered only scandium and titanium nuclei plus their mirrors and cross-conjugates, and which used guessed values for some \(T=0\) matrix elements that were later found to be incorrect. By using the now-known correct experimental spectrum of \(^{42}\)Sc, the present compilation provides wave functions for all nuclei in the \(f_{7/2}\) shell, enabling educational exercises and revealing symmetries such as the seniority-based quantum number in \(^{48}\)Cr. The authors note that the approach of taking matrix elements from experiment is present in the book of de Shalit and Talmi (1963), and that for the upper half of the \(f_{7/2}\) shell one might instead use the spectrum of \(^{54}\)Co.
