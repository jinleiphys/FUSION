# Magnetic field-induced squeezing effect at RHIC and at the LHC

> In off-central heavy-ion collisions, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is exposed to the strongest magnetic fields ever created in the universe. Due to the paramagnetic nature of the QGP at high temperatures, the spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field configuration exerts an anisotropic force density that competes with the pressure gradients resulting from purely geometric effects. In this paper, we simulate (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with external magnetic fields to estimate the effect of this force density on the anisotropic expansion of the QGP in collisions at RHIC and at the LHC. While negligible for quickly decaying magnetic fields, we find that long-lived fields generate a substantial force density that suppresses the momentum anisotropy of the plasma by up to $20\%$ at the LHC energy, and also leaves its imprint on the elliptic flow $v_2$ of charged pions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that paramagnetic squeezing from long-lived magnetic fields can suppress the momentum anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma by up to 20% at LHC energy, but this effect is negligible for quickly decaying fields and completely negligible at RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the CLVisc code, adding the paramagnetic squeezing force density as a source term in the hydrodynamic equations. The magnetic field was treated as an external degree of freedom with a Gaussian spatial profile and exponential time decay, using the magnetic susceptibility from lattice QCD calculations. The initial energy density was given by the optical Glauber model, and the simulations used the lattice QCD equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (2014).

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field amplitude: 0.09 GeV^2 (about 5 m_pi^2) for RHIC Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV; 1.33 GeV^2 (about 70 m_pi^2) for LHC Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Maximum squeezing force density at thermalization time tau_0=0.2 fm: 2-4 GeV/fm^4 for long-lived fields at LHC; 0.08 GeV/fm^4 for short-lived fields with t_d=0.1 fm at LHC
- Momentum anisotropy epsilon_p reduced by 20% for setting (F) with eB_0=1.33 GeV^2, sigma_x=2.4 fm, sigma_y=4.8 fm, t_d=1.9 fm
- Elliptic flow v_2 of charged pions reduced by 6% for setting (F) and by 1-2% for setting (E) with narrower spatial distribution
- Ratio of maximal squeezing force density between RHIC and LHC: proportional to (0.09/1.33)^2 approximately 0.0046
- Maximum pressure gradients: 32 GeV/fm^4 at LHC; about 1/3 of that at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the initial estimate of paramagnetic squeezing by Bali (2013) by performing full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations rather than comparing force densities at a single time. The study is motivated by lattice QCD calculations of the magnetic susceptibility by Bali (2014) and magnetic field profiles in heavy-ion collisions from Skokov (2009), Bzdak (2011), and Deng (2012). The results suggest that interpreting experimental v_2 measurements without accounting for paramagnetic squeezing may slightly overestimate the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s of the QGP. The authors note that electromagnetic probes such as thermal photons and di-leptons might be more sensitive to the squeezing effect since they are emitted throughout the entire QGP evolution.
