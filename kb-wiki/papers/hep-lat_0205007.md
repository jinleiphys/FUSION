# Light quark masses in quenched QCD with exact chiral symmetry

> The parameters in the pseudoscalar meson mass formula in quenched chiral perturbation theory to one-loop order are determined by quenched lattice QCD with overlap Dirac operator, and from which the light quark masses are determined with the experimental inputs of pion and kaon masses, and the pion decay constant. Our results are $ m_{u,d} = 5.3 \pm 0.3 $ MeV, and $ m_s = 115\pm 8 $ MeV, in the $ \bar{MS} $ scheme at scale $ μ= 2 $ GeV.

## Key claim

The light quark masses are determined from quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry, but the authors note that the results are exploratory and do not include estimates of finite volume and discretization errors, and that the renormalization to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme uses one-loop perturbation theory rather than non-perturbative renormalization.

## Method

The authors computed quenched quark propagators for 12 bare quark masses on 100 gauge configurations generated with the Wilson gauge action at \(\beta = 5.8\) on an \(8^3 \times 24\) lattice using the overlap Dirac operator. They extracted the pion decay constant from the pion propagator to fix the lattice spacing, then fitted the one-loop quenched chiral perturbation theory formula for the pion mass to their data to determine the parameters \(\delta\), \(C\), and \(B\). Using these parameters and experimental inputs for the pion and kaon masses, they solved for the bare quark masses and then converted them to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV using a one-loop perturbative renormalization constant.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.147(1)\) fm, determined from the pion decay constant \(f_\pi a = 0.0984(3)\).
* Quenched chiral logarithm coefficient: \(\delta = 0.2034(140)\).
* Quark mass ratio: \(m_s / m = 21.92(23)\).
* Bare up/down quark mass: \(m = 6.3 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
* Bare strange quark mass: \(m_s = 138 \pm 8\) MeV.
* Renormalized up/down quark mass in \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(m_{u,d} = 5.3 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
* Renormalized strange quark mass in \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: \(m_s = 115 \pm 8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous quenched QCD determinations of light quark masses using the overlap Dirac operator, which had obtained \((m_{u,d} + m_s)\) using zeroth-order chiral perturbation theory (Giusti, 2001; Hernandez, 2001) or \(m_{u,d}\) via the axial Ward identity (Dong, 2000). By including one-loop quenched chiral logarithms, the authors provide a more complete determination of the individual quark masses. The results are in good agreement with the current lattice world average at the time, and the authors state that this approach enables a viable method for determining light quark and hadron masses, though they note that a systematic study with larger volumes, smaller lattice spacings, and better statistics is needed.
