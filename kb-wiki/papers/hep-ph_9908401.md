# Color Screening Effects on Hadronization in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The effects of color screening on the hadronization of a parton plasma into a hadron gas are examined at the energies of the relativistic heavy ion collider. It is found to have the tendency to prevent hadronization and therefore delaying the conversion of the partons into a hadron gas. Because of the continual expansion, the resulting hadron gas number densities are lower when screening is included. This should reduce the hadronic noise to genuine signals of the quark-gluon plasma. In this sense, color screening is favorable and should be included in numerical models. In any case, we advocate that numerical models should allow the confining forces and color screening to act on each other so as to undergo the phase transition in a natural way. Hadronization is also shown to seriously disrupt parton equilibration and is yet another reason why full parton chemical equilibration should not be expected.

## Key claim

Color screening in a dense parton plasma weakens the confining forces responsible for hadronization, thereby delaying the conversion of partons into hadrons and reducing the resulting hadron gas number densities; the paper argues that numerical models should allow confining forces and color screening to act on each other rather than imposing a fixed hadronization temperature or density.

## Method

The author extends a previously developed time-evolution scheme for a parton plasma (based on a reduced Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation) by adding hadronization via a simplified parton clustering mechanism where only quark-antiquark pairs form color-singlet resonance clusters, which then decay into pions and kaons. A non-perturbative gluon splitting term is introduced to convert the gluon-dominated plasma into quark-antiquark pairs, and the screening mass is incorporated via leading-order perturbative calculations and non-perturbative lattice results to determine when hadronization can occur.

## Key numbers

* The screening length at leading order at the end of the run when temperature estimates fell to about 200 MeV was about 0.4 fm.
* Using lattice results for SU(N=3) up to O(g^3), the Debye screening mass m_D is approximately 3.3 times the leading-order result m_D^LO.
* With two flavors of quarks (n_f=2) and Lambda_MSbar = 234 MeV, m_D^LO = 487.94 MeV at T = 200 MeV and m_D^LO = 398.63 MeV at T = 150 MeV.
* The non-perturbative screening length derived from m_D up to O(g^3) is l_D = 0.123 fm at T = 200 MeV and l_D = 0.150 fm at T = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigations of parton plasma time evolution at RHIC and LHC energies (Wong, 1999) by incorporating hadronization with color screening effects, which had been neglected in earlier parton cascade model implementations such as Geiger (1995) that used the same hadronization scheme as in e+e- annihilation. The results suggest that color screening should be included in numerical models of heavy ion collisions, and that full parton chemical equilibration should not be expected because hadronization seriously disrupts parton equilibration.
