# Nuclear shape evolution of neutron-deficient Au and kink structure of Pb isotopes

> Recent experiments using advanced laser spectroscopy technique revealed that the charge radii of neutron-deficient gold (Au) isotopes exhibit significant changes in ground state deformation: odd-even shape staggering in the $N = 98 \sim 100$ region and abrupt change of charge radii from $N =$ 108. In this study, we examine the abnormal shape evolution of the nuclear charge radii. To understand the nuclear structure underlying this phenomenon, we exploit the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). The significant change in mean-squared charge radii ($δ{< r^2 >}$) turns out to originate from nuclear shape transitions between prolate deformation and small oblate deformation due to the shape coexistence possibility. We elucidate the nuclear shape evolution by analyzing the evolution of occupation probability for single-particle states. In addition, the abrupt kink structure in the nuclear charge radius of lead (Pb) isotopes near the $N =$ 126 shell is also investigated and reproduced quite well.

## Key claim

The observed odd-even shape staggering in neutron-deficient Au isotopes (N = 98–100) and abrupt change of charge radii above N = 108 originate from nuclear shape transitions between prolate and small oblate deformation due to shape coexistence, where the energy difference between competing minima is about 1 MeV for Au and about 1.5 MeV for Pb.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed relativistic Hartree–Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with a meson-exchange density functional, solving the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis. For odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, the equal filling approximation with automatic blocking was used to handle the unpaired nucleon. Constrained calculations of total binding energy curves as a function of quadrupole deformation β₂ were performed to identify shape coexistence minima, and the ground-state deformations were then used to compute relative changes in mean-square charge radii.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of total binding energy for Au isotopes: less than 1.43 MeV compared to experimental data from AME2020
- Root-mean-square deviation of total binding energy for Pb isotopes: less than 1.93 MeV compared to experimental data from AME2020
- Root-mean-square deviation of binding energy per nucleon for both Au and Pb isotopes: less than 0.01 MeV compared to experimental data from AME2020
- Energy difference between prolate and oblate minima in Au isotopes showing shape coexistence: about 1 MeV
- Energy difference among three deformations in Pb isotopes showing shape coexistence: about 1.5 MeV
- Relative charge radii of Au isotopes described within δ⟨r²⟩ ≤ 0.25 fm² when shape coexistence is considered
- Relative charge radii of Pb isotopes described within δ⟨r²⟩ ≤ 0.2 fm² when shape coexistence is considered
- Pairing strength used: V₀ = –325.0 MeV fm³ with pairing window of 100 MeV and saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³
- Energy cutoff E_cut⁺ = 300 MeV and angular momentum cutoff J_max = (23/2)ℏ for the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis
- Legendre expansion truncation λ_max = 8

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DRHBc study of shape staggering and kink structure in Hg isotopes (Mun 2023) to the neighboring Au and Pb isotopic chains. The results explain recent laser spectroscopy measurements of charge radii in neutron-deficient Au isotopes (Cubiss 2023) and the well-known kink in Pb charge radii near N = 126. The analysis of occupation probabilities for neutron ν1i₁₃/₂ and proton π1h₉/₂ states provides a microscopic mechanism for the shape transitions, while the Pb kink is attributed to neutron swelling of the ν1i₁₁/₂ state beyond N = 126, consistent with earlier work on Hg isotopes (Mun 2023) and general discussions of pairing-induced core expansion (Hori 2022). The authors plan to extend this approach to K, Ca, and Bi isotopes near magic shells.
