# Pion axioproduction: The Delta resonance contribution

> The process of pion axioproduction, $aN\toπN$, with an intermediate $Δ$ resonance is analyzed using baryon chiral parturbation theory. The $Δ$ resonance is included in two ways: First, deriving the $aΔN$-vertices, the axion is brought into contact with the resonance, and, second, taking the results of $πN$ elastic scattering including the $Δ$, it is implicitly included in the form of a pion rescattering diagram. As a result, the partial wave cross section of axion-nucleon scattering shows an enhancement in the energy region around the $Δ$ resonance. Because of the isospin breaking, the enhancement is not as pronounced as previously anticipated. However, since the isospin breaking here is much milder than that for usual hadronic processes, novel axion search experiments might still exploit this effect.

## Key claim

The Delta resonance enhances the partial wave cross section of axion-nucleon scattering in the energy region around the Delta resonance, but this enhancement is at least an order of magnitude less pronounced than previously anticipated by Carenza et al. (2020) due to isospin breaking effects, though the isospin breaking here is much milder than for usual hadronic processes.

## Method

The authors analyze pion axioproduction \(aN \to \pi N\) using baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) with the Delta resonance included in two ways: first, by deriving the \(a\Delta N\)-vertices to bring the axion into direct contact with the resonance, and second, by taking results from \(\pi N\) elastic scattering including the Delta, which is implicitly included via a pion rescattering diagram. The amplitudes are decomposed into partial waves, focusing on the \(P_{33}\) channel where the Delta resonance chiefly appears, and the \(\pi N\) scattering amplitude is unitarized using the \(N/D\) method with a fit to Roy-Steiner analysis results.

## Key numbers

- The Delta resonance Breit-Wigner mass is \(m_\Delta \approx 1232\) MeV and width \(\Gamma_\Delta \approx 117\) MeV
- The fit to the \(P_{33}\) phase shift yields coupling constants \(g = 1.249(16)\), \(z_0 = -0.21(56)\), and subtraction constant \(a_0 = -0.959(12)\)
- The isospin-averaged nucleon mass used is \(m_N = 938.92\) MeV, isospin-averaged pion mass \(M_\pi = 138.03\) MeV, and pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV
- The approximation of equal pion/nucleon masses gives an average deviation of only \(\lesssim 2\%\) compared to the full isospin-breaking treatment for the KSVZ axion and DFSZ axion at \(\sin^2\beta \lesssim 0.95\), reaching a maximum deviation of about \(15\%\) only for \(\sin^2\beta \to 1\) at c.m. energies \(W \gtrsim 1.15\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Carenza et al. (2020), who proposed that pion axioproduction with Delta resonance enhancement could make axion detection accessible for underground water Cherenkov detectors, and builds on the authors' previous CHPT treatment of axion-nucleon interactions (Vonk, Guo, Meißner, 2020). The results enable more accurate predictions for axion production in protosupernova cores, where the pion-induced process \(\pi N \to aN\) may play a more important role than previously thought, and provide a framework for evaluating whether novel axion search experiments can exploit the Delta resonance enhancement despite its reduced magnitude.
