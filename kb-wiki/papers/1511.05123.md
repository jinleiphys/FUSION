# Diffusion Monte Carlo study of strongly interacting two-dimensional Fermi gases

> Ultracold atomic Fermi gases have been a popular topic of research, with attention being paid recently to two-dimensional (2D) gases. In this work, we perform T=0 ab initio diffusion Monte Carlo calculations for a strongly interacting two-component Fermi gas confined to two dimensions. We first go over finite-size systems and the connection to the thermodynamic limit. After that, we illustrate pertinent 2D scattering physics and properties of the wave function. We then show energy results for the strong-coupling crossover, in between the Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC) and Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) regimes. Our energy results for the BEC-BCS crossover are parametrized to produce an equation of state, which is used to determine Tan's contact. We carry out a detailed comparison with other microscopic results. Finally, we calculate the pairing gap for a range of interaction strengths in the strong coupling regime, following from variationally optimized many-body wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper presents T=0 diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) results for the strongly interacting two-dimensional Fermi gas, claiming that the fixed-node approximation introduces only a small error (at most 0.014 E_FG) compared to the in-principle exact auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method, but only for the specific system sizes and interaction strengths studied.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations for a two-component Fermi gas in two dimensions, using a modified Poschl-Teller potential to model interactions between opposite-spin particles. They employed a Jastrow-BCS trial wave function with 10 variational parameters optimized via variational Monte Carlo (VMC) energy minimization for each interaction strength, and applied finite-size corrections to extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- Energy improvement over previous DMC results (Bertaina and Giorgini, 2011): approximately 12% at eta=0.75, 12% at eta=1.44, and 6% at eta=2.15 on the scale of the subtracted binding energy plot
- Maximum difference between DMC and AFQMC energies: 0.014 E_FG, where E_FG = hbar^2 k_F^2 / 4m
- Finite-size correction for N=26 at eta>4 from kinetic energy: approximately 0.041 E_FG
- AFQMC finite-size corrections for N=26: 0.003 E_FG or less for eta<1, maximum 0.01 E_FG at eta approximately 2
- Constant effective range maintained throughout: k_F r_e = 0.006

## Context

This work extends the previous DMC study of the 2D Fermi gas by Bertaina and Giorgini (2011), who used a square well potential and fewer variational parameters, achieving notably lower ground-state energies through more extensive optimization of the pairing function. The results enable more accurate comparisons with AFQMC calculations by Shi et al. (2015) and provide a benchmark for other many-body approaches such as those by Bauer (2014), He (2015), and Klawunn (2015), while also supporting connections to neutron pairing in compact stars and high-temperature superconductors.
