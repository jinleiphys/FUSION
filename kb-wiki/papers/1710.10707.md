# Modification of the masses of the lightest neutral mesons in a hadronic medium under an external magnetic field

> The effective masses of the neutral mesons in a hadronic medium and under an external magnetic field are evaluated as functions of the baryonic density and the field intensity. For this purpose the meson polarization is evaluated in the one loop approximation using a Quantum Hadrodynamics model which includes pions, sigma, omega, and rho mesons. The propagators of the baryons include the full effect of the coupling to the magnetic field through their charges and their anomalous magnetic moments. Within the range of magnetic intensities considered here $10^{17}$ G $< B < 10^{19}$ G, the dependence on B is moderate for the pion and the longitudinal component of the omega meson, and negligible for the remaining mesons.

## Key claim

The effective masses of neutral mesons in a dense hadronic medium under an external magnetic field show only moderate dependence on the field intensity for the pion and longitudinal omega meson, and negligible dependence for the sigma, rho, and transverse omega mesons, within the range \(10^{17}\) G \(< B < 10^{19}\) G, contrary to the abstract's implication that the full range \(10^{16}\) G to \(10^{19}\) G was studied for all mesons.

## Method

The meson polarization was evaluated in the one-loop approximation using a Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model including \(\pi, \sigma, \omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, with baryon propagators that fully incorporate coupling to the external magnetic field through both electric charge and anomalous magnetic moments. The mean field approximation (MFA) was used to obtain the quasi-particle picture of nucleons, and the effective meson masses were defined as solutions to the equations \(\text{Re}(\varepsilon_{S,T,L}) = 0\) at zero spatial momentum, where \(\varepsilon\) are dielectric functions constructed from the polarization insertions and mixing components. Calculations were performed at zero temperature for baryonic densities \(0 < n_B < 3 n_0\) and magnetic intensities \(10^{16}\) G \(\leq B \leq 10^{19}\) G, considering both isospin symmetric nuclear matter and neutral stellar matter in beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.1484\) fm\(^{-3}\), with binding energy \(E_B = -16.24\) MeV and incompressibility \(K = 271\) MeV (model parameter set Z271v6).
- Pion effective mass increases by \(10-15\%\) at \(n/n_0 = 3\) for symmetric nuclear matter, compared to a \(15-40\%\) increase in prior work neglecting heavier meson mixing (Aguirre, 2016).
- Sigma meson mass shows a mean growth of \(15\%\) at \(n/n_0 = 3\), with at most \(2\%\) dispersion across magnetic field intensities.
- Longitudinal omega meson mass exceeds \(30\%\) growth at mid-range densities; longitudinal rho meson mass grows around \(20\%\) at \(n/n_0 \approx 2.8\).
- At fixed density \(n/n_0 = 1\) and \(10^{17}\) G \(\leq B \leq 10^{19}\) G, the pion and longitudinal omega masses vary by roughly \(3\%\); other mesons show negligible variation.
- For stellar matter at \(n/n_0 = 3\) and \(B = 10^{19}\) G, the pion mass enhancement reaches \(20\%\).
- The abnormal pion branch appears near \(eB \approx 0.05\) GeV\(^2\) with an initial value \(25\%\) below the vacuum pion mass; the normal pion branch ends abruptly at \(eB \approx 0.08\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using hadronic models, such as those by Andersen (2012), Colucci (2014), Cheoun (2013), Adhya (2016), and Aguirre (2016), by including the full mixing of neutral meson channels (pion, sigma, omega, rho) and the effect of anomalous magnetic moments. The results contrast with calculations using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL2, 2015) which predict decreasing pion mass with magnetic field at zero density, and with earlier hadronic models (Adhya, 2016) that found only a slight monotonic increase. The findings enable more realistic modeling of meson propagation in magnetar interiors and heavy-ion collision environments, though the author notes that quark structure effects and zero-density fermion loop contributions are not included.
