# Validity of the Impulse Approximation and Quasielastic (e,e'p) Reactions from Nuclei

> We assess the combined effect of ground-state correlations, meson-exchange and isobar currents upon the cross sections for quasielastic (e,e'p) reactions from nuclei. Four-momenta in the range 0.1 \leq Q^2 \leq 1 GeV^2 are addressed. We observe that for $Q^2$ values exceeding 0.2 GeV^2, quasielastic conditions and missing momenta below the Fermi momentum, the ground-state correlations and two-body currents do not dramatically alter the $(e,e'p)$ predictions as they are obtained in the impulse approximation. Moreover, deviations from the impulse approximation exhibit a rather modest Q^2 dependence.

## Key claim

For quasielastic (e,e'p) reactions at four-momentum transfers Q^2 exceeding 0.2 GeV^2 and missing momenta below the Fermi momentum, ground-state correlations and two-body currents do not dramatically alter the predictions obtained in the impulse approximation, and deviations from the impulse approximation exhibit a rather modest Q^2 dependence.

## Method

The author performed (e,e'p) calculations for 16O that systematically added mechanisms beyond the impulse approximation: central and tensor ground-state correlations, meson-exchange currents from pion exchange, and Δ33-isobar currents. The calculations used non-relativistic quasi-hole wave functions from a Hartree-Fock calculation with an effective Skyrme force, with bound and continuum states generated by the same Hamiltonian to avoid orthogonality defects. The study covered four-momenta in the range 0.1 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 1 GeV^2 under quasielastic conditions and parallel kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For knockout from the 1s1/2 state in 16O, the predicted variation in the extracted spectroscopic factor z_lj as one moves from the lowest to the highest Q^2 is about 25%
- For knockout from the p-shell in 16O, the estimated error on the extracted spectroscopic factors attributed to limitations of the impulse approximation is of the order 5-10%
- The study addressed four-momenta in the range 0.1 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 1 GeV^2
- The threshold for modest deviations from the impulse approximation is Q^2 ≥ 0.2 GeV^2
- The Fermi momentum k_F is approximately 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework reviewed by Kelly and by Boffi, which has been the standard single-particle approach for analyzing (e,e'p) data since the 1970s. The calculations directly address the puzzling Q^2 dependence of spectroscopic factors reported by Lapikas and collaborators (2000) from a DWIA analysis of 12C(e,e'p) world data, which showed extracted z_lj values exhausting only 50% of the sum rule value up to 0.6 GeV^2 but approaching the sum rule at higher momentum transfers. The results indicate that two-body currents and ground-state correlations cannot explain either the very low spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) experiments or their possible Q^2 dependence, and they enable future (e,e'pn) research programs at Mainz and Jefferson Lab to better constrain tensor correlation functions.
