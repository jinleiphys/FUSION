# Transport properties of isospin effective mass splitting

> We investigate in detail the momentum dependence ($MD$) of the effective in medium Nucleon-Nucleon ($NN$) interaction in the isovector channel. We focus the discussion on transport properties of the expected neutron-proton ($n/p$) effective mass splitting at high isospin density. We look at observable effects from collective flows in Heavy Ion Collisions ($HIC$) of charge asymmetric nuclei at intermediate energies. Using microscopic kinetic equation simulations nucleon transverse and elliptic collective flows in $Au+Au$ collisions are evaluated. In spite of the reduced charge asymmetry of the interacting system interesting $isospin-MD$ effects are revealed. Good observables, particularly sensitive to the $n/p$-mass splitting, appear to be the differences between neutron and proton flows. The importance of more exclusive measurements, with a selection of different bins of the transverse momenta ($p_t$) of the emitted particles, is stressed. In more inclusive data a compensation can be expected from different $p_t$-contributions, due to the microscopic $isospin-MD$ structure of the nuclear mean field in asymmetric matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the difference between neutron and proton collective flows (transverse and elliptic) in semi-central Au+Au collisions at 250 AMeV is sensitive to the sign of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting, but this sensitivity is most clearly visible in exclusive measurements with transverse momentum selection rather than in inclusive data integrated over all transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport simulations of semi-central Au+Au collisions at 250 AMeV using a modified BGBD effective interaction that allows opposite signs of the n/p effective mass splitting while keeping the symmetry energy at saturation fixed at 33 AMeV. They evaluated the transverse flow (V1) and elliptic flow (V2) for neutrons and protons as functions of transverse momentum in selected rapidity bins, and compared the differences between neutron and proton flows for the two mass-splitting parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: E_sym(rho_0) = 33 AMeV for both parametrizations
- Normal density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Beam energy: 250 AMeV for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter: b/b_max = 0.5 (semi-central collisions)
- Effective mass splitting up to approximately 15% at normal density for asymmetry beta = 0.2 (the asymmetry of Au-197)
- Compressibility of symmetric matter: K_NM approximately 210 MeV
- Rapidity selection for main results: 0.7 <= |y^(0)| <= 0.9 (high rapidity)
- Transverse momentum range of interest for reliable statistics: p_t^(0) <= 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flows in heavy ion collisions that investigated compressibility and in-medium cross sections (Bao et al., 1999) and isospin dependence (Scala et al., 1999; Bao et al., 2000; B.S. Zou et al., 2001). The paper addresses the open problem of the momentum dependence in the isovector channel, where Skyrme forces (SIII, SGII, SKM* from Krewald et al., 1980) and Skyrme-Lyon forces (SLy230a, SLy230b from Lyon group, 1997) give opposite signs of the n/p effective mass splitting, with the Lyon forces predicting m*_n < m*_p in neutron-rich systems, consistent with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations (Hole et al., 2001) and relativistic mean field approaches (Liu et al., 2002; G. Greiner et al.), but opposite to non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Zuo et al., 1999; Zuo et al., 2002). The results suggest that exclusive measurements of neutron-proton flow differences with transverse momentum selection can directly probe the isospin-dependent momentum dependence of the nuclear equation of state, largely independent of the stiffness of the symmetry energy.
