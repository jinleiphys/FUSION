# The resonance amplitude associated with the Gamow states

> The Gamow states describe the quasinormal modes of quantum systems. It is shown that the resonance amplitude associated with the Gamow states is given by the complex delta function. It is also shown that under the near-resonance approximation of neglecting the lower bound of the energy, such resonance amplitude becomes the Breit-Wigner amplitude. This result establishes the precise connection between the Gamow states, Nakanishi's complex delta function and the Breit-Wigner amplitude. In addition, this result provides another theoretical basis for the phenomenological fact that the almost-Lorentzian peaks in cross sections are produced by intermediate, unstable particles.

## Key claim

The resonance amplitude associated with a Gamow state is exactly given by the complex delta function, and the Breit-Wigner amplitude is only an approximation to this exact amplitude that becomes valid when the lower bound of the energy (threshold) can be neglected.

## Method

The author proves that the energy representation of the Gamow functional is proportional to the antilinear complex delta functional by working with the spherical shell potential for zero angular momentum and using the theory of distributions. The transition amplitude from a Gamow state to a scattering state is then derived by inserting the identity in the energy representation. To obtain the Breit-Wigner approximation, the author extends the energy integration to the whole real line using a regulator and Cauchy's theorem, showing that the exact amplitude becomes the Breit-Wigner form only when the threshold at E=0 is ignored.

## Key numbers

- The resonance amplitude is given by A(z_R to E) = i sqrt(2pi) N_R delta(E - z_R), where N_R^2 = i * res[S(E)]_{E=z_R} and S is the S matrix
- Under the near-resonance approximation, the amplitude becomes A(z_R to E) approximately = -(N_R / sqrt(2pi)) * 1/(E - z_R) for E >= 0
- The approximate decay probability is |A(z_R to E_f)|^2 approximately = (|N_R|^2 / 2pi) * 1/[(E_f - E_R)^2 + (Gamma_R/2)^2]
- The relation between width and lifetime is Gamma_R = hbar / tau_R, which the author notes was experimentally verified for the 3p ^2P_{3/2} state of Na

## Context

This work establishes the precise mathematical connection between Gamow states (introduced by Gamow in his 1928 paper on alpha-decay), Nakanishi's complex delta function, and the Breit-Wigner amplitude used to describe resonance lineshapes. The result provides a theoretical basis for interpreting almost-Lorentzian peaks in cross sections as intermediate unstable particles, and extends the rigorous formulation of Gamow states within the rigged Hilbert space framework. The author distinguishes this approach from the Kapur-Peierls states, which satisfy a purely outgoing boundary condition at a finite radial distance and whose complex eigenenergies depend on an external parameter, unlike the Gamow states whose complex energies coincide with the poles of the S matrix.
