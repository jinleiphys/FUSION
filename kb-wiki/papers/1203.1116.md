# Light quark mass dependence in heavy quarkonium physics

> The issue of chiral extrapolations in heavy quarkonium systems is discussed. We show that the light quark mass dependence of the properties of heavy quarkonia is not always suppressed. For quarkonia close to an open flavor threshold, even a nonanalytic chiral extrapolation is needed. Both these nontrivial facts are demonstrated to appear in the decay widths of the hindered M1 transitions between the first radially excited and ground state P-wave charmonia. The results at a pion mass of about 500 MeV could deviate from the value at the physical pion mass by a factor of two. Our findings show the necessity of performing chiral extrapolations for lattice simulations of heavy quarkonium systems.

## Key claim

The light quark mass dependence of heavy quarkonium properties is not always suppressed; for states close to open flavor thresholds, the chiral extrapolation can be nonanalytic, and for hindered M1 transitions between the first radially excited and ground state P-wave charmonia, results at a pion mass of about 500 MeV could deviate from the physical-pion-mass value by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors analyze the pion mass dependence of heavy quarkonium masses and radiative decay widths using a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) that includes coupled-channel effects from virtual heavy meson loops. They compute the self-energy of P-wave charmonia due to coupling to pseudoscalar and vector charmed mesons, and evaluate the three-point scalar loop function that dominates the hindered M1 transition amplitudes, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization with \(\lambda = 0.63\) GeV and the SU(3) mass-splitting parameter \(h_1 = 0.44\).

## Key numbers

- The scale \(R \equiv M_\pi^2 / \overset{\circ}{\mu}_{12}\) is about 20 MeV at the physical pion mass but around 270 MeV for a pion mass of 500 MeV, indicating when the unitary cut cannot be expanded in a polynomial in \(M_\pi\).
- The value of \(\Gamma(h_c' \to \chi_{c0}\gamma)\) at a pion mass of 485 MeV is only about half of that at the physical pion mass.
- The cusp in \(\Gamma(h_c' \to \chi_{cJ}\gamma)\) appears at about \(M_\pi = 300\) MeV, the same value as the cusp in the \(h_c'\) mass dependence.
- The lattice simulation in Liu (2012) was performed at a single pion mass \(M_\pi = 396\) MeV; the simulation in Chen (2011) was performed at \(M_\pi = 485\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of chiral extrapolations from low-lying heavy quarkonia (where a simple linear formula in \(M_\pi^2\) suffices, as in Burch (2009) and Meinel (2010)) to higher excited states near open flavor thresholds, where nonanalytic behavior arises. It builds on the NREFT framework for charmonium radiative transitions developed by Guo (2010, 2011) and the observation that hindered M1 transitions are dominated by triangle diagrams with charmed mesons (Guo 2011). The results enable lattice practitioners to perform proper chiral extrapolations for excited quarkonium states and to extract the product of coupling constants \(g_1 g_1'\) from simulations of these transitions, which would also test the role of coupled-channel effects in heavy quarkonium physics.
