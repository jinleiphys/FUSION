# Coarse grained Potential analysis of neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering below pion production threshold

> Using the delta-shell representation we present a successful fit to neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below pion production threshold. A detailed overview of the theory necessary to calculate observables with this potential is presented. A new data selection process is used to obtain the largest mutually consistent data base. The analysis includes data within the years 1950 to 2013. Using 46 parameters we obtain chi^2/Ndata = 1.04 with Ndata = 6713 including normalization data. Phase shifts with error bars are provided.

## Key claim

Using a delta-shell representation with 46 parameters, the authors achieve a fit to neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below pion production threshold with chi-squared per data point of 1.04, but this result depends on a new data selection process that rescues data previously rejected by other analyses through a model-independent pairwise comparison of experimental datasets.

## Method

The authors parameterize the short- and intermediate-range nucleon-nucleon interaction using a sum of Dirac delta functions (delta-shells) spaced at 0.6 fm intervals out to a cutoff radius of 3.0 fm, with the long-range part described by charge-dependent one-pion exchange and electromagnetic interactions. They fit the strength coefficients in 15 independent partial waves to scattering data using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, constraining the potential to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and the np singlet S-wave scattering length. A new data selection criterion analyzes tension among pairs of experimental datasets from 1950 to 2013 to identify mutually compatible data, yielding a database of 6713 data points including normalization data.

## Key numbers

* Chi-squared per data point of 1.04 achieved with 46 parameters and Ndata = 6713 including normalization data
* Cutoff radius between intermediate and long-range parts set at r_c = 3.0 fm
* Delta-shell spacing of Delta r = 0.6 fm between concentration radii
* Deuteron binding energy constrained to B = 2.224575(9) MeV
* np singlet S-wave scattering length constrained to alpha = -23.74(2) fm
* Maximum angular momentum for partial wave summation set to J_max = 20
* Database includes 2972 pp and 4737 np published scattering data up to 2013 with E_LAB less than or equal to 350 MeV

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis tradition of the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993), the CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt 2000), and the AV18 potential (Wiringa 1994), which all achieved chi-squared per degree of freedom less than or equal to 1 but used different data selection criteria. The authors improve upon previous approaches by implementing a model-independent pairwise comparison of experimental datasets, following a suggestion by Gross and Stadler (2008), which rescues a large fraction of data that would otherwise be rejected. The resulting potential enables uncertainty propagation to nuclear structure calculations, addressing the authors' earlier finding (Navarro Perez 2012, Perez 2012) that discrepancies among high-quality fits exceeded their declared statistical uncertainties.
