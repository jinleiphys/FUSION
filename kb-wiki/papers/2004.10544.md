# Light nuclei in the hadron resonance gas

> The description of the production of light (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei in a hadron resonance gas, or statistical-thermal model approach, has proven to be rather successful, despite the fact that the binding energies of these compound objects are small compared to the emitting fireball temperature. We summarise some recent developments and findings in this approach.

## Key claim

The statistical hadron resonance gas (thermal) model successfully describes light (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei production yields in heavy-ion collisions across nine orders of magnitude, despite the fact that the binding energies of these nuclei (e.g., 2.2 MeV for the deuteron) are far smaller than the chemical freeze-out temperature of about 156 MeV, implying that the model describes hadronisation rather than the survival of pre-formed nuclei in the fireball.

## Method

The author reviews the application of the grand-canonical hadron resonance gas model, where particle yields are computed from the partition function using three fit parameters: chemical freeze-out temperature T, baryo-chemical potential μ_B, and volume V. The model treats light nuclei as ordinary hadrons characterised only by their quantum numbers and mass, with feed-down from higher-mass states found to be negligible at LHC energies (suppression factor of 330). The review compares results from three thermal model implementations (GSI-Heidelberg, THERMUS, SHARE) and discusses extensions including eigenvolume corrections, canonical treatment for small systems, multiple freeze-out scenarios, and Saha equation approaches for chemical evolution after freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch ≈ 156 MeV from thermal fits to LHC data (ALICE Collaboration)
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2 MeV, compared to T_ch ≈ 156 MeV
- Hypertriton (³_ΛH) Λ separation energy: 130 keV
- Feed-down suppression factor from higher-mass states at LHC: factor 330
- Deuteron-to-proton ratio at LHC approaches constant value ≈ 3 × 10⁻³
- At SIS18 energies (Au-Au at 1 A·GeV): T ≈ 50 MeV, μ_B ≈ 822 MeV, volume V ≈ 1000 fm³
- Two freeze-out scenario temperatures differ by about 10 MeV (155 vs 163 MeV) at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature from blast-wave fits: T_kin = 113 ± 12 MeV
- Hypertriton root-mean-square radius: √⟨r_dΛ²⟩ = 10.6 fm

## Context

This review extends the long-standing application of the statistical-thermal model to light nuclei, which dates back to Hagedorn (1960) and was developed through work by Cleymans (1992), Braun-Munzinger (1994-1998), and Becattini (2000). The approach successfully predicted LHC yields before data taking (Andronic 2010) and is contrasted with the coalescence model (Butler and Pearson 1961, Chen 2018, Braun-Munzinger 2018). Recent developments address the "snowballs in hell" problem (Braun-Munzinger 1994, 2001) through ideas such as multi-quark state formation (Andronic 2017) and Saha equation treatments (Vovchenko 2019). The review enables future discrimination between thermal and coalescence models via measurements of ³_ΛH, ³He, and ³H yields across different collision systems and multiplicities, as planned for upcoming LHC runs (Citron 2018).
