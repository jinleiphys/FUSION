# Generic algorithm for multi-particle cumulants of azimuthal correlations in high energy nucleus collisions

> Multi-particle cumulants of azimuthal angle correlations have been compelling tools to probe the properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) created in the ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the search for the QGP in small collision systems at RHIC and the LHC. However, only very few of them are available and have been studied in theoretical calculations and experimental measurements. In this paper, we present a generic recursive algorithm for multi-particle cumulants, which enables the calculation of arbitrary order multi-particle cumulants. Among them, the new 10-, 12-, 14-, and 16-particle cumulants of a single harmonic, named $c_{n}\{10\}$, $c_{n}\{12\}$, $c_{n}\{14\}$, and $c_{n}\{16\}$, and the corresponding $v_n$ coefficients, will be discussed for the first time. These new multi-particle cumulants can be readily used along with updates to the generic framework of multi-particle correlations to a very high order. Finally, we propose a particular series of mixed harmonic multi-particle cumulants, which measures the general correlations between any moments of different flow coefficients. The predictions of these new observables are shown based on an initial state model MC-Glauber, a toy Monte Carlo model, and the HIJING transport model for future comparisons between experimental data and theoretical model calculations. The study of these new multi-particle cumulants in heavy-ion collisions will significantly improve the understanding of the joint probability density function which involves both different harmonics of flow and also the symmetry planes. This will pave the way for more stringent constraints on the initial state and help to extract more precisely how the created hot and dense matter evolves. Meanwhile, the efforts applied to small systems could be very helpful in the understanding of the origin of the observed collectivity at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generic recursive algorithm that enables the calculation of arbitrarily high-order multi-particle cumulants of azimuthal correlations, including the first-ever formulation of 10-, 12-, 14-, and 16-particle cumulants of a single harmonic; the authors hedge that while these new cumulants can be readily implemented, the differences between high-order cumulants (e.g., \(v_2\{4\}\) through \(v_2\{12\}\)) are already below 0.5% in MC-Glauber simulations, making experimental discrimination extremely challenging given current systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors developed a recursive C++ algorithm that computes multi-particle cumulants from Q-vectors (sums of \(w_k^p e^{i n \varphi_k}\) over particles in an event), using the mathematical formalism of cumulants as sums over partitions of harmonics. They validated the algorithm with a toy Monte Carlo model where \(v_2 = 0.10\) and \(v_3 = 0.05\) were fixed with no event-by-event fluctuations, then applied it to MC-Glauber initial-state simulations (assuming \(v_n = \kappa_n \varepsilon_n\) with \(\kappa_2\) scaled from ALICE data) and to the HIJING transport model to study non-flow effects. The algorithm was used to derive explicit formulas for \(v_n\{10\}\), \(v_n\{12\}\), \(v_n\{14\}\), and \(v_n\{16\}\) in terms of moments \(\langle v_n^{2k}\rangle\), and to propose new mixed-harmonic cumulants measuring correlations between any moments of different flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

* In the toy Monte Carlo validation with fixed input \(v_2 = 0.10\) and \(v_3 = 0.05\), all multi-particle cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\) through \(v_2\{16\}\) retrieved the input value with high precision.
* In MC-Glauber simulations scaled to ALICE Pb-Pb data at 5.02 TeV, the difference between high-order cumulants of \(v_2\) (e.g., \(v_2\{4\}\) through \(v_2\{12\}\)) was found to be below 0.5%.
* The number of terms in the "full cumulant" for 6-particle cumulants is 203, and for 8-particle cumulants is 4140; after removing terms that vanish due to isotropic symmetry, these reduce to 41 and 715 terms respectively.
* The explicit formula for \(v_n\{10\}^{10}\) includes a prefactor of \(1/456\), for \(v_n\{12\}^{12}\) a prefactor of \(-1/9460\), for \(v_n\{14\}^{14}\) a prefactor of \(1/274800\), and for \(v_n\{16\}^{16}\) a prefactor of \(-1/10643745\).

## Context

This work extends the established framework of multi-particle cumulants for anisotropic flow, which previously only included cumulants up to 8 particles (Bilandzic et al., 2013; Acharya et al., 2018). The generic algorithm enables the first study of 10-, 12-, 14-, and 16-particle cumulants of a single harmonic, as well as new mixed-harmonic cumulants that generalize symmetric cumulants \(SC(m,n)\) (Bilandzic et al., 2013) to correlations between any moments of different flow coefficients. These new observables can provide more stringent constraints on the joint probability density function of flow harmonics and symmetry planes, helping to distinguish between initial-state models (e.g., MC-Glauber) and transport models (e.g., HIJING), and to probe the origin of collectivity in small collision systems at RHIC and the LHC.
