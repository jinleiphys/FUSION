# Equation of state of superfluid neutron matter and the calculation of $^1S_0$ pairing gap

> We present a Quantum Monte Carlo study of the zero temperature equation of state of neutron matter and the computation of the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in the low-density regime with $ρ<0.04$ fm$^{-3}$. The system is described by a non-relativistic nuclear Hamiltonian including both two-- and three--nucleon interactions of the Argonne and Urbana type. This model interaction provides very accurate results in the calculation of the binding energy of light nuclei. A suppression of the gap with respect to the pure BCS theory is found, but sensibly weaker than in other works that attempt to include polarization effects in an approximate way.

## Key claim

The authors find that the superfluid pairing gap in low-density neutron matter is suppressed relative to pure BCS theory, but the suppression is "sensibly weaker" than in other works that include polarization effects in an approximate way; the maximum gap is of order 2 MeV, leaning toward the larger end of recent estimates.

## Method

The authors performed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of neutron matter in a periodic box using a non-relativistic Hamiltonian with the Argonne v8' two-body and Urbana IX three-body interactions. They computed the equation of state for both normal and BCS states using Jastrow-BCS trial wave functions, and extracted the pairing gap from the energy difference Δ(N) = E(N) − ½[E(N+1) + E(N−1)] for systems of 12–68 neutrons at fixed volume and density.

## Key numbers

- The maximum pairing gap from mean-field BCS treatment is Δ ≈ 3 MeV at k_F ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹, "almost irrespective of the NN potential."
- The AFDMC calculations give a maximum gap of order 2 MeV, compared to other methods that give maxima between 0.7 and 1.0 MeV (Schulze 1996, Schwenk 2003) or ∼2.5 MeV (Cao 2006, Margueron 2008).
- At k_F = 0.55 fm⁻¹, the Gezerlis and Carlson (2008) QMC calculation predicts a gap about 30% smaller than the AFDMC estimate.
- The Cooper pair size is estimated as δx ≈ 6.6 fm at Δ = 2 MeV and k_F = 0.8 fm⁻¹, much smaller than the simulation box size of ∼16 fm for 66 neutrons.
- The ratio of the energy of dilute cold Fermions to the Fermi gas energy in the unitary limit is ξ = 0.42(1) (Carlson 2003, Astrakharchik 2004, Carlson 2005).

## Context

This work extends previous AFDMC calculations (Fabrocini 2005) by using larger particle numbers and the fixed phase approximation instead of the constrained path approximation, resolving discrepancies with Green's Function Monte Carlo results. The results contradict the strong suppression of the gap found by Schulze (1996) and Schwenk (2003) and are intermediate between those and the larger gaps from Cao (2006) and Margueron (2008). The calculations enable benchmarking of the equation of state and pairing gap for neutron matter at densities relevant for neutron star crusts and neutron-rich nuclei, and provide a reference for future many-body calculations using realistic interactions.
