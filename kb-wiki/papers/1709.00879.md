# Higher order net-proton number cumulants dependence on the centrality definition and other spurious effects

> We study the dependence of the normalized moments of the net-proton multiplicity distributions on the definition of centrality in relativistic nuclear collisions at a beam energy of $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}= 7.7$ GeV. Using the UrQMD model as event generator we find that the centrality definition has a large effect on the extracted cumulant ratios. Furthermore we find that the finite efficiency for the determination of the centrality introduces an additional systematic uncertainty. Finally, we quantitatively investigate the effects of event-pile up and other possible spurious effects which may change the measured proton number. We find that pile-up alone is not sufficient to describe the data and show that a random double counting of events, adding significantly to the measured proton number, affects mainly the higher order cumulants in most central collisions.

## Key claim

The centrality definition used in relativistic nuclear collisions has a large effect on extracted cumulant ratios of net-proton multiplicity distributions, and the finite efficiency for centrality determination introduces additional systematic uncertainty; pile-up alone is not sufficient to describe the data, and random double counting of events affects mainly the higher order cumulants in most central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model in cascade mode to generate 11 million minimum bias Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of √sNN = 7.7 GeV. They compared three centrality definitions: number of charged particles (Ncharge) with |η| ≤ 1 and 0.15 < pT < 2.0 GeV, number of participants (Npart), and number of projectile participants (Npart-projectile). They then calculated the first four cumulants (C1 through C4) and their ratios (C2/C1, C3/C2, C4/C2) of net-proton distributions at mid-rapidity, and studied the effects of different centrality definitions, acceptance cuts, detector efficiency, and pile-up on these cumulant ratios.

## Key numbers

- The correlation coefficient between Ncharge and Npart falls to values around 0.5 for central and peripheral collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV.
- For a 70% binomial efficiency for charged particles, the systematic uncertainties on cumulant ratios are of the order of 10-20%, with up to 50% deviation for the larger pT acceptance window.
- A pile-up probability of 5% showed no change in the kurtosis of the net-proton number.
- The additional number of protons added in a central event from double counting had to be on the order of 25 (the mean of the surplus distribution) to produce a significant effect on the kurtosis.

## Context

This work extends studies of net-proton number cumulants as probes of the QCD phase structure, following theoretical calculations of higher order susceptibilities (Gupta, 2011; Luo, 2011; Karsch, 2011) and experimental measurements at RHIC (Aggarwal, 2010; Adamczyk, 2013, 2014; Luo, 2011) and LHC (Rustamov, 2017; Abelev, 2012). The paper uses the UrQMD model (Bass, 1998; Bleicher, 1999) as a baseline event generator to quantify systematic uncertainties from centrality definition, efficiency, and pile-up that complicate the interpretation of experimental results. The findings enable better understanding of systematic uncertainties in experimental analyses and highlight that global baryon number conservation effects observed in the model are in contrast to STAR data, suggesting additional experimental artifacts may be present.
