# Skyrme crystals, nuclear matter and compact stars

> A general review of the crystalline solutions of the generalized Skyrme model and their application to the study of cold nuclear matter at finite density and the Equation of State (EOS) of neutron stars is presented. For the relevant range of densities, the ground state of the Skyrme model in the three torus is shown to correspond to configurations with different symmetries, with a sequence of phase transitions between such configurations. The effects of nonzero finite isospin asymmetry are taken into account by the canonical quantization of isospin collective coordinates, and some thermodynamical and nuclear observables (such as the symmetry energy) are computed as a function of the density. We also explore the extension of the model to accommodate strange degrees of freedom, and find a first order transition for the condensation of kaons in the Skyrme crystal background in a thermodynamically consistent, non-perturbative way. Finally, an approximate EOS of dense matter is constructed by fitting the free parameters of the model to some nuclear observables close to saturation density, which are particularly relevant for the description of nuclear matter. The resulting neutron star mass-radius curves already reasonably satisfy current astrophysical constraints.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how crystalline solutions of the generalized Skyrme model (including a sextic term) can describe cold nuclear matter above saturation density and neutron star interiors, but the authors explicitly state that currently available Skyrme matter solutions only allow modeling above nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), and that the region below saturation must be described by standard nuclear physics methods and joined to the Skyrme crystal results.

## Method

The authors construct Skyrme crystals by imposing periodic boundary conditions on the Skyrme field in a cubic unit cell, expanding the fields in Fourier series truncated to about 30 coefficients, and minimizing the energy functional for different crystal symmetries (simple cubic, face-centered cubic, body-centered cubic). They include the sextic term \(\lag_6 = \lambda^2\pi^4 B_\mu B^\mu\) as the leading contribution at high density, fix the pion mass to its physical value \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, and treat the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\), the Skyrme parameter \(e\), and \(\lambda^2\) as free parameters fitted to nuclear observables near saturation density. For isospin asymmetry, they perform canonical quantization of isospin collective coordinates, and for strange degrees of freedom they extend the Skyrme field to SU(3) and study kaon condensation in the crystal background.

## Key numbers

- The physical pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 186\) MeV in the conventions used, but the authors do not fix \(f_\pi\) to this value; instead they treat it as a free parameter to partly account for neglected terms.
- The pion mass is fixed to its physical value \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV.
- Nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the FCC crystal in the original Skyrme model, the energy at the minimum lattice spacing is only about \(3.7\%\) above the topological energy bound.
- The Skyrme model without the sextic term leads to a maximum neutron star mass of about \(1.5 M_\odot\), whereas neutron stars of up to \(2 M_\odot\) are firmly established and the maximum may be as high as \(2.3-2.5 M_\odot\).
- The number of nucleons inside a neutron star is of order \(M_\odot / m_p \sim 10^{57}\).
- The Fourier coefficients beyond the first order decay to approximately \(4\%\), \(0.3\%\), and \(0.06\%\) of the first-order results.

## Context

This review extends prior work on Skyrme crystals initiated by Klebanov (1985) for the standard Skyrme model and by Kugler and Shtrikman (1988, 1989) for FCC and other symmetries. It builds on the authors' own recent papers (Adam et al., 2021, 2022) on crystal phase transitions, symmetry energy calculations, and kaon condensation. The paper enables the construction of approximate equations of state for neutron star matter that can be compared with astrophysical constraints from gravitational wave measurements, and it identifies the need for future work on coupling Skyrme crystals to electromagnetism to describe the nuclear pasta phase and the low-density crust of neutron stars.
