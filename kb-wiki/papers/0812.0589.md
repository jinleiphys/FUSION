# Bound States and Superconductivity in Dense Fermi Systems

> A quantum field theoretical approach to the thermodynamics of dense Fermi systems is developed for the description of the formation and dissolution of quantum condensates and bound states in dependence of temperature and density. As a model system we study the chiral and superconducting phase transitions in two-flavor quark matter within the NJL model and their interrelation with the formation of quark-antiquark and diquark bound states. The phase diagram of quark matter is evaluated as a function of the diquark coupling strength and a coexistence region of chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity is obtained at very strong coupling. The crossover between Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of diquark bound states and condensation of diquark resonances (Cooper pairs) in the continuum (BCS) is discussed as a Mott effect. This effect consists in the transition of bound states into the continuum of scattering states under the influence of compression and heating. We explain the physics of the Mott transition with special emphasis on role of the Pauli principle for the case of the pion in quark matter.

## Key claim
The paper develops a quantum field theoretical approach to describe the formation and dissolution of quantum condensates and bound states in dense Fermi systems, with the specific claim that the crossover between Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of diquark bound states and condensation of diquark resonances (Cooper pairs) in the continuum (BCS) can be understood as a Mott effect. This effect is conditioned on the diquark coupling strength: a coexistence region of chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity is obtained only at very strong coupling, and the BEC-BCS crossover occurs when the chiral transition (coincident with the Mott transition for mesonic and diquark bound states) occurs inside the 2SC phase.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors and three colors, applying a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to bosonize the four-fermion interaction. They then evaluate the partition function in the mean-field approximation to obtain gap equations for the chiral condensate (dynamical quark mass m) and the diquark pairing gap Δ, and extend the analysis to Gaussian fluctuations to study mesonic bound and scattering states via the pion spectral function. The phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane is constructed by solving the gap equations self-consistently for different values of the diquark coupling strength η_D = G_D/G_S.

## Key numbers
- The Mott transition temperature for pions at vanishing chemical potential (μ_B = 0) and strong diquark coupling η_D = 1.0 is T_Mott = 212.7 MeV.
- For finite chemical potential μ_B = 320 MeV and η_D = 1.0, the critical temperature for the second-order transition from the 2SC phase to normal quark matter is T_c = 95 MeV.
- At T = 0 and η_D = 1.0, the pion remains a true bound state in the 2SC phase even when its mass exceeds the 2m threshold, due to compensation of gapped and ungapped quark modes.
- The diquark coupling strength η_D is treated as a free parameter; for η_D > 1.3 the chiral transition becomes a crossover at all temperatures.

## Context
This work extends the BEC-BCS crossover concept from ultracold atomic gases (Greiner 2003, Zwierlein 2003, 2005) to relativistic quark matter, building on prior NJL model studies of color superconductivity (Buballa 2003) and the Mott transition for pions (Hufner 1996). It enables future theoretical developments such as evaluating the full diquark spectrum, including backreaction of correlations on the mean field, and studying color and charge neutrality conditions relevant for applications to heavy-ion collisions at FAIR Darmstadt (CBM experiment) and JINR Dubna (NICA project), as well as neutron star interiors.
