# Remarks on non-perturbative three--body dynamics and its application to the $KK\bar K$ system

> A formalism is discussed that allows for a straightforward treatment of the relativistic three-body problem while keeping the correct analytic structure. In particular it is demonstrated that sacrificing covariance for analyticity can be justified by the hierarchy of different contributions in the spirit of an effective field theory. For definiteness the formalism is applied to the $KK\bar K$ system allowing for the emergence of the $a_0(980)$ and the $f_0(980)$ as hadronic molecules. For simplicity all inelastic channels are switched off.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a relativistic three-body formalism based on time-ordered perturbation theory, which sacrifices exact covariance for correct analytic structure, can be justified within an effective field theory hierarchy; for the \(KK\bar K\) system, the emergence of three-body bound states depends critically on whether the self-energy of the two-body subsystems is treated fully (including its analytic continuation below threshold) or approximated.

## Method
The authors employ a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation in the isobar approach, where the two-body \(K\bar K\) interaction is parametrized via the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) as stable bound states. The scattering potential includes \(t\)-channel and \(s\)-channel one-kaon exchange diagrams derived from time-ordered perturbation theory, and the propagators incorporate a renormalized self-energy that depends explicitly on both the total energy \(E\) and the loop momentum \(k\). The integral equation is regularized with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) varied from 0.5 GeV to 2.0 GeV, and solved by numerical matrix inversion using a 40-point Gaussian quadrature.

## Key numbers
- The coupling constant for the \(f_0(980)K\bar K\) and \(a_0(980)K\bar K\) vertices is fixed as \(f_S = 3.74\) GeV, derived from the bound-state normalization condition using \(m_K = 495\) MeV and \(m_{f_0}=m_{a_0}=980\) MeV.
- The renormalization factor \(Z\) ranges from 0.223 (for \(\Lambda=0.5\) GeV) to 0.148 (for \(\Lambda=2.0\) GeV) at \(E=0.495\) GeV with \(\alpha=1\).
- For the coupled-channel \(Kf_0(980)\)-\(Ka_0(980)\) calculation with \(\alpha=0\) (self-energy omitted), a three-body bound state appears very close to threshold; with \(\alpha=1\) (full self-energy included), the bound state disappears for the potential truncated to second order in \(f_S^2\).
- The bare kaon mass \(m_K^{(0)}\) in the coupled-channel case with \(\alpha=1\) varies from 0.567 GeV (\(\Lambda=0.5\) GeV) to 1.005 GeV (\(\Lambda=1.5\) GeV), illustrating sizeable renormalization effects.

## Context
This work extends the study of three-body dynamics in hadronic systems, building on the isobar approach used by Albaladejo (2010) and Torres (2011) for the \(KK\bar K\) system, and on the Faddeev-equation studies of Filikhin (2020). It contrasts with the fully covariant formalism of Jackura (2018) and the Khuri-Treiman approach of Niecknig (2012), by providing a simpler framework that preserves correct analytic structure while only mildly violating covariance. The results enable systematic investigation of how approximations to subsystem self-energies affect the existence of three-body bound states, and motivate future inclusion of inelastic channels such as \(\pi\pi K\) and \(\pi\eta K\).
