# Novel Method for Precisely Measuring the $X(3872)$ Mass

> The $X(3872)$ is the first and the most interesting one amongst the abundant $XYZ$ states. Its mass coincides exactly with the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold with an uncertainty of 180 keV. Precise knowledge of its mass is crucial to understand the $X(3872)$. However, whether it is above or below the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold is still unknown. We propose a completely new method to measure the $X(3872)$ mass precisely by measuring the $X(3872)γ$ line shape between 4010 and 4020 MeV, which is strongly sensitive to the $X(3872)$ mass relative to the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold due to a triangle singularity. This method can be applied to experiments which produce copious $D^{*0}\bar D^{*0}$ pairs, such as electron-positron, proton-antiproton and other experiments, and may lead to much more precise knowledge about the $X(3872)$ mass.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the X(3872) mass relative to the D0D̅*0 threshold (δ) can be determined by measuring the X(3872)γ line shape between 4010 and 4020 MeV, which is strongly sensitive to δ due to a triangle singularity; the abstract does not mention that this method requires a short-distance D*0D̅*0 source and that the X(3872) must be reconstructed in modes other than D0D̅0π0 to avoid interference.

## Method

The author calculates the scalar three-point loop integral for the production of X(3872)γ from an S-wave D*0D̅*0 pair using nonrelativistic effective field theory, with the D*0 width fixed at 55.3±1.4 keV from isospin symmetry. The normalized line shape F(E_Xγ) is computed for the X(3872)γ invariant mass range 4010–4020 MeV for different values of δ = m_D0 + m_D*0 − m_X. A Monte Carlo simulation generates synthetic data sets with varying event numbers (127–5015 events) and bin widths (1 MeV and 2 MeV), from which δ is extracted by fitting to the line shape formula.

## Key numbers

- δ ≡ m_D0 + m_D*0 − m_X = (0.00 ± 0.18) MeV from current PDG averages (m_D0 = 1864.84 ± 0.05 MeV, m_D*0 = 2006.85 ± 0.05 MeV, m_X = 3871.69 ± 0.17 MeV)
- D*0 width derived as Γ_* = 55.3 ± 1.4 keV from isospin relations using the charged D*± width of 83.4 ± 1.8 keV
- With ~600 events for δ_in = −50 keV and ~1000 events for δ_in = 50 keV, the sign of δ can be determined at >95% confidence level
- For data set C (3133–5015 events), fitting with 10 bins yields δ = (−53+7−8) keV for δ_in = −50 keV, δ = (−2 ± 5) keV for δ_in = 0 keV, and δ = (55+13−11) keV for δ_in = 50 keV
- The triangle singularity location varies from (4015.2 − i 0.1) MeV for δ = −180 keV to (4016.0 − i 0.4) MeV for δ = 0 keV

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities in hadronic reactions, which have been discussed since the 1960s and reviewed in the context of XYZ states by Guo (2017). The method is designed for experiments producing copious D*0D̅*0 pairs, such as BES-III at electron-positron colliders and the PANDA experiment at proton-antiproton facilities, and can also be adapted if the Z_c(4020) resonance is present. The approach enables a much more precise measurement of δ than the usual method of measuring the X(3872) line shape, which is crucial for understanding whether the X(3872) is a hadronic molecule or another exotic state.
