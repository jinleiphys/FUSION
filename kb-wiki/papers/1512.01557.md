# Progress in Neutron EM Couplings

> An overview of the GW SAID and ITEP groups' effort to analyze pion photoproduction on the neutron-target will be given. The disentanglement of the isoscalar and isovector EM couplings of N* and Delta* resonances does require compatible data on both proton and neutron targets. The final-state interaction plays a critical role in the state-of-the-art analysis in extraction of the gn-->piN data from the deuteron target experiments. Then resonance couplings determined by the SAID PWA technique are then compared to previous findings. The neutron program is important component of the current JLab, MAMI-C, SPring-8, ELSA, and ELPH studies.

# Progress in Neutron EM Couplings

## Key claim

The GW SAID and ITEP groups have extracted neutron electromagnetic couplings of N* and Delta* resonances from pion photoproduction data on deuteron targets, but the results remain significantly less certain than proton couplings because neutron-target data constitute only about 15% of the worldwide database and the final-state interaction corrections, while small (Δσ/σ < 10% for CLAS and MAMI kinematics), must be carefully accounted for to extract elementary γn→πN cross sections from γd→πNN measurements.

## Method

The authors performed partial-wave analyses (PWA) of pion photoproduction on the neutron using the SAID formalism, fitting differential cross sections and polarization observables from γd→π⁻pp and γd→π⁰pn measurements after applying final-state interaction (FSI) corrections developed by the GW-ITEP Collaboration. The FSI corrections included impulse approximation, NN-FSI, and πN-FSI diagrams calculated using SAID phenomenological amplitudes for γN→πN, NN→NN, and πN→πN as inputs, with the Bonn potential for the deuteron wavefunction. The resulting neutron multipoles and helicity amplitudes were compared to previous SAID SN11, BnGa13, Kent12, and PDG14 determinations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-target data constitute only about 15% of the worldwide GW SAID database for pion photoproduction
- Uncertainties of neutral EM couplings (Δ(nA₁/₂)) for 4-star low-lying N* resonances vary between 25% and 140%, while charged EM couplings (Δ(pA₁/₂)) vary between 7% and 42%
- FSI correction factor for CLAS and A2 kinematics was found to be small: Δσ/σ < 10%
- The FSI correction factor R(E,θ) varies from 10% to 30% depending on kinematics, with R < 1 overall
- New CLAS cross sections quadrupled the world database for γn→π⁻p above E = 1 GeV
- CLAS energy binning is 50 MeV or 100 MeV; A2 at MAMI achieves 2 to 4 MeV binning
- New 589 dσ/dΩ from A2 at MAMI contribute about 160% to the previous world π⁰n database, covering E = 180–800 MeV with ΔE = 20 MeV and relative errors of 1.5–3%
- Neutron helicity amplitude for N(1535)1/2⁻: nA₁/₂ = −58 ± 6 (GeV)⁻¹/² × 10⁻³ (SAID GB12)
- Neutron helicity amplitude for N(1520)3/2⁻: nA₁/₂ = −46 ± 6, nA₃/₂ = −115 ± 5 (GeV)⁻¹/² × 10⁻³ (SAID GB12)
- Neutron helicity amplitude for N(1675)5/2⁻: nA₁/₂ = −58 ± 2, nA₃/₂ = −80 ± 5 (GeV)⁻¹/² × 10⁻³ (SAID GB12)
- Upcoming JLab CLAS g13 data will bring about 11,000 new measurements, quadrupling the world γn→π⁻p database, covering E = 400–2500 MeV and θ = 18°–152°

## Context

This work extends the SAID partial-wave analysis program (previous solutions SN11, GB12, GZ12) by incorporating new CLAS and MAMI deuteron-target measurements with FSI corrections developed by the GW-ITEP Collaboration. The results are compared to the BnGa13 analysis from the Bonn-Gatchina group, the Kent12 analysis from Kent State University, and the PDG14 averaged values. The neutron program is a critical component of current studies at JLab, MAMI-C, SPring-8, ELSA, and ELPH, and the new high-precision data will enable more reliable determination of isoscalar and isovector electromagnetic couplings of low-lying baryon resonances, particularly for states like N(1675)5/2⁻ which have stronger couplings to the neutron than to the proton.
