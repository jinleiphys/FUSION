# Application of superconducting-superfluid magnetohydrodynamics to nuclear "pasta" in neutron stars

> A mixture of superconducting and superfluid nuclear liquids of protons coupled to the ultrarelativistic electron gas, and neutrons is considered. In the magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) approximation, the energy-momentum (stress) tensor is derived, and the entrainment contribution is found in the explicit form. It is shown that this contribution generates a force density, when a superfluid velocity lag and the magnetic field are simultaneously present. This force may be important in the nuclear "pasta" phase in neutron stars, if the proton and neutron Cooper pairing in the pasta phase is taken into account. It is found that if the liquid-crystalline matter of the pasta phase is superfluid and superconducting, then magnitude of the forces acting upon element of matter at typical magnetic field and the superfluid velocity lag, under certain conditions may become large enough to induce a critical stress in the neutron star crust. As an application, the necessary conditions for triggering of a starquake are found in the pasta phase of neutron stars, assuming that the nuclei are flat slabs in parallel magnetic field. The present model includes two independent local parameters: the superfluid velocity lag and the magnetic field. Possible links between the entrainment force and the magnetar starquake triggering mechanism, and some open problems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the nuclear "pasta" phase of neutron stars, if the matter is both superfluid and superconducting, the entrainment force generated by a superfluid velocity lag and a magnetic field may become large enough to induce critical stress in the crust, potentially triggering a starquake, but only under specific conditions where the magnetic field penetrates the pasta structure and the velocity lag is perturbed.

## Method

The author derives the energy-momentum (stress) tensor for a mixture of superconducting protons, superfluid neutrons, and ultrarelativistic electrons in the magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) approximation, including the entrainment contribution in explicit form. The formalism is then applied to a toy model of the "lasagna" phase (flat nuclear slabs) in a parallel uniform magnetic field, assuming negligible Josephson tunneling between slabs and that the slab width is much smaller than the London penetration depth. The total force on a column of lasagna matter is computed by linearizing the force density to first order in a perturbation of the superfluid velocity lag.

## Key numbers

- The entrainment number density inside the slab is \(n_{np} \sim -3.692 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-3}\) at baryon density 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Typical slab width is \(2r_N \sim 10\) fm, and typical separation between slabs is \(2r_c \sim 20\) fm.
- The total force on a \(1\) cm\(^2 \times d\) column of lasagna matter is approximately \(1.971 \times 10^{30} \times (d/1\,\mathrm{cm}) \times (\delta\mathrm{w}/1\,\mathrm{cm\,s^{-1}}) \times (B/10^{14}\,\mathrm{G})\) dyn.
- The perturbation of the velocity lag \(\mathbf{v}_p - \mathbf{v}_n\) is assumed to be \(\sim 1\) cm s\(^{-1}\) in typical conditions.
- The effective shear modulus of the polycrystalline crust is given by \(\mu_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.3778 \frac{n_N Z^2 e^2}{2a}\), evaluated at baryon density \(7.943 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\) with \(n_N = 1.750 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the MHD formalism for superfluid-superconducting mixtures developed by Mendell (1991) and Sedrakian (1995), and builds on the stress tensor results independently obtained by Chamel (2017). It applies the formalism to the nuclear pasta phase, using an analogy with layered superconductors from condensed matter physics (Tinkham, 1996). The paper enables future studies of starquake triggering mechanisms in magnetars, linking the entrainment force to crustal stresses, and identifies open problems such as the ground state structure of the pasta phase and the effective penetration depth of magnetic fields in realistic pasta configurations.
