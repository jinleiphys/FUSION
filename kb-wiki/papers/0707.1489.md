# Is there an eta-3He quasi--bound state ?

> The observed variation of the total cross section for the dp -> 3He eta reaction near threshold means that the magnitude of the s--wave amplitude falls very rapidly with the eta centre--of--mass momentum. It is shown here that recent measurements of the momentum dependence of the angular distribution imply a strong variation also in the phase of this amplitude. Such a behaviour is that expected from a quasi--bound or virtual eta-3He state. The interpretation can be investigated further through measurements of the deuteron or proton analysing powers and/or spin--correlations.

## Key claim

The rapid variation of both the magnitude and phase of the s-wave amplitude for the dp ÔåÆ ┬│He ╬À reaction near threshold is consistent with a nearby pole in the complex momentum plane, which could correspond to either a quasi-bound or a virtual ╬À-┬│He state; however, no production observable can determine whether the pole lies on the bound or virtual state plane.

## Method

The authors analyzed total cross section and angular distribution data for the dp ÔåÆ ┬│He ╬À reaction measured at COSY-ANKE and COSY-11 near threshold (excess energy Q < 11 MeV). They parametrized the s-wave amplitude using a two-pole form and introduced a complex constant for the p-wave amplitudes, then fitted simultaneously the total cross section and the asymmetry parameter ╬▒ (derived from the linear cos╬©╬À dependence of the differential cross section) to extract the pole positions and amplitude parameters.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of total cross section data from COSY-ANKE (Timo, 2007) and COSY-11 (Smyrski, 2007) by incorporating angular distribution measurements to demonstrate that the s-wave phase variation, not just the magnitude, is consistent with a nearby pole. The concept of ╬À-mesic nuclei was introduced by Liu and Haider (1988), who estimated that ┬╣┬▓C would be the lightest binding nucleus based on small ╬À-nucleon scattering lengths; later larger values of Re(a_╬ÀN) suggested binding even in light systems like ╬À-┬│He. The interpretation can be further tested through measurements of deuteron and proton analyzing powers and spin correlations, which are planned at COSY-ANKE, and through photoproduction data from MAMI (Krusche, in analysis).
