# Is there an eta-3He quasi--bound state ?

> The observed variation of the total cross section for the dp -> 3He eta reaction near threshold means that the magnitude of the s--wave amplitude falls very rapidly with the eta centre--of--mass momentum. It is shown here that recent measurements of the momentum dependence of the angular distribution imply a strong variation also in the phase of this amplitude. Such a behaviour is that expected from a quasi--bound or virtual eta-3He state. The interpretation can be investigated further through measurements of the deuteron or proton analysing powers and/or spin--correlations.

## Key claim

The rapid variation of both the magnitude and phase of the s-wave amplitude for the dp → ³He η reaction near threshold is consistent with a nearby pole in the complex momentum plane, which could correspond to either a quasi-bound or a virtual η-³He state; however, no production observable can determine whether the pole lies on the bound or virtual state plane.

## Method

The authors analyzed total cross section and angular distribution data for the dp → ³He η reaction measured at COSY-ANKE and COSY-11 near threshold (excess energy Q < 11 MeV). They parametrized the s-wave amplitude using a two-pole form and introduced a complex constant for the p-wave amplitudes, then fitted simultaneously the total cross section and the asymmetry parameter α (derived from the linear cosθη dependence of the differential cross section) to extract the pole positions and amplitude parameters.

## Key numbers

- Pole position from combined fit: p₁ = [(-4 ± 7⁺²₋₁) - i(19 ± 2 ± 1)] MeV/c, corresponding to Q₀ = [(-0.30 ± 0.15_stat ± 0.04_syst) ± i(0.21 ± 0.29_stat ± 0.06_syst)] MeV (sign of imaginary part undetermined)
- Second pole: p₂ = [(103 ± 4) - i(74 ± 12⁺¹₋₂)] MeV/c
- Normalization constant f_B = (50 ± 8) (nb/sr)^(1/2)
- p-wave amplitude ratio: C/f_B = [(-0.47 ± 0.08 ± 0.20) + i(0.33 ± 0.02 ± 0.12)] (GeV/c)^(-1)
- η-³He scattering length: a = (± 10.9 + 1.0 i) fm (two signs of real part reflect quasi-bound or virtual state ambiguity)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of total cross section data from COSY-ANKE (Timo, 2007) and COSY-11 (Smyrski, 2007) by incorporating angular distribution measurements to demonstrate that the s-wave phase variation, not just the magnitude, is consistent with a nearby pole. The concept of η-mesic nuclei was introduced by Liu and Haider (1988), who estimated that ¹²C would be the lightest binding nucleus based on small η-nucleon scattering lengths; later larger values of Re(a_ηN) suggested binding even in light systems like η-³He. The interpretation can be further tested through measurements of deuteron and proton analyzing powers and spin correlations, which are planned at COSY-ANKE, and through photoproduction data from MAMI (Krusche, in analysis).
