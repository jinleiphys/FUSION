# Investigation of Heat Conductivity in Relativistic Systems using a Partonic Cascade

> Motivated by the classical picture of heat flow we construct a stationary temperature gradient in a relativistic microscopic transport model. Employing the relativistic Navier-Stokes ansatz we extract the heat conductivity κ for a massless Boltzmann gas using only binary collisions with isotropic cross sections. We compare the numerical results to analytical expressions from different theories and discuss the final results. The directly extracted value for the heat conductivity can be referred to as a literature reference within the numerical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The authors extract the heat conductivity coefficient \(\kappa\) for a massless classical Boltzmann gas from numerical solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, finding that the value \(\kappa_{\rm BAMPS} = (2.59 \pm 0.07)/\sigma_{22}\) agrees best with the resummed transient relativistic fluid dynamics (RTRFD) prediction, while the standard Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation underestimates it by about 22%.

## Method

A stationary temperature gradient was established in a static box using thermal reservoirs at fixed temperatures (0.5 GeV left, 0.3 GeV right) and fugacities, with no net pressure gradient. The relativistic Boltzmann equation was solved numerically using the partonic cascade BAMPS, considering only binary collisions with constant isotropic cross sections. The heat flow \(q^x\) was extracted from the energy-momentum tensor and particle four-flow in the Eckart frame, and the heat conductivity was obtained via the relativistic Navier-Stokes relation \(q^x = -\kappa \partial_x T(x)\), using an analytic expression for the temperature gradient.

## Key numbers

*   Extracted heat conductivity: \(\kappa_{\rm BAMPS} = (2.59 \pm 0.07)/\sigma_{22}\) for a massless classical Boltzmann gas with isotropic binary collisions.
*   For cross section \(\sigma_{22} = 43\) mb, \(\kappa = 0.607672 \pm 0.0411\) fm\(^{-2}\).
*   For cross section \(\sigma_{22} = 0.043\) mb, \(\kappa = 609.695698 \pm 8.4513\) fm\(^{-2}\).
*   For cross section \(\sigma_{22} = 430\) mb, \(\kappa = 0.060449 \pm 0.0013\) fm\(^{-2}\).
*   Reservoir temperatures: \(T_L^{\rm res} = 0.5\) GeV, \(T_R^{\rm res} = 0.3\) GeV; reservoir fugacities: \(\lambda_L^{\rm res} = 1.0\), \(\lambda_R^{\rm res} = 7.72\); resulting constant pressure \(p = 13.25\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the stationary gradients method previously applied to shear viscosity (Wesp, Reining2) to the heat conductivity of a relativistic dilute gas. It directly tests several theoretical predictions: the Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation (Israel, Groot) giving \(\kappa = 2/\sigma_{22}\), an alternative 14-moment choice (Denicol 2010) giving \(\kappa = 3/\sigma_{22}\), Chapman-Enskog theory (Groot) giving \(\kappa = 2.44/\sigma_{22}\), and the resummed transient relativistic fluid dynamics (Denicol 2012) giving \(\kappa = 2.5536/\sigma_{22}\). The BAMPS result confirms the RTRFD prediction and provides a benchmark for future studies including inelastic scatterings and more realistic cross sections.
