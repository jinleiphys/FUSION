# Comments on some recent papers on the hyperspherical approach in few-body systems

## Key claim

The techniques for treating few-body reactions and bound states within the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) approach presented as original by Kievsky, Viviani, and Rosati were in fact previously known and applied in the literature, and the authors were aware of this prior work.

## Method

The paper compares the variational technique for calculating scattering states described by Kievsky et al. (1995) with the earlier K-harmonics method generalized to the three-nucleon scattering problem by Zickendraht et al. (1968), showing that both approaches write the wave function as a sum of a compact interaction-region term and an asymptotic term. For bound-state calculations, the author demonstrates that the selection prescription for reducing the number of HH basis states used by Kievsky et al. (1997) is identical to that proposed earlier in Ref. [2] and subsequently used in Refs. [3,4,5,6] for three- and four-nucleon calculations.

## Key numbers

- No original numerical results are presented in this comment paper.

## Context

This comment addresses the priority and attribution of methodological developments in the hyperspherical harmonic approach to few-body nuclear physics. It extends the historical record by pointing out that the variational technique for continuum states was first developed by Zickendraht et al. (1968) and subsequently refined by other authors (1971, 1973), with applications to photodisintegration of 3H by Vashakidze and Zickendraht (1981). The bound-state selection prescription for HH basis states was originally proposed in Ref. [2] and used in Refs. [3,4,5,6] before being presented as new by Kievsky et al. (1997). The comment enables proper attribution of these methodological developments and clarifies the historical progression of the HH approach for few-body systems.
