# Non-global Structure of the O(α_s^2) Dijet Soft Function

> High energy scattering processes involving jets generically involve matrix elements of light- like Wilson lines, known as soft functions. These describe the structure of soft contributions to observables and encode color and kinematic correlations between jets. We compute the dijet soft function to O(α_s^2) as a function of the two jet invariant masses, focusing on terms not determined by its renormalization group evolution that have a non-separable dependence on these masses. Our results include non-global single and double logarithms, and analytic results for the full set of non-logarithmic contributions as well. Using a recent result for the thrust constant, we present the complete O(α_s^2) soft function for dijet production in both position and momentum space.

## Key claim

The paper computes the full O(α_s^2) dijet soft function analytically, including all non-global structures (logarithmic and non-logarithmic) that depend on the ratio of the two jet invariant masses, which were previously unknown and only conjectured. The authors show that non-global logarithms in observables can be understood as logs of ratios of multiple soft scales left over in the perturbative expansion of the relevant soft function after a standard factorization theorem has been established.

## Method

The authors calculate the dijet hemisphere soft function S(l1,l2,μ) and its Fourier transform to position space S̃(x1,x2,μ) at O(α_s^2) using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET). They organize contributions by whether one or both hemispheres are populated by final-state particles, obtaining all new non-global structure from two-particle final states (gluons or quark-antiquark pairs) going into opposite hemispheres. The renormalization group evolution and known anomalous dimensions constrain the logarithmic terms, while the remaining non-global functions are computed analytically from the relevant Feynman diagrams.

## Key numbers

- One-loop constant t1 = -C_F π^2/2 in the exponentiated position-space soft function
- Two-loop thrust constants from Monni et al. (2011): s2[C_F C_A] = -28.242 ± 0.003, s2[n_f] = 21.692 ± 0.003
- The non-global double logarithm in the double cumulant soft function has color structure C_F C_A and matches the non-global log first identified by Dasgupta and Salam (2001) from the single-hemisphere mass distribution
- A single non-global logarithm appears in both color structures C_F C_A and C_F T_R n_f when the ratio of hemisphere scales becomes large
- The power-suppressed contribution to the single-hemisphere cumulant contains a double log with coefficient -(α_s/2π)^2 (C_F C_A/2)[2 ln^2 2 - (5/4)ln 3 + 4 Li_2(-1/2) + π^2/3 - 1/6] ln^2 ρ_R

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework for dijet invariant mass distributions in e+e- annihilation developed by Fleming et al. (2007, 2008) and provides the missing O(α_s^2) soft function needed for NNLL and N^3LL resummation. It directly addresses the non-global logarithms first identified by Dasgupta and Salam (2001, 2002) and provides analytic results that replace previous numerical extractions from EVENT2 by Becher and Schwartz (2008), Hoang and Kluth (2008), and Chien and Schwartz (2010). The results enable resummation to N^3LL accuracy in doubly-differential cross sections and are also applicable to event shapes in hadron-hadron collisions such as beam thrust or 0-jettiness introduced by Stewart et al. (2009, 2010).
