# Lepton-nucleon interactions at the next-to-the-leading order

> Next-to-Leading-Order (NLO) effects play a crucial role in tests of the Standard Model (SM), and require careful theoretical evaluation. Electroweak physics, which has just entered the precision age, is an excellent place to search for new physics, but also requires considerable theoretical input. We show how we applied computational packages such as FeynArts, FormCalc, Form and LoopTools for the evaluation of one-loop electroweak and hadronic radiative corrections.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that next-to-leading-order (NLO) electroweak and hadronic radiative corrections can be computed using automated packages (FeynArts, FormCalc, Form, LoopTools), but the parity-violating asymmetry results shown are model-independent only for the asymmetry itself, not for absolute cross sections, and were computed without strange quark contributions.

## Method

The authors extended FeynArts with Dirac and Pauli form factors in dipole/monopole form (no strange quark contribution) and used the on-shell renormalization scheme in Feynman gauge to compute one-loop electroweak radiative corrections to parity-violating asymmetries in electron-proton scattering. For the hadronic sector, they developed a Computational Hadronic Model (CHM) based on Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPTh) and used it to extract strong coupling constants from experimental branching ratios of baryon resonance decays. Infrared divergences were treated with soft and hard photon bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- NLO parity-violating effects in electron-proton scattering are of the order of 20% for momentum transfers up to 1.0 GeV² in forward scattering.
- Branching ratios for the decays Σ*⁺ → Σ⁰ + π⁺, Σ*⁻ → Λ + π⁺, Ξ*⁰ → Ξ*⁻ + π⁺, and Δ⁺⁺ → p + π⁺ are centered about 1.4, 1.4, 1.3, and 1.5 respectively.
- The extracted strong coupling constant C is |C| = 1.0 ± 0.2, and the H coupling constant is H = -1.5 ± 0.5.
- These results are consistent with prior work (Butler et al.) giving |C| = 1.2 ± 0.1 and H = -2.1 ± 0.7.

## Context

This work extends automated NLO calculation techniques from the Standard Model to both electroweak and hadronic sectors, building on the FeynArts/FormCalc framework originally designed for high-energy physics. The electroweak results are compared with the G0 experimental asymmetry and theoretical predictions from the G0 collaboration, showing a discrepancy attributed to possible non-negligible strange quark content in the nucleon. The hadronic CHM model enables future automatization of NLO radiative corrections for production and decay channels in hadronic physics, with results consistent with earlier extractions by Butler et al.
