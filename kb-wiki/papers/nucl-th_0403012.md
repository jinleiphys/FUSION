# Aspects and consequences of a dressed-quark-gluon vertex

> Features of the dressed-quark-gluon vertex and their role in the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations are explored. It is argued that quenched lattice data indicate the existence of net attraction in the colour-octet projection of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel. This attraction affects the uniformity with which solutions of truncated equations converge pointwise to solutions of the complete gap and vertex equations. For current-quark masses less than the scale set by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, the dependence of the dressed-quark-gluon vertex on the current-quark mass is weak. The study employs a vertex model whose diagrammatic content is explicitly enumerable. That enables the systematic construction of a vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel and thereby an exploration of the consequences for the strong interaction spectrum of attraction in the colour-octet channel. With rising current-quark mass the rainbow-ladder truncation is shown to provide an increasingly accurate estimate of a bound state's mass. Moreover, the calculated splitting between vector and pseudoscalar meson masses vanishes as the current-quark mass increases, which argues for the mass of the pseudoscalar partner of the Υ(1S) to be above 9.4 GeV. The absence of colour-antitriplet diquarks from the strong interaction spectrum is contingent upon the net amount of attraction in the octet projected quark-antiquark scattering kernel. There is a window within which diquarks appear. The amount of attraction suggested by lattice results is outside this domain.

## Key claim

Quenched lattice data indicate net attraction in the colour-octet projection of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, and this attraction affects the convergence of truncated solutions to the complete gap and vertex equations; the amount of attraction suggested by lattice results lies outside the window where colour-antitriplet diquarks would appear in the strong interaction spectrum.

## Method

The authors employ a model for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex in which the sum of the Abelian-like one-loop term and the three-gluon vertex term is replaced by a single term with a global coupling strength modifier C, enabling the effects of attraction in the colour-octet channel to be mimicked without specifying a detailed form for the three-gluon vertex. Using a confining delta-function model for the dressed-gluon interaction, the gap and vertex equations reduce to coupled algebraic equations that are solved self-consistently. The resulting vertex is then used to construct a vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel for meson and diquark bound states.

## Key numbers

* The fit to quenched lattice data at a current-quark mass of 60 MeV yields C = 0.51, with average relative error 25% and standard deviation 70%.
* For C = 0.51, the infrared values of the vertex functions at zero momentum are: alpha_1(0) = 1 + C/2 = 1.255; alpha_2(0) = -C(2-C)(2+3C)/(2+C)^2; alpha_3(0) = -sqrt(8) C / sqrt(2+C).
* The mass of the pseudoscalar partner of the Upsilon(1S) is argued to be above 9.4 GeV.
* In the chiral limit with C = 0.51, the infrared mass function M(0,0) = mu_0^0 = sqrt(2)*(2+C)^(3/2)/(4+6C).

## Context

This work extends the systematic Dyson-Schwinger equation truncation scheme of Detmold (2003) by including the effect of the three-gluon vertex through the colour-octet attraction parameter C, which was neglected in that earlier study. The results enable a quantitative assessment of the accuracy of the rainbow-ladder truncation for meson masses, showing it becomes increasingly accurate as current-quark mass rises. The analysis also identifies the window of octet-channel attraction within which colour-antitriplet diquarks would appear, and shows that the attraction suggested by lattice data lies outside that window, explaining the absence of such diquarks from the spectrum.
