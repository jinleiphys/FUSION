# Nuclear structure effects in light muonic atoms

> Nuclear structure corrections to energy levels of light muonic atoms are derived with particular attention to the nuclear mass dependence. The obtained result for the 2P-2S transition of 1.717(19) meV serves for determination of the nuclear charge radius from the spectroscopic measurement in muonic deuterium.

## Key claim

Nuclear structure corrections to energy levels of light muonic atoms are derived with particular attention to nuclear mass dependence, yielding a 2P-2S transition energy of 1.717(20) meV for muonic deuterium; the abstract's value of 1.717(19) meV omits the final uncertainty of 0.020 meV that includes contributions from neutron polarizability, neglected corrections to the electric dipole operator, and higher-order terms.

## Method

The authors derive nuclear polarizability shifts using a perturbative expansion in the small parameter of nuclear excitation energy over muon mass, starting from second-order Coulomb interaction in the nonrelativistic approximation and extending to relativistic (Dirac) muon Hamiltonians. They separate contributions into electric dipole polarizability, Coulomb corrections, quadrupole excitations, finite nucleon size effects, modified Zemach moments, magnetic interactions, and higher-order recoil corrections, then evaluate these numerically for muonic deuterium using the AV18 potential solved with a discrete variable representation method.

## Key numbers

- Total nuclear structure correction for the 2P-2S transition in muonic deuterium: 1.717(20) meV
- Leading electric dipole polarizability contribution (δ₀E): 1.910 meV
- Coulomb correction δC1E: -0.255 meV; δC2E: -0.006 meV
- Quadrupole contributions: δQ0E = -0.042 meV, δQ1E = 0.139 meV, δQ2E = -0.061 meV
- Finite size correction δFSE: 0.020 meV; δFZE: -0.018 meV
- Relativistic correction δRE: -0.026 meV
- Higher-order correction δHOE: 0.004 meV
- Magnetic correction δME: -0.008 meV
- Proton polarizability contribution δPE: 0.043(3) meV
- Neutron polarizability contribution δNE: 0.016(8) meV
- Proton elastic contribution from muonic hydrogen: -36.9(2.4) μeV (for 2S state)
- Assumed neutron inelastic contribution: 13.5 μeV with 50% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the perturbative formalism previously developed by the same authors for muonic deuterium (Pachucki and Wienczek, 2015) and improves upon calculations by Bacca et al. (2012) for muonic helium and muonic deuterium by correcting mass dependencies and including higher-order terms. It also addresses discrepancies with the dispersion relation approach of Carlson et al. (2011), which suffered from insufficient inelastic scattering data from the deuteron. The results enable more precise determination of nuclear charge radii from muonic atom spectroscopy, particularly for resolving the proton charge radius puzzle highlighted by Pohl et al. (2010) and Antognini et al. (2013).
