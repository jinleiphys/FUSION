# Global Properties of Spherical Nuclei Obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculations with the Gogny Force

> Selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations have been performed with the Gogny force for nuclei along several constant Z and constant N chains, with the purpose of extracting the macroscopic part of the binding energy using the Strutinsky prescription. The macroscopic energy obtained in this way is compared to current liquid drop formulas. The evolution of the single particle levels derived from the HFB calculations along the constant Z and constant N chains and the variations of the different kinds of nuclear radii are also analysed. Those radii are shown to follow isospin-dependent three parameter laws close to the phenomenological formulas which reproduce experimental data.

## Key claim

The macroscopic part of the binding energy extracted from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny force agrees with the Myers and Swiatecki liquid-drop formula to within 3 MeV, but with a significantly larger correction to the Coulomb energy for the diffuseness of the proton density and with unequal asymmetry coefficients in the volume and surface terms.

## Method

Self-consistent HFB calculations using the Gogny D1S effective interaction were performed for spherical nuclei along constant-Z and constant-N chains, including Ca, Sr, Sn, Sm, Pb, and Th isotopes and the N=50, 82, and 126 isotone chains. The Strutinsky smoothing procedure was applied to the HF single-particle level schemes to extract shell corrections, and the macroscopic part of the binding energy was obtained by subtracting both the neutron and proton shell corrections and the pairing correlation energy from the HFB energy. The resulting macroscopic energies were fitted to a liquid-drop formula, and the HFB root-mean-square radii for neutron, proton, charge, and mass distributions were fitted to isospin-dependent three-parameter formulas.

## Key numbers

* The fitted liquid-drop formula for the macroscopic energy is: E_LD = [15.65(1-1.92I^2) A - 18.92(1-2.1I^2) A^(2/3) - 0.73 Z^2/A^(1/3) + 1.99 Z^2/A] MeV, where I=(N-Z)/A.
* The difference between the calculated macroscopic energy and the fitted liquid-drop formula does not exceed 3 MeV, representing less than 0.5% of the total HF energy.
* The fitted neutron radius constant is r^n_0 = 1.17 (1 + 0.12 I + 3.29/A) fm.
* The fitted proton radius constant is r^p_0 = 1.21 (1 - 0.14 I + 1.83/A) fm.
* The fitted charge radius constant is r^ch_0 = 1.22 (1 - 0.15 I + 2.32/A) fm.
* The fitted ratio of proton to neutron r.m.s. radii is r_p/r_n = 1.04 (1 - 0.27 I - 1.12/A).
* The leading coefficient for the total mass radius constant is r^tot_0 = 1.19 (1 + 0.03 I + 2.70/A) fm, almost equal to the usual value of 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous preliminary studies by Pomorska and Kleban (2000) on a smaller set of nuclei and provides a systematic parametrization of nuclear properties from Gogny-force HFB calculations. The results are compared to the liquid-drop formula of Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and to relativistic mean field calculations by Reinhard (1995) and others, showing close agreement. The parametrization of the proton-to-neutron radius ratio is found to be robust enough for predicting neutron radii of nuclei near the neutron drip line or superheavy elements, and has been validated for all nuclei between the drip lines in the range 20 < A < 318.
