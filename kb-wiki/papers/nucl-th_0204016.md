# Dynamical Interpretation of the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Exchange Currents in the Large $N_C$ Limit

> Expression of the nucleon-nucleon Interaction to order $1/N_C$ in terms of Fermi Invariants allows a dynamical interpretation of the interaction and leads to a consistent construction of the associated interaction currents to order $1/N_C$. The numerically significant components of 4 different modern realistic phenomenological interaction models are shown to admit very similar meson exchange interpretations in the large $N_C$ limit. Moreover the ratio of the volume integrals of the leading, next-to-leading and next-to-next leading order terms in these interaction models is roughly 300:5-10:0.1, which corresponds fairly well to the ratios of $1/N_C^2$ between the terms that would be suggested by the $1/N_C$ expansion if $N_C=3$. The $N_C$ dependence of the electromagnetic and axial interaction currents that are associated with these interaction components is derived and compared to that of the corresponding single nucleon currents.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the numerically significant components of four modern realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models (V18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen(93), and Paris) admit very similar meson exchange interpretations in the large \(N_C\) limit, with the caveat that the large \(N_C\) limit itself cannot determine the radial behavior of the potential components.

## Method

The author expressed the nucleon-nucleon interaction in terms of the five Fermi invariants (S, V, T, A, P) and derived the leading-order \(1/N_C\) expansion of these components. The Fermi invariant potential components were then computed from four phenomenological interaction models (V18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen(93), and Paris) by transforming their standard spin-representation potentials using relations that retain only leading terms in \(1/m_N^2\) (i.e., \(1/N_C^2\)). Volume integrals of the leading-order components were calculated and compared across models, and effective meson-nucleon coupling constants were extracted from these volume integrals.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of volume integrals of leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next leading order terms in the interaction models is roughly 300:5-10:0.1, corresponding to ratios of \(1/N_C^2\) between terms if \(N_C=3\).
- Volume integrals of the isospin independent scalar potential \(v_S^+\) range from -6.3 fm\(^2\) (Paris) to -13.5 fm\(^2\) (CD-Bonn).
- Volume integrals of the isospin independent vector potential \(v_V^+\) range from 8.7 fm\(^2\) (Nijmegen(93)) to 11.6 fm\(^2\) (CD-Bonn).
- Volume integrals of the isospin dependent pseudoscalar potential \(v_P^-\) range from 323 fm\(^2\) (Nijmegen(93)) to 360 fm\(^2\) (V18).
- Effective scalar meson coupling constants \(g_\sigma\) range from 7.6 (Paris) to 11.2 (CD-Bonn), assuming scalar meson mass 600 MeV.
- Effective pion-nucleon coupling constants \(g_\pi\) range from 12.7 (Nijmegen(93)) to 13.4 (V18).
- Effective \(\rho\) meson tensor coupling \(\kappa_\rho\) ranges from 6.3 (Nijmegen(93)) to 10.1 (Paris), using \(g_\rho^2/4\pi \sim 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the Manohar-Kaplan scaling relations (Kaplan, 1990s) for the \(1/N_C\) expansion of nucleon-nucleon interaction components by showing that four modern phenomenological potentials (V18, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen(93), and Paris) all satisfy these scaling relations and admit consistent meson exchange interpretations. The paper also derives the \(N_C\) scaling of the associated electromagnetic and axial exchange currents, showing that axial exchange currents scale as \(1/N_C^3\) (smaller by \(1/N_C^4\) than the single nucleon axial current), which corresponds well with established nuclear phenomenology. This enables a systematic connection between QCD-based large \(N_C\) expansions and realistic nuclear force models, and provides a framework for constructing consistent interaction currents for nuclear reactions.
