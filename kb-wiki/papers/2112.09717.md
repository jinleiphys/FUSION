# Incorporating the weak mixing angle dependence to reconcile the neutron skin measurement on 208Pb by PREX-II

> The only available electroweak measurement of the $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ neutron skin, $ΔR_{\rm{np}}$, performed by the PREX-II Collaboration through polarized electron-lead scattering, shows a mild tension with respect to both the theoretical nuclear-model predictions and a host of measurements. However, the dependence on the weak mixing angle should be incorporated in the calculation, since its low-energy value is experimentally poorly known. We first repeat the PREX-II analysis confirming their measurement by fixing the weak mixing angle to its standard model value. Then, we show the explicit dependence of the PREX-II measurement on the weak mixing angle, obtaining that it is fully degenerate with the neutron skin. To break this degeneracy, we exploit the weak mixing angle measurement from atomic parity violation on lead, obtaining a slightly thinner neutron skin but with about doubled uncertainties, possibly easing the PREX tension. Relying on the theoretical prediction, $ΔR_{\rm{np}}^{\mathrm{th}}\approx 0.13-0.19\ \mathrm{fm}$, and using it as a prior in the fit, we find a weak mixing angle value about $1.2σ$ smaller than the standard model prediction. Thus, we suggest a possible solution of the PREX-II tension by showing that, considering its underlying dependence on the weak mixing angle, the PREX-II neutron skin determination could be in agreement with the other available measurements and predictions if the weak mixing angle at the proper energy scale is smaller than the standard model prediction.

# Incorporating the weak mixing angle dependence to reconcile the neutron skin measurement on 208Pb by PREX-II

## Key claim

The PREX-II neutron skin measurement on 208Pb is fully degenerate with the weak mixing angle sin²θ_W, so that a thinner neutron skin compatible with theoretical predictions can be obtained if the weak mixing angle at the PREX-II energy scale is smaller than the Standard Model prediction; the authors find that using the theoretical neutron skin prediction as a prior yields a weak mixing angle value about 1.2σ smaller than the Standard Model value.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the PREX-II parity-violating asymmetry measurement using the DREPHA code under the distorted-wave Born approximation, computing asymmetries for a range of weak radii and weak mixing angles with a symmetrized two-parameter Fermi parametrization of the weak nuclear density. They then performed a combined fit of the PREX-II data with the atomic parity violation measurement on 208Pb, exploiting the different sensitivities of the two experiments to break the degeneracy between the neutron skin and the weak mixing angle. Finally, they incorporated a Gaussian prior from theoretical energy density functional predictions of the neutron skin.

## Key numbers

- PREX-II measured parity-violating asymmetry: A_pv^meas = 550 ± 16 (stat.) ± 8 (syst.) ppb
- Reanalysis with fixed Standard Model weak mixing angle gives ΔR_np = 0.276 ± 0.078 fm, confirming the published PREX-II result of 0.278 ± 0.078 fm
- Combined PREX-II + APV(Pb) fit yields ΔR_np^Comb = 0.262 ± 0.136 fm and sin²θ_W = 0.237 ± 0.014 at momentum transfer 8 ≲ Q ≲ 78 MeV
- Combined fit including theoretical prior (ΔR_np^th = 0.16 ± 0.03 fm) yields ΔR_np^comb+th = 0.164 ± 0.029 fm and sin²θ_W = 0.228 ± 0.008
- Theoretical neutron skin prediction range: ΔR_np^th ≈ 0.13–0.19 fm
- Standard Model weak mixing angle at low energies: s²W_SM = 0.23857 ± 0.00005
- APV(Pb) weak charge measurement: Q_W^APV(Pb) = −117 ± 5
- PREX-II mean momentum transfer: Q_PREX-II ≈ 78 MeV
- APV(Pb) momentum transfer: Q_APV(Pb) ∼ 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the PREX-II measurement (PhysRevLett.126.172502) by incorporating the previously neglected dependence on the weak mixing angle, which is poorly constrained at low energies. The PREX-II result (ΔR_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm from the combined PREX-I+II analysis) shows tension with multiple non-electroweak determinations from electric-dipole polarizability (PhysRevC.85.041302, PhysRevC.92.064304, PhysRevC.104.024329), antiprotonic atoms (PhysRevLett.87.082501), proton-nucleus scattering (PhysRevC.82.044611), coherent pion photoproduction (PhysRevLett.112.242502), and neutron star observables (PhysRevLett.127.192701, PhysRevLett.120.172702), all favoring a thinner neutron skin. The authors suggest that the upcoming CREX experiment on calcium, operating at higher momentum transfer (Q ≈ 170 MeV) where the weak mixing angle is better constrained, could help resolve the tension between the twin experiments.
