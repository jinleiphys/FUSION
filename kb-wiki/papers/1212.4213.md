# Quark-hybrid matter in the cores of massive neutron stars

> Using a nonlocal extension of the SU(3) Nambu-Jona Lasinio model, which reproduces several of the key features of Quantum Chromodynamics, we show that mixed phases of deconfined quarks and confined hadrons (quark-hybrid matter) may exist in the cores of neutron stars as massive as around 2.1 M_Sun. The radii of these objects are found to be in the canonical range of $\sim 12-13$ km. According to our study, the transition to pure quark matter does not occur in stable neutron stars, but is shifted to neutron stars which are unstable against radial oscillations. The implications of our study for the recently discovered, massive neutron star PSR J1614-2230, whose gravitational mass is $1.97 \pm 0.04 M_Sun$, are that this neutron star may contain an extended region of quark-hybrid matter at it center, but no pure quark matter.

## Key claim
Using a nonlocal SU(3) Nambu-Jona Lasinio model with vector interactions, the authors show that mixed phases of deconfined quarks and confined hadrons (quark-hybrid matter) may exist in the cores of neutron stars as massive as around 2.1 solar masses, but the transition to pure quark matter does not occur in stable neutron stars and is shifted to stars unstable against radial oscillations.

## Method
The authors describe hadronic matter using the nonlinear relativistic mean-field model (GM1 parameter set) and quark matter using a nonlocal extension of the SU(3) Nambu-Jona Lasinio model with a Gaussian form factor and vector coupling. The mixed quark-hadron phase is determined via the Gibbs condition for phase equilibrium with global (rather than local) electric charge conservation, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relationships for neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star masses increase from 1.87 solar masses for vector coupling G_V = 0, to 2.00 solar masses for G_V = 0.05 G_s, to 2.07 solar masses for G_V = 0.1 G_s.
- The mixed phase density ranges are 3.2 to 8.2 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}) for G_V = 0, 3.8 to 8.5 rho_0 for G_V = 0.05 G_s, and 4.5 to 8.9 rho_0 for G_V = 0.1 G_s.
- Neutron star radii are found in the canonical range of approximately 12 to 13 km.
- The model parameters are: current quark mass m_bar = 6.2 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 706.0 MeV, G_s Lambda^2 = 15.04, H Lambda^5 = -337.71, and strange quark mass m_s = 140.7 MeV.
- The pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has a gravitational mass of 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Demorest 2010).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quark deconfinement in neutron stars using local NJL models (Masuda 2012, Lenzi 2012) by employing a nonlocal NJL model with momentum-dependent interactions and global charge neutrality. Unlike the local NJL studies which found stable pure quark matter cores, this work finds that pure quark matter appears only on the unstable branch of the mass-radius relationship. The results enable predictions about the composition of massive neutron stars like PSR J1614-2230, suggesting they may contain extended mixed-phase regions but no pure quark matter cores.
