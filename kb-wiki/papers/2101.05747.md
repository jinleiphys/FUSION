# Hadron yields in central nucleus-nucleus collisions, the statistical hadronization model and the QCD phase diagram

> The description of hadron production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the statistical hadronization model is very good, over a broad range of collision energy. We outline this both for the light (u, d, s) and heavy (charm) quarks and discuss the connection it brings to the phase diagram of QCD.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model (SHM) provides a very good description of hadron yields in central nucleus-nucleus collisions across a broad range of collision energies, but the agreement depends on using an S-matrix treatment of hadronic interactions rather than a simple excluded-volume correction, and the chemical freeze-out temperature saturates near the QCD crossover temperature only for collision energies above about 20 GeV per nucleon pair.

## Method

The authors perform grand-canonical statistical model fits to measured hadron yields (at midrapidity and in full phase space) from central nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature T_CF, baryochemical potential mu_B, and volume V. For light-quark hadrons, the fit includes an S-matrix treatment of pion-nucleon interactions instead of the traditional excluded-volume correction; for charm hadrons, a charm fugacity g_c is introduced and fixed via a balance equation that distributes all initially produced charm quarks into hadrons at the phase boundary using thermal weights.

## Key numbers

- For central (0-10%) Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: T_CF = 156.6 +/- 1.7 MeV, mu_B = 0.7 +/- 3.8 MeV, V = 4175 +/- 380 fm^3 (for one unit of rapidity at midrapidity)
- Lattice QCD crossover temperature at vanishing mu_B: 156.5 +/- 1.5 MeV (Bazavov 2018) and 158.0 +/- 0.6 MeV (Borsanyi 2020)
- Limiting temperature from parametrization: T_CF^lim = 158.4 +/- 1.4 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature from light nuclei yields: T_nuclei = 159 +/- 5 MeV
- Saturation of T_CF occurs for sqrt(s_NN) > 20 GeV
- Deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV, much less than T_CF approximately 157 MeV at LHC

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-standing program of statistical hadronization analysis (Andronic 2006, Braun-Munzinger 2000) to include the S-matrix treatment of interactions (Andronic 2018) and to cover both light and charm quark sectors. The saturation of T_CF at high energies supports the earlier proposal by Braun-Munzinger (1998) and Stock (1999) that chemical freeze-out occurs near the QCD hadronization temperature, connecting heavy-ion data to the QCD phase diagram. The success in describing J/psi production via statistical hadronization of thermalized charm quarks provides evidence for charm quark deconfinement in the hot medium, complementing transport model approaches (Zhao 2011).
