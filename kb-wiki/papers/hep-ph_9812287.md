# Superconductivity by long-range color magnetic interaction in high-density quark matter

> We argue that in quark matter at high densities, the color magnetic field remains unscreened and leads to the phenomenon of color superconductivity. Using the renormalization group near the Fermi surface, we find that the long-range nature of the magnetic interaction changes the asymptotic behavior of the gap $Δ$ at large chemical potential $μ$ qualitatively. We find $Δ\simμg^{-5}\exp(-{3π^2\over\sqrt{2}}{1\over g})$, where $g$ is the small gauge coupling. We discuss the possibility of breaking rotational symmetry by the formation of a condensate with nonzero angular momentum, as well as interesting parallels to some condensed matter systems with long-range forces.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in high-density quark matter, the color magnetic field remains unscreened (contrary to earlier assumptions that a magnetic mass of order \(g^2\mu\) is generated) and that this long-range magnetic interaction changes the asymptotic behavior of the BCS gap from the conventional \(\exp(-c/g^2)\) to \(\exp(-c/g)\), where \(g\) is the small gauge coupling. The key hedge is that this result applies only at asymptotically high densities where the coupling is small, and the RG calculation yields the scale of the gap rather than its precise value.

## Method

The author uses a renormalization group (RG) approach near the Fermi surface, modified to handle the long-range color magnetic interaction. The RG procedure divides the four-fermion interaction into an instantaneous part (mediated by gluons with momentum \(q \gtrsim m_D^{2/3}\delta^{1/3}\), where Landau damping is negligible) and a non-instantaneous part (mediated by softer gluons where Landau damping dominates). As the RG scale \(\delta\) is lowered, part of the non-instantaneous interaction becomes instantaneous and contributes to the partial-wave scattering amplitudes \(f_l\), leading to an RG equation that includes both a constant driving term from the magnetic interaction and the conventional quadratic BCS term.

## Key numbers

* The gap at asymptotically high densities scales as \(\Delta \sim \mu g^{-5} \exp\left(-\frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{g}\right)\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(g\) is the gauge coupling.
* The constant in the exponential is \(c = \frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}}\).
* The Debye screening mass is \(m_D \sim g\mu\).
* The dynamical magnetic screening scale for gluons with energy \(q_0\) is \(q \sim m_D^{2/3} q_0^{1/3}\).
* The tree-level s-wave scattering amplitude at the initial RG scale is \(f_0(0) = -\frac{2g^2}{3\mu^2} \ln\frac{1}{g}\).
* The increment in the s-wave amplitude per RG step from the magnetic interaction is \(\Delta f_0 = -\frac{g^2}{9\mu^2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color superconductivity in high-density QCD, which had been hampered by the inability to handle the long-range nature of the color magnetic force. Prior treatments by Bailin and Love (1984), Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998), Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), and Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1998) had assumed either a magnetic mass or used four-fermion interactions that gave gaps scaling as \(\exp(-c/g^2)\). The paper also draws parallels to condensed matter systems with long-range forces, such as electron-phonon systems treated by Eliashberg theory and double-layer electron systems where a 2d magnetic interaction yields a power-law gap. The result enables future work on the possibility of breaking rotational symmetry by condensates with nonzero angular momentum and on numerical estimates of the gap at moderate densities relevant for heavy-ion collisions.
