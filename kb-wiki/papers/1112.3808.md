# Differential cross section and analysing power of the quasi-free pn -> {pp}_s pi- reaction at 353 MeV

> In order to establish links between p-wave pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions and low energy three-nucleon scattering, an extensive programme of experiments on pion production is currently underway at COSY-ANKE. The final proton pair is measured at very low excitation energy, leading to an S-wave diproton, denoted here as {pp}_s. By using a deuterium target we have obtained data on the differential cross section and analysing power of the quasi-free pol{p}n -> {pp}_s pi^- reaction at 353 MeV. The spectator proton p_sp was either measured directly in silicon tracking telescopes or reconstructed using the momentum of a detected pi^-. Both observables can be described in terms of s-, p-, and d-wave pion production amplitudes. Taken together with the analogous data on the pol{p}p -> {pp}_s pi^0 reaction, full partial wave decompositions of both processes were carried out.

## Key claim

The differential cross section and analysing power of the quasi-free polarized pn -> {pp}_s pi- reaction at 353 MeV were measured over the full angular domain, and the data can be described in terms of s-, p-, and d-wave pion production amplitudes; the analysis reveals that the reaction is dominated by the I=0 p-wave amplitudes, particularly the 3D1 -> 1S0 p transition, which the abstract does not specify.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the COSY-ANKE facility using a polarized proton beam incident on a deuterium cluster-jet target. The final proton pair was measured at very low excitation energy (E_pp < 3 MeV) to select the 1S0 diproton state, and the spectator proton was either detected directly in silicon tracking telescopes or reconstructed using the momentum of a detected pi-; only events with spectator energy E_sp < 6 MeV and effective beam energy T_free = 353 +/- 20 MeV were retained. The differential cross section and analysing power were extracted within the impulse approximation using the Bonn deuteron wave function, and a partial wave decomposition was performed by combining these data with analogous results on the polarized pp -> {pp}_s pi0 reaction.

## Key numbers

- Effective beam energy: T_free = 353 +/- 20 MeV, with individual event resolution sigma(T_free) = 2-4 MeV.
- Diproton excitation energy cut: E_pp < 3 MeV, with resolution sigma(E_pp) < 0.6 MeV.
- Spectator proton energy range: 2.5 < E_sp < 6 MeV, with energy resolution sigma(E_sp)/E_sp approximately 10%.
- Integrated luminosity: L = 2310 +/- 110 nb^-1.
- Beam polarisation: P = 0.66 +/- 0.06.
- Luminosity ratio between spin states: L_up/L_down = 1.017 +/- 0.005.
- Systematic uncertainties: 6% in cross section, 9% in analysing power for the pi- case.
- Fitted amplitude values (in sqrt(nb/sr)): M_s^P = (55.3 +/- 0.4) - (14.7 +/- 0.1)i; M_d^P = -(26.6 +/- 1.1) - (8.6 +/- 0.4)i; M_d^F = 5.3 +/- 2.3; M_p^S = -(32.4 +/- 2.2) + (17.3 +/- 2.7)i; M_p^D = -(109.6 +/- 9.6) + (140.7 +/- 4.0)i.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for global fit: chi^2/NDF = 89/82.
- Incident center-of-mass momentum: p = 407 MeV/c; produced pion momentum: k approximately 94 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the programme of near-threshold NN -> {pp}_s pi measurements at COSY-ANKE, complementing the earlier polarized pp -> {pp}_s pi0 data from Tsirov (2011) with the first full angular coverage for the pn -> {pp}_s pi- reaction. The results enable a complete partial wave decomposition up to pion d-waves, which will be analyzed using chiral perturbation theory to provide a deeper understanding of low-energy pion dynamics; the authors note that future measurements of the transverse spin correlation parameter A_x,x will make the amplitude analysis more robust.
