# Rescaling strange-cluster stars and its implications on gravitational-wave echoes

> Solid states of strange-cluster matter called strangeon matter can form strangeon stars that are highly compact. We show that strangeon matter and strangeon stars can be recast into dimensionless forms by a simple reparametrization and rescaling, through which we manage to maximally reduce the number of degrees of freedom. With this dimensionless scheme, we find that strangeon stars are generally compact enough to feature a photon sphere that is essential to foster gravitational-wave (GW) echoes. Rescaling the dimension back, we illustrate its implications on the expanded dimensional parameter space, and calculate the GW echo frequencies associated with strangeon stars, showing that the minimum echo frequency is $\sim 8$ kHz for empirical parameter space that satisfies the GW170817 constraint, and can reduce to $\mathcal O(100)$ Hertz at the extended limit.

## Key claim

Strangeon stars composed of solid strange-cluster matter are generally compact enough to possess a photon sphere and generate gravitational-wave echoes, but the minimum echo frequency depends critically on the model parameter space: approximately 8 kHz for the empirical range satisfying the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, potentially dropping to O(100) Hz only if the parameter \(\bar{\epsilon}\) is extended to its theoretical upper limit of about 0.1757.

## Method

The authors model strangeon matter using a Lennard-Jones potential between strangeons, deriving the equation of state with parameters \(\epsilon\) (potential depth), \(n_s\) (surface baryon number density), and \(N_q\) (number of quarks per strangeon). They introduce a dimensionless rescaling scheme that reduces these three parameters to a single dimensionless parameter \(\bar{\epsilon} = \epsilon/(N_q m_q)\), then solve the dimensionless Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain rescaled mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. The effective potential for axial gravitational perturbations (\(l=s=2\) mode) is computed in the rescaled framework, and echo times are obtained by integrating the light travel time from the star center to the photon sphere at \(R=3M\).

## Key numbers

- Minimum echo frequency for empirical parameter space satisfying GW170817 constraint: \(f_{\rm echo}^{\rm min} \approx 8\) kHz, corresponding to \(\bar{\epsilon} = \bar{\epsilon}_{\rm max}^{\rm em} = 0.13\) with rescaled minimum echo frequency \(\bar{f}_{\rm echo}^{\rm min} = 0.655\)
- At the theoretical upper limit \(\bar{\epsilon} \approx 0.175\) (close to \(\bar{\epsilon}_{\rm max}^{\rm theo} = 2/a \approx 0.1757\)), the minimum echo frequency reduces to \(f_{\rm echo}^{\rm min} \approx 67\) Hz (for \(n_s = 0.24\) fm\(^{-3}\)), with rescaled value \(\bar{f}_{\rm echo}^{\rm min} = 0.023\)
- At \(\bar{\epsilon} = \bar{\epsilon}_{\rm max}^{\rm em} = 0.13\), the rescaled maximum mass and radius are \((\bar{M}_{\rm TOV}, \bar{R}_{\rm TOV}) \approx (0.149, 0.348)\)
- At \(\bar{\epsilon} \approx 0.175\), the rescaled maximum mass and radius become \((\bar{M}_{\rm TOV}, \bar{R}_{\rm TOV}) \approx (1.28, 2.89)\)
- The theoretical upper bound for \(\bar{\epsilon}\) is \(\bar{\epsilon}_{\rm max}^{\rm theo} = 2/a \approx 0.1757\), where \(a = A_6^2/A_{12} \approx 11.38\)
- The empirical upper bound adopted is \(\bar{\epsilon}_{\rm max}^{\rm em} = 0.13\), based on \(\epsilon \sim 120\) MeV and \(N_q m_q \sim 3 \times 310\) MeV
- If the GW170817 constraint is dropped, the minimum echo frequency can reduce to 5 kHz

## Context

This work extends the study of gravitational-wave echoes from exotic compact objects, previously explored for quark stars by Zhang (2021) which required perturbative QCD corrections and color superconductivity effects to achieve sufficient compactness. The authors build on the strangeon star model developed by Xu (2003) and Lai (2009), which had already been shown to support massive pulsars above \(2 M_\odot\) before the discovery of PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest (2010). The rescaling scheme enables a systematic exploration of the full parameter space, showing that strangeon stars are more generically compact enough for echoes than previously considered quark star models. The results enable future work on rotation effects (expected to reduce echo frequencies further toward LIGO sensitivity) and on hybrid strangeon-strange quark matter stars.
