# Chiral doublet model for the positive and negative parity nucleons

> We discuss the properties of the nucleon (N) and its excited state with odd parity (N*) under the aspect of chiral symmetry, identifying them with suitable representations of the chiral group. It is shown that there are two distinctive schemes to assign the chiral multiplet to N and N*. We construct linear sigma models for N and N* with the two assignments to show their physical implications. We also discuss the in-medium properties of N(1535), comparing the present model with the other chiral model based on the different picture of N*.

# Chiral doublet model for the positive and negative parity nucleons

## Key claim
The nucleon (N) and its odd-parity excited state N(1535) can be assigned to chiral multiplets in two distinct ways, the "naive model" and the "mirror model", which produce qualitatively different predictions for masses, axial charges, and in-medium behavior, but current data do not yet determine which realization is physical.

## Method
Linear sigma models were constructed for the nucleon and N(1535) under two chiral transformation assignments: the naive model, where both baryons transform identically under chiral rotations, and the mirror model, where they transform oppositely under axial transformations and serve as chiral partners. The models were then applied to calculate the in-medium mass difference of N and N* using a linear density-dependent parametrization of the chiral condensate, and the resulting eta optical potential was used to compute (d,³He) reaction spectra on a ¹²C target.

## Key numbers
- In the mirror model, fitted parameters are g₁ = 9.8, g₂ = 16, and m₀ = 270 MeV, yielding a mixing angle θ = 6.3°.
- The free-space mass difference between N and N* is approximately 50 MeV (N* lies 50 MeV above the ηN threshold).
- The density dependence of the chiral condensate is parametrized as ⟨σ⟩ = (1 − C ρ/ρ₀) σ₀ with C = 0.1–0.3, where ρ₀ is saturation density.
- The isovector axial charge of N* in the mirror model has opposite sign to that of N, with diagonal components given by cos 2θ and −cos 2θ respectively.
- The empirical πNN* coupling is g_{πNN*} ≈ 0.7, compared to g_{πNN} ≈ 13; the transition axial charge g_A^{NN*} ≈ 0.2 is obtained from the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Context
This work extends the chiral doublet approach of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) and earlier work by Jido et al. (1998, 2001) on parity doubling in baryons. It contrasts the chiral partner picture with the alternative description of N(1535) as a dynamically generated resonance in meson-baryon scattering, as formulated by Kaiser et al. (1995) and Inoue et al. (2001). The in-medium predictions enable experimental discrimination between these pictures via (d,³He) reactions for eta-mesic nuclei, as proposed by Jido et al. (2002).
