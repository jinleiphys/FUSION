# Equivalence of relativistic three-particle quantization conditions

> We show that a recently derived alternative form of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition for identical particles can be rewritten in terms of the R matrix introduced to give a unitary representation of the infinite-volume three-particle scattering amplitude. Combined with earlier work, this shows the equivalence of the relativistic effective field theory approach of Hansen and Sharpe (Refs.[1,2]) and the "finite-volume unitarity" approach of Mai and Döring (Refs.[3,4]). It also provides a generalization of the latter approach to arbitrary angular momenta of two-particle subsystems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the relativistic three-particle quantization condition derived in the relativistic effective field theory (RFT) approach of Hansen and Sharpe can be rewritten exactly in terms of the R matrix from the "finite-volume unitarity" (FVU) approach of Mai and Döring, thereby proving the equivalence of the two formalisms for identical particles with arbitrary angular momenta in two-particle subsystems, not only for s-wave dimers as previously shown.

## Method

The authors start from the asymmetric form of the RFT quantization condition derived in their companion paper (BS1), which depends on an asymmetric three-particle K matrix denoted $\Kdfuun$. They then derive the infinite-volume relationship between this asymmetric K matrix and the R matrix $\cR^\uu$ by equating expressions for asymmetric three-particle scattering amplitudes. Using this relationship, they show that the finite-volume quantity $X^\uu$ appearing in the RFT quantization condition converges to $(2\omega L^3)^{-1} \cR^\uu (2\omega L^3)^{-1}$ up to exponentially suppressed corrections, allowing the quantization condition to be rewritten in terms of $\cR^\uu$.

## Key numbers

- The relation between the asymmetric K matrix $\Kdfuun$ and the R matrix $\cR^\uu$ is given by $\Kdfuun = \overline\cK_2 \cR^\uu \overline\cK_2 (1-\cR^\uu \overline\cK_2)^{-1}$, where $\overline\cK_2$ contains the two-particle K matrix
- The finite-volume corrections to the relation between $X^\uu$ and $\cR^\uu$ are of order $\cO(e^{-mL})$, where $m$ is the particle mass and $L$ is the box size
- The final quantization condition takes the form $\det[\wt H - (2\omega L^3)^{-1} \cR^\uu (2\omega L^3)^{-1}] = 0$, where $\omega$ is the on-shell single-particle energy

## Context

This work extends the earlier comparison by Hansen and Sharpe (2019) which showed equivalence only for s-wave dimers with constant three-particle interactions. It also generalizes the FVU approach of Mai and Döring (2017, 2018) to arbitrary angular momenta of two-particle subsystems. The result enables practitioners to use either formalism interchangeably for lattice QCD calculations of three-particle systems, including the $3\pi^+$ system currently under study, and clarifies that all sources of power-law volume dependence are properly accounted for in the FVU approach.
