# No planar degeneracy for the Landau gauge quark-gluon vertex

> Based on a suitable basis system for the quark-gluon vertex' transverse tensor structures and on carefully chosen kinematical variables, the transverse part of the quark-gluon vertex in quenched QCD in the Landau gauge is obtained from a system of Dyson-Schwinger equations. We demonstrate by analysing this solution that the angular dependence of these transverse quark-gluon vertex form factors is seemingly weak. We nevertheless argue that this does not imply a planar degeneracy for this vertex because even this mild dependence cannot be neglected when aiming for reasonably precise results for derived quantities. Last but not least, for a self-consistently coupled systems of 3PI Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator and the quark-gluon vertex we confirm that the core ingredient to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is the dynamically generated tensor coupling of glue to quarks which itself is only possible because of chiral symmetry breaking. Furthermore, we find (i) a relation in between the calculated chirality violating vertex form factors; (ii) that the quark propagator is identical within numerical errors when obtained either from a decoupling solution or the scaling solution for the Yang-Mills propagators and vertex functions; and (iii) that the resulting quark propagator is consistent with possessing poles only on the real time-like half-axis. Furthermore, we provide high-precision fits for the form factors based on sometimes astonishingly simple model functions.

## Key claim

The angular dependence of the transverse quark-gluon vertex form factors is seemingly weak, but this does not imply a planar degeneracy for this vertex because even this mild dependence cannot be neglected when aiming for reasonably precise results for derived quantities.

## Method

The authors solved a system of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator and the quark-gluon vertex in quenched QCD in the Landau gauge, using a 3PI-3-loop truncation that includes the non-Abelian triangle diagram and optionally the Abelian diagram. The vertex was decomposed into eight transverse tensor structures (four chirally symmetric and four chirality violating) using a carefully chosen basis, with the momentum dependence parametrized by the gluon momentum squared, the average quark momentum squared, and the cosine of the angle between them. Input for the Yang-Mills sector (gluon propagator and three-gluon vertex) was taken from Huber (2020), and the three-gluon vertex was approximated using only its tree-level tensor structure with an enhancement factor of eta = 1.5 to achieve dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- The three-gluon vertex was enhanced by a factor eta = 1.5 to obtain dynamical chiral symmetry breaking within the quenched approximation.
- The Abelian diagram is suppressed by a factor 1/N_c^2 = 1/9 compared to the non-Abelian diagram from colour algebra.
- The leading-order anomalous dimension of the tree-level dressing function Gamma^(1) in the Landau gauge and quenched approximation is gamma_{Aqq} = -9/22.
- The quark propagator was found to be identical within numerical errors when obtained from either the decoupling solution or the scaling solution for the Yang-Mills propagators and vertex functions.

## Context

This work extends the study of the quark-gluon vertex beyond earlier DSE investigations such as Alkofer (2008) and Windisch (2014), and addresses the question of whether the vertex exhibits a planar degeneracy similar to that found for the three-gluon vertex. The results confirm that the core ingredient to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is the dynamically generated tensor coupling of glue to quarks, which itself is only possible because of chiral symmetry breaking. The high-precision fits provided for the form factors enable future applications in bound state studies using the 3PI-3-loop truncation, as previously employed by Williams (2015) and Eichmann (2016).
