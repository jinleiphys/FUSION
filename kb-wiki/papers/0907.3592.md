# Describing transverse dynamics and space-time evolution at RHIC in a hydrodynamic model with statistical hadronization

> A hydrodynamic model coupled to the statistical hadronization code Therminator is used to study a set of observables in the soft sector at RHIC. A satisfactory description of the pT-spectra and elliptic flow is obtained, similarly to other hydrodynamic models. With the Gaussian initial conditions the transverse femtoscopic radii are also reproduced, providing a possible solution of the RHIC HBT puzzle.

## Key claim
A hydrodynamic model with Gaussian initial conditions, an early starting time of 0.25 fm, and a single freeze-out scenario can simultaneously reproduce the transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii at RHIC, offering a possible resolution of the RHIC HBT puzzle. The success depends on the specific choice of initial profile and equation of state, with the model slightly overshooting proton elliptic flow data.

## Method
The authors coupled a hydrodynamic code to the statistical hadronization model Therminator, using a realistic equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD results at high temperatures and hadron-gas results at lower temperatures. Initial energy density profiles in the transverse plane were taken as Gaussian distributions with width parameters from Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations using Glissando, with an initial thermalization time of 0.25 fm and a single freeze-out temperature of 145 MeV. Two-particle correlation functions were evaluated with Coulomb corrections to compute HBT radii.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature: T_i = 460 MeV for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Final freeze-out temperature: T_f = 145 MeV
- Initial thermalization time: tau_i = 0.25 fm
- Gaussian width parameters for centrality class 20-30%: a = 2.00 fm, b = 2.59 fm
- HBT radii R_out, R_side, R_long and ratio R_out/R_side reproduced for centrality class 20-30% across k_T bins of 0.15-0.25 GeV, 0.25-0.35 GeV, and 0.35-0.6 GeV
- Azimuthally sensitive femtoscopic observables calculated for multiple centralities (characterized by N_part) and the three k_T bins above

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Heinz (2002), Hirano (2004), and others who identified the RHIC HBT puzzle as the difficulty in simultaneously describing spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii. The authors' use of Gaussian initial conditions and early thermalization time builds on their previous work (Broniowski 2008) and contrasts with the viscous hydrodynamic approach of Pratt (2008), which uses tau_i = 0.1 fm but does not evaluate v_2. The results enable further studies of azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy and suggest that proper initial profiles and equation of state are key to resolving the HBT puzzle.
