# An approximation of the $S$ matrix for solving the Marchenko equation

> I present a new approximation of the $S$-matrix dependence on momentum $q$, formulated as a sum of a rational function and a truncated Sinc series. This approach enables pointwise determination of the $S$ matrix with specified resolution, capturing essential features such as resonance behavior with high accuracy. The resulting approximation provides a separable kernel for the Marchenko equation (fixed-$l$ inversion), reducing it to a system of linear equations for the expansion coefficients of the output kernel. Numerical results demonstrate good convergence of this method, applicable to both unitary and non-unitary $S$ matrices. Convergence is further validated through comparisons with an exactly solvable square-well potential model. The method is applied to analyze $S_{31}$ $πN$ scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new approximation of the S-matrix as a sum of a rational function and a truncated Sinc series that yields a separable kernel for the Marchenko equation, but the method's accuracy depends on assuming a finite interaction range R and approximating the Heaviside step function H(2R-|x+y|) as H(R-|x|)H(R-|y|), which becomes accurate only for potentials with a well-defined range.

## Method

The author parametrizes the S-matrix as S(q) = S_asymp(q) + Delta S(q), where S_asymp is a rational function fit (Eq. Su_frac for unitary cases or Eq. SmatrixN2 for non-unitary cases) and Delta S(q) is a truncated Sinc series with coefficients determined by the S-matrix values at points q = k*tau. This parametrization produces a separable kernel for the Marchenko equation (fixed-l inversion), reducing the integral equation to a system of linear equations for the expansion coefficients of the output kernel L(x,y). The potential is then obtained directly from the analytical derivatives of the output kernel, avoiding numerical differentiation.

## Key numbers

- For the two-channel square-well test model: channel masses m1 = 1 GeV and m2 = 1.05 GeV; threshold momentum squared q0^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 (approximately 2.57 fm^-2); potential strengths Vi = 10 MeV; coupling potentials V12 = V21 = -10 MeV; common range a = 1.973 fm
- The S31 piN scattering resonance occurs at E_lab approximately 800 MeV
- The resulting optical potential for piN scattering exhibits a characteristic range of approximately 2 fm, with calculations requiring extension to r approximately 15 fm
- Wave functions were calculated at three momenta: q = 0.278 fm^-1, q = 1.556 fm^-1, and q = 3.440 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the Marchenko inversion method beyond the traditional rational fraction expansions used by Geramb (1994), Kohlhoff (1994), and Khokhlov (2006, 2007) for NN and piN scattering. It improves upon the triangular-pulse decomposition method of Khokhlov (MyAlg2, MyAlg3) by enabling direct analytical differentiation of the output kernel rather than requiring numerical differentiation. The method is validated against the exactly solvable two-channel square-well model of Kamal and Kreuzer (1970) and applied to S31 piN scattering data, enabling future extension to coupled-channel cases for NN and piN scattering.
