# Model-independent determination of nuclear charge radii from Li-like ions

> We demonstrate that recent advances in QED theory of Li-like ions [V. A. Yerokhin et al., Phys. Rev. A 112, 042801 (2025)] enable determinations of absolute nuclear charge radii for heavy elements. By incorporating constraints derived from electron-scattering data, we obtain radii that are independent of the assumed model of the nuclear charge distribution. Our approach is validated for $^{208}$Pb, a well-studied spherical nucleus, and is then applied to $^{209}$Bi, where low-lying nuclear excitations complicate the interpretation of muonic-atom data.

## Key claim

By constraining the ratios of the first three nuclear charge moments ($\langle r^2\rangle^{1/2}$, $\langle r^4\rangle^{1/4}$, and $\langle r^6\rangle^{1/6}$) using electron-scattering data, the finite nuclear size correction to atomic energy levels becomes essentially independent of the assumed nuclear charge distribution model, with residual model dependence reduced to the $10^{-5}$ level.

## Method

The authors computed finite nuclear size corrections to $2p_j$–$2s$ transition energies in Li-like ions using multiple nuclear charge distribution models (Fermi, homogeneous, Gaussian, exponential) with one, two, or three free parameters, each constrained to reproduce the same low-order radial moments. They extracted the moment ratios $V_{24} = r_C / \langle r^4\rangle^{1/4}$ and $V_{26} = r_C / \langle r^6\rangle^{1/6}$ from published electron-scattering analyses of $^{208}$Pb and $^{209}$Bi, then used a field-shift parametrization to adjust the rms charge radius $r_C$ until the theoretical transition energies matched experimental values from Brandau (2003), Zhang (2008), and Beiersdorfer (1998).

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb from the $D_1$ ($2p_{1/2}$–$2s$) transition: $r_C = 5.558\,(27)_{\rm exp}(10)_{\rm the}$ fm
- For $^{208}$Pb from the $D_2$ ($2p_{3/2}$–$2s$) transition: $r_C = 5.517\,(31)_{\rm exp}(9)_{\rm the}$ fm
- Weighted average for $^{208}$Pb: $r_C = 5.540\,(20)_{\rm exp}(10)_{\rm the}$ fm, consistent with the muonic-atom value $5.5062\,(17)$ fm from Sun (2025) within 1.5 combined standard uncertainties
- For $^{209}$Bi: $r_C = 5.514\,(11)_{\rm exp}(10)_{\rm the}$ fm
- When only $r_C$ is fixed, fns corrections differ by about $1\%$ across models; constraining $\langle r^4\rangle$ reduces spread to $0.02\%$; constraining all three moments reduces spread to $0.001\%$
- Adopted $V_{24}$ and $V_{26}$ for $^{208}$Pb: $0.9409(2)$ and $0.8990(8)$, respectively, from averaging four electron-scattering analyses

## Context

This work extends the recent QED calculations for Li-like ions by Yerokhin et al. (2025, Phys. Rev. A 112, 042801) to enable absolute nuclear charge radius determinations from electronic atoms, which previously were limited to hydrogen and deuterium (Mohr 2025). The approach contrasts with muonic-atom methods (Fricke 2004, Sun 2025) where nuclear shape uncertainties are significant and difficult to address reliably, and with earlier electronic-atom determinations that relied on comparing just two nuclear models (Fermi and homogeneous sphere). The results enable future absolute radius determinations with projected accuracy of about 0.005 fm from improved experimental transition energies and ongoing two-loop QED calculations.
