# Dissociation rates and recombination likelihood of bottomonium states in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV

> In the medium of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, dissociation of the quarkonium and their survival have been studied to understand the properties of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). The coupled rates of dissociation and recombination reactions in QGP are commonly solved with the Boltzmann transport equation in which the formation and dissociation reactions compete with each other. Since the dissociation of newly formed bound-states is not accounted in the Boltzmann equation, a framework of decoupled rates is developed to assess the combined effect of gluon-induced dissociation and recombination (though it is small for $Υ$) together with color screening on bottomonium production in heavy-ion collisions at center of mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$) = 5.02 TeV. To calculate the recombination rates, we have employed an effective method of Bateman solution which makes sure the correlated effect between the recombination and the dissociation of the newly combined bottomonium in the QGP medium. The modifications of bottomonium have been estimated in an inflating QGP with the constraints matching with the dynamics of Pb+Pb collision events at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors develop a framework of decoupled rate equations for dissociation and recombination of bottomonium states in the Quark Gluon Plasma, explicitly accounting for the dissociation of newly formed bound states, a process not captured by the standard Boltzmann transport equation, and find that recombination is small for Upsilon states but becomes nontrivial at LHC energies of 5.02 TeV.

## Method

The authors model the QGP as an isotropically expanding fireball with Bjorken boost-invariant dynamics and an accelerating transverse expansion. They decouple the gluon-induced dissociation and recombination processes from the Boltzmann transport equation, solving the recombination equation using the Bateman solution for chain decay reactions to incorporate the correlated effect of dissociation of newly formed bottomonia. The total nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed as the product of survival probabilities from color screening and gluon dissociation, plus the recombination fraction, with feed-down corrections included.

## Key numbers

- Initial QGP formation time tau_0 = 0.15 fm/c at LHC, with initial temperature T_0 = 0.65 GeV in most central (0-5%) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- Direct cross-sections per nucleon pair for bottomonium states in Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV: Upsilon(1S) = 0.15 microbarn, Upsilon(2S) = 0.092 microbarn, Upsilon(3S) = 0.055 microbarn, chi_b(1P) = 0.03 microbarn, chi_b(2P) = 0.023 microbarn.
- Branching fractions for decay into mu+ mu-: Upsilon(1S) = 2.482%, Upsilon(2S) = 1.93%, Upsilon(3S) = 2.18%, chi_b(1P) = 0.48%, chi_b(2P) = 0.266%.
- Binding energies: Upsilon(1S) = 1.10 GeV, Upsilon(2S) = 0.53 GeV, Upsilon(3S) = 0.20 GeV; dissociation temperatures relative to T_c: Upsilon(1S) = 2.0, Upsilon(2S) = 1.3, Upsilon(3S) = 1.0.
- Formation times tau_F: Upsilon(1S) = 0.76 fm/c, Upsilon(2S) = 1.9 fm/c, Upsilon(3S) = 2.0 fm/c.
- Collision luminosity L = 368 inverse microbarns for Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV.
- Upsilon production cross-section in p+p collisions = 0.16 mb.
- QGP lifetime at LHC energy approximately 8-10 fm/c.
- Transverse acceleration a = 0.1 c^2/fm.

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic model studies of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, such as those using the Boltzmann transport equation by Stachel and Thews, and the color screening model of Abdulsalam (2020). It addresses the limitation that the Boltzmann equation does not account for dissociation of newly formed bound states, a gap noted in earlier coupled-rate approaches. The framework enables more precise quantification of the competing effects of color screening, gluon dissociation, and recombination on bottomonium yields, and can be applied to future LHC data from CMS and ALICE experiments to refine constraints on QGP properties.
