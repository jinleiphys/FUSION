# Level spacing distribution of pseudointegrable billiard

> In this paper, we examine the level spacing distribution $P(S)$ of the rectangular billiard with a single point-like scatterer, which is known as pseudointegrable. It is shown that the observed $P(S)$ is a new type, which is quite different from the previous conclusion. Even in the strong coupling limit, the Poisson-like behavior rather than Wigner-like is seen for $S>1$, although the level repulsion still remains in the small $S$ region. The difference from the previous works is analyzed in detail.

# Level spacing distribution of pseudointegrable billiard

## Key claim

The level spacing distribution $P(S)$ of the rectangular billiard with a single point-like scatterer in the strong coupling limit does not become Wigner-like as previously claimed, but instead shows Poisson-like behavior for $S>1$ while retaining level repulsion at small $S$, constituting a new class of distribution. This result depends critically on using a sufficiently large Fourier basis (truncation II with $n_{min}=1$ and $n_{max}=100000$) rather than the narrower basis (truncation I with $n_{min}=l-500$ and $n_{max}=l+500$) used in prior work by Seba and Zyczkowski (1991).

## Method

The authors used the Green's function method for a two-dimensional rectangular billiard of side lengths $l_x=\pi/3$ and $l_y=3/\pi$ with a point-like scatterer at the center, with mass $M=8\pi$ chosen so that the average density of even-even parity states equals one. They solved the transcendental equation $G^{(0)}(x_0,y_0;x_0,y_0;z)=1/v_0$ for eigenvalues in the strong coupling limit ($v_0=\infty$), comparing two truncation schemes for the summation over unperturbed states: truncation (I) using 1001 terms centered around the eigenvalue index, and truncation (II) using terms from $n=1$ to $n=100000$. They estimated the numerical error in the Green's function from truncation by converting the summation to an integral and evaluating the resulting logarithmic terms.

## Key numbers

- System parameters: $M=8\pi$, $l_x=\pi/3$, $l_y=3/\pi$, scatterer at center $(l_x/2, l_y/2)$
- Truncation (I) uses $n_{min}=l-500$ and $n_{max}=l+500$ for eigenvalue $E_l$; truncation (II) uses $n_{min}=1$ and $n_{max}=100000$
- For truncation (I) at $E_{1000} \simeq 1000$, the numerical error in the Green's function is estimated as $\delta G^{(0)}(1000) \simeq 4(\log 1000 + \log(500/1500)) \simeq 23.2$
- For truncation (II) at $E_{4000} \simeq 4000$, the numerical error is $\delta G^{(0)}(4000) \simeq -0.16$
- Typical eigenvalue error with truncation (II) is at most $10^{-3}$, or $0.1\%$ of the mean level spacing

## Context

This work corrects the earlier conclusion of Seba and Zyczkowski (1991), who used a narrower truncation scheme and reported Wigner-like level spacing distributions for the same pseudointegrable system. The paper shows that the singularity of the point-like interaction couples all even-even parity states with equal strength, requiring a much larger basis than previously appreciated. The result establishes that the rectangular billiard with a point scatterer belongs to a new class of quantum chaos distinct from both regular (Poisson) and chaotic (Wigner) statistics, and the authors indicate that further analysis of other statistical properties will appear in a forthcoming paper (Shigehara, Yoshinaga, Cheon, Mizusaki, 1992).
