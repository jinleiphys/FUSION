# Color superconductivity with self-consistent NJL-type model

> In this paper, the NJL-type model is used to investigate the color superconductivity. The four-fermion interactions of the NJL-type model are Fierz-transformed into two different classes, i.e., the quark-antiquark and the quark-quark interaction channels, associated with the chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity respectively. We conclude that the weighting factor between quark-antiquark and quark-quark interaction channels has significance on the phase structure when the mean-field approximation is employed, and the baryon number density gives a tight constraint on the weighting factor of quark-antiquark interaction channels. Besides, the susceptibilities show that the color superconducting phase transition is of the second-order and takes place before the chiral crossover transition as quark number density increases. In the end, we study the critical temperatures $T_c$ of the color superconductivity and it agrees with the perturbative result of diquark condensate $Δ\approx0.57T_c$.

## Key claim
The weighting factor α between quark-antiquark and quark-quark interaction channels significantly affects the phase structure under mean-field approximation, and the baryon number density constrains α to be no less than 0.44, contrary to the common choice of α = 1 (which corresponds to the standard Fierz-transformed coupling ratio G_D = (3/4)G_s).

## Method
The authors start from a two-flavor NJL-type model with four-fermion interactions, Fierz-transform the Lagrangian into quark-antiquark and quark-quark channels with a weighting factor α and (1-α) respectively, then apply the mean-field approximation to obtain gap equations for the chiral condensate and diquark condensate. They solve these self-consistently at zero and finite temperature using a three-momentum cutoff Λ = 631 MeV, current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, and coupling g = 5.074 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², varying α between 0.4 and 0.5. The order of phase transitions is determined via chiral and color superconducting susceptibilities.

## Key numbers
- The three-momentum cutoff is Λ = 631 MeV, current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, and coupling constant g = 5.074 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻².
- The effective quark mass starts to fall at chemical potentials μ = 268, 292, 311, 324, 333, 337 MeV as α increases from 0.4 to 0.5 at zero temperature.
- Maximum color superconducting gaps are 245, 216, 219, 170, 150, 132 MeV for α ranging from 0.4 to 0.5 at zero temperature.
- The baryon number density n_B = 2n_0 (with n_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³) occurs in the chemical potential range 300–350 MeV when diquark condensate is included.
- The parameter α is constrained to be over 0.44 by the requirement that baryon number density appears at μ ≈ 313 MeV.
- Color superconducting phase transition peaks occur at μ_s = 267, 292, 310, 323, 332, 336 MeV for α from 0.4 to 0.5.
- Critical temperatures T_c = 69, 88, 112 MeV for α = 0.50, 0.46, 0.42 respectively, with the relation Δ ≈ 0.57 T_c.

## Context
This work extends the NJL-type model studies of color superconductivity (Buballa 2005, Alford and Wilczek 1998) by introducing a weighting factor α between Fierz-transformed channels, similar to the approach used for scalar and vector channels by Wang (2019) and Zhao (2019). The finding that color superconducting phase transition is second-order and occurs before chiral crossover as density increases contradicts some earlier studies that found a first-order chiral transition (Buballa 2005, Wu 2017). The constraint α > 0.44 from baryon density provides a testable condition for future compact star observations, including x-ray, pulsar, and gravitational wave measurements of neutron star radii, masses, and tidal deformabilities.
