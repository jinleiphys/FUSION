# First complete description of low-lying spectroscopy in $^{254}$No

> In this work, we report the first complete shell-model description of low-lying structures of $^{254}$No. Employing the Kuo-Herling effective interaction, the calculations are performed within the Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell Model recently implemented with the angular-momentum Variation After Projection applied on non-axial wavefunctions. Our calculations show a striking agreement with the experimentally known spectroscopy: the \textit{Yrast} band, the $8^{-}$ ($K=8$), $3^{+}$ ($K=3$) and $10^+$ ($K=10$) isomers together with associated $K$ bands. We reproduce the recently measured Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting between the $3^+$ ($K=3$) and $4^+$ ($K=4$) band heads. We predict the appearance of a second $0^+$ state, in excellent agreement with recent new observation. In addition, we systematically examine the ground state spectra, dipole and spectroscopic quadrupole moments of even-even, odd-even, odd-odd nuclei from $A=251$ to $A=256$, which are favourably reproduced. The present description of $^{254}$No shows the ability of the Shell Model framework to describe the low-lying properties for such an exotic nucleus at the frontier of modern experimental nuclear physics research.

## Key claim
The authors report the first complete shell-model description of the low-lying spectroscopy of $^{254}$No, achieving striking agreement with experimental data including the yrast band, isomers, and newly observed states, but only when using the Variation-After-Projection (VAP) scheme rather than the simpler Projection-After-Variation (PAV) scheme; the abstract's claim of "striking agreement" is conditional on the VAP method capturing correlations that the PAV method misses, as shown by the fact that PAV calculations give incorrect signs for magnetic moments in some nuclei.

## Method
The authors extended their Discrete Non-Orthogonal Shell Model (DNO-SM) by implementing a Variation-After-Projection (VAP) scheme, where the symmetry-breaking Slater determinants are determined variationally using the fully angular-momentum- and parity-projected wave function as the trial state. Calculations were performed using the Kuo-Herling effective interaction, with a valence space of six proton orbitals and seven neutron orbitals above the $^{208}$Pb core, and no modifications were made to the interaction for this mass region. The method was applied to nuclei from $A=251$ to $A=256$, with up to 32 Slater states used for the $^{254}$No calculations.

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of $^{254}$No: $-250.187$ MeV (VAP with 1 Slater state), compared to $-249.568$ MeV (PAV with 1 Slater state), showing VAP provides a more bound solution by about 0.6 MeV.
- Magnetic dipole moment of $^{253}$No: $-0.493$ $\mu_N$ (VAP) versus experimental $-0.527(33)(75)$ $\mu_N$; PAV gave $+0.591$ $\mu_N$ (wrong sign).
- Magnetic dipole moment of $^{255}$Es: $+3.94$ $\mu_N$ (VAP) versus experimental $+4.14(10)$ $\mu_N$; PAV gave $-1.10$ $\mu_N$ (wrong sign).
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of $^{253}$No: $+7.2$ eb (VAP) versus experimental $+5.9(1.4)(0.9)$ eb; PAV gave $-3.5$ eb (wrong sign).
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of $^{254}$Es: $+8.4$ eb (VAP) versus experimental $+9.6(1.2)$ eb; PAV gave $+1.8$ eb.
- Yrast band energies reproduced within approximately 10-50 keV for nuclei from $A=251$ to $A=256$.
- The $16^+$ ($K=16$) isomer in $^{254}$No is predicted at 2.68 MeV excitation energy.
- The $8^-$ and $3^+$ bands in $^{254}$No have well-conserved $K$ quantum numbers up to approximately 99%.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous DNO-SM calculations (Dao and Nowacki, 2022) which used a Projection-After-Variation (PAV) scheme with 15 constrained Hartree-Fock states and could describe the rotational band and $8^-$, $3^+$ isomers but underestimated the $3^+$ isomer position. The new VAP scheme captures correlations more efficiently, resolving this issue and reproducing the newly observed second $0^+$ state and $K=4$ band from recent experimental work (Forge, 2023; No254PRL). The results demonstrate that the shell-model framework can now compete with Energy Density Functional methods (Bender, 2003; Ring, 2005) for describing superheavy nuclei, and the authors suggest this provides a benchmark for future ab-initio Valence-Space IM-SRG approaches (Stroberg, 2019).
