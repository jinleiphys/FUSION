# Elastic lepton-proton two-photon exchange scattering: An exact HB$χ$PT analysis including hadronic effects at NNLO

> We present an exact analytical evaluation of the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the elastic lepton-proton differential scattering cross section at low-energies within the framework of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. Our analysis focuses on the kinematic regime relevant to the ongoing MUSE experiment, and we therefore restrict the intermediate states to the dominant elastic channel. All loop integrals are evaluated analytically without approximations. Radiative and chiral recoil contributions of the proton are included, retaining kinematical and dynamical TPE corrections to the cross section through next-to-next-to-leading order [i.e., ${\mathcal O}(α/M^2)$] accuracy in the recoil expansion where $M$ is the proton mass. At this chiral order, pion-loop contributions demonstrate that structure-dependent TPE effects arise through the proton form factors. Our analytical results for the scattering cross section reveal non-vanishing residual proton structure effects of ${\mathcal O}(α/M^2)$, despite substantial cancellations between TPE box and crossed-box contributions. Such effects were entirely absent at this accuracy in our earlier analysis based on the soft-photon approximation. Although the next-to-leading-order contributions are numerically sizable, the next-to-next-to-leading-order TPE corrections are found to be small, thereby indicating that the chiral expansion exhibits reasonably good perturbative convergence.

## Key claim
The paper presents an exact analytical evaluation of the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic lepton-proton scattering at low energies, finding that non-vanishing proton structure effects of ${\mathcal O}(\alpha/M^2)$ survive despite substantial cancellations between box and crossed-box diagrams, a result that was entirely absent in the authors' earlier analysis based on the soft-photon approximation (SPA). The next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) TPE corrections are found to be small, indicating reasonably good perturbative convergence of the chiral expansion, though the analysis is restricted to the dominant elastic intermediate state and omits certain non-factorizable two-loop diagrams.

## Method
The authors performed an exact analytical evaluation of the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the elastic lepton-proton differential cross section within heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT). All loop integrals were evaluated analytically without approximations, retaining kinematical and dynamical TPE corrections through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, ${\mathcal O}(\alpha/M^2)$) in the recoil expansion, focusing on the low-energy kinematic regime of the MUSE experiment. The analysis extended the previous exact NLO work of Choudhary et al. (2023) by including NNLO recoil corrections from LO and NLO TPE diagrams, as well as genuine dynamical NNLO corrections from pion-loop contributions that introduce proton structure effects via form factors.

## Key numbers
* The analysis is performed through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), corresponding to ${\mathcal O}(\alpha/M^2)$ accuracy in the recoil expansion, where $M$ is the proton mass (approximately 938 MeV).
* The kinematic regime of interest is for momentum transfers $Q^2 < 0.1$ GeV$^2/c^2$, relevant to the MUSE experiment.
* The proton's anomalous magnetic moment, $\kappa_p = 1.795$, is included as a low-energy constant in the NNLO corrections.
* The LO OPE cross section is given by $\frac{\alpha^2 E^\prime\beta^\prime}{Q^2E\beta}\left(1-\frac{Q^2}{4M^2}\right)\left[\frac{Q^2+4EE^\prime}{Q^2}\right]$, where $E$ and $\beta$ are the incident lepton beam variables.

## Context
This work extends the exact NLO TPE analysis of Choudhary et al. (2023) by incorporating the dominant NNLO proton finite-size contributions via the heavy baryon formalism, moving beyond the soft-photon approximation (SPA) used in the earlier analysis by Goswami et al. (2025). The results are directly relevant to the MUSE experiment (Gilman et al., 2013), which will measure the lepton charge asymmetry at unprecedentedly low momentum transfers ($Q^2 < 0.1$ GeV$^2/c^2$), and they enable a more robust estimate of higher-order systematic uncertainties in the extraction of proton form factors. The paper contradicts the earlier SPA-based finding of Goswami et al. (2025) that NNLO TPE structure effects either cancel or appear only at higher recoil order, by demonstrating that such effects are non-vanishing at ${\mathcal O}(\alpha/M^2)$ when the exact loop kinematics are retained.
