# Decay $B\to K^\ast(\to Kπ) \ell^+ \ell^-$ in covariant quark model

> Our article is devoted to the study of the rare $B\to K^\ast \ell^+\ell^-$~decay where $\ell=e,μ,τ$. We compute the relevant form factors in the framework of the covariant quark model with infrared confinement in the full kinematical momentum transfer region. The calculated form factors are used to evaluate branching fractions and polarization observables in the cascade decay $B\to K^\ast(\to Kπ)\ell^+\ell^-$. We compare the obtained results with available experimental data and the results from other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The covariant quark model with infrared confinement can successfully describe the $B\to K^\ast \ell^+\ell^-$ decay form factors across the full kinematical $q^2$ region, and the resulting predictions for branching fractions and angular observables are consistent with available experimental data, though the authors note that the charm-loop resonance contributions are dropped by setting $\kappa=0$ in the effective Wilson coefficient $C_9^{\rm eff}$.

## Method

The authors compute the $B\to K^\ast$ transition form factors using the covariant confined quark model, which implements quark confinement via an infrared cutoff on the scale integration in the Schwinger parameter representation. The form factors are then combined with the Standard Model effective Hamiltonian (including Wilson coefficients $C_7^{\rm eff}$, $C_9^{\rm eff}$, and $C_{10}$) to evaluate the four-fold angular decay distribution for the cascade $B\to K^\ast(\to K\pi)\ell^+\ell^-$, from which branching fractions, forward-backward asymmetry, longitudinal polarization, and the "clean" observables $P_i$ are derived.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used in the model: $m_{u/d}=0.241$ GeV, $m_s=0.428$ GeV, $m_c=1.67$ GeV, $m_b=5.05$ GeV, with infrared cutoff $\lambda=0.181$ GeV
- $B\to K^\ast$ form factor values at $q^2=0$: $A_0(0)=0.459$, $A_+(0)=0.310$, $A_-(0)=-0.335$, $V(0)=0.354$, $a_0(0)=0.326$, $a_+(0)=0.323$, $g(0)=0.323$
- Size parameter for $K^\ast$ meson: $\Lambda_{K^\ast}=0.75$ GeV; for $B$ meson: $\Lambda_B=1.96$ GeV
- The $R(D^\ast)$ ratio from experiment is $0.321 \pm 0.021$ compared to the SM prediction $0.252 \pm 0.003$, showing a $3.3\,\sigma$ deviation

## Context

This work extends the covariant confined quark model previously developed by Branz et al. (2009) and Ivanov et al. (2011) to the rare $B\to K^\ast\ell^+\ell^-$ decay, which was not accessible in the earlier model due to the "threshold inequality" limitation. The authors compare their form factor results with other theoretical approaches referenced in Ivanov et al. (2007) and note that a local discrepancy of $3.7$ standard deviations was observed by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2013) in one $q^2$ bin for one angular observable. The results enable further tests of lepton flavor universality and searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model, particularly in light of the $R(D^\ast)$ anomaly.
