# Effects of $ΛΛ- ΞN$ mixing in the decay of $S = -2$ hypernuclei

> We study the non-mesonic weak decay of the doubly-strange hypernucleus $^{ΛΛ}_6$He within a model which considers the exchange of pseudoscalar and vector mesons. Special attention is paid into quantifying the strong interaction effects, focussing on the interaction among the two Λ hyperons which induces novel weak transitions, whereby ΛΛ, ΞN and ΣΣ states decay into a hyperon-nucleon pair. The initial strangeness -2 wave function is obtained from the solution of a G-matrix equation with the input of realistic strong baryon-baryon potentials, while the final hyperon-nucleon wave functions are derived analogously from a microscopic T-matrix calculation. The new ΛΛ $\to$ Y N decay rate studied in this work, $Γ_{Λn} + Γ_{Σ^0 n} + Γ_{Σ^- p}$ , represents 3-4% of the total one-baryon induced non-mesonic decay and is remarkably affected by strong interaction effects. In particular, the relative importance of the partial decay rates, encoded in the ratio $Γ_{Λn} / (Γ_{Σ^0 n} + Γ_{Σ^- p})$, gets inverted when the mixing to ΞN states is incorporated in the initial correlated ΛΛ wave function. This sensitivity can be used experimentally to learn about the strong interaction in the strangeness -2 sector.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies for the first time the contribution of novel weak decay modes in the doubly-strange hypernucleus \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He that arise when the strong interaction couples the initial \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pair to \(\Xi N\) and \(\Sigma\Sigma\) states; the relative importance of the partial decay rates, specifically the ratio \(\Gamma_{\Lambda n} / (\Gamma_{\Sigma^0 n} + \Gamma_{\Sigma^- p})\), becomes inverted when \(\Lambda\Lambda\)-\(\Xi N\) mixing is incorporated, a sensitivity that could be used experimentally to probe the strong interaction in the strangeness \(-2\) sector.

## Method

The non-mesonic weak decay rate of \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He was computed using a one-meson exchange model with pseudoscalar and vector mesons, where the weak transition occurs from \(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Xi N\), or \(\Sigma\Sigma\) states that are coupled via the strong interaction to the initial \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pair. The initial correlated \(\Lambda\Lambda\) wave function was obtained by solving a G-matrix equation with realistic baryon-baryon potentials in infinite nuclear matter, including Pauli blocking effects, while the final hyperon-nucleon wave functions were derived from a microscopic T-matrix calculation via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The weak decay constants for the novel \(\Xi N \to YN\) transitions were derived using SU(3) symmetry for pseudoscalar mesons and Hidden Local Symmetry for vector mesons, with SU(6)\(_W\) for the weak vertices.

## Key numbers

* The total \(\Lambda\Lambda \to YN\) decay rate, \(\Gamma_{\Lambda n} + \Gamma_{\Sigma^0 n} + \Gamma_{\Sigma^- p}\), represents 3–4% of the total one-baryon induced non-mesonic decay of \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He.
* The oscillator parameter for the \(\Lambda\) particle was set to \(b_\Lambda = 1.6\) fm, and for the nucleon to \(b_N = 1.4\) fm, the latter fixed by the \(^4\)He charge form factor.
* The relative momentum \(k^{*}\) used to define the correlation function for the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pair in the finite hypernucleus was taken to be 100 MeV, representative of the average momentum of the pair in \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Parreno (2001) on double-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei by including, for the first time, weak decay processes from \(\Xi N\) and \(\Sigma\Sigma\) intermediate states coupled to the initial \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pair via the strong interaction, requiring new weak decay constants for \(\Xi N \to YN\) transitions. The results are relevant for interpreting data from the J-PARC E05 experiment, which aims to study \(\Xi\)-hypernuclear spectroscopy, and the E07 experiment, which will produce double-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei in emulsion with ten times the statistics of the KEK-E373 experiment that observed the Nagara event (\(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He). The sensitivity of the decay rate ratio to \(\Lambda\Lambda\)-\(\Xi N\) mixing provides a new experimental handle to constrain the strong baryon-baryon interaction in the strangeness \(-2\) sector.
