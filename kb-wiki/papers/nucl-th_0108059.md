# Quartet S-wave p-d scattering in EFT

> We present a power counting to include Coulomb effects in the three-nucleon system in a low-energy pionless effective field theory (EFT). With this power counting, the quartet S-wave proton-deuteron elastic scattering amplitude is calculated. The calculation includes next-to-leading order (NLO) Coulomb effects and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) strong interaction effects, with an estimated theoretical error of about 7 %. The EFT results agree with potential model calculations and phase shift analysis of experimental data within the estimated errors.

## Key claim

The paper presents a power counting scheme to include Coulomb effects in the three-nucleon system within a low-energy pionless effective field theory (EFT), and calculates the quartet S-wave proton-deuteron elastic scattering amplitude including NLO Coulomb effects and N2LO strong interaction effects with an estimated theoretical error of about 7%.

## Method

The authors develop a power counting for Coulomb photons in the pionless EFT framework, distinguishing between the external momentum scale p and the deuteron binding momentum gamma, and then solve the integral equation for the full amputated scattering amplitude that includes both the strong neutron-exchange kernel and the Coulomb ladder contributions. The calculation uses dimensional regularization and includes effective range corrections to all orders by using the dressed dibaryon propagator with the effective range rho, while neglecting certain subleading diagrams such as single photon exchange with strong vertices (estimated as 7% effects) and two-photon bubble corrections (estimated as 2% effects).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding momentum gamma = 45 MeV (derived from binding energy B = 2.224575(9) MeV)
- Effective range rho = 1.765(4) fm for the triplet channel
- Estimated theoretical error of about 7% for the calculated amplitude
- Two-photon bubble corrections estimated at approximately 2% and neglected
- The calculation is valid for momenta p greater than approximately 20 MeV, below which the numerical procedure does not yield reliable results

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT calculations previously applied to the n-d system (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck 1999; Gabbiani, Bedaque, Griesshammer 1999) to the p-d system by incorporating Coulomb effects, which is a natural extension needed for calculations of low-energy processes such as pd -> gamma 3He and triton beta decay. The power counting for the strong interaction follows the established framework of Kaplan, Savage, Wise (1998) and the results agree with potential model calculations and phase shift analysis of experimental data within the estimated errors, enabling future EFT calculations of inelastic three-nucleon processes involving external currents.
