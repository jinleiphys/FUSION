# QCD and the Strange Baryon Spectrum

> The strange quark plays a unique role in QCD, reflecting its intermediate mass between the light and heavy quarks. In recent years, remarkable progress has been made in the spectroscopy of baryons with strangeness. Many new features of the strange baryon spectrum have been revealed by accurate experimental data with novel techniques, as well as systematic developments of theoretical framework to describe hadron resonances. The basic properties of strange baryons, namely, the pole positions, spin and parity, and decay branching ratios, are being determined accurately. As a consequence, the Particle Data Group have added new entries in the particle listings, such as the $Λ(1380)$ and the $Ω(2012)$. The developments of the spectroscopy stimulate intensive discussion on the exotic internal structure of strange baryons beyond the ordinary three-quark configuration. In this review, we introduce the basics of QCD, the scattering theory, and the exotic internal structure of hadrons, emphasizing the importance of the pole positions of the scattering amplitude for the characterization of hadron resonances. We then summarize the current status of selected strange baryon resonances; $Λ(1405)$, $Λ(1670)$, $Ξ(1620)$, $Ξ(1690)$, and $Ω(2012)$, from theoretical and experimental viewpoints.

## Key claim

This review argues that the pole positions of the scattering amplitude, rather than simple Breit-Wigner masses, are the essential quantities for characterizing hadron resonances, and that recent experimental and theoretical progress has enabled the accurate determination of these poles for several strange baryons, leading to new Particle Data Group entries such as the Λ(1380) and Ω(2012).

## Method

The authors provide a pedagogical introduction to QCD, scattering theory, and exotic hadron structure, emphasizing the role of pole positions in the complex energy plane. They then survey the current experimental and theoretical status of five selected strange baryon resonances (Λ(1405), Λ(1670), Ξ(1620), Ξ(1690), and Ω(2012)), reviewing results from coupled-channel scattering analyses, lattice QCD calculations, and photoproduction experiments.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1405) resonance is described as having two poles in the complex energy plane, a common feature of coupled-channel dynamics; the lower pole is located near 1420 MeV with a width of about 40 MeV, and the higher pole near 1390 MeV with a width of about 120 MeV.
- The Ω(2012) resonance, newly added to the Particle Data Group listings, has a mass near 2012 MeV and a width of approximately 6 MeV.
- The Ξ(1620) resonance has a mass around 1620 MeV and a width of about 40 MeV.
- The Ξ(1690) resonance has a mass near 1690 MeV and a width of roughly 30 MeV.
- The Λ(1670) resonance has a mass around 1670 MeV and a width of about 50 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on baryon spectroscopy using coupled-channel approaches, such as the chiral unitary approach developed by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998), and the lattice QCD studies by the HAL QCD Collaboration (Aoki et al., 2012). It enables future experimental programs at J-PARC (proposals E57 and E62) and further lattice QCD calculations to resolve the internal structure of these resonances, particularly the question of whether they are ordinary three-quark states or have exotic molecular or pentaquark configurations.
