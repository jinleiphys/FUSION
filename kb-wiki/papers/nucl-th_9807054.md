# The Power Of Effective Field Theories In Nuclei: The Deuteron, NN Scattering and Electroweak Processes

> We show how effectively effective quantum field theories work in nuclear physics. Using the physically transparent cut-off regularization, we study the simplest nuclear systems of two nucleons for both bound and scattering states at a momentum scale much less than the pion mass. We consider all the static properties of the deuteron, the two-nucleon scattering phase-shifts, the n + p --> d + γprocess at thermal energy and the solar proton fusion process p + p --> d + e(+) + ν(e), and we demonstrate that these are all described with great accuracy in the expansion to the next-to-leading order. We explore how a "new" degree of freedom enters in an effective theory by turning on and off the role of the pion in the Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) with finite cut-off regularization, expanded to next-to-leading order (NLO), can describe two-nucleon bound and scattering observables with great accuracy for momenta much less than the pion mass, but only when the expansion is carried out to NLO rather than leading order (LO), as LO calculations show strong cutoff dependence.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for two nucleons at momenta below the pion mass, using a Gaussian momentum cutoff regulator and including contact interactions up to NLO (terms with up to two spatial derivatives). They solve the Schrödinger equation for the resulting potential in the \(np\) and \(pp\) \(^1S_0\) channels and the deuteron \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) coupled channel, fixing the low-energy constants from scattering lengths and effective ranges (for \(^1S_0\) channels) or from the deuteron binding energy, asymptotic S-wave normalization, and D/S ratio (for the deuteron channel). They then compute electroweak matrix elements for \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\) and \(p+p\rightarrow d+e^++\nu_e\) without further adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- \(np\) \(^1S_0\) scattering length: \(a_0^{\rm exp}= -23.749(8)\ \text{fm}\), effective range: \(r_0^{\rm exp}= 2.81(5)\ \text{fm}\)
- \(pp\) \(^1S_0\) scattering length: \(a^C_{\rm exp}= -7.8196(26)\ \text{fm}\), effective range: \(r^C_{\rm exp}= 2.790(14)\ \text{fm}\)
- Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d^{\rm exp} = 2.224575(9)\ \text{MeV}\), asymptotic S-wave normalization: \(A_s^{\rm exp} = 0.8846(8)\ \text{fm}^{1/2}\), D/S ratio: \(\eta_d^{\rm exp} = 0.0256(4)\)
- Without pion, for cutoff \(\Lambda=200\ \text{MeV}\): deuteron charge radius \(r_d = 1.978\ \text{fm}\), quadrupole moment \(Q_d = 0.302\ \text{fm}^2\), magnetic moment \(\mu_d = 0.845\), M1 matrix element \(\mathcal{M}_{\rm M1} = 3.955\ \text{fm}\), GT matrix element \(\mathcal{M}_{\rm GT} = 4.846\ \text{fm}\)
- Vacuum polarization correction to GT matrix element: \(\delta_{\rm 1B}^{\rm VP} = -0.45\%\) (for \(\Lambda=200\ \text{MeV}\))

## Context

This work extends the program of applying effective field theory to nuclear systems, following the counting rules of Weinberg (1990) and using cut-off regularization as advocated by Lepage (1997) and Donoghue (1995). The paper provides a quantitative justification from "first principles" for the previously formulated "hybrid method" of Park, Min, and Rho (1996), which used high-quality phenomenological wavefunctions from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential (Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla, 1995) together with chiral perturbation theory operators. The results enable parameter-free predictions for the solar proton fusion process \(p+p\rightarrow d+e^++\nu_e\), which is crucial for understanding the solar neutrino problem.
