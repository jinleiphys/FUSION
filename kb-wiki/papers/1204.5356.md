# Empirical determination of charm quark energy loss and its consequences for azimuthal anisotropy

> We propose an empirical model to determine the form of energy loss of charm quarks due to multiple scatterings in quark gluon plasma by demanding a good description of production of D mesons and non-photonic electrons in relativistic collision of heavy nuclei at RHIC and LHC energies. Best results are obtained when we approximate the momentum loss per collision $Δp_T \propto α\, p_T$, where $α$ is a constant depending on the centrality and the centre of mass energy. Comparing our results with those obtained earlier for drag coefficients estimated using Langevin equation for heavy quarks we find that up to half of the energy loss of charm quarks at top RHIC energy could be due to collisions while that at LHC energy at 2760 GeV/A the collisional energy loss could be about one third of the total. Estimates are obtained for azimuthal anisotropy in momentum spectra of heavy mesons, due to this energy loss. We further suggest that energy loss of charm quarks may lead to an enhanced production of D-mesons and single electrons at low $p_T$ in AA collisions.

# Empirical determination of charm quark energy loss and its consequences for azimuthal anisotropy

## Key claim
The authors propose that charm quark energy loss in quark-gluon plasma can be empirically described by a momentum-loss-per-collision proportional to the quark's momentum (Δp_T ∝ α p_T), and that this form provides a good description of nuclear modification data for D-mesons and non-photonic electrons at both RHIC and LHC energies. However, the model is phenomenological rather than derived from first principles, and the best-fit parameter α depends on both centrality and center-of-mass energy.

## Method
The authors implement a Monte Carlo model where charm quarks produced via leading-order pQCD (gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation) undergo multiple scatterings in the QGP, with momentum loss per collision given by Δp = α p^β. They test three values of β (0, 0.5, 1.0) corresponding to different energy-loss regimes (Bethe-Heitler, LPM, and complete coherence), and vary α to fit the nuclear modification factor R_AA for single electrons at RHIC (√s = 200 A GeV) and for D-mesons at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV/nucleon). The charm quarks are then fragmented into D-mesons using the Peterson fragmentation function, and semileptonic decays produce the electron spectra. Azimuthal anisotropy v_2 is computed from the path-length dependence of energy loss in non-central collisions.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit model uses Δp_T ∝ α p_T (β=1); α is a centrality- and energy-dependent constant
- For RHIC Au+Au at √s = 200 A GeV, 0-10% centrality: T_AA = 225 fm^{-2}
- For LHC Pb+Pb at √s = 2.76 TeV/nucleon, 0-20% centrality: T_AA = 195 fm^{-2}
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV; mean free path λ = 1 fm
- Effective drag coefficient at RHIC: A_eff ≈ 0.04 fm^{-1}, compared to 0.02 fm^{-1} from collisional energy loss alone (Moore and Teaney 2005), implying up to half of charm quark energy loss at RHIC could be due to collisions
- At LHC (2.76 TeV/nucleon): collisional energy loss accounts for about one-third of total energy loss
- Peterson fragmentation parameter ε_p = 0.13
- α_s fixed at 0.3 for initial charm distribution
- Varying renormalization scale by factor C=1 or 2 changes R_AA by 7-10%, offset by decreasing α_B by about 12%

## Context
This work extends the multiple-scattering energy-loss approach previously applied to light partons (Somnath 2010) to heavy quarks, and compares with drag coefficients from Langevin/Fokker-Planck treatments of heavy quark propagation (Svetitsky 1988; Moore and Teaney 2005). The authors find that collisional energy loss alone cannot account for the observed suppression, consistent with the need for radiative energy loss. The model predicts enhanced D-meson and single-electron production at low p_T due to energy loss of higher-momentum charm quarks, a signature distinct from the Cronin effect. The results enable future studies of the centrality and energy dependence of the momentum-loss coefficient α, and may inform calculations of low-mass dilepton production from correlated charm decays.
