# Amplitude/Operator Basis in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We establish a systematic construction of the on-shell amplitude/operator basis for Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) in $D=4$ spacetime dimensions and with an arbitrary number of flavors $N_f$. For kinematic factors, we employ spinor-helicity variables to construct the soft blocks, which are local amplitudes satisfying the Adler's zero condition, as well as to take into account the reduction in the kinematic basis due to the Gram determinant, which arises at $O(p^{10})$ when the number of multiplicity $N$ in an amplitude becomes large: $N>D$. For flavor factors, we include group-theoretic relations at small $N_f$, $N_f\le N$, which decreases the flavor basis. The result is obtained by adapting the Young tensor method of constructing the operator basis for generic effective field theories to the case of non-linearly realized symmetries. Working in the massless quark limit, we present purely mesonic operators for both even- and odd-parity at $O(p^6)$ and $O(p^8)$ for $N=6$ and arbitrary $N_f$, and establish a direct correspondence between the amplitude basis and the operator basis. Furthermore, the redundancy due to the Gram determinant is studied at $O(p^{10})$ for $N=6, 8$ and 10.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a systematic on-shell amplitude/operator basis for Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) in D=4 spacetime dimensions with arbitrary flavor number N_f, incorporating constraints from the Gram determinant (which becomes effective at O(p^{10}) for N>4) and group-theoretic relations at small N_f (N_f ≤ N), thereby reducing the independent operator count compared to previous constructions that assumed large N_f and ignored these redundancies.

## Method

The authors adapt the Young tensor method (y-basis) previously developed for generic effective field theories to the case of nonlinearly realized symmetries in ChPT, using spinor-helicity variables to construct "soft blocks" that satisfy Adler's zero condition. For kinematic factors, they employ the spinor-helicity formalism which automatically incorporates Gram determinant constraints through Schouten identities, and construct the independent basis as Semi-Standard Young Tableaux (SSYTs). For flavor factors, they include group-theoretic relations at small N_f (N_f ≤ N) to reduce the flavor basis. The soft blocks are obtained by imposing Adler's zero conditions on all external particles, either through a general algorithm using the ABC4EFT package or through a direct construction using monomials of Mandelstam variables s_{ij} and epsilon tensors.

## Key numbers

- At O(p^{10}) for 6-point amplitudes, the number of parity-even kinematic invariants reduces from 1287 (general D) to 1286 (D=4) due to Gram determinant constraints
- At O(p^{10}) for 6-point amplitudes, parity-odd kinematic invariants reduce from 825 (general D) to 600 (D=4)
- At O(p^{10}) for 8-point amplitudes, parity-even invariants reduce from 42504 (general D) to 42308 (D=4)
- At O(p^{10}) for 8-point amplitudes, parity-odd invariants reduce from 53900 (general D) to 28196 (D=4)
- For 6-point amplitudes at O(p^6), the number of independent soft blocks is 25, obtained from 205 SSYTs after imposing Adler's zero conditions
- For 6-point amplitudes at O(p^6), there are 40 independent parity-odd kinematic invariants in D=4 (versus 45 in general D)

## Context

This work extends the on-shell amplitude approach to ChPT developed by Low (2014) and Cheung (2014, 2015), and the operator basis construction up to O(p^{10}) by Low (2019) and Dai (2020), which did not include Gram determinant constraints or finite N_f effects. The method adapts the Young tableau technique (y-basis) from Li (2020) for SMEFT to the nonlinearly realized symmetry case of ChPT. The results enable a complete and minimal operator basis for ChPT at higher orders, with direct correspondence between amplitude basis and operator basis, and can be extended to include external sources of fermions or vectors as shown by Sun (2022).
