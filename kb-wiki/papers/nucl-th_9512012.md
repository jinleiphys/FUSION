# Strange Neutral Currents in Nuclei

> We examine the effects on the nuclear neutral current Gamow-Teller (GT) strength of a finite contribution from a polarized strange quark sea. We perform nuclear shell model calculations of the neutral current GT strength for a number of nuclei likely to be present during stellar core collapse. We compare the GT strength when a finite strange quark contribution is included to the strength without such a contribution. As an example, the process of neutral current nuclear de-excitation via $ν{\overlineν}$ pair production is examined for the two cases.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a polarized strange quark sea (nonzero $\Delta s$) in the nucleon introduces an isoscalar component to the neutral current Gamow-Teller operator, which can redistribute the GT strength in nuclei relevant to supernova core collapse; however, the authors treat their results as upper limits because they use the larger EMC value for the strange axial coupling ($G_A^s = -0.38$) while quenching the isovector coupling to $g_A = 1.0$, and note that the SMC experiment finds a smaller value.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations of neutral current Gamow-Teller strength using the code CRUNCHER for nuclei in the $p$, $sd$, and $fp$ shells. They used the Cohen-Kurath interaction for $p$-shell nuclei ($^{12}$C, $^{14}$N), the universal $sd$ (W) interaction for $sd$-shell nuclei ($^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si), and the fpvh interaction for $fp$-shell nuclei (a series of iron isotopes from $^{50}$Fe to $^{66}$Fe plus $^{56}$Ni), employing truncated model spaces for the $fp$-shell calculations. They compared the GT strength and its energy-weighted centroid for the case $G_A^s = 0$ versus $G_A^s = -0.38$, and computed the neutrino pair energy emission rate from neutral current nuclear de-excitation ($A^* \rightarrow A \nu \bar{\nu}$) as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The strange axial coupling adopted is $G_A^s(0) = -0.38 \pm 0.12$ from EMC data; the isovector coupling is quenched to $g_A = 1.0$ (canonical value $g_A = 1.262$).
- For $G_A^s = -0.38$ and $g_A = 1.0$, the single-particle GT operator values are $GT|_{protons} = -0.595 \sigma$ and $GT|_{neutrons} = 0.405 \sigma$.
- The ratio of proton to neutron cross section is $\sigma_p/\sigma_n = 1.76$ for $g_A = 1.0$ (or $1.60$ for $g_A = 1.262$).
- For $^{66}$Fe ($N-Z = 14$), the total GT strength increases by 41.6% ($B(GT_0)$ goes from 8.4445 to 11.9583), and the neutrino energy emission rate at $T \sim 1$ MeV increases by approximately 40%.
- For $T=0$ nuclei, the total GT strength changes by 3-6% (e.g., $^{56}$Ni: 3.2%, $^{28}$Si: 3.2%, $^{14}$N: 5.8%).
- The energy-weighted centroid shifts are small, typically less than 0.16 MeV, with $^{66}$Fe showing zero centroid shift.

## Context

This work extends the study of strange quark effects in neutral current interactions by Kolbe et al. (1992), who used the continuum random phase approximation for $\nu$-process nucleosynthesis, by performing detailed nuclear shell model calculations for a wider range of nuclei relevant to supernova core collapse. The paper enables quantitative assessment of how a nonzero strange quark contribution affects neutrino energy emission and transport in collapsing stellar cores, and suggests that $T=0$ nuclei could provide a method for measuring $\Delta s$ via neutrino-induced transitions to $T=0$ excited states, as previously proposed by Suzuki et al. (1990) and Henley et al. (1991). The results indicate that for most nuclei the effect is modest (a few percent), but for very neutron-rich nuclei near neutron shell closure ($N=40$) the enhancement can reach about 40%, which could significantly affect cooling if such nuclei are abundant during collapse.
