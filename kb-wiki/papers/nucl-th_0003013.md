# Quantum interference in the e^+e^- decays of rho^0 and omega-mesons produced in pi^- p reactions

> The study of the pi^- p -> rho^0 n and pi^- p -> omega n amplitudes close and below the vector meson production threshold (1.2< sqrt(s) <1.8 GeV) reveals a rich dynamics arising from the presence of specific baryon resonances in this energy range. The interference pattern of the e^+e^- decays of the rho^0 and omega mesons produced in pi^- p reactions reflects directly this dynamics. We discuss this interference pattern in the pi^- p -> e^+e^- n reaction as function of the total center of mass energy sqrt(s). We emphasize the importance of an experimental study of this reaction, which could be made with the HADES detector and the available pion beam at GSI.

## Key claim

The interference pattern in the e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ decays of rho‚Āį and omega mesons produced in pi‚ĀĽ p reactions is very sensitive to the magnitudes and relative phase of the production amplitudes, which are themselves strongly influenced by specific baryon resonances (particularly the N*(1520)) in the energy range 1.2 < sqrt(s) < 1.8 GeV; however, the model used is restricted to s- and d-wave pion-nucleon resonances and should be extended to include p-wave resonances for completeness.

## Method

The authors compute the pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ n reaction cross section using a relativistic coupled-channel model (Lutz et al.) that describes pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí rho‚Āį n and pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí omega n amplitudes, restricted to s-wave scattering in the rho N and omega N channels with dynamically generated s- and d-wave resonances in the pi N channel. The vector meson production amplitudes are related to the e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ final state through the Vector Dominance assumption, using coupling constants f_rho = 0.036 GeV¬≤ and f_omega = 0.011 GeV¬≤ determined from measured partial widths of rho‚Āį and omega decays into e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ pairs. The relative phase of the rho and omega amplitudes in each channel is determined by comparing with photon-decay helicity amplitudes of the corresponding resonances, assuming Vector Meson Dominance.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel model of Lutz et al. (2000) for pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí rho‚Āį n and pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí omega n amplitudes to predict the pi‚ĀĽ p ‚Üí e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ n reaction, providing a test of vector meson-baryon resonance couplings that are crucial for understanding in-medium propagation of vector mesons. The importance of measuring the rho-omega phase has been evidenced by similar interference studies in other processes, such as the photoproduction of rho‚Āį and omega mesons in the diffractive regime (Alvensleben et al.) and the e‚Āļe‚ĀĽ ‚Üí pi‚Āļ pi‚ĀĽ reaction (Benakas et al., Connell et al.). The authors propose that such measurements could be performed with the HADES detector and the available pion beam at GSI, and note that data on this reaction are currently not available.
