# The Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature at next to leading order in the 1/N expansion

> We present new results on the Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature and at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion. In particular, a new expression is obtained for the effective potential which is explicitly invariant under renormalization group transformations. The model is used as a playground to investigate various features of field theory at finite temperature. For example we verify that, as expected from general arguments, the cancellation of ultraviolet divergences takes place at finite temperature without the need for introducing counterterms beyond those of zero-temperature. As well known, the discrete chiral symmetry of the 1+1 dimensional model is spontaneously broken at zero temperature and restored, in leading order, at some temperature T_c; we find that the 1/N approximation breaks down for temperatures below T_c: As the temperature increases, the fluctuations become eventually too large to be treated as corrections, and a Landau pole invalidates the calculation of the effective potential in the vicinity of its minimum. Beyond T_c, the 1/N expansion becomes again regular: it predicts that in leading order the system behaves as a free gas of massless fermions and that, at the next-to-leading order, it remains weakly interacting. In the limit of large temperature, the pressure coincides with that given by perturbation theory with a coupling constant defined at a scale of the order of the temperature, as expected from asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the 1/N expansion for the Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature breaks down for temperatures below the critical temperature T_c, where fluctuations become too large to be treated as corrections and a Landau pole invalidates the calculation of the effective potential near its minimum; above T_c the expansion becomes regular again, and at high temperature the system behaves as a weakly interacting gas of massless fermions.

## Method

The authors compute the effective potential for the Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion using the imaginary time formalism. They introduce an auxiliary scalar field sigma, perform a saddle point expansion of the path integral, and evaluate the Gaussian fluctuations about the saddle point to obtain the 1/N correction. The renormalization is carried out explicitly at zero temperature, and the finite-temperature calculation verifies that ultraviolet divergences cancel using only zero-temperature counterterms.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature T_c at which chiral symmetry is restored in leading order of the 1/N expansion is identified, though no explicit numerical value is given in the text.
- The fermion mass arising from symmetry breaking is of order M_f, with kink energies of order epsilon ~ N M_f.
- The number of kinks in the system is ~ L exp(-epsilon/T), where L is the length of the system.
- At high temperature, the pressure coincides with perturbation theory using a running coupling defined at a scale of order the temperature, consistent with asymptotic freedom.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Gross-Neveu model at finite temperature, which mostly concentrated on mean-field physics and chiral symmetry breaking (Jacobs (1974), Harrington (1975), Wolff (1985), Barducci (1985), Bernard (1987), Hatsuda (1986), Asakawa (1989)). It goes beyond these by providing a systematic next-to-leading order calculation in the 1/N expansion with explicit renormalization group invariance. The results enable future investigations of how the 1/N expansion handles the interplay between fluctuations and symmetry restoration, and leave open the question of whether including kink configurations (discussed qualitatively by Dashen (1975)) would cure the breakdown of the expansion below T_c.
