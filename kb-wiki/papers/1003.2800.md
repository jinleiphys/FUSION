# Tracing a phase transition with fluctuations of the largest fragment size: Statistical multifragmentation models and the ALADIN S254 data

> A phase transition signature associated with cumulants of the largest fragment size distribution has been identified in statistical multifragmentation models and examined in analysis of the ALADIN S254 data on fragmentation of neutron-poor and neutron-rich projectiles. Characteristics of the transition point indicated by this signature are weakly dependent on the A/Z ratio of the fragmenting spectator source. In particular, chemical freeze-out temperatures are estimated within the range 5.9 to 6.5 MeV. The experimental results are well reproduced by the SMM model.

## Key claim

The cumulant signature of a phase transition (skewness K3=0 and kurtosis K4 minimum) previously identified in percolation models also appears at the liquid-gas phase transition in statistical multifragmentation models, and this signature is weakly dependent on the A/Z ratio of the fragmenting spectator source when applied to the ALADIN S254 experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed canonical thermodynamic model calculations for systems of 64, 216, and 1000 nucleons at a freeze-out volume three times the normal nuclear volume, then conducted SMM (statistical multifragmentation model) simulations with secondary decays at one-third normal nuclear density. They applied the cumulant analysis of the largest fragment charge (Zmax) distribution to ALADIN S254 experimental data from 600 AMeV beams of 107Sn, 124Sn, and 124La projectiles, sorting events by Zbound as the control parameter.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperatures estimated in the range 5.9 to 6.5 MeV for the fragmenting spectator sources
- For 107Sn projectile: Zbound = 28 at transition, Z0 = 32.2 ± 2, THeLi = 5.9 ± 0.3 MeV, SMM Tb = 5.87 ± 0.05 MeV
- For 124Sn projectile: Zbound = 29 at transition, Z0 = 33.5 ± 2, THeLi = 6.5 ± 0.3 MeV, SMM Tb = 6.18 ± 0.05 MeV
- For 124La projectile: Zbound = 30 at transition, Z0 = 34.5 ± 2, THeLi = 6.2 ± 0.3 MeV, SMM Tb = 5.82 ± 0.05 MeV
- Mean compositions of nominal 107Sn beam: ⟨Z⟩ = 49.7, ⟨A/Z⟩ = 2.16; for 124La beam: ⟨Z⟩ = 56.8, ⟨A/Z⟩ = 2.19
- Isotope temperatures THeLi were raised by 15% with possible error of ±5% to correct for secondary decays

## Context

This work extends the cumulant analysis method previously applied to percolation models by Botvina (various years) and others to the liquid-gas phase transition in statistical multifragmentation models, and applies it to experimental data from the ALADIN S254 experiment. The results confirm the weak isotopic dependence of projectile fragmentation previously reported by Sfienti (various years) and show that SMM ensemble calculations, with parameters adopted from earlier work on 197Au fragmentation by Botvina (various years), provide satisfactory agreement with the experimental Zmax distributions without parameter optimization. The findings enable future use of cumulant analysis as a robust tool for identifying phase transitions in finite nuclear systems, even when the small system size prevents clear observation of bimodality.
