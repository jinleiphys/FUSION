# Partial Dynamical SU(3) Symmetry and the Nature of the Lowest K=0 Collective Excitation in Deformed Nuclei

> We discuss the implications of partial dynamical SU(3) symmetry (PDS) for the structure of the lowest K=0^{+} (K=0_2) collective excitation in deformed nuclei. We consider an interacting boson model Hamiltonian whose ground and gamma bands have good SU(3) symmetry while the K=0_2 band is mixed. It is shown that the double-phonon components in the K=0_2 wave function arise from SU(3) admixtures which, in turn, can be determined from absolute E2 rates connecting the K=0_2 and ground bands. An explicit expression is derived for these admixtures in terms of the ratio of K=0_2 and gamma bandhead energies. The SU(3) PDS predictions are compared with existing data and with broken-SU(3) calculations for ^{168}Er.

# Partial Dynamical SU(3) Symmetry and the Nature of the Lowest K=0 Collective Excitation in Deformed Nuclei

## Key claim

Under partial dynamical SU(3) symmetry (PDS), the lowest K=0₂ collective excitation in deformed nuclei is predominantly a single-phonon (β) mode with small but significant double-γ-phonon admixtures, where the mixing is of order 1/N and depends critically on the ratio of K=0₂ and γ bandhead energies; the double-phonon admixture is at most ~15% for the empirically relevant bandhead ratio range of 0.8–1.65.

## Method

The authors constructed an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian with partial SU(3) symmetry, where the ground and γ bands have good SU(3) symmetry while the K=0₂ band is mixed. They performed three types of calculations: exact diagonalization, a 3-band mixing calculation using large-N intrinsic states, and an analytic approximation to the latter. The SU(3) admixtures in the K=0₂ wave function were extracted from absolute E2 rates connecting the K=0₂ and ground bands, and the predictions were compared with existing data and broken-SU(3) calculations (Warner-Casten-Davidson and consistent-Q formalism) for ¹⁶⁸Er.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁸Er (N=16 bosons), the K=0₂ band contains 9.6% (26,0) and 2.9% (24,4) SU(3) admixtures into the dominant (28,2) irreducible representation.
- The K=0₂ wave function in ¹⁶⁸Er contains 12.4% γ²_{K=0} and 0.1% β² admixtures into the β mode, giving 12.5% total double-phonon admixtures.
- The empirical ratio of K=0₂ and γ bandhead energies E(0⁺₂)/[E(2⁺_γ)-E(2⁺_g)] = 0.8–1.8 in the rare-earth region constrains the Hamiltonian parameter ratio to 0.7 ≤ h₀/h₂ ≤ 2.4.
- For ¹⁶⁸Er (h₀=2h₂, y=2/3, N=16), the analytic approximation yields a bandhead ratio estimate of 1.62 compared to the exact value 1.64.
- The E2 parameter θ for ¹⁶⁸Er was determined from the known 2⁺_γ→0⁺_g E2 rate to be θ²=2.175 W.u.
- The Coulomb excitation measurement by Hartelin et al. (1998) gives B(E2; 2⁺_{K=0₂}→0⁺_g) = 0.058 ± 0.007 W.u., while the lifetime measurement by Lehmann et al. (1998) gives a value marginally within the extreme range of that measurement.
- The Lehmann et al. (1998) value of 6.2 W.u. (or 3.1 W.u. assuming 50% E2 multipolarity) for the 2⁺_{K=0₂}→2⁺_γ transition translates via Alaga rule to 21.7 (or 10.85) W.u. for the 0⁺_{K=0₂}→2⁺_γ transition, a factor of 7.8 (or 3.9) larger than the Hartelin et al. value of 2.8 ± 0.4 W.u.

## Context

This work extends the partial dynamical SU(3) symmetry framework previously introduced by Leviatan (1996) to address the longstanding controversy over the nature of the lowest K=0⁺ excitation in deformed nuclei. It directly addresses the debate between Casten and von Brentano (1994), who proposed double-γ-phonon character for these bands based on preferential decay to the γ band, and Burke and Sood (1995) and Gunther et al. (1996), who argued that minor double-γ-phonon admixtures or band mixing could explain the observations. The PDS predictions are compared with broken-SU(3) calculations by Warner, Casten, and Davidson (WCD) and the consistent-Q formalism (CQF), and with new lifetime and Coulomb excitation data for ¹⁶⁸Er from Lehmann et al. (1998) and Hartelin et al. (1998). The results support the conventional single-phonon interpretation with small double-γ-phonon admixtures, but highlight serious discrepancies between the two experimental measurements that require independent lifetime measurement of the 0⁺_{K=0₂} state in ¹⁶⁸Er to resolve.
