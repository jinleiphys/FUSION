# Reaction and interaction nucleus-nucleus cross sections in the complete Glauber theory

> The straightforward calculations of the reaction and interaction cross sections of the nuclear scattering are carried out in Glauber approach using the generating function method. It allows for the resummation of all orders of Glauber theory. The results are obtained for $^4$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{12}$C scattering on $^{12}$C target. The difference between the reaction and the differential cross section is shown to be not exceeding several percents

## Key claim

The difference between the reaction cross section and the interaction cross section for nucleus-nucleus scattering is shown to be not exceeding several percent, but this difference must be taken into account for correct analysis of experimental data, particularly when extracting nuclear radii.

## Method

The authors perform complete analytical Glauber calculations using the generating function method, which allows resummation of all orders of Glauber theory. The generating function approach reduces the Glauber integrals to products of independent integrals at each spatial point, enabling exact evaluation of both the reaction cross section and the interaction cross section. The nucleon-nucleon amplitude is parametrized with a Gaussian form, and nuclear densities are taken as Gaussian distributions with parameters adjusted to match experimental interaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For $^4$He on $^{12}$C: experimental interaction cross section 503 ± 5 mb, reaction cross section 523 mb (difference ~4%)
- For $^{12}$C on $^{12}$C: experimental interaction cross section 853 ± 6 mb, reaction cross section 864 mb (difference ~1.3%)
- For $^{11}$Li on $^{12}$C: experimental interaction cross section 1047 ± 40 mb, reaction cross section 1057 mb (difference ~1%)
- Mean square radius of $^{11}$Li extracted from interaction cross section: 3.28 fm; from reaction cross section: 0.13 fm larger
- Halo radius of $^{11}$Li: 5.48 fm
- $^{12}$C radius increases by 0.02 fm when using reaction cross section instead of interaction cross section
- Nucleon-nucleon total cross section: 43 mb at ~1000 MeV per nucleon; slope parameter β = 0.2 fm²

## Context

This work extends the generating function method developed by Shabelski (2021) to compute both reaction and interaction cross sections analytically, confirming the Monte-Carlo estimate of Novikov (2013) that the difference is 2-3% of the reaction cross section. The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear radii, particularly for halo nuclei like $^{11}$Li, by accounting for the systematic difference between the experimentally measured interaction cross section and the reaction cross section used in radius determinations. The authors note that the core-halo structure of $^{11}$Li may be more complex than the simple two-component Gaussian density assumed.
