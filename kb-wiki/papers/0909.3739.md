# Equation of state for supernova matter

> We provide an equation of state for high density supernova matter by applying a momentum-dependent effective interaction. We focus on the study of the equation of state of high-density and high-temperature nuclear matter containing leptons (electrons and neutrinos) under the chemical equilibrium condition. Thermal effects on the properties and equation of state of nuclear matter are evaluated and analyzed in the framework of the proposed effective interaction model. Since supernova matter is characterized by a constant entropy we also present the thermodynamic properties for the isentropic case.

## Key claim

The paper provides an equation of state for high-density supernova matter using a momentum-dependent effective interaction model, but the results are limited to matter in chemical equilibrium under the assumption of constant lepton fraction and constant entropy, which is a simplification of the actual dynamic conditions during a supernova explosion.

## Method

The author applies a momentum-dependent effective interaction model (MDIM) to calculate the equation of state of hot asymmetric nuclear matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, and neutrinos under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. The model separates the energy density into kinetic and interaction contributions, with the interaction part parameterized to reproduce microscopic calculations at zero temperature and extended to finite temperature using Fermi-Dirac distributions. The thermodynamic properties are evaluated for both isothermal and isentropic cases, with the latter obtained by converting isothermal results using a temperature-density relation constrained by constant entropy per baryon.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: E(n=n0) - mc^2 = -16 MeV
- Incompressibility: K = 240 MeV
- Entropy per baryon for supernova matter: S = 1-2 (in units of Boltzmann constant kB)
- Lepton fraction for supernova matter: Yl = 0.3-0.4
- For the T-n relation at S=1 and Yl=0.3: T(n) = 35.412 * n^0.70481 (T in MeV, n in fm^-3)
- For the T-n relation at S=1 and Yl=0.4: T(n) = 32.35706 * n^0.67694 (T in MeV, n in fm^-3)
- Lepton energy dominates internal energy up to n ~ 0.6 fm^-3 for Yl=0.3 and n ~ 0.8 fm^-3 for Yl=0.4
- Lepton pressure is comparable to baryon pressure only up to n ~ 0.2 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of hot beta-stable nuclear matter without neutrinos (Moustakidis 2008) to include neutrino-trapped supernova matter, and builds on the framework of Prakash (1997) for momentum-dependent interactions. The results are compared with the microscopic calculations of Sammarruca (2008) for the nuclear symmetry energy and with the earlier work of Takatsuka (1994) and Lattimer (1985) on supernova matter properties. The equation of state provided can be applied to evaluate the gross properties of hot neutron stars, such as mass and radius, and to study supernova explosion mechanisms.
