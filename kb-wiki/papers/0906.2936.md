# Three-loop HTL Free Energy for QED

> We calculate the free energy of a hot gas of electrons and photons to three loops using the hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory reorganization of finite-temperature perturbation theory. We calculate the free energy through three loops by expanding in a power series in m_D/T, m_f/T, and e^2, where m_D and m_f are thermal masses and e is the coupling constant. We demonstrate that the hard-thermal-loop perturbation reorganization improves the convergence of the successive approximations to the QED free energy at large coupling, e ~ 2. The reorganization is gauge invariant by construction, and due to cancellation among various contributions, we obtain a completely analytic result for the resummed thermodynamic potential at three loops. Finally, we compare our result with similar calculations that use the Phi-derivable approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) improves the convergence of successive approximations to the QED free energy at large coupling, specifically for coupling values up to e ~ 2.5, whereas the abstract only claims improvement at e ~ 2.

## Method

The authors calculate the free energy of a hot gas of electrons and photons to three-loop order using HTLpt, expanding in a power series in m_D/T, m_f/T, and e^2, where m_D and m_f are thermal masses. They determine leading-order (LO), next-to-leading-order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) expressions for the HTLpt pressure, obtaining a completely analytic result for the resummed thermodynamic potential at three loops due to cancellations among various contributions. The variational parameters m_D and m_f are determined by minimizing the free energy through gap equations.

## Key numbers

* The ideal gas free energy of photons is -pi^2/45 T^4 (one-loop result).
* The ideal gas free energy of a single massless fermion is -7pi^2/180 T^4 (one-loop result).
* The two-loop contribution from the perturbative expansion is (5 pi^2/72)(alpha/pi) T^4.
* The HTLpt method shows improved convergence for couplings as large as e ~ 2.5 (compared to e ~ 2 stated in the abstract).
* The soft contribution from the one-loop longitudinal term gives -m_D^3 T/(12 pi) at leading order.

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt method, previously applied to two loops in QED by Andersen, Strickland, and Su (2001-2005), to three-loop order, setting the stage for the corresponding calculation in full QCD. The paper compares results with the Phi-derivable approach (2PI formalism) as applied by Blaizot, Iancu, and collaborators (2007) and shows good agreement at three loops. The HTLpt method is a gauge-invariant extension of screened perturbation theory (SPT) developed by Karsch, Patkos, and Petreczky (1997) for scalar field theory, and addresses the poor convergence of standard weak-coupling expansions for QED at couplings larger than e ~ 1.
