# Neutron rich Carbon and Oxygen isotopes with an odd number of neutrons

> We describe odd isotopes as formed of a core plus one neutron. We have calculated the modification of single neutron energies brought by couplings of the neutron with collective $2^+$ phonons in the cores. The results reproduce very well the inversion of $2s$ and $1d_{5/2}$ shells in carbon isotopes up to $^{19}$C while in oxygen isotopes the correction is also large but do not show any inversion in agreement with experiments. The calculated energies are close to the experimental ones in both series of isotopes except in $^{21}$C for the $2s$ state for which our coupling is too weak.

## Key claim

The inversion of the $2s$ and $1d_{5/2}$ shells in carbon isotopes up to $^{20}$C is reproduced by coupling the odd neutron to the lowest collective $2^+$ phonon of the core, while in oxygen isotopes the same coupling mechanism yields large corrections but no inversion, in agreement with experiment; however, for $^{21}$C the calculated coupling is too weak to make the $2s$ state unbound as observed.

## Method

The authors model odd-neutron isotopes as a neutron plus an inert core, first calculating single-neutron energies in a Saxon-Woods one-body potential. They then compute the modification of these energies due to two-body correlations by coupling the neutron to the lowest $2^+$ collective phonon of the core, using the known excitation energy and $B(E2)$ of that phonon. The correction $\Delta\epsilon_n$ is obtained from an iterative solution of the self-consistent equation involving the neutron-phonon coupling amplitude and the intermediate neutron states.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C (core $^{12}$C): the $2s_{1/2}$ energy shifts from $\epsilon_n = -1.02$ MeV to $E_n = -1.95$ MeV after coupling, compared to the experimental value of $-1.86$ MeV; the $1d_{5/2}$ shifts from $-0.76$ MeV to $-1.14$ MeV (experiment: $-1.12$ MeV).
- For $^{20}$C (core $^{20}$C): the $2s_{1/2}$ energy shifts from $\epsilon_n = -0.93$ MeV to $E_n = -0.56$ MeV, while the experimental value is $+0.5$ MeV (unbound); the $1d_{5/2}$ shifts from $-1.02$ MeV to $-1.20$ MeV.
- For $^{16}$O (core $^{16}$O): the $2s_{1/2}$ energy shifts from $\epsilon_n = -3.2$ MeV to $E_n = -3.81$ MeV (experiment: $-3.3$ MeV); the $1d_{5/2}$ shifts from $-4.52$ MeV to $-4.05$ MeV (experiment: $-4.14$ MeV).
- For $^{22}$O (core $^{22}$O): the $2s_{1/2}$ energy shifts from $\epsilon_n = -2.85$ MeV to $E_n = -2.73$ MeV (experiment: $-2.74$ MeV); the $1d_{5/2}$ shifts from $-4.1$ MeV to $-3.95$ MeV (experiment: $-3.8$ MeV).
- The $2^+$ phonon in $^{12}$C has excitation energy 4.43 MeV and $B(E2) = 39.7$ $e^2$fm$^4$; in $^{16}$C, 1.76 MeV and 13.0 $e^2$fm$^4$; in $^{14}$O, 6.59 MeV and 43 $e^2$fm$^4$; in $^{22}$O, 3.19 MeV and 21 $e^2$fm$^4$.

## Context

This work extends the particle-phonon coupling approach previously applied to $^{11}$Be, $^{10}$Li, and $^{13}$Be (Vinh Mau 1995) to neutron-rich carbon and oxygen isotopes. It contrasts with shell-model calculations by using a simple core-plus-neutron picture with collective phonons, and it shows that the same mechanism explains the $2s$-$1d_{5/2}$ inversion in carbon but not in oxygen. The results enable further studies of pairing vibrations (e.g., in $^{18}$C and $^{22}$C) and suggest that a low-lying $2^+$ state in $^{24}$O, not yet measured, may be needed to reproduce the experimental neutron energies in $^{25}$O.
