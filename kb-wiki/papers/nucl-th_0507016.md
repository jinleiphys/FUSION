# Weak Pion Production From Nuclei

> The charged current pion production induced by neutrinos in $^{12}{C}$, $^{16}{O}$ and $^{56}{Fe}$ nuclei has been studied. The calculations have been done for the coherent as well as the incoherent processes assuming $Δ$ dominance and take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion and the renormalization of $Δ$ in the nuclear medium. The pion absorption effects have also been taken into account.

# Weak Pion Production From Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors calculate charged-current weak pion production from nuclei assuming Delta resonance dominance, but the actual cross sections are reduced by 30-35% in the peak region for incoherent processes and by 60-80% for coherent processes when nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Delta self-energy renormalization) and pion absorption are included, with the magnitude of reduction depending on neutrino energy and target nucleus.

## Method

The calculation uses a local density approximation with three-parameter Fermi (for 12C and 16O) and three-parameter Gaussian (for 56Fe) nuclear densities, modeling the weak N-Delta transition with vector form factors from CVC and axial form factors from ANL and BNL neutrino-deuteron data. The Delta propagator in the nuclear medium is modified by a density-dependent self-energy (real part +40 MeV at nuclear saturation density, imaginary part parametrized for two-body and three-body absorption channels), and final-state pion absorption is treated via an eikonal approximation with an energy-dependent mean free path. Both incoherent production (where the nucleus breaks up) and coherent production (where the nucleus remains in the ground state) are computed separately.

## Key numbers

* Incoherent production in 16O at E_nu = 1 GeV: lepton momentum distribution reduced by 15-30% around the peak due to nuclear medium effects, with a further 15-20% reduction from pion absorption, giving a total reduction of approximately 30-35% in the peak region.
* Incoherent total cross section in 16O at E_nu = 1 GeV: medium modification reduces the cross section by about 10%, and pion absorption gives a further reduction of about 10%.
* Coherent production in 16O at E_nu = 1 GeV: pion momentum distribution reduced by 60-80% around the peak due to medium effects, with a further 15-30% reduction from pion absorption.
* Coherent total cross section in 16O: reduction due to medium modification is approximately 40% at E_nu = 0.7 GeV, 30% at E_nu = 1 GeV, and 20% at E_nu = 1.5 GeV; pion absorption gives a further 25-30% reduction at these energies.
* Coherent angular distribution in 16O at E_nu = 1 GeV: forward-direction reduction of 15-20% from medium effects and an additional 8-10% from pion absorption.
* Vector form factor parameterization: C3^V(q^2) = 2.05 / (1 - q^2/0.54 GeV^2)^2, with C4^V = -(M/M_Delta) C3^V and C5^V = 0.
* Axial form factor parameters: C5^A(0) = 1.2, C4^A(0) = -0.3, C3^A(0) = 0, with axial mass M_A = 1.28 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of weak pion production from nucleons (Adler, 1968; Barish, 1979; Kitagaki, 1986) to nuclear targets relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments at K2K and MiniBooNE, incorporating nuclear medium effects following the methods of Oset (1987) for Delta self-energy in the nuclear medium. The results quantify how Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Delta renormalization, and pion absorption reduce cross sections compared to free-nucleon calculations, which is essential for interpreting atmospheric neutrino data at the Kamioka site where pion production contributes about 20% of quasielastic lepton production. The coherent production calculation is compared with the non-relativistic approach of Kelkar (1997) and the relativistic mean-field approach of Kim (1995), finding agreement that the axial vector current dominates and that neutrino and antineutrino cross sections are nearly equal.
