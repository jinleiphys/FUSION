# Comparison of Experimental Data to the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model For Si+Au Collisions at 14.6 A GeV/c

> Predictions from the RQMD model are systematically compared to recently published charged hadron distributions of AGS Experiment 802 for central Si+Au collisions at 14.6$A$ GeV/$c$, taking into account both the experimental trigger condition and acceptance. The main features of the data, including K$^+$ production, can be understood quantitatively to better than 20\% within the framework of the model, although several discrepancies are found, most importantly for the proton spectra.

## Key claim

The RQMD model reproduces the main features of charged hadron distributions from central Si+Au collisions at 14.6 A GeV/c to better than 20%, but the agreement is conditional on including the experimental trigger condition and acceptance in the model analysis; several discrepancies remain, most notably for proton spectra where the model produces too many protons at midrapidity and underpredicts the inverse slope parameters.

## Method

The authors generated 1537 RQMD (version 1.07) events for Si+Au collisions at 14.6 A GeV/c with impact parameters 0<b<4 fm, then selected 806 events matching the E802 trigger condition (apparent charged multiplicity >173, corresponding to 7% of the total inelastic cross section). They compared the model's invariant cross sections, rapidity distributions, inverse slope parameters, multiplicities, and momentum sums to published E802 data for protons, pions, and kaons, fitting spectra to exponentials within the experimental m_t-m acceptance. A second sample (RQMD2) of 520 events with 0<b<2.5 fm used modified quasi-potentials with delta-delta and nucleon-baryon attractions turned off.

## Key numbers

- Experimental trigger cross section: sigma_trig = 263 mb, corresponding to about 7% of the total inelastic cross section for Si+Au
- RQMD sample: 1537 events with 0<b<4 fm, weighting proportional to b, corresponding to a cross section of 503 mb
- Central sample: 806 events selected by demanding n_ch >= 154 in the ideal experimental acceptance
- Proton multiplicity (p+d) in rapidity 0.4-2.2: E802 = 33.0 +/- 0.2, RQMD = 45.2 +/- 0.2, ratio E802/RQMD = 0.73 +/- 0.01
- Pi+ multiplicity in rapidity 0.6-2.8: E802 = 27.0 +/- 0.3, RQMD = 32.4 +/- 0.2, ratio = 0.83 +/- 0.01
- Pi- multiplicity in rapidity 0.6-2.8: E802 = 29.5 +/- 0.3, RQMD = 37.3 +/- 0.3, ratio = 0.79 +/- 0.01
- K+ multiplicity in rapidity 0.6-2.2: E802 = 4.3 +/- 0.1, RQMD = 3.5 +/- 0.1, ratio = 1.22 +/- 0.05
- K- multiplicity in rapidity 0.7-2.3: E802 = 0.90 +/- 0.06, RQMD = 1.03 +/- 0.04, ratio = 0.87 +/- 0.07
- Pion ratio R(pi) = Delta n(pi-)/Delta n(pi+): E802 = 1.09 +/- 0.02, RQMD = 1.15 +/- 0.01
- Experimental K+/pi+ ratio: 19 +/- 1% for central Si+Au collisions at 14.6 A GeV/c (from earlier E802 data)
- Experimental systematic error for the data: 10-15%

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of RQMD predictions to AGS heavy ion data (Sorge et al. 1992, Mattiello et al. 1989) that focused on global observables like transverse energy and charged particle multiplicities. The present study provides a more systematic comparison by incorporating the experimental trigger condition and acceptance for all measured hadron species including strangeness production. The results enable further refinement of transport models for AGS energies, particularly regarding baryon stopping power, the role of hadron resonance rescattering, and the momentum dependence of nuclear mean fields in dense matter. The authors note that the collective flow component in RQMD is the most important mechanism leading to the observed splitting between proton and pion slope parameters.
