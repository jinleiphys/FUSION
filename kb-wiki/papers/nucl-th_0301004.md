# Centrality Scaling of the $p_T$ Distribution of Pions

> From the preliminary data of PHENIX on the centrality dependence of the $π^0$ spectrum in $p_T$ at midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions, we show that a scaling behavior exists that is independent of the centrality. It is then shown that $<p_T>$ degrades with increasing $N_{\rm part}$ exponentially with a decay constant that can be quantified. A scaling distribution in terms of an intuitive scaling variable is derived that is analogous to the KNO scaling. No theoretical models are used in any part of this phenomenological analysis.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $p_T$ distribution of pions in heavy-ion collisions exhibits a universal scaling behavior that is independent of centrality, when expressed in terms of a scaling variable $z = p_T / K(N)$. This scaling is presented as a phenomenological observation without theoretical assumptions, and the authors explicitly note that the existence of such scaling presents an obstacle to theoretical expectations that deconfinement would produce an anomalous centrality dependence.

## Method
The authors analyzed preliminary PHENIX data on $\pi^0$ production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s = 200$ GeV across 9 centrality bins (0-10% to 80-92%). They defined a scaling variable $z = p_T / K(N)$ where $K(N)$ depends on the number of participants $N_{\rm part}$, and adjusted $K(N)$ and a normalization factor $A(N)$ so that all centrality bins collapse onto a single universal curve $\Phi(z)$. They then derived a second scaling function $\Psi(u)$ in terms of $u = p_T / \langle p_T \rangle$, analogous to KNO scaling for multiplicity distributions.

## Key numbers
- $K(N) = 1.226 - 6.36 \times 10^{-4} N$ (GeV/c), with $K(N_{\rm max}=350) = 1$ GeV/c
- $A(N_c) = 530 N_c^{-0.9}$ for the normalization factor, where $N_c$ is the number of binary collisions
- $\Phi(z) = 1200 (z^2 + 2)^{-4.8} (1 + 25 e^{-4.5 z})$ fits the scaling distribution
- $\lambda = 5.542 \times 10^{-4}$ describes the exponential degradation: $d/dN \ln P_n(N) = -\lambda n$
- $\langle x \rangle_{N_{\rm max}} / \langle x \rangle_2 = 0.825$, quantifying the degree of $p_T$ degradation from peripheral to central collisions
- $\gamma = \langle z \rangle = 0.414$ (the constant relating $z$ and $u$)
- For $N=200$, $dN_{\pi^0}/d\eta = 149$ from the normalization check

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier finding of energy-independent scaling (Hwa and Yang, 2002) to include centrality variations, and is analogous to KNO scaling (Koba, Nielsen, and Olesen, 1972) for multiplicity distributions. It contrasts with the color glass condensate approach of Jalilian-Marian, Schlichting, and others (2002) that motivated scaling of the transverse-mass spectrum. The scaling distribution provides a simple phenomenological target for dynamical models of parton energy loss (such as those by Wang, 1998) and for hydrodynamical descriptions up to $p_T = 3$ GeV/c. The authors note that the absence of scaling violation across centralities challenges theoretical expectations that deconfinement would produce anomalous behavior.
