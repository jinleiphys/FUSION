# Space-time evolution of bulk QCD matter

> We introduce a combined fully three-dimensional macroscopic/microscopic transport approach employing relativistic 3D-hydrodynamics for the early, dense, deconfined stage of the reaction and a microscopic non-equilibrium model for the later hadronic stage where the equilibrium assumptions are not valid anymore. Within this approach we study the dynamics of hot, bulk QCD matter, which is being created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. Our approach is capable of self-consistently calculating the freezeout of the hadronic system, while accounting for the collective flow on the hadronization hypersurface generated by the QGP expansion. In particular, we perform a detailed analysis of the reaction dynamics, hadronic freezeout, and transverse flow.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a hybrid approach combining relativistic 3D hydrodynamics for the early deconfined stage with a microscopic hadronic cascade (UrQMD) for the later hadronic stage, claiming this method can self-consistently calculate freezeout without requiring a fixed freezeout temperature as a free parameter. The hedge is that the approach still requires tuning of initial conditions and a switching temperature between the macroscopic and microscopic descriptions, and the equation of state used is a simple first-order phase transition model rather than a lattice-QCD based one.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic hydrodynamic equations in three dimensions using Lagrangian hydrodynamics in coordinates of proper time and spatial rapidity, with an equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition between a QGP phase (massless u,d,s quarks and gluons with bag constant B=385 MeV/fm^3) and a hadron gas phase with excluded volume correction. At a switching temperature T_SW, the Cooper-Frye formula is used to convert the hydrodynamic fluid into particle distributions that serve as initial conditions for the UrQMD microscopic transport model, which then evolves the hadronic system through binary collisions and resonance decays until freezeout occurs naturally.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential (mu=0) used in the equation of state
- Bag constant B = 385 MeV/fm^3 chosen to yield T_c = 160 MeV
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c for both pure hydro and hybrid calculations
- Maximum initial energy density epsilon_max = 55 GeV/fm^3 for pure hydro, 40 GeV/fm^3 for hydro + UrQMD in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Maximum initial baryon number density n_Bmax = 0.15 fm^{-3} for both calculations
- Maximum entropy density of 176 fm^{-3} in the pure hydro calculation, compared to 110 fm^{-3} in a prior calculation by Kolb and Rapp (2003)
- Weight factor for binary collision scaling w = 0.6 in the initial condition parameterization
- Total nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma = 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Woods-Saxon parameters: a = 0.54 fm, R_A = 6.38 fm, rho_0 = 0.1688 fm^{-3}
- Excluded volume radius for hadrons fixed to 0.7 fm
- Hadronic states included up to masses of approximately 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydro-plus-micro approach pioneered by Bass and Dumitru (2000) and later adopted by Teaney, Lauret, and Shuryak (2001) and Hirano (2005), combining a fully three-dimensional hydrodynamic model with the UrQMD microscopic transport model. The approach enables self-consistent calculation of hadronic freezeout without a fixed freezeout temperature parameter, and allows direct study of how the equation of state and initial conditions affect final observables such as transverse momentum spectra and collective flow at RHIC. The authors note that future work will incorporate a lattice-QCD based equation of state with a crossover transition and the QCD critical point, and that the sensitivity of results to different initial condition choices (such as those from the color glass condensate model) remains to be investigated.
