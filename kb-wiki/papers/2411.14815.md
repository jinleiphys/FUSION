# Gluon Saturation Effects in Exclusive Heavy Vector Meson Photoproduction

> We study exclusive $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ photoproduction for proton and Pb targets in the high-energy limit, with the energy dependence computed using the linear Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov and the nonlinear Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equations. The difference between these two evolution equations can be directly attributed to gluon saturation physics. We find that for proton targets there is no difference between the two approaches at the energies of the currently available data, while for Pb targets in $J/ψ$ production the data shows a clear preference for the evolution with gluon saturation.

## Key claim

Exclusive J/ψ photoproduction on lead targets shows a clear preference for the nonlinear Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution over the linear Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) evolution, which the authors attribute directly to gluon saturation; however, for proton targets there is no difference between the two approaches at the energies of currently available data.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive J/ψ and Υ photoproduction cross sections for proton and lead targets using the dipole picture, factorizing the process into photon wave functions, dipole-target scattering amplitudes, and vector meson wave functions. The energy dependence of the impact-parameter-dependent dipole amplitude is evolved using both the linear BFKL and nonlinear BK evolution equations with the same initial condition, allowing direct attribution of differences to gluon saturation. The vector meson wave functions are modeled using the Boosted Gaussian approach with parameters fixed by normalization and leptonic widths.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass: 3.097 GeV; charm quark mass: 1.3528 GeV; wave function parameters N = 0.5890, R = 1.5070 GeV^{-1}
- Υ mass: 9.460 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.6000 GeV; wave function parameters N = 0.4555, R = 0.77227 GeV^{-1}
- Infrared regulator m_IR = 0.4 GeV used in the evolution kernel
- Lambda_QCD = 0.025 GeV for the standard BK setup; adjusted BFKL uses Lambda_QCD = 0.01 GeV tuned to proton J/ψ data
- Saturation scale in protons estimated to be of order 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in the dipole picture, previously investigated by Bautista (2016), Hentschinski (2020), and Mantysaari (2018), and within the collinear factorization framework by Guzey (2013), Eskola (2022), and Flett (2020). The key advance is the direct comparison of BFKL and BK evolution with impact-parameter dependence, which enables unambiguous attribution of differences to gluon saturation. The results enable future measurements at the LHC and the Electron-Ion Collider to provide quantitative constraints on saturation effects, particularly through combined J/ψ and Υ measurements that probe the onset of saturation at different dipole sizes.
