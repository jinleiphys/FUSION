# From deformed Hartree-Fock to the nucleon-pair approximation

> The nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) can be a compact alternative to full configuration-interaction (FCI) diagonalization in nuclear shell-model spaces, but selecting good pairs is a long-standing problem. While seniority-based pairs work well for near-spherical nuclides, they do not work well for deformed nuclides with strong rotational bands. We propose an alternate approach. We show how one can write any Slater determinant for an even number of particles as a general pair condensate, from which one can project out pairs of good angular momentum. We implement this by generating unconstrained Hartree-Fock states in a shell model basis and extracting $S$, $D$, and $G$ pairs. The subsequent NPA calculations yield good agreement with FCI results using the same effective interactions.

# From deformed Hartree-Fock to the nucleon-pair approximation

## Key claim

The authors show that unconstrained Hartree-Fock states in a shell-model basis can be rewritten as a pair condensate, from which one can project out S, D, and G pairs of good angular momentum for use in the nucleon-pair approximation; the subsequent NPA calculations yield good agreement with full configuration-interaction results for rotational nuclei, but the method is currently demonstrated only for even-even nuclides and the choice of phases in the pair condensate remains arbitrary.

## Method

The authors perform unconstrained Hartree-Fock calculations in a shell-model single-particle basis, generating deformed Slater determinants for even numbers of valence protons or neutrons. They rewrite each Slater determinant as a pair condensate, then use angular momentum projection via rotation operators and Wigner D-matrices to extract collective pairs with good angular momentum (S, D, G, and I pairs). The resulting pairs are used to construct truncated NPA model spaces, which are diagonalized and compared to full configuration-interaction calculations using the same effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{52}$Fe in the $fp$ shell with KB3G interaction: the $SDG$-pair approximation yields $B(E2: 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 12.9$ W.u. compared to FCI value of 16.0 W.u. and experimental value of 14.2(19) W.u.
- For $^{68}$Se with JUN45 interaction: the $SDG$ (I) calculation gives $B(E2: 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 38.7$ W.u. for the ground (oblate) band, compared to FCI value of 35.6 W.u. and experimental value of 27(4) W.u.
- For $^{68}$Ge with JUN45 interaction: the $SDG$ (II) calculation (mixing two HF states) gives $B(E2: 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 25.4$ W.u. for the ground band, compared to FCI value of 28.1 W.u. and experimental value of 15.3(8) W.u.
- For $^{108}$Xe with monopole-optimized effective interactions: the $SDGI$-pair approximation improves agreement with FCI for high-spin states, with $B(E2: 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 28.3$ W.u. compared to FCI value of 30.9 W.u.
- The largest FCI calculation ($^{68}$Se) had an $M$-scheme dimension of 165 million basis states; the largest NPA calculation had a dimension of 7,253 basis states for $J=4$ in $^{68}$Se.
- On an 8-core 4 GHz CPU, the FCI code took about 400 minutes while the NPA code took 4 minutes for $^{68}$Se.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-pair approximation approach, which has been widely applied to near-spherical nuclei using seniority-based pairs (generalized seniority scheme of PhysRevC.79.054315), to deformed rotational nuclei where seniority-based pairs fail. The method connects the spherical shell model with deformed models, and the authors note that the approach suggests a microscopic foundation for the interacting boson model (IBM) of Iachello and Arima (1987). The technique enables NPA calculations for heavy nuclei beyond the reach of full configuration-interaction diagonalization, with potential applications to quadrupole deformation, shape-phase transitions, and octupole collectivity.
