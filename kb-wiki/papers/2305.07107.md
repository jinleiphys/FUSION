# Neutrino oscillations in the interaction picture

> We study the mixing of different kind of fields (scalar in 0+1D, scalar in 3+1D, fermion in 3+1D) treating the mixing term as an interaction. To this aim, we employ the usual perturbative series in the interaction picture. We find that expression for flavor changing probability exhibits corrections with respect to the usual quantum mechanical (e.g. neutrino) oscillation formula, in agreement with the result previously obtained in the non-perturbative flavor Fock space approach.

## Key claim
Treating the neutrino mixing term as a perturbative interaction in the interaction picture yields flavor oscillation probabilities that contain corrections to the standard quantum-mechanical Pontecorvo formula, specifically a high-frequency term depending on the sum of neutrino energies, in agreement with the non-perturbative flavor Fock space approach of Blasone et al. (1995) and Blasone et al. (1999).

## Method
The authors decompose the Lagrangian into a free part diagonal in flavor fields and an interaction part containing only the mixing term, then compute transition amplitudes using the Dyson series for the time evolution operator up to second order. They analyze three systems: a 0+1D quantum-mechanical toy model of two coupled harmonic oscillators, a 3+1D scalar field model, and a 3+1D fermion (neutrino) model, each with two flavors and a mixing angle theta.

## Key numbers
- In the 0+1D toy model, the total transition probability from state A is:  
  P_D^A(Delta t) = (omega_AB^4)/(omega_A omega_B) [ sin^2((omega_A - omega_B)Delta t/2)/(omega_A - omega_B)^2 + 2 sin^2((omega_A + omega_B)Delta t/2)/(omega_A + omega_B)^2 ].
- In the 3+1D scalar model, the oscillation probability (after subtracting a vacuum term) is:  
  P_D^A(p; Delta t) = (m_AB^4)/(omega_{p,A} omega_{p,B}) [ sin^2((omega_{p,A} - omega_{p,B})Delta t/2)/(omega_{p,A} - omega_{p,B})^2 + sin^2((omega_{p,A} + omega_{p,B})Delta t/2)/(omega_{p,A} + omega_{p,B})^2 ].
- For the 0+1D model at short times, P_D^A(Delta t) approx (3 omega_AB^4 Delta t^2)/(4 omega_A omega_B).
- For the 0+1D model, the survival probability of the exact eigenstate |a> is P_S^a(Delta t) = 1 - sin^2(2 theta) sin^2[(omega_1 - omega_2)Delta t/2], which differs from the survival probability of the bare state |A> by a factor 4 omega_A omega_B/(omega_A + omega_B)^2 in the coefficient of the low-frequency term.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative treatment of field mixing previously applied to unstable particles by Facchi and Pascazio (1999) and Giacosa (2010, 2011) to the case of neutrino oscillations. It directly compares with the non-perturbative flavor Fock space approach of Blasone et al. (1995) and Blasone et al. (1999), finding agreement for fermions but noting that the boson case cannot be compared because the flavor charge expectation value is not positive-definite. The results enable a perturbative framework for computing oscillation probabilities in situations where the non-perturbative approach is difficult, such as for bosonic fields.
