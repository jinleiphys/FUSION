# Equation of State and Phase Fluctuations near the Chiral Critical Point

> The thermodynamics and critical exponents and amplitudes of high temperature and dense matter near the chiral critical point are studied. The parameterized equation of state matches on to that calculated with lattice QCD at zero chemical potential and to the known properties of nuclear matter at zero temperature. The extent to which finite size effects wash out the phase separation near the critical point is determined.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a parameterized equation of state for matter near the QCD chiral critical point that incorporates the correct non-mean-field critical exponents (from the 3D Ising model universality class) and matches lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential and nuclear matter properties at zero temperature, but the numerical results depend on the chosen critical temperature \(T_c\), which is not precisely known.

## Method

The Helmholtz free energy density is parameterized as a function of temperature \(T\) and baryon density \(n\) with terms \(f_0(t) + f_1(t)\eta + f_2(t)\eta^2 + f_{\sigma}(t)|\eta|^{\sigma}\), where \(t = (T-T_c)/T_c\) and \(\eta = (n-n_c)/n_c\). The critical exponent \(\delta = \sigma - 1\) is set to 4.815 (3D Ising model value), and the functions \(f_0(t)\) and \(f_2(t)\) are chosen to reproduce the universal amplitude ratios for the heat capacity and isothermal compressibility. Parameters are fixed by matching to a parameterized quark-gluon plasma equation of state at \(\mu = 0\) (motivated by lattice QCD) and to two parameterizations of cold nuclear matter (Cases I and II) at \(T = 0\), using a coexistence curve of elliptical form \( (T/T_0)^2 + (\mu_x/\mu_0)^2 = 1\).

## Key numbers

* The critical exponents used are \(\alpha = 0.11\), \(\beta = 0.325\), \(\gamma = 1.24\), and \(\delta = 4.815\), taken from the 3D Ising model.
* The universal ratio of thermal compressibility amplitudes is \(\kappa_+/\kappa_- \approx 5\), and the ratio of heat capacity amplitudes is \(c_+/c_- \approx 0.5\).
* The nucleon mass is taken as \(m_N = 939\) MeV, the average binding energy per nucleon at nuclear matter density is \(E_0(n_0) = -16.3\) MeV, and nuclear matter density is \(n_0 = 0.153/\mathrm{fm}^3\).
* The compressibility of nuclear matter is fixed at \(K = 250\) MeV.
* The estimated chemical potential at \(T=0\) is \(\mu_0 = 1230 \pm 150\) MeV, and the estimated temperature parameter is \(T_0 = 170 \pm 20\) MeV.
* The ratio \(\mu_0/T_0 = 6.67173\) is derived from requiring the coefficient of \(T^4\) in the pressure to vanish, using \(N_f = 2.5\).
* The critical pressure is computed as \(P_c = 0.749 T_0^4\).
* The constant \(f_{\sigma}\) is taken as \(f_{\sigma} = 5P_c \approx 512\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
* For \(T_c\) between 60 and 140 MeV, the latent heat at \(T=0\) is approximately 300 MeV/fm\(^3\).
* The entropy density at the critical point is 1.741, 3.416, and 5.861 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(T_c = 60\), 100, and 140 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Goodman (year not given) for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition to the chiral phase transition between quark matter and hadronic matter, using the same universality class (3D Ising model). It contrasts with the earlier work of Attract (year not given), which blended a 3D Ising model parameterization into quark and hadron equations of state but had an ambiguity in relating magnetization to baryon chemical potential; the present parameterization avoids that issue. The paper enables quantitative studies of phase separation and fluctuations in heavy ion collisions at facilities such as FAIR and low-energy RHIC, and provides a framework for interpreting experimental signatures like large event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantities.
