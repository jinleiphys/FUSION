# Phi, Omega and Rho production from deconfined matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS

> We investigate the production of the Phi meson and the Omega baryon which interact weakly with hot hadronic matter, and thus their spectra reflect the early stage of the heavy ion collisions. Our analysis shows that the hadronization temperature, T_had, and the transverse flow, v_T, of the initial deconfined phase are strongly correlated: T_had + a * (v_T)**2 = 0.25 GeV, where a=0.37 GeV in the Pb+Pb collision at 158 AGeV/c. When choosing appropriate initial values of T_had and v_T from the temperature region T_had=175 +- 15 MeV, the measured Rho meson spectra was reproduced surprisingly well by the MICOR model. We have found weak influence of final state hadronic interactions on the transverse hadron spectra at m_T - m_i > 0.3 GeV.

# Phi, Omega and Rho production from deconfined matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS

## Key claim

The hadronization temperature and transverse flow of the initial deconfined phase are strongly correlated by the relation T_had + 0.37 GeV * (v_T^0)^2 = 0.25 GeV, but this correlation allows a large temperature region of T_had = 160-230 MeV when paired with appropriate transverse flow values; only within the narrower region T_had = 175 ± 15 MeV and v_T^0 = 0.46 ± 0.05 does the MICOR model also reproduce the measured rho meson slope.

## Method

The authors used the MICroscopical COalescence Rehadronization (MICOR) model, which assumes a thermalized massive quark-antiquark plasma and produces hadrons via quark-antiquark coalescence into prehadrons that then become on-shell resonances. The model calculates transverse momentum spectra for phi mesons (one-step coalescence) and Omega baryons (two-step coalescence via ss diquark formation) from a deconfined phase characterized by hadronization temperature T_had and initial transverse flow v_T^0. The theoretical slopes were fitted in the measured transverse momentum regions 0.5 < m_T - m_phi < 2.2 GeV for phi and 0.3 < m_T - m_Omega < 1.5 GeV for Omega, then compared to experimental data from the NA50 and WA97 Collaborations via chi-squared analysis.

## Key numbers

- Correlation relation: T_had + 0.37 GeV * (v_T^0)^2 = 0.25 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV/c
- Best-fit parameter region: T_had = 175 ± 15 MeV and v_T^0 = 0.46 ± 0.05
- Experimental inverse slopes used for comparison: T(phi) = 222 ± 6 MeV (NA50), T(rho/omega) = 219 ± 5 MeV (NA50), T(Omega) = 238 ± 17 MeV (WA97)
- Constituent quark masses used in the model: m_Q = 300 MeV, m_S = 450 MeV
- Fitted slopes for other particles using parametrization dN/m_T dm_T proportional to m_T^(1/2) exp(-m_T/T) in region m_T - m_i > 0.3 GeV: T(pi+) = 150 MeV, T(pi-) = 145 MeV, T(K+) = 225 MeV, T(K-) = 216 MeV, T(p+) = 234 MeV, T(anti-p-) = 223 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phi and Omega production from deconfined matter, following experimental results from the NA50 Collaboration (1999) on phi and rho/omega spectra and the WA97 Collaboration (1998) on Omega spectra. The authors note that earlier analyses by Csorgo and Lorstad (1996) and the NA44 Collaboration found a linear dependence of inverse slopes on hadron masses for pions, kaons, protons, phi, Lambda and deuterons, but the Xi and Omega slopes did not follow this trend. The MICOR model results suggest that final state hadronic interactions have weak influence on transverse hadron spectra at m_T - m_i > 0.3 GeV, contradicting earlier expectations that the strongly interacting rho meson would have its spectra modified by final state interactions. This work enables further investigation into whether all hadronic slopes can be reproduced by quark coalescence, which would support the interpretation of deconfined phase formation at CERN SPS energies.
