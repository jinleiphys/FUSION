# Fluidity and supercriticality of the QCD matter created in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In this paper we discuss the fluidity of the hot and dense QCD matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions in comparison with various other fluids, and in particular suggest its possible supercriticality. After examining the proper way to compare non-relativistic and relativistic fluids from both thermodynamic and hydrodynamic perspectives, we propose a new fluidity measure which shows certain universality for a remarkable diversity of critical fluids. We then demonstrate that a fluid in its supercritical regime has its fluidity considerably enhanced. This may suggest a possible relationship between the seemingly good fluidity of the QCD matter produced in heavy ion collisions at center of mass energy of $\sqrt{s}=200 \rm AGeV$ and the supercriticality of this matter with respect to the Critical-End-Point on the QCD phase diagram. Based on such observation, we predict an even better fluidity of the matter to be created in heavy ion collisions at LHC energy and the loss of good fluidity at certain lower beam energy. Finally based on our criteria, we analyze the suitability of a hydrodynamic description for the fireball evolution in heavy ion collisions at various energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the good fluidity of QCD matter created at RHIC's top energy may be due to the matter being in a supercritical regime with respect to the QCD Critical-End-Point, rather than being a near-perfect fluid near a phase transition; the authors hedge that this is a "possible" relationship and that the fluidity measure shows "certain universality" across diverse critical fluids.

## Method

The authors define a new dimensionless fluidity measure F = L_eta / L_n, where L_eta = eta / (rho * c_s) for non-relativistic fluids or eta / ((w/c^2) * c_s) for relativistic fluids (the viscous damping length for sound waves), and L_n = 1 / n^(1/3) is the interparticle distance. They compute this measure for fifteen different substances using data from the NIST WebBook, and then apply it to QCD matter using a parametrization of shear viscosity from Hirano and Gyulassy (2005) combined with lattice QCD results for enthalpy density and speed of sound from Karsch et al (2008).

## Key numbers

* The minimum fluidity measure for water at critical pressure P_c = 22 MPa is F_min(P_c) approximately 0.33, while at P = 1000 MPa (about 45 P_c) the minimum drops to F_min(45 P_c) approximately 0.11, a factor of 3 improvement.
* For the eleven non-organic substances examined at critical pressure, the minimum fluidity measure falls in a narrow band F in (0.3, 0.45) despite spanning two orders of magnitude in molar mass.
* The upper limit on eta/s for QCD matter at RHIC from viscous hydrodynamic calculations is eta/s less than or equal to 6/(4 pi), while the AdS/CFT lower bound is eta/s greater than or equal to 1/(4 pi).

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of QCD fluidity to common substances by Csernai et al. (2006) and Lacey et al. (2006), but differs by carefully distinguishing relativistic from non-relativistic fluids and by introducing a fluidity measure that accounts for the speed of sound and interparticle distance rather than using eta/s alone. The paper enables predictions for the beam energy scan at RHIC and for LHC energies, suggesting that fluidity will improve at LHC energies and degrade at lower beam energies, with implications for the search for the QCD Critical-End-Point.
