# Structure of charmed baryons studied by pionic decays

> We investigate the decays of the charmed baryons aiming at the systematic understanding of hadron internal structures based on the quark model by paying attention to heavy quark symmetry. We evaluate the decay widths from the one pion emission for the known excited states, Λ_c^*(2595), Λ_c^*(2625), Λ_c^*(2765), Λ_c^*(2880) and Λ_c^*(2940), as well as for the ground states Σ_c(2455) and Σ_c^*(2520). The decay properties of the lower excited charmed baryons are well explained, and several important predictions for higher excited baryons are given. We find that the axial-vector type coupling of the pion to the light quarks is essential, which is expected from chiral symmetry, to reproduce the decay widths especially of the low lying Λ_c^* baryons. We emphasize the importance of the branching ratios of Γ(Σ_c^*π)/Γ(Σ_cπ) for the study of the nature of higher excited Λ_c^* baryons.

## Key claim

The authors show that a non-relativistic constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator confinement, combined with an axial-vector (derivative) pion-quark coupling required by chiral symmetry, can reproduce the observed pionic decay widths of low-lying excited charmed baryons, but the spin-parity assignments of higher excited states like Λc*(2880) remain ambiguous because many configurations are incompatible with the measured branching ratio when p-wave contributions are properly included.

## Method

The authors construct charmed baryon wave functions in a quark model using Jacobi coordinates (ρ-mode between the two light quarks, λ-mode between their center of mass and the charm quark) with harmonic oscillator potentials, classifying states by brown muck spin j (heavy quark symmetry). They compute one-pion emission decay widths using a non-relativistic reduction of the πqq axial-vector coupling, evaluating matrix elements for transitions from excited Λc* states to Σcπ and Σc*π final channels, and from Σc and Σc* ground states to Λcπ.

## Key numbers

- Λc*(2595)+ total experimental width: 2.6 ± 0.6 MeV; calculated width for (nλ,ℓλ)=(0,1), (nρ,ℓρ)=(0,0) with JΛ(j)P=1/2(1)- is 1.5–2.9 MeV (full), with Σc++π- partial width 0.13–0.25 MeV and Σc0π+ partial width 0.15–0.28 MeV
- Λc*(2625)+ experimental width: < 0.97 MeV; calculated width for JΛ(j)P=3/2(1)- is 0.024–0.039 MeV (Σc++π- channel), while for JΛ(j)P=1/2(1)- it is 5.4–10.7 MeV
- Λc*(2765)+ experimental width: 50 MeV; calculated total [Σc(*)π] width for (0,1),(0,0) with JΛ(j)P=1/2(1)- is 65.1–146.3 MeV, and for 3/2(1)- is 52.2–104.2 MeV; for (0,0),(0,1) with 1/2(1)- it is 325.8–676.3 MeV
- Λc*(2880)+ experimental width: 5.8 ± 1.1 MeV with branching ratio R = Γ(Σc*π)/Γ(Σcπ) = 0.225; calculated R for (0,2),(0,0) with JΛ(j)P=5/2(2)+ is 8.1–8.4, and for (0,0),(0,2) with 5/2(2)+ is 18.7–18.9; only the (0,1),(0,1) configuration with 5/2(2)2+ gives R = 27.5–30.1
- Λc*(2940)+ experimental width: 17+8-6 MeV; calculated total [Σc(*)π] width for (0,1),(0,0) with 1/2(1)- is 144.8–313.8 MeV, and for 3/2(1)- is 182.2–332.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends quark model studies of charmed baryon strong decays, notably the calculations by Zhong (2007) which used LS coupling, by instead employing jj coupling (brown muck spin) to make heavy quark symmetry relations transparent. The authors find that the axial-vector πqq coupling (from chiral symmetry) is essential to reproduce the low-lying Λc* decays, contrasting with simpler pseudoscalar coupling. The analysis of Λc*(2880) challenges the spin-5/2+ assignment inferred from heavy quark symmetry by Abe (2006), showing that most 5/2+ configurations give branching ratios far from the measured value of 0.225 when p-wave contributions are included. The results enable future experimental discrimination of spin-parity assignments for Λc*(2765), Λc*(2880), and Λc*(2940) through precise measurement of the Σc*π to Σcπ branching ratio.
