# Baryon Magnetic Moments in the 1/N_c Expansion with Flavor Symmetry Breaking

> The magnetic moments and transition magnetic moments of the ground state baryons are analyzed in an expansion in 1/N_c, SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking and isospin symmetry breaking. There is clear evidence in the experimental data for the hierarchy of magnetic moments of the combined expansion in 1/N_c and flavor breaking. SU(3) breaking in the magnetic moments is expected to be enhanced relative to that of other hadronic observables, and significant SU(3) breaking is found.

# Baryon Magnetic Moments in the 1/N_c Expansion with Flavor Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim

The combined expansion in 1/N_c and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking is necessary to understand the hierarchy of baryon magnetic moments found in nature, with SU(3) breaking enhanced relative to other hadronic observables (expected at the ~60% level from chiral loop corrections proportional to m_q^{1/2}) and significant SU(3) breaking confirmed by experimental data.

## Method

The author constructs a complete operator basis of 27 independent 1/N_c operators transforming under definite SU(3) representations (singlet, octet, decuplet+antidecuplet, 27-plet, and 64-plet) to parametrize all 27 ground-state baryon magnetic moments (octet, decuplet, and decuplet-octet transition moments). The analysis includes SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking to all orders and isospin symmetry breaking, with operator coefficients classified by their order in 1/N_c and their functional dependence on light quark masses from chiral perturbation theory. Fits to experimental data at successive orders in the combined expansion are performed to extract the leading coefficients and predict unmeasured magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The four 1/N_c coefficients in the SU(3) symmetry limit (with N_c=3) are extracted as: a_{(1)}^{1,8} = 5.88 ± 0.09, b_{(2)}^{1,8} = -0.459 ± 0.000, b_{(3)}^{1,8} = 0.63 ± 0.36, c_{(3)}^{1,8} = 3.87 ± 0.45 (in units of nuclear magnetons)
- Experimental accuracies of nine SU(3) relations are given, including: p = Σ^+ at 12.8 ± 0.4%, Σ^- = Ξ^- at 56.3 ± 2.9%, n = 2Λ at 43.8 ± 0.5%, n = (2/√3)ΛΣ^0 at 2.8 ± 4.8%, (1/2)Δ^{++} = -Ω^- at 41.4 ± 10.5%
- The SU(6) relation p = -Ω^- holds at 32.1 ± 2.1%
- The SU(6) relation p = -(3/2)n holds at 2.7 ± 0.0%
- The SU(6) relation (1/2)Δ^{++} - 3n = 3p holds at 5.0 ± 0.1%

## Context

This work extends the 1/N_c expansion analysis of baryon magnetic moments previously performed by Dashen, Jenkins, Manohar (various years) and others, and is analogous to the combined 1/N_c and SU(3) breaking analysis of baryon masses by Jenkins and Lebed (1995). The analysis is motivated by new experimental measurements of two additional decuplet-octet transition magnetic moments from the CLAS collaboration (2009-2010). The SU(3) chiral perturbation theory predictions of Caldi and Pagels (1974), Gasser (1982), Krause (1990), and others are used to interpret the pattern of SU(3) breaking, which is expected to be dominated by nonanalytic chiral loop corrections of order m_q^{1/2}. The work enables predictions for the many unmeasured baryon magnetic moments and provides a framework for interpreting future experimental measurements.
