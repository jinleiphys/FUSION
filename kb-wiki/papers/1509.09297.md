# Low Energy Effective Theory of QCD at High Isospin Chemical Potential

> The goal of this paper is to arrive at a low energy effective theory of QCD with two massless flavors of quarks at very high isospin density and zero baryon density. In a seminal paper by Son and Stephanov in the year 2001, it was conjectured that the low energy dynamics of QCD with two light flavors at asymptotically high isospin density was described by that of a pure Yang-Mills effective Lagrangian. Since the existence of a first order deconfinement phase transition with increasing temperature is a feature of every pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory with N greater than or equal to 3, the regime considered in this paper is also expected to exhibit a first order deconfinement phase transition with increasing temperature. However, the low energy constants(LEC) of this pure Yang-Mills theory have not been calculated till date. We calculate the LEC s for this effective theory which in turn enables us to calculate the critical temperature of the deconfinement transition as a function of the isospin chemical potential $μ_I$.The scaling of the critical temperature as a function of the isospin chemical potential that we find in this paper can be compared with future lattice calculations at finite isospin density.

## Key claim

The paper calculates for the first time the low-energy constants (chromodielectric constant and chromomagnetic permeability) of the effective pure Yang-Mills theory that describes QCD with two massless flavors at asymptotically high isospin chemical potential and zero baryon density, enabling a quantitative prediction of the critical temperature for the first-order deconfinement phase transition as a function of the isospin chemical potential. The authors note that this scaling can be compared with future lattice calculations, but emphasize that the calculation is valid only in the asymptotically high density regime where perturbation theory is justified.

## Method

The authors start from the QCD Lagrangian with two massless quarks at finite isospin chemical potential and zero baryon chemical potential, introduce an auxiliary field to describe the pion-condensate (Cooper pairing of anti-up and down quarks), and compute the one-loop gluon polarization tensor by integrating out quark quasiparticles near the Fermi surface up to energies of order the gap. They match the resulting quadratic effective action for gluons to a pure Yang-Mills form with modified chromodielectric constant and chromomagnetic permeability, then use renormalization group running to determine the effective confinement scale of this emergent Yang-Mills theory.

## Key numbers

- The chromodielectric constant is given by \(\epsilon = 1 + \frac{g^2 \mu_I^2}{18\pi^2 (\Delta^{+})^2}\), where \(\Delta^{+}\) is the superconducting gap and \(g\) is the QCD coupling at scale \(\mu_I\).
- The chromomagnetic permeability is \(\lambda = 1\).
- The speed of gluons in the effective theory is \(c = 1/\sqrt{\epsilon} \approx 3\sqrt{2}\pi\Delta/(g\mu_I)\) for \(\mu_I \gg \Delta\).
- The gap is estimated as \(\Delta^{+} = b |\mu_I| g(\mu_I)^{-5} \exp(-3\pi^2/(2g(\mu_I)))\) with \(b = 10^4\).
- The effective confinement scale is \(\tilde{\lambda} = \Delta \exp\left(-\frac{2\pi}{b_0 \alpha_s'(\Delta)}\right)\) where \(b_0 = 29/3\) for two-flavor QCD.

## Context

This work extends the seminal conjecture by Son and Stephanov (2001) that QCD at asymptotically high isospin density becomes equivalent to a pure Yang-Mills theory, by providing the first quantitative calculation of the low-energy constants of that effective theory. The authors note that the calculation is analogous to the treatment of unbroken SU(2) gluon polarization in the two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase at high baryon density studied by Rischke (2000), and they use similar integration techniques. The results enable future lattice calculations at finite isospin density (which do not suffer from the sign problem) to test the predictions, and also allow computation of the equation of state in this regime by relating it to pure Yang-Mills theory, as further elaborated in Cohen and Sen (2015).
