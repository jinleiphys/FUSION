# $\mathcal{F}t$ values of the mirror $β$ transitions and the weak magnetism induced current in allowed nuclear $β$ decay

> In recent years a number of correlation measurements in nuclear $β$ decay have been performed reaching a precision of the order of 1\% and below and it is expected that even higher precision will be reached in the near future. At these levels of precision higher-order corrections due to e.g. recoil terms induced by the strong interaction and radiative corrections cannot necessarily be neglected anymore when interpreting these results in terms of new physics or extracting a value for the $V_{ud}$ quark-mixing matrix element. We provide here an update of the $\mathcal{F} t$ values of the $T=1/2$ mirror $β$ decays as well as an overview of current experimental and theoretical knowledge of the most important recoil term, weak magnetism, for both the $T=1/2$ mirror $β$ transitions and a large set of $β$ decays in higher isospin multiplets. The matrix elements determining weak magnetism were calculated in the nuclear shell model and cross-checked against experimental data, showing overall good agreement. Additionally, we show that further insight can be obtained from properly deformed nuclear potentials, in particular for mirror $T=1/2$ decays.. The results provide new insights in the size of weak magnetism, extending the available information to $β$ transitions of nuclei with masses up to $A =$ 75. This provides important guidance for the planning and interpretation of ongoing and new precise correlation measurements in nuclear $β$ decay searching for new physics or to extract the $V_{ud}$ quark-mixing matrix element in mirror $β$ decays. This more detailed knowledge of weak magnetism can also be of interest for further theoretical work related to the reactor neutrino problem.

## Key claim
The paper provides an updated analysis of corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ values for $T=1/2$ mirror $\beta$ transitions up to mass $A=75$ and a comprehensive evaluation of weak magnetism form factors, showing that the weak magnetism term exhibits a strong nuclear structure and mass dependence that cannot be approximated by a simple universal value, particularly relevant for interpreting precision correlation measurements and the reactor antineutrino anomaly.

## Method
The authors compiled and statistically analyzed experimental half-lives, branching ratios, and $Q_{EC}$ values from literature up to April 2021 for mirror $\beta$ transitions, applying Particle Data Group statistical procedures with scale factors for weighted averages. Weak magnetism form factors were extracted using the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis, combining corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ values with nuclear magnetic moments for mirror transitions, and for Gamow-Teller transitions in higher isospin multiplets ($T=1, 3/2, 2$) by relating weak magnetism to analog $E1$ gamma transition widths. Shell model calculations were performed and cross-checked against experimental data for both the mirror and higher-multiplet transitions.

## Key numbers
- Corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ value for superallowed $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ pure Fermi transitions: $3072.24(185)$ s (from Hardy 2020)
- Tritium half-life adopted value: $4497 \pm 4$ days (from Ma06)
- $^{11}$C half-life adopted value: $20.3401 \pm 0.0053$ minutes (scale factor $S=1.3$)
- $^{13}$N half-life adopted value: $9.9647 \pm 0.0039$ minutes (scale factor $S=1$)
- Weak magnetism information extended to $\beta$ transitions of nuclei with masses up to $A=75$
- Ratio of experimentally detected versus theoretically predicted reactor antineutrinos: $R=0.943 \pm 0.023$ (from Mention 2011)
- Current value for $V_{ud}$ from mirror $\beta$ transitions: $|V_{ud}| = 0.9739(10)$ (from Hayen 2021)

## Context
This work updates the previous $\mathcal{F}t$ value compilation for mirror $\beta$ transitions by Severijns (2008), which covered masses up to $A=45$, now extending to $A=75$ with new half-life and branching ratio data. The weak magnetism analysis extends earlier work by Calaprice (1976) and Azuelos (1977) for $T=1/2$ mirror transitions up to $^{43}$Ti, and by Calaprice (1976) and Huber (2011) for $T=1$ to $T=0$ decays up to $A=32$. The results enable improved interpretation of ongoing precision correlation measurements searching for new physics or extracting $V_{ud}$, and provide guidance for theoretical work on the reactor neutrino anomaly, where weak magnetism contributions have previously been treated with a simplified universal approximation.
