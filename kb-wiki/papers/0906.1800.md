# Electromagnetic Currents and Magnetic Moments in $χ$EFT

> A two-nucleon potential and consistent electromagnetic currents are derived in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) at, respectively, $Q^{2}$ (or N$^2$LO) and $e Q$ (or N$^3$LO), where $Q$ generically denotes the low-momentum scale and $e$ is the electric charge. Dimensional regularization is used to renormalize the pion-loop corrections. A simple expression is derived for the magnetic dipole ($M1$) operator associated with pion loops, consisting of two terms, one of which is determined, uniquely, by the isospin-dependent part of the two-pion-exchange potential. This decomposition is also carried out for the $M1$ operator arising from contact currents, in which the unique term is determined by the contact potential. Finally, the low-energy constants (LEC's) entering the N$^2$LO potential are fixed by fits to the $np$ S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 100 MeV lab energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives a consistent two-nucleon potential at N²LO and electromagnetic currents at N³LO in chiral effective field theory, but the magnetic dipole operator from loop corrections can be separated into a center-of-mass dependent term uniquely determined by current conservation from the isospin-dependent part of the two-pion-exchange potential, plus a translationally-invariant term that differs in isospin structure from prior heavy-baryon formalism results by Park et al. (1996).

## Method

The NN potential up to N²LO was constructed using dimensional regularization to renormalize pion-loop corrections, with nine low-energy constants (C_S, C_T, and C_1 through C_7) fixed by fitting np S- and P-wave phase shifts up to 100 MeV lab energies from the partial-wave analysis of Gross and Stadler (2008). Electromagnetic currents up to N³LO were derived in time-ordered perturbation theory using non-relativistic Hamiltonians from the chiral Lagrangian, including minimal substitution in contact interactions, non-minimal couplings, and one-loop corrections, with renormalization performed via dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The N²LO potential is constructed at order Q² (N²LO) and currents at order e Q (N³LO), where Q denotes the low-momentum scale and e the electric charge
- The LO contact potential contains two LEC's, C_S and C_T, while the N²LO contact potential contains seven independent operator structures with coefficients C_1 through C_7
- The two-pion-exchange potential renormalization involves the loop function G(k) = sqrt(4 m_π² + k²)/k * ln[(sqrt(4 m_π² + k²) + k)/(sqrt(4 m_π² + k²) - k)]
- The isoscalar and isovector anomalous magnetic moments used are κ_S = -0.12 μ_N and κ_V = 3.706 μ_N
- Phase shifts are fitted up to 100 MeV lab energies using the Gross and Stadler (2008) partial-wave analysis

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Pastore et al. (2008) by constructing consistent NN potentials and electromagnetic currents up to N³LO, addressing the unsatisfactory description of experimental data found in earlier N²LO hybrid calculations, particularly for the suppressed ²H(n,γ)³H process which showed pronounced sensitivity to the cutoff parameter. The approach differs from the heavy-baryon formalism of Park et al. (1996) in the treatment of reducible diagrams, leading to differences in the isospin structure of the translationally-invariant M1 operator. The derived currents enable consistent χEFT calculations of electromagnetic properties and radiative captures in A=2-4 nuclei, with thermal neutron n-d and n-³He captures and keV p-d captures being especially interesting since their M1 transitions are inhibited at the one-body level and significantly enhanced by many-body current components.
