# Anomalous decays of eta' and eta into four pions

> We calculate the branching ratios of the yet unmeasured eta' decays into four pions, based on a combination of chiral perturbation theory and vector-meson dominance. The decays eta' --> 2(pi+ pi-) and eta' --> pi+ pi- 2pi0 are P-wave dominated and can largely be thought to proceed via two rho resonances; we predict branching fractions of (1.0+-0.3)*10^-4 and (2.4+-0.7)*10^-4, respectively, not much lower than the current experimental upper limits. The decays eta' --> 4pi0 and eta --> 4pi0, in contrast, are D-wave driven as long as conservation of CP symmetry is assumed, and are significantly further suppressed; any experimental evidence for the decay eta --> 4pi0 could almost certainly be interpreted as a signal of CP violation. We also calculate the CP-violating amplitudes for eta' --> 4pi0 and eta --> 4pi0 induced by the QCD theta-term.

## Key claim

The decays eta' to 2(pi+ pi-) and eta' to pi+ pi- 2pi0 are P-wave dominated and proceed largely via two rho resonances, with predicted branching fractions not much lower than current experimental upper limits; the decays eta' to 4pi0 and eta to 4pi0 are D-wave driven under CP conservation and significantly further suppressed, such that any experimental evidence for eta to 4pi0 could almost certainly be interpreted as a signal of CP violation.

## Method

The authors combine chiral perturbation theory at Order(p^6) with vector-meson dominance via hidden local symmetry to calculate the anomalous five-pseudoscalar amplitudes for eta' and eta decays into four pions. For the charged-pion final states, they use the full vector-meson-exchange amplitudes from the HLS formalism with rho-meson propagators including the finite width, neglecting the negligible kaon-loop contributions. For the neutral-pion final states, they construct a D-wave decay mechanism via a pion-loop diagram with pi+ pi- intermediate states and rescattering to pi0 pi0, approximated by f2(1270) exchange in the s channel.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction for eta' to 2(pi+ pi-): (1.0 +/- 0.3) x 10^-4
- Branching fraction for eta' to pi+ pi- 2pi0: (2.4 +/- 0.7) x 10^-4
- Current experimental upper limit for eta' to 2(pi+ pi-): less than 2.4 x 10^-4
- Current experimental upper limit for eta' to pi+ pi- 2pi0: less than 2.6 x 10^-3
- Eta mass minus 4 times neutral pion mass: 7.9 MeV, while eta mass minus 2 times (charged pion mass plus neutral pion mass): -1.2 MeV
- Pion decay constant F_pi = 92.2 MeV
- Standard eta-eta' mixing angle theta_P = arcsin(-1/3) approximately -19.5 degrees
- Rho meson mass M_rho = 775.5 MeV, width Gamma_rho = 149.1 MeV
- Total width of eta' used for normalization: Gamma_eta' = (0.199 +/- 0.009) MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of anomalous decays involving five pseudoscalars, specifically the pentagon anomaly, which had previously been studied only in a quark model by Parashar whose predictions were ruled out by experimental upper limits. The decays eta' to 4pi are not isospin forbidden nor required to proceed via electromagnetic interactions, unlike many other eta' decay channels. The results enable experimental searches at facilities such as BES-III, WASA-at-COSY, ELSA, CB-at-MAMI-C, and CLAS at Jefferson Lab, and provide a baseline for identifying CP-violating signals in eta to 4pi0 decays induced by the QCD theta-term.
