# Quantum states of indefinite spins: From baryons to massive gravitino

> I review theory and phenomenology of (K/2,K/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] states. First I make the case that the observed nucleon and Delta (1232) excitations (up to Delta(1600)) are exhausted by unconstrained (K/2,K/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] states with K=1,3, and 5, which originate from rotational and vibrational excitations of an underlying quark--diquark configuration. Second, I consider the simplest case of K=1 and show that the γ^μψ_μ=0 constraint of the Rarita-Schwinger framework is a short-hand of: - 1/3 (1/m^2 W^2 +3/4)ψ_μ= ψ_μ, the covariant definition of the unique invariant subspace of the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector, W^2, that is a parity singlet and of highest spin-3/2 at rest. I suggest to work in the 16 dimensional vector spinor space Ψ= A *ψrather than keeping Lorentz and spinor indices separated and show that the above second order equation guarantees the covariant description of a has-been spin-3/2 states at rest without invoking further supplementary conditions. In gauging the latter equation minimally and, in calculating the determinant, one obtains a pathology-free energy-momentum dispersion relation, thus avoiding the classical Velo-Zwanziger problem of imaginary energies in the presence of an external electromagnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper argues that observed nucleon and Delta (1232) excitations up to Delta(1600) are exhausted by unconstrained (K/2,K/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] states with K=1, 3, and 5, and that the Rarita-Schwinger constraint gamma^mu psi_mu = 0 is a shorthand for a second-order equation that can describe spin-3/2 states without supplementary conditions, thereby avoiding the Velo-Zwanziger problem of imaginary energies in external electromagnetic fields.

## Method

The author analyzes baryon spectra by classifying observed nucleon and Delta resonances into multiplets of the type (K/2,K/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] corresponding to rotational and vibrational excitations of a quark-diquark system modeled after the rovibron model from molecular physics. For the K=1 case, the author works in a 16-dimensional vector spinor space Psi = A * psi (direct product of four-vector and Dirac spinor) and derives a second-order equation using the squared Pauli-Lubanski vector W^2 to define the parity-singlet spin-3/2 subspace, then gauges this equation minimally to obtain the energy-momentum dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

* Observed nucleon and Delta excitations up to Delta(1600) are exhausted by states with K=1, 3, and 5
* The first group of states contains two spin-1/2 states of opposite parities and a parity singlet spin-3/2^- state
* The second group has three parity degenerate states with spins from 1/2^pm to 5/2^pm and a single spin-7/2^+ state
* The third group has five parity degenerate states with spins from 1/2^pm to 9/2^pm and a single spin-11/2^+ state
* The Delta(1600) resonance is identified as the only state that drops out of the systematics, likely an independent hybrid state
* The parity singlet subspace for K=1 is defined by the projector -1/3(1/m^2 W^2 + 3/4)psi_mu = psi_mu

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Kirchbach, Ref. [6]) of baryon spectra clustering into Lorentz group representations, and contrasts with the standard Rarita-Schwinger framework (Rarita and Schwinger, 1941) which requires supplementary conditions and suffers from the Velo-Zwanziger problem (Velo and Zwanziger, 1969) of complex energies in electromagnetic fields. The paper also builds on the rovibron model from molecular physics (Iachello, 1981) applied to the quark-diquark system, and references earlier work on parity doublets by Dashen, Donau and Reinhardt, Iachello, and Robson. The approach enables a pathology-free description of massive spin-3/2 states such as the gravitino, and suggests that baryon resonances should be understood as states of indefinite (fuzzy) spin rather than definite SU(2) spin.
