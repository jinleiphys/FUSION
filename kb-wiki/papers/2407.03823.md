# Role of clustered nuclear geometry in particle production through p-C and p-O collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Long-range multi-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions have shown conclusive evidence of the hydrodynamic behavior of strongly interacting matter and are associated with the final-state azimuthal momentum anisotropy. In small collision systems, azimuthal anisotropy can be influenced by the hadronization mechanism and residual jet-like correlations. Thus, one of the motives of the planned p--O and O--O collisions at the LHC and RHIC is to understand the origin of small system collectivity. As the anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n$) are sensitive to the initial-state effects including nuclear shape, deformation, and charge density profiles, studies involving $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O nuclei are transpiring due to the presence of exotic $α$ ($^{4}$He) clusters in such nuclei. In this study, for the first time, we investigate the effects of nuclear $α$--clusters on the azimuthal anisotropy of the final-state hadrons in p--C and p--O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}= 9.9$~TeV within a multi-phase transport model framework. We report the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) and pseudorapidity ($η$) spectra, participant eccentricity ($ε_2$) and triangularity ($ε_3$), and estimate the elliptic flow ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) of the final-state hadrons using the two-particle cumulant method. These results are compared with a model-independent Sum of Gaussians (SOG) type nuclear density profile for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O nuclei.

## Key claim
For the first time, this study investigates how the exotic α-cluster geometry of ¹²C and ¹⁶O nuclei affects azimuthal anisotropy in p-C and p-O collisions at √sNN = 9.9 TeV, using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT). The abstract implies a direct comparison with a Sum-of-Gaussians density profile, but the actual claim is conditional: the effects of α-clustering are demonstrated through differences in transverse momentum spectra, pseudorapidity distributions, participant eccentricity/triangularity, and elliptic/triangular flow, with the magnitude of these differences depending on collision centrality and the specific nuclear density profile.

## Method
The authors implemented two nuclear density profiles for ¹²C and ¹⁶O nuclei within the AMPT framework: an α-clustered geometry (equilateral triangle for ¹²C, regular tetrahedron for ¹⁶O) and a model-independent Sum of Gaussians (SOG) profile. They simulated p-C and p-O collisions at √sNN = 9.9 TeV, using the two-particle Q-cumulant method with a pseudorapidity gap to suppress non-flow correlations, and extracted transverse momentum (pT) and pseudorapidity (η) spectra, participant eccentricity (ε₂) and triangularity (ε₃), and elliptic flow (v₂) and triangular flow (v₃) as functions of collision centrality.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √sNN = 9.9 TeV for both p-C and p-O systems.
- For ¹⁶O α-clustered geometry: tetrahedron side length 3.42 fm, giving rms radius 2.699 fm; α-particle parameters: r₀ = 0.964 fm, w = 0.517, a = 0.322 fm, with α-particle rms radius 1.676 fm.
- For ¹²C α-clustered geometry: equilateral triangle side length 3.10 fm, giving rms radius 2.47 fm.
- Minimum separation distance between nucleons: 0.4 fm.
- Rapidity shift for both p-O and p-C collisions: Δy = 0.346 units in the direction of the proton beam.
- For central (0-5)% p-O collisions with SOG: ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 6.64 ± 2.12, ⟨Npart⟩ = 7.64 ± 2.12; with α-cluster: ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 5.65 ± 1.64, ⟨Npart⟩ = 6.65 ± 1.64.
- For central (0-5)% p-C collisions with SOG: ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 5.49 ± 1.84, ⟨Npart⟩ = 6.49 ± 1.84; with α-cluster: ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 6.05 ± 1.89, ⟨Npart⟩ = 7.05 ± 1.89.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of α-cluster effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions, such as Bozek (2014) who found enhanced triangular flow in C-Au collisions with α-clustered ¹²C, and Behera (2023) who reported high triangular flow in central O-O collisions with α-clustered ¹⁶O. The novel contribution is applying these clustered geometries to asymmetric p-C and p-O systems, which fills the multiplicity gap between pp and p-Pb collisions and can help interpret the origin of collectivity in small systems, as well as inform cosmic-ray interaction models (protons with atmospheric ¹⁴N and ¹⁶O). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming LHC and RHIC data from p-O and O-O runs.
