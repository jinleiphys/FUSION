# The time evolution of light nuclei cumulants and ratios with a first-order phase transition in the UrQMD transport model

> The UrQMD model with a density dependent equation of state, including a first-order phase transition, is used to study the time dependence of baryon number and proton number susceptibilities up to third order in heavy ion reactions of $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=2-3 A$ GeV. A significant deviation from the Gaussian fluctuations of the baryon number fluctuation in coordinate space is observed. The proton number fluctuations are always suppressed as they constitute only a small fraction of the total baryon number during the dense phase of the collision. It is found that the only measurable, but small, signal would be an enhancement of the third order (or higher) proton cumulant in a finite rapidity window $Δy$ that is larger than one unit of rapidity. In addition, it is found that the coordinate fluctuations will lead to an enhancement of cluster production due to the correlations in coordinate space. However, this enhancement is small and mainly occurs during the dense part of the collision before the system actually freezes out.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model with a first-order phase transition produces enhanced baryon number fluctuations in coordinate space, but these fluctuations are not translated into measurable signals in momentum space except for a small enhancement of the third-order (or higher) proton cumulant in a rapidity window larger than one unit of rapidity; the enhancement in light nuclei ratios is only visible at intermediate times and disappears by freeze-out.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model with a density-dependent equation of state that includes a first-order phase transition between 2.5 and 4 times nuclear saturation density, comparing it to a crossover equation of state based on the chiral mean field model. They simulated central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 2 and 3 A GeV (64000 and 80000 events respectively, with impact parameter b < 4.7 fm) and calculated the time evolution of second and third order cumulants of baryon and proton number distributions in both coordinate space (a spherical volume of radius 2 fm) and momentum space (rapidity windows). Light nuclei production was modeled using phase-space coalescence with refitted coalescence parameters for deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and helium-4.

## Key numbers

* Beam energies studied: E_lab = 2 and 3 A GeV for central Au+Au collisions with impact parameter b < 4.7 fm
* The phase transition equation of state has an unstable phase between 2.5 and 4 times nuclear saturation density n_0
* Coalescence parameters: maximum relative distance for deuterons is 4.0 fm, for tritons and He-3 is 3.5 fm, for He-4 is 3.5 fm; maximum relative momentum for deuterons is 0.3 GeV, for tritons and He-3 is 0.45 GeV, for He-4 is 0.55 GeV
* The light nuclei ratio (N_He4 x N_p)/(N_He3 x N_d) shows a roughly 10% larger signal during intermediate times for the phase transition scenario compared to crossover
* The phase transition is reached for central Au+Au collisions at beam energies above E_lab > 1.5 A GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Savchuk (2022) and Kuznietsov (2023) on baryon number fluctuations in coordinate space with a first-order phase transition, and is the first time the third order cumulant is calculated within a realistic microscopic simulation with such a transition. The study builds on the suggestion by Sun (2020) that light nuclear cluster production could reveal coordinate space correlations, and compares results to STAR experiment measurements at E_lab = 3 A GeV (STAR, 2023). The findings indicate that while coordinate space fluctuations are enhanced, the signal in final momentum space observables is weak, suggesting that experimental detection of a first-order phase transition through these observables at low beam energies will be challenging.
