# Impact of DCSB and dynamical diquark correlations on proton GPDs

> We calculate the leading-twist, helicity-independent generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton, at finite skewness, in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of quantum chomodynamics (QCD). The NJL model reproduces low-energy characteristics of QCD, including dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB). The proton bound-state amplitude is solved for using the Faddeev equation in a quark-diquark approximation, including both dynamical scalar and axial vector diquarks. GPDs are calculated using a dressed non-local correlator, consistent with DCSB, which is obtained by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation. The model and approximations used observe Lorentz covariance, and as a consequence the GPDs obey polynomiality sum rules. Extractions of electromagnetic and gravitational form factors are performed. We find a D-term of $-1.08$ when the non-local correlator is properly dressed, and $0.85$ when the bare correlator is used instead, suggesting that within this framework proton stability requires the constituent quarks to be dressed consistently with DCSB. We also find that the anomalous gravitomagnetic vanishes, as required by Poincaré symmetry.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation in a quark-diquark approximation for the proton bound-state amplitude, including both dynamical scalar and axial vector diquarks. GPDs are calculated using a dressed non-local correlator obtained by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the NJL model employing proper time regularization to simulate confinement. The body GPDs of the proton are combined with the GPDs of dressed constituent quarks and diquarks using a convolution formula, and the model results are evolved to higher scales using leading-order evolution kernels.

## Key numbers

- D-term of -1.08 when the non-local correlator is properly dressed, and 0.85 when the bare correlator is used instead
- Model renormalization scale of mu^2 = 0.16 GeV^2 (equal to M^2, the dressed quark mass M ~ 400 MeV)
- Average momentum fraction at forward limit: <x_u(0)> = 0.34 and <x_d(0)> = 0.32
- Average momentum fraction at t = -2 GeV^2: <x_u(-2 GeV^2)> = 0.45 and <x_d(-2 GeV^2)> = 0.50
- Anomalous gravitomagnetic moment B(0) = 0, as required by Poincare symmetry

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of proton electromagnetic form factors by Cloet et al. (2014) to the calculation of gravitational form factors and GPDs at finite skewness. The fully covariant calculation allows self-consistent results in the ERBL region where |x| < |xi|, which is problematic for truncated light-front basis approaches. The results enable future studies of how DCSB and diquark correlations manifest in hard exclusive reactions such as deeply virtual Compton scattering, and provide a framework for understanding the mass and spin decompositions of the proton.
