# Estimating the nuclear saturation parameter via low-mass neutron star asteroseismology

> We examine the fundamental ($f$-) and the 1st pressure ($p_1$-) mode frequencies in gravitational waves from cold neutron stars constructed with various unified realistic equations of state. With the calculated frequencies, we derive the empirical formulae for the $f$- and $p_1$-mode frequencies, $f_f$ and $f_{p_1}$, as a function of the square root of the stellar average density and the parameter ($η$), which is a combination of the nuclear saturation parameters. With our empirical formulae, we show that by simultaneously observing the $f$- and $p_1$-mode gravitational waves, when $1.5\lesssim f_{p_1}/f_f\lesssim 2.5$ (which corresponds to neutron star models with the mass of $\lesssim 0.9M_\odot$), one could estimate the value of $η$ within $\sim 10\%$ accuracy, which makes a strong constraint on the EOS for neutron star matter. In addition, we find that the maximum $f$-mode frequency is strongly associated with the minimum radius of neutron star. That is, if one would observe a larger frequency of the $f$-mode, one might constrain the upper limit of the minimum neutron star radius.

# Estimating the nuclear saturation parameter via low-mass neutron star asteroseismology

## Key claim

By simultaneously observing the fundamental (f-) and first pressure (p1-) mode gravitational waves from neutron stars with masses less than approximately 0.9 solar masses, the nuclear saturation parameter η can be estimated within about 10% accuracy, but only when the ratio of the p1-mode to f-mode frequencies lies between 1.5 and 2.5; the method is workable only if neutron stars with masses below about 0.9 solar masses exist, which is significantly lower than the observed minimum neutron star mass of 1.174 solar masses for PSR J0453+1559.

## Method

The author constructed cold, non-rotating neutron star models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using eight unified realistic equations of state (EOSs) that are consistently constructed for both core and crust regions. The f- and p1-mode gravitational wave frequencies were calculated via linear perturbation analysis in the relativistic Cowling approximation, where metric perturbations are neglected during fluid oscillations. Empirical formulae for the f- and p1-mode frequencies were derived as functions of the square root of the normalized stellar average density (x) and the parameter η, which is a combination of nuclear saturation parameters defined as η = (K0 L^2)^(1/3).

## Key numbers

- The empirical formula for the f-mode frequency is f_f (kHz) = 1.6970x + f_0(η), where x = (M/1.4M_sun)^(1/2) (R/10 km)^(-3/2); this formula is applicable within 20% accuracy even for neutron star models with maximum mass.
- The empirical formula for the p1-mode frequency in the region between the second and third avoided crossing is f_p1 - f_f,AC (kHz) = 11.5877(x - x_AC)^2 + 8.8014(x - x_AC) + 0.087307, where f_f,AC and x_AC are functions of η.
- The fitting formulae for the avoided crossing values are f_f,AC (kHz) = 0.7705 η_100^(-1) + 0.2912 + 0.2642 η_100 and x_AC = 0.1376 η_100^(-1) + 0.01600 + 0.05860 η_100, where η_100 = η/100 MeV.
- The maximum f-mode frequency is related to the minimum neutron star radius by f_f,max (kHz) = 3.7739 (R_min/10 km)^2 - 10.6289 (R_min/10 km) + 9.7550.
- The fiducial range of η constrained by terrestrial experiments is 70.5 ≤ η ≤ 114.8 MeV, based on K0 = 230 ± 40 MeV and L = 58.9 ± 16 MeV.
- The EOS parameters for the eight unified EOSs range from η = 68.2 MeV (FPS) to η = 151 MeV (Shen), with maximum neutron star masses from 1.80 solar masses (FPS) to 2.41 solar masses (DD2).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Sotani 2020b), which derived an empirical formula for the f-mode frequency using phenomenological EOSs but failed to estimate lower values of η. The present study overcomes that defect by using unified realistic EOSs and additionally derives a new empirical formula for the p1-mode frequency. The approach builds on earlier work showing that the f-mode frequency is a linear function of the square root of stellar average density almost independently of EOS (Andersson and Kokkotas 1996, 1998). The method enables future gravitational wave observations to constrain nuclear saturation parameters, complementing terrestrial constraints on K0 and L. The correlation between maximum f-mode frequency and minimum neutron star radius provides a way to exclude stiff EOSs if large f-mode frequencies are observed.
