# Imaging Shapes of Atomic Nuclei in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Atomic nuclei are self-organized, many-body quantum systems bound by strong nuclear forces within femtometer-scale space. These complex systems manifest a variety of shapes, traditionally explored using non-invasive spectroscopic techniques at low energies. However, at these energies, their instantaneous shapes are obscured by long-timescale quantum fluctuations, making direct observation challenging. Here we introduce the ``collective flow assisted nuclear shape imaging'' method, which images the nuclear global shape by colliding them at ultrarelativistic speeds and analyzing the collective response of outgoing debris. This technique captures a collision-specific snapshot of the spatial matter distribution within the nuclei, which, through the hydrodynamic expansion, imprints patterns on the particle momentum distribution observed in detectors. We benchmark this method in collisions of ground state Uranium-238 nuclei, known for their elongated, axial-symmetric shape. Our findings show a large deformation with a slight deviation from axial symmetry in the nuclear ground state, aligning broadly with previous low-energy experiments. This approach offers a new method for imaging nuclear shapes, enhances our understanding of the initial conditions in high-energy collisions and addresses the important issue of nuclear structure evolution across energy scales.

# Imaging Shapes of Atomic Nuclei in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The paper introduces "collective flow assisted nuclear shape imaging" as a method to capture a collision-specific snapshot of nuclear spatial matter distribution by colliding nuclei at ultrarelativistic speeds and analyzing the collective response of outgoing debris. The authors benchmark this method in collisions of ground state Uranium-238 nuclei and find a large deformation with a slight deviation from axial symmetry, aligning broadly with previous low-energy experiments, though they note that high-energy collisions also probe nuclear structure at sub-nucleonic levels such as quark and gluon correlations, meaning deformations observed at high energy may differ from those at low energy.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration analyzed ultra-central (near zero impact parameter) collisions of Uranium-238 nuclei at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV and Gold-197 nuclei at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using the STAR detector at RHIC. They measured three observables, the mean squared elliptic flow (v_2^2), the variance of mean transverse momentum ((delta pT)^2), and their correlation (v_2^2 delta pT), and compared ratios between U+U and Au+Au collisions to state-of-the-art IP-Glasma+MUSIC hydrodynamic model calculations. The nuclear shape parameters beta_2 (quadrupole deformation magnitude) and gamma (triaxiality parameter) were extracted by fitting the model predictions to the data, with the key insight that the three-body observable v_2^2 delta pT scales as beta_2^3 cos(3 gamma), enabling simultaneous determination of both parameters.

## Key numbers
- Extracted beta_2 for Uranium-238: 0.297 ± 0.015 (from combined analysis of R_(delta pT)^2 and R_(v_2^2 delta pT) using IP-Glasma+MUSIC model)
- Extracted gamma for Uranium-238: 8.5° ± 4.8° (from combined analysis using IP-Glasma+MUSIC model)
- Combined constraints from two hydrodynamic models yield beta_2 = 0.286 ± 0.025 and gamma = 8.7° ± 4.5°
- Low-energy estimate of beta_2 for Uranium-238: 0.287 ± 0.007 (from Pritychenko 2013)
- Low-energy estimate of gamma for Uranium-238: 6°–8° (from rigid-rotor assumption, PhysRevC.54.2356)
- R_(v_2^2) and R_((delta pT)^2) increase by up to 60% in central collisions
- R_(v_2^2 delta pT) decreases by up to threefold across centralities
- Exposure time of the collision: tau_expo = 2R_0/Gamma less than 0.1 fm/c
- Rotational fluctuation timescale for well-deformed nuclei: tau_rot ~ 10^3–10^4 fm/c
- Lorentz contraction factor Gamma = (1/2) sqrt(s_NN)/m_0 greater than 100

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical proposals that elliptic flow v_2 could be enhanced by nuclear deformation (Li 1999, Shuryak 1999, Filip 2009, Shou 2014, Goldschmidt 2015) and previous experimental studies at RHIC (Adamczyk 2015) and LHC (Acharya 2018, Sirunyan 2019, Aad 2019) that indicated beta_2's influence on v_2 in Uranium and Xenon collisions. The method enables the first extraction of nuclear ground state triaxiality without involving transitions to excited states, contrasting with traditional spectroscopic techniques (Cline 1986, Yang 2022) that capture orientation-averaged shapes due to long-timescale quantum fluctuations. This approach can be applied to study odd-mass nuclei, octupole and hexadecapole deformations, dynamic deformations in soft nuclei, and could reduce nuclear matrix element uncertainties for neutrinoless double beta decay experiments (Engel 2016).
