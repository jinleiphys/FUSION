# Hydrodynamic description of the baryon-charged quark-gluon plasma

> One of the primary goals of nuclear physics is studying the phase diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics, where a hypothetical critical point serves as a landmark. A systematic model-data comparison of heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energies between 1 and 100 GeV per nucleon is essential for locating the critical point and the phase boundary between the deconfined quark-gluon plasma and the confined hadron resonance gas. At these energies the net baryon density of the system can be high and critical fluctuations can become essential in the presence of the critical point. Simulating their dynamical evolution thus becomes an indispensable part of theoretical modeling. In this thesis we first present the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic code BEShydro, which solves the equations of motion of second-order Denicol-Niemi-Molnar-Rischke theory, including bulk and shear viscous components as well as baryon diffusion current. We then study the effects caused by the baryon diffusion on the longitudinal dynamics and on the phase diagram trajectories of fluid cells at different space-time rapidities of the system, and how they are affected by critical dynamics near the critical point. We finally explore the evolution of non-hydrodynamic slow processes describing long wavelength critical fluctuations near the critical point, by extending the conventional hydrodynamic description by coupling it to additional explicitly evolving slow modes, and their back-reaction to the bulk matter properties.

# Hydrodynamic description of the baryon-charged quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic code BEShydro, based on second-order Denicol-Niemi-Molnar-Rischke theory with bulk and shear viscosity and baryon diffusion, can be extended to include the dynamical evolution of non-hydrodynamic slow modes describing long-wavelength critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point, but the static correlation length parametrization used as input employs an infrared cutoff of 10 fm that the system is unlikely to reach in practice due to finite-size and finite-time effects.

## Method
The author parametrizes the equilibrium correlation length as a function of temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ using a hyperbolic tangent interpolation between a non-critical correlation length (1 fm) and a maximum cutoff (10 fm), with critical exponents from the 3-dimensional Ising universality class (ν=2/3, β=1/3, δ=5). The critical region size is estimated by requiring the singular part of the susceptibility to be comparable to its regular part, using a linear mapping from Ising variables to QCD coordinates with parameters Δμ=92 MeV and ΔT=18 MeV. The slow mode equations are solved numerically using the Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm with second-order Runge-Kutta time integration, validated against semi-analytical solutions for ideal Gubser flow.

## Key numbers
- Non-critical correlation length ξ₀ = 1 fm; maximum correlation length ξ_max = 10 fm
- Critical point location: μ_c = 250 MeV, T_c = 149 MeV, with crossover angle α₁ = 4.6°
- Critical region extent: Δμ = 92 MeV, ΔT = 18 MeV
- Transition temperature at μ=0: T₀ = 155 MeV; curvature κ₂ = 0.0149
- Critical exponents: ν = 2/3, β = 1/3, δ = 5; γ = β(δ-1) = 4/3
- Mapping parameters: Δα = 3°, ρ = 1/2, w = 1/2, a = 1
- Beam energy used for simulations: √s_NN = 19.6 GeV
- Robust estimate for largest correlation length achievable in heavy-ion collisions: about 3 fm
- Validation performed with Q = 0.5 fm and Q = 1.2 fm for slow modes, with τ₀ = 1 fm, q = 1 fm, C = 1.2

## Context
This work extends the BEShydro code (Du, 2019) to incorporate critical dynamics via the hydro+ framework, building on the mapping from the 3-dimensional Ising model to QCD coordinates developed by Parotto (2018), Pradeep (2019), and Mroczek (2020). The parametrization of the crossover line follows Bellwied (2015). The analysis is motivated by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and earlier theoretical studies by Monnai (2016) and Denicol (2018). The causality analysis shows that near the critical point the relaxation time τ_n grows as ξ², which is faster than the diffusion coefficient D_p scaling as ξ⁻¹, ensuring causal propagation. The code is open source and available for further phenomenological studies of critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies between 1 and 100 GeV per nucleon.
