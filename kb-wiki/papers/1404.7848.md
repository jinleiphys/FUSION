# Probing nuclear rates with Planck and BICEP2

> Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) relates key cosmological parameters to the primordial abundance of light elements. In this paper, we point out that the recent observations of Cosmic Microwave Background anisotropies by the Planck satellite and by the BICEP2 experiment constrain these parameters with such a high level of accuracy that the primordial deuterium abundance can be inferred with remarkable precision. For a given cosmological model, one can obtain independent information on nuclear processes in the energy range relevant for BBN, which determine the eventual ^2H/H yield. In particular, assuming the standard cosmological model, we show that a combined analysis of Planck data and of recent deuterium abundance measurements in metal-poor damped Lyman-alpha systems provides independent information on the cross section of the radiative capture reaction d(p,γ)^3He converting deuterium into helium. Interestingly, the result is higher than the values suggested by a fit of present experimental data in the BBN energy range (10 - 300 keV), whereas it is in better agreement with ab initio theoretical calculations, based on models for the nuclear electromagnetic current derived from realistic interactions. Due to the correlation between the rate of the above nuclear process and the effective number of neutrinos Neff, the same analysis points out a Neff>3 as well. We show how this observation changes when assuming a non-minimal cosmological scenario. We conclude that further data on the d(p,γ)^3He cross section in the few hundred keV range, that can be collected by experiments like LUNA, may either confirm the low value of this rate, or rather give some hint in favour of next-to-minimal cosmological scenarios.

## Key claim
Assuming the standard cosmological model, a combined analysis of Planck CMB data and recent deuterium abundance measurements in metal-poor damped Lyman-alpha systems provides independent information on the cross section of the radiative capture reaction d(p,gamma)3He, finding a value higher than the fit of present experimental data in the BBN energy range (10-300 keV) but in better agreement with ab initio theoretical calculations.

## Method
The authors used the public BBN code PArthENoPE to compute primordial deuterium abundances, parameterizing the uncertainty in the d(p,gamma)3He reaction rate through a constant rescaling factor A2 applied to the experimental best-fit thermal rate. They performed Monte Carlo Markov Chain analyses using CosmoMC and Monte Python codes, combining Planck temperature data with WMAP9 polarization, BICEP2 B-mode polarization data, and the deuterium abundance likelihood from Cooke et al. (2013) for various cosmological models including minimal LambdaCDM and extensions with free Neff, spatial curvature, lensing amplitude, and tensor modes.

## Key numbers
- Planck constraint on baryon density: Omega_b h^2 = 0.02207 +/- 0.00027 (68% C.L.)
- Primordial deuterium fraction predicted from Planck assuming LambdaCDM: 2H/H = (2.65 +/- 0.07) x 10^-5 (68% C.L.)
- Astrophysical deuterium measurement from Cooke et al. (2013): 2H/H = (2.53 +/- 0.04) x 10^-5 (68% C.L.)
- For minimal LambdaCDM with Planck+WP+BBN: A2 = 1.155 +/- 0.082 (68% C.L.), indicating a rate 15.5% above the experimental best fit
- For minimal LambdaCDM with Planck+WP+BBN+BAO: A2 = 1.138 +/- 0.076 (68% C.L.)
- For LambdaCDM + free Neff with Planck+WP+BBN: Neff = 3.56 +/- 0.40 and A2 = 1.29 +/- 0.15 (68% C.L.)
- For LambdaCDM + free Neff with Planck+WP+BBN+HST: Neff = 3.76 +/- 0.27 and A2 = 1.33 +/- 0.14 (68% C.L.)
- For Planck+WP+BICEP2+BBN with free tensor modes: r_0.05 = 0.134 +/- 0.045 and A2 = 1.145 +/- 0.081 (68% C.L.)
- For Planck+WP+BICEP2+BBN with free running spectral index: dn_s/dlnk = -0.0256 +/- 0.0097 and A2 = 1.080 +/- 0.079 (68% C.L.)
- Propagated uncertainty on deuterium from d(p,gamma)3He cross section uncertainty: sigma = 0.062 x 10^-5
- The theoretical calculation of Marcucci et al. (2005) corresponds to A2 = 1.055

## Context
This work extends the interplay between CMB cosmology and nuclear physics, following the approach of using Planck data to constrain BBN parameters as in Planck Collaboration (2013). The analysis builds on the deuterium abundance measurements of Cooke et al. (2013) and the experimental review of the d(p,gamma)3He cross section by Adelberger et al. (2010). The results support the ab initio nuclear theory calculations of Viviani et al. (1999) and Marcucci et al. (2005), and show that future measurements of this cross section by experiments like LUNA could discriminate between minimal and next-to-minimal cosmological scenarios, particularly regarding the effective number of neutrinos Neff.
