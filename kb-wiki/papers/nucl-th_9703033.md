# Linear Extrapolation of Ultrarelativistic Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering to Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We use a Glauber-like approach to describe very energetic nucleus-nucleus collisions as a sequence of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions. No free parameters are needed: all the information comes from simple parametrizations of nucleon-nucleon collision data. Produced mesons are assumed not to interact with each other or with the original baryons. Comparisons are made to published experimental measurements of baryon rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, negative hadron rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, average multiplicities of pions, kaons, hyperons, and antihyperons, and zero degree energy distributions for sulfur-sulfur collisions at 200 GeV/c per nucleon and for lead-lead collisions at 158 GeV/c per nucleon. Good agreement is found except that the number of strange particles produced, especially antihyperons, is too small compared with experiment. We call this model LEXUS: it is a baseline linear extrapolation of ultrarelativistic nucleon-nucleon scattering to heavy ion collisions.

# Linear Extrapolation of Ultrarelativistic Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering to Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
LEXUS (Linear EXtrapolation of Ultrarelativistic Scattering) is a parameter-free Glauber-like model that reproduces most bulk observables in S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, but systematically underpredicts strange particle production, predicting only about 80% of charged kaons, 50% of neutral kaons and lambdas, and 10% of antilambdas observed in central S+S collisions, which may indicate where new physics beyond linear extrapolation lies.

## Method
The model treats nucleus-nucleus collisions as an ensemble of independent binary nucleon-nucleon collisions along straight-line trajectories, using a Markovian evolution kernel parametrized from proton-proton scattering data with a hard-scattering fraction λ = 0.6. Produced mesons are assumed not to interact with each other or with baryons. The nuclear geometry is handled via row-on-row collisions using binomial distributions for nucleon numbers per row, with Woods-Saxon (lead) and three-parameter Gaussian (sulfur) density distributions, and comparisons to data are made by applying sharp impact parameter cuts to match experimental centrality selections.

## Key numbers
- λ = 0.6: fraction of nucleon-nucleon scatterings treated as hard collisions, determined from p+p → p+X data in the momentum range 12 to 400 GeV/c
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section fixed at 4 fm², appropriate for beam energies from tens to hundreds of GeV
- ⟨p_T²⟩_NN = 0.282 (GeV/c)² for baryons in elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions, corresponding to T₁ = 113 MeV in a thermal fit
- ⟨p_T²⟩_π = 0.155 (GeV/c)² for pions in elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions, corresponding to a pion temperature of 133 MeV
- ⟨p_T²⟩_K = 0.290 (GeV/c)² for kaons in elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions
- LEXUS predicts about 80% of observed charged kaons, 50% of observed neutral kaons and lambdas, and 10% of observed antilambdas in central S+S collisions at 200 GeV/c per nucleon
- Speed of sound parameter c₀² = 0.32 used for the Landau-model width of negative hadron rapidity distributions

## Context
This work extends the Glauber (1950s) and rows-on-rows (Hüfner and Knoll, 1970s) approaches to ultrarelativistic energies, providing a baseline linear extrapolation model distinct from cascade models such as ARC, RQMD, VENUS, FRITIOF, PYTHIA, and QGSM. The model enables identification of where new physics (e.g., quark-gluon plasma formation) may be required by quantifying the shortfall in strange particle production, particularly antihyperons, compared to SPS data from NA35 and NA44. The authors note that LEXUS is not applied to AGS energies (10–14.6 GeV per nucleon) as those are too low for the straightline trajectory assumption.
