# Polarization Phenomena by Deuteron Fragmentation into Pions

> The fragmentation of deuterons into pions emitted forward in the kinematic region forbidden for free nucleon-nucleon collisions is analyzed. The inclusive relativistic invariant spectrum of pions and the tensor analyzing power T_{20} are investigated within the framework of an impulse approximation using different kinds of the deuteron wave function. The influence of P-wave inclusion in the deuteron wave function is studied, too. The invariant spectrum is shown to be more sensitive to the amplitude of the $NN \to πX$ process than the tensor analyzing power T_{20}. It is shown that the inclusion of the non-nucleon degrees of freedom in a deuteron results a satisfactory description of experimental data about the inclusive pion spectrum and improves the description of data about T_{20}. According to the experimental data, T_{20} has the positive sign and very small values, less than 0.2, what contradicts to the theoretical calculations ignoring these degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the inclusion of non-nucleon degrees of freedom (such as six-quark states or ΔΔ components) in the deuteron wave function is necessary to describe the experimental data on the tensor analyzing power T₂₀ for deuteron fragmentation into pions, because calculations using only nucleon degrees of freedom fail to reproduce the observed positive sign and small values (less than 0.2) of T₂₀ at cumulative numbers x_C ≥ 1.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic invariant analysis within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism using an impulse approximation for the reaction D + p → π(0°) + X. They calculated the inclusive relativistic invariant pion spectrum and the tensor analyzing power T₂₀ using different deuteron wave functions, including non-relativistic Paris and Reid wave functions, relativistic Bethe-Salpeter and Gross wave functions with P-wave components, and wave functions modified to include non-nucleon degrees of freedom with a mixing parameter α₂₍₃q₎.

## Key numbers

- The tensor analyzing power T₂₀ has positive sign and values less than 0.2 according to experimental data from Afanasyev et al. (1998)
- The probability of the non-nucleon component α₂₍₃q₎ used in the calculations is 0.2 to 0.4
- The optimal description of T₂₀ data is achieved with the mixing parameter a = 2.3
- The non-relativistic Paris and Reid deuteron wave functions reproduce experimental data for internal momenta k up to 0.25 GeV/c
- The internal momentum threshold for non-nucleon degrees of freedom is 0.20 GeV/c, corresponding to inter-nuclear distances less than 1 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cumulative hadron production in deuteron fragmentation, particularly the reaction D p → p X studied by Lykasov (1993) and the experimental measurements of T₂₀ in D p → π X by Afanasyev et al. (1998). The authors show that while the inclusive pion spectrum can be described by including non-nucleon components following the approach of Efremov (1988) and Lykasov (1993), the tensor analyzing power T₂₀ for pion production cannot be described by any purely nucleonic deuteron wave function over the entire cumulative region x_C ≥ 1, unlike the proton fragmentation case where nucleon-only descriptions fail only at x_C ≥ 1.7. This enables new tests of deuteron structure models at short distances by comparing calculations of T₂₀ for different final hadrons.
