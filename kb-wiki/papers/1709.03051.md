# Mass-15 nuclei and predicting narrow states beyond the proton drip line

> In a previous letter (Phys. Rev. Lett. 96, 072502 (2006)), the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) technique was used to calculate spectral properties for proton-unstable $^{15}$F and its mirror, $^{15}$C. MCAS achieved a close match to the then-new data for $p+^{14}$O elastic scattering and predicted several unusually narrow resonances at higher energies. Subsequently, such narrow resonance states were found. New cross section data has been published characterising the shape of the $J^π=\frac{1}{2}^-$ resonance. Herein we update that first MCAS analysis and its predictions. We also study the spectra of the set of mass-15 isobars, ${}^{15}$C, ${}^{15}$N, ${}^{15}$O, and ${}^{15}$F, using the MCAS method and seeking a consistent Hamiltonian for clusterisation with a neutron and a proton, separately, coupled to core nuclei ${}^{14}$C and ${}^{14}$O.

# Mass-15 nuclei and predicting narrow states beyond the proton drip line

## Key claim

The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) technique, updated with five core states rather than three and using exact masses, can describe the low-energy spectra of all four mass-15 isobars (¹⁵C, ¹⁵N, ¹⁵O, ¹⁵F) with a single consistent Hamiltonian, while predicting additional narrow resonances in ¹⁵F beyond the three currently established states, though small adjustments to orthogonalizing pseudopotential (OPP) values are required to match the ¹⁵F spectrum due to imperfect mirror symmetry between the ¹⁴C and ¹⁴O cores.

## Method

The MCAS method solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space using finite-rank expansions of nucleon-nucleus interaction matrices, with Sturmian functions as the expansion basis and orthogonalizing pseudopotentials to enforce the Pauli principle. The nucleon-nucleus interactions were specified using a vibrational collective model with quadrupole and octupole deformations to second order, coupling the added nucleon to five states of the core nuclei ¹⁴C and ¹⁴O (ground 0⁺, 1⁻, 0₂⁺, 3⁻, and 2⁺ states), with Coulomb interactions derived from a three-parameter Fermi charge distribution for ¹⁴O.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of ¹⁵C lies 1.217 MeV below the n+¹⁴C threshold (MCAS), compared to the experimental value of 1.218 MeV
- Ground state resonance of ¹⁵F lies at 1.279 MeV in the p+¹⁴O center of mass (MCAS), compared to the experimental centroid of 1.270 MeV with width 0.376±0.070 MeV
- ¹⁵F first excited state (5/2⁺) centroid at 2.651 MeV with width 0.336 MeV (MCAS), compared to experimental centroid 2.794 MeV with width 0.30±0.010 MeV
- ¹⁵F 1/2⁻ resonance centroid at 4.755 MeV with width 0.106 MeV (MCAS), compared to experimental centroid 4.757 MeV with width 0.036±0.014 MeV
- ¹⁵F predicted narrow 5/2⁻ state at 6.717 MeV with width 0.074 MeV, and 7/2⁻ state at 7.027 MeV with width 0.176 MeV
- ¹⁵O ground state lies 13.22 MeV below the neutron emission threshold (MCAS)
- ¹⁵N ground state lies 10.207 MeV below the proton emission threshold
- Neutron emission threshold from ¹⁴C is 8.176 MeV; proton emission threshold from ¹⁴O is 4.628 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ = -0.36 and octupole deformation parameter β₃ = -0.48 used for both cores
- 3pF charge distribution parameters for ¹⁴O: Rc = 2.59 fm, ac = 0.6 fm, wc = -0.051

## Context

This work extends the original MCAS analysis of ¹⁵F and ¹⁵C from Fraser et al. (2006, Physical Review Letters 96, 072502), which predicted unusually narrow resonances later confirmed experimentally by Mukha et al. (2008, 2009, 2010). The updated calculation uses five core states instead of three, exact masses, and a 3pF charge distribution, responding to new cross-section data from Grigorenko et al. (2016) that characterized the shape of the 1/2⁻ resonance. The study also extends the MCAS approach to the bound mirror pair ¹⁵O and ¹⁵N, comparing with shell model calculations by Meyer et al. (2015) using the COSMO code and Fortune (2016). The results enable predictions of additional narrow states in ¹⁵F above 6 MeV excitation, relevant to understanding two-proton decay of ¹⁶Ne and the phenomenon of Pauli hindrance producing narrow resonances in proton-rich nuclei.
