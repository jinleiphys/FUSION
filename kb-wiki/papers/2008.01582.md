# Towards mitigation of apparent tension between nuclear physics and astrophysical observations by improved modeling of neutron star matter

> Observations of neutron stars (NSs) by the LIGO-Virgo and NICER collaborations have provided reasonably precise measurements of their various macroscopic properties. In this paper, we employ a Bayesian framework to combine them and place improved joint constraints on the properties of NS equation of state (EoS). We use a hybrid EoS formulation that employs a parabolic expansion-based nuclear empirical parameterization around the nuclear saturation density augmented by a generic 3-segment piecewise polytrope model at higher densities. Within the $90 \%$ credible level this parameterization predicts $R_{1.4} = 12.57_{-0.92}^{+0.73}$ km and $Λ_{1.4} = 550_{-225}^{+223}$ for the radius and dimensionless tidal deformability, respectively, of a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ NS. Finally, we show how the construction of the full NS EoS based solely on the nuclear empirical parameters at saturation density leads to certain tension with the astrophysical data, and how the hybrid approach provides a resolution to it.

# Towards mitigation of apparent tension between nuclear physics and astrophysical observations by improved modeling of neutron star matter

## Key claim

The apparent tension between nuclear empirical parameter-based modeling of neutron stars and astrophysical observations arises from deficiencies in EoS modeling at high densities, specifically from applying a Taylor expansion around saturation density to densities where the expansion parameter exceeds unity; a hybrid approach combining nuclear empirical parameters at low densities with a three-segment piecewise polytrope at high densities resolves this tension.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state using a parabolic expansion of the binding energy per nucleon around nuclear saturation density (with parameters K0, e_sym, L, K_sym) for low densities, augmented by a three-segment piecewise polytrope (with adiabatic indices Gamma1, Gamma2, Gamma3) at higher densities, with the first transition fixed at 1.25 n0 based on Bayesian evidence calculation. They perform Bayesian inference using nested sampling to combine data from the PSR J0740+6620 mass measurement, the gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425, and NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451, marginalizing over mass ratio and using Gaussian kernel density estimators for the likelihood.

## Key numbers

- R_1.4 = 12.57_{-0.92}^{+0.73} km (radius of a 1.4 solar mass NS, 90% credible level, hybrid model with all data combined)
- Lambda_1.4 = 550_{-225}^{+223} (dimensionless tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass NS, 90% credible level, hybrid model with all data combined)
- L = 61.2_{-25.2}^{+29.6} MeV (slope of symmetry energy, 90% credible level, hybrid model with all data combined)
- K_sym = -181_{-182}^{+204} MeV (curvature of symmetry energy, 90% credible level, hybrid model with all data combined)
- M_max = 2.23_{-0.18}^{+0.21} solar masses (maximum NS mass, 90% credible level, hybrid model with all data combined)
- R_1.4 uncertainty shrinks from ~2.19 km (PSR J0740+6620 + GW only) to ~1.65 km (adding NICER)
- Transition density between low-density and high-density models fixed at 1.25 n0 based on Bayesian evidence

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian EoS inference approaches such as those by Landry (2020) and Raaijmakers (2019) by introducing a hybrid parameterization that incorporates nuclear physics information at low densities while remaining agnostic at high densities. It directly addresses the apparent tension reported by Guven (2020) between empirical parameter-based modeling and astrophysical observations, showing this tension is an artifact of applying the Taylor expansion beyond its regime of validity. The approach differs from Steiner (2010) by using a three-segment polytrope instead of two segments, and from Zimmerman (2020) by directly sampling the posterior space of EoS parameters rather than using theoretically derived correlations. The results enable more reliable joint constraints on nuclear empirical parameters from multi-messenger observations, particularly for L and K_sym.
