# Quantum spin dynamics of heavy quarks and polarization observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a quantum spin-density-matrix framework for heavy-quark spin dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Starting from an initial polarization induced along the magnetic-field direction, we derive the evolution equation for spin polarization within this framework and obtain analytic solutions. The evolved polarization is connected to open heavy-flavor observables via a fragmentation-based hadronization prescription. For vector mesons, the spin-alignment parameter $ρ_{00}$ is constructed by coupling the heavy-quark spin to that of the light antiquark produced during fragmentation. We confront our results with recent ALICE measurements of prompt $D^{*+}$ spin alignment in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~{\rm TeV}$ and extract an effective depolarization strength that determines the spin-relaxation time scale. Using this fitted parameter, we provide benchmark estimates for $Λ_c^+$ and $\barΛ_c^-$ polarization, up to an overall spin-transfer normalization. We further estimate the recently proposed elliptic polarization harmonic arising from path-length-dependent depolarization in an anisotropic fireball.

## Key claim

The paper develops a quantum spin-density-matrix framework for heavy-quark spin dynamics, showing that the observed $D^{*+}$ spin alignment in Pb--Pb collisions can be explained only if a fragmentation-based hadronization mechanism produces an anticorrelation between heavy-quark and light-antiquark spins, rather than through coalescence with unpolarized medium quarks.

## Method

The authors formulate heavy-quark spin evolution using a spin density matrix initialized by an early magnetic field, with subsequent depolarization described by spin-flip transition rates that yield a relaxation equation for the polarization vector. The evolved heavy-quark polarization is connected to open heavy-flavor observables through a fragmentation-based hadronization prescription, where for vector mesons the spin-alignment parameter $\rho_{00}$ is constructed by coupling the heavy-quark spin to that of the light antiquark produced during fragmentation. A two-parameter Bayesian fit of the model to ALICE $D^{*+}$ spin-alignment data is performed, and the fitted depolarization strength is used to estimate $\Lambda_c^+$ polarization and elliptic polarization harmonics.

## Key numbers

- ALICE measurement of prompt $D^{*+}$ spin alignment in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~{\rm TeV}$ for the 30--50% centrality class and rapidity window $0.3<|y|<0.8$ is used as the experimental constraint
- Fitted spin-alignment amplitude $A = 0.73^{+0.19}_{-0.26}$ and effective depolarization strength $\kappa = 10.1^{+3.9}_{-3.1}$ from the Bayesian fit
- For representative path lengths $L_0=5~{\rm fm}$ and $L_0=10~{\rm fm}$, the extracted spin-relaxation times are $\tau_s=0.50^{+0.22}_{-0.14}~{\rm fm}/c$ and $\tau_s=0.99^{+0.44}_{-0.28}~{\rm fm}/c$, respectively
- Charm-quark mass taken as $m_c=1.27~{\rm GeV}$ for the calculations
- Elliptic geometric eccentricity $\epsilon_2=0.38$ used for the polarization harmonic estimates, taken from Monte Carlo Glauber calculations for Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02~{\rm TeV}$ in the 30--50% centrality class

## Context

This work extends the stochastic rotational Brownian motion framework for heavy-quark spin dynamics developed by Dey (2025) by providing a complementary quantum spin-density-matrix formulation. The results are confronted with recent ALICE measurements (2025) of $D^{*+}$ spin alignment, and the extracted parameters enable benchmark estimates for $\Lambda_c^+$ polarization and the elliptic polarization harmonics proposed by Jaiswal (2026) as probes of initial-state eccentricity. The framework enables quantitative connections between early-time magnetic field effects, heavy-quark spin relaxation in the QCD medium, and final-state open heavy-flavor observables.
