# Nuclear Self-energy and Realistic Interactions

> The structure of nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter is evaluated for various realistic models of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. Starting from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation without the usual angle-average approximation, the effects of hole-hole contributions and a self-consistent treatment within the framework of the Green function approach are investigated. Special attention is paid to the predictions for the spectral function originating from various models of the NN interaction which all yield an accurate fit for the NN phase shifts.

# Nuclear Self-energy and Realistic Interactions

## Key claim

The structure of the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter depends significantly on the choice of single-particle spectrum (conventional vs. continuous) and on whether the exact Pauli operator is used instead of the angle-average approximation, with the continuous choice and exact Pauli operator yielding larger binding energies; however, the hole-hole ladder contributions are found to be small (around 5 MeV correction to the Fermi energy) compared to particle-particle contributions (up to -90 MeV), justifying a perturbative treatment of hole-hole terms at densities around saturation.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature without the usual angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator, using a self-consistent continuous choice for the single-particle spectrum. They then add hole-hole ladder contributions perturbatively (the 2h1p term) and analyze the spectral functions, effective k-mass and E-mass, and the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy. Calculations are performed for several realistic NN interactions: Bonn A, Bonn C, CD Bonn, Argonne V18, and the chiral Idaho A and B potentials.

## Key numbers

- The angle-average approximation in the Pauli operator leads to an underestimation of calculated binding energies of around 0.5 MeV per nucleon when using an effective mass parametrization.
- The effective E-mass reaches values up to 1.4 times the bare nucleon mass m at energies slightly above the Fermi energy.
- The particle-particle ladder contribution to the Fermi energy is as large as -90 MeV for Bonn C or Argonne V18 interactions.
- The correction from the 2h1p (hole-hole) term to the Fermi energy is only around 5 MeV.
- At the empirical saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, the solution of the gap equation in the 3S1-3D1 partial wave leads to an energy gap of around 10 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the self-energy for the Idaho A interaction tends to zero for energies larger than 500 MeV, and the real part becomes positive at around 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach by removing the angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator, following methods developed by Schiller et al. and Suzuki et al. It also investigates the continuous choice for the single-particle spectrum advocated by Mahaux and collaborators, which is motivated by the self-consistent Green function method. The paper compares results from multiple modern NN potentials (CD Bonn by Machleidt, Argonne V18 by Wiringa et al., Nijmegen potentials, and chiral Idaho potentials by Entem and Machleidt) and addresses the pairing instabilities encountered when including hole-hole ladders via the Galitskii-Feynman equation, as previously observed by von der Linden, Alm, and Bozek. The findings enable more accurate calculations of nuclear matter properties and provide guidance for treating self-consistency in finite nuclei, where similar instabilities have been observed by Heinz and Mavromatis.
