# Absorption in a Particle Oscillations

> In the framework of field approach with finite time interval the particle oscillations in a medium are considered. The absorption in a final states do not lead to dramatic suppression of ab-transitions. Also we touch on the problem of infrared singularities. The approach under study is infrared-free. It is shown that "correction" published recently [A.Gal, Phys. Rev. C61 (2000) 028201] is clearly wrong.

# Absorption in a Particle Oscillations

## Key claim

The authors argue that absorption in the final state does not dramatically suppress particle oscillations (such as neutron-antineutron transitions) in a medium, contrary to the standard potential model which predicts a suppression factor of order \(10^{-31}\). They claim that a "correction" published by Gal (2000) is clearly wrong, and that the field approach with finite time interval is infrared-free and correctly describes the process.

## Method

The authors employ a field-theoretic approach with a finite time interval (FTA) to calculate the probability of \(n\bar{n}\) transitions in nuclear matter, treating the \(\bar{n}\)-medium interaction via a Hamiltonian \(H\) rather than a constant optical potential. They compare the FTA results with the standard potential model (solving coupled Schrödinger equations with a complex potential \(V = -i\Gamma/2\)) and with \(S\)-matrix theory, analyzing both singular (zero momentum transfer) and non-singular diagrams for processes where annihilation products are detected.

## Key numbers

- The standard potential model gives a suppression factor \(R_{pot} = W_{pot}/W_f = 4/\Gamma t\); for \(\Gamma = 100\) MeV and observation time \(t = T_0 = 1.3\) yr, this yields \(R_{pot} \sim 10^{-31}\).
- The field approach gives \(W_{ann}(t) \approx \epsilon^2 t^2 = W_f\), i.e., the \(n\bar{n}\) conversion probability in the medium equals the free-space probability, with no suppression (\(R_a \sim 1\)).
- For the imaginary process \(n \rightarrow \bar{n} + \Phi\) (Fig. 1b), the width ratio is \(\Gamma_b / \Gamma_{free} \approx 1/\pi\) when \(m_\Phi = \Gamma \sim 100\) MeV.
- The annihilation width of \(\bar{n}\) in nuclear matter is \(\Gamma \sim 100\) MeV, with characteristic interaction time \(\sim 10^{-23}\) s.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finite-time-interval approach (Kuzmin and Nazaruk, 2000) to particle oscillations in media, directly contradicting the standard potential model used in earlier calculations of \(n\bar{n}\) transitions (Dover, Gal, and Richard, 1995). The paper also refutes a specific "correction" by Gal (2000) that attempted to modify the FTA result. The approach enables a re-evaluation of \(n\bar{n}\) oscillation searches in underground experiments (such as proton-decay-type experiments with observation times of order 1 year), suggesting that medium absorption does not suppress the oscillation probability, which could significantly affect experimental sensitivity and interpretation.
