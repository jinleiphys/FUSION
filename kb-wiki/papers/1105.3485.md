# A derivation of the source term induced by a fast parton from the quark energy-momentum tensor

> The distribution of energy and momentum deposited by a fast parton in a medium of thermalized quarks, or the source term, is evaluated in perturbative thermal field theory. The calculation is performed by directly evaluating the thermal expectation value of the quark energy-momentum tensor. The fast parton is coupled to the medium by adding an interaction term to the Lagrangian. I show that this approach is very general and can be modified to consider more realistic modeling of fast parton propagation, such as a fast parton created in an initial hard interaction or the evolution of a parton shower due to medium induced radiation. For the scenario considered here, it is found that local excitations fall sharply as a function of the energy of the fast parton. These local excitations couple directly to the sound mode in hydrodynamics and are important for generating an observable shockwave structure. This may have implications for the trigger pT dependence of measurements of azimuthal dihadron particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions. In particular, one would be less likely to observe a conical emission pattern for increasing trigger pT.

# A derivation of the source term induced by a fast parton from the quark energy-momentum tensor

## Key claim
The source term (divergence of the energy-momentum tensor) for a medium of thermalized quarks perturbed by an asymptotically propagating fast parton is derived directly from thermal field theory, and the resulting local excitations (parameterized by lambda) fall sharply as a function of the fast parton's Lorentz factor gamma, implying that a conical Mach-like emission pattern in heavy-ion collisions would be less likely to be observed for increasing trigger pT.

## Method
The fast parton is modeled as an external color current coupled to the QCD Lagrangian via an interaction term, preserving non-Abelian gauge symmetry. The thermal expectation value of the divergence of the quark energy-momentum tensor (the source term) is evaluated at lowest order in perturbative thermal field theory using real-time formalism, with Feynman rules derived for the energy-momentum tensor and external current insertions. The calculation considers a medium of massless quarks and antiquarks (leaving gluons for a future study) and yields the source term in equation (fullsourceterm) as an integral over thermal distributions and retarded Green's functions.

## Key numbers
* The parameter lambda, which characterizes local (non-energy-loss) contributions to the source term, is calculated for g = 1 and T = 400 MeV and shown to drop sharply as a function of gamma = E/m (the fast parton's Lorentz factor).
* The collisional energy loss rate in the hard thermal loop (HTL) limit is given by dE/dt_HTL = (m_D^2 * C_2 * alpha_s / 2) * (1 - tanh^{-1}[u] / (gamma^2 * u)) * ln(p_max / p_min), with p_max ~ T and p_min ~ m_D.
* In the source term ansatz of Neufeld and Renk (2010), a double-peaked azimuthal emission spectrum in linearized hydrodynamics required lambda values on the order of 0.5 fm or higher for 20 GeV total energy deposited into the medium.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic theory calculation of the source term by Neufeld (2008) by deriving it directly from the energy-momentum tensor in thermal field theory, and reproduces that earlier result in the hard thermal loop approximation. The approach is more general than previous work and can be extended to consider back-to-back fast partons from initial hard interactions or medium-induced parton shower evolution (Neufeld 2011). The result enables phenomenological studies of shockwave (Mach cone) formation in the quark-gluon plasma, and the finding that lambda decreases with increasing parton energy provides a theoretical basis for the observed trigger pT dependence of azimuthal dihadron correlations (Adare 2010).
