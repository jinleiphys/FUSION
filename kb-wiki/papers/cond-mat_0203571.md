# Strongly correlated wave functions for artificial atoms and molecules

> A method for constructing semianalytical strongly correlated wave functions for single and molecular quantum dots is presented. It employs a two-step approach of symmetry breaking at the Hartree-Fock level and of subsequent restoration of total spin and angular momentum symmetries via Projection Techniques. Illustrative applications are presented for the case of a two-electron helium-like single quantum dot and a hydrogen-like quantum dot molecule.

## Key claim

The paper presents a two-step method for constructing semianalytical strongly correlated wave functions for quantum dots, where symmetry is first broken at the Hartree-Fock level and then restored via Projection Techniques; the authors emphasize that this approach is largely untested in two-dimensional quantum dots and focus on methodological aspects for two-electron systems.

## Method

The authors employ spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock (sS-UHF) to generate symmetry-broken single-determinantal wave functions for two electrons in a single quantum dot (He-QD) and a lateral double quantum dot (H₂-QDM). They then restore total spin symmetry via a spin-projection operator and, for the circular single dot, also restore total angular momentum symmetry via an angular-momentum projection operator, producing multi-determinantal wave functions with good quantum numbers. The single-particle Hamiltonian includes a two-center-oscillator potential for the quantum dot molecule and a parabolic confinement for the single dot, with the Coulomb repulsion controlled by varying the dielectric constant κ.

## Key numbers

- For the H₂-QDM at B=0, with parameters ℏω₀=5 meV, interdot distance d=30 nm, barrier height Vb=3.71 meV, effective mass m*=0.067 mₑ, and dielectric constant κ=45, the singlet-triplet energy gap Δε starts from a negative minimum and crosses zero as magnetic field increases.
- For the He-QD at B=0, the onset of strong correlations and symmetry-broken HF solutions occurs at κ=16.5 (R_W=1.16).
- For the He-QD at B=0 with κ=8 (R_W=2.39), the exact ground-state energy is 19.80 meV and the S&AMP approximation yields a relative error of 4%.
- For the He-QD at B=0 with κ=1 (R_W=19.09), the exact ground-state energy is 51.83 meV and the S&AMP approximation yields a relative error of 0.7%.
- The percentage of correlation energy captured by the S&AMP approximation is 73.1% for κ=8 and 98.5% for κ=1.

## Context

This work extends the use of Projection Techniques, previously introduced in Quantum Chemistry for restoring total spin (Lowdin) and in Nuclear Physics for restoring 3D angular momentum (Pyykkö, Ring and Schuck), to the simultaneous restoration of both spin and angular momentum symmetries in two-dimensional quantum dots. The method enables accurate description of strongly correlated electrons in artificial atoms and molecules, including the Wigner molecule regime, and has potential applications in quantum computing studies of two-electron entanglement, building on earlier work by Burkard et al. (1999) on the H₂-QDM as a two-qubit logic gate.
