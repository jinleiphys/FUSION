# Dissipative effects from transport and viscous hydrodynamics

> We compare 2->2 covariant transport theory and causal Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics in 2+1D longitudinally boost invariant geometry with RHIC-like initial conditions and a conformal e = 3p equation of state. The pressure evolution in the center of the collision zone and the final differential elliptic flow v2(pT) from the two theories agree remarkably well for a small shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s ~ 1/(4 pi), and also for a large cross section sigma ~ 50 mb. A key to this agreement is keeping ALL terms in the Israel-Stewart equations of motion. Our results indicate promising prospects for the applicability of Israel-Stewart dissipative hydrodynamics at RHIC, provided the shear viscosity of hot and dense quark-gluon matter is indeed very small for the relevant temperatures T ~ 200-500 MeV.

# Dissipative effects from transport and viscous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Israel-Stewart dissipative hydrodynamics agrees remarkably well with covariant 2→2 transport theory for RHIC-like conditions, provided *all* terms in the Israel-Stewart equations are kept, including the vorticity term and the term involving the derivative of the relaxation coefficient, which are often omitted in other implementations.

## Method

The authors solved 2+1D longitudinally boost-invariant Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics and covariant 2→2 transport theory (using the MPC algorithm) for an ideal gas of massless particles with a conformal ε = 3p equation of state and conserved particle number. Initial conditions modeled Au+Au collisions at √s_NN ≈ 200 GeV and impact parameter b = 8 fm, with uniform initial temperature T₀ = 385 MeV at thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm. The shear viscosity and relaxation time were matched to kinetic theory values using an isotropic two-body cross section, and freezeout was performed via the Cooper-Frye prescription on a constant density hypersurface n = 0.365 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ≈ 1/(4π) (the conjectured "minimal" value) was used as one scenario, corresponding to a cross section σ ~ τ²/³ that grows with proper time to keep η/s approximately constant
- Large cross section scenario: σ_gg→gg ≈ 47 mb, corresponding to η ≈ 6T/(5σ) from kinetic theory
- Initial conditions: dN/dη(b=0) = 1000, thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm, uniform initial temperature T₀ = 385 MeV
- Freezeout density: n = 0.365 fm⁻³, corresponding to an ideal gas of massless gluons at T = 120 MeV (3 colors)
- Dissipative corrections reduce elliptic flow by approximately 30% relative to ideal hydrodynamics even at η/s ≈ 1/(4π)
- At least one third of the reduction comes from corrections to the ideal hydrodynamic variables, while up to two-thirds come from shear stress altering local momentum distributions
- For η/s ≈ 1/(4π), IS hydro somewhat underpredicts transport results at p_T > 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends comparisons between transport theory and dissipative hydrodynamics to RHIC energies, building on earlier kinetic theory estimates of minimal viscosity effects (Molnar, 2005). The results agree with the dissipative corrections found by Romatschke (2007) but are much smaller than those reported by Song (2007), which the authors attribute to Song's omission of key terms in the Israel-Stewart equations. The findings support the applicability of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics at RHIC for temperatures T ~ 200-500 MeV, provided the shear viscosity is indeed very small, and enable further studies of dissipative effects on flow observables in heavy-ion collisions.
