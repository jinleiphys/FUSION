# Fate of the Tetraquark Candidate Zc(3900) from Lattice QCD

> The possible exotic meson $Z_{c}(3900)$, found in $e^+ e^-$ reactions, is studied by the method of coupled-channel scattering in lattice QCD. The interaction among $πJ/ψ$, $ρη_{c}$ and $\bar{D}D^{*}$ channels is derived from (2+1)-flavor QCD simulations at $m_π=410$-$700$ MeV. The interaction is dominated by the off-diagonal $πJ/ψ$-$\bar{D}D^{*}$ and $ρη_{c}$-$\bar{D}D^{*}$ couplings, which indicates that the $Z_{c}(3900)$ is not a usual resonance but a threshold cusp. Semi-phenomenological analyses with the coupled-channel interaction are presented to confirm this conclusion.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the Zc(3900) is not a conventional resonance but a threshold cusp, based on lattice QCD coupled-channel scattering analyses at pion masses of 410-700 MeV, with the caveat that the method has not yet been experimentally tested in other coupled-channel systems.

## Method
The HAL QCD Collaboration extracted energy-independent coupled-channel potentials among the πJ/ψ, ρηc, and D̄D* channels from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions. They solved the coupled-channel Schrödinger-type equation to obtain the S-matrix and searched for complex poles on the [bbb] Riemann sheet. Semiphenomenological three-body decay analyses of Y(4260) → ππJ/ψ and πD̄D* were performed by combining the lattice-derived potentials with experimental data from BESIII.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses used: 411(1) MeV (Case I), 570(1) MeV (Case II), 701(1) MeV (Case III)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm, spatial volume L³ ≈ (2.9 fm)³
- Complex pole on [bbb] sheet for Case I: z - (m_D̄ + m_D*) = -167(94)(27) - i183(46)(19) MeV
- Complex pole for Case II: -128(76)(33) - i157(32)(19) MeV
- Complex pole for Case III: -190(56)(42) - i44(27)(27) MeV
- Best-fit ratio of couplings: R = |C^{Y→π(D̄D*)} / C^{Y→π(πJ/ψ)}| = 0.95(18)
- Best-fit relative phase: θ = -58(44) degrees

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD coupled-channel method (Aoki 2011, Aoki 2012, Sasaki 2015) to the tetraquark candidate Zc(3900), which was previously studied phenomenologically as a hadro-charmonium, compact tetraquark, or hadronic molecule (Voloshin, Cleven) as well as a threshold effect (Matsuki, Swanson). Direct lattice studies using temporal correlations (Sasa, Lee) found no Zc(3900) eigenstate. The results enable future full QCD simulations near the physical point and application of the same method to pentaquark candidates Pc+(4380) and Pc+(4450).
