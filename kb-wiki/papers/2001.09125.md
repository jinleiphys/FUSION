# Hydrodynamic generators in relativistic kinetic theory

> We resum the non-equilibrium gradient corrections to a single-particle distribution function evolved by the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA). We first study a system undergoing Bjorken expansion and show that, for a constant relaxation time, the exact solution of the RTA Boltzmann equation at late times (i.e. after the decay of non-hydrodynamic modes) generates the Borel resummed Chapman-Enskog series. Extending this correspondence to systems without Bjorken symmetry, we construct a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic generator for RTA kinetic theory, which is an integral representation of the Chapman-Enskog series in the limit of vanishing non-hydrodynamic modes. Relaxing this limit we find at earlier times a set of non-hydrodynamic modes coupled to the Chapman-Enskog expansion. Including the dynamics of these non-hydrodynamic modes is shown to control the emergence of hydrodynamics as an effective field theory description of non-equilibrium fluids, which works well even for far-off-equilibrium situations where the Knudsen number is initially large.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the exact solution of the RTA Boltzmann equation, after non-hydrodynamic modes have decayed, generates the Borel resummed Chapman-Enskog series; at earlier times, the gradient corrections are suppressed by non-hydrodynamic modes whose decay controls the emergence of hydrodynamics as an effective field theory, even for far-off-equilibrium situations where the Knudsen number is initially large.

## Method

The authors first study a system undergoing (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken expansion with a constant relaxation time, showing that the exact solution of the RTA Boltzmann equation at late times reduces to the Borel resummed Chapman-Enskog series via an integral representation they call the "hydrodynamic generator." They then extend this construction to (3+1)-dimensional Minkowski spacetime without Bjorken symmetry by formulating the hydrodynamic generator as a path integral along free-streaming characteristics, and verify up to finite order that its expansion reproduces the Borel resummed Chapman-Enskog series. The evolution of the pressure anisotropy is computed numerically using fixed-point iteration to solve the integral equation for the temperature, and the contributions from the non-hydrodynamic mode and gradient corrections up to third order are compared with Navier-Stokes, Burnett, Super-Burnett, and DNMR viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* For Bjorken flow with initial conditions at tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c, initial temperature T(tau_0) = 0.6 GeV, shear stress pi(tau_0) = 0, and eta/s = 3/(4 pi), the system "hydrodynamizes" at tau = 2.47 fm/c (z_0 = 3.6), defined as when the leading non-hydrodynamic mode decays to 10% of its maximum value.
* The first-order Navier-Stokes solution for the normalized shear stress is given by bar_pi^(NS) = (16 tau_pi)/(15 tau).
* The second-order Burnett solution is bar_pi^(B) = (16 tau_pi)/(15 tau) + (64 tau_pi^2)/(315 tau^2).
* The third-order Super-Burnett solution is bar_pi^(SB) = (16 tau_pi)/(15 tau) + (64 tau_pi^2)/(315 tau^2) - (832 tau_pi^3)/(1575 tau^3).
* By tau = 3.9 fm/c, the non-hydrodynamic mode suppressing the second-order gradient correction has decayed by 90%, at which point the Knudsen number Kn = tau_pi/tau is approximately 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of the hydrodynamic attractor phenomenon studied by Heller and Spalinski (2015), Romatschke (2017), and Strickland (2018) by providing a physical mechanism for the emergence of hydrodynamics from kinetic theory. It builds on the known divergence of the Chapman-Enskog expansion in RTA kinetic theory (Grad, 1963; Denicol, 2016) and the Borel resummation approach for Bjorken flow (Heller, 2016; Heller, 2018). The results explain the empirically observed success of causal second-order viscous hydrodynamics (e.g., DNMR by Denicol, 2012) in far-from-equilibrium systems such as ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the gradient expansion would naively be expected to fail.
