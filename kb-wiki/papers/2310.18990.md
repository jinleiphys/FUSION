# Studying the production mechanisms of light meson resonances in two-pion photoproduction: a Regge approach

> A calculation of the angular moments of two-pion photoproduction is presented. The underlying theoretical model encodes the prominent $ρ(770)$ resonance and the expected leading background contribution coming from the Deck mechanism. The model contains a number of free parameters which are fit to experimental data. A good description of the angular moments is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical model of two-pion photoproduction that includes both the prominent ρ(770) resonance and the Deck mechanism as the leading background, and after fitting free parameters to experimental data, a good description of the angular moments is obtained, except possibly for two-pion invariant masses above 1.2 GeV where additional resonances not included in the model are expected to contribute.

## Method

The authors construct a Regge model for the process γ + p → π⁺ + π⁻ + p, incorporating resonant contributions from meson resonances (σ(500), f₀(980), and ρ(770)) produced via Reggeon-photon fusion, and a continuum contribution from the Deck mechanism. The model uses energy-dependent Breit-Wigner distributions for resonances and Regge propagators with linear trajectories for the exchange particles (Pomeron, f₂, and ρ/ω). The πN scattering amplitudes in the Deck contribution are taken from the SAID parameterization at low energies and interpolated to a Regge description at high energies, and the free parameters are fit to CLAS experimental data on angular moments.

## Key numbers

- The two-pion invariant mass range considered is from near threshold at m₁₂ ∼ 0.4 GeV up to m₁₂ ∼ 1.4 GeV, based on CLAS experimental data.
- Only resonances with mass less than 1 GeV are included: the σ(500), f₀(980), and ρ(770).
- The hadronic form factor cutoff parameter in the Deck model is Λ_π = 0.9 GeV.
- The mass scale introduced in the Regge propagator is s₀ = 1 GeV².
- At −t = 0.45 GeV, the hierarchy of P-wave partial waves follows s-channel helicity conservation with |P₊| > |P₀| > |P₋|, but this hierarchy is violated at larger −t.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of two-pion photoproduction by combining resonant and Deck mechanism contributions within a Regge framework, building on the formalism of Mathieu (2019) for angular moments. The model reproduces CLAS experimental data (CLAS Collaboration, 2009) and explains the violation of s-channel helicity conservation observed at larger momentum transfers, which was previously noted by Ballam (1970) at smaller −t values. The approach enables predictions for photon energies relevant to the CLAS12 and GlueX experiments.
