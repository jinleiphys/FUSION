# Charm decay as a source of multistrange hadrons

> We describe a newly formulated approach to account for charm production and decay in Statistical Hadronization approach. Considering Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV at LHC we show that charm hadron decays can be a significant contributor to the multistrange hadron abundance. We discuss the magnitude of expected effects as a function of charm yield.

# Charm decay as a source of multistrange hadrons

## Key claim
Charm hadron decays can be a significant contributor to the multistrange hadron abundance in Pb–Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only if the total charm yield exceeds about 25 charm-plus-anticharm quarks (13 pairs); at RHIC energies the charm contribution is negligible. The effect becomes observable only when the total charm yield is large enough to replace strangeness production in the quark-gluon plasma at hadronization.

## Method
The authors extended the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHARE) with a CHARM module that computes the distribution of charm among charmed hadrons using statistical hadronization at a prescribed temperature, then decays each charmed hadron using a complete decay table built from experimental data and symmetry principles. The resulting feed-down hadrons are added to the initial SHM yields, and a MINUIT-based fit to experimental hadron yields is performed with the total charm yield \(N_{cc}\) as an additional fit parameter.

## Key numbers
- Total charm yield at RHIC (central Au–Au, 0–5% centrality): \(N_{cc}^\mathrm{RHIC} \equiv dN_{c+\bar{c}}/dy \simeq 4.3\) for \(y \in (-0.5, 0.5)\)
- Theoretical initial charm yield at LHC (Pb–Pb, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV): \(N_{cc} = 246 \pm 154\) (\(123 \pm 77\) pairs)
- Best direct experiment-based estimate from ALICE \(D^0\) data (0–5% centrality): \(N_{cc}^\mathrm{LHC} \in (10, 60)\)
- Charm phase space occupancy \(\gamma_c > 500\) for \(N_{cc} > 50\), indicating significant charm reannihilation
- At LHC, charm contribution to \(\Xi^-\) yield exceeds experimental error for \(N_{cc} > 25\) (13 or more pairs)
- For \(N_{cc} = 400\), strangeness in QGP at hadronization would be practically erased

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization approach previously applied to RHIC data (Rafelski 2004) and to LHC Pb–Pb collisions (Petran 2013) by incorporating charm production and decay as a source of light hadrons. It confirms an earlier result (Kuznetsova 2006) that charm decays contribute substantially to multistrange hadrons such as \(\phi\), \(\Xi\), and \(\Omega\). The new SHARE with CHARM program enables future fits that can determine the charm hadronization temperature separately from the light-hadron temperature, and will allow tests of whether statistical hadronization applies to charmed hadron production once multiple charmed hadron yields are measured directly.
