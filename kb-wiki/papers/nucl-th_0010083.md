# Simulation of Nucleus-Nucleus Interactions in the Framework of the FRITIOF Model at the Energy of 3.3 GeV/nucleon

> The intranuclear cascade model overestimates the multiplicity of produced mesons in nucleus-nucleus interactions without taking into account meson and baryon resonance production. Inclusion of the resonances leads to decreasing multiplicity of mesons, neutrons and protons. In order to overcome the problem, it is proposed to use the FRITIOF model adapted to low energies in a combination with the reggeon theory inspired model of nuclear destruction. It is shown that the combination allows one to reproduce satisfactory the meson and baryon yields in the nucleus-nucleus collisions at the energy of 3.3 GeV/nucleon. The combined model works faster than typical quantum molecular dynamic model, and allows one to estimate the data needed for creation of electro-nuclear amplifier.

# Simulation of Nucleus-Nucleus Interactions in the Framework of the FRITIOF Model at the Energy of 3.3 GeV/nucleon

## Key claim

The authors show that a modified FRITIOF model, adapted for low energies and combined with a reggeon-theory-inspired model of nuclear destruction, can reproduce meson and baryon yields in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 3.3 GeV/nucleon, whereas the standard intranuclear cascade model overestimates meson multiplicity. The combined model works faster than typical quantum molecular dynamics models, but the agreement is described as "satisfactory" rather than exact, and the model parameters (such as the nuclear destruction constants C_nd and r_nd^2) must be tuned separately for light and heavy target nuclei.

## Method

The authors modified the FRITIOF Monte Carlo code by: (1) lowering the minimal excited nucleon mass from 1.2 GeV to 1.1 GeV; (2) simulating isotropic decay in the center-of-mass system for two-particle decay channels of strings with mass below 1.7 GeV; (3) allowing charge exchange between colliding nucleons in 50% of two-vertex diffraction events; and (4) increasing the average squared transverse momentum transfer from 0.08 (GeV/c)^2 to 0.15 (GeV/c)^2. They combined this modified FRITIOF model with a reggeon-theory-inspired model of nuclear destruction, where "cascade" interactions occur in the two-dimensional impact-parameter plane rather than in three-dimensional space, using a probability function P(|b_i - b_j|) = C_nd exp(-(b_i - b_j)^2 / r_nd^2) to determine which spectator nucleons are involved. Excitation energy of the residual nucleus was calculated from recoil energies transferred to neighboring spectator nucleons within a 2 fm sphere, using an exponential distribution with mean energy 8 MeV, followed by a standard evaporation model for de-excitation.

## Key numbers

* Minimal excited nucleon mass reduced from 1.2 GeV to 1.1 GeV in the modified FRITIOF code.
* Isotropic decay applied for strings with mass lower than 1.7 GeV in the two-particle decay channel.
* Average squared transverse momentum transfer increased from 0.08 (GeV/c)^2 to 0.15 (GeV/c)^2.
* Charge exchange allowed in 50% of two-vertex diffraction events.
* For nuclear destruction in light nuclei (CC interactions), parameters used: C_nd = 1, r_nd^2 = 1.4 fm^2.
* For heavy nuclei (Ta), parameters used: C_nd = 0.2, r_nd^2 = 1.1 fm^2.
* Mean recoil energy for excitation energy calculation: <E> = 8 MeV.
* Average transverse momentum of spectator fragments: sqrt(<p_perp^2>) = 0.07 GeV/c.
* For knocked-out nucleons, new values used: <p_perp^2> = 0.385 (GeV/c)^2 and d_x = 0.2.
* The 3-pomeron vertex constant cited: G_PPP = 1.35 x 10^(-2) (GeV)^2.

## Context

This work extends the FRITIOF model, originally developed for high-energy hadron-hadron and hadron-nucleus interactions (Fritiof1, Fritiof2), down to the intermediate energy of 3.3 GeV/nucleon for nucleus-nucleus collisions. It addresses the known failure of intranuclear cascade models (CEM, as developed by Mashnik (1994) and others) to correctly describe meson yields in nucleus-nucleus interactions, where the cascade model overestimates meson multiplicity. The approach combines the modified FRITIOF model with a reggeon-theory-inspired nuclear destruction model (RTIM, Olek) that treats cascading in impact-parameter space rather than three-dimensional space, following the Abramovski-Gribov-Kancheli cutting rules (AGK) and the work of BKKS on enhanced diagrams. The resulting model enables practical calculations for applications such as electro-nuclear amplifier design, radioactive waste transmutation, and cosmic ray effects on spacecraft, where experimental data are costly to obtain.
