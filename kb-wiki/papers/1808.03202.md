# Updating spin-dependent Regge intercepts

> We use new high statistics data from CLAS and COMPASS on the nucleon's spin structure function at low Bjorken x and low virtuality, Q^2 < 0.5 GeV^2, together with earlier measurements from the SLAC E-143, HERMES and GDH experiments to estimate the effective intercept(s) for spin dependent Regge theory. We find alpha_{a_1} = 0.31 \pm 0.04 for the intercept describing the high-energy behaviour of spin dependent photoabsorption together with a new estimate for the high-energy part of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule, -15 \pm 2 mu b from photon-proton centre-of-mass energy greater than 2.5 GeV. Our value of alpha_[a_1} suggests QCD physics beyond a simple straight-line a_1 trajectory.

# Updating spin-dependent Regge intercepts

## Key claim
The effective Regge intercept for spin-dependent photoabsorption is found to be α_{a₁} = 0.31 ± 0.04 at low virtuality Q² < 0.5 GeV², a value close to the deep inelastic scattering result of 0.22 ± 0.07 at Q² = 3 GeV², which suggests the presence of either a hard exchange contribution (such as an a₁ hard-pomeron cut) or a curved Regge trajectory rather than a simple straight-line a₁ trajectory.

## Method
The authors performed a Regge-motivated fit to the spin asymmetry A₁ᵖ = (σ_A − σ_P)/(σ_A + σ_P) for photon-proton collisions, using data from CLAS, COMPASS, SLAC E-143, HERMES, and the GDH experiment with constraints √s ≥ 2.5 GeV and Q² < 0.5 GeV². They assumed A₁ᵖ to be Q²-independent in this kinematic range, took the isoscalar deuteron asymmetry A₁ᵈ as zero, and used a Regge fit to unpolarised total cross-section data from HERA to obtain σ_tot. The fit form (σ_A − σ_P) ∼ N s^α was applied to 129 data points.

## Key numbers
- Effective Regge intercept: α_{a₁} = 0.31 ± 0.04, from fit to (σ_A − σ_P) = (35.3 ± 3.6) s^(−0.69 ± 0.04) μb with χ²/ndf = 0.98
- High-energy contribution to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule from √s ≥ 2.5 GeV: −15 ± 2 μb, a factor of 3.5 times more accurate than the previous most accurate determination
- Total Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule integral from threshold: 211 ± 13 μb, compared with the theoretical prediction 205 μb
- Predicted low-Q² asymmetries: A₁ᵖ = (1.7 ± 0.5) × 10⁻³ at √s = 40 GeV and A₁ᵖ = (2.5 ± 1.0) × 10⁻⁴ at √s = 140 GeV
- Deep inelastic scattering intercept from COMPASS: α_{a₁}(Q²) = 0.22 ± 0.07 at Q² = 3 GeV²

## Context
This work extends earlier Regge analyses of spin-dependent photoabsorption, updating previous intercept estimates such as α_{a₁} = 0.42 ± 0.23 from Helbing (2006) and α_{a₁} = −0.03 ± 0.07 from the curved-trajectory model of Brisudova (1999). The new high-statistics CLAS and COMPASS data allow a significantly more precise determination of the high-energy part of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule. The results provide input for perturbative QCD evolution calculations at deep inelastic Q² and motivate further measurements at an electron-ion collider with √s up to 140 GeV to distinguish between hard-exchange and curved-trajectory interpretations.
