# Signatures of Chiral Magnetic Effect in the Collisions of Isobars

> Quantum anomaly is a fundamental feature of chiral fermions. In chiral materials the microscopic anomaly leads to nontrivial macroscopic transport processes such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), which has been in the spotlight lately across disciplines of physics. The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in relativistic nuclear collisions provides the unique example of a chiral material consisting of intrinsically relativistic chiral fermions. Potential discovery of CME in QGP is of utmost significance, with extensive experimental searches carried out over the past decade. A decisive new collider experiment, dedicated to detecting CME in the collisions of isobars, was performed in 2018 with analysis now underway. In this paper, we develop the state-of-the-art theoretical tool for describing CME phenomenon in these collisions and propose an appropriate isobar subtraction strategy for best background removal. Based on that, we make quantitative predictions for signatures of CME in the collisions of isobars. A new and robust observable that is independent of axial charge uncertainty -- the ratio between isobar-subtracted $γ-$ and $δ-$ correlators, is found to be $- ( 0.41 \pm 0.27 )$ for event-plane measurement and $ - ( 0.90 \pm 0.45 )$ for reaction-plane measurement.

# Signatures of Chiral Magnetic Effect in the Collisions of Isobars

## Key claim
The ratio between isobar-subtracted gamma and delta correlators provides a robust observable for the Chiral Magnetic Effect that is independent of the uncertain initial axial charge density, with predicted values of -(0.41 ± 0.27) for event-plane measurement and -(0.90 ± 0.45) for reaction-plane measurement, but these predictions depend on the effectiveness of a joint (multiplicity + elliptic flow) event selection strategy to ensure identical bulk properties between RuRu and ZrZr systems.

## Method
The authors developed the Event-By-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (EBE-AVFD) framework, which combines event-by-event initial state sampling for bulk entropy density and fermion axial charge density with VISHNU hydrodynamic simulations and URQMD hadron cascade. They generated ten million collision events for each isobar system (RuRu and ZrZr) at 40-50% centrality, applying a joint event selection cut on multiplicity (65 ≤ Nch ≤ 96) and elliptic flow (0.05 < v2 < 0.25) to ensure identical bulk properties. The CME signal was isolated by subtracting ZrZr observables from RuRu observables, exploiting the difference in nuclear charge (Z=44 for Ru versus Z=40 for Zr) while maintaining identical nucleon number (A=96).

## Key numbers
- The relative difference in geometry between isobar pairs is reduced to approximately 0.1% after joint event selection, while the relative difference in magnetic field remains at the 10-25% level.
- Post-selection events from both systems have mean multiplicity ⟨Nch⟩ = 80.4 (standard deviation 8.5) and mean elliptic flow ⟨v2⟩ = 0.1132 (standard deviation 0.046).
- The ratio ζisobar between isobar-subtracted gamma and delta correlators is -(0.41 ± 0.27) for event-plane measurement and -(0.90 ± 0.45) for reaction-plane measurement.
- The gamma correlator for event-plane measurement scales as (0.89 ± 0.51) × 10^(-3) × (n5/s)^2, and for reaction-plane as (1.94 ± 0.72) × 10^(-3) × (n5/s)^2.
- The delta correlator for both event-plane and reaction-plane measurements scales as -(2.17 ± 0.72) × 10^(-3) × (n5/s)^2.

## Context
This work extends the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework developed by Shi et al. (2017) and Jiang et al. (2016) by adding event-by-event fluctuations, background correlation implementations, and a hadron cascade stage. It directly addresses the isobar collision experiment performed at RHIC in 2018, initially suggested by Voloshin (2010), which compares RuRu and ZrZr systems to detect CME. The joint event selection strategy builds on earlier work by Shi et al. (2018) addressing concerns about nucleon distribution uncertainties raised by Xu et al. (2017) and Hammelmann et al. (2019). The results enable experimental validation of CME signatures through comparison of event-plane and reaction-plane measurements, event-shape dependence studies, and R-correlator analysis, potentially providing the first observation of CME in a subatomic chiral material and evidence for QCD chiral symmetry restoration.
