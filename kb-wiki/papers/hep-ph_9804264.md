# Production of relativistic positronium in collisions of photons and electrons with nuclei and atoms

> We consider the production of ultrarelativistic positronium (Ps) in $γA \to Ps + A$ and $e A \to Ps + e A$ processes where $A$ is an atom or a nucleus with charge $Ze$. For the photoproduction of para- and ortho-Ps and the electroproduction of para-Ps we obtain the most complete description compared with previous works. It includes high order $Z α$ corrections and polarization effects. The accuracy of the obtained cross sections is determined by omitted terms of the order of the inverse Ps Lorentz factor squared. The studied high order multi-photon electroproduction of ortho-Ps dominates for the collision of electrons with heavy atoms over the bremsstrahlung production from the electron via a virtual photon proposed by Holvik and Olsen. Our results complete and correct the studies of those authors.

## Key claim

The paper provides the most complete description to date of ultrarelativistic positronium photoproduction and electroproduction, including high-order Zα corrections and polarization effects, with an accuracy limited only by omitted terms of order the inverse Ps Lorentz factor squared. The authors claim that a new multi-photon mechanism for ortho-Ps electroproduction dominates over the previously considered bremsstrahlung mechanism for heavy atoms, thereby completing and correcting the studies of Holvik and Olsen.

## Method

The authors calculate matrix elements for virtual photon-nucleus scattering to produce para- and ortho-positronium, using the known amplitudes for e⁺e⁻ pair production in a nuclear field and substituting the positronium wave function at the origin. They then integrate these matrix elements over phase space to obtain differential and total cross sections for photoproduction (real photons) and electroproduction (virtual photons from electron projectiles), including screening effects via a Thomas-Fermi-Molière atomic form factor and summing the full series in ν = Zα.

## Key numbers

- Para-Ps photoproduction total cross section on nuclei: σ_singlet = σ₀ ζ(3) [ln(ω/mₑ) − 1 − C(ν)], where σ₀ = π ν² α⁴ / mₑ² and C(ν) ≈ 2.9798 ν² for small ν
- Ortho-Ps photoproduction total cross section: σ_triplet = 4 (Zα)² σ₀ ζ(3) B(ν), with B(ν) ≈ 0.6137 − 1.0729 ν² for ν ≪ 1
- High-order Zα effects decrease ortho-Ps photoproduction cross section by 3.61% for Ca, 40.5% for Pb, and 46.5% for U nuclei
- Ratio σ_triplet/σ_singlet at high energies (ω ≫ 222 mₑ/Z¹/³): 1.51% for Ca, 23.5% for Pb, 28.5% for U
- Para-Ps electroproduction spectrum: the Holvik-Olsen approximation exceeds the correct result by up to 30% for Pb and 40% for U in the x < 0.4 region
- Ortho-Ps multi-photon production total cross section: σ_MP ≈ (4α/π) (Zα)² σ₀ ζ(3) B(ν) I_MP, where I_MP ≈ (ln(Eₑ/mₑ))² − 2 ln(Eₑ/mₑ) − 0.3949
- Ortho-Ps bremsstrahlung production total cross section for complete screening: I_br = 0.303 ln(111/Z¹/³) + 0.362

## Context

This work extends and corrects earlier calculations of positronium production by Holvik and Olsen (1994) and by Anisimov and Bystritsky (1984), who neglected high-order Zα corrections and the multi-photon mechanism for ortho-Ps electroproduction. The results also improve upon the photoproduction calculations of Olsen (1983) and of Ternov and Chizhov (1994) by including polarization effects and a complete treatment of screening. The paper enables more accurate predictions for relativistic positronium beam production at high-energy accelerators and provides a foundation for studying dimuonium production, which requires different treatment due to nuclear form factor effects.
