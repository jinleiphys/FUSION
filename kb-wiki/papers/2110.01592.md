# Proton inelastic scattering reveals deformation in $^8$He

> A measurement of proton inelastic scattering of $^8$He at $8.25A$~MeV at TRIUMF shows a resonance at 3.54(6)~MeV with a width of 0.89(11)~MeV. The energy of the state is in good agreement with coupled cluster and no-core shell model with continuum calculations, with the latter successfully describing the measured resonance width as well. Its differential cross section analyzed with phenomenological collective excitation form factor and microscopic coupled reaction channels framework consistently reveals a large deformation parameter $β_2$ = 0.40(3), consistent with no-core shell model predictions of a large neutron deformation. This deformed double-closed shell at the neutron drip-line opens a new paradigm.

## Key claim
Proton inelastic scattering of $^8$He at 8.25$A$ MeV reveals a resonance at 3.54(6) MeV with width 0.89(11) MeV, and the differential cross section analysis yields a large quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.40(3)$, indicating that the $N=6$ sub-shell closure at the neutron drip-line is deformed rather than spherical.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the IRIS facility at TRIUMF using a $^8$He beam at 8.25$A$ MeV impinging on a solid hydrogen target. Scattered protons and $^6$He recoils were detected in coincidence using silicon strip detector telescopes, and the excitation energy spectrum was reconstructed via the missing mass technique. The resonance parameters were extracted by fitting the spectrum with a Voigt function plus simulated non-resonant background from $p+^8$He $\rightarrow p+^6$He+$n+n$ and $p+^8$He $\rightarrow p+^7$He+$n$ channels. Differential cross sections were analyzed with coupled-channel (CC) and distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations using collective form factors, and with microscopic coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculations using no-core shell model (NCSM) densities.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy: $E^* = 3.54(6)$ MeV
- Resonance width: $\Gamma = 0.89(11)$ MeV (FWHM)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter: $\beta_2 = 0.40(3)$
- Deformation length from collective model fit: $\delta_2^{ex} = 1.24$–$1.40$ fm
- Deformation length from NCSM transition density in CRC: $\delta_2 = 1.39$ fm
- Excitation energy resolution: 0.15 MeV ($\sigma$) at 3.5 MeV
- NCSMC extrapolated $2^+_1$ excitation energy: 3.58(6) MeV
- NCSMC calculated $2^+_1$ width: 750(50) keV
- NCSM neutron quadrupole moment for $2^+_1$ state: $Q_n = 6.15$ $e$fm$^2$
- NCSM proton quadrupole moment for $2^+_1$ state: $Q_p = 0.60$ $e$fm$^2$

## Context
This work resolves inconsistencies among previous measurements of the first excited state in $^8$He, which had been reported at varying energies and widths from inelastic proton scattering at 72$A$ MeV (Korsheninnikov 1993), Coulomb excitation and fragmentation at 227$A$ MeV (Meister 2002; Markenroth 2001), and transfer reactions (Golovkov 2009; Fomichev 2009). The high-statistics, high-resolution data confirm a $2^+$ assignment and rule out a low-energy dipole resonance suggested by breakup experiments. The large deformation parameter $\beta_2 = 0.40(3)$ is comparable to that of heavier $N=6$ isotones ($^{10}$Be: 1.14(6); $^{12}$C: 0.582(24)) and is supported by ab initio coupled-cluster and NCSMC calculations. This result opens the question of whether deformation persists in other neutron-rich nuclei with closed sub-shells, such as neutron-rich Ca isotopes.
