# System Response Kernel Calculation for List-mode Reconstruction in Strip PET Detector

> Reconstruction of the image in Positron Emission Tomographs (PET) requires the knowledge of the system response kernel which describes the contribution of each pixel (voxel) to each tube of response (TOR). This is especially important in list-mode reconstruction systems, where an efficient analytical approximation of such function is required. In this contribution, we present a derivation of the system response kernel for a novel 2D strip PET.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical approximation for the system response kernel for a 2D strip PET detector using a saddle-point (Gaussian) approximation, but the derivation assumes negligible scintillator thickness and a weakly position-dependent correlation matrix, which are simplifications that may not hold in the final detector.

## Method

The authors model a 2D PET detector as two parallel scintillator strips of length L separated by distance 2R, with position estimates obtained from time-of-flight measurements. They assume normally distributed measurement errors with a correlation matrix C, and derive the system response kernel P(tev|i) by integrating over emission angle θ using a saddle-point approximation, truncating the expansion of the error vector to quadratic order. The final kernel is evaluated at the center of each pixel (yi, zi) and normalized by the pixel sensitivity s(yi, zi).

## Key numbers

- Effective speed of light in the scintillator: approximately 1.3 × 10^8 m/s
- Estimated position resolution: σz ≈ 10 mm
- Estimated difference-of-distances resolution: σΔl ≈ 63 mm
- Detector geometry: two parallel strips of length L at distance 2R, with R = 300 mm used in validation
- Validation performed for events at y = 300 mm with angles 0° and 45°
- Three-sigma ellipse (R = 3) used to define the region of interest for validation

## Context

This work extends the list-mode likelihood maximization approach of Shepp and Vardi (1982) and the list-mode EM algorithm to a novel strip PET detector design using plastic scintillators with time-of-flight measurement, as described in prior work by the group (Moskal et al., 2011; Strip PET collaboration). The derived kernel provides a starting point for further approximations once the detector geometry is fixed, and the authors note that incorporating finite scintillator thickness (up to 20 mm) leads to non-Gaussian errors, which is under ongoing investigation.
