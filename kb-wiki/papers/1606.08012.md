# Yang-Mills correlators across the deconfinement phase transition

> We compute the finite temperature ghost and gluon propagators of Yang-Mills theory in the Landau-DeWitt gauge. The background field that enters the definition of the latter is intimately related with the (gauge-invariant) Polyakov loop and serves as an equivalent order parameter for the deconfinement transition. We use an effective gauge-fixed description where the nonperturbative infrared dynamics of the theory is parametrized by a gluon mass which, as argued elsewhere, may originate from the Gribov ambiguity. In this scheme, one can perform consistent perturbative calculations down to infrared momenta, which have been shown to correctly describe the phase diagram of Yang-Mills theories in four dimensions as well as the zero-temperature correlators computed in lattice simulations. In this article, we provide the one-loop expressions of the finite temperature Landau-DeWitt ghost and gluon propagators for a large class of gauge groups and present explicit results for the SU(2) case. These are substantially different from those previously obtained in the Landau gauge, which corresponds to a vanishing background field. The nonanalyticity of the order parameter across the transition is directly imprinted onto the propagators in the various color modes. In the SU(2) case, this leads, for instance, to a cusp in the electric and magnetic gluon susceptibilities as well as similar signatures in the ghost sector. We mention the possibility that such distinctive features of the transition could be measured in lattice simulations in the background field gauge studied here.

## Key claim

The one-loop ghost and gluon propagators in the Landau-DeWitt gauge show a clear nonanalytic behavior across the deconfinement phase transition, unlike the same correlators computed in the standard Landau gauge (which corresponds to a vanishing background field), because the background field order parameter directly imprints the transition onto the propagators in the various color modes.

## Method

The authors compute the finite-temperature ghost and gluon self-energies at one-loop order in the massive extension of the Landau-DeWitt gauge, using a canonical (Cartan-Weyl) color basis where the background covariant derivative is diagonal. They give general expressions for a large class of gauge groups and present explicit numerical results for the SU(2) case, using the two-loop background field potential from Reinosa et al. (2014) to determine the temperature-dependent background field.

## Key numbers

* The transition temperature for SU(2) Yang-Mills theory obtained from the two-loop background field potential agrees well with known lattice results (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
* The background field takes nonzero values below and above the transition temperature, and the vanishing background field (Landau gauge) is never a minimum of the background potential in the relevant range of temperatures.
* In the SU(2) case, the nonanalyticity of the order parameter across the transition leads to a cusp in the electric and magnetic gluon susceptibilities as well as similar signatures in the ghost sector.

## Context

This work extends the massive Landau-DeWitt gauge approach of Reinosa et al. (2014, 2015) to the calculation of two-point correlators, complementing previous calculations of the background field potential and the phase diagram. It contrasts with lattice calculations in the Landau gauge by Cucchieri et al. (2011) and Mendes et al. (2014), which found no clear sign of the transition in the ghost or magnetic gluon propagators, and with continuum calculations by Fischer et al. (2012) and Reinosa et al. (2013) that found only a slight nonmonotonous behavior of the electric susceptibility. The results suggest that lattice simulations in the background field gauge studied here could measure these distinctive signatures of the deconfinement transition.
