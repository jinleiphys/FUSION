# Vacuum creation of quarks at the time scale of QGP thermalization and strangeness enhancement in heavy-ion collisions

> The vacuum parton creation in quickly varying external fields is studied at the time scale of order 1 fm/$c$ typical for the quark-gluon plasma formation and thermalization. To describe the pre-equilibrium evolution of the system the transport kinetic equation is employed. It is shown that the dynamics of production process at times comparable with particle inverse masses can deviate considerably from that based on classical Schwinger-like estimates for homogeneous and constant fields. One of the effects caused by non-stationary chromoelectric fields is the enhancement of the yield of $s\bar{s}$ quark pairs. Dependence of this effect on the shape and duration of the field pulse is studied together with the influence of string fusion and reduction of quark masses.

# Vacuum creation of quarks at the time scale of QGP thermalization and strangeness enhancement in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The dynamics of vacuum quark pair production in time-dependent chromoelectric fields at time scales of order 1 fm/c (comparable to particle inverse masses) can deviate considerably from classical Schwinger-like estimates for homogeneous constant fields, leading to enhancement of strange quark pair yields by at least a factor of 1.5 relative to the stationary-field prediction, provided the field pulse duration is short enough.

## Method

The authors employ a kinetic transport equation with a source term derived from the Dirac equation via canonical Bogoliubov transformation, describing vacuum fermion pair creation in a spatially homogeneous, time-dependent electric field in the collisionless (no rescattering) approximation. The system of three ordinary differential equations for the distribution function and two auxiliary functions is solved numerically on a two-dimensional momentum grid (typical step 0.1m, boundary 15-20m) for a soliton-like pulse and a family of Gaussian-like pulses with variable steepness exponent n. The back reaction of produced particles on the field is included via the Maxwell equation with a regularized current density.

## Key numbers

- For the soliton-like field pulse with width τ = 1 fm/c, the asymptotic strangeness suppression factor f_s is at least 1.5 times larger than the constant-field Schwinger value of γ_s = 0.3.
- For τ ≥ 3 fm/c, the time-dependent result approaches the Schwinger formula value γ_s = 0.3.
- The intermediate (time-dependent) strangeness suppression factor varies from about 0.6 to 0.39 for the soliton-like pulse and from 0.75 to 0.52 for the rectangular-like pulse (n=6) with τ = 1 fm/c.
- Input quark masses: m_s = 350 MeV, m_{u,d} = 230 MeV; string tension κ = 0.9 GeV/fm.
- Initial energy density estimates: 50 GeV/fm³ at RHIC, 520 GeV/fm³ at LHC, corresponding to effective charges e ≈ 0.22 and e ≈ 0.07 respectively.
- Reducing quark masses to m_s = 0.23 GeV and m_q = 0.01 GeV (current quark masses) increases the constant-field γ_s from 0.3 to 0.4, equivalent to increasing string tension from 0.9 GeV/fm to 1.22 GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the chromoelectric flux tube (string) model of Casher, Neuberger, and Nussinov (1979) and the Schwinger mechanism for pair production in constant fields to the realistic time-dependent fields expected in heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the long-standing underestimation of strangeness yield in microscopic string models (noted by several authors in the late 1990s and early 2000s), which is important because enhanced strangeness was predicted by Rafelski (1991) as a signal of quark-gluon plasma formation. The results enable more quantitative modeling of the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and suggest that the time-dependent field dynamics should be incorporated consistently into color flux tube models for accurate description of parton production.
