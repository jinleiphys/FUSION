# The collectivity of transverse momentum fluctuations

> We study the observable $v_0(p_T)$, which quantifies the relative change of $p_T$ spectra induced by event-by-event density fluctuations in the medium created in heavy-ion collisions. This quantity provides a direct measure of radial flow and serves as a probe of collectivity, complementing anisotropic flow coefficients. Using hydrodynamic model calculations, we predict the behavior of $v_0(p_T)$ and show that the scaled quantity $v_0(p_T)/v_0$ exhibits very little dependence on centrality and transport coefficients. We further find that the apparent influence of transport coefficients$-$particularly bulk viscosity$-$ on $v_0(p_T)$ largely originates from modifications of the event-averaged mean transverse momentum, $\langle p_T \rangle$. By expressing $v_0(p_T)/v_0$ as a function of $p_T/\langle p_T \rangle$, the genuine sensitivity of $v_0(p_T)$ to transport coefficients can be isolated. Moreover, since $v_0(p_T)$ is the $p_T$-differential measure of event-by-event $[p_T]$ fluctuations, it naturally explains the observed $p_T$-cut dependence of $σ_{p_T}$ measured by ATLAS collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the scaled observable \(v_0(p_T)/v_0\), when expressed as a function of \(p_T/\langle p_T \rangle\), is largely independent of centrality and transport coefficients, but a residual influence of bulk viscosity remains visible at higher \(p_T\) even after this rescaling.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, comparing standard fluctuating initial conditions (TRENTo) with smooth initial conditions obtained by averaging over multiple events. They extracted \(v_0(p_T)/v_0\) from both approaches and also computed it from smooth initial conditions with a 5% increase in entropy normalization to isolate the effect of temperature fluctuations. The results were compared with experimental data from the ATLAS collaboration on the \(p_T\)-cut dependence of \(\sigma_{p_T}\).

## Key numbers

- \(v_0(p_T)/v_0\) exhibits a sign change: negative at lower \(p_T\) and positive at higher \(p_T\), reflecting anti-correlation between the \(p_T\) spectrum and \([p_T]\) at low \(p_T\) and positive correlation at high \(p_T\).
- Hydrodynamic simulations were performed for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV at impact parameters \(b=0\) fm and \(b=7\) fm.
- The smooth initial condition results were obtained by increasing entropy normalization by 5% to produce a higher initial temperature.
- The quantity \(c_k = \sigma_{p_T}^2\) was plotted as a function of multiplicity (\(N_{ch}\)) for three different \(p_T\) cuts in ATLAS data.
- The ratio \(\Delta p_T / \langle p_T \rangle\) was presented for 0–1% central collisions with two different \(p_T\) cuts: \(0.5 < p_T < 2\) GeV/c (used as input) and \(0.5 < p_T < 5\) GeV/c (predicted).

## Context

This work extends the concept of size-flow transmutation introduced by Bozek (2017) and builds on the observable \(v_0(p_T)\) defined by Schenke, Shen, and Teaney (2020). It complements studies of anisotropic flow coefficients such as elliptic flow, and the scaling behavior of \(v_0(p_T)/v_0\) mirrors the scaling observed in \(v_n(p_T)/v_n\) reported by ATLAS (2018). The paper demonstrates that \(v_0(p_T)\) can explain the \(p_T\)-cut dependence of \(\sigma_{p_T}\) measured by ATLAS (2019, 2024), and the mass ordering in identified hadrons resembles that seen in other flow coefficients as reported by ALICE (2022, 2025). Future work could explore \(v_0(p_T)\) in small systems to probe collective dynamics and investigate meson–baryon splitting at higher \(p_T\).
