# Scaling p_T distributions for p and \bar{p} produced in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> With the experimental data from STAR and PHENIX on the centrality dependence of the $p_T$ spectra of protons and anti-protons produced at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, we show that for protons and anti-protons there exists a scaling distribution independent of the colliding centrality. The scaling functions can also describe data from BRAHMS for both proton and anti-proton spectra at $y=2.2$ and 3.2. The scaling behaviors are shown to be incompatible with the usual string fragmentation scenario for particle production.

## Key claim

For protons and anti-protons produced in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the transverse momentum spectra at different centralities can be collapsed onto a single scaling curve via a linear transformation, but this scaling behavior is incompatible with the usual string fragmentation scenario because the required scaling parameters differ between pions and (anti)protons.

## Method

The authors defined a scaled variable \(z = p_T / K\) and a scaled spectrum \(\Phi(z) = A \, d^2N/(2\pi p_T dp_T dy)\) with free parameters \(K\) and \(A\), setting \(K = A = 1\) for the most central collisions. They then adjusted \(K\) and \(A\) for other centrality bins to coalesce all data points onto a single curve, using experimental data from STAR and PHENIX at mid-rapidity and from BRAHMS at forward rapidities \(y = 2.2\) and \(3.2\). A normalized scaling function \(\Psi(u)\) was also constructed using the variable \(u = p_T / \langle p_T \rangle\) to remove the dependence on the arbitrary choice of reference centrality.

## Key numbers

- The scaling function for protons at mid-rapidity is parameterized as \(\Phi_p(z) = 0.052 \exp(14.9v - 16.2v^2 + 3.3v^3)\) with \(v = \ln(1+z)\).
- The scaling function for anti-protons at mid-rapidity is \(\Phi_{\bar p}(z) = 0.16 \exp(13v - 14.9v^2 + 2.9v^3)\).
- The ratio of experimental data to the fitted scaling curve for protons lies within 0.7 to 1.3, corresponding to an accuracy of 30% over about six orders of magnitude in the spectrum.
- The mean scaled variable \(\langle z \rangle\) is 1.14 for protons and 1.08 for anti-protons, integrated over \(z\) from 0 to 12.
- For the ratio of moments \(\langle p_T^n \rangle / \langle p_T \rangle^n\): for \(n=2\), protons give 1.194 and anti-protons give 1.215; for \(n=6\), protons give 19.045 and anti-protons give 17.253.
- The ratio \(\Psi_p(u)/\Psi_\pi(u)\) reaches a maximum of about 1.6 near \(u \approx 1\).

## Context

This work extends the scaling behavior previously found for pions in Au+Au collisions at RHIC by Hwa and Yang (2005) and Yang (2006) to protons and anti-protons, using the same methodology of linear variable transformation. The scaling functions for protons and anti-protons differ from each other only slightly, but differ substantially from the pion scaling function, which the authors interpret as evidence against a universal string fragmentation mechanism. The results motivate further studies of particle production mechanisms such as quark recombination models, and provide testable predictions for the centrality- and rapidity-independence of moment ratios \(\langle p_T^n \rangle / \langle p_T \rangle^n\).
