# Chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions at large baryon densities

> We argue that the chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions at high baryon density is not associated to a phase transition or rapid crossover. We employ the linear nucleon-meson model with parameters fixed by the zero-temperature properties of nuclear matter close to the liquid-gas quantum phase transition. For the parameter region of interest this yields a reliable picture of the thermodynamic and chiral properties at non-zero temperature. The chemical freeze-out observed in low-energy experiments occurs when baryon densities fall below a critical value of about 15 percent of nuclear density. This region in the phase diagram is far away from any phase transition or rapid crossover.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out observed in low-energy heavy ion collisions at high baryon density is not associated with a phase transition or rapid crossover, contrary to the situation at low baryon density. Instead, freeze-out occurs when baryon densities fall below a critical value of about 15 percent of nuclear density, a region far from any phase transition in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors employ the linear nucleon-meson model with parameters fixed by zero-temperature properties of nuclear matter near the liquid-gas quantum phase transition. They compute the effective potential for constant scalar and vector meson fields using a relativistic mean field approximation, treating nucleons as a free gas with field-dependent masses, and minimize with respect to the chiral order parameter and vector meson expectation value to obtain thermodynamic quantities at non-zero temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out occurs when baryon densities fall below about 15 percent of nuclear saturation density (nuclear saturation density = 0.153/fm³)
- For a chemical potential μ = 760 ± 22.8 MeV, the measured freeze-out temperature is T_ch = 56^{+9.6}_{-2.0} MeV
- The critical chemical potential for the gas-liquid phase transition at zero temperature is μ_c = 922.7 MeV (from nuclear binding energy 16.3 MeV subtracted from nucleon mass 939 MeV)
- The critical endpoint of the first-order gas-liquid transition occurs at T_* = 20.7 MeV and μ = 901 MeV
- The chiral order parameter at the nuclear matter phase transition is σ_nucl = 69.8 MeV
- The Yukawa coupling h = 10 (from h f_π = nucleon mass 939 MeV with f_π = 93 MeV)
- The vector meson coupling g = 9.5, with ω_0,nuc = -18 MeV at the phase transition

## Context

This work extends the discussion of chemical freeze-out from low baryon density (where it coincides with the QCD phase transition temperature, as argued by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, and Wambach) to high baryon density. The authors contrast their conclusion with proposals connecting freeze-out to a transition to quarkyonic matter (as suggested by Baym and Lee). The results enable a reinterpretation of freeze-out data from low-energy experiments (AGS energies and below) as reflecting a constant baryon density condition rather than a phase transition line, and the authors note that previous parameterizations by Randrup and Cleymans overestimated baryon number densities at freeze-out.
