# Hadron tomography and its application to gravitational radii of hadrons

> Hadron tomography has been investigated by three-dimensional structure functions, such as generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). The GDAs are $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs, and both functions probe gravitational form factors for hadrons. We determined the pion GDAs by analyzing Belle data on the differential cross section for the two-photon process $γ^* γ\to π^0 π^0$. From the determined GDAs, we calculated timelike gravitational form factors of the pion and they were converted to the spacelike form factors by using the dispersion relation. These gravitational form factors $Θ_1$ and $Θ_2$ indicate mechanical (pressure, shear force) and gravitational-mass (or energy) distributions, respectively. Then, gravitational radii are calculated for the pion from the form factors, and they are compared with the pion charge radius. We explain that the new field of gravitational physics can be developed in the microscopic level of quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The authors determine the pion's gravitational form factors and radii from an analysis of Belle experimental data on the two-photon process γ*γ → π⁰π⁰, finding that the gravitational-mass radius is similar to or slightly smaller than the charge radius while the mechanical radius is significantly larger, though the results carry ambiguities from phase-factor assignment.

## Method

The authors performed a χ² analysis of Belle differential cross-section data for γ*γ → π⁰π⁰ to determine the pion generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), parameterizing the GDAs with S-wave and D-wave contributions including the f₀(500) and f₂(1270) resonances plus a continuum term. From the extracted GDAs, they calculated the timelike gravitational form factors Θ₁ and Θ₂ of the pion, then converted them to spacelike form factors using dispersion relations. Finally, they obtained spatial densities and root-mean-square radii by Fourier transformation of the spacelike form factors.

## Key numbers

- Gravitational-mass radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mass = 0.69 fm (range 0.56–0.69 fm considering phase-factor ambiguities)
- Mechanical radius of the pion: √⟨r²⟩_mech = 1.45 fm (range 1.45–1.56 fm considering phase-factor ambiguities)
- Pion charge radius for comparison: √⟨r²⟩_charge = 0.672 ± 0.008 fm (from Particle Data Group 2016)
- Q² values of Belle data used in analysis: 8.92, 13.37, 17.23, and 24.25 GeV²
- Resonance masses considered: f₀(500) and f₂(1270)

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron tomography from generalized parton distributions (GPDs) to generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), which are s-t crossed quantities of GPDs and probe gravitational form factors. The analysis uses Belle collaboration data (Masuda et al., 2015) on γ*γ → π⁰π⁰, providing the first determination of gravitational radii from actual experimental measurements. This enables a new field of gravitational physics at the microscopic quark and gluon level, where gravitational interactions are normally too weak to study in particle scattering experiments.
