# Low-energy pi pi photoproduction off nuclei

> In the present paper we investigate pi0 pi0 and pi(+/-)pi0 photoproduction off complex nuclei at incident beam energies of 400-460 MeV. Simulations of two pion photoproduction on protons and nuclei are performed by means of a semi-classical BUU transport model including a full coupled-channel treatment of the final state interactions. Elastic scattering of the final state pions with the nucleons in the surrounding nuclear medium is found to yield a downward shift of the pi pi invariant mass distribution. We show that the target mass dependence of the pi0 pi0 invariant mass spectrum as measured by the TAPS collaboration can be explained without introducing medium effects beyond absorption and quasi-elastic scattering of the final state particles. On the other hand, we find considerable discrepancies with the data in the pi(+/-)pi0 channel, which are not understood.

# Low-energy ππ photoproduction off nuclei

## Key claim
The observed downward shift and target-mass dependence of the π⁰π⁰ invariant mass spectrum in photoproduction off nuclei can be explained entirely by conventional final-state interactions (elastic scattering, absorption, and charge exchange) without invoking in-medium modification of the ππ interaction or partial restoration of chiral symmetry. However, the model fails to reproduce the π±π⁰ data, producing cross sections considerably higher than measured, and this discrepancy remains unexplained.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of two-pion photoproduction on protons and nuclei using a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a full coupled-channel treatment of final-state interactions. The elementary γN → ππN amplitudes were taken from the model of Nacher et al. (2000), and final-state interactions included pion absorption via the optical potential of Nieves et al. (1993) and Garcia-Recio et al. (1991), elastic scattering using vacuum πN cross sections from the SAID analysis, and charge-exchange reactions assuming isospin symmetry. Baryonic resonances were not explicitly propagated, and pions moved along straight-line trajectories between collisions.

## Key numbers
- Incident beam energies: 400–460 MeV
- Pion kinetic energy threshold for the optical potential: Tπ = 85 MeV (low-energy potential below this value, Δ-resonance region above)
- Mean single-pion momenta in the relevant kinematics: 100–150 MeV
- Contribution from single-pion photoproduction via inelastic πN → ππN collisions: approximately 5% correction to total cross sections (omitted from calculations)
- Pion absorption cross sections described down to energies as low as 30 MeV (Oliver, unpublished)

## Context
This work extends the study of two-pion photoproduction off nuclei previously performed by Roca et al. (2002) and Oset et al. (2001), who used a many-body approach with chiral-unitary dynamics and found evidence for in-medium modification of the σ meson. The present authors argue that those earlier calculations treated final-state interactions as purely absorptive via Glauber-type damping, omitting elastic scattering and charge exchange. The current results show that conventional final-state interactions can reproduce the π⁰π⁰ data without medium effects, but the persistent discrepancy in the π±π⁰ channel (also seen in the TAPS data of Messchendorp et al., 2002) remains an open problem requiring further experimental and theoretical work.
