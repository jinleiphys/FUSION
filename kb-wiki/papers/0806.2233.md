# QCD at finite chemical potential with six time slices

> We investigate the Taylor expansion of the baryon number susceptibility, and hence, pressure, in a series in the baryon chemical potential (mu_B) through a lattice simulation with light dynamical staggered quarks at a finer lattice cutoff a=1/6T. We determine the QCD cross over coupling at mu_B=0. We find the radius of convergence of the series at various temeperatures, and bound the location of the QCD critical point to be T^E/T_c = 0.94 and mu_B^E/T < 1.8. We also investigate the extrapolation of various susceptibilities and linkages to finite chemical potential.

## Key claim

Using a Taylor expansion of the baryon number susceptibility from lattice QCD simulations with six time slices (lattice cutoff a=1/6T), the authors bound the QCD critical endpoint to T^E/T_c = 0.94 and mu_B^E/T < 1.8, with the caveat that this result is obtained at finite spatial volume (L=4/T) and has not been extrapolated to the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-flavor QCD with dynamical staggered quarks at a lattice spacing a=1/6T, using the R-algorithm for hybrid molecular dynamics with step size delta t=0.01. They computed Taylor expansion coefficients (non-linear susceptibilities) of the pressure and baryon number susceptibility up to eighth order in the baryon chemical potential mu_B, using stochastic estimators with 500 random vectors per trace evaluation. The radius of convergence of the series was estimated from ratios of successive coefficients, and Padé approximants were used to extrapolate susceptibilities to finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling identified at beta_c = 5.425 ± 0.005 from the peak of the Polyakov loop susceptibility
- Quark mass tuned to m/T_c = 0.1, corresponding to m_pi/m_rho ≈ 0.3
- At T/T_c = 0.94 ± 0.01, the radius of convergence is mu_B^*/T = 1.8 ± 0.1, giving the critical endpoint estimate T^E/T_c = 0.94 ± 0.01 and mu_B^E/T^E = 1.8 ± 0.1
- At T_c on the largest volume (6×24^3), chi_20 gives a signal at 53 sigma, chi_40 at 23 sigma, chi_60 at 5 sigma, and chi_80 at 3 sigma
- The longest autocorrelation time at beta_c was tau_int ≈ 267 trajectories for trajectory length T=1, reducing to 36 for T=3

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies at finite chemical potential using Taylor expansions, specifically the coarser lattice (a=1/4T) results of Gavai and Gupta (2005) on 4×16^3 lattices, where the critical endpoint estimate was mu_B^E/T^E = 1.3 ± 0.3 at the same quark mass and spatial volume. The new result at finer lattice spacing shows a statistically significant 26% increase in the estimated mu_B^E/T^E. The study also investigates linkages between quantum numbers, which could provide insight into the nature of the crossover and possible quarkyonic matter at large N_c as discussed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The results enable improved estimates of baryon number fluctuations relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments searching for the QCD critical point.
