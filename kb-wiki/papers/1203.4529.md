# Baryon number conservation and the cumulants of the net proton distribution

> We discuss the modification of the cumulants of the net baryon and net proton distributions due to the global conservation of baryon number in heavy-ion collisions. Corresponding probability distributions and their cumulants are derived analytically. We show that the conservation of baryon number results in a substantial decrease of higher order cumulants. Based on our studies, we propose an observable that is insensitive to the modifications due to baryon number conservation.

## Key claim

Global conservation of baryon number in heavy-ion collisions substantially suppresses higher-order cumulants of net baryon and net proton distributions compared to the Skellam (Poisson) baseline, and the authors propose an observable \(D\) that is exactly zero under baryon-number conservation alone, so that any deviation from zero signals additional dynamical correlations (e.g., from a critical point or crossover).

## Method

The authors start with independent Poisson distributions for baryons and anti-baryons in full phase space, then model finite experimental acceptance by a binomial distribution with probability \(p\) for observing a baryon (or anti-baryon). They impose exact global baryon-number conservation by summing over unobserved particles, using Graf’s addition formula for Bessel functions to obtain an analytic probability distribution \(P_B(n)\) and its cumulant generating function. Cumulants up to sixth order are derived in closed form for the case \(p_B = p_{\bar{B}} = p\), and the results are extended to net protons by reinterpreting the binomial probability as the fraction of observed protons among all baryons.

## Key numbers

* For STAR Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the fraction of measured protons to total baryons is estimated as \(p \approx 0.013\); with \(B=350\) this gives \(R_{4,2} \approx 0.95\) and \(R_{6,2} \approx 0.77\).
* At low energies where \(\langle N_B \rangle \gg \langle N_{\bar{B}} \rangle\) and \(z \ll B\), taking \(\langle n_p \rangle = 9\) and \(\langle N_B \rangle = 350\) gives \(p \approx 0.026\), leading to \(R_{4,2} \approx 0.85\) and \(R_{6,2} \approx 0.32\).
* For the example parameters \(B=300\), \(\langle N_B \rangle = 400\), \(\langle N_{\bar{B}} \rangle = 100\), the odd-order cumulant ratios \(R_{3,1}\) and \(R_{5,1}\) are shown as functions of \(p\) in Figures 1 and 2; for the Skellam distribution these ratios equal unity.
* The observable \(D\) (Eq. ## Context

This work extends earlier studies of global charge conservation on variances (Bleicher (2000), Begun (2004)) to higher-order cumulants, which are more sensitive to critical fluctuations. It provides an improved baseline for the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model in the canonical ensemble (Cleymans (1990)) by incorporating baryon-number conservation analytically. The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical end point and crossover by comparing measured cumulant ratios to this baseline; the proposed observable \(D\) is designed to isolate dynamical correlations beyond global conservation, complementing the beam energy scan program at RHIC (Aggarwal (2010)) and earlier NA49 measurements (Alt (2008)).
