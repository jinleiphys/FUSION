# Particle Interferometry: New Theoretical Results

> By measuring hadronic single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions, the size and dynamical state of the collision fireball at freeze-out can be reconstructed. I discuss the relevant theoretical methods and their limitations. By applying the formalism to recent pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN we demonstrate that the collision zone has undergone strong transverse growth before freeze-out (by a factor 2-3 in each direction), and that it expands both longitudinally and transversally. From the thermal and flow energy density at freeze-out the energy density at the onset of transverse expansion can be estimated from conservation laws. It comfortably exceeds the critical value for the transition to color deconfined matter.

## Key claim

By applying the formalism to recent pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN, the collision zone is shown to have undergone strong transverse growth before freeze-out (by a factor 2-3 in each direction), and the energy density at the onset of transverse expansion comfortably exceeds the critical value for the transition to color deconfined matter, though the extraction relies on model assumptions and conservation laws rather than direct measurement.

## Method

The author derives the theoretical framework linking the emission function (a Wigner transform of the source current density matrix) to single-particle spectra and two-particle correlation functions via Fourier transforms, then applies a Gaussian approximation to the emission function that retains only its second central space-time moments. The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization is used to separate spatial and temporal source aspects, and the formalism is applied to pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN to extract source sizes and expansion dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Transverse growth of the collision zone before freeze-out: a factor of 2-3 in each direction, extracted from pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN.
- The energy density at the onset of transverse expansion, estimated from conservation laws using thermal and flow energy density at freeze-out, comfortably exceeds the critical value for the transition to color deconfined matter (no specific numerical critical value given in the text).
- The Compton wavelength of pions at rest (1.4 fm) is noted as comfortably smaller than any typical nuclear source size, justifying the on-shell approximation for the emission function.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadronic single-particle spectra from heavy-ion collisions at AGS and SPS energies, which had previously been interpreted using a model of locally thermalized momentum distributions with collective hydrodynamical expansion (Heinz 1989, Stachel 1994). The paper addresses the ambiguity of single-particle spectra by incorporating two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations (HBT interferometry) to directly access space-time structure, building on earlier theoretical work by Pratt (1984), Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988), and Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995). The results enable more model-independent extraction of transverse flow and freeze-out conditions in future high-statistics measurements with pions and kaons.
