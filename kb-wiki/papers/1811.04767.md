# A Hole torn by QCD Fields

> In this paper we determine that a local negative correlation(the Hole) in $Δη$ $Δϕ$ space of Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV is caused by strong color QCD electric and magnetic fields which are present in the Glasma Flux Tubes that are generated by the initial conditions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a local negative correlation (the "Hole") observed in $\Delta\eta$ $\Delta\phi$ space for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV is caused by strong color QCD electric and magnetic fields present in Glasma Flux Tubes, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the surface flux tubes retain a significant fraction of the initial longitudinal color fields after being pushed by the blast wave, which the author describes as "a speculation of this paper for measuring strong CP violation."

## Method

The author uses a Glasma Flux Tube Model (GFTM) where twelve flux tubes form a ring on the surface of the fireball, each tube containing 3-4 partons that fragment using PYTHIA fragmentation functions. Two-particle correlation functions in $\Delta\phi$ versus $\Delta\eta$ space are constructed from the ratio of same-event pairs to mixed-event pairs, with multiplicity scaling applied across 28 independent $p_t$ bin combinations. For charge-dependent correlations, the signal from a single flux tube is isolated by subtracting the background (all pairs except those from the same tube) from the total pair distribution, and the color electric and magnetic field effects are simulated by adding 100 MeV/c boosts to quarks in opposite directions along the beam axis (electric) or in transverse momentum (magnetic).

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum $Q_s$ is around 1 GeV/c, giving a flux tube transverse size of about 1/4 fm
- The $\phi$ angle wedge of a flux tube is approximately 1/20 radians or about $3^\circ$
- On average there are 12 final state tubes on the surface of the fireball per central Au+Au collision
- Each tube on average showers into 49 charged particles; soft uncorrelated particles account for 873 or 60% of charged particles
- Approximately one half of particles emitted from a tube escape the fireball, meaning 20% of charged particles come from tube emission (294 particles added to soft particles, total 1167)
- For the highest $p_t$ bin (4.0 to 1.1 GeV/c), each tube contributes 4.2 particles with 100% survival rate; for the lowest bin (0.3 to 0.2 GeV/c), each tube contributes 2.6 particles with 25% survival
- The unlike-sign charge pairs are larger than like-sign charge pairs by 0.8% $\pm$ 0.002% in the region near $\Delta\phi$ = $\Delta\eta$ = 0.0
- The dip in the combined correlation has a statistical significance of $14\sigma$
- For the color electric field analysis, aligned pairs (positive $\Delta\eta_1 + \Delta\eta_2$) are 0.2% greater than not-aligned pairs in the data
- The ratio of not-aligned over aligned pairs shows a dip of 0.2% at $\Delta\eta_1 + \Delta\eta_2$ = 1.4 with an average shift of 0.14%

## Context

This work extends the Glasma Flux Tube Model (GFTM) developed by Dumitru and collaborators, and builds on the Parton Bubble Model (PBM) shown to be a good approximation for surface flux tube formation in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV. The paper compares its model predictions to high-precision STAR data from two independent experiments (centralproduction and centralitydependence) gathered two years apart. The results enable a proposed method to measure strong CP violation through pionic correlations, specifically using the color electric field effect on longitudinal momentum and the color magnetic field effect on transverse momentum of $\pi^+$ $\pi^-$ pairs from the same flux tube.
