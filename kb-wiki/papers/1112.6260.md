# Magnetic rotations in 198Pb and 199Pb within covariant density functional theory

> Well-known examples of shears bands in the nuclei 198Pb and 199Pb are investigated within tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field theory. Energy spectra, the relation between spin and rotational frequency, deformation parameters and reduced $M1$ and $E2$ transition probabilities are calculated. The results are in good agreement with available data and with calculations based on the phenomenological pairing plus-quadrupole-quadrupole tilted-axis cranking model. It is shown that covariant density functional theory provides a successful microscopic and fully self-consistent description of magnetic rotation in the Pb region showing the characteristic properties as the shears mechanism and relatively large B(M1) transitions decreasing with increasing spin.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory, implemented via the tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field (TAC-RMF) model with a point-coupling interaction, provides a fully microscopic and self-consistent description of magnetic rotation in 198Pb and 199Pb, reproducing the characteristic shears mechanism and the decreasing B(M1) transitions with increasing spin, although the absolute B(M1) values require an empirical attenuation factor of 0.3 to match experiment.

## Method

The TAC-RMF model with the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 was applied to four shears bands: bands 1 and 3 in 198Pb and bands 1 and 2 in 199Pb. The Dirac equation was solved in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 12 major shells, assuming rotation around an axis in the xz plane, and pairing correlations were neglected. Energy spectra, total Routhians, spin versus rotational frequency, deformation parameters, and reduced M1 and E2 transition probabilities were calculated and compared with experimental data and with results from the phenomenological pairing plus-quadrupole-quadrupole tilted-axis cranking (PQTAC) model.

## Key numbers

- For the ground state of 198Pb, increasing the oscillator basis from N_f=12 to N_f=14 changes total energies by less than 0.04% and mass quadrupole moments by less than 5%.
- The TAC-RMF calculations yield quadrupole deformations mainly around β = 0.15 for each configuration, while PQTAC gives smaller deformations around β = 0.11.
- The γ deformation values from TAC-RMF vary between 47° and 59°, indicating small triaxiality close to oblate axial symmetry.
- The calculated B(M1) values must be attenuated by a factor of 0.3 to reproduce the absolute experimental B(M1) values.
- Before backbending, spin values from TAC-RMF and PQTAC differ from experimental values by up to 2ħ; after backbending, PQTAC results for bands 1 and 3 in 198Pb are nearly 3ħ larger than experimental and TAC-RMF values.

## Context

This work extends the application of self-consistent TAC-RMF from lighter nuclei (e.g., 84Rb in Madokoro (2000) and 142Gd in Peng (2008)) to the A~190 Pb region for the first time, providing a fully microscopic alternative to the phenomenological PQTAC model of Chmel (2007). The results enable future systematic studies of magnetic rotation in Pb isotopes, including the inclusion of pairing correlations and tests with other covariant density functionals to clarify deformation parameters and transition probabilities.
