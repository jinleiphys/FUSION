# Systematic study of proton-neutron pairing correlations in the nuclear shell model

> A shell-model study of proton-neutron pairing in $2p1f$ shell nuclei using a parametrized hamiltonian that includes deformation and spin-orbit effects as well as isoscalar and isovector pairing is reported. By working in a shell-model framework we are able to assess the role of the various modes of proton-neutron pairing in the presence of nuclear deformation without violating symmetries. Results are presented for $^{44}$Ti, $^{45}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti, $^{46}$V and $^{48}$Cr to assess how proton-neutron pair correlations emerge under different scenarios. We also study how the presence of a one-body spin-obit interaction affects the contribution of the various pairing modes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a parametrized shell-model Hamiltonian including deformation, spin-orbit effects, and both isoscalar and isovector pairing can systematically describe proton-neutron pairing correlations in 2p1f shell nuclei, but the abstract's claim of assessing "the role of the various modes" is qualified by the finding that isoscalar pairing is strongly suppressed by the spin-orbit interaction and by even a small neutron excess, making its effects appreciable only in N=Z nuclei without spin-orbit splitting.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for neutrons and protons in the 2p1f shell (outside a ⁴⁰Ca core) using a schematic Hamiltonian: H = χ(Q·Q + a P†·P + b S†·S + α Σ lᵢ·sᵢ), where Q is the mass quadrupole operator, P† creates isoscalar (T=0, J=1) pairs, and S† creates isovector (T=1, J=0) pairs. They systematically varied the pairing strengths a and b and the spin-orbit strength α, starting from pure SU(3) rotational motion and gradually adding symmetry-breaking terms, studying the nuclei ⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁵Ti, ⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁶V, and ⁴⁸Cr.

## Key numbers

- Optimal Hamiltonian parameters: χ = -0.05 MeV, a = b = 12, α = 20, giving an acceptable fit to spectra of all nuclei considered.
- For ⁴⁶V, the experimental splitting between the Jπ=0⁺ and Jπ=1⁺ states is 1.23 MeV; the optimal Hamiltonian produces a splitting of 1.05 MeV.
- For ⁴⁶V, the lowest 3⁺ state is experimentally at 801 keV; the calculation places it at 1.89 MeV.
- In ⁴⁸Cr, the backbend near I=12 is reproduced by the optimal Hamiltonian and requires isovector pairing; pure isoscalar pairing cannot reproduce it.
- In ⁴⁴Ti with no spin-orbit interaction, isoscalar and isovector pairing have identical effects on ground-state rotational band properties; with spin-orbit strength α=20, isovector pairing produces a much more rapid loss of rotational collectivity than isoscalar pairing.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using exactly-solvable models such as the SO(8) model (discussed by Dobes and Pittel) and non-degenerate orbital models (Errea et al.), which suggested isoscalar pairing is most important near N=Z but could not fully restore rotational symmetry. By using the nuclear shell model, the authors maintain all symmetries while including deformation, enabling them to confirm that the backbend in ⁴⁸Cr originates from isovector pairing (consistent with the earlier shell-model study by Poves) and to show that the non-rotational character of ⁴⁴Ti requires pairing, not just spin-orbit effects as previously thought (Bhatt). The results enable future studies of how isoscalar and isovector pairing behave differently in excited bands (YRARE vs. YRAST) in N=Z nuclei.
