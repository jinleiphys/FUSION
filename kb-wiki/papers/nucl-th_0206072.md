# Direct neutron capture cross sections of 62Ni in the s-process energy range

> Direct neutron capture on 62Ni is calculated in the DWBA and the cross sections in the energy range relevant for s-process nucleosynthesis are given. It is confirmed that the thermal value of the capture cross section contains a subthreshold resonance contribution. Contrary to previous investigations it is found that the capture at higher energies is dominated by p-waves, thus leading to a considerably increased cross section at s-process energies and a modified energy dependence.

## Key claim

Direct neutron capture on 62Ni in the s-process energy range (10–50 keV) is dominated by p-wave capture, not s-wave capture as previously assumed, leading to a considerably increased cross section at s-process energies and a modified energy dependence; the thermal cross section contains a subthreshold resonance contribution.

## Method

The direct capture (DC) cross section was calculated using the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) with the code TEDCA, including E1, M1, and E2 transitions. Folding potentials were employed for the optical potentials, with the imaginary part neglected because no other reaction channels are open at low energies; the potential strength parameter λᵢ in the entrance channel was fixed by reproducing the potential scattering length of 6.3 ± 0.4 fm, leaving no free parameters fitted to reaction data. Transitions to 20 final states in 63Ni were summed, using spectroscopic factors from 62Ni(d,p)63Ni experiments, and the cross section was parameterized as σ = X/√E + Y√E + ZE³/² for s-, p-, and d-wave contributions.

## Key numbers

- Thermal direct capture cross section: 5.21 ± 0.76 barn (purely direct contribution), compared to the measured thermal cross section of 14.5 ± 4 barn (the difference attributed to subthreshold resonance tail).
- Direct capture Maxwellian Averaged Cross Section (MACS) at 30 keV: 30.1 ≤ ⟨σ⟩DC ≤ 40.2 mb.
- Resonant contribution to MACS at 30 keV: 5.13 mb.
- Potential scattering length used to fix the potential: 6.3 ± 0.4 fm.
- Parameterization coefficients for the direct capture cross section (in barn, with E in keV): Xhigh = 2.985×10⁻² barn keV¹/², Yhigh = 4.235×10⁻³ barn keV⁻¹/²; Xlow = 2.232×10⁻² barn keV¹/², Ylow = 3.1725×10⁻³ barn keV⁻¹/².
- d-wave terms are smaller than p-wave terms by six orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between two recommended 30 keV MACS values for 62Ni: 35.5 ± 4 mb from Bao (1987) and 12.5 ± 4 mb from Bao (2000), both based on the same thermal cross section but with different extrapolation assumptions. The new calculation shows that the earlier Bao (1987) value was correct, but for the wrong reason (it assumed s-wave dominance, while the actual mechanism is p-wave dominance). The result cures a pronounced overproduction of 62Ni found in type II supernova nucleosynthesis simulations by Rauscher (2001) that used the lower Bao (2000) value, and enables more accurate s-process nucleosynthesis calculations in AGB stars and massive stars.
