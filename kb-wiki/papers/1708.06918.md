# $D D^{*}$ potentials in chiral perturbation theory and possible molecular states

> The $DD^{*}$ potentials are studied within the framework of heavy meson chiral effective field theory. We obtain the effective potentials of the $DD^{*}$ system up to $O(ε^2)$ at the one-loop level. In addition to the one-pion exchange contribution, the contact and two-pion exchange interactions are also investigated in detail. Furthermore, we search for the possible molecular states by solving the Schrödinger equation with the potentials. We notice that the contact and two-pion exchange potentials are numerically non-negligible and important for the existence of a bound state. In our results, no bound state is found in the $I=1$ channel within a wide range of cutoff parameter, while there exists a bound state in the $I=0$ channel as the cutoff is near $m_ρ$ in our approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within heavy meson chiral effective field theory, no bound state exists in the isospin-1 \(DD^{*}\) channel for a wide range of cutoff parameters, while a bound state appears in the isospin-0 channel only when the cutoff is near the \(\rho\) meson mass \(m_\rho\), indicating that the contact and two-pion exchange contributions are numerically non-negligible for binding.

## Method

The authors derived the effective potentials for the \(DD^{*}\) system up to chiral order \(O(\epsilon^2)\) at one-loop level using heavy meson chiral effective field theory, including contact, one-pion exchange (OPE), and two-pion exchange (TPE) interactions. They applied Weinberg's power counting scheme to separate two-particle-irreducible (2PI) diagrams from reducible ones, then solved the Schrödinger equation with the resulting potentials in coordinate space to search for bound molecular states. The low-energy constants (LECs) for contact terms were estimated using resonance saturation from \(\rho\), \(\phi\), scalar, and axial-vector meson exchanges.

## Key numbers

* The mass difference \(\delta\) between \(D\) and \(D^{*}\) is 0.142 GeV.
* The bare coupling constant \(g\) for the \(DD^{*}\pi\) vertex is 0.65, obtained from the renormalized coupling via \(O(\epsilon^2)\) corrections.
* The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) is 0.086 GeV, and the pion mass \(m_\pi\) is 0.139 GeV.
* The renormalization scale \(\mu\) is set to \(4\pi f\).
* In the isospin-0 channel, a bound state exists when the cutoff parameter is near \(m_\rho\) (approximately 0.77 GeV); no bound state is found in the isospin-1 channel within a wide range of cutoff values.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory from nucleon-nucleon systems (Weinberg 1990, 1991) to doubly charmed meson systems, specifically the \(DD^{*}\) system, which is analogous to the deuteron due to the absence of annihilation channels. It builds on prior one-boson-exchange model studies of \(D^{(*)}D^{(*)}\) systems (Ohkoda 2012; Li 2012) that found bound states in the isospin-0 channel with binding energies of 5–62 MeV, and on the authors' own previous work on \(\bar{B}\bar{B}\) interactions (Liu 2012). The results enable future systematic studies of heavy-flavor molecular states and provide a chiral EFT framework for comparing with lattice QCD calculations (Francis 2016) and experimental searches for doubly charmed bosons.
