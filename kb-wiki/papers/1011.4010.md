# Exclusive Processes Induced By Antiprotons: Opportunities for QCD Studies

> The detection of exclusive processes induced by antiproton beams in the GeV range at PANDA(FAIR) opens possibilities for testing QCD predictions on the nucleon structure. In particular, the measurement of electromagnetic nucleon form factors through the proton-antiproton annihilation in a lepton pair can be studied. However, other processes, although interesting by themselves, play the role of background. We will focus on two examples: an 'electromagnetic process', the S-channel annihilation of proton and antiproton into a neutral pion and a virtual photon (followed by lepton pair emission) and a possible mechanism for the production of kaon and pion pairs, through physical vacuum excitations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that exclusive antiproton-proton annihilation processes, specifically the s-channel vector-meson exchange mechanism for $\bar p + p \to e^+ + e^- + \pi^0$ and vacuum-excitation production of kaon pairs, can be used to test QCD predictions on nucleon structure, but these processes are primarily studied as physical background to the main electromagnetic form factor measurements.

## Method

The authors calculate the double differential cross section for $\bar p + p \to e^+ + e^- + \pi^0$ in the Born approximation, modeling the s-channel exchange of vector mesons ($\omega$, $\rho$, $\phi$) with parametrized strong form factors $F_{1,2}^V(s)$ and coupling constants $G_{V\pi\gamma^*}$ derived from radiative decay widths and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. For kaon-pion pair production ratios, they compute the yield from vacuum excitation in the singlet $^1S_0$ state, assuming democratic production of $u\bar u$, $d\bar d$, and $s\bar s$ current quark pairs, and compare to the triplet-state quark rearrangement mechanism.

## Key numbers

- The accessible kinematical region for $\bar p + p \to e^+ + e^-$ corresponds to $s \ge 4M^2 = 3.5$ GeV$^2$, where $M$ is the proton mass.
- The coupling constants for radiative decay are $g_{\omega\pi\gamma} = 1.8$ for the $\omega$ meson and $g_{\rho\pi\gamma} = 0.56$ for the $\rho$ meson.
- The relative phase of the $\rho$-meson contribution is $\phi_\rho \approx 110^\circ$.
- The predicted ratio of kaon to pion pair production at threshold is $R = 0.108$, with an attributed error of $5\%$ related to constituent quark masses.
- The experimental value for this ratio is $R = 0.108 \pm 0.007$ (Ab94).
- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = 280$ MeV, $m_s = 400$ MeV.
- The reaction $\bar p + p \to \gamma + \pi^0$ was measured in the region $2.911 \le \sqrt{s} \le 3.686$ GeV by the Fermilab E760 Collaboration (Arm97).

## Context

This work extends the study of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region, previously addressed by Re65 and Ad07 for three-body reactions like $\bar p + p \to e^+ + e^- + \pi^0$ via the scattering channel exchange, by calculating the s-channel annihilation mechanism as a physical background. The kaon-to-pion ratio prediction builds on the vacuum excitation mechanism proposed in EK10 and agrees with existing data from Ab94. The results are directly relevant to the PANDA experiment at FAIR, which will provide antiproton beams in the GeV range, enabling tests of QCD predictions on nucleon structure and meson-nucleon interactions.
