# Anisotropic hydrodynamic modeling of 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions

> We compare phenomenological results from 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) with experimental data collected in LHC 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. In particular, we present comparisons of particle spectra, average transverse momentum, elliptic flow, and HBT radii. The aHydroQP model relies on the introduction of a single temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass which is fit to lattice QCD data. By taking moments of the resulting Boltzmann equation, we obtain the dynamical equations used in the hydrodynamic stage which include the effects of both shear and bulk viscosities. At freeze-out, we use anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out performed on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface to convert to hadrons. To model the production and decays of the hadrons we use THERMINATOR 2 which is customized to sample from ellipsoidal momentum-space distribution functions. Using smooth Glauber initial conditions, we find very good agreement with many heavy-ion collision observables.

## Key claim

The 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model, using smooth Glauber initial conditions and a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, achieves very good agreement with many heavy-ion collision observables from LHC 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, but the agreement is limited to specific transverse momentum ranges and centrality classes, with discrepancies observed at high pseudorapidity and for some elliptic flow measurements.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics equations derived from moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a single temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass fit to lattice QCD entropy density from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration. The evolution was started at proper time 0.25 fm/c with smooth Glauber initial conditions using a tilted profile, and freeze-out was performed on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface using anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out with THERMINATOR 2 customized for ellipsoidal momentum-space distribution functions. The three free parameters (initial central temperature, freeze-out temperature, and constant eta/s) were fixed by fitting to ALICE data for pion, kaon, and proton spectra in 0-5% and 30-40% centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature T0 = 600 MeV for perfectly central collisions
- Freeze-out temperature TFO = 130 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.159 (constant, temperature-independent)
- Initial proper time tau0 = 0.25 fm/c
- Binary-collision mixing factor chi = 0.15
- Central plateau width Delta-varsigma = 2.3 and Gaussian tail width sigma_varsigma = 1.6 for longitudinal profile
- Peak bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio zeta/s approximately 0.05 in the quasiparticle model, compared to approximately 0.3 in prior work by Ryu (2015)
- Lattice spacing Delta x = Delta y = 0.5 fm and Delta varsigma = 0.375 on a 64^3 lattice
- Temporal step size Delta tau = 0.02 fm/c with fourth-order Runge-Kutta
- Between 7,400 and 36,200 hadronic events per centrality class generated in THERMINATOR 2

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach beyond previous phenomenological applications that relied on approximate conformal factorization of the energy-momentum tensor (Martinez 2012, Ryblewski 2012, Nopoush 2016, Strickland 2016) or used isotropic freeze-out (Ryblewski 2012). The aHydroQP model incorporates three momentum-space anisotropy parameters, a realistic equation of state via quasiparticle method, and self-consistent anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out. The results enable future investigations including temperature-dependent eta/s, event-by-event initial conditions, and off-diagonal components of the anisotropy tensor to improve agreement with observables such as the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow and high-centrality particle spectra.
