# Chiral approach to antikaons in dense matter

> Antikaons in dense nuclear matter are studied using a chiral unitary approach which incorporates the $s$- and p-waves of the $\bar K N$ interaction. We include, in a self-consistent way, Pauli blocking effects, meson self-energies modified by nuclear short-range correlations and baryon binding potentials. We show that the on-shell factorization cannot be applied to evaluate the in-medium corrections to p-wave amplitudes. We also obtain an attractive shift for the $Λ$ and $Σ$ masses of -30 MeV at saturation density while the $Σ^*$ width gets sensibly increased to about 80 MeV. The moderate attraction developed by the antikaon does not support the existence of very deep and narrow bound states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the moderate attraction developed by antikaons in dense nuclear matter does not support the existence of very deep and narrow bound states, with the antikaon potential yielding bound states of no more than about 50 MeV but with a sizeable width of about 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with tree-level kernels from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for s-waves and from Λ, Σ, and Σ* pole terms for p-waves. Medium modifications are incorporated self-consistently through Pauli blocking, meson self-energies (including pion dressing with short-range correlations), and baryon binding potentials within a mean-field approach. The in-medium p-wave amplitudes require a modified treatment because on-shell factorization fails, so the authors add medium corrections using the full off-shell q² dependence of the vertices.

## Key numbers

- Attractive shift for Λ and Σ masses of -30 MeV at saturation density ρ₀
- Σ* width increases from 30 MeV in free space to about 80 MeV when pions are dressed
- Λ(1115) acquires an attractive shift of -28 MeV when pions are dressed (increased from -10 MeV without pion dressing)
- Σ(1195) shows an attraction of -35 MeV when pions are dressed
- Optical potential for antikaons at zero momentum changes from -40 MeV at ρ₀/2 to -70 MeV at 2ρ₀ when only kaons are dressed self-consistently
- Optical potential shifts from -30 MeV at ρ₀/2 to -80 MeV at 2ρ₀ when pions are also dressed
- Antikaon bound states are no deeper than about 50 MeV with a width of about 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral unitary studies of antikaons in matter, particularly the self-consistent calculations by Ramos (1999) and the Juelich meson exchange approach by Tolos, by incorporating both s- and p-wave contributions with proper treatment of off-shell effects. The results contradict the possibility of very deep and narrow antikaon bound states suggested by some earlier phenomenological analyses, and instead agree with other self-consistent calculations that find moderate attraction. The findings enable improved predictions for antikaon production in heavy-ion collisions and for the interpretation of kaonic atom data.
