# Probing the hadron mass spectrum in dense two-color QCD with the linear sigma model

> We investigate modifications of hadron masses at finite quark chemical potential in two-flavor and two-color QCD, of which the data are available from lattice simulations, within a linear sigma model based on approximate Pauli-Gursey $SU(4)$ symmetry. The model describes not only ground-state scalar diquarks and pseudo-scalar mesons but also the excited pseudo-scalar diquarks and scalar mesons; each ground-state diquark (meson) has the corresponding excited diquark (hadron) with opposite parity as a chiral partner. Effects of chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensates are incorporated by a mean-field treatment. We show that various mixings among the hadrons, which are triggered by the breakdown of baryon number conservation in the superfluid phase, lead to a rich hadron mass spectrum. We discuss the influence of $U(1)_A$ anomaly on the density dependence of the mass spectrum and also manifestations of the chiral partner structures as density increases in the superfluid phase. The predicted hadron masses are expected to provide future lattice simulations with useful information on such symmetry properties in dense two-color QCD.

## Key claim

The paper predicts modifications of hadron masses in dense two-color QCD using a linear sigma model, showing that mixings among mesons and diquarks triggered by baryon number violation in the superfluid phase lead to a rich mass spectrum, but these predictions are contingent on input parameters from recent lattice simulations that are still tentative and carry significant statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors construct a linear sigma model based on approximate Pauli-Gursey SU(4) symmetry for two-flavor two-color QCD, incorporating both ground-state scalar diquarks and pseudo-scalar mesons as well as their excited chiral partners with opposite parity. They treat chiral symmetry breaking and diquark condensates at mean-field level, then determine hadron masses by expanding the Lagrangian to quadratic order in each hadron field around the mean-field ground state. Model parameters are fixed using recent lattice simulation results for hadron masses in the hadronic phase.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass from lattice: \(m_\pi = 738\) MeV (used as input)
- Critical chemical potential for baryon superfluid phase: \(\mu_q^* = m_\pi^{\rm vac}/2\) (analytically derived)
- Vacuum mass of negative-parity diquark baryons from lattice: \(m_{B'(\bar{B}')}^{\rm vac} \approx 1611\) MeV, giving mass ratio \(m_{B'(\bar{B}')}^{\rm vac}/m_\pi^{\rm vac} \approx 2.18\)
- Scalar meson mass in hadronic phase from lattice: \(m_\sigma = [1453 \pm 84.7({\rm stat})^{+103}_{-84.7}({\rm syst})]\) MeV at \(\mu_q = 119\) MeV and \(m_\sigma = [1452 \pm 101({\rm stat})^{+109}_{-106}({\rm syst})]\) MeV at \(\mu_q = 238\) MeV, implying \(1.75 \lesssim m_\sigma^{\rm vac}/m_\pi^{\rm vac} \lesssim 2.2\)
- Model parameters: \(m_0^2 = -(693\ {\rm MeV})^2\), \(h_q = (364\ {\rm MeV})^3\) for Set (I); \(m_0^2 = -(206\ {\rm MeV})^2\) for Set (II); \(\lambda_2 = 65.6\) for Sets (I) and (II)

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of dense two-color QCD using effective models including chiral perturbation theory (Kogut 1999, Kogut 2000), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Ratti 2004), and hidden local symmetry (Harada 2010), and is directly motivated by recent lattice simulations that measured hadron masses including orbitally excited states (Murakami 2022). The predictions are expected to guide future lattice simulations in probing symmetry properties such as chiral partner structures and the influence of the \(U(1)_A\) anomaly on the density dependence of the hadron mass spectrum in the superfluid phase.
