# Relativistic chiral description of the $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon scattering

> Recently, a relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction is formulated up to leading order which provides a good description of the phase shifts of $J\leq1$ partial waves [Chin. Phys. C 42 (2018) 014103]. Nevertheless, a separable regulator function that is not manifestily covariant was used in solving the relativistic scattering equation. In the present work, we first propose a covariant and separable form factor to regularize the kernel potential and then apply it to study the simplest but most challenging $^1S_0$ channel which features several low-energy scales. In addition to being self-consistent, we show that the resulting relativistic potential can describe quite well the unique features of the $^1S_0$ channel at leading order, in particular the pole position of the virtual bound state and the zero amplitude at the scattering momentum $\sim 340$ MeV, indicating that the relativistic formulation might be more natural from the point of view of effective field theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at leading order, when regularized with a newly proposed covariant and separable form factor (CSFF), can simultaneously describe the zero amplitude at scattering momentum ~340 MeV and the virtual bound state pole position in the $^1S_0$ channel, suggesting the relativistic formulation may be more natural from an effective field theory perspective than non-relativistic approaches.

## Method

The authors constructed a leading-order relativistic chiral $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon potential with two low-energy constants ($C_{1S0}$ and $\hat{C}_{1S0}$) and regularized it using a covariant and separable form factor (CSFF) that depends on both the off-shell momentum and the total energy. They solved the Kadyshevsky equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, to resum the potential non-perturbatively, and fitted the two parameters to six Nijmegen phase shift data points for laboratory energies $T_\mathrm{lab.}\leq 100$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Best fit $\chi^2 = 1.64$ for 4 degrees of freedom achieved with CSFF at cutoff $\Lambda = 460$ MeV, compared to $\chi^2 = 7.86$ with the traditional separable form factor (SFF) at $\Lambda = 695$ MeV.
- Predicted scattering length $a = -23.0$ fm (CSFF) and $-22.0$ fm (SFF), compared to Nijmegen PWA value $-23.7$ fm.
- Predicted effective range $r = 2.61$ fm (CSFF) and $2.53$ fm (SFF), compared to Nijmegen PWA value $2.70$ fm.
- Predicted virtual bound state pole position $i\gamma = -i8.2$ MeV (CSFF) and $-i8.5$ MeV (SFF), compared to empirical $-i10.0$ MeV.
- Zero amplitude reproduced at center-of-mass momentum $k_0 \sim 340$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction framework previously formulated by Ren et al. (2016) for partial waves with $J\leq 1$, which used a non-covariant separable regulator. The study is inspired by Sanchez Sanchez et al. (2017), who showed that rearranging short-range interactions in non-relativistic chiral perturbation theory could simultaneously describe the unique features of the $^1S_0$ channel. The results enable further investigation into whether cutoff independence can be achieved in the relativistic framework, addressing an open question noted by Kaplan et al. (1998) and Nogga et al. (2005) regarding the cutoff dependence of chiral nuclear potentials.
