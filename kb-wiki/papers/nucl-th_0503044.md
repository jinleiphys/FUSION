# Chiral perturbation theory for electroweak reactions on deuterium

> I summarize two recent applications of chiral perturbation theory to electromagnetic reactions on deuterium: elastic electron-deuteron scattering, and Compton scattering on deuterium. Both calculations have now been carried out to three orders in the chiral expansion. The expansion shows good convergence and is able to reproduce data for q < 600 MeV in e-d and for omega=55-95 MeV in gamma-d. These results demonstrate that ChiPT can be used to reliably compute operators and wave functions for low-momentum-transfer reactions in light nuclear systems.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChiPT) can reliably compute operators and wave functions for low-momentum-transfer electromagnetic reactions on deuterium, but only when single-nucleon form factor information is included separately; the expansion shows good convergence for momentum transfers below 600 MeV in electron-deuteron scattering and for photon energies between 55 and 95 MeV in Compton scattering on deuterium, though the power counting may need modification to properly renormalize gamma-NN operators.

## Method

The author applied chiral perturbation theory to compute elastic electron-deuteron scattering and Compton scattering on deuterium to three orders in the chiral expansion (LO, NLO, NNLO). For electron-deuteron scattering, the deuteron charge and quadrupole form factors were computed using a "factorized" approach where ChiPT calculated the ratio G_C/G_E^(s) while experimentally-measured single-nucleon form factors were used separately. For Compton scattering on deuterium, the amplitude was evaluated using the integral of single-nucleon and two-nucleon transition operators with deuteron wave functions obtained from ChiPT-derived NN potentials, with fits to gamma-d data performed at O(e^2 P^2) to extract isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- Electron-deuteron scattering: ChiPT reproduces data for momentum transfers |q| less than 600 MeV
- Compton scattering on deuterium: ChiPT reproduces data for photon energies omega = 55-95 MeV
- Proton polarizabilities from gamma-p fit at O(e^2 P^2): alpha_p = (12.1 +/- 1.1)_(-0.5)^(+0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_p = (3.4 +/- 1.1)_(-0.1)^(+0.1) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities from gamma-d fit at O(e^2 P^2): alpha_N = (13.0 +/- 1.9)_(-1.5)^(+3.9) x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_N = (-1.8 +/- 1.9)_(-0.9)^(+2.1) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- ChiPT prediction for proton polarizabilities at O(e^2 P): alpha_p = 12.2 x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_p = 1.2 x 10^(-4) fm^3
- pp-fusion S factor estimated error from higher-order terms: less than 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory from single-nucleon reactions to two-nucleon electromagnetic processes on deuterium, building on the Weinberg (1990) proposal to power-count the NN potential rather than the amplitude. The calculations use NN potentials derived from ChiPT by Ordonez (1996), Kaiser (1997), Epelbaum (1999), and Entem-Machleidt (2003), with N3LO potentials now available. The results enable systematic extraction of isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities from gamma-d data and demonstrate that the same ChiPT electroweak operators can be applied to A=2, 3, and 4 systems, as shown in the work of Park (2003) on pp fusion and Ando (2003) on neutrino-deuteron scattering.
