# Clusterization in nuclear states at the edge of stability

> The open quantum system eigenstate in the vicinity of low-energy decay channel may mimic its features, in particular the characteristic clustering properties of the decay channel. This generic mechanism of clusterization, the so-called mimicry mechanism of clusterization, is discussed here on example of the ground state wave function of $^8$Be. At higher excitation energies, when the density of states and reaction channels is high, the quantal aspects in the clusterization process disappear and the statistical mechanism of clusterization which is rooted in the Central Limit Theorem, begin to dominate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that clusterization in nuclear states near particle emission thresholds is a generic emergent phenomenon of open quantum systems, not a result of specific nucleon-nucleon interactions or dynamical symmetries. The "mimicry mechanism" describes how eigenstates near a low-energy decay channel adopt the clustering properties of that channel, but the authors hedge that this quantal regime only dominates at low excitation energies and low channel density, while at higher energies a statistical mechanism rooted in the Central Limit Theorem takes over.

## Method

The authors performed Gamow Shell Model calculations in the coupled reaction channels representation (GSM-CC) for the nucleus $^8$Be, using a $^4$He core with valence nucleons in a model space including resonant and scattering-like harmonic oscillator shells up to 8$\hbar\omega$ excitation. The N$^3$LO realistic interaction was used without three-body forces, and the channel basis included $^4$He+$^4$He, $^7$Li+p, $^7$Be+n, and $^6$Li+d mass partitions. The energy dependence of channel probability weights, spectroscopic factors, and $\alpha$-decay widths was analyzed as a function of distance from the $\alpha$-decay threshold.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ground state energy of $^4$He is -26.32 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -28.30 MeV, using an oscillator length of $b$ = 1.7 fm.
- The calculated deuteron projectile energy is -1.95 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -2.2 MeV.
- The real part of the $\alpha$-channel probability weight $b^2_c$ for the $0^+_1$ state of $^8$Be has a maximum at approximately 0.7 MeV above the $^4$He+$^4$He threshold.
- The imaginary part of the $\alpha$-channel weight for the $0^+_1$ state peaks at approximately 1.0 MeV above the $^4$He+$^4$He threshold.
- For the $2^+_1$ state, the $\alpha$-channel probability decreases from 11% to about 5% over an energy interval of approximately 4 MeV above threshold.
- The maximum of the real part of the $\alpha$-cluster spectroscopic factor for $^8$Be is at approximately 0.8 MeV above the $\alpha$-decay threshold.

## Context

This work extends the Ikeda diagram concept from 1968, which noted that $\alpha$-cluster states appear near $\alpha$-decay thresholds, to a general mechanism applicable to many cluster types ($n$, $2n$, $p$, $2p$, $^2$H, $^3$He, $^3$H, $^4$He) in nuclei such as $^6$He, $^6$Li, $^7$Be, $^7$Li, $^{11}$O, $^{11}$C, $^{17}$O, $^{20}$Ne, and $^{26}$O. The statistical mechanism at higher energies is contrasted with the aggregation scenario found in heavy-ion collisions at $E_{\rm lab} \geq 25$ MeV (Botet and Płoszajczak, 2002; PhysRevLett.86.3514). The work enables future studies of wave function collectivization due to decay channel coupling, near-threshold modifications of nucleon-nucleon interaction and spectroscopic factors, and the consequences of coalescing resonances in nuclear spectroscopy and reactions.
