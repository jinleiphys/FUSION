# Separating the impact of nuclear skin and nuclear deformation in high-energy isobar collisions

> Bulk nuclear structure properties, such as radii and deformations, leave distinct signatures in the final state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Isobaric collisions offer an easy route to establish explicit connections between the colliding nuclei's structure and the observable outcomes. Here, we investigate the effects of nuclear skin thickness and nuclear deformations on the elliptic flow ($v_2$) and its fluctuations in high-energy $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions. Our findings reveal that the difference in skin thickness between these isobars only influences the inherent ellipticity of the collision systems, $v_2^{\mathrm{rp}}$. In contrast, differences in nuclear deformations solely impact the fluctuations of $v_2$ around $v_2^{\mathrm{rp}}$. Hence, we have identified a data-driven method to disentangle the effects of nuclear skin and nuclear deformations, marking a significant step toward assessing the consistency of nuclear phenomena across energy scales.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in high-energy isobar collisions, the nuclear skin thickness (and half-density radius) primarily affects the reaction-plane elliptic flow \(v_2^{\mathrm{rp}}\) (captured by \(v_2\{4\}\)), while nuclear deformations (\(\beta_2\) and \(\beta_3\)) primarily affect the fluctuations \(\delta\) of elliptic flow around \(v_2^{\mathrm{rp}}\). This separation allows a data-driven method to disentangle one-body nuclear structure effects from many-body correlations, but the authors note that the analysis relies on the approximate Gaussianity of the \(V_2\) distribution and that non-Gaussian effects become important in peripheral collisions.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model (v2.26t5, string-melting mode) at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV with a partonic cross section of 3.0 mb to simulate \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr collisions. They varied the Woods-Saxon parameters (\(R_0\), \(a_0\), \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\)) in five cases (Table 1) to isolate the effects of each parameter on elliptic flow cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_2\{4\}\), and the fluctuation \(\delta = \sqrt{v_2\{2\}^2 - v_2\{4\}^2}\). The isobar ratios of these observables were calculated as functions of the number of participating nucleons \(N_{\mathrm{part}}\) and compared to STAR data.

## Key numbers
- The difference in skin thickness between Ru and Zr is \(a_{\mathrm{0Ru}} - a_{\mathrm{0Zr}} = 0.06\) fm (from Table 1: Case1 \(a_0=0.46\) fm, Case5 \(a_0=0.52\) fm).
- For this skin thickness difference, the value of \(v_2^{\mathrm{rp}}\) is enhanced by about 10% in \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru collisions relative to \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr.
- The ratio \(r = v_2\{4\}^2 / v_2\{2\}^2\) is approximately 0 in central collisions and increases to around 0.8 in mid-central collisions.
- The AMPT model uses a partonic cross section of 3.0 mb and hadrons with \(0.2 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2\) GeV for flow analysis.

## Context
This work extends the program of using isobar collisions to study nuclear structure in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, following the STAR isobar run (STAR, 2021) which measured ratios of observables between \(^{96}\)Ru+\(^{96}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Zr+\(^{96}\)Zr. It builds on prior model studies by Zhang (2021) and Giacalone (2021) that showed isobar ratios are controlled by differences in Woods-Saxon parameters. The key advance is separating the effects of radial profile parameters (\(a_0\), \(R_0\)) from deformation parameters (\(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\)), which are typically entangled in low-energy nuclear structure experiments. This enables separate constraints on \(\Delta a_0\), \(\Delta \beta_2^2\), and \(\Delta \beta_3^2\) when combined with previous findings on triangular flow (Zhang, 2021; Nijs and van der Schee, 2021). The technique can refine QGP initial conditions and motivates further isobar studies at the LHC (Citron, 2018).
