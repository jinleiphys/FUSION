# Nuclear configurational complexity in high-energy hadron-hadron scatterings

> This paper investigates high-energy hadron-hadron scattering utilizing AdS/QCD correspondence, gravitational form factors, and the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan pomeron exchange kernel. We use the configurational complexity to estimate the slope of the total cross section for hadron-hadron interactions at the high-energy regime. Our approach agrees well with the phenomenological cross sections regulated by Pomeron exchange involving the pion-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, and pion-pion, in data from the TOTEM collaboration (LHC). In the case of pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering, the agreement for the global critical point of the configurational complexity has good accuracy within 2.3\%.

# Nuclear configurational complexity in high-energy hadron-hadron scatterings

## Key claim
The configurational complexity (CC) method predicts the slope parameter of the total hadron-hadron cross section at high energies, yielding a value that agrees with phenomenological Pomeron-exchange fits to TOTEM data within 2.3% for pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering, but only when the nucleon-nucleon parameters are fixed first and no additional adjustable parameters are introduced.

## Method
The author computes total cross sections for nucleon-nucleon, pion-nucleon, and pion-pion scattering using the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel in holographic QCD (AdS/QCD), with hadron density distributions derived from gravitational form factors of the nucleon and pion. The configurational complexity is then calculated from the Fourier transform of the cross section as a function of the energy-scale coordinate, and its global minimum is identified as the critical point corresponding to the most stable nuclear configuration. The slope parameter rho is extracted from this global minimum and compared to the phenomenological fit value from TOTEM data.

## Key numbers
- The global minimum of the configurational complexity occurs at rho = 0.843, with CC = 375.02 nat (natural unit of information).
- The phenomenological value of the slope parameter from TOTEM data is rho = 0.824, giving agreement within 2.3%.
- The nucleon mass is taken as m_N ~ 1 GeV, with the nucleon cutoff parameter z_0^N = 4.081 keV.
- The pion cutoff parameter is z_0^pi = 3.1051 keV, with sigma approximately (332 MeV)^3.
- The two adjustable parameters from TOTEM data are rho = 0.824 and g_0^2 = 6.27 x 10^2.
- The analysis covers the energy range 10^2 < sqrt(s) < 10^5 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the configurational entropy approach previously applied to hadronic cross sections by Karapetyan (2019) and others, using the complementary CC method based on Shannon information entropy developed by Gleiser and collaborators (2012-2018). It builds on the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel from Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan (2006) and the holographic QCD framework for hadron-hadron scattering developed by Watanabe and collaborators. The results enable future applications of CC to other high-energy scattering processes and provide a stability criterion for nuclear systems in the non-perturbative regime of QCD.
