# Lee Yang edge singularities of QCD in association with Roberge-Weiss phase transition and chiral phase transition

> We study the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase transitions in the complex chemical potential plane in the framework of Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, in the presence of a constant gluonic background field that represents confining dynamics. We solve the quark gap equation and the background field equation self consistently, which allows us to directly explore the confinement phase transition and furthermore, evaluate the impact of the back-coupling of confinement on chiral symmetry breaking. Moreover, within such a coupled framework towards the complex chemical potential region, we demonstrate the emergence of Roberge-Weiss (RW) symmetry and investigate the trajectory of Lee-Yang edge singularities (LYES). Our analysis reveals that the LYES scaling behavior is similar to our previous findings without the background field condensate. However, a significant difference from our earlier work is that the trajectory of LYES terminates when the imaginary part of the singularity becomes $1/3 \, πT$. We elaborate that this cut-off behavior is caused by the RW symmetry that is symmetric to the imaginary chemical potential $μ_i=1/3 \, πT$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that when the gluonic background field (representing confinement) is solved self-consistently with the quark gap equation in the Dyson-Schwinger approach, the trajectory of Lee-Yang edge singularities (LYES) in the complex chemical potential plane terminates when the imaginary part of the singularity reaches \(1/3 \, \pi T\), a cut-off behavior caused by Roberge-Weiss (RW) symmetry, a result that differs from their earlier work without the background field condensate.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator and the background gluon field equation at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a truncation scheme that includes the "functional-lattice" gluon propagator and a dressed gluon-quark vertex. The physical value of the gluonic background field \(\bar{A}_4\) is determined by finding the minimum of the Polyakov loop potential, computed from the one-loop DSE for the background gluon one-point function. The coupled system is then solved for complex chemical potential to extract the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop, from which the Lee-Yang edge singularities are identified.

## Key numbers

* The LYES trajectory terminates when the imaginary part of the singularity reaches \(1/3 \, \pi T\), which is the RW symmetry line.
* The parameters for the gluon propagator include \(\{a,b,c,d,e\} = \{1\,\mathrm{GeV}, 0.735\,\mathrm{GeV}, 0.12, 0.0257\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}, 0.081\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\}\) and \(\{f,g\} = \{0.65\,\mathrm{GeV}, 0.87\,\mathrm{GeV}\}\).
* The vertex parameters are \(\Lambda = 1.4\,\mathrm{GeV}\), \(d_1 = 6.8\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\), and \(d_2 = 0.5\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\).
* The renormalization scale is set to \(\zeta = 40\,\mathrm{GeV}\) for computing the Polyakov loop potential.
* The ghost dressing function parameters are \(\{a_1, b_1, c_1, d_1, e_1\} = \{0.152\,\mathrm{GeV}^2, 0.697\,\mathrm{GeV}, 0.0055\,\mathrm{GeV}^2, 0.016\,\mathrm{GeV}, 0.045\}\) and \(\{f_1, g_1\} = \{0.025\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}, 0.0237\,\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\}\).
* The temperature range for the study of the chiral condensate and Polyakov loop is \(T \in (0.1, 0.17) \, \mathrm{GeV}\) at zero chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSE study of LYES in QCD (Wan 2024) by incorporating a self-consistently determined gluonic background field that captures confinement dynamics, building on the background-field DSE framework developed by Fister (2013) and Fischer (2013, 2014). The results show that the RW symmetry, originally identified by Roberge and Weiss (1986), imposes a cut-off on the LYES trajectory at \(\mu_i = 1/3 \, \pi T\), which was absent in the earlier work without the background field condensate. This enables future studies of the interplay between confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and the location of the QCD critical endpoint in the complex chemical potential plane.
