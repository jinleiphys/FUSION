# An empirical model of low-energy np scattering: Insights beyond ERT

> A model is presented of s-wave np elastic scattering as proceeding through an intermediate, off-shell dibaryon d*, with corrections to the npd vertex and d* propagator. The model relies on plausible conjectures and hypotheses to match identically the form of the simple amplitude given by the shape-independent effective range theory (SI ERT), which exactly describes the extant np elastic data (within errors) to at least 3 MeV. The result provides insight into the mechanisms involved in np scattering, which go beyond what ERT can reveal because ERT is a product of wave-mechanics and is therefore generally independent of mechanism. For example, in this model, the signs of the triplet and singlet scattering lengths are determined by the (opposite) spatial symmetries of the triplet and singlet np wavefunctions and pion exchange in the vertex corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents a diagrammatic model of low-energy s-wave np elastic scattering that proceeds through an intermediate off-shell dibaryon d*, with vertex and propagator corrections, and shows that this model can match identically the form of the shape-independent effective range theory (SI ERT) amplitude, thereby providing mechanistic insight that ERT cannot offer because ERT is a product of wave-mechanics and independent of mechanism.

## Method

The author constructs a model amplitude consisting of an off-shell dibaryon propagator D_d, an npd vertex operator Y_d with vertex correction v_d, and a propagator correction q_d, where pion exchange between the np legs is implemented via commuting operators H_0 and H_1 with eigenvalues h_0 and h_1. The vertex correction is derived from an infinite series of pion exchanges at the npd vertex, yielding v_t = (1-h_0-h_1)^{-1} for triplet and v_s = (1-h_0+h_1)^{-1} for singlet, while the propagator correction incorporates obligatory pion exchange in the loop and phase space factors. The model amplitude is then equated to the SI ERT amplitude to extract the uncorrected propagator and corrections.

## Key numbers

- Triplet scattering length a_t = 5.4112(15) fm and effective range r_t = 1.7436(19) fm (from Ref. [2000])
- Singlet scattering length a_s = -23.7148(43) fm and effective range r_s = 2.750(59) fm (from Ref. [2000])
- The SI ERT exactly describes extant np elastic data (within errors) to at least 3 MeV
- The factor n_d in the relativistic kinematic relation differs from unity by less than 5 x 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the effective-field theoretic treatments employing dibaryons (dEFT) as used by previous authors (e.g., Refs. [1838,1859,1841,1896,1861,2141]) by providing a diagrammatic model that lifts the uncorrected propagator and corrections directly from SI ERT. The model enables physical interpretation of the signs of triplet and singlet scattering lengths in terms of spatial symmetries of np wavefunctions and pion exchange, and gives a physical interpretation to the "unphysical deep" poles of the ERT amplitude as momentum scales in the propagator correction determined by pole masses and obligatory pion exchange.
