# Experimental overview on small collision systems at the LHC

## Key claim
The paper summarizes experimental findings from small collision systems (pp, pPb) at the LHC presented at Quark Matter 2015, showing that high-multiplicity events in these systems exhibit many features similar to those observed in PbPb collisions that are attributed to quark-gluon plasma formation. However, the author notes that the interpretation remains debated, as alternative explanations based on initial-state correlations from gluon saturation or color reconnection mechanisms in event generators like PYTHIA can also qualitatively describe the data without requiring hydrodynamic final-state interactions.

## Method
The paper compiles experimental results from the LHC collaborations (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) on bulk observables in pp collisions at 7 and 13 TeV, pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV. Measurements of particle spectra, particle ratios, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients (v_n) from two- and multi-particle correlations, HBT radii, and nuclear modification factors are compared across systems, with emphasis on high-multiplicity event classes selected by charged particle multiplicity at mid- or forward rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Minimum bias pPb data described by superimposing N_coll = A*sigma_pp/sigma_pPb ≈ 7 independent pp collisions
- v_2 values in high-multiplicity pp collisions at 13 TeV of about 5% and v_3 of about 1%, approximately 30% and 50% smaller than in pPb at similar multiplicity
- Possible non-zero v_2 of about 5% at pT up to 10 GeV/c in pPb collisions, with large uncertainty
- In PbPb collisions, initial temperature of QGP exceeds about 400 MeV in central collisions from direct photon measurements
- Statistical model describes PbPb yields with gamma_s = 1 and deviations of 10-30% from thermal yields; pPb yields with gamma_s ≈ 1 and deviations of 20-40%; minimum bias pp yields with gamma_s < 1 and deviations of 20-40%
- HBT radii in pPb and pp show R_out/R_side ≈ 1 at low kT, similar to PbPb
- v_2{4} ≈ v_2{6} ≈ v_2{8} ≈ v_2{LYZ} within 10% in high multiplicity pPb collisions
- Near-side ridge yields in pp at 13 TeV are a factor of 10 smaller than in PbPb and a factor of 4 smaller than in pPb at the same multiplicity

## Context
This conference proceedings extends the discussion of collective effects from large AA systems (where QGP formation is established) to small collision systems, following the initial observation of ridge-like structures in high-multiplicity pp events at 7 TeV by CMS (Khachatryan 2010) and in pPb events by CMS (2012), ALICE (Abelev 2012), and ATLAS (Aad 2012). The work builds on control experiments at RHIC in dAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (Back 2003, Adler 2003, Adams 2003, Arsene 2003) and at the LHC in pPb collisions that demonstrated absence of strong final state effects in minimum bias data. The results enable further theoretical work to distinguish between hydrodynamic interpretations (Bozek 2013) and initial-state correlation models from gluon saturation (Dusling 2013), and motivate future measurements of event-by-event v_n distributions and factorization breaking in pp collisions.
