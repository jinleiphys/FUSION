# Adiabatic hyperspherical approach to large-scale nuclear dynamics

> We formulate a fully microscopic approach to large-scale nuclear dynamics using a hyperradius as a collective coordinate. An adiabatic potential is defined by taking account of all possible configurations at a fixed hyperradius, and its hyperradius dependence plays a key role in governing the global nuclear motion. In order to go to larger systems beyond few-body systems, we suggest basis functions of a microscopic multicluster model, propose a method for calculating matrix elements of an adiabatic Hamiltonian with use of Fourier transforms, and test its effectiveness.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hyperradius can serve as a true collective coordinate for describing large-scale nuclear dynamics, but the approach is limited by the practical difficulty of calculating matrix elements for systems beyond few-body nuclei, and the method's effectiveness is only tested for the Fourier-transform technique rather than for a full many-body calculation.

## Method

The author formulates an adiabatic hyperspherical approach where the total wave function is expanded in channel wave functions obtained by diagonalizing the adiabatic Hamiltonian at fixed hyperradius. Basis functions are taken from a microscopic multicluster model using correlated Gauss functions for relative motion and harmonic-oscillator shell-model wave functions for fragment intrinsic states. Matrix elements integrated over hyperangles are computed using a Fourier integral technique combined with the Whittaker-Shannon interpolation formula.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of spatial coordinates excluding center-of-mass motion is d = 3(N-1) for an N-nucleon system.
- The lowest adiabatic potential for the three-alpha system (12C) has a minimum at R ≈ 3.5 fm, supporting the ground state of 12C.
- The lowest adiabatic potential for the three-alpha system reaches a broad peak around 12 fm, corresponding to the Hoyle resonance state.
- An avoided crossing in the three-alpha system begins at R ≈ 140 fm between the two-body 8Be+alpha channel and three-alpha continuum states.
- The alpha+248Cm threshold lies 6.2 MeV below the ground state of 252Cf, while the spontaneous fission threshold (140Xe+108Ru+4n) lies 200.4 MeV below the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic hyperspherical approach from atomic and molecular physics (Fano 1981) to nuclear systems, building on prior hyperspherical methods for few-body problems (Zhukov 1993, Lin 1995, Nielsen 2001) and N-body systems (Barnea 2001, Timofeyuk 2008, Mehta 2009, Rakshit 2012, Daily 2014). The approach contrasts with constrained energy-surface methods (Ring 1980, Brack 1972) and relative-distance-based fusion potentials by treating different fragment decompositions on equal footing. The method enables microscopic description of large-amplitude collective motion including fission, sub-barrier fusion, and cluster decay, with the three-alpha system of 12C (Suno 2015) as a demonstrated test case.
