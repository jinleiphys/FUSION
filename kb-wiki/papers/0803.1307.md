# Pion-less Effective Field Theory on Low-Energy Deuteron Electro-Disintegration

> In view of its relation to Big-Bang Nucleo-Synthesis and a reported discrepancy between nuclear models and data taken at S-DALINAC, electro-induced deuteron break-up 2H(e,e' p)n is studied at momentum transfer q<100MeV and close to threshold in the low-energy nuclear Effective Field Theory without dynamical pions. The result at N2LO for electric dipole currents and at NLO for magnetic ones converges order-by-order better than quantitatively predicted and contains no free parameter. It is at this order determined by simple, well-known observables. Decomposing the doubly differential cross-section into the longitudinal-plus-transverse (L+T), transverse-transverse (TT) and longitudinal-transverse interference (LT) terms, we find excellent agreement with a potential-model calculation by Arenh"ovel et al using the Bonn potential. Theory and data also agree well on σ_{L+T}. There is however no space on the theory-side for the discrepancy of up to 30%, 3-σbetween theory and experiment in σ_{LT}. From universality, we conclude that no theoretical approach with the correct deuteron asymptotic wave-function can explain the data. Un-determined short-distance contributions that could affect σ_{LT} enter only at high orders, i.e. at the few-percent level. We notice some issues with the kinematics and normalisation of the data reported.

## Key claim

The authors find that pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT(π/)) calculations, which converge order-by-order with no free parameters, cannot accommodate the reported 30% discrepancy between theory and experiment in the longitudinal-transverse interference cross-section σLT for deuteron electro-disintegration at low momentum transfer, concluding that no theoretical approach with the correct deuteron asymptotic wave-function can explain the data.

## Method

The authors employed pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT(π/)) with dimensional regularisation and the Power Divergence Subtraction scheme, calculating the triple-differential cross-section for 2H(e,e'p)n at momentum transfer q<100 MeV and near threshold. They decomposed the cross-section into longitudinal-plus-transverse (L+T), transverse-transverse (TT), and longitudinal-transverse interference (LT) terms, computing electric dipole currents to N2LO and magnetic isovector dipole currents to NLO, with all parameters fixed from known observables such as scattering lengths, effective ranges, the deuteron binding energy, and the thermal neutron-proton capture cross-section.

## Key numbers

* The experiment was performed at incident electron energies E0lab ∈ {50; 85} MeV, photon energies EXlab ∈ [8; 16] MeV, electron scattering angle Θelab = 40°, and azimuthal angle Φp = 45°, yielding photon momentum transfers q ∈ [32; 65] MeV and outgoing proton momenta p ∈ [74; 106] MeV in the proton-neutron centre-of-mass frame.
* The discrepancy between theory and experiment in σLT is up to 30% at the 3-σ level.
* The deuteron binding energy is B = 2.225 MeV, corresponding to a momentum γ = 45.70 MeV; the effective range in the spin-triplet channel is ρd = 1.764 fm.
* The spin-singlet np scattering length is a0 = -23.71 fm with effective range r0 = 2.73 fm.
* The thermal cross-section for np → dγ at E = 1.264·10-8 MeV is (334.2 ± 0.5) mb.
* The asymptotic D/S-wave ratio of the deuteron is ηsd = 0.0254, and the deuteron quadrupole moment is μQ = 0.2859 fm2.
* The expansion parameter is Q ≈ 1/3, set by the ratio of typical low-momentum scale to the breakdown scale Λπ/ ≈ mπ.

## Context

This work addresses a discrepancy reported by an experiment at the S-DALINAC accelerator (2002) between data and potential-model calculations by Arenhövel et al. using the Bonn potential for deuteron electro-disintegration at low momentum transfer. The study extends previous EFT(π/) calculations of np → dγ relevant to Big-Bang Nucleo-Synthesis (e.g., Rupak (2000) at N4LO with <1% accuracy) to the electro-disintegration process, and concludes that the data cannot be explained by any consistent theoretical approach with the correct deuteron asymptotic wave-function, while noting issues with the kinematics and normalisation of the reported data.
