# Nucleon Resonances in Kaon Photoproduction

> Nucleon resonances are investigated through the electromagnetic production of K-mesons. We study the kaon photoproduction process at tree-level and compare to a recently developed unitary K-matrix approach. Employing hadronic form factors along with the proper gauge prescription yields suppression of the Born terms and leads a resonance dominated process for both K-Lambda and K-Sigma photoproduction. Using new SAPHIR data we find the K+-Lambda photoproduction to be dominated by the S11(1650) at threshold, with additional contributions from the P11(1710) and P13(1720) states. The K-Sigma channel couples to a cluster of Delta resonances around W = 1900 MeV. We briefly discuss some tantalizing evidence for a missing D13 resonance around 1900 MeV with a strong branching ratio into KLambda channel.

# Nucleon Resonances in Kaon Photoproduction

## Key claim
Kaon photoproduction at tree level, when combined with hadronic form factors and the proper gauge prescription (specifically the Haberzettl method), yields a resonance-dominated process for both K-Lambda and K-Sigma channels, with the leading Born coupling constants remaining close to SU(3) values. The abstract glosses over the condition that this agreement with SU(3) values is only achieved when using the appropriate gauge procedure; without it, fits with free coupling constants produce values far from SU(3) predictions.

## Method
The authors performed tree-level and K-matrix coupled-channels calculations of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon, comparing three different methods for restoring gauge invariance after introducing hadronic form factors: an overall form factor, Ohta's prescription, and Haberzettl's method. They fitted the model to differential cross section and recoil polarization data from SAPHIR for the p(gamma,K+)Lambda, p(gamma,K+)Sigma0, and p(gamma,K0)Sigma+ channels, including contributions from the S11(1650), P11(1710), and P13(1720) resonances for K-Lambda, and Delta resonances around 1900 MeV for K-Sigma.

## Key numbers
- The S11(1650) resonance decays into the K-Lambda channel with a branching ratio of about 6%, and the P11(1710) with about 14%.
- With coupling constants fixed at SU(3) values (g_{K-Lambda-N}/sqrt(4pi) = -3.80, g_{K-Sigma-N}/sqrt(4pi) = 1.20), the model without hadronic form factors gave chi^2/N = 55.76; with free couplings, the fit gave g_{K-Lambda-N}/sqrt(4pi) = -1.90 and g_{K-Sigma-N}/sqrt(4pi) = -0.37 with chi^2/N = 3.33.
- Using the Haberzettl gauge prescription with fixed SU(3) couplings gave chi^2/N = 4.63 with a form factor cutoff Lambda = 1.128 GeV.
- The tree-level fit (Set II) achieved chi^2/N = 3.45 with background form factor cutoff Lambda_1 = 0.798 GeV and resonance cutoff Lambda_2 = 1.883 GeV.
- The K*(892) decay widths are Gamma_{K*+ -> K+ gamma} = 50 +/- 5 keV and Gamma_{K*0 -> K0 gamma} = 117 +/- 10 keV.
- Including a missing D13 resonance with mass 1902 MeV and total width 315 MeV produced a significant reduction in chi^2/N for the K+Lambda channel around W = 1900 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channels K-matrix approach of Feuster and Mosel (1998) and earlier tree-level isobaric models by Mart, Bennhold, and collaborators (1995, 1996) to include a more sophisticated gauge-invariance prescription from Haberzettl (1997, 1998). It uses new SAPHIR data to demonstrate that kaon photoproduction can serve as a tool for discovering missing nucleon resonances, specifically a predicted D13 state around 1960 MeV from the quark model study of Capstick and Roberts (1998). The results enable future coupled-channels analyses of K-Sigma production and searches for undiscovered nucleon resonances in the 1.8-2.3 GeV energy region.
