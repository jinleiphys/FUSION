# A Parton-Based Description of Forward-Backward Correlation in pp Collisions

> Forward-backward correlation in pp collisions is studied in an approach that emphasizes the partonic scattering angles and circumvents the intractable problem related to the transverse momenta that are low. Assuming the back-to-back scattering of partons to be the origin of hadronic correlation, the properties of forward-backward multiplicity covariance can be derived essentially independent of details of hadronization. The range of correlation in pseudo-rapidity emerges from the study without any dynamical input, thus dispelling the notion that correlation length has any fundamental significance. An attempt is made to relate the results to the two-component structure seen in autocorrelation.

# A Parton-Based Description of Forward-Backward Correlation in pp Collisions

## Key claim
Forward-backward multiplicity covariance in pp collisions can be derived from back-to-back parton scattering without adjustable parameters for the correlation range, provided one accepts that the observed "correlation length" in pseudorapidity has no fundamental dynamical significance and arises instead from the Jacobian of the angular transformation and from hadronization cone spillover between windows.

## Method
The authors model the correlated two-parton distribution as a broadened delta function in the sum of cosines of parton polar angles, representing back-to-back scattering with a variable spread parameter m to account for mismatch between the parton and pp center-of-mass systems. They then convolve this parton distribution with a Gaussian hadronization cone of width sigma (0.1-0.2 rad) that maps parton polar angles to hadron pseudorapidities, and integrate over forward and backward windows of width delta_eta = 0.2 to obtain the correlated forward-backward multiplicity covariance D_fb^2 as a function of window separation eta_delta.

## Key numbers
- The hadronization cone width sigma is estimated to be between 0.1 and 0.2 rad, based on the pion mass (0.14 GeV) and typical parton transverse momentum (0.5 GeV/c).
- The best-fit combined parameter B_m = A_0 * R_m(eta_delta=0.6) equals 14 for m < 3, with sigma = 0.2.
- For m=1, A_0 = 14, which corresponds to 28 correlated parton pairs; this is less than 2% of all parton pairs (since average charged hadron multiplicity <N> is about 20, implying roughly twice as many partons).
- For the s+p wave decomposition, A_0 = 13.2 and A_1 = 0.2 * A_0 = 2.64, giving a hard component roughly A_s/10 at small eta_delta.

## Context
This work extends the parton model approach to forward-backward correlations, contrasting with string-based models such as the Lund model (Andersson et al., 1983) and the dual parton model (Capella et al., 1994) that dominated soft production descriptions. It directly addresses the STAR collaboration data on D_fb^2 from Srivastava et al. (2005) and the two-component structure seen in Porter-Trainor autocorrelation analysis (Trainor et al., 2005). The authors explicitly avoid the color glass condensate framework (Armesto et al., 2005) due to its lack of quantitative results, and they note that the nuclear collision problem is "vastly more complicated" and largely separate from the pp case treated here.
