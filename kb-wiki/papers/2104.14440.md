# Fourth- and fifth-order virial expansion of harmonically trapped fermions at unitarity

> By generalizing our automated algebra approach from homogeneous space to harmonically trapped systems, we have calculated the fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients of universal spin-1/2 fermions in the unitary limit, confined in an isotropic harmonic potential. We present results for said coefficients as a function of trapping frequency (or, equivalently, temperature), which compare favorably with previous Monte Carlo calculations (available only at fourth order) as well as with our previous estimates in the untrapped limit (high temperature, low frequency). We use our estimates of the virial expansion, together with resummation techniques, to calculate the compressibility and spin susceptibility.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients for harmonically trapped spin-1/2 fermions at unitarity, but the results for $\Delta b_4$ show clear disagreement with previous Monte Carlo calculations at low trapping frequencies (small $\beta\omega$), while agreement is good for $\beta\omega \geq 1$.

## Method

The authors use a Suzuki-Trotter decomposition to discretize imaginary time, factorize the transfer matrix in coordinate space, and employ automated algebra to symbolically expand the resulting expressions for varying numbers of time steps $N_\tau$. They calculate canonical partition functions for subspaces up to five particles (specifically $\Delta b_{21}$, $\Delta b_{31}$, $\Delta b_{22}$, $\Delta b_{41}$, and $\Delta b_{32}$) and extrapolate to the continuous-time ($N_\tau \to \infty$) limit, exploring up to $N_\tau = 20$ for the smallest subspace and $N_\tau = 8$ for the largest.

## Key numbers

* $\Delta b_2 = \left[ 4 \cosh(\beta \omega/2) \right]^{-1}$ is used as the renormalization condition at unitarity, reproduced exactly by tuning the bare coupling.
* For $\Delta b_3$, results show "superb agreement" with quantum Monte Carlo data of Yan and Blume (2016) across the full $\beta\omega$ range studied ($0 < \beta\omega < 3$).
* For $\Delta b_4$, agreement with Yan and Blume (2016) is good for $\beta\omega \geq 1$, but a "clear difference remains at low frequencies."
* The authors explored up to $N_\tau = 20$ for $\Delta b_{21}$, $N_\tau = 16$ for $\Delta b_{31}$, $N_\tau = 12$ for $\Delta b_{22}$ and $\Delta b_{41}$, and $N_\tau = 8$ for $\Delta b_{32}$.
* For $0.3 < \beta\omega < 1.4$, the authors find $\Delta b_5 > |\Delta b_4|$, though $|\Delta b_2| \gg |\Delta b_3| \gg |\Delta b_4|$ overall.
* The Padé resummation yields sensible results for compressibility and spin susceptibility even as far as fugacity $z = 3$.

## Context

This work extends the automated algebra approach previously applied to homogeneous systems (Hou and Drut, 2020) to harmonically trapped fermions, and goes beyond the semiclassical approximation of Morrell et al. (2019). The results for $\Delta b_3$ and $\Delta b_4$ are compared with quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Yan and Blume (2016), and the homogeneous-limit results of Hou and Drut (2020) are recovered in the $\beta\omega \to 0$ limit. The fifth-order virial coefficient $\Delta b_5$ is presented for the first time in the literature, enabling Padé-resummed calculations of thermodynamic response functions such as compressibility and spin susceptibility.
