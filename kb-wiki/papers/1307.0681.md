# Determination of quark-hadron transition from lattice QCD and neutron-star observation

> We determine the quark-hadron transition line in the whole region of temperature (T) and baryon-number chemical potential (μ_B) from lattice QCD results and neutron-star mass measurements, making the quark-hadron hybrid model that is consistent with the two solid constraints. The quark part of the hybrid model is the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with entanglement vertex that reproduces lattice QCD results at mu_B/T=0, while the hadron part is the hadron resonance gas model with volume-exclusion effect that reproduces neutron-star mass measurements and the neutron-matter equation of state calculated from two- and three-nucleon forces based on the chiral effective field theory. The lower bound of the critical mu_B of the quark-hadron transition at zero T is mu_B = 1.6 GeV. The interplay between the heavy-ion collision physics around mu_B/T =6 and the neutron-star physics where mu_B/T is infinity is discussed.

## Key claim

The quark-hadron transition line in the full temperature and baryon-number chemical potential plane is determined by constructing a hybrid model that simultaneously reproduces lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential and neutron-star mass measurements, yielding a lower bound of the critical baryon-number chemical potential at zero temperature of approximately 1.6 GeV, but this bound depends on the strength of the vector-type four-body interaction in the quark model, which is treated as a free parameter.

## Method

The authors construct a two-phase quark-hadron hybrid model assuming a first-order transition. The quark phase is described by the entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model with a vector-type four-body interaction, fitted to lattice QCD data at zero baryon-number chemical potential. The hadron phase uses a hadron resonance gas model with a volume-exclusion effect, where the exclusion radius is parameterized as a quadratic function of the chemical potential and fitted to reproduce the neutron-matter equation of state calculated from chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon forces using the lowest-order Brueckner theory, as well as empirical constraints from heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star mass measurements.

## Key numbers

* Lower bound of the critical baryon-number chemical potential for the quark-hadron transition at zero temperature: mu_B ~ 1.6 GeV.
* Normal nuclear density used for normalization: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
* Parameters for the neutron exclusion radius (in fm, fm/GeV, fm/GeV^2): r_0 = 0.50, r_1 = 0.50, r_2 = -0.34.
* Neutron exclusion radius value: approximately 0.6 fm, compared to the proton charge radius of 0.877 fm.
* Maximum neutron-star mass constraint from pulsar J1614-2230: M = (1.97 +/- 0.04) solar masses.
* Pseudocritical temperature of deconfinement transition reproduced by the EPNJL model: T_c = 174 MeV, with the Polyakov-loop potential parameter T_0 rescaled to 190 MeV.
* NJL sector parameters: m_l = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 631.5 MeV, G_S = 5.498 GeV^{-2}.
* Polyakov-loop potential parameters: a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75.
* Entanglement vertex parameters: alpha_1 = 0.2, alpha_2 = 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using effective models constrained by lattice QCD and neutron-star observations to determine the QCD phase diagram, building on prior PNJL model studies by Sakai et al. (2008-2012) and Sasaki et al. (2011) that reproduced lattice data at imaginary chemical potential. The hadron sector uses chiral effective field theory interactions from the Julich group (Epelbaum et al., 2009) and the lowest-order Brueckner theory, following the variational calculations of the Illinois group (Akmal, Pandharipande, Ravenhall, 1998). The results enable future studies of whether hyperons appear in neutron stars and provide a consistent framework for connecting heavy-ion collision physics at mu_B/T ~ 6 with neutron-star physics at mu_B/T = infinity.
