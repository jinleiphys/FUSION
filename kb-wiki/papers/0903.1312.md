# Neutron Properties in the Medium

> We demonstrate that for small values of momentum transfer, Q^2, the in-medium change of the G_E/G_M form factor ratio for a bound neutron is dominated by the change in the electric charge radius and predict in a model independent manner that the in-medium ratio will increase relative to the free result. This effect will act to increase the predicted cross-section for the neutron recoil polarization transfer process 4He(e,e'n)3He. This is in contrast to medium modification effects on the proton G_E/G_M from factor ratio, which act to decrease the predicted cross-section for the 4He(e,e'p)3H reaction. Experiments to measure the in-medium neutron form factors via neutron knockout reactions are currently feasible in the range 0.1 < Q^2 < 1 GeV^2.

## Key claim

The authors predict in a model-independent manner that for small momentum transfer \(Q^2\), the in-medium ratio \(G_E/G_M\) for a bound neutron will increase relative to the free result, because the leading term of the neutron super-ratio is given by \((M/M^*)^2\) where \(M^* < M\) due to binding effects. This is in contrast to the proton, where the in-medium ratio decreases.

## Method

The authors analyze the low-\(Q^2\) expansion of the Sachs electric and magnetic form factors for both free and bound nucleons, deriving expressions for the form factor ratios \(\mathcal{R}_p\) and \(\mathcal{R}_n\). They then examine how medium modifications to the nucleon mass, magnetic moments, and charge radii affect these ratios, using general relations from light-front quark model formalism and charge symmetry. The analysis is supported by comparing predictions from three specific models: a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio quark-diquark model (Horikawa et al., 2005; Cloët et al., 2006), a chiral soliton model (Smith and Miller, 2004), and a relativistic light-front constituent quark model (Chung and Coester, 1991).

## Key numbers

- The experimentally feasible range for neutron knockout polarization transfer measurements is \(0.1 < Q^2 < 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the neutron at small \(Q^2\), the form factor ratio is \(\mathcal{R}_n \equiv G_{En}/G_{Mn} \simeq -\frac{1}{\mu_n}\frac{1}{6}Q^2\hat{R}_{En}^2\), where the effective magnetic radius contributes only at \(Q^4\) order.
- The neutron super-ratio at small \(Q^2\) is \(\mathcal{R}_n^*/\mathcal{R}_n \simeq (M/M^*)^2\), with \(M^* < M\) from binding effects.
- In the relativistic light-front constituent quark model, assuming medium effects increase radii by 10% and reduce quark mass by 20%, the neutron super-ratio increases above unity (shown in Fig. 2).
- For the proton, the free form factor ratio is \(\mathcal{R}_p \simeq \frac{1}{\mu_p}[1 - \frac{1}{6}Q^2(\hat{R}_{Ep}^2 - \hat{R}_{Mp}^2)]\), and the super-ratio \(\mathcal{R}_p^*/\mathcal{R}_p\) is less than one.

## Context

This work extends the search for medium modifications of nucleon structure, motivated by the EMC effect discovered over twenty years ago, where valence quarks in bound nucleons carry less momentum than in free nucleons. Previous experiments on \(^4\text{He}(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})^3\text{H}\) have shown that the proton \(G_E/G_M\) ratio decreases in the medium, but the interpretation is complicated by strong charge-exchange final-state interactions (Schiavilla et al., 2004). The authors propose that measuring neutron recoil polarization in \(^4\text{He}(\vec{e},e'\vec{n})^3\text{He}\) provides a complementary test that can distinguish between medium modification models and final-state interaction effects. This work enables experimental proposals at Jefferson Lab to measure both proton and neutron polarization transfer reactions on \(^4\)He and deuterium targets.
