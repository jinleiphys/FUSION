# Local spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and its interaction corrections

> We have computed the second Fourier sine coefficient of the longitudinal spin polarization, $\langle P_{z} \sin 2(ϕ_{p} - Ψ_{2}) \rangle$, as a function of multiplicity or centrality in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and in $p$+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16$ TeV using the CLVisc hydrodynamic framework. The numerical results successfully describe the data in Au+Au collisions. However, understanding the data in $p$+Pb collisions remains a puzzle. Additionally, we have reported some recent developments in quantum kinetic theory and spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The CLVisc hydrodynamic framework successfully describes the second Fourier sine coefficient of longitudinal spin polarization, ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p - Ψ_2)⟩, in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, but fails to reproduce the data in p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV, where the sign of the observable remains negative in all three equilibrium scenarios while the experimental data show positive values.

## Method

The authors implemented the (3+1)-D CLVisc hydrodynamic framework to compute the local spin polarization of Λ hyperons under three scenarios: Λ equilibrium, s-quark equilibrium, and iso-thermal equilibrium. They calculated ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p - Ψ_2)⟩ as a function of centrality or multiplicity for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV and p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV, with simulation parameters tuned to reproduce observed multiplicity and elliptic flow. They also reviewed recent developments in quantum kinetic theory and spin hydrodynamics, including interaction corrections from collisional terms and the side-jump mechanism in the phase-space spin Hall effect.

## Key numbers

- ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p - Ψ_2)⟩ computed for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV as a function of centrality; results in s-quark equilibrium and iso-thermal equilibrium scenarios agree with experimental data from STAR (2019).
- ⟨P_z sin 2(φ_p - Ψ_2)⟩ computed for p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 8.16 TeV as a function of multiplicity; all three scenarios yield negative values, inconsistent with positive experimental data from CMS (2025).
- The initial orbital angular momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is on the order of 10^7 ħ.
- The skew-scattering contribution from spin-dependent collisions is of order O((g^4 ln g^{-1}) × ∂^2).

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of local spin polarization in Au+Au collisions by Fu (2021), Becattini (2021), and Yi (2021), which showed that shear-induced polarization is crucial for understanding the data. The failure to describe p+Pb data from CMS (2025) highlights a puzzle in small collision systems. The paper reviews recent quantum kinetic theory developments by Weickgenannt (2022), Wagner (2024), and Fang (2024), which compute interaction corrections to spin polarization, and discusses open questions in spin hydrodynamics regarding pseudo-gauge dependence and acausal modes identified by Xie (2023) and Daher (2022).
