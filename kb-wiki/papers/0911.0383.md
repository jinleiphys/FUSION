# Critique of multinomial coefficients method for evaluating Tsallis and Renyi entropies

> Oikonomou [Physica A 386 (2007) 119] has published a calculation which purports to show that the Tsallis and Renyi entropies can be obtained from the generalized multinomial coefficients. In this paper, we prove that the method of generalized multinomial coefficients failed to determine the Tsallis entropy at equilibrium. Moreover, it is shown that Oikonomou's analysis contains mistakes which led him to misleading statements related to the Jaynes principle of maximum entropy, the Tsallis and the Renyi statistics.

# Critique of multinomial coefficients method for evaluating Tsallis and Renyi entropies

## Key claim
The method of generalized multinomial coefficients proposed by Oikonomou (2007) fails to determine the Tsallis entropy at equilibrium for the ideal Maxwell-Boltzmann gas, and contains mistakes that lead to misleading statements about the Jaynes principle of maximum entropy. The Tsallis entropy derived by Oikonomou from generalized multinomial coefficients is incompatible with the entropy of the ideal gas obtained from the self-consistent Tsallis statistics based on first principles.

## Method
The author derives the Tsallis and Renyi entropies for the ideal Maxwell-Boltzmann gas without the Gibbs correcting term from their general definitions (sum over microstates with probabilities f_n) in the microcanonical ensemble, using the equiprobable distribution function f_n = 1/Γ_N. These results are then compared directly with the entropies obtained by Oikonomou from generalized multinomial coefficients, which were derived in the approximation of degenerate single-particle states (p_i = 1/W). The analysis also examines the discrepancy between Oikonomou's distribution function for Tsallis statistics and the correct microcanonical distribution function.

## Key numbers
- The Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy for the ideal gas in the degenerate single-particle approximation is S_BG = N ln W (Equation 3a).
- The statistical weight for the ideal Maxwell-Boltzmann gas without the Gibbs correcting term in the degenerate approximation is Γ_N = W^N (Equation 22).
- The mean occupation number in this approximation is ⟨n_i⟩ = N/W (Equation 23).
- The Tsallis entropy from first principles in this approximation is S_q^T = ln_q(W^N) (Equation 27).
- The Renyi entropy from first principles in this approximation is S_r^R = N ln W (Equation 28), which equals the Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy.

## Context
This work directly critiques the calculation of Oikonomou (Physica A 386, 2007, p. 119), who claimed that Tsallis and Renyi entropies can be obtained from generalized multinomial coefficients. The paper extends the self-consistent formulations of Tsallis statistics (Tsallis 1988, 1998; Toral; Parvan 2006, 2007) and Renyi statistics (Renyi; Wehrl; Lenzi 2000; Parvan and Biro 2005, 2006) by showing that Oikonomou's method is unfounded for Tsallis statistics. The result enables correct application of the Jaynes principle of maximum entropy in nonextensive statistical mechanics and clarifies that the Renyi statistics in the microcanonical ensemble is equivalent to Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, contrary to Oikonomou's claims.
