# Coherent neutrino scattering and the Migdal effect on the quenching factor

> Recent measurements of the germanium quenching factor deviate significantly from the predictions of the standard Lindhard model for nuclear recoil energies below a keV. This departure may be explained by the Migdal effect in neutron scattering on germanium. We show that the Migdal effect on the quenching factor can mimic the signal of a light Z' or light scalar mediator in coherent elastic neutrino nucleus scattering experiments with reactor antineutrinos. It is imperative that the quenching factor of nuclei with low recoil energy thresholds be precisely measured close to threshold to avoid such confusion. This will also help in experimental searches of light dark matter.

## Key claim
The Migdal effect on the germanium quenching factor, parameterized by a negative value of the parameter q in a modified Lindhard model, can produce a CEνNS spectrum that is indistinguishable from the signal of a light Z' or light scalar mediator when the standard Lindhard model is assumed for the quenching factor. This degeneracy means that what appears to be new physics could instead be a standard-model process with a modified quenching factor.

## Method
The authors parameterize the Migdal effect on the germanium quenching factor using a modified Lindhard model with an additional term q/ε, where a negative q produces the observed upturn at low recoil energies. They simulate the expected CEνNS event rate from reactor antineutrinos in a germanium detector with a 0.12 keV_ee threshold, comparing the spectra from two scenarios: (i) a light Z' or scalar mediator with the standard Lindhard model, and (ii) the standard model with the Migdal-affected quenching factor. A chi-squared analysis with 4 degrees of freedom is used to quantify how well the spectra match.

## Key numbers
- The germanium quenching factor data deviate from the standard Lindhard model (k=0.157) for nuclear recoil energies below approximately 1 keV_nr.
- For a Z' mediator with mass 24.6 MeV and coupling g'=5.40×10^{-5}, the best-fit Migdal parameters are k=0.153 and q=-15×10^{-5}, with χ²_min=1.32 for 4 degrees of freedom.
- For a scalar mediator with mass 5.0 MeV and coupling g_φ=5.52×10^{-6}, the best-fit Migdal parameters are k=0.153 and q=-15×10^{-5}, with χ²_min=0.32 for 4 degrees of freedom.
- The simulated experiment assumes a reactor thermal power of 3.9 GW, a detector distance of 20 m, an exposure of 7 kg·year, a signal efficiency of 80%, and a background of 15 counts/keV_ee/kg/day.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Collar (2021), who measured the germanium quenching factor and found a deviation from the standard Lindhard model that could be explained by the Migdal effect. The authors show that this effect can mimic new physics signals in CEνNS experiments, specifically those searching for light Z' or scalar mediators as described in the review by the authors (2020). The results highlight the need for precise measurements of the quenching factor near threshold, which will also benefit light dark matter searches as noted by Baxter (2019).
