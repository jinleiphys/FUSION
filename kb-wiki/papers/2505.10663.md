# Distribution Functions of $Λ$ and $Σ^0$ Baryons

> Treating baryons as quark + interacting-diquark bound states, a symmetry-preserving formulation of a vector$\,\times\,$vector contact interaction (SCI) is used to deliver an extensive, coherent set of predictions for $Λ, Σ^0$ baryon unpolarised and polarised distribution functions (DFs) -- valence, glue, and four-flavour separated sea -- and compare them with those of a like-structured nucleon. $Λ, Σ^0$ baryons are strangeness negative-one isospin partners within the SU$(3)$-flavour baryon octet. This makes such structural comparisons significant. The study reveals impacts of diquark correlations and SU$(3)$-flavour symmetry breaking on $Λ$, $Σ^0$ structure functions, some of which are significant. For instance, were it not for the presence of axialvector diquarks in the $Σ^0$ at the hadron scale, the $s$ quark could carry none of the $Σ^0$ spin. The discussion canvasses issues that include helicity retention in hard scattering processes; the sign and size of polarised gluon DFs; and the origin and decomposition of baryon spins. Interpreted judiciously, the SCI analysis delivers an insightful explanation of baryon structure as expressed in DFs.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) to model baryons as quark + diquark bound states, the authors predict that the spin-flavour structure of the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^0$ baryons, specifically the differing roles of scalar versus axialvector diquark correlations, produces significant, calculable differences in their unpolarised and polarised parton distribution functions, with the caveat that the SCI is not a precision tool but provides qualitative insight.

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^0$ baryons using a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) with parameters fixed by pseudoscalar meson properties. The baryon Faddeev amplitudes are decomposed into scalar and axialvector diquark components, and the hadron-scale distribution functions are computed algebraically from these amplitudes. The resulting valence, glue, and sea DFs are then evolved to higher scales using the all-orders (AO) evolution scheme based on effective charges.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ baryon Faddeev equation mass: 1.33 GeV; $\Sigma^0$ mass: 1.38 GeV (compared to empirical masses of approximately 1.12 GeV and 1.19 GeV, respectively)
- Scalar diquark $[ud]$ mass: 0.78 GeV; axialvector diquark $\{ud\}$ mass: 1.06 GeV; scalar diquark $[ls]$ mass: 0.94 GeV; axialvector diquark $\{ls\}$ mass: 1.22 GeV
- Canonical normalisation factor for $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^0$ amplitudes: ${\mathpzc n}_c^{\Lambda}=0.217 = {\mathpzc n}_c^{\Sigma}$; for the nucleon: ${\mathpzc n}_c^{p,n}=0.287$
- Dressed quark masses: $M_{u/d}=0.37$ GeV, $M_s=0.53$ GeV
- Pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants from the SCI: $m_\pi=0.14$ GeV, $f_\pi=0.10$ GeV; $m_K=0.50$ GeV, $f_K=0.11$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark + diquark Faddeev equation approach previously applied to nucleon parton distribution functions (Lu 2022, Cheng 2023, Yu 2024) to the strangeness-bearing octet baryons $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^0$. It contrasts with earlier studies of octet baryon DFs using other methods (Boros 1999, Ma 2001, Gockeler 2002, Erkol 2009, CSSM 2014, Peng 2022) by providing a unified, parameter-free SCI treatment that reveals the distinct roles of scalar versus axialvector diquark correlations. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^0$ structure functions and inform discussions of baryon spin decomposition and helicity retention in hard scattering.
