# On the Penetrating Nature of Electromagnetic Probes

> We establish the extent to which photons and dileptons are penetrating probes of hot hadronic matter by computing mean free paths. Absorption is indeed negligible for real photons as mean free paths are roughly tenths of \AAngströms. However, vector dominance effects, which provide rich structure in virtual photon mass spectra, introduce nonnegligible reabsorption and rehadronization resulting in mean free paths of the order of tens of femtometers. Consequences are illustrated by considering the net dilepton yield near the rho peak via thermal ππannihilation from a short-lived hadronic fireball. Results suggest that disappearance of the rho in heavy-ion collisions can be due in part to reabsorption/rehadronization rather than solely due to collective effects.

# On the Penetrating Nature of Electromagnetic Probes

## Key claim
Real photons are completely penetrating probes of hot hadronic matter, but virtual photons (dileptons) near vector meson masses can suffer nonnegligible reabsorption and rehadronization, with mean free paths of order tens of femtometers at the rho mass. This means that part of the observed suppression of the rho peak in heavy-ion dilepton spectra may be due to reabsorption/rehadronization rather than solely to collective in-medium effects.

## Method
Mean free paths for real and virtual photons in hot hadronic matter were computed using thermal field theory, assuming dominance of the s-channel a1-exchange process (gamma + pi -> a1 -> rho + pi). For virtual photons, the calculation included both electromagnetic decay and hadronic decay via vector dominance (reverse vector dominance), with the cross section modified by a VDM form factor and polarization-state factors. The net dilepton yield from thermal pi+ pi- annihilation near the rho peak was then estimated by multiplying the bare production rate by a survival probability that accounts for reabsorption and rehadronization during the fireball evolution.

## Key numbers
- Real photon mean free paths at temperatures 100, 150, and 200 MeV: 1.80, 0.08, and 0.02 Angstroms respectively
- Virtual photon mean free path at the rho mass and T = 170 MeV: approximately 10 fm (including both absorption and thermal reconversion to hadrons)
- Net dilepton yield suppression at the rho peak: 40% reduction compared to the bare yield, for a fireball with average temperature 170 MeV and four-volume 3 x 10^4 fm^4
- Vector dominance coupling constant for rho: f_rho approximately 5.1
- a1 width at centroid: 400 MeV

## Context
This work extends the long-standing suggestion by Feinberg (1976) and Shuryak (1978, 1980) that photons and dileptons are penetrating probes of hadronic matter, providing the first quantitative calculation of mean free paths beyond back-of-the-envelope estimates. Previous model calculations for dilepton production (e.g., Gale and Kapusta, 1991; Xiong, Shuryak and Brown, 1992) used "bare" production rates assuming negligible losses. The results imply that future dynamical models of dilepton production near vector meson masses must account for possible loss effects, and that conclusions about rho melting or collision broadening (Haglin, 1995, 1996; Gao et al., 1999) are complicated by these reabsorption/rehadronization effects.
