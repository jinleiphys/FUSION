# Strange Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> Disoriented chiral condensate can produce novel fluctuations of kaons as well as pions. Robust statistical observables can be used to extract the novel fluctuations from background contributions in K-short and K-charged correlation measurements in nuclear collisions. To illustrate how this can be done, I present new event-generator computations of these observables.

# Strange Disoriented Chiral Condensate

## Key claim

Disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) can produce novel fluctuations of kaons as well as pions, but only if many small DCC regions (approximately 2 fm in size) are produced per event; robust statistical observables can extract these fluctuations from background contributions in K-short and K-charged correlation measurements, though the effect is subtle and requires statistical analysis rather than event-by-event reconstruction.

## Method

The author computes the probability distribution for the neutral kaon fraction from many small, independent DCC domains by folding the single-domain distribution (uniform in the neutral fraction) with a Gaussian thermal background distribution. Event-generator simulations using HIJING (50,000 events for Au+Au at 200 A GeV) are performed to compute the robust isospin covariance and the dynamic isospin observable, and these are compared to wounded nucleon model predictions to isolate DCC effects from conventional fluctuation sources.

## Key numbers

- DCC domain size of about 2 fm, consistent with predictions from two-flavor linear sigma model simulations (GGP1) and with the Kapusta-Wong (2002) explanation of Omega and anti-Omega baryon enhancement at 17 A GeV at the CERN SPS
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry breaking: T_c ~ 150 MeV
- Pion mass: 140 MeV; leading sigma candidate mass: 800 ± 400 MeV
- HIJING simulations of central Au+Au at 200 A GeV in rapidity range -0.5 < y < 0.5 yield nu_dyn^{c0} ≈ -0.002 for 47 K+ and 44 K_S^0 on average
- For many small DCC domains (n >> 1), the neutral kaon fraction distribution tends toward a Gaussian with mean <f> = 1/2 and standard deviation sigma = {12n}^{-1/2}
- DCC contribution to the dynamic isospin observable for kaons: nu_dyn^{c0}(K DCC) = 4*beta_K*(beta_K/(3n) - 1/N_K), where beta_K is the fraction of kaons from DCC and n is the number of domains
- Estimates assume 10 DCC domains contribute kaons in the range -0.5 < y < 0.5 for b_0 ~ 6 fm, with DCC fraction beta_0 between 10% and 20%

## Context

This work extends the DCC signal analysis from pions to kaons, motivated by the Kapusta-Wong (2002) explanation of Omega and anti-Omega baryon enhancement at the CERN SPS in terms of many small DCC regions. It builds on prior work by Gavin and Kapusta (2002) on kaon fluctuations from DCC, and on studies of pion fluctuations from many small DCC by Amado and Lu (2002) and Chow and Cohen (2002). The paper provides statistical observables (robust covariance and dynamic isospin observable) that can be applied at RHIC and LHC to search for DCC effects, and enables future work using URQMD simulations to study the effect of final state scattering on these observables.
