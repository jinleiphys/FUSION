# The ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\,\,\,\,4}n$ system

> Using local central Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon interactions reproducing the low-energy parameters and phase shifs of the $nn$ system and the latest updates of the $nΛ$ and $ΛΛ$ Nijmegen ESC08c potentials we study the possible existence of a ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\,\,\,\,4}n$ bound state. Our results indicate that the ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\,\,\,\,4}n$ is unbound, being just above threshold. We discuss the role played by the $^1S_0$ $nn$ repulsive term of the Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon interaction.

# The ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\,\,\,\,4}n$ system

## Key claim

The ${}_{ΛΛ}^{\,\,\,\,4}n$ (nnΛΛ) system with quantum numbers (I)J^P=(1)0^+ is unbound and appears just above threshold, contrary to earlier suggestions that it might be a Borromean state. The system only becomes bound if the attractive part of the NΛ interaction is artificially increased by a factor of 1.1 or the ΛΛ attraction by a factor of 1.8, both of which would destroy agreement with established phase-shift data.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body problem using a generalized variational method with nonrelativistic Hamiltonian and local central Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon interactions. For the nn subsystem they used the Malfliet-Tjon I model with parameters from Gib90, while for the NΛ and ΛΛ subsystems they fitted the attractive and repulsive Yukawa parameters to reproduce the low-energy parameters and phase shifts of the most recent ESC08c Nijmegen potentials from Nae15 and Nag15. The variational spatial wave function was constructed as a linear combination of generalized Gaussians depending on six scalar quantities built from Jacobi coordinates, with proper permutation symmetry under exchange of identical neutrons and identical Λ's.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton (³_ΛH) binding energy: 144 keV (calculated with the same two-body interactions used for the four-body study)
- nnΛ system: unbound (no binding energy reported)
- The nnΛΛ system becomes bound only when the attractive part of the NΛ interaction is multiplied by g_{NΛ} = 1.1 (with g_{NN} = g_{ΛΛ} = 1)
- The nnΛΛ system becomes bound only when the attractive part of the ΛΛ interaction is multiplied by g_{ΛΛ} ≥ 1.8
- nn scattering length: a = −23.56 fm, effective range: r₀ = 2.88 fm (from the Malfliet-Tjon I model)
- ΛN ¹S₀ scattering length: a = −2.62 fm, effective range: r₀ = 3.17 fm (from fit to ESC08c)
- ΛN ³S₁ scattering length: a = −1.72 fm, effective range: r₀ = 3.50 fm (from fit to ESC08c)
- ΛΛ ¹S₀ scattering length: a = −0.853 fm, effective range: r₀ = 5.126 fm (from fit to ESC08c)

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Ric15, which used simpler single-Yukawa or Morse parametrizations and suggested the possibility of a Borromean nnΛΛ state. The present calculation improves on that by using potentials that reproduce not only low-energy parameters but also the full phase shifts of the ESC08c Nijmegen potentials, and by including the repulsive core in the ¹S₀ nn interaction, which is known from Mal70 to be crucial for correctly describing the triton binding energy. The result that the system is unbound is consistent with the earlier work of Lek14, though that study used repulsive gaussian-type potentials for all two-body subsystems. The authors note that three-body forces (as studied by Bev77 for the triton and ⁴He) and explicit treatment of the ΛN ↔ ΣN coupling (which Gar14, Hiy14, and others have shown is necessary to bind the hypertriton) remain as future refinements that could potentially affect the conclusion.
