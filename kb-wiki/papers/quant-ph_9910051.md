# Barrier Penetration for Supersymmetric Shape-Invariant Potentials

> Exact reflection and transmission coefficients for supersymmetric shape-invariant potentials barriers are calculated by an analytical continuation of the asymptotic wave functions obtained via the introduction of new generalized ladder operators. The general form of the wave function is obtained by the use of the F-matrix formalism of Froman and Froman which is related to the evolution of asymptotic wave function coefficients.

## Key claim

Exact reflection and transmission coefficients for supersymmetric shape-invariant potential barriers are derived by analytically continuing asymptotic wave functions obtained via generalized ladder operators, using the F-matrix formalism of Froman and Froman. The method works for any shape-invariant potential whose superpotential satisfies the condition \(W(x,a_n) = W(x,a_1) + (n-1)\zeta(x)\), though the authors note that if this condition is not satisfied the analytic continuation "may still be done, but will be more complicated."

## Method

The authors construct ladder operators \(\hat B_+\) and \(\hat B_-\) from the supersymmetric shape-invariance algebra and use them to generate exact wave functions for barrier problems. They then evaluate these wave functions asymptotically using the F-matrix formalism, which relates the coefficients of incoming and outgoing waves at \(x \to -\infty\) and \(x \to +\infty\). The transmission and reflection coefficients are extracted from the F-matrix elements for three specific shape-invariant potentials: the parabolic barrier, the Morse barrier, and the Eckart barrier.

## Key numbers

- Parabolic barrier: transmission coefficient \(T = 1/(1 + e^{\pi\lambda})\) and reflection coefficient \(R = e^{\pi\lambda}/(1 + e^{\pi\lambda})\), where \(\lambda = (V_0 - E)/\varepsilon_0\) and \(\varepsilon_0 = \frac{1}{2}\hbar\Omega\).
- Morse barrier: transmission coefficient \(T = e^{-\frac{\pi}{2}(s+r)} \sinh(\pi r) / \cosh[\frac{\pi}{2}(s-r)]\), where \(s = \sqrt{V_0/\varepsilon}\), \(r = \sqrt{E/\varepsilon}\), and \(\varepsilon = \hbar^2/(8mb^2)\).
- Morse barrier: reflection coefficient \(R = e^{-\pi r} \cosh[\frac{\pi}{2}(s+r)] / \cosh[\frac{\pi}{2}(s-r)]\).
- Eckart barrier: transmission and reflection coefficients are given implicitly through the F-matrix elements derived from the asymptotic wave functions; explicit numerical values are not provided in the text.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to supersymmetric shape-invariant potentials developed by Gendenshtein (1983), Balantekin (1985), and Cooper et al. (1995) from bound-state problems to barrier penetration. It also builds on the operator method for scattering amplitudes introduced by Khare and Sukhatme (1988). The paper provides exact tunneling probabilities for shape-invariant barriers, which previous work by the same community had only treated approximately via supersymmetric semiclassical methods (1994). The results enable exact calculations of quantum tunneling for a class of potentials relevant to fusion reactions and metastable state decay.
