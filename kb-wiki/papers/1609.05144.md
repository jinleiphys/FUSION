# Photoproduction of a large invariant mass $γρ$ pair at small momentum transfer

> The collinear factorization framework allows to describe the exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,ρ$ pair in the generalized Bjorken regime in terms of a perturbatively calculable coefficient function and universal generalized parton distributions. The kinematics are defined by a large invariant mass of the $γρ$ pair and a small transverse momentum of the final nucleon. We calculate the scattering amplitude at leading order in $α_s$ and the differential cross sections for the process where the $ρ-$meson is either longitudinally or transversely polarized, in the kinematics of the near future Jlab experiments. Our estimate of the cross section demonstrates that this process is measurable at JLab 12-GeV.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma \rho$ pair with large invariant mass and small transverse momentum of the final nucleon can be described within collinear QCD factorization, and the cross section estimates indicate that this process is measurable at JLab 12-GeV, provided that the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are not too small.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for the process $\gamma + N \rightarrow \gamma + \rho^0 + N'$ at leading order in $\alpha_s$ using collinear factorization, where the hard subprocess $\gamma (q\bar q) \to \gamma \rho$ factorizes from universal GPDs and the $\rho$ meson distribution amplitude. The amplitude is computed separately for longitudinally polarized $\rho$ (chiral-even GPDs) and transversely polarized $\rho$ (chiral-odd GPDs), with 20 Feynman diagrams contributing in the chiral-even case and 8 in the chiral-odd case. The differential cross sections are then evaluated for kinematics accessible at JLab 12-GeV.

## Key numbers

* For $S_{\gamma N} = 20$ GeV$^2$, $M^2_{\gamma\rho} = 4$ GeV$^2$, and $-t = (-t)_{min}$, the differential cross section $\frac{d\sigma}{dt \, du' \, dM^2_{\gamma\rho}}$ is shown as a function of $-u'$ (in GeV$^2$), with the $u$-quark contribution dominating over the $d$-quark contribution due to the charge effect.
* The less differential cross section $\frac{d\sigma}{dM^2_{\gamma\rho}}$ is presented for $S_{\gamma N}$ values in the set 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20 GeV$^2$, covering the JLab 12-GeV energy range, with units of nb $\cdot$ GeV$^{-2}$.
* The invariant mass squared $M^2_{\gamma\rho}$ is approximately given by $\vec{p}_t^2 / (\alpha \bar{\alpha})$ in the generalized Bjorken limit, with $\bar{\alpha} \approx \alpha_\rho$ and $\xi = 2\tau/(2-\tau)$ where $\tau \approx M^2_{\gamma\rho} / (S_{\gamma N} - M^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) to the exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma \rho$ pair. It builds on the factorization of exclusive scattering at fixed angle and large energy by Lepage and Brodsky (1980), and on the factorization procedure for exclusive meson electroproduction near the forward region. The study of such $2\to3$ processes was initiated in Ref. IPST-eps for diffractive production of two vector mesons, and a similar strategy was advocated by Beiyad (2010) and Kumano to extract chiral-even GPDs. The results enable a new experimental channel for extracting both chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs at JLab 12-GeV, providing a test of GPD universality.
