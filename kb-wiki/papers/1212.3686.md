# Symmetry energy softening in nuclear matter with non-nucleonic constituents

> We study the trend of the nuclear symmetry energy in relativistic mean-field models with appearance of the hyperon and quark degrees of freedom at high densities. On the pure hadron level, we focus on the role of $Λ$ hyperons in influencing the symmetry energy both at given fractions and at charge and chemical equilibriums. The softening of the nuclear symmetry energy is observed with the inclusion of the $Λ$ hyperons that suppresses the nucleon fraction. In the phase with the admixture of quarks and hadrons, the equation of state is established on the Gibbs conditions. With the increase of the quark volume fraction in denser and denser matter, the apparent nuclear symmetry energy decreases till to disappear. This softening would have associations with the observations which need detailed discriminations in dense matter with the admixture of new degrees of freedom created by heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy softens (decreases) at high densities when non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, specifically Λ hyperons and quarks, are included, but this softening is model-dependent and depends on parameters such as the bag constant in the MIT bag model; the effect is most apparent in the mixed hadron-quark phase where the nuclear symmetry energy eventually vanishes as the quark volume fraction reaches unity.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (SLC, SLCd, NL3, NL3w3, TM1) to compute the nuclear symmetry energy in pure hadronic matter with Λ hyperons, and then constructed the hadron-quark mixed phase using Gibbs conditions for mechanical and chemical equilibrium, with quark matter described by the MIT bag model. The symmetry energy was derived from the parabolic approximation of the energy per nucleon, with formulas accounting for hyperon fractions and the quark volume fraction Y in the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for the hadron-quark phase transition ranges from approximately 1.6–2.2 ρ₀ for a bag constant B = (160 MeV)⁴, with the symmetry energy vanishing below 3 ρ₀ for all models used.
- For B = (180 MeV)⁴, the symmetry energy shows an apparent decrease after the hadron-quark phase transition, with the critical density varying by model.
- The symmetry energy in hyperonized matter is suppressed by the factor ρ_N/ρ_B, where ρ_N is nucleon density and ρ_B is total baryon density, as given in Eq. (esymy).
- For a larger bag constant B = (200 MeV)⁴, the divergence in critical density and vanishing density of the symmetry energy becomes more appreciable across different models.

## Context

This work extends studies of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-normal densities, where theoretical predictions remain uncertain (Xiao 2009, Feng 2010, Ru 2011), and addresses the role of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom that appear around 2–4 ρ₀ (Glendenning 2001, Jiang 2006, 2012). The softening of the symmetry energy due to hyperons and quarks has implications for interpreting heavy-ion collision data (e.g., π⁻/π⁺ ratios from FOPI/GSI) and for neutron star structure, particularly in light of the 2 M⊙ pulsar J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010), which requires that the equation of state not be softened too much. The authors note that future work should investigate whether quark interactions stiffen the equation of state and affect the symmetry energy.
