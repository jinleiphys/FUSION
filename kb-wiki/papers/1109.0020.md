# Anomaly-induced charges in baryons

> We show that quantum chiral anomaly of QCD in magnetic backgrounds induces a novel structure of electric charge inside baryons. To illustrate the anomaly effect, we employ the Skyrme model for baryons, with the anomaly-induced gauged Wess-Zumino term (π_0 + (multi-pion)) E_i B_i. Due to this term, the Skyrmions giving a local pion condensation ((π_0 + (multi-pion)) \neq 0) necessarily become a local charge source, in the background magnetic field B_i. We present detailed evaluation of the anomaly effects, and calculate the total induced charge, for various baryons in the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly of QCD in a constant magnetic field background induces an additional electric charge distribution inside baryons, which can modify the Gell-Mann–Nishijima formula by a term \(Q_{\rm anm}/2\); this effect is demonstrated using the Skyrme model with the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term, and the total induced charge depends on the orientation of the magnetic field relative to the baryon.

## Method

The authors employ the Skyrme model for baryons, treating baryons as topological solitons (Skyrmions) made of a local pion condensate, and couple it to electromagnetism via the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term. They solve the classical Skyrme equation for a hedgehog ansatz with a constant background magnetic field, then quantize the Skyrmion collective coordinates to evaluate the anomaly-induced electric current and charge for baryon quantum states (nucleons and \(\Delta\) baryons). The induced charge is computed from the spatial integral of the zero-th component of the anomalous current \(j^\mu_{\rm anm} = -\epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \frac{e^2}{16\pi^2} (\partial_\nu \bar A_\rho) P_\sigma\), where \(P_\sigma\) involves the pion field.

## Key numbers

* For the physical pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.263\) (dimensionless, in units of \(1/(e_s F_\pi)\)), the dimensionless coefficient \(c_0\) that determines the induced charge for a magnetic field along the \(x_3\)-axis is \(c_0 = -10.2\).
* For massless pions (\(m_\pi = 0\)), the coefficient is \(c_0 = -14.1 + C\), where \(C \simeq 8.638\) is an asymptotic factor from the profile function.
* The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 108\) MeV, the pion mass is \(M_\pi = 137\) MeV, and the Skyrme coupling constant is \(e_s = 4.84\).
* The induced charge is zero when the magnetic field is perpendicular to the \(x_3\)-axis (e.g., \(\vec B \propto (1,0,0)\)), and non-zero when \(\vec B \propto (0,0,1)\).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chiral anomaly effects in baryons, analogous to the Witten effect (Witten, 1979) where monopoles acquire electric charge via a \(\theta\) term, and the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev, 2008; Fukushima, 2008) in heavy ion collisions. The results provide a basis for studying baryon charge structure in strong magnetic fields relevant to neutron stars and heavy ion collisions, and correct the Gell-Mann–Nishijima formula under such conditions. The paper builds on the Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961) and the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term (Brihaye, 1984) to compute the anomaly-induced charge for quantized baryon states.
