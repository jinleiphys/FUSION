# Hard Probes in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> Hard QCD processes in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions become increasingly relevant and they can be used as probes of the dense matter formed during the violent scatterings. We will discuss how one can use these hard probes to study the properties of the dense matter and the associated phenomenologies. In particular, we study the effect of jet quenching due to medium-induced energy loss on inclusive particle $p_T$ distributions and investigate how one can improve the measurement of parton energy loss in direct photon events

## Key claim

Medium-induced radiative energy loss of a high-energy parton traversing a dense QCD medium can be used as a probe of the dense matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the energy loss cannot be measured directly from single-particle spectra because the suppression of high-\(p_T\) particles results from jet quenching over a wide range of initial transverse energies; instead, measuring the \(p_T\) distribution of charged hadrons opposite a tagged direct photon provides a direct measurement of the modified jet fragmentation function and thus the parton energy loss.

## Method

The author models the modification of jet fragmentation functions due to parton energy loss using a phenomenological Poisson distribution for the number of inelastic scatterings, with a constant energy loss per scattering \(\epsilon_a\) and mean-free-path \(\lambda_a\). The modified fragmentation function is then convolved with the jet cross section, including nuclear parton shadowing, to compute the inclusive charged-hadron \(p_T\) spectrum in central \(A+A\) collisions. For \(\gamma+\)jet events, the \(p_T\) distribution of hadrons opposite a tagged direct photon is related to the inclusive fragmentation function, averaged over the \(E_T\) smearing of the jet from initial-state radiation and over the production position in the transverse plane.

## Key numbers

* In central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the ratio \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) of the charged-particle \(p_T\) spectrum to that in \(pp\) collisions (normalized by binary collisions) is calculated for assumed quark energy losses \(dE_q/dx = 1\) GeV/fm and 2 GeV/fm, showing suppression at high \(p_T\).
* For \(\gamma+\)jet events at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the inclusive fragmentation function ratio (with vs. without energy loss) at \(z=0.4\) is plotted as a function of \(dE_q/dx\) for mean-free-paths \(\lambda_q = 0.5\) fm and 1 fm; the ratio decreases with increasing \(dE_q/dx\).
* The suppression factor at \(z=0.4\) as a function of \(A^{1/3}\) is shown for constant \(dE_q/dx = 0.5\) GeV/fm and for distance-dependent \(dE_q/dx = 0.2 (L/{\rm fm})\) GeV/fm.
* The rate of direct photon production in central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with luminosity \({\cal L}=2\times 10^{26}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) and 100 operation days per year is: 20500 events/year for \(E_T^{\gamma}=7\) GeV, 3500 for 10 GeV, 400 for 15 GeV, and 70 for 20 GeV.
* The \(\pi^0\) production rate at \(p_T=10\) GeV/\(c\) is about 20 times larger than direct photons without energy loss; with \(dE_q/dx=1\) GeV/fm and \(\lambda_q=1\) fm, the \(\pi^0\) rate is about 4 times higher than direct photons.

## Context

This work extends the author’s earlier proposal with Gyulassy (1992) to use single-particle spectra to study jet energy loss, and the proposal with Huang and Sarcevic to use \(\gamma+\)jet events for a direct measurement of the modified fragmentation function. It builds on theoretical studies of medium-induced radiative energy loss and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect by Baier et al. (1995) and others, which showed that the energy loss per distance is proportional to the total path length. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC of the relationship between parton energy loss and the density of the produced medium, and can distinguish between constant and distance-dependent energy loss models by comparing different nucleus-nucleus collisions or centralities.
