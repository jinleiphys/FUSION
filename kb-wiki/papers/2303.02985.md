# Description of inclusive $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ reaction with the semiclassical distorted wave model

> The description of deuteron-induced inclusive reactions has been an important subject in direct nuclear reaction studies and nuclear data science. For proton-induced inclusive processes, the semiclassical distorted wave model (SCDW) is one of the most successful models based on quantum mechanics. We improve SCDW for deuteron-induced inclusive processes and clarify the importance of the proper treatment of the kinematics of the deuteron inside a nucleus. The double differential cross section (DDX) of the inclusive deuteron-emission process $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ is described by one-step SCDW. The changes in the kinematics due to the distortion effect, the refraction effect, is taken into account by the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA). The calculated DDXs of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ reasonably reproduce experimental data in the small energy-transfer region and at forward and middle angles with some exceptions. The angular distributions of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ are improved by including the refraction effect. The proper treatment of the changes in the kinematics of the deuteron inside a nucleus is necessary in describing the ($d$,$d'x$) reaction. The effect of the changes on the DDX of $(d,d^{\prime}x)$ is significant compared to on the proton-induced inclusive process $(p,p^{\prime}x)$ because of the stronger distortion effect on the deuteron.

## Key claim

The semiclassical distorted wave model (SCDW) is extended to deuteron-induced inclusive reactions, and the proper treatment of the changes in deuteron kinematics inside the nucleus, the refraction effect, is shown to be necessary for reproducing experimental double differential cross sections, particularly at middle angles where the asymptotic momentum approximation fails.

## Method

The double differential cross section (DDX) of the inclusive deuteron-emission process (d,d'x) is described by a one-step SCDW using the local Fermi-gas model for nuclear single-particle states and the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA) for the deuteron distorted waves. The LSCA incorporates the refraction effect by using a real local momentum derived from the complex distorting potential via local energy conservation, while the asymptotic momentum approximation (AMA) neglects this effect by fixing the momentum to its asymptotic value. The free d-N scattering cross section is taken from a numerical table fitted to experimental p-d scattering data from 5 to 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- DDXs are calculated for targets including 27Al, 58Ni, 208Pb, 93Nb, 181Ta, 90Zr, and 232Th at incident energies of 100, 80, and 70 MeV.
- The LSCA extends the kinematically-allowed center-of-mass scattering angle for 58Ni(d,d'x) at 80 MeV with energy transfer ω=5 MeV from about 42° (AMA limit) up to 73°.
- The range of the deuteron-nucleon interaction is about 2.2 fm, and LSCA is required to be valid for propagation up to about 0.7 fm based on the factor 1/(Ad+1)=1/3 for the relative coordinate s.
- The Perey factor for nonlocality of the deuteron distorting potential uses a range parameter β=0.54.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density uses radial parameter rρ=1.15 fm and diffuseness aρ=0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the SCDW, previously successful for proton-induced inclusive processes (p,p'x) as developed by Luo (1991) and Kawai (1992), to deuteron-induced reactions. It contrasts with the DEURACS code system by Nakayama (2016, 2020, 2021), which uses the Glauber model with eikonal and adiabatic approximations that are questionable at low incident energy or for large momentum transfer. The present model enables future description of the inclusive (d,nx) reaction important for nuclear data science, particularly for the IFMIF neutron source project, and will require extension to multi-step processes and explicit treatment of deuteron breakup.
