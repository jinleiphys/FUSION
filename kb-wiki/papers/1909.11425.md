# Spin-dipole mode in a trapped Fermi gas near unitarity

> We theoretically investigate the spin-dipole oscillation of a strongly interacting Fermi gas in a harmonic trap. By using a combined diagrammatic strong-coupling theory with a local density approximation and a sum rule approach, we clarify the temperature dependence of the spin-dipole frequency near the unitarity, which is deeply related to the spin susceptibility, as well as pairing correlations. While the spin-dipole frequency exactly coincides with the trap frequency in a non-interacting Fermi gas, it is shown to remarkably be enhanced in the superfluid state, because of the suppression of the spin degree of freedom due to the spin-singlet Cooper-pair formation. In strongly interacting Fermi gases, this enhancement occurs even above the superfluid phase transition temperature, due to the strong pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The spin-dipole frequency in a trapped unitary Fermi gas is enhanced above the trap frequency not only in the superfluid state but also above the superfluid critical temperature, due to strong pairing correlations (preformed Cooper pairs) that suppress the spin susceptibility; the enhancement is therefore a signature of pairing fluctuations rather than solely of the superfluid order parameter.

## Method

The authors combine an extended T-matrix approximation (ETMA) with a local density approximation (LDA) to compute the local spin susceptibility of a trapped two-component Fermi gas with a contact interaction. They then use a sum-rule approach to obtain an upper bound for the spin-dipole frequency from the ratio of the energy-weighted sum rule m1 to the inverse-energy-weighted sum rule m-1, which depends on the second spatial moment of the local spin susceptibility. The superfluid order parameter is determined self-consistently from the gapless condition of the Nambu-Goldstone mode.

## Key numbers

* At unitarity, the calculated spin-dipole frequency shows excellent agreement with the experimental result at T = 0.151 TF (TF is the Fermi temperature of an ideal two-component gas).
* The LDA critical temperature for the unitary gas is Tc = 0.29 TF in ETMA, compared to Tc = 0.37 TF in BCS mean-field theory.
* The frequency shift due to pairing correlations beyond mean field, δωSD = (ωSD_ETMA - ωSD_BCS)/ωSD_ETMA, peaks near T = 0.77 Tc = 0.23 TF.
* In the strong-coupling high-temperature regime, the spin-dipole frequency deviates from the trap frequency when T is less than or comparable to the two-body binding energy Eb = 1/(m as^2).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of spin-dipole oscillations in ultracold Fermi gases, building on the sum-rule approach of Recati (2003) and the ETMA formalism of Kashimura (2012) and Tajima (2016, 2017). It directly addresses the experimental observation of enhanced spin-dipole frequency in a unitary 6Li gas by Valtolina (2017). The results connect to the broader study of spin-gap phenomena and pseudogap physics in strongly interacting Fermi gases, and the authors note an analogy with the giant dipole resonance in nuclei (Lipparini, 1989). The work enables future studies of mass-imbalanced Fermi-Fermi mixtures, such as dysprosium-potassium mixtures.
