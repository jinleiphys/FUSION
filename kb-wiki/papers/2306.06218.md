# Evidence against a strong first-order phase transition in neutron star cores: impact of new data

> With the aim of exploring the evidence for or against phase transitions in cold and dense baryonic matter, the inference of the sound speed and equation-of-state for dense matter in neutron stars is extended in view of recent new observational data. The impact of the heavy (2.35 $M_\odot$) black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and of the unusually light supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 is inspected. In addition a detailed re-analysis is performed of the low-density constraint based on chiral effective field theory and of the perturbative QCD constraint at asymptotically high densities, in order to clarify the influence of these constraints on the inference procedure. The trace anomaly measure, $Δ= 1/3 - P/\varepsilon$, is also computed and discussed. A systematic Bayes factor assessment quantifies the evidence (or non-evidence) of low averaged sound speeds $(c_s^2 \leq 0.1)$, a prerequisite for a phase transition, within the range of densities realized in the core of neutron stars. One of the consequences of including PSR J0952-0607 in the data base is a further stiffening of the equation-of-state, resulting for a 2.1 solar-mass neutron star in a reduced central density of less than five times the equilibrium density of normal nuclear matter at the 68\% level. The evidence against small sound speeds in neutron star cores is further strengthened. Within the inferred 68\% posterior credible bands, only a weak first-order phase transition with a coexistence density interval $Δn/n \lesssim 0.2$ would be compatible with the observed data.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, within the 68% posterior credible bands, only a weak first-order phase transition with a coexistence density interval Δn/n ≲ 0.2 would be compatible with current observational data, contradicting the possibility of a strong first-order phase transition in neutron star cores.

## Method

The authors perform a Bayesian inference of the neutron star equation of state using a parametrization of the squared sound speed as segment-wise linear interpolations, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. They incorporate likelihoods from Shapiro time delay measurements, NICER x-ray data, gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425, and the newly reported black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, along with constraints from chiral effective field theory up to 1.3 times nuclear saturation density and perturbative QCD at asymptotically high densities.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0952-0607 mass: M = 2.35 ± 0.17 solar masses (Romani 2022)
- HESS J1731-347 mass: M = 0.77 (+0.20, -0.17) solar masses, radius R = 10.4 (+0.86, -0.78) km (Doroshenko 2022)
- For a 2.1 solar-mass neutron star, including PSR J0952-0607 yields a reduced central density of less than five times the equilibrium density of normal nuclear matter at the 68% level
- The maximum density for chiral effective field theory applicability is taken as n_ChEFT = 1.3 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Perturbative QCD is applied at chemical potential μ_pQCD = 2.6 GeV, corresponding to densities above about 40 n_0
- The coexistence density interval compatible with data is Δn/n ≲ 0.2 at the 68% level

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian studies (Brandes 2023) by incorporating new observational data, particularly the heavy black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 and the unusually light supernova remnant HESS J1731-347. The results strengthen the evidence against small sound speeds in neutron star cores found in prior work, and the Bayes factor analysis quantifies the non-evidence of low averaged sound speeds (c_s^2 ≤ 0.1) that would be a prerequisite for a phase transition. The findings enable tighter constraints on the possible phase structure of dense baryonic matter and inform future searches for phase transitions in neutron star interiors.
