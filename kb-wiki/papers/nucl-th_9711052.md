# Delta excitation in K^+-nucleus collisions

> We present calculations for Δexcitation in the (K^+,K^+) reaction in nuclei. The background from quasielastic K^+ scattering in the Δregion is also evaluated and shown to be quite small in some kinematical regions, so as to allow for a clean identification of the Δexcitation strength. Nuclear effects tied to the Δrenormalization in the nucleus are considered and the reaction is shown to provide new elements to enrich our knowledge of the Δproperties in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculations for Delta excitation in (K+,K+) reactions on nuclei, showing that the quasielastic background is sufficiently small at certain kinematical regions (particularly at larger angles of 20° and 30°) to allow clean identification of the Delta excitation strength, though the authors hedge that the background at 10° is substantial.

## Method

The authors evaluate the K+ selfenergy in nuclear matter from a model where the elementary K+N→K+Δ reaction proceeds via ρ-exchange, using coupling constants fitted to data from K+p→KNπ. They then obtain the nuclear cross section via the local density approximation, incorporate the Δ selfenergy in the medium from prior work (Oset et al., 1987), include RPA corrections to the Δh propagation in the transverse channel, and apply eikonal distortion factors for the K+ waves using K+N cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At K+ momentum 1 GeV/c on 12C, the Δ excitation cross section is shown for angles 10°, 20°, and 30°; the quasielastic background is relatively smaller at 20° and 30° than at 10°.
- At K+ momentum 1.25 GeV/c, the relative strength of Δ excitation with respect to quasielastic background is bigger than at 1 GeV/c.
- At K+ momentum 1.5 GeV/c and angle 30°, the Δ strength is bigger than the quasielastic one, but the quasielastic contribution has a wide bump that induces appreciable background below the Δ peak.
- The pionic decay content of the Δ strength at 1.5 GeV/c and 10° is only about 70% of the corresponding strength for a free Δ, with about 30% of produced pions reabsorbed in 12C.
- The ρ NΔ vertex uses a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 2 GeV, and the coupling constants are Cρ = 2, f*2/4π = 0.36, and f̃ρ = 4.2 (30% higher than the SU(3) value of 3.1).
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' is taken as 0.6.

## Context

This work extends studies of Δ excitation in nuclei from pion-nucleus collisions, photonuclear reactions, electron scattering, and proton-induced reactions to the unexplored K+-nucleus channel. The authors use the Δ selfenergy from Oset et al. (1987), which had been tested in elastic pion-nucleus scattering, quasielastic reactions, charge exchange, absorption, photonuclear reactions, and electronuclear reactions. The reaction offers advantages over the (3He,t) reaction studied by other groups, where the cross section falls by about two orders of magnitude by 5°, whereas here the cross section remains sizeable up to about 30°, providing a wider spectrum of excitation energies and momenta to study Δ properties in nuclei.
