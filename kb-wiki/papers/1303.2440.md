# Heavy dilepton in nucleus nucleus collision at LHC energy

> We present a study of $τ^+ τ^-$ lepton pair production in Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV. The larger mass of tau lepton compared to electron and muon leads to considerably small hadronic contribution to the $τ^+ τ^-$ pair invariant mass (M) distribution relative to the production from thermal partonic sources. The quark-antiquark annihilation processes via intermediary virtual photon, Z and Higgs bosons have been considered for the tau lepton production. The contribution from Drell-Yan process is found to dominate over thermal yield for $τ^+ τ^-$ pair mass from 4 to 20 GeV at the LHC energy. We also present the ratio of $τ$ lepton pair yields for nucleus-nucleus collisions relative to yields from p + p collisions scaled by number of binary collisions at LHC energies as a function $τ$ lepton pair inavariant mass. The ratio is found to be significantly above unity for the mass range 4 to 6 GeV. This indicates the possibility of detecting $τ^+ τ^-$ pair from quark gluon plasma (QGP) in the mass window 4 to 6 GeV.

## Key claim

The Drell–Yan process dominates over thermal partonic production for tau lepton pair invariant masses from 4 to 20 GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, but the ratio of nucleus-nucleus yields to binary-collision-scaled p+p yields rises above unity (starting at 1.4 at 4 GeV) specifically in the 4 to 6 GeV mass window, indicating a possible signal of quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors computed tau lepton pair production from quark-antiquark annihilation via virtual photon, Z boson, and Higgs boson mediators using the corresponding matrix elements with standard model parameters (m_Z = 91 GeV, m_H = 120 GeV, sinθ_w = 0.234). The Drell–Yan contribution was obtained by folding the parton-level cross section with CTEQ5M parton distribution functions and applying a Glauber model for Pb+Pb collisions at 0–5% centrality (impact parameter b = 3.6 fm). The thermal partonic contribution was calculated using ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance, an initial temperature T_i = 700 MeV, thermalization time τ_i = 0.08 fm/c, and a lattice QCD equation of state from the MILC collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 5.5 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Tau lepton mass: M_τ = 1.78 GeV (used in calculations; text also states ~1.77 GeV)
- Initial temperature: T_i = 700 MeV; thermalization time: τ_i = 0.08 fm/c
- Transition temperature: T_c = 175 MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_f = 120 MeV
- Number of binary collisions for 0–5% centrality: N_coll = 1369.477 at b = 3.6 fm
- Ratio of Pb+Pb yield to binary-collision-scaled p+p yield: 1.4 at invariant mass 4 GeV, decreasing to unity above 6 GeV
- Mass range where Drell–Yan dominates over thermal yield: 4 to 20 GeV
- Mass window where ratio exceeds unity: 4 to 6 GeV

## Context

This work extends studies of heavy dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions to the tau lepton channel, motivated by the tau's larger mass (≈1.77 GeV) which avoids the hadronic resonance background (ω, ρ, φ) that dominates in the electron and muon sectors. The calculation uses the hydrodynamic framework of Bjorken (1983) with initial conditions from Armesto (2008) and the lattice QCD equation of state from the MILC collaboration. The results suggest that the 4–6 GeV mass window for tau pairs could provide a cleaner probe of quark-gluon plasma at LHC energies than lighter dileptons, and the ratio method using p+p baseline measurements from the same energy enables experimental extraction of the thermal signal.
