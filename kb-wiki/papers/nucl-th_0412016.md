# Approach to the Rotation Driven Vibrational to Axially Rotational Shape Phase Transition Along the Yrast Line of a Nucleus

> By analyzing the potential energy surface, the shape phase diagram and the energy spectrum of the nucleus in U(5) symmetry in the IBM, we propose that the U(5) symmetry with parameters $(A+B) < 0$ may be a model to describe the rotation driven vibrational to axially rotational phase transition along the yrast line. With such a model, we have described successfully the observed rotation driven shape phase transition along the yrast line of individual nucleus and proposed some other empirical evidences.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the U(5) dynamical symmetry of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), when the Hamiltonian parameters satisfy \((A+B) < 0\), can serve as a model for the rotation-driven vibrational to axially rotational shape phase transition along the yrast line of an individual nucleus. The authors hedge that this is a "may be" model, and the transition is specifically a first-order phase transition occurring at a critical angular momentum \(L_c\).

## Method

The authors analyze the potential energy surface (PES) of a nucleus in the U(5) symmetry limit of the IBM using a coherent state formalism with angular momentum projection. They derive an approximate Landau-type energy functional in terms of the deformation parameter \(\beta\) and angular momentum \(L\), and examine how the shape of the PES changes with \(L\) when \((A+B) < 0\). They then fit the resulting energy formulas (Eqs. 9 and 10) to experimental yrast-band data for several even-even nuclei to extract model parameters and identify the critical angular momentum where the transition occurs.

## Key numbers

- For a system with \(N=15\) bosons and parameters \(\varepsilon_d = 0.80\) MeV, \(A = -0.025\) MeV, \(B = -0.01\) MeV, \(C = 0.004\) MeV, the critical angular momentum is \(L_c = 8\hbar\).
- For \(^{102}\)Ru, the fitted parameters are \(\varepsilon_d = 0.5281\) MeV, \(A = -0.009376\) MeV, \(B = -0.008819\) MeV, \(C = 0.01475\) MeV, with total boson number \(N = 20\) and critical angular momentum \(L_c = 12\).
- For \(^{112}\)Cd: \(\varepsilon_d = 0.6581\) MeV, \(A = -0.007640\) MeV, \(B = -0.01327\) MeV, \(C = 0.01440\) MeV, \(N = 25\), \(L_c = 10\).
- For \(^{114}\)Cd: \(\varepsilon_d = 0.5695\) MeV, \(A = -0.0003789\) MeV, \(B = -0.01586\) MeV, \(C = 0.01393\) MeV, \(N = 29\), \(L_c = 10\).
- For \(^{114}\)Te: \(\varepsilon_d = 0.7747\) MeV, \(A = -0.01678\) MeV, \(B = -0.01423\) MeV, \(C = 0.01269\) MeV, \(N = 17\), \(L_c = 12\).
- For \(^{142}\)Sm: \(\varepsilon_d = 1.101\) MeV, \(A = -0.06263\) MeV, \(B = -0.01165\) MeV, \(C = 0.01713\) MeV, \(N = 7\), \(L_c = 10\).
- For \(^{188}\)Hg: \(\varepsilon_d = 0.5957\) MeV, \(A = -0.03057\) MeV, \(B = -0.002976\) MeV, \(C = 0.009193\) MeV, \(N = 9\), \(L_c = 12\).

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation by Regan (2003) of a rotation-driven vibrational-to-rotational shape phase transition along the yrast line of individual nuclei, providing a theoretical framework within the IBM. It contrasts with earlier IBM treatments of shape phase transitions that focused on isotopic chains (e.g., Iachello and Arima, 1987) and with the cranked shell model (Bengtsson, Ragnarsson, and Molinari, 1986) which describes band crossing but not the full shape transition. The model enables systematic identification of candidate nuclei for this transition, predicting that \(^{114}\)Te, \(^{142}\)Sm, and \(^{188}\)Hg also exhibit the phenomenon, and suggests that effective boson numbers (rather than half the valence nucleon count) are needed for nuclei in the \(A \sim 110\) mass region.
