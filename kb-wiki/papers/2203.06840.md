# Renormalization of One-Pion Exchange in Chiral Effective Field Theory for Antinucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> The renormalization of iterated one-pion exchange (OPE) is studied in Chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) for the antinucleon-nucleon ($\overline{N}\!N$) system. The OPE potential is cut off at a certain distance and contact interactions are represented by a complex spherical well with the same radius. We investigate the dependence on the cutoff radius of the phase shifts, inelasticities, and mixing angles for the low partial waves in $\overline{N}\!N$ scattering. We show that renormalization requires additional contact interactions compared to the expectation based on naive dimensional analysis. Results after renormalization are compared with the state-of-the-art energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of $\overline{N}\!N$ data. We compare our conclusions with applications of $χ$EFT to the nucleon-nucleon system.

## Key claim
Renormalization of iterated one-pion exchange (OPE) in antinucleon-nucleon scattering requires additional contact interactions in several partial waves beyond those predicted by naive dimensional analysis (NDA), specifically in the uncoupled spin-triplet \(^{13}P_0\) and \(^{33}P_1\) channels and in the lower wave of each coupled channel, because the attractive tensor part of OPE generates strong cutoff dependence that must be absorbed by low-energy constants.

## Method
The authors solved the partial-wave Schrodinger equation for antinucleon-nucleon scattering with a coordinate-space regulator: a cutoff radius \(b\) (with \(\Lambda = 1/b\)) outside which only the OPE potential acts, and inside which contact interactions are represented by a complex spherical well with the same radius. They studied the cutoff dependence of phase shifts, inelasticities, and mixing angles for low partial waves (uncoupled \(S\) and \(P\), coupled \(S\)-\(D\) and \(P\)-\(F\)), fitting the real and imaginary parts of the well parameters to the Groningen energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) at a laboratory energy of 20 MeV, then comparing predictions at other momenta.

## Key numbers
- The OPE potential strength is set by \(g_A^2 m_\pi^3 / (16\pi f_\pi^2)\) with \(g_A \simeq 1.29\), \(m_\pi \simeq 138\) MeV, and \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\) MeV.
- The cutoff radius is varied; a representative large-cutoff value is \(b = 0.2\) fm (\(\Lambda = 5\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- For the \(^{11}S_0\) channel at \(b = 0.2\) fm, the fitted short-range potential has \(V_c = -13.9\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(W_c = -2.6\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(^{31}S_0\) channel at \(b = 0.2\) fm, the fitted short-range potential has \(V_c = -14.6\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(W_c = -1.4\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(^{13}P_0\) channel at \(b = 0.2\) fm, the fitted short-range potential has \(V_c = -147.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(W_c = -25.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(^{33}P_1\) channel at \(b = 0.2\) fm, the fitted short-range potential has \(V_c = -3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(W_c = -22.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The PWA data cover antiproton laboratory momenta up to 925 MeV/\(c\); the EFT provides a good description for \(p_{\rm lab} \lesssim 250\) MeV/\(c\) in spin-singlet \(P\) waves without counterterms.

## Context
This work extends to the antinucleon-nucleon system the renormalization analysis previously performed for nucleon-nucleon scattering by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2005), which showed that attractive singular tensor forces from OPE require additional contact interactions beyond NDA. The authors use the same coordinate-space regulator as Beane, Bedaque, and van Kolck (2000) and compare their results to the Groningen PWA of Zhou and Timmermans (2012, 2013). The findings enable a systematically improvable chiral EFT for antinucleon-nucleon scattering, with future work needed on perturbative treatment of OPE in higher partial waves and inclusion of two-pion exchange.
