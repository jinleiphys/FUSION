# Chiral symmetry aspects in the open charm sector

> QCD sum rules serve as tools to investigate changing hadronic properties in a hot and/or dense nuclear medium. The role of chiral symmetry breaking and restoration effects in a medium can be addressed also in the heavy-light meson sector. Thus, we consider Weinberg sum rules which refer to chiral partner mesons composed of a light and a heavy quark.

# Chiral symmetry aspects in the open charm sector

## Key claim

Weinberg sum rules for chiral partner mesons in the open charm sector (pseudo-scalar/scalar and vector/axial-vector channels) can be extended to mass dimension 6 by including chirally odd four-quark condensate contributions, but chiral symmetry restoration requires vanishing of all chirally odd condensates simultaneously, not just a single one.

## Method

The authors derive Weinberg-type sum rules as differences of chiral partner QCD sum rules for heavy-light mesons, separating the operator product expansion (OPE) into even and odd parts with respect to meson energy. They evaluate the even OPE contribution to mass dimension 5 (including the chiral condensate and mixed quark-gluon condensate) and extend the odd OPE to mass dimension 6 by adding the chirally odd four-quark condensate contribution. The sum rules are formulated for both the pseudo-scalar/scalar and vector/axial-vector channels at finite temperature and/or baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mass splitting of chiral partners (e.g., pseudo-scalar $D^0$ at 1865 MeV vs. scalar $D^0$ at 2318 MeV, and vector $D^0$ at 2007 MeV vs. axial-vector $D^0$ at 2421 MeV) is approximately 400 MeV for both light unflavoured and heavy-light mesons.
- The chiral condensate contribution dominates the pseudo-scalar $D$ meson OPE in the Borel window both in vacuum and at nuclear saturation density, as shown in Fig. 1 (numerical values from the figure are not explicitly given in the text).
- The even OPE Weinberg sum rule (Eq. 4) contains terms proportional to $m_Q \langle \bar q q \rangle$, $\langle \bar q g \sigma G q \rangle$, and $\langle \Delta \rangle$, all scaled by $e^{-m_Q^2/M^2}$.
- The odd OPE Weinberg sum rule (Eq. 5) contains the term $\frac{2e^{-m_Q^2/M^2}m_Q}{M^4} g^2 \langle \bar q t^a q \sum_f q^\dagger_f t^a q_f \rangle$.

## Context

This work extends previous Weinberg sum rule analyses in the light meson sector (e.g., studies of $\rho$ and $a_1$ mesons by Hohler and Rapp (2014), Hohler and Rapp (2012), and Holt, Hohler, and Rapp (2013)) to the open charm sector, where the chiral condensate rather than four-quark condensates dominates the OPE. The authors build on their prior work on $D$ meson sum rules (Hilger, 2009; Buchheim et al., 2015) and earlier formulations of Weinberg-type sum rules for heavy-light mesons (Hilger, 2011; Hilger, 2009; Hilger, 2010). The results enable tests of hadronic models (e.g., Sasaki, 2014) against these sum rules and provide a framework for studying chiral restoration in nuclear matter at facilities such as FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC where charm production is expected.
