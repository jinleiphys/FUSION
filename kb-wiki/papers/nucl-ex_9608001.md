# Double beta decay of $^{48}$Ca

> $^{48}$Ca, the lightest double beta decay candidate, is the only one simple enough to be treated exactly in the nuclear shell model. Thus, the $ββ(2ν)$ half-life measurement, reported here, provides a unique test of the nuclear physics involved in the $ββ$ matrix element calculation. Enriched $^{48}$Ca sources of two different thicknesses have been exposed in a time projection chamber, and yield T$_{1/2}^{2ν} = (4.3^{+2.4}_{-1.1} [{\rm stat.}] \pm 1.4 [{\rm syst.}]) \times 10^{19}$ years, compatible with the shell model calculations.

# Double beta decay of $^{48}$Ca

## Key claim

The measured two-neutrino double beta decay half-life of $^{48}$Ca is $(4.3^{+2.4}_{-1.1} [\text{stat.}] \pm 1.4 [\text{syst.}]) \times 10^{19}$ years, which is compatible with shell model calculations but cannot make a definitive statement about Gamow-Teller quenching because of the range of theoretical lifetimes and experimental uncertainties.

## Method

Enriched $^{48}$CaCO$_3$ powder (73% enrichment) was formed into two sources of different thicknesses (42.2 g at 18.5 mg/cm$^2$ and 10.3 g at 5.4 mg/cm$^2$) and exposed in a time projection chamber (TPC) located in a tunnel under 72 m of rock at Hoover Dam. Backgrounds from $^{90}$Sr, $^{226}$Ra, $^{228}$Ra, and their daughters were characterized using the lone electron spectrum and Monte Carlo simulations, with $^{214}$Bi events tagged by the subsequent $^{214}$Po alpha particle. Residual spectra after background subtraction were used to extract half-lives for multiple energy thresholds and both analysis methods.

## Key numbers

- $T_{1/2}^{2\nu} = (4.3^{+2.4}_{-1.1} [\text{stat.}] \pm 1.4 [\text{syst.}]) \times 10^{19}$ years for $^{48}$Ca double beta decay
- $^{48}$Ca natural abundance is 0.187%; enriched source was 73% $^{48}$Ca
- $Q_{\beta\beta} = 4.271 \pm 0.004$ MeV for $^{48}$Ca
- Kurie plot intercept at $4.2 \pm 0.1$ MeV, consistent with the $Q_{\beta\beta}$ value
- $^{90}$Sr activity: 2250 $\mu$Bq/g; $^{226}$Ra activity: 530 $\mu$Bq/g; $^{137}$Cs activity: 940 $\mu$Bq/g; $^{228}$Ra activity: 90 $\mu$Bq/g in the thick source
- Alpha escape probability $P_{\alpha} = 0.24 \pm 0.01$ for thick source, 0.69 for thin source
- Previous lower limit: $T_{1/2}^{2\nu} \geq 3.6 \times 10^{19}$ years from Bardin, Gollon, Ullman and Wu (1970)
- Shell model upper limit from Poves, Bahukutumbi, Langanke and Vogel (1995): $T_{1/2}^{2\nu} \leq 10^{20}$ years

## Context

This measurement provides the first positive observation of $^{48}$Ca double beta decay, replacing the lower limit set by Bardin, Gollon, Ullman and Wu (1970). The result tests the nuclear shell model calculations of Poves, Bahukutumbi, Langanke and Vogel (1995), which constrained the half-life using spectroscopy of $A=48$ nuclei. The agreement supports the shell model approach for this lightest double beta decay candidate, which is the only one tractable without truncation in the $fp$ shell, and has implications for understanding nuclear matrix elements in more complex nuclei including those relevant for neutrinoless double beta decay. Some unconstrained calculations by Muto, Bender and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1991) and Cheoun, Bobyk, Faessler, Simkovic and Teneva (1993) predicted much longer half-lives in disagreement with this experiment.
