# Comprehensive simulation of heavy-ion collisions at non-zero baryon chemical potential

> We present results of hydrodynamic modelling of Au-Au collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV. Our simulations have three novel components. Firstly, we use a Linear EXtrapolation of Ultrarelativistic nucleon-nucleon Scattering to nucleus-nucleus collisions (LEXUS) inspired Monte-Carlo initial state model. Secondly, we use a crossover equation of state at finite baryon densities without a critical point. Finally, we use departure functions derived from the quasiparticle theory of transport coefficients for hadronic matter at non-zero baryon densities.

## Key claim

The authors present a comprehensive hydrodynamic simulation of Au-Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV that serves as a baseline study without a critical point, using a crossover equation of state at finite baryon densities and a LEXUS-inspired Monte-Carlo initial state model that is the only free parameter (the thermalization time) in an otherwise parameter-free initial state.

## Method

The simulation uses a 3+1D hydrodynamic model (MUSIC) with dynamical initialization from a LEXUS-inspired Monte-Carlo initial state, where nucleon positions are sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution and binary collisions are determined geometrically using a constant 42 mb nucleon-nucleon cross section with 60% accepted as inelastic non-diffractive collisions. Energy deposition from each binary collision is sampled from a cosh distribution for rapidity loss and spread as a Gaussian in rapidity space, while participant nucleons deposit their remaining energy and baryon charge after a fixed thermalization time of 0.5 fm. The hydrodynamic evolution uses a crossover equation of state that matches hadron resonance gas to perturbative QCD via a switching function, and departure functions for particlization are derived from quasiparticle theory at finite baryon chemical potential within the relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV for Au-Au collisions
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: constant 42 mb (average for energies considered)
- Inelastic non-diffractive fraction: 60% of total collisions
- Thermalization time: t_thermalize = 0.5 fm (only free parameter in initial state)
- Transverse smearing width: sigma_perp = 0.5 fm for both binary collisions and participants
- Longitudinal smearing width: sigma_eta = 0.2 for participants
- Overlap time at lower BES energies: tau_overlap ~ 2-3 fm
- Equation of state parameters: T_0 = 177.12 MeV, r = 5, mu_0 = 3*pi*T_0, epsilon_0 = 1.15 GeV/fm^3, C_M = 3.352, C_S = 4.28, Lambda_MSbar = 290 MeV

## Context

This work extends the standard model of heavy-ion collisions, which is well-established at zero baryon density (Heinz 2013, Romatschke 2017, Shen 2020), to the Beam Energy Scan regime at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA where net baryon densities are non-zero. The LEXUS-inspired initial state builds on the original LEXUS model (Jeon 1997) by adding 3D spatial information through Monte-Carlo sampling, while the crossover equation of state without a critical point (Albright 2014) and departure functions at finite baryon chemical potential (Albright 2015) provide a baseline for future studies that include a critical point or first-order phase transition, as recommended by the BEST collaboration report (An 2021).
