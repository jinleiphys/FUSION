# Pion Properties in the 1/N_c-corrected NJL model

> We investigate the effect of mesonic fluctuations on the pion propagator in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by explicitly taking into account 1/N_c corrections. Because of the non-renormalizability of the model we have to regularize the meson loops with an independent cutoff parameter Lambda_M. Whereas for moderate values of Lambda_M the pion properties change only quantitatively we encounter strong instabilities for larger values of Lambda_M.

## Key claim

The effect of mesonic fluctuations on the pion propagator in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model depends strongly on the choice of an independent meson-loop cutoff parameter Lambda_M; for moderate values the pion properties change only quantitatively, but for larger values strong instabilities appear, including unphysical poles and negative wave function renormalization constants.

## Method

The authors extended the standard NJL model for two flavors and three colors to next-to-leading order in 1/N_c by explicitly including meson-loop correction diagrams to the inverse meson propagator. Quark loops were regularized using a Pauli-Villars scheme with a quark cutoff Lambda_q = 800 MeV, while the meson loops were regularized with an independent three-dimensional sharp cutoff Lambda_M. The pion propagator was then computed as a function of the meson cutoff, with the leading-order parameters fixed by fitting the pion mass, decay constant, and quark condensate.

## Key numbers

* Leading-order fit (Lambda_M = 0): m_pi^(0) = 140 MeV, f_pi^(0) = 93.5 MeV, quark condensate = -2 (241.1 MeV)^3, with constituent quark mass 260 MeV.
* For Lambda_M up to about 900 MeV, the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation holds within 30%.
* A second unphysical pole in the pion propagator appears for Lambda_M between about 1250 MeV and 1350 MeV; at Lambda_M ~ 1350 MeV the two poles merge and disappear from the positive real q^2 axis.
* Without refit, at Lambda_M = 1250 MeV the pion mass is 216 MeV and f_pi = 35 MeV; rescaling to f_pi = 93 MeV shifts the instability onset to Lambda_M ~ 3300 MeV.
* A simultaneous fit of f_pi and the quark condensate is only possible for Lambda_M less than about 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL studies of 1/N_c corrections by DSTL and Niko (both late 1990s) by computing the full momentum dependence of the correction diagrams, and directly addresses the controversial claim by Kleinert and Van den Bossche (1998) that for N_c=3 the spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry does not occur due to strong fluctuations. The results show that the stability of the pion propagator depends critically on the meson cutoff, and that for realistic parameter sets the instability region appears far away, enabling further applications of the model to physical processes such as vector meson decay widths.
