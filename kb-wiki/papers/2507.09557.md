# Off-shell modifications of the pion generalized parton distributions and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions

> The off-shell characteristics of pion generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) are examined within the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In our previous papers, we separately investigated the properties of on-shell pion GPDs and light-front wave functions. It is particularly intriguing to compare the differences between on-shell and off-shell pion GPDs, which allows us to explore the effects associated with off-shellness. Due to the absence of crossing symmetry, the moments of GPDs also incorporate odd powers of the skewness parameter, resulting in new off-shell form factors. Through our calculations, we derived correction functions that account for modifications in pion GPDs due to off-shell effects. Unlike their on-shell counterparts, certain properties break down in the off-shell scenario; for instance, symmetry properties and polynomiality conditions may no longer hold. Additionally, we evaluate off-shell TMDs and compare them with their on-shell equivalents while also investigating their dependence on $\bm{k}_{\perp}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that off-shell pion generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), calculated within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, exhibit significant modifications compared to their on-shell counterparts, with the off-shell effects reaching approximately 15% to 25% for the maximum values of the vector GPD at specific off-shell momenta, and with the loss of symmetry properties and polynomiality conditions that hold for on-shell distributions.

## Method

The author calculates off-shell pion GPDs and TMDs using the SU(2) flavor NJL model with proper time regularization (PTR), employing an infrared cutoff of 0.240 GeV to mimic confinement and an ultraviolet cutoff of 0.645 GeV. The off-shell GPDs are defined as off-forward matrix elements with incoming pion momentum \(p\) and outgoing momentum \(p'\) satisfying \(p^2 \neq p'^2 \neq m_\pi^2\), and are computed from one-loop quark diagrams with a pseudoscalar pion-quark vertex \(\Gamma_\pi = \sqrt{Z_\pi}\gamma_5\). The x-moments of the off-shell GPDs are expanded in powers of the skewness parameter \(\xi\) to extract off-shell form factors, and the impact parameter dependent parton distributions are obtained via two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the GPDs at \(\xi=0\).

## Key numbers

- The infrared cutoff \(\Lambda_{\text{IR}} = 0.240\) GeV and ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} = 0.645\) GeV are used in the proper time regularization scheme.
- The dressed quark mass is \(M = 0.4\) GeV, the pion mass is \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV, and the four-fermion coupling constant is \(G_\pi = 19.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- For the half-off-shell vector GPD \(H(x,0.5,0,p^2,m_\pi^2)\), the off-shell effect relative to the on-shell case is approximately 15% for the maximum value at \(p^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) and approximately 25% at \(p^2 = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).
- The off-shell electromagnetic form factor \(G(t,p^2,p'^2)\) satisfies \(G(t,p^2,p^2)=0\) and the relation \(G(t,p^2,p'^2) = (p'^2 - p^2)[F(0,p^2,p'^2) - F(t,p^2,p'^2)]/t\) is numerically verified for the vector GPD moment \(A_{1,1}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of on-shell pion GPDs and light-front wave functions within the NJL model (Zhang 2021) to the off-shell regime, which is relevant for the Sullivan process where the pion is virtual. The off-shell GPDs are compared with results from the Spectral Quark Model (Broniowski 2022, 2023) and a quark model study of the off-shell form factor \(G(t,p^2,m_\pi^2)\) (Choi 2019). The paper enables future investigations of off-shell gravitational form factors of pions and provides a framework for understanding how off-shellness modifies parton distributions in exclusive processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering.
