# Lawson Method for Obtaining Wave Functions and $g$ Factors of Ar Isotopes

> Lawson has shown that one can obtain sensible wave functions even in the weak deformation limit of the Nilsson model as long as one projects out states of good total angular momentum. We apply this method to obtain wave functions and magnetic $g$ factors of excited states of select even-even Ar isotopes.

# Lawson Method for Obtaining Wave Functions and $g$ Factors of Ar Isotopes

## Key claim
The Lawson method, which projects states of good total angular momentum from intrinsic states in the weak deformation limit of the Nilsson model, can produce sensible wave functions and magnetic $g$ factors for excited states of even-even Ar isotopes, but the results for $^{40}$Ar are somewhat more negative than the two measured values of -0.02(4) and -0.1(1).

## Method
The authors construct intrinsic states by placing two proton holes in $d_{3/2}$ Nilsson orbits and valence neutrons in $f_{7/2}$ orbits at the weak deformation limit, then expand these intrinsic states into states of definite total angular momentum using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The $g$ factors are computed from the resulting wave function amplitudes using an expression that separates isoscalar and isovector contributions, with both bare and empirical single-particle $g$ factors as input.

## Key numbers
- Schmidt magnetic moment of a $d_{3/2}$ proton: $0.126 \mu_N$ (bare), measured value $0.39187 \mu_N$ (empirical)
- Schmidt magnetic moment of a $f_{7/2}$ neutron: $-1.913 \mu_N$ (bare), measured value $-1.59478 \mu_N$ (empirical)
- Bare $g$ factors used: $g_\pi = 0.0841$, $g_\nu = -0.5466$
- Empirical $g$ factors used: $g_\pi = 0.2610$, $g_\nu = -0.4557$
- Prolate Lawson $g(^{40}\text{Ar})$ with bare $g$ factors: $-0.3306$
- Prolate Lawson $g(^{44}\text{Ar})$ with bare $g$ factors: $-0.3638$
- Prolate Lawson $g(^{40}\text{Ar})$ with empirical $g$ factors: $-0.2102$
- Prolate Lawson $g(^{44}\text{Ar})$ with empirical $g$ factors: $-0.2479$
- Measured $g$ factor for $^{38}\text{Ar}$ $2_1^+$ state: $+0.24(12)$ (Speidel 2006)
- Measured $g$ factors for $^{40}\text{Ar}$ $2^+$ state: $-0.02(4)$ and $-0.1(1)$
- Shell model calculation with WBT interaction in the $d_{3/2}$ proton and $f_{7/2}$ neutron space gives bare $g(^{40}\text{Ar})$ = $-0.441$
- $g$ factor of $^{39}\text{K}$ (single hole nucleus): $0.26098$ (empirical), $0.084$ (bare)
- For $J=8$ state: bare $g = -0.3890$, empirical $g = -0.2764$ (identical for $^{40}\text{Ar}$ and $^{44}\text{Ar}$)

## Context
This work extends the Lawson projection method (Lawson 1961, Lawson 1980) to compute $g$ factors of excited states in $^{36,38,40,44,46}$Ar, building on large-space shell model calculations by Stephan (2005), Speidel (2006), and Speidel (2008). The simple wave functions provide explicit insight into the interplay of proton and neutron contributions, and the authors note an unusual symmetry where shell model calculations in the $d_{3/2,\pi}$ $f_{7/2,\nu}$ space yield identical energy levels and $g$ factors for $^{40}$Ar and $^{44}$Ar. The method enables predictions for $^{44}$Ar $g$ factors, which have not yet been measured, and suggests that for $^{46}$Ar the $g$ factor may be closer to the bare value of 0.084 rather than the effective value of 0.28 due to the better closed-shell nature of $^{48}$Ca compared to $^{40}$Ca.
