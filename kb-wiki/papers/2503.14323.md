# Investigation of $β$-decay properties of neutron-rich Cerium isotopes

> Reliable and precise knowledge of the $β$-decay properties of neutron-rich nuclei is important for a better understanding of the $r$-process. We report the computation of $β$-decay properties of neutron-rich Cerium isotopes calculated within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) approach. A total of 34 isotopes of Ce in the mass range $120 \leq A \leq 157$ were considered in our calculation. Pairing gaps are recognized amongst the key parameters in the pn-QRPA model to compute Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions. We employed two different values of the pairing gaps obtained from two different empirical formulae in our computation. The GT strength distributions changed considerably with changes in the pairing gap values. This, in turn, resulted in contrasting centroid and total strength values of the GT distributions and led to differences in calculated half-lives using the two schemes. The traditional pairing gaps resulted in significant fragmentation of GT strength. However, the pairing gaps, calculated employing the formula based on separation energies of neutron and proton, led to computed half-lives in better agreement with the measured data.

## Key claim

The paper reports that using pairing gaps calculated from a three-term formula based on neutron and proton separation energies (3TF) yields computed β-decay half-lives for neutron-rich Cerium isotopes that are in better agreement with measured data than those obtained using the traditional mass-dependent pairing gap formula (TF), though the choice of pairing gap scheme significantly alters Gamow-Teller strength distributions and centroid values.

## Method

The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a separable pairing plus quadrupole nuclear Hamiltonian to compute β-decay half-lives and Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for 34 Cerium isotopes in the mass range 120 ≤ A ≤ 157. Two different empirical pairing gap schemes were used: the traditional TF formula (Δp = Δn = 12/√A MeV) and the 3TF formula based on proton and neutron separation energies. Single-particle energies and wavefunctions were computed using the Nilsson model including nuclear deformation, and the interaction strengths were chosen according to a 1/A^0.7 relationship.

## Key numbers

- 34 isotopes of Cerium in the mass range 120 ≤ A ≤ 157 were considered; 16 decay via positron emission, 16 via electron emission, and 2 via electron capture; 136,138,140,142Ce are stable.
- The 3TF scheme yielded calculated half-lives within a factor 2 for 20 cases and within a factor 5 for 6 cases, while 8 cases fell beyond factor 5 difference compared to measured data.
- The TF scheme resulted in 12 cases falling beyond factor 5 difference compared to measured half-lives.
- Up to two orders of magnitude difference between calculated and measured half-lives was noted for odd-A isotopes 139,141,143Ce, attributed to the N=82 shell closure effect.
- The ratio of axial to vector coupling constant gA/gV was set to -1.254, and the constant C in the half-life formula was 6295 s.
- The oscillator constant was calculated as ħω = 41A^(1/3) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA-based calculations of β-decay properties (Hirsch 1993, Nabi 1999, Escrig 2010, Wang 2016) and shell model calculations (Martinez-Pinedo 1999) to neutron-rich Cerium isotopes, which are important for r-process nucleosynthesis and supernova dynamics. The study specifically investigates the sensitivity of computed β-decay half-lives to pairing gap values, a key parameter in the pn-QRPA model, and enables improved predictions for the lanthanide region (Z=57-71) where Cerium is the most abundant element in Ap Stars (Cowley 1976). The authors note that future work should include hundreds of unstable nuclei to study correlations between pairing gap values and calculated half-lives, and consider using a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential instead of the schematic separable potential.
