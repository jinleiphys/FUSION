# Multiquark interactions and heavy hybrid stars

> We introduce a two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with 8-quark interactions in the scalar and the vector channel. With the lower density region described by the density-dependent relativistic mean field model we construct a hybrid equation of state. We especially focus on the 4-quark vector couplings and the 8-quark vector NJL couplings and allocate a region in this parameter subspace where hybrid stars with masses larger than $2M_\odot$ exist.

# Multiquark interactions and heavy hybrid stars

## Key claim
Hybrid stars with masses larger than 2 solar masses can exist within a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that includes 8-quark interactions, but only within a specific subspace of the 4-quark and 8-quark vector coupling parameters; without vector interactions, a 2-solar-mass hybrid star is not possible in this model.

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid equation of state by combining a density-dependent relativistic mean field model (DD2) for nuclear matter at lower densities with a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model extended by 8-quark interactions in both the scalar and vector channels for quark matter at higher densities. The nuclear-quark phase transition was assumed to be first order. The authors then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and scanned the parameter subspace of the dimensionless 4-quark vector coupling (η₂) and 8-quark vector coupling (η₄) to identify regions where hybrid stars exceed 2 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- Hybrid stars with masses larger than 2 solar masses exist in a region of the η₂-η₄ parameter plane; the lower values of η₂ and η₄ are excluded by the 2-solar-mass constraint.
- For fixed η₂ = 0.0, several values of η₄ produce hybrid stars with masses above 2 solar masses, as shown in the mass-radius relationship.
- The speed of sound at densities reached in the cores of all stable configurations is below unity (no numerical value given for the speed of sound).
- The DD2 nuclear equation of state alone yields a maximum mass for a pure neutron star well above 2 solar masses (no exact numerical value given).

## Context
This work extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio framework by introducing 8-quark interactions as a microscopic mechanism to stiffen quark matter at high densities, addressing the challenge posed by the discovery of 2-solar-mass compact stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). It builds on prior studies of hybrid star equations of state (Alford 2013, Klahn 2006, Pagliara 2007, Weissenborn 2011, Lenzi 2012, Klahn 2013) and the author's own earlier work (Benic 2014a, 2014b). The results enable future exploration of the role of strangeness (Moreira 2014) and are likely to be refined by further constraints on vector interactions in quark matter (Steinheimer 2014, Alvarez-Castillo 2014). The parameter window identified is sensitive to details of the nuclear-quark transition, and increasing the latent heat could produce twin stars at 2 solar masses (Benic 2014b).
