# Alternate 1/N_c Expansions and SU(3) Breaking from Baryon Lattice Results

> A combined expansion in the number of QCD colors 1/N_c and SU(3) flavor breaking parameter epsilon has long been known to provide an excellent accounting for the mass spectrum of the lightest spin-1/2, 3/2 baryons when the quarks are taken to transform under the fundamental SU(N_c) representation, and in the final step N_c \to 3 and epsilon is set to its physical value ~0.3. Subsequent work shows that placing quarks in the two-index antisymmetric SU(N_c) representation leads to quantitatively equally successful mass relations. Recent lattice simulations allow for varying the value of epsilon and confirm the robustness of the original 1/N_c relations. In this paper we show that the same conclusion holds for the antisymmetric quarks, and demonstrate that the mass relations also hold under alternate prescriptions for identifying physical baryons with particular members of the large N_c multiplets.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both the large \(N_c^{\rm F}\) (fundamental representation) and large \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) (two-index antisymmetric representation) expansions fit baryon mass lattice data equally well across a range of SU(3) flavor-breaking strengths, and that the mass relations are robust under different prescriptions for identifying physical baryons with large \(N_c\) multiplet members, even though individual baryon masses are sensitive to these ambiguities.

## Method

The authors used lattice simulation data from Jenkins et al. (2009) for baryon masses with varying SU(3) breaking parameter \(\epsilon\), and computed accuracy factors \(A_i \equiv \log_3 (R_i / S_i)\) for eight mass combinations \(M_i\) (Table 1), where \(R_i\) is a dimensionless ratio of the mass combination to a weighted average of the masses, and \(S_i\) is the theoretical suppression factor from either the \(N_c^{\rm F}\) or \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansion. They compared the predictions of both expansions to the lattice data as a function of \(\epsilon\), and also analyzed the effect of two different prescriptions (strangeness \(S\) versus hypercharge \(Y\)) for matching \(N_c=3\) baryons to large \(N_c\) states, showing that the mass relations are insensitive to this choice due to operator cancellations.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) flavor-breaking parameter \(\epsilon\) is defined as \((m_K^2 - m_\pi^2)/\Lambda_\chi^2 \approx 0.226\) at the physical point, with \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV.
- The lattice data cover the range \(\epsilon \in (0, 0.26)\).
- The accuracy factors \(A_i\) are expected to lie in the range \(-1 \lesssim A_i \lesssim 1\) if the theoretical suppression factors are correct; both \(N_c^{\rm F}\) and \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansions produce \(A_i\) values in this range across the full \(\epsilon\) range.
- In the large \(N_c^{\rm F}\) expansion, \(n\)-body operators are suppressed by \(1/N_c^n\); in the large \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansion, the suppression is \(1/N_c^{2n}\).
- The mass combination \(M_7\) (the Gell-Mann–Okubo-like combination \(\Delta_0 - 3\Sigma^*_0 + 3\Xi^*_0 - \Omega\)) has suppression \(\epsilon^3 / N_c^2\) in the \(N_c^{\rm F}\) expansion and \(\epsilon^3 / N_c^4\) in the \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansion.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cherman, Cohen, and Lebed (2009), which showed that both large \(N_c^{\rm F}\) and large \(N_c^{\rm AS}\) expansions fit experimental baryon masses comparably well, by testing the predictions against lattice data from Jenkins et al. (2009) that vary the SU(3) breaking strength. The paper also addresses the ambiguity in matching \(N_c=3\) baryons to large \(N_c\) multiplets, a challenge noted in earlier work by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994) and Jenkins (1995), and demonstrates that the mass relations are robust under different prescriptions (strangeness-based \(S\) versus hypercharge-based \(Y\)), which enables future studies to use these relations without needing to resolve the matching ambiguity.
