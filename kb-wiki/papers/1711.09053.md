# Drag Induced Radiation and Multi-Stage Effects in Heavy-Flavor Energy Loss

> It is argued that heavy-quarks traversing a Quark Gluon Plasma, undergo a multi-stage modification process, similar to the case of light flavors. Such an approach is applied to heavy-quark energy loss, which includes a rare-scattering, multiple emission formalism at momenta large compared to the mass (sensitive only to the transverse diffusion coefficient $\hat{q}$), and a single scattering induced emission formalism (Gunion-Bertsch) at momenta comparable to the mass of the quark [sensitive to $\hat{q}$, and the longitudinal drag ($\hat{e}$) and diffusion ($\hat{e}_2$) coefficients]. The application of such a multi-stage approach within a 2+1D viscous fluid-dynamical simulation leads to simultaneous agreement with experimental data for the nuclear modification factor of both $B$ and $D$ mesons, as measured by the CMS collaboration at the LHC. The extracted transport coefficients are found to be consistent with those for light flavors.

## Key claim

Heavy-quark energy loss in the Quark Gluon Plasma requires a multi-stage formalism where, at momenta comparable to the quark mass, the radiation rate depends not only on the transverse diffusion coefficient \(\hat{q}\) but also on the longitudinal drag (\(\hat{e}\)) and diffusion (\(\hat{e}_2\)) coefficients through mass-dependent terms; this approach yields simultaneous agreement with CMS data for both \(B\) and \(D\) meson nuclear modification factors using transport coefficients consistent with those for light flavors.

## Method

The authors implement a two-stage heavy-quark energy loss model within a 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulation of the QGP. High-momentum heavy-quarks (\(p_Q \gg M\)) undergo a medium-modified DGLAP shower, while semi-hard quarks with momentum comparable to their mass lose energy via a Gunion-Bertsch single-scattering induced emission formalism that includes contributions from \(\hat{q}\), \(\hat{e}\), and \(\hat{e}_2\). The evolution below a separation scale \(Q_0\) (2 GeV for \(c \to D\), 5 GeV for \(b \to B\)) uses an improved Langevin transport approach with a rate equation derived from the single-gluon emission cross section, and the medium-modified fragmentation functions are then evolved up to the hard scale \(Q = p_T\) using higher-twist medium-modified DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

- The separation scale \(Q_0\) is set to 2 GeV for \(c \to D\) meson fragmentation and 5 GeV for \(b \to B\) meson fragmentation.
- The transverse diffusion coefficient is normalized as \(\hat{q} = 5.03 \, T^3\), which is within one standard deviation of the central value for light flavors obtained by the JET collaboration at LHC energies.
- The calculation uses a \(K\)-factor of 1.7 to account for NLO corrections in the \(p_T\) range explored.
- The nuclear modification factor is computed for \(D\) and \(B\) mesons in the intermediate momentum range from 10 to 50 GeV in minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the multi-stage energy loss formalism previously applied to light quarks and gluons by Cao et al. (2017) to the heavy-flavor sector, addressing the long-standing puzzle that heavy-flavor suppression appeared larger than expected from the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer, 2001). The authors build on theoretical developments by Abir et al. (2014, 2015) who identified mass-dependent contributions of longitudinal drag and diffusion to radiative loss, and on prior calculations of drag and diffusion effects on stimulated emission by Qin et al. (2012, 2014) and Zhang et al. (2016). The results enable a unified pQCD-based description of both light and heavy flavor energy loss and motivate future implementation within multi-stage Monte-Carlo event generators such as those developed by the JETSCAPE collaboration.
