# Magnetic field dependence of nucleon parameters from QCD sum rules

> Finite energy QCD sum rules involving nucleon current correlators are used to determine several QCD and hadronic parameters in the presence of an external, uniform, large magnetic field. The continuum hadronic threshold $s_0$, nucleon mass $m_N$, current-nucleon coupling $λ_N$, transverse velocity $v_\perp$, the spin polarization condensate $\langle\bar qσ_{12} q\rangle$, and the magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate $χ_q$, are obtained for the case of protons and neutrons. Due to the magnetic field, and charge asymmetry of light quarks up and down, all the obtained quantities evolve differently with the magnetic field, for each nucleon or quark flavor. With this approach it is possible to obtain the evolution of the above parameters up to a magnetic field strength $eB < 1.4$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

Finite energy QCD sum rules (FESR) for nucleon current correlators can determine the magnetic field evolution of several QCD and hadronic parameters up to eB < 1.4 GeV², but the analysis is restricted to magnetic fields below the production threshold of the N*(1440) resonance, limiting the reliable range to eB < 0.07 GeV² for protons and eB < 0.14 GeV² for neutrons.

## Method

The authors use Finite Energy QCD Sum Rules (FESR) with nucleon interpolating currents of the Ioffe type, integrating correlators around a circular contour in the complex s-plane to relate hadronic spectral functions to the QCD operator product expansion. The fermion propagator is expanded in powers of the magnetic field including anomalous magnetic moment terms, and the correlator is decomposed into scalar, vector, and tensor structures. The resulting system of four FESR equations is solved numerically using the magnetic field dependent quark condensate from lattice QCD as input, with the gluon condensate held constant.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in vacuum: m_N = 0.94 GeV
- Quark condensate in vacuum: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.24 GeV)³
- Gluon condensate in vacuum: ⟨α_s G²⟩ = 0.037 GeV⁴
- Current-nucleon coupling in vacuum: λ_N = 0.017 GeV³
- Hadronic continuum threshold in vacuum: s₀ = 1.26 GeV²
- Down-quark magnetic susceptibility at B=0 from proton FESR: χ_d(0) = -5.50 GeV⁻²
- Down-quark magnetic susceptibility at B=0 from neutron FESR: χ_d(0) = -3.83 GeV⁻²
- Average magnetic susceptibility at B=0: χ̄_q(0) = -4.67 GeV⁻²
- Average light quark mass in vacuum: m_q(0) = 6.05 MeV at scale 1 GeV
- Maximum magnetic field for proton analysis: eB < 0.07 GeV² (≈ 3.6 m_π²)
- Maximum magnetic field for neutron analysis: eB < 0.14 GeV² (≈ 7 m_π²)

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of nucleon magnetic moments by Ioffe (1983) and Wang (2008) beyond linear magnetic field dependence, using the FESR formalism developed by Ayala (2015) and Dominguez (2018). The results for the spin polarization condensate agree with the linear behavior at weak fields found by Frasca (2011) and with lattice QCD results by Bali (2020). The nucleon mass behavior at low B agrees with lattice results by Endrodi (2019), showing an increasing proton mass and decreasing neutron mass, and with the Walecka model analysis by Mukherjee (2018) when including the anomalous magnetic moment. The approach enables future studies including baryonic density effects for magnetar applications.
