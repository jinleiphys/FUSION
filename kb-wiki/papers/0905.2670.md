# A Monte Carlo generator of nucleon configurations in complex nuclei including Nucleon-Nucleon correlations

> We developed a Monte Carlo event generator for production of nucleon configurations in complex nuclei consistently including effects of Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) correlations. Our approach is based on the Metropolis search for configurations satisfying essential constraints imposed by short- and long-range NN correlations, guided by the findings of realistic calculations of one- and two-body densities for medium-heavy nuclei. The produced event generator can be used for Monte Carlo (MC) studies of pA and AA collisions. We perform several tests of consistency of the code and comparison with previous models, in the case of high energy proton-nucleus scattering on an event-by-event basis, using nucleus configurations produced by our code and Glauber multiple scattering theory both for the uncorrelated and the correlated configurations; fluctuations of the average number of collisions are shown to be affected considerably by the introduction of NN correlations in the target nucleus. We also use the generator to estimate maximal possible gluon nuclear shadowing in a simple geometric model.

## Key claim
The authors develop a Monte Carlo event generator for nucleon configurations in complex nuclei that consistently includes central Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) correlations, but they explicitly implement only effective central correlations rather than the full state-dependent correlation operator, which would be computationally prohibitive for heavy nuclei.

## Method
The generator uses a Metropolis random search starting from random nucleon positions in a cube with nuclear density (0.17 nucleons/fm³), imposing a Woods-Saxon single-particle probability distribution and a central correlation function (step function or Gaussian) to enforce short-range repulsion. The resulting configurations are tested by comparing the radial two-body density and the pair distribution function C(r) against realistic calculations from Alvioli (2005). The generator is then applied to compute probability distributions P_N(b) for the number of inelastic collisions in proton-nucleus scattering using Glauber multiple scattering theory, and to estimate the lower limit on gluon nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter density used for initial random positions: 0.17 nucleons/fm³.
- For the Gaussian correlation, the parameter β = 0.9 fm⁻² gives a healing distance similar to realistic calculations.
- For the Glauber calculations at √s = 42 GeV: σ_tot = 41.6 mb, B = 12.6 GeV⁻².
- For LHC energies at √s = 5500 GeV: σ_tot = 94.8 mb, B = 17.3 GeV⁻².
- For the gluon shadowing calculation, m²_g = 0.6 GeV² (corresponding to x ~ 10⁻⁴) and B_g = 3.24/m²_g.
- The transverse gluon density radius r^{tr}_g ≤ 0.5 fm.
- For A → ∞, the two gluon GPD models differ by a factor F^{(1)}_g(0)/F^{(2)}_g(0) = 2/3.24 = 0.62.

## Context
This work extends the observation of Baym (1995) that NN correlations significantly modify the variance of the distribution over the number of collisions in proton-nucleus scattering, by implementing a practical Monte Carlo generator that preserves the correct single-nucleon density while including central correlations. It builds on realistic calculations of one- and two-body densities for medium-heavy nuclei by Alvioli (2005) and Pieper (1992). The generator enables event-by-event studies of pA and AA collisions with more realistic initial nuclear configurations, and can be used to estimate maximal gluon nuclear shadowing, with the finding that the asymptotic limit for shadowing is reached only at extremely large A (up to 10⁵) due to the small transverse gluon density radius.
