# Sensitivity of the excitation functions of collective flow to relativistic scalar and vector meson interactions in the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model RQMD.RMF

> Relativistic quantum molecular dynamics with scalar and vector interactions based on the relativistic mean meson field theory, RQMD.RMF, is developed.It is implemented into the microscopic transport code JAM.The sensitivity of the directed and of the elliptic proton flow in high energy heavy-ion collisions on the stiffness of the RMF equation of state (EoS) is examined. These new calculations are compared to experimental data at mid-central Au + Au collisions in the beam energy range $2.5 < \sqrt{s_{NN}} < 20$ GeV. This new RQMD model with the relativistic mean field scalar and vector meson interactions does describe consistently, with one RMF parameter set,the beam energy dependence of both the directed flow and the elliptic flow,from SIS18 to AGS and RHIC BES-II energies, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}< 10$ GeV.There are different sensitivities of these different kinds of flow to the EoS: elliptic flow is most sensitive to the nuclear incompressibility constant,at the moderate beam energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<3$ GeV,whereas the directed flow is most sensitive to the effective baryon mass at saturation density at $3< \sqrt{s_{NN}}<5 $ GeV. Matters abruptly change in the next higher energy range,$\sqrt{s_{NN}}\gtrsim 10-20$ GeV:the directed flow data show a double change of sign of the slope of $v_1$, inverting twice in this energy range,in sudden contradiction to the RQMD.RMF calculation for a monotonous, stiff EoS. This surprising oscillating behavior,a double change of sign of the $v_1$ slope, points to the appearance of a hitherto unknown first-order phase transition in excited QCD matter at high baryon densities in mid-central Au + Au collisions.

## Key claim

The RQMD.RMF model, using a single parameter set for the relativistic mean-field equation of state, consistently describes the beam energy dependence of both directed and elliptic flow for Au+Au collisions up to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 10\) GeV, but fails to reproduce the observed double change of sign of the directed flow slope at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrsim 10-20\) GeV, which the authors interpret as evidence for a first-order phase transition in high-density QCD matter.

## Method

The authors implemented relativistic scalar (\(\sigma\)) and vector (\(\omega\)) meson mean-field interactions into the \(N\)-body relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) framework within the JAM transport code, which includes hadron resonances from the PDG and string degrees of freedom. The non-linear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model with Boguta's self-interactions was used with three parameter sets (NS1, NS2, NS3) having different incompressibility constants and effective nucleon masses at saturation density. The model was applied to simulate mid-central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.5\) to 20 GeV, and the resulting proton directed flow (\(v_1\)) and elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) were compared to experimental data from E895, NA49, STAR, FOPI, and E877/E897 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The RQMD.RMF model with parameter sets NS1 (\(K = 230\) MeV, \(m^*/m = 0.800\)) and NS2 (\(K = 380\) MeV, \(m^*/m = 0.800\)) shows good agreement with directed flow data up to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV
- The NS3 parameter set (\(K = 380\) MeV, \(m^*/m = 0.722\)) significantly overestimates the \(v_1\) data, demonstrating strong sensitivity to the effective baryon mass at saturation density
- Elliptic flow is most sensitive to the nuclear incompressibility constant at beam energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 3\) GeV, where NS1 (\(K = 230\) MeV) shows less squeeze-out than NS2 (\(K = 380\) MeV)
- Directed flow is most sensitive to the effective baryon mass at saturation density in the range \(3 < \sqrt{s_{NN}} < 5\) GeV
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 8\) GeV, the model predicts positive \(v_1\) slopes, while experimental data show negative \(v_1\) slopes at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10-20\) GeV
- The ground state binding energy used is \(B = -16\) MeV at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.168\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic mean-field transport approaches such as RBUU (Sahu et al., 1998) and RVUU, which were limited to lower energies or single-particle distribution functions, by implementing the RMF into a full \(N\)-body RQMD framework with a comprehensive hadron resonance list. The results contradict earlier non-relativistic Skyrme force approaches (Danielewicz, 2002) which could not reproduce the beam energy dependence of both flow types with a single parameter set. The failure of the RQMD.RMF model to reproduce the negative directed flow slopes observed by the NA49 Collaboration (2002) and the STAR Collaboration (2018) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10-20\) GeV, combined with the success of the JAM attractive orbit mode (Nara, 2016) in reproducing this collapse, points toward the need for a first-order phase transition in the equation of state, enabling future searches for the QCD critical point at high baryon density in experiments such as RHIC-BESII, CBM at FAIR, and J-PARC-HI.
