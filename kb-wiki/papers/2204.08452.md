# Deconstructing Ünsal-Yaffe Reconfinement

## Key claim

The Ünsal-Yaffe (UY) deformed finite-temperature lattice gauge theory, in which the thermal trace includes states transforming in all SU(N)/Z(N) irreducible representations rather than only SU(N) singlets, produces a Hilbert space that becomes orthogonal to the usual physical Hilbert space as N → ∞ on a finite lattice. This raises concerns about whether the extended UY proposal for large-N Eguchi-Kawai (EK) reduction can correctly reproduce ordinary planar gauge theory.

## Method

The author applies "deconstruction" to replace the continuum fourth direction by a finite periodic lattice, keeping the theory purely three-dimensional in the UV. The deformed thermal trace is analyzed by comparing the standard projector onto gauge-singlet states with the UY-modified operator that includes a Z(N)-invariant deformation weight. The trace of the modified operator in the singlet sector is computed analytically using a one-link integral over eigenvalue distributions, and the resulting expression is evaluated in the N → ∞ limit using modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The one-link integral ratio J(2h)/J(0) equals [I₀(2h) − I₁(2h)]^{N(N−1)/2}, where Iₙ are modified Bessel functions
- For all h > 0, the quantity I₀(2h) − I₁(2h) lies strictly between 0 and 1; for h < 0 it exceeds 1
- At N = ∞, J(2h)/J(0) becomes +∞ when h < 0 and 0 when h > 0, making the physical Hilbert space a thermodynamically negligible subset of the deformed Hilbert space
- The order parameter P_μ = (1/N²) Σ_{i,j} sin²(½(θᵢ − θⱼ)) ≤ ½; saturation at ½ does not guarantee full Z(N) invariance

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ünsal and Yaffe (2008) on deformed finite-temperature gauge theory and its proposed application to large-N reduction. It contradicts the conclusions of Bringoltz and Sharpe (2008) regarding the viability of Deformed Eguchi-Kawai (DEK) reduction, and revisits earlier concerns about the Twisted Eguchi-Kawai (TEK) model raised by Azeyanagi (2007) and Teper (2007). The author argues that targeting Z(N)⁴ restoration directly, as in the UY deformation, is insufficient to guarantee correct Feynman diagram structure, and that a credible cure for EK reduction requires a clear mechanism for how continuous momentum emerges from SU(N)-valued dynamical variables.
