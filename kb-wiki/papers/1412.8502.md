# Pasta Nucleosynthesis: Molecular dynamics simulations of nuclear statistical equilibrium

> Background: Exotic non-spherical nuclear pasta shapes are expected in nuclear matter at just below saturation density because of competition between short range nuclear attraction and long range Coulomb repulsion. Purpose: We explore the impact of nuclear pasta on nucleosynthesis, during neutron star mergers, as cold dense nuclear matter is ejected and decompressed. Methods: We perform classical molecular dynamics simulations with 51200 and 409600 nucleons, that are run on GPUs. We expand our simulation region to decompress systems from an initial density of 0.080 fm^{-3} down to 0.00125 fm^{-3}. We study proton fractions of Y_P=0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, and 0.40 at T =0.5, 0.75, and 1.0 MeV. We calculate the composition of the resulting systems using a cluster algorithm. Results: We find final compositions that are in good agreement with nuclear statistical equilibrium models for temperatures of 0.75 and 1 MeV. However, for proton fractions greater than Y_P=0.2 at a temperature of T = 0.5 MeV, the MD simulations produce non-equilibrium results with large rod-like nuclei. Conclusions: Our MD model is valid at higher densities than simple nuclear statistical equilibrium models and may help determine the initial temperatures and proton fractions of matter ejected in mergers.

## Key claim
Classical molecular dynamics simulations of decompressing nuclear pasta show final compositions that agree with nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) models at temperatures of 0.75 and 1.0 MeV, but at T = 0.5 MeV and proton fractions above Y_P = 0.2 the simulations produce non-equilibrium results with large rod-like nuclei, indicating the MD model is valid at higher densities than simple NSE models.

## Method
The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations using the IUMD GPU code with 51,200 and 409,600 nucleons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They decompressed systems from an initial density of 0.080 fm^{-3} down to 0.00125 fm^{-3} by expanding the simulation volume, studying proton fractions Y_P = 0.05, 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, and 0.40 at temperatures T = 0.5, 0.75, and 1.0 MeV. The composition of resulting systems was calculated using a Minimum Spanning Tree cluster algorithm with a proton-proton cut-off distance of 4.5 fm and a proton-neutron cut-off distance of 3 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous molecular dynamics studies of nuclear pasta by Horowitz et al. (2004-2014) and Schneider et al. (2013) that identified pasta phases and their transitions, but did not focus on nucleosynthesis applications. The paper addresses the role of nuclear pasta in r-process nucleosynthesis during neutron star mergers, where relativistic hydrodynamic simulations by Goriely et al. (2011) and Korobkin et al. (2012) found that inner crust ejecta provides the largest portion of ejecta mass. The results enable future work to determine initial temperatures and proton fractions of matter ejected in mergers, and suggest that pasta properties could influence the evolution of temperature and entropy in ejecta material.
