# The multiplets of finite width 0++ mesons and encounters with exotics

> Complex-mass (finite-width) $0^{++}$ nonet and decuplet are investigated by means of exotic commutator method. The hypothesis of vanishing of the exotic commutators leads to the system of master equations (ME). Solvability conditions of these equations define relations between the complex masses of the nonet and decuplet mesons which, in turn, determine relations between the real masses (mass formulae), as well as between the masses and widths of the mesons. Mass formulae are independent of the particle widths. The masses of the nonet and decuplet particles obey simple ordering rules. The nonet mixing angle and the mixing matrix of the isoscalar states of the decuplet are completely determined by solution of ME; they are real and do not depend on the widths. All known scalar mesons with the mass smaller than $2000MeV$ (excluding $σ(600)$) and one with the mass $2200\div2400MeV$ belong to two multiplets: the nonet $(a_0(980), K_0(1430), f_0(980), f_0(1710))$ and the decuplet $(a_0(1450), K_0(1950), f_0(1370), f_0(1500), f_0(2200)/f_0(2330))$. It is shown that the famed anomalies of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ widths arise from an extra "kinematical" mechanism, suppressing decay, which is not conditioned by the flavor coupling constant. Therefore, they do not justify rejecting the $q\bar{q}$ structure of them. A unitary singlet state (glueball) is included into the higher lying multiplet (decuplet) and is divided among the $f_0(1370)$ and $f_0(1500)$ mesons. The glueball contents of these particles are totally determined by the masses of decuplet particles. Mass ordering rules indicate that the meson $σ(600)$ does not mix with the nonet particles.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed narrow widths of the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) mesons (hadronic widths of 40–100 MeV) do not contradict a \(q\bar{q}\) structure, because these widths arise from a "kinematical" suppression mechanism that is independent of the flavor coupling constant; the flavor widths predicted by the Schwinger nonet model (≈535 MeV) are consistent with the once-expected \(q\bar{q}\) widths of 500–1000 MeV.

## Method

The author applies the exotic commutator method (ECM), assuming the vanishing of exotic commutators of \(SU(3)_F\) generators with time derivatives of the squared-mass operator, to derive master equations (ME) for the octet contents of isoscalar states in complex-mass (finite-width) meson multiplets. Solving the ME for a nonet yields the Schwinger mass formula, mixing angle, and a flavor stitch line (FSL) relating masses and widths; for a decuplet, the ME with four equations gives a mass formula, width sum rule, and mass ordering rules that determine the mixing of a glueball singlet with the \(q\bar{q}\) nonet states.

## Key numbers

- The experimental hadronic width of \(f_0(980)\) is \(40\div100\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and of \(a_0(980)\) is \(50\div100\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the predicted flavor width from the FSL is \(\Gamma\approx 535\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for both.
- The Schwinger nonet mass formula yields a mixing angle \(\theta = (33.5 \pm 2.0)^\circ\) for the scalar nonet \((a_0(980), K_0(1430), f_0(980), f_0(1710))\).
- The slope of the flavor stitch line for the scalar nonet is \(k_s = -0.56\), consistent with the typical value \(k_s = -0.5 \pm 0.1\) for S nonets.
- For the decuplet, the mass of the heaviest isoscalar candidate is \(m_3 = 2330\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (solution 1) or \(m_3 = 2205.34\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (solution 2), with the octet content of \(f_0(1500)\) being \(|l_2|^2 = 0.07691\) or \(0.09590\), indicating a mostly glueball state.
- The mass of the \(K_0(1950)\) candidate is \(1945 \pm 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and of \(a_0(1450)\) is \(1474 \pm 19\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the ECM approach previously applied to zero-width mesons (Majewski and collaborators, earlier papers) and to finite-width nonets of various \(J^{PC}\) (Majewski, 2004) by fitting the scalar \(0^{++}\) nonet and, for the first time, a finite-width decuplet that includes a glueball. It contradicts exotic models (e.g., Jaffe, 1977; Weinstein and Isgur, 1983) that interpret scalar mesons as \(qq\bar{q}\bar{q}\) states, arguing that the width anomalies do not rule out \(q\bar{q}\) structure. The results enable a systematic classification of all known scalar mesons below 2000 MeV (except \(\sigma(600)\)) into a nonet and a decuplet, with the glueball content of \(f_0(1370)\) and \(f_0(1500)\) determined purely by masses.
