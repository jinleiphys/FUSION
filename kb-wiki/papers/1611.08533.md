# From $J/ψ$ to LHCb pentaquark

> The original charmonium two-body problem, like the $c\bar c$ structure of the $J/ψ$ meson, has become more involved in the last few years with the discovery of new resonances such as the tentative molecular state $X(3872)$ or the possible pentaquark one $P_{c}(4380)^{+}$. We discuss herein how these exotic states (and others) can be described in a unified way adding higher Fock state components to the naive quark model picture. In particular, we present our theoretical results on the pentaquark states $P_{c}(4380)^{+}$ and $P_{c}(4450)^{+}$, and on the new charmonium-like resonances $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$ and $X(4700)$ that have been reported in $2016$ by the LHCb Collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the LHCb pentaquark states \(P_{c}(4380)^{+}\) and \(P_{c}(4450)^{+}\) can be described as molecular \(\bar{D}\Sigma_{c}^{*}\) and \(\bar{D}^{*}\Sigma_{c}\) bound states, respectively, within a constituent quark model that includes higher Fock components; however, the \(X(4140)\) resonance is not a bound state but a threshold cusp, while the \(X(4274)\), \(X(4500)\), and \(X(4700)\) are consistent with conventional charmonium states.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model that incorporates dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, a linear screened confining potential, and one-gluon exchange. Two-body (quark-antiquark) systems are solved via the Gaussian Expansion method, while four- and five-body (molecular and pentaquark) systems are solved using the Resonating Group Method, with couplings between configurations handled by the \(^{3}P_{0}\) model. For the \(X(4140)\) region, a coupled-channel calculation including \(D_{s}D_{s}^{*}\), \(D_{s}^{*}D_{s}^{*}\), and \(J/\psi\phi\) thresholds is performed, and the \(J/\psi\phi\) line shape is analyzed to test for a threshold cusp.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\bar{D}\Sigma_{c}^{*}\) molecular state with \(J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}\) has a mass of 4385.0 MeV, decaying to \(J/\psi p\) with a width of 10.0 MeV and to \(\bar{D}^{*}\Lambda_{c}\) with a width of 14.7 MeV.
*   Three nearly degenerate \(\bar{D}^{*}\Sigma_{c}\) states are found near 4460 MeV: a \(J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}\) state at 4458.9 MeV (decay width to \(J/\psi p\) = 5.3 MeV, to \(\bar{D}^{*}\Lambda_{c}\) = 63.6 MeV), a \(J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}\) state at 4461.3 MeV (0.8 MeV and 21.2 MeV), and a \(J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{+}\) state at 4462.7 MeV (0.2 MeV and 6.3 MeV).
*   The \(X(4274)\) is identified as a \(3^{3}P_{1}\) charmonium state; the coupled-channel calculation yields a mass of 4242.4 MeV and a total width of 25.9 MeV, with a wavefunction composed of 48.7% \(c\bar{c}\) (3P) and 43.5% \(D_{s}D_{s}^{*}\).
*   The \(X(4500)\) and \(X(4700)\) are assigned as \(4^{3}P_{0}\) and \(5^{3}P_{0}\) charmonium states, with theoretical masses of 4497.2 MeV and 4697.6 MeV, respectively, compared to experimental values of \(4506\pm 11^{+12}_{-15}\) MeV and \(4704\pm 10^{+14}_{-24}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach previously applied to two- and four-quark systems (e.g., the \(X(3872)\) as a \(DD^{*}\) molecule) to the five-quark pentaquark states discovered by LHCb in 2015 (Aaij 2015). It also addresses the 2016 LHCb observation of four new charmonium-like resonances (Aaij 2016), offering an interpretation that contrasts with the multiquark proposal of Chen (2016). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted \(\bar{D}^{*}\Lambda_{c}\) decay channel of the pentaquark states and refine the understanding of the \(X(4140)\) as a kinematic effect rather than a genuine resonance.
