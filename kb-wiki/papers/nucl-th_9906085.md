# Off-shell effects on particle production

> We investigate the observable effects of off-shell propagation of nucleons in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies. Within a semi-classical BUU transport model we find a strong enhancement of subthreshold particle production when off-shell nucleons are propagated.

# Off-shell effects on particle production

## Key claim
Off-shell propagation of nucleons in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies produces a strong enhancement of subthreshold particle production, but the effect is primarily driven by collisional broadening during the high-density phase rather than by the mechanism that restores particles to their mass shell when they propagate to vacuum.

## Method
The authors extended a semi-classical BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to include dynamical spectral functions for nucleons, treating them on the same footing as baryonic resonances. They solved the transport equation using a test particle method, where the nucleon collisional width was calculated as a function of momentum in the local rest frame, density, and temperature, and they introduced a restoring potential to shift particles back to their mass shell when propagating out of the nuclear environment, with a back-coupling term to conserve total energy.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon collisional width is of the order of 300 MeV for a temperature of 100 MeV and a momentum of 1 GeV at density 2ρ₀.
- The spectral distribution of nucleons in the high-density phase has a width on the order of 200 MeV.
- The nucleon mass distribution at the end of the collision (t = 40 fm) is peaked around the nucleon pole mass with a full width at half maximum (FWHM) of approximately 10 MeV.
- The number of high-energy pions is increased by about a factor of 2 due to off-shell propagation.
- The K⁺ spectrum shows an enhancement by about a factor of 2 due to off-shell propagation.
- Total energy conservation is maintained at a level better than 1% with the back-coupling potential; neglecting it leads to a violation of about 10%.
- Calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV bombarding energy using an equation of state with compressibility K = 380 MeV (results were quantitatively the same with K = 215 MeV).
- A lower mass cut-off of 400 MeV was employed to avoid unphysical test particle velocities.

## Context
This work extends the semi-classical transport models that have been applied to heavy-ion collisions from SIS to SPS energies (Bratkovskaya, 1990s; Bass, 1990s), which previously used an on-shell approximation for nucleons while propagating resonances with spectral functions. The authors had earlier developed a model incorporating dynamical spectral functions for ρ and ω mesons (Effenberger and Mosel, 1999), and here they treat nucleons on the same footing. The results are consistent with the quantum-mechanical off-shell investigation of Bozek (1990s) which also found higher particle yields when in-medium correlations are taken into account. This work enables future studies of off-shell effects on particle production yields in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that improvements are needed regarding detailed balance, analyticity of the self-energy, and dynamical spectral functions for pions.
