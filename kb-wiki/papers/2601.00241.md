# Revisiting p-$^{11}$B Fusion: Updated Cross-sections, Reactivity, and Energy Balance

> Recent experimental progress has substantially improved the available cross-section data for the p-$^{11}$B fusion reaction, particularly in energy regions that previously lacked direct measurements. In this study, we develop a high-precision analytical parameterization of the p-$^{11}$B reaction cross-section over the 0--10 MeV energy range, incorporating the new experimental data into a continuous and numerically efficient representation. Using this parameterization, we evaluate the thermonuclear reactivity of the p-$^{11}$B reaction and examine the effects of the dominant resonance at 0.6 MeV and a newly observed resonance around 4.7 MeV. Furthermore, we assess the energy balance by analyzing the fusion power density and the electron bremsstrahlung power density. Our results indicate that p-$^{11}$B fusion is not precluded by bremsstrahlung constraints when contemporary cross-section data and self-consistent thermal modeling are employed.

# Revisiting p-¹¹B Fusion: Updated Cross-sections, Reactivity, and Energy Balance

## Key claim

p-¹¹B fusion is not fundamentally excluded by bremsstrahlung constraints when the energy balance is evaluated using updated cross-section data and a self-consistent treatment of electron temperature (rather than assuming electron-ion thermal equilibrium), but the conclusion depends on the existence of a finite ion-temperature window between approximately 0.17 and 0.38 MeV where fusion power exceeds bremsstrahlung losses.

## Method

The authors developed a piecewise analytical parameterization of the astrophysical S-factor for the p-¹¹B reaction over 0–10 MeV center-of-mass energy, using experimental data from Becker et al. (1987), Mazzucconi et al. (2025), and Buck et al. (1983). They then computed the Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reactivity by convolving the cross-section with a velocity distribution, and assessed energy balance by comparing normalized fusion power density (using Y = 8.7 MeV per reaction) against electron bremsstrahlung power density calculated with the Rider (1995) formula, with electron temperature determined self-consistently from the electron-ion energy balance equation.

## Key numbers

- Gamow energy of the p-¹¹B system: EG = 22.589 MeV
- Dominant resonance energy: 0.6222 MeV (E0 parameter); newly observed resonance energy: 4.7036 MeV (E4 parameter)
- Narrow resonance at 148 keV with width δEL = 2.35 keV
- Fit quality: R² = 0.97 for 0.4–0.7 MeV range and for 0.7–5.0 MeV range; mean relative deviation 3% (0.4–0.7 MeV) and 6.2% (0.7–5.0 MeV)
- For 5–10 MeV range: R² = 0.62, mean relative deviation 12.4%
- A ±10% variation in the 0.6 MeV resonance peak strength produces a 3–5% variation in reactivity over T = 0.1–0.4 MeV, approaching 10% total spread near 0.2 MeV
- Self-consistent energy balance yields two intersections between fusion power and bremsstrahlung loss at T ≈ 0.17 MeV and 0.38 MeV
- Reference composition: f₁ = 1/2 (protons), f₂ = 1/10 (¹¹B ions)

## Context

This work extends the parameterization framework of Nevins and Swain (2000) and Tentori et al. (2023) by incorporating new experimental data from Mazzucconi et al. (2025), which provides direct cross-section measurements using a monolithic silicon telescope (avoiding model-dependent normalization issues present in Sikora et al. (2016)). The energy balance analysis builds on earlier assessments by Nevins (1998) and Putvinskiy (2019), and the authors find that, contrary to the conventional conclusion from McNally (1970) and Moreau (1977) that bremsstrahlung precludes p-¹¹B fusion, a positive net power balance is achievable when electron temperature is treated self-consistently. This enables refined assessments of p-¹¹B as an aneutronic fusion fuel and motivates further experimental work to reduce uncertainties in the dominant 0.6 MeV resonance strength.
