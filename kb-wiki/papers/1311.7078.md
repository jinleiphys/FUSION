# Heavy quarkonium fragmentation functions from a heavy quark pair. I. $S$ wave

> A QCD factorization formalism was recently proposed for evaluating heavy quarkonium production at large $p_T$ at collider energies. With systematically calculated short-distance partonic hard parts and evolution kernels of fragmentation functions (FFs), the predictive power of this factorization approach relies on our knowledge of a large number of universal FFs at an input factorization scale $μ_0\gtrsim 2m_Q$ with heavy quark mass $m_Q$. With the large heavy quark mass, the relative motion of the heavy quark pair inside a heavy quarkonium is effectively non-relativistic. We evaluate these universal input FFs using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) factorization, and express the large number of FFs in terms of a few universal NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) with perturbatively calculated coefficients. We derive complete contributions to the single-parton FFs at both ${\cal O}(α_s)$ and ${\cal O}(α_s^2)$, and the heavy quark pair FFs at ${\cal O}(α_s)$. We present detailed derivation for all contributions involving LDMEs of $S$-wave NRQCD $Q\bar{Q}$-states ($P$-wave contributions in a companion paper). Our results bridge the gap between the QCD factorization formalism and its phenomenological applications.

## Key claim

The authors use NRQCD factorization to compute all heavy quarkonium fragmentation functions (FFs) at an input scale μ₀ ≳ 2m_Q, expressing the many unknown FFs in terms of a few universal NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs), but note that this NRQCD factorization for FFs remains a conjecture without formal proof to all orders.

## Method

The authors apply NRQCD factorization to both single-parton and heavy quark-pair fragmentation functions at the input scale μ₀, matching perturbative QCD calculations of FFs for asymptotic partonic states onto NRQCD LDMEs. They derive complete contributions to single-parton FFs at O(α_s) and O(α_s²), and heavy quark-pair FFs at O(α_s), for all S-wave NRQCD Q\bar{Q} states, using the projection operators for vector, axial-vector, and tensor spin states in both color-singlet and color-octet configurations.

## Key numbers

- Input factorization scale: μ₀ ≳ 2m_Q, where m_Q is the heavy quark mass
- Single-parton FFs calculated at both O(α_s) and O(α_s²)
- Heavy quark-pair FFs calculated at O(α_s)
- For J/ψ production, the most important LDMEs up to order v⁴ are n = ³S₁^{[1]}, ¹S₀^{[8]}, ³S₁^{[8]}, and ³P_J^{[8]}
- A minimum of four single-parton input FFs and six Q\bar{Q}-pair input FFs are needed as boundary conditions for each heavy quarkonium state

## Context

This work bridges the gap between the recently proposed QCD factorization formalism for high-p_T heavy quarkonium production (Kang, Qiu, et al., 2011-2013) and its phenomenological applications by providing calculable input FFs. It extends the NRQCD factorization approach (Bodwin, Braaten, Lepage, 1995) to fragmentation functions, complementing earlier calculations of single-parton FFs by Braaten et al. (1993-2000) and Jia (2012). The results enable first numerical predictions for heavy quarkonium production at colliders using the QCD factorization approach, with P-wave contributions deferred to a companion paper.
