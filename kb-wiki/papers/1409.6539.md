# Matrix Models for Deconfinement and Their Perturbative Corrections

> Matrix models for the deconfining phase transition in $SU(N)$ gauge theories have been developed in recent years. With a few parameters, these models are able to reproduce the lattice results of the thermodynamic quantities in the semi-quark gluon plasma(QGP) region. They are also used to compute the behavior of the 't Hooft loop and study the exceptional group $G(2)$. In this paper, we review the basic ideas of the construction of these models and propose a new form of the non-ideal corrections in the matrix model. In the semi-QGP region, our new model is in good agreement with the lattice simulations as the previous ones, while in higher temperature region, it reproduces the upward trend of the rescaled trace anomaly as found in lattice which, however, can not be obtained from the previous models. In addition, we discuss the perturbative corrections to the thermal effective potential which could be used to systematically improve the matrix models at high temperatures. In particular, we provide, for the first time, an analytical proof of the relation between the one- and two-loop effective potential: two-loop correction is proportional to the one-loop result, independent of the eigenvalues of the Polyakov loop. This is a very general result, we prove it for all classic groups, including $SU(N)$, $SO(2N+1)$, $SO(2N)$ and $Sp(2N)$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new form of non-ideal corrections in the matrix model for deconfinement that, unlike previous models, reproduces the upward trend of the rescaled trace anomaly above approximately 4 T_c as found in lattice simulations, while still matching lattice data in the semi-QGP region from T_c to about 4 T_c.

## Method

The author constructs a matrix model using the one-loop thermal effective potential as the ideal contribution, then adds non-ideal corrections where the mass scale M(T) depends on temperature via a running coupling at one-loop level, M^2(T) = c_1'' g^2(T, c_2') T T_c. The eigenvalues of the Polyakov loop are parameterized under a straight-line ansatz, and the four temperature-independent parameters are fixed by requiring the phase transition at T_c, matching the latent heat from lattice data, and fitting the lattice pressure for SU(N) with N=3, 4, and 6. Separately, the author provides an analytical proof of the relation between one- and two-loop effective potentials using Bernoulli polynomials for all classic groups including SU(N), SO(2N+1), SO(2N), and Sp(2N).

## Key numbers

* For SU(3), the four parameters in the new model are c_1' = 0.1352, c_2' = 1.6885, c_3' = -0.3994, c_4' = 0.2883, with a bag constant B^{1/4} = 228 MeV (T_c = 270 MeV assumed).
* For SU(4), the parameters are c_1' = 0.1989, c_2' = 1.8878, c_3' = -0.4079, c_4' = 0.3454, with B^{1/4} = 279 MeV.
* For SU(6), the parameters are c_1' = 0.3537, c_2' = 2.2342, c_3' = -0.4542, c_4' = 0.4264, with B^{1/4} = 363 MeV.
* The latent heat values from lattice used for constraints are L(3) = 0.209, L(4) = 0.287, and L(6) = 0.342, renormalized by the number of perturbative gluons (N^2-1).
* The two-loop effective potential is proportional to the one-loop result with the relation Gamma^{(2)}/Gamma^{(1)} = -5 g^2 C_2(A) / (16 pi^2), where C_2(A) is the quadratic Casimir invariant in the adjoint representation.

## Context

This work extends the matrix model approach for the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theories originally proposed by Meisinger, Miller, and Ogilvie (2002) and later improved by Dumitru, Guo, Hidaka, Korthals Altes, and Pisarski (2010, 2012). The new model addresses the upward trend of the rescaled trace anomaly above 4 T_c observed in lattice simulations by the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2012), which previous matrix models could not reproduce. The analytical proof of the one- to two-loop effective potential relation completes a calculation studied for about twenty years, generalizing earlier results for SU(N) along straight paths (Bhattacharya et al., 1992; Giovannangeli and Korthals Altes, 2002) to all classic groups and arbitrary paths inside the Weyl chamber.
