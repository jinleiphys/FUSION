# Deeply bound dibaryon $d^*(2380)$ from meson-exchange saturation $ΔΔ$ effective field theory

> We propose an RG-improved effective-field-theory framework for the deeply bound dibaryon $d^*(2380)$, a $ΔΔ$ bound state in the $(J,I)=(3,0)$ ${}^7S_3$ channel. Its binding momentum $γ\simeq 320$ MeV gives $γ/m_π\simeq 2.3$, indicating the need to re-organize the short-range dynamics beyond a formal pionless EFT. We match the large-$N_c$-constrained pionless contact potential to a meson-exchange-saturated contact interaction in which the $σ,ρ,ω$ dynamics are integrated out at the hadronic scale $m_V$, yielding the controlled expansion parameter $γ/m_V\simeq 0.42$. Normalizing the contact coupling to the deuteron and substituting the phenomenological CD-Bonn couplings gives $B_{ΔΔ}\simeq 96$ MeV. The $\simeq 14\%$ discrepancy from $B_{\rm exp}=84$ MeV is of the natural size of $\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\simeq 11\%$ corrections to the $NN$ potential, confirming compatibility with a controlled EFT expansion organized around the finite-range hadronic scale. As a result, the observed $d^*(2380)$ pole emerges from the virtual state to bound state by using the EFT re-organization in this work.

## Key claim
The deeply bound dibaryon $d^*(2380)$ can be understood as a consequence of reorganizing the effective field theory from a pionless expansion (which breaks down at $\gamma/m_\pi \simeq 2.3$) to a meson-exchange-saturated EFT with expansion parameter $\gamma/m_V \simeq 0.42$, where the leading contact interaction absorbs the unresolved $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ dynamics at the hadronic scale. The predicted binding energy $B_{\Delta\Delta} \simeq 96$ MeV exceeds the experimental value $B_{\rm exp} = 84$ MeV by about 14%, which the authors argue is consistent with the natural size of $\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2) \simeq 11\%$ corrections.

## Method
The authors construct two contact effective field theories for the $(J,I)=(3,0)$ $^7S_3$ $\Delta\Delta$ channel. In the pionless EFT, the leading contact coupling is fixed by large-$N_c$ sum rules from $NN$ and $\Lambda N$ scattering data. In the meson-exchange-saturated EFT, the same contact operator absorbs the zero-momentum limits of $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ exchange, with the overall normalization fixed by reproducing the deuteron binding energy. The bound-state pole is obtained from the algebraic Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator, and the prediction depends only on the dimensionless saturation ratio $R_{\Delta\Delta/d}$ after deuteron normalization.

## Key numbers
- Binding momentum $\gamma \simeq 320$ MeV for $d^*(2380)$, giving $\gamma/m_\pi \simeq 2.3$ and $\gamma/m_V \simeq 0.42$
- Large-$N_c$-constrained contact coupling $C_0^{(3,0)} = -10.3 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$ at $\mu_R = m_V$, which is subcritical: $|C_0|/|C_0^{(\rm crit)}| \simeq 0.40$ with critical coupling $|C_0^{(\rm crit)}| = 25.6 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$ at $\Lambda = 1000$ MeV
- Deuteron-normalized saturation coupling $|C_0^{({\rm sat})}| = 55.3 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$ at $\Lambda = 1000$ MeV, giving $|C_0^{({\rm sat})}|/|C_0^{(\rm crit)}| \simeq 2.16$
- Predicted binding energy $B_{\Delta\Delta} \simeq 96$ MeV with CD-Bonn couplings ($g_\sigma^2/(4\pi)=5.69$, $g_\omega^2/(4\pi)=20.0$, $g_\rho^2/(4\pi)=0.84$) and $\alpha=1$, compared to experimental $B_{\rm exp}=84$ MeV
- Saturation ratio $R_{\Delta\Delta/d} \simeq 1.9$ for CD-Bonn couplings, with the empirical $B=84$ MeV corresponding to $R \simeq 1.85$
- Cutoff variation $\Lambda \in [800, 1250]$ MeV gives $B_{\Delta\Delta} \in [67, 141]$ MeV at $R \simeq 1.9$
- Combined uncertainty estimate: $B_{\Delta\Delta}^{(3,0)}|_{\mathrm{Bonn}} = 96\,^{+45}_{-30}$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the meson-exchange saturation approach previously applied to heavy-hadron molecules by Peng et al. (2020, 2022) and pentaquarks by Yang et al. (2025) and Yan et al. (2024) to the $\Delta\Delta$ dibaryon sector. It contrasts with conventional one-boson-exchange potentials by Gal et al. (2014) and Machleidt (1989, 2001) that iterate heavy mesons as dynamical degrees of freedom; here the heavy mesons only saturate the leading contact interaction. The framework enables systematic improvement through finite-$N_c$ corrections, finite-range operators, explicit pion exchange, and the finite $\Delta$ width, and predicts correlated pole patterns across decuplet-decuplet channels that can be tested against lattice QCD results such as those from the HAL QCD collaboration (2020).
