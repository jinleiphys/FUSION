# Axial exchange currents and the spin content of the nucleon

> In a chiral quark model where chiral symmetry is introduced via a non-linear sigma model, we evaluate the axial couplings g_A(0), g_A^8(0) and g_A^0(0 )related to the spin structure of the nucleon. Our calculation includes one-body and two-body axial current and pion absorption operators, which satisfy the Partial Conservation of Axial Current (PCAC) condition. While g_A(0) is dominated by the one-body axial current we find significant corrections due to two-body axial exchange currents in g_A^8(0) and g_A^0(0). Interestingly, the axial current associated with gluon exchange reduces g_A^8(0) from 1 to 0.6. Our analysis shows that the so-called ``proton spin crisis'' can be resolved in a constituent quark model in which PCAC is satisfied. Furthermore, we use the PCAC constraint in order to determine the couplings of the eta and eta' mesons to nucleons.

## Key claim

In a chiral quark model with a non-linear sigma model, the "proton spin crisis" can be resolved within a constituent quark framework once two commonly used approximations are removed: (i) the axial charge of constituent quarks is not set to unity but is instead determined by a Goldberger-Treiman relation at the quark level, and (ii) two-body axial exchange currents are included to satisfy the Partial Conservation of Axial Current (PCAC) condition.

## Method

The authors evaluate the axial couplings g_A(0), g_A^8(0), and g_A^0(0) using a nonrelativistic chiral quark model based on the non-linear sigma model of Manohar and Georgi. They include one-body and two-body axial current operators (from gluon exchange, pion exchange, and confinement) along with pion absorption operators, all constructed to satisfy the PCAC constraint. The nucleon wave function is expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis up to N=2 excitation quanta, with mixing coefficients determined by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian that includes confinement, one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and one-eta_8 exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

- g_A(0) = 1.26 ± 0.04 (experimental value from neutron beta-decay and deep inelastic scattering)
- g_A^8(0) = 0.60 ± 0.08 (experimental value from hyperon beta-decays and deep inelastic scattering)
- g_A^0(0) = 0.33 ± 0.06 (experimental value from deep inelastic scattering)
- The axial current associated with gluon exchange reduces g_A^8(0) from the naive quark model value of 1 to 0.6
- Constituent quark axial coupling g_Aq = 0.774, obtained from the Goldberger-Treiman relation at the quark level using m_q = 313 MeV and f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Experimental spin fractions: Delta u = 0.84 ± 0.02, Delta d = -0.42 ± 0.02, Delta s = -0.09 ± 0.02 (from Abe et al.)
- Cut-off parameter Lambda = 4.2 fm^{-1} (from fitting the q-anti-q component of the pion)
- Pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV; eta_8 mass m_eta8 = 565 MeV (from Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of nucleon axial form factors (Barquilla-Cano et al., 2002) from the isovector axial coupling to the flavor octet and singlet couplings. It addresses the "proton spin crisis" identified by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) in the late 1980s, where deep inelastic scattering showed that only about one-third of the proton spin is carried by quark spins, contradicting the naive nonrelativistic quark model prediction of 1. The results show that once PCAC-violating approximations (setting g_Aq = 1 and neglecting two-body axial exchange currents) are removed, the constituent quark model can reproduce the experimental spin fractions, and the PCAC constraint further determines the eta-N and eta'-N coupling constants.
