# Single Charge-Exchange Reactions and the Neutron Density at the Surface of the Nucleus

> In this work we study the charge-exchange reaction to Isobaric Analog State using two types of transition densities. We show that for projectiles that do not probe the interior of the nucleus but mostly the surface of this nucleus, distinct differences in the cross-section arise when the two types of transition densities are employed. We demonstrate this by considering the (3He,t) reaction.

## Key claim

For strongly absorbed projectiles like \(^3\)He that probe only the nuclear surface, the choice between the full transition density \(\rho_n(r) - \rho_p(r)\) and the excess-neutron-only density \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) produces distinct differences in the calculated charge-exchange cross-section to the Isobaric Analog State (IAS); the effect is largest in nuclei with few excess neutrons, where using \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) gives better agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The single charge-exchange (SCX) reaction to the IAS was described within the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) using the code ECIS06 with relativistic kinematics. The SCX form factor was computed via the double-folding model using the Franey-Love \(t\)-matrix for the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction, with target densities obtained from Hartree-Fock (HF) or HF-BCS calculations using the BSk17 Skyrme parametrization. Two transition densities were compared: the full isovector difference \(\rho_n(r) - \rho_p(r)\) and the excess neutron density \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\), with the Coulomb-polarization term \(\delta\rho(r)\) being the difference between them.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{58}\)Ni, the cross-section in the forward direction using \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) is increased by more than a factor of 2 compared to using \(\rho_n(r) - \rho_p(r)\), agreeing with experimental data.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 420\) MeV, the cross-section calculated with \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) is slightly higher than with \(\rho_n(r) - \rho_p(r)\), but the difference is not large.
- For \(^{90}\)Zr at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 420\) MeV, the cross-section with \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) is larger and closer to the experimental results.
- The surface integral of the Coulomb polarization density \(S_{\delta\rho(\text{sur})}\) is \(-0.531\) for \(^{58}\)Ni, \(-1.691\) for \(^{90}\)Zr, \(-1.453\) for \(^{120}\)Sn, and \(-3.683\) for \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The neutron-proton radius difference for the core \((r_n - r_p)_{\text{core}}\) is \(-0.047\) fm for \(^{58}\)Ni, \(-0.103\) fm for \(^{90}\)Zr, \(-0.070\) fm for \(^{120}\)Sn, and \(-0.130\) fm for \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical framework of Auerbach (1983) on the Coulomb polarization of the core in IAS transitions, and the analog spin scheme of Mekjian (1968) and Auerbach (1981, 1982) which identifies \(\rho_{n(exc)}(r)\) as the proper transition density for the physical IAS. It applies these ideas to recent (\(^3\)He,t) experiments analyzed by Loc (2014), showing that strongly absorbed projectiles can serve as surface probes of the neutron-proton distribution, in contrast to (\(p,n\)) reactions which probe the interior and show negligible sensitivity to the choice of transition density, as demonstrated for \(^{120}\)Sn at 170 MeV.
