# Observation of Non-Exponential Orbital Electron Capture Decays of Hydrogen-Like $^{140}$Pr and $^{142}$Pm Ions

> We report on time-modulated two-body weak decays observed in the orbital electron capture of hydrogen-like $^{140}$Pr$^{59+}$ and $^{142}$Pm$^{60+}$ ions coasting in an ion storage ring. Using non-destructive single ion, time-resolved Schottky mass spectrometry we found that the expected exponential decay is modulated in time with a modulation period of about 7 seconds for both systems. Tentatively this observation is attributed to the coherent superposition of finite mass eigenstates of the electron neutrinos from the weak decay into a two-body final state.

## Key claim

The expected exponential decay of hydrogen-like \(^{140}\)Pr\(^{59+}\) and \(^{142}\)Pm\(^{60+}\) ions via orbital electron capture is modulated in time with a period of about 7 seconds in the laboratory frame, tentatively attributed to the coherent superposition of finite mass eigenstates of the electron neutrino emitted in the two-body weak decay.

## Method

Hydrogen-like \(^{140}\)Pr\(^{58+}\) and \(^{142}\)Pm\(^{60+}\) ions were produced by fragmentation of a \(^{152}\)Sm primary beam, separated in-flight with the FRS, and injected as single bunches (average of two parent ions) into the ESR storage ring at 400 MeV/u. The ions were cooled by stochastic and electron cooling, and their revolution frequencies were monitored continuously via non-destructive, time-resolved Schottky mass spectrometry (SMS) at the 30th harmonic. The correlated disappearance of a parent ion and appearance of an EC-daughter ion (identified by a frequency shift of about 270 Hz for \(^{140}\)Pr and 310 Hz for \(^{142}\)Pm) provided the decay time of each stored ion; the data from multiple injections were binned in time and fitted with both an exponential decay function and a modulated exponential function.

## Key numbers

- Modulation period in the laboratory frame: 7.06(8) s for \(^{140}\)Pr and 7.10(22) s for \(^{142}\)Pm.
- Modulation amplitude \(a\): 0.18(3) for \(^{140}\)Pr and 0.23(4) for \(^{142}\)Pm; average \(\langle a \rangle = 0.20(2)\).
- Angular frequency \(\omega\) of the modulation: 0.89(1) rad/s for \(^{140}\)Pr and 0.89(3) rad/s for \(^{142}\)Pm.
- Exponential decay constant \(\lambda\) for \(^{140}\)Pr: 0.00147(10) s\(^{-1}\) (modulated fit); for \(^{142}\)Pm: 0.0224(41) s\(^{-1}\) (modulated fit, first 33 s).
- Ratio \(\lambda_{EC}/\lambda_{\beta^+}\) for H-like \(^{140}\)Pr: 0.95(8).
- The \(\chi^2\)/DoF for a pure exponential fit is 107.2/73 (\(^{140}\)Pr) and 63.77/38 (\(^{142}\)Pm); for the modulated fit it is 67.18/70 and 31.82/35, respectively, allowing rejection of the pure exponential hypothesis at the 99% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of orbital electron capture in hydrogen-like and helium-like \(^{140}\)Pr ions at GSI (Litvinov et al., 2007), which found an enhanced EC decay rate in the hydrogen-like charge state, and is motivated by the question of whether neutrinos emitted as coherent superpositions of mass eigenstates could affect the exponential decay law (Litvinov and Bosch, proposal). The observed modulation period of about 7 seconds is similar for both nuclides despite different \(Q_{EC}\) values and lifetimes, and the authors note a possible connection to the KARMEN time anomaly (KARMEN collaboration, 1990s). The results challenge the standard exponential decay law for two-body weak decays and call for further studies of other electron-capture and bound-state beta decays, as well as three-body beta decays where the modulation should be washed out.
