# Proton Differential Elliptic Flow and the Isospin-Dependence of the Nuclear Equation of State

> Within an isospin-dependent transport model for nuclear reactions involving neutron-rich nuclei, we study the first-order direct transverse flow of protons and their second-order differential elliptic flow as a function of transverse momentum. It is found that the differential elliptic flow of mid-rapidity protons, especially at high transverse momenta, is much more sensitive to the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state than the direct flow. Origins of these different sensitivities and their implications to the experimental determination of the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state by using neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies are discussed.

## Key claim

The differential elliptic flow of mid-rapidity protons, especially at high transverse momenta, is much more sensitive to the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state than the first-order direct transverse flow, but this sensitivity is only clearly observable above statistical error bars for transverse momenta up to about 0.5 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate reactions of neutron-rich nuclei, specifically \(^{124}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn at a beam energy of 50 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 5 fm. They parameterized the symmetry energy as \(S(\rho)=S_0(\rho_0)\cdot u^{\gamma}\) with \(S_0(\rho_0)=30\) MeV and varied the parameter \(\gamma\) between 0.5 and 2.0 to explore a large uncertain range of the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state. They then computed the first-order transverse flow and the second-order differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_t)\) for unbound protons as functions of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density \(S_0(\rho_0)\) was taken as a constant value of 30 MeV.
- Two values of the \(\gamma\) parameter were used: \(\gamma=0.5\) and \(\gamma=2.0\), corresponding to symmetry energy curvature parameters \(K_{\text{sym}} = -68\) MeV and \(K_{\text{sym}} = 540\) MeV, respectively.
- The reaction studied was \(^{124}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn at a beam energy of 50 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 5 fm.
- The elliptic flow signal was found to be unambiguous beyond statistical error bars for transverse momenta up to 0.5 GeV/c.
- The stiff isoscalar equation of state used had a compressibility \(K_0 = 380\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of neutron-proton differential flow by Li (2000) as a probe of the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state, but focuses on a charged-particle-only observable (proton elliptic flow) which is experimentally easier to measure. The study uses the same IBUU transport model framework as Li (1997, 1998) and Li, Ko, and Ren (1997), and the symmetry energy parameterization follows Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (2000). The finding that proton differential elliptic flow is more sensitive than direct transverse flow provides a practical guide for planning experiments at radioactive beam facilities and the future Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA).
