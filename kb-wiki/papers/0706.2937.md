# On the dependence of the wave function of a bound nucleon on its momentum and the EMC effect

> It is widely discussed in the literature that the wave function of the nucleon bound in a nucleus is modified due to the interaction with the surrounding medium. We argue that the modification should strongly depend on the momentum of the nucleon. We study such an effect in the case of the point-like configuration component of the wave function of a nucleon bound in a nucleus A, considering the case of arbitrary final state of the spectator A-1 system. We show that for non relativistic values of the nucleon momentum, the momentum dependence of the nucleon deformation appears to follow from rather general considerations and discuss the implications of our theoretical observation for two different phenomena: i) the search for medium induced modifications of the nucleon radius of a bound nucleon through the measurement of the electromagnetic nucleon form factors via the A(e,e'p)X process, and ii) the A-dependence of the EMC effect; in this latter case we also present a new method of estimating the fraction of the nucleus light-cone momentum carried by the photons and find that in a heavy nuclei protons loose about 2% of their momentum.

## Key claim

The modification of a bound nucleon's wave function due to the nuclear medium should strongly depend on the nucleon's momentum, not just on the average nuclear density; for non-relativistic nucleon momenta, this momentum dependence follows from general considerations based on the analyticity of the scattering amplitude near the nucleon pole.

## Method

The authors derived expressions for the suppression of point-like configurations (PLCs) in bound nucleons using the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for the nucleus and the closure approximation, extending previous work (Frankfurt and Strikman, 1985, 1988) to three-body and complex nuclei. They expressed the suppression factor δ_A(|p|,E) in terms of the nucleon momentum p and removal energy E, showing it is equivalent to a function of the nucleon virtuality in the non-relativistic limit. The average suppression was then computed using realistic spectral functions for ³He and complex nuclei, and applied to calculate the A-dependence of the EMC effect within the light-cone convolution model.

## Key numbers

- The energy denominator ΔE^(N/A) parametrizing the average virtual excitation of a nucleon in a nucleus is ~600-800 MeV.
- In heavy nuclei, protons lose about 2% of their light-cone momentum due to the coherent Weizsäcker-Williams photon field.
- The average suppression factor for PLCs in the lowest order approximation is ⟨δ_A⟩_LO ≈ 1 − 4(⟨T⟩ + |ε_A|)/ΔE^(N/A), where ⟨T⟩ is the average kinetic energy per nucleon and ε_A is the ground-state energy per particle.
- For the deuteron, the suppression factor for a nucleon with momentum p is δ_D(p) = (1 + (2(p²/2m_N) − E_D)/ΔE^(N/D))⁻², where E_D is the deuteron binding energy.
- For ³He, the suppression factor for a given channel f is δ_3^(f)(p,E) = (1 + (E + (3p²/4m_N))/ΔE^(N/3))⁻², where E is the removal energy.
- For complex nuclei, the general suppression factor is δ_A^(f)(p,E) = (1 + (E + (A/(A-1))(p²/2m_N))/ΔE^(N/A))⁻².
- The average virtuality for a nucleus A is ⟨v_NR⟩ = −4m_N[⟨T⟩ + |ε_A|], derived from the Koltun sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the PLC suppression model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1985, 1988) from the deuteron to ³He and complex nuclei, explicitly incorporating the dependence on the excitation energy of the spectator (A-1) system. It provides a theoretical framework connecting the momentum-dependent suppression of PLCs to the EMC effect, and suggests that semi-inclusive processes (tagged structure functions) and measurements of the momentum dependence of nucleon form factors via A(e,e'p)X can test these predictions. The results enable improved estimates of the A-dependence of the EMC effect and motivate dedicated experiments at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade.
