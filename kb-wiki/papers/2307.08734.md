# Equation of State of Cold Quark Matter to $O(α_s^3 \ln α_s)$

> Accurately understanding the equation of state (EOS) of high-density, zero-temperature quark matter plays an essential role in constraining the behavior of dense strongly interacting matter inside the cores of neutron stars. In this Letter, we study the weak-coupling expansion of the EOS of cold quark matter and derive the complete, gauge-invariant contributions from the long-wavelength, dynamically screened gluonic sector at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in the strong coupling constant $α_s$. This elevates the EOS result to the $O(α_s^3 \ln α_s)$ level, leaving only one unknown constant from the unscreened sector at N3LO, and places it on par with its high-temperature counterpart from 2003.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete, gauge-invariant contributions from the long-wavelength, dynamically screened gluonic sector at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in the strong coupling constant αs for cold quark matter, but leaves one unknown constant from the unscreened hard sector at N3LO, meaning the result is at the O(αs³ ln αs) level rather than the full O(αs³).

## Method

The authors computed the next-to-leading-order (NLO) screening corrections to long-wavelength gluon propagation in cold quark matter by extending existing hard-thermal-loop (HTL) results to zero temperature. They derived the mixed contribution to the pressure from two-loop corrections and power corrections to the NLO soft gluon self-energies, then combined these with previously known soft and hard contributions to obtain the full N3LO pressure expression.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient c3,2 of the leading logarithm is 11/12
- The coefficient c3,1 of the next-to-leading logarithm is -6.5968(12) - 3 ln X, where X ≡ 3Λ̄/(2μB)
- The coefficient c3,0 is 5.1342(48) + (2/3)c0 - 18.284 ln X - (9/2) ln² X, where c0 is the remaining unknown constant
- The mixed contribution coefficients are p^m_{-2} = -11, p^m_{-1}(X) = 9 ln X - 4.8095, and p^m_0(X) = -(9/2) ln² X + 2.0598 ln X - 5.6316
- The Bayesian posterior gives a 68% credible interval for c0 of [-29, -6], with the most consistent value at c0 = -23
- At the most consistent c0 = -23, the N3LO pressure reaches ±25% errors at μB = 2.2 GeV, corresponding to about n = 27 n0 (where n0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD equation of state for cold quark matter to the O(αs³ ln αs) level, placing it on par with the high-temperature counterpart from Kajantie (2003). The calculation generalizes recent results from Gorda (2023) and Ekstedt (2023) to zero temperature, and confirms a prediction from Gorda (2021) that p^m_{-2} = -2p^s_{-2}. The result enables more robust high-density constraints for neutron-star equation-of-state inference, with the remaining hard constant requiring future computation of four-loop vacuum diagrams.
