# Shape evolutions of $^{72,74}$Kr with temperature in the covariant density functional theory

> The rich phenomena of deformations in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes such as the shape evolution with neutron number and the shape coexistence attract the interests of nuclear physicists for decades. It will be interesting to study such shape phenomena using a novel way, i.e., by thermally exciting the nucleus. So in this work, we develop the finite temperature covariant density functional theory for axially deformed nuclei with the treatment of pairing correlations by BCS approach, and apply this approach for the study of shape evolutions in $^{72,74}$Kr with increasing temperatures. For $^{72}$Kr, with temperature increasing, the nucleus firstly experiences a relatively quick weakening in oblate deformation at temperature $T \sim0.9$ MeV, and then changes from oblate to spherical at $T \sim2.1$ MeV. For $^{74}$Kr, its global minimum locates at quadroupole deformation $β_2 \sim -0.14$ and abruptly changes to spherical at $T\sim 1.7$ MeV. The proton pairing transition occurs at critical temperature 0.6 MeV following the rule $T_c =0.6 Δ_p (0)$ where $Δ_p(0)$ is the proton pairing gap at zero temperature. The signatures of the above pairing transition and shape changes can be found in the curve of the specific heat. The single-particle level evolutions with the temperature are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for $^{72}$Kr and $^{74}$Kr, increasing temperature causes a shape transition from oblate to spherical, but the specific path differs: $^{72}$Kr undergoes a relatively quick weakening in oblate deformation at $T \sim 0.9$ MeV followed by an abrupt change to spherical at $T \sim 2.1$ MeV, while $^{74}$Kr changes abruptly from oblate to spherical at $T \sim 1.7$ MeV. The abstract glosses over the fact that these results are obtained within a mean-field approach (finite-temperature covariant density functional theory with BCS pairing) and that quantal fluctuations, which would wash out the sharp transitions, are not included.

## Method

The authors developed a finite-temperature covariant density functional theory (CDFT) for axially deformed nuclei, using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 and treating pairing correlations via the BCS approach with a delta pairing force. They performed constrained calculations to obtain free energy curves as functions of the quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ at temperatures from 0 to 4 MeV for $^{72}$Kr and $^{74}$Kr, and extracted the global minimum deformation, pairing gaps, excitation energy, entropy, and specific heat at each temperature.

## Key numbers

- For $^{72}$Kr, the nucleus experiences a relatively quick weakening in oblate deformation at $T \sim 0.9$ MeV and changes from oblate to spherical at $T \sim 2.1$ MeV.
- For $^{74}$Kr, the global minimum locates at quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 \sim -0.14$ and abruptly changes to spherical at $T \sim 1.7$ MeV.
- The proton pairing transition occurs at critical temperature $T_c = 0.6$ MeV, following the rule $T_c = 0.6 \Delta_p(0)$ where $\Delta_p(0) = 1.03$ MeV is the proton pairing gap at zero temperature for $^{74}$Kr.
- For $^{72}$Kr at zero temperature, four local minima exist at $\beta_2 = -0.34$, $-0.19$, $0.14$, and $0.39$, with energies relative to the ground state of 0, 1.36, 2.82, and 2.24 MeV, respectively.
- For $^{74}$Kr at zero temperature, four local minima exist at $\beta_2 = -0.36$, $-0.14$, $0.07$, and $0.47$, with the two lowest minima at $\beta_2 = -0.14$ and $0.47$ separated by only 0.20 MeV.
- The prolate minimum at $\beta_2 \sim 0.47$ for $^{74}$Kr becomes 0.5 MeV higher than the oblate minimum at $T = 0.75$ MeV and 1 MeV higher at $T = 1.05$ MeV, and vanishes at $T = 1.45$ MeV.
- The temperature range studied is 0 to 4 MeV, limited because vapor phase effects become important above $T \geq 4.0$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature covariant density functional theory, previously applied to spherical nuclei by Niu (2013) and Long (2015), to axially deformed nuclei for the first time. It applies this framework to study shape evolution in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes, which have been extensively studied experimentally (e.g., Bouchez 2003, Clement 2007) and theoretically using methods such as the generator coordinate method by Yao (2013) and total Routhian surface calculations by Bai (2015). The results enable future beyond-mean-field studies (e.g., with the generator coordinate method) that would include quantal fluctuations to provide more quantitative descriptions of shape coexistence in these nuclei.
