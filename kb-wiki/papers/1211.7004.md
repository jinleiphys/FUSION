# Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry and SU(3)-Flavour Partners of the X(3872)

> In this work, an Effective Field Theory (EFT) incorporating light SU(3)-flavour and heavy quark spin symmetry is used to describe charmed meson-antimeson bound states. At Lowest Order (LO), this means that only contact range interactions among the heavy meson and antimeson fields are involved. Besides, the isospin violating decays of the X(3872) will be used to constrain the interaction between the $D$ and a $\bar{D}^*$ mesons in the isovector channel. Finally, assuming that the X(3915) and Y(4140) resonances are $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ molecular states, we can determine the four Low Energy Constants (LECs) of the EFT that appear at LO and, therefore, the full spectrum of molecular states with isospin I=0, 1/2 and 1.

## Key claim

Assuming the X(3872), X(3915), and Y(4140) are all molecular states, the four leading-order low-energy constants of an effective field theory incorporating heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(3) flavour symmetry can be fixed, yielding predictions for the full spectrum of charmed meson-antimeson bound states with isospin I=0, 1/2, and 1; the predictions are subject to systematic uncertainties from the approximate nature of HQSS, estimated at about 15%.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order effective field theory with contact-range interactions between charmed meson and antimeson fields, consistent with heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(3) flavour symmetry. They fix two of the four low-energy constants by using the X(3872) binding energy and its isospin-violating decay ratio R_X (derived from the Belle measurement of the J/ψ 3π to J/ψ 2π branching ratio), and the remaining two by assuming the X(3915) and Y(4140) are 0⁺⁺ D*D̅* and D_s*D̅_s* molecules, respectively. Bound-state masses are then obtained by solving the Schrödinger/Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator at cutoffs Λ = 0.5 GeV and 1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) isospin-violating decay amplitude ratio: R_X = 0.26⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·₀₅, derived from the Belle branching ratio B_X = 0.8 ± 0.3
- Predicted mass of the 0⁺⁺ D̅D isoscalar partner: 3709 ± 10 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and 3715⁺¹²₋₁₅ MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the 1⁺⁻ D*D̅ isoscalar partner: 3815 ± 17 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and 3821⁺²³₋₂₆ MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the 1⁺⁻ D*D̅* isoscalar partner: 3955 ± 17 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and 3958⁺²⁴₋₂₇ MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the 2⁺⁺ D*D̅* isoscalar partner: 4013⁺⁰₋₉ MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and 4013⁺⁰₋₁₂ MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the 0⁺ D_s⁺D̅⁻ isospinor partner: 3835.8⁺²·³₋₃·₉ MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and 3837.7⁺⁰·⁴₋₄·₃ MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the 0⁺⁺ D_s*D̅_s* hidden strange partner (input from Y(4140)): used as input
- Estimated HQSS violation scale: Λ_QCD/m_c ≈ 200 MeV / 1.5 GeV ≈ 15%

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) analysis of Nieves (2012) by incorporating SU(3) flavour symmetry and strangeness, and uses the isospin-violating decay analysis of Hanhart (2011) to constrain the isovector interaction. The predictions enable experimental searches for 15 molecular partner states of the X(3872), X(3915), and Y(4140), with the 2⁺⁺ D*D̅* isoscalar partner identified as the most reliable prediction; the framework can accommodate future molecular candidates if the assumed identifications of X(3915) or Y(4140) prove incorrect.
