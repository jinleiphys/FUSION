# Open-charm Euclidean correlators within heavy-meson EFT interactions

> The open-charm Euclidean correlators have been computed for the first time using the thermal spectral functions extracted from a finite-temperature self-consistent unitarized approach based on a chiral effective field theory that implements heavy-quark spin symmetry. The inclusion of the full-energy dependent open-charm spectral functions in the calculation of the Euclidean correlators leads to a similar behaviour as the one obtained in lattice QCD for temperatures well below the transition deconfinement temperature. The discrepancies at temperatures close or above the transition deconfinement temperature could indicate that higher-energy states, that are not present in the open-charm spectral functions, become relevant for a quantitative description of the lattice QCD correlators at those temperatures. In fact, we find that the inclusion of a continuum of scattering states improves the comparison at small Euclidean times, whereas differences still arise for large times.

## Key claim

The Euclidean correlators for open-charm mesons computed from thermal spectral functions obtained within a finite-temperature self-consistent unitarized chiral effective field theory with heavy-quark spin symmetry agree with lattice QCD results for temperatures well below the deconfinement transition temperature, but discrepancies arise at temperatures close to or above that transition, which the authors attribute to missing higher-energy states and continuum contributions in their spectral functions.

## Method

The authors computed the thermal spectral functions of D and D_s mesons in a pionic bath using a finite-temperature self-consistent unitarized approach based on a chiral effective field theory that implements heavy-quark spin symmetry, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the imaginary time formalism. They then obtained the Euclidean correlators from these spectral functions via the integral relation G_E(tau;T) = integral domega K(tau,omega;T) rho(omega;T) and compared them to lattice QCD data from Kelly et al. (2018), using the unphysical meson masses from that lattice setup (m_pi=384 MeV, m_D=1880 MeV, m_Ds=1943 MeV). To improve agreement, they added a continuum contribution to the spectral functions using a free quark-antiquark spectral function parametrization weighted by a factor a.

## Key numbers

* The lattice setup used for comparison has a_s = 0.123 fm, a_tau^{-1} = 5.63 GeV, with anisotropy parameter xi = 3.5, and pseudocritical temperature T_c = 185 MeV.
* The unphysical meson masses used in the calculation are: m_pi = 384 MeV, m_K = 546 MeV, m_eta = 589 MeV, m_D = 1880 MeV, m_Ds = 1943 MeV.
* Correlators were computed and compared at temperatures T = (44, 141, 156, 176, 201, 235, 281, 352) MeV.
* For the continuum spectral function, the charm quark mass was taken as m_c = 1.5 GeV, the light quark mass as m_l = 0, and the strange quark mass as m_s = 100 MeV.
* The ratio of correlators at T = 141 MeV lies within the error bars of the lattice data, while for higher temperatures the calculated ratios deviate significantly.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent calculations of open-charm spectral functions in a hot pionic medium (Montana et al., 2020) by computing the corresponding Euclidean correlators for direct comparison with lattice QCD simulations. It provides the first computation of Euclidean correlators for open-charm mesons from an effective field theory approach, enabling a test of hadronic models against lattice QCD results without the ill-posed inversion problem of extracting spectral functions from lattice correlators. The comparison with lattice data from Kelly et al. (2018) shows that the effective theory works well at low temperatures but requires additional continuum and excited-state contributions for quantitative agreement near and above the deconfinement transition, pointing to the need for including deconfined degrees of freedom at those temperatures.
