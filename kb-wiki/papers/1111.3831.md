# Consistency of Lambda-Lambda hypernuclear events

> Highlights of Lambda-Lambda emulsion events are briefly reviewed. Given three accepted events, shell-model predictions based on p-shell Lambda hypernuclear spectroscopic studies are shown to reproduce the Lambda-Lambda (LL) binding energies of LL10Be and LL13B in terms of the LL binding energy of LL6He. Predictions for other species offer judgement on several alternative assignments of the LL13B KEK-E176 event, and on the assignments LL11Be and LL12Be suggested recently for the KEK-E373 HIDA event. The predictions of the shell model, spanning a wide range of A values, are compared with those of cluster models, where the latter are available.

## Key claim

Shell-model predictions, normalized to the NAGARA event value for \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}\), can reproduce the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) binding energies of \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~10}{\rm Be}\) and \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}{\rm B}\) only if the weak decays of these hypernuclei are assumed to proceed to excited states of the daughter \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei, not to their ground states.

## Method

The authors used a shell-model approach in which the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) excess binding energy \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\) is replaced by a constant matrix element identified with \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}) = 0.67 \pm 0.17\) MeV from the NAGARA event. They computed \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\) values for \(p\)-shell \(\Lambda\Lambda\) hypernuclei using known single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclear ground-state binding energies and ground-state doublet splittings from prior spectroscopic studies. These shell-model predictions were compared with experimental binding energies from emulsion events and with cluster-model calculations where available.

## Key numbers

- \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}) = 6.91 \pm 0.16\) MeV, with \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~~6}{\rm He}) = 0.67 \pm 0.17\) MeV (NAGARA event, KEK-E373).
- Reinterpreted \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~10}{\rm Be}) = 14.5 \pm 0.4\) MeV (Danysz event) or \(14.94 \pm 0.13\) MeV (Demachi-Yanagi event), compared with shell-model prediction \(14.97 \pm 0.22\) MeV.
- Reinterpreted \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~13}{\rm B}) = 23.3 \pm 0.7\) MeV, compared with shell-model prediction \(23.21 \pm 0.21\) MeV.
- For the HIDA event, \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\rm exp}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~11}{\rm Be}) = 20.83 \pm 1.27\) MeV and \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\rm exp}(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{~12}{\rm Be}) = 22.48 \pm 1.21\) MeV, both significantly higher than shell-model predictions of \(18.40 \pm 0.28\) MeV and \(20.72 \pm 0.20\) MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model analysis of Gal and Millener (2011) to reconcile the three accepted \(\Lambda\Lambda\) emulsion events (NAGARA, Danysz, and KEK-E176) under a consistent, weak \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction. It contradicts earlier interpretations that implied a much stronger \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction with \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \sim 4.5\) MeV, and it provides a framework for judging alternative assignments of the KEK-E176 and KEK-E373 HIDA events. The results enable future searches for \(A=5\) \(\Lambda\Lambda\) hypernuclei, which would further constrain the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) interaction and its coupling to the \(\Xi N\) channel.
