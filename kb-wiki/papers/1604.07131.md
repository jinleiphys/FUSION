# Isospin Projected Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics and its Application to ${}^{10}$B

> To investigate $pn$ pair correlations in $N=Z=\textrm{odd}$ nuclei, we develop a new framework based on the generator coordinate method of the $βγ$ constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. In the framework, the isospin projection is performed before the energy variation to obtain the wave function optimized for each isospin. We apply the method to ${}^{10} \textrm{B}$ and show that it works well to describe coexistence of $T=0$ and $T=1$ states in low-energy spectra. Structures of low-lying states and $pn$ correlations are investigated. Strong $M1$($0^+_1\rightarrow 1^+_1$) and $E2$($1^+_1\rightarrow 1^+_2$) transitions are understood by the spin excitation of the $pn$ pair and the rotation of a deformed core, respectively.

## Key claim

The isospin projection before energy variation (VAP^T) is essential to separately optimize wave functions for T=0 and T=1 states in N=Z=odd nuclei, because the conventional variation-before-projection method fails to obtain smooth energy surfaces for each isospin channel due to the near-degeneracy of these states.

## Method

The authors developed the Tβγ-AMD method, which performs isospin and parity projections before energy variation under constraints on quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ, then applies angular momentum projection after variation. They combined this with the generator coordinate method (GCM) by superposing 91 wave functions on the βγ plane for each isospin, and applied the framework to ¹⁰B to study pn pair correlations around a 2α core.

## Key numbers

- The energy minimum for T=1 states on the βγ plane is located at (βcosγ, βsinγ) = (0.4, 0.1) and for T=0 states at (0.38, 0)
- The energy minimum for the J^πT=0⁺1 state is at (βcosγ, βsinγ) = (0.54, 0.12) corresponding to β=0.55, γ=12.5°
- The energy minimum for the 3⁺0 ground state is at (0.52, 0.08) corresponding to β=0.53, γ=8.7°
- The calculated point-proton radius r_p(3₁⁺0) = 2.4 fm from Tβγ-AMD+GCM, compared to experimental 2.28(5) fm
- The calculated electric quadrupole moment Q(3₁⁺0) = 8.4 e·fm² from Tβγ-AMD+GCM, compared to experimental 8.47(6) e·fm²
- The calculated magnetic moment μ(3₁⁺0) = 1.8 μ_N from Tβγ-AMD+GCM, compared to experimental 1.8006 μ_N
- B(E2;1₁⁺0→1₂⁺0) = 9.2 e²fm⁴ from Tβγ-AMD+GCM, compared to experimental 15.6(17) e²fm⁴
- B(M1;0₁⁺1→1₁⁺0) = 15.0 μ_N² from Tβγ-AMD+GCM, compared to experimental 7.5(32) μ_N²
- The expectation value ⟨S²⟩ = 2.0 for T=0 states (3₁⁺0, 1₁⁺0, 1₂⁺0) indicating pure S=1 pn pair, and ⟨S²⟩ = 0.3 for the 0₁⁺1 state indicating dominant S=0 component

## Context

This work extends the βγ-constraint AMD method developed by Kimura and Suhara by adding isospin projection before variation, enabling separate treatment of T=0 and T=1 states in N=Z=odd nuclei. The method is applied to ¹⁰B, where previous AMD+VAP^J calculations by En'yo, Morita, and Kobayashi had studied pn correlations, and where no-core shell model calculations by Navratil had discussed the importance of three-nucleon forces for the 3⁺ ground state. The framework enables future studies of pn pair correlations in heavier N=Z odd nuclei and investigation of how cluster structure affects pn pair dynamics.
