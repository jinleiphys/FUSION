# Doubly heavy baryon spectra guided by lattice QCD

> This paper provides results for the ground state and excited spectra of three-flavored doubly heavy baryons, $bcn$ and $bcs$. We take advantage of the spin-independent interaction recently obtained to reconcile the lattice SU(3) QCD static potential and the results of nonperturbative lattice QCD for the triply heavy baryon spectra. We show that the spin-dependent potential might be constrained on the basis of nonperturbative lattice QCD results for the spin splittings of three-flavored doubly heavy baryons. Our results may also represent a challenge for future lattice QCD work, because a smaller lattice error could help in distinguishing between different prescriptions for the spin-dependent part of the interaction. Thus, by comparing with the reported baryon spectra obtained with parameters estimated from lattice QCD, one can challenge the precision of lattice calculations. The present work supports a coherent description of singly, doubly and triply heavy baryons with the same Cornell-like interacting potential. The possible experimental measurement of these states at LHCb is an incentive for this study.

# Doubly heavy baryon spectra guided by lattice QCD

## Key claim

The spin-independent part of the quark-quark interaction, constrained by triply heavy baryon spectra from lattice QCD, can be combined with a flavor-dependent spin-spin regularization to predict the spectra of three-flavored doubly heavy baryons (bcn and bcs). However, the spin-dependent potential cannot be uniquely determined from current lattice data because the predicted spin splittings (7 and 12 MeV) fall within 2 sigma of the lattice central values (35±9±25 and 62±9±25 MeV), meaning smaller lattice errors are needed to distinguish between different prescriptions.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic three-body Schrodinger equation using Faddeev equations for three non-identical particles, expanding two-body t-matrices in Legendre polynomials. The quark-quark interaction was a Cornell-like potential (Coulomb plus linear confining) with a screened long-range term and a regularized spin-spin term, where the spin-independent parameters were fixed from prior fits to lattice QCD results for bbb and ccc baryons, and the spin-dependent regularization was taken from either the AL1 model (Silvestre-Brac, 1996) or a flavor-dependent prescription (Valcarce et al., 2008).

## Key numbers

- Coulomb strength A = 0.1875, confining strength B = 0.1374 GeV^2, used for all baryon calculations
- Quark masses: m_b = 5.034 GeV, m_c = 1.659 GeV, m_s = 0.545 GeV, m_u = m_d = 0.313 GeV
- Long-distance screening parameter mu = 0.7 fm^{-1}
- Predicted spin splitting E_{Omega*_bc} - E_{Omega_bc} = 12 MeV using the AL1 regularization (Silvestre-Brac, 1996), compared to lattice QCD result 62 ± 9 ± 25 MeV (Brown et al., 2014)
- Predicted spin splitting E_{Omega'_bc} - E_{Omega_bc} = 7 MeV using the AL1 regularization, compared to lattice QCD result 35 ± 9 ± 25 MeV (Brown et al., 2014)
- Using the flavor-dependent regularization (Valcarce et al., 2008), the spin splitting E_{Omega*_bc} - E_{Omega_bc} becomes 63 MeV, close to the lattice central value
- Convergence of binding energy for bcs 3/2^+ state: E = 995 MeV with 3 Faddeev amplitudes, stable to 995 MeV with 6 amplitudes

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies (Vijande et al., 2014; Vijande et al., 2015) that showed the static three-quark potential from SU(3) lattice QCD does not reproduce triply heavy baryon spectra, and that a Cornell-like potential with larger Coulomb strength than predicted by SU(3) lattice QCD is needed. The present calculation provides parameter-free predictions for bcn and bcs baryons by combining the spin-independent interaction from triply heavy baryons with the spin-dependent regularization from singly heavy baryons (Valcarce et al., 2008). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to distinguish between spin-dependent interaction prescriptions and can be tested against experimental searches at LHCb, where B_c meson production has already been observed (Aaij et al., 2014).
