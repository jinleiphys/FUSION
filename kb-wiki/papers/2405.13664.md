# Isovector giant dipole resonance mode with an improved propagation approach in the framework of EQMD model

> The Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) model is one of the few QMD-like transport approaches that can describe the $α$-clustering structure with efficient computational power. However, compared to most QMD-like models, the choice of equation of state (EOS) for nuclear matter is very limited. In this work, a Monte Carlo integral method is employed to calculate the density integration with non-integer exponent. We demonstrate the superiority of our approach by studying the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR). This improvement will be beneficial for the EQMD model to study more valuable effects for heavy ion collisions in the near future.

# Isovector giant dipole resonance mode with an improved propagation approach in the framework of EQMD model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Monte Carlo integral method for calculating the density integration with non-integer exponent allows the EQMD model to use equations of state with softer incompressibility (γ between 1 and 2) without the system divergence or initialization failure that occurs with the standard approximate method, though the peak energies of the isovector giant dipole resonance still show systematic deviations from experimental data depending on the parameter set used.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo integration method (Method II) to compute the three-body Skyrme interaction term in the EQMD model, replacing the standard approximate summation over pairwise densities (Method I) that causes the real part of the complex wave packet width to become negative during propagation. They test this approach by simulating the isovector giant dipole resonance in various nuclei using a linear response perturbation method, applying a dipole boost operator and Fourier-transforming the resulting dipole moment time evolution to obtain the strength function. Binary nucleon-nucleon collisions are omitted to isolate the effects of the mean-field treatment.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility K∞ favored by current understanding is 230 ± 40 MeV, corresponding to γ values between 1 and 2 rather than the original EQMD value of γ = 2.0
- For 208Pb, the experimental GDR resonance peak from (p,p') reactions at RCNP is 13.4 MeV
- The phenomenological GDR energy formula used for comparison is 31.2A^{-1/3} + 20.6A^{-1/6} MeV
- With 900 Monte Carlo samples, the radial density of 208Pb remains stable over 1000 fm/c, while 90 samples shows clear instability
- The Pauli potential contributes approximately 4 MeV per nucleon, compared to total interaction energy of about 26 MeV/u and kinetic energy of about 15 MeV/u for 208Pb with SLy6 settings
- The SkP parameter set uses γ = 7/6, β = 303.0 MeV, and yields K∞ = 200 MeV
- The SLy4, SLy6, and SLy7 parameter sets all use γ = 7/6 and yield K∞ = 230, 230, and 229 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the EQMD model, originally developed by Maruyama et al., which previously could only use stiff equations of state with γ = 2 (corresponding to incompressibilities around 240-250 MeV), by enabling the use of softer EOS parameter sets such as SkP, SkM*, RATP, SLy4, SLy6, and SLy7 that are widely used in other QMD-like models including ImQMD, LQMD, and IQMD-BNU. The improvement allows the EQMD model to study isovector giant dipole resonances with more realistic nuclear matter incompressibilities, and the authors note that a similar Monte Carlo approach for the three-body interaction was first reported by Yang et al. Future work is needed to address the systematic underestimation of GDR peak energies in light nuclei and to incorporate the symmetry energy dependence more carefully.
