# Transport in strongly coupled quark matter

> Motivated by the possible presence of deconfined quark matter in neutron stars and their mergers and the important role of transport phenomena in these systems, we perform the first-ever systematic study of different viscosities and conductivities of dense quark matter using the gauge/gravity duality. Utilizing the V-QCD model, we arrive at results that are in qualitative disagreement with the predictions of perturbation theory, which highlights the differing transport properties of the system at weak and strong coupling and calls for caution in the use of the perturbative results in neutron-star applications.

## Key claim

Using the V-QCD holographic model, the authors find that transport coefficients of dense quark matter at strong coupling show qualitative disagreement with perturbative QCD predictions, particularly in their temperature dependence, which calls for caution when applying perturbative results to neutron-star phenomenology.

## Method

The authors compute shear and bulk viscosities, electrical conductivity, and thermal conductivity of dense unpaired quark matter using the V-QCD bottom-up holographic model, which incorporates unquenched flavor effects through a Dirac-Born-Infeld action. They compare these results to predictions from the D3-D7 probe brane model (where flavors are quenched) and to leading-order perturbative QCD calculations from Heiselberg (1993). The transport coefficients are evaluated at chemical potentials of 450 MeV and 600 MeV over a range of temperatures relevant for neutron star cores.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio saturates the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound at eta/s = 1/(4 pi) for both holographic models
* In V-QCD, shear viscosity at low temperatures approaches a constant, crossing the perturbative QCD result around T ~ 5-50 MeV
* Bulk viscosity in V-QCD reaches about 10% of the shear viscosity at high temperatures
* Electrical conductivity in V-QCD increases with temperature, while perturbative QCD predicts a decreasing trend
* Perturbative QCD shear viscosity formula: eta approximately 4.4 x 10^{-3} mu^2 m_D^{2/3} / (alpha_s^2 T^{5/3})
* Perturbative QCD electrical conductivity: sigma^{xx} approximately 0.01 mu^2 m_D^{2/3} / (alpha_s T^{5/3})
* Perturbative QCD thermal conductivity: kappa^{xx} approximately 0.5 m_D^2 / alpha_s^2
* QCD scale Lambda_QCD set to 378 MeV, obtained by demanding alpha_s(bar-Lambda = 2 GeV) = 0.2994
* V-QCD fits use Lambda_UV values of 226.24, 210.76, and 156.68 MeV for fits 5b, 7a, and 8b respectively
* D3-D7 model uses quark masses M_q = 210.76 MeV and 308.55 MeV

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach previously applied to neutron star equations of state by Hoyos (2016), Jokela (2018), and Chesler (2019) to the more challenging domain of transport coefficients. The results contradict the leading-order perturbative QCD calculation by Heiselberg (1993), which has been the only first-principles determination of these quantities in unpaired quark matter. The findings enable more reliable input for relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of neutron star mergers and for studies of r-mode damping in rapidly rotating stars, while highlighting the need for further development of holographic methods for dense matter applications.
