# Near-threshold production of a0(980) mesons in the reaction pp -> d K^+ \bar{K}^0

> Using an effective Lagrangian approach as well as the Quark-Gluon Strings Model we analyze near-threshold production of a0(980)-mesons in the reaction NN -> d K \bar{K} as well as the background of non-resonant K\bar{K}-pair production. We argue that the reaction pp -> d K^+ \bar{K}^0 at an energy release Q<=100 MeV is dominated by the intermediate production of the a0(980)-resonance. At larger energies the non-resonant K^+\bar{K}^0-pair production - where the kaons are produced in a relative P-wave - becomes important. Then effects of final-state interactions are evaluated in a unitarized scattering-length approach and found to be in the order of a 20% suppression close to threshold. Thus in present experiments at the Cooler Synchrotron COSY-Jülich for Q<=107 MeV the a_0^+ signal can reliably be separated from the non-resonant K^+\bar{K^0} background.

## Key claim

The reaction \(pp \rightarrow d K^+ \bar{K}^0\) at an energy release \(Q \leq 100\) MeV is dominated by the intermediate production of the \(a_0(980)\) resonance, but at larger energies the non-resonant \(K^+\bar{K}^0\)-pair production (where the kaons are produced in a relative P-wave) becomes important; the authors argue that in present experiments at COSY-Juelich for \(Q \leq 107\) MeV the \(a_0^+\) signal can reliably be separated from the non-resonant background.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach (two-step model with one-pion exchange) and the Quark-Gluon Strings Model (QGSM) to analyze resonant \(a_0(980)\) production and non-resonant \(K\bar{K}\) background in the reaction \(pp \rightarrow d K^+ \bar{K}^0\). They evaluated contributions from u-channel and s-channel nucleon exchanges, eta and f1(1285) meson exchanges, b1 and rho2 Reggeon exchanges for the resonant part, and pi-K*-pi(eta) and K-exchange mechanisms for the non-resonant background. Final-state interactions were evaluated in a unitarized scattering-length approach.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section of \(pp \rightarrow d a_0^+\) at \(p_{\mathrm{lab}}=3.46\) GeV (\(Q=46\) MeV) in the narrow \(a_0\) width limit is about 1.2 \(\mu\)b.
* After convolution with the Flatte mass distribution, \(\sigma(pp \rightarrow d a_0^+ \rightarrow K^+ \bar{K}^0)\) is about 28 nb at \(Q=46\) MeV.
* The effective branching ratio for the \(a_0\) decay to the \(K \bar K\) mode is 0.023 at \(Q=46\) MeV.
* The non-resonant P-wave \(K \bar{K}\) background contribution was normalized to 6.5 nb at \(Q=46\) MeV.
* The cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_{K^{\star}}(K^{\star} \pi K)=1.25\) GeV was used to describe the non-resonant background.
* The \(a_0\) resonance mass parameter from the Flatte distribution is \(m_0=999\) MeV, with \(g_{\pi \eta}=324\) MeV and \(g^2_{K \bar K}/g^2_{\pi \eta}=1.03\).
* Final-state interactions cause a suppression of about 20% close to threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of \(a_0\) production in \(\pi N \rightarrow a_0 N\) and \(NN \rightarrow d a_0\) reactions (Grishina et al., 2001) and provides theoretical support for the experimental program at COSY-Juelich using the ANKE spectrometer (Buescher et al., 1999-2003). The study addresses the ongoing debate about the structure of light scalar mesons \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\), which have been described as conventional \(q\bar{q}\) states (Morgan, 1974; Anisovich, 1995), \(K\bar{K}\) molecules (Weinstein, 1990; Oller, 1998), or four-quark cryptoexotic states (Jaffe, 1977; Achasov, 1982). The results enable separation of the \(a_0^+\) signal from non-resonant background in ongoing and future COSY experiments.
