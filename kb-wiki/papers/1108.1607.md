# Exact Eigenfunctions of $N$-Body system with Quadratic Pair Potential

> We obtain all the exact eigenvalues and the corresponding eigenfunctions of $N$-body Bose and Fermi systems with Quadratic Pair Potentials in one dimension. The originally existed first excited state level is missing in one dimension, which results from the operation of symmetry or antisymmetry of identical particles. In two and higher dimensions, we give all the eigenvalues and the analytical ground state wave functions and the number of degeneracy. Through the comparison with Avinash Khare's results, we have perfected his results.

## Key claim

The paper claims to obtain all exact eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for N-body Bose and Fermi systems with quadratic pair potentials in one dimension, and to provide the ground state energy and wave function in higher dimensions. However, the authors qualify that their ansatz for constructing all states, changing only the last quantum number when the first N-1 particles are in their ground state, is "just one way to obtain all the states" and that its correctness is verified by substitution into the Hamiltonian, not by a rigorous proof of completeness.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the N-body Hamiltonian with quadratic pair potentials by transforming to Jacobi coordinates, which decouples the center-of-mass motion from N-1 independent harmonic oscillators with frequency √N ω. For identical particles, they construct symmetrized (Bose) or antisymmetrized (Fermi) wave functions as sums over permutations of products of single-oscillator eigenfunctions in the Jacobi coordinates. For the Fermi system in one dimension, they use an ansatz where all states are obtained by varying only the last quantum number n_{N-1} while keeping the first N-2 quantum numbers fixed at their ground-state values, and they verify the resulting wave functions using a hyper-Vandermonde determinant identity. For D-dimensional Fermi systems, they construct the ground state as a Slater determinant of monomials in the Cartesian coordinates multiplied by a Gaussian factor.

## Key numbers

- One-dimensional Bose ground state energy: E_0^B = (N-1)/2 √N ħ ω.
- One-dimensional Bose excited state energies: E_k^B = E_0^B + (k+1) √N ħ ω for k ≠ 0.
- One-dimensional Fermi ground state energy: E_0 = (1/2)(N² - 1) √N ħ ω.
- One-dimensional Fermi excited state energies: E_i = E_0 + (i+1) ħ √N ω for i > 0.
- D-dimensional Fermi ground state energy: E_{0,f}^{(D)} = ħ ω √N [ (K + D/2)N - (1/(D+1)!) ∏_{i=0}^D (K+i) - D/2 ], where K satisfies (1/D!) ∏_{i=0}^{D-1} (K+i) ≤ N-1 < (1/D!) ∏_{i=1}^D (K+i).
- Asymptotic D-dimensional Fermi ground state energy for large N: E_{0,f}^{(D)} → ħ ω √N { (D N)/(D+1) [D! (N-1)]^{1/D} + O(N) }.
- Ground state degeneracy in D dimensions: binomial coefficient ( (1/(D-1)!) ∏_{i=1}^{D-1} (K+i) choose N - (1/D!) ∏_{i=0}^{D-1} (K+i) ).
- Normalization constant for one-dimensional N-particle Fermi ground state: C_0 = [ (m ω/ħ)^{(N²-1)/2} π^{(1-N)/2} N^{(N²-3)/4} ∏_{i=1}^N (2^{i-1}/i!) ]^{1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the exact solution of the N-body problem with quadratic pair potentials, previously studied by Post (who obtained the ground state energy), Levy-Leblond (who obtained the one-dimensional energy spectrum and three-dimensional ground state energy), and Ma (who noted state disappearance for three particles). The authors claim to perfect the results of Khare by providing the exact expressions for the ground state wave function factor ψ_S and the ground state energy E_0^f of the Calogero model, which Khare had given only partially. The results enable computation of correlation functions for this exactly solvable model and open the question of angular excitations of the Calogero model for future work.
