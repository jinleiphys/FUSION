# Coulomb Corrections for Coherent Neutrino Nucleus Scattering

> In this work we consider sub-leading $O(Z^2α^3)$ corrections to coherent elastic neutrino nucleus scattering (CEvNS). These corrections are not negligible by power counting since nuclei with large coherent cross sections have sizeable nuclear charges e.g.\ $Zα\sim 0.4$. We find that the corrections are much smaller than naive power counting in $Zα$ would suggest and that Coulomb corrections do not substantially alter predictions for CEvNS in the Standard Model. We comment on similarities to older literature on mesonic and muonic atoms.

# Coulomb Corrections for Coherent Neutrino Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim
The O(Z²α³) Coulomb corrections to coherent elastic neutrino nucleus scattering (CEvNS) are much smaller than naive power counting in Zα would suggest, and do not substantially alter Standard Model predictions for CEvNS, despite the fact that nuclei with large coherent cross sections have Zα ~ 0.4 where such corrections might be expected to be significant.

## Method
The author computed the Wichmann-Kroll vacuum polarization contribution to CEvNS using known integral representations from the muonic atom literature, working in the point-like nucleus limit. The correction was evaluated numerically for the lepton-flavor asymmetry observable, which is scheme-independent and free of residual scale dependence. Finite size effects and hadronic contributions were estimated by dimensional analysis and comparison to existing literature on mesonic and muonic atoms.

## Key numbers
- The flavor asymmetry coefficient A_e,mu^(0) (leading order) is 3.36 at Q = 1 MeV, while the Coulomb correction coefficient A_e,mu^(1) is only 0.02 at the same energy
- The flavor asymmetry coefficient A_e,tau^(0) is 5.24 at Q = 1 MeV, while A_e,tau^(1) is 0.02
- At Q = 10 MeV, A_e,mu^(0) = 2.12 and A_e,mu^(1) = 0.03; at Q = 30 MeV, A_e,mu^(0) = 1.40 and A_e,mu^(1) = 0.03
- The high-Q limit of the Wichmann-Kroll vacuum polarization is approximately 0.03, given by (1/2)[pi²/6 - (2/3)zeta(3) - 7/9]
- Hadronic loop contributions are suppressed by Q²/m_pi², which is typically less than 0.1 for CEvNS kinematics

## Context
This work extends the radiative correction study of Tomalak (2020) by computing the only relevant correction not previously considered: Coulomb corrections from charged lepton loops. The author draws on older literature on mesonic and muonic atoms, particularly the Wichmann-Kroll potential from Wichmann and Kroll (1956) and the all-orders analysis of Brown (1974). The results demonstrate that Coulomb corrections are under control for a precision CEvNS program, complementing recent work on inelastic invisible contributions by Dutta (2022) and percent-level neutrino flux determinations by the COHERENT collaboration. Further progress toward sub-percent predictions requires work on hadronic contributions via the mixed correlator and reduced uncertainties in nuclear neutron and proton distributions.
