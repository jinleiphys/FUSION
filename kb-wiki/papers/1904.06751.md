# Diabatic scission paths

> An outstanding problem in the theory of nuclear fission is to understand the Hamiltonian dynamics at the scission point. In this work the fissioning nucleus is modeled in self-consistent mean-field theory as a set of Generator Coordinate (GCM) configurations passing through the scission point. In contrast to previous methods, the configurations are constructed in the Hartree-Fock approximation with axially symmetric mean fields and using the K-partition numbers as additional constraints. The goal of this work is to find paths through the scission point where the overlaps between neighboring configurations are large. A measure of distance along the path is proposed that is insensitive to the division of the path into short segments. For most of the tested K-partitions two shape degrees of freedom are adequate to define smooth paths. However, some of the configurations and candidate paths have sticking points where there are substantial changes in the many-body wave function, especially if quasiparticle excitations are present. The excitation energy deposited in fission fragments arising from thermal excitations in the pre-scission configurations is determined by tracking orbital occupation numbers along the scission paths. This allows us to assess the validity of the well-known scission-point statistical model, in which the scission process is assumed to be fully equilibrated up to the separated fission fragments. The nucleus 236U is taken as a representative example in the calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constructing continuous diabatic paths through the nuclear scission point is possible using Hartree-Fock configurations with K-partition constraints, but contrary to the authors' initial hope, a single shape constraint (quadrupole moment) is typically insufficient; two shape degrees of freedom are usually adequate, though some configurations (particularly partition A with the D1S functional) still exhibit sticking points where the many-body wave function changes substantially.

## Method

The authors model the fissioning nucleus 236U using self-consistent mean-field theory with axially symmetric Hartree-Fock (HF) configurations, constraining the K-partition (the set of occupied orbital angular momentum projections about the fission axis) to enforce diabatic dynamics. They construct Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) paths through the scission point by adding a neck-size constraint (N_neck) as a second generator coordinate alongside the quadrupole moment Q_2, then compute overlaps between neighboring configurations and a distance measure ζ that is insensitive to the division of the path into segments. The excitation energy deposited in fission fragments is calculated by tracking orbital occupation numbers along the scission paths using a grand canonical ensemble, comparing pre-scission and post-scission single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- The overlap between the pre-scission configuration |166⟩ and post-scission configuration |168p⟩ for Glider is 0.29, while the intermediate configuration |167m⟩ has overlaps of 0.79 with |166⟩ and 0.67 with |168p⟩.
- The distance measure ζ along the Glider path is 1.13, 1.12, and 1.13 with 0, 1, and 3 intermediate configurations, demonstrating insensitivity to step size.
- For Glider with the D1S functional, the neck parameter N_neck jumps from approximately 1.7 to 0.6 at the scission point, with two local minima at Q_2 = 168 b and 170 b.
- The excitation energy fraction in the heavy fragment f_H for Glider ranges from 0.46 to 0.57 across different ensembles at starting energies of 10 and 20 MeV.
- For partition A with D1S, the coexistence of two configurations extends over a much larger Q_2 range (143 to 171 b) than for other cases, and the final diabatic excitation energy is 18.9 MeV (nearly double the initial 10 MeV).
- The effective temperature T for D1S is approximately 0.85-0.9 MeV, while for BCPM it is approximately 0.70-0.76 MeV, with the ratio of squares (0.7) close to the ratio of effective masses (m*_D1S/m*_BCPM = 0.67).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exploration of diabatic configurations in 236U (Bertsch, Younes, Robledo 2018) and addresses the long-standing difficulty of understanding Hamiltonian dynamics at the scission point in self-consistent mean-field theory. The approach contrasts with previous GCM methods that break down at scission due to abrupt configuration changes (Dubray et al. 2012), and it enables assessment of the scission-point statistical model (Wilkins et al. 1976, Lemaitre et al. 2015, Al-Adili et al. 2018) which assumes full equilibration up to separated fragments. The results suggest that while most K-partitions evolve nearly adiabatically (contradicting the expectation that diabatic transformations increase internal energy), some partitions like A show explicit excitation energy injection, and the computed energy sharing fractions (f_H ≈ 0.48 ± 0.07 for normal cases) are broadly consistent with the statistical model's predictions (f_H ≈ 0.36 at E* = 10 MeV for certain mass splits).
