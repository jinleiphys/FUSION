# Collective treatment of the isovector pair correlations. Boson representation

> The theoretical approach to consideration of the Hamiltonian with pairing forces using a technique of the finite boson representation is developed. It is shown that a simultaneous description of the pairing vibrational state in $^{56}$Ni and the pairing rotational states with $T$=0 in the neighboring $N=Z$ nuclei is possible if the pairing Hamiltonian takes into account only isovector monopole pairing. However, the calculated energies of the pairing rotational states of $N=Z$ nuclei removed from $^{56}$Ni by 12 and more nucleons exceed significantly the experimental values. The possible reason of this discrepancy is discussed.

## Key claim

A Hamiltonian that includes only isovector monopole pairing can simultaneously describe the pairing vibrational state in $^{56}$Ni and the pairing rotational states with $T=0$ in neighboring $N=Z$ nuclei, but the calculated energies of pairing rotational states for nuclei removed from $^{56}$Ni by 12 or more nucleons significantly exceed the experimental values, suggesting that other pairing modes or structural effects may become important.

## Method

The authors developed a theoretical approach using a finite Dyson-type boson representation of bifermion operators to treat the pairing Hamiltonian with constant pairing forces. They restricted the Hamiltonian to isovector monopole pairing (T=1, J=0), applied an RPA diagonalization to separate the collective isovector monopole mode, and constructed a collective Hamiltonian in terms of collective coordinates and conjugate momenta. The resulting Schrodinger equation was solved for T=0 states in nuclei around $^{56}$Ni, with the pairing strength $G_1$ fixed to reproduce the experimental excitation energy of the pairing vibrational state in $^{56}$Ni.

## Key numbers

- The pairing vibrational $0^+$ state in $^{56}$Ni has excitation energy $E^* = 5.004$ MeV (experimental value used to fix $G_1$).
- The fitted pairing strength constant is $G_1 = 14.4/A$ MeV, based on the commonly accepted nucleon number dependence.
- The parameter $d$, characterizing the ratio of pairing strength to the single-particle shell gap at nucleon number 28, is set to $d = 0.6$ using the Woods-Saxon potential.
- The calculated energy of the first excited state of the pairing rotational band based on $^{56}$Ni (the ground state of $^{60}$Zn) is 2.22 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.39 MeV.
- The critical value of $d$ at which the minimum of the collective potential shifts from zero to nonzero $\Delta$ is $d_{crit} = 0.73$.
- For nuclei around $^{28}$Si, the excited pairing vibrational state in $^{32}_{16}$S is known at excitation energy 5.14 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the collective model of pairing correlations developed by Bes, Dussel, and Jolos (1970s) and applies the finite boson representation technique to treat isovector monopole pairing in $N \approx Z$ nuclei. The approach enables a simultaneous description of pairing rotational and pairing vibrational states, which previous work by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) discussed in the context of long rotational bands. The results suggest that for nuclei far from the double magic $^{56}$Ni (beyond $N=6$), the simple isovector monopole pairing picture breaks down, and the authors discuss whether such states should instead be considered as belonging to pairing rotational bands based on other closed-shell nuclei like $^{28}$Si or $^{80}$Zr.
