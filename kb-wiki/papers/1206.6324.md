# Incoherent Neutrinoproduction of Photons and Pions in a Chiral Effective Field Theory for Nuclei

> We study the incoherent neutrinoproduction of photons and pions with neutrino energy E_ν $\leqslant$ 0.5 GeV. These processes are relevant to the background analysis in neutrino-oscillation experiments, for example MiniBooNE [A. A. Aquilar-Arevalo \textit{et al.} (MiniBooNE Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. 100, 032301(2008)]. The calculations are carried out using a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory (EFT) which contains nucleons, pions, the Delta (1232) ($Δ$ s), isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) fields, and isovector vector ($ρ$) fields, and has SU(2)_{L} $\otimes$ SU(2)_{R} chiral symmetry realized nonlinearly. The contributions of one-body currents are studied in the local fermi gas approximation. The current form factors are generated by meson dominance in the EFT Lagrangian. The conservation of the vector current and the partial conservation of the axial current are satisfied automatically, which is crucial for photon production. The $Δ$ dynamics in nuclei, as a key component in the study, are explored. Introduced $Δ$-meson couplings explain the $Δ$ spin-orbit (S-L) coupling in nuclei, and this leads to interesting constraints on the theory. Meanwhile a phenomenological approach is applied to parametrize the $Δ$ width. To benchmark our approximations, we calculate the differential cross sections for quasi-elastic scattering and incoherent electroproduction of pions without a final state interaction (FSI). The FSI can be ignored for photon production.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incoherent neutrinoproduction of photons and pions from nuclei at neutrino energies up to 0.5 GeV can be described using a Lorentz-covariant chiral effective field theory with nucleons, pions, Delta(1232) resonances, and meson fields, but the calculations are limited to one-body currents in the local Fermi gas approximation without final-state interactions for pions, and the Delta dynamics in the nuclear medium require phenomenological parametrization of the width and spin-orbit coupling.

## Method

The authors used a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory (quantum hadrodynamics) with SU(2)L x SU(2)R chiral symmetry, containing nucleons, pions, Delta(1232), sigma, omega, and rho fields, to calculate cross sections for incoherent neutrino-induced photon and pion production from carbon-12 nuclei. They employed the local Fermi gas approximation for the nuclear ground state, generated current form factors via meson dominance, and introduced Delta-meson couplings to generate the Delta spin-orbit coupling in nuclei, while parametrizing the Delta width phenomenologically with a density-dependent spreading potential.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range: E_nu <= 0.5 GeV
- For electron scattering benchmark at Ei=0.62 GeV and theta=60 degrees: Q^2 ≈ 0.3 GeV^2 and |q| ≈ 0.55 GeV at the quasi-elastic peak
- Delta spreading potential: V0 ≈ 80 MeV for the phenomenological width parametrization
- Constant Delta width shift used for comparison: delta_Gamma ≈ 40 MeV, giving Gamma_Delta → 120 + 40 MeV
- For carbon-12: nucleon effective mass M* = 0.75 M, g_s<phi> ≈ 0.235 GeV, g_v<V^0> ≈ 0.75 g_s<phi> in the global Fermi gas estimate
- Delta effective mass: m* = m - r_s g_s<phi> ≈ 0.995 GeV (for r_s=1)
- For Ei=0.62 GeV and theta=60 degrees: q^0 = 0.43 GeV (r_v=1) or q^0 = 0.39 GeV (r_v=0.8) at the Delta peak location
- Estimated Delta width components: 0.04 <= Gamma_sp <= 0.08 GeV and 0.08 <= Gamma_pi <= 0.12 GeV averaged over carbon-12

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutrino reactions on free nucleons (Zhang and Serot, 2012) to nuclear targets, specifically carbon-12, using the quantum hadrodynamics effective field theory framework developed by Serot and Walecka (1986, 1997) and Furnstahl et al. (1997-2000). The calculations are motivated by the MiniBooNE experiment (Aquilar-Arevalo et al., 2008) where neutral-current photon and pion production are potential backgrounds for neutrino oscillation measurements. The approach contrasts with nonrelativistic treatments of Delta dynamics (Oset et al., 1987) and earlier relativistic work (Wehrberger et al., 1989-1993) by incorporating chiral symmetry, meson dominance for form factors, and explicit Delta-meson couplings to generate spin-orbit coupling. The results enable comparison with event generators like NUANCE and provide a framework for future inclusion of two-body currents and final-state interactions.
