# Charge radii of $^{55,56}$Ni reveal a surprisingly similar behavior at $N=28$ in Ca and Ni isotopes

> Nuclear charge radii of $^{55,56}$Ni were measured by collinear laser spectroscopy. The obtained information completes the behavior of the charge radii at the shell closure of the doubly magic nucleus $^{56}$Ni. The trend of charge radii across the shell closures in calcium and nickel is surprisingly similar despite the fact that the $^{56}$Ni core is supposed to be much softer than the $^{48}$Ca core. The very low magnetic moment $μ(^{55}\mathrm{Ni})=-1.108(20)\,μ_N$ indicates the impact of M1 excitations between spin-orbit partners across the $N,Z=28$ shell gaps. Our charge-radii results are compared to \textit{ab initio} and nuclear density functional theory calculations, showing good agreement within theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim
The charge radii of nickel isotopes across the N=28 shell closure show a surprisingly similar kink strength to those of calcium isotopes, despite the 56Ni core being much softer than the 48Ca core, suggesting that the kink in charge radii does not directly reflect the strength of a shell closure.

## Method
Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at the BECOLA facility at NSCL/MSU on 54,55,56Ni ions produced by fragmentation of a 160 MeV/u 58Ni beam on a Be target, separated in the A1900 fragment separator, and thermalized in a gas stopper. The isotope shifts were measured relative to 60Ni and converted to differential mean-square charge radii using mass-shift and field-shift factors determined from a King plot analysis of stable nickel isotopes. The magnetic moment of 55Ni was extracted from hyperfine structure A-factors.

## Key numbers
- Charge radius of 56Ni: 3.7226(3)(27) fm
- Charge radius of 55Ni: 3.7252(32)(21) fm
- Charge radius of 54Ni: 3.7366(13)(31) fm
- Charge radius of 58Ni: 3.7695(2)(19) fm
- Magnetic moment of 55Ni: -1.108(20) mu_N, which is 55% of the single-particle nu f7/2 value
- The three-point indicator Delta^(3)_2n R_c at N=28 is approximately 0.030 fm in both the Ca and Ni isotopic chains

## Context
This work completes the charge radii trend across the N=28 shell closure for nickel isotopes, enabling direct comparison with the calcium chain where data already existed for K, Ca, Mn, and Fe isotopes (Koszorus 2021, Garcia Ruiz 2016, Minamisono 2016). The results challenge the previous understanding that the kink strength in charge radii reflects shell closure strength, as 48Ca has a strong N=28 shell closure with B(E2)=1.7 W.u. while 56Ni has a soft core with B(E2)=7.1 W.u. (Pritychenko 2016). The measured magnetic moment corrects a previous beta-NMR measurement by Berryman (2009) and agrees with shell-model calculations using the GXPF1 interaction.
