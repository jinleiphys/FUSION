# Neutrino and axion emissivities of neutron stars from nucleon-nucleon scattering data

> Neutrino and axion production in neutron stars occurs mainly as bremsstrahlung from nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering. The energy radiated via neutrinos or axions is typically very small compared to other scales in the two-nucleon system. The rate of emission of such "soft" radiation is directly related to the on-shell NN amplitude, and thereby to the NN experimental data. This facilitates the model-independent calculation of the neutrino and axion radiation rates which is presented here. We find that the resultant rates are a factor of four below earlier estimates based on a one-pion-exchange NN amplitude.

## Key claim

The neutrino and axion emissivities from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung in neutron stars can be computed in a model-independent way using the soft-neutrino approximation (SNA), which relates the emission rate directly to the on-shell NN scattering amplitude derived from experimental data; the resulting rates are roughly a factor of four smaller than earlier estimates based on the one-pion-exchange (OPE) approximation, but the calculation is valid only when the radiated energy is small compared to the pion mass and when many-body effects such as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect are negligible.

## Method

The authors relate the matrix element for NN → NN νν̅ to the commutator of the on-shell NN T-matrix (constructed from experimental phase shifts) with the spin operator, keeping only the leading 1/ω term in the soft-neutrino approximation. They then compute the dynamical spin structure function Sσ(ω) by numerically integrating the phase-space integrals for a neutron gas at various densities and temperatures, using Fermi-Dirac distributions for the nucleons. The neutrino emissivity is obtained from Sσ(ω) via an ω⁶-weighted integral, and the axion emissivity follows from the same Sσ(ω) via an ω⁴-weighted integral with the axion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R_{ℰ_ν} = ℰ_{νν̅}^{SNA} / ℰ_{νν̅}^{OPE} for nn pairs at density 0.16 fm⁻³ is 0.24 at T = 1 MeV and 0.23 at T = 10 MeV.
- At density 0.08 fm⁻³, R_{ℰ_ν} for nn pairs is 0.29 at T = 1 MeV and 0.27 at T = 10 MeV.
- At density 0.48 fm⁻³, R_{ℰ_ν} for nn pairs is 0.16 at both T = 1 MeV and T = 10 MeV.
- The soft-neutrino approximation is expected to be valid for ω < m_π (≈ 140 MeV) and for temperatures T ≲ 10 MeV at nuclear saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³).
- The effective axion-nucleon coupling is g_{ann} = 10⁻⁸ (m_a / 1 eV), where m_a is the axion mass.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculation of Friman and Maxwell (1979), who computed neutrino bremsstrahlung rates using only the one-pion-exchange NN amplitude, by replacing that amplitude with the full on-shell NN T-matrix from experimental data. The results provide a model-independent benchmark for neutrino and axion emissivities that can serve as a foundation for future work incorporating many-body effects such as the LPM suppression (studied by Raffelt and Seckel, 1991; and others) and in-medium pion modifications; the reduced axion emissivity will weaken the axion-mass bound obtained from SN1987A by Keil et al. (1997) and Burrows et al. (1989).
