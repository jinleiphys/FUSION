# Chiral symmetry breaking and inhomogeneous phases in thermal anti-de Sitter spacetime

> We study the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in an AdS spacetime at finite temperature using the quark-meson model. The condensate $σ$ is typically inhomogeneous in AdS and is determined from the differential gap equation. We demonstrate that there are no free integration constants in the regular solutions to the differential equation and find that the solution to the boundary value problem is unique. We find that chiral symmetry is always broken close to the AdS boundary. We construct the phase diagram of the system as a function of the AdS curvature and temperature. These two parameters have opposing effects: temperature tends to restore chiral symmetry, whereas negative curvature favors its spontaneous breaking. We also consider how the phase diagram is modified when the Hawking-Page phase transition is taken into account.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry is always broken close to the AdS boundary regardless of temperature, and the system's phase is determined by the value of the condensate at the center of AdS; the authors demonstrate that the solution to the differential gap equation is unique, with no free integration constants in the regular solutions.

## Method

The authors use the quark-meson model with two flavors, minimally coupled to gravity in a 3+1-dimensional AdS spacetime written in global coordinates. They solve the gap equation for the condensate σ in two ways: (a) a local density approximation (LDA) neglecting gradient terms, and (b) a full boundary value problem solving the Klein-Gordon equation for σ including gradients, using a closed-form expression for the fermion condensate in thermal AdS obtained by Ambrus (2017). The asymptotic behavior around the center and boundary of AdS is analyzed via Taylor and Frobenius expansions to determine the integration constants.

## Key numbers

- The quark-meson model parameters are set to: λ = 19.71, g = 3.3, v = 87.75 MeV, h = 1.77 × 10⁶ MeV³, reproducing fπ ≈ 93 MeV, mπ ≈ 138 MeV, mσ ≈ 600 MeV, and mq ≈ 307 MeV.
- The minimum value of the condensate at the AdS boundary is σ_min ≈ 0.64 fπ at ℓ ≈ 2.6 GeV⁻¹.
- In the limit ℓ → ∞ (Minkowski spacetime), the critical temperature is about T ≈ 150 MeV in both the LDA and local inhomogeneous treatments.
- The exponents controlling the non-analytic asymptotic solution at the boundary are Δ± = (1/2)(3 ± √(9 + 4 m_σ,eff² ℓ²)), with Δ₊ > 0 and Δ₋ < 0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral symmetry breaking in curved spacetimes, which began with the Gross-Neveu model (Kanemura 1994, Inagaki 1995) and the NJL model (Geyer 1996, Elizalde 1997), where a curvature-induced phase transition was predicted for positively curved spaces while negatively curved spaces remained in the broken phase. It builds on recent studies of inhomogeneous phases in black hole backgrounds (Flachi 2011, Quinta 2019, DeMott 2022) and uses the closed-form fermion condensate for thermal AdS derived by Ambrus (2017). The results enable future work on the holographic dual of the quark-meson model in AdS and on the effects of backreaction when the AdS curvature becomes large.
