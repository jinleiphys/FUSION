# Photoproduction of $J/ψ$ mesons in peripheral and semi-central heavy ion collisions

> We calculate total and differential cross sections for $J/ψ$ photoproduction in ultrarelativistic lead-lead collisions at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. In the present approach we use a simple model based on vector dominance picture and multiple scattering of the hadronic ($c \bar c$) state in a cold nucleus as an example. In our analysis we use both the classical mechanics and quantum (Glauber) formulae for calculating $σ_{tot}(J/ψPb)$ which is a building block of our model. We compare our UPC results with ALICE and CMS data. For semi-central collisions ($b<R_A+R_B$) a modification of the photon flux seems necessary. We discuss different physics motivated approximations. We try to estimate the cross sections for different centrality bins and for $J/ψ$ mesons emitted in forward rapidity range ($2.5<y<4$) corresponding to recent ALICE experimental results. Reasonable results are obtained but open questions are discussed.

# Photoproduction of $J/\psi$ mesons in peripheral and semi-central heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The photoproduction mechanism, which is well-established for ultraperipheral collisions (UPC), can also contribute significantly to $J/\psi$ production in semi-central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, potentially explaining the observed enhancement of $R_{AA} > 1$ at low transverse momentum and small multiplicity, but only if the photon flux is modified to account for nuclear overlap and absorption effects.

## Method
The authors use a vector dominance model (VDM) combined with multiple scattering of the hadronic ($c\bar{c}$) state in cold nuclear matter to calculate the $\gamma A \to J/\psi A$ cross section. They compute the photon flux using three different nuclear form factors (point-like, monopole, and realistic Woods-Saxon) and apply both classical mechanics and quantum (Glauber) formulas for the total $J/\psi$-Pb cross section. For semi-central collisions ($b < R_A + R_B$), they introduce modified photon fluxes that include geometric constraints and absorption effects, comparing three different approximations: the standard UPC flux, an upper limit allowing production only inside the target nucleus, and a lower limit excluding the overlapping region.

## Key numbers
- LHC energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV for Pb-Pb collisions
- For UPC with realistic form factor and classical rescattering: total cross section $\sigma_{tot}^{REAL} = 16.32$ mb
- Differential cross section for UPC at forward rapidity ($2.5 < y < 4$) with realistic form factor: approximately 0.3 mb (from figure comparison with ALICE data)
- For centrality 30-50% with realistic form factor and standard flux: $\mathrm{d}\sigma^{REAL}/\mathrm{d}y_{J/\psi} = 196$ $\mu$b over $\Delta y = 1.5$, compared to ALICE data of $73 \pm 44^{+26}_{-27}$ $\mu$b
- For centrality 70-90% with realistic form factor and standard flux: $\mathrm{d}\sigma^{REAL}/\mathrm{d}y_{J/\psi} = 55$ $\mu$b, compared to ALICE data of $59 \pm 11^{+7}_{-10}$ $\mu$b
- For the lower-limit flux approximation (Eq. 7) with realistic form factor: $\mathrm{d}\sigma^{REAL}/\mathrm{d}y_{J/\psi} = 72$ $\mu$b for centrality 70-90%
- Nuclear radius parameters for Pb: $c = 6.62$ fm, $a = 0.546$ fm (Woods-Saxon distribution)
- Monopole form factor parameter: $\Lambda = 88$ MeV for $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 5.5$ fm
- Lorentz factor: $\gamma = 1471$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of exclusive $J/\psi$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) by authors such as Klein and Nystrand (1999), Baltz et al. (2012), and Goncalves and Machado (2011) to the regime of semi-central collisions where nuclei overlap. The authors address a recent observation by the ALICE Collaboration (2015) of an enhancement in $R_{AA} > 1$ at low $J/\psi$ transverse momentum that had not been explained by existing models. The paper enables future experimental analyses to separate photoproduction contributions from other mechanisms (such as thermal charmonia production discussed by Andronic et al. and Rapp et al.) in peripheral and semi-central heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that open questions remain regarding the role of quark-gluon plasma in damping the photoproduction signal in the overlapping region.
