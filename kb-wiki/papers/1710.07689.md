# Direct photon spectrum and elliptic flow produced from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider within an integrated hydrokinetic model

> The photon transverse momentum spectrum and its anisotropy from Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider energy $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV are investigated within the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM). Photon production is accumulated from the different processes at the various stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions: from the primary hard photons of very early stage of parton collisions to the thermal photons from equilibrated quark-gluon and hadron gas stages. Along the way a hadronic medium evolution is treated in two distinct, in a sense opposite, approaches: chemically equilibrated and chemically frozen system expansion. Studying the centrality dependence of the results obtained allows us to conclude that a relatively strong transverse momentum anisotropy of thermal radiation is suppressed by prompt photon emission which is an isotropic. We find out that this effect is getting stronger as centrality increases because of the simultaneous increase in the relative contribution of prompt photons in the soft part of the spectra. The substantial results obtained in iHKM with nonzero viscosity ($η/s=0.08$) for photon spectra and $v_2$ coefficients are mostly within the error bars of experimental data, but there is some systematic underestimation of both observables for the near central events. We claim that a situation could be significantly improved if an additional photon radiation that accompanies the presence of a deconfined environment is included. Since a matter of a space-time layer where hadronization takes place is actively involved in anisotropic transverse flow, both positive contributions to the spectra and $v_2$ are considerable, albeit such an argument needs further research and elaboration.

## Key claim

The integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) with nonzero viscosity (η/s=0.08) reproduces the ALICE photon transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow coefficients mostly within experimental error bars, but systematically underestimates both observables for near-central events; the authors claim that including additional photon radiation from the hadronization space-time layer (synchrotron-like radiation in the deconfined environment) could significantly improve the description, though this requires further research.

## Method

The authors used the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN=2.76 TeV, evolving the system through five stages: initial state generation via GLISSANDO with a mixed wounded-nucleon and binary-collision model, a prethermal stage described by a relaxation model from τ0=0.1 fm/c to τth=1.0 fm/c, viscous hydrodynamic evolution with η/s=0.08 using the Laine-Schroeder equation of state, particlization at T=165 MeV via the Cooper-Frye formula with Grad's 14-momentum viscous corrections, and a final hadronic stage treated in two opposite approximations (chemically equilibrated and chemically frozen expansion). Prompt photon spectra were calculated using the JETPHOX package with pQCD at scales Q=0.5 pT, scaled by binary collision numbers from a Monte Carlo Glauber model, while thermal photon emission from QGP and hadron gas was computed using the rates from Arnold et al. (2001) and Turbide et al. (2004) respectively.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.08 (minimal value used throughout)
- Initial-state momentum anisotropy parameter: Λ = λ⊥/λ∥ = 100
- Initial formation time: τ0 = 0.1 fm/c; thermalization time: τth = 1.0 fm/c; relaxation time: τrel = 0.25 fm/c
- Hadronization/switching temperature: Th = 165 MeV; hadronic evolution continued down to T = 100 MeV
- Weight coefficient for mixed wounded-nucleon/binary-collision model: α = 0.24
- Prompt photon spectra calculated for transverse momentum range 1 GeV/c < pT < 4 GeV/c
- Additional hadronization emission parameter α ≡ dcγhadr = 0.02 for most central events
- Number of quark flavors used in thermal QGP emission: nf = 3
- QCD scales set to Q = 0.5 pT for prompt photon calculations

## Context

This work extends the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) previously developed by the same group (Naboka et al., 2016) to direct photon observables at LHC energies, following earlier studies of hadron yields, spectra, anisotropic flows, and interferometry radii. The paper addresses the "direct-photon flow puzzle" observed by PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at CERN, where unexpectedly large direct-photon yields and elliptic flow were measured. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming experimental data for different centrality bins and with RHIC Au+Au collisions at lower energies, and the authors plan to extend the analysis to RHIC energies in a forthcoming work.
