# Modern topics in theoretical nuclear physics

> Over the past five years there have been profound advances in nuclear physics based on effective field theory and the renormalization group. In this brief, we summarize these advances and discuss how they impact our understanding of nuclear systems and experiments that seek to unravel their unknowns. We discuss future opportunities and focus on modern topics in low-energy nuclear physics, with special attention to the strong connections to many-body atomic and condensed matter physics, as well as to astrophysics. This makes it an exciting era for nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) and renormalization group (RG) methods have fundamentally changed the understanding of nuclear interactions, showing that all microscopic nuclear force models evolve to a universal low-momentum interaction when high-momentum modes are integrated out, but this unification is conditional on using a cutoff below approximately 2.1 fm\(^{-1}\) and applies only for nucleons with momenta \(k \lesssim 4 M_\text{nuc}\).

## Method

The authors review the application of chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces, where nucleon and pion fields are the relevant degrees of freedom and interactions are organized by Weinberg's power counting in powers of \(Q/\Lambda_\chi\). They then describe the renormalization group evolution of nucleon-nucleon interactions, where high-momentum modes above a cutoff \(\Lambda\) are integrated out to produce a low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) that preserves the low-momentum scattering amplitude. The RG approach is applied to various potential models and EFT interactions to demonstrate their universal behavior at low momenta.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is \(m_\pi \approx 140\) MeV, and the EFT breakdown scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 700\) MeV, where heavier mesons like the \(\rho\) meson (\(m_\rho \approx 770\) MeV) are resolved.
- Nuclear EFT interactions have been developed to N\(^3\)LO, or up to \((Q/\Lambda_\chi)^4\), with 24 low-energy constants fit to approximately 4500 neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data, achieving \(\chi^2/\text{datum} \approx 1\).
- The universal low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) is obtained for all cutoffs \(\Lambda \lesssim 2.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), where 1 fm\(^{-1} \approx 200\) MeV.
- The first three-nucleon interaction appears at N\(^2\)LO, suppressed by \((Q/\Lambda_\chi)^3\) compared to NN interactions, and the N\(^3\)LO three-nucleon force has only two low-energy constants.

## Context

This paper summarizes the paradigm shift in nuclear theory following the application of EFT and RG methods, extending the work of Weinberg (1990) on chiral power counting and the low-momentum interaction developments by Bogner, Kuo, and Schwenk (2003). The review connects these advances to many-body methods including the coupled cluster technique (originally from nuclear physics, later developed in quantum chemistry) and the nuclear shell model, and it highlights overlaps with cold atom physics (e.g., BEC-BCS crossover in low-density neutron matter) and astrophysics (supernova simulations, neutron star cooling). The work enables systematic calculations with microscopic three-nucleon forces beyond light nuclei and provides a framework for estimating theoretical uncertainties through cutoff variation.
