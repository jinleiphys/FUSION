# The black hole spin influence on accretion disk neutrino detection

> Neutrinos are copiously emitted from black hole accretion disks playing a fundamental role in their evolution, as well as in the production of gamma ray bursts and r-process nucleosynthesis. The black hole generates a strong gravitational field able to change the properties of the emerging neutrinos. We study the influence of the black hole spin on the structure of the neutrino surfaces, neutrino luminosities, average neutrino energies, and event counts at SuperK. We consider several disk models and provide estimates that cover different black hole efficiency scenarios. We discuss the influence of the detector's inclination with respect to the axis of the torus on neutrino properties. We find that tori around spinning black holes have larger luminosities, energies and rates compared to tori around static black holes, and that the inclination of the observer causes a reduction in the luminosities and detection rates but an increase in the average energies.

## Key claim

The black hole spin significantly increases neutrino luminosities, average energies, and detection rates from accretion disks compared to non-spinning black holes, while the observer's inclination relative to the disk axis reduces luminosities and detection rates but increases average neutrino energies.

## Method

The authors constructed two types of accretion disk models: a fully general relativistic steady-state disk in the Kerr metric (Chen-Beloborodov models with spin parameters a=0 and a=0.95) and a two-dimensional time-dependent hydrodynamical simulation with a pseudo-relativistic potential (Just et al. models with a=0 and a=0.8). They calculated neutrino surfaces where the optical depth reaches τ=2/3, then used ray-tracing in the Schwarzschild metric for trajectory bending and the Kerr metric for energy shifts to compute observed neutrino fluxes, luminosities, and detection rates at a distant observer (10 kpc) at Super-Kamiokande.

## Key numbers

- For the Ca model (a=0.95, 3 M⊙ BH, accretion rate 5 M⊙/s): observed electron antineutrino luminosity L^o_ν̄_e = 9.8×10^53 ergs/s, observed average energy E^o_ν̄_e = 11.8 MeV, detection rate R = 58,400 s⁻¹ at SuperK
- For the C0 model (a=0, same BH mass and accretion rate): L^o_ν̄_e = 4.8×10^53 ergs/s, E^o_ν̄_e = 10.3 MeV, R = 23,900 s⁻¹
- For the Ja model (a=0.8, 3 M⊙ BH): L^o_ν̄_e = 3.7×10^53 ergs/s, E^o_ν̄_e = 13.4 MeV, R = 23,600 s⁻¹
- For the J0 model (a=0): L^o_ν̄_e = 2.7×10^53 ergs/s, E^o_ν̄_e = 12.7 MeV, R = 15,000 s⁻¹
- Neutrino surface temperatures for Ca model: T_ν_x ≈ 10.5 MeV, T_ν̄_e ≈ 9 MeV, T_ν_e ≈ 8 MeV
- For C0 model: T_ν̄_e ≈ 4.5 MeV; for Ja model: T_ν̄_e maximum 7 MeV; for J0 model: T_ν̄_e maximum ≈ 6 MeV
- Predicted signal durations: 0.14 to 0.34 seconds for a=0, 0.16 to 0.39 seconds for a=0.95
- Total event counts at SuperK for Just et al. model: ~30,750 for a=0, ~48,300 for a=0.8 (using mass-to-accretion-rate method); ~34,900 for a=0, ~55,200 for a=0.8 (using efficiency method)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Caballero (2011) and Surman (2013) on neutrino emission from black hole accretion disks by specifically examining the influence of black hole spin on observable neutrino properties. The results are compared to fully relativistic simulations by Deaton (2013) and Foucart (2015), which found similar neutrino luminosities and energies for spinning black holes despite different initial conditions and treatments of gravity and neutrino physics. The study enables predictions for neutrino detection at Super-Kamiokande from black hole accretion disks, showing that rapidly accreting tori around spinning black holes could produce up to four times the number of events expected from a supernova, and provides a framework for interpreting future neutrino observations from compact binary mergers.
