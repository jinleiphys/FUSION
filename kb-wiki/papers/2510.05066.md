# Barnett effect as a new source of magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

> The Barnett effect is a fundamental magnetomechanical phenomenon in which a ferromagnetic material becomes magnetized under rotation. Using a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model under rigid rotation, we compute the Barnett magnetization ($M_{\rm Barnett}$) and show that it produces a magnetic field ($B_{\text{ind}}$) comparable in magnitude to the well-known external field ($B_{\text{ext}}$) from spectator protons at low energy heavy-ion collisions. This finding establishes the Barnett effect as a previously overlooked but essential source of magnetization and magnetic field in the heavy-ion collisions, with profound implications for understanding spin dynamics and anomalous transport in quantum chromodynamics under extreme rotation.

# Barnett effect as a new source of magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Using a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model under rigid rotation, the Barnett magnetization produces an induced magnetic field comparable in magnitude to the external field from spectator protons, but only at low energy heavy-ion collisions where the external field is significantly weaker; the effect requires finite baryochemical potential to survive, as at zero chemical potential matter and antimatter contributions cancel.

## Method
The author employs the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model under rigid rotation, computing the pressure of each hadron species with rotation-dependent energy eigenvalues that include orbital and spin angular momentum couplings. The Barnett magnetization is derived by equating the energy shift from spin-rotation coupling to the Zeeman energy shift, yielding an effective magnetic field Beff = ω/(g μN), from which the magnetization is obtained as M_Barnett,i = g_i μN (∂P/∂ω)_T,μ. The induced magnetic field Bind is then approximated as Bind = μ0 M for a uniformly magnetized infinite cylinder, using freeze-out parameters from the parametrization of Cleymans et al. (2006) and vorticity values from STAR (2017).

## Key numbers
- The induced magnetic field Bind from the Barnett effect is compared with the external magnetic field Bext estimated by Panda et al. (2024) for two impact parameters; Bind shows a decreasing trend with center-of-mass energy, while Bext increases with energy.
- For a system radius r = 2 fm, Bind from protons is of the same order as Bext at low collision energies; three radius values are considered: r = 2, 5, and 10 fm.
- The Barnett magnetization is computed for angular velocities ω = 0.01 GeV and ω = 0.05 GeV, showing monotonic increase with temperature and baryochemical potential.
- The proton magnetic moment is +2.79 μN with g-factor +5.5857; the Λ hyperon magnetic moment is -0.61 μN with g-factor -1.226 (values from Particle Data Group 2024).
- The freeze-out parametrization uses T(μB) = 0.166 GeV - 0.139 GeV⁻¹ μB² - 0.053 GeV⁻³ μB⁴ and μB(√sNN) = 1.308 GeV / (1 + 0.273 GeV⁻¹ √sNN).

## Context
This work extends the study of rotation effects in QCD matter, previously explored in the HRG model by Fujimoto et al. (2021) for the deconfining phase boundary and by Pradhan et al. (2023) and Mukherjee et al. (2023) for thermodynamic and transport properties. It introduces the Barnett effect as a previously overlooked mechanism in heavy-ion collisions, distinct from the chiral magnetic effect (Fukushima et al., 2008) and chiral vortical effect (Shitade et al., 2020), and predicts a splitting between Λ and anti-Λ global polarization (PΛ < Panti-Λ) that aligns with experimental data from STAR (2017, 2023). The work enables future studies of spin dynamics and anomalous transport in QCD matter under extreme rotation, and suggests applications to neutron star magnetization.
