# Note on neutron star equation of state in the light of GW170817

> From the very first multimessenger event of GW170817, clean robust constraints can be obtained for the tidal deformabilities of the two stars involved in the merger, which provides us unique opportunity to study the equation of states (EOSs) of dense stellar matter. In this contribution, we employ a model from the quark level, describing consistently a nucleon and many-body nucleonic system from a quark potential. We check that our sets of EOSs are consistent with available experimental and observational constraints at both sub-nuclear saturation densities and higher densities. The agreements with ab-initio calculations are also good. Especially, we tune the density dependence of the symmetry energy (characterized by its slope at nuclear saturation $L$) and study its influence on the tidal deformability. The so-called $QMF18$ EOS is named after the case of $L=40~\rm MeV$, and it gives $M_{\rm TOV} =2.08~M_\odot$ and $R= 11.77~\rm km$, $Λ=331$ for a $1.4\,M_\odot$ star. The tidal signals are demonstrated to be insensitive to the uncertain crust-core matching, despite the good correlation between the symmetry energy slope and the radius of the star.

# Note on neutron star equation of state in the light of GW170817

## Key claim
The slope of the symmetry energy at saturation (L) is found to be in the range of 30-60 MeV within the quark mean-field (QMF) model, and within this range neither the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass star nor the mass-weighted tidal deformability shows a good correlation with L, meaning that claims about constraining symmetry energy parameters from gravitational-wave tidal signals should be treated with caution.

## Method
The authors employ a quark mean-field (QMF) model that starts from a confining quark potential to describe nucleons and then builds nuclear matter from point-like nucleons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange. They tune the symmetry energy slope L between 20 and 80 MeV while fixing other saturation properties (density 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV, effective mass 0.77) and compute neutron star equations of state under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. The resulting EOSs are glued to a NV + BPS crust EOS and solved with the TOV equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- The QMF18 EOS (L = 40 MeV) gives a maximum TOV mass of 2.08 solar masses, a radius of 11.77 km for a 1.4 solar mass star, and a tidal deformability Lambda = 331 for a 1.4 solar mass star.
- The allowed range of the symmetry energy slope L within QMF is 30-60 MeV, based on compatibility with chiral effective field theory constraints on neutron matter.
- Cases with L = 20, 25, and 80 MeV are incompatible with the low-density band from chiral effective field theory.
- The mass-weighted tidal deformability is calculated for a binary system with chirp mass 1.188 solar masses and mass ratio 0.7.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QMF study (Li et al. 2018) on neutron star equations of state in light of the GW170817 merger event. It confronts the QMF model with ab-initio calculations from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler et al. 2013) and perturbative QCD, finding that the symmetry energy slope is constrained to 30-60 MeV. The paper also addresses the finding by Lim and Holt (2018) that tidal deformability shows weak correlation with L, and discusses the analysis of Gamba et al. (2019) which argued that crust EOS choice affects radii by about 3% but tidal parameters are insensitive to low-density crust EOS. The results enable future studies of strangeness phase transitions at higher densities and the development of a unified QMF18 EOS with consistent crust-core properties for supernova simulations and pulsar studies.
