# Quantitative study of coherent pairing modes with two neutron transfer: Sn-isotopes

> Pairing rotations and pairing vibrations are collective modes associated with a field, the pair field, which changes the number of particles by two. Consequently, they can be studied at profit with the help of two-particle transfer reactions on superfluid and in normal nuclei, respectively. The advent of exotic beams has opened, for the first time, the possibility to carry out such studies in medium heavy nuclei, within the same isotopic chain. In the case studied in the present paper that of the Sn-isotopes (essentially from closed (Z=N=50) to closed (Z=50,N=82) shells). The static and dynamic off-diagonal, long range order phase coherence in gauge space displayed by pairing rotations and vibrations respectively, leads to coherent states which behave almost classically. Consequently, these modes are amenable to an accurate nuclear structure description in terms of simple models containing the right physics, in particular BCS plus QRPA and HF mean field plus RPA respectively. The associated two- nucleon transfer spectroscopic amplitudes predicted by such model calculations can thus be viewed as essentially "exact". This fact, together with the availability of optical potentials for the different real and virtual channels involved in the reactions considered, namely (A+2)Sn+p, (A+1)Sn+d and (A)Sn+t, allows for the calculation of the associated absolute cross sections without, arguably, free parameters. The numerical predictions of the absolute differential cross sections, obtained making use of the above mentioned nuclear structure and optical potential inputs, within the framework of second order DWBA, taking into account simultaneous, successive and non-orthogonality contributions provide, within experimental errors an overall account of the experimental findings for all of the measured (A+2)Sn(p,t)(A)Sn(gs) reactions, for which absolute cross sections have been reported to date.

## Key claim

The paper claims that absolute differential cross sections for (p,t) two-neutron transfer reactions along the Sn isotopic chain from closed shell (Z=N=50) to closed shell (Z=50,N=82) can be calculated without free parameters, using BCS plus QRPA or HF plus RPA nuclear structure models combined with second-order DWBA reaction formalism, and that these predictions agree with experimental data within experimental errors, generally below 10% uncertainty.

## Method

The authors calculated absolute differential cross sections for (A+2)Sn(p,t)(A)Sn(gs) reactions using second-order Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), including simultaneous, successive, and non-orthogonality contributions. Nuclear structure inputs for two-nucleon transfer spectroscopic amplitudes came from BCS plus QRPA calculations for superfluid nuclei and HF mean field plus RPA calculations for normal (closed shell) nuclei. Optical potentials for the real and virtual channels (A+2)Sn+p, (A+1)Sn+d, and (A)Sn+t were taken from available literature.

## Key numbers

- For 132Sn (doubly magic, A0=132), the correlation energy for pair addition is E_corr(A+2) = 1.17 MeV, and for pair removal is E_corr(A-2) = 2.14 MeV, giving an average E_corr = 1.66 MeV.
- The ratio E_corr/G for 132Sn is approximately (1/2)[8.9 + 13.6] ≈ 11, comparable to the value of Δ'/G ≈ 7 for the superfluid nucleus 120Sn.
- The dynamical order parameter α_dyn for 132Sn is approximately (Λ/G) ≈ 2√30 ≈ 11 from the two-level model, and from actual values gives (1/2)[1.08/0.131 + 1.60/0.157] ≈ 9.
- The Cooper pair correlation length ξ ≈ 20-30 fm, compared to nuclear radius R ≈ 5 fm.
- The pairing coupling constant G ≈ 25 MeV/A (major j-shell approximation).
- The modulus of the order parameter α'_0 ≈ Δ'/G ≈ 1.4 MeV/G ≈ 7 for medium heavy nuclei (A ≈ 120).

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing rotations and pairing vibrations, collective modes associated with the pair field that changes particle number by two, from light nuclei to medium-heavy nuclei within a single isotopic chain, made possible by the advent of exotic beams. It builds on the theoretical framework of Anderson (1958), Bes (1966), and Broglia (1973) for pairing correlations in nuclei, and on the pairspin (quasispin) formulation of superconductivity. The results enable quantitative predictions of two-nucleon transfer cross sections without free parameters, and demonstrate that normal (closed shell) nuclei like 132Sn display dynamical pairing correlations comparable in strength to the static pairing condensate in superfluid nuclei like 120Sn.
