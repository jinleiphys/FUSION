# Parton energy loss in a classical strongly coupled QGP

> We investigate the energy loss of heavy quarks in the gas, liquid and solid phase of a classical quark-gluon plasma (cQGP) using molecular dynamics simulations. The model consists of massive quarks and gluons interacting as a classical non-relativistic colored Coulomb gas. We show that the electric force decorrelates on a short time scale causing the energy loss to be mostly diffusive and langevin-like in the cQGP. We find that the drag coefficient changes with the heavy quark mass, while the diffusion constant does not. The fractional collisional energy loss is much larger than the leading order estimates from a wQGP because of the core repulsion. Following recent suggestions, we show how the cQGP results can be translated to the sQGP results in the $T=(1-3)T_c$ range.

## Key claim

The fractional collisional energy loss of heavy quarks in a strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) is much larger, up to a factor of five in the high-momentum region, than leading-order perturbative estimates from a weakly coupled QGP (wQGP), but this result depends on modeling the sQGP as a classical non-relativistic colored Coulomb gas with a short-range repulsive core potential and using quasiparticle masses of approximately 3T.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics (MD) simulations of 64 massive quark and gluon quasiparticles confined in a periodic box, interacting via a non-relativistic colored Coulomb potential plus a short-range repulsive core potential. An external heavy probe particle with mass M greater than the quasiparticle mass m was injected into the equilibrated system, and its phase-space coordinates were tracked over time to extract the momentum distribution function, from which the drag coefficient η(p) and longitudinal momentum diffusion constant κ_L were measured. The results were then translated from simulation units to physical sQGP units by rescaling length, time, and mass using lattice QCD estimates for quasiparticle masses and densities at T = 1.5–3 T_c.

## Key numbers

* Drag coefficient η = 0.35, 0.2, and 0.1 (simulation units) for heavy quark masses M = 2m, 6m, and 10m respectively, measured in the liquid phase at Γ = 3.
* Longitudinal momentum diffusion constant κ_L = 0.25 E (simulation units), independent of the heavy probe mass within uncertainties.
* After translation to sQGP at T = 250 MeV ≈ 1.5 T_c, the drag coefficient η = 2.3, 1.3, and 0.65 fm⁻¹ for probe masses M = 1.5, 4.5, and 7.5 GeV respectively.
* The fractional collisional energy loss in the sQGP is up to a factor of five larger than the leading-order perturbative result from equation (4) in the high-momentum region.
* The electric force decorrelation time in the liquid phase (Γ = 3) is t_F = τ/2 in simulation units, corresponding to t_F ≈ 1/(10T) for the sQGP.

## Context

This work extends the classical molecular dynamics approach to the sQGP previously introduced by the same authors (Dusling and Zahed, 2008) for studying decorrelation times, diffusion, and viscosity, and applies it specifically to heavy quark energy loss. The results contradict leading-order perturbative collisional energy loss calculations (Bjorken, 1982; Braaten and Thoma, 1991) which predict much smaller fractional energy loss, and the authors note that even the most advanced perturbative calculations (Djordjevic, 2006) are bounded from above by the leading-order result. The findings enable improved modeling of jet quenching at RHIC energies, where α_s ≈ 0.5 and the medium is strongly coupled, and the authors note that the same MD results can be rescaled to accommodate updated lattice QCD data on quasiparticle masses and densities.
