# Mesonic and nucleon fluctuation effects at finite baryon density

> Mesonic and nucleon fluctuation effects are investigated in medium. We couple the nucleon field to the $2+1$ flavor meson model and investigate the finite temperature and density behavior of the system, in particular, the axial anomaly function. Somewhat contrary to earlier expectations we find that it tends to strengthen at finite density. At lower temperatures nucleon density fluctuations can cause a relative difference in the $U_A(1)$ axial anomaly of about $20\%$. This has important consequences on the mesonic spectra, especially on the $η-η'$ system, as we observe no drop in the $η'$ mass as a function of the baryochemical potential, irrespective of the temperature. Based on the details of chiral symmetry restoration, it is argued that there has to be a competition between underlying QCD effects of the anomaly and fluctuations of the low energy hadronic degrees of freedom, and the fate of the $U_A(1)$ coefficient should be decided by taking into account both effects simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, contrary to earlier expectations, the $U_A(1)$ axial anomaly tends to strengthen at finite baryon density when both mesonic and nucleon fluctuations are included via the functional renormalization group, with nucleon density fluctuations causing a relative difference of about 20% in the anomaly at lower temperatures. The authors argue that the fate of the $U_A(1)$ coefficient should be decided by taking into account both underlying QCD effects of the anomaly and fluctuations of low-energy hadronic degrees of freedom simultaneously.

## Method

The authors couple a nucleon field to a 2+1 flavor linear sigma model via a Yukawa-type interaction and use the functional renormalization group (FRG) method at leading order of the derivative expansion, equipped with a chiral invariant expansion technique. They employ a 3D Litim regulator for both bosons and fermions, integrate the flow equations from a UV cutoff of 1 GeV down to zero, and project the flow onto each operator to obtain individual flow equations for the coefficient functions. The nucleon-induced mesonic interactions are treated by expanding the fermionic contribution to the flow equation in powers of the Yukawa coupling $g$.

## Key numbers

- The absolute value of the 't Hooft coupling $|A|$ at $T=0$ and $\mu_B=0$ GeV is 4.64 GeV for the nucleon mass parameter $m_N$; at $\mu_B=1.0$ GeV it increases to 5.19 GeV for the same $m_N$.
- At lower temperatures, nucleon density fluctuations cause a relative difference in the $U_A(1)$ axial anomaly of about 20% as a function of the baryochemical potential.
- The $\eta'$ mass shows no drop as a function of the baryochemical potential, irrespective of the temperature, with the anomaly function $A_{k=0}$ in the minimum of the effective potential around 5 GeV in the temperature range $T \simeq 0$–200 MeV.
- The critical temperature $T_C$ at zero chemical potential is missed by a factor of 2 compared to recent lattice simulations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRG study of mesonic fluctuations on the axial anomaly (Fejos 2016) by adding nucleon degrees of freedom and finite baryochemical potential. It contradicts earlier model calculations (Costa 2003, Nagahiro 2006) that found a reduction of the $\eta'$ mass of order 100 MeV at normal nuclear density, which suggested a possible $\eta'N$ bound state (Sakai 2013, Sakai 2016). The results indicate that both mesonic and nucleon fluctuations must be taken into account alongside underlying instanton effects of QCD to properly describe the $U_A(1)$ anomaly at finite temperature and density.
