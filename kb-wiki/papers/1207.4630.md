# Local-Duality QCD Sum Rules for Pseudoscalar-Meson Form Factors

> We scrutinize recent findings on the charged-pion elastic form factor and the form factors entering in neutral-meson-to-photons transition amplitudes within the framework of QCD sum rules.

# Local-Duality QCD Sum Rules for Pseudoscalar-Meson Form Factors

## Key claim
The authors argue that recent claims of large deviations from local-duality (LD) expectations for the charged-pion elastic form factor in the range Q² = 20–50 GeV² are unlikely to be correct, and that the BaBar data for the neutral-pion transition form factor must be treated with caution given the conflicting Belle measurement.

## Method
The authors apply local-duality QCD sum rules, which are the infinite-Borel-parameter limit of standard QCD sum rules, to compute the charged-pion elastic form factor and the transition form factors for π⁰, η, and η′ mesons decaying to two photons. The sum rules involve only perturbative spectral densities (known to O(αₛ) or two-loop accuracy) and effective thresholds s_eff(Q²) that encode all nonperturbative dynamics; the authors introduce the concept of an "equivalent effective threshold" that exactly reproduces experimental data or theoretical predictions when inserted into the sum rule.

## Key numbers
- Charged-pion decay constant: f_π = 130 MeV
- Asymptotic LD threshold limit for both elastic and transition form factors: 4π²f_π² ≈ 0.671 GeV²
- Charged-pion elastic form factor analyzed for momentum transfers Q² ≈ 5–50 GeV²
- For the charged-pion elastic form factor, the authors find that some recent theoretical analyses miss local duality up to Q² ≈ 20 GeV²
- For the neutral-pion transition form factor, BaBar data show a rapid growth with Q² that deviates from LD expectations, while Belle data contradict the BaBar findings

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Balakireva et al., 2011; Lucha and Melikhov, 2011) of pseudoscalar-meson form factors using LD sum rules. The paper scrutinizes recent controversial findings for the charged-pion elastic form factor and the π⁰ → γγ* transition form factor, comparing LD predictions with experimental data from CELLO, CLEO, BaBar, and Belle. The results enable a clearer assessment of where the simple LD approximation (with constant effective threshold) works well (η and η′ transitions) versus where it may fail or where experimental discrepancies exist (π⁰ transition).
