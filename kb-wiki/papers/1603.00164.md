# Nuclear medium effects in $F_{2A}^{EM}(x,Q^2)$ and $F_{2A}^{Weak}(x,Q^2)$ structure functions

> Recent phenomenological analysis of experimental data on DIS processes induced by charged leptons and neutrinos/antineutrinos beams on nuclear targets by CTEQ collaboration has confirmed the observation of CCFR and NuTeV collaborations, that weak structure function $F_{2A}^{Weak} (x,Q^2)$ is different from electromagnetic structure function $F_{2A}^{EM} (x,Q^2)$ in a nucleus like iron, specially in the region of low $x$ and $Q^2$. In view of this observation we have made a study of nuclear medium effects on $F_{2A}^{Weak} (x,Q^2)$ and $F_{2A}^{EM} (x,Q^2)$ for a wide range of $x$ and $Q^2$ using a microscopic nuclear model. We have considered Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions from pion and rho mesons and shadowing effects to incorporate nuclear medium effects. The calculations are performed in a local density approximation using a relativistic nucleon spectral function which includes nucleon correlations. The numerical results in the case of iron nucleus are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects on the weak structure function \(F_{2A}^{Weak}(x,Q^2)\) differ from those on the electromagnetic structure function \(F_{2A}^{EM}(x,Q^2)\) in a nucleus like iron, especially in the low-\(x\) and low-\(Q^2\) region, because nuclear shadowing and mesonic contributions are not identical for photons and \(W^\pm/Z\) bosons, and the axial current present in weak interactions may further influence the behavior.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear structure functions using a microscopic, field-theoretical model based on a relativistic nucleon spectral function obtained from nuclear many-body theory in a local density approximation. They incorporate Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions from pion and rho mesons, and shadowing effects, and perform the calculations without assuming the Callan-Gross relation between \(F_{1A}\) and \(F_{2A}\). The nucleon structure functions are expressed in terms of CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions at next-to-leading order, and the formalism is applied separately for protons and neutrons to account for nonisoscalarity in nuclear targets like iron.

## Key numbers

* For an isoscalar iron target, the phenomenological nuclear correction factor \(f(x)\) used by CCFR is given by \(f(x) = 1.0963 - 0.36427 x - 0.27805 e^{-21.936 x} + 2.7715 x^{14.417}\) (Eq. 13), independent of \(Q^2\).
* The ratio \(R(x,Q^2) = F_2^{EM}(x,Q^2) / F_2^{Weak}(x,Q^2)\) for an isoscalar nucleon target equals \(5/18\) when strange and charm quark distributions are equal, and any deviation gives information about heavy quark distributions.
* Results are presented for \(^{56}\text{Fe}\) over a wide range of \(x\) and \(Q^2\) and compared with experimental data from EMC, JLab, CDHSW, CCFR, and NuTeV collaborations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic studies of nuclear medium effects in DIS (Haider et al., 2011; Haider et al., 2012; Haider et al., 2014; Haider et al., 2015) and addresses a disagreement between the nCTEQ collaboration (Kovarik et al., 2015) and de Florian et al. (2011) regarding whether the same nuclear correction factor describes both charged-lepton and neutrino DIS. The results enable a better understanding of sea quark modifications and higher twist effects in nuclei, and are relevant for interpreting data from MINERvA and future DUNE experiments.
