# Quarkonium Formation Time in a Model-Independent Approach

> We use dispersion relations to reconstruct, in a model-independent way, the formation dynamics of heavy quarkonium from the experimental data on e+ e- annihilation to Q-bar Q. We extract a distribution of formation times with a mean value for the J/psi, tau{J/psi} = 0.44 fm; and for the Upsilon, tau{Upsilon} = 0.32 fm. The corresponding widths of these distributions are given by Delta-tau{J/psi} = 0.31 fm and Delta-tau{Upsilon} = 0.28 fm. This information can be used as an input in modeling of heavy quarkonium production on nuclear targets.

## Key claim

Using dispersion relations and experimental data on $e^+e^-$ annihilation to heavy quark pairs, the authors extract a distribution of formation times for quarkonium states rather than a single fixed value; the mean formation times are comparable to the inverse mass splittings between ground and first excited states, but the widths of the distributions are also comparable to the means, indicating that a single universal formation time parameter is misleading.

## Method

The authors use dispersion relations in coordinate representation to relate the space-time correlator of the heavy quark current to the experimentally measured cross section for $e^+e^- \to \bar{Q}Q$. They parametrize the ratio $R(s) = \sigma(e^+e^- \to \bar{Q}Q)/\sigma(e^+e^- \to \mu^+\mu^-)$ in terms of narrow resonances plus a continuum contribution, then compute the Euclidean-time correlator $\Pi(\tau)$ and define the fraction $F_i(\tau)$ of each state present at a given time. The derivative $dF_i/d\tau$ is interpreted as a continuous distribution of formation times.

## Key numbers

* Mean formation time for $J/\psi$: $\langle \tau_{J/\psi} \rangle = 0.44$ fm
* Mean formation time for $\Upsilon$: $\langle \tau_{\Upsilon} \rangle = 0.32$ fm
* Width of formation time distribution for $J/\psi$: $\Delta\tau_{J/\psi} = 0.31$ fm
* Width of formation time distribution for $\Upsilon$: $\Delta\tau_{\Upsilon} = 0.28$ fm

## Context

This work extends the quantum-mechanical approach to quarkonium formation (Gribov, 1970; Farrar et al., 1988; Thews, 1994) by providing a model-independent extraction of formation time distributions directly from experimental data, rather than relying on universal parameters such as $\tau_f \sim m_\rho^{-1}$ or classical expansion models (Gunion and Soper, 1977). The results can be used as input for phenomenological modeling of quarkonium production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, where formation time effects influence nuclear suppression patterns and color transparency phenomena.
