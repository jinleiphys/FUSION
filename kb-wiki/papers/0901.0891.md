# Higher Order QED Calculation of Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Production of mu+ mu- Pairs

> A higher order QED calculation of the ultraperipheral heavy ion cross section for mu+ mu- pair production at RHIC and LHC is carried out. The so-called "Coulomb corrections" lead to an even greater percentage decrease of mu+ mu- production from perturbation theory than the corresponding decrease for e+ e- pair production. Unlike the e+ e- case, the finite charge distribution of the ions (form factor) and the necessary subtraction of impact parameters with matter overlap are significant effects in calculation an observable ultraperipheral mu+ mu- total cross section.

# Higher Order QED Calculation of Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Production of mu+ mu- Pairs

## Key claim

Higher order QED Coulomb corrections to muon pair production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions are not small (contrary to earlier approximate analytical claims by Hencken, Kuraev, and Serbo), and the finite nuclear charge distribution (form factor) plus the necessary exclusion of impact parameters with nuclear overlap produce additional significant reductions beyond the Coulomb corrections alone.

## Method

The author extends previous numerical methods for higher order e+e- pair production to mu+ mu- pairs, using a regularized transverse integral expression involving the modified Bessel function K0 to incorporate Coulomb corrections beyond perturbation theory. For impact parameter dependent calculations, the amplitude is integrated before squaring using piecewise analytical integration to handle the rapidly oscillating phase exp(i k·b). A form factor g(k) = 1/(1 + k^2/Λ^2) with Λ ≈ 80 MeV is applied to the transverse potential, and unitarity corrections from nuclear overlap are subtracted using an impact parameter representation with a Poisson distribution for competing processes.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of perturbative mu+ mu- to e+ e- cross sections without form factor is (m_e/m_mu)^2 = 2.34 × 10^{-5}
- With form factor, the perturbative ratio is 0.61 × 10^{-5} for RHIC and 1.16 × 10^{-5} for LHC
- At RHIC (Au + Au, γ = 100): perturbative b-independent cross section is 211 mb; exact (with Coulomb corrections) is 164 mb (22% reduction); after subtracting ion overlap the best value is 144 mb (32% reduction from perturbation theory)
- At LHC (Pb + Pb, γ = 2760): perturbative b-independent cross section is 2.43 b; exact is 2.09 b (14% reduction); after subtracting ion overlap the best value is 2.03 b (16% reduction from perturbation theory)
- The Coulomb correction reduction for mu+ mu- (22% at RHIC, 14% at LHC) is greater than the corresponding reduction for e+ e- (17% at RHIC, 11% at LHC)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous higher order QED calculations for e+ e- pair production (Baltz 2005, 2006, 2008) to mu+ mu- pairs, and directly contradicts the approximate analytical results of Hencken, Kuraev, and Serbo (2008) who argued that Coulomb corrections to mu+ mu- production are small. The calculations are relevant for the ultraperipheral heavy ion program at the LHC, where muon pair detection in ALICE had been proposed as a luminosity monitor by Kharlov and Sadovsky; the author argues that the significant higher order corrections found here would require inclusion for a meaningful luminosity measurement. The method builds on the Lee and Milstein (2000, 2001) regularization of the transverse integral and the Bethe-Maximon theory of Coulomb corrections to photoproduction.
