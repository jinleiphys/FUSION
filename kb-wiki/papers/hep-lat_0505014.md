# Quarks with Twisted Boundary Conditions in the Epsilon Regime

> We study the effects of twisted boundary conditions on the quark fields in the epsilon regime of chiral perturbation theory. We consider the $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ chiral theory with non-degenerate quarks and the $SU(3)_L\times SU(3)_R$ chiral theory with massless up and down quarks and massive strange quarks. The partition function and condensate are derived for each theory. Because flavor-neutral Goldstone bosons are unaffected by twisted boundary conditions chiral symmetry is still restored in finite volumes. The dependence of the condensate on the twisting parameters can be used to extract the pion decay constant from simulations in the epsilon regime. The relative contribution to the partition function from sectors of different topological charge is numerically insensitive to twisted boundary conditions.

## Key claim

Twisted boundary conditions on quark fields in the epsilon regime of chiral perturbation theory do not alter the qualitative restoration of chiral symmetry in finite volumes because flavor-neutral Goldstone bosons remain unaffected by the twisting, but the quantitative dependence of the condensate on twisting parameters can be used to extract the pion decay constant from lattice simulations, provided the twist is sufficiently small (B_mu ~ m_pi ~ epsilon^2) so that all Goldstone boson zero modes remain strongly coupled.

## Method

The authors computed the partition function and chiral condensate for SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R chiral perturbation theory with non-degenerate quarks and SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R theory with massless up and down quarks and massive strange quarks, treating the zero-mode path integral exactly while treating nonzero modes perturbatively. They considered two power-counting regimes: the "twisted epsilon regime" where B_mu ~ m_pi ~ epsilon^2 and all Goldstone boson zero modes are strongly coupled, and the "remnant epsilon regime" where B_mu ~ epsilon, m_pi ~ epsilon^2 and only flavor-neutral meson zero modes are strongly coupled. The partition function projection onto sectors of fixed topological charge was evaluated by including a theta-term and Fourier transforming with respect to the theta parameter.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless variable s = (1/4) f_pi^2 beta V m_pi^2 = -(m_u+m_d)/2 <q_bar q>_infty beta V, where beta V is the four-dimensional volume
- The dimensionless twisting parameter t = (1/4) (B^u - B^d)^2 f_pi^2 beta V = (theta^u - theta^d)^2/(4 L^2) f_pi^2 beta V
- The SU(2) partition function for t=0 reduces to Z(s,0) = I_1(2s)/s, where I_1 is a modified Bessel function
- The SU(2) partition function in the remnant epsilon regime (large t) is proportional to I_0(2s)
- The SU(3) strangeness condensate partition function with isospin-symmetric twists and massless up/down quarks is Z(s^s, t') = N'' integral_0^1 dx x(1-x^2) I_0(s^s x) exp(t'(x^2 - 1/2))

## Context

This work extends the epsilon-regime chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) and Leutwyler (1992) to include twisted boundary conditions, which have been applied in lattice QCD by Bedaque (2004), de Divitiis (2004), and Sachrajda (2004) as a method to access non-integer momenta. The results show that the dependence of the finite-volume condensate on twisting parameters provides an alternative method to extract the pion decay constant from epsilon-regime simulations, complementing the usual extraction from axial correlator late-time behavior. The finding that the topological charge distribution remains dominated by the zero-charge sector when m_q <q_bar q>_infty beta V < 1, even with twisted boundary conditions, confirms that the conclusion of Leutwyler (1992) is robust to this modification of boundary conditions.
