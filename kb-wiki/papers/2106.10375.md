# Improved neutron lifetime measurement with UCN$τ$

> We report an improved measurement of the free neutron lifetime $τ_{n}$ using the UCN$τ$ apparatus at the Los Alamos Neutron Science Center. We counted a total of approximately $38\times10^{6}$ surviving ultracold neutrons (UCN) after storing in UCN$τ$'s magneto-gravitational trap over two data acquisition campaigns in 2017 and 2018. We extract $τ_{n}$ from three blinded, independent analyses by both pairing long and short storage-time runs to find a set of replicate $τ_{n}$ measurements and by performing a global likelihood fit to all data while self-consistently incorporating the $β$-decay lifetime. Both techniques achieve consistent results and find a value $τ_{n}=877.75\pm0.28_{\text{ stat}}+0.22/-0.16_{\text{ syst}}$~s. With this sensitivity, neutron lifetime experiments now directly address the impact of recent refinements in our understanding of the standard model for neutron decay.

## Key claim

The paper reports a neutron lifetime of τₙ = 877.75 ± 0.28_stat +0.22/−0.16_syst s, claiming this is the first measurement precise enough to directly confront the theoretical uncertainty from recent refinements in the standard model electroweak radiative corrections, which shift the extracted |V_ud| value and create a ~3σ tension with CKM unitarity.

## Method

Ultracold neutrons (UCNs) with kinetic energy ≤180 neV from the Los Alamos spallation source were loaded into a magneto-gravitational Halbach-array trap, cleaned of higher-energy neutrons, stored for times ranging from 20 to 1550 s, and then counted by a ¹⁰B-coated ZnS scintillator detector. Three blinded, independent analyses extracted τₙ using both a paired method (comparing short and long storage-time runs) and a global likelihood fit that self-consistently incorporated β-decay losses, depolarization, residual gas up-scattering, and escape rates. Systematic corrections were assessed for UCN event definition, normalization weighting, depolarization, uncleaned UCNs, heated UCNs, an aluminum block that fell into the trap during the 2017 campaign, and residual gas scattering.

## Key numbers

- τₙ = 877.75 ± 0.28_stat +0.22/−0.16_syst s (combined result from three analyses)
- Total uncertainty of 0.34 s (0.039%), improving upon the authors' past results by a factor of 2.25
- Approximately 38 × 10⁶ surviving ultracold neutrons counted over two data acquisition campaigns in 2017 and 2018
- Storage times varied from 20 to 1550 s
- The aluminum block introduced an additional ~424 s decay component to the yields, with a correction of +0.06 ± 0.05 s to the final τₙ
- Residual gas up-scattering correction of +0.11 ± 0.06 s
- Depolarization rate assigned as λ_dp = 0.0⁺¹·⁰₋₀·₀ × 10⁻⁷ s⁻¹
- Escape rate for uncleaned UCNs estimated as λ_escape = 2 × 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹

## Context

This measurement extends the UCNτ collaboration's previous work (Pattie 2018) with improved experimental and analysis techniques, including a buffer volume introduced in 2018 to smooth UCN production fluctuations. The result addresses the discrepancy between "beam" methods (Yue 2013) and "bottle" methods (Serebrov 2005, Pichlmaier 2010, Steyerl 2012, Arzumanov 2015, Ezhov 2018, Serebrov 2018) for neutron lifetime measurement, and is precise enough to test the recent re-assessment of inner electroweak radiative corrections (Seng 2019, Czarnecki 2019, Hayen 2021) that shifts |V_ud| extracted from superallowed 0⁺→0⁺ decays. This enables independent probes of first-row CKM unitarity without nuclear structure corrections, and can constrain beyond-standard-model interactions at energy scales above 10 TeV.
