# Semihard interactions at TeV energies

> We investigate the high-energy behavior of the total cross section, $σ_{\text{tot}}$, and the ratio of the real to imaginary parts of the scattering amplitude, $ρ$, in both proton-proton and antiproton-proton channels. Our analysis is based on a QCD-inspired model in which the rise of the cross sections is predominantly driven by semihard processes involving gluons. We address the tension between measurements from the ATLAS/ALFA and TOTEM Collaborations, showing that independent analyses of their datasets can provide statistically consistent descriptions of the overall data, even though they do not fully reproduce the central values of the $ρ$ parameter at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV. The slight discrepancy between these central values and the model's predicted values, obtained using an asymptotic dominant crossing-even elastic scattering amplitude, points to the potential presence of an odd component in the semihard amplitude at high energies.

# Semihard interactions at TeV energies

## Key claim

The rise of total cross sections at high energies is predominantly driven by semihard (minijet) processes involving gluons, and the tension between ATLAS/ALFA and TOTEM measurements can be statistically reconciled through independent analyses of their datasets; however, the model's predicted values for the rho parameter at 13 TeV do not fully reproduce the central values reported by either collaboration, suggesting a potential odd-under-crossing component (Odderon) in the semihard amplitude at high energies.

## Method

The authors employ a QCD-inspired eikonal model where the eikonal function is decomposed into soft and semihard components, with the semihard part factorized as the product of the QCD jet cross section and an overlap density derived from dipole form factors. Global fits to forward scattering data (total cross section and rho parameter) for pp and antiproton-proton channels from 10 GeV to LHC energies are performed using two distinct data ensembles: one including ATLAS/ALFA measurements and the other including TOTEM measurements, with NLO parton distribution functions from NNPDF4.0 and CT18. The semihard eikonal is treated as crossing-even, while the soft eikonal includes both even and odd components, with the odd component vanishing at high energies.

## Key numbers

- TOTEM measurement of total cross section at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: sigma_tot = 98.58 ± 2.23 mb, differing from ATLAS/ALFA value of 95.35 ± 1.36 mb at the same energy by 1.4 sigma
- ATLAS/ALFA total cross section at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV: sigma_tot = 96.07 ± 0.92 mb, differing from lowest TOTEM measurement of 101.5 ± 2.1 mb by 2.6 sigma
- ATLAS/ALFA rho parameter at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: rho = 0.098 ± 0.011; TOTEM values at same energy: rho = 0.09 ± 0.01 and rho = 0.10 ± 0.01
- For Ensemble A (ATLAS/ALFA) at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: CT18 fit yields sigma_tot = 103.2 mb and rho = 0.113; NNPDF4.0 fit yields sigma_tot = 102.7 mb and rho = 0.105
- For Ensemble T (TOTEM) at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: CT18 fit yields sigma_tot = 107.8 mb and rho = 0.120; NNPDF4.0 fit yields sigma_tot = 107.1 mb and rho = 0.111
- ATLAS/ALFA measurement of sigma_tot at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: 104.68 ± 1.08 mb; average of three TOTEM values: 110.1 ± 2.0 mb; average of three rho measurements from both collaborations: 0.096 ± 0.006
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom: Ensemble A with CT18 gives 1.10 (158 d.o.f.), with NNPDF4.0 gives 1.07 (158 d.o.f.); Ensemble T with CT18 gives 1.18 (168 d.o.f.), with NNPDF4.0 gives 1.11 (168 d.o.f.)

## Context

This work extends the minijet-model formalism previously developed by Luna et al. (various years) and Iser et al. (2021) to describe forward scattering observables at LHC energies, addressing the persistent tension between ATLAS/ALFA and TOTEM measurements of total cross sections at 7, 8, and 13 TeV. The analysis demonstrates that while a crossing-even dominant amplitude can provide statistically acceptable fits, the systematic discrepancy between predicted and measured central values of the rho parameter at 13 TeV points toward the need for an Odderon contribution (a C-odd color-singlet state of three reggeized gluons) in the semihard sector, a possibility currently under investigation by the authors. The use of different PDF sets (NNPDF4.0, CT18, MSHT20) shows that the growth rate of the QCD cross section is sensitive to the gluon distribution at small x, with MSHT20 producing excessively steep growth leading to poor fits.
