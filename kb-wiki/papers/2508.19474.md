# Reducing parametric uncertainties through information geometry methods

> Information geometry is a study of applying differential geometry methods to challenging statistical problems, such as uncertainty quantification. In this work, we use information geometry to study how measurement uncertainties in pre-neutron emission mass distributions affect the parameter estimation in the Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay code, CGMF. We quantify the impact of reduced uncertainties on the pre-neutron mass yield of specific masses to these parameters, for spontaneous fission of ${}^{252}$Cf, first using a toy model assuming Poissonian uncertainties, then an experimental measurement taken from Göök et al., 2014 in EXFOR. We achieved a reduction of up to $\sim15\%$ in CGMF parameter errors, predominantly in $w_0^{(1)}$ and $w_1^{(0)}$.

## Key claim

Information geometry methods can reduce parametric uncertainties in the Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay code CGMF by up to approximately 15%, predominantly in the parameters \(w_0^{(1)}\) and \(w_1^{(0)}\), but this reduction is conditional on the specific choice of scaling parameter \(\alpha\) and applies only to the spontaneous fission of \({}^{252}\)Cf.

## Method

The authors applied information geometry to connect the space of measurement residuals to the space of CGMF model parameters via the Fisher information metric and its eigendecomposition. They computed error shifts \(d\sigma^i\) for each atomic mass bin by varying a scaling parameter \(\alpha\) that reduces the largest-uncertainty eigendirection of the parameter covariance matrix, then projected this reduction back onto the measurement-error space. The method was tested first on a toy model assuming Poissonian uncertainties for CGMF-simulated mass yields, then on experimental pre-neutron emission mass yields of \({}^{252}\)Cf from Gook et al. (2014) in EXFOR, using 100 Monte Carlo simulations centered on default CGMF parameter values.

## Key numbers

* Up to approximately 15% reduction in CGMF parameter errors was achieved for the parameters \(w_0^{(1)}\) and \(w_1^{(0)}\) when using experimental measurement errors from Gook et al. (2014).
* For the Poissonian error model with \(\alpha=1\), the median relative error shift \(d\sigma^i/\sigma^i\) for mass number 126 was \(0.026_{-0.017}^{+0.035}\).
* For the experimental measurement errors with \(\alpha=1.0\), the median relative error shift for mass number 129 was \(0.081_{-0.067}^{+0.141}\).
* The inverse Fisher information matrix eigenvalues and parameter errors for the real measurement case were approximately 5-10 times higher than for the Poissonian toy problem.

## Context

This work extends the application of information geometry, previously used in nuclear energy density functionals by Niksic (2016) and Imbrisak (2023), to the uncertainty quantification of fission model parameters in CGMF. It addresses the need for quantifying how experimental uncertainties affect phenomenological inputs to theoretical fission models, a topic with few prior studies such as Jaffke (2018) and Randrup (2019). The method provides a lookup-table framework connecting measurement precision to parameter precision, enabling experimentalists to identify which atomic mass bins most effectively reduce CGMF parameter uncertainties when measured with greater accuracy.
