# Iterative solutions of the ATDHFB equations to determine the nuclear collective inertia

> An iterative adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) method is developed within the framework of Skyrme density functional theory. The ATDHFB equation is solved iteratively to avoid explicitly calculating the stability matrix. The contribution of the time-odd mean fields to the ATDHF(B) moment of inertia is incorporated self-consistently, and the results are verified by comparing them with the dynamical cranking predictions. The inertia mass tensor is calculated with the density-derivative term evaluated by numerical differentiation.

## Key claim

The iterative ATDHFB method avoids explicitly calculating the two-body stability matrix by solving the ATDHFB equation through a fixed-point iteration, and it incorporates time-odd mean-field effects self-consistently; the results match dynamical cranking predictions only when all particle states are included in the single-particle space.

## Method

The ATDHF(B) equation is expressed in the Hartree-Fock single-particle basis, and the particle-hole matrix elements of the time-odd density are solved via a fixed-point iterative method starting from a vanishing time-odd mean field. In each iteration, the time-odd density defines an adiabatic basis via singular value decomposition, from which time-odd spatial densities and currents are calculated to update the time-odd mean fields. The collective inertia is evaluated at the end of each iteration until convergence, and the density-derivative term required for the inertia mass tensor is computed by numerical differentiation.

## Key numbers

* Rotational moment of inertia of triaxial $^{126}$Ba along the y-axis: ATDHF value 13.14124 $\hbar^2$/MeV, dynamical cranking value 13.1417 $\hbar^2$/MeV, compared to the Inglis-Belyaev value of 9.84689 $\hbar^2$/MeV.
* For $^{74}$Ge, the diagonal components of the IB and ATDHFB mass tensor stabilize with uncertainties within 1% as the numerical difference $\Delta$ decreases from 0.1 b to 0.02 b.
* The off-diagonal component of the mass tensor for $^{74}$Ge shows uncertainty around 4% when evaluated with $\Delta = 0.02$ b, with ATDHFB values ranging from 0.03144 to 0.03447 $b^{-2}$MeV$^{-1}$ depending on the sign of the numerical difference.
* The intrinsic quadrupole moments of $^{126}$Ba are $Q_{20} = 6.5945$ b and $Q_{22} = 5.5582$ b, giving $\beta = 0.18$ and $\gamma = 40.13^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the iterative ATDHFB approach previously used by Dobaczewski and Skalski (1981) for quadrupole vibrational inertia in samarium and barium nuclei, and complements the finite-amplitude method implementations by Petrik and Kortelainen (2018) and Washiyama et al. (2014). The method enables microscopic evaluation of collective inertia for rotations, surface vibrations, and fission in arbitrarily deformed superfluid nuclei without computing the computationally prohibitive two-body stability matrix.
