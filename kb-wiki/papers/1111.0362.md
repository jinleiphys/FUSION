# Study of pygmy dipole resonance with a new time-dependent mean field theory

> We carry out a systematic investigation on the low-energy electric dipole strength, which is often called pygmy dipole resonances (PDR), using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) method. The Cb-TDHFB is a new method which is derived from TDHFB with an approximation analogous to the BCS theory that the pair potential is assumed to be diagonal in the time-dependent canonical basis. We apply the method to linear-response calculation for even-even nuclei. We report the neutron-number dependence of PDR in light (A < 70) and heavy isotopes (A > 100) around N = 82.

# Study of pygmy dipole resonance with a new time-dependent mean field theory

## Key claim

The canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) method reveals that the low-lying electric dipole strength (pygmy dipole resonance) shows a characteristic neutron-number dependence, with sudden increases at neutron shell closures (N=16, N=30, N=82) that correlate with occupation of single-particle states having small orbital angular momentum, though the pairing correlation smooths these transitions in some cases.

## Method

The authors developed the Cb-TDHFB method, which approximates the full time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory by assuming the pair potential is diagonal in the time-dependent canonical basis (analogous to the BCS approximation). They applied this method to linear-response calculations for even-even nuclei by first constructing HF+BCS ground states, then applying a weak isovector dipole impulse and solving the time evolution in real time and real space on a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate grid, using the SkM* Skyrme functional for particle-hole channels and a simple pairing functional with a cut-off function.

## Key numbers

- For light nuclei (O, Ne, Mg isotopes): nuclei with N=8-14 have low-lying E1 strength fractions less than 1.0%, with a sudden jump at N=14→16
- For S, Ar, Ca isotopes: a sudden jump from N=28 to N=30 is observed in HF+RPA, while Cb-TDHFB shows smooth evolution around N=28 due to pairing
- For heavy isotopes (Sr, Zr, Mo, Ru, Pd, Cd, Sn, Te, Xe) with N=78-86: a sudden increase of the PDR ratio from N=82 to N=84 for all isotopic chains
- Stable nuclei such as 132-136Xe show 1.5% low-lying E1 strength fraction
- Cut-off energies used: ε_c = λ̃ + 5.0 MeV, e_c = ε_c + 2.3 MeV
- Mesh spacing: 0.8 fm for light isotopes (A < 70) in a sphere of radius 12 fm; 1.0 fm for heavy isotopes (A > 100) in a sphere of radius 15 fm
- Cut-off energy E_c = 10 MeV used for the ratio m₁(E_c)/m₁

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of pygmy dipole resonances using the finite amplitude method by Inakura et al. (2011), which neglected pairing correlations, by incorporating BCS-type pairing through the Cb-TDHFB approach. The method builds on prior time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations in three-dimensional coordinate space by Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2005) and addresses the practical difficulty of TDHFB calculations noted by Hashimoto and Nodeki (2007) and Avez et al. (2008). The results enable systematic investigation of pairing effects on low-energy dipole strength across isotopic chains and identify neutron shell effects as a key mechanism for PDR emergence, with open questions about the origin of constant PDR fractions in heavy nuclei with N ≤ 82.
