# The production of primordial $J/ψ$ in p+p and relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> $J/ψ$ observables of p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV are calculated using the PYTHIA event generator for the production of $c$ and $\bar c$ and the Wigner density formalism. The charm quark momentum from PYTHIA is tuned in such a way that the transverse momentum and rapidity distribution correspond to the Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations and hence to the experimental data for open charm mesons. Using the Wigner density of the charmonium wave function we calculate for each charm quark pair the probability to form a charmonium. As a result, the experimental data on the total $J/ψ$ yield, its rapidity and transverse momentum distribution as well as the fractions of feed-down from excited states of charmonium are reproduced. Applying the same approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we find, if quark gluon plasma (QGP) effects are ignored, a considerable enhancement of the primordial $J/ψ$'s.

## Key claim

The authors show that primordial J/psi production in p+p collisions can be described by projecting charm quark pair phase-space distributions from PYTHIA onto charmonium Wigner densities, but only after tuning PYTHIA to match FONLL calculations; the approach then predicts a considerable enhancement of primordial J/psi in heavy-ion collisions when quark-gluon plasma effects are ignored.

## Method

The authors use the PYTHIA event generator to produce charm quark pairs, tuning the charm quark transverse momentum and rapidity to match Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) calculations. They then apply the Wigner density formalism, projecting the relative position and momentum of each charm-anticharm pair onto the Wigner density of charmonium eigenstates (using simple harmonic oscillator wavefunctions) to compute the formation probability. For heavy-ion collisions, they use the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach with a Monte-Carlo Glauber model for charm pair production, and allow charmonium formation from charm and anticharm quarks originating from different binary nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key numbers

- Charm production cross sections: 0.8 +/- 0.21 mb at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 4.8 +/- 0.8 mb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV
- At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, charm quark transverse momentum and rapidity from PYTHIA are both reduced by 15% to match FONLL; at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, only rapidity is reduced by 9%
- RMS radii used for charmonia: 0.5 fm for J/psi (1S), 0.55 fm for chi_c (1P), and 0.9 fm for psi' (2S)
- About 16.5 charm quark pairs produced in 0-20% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; about 108 pairs in 0-20% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV
- Primordial J/psi enhancement from cross-pair formation: 46% in 0-20% central Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV; factor of 2.7 in 0-20% central Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV
- With charmonium radii increased by a factor of two to simulate QGP effects: primordial J/psi reduces to 32% in 0-20% central Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, and to 86% in 0-20% central Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends the Wigner density coalescence approach previously applied to deuteron formation (Butler 1961, Gyulassy 1982, Aichelin 1987) and to charmonium regeneration in heavy-ion collisions (Baltz 1995, Greco 2003, Song 2016) by applying it to primordial J/psi production in both p+p and heavy-ion systems. The approach differs from the Color Singlet Model (Chang 1979, Berger 1980, Baier 1983), which underestimates charmonium production by more than an order of magnitude at the Tevatron, and from the Color Evaporation Model (Fritzsch 1977, Halzen 1977), by incorporating spatial information of the charm quark pair. The results enable future microscopic studies within the PHSD approach that will include QGP interactions, charmonium dissociation, regeneration, and cold nuclear matter effects such as shadowing.
