# Influence of collective nuclear vibrations on initial state eccentricities in Pb+Pb collisions

> We study within the Monte Carlo Glauber model the influence of collective quantum effects in the Pb nucleus on the azimuthal anisotropy coefficients $ε_{2,3}$ in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies. To account for the quantum effects, we modify the sampling of the nucleon positions by applying suitable filters that guarantee that the colliding nuclei have the mean squared quadrupole and octupole moments consistent with the ones extracted from the experimental quadrupole and octupole strength functions for the Pb nucleus with the help of the energy weighted sum rule. Our Monte Carlo Glauber model with the modified sampling of the nucleon positions leads to $ε_2\{2\}/ε_3\{2\}\approx 0.8$ at centrality $\lesssim 1$\%, which allows to resolve the $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle.

## Key claim

The paper claims that modifying the Monte Carlo Glauber sampling of nucleon positions to enforce mean squared quadrupole and octupole moments consistent with experimental strength functions via the energy-weighted sum rule yields an ellipticity-to-triangularity ratio epsilon_2{2}/epsilon_3{2} approximately 0.8 at centrality less than or about 1%, which can resolve the v_2-to-v_3 puzzle. The hedge is that this result depends on the specific choice of filters and the use of n-centrality (multiplicity-based centrality) rather than b-centrality (impact-parameter-based centrality), and that the octupole moment filtering involves considerable uncertainties from experimental data.

## Method

The author used a Monte Carlo Glauber model with wounded nucleon and binary collision entropy sources, generating approximately 6 million Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. To incorporate collective nuclear vibrations, the sampling of nucleon positions was modified using smooth and sharp filters on the squared quadrupole moment Q_2^2 and squared octupole moment Q_3^2, ensuring the mean squared moments matched values extracted from experimental strength functions via the energy-weighted sum rule. Calculations were performed both with and without a meson-baryon component in the nucleon wave function, and results were obtained for n-centrality (multiplicity-based centrality) across the full centrality range.

## Key numbers

- epsilon_2{2}/epsilon_3{2} approximately 0.8 at centrality less than or about 1% for 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, obtained with quadrupole moment filtering using r_2 approximately 2.25 (the factor by which the Woods-Saxon sampling overpredicts the mean squared quadrupole moment)
- The mean squared quadrupole moment from the Woods-Saxon nuclear density is larger than the energy-weighted sum rule value by a factor r_2 approximately 2.25
- The ratio of the mean squared octupole moment predicted by the Woods-Saxon density to the true value lies within the range 0.7 less than r_3 less than 0.84
- Approximately 6 times 10^6 Pb+Pb collisions were generated, yielding about 6 times 10^4 events in the centrality region less than or about 1%
- The Gaussian width parameter for entropy sources was sigma = 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Zakharov 2021) that used only quadrupole moment filtering at zero impact parameter (b-centrality) to include octupole fluctuations and n-centrality (multiplicity-based centrality) for the full centrality range. It addresses the v_2-to-v_3 puzzle identified in hydrodynamic calculations with standard Monte Carlo Glauber and TRENTO initial conditions, which gave v_2{2}/v_3{2} approximately 1.25-1.4 (Heinz 2013, Luzum 2015), while experimental data from CMS and ALICE show v_2{2} and v_3{2} are close to each other in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions. The approach enables more realistic initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations by incorporating collective nuclear vibration effects that are ignored in the standard Woods-Saxon sampling of nucleon positions.
