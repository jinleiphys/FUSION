# Recent Progress in the Theory of Nuclear Forces

> During the past two decades, it has been demonstrated that chiral effective field theory represents a powerful tool to deal with nuclear forces in a systematic and model-independent way. Two-, three-, and four-nucleon forces have been derived up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and (partially) applied in nuclear few- and many-body systems---with, in general, a good deal of success. This may suggest that we are finally done with the nuclear force problem; but that would be too optimistic. There are still some pretty basic open issues that have been swept under rug and, finally, need our full attention, like the proper renormalization of the two-nucleon potential. Moreover, the order-by-order convergence of the many-body force contributions is at best obscure at this time.

## Key claim

Despite the widespread success of chiral effective field theory in deriving nuclear forces up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), the nuclear force problem is not solved; two major open issues remain: the proper renormalization of the two-nucleon potential and the obscure order-by-order convergence of many-body force contributions.

## Method

The authors review the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) framework for nuclear forces, where the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential is constructed order by order (LO, NLO, NNLO, N3LO) from irreducible graphs including contact terms and pion exchanges. They investigate nonperturbative renormalization by inserting the chiral NN potential into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a regulator function, examining cutoff dependence of predictions for neutron-proton elastic scattering data. For few-nucleon forces, they analyze the topological structure of three-nucleon forces (3NF) at NNLO, N3LO, and N4LO using the counting rule ν = 2 + 2L + ΣΔi.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff parameter Λ ≈ 0.5 GeV is used as a typical choice, below the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ ≈ 1 GeV.
- For NNLO chiral np potentials, χ²/datum for reproducing np elastic scattering data shows plateaus of constant low values for cutoff parameters ranging from 450 to 850 MeV in the energy range 35-125 MeV and 125-183 MeV.
- The leading 4NF at N3LO contributes approximately 0.1 MeV to the ⁴He binding energy (total ⁴He binding energy: 28.3 MeV).
- The first non-vanishing 3NF occurs at ν = 3 (NNLO), obtained with no loops (L=0) and ΣΔi = 1.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT program for nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1992) and developed by Epelbaum et al. (2003, 2005) and Machleidt and Entem (2011), who constructed high-precision NN potentials at N3LO. The paper critically examines the infinite-cutoff renormalization approach of Nogga et al. (2005) and Zeoli et al. (2012), finding it inconsistent with power counting, and instead advocates for the Lepage (1997) approach of identifying cutoff-independent plateaus below the hard scale. The unresolved Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering and the need for improved light-nucleus spectra motivate the development of subleading 3NF at N3LO and N4LO, with the N4LO contact 3NF from Girlanda et al. (2011) identified as a promising candidate for resolving these discrepancies.
