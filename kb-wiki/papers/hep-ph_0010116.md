# Implications of the ALEPH tau-Lepton Decay Data for Perturbative and Non-Perturbative QCD

> We use ALEPH data on hadronic $τ$ decays in order to calculate Euclidean coordinate space correlation functions in the vector and axial-vector channels. The linear combination $V-A$ receives no perturbative contribution and is quantitatively reproduced by the instanton liquid model. In the case of $V+A$ the instanton calculation is in good agreement with the data once perturbative corrections are included. These corrections clearly show the evolution of $α_s$. We also analyze the range of validity of the Operator Product Expansion (OPE). In the $V-A$ channel we find a dimension $d=6$ contribution which is comparable to the original SVZ estimate, but the instanton model provides a different non-singular term of the same magnitude. In the $V+A$ case both the OPE and the instanton model predict the same $d=4$ power correction induced by the gluon condensate, but it is masked by much larger perturbative contributions. We conclude that the range of validity of the OPE is limited to $x\lsim0.3$ fm, whereas the instanton model describes the data over the entire range.

## Key claim

The ALEPH tau-decay data show that the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) is only valid for Euclidean distances \(x \lesssim 0.3\) fm in the vector and axial-vector channels, whereas the instanton liquid model quantitatively reproduces the correlation functions over the entire measured range, including the \(V-A\) combination where perturbative QCD contributions cancel.

## Method

The authors used ALEPH spectral functions \(\rho_V(s)\) and \(\rho_A(s)\) from hadronic \(\tau\) decays to reconstruct Euclidean coordinate-space correlation functions \(\Pi_V(x)\) and \(\Pi_A(x)\) via a spectral representation. They compared these data with predictions from the random instanton liquid model (RILM) and with the Operator Product Expansion (OPE), fitting OPE coefficients over short-distance ranges to extract power corrections and the running coupling \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

- The instanton liquid model parameters are fixed at density \((N/V) = 1\ \text{fm}^{-4}\) and average instanton size \(\rho = 1/3\ \text{fm}\).
- In the \(V-A\) channel, the OPE fit over \(x_m = 0.3\ \text{fm}\) yields a dimension \(d=4\) term \((x/1.66\ \text{fm})^4\) and a dimension \(d=6\) term \((x/0.66\ \text{fm})^6\).
- The instanton model gives a non-singular \(d=6\) term \((x/0.64\ \text{fm})^6\) in the \(V-A\) channel, close to the OPE value.
- In the \(V+A\) channel, the OPE fit gives a \(d=4\) term \((x/1.52\ \text{fm})^4\) and a \(d=6\) term \((x/0.85\ \text{fm})^6\), with \(\alpha_s(m_\tau) \simeq 0.35\).
- The range of validity of the OPE is limited to \(x \lesssim 0.3\ \text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model of Shuryak (1982) and the earlier review of hadronic correlation functions by Shuryak (1993) by using high-statistics ALEPH data to test both the OPE and instanton predictions. It contradicts the expectation that the OPE has a useful "window" in vector channels, showing instead that instantons provide a quantitative description at all distances. The results enable improved lattice QCD comparisons and constrain the gluon condensate extraction, suggesting that the gluon condensate cannot be reliably extracted from the \(V+A\) channel due to the smallness of power corrections relative to perturbative contributions.
