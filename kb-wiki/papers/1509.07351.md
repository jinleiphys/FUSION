# Estimating $η/s$ of QCD matter at high baryon densities

> We report on the application of a cascade + viscous hydro + cascade model for heavy ion collisions in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range, $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=6.3\dots200$ GeV. By constraining model parameters to reproduce the data we find that the effective(average) value of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$ decreases from 0.2 to 0.08 when collision energy grows from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\approx7$ to 39 GeV.

# Estimating $\eta/s$ of QCD matter at high baryon densities

## Key claim
The effective (average) shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ of QCD matter decreases from 0.2 to 0.08 as collision energy increases from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\approx7$ to 39 GeV, but this $\eta/s$ is taken as independent of temperature and chemical potential within each collision energy, so the extracted values are effective rather than fundamental; the authors interpret the energy dependence as evidence that a realistic $(\eta/s)(T,\mu_B)$ should increase with baryon chemical potential $\mu_B$.

## Method
The authors apply a hybrid model consisting of a pre-equilibrium UrQMD cascade, a 3-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic stage (vHLLE) using a Chiral model equation of state, and a final hadronic UrQMD cascade. They adjust five model parameters (starting time $\tau_0$, Gaussian smearing radii $R_\perp$ and $R_\eta$, switching energy density $\epsilon_{\rm sw}$, and $\eta/s$) at each collision energy from $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ to 200 GeV to reproduce experimental data on rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow coefficients from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key numbers
- Effective $\eta/s$ decreases from 0.2 at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ GeV to 0.08 at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=39$ GeV, and remains 0.08 for $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}>39$ GeV up to 200 GeV.
- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ GeV, the model parameters are: $\tau_0=3.2$ fm/c, $R_\perp=1.4$ fm, $R_\eta=0.5$ fm, $\eta/s=0.2$.
- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=39$ GeV, the model parameters are: $\tau_0=0.9$ fm/c, $R_\perp=1.0$ fm, $R_\eta=0.7$ fm, $\eta/s=0.08$.
- The switching energy density $\epsilon_{\rm sw}=0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ is kept fixed at all collision energies.
- The default scenario uses $R_\perp=R_\eta=1.0$ fm, $\tau_0=2R/(\gamma v_z)$, $\eta/s=0$, and $\epsilon_{\rm sw}=0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$.

## Context
This work extends hybrid (cascade + viscous hydro + cascade) modeling to the lower collision energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, where baryon densities are high, complementing earlier studies at full RHIC and LHC energies that found a small $\eta/s$ near the minimum of the QCD phase diagram. The results suggest that the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio increases with baryon chemical potential, providing a constraint for future microscopic calculations of transport coefficients and for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at facilities like FAIR and NICA.
