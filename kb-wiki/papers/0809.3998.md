# Spin structure functions of 3He at finite Q^2

> Using recently derived relations between spin-dependent nuclear and nucleon g_1 and g_2 structure functions at finite Q^2, we study nuclear effects in 3He in the nucleon resonance and deep inelastic regions. Comparing the finite-Q^2 results with the standard convolution formulas obtained in the large-Q^2 limit, we find significant broadening of the effective nucleon momentum distribution functions, leading to additional suppression of the nuclear g_1 and g_2 structure functions around the resonance peaks.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that at finite $Q^2$, the effective nucleon momentum distribution functions in $^3$He acquire an explicit $Q^2$ dependence and become significantly broadened compared to the Bjorken limit, leading to additional suppression of the nuclear $g_1$ and $g_2$ structure functions around the resonance peaks, with the effect being most pronounced at lower $Q^2$ values typical of current experiments.

## Method

The authors apply the weak binding approximation to derive finite-$Q^2$ convolution formulas relating the nuclear $g_1$ and $g_2$ structure functions of $^3$He to the off-shell nucleon structure functions and spin-dependent nuclear spectral functions. They compute the nuclear structure functions using two different $^3$He spectral function models: the KPSV model (based on variational wave functions with the AV18 $NN$ potential and Urbana IX three-body force) and the SS model (based on Faddeev solutions with the Paris $NN$ potential), evaluating the effective light-cone momentum distributions $f_{ab}^\tau(y,\gamma)$ for several values of the photon velocity parameter $\gamma = |\bm{q}|/q_0$.

## Key numbers

- Neutron polarization in $^3$He: $\langle \sigma_z \rangle^n = 0.86$ (KPSV model) and $0.89$ (SS model)
- Total proton polarization in $^3$He: $\langle \sigma_z \rangle^p = -0.059$ (KPSV model) and $-0.044$ (SS model)
- Deuteron pole contribution to proton polarization: $\langle \sigma_z \rangle^{p(d)} = -0.445$ (KPSV) and $-0.453$ (SS)
- Continuum contribution to proton polarization: $\langle \sigma_z \rangle^{p(\rm cont)} = 0.386$ (KPSV) and $0.409$ (SS)
- Neutron tensor polarization: $\langle T_{zi} \sigma_i \rangle^n = 0.045$ (KPSV) and $0.038$ (SS)
- Proton tensor polarization: $\langle T_{zi} \sigma_i \rangle^p = -0.226$ (KPSV) and $-0.235$ (SS)
- $^3$He binding energy: $\ceps_{^3{\rm He}} = -7.72$ MeV; deuterium binding energy: $\ceps_d = -2.22$ MeV
- Deuteron pole provides approximately 67% (KPSV) to 68% (SS) of the spin-averaged spectral function normalization

## Context

This work extends the finite-$Q^2$ formalism previously applied to the deuteron by the same authors (Kulagin and Melnitchouk, 2007) to the $^3$He nucleus, which is commonly used as an effective neutron target in polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments. The results differ from earlier impulse approximation treatments by Ciofi degli Atti (1993), Scopetta (2003), and the SS group (1994) in the treatment of off-shell nucleon effects and higher-order terms in nucleon momentum, which become important at low $Q^2$ values (1-10 GeV$^2$) typical of current experiments. The formalism enables more reliable extraction of neutron spin structure information from polarized $^3$He data and provides a framework for studying Bloom-Gilman duality in nuclear targets.
