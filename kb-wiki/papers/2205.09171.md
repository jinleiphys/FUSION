# At the heart of baryonic matter the ARBUZ model favored by recent hadron form factor data

> Data on electromagnetic form factors of proton, neutron, and $Λ$ from annihilation and scattering reactions are collected and interpreted in the frame of a generalized picture of the internal structure of baryons which holds in space-like and time-like regions. It is shown that these data give an insight of the space structure of the baryon for distances one hundred times smaller than the baryon size and, in the time-like region, a vision of the time evolution of the hadronic matter for times up to $10^{-25}$~s, that is two orders of magnitude shorter than the time taken by the light to cross the volume of a proton. In the proposed interpretation, the electric form factor of the proton in the space-like region can not cross zero, but vanishes or stays very small, as an extrapolation of the data seems to show. In the time-like region specific structures appearing in the data give evidence of the dominance of the quark-diquark structure in a specific range of time of the evolution of the system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that recent electromagnetic form factor data for proton, neutron, and Λ baryon support the ARBUZ model, which posits a chromo-electrically neutral central region in baryons; however, the claim is hedged by the statement that the electric form factor of the proton in the space-like region "can not cross zero, but vanishes or stays very small" based on extrapolation, rather than definitively crossing zero as earlier data had suggested.

## Method

The authors collected and analyzed published data on electromagnetic form factors from elastic scattering (space-like region) and annihilation reactions (time-like region) for proton, neutron, and Λ hyperon. They reinterpreted these data in terms of a spatial scale r = ħc/√Q² for the space-like region and a time scale t = ħc/q₀ for the time-like region, comparing the observed behaviors to predictions of the ARBUZ model from Kuraev (2011). The analysis focused on the ratio of electric to magnetic form factors for the proton, the electric form factor of the neutron, and the effective form factors of all three baryons as functions of these scales.

## Key numbers

- The proton form factor ratio μₚG_{Ep}/G_{Mp} can be fitted by a monopole form with m_r² = 5.6 GeV², and approaches zero for r ≤ 0.06 fm, corresponding to the largest Q² measured by the GEp Collaboration.
- The screening region size is estimated as about 0.06 fm from a linear extrapolation of high-Q² points.
- The dipole form factor G_D = (1 + Q²/0.71 GeV²)⁻² describes the magnetic form factors of both proton and neutron when normalized to their magnetic moments.
- The time scale for nucleon form factor data ranges from (1–3)×10⁻²⁵ s, corresponding to 0.01–0.03 in units of 10⁻²³ s.
- The diquark configuration dominance occurs at time scales of (0.028–0.035)×10⁻²³ s for nucleons and (0.018–0.022)×10⁻²³ s for strange baryons.
- Two breaking points in the neutron-proton correlation occur at (F_p, F_n) ≈ (0.1, 0.085) around p_L = 1.3 GeV (q² = 4.7 GeV²) and at (F_p, F_n) = (0.18, 0.085) around p_L = 1.9 GeV (q² = 5.7 GeV²).
- For Λ-proton correlation, regime changes occur around (F_p, F_Λ) ≈ (0.025, 0.018) at p_L = 2.4 GeV (q² = 8.4 GeV²) and at (F_p, F_Λ) = (0.08, 0.018) at p_L = 5.1 GeV (q² = 14.2 GeV²).

## Context

This work extends the ARBUZ model proposed by Kuraev (2011), which was previously limited by few measurements especially in the time-like region. The analysis incorporates recent precise data from the BESIII Collaboration on proton and neutron form factors in the time-like region (BESIII 2019, BESIII 2021), as well as data from BABAR (Lees 2013) and CMD-3 (2018), and space-like data from the GEp Collaboration (Puckett 2017). The findings enable a unified description of baryon structure across both space-like and time-like regions, with predictions that can be tested by planned experiments at Jefferson Lab up to Q² = 15 GeV² (PR12-07-109).
