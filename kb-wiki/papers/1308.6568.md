# Elliptic flow from thermal photons with magnetic field in holography

> We compute the elliptic flow $v_2$ of thermal photons in a strongly coupled plasma with constant magnetic field via gauge/gravity duality. The D3/D7 embedding is applied to generate the contributions from massive quarks. By considering the cases in 2+1 flavor SYM analogous to the photon production in QGP, we obtain the thermal-photon $v_2$, which is qualitatively consistent with the direct-photon $v_2$ measured in RHIC at intermediate energy. However, due to the simplified setup, the thermal-photon $v_2$ in our model should be regarded as the upper bound for the $v_2$ generated by solely magnetic field in the strongly coupled scenario.

## Key claim

The authors compute the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of thermal photons in a strongly coupled plasma with a constant magnetic field using gauge/gravity duality, finding results qualitatively consistent with direct-photon \(v_2\) measured at RHIC at intermediate energy; however, due to the simplified static setup with constant magnetic field, the computed \(v_2\) should be regarded as an upper bound for the flow generated solely by the magnetic field in the strongly coupled scenario.

## Method

The authors employ the D3/D7 embedding in the AdS-Schwarzschild background to model a strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang-Mills plasma with a constant magnetic field along the \(z\) direction, incorporating both massless and massive quarks via the D7-brane embedding function \(\psi(u)\). They perturb the D7 branes with worldvolume U(1) gauge fields, solve the resulting Maxwell equations numerically with incoming-wave boundary conditions near the horizon, and extract the spectral functions for in-plane and out-plane photon polarizations. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) at central rapidity is then computed from the angular dependence of the spectral density integrated over the azimuthal angle \(\theta\).

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field is set to \(B_z = 1 (\pi T)^2\), corresponding to \(eB = 0.39\ \text{GeV}^2\) in the regular scheme for \(\lambda = 6\pi\) and \(T = T_{\text{QGP}} = 200\ \text{MeV}\), or \(eB = 0.12\ \text{GeV}^2\) in the alternative scheme for \(\lambda = 5.5\) and \(T = 150\ \text{MeV}\).
- The massive quark embedding uses \(\psi_0 \approx 0.95\), yielding \(m = 1.143\) from the asymptotic solution, corresponding to a bare quark mass \(M_q = 204\ \text{MeV}\) at \(T_{\text{QGP}} = 200\ \text{MeV}\) in the alternative scheme.
- The regime where thermal photons contribute substantially in QGP is \(p_T \approx 1\ \text{GeV}\) to \(4\ \text{GeV}\) at central rapidity, which rescales to \(\omega/(\pi T) \approx 1.5\) to \(6\) at \(T_{\text{QGP}} = 200\ \text{MeV}\).
- For a time-dependent magnetic field approximated by a power-law drop-off \(B(t) \sim 1/t^{1.2}\) with initial temperature \(T_i = 430\ \text{MeV}\) and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 150\ \text{MeV}\), the average magnetic field is \(B_{\text{avg}} \sim 0.1 B_0\), leading to a \(v_2\) drop of about 100 times; using the average of \((eB)^2\) gives \(B_{\text{avg}} \sim 0.18 B_0\) and a \(v_2\) drop of 25 times.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of thermal photon production with magnetic fields, such as Mamo (2012), PhysRevD.87.026005, Yee (2013), Wu (2013), and Arciniega (2013), by computing the elliptic flow \(v_2\) from both massless and massive quarks in the D3/D7 embedding. The results are qualitatively consistent with the direct-photon \(v_2\) measured at RHIC by Adare (2011) and Lohner (2012), and the resonance features from massive quarks suggest possible meson-photon transitions in the presence of a magnetic field. The work enables future studies incorporating time-dependent magnetic fields and viscous hydrodynamic contributions, as noted by Shen (2013), to construct a full description of thermal-photon \(v_2\) in heavy ion collisions.
