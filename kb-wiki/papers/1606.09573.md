# Diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities of conserved quantities in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Susceptibilities of conserved quantities, such as baryon number, strangeness and electric charge are sensitive to the onset of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase transition and are expected to provide information on the matter produced in heavy-ion collision experiments. A comprehensive study of the second-order diagonal susceptibilities and cross correlations has been made within a thermal model approach of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model as well as with a hadronic transport model, UrQMD. We perform a detailed analysis of the effect of detector acceptances and choice of particle species in the experimental measurements of the susceptibilities for heavy-ion collisions corresponding to \sNN = 4 GeV to 200 GeV. The transverse momentum cutoff dependence of suitably normalised susceptibilities are proposed as useful observables to probe the properties of the medium at freezeout.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that suitably normalized second-order susceptibilities of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) exhibit a clear mass-ordering in their transverse momentum cutoff dependence, which can serve as a robust observable to confirm the presence of a hadronic medium at freezeout, provided detector acceptance effects are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors computed second-order diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in the grand canonical ensemble and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.3) transport model for central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 4 to 200 GeV. They systematically varied the particle species sets (all hadrons, with/without Lambda baryons, and only charged pions, kaons, and protons) and the kinematic acceptance windows in pseudorapidity and transverse momentum to quantify experimental biases.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of net baryon number in the system to the total (system plus bath) for the acceptance window |eta|<0.5 stays around 0.25 at low collision energies, while for larger acceptance windows this ratio exceeds 0.5, signaling inapplicability of the grand canonical ensemble for baryon number fluctuations at low sqrt(s_NN).
- For the ratio C_BS = -3*chi^11_BS/chi^2_S, values decrease with increasing collision energy and remain constant after sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV; for C_QS = 3*chi^11_QS/chi^2_S, values increase and then remain constant after sqrt(s_NN) = 20 GeV.
- The normalized susceptibilities (normalized to their values at p_T,max = 2 GeV and eta_max = 0.5) show that chi^2_Q saturates at the smallest p_T,max, followed by chi^2_S at intermediate p_T,max, and chi^2_B at the largest p_T,max, consistent with the mass ordering of pions, kaons, and protons respectively.
- In the UrQMD model, the eta_max dependence of susceptibilities shows a peak at eta_max ~ 1-2 units, with baryonic moments peaking at larger eta_max than mesonic moments, and all moments going to zero at full phase space due to global charge conservation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of conserved charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly comparisons of experimental data from RHIC to HRG model calculations (STAR Collaboration, 2014; PHENIX Collaboration, 2014) which showed good agreement at high beam energies but discrepancies at lower energies. The paper systematically addresses experimental complications raised in earlier work by Koch and Bzdak (2013) and Gupta (2010), including the effects of limited particle identification (missing neutrons, Lambda baryons) and the strong sqrt(s_NN) dependence of baryon stopping. The proposed normalized susceptibilities as a function of p_T cutoff provide a new observable that is independent of fireball volume and can be directly compared between experiments and theory, enabling future tests of whether critical fluctuations modify the mass-ordering pattern.
