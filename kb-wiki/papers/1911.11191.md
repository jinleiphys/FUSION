# Valence structures of light and strange mesons from the basis light-front quantization framework

> We apply the basis light-front quantization framework to solve for the structures of mesons with light and strange valence quarks. Our approach treats mesons as relativistic bound states with quarks confined in both the transverse direction and the light-front longitudinal direction. The spin-orbit interactions of these confined quarks are further specified by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We address the $\mathrm{U}(1)_{\mathrm{A}}$ axial anomaly by including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft interaction regularized by our basis. We present the structures of the pion, the kaon, the eta meson, and the eta-prime meson in terms of their valence light-front wave functions obtained from the eigenvalue problem of our light-front Hamiltonian.

## Key claim
The paper extends the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) framework with Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interactions, previously applied to charged light and strange mesons, to describe the valence structures of flavor-nonet mesons including neutral pions, eta, and eta-prime mesons. The results are preliminary, obtained with a rough estimate of model parameters, and the authors expect future parameter searches to yield a consistent description.

## Method
The authors solve the light-front Schrodinger equation for meson bound states using a basis expansion of the valence light-front wave function in transverse and longitudinal basis functions that are eigenfunctions of a confining Hamiltonian H0. They supplement H0 with chiral interactions from the three-flavor NJL model, including Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant terms to address the U(1)A axial anomaly, and regularize the divergent integrals arising from reducing six-fermion to four-fermion interactions using the basis truncation parameters Nmax and Lmax. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the basis representation to obtain meson masses and wave functions, with the isospin symmetric limit applied to decompose the wave function into isospin scalar and vector components.

## Key numbers

- BLFQ result for eta-prime mass: 939 MeV (experimental value from Tanabashi (2018): 957.8 MeV)
- BLFQ mixing angle theta_8: 4.23 degrees (Feldmann (1998) value: -21.2 degrees)
- BLFQ mixing angle theta_0: 16.4 degrees (Feldmann (1998) value: -9.2 degrees)
- BLFQ decay constant f_8: 211 MeV (Feldmann (1998) value: 164.1 MeV)
- BLFQ decay constant f_0: 89.4 MeV (Feldmann (1998) value: 152.3 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to heavy quarkonia and Bc mesons by Li (2017) and Tang (2018), and to charged light and strange mesons by Jia (2018), by incorporating the NJL model with determinant interactions to handle the flavor-nonet sector including eta and eta-prime mesons. The results enable future systematic parameter searches to describe the valence structures of eta, eta-prime, omega, and phi mesons within the BLFQ-NJL model, and provide a basis-regularized treatment of multi-fermion vertex divergences that can be applied to other light-front calculations.
