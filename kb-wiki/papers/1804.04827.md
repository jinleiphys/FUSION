# On the spin-isospin decomposition of nuclear symmetry energy

> The decomposition of nuclear symmetry energy into spin and isospin components is discussed to elucidate the underlying properties of the NN bare interaction. This investigation was carried out in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory of asymmetric nuclear matter with consistent two and three body forces. It is shown the interplay among the various two body channels in terms of isospin singlet and triplet components as well as spin singlet and triplet ones. The broad range of baryon densities enables to study the effects of three body force moving from low to high densities.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy can be decomposed into spin and isospin components within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) framework, and the spin-triplet two-body channel (specifically the \(^3S_1\) partial wave) gives the largest contribution at saturation density, while at supra-saturation densities the three-body force (3BF) reinforces the spin-triplet component and causes the isospin-triplet contribution to reach 95% of the total symmetry potential energy at the highest density considered.

## Method

The authors performed BHF calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter using two realistic two-body forces (Argonne V18 and Bonn B) and their consistent meson-exchange three-body forces. They decomposed the potential energy per particle into isospin singlet (T=0) and triplet (T=1) components, further separated by spin and angular momentum partial waves (L odd/even), for both pure neutron matter (PNM) and symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) across a range of baryon densities.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (\(\rho = 0.175\) fm\(^{-3}\)) with AV18 two-body force only, the symmetry energy \(E_{sym} = 30.038\) MeV; with Bonn B only, \(E_{sym} = 30.230\) MeV.
- At \(\rho = 0.400\) fm\(^{-3}\) with AV18 two-body force only, \(E_{sym} = 50.866\) MeV; with Bonn B only, \(E_{sym} = 50.470\) MeV.
- At \(\rho = 0.175\) fm\(^{-3}\) with AV18 plus 3BF, \(E_{sym} = 32.092\) MeV; with Bonn B plus 3BF, \(E_{sym} = 33.800\) MeV.
- At \(\rho = 0.400\) fm\(^{-3}\) with AV18 plus 3BF, \(E_{sym} = 73.758\) MeV; with Bonn B plus 3BF, \(E_{sym} = 77.110\) MeV.
- At the highest density \(\rho = 0.6\) fm\(^{-3}\) with 3BF, the isospin-triplet contribution reaches 95% of the total symmetry potential energy.
- The difference in symmetry energy between the two 3BF versions is about 5%.

## Context

This work extends earlier ab initio calculations of the symmetry energy by Nakada (1993) and Bombaci (1996) by providing a detailed spin-isospin decomposition within the BHF framework. The results show that the isospin-singlet term dominates at saturation density due to the rotational invariance in isospin space of the bare NN interaction, while at supra-saturation densities the three-body force breaks this symmetry and the isospin-triplet becomes competitive. This decomposition is a preliminary step toward calculating the nuclear symmetry energy beyond the mean field approximation by including medium polarization effects.
