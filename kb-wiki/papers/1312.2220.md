# Present status of the Bethe-Salpeter approach in Minkowski space

> Recently developed methods allowing to find the solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equations in Minkowski space, both for the bound and scattering states, are reviewed. For the bound states, one obtains the bound state mass and the corresponding BS amplitude. For the scattering states, the phase shifts (complex above the meson creation threshold) and the half-off-shell BS amplitude are found. Using these solutions, the elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in solving the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation directly in Minkowski space, overcoming the singularities that had previously limited such solutions to Euclidean space; the key qualifier is that the methods work for spinless particles and are being extended to fermions, with the scattering-state solution via the Nakanishi representation still awaiting numerical implementation.

## Method

The review describes two complementary approaches: (1) the Nakanishi integral representation method, where the BS amplitude is expressed via a non-singular weight function and the equation is transformed by a light-front projection to eliminate singularities; (2) a direct treatment of singularities, where the partial-wave decomposed BS equation is reduced to a non-singular form by analytically integrating delta-function contributions from the propagator poles and applying subtraction techniques for principal-value integrals. For scattering states, the half-off-shell amplitude is computed and the phase shifts are extracted from the on-shell value.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy from the direct method coincides within four-digit accuracy with results from the Nakanishi representation method (bs1).
* BS scattering length a0 as a function of coupling constant alpha is computed for mu=0.50; the first bound state appears at alpha0=1.02 for BS versus alpha0=0.840 for the non-relativistic Yukawa potential.
* For alpha=1.2 and mu=0.50, the real part of the phase shift starts at 180 degrees (bound state present) and the imaginary part appears above the first inelastic threshold k_s^(1)=0.75, with quadratic behavior Im(delta) ~ (k_s - k_s^(1))^2 near threshold.
* On-shell amplitude for alpha=0.5, k_s=mu=0.5: F_0^on = 0.753 + i0.292, giving phase shift delta=21.2 degrees.
* Transition form factor F(Q^2) computed for bound state mass M_i=1.99 and final state masses M_f=2.01 (k_s=0.1) and M_f=2.236 (k_s=0.5), with constituent mass m=1, exchange boson mass mu=0.5, and coupling alpha=1.437.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach from its traditional Euclidean-space formulation (Wick, 1954) to Minkowski space, which is necessary because the Wick rotation cannot be applied to the integral expressions for electromagnetic form factors (ck-trento). The review builds on prior developments: the Nakanishi representation (Nak, 1963; Nak, 1971) for bound states, the on-shell scattering solutions from Tjon and from Schwartz, Morris, and Haymaker, and the recent direct method for scattering states (KC_LCM_Cracovie_2012; kc_FB20; CK_Baldin_2012; CK_PLB_2013). The results enable calculation of elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors for composite systems, and the off-shell amplitudes provide a kernel for relativistic three-body BS-Faddeev equations.
