# phi puzzle in heavy-ion collisions at 2 AGeV: how many K-from phi decays?

> The preliminary experimental data on $ϕ$ production in the reaction Ni(1.93 AGeV) + Ni point to a puzzling high $ϕ$ yield which can not be reproduced with present transport codes. We survey the experimental situation and present prospects for dedicated measurements of the $ϕ$ multiplicities with the $K^+ K^-$ and $e^+ e^-$ channels at HADES and FOPI.

## Key claim

The preliminary experimental data for Ni(1.93 AGeV) + Ni collisions show a ϕ yield and ϕ/K⁻ ratio near unity that cannot be reproduced by present transport codes, but this puzzling result may be caused by the restricted and partially disjunct phase-space regions where ϕ and K⁻ are measured.

## Method

The authors performed transport calculations using the Nantes IQMD code with newly parameterized elementary cross sections for ϕ production channels (NN, NΔ, ΔΔ, πN, πΔ) based on a one-boson exchange model adjusted to near-threshold data from DISTO. They compared the calculated ϕ yields and phase-space distributions to preliminary FOPI data and simulated expected count rates for future measurements at HADES and FOPI using an isotropic source parametrization with Teff = 70 MeV.

## Key numbers

- FOPI collected about 4.7 × 10⁶ central events (10% σtot, ⟨Apart⟩ = 90) in Ni(1.93 AGeV) + Ni
- The ϕ production probability was initially estimated as 4 × 10⁻⁴ with Teff = 125 MeV, later revised to larger values with Teff ≈ 70 MeV
- The preliminary ϕ/K⁻ ratio is about 0.1 from initial analyses, later increased to approximately unity (order of 1)
- The pp → ppϕ cross section parametrization: 5.78 μb · ([√s − 2m_n − m_ϕ]/1 GeV)^1.309
- The pn → pnϕ cross section parametrization: 41.3 μb · ([√s − 2m_n − m_ϕ]/1 GeV)^1.438
- The π⁺n → pϕ cross section parametrization: 101 μb · ([√s − m_n − m_ϕ]/1 GeV)^0.466
- Maximum ϕ count rates per day for C(2.0 AGeV) + C: 30 (HADES, e⁺e⁻ channel, 18° ≤ Θ_e ≤ 88°), 5 (HADES, K⁺K⁻ channel, 44° ≤ Θ_K ≤ 88°, p_K < 1 GeV/c), 160 (HADES, K⁺K⁻ channel, 18° ≤ Θ_K ≤ 88°, p_K < 1 GeV/c), 50 (FOPI, K⁺K⁻ channel, combined detector acceptances)

## Context

This work extends earlier transport calculations by Ko and collaborators (1990s) using a BUU approach that included NN, NΔ, ΔΔ, πN, πΔ, and K⁻K⁻ → ϕX channels, and responds to the DISTO measurements of near-threshold pp → ppϕ. The authors note that HSD transport calculations by Cassing (2000) give ϕ/K⁻ ≈ 1 at 1.5 AGeV for central Au+Au but drop below unity at 1.9 AGeV. The paper enables future dedicated ϕ measurements at HADES and FOPI at SIS/GSI Darmstadt, particularly to resolve whether the high ϕ/K⁻ ratio is real or an artifact of phase-space coverage.
