# Meson Mass at Large Baryon Chemical Potential in Dense QCD

> We reexamine the quark mass induced term in chiral Lagrangian in color-flavor locking phase in dense QCD, and show that the meson mass term is determined by three independent invariants under chiral-axial symmetry, and a meson mass is given in terms of the quark mass, gap, and the chemical potential by $m_π^2\sim m_q^2Δ\barΔ/μ^2\ln(μ^2/Δ^2)$. Thus mesons become massless as $μ\to\infty$.

# Meson Mass at Large Baryon Chemical Potential in Dense QCD

## Key claim
The meson mass in the color-flavor locking phase of dense QCD is gap-dependent and scales as $m_\pi^2 \sim m_q^2 \Delta \bar{\Delta} / \mu^2 \ln(\mu^2 / \Delta^2)$, contrary to previous claims that it is independent of the gap. Mesons become massless only at asymptotically large chemical potential, but remain massive at next-to-leading order even when two quarks become massless.

## Method
The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian for mesons in the color-flavor locking phase, including quarks and Nambu-Goldstone bosons, and integrate out quarks in a constant background of the Goldstone fields to obtain the quark-mass-induced meson mass term. They perform a one-loop calculation of the quark vacuum energy with two quark mass insertions, evaluating the resulting integrals near the Fermi surface to determine the coefficients of the three independent chiral-axial invariants that appear in the mass term.

## Key numbers
- Meson mass scaling: $m_\pi^2 \sim m_q^2 \Delta \bar{\Delta} / \mu^2 \ln(\mu^2 / \Delta^2)$, where $\Delta$ and $\bar{\Delta}$ are the particle and antiparticle gaps, $m_q$ is the quark mass, and $\mu$ is the baryon chemical potential.
- Coefficients of the invariants: $A \sim B \sim \Delta \bar{\Delta} \ln(\mu^2 / \Delta^2)$ and $C \sim (\Delta^2 \bar{\Delta}^2 / \mu^2) \ln(\mu^2 / \Delta^2)$.
- Pion decay constant: $F_\pi \sim \mu$, in agreement with previous calculations.
- Next-to-leading order meson mass: $m_\pi^2 \sim m_q^2 \Delta^2 \bar{\Delta}^2 / \mu^4 \ln(\mu^2 / \Delta^2)$, which remains nonzero even if two quarks become massless.
- Meson velocity: $v = 1/\sqrt{3}$, as previously calculated by Son and Stephanov (1999) and Rho (1999).

## Context
This work reexamines the meson mass in the color-flavor locking phase of dense QCD, originally proposed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998). It contradicts the earlier result of Son and Stephanov (1999), who found $m_\pi^2 \sim m_q^2$ independent of the gap, and the work of Gatto (1999), which contained a non-analytic term in the quark mass that the authors argue cannot appear. The paper shows that three independent chiral-axial invariants determine the mass term, not a single one as previously claimed, and that the leading term vanishes only when two quarks become massless, with a subleading term surviving. This result is relevant for understanding the spectrum of low-lying excitations in neutron star interiors.
