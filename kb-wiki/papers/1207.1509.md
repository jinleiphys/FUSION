# Three-body forces and proton-rich nuclei

> We present the first study of three-nucleon (3N) forces for proton-rich nuclei along the N=8 and N=20 isotones. Our results for the ground-state energies and proton separation energies are in very good agreement with experiment where available, and with the empirical isobaric multiplet mass equation. We predict the spectra for all N=8 and N=20 isotones to the proton dripline, which agree well with experiment for 18Ne, 19Na, 20Mg and 42Ti. In all other cases, we provide first predictions based on nuclear forces. Our results are also very promising for studying isospin symmetry breaking in medium-mass nuclei based on chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first study of three-nucleon (3N) forces for proton-rich medium-mass nuclei, showing that 3N forces provide repulsive contributions that improve agreement with experiment, but the location of the proton dripline remains uncertain for the N=8 isotones because the two-proton decay candidate 22Si could be either bound or unbound within theoretical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for N=8 and N=20 isotones using chiral effective field theory interactions, with NN forces at N3LO evolved to a low-momentum interaction via renormalization-group methods and 3N forces included at N2LO. The 3N couplings were fit to the 3H binding energy and 4He radius without further adjustments. Valence-space interactions were derived using many-body perturbation theory to third order in spaces of 13 major harmonic-oscillator shells, including normal-ordered one- and two-body parts of 3N forces in 5 major shells.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energies of N=8 isotones from 18Ne to 24S: NN-only calculations show systematic overbinding that becomes more pronounced with larger mass number; NN+3N calculations in the extended sdfp valence space give 22Si bound by only 0.1 MeV relative to 20Mg (compared to 1.6 MeV in the sd-shell)
- One-proton separation energy Sp of 22Si: calculated as 0.90 MeV (sd-shell) and 1.71 MeV (sdfp space), compared to IMME value of 1.35 MeV
- Two-proton separation energy S2p of 22Si: calculated as -1.63 MeV (sd-shell) and -0.12 MeV (sdfp space), compared to IMME value of 0.01 MeV
- For N=20 isotones, the proton dripline is predicted at 46Fe in all calculations
- Two-proton separation energy S2p of 48Ni: calculated as -2.73 MeV (pf-shell) and -1.02 MeV (pfg space), compared to experimental value of -1.28(6) MeV
- Excitation energy differences between proton-rich N=8 nuclei and mirror oxygen isotopes are less than 0.8 MeV; for N=20 isotones, differences with mirror calcium isotopes are smaller than 0.3 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model studies of 3N forces in neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes (Holt 2010, Gallant 2012) to the proton-rich side of the nuclear chart, where 3N forces had not been previously explored. The results enable future studies of isospin symmetry breaking in medium-mass nuclei based on chiral effective field theory interactions, and provide predictions for the unexplored spectra of N=8 and N=20 isotones to the proton dripline. The calculations are compared with the empirical isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) and with experimental data from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2011.
