# Bayesian inference of strange star equation of state using the GW170817 and GW190425 data

> The observations of compact star inspirals from LIGO/Virgo provide a valuable tool to study the highly uncertain equation of state (EOS) of dense matter at the densities in which the compact stars reside. It is not clear whether the merging stars are neutron stars or quark stars containing self-bound quark matter. In this work, we explore the allowed bag-model-like EOSs by assuming the merging stars are strange quark stars (SQSs) from a Bayesian analysis employing the tidal deformability observational data of the GW170817 and GW190425 binary mergers. We consider two extreme states of strange quark matter, either in nonsuperfluid or color-flavor locked (CFL) and find the results in these two cases essentially reconcile. In particular, our results indicate that the sound speed in the SQS matter is approximately a constant close to the conformal limit of $c/\sqrt{3}$. The universal relations between the mass, the tidal deformability and the compactness are provided for the SQSs. The most probable values of the maximum mass are found to be $M_{\rm TOV}=2.10_{-0.12}^{+0.12}~(2.15_{-0.14}^{+0.16})\,M_{\odot}$ for normal (CFL) SQSs at a $90\%$ confidence level. The corresponding radius and tidal deformability for a $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ star are $R_{\rm 1.4}= 11.50_{-0.55}^{+0.52}~({11.42}_{-0.44}^{+0.52})~\rm km$ and $Λ_{1.4}= {650}_{-190}^{+230}~({630}_{-150}^{+220})$, respectively. We also investigate the possibility of GW190814's secondary component $m_2$ of mass $2.59_{-0.09}^{+0.08}\,M_{\odot}$ being an SQS, and find that it could be a CFL SQS with the pairing gap $Δ$ larger than $244~\rm MeV$ and the effective bag parameter $B_{\rm eff}^{1/4}$ in the range of $170$ to $192$ MeV, at a $90\%$ confidence level.

## Key claim

Assuming the merging compact stars in GW170817 and GW190425 are strange quark stars (SQSs) rather than neutron stars, the sound speed in strange quark matter is approximately constant and close to the conformal limit of c/√3, in contrast to the rapid growth seen in neutron star matter; the results for normal (nonsuperfluid) and color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter are essentially reconciled.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference using the tidal deformability observational data from the GW170817 and GW190425 binary mergers, treating both events as SQS-SQS mergers. They employed a bag-model-like equation of state (EOS) characterized by the effective bag constant B_eff, the QCD correction parameter a_4, and the CFL pairing gap Δ, with two extreme cases considered: normal nonsuperfluid strange quark matter (Δ=0) and CFL quark matter. The analysis used the IMRPhenomD_NRTidal waveform model, interpolated likelihood tables that marginalized over all parameters except masses and tidal deformabilities, and the Bilby and PyMultiNest packages for nested sampling.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass (TOV): M_TOV = 2.10_{-0.12}^{+0.12} M⊙ for normal SQSs and 2.15_{-0.14}^{+0.16} M⊙ for CFL SQSs, at 90% confidence level.
- Radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star: R_1.4 = 11.50_{-0.55}^{+0.52} km (normal) and 11.42_{-0.44}^{+0.52} km (CFL), at 90% confidence level.
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 M⊙ star: Λ_1.4 = 650_{-190}^{+230} (normal) and 630_{-150}^{+220} (CFL), at 90% confidence level.
- Effective bag parameter: B_eff^{1/4} ≈ 137.6_{-4.4}^{+4.8} MeV (normal) and 144.3_{-7.9}^{+5.0} MeV (CFL), at 90% confidence level.
- QCD correction parameter: a_4 ≈ 0.70_{-0.12}^{+0.16} (normal) and 0.74_{-0.20}^{+0.22} (CFL), at 90% confidence level.
- For GW190814's secondary component (mass 2.59_{-0.09}^{+0.08} M⊙) as a CFL SQS: Δ > 244 MeV and B_eff^{1/4} in the range 170 to 192 MeV, at 90% confidence level; radius R_2.6 between 11.35 km and 13.67 km; tidal deformability Λ_2.6 between 4.6 and 26.3.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of gravitational-wave data that assumed neutron star EOSs (LIGO/Virgo 2018, 2019) by instead assuming the merging objects are self-bound strange quark stars, updating the parameter space for quark star EOSs. The results contradict the common finding from neutron star studies that a sound speed substantially exceeding the conformal limit is needed to reach two-solar-mass stars (Bedaque and Steiner 2015, Tews et al. 2018), showing that early stiffening of the EOS can produce heavy stars without superconformal sound speed. The universal relations provided for SQS mass, tidal deformability, and compactness enable future identification of quark stars, and the analysis of GW190814's secondary component as a possible CFL quark star builds on the work of Roupas et al. (2021).
