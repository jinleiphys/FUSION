# QCD with Two Light Dynamical Chirally Improved Quarks

> Results for the excited meson and baryon spectrum with two flavors of Chirally Improved sea quarks are presented. We simulate several ensembles with pion masses ranging from 250 to 600 MeV and extrapolate to the physical pion mass. Strange quarks are treated within the partially quenched approximation. Using the variational method, we investigate the content of the states. Among others, we discuss the flavor singlet/octet content of Lambda states. In general, our results compare well with experiment, in particular we get very good agreement with the Lambda(1405) and confirm its flavor singlet nature.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD results for the excited meson and baryon spectrum using two flavors of Chirally Improved sea quarks, claiming good agreement with experiment for the Λ(1405) and confirming its flavor singlet nature. However, the results are obtained in finite volume and extrapolated to the physical pion mass using a linear fit in the pion mass squared, with the caveat that some energy levels (such as the first excitation in the nucleon 1/2⁺ channel) deviate from experiment, possibly due to weak overlap of the interpolators with the physical states.

## Method

The authors use the Chirally Improved fermion action (an approximate solution to the Ginsparg-Wilson equation) with tadpole-improved Lüscher-Weisz gauge action, simulating two light dynamical sea quarks and treating the strange quark in the partially quenched approximation. They generate seven ensembles with pion masses from 255 to 596 MeV on 16³×32 lattices (with additional 12³×24 and 24³×48 lattices for two ensembles), determine energy levels via the variational method using large sets of quark-antiquark and three-quark interpolators, and extrapolate results to the physical pion mass linearly in mπ².

## Key numbers

- Pion mass range: 255 to 596 MeV across seven ensembles
- Lattice size: 16³×32, with additional 12³×24 and 24³×48 lattices for two ensembles
- Lattice spacing: between 0.1324 and 0.1398 fm
- Sommer parameter r₀ = 0.48 fm used to set the lattice spacing
- For the Λ(1405) ground state: mixing of 15-20% with octet interpolators (dominance of singlet)
- Linear box size L ≈ 2.2 fm for ensemble A66 (mπ ≈ 255 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies by Engel et al. (2011, 2012, 2013) and continues the approach of Engel et al. (2010) with more ensembles, larger statistics, and more observables. It uses the variational method as developed by Lüscher and Wolff (1990) and Michael (1985), and relates finite-volume energy levels to resonance physics via the Lüscher method (1990, 1991). The results enable future lattice studies to investigate states where the current interpolator basis shows weak overlap (e.g., the Roper resonance and Λ(1600)), and motivate the inclusion of multi-hadron interpolators to better capture meson-meson and meson-baryon components.
