# Long-Range Rapidity Correlations in Heavy-Light Ion Collisions

> We study two-particle long-range rapidity correlations arising in the early stages of heavy ion collisions in the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework, assuming for simplicity that one colliding nucleus is much larger than the other. We calculate the two-gluon production cross section while including all-order saturation effects in the heavy nucleus with the lowest-order rescattering in the lighter nucleus. We find four types of correlations in the two-gluon production cross section: (i) geometric correlations, (ii) HBT correlations accompanied by a back-to-back maximum, (iii) away-side correlations, and (iv) near-side azimuthal correlations which are long-range in rapidity. The geometric correlations (i) are due to the fact that nucleons are correlated by simply being confined within the same nucleus and may lead to long-range rapidity correlations for the produced particles without strong azimuthal angle dependence. Somewhat surprisingly, long-range rapidity correlations (iii) and (iv) have exactly the same amplitudes along with azimuthal and rapidity shapes: one centered around Δϕ=π with the other one centered around Δϕ=0 (here Δϕ is the azimuthal angle between the two produced gluons). We thus observe that the early-time CGC dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions generates azimuthal non-flow correlations which are qualitatively different from jet correlations by being long-range in rapidity. If strong enough, they have the potential of mimicking the elliptic (and higher-order even-harmonic) flow in the di-hadron correlators: one may need to take them into account in the experimental determination of the flow observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that early-time Color Glass Condensate dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions generates azimuthal non-flow correlations that are long-range in rapidity, which could potentially mimic elliptic and higher-order even-harmonic flow in di-hadron correlators and must be accounted for in experimental flow measurements.

## Method

The authors calculate the two-gluon production cross section in heavy-light ion collisions within the saturation/CGC framework, assuming one nucleus is much larger than the other (A1 << A2) so that all-order saturation effects are included only in the larger nucleus while lowest-order rescattering is kept in the lighter nucleus. They use light-cone perturbation theory to construct gluon wave functions and describe interactions with the target nucleus via Wilson lines along the light cone, which incorporate both Glauber-Mueller multiple rescatterings and BK/JIMWLK evolution. The two-gluon correlation function is then derived from the ratio of double- to single-gluon production cross sections, with the nuclear geometry encoded through profile functions of the projectile nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The calculation assumes 1 << A1 << A2, with as^2 A2^(1/3) ~ 1 and as^2 A1^(1/3) << 1, where as is the strong coupling constant
- Gluon production is considered for transverse momentum kT >= Qs1, where Qs1 is the saturation scale of the smaller nucleus and Qs2 >> Qs1 >> Lambda_QCD
- The classical gluon field description is valid over rapidity intervals up to Delta y less than or similar to 1/as
- The near-side (Delta phi = 0) and away-side (Delta phi = pi) long-range rapidity correlations have exactly the same amplitudes and azimuthal shapes, such that the correlator expands into a Fourier series containing only even harmonics cos(2n Delta phi)

## Context

This work extends earlier CGC-based studies of the "ridge" correlation by Dumitru et al. (2008), Dusling et al. (2009), and Dumitru et al. (2010) by analytically including saturation effects in the two-gluon correlation function beyond lowest order. The paper also identifies a new "geometric correlation" arising simply from nucleons being confined within the same nucleus, which is not specific to the CGC framework and should be present in any model of AA collisions that properly accounts for collision geometry. The results enable more precise calculations of non-flow correlations that must be subtracted when extracting true hydrodynamic flow observables v_{2n} from experimental data.
