# Continuous evolution of electromagnetic field in heavy-ion collisions

> In heavy-ion collisions the electromagnetic field exists before the hot nuclear matter emergence. Requiring the field continuity we compute it in the central rapidity region by taking into account the electromagnetic response of the Quark Gluon Plasma. We show that the electromagnetic field is nearly time-independent from about 1~fm/c after the collision until the freezeout.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic field in the central rapidity region of heavy-ion collisions becomes nearly time-independent from about 1 fm/c after the collision until freezeout, provided the field continuity is enforced by matching the pre-collision vacuum field to the field inside the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) at the emergence time \(t_0\).

## Method

The authors solve the Maxwell equations for the electromagnetic potential in the laboratory frame, using a Green's function method that incorporates the electrical conductivity \(\sigma\) of the QGP. The total field is split into a "valence" part sourced by the continuing currents of the undeflected valence quarks and an "initial" part arising from the continuity conditions at the medium emergence time \(t_0\). The initial part is computed numerically at early times and matched to an analytical late-time formula; the valence part is computed numerically at all times. The point-charge results are then integrated over the Woods-Saxon nuclear density to obtain the field for Au-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The electrical conductivity of QGP is set to \(\sigma = 5.8\) MeV.
- For Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the transverse electromagnetic field becomes nearly time-independent after \(t \sim 0.5\) fm/c.
- For Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 66.5\) GeV, the transverse electromagnetic field becomes nearly time-independent after \(t \sim 1\) fm/c.
- The transition from pulse-dominated to wake-dominated field occurs around time \(4/\sigma \sim 10^2\) fm, which is an order of magnitude longer than the plasma lifetime.

## Context

This work extends the model proposed by Tuchin (2015) that ascribes an effective electrical conductivity to the nuclear medium at the earliest times, allowing Maxwell-equation solutions to be extended from \(t=t_0\) onward. It contradicts the expectation of a rapidly decaying field by showing that the sum of the valence and initial contributions yields a nearly constant total field in the QGP. The results enable more reliable modeling of electromagnetic-field effects on QGP observables, such as those studied by Roy (2017), Pu (2016), and Gursoy (2014, 2018), by providing a realistic time-independent field profile for the plasma lifetime.
