# Neutrinoless double beta decay from lattice QCD

> While the discovery of non-zero neutrino masses is one of the most important accomplishments by physicists in the past century, it is still unknown how and in what form these masses arise. Lepton number-violating neutrinoless double beta decay is a natural consequence of Majorana neutrinos and many BSM theories, and many experimental efforts are involved in the search for these processes. Understanding how neutrinoless double beta decay would manifest in nuclear environments is key for understanding any observed signals. In these proceedings we present an overview of a set of one- and two-body matrix elements relevant for experimental searches for neutrinoless double beta decay, describe the role of lattice QCD calculations, and present preliminary lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary lattice QCD results for short-range four-quark operator matrix elements relevant to neutrinoless double beta decay, but emphasizes that these are early-stage calculations that have not yet been renormalized, so the absolute scale of discretization effects remains unknown.

## Method

The authors compute pion-to-pion transition matrix elements for five four-quark operators (three color-contracted and two color-mixed) using a mixed-action lattice setup with HISQ gauge configurations from the MILC collaboration and Möbius domain wall valence quarks. Pion blocks are created at source and sink times, fully contracted with the operator at a single spacetime point, allowing exact momentum projection without all-to-all propagators. The calculation uses multiple lattice spacings (0.09, 0.12, 0.15 fm), volumes, and pion masses including the physical pion mass, with gradient flow smearing and both wall and point sources to assess excited state contamination.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses studied: 310 MeV, 220 MeV, and 135 MeV (physical pion mass)
* Lattice spacings used: 0.09 fm, 0.12 fm, and 0.15 fm
* On the physical pion mass ensemble (m_pi ~ 135 MeV, a = 0.12 fm, L = 48), the operator O_{3+}^{++} is roughly two orders of magnitude smaller than the other operators
* The current bound on the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life is about 10^25 years
* In the seesaw mechanism estimate: M_D ~ 200 GeV, m_l ~ 0.05 eV, m_h ~ M_R ~ 10^15 GeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD methodology previously applied to K^0-, D^0-, and B^0-meson mixing (reviewed by Aoki, 2016) and to n-nbar oscillations (Buchoff, 2012) to the neutrinoless double beta decay problem. The calculations target the short-range operator contributions identified in chiral effective theory by Prezeau (2003), which are essentially unknown from other approaches. The results will enable quantitative comparison of short-range versus long-range contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay, and ultimately allow experimental constraints to be connected to beyond-Standard-Model theories such as left-right symmetric models and R-parity violating supersymmetry.
