# Jastrow functions in double-beta decay

> We use simple analytic considerations and a Monte Carlo calculation of nucleons in a box to argue that the use of Jastrow functions as short-range correlators in the commonly employed two-body-cluster approximation causes significant errors in the matrix elements for double-beta decay. The Jastrow approach appears to agree with others, however, if many-body clusters are included. A careful treatment of the charge-changing analog of the nuclear pair density shows, in addition, that differences between Unitary Correlator Operator Method and Brueckner methods for treating short-range correlations in double-beta are less significant than suggested by previous work.

## Key claim

The use of Jastrow functions in the two-body-cluster approximation suppresses short-range contributions to double-beta decay matrix elements too much, but this problem is fixed by including many-body correlations; the Jastrow approach agrees with other methods when many-body clusters are included, and differences between Unitary Correlator Operator Method and Brueckner methods for treating short-range correlations are less significant than previously thought.

## Method

The authors used analytic considerations and a quantum Monte Carlo calculation of 2 protons and 16 neutrons in a cubic box with side L=4.85 fm and periodic boundary conditions, at nuclear-matter density. They evaluated the charge-changing two-body density P_F(r) and the corresponding double-beta decay matrix element contributions C_F(r) using Jastrow correlators in both the two-body-cluster approximation and with full many-body correlations included. They also compared these results with Brueckner-based calculations and reanalyzed the UCOM prescription for defining distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The integral of P_F(r) (the charge-changing two-body density) using the two-body Jastrow function from Akmal et al. (1997) gave 0.006, while the Miller-Spencer Jastrow function gave 0.0075, both nonzero despite the requirement that the integral vanish due to isospin conservation
- The cubic box had side L=4.85 fm, containing 2 protons and 16 neutrons in the initial state decaying to 4 protons and 14 neutrons
- The Miller-Spencer Jastrow function has the form f(r) = 1 - e^(-1.1r^2)(1 - 0.68r^2) with r in fm
- Corrections to the two-body-cluster approximation are barely noticeable below about r=0.7 fm but become large at r≈1 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of short-range correlations in double-beta decay beyond the traditional phenomenological Jastrow approach of Miller and Spencer (1976). It addresses discrepancies between the Jastrow method and more recent treatments using the Unitary-Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) by Kortelainen et al. (2007) and Brueckner-based methods by Engel et al. (2009) and Simkovic et al. (2009). The paper enables a reconciliation of these different approaches by showing that many-body correlations restore agreement, and clarifies that apparent differences between UCOM and Brueckner results were largely due to different definitions of the distribution functions.
