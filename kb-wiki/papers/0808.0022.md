# Parton transport and hadronization from the dynamical quasiparticle point of view

> The hadronization of an expanding partonic fireball is studied within the Parton-Hadron-Strings Dynamics (PHSD) approach which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. Apart from strong parton interactions the expansion and development of collective flow is driven by strong gradients in the parton mean-fields. An analysis of the elliptic flow $v_2$ demonstrates a linear correlation with the spatial eccentricity $ε$ as in case of ideal hydrodynamics. The hadronization occurs by quark-antiquark fusion or 3 quark/3 antiquark recombination which is described by covariant transition rates. Since the dynamical quarks become very massive, the formed resonant 'pre-hadronic' color-dipole states ($q\bar{q}$ or $qqq$) are of high invariant mass, too, and sequentially decay to the groundstate meson and baryon octets increasing the total entropy. This solves the entropy problem in hadronization in a natural way. The resulting particle ratios turn out to be in line with those from a grandcanonical partition function at temperature $T \approx 170$ MeV rather independent from the initial temperature and indicate an approximate strangeness equilibration.

## Key claim

The hadronization process in the Parton-Hadron-Strings Dynamics (PHSD) approach solves the entropy problem inherent in coalescence models because the dynamical quarks are very massive, so the formed resonant pre-hadronic color-dipole states have high invariant mass and sequentially decay to groundstate meson and baryon octets, increasing total entropy rather than decreasing it.

## Method

The authors model an expanding partonic fireball initialized at temperature T = 1.7 Tc (Tc = 0.185 GeV) using the PHSD approach, which combines the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM) for partonic degrees of freedom with off-shell transport equations. Hadronization is implemented via covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion to mesons and three-quark fusion to baryons, where the formed pre-hadronic states are treated as resonant color-dipoles that decay via the JETSET algorithm. The parton dynamics include elastic and inelastic interactions (qq, gg, qg scattering, as well as qqbar ↔ g and gg ↔ g processes) with cross sections determined from the DQPM, and the expansion is driven by gradients in repulsive parton mean-fields.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of the expanding partonic fireball: T = 1.7 Tc, with Tc = 0.185 GeV
- Total energy Etot conserved within 3% throughout the partonic expansion and hadronization phase
- Hadronization proceeds mainly during the time interval 1 fm/c < t < 7 fm/c
- The Gaussian width for meson pre-hadron phase-space distribution: sqrt(<r^2>) = 0.66 fm (in the rest frame)
- The Gaussian width for baryon phase-space distribution: 1 fm in coordinate space
- gg scattering cross sections up to 20 mb at parton density ρp ≈ 2.5 fm^-3
- Final particle ratios from PHSD: p/π+ = 0.086, Λ/K+ = 0.28, K+/π+ = 0.157
- Statistical model at T = 170 MeV gives: p/π+ = 0.086, Λ/K+ = 0.26, K+/π+ = 0.180
- Ratio v2/ε is practically constant at ≈ 0.2, as in ideal hydrodynamics
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in DQPM: η/s ≈ 0.2
- Total entropy increases by about 15% during hadronization

## Context

This work extends the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM) developed by Cassing (2006, 2007) and Andre (2005) by incorporating it into a full off-shell transport framework (PHSD) that dynamically describes the transition from partonic to hadronic matter. The approach contradicts simple coalescence models (Koal1, Koal2; AMPT) that reduce entropy during hadronization, instead producing particle ratios compatible with the statistical hadronization model (PBM, PBM2; Anton) at T ≈ 170 MeV. The results enable future studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, where the PHSD approach can be applied to realistic initial conditions to test predictions for elliptic flow, baryon-to-meson ratios at high pT, and strangeness equilibration.
