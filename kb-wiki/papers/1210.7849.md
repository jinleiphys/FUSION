# Spontaneous Chiral Symmetry Breaking as Condensation of Dynamical Chirality

> The occurrence of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (SChSB) is equivalent to sufficient abundance of Dirac near-zeromodes. However, dynamical mechanism leading to breakdown of chiral symmetry should be naturally reflected in chiral properties of the modes. Here we offer such connection, presenting evidence that SChSB in QCD proceeds via the appearance of modes exhibiting dynamical tendency for local chiral polarization. These modes form a band of finite width Lambda_ch (chiral polarization scale) around the surface of otherwise anti--polarized Dirac sea, and condense. Lambda_ch characterizes the dynamics of the breaking phenomenon and can be converted to a quark mass scale, thus offering conceptual means to determine which quarks of nature are governed by broken chiral dynamics. It is proposed that, within the context of SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental Dirac quarks, mode condensation is equivalent to chiral polarization. This makes Lambda_ch an "order parameter" of SChSB, albeit without local dynamical field representation away from chiral limit. Several uses of these features, both at zero and finite temperature, are discussed. Our initial estimates are Lambda_ch~150 MeV (N_f=0), Lambda_ch~80 MeV (N_f=2+1, physical point), and that the strange quark is too heavy to be crucially influenced by broken chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (SChSB) in QCD is equivalent to the condensation of Dirac modes that exhibit a dynamical tendency for local chiral polarization, and that this condensation is synonymous with chiral polarization in SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental Dirac quarks. The authors present this as a conjecture rather than a proven result, with the hedge that the precise value of the chiral polarization scale depends on regularization details and may require a finite normalization procedure to become unique.

## Method

The authors analyzed low-lying eigenmodes of the overlap Dirac operator (with parameters r=1, rho=26/19) on lattice QCD configurations, computing the correlation coefficient of chiral polarization C_A(lambda) and the spectral chiral polarization density rho_ch(lambda) as functions of eigenvalue lambda. For quenched QCD, they used Wilson lattice gauge theory at beta=6.054 (a=0.085 fm) on symmetric N^4 lattices with N=16,20,24,32 and 100 configurations each. For dynamical QCD, they used 32^3 x 64 domain wall ensembles from RBC/UKQCD with three light bare masses (m_l a = 0.008, 0.006, 0.004) and fixed heavy mass m_h a = 0.03, computing overlap eigenmodes on 50 configurations per ensemble. Finite temperature studies used 20^3 x N_t lattices at T/T_c = 0.84 (N_t=10) and T/T_c = 1.39 (N_t=6) with 200 and 100 configurations respectively.

## Key numbers

- Chiral polarization scale in quenched QCD (N_f=0): Lambda_ch approximately 150 MeV in the continuum limit, with the lattice calculation at a=0.085 fm giving Lambda_ch approximately 160 MeV in infinite volume
- Chiral polarization scale in N_f=2+1 QCD at physical point: Lambda_ch approximately 80 MeV, with the lattice calculation at a=0.085 fm giving Lambda_ch approximately 86 MeV in the massless limit
- Light quark masses studied correspond to pion masses m_pi = 397, 350, and 295 MeV for m_l a = 0.008, 0.006, and 0.004 respectively
- Finite temperature study at T/T_c = 0.84 shows mode condensation with positive chiral polarization, while at T/T_c = 1.39 mode condensation is absent with strictly negative C_A(lambda)
- The strange quark is stated to be too heavy to be crucially influenced by broken chiral symmetry

## Context

This work extends the local chirality approach of Horvath (2001) and builds on the dynamical polarization measures constructed by Alexandru and Horvath (2010), where the chiral polarization scale was originally denoted Lambda_T. The paper proposes that the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1980), which links Dirac near-zeromode accumulation to chiral symmetry breaking, does not reveal dynamical specifics of SChSB, and the authors aim to fill this gap by connecting mode condensation to chiral polarization. The findings enable a new way to characterize whether quarks are "light" or "heavy" based on the chiral polarization mass scale m_ch, and provide a framework for studying strong dynamics at finite temperature and for judging the applicability of chiral perturbation theory.
