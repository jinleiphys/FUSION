# Comparison of potential models with the pp scattering data below 350 MeV

> We calculate the chi**2 of various NN potential models with respect to the pp scattering data. We find that only the potential models which were explicitly fitted to the pp data give a reasonable description of these data. Most models give a pretty large chi**2 on the very low-energy pp data, due to incorrect 1S0 phase shifts.

## Key claim

Only potential models explicitly fitted to the pp data (Nijm78, Paris80, Bonn89) give a reasonable description of those data; most models produce a large chi-squared on the very low-energy pp data because their 1S0 phase shifts are incorrect, even when the models claim to fit the scattering length and effective range.

## Method

The authors used the Nijmegen representation of the chi-squared hypersurface of the pp scattering data, obtained from single-energy analyses at 10 energies between 382.54 keV and 320 MeV, to compute the chi-squared of each NN potential model. For each model, phase shifts were calculated by solving the radial Schrodinger equation including the Coulomb interaction, vacuum polarization, and two-photon-exchange modifications for the 1S0 partial wave; the Bonn momentum-space potential was an exception, using phase shifts provided by the authors with adjustments for low-energy electromagnetic effects. The chi-squared was then computed from the deviation of the model phase shifts from the single-energy phase shifts, weighted by the inverse error matrices.

## Key numbers

- The Nijmegen single-energy analyses of 1787 pp scattering data below 350 MeV gave a total chi-squared of 1676.3.
- The multi-energy analysis reached chi-squared = 1786.4; the representation via Eq. (1) gave chi-squared(model) = 1786.8, a difference of only 0.4 (0.02%).
- For the Nijm78 potential, direct comparison with data gave chi-squared(data) = 3387.5, while the representation gave chi-squared(model) = 3462.8, a 2% difference.
- The NijmRdl model achieved chi-squared per data point near 1, comparable to the partial-wave analysis.
- The Arg84 potential in the 2-350 MeV range gave chi-squared/N_data = 7.1; replacing its 1S0 phase shifts with multi-energy values reduced chi-squared/N_data to about 4.
- The coordinate-space Bonn A and Bonn B potentials gave chi-squared/N_data > 8 in the 2-350 MeV range.

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of NN potential models initiated by the Nijmegen group, using the accurate database and partial-wave analysis of Bergervoet et al. (1990). It contradicts the common assumption that models fitted to np data (e.g., Argonne, Urbana, coordinate-space Bonn) automatically describe pp data, and it demonstrates that only models explicitly fitted to pp data (Nijm78, Paris80, Bonn89) achieve chi-squared/N_data near 2. The results enable future work to use the high-quality NijmRdl model (chi-squared/N_data near 1) as a benchmark for few-nucleon and nuclear-matter calculations, and caution against drawing conclusions about three-nucleon forces from models that cannot adequately describe two-nucleon scattering.
