# $η$ production off the proton in a Regge-plus-chiral quark approach

> A chiral constituent quark model approach, embodying s- and u-channel exchanges,complemented with a Reggeized treatment for t-channel is presented. A model is obtained allowing data for $π^- p \to ηn$ and $γp \to ηp$ to be describe satisfactorily. For the latter reaction, recently released data by CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS Collaborations in the system total energy range $1.6 \lesssim W \lesssim 2.8$ GeV are well reproduced due to the inclusion of Reggeized trajectories instead of simple $ρ$ and $ω$ poles. Contribution from "missing" resonances is found to be negligible in the considered processes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) combined with a Reggeized treatment of t-channel exchanges can satisfactorily describe both π⁻p → ηn and γp → ηp data up to W ≈ 2.8 GeV, but the model's success depends on replacing simple ρ and ω poles with Regge trajectories; the contribution from "missing" resonances with masses below 2 GeV is found to be negligible.

## Method

The authors combine a chiral constituent quark model for s- and u-channel exchanges with a Reggeized treatment for t-channel vector meson (ρ and ω) exchanges, substituting the standard meson propagator with a Regge propagator using linear trajectories α_ρ(t)=0.55+0.8t and α_ω(t)=0.44+0.9t. They simultaneously fit 3887 experimental data points for γp → ηp (differential cross sections and polarized beam asymmetries) and π⁻p → ηn (differential cross sections) using the CERN MINUIT code with 19 free parameters, including masses and widths of 12 known nucleon resonances with M ≲ 2 GeV and one additional S₁₁ resonance.

## Key numbers

- Total energy range covered: 1.6 ≲ W ≲ 2.8 GeV for γp → ηp data from CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS Collaborations
- 3887 experimental values fitted simultaneously
- 19 free parameters in the fit
- Extracted quark mass m_q = 310 ± 5 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator strength α_ho = 309 ± 2 MeV
- QCD coupling constant α_s = 1.60 ± 0.02
- g_ηNN = 0.276 ± 0.005
- S₁₁(1535) extracted mass: 1532 ± 1 MeV; extracted width: 140 ± 1 MeV
- New S₁₁ extracted mass: 1700 ± 1 MeV; extracted width: 473 ± 10 MeV
- g_ρqq = 1.90 ± 0.22; κ_ρqq = -0.20 ± 0.01
- g_ωqq = 4.88 ± 0.16; κ_ωqq = -0.26 ± 0.02
- Total χ²/dp = 2.40 for all fitted data
- χ²/dp for γp → ηp differential cross sections: MAMI94 = 1.84, CLAS02 = 4.90, CLAS09 = 2.40, ELSA05 = 1.98, ELSA09 = 2.98, LNS06 = 1.74, GRAAL07 = 1.29
- χ²/dp for π⁻p → ηn differential cross sections: Prakhov et al. = 0.47, Deinet et al. = 1.59, Richards et al. = 1.91, Debenham et al. = 0.70, Brown et al. = 1.57
- u-channel contribution maximum ≈ 8% around W ≈ 1.65 GeV, vanishing above W ≈ 1.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous χCQM studies by He and Saghai (2009) and He et al. (2008a) that focused on W below 2 GeV with a degenerate treatment of higher-shell resonances, replacing that approximation with a Reggeized t-channel that mimics higher mass resonances (M > 2 GeV). The approach follows the MAID Group (Chiang et al., 2003) who showed Reggeized models are more appropriate than simple ρ and ω poles, and builds on successful Regge-plus-effective-Lagrangian treatments for π (Guidal et al., 1997; Sibirtsev et al., 2007-2009), η (Chiang et al., 2003), and kaon photoproduction (Guidal et al., 2003; Corthals et al., 2006-2007; Vancraeyveld et al., 2009-2010). The work enables combined analysis of hadronic and electromagnetic η production up to higher energies and provides a framework for investigating the role of individual resonances, with the new S₁₁(1700) emerging as the second most important ingredient after S₁₁(1535).
