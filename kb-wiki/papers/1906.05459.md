# Do Delta Baryons Play a Role in Neutron Stars?

> The presence of exotic hadrons, such as hyperons and $Δ$ isobars, in the dense nuclear matter in their cores has been shown to produce important changes in the properties of neutron stars. Within the quark-meson coupling model, we show that the many-body forces generated by the change in the internal quark structure of the baryons in the strong scalar mean fields generated in dense nuclear matter prohibit the appearance of $Δ$ isobars.

# Do Delta Baryons Play a Role in Neutron Stars?

## Key claim
Within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the many-body forces arising from changes in the internal quark structure of baryons in dense nuclear matter prohibit the appearance of Δ isobars in neutron stars at any density relevant to neutron star physics. The key physical mechanism is the in-medium enhancement of the hyperfine interaction between non-strange quarks, which increases the Δ–nucleon mass splitting by almost 100 MeV already at twice nuclear matter density.

## Method
The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which starts from a quark model (MIT bag model) for baryon structure and solves self-consistently for the changes in baryon structure induced by strong scalar mean fields in dense nuclear matter. They compute the chemical potential of the Δ⁻ baryon under β-equilibrium conditions and compare it to the sum μ_n + μ_e, which must be equal for the Δ⁻ to appear. The coupling constants for σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons are fixed to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy, symmetry energy, and its slope), and the couplings to all other baryons are then determined with no additional parameters.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is taken as ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Nuclear matter properties at saturation: binding energy per nucleon ε = 15.8 MeV, symmetry energy S = 30 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L₀ = 63 MeV.
- The Δ–nucleon mass splitting is enhanced by almost 100 MeV already at twice nuclear matter density (2ρ₀).
- The meson coupling constants used: G_σ = 10.0 fm², G_δ = 3.0 fm², G_ω = 6.4 fm², G_ρ = 4.27 fm².
- The difference μ_Δ⁻ − (μ_n + μ_e) grows with baryon density, with no crossing point at any density.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QMC model studies of hyperons in neutron stars (Rikovska-Stone 2006, Motta 2019), which showed that Σ hyperons are absent in neutron stars due to the same in-medium hyperfine interaction mechanism. The result contradicts many other studies (Li 2018, Li 2019, Cai 2015, Drago 2014, Chen 2009) that predict Δ⁻ appearance at lower critical densities, typically with more parameters. The finding is testable against future gravitational wave data (GW170817) and the NICER mission's precision radius measurements of neutron stars.
