# Microscopic study of low-lying states in odd-mass nuclei for atomic electric dipole moment searches

> We present a microscopic study of the low-lying states of five odd-mass nuclei of particular interest for experimental searches of atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs): $^{129}$Xe, $^{199}$Hg, $^{225}$Ra, $^{229}$Th, and $^{229}$Pa. The analysis is performed within the recently developed multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT), which incorporates symmetry restoration and configuration mixing based on self-consistent mean-field solutions. The calculated energy spectra and electromagnetic observables of these nuclei are reasonably well reproduced without introducing any parameters beyond those of the underlying universal relativistic energy density functional. The results demonstrate the reliability of MR-CDFT in describing the structure of these nuclei and in providing essential input on nuclear Schiff moments relevant to ongoing EDM searches.

## Key claim

The multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) can reproduce the low-lying energy spectra and electromagnetic observables of five odd-mass nuclei relevant to atomic electric dipole moment searches without introducing any parameters beyond those of the underlying universal relativistic energy density functional, thereby providing reliable nuclear Schiff moment input for ongoing EDM experiments.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) calculations for five odd-mass nuclei (129Xe, 199Hg, 225Ra, 229Th, and 229Pa), using the PC-PK1 parameterization of the relativistic energy density functional. The wave functions were constructed as configuration mixing of one-quasiparticle states with different quadrupole (β2) and octupole (β3) deformations, with symmetry restoration via angular-momentum and particle-number projection, and the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation was solved to obtain the low-lying states.

## Key numbers

- 129Xe: predicted ground-state magnetic moment μ(1/2+) = -0.785 μN, compared to experimental value -0.778 μN; spectroscopic quadrupole moment Qs(3/2+) = -40.1 e·fm2, compared to experimental -39.3(10) e·fm2; Qs(11/2-) = 58.5 e·fm2, compared to experimental 63(2) e·fm2
- 199Hg: energies of global energy-minimum configurations for Jπ=1/2-, 3/2-, and 5/2- states with K=J are -172.333, -172.156, and -172.087 MeV, respectively
- 129Xe: mean-field energy minimum at β2 ≈ 0.16, β3 = 0; after projection, Jπ=1/2+ state minimum at β2 ≈ -0.08 with octupole deformation β3 ≈ 0.1, and Jπ=3/2+ state minimum at β2 ≈ 0.10 with β3 ≈ 0.1
- 129Xe: magnetic moment of 11/2- state calculated as μ(11/2-) = -0.78 μN for ν9/2[514] configuration, compared to experimental -0.891223(4) μN; for ν11/2[505] configuration the value is -0.42 μN

## Context

This work extends the MR-CDFT framework developed by Zhou and Yao (2024) to odd-mass nuclei of experimental interest for atomic EDM searches, providing the first beyond-mean-field description of these systems using a universal energy density functional without adjustable parameters. The results enable improved calculations of nuclear Schiff moments, which are essential for interpreting EDM experiments, and build upon prior studies using the particle-rotor model (Meyer-Ter-Vehn, 1975), Skyrme Hartree-Fock approaches (Dobaczewski, 2005), random-phase approximation (Ban, 2010), and valence-space shell models (Yanase, 2020). The approach also allows direct comparison with experimental electromagnetic observables, which can help constrain theoretical uncertainties in Schiff moment predictions.
