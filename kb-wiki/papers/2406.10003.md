# Steady state, displacement current and spin polarization for massless fermion in a shear flow

> We consider spin polarization of massless fermions in a shear flow, whose complete contributions contain magnetization current and side-jump current known from collisional chiral kinetic theory. We argue that the side-jump current adopts interpretation of displacement current. We explicitly determine the displacement current contribution in the steady state reached in shear flow for a QED plasma. We find the displacement contribution enhances the magnetization contribution at small and large momenta, but leads to a suppression effect at intermediate momenta. Major differences from previous studies on collisional effect are: (i) the fermions are in the same steady state as the medium rather than being probes; (ii) Compton scattering and pair annihilation are also included in addition to the Coulomb scattering considered before.

## Key claim

The side-jump current in collisional chiral kinetic theory can be reinterpreted as a displacement current, and in a shear flow the displacement contribution enhances the magnetization contribution at small and large momenta but suppresses it at intermediate momenta, provided the fermions are part of the medium (not probes) and Compton scattering and pair annihilation are included alongside Coulomb scattering.

## Method

The authors use collisional quantum kinetic theory for massless Dirac fermions in a neutral QED plasma with a shear flow, working in the leading logarithmic approximation where only elastic scatterings (Coulomb, Compton, and pair annihilation) are relevant. They solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for the spin-averaged distribution function to obtain the steady-state deviation from local equilibrium, then compute the axial vector component of the Wigner function to extract the spin polarization from the magnetization current (gradient term) and displacement current (self-energy term). The displacement current is identified with the side-jump current by averaging the side-jump vector over the collision kernel.

## Key numbers

* For large momentum \(p \gg T\), the deviation functions for fermions and photons are \(\chi_p = \frac{(2\pi)^3}{e^4 \ln e^{-1}} \beta^2 p^2 C_f\) and \(\gamma_p = \frac{(2\pi)^3}{e^4 \ln e^{-1}} \beta^2 p^2 \left( \frac{1+f_{\gamma,p}}{1-f_p}(C_b - C_f) + C_f \right)\), with \(C_f = \frac{3(1+2N_f)}{4\pi^2 N_f^2}\) and \(C_b - C_f = \frac{2}{\pi^2 N_f}\).
* In the large \(N_f\) limit, \(C_f \simeq \frac{3}{2\pi^2 N_f}\) and \(C_b \simeq \frac{7}{2\pi^2 N_f}\).
* The conducting current contribution (spin carried by individual particles) vanishes identically for massless fermions in the fluid rest frame because no pseudoscalar can be constructed from the shear tensor and momentum alone.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for massive probe fermions in a QED medium (Lin 2022), which found collisional contributions parametrically equal to kinematic ones but with a suppression effect. The present paper improves on that by treating the fermions as part of the medium (not probes) and including Compton scattering and pair annihilation in addition to the Coulomb scattering considered before. The results are relevant for understanding local spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, where shear flow contributions have been identified as a main source alongside vorticity (Fu 2021, Becattini 2021, Yi 2021), and where recent STAR data (2023) show deviations from predictions based on vorticity and shear combined.
