# Charmonium-nucleon femtoscopic correlation function

> This study investigates the femtoscopic correlation functions of charmonium-nucleon pairs, utilizing the lattice QCD phase shifts provided by the HAL QCD Collaboration. A ``model-independent'' formalism is employed to transform scattering phase shifts directly into momentum correlation functions, thereby circumventing the approximations inherent in traditional methods, such as the Lednický-Lyuboshits model. The $J/ψ$-$p$ correlation functions, including spin-averaged and partial-wave results, are predicted using near-physical pion mass lattice results. The $η_c$-$p$ correlation function is calculated for the first time. The derived correlation functions provide critical references for future experiments, such as those at the LHC, where high-precision measurements of charmonium-nucleon correlations could unveil valuable insights into non-perturbative QCD dynamics.

# Charmonium-nucleon femtoscopic correlation function

## Key claim
The authors predict that the $J/\psi$-$p$ and $\eta_c$-$p$ femtoscopic correlation functions can be computed directly from lattice QCD phase shifts using a "model-independent" formalism, bypassing the approximations of the Lednický-Lyuboshits model. However, the formalism still depends on specific treatments of the source function and scattering wave function (e.g., $S$-wave approximation, coupled-channel effects, and off-shell effects), so the "model-independent" label refers strictly to the relation between correlation function and phase shift, not to the overall calculation.

## Method
The authors employ the Koonin-Pratt formula to relate the femtoscopic correlation function to the scattering wave function, then derive an expression that connects the correlation function directly to the scattering phase shifts via two auxiliary functions $\mathcal{F}_1$ and $\mathcal{F}_2$ that depend only on the source size and kinematics. They input the $J/\psi$-$N$ and $\eta_c$-$N$ phase shifts from the HAL QCD Collaboration's lattice simulations at nearly physical pion mass ($m_\pi \approx 146$ MeV) for the $^2S_{1/2}$ and $^4S_{3/2}$ partial waves, assuming a single Gaussian source profile and neglecting coupled-channel effects based on heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key numbers
- $J/\psi$-$N$ scattering lengths from HAL QCD: $a_{^4S_{3/2}} = -0.30(2)(^{+0}_{-2})$ fm and $a_{^2S_{1/2}} = -0.38(4)(^{+0}_{-3})$ fm
- $\eta_c$-$N$ scattering parameters from HAL QCD: $a_0 = -0.21$ fm and $r_{\rm eff} = 3.65$ fm
- Phase shifts plateau at approximately $14^\circ$, $11^\circ$, and $9^\circ$ for $J/\psi$-$N$ $^2S_{1/2}$, $^4S_{3/2}$, and $\eta_c$-$N$ $^2S_{1/2}$ partial waves, respectively, beyond $E_{\rm c.m.} \approx 30$ MeV
- For $R = 0.75$ fm, the deviation between the LL model and the phase-shift formalism reaches $0.07$ in the low-momentum region for $\eta_c$-$p$
- The soft-gluon exchange mechanism yields a scattering length $< -0.16$ fm, while the coupled-channel mechanism gives results between $-10\times10^{-3}$ and $-0.1\times10^{-3}$ fm

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic studies of charmonium-nucleon interactions beyond earlier calculations by Krein et al. (2020, 2022, 2023), which used the Lednický-Lyuboshits model and older lattice results. It directly utilizes the latest HAL QCD lattice simulations by Lyu et al. (2024) at nearly physical pion mass, and applies the recently developed "model-independent" formalism by Liu et al. (2024) that converts phase shifts to correlation functions without the approximations inherent in the LL model. The predictions provide direct references for future measurements at the LHC, particularly with the upgraded ALICE detector in runs 3, 4, and 5, where high-precision $J/\psi$-$p$ and $\eta_c$-$p$ correlation measurements could probe non-perturbative QCD dynamics and the gluonic van der Waals interaction between charmonia and nucleons.
