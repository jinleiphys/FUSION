# Inner fission barriers of uranium isotopes in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The inner fission barriers of the even-even uranium isotopes from the proton to the neutron drip line are studied with the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum. A periodic evolution for the ground state shapes is shown with the neutron number, i.e., spherical shapes at shell closures $N=$126, 184, 258, and prolate dominated shapes between them. In analogy to the shape evolution, the inner fission barriers also exhibit a periodic behavior: peaks at the shell closures and valleys in the mid-shells. The triaxial effect to the inner fission barrier is evaluated using the triaxial relativistic mean field calculations plus a simple BCS method for pairing. With the triaxial correction included, good consistency in the inner barrier heights is found with the available empirical data. Besides, the evolution from the proton to the neutron drip line is in accord with the results by the multi-dimensionally constrained relativistic mean field theory. A flat valley in the fission barrier height is predicted around the neutron-rich nucleus $^{318}$U which may play a role of fission recycling in the astrophysical $r$-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The inner fission barriers of even-even uranium isotopes exhibit a periodic behavior with neutron number, peaking at shell closures (N=126, 184, 258) and forming valleys in mid-shell regions, but the quantitative barrier heights from axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) calculations are systematically about 2 MeV higher than empirical data; only after including a triaxial correction via a simpler relativistic mean field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) model does the theory achieve good consistency with available empirical barrier heights.

## Method

The authors performed constrained DRHBc calculations using the PC-PK1 density functional to obtain potential energy curves (PECs) for even-even uranium isotopes from the proton drip line (²¹²U) to the neutron drip line (³⁵⁰U), extracting inner fission barrier heights as the energy difference between the ground state and the top of the first barrier. To evaluate the triaxial effect, which the axially symmetric DRHBc cannot treat, they carried out separate triaxial RMF+BCS calculations in a harmonic oscillator basis with the same PC-PK1 functional, computing the reduction ΔB_f = B_f^{BCS}(triaxial) − B_f^{BCS}(axial) and adding this correction to the DRHBc barrier heights.

## Key numbers

- Ground state deformations β₂ for ²³⁰,²³²,²³⁴,²³⁶,²³⁸U from DRHBc: 0.246, 0.258, 0.276, 0.290, 0.288; empirical values from B(E2) data: 0.260, 0.264, 0.266, 0.274, 0.274.
- Inner fission barrier heights from axially symmetric DRHBc show peaks ≈20 MeV at shell closures N=126, 184, 258; minimum of 2.44 MeV at ³¹⁸U.
- Triaxial correction −ΔB_f for ²³⁸U is 1.87 MeV, bringing the DRHBc barrier into agreement with the empirical value.
- Triaxial correction −ΔB_f reaches as large as 2.5 MeV for ²¹⁴U and is nearly zero for ³²⁶U.
- After triaxial correction, the barrier height at ³¹⁸U is 1.9 MeV, forming a flat valley.
- For ³²⁶U, the prolate and oblate minima differ by 0.05 MeV with a barrier of 2.18 MeV between them, indicating possible shape coexistence.

## Context

This work extends the application of the DRHBc theory, previously used for masses, radii, and halo phenomena, to fission barriers for the first time, covering the full range of uranium isotopes from proton to neutron drip line. The results are compared with prior calculations from the macroscopic-microscopic FRLDM (Moller 2009), ETFSI (Mamdouh 2001), HFB-14 (Goriely 2009), and multi-dimensionally constrained RMF (Deng 2023) models. The predicted flat valley around ³¹⁸U with a barrier of 1.9 MeV suggests a possible role for fission recycling in astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis, motivating future dynamical studies of fission in neutron-rich environments.
