# Muon Charge Information from Geomagnetic Deviation in Inclined Extensive Air Showers

> We propose to extract the charge information of high energy muons in very inclined extensive air showers by analyzing their relative lateral positions in the shower transverse plane. We calculate the muon lateral deviation under the geomagnetic field and compare it to dispersive deviations from other causes. By our criterion of resolvability, positive and negative muons with energies above $10^4$ GeV will be clearly separated into two lobes if the shower zenith angle is larger than $70^\circ$. Thus we suggest a possible approach to measure the $μ^+ / μ^-$ ratio for high energy muons.

## Key claim

The authors propose that positive and negative muons with energies above 10^4 GeV in extensive air showers with zenith angles larger than 70° can be distinguished by their opposite geomagnetic deviations, provided the separation between the two charge lobes exceeds twice the attenuation radius of the background lateral distribution. The claim is conditional on the shower being sufficiently inclined and on the muon energy being high enough that the geomagnetic separation dominates over dispersive effects from transverse momentum and multiple scattering.

## Method

The authors developed a revised Heitler model incorporating energy-dependent charged-pion multiplicity and interaction length, fitted to QGSJet-II simulations, to derive the relation between muon production height and energy. They calculated muon lateral deviations in the shower transverse plane from three independent sources: transverse momentum (Gaussian distribution with mean transverse momentum ~0.5 GeV), multiple Coulomb scattering (using the Gaussian approximation with radiation length 36.7 g/cm^2 in air), and geomagnetic bending (assuming a perpendicular field component of 40 μT). The condition for resolvability was defined as the separation between positive and negative muon centers exceeding twice the combined standard deviation of the background distributions.

## Key numbers

- Muon energies above 10^4 GeV are required for clear charge separation when the shower zenith angle is larger than 70°.
- The critical zenith angle is 70°, at which the separation s equals 2σ for muon energies above ~10^3.5 GeV (approximately 3,162 GeV).
- The optimum zenith range for the approach is 75° ≤ θ ≤ 85°, which contributes over 17% of the half total solid angle.
- The geomagnetic separation between positive and negative muon centers is given by s = 12 (L/km)^2 / (E/GeV) meters, assuming B_perp = 40 μT.
- The muon energy spectrum follows a power law D_μ ∝ E^γ with index γ ≈ -3 for showers with primary energy E_0 = 10^11 GeV and zenith angle θ = 80°.
- The probability of pion decay at energies above 10^4 GeV is very small, calculated as P(E) = (115/E) × (145 - 10.5 lg E) / (X(E) cosθ).

## Context

This work extends the Heitler model approach of Matthews (2005) by incorporating energy-dependent multiplicity and interaction length to describe high-energy muon production in inclined air showers. It builds on prior studies of geomagnetic effects on muon propagation by Ave (2000) and Hansen (2004), which showed double-lobed muon lateral distributions in very inclined showers but did not focus on the high-energy muon charge separation. The proposed method enables a possible measurement of the μ⁺/μ⁻ ratio for muons above 10^4 GeV, which could help discern primary cosmic-ray composition and test hadronic interaction models at ultra-high energies.
