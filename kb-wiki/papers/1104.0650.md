# Event-by-event shape and flow fluctuations of relativistic heavy-ion collision fireballs

> Heavy-ion collisions create deformed quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fireballs which explode anisotropically. The viscosity of the fireball matter determines its ability to convert the initial spatial deformation into momentum anisotropies that can be measured in the final hadron spectra. A quantitatively precise empirical extraction of the QGP viscosity thus requires a good understanding of the initial fireball deformation. This deformation fluctuates from event to event, and so does the finally observed momentum anisotropy. We present a harmonic decomposition of the initial fluctuations in shape and orientation of the fireball and perform event-by-event ideal fluid dynamical simulations to extract the resulting fluctuations in the magnitude and direction of the corresponding harmonic components of the final anisotropic flow at midrapidity. The final harmonic flow coefficients are found to depend non-linearly on the initial harmonic eccentricity coefficients. We show that, on average, initial density fluctuations suppress the buildup of elliptic flow relative to what one obtains from a smooth initial profile of the same eccentricity, and discuss implications for the phenomenological extraction of the QGP shear viscosity from experimental elliptic flow data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that initial density fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions suppress the buildup of elliptic flow relative to what would be obtained from a smooth initial profile with the same eccentricity, implying that phenomenological extractions of QGP shear viscosity from elliptic flow data must account for event-by-event fluctuations rather than relying on single-shot hydrodynamic evolution of averaged initial conditions.

## Method

The authors generated fluctuating initial conditions for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions using Monte Carlo versions of the Glauber and fKLN models, then performed event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional ideal fluid dynamical simulations using the VISH2+1 code to evolve each event separately. They defined harmonic decomposition coefficients for both initial spatial eccentricities (using r^2-weighted and r^n-weighted moments) and final anisotropic flow coefficients, and analyzed the correlations between initial eccentricity coefficients and final flow coefficients across different centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- For the MC-Glauber model at b=2.2 fm (0-5% centrality), the average participant ellipticity ⟨ε_part⟩ is approximately 0.05, while the standard eccentricity ε_s is approximately 0.01
- The hard fraction parameter δ=0.14 was used for the two-component (soft+hard) MC-Glauber model
- Kinetic decoupling temperature T_dec=140 MeV was used for the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem=165 MeV was used for the equation of state s95p-PCE
- The centrality table lists impact parameter ranges from b_min=0.0 fm to b_max=14.3 fm, with average participant numbers ranging from N_part=352.2 (0-5%) to N_part=4.4 (80-90%)
- 10,000 initial profiles were generated for each impact parameter (30,000 for b=0, 1, and 2 fm) and for each centrality bin

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow and its relation to initial eccentricity by Song (2008, 2010, 2011), Hirano (2009, 2010), and others who treated event-by-event fluctuations only on average by propagating smooth initial profiles. It directly tests the assumption of linear hydrodynamic response (v_2 proportional to ε_x) used by Bhalerao (2006) and Drescher (2007), and addresses the recently discovered fact by Alver (2010) that initial-state shape fluctuations simultaneously generate all higher-order harmonic eccentricity coefficients. The results enable more quantitatively precise extraction of QGP shear viscosity from experimental elliptic flow data by accounting for the nonlinear dependence of final harmonic flow coefficients on initial harmonic eccentricity coefficients.
