# Shedding light on charmonium

> We investigate E1 radiative transitions within charmonium in a relativistic approach based on light-front QCD. In quantum field theory, two sets of processes are pure E1: $χ_{c0} \to J/ψγ$ ($ψ\to χ_{c0}γ$) and $h_c \to η_cγ$ ($η_c' \to h_cγ$), both involving the $P$-wave charmonia. We compute the E1 radiative decay widths as well as the corresponding transition form factors of various processes including those involving $2P$ states. These observables provide an access to the microscopic structures of the $P$-wave charmonium. We show that our parameter-free predictions are in excellent agreement with the experimental measurements as well as lattice simulations whenever available.

## Key claim
The authors claim that their parameter-free predictions for E1 radiative decay widths and transition form factors in charmonium, computed using light-front wave functions from basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), are in excellent agreement with experimental measurements and lattice simulations. However, this agreement is conditional on the assumption that the charmonium states are pure \(c\bar c\) configurations, and any significant deviation from experiment would imply a deviation from the conventional quark-model picture.

## Method
The authors compute E1 radiative transition amplitudes and form factors using light-front wave functions (LFWFs) obtained from basis light-front quantization (BLFQ), with the charm quark mass \(m_c\) and basis scale \(\kappa\) fitted to the charmonium mass spectrum. They adopt the Drell-Yan frame (\(q^+ = 0\)) and the "good current" \(J^+\) to extract the E1 form factor \(E_1(Q^2)\) from the hadronic matrix element, then calculate decay widths proportional to \(|E_1(0)|^2\). The calculations include transitions between \(P\)-wave scalar charmonia (\(\chi_{c0}\)) and vector charmonia (\(\psi\)), as well as between \(C\)-odd axial vector (\(h_c\)) and pseudoscalar (\(\eta_c\)) states, including \(2P\) states.

## Key numbers
- \(\Gamma_{\chi_{c0} \to J/\psi \gamma}\): BLFQ prediction compared with PDG values and lattice QCD results (see Fig. 1 in paper).
- \(\Gamma_{\psi(2S) \to \chi_{c0} \gamma}\): BLFQ prediction compared with experimental measurements from CLEO and BESIII.
- \(\Gamma_{\psi(3770) \to \chi_{c0} \gamma}\): BLFQ prediction compared with experimental data.
- \(\Gamma_{h_c \to \eta_c(1S) \gamma}\): BLFQ prediction compared with BESIII and CLEO measurements.
- Product \(\Gamma(R \to \psi' \gamma) \Gamma(R\to\gamma\gamma)/\Gamma_\text{total} = 9.8(3.6)(1.3) \,\mathrm{eV}\) from Belle (2021) for a resonance at mass 3.922 GeV, identified as \(\chi_{c0}(3915)\) or \(\chi_{c2}(3930)\).
- UV scale of BLFQ LFWFs: \(\Lambda_\text{uv} = \kappa \sqrt{N_\text{max}} = 2.8\,\mathrm{GeV}\) for \(N_\text{max}=8\).
- Model uncertainty estimated as difference between \(N_\text{max}=8\) and \(N_\text{max}=16\) results.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous parameter-free predictions for two-photon widths (Li 2021) and M1 widths (Li 2018) in charmonium using the same BLFQ framework. It addresses discrepancies between nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) and experiment, such as the \(7\sigma\) deviation in the two-photon decay width at NNLO (Feng 2017). The results provide benchmarks for interpreting charmonium-like states like \(\chi_{c0}(3915)\) and \(\chi_{c1}(3872)\), and enable future studies of gluon distributions, generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and hadronic anomalous energy at proposed electron-ion colliders.
