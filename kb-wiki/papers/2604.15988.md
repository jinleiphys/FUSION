# Testing Scalar Field Self-Dualities in d=2 using a Variational Method

> Recently, self-dualities based on saddle-point expansions have been proposed as a means to obtain qualitative non-perturbative information in scalar field theories. In this work, we test this proposition quantitatively by studying the phase transition for critical scalar $ϕ^4$ field theory in 1+1 dimensions using a variational method. We find that saddle-point methods obtain quantitative agreement for the free energy, but differ on the order of 25 percent for the peak location of the correlation length.

# Testing Scalar Field Self-Dualities in d=2 using a Variational Method

## Key claim

Saddle-point self-duality methods achieve quantitative agreement with variational results for the free energy of critical scalar φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions, but differ by approximately 25 percent for the peak location of the correlation length, and by approximately 6 percent for the continuum-extrapolated critical coupling.

## Method

The authors discretize scalar φ⁴ theory on a lattice with D points along one spatial Euclidean direction, treating the time direction as continuous. They construct a variational method using truncated sets of Hermite function basis states (parity-even and parity-odd sectors) to diagonalize the Hamiltonian for small transverse lattice sizes (D ≤ 7). They compare the resulting energy eigenvalues and correlation lengths to analytic saddle-point expansions in the R1-level resummation scheme from Romatschke (2026), evaluating both methods on identical lattice configurations.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling from continuum lattice field theory (Schaich 2009): g_c ≡ λ/m²_crit ≃ 2.7⁺⁰·⁰²⁵₋₀·₀₁₂₅
- Continuum result from saddle-point method: g_c ≃ 2.5527 (approximately 6 percent below the Schaich 2009 result)
- For D=5, λa²=0.05: variational method gives m²_{B,crit}a² = -0.514 (K=16k basis states); saddle-point method gives m²_{B,crit}a² = -0.408
- For D=5, λa²=0.2: variational method gives m²_{B,crit}a² = -1.954 (K=16k); saddle-point method gives -1.22
- For D=5, λa²=0.01: variational method gives m²_{B,crit}a² = -0.141 (K=16k); saddle-point method gives -0.118
- Ground state energy of pure quartic oscillator (quantum mechanics warm-up): Ê₀ ≃ 0.667986; variational method with K=2 gives 0.668668 (relative error 10⁻³), with K=5 gives 0.667987 (relative error 10⁻⁶)
- Other continuum results cited for comparison: g_c = 2.7637(35) from lattice Monte Carlo (Bronzin 2018); g_c = 2.807(34) from perturbative methods (Serone 2018); g_c = 2.728(14) from tensor networks (Kadoh 2018); g_c = 2.76(3) from Hamiltonian truncation (Elias-Miro 2017); g_c ≃ 2.765 from matrix product states (Vanhecke 2019)

## Context

This work tests the quantitative performance of analytic saddle-point self-dualities (Romatschke 2026) against a variational method applied to the well-studied critical φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions, which has known critical properties from lattice field theory (Schaich 2009, Loinaz 1997). The variational approach extends techniques from quantum mechanical quartic oscillators (Bender 1977, Hioe 1978) to field theory on small transverse lattices. The results validate the saddle-point method for free energy but reveal 10-25 percent discrepancies for derivative quantities like correlation length, establishing reliability bounds for applying these self-dualities to higher-dimensional scalar field theories.
