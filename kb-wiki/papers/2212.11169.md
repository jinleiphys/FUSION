# Odderon as Regge oddball spin-3 in $pp$ and $p\bar p$ elastic scattering

> In this work, we propose that the odderon is a Regge odd-glueball tensor spin-3. To demonstrate our proposal, we study the $pp$ and $p\bar p$ elastic scattering by including contributions of the spin-3 odderon and spin-2 pomeron exchange in the processes. The phenomenological effective Lagrangian approach is used to calculate the $pp$ and $p\bar p$ elastic scattering amplitudes at the tree level. In addition, a Donnachie-Landschoff ansatz of the odderon and pomeron propagators has been used in this work. We fit the theoretical results with the various experimental data of the $pp$ and $p\bar p$ scattering at the TeV scale to determine the model parameters in the present work. By using the model parameters, the Chew-Frautschi plot of the tensor odderon Regge trajectory is evaluated. As a result, the odderon spin-3 mass is predicted to be 3.2 GeV. In addition, the total cross-section of our model is compatible with the results from TOTEM and its extrapolation from D0 collaboration. Moreover, the total cross-section also satisfies the Friossart bound at the Regge limit.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the odderon is a Reggeized odd-glueball with spin-3 (tensor field), not spin-1, based on the argument that the lightest three-gluon bound state in an s-wave has quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=3^{--}\); the claim is demonstrated by fitting a tree-level effective Lagrangian model to TeV-scale \(pp\) and \(p\bar p\) elastic scattering data, with the condition that the odderon intercept \(\epsilon_{\mathbb{O}}\) must be smaller than the pomeron intercept \(\epsilon_{\mathbb{P}}\) to ensure pomeron dominance at very high energies.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians for a spin-2 pomeron (following Ewerz (2013)) and a spin-3 odderon coupled to protons, using a Donnachie-Landschoff ansatz for the Regge propagators. They calculated tree-level scattering amplitudes for \(pp\) and \(p\bar p\) elastic scattering, including both pomeron and odderon exchange, and fitted the model parameters (odderon coupling \(g_{\mathbb{O}}\), trajectory parameters \(\epsilon_{\mathbb{O}}\) and \(\alpha_{\mathbb{O}}'\), and form-factor parameters \(A, B, C\)) to experimental data at TeV energies. The Chew-Frautschi plot of the odderon Regge trajectory was then evaluated from the fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

* The odderon spin-3 mass is predicted to be 3.2 GeV.
* The pomeron trajectory parameters are fixed as \(\epsilon_{\mathbb{P}} = 0.0808\) and \(\alpha_{\mathbb{P}}' = 0.25\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The pomeron-proton coupling is set to \(g_{\mathbb{P}} = 3 \times 1.87\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The proton form factor uses \(m_D^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\mu_p/\mu_N = 2.7928\).
* The total cross-section from the model is compatible with TOTEM results and D0 collaboration extrapolation at 1.96 TeV, and satisfies the Froissart bound at the Regge limit.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of the Heidelberg group (Ewerz 2013, 2016) by replacing the odderon spin-1 assumption with a spin-3 tensor field, motivated by lattice QCD and Coulomb gauge QCD calculations (Llanes-Estrada 2005) that identify the odderon as a \(3^{--}\) oddball. It builds on the TOTEM and D0 (2020) discovery of the odderon and provides a systematic field-theoretical framework for calculating amplitudes, enabling future combination with other models or inclusion of additional Reggeon exchanges.
