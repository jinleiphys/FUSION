# Chiral 1/M^2 corrections to B^(*) -> D^(*) at Zero Recoil in Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Heavy quark effective theory can be used to calculate the values of the semileptonic B^(*) -> D^(*) decays in the limit that the heavy quark masses are infinite. We calculate the lowest order chiral corrections, which are of O(1/M^2), from the breaking of heavy quark symmetry at the zero recoil point in quenched chiral perturbation theory. These results will aid in the extrapolation of quenched lattice calculations from the light quark masses used on the lattice down to the physical ones.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the formally dominant chiral corrections of order $1/M^2$ to the semileptonic $B^{(*)}\to D^{(*)}$ form factors at zero recoil in quenched chiral perturbation theory (Q$\chi$PT), finding that these corrections are of order $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^{3n/2}/(M^n m_q^{n/2})$ for $n\ge 2$ and are therefore larger than tree-level heavy quark symmetry breaking operators suppressed by $\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/M$, though the actual numerical size depends on the unknown parameters $\mu_0$, $g$, $\alpha$, and $\gamma$.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop corrections from wavefunction renormalization and vertex diagrams in quenched chiral perturbation theory (Q$\chi$PT), including the effects of hyperfine splitting between heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons ($\Delta_D$ and $\Delta_B$) from $O(1/M)$ operators in the heavy quark Lagrangian. They evaluate the form factors $h_+(1)$, $h_{A_1}(1)$, and $h_1(1)$ at zero recoil ($\omega=1$) by combining these loop contributions with local counterterms that cancel the renormalization scale dependence, and expand the results in powers of the hyperfine splittings. The calculation also yields the corrections to the double ratios $\mathcal{R}_+$, $\mathcal{R}_1$, and $\mathcal{R}_{A_1}$ used in lattice QCD simulations.

## Key numbers

- $\Delta_D \approx 142$ MeV and $\Delta_B \approx 46$ MeV are the hyperfine splittings between vector and pseudoscalar mesons for $D$ and $B$ mesons respectively
- The leading corrections to $h_+(1)$, $h_{A_1}(1)$, and $h_1(1)$ are proportional to $(\Delta_B - \Delta_D)^2$ for $h_+(1)$ and $h_1(1)$, and to $(3\Delta_B^2 + \Delta_D^2 + 2\Delta_B\Delta_D)$ for $h_{A_1}(1)$, with coefficients involving $1/(4\pi f)^2$ where $f=132$ MeV
- The parameter $\mu_0$ is estimated to be $\mu_0 \approx 500-900$ MeV from $\eta$-$\eta'$ mass splitting
- The axial coupling is taken as $g^2 = 0.4$, with $\alpha$ values of $0$ and $0.7$, and $\gamma$ in the range $-g \le \gamma \le g$
- The term proportional to $\mu_0^2$ in the chiral limit has a $1/m^2$ singularity, dominating over the log-divergent terms proportional to $\alpha$ and $\gamma$

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Randall and Wise (1993) for $D$ meson hyperfine splitting corrections to the quenched approximation (Q$\chi$PT), and also includes $B$ meson hyperfine splitting effects that were treated more completely by Boyd et al. (1995). The results are intended to improve the chiral extrapolation of quenched lattice QCD calculations of $B\to D^*$ form factors, such as those by Hashimoto et al. (2001) and Kronfeld et al. (2002), by replacing the previous $\chi$PT-based extrapolation with a Q$\chi$PT calculation that better accounts for the quenched approximation used in those lattice simulations.
