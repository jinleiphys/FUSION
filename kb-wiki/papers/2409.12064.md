# Quantum Magic and Multi-Partite Entanglement in the Structure of Nuclei

> Motivated by the Gottesman-Knill theorem, we present a detailed study of the quantum complexity of $p$-shell and $sd$-shell nuclei. Valence-space nuclear shell-model wavefunctions generated by the BIGSTICK code are mapped to qubit registers using the Jordan-Wigner mapping (12 qubits for the $p$-shell and 24 qubits for the $sd$-shell), from which measures of the many-body entanglement ($n$-tangles) and magic (non-stabilizerness) are determined. While exact evaluations of these measures are possible for nuclei with a modest number of active nucleons, Monte Carlo simulations are required for the more complex nuclei. The broadly-applicable Pauli-String $IZ$ exact (PSIZe-) MCMC technique is introduced to accelerate the evaluation of measures of magic in deformed nuclei (with hierarchical wavefunctions), by factors of $\sim 8$ for some nuclei. Significant multi-nucleon entanglement is found in the $sd$-shell, dominated by proton-neutron configurations, along with significant measures of magic. This is evident not only for the deformed states, but also for nuclei on the path to instability via regions of shape coexistence and level inversion. These results indicate that quantum-computing resources will accelerate precision simulations of such nuclei and beyond.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that significant multi-nucleon entanglement and magic (non-stabilizerness) exist in sd-shell nuclei, particularly in deformed states and nuclei near regions of shape coexistence and level inversion, but these measures can only be evaluated exactly for nuclei with modest numbers of active nucleons; Monte Carlo simulations are required for more complex nuclei, and the newly introduced PSIZe-MCMC technique accelerates magic evaluation by factors of approximately 8 for some deformed nuclei with hierarchical wavefunctions.

## Method

Valence-space nuclear shell-model wavefunctions for p-shell and sd-shell nuclei were generated using the BIGSTICK code and mapped to qubit registers via the Jordan-Wigner mapping (12 qubits for p-shell, 24 qubits for sd-shell). The authors computed n-tangles to measure multipartite entanglement and stabilizer Renyi entropies (SREs) to measure magic, using exact evaluation for simpler nuclei and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling of Pauli strings for more complex nuclei. A new algorithm called Pauli-String IZ exact (PSIZe-) MCMC was developed to accelerate sampling of Pauli string expectation values in deformed nuclei with collective wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- 12 qubits required for p-shell nuclei and 24 qubits for sd-shell nuclei under the Jordan-Wigner mapping
- Speedup factor of approximately 8 for evaluating measures of magic in some deformed nuclei using the PSIZe-MCMC technique
- The study covers nuclei with proton and neutron numbers between 2 and 20 (2 ≤ Z,N ≤ 20)
- p-shell nuclei use the Cohen-Kurath interaction; sd-shell nuclei use the usdb interaction

## Context

This work extends recent investigations of magic and entanglement in nuclear systems, including studies of the magic-power of nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon S-matrices by the same group, and complements prior shell-model entanglement studies such as Johnson (2022) which found low proton-neutron entanglement entropy for nuclei away from N=Z, and Perez-Obiol (2023) which calculated single-orbital entanglement entropy and two-orbital mutual information. The paper also builds on MCMC techniques for evaluating SREs developed by Tarabunga (2023) for 2D lattice gauge theories. The results indicate that quantum-computing resources will be needed for precision simulations of deformed nuclei and those near the island of inversion, such as the Mg isotopic chain where shape coexistence and level inversion occur around 32Mg.
