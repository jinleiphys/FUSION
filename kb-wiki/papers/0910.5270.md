# Self-consistent symmetries in the proton-neutron Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach

> Symmetry properties of densities and mean fields appearing in the nuclear Density Functional Theory with pairing are studied. We consider energy functionals that depend only on local densities and their derivatives. The most important self-consistent symmetries are discussed: spherical, axial, space-inversion, and mirror symmetries. In each case, the consequences of breaking or conserving the time-reversal and/or proton-neutron symmetries are discussed and summarized in a tabulated form, useful in practical applications.

## Key claim

The paper systematically classifies self-consistent symmetries (spherical, axial, space-inversion, mirror, and D$_{2\mathrm{h}}$) that can appear in the proton-neutron Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach to nuclear Density Functional Theory (DFT), extending previous work on Hartree-Fock symmetries to include the pairing channel and arbitrary mixing between protons and neutrons. The key condition is that a self-consistent symmetry is defined not only by invariance of the energy density itself, but also by covariance of the energy density under the symmetry transformation.

## Method

The authors analyze the transformation properties of the particle-hole and particle-particle (pairing) density matrices under rotations, space inversion, time reversal, and isorotations, using the coordinate-space HFB theory with local densities and their derivatives. They derive the constraints imposed by time-reversal and proton-neutron symmetries on the local DFT densities for each spatial geometry, and summarize the allowed non-zero density components in tabulated form. The formalism incorporates an arbitrary mixing between protons and neutrons in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, following the framework established in Perlińska et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- The particle number constraint is given by $\int d^3\mathbf{r} \rho_0(\mathbf{r}) = A$, where $A$ is the mass number.
- The isospin projection constraint is $\int d^3\mathbf{r} \rho_3(\mathbf{r}) = N - Z = 2T_3$, where $N$ and $Z$ are neutron and proton numbers.
- The Lagrange multiplier matrix for the Fermi levels is $\hat{\lambda} = \frac{1}{2}(\lambda_n + \lambda_p)\hat{I} + \frac{1}{2}(\lambda_n - \lambda_p)\hat{\tau}^{(3)}$, where $\lambda_n$ and $\lambda_p$ are the neutron and proton Fermi levels.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry analysis of Dobaczewski et al. (2000a, 2000b) from the Hartree-Fock case to the full HFB theory with pairing, and generalizes the discussion to include arbitrary proton-neutron mixing motivated by phenomena in $N \approx Z$ nuclei such as proton-neutron pairing, Wigner energy, and isospin mixing. The results provide a systematic framework for classifying self-consistent symmetries in nuclear DFT calculations, enabling practitioners to avoid erroneous estimates of DFT energies and deformations when imposing symmetry restrictions on the initial density matrix.
