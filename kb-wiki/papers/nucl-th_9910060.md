# $NΩ$ and $ΔΩ$ dibaryons in SU(3) chiral quark model

> The binding energy of the six quark system with strangeness s=-3 is investigated under the chiral SU(3) constituent quark model in the framework of $RGM$. The calculations of the single $NΩ$ channel with spin S=2 and the single $ΔΩ$ channel with spin S=3 are performed. The results show that both systems could be dibaryons and the interaction induced by the chiral field plays a very important role on forming bound states in the systems considered. The phase shifts and scattering lengths in corresponding channels are also given.

## Key claim

The NΩ (S=2, T=1/2, L=0) and ΔΩ (S=3, T=3/2, L=0) six-quark systems with strangeness s=-3 may form dibaryons, but the binding energies depend strongly on the chiral model used: the SU(3) chiral quark model gives binding energies of 3.5-12.7 MeV for NΩ and 4.4-14.2 MeV for ΔΩ, while simpler SU(2) models predict much larger binding.

## Method

The authors used the Resonating Group Method (RGM) within the chiral SU(3) constituent quark model to solve the six-quark bound state problem for single-channel NΩ and ΔΩ systems. Two parameter sets were employed: Set 1 used previously established parameters, while Set 2 adopted experimentally measured masses for the κ and ε mesons from the Particle Data Table. The calculations were repeated with extended SU(2) and SU(2) chiral quark models (omitting various scalar mesons) to isolate the role of chiral field interactions.

## Key numbers

- NΩ binding energy: 3.5 MeV (Set 1) and 12.7 MeV (Set 2), with corresponding RMS radii of 1.18 fm and 0.98 fm
- ΔΩ binding energy: 4.4 MeV (Set 1) and 14.2 MeV (Set 2), with corresponding RMS radii of 1.15 fm and 0.96 fm
- NΩ scattering length: -3.68 fm (Set 1) and -2.32 fm (Set 2)
- ΔΩ scattering length: -3.40 fm (Set 1) and -2.22 fm (Set 2)
- Extended SU(2) model gave NΩ binding of 31.8 MeV (RMS 0.81 fm) and ΔΩ binding of 34.3 MeV (RMS 0.80 fm)
- SU(2) model gave NΩ and ΔΩ binding of 49.5 MeV (RMS 0.74 fm) for both channels

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multi-strangeness dibaryons by Yu et al. (predicted ΩΩ and ΞΩ dibaryons) and by Li and Shen (predicted Ξ*Ω and coupled ΞΩ-Ξ*Ω dibaryons) to the s=-3 sector. The results show that the chiral field interaction, particularly the repulsive ε meson contribution, is crucial for determining whether these systems form bound states. The NΩ dibaryon is identified as a promising experimental target because Ω decays only weakly, giving a narrow width, and nucleon beams are readily available for production in heavy ion collisions.
