# Number conserving particle-hole RPA for superfluid nuclei

> We present a number conserving particle-hole RPA theory for collective excitations in the transition from normal to superfluid nuclei. The method derives from an RPA theory developed long ago in quantum chemistry using antisymmetric geminal powers, or equivalently number projected HFB states, as reference states. We show within a minimal model of pairing plus monopole interactions that the number conserving particle-hole RPA excitations evolve smoothly across the superfluid phase transition close to the exact results, contrary to particle-hole RPA in the normal phase and quasiparticle RPA in the superfluid phase that require a change of basis at the broken symmetry point. The new formalism can be applied in a straightforward manner to study particle-hole excitations on top of a number projected HFB state.

## Key claim

The paper presents a number-conserving particle-hole RPA (NCphRPA) that, within a minimal model of pairing plus monopole interactions, evolves smoothly across the superfluid phase transition close to exact results, whereas conventional phRPA in the normal phase and QRPA in the superfluid phase require a change of basis at the broken symmetry point and produce a kink.

## Method

The authors derive NCphRPA by constructing a complete set of particle-hole operators that kill a number-projected HFB (PHFB) reference state, analogous to how standard RPA uses killers of a Hartree-Fock Slater determinant. They then apply the equation-of-motion method to build the RPA matrices A and B from double commutators of the Hamiltonian with these killers, evaluating expectation values in the PHFB state. The method is benchmarked on the Agassi model, a two-level system with pairing and monopole particle-hole interactions, at half filling along the line χ=0.5 where the system crosses from normal to superfluid as the pairing strength Σ increases.

## Key numbers

* The first excited particle-hole state energy ω = √(A²−B²) is computed along the line χ=0.5 as a function of Σ from 0 to beyond Σ=1, showing smooth behavior across the transition at Σ=1.
* The transition matrix element of Jx = ½(J+ + J−) between the excited state and ground state is evaluated, showing improvement over conventional RPA methods in the transitional region.
* The PHFB reference state parameter z = z+/z− displays a smooth behavior across the transition, in contrast to the abrupt change at the mean-field level.
* The model uses a system with degeneracy j=10 at half filling, with coupling parameters Σ and χ for pairing and particle-hole interactions respectively.

## Context

This work extends an RPA formalism originally developed in quantum chemistry using antisymmetric geminal powers (Sangfelt, Linderberg) to nuclear physics, where attractive pairing correlations make it particularly useful. It addresses the known problem that conventional phRPA and QRPA produce a sharp transition with a significant kink when crossing from normal to superfluid phases (Krumlinde), and that number-projected QRPA (Civitarese, Suhonen) requires cumbersome projection of two-quasiparticle states with subsequent orthogonalization. The NCphRPA formalism is simpler than number-projected QRPA and can be applied to study particle-hole excitations on top of a number-projected HFB state throughout the mass table, including transitional and superfluid nuclei.
