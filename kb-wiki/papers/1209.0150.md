# Tensor-optimized shell model for the Li isotopes with a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction

> We study the Li isotopes systematically in terms of the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) by using a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction as the AV8' interaction. The short-range correlation is treated in the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Using the TOSM+UCOM approach, we investigate the role of the tensor force on each spectrum of the Li isotopes. It is found that the tensor force produces quite a characteristic effect on various states in each spectrum and those spectra are affected considerably by the tensor force. The energy difference between the spin-orbit partner, the p1/2 and p3/2 orbits of the last neutron, in 5Li is caused by opposite roles of the tensor correlation. In 6Li, the spin-triplet state in the LS coupling configuration is favored energetically by the tensor force in comparison with jj coupling shell model states. In 7,8,9Li, the low-lying states containing extra neutrons in the p3/2 orbit are favored energetically due to the large tensor contribution to allow the excitation from the 0s orbit to the p1/2 orbit by the tensor force. Those three nuclei show the jj coupling character in their ground states which is different from 6Li.

## Key claim

The tensor force produces a characteristic effect on each spectrum of the Li isotopes, but the calculated total binding energies underestimate experimental values, with the discrepancy growing for neutron-rich isotopes; the authors attribute this to missing higher-order configurations (beyond 2p2h), genuine three-body interactions, and possible improvements to the UCOM correlation function.

## Method

The authors combined the Tensor-Optimized Shell Model (TOSM) with the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) using the bare AV8' nucleon-nucleon interaction. TOSM includes up to 2p2h excitations from the standard shell-model space, with particle states described by Gaussian basis functions to capture high-momentum components from the tensor force, while UCOM treats short-range central correlations via a unitary transformation. The variational principle was applied to determine both the linear amplitudes of the basis states and the nonlinear length parameters of the Gaussian functions, with the center-of-mass motion handled by the Lawson method.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of 4He: -22.30 MeV in TOSM+UCOM with AV8' interaction.
- Energy difference between the spin-orbit partner states (3/2- and 1/2-) in 5Li: 1.7 MeV.
- Tensor energy contribution in 5Li: -3.07 MeV for the 3/2- state, -0.41 MeV for the 1/2- state (measured from 4He ground state).
- Matter radii: 6Li 2.22 fm (experiment 2.35(3) fm), 7Li 2.32 fm (experiment 2.35(3) fm), 8Li 2.42 fm (experiment 2.38(2) fm), 9Li 2.46 fm (experiment 2.32(2) fm).
- Probability of the (0s)^4 configuration in 4He: 84.14% with AV8', 96.6% with Minnesota interaction (no tensor force).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOSM+UCOM studies of He isotopes (Myo et al., 2011) to the Li isotope chain from 5Li to 9Li, and complements earlier cluster-model analyses of 10Li and 11Li (Ikeda et al., 2010; Myo et al., 2007, 2008) that used simplified TOSM wave functions for the 9Li core. The results show that the tensor force selectively favors p3/2 neutron occupation in 7,8,9Li (producing jj coupling character in ground states) while favoring LS coupling in 6Li, and that the Pauli blocking of specific 2p2h tensor correlations in 4He explains the spin-orbit splitting in 5Li. The underbinding observed in heavier isotopes points to the need for including 4p4h configurations, three-body forces as in Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Pieper et al., 2001), or improved UCOM treatments.
