# A Study of the Roper Resonance as a Hybrid State from $J/ψ$ Decays

> The structure of the Roper resonance as a hybrid baryon is investigated through studying the transitional amplitudes in J/psi-> p\barN*, N*\barN* decays. We begin with perturbative QCD to describe the dynamical process for the J/psi-> 3\bar q+3q decay to the lowest order of α_s, and by extending the modified quark creation model to the J/psi energy region to describe the J/psi-> 3\bar q+3q +g process. The non-perturbative effects are incorporated by a simple quark model of baryons to evaluate the angular distribution parameters and decay widths for the processes J/psi-> pbar N*,N*bar N*. From fitting the decay width of J/psi->gamma p pbar to the experimental data, we extract the quark-pair creation strength g_I=15.40 GeV. Our numerical results for J/psi->pbar N*,N* bar N* decays show that the branching ratios for these decays are quite different if the Roper resonance is assumed to be a common $3q$ state or a pure hybrid state. For testing its mixing properties, we present a scheme to construct the Roper wave function by mixing |qqqg> state with a normal |qqq,2s> state. Under this picture, the ratios of the decay widths to that of the J/psi->p pbar decay are re-evaluated versus the mixing parameter. A test of the hybrid nature of the Roper resonance in J/psi decays is discussed.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance N*(1440) may be a hybrid baryon (a state containing explicit gluonic degrees of freedom, |qqqg⟩) rather than a conventional three-quark radially excited state (|qqq,2s⟩), and its nature can be tested by measuring the branching ratios and angular distributions of J/ψ decays into pN̅* and N̅*N̅* pairs, which differ significantly between the two interpretations.

## Method

The authors model J/ψ decays using perturbative QCD at lowest order in α_s for the process J/ψ → 3q + 3q̅ (three-gluon annihilation), and extend a modified quark-pair creation model to describe J/ψ → 3q̅ + 3q + g (one gluon bound into a hybrid baryon). Non-perturbative effects are incorporated via harmonic-oscillator quark-model wave functions for baryons and hybrid baryons in their center-of-mass frames, with Lorentz boosts applied to spatial wave functions but Melosh rotations neglected. The quark-pair creation strength g_I is extracted by fitting the calculated width of J/ψ → γ p p̅ to experimental data, and the Roper resonance wave function is constructed as a mixture of |qqq,2s⟩ and |qqqg⟩ components parameterized by a mixing parameter δ.

## Key numbers

- Quark-pair creation strength extracted from J/ψ → γ p p̅: g_I = 15.40 GeV (using light quark mass m_q = 0.22 GeV and harmonic oscillator constant 0.08 GeV²)
- Branching fraction used for J/ψ → γ p p̅: 3.8 × 10⁻⁴ (from PDG)
- Color factor ratio ⟨C₂⟩/⟨C₁⟩ ≈ 0.2, indicating color suppression for hybrid baryon production
- The extracted g_I = 15.40 GeV is significantly larger than the value g_I = 7.3 GeV obtained from fitting light meson decays by Ackleh et al., suggesting energy dependence of the strength

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Barnes and Close (bag model prediction of lightest hybrid baryon at ~1.6 GeV) and Golowich, Haqq, and Karl (identification with the Roper resonance), as well as QCD sum rule calculations by Martynenko (~2.1 GeV) and Kisslinger (~1.5 GeV). It builds on the proposal by Zou that J/ψ decays at BEPC provide an isospin I=1/2 filter for studying N* spectroscopy without contamination from Δ states. The results enable experimental tests using the 58 million J/ψ events collected by BESII, where partial wave analysis of J/ψ → p̅N*(1440) and J/ψ → N̅*(1440)N*(1440) can distinguish between the pure three-quark, pure hybrid, or mixed interpretations of the Roper resonance.
