# Electron and neutrino scattering off the deuteron in a relativistic framework

> We build a relativistic model to perform calculations of exclusive, semi-exclusive and inclusive unpolarized cross sections and various polarization observables in electron and neutrino scattering experiments with deuteron targets. The strong interaction dynamics is defined by an explicit dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group, where representations of space translations and rotations in the interacting and non-interacting representations are the same. The Argonne V18 potential is used to construct a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction reproducing the experimental deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering observables. Our formalism does not include the pion production channel and neglects two-body contributions in the electromagnetic as well as in the weak nuclear current operator. We show that it is applicable to processes at kinematics, where the internal two-nucleon energy remains below the pion production threshold but the magnitude of the three-momentum transfer extends at least to several GeV.

## Key claim

The authors construct a relativistic model for electron and neutrino scattering off deuterons that is applicable when the internal two-nucleon energy remains below the pion production threshold, even though the three-momentum transfer can extend to several GeV; the model explicitly omits pion production channels and neglects two-body contributions to the electromagnetic and weak nuclear current operators.

## Method

The strong interaction dynamics is defined by a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group constructed using the Bakamjian-Thomas approach, where the Argonne V18 potential is reinterpreted relativistically to reproduce the experimental deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering observables. The electroweak interaction is treated in the one-boson-exchange approximation, with the transition matrix elements expressed as contractions of lepton and nuclear current matrix elements. The nuclear current matrix elements are computed using Poincaré Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and relativistic partial wave expansions of the deuteron state, with the deuteron wave function decomposed into s-wave (l=0) and d-wave (l=2) components.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is reproduced using the Argonne V18 potential, though the exact numerical value is not stated in the text.
- The fine structure constant used is α ≈ 1/137.
- The three-momentum transfer extends at least to several GeV, as stated in the abstract.
- The electron scattering angle θ_e ranges from 0° to 180° with no restriction.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of deuteron electroweak scattering beyond previous non-relativistic approaches by using an explicit dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group, building on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) and the relativistic reinterpretation of the Argonne V18 potential (Kamada et al., 2007). The formalism enables consistent calculations of spin-dependent observables in elastic and inelastic scattering, and can be extended in future work to include pion production channels and two-body current contributions, which are neglected here to identify which observables are sensitive to those effects.
