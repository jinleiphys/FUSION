# Evidence for Triangular D'(3h) Symmetry in 13C

> We derive the rotation-vibration spectrum of a 3alpha+1 neutron (proton) configuration with triangular D(3h) symmetry by exploiting the properties of the double group D'(3h), and show evidence for this symmetry to occur in the rotation-vibration spectra of 13C. Our results, based on purely symmetry considerations, provide benchmarks for microscopic calculations of the cluster structure of light nuclei.

# Evidence for Triangular D'(3h) Symmetry in 13C

## Key claim
The rotation-vibration spectrum of 13C shows strong evidence for D'(3h) triangular symmetry arising from a 3-alpha-plus-one-neutron cluster configuration, but the assignment of states above 10 MeV is tentative and some predicted bands (such as K^P=7/2^-) have no observed evidence.

## Context
This work extends the study of triangular D(3h) symmetry previously established in 12C (Bijker and Iachello) to the odd-neutron nucleus 13C by constructing the double group D'(3h) representations, which had not been applied to nuclear physics before. The results provide benchmarks for microscopic calculations such as Fermion Molecular Dynamics (Feldmeier, Roth, Neff), Antisymmetric Molecular Dynamics (Kanada-En'yo), and the full four-body 3öÝ+n model (Yamada), as well as planned no-core shell-model calculations (Navratil, Maris). The cluster interpretation contrasts with shell-model calculations (Millener, Lee, Kurath) that require adjusted single-particle energies and effective charges to reproduce the observed positive-parity states.
