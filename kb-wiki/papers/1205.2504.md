# The (2j-1) rule with other interactions

> We recently formulated a rule for isomeric states for a system of 4 nucleons with isospin T=1, namely that if the nucleons are in a single j shell then states with angular momenta J=2 and J=(2j-1) are either isomeric or ground states [ze12]. To show that this is a robust result, we here consider a new interaction from the literature that was used to discuss even-even and odd-even nuclei. We here apply it to an odd-odd nucleus.

## Key claim

The (2j-1) rule for isomeric states in four-nucleon T=1 systems is shown to be robust by testing it with a new interaction (CCGI) from the literature, originally used for even-even and odd-even nuclei, and applying it to the odd-odd nucleus 96Ag. The rule holds qualitatively: the J=2 and J=(2j-1) states remain the lowest-lying states, with J=8 being the ground state and J=2 the first excited state at 0.180 MeV.

## Method

Single j-shell calculations were performed for four nucleons with isospin T=1 in the f7/2 and g9/2 shells, using two-body matrix elements taken from experimental spectra of two-particle systems (INTa, INTc) and two-hole systems (INTb, INTd). The authors also tested a new effective interaction (CCGI) from Coraggio et al. (2012) for the g9/2 shell, and derived interactions from the particle-hole spectrum of 90Nb via a Racah transformation. Yrast spectra were computed for cross-conjugate pairs (e.g., 44Sc/52Mn and 96Ag/96Cd) and compared with experiment.

## Key numbers

- In 44Sc, the J=6 state is calculated at 0.381 MeV (INTa) and has an experimental half-life of 58.6 hours, confirming isomerism.
- In 52Mn, the J=6 state is the ground state (0.000 MeV with INTb) with a half-life of 5.591 days.
- In 96Ag with the CCGI interaction, the J=8 state is the ground state (0.000 MeV) and the J=2 state is at 0.180 MeV.
- In 96Ag with INTd, the J=8 state is the ground state (0.000 MeV) and the J=2 state is at 0.097 MeV.
- The J=16 state in 96Cd is calculated at 5.506 MeV (INTc) and 4.937 MeV (INTd), below the J=15 state in both cases.
- In 96Ag, the J=15 state is at 2.645 MeV (INTd) and the J=13 state at 2.556 MeV, with near degeneracy supporting isomerism.

## Context

This work extends the (2j-1) rule previously formulated by Zamick and Escuderos (2012) for isomeric states in four-nucleon T=1 systems. It tests the rule with the CCGI interaction of Coraggio et al. (2012), which was developed for nuclei near 100Sn, and finds the rule holds. The results are consistent with experimental findings on 96Ag by Nara Singh et al. (2011) and others, and with the Gallagher-Moszkowski (1958) rotational model predictions for ground-state spins. The work enables further study of isomerism in cross-conjugate pairs and suggests that single j-shell calculations provide useful qualitative guidance for identifying isomeric states in heavier systems.
