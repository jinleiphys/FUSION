# Useful Bases for Problems in Nuclear and Particle Physics

> A set of exactly computable orthonormal basis functions that are useful in computations involving constituent quarks is presented. These basis functions are distinguished by the property that they fall off algebraically in momentum space and can be exactly Fourier-Bessel transformed. The configuration space functions are associated Laguerre polynomials multiplied by an exponential weight, and their Fourier-Bessel transforms can be expressed in terms of Jacobi polynomials in $Λ^2/(k^2 + Λ^2)$. A simple model of a meson containing a confined quark-antiquark pair shows that this basis is much better at describing the high-momentum properties of the wave function than the harmonic-oscillator basis.

# Useful Bases for Problems in Nuclear and Particle Physics

## Key claim

The paper presents a set of orthonormal basis functions (Sturmians) that fall off algebraically in momentum space and have exact Fourier-Bessel transforms, claiming they are "much better at describing the high-momentum properties of the wave function than the harmonic-oscillator basis" for constituent quark models, but only for the specific tested model of a meson with a linear confinement plus one-gluon-exchange interaction.

## Method

The authors construct configuration-space basis functions as associated Laguerre polynomials multiplied by an exponential weight, then compute their analytic Fourier-Bessel transforms, which become Jacobi polynomials in the variable Λ²/(k² + Λ²). They diagonalize a constituent quark mass operator for a meson (containing relativistic kinetic energy, Coulomb interaction, linear confinement, and a smeared spin-spin interaction) using 10, 20, 40, and 80 basis states, comparing convergence against the standard harmonic-oscillator basis. Recursion relations for both Laguerre and Jacobi polynomials are used for numerical computation.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass eigenvalue using polynomial basis with 10 states: 0.14071 GeV; with 80 states: 0.14013 GeV
- Pion mass eigenvalue using harmonic-oscillator basis with 10 states: 0.14826 GeV; with 80 states: 0.14109 GeV
- Momentum scale Λ = 2.0 GeV chosen to minimize the mass eigenvalue for 10 polynomial basis states
- Momentum scale Λ = 0.9 GeV chosen to minimize the mass eigenvalue for 10 harmonic-oscillator basis states
- Model parameters: α_s = 0.5, β = 0.197 GeV², γ = 0.77 GeV, quark mass m = 0.36 GeV, r₀ = 0.66 GeV⁻¹
- Physical π and ρ masses used as fit targets: 0.140 GeV and 0.784 GeV respectively
- With 10 polynomial basis states, the wave function is stable up to k = 15 GeV; with 10 oscillator basis states, cutoff is about 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the use of Sturmian basis functions, previously successful in atomic and chemical physics (Rotenberg 1961; Heller, Rescigno, and Reinhardt 1973), to nuclear and particle physics problems involving constituent quarks. It directly addresses the inefficiency of the harmonic-oscillator basis (used by Stanley and Robson 1983) for representing momentum-space wave functions that fall off algebraically rather than as Gaussians, as required by the Coulomb part of one-gluon-exchange interactions and asymptotic QCD predictions. The basis enables efficient computation of matrix elements in both coordinate and momentum representations, which is essential for relativistic treatments where kinetic energy operators involve square roots and momentum-dependent Wigner or Melosh rotations must be evaluated in momentum space while confining interactions are simpler in configuration space.
