# Components of the elliptic flow in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

> We calculate the elliptic flow of charged particles in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. The recent data of the ALICE Collaboration on the elliptic flow as function of the centrality can be very well described using the hydrodynamic expansion of a fluid with a small shear viscosity eta/s=0.08. The elliptic flow as function of the transverse momentum shows systematic deviations from a hydrodynamic behavior in the small momenta region pt<800MeV. It indicates that a non-negligible contribution of non-thermalized particles from jet fragmentation is present.

# Components of the elliptic flow in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

## Key claim
Viscous hydrodynamics with a small shear viscosity (eta/s=0.08) reproduces the integrated elliptic flow data from the ALICE Collaboration, but the differential elliptic flow v2(pT) shows systematic deviations from hydrodynamic behavior at low transverse momentum (pT < 800 MeV), indicating a non-negligible contribution from non-thermalized jet fragmentation particles even in the soft momentum region.

## Method
The author performed 2+1-dimensional boost-invariant relativistic viscous hydrodynamic calculations with shear viscosity eta/s=0.08 and bulk viscosity zeta/s=0.04, using Glauber model initial entropy density profiles with a binary collision fraction alpha=0.15 tuned to reproduce the ALICE charged particle multiplicity. Statistical emission at freeze-out and resonance decays were simulated using the THERMINATOR event generator, and the elliptic flow was decomposed into a hydrodynamic component and a jet fragmentation component using a weighted mixture model.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio eta/s = 0.08 and bulk viscosity to entropy ratio zeta/s = 0.04 used in the hydrodynamic calculation
- Binary collision fraction alpha = 0.15 reproduces the centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density
- Systematic deviation of data from hydrodynamic calculation observed in the range 200 MeV < pT < 800 MeV, with an excess of approximately 0.01-0.02 in v2
- Linear fit to data points with pT < 1 GeV gives an intercept of 0.06-0.01 +/- 10^-5, a statistically significant deviation from zero
- For centrality 30-40%, the jet component v2_jet = 0.11 with transition momentum p_w = 2.8 GeV and width Delta p = 2.8 GeV
- For centrality 10-20%, the jet component v2_jet = 0.085 with p_w = 3.2 GeV and Delta p = 3.0 GeV
- The jet contribution at low pT corresponds to 10-20% of particles originating from jet fragmentation

## Context
This work extends viscous hydrodynamic analyses previously applied at RHIC energies (Luzum 2008, Song 2008) to LHC Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and compares directly to ALICE Collaboration data (Aamodt 2010). The finding that soft particles (pT < 800 MeV) show deviations from the linear v2(pT) behavior expected from collective flow contradicts the standard hydrodynamic picture and suggests that jet fragmentation occurs outside the thermal fireball. This enables new studies of jet formation, attenuation, and fragmentation in heavy-ion environments, and indicates that non-thermalized jet particles must be accounted for in quantitative hydrodynamic model comparisons at LHC energies.
