# The lifetime of unstable particles in electromagnetic fields

> We show that the electromagnetic moments of unstable particles (resonances) have an absorptive contribution which quantifies the change of the particle's lifetime in an external electromagnetic field. To give an example we compute here the imaginary part of the magnetic moment for the cases of the muon and the neutron at leading order in the electroweak coupling. We also consider an analogous effect for the strongly-decaying $Δ$(1232) resonance. The result for the muon is Im$ μ= e G_F^2 m^3/768 π^3$, with $e$ the charge and $m$ the mass of the muon, $G_F$ the Fermi constant, which in an external magnetic field of $B$ Tesla give rise to the relative change in the muon lifetime of $3\times 10^{-15} B$. For neutron the effect is of a similar magnitude. We speculate on the observable implications of this effect.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic moments of unstable particles have an absorptive (imaginary) part that quantifies the change in the particle's lifetime when placed in an external electromagnetic field; the effect is tiny for ordinary field strengths but scales inversely with the available phase space for decay, potentially becoming significant in environments where phase space is reduced.

## Method

The authors compute the imaginary part of the magnetic moment for the muon and the neutron at leading order in the electroweak coupling using two-loop self-energy diagrams in Fermi theory, with the W-boson propagators treated as static. For the muon, dimensional regularization in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions is used to extract the absorptive part of the self-energy and the vertex correction. For the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, the leading-order chiral effective-field theory calculation of the self-energy and vertex graphs is performed in the chiral limit, with the pion mass neglected.

## Key numbers

* Muon: \(\text{Im}\, \mu = e G_F^2 m^3 / 768 \pi^3\), where \(e\) is the charge and \(m\) the muon mass, \(G_F\) the Fermi constant; this gives a relative change in the muon lifetime of \(3 \times 10^{-15} B\) for a magnetic field of \(B\) Tesla.
* Muon lifetime in vacuum: \(\tau = 192\pi^3/(G_F^2 m^5) \simeq 2.187 \times 10^{-6}\) sec; experimental value is \((2.19703 \pm 0.00004) \times 10^{-6}\) sec.
* Muon lifetime change in a 1 Tesla field: \(|\Delta\tau| \lesssim 6 \times 10^{-21}\) sec.
* Neutron: relative change in width \(\lesssim 3 \times 10^{-14} B\) T\(^{-1}\), with \(\mu_N \simeq 3.15 \times 10^{-14}\) MeV T\(^{-1}\) the nuclear magneton.
* \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance: \(\text{Im}\, \mu_{\Delta^+} = (4/3)\pi \mu_N C^2 \lambda + O(\lambda^2)\), where \(\lambda \approx (m_\Delta - m_N)/m_N \sim 1/3\) and \(C = h_A m_\Delta / 8\pi f_\pi \simeq 1.5\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of complex electromagnetic moments for unstable particles, previously discussed by Avdeev (1998) and Pascalutsa (2004), by providing a physical interpretation: the imaginary part of the magnetic moment quantifies the change in lifetime in an external magnetic field, as suggested by Holstein (2006). The effect is analogous to the well-known magnetic-field-induced lifetime change in positronium, where a field of 0.2 Tesla reduces ortho-positronium lifetime by nearly a factor of two. The results enable future investigations of lifetime changes in environments with reduced phase space, such as neutron stars with fields up to \(10^{10}\) Tesla, and suggest a method to compute absorptive parts of hadron electromagnetic moments using lattice QCD with strong magnetic fields.
