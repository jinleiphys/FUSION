# Property of the low-lying states at the critical point of the phase transition in U(4) vibron model

> We study the properties of the low-lying states at the critical point of the phase transition from U(3) to O(4) symmetry in the U(4) vibron model in detail. By analyzing the general characteristics and comparing the calculated results of the energy spectra and the E1, E2 transition rates in E(3) symmetry, in $r^4$ potential model and the finite boson number case in boson space, we find that the results in the $r^4$ potential demonstrates the characteristic of the classical limit at the critical point well and the E(3) symmetry over-predict the energy levels and under-predict the E1 and E2 transition rates of the states at the critical point. However, the E(3) symmetry may describe part of the properties of the system with boson number around 10 to 20. We also confirm that the 12C+12C system is an empirical evidence of the state at the critical point of the phase transition in the U(4) model when concerning the energies of the low-lying resonant states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the critical point of the U(3)-O(4) phase transition in the U(4) vibron model is best described by an \(r^{4}\) potential in the classical (infinite boson number) limit, while the E(3) symmetry (infinite square well) over-predicts energy levels and under-predicts E1 and E2 transition rates; however, the E(3) symmetry may describe part of the properties for systems with boson number around 10 to 20.

## Method

The authors studied the low-lying states at the critical point (\(\eta_c = 3/7\)) of the U(3)-O(4) phase transition in the U(4) vibron model. They solved the Schrodinger equation with an \(r^{4}\) potential (the classical limit potential at the critical point) and with an E(3) symmetry (three-dimensional infinite square well) potential, and also performed finite-boson-number calculations in boson space using the Hamiltonian \(\hat{H} = \varepsilon[(1-\eta)\hat{n} - (\eta/f(N))\hat{D}\cdot\hat{D}]\). They compared the resulting energy spectra and E1, E2 transition rates across these approaches and against experimental data from the \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C system.

## Key numbers

* The critical control parameter for the U(3)-O(4) phase transition is \(\eta_c = 3/7\).
* For the \(L=0\) ground state band (\(n=1\)), the excitation energy (normalized to the \(1^{-}_1\) state) in the E(3) symmetry is 0.00, while in the \(r^{4}\) potential it is 0.00; for the \(L=2\) state (\(n=1\)), the E(3) value is 2.26 and the \(r^{4}\) value is 2.13.
* For the \(L=4\) state (\(n=1\)), the E(3) excitation energy is 5.53 and the \(r^{4}\) value is 4.68 (both normalized to the \(1^{-}_1\) state).
* The experimental energy ratio \(E_{4_1}/E_{2_1}\) for the \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C system is 2.22, compared to 2.45 in E(3) symmetry and 2.20 in the \(r^{4}\) potential.
* The experimental ratio \(E_{6_1}/E_{2_1}\) for \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C is 5.72, compared to 4.31 in E(3) and 3.55 in the \(r^{4}\) potential.
* The experimental ratio \(E_{0_2}/E_{2_1}\) for \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C is 0.31, compared to 1.27 in E(3) and 1.05 in the \(r^{4}\) potential.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in the U(4) vibron model, which describes two-body systems such as diatomic molecules and nuclear molecules. It builds on the earlier proposal by Liu (2008) that the \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C system may exhibit E(3) critical symmetry, and on prior work by Erb (1981) and Cseh (1985) who described the same system using O(4) and U(3) limits, respectively. The paper shows that the \(r^{4}\) potential provides a better description than the E(3) symmetry for the classical limit, and confirms that the \({}^{12}\)C+\({}^{12}\)C system is an empirical example of the critical point, though the authors note that electromagnetic transition data would be needed for a more solid confirmation.
