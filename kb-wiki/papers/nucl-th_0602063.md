# A Mass Formula from Light to Hypernuclei

> Simultaneous description of ordinary and hypernuclei masses by a single mass formula has been a great challenge in nuclear physics. Hyperon-separation energies of about forty Lambda($Λ$), three Lambda-Lambda($ΛΛ$), one Sigma($Σ$) and seven Cascade($Ξ$) hypernuclei have been experimentally found. Many of these nuclei are of light masses. We prescribe a new mass formula, called BWMH, which describes the normal and hypernuclei on the same footing. It is based on the modified-Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula (BWM). BWM is basically an extension of the Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula (BW) for light nuclei. The parameters of BWM were optimized by fitting about 3000 normal nuclei available recently. The original Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula (BW) was designed for medium and heavy mass nuclei and it fails for light nuclei. Two earlier works on hypernuclei based on this BW show some limitations. The BWMH gives improved agreement with the experimental data for the line of stability, one-neutron separation energy versus neutron number spectra of normal nuclei, and the hyperon-separation energies from hypernuclei. The drip lines are modified for addition of a $Λ$ hyperon in a normal nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper presents a single mass formula (BWMH) that simultaneously describes binding energies of both normal nuclei and hypernuclei, claiming improved agreement with experimental data over prior attempts, but the formula is explicitly not applicable for repulsive hyperon-nucleus potentials and therefore does not predict negative separation energies for heavier Sigma hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors extended the modified-Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula (BWM), which was originally optimized by fitting approximately 3000 normal nuclei, by adding explicit hyperon-mass and strangeness-dependent terms that break SU(3) flavor symmetry. The hypernucleus is treated as a core of normal nucleus plus hyperon(s), and the hyperon separation energy is defined as the difference between the binding energy of the hypernucleus and its non-strange core nucleus. The formula was tested against experimental data for about 40 Lambda, three Lambda-Lambda, one Sigma, and seven Cascade hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The BWMH mass formula contains coefficients: 15.777 MeV for the volume term, 18.34 MeV for the surface term, 0.71 for the Coulomb term, and 23.21 for the symmetry term.
- The pairing term delta is +/-12 A^{-1/2} for even-even and odd-odd (N, Z_c) combinations respectively, and 0 otherwise.
- For the Sigma hypernucleus ^4_Sigma He, BWMH predicts binding energies of 2.69 MeV for Sigma^0 (mass 1192.55 MeV) and 1.6 MeV for Sigma^+ (mass 1189.37 MeV).
- For Sigma + ^2H systems, BWMH predicts negative separation energies: -3.53 MeV for Sigma^0, -4.62 MeV for Sigma^+, and -3.37 MeV for Sigma^-.
- Experimental binding energy values for Sigma^+ in ^4_Sigma He are reported as 4.4 +/- 0.3 +/- 1 MeV, 2.8 +/- 0.7 MeV, and 4 +/- 1 MeV from different measurements.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula (BW), which was designed for medium and heavy nuclei and fails for light nuclei, by using the modified version BWM that covers nuclei from Z=3 to Z=83. It addresses limitations of two earlier hypernuclei mass formula approaches: Dover and Gal (1993) prescribed separate formulae for Lambda and Xi hypernuclei but did not reproduce experimental data, while Levai et al. (1998) gave reasonable description of Lambda and Lambda-Lambda separation energies but had binding energy per nucleon diverging at large mass number. The BWMH formula enables prediction of binding energies for all hypernuclei on the same footing, including the prediction that Theta hypernuclei would be more strongly bound than Lambda hypernuclei, and that addition of a single Lambda hyperon modifies neutron and proton driplines, potentially relevant for astrophysical objects such as strange stars.
