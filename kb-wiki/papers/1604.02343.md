# Degrees of Freedom of the Quark Gluon Plasma, tested by Heavy Mesons

> Heavy quarks (charm and bottoms) are one of the few probes which are sensitive to the degrees of freedom of a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), which cannot be revealed by lattice gauge calculations in equilibrium. Due to the rapid expansion of the QGP energetic heavy quarks do not come to an equilibrium with the QGP. Their energy loss during the propagation through the QGP medium depends strongly on the modelling of the interaction of the heavy quarks with the QGP quarks and gluons, i.e. on the assuption of the degrees of freedom of the plasma. Here we compare the results of different models, the pQCD based Monte-Carlo (MC@sHQ), the Dynamical Quasi Particle Model (DQPM) and the effective mass approach, for the drag force in a thermalized QGP and discuss the sensitivity of heavy quark energy loss on the properties of the QGP as well as on non-equilibrium dynamics

# Degrees of Freedom of the Quark Gluon Plasma, tested by Heavy Mesons

## Key claim

Heavy quark energy loss in the QGP is strongly sensitive to whether the plasma constituents are treated as massless partons or as massive quasiparticles, but the drag force alone cannot disentangle the effects of finite parton masses from those of a temperature-dependent coupling constant because these two modifications shift the drag force in opposite directions. The paper shows that three different models (MC@sHQ, DQPM, and effective mass approach) can all reproduce experimental D-meson R_AA data despite having very different drag force values, implying that non-equilibrium dynamics and the temperature dependence of the coupling play a decisive role.

## Method

The authors compare the drag coefficient A(p,T) for charm quarks in a thermalized QGP as calculated from three approaches: (1) MC@sHQ, which uses massless light partons with a momentum-transfer-dependent running coupling and HTL-regulated propagators; (2) the effective mass approach, which assigns temperature-dependent thermal masses to quarks and gluons by fitting the lattice QCD entropy density; and (3) the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), which uses temperature-dependent masses, widths, and a temperature-dependent coupling constant g(T) fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics. The drag force is computed from 2-to-2 Born matrix elements integrated over thermal distributions of the scattering partners, and the results are compared as functions of heavy quark momentum and medium temperature.

## Key numbers

- At T = 2 T_c (T_c = 0.158 GeV), the drag force for a charm quark with momentum p_Q = 10 GeV in the DQPM is always smaller than that from elastic collisions in MC@sHQ (massless partons).
- For a heavy quark with momentum 10 GeV, the reduction factor of the drag force when using finite parton masses (effective mass approach) compared to massless partons is approximately a factor of 4, independent of temperature and collision type.
- The DQPM coupling constant is parametrized with parameters lambda = 2.42, c = 14.4, and T_s = 73 MeV, with a transition temperature T* = 1.19 T_c.
- The effective mass approach uses the HTL relation m_g = sqrt(3) m_q and fits masses starting from T = 134 MeV, which coincides with the EPOS transition temperature T_f.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of heavy-quark energy-loss models initiated by Gossiaux et al. (2008) and Aichelin et al. (2013) for MC@sHQ, by Cassing (2009) for DQPM, and by Nahrgang et al. (2016) for the effective mass approach. It directly compares the drag coefficients of these three models, which had previously only been compared through final observables like R_AA and v_2. The study enables future work to disentangle the roles of parton masses, coupling constant running, and non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, and identifies that models with a momentum-transfer-dependent coupling (MC@sHQ) show strongest energy loss at high temperature early in the expansion, while models with a temperature-dependent coupling (DQPM) shift energy loss toward T_c.
