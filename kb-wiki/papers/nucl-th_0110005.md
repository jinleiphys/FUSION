# Temperature dependence of rho- and a_1-meson masses and mixing of vector and axial-vector correlators

> Within a chiral model which provides a good description of the properties of rho and a_1 mesons in vacuum, it is shown that, to order T^2, the rho- and a_1-meson masses remain constant in the chiral limit, even if at tree level they are proportional to the chiral condensate, sigma_0. Numerically, the temperature dependence of the masses turns out to be small also for realistic parameter sets and high temperatures. The weak temperature dependence of the masses is consistent with the Eletsky-Ioffe mixing theorem, and traces of mixing effects can be seen in the spectral function of the vector correlator at finite temperature.

## Key claim

To order T², the ρ- and a₁-meson masses remain constant in the chiral limit, even though at tree level they are proportional to the chiral condensate σ₀; this cancellation occurs because the self-energy contribution proportional to Δσ₀ is exactly canceled by other diagrams. Numerically, the temperature dependence of the masses is also small for realistic parameter sets and high temperatures.

## Method

The authors use a chiral model based on the linear σ model extended to include ρ and a₁ mesons as elementary fields, which was previously shown to describe vacuum vector and axial-vector spectral functions. They compute the one-loop self-energies of the ρ and a₁ mesons at finite temperature by replacing loop integrals over k₄ with sums over discrete Matsubara frequencies, and determine the temperature-dependent masses from the maxima of the spectral functions. The dominant medium contributions to the self-energies are evaluated analytically in the chiral limit to order T², and numerically for realistic parameters including a finite pion mass.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit, the condensate ⟨σ⟩ decreases as ⟨σ⟩_{T≠0} = ⟨σ⟩_{T=0} (1 − T²/(8 f_π²) + …) to order T².
- The mixing coefficient ε in the Eletsky-Ioffe mixing theorem is ε = T²/(6 f_π²).
- For the realistic parameter set with m_π = 140 MeV, the ρ mass remains almost constant below T ≈ 50 MeV due to suppression by e^{-m_π/T}.
- At T = 150 MeV in the chiral limit, the ρ-meson peak in the vector correlator broadens significantly, and a peak at q₀ = m_{a₁} is washed out by thermal motion of pions.
- For the realistic parameter set at T = 150 MeV, a sharp peak appears in Im Π_V at q₀ = m_{a₁} − m_π.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier chiral model (Urban, Buballa, Wambach, 2000) that described vacuum ρ and a₁ spectral functions, applying it to finite temperature. The result that ρ and a₁ masses remain constant to order T² confirms earlier findings within the gauged linear σ model (Pisarski, 1990s) and is consistent with the Eletsky-Ioffe mixing theorem (Dey, Eletsky, 1990s), which forbids a T² change of the masses in the chiral limit. The paper enables future work on dilepton rates in heavy-ion experiments by showing that the dominant medium effect is broadening of the ρ peak rather than mass dropping, and identifies the need to include finite baryon density and self-consistent dressing of particles inside loops (van Hees, 2000s) for quantitative conclusions.
