# Beta decay of deformed r-process nuclei near A = 80 and A= 160, including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, with the Skyrme finite-amplitude method

> After identifying the nuclei in the regions near A =80 and A = 160 for which beta-decay rates have the greatest effect on weak and main r-process abundance patterns, we apply the finite-amplitude method (FAM) with Skyrme energy-density functionals (EDFs) to calculate beta-decay half-lives of those nuclei in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA). We use the equal filling approximation to extend our implementation of the charge-changing FAM, which incorporates pairing correlations and allows axially symmetric deformation, to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. Within this framework we find differences of up to a factor of seven between our calculated beta-decay half-lives and those of previous efforts. Repeated calculations with nuclei near A = 160 and multiple EDFs show a spread of two to four in beta-decay half-lives, with differences in calculated Q values playing an important role. We investigate the implications of these results for r-process simulations.

# Beta decay of deformed r-process nuclei near A = 80 and A = 160, including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, with the Skyrme finite-amplitude method

## Key claim

The finite-amplitude method (FAM) with Skyrme energy-density functionals can be extended to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei using the equal filling approximation, yielding beta-decay half-lives that differ by up to a factor of seven from previous calculations, though the spread across different Skyrme functionals for nuclei near A = 160 is a factor of two to four, with differences in calculated Q values playing an important role.

## Method

The authors extended the proton-neutron finite-amplitude method (pnFAM), which implements the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme energy-density functionals, to treat odd-A and odd-odd nuclei by applying the equal filling approximation (EFA) to average over time-reversed quasiparticle states. They first identified the most influential beta-decay rates for r-process abundance patterns near A = 80 and A = 160 through sensitivity studies using hot, cold, and neutron star merger astrophysical conditions, then calculated beta-decay half-lives for those key nuclei using the EFA-pnFAM with axially symmetric deformation and pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- Differences of up to a factor of seven between calculated beta-decay half-lives and those of previous efforts
- Spread of two to four in beta-decay half-lives for nuclei near A = 160 when using multiple Skyrme energy-density functionals
- For the sensitivity study in the rare-earth region, individual beta-decay rates were changed by a factor of K = 5 across six different astrophysical trajectories
- For the A = 80 sensitivity study, individual beta-decay lifetimes were varied by a factor of K = 10
- The EFA reproduces full one-quasiparticle energies to within a few hundred keV compared to the blocking approximation, as shown in prior work by Schunck (2010)
- The Gamow-Teller strength for 71Ga shows low-energy one-quasiparticle transition strength near E_QRPA = 1 MeV that is absent in even-even pnFAM calculations

## Context

This work extends the finite-amplitude method previously developed by Nakatsukasa (2007) for RPA response and by Mustonen (2014) for charge-changing transitions (pnFAM), which had been limited to even-even nuclei. The authors build on the equal filling approximation formalism of Perez-Martin (2008) and compare their results to the macroscopic-microscopic QRPA calculations of Moller (1997, 2003) and to finite Fermi system calculations by Borzov (1995). The new capability to compute beta-decay half-lives for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei across the nuclear chart enables more accurate r-process simulations for both the weak r-process (A ~ 80) and the main r-process rare-earth peak (A ~ 160), where sensitivity studies by Mumpower (2012, 2014, 2016) and Surman (2014) have identified the most influential decay rates.
