# Quantum Color Transparency and Nuclear Filtering

> Color transparency is the proposal that under certain circumstances the strong interactions can be reduced in magnitude. We give a comprehensive review of the physics, which hinges on the interface of perturbative QCD with non--perturbative strong interactions. Color transparency is complementary to {\it nuclear filtering}, which is the conversion of quark wave functions in hadrons to small transverse space dimensions by interaction with a nuclear medium. We review current approaches, including pictures based on modeling the time evolution of hadronic wave--packets as well as the use of light cone matrix elements. Spin plays an intrinsic role in testing and understanding the physics and is discussed at length. We emphasize the use of data analysis procedures which have minimal model dependence. We also review existing experimental data and the experimental program planned at various facilities. The subject has strong scientific complementarity and potential to make progress in exploring hadron physics at current and future facilities.

## Key claim

Color transparency is the predicted phenomenon that under certain conditions the strong interactions can be reduced in magnitude or effectively "turned off," but the experimental situation is not settled and there is much discussion whether color transparency has actually been observed or will be observed at laboratory energies and in realistic experimental circumstances.

## Method

The paper provides a comprehensive review of color transparency theory, which hinges on the interface of perturbative QCD with non-perturbative strong interactions, and its complementarity with nuclear filtering, the conversion of quark wave functions in hadrons to small transverse space dimensions by interaction with a nuclear medium. The authors review multiple approaches including pictures based on modeling the time evolution of hadronic wave-packets, the use of light-cone matrix elements, and hadronic interpolating states, while emphasizing data analysis procedures with minimal model dependence.

## Key numbers

- The proton-nucleon inelastic cross section is about 30-35 mb in the energy region of 2-30 GeV, corresponding to a mean free path of about 2 fm inside nuclear matter
- The nuclear target length scales as R_A ~ 1.2 A^{1/3} Fm, which regulates the transverse smallness for partons not to have an interaction
- The transverse separation b between quarks in hard scattering scales as b^2 ~ 1/Q^2, with the important integration region being b^2 ~ 1/Q^2
- For electron beam experiments, a typical experiment at 15 GeV needs a beam energy resolution of about 5 MeV; at 30 GeV the proposed experiments require only resolving pion emission
- The Hermes detector at HERA operates on a 25-30 GeV electron (or positron) beam, with energy range 10 ≤ ν ≤ 22 GeV
- The EVA detector at BNL has been taking data with beam energies in the 6-20 GeV range, expected to expand data taken by a factor of approximately 200

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on color transparency by Brodsky and Mueller (1982, 1988) and the concept of nuclear filtering developed by Ralston and Pire (1988, 1989) and others. The paper synthesizes the quark-basis perturbative QCD approach with hadronic-basis models, noting that current calculations in the hadronic basis are intrinsically neither more nor less rigorous than those using the quark and gluon Fock space. The work enables a future experimental program at facilities including CEBAF, BNL, HERMES at HERA, and the proposed ELFE and LISS facilities, with the goal of using the nucleus as a test medium to study the interface of perturbative and non-perturbative QCD.
