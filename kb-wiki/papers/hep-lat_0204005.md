# Chiral Magnetism of the Nucleon

> We study the quark mass expansion of the magnetic moments of the nucleon in a chiral effective field theory including nucleons, pions and delta resonances as explicit degrees of freedom. We point out that the usual powercounting applied so far to this problem misses important quark mass structures generated via an intermediate isovector M1 nucleon-delta transition. We propose a modified powercounting and compare the resulting chiral extrapolation function to available (quenched) lattice data. The extrapolation is found to work surprisingly well, given that the lattice data result from rather large quark masses. Our calculation raises the hope that extrapolations of lattice data utilizing chiral effective field theory might be applicable over a wider range in quark masses than previously thought, and we discuss some open questions in this context. Furthermore, we observe that within the current lattice data uncertainties the extrapolations presented here are consistent with the Pade fit ansatz introduced by the Adelaide group a few years ago.

## Key claim

The standard chiral power counting applied to nucleon magnetic moments misses important quark-mass structures generated by the intermediate isovector M1 nucleon-delta transition; promoting this transition to the leading-order Lagrangian produces a chiral extrapolation function that works surprisingly well for quenched lattice data even at large quark masses, suggesting that chiral effective field theory extrapolations may be applicable over a wider range in quark masses than previously thought.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective field theory with explicit nucleon, pion, and delta resonance degrees of freedom, using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with a modified power counting that treats the finite nucleon-delta mass splitting Δ as a small scale alongside the pion mass and momentum. They computed the leading one-loop contributions to the nucleon anomalous magnetic moments from the diagrams involving pions, nucleons, and deltas, and introduced the necessary counterterms (D1–D4, E1, E2) to renormalize the theory. The resulting chiral extrapolation function was then compared to available quenched lattice QCD data for the proton and neutron magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The physical pion mass is mπ ≈ 0.14 GeV, while typical "light" quark masses on the lattice correspond to pion masses well above 0.5 GeV, with average u- and d-quark masses mq ∼ 8 MeV at a renormalization scale around 1 GeV.
- The nucleon-delta mass splitting Δ is less than 0.3 GeV, and the delta excitation is separated from the nucleon mass by hardly more than twice mπ.
- The leading-order axial NΔ transition coupling is denoted cA, and the isovector magnetic NΔ transition coupling cV is assigned an intrinsic power ε⁻¹, with cV ≡ cV^(−1).
- The β-functions for the counterterms include: D1: +cA²/(18π²fπ²), D3: −cA cV gA/(27π²fπ²), E1: +cA cV gA/(36π²fπ²); D2, D4, and E2 have zero β-functions in the chosen representation.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon magnetic moments pioneered by Gasser (late 1980s) and Jenkins (1990s), who included octet and decuplet baryons but used standard power counting. It directly addresses the interpolation scheme introduced by the Adelaide group (Leinweber et al.), who used a parameterized Pade fit ansatz for the pion mass dependence of proton and neutron magnetic moments. The modified power counting proposed here enables a systematic chiral extrapolation that is consistent with the Adelaide Pade fit within current lattice data uncertainties, and it opens the possibility of applying chiral effective field theory extrapolations to lattice data at larger quark masses than previously considered feasible.
