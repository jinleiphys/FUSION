# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in a sphere

> We study the chiral phase transition of the two flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in a sphere with the MIT boundary condition. We find that the MIT boundary condition results in much stronger finite size effects than the antiperiodic boundary condition. Our work may be helpful to study the finite size effects in heavy-ion collision in a more realistic way.

# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in a sphere

## Key claim
The spherical MIT boundary condition produces much stronger finite-size effects on chiral symmetry breaking than the commonly used antiperiodic boundary condition, with the constituent quark mass only approaching the infinite-volume limit at a radius of about 14 fm, whereas a box with antiperiodic boundary conditions is effectively infinite for sizes above 3 fm. The authors argue that this stronger effect arises from the confinement character of the MIT boundary condition, and that previous estimates of finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions may therefore be too weak.

## Method
The authors solve the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, using proper-time regularization to handle ultraviolet divergences. For a spherical geometry, they impose the MIT bag boundary condition on the free Dirac equation, which yields discrete momentum eigenvalues from the eigen-equation involving spherical Bessel functions. The momentum integral in the gap equation is replaced by a sum over these discrete modes, and the resulting gap equation is solved numerically at zero and finite temperature for various sphere radii.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass approaches the infinite-volume value when the sphere radius reaches about **14 fm** under MIT boundary condition; under antiperiodic boundary condition, the box is effectively infinite for sizes above **3 fm**.
- Parameters used: current quark mass **m = 5 MeV**, proper-time cutoff **τ_UV = 1/1080^2 MeV^{-2}**, coupling constant **G = 3.26 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}**.
- QGP fireball sizes in heavy-ion collisions are estimated to be between **2 fm and 10 fm** (citing Palhares 2011), meaning these systems experience considerable finite-size effects under the MIT boundary condition.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of finite-size effects in QCD effective models, which typically treat the system as a box with periodic or antiperiodic boundary conditions (e.g., Palhares 2011, Xia). The authors note that the multiple reflection expansion method used in earlier spherical treatments (Kiriyama 2003) becomes invalid for very small volumes, motivating their direct mode-sum approach. The results suggest that the choice of boundary condition is critical for realistic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, and that the MIT boundary condition may be more appropriate than the commonly used (anti)periodic conditions.
