# Heavy ion collisions with non-equilibrium Dirac-Brueckner mean fields

> The influence of realistic interactions on the reaction dynamics in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions is investigated. The mean field in relativistic transport calculations is derived from microscopic Dirac-Brueckner (DB) self-energies, taking non-equilibrium effects, in particular the anisotropy of the local phase space configurations, into account. Thus this approach goes beyond the local density approximation. A detailed analysis of various in-plane and out-of-plane flow observables is presented for Au on Au reactions at incident energies ranging from 250 to 800 A.MeV and the results are compared to recent measurements of the FOPI collaboration. An overall good agreement with in-plane flow data and a reasonable description of the out-of-plane emission is achieved. For these results the intrinsic momentum dependence of the non-equilibrium mean fields is important. On the other hand, the local density approximation with the same underlying DB forces as well as a standard non-linear version of the $σω$ model are less successful in describing the present data. This gives evidence of the applicability of self energies derived from the DB approach to nuclear matter also far from saturation and equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating non-equilibrium effects, specifically the anisotropy of local phase-space configurations, into relativistic transport calculations via Dirac-Brueckner (DB) self-energies yields good agreement with in-plane flow data and reasonable description of out-of-plane emission for Au+Au collisions at 250–800 A.MeV, whereas the local density approximation (LDA) with the same DB forces and a standard non-linear σω model are less successful.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport calculations for Au+Au reactions at incident energies of 250, 400, 600, and 800 A.MeV, using mean fields derived from Dirac-Brueckner self-energies. The non-equilibrium mean fields were constructed in the local configuration approximation (LCA), which approximates the local phase space by two interpenetrating Fermi ellipsoids (colliding nuclear matter), with vertex functions parametrized as functions of subsystem densities and relative velocity. Results were compared to the local density approximation (DB/LDA) using the same DB forces and to the non-linear σω model (NL2 parametrization), and all were confronted with FOPI collaboration flow data.

## Key numbers

* Au+Au reactions at incident energies of 250, 400, 600, and 800 A.MeV were simulated and compared to FOPI data.
* The DB model yields an effective mass m*/M = 0.586 at saturation density, compared to m*/M = 0.83 for the NL2 model.
* The DB equation of state is relatively soft and comparable to the NL2 model, which has a compression modulus corresponding to a soft version.
* The vertex functions for colliding nuclear matter were parametrized for streaming velocities v in the c.m. system, with the relative velocity given by v_rel = 2v/(1+v^2).
* The corresponding σω-model coupling constants are g_σ^2/m_σ^2 * M^2/(4π) = 21.25 and g_ω^2/m_ω^2 * M^2/(4π) = 15.95.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the Tübingen group (Fuchs, Gaitanos, Wolter 1996) that reported transverse flow results at 400 A.MeV, now covering a wider energy range and additional observables including fragment flow and squeeze-out. It builds on the Dirac-Brueckner approach to nuclear matter (Brockmann and Machleidt 1990; ter Haar and Malfliet 1987) and the non-equilibrium colliding nuclear matter formalism developed by Sehn, Fuchs, and Wolter (1996). The results enable further testing of microscopic DB self-energies far from saturation and equilibrium, and provide a benchmark for comparing relativistic transport approaches with exclusive FOPI data.
