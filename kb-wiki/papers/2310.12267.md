# Does Quarkonia Suppression serve as a probe for the deconfinement in small systems?

> In high multiplicity proton-proton $(p-p)$ collisions, the formation of a deconfined state of quarks and gluons akin to Heavy Ion Collisions (HIC) has been a subject of significant interest. In proton-proton ($p-p$) collisions, the transverse size of the system is comparable to the longitudinal (Lorentz contracted) dimension, unlike the case in Nucleus-Nucleus ($A-A$) collision, leading to a hitherto unexplored effect of rapid decrease of temperature of the medium on quark-antiquark bound states. This allows us to probe a unique possibility of hadronization occurring before quarkonia dissociation within the medium. In small systems, a rapid change in temperature also introduces sudden changes in the Hamiltonian. This scenario prompts consideration of non-adiabatic evolution, challenging the traditional adiabatic framework. We demonstrate that non-adiabatic evolution may extend the longevity of quark-anti-quark bound states in $p-p$ collisions, even at higher multiplicities, offering new insights into the dynamics of strongly interacting matter produced in smaller collision systems.

## Key claim

Non-adiabatic evolution, arising from the rapid temperature decrease in small proton-proton collision systems, may extend the longevity of quark-antiquark bound states even at higher multiplicities, challenging the traditional adiabatic framework where quarkonia suppression is taken as a clear signature of deconfinement.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of the quark-antiquark potential using a finite-temperature screened Cornell potential, where the screening mass depends on the medium temperature. They compute the dissociation probability of J/Ψ via first-order time-dependent perturbation theory, using the formalism of Landau and Lifshitz, with the transition amplitude quantifying the probability that the initial J/Ψ state evolves into states orthogonal to J/Ψ, χ, and Ψ'. The temperature evolution is modeled in two stages: a pre-equilibrium stage (using either a power-law or linear rise of effective temperature) followed by a hydrodynamic stage using Gubser flow with third-order viscous corrections, which accounts for transverse expansion important in small systems.

## Key numbers

- For a system with transverse size r_T = 1.5 fm, the dissociation probability of J/Ψ is approximately 10%.
- For system sizes less than 3 fm, the dissociation probability stays of the order of 20%.
- The hydrodynamic initial temperature T_Hydro is taken as 350 MeV, with thermalization time τ_Hydro = 0.3 fm.
- The QCD phase transition temperature T_c is taken as approximately 150 MeV.
- The transition time scale t_tr from the J/Ψ ground state to its next excited state is around 1 fm, comparable to the Hamiltonian evolution time scale t_H of 1-2 fm for small systems.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of quarkonia suppression as a probe of deconfinement, originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), to small collision systems such as proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at the LHC. It directly challenges the adiabatic assumption used in a recent study by Singh (2021) that argued quarkonia suppression could signal QGP formation in p-p collisions. The non-adiabatic formalism builds on prior work by Bagchi (2015), Dutta (2012, 2019), and others who studied non-adiabatic evolution of quarkonia in heavy ion collisions. The results suggest that quarkonia suppression may not be a clear signature for deconfinement in small systems, even at high multiplicity, and that the technique can be generalized to calculate ionization of atoms or molecules exposed to electromagnetic pulses.
