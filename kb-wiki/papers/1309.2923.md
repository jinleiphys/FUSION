# $Υ$ absorption cross sections by nucleons

> The cross sections of $Υ$\ absorption by nucleons are calculated in meson-baryon exchange model using an effective hadronic Lagrangian. Calculated cross sections are found to peak near threshold followed by an increase which tends to settle at large center of mass energy. Peak values are found in range about 1.8 to 4 mb when cutoff parameter of monopole form factor is varied from 1.2 to 1.8 GeV. The results disagree with pQCD calculations but are consistent with the effective value of the absorption cross section extracted from p-A scattering data in E772 experiment at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39$ GeV. We use this data value to find a best fit of the cutoff parameter which enables us provide definite predictions for the values of $Υ$ absorption cross sections by nucleons for upcoming p-A scattering experiments at RHIC and LHC energies for zero rapidity $b\bar{b}$ states. The results could be useful in studying the effect of $Υ$ absorption by nucleons at its production stage in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. We also find that thermal averaged values of total absorption cross sections are small enough to rule out the effect of absorption of $Υ$ by the comovers in the hadronic matter.

# Υ absorption cross sections by nucleons

## Key claim

The authors calculate Υ absorption cross sections by nucleons using a meson-baryon exchange model and find peak values ranging from about 1.8 to 4 mb depending on the cutoff parameter, but these results disagree with perturbative QCD calculations while being consistent with the effective value extracted from p-A scattering data in the E772 experiment at √sNN = 39 GeV. The thermal averaged values of total absorption cross sections are small enough to rule out the effect of Υ absorption by comovers in hadronic matter.

## Method

The authors use an effective hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(5) flavor symmetry to compute cross sections for two-body processes (NΥ → BΛb and NΥ → B*Λb) and three-body processes (NΥ → NB*B̅, NΥ → NBB̅*, NΥ → NBB̅, and NΥ → NB*B̅*). They employ a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Λ varied from 1.2 to 1.8 GeV, and use two sets of coupling constants: one derived from empirical KNΛ and K*NΛ couplings via SU(5) symmetry, and another from QCD sum-rule values of DNΛc and D*NΛc couplings.

## Key numbers

- Peak total cross sections for two-body processes range from 1.6 to 3.6 mb (coupling set 1) and 1.8 to 4.2 mb (coupling set 2) when cutoff parameter Λ is varied from 1.2 to 1.8 GeV.
- The effective value of total σNΥ extracted from E772 experiment at √sNN = 39 GeV is about 2.5 mb at √sNΥ = 19.2 GeV for zero rapidity.
- Fitted cutoff parameters are Λ = 1.48 GeV (set 1) and Λ = 1.45 GeV (set 2) using the E772 data value.
- Using the fitted cutoff, predicted σNΥ = 1.8 mb at √sNN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and 2.2 mb at √sNN = 5.5 TeV (LHC).
- Total σNΥ varies in ranges 1.0 to 3.0 mb at RHIC and 1.5 to 3.4 mb at LHC energies for either coupling set.
- Two-body process thresholds are at c.m. energies 10.90 GeV (NΥ → BΛb) and 10.95 GeV (NΥ → B*Λb).
- Three-body process thresholds are at 10.54 GeV (NΥ → NB*B̅), 10.50 GeV (NΥ → NBB̅), and 10.59 GeV (NΥ → NB*B̅*).

## Context

This work extends the meson-baryon exchange model previously applied to Υ absorption by π and ρ mesons in Lin (2001) and to Bc absorption by nucleons in Akram (2012). The results disagree with pQCD calculations by Song (2005) but are consistent with the effective cross section extracted from the E772 experiment at Fermilab. The predictions for RHIC and LHC energies are lower than the naive extrapolation σNΥ = 2.5(√sNN/39)^0.125 used in previous kinematical studies by Grandchamp (2006), suggesting those studies should be reconsidered especially for LHC.
