# Coulomb excitation of $^{124}$Te: Emerging collectivity and persisting seniority structure in the $6_1^+$ level

> The low-lying energy spectra of even-even tellurium isotopes near midshell have long been interpreted as `textbook' examples of vibrational collective motion. However, in many cases electric-quadrupole observables, which are a particularly sensitive probe of collectivity, remain undetermined. Coulomb-excitation measurements were performed to measure transition strengths connecting the ground and low-excitation states in $^{124}$Te. This isotope lies at a transitional point between collective structure near the neutron midshell and seniority structures near the $N=82$ shell. A transition strength, $B(E2; 6_1^+ \to 4_1^+)$, of 27(9)~W.u. was measured for the $6^+_1\rightarrow4^+_1$ transition for the first time in this nucleus; this value is significantly below that expected for a spherical vibrator, as well as other collective models. We examine the transition strengths in $^{124}$Te and its neighbors by comparison with large-basis shell-model calculations and by comparison with General Collective Model (GCM) fits. A GCM description of $^{120}$Te agrees with experimental $E2$ transition strengths, but no comparable description of $^{124}$Te is possible with the GCM. In contrast, there is remarkably good agreement between the $B(E2; 6_1^+ \to 4_1^+)$ values and shell-model calculations for $^{124-134}$Te. It appears that, despite approaching midshell, $^{124}$Te retains a seniority structure for the $6^+_1$ level, i.e. a significant $π0g_{7/2}^2$ contribution. This persistence of the shell structure at the $6^+_1$ state is in contrast to the $B(E2)$ values of the lower-excitation $2^+_1$ and $4^+_1$ states in $^{124}$Te, and neighboring $^{120}$Te and $^{122}$Te, for which the collectivity becomes enhanced as more neutrons are removed from $N=82$.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of the $B(E2; 6_1^+ \to 4_1^+)$ transition strength in $^{124}$Te, finding it to be 27(9) W.u., which is significantly below the value expected for a spherical vibrator or other collective models; this indicates that, despite approaching the neutron midshell, the $6_1^+$ level retains a seniority structure with a significant $\pi 0g_{7/2}^2$ contribution, in contrast to the lower-excitation $2_1^+$ and $4_1^+$ states where collectivity becomes enhanced.

## Method

Coulomb-excitation measurements were performed at the Heavy Ion Accelerator Facility at the Australian National University using beams of 185.5-MeV $^{58}$Ni and 45-MeV $^{16}$O incident on a 300-$\mu$g/cm$^2$ thick $^{124}$Te target. Particle-$\gamma$ coincidence spectroscopy was conducted using the CAESAR array of eight Compton-suppressed HPGe detectors and a new array of silicon photodiodes, and reduced $E2$ matrix elements were extracted from the measured $\gamma$-ray yields using the semiclassical Coulomb-excitation code Gosia.

## Key numbers

* $B(E2; 6_1^+ \to 4_1^+)$ = 27(9) W.u. in $^{124}$Te, measured for the first time in this nucleus.
* The fixed matrix element for the $2_1^+ \to 0_1^+$ transition was set using $B(E2;0_1^+ \to 2_1^+) = 0.567(5)$ $e^2$b$^2$ from Saxena (2014), corresponding to $\langle 2_1^+ || M(E2) || 0_1^+ \rangle = 0.753$ $e$b.
* The spectroscopic quadrupole moment $Q_s(2_1^+) = -0.45(5)$ $e$b from Stone (2021) was used to fix the diagonal matrix element $\langle 2_1^+ || M(E2) || 2_1^+ \rangle = -0.594$ $e$b.
* The $2_2^+$ state lifetime was 0.85(17) ps, and the $4_2^+$ state lifetime was 0.52(11) ps, taken from Hicks (2017) $(n,n'\gamma)$ measurements.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of transition strengths in tellurium isotopes, addressing the absence of $B(E2)$ measurements for states above the notional second-phonon triplet in $^{124}$Te, which had previously limited assessment of seniority-like behavior. The results contradict the spherical vibrator description and challenge the General Collective Model (GCM) description for $^{124}$Te, while showing good agreement with large-basis shell-model calculations for $^{124-134}$Te, consistent with the proposal by Lee (1991) that $6_1^+$ states arise from two-proton coupling. The findings enable a deeper understanding of the transition from seniority structure near $N=82$ to collective behavior near midshell, and the new experimental setup at the Australian National University facilitates future Coulomb-excitation measurements in this region.
