# Role of $Σ^*(1385)$ on $Λ$ hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The effect of $Σ^*(1385)$ baryon resonance on the time evolution of the $Λ$ hyperon polarization in hadronic matter is studied using a kinetic approach. This approach explicitly includes the production of the $Σ^*$ resonance from the $Λ-π$ and $Σ(1192)-π$ scatterings as well as its decay into $Λ+π$ or $Σ+π$. The resulting coupled kinetic equations governing the time evolution of $Λ$, $Σ$ and $Σ^*$ numbers and polarizations are solved for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV and 20-50\% centrality, using initial values determined by thermal yields and the thermal vorticity at chemical freeze-out temperature. As the hadronic matter expands and cools, the $Λ$ polarization is found to increase slightly during early times and then decreases very slowly afterwards, while the $Σ$ polarization remains nearly constant and the $Σ^*$ polarization continuously decreases. Including feed-down contributions to the $Λ$ polarization from the decays of partially polarized $Σ^0$, $Σ^*$, and $Ξ(1322)$ hyperons, where the $Ξ$ polarization is obtained by solving coupled kinetic equations for the $Ξ$ and $Ξ^*(1532)$ system, the resulting $Λ$ polarization becomes smaller and decreases over time. In both cases, however, the time variation of the $Λ$ polarization is sufficiently small to support the assumption of an early freeze-out of $Λ$ spin degree of freedom in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper finds that when the Σ*(1385) resonance is treated as a dynamic degree of freedom (rather than an instantaneous scattering approximation), the Λ hyperon polarization increases slightly during the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions, contrary to the slight decrease found in prior work. However, when feed-down contributions from Σ0, Σ*, and Ξ decays are included, the total Λ polarization becomes smaller and decreases over time; in both cases, the time variation is sufficiently small (10-15%) to support the assumption that the Λ spin degree of freedom effectively decouples at chemical freeze-out.

## Method
The authors developed spin-dependent coupled kinetic equations for the numbers and polarizations of Λ, Σ, and Σ* hyperons, explicitly including the production of Σ* from Λ-π and Σ-π scatterings and its decay back to Λ+π or Σ+π via Breit-Wigner resonance cross sections. These equations were solved for Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV and 20-50% centrality, using initial thermal yields and polarizations determined from thermal vorticity at chemical freeze-out temperature TC = 146 MeV, with the hadronic matter evolution modeled from chemical freeze-out (τC = 5.13 fm/c) to kinetic freeze-out (τK = 6.46 fm/c). Feed-down contributions to Λ polarization from Σ0, Σ*, and Ξ decays were included by solving analogous coupled kinetic equations for the Ξ and Ξ*(1532) system.

## Key numbers
- Initial Λ polarization at chemical freeze-out: PΛ(0) = 0.025, taken from AMPT model calculations for Au-Au at √sNN = 7.7 GeV.
- Initial Σ* polarization: PΣ*(0) = 0.042, derived as (5/3) × PΛ(0) from the non-relativistic vorticity relation.
- Λ polarization at kinetic freeze-out (τK = 6.46 fm/c) without feed-down: approximately 0.028, a slight increase from 0.025.
- Λ polarization including feed-down at τK = 6.46 fm/c: approximately 0.019, which is 34% smaller than the primary Λ polarization at that time.
- At an extended time τ = 8 fm/c (T = 112 MeV), Λ polarization including feed-down becomes 0.017, 39% smaller than the primary value.
- Σ* resonance total width at its mass: ΓΣ*(mΣ*) = 38 MeV, with branching ratios BΣ*→Λ+π = 0.88 and BΣ*→Σ+π = 0.12.
- Ξ*(1532) resonance decay width: ΓΞ*(mΞ*) = 10 MeV.
- Chemical freeze-out parameters: TC = 146 MeV, VC = 240 fm³, μB = 376 MeV, μS = 88 MeV; kinetic freeze-out: TK = 129 MeV, VK = 403 fm³.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic approach of Sung (2024) by treating the Σ* resonance as a dynamic degree of freedom rather than using an instantaneous Λ-π scattering approximation, and by including feed-down contributions from Σ0, Σ*, and Ξ decays. It builds on the proposal by Ko (2023) that early spin freeze-out occurs because Λ-π scattering proceeds through the spin-3/2 Σ* resonance, and on the polarization relaxation time calculations of Hidaka (2023). The results validate the common practice in hydrodynamic models (e.g., Karpenko 2017, Becattini 2021) and the coarse-grained transport model of Li (2017) of comparing theoretical Λ polarization at chemical freeze-out with experimental data, by showing that the spin degree of freedom changes by only 10-15% during the hadronic stage.
