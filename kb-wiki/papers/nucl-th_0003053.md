# Does localization occur in a hierarchical random-matrix model for many-body states?

> We use random-matrix theory and supersymmetry techniques to work out the two-point correlation function between states in a hierarchical model which employs Feshbach's chaining hypothesis: Classes of many-body states are introduced. Only states within the same or neighboring classes are coupled. We assume that the density of states per class grows monotonically with class index. The problem is mapped onto a one-dimensional non-linear sigma model. In the limit of a large number of states in each class we derive the critical exponent for the growth of the level density with class index for which delocalization sets in. From a realistic modelling of the class-dependence of the level density, we conclude that the model does not predict Fock-space localization in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a hierarchical random-matrix model for many-body states, localization is destroyed whenever the density of states increases more strongly than linearly with increasing complexity index m; for nuclei, where the density grows approximately as (E/Δ)^(2m), the model does not predict Fock-space localization.

## Method

The authors construct a hierarchical model based on Feshbach's chaining hypothesis, where classes of many-body states (m-particle, (m-1)-hole states) are introduced and only states within the same or neighboring classes are coupled. The Hamiltonian within each class is modeled by a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), and the two-point correlation function between states in different classes is computed using supersymmetry techniques and a one-dimensional non-linear sigma model. The problem is reduced to a modified heat equation, and the correlation function's dependence on class index distance is analyzed through a reparameterization that maps the system to the standard heat equation solved in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent for the growth of level density with class index is β = 1: for α(t) ∝ t^β, β = 1 marks the transition from localization to delocalization.
- The density of states in class m at excitation energy E is given by ρ_m(E) = (1/(m!(m+1)!(2m)!Δ)) (E/Δ)^(2m), where Δ is the mean level spacing of single-particle states.
- For the nuclear case with high excitation energy (E/Δ ≫ m), α_{m,m+1} ∝ (E/Δ)^(2m), yielding s_m ∝ 1 - (E/Δ)^(-2m), which approaches a finite constant asymptotically.
- The rescaled correlation function takes the form ~K(s) = (π^4 √π)/(16 ζ λ^2) (4ζ/s)^(3/2) exp(-s/(4ζ)), valid for s ≫ ζ.

## Context

This work extends the hierarchical random-matrix model of Nishioka, Verbaarschot, Weidenmueller, and Yoshida (1990) to investigate Fock-space localization in many-body Fermi systems. It contradicts the prediction of localization by Altshuler, Gefen, Kamenev, and Levitov (1997), who mapped the chain of m-particle (m-1)-hole states onto a Bethe lattice and found localization, but that mapping neglected interactions between states within each class. The numerical work of Leyronas et al. (1999) found evidence for localization in quantum dots, but the present authors note that constraint to a small energy neighborhood may affect localization properties. The result enables future work to refine the treatment of intra-class mixing in hierarchical many-body models.
