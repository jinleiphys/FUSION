# Optimizing the relativistic energy density functional with nuclear ground state and collective excitation properties

> We introduce a new relativistic energy density functional constrained by the ground state properties of atomic nuclei along with the isoscalar giant monopole resonance energy and dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb. A unified framework of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model and random phase approximation based on the relativistic density-dependent point coupling interaction is established in order to determine the DD-PCX parameterization by $χ^2$ minimization. This procedure is supplemented with the co-variance analysis in order to estimate statistical uncertainties in the model parameters and observables. The effective interaction DD-PCX accurately describes the nuclear ground state properties including the neutron-skin thickness, as well as the isoscalar giant monopole resonance excitation energies and dipole polarizabilities. The implementation of the experimental data on nuclear excitations allows constraining the symmetry energy close to the saturation density, and the incompressibility of nuclear matter by using genuine observables on finite nuclei in the $χ^2$ minimization protocol, rather than using pseudo-observables on the nuclear matter, or by relying on the ground state properties only, as it has been customary in the previous studies.

## Key claim
The authors introduce a new relativistic energy density functional (DD-PCX) that is constrained not only by nuclear ground state properties but also by direct implementation of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energy and dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb, rather than using pseudo-observables on nuclear matter as in previous studies. The resulting interaction yields lower incompressibility and symmetry energy parameters than earlier relativistic functionals, but the authors note that the ISGMR energy value used (13.5 MeV from Patel et al. 2013) directly determines the low compression modulus, and using the alternative Texas A&M value (14.18 MeV) would lead to a higher value of $K_0$.

## Method
The authors established a unified framework combining the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model for ground states with self-consistent relativistic (quasiparticle) random phase approximation (Q)RPA for collective excitations, based on a relativistic density-dependent point-coupling interaction. The 12 model parameters (10 interaction parameters plus 2 pairing strength parameters) were determined by minimizing a $\chi^2$ objective function using binding energies (34 nuclei), charge radii (26 nuclei), mean pairing gaps (15 nuclei), the constrained ISGMR energy, and the dipole polarizability for $^{208}$Pb. The optimization was supplemented with covariance analysis to estimate statistical uncertainties of parameters and observables.

## Key numbers
- ISGMR energy in $^{208}$Pb: constrained value taken as 13.5 MeV (from Patel et al. 2013), with adopted error of 1.0% in the fitting protocol; alternative experimental value 14.18 $\pm$ 0.11 MeV (from Youngblood et al. 1999) also noted
- Dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb: 19.6 $\pm$ 0.6 fm$^3$ (from Roca-Maza et al. 2015), with adopted error of 0.5% in the fitting protocol
- Nuclear matter incompressibility $K_0$ from DD-PCX: 213.03 $\pm$ 3.54 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation $J$ from DD-PCX: 31.12 $\pm$ 0.32 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation $L$ from DD-PCX: 46.32 $\pm$ 1.68 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb predicted by DD-PCX: 0.159 $\pm$ 0.005 fm
- Neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca predicted by DD-PCX: 0.172 $\pm$ 0.003 fm
- Neutron skin thickness in $^{132}$Sn predicted by DD-PCX: 0.218 $\pm$ 0.005 fm
- Root mean square error for binding energies (65 nuclei) with DD-PCX: 1.38 MeV (0.21% relative)
- Root mean square error for charge radii (46 nuclei) with DD-PCX: 0.016 fm (0.47% relative)
- Root mean square error for mean pairing gaps (56 nuclei) with DD-PCX: 0.18 MeV (15.40% relative)

## Context
This work extends the line of relativistic energy density functional development from earlier interactions such as DD-PC1 (Niksic et al. 2008), DD-ME2 (Lalazissis et al. 2005), and PC-PK1 (Zhao et al. 2010), which were constrained only by ground state properties and sometimes by pseudo-observables on nuclear matter. The only prior direct use of ISGMR in functional optimization was in the FSUGold2 interaction (Piekarewicz et al. 2014). The new DD-PCX interaction enables reliable predictions of dipole polarizabilities and neutron skin thicknesses across nuclei, and demonstrates the need to resolve experimental ambiguities in ISGMR data for $^{208}$Pb to accurately constrain nuclear matter incompressibility.
