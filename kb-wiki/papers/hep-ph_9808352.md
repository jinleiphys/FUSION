# Coherence Time in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> Precisely measured Drell-Yan cross sections for 800 GeV protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets exhibit a deviation from linear scaling in the atomic number A. We show that this deviation can be accounted for by energy degradation of the proton as it passes through the nucleus if account is taken of the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence. We infer an average proper coherence time of 0.4 +- 0.1 fm/c, corresponding to a coherence path length of 8 +- 2 fm in the rest frame of the nucleus.

## Key claim
The deviation from linear scaling in atomic number A observed in precisely measured Drell-Yan cross sections for 800 GeV protons on nuclear targets can be accounted for by energy degradation of the proton if a finite, nonzero quantum coherence time is included; the inferred average proper coherence time is 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c, corresponding to a coherence path length of 8 ± 2 fm in the rest frame of the nucleus.

## Method
The authors used a linear extrapolation from proton-proton scattering (LEXUS) to model proton-nucleus collisions, parameterizing the inclusive rapidity distribution of the beam proton and folding it with the nuclear density distribution. They computed Drell-Yan yields using the parton model with GRV structure functions to leading order with a K factor, and introduced a variable time delay by assuming the energy available for Drell-Yan production is that of the proton after n previous collisions. The collision number shift n was converted to a coherence time by boosting from the center-of-mass frame to the target rest frame and equating the path length to n times the mean free path.

## Key numbers
- Proper coherence time: 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c, inferred from the best representation of the data corresponding to n = 5 ± 1 collisions.
- Coherence path length in the rest frame of the nucleus: 8 ± 2 fm.
- Total nucleon-nucleon cross section used: 40 mb.
- Nuclear matter density used: 0.155 nucleons/fm³.
- Beam energy: 800 GeV protons incident on nuclear targets.
- Drell-Yan muon pair invariant mass range: 4 to 9 GeV and greater than 11 GeV (to eliminate J/ψ and Υ contributions).
- Feynman xF bins: 0.05 to 0.65, with data shown for 0.05, 0.35, and 0.65.
- Parameter λ in the rapidity distribution: 0.6, independent of beam energy in the range 12–400 GeV.
- Rapidity shift for produced hadrons encountering a struck target nucleon: 0.16.

## Context
This work extends the LEXUS model (Gale and Kapusta, 1991) for high-energy proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions by applying it to Drell-Yan production and incorporating quantum coherence effects. It addresses deviations from A scaling observed in the E772 experiment at Fermilab (Alde et al., 1990), and the inferred coherence time is relevant for understanding the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect and for modeling particle production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The analysis enables future improvements by adding quantum mechanical amplitudes for multiple scattering and has implications for nucleus-nucleus collisions under investigation.
