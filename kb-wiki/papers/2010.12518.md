# Comparing different density-matrix expansions for long-range pion exchange

> Empirical energy density functionals (EDFs) are generally successful in describing nuclear properties across the table of nuclides. But their limitations motivate using the density-matrix expansion (DME) to embed long-range pion interactions into a Skyrme functional. Recent results on the impact of the pion were both encouraging and puzzling, necessitating a careful re-examination of the DME implementation. Here we take the first steps, focusing on two-body scalar terms in the DME. Exchange energies with long-range one-pion contributions are well approximated by all DME implementations considered, with preference for variants that do not truncate at two derivatives in every EDF term. The use of the DME for chiral pion contributions is therefore supported by this investigation. For scalar-isovector energies it is important to treat neutrons and protons separately. The results are found to apply under broad conditions, although self-consistency is not yet tested.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares different density-matrix expansion (DME) variants for approximating exchange energies from long-range one-pion exchange, finding that all considered DME implementations perform well for scalar-isoscalar energies but that variants which do not truncate at two derivatives in every EDF term are preferred, and that for scalar-isovector energies neutrons and protons must be treated separately; however, self-consistency has not yet been tested.

## Method

The authors performed non-self-consistent tests of nine different DME variants (Slater, PSA, NV, SVCK, DT, CB, BZ, Gaussian, and MG) on the scalar part of the one-body density matrix for a range of closed-shell nuclei. They compared the DME-approximated exchange energies from long-range one-pion exchange against exact Hartree-Fock results using orbitals generated from self-consistent HF calculations with the SLy4 energy density functional. The analysis focused on two-body scalar terms and employed both truncated and full-square treatments of the density-matrix square.

## Key numbers

- The Slater approximation uses a momentum scale \(k_F = [3\pi^2 \rho_q(\mathbf{R})]^{1/3}\) as the local density approximation for the Fermi momentum.
- The Campi-MacKellar momentum scale is defined as \(k_{FC} = \{ (5/[3\rho_q(\mathbf{R})]) [\tau_q(\mathbf{R}) - (1/4) \nabla^2 \rho_q(\mathbf{R})] \}^{1/2}\).
- For the MG-DME, the parameter \(Y = 21.5\) was obtained as an average over neutrons and protons across the nuclei considered.
- The NV-DME density-matrix square becomes negative for \(2.8\ \text{fm} \lesssim r \lesssim 5.9\ \text{fm}\) at \(R = 6.7\ \text{fm}\) in \(^{132}\text{Sn}\).
- The PSA-DME \(\Pi_2\) function falls off like \(1/r^2\) for large \(r\), causing the integrated-idempotency constraint to yield an infinite right-hand side.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach of Gebremariam (2011), Stoitsov (2010), Dyhdalo (2017), and Navarro Perez (2018), which used a simplified phase-space-averaging DME to embed long-range chiral interactions into Skyrme functionals. The puzzling results from Navarro Perez (2018), where leading-order one-pion exchange showed no overall improvement and three-nucleon forces broke the trend, motivated this systematic comparison of DME variants. The findings support using DMEs for chiral pion contributions and enable future self-consistent implementations and extensions to vector parts of the OBDM and three-nucleon sectors.
