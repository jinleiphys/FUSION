# Real-time dynamics of axial charge and chiral magnetic current in a non-Abelian expanding plasma

> Understanding axial charge dynamics driven by changes in Chern-Simons number densities is a key aspect in understanding the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions. Most phenomenological simulations assume that a large amount of axial charge is produced in the initial stages and that axial charge is conserved throughout the simulation. Within an (expanding) homogeneous holographic plasma, we investigate the real-time axial charge relaxation dynamics and their impact on the chiral magnetic current. Moreover, we discuss the real-time interplay of the non-Abelian and the Abelian chiral anomaly in the presence of a strong magnetic field. In the expanding plasma, the Chern-Simons diffusion rate and thus the axial charge relaxation rate are time dependent due to the decaying magnetic field. We quantify the changes in the late time falloffs and establish a horizon formula for the chiral magnetic current.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in an expanding holographic plasma, axial charge is not conserved but relaxes due to Chern-Simons diffusion, and this relaxation accelerates at late times because the decaying magnetic field weakens the "protective" effect of the Abelian anomaly, contrary to the common assumption in phenomenological simulations that axial charge is conserved throughout the plasma evolution.

## Method

The authors use a holographic bottom-up Stückelberg model with a massive axial gauge field coupled to a vector gauge field via Chern-Simons terms, dual to a strongly coupled plasma with both non-Abelian and Abelian chiral anomalies. They solve the Einstein-Maxwell equations numerically in the small axial charge limit, first for a static plasma background and then for a boost-invariant expanding plasma (Bjorken flow), extracting the real-time evolution of axial charge density and chiral magnetic current from the near-boundary asymptotics of the gauge fields.

## Key numbers

- The axial charge relaxation rate \(\Gamma/T\) increases with the non-Abelian anomaly strength \(m_s\); for \(B/T^2=0.01\) the curves for \(\alpha=0\) and \(\alpha=6/19\) are indistinguishable by eye, while for \(B/T^2=2.96\) the green curve (\(\alpha=0\)) shows larger \(\Gamma/T\) than the black curve (small B), but the red curve (\(\alpha=6/19\)) shows smaller \(\Gamma/T\) than the green curve at the same strong field.
- For \(\alpha \lesssim 0.15\) increasing the magnetic field shortens the axial charge lifetime, but for \(\alpha \gtrsim 0.15\) increasing \(B/T^2\) protects axial charge and it relaxes slower.
- The matching to three-flavor QCD yields \(\alpha/(2\kappa_5^2) = 1/(8\pi^2)\) and \(\kappa_5^2 = 24\pi^2/19\), giving \(\alpha = 6/19\).
- In the expanding plasma, the magnetic field decays as \(B \sim \tau^{-1}\) at late times, and if axial charge were conserved the density would decay as \(n_5 \sim \tau^{-1}\) and the CME current as \(\sim \tau^{-4/3}\); the actual decay is accelerated due to the explicit \(U(1)_A\) breaking.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of the chiral magnetic effect in static plasmas (Lin 2013, Ammon 2016, Grieninger 2016, Ghosh 2021, Grieninger 2021) and expanding plasmas (Cartwright 2021) by incorporating both the non-Abelian anomaly (via the Stückelberg mechanism) and the Abelian anomaly simultaneously, with dynamical gauge fields and strong magnetic fields. It enables more realistic simulations of the CME in heavy-ion collisions by quantifying how axial charge relaxation modifies the time-dependent chiral magnetic current, addressing a key uncertainty in interpreting the RHIC isobar run data (STAR 2021, Kharzeev 2022, Lacey 2022).
