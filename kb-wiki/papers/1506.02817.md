# The torque effect and fluctuations of entropy deposition in rapidity in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> The decorrelation of the orientation of the event-plane angles in the initial state of relativistic Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions, the "torque effect," is studied in a model of entropy deposition in the longitudinal direction involving fluctuations of the longitudinal source profile on large scales. The radiation from a single wounded nucleon is asymmetric in space-time rapidity. It is assumed that the extent in rapidity of the region of deposited entropy is random. Fluctuations in the deposition of entropy from each source increase the event-plane decorrelation: for Pb-Pb collisions they improve the description of the data, while for p-Pb collisions the mechanism is absolutely essential to generate any sizable decorrelation. We also show that the experimental data for rank-four flow may be explained via folding of the elliptic flow. The results suggest the existence of long range fluctuations in the space-time distribution of entropy in the initial stages of relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The decorrelation of event-plane angles as a function of pseudorapidity (the "torque effect") can be explained by introducing fluctuations in the longitudinal extent of entropy deposition from each wounded nucleon, where the endpoint in rapidity is randomly distributed. For Pb-Pb collisions this mechanism improves the description of experimental data, while for p-Pb collisions it is absolutely essential to generate any sizable decorrelation that matches CMS measurements.

## Method

The authors model entropy deposition in space-time rapidity using wounded nucleons with a string-like emission profile where each source deposits entropy uniformly over a random interval in rapidity, with the endpoint distributed uniformly in the available range. The fireball density is constructed as a sum over right-moving and left-moving wounded nucleons with an admixture of binary collisions, using the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo generator. The event-plane decorrelation is quantified via the factorization ratio r_n defined by the CMS collaboration, which compares two-particle cumulants between forward and backward pseudorapidity bins. The initial-state torque in space-time rapidity is taken as a proxy for the final-state event-plane decorrelation in pseudorapidity, based on the approximate preservation of the torque angle through hydrodynamic evolution.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion of event-plane decorrelation as a function of pseudorapidity by Bozek (2010) and the factorization ratio method proposed by the CMS collaboration (2015). The model builds on the wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and the asymmetric rapidity emission idea from Brodsky (1977). The results contradict the expectation from average Glauber models that p-Pb collisions would show negligible decorrelation, and instead demonstrate that long-range fluctuations in entropy deposition are necessary. The study enables future work using full 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations and comparisons with the color glass condensate approach of McLerran (1993).
