# Surface Tension at Finite Tempearture in the MIT Bag Model

> At $ T = 0 $ the surface tension $ σ^{1/3} $ in the MIT bag model for a single hadron is known to be negligible as compared to the bag pressure $ B^{1/4}$. We show that at finite temperature it has a substantial value of 50 - 70 MeV which also differ from hadron to hadron. We also find that the dynamics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma is such that the creation of hybrids $(s\bar{s}g)$ with massive quarks will predominate over the creation of $ (s\bar{s}) $ mesons.

## Key claim

At finite temperature, the intrinsic surface tension σ^{1/3} in the MIT bag model for a single hadron becomes substantial (50–70 MeV) and varies from hadron to hadron, whereas at T = 0 it is negligible compared to the bag pressure B^{1/4}. The authors also find that during hadronisation of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, the creation of strange hybrids (s\bar{s}g) with massive quarks is favoured over the creation of (s\bar{s}) mesons.

## Method

The authors treat a single hadron as a hot bag in contact with a heat bath, using the grand canonical ensemble for non-interacting quarks, antiquarks, and gluons confined in an MIT bag. For a system of one s-quark (mass m_s = 150 MeV) and one gluon, they vary the bag radius R at fixed temperature to minimise the free energy, obtaining the equilibrium radius and mass for the hybrid (s\bar{s}g) and the meson (s\bar{s}). They then extract the intrinsic surface tension σ^{1/3} by equating the equilibrium mass to the expression M = 4 B V + 12 π R^2 σ, which includes a surface energy term for massive quarks.

## Key numbers

- For B^{1/4} = 200 MeV, the hybrid (s\bar{s}g) at T = 156 MeV has radius R = 0.943 fm and mass M = 3.086 GeV, giving σ^{1/3} = 57 MeV; the meson (s\bar{s}) at the same temperature gives σ^{1/3} = 63 MeV.
- For B^{1/4} = 250 MeV, the hybrid at T = 195 MeV has R = 0.758 fm and M = 3.87 GeV, giving σ^{1/3} = 66 MeV; the meson has R = 0.647 fm and M = 2.459 GeV, giving σ^{1/3} = 72 MeV.
- For B^{1/4} = 150 MeV, σ^{1/3} = 48 MeV for the hybrid and 53 MeV for the meson.
- For B^{1/4} = 200 MeV, as the strange quark mass m_s varies from 50 MeV to 300 MeV, σ^{1/3}(s\bar{s}g) increases from 39 MeV to 75 MeV and σ^{1/3}(s\bar{s}) from 43 MeV to 82 MeV, with temperature changing only slightly (155–157 MeV).
- For the (s\bar{s}) system at B^{1/4} = 200 MeV, σ^{1/3} decreases slightly from 68 MeV to 63 MeV as temperature increases from 5 MeV to 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Paria, Mustafa, and Abbas (1997) on non-strange hybrids in QGP hadronisation, now incorporating massive strange quarks. It builds on the finding by Farhi and Jaffe (1984) that intrinsic surface tension is negligible at T = 0, and on the work of Mardor and Svetitsky (1991) who calculated free energies of finite QGP droplets and hadronic bubbles near the confinement phase transition. The results enable further study of hybrid mesons as possible signatures of QGP, and suggest that the mixed phase during hadronisation may contain massive hybrids, potentially reducing the lifetime of the mixed phase.
