# Towards the determination of the gluon helicity distribution in the nucleon from lattice quantum chromodynamics

> We present the first exploratory lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculation of the polarized gluon Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution in the nucleon. The Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution provides a frame-independent and gauge-invariant framework to determine the gluon helicity in the nucleon from first principles. We employ a high-statistics computation using a $32^3\times 64$ lattice ensemble characterized by a $358$ MeV pion mass and a $0.094$ fm lattice spacing. We establish the pseudo-distribution approach as a feasible method to address the proton spin puzzle with successive improvements in statistical and systematic uncertainties anticipated in the future. Within the statistical precision of our data, we find a good comparison between the lattice determined polarized gluon Ioffe-time distribution and the corresponding expectations from the state-of-the-art global analyses. We find a hint for a nonzero gluon spin contribution to the proton spin from the model-independent extraction of the gluon helicity pseudo-distribution over a range of Ioffe-time, $ν\lesssim 9$.

## Key claim

This first exploratory lattice QCD calculation of the polarized gluon Ioffe-time pseudo-distribution finds a hint for a nonzero gluon spin contribution to the proton spin over a range of Ioffe-time ν ≲ 9, but the result is qualified by large statistical uncertainties and the authors state that within the statistical precision of their data, the lattice-determined distribution compares well with expectations from global analyses.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlators on a 32³ × 64 lattice ensemble with 358 MeV pion mass and 0.094 fm lattice spacing, using 1901 gauge configurations with 64 temporal sources each. They constructed the gluonic operators using gradient flow at eight flow times τ/a² = 1.0 to 3.8, applied momentum smearing to access nucleon boosts up to p_z = 2.46 GeV, and extracted ground-state matrix elements via a summed generalized eigenvalue problem (sGEVP) with a reduced basis of nucleon interpolators. The polarized gluon reduced pseudo-ITD was formed from the ratio of spin-dependent to spin-averaged matrix elements, canceling multiplicative renormalization factors.

## Key numbers

* Lattice ensemble: 32³ × 64 volume, pion mass m_π = 358(3) MeV, lattice spacing a = 0.094(1) fm.
* Nucleon boosts accessed: p_z = 0, 0.41, 0.82, 1.23, 1.64, 2.05, and 2.46 GeV (n = 0 to 6 in units of 2π/La).
* Field separations computed: z = a to 8a, corresponding to 0.094 fm to 0.752 fm.
* Flow times used: τ/a² = 1.0, 1.4, 1.8, 2.2, 2.6, 3.0, 3.4, and 3.8.
* The analysis covers Ioffe-time ν ≲ 9, where a hint for nonzero gluon spin contribution is reported.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-PDF approach of Radyushkin (2017) and Balitsky (2021) to the polarized gluon case, providing the first lattice QCD calculation of the x-dependent gluon helicity distribution in the nucleon. It contrasts with the earlier direct lattice calculation of ΔG by Yang (2016), which used a different formalism and suffered from large matching systematic errors. The results enable future high-statistics lattice determinations of Δg(x,Q²) and ΔG(Q²) that can be compared with experimental programs at RHIC, JLab, COMPASS, and the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
