# Melting of antikaon condensate in protoneutron stars

> We study the melting of a $K^-$ condensate in hot and neutrino-trapped protoneutron stars. In this connection, we adopt relativistic field theoretical models to describe the hadronic and condensed phases. It is observed that the critical temperature of antikaon condensation is enhanced as baryon density increases. For a fixed baryon density, the critical temperature of antikaon condensation in a protoneutron star is smaller than that of a neutron star. We also exhibit the phase diagram of a protoneutron star with a $K^-$ condensate.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for antikaon condensation in hot, neutrino-trapped protoneutron stars is smaller than that in neutrino-free neutron stars at the same baryon density, and the condensate melts at a lower temperature in protoneutron stars due to their higher heat content.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean-field models to describe both the hadronic phase (with nucleons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange) and the antikaon condensed phase, treating the kaon-baryon interaction via minimal coupling. The antikaon condensation is treated as a second-order phase transition, and the thermodynamic potential is computed at finite temperature for matter in beta-equilibrium with trapped neutrinos and a fixed lepton fraction of Y_L = 0.35. The critical temperature is determined by the temperature at which the condensate density vanishes for a given baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for K- condensation in protoneutron star matter at entropy per baryon S=2 is 4.2 n_0 (where n_0 = 0.153 fm^-3 is nuclear saturation density); at zero temperature it is 3.68 n_0.
- Threshold densities for antikaon condensation in neutrino-free neutron stars with U_K = -120 MeV are 3.16 n_0 for S=2 and 3.05 n_0 for S=0.
- Maximum protoneutron star masses are 2.228 M_sun for S=2 and 2.214 M_sun for S=0; corresponding central densities are 5.3 n_0 and 5.59 n_0, respectively.
- The antikaon optical potential depth used is U_K = -120 MeV, which yields a maximum neutron star mass of 2.08 M_sun.
- At temperature T=40 MeV, entropy per baryon for Y_L=0.35 is 1.6 and for Y_nu_e=0 is 1.3.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars by Banik et al. (2012) and Pons et al. (2001) to the specific case of hot, neutrino-trapped protoneutron stars, where no prior calculations of the critical temperature existed. The results are relevant for understanding thermal nucleation of antikaon condensed droplets in protoneutron stars, as studied earlier by Banik et al. (2011), and show that the condensate melts at lower temperatures in protoneutron stars than in neutrino-free neutron stars, which may affect the dynamical evolution of the condensation.
