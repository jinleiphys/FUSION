# Multi-strange and charmed hadrons: A novel probe for the QCD equation of state at high baryon densities

> Nuclear experiments near and below the threshold of hyperon production have shown that the production of Kaons is a sensitive probe for the dense QCD equation of state. At beam energies up to 1.5AGeV, strangeness production can probe the equation of state for densities up to approximately twice nuclear saturation. In this paper we will discuss the possibilities of extending this range in density by the study of multi-strange baryons as well as charmed hadrons in the SIS100 beam energy range up to $10A$GeV. Here, densities up to five times nuclear saturation can be reached and the production of multi-strange and charmed hadrons shows a strong sensitivity to the equation of state. On the other hand a precise prediction of the effect of the equation of state will require knowledge of the fundamental production cross section near the elementary production threshold in p+p collisions which is yet not measured for the hadrons discussed.

## Key claim

Multi-strange baryons and charmed hadrons produced in the SIS100 beam energy range (up to 10A GeV) can probe the QCD equation of state at densities up to five times nuclear saturation, extending the density range accessible via kaon production at lower energies; however, precise predictions require knowledge of the fundamental production cross sections in p+p collisions near the elementary threshold, which are not yet measured for these hadrons.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v4.0 microscopic transport model, which propagates hadrons in phase-space with binary scattering terms and long-range QMD potentials. They implemented a density- and momentum-dependent chiral mean-field (CMF) equation of state for all baryons, consistent with neutron star observations and lattice QCD results, and compared results to the cascade mode (no potentials, effectively a softer hadron resonance gas equation of state). The centrality dependence of hadron multiplicities was characterized by the parameter alpha, extracted from fits to the four most central centrality bins in Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies up to 10A GeV at SIS100 can reach densities up to five times nuclear saturation, extending from approximately twice nuclear saturation accessible at beam energies up to 1.5A GeV
- Elementary threshold energies in p+p collisions: Lambda and Kaon at 2.55 GeV, anti-Kaon at 2.87 GeV, Xi at 3.25 GeV, Omega at 4.10 GeV, Lambda_C at 5.09 GeV, J/Psi at 4.97 GeV (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)
- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.41 GeV, the number of participants is 303 for 0-10% centrality; for sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0-5.0 GeV, it is 310 for 0-10% centrality
- The alpha parameter for Kaons from UrQMD with CMF equation of state gives a good description of experimental data over a wide energy range, while the cascade mode (softer equation of state) shows a drastic increase in alpha below the elementary threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies that established kaon and hyperon production as sensitive probes of the dense QCD equation of state at beam energies below 2A GeV (Hartnack 1993, Fuchs 2005, Hartnack 2005, Hartnack 2011). The authors build on the UrQMD model framework (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999) and the CMF equation of state (Motornenko 2019, Steinheimer 2024) that was shown to describe flow and hadron production at SIS18 energies (Steinheimer 2025). The paper identifies that for multi-strange and charmed hadrons, the alpha parameter is strongly influenced not only by the equation of state but also by poorly constrained production cross sections and strangeness exchange reactions, as demonstrated by the discrepancy between UrQMD simulations and preliminary STAR data for Xi baryons. The work calls for dedicated baseline measurements of elementary production cross sections in p+p and p+A collisions at SIS100 as a prerequisite for interpreting the heavy-ion data.
