# Strong Orientation Dependence of Multinucleon Transfer Processes in $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn Reaction

> We theoretically investigate multinucleon transfer (MNT) processes in $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction at $E_\mathrm{lab}=5.7$ MeV/$A$ using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. For this reaction, measurements of MNT processes have been reported, showing substantial MNT cross sections accompanying more than ten protons. From the calculation, we find that the amount of transferred nucleons depends much on the relative orientation between the deformation axis of $^{238}$U and the relative vector connecting centers of $^{238}$U and $^{124}$Sn nuclei. We find a formation of thick neck when the $^{238}$U collides from its tip with $^{124}$Sn. However, the neck formation is substantially suppressed when $^{238}$U collides from its side. We have found that a large number of protons are transferred in the tip collision. This is caused by the breaking of the neck and subsequent absorption of nucleons in the neck region. We thus conclude that the measured MNT processes involving about ten protons originate from the neck breaking dynamics in the tip collisions of a deformed $^{238}$U nucleus.

## Key claim

The measured multinucleon transfer (MNT) cross sections involving more than ten protons in the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction can only be explained by tip collisions of the deformed $^{238}$U nucleus, where an elongated thick neck forms and breaks, rather than by side collisions where neck formation is suppressed.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy5 parameter set on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 0.8 fm spacing, applying a particle number projection (PNP) technique to compute transfer probabilities. They simulated $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn collisions at $E_\mathrm{lab}=5.7$ MeV/$A$ for impact parameters from 0 to 10 fm, considering three initial orientations of the prolately deformed $^{238}$U (symmetry axis parallel to the collision axis, to the impact parameter vector, or perpendicular to the collision plane). Production cross sections for $^{124}$Sn-like fragments were evaluated by integrating transfer probabilities over impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- $E_\mathrm{lab}=5.7$ MeV/$A$: incident energy per nucleon for the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn reaction
- $^{238}$U ground state deformation: prolately deformed with $\beta \sim 0.27$; $^{124}$Sn ground state: oblately deformed with $\beta \sim 0.11$
- For tip collision (symmetry axis parallel to collision axis): cross section of $10^{-3}$ mb for production of $^{116}_{44}$Ru$_{72}$ (transfer of 2 neutrons and 6 protons from $^{124}$Sn) and $10^{-3}$ mb for $^{150}_{64}$Gd$_{86}$ (transfer of 12 neutrons and 14 protons to $^{124}$Sn)
- For side collision (symmetry axis perpendicular to collision axis): cross section of $10^{-3}$ mb for production of $^{134}_{56}$Ba$_{78}$ (transfer of 4 neutrons and 6 protons to $^{124}$Sn)
- In tip collision head-on ($b=0$ fm): average transfer of about 11 neutrons and 7 protons from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn, producing fragments resembling $^{142}_{57}$La$_{85}$ and $^{219}_{85}$At$_{134}$
- In side collision head-on: average transfer of only 1 neutron and 1 proton from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn
- Measured cross sections extend to mass number $A \sim 148$ and proton number $Z \sim 64$, corresponding to transfer of 8 neutrons and 14 protons from $^{238}$U to $^{124}$Sn

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Mayer and collaborators (about 30 years prior) which showed substantial MNT cross sections in $^{238}$U-induced dissipative collisions, including the $^{238}$U+$^{124}$Sn system. The authors had previously developed the TDHF-based method with particle number projection (Sekizawa and Yabana, 2014) and demonstrated its quantitative capability for MNT processes. The results enable future studies of projectile-target combinations, incident energy dependence, and particle evaporation effects to further clarify the reaction mechanism.
