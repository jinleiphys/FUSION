# Nuclear Energy Density Optimization: UNEDF2

> The parameters of the UNEDF2 nuclear energy density functional (EDF) model were obtained in an optimization to experimental data consisting of nuclear binding energies, proton radii, odd-even mass staggering data, fission-isomer excitation energies, and single particle energies. In addition to parameter optimization, sensitivity analysis was done to obtain parameter uncertainties and correlations. The resulting UNEDF2 is an all-around EDF. However, the sensitivity analysis also demonstrated that the limits of current Skyrme-like EDFs have been reached and that novel approaches are called for.

## Key claim

The UNEDF2 energy density functional achieves a balanced all-around description of nuclear properties, but the sensitivity analysis demonstrates that the limits of current Skyrme-like EDFs have been reached and that novel approaches are called for, with any major further improvement unlikely given the current observables.

## Method

The parameters of the Skyrme-like energy density functional were optimized by minimizing a chi-squared objective function against experimental data including binding energies of 29 spherical and 47 deformed nuclei, 13 odd-even mass staggering data points, 28 proton radii, 4 fission isomer excitation energies, and 9 single-particle level energy splittings. The optimization was performed at the axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov level using the HFBTHO code with 20 major harmonic oscillator shells, employing the POUNDERS algorithm with a hybrid parallel OpenMP+MPI scheme. A complete sensitivity analysis was performed to obtain parameter uncertainties and correlations, and the functional was verified to satisfy the linear-response criterion for finite-size instabilities in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

* Total root-mean-square deviation from experimental binding energies: 1.95 MeV (compared to 1.43 MeV for UNEDF0 and 1.91 MeV for UNEDF1)
* Root-mean-square deviation for two-neutron separation energies: 0.84 MeV
* Root-mean-square deviation for two-proton separation energies: 0.78 MeV
* Root-mean-square deviation for proton radii: 0.018 fm
* Root-mean-square deviation for single-particle levels in doubly-magic nuclei: 1.38 MeV
* Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.15631 fm^-3 with standard deviation 0.00112 fm^-3
* Nuclear matter incompressibility: 239.930 MeV with standard deviation 10.119 MeV
* Symmetry energy: 29.131 MeV with standard deviation 0.321 MeV
* Slope of symmetry energy: 40.0 MeV (not constrained by data)
* Energy per particle in infinite nuclear matter: -15.8 MeV
* Scalar effective mass: 1.074 (unitless) with standard deviation 0.052

## Context

This work extends the UNEDF series of Skyrme energy density functionals, following UNEDF0 (Kortelainen 2010) which established the optimization procedure at the deformed HFB level, and UNEDF1 (Kortelainen 2012) which improved fission properties by including fission isomer excitation energies. UNEDF2 focuses on shell structure by including tensor coupling constants and single-particle level data, achieving a balanced all-around functional. The sensitivity analysis confirms conclusions from Kortelainen (2008) and Tarpanov (2014) that further major improvements with Skyrme-like EDFs are unlikely, motivating development of novel EDFs with higher-order terms or enriched density dependence as pursued by Carlsson (2008), Dobaczewski (2012), Raimondi (2014), and Becker (2014).
