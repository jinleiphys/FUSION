# Semi-inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering off Few-Nucleon Systems: Tagging the EMC Effect and Hadronization Mechanisms with Detection of Slow Recoiling Nuclei

> The semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering of electrons off the deuteron ($^2H \equiv D$) and $^3He$ with detection of slow protons and deuterons, respectively, i.e. the processes $D(e,e'p)X$ and $^3He(e,e'D)X$, are calculated within the spectator mechanism, taking into account the final state interaction of the hadronizing quark with the detected protons and deuterons, respectively. It is shown that by a proper choice of the kinematics the origin of the EMC effect and the details of the interaction between the hadronizing quark and the nuclear medium can be investigated at a level which cannot be reached by inclusive deep inelastic scattering. A comparison of our calculations with recently available experimental data on the process $D(e,e'p)X$ shows a good agreement in the backward hemisphere of the emitted nucleons. Theoretical predictions at the energies thyat will be available at the upgraded Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facilty are presented, and the possibility to investigate the proposed semi-inclusive processes at electron-ion colliders is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off few-nucleon systems with detection of slow recoiling nuclei can disentangle the origin of the EMC effect from hadronization mechanisms, provided the kinematics is chosen to minimize contamination from unknown nuclear structure effects or from the poorly known neutron structure function.

## Method

The authors calculated the SIDIS cross sections for \(^2H(e,e'p)X\) and \(^3He(e,e'd)X\) within the spectator mechanism, using realistic AV18 nucleon-nucleon interactions for the nuclear wave functions. Final state interactions (FSI) between the hadronizing quark debris and the detected spectator nucleons were included via an eikonal approximation with a \(Q^2\)- and \(x_{Bj}\)-dependent effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}(z)\) that models hadronization through pion multiplicities from string breaking and gluon radiation. The calculations used the nucleon structure function parametrization from Ref. [effe2] with off-mass-shell corrections via the x-rescaling model.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy \(E_e = 5.75\) GeV, four-momentum transfer \(1.2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 5.0\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), recoiling proton momentum \(0.28 \lesssim |\mathbf{p}_p| \leq 0.7\) GeV/c, and invariant mass \(1.1 \leq W_X \leq 2.7\) GeV for the \(^2H(e,e'p)X\) data from JLab.
- For the \(^3He(e,e'd)X\) predictions at upgraded JLab: \(Q^2 = 8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), beam energy \(E_e = 12\) GeV, deuteron emission angle \(\theta_D = 145^\circ\), and Bjorken variables \(x_{Bj} = 0.45\) and \(x_{Bj}' = 0.35\).
- At \(|\mathbf{P}_D| \simeq 0.4\) GeV/c, the two medium-dependent structure function models differ by about 40% in the ratio \(R(x_{Bj}, x_{Bj}', |\mathbf{P}_{A-1}|)\).
- Effective cross section parameters: \(\sigma_{NN} = 40\) mb and \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 30\) mb used in the FSI model.

## Context

This work extends the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) treatment of SIDIS by Scopetta (2004) to include FSI via the hadronization model of Kopeliovich (2004), and generalizes earlier FSI calculations by Ciofi degli Atti and Kaptari (2008) from asymptotic \(Q^2\) to finite \(Q^2\) values relevant for JLab. The approach enables discrimination between different models of the EMC effect (e.g., x-rescaling versus point-like configuration suppression) that are indistinguishable in inclusive DIS, and provides a method to study hadronization mechanisms free from EMC contamination by taking ratios of cross sections on different nuclei.
