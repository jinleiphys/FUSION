# Isospin effect on baryon and charge fluctuations from the pNJL model

> We have studied the possible isospin corrections on the skewness and kurtosis of net-baryon and net-charge fluctuations in the isospin asymmetric matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC-BES energies, based on a 3-flavor polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. With typical scalar-isovector and vector-isovector couplings leading to the splitting of $u$ and $d$ quark chiral phase transition boundaries and critical points, we have observed dramatic isospin effects on the susceptibilities, especially those of net-charge fluctuations. Reliable experimental measurements at even lower collision energies are encouraged to confirm the observed isospin effects.

## Key claim

The isospin effect on net-baryon and net-charge fluctuations, arising from scalar-isovector and vector-isovector couplings in the pNJL model, is robust and dramatically alters the shape of higher-order susceptibilities, especially for net-charge fluctuations, but the results rely on an ideal thermodynamic calculation and a hypothetical chemical freeze-out line very close to the chiral phase transition boundary.

## Method

The authors used a 3-flavor Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (pNJL) model with scalar-isovector and vector-isovector couplings to compute the second-, third-, and fourth-order susceptibilities of net-baryon and net-charge fluctuations in isospin asymmetric quark matter. They employed an empirical relation between isospin chemical potential and baryon chemical potential (mu_I = -0.293 - 0.0264 mu_B, in MeV) extracted from statistical model fits of Au+Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies, and defined hypothetical chemical freeze-out lines by rescaling the baryon chemical potential of the averaged chiral phase transition boundary by factors of 0.98, 0.95, and 0.90.

## Key numbers

- The empirical relation between isospin chemical potential and baryon chemical potential is mu_I = -0.293 - 0.0264 mu_B, with both mu_B and mu_I in MeV, determined from statistical model fits of Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV.
- The strangeness chemical potential empirical relation is mu_S = 1.032 + 0.232 mu_B, with both in MeV.
- The scalar-isovector coupling strength R_IS = G_IS/G_S = 0.14 and vector-isovector coupling strength R_IV = G_IV/G_S = 0.5 were used as the extreme case leading to maximum splitting of u and d quark chiral phase transition boundaries.
- The cutoff Lambda = 750 MeV, current quark masses m_u = m_d = 3.6 MeV and m_s = 87 MeV, scalar-isoscalar coupling G_S * Lambda^2 = 3.6, and KMT coupling K * Lambda^5 = 8.9 were adopted.
- The Polyakov-loop effective potential parameters are a = 664 MeV and b = 0.015 * Lambda^3.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram and critical point using the pNJL model (Fukushima 2008, Carignano 2010, Bratovic 2013) by incorporating isospin effects that had not been seriously investigated. It builds on the authors' earlier work (Liu 2016) showing that isovector couplings cause splitting of u and d quark chiral phase transition boundaries and critical points. The results enable experimental tests at lower RHIC-BES collision energies to distinguish different isovector coupling scenarios, and motivate transport model studies (Xu 2014) that include hadronic effects for direct comparison with STAR and PHENIX data.
