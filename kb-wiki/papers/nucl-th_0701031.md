# Superscaling in electroweak excitation of nuclei

> Superscaling properties of 12C, 16O and 40Ca nuclear responses, induced by electron and neutrino scattering, are studied for momentum transfer values between 300 and 700 MeV/c. We have defined two indexes to have quantitative estimates of the scaling quality. We have analyzed experimental responses to get the empirical values of the two indexes. We have then investigated the effects of finite dimensions, collective excitations, meson exchange currents, short-range correlations and final state interactions. These effects strongly modify the relativistic Fermi gas scaling functions, but they conserve the scaling properties. We used the scaling functions to predict electron and neutrino cross sections and we tested their validity by comparing them with the cross sections obtained with a full calculation. For electron scattering we also made a comparison with data. We have calculated the total charge-exchange neutrino cross sections for neutrino energies up to 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that several nuclear effects beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, finite size, collective excitations, meson exchange currents, short-range correlations, and final state interactions, strongly modify the RFG scaling functions but still conserve the superscaling properties, provided the momentum transfer is not too low (above about 400 MeV/c for scaling of first kind).

## Method

The authors calculated longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions for 12C, 16O, and 40Ca using a continuum shell model and continuum Random Phase Approximation (RPA), incorporating finite-size effects, collective excitations, short-range correlations, meson exchange currents, and final state interactions. They defined two quantitative indexes, R and D, to measure scaling quality and compared theoretical scaling functions against empirical scaling functions extracted from experimental electron scattering data from Joubert (1996). The scaling functions were then used to predict electron and neutrino cross sections, which were compared with full calculations and, for electron scattering, with experimental data.

## Key numbers

*   Momentum transfer range studied: 300 to 700 MeV/c.
*   Fermi momentum values used: k_F = 215 MeV/c for 12C and 16O; k_F = 245 MeV/c for 40Ca and 56Fe.
*   Energy shift used for all nuclei: E_shift = 15 MeV.
*   Empirical reference thresholds for scaling violation: R > 0.096 or D > 0.11, derived from the longitudinal scaling functions at q = 570 MeV/c.
*   For the full calculation including all effects, scaling of second kind for f_L at 570 MeV/c gave R = 0.03 and D = 0.05; at 300 MeV/c, R = 0.05 and D = 0.15.
*   For scaling of first kind in f_L, the minimum R value was 0.13 (for 12C) and maximum 0.15 (for 40Ca); minimum D was 0.18 and maximum 0.30.
*   Axial mass value used for the axial-vector form factor: 1030 MeV.
*   Neutrino cross sections were calculated for neutrino energies up to 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of Donnelly (1999) and Maieron (2002) by quantitatively testing how specific nuclear many-body effects beyond the RFG model affect scaling quality. The authors define numerical indexes to replace qualitative visual comparisons, enabling systematic benchmarking. The extracted empirical universal scaling function and the theoretical universal scaling function (chosen as the 16O longitudinal function at q = 570 MeV/c) can be used to predict neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the quasi-elastic region, which is relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments.
