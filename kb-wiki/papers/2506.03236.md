# Extremely large oblate deformation of the first excited state in $^{12}$C: a new challenge to modern nuclear theory

> A Coulomb-excitation study of the high-lying first excited state at 4.439 MeV in the nucleus $^{12}$C has been carried out using the $^{208}$Pb($^{12}$C,$^{12}$C$^*$)$^{208}$Pb$^*$ reaction at 56 MeV and the {\sc Q3D} magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratorium in Munich. High-statistics achieved with an average beam intensity of approximately 10$^{11}$ ions/s together with state-of-the-art {\it ab initio} calculations of the nuclear dipole polarizability permitted the accurate determination of the spectroscopic quadrupole moment, $Q_{_S}(2_{_1}^+) = +0.076(30)$~eb, in agreement with previous measurements. Combined with previous work, a weighted average of $Q_{_S}(2_{_1}^+) = +0.090(14)$ eb is determined, which includes the re-analysis of a similar experiment by Vermeer and collaborators, $Q_{_S}(2_{_1}^+) = +0.103(20)$~eb. Such a large oblate deformation challenges modern nuclear theory and emphasizes the need of $α$ clustering and associated triaxiality effects for full convergence of $E2$ collective properties.

## Key claim

The first excited state of ¹²C at 4.439 MeV has an extremely large oblate deformation, with a weighted average spectroscopic quadrupole moment of \(Q_S(2_1^+) = +0.090(14)\) eb, which challenges modern nuclear theory because state-of-the-art ab initio calculations predict values in the range 0.045–0.065 eb, indicating that alpha clustering and associated triaxiality effects are needed for full convergence of E2 collective properties.

## Method

A Coulomb-excitation study using the reorientation effect was performed with the ²⁰⁸Pb(¹²C,¹²C*)²⁰⁸Pb* reaction at 56 MeV, employing the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratorium in Munich. Particle spectra were collected at scattering angles of 80°, 85°, and 90°, and the semi-classical coupled-channel code GOSIA was used to simulate population probabilities and extract the spectroscopic quadrupole moment, with state-of-the-art ab initio calculations of the nuclear dipole polarizability used to correct for E1 polarization effects.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment from this experiment: \(Q_S(2_1^+) = +0.076(30)\) eb
- Weighted average combining previous measurements: \(Q_S(2_1^+) = +0.090(14)\) eb
- Re-analysis of Vermeer and collaborators' experiment: \(Q_S(2_1^+) = +0.103(20)\) eb
- Beam energy: \(T_{lab} = 56\) MeV with average beam intensity of approximately \(10^{11}\) ions/s
- Excitation energy of the first excited state: 4.439 MeV
- Reduced transition probability: \(B(E2; 0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+) = 0.00390(8)\) e²b²
- Sommerfeld parameter: \(\eta = 36 \gg 1\), confirming safe Coulomb excitation conditions

## Context

This work extends previous Coulomb-excitation measurements by Vermeer (1983), Raju (2018), and Saiz (2021), and provides the most accurate determination of the spectroscopic quadrupole moment in ¹²C to date. The result contradicts ab initio calculations using the No-Core Shell Model with CD-Bonn 2000 and chiral NN+3N interactions by Calci (2016) and D'Alessio (2020), which predict \(Q_S(2_1^+)\) values of 0.045–0.065 eb, as well as Monte Carlo lattice calculations by Epelbaum (2012) and cluster models. The finding emphasizes the need for improved theoretical treatment of alpha clustering and triaxiality to reproduce E2 collective properties in light nuclei.
