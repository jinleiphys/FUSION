# Polarization transfer observables for quasielastic proton-nucleus scattering in terms of a complete Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix

> For the calculation of polarization transfer observables for quasielastic scattering of protons on nuclei, a formalism in the context of the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation is developed, in which the interaction matrix is expanded in terms of a complete set of 44 independent invariant amplitudes. A boson-exchange model is used to predict the 39 amplitudes which were omitted in the formerly used five-term parameterization(the SPVAT form) of the nucleon-nucleon scattering matrix. Use of the complete set of amplitudes eliminates the arbitrariness of the five-term representation.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism for calculating polarization transfer observables in quasielastic proton-nucleus scattering using a complete set of 44 independent invariant amplitudes (IA2 representation) for the nucleon-nucleon scattering matrix, rather than the previously used five-term SPVAT (IA1) representation. The authors state that the five-term representation is "necessarily ambiguous" and that the complete set eliminates this arbitrariness, but they note that a systematic survey comparing the model to experimental data will be presented in a future paper.

## Method

The authors employ the Relativistic Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA), modeling quasielastic scattering as a single-step process where the projectile knocks out a single bound nucleon from the nucleus, with free nucleon masses shifted to effective masses to account for nuclear medium effects. The nucleon-nucleon scattering matrix is expanded in terms of 44 independent invariant amplitudes (IA2 representation) derived from Tjon and Wallace's general Lorentz invariant formalism, where five amplitudes in subclass F^11 are fit to free NN scattering data and the remaining 39 off-shell amplitudes are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a meson-exchange model using pure pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling. The invariant amplitudes are transformed into eight effective amplitudes (b_n, n=1-8) expressed in a Pauli matrix basis, from which analytical expressions for all spin observables (unpolarized cross section, analyzing power, and polarization transfer coefficients D_l'l, D_s's, D_s'l, D_l's, D_nn) are derived.

## Key numbers

- The complete Lorentz invariant representation of the NN scattering matrix contains 44 independent invariant amplitudes, compared to the 5 amplitudes used in the SPVAT (IA1) representation.
- The IA2 representation includes 13 kinematic covariants K_n (n=1-13) constructed from Dirac matrices, with 8 effective amplitudes b_n (n=1-8) in the Pauli matrix expansion.
- Calculations are presented for quasielastic 40Ca(p,p') scattering at 500 MeV incident laboratory energy.
- The effective nucleon masses M_1 (projectile) and M_2 (target) are determined from the Walecka model, with values for specific nuclei and energies referenced to Table II of Hillhouse (1994).

## Context

This work extends the RPWIA formalism of Horowitz and Murdock (1988) by replacing the five-term SPVAT (IA1) representation of the NN scattering matrix with the complete 44-amplitude IA2 representation developed by Tjon and Wallace (1985, 1987). The paper addresses the ambiguity first pointed out by Adams and Bleszynski (1984) that any five-term representation is necessarily ambiguous, and the finding by Hillhouse (1994, 1995, 1998) that quasielastic (p,p') and (p,n) data prefer different five-term representations. The formalism enables systematic investigation of whether spin observable differences arise from genuine nuclear medium effects or from the incomplete representation of the NN interaction, and provides a foundation for future comparison with experimental data.
