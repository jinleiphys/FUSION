# Dispersion relations of relativistic radiation hydrodynamics

> We compute the linearised dispersion relations of shear waves, heat waves, and sound waves in relativistic ''matter+radiation'' fluids with grey absorption opacities. This is done by solving radiation hydrodynamics perturbatively in the ratio ''radiation stress-energy''/''matter stress-energy''. The resulting expressions $ω\, {=} \, ω(k)$ accurately describe the hydrodynamic evolution for any $k\, {\in}\, \mathbb{R}$. General features of the dynamics (e.g., covariant stability, propagation speeds, and damping of discontinuities) are argued directly from the analytic form of these dispersion relations.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytic dispersion relations for shear waves, heat waves, and sound waves in relativistic radiation hydrodynamics under the specific conditions that scattering is neglected, the opacity is grey, and the radiation stress-energy tensor is small compared to that of matter (e.g., the ratio of radiation pressure to gas pressure is less than about 0.01). The resulting formulas accurately describe the hydrodynamic evolution for any real wavenumber k, not just in the long-wavelength limit.

## Method

The author linearizes the coupled equations of radiation hydrodynamics around a uniform equilibrium state, where matter is treated as an ideal relativistic fluid and radiation is described by the Boltzmann equation with a grey absorption opacity. The linearized system is solved perturbatively by expanding in the small parameter lambda (the ratio of radiation stress-energy to matter stress-energy), yielding explicit dispersion relations for each mode. The angular integrals arising from the radiation kinetic equation are evaluated analytically, producing closed-form expressions valid for all k.

## Key numbers

* Shear wave diffusivity: D_s = (4 pi^2 T^4 tau) / (225 (rho + P)), where tau is the photon mean free path, T is temperature, rho is matter energy density, and P is matter pressure
* Heat wave diffusivity: D_h = (4 pi^2 T^3 tau) / (45 n c_p), where n is baryon density and c_p is specific heat at constant pressure
* The small expansion parameter for shear waves is lambda = (2 pi^2 T^4) / (15 (rho + P)), which scales as the ratio of radiation energy density to matter energy density
* The small expansion parameter for heat waves is nu = (4 pi^2 T^3) / (15 n c_p), which scales as the ratio of radiation pressure to matter pressure
* For shear waves, the low-k expansion gives omega = -i (4 pi^2 T^4) / (225 (rho + P) tau) [(k tau)^2 - (3/7)(k tau)^4 + (5/21)(k tau)^6 - ...]
* For heat waves, the low-k expansion gives omega = -i (4 pi^2 T^3) / (45 n c_p tau) [(k tau)^2/3 - (k tau)^4/5 + ...]

## Context

This work extends the classic result of Spiegel (1957) for heat waves in radiation hydrodynamics by treating the matter component self-consistently (rather than holding it at rest) and by using fully relativistic dynamics. The shear wave dispersion relation matches a previous purely geometrical derivation by Gavassino (2024). The results enable accurate modeling of radiation-hydrodynamic modes across all wavenumbers, from the optically thick to optically thin regimes, and provide a foundation for testing approximate closure schemes used in numerical simulations.
