# Deformed in-medium similarity renormalization group

> We have developed an {\it ab initio} deformed in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) for open-shell nuclei. This is a single-reference IMSRG in deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) basis. Deformed wave functions are more efficient in describing deformed nuclei. The broken spherical symmetry needs to be restored by angular momentum projection, which is computational expensive. The angular momentum mainly capture the static correlations and can be estimated by the projection of the HF state. In this work, we do deformed IMSRG calculation and add the correlation energy from projected HF as a leading order approximation. As the test ground, we have calculated the deformed $^{8,10}\rm Be$ isotopes with the optimized chiral interaction NNLO$_{\rm opt}$. The results are benchmarked with the no-core shell model and valence space IMSRG calculations. Then we systematically investigated the ground-state energies and charge radii of even-even isotopes from light beryllium to medium-mass magnesium. The calculated energies are extrapolated to infinite basis space by an exponential form, and compared with the extrapolated valence-space IMSRG results and experimental data available.

## Key claim

The authors develop a single-reference deformed in-medium similarity renormalization group (D-IMSRG) in a deformed Hartree-Fock basis for open-shell nuclei, but they approximate the computationally expensive angular momentum projection by adding the projection correction from the Hartree-Fock state as a leading-order estimate rather than projecting the full D-IMSRG wave function.

## Method

The method starts by solving an axially deformed Hartree-Fock equation in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis for an even-even nucleus, then fills the deformed single-particle levels in pairs of ±m to create a prolate deformed reference state. The IMSRG(2) flow equations are solved using the White generator to drive the Hamiltonian to a block-diagonal form, and the ground-state energy is obtained from the transformed Hamiltonian. The angular momentum projection correction is estimated by computing the energy difference between the projected and unprojected Hartree-Fock state, and this correction is added to the D-IMSRG energy.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸Be, the angular momentum projection correction is −7.2 MeV; for ¹⁰Be, the correction is −5.9 MeV.
- For ¹²C and ²²C, the angular momentum projection corrections are −5.5 MeV and −2.7 MeV, respectively.
- For Ne and Mg isotopes near neutron number N=20, the projection results in energy gains of about 3–6 MeV.
- The D-IMSRG calculations used the chiral NNLO_opt interaction with ħω = 24 MeV and a maximum basis-space size of N_shell = 10.
- The charge radius calculation uses ⟨r_p²⟩ = 0.709 fm², ⟨r_n²⟩ = −0.106 fm², and the Darwin-Foldy term ⟨r_DF²⟩ = 0.033 fm².

## Context

This work extends the single-reference IMSRG method, previously limited to closed-shell nuclei, to open-shell nuclei by using a deformed Hartree-Fock basis in the m-scheme. It builds on the development of deformed coupled cluster methods by Duguet (2014) and the projected coupled cluster approach (2022), which showed that deformed single-reference methods can efficiently describe open-shell nuclei. The D-IMSRG enables systematic ab initio calculations of ground-state energies and charge radii for even-even isotopes from beryllium to magnesium, providing a computationally simpler alternative to multi-reference IMSRG or valence-space IMSRG approaches.
