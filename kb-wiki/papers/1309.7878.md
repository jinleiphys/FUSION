# Strongly Intensive Measures for Transverse Momentum and Particle Number Fluctuations

> The strongly intensive measures $Δ[P_T,N]$ and $Σ[P_T,N]$ are used to study the event-by-event fluctuations of the transverse momentum $P_T$ and particle multiplicity $N$ in nucleus-nucleus collisions. A special normalization for these fluctuation measures ensures that they are dimensionless and yields a common scale required for a quantitative comparison of fluctuations. In this paper basic properties of the $Δ[P_T, N]$ and $Σ[P_T, N]$ measures are tested within different phenomenological models using the Monte Carlo simulations (the so-called fast generators) and analytical solutions. The obtained results are helpful to elucidate the properties of $Δ[P_T,N]$ and $Σ[P_T,N]$ measures.

## Key claim

The strongly intensive measures Δ[P_T,N] and Σ[P_T,N] are dimensionless and independent of both the average volume and volume fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but their values deviate from unity when source-by-source temperature fluctuations are present, even when the sources remain statistically independent.

## Method

The authors tested the properties of Δ[P_T,N] and Σ[P_T,N] using Monte Carlo simulations (fast generators) and analytical solutions within several phenomenological models: the independent particle model (IPM), the model of independent sources (MIS), and models with fixed or fluctuating temperature parameters. Particles were generated from Boltzmann transverse momentum distributions with pion mass m_π ≈ 140 MeV, and the number of sources per event was drawn from Poisson or Negative Binomial distributions. Statistical errors were estimated by dividing the event sample into 30 independent sub-samples and calculating the dispersion of the results.

## Key numbers

- For fixed temperature T = 150 MeV with sources emitting particles from a Poisson distribution (mean 5 particles per source), Δ[P_T,N] and Σ[P_T,N] equal 1.0 within statistical errors, independent of the number of sources per event (tested from ⟨N_S⟩ = 5 to 800) and independent of whether source number fluctuations follow Poisson or Negative Binomial distributions (with ω[N] ranging from 1 to approximately 1000).
- For source-by-source temperature fluctuations with Gaussian distribution (mean T = 150 MeV, σ_T = 25 MeV) and Poisson-distributed source number, Δ[P_T,N] = Σ[P_T,N] ≈ 1.227 for massive pions (m = 140 MeV) and ≈ 1.256 for massless particles, independent of ⟨N_S⟩.
- The analytical result for source temperature fluctuations gives Δ[P,N] = Σ[P,N] = 1 + (1/ω[p])·((p̂² − p̄²)/p̄)·[n̄ + ω[n] − 1], where for the massive case p̄ ≈ 0.328 GeV/c, p̄² ≈ 0.158 (GeV/c)², and p̂² ≈ 0.110 (GeV/c)².

## Context

This work extends the formalism of strongly intensive measures introduced by Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2011) and the normalization procedure proposed by Gorenstein, Gazdzicki, and Pawlowska (2013). It provides reference calculations for the NA61/SHINE Collaboration's energy and system size scan program at CERN SPS and the STAR/PHENIX low energy scan at BNL RHIC. The results enable experimental comparisons of event-by-event fluctuations across different collision systems and energies, with the IPM value of unity serving as a baseline analogous to the Poisson distribution for multiplicity fluctuations.
