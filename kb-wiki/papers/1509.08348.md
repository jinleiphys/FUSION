# Peculiarities of quasifission reactions in heavy ion collisions

> The probability of the formation and decay of a dinuclear system is investigated for a wide range of relative orbital angular momentum values. The mass and angular distributions of the quasifission fragments are studied to understand the reaction mechanisms of the heavy ion collision of $^{78}$Kr(10$A$ MeV) + $^{40}$Ca within dinuclear system model. The quasifission products are found to contribute to the mass-symmetric region of the mass distribution in collisions with a large orbital angular momentum. The analysis of mass and angular distributions of quasifission fragments shows the possibility of the $180^\circ$ rotation of the system so that projectile-like products can be observed in the forward hemisphere with large cross sections, which can explain the phenomenon observed recently in the ISODEC experiment.

## Key claim

The yield of quasifission products can contribute to the heavy products emitted in the forward hemisphere in the reaction of 78Kr(10 A MeV) + 40Ca at relative orbital angular momenta of L = (60–75)ℏ, which can explain the phenomenon observed in the ISODEC experiment without invoking a new "shock-induced fission" mechanism. The analysis shows that the 180° rotation of the dinuclear system allows projectile-like products to be observed in the forward hemisphere with large cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system (DNS) model to solve equations of motion for the relative distance, angular momentum, and rotational angles of the colliding nuclei, incorporating microscopically calculated radial and tangential friction coefficients. The decay width of the DNS was calculated using Kramer's rate formalism with the quasifission barrier determined from the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential, and the evolution of charge asymmetry was solved via a transport master equation with multinucleon transfer coefficients. The rotational angle of the DNS at break-up was estimated from the angular velocity and lifetime of the system to determine the angular distribution of quasifission fragments.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied is 78Kr(10 A MeV) + 40Ca with centre-of-mass energy E_c.m. = 264 MeV
- Capture processes occur for collisions with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 70ℏ
- The maximum rotational angle of the DNS (close to 180°) occurs for orbital angular momenta in the range 40ℏ – 70ℏ
- The relative velocity of fragments from DNS decay is in the range 2.4 – 2.7 cm/ns, overlapping with experimental data from the ISODEC experiment
- The interaction time for solving the master equation was taken as t_int = 5 × 10^(-21) s
- The DNS decay width Λ^qf_Z is proportional to exp[-B_qf(Z)/(kT)] with temperature T_Z = sqrt(8 E*_Z(ℓ)/(A_P + A_T))

## Context

This work extends the DNS model approach of Nasirov et al. (2005) and provides an alternative explanation to the "shock-induced fission following fusion" mechanism proposed by the ISODEC collaboration (2014) and Schroeder et al. (2015) for the 78Kr + 40Ca reaction. The analysis demonstrates that quasifission with large rotational angles can reproduce the observed forward emission of heavy fragments, and the authors connect their findings to similar phenomena such as the "slow" evaporation residues observed in the 20Ne + 208Pb reaction by Hinde et al. (1994) and the two-peak velocity structure seen for Rn, Fr, and Ra isotopes by Hinde et al. (2008).
