# Simulating Binary Neutron Star Mergers with Finite-temperature Equations of State: The influences of the slope of the symmetry energy and artificial heating

> We present a new set of numerical-relativity simulations of merging binary neutron stars, aiming to identify possible observable signatures of the slope of the symmetry energy $L_{\rm sym}$. To achieve this goal, we employ a set of equations of state based on a parameterization of the covariant density functional theory of nuclear matter that allows controlled variations of $L_{\rm sym}$ and the skewness $Q_{\rm sat}$, holding the latter fixed. For a set of our simulations, we identify a steep energy gradient in the equation of state at subsaturation densities, which acts as a source of heating with subsequent stiffening produced by thermal support. Accounting for related structural modifications in the tidal deformability reconciles our results with theoretical expectations. On the other hand, we show that gravitational waves are unlikely to distinguish the role of $L_{\rm sym}$. In contrast to this, we find that the ejecta composition is significantly altered in our simulations, which employ an M1 moment scheme, when $L_{\rm sym}$ is varied. Based on our extracted dynamical ejecta properties, we compute r-process yields and find that they are distinct for the different $L_{\rm sym}$, especially at lower mass numbers $A \lesssim 120$. This suggests that electromagnetic counterparts are more likely to exhibit signatures; however, a direct connection to $L_{\rm sym}$ remains a challenge, given the complex interplay between details of the ejecta properties and the kilonova signal.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the slope of the symmetry energy \(L_{\rm sym}\) leaves observable signatures in binary neutron star mergers, but gravitational waves are unlikely to distinguish its role; instead, electromagnetic counterparts from r-process yields show distinct features for different \(L_{\rm sym}\) values, especially at lower mass numbers \(A \lesssim 120\), though a direct connection to \(L_{\rm sym}\) remains challenging due to the complex interplay between ejecta properties and kilonova signals.

## Method

The authors performed numerical-relativity simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers (each with gravitational mass \(1.35 M_\odot\)) using the BAM code, employing three nucleonic equations of state (DDLS30, DDLS50, DDLS70) from covariant density functional theory with fixed skewness \(Q_{\rm sat}=400\) MeV and varying symmetry energy slope \(L_{\rm sym}=30, 50, 70\) MeV. They included finite-temperature effects, an M1 neutrino transport scheme, and artificial atmosphere treatment; they computed tidal deformabilities under simulation conditions by extracting thermal profiles and solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations at the same baryonic mass as the initial condition.

## Key numbers

* Tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) for cold stars: DDLS30 = 841.2, DDLS50 = 866.3, DDLS70 = 954.6; under simulation conditions \(\Lambda_{\rm sim}\) increased to 980.3 for DDLS30 (a 17% increase), while DDLS50 and DDLS70 showed only \(\approx 1\%\) increases (876.4 and 966.5 respectively)
* Central baryon density of remnants: DDLS30 had a central density about 2.3% smaller than DDLS50 and DDLS70; DDLS30 remnant density \(n_{b,\rm max}\) evolved to approximately the same value as DDLS50, while DDLS70 evolved more slowly
* Ejecta masses: DDLS30 = \(0.28 \times 10^{-2} M_\odot\), DDLS50 = \(0.25 \times 10^{-2} M_\odot\), DDLS70 = \(0.22 \times 10^{-2} M_\odot\)
* Average electron fraction of ejecta: DDLS30 = 0.24, DDLS50 = 0.28, DDLS70 = 0.31
* Average asymptotic velocity of ejecta: DDLS30 = 0.16, DDLS50 = 0.15, DDLS70 = 0.14 (in units of \(c\))
* Remnant baryonic masses: DDLS30 = \(2.76 M_\odot\), DDLS50 = \(2.74 M_\odot\), DDLS70 = \(2.76 M_\odot\)
* Nuclear saturation density for all models: \(n_{\rm sat}=0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends previous numerical-relativity studies using the BAM code (Gieg 2022, Schianchi 2023) by employing a new set of finite-temperature nucleonic equations of state from Tsiopelas (2024) that allow controlled variations of \(L_{\rm sym}\) while fixing \(Q_{\rm sat}\), unlike earlier work that used heterogeneous parameterizations. The study contrasts with Kochankovski (2025), which used the same EOS models but focused on hyperon effects rather than symmetry energy slope variations. The results enable future multi-messenger analyses linking nuclear matter parameters to kilonova observations, though the authors note that disentangling \(L_{\rm sym}\) signatures from other ejecta properties remains a challenge.
