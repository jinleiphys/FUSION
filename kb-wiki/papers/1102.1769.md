# Alpha particle condensation and nuclear rainbow scattering

> It is shown that a dilute property of an $α$ particle condensate can be seen in the Airy structure of nuclear rainbow and prerainbow scattering. The dilute property of the Hoyle state of $^{12}$C with a developed $α$ cluster structure is discussed by studying refractive $^3$He+$^{12}$C and $α$+$^{12}$C scattering.

# Alpha particle condensation and nuclear rainbow scattering

## Key claim
The dilute density distribution of the Hoyle state (0₂⁺ at 7.65 MeV in ¹²C), which is nearly equivalent to an alpha-particle condensate wavefunction, can be detected through the Airy structure of nuclear rainbow and prerainbow scattering; the effect is more clearly visible at lower incident energies where prerainbow oscillations appear, rather than at the higher energies where full rainbow scattering with a falloff of cross sections is observed.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel calculations using a double folding model with the DDM3Y effective interaction, employing the three-alpha cluster wavefunctions of Kamimura et al. (1981) for ¹²C states (0₁⁺, 2⁺, 0₂⁺, 3⁻). They analyzed elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions for the ³He+¹²C system at 34.7 MeV and 72 MeV, and for the α+¹²C system at 110 MeV, decomposing the scattering amplitudes into farside and nearside contributions following Fuller's prescription.

## Key numbers
- For ³He+¹²C at 34.7 MeV: the Airy minimum A1 appears at 60° for elastic scattering and at 75° for the 0₂⁺ (Hoyle) state, a shift of 15° to larger angle.
- For ³He+¹²C at 72 MeV: the A1 minimum for the 0₂⁺ state is clearly visible in the farside cross section, while the elastic A1 minimum is not clearly seen in the experimental data.
- For α+¹²C at 110 MeV: the peak of the partial cross section for elastic scattering is located at orbital angular momentum L=13, while for inelastic scattering to the Hoyle state the peak is at L=18.
- The normalization factor for the real part of the double folding potential was set to N_R=1.28 for the ³He+¹²C analysis.

## Context
This work extends the earlier conjecture by Tohsaki et al. (2001) that the Hoyle state is a dilute Bose-Einstein condensate of three alpha particles, and builds on the nuclear rainbow scattering studies by Khoa (2007) and Michel (2002) which established rainbow scattering as a probe of the nuclear potential. The authors previously studied α+¹²C at energies above 139 MeV (Ohkubo, 2004) and ³He+¹²C (Ohkubo, 2007); the present paper extends that analysis to lower energies where prerainbow oscillations are more sensitive to the dilute structure. The results enable future experimental measurements of inelastic scattering to the Hoyle state at low energies to directly probe the condensate nature of this state.
