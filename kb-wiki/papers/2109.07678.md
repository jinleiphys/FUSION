# Effects of $ϕ$-meson on properties of hyperon stars in density dependent relativistic mean field model

> The effects of $ϕ$-meson on properties of hyperon stars are studied systematically in the framework of the density dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) model. The $ϕ$-meson shifts hyperon threshold to a higher density and reduces the hyperon fractions in neutron star cores. It also strongly stiffens the equation of state (EoS) calculated with various DDRMF effective interactions and increases the maximum mass of hyperon stars, but only a few effective interactions survive under the constraints from recent astrophysical observations. In the DDRMF model, the conformal limit of sound velocity is still in a strong tension with the fact that the maximum mass of neutron stars obtained in theoretical calculations reaches about two solar masses. Based on different interior composition assumptions, we discuss the possibility of the secondary object of GW190814 as a neutron star. When $ϕ$-meson is considered, DD-ME2 and DD-MEX support that the secondary object of GW190814 is a hyperon star rapidly rotating with Kepler frequency.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the $\phi$-meson shifts hyperon thresholds to higher densities and reduces hyperon fractions in neutron star cores, stiffening the equation of state and increasing the maximum mass of hyperon stars; however, only a few of the eight DDRMF effective interactions tested survive constraints from recent astrophysical observations, and the conformal limit of sound velocity remains in strong tension with the fact that theoretical maximum masses reach about two solar masses.

## Method

The authors systematically studied the effects of the $\phi$-meson on hyperon star properties within the density dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) model, considering all octet baryons and the isoscalar mesons $\sigma$ and $\omega$, isovector mesons $\rho$ and $\delta$, and the hidden-strangeness vector meson $\phi$. They used eight DDRMF effective interactions (DD-ME2, DD-ME$\delta$, PKDD, TW99, DD-MEX, DDV, DDVT, and DDVTD) and applied SU(6) naive quark model relations for hyperon-meson coupling constants, with hyperon-$\sigma$ couplings determined by fitting empirical hypernuclear potentials ($U_{\Lambda}^{(N)}=-30$ MeV, $U_{\Sigma}^{(N)}=+30$ MeV, $U_{\Xi}^{(N)}=-15$ MeV). The mass-radius relation was obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation.

- Saturation density $\rho_0$ ranges from 0.150 fm$^{-3}$ (PKDD) to 0.1536 fm$^{-3}$ (DDVT, DDVTD)
- Binding energy per particle $E/A$ at saturation ranges from $-$16.915 MeV (DDVTD) to $-$16.097 MeV (DDV)
- Incompressibility $K_0$ ranges from 219.1 MeV (DD-ME$\delta$) to 267.1 MeV (DD-MEX)
- Symmetry energy $E_{\mathrm{sym}}$ ranges from 31.56 MeV (DDVT) to 36.86 MeV (PKDD)
- Slope of symmetry energy $L$ ranges from 42.35 MeV (DDVT) to 90.21 MeV (PKDD)
- Effective mass of neutron $M_n^\star/M_n$ ranges from 0.555 (TW99) to 0.667 (DDVT, DDVTD)
- Hypernuclear potentials used: $U_{\Lambda}^{(N)}=-30$ MeV, $U_{\Sigma}^{(N)}=+30$ MeV, $U_{\Xi}^{(N)}=-15$ MeV
- Observed neutron star masses referenced: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.908$\pm$0.016 $M_\odot$, PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01$\pm$0.04 $M_\odot$, MSP J0740+6620 at 2.08$_{-0.07}^{+0.07}$ $M_\odot$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\phi$-meson effects in the non-linear RMF (NLRMF) model by Weissenborn (2012), Banik (2014), Biswal (2019), and Lopes (2020) to the DDRMF framework, where such systematic analysis was missing. The study addresses the "hyperon puzzle" (Schulze 2006, Vidana 2013) by testing whether the additional repulsive interaction from the $\phi$-meson can stiffen the equation of state enough to satisfy the two-solar-mass constraint from pulsar observations (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Fonseca 2021). The results enable future work to assess whether the secondary object of GW190814 could be a rapidly rotating hyperon star, with DD-ME2 and DD-MEX supporting this possibility when the $\phi$-meson is included.
