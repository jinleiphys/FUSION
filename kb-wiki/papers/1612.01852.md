# Supernova equations of state including full nuclear ensemble with in-medium effects

> We construct new equations of state for baryons at sub-nuclear densities for the use in core-collapse supernova simulations. The abundance of various nuclei is obtained together with thermodynamic quantities. The formulation is an extension of the previous model, in which we adopted the relativistic mean field theory with the TM1 parameter set for nucleons, the quantum approach for $d$, $t$, $h$ and $α$ as well as the liquid drop model for the other nuclei under the nuclear statistical equilibrium. We reformulate the model of the light nuclei other than $d$, $t$, $h$ and $α$ based on the quasi-particle description. Furthermore, we modify the model so that the temperature dependences of surface and shell energies of heavy nuclei could be taken into account. The pasta phases for heavy nuclei and the Pauli- and self-energy shifts for $d$, $t$, $h$ and $α$ are taken into account in the same way as in the previous model. We find that nuclear composition is considerably affected by the modifications in this work, whereas thermodynamical quantities are not changed much. In particular, the washout of shell effect has a great impact on the mass distribution above $T \sim 1$ MeV. This improvement may have an important effect on the rates of electron captures and coherent neutrino scatterings on nuclei in supernova cores.

## Key claim
The authors construct new supernova equations of state that include temperature-dependent surface and shell energies for heavy nuclei and a quasi-particle description for light nuclei, finding that nuclear composition is considerably affected by these modifications while thermodynamic quantities remain largely unchanged. The washout of shell effects above approximately 1 MeV has a particularly great impact on the mass distribution of nuclei.

## Method
The authors extend their previous nuclear statistical equilibrium model by reformulating light nuclei (Z ≤ 5) other than deuteron, triton, He-3, and He-4 using a quasi-particle description, and by introducing temperature-dependent surface tension and shell-energy washout for heavy nuclei. The free energy is minimized with respect to number densities of all nuclear species and free nucleons under baryon number and charge conservation, using the relativistic mean field theory with the TM1 parameter set for nucleons and the liquid drop model for heavy nuclei with in-medium effects including pasta phases.

## Key numbers
- The washout of shell effects is modeled with a factor τ_i/sinh(τ_i) where τ_i = 2π²T/ε_i^sh and ε_i^sh = 41 A_i^{-1/3} MeV, causing shell effects to disappear around T ~ 2.0-3.0 MeV
- The surface tension temperature dependence uses the factor ((T_c²-T²)/(T_c²+T²))^(5/4) with critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV
- The surface tension constants are σ_0 = 1.15 MeV/fm³ and S_s = 45.8 MeV, adopted from Lattimer (1991)
- The critical density for shell-effect density dependence is set at 10¹² g/cm³
- The model covers heavy nuclei with proton numbers Z ≤ 1000 and neutron numbers N ≤ 1000
- The mass fraction of light nuclei other than d, t, h, and α decreases in the new model compared to the previous version at ρ_B = 10¹² g/cm³ and Y_p = 0.2 and 0.4

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous supernova equation of state (Furusawa 2011, 2013) by adding temperature-dependent surface and shell energies that were previously lacking. It addresses a gap in multi-nucleus EOS models: the SMSM EOS (Botvina 2004, Buyukcizmeci 2014) ignores shell effects entirely, while the HS EOS (Hempel 2010) and the authors' previous model assume full shell effects at all temperatures despite experimental and theoretical evidence that shell effects disappear at T ~ 2.0-3.0 MeV (Brack 1974, Bohr 1987). The improved treatment of nuclear composition may enable more accurate calculations of electron capture rates and coherent neutrino scattering on nuclei in supernova cores.
