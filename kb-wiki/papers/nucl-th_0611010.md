# Extraction of a_nn from pi- d -> n n gamma

> I present a calculation of the pi- d -> n n gamma reaction to third order in chiral perturbation theory. The short-distance physics of this reaction can be constrained by relating it to several important low-energy weak reactions. The theoretical error in a_nn extracted from this reaction can thus be reduced by a factor larger than three to +-0.05 fm.

# Extraction of \(a_{nn}\) from \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\)

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory at third order can reduce the theoretical error in the neutron-neutron scattering length \(a_{nn}\) extracted from the \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) reaction by a factor larger than three, down to \(\pm 0.05\) fm, but only if the fit is restricted to the final-state-interaction (FSI) peak region rather than the entire spectrum.

## Method
The \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) reaction was calculated to third order (\(\mathcal{O}(Q^3)\)) in chiral perturbation theory, where \(Q \sim m_\pi\) is a small momentum scale. Bound and scattering state wave functions were obtained from the known asymptotic behavior integrated inward from \(r = \infty\) using the one-pion-exchange potential, with short-distance physics parametrized by matching to a spherical well solution at a matching radius \(R\) between 1.4 fm and 3 fm. The short-distance physics was constrained by relating the reaction to the Gamow-Teller matrix element of solar \(pp\) fusion and tritium beta decay, allowing simultaneous renormalization by a single low-energy constant (LEC).

## Key numbers
- The presently accepted value of the neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a_{nn} = -18.59 \pm 0.4\) fm, based on \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) experiments.
- Previous theoretical models (Gibbs, Gibson, and Stephenson; de Téramond et al.) carried a theoretical error of \(\pm 0.3\) fm.
- The chiral perturbation theory calculation reduces the theoretical error to \(\pm 0.05\) fm when fitting only the FSI peak region.
- The matching radius \(R\) is varied in the range \(1.4\ \text{fm} < R < 3\ \text{fm}\) to test sensitivity to short-distance physics.

## Context
This work extends earlier extractions of \(a_{nn}\) from \(\pi^- d \to nn\gamma\) using models by Gibbs, Gibson, and Stephenson and by de Téramond et al., which had a theoretical error of \(\pm 0.3\) fm. By applying chiral perturbation theory, the calculation links the same short-distance physics to solar \(pp\) fusion and tritium beta decay, enabling a simultaneous renormalization that reduces the theoretical uncertainty. This also allows extracting part of the chiral three-nucleon force from two-nucleon systems, and future work can further refine the extraction by fitting the entire spectrum with the constrained LEC.
