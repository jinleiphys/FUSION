# Asymptotic characteristics of decay channels of light nuclei states in the ab initio approach

> A new convenient method for precise theoretical calculations of quantities of traditional theory of nuclear reactions such as widths of resonances (including sub-threshold), and asymptotic normalization coefficients is proposed. This method may be considered as a step on the road to full theoretical ab initio description of light nuclei spectroscopic data. As an illustration of this method the computational results for all relevant two-body channels for all known and some theoretically predicted states of 7Li nucleus are shown. Well-proven on a large amount of data Daejeon16 potential was used in the calculations. The most part of the results turn out to be in a good agreement with the experimental data contained in spectroscopic tables.

## Key claim
The authors propose a method that combines No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) wave functions with cluster-channel wave functions from the Cluster Channel Orthogonal Functions Method (CCOFM) to calculate resonance widths and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for light nuclei, but the accuracy depends critically on using experimental fragmentation energies rather than theoretical ones, as the computed asymptotic characteristics are highly sensitive to small (tens of keV) differences in decay energy.

## Method
The method projects NCSM wave functions of the A-nucleon system onto orthonormalized cluster-channel wave functions built from internal wave functions of the fragments and oscillator-basis relative-motion functions, using cluster coefficients to express the channel wave functions as superpositions of Slater determinants. The resulting cluster form factor (CFF) is matched to asymptotic scattering solutions (irregular Coulomb functions for narrow resonances, Whittaker functions for bound states) at a matching point determined by equality of logarithmic derivatives, yielding decay widths via equation (17) and ANCs via equation (18). For wide resonances, a simplified R-matrix formula (equation 20) is used instead.

## Key numbers
- For the 3/2⁻ ground state of ⁷Li in the ⁴He+³H channel, the computed ANC is 3.44 fm⁻¹/² (experimental: 3.57 ± 0.15 fm⁻¹/²), using experimental decay energy E_exp = -2.467 MeV; using the theoretical energy E_th = -2.529 MeV changes the ANC to 3.30 fm⁻¹/² (a 3% shift for a 62 keV energy difference).
- For the 7/2⁻₁ state of ⁷Li in the ⁴He+³H channel, the computed decay width is 65 keV (experimental: 69 keV), using experimental resonance energy 2.195 MeV; using the calculated energy 2.172 MeV gives 62.2 keV (a 5% change for a 23 keV difference).
- For the 1/2⁻₁ state of ⁷Li in the ⁴He+³H channel, the computed ANC is 2.95 fm⁻¹/² (experimental: 3.0 ± 0.15 fm⁻¹/²).
- The NCSM basis cutoff parameter used was N^{max}_{tot} = 15, with oscillator parameter ℏω = 20.0 MeV and the Daejeon16 NN-potential.
- For the 5/2⁻ state at E_exp = 32.641 MeV in the ⁴He+³H channel, the computed width is 564 keV (experimental total: 918 keV).
- For the 3/2⁻, T=3/2 state at E_exp = 28.005 MeV in the ⁶He(gr.)+p channel, the computed width is 111 keV (experimental total: 260 keV).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier single-channel approach (Rodkin and Tchuvil'sky, 2019) to a multi-channel version and applies it to all relevant two-body decay channels of ⁷Li. It contrasts with the NCSMC method (Navratil et al., 2016) which is limited to one-channel problems and light fragments with A ≤ 3, whereas the present method can handle multiple channels simultaneously and heavier fragments. The approach enables ab initio calculation of resonance widths and ANCs for light nuclei using the well-tested Daejeon16 potential, with the flexibility to use experimental decay energies to improve accuracy.
