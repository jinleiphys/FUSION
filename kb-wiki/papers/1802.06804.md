# Azimuthal Anisotropies at High Momentum from Purely Non-Hydrodynamic Transport

> In the limit of short mean free path, relativistic kinetic theory gives rise to hydrodynamics through a systematically improvable gradient expansion. In the present work, a systematically improvable expansion in the opposite limit of large mean free path is considered, describing the dynamics of particles which are almost, but not quite, non-interacting. This non-hydrodynamic "eremitic" expansion does not break down for large gradients, and may be useful in situations where a hydrodynamic treatment is not applicable. As applications, azimuthal anisotropies at high transverse momentum in Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV are calculated from the first order eremitic expansion of kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation.

# Azimuthal Anisotropies at High Momentum from Purely Non-Hydrodynamic Transport

## Key claim

The eremitic (large mean free path) expansion of relativistic kinetic theory provides a systematically improvable description of transport in regimes where hydrodynamics fails, and its first-order calculation of azimuthal anisotropies at high transverse momentum in Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV suggests that non-hydrodynamic modes alone can generate elliptic flow without any contribution from hydrodynamic sound modes.

## Method

The author develops a "eremitic" expansion of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, treating the inverse relaxation time (1/τ_R) as a small parameter rather than the gradient expansion parameter used in hydrodynamics. The zeroth order corresponds to free-streaming (ballistic) particle evolution solved by the method of characteristics, while the first order correction integrates the collision kernel along the free-streaming trajectories. The method is applied to initial conditions consisting of Gaussian hot-spots in both 3+1 dimensions and boost-invariant (Milne coordinate) setups, from which the energy-momentum tensor and elliptic flow coefficients are computed numerically.

## Key numbers

- For a single Gaussian hot-spot of width σ, the mean square momentum at early times increases as ⟨p²⟩ ≈ 12 T_init² (3/5)^(3/2) (1 + 0.000406634 t^5 T_init / (σ^4 τ_R T) + O(t^6))
- For two Gaussian hot-spots separated by 2σ, the elliptic momentum anisotropy at early times is ⟨e₂⟩ ≈ 0.0149 t^3 T_init / (σ^2 τ_R T)
- The critical momentum scale separating hydrodynamic from non-hydrodynamic dominance is k_c τ_R ≈ 4.5313912, above which hydrodynamic poles move onto a different Riemann sheet
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the kinetic theory is η/s = τ_R T / 5
- Numerical results for differential elliptic flow v₂(p_T) are shown for τ_R T = 2.5 (corresponding to η/s = 0.5) with Gaussian hot-spots of width σ = 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the pioneering analytic treatment of rarefied gas dynamics by Borghini and Gombeaud (2010) to the case of high momentum particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with the standard hydrodynamic gradient expansion (Burnett, 1935; Chapman-Enskog) which breaks down for large gradients, and with the collective mode analysis of Romatschke (2015) which identified the transition scale k_c where non-hydrodynamic modes dominate. The eremitic expansion enables analytic or semi-analytic calculations of observables like elliptic flow in regimes where full numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation are challenging, and provides a framework for interpreting experimental data on azimuthal anisotropies at high p_T as signatures of purely non-hydrodynamic transport.
