# Ambiguities in the definition of local spatial densities in light hadrons

> The relationship between the matrix element of a local operator and the Fourier transform of the associated form factor fails for systems such as the nucleon where its intrinsic size is of order its Compton wavelength. Although one can conceive of an intrinsic charge density distribution in the proton, there does not seem to be an unambiguous way to define, compute, or measure it precisely.

# Ambiguities in the definition of local spatial densities in light hadrons

## Key claim

The Fourier transform of a form factor cannot be unambiguously interpreted as a local spatial density distribution for systems like the nucleon, where the intrinsic size (≈0.85 fm) is comparable to the Compton wavelength (≈0.21 fm). The charge density distribution extracted from a form factor depends unavoidably on the wave packet used to localize the system, and no localization-independent definition exists for such systems.

## Method

The author constructs a spinless model "nucleon" with a gaussian form factor parameterized by a length scale Δ (the naive RMS charge radius) and a gaussian wave packet with RMS radius R to localize the system. The charge density distribution ρ(r) in the localized state is computed by integrating the matrix element of the charge density operator between momentum eigenstates weighted by the wave packet. The result is evaluated analytically in the non-relativistic limit (large mass expansion) and numerically for the nucleon, deuteron, and pion cases where the expansion fails.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon Compton wavelength: 0.21 fm; nucleon intrinsic size (naive RMS charge radius): 0.85 fm
- For atomic hydrogen: Δ ≈ 5×10⁻¹¹ m (Bohr radius), m = m_p = 1/(2.1×10⁻¹⁶ m); the naive charge density agrees with the localized distribution to ≈0.02% for R between 10 fm and a₀/10
- For carbon (A=12): Δ ≈ 3 fm; the difference between localized (R=0.2 fm) and naive radial charge density is less than 0.0005 over all r
- For the nucleon: fractional differences between naive and localized charge density are of order one for R=0.2 fm and R=0.5 fm, demonstrating the distribution is not well-defined
- For the deuteron: Δ ≈ 2.15 fm, Compton wavelength ≈ 0.1 fm; naive charge density agrees with localized distribution to ≈20% for R near 0.5 fm between r=0 and ≈1.5Δ
- For the pion: intrinsic size Δ ≈ 0.5 fm, Compton wavelength ≈ 1.4 fm; the Fourier transform relation fails completely

## Context

This work extends and quantifies earlier criticisms by Burkardt (2000) and Miller (multiple papers, most recently 2018) of the standard Sachs identification of the nucleon charge density with the Fourier transform of the electric form factor. The analysis shows that the problem applies not only to charge density but to any local operator matrix element in light hadrons, including recent lattice calculations of pressure and shear force distributions (Shanahan, 2018). The paper demonstrates that the condition Δ ≫ R ≫ 1/m (intrinsic size much larger than localization scale, which is much larger than Compton wavelength) is required for a localization-independent density, a condition satisfied by atoms and heavy nuclei but not by the nucleon, deuteron, or pion.
