# Studying Diquark Structure of Heavy Baryons in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We propose the enhancement of $Λ_c$ yield in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC as a novel signal for the existence of diquarks in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in these collisions as well as in the $Λ_c$. Assuming that stable bound diquarks can exist in the quark-gluon plasma, we argue that the yield of $Λ_c$ would be increased by two-body collisions between $ud$ diquarks and $c$ quarks, in addition to normal three-body collisions among $u$, $d$ and $c$ quarks. A quantitative study of this effect based on the coalescence model shows that including the contribution of diquarks to $Λ_c$ production indeed leads to a substantial enhancement of the $Λ_c/D$ ratio in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The enhancement of the $\Lambda_c$ yield in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC is proposed as a signal for the existence of diquarks in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma, but this result depends on the assumption that stable bound diquarks can exist in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used the quark coalescence model to estimate the yield of $\Lambda_c$ baryons from the quark-gluon plasma. The yield was calculated as the sum of two contributions: two-body collisions between $ud$ diquarks and $c$ quarks, and three-body collisions among $u$, $d$, and $c$ quarks. The light quarks were assumed to be in thermal and chemical equilibrium, while the number of $c$ quarks was estimated from initial hard nucleon-nucleon collisions with the assumption that they also reach thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c/D^0 = 0.11$ for the case without diquarks at critical temperature $T_C = 175$ MeV
- $\Lambda_c/D^0 = 0.44$ for the case with diquarks of mass $m_{[ud]} = 450$ MeV at $T_C = 175$ MeV
- $\Lambda_c/D^0 = 0.89$ for the case with diquarks of mass $m_{[ud]} = 600$ MeV at $T_C = 175$ MeV
- Enhancement factor of 4 to 8 for the $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio when including diquarks, depending on diquark binding energy
- $\Lambda_b/B = 0.098$ for the case without diquarks, and $\Lambda_b/B = 0.38$ and $0.82$ for diquark masses 450 MeV and 600 MeV respectively
- Diquark mass of 450 MeV corresponds to maximum binding energy of 150 MeV; diquark mass of 600 MeV corresponds to minimum binding energy at threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Lee et al., 2007) that diquark correlations in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma would enhance $\Lambda_c$ production. The predicted $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio of 0.11 without diquarks is consistent with the statistical model estimate of approximately 0.12 (Fries et al., 2003) and with values extracted from p+p collisions by the SELEX collaboration (0.159) and from B meson decay (0.14). The study enables experimental verification of diquark correlations in the quark-gluon plasma through charmed and bottom hadron measurements at RHIC and LHC, with detector upgrades underway by the ALICE, STAR, and PHENIX collaborations.
