# Light Scalar Mesons

> The crucial points of physics of light scalar mesons are discussed. The special attention is given to the chiral shielding of lightest scalar mesons and the production of the well established light scalar mesons in the ϕradiative decays. Arguments in favor of the four-quark model of the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the production of the scalar mesons \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) in \(\phi\) radiative decays is established as a four-quark transition via a \(K^+K^-\) loop, providing strong evidence for the four-quark (\(q^2\bar q^2\)) nature of these mesons, rather than a conventional \(q\bar q\) interpretation.

## Method

The author analyzes the mechanism of \(\phi \to \gamma a_0(980)\) and \(\phi \to \gamma f_0(980)\) decays using a \(K^+K^-\)-loop model, where the intermediate \(K^+K^-\) state generates both the real and imaginary parts of the decay amplitude. The model is tested against experimental mass spectra from SND, CMD-2, and KLOE collaborations, and the gauge invariance constraint on the amplitude is examined. The large \(N_C\) expansion is applied to compare the suppression factors for two-quark versus four-quark transition scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The photon energy at \(m = 990\) MeV is \(\omega(990\,\text{MeV}) = 29\) MeV, used to illustrate the need for a form-factor with \(R \approx 100\) GeV\(^{-1}\) to stop the \(\omega(m)^2\) dependence, which is deemed implausible.
- For an isolated resonance with mass 980 MeV and width 60 MeV, the relative spectral strengths are \(S_R(920\,\text{MeV}):S_R(950\,\text{MeV}):S_R(970\,\text{MeV}):S_R(980\,\text{MeV}) = 3:2.7:1.8:1\), showing the shift of the maximum toward lower masses without the \(K^+K^-\)-loop mechanism.
- The \(\phi \to \gamma a_0(980)\) decay intensity in the two-quark model is suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_c^3\) compared to the four-quark model, while in the \(f_0 = s\bar s\) model the suppression is only \(1/N_c\).

## Context

This work extends the four-quark model originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) for the light scalar mesons \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\), and builds on the author's earlier papers (1979-1984) on chiral shielding and the linear \(\sigma\)-model. The analysis of \(\phi\) radiative decays provides a new experimental test that distinguishes between \(q\bar q\) and \(q^2\bar q^2\) interpretations, and the large \(N_C\) expansion arguments offer a theoretical framework to quantify the OZI rule suppression. The results enable future studies of the light scalar nonet, including the putative \(\sigma(600)\) and \(\kappa(700-900)\) mesons, within the context of non-perturbative QCD.
