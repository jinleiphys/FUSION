# Pions in isospin asymmetric nuclei

> Using a pair of the lightest mirror nuclei, $^3$He and $^3$H, we study the effect of the medium modification of pion fields on the flavor non-singlet structure function. The change of the pion fields leads to an enhancement of the flavor asymmetry of the antiquark distributions in a nucleus.

## Key claim

The medium modification of pion fields in mirror nuclei leads to an enhancement of the flavor asymmetry of antiquark distributions, but the effect is small compared to nuclear shadowing at very low x (around 10⁻³–10⁻⁴), where shadowing dominates the non-singlet structure function.

## Method

The authors calculated the non-singlet structure function difference between ³He and ³H using a convolution model where the nucleon structure functions in the nucleus are expressed in terms of bare nucleon and pion structure functions, with the pion light-cone momentum distributions modified by nuclear many-body effects. They introduced a scaling assumption with two parameters: α representing strong-interaction changes (Pauli blocking, RPA correlations) and β representing Coulomb-induced shifts in the pion momentum distributions, and combined this with the Gribov-Glauber multiple scattering formalism for nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers

- The average number of π⁰ per nucleon ⟨f_π⁰N⟩ = 0.083, obtained with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV
- The free nucleon Gottfried integral I_G^N(0) = 0.223, consistent with the measured value 0.235 ± 0.026
- For α = 0.05 (0.1) [0.2] and β = 0, the change in the Gottfried integral δI_G^(³He,³H)(0) = -0.0111 (-0.0221) [-0.0443], corresponding to a reduction of 5 (9) [20]% from the free value
- With α = 0 (no pion field change), the nuclear Gottfried integral I_G^(³He,³H)(10⁻⁴) = 0.2953 (case 1) and 0.3395 (case 2) at Q² = 4 GeV²
- With α = 0.1 (0.2), I_G^(³He,³H)(10⁻⁴) = 0.2699 (0.2444) for case 1 and 0.3142 (0.2829) for case 2
- The matter radii used were 1.769 fm for ³He and 1.524 fm for ³H, corresponding to Gaussian parameters b = 40.59 and 30.06 GeV⁻² respectively

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Korpa and Dieperink (1999) on pion fields in asymmetric nuclear matter to the specific case of the lightest mirror nuclei ³He and ³H, and builds on the authors' previous study of shadowing corrections to the non-singlet structure function (Guzey et al., 2000). The results enable future deep-inelastic scattering experiments on mirror nuclei to probe non-perturbative QCD dynamics, particularly the modification of the pion cloud in the nuclear medium, and could be extended to larger mirror nuclei where multi-pion Fock states and Δ isobars become important.
