# Massive neutron stars with hyperonic core : a case study with the IUFSU model

> The recent discoveries of massive neutron stars, such as PSR J$0348+0432$ and PSR J$1614-2230$, have raised questions about the existence of exotic matter such as hyperons in the neutron star core. The validity of many established equations of states (EoS's) like the GM1 and FSUGold are also questioned. We investigate the existence of hyperonic matter in the central regions of massive neutron stars using Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the recently proposed IUFSU model. The IUFSU model is extended by including hyperons to study the neutron star in $β$ equilibrium. The effect of different hyperonic potentials, namely $Σ$ and $Ξ$ potentials, on the EoS and hence the maximum mass of neutron stars has been studied. We have also considered the effect of stellar rotation since the observed massive stars are pulsars. It has been found that a maximum mass of $1.93M_{\odot}$, which is within the 3$σ$ limit of the observed mass of PSR J$0348+0432$, can be obtained for rotating stars, with certain choices of the hyperonic potentials. The said star contains a fair amount of hyperons near the core.

## Key claim

The IUFSU model, extended to include the full baryon octet, can produce a rotating neutron star with a maximum mass of 1.93 solar masses that contains a fair amount of hyperons near the core, but only for certain choices of the hyperonic potentials (specifically, a repulsive Sigma potential of +40 MeV and a Xi potential of 0 MeV or greater) and only when stellar rotation is included; static hyperonic stars with the same model reach only 1.62 solar masses, which is below the observed mass of PSR J0348+0432.

## Method

The authors used Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the IUFSU parameter set, extended by including the full baryon octet (p, n, Lambda, Sigma+, Sigma0, Sigma-, Xi-, Xi0) plus electrons and muons, under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. Hyperon-meson couplings were taken from the SU(6) quark model, with scalar couplings determined by fitting the hyperonic potentials U_Sigma and U_Xi, which were varied independently from -40 MeV to +40 MeV. The equations of state were computed both with and without the strange vector phi meson, and the resulting EoS were used to solve for static and rotating axisymmetric star properties using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu method implemented in the "rns" code.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of a static pure nuclear matter star with IUFSU: 1.92 solar masses, radius 11.24 km.
- Maximum mass of a static hyperonic star with IUFSU: 1.62 solar masses, radius 10.82 km, central energy density 2.46 x 10^15 g cm^-3 (for U_Sigma = +40 MeV, U_Xi = +40 MeV).
- Maximum mass of a rotating hyperonic star in the Keplerian limit: 1.93 solar masses, radius 14.7 km, angular velocity 0.86 x 10^4 s^-1 (for U_Sigma = +40 MeV, U_Xi >= 0 MeV).
- Maximum mass of a rotating pure nuclear matter star: 2.29 solar masses, radius 15.31 km.
- Saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter for IUFSU: saturation density n0 = 0.155 fm^-3, binding energy per nucleon -16.40 MeV, compression modulus K = 231.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the IUFSU model (FSUGold et al., 2012) by including hyperons to test whether the model can accommodate the observed ~2 solar mass pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, which had previously challenged many hyperonic equations of state (e.g., GM1, FSUGold) that predicted maximum masses below 1.6 solar masses. The results show that while static hyperonic stars with IUFSU fail to reach the observed masses, rotating hyperonic stars can reach 1.93 solar masses, consistent with the 3-sigma lower limit of PSR J0348+0432, suggesting that hyperonic cores remain viable if rotation is considered. The authors note that related work on whether such stars could also contain a quark core is in progress.
