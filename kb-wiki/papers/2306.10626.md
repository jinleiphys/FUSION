# Exclusive $η_c$ production from small-$x$ evolved Odderon at a electron-ion collider

> We compute exclusive $η_c$ production in high energy electron-nucleon and electron-nucleus collisions that is sensitive to the Odderon. In perturbative QCD the Odderon is a $C$-odd color singlet consisting of at least three $t$-channel gluons exchanged with the target. By using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory our result describes the Odderon exchange at the high collision energies that would be reached at a future electron-ion collider. The Odderon distribution is evolved to small-$x$ using the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation with running coupling corrections. We find that while at low momentum transfers $t$ the cross section off a proton is dominated by the Primakoff process, the Odderon becomes relevant at larger momentum transfers of $|t|\geq1.5$ GeV$^2$. We point that the Odderon could also be extracted at low-$t$ using neutron targets since the Primakoff component is strongly suppressed. In the case of nuclear targets, the Odderon cross section becomes enhanced thanks to the mass number of the nuclear target. The gluon saturation effect induces a shift in the diffractive pattern with respect to the Primakoff process that could be used as a signal for the Odderon.

## Key claim
Exclusive ηc production at a future electron-ion collider can probe the Odderon, but for proton targets the Odderon signal only dominates over the Primakoff background at momentum transfers |t| ≥ 1.5 GeV²; for neutron targets the Primakoff component is strongly suppressed even at low |t|, and for nuclear targets the Odderon cross section is enhanced by the mass number A.

## Method
The authors computed the amplitude for exclusive ηc photoproduction in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, using the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation with running coupling corrections to evolve the Odderon distribution to small x. The coupled Pomeron-Odderon BK system was solved numerically on a (r⊥, b⊥, φrb) grid with 500 radial points and 100 angular points, using a third-order Adams-Bashforth method up to rapidity Y=5. For the proton Odderon initial condition they used the Dumitru-Mäntysaari-Paatelainen (DMP) model from light-cone quark wavefunctions, and for nuclear targets they used the Jeon-Venugopalan (JV) model with a cubic term in the color source functional.

## Key numbers
- The Odderon becomes relevant at momentum transfers |t| ≥ 1.5 GeV² for proton targets
- The ηc meson light-cone wave function parameters: N_P = 0.547, R²_P = 2.48 GeV⁻², charm quark mass m_c = 1.4 GeV
- The BK evolution used N_f = 3, C² = 7.2, Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV, with α_fr = 0.7
- The Pomeron initial condition parameters: Q²_S,0 = 0.06 GeV², e_c = 18.9, σ_0/2 = 16.36 mb
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear profile parameters: d = 0.54 fm, R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3} fm
- The BK grid spanned r⊥ from 10⁻⁶ GeV⁻¹ to 10⁴ GeV⁻¹ with n_r⊥ = 500 points and n_φrb = 100 points, with step size ΔY = 0.1

## Context
This work extends earlier computations of ηc production via Odderon exchange (Czyzewski 1996, Engel 1997, Bartels 2001, Ma 2003, Dumitru 2019) by including small-x evolution and gluon saturation effects from the CGC framework. Prior work by Dumitru, Mäntysaari and Paatelainen (2022) provided the non-perturbative Odderon initial condition for protons, while the Jeon-Venugopalan model (2005) gave the nuclear Odderon initial condition. The results enable experimental searches for the Odderon at the future Electron-Ion Collider, with the diffractive pattern shift between Odderon and Primakoff processes serving as a distinctive signature.
