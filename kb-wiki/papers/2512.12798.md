# Single-step Quantum Simulation of Two Nucleons

> Quantum computing offers a scalable approach to solving the nuclear shell model, a highly complex and exponentially scaled many-body problem. This work presents a numerical simulation of the subspace search variational quantum eigensolver (SSVQE) combined with an adaptive derivative-assembles pseudo-trotter (ADAPT) ansatz to obtain the low-lying states of any nuclear system in a single optimization run. As an example, we apply this method in this work to a trivial identical nucleon system, two nucleons in the $0p_{3/2}$ orbital, mapped to 4 qubits depicting m-scheme single-particle states including a surface delta effective interaction using the Jordan-Wigner transformation. The ADAPT-SSVQE algorithm, by utilizing a symmetry-preserving double-excitation ADAPT operator pool, uniquely optimizes a weighted energy sum, forcing the simultaneous convergence of two lowest states within the total angular momentum $M_J=0$ subspace. We demonstrate the accuracy of the method by benchmarking against the exact diagonalization, confirming its potential for probing nuclear structure and pairing phenomena on current and near-future quantum devices without requiring multi-step procedure for excited states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ADAPT-SSVQE algorithm can simultaneously approximate the ground and first excited state of two identical nucleons in a single $0p_{3/2}$ orbital using a single optimization run, but only for this trivial test case; the authors acknowledge that limiting the operator pool to symmetry-preserving double-excitation operators reduces the possible iterative paths and can only be justified *a posteriori* if results remain successful for future larger-scale studies.

## Method

The authors map the nuclear shell-model Hamiltonian with a surface delta interaction for two identical nucleons in the $0p_{3/2}$ orbital onto 4 qubits using the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They apply the subspace-search variational quantum eigensolver (SSVQE) combined with an adaptive derivative-assembled pseudo-trotter (ADAPT) ansatz, using a symmetry-preserving pool of double-excitation operators that conserve total angular momentum projection $M_J=0$. The algorithm optimizes a weighted sum of energy expectation values from two initial orthogonal Hartree-Fock basis states, selecting operators by maximizing the weighted absolute gradient contribution until a tolerance of $10^{-2}$ is reached, with classical optimization performed using the COBYLA algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy $E_0 = -2.0$ MeV and excited state energy $E_1 = -0.4$ MeV, benchmarked against exact classical diagonalization.
- The ADAPT algorithm converged in only 2 iterations, reaching a final gradient of $1.8 \times 10^{-4}$, below the defined tolerance of $10^{-2}$.
- The gradient value of the selected double-excitation operator with the Hamiltonian was $1.6$, indicating initial Hartree-Fock states are far from true correlated eigenstates.
- The single optimized parameter $\theta_0$ was found to be $0.785$.
- The quantum circuit requires 48 CNOT gates, has a circuit depth of 72, a width of 4 qubits, 32 single-qubit $\sqrt{X}$ and $\sqrt{X}^\dagger$ gates, 32 Hadamard gates, and 8 rotation gates $R_z$.
- The interaction strength parameter was set to $V_1 = 1$ MeV for this schematic calculation.

## Context

This work extends the ADAPT-VQE approach previously applied to nuclear shell-model ground states (Spain, 2022) and the problem-specific ansatz approach for $^{58}$Ni (Bharti, 2022) by incorporating the SSVQE method from molecular simulations (Nakanishi, 2019) to obtain multiple excited states simultaneously. The method enables single-step spectroscopic calculations of low-lying nuclear states on near-term quantum devices without requiring multi-step procedures for excited states, with the authors reporting preliminary results extending to two nucleons in the $g_{9/2}$ orbital ($^{70}$Ni) within a single optimization run.
