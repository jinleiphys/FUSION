# Correlations in Nuclear Arrhenius-Type Plots

> Arrhenius-type plots for multifragmentation process, defined as the transverse energy dependence of the single-fragment emission-probability, -ln(p_{b}) vs 1/sqrt(E_{t}), have been studied by examining the relationship of the parameters p_{b} and E_{t} to the intermediate-mass fragment multiplicity <n>. The linearity of these plots reflects the correlation of the fragment multiplicity with the transverse energy. These plots may not provide thermal scaling information about fragment production as previously suggested.

## Key claim

The linearity observed in nuclear Arrhenius-type plots (ln(1/p_b) vs 1/sqrt(E_t)) does not provide thermal scaling information about fragment production; instead, it merely reflects the self-correlation arising from the linear dependence of transverse energy E_t on the intermediate-mass fragment multiplicity ⟨n⟩, with a non-zero offset.

## Method

The authors examined the relationship between the binomial parameters p_b and m, the transverse energy E_t, and the intermediate-mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity ⟨n⟩ for multifragmentation in heavy-ion reactions. They derived that E_t can be expressed as a linear function of ⟨n⟩ (E_t = a + b⟨n⟩), and showed that when the ratio a/b is approximately 0.5, the plot of 1/⟨n⟩ versus 1/sqrt(a/b + ⟨n⟩) becomes nearly linear. They then compared this self-correlation to published data for the 36Ar + 197Au reaction at E/A = 110 MeV, and also demonstrated that modified Poisson distributions constrained by charge conservation can be well described by binomial distributions, showing that p_b is not necessarily an elementary emission probability.

## Key numbers

- For the 36Ar + 197Au reaction at E/A = 110 MeV, the binomial parameter m = 12 and the slope parameter b = 213 MeV were used to reproduce the Arrhenius-type plot via self-correlation.
- The ratio a/b = 0.5 was found to produce a nearly linear curve in the self-correlation plot for ⟨n⟩ values ranging from 0.25 to 5.0.
- Temperature measurements show that temperature varies by less than 1 MeV from peripheral to central collisions, while E_t is strongly impact parameter dependent.
- The charge constraint factor α in the modified Poisson distribution is approximately 0.2, based on charged particle multiplicities from Ref. [24].

## Context

This work extends and contradicts the interpretation of Arrhenius-type plots proposed by Moretto et al. (1995) and Tso et al. (1995), who had inferred thermal scaling of multifragment processes from the linearity of ln(1/p_b) vs 1/sqrt(E_t) plots. The authors show that recent temperature measurements by Kwiatkowski et al. (1995) and Kunde et al. (1996) demonstrate that temperature is nearly independent of impact parameter, contradicting the assumption that sqrt(E_t) is proportional to temperature. This work enables a re-examination of previous interpretations of thermal scaling in emission probabilities, charge distributions, and azimuthal correlations reported by Moretto et al. (1995-1996) and Phair et al. (1992-1995).
