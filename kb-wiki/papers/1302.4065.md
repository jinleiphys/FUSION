# Finite volume effects and quark mass dependence of the N(1535) and N(1650)

> For resonances decaying in a finite volume, the simple identification of state and eigenvalue is lost. The extraction of the scattering amplitude is a major challenge as we demonstrate by extrapolating the physical S_{11} amplitude of pion-nucleon scattering to the finite volume and unphysical quark masses, using a unitarized chiral framework including all next-to-leading order contact terms. We show that the pole movement of the resonances N(1535)1/2^- and N(1650)1/2^- with varying quark masses is non-trivial. In addition, there are several strongly coupled S-wave thresholds that induce a similar avoided level crossing as narrow resonances. The level spectrum is predicted for two typical lattice setups, and ways to extract the amplitude from upcoming lattice data are discussed.

## Key claim

The pole movement of the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances with varying quark masses is non-trivial, and the level spectrum in a finite volume is dominated by avoided level crossings from strongly coupled S-wave thresholds rather than by the resonances themselves, complicating the straightforward extraction of resonance properties from lattice data.

## Method

The authors use a unitarized chiral framework including all next-to-leading order contact terms to describe the S11 partial wave of pion-nucleon scattering, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an on-shell approximation. They discretize the finite-volume propagator using the Lüscher zeta-function formalism and predict the volume dependence of energy levels for two different lattice setups (ETMC and QCDSF), applying both periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions for the strange quark.

## Key numbers

- Physical pole positions: N(1535) at (1487 - i 84) MeV, N(1650) at (1658 - i 94) MeV, and a hidden pole N' at (1675 - i 103) MeV
- Physical scattering length: a_piN^{1/2} = 1.13 GeV^{-1}
- ETMC setup (M_pi = 269 MeV): pole positions at (1714 - i 14) MeV (hidden), (1839 - i 67) MeV (hidden), and (1817 - i 56) MeV (hidden)
- ETMC scattering length: a_piN^{1/2} = 0.73 GeV^{-1}
- QCDSF setup (M_pi = 286 MeV): pole positions at (1562 - i 38) MeV and (1479 - i 34) MeV (hidden)
- QCDSF scattering length: a_piN^{1/2} = 1.18 GeV^{-1}
- Lattice size for both setups: L/a = 32, with lattice spacing a = 0.078 fm (ETMC) and a = 0.075 fm (QCDSF)

## Context

This work extends the unitarized chiral approach of Bruns (2010) and Mai (2012) to finite volume, combining it with the Lüscher method for coupled channels pioneered by Lage (2009) and applied to mesons by Bernard (2010) and Döring (2011). The results show that for the ETMC setup, the resonance poles are hidden and the level spectrum is dominated by threshold effects, while for the QCDSF setup a clear resonance signal appears below the K-Lambda threshold. The work enables strategies for extracting resonance parameters from upcoming lattice data, particularly by using twisted boundary conditions to distinguish threshold effects from genuine resonance signals.
