# Non-linear Supersymmetry for non-Hermitian, non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians: II. Rigorous results

> We continue our investigation of the nonlinear SUSY for complex potentials started in the Part I (math-ph/0610024) and prove the theorems characterizing its structure in the case of non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians. This part provides the mathematical basis of previous studies. The classes of potentials invariant under SUSY transformations for non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians are specified and the asymptotics of formal eigenfunctions and associated functions are derived. Several results on the normalizability of associated functions at infinities are rigorously proved. Finally the Index Theorem on relation between Jordan structures of intertwined Hamiltonians depending of the behavior of elements of canonical basis of supercharge kernel at infinity is proven.

## Key claim
The paper rigorously proves the structure of non-linear supersymmetry (SUSY) for non-Hermitian, non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians, specifically proving an Index Theorem that relates the Jordan structures of intertwined Hamiltonians based on the asymptotic behavior of the canonical basis of the supercharge kernel at infinity. The results are safely applicable to PT-symmetric non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with a "soft type" of non-Hermiticity, where the real part of the potential dominates over its imaginary part at both coordinate infinities.

## Method
The paper defines two classes of complex potentials, $K$ (physical) and $\cal K$ (auxiliary, for intermediate Hamiltonians), satisfying conditions on differentiability, a lower bound on the real part, and a ratio condition where the imaginary part is asymptotically negligible compared to the real part. It then derives rigorous asymptotics for formal eigenfunctions and associated functions of a Hamiltonian with a potential in $\cal K$, using these to prove lemmas on the normalizability of these functions and the invariance of the potential classes under intertwining. These results are used to prove the Index Theorem, which characterizes the relationship between the Jordan cells of SUSY partner Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers
*   The potential classes $K$ and $\cal K$ require that for $|x| \ge R_0$, ${\rm Re}\, V(x) \ge \varepsilon$ for some $\varepsilon > 0$.
*   The condition ${\rm Im}\, V(x) / {\rm Re}\, V(x) = o(1)$ as $x \to \pm\infty$ is imposed on potentials in both classes.
*   Lemma 5 establishes constants $C_1, C_2, C_3 > 0$ such that for $|x| \ge R_1$, $C_1 \le |V(x)|^2 / |V(x)-\lambda|^2 \le C_2$ and ${\rm Re} \sqrt{V(x)-\lambda} \ge C_3 \sqrt{|V(x)|}$.
*   Lemma 6 provides asymptotic estimates for integrals $I_{1,\uparrow\downarrow}(x;\lambda) = O(1/\xi_{\uparrow\downarrow}(x))$ and $I_{2,\uparrow\downarrow}(x;\lambda) = O(1/\xi_{\uparrow\downarrow}^2(x))$ as $x \to \pm\infty$, where $\xi_{\uparrow\downarrow}(x)$ is an integral of $\sqrt{|V(x)|}$.
*   Lemma 8 provides the leading asymptotic term for normalizable zero-modes: $\varphi_{0,\uparrow\downarrow}(x) \sim (V(x)-\lambda)^{-1/4} e^{-\xi_{\uparrow\downarrow}(x;\lambda)}$ as $x \to \pm\infty$.
*   Lemma 9 provides the asymptotics for formal associated functions $\varphi_{n,\uparrow\downarrow}(x)$, which include a factor of $(1/n!)(\pm \frac{1}{2} \int dx_1 / \sqrt{V(x_1)-\lambda})^n$.

## Context
This paper is the second part of a study by Sokolov (2006) that provides the rigorous mathematical foundation for the structure of non-linear SUSY in non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, extending the formal results of Part I. It specifically addresses the case of non-diagonalizable Hamiltonians, which are relevant to PT-symmetric quantum mechanics as studied by Bender and others. The proven Index Theorem enables a precise classification of the Jordan block structures of SUSY partner Hamiltonians, allowing for the construction of new complex potentials with specific spectral properties.
