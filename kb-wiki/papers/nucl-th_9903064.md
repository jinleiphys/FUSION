# Double Delta(1232) excitation and the ABC effect in the reaction n + p -> 2H (pi pi)

> The deuteron spectrum in n+p -> 2 H(pi pi) at k_n = 1.88 GeV/c and theta_d = 0^o is explained by considering a Delta Delta excitation as the dominant reaction mechanism for the 2 pi production. We present a new theoretical approach based on a coupled channel formalism which allows to include the residual interaction within the intermediate Delta Delta and Delta N systems. The corresponding interaction potentials V_{DeltaDelta} and V_{DeltaN} are adopted from a meson exchange model with pi, rho, omega, and sigma exchange taken into account. The influence of the residual interaction on the deuteron spectrum is studied. We also predict the angular distribution of the two pions. It is shown that this distribution is closely connected to the spin structure of the DeltaDelta excitation.

# Double Delta(1232) excitation and the ABC effect in the reaction n + p -> 2H (pi pi)

## Key claim

The ABC effect in the deuteron spectrum of n + p -> 2H (pi pi) at a neutron beam momentum of 1.88 GeV/c and forward deuteron angle is explained by a double Delta(1232) excitation as the dominant reaction mechanism, but only when residual interactions within the intermediate Delta-Delta and Delta-N systems are included via a coupled-channel formalism. The authors acknowledge that the theoretical cross section decreases too rapidly at larger deuteron scattering angles and is underestimated by nearly two orders of magnitude near the 2-pion production threshold, where other mechanisms must dominate.

## Method

The authors developed a non-relativistic coupled-channel model incorporating two reaction mechanisms: double Delta excitation (both Deltas decay into a pion and nucleon) and single Delta excitation with a Delta-N intermediate state. The residual interaction potentials V_DeltaDelta and V_DeltaN were constructed from a meson exchange model including pi, rho, omega, and sigma exchange, with the Delta resonance treated as a quasi-particle with energy-dependent intrinsic width. The transition amplitudes were evaluated using the Lanczos method to solve the coupled integro-differential equations, and the deuteron spectrum was calculated in the deuteron rest frame before transformation to the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- Neutron beam momentum: k_n = 1.88 GeV/c (laboratory system), with center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) approximately 2 M_Delta (twice the Delta(1232) mass)
- Deuteron measured at forward angle: theta_d = 0 degrees
- Maximal invariant mass of the 2-pion system: M_pipi = 511 MeV (at the central peak)
- Minimal invariant mass of the 2-pion system: M_pipi = 2 m_pi (at the outer peaks)
- The theoretical total cross section near the 2-pion production threshold (k_n = 1.19 GeV) underestimates experiment by nearly two orders of magnitude
- At larger scattering angles (theta_d = 4.5 degrees and 7.5 degrees), the theoretical cross section underestimates experimental data by a factor of 2 to 3

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Bar-Nir et al. (1975) and Risser et al. (1973), who first showed that the double Delta mechanism could explain the main features of the ABC effect in n + p -> 2H (pi pi). The ABC effect was originally observed by Abashian, Booth, and Crow (1961, 1963) in p + 2H -> 3He + (pi pi) and later in other reactions including n + p -> 2H + X by Plouin et al. (1978) and Abdivaliev et al. (1980). The present work improves upon prior calculations by explicitly including residual interactions in the intermediate Delta-Delta and Delta-N systems, which significantly improves agreement with the experimental deuteron spectrum at forward angles. The results enable future experimental measurements of the pion angular distribution to reveal the spin structure of the Delta-Delta excitation, and suggest that near the 2-pion production threshold, other mechanisms such as s-wave rescattering or N*(1440) resonance excitation (as discussed by Alvarez et al., 1998) become important.
