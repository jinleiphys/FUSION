# Cumulative effects of collision integral, strong magnetic field, and quasiparticle description on charge and heat transport in thermal QCD medium

> Our first aim is to explore the effect of the collision integral with the insurance of instantaneous conservation of particle number on charge and heat transport in a thermal QCD medium. The second aim is to see how the dimensional reduction due to strong magnetic field (B) modulates the transport through the entangled effects, {\em such as} collision-time and occupation probability etc. in collision integral. The final aim is to check how the quasiparticle description through dispersion relation of thermal QCD in strong B, alters the aforesaid conclusions. We observe that modified collision term expedites both transport, which is manifested by large magnitudes of electrical ($σ_{\rm el}$) and thermal ($κ$) conductivities, in comparison to relaxation-collision term. As a corollary, Lorenz number is dominated by the later and Knudsen number is by the former. However, strong B not only flips the dominance of collision term in heat transport, it also causes drastic enhancement of both $σ_{\rm el}$ and $κ$ and reduction in specific heat. As a result, the equilibration factor, Knudsen number becomes much larger than one, which defies physical interpretation. Finally, quasiparticle description in the absence of strong B impedes the transport of charge and heat, resulting in the meagre decrease of conductivities, however, strong B does noticeable observations: conductivities now gets reduced to physically plausible values, T-dependence of $σ_{\rm el}$ gets reversed, {\em i.e.} it now decreases with T, effect of collision integral gets smeared in $κ$ etc. Knudsen number thus becomes much smaller than one, implying that the system be remained in equilibrium. These findings attribute to the fact that the collective modes in the dispersion relation of thermal QCD in strong B sets in much larger scale, manifested by large in-medium masses.

## Key claim

The modified BGK collision integral, which ensures instantaneous particle-number conservation, enhances both electrical and thermal conductivities compared to the simpler relaxation-time approximation; however, in a strong magnetic field this enhancement becomes unphysically large (Knudsen number much greater than one) unless quasiparticle descriptions with in-medium masses are included, which restore physically plausible values and reverse the temperature dependence of electrical conductivity.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision term that conserves particle number instantaneously, computing the electrical conductivity from the linear response to an infinitesimal electric field and the thermal conductivity from the heat-flow four-vector in the Navier-Stokes form. They evaluated these transport coefficients for a thermal QCD medium of noninteracting quarks and gluons, then repeated the calculation in the presence of a strong magnetic field (where only the lowest Landau level contributes), and finally incorporated quasiparticle effects by replacing vacuum masses with in-medium thermal masses obtained from the pole of the full propagator in perturbative thermal QCD with a strong magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* Electrical conductivity \(\sigma_{\rm el}\) and thermal conductivity \(\kappa\) are both larger when computed with the BGK collision term than with the relaxation-time approximation; the correction term \(\sigma_{\rm el}^{\rm Corr}\) is positive.
* In a strong magnetic field (\(|q_iB| \gg T^2\) and \(|q_iB| \gg m_i^2\)), both \(\sigma_{\rm el}\) and \(\kappa\) are drastically enhanced and the specific heat is reduced, causing the Knudsen number to become "much larger than one."
* With quasiparticle description in strong magnetic field, \(\sigma_{\rm el}\) decreases with temperature (reversed T-dependence) and the Knudsen number becomes "much smaller than one," indicating the system remains in local equilibrium.
* The relaxation time is given by \(\tau_i(T) = 1 / [5.1 T \alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) (1+0.12(2N_i+1))]\), with the running coupling \(\alpha_s(T) = 6\pi / [(33-2N_f) \ln(Q/\Lambda_{\rm QCD})]\) where \(Q = 2\pi T\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Rath et al. (2019, 2020) that calculated transport coefficients using a mean-free-path (relaxation-time) treatment, which does not conserve particle number instantaneously. The authors introduce the BGK collision term previously applied to QCD plasma instabilities by Schenke et al. (2006) and incorporate strong magnetic field effects relevant to noncentral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where fields of order \(m_\pi^2\) at RHIC and \(15 m_\pi^2\) at LHC are produced. The quasiparticle description using in-medium masses from thermal QCD in strong magnetic field enables physically plausible conductivities and resolves the violation of local equilibrium seen in the noninteracting case.
