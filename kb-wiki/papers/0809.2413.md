# Hydrodynamic view in the NICA energy range

> Results for penetrating probes treated within a hybrid hydro-kinetic model are projected onto the energy range covered by the NICA and FAIR projects. A new source of dileptons emitted from a mixed quark-hadron phase, quark-hadron bremsstrahlung, is proposed. An estimate for the $ππ\toσ\to γγ$ process in nuclear collisions is given.

# Hydrodynamic View in the NICA Energy Range

## Key claim

The hybrid hydro-kinetic model, which combines a kinetic initial stage with subsequent isentropic hydrodynamic expansion using a mixed quark-hadron phase equation of state, suggests that a new dilepton source, quark-hadron bremsstrahlung, can improve agreement with existing SPS data, and that the difference between the Brown-Rho dropping-mass scenario and the strong-broadening scenario for the ρ meson should be more pronounced at NICA energies (≤ 40 AGeV) than at SPS energies.

## Method

The initial stage of heavy-ion collisions is treated kinetically using the Quark Gluon String Model (QGSM) on a 3D grid; at t_kin = 1.3 fm/c, the conserved components of the energy-momentum tensor and baryon density are evaluated and used as initial conditions for subsequent relativistic 3D hydrodynamic evolution. The equation of state is a thermodynamically consistent mixed-phase EoS that allows coexistence of hadrons and quarks/gluons, with a hard thermal loop term added to match lattice QCD asymptotics at high temperature. Dilepton emission rates are integrated over the full space-time evolution, including contributions from ππ → ρ → l⁺l⁻, the proposed quark-hadron bremsstrahlung channel, and the ππ → σ → γγ process evaluated within the NJL model.

## Key numbers

- For semi-central In+In collisions at E_lab = 158 AGeV, the entropy-to-baryon charge ratio S/Q_B becomes practically constant for t_kin ≥ 1.3 fm/c, marking the onset of isentropic expansion.
- The modified ρ mass in the Hatsuda-Lee relation is given by m*_ρ(x) = m_ρ (1 − 0.15 · n_B(x)/n_0).
- The quark-hadron bremsstrahlung estimate uses an arbitrary factor K = 10 to improve agreement with NA60 dimuon data at 158 AGeV.
- The antiquark mass is taken as m_q = 150 MeV in the bremsstrahlung calculation.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 40 AGeV, the total number of photon pairs from ππ → γγ sharply increases above the 2m_π threshold and then flattens at a level of approximately 10⁻², with the resonance channel lower by about an order of magnitude.
- The regime m_σ(T, μ_B) < 2m_π(T, μ_B) starts at T ∼ 165 MeV in the NJL model.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydro-kinetic model of Toneev and Skokov (2006) to the NICA and FAIR energy ranges, using a mixed-phase equation of state developed by Toneev et al. (2004) that incorporates a crossover deconfinement phase transition and is compared to lattice QCD data by Fodor (2003). The analysis of dilepton data from the NA60 collaboration discriminates between the Brown-Rho (1991) dropping-mass scenario and the Rapp-Wambach (2000) strong-broadening scenario; the authors propose quark-hadron bremsstrahlung as a new dilepton source that could help disentangle these scenarios at NICA energies, where baryon densities are higher than at SPS. The first estimate of the ππ → σ → γγ process in nuclear collisions is given, based on the NJL model of Voskresensky et al. (1998), and the results suggest that the predicted resonance maximum is washed out by the integration over the dynamical evolution, making experimental selection challenging against the hadron cocktail background.
