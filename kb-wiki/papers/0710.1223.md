# Jet broadening in unstable non-Abelian plasmas

> We perform numerical simulations of the QCD Boltzmann-Vlasov equation including both hard elastic particle collisions and soft interactions mediated by classical Yang-Mills fields. We provide an estimate of the coupling of jets to a hot plasma which is independent of infrared cutoffs. For weakly-coupled anisotropic plasmas the local rotational symmetry in momentum space is broken. The fields develop unstable modes, forming configurations where B_t>E_t and E_z>B_z. This provides a possible explanation for the experimental observation that high-energy jets traversing the plasma perpendicular to the beam axis experience much stronger broadening in rapidity than in azimuth.

## Key claim
For weakly-coupled anisotropic plasmas, unstable non-Abelian modes produce field configurations with \(B_\perp > E_\perp\) and \(E_z > B_z\), leading to a factor of approximately 1.5 stronger longitudinal than transverse jet broadening, which may partly explain the experimentally observed "ridge" asymmetry in di-hadron correlations.

## Method
The authors numerically solve the SU(2) Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for hard gluons, coupling classical Yang-Mills fields (soft interactions) with hard binary \(gg \rightarrow gg\) collisions via a stochastic method. A separation scale \(k^*\) is introduced to avoid double-counting between soft field modes and hard collisions, and the test particle method is used to evolve Wong's equations for the particle dynamics on a periodic lattice. Jet broadening is measured by tracking the momentum-space variances \(\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle\) and \(\langle p_z^2 \rangle\) of high-energy test particles traversing both isotropic and anisotropic plasmas.

## Key numbers
- For an isotropic plasma with \(n_g = 10/\text{fm}^3\), \(p/(3T) \approx 5\), and \(N_c = 2\), the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q} \simeq 2.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, independent of the separation scale \(k^*\).
- For an anisotropic plasma with \(p_{\text{h}} = 16\) GeV, \(L = 5\) fm, \(n_g = 10/\text{fm}^3\), and \(g = 2\), the ratio \(\hat{q}_L / \hat{q}_\perp \approx 2.3\) during the period of instability.
- Reducing \(p_{\text{h}}\) to 8 GeV gives \(\hat{q}_L / \hat{q}_\perp \approx 2.1\), though with significant lattice artifacts.
- Binary collisions reduce the growth rate of unstable field modes by approximately 5% for \(k^* \approx 1.7 p_{\text{h}}\), increasing to about 15% for \(k^* \approx 0.9 p_{\text{h}}\).

## Context
This work extends earlier simulations of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in anisotropic SU(2) plasmas (Dumitru 2005, Dumitru 2006) by including hard elastic collisions to obtain a cutoff-independent jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\). It contrasts with the Abelian case, where transverse magnetic fields dominate (Romatschke 2006, Majumder 2006), and shows that non-Abelian dynamics produce a more complex field configuration that still yields asymmetric broadening. The results enable future real-time simulations of jet energy loss and provide a possible microscopic explanation for the "ridge" phenomenon observed in di-hadron correlations at RHIC (Jacobs 2005, Putschke 2007).
