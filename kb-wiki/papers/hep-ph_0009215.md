# Enhanced photon production from quark-gluon plasma: Finite-lifetime effect

> Photon production from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma of finite lifetime is studied directly in real time with a nonequilibrium formulation that includes off-shell (energy nonconserving) effects. To lowest order we find that production of direct photons form a quark-gluon plasma of temperature T ~ 200 MeV and lifetime t ~ 10-20 fm/c is strongly enhanced by off-shell (anti)quark bremsstrahlung q(\bar{q})->q(\bar{q})γ. The yield from this nonequilibrium finite-lifetime effect dominates over those obtained from higher order equilibrium rate calculations in the range of energy E > 2 GeV and falls off with a power law for E >> T.

# Enhanced photon production from quark-gluon plasma: Finite-lifetime effect

## Key claim
Photon production from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of finite lifetime (10–20 fm/c at T ~ 200 MeV) is strongly enhanced by off-shell (anti)quark bremsstrahlung, dominating over higher-order equilibrium rate calculations for photon energies E > 2 GeV. This enhancement arises specifically because the finite lifetime prevents the assumption of completed collisions that underlies standard energy-conserving rates.

## Method
The authors used a real-time kinetic approach based on nonequilibrium quantum field theory to compute the time-dependent photon production rate and yield from a thermalized QGP of finite lifetime. They evaluated the lowest-order (order α) contribution to the photon yield, which vanishes identically under the infinite-lifetime assumption due to kinematic constraints, and compared it numerically to the one-loop and two-loop equilibrium rates from Kapusta et al. (1991) and Aurenche et al. (1998). The numerical analysis considered a QGP of temperature T = 200 MeV and lifetime t = 10–20 fm/c, with α_s ≈ 0.3 obtained from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers
- Temperature T ~ 200 MeV and lifetime t ~ 10–20 fm/c, corresponding to estimates for RHIC energies.
- For E >> T, the nonequilibrium yield falls off with a power law E^{-ν} with ν ≈ 2.14.
- The nonequilibrium contribution dominates over equilibrium one-loop and two-loop yields for photon energies E > 2 GeV, and is larger by several orders of magnitude for E > 3 GeV.
- For E << T, the finite-lifetime yield scales as (5/9)(α/2π²)(T³/E²)[ln(2Et) + γ – 1], where γ = 0.577 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
- The equilibrium contributions would require a QGP lifetime of order 10²–10³ fm/c or larger to dominate the yield for E >> T.

## Context
This work extends the study of direct photon production from a QGP by incorporating finite-lifetime effects that were neglected in previous equilibrium calculations by Kapusta et al. (1991), Baier et al. (1992), and Aurenche et al. (1998), which assumed an infinite QGP lifetime and energy conservation. The finite-lifetime effect produces a power-law spectrum for high-energy photons rather than the Boltzmann-suppressed exponential falloff of equilibrium rates. The next step identified by the authors is to include hydrodynamical expansion and photon production from the hadronic gas phase to connect with experimental observations at RHIC.
