# Dissipative Dynamics of Highly Anisotropic Systems

> In this paper we present a method to improve the description of 0+1 dimensional boost invariant dissipative dynamics in the presence of large momentum-space anisotropies. We do this by reorganizing the canonical hydrodynamic expansion of the distribution function around a momentum-space anisotropic ansatz rather than an isotropic equilibrium one. At leading order the result obtained is two coupled ordinary differential equations for the momentum-space anisotropy and typical momentum of the degrees of freedom. We show that this framework can reproduce both the ideal hydrodynamic and free streaming limits. Additionally, we demonstrate that when linearized the differential equations reduce to 2nd order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics. Finally, we make quantitative comparisons of the evolution of the pressure anisotropy within our approach and 2nd order viscous hydrodynamics in both the strong and weak coupling limits.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to improve the description of 0+1 dimensional boost-invariant dissipative dynamics in systems with large momentum-space anisotropies by reorganizing the hydrodynamic expansion around an anisotropic ansatz rather than an isotropic equilibrium one, claiming that at leading order this framework can reproduce both ideal hydrodynamic and free streaming limits while reducing to second-order Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics when linearized.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation and take the zeroth and first moments using a Romatschke-Strickland (RS) anisotropic distribution function ansatz parameterized by an anisotropy parameter ξ and a hard momentum scale p_hard. This yields two coupled ordinary differential equations for ξ(τ) and p_hard(τ) that describe the time evolution of the system under 0+1 dimensional boost-invariant expansion. The equations are solved numerically for various values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/S, and the results are compared to second-order viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions used in all numerical solutions: τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, p_hard(τ=τ₀) = 350 MeV, ξ(τ=τ₀) = 0
- For the free streaming limit (Γ=0), the solution gives ξ(τ) = (1+ξ₀)(τ/τ₀)² - 1 and p_hard = constant
- For the ideal hydrodynamic limit (ξ=0), the solution gives p_hard(τ) = p_hard(τ₀)(τ₀/τ)^(1/3)
- The relation between the relaxation rate Γ and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is Γ = 2T(τ)/(5η/S) = 2R^(1/4)(ξ)p_hard/(5η/S)
- In the small ξ expansion, the shear tensor Π is related to ξ by Π/E_eq = (8/45)ξ + O(ξ²)
- The Navier-Stokes value of the anisotropy parameter is ξ_NS = (10/(Tτ))(η/S) + O(Π²_NS)

## Context

This work extends the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution function approach (Romatschke and Strickland, 2003) to derive dynamical evolution equations, building on earlier studies of anisotropic plasmas in heavy ion collisions by Baym (1984) and Martinez (2008, 2009). The method provides a unified framework that bridges early-time free streaming behavior and late-time viscous hydrodynamical evolution, addressing the problem that second-order viscous hydrodynamics can predict negative longitudinal pressures at early times as shown by Martinez (2009). This enables calculations of time-evolving distribution functions that can be used as input for other observables in heavy ion collision phenomenology.
