# Saturation of Nuclear Binding from Lattice Hamiltonians

> There is a conundrum regarding the binding of $α$ particles in nuclei. On one hand, auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations of Hamiltonians on discrete spatial lattices proposed that attractive two-nucleon potentials, alone or together with attractive three-nucleon potentials, yield accurate nuclear binding. On the other hand, such Hamiltonians typically overbind all but the lightest nuclei in continuum-space approaches. We address this puzzle by performing Hartree-Fock computations of the light nuclei $^4$He, $^8$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O, and of nuclear and neutron matter using established lattice Hamiltonians. These variational upper bounds for the ground-state energies show that the Hamiltonians with only two-nucleon potentials do not yield accurate binding, in contrast to the results from auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations. The case is different for Hamiltonians with three-nucleon potentials although it is the dense packing on the lattice -- and not repulsive potentials -- that yield a constant binding energy per nucleon.

## Key claim
The authors show that previously published lattice Hamiltonians with only attractive two-nucleon potentials do not produce accurate nuclear binding, contrary to claims from auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations. For Hamiltonians with three-nucleon potentials, the apparent saturation is actually a lattice artifact arising from dense packing on the discrete lattice rather than from repulsive interactions.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock computations for the light nuclei 4He, 8Be, 12C, and 16O, and for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, using three established lattice Hamiltonians from chiral effective field theory at leading order and from pion-less effective field theory. They computed variational upper bounds for ground-state energies on discrete spatial lattices with spacings of 1.97 fm and 1.32 fm, and compared their results to auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations from the original publications.

## Key numbers
- For Hamiltonian H (Ref. Elhatisari 2017): Hartree-Fock binding energies per particle for 8Be, 12C, and 16O are 7.9, 11.4, and 13.3 MeV respectively, far above the natural 8 MeV per nucleon
- For Hamiltonian H (Ref. Elhatisari 2017): Hartree-Fock total energies are -19.81 MeV (4He), -63.17 MeV (8Be), -137.28 MeV (12C), -213.37 MeV (16O), compared to auxiliary-field Monte Carlo results of -25.4, -51.9, -83.8, and -128.2 MeV respectively
- For Hamiltonian HB (Ref. Elhatisari 2016): Hartree-Fock total energies are -32.19 MeV (4He), -76.37 MeV (8Be), -139.83 MeV (12C), -222.06 MeV (16O), compared to auxiliary-field Monte Carlo results of -29.2, -59.7, -95.0, and -135.4 MeV respectively
- For the Hamiltonian of Lu (2019): neutron matter Hartree-Fock energy per neutron at density 0.13 fm^-3 is 13.6 MeV (L=5, N=38) and 14.0 MeV (L=6, N=66), compared to auxiliary-field Monte Carlo results of 13.4 and 13.8 MeV respectively
- The lattice spacing for the chiral Hamiltonians is a=1.97 fm (momentum cutoff pi/a approximately 314 MeV); for the pion-less Hamiltonian it is a=1.32 fm (cutoff approximately 471 MeV)

## Context
This work resolves a conundrum between lattice and continuum approaches to nuclear binding. Previous auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations by Elhatisari (2016, 2017) and Lu (2019) claimed that simple attractive potentials could yield accurate nuclear binding, but continuum-space approaches (e.g., Maris 2021, Hebeler 2011) found that such Hamiltonians overbind nuclei or fail to saturate. The authors show that the earlier lattice simulations did not accurately solve their Hamiltonians, and that the saturation seen with three-nucleon potentials is a lattice artifact from dense packing. This work enables future studies to focus on proper ab initio methods for understanding alpha-particle clustering in nuclei, which remains an open challenge.
