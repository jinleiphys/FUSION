# Propagation of neutral mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate dispersion relations and propagators for the $σ$, $ω$, $ρ$ and $δ$ mesons in relativistic nuclear matter with a proton-neutron asymmetry. In addition to the $σ$-$ω$ and $δ$-$ρ$ mixings already present in symmetric matter, mixing occur between all components of the $σ$-$ω$-$δ$-$ρ$ system when the proton fraction differs from 1/2.

## Key claim

In asymmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction different from 1/2), mixing occurs between all components of the σ-ω-δ-ρ meson system, not only the σ-ω and δ-ρ mixings already present in symmetric matter; the full set of coupled dispersion relations must be solved to obtain the meson propagators.

## Method

The author performs a linear response analysis around an asymmetric Hartree ground state within a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model, where nucleon-nucleon interaction is mediated by σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons. The dispersion relations are derived by solving the linearized, Fourier-transformed field equations for the meson perturbations, and the propagators are obtained by inverting the resulting matrix of dispersion relations. The polarizations are computed at the one-loop level, with vacuum divergences handled by dimensional regularization and counterterm subtraction, using two renormalization schemes (scheme A of Kurasawa and Suzuki, and scheme B of Mornas et al. 1998).

## Key numbers

* Proton fraction in cold neutron stars is about 10% or lower; in supernovae and hot protoneutron stars, the proton fraction is about 30 to 40%.
* For a typical lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4, the neutrino chemical potential μ_ν is of order 200–250 MeV and Y_p ~ 0.3–0.36.
* The δ-NN coupling constant g_δ = 5 yields larger proton fractions at high density compared to g_δ = 0, for the same asymmetry energy at saturation.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of meson dispersion relations in symmetric nuclear matter by Diaz Alonso et al. (JDAetal) to systems with proton-neutron asymmetry. It builds on prior studies of ω-ρ mixing by Dumitru et al. (DMDRK97) and Bratkovskaya and Friman (BF98) for dilepton production, and on the δ-ρ mixing investigation by Tsushima et al. (TDMG00) in symmetric matter. The derived propagators are applied to neutrino-nucleon scattering rates in Mornas and Perez (MP01), finding that the reduction of opacities by RPA correlations is less efficient when all mixing channels are included.
