# Dynamics of in-medium quarkonia in SU(3) and SU(2) gauge theories

> Decoherence dynamics of quarkonia is studied in the high-temperature deconfined phase of SU($N_c$) gauge theories. In particular, we analyze the symmetry properties of SU($N_c$) stochastic potential model and find a novel "event-by-event" symmetry for $N_c=2$ case, similar to the $G$-parity of hadronic systems. This novel symmetry constrains the relation between diagonal and off-diagonal components of quarkonium density matrix, leaving the latter to be finite at late times. We also present one-dimensional numerical simulation of the model, which indicates the usefulness of the complex potential simulations for the quarkonium survival probabilities in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided that the effect of dissipation can be neglected.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a novel "event-by-event" symmetry for SU(2) gauge theories, analogous to G-parity in hadronic systems, which constrains the relation between diagonal and off-diagonal components of the quarkonium density matrix and leaves the latter finite at late times; the authors also find that the complex potential model provides a useful approximation for quarkonium survival probabilities in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided dissipative effects can be neglected.

## Method

The authors formulate an SU(N_c) stochastic potential model based on a stochastic Schrödinger equation with color-dependent noise fields, where the quarkonium Hamiltonian includes a Debye-screened potential and a noise term with finite correlation length. They analyze the symmetry properties of the model under global SU(N_c), parity, charge conjugation, and a newly discovered "spinor conjugation" for N_c=2, and perform one-dimensional numerical simulations of the stochastic Schrödinger equation for bottomonium and charmonium in both static and Bjorken-expanding quark-gluon plasmas.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass M_b = 4.8 GeV; charm quark mass M_c = 1.18 GeV
- Static QGP temperature T = 0.4 GeV; Bjorken expansion initial temperature T_0 = 0.4 GeV with initial time t_0 = 1 fm
- Effective coupling α_eff = 0.3; Debye mass m_D = T; noise amplitude γ = 0.3T; noise correlation length l_corr varied from 0.04 fm to 0.96 fm
- Simulation grid: bottomonium system of -2.56 fm ≤ x ≤ 2.56 fm with 512 cells (Δx = 0.01 fm); charmonium system of -5.12 fm ≤ x ≤ 5.12 fm with 1024 cells
- Time evolution from t=0 to t=9 fm with 90000 steps of Δt = 0.0001 fm; 1000 stochastic wave function realizations averaged
- Ground state wave function extent r_bb ≈ 0.20 fm for bottomonium at T=0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the stochastic potential model previously formulated by Akamatsu (2014) and numerically simulated for U(1) gauge theory by Kajimoto (2017) to SU(N_c) gauge theories with color internal degrees of freedom. The results support the complex potential model used by Strickland (2011) and Krouppa (2015-2017) as an effective phenomenological tool when dissipation is negligible, and the symmetry analysis for N_c=2 reveals constraints beyond those found in the SU(3) case. The findings enable more accurate modeling of quarkonium suppression patterns observed at RHIC and LHC, particularly for the bottomonium family Upsilon(nS) measured by the STAR, PHENIX, CMS, and ALICE collaborations.
