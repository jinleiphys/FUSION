# Chiral symmetry restoration by parity doubling and the structure of neutron stars

> Recent lattice QCD studies at vanishing density exhibit the parity-doubling structure for the low-lying baryons around the chiral crossover temperature. This finding is likely an imprint of the chiral symmetry restoration in the baryonic sector of QCD, and is expected to occur also in cold dense matter, which makes it of major relevance for compact stars. By contrast, typical effective models for compact star matter embody chiral physics solely in the deconfined sector, with quarks as degrees of freedom. In this contribution, we present a description of QCD matter based on the effective hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model. Its characteristic feature is that, under neutron-star conditions, the chiral symmetry is restored in a first-order phase transition deep in the hadronic phase, before the deconfinement of quarks takes place. We discuss the implications of the parity doubling of baryons on the mass-radius relation for compact stars obtained in accordance with the modern constraints on the mass from PSR J0348+0432, the compactness from GW170817, as well as the direct URCA process threshold. We show that the existence of high-mass stars might not necessarily signal the deconfinement of quarks.

# Chiral symmetry restoration by parity doubling and the structure of neutron stars

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a strong first-order phase transition in the high-mass part of the mass-radius sequence for neutron stars, which could produce a flattening or even a mass-twin phenomenon near 2 solar masses, does not necessarily signal quark deconfinement; instead, it can arise from chiral symmetry restoration within the hadronic phase via parity doubling, before any deconfinement transition occurs.

## Method
The authors employ the hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model in the mean-field approximation for two-flavor isospin-asymmetric matter under neutron-star conditions of beta equilibrium and charge neutrality. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for spherically symmetric compact stars using the resulting equations of state, varying the model parameter alpha b0 (350, 370, 400, and 450 MeV) while fixing the chirally invariant mass m0 = 790 MeV, and compare the resulting mass-radius sequences against observational constraints from PSR J0348+0432, GW170817, and the direct URCA process threshold.

## Key numbers
- The chiral phase transition is triggered at net-baryon densities of roughly 1.82 rho0 (alpha b0 = 350 MeV), 2.14 rho0 (370 MeV), 2.68 rho0 (400 MeV), and as a crossover at 3.81 rho0 (450 MeV), where rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is the nuclear saturation density.
- The mixed phase of chirally broken and restored phases persists up to 2.46 rho0 (350 MeV), 2.73 rho0 (370 MeV), and 3.11 rho0 (400 MeV).
- The maximum mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 is 2.01(4) solar masses; the allowed range for the maximum mass of nonrotating cold neutron stars is taken as 2.01 < M/M_sun < 2.16.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 requires Lambda(1.4 M_sun) < 800; this is met only for alpha b0 = 400 and 450 MeV.
- The allowed range for the chirally invariant mass m0 is roughly 780-810 MeV from the combined constraints.
- The critical proton fraction for the direct URCA process in the chirally broken phase is 14.8% (with muons) to 11.1% (muon-free); in the fully chirally restored phase it is 11.1% to 8.0%.
- The liquid-gas phase transition critical point occurs at T = 25 MeV; the chiral transition critical points occur at T = 13, 8, and 4 MeV for alpha b0 = 350, 370, and 400 MeV, respectively, while for 450 MeV the chiral transition is a crossover at all temperatures.

## Context
This work extends the hybrid QMN model developed by Marczenko et al. (2018) and contrasts with typical two-phase approaches (e.g., Alvarez-Castillo et al., 2016) that assume simultaneous chiral and deconfinement transitions signaled by a flattening of the mass-radius relation. The paper shows that parity doubling, as observed in lattice QCD studies at vanishing density around the chiral crossover temperature, can produce similar mass-radius features entirely within the hadronic phase. The results enable future studies incorporating hyperon degrees of freedom and repulsive interactions in the quark sector, which could establish stable hybrid star solutions and the possibility of high-mass twin stars.
