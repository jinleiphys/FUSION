# Second-order hyperfine correction to H, D, and $^3$He energy levels

> The complete second-order hyperfine-interaction correction is calculated for centroid energy levels of H, D, and $^3$He atoms. For $^3$He, the corrections of $-2.075$~kHz and $-0.305$~kHz beyond the leading hyperfine-mixing contribution are obtained for the $2^1S$ and $2^3S$ states, respectively. These results shift the nuclear charge radii difference derived from the $^3$He -$^4$He isotope shift and largely resolve the previously reported disagreement between the muonic and electronic helium determinations [van der Werf et al., arXiv:2306.02333 (2023); Schuhmann et al., arXiv:2305.11679 (2023)].

# Second-order hyperfine correction to H, D, and $^3$He energy levels

## Key claim
The complete second-order hyperfine-interaction correction to centroid energy levels of H, D, and $^3$He is calculated, and for $^3$He the results shift the nuclear charge radii difference derived from the $^3$He–$^4$He isotope shift, bringing the previously discrepant muonic and electronic helium determinations into $1.3\,\sigma$ agreement, but only when combined with an improved value of the field shift contribution.

## Method
The authors use nonrelativistic quantum electrodynamics (NRQED) with dimension regularization to derive the second-order hyperfine correction, separating it into three terms: a second-order perturbation term $E_\mathrm{sec}$, a diamagnetic term $E_\mathrm{diam}$, and a high-energy term $E_\mathrm{high}$. For hydrogenic systems, numerical calculations using B-spline pseudostates are performed and fitted to extract leading logarithmic coefficients, which are then compared to analytical results. For helium, the fully correlated exponential basis set method is used to evaluate first- and second-order matrix elements for the $2^1S$ and $2^3S$ states.

## Key numbers
- For $^3$He $2^1S$ state: correction of $-2.075$ kHz beyond the leading hyperfine-mixing contribution
- For $^3$He $2^3S$ state: correction of $-0.305$ kHz beyond the leading hyperfine-mixing contribution
- For the $1S$–$2S$ transition in hydrogen: $0.86$ kHz second-order HFS correction
- For the $1S$–$2S$ transition in deuterium: $0.05$ kHz second-order HFS correction
- For the $1S$–$2S$ transition in He$^+$: $6.84$ kHz second-order HFS correction
- For the H–D isotope shift of the $1S$–$2S$ transition: $0.81$ kHz contribution
- Leading logarithmic coefficient for hydrogenic $1S$ state: $a = 5.00\,(1)$
- Nonlogarithmic coefficient $B$ for hydrogenic $1S$ state: $B = 2.700\,(2)$
- Correction $\delta(2^1S)$ for hyperfine mixing with $n\neq 2$ states in $^3$He: $-0.034\,25$

## Context
This work extends the treatment of second-order hyperfine corrections beyond hydrogen and muonic hydrogen (Franziska et al., 2013; Franziska et al., 2014) to deuterium and $^3$He, and provides the complete calculation for few-electron atoms. The results address the discrepancy in nuclear charge radii difference between $^3$He and $^4$He reported by van der Werf et al. (2023) and Schuhmann et al. (2023) from muonic versus electronic measurements. The correction of 1.77 kHz found here is larger than the 1.37 kHz correction from mixing with $n>2$ excited states recently reported by Qi et al. (2024), and when combined with an improved field shift contribution, brings the two determinations into $1.3\,\sigma$ agreement. The authors note that for the H–D isotope shift, the inelastic three-photon exchange contribution must also be considered before updating the charge radius difference.
