# In medium eta' mass and eta' N interaction based on chiral effective theory

> The in-medium eta' mass and the eta'N interaction are investigated in an effective theory based on the linear realization of the SU(3) chiral symmetry. We find that a large part of the eta' mass is generated by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry through the U_A (1) anomaly. As a consequence of this observation, the eta' mass is reduced in nuclear matter where chiral symmetry is partially restored. In our model, the mass reduction is found to be 80MeV at the saturation density. Estimating the eta' N interaction based on the same effective theory, we find that the eta' N interaction in the scalar channel is attractive sufficiently to form a bound state in the eta' N system with a several MeV binding energy. We discuss the origin of attraction by emphasizing the special role of the sigma meson in the linear sigma model for the mass generation of eta' and N.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a large part of the eta' mass originates from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking via the U_A(1) anomaly, and therefore the eta' mass should decrease in nuclear matter where chiral symmetry is partially restored; they find a mass reduction of 80 MeV at saturation density, but note this is a lower bound because they do not include the density dependence of the U_A(1) anomaly parameter B.

## Method

The authors use a linear sigma model with SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R chiral symmetry, including a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft term for the U_A(1) anomaly. They introduce nucleon fields in the (3, anti-3) ⊕ (anti-3, 3) representation and compute the effective potential with nucleon one-loop diagrams including Pauli blocking. The in-medium sigma condensates are found by minimizing the effective potential, and the in-medium eta' mass is obtained from the tree-level mass expression evaluated with these condensates plus a particle-hole excitation term.

## Key numbers

* Eta' mass reduction of 80 MeV at normal nuclear density (saturation density) in the linear sigma model calculation.
* Using a simple linear relation between the eta'-eta mass difference and the chiral condensate, the mass reduction is estimated to be 80 to 100 MeV at normal nuclear density.
* The eta' N scattering length extracted from pp → pp eta' experiments is cited as about 0.8 fm (Moskal 2000a) or 0.1 fm with sign undetermined (Moskal 2000b).
* The quark condensate reduction at normal nuclear density is taken as 35%, based on analysis of pionic atoms by Suzuki (2004).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of in-medium eta' properties by Jido (2012) and others, using a linear sigma model approach that directly connects the eta' mass to the chiral condensate. It contrasts with approaches based on effective U_A(1) restoration from instanton density reduction (Gross 1981, Kapusta 1996) and with non-linear sigma models that cannot directly relate the eta' mass to the changing vacuum. The results enable predictions for eta' N bound states analogous to Lambda(1405) from kaon-nucleon interactions, and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting experimental data from pp → pp eta' (Moskal 2000a, 2000b) and gamma p → eta' p in nuclei (Nanova 2012).
