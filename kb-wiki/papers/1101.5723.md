# Study of the spectral properties of spin ladders in different representations via a renormalization procedure

> We implement an algorithm which is aimed to reduce the dimensions of the Hilbert space of quantum many-body systems by means of a renormalization procedure. We test the role and importance of different representations on the reduction process by working out and analyzing the spectral properties of strongly interacting frustrated quantum spin systems.

# Study of the spectral properties of spin ladders in different representations via a renormalization procedure

## Key claim
The authors implement a Hilbert-space renormalization algorithm that reduces the dimension of the Hilbert space while renormalizing a single coupling strength, and test its efficiency on frustrated two-leg spin-1/2 ladders. The efficiency of the reduction depends strongly on the choice of representation (SU(2) versus SO(4)) and on the relative strength of the rung coupling J_t compared to the leg and diagonal couplings J_l and J_c, with the SO(4) representation favored when J_t is large and the SU(2) representation favored when J_t is small.

## Method
The algorithm starts from an N-dimensional Hilbert space, computes the ground state energy and eigenvector via the Lanczos technique, then eliminates one basis state (the one with largest diagonal energy or smallest ground-state amplitude) and solves a quadratic equation to obtain a renormalized coupling strength g^(N-1) that preserves the ground state energy. This step-by-step reduction is iterated from dimension N down to a minimum dimension N_min where the spectrum becomes unstable. The method is applied to frustrated two-leg spin-1/2 ladders described by a Hamiltonian with rung coupling J_t, leg coupling J_l, and diagonal coupling J_c, using basis states in both the standard SU(2) M-scheme (with total magnetization M_tot = 0) and the SO(4) representation (where rung spins are coupled to S_i = 0 or 1).

## Key numbers
- For a ladder with L=6 sites per leg, J_t=15, J_l=5, J_c=3 in the SU(2) representation: the ground state remains stable down to a reduced space dimension n ~ 50, starting from an initial dimension N=924.
- For the same system with J_t=5.5, J_l=5, J_c=3 in the SU(2) representation: the ground state remains stable down to n ~ 100, and the coupling constant J_t starts to increase sharply between n=300 and n=200.
- The percentage deviation p(i) = |(e_i^(N) - e_i^(n))/e_i^(N)| x 100 is used to quantify energy accuracy for the ground state (i=1) and first three excited states (i=2,3,4), where e_i^(n) = lambda_i^(n)/(2L) is the energy per site.
- For J_t=15, J_l=5, J_c=3 in the SO(4) representation: the ground state is very stable, and the renormalization of J_t starts to work for n ~ 50.
- For J_t=5.5, J_l=5, J_c=3 in the SO(4) representation: the ground state energy experiences much stronger bunches of fluctuations than in the SU(2) representation.

## Context
This work extends a renormalization approach previously introduced by the same authors (Khalil and Richert, 2010) and builds on the Feshbach projection formalism. It tests the algorithm on frustrated quantum spin ladders, a system studied extensively with methods such as the Real Space Renormalization Group (RSRG) by Malozemoff and White, and the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) by White and by Henk. The SO(4) representation used here follows earlier work by Bohm and by Kikuchi and collaborators on dimerized spin systems. The results show that the choice of representation can significantly affect the efficiency of Hilbert-space reduction, and the authors note that the optimal basis is one whose symmetry properties are closest to those of the physical system. This work enables future application to larger ladders and higher-dimensional systems, as well as extension to finite-temperature systems and multi-coupling renormalization.
