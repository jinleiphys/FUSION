# Dynamics of a trapped Fermi gas in the BCS phase

> We derive semiclassical transport equations for a trapped atomic Fermi gas in the BCS phase at temperatures between zero and the superfluid transition temperature. These equations interpolate between the two well-known limiting cases of superfluid hydrodynamics at zero temperature and the Vlasov equation at the critical one. The linearized version of these equations, valid for small deviations from equilibrium, is worked out and applied to two simple examples where analytical solutions can be found: a sound wave in a uniform medium and the quadrupole excitation in a spherical harmonic trap. In spite of some simplifying approximations, the main qualitative results of quantum mechanical calculations are reproduced, which are the different frequencies of the quadrupole mode at zero and the critical temperature and strong Landau damping at intermediate temperatures. In addition we suggest a numerical method for solving the semiclassical equations without further approximations.

## Key claim

The authors derive semiclassical transport equations for a trapped atomic Fermi gas in the BCS phase that interpolate between superfluid hydrodynamics at zero temperature and the Vlasov equation at the critical temperature, but the derivation relies on a semiclassical expansion to first order in \(\hbar\) and assumes the system is in the collisionless regime where the collision rate is much smaller than the trap frequency.

## Method

Starting from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations, the authors perform a gauge transformation to separate superfluid collective motion from quasiparticle dynamics, then apply a Wigner transform and expand to first order in \(\hbar\) to obtain a quasiparticle transport equation resembling the Vlasov equation. The linearized version of this equation, valid for small deviations from equilibrium, is derived and coupled to the continuity equation, which determines the superfluid phase. The resulting system is solved analytically for two cases: a sound wave in a uniform medium and the quadrupole excitation in a spherical harmonic trap, using additional simplifying approximations.

## Key numbers

* The local critical temperature is given by \(T_c(\rv) = 0.57 \Delta_0(\rv;T=0)/k_B\), where \(\Delta_0\) is the zero-temperature pairing gap.
* The coefficient \(A(\rv)\) in the linearized equations is \(A(\rv) = \frac{mp_F(\rv)}{2\pi^2\hbar^3}[1-\varphi(\rv)]\), where \(\varphi = 0\) at \(T=0\) and \(\varphi = 1\) when \(\Delta_0 = 0\) (i.e., at \(T \geq T_c\)).
* The coefficient \(B(\rv)\) is suppressed by at least one power of \(\Delta_0/\epsilon_F\) or \(T/\epsilon_F\), with explicit form \(B(\rv) = \frac{\Delta_0(\rv)}{2\epsilon_F(\rv)}\Big( \frac{mp_F(\rv)}{2\pi^2\hbar^3}[2+\varphi(\rv)]-\frac{1}{g}\Big)\).
* Current experiments reach temperatures of order \(T \approx 0.03 T_F\), where \(T_F = \epsilon_F/k_B\) is the degeneracy temperature.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical two-fluid model developed for superconductivity by Betbeder-Matibet and Nozieres (1969) and for liquid \(^3\)He by Serene and Rainer (1983) to trapped atomic Fermi gases in the BCS phase. It contrasts with fully quantum-mechanical QRPA calculations (e.g., Grasso and Khan, 2004; Bruun and Mottelson, 2001; Ohashi and Griffin, 2003), which are limited to systems of \(\sim 10^4\) atoms and spherical traps, whereas the semiclassical approach enables calculations for larger atom numbers and realistic trap geometries. The results enable future studies of collective modes in the collisionless regime at intermediate temperatures where Landau damping is strong, and the authors suggest a numerical method for solving the equations without further approximations.
