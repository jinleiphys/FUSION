# Coherent J/$ψ$ photoproduction in hadronic heavy-ion collisions

> A significant excess of J/$ψ$ yield at very low transverse momentum ($p_T < 0.3$ GeV/c) was observed by the ALICE and STAR collaborations in peripheral A+A collisions, which points to evidence of coherent photoproduction of J/$ψ$ in violent hadronic interactions. Theoretically, the photoproduction of J$/ψ$ in hadronic collisions raises questions about how spectator and non-spectator nucleons participate in the coherent reaction. For the first time, we argue that the strong interactions in the overlapping region of incoming nuclei may disturb the coherent production, leaving room for different coupling assumptions. Furthermore, first considerations of the destructive interference between photoproduction on ions moving in opposite directions in hadronic heavy-ion collisions are included. This letter presents calculations of J$/ψ$ production from coherent photon-nucleus ($γ+ A \rightarrow \text{J}/ψ+ A$) interactions in hadronic A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies with both nucleus and spectator coupling hypotheses. The coherent J/$ψ$ yield as a function of centrality and differential distributions as a function of transverse momentum, azimuthal angle and rapidity in different centrality bins are shown and found to be significantly different for different coupling scenarios, calling for future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in hadronic heavy-ion collisions, the strong interactions in the overlapping region of incoming nuclei may disturb coherent J/ψ photoproduction, leaving room for different coupling assumptions (whole nucleus vs. spectator nucleons) for both photon emission and Pomeron emission, and that destructive interference between photoproduction on ions moving in opposite directions must be included.

## Method

The cross-section for J/ψ production via photon-Pomeron fusion is calculated by convoluting the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon spectrum with the photonuclear interaction cross-section, using a quantum Glauber approach coupled with parameterized γp → J/ψ p data. Four coupling scenarios are considered: nucleus-nucleus (N+N), nucleus-spectator (N+S), spectator-nucleus (S+N), and spectator-spectator (S+S), with the collision geometry and spectator density simulated by the optical Glauber model. The destructive interference between amplitudes from ions moving in opposite directions (INT2N) is included via a cosine term depending on impact parameter and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ yield excess observed at very low transverse momentum pT < 0.3 GeV/c by ALICE and STAR in peripheral hadronic A+A collisions
- For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the four coupling scenarios predict similar yields at impact parameter b = 2RA but differ dramatically as b decreases
- The correction factor C = 0.3 from the generalized vector dominance model is adopted for J/ψ calculations
- For the "S+S" scenario in 0-10% central collisions, a second bump from INT2N becomes visible above 0.1 GeV/c
- The rapidity distributions for 0-10% central collisions with the "S+S" scenario are scaled by 10^2 for clarity in the figures

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent J/ψ photoproduction from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) into hadronic heavy-ion collisions (HHICs), where the ALICE and STAR collaborations observed excess J/ψ yields at low pT. The authors build on the approach of Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek (2015), who assumed whole-nucleus coupling and did not include destructive interference, and incorporate the INT2N effect previously studied by Klein and Nystrand for vector meson production in UPCs. The calculations call for future experimental measurements to distinguish between coupling scenarios and better understand coherent interactions in hadronic collisions.
