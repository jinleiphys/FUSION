# Ground-state properties and structure evolutions of odd-$A$ transuranium Bk isotopes from deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The studies of transuranium nuclei are of vital significance in exploring the existence of the ``island of superheavy nuclei". This work presents the systematic investigations for the ground-state properties and structure evolutions of odd-$A$ transuranium Bk isotopes taking the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum~(DRHBc) with PC-PK1 density functional, in comparison with those by spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov~(RCHB) theory. The DRHBc calculations offer improved descriptions of the binding energies, closely aligning with the experimental data. The incorporation of deformation effects in DRHBc results in enhanced nuclear binding energies and a notable reduction in $α$-decay energies. With the rotational corrections further incorporated, the theoretical deviation by DRHBc from the experimental data is further reduced. Based on the two-neutron gap $δ_{\rm 2n}$ and the neutron pairing energy $E_{\rm pair}^n$, prominent shell closures at $N=184$ and $258$, as well as potential sub-shell structures at $N=142, 150, 162, 178, 218$, and $230$ are exhibited. A quasi-periodic variation among prolate, oblate, and spherical shapes as well as prolate deformation predominance have been shown in the evolutions of the quadrupole deformation. Possible shape coexistence is predicted in $^{331}$Bk with the oblate and prolate minima in close energies, which is further supported by the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum~(TRHBc) calculations. The neutron, proton, and charge radii predicted by DRHBc reveal pronounced kink structures at $N=184$ and $258$ in their evolutions with neutron number and compared to those by RCHB, deformation effect significantly enhances the radii of open-shell nuclei.

# Ground-state properties and structure evolutions of odd-A transuranium Bk isotopes from deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with PC-PK1 density functional provides systematically improved descriptions of binding energies, alpha-decay energies, and shell structures for odd-A Bk isotopes compared to spherical calculations, but the predicted shell closures and sub-shells (N=142, 150, 162, 178, 218, 230) are specific to Bk isotopes and may differ from those in superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z=117-120, where N=172 is also predicted as a large neutron shell and the N=184 shell closure vanishes in Z=119,120.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in a Dirac-Woods-Saxon basis with axial deformation and spatial reflection symmetry, using the PC-PK1 density functional and a zero-range pairing force with strength V0=-325 MeV·fm³ and saturation density ρsat=0.152 fm⁻³. For odd-A nuclei, the equal filling approximation was adopted to treat blocking effects from the unpaired nucleon. Constrained calculations with deformation step Δβ₂=0.05 were performed to obtain potential energy curves, and triaxial calculations using the TRHBc theory were carried out for selected nuclei to examine gamma-softness and shape coexistence.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip-line nucleus for Bk isotopes is predicted at N=260, corresponding to ³⁵⁵Bk, based on both S₂n becoming negative and neutron Fermi energy exceeding 0 MeV.
- The proton drip-line nucleus is predicted as ²²⁷Bk (N=130) by DRHBc, while RCHB predicts ²²⁹Bk (N=132).
- With rotational corrections included, the root-mean-square error between DRHBc binding energies and experimental data is 1.23 MeV, with rotational corrections reducing the deviation by almost 2 MeV.
- For ²⁶⁷Bk, the prolate and oblate minima have an energy difference of 0.35 MeV with a barrier height of 3.1 MeV between them.
- For ³³¹Bk, the two energy minima have an energy difference of 0.57 MeV with a barrier of 6.8 MeV between them.
- The last Bk isotope predicted to have alpha decay by DRHBc is at N=198, which has 16 fewer neutrons than the RCHB prediction at N=214.
- S₂n drops sharply at N=184 and N=258, and remains about 3 MeV within the large mass range between these shell closures.

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of DRHBc theory, previously used for uranium isotopes and other heavy nuclei (Zhang 2024, Kim 2022, Guo 2023), to the odd-A transuranium Bk isotopic chain from proton drip line to neutron drip line. The results are compared with spherical RCHB calculations (Xia 2018) and experimental data (Wang 2021). The predicted shell closures at N=184 and N=258 and sub-shells at N=142,150,162,178,218,230 differ from earlier predictions for superheavy nuclei with Z=117-120 (Yao 2024), where N=172 is also a large neutron shell and N=184 vanishes. The shape coexistence candidates ²⁶⁷Bk and ³³¹Bk provide targets for future experimental studies of deformation effects in transuranium nuclei, and the improved binding energy predictions enable more reliable estimates of alpha-decay chains relevant to superheavy element synthesis.
