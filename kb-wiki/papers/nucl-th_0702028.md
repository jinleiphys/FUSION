# Kaon condensation in neutron star using relativistic mean field models

> We use the modified quark-meson coupling and the quantum hadrodynamics models to study the properties of neutron star. Coupling constants of both models are adjusted to reproduce the same saturation properties. The onset of kaon condensation in neutron star matter is studied in detail over a wide range of kaon optical potential values. Once the kaon condensation takes place, the population of kaons increases very rapidly, and kaons become the dominant component, possibly making the neutron star matter a kaonic matter if the kaon optical potential is large.

## Key claim

The onset density of kaon condensation in neutron star matter is model-dependent, with the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model predicting lower critical densities than the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model, and the effect becomes less pronounced for larger kaon optical potential depths.

## Method

The authors employed two relativistic mean field models (MQMC and QHD) with coupling constants adjusted to reproduce identical nuclear saturation properties (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, binding energy 16.0 MeV, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, compression modulus 285 MeV, nucleon effective mass 0.78 m_N). They solved self-consistently the equations of motion for exchange mesons, charge neutrality, baryon number conservation, and β-equilibrium conditions for baryons and kaons, then used the resulting equation of state in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density assumed: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Saturation binding energy per nucleon: 16.0 MeV
- Symmetry energy: 32.5 MeV
- Compression modulus: 285 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: 0.78 m_N
- For U_K⁻ = -120 MeV, critical density ρ_crit = 5.9 ρ₀ (MQMC) and 9.8 ρ₀ (QHD)
- For U_K⁻ = -140 MeV, ρ_crit = 3.8 ρ₀ (MQMC) and 4.3 ρ₀ (QHD)
- For U_K⁻ = -160 MeV, ρ_crit = 3.0 ρ₀ (MQMC) and 3.3 ρ₀ (QHD)
- Maximum mass with U_K⁻ = -160 MeV in QHD: 1.32 M_⊙
- Maximum mass with U_K⁻ = -160 MeV in MQMC: 1.45 M_⊙
- With repulsive Σ hyperon potential (+30 MeV) and U_K⁻ = -160 MeV in QHD: maximum mass 1.32 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends studies of exotic degrees of freedom in neutron stars, particularly kaon condensation, building on earlier calculations by Schaffner, Oset, and Cieply (various years) that suggested shallow kaon optical potentials (≈ -50 MeV), as well as work by Gal (1994), Kaiser, and Batty (various years) that proposed deeper potentials up to -200 MeV. The results raise the question whether very deep kaon optical potentials (U_K⁻ ≈ -160 MeV) are compatible with observed neutron star masses, especially recent measurements by Nice (2005) and Page (2006) reporting masses in the range 1.7-2.1 M_⊙, which would require stiffer equations of state at high densities.
