# Chiral Restoration in Hot and/or Dense Matter

> Chiral restoration phase transition in hot and/or dense hadronic matter is discussed in terms of the BR scaling based on chiral symmetry and scale anomaly of QCD. The precise connection between the scalar field that figures in the trace anomaly and the sigma field that figures in the linear $σ$ model is established. It is suggested that in hot and/or dense medium, the nonlinear $σ$ model linearizes with the help of a dilaton to a linear $σ$ model with medium-renormalized constants. The relevance of Georgi's vector symmetry and/or Weinberg's ``mended symmetry" in chiral restoration is pointed out. Some striking consequences for relativistic heavy-ion collisions and dense matter in compact stars following stellar collapse are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in hot and/or dense hadronic matter, the nonlinear sigma model linearizes with the help of a dilaton field into a linear sigma model with medium-renormalized constants, but this is presented as a suggestion rather than a proven result, and the authors acknowledge that the precise connection between the scalar field in the trace anomaly and the sigma field in the linear sigma model requires the specific field redefinition Σ = Uχ.

## Method

The authors establish the connection between the scalar field χ that figures in the trace anomaly of QCD and the sigma field of the linear sigma model by transforming the dilatation-invariant low-energy chiral Lagrangian of Beane and van Kolck to a linear basis via the field redefinition Σ = Uχ, where Σ ≡ σ + iτ·π. They then analyze how the BR scaling relations for hadron masses follow from the hidden gauge symmetry implementation of vector mesons and the KSRF relation m_V² = 2g²f_π², which they argue holds in medium. The density dependence of the sigma meson mass is investigated through correlated two-pion exchange diagrams involving ρ-meson exchange in the crossed channel.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant for chiral symmetry restoration is B_χSB ≈ (140 MeV)^4 ≈ 50 MeV/fm³
- The bag constant for gluon condensation is B_glue ≈ (250 MeV)^4 ≈ 500 MeV/fm³
- The quark condensate in vacuum is ⟨0|ūu|0⟩ = -(240 MeV)³
- The sigma mass extracted from B_χSB^(1/4) ≈ 140 MeV gives m_σ ≈ 918 MeV in the bare vacuum
- By density ρ ≈ ρ₀, the scalar resonance mass drops to m_σ ≈ 500 MeV
- The axial coupling constant g_A drops from 1.26 to approximately 1 as density increases from zero to nuclear matter density ρ₀
- The gluon condensate contributes to the ρ-meson mass at approximately the 10% level at zero density

## Context

This work extends the BR scaling proposal of Brown and Rho (1991) by clarifying the misinterpretation that the scaling was tied to the gluon condensate, and instead establishes the connection to the quark condensate through the Beane and van Kolck (1995) dilatation-invariant Lagrangian. The paper builds on the hidden gauge symmetry approach of Bando and collaborators, the vector symmetry of Georgi, and Weinberg's mended symmetry concept. It enables a framework for understanding chiral restoration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and dense matter in compact stars, while also connecting to the chiral Fermi liquid picture of Lynn and the Walecka mean-field theory.
