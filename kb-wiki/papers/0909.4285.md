# New simple explicit solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics and phase-space evolution

> New exact solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics are described, including the first family of exact rotating solutions. The method used to search for them is an investigation of the relativistic hydrodynamical equations and the collisionless Boltzmann equation. Possible connections to the evolution of hot and dense partonic matter in heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents new exact solutions of relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamics, including the first known family of exact rotating solutions, but these solutions are valid only for the specific equation of state \(\varepsilon = \kappa p\) with \(\kappa = D\) (where \(D\) is the spatial dimensionality), and the collisionless (Knudsen flow) property holds only for massless particles.

## Method

The author simultaneously solves the relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamical equations (Euler, energy conservation, and particle number conservation) and the collisionless Boltzmann equation for a phase-space distribution of the Maxwell-Boltzmann form. By expanding the Boltzmann equation order by order in momentum, the constraints reduce to a set of linear differential equations for the four-vector \(A^\mu = u^\mu / T\), which are solved via a Taylor expansion in space-time coordinates, yielding explicit parametric forms for the velocity and temperature fields.

## Key numbers

- For the Hwa-Bjorken (Hubble-like) solution: \(T = T_0 \tau_0 / \tau\), where \(\tau = \sqrt{t^2 - r^2}\) is the proper time and \(T_0\), \(\tau_0\) are initial scale constants.
- For the accelerating finite solution: \(T = T_0 \tau_0^2 / \sqrt{ (\tau^2 + \rho)^2 + 4\rho \tau^2 \sinh^2 \eta - 4(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{r})t - a^2 }\), with \(\rho \ge 0\) a constant and \(\mathbf{a}\) a constant three-vector.
- For the rotating accelerating solution: \(T = T_0 \tau_0^2 / \sqrt{ (\tau^2 + \rho)^2 + 4\rho r^2 - (\mathbf{B}\times\mathbf{r} + \mathbf{a})^2 - 4t(\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{r}) }\), with \(\mathbf{B}\) a constant three-vector.
- For the rotating Hubble-like flow: \(T = T_0 \tau_0 / \sqrt{ \tau^2 + (\mathbf{B}\cdot\mathbf{r})^2 - B^2 r^2 - E^2 t^2 + (\mathbf{E}\cdot\mathbf{r})^2 }\), with constant vectors \(\mathbf{E}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\).

## Context

This work extends the known non-relativistic collisionless hydrodynamical solution of Csizmadia and Csorgo (1998) to the relativistic regime, and generalizes prior relativistic exact solutions such as the Hwa-Bjorken solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) and the accelerating solutions of Nagy and Csorgo (2007) and Csorgo (2006). The new rotating solutions enable the study of fireballs with nonzero angular momentum in heavy-ion collisions, and the finite-\(\eta\) accelerating solutions improve upon earlier work by providing a pressure profile that drops to zero at large space-time rapidity.
