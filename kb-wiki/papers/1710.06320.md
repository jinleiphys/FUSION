# Quark-antiquark antenna splitting in a medium

> We study a system in which a quark-antiquark antenna emits a hard gluon in a medium, and an extra very soft emission afterwards. Considering the coherence effects in terms of the survival probability, we can describe the interaction of the $q\bar{q}g$ configuration with the medium. We extrapolate previous analyzes of the antenna radiation to the case of two hard splittings inside the medium, and prove that this generalization keeps back the picture of jet quenching with effective emitters in the parton QCD cascade.

# Quark-antiquark antenna splitting in a medium

## Key claim
The authors show that the color coherence picture of jet quenching with effective emitters, previously established for a single in-medium splitting of a quark-antiquark antenna, can be generalized to the case of two hard splittings inside the medium, provided the extra gluon emission is very soft (ω → 0). The generalization holds because the survival probability for the soft gluon corresponds to that of the dipole from which it is emitted, even when multiple emitters are present.

## Method
The authors compute the medium-induced radiation spectrum for a quark-antiquark antenna that emits a hard gluon inside the medium, followed by a very soft gluon emission outside the medium. They evaluate the direct and interference terms of the squared matrix element for the three-emitter (q, q̄, g) configuration, expressing the interference contributions in terms of survival probabilities S(t,L) that describe the interaction of color dipoles with the medium via Wilson lines. The analysis is performed in the large-Nc limit and for a static medium of size L with transport coefficient q̂.

## Key numbers
- The decoherence parameter for a static medium is Δ_med ≃ 1 − exp[−(1/4) q̂ L (x_⊥ − x̄_⊥)²], where q̂ is the transport coefficient and L is the medium size.
- The direct term for the gluon emitter is proportional to Nc CF², while the direct terms for the quark and antiquark are each proportional to CF².
- The interference term M₁ ⊗ M₃* is proportional to S(t,L), and the interference term M₂ ⊗ M₃* is proportional to S(0,t) S(t,L).

## Context
This work extends the antenna radiation framework of Mehtar-Tani et al. (2011) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2012), which treated only a single in-medium splitting, to the case of two hard splittings inside the medium. The results maintain the picture of jet quenching with effective emitters in the parton QCD cascade, where each smaller antenna in the shower behaves according to the original antenna result. This enables progress toward a complete description of a full QCD shower in medium, addressing open questions about whether partons lose energy coherently and whether energy loss is predominantly caused by medium-induced radiation.
