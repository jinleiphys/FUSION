# Integrating out the Dirac sea in the Walecka model

> We derive a purely fermionic no-sea effective theory, featuring positive-energy states only for the Walecka model. In contrast to the so-called mean-field theory approach with the no-sea approximation, where the Dirac sea is simply omitted from the outset, we turn to the relativistic Hartree approximation and explicitly construct a no-sea effective theory from the underlying quantum field theory. Several results obtained within these two approaches are confronted with each other. This sheds new light on the reliability of the mean-field theory with the no-sea approximation as well as the role of the Dirac sea. Restricting to 1+1 dimensions, we obtain new analytical insights into nonuniform nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives a purely fermionic no-sea effective theory (NSET) for the Walecka model by explicitly integrating out the Dirac sea, rather than simply omitting it as in the mean-field theory with the no-sea approximation; the resulting effective theory contains new Dirac sea-induced interactions between positive-energy states that are not present in the original Lagrangian, and its reliability is tested against the standard relativistic Hartree approximation.

## Method

The author starts from the Walecka model Lagrangian in both 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions, employs counterterm renormalization with the physical couplings for cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions set to zero in the vacuum, and constructs the no-sea effective theory by evaluating fermion loop diagrams with negative-energy ("-") states to generate effective meson propagators and point couplings. The effective propagators are expanded in powers of small external momentum transfer, yielding a local effective Lagrangian with derivative and non-derivative interaction terms up to fifth order in the nucleon condensates \(\bar\psi\psi\) and \(\bar\psi\gamma^\mu\psi\); the coefficients are computed explicitly for both 1+1 and 3+1 dimensions.

## Key numbers

* In 1+1 dimensions, the coefficient for the \((\bar\psi\psi)^3\) interaction is \(c_{300} = N/(6\pi m)\), where \(N\) is the number of fermion flavors and \(m\) is the physical nucleon mass in the vacuum.
* In 1+1 dimensions, the coefficient for the \((\bar\psi\psi)^4\) interaction is \(c_{400} = \frac{N}{8\pi m^2}\left(\frac{1}{3} + \frac{N g_\sigma^2}{\pi m_\sigma^2}\right)\).
* In 1+1 dimensions, the coefficient for the \((\bar\psi\psi)^5\) interaction is \(c_{500} = \frac{N}{20\pi m^3}\left[\frac{1}{3} + \frac{5N g_\sigma^2}{\pi m_\sigma^2}\left(\frac{1}{3} + \frac{N g_\sigma^2}{2\pi m_\sigma^2}\right)\right]\).
* In 3+1 dimensions, the coefficients \(c_{300}\) and \(c_{400}\) vanish, while \(c_{500} = -N/(40\pi^2 m)\).
* In 3+1 dimensions, the coefficient for the derivative term \((\square\bar\psi\psi)(\bar\psi\psi)^2\) is \(c_{302} = -N/(16\pi^2 m)\).
* The effective scalar coupling expansion includes a term \(c_{204} = 1/m_\sigma^2 + N g_\sigma^2/(120\pi m^4)\) in 1+1 dimensions and \(c_{204} = 1/m_\sigma^2 + N g_\sigma^2/(80\pi^2 m^2)\) in 3+1 dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the no-sea effective theory method previously developed for the Gross-Neveu model family in 1+1 dimensions (Karbstein, 2007) to the Walecka model (also known as quantum hadrodynamics I), which is a more realistic relativistic quantum field theory of nuclear matter. The approach contrasts with the standard mean-field theory with the no-sea approximation, where the Dirac sea is simply omitted from the outset, and provides a systematic way to include Dirac sea effects through explicit integration, enabling new analytical insights into nonuniform nuclear matter in 1+1 dimensions and offering a framework for consistent approximations in 3+1 dimensions.
