# Anisotropic hydrodynamics with number-conserving kernels

> We compare anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) results obtained using the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and leading-order (LO) scalar λϕ^4 collisional kernels. We extend previous work by explicitly enforcing number conservation through the incorporation of a dynamical chemical potential (fugacity) in the underlying aHydro distribution function. We focus on the case of a transversally homogenous and boost-invariant system obeying classical statistics and compare the relevant moments of the two collisional kernels. We then compare the time evolution of the aHydro microscopic parameters and components of the energy-momentum tensor. We also determine the non-equilibrium attractor using both the RTA and LO conformal λϕ^4 number-conserving kernels. We find that the aHydro dynamics receives quantitatively important corrections when enforcing number conservation, however, the aHydro attractor itself is not modified substantially.

## Key claim

The paper finds that enforcing number conservation in anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) leads to quantitatively important corrections to the dynamical evolution of the system, but the non-equilibrium attractor itself is not modified substantially when using either the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) or leading-order scalar λφ⁴ collisional kernels.

## Method

The authors extended previous aHydro work by incorporating a dynamical chemical potential (fugacity) into the Romatschke-Strickland distribution function to enforce number conservation. They derived the 0th, 1st, and 2nd moment equations of the Boltzmann equation for a 0+1d transversally homogeneous and boost-invariant system obeying classical statistics, using both RTA and leading-order scalar λφ⁴ collisional kernels. The second-moment equations were matched in the near-equilibrium limit by equating leading-order coefficients of the small-ξ expansion of the collisional kernel moments.

## Key numbers

- The matching condition between RTA and scalar kernels gives λ² = 8/(15π² α γ η̄), where α ≈ 0.4394 ± 0.0002 is the leading-order coefficient from the small-ξ expansion of the scalar kernel's C̄^zz moment.
- For the scalar kernel, the large-ξ limit of the W function scales as lim_{ξ→∞} W_sc(ξ) = 1.3183 ξ^{3/2}.
- The polynomial fit for W_sc(ξ) used 101 points in the range -0.68 ≤ ξ ≤ 99, with coefficients up to 15th order (e.g., c_0 = 0, c_1 = 1, c_2 = 0.60658, c_3 = -0.068866).
- Initial conditions for dynamical evolution included τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c, Λ₀ = 0.5 GeV, γ₀ = 1, with specific shear viscosity values η̄ = 0.2 and η̄ = 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Almaalol, 2018) which compared RTA and LO scalar λφ⁴ kernels without number conservation, and builds on the aHydro framework developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Alqahtani (2017). The results enable future incorporation of more realistic quantum chromodynamics scattering kernels into aHydro codes, and demonstrate that number-conserving kernels produce opposite effects on momentum-space anisotropy depending on whether RTA or scalar kernels are used, while all kernels converge to the same late-time attractor when plotted against the scaled variable w̄.
