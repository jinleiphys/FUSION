# Exploring the $Λ$-deuteron interaction via correlations in heavy-ion collisions

> $Λ$-deuteron two-particle momentum correlation functions, to be measured in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, are investigated. In particular, the question is addressed whether such correlations can serve as an additional and alternative source of information on the elementary $ΛN$ interaction. The study is performed within the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formalism, utilizing an effective range expansion for the two relevant $S$-wave $Λd$ amplitudes with parameters taken from the literature. It is found that in collisions characterized by a large emitting source the $Λd$ correlation function is predominatly sensitive to the quartet state ($^4S_{3/2}$). In contrast, for small source sizes the contribution from the doublet partial wave ($^2S_{1/2}$) could be significant. Though the latter is constrained by the hypertriton binding energy, its present experimental uncertainty impedes an accurate determination of the doublet amplitude and, in turn, complicates conclusions on the quartet state.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether Λ-deuteron (Λd) two-particle momentum correlation functions, measurable in heavy-ion collisions, can serve as an alternative source of information on the elementary ΛN interaction, but finds that the utility depends strongly on the emitting source size and on the present experimental uncertainty in the hypertriton binding energy.

## Method

The study uses the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formalism with an effective range expansion for the two S-wave Λd amplitudes (doublet ²S₁/₂ and quartet ⁴S₃/₂), taking effective range parameters from prior three-body calculations and pionless effective field theory (π̸EFT) results in the literature. Correlation functions are computed for three Gaussian source radii (R = 1.2, 2.5, and 5.0 fm) motivated by ALICE and STAR measurements, with spin averaging using weights of 1/3 for the doublet and 2/3 for the quartet state.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton binding energy B_Λ = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV (Juric 1973), used to constrain the doublet amplitude via the Bethe formula
- Doublet scattering length from Hammer (2002): a_{1/2} = 16.8^{+4.4}_{-2.4} fm, effective range r_{1/2} = 2.3 ± 0.3 fm
- Doublet scattering length from Cobis (1997): a_{1/2} = 16.3 fm, r_{1/2} = 3.2 fm (with uncertainty a_{1/2} = 16.3^{+4.0}_{-2.1} fm from binding energy variation)
- Quartet scattering lengths from Schäfer (2020) based on three different ΛN inputs: a_{3/2} = -17.3 fm (Alexander), a_{3/2} = -10.8 fm (NSC97f), a_{3/2} = -7.6 fm (chiral EFT NLO13), with effective ranges 3.6–3.8 fm
- Alternative hypertriton binding energy from STAR (Adam 2019): B_Λ = 0.41 ± 0.12 MeV, implying a_{1/2} = 10.2^{+1.5}_{-0.9} fm (assuming Cobis r_{1/2})
- Source radii used: R = 1.2 fm (from ALICE Λp correlations at 7 TeV), R = 2.5 and 5.0 fm (from STAR pΩ in Au+Au at 200 GeV)

## Context

This work extends studies of two-particle momentum correlations as probes of hadron-hadron forces, previously applied to ΛΛ (Morita 2015, Ohnishi 2016), pΩ (STAR 2019), and ΩΩ (Morita 2020) systems, to the Λd system which has not been experimentally accessible via scattering. It builds on Faddeev-type calculations of Λd scattering by Schick (1965–1971), Cobis (1997), and Garcilazo (2007), as well as recent π̸EFT work by Hammer (2002) and Schäfer (2020). The results suggest that measurements in central heavy-ion collisions with large source sizes (R ≈ 5 fm) could constrain the quartet amplitude and thus the ΛN spin-triplet interaction, while small-source measurements (R ≈ 1.2 fm) require better knowledge of the hypertriton binding energy and doublet effective range to be useful.
