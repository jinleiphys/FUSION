# Deformation effects in Giant Monopole Resonance

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) in Samarium isotopes (from spherical $^{144}$Sm to deformed $^{148-154}$Sm) is investigated within the Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) for a variety of Skyrme forces. The exact RPA and its separable version (SRPA) are used for spherical and deformed nuclei, respectively. The quadrupole deformation is shown to yield two effects: the GMR broadens and attains a two-peak structure due to the coupling with the quadrupole giant resonance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quadrupole deformation in Samarium isotopes causes the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) to both broaden and split into a two-peak structure, but this effect is only clearly observed in the Texas A&M University (TAMU) experimental data, not in the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP) data, and the agreement with experiment depends on the choice of Skyrme force and its isoscalar effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations for the isoscalar monopole strength in a chain of Samarium isotopes from spherical 144Sm to deformed 154Sm. Exact RPA was used for the spherical 144Sm, while the separable RPA (SRPA) method was applied for the deformed isotopes 148-154Sm, using a set of five input operators including the monopole transition operator and the quadrupole operator r2Y20 to account for coupling between monopole and quadrupole modes. The calculations employed six different Skyrme forces (SkT6, SVbas, SkM*, SGII, SLy6, and SkI3) with varying isoscalar effective masses, and a Lorentz averaging parameter of Δ = 2 MeV was used to simulate smoothing effects beyond RPA.

## Key numbers

- The GMR in Sm isotopes is concentrated in the energy range 10-20 MeV, with the spurious mode lying at 2-7 MeV safely below this region.
- For the force SVbas, the monopole strength summed over the energy interval 9-45 MeV exhausts the energy-weighted sum rule by 100-105%, depending on the isotope.
- The calculations used a configuration space with particle-hole (two-quasiparticle) energies up to 70-75 MeV.
- The TAMU and RCNP experimental data give about the same GMR peak energy but deviate considerably at energies above 19 MeV, where RCNP indicates a large tail from 19 MeV to 33 MeV while TAMU shows vanishing strength.
- The first GMR peak in 154Sm is generally upshifted with decreasing isoscalar effective mass m0/m, with better agreement obtained for forces with large m0/m (from SkT6 to SGII).

## Context

This work revisits predictions from the 1970s and 1980s by Kishimoto (1975), Zawischa (1978), and Abgrall (1980) that deformation causes GMR broadening and a two-peak structure from coupling with the μ=0 branch of the giant quadrupole resonance, using modern self-consistent mean-field models based on Skyrme forces. The study addresses the unresolved discrepancy between experimental data from Texas A&M University (Youngblood 2004) and RCNP at Osaka University (Itoh 2003), which disagree on whether the GMR in deformed Sm isotopes exhibits a two-peak structure or a single broad peak. The results enable further exploration of nuclear incompressibility and call for harmonization of experimental data before definitive conclusions can be drawn.
