# Energy loss and equilibration of jets in a QCD plasma

> We investigate the medium induced fragmentation of jets in a high-temperature QCD plasma. Based on an effective kinetic theory of QCD, we study the non-equilibrium evolution of the jet shower and the chemical equilibration of jet fragments in the medium. By including radiative emissions as well as elastic interactions, our approach extends all the way from the jet energy scale to the temperature of the medium and includes important effects such as the recoil of the medium. We present results for the in-medium fragmentation, including chemical and kinetic equilibration of the soft fragments and discuss implications of our result to jet quenching physics and the problem of thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions.

# Energy loss and equilibration of jets in a QCD plasma

## Key claim
The in-medium evolution of jets from the jet energy scale down to the medium temperature is dominated by a turbulent inverse energy cascade driven by multiple successive radiative splittings, but the approach to equilibrium only occurs after nearly all jet energy has been lost, meaning jet quenching probes genuinely non-equilibrium features of QCD rather than near-equilibrium physics.

## Method
The authors use an effective kinetic theory of QCD, linearized around a static thermal medium, that includes both elastic 2↔2 interactions (treated in the small-angle Fokker-Planck approximation) and medium-induced radiative 1↔2 processes (including both radiation and inverse merging processes). The evolution of the energy distribution of jet partons D_a(x,τ) is solved numerically for initial jets with energy E = 10³ T, tracking the full evolution from the hard scale down to the thermal scale.

## Key numbers
- Initial jet energy: E = 10³ T (temperature of the medium)
- Dimensionless time variable: τ ≡ g⁴ T √(T/E) t, which accounts for the leading jet energy dependence
- Cutoff scale for defining jet particles: μ ≡ 2π T, with jet particles defined as those with x > μ/E
- The Kolmogorov-Zhakarov (KZ) spectrum for the turbulent cascade has the form D_g(x) = G/√x and D_S(x) = S/√x for intermediate scales T/E ≪ x ≪ 1
- Energy loss rates are compared for initial jet energies E = 30, 100, 300, 1000 T (in units of temperature)

## Context
This work extends the effective kinetic theory approach of AMY (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe) and builds on prior studies of turbulent cascades in QCD by Blaizot (2015) and Mehtar-Tani (2018). It provides a unified description from the jet energy scale to thermalization, including both chemical and kinetic equilibration of jet fragments. The results enable future Monte-Carlo studies of jet quenching and connect to the broader problem of quark-gluon plasma thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, with the finding that the medium acts as a chemical filter where at late times high-energy constituents are more likely to be quarks than gluons.
