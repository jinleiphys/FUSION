# Equation of State for Nucleonic Matter and its Quark Mass Dependence from the Nuclear Force in Lattice QCD

> Quark mass dependence of the equation of state (EOS) for nucleonic matter is investigated, on the basis of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with the nucleon-nucleon interaction extracted from lattice QCD simulations. We observe saturation of nuclear matter at the lightest available quark mass corresponding to the pseudoscalar meson mass ~ 469 MeV. Mass-radius relation of the neutron stars is also studied with the EOS for neutron-star matter from the same nuclear force in lattice QCD. We observe that the EOS becomes stiffer and thus the maximum mass of neutron star increases as the quark mass decreases toward the physical point.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear matter saturation emerges from first-principles lattice QCD nuclear forces at the lightest quark mass studied (pseudoscalar meson mass ~469 MeV), but the saturation point deviates from the empirical one due to the heavy quark masses used and the absence of three-nucleon forces in the calculation.

## Method

The authors combined the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory with nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials extracted from lattice QCD simulations in the flavor-SU(3) limit, using the HAL QCD method. The G-matrix was solved self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, keeping partial-wave decomposition only up to the ^1S_0, ^3S_1, and ^3D_1 channels. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for neutron star mass-radius relations under charge neutrality and beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Saturation point at the lightest quark mass (M_PS ~ 469 MeV): (k_F, E/A) = (1.83 ± 0.16 fm^-1, -5.4 ± 0.5 MeV)
- Empirical saturation point from the Weizsäcker mass formula: (k_F, E/A) = (1.36 fm^-1, -15.7 MeV)
- Maximum neutron star mass at M_PS ~ 469 MeV: 0.53 solar masses (M_⊙)
- Naive extrapolation to physical quark mass (M_PS = 137 MeV) gives M_max = 2.2 M_⊙ and R = 12 km
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.121(2) fm on a 32^3 × 32 lattice
- Pseudoscalar meson masses studied: 468.6(7) MeV, 672.3(6) MeV, 836.5(5) MeV, 1015.2(6) MeV, 1170.9(7) MeV

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory (Baldo 2011), quantum Monte Carlo simulations (Carlson 2012), and chiral effective theories (Hebeler 2013, Holt 2013) by providing the first direct connection between lattice QCD and nucleonic matter equation of state. The results enable future studies incorporating three-nucleon forces from lattice QCD (Doi 2011), spin-orbit forces (Murano 2013), hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions (Aoki 2012), and explicit flavor-SU(3) breaking to construct more realistic equations of state for neutron star physics.
