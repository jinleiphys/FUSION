# Ab-initio approach to effective single-particle energies in doubly closed shell nuclei

> The present work discusses, from an ab-initio standpoint, the definition, the meaning, and the usefulness of effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) in doubly closed shell nuclei. We perform coupled-cluster calculations to quantify to what extent selected closed-shell nuclei in the oxygen and calcium isotopic chains can effectively be mapped onto an effective independent-particle picture. To do so, we revisit in detail the notion of ESPEs in the context of strongly correlated many-nucleon systems and illustrate the necessity to extract ESPEs through the diagonalization of the centroid {\it matrix}, as originally argued by Baranger. For the purpose of illustration, we analyse the impact of correlations on observable one-nucleon separation energies and non-observable ESPEs in selected closed-shell oxygen and calcium isotopes. We then state and illustrate the non-observability of ESPEs. Similarly to spectroscopic factors, ESPEs can indeed be modified by a redefinition of inaccessible quantities while leaving actual observables unchanged. This leads to the absolute necessity to employ consistent structure and reaction models based on the same nuclear Hamiltonian to extract the shell structure in a meaningful fashion from experimental data.

## Key claim

Effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) in doubly closed shell nuclei are not observable quantities; they depend on the resolution scale of the nuclear Hamiltonian and can be changed by unitary transformations that leave actual observables invariant, making them analogous to spectroscopic factors as analysis tools rather than fundamental physical quantities.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster (CC) calculations for selected doubly closed shell oxygen and calcium isotopes, using the centroid matrix formalism originally developed by French and Baranger to extract ESPEs. The centroid matrix was computed as the first moment of the spectral function, combining one-nucleon addition and removal spectroscopic probabilities weighted by their corresponding separation energies, and diagonalized to obtain ESPEs and their associated single-particle wave functions. Calculations were limited to two-nucleon (2N) forces, with the inclusion of three-nucleon (3N) forces postponed to future work.

## Key numbers

- For the oxygen and calcium isotopic chains studied, the authors performed coupled-cluster calculations to quantify the mapping onto an effective independent-particle picture, though specific numerical ESPE values are not provided in the truncated text.
- The asymptotic behavior of centroid wave functions is governed by the decay constant ξ_p = (-2m e^{cent}_p / ħ^2)^{1/2}, where e^{cent}_p is the centroid energy, in contrast to overlap functions whose asymptotic decay is governed by observable separation energies E^+_μ or E^-_ν.
- The zeroth moment of the spectral function integrates to the identity matrix, M^{(0)}_{pq} = δ_{pq}, ensuring that the diagonal elements of the spectral function form a probability distribution that integrates to 1 for each single-particle basis state.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Baranger (1970) and French (1966) for defining ESPEs via centroid matrices, applying it within the modern ab-initio coupled-cluster framework. The authors demonstrate that ESPEs, like spectroscopic factors discussed by Furnstahl (2001) and Jennings (2011), are resolution-scale dependent and non-observable, requiring consistent structure and reaction models based on the same nuclear Hamiltonian for meaningful extraction from experimental data. The study enables future work incorporating three-nucleon forces and continuum degrees of freedom, as well as systematic comparisons between ab-initio ESPEs and those from more effective methods like the shell model and energy density functionals.
