# Neutrino Quasielastic Scattering on Nuclear Targets: Parametrizing Transverse Enhancement (Meson Exchange Currents)

> We present a parametrization of the observed enhancement in the transverse electron quasielastic (QE) response function for nucleons bound in carbon as a function of the square of the four momentum transfer ($Q^2$) in terms of a correction to the magnetic form factors of bound nucleons. The parametrization should also be applicable to the transverse cross section in neutrino scattering. If the transverse enhancement originates from meson exchange currents (MEC), then it is theoretically expected that any enhancement in the longitudinal or axial contributions is small. We present the predictions of the "Transverse Enhancement" model (which is based on electron scattering data only) for the $ν_μ, \barν_μ$ differential and total QE cross sections for nucleons bound in carbon. The $Q^2$ dependence of the transverse enhancement is observed to resolve much of the long standing discrepancy in the QE total cross sections and differential distributions between low energy and high energy neutrino experiments on nuclear targets.

# Neutrino Quasielastic Scattering on Nuclear Targets: Parametrizing Transverse Enhancement (Meson Exchange Currents)

## Key claim

The observed enhancement in the transverse electron quasielastic response function for nucleons bound in carbon can be parametrized as a correction to the magnetic form factors of bound nucleons, and this parametrization resolves much of the long-standing discrepancy between low-energy and high-energy neutrino quasielastic cross section measurements on nuclear targets without requiring an ad-hoc modification of the axial mass parameter.

## Method

The authors extracted the transverse enhancement ratio R_T from electron scattering data on carbon, using values from Carlson et al. (2002) at low Q^2 and from fits to JUPITER collaboration data (Jefferson Lab experiment E04-001) at higher Q^2, with the transverse excess separated from longitudinal QE, transverse QE, and inelastic pion production components. They parametrized R_T as a function of Q^2 and applied this enhancement to the magnetic form factors G_Mp and G_Mn for bound nucleons while keeping G_Ep, G_En, and the axial form factor F_A unchanged, then computed neutrino and antineutrino differential and total quasielastic cross sections for carbon targets with Pauli blocking corrections.

## Key numbers

- The transverse enhancement ratio R_T for carbon at Q^2 = 0.09 (GeV/c)^2 is 1.24 ± 0.1, at Q^2 = 0.15 (GeV/c)^2 is 1.59 ± 0.1, and at Q^2 = 0.33 (GeV/c)^2 is 1.77 ± 0.1, extracted from electron scattering data.
- The parametrization of R_T uses the form R_T = 1 + A Q^2 e^{-Q^2/B} with A = 6.0 and B = 0.34 (GeV/c)^2, with upper error band A = 6.7, B = 0.35 (GeV/c)^2 and lower error band A = 5.3, B = 0.33 (GeV/c)^2.
- The free nucleon axial mass is M_A = 1.014 ± 0.014 GeV from world data on hydrogen and deuterium, while low-energy neutrino experiments on nuclear targets yield effective axial masses of M_A^eff = 1.20 ± 0.12 GeV (K2K) and M_A^eff = 1.23 ± 0.20 GeV (MiniBooNE).
- The NOMAD collaboration measurement on carbon at neutrino energies above 4 GeV yields M_A = 1.05 ± 0.02 ± 0.06 GeV, consistent with free nucleon form factors.
- The transverse enhancement is maximal near Q^2 = 0.3 (GeV/c)^2 and is small for Q^2 greater than 1.5 (GeV/c)^2.

## Context

This work extends the observation from electron scattering studies (Donnelly et al., 2002; Carlson et al., 2002) that the longitudinal QE response function for nuclear targets can be described by independent nucleon models while the transverse response shows significant enhancement attributed to meson exchange currents. The parametrization resolves the discrepancy between low-energy neutrino experiments (MiniBooNE, K2K) that require M_A^eff ≈ 1.2-1.35 GeV and high-energy experiments (NOMAD) that are consistent with free nucleon form factors (M_A ≈ 1.014 GeV), without invoking an ad-hoc nuclear modification to the axial mass. The model enables implementation in neutrino Monte Carlo generators such as GENIE and provides predictions testable by future experiments including MINERvA.
