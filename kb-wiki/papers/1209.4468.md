# Chiral transition in dense, magnetized matter

> In the presence of a chemical potential, the effect of a magnetic field on chiral symmetry breaking goes beyond the well-known magnetic catalysis. Due to a subtle interplay with the chemical potential, the magnetic field may work not only in favor but also against the chirally broken phase. At sufficiently large coupling, the magnetic field favors the broken phase only for field strengths beyond any conceivable value in nature. Therefore, in the interior of magnetars, a possible transition from chirally broken hadronic matter to chirally symmetric quark matter might occur at smaller densities than previously thought.

## Key claim

In the presence of a chemical potential, a magnetic field can work against chiral symmetry breaking (inverse magnetic catalysis) at sufficiently large coupling, contrary to the well-known magnetic catalysis effect; this means that in magnetar interiors, the transition from chirally broken hadronic matter to chirally symmetric quark matter might occur at smaller densities than previously thought.

## Method

The authors analyze the chiral phase transition using two complementary models: a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for one flavor of massless fermions in the mean-field approximation, and the Sakai-Sugimoto model (a holographic model based on gauge/gravity duality). They compute the free energy difference between chirally restored and chirally broken phases at zero temperature as a function of chemical potential and magnetic field, considering both weak-coupling and strong-coupling regimes, and including the effect of the lowest Landau level (LLL).

## Key numbers

- In the NJL model, a dynamical fermion mass is generated only for dimensionless coupling strengths \(g > 1\), where \(g \equiv G\Lambda^2/(2\pi)\) with ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\).
- In the weak-coupling limit (\(g \ll 1\)) with a magnetic field, the dynamical mass is \(M \simeq \sqrt{B/\pi}\, e^{-\pi^2/(BG)}\).
- In the NJL model at supercritical coupling, inverse magnetic catalysis occurs up to \(B/\Lambda^2 \lesssim 0.5\), where the critical chemical potential decreases with increasing magnetic field.
- In the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a magnetic field of \(b = 0.1\) (dimensionless units) corresponds to \(B \simeq 1.0 \times 10^{19}\) G, assuming the phase transition at zero magnetic field occurs at \(\mu \sim 400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of inverse magnetic catalysis (Preis 2010) and contrasts with the standard magnetic catalysis found in NJL models at weak coupling and in lattice QCD at zero chemical potential. The results contradict the naive expectation from magnetic catalysis that the critical chemical potential increases with magnetic field, and instead show that at strong coupling the magnetic field can restore chiral symmetry at lower densities. This enables predictions for the chiral transition in magnetar interiors, where magnetic fields can reach \(10^{19}\) G, and provides a bridge between NJL model calculations and holographic approaches to QCD.
