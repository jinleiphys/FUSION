# GW170817 constraints on the properties of a neutron star in the presence of WIMP dark matter

> The properties of a neutron star are studied in the presence of dark matter. We have considered a relatively light Weakly Interacting Massive Particle (WIMP) as a dark matter candidate with properties suggested by the results of the DAMA/LIBRA collaboration, realized for instance within the framework of the Next-to-Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model. The dark matter particle interacts with the baryonic matter of a neutron star through Higgs bosons. The dark matter variables are essentially fixed using the results of the DAMA/LIBRA experiment, which are then used to build the Lagrangian density for the WIMP-nucleon interaction inside a neutron star. We have used the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field model to study the equations-of-state in the presence of dark matter. The predicted equations-of-state are used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain the mass-radius relations, the moment of inertia, and effects of the tidal field on a neutron star. The calculated properties are compared with the corresponding data of the GW170817 event.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how a light WIMP dark matter particle (mass 3–8 GeV, as suggested by DAMA/LIBRA) interacting with nucleons via a light Higgs boson (40 GeV) softens the neutron star equation of state, but the effect is smaller than in previous studies that used heavier dark matter particles (200 GeV) and the Standard Model Higgs.

## Method

The authors used an effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model with three parameter sets (IOPB-I, G3, NL3) to generate hadronic equations of state. They added a fermionic WIMP dark matter component interacting with nucleons through Higgs exchange, with dark matter parameters fixed by DAMA/LIBRA results (DM mass 3–8 GeV, spin-independent cross-section 3×10⁻⁴¹ to 5×10⁻³⁹ cm²). The combined equations of state were solved with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability, then compared to GW170817 constraints.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter particle mass range: 3 GeV ≤ m_DM ≤ 8 GeV (from DAMA/LIBRA interpretation)
- Spin-independent DM-nucleon cross-section range: 3×10⁻⁴¹ cm² ≤ σ_p^SI ≤ 5×10⁻³⁹ cm²
- Higgs boson mass assumed for DM sector: M_h = 40 GeV (to keep Yukawa coupling y < 1)
- Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter: f = 0.3 (central value from lattice computations)
- Dark matter Fermi momentum fixed at: k_f^DM = 0.06 GeV
- Maximum mass constraint from pulsar PSR J0348+0432: (2.01 ± 0.04) M_⊙
- Maximum mass constraint from GW170817: M_max ≲ 2.17 M_⊙ (or M ≲ 2.16^{+0.16}_{-0.15} M_⊙ from quasi-universal relation)
- Radius constraint at canonical mass from GW170817: R_1.4 ≤ 13.76 km
- Pressure at twice nuclear saturation density from GW170817: 21.88^{+16.88}_{-10.62} MeV·fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars by Panotopoulos and collaborators (2017, 2018) and by others using the Walecka model and NL3 parameter set, but uses lighter WIMP masses consistent with DAMA/LIBRA results rather than the 200 GeV DM particles considered earlier. The authors also include the G3 parameter set within E-RMF for the first time in this context and compute additional observables (moment of inertia) beyond mass-radius and tidal deformability. The results enable future comparisons with GW170817 and upcoming gravitational wave events to constrain both nuclear equations of state and dark matter properties simultaneously.
