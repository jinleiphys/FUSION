# Chiral Nonet Mixing in $πη$ Scattering

> The generalized linear sigma model for mixing among two- and four-quark components of scalar (and psedudosclar) mesons below and above 1 GeV is applied to the $πη$ channel in which the isovector scalars $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ are probed. In the leading order, the model parameters have been previously fixed by various low-energy experimental data, and then applied to $ππ$ and $πK$ channels in which the properties of the light and broad $σ$ and $κ$ mesons are extracted in agreement with estimates reported in the literature. With the same parameters fixed in the leading order, in the present work the prediction of the model for the $πη$ scattering amplitude in the elastic region is given and unitarized with the K-matrix method. The poles of the unitarized scattering amplitude, which determine the mass and decay width of $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ are computed. It is found that the model predicts an isovector scalar state below 1 GeV, with mass 984 $\pm$ 6 MeV and decay width 108 $\pm$ 30 MeV which is a clear signal for the $a_0(980)$. The $a_0$ pole extracted in this work, further supports the plausibility of the mixing patterns for scalar mesons predicted by this model according to which there is a significant underlying mixing among scalars below and above 1 GeV, with those below 1 GeV being generally of four-quark nature while those above 1 GeV being overall closer to quark-antiquark states. Predictions for various scattering lengths as well as for properties of $a_0(1450)$ are also presented.

## Key claim

The generalized linear sigma model, with parameters previously fixed by low-energy data and applied to ππ and πK scattering, predicts an isovector scalar state below 1 GeV with mass 984 ± 6 MeV and decay width 108 ± 30 MeV, which the authors identify as the a₀(980) resonance; this result supports the model's mixing pattern where scalars below 1 GeV are predominantly four-quark states while those above 1 GeV are closer to quark-antiquark configurations.

## Method

The authors apply the generalized linear sigma model (GLSM) formulated with two scalar and two pseudoscalar nonets (representing two-quark and four-quark components) to πη scattering in the elastic region, using the same leading-order parameters fixed in prior work. The tree-level scattering amplitude is computed from contact diagrams and s-channel/t-channel exchanges of isovector and isosinglet scalars, then unitarized using the K-matrix method without introducing additional parameters. Poles of the unitarized amplitude are extracted to determine physical masses and decay widths of the a₀(980) and a₀(1450) resonances.

## Key numbers

* Mass of the predicted isovector scalar state (a₀(980)): 984 ± 6 MeV
* Decay width of the predicted isovector scalar state (a₀(980)): 108 ± 30 MeV
* In the single nonet linear sigma model (SNLSM), the K-matrix unitarized pole gives physical mass 1.013 GeV and decay width 0.241 GeV for the isotriplet state
* S-wave scattering length a₀¹ in GLSM: (2.6–4.0) × 10⁻² (in units of pion scattering wavelength)
* S-wave scattering length b₀¹ in GLSM: (2.4–4.4) × 10⁻²
* S-wave scattering length c₀¹ in GLSM: (1.6–5.2) × 10⁻³
* Input masses used for parameter fixing: m[a₀(980)] = 980 ± 20 MeV, m[a₀(1450)] = 1474 ± 19 MeV, m[π(1300)] = 1300 ± 100 MeV, m_π = 137 MeV, F_π = 131 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of the generalized linear sigma model to ππ scattering (Fariborz et al., mixing_pipi) and πK scattering (Fariborz et al., mixing_piK), completing the probe of the scalar meson nonet below 1 GeV within this mixing framework. The results are compared with predictions from the single nonet linear sigma model (SNLSM), chiral perturbation theory at O(p⁴) (Bernard et al., ChPT_bernard), the nonlinear chiral Lagrangian approach (Black et al., pieta), and analyses of Belle data for γγ → π⁰η (Achasov et al., belledata). The model enables future studies of coupled-channel effects including πη → K K̅ and πη → πη' channels above 1 GeV.
