# Transverse electron scattering response function of $^3$He with $Δ$-isobar degrees of freedom

> A calculation of the $^3$He transverse $(e,e')$ inclusive response function, $R_T$, which includes $Δ$ degrees of freedom is performed using the Lorentz integral transform method. The resulting coupled equations are treated in impulse approximation, where the $NNN$ and $NNΔ$ channels are solved separately. As NN and NNN potentials we use the Argonne V18 and UrbanaIX models respectively. Electromagnetic currents include the $Δ$-isobar currents, one-body N-currents with relativistic corrections and two-body currents consistent with the Argonne V18 potential. $R_T$ is calculated for the breakup threshold region at momentum transfers near 900 MeV/c. Our results are similar to those of Deltuva {\it et al.} in that large $Δ$-isobar current contributions are found. However we find that these are largely canceled by the relativistic contribution from the one-body N-currents. Finally a comparison is made between theoretical results and experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for the transverse response function \(R_T\) of \(^3\)He near the breakup threshold at momentum transfers around 900 MeV/c, the large \(\Delta\)-isobar current contributions are largely canceled by the relativistic contribution from the one-body nucleon currents, a result that is conditional on the specific impulse approximation treatment and the chosen potentials (Argonne V18 and Urbana IX).

## Method

The authors used the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method to calculate the inclusive transverse electron scattering response function \(R_T\) for \(^3\)He, incorporating \(\Delta\)-isobar degrees of freedom via the impulse approximation. The coupled equations for the \(NNN\) and \(NN\Delta\) channels were solved separately, using the Argonne V18 NN potential and the Urbana IX NNN potential, with electromagnetic currents including one-body nucleon currents with relativistic corrections, two-body meson-exchange currents consistent with the Argonne V18 potential, and \(\Delta\)-isobar currents. The response function was computed for momentum transfers near 900 MeV/c and excitation energies up to 20 MeV above the breakup threshold, with the LIT inverted using a standard inversion method.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta\)-probability in the \(^3\)He ground state is 1.14% for the interaction model used (AV18+UIX), compared to 1.44% obtained by Deltuva et al. using a CDBonn+\(\Delta\) coupled channel potential.
- The calculation was performed for momentum transfers \(q = 862\) MeV/c and \(q = 927\) MeV/c, with the LIT computed using \(\sigma_I = 5\) MeV.
- The maximum total angular momentum \(\mathcal{J}\) was restricted to 5/2 for sufficient accuracy in the low-energy region.
- The response function was calculated for internal excitation energies \(\omega_{\rm int}\) up to 20 MeV above the breakup threshold.

## Context

This work extends previous LIT calculations of electron scattering response functions for three-nucleon systems (e.g., Efros et al. (2005), Deltuva et al. (2004)) by incorporating \(\Delta\)-isobar degrees of freedom for the first time in the LIT framework. It contrasts with the full coupled channel calculation of Deltuva et al. (2004), which treated \(N\) and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom on an equivalent level but did not include relativistic corrections to the one-body current; the present work finds that these relativistic corrections nearly cancel the \(\Delta\)-isobar current contributions, leading to good agreement with experimental data at very low energy transfer but underestimation at higher energies. The results enable further studies of \(\Delta\) effects in inclusive electron scattering on few-body nuclei, particularly at higher momentum transfers where the cancellation may differ.
