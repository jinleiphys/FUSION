# Quasiparticle transport equation with collision delay. II. Microscopic Theory

> For a system of non-interacting electrons scattered by neutral impurities, we derive a modified Boltzmann equation that includes quasiparticle and virial corrections. We start from quasiclassical transport equation for non-equilibrium Green's functions and apply limit of small scattering rates. Resulting transport equation for quasiparticles has gradient corrections to scattering integrals. These gradient corrections are rearranged into a form characteristic for virial corrections.

## Key claim

For a system of non-interacting electrons scattered by neutral impurities, the authors derive a modified Boltzmann equation that includes both quasiparticle and virial corrections, showing that these two types of corrections are of the same order and tend to mutually compensate, so one should either include both or neglect both.

## Method

The authors start from the generalized Kadanoff-Baym equation for non-equilibrium Green's functions and apply the quasiclassical limit (small gradients in time and space) together with the limit of small scattering rates. They use the averaged T-matrix approximation (ATA) for the self-energy to describe scattering by neutral impurities with resonant levels, then evaluate the transport vertex and rearrange its gradient corrections into a form characteristic of virial corrections. The pole and off-pole contributions to the correlation function are separated using the completed collision approach, and the quasiparticle distribution is extracted from the pole part.

## Key numbers

- The wave-function renormalization is given by \( z = 1 / (1 - \partial\sigma(\omega)/\partial\omega|_{\omega=\varepsilon}) \), where \(\sigma\) is the real part of the retarded self-energy.
- The spectral function in the limit of small scattering rates is approximated as \( a = 2\pi z \delta(\omega-\varepsilon) + \gamma \, \text{Re}[1/(\omega-\epsilon-\phi-i0)^2] \), with \(\gamma = -2\text{Im}\sigma^R\).
- The function \( s = \frac{1}{2}\gamma^3 / \left((\omega-\epsilon-\phi-\sigma)^2 + \frac{1}{4}\gamma^2\right)^2 \) reduces to \( s = 2\pi z \delta(\omega-\varepsilon) \) in the limit of small scattering rates.
- The quasiparticle energy is defined by \( \varepsilon = \epsilon + \phi + \sigma(\varepsilon) \), where \(\epsilon\) is the free-electron kinetic energy and \(\phi\) is the potential.

## Context

This paper (Paper II) extends the intuitive modification of the Boltzmann equation presented in Paper I (Špička, Lipavský, Morawetz 1997) by deriving it rigorously from quantum statistics. It addresses the contradictory predictions of gradient corrections found in prior work by Barker (1973), Khan, Davies, and Wilkins (1987), and others, showing that these contradictions arise from different treatments of quasiparticle corrections. The work enables a unified treatment of quasiparticle and virial corrections for transport in systems with resonant impurity scattering, and provides a foundation for evaluating single-electron observables such as electron density, current, and energy density.
