# On the charge distribution of $d^*(2380)$

> We calculate the charge distributions of $d^*(2380)$. Two different interpretations of the $d^*$ are considered for a comparison. One is a compact explanation with coupled $ΔΔ+CC$ two-channel approximation in the chiral constituent quark model. Another is a resonance state of $D_{12}π$. The remarkable differences of the charge distributions in the two pictures are shown and it is expected that the future experiments may provide a clear test for the different theoretical interpretations.

## Key claim

The charge distribution of the $d^*(2380)$ resonance is calculated under two competing interpretations, and the authors claim that the resulting charge radii differ by about a factor of 2.2, which could serve as a future experimental discriminant, but only if one can measure the charge form factor, which the authors acknowledge is experimentally challenging.

## Method

The authors compute the charge form factor $G_E(Q^2)$ of $d^*(2380)$ in two scenarios using the Breit-frame matrix element of the quark current $J^0$. In Scenario A (hexaquark dominant), the six-quark wave function is obtained from a bound-state RGM solution of the chiral constituent quark model with coupled $\Delta\Delta$ and $CC$ (color-octet) channels, and the charge distribution is the sum of contributions from both components. In Scenario B ($D_{12}\pi$ resonance), the $D_{12}$ subsystem is treated as a weakly bound $N\Delta$ state with $^5S_2$ dominance, and the $D_{12}\pi$ relative motion is taken as a P-wave; the pion contribution is included via a monopole form factor, and the total charge distribution is averaged over magnetic quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square radius of $d^*(2380)$ in Scenario A1 (single $\Delta\Delta$ channel): 1.09 fm.
- Root-mean-square radius of $d^*(2380)$ in Scenario A2 (coupled $\Delta\Delta+CC$ channel): 0.78 fm.
- Root-mean-square radius of the $D_{12}$ subsystem in Scenario B: 2.39 fm.
- Ratio of slopes of the charge distribution at $Q^2=0$ for Scenarios A1, A2, and B: 2.30 : 1.45 : 6.86.
- Binding energy of the $D_{12}$ state used in Scenario B: $\epsilon = 4.17$ MeV.
- Probabilities in the coupled-channel wave function: $\alpha^2 \sim 0.31$ ($\Delta\Delta$ component), $\beta^2 \sim 0.69$ ($CC$ component).
- Size parameters used: $b_N = b_\Delta = 0.5$ fm, $b_c = 0.45$ fm, $b_c^r = 0.45$ fm, $\tilde{b} = 1.2$ fm.
- Monopole parameter for pion form factor: $\Lambda_\pi^2 = 0.462$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hexaquark-dominant model of $d^*(2380)$ (Huang et al., 2014; Dong et al., 2016) by adding the charge distribution as a new observable, and contrasts it with the alternative $D_{12}\pi$ molecular interpretation proposed by Gal (2015). The calculation shows that the charge radius of the $D_{12}\pi$ scenario is about 2.2 times larger than that of the compact hexaquark scenario, which could in principle be tested by future experiments at BEPC, Belle, BaBar, or PANDA via time-like form factor measurements in $e^+e^-$ or $p\bar{p}$ annihilation.
