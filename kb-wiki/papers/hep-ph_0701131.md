# The two types of Cherenkov gluons at LHC energies

> Beside comparatively low energy Cherenkov gluons observed at RHIC, there could be high energy gluons at LHC, related to the high energy region of positive real part of the forward scattering amplitude. In both cases they give rise to particles emitted along some cone. The characteristics of the cones produced by these two types of gluons are different. Therefore different experiments are needed to detect them. The cosmic ray event which initiated this idea is described in detail.

## Key claim

Beside the comparatively low energy Cherenkov gluons already observed at RHIC (attributed to resonance effects), there could be high energy Cherenkov gluons at LHC, related to the high energy region where the real part of the forward scattering amplitude becomes positive again; the two types produce cones with different characteristics requiring different experimental strategies.

## Method

The paper uses the nuclear index of refraction formalism, where the excess of the index over 1 is proportional to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude and the number of scatterers. For low energy gluons, the real part is positive on the left wings of Breit-Wigner resonances (especially the rho meson); for high energy gluons, it becomes positive again at very high energies based on dispersion relations and hadronic data. The author applies these ideas to interpret the two-bump azimuthal structure observed in RHIC away-side jet data and a cosmic ray event with ring-like particle distributions, then extrapolates predictions to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear index of refraction extracted from RHIC data: n = 3.
- Parton density estimate from dilute medium formula: about 20 partons per nucleon volume; from dense medium (Clausius-Mossotti) formula: about 10 partons per nucleon.
- Energy loss of Cherenkov gluons: dE/dx ≈ 1 GeV/fm.
- Free path length of Cherenkov gluons: R_f ≈ 7 fm.
- Cosmic ray event energy: about 10^16 eV; observed ring radii: r1 = 1.75 cm and r2 = 5 cm; reconstructed height of interaction: H ≈ 68 m (with reliable estimates ranging from 50 m to 100 m).
- High energy Cherenkov gluon threshold: E_th about 70–300 GeV; fitted parameter a ≈ 2·10^{-3} GeV.
- Low energy Cherenkov gluon emission angle in the medium rest system: about 70° (for n=3); high energy gluon emission angle in c.m.s.: about 60°–70°.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of RHIC two-bump azimuthal distributions (observed by PHENIX and STAR, cited as references [5,6]) as Cherenkov gluon emission, first proposed by Dremin (references [1,2]) to explain cosmic ray data (reference [8]). It contrasts with standard "in-vacuum" DGLAP jet evolution by emphasizing collective medium effects in sQGP. The paper enables experimental searches at LHC: trigger experiments with large-angle jets for low energy Cherenkov gluons (analogous to RHIC) and inclusive experiments for high energy Cherenkov gluons, which would probe the nuclear index of refraction, parton density, and medium motion at much higher energies than accessible at RHIC.
