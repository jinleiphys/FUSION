# Coexistence of Anderson-Bogoliubov phonon and quadrupole cluster vibration in neutron star inner crust

> Background: Low-lying collective excitations of inner crust matter in neutron stars are expected to have impact on observables such as the quasi-periodic oscillation in giant flares and the cooling of inner crust in transient phenomena. Coupling between Anderson-Bogoliubov superfluid phonon (AB phonon) in superfluid neutron gas and collective excitations of nuclear clusters is a key issue. Purpose: We intend to predict the nature of low-lying excitation modes of superfluid inner crust matter with focus on quadrupole excitations around a spherical nuclear cluster. We study how the AB phonon of neutron superfluid and possible quadrupole shape vibration of clusters are affected by the inhomogeneous structure of inner crust matter. Results: The calculated results indicate emergence of both AB phonon and quadrupole shape vibration of the cluster with small mixing between the two collective modes. The quadruple AB phonon is similar to that in uniform superfluid apart from small admixture of the shape vibration of cluster. The excitation energy and the collectivity of the cluster vibration mode shows strong and oscillatory dependence on the neutron chemical potential (the neutron gas density), leading to softening and instability in certain situations. This is caused by the resonance shell effect where unbound but resonant single-particle states of neutrons play a central role. Conclusions: The AB phonon of superfluid neutron gas and the quadrupole shape vibration of nuclear cluster coexist in inner crust. The coupling between the AB phonon and the quadrupole shape vibration is weak. The collectivity of the quadrupole shape vibration is governed by the resonance shell effect, and it suggests that emergence of deformed nuclear clusters is possible at any layer of inner crust.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the inner crust of neutron stars, the Anderson-Bogoliubov (AB) superfluid phonon and the quadrupole shape vibration of nuclear clusters coexist with weak coupling between them, but this coexistence is conditional: the cluster vibration mode shows strong and oscillatory dependence on the neutron chemical potential, leading to softening and possible instability (deformation) of spherical clusters at certain densities, which the abstract presents as a conclusion rather than a conditional finding.

## Method

The authors used the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method to describe the ground state of inner crust matter within a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell of radius 20 fm, imposing von-Neumann-Dirichlet boundary conditions. They then applied the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) formulated in the same cell to compute quadrupole excitation strength functions for neutron and proton density operators and pair-addition/removal operators, systematically varying the neutron chemical potential (1–6 MeV) and proton number (Z = 20, 28, 40, 50). The Skyrme SLy4 interaction was used for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta interaction for the pairing channel, with the Landau-Migdal approximation for the residual particle-hole interaction renormalized to bring the displacement motion to zero energy in drip-line nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For Z = 28 with λ_n = 4.0 MeV, the AB-phonon-dominant mode appears at excitation energy E = 1.57 MeV with neutron quadrupole strength B(Q_n) = 1.49 × 10^6 fm^4, exhausting 67% of the total neutron quadrupole strength up to 50 MeV.
* For the same system, the cluster-vibration-dominant mode appears at E = 2.06 MeV with proton quadrupole strength B(Q_p) = 508 fm^4 and neutron quadrupole strength B(Q_n) = 3.46 × 10^5 fm^4.
* The AB-phonon-dominant mode excitation energy agrees with a hydrodynamic estimate using phonon velocity c_ph = sqrt((∂λ_n/∂ρ)ρ/m) and boundary condition dφ/dr = 0 at R_WS = 20 fm, with the neutron chemical potential λ_n varied from 1.0 to 6.0 MeV (corresponding to neutron gas density ρ_n ≈ 5 × 10^{-4} to 1 × 10^{-2} fm^{-3}).
* The cluster-vibration-dominant mode shows softening and instability intervals: for Z = 28, instability occurs at λ_n ≈ 1.6–2.0 MeV and again at λ_n ≈ 4.8–6.0 MeV, with finite excitation energy in the interval λ_n ≈ 2.1–4.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of dipole excitations in the inner crust (Inakura and Matsuo, 2017, referred to as Part I), which found that the dipole AB phonon is strongly influenced by the cluster but with weak coupling to the cluster displacement motion. It addresses unresolved questions from Khan et al. (2005), who predicted a "supergiant resonance" in quadrupole excitations using a similar HFB+QRPA model but could not distinguish between AB phonon and cluster vibration modes, and from Baroni et al. (2010), who used Hartree-Fock plus RPA (neglecting pair correlations) and found only quadrupole shape vibrations without the AB phonon. The results enable predictions that deformed nuclear clusters may emerge at any layer of the inner crust due to the resonance shell effect from unbound resonant single-particle neutron states, with implications for neutron star observables such as quasi-periodic oscillations in giant flares and thermal conductivity.
