# Gamow shell model description of the radiative capture reaction $^8$B$(p,γ)$$^9$C

> In low metallicity supermassive stars, the hot $pp$ chain can serve as an alternative way to produce the CNO nuclei. In the astrophysical environment of high temperature, the proton capture of $^8$B can be faster than its beta decay, thus $^8$B$(p,γ)$$^9$C reaction plays an important role in the hot $pp$ chain. Due to the unstable nature of $^8$B and the lack of the $^8$B beam, the measurement of $^8$B$(p,γ)$$^9$C reaction can only be achieved by the indirect method, and large uncertainties exist. The Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) is applied to study the proton radiative capture reaction $^8$B$(p,γ)$$^9$C. For the calculation of the $^8$B$(p,γ)$$^9$C astrophysical factors, all E1, M1, and E2 transitions from the initial continuum states to the final bound states ${3/2}_1^-$of $^9$C are considered. The resonant capture to the first resonant state ${1/2}_1^-$ of $^{9}$C is also calculated. The experimental low-energy levels and the proton emission threshold in $^9$C are reproduced by the GSM-CC. The calculated astrophysical factors agrees with the existed experimental data from the indirect measurements. The reaction rates from the direct capture and resonant capture are calculated for the temperature range of astrophysical interest. The calculated total astrophysical $S$ factor is dominated by the E1 transition to the ground state of $^9$C. The GSM-CC calculations suggest that $S$ first increase with the energy of the center of mass $E_{\rm c.m.}$, and then decrease with the energy. This agrees with the existing data, which has smaller values at around zero energy and larger value in the energy range of 0.2 MeV $\leq E_{\rm c.m.} \leq$ 0.6 MeV.

## Key claim
The Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) reproduces the existing indirect experimental data for the astrophysical S factor of the \(^8\)B\((p,\gamma)^9\)C reaction, with the total S factor dominated by E1 transitions to the ground state of \(^9\)C; the S factor first increases with center-of-mass energy and then decreases, consistent with data showing smaller values near zero energy and larger values in the 0.2–0.6 MeV range.

## Method
The GSM-CC framework was applied using a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with a Woods-Saxon core potential and a finite-range Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) two-body interaction, with parameters adjusted to reproduce low-energy spectra of \(^8\)B and \(^9\)C. Reaction channels were built by coupling the ground state \(2^+_1\), first excited state \(1^+_1\), and second excited state \(3^+_1\) of \(^8\)B to a projectile proton in partial waves \(s_{1/2}\), \(p_{1/2}\), \(p_{3/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), and \(d_{5/2}\). All E1, M1, and E2 transitions from initial continuum states to the final bound \(3/2^-_1\) state of \(^9\)C were considered, along with resonant capture to the first resonant state \(1/2^-_1\) of \(^9\)C.

## Key numbers
- The calculated ground-state energy of \(^8\)B relative to \(^4\)He is -9.609 MeV, close to the experimental value -9.441 MeV.
- The calculated root mean square (RMS) proton radius of \(^8\)B in GSM is 2.63 fm, close to the experimental value of 2.76(9) fm.
- The first excited state \(1/2^-_1\) of \(^9\)C is calculated at 2.231 MeV excitation energy with a width of 143.62 keV, compared to the experimental value of 2.218(11) MeV and width 52(11) keV.
- The second excited state \(5/2^-_1\) of \(^9\)C is calculated at 3.822 MeV with width 297.91 keV, versus experimental 3.549(20) MeV and width 673(50) keV.
- The electric quadrupole moment of the \(^8\)B ground state calculated with effective charges (1.26e, 0.47e) is +3.083 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\), compared to the experimental value +6.43(14) \(e^2\) fm\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends the application of the Gamow shell model in coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to radiative capture reactions, following prior studies of elastic and inelastic proton scattering (Jaganathen 2014), deuteron elastic scattering (Mercenne 2019), and other capture reactions (Fossez 2015; Dong 2017; Dong 2022). It addresses the large theoretical discrepancies in the \(^8\)B\((p,\gamma)^9\)C S factor, where prior potential model calculations (Wiescher 1989) gave very large S(0) values and microscopic cluster model results (Descouvemont 1993, 1999) showed strong potential dependence. The GSM-CC results enable more reliable reaction rate calculations for the hot pp chain in low-metallicity supermassive stars, where this reaction can bypass the three-alpha process to form CNO nuclei.
