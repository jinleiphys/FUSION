# Partial Wave Analysis of Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering below pion production threshold

> We undertake a simultaneous partial wave analysis to proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data below pion production threshold up to LAB energies of 350MeV. We represent the interaction as a sum of delta-shells in configuration space below 3fm and a charge dependent one pion exchange potential above 3fm together with electromagnetic effects. We obtain chi^2 = 2813|pp + 3985|np with a total of N = 2747|pp + 3691|np data obtained till 2013 and a total number of 46 fitting parameters yielding chi^2 /d.o.f = 1.06. Special attention is paid to estimate the errors of the phenomenological interaction as well as the derived effects on the phase-shifts and scattering amplitudes.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new high-quality nucleon-nucleon potential with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.06, but the authors note that the form of the potential is not unique, implying that systematic uncertainties are larger than the purely statistical errors estimated from the covariance matrix.

## Method

The authors perform a simultaneous partial wave analysis of proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data below pion production threshold up to laboratory energies of 350 MeV. The interaction is represented as a sum of delta-shells in configuration space below 3 fm, combined with a charge-dependent one pion exchange potential and electromagnetic effects above 3 fm. The fitting parameters are the delta-shell strengths, determined by minimizing chi-squared using the Levenberg-Marquardt method with explicit Hessian computation, and the Nijmegen 3-sigma criterion is applied to reject outliers.

## Key numbers

* Total chi-squared of 2813 for pp data and 3985 for np data, with N = 2747 pp data and 3691 np data, using 46 fitting parameters, yielding chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.06.
* The delta-shell grid uses N = 5 equidistant points with spacing Delta r = 0.6 fm between the origin and r_c = 3 fm.
* Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio eta = 0.02493(8), compared to the empirical value of 0.0256(5).
* Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.2679(9) fm^2, compared to the empirical value of 0.2859(3) fm^2.
* Deuteron D-state probability P_D = 5.62(5) percent, compared to the empirical value of 5.67(4) percent.

## Context

This work extends the approach of coarse-grained delta-shell potentials first proposed by Aviles (1973) and used by the authors in earlier threshold parameter studies (Pavon Valderrama 2005). It provides a new high-quality potential with error estimates from the covariance matrix, complementing previous analyses such as the Nijmegen PWA (Stoks 1993) and high-quality potentials like AV18 (Wiringa 1994) and CD-Bonn (Machleidt 2000). The results enable propagation of NN-scattering uncertainties into nuclear structure calculations, though the authors note that systematic errors from the non-uniqueness of the potential form remain to be addressed.
