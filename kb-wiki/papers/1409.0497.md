# Charmed bottom baryon spectroscopy from lattice QCD

> We calculate the masses of baryons containing one, two, or three heavy quarks using lattice QCD. We consider all possible combinations of charm and bottom quarks, and compute a total of 36 different states with $J^P = \frac12^+$ and $J^P = \frac32^+$. We use domain-wall fermions for the up, down, and strange quarks, a relativistic heavy-quark action for the charm quarks, and nonrelativistic QCD for the bottom quarks. Our analysis includes results from two different lattice spacings and seven different pion masses. We perform extrapolations of the baryon masses to the continuum limit and to the physical pion mass using $SU(4|2)$ heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory including $1/m_Q$ and finite-volume effects. For the 14 singly heavy baryons that have already been observed, our results agree with the experimental values within the uncertainties. We compare our predictions for the hitherto unobserved states with other lattice calculations and quark-model studies.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of masses for baryons containing any combination of charm and bottom quarks (singly, doubly, and triply heavy) with control over all major systematic uncertainties, including two lattice spacings, seven pion masses down to 227(3) MeV, and extrapolations to the continuum limit and physical pion mass using heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors used gauge field ensembles from the RBC/UKQCD collaborations with domain-wall fermions for up, down, and strange quarks at two lattice spacings (approximately 0.11 fm and 0.085 fm) and seven different pion masses. Charm quarks were treated with a relativistic heavy-quark action tuned nonperturbatively using charmonium dispersion relations, while bottom quarks were treated with improved nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) with one-loop matching of the spin-dependent coefficient. Baryon masses were extracted from two-point correlation functions using multiple Gaussian-smeared interpolating operators, and extrapolated to the physical point using SU(4|2) heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory including 1/m_Q and finite-volume effects.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses as low as 227(3) MeV were achieved (data set F23)
- Two lattice spacings used: approximately 0.11 fm (coarse, 24^3x64) and 0.085 fm (fine, 32^3x64)
- 36 different baryon states with J^P = 1/2^+ and J^P = 3/2^+ were computed
- 14 singly heavy baryons with experimental measurements were reproduced within uncertainties
- Charmonium hyperfine splitting (J/psi - eta_c) computed as 108.5(1.5) MeV on coarse and 109.0(1.5) MeV on fine lattices, compared to experimental 113.2(0.7) MeV
- Spin-averaged charmonium mass computed as 3062(43) MeV (coarse) and 3065(42) MeV (fine), compared to experimental 3068.6(0.2) MeV
- Speed of light in J/psi dispersion relation measured as 1.010(15) (coarse) and 1.000(30) (fine)

## Context

This work extends earlier unquenched lattice QCD calculations of heavy baryons by groups including Na (2006-2008), Detmold (2008), Meinel (2010, 2012), and Alexandrou (2012, 2014), and is the first to simultaneously include both charm and bottom quarks in all combinations with control over lattice spacing, finite volume, and quark mass extrapolations. The results provide benchmarks for quark-model studies and perturbative QCD predictions, and can help resolve experimental controversies such as the Omega_b mass discrepancy between D0 and CDF/LHCb, as well as guide searches for doubly and triply heavy baryons at LHC, BESIII, Belle II, and PANDA.
