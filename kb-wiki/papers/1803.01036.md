# The temperature dependence of the shell corrections

> We have examined the dependence of the shell correction to the nuclear liquid drop energy at finite excitations on the excitation energy (temperature). For this we have calculated the shell correction to the energy and free energy in very broad region of nuclei and deformations starting directly from their formal definitions. We have found out that the dependence of the shell corrections on the excitation energy differ substantially from the widely used approximation $δE(E^*)=δE(0)\exp(-E^*/E_d)$ both at small and large excitations. In particular, below the critical temperature at which the pairing effects vanish, the shell correction to the free energy is rather insensitive to the excitation energy. We suggest a more accurate approximation for the temperature dependence of the shell correction to the energy and free energy that is expressed in terms of the shell correction to the energy of independent particles and the shell correction to the pairing energy at T=0 and few fitted constants.

## Key claim

The widely used exponential damping formula for the shell correction at finite excitation energy, δE(E*) = δE(0)exp(-E*/Ed), is inaccurate both at small and large excitations; instead, the shell correction to the free energy remains nearly constant below the critical temperature where pairing vanishes, and only then decays approximately exponentially.

## Method

The authors calculated the shell correction to the energy and free energy directly from their formal definitions using the Strutinsky method for independent particles and the BCS approximation for pairing, for a broad range of nuclei and deformations. They averaged the ratio δF(T)/δF(0) over more than 1000 points in the deformation space of 236U and other nuclei along the beta-stability line, then fitted the average temperature dependence with analytic parameterizations. The pairing effects were treated separately, with the pairing gap and chemical potential determined from the gap equation and particle number conservation at each temperature.

## Key numbers

- For 236U, the fit parameters for the free-energy damping function Φ(E*) = (e^{-E1/E0} - 1)/(e^{(E*-E1)/E0} - 1) are E0 = 42.28 MeV and E1 = -18.54 MeV.
- The critical temperature for pairing vanishes approximately as Tcrit ≈ 7.37 MeV / √A, based on calculations for spherical nuclei with 50 ≤ A ≤ 250 along the beta-stability line.
- The level density parameter averaged over deformations is approximated by ã ≈ (0.0984 A - 0.253 A^{2/3} + 2.07 A^{1/3} - 4.04) MeV^{-1}.
- The fitted parameter δS0 for the entropy correction is δS0 = 2.5 MeV^{-1} for 236U, with the A-dependent polynomial δS0 ≈ 8.65 - 6.29 A^{1/3} + 1.45 A^{2/3} - 0.0949 A.
- The damping parameter Ed in the exponential approximation varies in the literature from Ed = 15 MeV (Capote) to Ed = 60 MeV (Randrup and Moller), compared to the authors' more accurate parameterization.

## Context

This work extends the direct calculations of temperature-dependent shell corrections by Adeev (1972, 1973), Moretto (1972), and Bohr and Mottelson (1975), and contradicts the simple exponential damping formula of Ignatyuk (1975) that is widely used in macroscopic-microscopic models such as those of Moller et al. (1995, 2000). The results enable more accurate treatment of shell and pairing effects in Langevin simulations of fission fragment distributions, as demonstrated by the authors' comparison with the Randrup and Moller (2011) approach, and are particularly relevant for understanding neutron multiplicity dependence on fragment mass at low excitation energies below 10 MeV.
