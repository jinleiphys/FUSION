# Gamow-Teller strength distributions in fp-shell nuclei

> We use the shell model Monte Carlo method to calculate complete 0f1p-shell response functions for Gamow-Teller (GT) operators and obtain the corresponding strength distributions using a Maximum Entropy technique. The approach is validated against direct diagonalization for 48Ti. Calculated GT strength distributions agree well with data from (n,p) and (p,n) reactions for nuclei with A=48-64. We also calculate the temperature evolution of the GT+ distributions for representative nuclei and find that the GT+ distributions broaden and the centroids shift to lower energies with increasing temperature.

# Gamow-Teller strength distributions in fp-shell nuclei

## Key claim

The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method combined with a Maximum Entropy inversion technique can reproduce experimentally observed Gamow-Teller strength distributions for nuclei in the fp-shell (A=48-64), but only when a universal renormalization factor of 1/1.26 is applied to the GT operator; the method recovers only gross features of the strength distribution compared to direct diagonalization, with the peak somewhat too narrow due to information loss in the Laplace transform.

## Method

The authors used the shell model Monte Carlo method to calculate complete 0f1p-shell response functions for Gamow-Teller operators in the full 0hw fp-shell model space, employing the KB3 residual interaction. They extracted strength distributions from the response functions via an inverse Laplace transform implemented using a Maximum Entropy technique with an iterative linear programming procedure, using a Gaussian default model for GT+ distributions and a two-Gaussian default model for GT- distributions. The SMMC calculations were performed at finite temperature for odd-A nuclei (T=0.8 MeV) and at various temperatures for even-even nuclei, with the response functions sampled from partition functions of neighboring even-even systems for odd-A cases.

## Key numbers

- Total GT+ strength for 48Ti: SMMC gives B(GT+) = 0.72 ± 0.11, compared to direct diagonalization value of 0.79
- Experimental B(GT+) for 48Ti sums to 1.42 ± 0.2, but the calculated 0hw GT+ strength extends only over E* < 8 MeV, where the experimental value is 0.77 ± 0.1
- For 58Ni GT- distribution: experimental integrated strength between 6.4 and 13.0 MeV is 5.5, SMMC obtains 6.1
- For 60Ni GT- distribution: experimental total strength is 7.2 ± 1.8, SMMC obtains 10.87 ± 0.23; between 4.0 and 14.0 MeV, experiment quotes 6.22, SMMC obtains 4.65
- The renormalized Ikeda sum rule B(GT-) - B(GT+) = 3(N-Z)/(1.26)^2 is obeyed by both SMMC and direct diagonalization
- Error in centroid position estimated at about 0.5 MeV from SMMC error bars on R(tau)
- Average deviation of calculated binding energies from experimental values ranges from 0.1 MeV (A=48) to 0.5 MeV (A=54)

## Context

This work extends earlier SMMC calculations of ground-state and finite-temperature properties of mid-fp shell nuclei (Alhassid 1994, Dean 1994, Langanke 1995, Dean 1995) to the dynamical problem of Gamow-Teller strength distributions. It validates the SMMC+MaxEnt approach against direct diagonalization for 48Ti (Caurier 1994) and demonstrates that the experimentally observed quenching of total GT strength is consistently reproduced by full fp-shell correlations with the universal spin-operator renormalization factor of 0.8 (equivalently 1/1.26) previously established in sd-shell (Wildenthal 1988) and lighter fp-shell nuclei (Caurier 1996, Poves 1997). The results enable reliable theoretical predictions of GT+ distributions for astrophysically important nuclei in presupernova collapse simulations, where electron capture rates depend sensitively on the full distribution rather than just total strength, and where experimental data are not accessible for the relevant nuclei.
