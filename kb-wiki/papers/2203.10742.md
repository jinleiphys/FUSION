# Particle production at large $p_T$ in Xe+Xe collisions with jet quenching using the higher twist approach

> The production of $π^0$, $η$, and $ϕ$ in the most central (0%-5%) Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV is investigated in the framework of the perturbative QCD (pQCD) improved parton model at an accuracy of next-to-leading order (NLO). The jet quenching effect is effectively incorporated by medium-modified fragmentation functions via the higher-twist approach. Predictions of the nuclear modification factors of $π^0$, $η$, and $ϕ$ as functions of the transverse momentum $p_{\rm T}$ are made with the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}_0$, which is extracted from the available experimental data of charged hadrons provided by ALICE and CMS. The particle ratios $η/π^0,\ ϕ/π^0$ as functions of $p_{\rm T}$ in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV as well as in 0%-5% Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV are also presented. The numerical simulations of the scaled ratios of charged hadron production in the Xe+Xe 5.44 TeV system over those in the Pb+Pb 5.02 TeV system give a good description of the CMS data, and the scaled ratios of $π^0$, $η$, and $ϕ$ production coincide with the curve of charged hadron production.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the nuclear modification factors of π⁰, η, and φ mesons in central Xe+Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV are slightly less suppressed than those in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, with the caveat that the jet transport parameter q̂₀ is extracted from charged hadron data and then applied to these mesons, and that theoretical uncertainties from nuclear PDFs, fragmentation functions, and scale variations are not included in the χ² fit.

## Method
The authors use the perturbative QCD improved parton model at next-to-leading order (NLO) with medium-modified fragmentation functions via the higher-twist approach to incorporate jet quenching. The jet transport parameter q̂₀ is extracted by fitting the nuclear modification factor RAA of charged hadrons to CMS and ALICE data in 0%-5% central Xe+Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV using a χ²/d.o.f minimization. The space-time evolution of the QCD medium is described by the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, and initial-state cold nuclear matter effects are included via EPPS16 NLO nuclear PDFs.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit jet transport parameter in Xe+Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV: q̂₀ = 1.0 GeV²/fm, extracted from charged hadron RAA data.
- Best-fit jet transport parameter in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV: q̂₀ = 1.5 GeV²/fm, extracted from charged hadron RAA data.
- Initial temperature in Xe+Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV: T₀ = 484 MeV at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, estimated by CLVisc.
- Initial temperature in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV: T₀ = 502 MeV at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, estimated by CLVisc.
- Nuclear radii: ²⁰⁷Pb ≈ 6.62 fm, ¹²⁹Xe ≈ 5.36 fm.
- Total pp cross sections: σpp at 5.44 TeV = (68.4 ± 0.5) mb; σpp at 5.02 TeV = (67.6 ± 0.6) mb.
- Particle ratio η/π⁰ in both p+p and A+A collisions reaches a constant of approximately 0.5 at large pT.
- Particle ratio φ/π⁰ in p+p collisions is approximately constant at about 0.1 as pT increases.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same group on identified hadron production in heavy-ion collisions (Dai 2015, Dai 2017, Ma 2018) from Au+Au at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV to the intermediate-size Xe+Xe system at 5.44 TeV. The comparison between Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb at similar collision energies isolates the system-size (path-length) dependence of jet quenching, as proposed by CMS (2018). The predictions for π⁰, η, and φ enable future experimental tests of whether the path-length effect is independent of hadron species, and the extracted q̂₀ values can be compared with the JET Collaboration (2013) results for the temperature dependence of q̂/T³.
