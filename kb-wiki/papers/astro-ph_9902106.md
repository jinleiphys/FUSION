# An Active-Sterile Neutrino Transformation Solution for r-Process Nucleosynthesis

> We discuss how matter-enhanced active-sterile neutrino transformation in both neutrino and antineutrino channels could enable the production of the rapid neutron capture (r-process) nuclei in neutrino-heated supernova ejecta. In this scheme the lightest sterile neutrino would be heavier than the electron neutrino and split from it by a vacuum mass-squared difference roughly between 3 and 70 eV$^2$ and vacuum mixing angle given by $\sin^2 2θ_{es} > 10^{-4}$.

## Key claim

Matter-enhanced active-sterile neutrino transformation in both neutrino and antineutrino channels could enable r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-heated supernova ejecta, provided the lightest sterile neutrino is heavier than the electron neutrino with a vacuum mass-squared difference between 3 and 70 eV² and vacuum mixing angle sin²2θ_es > 10⁻⁴. The solution makes r-process production robust to a wide range of uncertainties in neutrino-driven wind models, but the authors note that a completely self-consistent model may not be available for some time and that their results depend on representative outflow conditions.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional neutrino-driven wind model with constant expansion timescale, adopting density and electron fraction profiles fitted to the Mayle and Wilson supernova code results. They solved the Schroedinger-like neutrino amplitude evolution equation numerically (without using the Landau-Zener approximation) for active-sterile mixing in both ν_e ⇌ ν_s and ν̄_e ⇌ ν̄_s channels, including the nonlinear feedback from neutrino-neutrino forward exchange scattering and the coupled evolution of the electron fraction through charged-current reactions ν_e + n ⇌ p + e⁻ and ν̄_e + p ⇌ n + e⁺.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum mass-squared splitting range: 3 eV² ≲ δm²_es ≲ 70 eV²
- Vacuum mixing angle condition: sin²2θ_es > 10⁻⁴
- Expansion timescale range investigated: τ = 0.1 s to 0.9 s
- Entropy per baryon used: S₁₀₀ = 1 (i.e., 100 times Boltzmann's constant)
- Neutron star mass assumed: M_NS ≈ 1.4 M_⊙
- Mass outflow rate implied: Ṁ ∼ 10⁻⁶ M_⊙ s⁻¹ for τ ∼ a few tenths of a second and S₁₀₀ ∼ 1
- Temperature threshold for alpha particle formation: T < 700 keV
- Temperature threshold for neutron capture on seed nuclei: T < 300 keV
- Each nucleon must suffer ∼10 neutrino interactions (each ∼10 MeV) to be ejected to infinity

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of active-active neutrino mixing in supernova outflows by Qian et al. (1993), Qian and Fuller (1995), and Sigl (1995) to the active-sterile channel, and builds on schematic suggestions by Pellett (1997) that did not include expansion feedback. The paper addresses the neutron-to-seed nucleus deficit problem identified by Meyer (1993) and the alpha effect problem described by Meyer, McLaughlin, and Fuller (1998), which previous general relativistic solutions by Qian and Woosley (1996) and Cardall and Fuller (1997) could only resolve with fine tuning. The results enable future work connecting sterile neutrino properties to heavy element nucleosynthesis as a complementary constraint to accelerator experiments like LSND and Superkamiokande.
