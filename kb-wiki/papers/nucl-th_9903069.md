# On the Impossibility to Measure the Total Neutron- and Proton Induced Nonmesonic Decays for Hypertriton

> Based on realistic calculations for the nonmesonic decay rate of Hypertriton we demonstrate, that in principle it is not possible to measure the total n- and p- induced decay rates and as a consequence $Γ_n /Γ_p$ for that lightest hypernucleus.For the nonmesonic decay process the calculations are performed with modern YN forces based on various meson exchanges and taking the final state interaction among the three nucleons fully into account. Our findings have consequences also for the interpretation of experimental $Γ_n/Γ_p$ ratios for heavier hypernuclei where severe discrepancies exist to theoretical $Γ_n/Γ_p$ ratios.

## Key claim

Based on rigorous Faddeev calculations for the hypertriton (³ΛH), the authors demonstrate that it is in principle impossible to measure the total neutron-induced (Γₙ) and proton-induced (Γₚ) nonmesonic decay rates separately, and consequently the ratio Γₙ/Γₚ, because the two processes populate overlapping regions of phase space and interfere coherently.

## Method

The authors solved rigorous Faddeev equations for the hypertriton bound state and three-nucleon final scattering states, using the Nijmegen YN potential (including Λ-Σ conversion) and the Nijmegen '93 NN potential. They computed single differential decay rates dΓ/dEₙ and dΓ/dEₚ for the nonmesonic decay channel, separating the coherent total rate into contributions labeled as n-induced and p-induced, both with full final-state interactions (FULL) and in a symmetrized plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIAS) where final-state interactions were neglected.

## Key numbers

- Total nonmesonic decay rate for hypertriton with full final-state interactions: Γ_FULL = 0.57 × 10⁸ s⁻¹
- Neutron-induced rate: Γₙ_FULL = 0.17 × 10⁸ s⁻¹; proton-induced rate: Γₚ_FULL = 0.39 × 10⁸ s⁻¹; their sum (0.56 × 10⁸ s⁻¹) agrees with the coherent total to within about 2%
- Theoretical ratio Γₙ/Γₚ for hypertriton = 0.44
- For the n+d decay channel: Γₙ^(n+d) + Γₚ^(n+d) = 0.39 × 10⁷ s⁻¹, while the total n+d decay rate Γ^(n+d) = 0.66 × 10⁷ s⁻¹, showing strong interference
- In PWIAS, the sum dΓₙ/dEₙ + dΓₚ/dEₙ equals dΓ/dEₙ within 5%; in FULL the agreement is within about 12%
- For proton energy integration: at Eₚ ≈ 60 MeV the relative deviation |Γₚ(Eₚ) − Γ(Eₚ)|/Γₚ(Eₚ) is about 10%, increasing to about 20% at Eₚ ≈ 15 MeV, and up to 30% at lower energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior rigorous Faddeev calculations for hypertriton decays (Golak et al., 1997) to address the longstanding discrepancy between experimental Γₙ/Γₚ ratios (typically near 1) and theoretical values (0.05–0.2) for heavier hypernuclei, as reviewed by Ramos et al. (1998). The results show that even for the lightest hypernucleus, where exact calculations are possible, the standard experimental method of extracting Γₙ via Γₙ = Γₙₘ − Γₚ is invalid due to interference, and that Γₚ estimates from single-proton spectra carry uncertainties of at least 30%. This enables future comparisons of differential energy distributions dΓ/dEₚ and dΓ/dEₙ between theory and experiment for hypertriton, and motivates similar rigorous calculations for four-body hypernuclei (⁴ΛH, ⁴ΛHe) where data already exist from Outa (1998) and Zeps (1998).
