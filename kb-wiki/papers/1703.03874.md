# Two-photon exchange in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We review recent theoretical and experimental progress on the role of two-photon exchange (TPE) in electron-proton scattering at low to moderate momentum transfers. We make a detailed comparison and analysis of the results of competing experiments on the ratio of e+p to e-p elastic scattering cross sections, and of the theoretical calculations describing them. A summary of the current experimental situation is provided, along with an outlook for future experiments.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections beyond the standard model-independent radiative corrections can resolve the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor ratio, but the TPE effect is a few percent correction to cross sections and is most prominent at high momentum transfer and backward scattering angles where the cross section is suppressed.

## Method

The authors review theoretical calculations of TPE corrections using one-loop integration techniques with hadronic intermediate states (elastic nucleon and inelastic Delta resonance), evaluating the box and crossed-box diagrams with hadronic form factors fitted to empirical transition data. They also describe dispersive methods that relate the real and imaginary parts of the TPE amplitude, and compare these calculations to experimental measurements of the e+p to e-p elastic scattering cross section ratio from the VEPP-3, CLAS, and OLYMPUS experiments.

## Key numbers

- The elastic nucleon TPE correction ranges from approximately +1.5% at low Q2 to -6% at Q2 = 6 GeV2, with the most significant corrections at small epsilon (longitudinal photon polarization)
- The Delta(1232) resonance contribution to TPE is smaller and of opposite sign to the nucleon contribution, attenuating the nucleon contribution somewhat
- The gamma N to Delta transition form factor g1 is well approximated by a dipole with mass parameter Lambda_Delta = 0.76 GeV over the range 0 < Q2 < 3 GeV2
- The static limit for a structureless massive target gives delta_N approaching alpha*pi/(x+1) as Q2 goes to 0, where x = sqrt((1+eps)/(1-eps))
- Polarization transfer measurements have determined the ratio mu_p*G_E/G_M up to Q2 = 8.5 GeV2, showing an approximately linear decrease with Q2

## Context

This review extends the earlier reviews by Carlson and Vanderhaeghen (2007) and Arrington, Blunden, and Melnitchouk (2011), incorporating significant theoretical progress in TPE calculations over the past five years and new experimental results from VEPP-3, CLAS, and OLYMPUS. The work addresses the "proton form factor puzzle" where Rosenbluth separation measurements (consistent with Q2 scaling of G_E approx G_M/mu_p) disagree with polarization transfer measurements (showing a linear decrease of the ratio with Q2), and also relates to the "proton radius problem" where electron scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy disagree with muonic atom spectroscopy. The results enable improved extraction of proton form factors from electron scattering data and inform future experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and elsewhere.
