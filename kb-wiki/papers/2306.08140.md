# Neutron star matter based on a parity doublet model including the $a_0(980)$ meson

> We study the effect of the isovector-scalar meson $a_0$(980) on the properties of nuclear matter and the neutron star (NS) matter by constructing a parity doublet model with including the $a_0$ meson based on the chiral SU(2)$_L\times$SU(2)$_R$ symmetry. We also include the $ω$-$ρ$ mixing contribution to adjust the slope parameter at the saturation. We find that, when the chiral invariant mass of nucleon $m_0$ is smaller than about 800 MeV, the existence of $a_0$(980) enlarges the symmetry energy by strengthening the repulsive $ρ$ meson coupling. On the other hand, for large $m_0$ where the Yukawa coupling of $a_0$(980) to nucleon is small, the symmetry energy is reduced by the effect of $ω$-$ρ$ mixing. We then construct the equation of state (EoS) of a neutron star matter to obtain the mass-radius relation of NS. We find that, in most choices of $m_0$, the existence of $a_0$(980) stiffens the EoS and makes the radius of NS larger. We then constrain the chiral invariant mass of nucleon from the observational data of NS, and find that $580 \,\text{ MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860 \,\text{ MeV} $ for $L_0=57.7$ MeV.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector-scalar $a_0(980)$ meson in a parity doublet model affects the symmetry energy in opposite directions depending on the chiral invariant mass $m_0$: for $m_0$ smaller than about 800 MeV the symmetry energy is enlarged, while for large $m_0$ (where the Yukawa coupling of $a_0(980)$ to nucleon is small) the symmetry energy is reduced by the effect of $\omega$-$\rho$ mixing.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral SU(2)$_L \times$ SU(2)$_R$ parity doublet model including the $a_0(980)$ meson as an isovector-scalar field, with vector mesons ($\omega$ and $\rho$) introduced via hidden local symmetry and an $\omega$-$\rho$ mixing term included to adjust the slope parameter at saturation. They applied the mean-field approximation to derive the hadronic equation of state, then constructed a unified neutron star equation of state by interpolating between the hadronic EoS from the parity doublet model and a quark matter EoS from an NJL-type quark model following a crossover prescription. The mass-radius relation was obtained and compared to observational neutron star data to constrain the chiral invariant mass $m_0$.

## Key numbers

* The chiral invariant mass of the nucleon is constrained to $580 \text{ MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860 \text{ MeV}$ for a slope parameter $L_0 = 57.7$ MeV.
* The saturation properties used to determine model parameters are: saturation density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, binding energy $B_0 = 16$ MeV, incompressibility $K_0 = 240$ MeV, symmetry energy $S_0 = 31$ MeV, and slope parameter $L_0 = 40-80$ MeV.
* The Yukawa coupling of the $a_0$ meson to nucleon decreases as $m_0$ increases, with values ranging from $g_1 = 9.02$, $g_2 = 15.47$ at $m_0 = 500$ MeV to $g_1 = 5.96$, $g_2 = 12.41$ at $m_0 = 900$ MeV.
* The $\omega$-$\rho$ mixing parameter $\lambda_{\omega \rho}$ for the model with $a_0(980)$ and $\lambda'_6 = 0$ ranges from 0.0092 (at $m_0 = 500$ MeV, $L_0 = 80$ MeV) to 2.4595 (at $m_0 = 900$ MeV, $L_0 = 40$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous parity doublet model studies of neutron star matter (PhysRevC.103.045205, PhysRevC.106.065205, Minamikawa:2023eky) by including the isovector-scalar $a_0(980)$ meson, which had previously been studied only in Walecka-type relativistic mean-field models (Kubis 1997, Miyatsu 2022, Li 2022) and density-dependent RMF models (PhysRevC.90.055801, PhysRevC.84.054309). The constraint on $m_0$ obtained here ($580 \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 860$ MeV for $L_0=57.7$ MeV) updates earlier constraints from the same group, which were $600 \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 900$ MeV (PhysRevC.103.045205) and later $400 \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 700$ MeV (PhysRevC.106.065205, Minamikawa:2023eky) when including anomaly effects and new data. The results enable further study of how isovector-scalar mesons affect the symmetry energy and neutron star properties within chiral effective models.
