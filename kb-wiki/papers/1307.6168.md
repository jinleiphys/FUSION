# Decisive test of color coherence in proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC

> Proton-nucleus collisions (p+A) at LHC energies provide a rigorous test of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC), a model proposed to describe the high energy limit of Quantum Chromodynamics. In the CGC the average multiplicity of charged particles at midrapidity in p+A collisions depends logarithmically on the number of participants, $N_{part}$. In contrast, the Wounded Nucleon Model (WNM) of independent nucleon-nucleon scatterings, verified at RHIC energies, predicts that multiplicity in p+A depends linearly on $N_{part}$. We argue that for the first time, the dependence of mean multiplicity on $N_{part}$ in p+A collisions at LHC energies can single out a model of particle production, thus offering a stringent test of the CGC and the WNM. Based on this observation we propose a novel experimental test of color coherence in p+A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dependence of mean charged-particle multiplicity at midrapidity on the number of participants ($N_{\text{part}}$) in proton-nucleus collisions at LHC energies can distinguish between the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) and the Wounded Nucleon Model (WNM), but the authors add a note that preliminary ATLAS data appear close to linear (favoring the WNM), with the caveat that this conclusion is sensitive to how $N_{\text{part}}$ is extracted.

## Method

The authors compare the CGC prediction (logarithmic dependence of mean multiplicity on $N_{\text{part}}$) with the WNM prediction (linear dependence) for p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV. They compute the CGC curve numerically using the rcBK (running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov) model, with initial conditions from global fits to deep inelastic scattering data by the AAMQS collaboration, and verify the result with the IP-Glasma model and the KLN model. They also generate probability distributions of $N_{\text{part}}$ using a standard Glauber Monte-Carlo model with a Gaussian inelastic nucleon-nucleon profile and an inelastic cross section of 70 mb.

## Key numbers

- In central p+Pb collisions, corresponding roughly to $N_{\text{part}} > 18$, there is approximately a factor of 2 difference between the CGC and WNM predictions for mean multiplicity at midrapidity.
- At $N_{\text{ch}} = 140$ produced charged particles at midrapidity, the mean number of participants differs by approximately 50% between the two models, being larger in the WNM than in the CGC.
- The Glauber Monte-Carlo calculation uses an inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section of $\sigma = 70$ mb for p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV.
- The WNM uses $\langle n_{\text{ch}}^{\text{pp}} \rangle = 5$ and $k_{\text{pp}} = 1.1$ for the negative binomial distribution of particle production per participant.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of the CGC and WNM previously studied in d+Au collisions at RHIC by Kharzeev (2002) and Bialas (2004), where both models could describe the data. The paper proposes a novel experimental test using the relation between average $N_{\text{part}}$ and $N_{\text{ch}}$ at midrapidity, which can be measured via particles in the nucleus fragmentation region or high-transverse-momentum particles. This enables a decisive test of color coherence effects in p+A collisions at the LHC, with the note that preliminary ATLAS data suggest the WNM may be applicable, though the extraction of $N_{\text{part}}$ remains a sensitive issue.
