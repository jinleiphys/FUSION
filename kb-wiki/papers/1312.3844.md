# Accurate determination of the interaction between $Λ$ hyperons and nucleons from auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations

> An accurate assessment of the hyperon-nucleon interaction is of great interest in view of recent observations of very massive neutron stars. The challenge is to build a realistic interaction that can be used over a wide range of masses and in infinite matter starting from the available experimental data on the binding energy of light hypernuclei. To this end, accurate calculations of the hyperon binding energy in a hypernucleus are necessary. We present a quantum Monte Carlo study of $Λ$ and $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei up to $A=91$. We investigate the contribution of two- and three-body $Λ$-nucleon forces to the $Λ$ binding energy. Ground state energies are computed solving the Schrödinger equation for non-relativistic baryons by means of the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm extended to the hypernuclear sector. We show that a simple adjustment of the parameters of the $ΛNN$ three-body force yields a very good agreement with available experimental data over a wide range of hypernuclear masses. In some cases no experiments have been performed yet, and we give new predictions. The newly fitted $ΛNN$ force properly describes the physics of medium-heavy $Λ$ hypernuclei, correctly reproducing the saturation property of the hyperon separation energy.

## Key claim

The authors show that a simple adjustment of the parameters of the three-body ΛNN force yields good agreement with experimental Λ separation energies across a wide range of hypernuclear masses, but only when the three-body force is included; the two-body ΛN force alone cannot reproduce the saturation property of the hyperon separation energy.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations for single and double Λ hypernuclei up to total baryon number A=91, solving the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for systems of nucleons and Λ hyperons. They used the Argonne AV4' potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions, an Urbana-type ΛN potential with charge symmetry breaking, a three-body ΛNN potential with P-wave, S-wave, and dispersive contributions, and a Gaussian parametrization of the Nijmegen F interaction for ΛΛ forces. The three-body ΛNN parameters were refitted to reproduce experimental Λ separation energies, with CS fixed at 1.50 MeV while CP and WD were varied.

## Key numbers

- The experimental Λ separation energy for 5ΛHe is BΛ = 3.12(2) MeV (Juric 1973)
- The NAGARA event gave a double Λ separation energy for 6ΛΛHe of 7.25 ± 0.19 +0.18/-0.11 MeV, later revised to 6.93 ± 0.16 MeV (Nakazawa 2010) and then to 6.91 ± 0.16 MeV (Ahn 2013)
- The three-body ΛNN parameter set (I) used CP = 0.60 MeV, CS = 0.00 MeV, and WD = 0.015 MeV
- The CS parameter was fixed at 1.50 MeV following Usmani (2008)
- The ΛN potential parameters include vs = 6.33, 6.28 MeV, vt = 6.09, 6.04 MeV, v̄ = 6.15(5) MeV, vσ = 0.24 MeV, and Cτ = -0.050(5) MeV
- The ΛΛ Gaussian potential parameters include v0(1) = -10.67 MeV, v0(2) = -93.51 MeV, v0(3) = 4884 MeV with size parameters μ(k) = 0.555, 1.656, 8.163 fm-2

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo studies of hypernuclei by Lonardoni (2013) and variational Monte Carlo calculations by Bodmer, Usmani, and Carlson (1984-2008) that used similar ΛN and ΛNN interactions for light hypernuclei up to 17ΛO. The authors show that the three-body ΛNN force is necessary to reproduce the saturation of the Λ separation energy in medium-heavy hypernuclei, which was not achieved with two-body forces alone. This enables more reliable predictions for hypernuclear binding energies across a wide mass range, which can be used to constrain hyperon-nucleon interactions for applications to neutron star physics.
