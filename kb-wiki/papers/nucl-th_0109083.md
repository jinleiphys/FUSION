# Local Density Approximation for Pairing Correlations in Nuclei

> We introduce a natural and simple to implement renormalization scheme of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for the case of zero range pairing interaction. This renormalization scheme proves to be equivalent to a simple energy cut-off with a position or density dependent running coupling constant. Subsequently, we present self-consistent HFB calculations with a full consideration of the continuum of long chains of spherical tin and lead nuclei (essentially from one drip line to another) using the SLy5 interaction. In the pairing channel we use a zero range interaction, treated by means of this new regularization scheme.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a renormalization scheme for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations with zero-range pairing interactions that is equivalent to a simple energy cutoff with a position- or density-dependent running coupling constant, but the authors caution that the effective coupling constant depends on the local Fermi momentum and mean-field potential, leading to a stronger pairing interaction inside nuclear matter than outside, in contrast to the behavior obtained with a vacuum renormalization scheme.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate that the anomalous density matrix diverges as 1/|r₁−r₂| when the pairing field is local, using both infinite homogeneous matter and finite systems. They then develop a regularization procedure that introduces an explicit energy cutoff E_c and uses a Thomas-Fermi approximation for the HF propagator to construct a regulator, yielding a position-dependent effective coupling constant g_eff(r). Self-consistent HFB calculations are performed for long chains of spherical tin and lead nuclei (from one drip line to the other) using the SLy5 interaction in the mean-field channel and the regularized zero-range interaction in the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length for two neutrons is a = −18.5 fm (used as an analogy for the Cooper pair size).
- The Cooper pair rms radius in infinite matter is ℏ²k_F/mΔ, which significantly exceeds the interparticle separation 1/k_F in the weak coupling limit (Δ ≪ ε_F).
- The cutoff energy E_c must be of order ℏ²k_F²/2m (the Fermi energy) to ensure the regularized result is independent of E_c.
- The effective coupling constant g_eff(r) is defined via 1/g_eff(r) = 1/g − (m k_c(r))/(2π²ℏ²) [1 − (k_F(r))/(2k_c(r)) ln((k_c(r)+k_F(r))/(k_c(r)−k_F(r)))], where k_c(r) is the local momentum cutoff derived from E_c and the mean-field potential U(r).

## Context

This work extends the local density approximation (LDA) for pairing correlations in nuclei, building on the density functional theory ideas of Hohenberg-Kohn (1964) and Negele (1970) as well as the Kohn-Sham approach. It directly addresses the divergence problem identified by Bulgac (2000) and Bruun et al. (2000) for zero-range pairing interactions, and provides an alternative to the finite-range Gogny interaction approach used by Ring (1980) and others. The regularization scheme enables self-consistent HFB calculations for nuclei from one drip line to another, which was previously difficult with existing methods that required a 1–1 correspondence between HF and HFB states near the Fermi surface.
