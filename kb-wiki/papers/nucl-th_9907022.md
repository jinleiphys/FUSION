# Global features of proton-neutron interactions and symmetry energy

> We study global features of proton-neutron (p-n) interactions and symmetry energy over a wide range of nuclei, using a schematic model interaction with four forces proposed recently. Calculations are performed by the BCS approximation in N,Z=20-50 and N,Z=50-82 regions. The experimental double differences of binding energies and symmetry energy are reproduced quite well. It is shown that the isoscalar p-n interactions with all J are indispensable for explaining the binding energies of not only $N\approx Z$ but also N>Z nuclei in the A=40-160 region.

# Global features of proton-neutron interactions and symmetry energy

## Key claim

The systematic behaviors of double differences of binding energies and symmetry energy across a wide range of nuclei (A=40-160) can be reproduced by a schematic Hamiltonian with four forces within the BCS approximation, provided the isoscalar proton-neutron force strength takes the form k⁰ = ζ/A (1.0 − η/A¹/³), with region-dependent parameters η. The J-independent isoscalar p-n interaction is indispensable for explaining binding energies not only in N≈Z nuclei but also in N>Z nuclei far from the N=Z line.

## Method

The authors employed a schematic Hamiltonian consisting of a monopole pairing force (P₀), a quadrupole-quadrupole force (QQ), a quadrupole pairing force (P₂), and a J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force (H⁺⁼⁰_πν). Calculations were performed using the BCS approximation in two model spaces: the fpg shell (1f₇/₂, 2p₃/₂, 1f₅/₂, 2p₁/₂, 1g₉/₂) for N,Z=20-50 and the sdgh shell (2d₅/₂, 1g₇/₂, 3s₁/₂, 2d₃/₂, 1h₁₁/₂) for N,Z=50-82. The four force parameters were fixed by fitting experimental odd-even mass differences, lowest 2⁺ energies, and the observed 40/A trend of double differences of binding energies.

## Key numbers

- The average curve of the double differences of binding energies δV⁽¹⁾ and δV⁽²⁾ follows 40/A (MeV) across the mass region A=16-210.
- The isoscalar p-n force strength is parameterized as k⁰ = 224.0/A (1.0 − 2.2/A¹/³) for the N,Z=20-50 region and k⁰ = 224.0/A (1.0 − 3.0/A¹/³) for the N,Z=50-82 region.
- The monopole pairing force strength is g₀ = 24A⁻¹ for both regions; the quadrupole force strength is χ = 350A⁻⁵/³b⁻⁴ for N,Z=20-50 and χ = 450A⁻⁵/³b⁻⁴ for N,Z=50-82.
- The quadrupole pairing force strength is g₂ = 0.2g₀.
- The single-particle energy for the f₇/₂ orbital is ε_f₇/₂ − λ = −8.3633 MeV from the experimental binding energies of ⁴¹Ca and ⁴⁰Ca.
- The single-particle energy for the d₅/₂ orbital is ε_d₅/₂ − λ = −10.265 MeV from the experimental binding energies of ¹⁰³Sn and ¹⁰²Sn.
- In the SO(5) limit, the approximate symmetry energy coefficient is ã_sym = 31(1.0 − 1.987/A¹/³) MeV, which agrees with the empirical value 30.59(1.0 − 1.76/A¹/³) from Seeger.
- The contributions to ã_sym in the A=60 system are 12.3 MeV from the isoscalar p-n force and 3.0 MeV from the J=0 isovector pairing force.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-j shell model analysis (Kaneko, 1999) for N≈Z nuclei in the f₇/₂ and g₉/₂ subshells to a many-j shell BCS calculation covering N,Z=20-50 and N,Z=50-82 regions. The study confirms that the J-independent isoscalar p-n interaction, rather than only the J=1 isoscalar component as suggested by earlier work (Poves, 1998; Satula, 1997), is essential for reproducing the symmetry energy and Wigner energy across a wide mass range. The empirical mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients of Seeger (1969) and Duflo and Zucker (1995) are reproduced, and the residual p-n interaction between the last proton and neutron in odd-odd nuclei is identified as arising from the QQ force, consistent with the suggestion by Bohr and Mottelson of an attractive residual p-n interaction of about 20/A between the last odd nucleons.
