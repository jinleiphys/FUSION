# The Onset of Chaos with a Quadrupole--Quadrupole Interaction

> The transition from order to chaos in atomic nuclei has been studied analytically and numerically using a quadrupole--quadrupole residual interaction. This interaction leads to chaotic behaviour, but the critical energy $E_C\simeq 12.6$ MeV, corresponding to the onset of chaos, is higher than that of the experimental one.

## Key claim

The quadrupole–quadrupole residual interaction can produce a transition from order to chaos in a schematic two-nucleon model, but the critical energy for the onset of chaos is approximately 12.6 MeV, which is higher than the experimentally observed onset of chaotic behavior in atomic nuclei (around 8 MeV).

## Method

The authors studied a two-nucleon system moving in a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator mean field with a separable quadrupole–quadrupole residual interaction. They applied the curvature criterion of the potential energy surface to analytically estimate the critical energy for the onset of chaos, and they performed classical trajectory calculations using a fourth-order Runge–Kutta method to produce Poincaré sections at various energies. Quantum mechanical energy levels were obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the harmonic oscillator occupation number basis, and the distribution of nearest-neighbor level spacings was compared to the Brody distribution to quantify the degree of chaos.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy for the onset of chaos: \(E_C \simeq 12.6\) MeV, derived from the minimum of the zero-curvature line of the potential energy surface.
- The four saddle points of the potential energy occur at \(V = \epsilon^2 / \chi \simeq 23.3\) MeV.
- For the quantum calculation, the Brody parameter \(q = 0\) for energies \(0 \leq E \leq 12\) MeV (Poisson distribution, regular motion) and \(q = 0.15\) for energies \(12 \leq E \leq 23\) MeV (slight deviation toward chaos).
- The single-particle energy \(\epsilon = \hbar\omega \simeq 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV and the interaction strength \(\chi \simeq 74 A^{-2/3}\) MeV, with \(A\) the mass number.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the transition from order to chaos in atomic nuclei, where regular states are observed near the ground state (0–3 MeV) and chaotic states near the neutron emission threshold (6–8 MeV) as reported in experimental spectroscopy (references [5,6] in the paper). The authors note that previous work by other researchers (references [7]) showed that a surface delta interaction produces chaotic behavior while a pairing plus quadrupole force does not. The present result shows that the quadrupole–quadrupole interaction alone cannot reproduce the experimental onset energy, suggesting that higher multipole components must be added to the residual interaction to match the observed chaotic threshold.
