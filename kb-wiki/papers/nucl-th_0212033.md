# About some relativistic effects in the A(d,p)X reaction with the proton emission in a forward direction

> In a nonrelativistic quantum theory the longitudinal and transversal components of the internal momentum in the deuteron wave function (DWF) are bound together as they have to form the well known superposition of the S- and D-waves. In a relativistic case -- at least in light cone dynamics -- the dependence of the DWF on the longitudinal and transversal momenta can by considerably changed, because these components turn out in large part to be bound in another way. Previously such a possibility was pointed out by Blankenbecler et al. Later Karmanov et al. have developed a quantitative approach that can be used to describe this situation. Here we present some results of calculations of the tensor analyzing power of (d,p) reaction at relativistic deuteron momenta using Karmanov's DWF. The momentum behaviour of the tensor analyzing power obtained is compared with that calculated with standard DWFs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using Karmanov's relativistic deuteron wave function (DWF) in light-cone dynamics, the calculated tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}\) of the \(A(\vec d,p)X\) reaction at \(0^\circ\) does not cross the horizontal axis and agrees reasonably well with experimental data for internal momenta \(k\) from 0.4 to 0.8 GeV/\(c\), unlike calculations with standard nonrelativistic DWFs.

## Method

The authors calculated the tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}\) of the deuteron breakup reaction \(A(\vec d,p)X\) at relativistic deuteron momenta using Karmanov's relativistic DWF, which depends on both longitudinal and transverse internal momenta and includes an additional dynamical variable \(\omega^\mu\) (the normal to the light cone surface). The results were compared with calculations using standard nonrelativistic Paris and Bonn DWFs in the relativistic impulse approximation, for proton emission at \(0^\circ\) in the laboratory system.

## Key numbers

- The tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}(0^\circ)\) calculated with Karmanov's DWF shows reasonable agreement with experimental data in the region of internal momentum \(k\) from 0.4 to 0.8 GeV/\(c\).
- The calculations were performed for the reaction at 9 GeV/\(c\) deuteron momentum, as referenced from earlier experimental data.
- The solid curve from Karmanov's DWF does not cross the horizontal axis, in contrast to calculations with Paris and Bonn DWFs.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Blankenbecler et al. and Karmanov et al. on relativistic effects in the deuteron wave function, and addresses the significant discrepancy between experimental data on the tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}\) of the \(A(\vec d,p)X\) reaction at 9 GeV/\(c\) and calculations using standard nonrelativistic DWFs (Paris and Bonn). The results suggest that the new link between longitudinal and transverse momentum components in Karmanov's model can resolve this discrepancy, and the approach enables further investigation of spin phenomena in reactions with relativistic polarized deuterons, including the \(dp \rightarrow pd\) and \(dp \rightarrow ppn\) channels.
