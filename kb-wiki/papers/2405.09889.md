# Flow and Equation of State of nuclear matter at $\mathbf{E_{\mathrm{kin}}}$/A=0.25-1.5 GeV with the SMASH transport approach

> We present a comparison of directed and elliptic flow data by the FOPI collaboration in Au--Au, Xe--CsI, and Ni--Ni collisions at beam kinetic energies from 0.25 to 1.5 GeV per nucleon to simulations using the SMASH hadronic transport model. The Equation of State is parameterized as a function of nuclear density and momentum dependent potentials are newly introduced in SMASH. With a statistical analysis, we show that within the present status of the SMASH transport model, the collective flow data at lower energies is in the best agreement with a soft momentum dependent potential, while the elliptic flow at higher energies requires a harder momentum dependent Equation of State.

## Key claim

Within the present status of the SMASH hadronic transport model, collective flow data at lower beam energies (0.4–0.8 GeV per nucleon) are best described by a soft momentum-dependent equation of state, while elliptic flow at higher energies (1.0–1.5 GeV per nucleon) requires a harder momentum-dependent equation of state; the model shows clear limitations at the lowest energy studied (0.25 GeV per nucleon).

## Method

The authors performed simulations of Au–Au, Xe–CsI, and Ni–Ni collisions using the SMASH Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, incorporating Skyrme-type density-dependent potentials and newly introduced momentum-dependent potentials of the Welke form. They compared the calculated directed flow (v₁) and elliptic flow (v₂) coefficients to FOPI collaboration data across beam kinetic energies from 0.25 to 1.5 GeV per nucleon, using χ² per degree of freedom to quantify agreement for hard, medium, and soft equation-of-state parametrizations with and without momentum dependence.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility κ values: 380 MeV (hard EoS), 290 MeV (medium EoS), 215 MeV (soft EoS)
- Symmetry potential constant S_pot fixed to 18 MeV
- Coalescence thresholds: distance < 3 fm and momentum difference < 300 MeV/c in the two-particle center-of-mass frame
- At E_kin/A = 0.4 GeV, the soft EoS with momentum dependence (SM) reproduces directed flow data better than the hard EoS with momentum dependence (HM) across all collision systems
- For elliptic flow at E_kin/A = 0.4 GeV, the soft EoS with momentum dependence describes data best; at E_kin/A = 1.0 GeV, the hard EoS fits data better
- At E_kin/A = 0.25 GeV, the model strongly overestimates elliptic flow magnitude for both EoS parametrizations
- χ²/n.d.f. for all 435 data points is lower for soft EoS than for hard EoS; for directed flow data alone (155 points), χ²/n.d.f. for hard EoS increases by a factor of 2.3 while for soft EoS only by a factor of 1.64

## Context

This work extends previous SMASH studies that described HADES data at 1.2 GeV per nucleon (Mohs, 2020) and STAR BES data at higher energies (Oliinychenko, 2022), applying the model down to 0.25 GeV per nucleon for the first time. The results are consistent with earlier findings from the BUU transport model of Danielewicz (1998, 2002) which predicted a similar trend of transition from soft to hard EoS with energy, while a study within the IQMD model (LeFevre, 2015) found preference for a soft EoS throughout this energy range. The work enables further quantitative studies of systematic uncertainties in transport approaches and improved constraints on the nuclear equation of state relevant for neutron star properties.
