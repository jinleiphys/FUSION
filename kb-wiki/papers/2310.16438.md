# Baryons as Vortexes on the $η^{\prime}$ Domain Wall

> We show that the recent construction of $N_f=1$ baryons on the $η^\prime$ domain wall can be understood as vortexes of the principal effective theory -- the Chern-Simons-Higgs theory -- on a 2+1-dimensional sheet. This theory has a series of vertex solutions, and the vortex with unit topological charge naturally spins $N_c/2$, which coincides with the spin of the one-flavor baryon in QCD. Since the $N_c$ scaling of the vortexes is the same as that of baryons, baryons can be regarded as vortexes. By virtue of the particle-vortex symmetry, the dual Zhang-Hansson-Kivelson theory indicates that the quark carries topological charge $1/N_c$ and obeys fractional statistics. The generalization to arbitrary $N_f$ is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that one-flavor baryons on the $\eta^{\prime}$ domain wall can be understood as vortex solutions of the Chern-Simons-Higgs theory in 2+1 dimensions, but this identification is made in the large $N_c$ limit and relies on the assumption that the effective theory on the domain wall is governed by the Lagrangian in Eq. (3) with minimal coupling and gauge invariance.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Chern-Simons-Higgs theory on the 2+1-dimensional $\eta^{\prime}$ domain wall by applying level-rank duality to map the $SU(N_c)_{-1}$ gluon theory plus one fermion to a $U(1)_{N_c}$ Chern-Simons theory coupled to a complex scalar field. They analyze vortex solutions of this theory using a standard polar coordinate ansatz with winding number $n$, compute their topological charges, spins, and $N_c$ scaling properties, and compare these to the known properties of one-flavor baryons in large $N_c$ QCD. The generalization to $N_f>1$ is treated by gauging the $U(N_f)$ flavor symmetry and considering diagonal gauge field configurations.

## Key numbers

- The vortex with unit topological charge ($n=1$) has spin $s = N_c/2$, which coincides with the spin of the one-flavor baryon in QCD.
- The topological charge of a vortex is $Q = n N_c$, where $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the winding number.
- The baryon number is defined as $B = Q/N_c = n$, so the $n=1$ vortex carries baryon number 1.
- In the dual Zhang-Hansson-Kivelson theory, the vortex topological charge is $\tilde{Q} = n/N_c$, and the corresponding spin is $\tilde{s} = n^2/(2N_c)$.
- For $N_f=2$, the total spin of a vortex with winding numbers $n_1, n_2$ is $S = (n_1^2 + n_2^2) N_c/2$, and the baryon number is $B = n_1 + n_2$.
- The vortex energy (mass) scales as $\sim N_c$, the radii are $\sim N_c^0$, vortex-vortex scattering amplitudes are of order $N_c$, and meson-vortex scattering amplitudes are of order $N_c^0$.

## Context

This work extends the construction of one-flavor baryons as Hall droplets on $\eta^{\prime}$ domain walls by Gaiotto (2017) and Komargodski (2018), providing a concrete effective field theory description in terms of vortex solutions. The paper builds on the level-rank duality framework connecting $SU(N_c)_{-1}$ and $U(1)_{N_c}$ Chern-Simons theories, and on the earlier observation by Ma (2019) that baryons as quantum Hall droplets can be understood as chiral bags. The particle-vortex duality employed is the relativistic version of the Zhang-Hansson-Kivelson theory (1988) originally developed for the fractional quantum Hall effect. The results enable a unified description of baryons as topological objects on domain walls, connecting to the Skyrme model for low-spin baryons and to baby Skyrmion descriptions for multi-flavor cases, with potential applications to dense baryonic matter and connections between condensed matter and high-energy physics.
