# $S$-matrix calculation of $BQ$ correlation at finite baryon density

> We calculate the baryon number--electric charge susceptibility at non-vanishing baryo-chemical potential within the model of hadron gas where pion-nucleon interaction is accounted for by the $S$-matrix formalism. The susceptibility is largely increased when the chemical potential grows within a phenomenologically relevant interval. The results are then evaluated along the chemical freeze-out line. We also calculate the evolution of the susceptibility in a cooling fireball by making use of the Partial Chemical Equilibrium model.

# S-matrix calculation of BQ correlation at finite baryon density

## Key claim

The baryon number–electric charge susceptibility χ_{BQ} in a hadron gas is largely increased when the baryo-chemical potential grows within a phenomenologically relevant interval, but the S-matrix treatment of πN interactions makes the result slightly smaller than the simple Hadron Resonance Gas model. The calculation extends previous work at vanishing net-baryon density to non-zero chemical potential, with the caveat that the freeze-out parametrization used was extracted from fits with the HRG model rather than the S-matrix model.

## Method

The partition function is split into a free part (ground-state hadrons from meson octet, baryon octet and decuplet) and an interaction part. Pion-nucleon and eta-nucleon interactions are treated via the S-matrix formalism using explicit phase shifts from the GWU/SAID partial wave analysis, while other mesonic and baryonic resonances are included as zero-width states. Three-body ππN interactions are accounted for through three effective models (Δ(1232) model and two shifted N*/Δ models) tuned to lattice QCD results, and the spread among these models is used as an estimate of systematic uncertainty. The susceptibility is then evaluated along the chemical freeze-out line and in a cooling fireball using the Partial Chemical Equilibrium model with entropy conservation.

## Key numbers

- The shifted mass for the Δ(1232) model is 1.1799 GeV, chosen so that χ_{BQ} at T=155 MeV and μ_B=0 reproduces the lattice value.
- For the Shifted N* and Δ 1 model, mass factors are 0.966 for N*'s and 1.20 for Δ's, fitted to lattice QCD χ_{BQ} at T=135 MeV and 155 MeV.
- For the Shifted N* and Δ 2 model, mass factors are 0.952 for N*'s and 2.16 for Δ's, fitted over T=135 MeV to 160 MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out parametrization uses T(μ_B) = a - b μ_B^2 - c μ_B^4 with a=0.157 GeV, b=0.087 GeV^{-1}, c=0.0092 GeV^{-3}.
- Initial PCE conditions from STAR fits range from T=144.3 MeV, μ_B=398.2 MeV at √s_{NN}=7.7 GeV to T=164.3 MeV, μ_B=28.4 MeV at √s_{NN}=200 GeV.
- The systematic uncertainty due to different ππN models accounts to about 20%.
- χ_{BQ} drops to about 60% of its chemical freeze-out value when temperature is lowered to 100 MeV for √s_{NN}=7.7 GeV collisions.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix calculation of χ_{BQ} by Lo (2017) from vanishing baryo-chemical potential to finite baryon density, using updated lattice QCD results from Bollweg (2021) for tuning. The approach improves upon the standard Hadron Resonance Gas model by treating πN interactions via explicit phase shifts rather than zero-width resonances, which had previously resolved tensions with proton yields in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_{NN}=2.76 TeV (Stachel 2013, Andronic 2018). The results provide a baseline for searches for critical point signals in fluctuations at non-zero baryo-chemical potential, and the PCE calculations enable comparison with data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (STAR 2017).
