# Influences of ${Z=100}$ and ${N=152}$ deformed shells on ${ K^π=8^{-} }$ isomers and rotational bands in ${N = 150}$ isotones

> The $K^π=8^{-}$ isomeric states and rotational bands in the even-even $N = 150$ isotones with $94 \leqslant Z \leqslant 104$ are investigated by the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method. The experimental bandhead energies and kinematic moments of inertia (MOIs) are reproduced quite well by the PNC-CSM calculation. The two-neutron state with configuration $ν9/2^{-}[734] \otimes ν7/2^{+}[624]$ is the lowest $8^{-}$ state for these isomers. This is a demonstration of the deformed neutron shell at $N=152$. Low-lying two proton $π^{2}8^{-}$($π9/2^{+}[624] \otimes π7/2^{-}[514]$) configuration state is predicted only for $^{252}$No and $^{254}$Rf due to the deformed proton shell at $Z=100$. A distinct upbending is observed for the $ν^{2}8^{-}$ bands in the lighter isotones while it is absent for bands in the heavier ones. The upbending of the $ν^{2}8^{-}$ band at frequency $\hbarω\approx 0.20$ MeV in $^{244}$Pu attributes to the sudden proton alignment of the interference term $j_x(\pi5/2^{+}[642]\otimes\pi7/2^{+}[633])$. The irregularity of MOI observed in the $K^π=8^{-}$ band of $^{252}$No can be explained by the mixing of the $ν^{2}8^{-}$($ν9/2^{-}[734] \otimes ν7/2^{+}[624]$) and $π^{2}8^{-}$($π9/2^{+}[624] \otimes π7/2^{-}[514]$) configurations. The $20\%-30\%$ increase of the bandhead $J^{(1)}$ for the $8^{-}$ bands comparing to the ground-state band is attributed to the $\sim 5\%$ pairing gap reduction of the two-neutron $ν7/2^{+}[624] \otimes ν9/2^{-}[734]$ configuration state comparing to the ground-state band.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the systematic occurrence of low-lying two-neutron \(K^{\pi}=8^{-}\) states in \(N=150\) isotones confirms the deformed neutron shell at \(N=152\), while the prediction of low-lying two-proton \(8^{-}\) states only in \(^{252}\)No and \(^{254}\)Rf provides evidence for the deformed proton shell at \(Z=100\); however, the configuration assignment for the \(^{254}\)Rf isomer remains ambiguous because the calculated two-neutron state lies too low and the two-proton state better matches the experimental excitation energy.

## Method

The cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method was applied to even-even \(N=150\) isotones from \(^{244}\)Pu to \(^{254}\)Rf. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a truncated cranked many-particle configuration space constructed in the proton \(N=5,6\) shells and neutron \(N=6,7\) shells, with deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2\), \(\varepsilon_4\), and \(\varepsilon_6\) chosen close to available experimental values. The occupation probabilities of cranked Nilsson orbitals were analyzed to identify configuration assignments and understand the upbending mechanisms in rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- The \(K^{\pi}=8^{-}\) isomers in \(^{244}\)Pu, \(^{246}\)Cm, \(^{248}\)Cf, and \(^{250}\)Fm have excitation energies \(E \approx 1.20\) MeV, while the isomer in \(^{254}\)Rf lies at about 1.35 MeV.
- The deviation between calculated and experimental excitation energies for the two-neutron \(8^{-}\) states in \(^{244}\)Pu, \(^{246}\)Cm, \(^{248}\)Cf, and \(^{250}\)Fm is less than 0.05 MeV.
- The upbending of the \(\nu^{2}8^{-}\) band in \(^{244}\)Pu occurs at rotational frequency \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.20\) MeV, attributed to sudden proton alignment of the interference term \(j_x(\pi5/2^{+}[642]\otimes\pi7/2^{+}[633])\).
- The bandhead kinematic moment of inertia \(J^{(1)}\) for the \(8^{-}\) bands shows a \(20\%-30\%\) increase compared to the ground-state band, attributed to a \(\sim 5\%\) pairing gap reduction in the two-neutron configuration.
- The two-proton \(8^{-}\) state in \(^{250}\)Fm is predicted at 2.806 MeV, about 1.6 MeV above the experimental isomer energy.
- The two-proton \(8^{-}\) state in \(^{254}\)Rf is calculated at 1.390 MeV, closer to the experimental data than the two-neutron state.

## Context

This work extends previous PNC-CSM calculations that described high-\(K\) isomers and rotational bands in \(^{254}\)No (He, 2020) and provides the first systematic theoretical investigation of the rotational behaviors of \(K^{\pi}=8^{-}\) bands across the \(N=150\) isotonic chain. The results confirm configuration assignments suggested by earlier experimental studies (Hota, 2016; Greenlees, 2008; Sulignano, 2012) and theoretical calculations using the Woods-Saxon method with Lipkin-Nogami pairing (Tandel, 2006; Robinson, 2008) and the configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface model (Liu, 2014; Fu, 2014). The findings enable future studies of configuration mixing between two-neutron and two-proton \(8^{-}\) bands in heavier isotones and clarify the role of deformed shell gaps at \(Z=100\) and \(N=152\) in shaping the rotational properties of superheavy nuclei.
