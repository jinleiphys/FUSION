# Double shape quantum phase transitions in the SU3-IBM: new $γ$-soft phase and the shape phase transition from the new $γ$-soft phase to the prolate shape

> Shape quantum phase transition is an important topic in nuclear structure. In this paper, we begin to study the shape quantum phase transition in the SU3-IBM. In this new proposed model, spherical-like spectra was found to resolve the spherical nucleus puzzle, which is a new $γ$-soft rotational mode. In this paper, the shape phase transition along the new $γ$-soft line is first discussed, and then the neighbouring case at the prolate side is also studied. We find that double shape phase transitions occur along a single parameter path. The new $γ$-softness is really a shape phase and the shape phase transition from the new $γ$-soft phase to the prolate shape is found. The experimental support is also found and $^{108}$Pd may be the critical nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the SU3-IBM (interacting boson model with SU(3) higher-order interactions), a new gamma-soft rotational mode constitutes a genuine shape phase rather than a critical point, and that along a single parameter path from the U(5) symmetry limit to a point near the SU(3) degenerate point, double shape quantum phase transitions occur: first from spherical to the new gamma-soft phase, then from the new gamma-soft phase to the prolate shape. The authors hedge that for finite boson number N, the spherical-like spectra and the critical spectra may not be distinguishable.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the Hamiltonian H = c[(1-eta)n_d + eta(-C_2[SU(3)]/(2N) + kappa C_3[SU(3)]/(2N^2))] for boson numbers N=7, 10, 35, and 60 along two evolution paths in the phase diagram: the "real blue line" from the U(5) symmetry limit to the SU(3) degenerate point (kappa_0 = 3N/(2N+3)), and the "real red line" from U(5) to a point at kappa = 0.9 kappa_0. They computed ground-state energies, mean d-boson numbers, low-lying excitation energies, B(E2) transition strengths, and electric quadrupole moments Q(2_1^+) as functions of the control parameter eta.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) degenerate point is at kappa_0 = 3N/(2N+3); the red point is at 0.9 kappa_0.
- For N=60 along the real blue line, the spherical-to-new-gamma-soft transition occurs around eta=0.2.
- For N=60 along the real red line, a second shape phase transition (new gamma-soft to prolate) occurs around eta=0.5.
- For N=35 along the real red line, eta=0.5 is identified as the phase transition critical point from gamma-soft rotation to prolate shape.
- The energy ratio E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) is 3.33 for prolate shape and 2.55 for oblate shape in realistic nuclei (cited from Jolie (2003)).
- The beta ratio B(E2; 0_2^+ -> 2_1^+)/B(E2; 2_1^+ -> 0_1^+) for 104-110Pd shows an increase then decrease across the transition, with 108Pd identified as the possible critical nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously developed by Wang (2020, 2022) and applies it to shape phase transitions, which were extensively studied in the standard IBM by Casten (2006-2010), Cejnar and Jolie (2009), and others. The new gamma-soft phase differs fundamentally from the O(6) gamma-softness in the standard IBM, which Jolie (2001) showed is merely a first-order critical point between prolate and oblate shapes rather than a genuine phase. The results enable experimental identification of shape phase transitions in Pd isotopes, with 108Pd as a candidate critical nucleus, and suggest that many nuclei previously classified as spherical may actually belong to the new gamma-soft phase.
