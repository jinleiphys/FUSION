# Bottomonia suppression in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma

> A brief review of recent studies on suppression of bottomonia in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC is presented. A reasonable agreement between the model predictions for the inclusive $R_{\rm AA}$ suppression factor and the preliminary CMS experimental data is found. The values of the shear viscosity to the entropy density ratio extracted from the comparison with the data lie between one and two times the gauge/gravity duality lower bound. These values agree very well with the fluid dynamical fits to the light hadron correlation data and confirm that the quark-gluon plasma is a nearly-perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The model predictions for the inclusive \(R_{\rm AA}\) suppression factor of bottomonia in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma show reasonable agreement with preliminary CMS experimental data, but the agreement is worse for the \(\Upsilon(2s)\) state in the most peripheral collisions. The extracted shear viscosity to entropy density ratio lies between one and two times the gauge/gravity duality lower bound, confirming the quark-gluon plasma is a nearly-perfect fluid.

## Method

The study couples a potential-based non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) model for quarkonium binding to a (3+1)-dimensional leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics model for the quark-gluon plasma background evolution. The static heavy quarkonium potential is complex valued, with the real part based on the Karsch-Mehr-Satz form of the free energy and the imaginary part from leading-order perturbative calculations in the small anisotropy limit. The three-dimensional Schrodinger equation is solved for the complex binding energies of \(\Upsilon(1s)\) and \(\Upsilon(2s)\) states as functions of the anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) and transverse temperature \(\Lambda\), and the local decay rate is integrated over the plasma evolution to compute the \(R_{\rm AA}\) suppression factor, including feed-down from excited states.

## Key numbers

* The initial central temperature in central collisions is set to \(T_0 \in \{552, 546, 544\}\) MeV for \(4\pi\eta/s \in \{1, 2, 3\}\), respectively, to reproduce the final charged-particle multiplicity.
* The initial proper time for hydrodynamic simulations is \(\tau_0 = 0.3\) fm.
* The pseudocritical transition temperature is \(T_c \approx 165\) MeV.
* The rest-frame formation times used are \(\tau_{\rm form}^0 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.4, 0.6\) fm for \(\Upsilon(1s)\), \(\Upsilon(2s)\), \(\Upsilon(3s)\), \(\chi_{b1}\), and \(\chi_{b2}\) states, respectively.
* The feed-down fractions for \(\Upsilon(1s)\) are \(\{0.618, 0.105, 0.02, 0.207, 0.05\}\) for \(\Upsilon(1s)\), \(\Upsilon(2s)\), \(\Upsilon(3s)\), \(\chi_{b1}\), and \(\chi_{b2}\), respectively.
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is constrained to the range \(1/(4\pi) < \eta/s < 2/(4\pi)\) from comparison with data.

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD model developed by Strickland (2011) and upgraded by Krouppa (2015, 2016) by coupling it to a realistic (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics background evolution from Ryblewski (2015). The extracted \(\eta/s\) values agree with fluid dynamical fits to light hadron correlation data, confirming the quark-gluon plasma is a nearly-perfect fluid. The results enable further studies of heavy quarkonium as a probe of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.
