# Constrained many-body phases in a $\mathbb{Z}_2$-Higgs lattice gauge theory

> We study the ground-state phase diagram of a one-dimensional $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory coupled to soft-core bosonic matter at unit filling, inspired by the Higgs sector of the standard model. Through a combination of analytical perturbative approaches, exact diagonalization, and density-matrix-renormalization-group simulations, we uncover a rich phase diagram driven by gauge-field-mediated resonant pair hopping and the confinement of single particles. The pair hopping results in a bunching state with superextensive energy and macroscopic particle number fluctuations at strong electric field strengths and weak on-site interactions. The bunching state crosses over into a pair superfluid phase as the on-site interaction increases, characterized by a finite superfluid density and powerlaw-decaying pair correlations. At large on-site interaction strengths and driven by effective interactions induced by the gauge constraint, the superfluid transitions into an incompressible pair Mott insulator phase. At weak field strengths and on-site interactions, we find a plasma-like region, where single bosons exhibit large short-range correlations and the ground state is composed almost equally of states with even and odd local boson occupation. The presence of a bunching state with large number fluctuations, which is difficult to study using classical numerics, motivates experimental realizations in hybrid boson-qubit quantum simulation platforms such as circuit QED, neutral atoms, and trapped ions. Our findings highlight the rich interplay between gauge fields and soft-core bosonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a one-dimensional $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory coupled to soft-core bosonic matter at unit filling exhibits a bunching state with superextensive energy and macroscopic particle number fluctuations at strong electric field strengths and weak on-site interactions, but the authors acknowledge that this superextensivity likely does not prevail at large system sizes and that higher-order terms in the perturbative expansion most likely regulate the divergence.

## Method

The authors study the ground-state phase diagram of a one-dimensional $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory with soft-core bosonic matter using a combination of analytical perturbative approaches (Schrieffer-Wolff transformation to derive an effective Hamiltonian), exact diagonalization, and density-matrix-renormalization-group (DMRG) simulations. They analyze the Hamiltonian with electric field term, gauge-invariant kinetic energy, and on-site bosonic self-interaction, fixing the gauge via Gauss' law constraint $Z_{i-1,i} (-1)^{n_i} Z_{i,i+1}=1$.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle superfluid-to-Mott-insulator transition in the decoupled limit ($g=0$) occurs at $U/J=3.45$ (Elstner 1999).
- The estimated transition point from the bunching state occurs at $U_c = 6 J_\mathrm{pair}$, where $J_\mathrm{pair}=J^2/(2g)$.
- The pair-superfluid-to-Mott-insulator transition occurs at $g/J \approx 2U/J$, corresponding to $g=U/2$.
- The superfluid density converges to a finite value for large $g/J$ and agrees with the effective pair hopping strength $\tilde J_\mathrm{pair}=J^2/(g-U/2)$.
- The two-particle gap $\Delta E^{(2)}/J$ converges to a finite value for $g<2U$, confirming the pair Mott insulator phase.
- The parity $\langle e^{i\pi n_j}\rangle$ for a lattice condensate at unit filling is $e^{-2}\approx 0.14$, and the plasma region shows parity close to $-0.2$ for $g/J\lesssim 1$ at $U/J=2.8$.

## Context

This work extends studies of $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theories coupled to fermions (Borla 2020, Kebric 2021) to soft-core bosonic matter, which is relevant to the Higgs sector of the standard model. Prior work on $\mathbb{Z}_2$ models with bosons was limited to cases with explicitly broken gauge symmetry (Gonzalez-Cuadra 2019, Chanda 2022, Watanabe 2025), while studies on $U(1)$ lattice gauge theories with soft-core bosons exist (Chanda 2020, Chanda 2022a). The findings motivate experimental realizations in hybrid boson-qubit quantum simulation platforms such as circuit QED, neutral atoms, and trapped ions, and the authors note connections to the tilted-field Bose-Hubbard model (Sachdev 2002, Guardado-Sanchez 2020, Zechmann 2023) and constrained dynamics observed there (Sala 2020, Burchards 2022).
