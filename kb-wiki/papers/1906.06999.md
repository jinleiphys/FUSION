# Astrophysical implications of double-layer torsional oscillations in a neutron star crust as a lasagna sandwich

> In the crust of a neutron star, global torsional oscillations could occur in two elastic layers. The outer and inner layers are composed of spherical and cylindrical nuclei and of cylindrical holes (tubes) and spherical holes (bubbles), respectively, while between these two layers, a phase of slab-like (lasagna) nuclei with vanishingly small elasticity is sandwiched. In this work, we update systematic calculations of the eigenfrequencies of the fundamental oscillations in the inner layer by newly allowing for the presence of tubes. We find that the frequencies still depend strongly on the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, $L$, while being almost independent of the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter. We also find that the fundamental frequencies in the inner layer can become smaller than those in the outer layer because the tube phase has a relatively small shear modulus and at the same time dominates the inner layer in thickness. As a result, we can successfully explain not only the quasi-periodic oscillations originally discovered in the observed X-ray afterglow of the giant flare of SGR 1806--20 but also many others recently found by a Bayesian procedure.

## Key claim

The authors show that the 26 Hz quasi-periodic oscillation (QPO) observed in the giant flare of SGR 1806-20 can be explained as the fundamental torsional oscillation in the inner elastic layer of the neutron star crust (composed of tube and bubble phases), provided the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy L lies in a specific range that also simultaneously explains the other observed low-lying QPOs.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star crust models using the OI-EOSs (Oyamatsu and Iida, 2003, 2007), which depend on the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter K0 and the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy. They solved the linearized relativistic perturbation equation for torsional oscillations in the inner crust layer composed of tube and bubble phases, imposing zero-traction boundary conditions at the slab-tube and bubble-uniform matter interfaces and a continuity condition at the tube-bubble boundary. The eigenfrequencies were calculated for various combinations of K0, L, stellar mass M, and radius R, considering both maximum and minimum enthalpy scenarios for the entrainment of nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The 26 Hz QPO in SGR 1806-20 is identified as the ell=4 fundamental torsional oscillation in the tube-bubble layer for a neutron star with M=1.4 solar masses and R=12 km.
- The optimal value of L for explaining the observed low-lying QPOs (18, 29, 57, and 92.5 Hz) ranges between 70.4 and 73.4 MeV for neutron stars with M=1.4 solar masses and R=12 km.
- For the neutron star model with M=1.3 solar masses and R=13 km, the optimal L values are 67.5-70.8 MeV.
- For the neutron star model with M=1.8 solar masses and R=12 km, the optimal L values are 59.6-63.5 MeV.
- The fundamental frequencies in the tube-bubble layer can be fitted as a function of L via the formula: 0t_ell = c_ell^(0) + c_ell^(1)*sqrt(L) + c_ell^(2)*L, with adjustable parameters depending on M, R, and the entrainment parameter R.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Sotani et al., 2017a, 2018) that calculated torsional oscillations in the outer crust layer (spherical and cylindrical nuclei) but treated the tube phase as having zero elasticity. By including the tube phase with its small shear modulus, the authors obtain lower fundamental frequencies that can explain the 26 Hz QPO, which was previously difficult to account for. The results also accommodate newly found QPOs from a Bayesian analysis (Miller et al., 2018) and enable future constraints on the neutron star mass and radius by combining the L-dependence of frequencies in both elastic layers.
