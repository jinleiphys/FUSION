# A Large Solid Angle Study of Pion Absorption on He3

> Measurements have been made of pi+ absorption on He3 at T_pi+ = 118, 162, and 239 MeV using the Large Acceptance Detector System (LADS). The nearly 4pi solid angle coverage of this detector minimizes uncertainties associated with extrapolations over unmeasured regions of phase space. The total absorption cross section is reported. In addition, the total cross section is divided into components in which only two or all three nucleons play a significant role in the process. These are the first direct measurements of the total and three nucleon absorption cross sections.

## Key claim

The LADS collaboration reports the first direct measurements of total and three-nucleon absorption cross sections for π⁺ absorption on ³He at three energies near the Δ-resonance, with the caveat that the separation into two-nucleon (2NA) and three-nucleon (3NA) components depends on a specific model of the absorption process using Monte Carlo simulations, though the total cross section is relatively insensitive to this model.

## Method

The LADS detector, covering more than 98% of 4π solid angle with plastic scintillators and multi-wire proportional chambers, was used to detect outgoing protons from π⁺ absorption on a high-pressure ³He gas target at incident pion kinetic energies of 118, 162, and 239 MeV. Events were classified by the number of detected charged particles (two or three protons), corrected for detector inefficiencies and backgrounds, and the total and partial cross sections were extracted using Monte Carlo simulations based on two-nucleon quasi-free absorption (2NA) and three-body phase space (3NA) models.

- The fraction of the total cross section attributable to three-nucleon absorption (3NA) is 22% at 118 MeV, 29% at 162 MeV, and 30% at 239 MeV.
- The ratio of 2NA on ³He to absorption on ²H is 1.86 ± 0.10 at 118 MeV, 1.60 ± 0.09 at 162 MeV, and 1.63 ± 0.15 at 239 MeV.
- The weighted average correction to the total cross section for geometrical acceptance and energy threshold was 15% at 118 MeV, 13% at 162 MeV, and 10% at 239 MeV.
- Systematic uncertainties in the total cross sections had magnitudes up to 2.3%.

## Context

This work extends previous absorption experiments on ³He and ⁴He (Backenstoss 1985, Backenstoss 1988, Smith 1989, Stein 1990, Mukhopadhyay 1991, Weber 1991, Adimi 1992) that suffered from limited angular coverage or statistical precision, by using the nearly 4π solid angle LADS detector to minimize extrapolation uncertainties. The results confirm the significant role of multi-nucleon absorption mechanisms and bring the energy dependence of the 2NA and total cross sections on ³He into better agreement with those on ²H (Ritchie 1991) and ⁴He (Budagov 1962, Baumgartner 1983), enabling more reliable comparisons of absorption dynamics across light nuclei.
