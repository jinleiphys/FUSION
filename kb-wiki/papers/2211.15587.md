# Gluon helicity distribution in the nucleon from lattice QCD and machine learning

> We present the first lattice QCD determination of the light cone gluon helicity correlation parton distribution function (PDF) with numerical evidence toward disfavoring negative gluon polarization in the nucleon. We present a solution for eliminating an inevitable contamination term that dominates the Euclidean correlations and makes determining gluon helicity PDF unfeasible. The proposed synergy between lattice QCD and artificial intelligence offers a superior platform to alleviate the defining challenge of extracting quark and gluon PDFs from the lattice data that are available in a limited domain due to a finite range of accessible hadron momenta. We suggest a systematically improvable method to extract PDFs from the lattice data, independent of inadequate parametrizations. The result of the gluon helicity will improve our understanding of the role of spin in the strong interaction and the nucleon spin structure.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD determination of the light cone gluon helicity parton distribution function, with numerical evidence that disfavors negative gluon polarization in the nucleon, but the result is obtained by eliminating a contamination term through a mathematical relation combined with machine learning rather than from direct lattice data alone.

## Method

The authors computed gluon helicity Ioffe-time distributions from lattice QCD matrix elements of bilocal gluon field operators with a spatial Wilson line, using the pseudo-PDF approach. They derived a relation (Eq. 5 in the text) that eliminates the momentum-dependent contamination term by combining data at different nucleon boosts, then implemented a neural network to perform a correlated simultaneous fit to the unsubtracted and subtracted matrix elements across all available momenta and separations up to z_max = 6a (approximately 0.56 fm). The resulting contamination-free distribution was matched to the light cone Ioffe-time distribution using a factorization relation at next-to-leading order in the MSbar scheme, and the PDF was obtained via a sine transform without assuming any functional form.

## Key numbers

* Gluon helicity moment from lattice data integrated up to omega_max = 10: Delta G^L(mu) = 0.405(196) at mu = 2 GeV, where the central value is about 3.8 sigma away from the negative gluon helicity solution Delta G = -0.9(2) from Zhou (2022).
* The gluon momentum fraction used for normalization: <x>_g(mu = 2 GeV) = 0.427, taken from Alexandrou (2020).
* Lattice spacing: a = 0.094 fm, with spatial extent L = 32 and nucleon boosts given by p_n = 2 pi n / (L a).
* Data were used up to z_max = 6a (approximately 0.56 fm) and Ioffe time omega up to approximately 7 from lattice data, with neural network extrapolation considered reliable up to omega_max = 10.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-PDF approach previously applied to unpolarized gluon PDFs by Fan (2018, 2020, 2021) and the HadStruc Collaboration (2021, 2022), and directly addresses the contamination problem identified in HadStruc (2022) that had prevented gluon helicity PDF extraction. The result disfavors the negative gluon helicity solution from the JAM global analysis by Zhou (2022) and is consistent with the positive gluon polarization obtained from local operator calculations by Yang (2016). This enables future comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, and provides a systematically improvable method for extracting PDFs from lattice data without relying on inadequate parametrizations.
