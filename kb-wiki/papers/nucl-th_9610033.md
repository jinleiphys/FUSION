# Microscopic calculations of stopping and flow from 160AMeV to 160AGeV

## Key claim
Baryonic stopping is observed for Au+Au collisions from SIS up to SPS energies, and the excitation function of flow shows strong sensitivities to the underlying equation of state (EOS), allowing for systematic studies of the EOS; however, the density dependence of the rho meson pole alone does not suffice to explain the dilepton excess observed in S+Au around 0.4 GeV, and other effects such as temperature dependence of the chiral condensate or additional sources may be required.

## Method
The Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD) transport model, including 50 baryon species (nucleon, delta, hyperon and resonances up to 2.11 GeV) and 25 meson species with all isospin-projected states, was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions from SIS to SPS energies. The real part of the baryon optical potential was modeled according to the Skyrme ansatz including Yukawa and Coulomb forces, and for higher mass excitations a string model was invoked. The directed transverse momentum and dilepton spectra were calculated for various systems and centralities, with in-medium modifications of the rho meson propagator implemented via a density-dependent pole shift.

## Key numbers
- Baryon rapidity distributions for Au+Au at SIS (1 A GeV), AGS (10.6 A GeV) and Pb+Pb at CERN/SPS (160 A GeV) show gaussian distributions with peak around midrapidity y_s ~ ±0.2.
- For S+S at 200 A GeV, approximately 50% of baryon-baryon collisions involve constituents during their formation time, with cross sections reduced by factors of 2/3 (di-quarks) or 1/3 (quarks), most around sqrt(s) ~ 10 ± 5 GeV.
- The integrated directed transverse momentum per nucleon with a hard EOS is more than twice as high as for the cascade calculation for Au+Au from 0.1 to 4 A GeV incident kinetic energy.
- The URQMD equation of state yields about 4-5 times less energy density than a Hagedorn gas or QGP at T=150 MeV, with energy density ~1 GeV/fm^3, but approaches QGP values of ~10 GeV/fm^3 around T=300 MeV.
- The rho meson pole shift parameter lambda = 0.18, with the mass given by m_rho(rho/rho_0) = m_rho(0) / (1 + lambda * rho/rho_0).
- For S+Au at 200 A GeV, the average decay density of rho mesons is <rho> ~ 0.2 rho_0 (or 0.3 rho_0 for Pb+Au), and the rho lifetime increases to ~7 fm/c below its resonance mass in the region M ~ 0.3-0.5 GeV.
- Pion annihilation contributes only 40% of the rho peak for S+Au.

## Context
This work extends the URQMD model to consistently cover the entire energy range from SIS to SPS for heavy systems, building on earlier transport approaches such as those by Stoecker (1986) and Dosch (1986). The flow excitation function analysis follows the framework of Rischke and collaborators for phase transition signatures, while the in-medium rho meson studies are motivated by chiral symmetry restoration calculations by G.E. Brown (1991) and T. Hatsuda (1992). The results enable systematic comparisons with experimental data from CERES at SPS and provide predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN energies, with the finding that the density-dependent rho pole alone cannot explain the observed dilepton excess suggesting the need for additional mechanisms such as temperature-dependent chiral condensate effects or new source contributions.
