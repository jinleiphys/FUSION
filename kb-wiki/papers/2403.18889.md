# Prospects for PIONEER

> Pion $β$ decay, $π^+ \toπ^0 e^+ ν_e$, offers a pristine way to measure the CKM matrix element $V_{ud}$ in a purely mesonic system, with excellent control over the hadronic matrix elements. We review the physics goals and current status of the PIONEER experiment, which aims at major improvements in the branching fractions for the $π^+\to e^+ ν_e$ decay in Phase I and for pion $β$ decay in Phases II and III of its experimental program, potentially leading to a measurement of $V_{ud}$ competitive with determinations from $β$ decays involving nucleons.

# Prospects for PIONEER

## Key claim
PIONEER aims to measure the CKM matrix element V\_ud from pion beta decay at a precision of 3 × 10⁻⁴ in Phases II+III, potentially matching the best determinations from nuclear and neutron beta decays, but only if the branching ratio uncertainty, currently the dominant error, can be reduced by an order of magnitude before theory uncertainties become limiting.

## Method
The experiment stops a pion beam in a segmented active target (ATAR) built from silicon-strip low-gain avalanche detectors, providing 5D tracking (energy, time, three spatial dimensions). Positrons from pion decays are counted in a 3π, 25 radiation-length electromagnetic calorimeter with energy resolution δE/E ≤ 1.5%, using either liquid xenon or LYSO crystal technology. The two main decay modes (π⁺ → e⁺ νₑ and π⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ followed by Michel decay) are distinguished by calorimeter energy, ATAR energy deposition, event topology, and decay-time spectra.

## Key numbers
- R\_e/μ Standard Model prediction: 1.23524(15) × 10⁻⁴
- Current experimental R\_e/μ value (PIENU): 1.2327(23) × 10⁻⁴
- Ratio of SM to experimental R\_e/μ: 1.0010(9)
- Phase-space factor I\_πℓ for pion beta decay: 7.3767(41) × 10⁻⁸
- Radiative correction Δ\_RC^πℓ: 0.0332(3)
- Form-factor normalization f\_+^π(0): 1 − 7 × 10⁻⁶
- Current V\_ud from PIBETA: 0.97386(281)_BR(9)_τ_π(14)_Δ_RC(28)_I_πℓ[283]_total
- Pion mass difference M\_π± − M\_π0: 4.59364(48) MeV
- Target precision for V\_ud in Phase III: 3 × 10⁻⁴
- Target overall uncertainty for Phase I: O(0.01%)
- Planned event count: 2 × 10⁸ π⁺ → e⁺ νₑ events over three 5-month runs

## Context
This paper reviews the PIONEER experiment at PSI, which builds upon the legacy of the PIENU, PEN, and PIBETA experiments (Frlez 2003, PIENU 2015) to improve rare pion decay measurements. The current first-row CKM unitarity test shows a 2.8σ tension in the global fit (Cirigliano 2022), with V\_ud from superallowed beta decays (Hardy 2020) limited by nuclear corrections (Gorchtein 2018, 2023) and neutron decay complicated by tension between asymmetry measurements (Markisch 2018, Beck 2019). PIONEER's measurement of pion beta decay would provide a purely mesonic determination of V\_ud with excellent hadronic control, and its Phase I measurement of R\_e/μ at 10⁻⁴ precision can probe scalar currents up to PeV scales and test lepton flavor universality in connection with other hints (Crivellin 2020, 2021).
