# Temperature effects on the neutron matter equation of state obtained from chiral effective field theory

> Temperature effects on the neutron matter equation of state are investigated in the framework of chiral effective field theory. Latest, state-of-the-art chiral two-nucleon forces are applied from third to fifth order in the chiral expansion together with chiral three-nucleon forces, allowing for a determination of the truncation error of the theoretical predictions. The thermodynamic quantities considered include the chemical potential, the internal energy, the entropy, and the free energy. In general, good order-by-order convergence of all predictions is observed. As to be expected, temperature effects are largest at low density. The temperature dependence of the chiral three-nucleon force turns out to be weak.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral effective field theory predictions for the hot neutron matter equation of state show good order-by-order convergence from N2LO to N4LO, with temperature effects largest at low density and the temperature dependence of the chiral three-nucleon force being weak and noticeable only above normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Bethe-Goldstone equation to sum particle-particle ladder diagrams to infinite order, using chiral two-nucleon forces from third to fifth order (N2LO, N3LO, N4LO) from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) with cutoff values of 450 and 500 MeV. Temperature dependence was introduced by replacing zero-temperature step-function occupation numbers with Fermi-Dirac distributions and by including temperature effects in the density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions that represent the chiral three-nucleon force, following the formalism of Wellenhofer, Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2014). The chemical potential was determined self-consistently from the normalization condition, and the entropy per particle was computed directly from the occupation probability.

## Key numbers

- The internal energy per particle in neutron matter was computed at temperatures T = 0, 10, 20, and 30 MeV for densities up to approximately 0.32 fm^-3
- The N4LO two-nucleon potential reproduces NN data below pion production threshold with chi^2/datum = 1.15
- The difference between SCGF and BHF internal energy per particle was at most 1.5 MeV at the highest density of 0.32 fm^-3 when using the CD-Bonn potential (from prior work by Rios et al., 2009)

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature neutron matter studies by using the most recent high-quality chiral NN potentials up to N4LO from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017), whereas earlier chiral EFT studies of hot matter by Wellenhofer, Holt, and Kaiser (2015) and Carbone and Schwenk (2019) employed older N3LO potentials from Entem and Machleidt (2003) with the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward many-body perturbation series. The results enable future investigations of the symmetry energy at finite temperature, beta-stable matter for supernova and neutron star applications, and the liquid-gas phase transition in isospin-asymmetric matter, with quantified theoretical uncertainties from the chiral expansion.
