# r-Process Nucleosynthesis in Hot Accretion Disk Flows from Black Hole - Neutron Star Mergers

> We consider hot accretion disk outflows from black hole - neutron star mergers in the context of the nucleosynthesis they produce. We begin with a three dimensional numerical model of a black hole - neutron star merger and calculate the neutrino and antineutrino fluxes emitted from the resulting accretion disk. We then follow the element synthesis in material outflowing the disk along parameterized trajectories. We find that at least a weak r-process is produced, and in some cases a main r-process as well. The neutron-rich conditions required for this production of r-process nuclei stem directly from the interactions of the neutrinos emitted by the disk with the free neutrons and protons in the outflow.

## Key claim
The paper finds that hot accretion disk outflows from black hole-neutron star mergers produce at least a weak r-process in almost all wind trajectories, and in some cases a main r-process as well, but only under specific conditions: high entropy (s ≥ 30) and moderately accelerating outflows (β ≥ 0.8). The abstract's claim of "in some cases a main r-process" is thus conditional on these parameter ranges.

## Method
The authors used a three-dimensional numerical model of a 2.5 solar mass black hole merging with a 1.6 solar mass neutron star, evolved with the Piecewise Parabolic Method on nested grids. They calculated neutrino and antineutrino fluxes from the resulting accretion disk using neutrino surfaces determined from the model. They then constructed parameterized outflow trajectories with varying entropy (s = 10 to 50), acceleration parameter (β = 0.2 to 1.4), and starting radius (20 to 80 km), and followed the nucleosynthesis using a sequence of nuclear statistical equilibrium, charged particle reaction, and r-process network codes.

## Key numbers
- Black hole mass: 2.5 solar masses; neutron star mass: 1.6 solar masses; black hole spin parameter: 0.6
- Entropy range for main r-process production: s ≥ 30 (in units of Boltzmann's constant per baryon)
- Acceleration parameter range for main r-process: β ≥ 0.8
- Outflow final coasting velocity: 0.1c
- Starting radius range for trajectories: 20 km to 80 km
- Simulation evolved approximately 20 ms after initial contact
- For NS-NS mergers to be sole weak r-process contributor: 10^{-1} to 1 solar masses must be ejected per merger, given a Galactic rate of 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} per year
- Current estimates of mass ejected from compact object mergers: not more than 10^{-2} solar masses

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of r-process nucleosynthesis from compact object mergers, which had focused primarily on cold tidal ejecta from neutron star crusts (Meyer 1989, Goriely 2005, Freiburghaus 1999) or hot ejecta from neutron star contact surfaces in NS-NS mergers (Oechslin 2007). The paper specifically addresses the previously uncalculated hot outflows from the inner accretion torus in BH-NS mergers, enabled by state-of-the-art numerical models including neutrino emission (Ruffert and Janka 2001). The results enable future work combining these hot outflow calculations with tidal ejecta estimates to determine the complete r-process abundance pattern from BH-NS mergers, and they suggest that short-duration gamma-ray bursts associated with BH-NS mergers should produce r-process elements.
