# Pygmy resonances probed with electron scattering

## Key claim
Pygmy resonances in light neutron-rich nuclei can be studied via electron scattering at future electron-ion colliders such as ELISe/FAIR, where the virtual photon method in the plane wave Born approximation allows extraction of the same photo-nuclear cross section information as real-photon experiments, but without the non-perturbative distortion effects that complicate heavy-ion Coulomb excitation. However, the interpretation of the observed low-lying E1 strength remains ambiguous: it is unclear whether the peak in the response function arises from a direct transition to the continuum weighted by fragment phase space, or from a genuine soft dipole collective state.

## Method
The author derives the electron scattering cross section in the plane wave Born approximation using Siegert's theorem, expressing it as a product of a virtual photon spectrum and the photo-nuclear cross section. Response functions for direct breakup are calculated using a two-body model for one-neutron halo nuclei (with effective range expansion for final-state interactions) and a three-body hyperspherical harmonics model for two-neutron halo nuclei. A hydrodynamical model combining Goldhaber-Teller and Steinwedel-Jensen modes is also applied to estimate the energy and width of pygmy resonances in light weakly-bound nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For electron energy E = 500 MeV and excitation energy ΔE = 1 MeV, the virtual photon spectrum yields ratios dN_e^(E2)/dN_e^(E1) ≈ 500 and dN_e^(E3)/dN_e^(E2) ≈ 100.
- For E1 excitations in a two-body direct dissociation model, the peak occurs at E0 ≈ 3S_n/5, where S_n is the one-neutron separation energy.
- For the three-body case (e.g., 11Li), the E1 strength function peaks at E0^(E1) ≈ 1.8 S_2n, using an effective separation energy S_2n^eff = a S_2n with a ≈ 1.5.
- Using the hydrodynamical model for 11Li with a = 1 fm, R = 3 fm, and φ = S_2n = 0.3 MeV, the predicted pygmy resonance energy is E_PR = 1.3 MeV, yielding fragment kinetic energy E_r ≈ 1 MeV.
- The hydrodynamical model predicts a collective mode width Γ = ℏv̄/R; using v̄ = 3v_F/4 with E_F = 35 MeV and R = 6 fm gives Γ ≈ 6 MeV (typical for giant dipole resonances in heavy nuclei).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for studying pygmy resonances via electron scattering, building on earlier Coulomb excitation studies of neutron-rich isotopes (Lei01) and the debate over whether low-lying E1 strength represents soft dipole modes or genuine resonances (Iek93, Sac93, Sag95, Hus96). The direct dissociation model follows prior work on halo nuclei (BB86, BS92, Ots94, MOI95, KB96, TB04), while the hydrodynamical approach adapts the Goldhaber-Teller (1948) and Myers et al. (1977) collective models to weakly-bound systems. The results enable future experiments at ELISe/FAIR and RIKEN to distinguish between direct breakup and collective resonance interpretations, with implications for radiative neutron capture rates in r-process nucleosynthesis (Go98).
