# Gluon multiplication in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Hot gluons are the dominant components of the QCD plasma to be formed in future high energy heavy ion experiments. In this paper we study the elementary processes in the plasma medium for gluon multiplication based on all orders of the tree-diagrams in perturbative QCD. When applying to the chemical equilibration in the expanding system, we found that the gluons reaches chemical equilibrium well within its plasma phase. The inclusion of all the next-to-leading order processes makes the equilibration considerably faster than the simple $gg\leftrightarrow ggg$ one considered previously.

## Key claim

The paper claims that gluons in the QCD plasma formed in high-energy heavy ion collisions reach chemical equilibrium well within the plasma phase, and that including all next-to-leading order gluon multiplication processes (beyond the simple $gg\leftrightarrow ggg$ process) makes the equilibration considerably faster. However, this result depends critically on the initial gluon fugacity, and for very small initial gluon densities (around $\xi \sim 1/20$) the expansion may prevent equilibration before the critical temperature is reached.

## Method

The authors calculated reaction rates for gluon multiplication processes $gg \rightarrow (n-2)g$ using the exact matrix element for $n=5$ (Berends formula) and the Parke-Taylor formula with Kunszt-Stirling corrections for higher orders ($n=6$ to $8$), with a constant $\alpha_s = 0.3$ and a low-energy cutoff based on the Debye screening mass from lattice QCD ($2m_D = 2.1 T$). They then solved the rate equations for gluon fugacity and temperature in a longitudinally expanding gluon plasma assuming kinetic equilibration from $\tau_0 \sim 0.3$ fm/c onward, using a Boltzmann distribution parameterized by fugacity $\xi$ and temperature $T$.

## Key numbers

- Rate coefficient for $gg\rightarrow ggg$: $\alpha_5(\eta=1.1) = 2.32$ (dimensionless, where $R = \alpha_n T^4$)
- Rate coefficients for higher-order processes at $\eta=1.1$: $\alpha_6 = 0.924$, $\alpha_7 = 0.201$, $\alpha_8 = 0.0278$
- Total contribution to gluon multiplication rate from $n=6$ to $8$: $\Sigma_{n=6-8} (n-4)\alpha_n = 2.56$, comparable to the $gg\rightarrow ggg$ contribution
- Kinetic equilibration time: $\tau_0 \sim 0.3$ fm/c
- For initial fugacity $\xi_i > 0.5$, chemical equilibration happens within 1 fm/c
- For very small initial $\xi_i \approx 0.06$, the $gg\leftrightarrow ggg$ process alone can bring the system to chemical equilibrium within about 3 fm/c
- Initial temperature estimates for RHIC: $T_i \approx 500$ MeV (hot glue scenario) versus $T_i \approx 240$ MeV (standard scenario)
- Debye screening mass from lattice: $2m_D = 2.1 T$ for $T > 2-3 T_c$

## Context

This work extends the "hot glue scenario" proposed by Shuryak (1992) and provides a more complete perturbative QCD treatment of gluon multiplication than earlier studies. It directly addresses the problem of parton multiplication raised in the partonic cascade model (PCM) of Geiger and Muller (1992), questioning the validity of the Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions and the cascade approximation used there. The paper also improves upon the work of Biro et al. (1993), who studied only the $gg\rightarrow ggg$ process and concluded that gluon chemical equilibration takes too long, partly due to assuming a small initial gluon number from HIJING predictions. The results enable more accurate predictions for charm enhancement, dilepton and photon production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
