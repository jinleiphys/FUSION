# Is the Theta+ a K pi N bound state?

> Following a recent suggestion that the $Θ^+$ could be a $K πN$ bound state we perform an investigation under the light of the meson meson and meson baryon dynamics provided by the chiral Lagrangians and using methods currently employed to dynamically generate meson and baryon resonances by means of unitary extensions of chiral perturbation theory. We consider two body and three body forces and examine the possibility of a bound state below the three particle pion-kaon-nucleon and above the kaon-nucleon thresholds. Although we find indeed an attractive interaction in the case of isospin I=0 and spin-parity $1/2^+$, the interaction is too weak to bind the system. If we arbitrarily add to the physically motivated potential the needed strength to bind the system and with such strong attraction evaluate the decay width into $K N$, this turns out to be small. A discussion on further work in this direction is done.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether the Theta+ pentaquark could be a bound state of a kaon, pion, and nucleon (K pi N) using chiral Lagrangians and unitary extensions of chiral perturbation theory. Although they find an attractive interaction in the isospin I=0, spin-parity 1/2+ channel, the interaction is too weak to bind the system; only by artificially increasing the attractive potential by a factor of about five or six does a resonance appear near 1540 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a three-body scattering formalism for the K pi N system, first generating the correlated K pi pair (the kappa resonance) by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest-order chiral perturbation theory kernel and a three-momentum cutoff of 850 MeV. They then compute the kappa-nucleon scattering matrix by summing diagrams that include a contact three-body force from the meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian, a p-wave pi N exchange diagram with a monopole form factor (cutoff 1 GeV), and a nucleon-exchange diagram. The resulting t-matrix is iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation to search for poles below the K pi N threshold.

## Key numbers

- The kappa-nucleon loop function G_mB is approximately -0.05 (100 MeV)^3, and the meson-baryon potential t_mB is approximately -(2 to 3) (100 MeV)^{-3} for a cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV.
- To artificially produce a pole near sqrt(s) = 1540 MeV, an additional attractive potential of -16 (100 MeV)^{-3} must be added, which is about five to six times the calculated potential.
- With this artificially enhanced potential, the resulting resonance has a width of about Gamma = 40 MeV.
- Increasing the cutoff to Lambda = 4 GeV requires adding only -2.5 (100 MeV)^{-3} (about double the calculated potential) to produce a pole, but the resulting width becomes much larger than 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Bicudo (2003) that the Theta+ could be a K pi N bound state, studied in the context of chiral quark models. The authors use the same unitarized chiral perturbation theory techniques that have successfully generated low-lying baryonic resonances as meson-baryon quasibound states (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 2000, Jido 2003). The negative result suggests that the Theta+ is unlikely to be a K pi N molecule, and the authors recommend full Faddeev calculations and searches for a possible resonance above the K pi N threshold as next steps.
