# Relativistic three-body calculations of a Y=1, I=3/2, JP=2+ pi-Lambda-N -- pi-Sigma-N dibaryon

> The pi-Lambda-N - pi-Sigma-N coupled-channel system with quantum numbers (Y,I,JP) = (1,3/2,2+) is studied in a relativistic three-body model, using two-body separable interactions in the dominant p-wave pion-baryon and 3S1 YN channels. Three-body equations are solved in the complex energy plane to search for quasibound-state and resonance poles, producing a robust narrow pi-Lambda-N resonance about 10 - 20 MeV below the pi-Sigma-N threshold. Viewed as a dibaryon, it is a 5S2 quasibound state consisting of Sigma(1385)-N and Delta(1232)-Y components. Comparison is made between the present relativistic model calculation and a previous, outdated nonrelativistic calculation which resulted in a pi-Lambda-N bound state. Effects of adding a Kbar-N-N channel are studied and found insignificant. Possible production and decay reactions of this (Y,I,JP)=(1,3/2,2+) dibaryon are discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic three-body calculation produces a robust narrow πΛN resonance about 10–20 MeV below the πΣN threshold, in contrast to a previous nonrelativistic calculation that had predicted a πΛN bound state; the relativistic treatment removes the bound-state solution because nonrelativistic isobar propagators artificially boost the attraction by roughly a factor of three.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic three-body Faddeev equations for the πΛN–πΣN coupled-channel system with quantum numbers (Y,I,JP) = (1,3/2,2+), using energy-independent separable two-body interactions in the dominant p-wave pion-baryon channels (Δ(1232) and Σ(1385) resonances) and the 3S1 YN channel. They searched for poles in the complex energy plane by rotating the integration contour, and studied the sensitivity to the πY form-factor parametrization and to the inclusion of a K̅NN channel.

## Key numbers

- The πΛN resonance pole energy relative to the πΣN threshold ranges from E = −19.8 − i2.6 MeV (for A = 0.25 fm²) to E = −11.9 − i3.8 MeV (for A = 0.45 fm²), with sensitivity less than 10 MeV across the parametrization family.
- Neglecting the YN interaction raises the real part of the pole energy by approximately 10 MeV while the imaginary part remains almost unchanged.
- Adding the πN P13 partial wave changes the energy from E = −19.755 − i2.611 MeV to E = −19.734 − i2.613 MeV, a negligible effect.
- For the nonrelativistic model, the isobar propagators at W0 = mπ + mΛ + mN and qi = 0 are τπN = −0.091220 fm² and τπΛ = −0.042807 fm², about three times larger than the relativistic values of τπN = −0.035758 fm² and τπΛ = −0.016387 fm².
- Including the K̅NN channel with coupling parameter C = 0.2 shifts the resonance to E = −15.6 − i3.2 MeV; for C = 0.5 the resonance moves above the πΣN threshold to E = +1.8 − i6.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonrelativistic three-body calculations (Garcilazo and Gal, 2008, 2010) that had predicted a πΛN bound state, showing that a relativistic treatment dissolves that bound state into a resonance. The relativistic formalism follows the approach developed by Garcilazo and Gal (2011) for systems with p-wave two-body interactions, and is analogous to the relativistic three-body formalism applied by Ikeda and Sato (2007) to the K̅NN system. The results enable searches for this strangeness S = −1 dibaryon candidate in production reactions such as pp collisions (analogous to the DISTO collaboration's study of the K̅NN quasibound state at Tp = 2.85 GeV) and in K−d or π±d reactions, with the narrow width suggesting experimental feasibility.
