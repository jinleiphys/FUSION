# Effective Field Theory in The Study of Long Range Nuclear Parity Violation on Lattice

> A non-zero signal $A_γ^\mathrm{np}=(-3.0\pm1.4\pm0.2)\times 10^{-8}$ of the gamma-ray asymmetry in the neutron-proton capture was recently reported by the NPDGamma Collaboration which provides the first determination of the $ΔI=1$ parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_π^1=(2.6\pm 1.2\pm 0.2)\times 10^{-7}$. The ability to reproduce this value from first principles serves as a direct test of our current understanding of the interplay between the strong and weak interaction at low energy. To motivate new lattice studies of $h_π^1$, we review the current status of the theoretical understanding of this coupling, which includes our recent work that relates it to a nucleon mass-splitting by a soft-pion theorem. We further investigate the possibility of calculating the mass-splitting on the lattice by providing effective field theory parameterizations of all the involved quark contraction diagrams. We show that the lattice calculations of the easier connected diagrams will provide information of the chiral logarithms in the much harder quark loop diagrams and thus help in the chiral extrapolation of the latter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_\pi^1\) can be related to a neutron-proton mass splitting induced by an auxiliary parity-even Lagrangian through a soft-pion theorem, with the relation \(F_\pi h_\pi^1 \approx -(\delta m_N)_{4q}/\sqrt{2}\) holding at leading order in chiral perturbation theory; the authors emphasize that this tree-level matching receives quantifiable corrections from higher-order chiral loops and counterterms.

## Method

The authors apply the partially-conserved axial current (PCAC) relation to map the parity-odd matrix element \(\langle n\pi^+|\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PV}}^w|p\rangle\) to a flavor-diagonal, parity-even nucleon matrix element \(\langle p|\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PC}}^w|p\rangle\), where \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PC}}^w\) is an auxiliary Lagrangian with identical Wilson coefficients. They then use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to analyze the matching between \(h_\pi^1\) and the nucleon mass splitting \((\delta m_N)_{4q}\) at tree level and to quantify corrections from one-loop diagrams and counterterms. Finally, they provide effective field theory parameterizations of all quark contraction diagrams (connected and quark-loop) that contribute to the mass splitting, using partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) to express each diagram as a function of the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- The NPDGamma Collaboration reported a gamma-ray asymmetry in neutron-proton capture: \(A_\gamma^{\mathrm{np}} = (-3.0 \pm 1.4 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-8}\).
- From this asymmetry, the \(\Delta I=1\) parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling constant was determined as \(h_\pi^1 = (2.6 \pm 1.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-7}\).
- The DDH "reasonable range" for \(h_\pi^1\) is \((0-1) \times 10^{-6}\), with a "best guess" around \(4.6 \times 10^{-7}\).
- The only existing lattice calculation of \(h_\pi^1\) (Wasem, 2011) gave \(h_\pi^1 = (1.099 \pm 0.505^{+0.058}_{-0.064}) \times 10^{-7}\) using \(L=2.5\) fm, \(a=0.123\) fm, and \(m_\pi=389\) MeV.
- The Wilson coefficients at the scale \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV are: \(C^{(1)} = (-0.055,\; 0.810,\; -0.627)\) and \(S^{(1)} = (5.09,\; -2.55,\; 4.51,\; -3.36)\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(F_\pi = 92.1\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for the \(\Delta I=1\) parity-odd pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi^1\), building on the soft-pion theorem approach previously applied to parity- and time-reversal-odd couplings by deVries, Mereghetti, Seng, and others (2012-2016). It directly addresses the need for first-principles lattice QCD calculations of \(h_\pi^1\) to compare with the NPDGamma result, following the only prior lattice study by Wasem (2011) which had several unquantified assumptions. The paper enables future lattice calculations by showing that the easier connected quark contraction diagrams can determine the chiral logarithms needed for the chiral extrapolation of the much harder quark-loop diagrams, and by providing a parameterization of all contraction diagrams in terms of a small number of low-energy constants.
