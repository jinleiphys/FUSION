# Complex potential and open system applications in heavy-ions and cold atoms

> Since the discovery of the complex potential of quarkonium at high temperatures, quarkonium has been regarded as an open quantum system in the quark-gluon plasma. Recently, a similar issue regarding in-medium bound states of impurities has also emerged in particle physics and cold atomic physics. We will provide an overview of recent advancements in understanding key quantities such as complex potential and transport coefficients for heavy impurities in finite temperature QCD and cold atomic systems.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the complex potential for in-medium bound states, originally developed for quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma, can be extended to polarons in cold atomic gases, and that the imaginary part of the potential exhibits a universal \(1/r^2\) scaling at long distances across diverse systems, including the QGP where no massless excitations exist.

## Method
The author reviews the perturbative calculation of the complex potential for a static heavy quark pair using Hard-Thermal-Loop resummed propagators, including next-to-leading order corrections in a semi-hard regime. For quarkonium in the QGP, the Lindblad equation is derived in both the weak-coupling limit (for \(r\sim 1/gT\)) and the dipole limit (using pNRQCD), with transport coefficients expressed in terms of gauge-invariant correlation functions of color electric fields. For polarons in a superfluid, the impurity potential is calculated from two-phonon exchange using effective field theory, and the scaling of the imaginary part is analyzed via two-body scattering kinematics.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order complex potential in the soft regime (\(r\sim 1/gT\)) has a real part \(V_{\rm 1LO}(r)= -\frac{C_F g^2}{4\pi}\left(m_D + \frac{e^{-m_{\rm D}r}}{r}\right)\) and an imaginary part proportional to \(-i C_F g^2 T\int\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{\pi m_{\rm D}^2(1-e^{i\bm k\cdot\bm r})}{k(k^2+ m_{\rm D}^2)^2}\), with Debye mass \(m_D = gT\sqrt{\frac{N_c}{3}+\frac{N_f}{6}}\).
- At \(1/T \ll r\ll 1/m_D\), the binding energy is \(\sim g^2/r\) while the decay rate is \(\sim g^2 T m_D^2 r^2\); resonance peaks exist only when \(r\lesssim 1/g^{2/3}T\).
- The NLO contribution in momentum space is \(\Delta \tilde V_{\rm 2, exp}(p) = - \frac{g^4 C_F N_c T}{16\pi m_D p^2} \left[ 1 - \frac{3\pi^2}{16} + \frac{4\pi m_D}{p} + \frac{m_D^2}{p^2} \left( \frac{5\pi^2}{24} - \frac{4}{3} \right) + i\frac{\pi T m_{D}}{p^2} \left( \frac{56}{3\pi} - \left(1 - \frac{3\pi^2}{16}\right) \frac{m_D}{p} - \left(1 - \frac{N_f}{2N_c}\right) \frac{4p}{\pi T}\right) \right]\).
- For polarons in a superfluid, the imaginary part of the potential scales as \(V_{\rm Im}(r) = - \frac{a_{\rm{IM}}^2 T^7}{2\pi m^4 c_s^{10}} h(k_T r)\) with \(k_T \equiv T/c_s\), and at long distances it scales as \(1/r^2\).

## Context
This review extends the open quantum system framework for quarkonium in the QGP, originally developed from the complex potential discovery by Laine (2006) and subsequent Lindblad equation derivations by Akamatsu (2014) and Brambilla (2016, 2017), to polarons in cold atomic gases, where the impurity potential is calculated using effective field theory following Son (2005). The work contrasts lattice QCD results from Burnier (2014) (which found screening in the real part) with Bazavov (2023) (which reported no screening), and identifies a universal \(1/r^2\) scaling of the imaginary potential across superfluid, Fermi gas, and QGP systems, attributed to two-body scattering kinematics. This enables future cross-disciplinary studies of open quantum systems in high-energy and condensed matter physics.
