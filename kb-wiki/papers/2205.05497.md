# Factorial cumulants from short-range correlations and global baryon number conservation

> We calculate the baryon factorial cumulants assuming arbitrary short-range correlations and the global baryon number conservation. The general factorial cumulant generating function is derived. Various relations between factorial cumulants subjected to baryon number conservation and the factorial cumulants without this constraint are presented. We observe that for $n$-th factorial cumulant, the short-range correlations of more than $n$ particles are suppressed with the increasing number of particles. The recently published [1] relations between the cumulants in a finite acceptance with global baryon conservation and the grand-canonical susceptibilities are reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general factorial cumulant generating function for baryon number fluctuations under the combined influence of arbitrary short-range correlations and global baryon number conservation, showing that for the n-th factorial cumulant, short-range correlations involving more than n particles are suppressed as the total number of particles increases.

## Method

The authors divide a system of fixed volume into two subsystems that can exchange particles, assume short-range correlations are present only within each subsystem, and impose global baryon number conservation via a Kronecker delta. They derive the factorial cumulant generating function using the integral representation of the Kronecker delta and Faà di Bruno's formula with Bell polynomials, then relate the factorial cumulants in a finite acceptance (subject to global conservation) to the factorial cumulants without this constraint. For two-particle correlations, they also develop an approximate approach using the general Leibniz formula expanded in the correlation strength α₂.

## Key numbers

- For two-particle short-range correlations only (α₂ ≠ 0, αₖ = 0 for k ≥ 3), the first factorial cumulant is Ĉ₁^(1,B) = fB, where f is the fraction of particles in the acceptance and B is the total baryon number.
- The second factorial cumulant for two-particle correlations is Ĉ₂^(1,B) = -f²B + (1-f)f(B-1)α₂ + 3(1-f)f(B-1)/B α₂² - 5(1-f)f(B-1)(B-3)/B² α₂³ + ... (expanded in α₂).
- For large B and small αₖ, the second cumulant (not factorial) is κ₂^(1,B) ≈ (1-f)fB(α₂ + 1).
- For large B, the third cumulant is κ₃^(1,B) ≈ (1-f)f(1-2f)B(1 + 3α₂ + α₃).
- For large B, the fourth cumulant is κ₄^(1,B) ≈ (1-f)fB[(1 + 7α₂ + 6α₃ + α₄) - 3(1-f)f(2 + 12α₂ + 8α₃ + α₄)].
- The analytic results for two-particle correlations are illustrated for B = 300 and f = 0.2, with α₂ varied between -0.5 and 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Barej and Bzdak, 2020) of proton, antiproton, and mixed factorial cumulants assuming global baryon number conservation was the only source of correlations, where acceptance was governed by a binomial distribution. It addresses the argument by Vovchenko (2020) that binomial acceptance is incorrect when short-range correlations are present, instead using the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) proposed in that work. The paper reproduces the main results of Vovchenko (2020) relating cumulants in finite acceptance with global baryon conservation to grand-canonical susceptibilities, and enables more accurate modeling of baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions where both short-range correlations and conservation laws are present.
