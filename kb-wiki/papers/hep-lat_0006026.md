# Effective Chiral Lagrangians and Lattice QCD

> We propose a general method to obtain accurate estimates for some of the "low-energy constants" in the one-loop effective chiral Lagrangian by means of simulating lattice QCD. In particular, the method is sensitive to those constants whose values are required to test the hypothesis of a massless up-quark. Initial tests performed in the quenched approximation confirm that good statistical precision can be achieved. As a byproduct we obtain an accurate estimate for the ratio of pseudoscalar decay constants, F_K/F_pi, in the quenched approximation, which lies 10% below the experimental result. The quantities that serve to extract the low-energy constants also allow a test of the scaling behaviour of different discretizations of QCD and a search for the effects of dynamical quarks.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a general lattice QCD method to extract low-energy constants (LECs) of the one-loop effective chiral Lagrangian with good statistical precision, specifically targeting those constants needed to test the hypothesis of a massless up-quark. The method is tested in the quenched approximation, and the authors caution that systematic uncertainties from neglected higher-order chiral terms are estimated at ±0.2, while statistical accuracy reaches ±0.05 in the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors compute ratios of pseudoscalar decay constants (F_pi) and pseudoscalar density matrix elements (G_p) at multiple quark masses around a reference mass m_ref, defined by (M_P r_0)^2 = 3. They form the ratios R_FG(x) and R_F(x) as functions of the mass parameter x = m/m_ref, which cancel renormalization factors and allow precise continuum extrapolations. Using quenched lattice QCD with O(a) improved Wilson fermions at multiple lattice spacings, they extract LECs by forming differences Delta_R(x1,x2) and solving the one-loop quenched chiral perturbation theory expressions.

## Key numbers

- F_K/F_pi in the quenched approximation lies 10% below the experimental result (no explicit numerical value given for the ratio itself)
- Reference mass m_ref defined by (M_P r_0)^2 = 3, corresponding to y_ref = 0.3398 for F_0 = 93.3 MeV
- Simulated quark masses cover the range 0.75 ≤ x ≤ 1.4
- Statistical precision in the continuum limit: 0.4% for R_FG and 0.3% for R_F
- Low-energy constants obtained with absolute statistical accuracy of ±0.05 in the continuum limit, with systematic uncertainties estimated at ±0.2
- For the quenched parameter delta, the CP-PACS data are enclosed by delta = 0.08–0.12 when alpha_Phi = 0; with alpha_Phi = 0.5, delta must be lowered to 0.03–0.07

## Context

This work extends proposals by Cohen, Kaplan and Nelson (1999) for using lattice QCD to test the massless up-quark hypothesis, incorporating information from the mass dependence of matrix elements in addition to pseudoscalar masses. The method enables first-principles determinations of LECs such as alpha_5 and (2*alpha_8 - alpha_5) that are not directly accessible from phenomenology and are needed to resolve the ambiguity in the chiral Lagrangian identified by Kaplan and Manohar (1986). The approach also provides a framework for testing scaling behavior of different fermion discretizations and searching for effects of dynamical quarks in future unquenched simulations.
