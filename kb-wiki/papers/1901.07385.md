# $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$ as triangle singularities

> $Z_c(4430)$ discovered by the Belle and confirmed by the LHCb in $\bar{B}^0\toψ(2S)K^-π^+$ is generally considered to be a charged charmonium-like state that includes minimally two quarks and two antiquarks. $Z_c(4200)$ found in $\bar{B}^0\to J/ψK^-π^+$ by the Belle is also a good candidate of a charged charmonium-like state. We demonstrate that kinematical singularities in triangle loop diagrams induce a resonance-like behavior that can consistently explain the properties (mass, width, and Argand plot) of $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$ from the experimental analyses. The triangle diagrams include only experimentally well-established hadrons. Applying this idea to $Λ_b^0\to J/ψpπ^-$, we also identify triangle singularities that behave like $Z_c(4200)$, but no triangle diagram is available for $Z_c(4430)$. This is consistent with the LHCb's finding that their description of the $Λ_b^0\to J/ψpπ^-$ data is significantly improved by including a $Z_c(4200)$ contribution while $Z_c(4430)$ seems to hardly contribute. Even though the proposed mechanisms have uncertainty in the absolute strengths which are currently difficult to estimate, they are certainly a compelling alternative to tetraquark-based interpretations of $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$.

## Key claim

The charged charmonium-like states $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$, often regarded as genuine tetraquark states, can be consistently interpreted as kinematical triangle singularities (TS) from triangle loop diagrams formed by experimentally well-established hadrons, though the absolute strengths of these mechanisms are currently difficult to estimate.

## Method

The authors calculated triangle loop diagrams for $\bar{B}^0 \to \psi_f K^- \pi^+$ (with $\psi_f = J/\psi, \psi(2S)$) and $\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi p \pi^-$ using a model with $s$-wave interactions for the $\psi_f \pi$ final state and form factors with a cutoff $\Lambda = 1$ GeV. They identified kinematical conditions where all three intermediate particles in the loop can be simultaneously on-shell (the triangle singularity condition) and computed the resulting $\psi_f \pi$ invariant mass distributions, then fitted Breit-Wigner parameters to the TS-induced bumps and compared the Argand plot from the triangle diagram to LHCb data.

## Key numbers

* Triangle singularity for Fig. (a) ($Z_c(4430)$ mechanism) occurs at $m_{\psi(2S)\pi} = 4420$ MeV with intermediate momenta $p_1 = p_2 = 491$ MeV and $p_3 = 154$ MeV in the $\bar{B}^0$-at-rest frame.
* Triangle singularity for Fig. (b) ($Z_c(4200)$ mechanism) occurs at $m_{J/\psi\pi} = 4187$ MeV.
* Triangle singularities for Fig. (c) ($\Lambda_b^0$ decay) occur at $m_{J/\psi\pi} = 3970$ MeV (with $N(1440)$), 4004 MeV (with $N(1520)$), and 4116 MeV (with $N(1680)$).
* Breit-Wigner mass fitted to the TS bump for $Z_c(4430)$: $4463 \pm 13$ MeV (compared to Belle $4485 \pm 22^{+28}_{-11}$ MeV and LHCb $4475 \pm 7^{+15}_{-25}$ MeV).
* Breit-Wigner width fitted to the TS bump for $Z_c(4430)$: $195 \pm 16$ MeV (compared to Belle $200^{+41}_{-46}{}^{+26}_{-35}$ MeV and LHCb $172 \pm 13^{+37}_{-34}$ MeV).
* Breit-Wigner mass fitted to the TS bump for $Z_c(4200)$: $4233 \pm 48$ MeV (compared to Belle $4196^{+31}_{-29}{}^{+17}_{-13}$ MeV).
* Breit-Wigner width fitted to the TS bump for $Z_c(4200)$: $292 \pm 56$ MeV (compared to Belle $370 \pm 70^{+70}_{-132}$ MeV).
* The model reproduces the puzzling ratio $R^{\rm exp}_{Z_c(4430)} \equiv \mathcal{B}[Z_c^+(4430) \to \psi(2S)\pi^+] / \mathcal{B}[Z_c^+(4430) \to J/\psi\pi^+] \sim 11$ using $|c^R_{\psi\pi}| \sim 1.8$ derived from $\psi(4260)$ decay data.

## Context

This work extends the triangle singularity interpretation previously applied to $P_c(4450)^+$ (ruled out by recent LHCb data) and $Z_c(3900)$ (disfavored by Ref. TS-Zc3900-2) to the charged charmonium-like states $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$. It contradicts the prevailing tetraquark interpretation (diquark-antidiquark models by various authors, hadronic molecule models) and provides a natural explanation for why $Z_c(4430)$ is absent in $\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi p \pi^-$ and why $Z_c$ states observed in $e^+e^-$ annihilations and $B$ decays are mutually exclusive. The work enables a re-evaluation of whether these states are genuine tetraquarks or kinematic artifacts, and suggests that identifying a genuine tetraquark requires observing it in different processes with different initial states.
