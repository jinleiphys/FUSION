# Shell-model study for GT-strengths corresponding to $β$ decay of $^{60}$Ge and $^{62}$Ge

> In the present work, we have reported a comprehensive shell-model study of GT-strengths for recently available experimental data for $^{60}$Ga and $^{62}$Ga from RIKEN Nishina Center [Phys. Rev. C 103, 014324 (2021)] populated by $β$ decay of the $^{60}$Ge and $^{62}$Ge, respectively. We have performed shell-model calculations in two different model spaces, the first set of calculations in the $fp$ model space using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions, while the second set in $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space using JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions. Our shell-model results in $fp$ model space are in a reasonable agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The shell-model calculations in the $fp$ model space (using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions) show reasonable agreement with the experimental Gamow-Teller strengths for the $\beta^+$ decay of $^{60}$Ge and $^{62}$Ge, but the agreement is better for the $fp$ space than for the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space, indicating the importance of the $f_{7/2}$ orbital.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in two model spaces: the $fp$ space (with $^{40}$Ca as inert core, using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions) and the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space (with $^{56}$Ni as inert core, using JUN45 and jj44b interactions). Truncation was imposed in the $fp$ space due to computational limitations: for $^{60}$Ge, a minimum of six protons were fixed in the $f_{7/2}$ orbital; for $^{62}$Ge, a minimum of six protons/neutrons and two in the $f_{7/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ orbitals, respectively. The quenching factors used were q = 0.684 $\pm$ 0.015 for JUN45 and jj44b, and q = 0.660 $\pm$ 0.016 for KB3G and GXPF1A.

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{60}$Ga: experimental half-life 25.0(3) ms; calculated half-lives range from 23.5 ms (jj44b) to 87.9 ms (GXPF1A), with KB3G giving 42.7 ms and JUN45 giving 28.2 ms.
- For $^{62}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{62}$Ga: experimental half-life 73.5(1) ms; calculated half-lives range from 112.1 ms (jj44b) to 576.0 ms (GXPF1A), with KB3G giving 417.9 ms and JUN45 giving 117.8 ms.
- For $^{60}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{60}$Ga: experimental Q-value 12.338(27) MeV; calculated Q-values range from 12.907 MeV (KB3G) to 14.904 MeV (JUN45).
- For $^{62}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{62}$Ga: experimental Q-value 9.730(140$^\#$) MeV; calculated Q-values range from 10.839 MeV (JUN45 and jj44b) to 11.074 MeV (GXPF1A).
- Seven experimental GT transitions observed for $^{60}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{60}$Ga up to excitation energy 3.580 MeV; twelve experimental GT transitions observed for $^{62}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{62}$Ga up to excitation energy 3.595 MeV.
- For $^{60}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{60}$Ga, the lowest $1^+$ state B(GT) experimental value is 0.11(3); calculated values range from 0.0053 (JUN45) to 0.0636 (GXPF1A).
- For $^{62}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{62}$Ga, the lowest $1^+$ state B(GT) experimental value is 0.068(6); calculated values range from 0.0000 (KB3G and GXPF1A) to 0.1904 (jj44b).

## Context

This work extends the shell-model study of Gamow-Teller strengths for $fp$-shell nuclei, motivated by recent experimental data from RIKEN Nishina Center (Orrigo, 2021) which provided improved half-lives and new excited states for $^{60,62}$Ga. The calculations complement earlier experimental work on $\beta$ decay of $^{60}$Ge at NSCL, MSU (Ciemny, 2016) and $^{62}$Ge at GANIL (Lopez, 2001), as well as prior shell-model studies of GT transitions in the $A=42-48$ region by Pittel et al. The results demonstrate that the $f_{7/2}$ orbital plays a crucial role in reproducing the experimental GT strengths, and the present theoretical work adds more information to the experimental data reported by Orrigo (2021).
