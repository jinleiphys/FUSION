# Nuclear pasta and supernova neutrinos at late times

> Nuclear pasta, with nucleons arranged into tubes, sheets, or other complex shapes, is expected in core collapse supernovae (SNe) at just below nuclear density. We calculate the additional opacity from neutrino-pasta coherent scattering using molecular dynamics simulations. We approximately include this opacity in simulations of SNe. We find that pasta slows neutrino diffusion and greatly increases the neutrino signal at late times of 10 or more seconds after stellar core collapse. This signal, for a galactic SN, should be clearly visible in large detectors such as Super-Kamiokande.

# Nuclear pasta and supernova neutrinos at late times

## Key claim
Nuclear pasta structures in proto-neutron stars can slow neutrino diffusion and significantly enhance the late-time neutrino signal from a galactic core-collapse supernova at times of 10 or more seconds after core collapse, but this result depends on a parametric enhancement factor of ξ=5 and a critical pasta temperature of 10 MeV, and the authors note that changing these parameters will impact the details of how pasta affects their simulations.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale semiclassical molecular dynamics simulations with 51,200 nucleons to calculate the neutron static structure factor S_n(q) for nuclear pasta configurations at densities from 0.01 to 0.075 fm^{-3}, temperature T=1 MeV, and proton fraction Y_p=0.4. They then parametrically included the resulting opacity enhancement in spherically symmetric radiation hydrodynamics simulations of a 15 M_sun progenitor star evolved from core collapse through 100 seconds of proto-neutron star cooling, using the LS220 equation of state and mixing-length theory for convection.

## Key numbers
- The vector response S_V(E_ν) from molecular dynamics simulations can be as large as approximately 50 at certain densities and neutrino energies.
- The opacity enhancement factor ξ from pasta is estimated to be as large as approximately 5 from molecular dynamics, and up to 5 to 10 from spherical pasta models using the HS(DD2) equation of state.
- Pasta phases are found for baryon densities n_b = 0.03 to 0.11 fm^{-3} at Y_p=0.3 up to T=11 MeV, and for n_b = 0.04 to 0.09 fm^{-3} at Y_p=0.1 up to T=7 MeV.
- For a galactic supernova at 10 kpc in Super-Kamiokande (32 kT), the model without pasta yields 51 ± 7 counts after 10 seconds, while the model with pasta yields 181 ± 13 counts after 10 seconds.
- The total number of inverse beta decay events from anti-electron neutrinos is about 3400 for both models combined.
- During the infall phase, pasta suppresses ν_x luminosities by approximately 3-4 times in the millisecond preceding bounce, but the luminosity at that point is only about 10^49 erg/s.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of nuclear pasta structure (Schuetrumpf 2016, Watanabe 2012) and neutrino-pasta coherent scattering (Horowitz 2004) by incorporating the opacity into full proto-neutron star cooling simulations for the first time. The results contradict the expectation that pasta would have negligible observational impact, and instead suggest that the late-time neutrino signal in detectors such as Super-Kamiokande, Hyper-Kamiokande, and DUNE could provide a clear signature of nuclear pasta shapes. The work also connects to studies of pasta in older neutron stars where disordered pasta could cause magnetic field decay (Pons 2013) and slow crust cooling (PhysRevLett.114.031102, Deibel 2016).
