# Non-equilibrium phase transition in relativistic nuclear collisions: Importance of the equation of state

> Within the context of relativistic nuclear collisions aimed at exploring hot and baryon-dense matter, we investigate how the general features of the expansion dynamics, as well as a number of specific observables, depend on the equation of state used in dynamical simulations of the non-equilibrium confinement phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the qualitative nature of the expansion dynamics and observable signals in relativistic nuclear collisions depends critically on whether the equation of state exhibits phase coexistence between compressed nuclear matter and a dense quark phase (as in the HQ model) or between vacuum and a dense quark phase (as in liquid-gas-type models like the Polyakov Quark Meson model), with the latter producing unrealistic dynamics due to vanishing coexistence pressure at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors performed ideal fluid-dynamic simulations using the SHASTA code, augmenting the pressure with a gradient term to capture spinodal instabilities. They compared two equations of state: a hadron-quark (HQ) model constructed by interpolating between an ideal hadron gas with a density-dependent interaction and an idealized quark-gluon plasma with a bag constant, and a Polyakov Quark Meson (PQM) model representing liquid-gas-type behavior. Spherically symmetric systems were initialized with Woods-Saxon density profiles at a temperature of 100 MeV, with random density fluctuations of up to ±20% added as seeds for instability growth.

## Key numbers

- The HQ model has a critical temperature of approximately 143 MeV for the phase transition
- The PQM model has a critical endpoint at μ_crit ≈ 480 MeV and T_crit ≈ 165 MeV
- The HQ model uses a bag constant B = 300 MeV/fm³ for the quark phase
- The gradient term range parameter a = 0.033 fm yields a surface tension of ≈10 MeV/fm² for the HQ model and ≈2 MeV/fm² for the PQM model
- The pseudo-critical pressure at T× = 153 MeV and μ = 0 from lattice QCD is approximately 50 MeV/fm³, corresponding to cold nuclear matter at roughly three times ground state density (ρ = 3 ρ_s with ρ_s = 0.153 fm⁻³)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model presented by Steinheimer (2012) and Steinheimer (2013) to compare two qualitatively different classes of equations of state for the QCD phase transition. The HQ model follows the approach of Randrup (2010) and produces behavior consistent with lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2012) showing increasing pressure at lower temperatures, while the PQM model exhibits liquid-gas-type behavior similar to that seen in nuclear matter. The results enable future experimental searches for phase transition signals at facilities like RHIC and the future FAIR facility, though the authors note that current lattice QCD calculations cannot distinguish between the two scenarios due to differing definitions of the pseudo-critical line.
