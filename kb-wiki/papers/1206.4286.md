# Acceptance corrections to net baryon and net charge cumulants

> We show that the effect of finite acceptance drastically influences the net-baryon and net-charge cumulants, which are believed to be sensitive probes of the QCD phase diagram. We derive the general formulae that relate the true cumulants K_{n} which reflect the full dynamics of the system with the actually measured cumulants c_{n} for a given acceptance, modeled by a binomial probability parameter p. We find that this relation involves additional moments which cannot be expressed by cumulants and should be measured in order to extract any potential information about the QCD critical point. We demonstrate that for a wide range of the true cumulant ratios K_{n}/K_{m} the measured ratios c_{n}/c_{m} quickly converge if p < 1/2, which makes the interpretation of the data very challenging, especially in case of the net-proton cumulants. Our study further suggests that the measurement of net-charge cumulants may be more advantageous for the investigation of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

Finite acceptance, modeled by a binomial probability parameter \(p\), drastically influences measured net-baryon and net-charge cumulants; the relation between true cumulants \(K_n\) and measured cumulants \(c_n\) involves additional factorial moments that cannot be expressed by cumulants alone, so that measuring only cumulants is insufficient to extract information about the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors model acceptance effects by folding the true joint probability distribution \(P(N_1,N_2)\) (for baryons/anti-baryons or positive/negative charges) with a binomial distribution parameterized by acceptance probabilities \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) (assumed equal, \(p=p_1=p_2\), for the main results). They derive explicit relations connecting the measured cumulants \(c_n\) (up to \(c_4\)) to the true cumulants \(K_n\) and to factorial moments \(F_{ik}\) of the underlying distribution, then invert these relations to express \(K_n\) in terms of measurable cumulants \(c_m\) and factorial moments \(f_{ik}\). Numerical examples are presented for the ratios \(c_3/c_1\) and \(c_4/c_2\) as functions of \(p\) for various assumed values of the true cumulant ratios.

## Key numbers

- For net-proton cumulants at STAR, the effective acceptance parameter is estimated as \(p \approx 1/5\) (based on the fact that only protons are measured instead of all baryons, plus detection efficiencies and momentum cuts).
- At \(p=1/2\), the measured ratio \(c_3/c_1\) shifts to positive values even if the true ratio \(K_3/K_1\) is negative; for \(p=1/5\) it becomes practically impossible to distinguish different values of \(K_3/K_1\) unless \(K_3/K_1 \ll -1\).
- For \(c_4/c_2\) at \(p=1/5\), all lines for different \(K_4/K_2\) values are practically indistinguishable unless \(|K_4/K_2| \gg 5\) (of order 50).
- In the limit \(p \to 0\), the measured cumulant ratios converge to \(c_3/c_1 = 1\) and \(c_4/c_2 = 1\) (Skellam/Poisson limit).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of acceptance corrections to variance (second-order cumulants) of net charge and net baryon distributions (Bleicher 2000, Pruneau 2002, Bower 2001) and to factorial moments (Brooks 1996, Kirejczyk 2004), by focusing on higher-order cumulants central to QCD critical point searches. The results directly challenge the interpretation of net-proton cumulant measurements from the RHIC beam energy scan (Aggarwal 2010) and explain the large difference between net-baryon and net-proton cumulants observed in the UrQMD model (Schuster 2009). The paper enables a practical path forward: by measuring additional factorial moments \(f_{ik}\) alongside cumulants, one can extract the true cumulants \(K_n\) and thus recover information about the QCD phase diagram; it also suggests that net-charge cumulants may be more advantageous because acceptance corrections are smaller (no neutron problem, \(p\) likely larger than \(1/2\)).
