# The $πσ$-Axial Exchange Current

> The axial exchange current operator that arises from the coupling of the axial field to the pion and effective scalar field in nuclei is constructed. The spatial dependence of this exchange current operator may be determined directly from the nucleon-nucleon interaction. This "$πσ$"-axial exchange current contributes about 5\% to the quenching of the nucleon axial current coupling constant $g_A^{GT}$ in heavy nuclei.

# The πσ-Axial Exchange Current

## Key claim

The πσ-axial exchange current contributes about 5% to the quenching of the nucleon axial current coupling constant g_A^GT in heavy nuclei, but this estimate depends on the nucleon-nucleon interaction model used; the Paris potential gives only 2.3% quenching while the Bonn potential gives 5.5% at nuclear matter density after short-range correlations are included.

## Method

The authors constructed the axial exchange current operator arising from coupling of the axial field to the pion and effective scalar (σ) field using a modified linear σ-model Lagrangian. They reduced this two-body exchange current to an effective single-nucleon correction and evaluated it using the Fermi gas model with nuclear matter density, then computed numerical values using the parametrized Paris potential and the Bonn potential models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, including short-range correlations via G-matrix calculations.

## Key numbers

- The πσ-mechanism contributes δ_A = 0.024 (2.4% quenching) using the Paris potential at nuclear matter density, reducing to δ_A = 0.023 after short-range correlations
- The πσ-mechanism contributes δ_A = 0.060 (6.0% quenching) using the Bonn potential at nuclear matter density, reducing to δ_A = 0.055 after short-range correlations
- The qualitative estimate using m_σ ≈ m_N and k_F = 1.40 fm^{-1} yields δ_A ≈ 0.066 (6.6% quenching)
- Combined with Δ_33 excitation, πρ exchange and short range exchange currents (20-23% quenching from Tsushima and Riska (1992)), the predicted net exchange current quenching becomes 25-28% at nuclear matter density
- The integral in the Paris potential calculation gave -3.60 fm^4; the Bonn potential gave -8.98 fm^4

## Context

This work extends the study of axial current quenching mechanisms in nuclei, where shell model analyses by Brown and Wildenthal (1983) and studies of giant Gamow-Teller resonances by Gaarde et al. (1980) indicate about 30% quenching of g_A^GT already in sd-shell nuclei. The conventional explanation involves Δ_33-excitations (Rho (1974); Ohta and Wakamatsu (1974)) and core polarization (Arima et al. (1987)). The authors note that the 5% πσ contribution, when combined with the large 40% core polarization estimate from Arima et al. (1987) based on the Hamada-Johnston potential, would overpredict quenching by a factor of about 2, suggesting that core polarization calculation should be redone with a weaker modern tensor potential. The πσ mechanism also has implications for solar neutrino reactions including pp → d + e^+ + ν_e and p + ^3He → ^4He + e^+ + ν_e.
