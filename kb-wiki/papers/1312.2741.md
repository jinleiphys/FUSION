# Importance of $1n$-stripping process in the $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb reaction

> The inclusive cross sections of the $α$-particles produced in the reaction $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb have been measured at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The measured cross sections are found to be orders of magnitude larger than the calculated cross sections of $^{6}$Li breaking into $α$ and $d$ fragments, thus indicating contributions from other processes. The experimental cross sections of $1n$-stripping and $1n$-pickup processes have been determined from an entirely different measurement, reported earlier. Apart from incomplete fusion and/ $d$-transfer processes, the $1n$-stripping process is found to be a significant contributor to the inclusive $α$-particle cross sections in this reaction.

# Importance of $1n$-stripping process in the $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb reaction

## Key claim

The $1n$-stripping process is a significant contributor to the inclusive $\alpha$-particle cross sections in the $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb reaction at energies around the Coulomb barrier, but this conclusion is qualified by the fact that ground-state transfer cross sections could not be measured and the $1n$-stripping cross sections reported exclude that contribution.

## Method

Inclusive $\alpha$-particle angular distributions were measured using $\Delta$E-E Si-surface barrier telescopes at five beam energies (23, 25, 27, 30, and 35 MeV) from the BARC-TIFR Pelletron accelerator. The no-capture breakup (NCBU) cross sections were calculated using continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations with the code FRESCO, assuming a $^{6}$Li $\alpha$+$d$ cluster structure. Contributions from $d$-incomplete fusion (ICF), $1n$-stripping, and $1n$-pickup processes were extracted from previously measured $\gamma$-ray spectra of the same reaction, and $1n$-stripping cross sections were compared with distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive $\alpha$-particle cross sections were measured at $E_{lab}$ = 23, 25, 27, 30, and 35 MeV for the $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb reaction.
- The CDCC-calculated NCBU cross sections were found to be orders of magnitude smaller than the measured inclusive $\alpha$-particle cross sections at all energies.
- The $1n$-stripping cross sections (excluding ground-state transfer) were much larger than the calculated NCBU cross sections, in contradiction to the $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi result reported by Santra (2012).
- The sum of $d$-ICF and $1n$-stripping cross sections (excluding ground-state transfer) lay very close to the measured total inclusive $\alpha$-particle cross sections.
- DWBA calculations for $1n$-stripping to the 63.68 keV (1$^-$) state of $^{160}$Tb required a spectroscopic factor of 0.25 (rather than 1.0) to fit the measured cross sections at higher energies.
- The $1n$-stripping cross sections were extracted from the 63.68 keV $\gamma$-ray of $^{160}$Tb, corrected for an internal conversion coefficient $\alpha_T$ = 15.1 with an assumed 10% uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends systematic measurements of complete and incomplete fusion for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{159}$Tb and $^{10,11}$B+$^{159}$Tb by Mukherjee (2006, 2010) and Pradhan (2011), which showed that complete fusion suppression correlates with $\alpha$-breakup threshold. The finding that $1n$-stripping dominates over direct breakup in $^{6}$Li+$^{159}$Tb agrees with Luong (2011, 2013) for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{207,208}$Pb and $^{209}$Bi, but contradicts Santra (2012) for $^{6}$Li+$^{209}$Bi, where direct breakup was reported as dominant. The work enables future exclusive measurements and theoretical descriptions of transfer-breakup processes in weakly bound projectile reactions, particularly for lighter systems and halo nuclei.
