# String percolation and the first LHC data

> The results of string percolation on multiplicities and elliptic flow in AA and pp collisions are compared with LHC data showing a good agreement. We discuss the rapidity long range correlations and its relation to the height and longitudinal extension of the ridge structure. Finally we show that the dependence of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio on the temperature, presents a minimum close to the critical temperature remaining small in the range of the RHIC and LHC energies.

# String percolation and the first LHC data

## Key claim
String percolation theory successfully describes LHC data on multiplicities and elliptic flow in both AA and pp collisions, but the agreement depends on incorporating energy conservation effects that suppress string formation at lower energies, with the number of strings growing as $N_A^{1+\alpha(s)}$ where $\alpha(s)$ varies from 0 to 1/3 depending on collision energy.

## Method
The authors apply two-dimensional percolation theory to color strings modeled as disks of radius $r_0 \simeq 0.2$ fm in the impact parameter plane. They compute particle densities, transverse momentum distributions, and elliptic flow using the color reduction factor $F(\eta_t) = \sqrt{(1-e^{-\eta_t})/\eta_t}$, where $\eta_t$ is the transverse string density, and derive the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from relativistic kinetic theory combined with the Hawking-Unruh temperature of the percolating cluster.

## Key numbers
- String disk radius: $r_0 \simeq 0.2$ fm
- Critical string density for homogeneous surface: $\eta_t^c \simeq 1.2$; for realistic profiles: $\eta_t^c \simeq 1.5$
- Power law exponent for pp multiplicity energy dependence: $\lambda = 0.23$ in $dn/dy|_{pp} = a(s/m_p)^{\lambda/2}$
- The formula for $p_T$ distributions (equation 10) is valid up to $p_T \simeq 5$ GeV/c
- At high density, the forward-backward correlation parameter $b$ approaches $1/2$
- The critical percolation value for minimum bias pp collisions is reached close to $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV

## Context
This work extends the string percolation model previously applied to RHIC data (Bautista, Dias de Deus, Pajares, 2004-2010) to the first LHC measurements from ALICE. The approach is contrasted with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, which also produces negative binomial distributions and similar predictions for the ridge structure and forward-backward correlations. The results enable predictions for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as a function of temperature, showing a minimum near the critical temperature that remains small across the RHIC and LHC energy range.
