# Investigation of the neutron structure function via semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off the deuteron

> The production of slow nucleons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off the deuteron is investigated in the region $x \gsim 0.3$ for kinematical conditions accessible at $HERA$. Within the spectator mechanism the semi-inclusive cross section exhibits a scaling property, which can be used as a model-independent test of the dominance of the spectator mechanism itself, providing in this way an interesting tool to investigate the neutron structure function. The possibility of extracting model-independent information on the neutron to proton structure function ratio from semi-inclusive experiments is also illustrated.

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive cross section for slow nucleon production in electron-deuteron scattering exhibits a "spectator scaling" property that can serve as a model-independent test of the dominance of the spectator mechanism, but only in the region x ≳ 0.3 and at kinematical conditions accessible at HERA; the scaling is fulfilled within 10% at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)², and violations from target fragmentation become relevant only at x* ≲ 0.4.

## Method

The author calculates the semi-inclusive cross section for the processes ²H(e,e'p)X and ²H(e,e'n)X within the spectator mechanism, where the virtual photon is absorbed by a quark in one nucleon and the recoiling spectator nucleon is detected. The calculations use the Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction for the deuteron momentum distribution and the SLAC parametrization of nucleon structure functions, with electron beam energy E_e = 30 GeV and Q² = 10 (GeV/c)², varying the Bjorken variable x from 0.20 to 0.95 and the nucleon detection angle θ₂ from 10° to 150°. The spectator-scaling function F^(sp) is defined in terms of the variable x* that depends on both electron and nucleon kinematics, and the ratio R^(s.i.) of proton to neutron semi-inclusive cross sections is examined for its scaling properties.

## Key numbers

- Spectator scaling is fulfilled within **10%** at Q² = 10 (GeV/c)² for p₂ = 0.5 GeV/c across the x-range 0.20 to 0.95
- The Bjorken variable x is varied in the range **0.20 to 0.95** and nucleon detection angle θ₂ from **10° to 150°**
- Calculations are performed at spectator momenta p₂ = **0.1, 0.3, and 0.5 GeV/c**
- Target fragmentation effects produce relevant violations of spectator scaling only at x* ≲ **0.4**
- The relevance of fragmentation processes drastically decreases when p₂ < **0.5 GeV/c**

## Context

This work extends the spectator mechanism approach from Simula (1996) to HERA kinematics, addressing the long-standing problem of extracting the neutron structure function from deuteron data, which previously required subtraction of Fermi motion effects and contributions from mesons and isobars (as in the inclusive DIS data analyses). The proposed method enables model-independent extraction of the neutron to proton structure function ratio R^(n/p) from semi-inclusive data, and the pattern of spectator-scaling violations could provide information on off-shell nucleon structure function behavior, as suggested by the works of Dieperink and Tjon (1986) and Huang and Tjon (1990).
