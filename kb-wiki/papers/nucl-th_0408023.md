# Non-monotonic behavior of multiplicity fluctuations

> We discuss recently measured event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is shown that the non-monotonic behavior of the multiplicity fluctuations as a function of collision centrality can be fully explained by the correlations between produced particles.

# Non-monotonic behavior of multiplicity fluctuations

## Key claim
The non-monotonic dependence of the normalized variance Var(N)/⟨N⟩ on collision centrality (number of participants) can be fully explained by correlations between produced particles, specifically nuclear and electromagnetic interactions, without invoking more exotic mechanisms such as critical phenomena or percolation.

## Method
The authors derive an analytical expression linking the normalized variance of multiplicity to the two-particle correlation function ν₂(r). They model the correlation function using a combination of repulsive electrostatic (Debye) and attractive nuclear (Yukawa) potentials, and also consider a dipole-dipole interaction scenario. The integral for the normalized variance is calculated via Monte-Carlo method assuming a homogeneous spherical production volume proportional to the number of participants, and the results are fitted to NA49 data at 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- The ratio Var(N)/⟨N⟩ for negatively charged particles shows a peak at N_w ≈ 70 (number of wounded nucleons) and is close to unity at fully peripheral (N_w ≤ 10) and completely central (N_w ≥ 250) collisions.
- The measurement was performed at collision energy 158 AGeV in transverse momentum interval (0.005, 1.5) GeV and pion rapidity interval (4.0, 5.5), with about 20% of all produced negative particles used in the analysis.
- For the two-potential model, the nuclear interaction length λ_n = 1.5 fm and the electrostatic Debye length λ_e = 3.5 fm.
- For the dipole model, the correlation length parameter λ = 2.6 fm provides an approximate description of experimental data.
- The peak in Var(N)/⟨N⟩ for charged particles occurs at number of participants N_P ≈ 35.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Rybczynski 2004) connecting the Φ-measure of transverse momentum fluctuations to multiplicity fluctuations, and directly addresses the first NA49 data on multiplicity fluctuations as a function of collision centrality (Gazdzicki 2004). The paper shows that the observed non-monotonic behavior can be explained by standard nuclear and electromagnetic correlations, contradicting interpretations requiring critical phenomena or percolation. The results enable future studies of how the effect depends on energy, since particle density n changes with energy and the correlation length λ_e scales as 1/√n.
