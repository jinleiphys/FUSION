# Application of Nilsson model for deformed nucleus in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Electron scattering methods, involving nucleus which have little or no intrinsic deformation suggest nucleon distribution to be of Fermi type. This distribution is further parameterised as Wood Saxon (WS) distribution, where an uniform charge density with smoothed-out surface have been implemented. Incorporating shape modification in WS, earlier attempts were made to explain observables in deformed nuclear collisions, such as charged particle multiplicity. In this work, we use an alternate approach known as Nilsson model or Modified Harmonic Oscillator (MHO), to explain charged particle multiplicity in U+U collisions at top RHIC energy. We have implemented the formalism in HIJING model and we found that the model describes the experimental data to an extent.

## Key claim

The Nilsson (Modified Harmonic Oscillator) model, implemented within the HIJING event generator, describes charged particle multiplicity in U+U collisions at top RHIC energy "to an extent," but the paper does not claim that it fully reproduces experimental data; the model shows improvement over Wood-Saxon in central collisions but diverges in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors derived the leading-order Thomas-Fermi nucleon density distribution from the Nilsson potential (Modified Harmonic Oscillator) for a deformed uranium nucleus, including quadrupole deformation parameter epsilon_2. They implemented this density distribution in the Glauber formalism within the HIJING Monte Carlo model to calculate charged particle multiplicity and pseudorapidity distributions for U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV, considering random, tip-tip (theta = 0), and body-body (theta = pi/2) orientations of the deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV (top RHIC energy) were simulated.
- The surface radius of undistorted uranium nucleus is R_A = 1.2 * A^(1/3) with A = 238.
- The oscillator energy parameter is hbar * omega_00 = 41 * A^(-1/3) MeV, giving approximately 6.6 MeV for uranium.
- Tip-tip configuration yields non-zero probability for charged particle multiplicity approximately twice the magnitude of random configurations.
- For most central collisions (0-5%), ratios of particle yields from Au+Au to U+U tip-tip configurations differ by a factor of approximately 5.
- In peripheral collisions, tip-tip configuration shows 60% lower yields than Au+Au, while body-body or random configurations show 2% higher yields than Au+Au.
- Average transverse momentum <p_T> for body and random configurations is independent of centrality and remains flat, while other configurations show approximately 20% deviation from random configurations.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that used Modified Wood-Saxon (MWS) distributions for deformed uranium collisions, which overestimated particle multiplicities. The authors cite earlier electron scattering experiments (e.g., work by Hofstadter and collaborators) that suggested Fermi-type distributions for spherical nuclei, and theoretical work by Bengtsson et al. (1989) on the Nilsson model. The approach enables orientation-specific studies of deformed nuclei in heavy ion collisions, with potential applications for understanding initial-state geometry effects on quark-gluon plasma observables.
