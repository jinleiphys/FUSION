# Forging the link between nuclear reactions and nuclear structure

> A comprehensive description of all single-particle properties associated with the nucleus ${}^{40}$Ca is generated by employing a nonlocal dispersive optical potential capable of simultaneously reproducing all relevant data above and below the Fermi energy. The introduction of nonlocality in the absorptive potentials yields equivalent elastic differential cross sections as compared to local versions but changes the absorption profile as a function of angular momentum suggesting important consequences for the analysis of nuclear reactions. Below the Fermi energy, nonlocality is essential to allow for an accurate representation of particle number and the nuclear charge density. Spectral properties implied by $(e,e'p)$ and $(p,2p)$ reactions are correctly incorporated, including the energy distribution of about 10% high-momentum nucleons, as experimentally determined by data from Jefferson Lab. These high-momentum nucleons provide a substantial contribution to the energy of the ground state, indicating a residual attractive contribution from higher-body interactions for ${}^{40}$Ca of about 0.64 MeV/$A$.

## Key claim

A nonlocal dispersive optical potential can simultaneously describe all single-particle properties of ⁴⁰Ca above and below the Fermi energy, but only if nonlocality is introduced in the absorptive potentials; local absorptive potentials fail to reproduce particle number and charge density.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonlocal dispersive optical model (DOM) potential for ⁴⁰Ca by solving the Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator, using a self-energy that includes a nonlocal Hartree-Fock term and nonlocal volume and surface imaginary potentials. The imaginary parts were allowed different nonlocality parameters above and below the Fermi energy, and the potential was fitted simultaneously to elastic scattering data up to 200 MeV, total and reaction cross sections, charge density from electron scattering, and high-momentum spectral functions from Jefferson Lab (e,e'p) data on ²⁷Al and ⁵⁶Fe. The fit also incorporated bound-state properties including particle number, charge density, and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for quasi-hole states in ⁴⁰Ca: 0.78 for the 1s₁/₂ level and 0.76 for the 0d₃/₂ level.
- Nonlocality parameters for volume absorption: 0.64 fm above the Fermi energy and 0.81 fm below the Fermi energy.
- Nonlocality parameters for surface absorption: 0.94 fm above the Fermi energy and 2.07 fm below the Fermi energy.
- Particle numbers obtained: 19.88 protons and 19.79 neutrons (for ℓ ≤ 5 below ε_F).
- 10.6% of protons in ⁴⁰Ca have momenta above 1.4 fm⁻¹, consistent with Jefferson Lab data.
- Binding energy from two-body interactions: 7.91 MeV/A (kinetic energy 22.64 MeV/A), compared to experimental 8.55 MeV/A.
- Residual attractive contribution from higher-body interactions: 0.64 MeV/A for ⁴⁰Ca.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical model of Mahaux and Sartor (1991) by introducing nonlocality in the absorptive potentials, overcoming limitations of local treatments that could not reproduce particle number or charge density (Dickhoff, 2010). The nonlocal DOM provides a unified framework linking elastic scattering, bound-state properties, and high-momentum nucleon distributions, enabling future reanalysis of (e,e'p) reactions from NIKHEF (Kramer, 1989) and predictions for exotic nuclei where few weakly-interacting probes are available.
