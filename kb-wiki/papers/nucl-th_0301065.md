# Aspects of Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is a nonperturbative phenomenon that may be studied using QCD's gap equation. Model-independent results can be obtained with a nonperturbative and symmetry preserving truncation. The gap equation yields the massive dressed-quark propagator, which has a spectral representation when considered as a function of the current-quark mass. This enables an explication of the connection between the infrared limit of the QCD Dirac operator's spectrum and the quark condensate appearing in the operator product expansion.

## Key claim

Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) can be studied model-independently using QCD's gap equation with a nonperturbative and symmetry-preserving truncation, but the connection between the infrared limit of the QCD Dirac operator's spectrum and the quark condensate in the operator product expansion requires careful renormalisation and holds only on a specific domain where the current-quark bare mass is extremely small relative to the regularisation scale.

## Method

The paper uses QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), specifically the gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator, together with a systematic dressed-loop expansion of the quark-gluon vertex that preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity order by order. This truncation scheme is applied to a model with a momentum-independent gluon propagator (Munczek-Nemirovsky model) to obtain algebraic gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for pseudoscalar and vector mesons. The trace of the renormalised massive dressed-quark propagator is then analysed as a function of current-quark mass to establish a spectral representation and connect the Banks-Casher relation to the operator product expansion (OPE) condensate.

## Key numbers

- The infrared dressed-quark mass in the chiral limit is predicted to be M(0) ~ 0.5 GeV.
- In the Munczek-Nemirovsky model with G = 0.48 GeV, the chiral-limit pion mass is exactly 0 GeV at all truncation orders (n=0,1,2,infinity), while the rho meson mass converges from 0.678 GeV (n=0) to 0.754 GeV (n=infinity) in the chiral limit.
- Including the first correction beyond rainbow-ladder (n=1) gives a vector meson mass that differs from the fully resummed result by less than 1%.
- The OPE condensate is estimated as |<qbar q>^0| ~ Lambda_QCD^3.
- For a lattice coupling beta = 5.4, the spectral density at zero eigenvalue satisfies pi rho(0) a^3 / V ~ (0.95)^3.
- The domain where the Banks-Casher relation connects to the OPE condensate requires m_delta^bm(Lambda) ~ -<qbar q>^0 / Lambda^2 ~ 10^{-9} for Lambda = 2.0 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic truncation scheme for Dyson-Schwinger equations introduced by Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1996) and further developed by Bender, Detmold, Thomas, and Roberts (2002). It provides a rigorous connection between the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1980) and the OPE quark condensate, resolving a long-standing ambiguity about the regularisation dependence of the spectral representation. The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice-QCD simulations of the Dirac operator spectrum, such as those by Bittner, Markum, and Pullirsch (2001), and provide a framework for understanding how confinement and DCSB manifest in the eigenvalue density.
