# Hadron Structure: Perspective and Insights

> The bulk of visible mass is supposed to emerge from nonperturbative dynamics within quantum chromodynamics (QCD) -- the strong interaction sector of the Standard Model. Following years of development and refinement, continuum and lattice Schwinger function methods have recently joined in revealing the three pillars that support this emergent hadron mass (EHM); namely, a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses. One may argue that EHM and confinement are inextricably linked; and theory is now working to expose their manifold expressions in hadron observables and highlight the types of measurements that can be made in order to validate the paradigm. This contribution sketches the role played by EHM in shaping hadron electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, exciting nucleon resonances, and moulding hadron parton distributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that emergent hadron mass (EHM) is the overwhelmingly dominant source of visible mass, responsible for approximately 95% of the proton and rho-meson mass, and that this mechanism is now understood through three pillars: a nonzero gluon mass-scale, a process-independent effective charge, and dressed-quarks with constituent-like masses. The authors hedge that while EHM and confinement are "inextricably linked," theory is still working to expose their manifold expressions in observables and to validate the paradigm through specific measurements.

## Method

The authors combine continuum Schwinger function methods (Dyson-Schwinger equations) with lattice QCD results to define and calculate a unique, process-independent QCD effective charge, obtained from a modified gluon vacuum polarisation using the pinch technique and background field method. They then apply these methods to Faddeev equation studies of nucleon structure, computing electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, and use the Schlessinger point method for an objective statistical analysis of existing experimental data on the proton electric form factor.

## Key numbers

- The process-independent effective charge saturates at infrared momenta: $\hat\alpha(k^2\simeq 0) = 0.97(4)$
- The gluon mass scale at infrared momenta: $\mu_G = m_G(k^2 \simeq 0) = (0.5 \pm 0.2)$ GeV
- The proton electric form factor $G_E^p$ exhibits a zero at $Q^2 = 8.86_{-0.86}^{+1.92}$ GeV$^2$
- The neutron electric form factor exceeds the proton electric form factor on $Q^2 \geq 4.7$ GeV$^2$
- The nucleon D-term (pressure form factor at zero momentum transfer): $D(0) = -3.11(1)$
- The pion analogue value: $[-\theta_1(Q^2=0)] = -0.97$
- The gluon-to-total-quark contribution ratio at $\zeta=2$ GeV: ${\mathscr g}(Q^2)/{\mathscr q}(Q^2) = 0.71(4)$
- The mass radius relative to charge radius: $r_{\rm mass} = 0.81(5) r_{\rm ch}$, with $r_{\rm ch}=0.887(3)$ fm
- The mechanical radius: $r_{\rm mech} = 0.72(2) r_{\rm ch}$
- Higgs boson effects generate less than 2% of the proton mass, with current quark masses $\hat m_{u,d} \approx 0.006$ GeV and $\hat m_s \approx 0.16$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the foundational arguments of Cornwall (1981) on dynamical gluon mass generation and the continuum Schwinger function methods developed by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997-2000) for hadron observables. It provides parameter-free predictions for nucleon electromagnetic and gravitational form factors that can be tested at Jefferson Lab 12 GeV and other facilities, and establishes a framework for understanding how the three pillars of EHM manifest in observables. The results challenge many existing models and calculations of nucleon structure, particularly those that cannot reproduce the zero in the proton electric form factor.
