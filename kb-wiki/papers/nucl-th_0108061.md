# Can Recent Charge Fluctuations Be a Reliable signal for a QGP at RHIC?

> The recent papers of Jeon and Koch [1] and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller [2] argue that the event by event fluctuations of the ratio of the positively charged and negatively charged pions provide a distinct signal for a QGP at RHIC/LHC due to differences in those from the QGP phase and the Hadron Gas Phase.In this paper we point out that aside from the questionability of the many assumptions in the treatment used,even following their approach there are other effects not considered, e.g. color charge fluctuations, which could significantly or even completely wash out the proposed signal.Therefore lack of observation of these charge fluctuation signals cannot lead one to conclude that a QGP is not formed at RHIC. A general discussion of experimental requirements for observation of such signals(if they exist),annd how to interpret them is included.

## Key claim

Even if one follows the approach of Jeon and Koch (2001) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2001), color charge fluctuations not considered in those works could significantly or completely wash out the proposed QGP signal from event-by-event charge fluctuations of the pion ratio, meaning that a lack of observation of reduced charge fluctuations at RHIC cannot be taken as evidence that a QGP was not formed.

## Method

The authors follow the ideal-gas equilibrium treatment of Jeon and Koch (2001) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2001) for a hadron gas (HG) and a quark-gluon plasma (QGP), using the parameter D = <N_ch> <δR²> to quantify charge fluctuations. They then extend the calculation by including color charge and anti-color charge fluctuations in a sub-volume of the QGP, modeling how these fluctuations are frozen out and transferred into electric charge fluctuations during gluon fragmentation and hadronization, with the gluon fragmentation size as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- For a thermal light-quark meson and meson resonance system, D ≈ 3 from Jeon and Koch (2001) and possibly as low as 2 from Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2001).
- For a QGP, both Jeon and Koch (2001) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2001) estimate D ≈ 1, a factor of 2–3 smaller than the hadron gas value.
- In the authors' model including color fluctuations, the maximum transfer of color fluctuation into electric charge fluctuation occurs at a gluon fragmentation size of 3.5 fm, where the value of color fluctuations becomes equal to the value of charge fluctuations.
- After including color and anti-color fluctuations, D for a QGP increases to approximately 3.0, equal to the value for a light-quark resonance system.
- For Au on Au collisions at √s_NN = 130 GeV at RHIC, produced pions at mid-rapidity and low p_T show a source size radius up to about 6 fm; for p_T above 600 MeV/c the source size becomes 2 fm.

## Context

This paper directly critiques the charge fluctuation signal proposed by Jeon and Koch (2001) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2001) as a distinct signature of QGP formation at RHIC and LHC. The authors argue that color charge fluctuations, neglected in those prior works, can restore D to values indistinguishable from a hadron gas, and that kinematic mixing from longitudinal expansion (as observed by the STAR collaboration's HBT measurements) further complicates experimental extraction. The work enables a more cautious interpretation of any future charge fluctuation measurements and calls for additional supporting evidence before claiming QGP discovery.
