# The equation of state and symmetry energy of low density nuclear matter

> The symmetry energy of nuclear matter is a fundamental ingredient in the investigation of exotic nuclei, heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical phenomena. A recently developed quantum statistical (QS) approach that takes the formation of clusters into account predicts low density symmetry energies far above the usually quoted mean field limits. A consistent description of the symmetry energy has been developed that joins the correct low-density limit with values calculated from quasi-particle approaches valid near the saturation density. The results are confronted with experimental values for free symmetry energies and internal symmetry energies, determined at sub-saturation densities and temperatures below 10 MeV using data from heavy-ion collisions. There is very good agreement between the experimental symmetry energy values and those calculated in the QS approach

## Key claim

The paper argues that the symmetry energy of low-density nuclear matter is significantly higher than predicted by mean-field approaches, and that a quantum statistical (QS) approach incorporating cluster formation provides a consistent description that joins the correct low-density limit with quasi-particle approaches valid near saturation density, with very good agreement to experimental values.

## Method

The authors employ a quantum statistical (QS) approach based on a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck expansion that accounts for cluster correlations in the medium, using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with density-dependent couplings to describe single-nucleon quasiparticle properties. They interpolate between the exact low-density limit (where nuclear statistical equilibrium applies) and the RMF approach near saturation density, calculating the symmetry energy from the free energy per nucleon as a function of asymmetry. Experimental symmetry energy values are extracted from heavy-ion collision data at sub-saturation densities and temperatures below 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is given as \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) with a symmetry energy at saturation of \(E_{\rm sym}(n_0,0) = 28-34\) MeV from the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula
- A recent analysis gives the volume symmetry coefficient \(a_{V}^{(\rm sym)} = 32.1 \pm 0.3\) MeV
- The effective mass approximation gives \(m^{\ast} \approx m(1-0.17~n/n_0)\) for \(n<0.2\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The binding energy per nucleon at saturation is \(B/A \approx -16\) MeV
- The NSE model is valid below \(5 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), while clusters dissolve above approximately 1/10 of saturation density (the Mott point)

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach of Ropke (1983) and Typel (2009) to provide a unified description of the symmetry energy from low to saturation densities, contradicting mean-field approaches such as Skyrme Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean-field models that predict the symmetry energy going linearly to zero at low density (Li et al., 2008). The results enable improved equation of state inputs for supernova simulations and neutron star modeling, where the cluster formation in the neutrino-sphere region is of particular interest, as discussed by Sumiyoshi (2008) and O'Connor (2013).
