## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies using older NN potentials (Machleidt 1991, Machleidt 1993) and updates them with the CD-Bonn potential. It directly addresses the tension between values of g¤Ç extracted from different observables, a problem previously noted in analyses of the deuteron by Ericson (1983) and in phase shift analyses by the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993). The paper contradicts the possibility of substantial charge-splitting of g¤Ç by showing that such splitting would destroy the established explanation of charge-dependence in the ┬╣SÔéÇ scattering length, which is based on pion mass splitting and has been developed by Ericson and Miller (1983) and Li and Machleidt (1998). The work calls for future reinvestigation of meson-exchange current contributions to the deuteron quadrupole moment and for resolution of systematic errors in the data.
