# How sensitive are various NN observables to changes in the $πNN$ coupling constant?

> The deuteron, NN analyzing powers A_y, and the singlet scattering length show great sensitivity to the $πNN$ coupling constant $g_π$. While the pp A_y data favor $g^2_π/4π\leq 13.6$, the np A_y data and the deuteron quadrupole moment imply $g^2_π/4π\geq 14.0$. The two diverging values could be reconciled by the assumption of (substantial) charge-splitting of $g_π$. However, the established theoretical explanation of the charge-dependence of the $^1S_0$ scattering length (based upon pion mass splitting) is very sensitive to a difference between $g_{π^0}$ and $g_{π^\pm}$ and rules out any substantial charge-splitting of $g_π$. Thus, there are real and large discrepancies between the values for $g_π$ extracted from different NN observables. Future work that could resolve the problems is suggested.

# How sensitive are various NN observables to changes in the πNN coupling constant?

## Key claim

While the abstract states that the deuteron, NN analyzing powers, and singlet scattering length show great sensitivity to the πNN coupling constant, the paper actually demonstrates that different NN observables yield conflicting values for gπ, and that the apparent resolution via charge-splitting of gπ is ruled out by the established explanation of charge-dependence in the singlet scattering length. The author concludes that any value between g²π/4π = 13.2 and 14.4 is possible depending on which observable is chosen, and that large systematic errors in data or theoretical methods must exist.

## Method

The author uses the CD-Bonn potential, a high-precision NN potential fitting NN data below 350 MeV with χ²/datum near unity, to compute deuteron properties (quadrupole moment Q, asymptotic D/S ratio η, asymptotic S-state AS) and NN phase shifts for varying values of g²π/4π. The χ²/datum is then evaluated for fits to world pp and np analyzing power (Ay) data across multiple energy ranges, and the effect of charge-splitting of gπ on the charge-independence breaking (CIB) of the ¹S₀ scattering length is computed using the Bonn meson-exchange model.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron quadrupole moment Q requires g²π/4π ≥ 14.0 to be correctly reproduced; Model A with g²π/4π = 13.6 gives Q = 0.270 fm² versus the empirical value 0.276(2) fm².
- The pp ³P₀ phase shifts at low energy require g²π/4π ≤ 13.6; at 10 MeV, Model A (g²π/4π = 13.6) gives 3.726 degrees versus the Nijmegen analysis value 3.729(17) degrees.
- For pp Ay data at 9.85 MeV, χ²/datum is 0.98 for g²π/4π = 13.2, 2.02 for 13.6, and 9.05 for 14.4.
- For pp Ay data in the 0–17 MeV range (45 data), χ²/datum is 0.84 for g²π/4π = 13.2, 1.43 for 13.6, 2.71 for 14.0, and 4.66 for 14.4.
- For np Ay data at 12 MeV (9 data), χ²/datum is 2.81 for g²π/4π = 13.6 (no splitting), 1.79 for 14.4 (no splitting), and 1.00 for charge-splitting Model E (g²π⁰/4π = 13.6, g²π±/4π = 14.4).
- The empirical charge-independence breaking in the singlet scattering length is ΔaCIB = 5.7 ± 0.5 fm; without charge-splitting of gπ, the Bonn model predicts 4.64 fm, but with charge-splitting (g²π⁰/4π = 13.6, g²π±/4π = 14.4) the prediction reverses to –4.49 fm.
- The charged and neutral pion masses are mπ± = 139.570 MeV and mπ⁰ = 134.976 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies using older NN potentials (Machleidt 1991, Machleidt 1993) and updates them with the CD-Bonn potential. It directly addresses the tension between values of gπ extracted from different observables, a problem previously noted in analyses of the deuteron by Ericson (1983) and in phase shift analyses by the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993). The paper contradicts the possibility of substantial charge-splitting of gπ by showing that such splitting would destroy the established explanation of charge-dependence in the ¹S₀ scattering length, which is based on pion mass splitting and has been developed by Ericson and Miller (1983) and Li and Machleidt (1998). The work calls for future reinvestigation of meson-exchange current contributions to the deuteron quadrupole moment and for resolution of systematic errors in the data.
