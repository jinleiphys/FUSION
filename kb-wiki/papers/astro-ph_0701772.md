# The quark core of protoneutron stars in the phase diagram of quark matter

> We study the effect of neutrino trapping in new-born quark stars within a three-flavor Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with self-consistently calculated quark masses. The phase diagrams and equations of state for charge neutral quark matter in beta-equilibrium are presented, with and without trapped neutrinos. The compact star sequences for different neutrino untrapping scenarios are investigated and the energy release due to neutrino untrapping is found to be of the order of 10^53 erg. We find that hot quark stars characterized, e.g., by an entropy per baryon of 1-2 and a lepton fraction of 0.4, as models for the cores of newborn protoneutron stars, are in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) state. High temperatures and/or neutrino chemical potentials disfavor configurations with a color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. Stable quark star solutions with CFL cores exist only at low temperatures and neutrino chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The authors find that hot quark stars with an entropy per baryon of 1-2 and a lepton fraction of 0.4, which serve as models for the cores of newborn protoneutron stars, are in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) state; stable quark star solutions with color-flavor-locked (CFL) cores exist only at low temperatures and low neutrino chemical potentials, and high temperatures or neutrino chemical potentials disfavor the CFL phase.

## Method

The authors used a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with self-consistently calculated quark masses and diquark pairing gaps at the mean-field level, enforcing charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium. They solved the gap equations and neutrality conditions for two values of the relative diquark coupling strength (eta = 0.75 and eta = 1.0), both with and without trapped neutrinos (mu_nu = 200 MeV and mu_nu = 0), and then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain compact star sequences.

## Key numbers

- The energy release due to neutrino untrapping is found to be of the order of 10^53 erg.
- The model uses current quark masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 112.0 MeV, with a three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV and coupling G_S Lambda^2 = 2.319.
- For the fixed lepton fraction scenario, the initial state is characterized by Y_L_e = 0.4 and entropy per baryon s/n_B = 1-2.
- The neutrino chemical potential in the trapped scenario is mu_nu = 200 MeV, and the hot initial temperature considered is T = 40 MeV.
- The critical temperatures for the superconducting phases are approximately 60-70 MeV for intermediate coupling (eta = 0.75) and 100-110 MeV for strong coupling (eta = 1.0).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutrino trapping in color superconducting quark matter by Steiner (2002) and Ruster (2005) for homogeneous phases, and by Laporta (2005) for inhomogeneous LOFF phases, by focusing on the stability of quark star solutions and the energy release during cooling and untrapping transitions. The results enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star cores in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for understanding the role of color superconducting phases in neutrino transport and the energy budget of gamma-ray bursts.
