# Configuration mixing effects in neutron-rich carbon isotopes

> Shell model calculations are done to study the structure of neutron-rich carbon isotopes. For both even-A and odd-A neutron-rich carbon isotopes, the energy levels are strongly affected by the configuration mixing of valence neutrons. The calculated energy levels in the nucleus $^{17}$C are significantly improved compared with experimental values when the model space of the three valence neutrons is enlarged from pure $ν(0d_{5/2})^{3}$ configuration to full $sd$ space. We also investigate the configuration mixing effect on the $B(E2)$ values in even-even nuclei $^{16-20}$C.

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations show that the energy levels of neutron-rich carbon isotopes are strongly affected by configuration mixing of valence neutrons, particularly between the ν0d5/2 and ν1s1/2 orbits, and that the calculated energy levels in ¹⁷C are significantly improved when the model space is enlarged from pure ν(0d5/2)³ to the full sd space.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed in the psd model space (including 0p3/2, 0p1/2, 0d5/2, 1s1/2, and 0d3/2 orbits) using the WBP, WBT, and MK effective Hamiltonians. The authors restricted excitations from the p to sd shell to a maximum of zero or two nucleons (0ℏω and 2ℏω, respectively), and removed spurious center-of-mass states by adding βHc.m. with β = 100 MeV to the Hamiltonian. Calculations were carried out with a parallel shell-model code.

## Key numbers
- The N=14 shell gap in oxygen isotopes disappears in carbon isotopes, as indicated by the rapid decrease of E(2⁺₁) in N=14 isotones from oxygen to carbon.
- In neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, the ground states contain approximately 80% pure ν0d5/2 configuration, while neutron-rich carbon isotopes show strong configuration mixing between ν0d5/2 and ν1s1/2 orbits.
- The effective proton contribution e_p^eff A_p in ²⁰C is 1.5 times larger than that in ¹⁸C, leading to a large B(E2) value in ²⁰C.
- The B(E2) values for even-even ¹⁶–²⁰C were calculated and compared with experimental values from Petri (2011), Elekes (2009), Wiedeking (2008), and Ong (2008).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies on shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the disappearance of the N=14 magic number in carbon isotopes as previously discussed by Yuan (2012) and Stanoiu (2008). The paper builds on the finding that both proton-neutron tensor interactions and neutron-neutron interactions reduce the N=14 gap from oxygen to carbon, and it quantifies how configuration mixing between ν0d5/2 and ν1s1/2 orbits affects both energy levels and B(E2) transition strengths. The results enable improved shell-model descriptions of neutron-rich carbon isotopes and clarify the role of proton configuration mixing in producing the large B(E2) value observed in ²⁰C.
