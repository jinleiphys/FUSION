# Relativistic chiral representation of the $πN$ scattering amplitude I: The Goldberger-Treiman relation

> In this work we study the $πN$ scattering process within the Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory framework in the covariant scheme of Extended-On-Mass-Shell (EOMS). We compare the description obtained in this scheme with the previously obtained using the Infrared Regularization scheme and show that EOMS accomplishes the best convergence, being able to extract from partial wave analyses reliable values of important quantities as the Goldberger-Treiman deviation. In regard to the latter, we solve the long-standing problem concerning to the extraction of the Goldberger-Treiman deviation with covariant ChPT that jeopardized the applicability of ChPT to the $πN$ system. We also show the potential of the unitarization techniques applied to the perturbative calculation in the EOMS scheme, that allow us to increase the range of validity of our description up to $\approx 200$ MeV in $\sqrt{s}$.

## Key claim

The Extended-On-Mass-Shell (EOMS) scheme within covariant Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (BChPT) solves the long-standing problem of extracting a physically reasonable Goldberger-Treiman (GT) deviation from pion-nucleon scattering data, yielding values compatible with partial wave analyses (PWAs) rather than the huge, scale-dependent values (20-30%) obtained in the Infrared Regularization (IR) scheme.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral expansion of the πN scattering amplitude up to 𝒪(p³) using the covariant EOMS renormalization scheme, which removes power-counting-breaking terms by absorbing them into the low-energy constants (LECs). They fitted the resulting amplitude to the partial wave analyses of the Karlsruhe group (KA85) and the George Washington University group (WI08) up to √s_max = 1.13 GeV, extracting the LECs and the GT deviation Δ_GT. They then applied unitarization techniques using an interaction kernel matched to the perturbative result and a unitary pion-nucleon loop function g(s), including a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole for the P33 partial wave to account for the Δ(1232) resonance.

## Key numbers

- The EOMS fit to KA85 yields Δ_GT = 9 ± 4%; the fit to WI08 yields Δ_GT = 2 ± 4%, both compatible with the respective PWAs.
- The IR scheme gives Δ_GT in the range 20-30%, with strong scale dependence.
- The loop contribution to Δ_GT in EOMS is approximately 0.2%, of the size expected from explicit chiral symmetry breaking.
- The unitarized EOMS amplitude describes phase shifts up to √s ≈ 1.35 GeV, an increase of about 200 MeV in √s compared to the perturbative calculation and about 100 MeV higher than the IR unitarized description (limited to √s ≈ 1.25 GeV).
- The fitted LECs in EOMS (in GeV⁻¹ for c_i and GeV⁻² for d_i) include: c₁ = -1.26 ± 0.07 (KA85) and -1.50 ± 0.06 (WI08); c₃ = -6.74 ± 0.08 (KA85) and -6.63 ± 0.08 (WI08); d₁₈ = -2.96 ± 1.44 (KA85) and -0.50 ± 1.43 (WI08).
- The χ² per degree of freedom for the EOMS fits is 0.35 (KA85) and 0.22 (WI08), compared to ≲ 1 for the IR fits.

## Context

This work extends the covariant BChPT approach of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and the Infrared Regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) by applying the EOMS renormalization method of Fuchs, Gegelia, and Scherer (2003) to πN scattering. It resolves the problem of the unphysically large and scale-dependent GT deviation that had plagued the IR scheme, as previously noted by T. Becher and H. Leutwyler (1999) and by the authors themselves in earlier IR work. The improved analytical properties of the EOMS amplitude enable reliable extraction of the GT deviation and allow unitarization techniques to extend the description up to about 1.35 GeV, enabling future studies of the πN sigma term and the role of the Δ(1232) resonance.
