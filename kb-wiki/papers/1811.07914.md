# The topological objects near the chiral crossover transition in QCD

> We study the underlying topology of gauge fields in 2+1 flavor QCD with domain wall fermions on lattices of size $32^3\times 8$, at and immediately above the chiral crossover transition. Using valence overlap fermions with exact index theorem, we focus on its zero modes for different choices of periodicity phases along the temporal direction. Our studies show that the zero modes are due to fractionally charged topological objects, the instanton-dyons. We further provide qualitative study of the interactions between those and compare with the available semi-classical results, finding remarkably accurate agreement in all cases.

# The topological objects near the chiral crossover transition in QCD

## Key claim
The zero modes of the QCD Dirac operator near the chiral crossover temperature are due to fractionally charged instanton-dyons, not calorons, and the semi-classical theory of dyons reproduces the observed spacetime profiles with deviations not exceeding 10-20%, despite the deeply non-perturbative regime where typical action per dyon is only S ~ 3-4 ħ.

## Method
The authors used 2+1 flavor QCD configurations generated by the HotQCD collaboration with Möbius domain wall fermions and Iwasaki gauge action on 32³×8 lattices at T/Tc = 1.0 and 1.08. They placed valence overlap fermions (which satisfy an exact index theorem) on these configurations and computed the first 5 eigenvalues and eigenvectors using the Kalkreuter-Simma Ritz algorithm. By varying the temporal periodicity phase φ of the valence fermions to three values (π, π/3, -π/3), they tracked how the zero-mode positions shifted, which distinguishes dyons from calorons. The wavefunction density ρ(x) and chiral density ρ₅(x) profiles were compared to analytic dyon solutions from Chernodub et al. (1999).

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 32³ × 8, with spatial extent ~4 fm and lattice spacing ~0.1 fm
- Pseudo-critical temperature: Tc = 155(9) MeV
- Temperatures studied: T/Tc ~ 1.0 and 1.08
- Residual chiral symmetry breaking of domain wall fermions: ~2 × 10⁻³
- Numerical precision of overlap operator implementation: 10⁻⁹ or lower
- Error on computed eigenvalues: < 0.1%
- 13-14 statistically independent gauge configurations per temperature, all with topological charge |Q_top| = 1
- Typical action per dyon: S ~ 3-4 ħ
- Deviations between lattice data and analytic dyon profiles: ~10-20%

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies by Gattringer (2002) and by Mueller-Preussker, Ilgenfritz and collaborators (2015, 2016) who identified dyon-like structures in pure gauge theories using topological charge clusters from low-lying Dirac eigenmodes. Unlike those approaches, the present method uses exact fermion zero modes with an index theorem, requires no gauge fixing, and works directly on physical QCD configurations with dynamical quarks. The results provide the first evidence that semi-classical dyon theory can describe the QCD vacuum above Tc, enabling future quantitative measurements of dyon densities and correlations, and potentially a first-principles understanding of confinement through the back-reaction of dyons on the Polyakov loop potential.
