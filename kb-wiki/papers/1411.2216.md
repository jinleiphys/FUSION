# Phenomenological implications of the nucleon's meson cloud

> The long-distance structure of the interacting nucleon receives important contributions from its couplings to light hadronic degrees of freedom -- a light meson cloud -- while an analogous nonperturbative mechanism is expected to generate an intrinsic charm (IC) component to the proton wavefunction. We investigate both possibilities, keeping for the former a special eye to improving the theoretical understanding of the pion-nucleon vertex in light of proposed measurements. Regarding the latter possibility of IC, we highlight recent results obtained by a global QCD analysis of the light-front model proposed in Hobbs et al. (Phys Rev D 89:074008, 2014).

# Phenomenological implications of the nucleon's meson cloud

## Key claim
The nucleon's long-distance structure receives important contributions from a light meson cloud, and an analogous nonperturbative mechanism may generate an intrinsic charm (IC) component in the proton wavefunction; however, a global QCD analysis finds that world data sharply limit the size of IC, with a 5-sigma limit of ⟨x⟩_IC ≤ 0.1% when EMC data are excluded, and a preferred value of ⟨x⟩_IC = 0.13 ± 0.04% when EMC data are included.

## Method
The author computes meson-baryon splitting functions on the light front using chiral perturbation theory Lagrangians, then evaluates tagged structure functions for the Sullivan process in laboratory kinematics with finite integration ranges in baryon momentum and angle. For intrinsic charm, the meson-baryon Fock state formalism is extended to SU(4) hadrons, and the resulting charm distributions are parametrized as three-parameter functions and incorporated into a global QCD fit using the JR14 framework, which includes finite-Q² corrections from higher twist and target/quark mass effects.

## Key numbers
- The pion-nucleon splitting function f_πN(z) includes isospin factors c_I=1 for π⁰ (p→pπ⁰ or n→nπ⁰) and c_I=2 for π± (p→nπ⁺ or n→pπ⁻)
- Tagged structure function calculations assume the experimental range Δ|k| = [250, 400 MeV] for proposed JLab Hall A measurements
- At small momenta, single pion exchange dominates other SU(2) mechanisms by approximately an order of magnitude
- Sensitivity to tagged structure functions at the level of ∼4×10⁻⁵ is projected for the upgraded Hall A projection chamber
- The charm mass is taken as m_c = 1.3 GeV at threshold, with partonic threshold W² > (2m_c)²
- The global fit excluding EMC data yields a 5σ limit of ⟨x⟩_IC ≤ 0.1%
- The global fit including EMC data prefers ⟨x⟩_IC = 0.13 ± 0.04%
- The EMC data have χ²/n_d.o.f. ∼ 4.3 in the global fit, indicating tension with other data sets

## Context
This work extends the meson-cloud formalism previously applied to light quarks (as established by measurements of light quark sea asymmetries and Gottfried Sum Rule violation, cited as Tony) to investigate both the pion-nucleon vertex and intrinsic charm contributions. The intrinsic charm analysis builds on the light-front model of Hobbs et al. (2014) and the BHPS mechanism, and the global fit uses the JR14 framework developed by Jimenez-Delgado and collaborators, which allows less restrictive kinematic cuts than earlier efforts such as CT. The results enable future experimental programs at JLab Hall A for forward spectator tagging to constrain the pion-nucleon vertex, and they motivate new measurements of charm production at large x to resolve tension between EMC and HERA data.
