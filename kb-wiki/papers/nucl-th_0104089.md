# Hydrodynamic flow at RHIC

> We review the apparently hydrodynamic behaviour of low transverse momentum particles (p_T < 1.5 GeV/c) produced in central and semicentral (b < 7 fm) heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We investigate the impact parameter dependence of various observables, elaborating on radial and elliptic flow and particle multiplicities. We also discuss possible ambiguities in the initialization of the hydrodynamic system and present observables that should allow for their resolution.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the apparently hydrodynamic behavior of low-transverse-momentum particles (p_T ≤ 1.5 GeV/c) in central and semicentral (b ≤ 7 fm) RHIC collisions is consistent with rapid thermalization followed by hydrodynamic expansion, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the hydrodynamic approach is only valid for a limited timespan of the expansion and must eventually be supplemented by a microscopic kinetic treatment to check its validity.

## Method
The author performed relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with boost-invariance in the longitudinal direction, using an equation of state with a sharp first-order phase transition between a hadronic resonance gas (e < 0.45 GeV/fm³) and an ideal ultrarelativistic quark-gluon plasma (e > 1.6 GeV/fm³). Five different initialization parametrizations were studied: energy or entropy density proportional to wounded nucleon density (eWN, sWN) or binary collision density (eBC, sBC), plus a saturation model. The initial energy density at the origin for b=0 collisions was tuned to reproduce the final particle multiplicity observed in central RHIC collisions, resulting in a maximum energy density e₀ = 23 GeV/fm³ at equilibration time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c.

## Key numbers
- Maximum energy density e₀ = 23 GeV/fm³ at equilibration time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for RHIC simulations (compared to 9 GeV/fm³ at 0.8 fm/c for SPS simulations)
- Mean energy density e_mean = 4.8 GeV/fm³ at τ = 1.0 fm/c (PHENIX collaboration estimates approximately 5.0 GeV/fm³)
- Average radial flow velocity at freeze-out increases from 0.45 c at maximum SPS energies to 0.55 c at √s_NN = 130 GeV
- Critical energy density for phase transition: hadronic phase below e < 0.45 GeV/fm³, QGP phase above e > 1.6 GeV/fm³
- Hydrodynamic results agree with experimental elliptic flow data up to impact parameters of about 7 fm and p_T of about 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic simulations at SPS energies (KSH99) to RHIC energies, building on the observation that microscopic models with standard scattering cross sections fail to describe flow observables due to insufficient rescattering (BS00, MG01). The paper shows that while minimum-bias elliptic flow results are largely independent of initialization, the centrality dependence of particle production and transverse energy per particle can distinguish between wounded-nucleon and binary-collision scaling, enabling future studies to determine the relative contributions of soft and hard scattering processes in initial particle production.
