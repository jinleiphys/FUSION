# Elastic proton scattering at intermediate energies as a probe of the $^{6,8}$He nuclear matter densities

> The Glauber model analysis of the elastic $^{6,8}$He+$p$ scattering data at energies around 700 MeV/nucleon, measured in two separate experiments at GSI-Darmstadt, has been done using several phenomenological parametrizations of the nuclear matter density. By taking into account the new data points measured at the high momentum transfer, the nuclear matter radii of $^{6,8}$He were accurately determined from the Glauber model analysis of the data, with the spin-orbital interaction explicitly taken into account. The well-known geometry for the core and dineutron halo has been used with the new parametrizations of the $^{6}$He density to extract the detailed information on the structure of $^{6}$He in terms of the core and dineutron halo radii. An enhanced sensitivity of the data measured at the high momentum transfer to the core part of the $^{6,8}$He densities has been found.

# Elastic proton scattering at intermediate energies as a probe of the 6,8He nuclear matter densities

## Key claim
The Glauber model analysis of combined elastic 6,8He+p scattering data at low and high momentum transfer, with spin-orbit interaction explicitly included, yields nuclear matter radii and core/halo structure parameters that are slightly larger than those obtained from earlier analyses using only low-momentum-transfer data. The new high-momentum-transfer data show enhanced sensitivity to the core part of the nuclear density distributions, enabling more precise determination of core radii.

## Method
The authors performed a Glauber multiple-scattering model (GMSM) analysis of elastic 6He+p scattering at 717 MeV/nucleon and 8He+p scattering at 674 MeV/nucleon, combining data from two GSI experiments that covered low and high momentum transfer regions. Five phenomenological parametrizations of the nuclear matter density were used (symmetrized Fermi, Gaussian-Halo, Woods-Saxon, Gaussian-Gaussian, and Gaussian-Oscillator), with the core and halo nucleons treated explicitly in the latter two models. A chi-squared fitting procedure was employed to determine optimal density parameters, with the spin-orbit interaction included in the proton-nucleon scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers
- 6He matter radius: Rm = 2.44 ± 0.07 fm (averaged over five density parametrizations)
- 8He matter radius: Rm = 2.50 ± 0.08 fm (averaged over five density parametrizations)
- 6He core radius: Rc = 1.93 ± 0.06 fm (from GG and GO density models)
- 8He core radius: Rc = 1.75 ± 0.08 fm (from GG and GO density models)
- 6He halo radius: Rh = 3.28 ± 0.13 fm (from GG and GO density models)
- 8He halo radius: Rh = 3.06 ± 0.14 fm (from GG and GO density models)
- 6He neutron skin (Rn - Rp): 0.76 ± 0.10 fm
- 8He neutron skin (Rn - Rp): 0.94 ± 0.13 fm
- 6He neutron radius: Rn = 2.69 ± 0.09 fm
- 8He neutron radius: Rn = 2.75 ± 0.10 fm
- 6He two-neutron halo radius (R2n): 2.52 ± 0.13 fm
- 6He core-2n distance (Rc-2n): 3.79 ± 0.14 fm
- 6He neutron-neutron distance (Rn-n): 4.19 ± 0.49 fm

## Context
This work extends the earlier GMSM analysis by Alkhazov et al. (2002) that used only low-momentum-transfer data from Neumaier et al. (2002), by incorporating new high-momentum-transfer data from Kiselev et al. (2011) and including the spin-orbit interaction. The results are compared with empirical matter radii compiled by Tanihata et al. (2013) and with the cluster-orbital shell-model approximation (COSMA) densities from Korsheninnikov et al. (1997). The core radii obtained are consistent with proton radii from high-precision laser spectroscopy by Mueller et al. (2007), and the analysis enables improved parametrization of the COSMA density parameters and suggests that few-body calculations by Alkhazov et al. (1998) should be revisited using experimental alpha-core radii.
