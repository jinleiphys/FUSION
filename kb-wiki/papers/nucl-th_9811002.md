# On the equivalence of the Impulse Approximation and the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory for structure functions

> We derive for a non-relativistic system an approximation for Final State Interactions in a form, resembling a DWIA which corrects the structure function computed in the PWIA. We then compare the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith and the IA series for structure functions and prove that to order ${\cal O}(1/q^2)$ the above DWIA representation is contained in the GRS series to the same order. There is an additional term in the GRS series that is missing in the DWIA due to the eikonal approximations in the latter. This strongly suggests that the two approaches, when treated exactly, produce identical structure function to arbitrary order in $1/q$.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) series and the Impulse Approximation (IA) series for non-relativistic structure functions produce identical results to arbitrary order in 1/q when treated exactly, but the approximate Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) representation of the IA series misses an additional term present in the GRS series at order O(1/q^2) due to the eikonal approximations inherent in the DWIA.

## Method

The authors derive a DWIA representation for Final State Interactions (FSI) correcting the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) structure function for a non-relativistic system of point particles. They then expand both the GRS series and the DWIA representation to order O(1/q^2) and compare the coefficient functions term by term, separating the interaction into two components (V^(1) and V^(2)) and tracking how the GRS scaling variable y relates to the IA scaling variable y_0 through an average separation energy.

## Key numbers

- The equivalence proof is carried out to order O(1/q^2), corresponding to terms up to 1/v_q^2 where v_q = q/M is the recoil velocity
- The GRS series expansion is given as phi(q,y) = sum_{n>=0} (1/v_q)^n F_n(y), with the asymptotic limit F_0(y) = (1/4pi^2) integral_{|y|}^{infinity} dp p n(p)
- The relation between scaling variables is y = y_0 + (1/v_q)(y_0^2/(2M) + <Delta>), where <Delta> is the average separation energy
- The DWIA response is expressed as a convolution phi^{TI,DWIA}(q,y_0) = (1/A) integral dy_0' F_0(y_0 - y_0') R_q(y_0')

## Context

This work extends the formal comparison between the GRS theory (Gersch, Rodriguez, and Smith, 1973) and the perturbative IA series (Ciofi degli Atti, 1991; Benhar, 1991) for nuclear structure functions, resolving a long-standing question about their equivalence. The proof demonstrates that the GRS series naturally contains the DWIA terms plus an additional term F_2^(r) not reproduced in the DWIA, and establishes that the proper convolution variable for the GRS theory is the GRS-West scaling variable y, not the energy loss omega as used in prior work by Benhar and coworkers (1991, 1995). This enables future exact treatments of either approach to be compared on equal footing, and provides a foundation for relativistic extensions where scattering amplitudes replace inter-nucleon potentials.
