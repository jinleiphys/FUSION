# Inclusive hadroproduction of $χ_{c1}(3872)$, $X_b$ and pentaquarks

> We use the Born--Oppenheimer effective field theory factorization to compute the inclusive production cross sections of the $χ_{c1}(3872)$ and its partner in the bottomonium sector. In the same framework, we compute the production cross sections of the pentaquark states $P_{c\bar{c}}(4312)^+$, $P_{c\bar{c}}(4457)^+$, $P_{c\bar{c}}(4380)^+$ and $P_{c\bar{c}}(4440)^+$ within two possible scenarios for the Born--Oppenheimer potentials. Also for pentaquarks, we extend the results to the bottomonium sector. All our results are genuine predictions that do not involve fits to prompt hadroproduction data.

## Key claim
The authors use the Born-Oppenheimer effective field theory (BOEFT) factorization to compute inclusive production cross sections of the χc1(3872), Xb, and pentaquark states without fitting to prompt hadroproduction data, but the predictions depend on two possible scenarios for the Born-Oppenheimer potentials and on universal matrix elements fitted to B hadron decay data.

## Method
The authors start from the NRQCD factorization formula for inclusive hadroproduction, expressing the long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) in terms of wave functions at the origin and universal correlators using pNRQCD and its extension BOEFT. For the χc1(3872) and Xb, they solve coupled Schrödinger equations for the tetraquark wave functions in the adiabatic basis, then transform to a diabatic basis to separate S-wave and D-wave components. For pentaquarks, they apply the same BOEFT framework with two scenarios for the Born-Oppenheimer potentials, extending the results from the charmonium to the bottomonium sector.

## Key numbers
- The universal correlator ℰ(Λ) extracted at Λ = 1.5 GeV and evolved to m_c = 1.4 GeV at leading logarithmic accuracy gives ℰ(Λ = 1.4 GeV) = 2.5 ± 1.7.
- The charm mass used in the coupled Schrödinger equations is about 1.4 GeV (Cornell mass), corresponding to a mass of about 1.97 GeV in the parameterization of the potentials from Brambilla (2024) and Bulava (2024).
- The octet LDME for χc1(3872) production is expressed as (3/(2π)) [|φ_Σg+′(0)|² ℳ_Σg+′ + |φ_Πg(0)|² ℳ_Πg], where the wave functions at the origin are obtained from solving the coupled Schrödinger equations.

## Context
This work extends the pNRQCD factorization framework for conventional quarkonium production (Brambilla 2020, 2021, 2022) to exotic tetraquark and pentaquark states using the Born-Oppenheimer effective field theory developed by Berwein (2015, 2024), Oncala (2017), Brambilla (2017), Soto (2020), and Braaten (2024). The predictions for χc1(3872) and Xb production rely on wave functions from solving coupled Schrödinger equations (Berwein 2024, Brambilla 2024) and universal matrix elements that can be fitted to B hadron decay data. For pentaquarks, the results are computed within two scenarios for the BO potentials and extended to the yet-to-be-discovered bottomonium-sector partners, enabling future comparisons with LHC data.
