# Resonance State Wave Functions of $^{15}$Be using Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics

> The theoretical procedure of supersymmetric quantum mechanics is adopted to generate the resonance state wave functions of the unbound nucleus $^{15}$Be. In this framework, we used a density dependent M3Y microscopic potential and arrived at the energy and width of the 1.8 MeV (5/2$^+$) resonance state. We did not find any other nearby resonances for $^{15}$Be. It becomes apparent that the present framework is a powerful tool to theoretically complement the increasingly important accelerator based experiments with unbound nuclei.

## Key claim
Using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SQM) with a density-dependent M3Y microscopic potential, the authors claim to have generated resonance state wave functions for the unbound nucleus $^{15}$Be and to have confirmed only a single $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ resonance at 1.8 MeV, contradicting shell-model predictions of additional low-lying states. The hedge is that the experimental data do not resolve which of the $\frac{3}{2}^{+}$ and $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ states is the ground state, and the authors' calculation finds no evidence for the $\frac{3}{2}^{+}$ state as a resonance.

## Method
The authors treat $^{15}$Be as a two-body system ($^{14}$Be core plus a valence neutron) and construct a microscopic potential via single folding with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction, using a Gaussian-Halo density distribution for $^{14}$Be with an rms radius of 3.1 fm. They then apply the SQM formalism to generate a family of isospectral potentials that support a bound state in the continuum (BIC) at a selected positive energy; the probability $C(E)$ of the system being trapped in the well-barrier combination of the isospectral potential shows a sharp peak at the resonance energy. The resonance width $\Gamma$ is calculated semiclassically from the mean life using the WKB transmission probability through the barrier of the isospectral potential.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy $E_R = 1.8$ MeV for the $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ state of $^{15}$Be.
- Calculated resonance width $\Gamma = 470$ keV for the $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ state.
- Experimental width from Snyder et al. (2013) is $\Gamma_{exp} = 575 (200)$ keV.
- The $^{14}$Be core density parameterization uses an rms radius of 3.1 fm (Gaussian-Halo model from Ilyushkin et al. 2009).
- The $\lambda$ parameter values used in the isospectral potential construction are $5 \times 10^{-6}$, $1 \times 10^{-6}$, $7 \times 10^{-7}$, and $3 \times 10^{-7}$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SQM application to the weakly bound nucleus $^{11}$Be (Dutta et al. 2014) and addresses the unresolved theoretical ambiguities in $^{15}$Be level structure. Prior shell-model calculations by Warburton et al. (1992) and Brown et al. (2007) predicted a $\frac{3}{2}^{+}$ ground state with a $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ excited state near 300 keV, while Poppelier et al. (1985) predicted the opposite ordering; the present calculation supports only the $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ resonance observed experimentally by Snyder et al. (2013) at 1.8 MeV. The method provides resonance wave functions that can be used for further calculations such as differential cross sections, and it enables theoretical complement to accelerator experiments with unbound nuclei.
