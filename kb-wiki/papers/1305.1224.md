# Stimulation of static deconfined medium by multiple hard partons

> We investigate the response of non-expanding deconfined hot matter to energy and momentum deposition from a pair of partons moving with high energies. Several situations are examined with partons moving so that the generated wakes in the medium interact. The resulting energy and flow profiles are studied. Such cases are relevant for nuclear collisions at the LHC where several hard partons are produced in a single collision and their contribution to collective expansion of the fireball may be important.

## Key claim
The paper confirms that when two wakes generated by hard partons in a static deconfined medium approach each other, they merge and continue to stream united according to energy and momentum conservation, validating a previous toy model assumption that this effect contributes to elliptic flow. The authors stress this is a toy model simulation that remains to be checked against the effect of viscosity.

## Method
The authors solved (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations with a source term representing energy and momentum deposition from hard partons, using the SHASTA algorithm with multidimensional flux-correction. The medium was static with zero net baryon density and no collective velocity field, using the Laine-Schroder equation of state. Energy loss was modelled according to a simplified Bethe-Bloch prescription with a Bragg peak, with the jet depositing energy and longitudinal momentum over a Gaussian profile of width sigma = 0.3 fm.

## Key numbers
- Unperturbed static energy density of the medium: epsilon_0 = 20.0 GeV/fm^3
- Initial jet velocity: v_0 = 0.9999; parton mass: m = 0.3 GeV
- Initial energy loss rate: a = -4.148 GeV/fm (jet stops after Delta tau = 5.0 fm/c)
- In the one-jet scenario, at the moment of jet extinction, region (I) (tube of diameter 1.5 fm around the jet trajectory) contains 92% of all jet momentum; after another 2.5 fm/c this drops to 87%
- For the unequal-energy opposite-direction jets: left jet deposits 21.5 GeV of energy, right jet deposits 10.5 GeV
- For the perpendicular jets scenario with doubled energy deposition: dE/dx = -8.296 GeV/fm; jets are fully stopped 1.5 fm/c before they would meet

## Context
This work extends the single-jet hydrodynamic response studies by Betz et al. (2008, 2010) to multiple hard partons, using LHC-relevant energy densities (epsilon_0 = 20 GeV/fm^3) rather than the RHIC-focused values of Betz et al. (2008) (dE/dx = 1.5 GeV/fm, T_0 = 200 MeV). It validates the assumption made in the toy model of Tomasik (2011) that interacting wakes from isotropically produced hard partons can generate elliptic flow on the level of one or two per cent. The authors note that inclusion of viscosity (Bouras et al., 2012) will weaken the effect, and that quantitative details remain to be investigated in realistic fireball simulations.
