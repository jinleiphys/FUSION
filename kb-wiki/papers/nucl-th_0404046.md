# Analytical relationship for the cranking inertia

> The wave function of a spheroidal harmonic oscillator without spin-orbit interaction is expressed in terms of associated Laguerre and Hermite polynomials. The pairing gap and Fermi energy are found by solving the BCS system of two equations. Analytical relationships for the matrix elements of inertia are obtained function of the main quantum numbers and potential derivative. They may be used to test complex computer codes one should develop in a realistic approach of the fission dynamics. The results given for the $^{240}$Pu nucleus are compared with a hydrodynamical model. The importance of taking into account the correction term due to the variation of the occupation number is stressed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical relationships for the cranking inertia matrix elements using a spheroidal harmonic oscillator without spin-orbit interaction, but the authors explicitly note that this simple single-particle model has "limited interest" and cannot describe fission processes reaching scission, ground states with necked-in shapes, or the reduced mass limit of separated fragments.

## Method

The authors expressed the wave functions of a spheroidal harmonic oscillator (without spin-orbit coupling) in terms of associated Laguerre and Hermite polynomials. They solved the BCS system of two equations to obtain the pairing gap and Fermi energy, then derived analytical formulas for the cranking inertia matrix elements as functions of the main quantum numbers and the derivative of the potential with respect to the quadrupolar deformation parameter ε. The inertia was computed for 240Pu and compared with the irrotational hydrodynamical model, including the correction term Pε arising from the variation of occupation numbers with deformation.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu, the spherical irrotational inertia is given by B^(irr)(0) = 0.0048205 A^(5/3) ℏ²/MeV, which for A=240 yields approximately 0.0048205 × 240^(5/3) ℏ²/MeV.
- The irrotational inertia for spheroidal shapes is B_ε^(irr)(ε) = B^(irr)(0) × 81 / [27 − ε²(9 + 2ε)]^(4/3) × (9 + 2ε²)/(3 − 2ε)².
- The unit ℏω₀⁰ = 41 A^(−1/3) MeV, which for 240Pu gives ℏω₀⁰ = 6.597 MeV.
- The gap parameter from a fit to experimental data is taken as Δ̃ = 12/√A ℏω₀⁰, corresponding to Δ̃ = 0.117 ℏω₀⁰ for 240Pu.
- A superdeformed spheroid with ε = 0.6 has a semiaxes ratio c/a = 2.
- The hydrodynamical formula with a fit parameter uses d = d₀ = R₀/2.452 and k = 11.5, or d = 2d₀ and k = 6.5.

## Context

This work extends the cranking model introduced by Inglis (1954) and the BCS pairing correlations from Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957) and Beliaev (1959), as well as the formulations by Bès (1961) and Brack et al. (1972). The analytical results are intended to test complex computer codes developed for realistic two-center shell models, such as that of W. Pannert et al. (1983). The paper enables benchmarking of numerical cranking calculations and highlights that the correction term P_ij from occupation number variation, often assumed negligible, should be included in dynamical fission calculations because the action integral depends exponentially on the product of mass parameter and deformation energy.
