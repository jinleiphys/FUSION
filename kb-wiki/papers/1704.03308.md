# Bayesian truncation errors in chiral effective field theory: nucleon-nucleon observables

> Chiral effective field theory (EFT) predictions are necessarily truncated at some order in the EFT expansion, which induces an error that must be quantified for robust statistical comparisons to experiment. In previous work, a Bayesian model for truncation errors of perturbative expansions was adapted to EFTs. The model yields posterior probability distribution functions (pdfs) for these errors based on expectations of naturalness encoded in Bayesian priors and the observed order-by-order convergence pattern of the EFT. A first application was made to chiral EFT for neutron-proton scattering using the semi-local potentials of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (EKM). Here we extend this application to consider a larger set of regulator parameters, energies, and observables as a general example of a statistical approach to truncation errors. The Bayesian approach allows for statistical validations of the assumptions and enables the calculation of posterior pdfs for the EFT breakdown scale. The statistical model is validated for EKM potentials whose convergence behavior is not distorted by regulator artifacts. For these cases, the posterior for the breakdown scale is consistent with EKM assumptions.

## Key claim

The Bayesian model for truncation errors in chiral EFT is validated for the semi-local EKM potentials only when regulator artifacts do not distort the convergence behavior; for these cases, the posterior for the breakdown scale is consistent with the EKM assumptions, but the model fails for larger regulator parameters (R = 1.1 fm and 1.2 fm) where cutoff artifacts become significant.

## Method

The authors extracted dimensionless expansion coefficients c_n from order-by-order chiral EFT calculations (LO through N⁴LO) of neutron-proton scattering observables using the semi-local EKM potentials with regulator parameters R = 0.8, 0.9, 1.0, 1.1, and 1.2 fm. They applied Bayesian priors (sets A, B, and C) that encode naturalness expectations for the coefficients, computed posterior probability distributions for the truncation error Δ_k, and generated degree-of-belief (DoB) intervals at each order. The model was validated by checking whether the order-by-order coefficients follow a bounded random distribution about zero and by extracting posterior pdfs for the EFT breakdown scale Λ_b.

## Key numbers

- For R = 0.9 fm potentials with Λ_b = 600 MeV, the dimensionless coefficients c₂–c₅ for the np total cross section have a characteristic size of about one (in practice about three) across energies up to 350 MeV.
- For R = 1.2 fm potentials with Λ_b = 400 MeV, the N²LO and N⁴LO coefficients remain small for the full range of lab energy, while N³LO coefficients transition from negative (order -3) at R = 0.8 fm to positive (order +3) at R = 1.0 fm and above.
- The differential cross section coefficients at E_lab = 96 MeV have a characteristic scale of about four at angles less than 150°, which is several times larger than the scale for the integrated cross section at this energy.
- DoB intervals were computed using prior set C_eps and C_{0.25,10}; the 95% DoB intervals for NLO and NNLO appeared too large while those for N³LO were too small when compared to the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian truncation error model first applied to chiral EFT by Furnstahl et al. (2015) for neutron-proton scattering using the semi-local potentials of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (EKM). The earlier study examined only four energies for the total cross section with R = 0.9 fm and R = 1.2 fm; here the authors consider a larger set of regulator parameters, energies up to 350 MeV, and additional observables including differential cross section and spin observables (A_y, A, D, A_xx, A_yy). The results enable statistical validation of naturalness assumptions and provide a framework for quantifying truncation errors in future chiral EFT calculations, though the model fails for potentials with significant regulator artifacts.
