# On the renormalization of the one-pion exchange potential and the consistency of Weinberg's power counting

> Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck recently argued that Weinberg's power counting in the few-nucleon sector is inconsistent and requires modifications. Their argument is based on the observed cutoff dependence of the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude calculated by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the regularized one-pion exchange potential and the cutoff Lambda varied in the range Lambda = 2...20 fm^(-1). In this paper we discuss the role the cutoff plays in the application of chiral effective field theory to the two-nucleon system and study carefully the cutoff-dependence of phase shifts and observables based on the one-pion exchange potential. We show that (i) there is no need to use the momentum-space cutoff larger than Lambda ~ 3 fm^(-1); (ii) the neutron-proton low-energy data show no evidence for an inconsistency of Weinberg's power counting if one uses Lambda ~ 3 fm^{-1}.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the inconsistency of Weinberg's power counting claimed by Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (2005) is not supported by neutron-proton low-energy data, provided one restricts the momentum-space cutoff to values around 3 fm^(-1) rather than extending it to 20 fm^(-1).

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering using the regularized one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) at leading order, with an exponential regulator function. They included contact interactions in partial waves where the tensor part of the OPEP is attractive (^3P_0, ^3D_2, and the coupled channels ^3S_1-^3D_1 and ^3P_2-^3F_2), fitting the low-energy constants to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis for laboratory energies below 10 MeV. They then systematically varied the cutoff Lambda from 1 to 20 fm^(-1) and compared the resulting phase shifts and T-matrix elements to the Nijmegen PWA and Virginia Tech single-energy PWA.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff range studied was Lambda = 1...20 fm^(-1), corresponding to approximately 200 MeV to 4 GeV.
- For the ^3S_1-^3D_1 coupled channel, the critical cutoff value was Lambda ~ 2 fm^(-1) for T_{11}^{12}, T_{13}^{12} and T_{33}^{12}, and Lambda ~ 3 fm^(-1) for T^{11}_{00}.
- For the ^3P_2-^3F_2 coupled channel, the critical cutoff was Lambda ~ 4-5 fm^(-1) for T^{11}_{02} and T^{11}_{22}.
- The deuteron binding energy showed a critical cutoff value of Lambda ~ 3 fm^(-1).
- For Lambda = 20 fm^(-1), spurious deeply bound states appeared with binding energies of 1.69 and 34.27 GeV in the ^3S_1-^3D_1 channel, 0.22 and 21.48 GeV in the ^3P_0 channel, and 0.26 GeV in the ^3D_2 channel.
- The axial-vector coupling constant was taken as g_A = 1.29, the pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV, and the pion mass M_pi = 138.03 MeV.

## Context

This work directly responds to the claim by Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (2005) that Weinberg's power counting in the few-nucleon sector is inconsistent and requires promotion of additional contact interactions to leading order. The authors follow the methodology of Lepage (1997, 2000) regarding optimal cutoff choice in effective field theory, arguing that using cutoffs larger than the chiral symmetry breaking scale (~1 GeV) introduces unnecessary fine-tuning. The results enable continued use of Weinberg's original power counting scheme for chiral effective field theory calculations in the two-nucleon system, provided the cutoff is kept near 3 fm^(-1).
