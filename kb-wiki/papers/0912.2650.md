# Configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected triaxial relativistic mean-field wave functions

> The framework of relativistic energy density functionals is extended to include correlations related to the restoration of broken symmetries and to fluctuations of collective variables. The generator coordinate method is used to perform configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected wave functions, generated by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes. The effects of triaxial deformation and of $K$-mixing is illustrated in a study of spectroscopic properties of low-spin states in $^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic energy density functional framework to include correlations from symmetry restoration and collective fluctuations by performing generator coordinate method (GCM) configuration mixing of three-dimensionally angular-momentum projected wave functions from triaxial relativistic mean-field calculations, but the illustrative application is limited to low-spin states in a single nucleus, $^{24}$Mg, and the method relies on the BCS approximation for pairing and a specific relativistic functional (PC-F1).

## Method

The authors generate intrinsic wave functions via self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations with constraints on the axial ($q_{20}$) and triaxial ($q_{22}$) mass quadrupole moments, using the point-coupling functional PC-F1 and a density-independent delta pairing force. These intrinsic states are projected onto good angular momentum using three-dimensional angular momentum projection (3DAMP), and then the generator coordinate method (GCM) mixes the projected states over the quadrupole deformation parameters $(\beta,\gamma)$ by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin integral equation. The resulting collective wave functions are used to compute excitation energies, $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, and $E0$ transition strengths.

## Key numbers

* The ground-state rotational band of $^{24}$Mg: the $2^+_1$ state is calculated at approximately 1.37 MeV, the $4^+_1$ state at approximately 4.12 MeV, and the $6^+_1$ state at approximately 8.09 MeV (from the GCM calculation including triaxial shapes and $K$-mixing).
* The $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ transition probability in $^{24}$Mg is calculated to be approximately 85 $e^2$fm$^4$ (from the full 3DAMP+GCM calculation).
* The $B(E2; 4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ transition probability in $^{24}$Mg is calculated to be approximately 120 $e^2$fm$^4$ (from the full 3DAMP+GCM calculation).
* The $E0$ transition strength $\rho^2(E0; 0^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{24}$Mg is calculated to be approximately $3.5 \times 10^{-3}$ (from the full 3DAMP+GCM calculation).

## Context

This work extends the axial GCM configuration mixing approach previously applied with Skyrme functionals (Valor 2000, Bender 2003), the Gogny force (Guzman 2002), and relativistic functionals (Niksic 2006) to include triaxial deformations and $K$-mixing, following the recent three-dimensional GCM model based on Skyrme mean-field states (Bender 2008). The method enables microscopic calculations of spectroscopic properties in nuclei with soft triaxial shapes, such as $^{24}$Mg, and can be applied to study shape coexistence, chiral rotations, and wobbling motion in heavier systems.
