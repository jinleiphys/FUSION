# Chiral Symmetry in Dense Matter with Meson Condensation

> Kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter [($Y$+$K$) phase], which may be realized in neutron stars, is discussed on the basis of chiral symmetry. With the use of the effective chiral Lagrangian for kaon--baryon and kaon--kaon interactions; coupled with the relativistic mean field theory and universal three-baryon repulsive interaction, we clarify the effects of the $s$-wave kaon--baryon scalar interaction simulated by the kaon--baryon sigma terms and vector interaction (Tomozawa--Weinberg term) on kaon properties in hyperon-mixed matter, the onset density of kaon condensation, and the equation of state with the ($Y$+$K$) phase. In particular, the quark condensates in the ($Y$+$K$) phase are obtained, and their relevance to chiral symmetry restoration is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter, which may occur in neutron stars, can be understood through chiral symmetry, and that the quark condensates in this mixed phase serve as an order parameter connecting the hadronic phase to a chirally restored quark phase, but the results depend on the specific values of the kaon-baryon sigma terms and the choice of the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors use an effective chiral SU(3)L × SU(3)R Lagrangian for kaon-baryon and kaon-kaon interactions, coupled with a minimal relativistic mean field (MRMF) theory for baryon-baryon interactions mediated by meson exchange, and include a universal three-baryon repulsion (UTBR) from the string-junction model and a phenomenological three-nucleon attraction (TNA). The ground state of the (Y+K) phase is obtained under conditions of charge neutrality, baryon number conservation, and beta equilibrium, with the classical kaon field treated as spatially uniform and the meson mean fields solved self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³, with binding energy B0 = 16.3 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, and symmetry energy S0 = 31.5 MeV.
- The slope parameter L is taken as 60, 65, or 70 MeV, with corresponding coupling constants gσN ranging from 5.27 to 6.07 and gωN from 8.16 to 9.77.
- The effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation (M_N*/M_N)0 ranges from 0.71 to 0.78 depending on the L value.
- The kaon rest mass mK is set to 493.677 MeV, and the meson decay constant f is 93 MeV.
- The onset density of kaon condensation ρB(K⁻) is estimated to be (3–4)ρ0, depending on the value of the KN sigma term ΣKN.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter using effective chiral Lagrangians and relativistic mean field theory, building on the framework developed by Muto, Maruyama, and Tatsumi in their series of papers (Muto 2024, Muto et al. 2021, 2022). It contrasts with approaches using nonlinear self-interacting meson potentials by instead incorporating the universal three-baryon repulsion from the string-junction model (Tamagaki 2008) and three-nucleon attraction (Nishizaki, Takatsuka, and Hiura 1994). The results enable future work on the role of kaon condensation as a pathway to chiral symmetry restoration and strange quark matter in neutron star interiors.
