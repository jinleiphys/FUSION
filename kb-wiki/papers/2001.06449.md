# On gauge invariance of transverse momentum dependent distributions at small x

> The interplay between the small x limit of QCD amplitudes and QCD factorization at moderate x has been studied extensively in recent years. It was finally shown that semiclassical formulations of small x physics can have the form of an infinite twist framework involving Transverse Momentum Dependent (TMD) distributions in the eikonal limit. In this work, we demonstrate that small x distributions can be formulated in terms of transverse gauge links. This allows in particular for direct and efficient decompositions of observables into subamplitudes involving gauge invariant sub-operators which span parton distributions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that small-x transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions can be formulated in terms of transverse gauge links, preserving explicit gauge invariance of the operators, which provides a more direct derivation of the equivalence between semiclassical small-x formulations and infinite-twist TMD frameworks than previous approaches.

## Method

The authors use the residual gauge freedom in light-cone gauge (A‚Āļ=0) to rewrite pairs of Wilson line operators as transverse gauge links via parallel transport on the transverse plane. They then apply a non-Abelian Stokes theorem to express the dipole operator as a transverse QCD string built from gauge-rotated gluon fields, and perform a systematic power expansion that maintains explicit gauge invariance at each order.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Dominguez et al. (2011) who first showed how to extract the small-x limit of TMD distributions from Wilson line operators, and of Altinoluk et al. (2019) who demonstrated that small-x observables could be rewritten as the eikonal limit of an infinite-twist TMD framework. The authors provide a more direct derivation that uncovers the underlying geometric structure preserving explicit gauge invariance, enabling straightforward generalization to other classes of observables beyond the examples considered.
