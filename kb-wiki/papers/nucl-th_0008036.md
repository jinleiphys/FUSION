# Life time of resonances in transport simulations

> We calculate the life time of a resonance in our recently developed framework for a test-particle description of transport processes for states with continuous mass spectra. The result differs from the expression commonly used in transport simulations but agrees with the one derived by Danielewicz and Pratt relating the life time to the scattering phase shift.

## Key claim

The life time of a resonance in transport simulations should be calculated from the energy derivative of the scattering phase shift, not from the inverse decay width, though both expressions yield the same average life time for a sharp resonance when integrated over the spectral function.

## Method

The author derives a transport equation from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for relativistic scalar fields using a gradient expansion, then identifies an effective particle number density \(S^<_{\rm eff}\) that is conserved during elastic scattering. By restricting to a uniform distribution of non-interacting resonances in vacuum with no production channels, the transport equation simplifies to a decay equation from which the life time is extracted as \(\tau = 2 p_0 \, {\cal A}\,(1-K)\), which is shown to equal \(\partial \delta / \partial p_0\) where \(\delta\) is the scattering phase shift.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\Delta\) baryon example with an on-shell width of 120 MeV, the average life time \(\langle \tau \rangle \approx 1.68\) fm/c, while \(\langle 1/\Gamma \rangle \approx 1.55\) fm/c and \(1/\Gamma_{\rm on-shell} \approx 1.64\) fm/c.
- Near threshold, the life time \(\tau\) scales as \((\sqrt{s}-(m_N+m_\pi))^{1/2}\), while the spectral function \({\cal A}\) scales as \((\sqrt{s}-(m_N+m_\pi))^{3/2}\).
- In the rest frame of a sharp resonance, the average life time reduces to \(\langle \tau \rangle = 1/\Gamma_{\rm on-shell}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previously developed framework for test-particle transport with continuous mass spectra (Leupold, 2000) to relativistic systems and resolves a debate about resonance life times in transport simulations. The result confirms the relation derived by Danielewicz and Pratt (1996) connecting life time to the scattering phase shift, and contradicts the commonly used expression \(\tau = p_0/(\sqrt{s}\,\Gamma)\) which diverges near threshold. The formalism enables consistent treatment of broad resonances in heavy-ion collision simulations and provides guidance for practical implementation of test-particle decays.
