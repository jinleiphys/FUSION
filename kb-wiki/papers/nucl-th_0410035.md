# Dileptons and Open Charm: Probes of Chiral Restoration

> We summarize the status of electromagnetic probes of strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS with respect to indications of chiral symmetry restoration. Explorative results for studying the open charm dynamics at BNL-RHIC are presented.

# Dileptons and Open Charm: Probes of Chiral Restoration

## Key claim

The thermal electromagnetic radiation from heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS is compatible with achieving maximum temperatures above the deconfinement temperature, and the low-mass dilepton spectrum requires a drastic reshaping in the resonance region that is compatible with expectations from chiral restoration; however, the authors note that advanced models that resolve space-time evolution details do not improve agreement with data compared to their simpler space-time averaged approach.

## Method

The authors use a space-time averaged temperature approach, replacing the detailed space-time evolution integral with a normalization factor and an averaged temperature, to describe dilepton and photon emission rates. For the charm sector at RHIC, they employ PYTHIA with CTEQ 5L parton distribution functions and parameterize gluon-radiative energy loss of charmed quarks in a geometrical model, comparing single-electron and dilepton spectra with PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum (initial) temperature of order 200 MeV, above the deconfinement temperature, extracted from SPS data
- Space-time averaged temperature of 145 MeV for Pb(40 AGeV)+Au dielectron data
- Space-time averaged temperature of 170 MeV for Pb(158 AGeV)+Au dielectron data and Pb(158 AGeV)+Pb photon data
- Averaged transverse expansion velocity of 0.3 c for the WA98 photon data description
- Charm and bottom cross sections in pp collisions at 200 GeV are 650 microbarn and 4.3 microbarn, respectively
- A common K factor of 5 used for both open charm and bottom production

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gallmeister (2003) that showed a space-time averaged description could consistently describe dilepton experiments CERES, HELIOS-3, NA38, and NA50 at CERN-SPS. The results build on the theoretical framework of Rapp and Wambach (2000) and Gale and Haglin (2002) for dilepton emission rates, and connect to lattice QCD evaluations by Wizorke (2002). The paper enables future quantitative determination of charm quark energy loss through forthcoming dilepton data at RHIC, particularly from NA60, and identifies the need for higher precision in RHIC electromagnetic signals to compare with existing predictions.
