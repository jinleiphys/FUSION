# Symmetry properties of SU3 vector coupling coefficients

> A presentation of the problem of calculating the vector coupling coefficients for $SU3 \supset SU2 \otimes U1$ is made, in the spirit of traditional treatments of SU2 coupling. The coefficients are defined as the overlap matrix element between product states and a coupled state with good SU3 quantum numbers. A technique for resolution of the outer degeneracy problem, based upon actions of the infinitesimal generators of SU3 is developed, which automatically produces vector coupling coefficients with symmetries under exchange of state labels which parallel the familiar symmetries of the SU2 case. An algorithm for efficient computation of these coefficients is outlined, for which an ANSI C code is available.

## Key claim

The paper presents a technique for resolving the outer degeneracy problem in SU3 vector coupling coefficients that automatically produces coefficients with symmetries under exchange of state labels, paralleling the familiar symmetries of the SU2 case, but this symmetry property is only guaranteed for couplings where the multiplicity is resolved using the specific operator-based method described, and the resulting coefficients are real but not necessarily expressible as square roots of ratios of integers.

## Method

The author defines SU3 vector coupling coefficients as overlap matrix elements between product states and a coupled state with good SU3 quantum numbers, using the subgroup chain SU3 ⊃ SU2 ⊗ U1. For a given coupling of irreps (p₁,q₁) and (p₂,q₂) to a composite irrep (p,q), the method focuses on constructing the state of highest weight (SHW) with k=m=p+q, l=0, then applying the infinitesimal generators T₊, V₊, and U₋ to generate recursion relations that determine all coefficients. For multiplicities greater than one, additional conditions are imposed by selecting a specific series of VCCs parameterized by an integer j, which resolves the degeneracy while preserving particle-exchange symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of an SU3 irrep (p,q) is given by d = ½(p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)
- The isospin quantum number I is related to subspace labels by I = ½(k-l)
- The isospin projection I_z is given by I_z = m - ½(k+l)
- The hypercharge Y is given by Y = k+l - ⅔(p+2q)
- The maximum value j_max for the series resolving outer multiplicity is j_max = min(γ+2σ, p₂+σ-γ, q₂, p₂+q₂-2γ-σ), where γ = ⅓(p₁+p₂-p) and σ = ⅓(q₁+q₂-q)
- The multiplicity α for a coupling is given by α = max(α'(p₁,q₁,p₂,q₂,p,q), 0), where α' = 1 + min(q₁,q₂,p,p₁+σ-γ,p₂+σ-γ,q+σ-γ,p₁+q₁-2γ-σ,p₂+q₂-2γ-σ,γ+2σ)

## Context

This work extends the SU3 coupling algebra tradition established by Biedenharn, Hecht, Louck and collaborators (the "canonical" resolution) and by Draayer et al., who produced Fortran codes for computing SU3 vector coupling coefficients. The paper contrasts with the canonical approach by aiming to preserve particle-exchange symmetries that the canonical coefficients lack, and with the Pluhar et al. (1986) approach which produced irrational missing labels. The method enables efficient computation of SU3 vector coupling coefficients with desirable symmetry properties, for which an ANSI C code is made available, facilitating applications in nuclear physics (rotational states via the Elliott model) and particle physics (flavor SU3 and QCD color symmetry).
