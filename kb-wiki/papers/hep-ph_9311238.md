# Color Transparency after the Ne18 and E665 Experiments

> The 1993 witnessed two major news in the field of color transparency (CT): i) no effect of CT was seen in the SLAC NE18 experiment on $A(e,e'p)$ scattering at virtualities of the exchanged photon $Q^{2} \lsim 7$ GeV$^{2}$, ii) strong signal of CT was observed in the Fermilab E665 experiment on exclusive $ρ^{0}$-meson production in deep inelastic scattering in the same range of $Q^{2}$. Both are good news and rule in CT, since this striking difference in the onset of CT for two reactions was predicted theoretically, and we review the theory of both reactions and the origin of this difference. We discuss an importance of final state interaction effects for the theoretical interpretation of the NE18 data. We comment on possible CT effects in the $^{4}He(e,e'p)$ interactions at CEBAF.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the contrasting results of the SLAC NE18 experiment (no color transparency seen in A(e,e'p) scattering at Q^2 less than or approximately 7 GeV^2) and the Fermilab E665 experiment (strong color transparency signal in exclusive rho^0-meson production in the same Q^2 range) are both consistent with color transparency theory, because this difference in the onset of color transparency for the two reactions was predicted theoretically.

## Method

The authors review the lightcone approach to virtual photoproduction of vector mesons, where the transverse size of quark configurations is controlled by r_Q = 2/sqrt(m_V^2 + Q^2) and the cross section sigma(r) vanishes as r goes to 0. For A(e,e'p) scattering, they use the hadronic basis expansion and multiple scattering theory, deriving color transparency sum rules that show the ejectile state has the same transverse size as the proton, with the onset of color transparency controlled by how rapidly these sum rules are saturated under the coherency constraint.

## Key numbers

- For the E665 experiment, the produced rho^0 has an energy nu approximately 200 GeV
- The NE18 experiment explored Q^2 less than or approximately 7 GeV^2
- For rho^0 production at average Q^2 = 7 GeV^2, the E665 experiment gives T_Pb/T_C = 0.6 plus or minus 0.25
- The NMC result for real photoproduction of J/Psi gives T_Sn/T_C = 0.7 plus or minus 0.1
- For the deuteron, 1 - T_d is approximately sigma_tot(pn)/(2 pi R_d^2) approximately 0.07
- The shift in longitudinal momentum from final state interactions is approximately sigma_tot(pN) alpha_pN n_A approximately alpha_pN times 70 MeV/c, with alpha_pN approximately -0.5 giving Delta p_m,z approximately -35 MeV/c
- For the coherent production ratio in complete color transparency: R_coh(Ca/C) = 1.56 and R_coh(Pb/C) = 3.25
- For J/Psi real photoproduction: R_coh(Sn/C) = 2.15 plus or minus 0.10 (NMC), R_coh(Fe/Be) = 2.28 plus or minus 0.32, R_coh(Pb/Be) = 3.47 plus or minus 0.50 (E691)
- At Q^2 approximately 9 GeV^2, the nuclear attenuation for incoherent rho^0 production is predicted to equal that for real photoproduction of J/Psi

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior theoretical predictions (Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1991-1993) that color transparency onset would be slow in A(e,e'p) scattering but fast in vector meson production, which were confirmed by the NE18 and E665 experiments respectively. The paper reviews the ITEP-Julich-Krakow-Landau collaboration's work on A(e,e'p) scattering and the authors' work on vector meson production, and comments on possible color transparency effects in 4He(e,e'p) interactions at CEBAF. The results enable future tests of the scaling law relating rho^0 and J/Psi production at higher Q^2.
