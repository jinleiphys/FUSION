# Further application of a semi-microscopic core-particle coupling method to the properties of Gd155,157, and Dy159

> In a previous paper a semi-microscopic core-particle coupling method that includes the conventional strong coupling core-particle model as a limiting case, was applied to spectra and electromagnetic properties of several well-deformed odd nuclei. This work, coupled a large single-particle space to the ground state bands of the neighboring even cores. In this paper, we generalize the theory to include excited bands of the cores, such as beta and gamma bands, and thereby show that the resulting theory can account for the location and structure of all bands up to about 1.5 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including excited bands (beta, gamma, and higher) of the even-even cores in a semi-microscopic core-particle coupling model can account for the location and structure of all bands up to about 1.5 MeV in odd deformed nuclei, whereas the previous version of the model (which included only ground-state bands) had systematically missed one or more observed bands above about 1 MeV.

## Method

The authors extend the Kerman-Klein approach in the adaptation of Dönau and Frauendorf (KKDF) by coupling a large single-particle space (states from 5 major shells) to both the ground-state bands and the excited beta, gamma, and other low-lying bands of the neighboring even-even cores (154Gd, 156Gd, 158Dy, 160Dy). Excitation energies of the even cores are fitted to a rigid-rotor formula with band-dependent moments of inertia, and inter-band quadrupole transition matrix elements are described by the Bohr-Mottelson geometrical model. The Hamiltonian matrix for the odd nuclei is diagonalized using a projection-operator method that handles avoided crossings between states of the same angular momentum.

## Key numbers

* For 155Gd, a previously missing K=1/2 band at about 0.6 MeV was reproduced with the inclusion of excited bands; the quadrupole force strength was fitted to κ = 0.380 MeV/fm² (compared to 0.377 MeV/fm² without phonons).
* For 157Gd, a previously missing K=3/2 band at about 0.7 MeV was reproduced; the quadrupole force strength was κ = 0.401 MeV/fm² (compared to 0.397 MeV/fm² without phonons).
* For 159Dy, two previously missing bands (K=3/2 at about 0.7 MeV and K=5/2 at about 1 MeV) were reproduced when beta and gamma excited bands were included.
* Inter-band BE(2) transitions in the even cores are about two orders of magnitude smaller than intra-band transitions (i.e., quadrupole transition matrix elements are an order of magnitude smaller).
* The moments of inertia for different bands in the even cores differ from each other by up to 20%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the KKDF method (Protopapas, Klein, Walet, 1994) which coupled a large single-particle space only to ground-state bands of even cores and successfully reproduced low-lying bands without ad-hoc Coriolis attenuation factors, but failed to account for bands above about 1 MeV. The present inclusion of excited core bands (beta, gamma, and higher) fills those gaps, showing that the missing bands are essentially one-quasiparticle-plus-excited-core bands. The approach enables a more complete description of odd deformed nuclei up to about 1.5 MeV excitation energy, though the simplified Hamiltonian (monopole pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole) is retained; preliminary studies including quadrupole pairing and hexadecapole interactions showed only small effects when those interactions were an order of magnitude weaker than the leading terms.
