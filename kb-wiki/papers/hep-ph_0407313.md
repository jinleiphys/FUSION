# A Derivative of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule

> We derive a sum rule which establishes a linear relation between a particle's anomalous magnetic moment and a quantity connected to the photoabsorption cross-section. This quantity cannot be measured directly. However, it can be computed within a given theory. As an example, we demonstrate validity of the sum rule in QED at tree level--the renowned Schwinger's correction to the anomalous magnetic moment is readily reproduced. In the case of the strong interactions, we also consider the calculation of the nucleon magnetic moment within chiral theories.

# A Derivative of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule

## Key claim
The authors derive a new sum rule that establishes a linear relation between a particle's anomalous magnetic moment and a derivative of the photoabsorption cross-section with respect to the classical (tree-level) anomalous magnetic moment, evaluated at zero classical moment. This linearized sum rule allows loop corrections to the anomalous magnetic moment to be computed from tree-level cross sections, but the cross-section quantity entering the sum rule is not directly measurable in the theory with vanishing classical anomalous magnetic moment.

## Method
Starting from the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule, the authors introduce an explicit Pauli term (classical anomalous magnetic moment κ₀) into the Lagrangian. They subtract the GDH sum rule for the theory with the Pauli term from the original GDH sum rule, then take the first derivative with respect to κ₀ and set κ₀ = 0. The resulting sum rule relates the anomalous magnetic moment κ linearly to an integral over the derivative of the photoabsorption cross-section with respect to κ₀. The authors verify the sum rule in QED at tree level and in chiral pion-nucleon theory at tree level for single-pion photoproduction.

## Key numbers
- In QED, the linearized sum rule yields κ = α/2π (Schwinger's correction), obtained from the integral (1/π)∫₀^∞ (dω/ω) Δσ₁(ω) = 2α²/M².
- For the proton in chiral theory, the one-loop correction to the anomalous magnetic moment is κ_p^(loop) = (g²/(4π)²) {1 - μ(4 - 11μ² + 3μ⁴)/√(1 - μ²/4) arccos(μ/2) - 6μ² + 2μ²(-5 + 3μ²) ln μ}, where μ = m_π/M.
- For the neutron, κ_n^(loop) = (-2g²/(4π)²) {2 - μ(2 - μ²)/√(1 - μ²/4) arccos(μ/2) - 2μ² ln μ}.
- The chiral expansion of the proton loop correction gives leading nonanalytic term -2πμ (linear in pion mass), where μ = m_π/M.
- The fit to lattice data uses parameters a_p = 1.6/M² and a_n = 1.05/M² for the proton and neutron magnetic moments respectively, with M the physical nucleon mass.

## Context
This work extends the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (GDH, 1966) by deriving its first derivative with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment. The authors verify that in QED the sum rule reproduces Schwinger's one-loop correction (Schwinger, 1948) from tree-level cross sections, which is more economical than the one-loop verification of the GDH sum rule by Dicus and Vega (2001). For nucleons, the results agree with the one-loop calculation of Beg and Holstein (1972) but disagree with the infrared-regularized chiral perturbation theory calculation of Kubis and Meissner (2001), which the authors argue has incorrect analytic structure. The sum rule results provide a parametrization for chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data on nucleon magnetic moments, showing better agreement with lattice simulations than heavy-baryon or infrared-regularized approaches.
