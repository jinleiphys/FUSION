# Search for $α$ condensed states in $^{13}$C using $α$ inelastic scattering

> We searched for the $α$ condensed state in $^{13}$C by measuring the $α$ inelastic scattering at $E_α = 388$ MeV at forward angles including 0 degrees. We performed the distorted-wave Born-approximation calculation with the single-folding potential and the multipole decomposition analysis to determine the isoscalar transition strengths in $^{13}$C. We found a bump structure around $E_x = 12.5$ MeV due to the isoscalar monopole ($IS0$) transition. A peak-fit analysis suggested that this bump consisted of several $1/2^-$ states. We propose that this bump is due to the mirror state of the 13.5 MeV-state in $^{13}$N, which dominantly decays to the $α$ condensed state in $^{12}$C. It was speculated that the $1/2^-$ states around $E_x = 12.5$ MeV were candidates for the $α$ condensed state, but the $3α+ n$ orthogonality condition model suggests that the $α$ condensed state is unlikely to emerge as the negative parity states. We also found two $1/2^+$ or $3/2^+$ states at $E_x = 14.5$ and 16.1 MeV excited with the isoscalar dipole ($IS1$) strengths. We suggest that the 16.1-MeV state is a possible candidate for the $α$ condensed state predicted by the cluster-model calculations on the basis of the good correspondence between the experimental and calculated level structures. However, the theoretical $IS1$ transition strength for this state is significantly smaller than the measured value. Further experimental information is strongly desired to establish the $α$ condensed state in $^{13}$C.

## Key claim

The authors searched for alpha-condensed states in 13C and propose that a 16.1-MeV state is a possible candidate based on correspondence with cluster-model calculations, but they note that the theoretical isoscalar dipole transition strength for this state is significantly smaller than the measured value, and they state that further experimental information is strongly desired to establish the alpha-condensed state in 13C.

## Method

The authors measured alpha inelastic scattering on a 13C target at E_alpha = 388 MeV using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at forward angles including 0 degrees, with a recoil proton counter to reject background from hydrogen contaminants. They performed distorted-wave Born-approximation calculations with a single-folding potential and multipole decomposition analysis to extract isoscalar transition strengths for different multipolarities. A peak-fit analysis was applied to the excitation-energy spectra in the region 10.5–13.0 MeV to identify individual states and their contributions.

## Key numbers

- The bump structure around E_x = 12.5 MeV was found to consist of several 1/2- states, with isoscalar monopole (IS0) transition strengths for individual states ranging from B(IS0) = 0.92 ± 0.05 fm^4 (at E_x = 12.775 MeV) to B(IS0) = 19.2 ± 0.3 fm^4 (at E_x = 11.080 MeV)
- Two states at E_x = 14.5 and 16.1 MeV were excited with isoscalar dipole (IS1) strengths; the 16.1-MeV state is proposed as a candidate for the alpha-condensed state
- The 1/2- state at E_x = 11.080 MeV has B(IS0) = 19.2 ± 0.3 fm^4, corresponding to 3.2 ± 0.1% of the energy-weighted sum-rule fraction
- The 1/2+ state at E_x = 10.996 MeV has an upper limit of B(IS1) < 2.3 fm^6, corresponding to < 0.5% of the EWSR fraction
- The 1/2+ state at E_x = 12.140 MeV has an upper limit of B(IS1) < 1.8 fm^6, corresponding to < 0.5% of the EWSR fraction

## Context

This work extends previous searches for alpha-condensed states in A ≠ 4k nuclei, following studies in 11B by Kawabata et al. (2007) and theoretical predictions from the 3α + n orthogonality condition model by Yamada (2015) and AMD calculations by Chiba (2020). The 3α + n OCM calculation predicts that the 1/2_5+ state at E_x = 14.9 MeV is the alpha-condensed state with an excess neutron, while the AMD calculation predicts the 1/2_2+ state at E_x = 15.4 MeV as the alpha-condensed state. The present results provide experimental candidates for these predicted states but leave open the question of whether the alpha-condensed nature of the Hoyle state in 12C is preserved when an extra neutron is added.
