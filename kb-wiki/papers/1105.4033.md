# Empirical estimation the effects of flow on thermal photon angular distribution and spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC

> Experimental data for hadron radial and elliptic flow are used to investigate their influence on the shape of the thermal photon spectrum at RHIC. Leaving alone the actual mechanism of photon production and its time evolution, we concentrate on the spectrum shape. Radial and longitudinal flow of the bulk can change significantly the observed photon energy spectrum via the Doppler Effect. Experimental thermal photon data are described by local frame temperature parameter which depends on the assumption of longitudinal and radial flow. From the observed hadron elliptic flow we estimate the modulation of radial flow parameter versus the angle relative to the reaction plane. Based on this we calculate elliptic flow parameter for thermal photons, which was found to be very close to that for hadrons. Considering very similar amplitude for hadron elliptic flow at LHC and RHIC, we demonstrate that thermal photons at LHC should show large elliptic flow as well. All of these considerations are also valid for the low invariant mass dilepton pairs.

# Empirical estimation the effects of flow on thermal photon angular distribution and spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

Thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC can be largely explained by Doppler shifts from radial and longitudinal bulk flow alone, without specifying the exact photon production mechanism or time evolution. The author finds that the thermal photon elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ is very close to that for hadrons, but notes this estimate should be considered an upper limit because photons produced before bulk flow develops would dilute the signal.

## Method

The author uses experimental hadron radial and elliptic flow data to constrain a simple model where thermal photons are emitted from a source with a linear transverse velocity profile (maximum $\beta_T = 0.75c$) and longitudinal boost, using wounded nucleon density profiles for Au+Au collisions. The photon spectrum is calculated via relativistic Doppler shift formulas applied to a Boltzmann distribution, and the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ for thermal photons is estimated by converting the observed hadron $v_2$ into a modulation of radial flow amplitude between in-plane and out-of-plane directions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum transverse boost used: $\beta_T = 0.75c$, with results insensitive to variation in the range 0.7–0.8$c$
- Best fit to experimental thermal photon spectrum at RHIC for central 0–5% Au+Au collisions: temperature $T = 0.17$ GeV with $\beta_L = 0.5c$ and $\beta_T = 0.75c$
- Lowest possible temperature estimate (assuming no longitudinal flow, $\beta_L = 0$): $T = 0.15$ GeV
- Hadron $v_2$ per constituent quark saturates at $v_2 = 0.07$ at kinetic energy around 1 GeV per quark for mid-central collisions
- For constituent quark mass $m_q = 0.3$ GeV with $T_q = 0.1$ GeV and $v_2 = 0.07$: $\Delta\gamma = 0.047$, giving $\beta^{in} = 0.77$ and $\beta^{out} = 0.73$
- For constituent quark mass $m_q = 0.07$ GeV: $\Delta\gamma = 0.2$, giving $\beta^{in} = 0.81$ and $\beta^{out} = 0.65$

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of using hydrodynamic calculations for thermal photon production by isolating the Doppler shift effects from flow, which are common to all such models. The author uses wounded nucleon density profiles from Jia (2008) and radial flow parameters from PHENIX data (Adcox et al., 2005), and compares results to experimental thermal photon data from PHENIX (Adare et al., 2010). The finding that thermal photon $v_2$ should be large and comparable to hadron $v_2$ at both RHIC and LHC provides a testable prediction for LHC experiments, and the same analysis framework is noted to apply to low invariant mass dilepton pairs.
