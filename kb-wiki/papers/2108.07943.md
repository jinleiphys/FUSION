# Interplay between core and corona components in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We establish the updated version of dynamical core--corona initialization framework (DCCI2) as a unified description from small to large colliding systems and from low to high transverse momentum ($p_T$) regions. Using DCCI2, we investigate effects of interplay between locally equilibrated and non-equilibrated systems, in other words, core and corona components in high-energy nuclear collisions. Given experimental multiplicity distributions and yield ratios of $Ω$ baryons to charged pions as inputs, we extract the fraction of core and corona components in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. We find core contribution overtakes corona contribution as increasing multiplicity above $\langle dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/dη\rangle_{|η|<0.5} \sim 18$ regardless of the collision system or energy. We also see that the core contribution exceeds the corona contribution only in 0.0-0.95\% multiplicity class in p+p collisions. Notably, there is a small enhancement of corona contribution with $\sim20$\% below $p_T\sim 1$ GeV even in minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions. We find that the corona contribution at low $p_T$ gives $\sim 15$-$38$ $\%$ correction on $v_2\{2\}$ at $N_{\mathrm{ch}}\lesssim 370$. This raises a problem in conventional hydrodynamic analyses in which low $p_T$ soft hadrons originate solely from core components. We finally scrutinize the roles of string fragmentation and the longitudinal expansion in the transverse energy per unit rapidity, which is crucial in initial conditions for hydrodynamics from event generators based on string models.

## Key claim

The core (equilibrated) contribution overtakes the corona (non-equilibrated) contribution only above a multiplicity threshold of approximately 18 charged particles per unit pseudorapidity, regardless of collision system or energy; crucially, the corona component contributes a non-negligible 15-38% correction to the elliptic flow observable v2{2} at low transverse momentum (below about 1 GeV) even in heavy-ion collisions, challenging the conventional assumption that low-pT hadrons originate solely from the thermalized core.

## Method

The authors developed the DCCI2 framework, which combines PYTHIA8-generated initial partons with a dynamical initialization scheme that gradually converts partons into equilibrated QGP fluids via source terms in relativistic hydrodynamic equations, based on a local density-dependent fluidization rate. The core (equilibrated fluid) is evolved with (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics and hadronized via the Cooper-Frye formula using the iS3D Monte-Carlo sampler, while the corona (non-equilibrated) partons undergo string fragmentation in PYTHIA8; both components are then fed into the JAM hadronic cascade model for final-state rescatterings and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Core contribution overtakes corona contribution above a multiplicity threshold of ⟨dNch/dη⟩|η|<0.5 ∼ 18, independent of collision system or energy
- In p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the core contribution exceeds the corona contribution only in the 0.0-0.95% highest-multiplicity class
- There is a ∼20% enhancement of corona contribution below pT ∼ 1 GeV even in minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- The corona contribution at low pT gives a ∼15-38% correction on v2{2} for events with Nch ≲ 370

## Context

This work extends the dynamical core-corona initialization (DCCI) framework previously developed by Kanakubo, Tachibana, and Hirano (2018, 2019) and the dynamical initialization model by Okai, Tachibana, and Hirano (2017). It contrasts with the EPOS event generator approach of Pierog and Werner (2013, 2018), which separates core and corona components sharply at a fixed time based on density and transverse momentum thresholds. The DCCI2 framework enables a unified description from p+p to Pb+Pb collisions that respects total energy conservation, and its finding of significant corona contributions at low pT raises questions for conventional hydrodynamic analyses that assume low-pT hadrons come entirely from the thermalized core, which is critical for precision extraction of QGP transport coefficients.
