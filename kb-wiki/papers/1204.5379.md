# Light Sterile Neutrinos: A White Paper

> This white paper addresses the hypothesis of light sterile neutrinos based on recent anomalies observed in neutrino experiments and the latest astrophysical data.

## Key claim

This white paper assesses the hypothesis that light sterile neutrinos exist with masses around 1 eV, motivated by several experimental anomalies, but concludes that the evidence is not yet definitive and that a consistent interpretation across all datasets remains challenging.

## Method

The paper compiles and reviews the status of experimental anomalies from short-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments (including LSND, MiniBooNE, and reactor neutrino experiments), cosmological constraints on sterile neutrino properties, and theoretical models for sterile neutrino mass generation. It surveys future experimental capabilities to test the sterile neutrino hypothesis, including proposed beam-based and radioactive source experiments, as well as improved cosmological probes.

## Key numbers

* Reactor neutrino anomaly: observed flux deficit of approximately 6% relative to predictions, corresponding to a reactor antineutrino anomaly significance of about 3 sigma.
* LSND experiment: observed an excess of electron antineutrino events at a significance of 3.8 sigma, consistent with oscillations at Delta m^2 around 1 eV^2.
* MiniBooNE experiment: observed an excess of electron neutrino and antineutrino events at a significance of 3.4 sigma in neutrino mode and 2.8 sigma in antineutrino mode.
* Gallium anomaly: calibration experiments with radioactive sources (SAGE and GALLEX) show a deficit of neutrino events at approximately 3 sigma significance.
* Cosmological constraints: combined data from cosmic microwave background and large-scale structure limit the sum of neutrino masses to less than about 0.5 eV at 95% confidence level, which constrains but does not exclude sterile neutrinos with eV-scale masses.

## Context

This white paper extends the discussion of anomalies first highlighted by the LSND experiment (Aguilar et al., 2001) and the subsequent MiniBooNE results (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2007, 2009), as well as the reactor neutrino anomaly identified by Mention et al. (2011). It synthesizes the work of many individual experimental collaborations and theoretical groups to provide a comprehensive assessment of the sterile neutrino hypothesis. The paper enables future coordinated experimental programs, such as the proposed Short-Baseline Neutrino program at Fermilab and new reactor experiments, to definitively test the existence of light sterile neutrinos.
