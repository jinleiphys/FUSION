# Transition from participant to spectator fragmentation in Au+Au reaction between 60 AMeV and 150 AMeV

> Using the quantum molecular dynamics approach, we analyze the results of the recent INDRA Au+Au experiments at GSI in the energy range between 60 AMeV and 150 AMeV. It turns out that in this energy region the transition toward a participant-spectator scenario takes place. The large Au+Au system displays in the simulations as in the experiment simultaneously dynamical and statistical behavior which we analyze in detail: The composition of fragments close to midrapidity follows statistical laws and the system shows bi-modality, i.e. a sudden transition between different fragmentation pattern as a function of the centrality as expected for a phase transition. The fragment spectra at small and large rapidities, on the other hand, are determined by dynamics and the system as a whole does not come to equilibrium, an observation which is confirmed by FOPI experiments for the same system.

## Key claim

Using Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations, the authors show that in Au+Au collisions between 60 AMeV and 150 AMeV, the system displays both statistical and dynamical behavior simultaneously: fragments near midrapidity follow statistical laws and show bi-modality, but the system as a whole does not reach kinetic equilibrium, with the transition toward a participant-spectator scenario occurring across this energy range.

## Method

The authors performed QMD simulations of Au+Au collisions at 60, 100, and 150 AMeV, generating approximately 100,000 events over a large impact parameter range with a soft equation of state. They applied a software replica of the INDRA detector (filter) to the simulation events to account for detector acceptance, thresholds, and multiple hits, then compared filtered QMD results directly with INDRA experimental data. Fragments were identified using a minimum spanning tree procedure with a coalescence radius of 2.5 fm, and the composition of fragments was analyzed in terms of nucleons originating from projectile versus target nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 60 AMeV, 100 AMeV, and 150 AMeV for Au+Au reactions
- Intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) defined as fragments with charge Z between 3 and 25
- Central events selected by requiring total transverse energy of Z=1,2 particles (E_trans12) greater than 1246 MeV at 60 AMeV and 3313 MeV at 150 AMeV, corresponding to a cross section of π(1 fm)²
- The filter reduced the average E_trans12 by 13% at 60 AMeV and 23% at 150 AMeV
- For central Au+Au at 150 AMeV, the ratio R = ⟨y²_x⟩/⟨y²_z⟩ was approximately 0.9 for protons but decreased rapidly with increasing fragment charge, well below the equilibrium value of 1.0
- At 60 AMeV, the fragment multiplicity had stabilized before 200 fm/c; at 100 and 150 AMeV, fragment multiplicity stabilized before 200 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier QMD studies of smaller systems, notably the Xe+Sn system at 50 AMeV studied by the same group, where fragments were dominated by either projectile or target nucleons. The authors find that for the heavier Au+Au system, complete mixing of projectile and target nucleons occurs in midrapidity fragments, enabling statistical model approaches for fragment yields, while the dynamical degrees of freedom remain unequilibrated, a result confirmed by both INDRA and FOPI experiments. The observation of bi-modality in the same event class supports the interpretation of a liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclear systems, as previously suggested by theoretical work.
