# Spectrum of secondary electrons emitted during the nuclear $β^{-}$-decay of the tritium atom

> Ionization of the final ${}^{3}$He$^{+}$ ion during the nuclear $β^{-}$-decay of the tritium atom is discussed. The velocity spectrum of the emitted secondary electrons is derived in the explicit form. Our method allows to determine the relative and absolute probabilities of formation of the final states in few-electron atoms which include `free' secondary electrons moving with different velocities. \noindent PACS number(s): 23.40.-s, 12.20.Ds and 14.60.Cd

# Spectrum of secondary electrons emitted during the nuclear β⁻-decay of the tritium atom

## Key claim

The velocity spectrum of secondary electrons emitted during tritium β⁻-decay is derived in closed analytical form under the sudden approximation, which is valid because the fast β⁻-electron velocity is at least 100 times larger than typical atomic electron velocities. The method yields both relative and absolute probabilities for bound-free transitions, but the results are restricted to the case where the incident tritium atom is initially in its ground 1²s-state and the nucleus is treated as infinitely heavy.

## Method

The author applies the sudden approximation to compute the overlap integral between the non-relativistic ground-state wave function of the tritium atom (¹³H, 1²s-state) and the wave function of the final ³He⁺ ion combined with a free electron described by a Coulomb wave function with incoming spherical wave asymptotic. The probability amplitude is derived analytically by evaluating the radial integral involving confluent hypergeometric functions, yielding a closed-form expression for the velocity spectral function S_e(v; Q) of the secondary electron. The normalization constant is determined numerically by requiring the integral of the spectral function over velocity from 0 to v_max to equal unity.

## Key numbers

- Total probability of bound-bound transitions for the process ³H → ³He⁺ + e⁻(β) + ν̄: P_bb = 0.97372735(10)
- Total probability of bound-free transitions: P_bf = 1 − P_bb ≈ 0.02627265(10)
- Normalization constant for the velocity spectral function S_e(v; Q) for tritium β⁻-decay with infinitely heavy nucleus: S(Q) ≈ 196.611833628395
- The most important velocities of secondary electrons lie between v_min ≈ 0.4 a.u. and v_max ≈ 3.4 a.u.
- Maximum velocity for secondary electrons in light atoms: v_max = 50 α c (where α is the fine structure constant and c is the speed of light)

## Context

This work extends the classic calculations of bound-bound transition probabilities for tritium β⁻-decay performed by Migdal (MigK) and later by Frolov (2005), which previously achieved high accuracy for bound-bound probabilities but did not provide the velocity or momentum spectra of secondary electrons from bound-free transitions. The derived analytical formulas enable determination of partial probabilities p_bf(p) for bound-free transitions, which were previously unsolved. This approach can be generalized to few-electron atoms, where electron-electron correlations in the incident atom will significantly alter the secondary electron spectra, and it provides the necessary input for experiments requiring knowledge of the energy distribution of emitted secondary electrons.
