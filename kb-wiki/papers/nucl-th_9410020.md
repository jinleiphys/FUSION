# q-deformed pairing-vibrations

> Boson creation operators constructed from linear combinations of q- deformed zero coupled nucleon pair operators acting on the nucleus (A,0), are used to derive pp-RPA equations. The solutions of these equations are the pairing vibrations in (A${\underline{+}}$2) nuclei. For the $0^+_1$ and $0^+_2$ states of the nucleus $^{208}$Pb, the variations of relative energies and transfer cross-sections for populating these states via (t,p) reaction, with deformation parameter $τ$ have been analysed. For $τ=0.405$ the experimental excitation energy of 4.87MeV and the ratio $\frac{σ(0^+_2) }{σ(0^+_1)}=0.45$ are well reproduced. The critical value of pairing interaction strength for which phase transition takes place, is seen to be lower for deformed zero-coupled nucleon pair condensate with $τ$ real, supporting our earlier conclusion that the real deformation simulates the two-body residual interaction. For $τ$ purely imaginary a stronger pairing interaction is required to bring about the phase transition. The effect of imaginary deformation is akin to that of an antipairing type repulsive interaction. Using deformed zero coupled quasi-particle pairs, a deformed version of Quasi-boson approximation for $0^+$ states in superconducting nuclei is developed. For the test model of 20 particles in two shells, the results of q-deformed boson and quasi-boson approximations have been compared with exact results. It is found that the deformation effectively takes into account the anharmonicities and may be taken as a quantitative measure of the correlations not being accounted for in a certain approximate treatment.

# q-deformed pairing-vibrations

## Key claim

The paper shows that using a q-deformed (real) deformation parameter τ = 0.405 in a simple boson model reproduces both the experimental excitation energy of the double pairing vibration state in 208Pb and the ratio of (t,p) transfer cross-sections, which a linear calculation without deformation cannot achieve; the deformation parameter effectively simulates anharmonicities that would otherwise require more complex calculations.

## Method

The authors construct boson creation operators from linear combinations of q-deformed zero-coupled nucleon pair operators, using the generators of SU_q(2) with q = e^τ, and derive pp-RPA equations for nonsuperconducting nuclei. For the 208Pb case, they solve the dispersion relation using experimental single-particle energies for six hole levels and seven particle levels, and compute two-nucleon transfer amplitudes for populating the 0^+_1 ground state and 0^+_2 double pairing vibration state via (t,p) reaction. For superconducting nuclei, they develop a deformed quasi-boson approximation using q-deformed zero-coupled quasi-particle pairs and test it on a model of 20 particles distributed over two shell-model orbits.

## Key numbers

- For τ = 0.405, the experimental excitation energy of the 0^+_2 state in 208Pb of 4.87 MeV is reproduced.
- For τ = 0.405, the ratio σ(0^+_2)/σ(0^+_1) = 0.45 from (t,p) reaction data is reproduced.
- The linear calculation without deformation (Bes and Broglia, 1966) gave a ratio R = 1.3 for the same states.
- For the test model of N = 20 particles in two shells, deformed quasi-boson approximation with τ = 0.15 shows good agreement with exact results away from the phase transition region.
- For the test model, deformed boson approximation with τ = i0.104 shows good agreement with exact results away from the phase transition region.
- The critical pairing interaction strength G_c decreases with increasing real τ and increases with increasing imaginary τ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Sharma, 1992) on q-deformed zero-coupled nucleon pair states, where real deformation was found to simulate attractive residual interaction. It builds on the linear pairing vibration model of Bes and Broglia (1966) and the anharmonic boson model of Sorensen (1967), who showed that including anharmonicities increases the (t,p) cross-section to the 0^+_1 state and decreases it to the 0^+_2 state in 208Pb. The q-deformed approach provides a simpler way to incorporate these anharmonic effects, and the test model results suggest the deformation parameter can serve as a quantitative measure of correlations not accounted for in approximate treatments.
