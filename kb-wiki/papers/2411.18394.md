# On formation of the $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) and $^{12}$C(3$^-$) states in relativistic dissociation of light nuclei

> The formation of the excited states $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) and $^{12}$C(3$^-$) is investigated in the dissociation of $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$α$ and $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$α$ at the energy of 3.65 GeV per nucleon in the nuclear emulsion. The identification becomes possible by reconstructing the invariant mass from measurements of emission angles in the approximation of conservation of momentum per nucleon of the parent nucleus. The contribution of the decays $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) and $^{12}$C(3$^-$) to the dissociation $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$α$ is 11 and 19%, and in $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$α$ it is - 20 and 30%, correspondingly.

## Key claim

The excited states $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) and $^{12}$C(3$^-$) are identified in relativistic dissociation of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O at 3.65 GeV per nucleon by reconstructing the invariant mass from emission angle measurements, under the approximation of conservation of momentum per nucleon of the parent nucleus.

## Method

The emission angles of relativistic alpha particles were measured in nuclear emulsion exposed at the JINR Synchrophasotron, for 510 $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$\alpha$ dissociation events and 641 coherent $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$ dissociation events at 3.65 GeV per nucleon. The invariant mass $Q$ was reconstructed from these angles assuming conservation of momentum per nucleon of the parent nucleus. For $^{12}$C(3$^-$) identification, the condition $^8$Be(0$^+$) $Q_{2\alpha}$ $<$ 200 keV was applied to suppress combinatorial background from $^8$Be(2$^+$)$\alpha$ and higher excitations, and the range 1 MeV $<$ $Q_{3\alpha}$ $<$ 4 MeV was adopted.

## Key numbers

- In $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$\alpha$ dissociation: contribution of $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) decays is 11% and $^{12}$C(3$^-$) decays is 19%
- In $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$ dissociation: contribution of $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$)$\alpha$ channel is 20% and $^{12}$C(3$^-$)$\alpha$ channel is 30%
- The $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) peak in $^{12}$C dissociation has average $Q_{3\alpha}$(RMS) = 417 $\pm$ 27 (165) keV
- The $^{12}$C(3$^-$) distribution in $^{12}$C dissociation is described by a Rayleigh distribution with parameter $\sigma$ = 2.4 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV
- Contributions to $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$\alpha$: $^8$Be(0$^+$) 43 $\pm$ 4%, $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) 9 $\pm$ 1%, $^{12}$C(3$^-$) 19 $\pm$ 2%
- Ratio of $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) to $^{12}$C(3$^-$) in $^{12}$C dissociation is 0.47 $\pm$ 0.06
- In $^{16}$O dissociation: $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$)$\alpha$ channel 23 $\pm$ 2%, $^{12}$C(3$^-$)$\alpha$ channel 32 $\pm$ 2%, 2$^8$Be(0$^+$) 6 $\pm$ 1%
- Ratio of $^{12}$C(3$^-$)$\alpha$ to $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$)$\alpha$ channels in $^{16}$O dissociation is 1.4 $\pm$ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the relativistic fragmentation approach previously used to identify $^8$Be(0$^+$) decays in fragmentation of nuclei from $^9$Be to $^{197}$Au (Zaitsev and Zarubin, 2023 summary), and to identify $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) in $^{12}$C $\to$ 3$\alpha$ and $^{16}$O $\to$ 4$\alpha$ dissociation (earlier BECQUEREL experiment results). The identification of $^{12}$C(3$^-$) enables comparison with low-energy clustering studies (reviews by Freer, 2012 and others) where the ratio of $^{12}$C(3$^-$) to $^{12}$C(0$^+_2$) is approximately 1.5. The results support the prospect of observing 4$\alpha$-particle decays of $^{16}$O(0$^+_6$) and extending studies to unstable states in neighboring nuclei including radioactive ones such as $^{11}$C $\to$ $^7$Be$\alpha$.
