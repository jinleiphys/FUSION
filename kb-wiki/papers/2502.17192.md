# Stability conditions for bound states in antiprotonic atoms

> We study the stability of bound antiprotons in close proximity to the atomic nucleus. Using experimental data from X-ray spectroscopy measurements of antiprotonic atom transitions, we tune a minimal theoretical framework and estimate parameter ranges where the strong or electromagnetic decay channels govern the stability of the deepest bound states. In this way, we present an overview of the dominant decay mechanism of antiprotonic orbits for a given principle quantum number, to assist future spectroscopic experiments on antiprotonic atoms.

# Stability conditions for bound states in antiprotonic atoms

## Key claim

Using a minimal hydrogen-like framework tuned to existing X-ray spectroscopy data, the authors map where strong-interaction annihilation or electromagnetic decay dominates antiprotonic atom stability as a function of nuclear mass and principal quantum number, but note that precise quantitative predictions require more sophisticated models and that the qualitative boundaries remain robust to refinements.

## Method

The authors model the antiprotonic atom as a hydrogen-like system with circular Bohr orbits and describe the nuclear matter distribution with a two-parameter Fermi density. They extract the strong-interaction decay rate proportionality constant and nuclear diffuseness by fitting a linear overlap model to experimental X-ray spectroscopy data from LEAR-era measurements of antiprotonic atoms. Using these parameters, they compute ratios of strong to electromagnetic decay rates and strong to Bohr orbital decay rates to construct a stability diagram identifying regions where each interaction dominates.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radius for antiproton: \(a_0 \approx 28.8\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- Bohr energy for antiproton: \(E_0 \approx 50\,\mathrm{keV}\)
- Strong interaction strength parameter: \(\hbar\Gamma_0 = (12.1 \pm 0.6)\,\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Nuclear diffuseness from fit: \(\sigma = 1.02\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- Nuclear radius parameter: \(R_0 = 1.2\,\mathrm{fm}\)
- Saturation model fit gives \(\hbar\widetilde\Gamma_0 = (13.2\pm1.2)\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\gamma=(1.6\pm 1.6)\times 10^3\), with uncertainties too large to resolve saturation effects

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of antiprotonic atoms performed at the LEAR facility at CERN from the 1980s to the 1990s, which provided data on electromagnetic and strong interactions in these systems (Batty 1990, Backenstoss). The authors use data from Kanert (1986) and Trzcinska (2001) to calibrate their minimal model. The resulting stability map is intended to guide upcoming experiments at CERN's AD and ELENA facilities, including ASACUSA laser spectroscopy of antiprotonic helium (Hori 2011), pAX precision X-ray spectroscopy for strong-field QED tests (Paul 2021), PUMA studies of neutron-skin with short-lived nuclei (Aumann 2022), and AEgIS antiprotonic atom synthesis (Caravita 2025). The scarcity of data near predicted stability boundaries motivates new high-precision measurements.
