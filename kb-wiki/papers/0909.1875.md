# Gamow-Teller transitions from $^{9,11}$Li to $^{9,11}$Be

> Gamow-Teller(GT) transitions in the $β$ decays of $^9$Li and $^{11}$Li are investigated with theoretical calculations of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculated $B(GT)$ values are small for transitions to low-lying states in Be isotopes, while relatively large $B(GT)$ values are found for excited states at excitation energy $E_x\ge 10$ MeV. Sum of the $B(GT)$ values are discussed for each spin parity of final states. The calculated results seem to be inconsistent with the experimental report of the strongest GT-transition from $^9$Li to the $5/2^-$ state at 11.8 MeV of $^9$Be.

# Gamow-Teller transitions from $^{9,11}$Li to $^{9,11}$Be

## Key claim

The calculated $B(GT)$ values from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) are inconsistent with the experimentally reported strongest Gamow-Teller transition from $^9$Li to the $5/2^-$ state at 11.81 MeV in $^9$Be, which would require an implausibly large fraction of the Ikeda sum rule for a high-spin final state given an ordinary $^9$Li ground state structure.

## Method

The author performed AMD calculations with spin-parity projection after variation (VAP) for the ground states of $^9$Li and $^{11}$Li, then constructed basis wave functions for final states in $^9$Be and $^{11}$Be by combining VAP wave functions for low-lying states with additional basis states generated by applying one-body spin-isospin operators ($\sigma_\alpha \tau^-$) to the neutron wave functions of the parent Li nuclei. The final $B(GT)$ values were obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and norm matrices in the combined basis, using three different parameter sets (A, B, C) for the effective nuclear interaction (MV1 central force plus G3RS-type spin-orbit force).

## Key numbers

- For $^9$Li $\rightarrow$ $^9$Be: calculated $B(GT)=0.52$ for the $5/2^-_3$ state at $E_x=13.1$ MeV, compared to the experimental value $B(GT)=8.9$ for the $5/2^-$ state at $E_x=11.81$ MeV (which exhausts 65% of the Ikeda sum rule value $3(N-Z)\times (g_A/g_V)^2 = 13.59$)
- For $^9$Li $\rightarrow$ $^9$Be: calculated $B(GT)=0.94$ for the $3/2^-_3$ state at $E_x=12.1$ MeV, compared to the experimental value $B(GT)=1.44$ for the $E_x=11.28$ MeV state
- For $^9$Li $\rightarrow$ $^9$Be: calculated $B(GT)$ values to low-lying states are small, e.g., $B(GT)=0.065$ for the $3/2^-_1$ ground state, $B(GT)=0.057$ for $5/2^-_1$ at $E_x=2.0$ MeV, and $B(GT)=0.038$ for $1/2^-_1$ at $E_x=5.3$ MeV
- For $^9$Li $\rightarrow$ $^9$Be: total sum of $B(GT)$ values with interaction set (A) is 15.7, distributed as 6.9 to $1/2^-$ states, 5.2 to $3/2^-$ states, and 3.6 to $5/2^-$ states
- For $^{11}$Li $\rightarrow$ $^{11}$Be: total sum of $B(GT)$ values with interaction set (A) is 24.7, distributed as 10.0 to $1/2^-$ states, 8.5 to $3/2^-$ states, and 6.2 to $5/2^-$ states (Ikeda sum rule value = 22.65)
- For $^{11}$Li $\rightarrow$ $^{11}$Be: experimental $B(GT)=1.9$ for a state at 18 MeV in $^{11}$Be, while $B(GT)$ values for low-lying states ($E_x \le 10$ MeV) are less than 0.5
- The Gaussian width parameter used: $\nu=0.20$ fm$^{-2}$ for $^9$Be and $^9$Li, $\nu=0.18$ fm$^{-2}$ for $^{11}$Be and $^{11}$Li

## Context

This work extends AMD+VAP calculations, previously applied to $^{12}$C and $^{10}$Be by the same author (En'yo, 2000, 2001), to Gamow-Teller transitions in neutron-rich Li isotopes. The results contradict the experimental report by Prezado (2003) of an extraordinarily large $B(GT)=8.9$ for the $^9$Be $5/2^-$ state at 11.81 MeV, which also shows large mirror asymmetry compared to the $^9$C $\beta^+$ decay to $^9$B ($B(GT)=1.812$ at 12.19 MeV from Bergmann, 2001). The calculations instead support shell-model results by Millener (2005) that also fail to reproduce such a large value. The suppression of GT transitions to low-lying states is explained by the $2\alpha+n$ cluster structure of Be isotopes, as previously studied by cluster models (Zhan, 1976; Okabe, 1977; Arai, 1996). The method enables future studies of GT transitions to highly excited non-cluster states, though continuum coupling remains to be incorporated for widths above thresholds.
