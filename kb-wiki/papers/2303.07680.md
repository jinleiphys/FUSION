# Investigate the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization splitting effect with combined mechanisms

> The significant splitting of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization measured in STAR's Au+Au 7.7GeV collisions seems to be huge and unable to be described satisfactorily by any single mechanism, thus we revisit and combine there different mechanisms together on the basis of our PICR hydrodynamic model, to explain the experimental data. The three mechanisms, i.e. the meson field mechanism, the freeze-out space-time mechanism, and the QGP's magnetic field mechanism, lie on different stage of high energy collisions, and thus are not contradicted with each other. We find that the meson field mechanism is dominat, while the QGP's magnetic field mechanism is rather trivial, and freeze-out time effect is restricted by the small FZ time difference, leading to a hierarchy of $ΔP_J \gg ΔP_t \gg ΔP_m$. Besides, the combination of different mechanisms could promote the mean value of polarization splitting from about 3\%-4\% to 4.5\%, which is more close to the experimental measured mean value of 5.8\%.

## Key claim

The combined effect of three mechanisms (meson field, freeze-out space-time, and QGP magnetic field) can raise the predicted mean polarization splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda from about 3%-4% to 4.5%, which is closer to but still below the experimentally measured mean value of 5.8% in STAR's Au+Au 7.7 GeV collisions.

## Method

The authors used their (3+1)D particle-in-cell relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamic model to simulate Au+Au collisions at energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV with impact parameter ratios b0 = 0.45 and 0.7. They separately calculated the polarization splitting contributions from three mechanisms: the meson field mechanism (driven by rotational baryon current), the freeze-out space-time mechanism (assuming anti-Lambda freezes out earlier than Lambda by 0.169 or 0.677 fm/c), and the QGP magnetic field mechanism (from charged rotating QGP). The three contributions were then summed linearly, neglecting correlation effects.

## Key numbers

* At 7.7 GeV, the meson field mechanism alone gives a polarization splitting of about 2.9% (b0 = 0.45) and 4.6% (b0 = 0.7), with a simple average of about 3.7%.
* The QGP magnetic field mechanism yields a polarization splitting of only 0.038% at 7.7 GeV, which is "trivial" and one order of magnitude smaller than results in prior work by GLW (2019).
* The freeze-out time mechanism gives polarization splitting of about 0.15% for a time difference of 0.169 fm/c and about 0.6% for a time difference of 0.677 fm/c at 7.7 GeV.
* The combined polarization splitting at 7.7 GeV reaches up to 5.5% at most, with a mean value of about 4.5%, compared to the STAR experimental mean value of 5.8%.
* The hierarchy of contributions is Delta P_J (meson field, ~3%) >> Delta P_t (freeze-out time, ~0.6%) >> Delta P_m (QGP magnetic field, ~0.04%) at 7.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PICR hydrodynamic study of the meson field mechanism (Xie et al., 2021) by adding two additional mechanisms proposed in the literature: the freeze-out space-time mechanism from Vitiuk et al. (2020) and the QGP magnetic field mechanism from Guo et al. (2019). The paper addresses the inability of any single mechanism to explain the large polarization splitting observed by STAR at 7.7 GeV, and demonstrates that combining mechanisms on a uniform model improves agreement with experiment. The results enable future work to incorporate additional mechanisms or consider correlation effects between the freeze-out time difference and the other two mechanisms.
