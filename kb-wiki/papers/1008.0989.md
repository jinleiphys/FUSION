# Faddeev-type equations for three-body symmetry violating scattering amplitudes

> The equations which relate three-body and two-body symmetry violating scattering amplitudes are derived in the first order of symmetry violating interactions. They can be used to obtain three-body symmetry violating scattering amplitudes from two-body symmetry violating scattering amplitudes calculated in low energy effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives a set of decoupled Faddeev-type equations that relate three-body symmetry violating scattering amplitudes directly to two-body symmetry violating amplitudes, but only to first order in the symmetry violating interactions, neglecting second-order terms.

## Method

The authors start from the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of Faddeev equations for three-body transition operators and separate both the two-particle transition operators and the three-particle transition operators into symmetry-conserving (strong) and symmetry-violating (weak) parts. By substituting these expansions into the AGS equations and keeping only terms linear in the weak interaction, they obtain two decoupled equations: one for the strong transition operator identical to the standard AGS equations, and one for the weak transition operator that contains a free term built from two-body weak amplitudes plus an integral term with the same kernel as the strong interaction.

## Key numbers

* No explicit numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal derivation of equations.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory (EFT) approach for parity violating (PV) and time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) effects from two-body systems to three-body systems. The traditional DDH method (Desplanques, Donoghue, Holstein 1980) for calculating PV effects has been found inadequate for describing precise experimental data, motivating the EFT approach developed by Zhu et al. (2004) and Ramsey-Musolf et al. (2006). The derived equations enable a systematic extension of two-body EFT calculations to three-body systems, which is necessary because the number of experimentally measurable two-body PV effects is insufficient to constrain all low energy constants (LECs). Prior work by Schiavilla et al. (2008) calculated PV neutron spin rotation in neutron-deuteron scattering using DWBA with Faddeev wave functions, but that approach did not relate three-body and two-body symmetry violating amplitudes.
