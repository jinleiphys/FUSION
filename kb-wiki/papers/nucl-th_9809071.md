# A momentum subtraction scheme for two--nucleon effective field theory

> We introduce a momentum subtraction scheme which obeys the power counting of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW), developed for systems with large scattering lengths, $a$. Unlike the power divergence subtraction scheme, coupling constants in this scheme obey the KSW scaling for all $μ_R > 1/a$. We comment on the low-energy theorems derived by Cohen and Hansen. We conclude that there is no obstruction to using perturbative pions for momenta $p>m_π$.

# A momentum subtraction scheme for two-nucleon effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors introduce an off-shell momentum subtraction (OS) scheme for two-nucleon effective field theory that makes the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power counting manifest for all renormalization scales μ_R > 1/a, unlike the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme where the power counting breaks down for μ_R ≳ 300 MeV. They conclude that there is no obstruction to using perturbative pions for momenta p > m_π, contrary to the earlier KSW restriction to momenta below 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors define renormalized couplings C_0(μ_R), C_2(μ_R), and D_2(μ_R) by evaluating the four-nucleon amplitude at the unphysical momentum p = iμ_R with m_π = 0, imposing the renormalization condition order by order in the loop expansion. They compute the running couplings analytically in the OS scheme and compare the resulting amplitudes to those obtained in the PDS scheme, fitting to Nijmegen phase shift data between 7 and 100 MeV for both the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 channels.

## Key numbers

- Λ_NN = 8π f^2/(M g_A^2) = 300 MeV, the scale where PDS power counting breaks down
- For the OS scheme at μ_R = m_π = 137 MeV in the ^1S_0 channel: C_0(m_π) = -3.54 fm^2, C_2(m_π) = 3.00 fm^4, D_2(m_π) = 0.377 fm^4
- For the OS scheme at μ_R = m_π = 137 MeV in the ^3S_1 channel: C_0(m_π) = -5.81 fm^2, C_2(m_π) = 10.16 fm^4, D_2(m_π) = -4.128 fm^4
- Fitted scattering lengths: a(^1S_0) = -23.3 fm, a(^3S_1) = 5.41 fm
- In the ^3S_1 channel, the deuteron binding energy gives a pole at -ip = 45.7 MeV, while 1/a = 36.3 MeV
- For the ^3S_1 channel at p = 21.67 MeV: δ = 147.747 ± 0.010 degrees; at p = 48.45 MeV: δ = 118.178 ± 0.021 degrees
- Effective range parameters for ^3S_1: a = 5.420 ± 0.001 fm, r_0 = 1.753 ± 0.002 fm
- Extracted v_2 from lowest momentum point: v_2 = -0.50 ± 0.52 fm^3; from higher momentum point: v_2 = 0.03 ± 0.04 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the KSW power counting approach (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) for two-nucleon systems with large scattering lengths by introducing a scheme where the power counting remains manifest at all scales above 1/a. It addresses the strong μ_R dependence observed in next-to-leading order PDS calculations by Gegelia (1998) and by Steele and Furnstahl (1998), showing that this dependence is an artifact of the PDS scheme rather than a fundamental limitation. The authors comment on the low-energy theorems derived by Cohen and Hansen (1998) for the effective range expansion coefficients v_i, finding that the uncertainties in extracting v_2 from data are too large to make a definitive test of those theorems. The work enables the use of perturbative pions for momenta above m_π, extending the range of the effective field theory beyond the 300 MeV limit previously thought.
