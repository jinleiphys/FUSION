# Systematic Nuclear Uncertainties in the Hypertriton System

> The hypertriton bound state is relevant for inference of knowledge about the hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction. In this work we compute the binding energy of the hypertriton using the ab initio hypernuclear no-core shell model (NCSM) with realistic interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. In particular, we employ a large family of nucleon-nucleon interactions with the aim to quantify the theoretical precision of predicted hypernuclear observables arising from nuclear-physics uncertainties. The three-body calculations are performed in a relative Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator basis and we implement infrared correction formulas to extrapolate the NCSM results to infinite model space. We find that the spread of the predicted hypertriton binding energy, attributed to the nuclear-interaction model uncertainty, is about 100 keV. In conclusion, the sensitivity of the hypertriton binding energy to nuclear-physics uncertainties is of the same order of magnitude as experimental uncertainties such that this bound-state observable can be used in the calibration procedure to constrain the YN interactions.

## Key claim

The spread of the predicted hypertriton binding energy attributed to nuclear-interaction model uncertainty is about 100 keV, which is of the same order of magnitude as experimental uncertainties, meaning this bound-state observable can be used in calibration procedures to constrain hyperon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio hypernuclear no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the hypertriton in a relative Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator basis, using a family of 42 different chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) while keeping the leading-order Bonn-Julich chiral effective field theory hyperon-nucleon interaction fixed. They applied infrared correction formulas to extrapolate NCSM results to infinite model space and studied convergence properties across model-space truncations up to Nmax=66 and harmonic oscillator frequencies from 7 to 40 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The spread of predicted hypertriton binding energy attributed to nuclear-interaction model uncertainty is about 100 keV
- For one specific interaction (Lambda_NN=500 MeV, Tmax=290 MeV), the variational minimum energy was E(hypertrition) = -2.385 MeV at omega=9 MeV and Nmax=70
- The experimental binding energy for the hypertriton is E_exp = -2.35 +/- 0.05 MeV
- The infrared extrapolation for one interaction gave E_infinity = -2.385 MeV, with only a few keV difference from the variational minimum at Nmax=66
- Calculations used 42 different interactions with regulator cutoffs Lambda_NN in {450, 475, ..., 575, 600} MeV and six truncations of NN scattering calibration data with Tmax in [125, 290] MeV
- A UV cutoff of Lambda_UV=1200 MeV was found sufficient for convergence

## Context

This work extends the uncertainty quantification framework developed by Carlsson et al. (2015) for chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions to hypernuclear systems, specifically the hypertriton. The finding that the 100 keV spread is small compared to the wide uncertainty bands observed for He-4 and O-16 with the same family of interactions (Carlsson et al., 2015) is attributed to the fact that the hypertriton involves only two-body NN interactions while three-nucleon forces induce much larger uncertainties in heavier systems. This enables future use of the hypertriton binding energy as a constraint for hyperon-nucleon interaction models, and opens the door to quantifying uncertainties of other few- and many-body hypernuclear bound-state observables.
