# The Role of QCD Monopoles in Jet Quenching

> QCD monopoles are magnetically charged quasiparticles whose Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) at $T<T_c$ creates electric confinement and flux tubes. The "magnetic scenario" of QCD proposes that scattering on the non-condensed component of the monopole ensemble at $T>T_c$ is responsible for the unusual kinetic properties of QGP. In this paper, we study the contribution of the monopoles to jet quenching phenomenon, using the BDMPS framework and hydrodynamic backgrounds. In the lowest order for cross sections, we calculate the nuclear modification factor, $R_\text{AA},$ and azimuthal anisotropy, $v_2$, of jets, as well as the dijet asymmetry, $A_j$, and compare those to the available data. We find relatively good agreement with experiment when using realistic hydrodynamic backgrounds. In addition, we find that event-by-event fluctuations are not necessary to reproduce $R_\text{AA}$ and $v_2$ data, but play a role in $A_j$. Since the monopole-induced effects are maximal at $T\approx T_c$, we predict that their role should be significantly larger, relative to quarks and gluons, at lower RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that scattering of high-energy partons on uncondensed QCD monopoles, whose density peaks near the critical temperature \(T_c\), can simultaneously explain the observed nuclear modification factor \(R_\text{AA}\) and azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) of jets in heavy-ion collisions, provided that realistic (2+1)D hydrodynamic backgrounds are used. The authors find that event-by-event fluctuations are not necessary to reproduce \(R_\text{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data, contrary to some earlier claims, but do play a role in dijet asymmetry.

## Method

The authors calculate jet quenching using the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) framework, where the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) is derived from lowest-order cross sections for scattering on quarks, gluons, and magnetic monopoles. Monopole cross sections are taken as point-like with no factors of \(\alpha_s\) due to the Dirac condition, and use a magnetic screening mass \(\mu_M/T = 4.48\) from lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2015). The energy loss is integrated along parton trajectories through fireball backgrounds generated by (2+1)D hydrodynamics with either Glauber or IP-Glasma initial conditions, using temperature-dependent monopole densities from two lattice-based sources.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic screening mass: \(\mu_M/T = 4.48\); electric screening mass: \(\mu_E/T = 7.31\) (from Borsanyi 2015 lattice simulations with dynamical quarks).
* Monopole correlations reduce the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) by approximately 15% across the temperature range from \(T_c\) to \(4T_c\).
* Without monopoles, the azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) is roughly 0.015, much smaller than experimental data.
* The nuclear modification factor \(R_\text{AA}\) for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions is better fit by the monopole density measured directly on the lattice (peaking at \(T_c\)) than by the density derived from the equation of state (peaking at about \(1.5T_c\)).

## Context

This work extends the "magnetic scenario" of QCD proposed by Liao and Shuryak (2006, 2007, 2008), which attributes unusual kinetic properties of the quark-gluon plasma to scattering on uncondensed magnetic monopoles. It follows pioneering studies by Xu, Liao, and Gyulassy (2014, 2015) but differs by calculating all scattering effects from first principles rather than fitting model parameters. The results enable predictions for jet quenching at lower RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, where monopole-induced effects are expected to be larger relative to quark and gluon scattering.
