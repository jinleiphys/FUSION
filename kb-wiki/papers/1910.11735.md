# Third order viscous hydrodynamics from the entropy four current

> Non-equilibrium dynamics for relativistic fluid or quark gluon plasma (QGP) have already been calculated earlier upto third order using both kinetic and thermodynamic approaches. Calculations presented in this manuscript are based on thermodynamics principles. The expressions for third order dissipative fluxes have been derived from equation for entropy 4-current developed earlier by A. Muronga. The relaxation equations in the present work have been developed in a simple Bjorken (1+1)D scenario and Eckart frame. The relaxation equations are found to have slightly different values for the coupling coefficients as compared to calculations from earlier models. The solutions to the differential equations have been found to be sensitive to values of these coefficients. The shear relaxation equations derived in third order theory are discussed term by term. Effects of third order theory on shear relaxation time has been discussed. Thermodynamic quantities related to hot and dense matter have been calculated as functions of proper time. Moreover, various initial conditions for the relaxation equations have been assumed to study their effects on above mentioned observables. A LHC QGP formation time of $τ_0$ = 0.4 fm/c and temperature of T$_0$ = 500 MeV have been assumed.

# Third order viscous hydrodynamics from the entropy four current

## Key claim
The authors derive third-order dissipative flux equations from thermodynamic entropy principles rather than kinetic theory, and find that the coupling coefficients in the relaxation equations differ slightly from earlier models by El et al. and Jaiswal. The solutions to the differential equations are sensitive to these coefficient values, and the third-order corrections reduce the dissipative effects compared to second-order theory, particularly at early times.

## Method
The authors use Grad's 14-moment approximation to expand the entropy four-current up to cubic order in dissipative fluxes, then apply the entropy principle (∂_μ S^μ ≥ 0) to derive relaxation equations for shear stress. The equations are simplified to a (1+1)D Bjorken scaling scenario in the Eckart frame, assuming massless particles, zero heat flux, and ultra-relativistic equation of state ε = 3P. The resulting shear pressure differential equation is solved numerically for various initial conditions and η/s values.

## Key numbers
- Initial QGP formation time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c and initial temperature T₀ = 500 MeV, corresponding to LHC conditions.
- Shear relaxation time defined as τ_π = 2η S²₃, with the coefficient S²₃ ≈ 9/(4ε) in the ultra-relativistic limit.
- Pressure isotropy ratio P_L/P_T is computed for η/s values of 0.2 and 0.4, showing that higher η/s produces larger differences between second and third order results.
- For initial condition π₀ = P₀, the first-order result merges with higher-order curves around τ = 12 fm/c, while second and third order curves merge beyond 6 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic approach of Muronga (2010) from second-order to third-order dissipative hydrodynamics, complementing earlier third-order calculations by El et al. (2009) using thermodynamic methods and Jaiswal (2013, 2014) using kinetic Boltzmann equation approaches. The results are compared with BAMPS transport theory calculations and with Maxwell-Cattaneo and Muller-Israel-Stewart (M-IS) second-order theories. The work enables systematic study of how higher-order gradient corrections affect the evolution of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the convergence of hydrodynamic gradient expansions and the role of non-linear terms neglected in M-IS theory.
