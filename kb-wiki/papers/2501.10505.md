# Unnuclear matter at large-charge

> The utility of the non-relativistic large-charge EFT for physical systems, and neutron matter in particular, relies on controlled Schrödinger-symmetry breaking deformations due to scattering length and effective-range effects in the two-body system. A recently-found exact solution of the large-charge system is used to compute these effects for two-point correlation functions of large-charge operators in perturbation theory around the large-charge ground state. Notably, the leading effective-range effects are found to enter at second order in the effective range, in agreement with analogous calculations in the three-body system. The Schrödinger-symmetry breaking deformations are used -- together with input from Quantum Monte Carlo simulations -- to address the range of validity of the EFT with deformations both in general and in the special case of neutron matter. In particular, it is found that nuclear reactions with up to six low-energy neutrons in the final state can be described by the large-charge EFT with Schrödinger-symmetry breaking.

## Key claim
The large-charge effective field theory (EFT) with Schrödinger-symmetry breaking can describe nuclear reactions with up to six low-energy neutrons in the final state, but this utility depends critically on the detailed numerical behavior of the perturbative expansion around the unitary fixed point, where the leading effective-range corrections vanish and the first non-zero contributions enter at second order in the effective range.

## Method
The authors use a recently discovered exact master-field solution of the large-charge system to compute two-point correlation functions of large-charge operators in perturbation theory around the large-charge ground state. They apply a coordinate transformation to an oscillator frame where the operator insertions are shifted to infinite Euclidean time, making the equations of motion tractable, then compute Schrödinger-symmetry breaking deformations due to scattering length and effective-range effects order by order. The coefficients of the symmetry-breaking operators in the superfluid EFT are determined by matching to Quantum Monte Carlo simulations of the near-unitary Fermi gas.

## Context
This work extends the large-charge EFT approach developed by Beane (2024) for scattering-length corrections to include effective-range effects, and is consistent with the three-body results of Chowdhury (2023) where leading effective-range contributions vanish in conformal perturbation theory. The deformed large-charge EFT provides a tool for computing correlation functions of low-energy neutrons in final states of nuclear reactions, relevant to recent experiments at the Radioactive Ion Beam Factory at RIKEN using the SAMU spectrometer, which has studied reactions with four neutrons in the final state (Duer, 2022) and is exploring systems with six or more neutrons (Nakamura).
