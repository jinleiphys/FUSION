# Production of mini-(gluon)jets and strangeness enhancement in pA and AA collisions at relativistic energies

> The idea that effective string tension increases as a result of the hard gluon kinks on a string is applied to study the strange particle production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. It is found that the effective string tension increases with the increase of centrality and mass of the colliding system as a consequence of the mini-(gluon)jet production stemming from the collective string-string interaction. This mechanism leads to strangeness enhancement in pA and AA collisions through the enhanced production of the strange quark pairs from the color field of strings. We discuss different roles played by this mechanism and rescattering of the final state hadrons in the production of strange particles and compare our results with experimental data.

## Key claim
The effective string tension increases with centrality and mass of the colliding system in pA and AA collisions due to mini-(gluon)jet production from collective string-string interaction (the Firecracker model), which in turn enhances strangeness production; this mechanism provides a hadronic explanation for strangeness enhancement without requiring a quark-gluon plasma phase transition.

## Method
The authors used the LUCIAE event generator, which extends the FRITIOF string model to nucleus-nucleus collisions by including a Firecracker model for collective string-string interactions and a rescattering model for final-state hadron interactions. They related the effective string tension to the production of hard gluon kinks on strings via a parametrization (Eq. 1), then used this to calculate the strangeness suppression factor λ and other JETSET parameters for different collision systems and centralities. The results were compared with NA35 data for p+S, S+S, S+Ag, and S+Pb collisions at 200A GeV/c and Pb+Pb at 158A GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The strangeness suppression factor λ increases from 0.22 for p+S to 0.28 for S+S at 200A GeV/c, approaching saturation for heavier systems.
- For pp collisions at 200 GeV/c, the retuned parameters are PARJ(1)=0.046, PARJ(2)=0.2, PARJ(3)=0.3, and PARJ(21)=0.32 GeV/c (default values were 0.1, 0.3, 0.4, and 0.37 GeV/c respectively).
- For Pb+Pb at 158A GeV/c, λ values at three centralities (impact parameters 0.0-3.3 fm, 3.3-6.8 fm, and 6.8-10.0 fm) are 0.277, 0.287, and 0.290 respectively.
- The ratio of anti-Lambda to negative particle multiplicity in central Pb+Pb at 158A GeV/c is 0.017 at λ=0.290, but increases to 0.058 at λ=0.6 (a factor of 3.4 enhancement).
- Rescattering contributes only about 5% to anti-Lambda production in central S+S at 200A GeV/c when strangeness production cross sections are doubled.
- The parameter α in the effective string tension formula is about 3.5 and √s₀ is about 0.8 GeV, determined from hadron-hadron data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies using the LUCIAE model (Sa Ben-Hao and Tai An, 1995-1997) which showed that rescattering alone could not reproduce NA35 data on strangeness enhancement, requiring a reduction of strange quark suppression. The paper builds on the Firecracker model (Andersson et al., 1993) and the energy-dependent strangeness suppression scheme developed for hadron-hadron collisions (Kapat, 1997). It provides a hadronic alternative to the color rope mechanism used in RQMD (Sorge et al., 1992) and contrasts with the quark-gluon plasma interpretation of strangeness enhancement (Rafelski, 1982). The results enable future searches for QGP signals by establishing a baseline from which deviations would indicate new physics.
