# Low-Energy QCD in the Delta Regime

> We investigate properties of low-energy QCD in a finite spatial volume, but with arbitrary temperature. In the limit of small temperature and small cube size compared to the pion Compton wavelength, Leutwyler has shown that the effective theory describing low-energy QCD reduces to that of quantum mechanics on the coset manifold, which is the so-called delta regime of chiral perturbation theory. We solve this quantum mechanics analytically for the case of a $U(1)_L \times U(1)_R$ subgroup of chiral symmetry, and numerically for the case of $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$. We utilize the quantum mechanical spectrum to compute the mass gap and chiral condensate, and investigate symmetry restoration in a finite spatial volume as a function of temperature. Because we obtain the spectrum for non-zero values of the quark mass, we are able to interpolate between the rigid rotor limit, which emerges at vanishing quark mass, and the harmonic approximation, which is referred to as the p-regime. We find that the applicability of perturbation theory about the rotor limit largely requires lighter-than-physical quarks. As a stringent check of our results, we raise the temperature to that of the inverse cube size. When this condition is met, the quantum mechanics reduces to a matrix model. The condensate we obtain in this limit agrees with that determined analytically in the epsilon regime.

## Key claim

The authors solve the quantum mechanical Hamiltonian describing low-energy QCD in the delta regime for non-zero quark mass, enabling interpolation between the rigid rotor limit (vanishing quark mass) and the harmonic approximation (p-regime), but find that perturbation theory about the rotor limit largely requires lighter-than-physical quarks.

## Method

The authors derive the effective Hamiltonian for the spatial zero mode of the chiral field in the delta regime of chiral perturbation theory, where the pion Compton wavelength exceeds the spatial box size. They solve this quantum mechanics analytically for a U(1)_L × U(1)_R subgroup using Mathieu functions, and numerically for the SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R case by solving the reduced radial differential equation for the eigenfunctions. The spectrum is then used to compute the mass gap and chiral condensate as functions of quark mass and temperature.

## Key numbers

- The rigid rotor perturbation theory for the mass gap remains under control up to μ² ≲ 1/2, which for minimal volume L = 2.2 fm corresponds to M_π ≲ 65 MeV
- The chiral condensate expansion about the rigid rotor limit is under control up to μ² ≲ 1/4, corresponding to M_π ≲ 45 MeV on volume L = 2.2 fm
- The maximum temperature plotted for the U(1) case is τ = 2, corresponding to T = 90 MeV for minimal box size L = 2.2 fm
- For matching delta-regime and epsilon-regime results, the authors set F L = 2, corresponding to temperature T = 45 MeV
- The simple harmonic oscillator approximation for the mass gap works down to μ² ∼ 1

## Context

This work extends the original delta regime formulation by Leutwyler (1987) beyond the chiral limit, where previous studies by Hasenfratz (2009) and Niedermayer (2010) had determined corrections to the rotor spectrum using the next-to-leading order chiral Lagrangian. The results enable lattice QCD computations to access the delta regime with physical or near-physical quark masses, and the matching to the epsilon regime provides a stringent consistency check. The authors note that a lattice QCD computation by Bietenholz (2010) had previously focused on the mass gap in the delta regime but required extrapolation from the p-regime.
