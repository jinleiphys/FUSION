# Mean Field Quarks on the Light Front

> We present a new approach for the calculation of the valence quark distributions in the nucleon based on the scenario in which the spectrum of the valence quarks at x>0.05 is generated through three main mechanisms: interaction of valence quarks with the mean field generated by the residual nucleon system, two and three quark short range interactions through gluon exchanges. In the current report we present the first phase of the project in which we develop a non-perturbative model for valence quark interaction in the mean field of the nucleonic interior to describe their distribution in the moderate x region (0.05 < x < 0.4). The short range quark-quark interaction effects in our approach generate the high x tail of valence quark distributions. The presented non-perturbative model is based on the picture in which three relativistic valence quarks occupy the nucleon core at distances of $\le 0.5$ Fm while interacting in the mean field generated by the residual nucleon system. The calculations are based on the assumption of the a factorization of the internal interaction of short-range three valence quarks with the long-range interaction of these quarks with the residual system. The theoretical approach is based on effective light-front diagrammatic approach which allows us to introduce the valence quark and residual system wave functions in a consistent way The parameters of these wave functions are fixed by the position of the peak of the xf_q(x) distribution of valence quarks at Q_0 corresponding to the charm-quark mass. With few parameters we achieved a very reasonable description of the up and down valence quark distributions in the moderate x region (x < 0.4), where one expects the mean field dynamics to dominate. The model, however, systematically underestimates the high $x$ region where enhanced contributions from partonic short-range correlations are expected.

## Key claim

The authors present a non-perturbative model for valence quark distributions in the nucleon, based on the assumption that the spectrum at moderate x (0.05 < x < 0.4) is dominated by mean-field dynamics of three relativistic valence quarks, while short-range two- and three-quark correlations generate the high-x tail; the model systematically underestimates the high-x region where partonic short-range correlations are expected to contribute.

## Method

The authors derive valence quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) by calculating the nucleon structure function in deep inelastic scattering using an effective light-front diagrammatic approach, introducing light-front wave functions for the nucleon transition into a valence quark and a residual system, and for the three-valence-quark system. The three-quark system is modeled as relativistic coupled three harmonic oscillators, while the residual system is described by a Gaussian wave function; parameters are fixed by fitting the peak of the x-weighted down-valence quark distribution at Q² = M_c² ≈ 1.7 GeV², and the up-valence quark distribution is obtained assuming SU(6) symmetry (u_V = 2 d_V).

## Key numbers

- The model fits the x d_V(x) distribution at Q² = M_c² ≈ 1.7 GeV², with the peak of x d_V(x) occurring near x ≈ 0.2.
- For the down valence quark, the mean-field contribution yields ∫ d_V(x) dx = 0.83 (compared to the actual value of 1) and ∫ x d_V(x) dx = 0.126 (compared to 0.132).
- For the up valence quark, the mean-field contribution yields ∫ u_V(x) dx = 1.65 (compared to 2) and ∫ x u_V(x) dx = 0.252 (compared to 0.323).
- The mean field captures 83% of the normalization for both d and u quarks, leaving 13% as the minimal contribution from quark correlations; the mean field captures 95.4% of the momentum carried by d quarks and 78% of that carried by u quarks.

## Context

This work extends the multi-nucleon correlation model of nuclear momentum distributions (Frankfurt 2008) to the quark level, proposing that valence quark distributions arise from mean-field, two-quark, and three-quark short-range correlations analogous to nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclei. The approach is similar to the helicity selection rule observed in nuclear short-range correlations (Sargsian 2012), where tensor interactions cause proton-neutron dominance; here, vector gluon exchanges produce a helicity selection rule that breaks SU(6) symmetry at high x. The model enables predictions for processes dominated by mean-field versus correlated quark interactions, and suggests that the residual system's properties could be extracted from tagged semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering experiments.
