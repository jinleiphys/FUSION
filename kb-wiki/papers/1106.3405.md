# The Tsallis Distribution and Transverse Momentum Distributions in High-Energy Physics

> The Tsallis distribution has been used recently to fit the transverse momentum distributions of identified particles by the STAR collaboration \cite{Abelev:2006cs} at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and by the ALICE \cite{Aamodt:2011zj} and CMS \cite{Khachatryan:2011tm} collaborations at the Large Hadron Collider. Theoretical issues are clarified concerning the thermodynamic consistency of the Tsallis distribution in the particular case of relativistic high energy quantum distributions. An improved form is proposed for describing the transverse momentum distribution and fits are presented together with estimates of the parameter $q$ and the temperature $T$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a specific form of the Tsallis distribution (called Tsallis-B) that is thermodynamically consistent, in contrast to some other versions used in the literature, and shows that it can fit transverse momentum distributions from the CMS collaboration with a parameter q around 1.11, but the authors caution that whether this distribution provides a valid interpretation of high-energy collision data will need further theoretical work.

## Method

The authors derive the Tsallis distribution for quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein) by maximizing the Tsallis entropy under constraints on particle number and energy, where the constraints use an unusual power of q on the occupation numbers to achieve thermodynamic consistency. They then prove that the resulting distributions satisfy the first law of thermodynamics by verifying the Maxwell relations for pressure, energy density, and entropy density. Finally, they fit the Boltzmann approximation (Tsallis-B) to CMS data on strange particle transverse momentum distributions at 0.9 TeV and 7 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For K0_S at 0.9 TeV: T = 92 MeV, q = 1.13
- For K0_S at 7 TeV: T = 105 MeV, q = 1.15
- For Lambda at 0.9 TeV: T = 70 MeV, q = 1.11
- For Lambda at 7 TeV: T = 117 MeV, q = 1.12
- For Xi- at 0.9 TeV: T = 44 MeV, q = 1.11
- For Xi- at 7 TeV: T = 126 MeV, q = 1.11
- The parameter q is stated to deviate from 1 by at most 20%, with typical values near 1.1

## Context

This work extends the use of the Tsallis distribution in high-energy physics, which had been applied by the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Abelev 2006) and by the ALICE (Aamodt 2011) and CMS (Khachatryan 2011) collaborations at the LHC. The paper clarifies that some earlier formulations (e.g., Pereira 2007, Pereira 2009, Conroy 2010) were not thermodynamically consistent, and provides a corrected form that satisfies the first law of thermodynamics. The results enable more reliable extraction of the temperature T and non-extensivity parameter q from experimental transverse momentum spectra, though the authors note that the physical interpretation of the Tsallis distribution in heavy-ion collisions remains an open question.
