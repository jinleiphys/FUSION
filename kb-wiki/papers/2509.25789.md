# Shape polarization and coexistence of high-$K$ three-quasiparticle states in odd-mass $N=106$ isotones

> Three-quasiparticle $K$-isomeric states in odd-mass $N=106$ isotones within the $A\sim 180$ mass region are systematically investigated using configuration-constrained potential energy surface calculations. The calculations successfully reproduce the excitation energies and deformations of known high-$K$ isomers in the nuclei from $^{175}$Tm to $^{181}$Re. For the nuclei closer to the $Z=82$ shell closure ($^{183}$Ir, $^{185}$Au, and $^{187}$Tl), predictions for the configurations of observed and yet-to-be-observed isomers are provided. The results reveal strong shape polarization, where the three-quasiparticle states are driven to larger deformations compared to the often shape-soft or spherical ground states. A particularly rich spectrum of shape coexistence is predicted in $^{187}$Tl, where several high-$K$ three-quasiparticle configurations with distinct prolate, oblate, and triaxial shapes are found to coexist at similar excitation energies. Notably, the oblate-deformed $K^π=29/2^+$ configuration at $E_x = 1839$ keV is proposed to be responsible for a long-lived isomer. This study provides a comprehensive picture of shape evolution and coexistence in high-$K$ multi-quasiparticle states, offering valuable insights for future experimental research.

## Key claim

The paper claims that three-quasiparticle high-$K$ states in odd-mass $N=106$ isotones exhibit strong shape polarization, driving them to larger deformations compared to the often shape-soft or spherical ground states, with a particularly rich spectrum of shape coexistence predicted in $^{187}$Tl. The hedge is that these are predictions from configuration-constrained potential energy surface calculations, and the results for nuclei closer to $Z=82$ ($^{183}$Ir, $^{185}$Au, $^{187}$Tl) are proposed as candidate configurations for observed and yet-to-be-observed isomers, requiring further experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors employed configuration-constrained potential energy surface (PES) calculations based on the macroscopic-microscopic model, using a non-axial deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the universal parameter set and the Lipkin-Nogami version of the BCS method for pairing. A deformation mesh in ($\beta_2$, $\gamma$) was used with hexadecapole deformation $\beta_4$ variation at each mesh point, and single-particle orbitals were continuously tracked and adiabatically blocked throughout the deformation plane to identify specific quasiparticle configurations. The total energy of each multi-quasiparticle state was decomposed into deformation energy and configuration energy, with excitation energies computed as the energy difference between the PES minimum of the excited configuration and that of the ground-state configuration.

## Key numbers

- For $^{175}$Tm: the $K^\pi=17/2^-$ 3-qp state is calculated at 1176 keV (experimental 1004.8 keV) with $\beta_2=0.281$, $\gamma=0.06^\circ$; the $K^\pi=23/2^+$ state is calculated at 1343 keV (experimental 1517.7 keV) with $\beta_2=0.279$, $\gamma=0.06^\circ$.
- For $^{181}$Re: the $K^\pi=21/2^-$ 3-qp state is calculated at 1657 keV (experimental 1656.4 keV) with $\beta_2=0.230$, $\gamma=-0.36^\circ$; the $K^\pi=25/2^+$ state is calculated at 1864 keV (experimental 1880.6 keV) with $\beta_2=0.229$, $\gamma=-0.48^\circ$.
- For $^{185}$Au: the $K^\pi=17/2^+$ 3-qp state is predicted at 1967 keV with $\beta_2=0.230$, $\gamma=1.04^\circ$; the $K^\pi=19/2^+$ state is predicted at 1981 keV with $\beta_2=0.246$, $\gamma=1.14^\circ$.
- For $^{187}$Tl: the oblate-deformed $K^\pi=29/2^+$ configuration at $E_x=1839$ keV with $\beta_2=0.192$, $\gamma=-59.46^\circ$ is proposed to be responsible for a long-lived isomer; the $K^\pi=27/2^+$ configuration is calculated at 2312 keV with $\beta_2=0.225$, $\gamma=-12.13^\circ$.
- The ground state of $^{187}$Tl is nearly spherical with $\beta_2=0.024$, $\gamma=-2.27^\circ$, while the $K^\pi=27/2^+$ 3-qp state has $\beta_2\approx0.23$, showing the greatest difference in quadrupole deformation between the 3-qp state and the ground state.

## Context

This work extends the systematic configuration-constrained PES study of two-quasineutron $K^\pi=8^-$ isomers in even-even $N=106$ isotones by Xu et al. (1999) to the odd-proton neighbors, where the unpaired nucleon serves as a sensitive probe of shell structure. The calculations reproduce known 3-qp isomers in $^{175}$Tm, $^{177}$Lu, $^{179}$Ta, and $^{181}$Re studied experimentally by Hughes et al. (2012), Dracoulis et al. (2004), Barneoud et al. (1982), and Pearson et al. (2000), and provide predictions for $^{183}$Ir, $^{185}$Au, and $^{187}$Tl. The predicted shape coexistence in $^{187}$Tl, where multiple high-$K$ three-quasiparticle configurations with distinct prolate, oblate, and triaxial shapes coexist at similar excitation energies, offers guidance for future experimental searches for shape-isomeric high-$K$ states in this mass region.
