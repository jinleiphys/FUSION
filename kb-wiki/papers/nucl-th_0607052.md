# Solution of the Bohr hamiltonian for soft triaxial nuclei

> The Bohr-Mottelson model is solved for a generic soft triaxial nucleus, separating the Bohr hamiltonian exactly and using a number of different model-potentials: a displaced harmonic oscillator in $γ$, which is solved with an approximated algebraic technique, and Coulomb/Kratzer, harmonic/Davidson and infinite square well potentials in $β$, which are solved exactly. In each case we derive analytic expressions for the eigenenergies which are then used to calculate energy spectra. Here we study the chain of osmium isotopes and we compare our results with experimental information and previous calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exactly separable solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for soft triaxial nuclei using a potential of the form \(V(\beta,\gamma)=V_1(\beta)+V_2(\gamma)/\beta^2\), but the \(\gamma\)-angular part is solved only approximately by neglecting fluctuations of the moments of inertia (or treating them as free parameters) and by approximating \(\sin(3\gamma)\approx\sin(3\gamma_0)\).

## Method

The Bohr Hamiltonian is separated into a \(\beta\)-dependent equation and a \((\gamma,\theta_i)\)-dependent equation using the potential form \(V_1(\beta)+V_2(\gamma)/\beta^2\). The \(\gamma\)-angular part is solved by approximating the rotational kinetic coefficients \(A_i=1/[4\sin^2(\gamma_0-2\pi i/3)]\) (irrotational case) or treating them as free parameters, and by replacing \(\sin(3\gamma)\) with \(\sin(3\gamma_0)\); the resulting harmonic oscillator in \(\gamma\) is solved algebraically using the su(1,1) spectrum-generating algebra. The \(\beta\)-part is solved exactly for three potentials: Coulomb/Kratzer, harmonic/Davidson, and infinite square well, yielding analytic energy expressions. The model is applied to the chain of osmium isotopes using a random-walk fitting procedure.

## Key numbers

- For the Kratzer potential, the energy spectrum is given by \(\epsilon(n_\gamma,n_\beta,L,K^*)=A^2/4 / (\sqrt{9/4+B+\omega_{L,K^*,n_\gamma}}+1/2+n_\beta)^2\) (Eq. 23).
- For the Davidson potential, the spectrum is \(\epsilon_D(n_\gamma,n_\beta,L,K^*)=\sqrt{A_D}(2n_\beta+\widetilde\tau_{L,K^*,n_\gamma}+5/2)\) (Eq. 24).
- For the infinite square well, the spectrum is \(\epsilon_{SQ}(n_\beta,n_\gamma,L,K^*)=(x_{s,\omega}/\beta_w)^2\) where \(x_{s,\omega}\) is the s-th zero of the Bessel function \(J_{\sqrt{\omega+9/4}}\) (Eq. 27).
- For \(^{188}\)Os, the irrotational fit with the Davidson potential yields parameters \(B_D=62.301\), \(C=82.66\), \(A_3=2.3917\); the absolute error for the \(10^+\) state is \(\simeq 100\) keV or less.
- For \(^{192}\)Os, the irrotational fit yields parameters \(B_D=132.182\), \(C=44.6504\), \(A_3=1.39954\).
- The total error for the irrotational fit across the osmium chain is in the range \(0.01-0.09\).
- The relation \(\omega_{200}+\omega_{220}=\omega_{000}+\omega_{320}\) generalizes the well-known relation \(E(2^+)+E({2'}^+)=E(3^+)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier solution for a soft triaxial rotor at \(\gamma=\pi/6\) (Fortunato, 2005) to the full interval \(0^\circ<\gamma_0<60^\circ\), and generalizes the Meyer-ter-Vehn formula (1975) for rigid triaxial rotors to include \(\gamma\)-softness. The model incorporates the observation by Wood (2004) that irrotational-flow moments of inertia are not always correct, by allowing the three components of the moment of inertia to be free parameters. The results enable comparison with microscopic HFB calculations (Ansari, 2003) and provide analytic benchmarks for transitional nuclei between \(\gamma\)-rigid and \(\gamma\)-soft shapes, particularly in the osmium isotopic chain.
