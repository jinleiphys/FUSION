# P- and T-violating $πN N$ form factor

> The form factor of the parity and time-reversal violating (PTV) pion-nucleon interaction is calculated from one-loop vertex diagrams. The degrees of freedom included in the effective lagrangian are nucleons, pions, $η$, $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. We show that by studying the form factor one can constrain the PTV meson-nucleon coupling constants. We evaluate the mean square radius associated with the PTV $πN N$ vertex. Using the mean square radius, we estimate the effect of the PTV $πN N$ vertex on the neutron electric dipole moment, and find a very small correction. We also extract the renormalisation group $β$ function and use it to discuss evolution of the PTV $πN N$ coupling constant beyond the hadronic mass scale.

# P- and T-violating πNN form factor

## Key claim

The parity- and time-reversal-violating (PTV) pion-nucleon form factor is calculated from one-loop vertex diagrams and can, in principle, constrain the unknown PTV meson-nucleon coupling constants, but the experimental feasibility of such a measurement remains an open question. The authors find that the effect of the PTV πNN form factor on the neutron electric dipole moment introduces a very small correction (approximately -2%) to the leading chiral contribution, though this estimate is acknowledged as crude and based on simplifying assumptions.

## Method

The authors calculate the PTV πNN form factor from one-loop vertex diagrams using an effective Lagrangian that includes nucleons, pions, η, ρ, and ω mesons, with strong interaction coupling constants taken from the literature and PTV coupling constants left as free parameters. Loop integrals are evaluated in a relativistic field-theoretical approach using dimensional regularization with modified minimal subtraction to obtain ultraviolet- and infrared-convergent renormalized loop contributions. The form factor is defined as the ratio of the full renormalized vertex to the renormalized coupling constant, normalized to unity at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M = 0.938 GeV, pion mass m = 0.138 GeV, ρ mass m_ρ = 0.77 GeV, ω mass m_ω = 0.78 GeV, η mass m_η = 0.55 GeV
- Strong coupling constants: g_π = 13.07, g_η = 2.24, g_ρ = 2.75, κ_ρ = 3.7, g_ω = 8.25, κ_ω = -0.12, g_{ρπ} = 6.07
- Pion-loop contribution to the mean square radius: ⟨r²_{PT}⟩_π ≈ (0.19 - 0.00062 c_π²) fm², with leading term 5g_π²/(16π²M²) ≈ 0.22 fm²
- Total mean square radius (neglecting quadratic terms): ⟨r²_{PT}⟩ ≈ (0.19 ± 0.015 c_ρ/c_π ∓ 0.032 c_ω/c_π - 0.026 c_η/c_π) fm²
- Estimated correction to neutron EDM from form factor: δ^{FF}_{PT} ≈ -2%
- β function for pseudovector strong πNN vertex: β_π^{(pv)} = (3g_π²/16π²) c_π - (1/8π²) c_π³, with fixed point c_π* = √(3/2) g_π ≈ 16
- β function for pseudoscalar strong πNN vertex: β_π^{(ps)} = -(g_π²/8π²) c_π - (1/8π²) c_π³
- Using the pseudovector scenario with c_π(M) ≤ 7×10⁻¹², the fixed point would be approached at energies above μ ∼ 5 TeV

## Context

This work extends effective Lagrangian calculations of P- and T-violating interactions, particularly building on the model of Liu (2004) which evaluated the neutron EDM in terms of PTV meson-nucleon coupling constants. The authors connect to the broader literature on electric dipole moments, including the leading chiral logarithm calculation of Crewther (1979), the SU(3) chiral perturbation theory of Pich and de Rafael (1991), the MIT quark bag model of Baluni (1979), and QCD sum rule predictions of Pospelov and Ritz (2000). The results enable future detailed calculations of the neutron EDM that include the full Q²-dependence of the PTV πNN form factor, and provide a framework for constraining PTV coupling constants through possible future measurements of the form factor.
