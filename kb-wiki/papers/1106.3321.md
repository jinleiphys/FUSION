# New Way to Produce Dense Double-Antikaonic Dibaryon System, \bar{K}\bar{K} NN, through Lambda(1405)-Doorway Sticking in p+p Collisions

> A recent successful observation of a dense and deeply bound \bar{K} nuclear system, K^-pp, in the p + p \rightarrow K^+ + K^-pp reaction in a DISTO experiment indicates that the double-\bar{K} dibaryon, K^-K^-pp, which was predicted to be a dense nuclear system, can also be formed in p+p collisions. We find theoretically that the K^- -K^- repulsion plays no significant role in reducing the density and binding energy of K^-K^-pp and that, when two Λ(1405) resonances are produced simultaneously in a short-range p+p collision, they act as doorways to copious formation of K^-K^-pp, if and only if K^-K^-pp is a dense object, as predicted.

## Key claim

The authors theoretically find that the dense double-antikaonic dibaryon system K⁻K⁻pp can be formed copiously in p+p collisions via a "doorway" mechanism involving two simultaneously produced Λ(1405) resonances, but only if K⁻K⁻pp is a dense, deeply bound object as predicted; the K⁻-K⁻ repulsion plays no significant role in reducing the density and binding energy.

## Method

The authors extend the previously established reaction mechanism for single-K⁻ production (p + p → K⁺ + K⁻pp) to the double-K⁻ case (p + p → K⁺ + K⁺ + K⁻K⁻pp). They formulate the formation amplitude using a Λ*-Λ* model Hamiltonian with a complex potential, incorporate a momentum window function derived from a short-range interaction mediated by a boson of mass m_B (taken as m_ρ), and calculate differential cross sections via a spectral function approach for various bound-state energies and range parameters of the Λ*-Λ* potential.

## Key numbers

- The originally predicted binding energy of K⁻K⁻pp is B_KK = 117 MeV, compared to B_K = 48 MeV for K⁻pp.
- The mean p-p distance is 1.3 fm for K⁻K⁻pp, versus 1.9 fm for K⁻pp.
- The standard bound-state energy assumed for K⁻K⁻pp is E = -150 - i 75 MeV, produced by a Λ*-Λ* potential with V_0 = -543 MeV and W_0 = -181 MeV for b = 0.3 fm.
- The bare K⁻-K⁻ repulsive potential has v_0 = 313 MeV and b_KK = 0.47 fm; after finite-size correction (folding with a = 1.11 fm), the effective repulsion reduces the binding energy by only ~13 MeV.
- The free Λ* production cross section at 2.83 GeV is σ_Λ* ~ 4.5 μb, about 0.10 × σ_Λ (~50 μb).
- The estimated free double-Λ* production cross section is σ_Λ*+Λ* ~ 0.5 nb.
- The BS/QF (bound-state to quasi-free) ratio for K⁻K⁻pp production at T_p = 7.0 GeV is 0.43, 1.09, and 1.21 for E = -100, -150, and -200 MeV, respectively (b = 0.3 fm, θ₁₂ = 180°).
- The BS/QF ratio for varying range parameters (b = 0.2, 0.25, 0.3, 0.4 fm) is 2.51, 1.60, 1.09, and 0.36, corresponding to Λ*-Λ* rms distances of 0.74, 0.84, 0.94, and 1.13 fm, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the successful observation of K⁻pp in the DISTO experiment (Yamazaki 2010) to the double-Λ* case, building on the authors' prior predictions of deeply bound kaonic nuclear states (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, Yamazaki 2004, Yamazaki 2007a, 2007b). The proposed reaction mechanism provides a direct production route for K⁻K⁻pp, which serves as a precursor to kaon condensation (Nelson, Brown, Brown-Bethe) and opens the door to "Swan Nuclear Physics", the study of dense nuclear systems driven by the super-strong nuclear force from migrating K⁻ mesons. The authors suggest that such experiments could be performed at J-PARC in Japan and the FAIR project in Darmstadt.
