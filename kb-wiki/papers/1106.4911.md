# The self-consistent general relativistic solution for a system of degenerate neutrons, protons and electrons in beta-equilibrium

> We present the self-consistent treatment of the simplest, nontrivial, self-gravitating system of degenerate neutrons, protons and electrons in $β$-equilibrium within relativistic quantum statistics and the Einstein-Maxwell equations. The impossibility of imposing the condition of local charge neutrality on such systems is proved, consequently overcoming the traditional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff treatment. We emphasize the crucial role of imposing the constancy of the generalized Fermi energies. A new approach based on the coupled system of the general relativistic Thomas-Fermi-Einstein-Maxwell equations is presented and solved. We obtain an explicit solution fulfilling global and not local charge neutrality by solving a sophisticated eigenvalue problem of the general relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation. The value of the Coulomb potential at the center of the configuration is $eV(0)\simeq m_πc^2$ and the system is intrinsically stable against Coulomb repulsion in the proton component. This approach is necessary, but not sufficient, when strong interactions are introduced.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the traditional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff treatment assuming local charge neutrality is inconsistent with the Einstein-Maxwell equations and microphysical equilibrium conditions for a system of degenerate neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta-equilibrium; instead, a self-consistent solution exists only with global (not local) charge neutrality, obtained by solving a general relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation coupled to the Einstein-Maxwell system.

## Method

The authors formulated and solved the coupled system of the general relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation, the Einstein-Maxwell equations, and the hydrostatic equilibrium equation for a spherically symmetric, non-rotating configuration of degenerate neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta-equilibrium. They imposed the constancy of the generalized Fermi energies for each particle species as the equilibrium condition, replacing the local charge neutrality assumption, and solved the resulting eigenvalue problem with boundary conditions at the radii where each particle species' Fermi momentum vanishes. The numerical integration was performed for a central density of 3.94 times nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- Central density: \(\rho(0) = 3.94 \rho_{\rm nuc}\), where \(\rho_{\rm nuc} \simeq 2.7 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)
- Neutron radius: \(R_n \simeq 12.735\) km
- Proton radius: \(R_p \simeq 12.863\) km
- Electron radius: \(R_e \simeq R_p + 10^3 \lambda_e\), where \(\lambda_e = \hbar/(m_e c)\) is the electron Compton wavelength
- Coulomb potential at the center: \(eV(0) \simeq 0.85 m_\pi c^2\) for the exemplified configuration with \(\eta = 3.94\)
- Lower bound on central Coulomb potential for central densities above nuclear density: \(eV(0) \gtrsim 0.35 m_\pi c^2\)
- Gravitational potential at center: \(1 - e^{\nu(0)/2} \simeq m_\pi/m_p\)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Thomas-Fermi treatment of compressed atoms by Rotondo et al. (2011) to include general relativistic gravity, and generalizes the equilibrium conditions of Klein (1949) and of Kodama and Yamada (1972) to degenerate multicomponent fluids with charged species. It contradicts the standard Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff approach that assumes local charge neutrality, and provides the necessary foundation for including strong interactions in neutron star cores, as planned in subsequent work by Pugliese et al. (2011). The solution enables the study of overcritical electric fields in neutron stars and their role in gravitational collapse to black holes.
