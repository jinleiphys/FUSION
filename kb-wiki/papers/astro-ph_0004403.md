# Cosmic Phase Transitions

> The sequence of phase transitions during the hot history of the universe is followed within a phenomenological framework. Particular emphasis is put on the QCD confinement transition, which is at reach under earth laboratory conditions. A tepid inflationary scenario on the GUT scale with bubble grow at moderate supercooling is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the sequence of phase transitions during the hot history of the universe can be followed within a phenomenological framework, but it explicitly restricts itself to describing transitions only in terms of the equation of state, without going into a microscopic theory of phase transitions, and limits consideration to the standard model and GUT models of the strong and electroweak interactions.

## Method

The author uses the Friedmann equations for the Big Bang and the corresponding boost-invariant hydrodynamic equations for the Little Bang (relativistic heavy-ion collisions), both derived from the Einstein equations and the perfect-fluid energy-momentum tensor. The QCD confinement transition is modeled using a MIT-type bag model equation of state for the quark-gluon plasma and a pion gas equation of state for hadronic matter, with the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium applied at the crossing point. Nucleation theory is employed to handle the phase transformation dynamics, and a tepid inflationary scenario on the GUT scale is discussed using bubble growth at moderate supercooling.

## Key numbers

- The QCD confinement transition temperature is estimated as \(T_c \sim 160\) MeV \(\sim 1.6 \times 10^{12}\) K for a quark-gluon plasma, while for a pure gluon gas it would be \(T_c^{\rm glue} \sim 240\) MeV.
- The present critical energy density of the universe is \(h^2 1.88 \times 10^{-29}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) with \(h \approx 0.68\).
- The present vacuum energy density is \(e^{\rm vac} \approx 5 \times 10^{-30}\) g cm\(^{-3} \approx 0.6 \, e^{\rm crit} \approx 10^{-123} M_{\rm Pl}^4\), where \(M_{\rm Pl} = 1.22 \times 10^{19}\) GeV.
- The dynamical time scale for the Little Bang is \(\tau \sim 1\) fm/c, while for the Big Bang it is \(\sim 10^{19}\) fm/c at a temperature scale of \(T \sim 0.2\) GeV.
- Nuclear matter near ground state has density \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3} = 1.5 \times 10^{38}\) cm\(^{-3}\) and energy density \(e_0 = 2.7 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- The maximum temperatures in heavy-ion collisions are estimated as \({\cal O}(200)\) MeV for CERN-SPS, \({\cal O}(550)\) MeV for BNL-RHIC, and \({\cal O}(1000)\) MeV for CERN-LHC.
- The present microwave background radiation temperature is \(T = 2.7277\) K with relative fluctuations less than \(10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach to cosmic phase transitions outlined by Boyanowski, focusing specifically on the QCD confinement transition and its comparison with heavy-ion collision dynamics. The paper builds on lattice QCD results from the Bielefeld group and quasi-particle models from the author's own work (Kampfer, 2000), and discusses the phase diagram in the context of work by Karsch on the order of the transition and by Ragagopal on di-quark condensates and color-flavor locking. The analysis enables a schematic comparison between the confinement dynamics of the Big Bang and the Little Bang, and provides a framework for incorporating nucleation theory into cosmic expansion scenarios, including a tepid GUT-scale inflationary model.
