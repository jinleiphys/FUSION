# Nuclei in a chiral SU(3) model

> Nuclei can be described satisfactorily in a nonlinear chiral SU(3)-framework, even with standard potentials of the linear $σ$-model. The condensate value of the strange scalar meson is found to be important for the properties of nuclei even without adding hyperons. By neglecting terms which couple the strange to the nonstrange condensate one can reduce the model to a Walecka model structure embedded in SU(3). We discuss inherent problems with chiral SU(3) models regarding hyperon optical potentials.

## Key claim

Nuclei can be described within a nonlinear chiral SU(3) framework using standard potentials of the linear sigma model, but only if the strange scalar condensate is allowed to couple to nucleons; the model still cannot simultaneously reproduce the experimentally extracted Lambda central potential of 28 MeV and the nucleon central potential of approximately -70 MeV without introducing additional explicit symmetry breaking terms.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian in the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry, including scalar, vector, and baryon octets plus a dilaton field for broken scale invariance. They solve the equations of motion in the mean-field approximation for spherically symmetric finite nuclei with zero net strangeness, fitting parameters to reproduce vacuum hadron masses, nuclear matter equilibrium at saturation density, and properties of finite nuclei. Different parameter sets are explored, including cases where the strange and nonstrange condensates are decoupled (recovering SU(2) chiral models) and cases where they are coupled via a linear sigma model potential.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Nucleon mass fitted to m_N = 939 MeV; Lambda mass to m_Lambda = 1115 MeV; Sigma mass to m_Sigma = 1196 MeV; Xi mass to m_Xi = 1331.5 MeV
- At 4 times normal nuclear density, the nonstrange condensate sigma drops to 30 percent of its vacuum expectation value
- The strange condensate zeta is reduced by only about 10 percent from its vacuum expectation value at finite density
- Pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV; kaon mass m_K = 498 MeV
- Lambda central potential experimentally extracted as U_Lambda = 28 +/- 1 MeV
- Nucleon central potential approximately U_N = -70 MeV
- Xi central potential ranges from 10 to 30 MeV depending on the model and explicit symmetry breaking term
- Vacuum expectation values constrained by sigma_0 = -f_pi and zeta_0 = -(1/sqrt(2))(2 f_K - f_pi)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous linear SU(3) chiral model (Papazoglou et al., 1998) which successfully described hadronic masses and nuclear matter but produced unrealistically deep hyperon central potentials. The nonlinear realization solves three specific problems identified in the linear approach: the restriction to d-type scalar coupling versus f-type vector coupling, the direct coupling of sigma to the strange condensate providing uncontrolled attraction, and the inability to add explicit symmetry breaking without destroying PCAC relations. The model recovers results of the SU(2) chiral model of the Minnesota group (Heide et al., 1994) and the nonlinear Boguta-Bodmer model (Boguta and Bodmer, 1977) as special cases, and enables future studies of strange hadronic matter, kaon in-medium properties, and spin-3/2 resonances within a single chiral framework.
