# Gluon mini-jet production in nuclear collisions at high energies

> We show explicitly that the leading soft gluon $p_T$ distribution, predicted by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert after solving classical Yang-Mills equations, can be understood in terms of conventional QCD perturbation theory. We also demonstrate that the key logarithm in their result represents the logarithm in DGLAP evolution equations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the leading soft gluon transverse momentum distribution derived by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) from solving classical Yang-Mills equations can be reproduced by conventional QCD perturbation theory through the partonic subprocess $qq\rightarrow qqg$, but only under the specific approximations of the collinear and soft gluon limits, and with the effective number of valence quarks $N_q$ set to one.

## Method

The author calculates the leading contribution to gluon production from the partonic subprocess $qq\rightarrow qqg$ in the soft gluon limit using conventional perturbative QCD, factorizing the cross section into a quark-to-gluon splitting function and a hard scattering part. The calculation is performed in the center-of-mass frame using $n\cdot A=0$ gauge for the splitting function and $\bar{n}\cdot A=0$ gauge for the hard scattering, with a collinear cutoff $\Lambda_{cutoff}$ introduced to regulate divergences when the intermediate gluon momentum $k_1^2$ approaches zero.

## Key numbers

- The final cross section obtained is $\frac{d\sigma_{qq\rightarrow g}}{dy d^2 p_T} = \frac{2g^6}{(2\pi)^4} \left(\frac{1}{2N_c}\right)^2 N_c (N_c^2-1) \frac{1}{p_T^4} \ell n \left(\frac{p_T^2}{\Lambda^2_{cutoff}}\right)$, for the process $qq\rightarrow qqg$ with $N_q=1$
- The splitting function yields $P_{l_1 \rightarrow k_1}(x,k_{1T}<p_T) = \frac{N_c^2-1}{2N_c} \left( \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2} \frac{1+(1-x)^2}{x} \right) \ell n \left(\frac{p_T^2}{\Lambda^2_{cutoff}}\right)$
- The KMW result (Eq. e1) gives $\frac{dN}{dyd^2 p_T}=S_T \frac{2g^6 \mu^4}{(2\pi)^4} N_c(N_c^2-1) \frac{1}{p_T^4} \ell n \left(\frac{p_T^2}{\Lambda^2_{cutoff}}\right)$, which differs from the perturbative result by a factor of $N_q^2$

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (1994) formalism for soft gluon distributions in large nuclei and the Kovner-McLerran-Weigert (1995) calculation of gluon production in nuclear collisions, showing that their classical Yang-Mills result can be understood within conventional QCD factorization. The paper demonstrates that the key logarithm in the KMW result represents the DGLAP evolution logarithm from quark-to-gluon splitting, and shows that the KMW result can be reduced to the factorized perturbative QCD formula by replacing the classical color charge density $\mu^2$ with valence quark distributions and absorbing the logarithm into the gluon distribution. This enables connecting the classical field approach to standard parton model calculations for mini-jet production at RHIC, while noting that higher-order iterations in the KMW approach may capture multi-parton dynamics beyond conventional perturbation theory.
