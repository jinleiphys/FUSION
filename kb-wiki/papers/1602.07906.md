# Unexpectedly large charge radii of neutron-rich calcium isotopes

> Despite being a complex many-body system, the atomic nucleus exhibits simple structures for certain "magic" numbers of protons and neutrons. The calcium chain in particular is both unique and puzzling: evidence of doubly-magic features are known in 40,48Ca, and recently suggested in two radioactive isotopes, 52,54Ca. Although many properties of experimentally known Ca isotopes have been successfully described by nuclear theory, it is still a challenge to predict their charge radii evolution. Here we present the first measurements of the charge radii of 49,51,52Ca, obtained from laser spectroscopy experiments at ISOLDE, CERN. The experimental results are complemented by state-of-the-art theoretical calculations. The large and unexpected increase of the size of the neutron-rich calcium isotopes beyond N = 28 challenges the doubly-magic nature of 52Ca and opens new intriguing questions on the evolution of nuclear sizes away from stability, which are of importance for our understanding of neutron-rich atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The first measurements of charge radii for neutron-rich calcium isotopes ⁴⁹,⁵¹,⁵²Ca reveal a large and unexpected increase beyond neutron number N=28, challenging the proposed doubly-magic nature of ⁵²Ca and contradicting theoretical predictions from ab initio calculations, density functional theory, and configuration interaction models.

## Method

High-resolution bunched-beam collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE, CERN, using the 393-nm 4s ²S₁/₂ → 4p ²P₃/₂ ionic transition in Ca⁺ to measure hyperfine structure spectra. Isotope shifts relative to ⁴⁰Ca were extracted from Voigt profile fits and converted to changes in mean-square charge radii using a King-plot analysis with electronic field factor F = -276(8) MHz/fm² and specific mass shift K_SMS = -8.8(5) GHz·u. The measurements were complemented by ab initio coupled-cluster calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions (NNLO_sat, SRG1, SRG2) and compared to density functional theory and configuration interaction predictions.

## Key numbers

- Change in mean-square charge radius for ⁴⁹Ca relative to ⁴⁸Ca: δ⟨r²⟩⁴⁸,⁴⁹ = 0.097(4) fm²
- Change in mean-square charge radius for ⁵²Ca relative to ⁴⁸Ca: δ⟨r²⟩⁴⁸,⁵² = 0.530(5) fm²
- Measured isotope shift for ⁵²Ca relative to ⁴⁰Ca: δν⁴⁰,⁵² = 2219.2(14)[56] MHz
- Measured isotope shift for ⁴⁹Ca relative to ⁴⁰Ca: δν⁴⁰,⁴⁹ = 1854.7(10)[43] MHz
- Measured isotope shift for ⁵¹Ca relative to ⁴⁰Ca: δν⁴⁰,⁵¹ = 2102.6(9)[51] MHz
- Reference charge radius of ⁴⁰Ca: R_ch = 3.478(2) fm
- Production yield for ⁵²Ca: only a few hundred ions per second, representing one of the highest sensitivities ever reached with fluorescence detection techniques

## Context

This work extends the charge radius measurements of the calcium isotopic chain beyond ⁴⁸Ca, where previous measurements existed only for ⁵⁰Ca (Vermeeren, 1996) and a lower limit was suggested for ⁴⁹Ca (Andl, 1982). The results challenge the doubly-magic nature of ⁵²Ca, which had been suggested based on precision mass measurements (Wienholtz, 2013), and contradict predictions from ab initio coupled-cluster calculations, density functional theory with the UNEDF0 functional (Kortelainen, 2010), and configuration interaction calculations with the ZBM2 interaction (Caurier, 2001; Bissell, 2014). The findings open questions about the evolution of nuclear sizes away from stability and motivate future measurements of ⁵⁴Ca to further probe shell structure at the neutron-rich frontier.
