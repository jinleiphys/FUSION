# Structure Effects on Coulomb Dissociation of $^8$B

> Coulomb Dissociation provides an alternative method for determining the radiative capture cross sections at astrophysically relevant low relative energies. For the breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni, we calculate the total Coulomb Dissociation cross section and the angular distribution for E1, E2 and M1. Our calculations are performed first within the standard first order semiclassical theory of Coulomb Excitation, including the correct three body kinematics, and later including the projectile-target nuclear interactions.

# Structure Effects on Coulomb Dissociation of $^8$B

## Key claim
The Coulomb Dissociation cross section for $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni depends significantly on the structure model assumed for the projectile, and the measured low-energy total cross section cannot be reproduced by any of the potential models tested, suggesting either that the $^7$Be-p cluster component is very small or that higher-order reaction mechanisms (E1-E2 interference or Coulomb-nuclear interference) play a more important role than initially thought.

## Method
The authors performed first-order semiclassical Coulomb excitation calculations with proper three-body kinematics for the breakup of $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni, computing total and differential cross sections for E1, E2, and M1 multipoles. They then improved the reaction theory by performing prior-DWBA breakup calculations that discretise the continuum and include projectile-target nuclear interactions, using partial waves up to 3 MeV relative energy and Becchetti-Greenlees proton potentials with a $^7$Li potential for $^7$Be.

## Key numbers
- The total Coulomb Dissociation cross section for $^8$B on $^{58}$Ni at $E_{beam}=3.225$ MeV/A, integrated over $\theta=39^{\circ}-51^{\circ}$, ranges from $2.07 \times 10^{-2}$ (model min) to $4.41 \times 10^{-2}$ (model a2) in units of $\sigma/\sigma_{ruth}$, while the experimental value is $(0.68 - 1.09)\times 10^{-2}$.
- The E1 component alone ranges from $0.75 \times 10^{-2}$ (model min) to $1.27 \times 10^{-2}$ (model a2) in $\sigma/\sigma_{ruth}$, exceeding the entire experimental cross section for most models.
- The ratio $\sigma(E2)/\sigma(E1)$ varies from 1.74 (model min) to 2.48 (model a2) across the structure models.
- The S-factor at 50 keV ranges from 12.7 eV b (model min) to 24.7 eV b (model a2).
- The $1^+$ resonance in $^8$B is at $E=0.63$ MeV and the $3^+$ resonance at $E=2.18$ MeV.
- The elastic Coulomb plus nuclear cross section drops to 90% of the Rutherford cross section at $70^{\circ}$.

## Context
This work extends earlier semiclassical treatments of Coulomb Dissociation (Alder, 1956) and prior calculations by Shyam (1996) that included proper three-body kinematics, by systematically comparing a range of $^8$B structure models including Woods-Saxon potentials (esb, kim), deformed-core models (a1, a2), and models with inelastic channels to $^7$Be excited states (c1, c2). The calculations address the discrepancy between theoretical predictions and the low-energy Notre Dame experiment (1996) and show that nuclear effects and Coulomb-nuclear interference become significant beyond $25^{\circ}$, producing an interference minimum between $30^{\circ}$ and $50^{\circ}$ that may explain the low measured cross sections. The work enables future experiments at different beam energies, angular ranges, or with heavier targets such as $^{197}$Au to better constrain the E1/E2 decomposition and extract the astrophysical S-factor $S_{17}$.
