# Dense QCD: a Holographic Dyonic Salt

> Dense QCD at zero temperature with a large number of colors is a crystal. We show that in the holographic dual description, the crystal is made out of pairs of dyons with $e=g=\pm 1$ charges in a salt-like arrangement. We argue that with increasing density the dyon masses and topological charges equalize, turning the salt-like configuration to a bcc of half-instantons. The latter is dual to a cubic crystal of half-skyrmions. We estimate the transition from an fcc crystal of instantons to a bcc crystal of dyons to about 3 times nuclear matter density with a dyon binding energy of about 180 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that dense QCD at large N_c forms a crystal of dyons (rather than instantons) in the holographic dual description, with the transition from an fcc instanton crystal to a bcc dyon crystal occurring at approximately 3 times nuclear matter density. The authors hedge that this is a "plausible" geometrical argument rather than a rigorous computational result, and that the detailed dyon interactions are "not important" in the non-BPS regime.

## Method

The authors consider a 3-dimensional array of flavor instantons on T^3 × R in the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model, initially arranged in an fcc configuration. They apply T-duality along one spatial direction to show that a nontrivial spatial holonomy splits each instanton into a pair of BPS dyons with opposite electric and magnetic charges e = g = ±1. The dyons arrange in a salt-like configuration to screen Coulomb forces, and at next-to-leading order (non-BPS regime) the topological repulsion forces the Higgs vev to v = 1/2, equalizing dyon masses and topological charges to produce a bcc crystal of half-instantons.

## Key numbers

- Transition density from fcc instanton crystal to bcc dyon crystal: n_B ≈ 3 n_NM (approximately 3 times nuclear matter density), corresponding to n_B ≈ 1/2 fm^{-3}
- Dyon binding energy in the bcc configuration: Δ ≈ 180 MeV
- Nearest neighbor distance in the bcc configuration: L ≈ 1 fm (compared to L = 1.08 fm from numerical skyrmion calculations by Park and Vento)
- Instanton BPS mass set to nucleon mass: M ≈ 1 GeV
- Empirical moment of inertia parameter: 1/I ≈ 200 MeV
- Instanton size from rotational inertia: ρ ≈ √(2/5) fm
- Madelung constant for salt: M_D = 1.748

## Context

This work extends the holographic description of dense QCD from single baryons as flavor instantons (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) and preliminary Wigner-Seitz crystal studies (Wigner, 1934) to a full crystalline description using instanton splitting into dyons. The half-skyrmion cubic crystal phase at high density was previously explored numerically by Park and Vento and by the crystal collaboration, and the authors note their density and binding energy estimates are "surprisingly close" to those numerical results. The paper also draws an analogy to the half-skyrmion phase in the Neel magnet-VBS paramagnet transition in condensed matter (Senthil, 2004). The dyonic salt configuration may be relevant to the finite-temperature confinement-deconfinement transition studied by Diakonov and collaborators, where instantons ionize to BPS dyons at high temperature.
