# $О›\barО›$ final-state interaction in the reactions $e^+e^-\to П•О›\bar О›$ and $e^+e^- \to О·О›\bar О›$

> Near-threshold $О›\barО›$ mass spectra for the reactions $e^+e^- \to О·О›\barО›$ and $e^+e^- \to П•О›\barО›$ are investigated with an emphasis on the role played by the interaction in the $О›\barО›$ system. A variety of $О›\barО›$ potential models is employed that have been established in the analysis of data on $p\bar p\to О›\barО›$ in the past. It is shown that the near-threshold enhancement observed for the two $e^+e^-$ reactions can be reproduced by considering the $О›\barО›$ final-state interaction in the partial waves suggested by the helicity-angle analysis of the experiments. For $e^+e^- \to О·О›\barО›$ the same $О›\barО›$ $S$-wave interaction as in $e^+e^- \to О›\barО›$ is relevant and with it a consistent description of the pertinent measurements can be achieved. It is pointed out that a nonzero threshold cross section as observed for the latter reaction is not supported by the new $О·О›\barО›$ data.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ invariant-mass spectra for both $e^+e^- \to \eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ and $e^+e^- \to \phi\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ can be reproduced by the final-state interaction in the $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ system alone, without invoking a narrow near-threshold resonance; the nonzero threshold cross section observed in $e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ by BESIII is not supported by the new $\eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ data.

## Method

The authors employed a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) in which the $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ final-state interaction is treated rigorously by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for a set of four phenomenological $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potentials (models I, II, III, and IV) that were previously established in analyses of $p\bar p \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ data from the LEAR experiment. For $e^+e^- \to \eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ the $^3S_1$ partial wave was used, while for $e^+e^- \to \phi\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ the $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$, and $^3P_2$ partial waves were considered, with a Gaussian cutoff of 500-600 MeV/c applied to the $P$-wave momentum factors to avoid artificial enhancement.

## Key numbers

* The $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ threshold is at $2m_\Lambda = 2231.4$ MeV; the BESIII data point for $e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ at 2232.4 MeV (about 1 MeV above threshold) is not reproduced by the FSI-only calculation.
* The narrow resonance required to describe that BESIII point has $M_x = 2230.9$ MeV and $\Gamma_x = 4.7$ MeV (from Li (2022)).
* The $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ scattering lengths (in fm) for the four potentials in the $^3S_1$ partial wave range from $(0.66 - i 0.37)$ fm (model II) to $(1.00 - i 0.44)$ fm (model III); the spin-averaged ALICE value is $(0.90 \pm 0.16) - i(0.40 \pm 0.18)$ fm.
* The $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potentials describe $p\bar p \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ data up to $p_{\rm lab} \approx 1700$ MeV/c, corresponding to $\sqrt{s} \approx 2.32$ GeV or an excess energy $Q \approx 90$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ (Haidenbauer (2016)) to the reactions $e^+e^- \to \eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ and $e^+e^- \to \phi\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ using the same $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ potentials developed for $p\bar p \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ (Haidenbauer (1992, 1992A, 1992B, 1993)). It contradicts the need for a narrow near-threshold resonance proposed by Cao (2018) and Li (2022) to explain the BESIII $e^+e^- \to \Lambda\bar\Lambda$ threshold data, since the $\eta\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ data show no such feature. The results enable a consistent description of multiple reactions producing $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ pairs and provide predictions for the $\Lambda\bar\Lambda$ correlation function measured by ALICE (2022).
