# $\bar K^*$ meson in dense matter

> We study the properties of $\bar K^*$ mesons in nuclear matter using a unitary approach in coupled channels within the framework of the local hidden gauge formalism and incorporating the $\bar K π$ decay channel in matter. The in-medium $\bar K^* N$ interaction accounts for Pauli blocking effects and incorporates the $\bar K^*$ self-energy in a self-consistent manner. We also obtain the $\bar K^*$ (off-shell) spectral function and analyze its behaviour at finite density and momentum. At normal nuclear matter density, the $\bar K^*$ meson feels a moderately attractive potential while the $\bar K^*$ width becomes five times larger than in free space. We estimate the transparency ratio of the $γA \to K^+ K^{* -} A^\prime$ reaction, which we propose as a feasible scenario at present facilities to detect the changes of the properties of the $\bar K^*$ meson in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The authors find that at normal nuclear matter density, the $\bar K^*$ meson experiences a moderately attractive optical potential, while its width increases dramatically to about five times its free-space value of approximately 50 MeV, a result that depends on the self-consistent treatment of the $\bar K^*$ self-energy and the inclusion of medium modifications to the $\bar K \pi$ decay channel.

## Method

The authors use a unitary coupled-channel approach based on the local hidden gauge formalism to describe the $\bar K^* N$ interaction in free space, then extend it to nuclear matter by incorporating Pauli blocking effects and the $\bar K^*$ self-energy in a self-consistent manner. The in-medium $\bar K^*$ self-energy receives contributions from two sources: the decay mode $\bar K^* \to \bar K \pi$ modified by the nuclear medium effects on the $\bar K$ and $\pi$ mesons, and the $\bar K^* N$ interaction accounting for quasi-elastic and absorption channels. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in nuclear matter with in-medium loop functions that include the $\bar K^*$ spectral function, and the self-energy is determined self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The free $\bar K^*$ width is 50.5 MeV (the experimental value is $50.8 \pm 0.9$ MeV); the model calculation gives 42 MeV for the $K^{*-}$ width from $\bar K \pi$ decay.
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, the $\bar K^*$ width becomes five times larger than in free space.
- The momentum of the emitted mesons in the free $\bar K^*$ decay at rest is $|\vec{k}| = 288.7$ MeV.
- The $\bar K$ optical potential from the chiral unitary model is $U_{K^-} = \text{Re}\,\Pi_{K^-} / (2 m_{K^-}) \sim -66$ MeV.
- The regularization scale for the loop function is $\mu = 630$ MeV, with a subtraction constant of $-2$.

## Context

This work extends the study of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, which has been controversial for the $\rho$ and $\omega$ mesons (e.g., Rapp (1997), Peters (1997), Urban (1999), Cabrera (2000), Post (2003) for the $\rho$; Caillon (1995), Saito (1998), Lutz (2001), Muehlich (2006) for the $\omega$), to the strange vector meson $\bar K^*$, which had not been previously investigated in this context. The formalism builds on the hidden gauge approach for vector meson-baryon interactions developed by Oset (2009) and the self-consistent treatment of antikaons in matter by Ramos (1999). The authors propose the $\gamma A \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction as a feasible experimental scenario to detect the predicted medium modifications, using the transparency ratio method previously applied to the $\omega$ meson by Kaskulov (2006).
