# Proton decays in $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg and isospin-symmetry breaking in carbon isotopes and isotones

> Proton-rich nuclei possess unique properties in the nuclear chart. Due to the presence of both continuum coupling and Coulomb interaction, phenomena such as halos, Thomas-Ehrman shift, and proton emissions can occur. Experimental data are difficult to be obtained therein, so that theoretical calculations are needed to understand nuclei at drip-lines and guide experimentalists for that matter. In particular, the $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg isotopes are supposed to be one-proton and/or two-proton emitting nuclei, but associated experimental data are either incomplete or even unavailable. Consequently, we performed Gamow shell model calculations of carbon isotones bearing $A=15\text{-}18$. Isospin-symmetry breaking occurring in carbon isotones and isotopes is also discussed. It is hereby shown that the mixed effects of continuum coupling and Coulomb interaction at drip-lines generate complex patterns in isospin multiplets. Added to that, it is possible to determine the one-proton and two-proton widths of $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg. Obtained decay patterns are in agreement with those obtained in previous experimental and theoretical works. Moreover, up to the knowledge of authors, this is the first theoretical calculation of binding energy and partial decay widths of $^{18}$Mg in a configuration interaction picture.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations can determine the one-proton and two-proton decay widths of $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg, with the caveat that the two-proton width is assumed to be approximately independent of energy based on the behavior observed when varying the Woods-Saxon central potential depth.

## Method

GSM calculations were performed for carbon isotones with mass numbers A=15-18 using a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonance, and complex-energy scattering states. The Hamiltonian employed a Woods-Saxon core potential fitted to the single-particle spectrum of $^{15}$C, with a residual nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from effective field theory, and the Coulomb interaction was added for valence protons in carbon isotones. To separate one-proton and two-proton decay widths, the central potential depth of the Woods-Saxon potential was varied to shift the one-proton separation energy, allowing identification of the regime where only two-proton decay is open.

## Key numbers

- The calculated width of the $^{16}$Ne ground state is about 10 keV, smaller than the experimental value of 122 keV but close to the three-body model estimate of 0.15-3.1 keV by Grigorenko et al.
- The $2^+$ first excited state of $^{16}$Ne has a calculated width of about 40 keV, close to values measured in experiments and calculated in theoretical analyses.
- The $1/2^+$ ground state width of $^{17}$Na is 845 keV in the GSM model, differing by about a factor of 2 from the 1.6 MeV value in an empirical potential model.
- The two-proton decay width is about 10-15 keV for both $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg.
- The one-proton width of $^{18}$Mg is estimated to be about 85-90 keV.
- The Thomas-Ehrman shift is about 150 keV for the $0^+$ and $2^+$ states of $^{16}$Ne and $^{18}$Mg, and typically 500 keV to 1 MeV for A=15 nuclei and the highest excited states of A=16 nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous GSM studies of proton-rich oxygen isotones (Michel et al., 2019) to carbon isotones, and provides the first theoretical calculation of binding energy and partial decay widths of $^{18}$Mg in a configuration interaction picture. The results support a small particle-emission width for $^{16}$Ne (of order 10 keV) consistent with the three-body model of Grigorenko et al. (2003) and experimental upper bounds of 80 keV, while also demonstrating that isospin-symmetry breaking in mirror nuclei of bound and unbound nature produces complex patterns that cannot be predicted from separation energy and width considerations alone. The method for separating one-proton and two-proton widths enables future experimental guidance for studying these proton-rich nuclei at drip-lines.
