# $ππ$ scattering in the $ρ$-meson channel at finite temperature

> We study $ππ$ scattering in the I=1, $J^P=1^-$ channel at finite temperature in the framework of the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that explicitly includes vector and axial-vector degrees of freedom in addition to the usual scalar and pseudoscalar sector. The S-matrix in the coupled channels $q\bar q$ and $ππ$ is constructed via $ρ$-exchange in the $s$-channel. The self-energy of the $ρ$-meson contains both quark and pion loop contributions. The analytic structure of the S-matrix for $T\geq 0$ is investigated and the motion of the $ρ$-pole as a function of coupling constant and temperature is followed in the complex $\sqrt{s}$-plane. For numerical calculations, parameters are chosen in order that $m_π$, $f_π$ and the experimental $ππ$ phase shifts $δ_1^1$ at zero temperature are reproduced, and then the behavior of the $ρ$-pole as well as the $ππ$ cross section is investigated as a function of the temperature. We find that the position of the $ρ$ mass stays practically constant for $0\leq T\leq 130$ MeV, and then moves down in energy by about 200 MeV for 130 MeV$\leq T\leq 230$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors find that the ρ-meson mass remains practically constant for temperatures up to 130 MeV, then decreases by about 200 MeV between 130 MeV and 230 MeV, but this result depends on defining the ρ-meson mass as a pole in the complex energy plane rather than on the real axis, and on including both quark loop and pion loop contributions to the self-energy within the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Method

The authors constructed the S-matrix for ππ scattering in the I=1, J^P=1^- channel using ρ-exchange in the s-channel within an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that includes vector and axial-vector degrees of freedom. They calculated the ρ-meson self-energy including both quark loop contributions (leading order in 1/N_c) and pion loop contributions (next order in 1/N_c), then investigated the analytic structure of the S-matrix by following the ρ-pole in the complex √s-plane as a function of temperature. Parameters were chosen to reproduce m_π, f_π, and experimental ππ phase shifts δ_1^1 at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The ρ mass stays practically constant for 0 ≤ T ≤ 130 MeV
- The ρ mass moves down in energy by about 200 MeV for 130 MeV ≤ T ≤ 230 MeV
- The model parameters reproduce m_π, f_π, and experimental ππ phase shifts δ_1^1 at zero temperature
- The ππ scattering is studied at center-of-mass energies √s between approximately 400 MeV and 900 MeV, corresponding to thermal pions with kinetic energy E_π ∼ 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of ππ scattering that calculated only scattering lengths at zero temperature without vector mesons (Rui et al., 1994) and with vector mesons (Party et al.), as well as scattering lengths at finite temperature (Quack et al., 1995). It also addresses the open question of whether the ρ mass increases or decreases with temperature, where earlier work using vector dominance models (Gale et al., 1991) found a weak rising temperature dependence, while lattice calculations (Boyd et al., 1995) found no sign of temperature dependence in meson properties up to 0.92 T_c. The results enable improved input for transport equations simulating the dynamical evolution of heavy-ion collision fireballs and contribute to understanding dilepton production in the 200-800 MeV range observed by CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations.
