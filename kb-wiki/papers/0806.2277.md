# Exact Relations for a Strongly-interacting Fermi Gas near a Feshbach Resonance

> A set of universal relations between various properties of any few-body or many-body system consisting of fermions with two spin states and a large but finite scattering length have been derived by Shina Tan. We derive generalizations of the Tan relations for a two-channel model for fermions near a Feshbach resonance that includes a molecular state whose detuning energy controls the scattering length. We use quantum field theory methods, including renormalization and the operator product expansion, to derive these relations. They reduce to the Tan relations as the scattering length is made increasingly large.

## Key claim

The paper derives generalizations of Shina Tan's universal relations for a two-channel model of fermions near a Feshbach resonance, where the scattering length is controlled by the detuning energy of a molecular state; these generalized relations reduce to the original Tan relations only in the limit where the scattering length becomes arbitrarily large.

## Method

The authors formulated the Resonance Model (which includes a molecular field coupled to fermionic atoms) and the Effective Range Model as renormalizable local quantum field theories. They applied standard renormalization methods and the operator product expansion (OPE) to derive the generalized Tan relations, calculating Wilson coefficients for the relevant composite operators. The derivations follow the approach previously used by Braaten and Platter (2008) for the Zero-Range Model, extended to the more complex two-channel framework.

## Key numbers

* The scattering length in the Resonance Model is given by \( a = \frac{1}{4\pi} \left( \lambda - \frac{g^2}{m\nu} \right) \), where \(\nu\) is the detuning energy parameter.
* The effective range in the Resonance Model is \( r_s = -8\pi \left( \lambda - \frac{g^2}{m\nu} \right)^{-2} \frac{g^2}{m^2 \nu^2} \), which is negative definite.
* In the limit \(\nu \to 0\) (large scattering length), the effective range approaches \( r_s \to -8\pi/g^2 \).
* The contact for a dimer (shallow diatomic molecule with \(a>0\)) is \( C = 8\pi/a \).
* For the homogeneous gas in the BCS limit (\(a \to 0^-\)), the contact density is \( \mathcal{C} \to 4\pi n/a \).
* For the homogeneous gas in the unitary limit (\(a \to \pm\infty\)), the contact density is \( \mathcal{C} \to \frac{2\zeta}{5\pi} (3\pi^2 n)^{4/3} \), where \(\zeta\) is a universal constant approximately equal to 1.
* For the homogeneous gas in the BEC limit (\(a \to 0^+\)), the contact density is \( \mathcal{C} \to 4\pi^2 n^2 a^2 \).

## Context

This work extends the universal relations derived by Shina Tan (2005, 2008) for fermions with two spin states and a large scattering length, which were originally formulated within the many-body Schrodinger equation framework using the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition. Braaten and Platter (2008) had previously rederived the Tan relations using quantum field theory and the OPE for the Zero-Range Model; the present paper generalizes that derivation to the Resonance Model of Kaplan (1996) and Kokkelmans et al. (2002), which provides a natural description of fermions near a Feshbach resonance. The results enable experimental tests of the generalized relations using cold trapped atoms near a Feshbach resonance, where the scattering length can be controlled magnetically and the momentum distributions can be measured via time-of-flight imaging.
