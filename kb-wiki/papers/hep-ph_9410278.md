# Right Handed Weak Currents in Sum Rules for Axialvector Constant Renormalization

> The recent experimental results on deep inelastic polarized lepton scattering off proton, deuteron and $^{3}$He together with polari% zed neutron $β$-decay data are analyzed. It is shown that the problem of Ellis-Jaffe and Bjorken sum rules deficiency and the neutron paradox could be solved simultaneously by assuming the small right handed current (RHC) admixture in the weak interaction Lagrangian. The possible RHC impact on pion-nucleon $σ$-term and Gamow-Teller sum rule for $(p,n)$ nuclear reactions is pointed out.

# Right Handed Weak Currents in Sum Rules for Axialvector Constant Renormalization

## Key claim
The discrepancy between the Ellis-Jaffe and Bjorken sum rule predictions and experimental data, together with the neutron paradox (a 2.6σ difference between the axialvector constant extracted from neutron lifetime versus electron asymmetry), can be resolved simultaneously by assuming a small admixture of right-handed currents (RHC) in the weak interaction Lagrangian, with the bare axialvector constant renormalization λ_N differing from the values extracted from neutron beta decay.

## Method
The author analyzes experimental data on polarized deep inelastic lepton scattering off proton, deuteron, and ³He targets together with polarized neutron beta decay data within the framework of the SU(2)ₗ × SU(2)ᵣ × U(1) left-right symmetric model. The experimental values of λ_τ (from neutron lifetime) and λ_c (from electron asymmetry) are treated as functions of the model parameters η (squared mass ratio of W₁ and W₂ bosons), ζ (mixing angle), and λ_N (bare axialvector constant renormalization), and constraints are derived from neutron, muon, and hyperon decay data. The Ellis-Jaffe sum rule is rewritten in terms of neutron and Σ⁻ hyperon beta decay ratios to incorporate RHC effects, and the Bjorken sum rule is used to extract λ_N from the difference of proton and neutron spin structure functions.

## Key numbers
- λ_τ = −1.270 ± 0.002 (extracted from neutron lifetime τ_n = 886.7 ± 1.5 s and ft₀₋₀ = 3074.0 ± 3.5 s)
- λ_c = −1.257 ± 0.003 (extracted from electron asymmetry A = −0.1126 ± 0.0011)
- Allowed RHC parameter ranges: 0.003 ≤ ζ ≤ 0.054, η ≤ 0.036, −1.265 ≤ λ_N ≤ −1.131 (first region); −0.054 ≤ ζ ≤ −0.020, η ≤ 0.024, −1.415 ≤ λ_N ≤ −1.20 (second region)
- Lower limit on W_R mass: M_WR ≥ 427 GeV (98% c.l.)
- Experimental proton spin structure function integral: Γᵖ = 0.126 ± 0.010 ± 0.015
- QCD-corrected Ellis-Jaffe prediction: Γᵖ = 0.189 ± 0.005 (using g_A/g_V = 1.254 ± 0.006 and F/D = 0.632 ± 0.024)
- Experimental neutron spin structure function: Γⁿ = −0.22 ± 0.11
- Bjorken sum rule gives: 0.833 ≤ |λ_N| ≤ 1.187 (68% c.l.), implying 0.03 ≤ ζ ≤ 0.17
- Σ⁻ hyperon decay: |g_A/g_V|^Σn = 0.36 ± 0.04
- Pion-nucleon σ-term: σ_πN = 45 ± 10 MeV (experimental) versus 23 ± 5 MeV (theoretical), reconciled for ζ ≈ 0.05
- Gamow-Teller sum rule quenching of about 40% can be partially explained by ζ ≈ 0.05, giving relation [S⁺(GT)−S⁻(GT)]_strong / [S⁺(GT)−S⁻(GT)]_weak ≈ (1−4ζ)

## Context
This work extends the left-right symmetric model analysis of the neutron paradox from earlier studies (referenced as papers [5,6]) to simultaneously address the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule deficiency (the "proton spin crisis" from EMC data) and the Bjorken sum rule. The author notes that most theoretical explanations for the proton spin crisis invoke gluons or orbital angular momentum, but RHC provides a simpler alternative. The analysis connects low-energy weak processes (neutron and hyperon beta decay) with high-energy scattering via current algebra, and suggests that the Adler-Weisberger sum rule (Adler 1965, Weisberger 1965) should be reanalyzed with modern pion-proton scattering data to extract the bare λ_N. The work also points to implications for solar neutrino detector counting rates that use (p,n) experimental data.
