## Context
This work extends the left-right symmetric model analysis of the neutron paradox from earlier studies (referenced as papers [5,6]) to simultaneously address the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule deficiency (the "proton spin crisis" from EMC data) and the Bjorken sum rule. The author notes that most theoretical explanations for the proton spin crisis invoke gluons or orbital angular momentum, but RHC provides a simpler alternative. The analysis connects low-energy weak processes (neutron and hyperon beta decay) with high-energy scattering via current algebra, and suggests that the Adler-Weisberger sum rule (Adler 1965, Weisberger 1965) should be reanalyzed with modern pion-proton scattering data to extract the bare őĽ_N. The work also points to implications for solar neutrino detector counting rates that use (p,n) experimental data.
