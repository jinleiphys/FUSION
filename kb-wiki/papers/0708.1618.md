# Gluon Vortices and Induced Magnetic Field in Compact Stars

> The natural candidates for the realization of color superconductivity are the extremely dense cores of compact stars, many of which have very large magnetic fields, especially the so-called magnetars. In this paper we discuss how a color superconducting core can serve to generate and enhance the stellar magnetic field without appealing to a magnetohydrodynamic dynamo mechanism.

## Key claim

A color-superconducting core in a compact star can generate and enhance the stellar magnetic field through gluon condensation and vortex formation, without requiring a magnetohydrodynamic dynamo mechanism; the effect depends on the existence of a Meissner-unstable phase at moderate densities.

## Method

The author analyzes the Ginzburg-Landau-type free energy for the color-flavor-locked (CFL) and two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phases, including the rotated electromagnetic field \(\widetilde{A}_\mu\) and charged gluon fields. By diagonalizing the gluon mass matrix in an external rotated magnetic field \(\widetilde{H}\), the tachyonic instability is identified when \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{H} > m_M^2\). The ground state is restructured by minimizing the Gibbs free energy with respect to a gluon condensate amplitude \(G\) and the induced magnetic field \(\widetilde{B}\), following Abrikosov's approach for type-II superconductors. For the 2SC phase at zero applied field, a similar analysis is performed with an inhomogeneous gluon condensate ansatz to find the induced field.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic fields of neutron stars range from \(B = 1.7 \times 10^8\) G (PSR B1957+20) to \(2.1 \times 10^{13}\) G (PSR B0154+61), with a typical value of \(10^{12}\) G.
- Magnetars have surface magnetic fields of order \(B \sim 10^{14} - 10^{16}\) G.
- The interior field in compact stars can reach values of order \(B \sim 10^{18}\) G, based on the scalar virial theorem.
- The critical field for the gluon tachyonic instability is \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{H}_C = m_M^2\).
- Some magnetars emit bursts of energy in the range \(10^{42} - 10^{46}\) erg.
- In the Meissner-unstable 2SC phase, the gluon condensate could produce a magnetic field \(\sim 10^{16} - 10^{17}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity in compact stars, building on prior analyses of the CFL phase by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the 2SC phase by Huang and Shovkovy (2003). It addresses observational criticisms of the standard magnetar model raised by Vink and Kuiper (2006) and Safi-Harb (2006), which note that magnetar supernova remnants lack expected energetic signatures and pulsar wind nebulae. The proposed mechanism enables a new explanation for magnetar-strength magnetic fields without requiring rapid rotation or a dynamo, and suggests that magnetars may be the most promising astrophysical sites for realizing color superconductivity.
