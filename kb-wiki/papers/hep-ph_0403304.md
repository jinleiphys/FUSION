# Worldline Variational Approximation: A New Approach to the Relativistic Binding Problem

> We determine the lowest bound-state pole of the density-density correlator in the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model where two equal-mass constituents interact via the exchange of mesons. This is done by employing the worldline representation of field theory together with a variational approximation as in Feynman's treatment of the polaron. Unlike traditional methods based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation, self-energy and vertex corrections are (approximately) included as are crossed diagrams. Only vacuum-polarization effects of the heavy particles are neglected. The well-known instability of the model due to self-energy effects leads to large qualitative and quantitative changes compared to traditional approaches which neglect them. We determine numerically the critical coupling constant above which no real solutions of the variational equations exist anymore and show that it is smaller than in the one-body case due to an induced instability. The width of the bound state above the critical coupling is estimated analytically.

## Key claim

The worldline variational method, applied to the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model, includes self-energy and vertex corrections (and crossed diagrams) approximately, leading to much stronger binding than traditional ladder Bethe-Salpeter approaches; however, the model's inherent instability due to self-energy effects imposes a critical coupling constant above which no real solutions exist, and this critical coupling is smaller for the two-body system than for the one-body case.

## Method

The authors express the density-density correlator (polarization propagator) as a double worldline path integral in the quenched approximation (nucleon loops neglected). They then approximate the highly nonlinear, retarded action by a quadratic trial action (a retarded harmonic oscillator) and apply Jensen's inequality (stationarity in real time), analogous to Feynman's variational treatment of the polaron. The variational parameters (a modified coupling parameter λ and profile functions A±(E)) are determined by solving coupled variational equations numerically in Euclidean time, and the bound-state pole position is extracted from Mano's equation in the limit of infinite proper time.

## Key numbers

* Critical coupling constant for the two-body bound state: α_crit^(2) ≃ 0.54 (dimensionless, defined as α = g²/(4πM²)), below which real variational solutions exist.
* Critical coupling constant for the one-body case: α_crit^(1) = 0.815 (dimensionless), from prior work.
* For massless mesons (m = 0) in the weak-coupling limit, the variational method gives leading binding coefficient b₂^var = 1/π and radiative correction coefficient r₂₁^var = 7/2, compared to the exact Coulomb value b₂ = 1/2 and the one-loop radiative coefficient r₂₁ = 4.
* Width of the bound state near the critical coupling: Γ ≃ (2/3) × 2M × ((α − α_crit^(2))/α_crit^(2))^(3/2) × f_corr(Z), with f_corr(1) = 1.0885.
* For the simplified analytic ansatz (harmonic oscillator profile), the critical coupling is α_crit(Z) ≃ (π/8) × (1 + √(1 + 3z))³/(1 + z + √(1 + 3z))² with z ≡ 2πZ², giving α_crit^(2) ≃ 0.463 for Z = 1.

## Context

This work extends the worldline variational method, previously applied to the one-body (self-energy) problem by Rosenfelder and collaborators, to the two-body relativistic bound-state problem in the scalar Wick-Cutkosky model. It contrasts with traditional ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation approaches (Efimov, 1979) and with Monte-Carlo calculations in the "generalized ladder approximation" (Nieuwenhuis and Tjon, 1996) which neglect self-energy and vertex corrections. The method enables a consistent treatment of two-point (self-energy), three-point (vertex correction), and four-point (binding) effects in one approximation, and suggests possible extensions to gauge theories where the worldline method is gauge-parameter independent, unlike Dyson-Schwinger approaches.
