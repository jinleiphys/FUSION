# Impact of Two-Body Currents on Semi-Exclusive Lepton-Nucleus Reactions

> We generalize the spectral-function formalism to describe two-nucleon knockout processes in exclusive kinematics. Significant improvements are introduced both in the treatment of the current operators entering the $Δ$-current contribution and in the modeling of correlations between the two struck nucleons, including a consistent treatment of isospin dependence and the explicit incorporation of angular correlations. The framework is validated through comparisons with relativistic Fermi-gas calculations and with semi-exclusive electron-nucleus scattering data. Our results demonstrate that an accurate description of nuclear dynamics plays a crucial role in modeling this reaction mechanism. We further present a study of selected electroweak observables relevant to neutrino-scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the spectral-function formalism to describe two-nucleon knockout in exclusive kinematics, but the validation is limited to comparisons with relativistic Fermi-gas calculations and semi-exclusive electron-nucleus scattering data; the neutrino-scattering results are presented as predictions without direct data comparison, and the framework's accuracy for neutrino observables remains to be tested against experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors factorize the two-nucleon knockout cross section by decomposing the nuclear current into one- and two-body operators, using a plane-wave ansatz for the final two-nucleon state and a Monte Carlo event generator that samples the full kinematics of both outgoing nucleons and the outgoing lepton on an event-by-event basis. The two-body current operator includes seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta-excitation contributions, with the Delta propagator updated from the Rarita-Schwinger form to a pure spin-3/2 projector operator and using updated axial and vector form factors. The two-nucleon spectral function is refined by incorporating angular correlations derived from the Argonne nucleon-nucleon potentials, retaining the full angular dependence of the relative and center-of-mass momenta of the struck pair.

## Key numbers

* The updated Delta propagator with the pure spin-3/2 projector leads to a reduction of 30% in the inclusive 2p2h cross section at neutrino energy \(E_{\nu} = 600\) MeV and 10% at \(E_{\nu} = 2000\) MeV.
* The axial form factor parameters used are \(C_5^A(0) = 1.18\) and \(M_{A\Delta} = 950\) MeV, from the fit of Hyper-Kamiokande (2018).
* The strong \(\pi N\Delta\) form factor cutoff is \(\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta} = 1200\) MeV, and the coupling constants are \(f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 0.08\) and \(f^\ast = 2.15\).

## Context

This work extends the spectral-function approach of Benhar (2006) and Rocco (2015) to two-nucleon knockout, improving upon earlier factorized frameworks that treated the two-body spectral function as a product of a momentum distribution and an energy-conserving delta function with fixed binding energy. The results are compared with relativistic Fermi-gas calculations and with semi-exclusive electron-nucleus scattering data in the dip region between the quasi-elastic and Delta-production peaks, and the framework is designed to be interfaced with neutrino event generators for use in oscillation experiments such as T2K, MicroBooNE, MINERvA, and NOvA.
