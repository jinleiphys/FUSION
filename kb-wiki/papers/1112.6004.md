# Self-consistent calculations of quadrupole moments of the first 2+ states in Sn and Pb isotopes

> A method of calculating static moments of excited states and transitions between excited states is formulated for non-magic nuclei within the Green function formalism. For these characteristics, it leads to a noticeable difference from the standard QRPA approach. Quadrupole moments of the first 2+ states in Sn and Pb isotopes are calculated using the self-consistent TFFS based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. with the set of parameters DF3-a fixed previously. A reasonable agreement with available experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a method for calculating static moments of excited states and transitions between excited states in non-magic nuclei within the Green function formalism that includes all terms of order g² (including phonon tadpole diagrams), which leads to a noticeable difference from the standard QRPA approach; the method achieves reasonable agreement with available experimental data for quadrupole moments of first 2⁺ states in Sn and Pb isotopes, except for ¹¹²Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Method

The authors extended the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. with the DF3-a parameter set to calculate matrix elements for static quadrupole moments of excited 2⁺ states. For non-magic nuclei with pairing, they derived an expression involving eight terms of three-Green-function integrals, generalizing the standard QRPA approach by including ground state correlation contributions and using the effective field V instead of the bare external field. The calculations were performed in the λ-representation with self-consistent single-particle wave functions obtained within the EDF method, using a spherical box of radius 16 fm and a cut-off energy of 100 MeV to ensure 1% accuracy, omitting the terms with δF and δF^ξ.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹²Sn: Q_tot = -0.43 e·b, compared to Q_exp = -0.03(11) e·b (disagreement)
- For ¹¹⁶Sn: Q_tot = -0.10 e·b, compared to Q_exp = -0.17(4) e·b and +0.08(8) e·b
- For ¹²⁰Sn: Q_tot = +0.05 e·b, compared to Q_exp = +0.022(10) e·b and -0.05(10) e·b
- For ²⁰⁴Pb: Q_tot = +0.11 e·b, compared to Q_exp = +0.23(9) e·b
- For ²⁰⁶Pb: Q_tot = +0.10 e·b, compared to Q_exp = +0.05(9) e·b
- For ²⁰⁸Pb: Q_tot = +0.05 e·b, compared to Q_exp = -0.7(3) e·b (disagreement)
- For the 3⁻ level in ²⁰⁸Pb: Q_theor = -0.26 b, compared to Q_exp = -0.34±0.15 b (from prior work)
- The term with δ_L F contributed approximately 1% compared to the first term for the ²⁰⁸Pb 3⁻ case
- Ground state correlation contributions in Eq. (9) reached about 60% of the total triangle contribution for some nuclei

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously applied to magic nuclei by Khodel and Saperstein (1982) to non-magic nuclei with pairing, generalizing earlier calculations of quadrupole moments performed within the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model by Vdovin and Stoyanov (1974) and the Nuclear Field Theory by Broglia, Liotta, and Paar (1972). The key advance is full self-consistency at the (Q)RPA level without any phenomenological or fitted parameters, which enables future calculations for unstable nuclei where experimental data are scarce; the authors note that the large experimental errors in existing data require better measurements to test the approach.
