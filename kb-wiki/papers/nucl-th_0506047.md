# Renormalization of NN Interaction with Chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential. Central Phases and the Deuteron

> We analyze the renormalization of the NN interaction at low energies and the deuteron bound state through the Chiral Two Pion Exchange Potential assumed to be valid from zero to infinity. The short distance Van der Waals singularity structure of the potential as well as the requirement of orthogonality conditions on the wave functions determines that, after renormalization, in the 1S0 singlet channel and in the 3S1-3D1 triplet channel one can use the deuteron binding energy, the asymptotic D/S ratio and the S-wave scattering lengths as well as the chiral potential parameters as independent variables. We use then the asymptotic wave function normalization As of the deuteron and the singlet and triplet effective ranges to determine the chiral constants yielding c1, c3 and c4. The role of finite cut-off corrections, the loss of predictive power due to uncertainties in the input data and the connection to OPE distorted waves perturbative approaches is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) NN potential at NNLO can be fully renormalized by removing the short-distance cutoff, provided one imposes orthogonality constraints on the wave functions; this yields cutoff-independent results for low-energy NN observables and the deuteron, but the extracted chiral constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) show significant sensitivity to the input data uncertainties, limiting predictive power.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space for the \(^1S_0\) singlet and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) triplet channels using the chiral NN potential up to NNLO, including the two-pion exchange terms. They remove the short-distance cutoff \(r_c \to 0^+\) by imposing energy-independent boundary conditions derived from the requirement that the wave function be small in the unknown inner region, which enforces orthogonality of states. The deuteron binding energy, asymptotic D/S ratio, and S-wave scattering lengths are used as independent inputs, while the asymptotic normalization \(A_S\) and the singlet and triplet effective ranges determine the chiral constants \(c_1, c_3, c_4\).

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length: \(\alpha_0 = -23.74\ \text{fm}\) (experimental input)
- Singlet effective range: \(r_0 = 2.77\ \text{fm}\) (experimental input)
- Deuteron binding energy: fixed to experimental value (explicit number not given in truncated text)
- Asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta = 0.0256\) (experimental value used)
- Asymptotic normalization \(A_S\) of the deuteron: used as input to determine chiral constants (explicit value not given in truncated text)
- Chiral constants extracted (units of \(\text{GeV}^{-1}\)): \(c_1 = -0.81 \pm 0.15\), \(c_3 = -4.69 \pm 1.34\), \(c_4 = 3.40 \pm 0.04\) from Buettiker (1999) \(\pi N\) analysis; the authors' own determination is discussed but explicit fitted numbers are not provided in the truncated text
- Wigner bound violation occurs for pure contact interaction at \(r_c = 1.4\ \text{fm}\); for NNLO TPE potential there is no lower bound on \(r_c\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous renormalized OPE calculations (Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola, 2005) to NNLO TPE, and contrasts with finite-cutoff approaches such as those of Epelbaum et al. (2003, 2004) and Entem and Machleidt (2003), which use momentum-space cutoffs \(\Lambda = 0.4-0.8\ \text{GeV}\) and obtain different values for \(c_1, c_3, c_4\). The authors identify finite-cutoff effects as large as 30% in the triplet effective range, and argue that cutoff-independent renormalization is necessary to avoid spurious systematic errors. The results enable model-independent extraction of chiral constants from low-energy NN data and deuteron properties, but also reveal that experimental uncertainties in input data limit the predictive power of the chiral expansion.
