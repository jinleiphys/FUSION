# Evaluation of bound-state $β^-$-decay half-lives of fully ionized atoms

> Bound-state $β^-$-decay is a rare radioactive process where the created electron is trapped in an atomic orbital instead of being emitted. It can be observed in highly ionized atoms in particular when normal beta decay is energetically forbidden, but bound-state decay is still possible. In this work we present a systematic theoretical study on the bound-state $β^-$-decay of fully ionized atoms where key nuclear inputs include the nuclear matrix elements (expressed through $ft$ values) and the lepton phase-space volume function. We present a method to evaluate nuclear matrix elements for fully forbidden $β^-$ transitions in neutral atoms, from the inverse electron capture process using the Takahashi-Yokoi model and account for the impact of electron capture to different atomic orbitals on the resulting half-lives. Decay rates for bound-state $β^-$-decays of nuclei $^{163}$Dy, $^{193}$Ir, $^{194}$Au, $^{202}$Tl, $^{205}$Tl, $^{215}$At, $^{222}$Rn, $^{243}$Am, and $^{246}$Bk are calculated, where the normal beta decay is forbidden. In addition, we compute the bound-state $β^-$ decay rates for nuclei $^{187}$Re, $^{227}$Ac, and $^{228}$Ra, observing enhancements by factors of $10^2$ to $10^4$ relative to their neutral-atom counterparts. Our results show that the half-lives of certain bare nuclei are significantly shorter than those of the corresponding neutral atoms, identifying them as promising candidates for future experimental investigation. The theoretically predicted half-lives of the bound-state $β^-$ decay could provide valuable inputs for various astrophysical studies.

## Key claim

The authors present a systematic theoretical study of bound-state β⁻-decay half-lives for fully ionized atoms, claiming that for nuclei where normal beta decay is energetically forbidden in neutral atoms, the bound-state decay channel becomes possible in bare atoms, and for certain nuclei the decay rates are enhanced by factors of 10² to 10⁴ relative to neutral-atom counterparts, but the calculated half-lives depend sensitively on which atomic orbitals are included in the phase-space calculation and on the choice of electron binding energies.

## Method

The authors extract nuclear matrix elements (expressed as logft values) from experimental electron-capture half-lives of neutral atoms for transitions where normal β⁻ decay is forbidden, using the Takahashi-Yokoi model to relate the inverse processes. For transitions with positive Qn values, they adopt experimental β⁻ decay half-lives of neutral atoms. The bound-state β⁻ decay rates are then calculated using the lepton phase-space volume function f_m*, which incorporates Coulomb amplitudes for bound electron wave functions solved from the Dirac bound-state radial equation, with the emitted electron captured into vacant K, L, M, N, or O orbitals of the bare daughter atom.

## Context

This work extends the Takahashi-Yokoi model (Takahashi and Yokoi, 1983, 1987) and recent calculations by Liu et al. (2021, 2022) for bound-state β⁻ decay of highly ionized atoms, using updated AME2020 atomic mass data and high-precision Qn values from Schweiger et al. (2024). The calculations provide theoretical predictions for nine nuclei where normal beta decay is forbidden (¹⁶³Dy, ¹⁹³Ir, ¹⁹⁴Au, ²⁰²Tl, ²⁰⁵Tl, ²¹⁵At, ²²²Rn, ²⁴³Am, ²⁴⁶Bk) and three nuclei with enhanced decay rates (¹⁸⁷Re, ²²⁷Ac, ²²⁸Ra), enabling future experimental investigations at storage ring facilities and providing inputs for astrophysical studies of s-process nucleosynthesis and nuclear chronometry.
