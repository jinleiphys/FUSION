# Time-odd components in the rotating mean field and identical bands

> A systematic construction of the energy-density functional within the local density approximation is presented. The Hartree-Fock equations corresponding to such a functional are solved in case of rotating superdeformed nuclei. The identical bands in $^{152}$Dy, $^{151}$Tb, and $^{150}$Gd are investigated and the time-odd components in the rotating mean field are analyzed.

## Key claim

The sameness of dynamical moments of inertia in identical superdeformed bands does not depend on whether time-odd terms in the mean field are included or omitted, whereas the sameness of the corresponding rotational alignments does depend on these time-odd terms.

## Method

The authors constructed an energy-density functional within the local density approximation, systematically enumerating all possible terms up to second order in derivatives of the one-body density matrix. They solved the Hartree-Fock equations using the cranking method for rotating superdeformed nuclei in the A approximately 150 region, using three Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SkP, and SIII) and comparing results with and without time-odd components in the functional.

## Key numbers

- For SkM* and SIII interactions, omission of time-odd terms significantly decreases the values of the dynamical moment of inertia J(2); for SkP the changes are much smaller because the isoscalar effective mass equals the free nucleon mass (m* = m)
- The isoscalar effective mass for SkM* is 0.79 times the free nucleon mass; for SIII it is 0.76 times the free nucleon mass
- Omission of time-odd terms can change the dynamical moment of inertia by as much as 15 hbar^2/MeV, but the relative differences between identical band pairs remain well below 2 hbar^2/MeV
- For the SkP interaction, the isoscalar coupling constant C^j_0 is equal to zero, rendering the dynamical moments almost independent of the C^j coupling constants

## Context

This work extends the systematic construction of energy-density functionals from the Skyrme interaction framework of Engel et al. (1975) and the authors' own prior work (Dobaczewski and Dudek, 1995). It analyzes the identical band phenomenon in superdeformed nuclei previously studied by Nazarewicz et al. (1989) and Ragnarsson (1993). The results enable future systematic investigations of rotational alignments as a tool for establishing properties of time-odd components in the rotating nuclear mean field.
