# The molecular systems composed of the charmed mesons in the $H\bar{S}+h.c.$ doublet

> We study the possible heavy molecular states composed of a pair of charm mesons in the H and S doublets. Since the P-wave charm-strange mesons $D_{s0}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ are extremely narrow, the future experimental observation of the possible heavy molecular states composed of $D_s/D_s^\ast$ and $D_{s0}(2317)/D_{s1}(2460)$ may be feasible if they really exist. Especially the possible $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ states may be searched for via the initial state radiation technique.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether loosely bound heavy molecular states can form from a pair of charmed mesons where one meson belongs to the H doublet (0⁻, 1⁻) and the other to the S doublet (0⁺, 1⁺), using a meson exchange model with a phenomenological cutoff parameter Λ around 1 GeV; the existence of such bound states depends sensitively on the coupling constants, the charge parity c = ±1, and the specific subsystem, with many cases requiring enhanced coupling constants (e.g., 2h or 3h) to produce a bound state.

## Method

The authors constructed effective potentials for the H̄S + h.c. system using a one-boson-exchange model with light pseudoscalar (π, η), scalar (σ), and vector (ρ, ω, φ) meson exchange, derived from an effective Lagrangian respecting chiral and heavy quark symmetries. They solved the Schrödinger equation with these potentials, introducing a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ at each vertex, and searched for bound state solutions across four subsystems ([PP₀*], [PP₁′], [P*P₀*], [P*P₁′]) for various flavor combinations (non-strange, singly strange, doubly strange) and charge parity values.

## Key numbers

- Masses of charmed mesons used: D⁰ = 1864.5 MeV, D⁺ = 1869.3 MeV, D*⁰ = 2006.7 MeV, D*⁺ = 2010.0 MeV, D_s⁺ = 1968.2 MeV, D_s*⁺ = 2112.0 MeV, D₀⁰ = 2352 MeV, D₀⁺ = 2403 MeV, D₁′⁰ = 2427 MeV, D_{s0}⁺ = 2317.3 MeV, D_{s1}′⁺ = 2458 MeV
- The pion decay constant f_π = 132 MeV is used in the effective Lagrangian
- The cutoff parameter Λ is taken around 1 GeV, with specific bound state solutions found for Λ values ranging from below 0.75 GeV to about 3 GeV depending on the subsystem and coupling strength
- For the [PP₀*]^{±,0} system with c = +1, a bound state exists with coupling h ≡ -0.56 and Λ < 0.75 GeV; with 1.3h a shallow binding energy appears at reasonable Λ; with 2h and 3h only deeply bound states exist
- For the [PP₀*]^{±,0} system with c = -1, no bound state exists with 1h; with 2h and Λ = 2.6-3 GeV a non-oscillating wave function appears; with 3h a bound state exists with Λ near 1 GeV
- For the [PP₁′]^{±,0} system with c = -1, no bound state exists with standard couplings; with 2h_σ a bound state appears but with Λ far larger than 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of heavy meson molecules (Liu et al., 2008-2009) by considering the H̄S + h.c. doublet combination, complementing earlier investigations of H̄H and S̄S systems. The study is motivated by the observation of numerous charmonium-like states such as X(3872), Y(4260), and Z⁺(4430) that sit near two-charmed-meson thresholds and are candidates for heavy meson molecules (Swanson, 2004; Close, 2004). The results suggest that future experimental searches for J^{PC} = 1^{--} states via initial state radiation techniques may be feasible, particularly if the narrow P-wave charm-strange mesons D_{s0}(2317) and D_{s1}(2460) are involved.
