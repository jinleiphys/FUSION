# Semi-classical approaches to heavy-ion reactions: fusion, rainbow, and glory

> A semi-classical approximation has been a powerful tool in understanding the dynamics of low-energy heavy-ion reactions. Here we discuss two topics in this regard, for which Mahir Hussein was a world leading pioneer. The first topic is heavy-ion fusion reactions of neutron-rich nuclei, in which the breakup process of the projectile nucleus plays a crucial role. The second is rainbow and glory scattering, for which characteristic oscillatory patterns in differential cross sections can be well understood in terms of intereferences among several semi-classical trajectories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that semi-classical approximations remain a powerful tool for understanding low-energy heavy-ion reactions, specifically for fusion of neutron-rich nuclei and for rainbow/glory scattering, but the abstract's claim that these approximations "work well" glosses over the condition that they require the variation of a potential within a wavelength to be negligibly small, which is satisfied only when the energy and/or mass of a particle is large.

## Method

The authors review two semi-classical approaches: (1) For fusion of weakly bound nuclei like ¹¹Li, they discuss a model where fusion cross sections are computed by multiplying transmission probabilities through the Coulomb barrier (with energy shifts ±F to account for soft dipole mode couplings) by a survival probability against prompt breakup, estimated from the imaginary part of a dynamical polarization potential along the incoming part of the classical trajectory. (2) For elastic scattering, they compute deflection functions from nuclear and Coulomb phase shifts using a Woods-Saxon optical potential, then analyze differential cross sections for rainbow and glory scattering via semi-classical interference formulas involving Airy functions and Bessel functions.

- Two-neutron separation energy of ¹¹Li: S₂ₙ = 378 ± 5 keV (from BAG08)
- Opening angle of valence neutrons in ¹¹Li: θₙₙ = 66⁺²²₋₁₈ degrees (from BeH07), and θₙₙ = 56.2⁺¹⁷·⁸₋₂₁·₃ degrees (from HaS07)
- For α+⁹⁰Zr scattering at E_lab = 141.7 MeV: rainbow occurs at λ = 50.5 with rainbow angle Θ_R = 4.34 degrees (Coulomb rainbow) and at λ = 31.5 with Θ_R = -73.4 degrees (nuclear rainbow), using a Woods-Saxon potential with V₀ = 117.5 MeV, R = 1.267 × 90¹/³ fm, a = 0.783 fm, and imaginary part W = 21.02 MeV, R_w = 1.267 × 90¹/³ fm, a_w = 0.783 fm (from GSB74)
- Glory scattering in α+⁹⁰Zr: deflection function crosses zero at λ ≈ 40, leading to forward-angle oscillations characterized by J₀ Bessel function

## Context

This review extends the pioneering semi-classical work of Mahir Hussein on heavy-ion reactions, particularly his early treatment of breakup effects in fusion of weakly bound nuclei (Hussein et al. 1992) and his concept of glory in the shadow of rainbow (Ueda et al. 1998, 1999). The paper contrasts the early approximate calculations of Hussein et al. (1992) and Dasso and Vitturi (1994) with more recent quantitative methods including continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations (Sakuragi et al. 1983, 1986; Austern et al. 1987), the classical three-body model of Dasgupta et al. (2002), and the spectator-participant inclusive breakup model of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985). The work enables future studies of complete versus incomplete fusion in weakly bound systems and further application of the sum-of-differences method to extract nuclear scattering amplitudes and fusion cross sections from experimental data.
