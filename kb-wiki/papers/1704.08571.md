# Clear indication of a strong I=0 Kbar-N attraction in the Lambda (1405) region from the CLAS photo-production data

> Possible existence of deeply bound kaonic nuclear systems was proposed Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002} more than a decade ago, based on an ansatz that the Lambda* = Lambda(1405) mass is 1405 MeV/c2, where the Lambda* is a Kbar-N quasi-bound state decaying to Sigma-pi. Recently, a large number of data on photo-production of Lambda(1405) in the gamma p to K+ pi{0+-} Sigma{0-+} reaction were provided by the CLAS collaboration Moriya et al.(2013}, and double-pole structure of the Lambda* has been intensively discussed by chiral dynamics analyses {Roca (2013),Mai 2015}, whereas we show that a Lambda* mass of 1405 MeV/c2 is deduced from the same CLAS data.

# Clear indication of a strong I=0 Kbar-N attraction in the Lambda(1405) region from the CLAS photo-production data

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Lambda(1405) pole position extracted from the CLAS photo-production data is 1405.5 MeV/c^2, consistent with the deep Kbar-N binding PDG value, and they reject the chiral dynamics double-pole positions (1421–1434 MeV/c^2) with more than 99% statistical significance, contrary to the interpretation that the CLAS data support a shallow-binding double-pole structure.

## Method

The authors analyzed the CLAS Sigma0 pi0 invariant mass spectra from the gamma p to K+ pi Sigma reaction at total energies W = 2.0–2.8 GeV. They classified chi-squared fitting parameters into "inner" parameters (which vary the pole position and width of the Lambda* inside the T-matrix) and "outer" parameters (which do not affect the pole position), and performed chi-squared minimization with the Lambda* pole position as a free fitting parameter.

## Key numbers

- Lambda* pole position from chi-squared minimum: 1405.5 MeV/c^2
- Chi-squared confidence levels: Delta chi-squared = 2.36, 4.74, 9.23 correspond to confidence levels of 68.3%, 95%, and 99.9%, respectively
- PDG value quoted: 1405.1 (+1.3 / -1.0) MeV/c^2
- Chiral double-pole positions rejected: 1421–1434 MeV/c^2
- CLAS data energy range: W = 2.0–2.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the original proposal of deeply bound kaonic nuclear systems by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), which assumed the Lambda(1405) mass of 1405 MeV/c^2 as a Kbar-N quasi-bound state. It directly contradicts the chiral dynamics analyses by Roca (2013), Mai (2015), and Meissner (2015), which interpreted the same CLAS data as supporting a double-pole structure with shallower binding. The result enables renewed confidence in the deep-binding scenario for Kbar-N interactions and supports the search for deeply bound kaonic nuclear clusters such as Kbar-NN and Kbar-Kbar-NN systems.
