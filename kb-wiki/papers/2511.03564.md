# ENDF/B-VIII.1: Updated Nuclear Reaction Data Library for Science and Applications

> The ENDF/B-VIII.1 library is the newest recommended evaluated nuclear data file by the Cross Section Evaluation Working Group (CSEWG) for use in nuclear science and technology applications, and incorporates advances made in the six years since the release of ENDF/B-VIII.0. Among key advances made are that the $^{239}$Pu file was reevaluated by a joint international effort and that updated $^{16,18}$O, $^{19}$F, $^{28-30}$Si, $^{50-54}$Cr, $^{55}$Mn, $^{54,56,57}$Fe, $^{63,65}$Cu, $^{139}$La, $^{233,235,238}$U, and $^{240,241}$Pu neutron nuclear data from the IAEA coordinated INDEN collaboration were adopted. Over 60 neutron dosimetry cross sections were adopted from the IAEA's IRDFF-II library. In addition, the new library includes significant changes for $^3$He, $^6$Li,$^9$Be, $^{51}$V, $^{88}$Sr, $^{103}$Rh, $^{140,142}$Ce, Dy, $^{181}$Ta, Pt, $^{206-208}$Pb, and $^{234,236}$U neutron data, and new nuclear data for the photonuclear, charged-particle and atomic sublibraries. Numerous thermal neutron scattering kernels were reevaluated or provided for the very first time. On the covariance side, work was undertaken to introduce better uncertainty quantification standards and testing for nuclear data covariances. The significant effort to reevaluate important nuclides has reduced bias in the simulations of many integral experiments with particular progress noted for fluorine, copper, and stainless steel containing benchmarks. Data issues hindered the successful deployment of the previous ENDF/B-VIII.0 for commercial nuclear power applications in high burnup situations. These issues were addressed by improving the $^{238}$U and $^{239,240,241}$Pu evaluated data in the resonance region. The new library performance as a function of burnup is similar to the reference ENDF/B-VII.1 library. The ENDF/B-VIII.1 data are available in ENDF-6 and GNDS format at https://doi.org/10.11578/endf/2571019.

## Key claim

The ENDF/B-VIII.1 library is the newest recommended evaluated nuclear data file by the Cross Section Evaluation Working Group (CSEWG), incorporating advances made in the six years since ENDF/B-VIII.0, but its performance as a function of burnup is similar to the reference ENDF/B-VII.1 library, indicating that while biases in high-burnup simulations have been reduced, the new library does not surpass the older standard for burnup-dependent applications.

## Method

The library was produced by reevaluating neutron nuclear data for over 30 nuclides through joint international efforts (including the IAEA INDEN collaboration and IRDFF-II dosimetry library adoption), updating resonance-region evaluations for 238U and 239,240,241Pu to address high-burnup issues, and revising thermal neutron scattering kernels for numerous materials. Validation was performed by calculating criticality benchmarks from the ICSBEP Handbook using Monte Carlo neutron transport and comparing computed multiplication factors (C) to experimental values (E) for both ENDF/B-VIII.1 and ENDF/B-VIII.0.

## Key numbers

- Over 60 neutron dosimetry cross sections were adopted from the IAEA's IRDFF-II library.
- For the Jezebel benchmark (PU-MET-FAST-001-001-d-rev5), ENDF/B-VIII.1 gives C/E = 1.00004 with computed uncertainty 0.00002, compared to ENDF/B-VIII.0 C/E = 1.00050.
- For HEU-MET-FAST-101-001, ENDF/B-VIII.1 gives C/E = 0.99937 with computed uncertainty 0.00002, compared to ENDF/B-VIII.0 C/E = 0.99976.
- For HEU-MET-INTER-011-001, ENDF/B-VIII.1 gives C/E = 1.00654 with computed uncertainty 0.00002, compared to ENDF/B-VIII.0 C/E = 1.01742, showing a reduction in bias.
- For PU-MET-MIXED-003-004-d-rev0, ENDF/B-VIII.1 gives C/E = 0.99460 with computed uncertainty 0.00008, compared to ENDF/B-VIII.0 C/E = 1.00212.

## Context

This work extends the ENDF/B series of evaluated nuclear data libraries, directly superseding ENDF/B-VIII.0 (released 2018) by addressing data issues that hindered its deployment for commercial nuclear power applications in high burnup situations. The reevaluations draw on the IAEA INDEN collaboration (which provided updated data for 239Pu and many other nuclides) and the IRDFF-II dosimetry library. The library enables improved simulations of integral experiments involving fluorine, copper, and stainless steel, and provides updated data for photonuclear, charged-particle, and atomic sublibraries, supporting applications from reactor design to nuclear nonproliferation.
