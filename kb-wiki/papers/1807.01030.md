# Calculation of a complete set of spin observables for proton elastic scattering from stable and unstable nuclei

> A microscopic study of proton elastic scattering from unstable nuclei at intermediate energies using a relativistic formalism is presented. We have employed both the original relativistic impulse approximation (IA1) and the generalised impulse approximation (IA2) formalisms to calculate the relativistic optical potentials, with target densities derived from relativistic mean field (RMF) theory using the NL3 and FSUGold parameter sets. Comparisons between the optical potentials computed using both IA1 and IA2 formalisms, and the different RMF Lagrangians are presented for both stable and unstable targets. The comparisons are required to study the effect of using IA1 versus IA2 optical potentials, with different RMF parameter sets, on elastic scattering observables for unstable targets at intermediate energies. We also study the effect of full-folding versus the factorized form of the optical potentials on elastic scattering observables. As with the case for stable nuclei, we found that the use of the full-folding optical potential improves the scattering observables (especially spin observables) at low intermediate energy (e.g. 200MeV). No discernible difference is found at a projectile incident energy of 500 MeV. To check the validity of using localized optical potential, we calculate the scattering observables using non-local potentials by solving the momentum space Dirac equation. The Dirac equation is transformed to two coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations, which are then numerically solved to obtain elastic scattering observables. The results are discussed and compared to calculations involving local coordinate-space optical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the generalized impulse approximation (IA2) formalism provides a better description of elastic proton scattering observables (especially spin observables) than the original impulse approximation (IA1) at lower intermediate energies (200 MeV), but the two formalisms yield similar results at 500 MeV, with the caveat that this improvement is most pronounced for stable nuclei while for unstable nuclei like $^{58}$Ca the discrepancy between IA1 and IA2 persists even at 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic optical potentials using both the original impulse approximation (IA1) and the generalized impulse approximation (IA2) formalisms, with target nucleon densities derived from relativistic mean field (RMF) theory using the NL3 and FSUGold parameter sets. They solved the Dirac equation both in coordinate space using localized optical potentials and in momentum space using non-local optical potentials transformed into two coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations, then calculated the differential cross section, analyzing power, and spin rotation function for proton elastic scattering from stable ($^{40,48}$Ca, $^{120}$Sn) and unstable ($^{54,58,60}$Ca, $^{132}$Sn, $^{206}$Hg) nuclei at laboratory kinetic energies of 200, 300, 500, and 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca at $T_{\mathrm{lab}} = 200$ MeV, the IA2 formalism gives a very good description of scattering observables while IA1 overestimates the scattering cross section; at $T_{\mathrm{lab}} = 500$ MeV the two formalisms show competition in describing experimental data
- For $^{48}$Ca at $T_{\mathrm{lab}} = 200$ MeV, IA2 gives very good descriptions of all three scattering observables while IA1 overestimates the scattering cross section data
- For $^{60}$Ca at $T_{\mathrm{lab}} = 200$ and 500 MeV, the NL3 and FSUGold calculations give identical values of scattering cross section at the first minimum, in agreement with the theoretical calculations of Kaki (2009)
- The root-mean-square charge radius for $^{48}$Ca is 3.4723 fm (NL3) and 3.4597 fm (FSUGold), compared to the experimental value of 3.4771 fm from Angeli (2013)
- The root-mean-square charge radius for $^{54}$Ca is 3.5939 fm (NL3) and 3.5741 fm (FSUGold), compared to the experimental value of 3.5640 fm from Garcia (2016)
- The root-mean-square charge radius for $^{132}$Sn is 4.7119 fm (NL3) and 4.7225 fm (FSUGold), compared to the experimental value of 4.7093 fm from Angeli (2013)
- For $^{206}$Hg, the neutron-proton radius difference $\Delta r = r_n - r_p$ is 0.46115 fm (NL3) and 0.36882 fm (FSUGold)
- For $^{58}$Ca, the neutron-proton radius difference $\Delta r = r_n - r_p$ is 0.53514 fm (NL3) and 0.47589 fm (FSUGold)
- The use of full-folding optical potential improves scattering observables (especially spin observables) at 200 MeV, but no discernible difference is found at 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation studies of proton elastic scattering from stable nuclei to unstable (neutron-rich) nuclei, building on the IA1 and IA2 formalisms developed by Tjon and Wallace (1987) and the relativistic mean field calculations using NL3 and FSUGold parameter sets from Lalazissis (1997) and Todd-Rutel (2003, 2005). The calculations for $^{60}$Ca are compared with the theoretical work of Kaki (2009), and the results for stable nuclei are validated against experimental data from Angeli (2013) and Garcia (2016). The paper enables future experimental studies at radioactive ion beam facilities such as RIKEN RIBF to interpret proton elastic scattering data from unstable nuclei in inverse kinematics, particularly for determining neutron density distributions and testing the validity of localized optical potentials at intermediate energies.
