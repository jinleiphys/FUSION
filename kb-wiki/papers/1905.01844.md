# Effective lambda-proton and lambda-neutron potentials from subthreshold inverse scattering

> Potentials are constructed for the lambda-nucleon interaction in the $^1\text{S}_0$ and $^3\text{S}_1$ channels. These potentials are recovered from scattering phases below the inelastic threshold through Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko theory. Experimental data with good statistics is not available for lambda-nucleon scattering. This leaves theoretical scattering phases as the only option through which the rigorous theory of quantum inverse scattering can be used in probing the lambda-nucleon force. Using rational-function interpolations on the theoretical scattering data, the kernels of the Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko integral equation become degenerate, resulting in a closed-form solution. The new potentials restored, which are shown to be unique through the Levinson theorem, bear the expected features of short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction. Charge symmetry breaking, which is perceptible in the scattering phases, is preserved in the new potentials. The lambda-nucleon force in the $^1\text{S}_0$ channel is observed to be stronger than in the $^3\text{S}_1$ channel, as expected. In addition, the potentials bear certain distinctive features whose effects on hypernuclear systems can be explored through Schrödinger calculations.

## Key claim
The authors construct energy-independent lambda-proton and lambda-neutron potentials from theoretical scattering phases below the inelastic threshold using Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko (GLM) inverse scattering theory, but note that the potentials do not incorporate Lambda-Nucleon to Sigma-Nucleon coupling, for which no mechanism exists in inverse scattering theory.

## Method
The authors applied single-channel Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko (GLM) inverse scattering theory at fixed angular momentum (s-wave, l=0) to theoretical scattering phase shifts computed by the Nijmegen group using the NSC97f potential. They used rational-function (Pade) interpolations of the scattering matrix with 20 poles to make the GLM integral equation kernels separable, enabling a closed-form solution. The potentials were recovered from the diagonal entries of the output kernel of the GLM equation, and phase equivalence was verified by recomputing phase shifts from the new potentials.

## Key numbers
- Lambda-proton potential attraction depth: -41.96 MeV in the 1S0 channel; -13.28 MeV in the 3S1 channel.
- Lambda-neutron potential attraction depth: -33.45 MeV in the 1S0 channel; -11.87 MeV in the 3S1 channel.
- Spin-averaged effective lambda-proton potential attraction depth: -19.01 MeV.
- Spin-averaged effective lambda-neutron potential attraction depth: -16.80 MeV.
- Inelastic threshold for Lambda-proton scattering: 640 MeV; for Lambda-neutron scattering: 650 MeV.
- Scattering data covered momentum range up to 500 MeV (below the inelastic threshold).
- A small repulsion barrier was observed in the region 0.55 to 1.00 fm.

## Context
This work extends the application of quantum inverse scattering theory, previously used for nucleon-nucleon interactions (e.g., Kirst (1989) using Reid soft core potential phase shifts), to the hyperon-nucleon sector for the first time. The potentials are derived from theoretical phase shifts of the Nijmegen NSC97f potential (Rijken et al., 1999) and show charge symmetry breaking consistent with known lambda-nucleon force properties. The new energy-independent potentials enable future few-body Schrodinger calculations on lambda hypernuclei to test their accuracy, particularly for properties like binding energies and lifetimes where existing meson-exchange potentials show discrepancies with experiment (e.g., Gal (2018) noting 30-50% longer hypertriton lifetimes than observed by Adam (2018)).
