# Pionic Matrix Elements in Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay

> Physics beyond the standard model of electroweak interactions that violates lepton number will, in general, induce operators involving the quark and lepton fields that violate lepton number. I point out that some matrix elements of particular operators are known. In the limit of exact SU(3) flavor symmetry the operator overline{u} gamma_mu (1-gamma_5)d overline{u} gamma_mu (1-gamma_5)d is part of the ${\bf 27}$ representation responsible for Delta I=3/2 strangeness changing decays in the standard model. At leading order in the chiral expansion there is only one coupling constant that describes the weak interactions of the pseudo-Goldstone bosons induced by the 27 representation. The observed branching fraction for K+ to pi+ pi0 determines this coupling constant. Contributions of the form m_s log m_s are used to estimate SU(3) breaking corrections to the relation between K+ to pi+ pi0 and the pi- to pi+ e- e- interaction.

# Pionic Matrix Elements in Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay

## Key claim

The hadronic matrix elements between pion states of certain lepton-number-violating four-quark operators that could contribute to neutrinoless double-beta decay can be normalized to the experimentally measured rate for $K^+ \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^0$ in the limit of exact SU(3) flavor symmetry, but SU(3) breaking corrections estimated from chiral logarithms are expected at the 30% level, with a specific one-loop calculation giving an enhancement of about 12% for the pionic matrix element relative to the kaon decay.

## Method

The paper uses chiral perturbation theory at leading order to relate the $\Delta L=2$ operator $\overline{u}\gamma_\mu(1-\gamma_5)d\ \overline{u}\gamma^\mu(1-\gamma_5)d$ (which transforms as a component of the ${\bf 27}$ representation of SU(3) flavor) to the $\Delta I=3/2$, $\Delta s=1$ operator responsible for $K^+ \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^0$ in the standard model. The single low-energy coupling constant $g^{\bf (27)}$ that describes both processes at leading order in the chiral expansion is determined from the measured $K^+ \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^0$ branching fraction. SU(3) breaking is estimated via one-loop chiral corrections of the form $m_s \log m_s$ from kaon and eta loops, and the relation to baryon operators is examined using the octet baryon effective lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 131\ {\rm MeV}$.
- The leading-order coupling constant extracted from $K^+ \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^0$ is $g^{\bf (27)} = 0.12$ in the SU(3) limit (a factor of 3/4 smaller than the convention used in Valencia (1998) due to differing definitions of $f_\pi$ and $T^{ab}_{cd}$).
- The one-loop SU(3) breaking correction enhances the pionic matrix element $g^{\bf (27)}_{\pi^-\pi^-}$ relative to $g^{\bf (27)}_{K^+\pi^+\pi^0}$ by about $12\%$, estimated from kaon loops with the chiral symmetry breaking scale $\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ {\rm GeV}$.
- The best fit to hyperon nonleptonic decay data gives $\beta_{27} \sim -0.16$ for the baryon coupling constant, but with large uncertainties as determined by El-Hady, Tandean, and Valencia (1998).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, connecting them to the well-studied $\Delta I=3/2$ nonleptonic kaon decay sector. Prior work by Haxton and Stephenson (1984) and others (Vergados, 1981; Faessler and collaborators, 1996) had discussed momentum-dependent pion interactions for $\beta\beta_{0\nu}$ but without the SU(3) normalization to $K^+ \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^0$ presented here. The paper enables future calculations of $\beta\beta_{0\nu}$ rates in specific short-distance models (such as left-right symmetric models by Mohapatra, 1998, or supersymmetry with R-parity violation by Faessler et al., 1997) by providing the hadronic matrix elements for pion states, though it notes that current hyperon decay data are too imprecise to constrain the nucleon-level operators needed for nuclear matrix element calculations.
