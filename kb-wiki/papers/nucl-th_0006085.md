# Complex Scaling of the Faddeev Operator

> The work is devoted to comparison of two different approaches to calculation of three-body resonances on the basis of the Faddeev differential equations. The first one is the well known complex scaling approach. The second method is based on an immediate calculation of the zeros of the scattering matrix continued to the physical sheet.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that complex scaling of the Faddeev differential equations yields resonance energies that agree with those obtained from a scattering-matrix approach, but only for systems where resonances exist on accessible unphysical sheets; the scaling method cannot access virtual states that lie on different Riemann sheets.

## Method
The authors performed complex scaling on the three-body Faddeev differential equations in coordinate space, solving the partial-wave version for total angular momentum L=0 using a finite-difference approximation in polar coordinates with up to 400 knots in both hyperradius and hyperangle variables and a cut-off hyperradius of 40 fm. They compared the resulting resonance poles from the scaled Faddeev operator with those obtained from a direct calculation of scattering-matrix zeros on the physical sheet, using the same Gaussian-type potential with a barrier term for a model three-boson system and the MT I–III potential for the nnp system.

## Key numbers
- For the model three-boson system with nucleon masses and a Gaussian potential with barrier Vb=1.5, the complex-scaling resonance energy is z_res = -5.9526 - 0.4033 i MeV (stable across scaling angles θ = 0.25 to 0.70 rad, with variations only in the fourth decimal place).
- The scattering-matrix approach for the same system gives z_res = -5.952 - 0.403 i MeV, agreeing with the complex-scaling result to three decimal places.
- For the nnp system with the MT I–III potential, the triton virtual state energy is -2.690 MeV (0.47 MeV below the two-body threshold), obtained with 1000 knots in both hyperradial variables and a cut-off hyperradius of 120 fm.
- No resonances were found in the nnp system on the two-body unphysical sheet or in the three-body sheet accessible via scaling, contradicting an experimental claim of a resonance at -1.5 ± 0.3 - i(0.3 ± 0.15) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the complex scaling method, previously applied to the Schrodinger equation, to the Faddeev differential equations for the first time, and compares it with the scattering-matrix approach of Motovilov et al. (1998) and Kolganova et al. (1998) that computes zeros of the scattering matrix on the physical sheet. The results confirm that both methods yield identical resonance energies for systems where resonances exist, but the scaling method cannot access virtual states (such as the triton virtual state at -2.690 MeV) that lie on different Riemann sheets. This enables future applications of complex scaling to Faddeev equations for three-body resonance calculations, while clarifying the limitations of the method compared to scattering-matrix approaches.
