# Shell Model Monte Carlo method in the $pn$-formalism and applications to the Zr and Mo isotopes

> We report on the development of a new shell-model Monte Carlo algorithm which uses the proton-neutron formalism. Shell model Monte Carlo methods, within the isospin formulation, have been successfully used in large-scale shell-model calculations. Motivation for this work is to extend the feasibility of these methods to shell-model studies involving non-identical proton and neutron valence spaces. We show the viability of the new approach with some test results. Finally, we use a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction in the model space described by (1p_1/2,0g_9/2) proton and (1d_5/2,2s_1/2,1d_3/2,0g_7/2,0h_11/2) neutron orbitals above the Sr-88 core to calculate ground-state energies, binding energies, B(E2) strengths, and to study pairing properties of the even-even 90-104 Zr and 92-106 Mo isotope chains.

# Shell Model Monte Carlo method in the $pn$-formalism and applications to the Zr and Mo isotopes

## Key claim

The authors develop a new Shell Model Monte Carlo algorithm in the proton-neutron (pn) formalism that can treat Hamiltonians which are not isospin invariant or use different valence spaces for protons and neutrons, and demonstrate its viability through test cases and applications to Zr and Mo isotopes. The method relies on extrapolation from sign-problem-free Hamiltonians to the physical interaction, and the results for ground-state energies agree with exact diagonalization within error bars, but the calculated B(E2) strengths fall below experimental values for heavier isotopes, possibly due to the choice of single-particle energy for the 0h11/2 orbital or missing correlations.

## Method

The authors implement a Shell Model Monte Carlo method in the pn-formalism (SMMCpn) where proton and neutron valence spaces are combined into a single valence space, and canonical traces are obtained by applying projection operators to fix total particle number A and the z-component of isospin T_z. The two-body interaction is split into sign-problem-free and sign-problematic parts, and observables are evaluated at several values of an extrapolation parameter g in the interval -1 to 0, then extrapolated to the physical value g=1 using polynomial fits. The continuous integral over auxiliary fields is approximated by a discrete sum derived from Gaussian quadrature, and Metropolis Monte Carlo sampling is used to evaluate the resulting path integrals.

## Context

This work extends the Shell Model Monte Carlo method beyond the isospin-conserving formulation (original SMMC by Koonin, Lang, and others) to handle non-identical proton and neutron valence spaces, enabling studies of nuclei where isospin symmetry is broken or different model spaces are needed for protons and neutrons. The authors apply the method to Zr and Mo isotopes in the mass region where prior experimental studies (1970s) showed abrupt spherical-to-deformed shape transitions, and where Holt et al. (2000s) performed shell-model diagonalization calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential. The calculations use a slightly modified version of the Holt et al. interaction and extend the study to neutron numbers up to N=64, enabling future investigations of level densities and parity dependence relevant to astrophysical nucleosynthesis.
