# Two- and three-cluster decays of light nuclei with the hyperspherical harmonics

> We consider a set of three-cluster systems ($^{4}$He, $^{7}$Li, $^{7}$Be, $^{8}$Be, $^{10}$Be) within a microscopic model which involves the hyperspherical harmonics to represent intercluster motion. We selected such three-cluster systems which have at least one binary channel. Our aim is to study whether the hyperspherical harmonics are able and under what conditions to describe two-body channel(s) (nondemocratic motion) or they are suitable for describing three-cluster continuum only (democratic motion). It is demonstrated that a rather restricted number of the hyperspherical harmonics allows us to describe bound states and scattering states in two-body continuum for a three-cluster system.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonics method, using a restricted number of basis functions, can describe both bound states and scattering states in the two-body continuum for three-cluster systems, provided the basis is large enough to capture the correct asymptotic behavior; the method's ability to represent nondemocratic (binary) decay channels depends on the number of hyperspherical harmonics included and the energy proximity to the corresponding binary threshold.

## Method

The authors employ a microscopic three-cluster model using hyperspherical oscillator functions to expand the intercluster wave function, reducing the coupled integro-differential equations to an algebraic eigenvalue problem. They first diagonalize the norm kernel matrix to identify and eliminate Pauli forbidden states, then diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrix in the basis of normalized Pauli-allowed states to obtain eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for bound and pseudo-bound states. The analysis focuses on the convergence of the spectrum and the structure of wave functions for the three-cluster systems ⁴He, ⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁸Be, and ¹⁰Be, using only one set of Jacobi vectors within the Zernike-Brinkman formulation of hyperspherical harmonics.

## Key numbers

- ⁷Li: experimental spectrum includes the lowest two-body decay thresholds at ⁴He+³H and ⁶Li+n, and the three-body threshold α+d+n; the paper studies whether hyperspherical harmonics can reproduce bound states with dominant ⁴He+³H structure.
- The hypermomentum quantum number K has a minimal value K_min = L for normal parity states (π = (-1)^L) and K_min = L+1 for abnormal parity states (π = (-1)^(L+1)).
- The oscillator shell number N_sh is defined by N_os = 2n_ρ + K = 2N_sh + K_min, where n_ρ is the number of hyperradial excitations.
- The hyperspherical angle θ ranges from 0 to π/2, with x = ρ cos θ and y = ρ sin θ, where ρ = √(x² + y²) is the hyperspherical radius.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic three-cluster model formulated in Vasilevsky (2001) and previously applied to Borromean nuclei (⁶He, ⁹Be, ¹²C) and nuclei with prominent three-cluster features (⁵H, ⁶Be, ⁹B). It contrasts with the hyperspherical adiabatic approximation used by other authors (e.g., PhysRevC.26.2288, PhysRevC.26.2301, 2001PhR...347..373N, 2015FBS....56..753D), which works for structureless particles but is unsuitable for three interacting clusters due to nonlocal and energy-dependent potentials from the Pauli principle. The present paper aims to create a unified microscopic model for describing both binary and three-cluster decay channels, enabling future studies of the continuous spectrum between binary thresholds and the three-body threshold.
