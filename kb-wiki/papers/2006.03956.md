# Study of the ground state energies of some nuclei using hybrid model

> The quark-quark QQ interaction as a perturbed term to the nucleon-nucleon interaction NN without any coupling between them is studied in a hybrid model. This model is used to calculate the ground-state energies of 2H1 and 4He2 nuclei. In a semi-relativistic framework, this model is encouraged for light nuclei and the instanton induced interaction by using the QQ potential and the NN interaction for a small scale around the hadron boundaries. This hybrid model depends on two theories, the one-boson exchange potential OBEP and the Cornell-dressed potential CDP for QQ. A small effect of quark-quark interaction is obtained on the values of the ground state energies, around 6.7 for 2H1 and 1.2 for 4He2 by using the considered hybrid model.

## Key claim

The authors propose a hybrid model in which the quark-quark (QQ) interaction is added as a perturbative term to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, with no coupling between the two sectors, and find that the QQ contribution has only a small effect on the calculated ground-state energies of light nuclei.

## Method

The ground-state energies of ²H₁ and ⁴He₂ are computed in a semi-relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock framework using a Hamiltonian that sums a one-boson exchange potential (OBEP) for the NN interaction and a Cornell-dressed potential (CDP) for the QQ interaction. The OBEP includes exchanges of pseudoscalar (π), scalar (σ), and vector (ω, ρ) mesons, while the QQ potential incorporates a Coulomb-like term, a linear confinement term, and an instanton-induced interaction. The wave functions are expanded in harmonic-oscillator basis states with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and Talmi-Moshinsky brackets, and the total energy is minimized via the steepest-descent method.

## Key numbers

- For ²H₁ (deuteron), the quark-quark interaction contributes approximately 6.7 (units not specified in the text) to the ground-state energy.
- For ⁴He₂ (alpha particle), the quark-quark interaction contributes approximately 1.2 (units not specified in the text) to the ground-state energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Bonn-group OBEP (Machleidt, 1989; Erkelenz, 1974; Holinde, 1981) by adding quark degrees of freedom via the Cornell-dressed potential, which has been used successfully for meson and baryon spectra (Blask, 1990; Resag, 1992). The instanton-induced interaction follows the approach of 't Hooft (1976) and Shifman (1979). The hybrid model is intended for light nuclei and could enable future calculations that include coupling between the NN and QQ sectors or extend the method to heavier systems.
