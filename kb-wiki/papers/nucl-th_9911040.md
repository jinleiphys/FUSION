# Photoproduction of meson and baryon resonances in a chiral unitary approach

> By means of a coupled channel non-perturbative unitary approach, it is possible to extend the strong constrains of Chiral Perturbation Theory to higher energies. In particular, it is possible to reproduce the lowest lying resonances in meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV using the parameters of the O(p^2) and O(p^4) Chiral Lagrangian. The meson baryon sector can also be tackled along similar lines. We report on an update of these results showing some examples of photon induced reactions where the techniques have been recently applied.

## Key claim

By using a coupled-channel non-perturbative unitary approach, the strong constraints of Chiral Perturbation Theory can be extended to higher energies, reproducing the lowest-lying meson-meson resonances up to 1.2 GeV using only the parameters of the O(p^2) and O(p^4) Chiral Lagrangian, with the caveat that the method relies on a cut-off regularization or subtraction constants whose values are fitted to data.

## Method

The authors implement unitarity exactly by expanding Re T^{-1} in powers of momentum rather than expanding the T matrix itself, using a coupled-channel generalization of the inverse amplitude method. For meson-meson scattering, they fit the eight L_i coefficients from the O(p^4) chiral Lagrangian to phase shifts and inelasticities up to 1.2 GeV; for the meson-baryon sector, they solve a Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest-order chiral amplitude as the kernel, factorizing the on-shell amplitudes outside the loop integral. In the S = -1 meson-baryon sector, a single cut-off of 630 MeV is chosen to reproduce K^- p threshold branching ratios, while in the S = 0 sector different subtraction constants per channel are used.

## Key numbers

* The cut-off used in the meson-baryon calculation for the S = -1 sector is q_max = 630 MeV, chosen to reproduce K^- p scattering branching ratios at threshold.
* The weak decay constant is taken as f = 1.15 f_pi, between the pion and kaon values, to optimize the position of the Lambda(1405) resonance.
* The calculated K^- p scattering length is a = -1.00 + i 0.94 fm, compared to experimental values of (-0.67 + i 0.64) fm (Adm81) and (-0.78 +/- 0.18) + i (0.49 +/- 0.37) fm (Iw97).
* The calculated K^- n scattering length is a = 0.53 + i 0.62 fm, compared to the experimental value of 0.37 + i 0.60 fm (Adm81).
* The threshold ratio gamma = Gamma(K^- p -> pi+ Sigma-) / Gamma(K^- p -> pi- Sigma+) is calculated as 2.32, compared to the experimental value 2.36 +/- 0.04 (To71).
* The photon energy chosen for the gamma p -> K+ Lambda(1405) reaction is E_gamma = 1.7 GeV.
* The coupled-channel treatment increases the K^- p -> gamma Sigma^0 rate by about a factor of 16 relative to the Born approximation.
* The meson-meson scattering results reproduce resonances up to 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Dobado and Pelaez (1997) and Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1998) for meson-meson scattering, and by Oset and Ramos (1998) for meson-baryon scattering, to photon-induced reactions. The method enables the photoproduction of dynamically generated resonances such as the Lambda(1405), f0(980), and a0(980) to be calculated from first principles, providing predictions for experiments at SPring8/RCNP and TJNAF. The approach contrasts with earlier work by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) which introduced higher-order Lagrangians for the S = 0 meson-baryon sector, and with the N/D unitarization method of Oller and Oset (1999) which added explicit heavier scalar resonances to the lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory Lagrangian.
