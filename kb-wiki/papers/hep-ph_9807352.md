# Charmonium and lepton pair production with medium energy antiprotons

> The medium energy antiproton beam facility which is under discussion at GSI offers new opportunities to study the QCD of heavy flavours, nuclear structure and exotic mesons. We discuss these topics and briefly highlight other problems in charm production and proton structure.

# Charmonium and lepton pair production with medium energy antiprotons

## Key claim
The proposed medium-energy antiproton beam facility at GSI could resolve long-standing discrepancies in charmonium-nucleon absorption cross sections by exploiting the unique kinematics of direct $\bar pp \to J/\Psi(\Psi')$ production, provided that the beam momentum resolution and Fermi motion effects are properly accounted for.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical calculations for charmonium production in $\bar pA$ collisions, using a two-channel approach that accounts for interference between $J/\Psi$ and $\Psi'$ states. The production rate off nuclei is evaluated using a Gaussian Fermi momentum distribution with $k_F=200$ MeV, and the dependence on beam momentum is derived from the ratio of partial widths and nuclear thickness functions. For Drell-Yan production of 4-quark mesons, the cross section is parameterized as a function of the lepton pair mass and the Mandelstam variable $\tau$.

## Key numbers
- $\sigma_{tot}(J/\Psi N)=1.2$ mb from photoproduction data at $\sqrt{s_{\Psi}}=10$ GeV, versus $\approx 6$ mb from hadroproduction off nuclei using the optical model
- The laboratory momentum for direct production is $p_L(J/\Psi)=4.06$ GeV and $p_L(\Psi')=6.2$ GeV
- At $\Delta p_b = 1$ MeV beam momentum resolution, the production rate off nuclei is suppressed by three orders of magnitude relative to $\bar pp\to\Psi$
- The difference in absorption cross sections is $\Delta\sigma=\sigma_{tot}(\Psi' N)-\sigma_{tot}(J/\Psi N) \approx 9$ mb
- The Drell-Yan cross section parameterization: $d\sigma(\bar pp\to e^+e^- X)/dM_{e^+e^-} = (0.21/M^3_{e^+e^-})\,e^{-11.7\,\tau}$ $\mu$b, where $\tau=M^2_{e^+e^-}/s$ and $M_{e^+e^-}$ is in GeV
- With luminosity $10^{32}$ cm$^{-2}$ sec$^{-1}$, overall efficiency 0.5 and trigger efficiency 0.7, one can expect $\sim 1200$ events per day with a 12 GeV antiproton beam
- For a light 4-quark system ($0.9 < M_{4q} < 2$ GeV), the expected rate is $\sim 300$ events per day, maximal for beam momentum $5-8$ GeV

## Context
This work extends the two-channel approach to charmonium production developed by Hüfner and Kopeliovich (1998) to resolve the discrepancy between photoproduction and hadroproduction measurements of $\sigma_{tot}(J/\Psi N)$. It builds on earlier analyses by Buchmüller (1982) for potential models of charmonium and by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) for Fermi motion effects. The proposed measurements would enable direct determination of charmonium-nucleon absorption cross sections, study of the high-momentum tail of the Fermi distribution in nuclei (complementing the E850 experiment at BNL by Heppelmann (1997) on $A(p,2p)A'$), and search for 4-quark meson states such as the $C(1480)$ candidate found by Landua (1992). The Drell-Yan production mechanism for 4-quark states was previously suggested by the Dubna group (1997).
