# phi production at RHIC: characterization of co-existence phase

> We extract the effective degrees of freedom that characterize the co-existing phase of quark gluon plasma and hadrons. Experimental data on phi at mid-rapidity is used to set a lower bound to the critical temperature of quark hadron phase transition. The production and evolution of strangeness have been studied by using Boltzmann equation. The results have been contrasted with the experimental data obtained by STAR collaboration at RHIC for Au + Au collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV. Our study reveals that the phi mesons freeze out at a temperature ~ 160 MeV, a value close to the transition temperature for quark-hadron phase transition.

# φ production at RHIC: characterization of co-existence phase

## Key claim

The φ meson freeze-out temperature is approximately 160 MeV, close to the quark-hadron transition temperature, but the paper's analysis shows that the critical temperature must be *greater than* 160 MeV to explain the observed φ enhancement, not simply equal to it. The overproduction of φ mesons at mid-rapidity can only be explained if the system passes through a mixed phase of QGP and hadrons during its evolution.

## Method

The authors use (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with boost invariance and an equation of state from lattice QCD simulations to model the evolution of matter formed in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They extract the freeze-out temperature from the transverse momentum spectra of φ mesons using the relation Teff = Ttrue + (1/2)mφ⟨vr⟩², where the average radial velocity ⟨vr⟩ is obtained from the hydrodynamic calculation. They also employ momentum-integrated Boltzmann equations to study the time evolution of strangeness density, considering production reactions for strange quarks in QGP (q q̅ → s s̅ and gg → s s̅) and strange hadrons in the hadronic phase (meson-meson and meson-baryon channels), with the system cooling governed by a modified Bjorken cooling law that includes a term from strangeness production.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of φ mesons: Ttrue ≈ 162 ± 8 MeV, extracted from the effective temperature Teff = 257 ± 8 MeV (for 0.45 ≤ pT (GeV) ≤ 2) and average radial velocity ⟨vr⟩ ≈ 0.43
- Experimental yield of φ mesons at mid-rapidity for 0-5% central collisions: dNφ/dy = 7.7 ± 0.30 at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Ratio dNφ/dy / dN/dy from experiment: 7.7 × 10⁻³; from thermal model at Tc = 170 MeV with geff = 18: 4.77 × 10⁻³
- Enhancement factor γφ required by experimental data: approximately 1.3 (γφ = dNφ/dy|expt / dN/dy|eq)
- Enhancement factor from Boltzmann equation at Tc > 160 MeV: γφ ≈ γK² ≈ 1.24
- Initial temperatures for two thermalization times: Ti = 590 MeV (τi = 0.15 fm/c) and Ti = 480 MeV (τi = 0.2 fm/c)
- Effective statistical degeneracy values from lattice QCD: geff ≈ 9 at T = 150 MeV, 26 at T = 170 MeV, and 31 at T = 195 MeV
- Lower bound on critical temperature from this analysis: Tc > 160 MeV
- Lower bound on effective statistical degeneracy: geff ≈ 18

## Context

This work extends the study of φ meson production as a probe of the quark-hadron phase transition, building on the suggestion by Shor (1985) that φ mesons are a useful signal for QGP formation due to their suppressed interaction with nuclear matter via the OZI rule. The results are contrasted with experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC and with lattice QCD simulations for the equation of state and critical parameters. The analysis provides a lower bound on the transition temperature (Tc > 160 MeV) and effective statistical degeneracy (geff ≈ 18), which constrains the properties of the mixed phase. The extracted hadronization temperature of approximately 162 MeV is consistent with the statistical hadronization model value of Tc = 155 ± 8 MeV from Rafelski and Letessier (2000) and similar values obtained by Gorenstein (2003) from heavier hadron data. This work enables future studies of K/π ratios in the same framework and provides a method to characterize the co-existence phase using φ meson production data.
