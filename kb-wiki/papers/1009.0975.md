# Breathing Oscillations in Bose - Fermi Mixing Gases with Yb atoms in the Largely Prolate Deformed Traps

> We study the breathing oscillations in bose-fermi mixtures with Yb isotopes in the largely prolate deformed trap, which are realized by Kyoto group. We choose the three combinations of the Yb isotopes, Yb170-Yb171, Yb170-Yb173 and Yb174-Yb173, whose boson-fermion interactions are weakly repulsive, strongly attractive and strongly repulsive. The collective oscillations in the deformed trap are calculated in the dynamical time-development approach, which is formulated with the time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii and the Vlasov equations. We analyze the results in the time-development approach with the intrinsic oscillation modes of the deformed system, which are obtained using the scaling method, and show that the damping and forced-oscillation effects of the intrinsic modes give time-variation of oscillations, especially, in the fermion transverse mode.

## Key claim

The paper claims that breathing oscillations in Bose-Fermi mixtures with Yb isotopes in largely prolate deformed traps can be understood through four intrinsic collective modes (in-phase transverse breathing, out-of-phase transverse breathing, fermion longitudinal breathing, and boson axial-breathing modes), but only when the boson-fermion interaction is weak; for strong interactions, the dynamical time-development approach shows discrepancies from the scaling method predictions, particularly in the fermion transverse mode where damping and forced-oscillation effects become important.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the boson condensate and the Vlasov equation for the fermions using a test-particle method, with initial conditions given by boosted ground-state wave functions. They analyze the resulting time evolution of the root-mean-square radii along longitudinal and transverse directions, and extract oscillation frequencies via Fourier transforms of these quantities. The results are compared with intrinsic oscillation modes obtained from a scaling method that expands the total energy to second order in collective coordinates describing boson and fermion longitudinal and transverse breathing deformations.

## Key numbers

- Three Yb isotope combinations studied: Yb170-Yb171 (h_BF/g_BB = 0.573, weakly repulsive), Yb170-Yb173 (h_BF/g_BB = -1.273, strongly attractive), Yb174-Yb173 (h_BF/g_BB = 1.325, strongly repulsive)
- Boson number N_b = 10000, fermion number N_f = 1000
- Boson transverse trapping frequency Omega_B = 2 pi x 300 Hz
- Deformation parameter kappa_L = 1/6 (prolate trap)
- Boson-boson coupling g_BB = 0.154 for Yb174-Yb173 and 0.0964 for the other mixtures
- Test-particle number per fermion N_tilde = 100, integration time 0 < tau < 200
- For the boson axial-breathing mode in the large deformation limit (kappa_L << 1), the frequency squared is approximately omega^2 ≈ 5 kappa_L^2 (1 - v_B/10)/2, with eigenvector components (lambda_BT, lambda_BL) ≈ (-1, 4)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of monopole oscillations (Maruyama and Yabu, 2009) and dipole oscillations (Maruyama and Yabu, 2010) in Bose-Fermi mixtures, as well as quadrupole oscillations in spherical traps (Maruyama and Yabu, 2010). The scaling method used is consistent with the energy-weighted sum rule approach of Bohigas and Lane, and the nonlinear scaling method was previously applied to BEC oscillations by Castin and Dum (1996), which reproduced experimental results by Mewes et al. (1996). The paper enables future experimental studies by the Kyoto group on Yb isotope mixtures in deformed traps, where the predicted mode structure and interaction-dependent damping can be tested.
