# Entangling Power: A Probe of Symmetry and Integrability in Quantum Many-Body Systems

> The entangling power of a unitary operator quantifies its ability to generate entanglement from product states and provides a natural probe of quantum many-body dynamics. Entanglement extremization at points of enhanced symmetry has previously been observed in high-energy scattering. In this work we compute the time-averaged entangling power of anisotropic Heisenberg spin chains across two-site models and finite-size systems, as well as the entangling power of the two-magnon $S$-matrix in the thermodynamic limit. For two-site models we establish a monotonic hierarchy: the entangling power decreases as the symmetry group grows, reaching its minimum at the $SU(2)$ XXX point. Finite-size XXZ chains exhibit sharp dips at the $SU(2)$ points $Δ=\pm 1$ and the free-fermion point $Δ=0$, with the free-fermion dip decaying much more slowly with system size. In the thermodynamic limit, we decompose the two-magnon $S$-matrix into quantum logic gates -- Identity, SWAP, and $σ_z\otimesσ_z$ -- and show that the entangling power vanishes for all scattering energies at the $SU(2)$ points, where the $S$-matrix reduces to the Identity gate, while the free-fermion point achieves the maximum -- the opposite of the finite-size many-body behavior. The entangling power can serve as an {\em operator} diagnostic for symmetry and selected aspects of integrability in quantum simulations of spin-chain dynamics.

## Key claim

The time-averaged entangling power of anisotropic Heisenberg spin chains is suppressed at points of enhanced symmetry across all regimes studied (two-site gates, finite-size chains, and the thermodynamic limit), but the free-fermion point shows opposite behavior between finite-size systems (dip) and the two-magnon S-matrix (maximum), indicating that the entangling power diagnoses symmetry rather than integrability alone.

## Method

The authors computed the time-averaged entangling power for two-site spin-1/2 and spin-1 Hamiltonians analytically, for finite-size XXZ chains using exact diagonalization with an eigenvalue-grouping algorithm that retains only diagonal-ensemble contributions, and for the two-magnon S-matrix in the thermodynamic limit by decomposing the six-vertex R-matrix into quantum logic gates (Identity, SWAP, and σ_z⊗σ_z). The entangling power was defined as the Haar-averaged linear entropy of output states from product-state inputs, normalized to range from 0 to 1.

## Key numbers

- Two-site spin-1/2 XXX model: time-averaged entangling power = 1/12 ≈ 0.0833 (minimum among two-qubit models)
- Two-site spin-1/2 XYZ model: time-averaged entangling power = 1/6 ≈ 0.1667 (maximum among two-qubit models)
- Two-site spin-1 XXX model: time-averaged entangling power = 13/54 ≈ 0.241
- Two-site spin-1 Ising model: time-averaged entangling power = 1/4 = 0.250
- Two-site spin-1 XXZ model at generic Δ: analytic formula avg{ep}(Δ) = (11Δ^4 + 189Δ^2 + 781) / [36(Δ^2 + 8)^2]
- Two-site spin-1 XX model (Δ=0): time-averaged entangling power = 725/2304 ≈ 0.315
- Two-site spin-1/2 XXZ/XX models: time-averaged entangling power = 5/36 ≈ 0.1389 (both equal due to coupling-independent eigenvectors)
- Two-site spin-1/2 Ising model: time-averaged entangling power = 1/9 ≈ 0.1111

## Context

This work extends the observation from high-energy scattering (Beane 2018, Low 2021) that entanglement extremization occurs at points of enhanced symmetry, applying it to quantum many-body systems. It contrasts with prior state-level entanglement diagnostics (Calabrese 2004, Vidal 2002) that characterize ground states rather than dynamics, and with symmetry-resolved entanglement (Goldstein 2017) that requires a priori knowledge of conserved charges. The results enable using entangling power as an operator-based symmetry witness in quantum simulations of spin-chain dynamics, without reference to any particular eigenstate or ground state.
