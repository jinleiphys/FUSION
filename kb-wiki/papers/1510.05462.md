# The calorimetric spectrum of the electron-capture decay of $^{163}$Ho. A preliminary analysis of the preliminary data

> It is in principle possible to measure directly the electron neutrino mass (or masses and mixing angles) in weak electron-capture decays. The optimal nuclide in this respect is $^{163}$Ho. The favoured experimental technique, currently pursued in various experiments (ECHo, HOLMES and NuMECS) is "calorimetric". The calorimetric energy spectrum is a sum over the unstable vacant orbitals, or "holes", left by the electrons weakly captured by the nucleus. We discuss the current progress in this field and analize the preliminary data. Our conclusion is that, as pointed out by Robertson, the contribution of two-hole states is not negligible. But --in strong contradistinction with the tacit conclusion of previous comparisons of theory and observations-- we find a quite satisfactory agreement. A crucial point is that, in the creation of secondary holes, electron shakeoff and not only electron shakeup must be taken into account.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, contrary to the tacit conclusion of previous comparisons between theory and observations (Robertson 2015; Faessler 2015), the contribution of two-hole states to the calorimetric spectrum of 163Ho electron-capture decay can be brought into satisfactory agreement with preliminary data, provided that electron shake-off (not only shake-up) is taken into account for the creation of secondary holes.

## Method

The authors performed a preliminary analysis of low-statistics calorimetric data from the ECHo and NuMECS collaborations, comparing the measured spectra in the N1 region (approximately 300–700 eV) to theoretical predictions that include both single-hole contributions (N1, N2, M1, M2) and two-hole contributions (N1O1 and N1N4/5). For the shake-off component of N1 capture with O1 shake-off, they applied the method of Intemann and Pollock (1967) using non-relativistic Coulomb wave functions with effective nuclear charges (Z_eff(N1)=43.2, Z_eff(O1)=24.5) to estimate the shape of the shake-off distribution, then convolved it with Breit-Wigner functions including natural hole widths and experimental resolution.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for 163Ho EC decay: Q = 2833 eV (with statistical uncertainty 30 eV and systematic uncertainty 15 eV)
- N1O1 two-hole peak position: E_c = 464 eV (sum of N1 and O1 binding energies)
- Natural width of N1O1 two-hole state: Γ(N1)+Γ(O1) = 11.4 eV
- Experimental resolution for ECHo data: Γ_exp = 8.4 eV (added in quadrature to natural widths)
- Experimental resolution for NuMECS data: Γ_exp = 16 eV
- Ratio of O1 shake-off to O1 shake-up areas (integrated up to E_c=Q): approximately 1.16
- Chosen width for N2 single-hole: Γ(N2) = 16 eV
- Chosen width for O1 single-hole: Γ(O1) = 6 eV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for calorimetric electron-capture neutrino mass measurements developed by De Rújula and Lusignoli (2015) and addresses the concern raised by Robertson (2015) that two-hole contributions are not negligible. It directly compares with state-of-the-art calculations by Faessler et al. (2015) that used relativistic, fully antisymmetrized wave functions but considered only shake-up (not shake-off) for secondary holes. The analysis enables future high-statistics experiments (ECHo, HOLMES, NuMECS) to properly model the full calorimetric spectrum, which is necessary for extracting a competitive neutrino mass limit from the spectral endpoint.
