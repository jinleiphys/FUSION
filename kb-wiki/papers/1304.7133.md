# Conserved number fluctuations in a hadron resonance gas model

> Net-baryon, net-charge and net-strangeness number fluctuations in high energy heavy-ion collisions are discussed within the framework of a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. Ratios of the conserved number susceptibilities calculated in HRG are being compared to the corresponding experimental measurements to extract information about the freeze-out condition and the phase structure of systems with strong interactions. We emphasize the importance of considering the actual experimental acceptances in terms of kinematics (pseudorapidity ($η$) and transverse momentum ($p_{T}$)), the detected charge state, effect of collective motion of particles in the system and the resonance decay contributions before comparisons are made to the theoretical calculations. In this work, based on HRG model, we report that the net-baryon number fluctuations are least affected by experimental acceptances compared to the net-charge and net-strangeness number fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, net-baryon number fluctuations are least affected by experimental acceptances (kinematic cuts, charge state selection, and resonance decay contributions) compared to net-charge and net-strangeness fluctuations, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific kinematic regions and model assumptions used in the study.

## Method

The authors compute ratios of conserved number susceptibilities (chi^(3)/chi^(2) and chi^(4)/chi^(2)) for net-baryon, net-charge, and net-strangeness within the HRG model using a grand canonical ensemble framework. They incorporate experimental acceptances by restricting integration ranges in pseudorapidity (eta) and transverse momentum (p_T), selecting specific charge states (|Q|=1, |Q|>1) and strangeness numbers (|S|=1, |S|>1), including collective flow effects via a boosted fireball with radial and longitudinal expansion, and modeling resonance decay contributions using a toy Monte Carlo simulation to estimate efficiency factors.

## Key numbers

* For net-charge at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with acceptance |eta|<0.5 and 0.2<p_T<2.0 GeV/c: chi_Q^(3)/chi_Q^(2)=0.025, chi_Q^(4)/chi_Q^(2)=1.342 (Table 1).
* For net-charge at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with acceptance |eta|<0.35 and 0.3<p_T<1.0 GeV/c: chi_Q^(3)/chi_Q^(2)=0.020, chi_Q^(4)/chi_Q^(2)=1.193 (Table 2).
* For net-kaon at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with acceptance |eta|<0.5 and 0.2<p_T<2.0 GeV/c: chi_K^(3)/chi_K^(2)=0.035, chi_K^(4)/chi_K^(2)=1.128 (Table 3).
* For net-proton at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with acceptance |eta|<0.5 and 0.4<p_T<0.8 GeV/c: chi_p^(3)/chi_p^(2)=0.139, chi_p^(4)/chi_p^(2)=0.997 (Table 4).
* The effect of collective flow on chi_x^(4)/chi_x^(2) for net-baryon, net-charge, and net-strangeness is less than 2-4% compared to static fireball values.
* The temperature parameterization used is T(mu_B)=a-b*mu_B^2-c*mu_B^4 with a=0.166+/-0.002 GeV, b=0.139+/-0.016 GeV^{-1}, c=0.053+/-0.021 GeV^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the use of the HRG model as a baseline for fluctuation observables in heavy-ion collisions, following prior studies by Gupta (2011), Gavai (2010), Karsch (2010), and Bazavov (2012) that linked higher moments of conserved number distributions to QCD susceptibilities. The paper emphasizes that experimental acceptances must be carefully accounted for when comparing HRG model predictions to data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at LHC, particularly for net-charge and net-strangeness, while net-baryon fluctuations are more robust. This enables more reliable extraction of freeze-out conditions and the search for the QCD critical point in the phase diagram.
