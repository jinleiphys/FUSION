# Relativistic Mean-Field Model with Scaled Hadron Masses and Couplings

> Here we continue to elaborate properties of the relativistic mean-field based model (SHMC) proposed in ref. [6] where hadron masses and coupling constants depend on the $σ$-meson field. The validity of approximations used in [6] is discussed. We additionally incorporate contribution of meson excitations to the equations of motion. We also estimate the effects of the particle width. It is demonstrated that the inclusion of the baryon-baryon hole and baryon-antibaryon loop terms, if performed perturbatively, destroys the consistency of the model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including baryon-baryon hole and baryon-antibaryon loop terms perturbatively destroys the consistency of the Scaled Hadron Mass-Coupling (SHMC) model, while incorporating meson excitation contributions self-consistently into the equations of motion (rather than perturbatively) preserves thermodynamic consistency.

## Method

The authors construct a pressure functional for the SHMC model that includes mean-field contributions from σ, ω, and ρ mesons, baryons (including nucleons, Δ resonances, and hyperons), and all meson excitations (π, K, η, K*, η', φ, and σ', ω', ρ' fluctuations). They derive equations of motion by minimizing the pressure with respect to the mean fields ω₀ and R₀ and the scaling variable f, then compare the perturbative treatment (where boson excitation terms are evaluated at mean-field solutions found without them) against a self-consistent treatment (where boson excitation terms contribute to the equations of motion). The effects of finite particle widths are estimated, and the consequences of including fermion loop terms are examined in an appendix.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon optical potential: the model predicts antinucleon optical potentials within the phenomenological range of -100 to -350 MeV, in contrast to the standard Walecka model.
- The η meson effective mass is computed using parameters Σ_{ηb} = 140 MeV and κ_{ηb} = 0.2 in the expression for m_η*².
- The scaling function Φ is defined as Φ = 1 - f, where f = g_{σN} χ_σ σ / m_N, with the relation Φ(σ) = [1 + g_{σN} σ / m_N]⁻¹.
- The nonlinear self-interaction potential U is expressed as U = m_N⁴ (b f³/3 + c f⁴/4), where b and c are parameters that accommodate realistic nuclear compressibility and effective nucleon mass at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the SHMC model of Khvorostukhin, Toneev, and Voskresensky (2006) by checking the validity of approximations used there, particularly the perturbative treatment of meson excitations and the neglect of baryon loop terms. The model follows the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (1991) in its simplest form, where hadron masses and couplings decrease with density via a universal scaling function. The paper contrasts with standard RMF models (Walecka, 1974) that use constant meson masses, and with approaches that increase meson effective masses with density (Heinz and Pradler, 2006; Friman and Schwenk, 2006). The results enable improved hydrodynamical calculations of heavy ion collisions across a broad energy range, planned as future work.
