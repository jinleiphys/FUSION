# Beyond the Neutron Drip-Line: The Unbound Oxygen Isotopes 25O and 26O

> The very neutron-rich oxygen isotopes 25O and 26O are investigated experimentally and theoret- ically. In this first R3B-LAND experiment, the unbound states are populated at GSI via proton- knockout reactions from 26F and 27F at relativistic energies around 450 MeV/nucleon. From the kinematically complete measurement of the decay into 24O plus one or two neutrons, the 25O ground- state energy and lifetime are determined, and upper limits for the 26O ground state are extracted. In addition, the results provide evidence for an excited state in 26O at around 4 MeV. The ex- perimental findings are compared to theoretical shell-model calculations based on chiral two- and three-nucleon (3N) forces, including for the first time residual 3N forces, which are shown to be amplified as valence neutrons are added.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the unbound oxygen isotopes 25O and 26O are populated via proton-knockout reactions, and while the ground-state energy and width of 25O are determined with moderate precision, only upper limits for the 26O ground-state energy and lifetime are extracted, with the caveat that the 26O ground state may lie at an energy as low as 25 ± 25 keV, potentially leading to a long-lived state that could constitute the first example of neutron radioactivity.

## Method

Unbound states in 25O and 26O were populated via one-proton knockout reactions from 26F and 27F beams at relativistic energies of 442 MeV/nucleon and 414 MeV/nucleon, respectively, using the R3B-LAND setup at GSI. The decay products (24O plus one or two neutrons) were measured kinematically complete, and the relative-energy spectra were reconstructed from the momenta of outgoing fragments and neutrons detected by the LAND neutron detector. The 25O ground-state resonance was fitted with a Breit-Wigner distribution including an energy-dependent width and a non-resonant background, while the 26O ground state was analyzed via a simultaneous statistical analysis of one-neutron and two-neutron coincidence data using response matrices and a Bayesian approach.

## Key numbers

- 25O ground-state resonance energy: Er = 725 (+54 / -29) keV, with width Γ = 20 (+60 / -20) keV
- Averaged 25O ground-state energy (this work combined with Hoffman et al. 2008): Er = 768 (+19 / -9) keV, width Γ = 160 (+30 / -30) keV
- 26O ground-state energy: 25 ± 25 keV (most probable fit), with a Bayesian interval from 0 to 40/120 keV at 68%/95% confidence level
- Upper limit on 26O ground-state lifetime: ≤ 5.7 ns at 95% confidence level
- 26O excited state energy: 4225 (+227 / -176) keV
- 25O ground-state lifetime lower limit: ≥ 8.2 × 10^(-12) s (from this work)
- Averaged 25O ground-state lifetime: 4.1 (+0.9 / -0.6) × 10^(-12) s
- Beam energies: 442 MeV/nucleon for 26F, 414 MeV/nucleon for 27F
- LAND neutron detector efficiency: 92% for single neutrons

## Context

This work extends previous studies of unbound oxygen isotopes beyond the neutron drip line, specifically following the measurement of the 25O ground-state resonance by Hoffman et al. (2008) and the 26O ground-state position by Lunderberg et al. (2012). The results are compared to shell-model calculations based on chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon (3N) forces, including for the first time residual 3N forces, which are shown to be amplified as valence neutrons are added. The upper limit on the 26O lifetime addresses the speculation by Grigorenko et al. (2011) about possible neutron radioactivity in 26O and 28O, and the paper suggests that a more precise lifetime measurement could be performed at facilities like RIBF at RIKEN using the SAMURAI setup.
