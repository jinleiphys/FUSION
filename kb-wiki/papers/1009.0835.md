# Combined Influence of Off-diagonal System Tensors and Potential Valley Returning of the Optimal Path

> The two-dimensional barrier passage is studied in the framework of Langevin statistical reactive dynamics. The optimal incident angle for a particle diffusing in the dissipative non-orthogonal environment with various strengths of coupling between the two degrees of freedom is systematically calculated. The optimal diffusion path of the particle in a non-Ohmic damping system is revealed to have a probability to return to the potential valley under the combined influence of the off-diagonal system tensors.

## Key claim
The optimal diffusion path in a two-dimensional non-Ohmic damping system can return to the potential valley direction (incident angle of zero) under the combined influence of off-diagonal system tensors and the coupling between degrees of freedom, a behavior not seen in the Ohmic damping case where the optimal path typically deviates from the valley.

## Method
The author performed Langevin statistical reactive dynamics calculations for a particle surmounting a two-dimensional potential barrier, systematically varying the off-diagonal components of the inertia, friction, and potential-curvature tensors. The optimal incident angle was computed from the critical initial velocity using analytical expressions derived from Laplace-transformed coupled Langevin equations, first for Ohmic damping (constant tensors) and then generalized to non-Ohmic damping by replacing friction components with a power-law spectral density \(J_{ij}(\omega)=\gamma_{ij}(\omega/\omega_r)^\delta\).

## Key numbers
- Optimal incident angle varies almost linearly from positive to negative as \(\omega_{12}\) or \(\beta_{12}\) increases, while \(m_{12}\) has the opposite effect (Fig. 1, no explicit numerical values given for the angle range).
- Critical initial velocity values for Fig. 4(a) parameters at incident angle \(\Phi=0.258\,\text{rad}\): \(v^c_0|_{\delta=1.8} \cong 2.6417\), \(v^c_0|_{\delta=0.6} \cong 2.3477\), \(v^c_0|_{\delta=1.0} \cong 1.8426\), and \(v^c_0|_{\delta=1.1} \cong 1.8251\).
- Diagonal tensor parameters used throughout: \(m_{11}=1.5\), \(m_{22}=2.0\), \(\beta_{11}=1.8\), \(\beta_{22}=1.2\), \(\omega_{11}=-2.0\), \(\omega_{22}=1.5\) (dimensionless units).
- Non-Ohmic calculations used \(\omega_{12}=-0.5\) and \(m_{12}=0.6\) with varying effective friction constants \(\gamma_{ij}\).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous two-dimensional generalization of one-dimensional Brownian motion models for nuclear fusion reactions (Wang, 2009; Wang, 2010). It systematically investigates the role of non-orthogonal system tensors, which had been noted in earlier studies (e.g., "off-dia1", "off-dia2") but never directly analyzed via the optimal incident angle. The potential valley returning behavior in non-Ohmic damping provides a new criterion for selecting collision geometries and dissipative environments in experimental studies of massive nuclei fusion and super-heavy element synthesis.
