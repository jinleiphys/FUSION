# Khuri-Treiman equations for $3π$ decays of particles with spin

> Khuri-Treiman equations have proven to be a useful theoretical tool in the analysis of 3-body decays, specially into the $3π$ final state. In this work we present in full detail the necessary generalization of the formalism to study the decays of particles with arbitrary spin, parity, and charge conjugation. To this extent, we find it most convenient to work with helicity amplitudes instead of the so-called invariant amplitudes, specially when dealing with the unitarity relations. The isobar expansions in the three possible ($s$-, $t$-, and $u$-) final channels are related with the appropriate crossing matrices. We pay special attention to the kinematical singularities and constraints of the helicity amplitudes, showing that these can be derived by means of the crossing matrix.

## Key claim

The paper presents a full generalization of the Khuri-Treiman formalism to three-pion decays of particles with arbitrary spin, parity, and charge conjugation, using helicity amplitudes rather than invariant amplitudes to handle the unitarity relations and crossing symmetry more conveniently. The authors emphasize that while the formalism has been applied mostly to pseudoscalar and vector meson decays, the generalization to arbitrary quantum numbers requires careful treatment of kinematical singularities and constraints, which they derive via the crossing matrix.

## Method

The authors formulate the decay \(X_J \to 3\pi\) in a scattering picture \(X_J \pi \to \pi\pi\) and define helicity amplitudes in the \(s\)-, \(t\)-, and \(u\)-channel center-of-mass frames. They relate these amplitudes through crossing symmetry using the Trueman-Wick crossing relations, which involve Wigner \(d\)-functions with an angle \(\omega\) determined by the Lorentz transformation between frames. The isobar expansions in the three channels are connected via crossing matrices, and the authors derive reduced isobar amplitudes free of kinematical singularities by factoring out kinematic functions \(K_\lambda(s,t)\) that depend on the spin and naturality of the decaying particle. Unitarity relations are then imposed on these reduced amplitudes to obtain the Khuri-Treiman integral equations.

## Key numbers

* The exponent \(Y_X = J - \frac{1}{2}[1 + \eta_X]\) (where \(\eta_X = P(-1)^J\) is the naturality) determines the kinematic factor \(K_\lambda(s,t)\) for a particle of spin \(J\) and parity \(P\).
* The Kibble function \(\phi(s,t,u) = s t u - m^2 (M^2 - m^2)^2\) defines the physical region boundaries via \(\phi(s,t,u) = 0\).
* The crossing angle \(\omega\) satisfies \(\cos\omega = n(s,t) / [4\sqrt{st}\, p(s) p(t)]\) and \(\sin\omega = M\sqrt{\phi(s,t,u)} / [2\sqrt{st}\, p(s) p(t)]\), with \(n(s,t) = (s+M^2-m^2)(t+M^2-m^2) - 2M^2(M^2-m^2)\).
* For the \(a_1\) (\(J^{PC}=1^{++}\)) example, the kinematic singularity matrix is \(\mathbb{K}(s,t) = \text{diag}(s^{-1/2}\sin\theta_s\,\lambda_\pi^{1/2}(s),\; \lambda_X^{-1/2}(s))\), where \(\lambda_X(s) = \lambda(s, M^2, m^2)\) and \(\lambda_\pi(s) = \lambda(s, m^2, m^2)\).
* The kinematic constraint at threshold and pseudo-threshold for the \(a_1\) is: \(\sqrt{2}\, n(s,t)\,\hat{\mathcal{A}}^{(s)}_+(s,t,u) - M\,\hat{\mathcal{A}}^{(s)}_0(s,t,u) \propto \lambda_X(s)\) as \(s \to (M\pm m)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Khuri-Treiman formalism, originally developed for \(K\to 3\pi\) decays by Khuri and Treiman (1960) and later applied to \(\eta\to 3\pi\) by Neveu and collaborators (1970), to particles with arbitrary spin, parity, and charge conjugation. The formalism has been previously applied to light and heavy meson decays by Kambor (1995), Anisovich (1996), Descotes-Genon (2014), Guo (2015, 2016), Colangelo (2016), Niecknig (2012, 2015), Danilkin (2014), Isken (2017), Pilloni (2016), Albaladejo (2017), and Gasser (2018), among others. The generalization enables the analysis of three-pion decays of resonances with higher spin (e.g., \(a_1\), \(\pi_2\)) and provides a systematic framework for incorporating crossing symmetry and kinematic constraints in dispersive treatments of such decays.
