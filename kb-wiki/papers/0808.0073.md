# X(3872) and Other Possible Heavy Molecular States

> We perform a systematic study of the possible molecular states composed of a pair of heavy mesons such as $D\bar D$, $D^\ast\bar D$, $D^\ast \bar D^\ast$ in the framework of the meson exchange model. The exchanged mesons include the pseudoscalar, scalar and vector mesons. Through our investigation, we find that (1) the structure X(3764) is not a molecular state; (2) There exists strong attraction in the range $r < 1$ fm for the $D^*\bar D^*$ system with $J=0, 1$. If future experiments confirm $Z^+(4051)$ as a loosely bound molecular state, its quantum number is probably $J^{P}=0^+$. Its partner state $Φ^{**0}$ may be searched for in the $π^0χ_{c1}$ channel; (3) The vector meson exchange provides strong attraction in the $D^\ast \bar D$ channel together with the pion exchange. A bound state solution exists with a reasonable cutoff parameter $Λ\sim 1.4$ GeV. X(3872) may be accommodated as a molecular state dynamically although drawing a very definite conclusion needs further investigation; (4) The $B^\ast \bar B$ molecular state exists.

## Key claim

X(3872) may be accommodated as a molecular state dynamically, although drawing a very definite conclusion needs further investigation; the study finds that a bound state solution exists for the D*D̅ channel with a reasonable cutoff parameter Λ ∼ 1.4 GeV, but the authors stop short of claiming X(3872) is definitively a molecular state.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study of possible molecular states composed of pairs of heavy mesons (D D̅, D* D̅, D* D̅*) using the meson exchange model, including pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector meson exchanges. They derived effective potentials in momentum space from tree-level effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy quark symmetries, introduced a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Λ to regularize the potentials, performed Fourier transformation to obtain coordinate-space potentials, and solved the Schrödinger equation numerically using the MATSLISE package to search for bound state solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the D* D̅* system with J=0,1, strong attraction exists in the range r < 1 fm
- A bound state solution for the D* D̅ channel exists with cutoff parameter Λ ∼ 1.4 GeV
- For the Φₛ₁⁰ state (Dₛ⁻ Dₛ⁺) in the D-D̅ system: binding energy E = -10.14 MeV with Λ = 0.58 GeV, r_rms = 1.84 fm; and E = -3.46 MeV with Λ = 0.60 GeV, r_rms = 2.80 fm
- For the Φ₈⁰ state in the D-D̅ system: binding energy E = -23.42 MeV with Λ = 0.50 GeV, r_rms = 1.44 fm; and E = -4.93 MeV with Λ = 0.53 GeV, r_rms = 2.52 fm
- For the Ωₛ₁⁰ state (Bₛ⁰ B̅ₛ⁰) in the B-B̅ system: binding energy E = -9.24 MeV with Λ = 0.70 GeV, r_rms = 1.84 fm; and E = -1.78 MeV with Λ = 0.72 GeV, r_rms = 2.86 fm
- For the Ω₈⁰ state in the B-B̅ system: binding energy E = -7.13 MeV with Λ = 1.10 GeV, r_rms = 1.95 fm; and E = -23.34 MeV with Λ = 1.15 GeV, r_rms = 1.23 fm
- Z⁺(4051) mass: (4051 ± 14 +20 -41) MeV, width: (82 +21+47 -17-22) MeV
- Z⁺(4248) mass: (4248 ± 14 +44+180 -29-35) MeV, width: (177 +54+316 -39-61) MeV

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange model approach previously applied to X(3872) and Z⁺(4430) by Liu et al. (2008) to systematically study three types of heavy meson pairs (P-P, V-V, P-V) for both charmed and bottom sectors. The study was motivated by experimental observations from Belle (Z⁺(4051), Z⁺(4248)) and BES (X(3764)), and builds on earlier theoretical work by Voloshin and Okun (1976), De Rujula, Georgi and Glashow (1977), Tornqvist (1994), and Dubynskiy and Voloshin (2008). The results enable future experimental searches for predicted partner states such as Φ**⁰ in the π⁰χc1 channel and provide a framework for interpreting near-threshold charmonium-like states as heavy molecular candidates.
