# On Participants Number Fluctuations for Given Centrality AA-Interactions in the Classical Glauber Approach

> In the framework of the classical Glauber approach the exact analytical expression for the variance of the number of participants (wounded nucleons) for given centrality AA interactions is presented. It's shown, that in the case of nucleus-nucleus collisions along with the optical approximation term the additional "contact" term appears. The numerical calculations for PbPb collisions at SPS energies show that at intermediate values of the impact parameter the "optical" and "contact" terms contributions to the variance of the participants number are of the same order and their sum is in a good agreement with the results of independent MC simulations of this process. The correlation between the numbers of participants in colliding nuclei is taken into account. In particular it's demonstrated that in PbPb collisions at SPS energies the variance of the total number of participants approximately three times exceeds the Poisson one in the impact parameter region 10-12 Fm. The fluctuations of the number of collisions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact analytical expression for the variance of the number of participants (wounded nucleons) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at fixed impact parameter, showing that the classical Glauber approach yields an additional "contact" term beyond the optical approximation, which depends on the shape of the inelastic nucleon-nucleon interaction profile rather than only on the total cross-section.

## Method

The author starts from the classical probabilistic Glauber approach of Bialas (1976), defining indicator variables for each nucleon's interaction status and averaging over nucleon positions in both nuclei using profile functions. The variance is computed analytically by evaluating the second moment of the sum of these indicator variables, separating the diagonal and off-diagonal contributions, and then performing the averaging over the nuclear profile functions. Numerical calculations for lead-lead collisions at SPS energies use either a "black disk" or Gaussian approximation for the nucleon-nucleon interaction probability, with Woods-Saxon nuclear profiles, and are compared to independent Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

* For PbPb collisions at SPS energies (sigma_NN_in = 31 mb, r_N = 1 Fm), the variance of the total number of participants is approximately three times larger than the Poisson variance in the impact parameter region 10-12 Fm.
* At intermediate impact parameter values for PbPb collisions, the "optical" and "contact" term contributions to the variance of participants in one nucleus are of the same order.
* The correlator between numbers of participants in the two colliding nuclei contributes about half of the variance in one nucleus at intermediate impact parameter values, and its relative contribution is even greater at b >= 10 Fm.
* For peripheral PbPb collisions (b > 7 Fm), the variance of participants in one nucleus exceeds the Poisson variance due to the contact term.

## Context

This work extends the classical Glauber approach of Bialas (1976) for nucleus-nucleus collisions by providing an exact analytical variance expression, going beyond the optical approximation which only gives the correct mean value. The results show that the "contact" term corresponds to loop diagrams in the quantum Glauber approach discussed by Boreskov (1988), Pak (1979), and Braun (1987-1990), and the good agreement with Monte Carlo simulations enables use of the developed algorithm for future experimental setup calculations.
