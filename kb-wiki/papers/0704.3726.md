# Pion-mass dependence of three-nucleon observables

> We use an effective field theory (EFT) which contains only short-range interactions to study the dependence of a variety of three-nucleon observables on the pion mass. The pion-mass dependence of input quantities in our ``pionless'' EFT is obtained from a recent chiral EFT calculation. To the order we work at, these quantities are the 1S0 scattering length and effective range, the deuteron binding energy, the 3S1 effective range, and the binding energy of one three-nucleon bound state. The chiral EFT input we use has the inverse 3S1 and 1S0 scattering lengths vanishing at mpi_c=197.8577 MeV. At this ``critical'' pion mass, the triton has infinitely many excited states with an accumulation point at the three-nucleon threshold. We compute the binding energies of these states up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the pionless EFT and study the convergence pattern of the EFT in the vicinity of the critical pion mass. Furthermore, we use the pionless EFT to predict how doublet and quartet nd scattering lengths depend on mpi in the region between the physical pion mass and mpi=mpi_c.

## Key claim

Using a pionless effective field theory (EFT) with input from chiral EFT, the authors compute three-nucleon observables as functions of the pion mass up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), finding that near the critical pion mass where both inverse S-wave scattering lengths vanish, the limiting factor for accuracy is corrections scaling as κR (momentum times range) rather than the expansion parameter R/|a|, which becomes very small.

## Method

The authors used a pionless EFT (EFT(π̸)) that describes nucleons as point particles with zero-range interactions, expanded in powers of R/|a| where R is the range of the two-body potential and a is the scattering length. They obtained the pion-mass dependence of the 1S0 and 3S1 scattering lengths, effective ranges, deuteron binding energy, and one three-nucleon bound state from a chiral EFT calculation at next-to-leading order (NLO). The three-nucleon observables were computed by solving integral equations for the neutron-deuteron K-matrix up to N2LO in the pionless EFT, using the two-body propagator expanded to second order in the effective range.

## Key numbers

- Critical pion mass where inverse 3S1 and 1S0 scattering lengths vanish: mπc = 197.8577 MeV
- Ratio of successive Efimov state binding energies at leading order: B3(n)/B3(n+1) ≈ 515.035
- For the NLO χEFT input, the ratio B3(0)/B3(1) = 543 and B3(1)/B3(2) = 5.2 × 10^2
- At NLO in the pionless EFT, the ratio B3(n)/B3(n+1) receives corrections of at most 0.1% for all n ≠ 0
- The effective ranges in both spin channels remain close to 2 fm throughout the region of interest
- The critical pion mass including nucleon mass dependence is approximately 220 MeV, with accuracy limited to two digits

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study of Braaten (2003) and the detailed investigation of Epelbaum (2006) by computing three-nucleon observables up to N2LO in the pionless EFT, rather than only at leading order. The authors use the same NLO chiral EFT input as Epelbaum (2006) but incorporate effective range corrections following the formalism of Platter (2006a, 2006b). This enables predictions for the doublet and quartet nd scattering lengths as functions of pion mass between the physical value and the critical value, and demonstrates the convergence pattern of the pionless EFT near the limit cycle where the triton has infinitely many excited states.
