# Polarization phenomena in the reaction $e^+ + e^- \to p + \bar p +π^0$ in frame of the non-resonant mechanism

> The dependence of the nucleon polarization in the reaction $e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar{N} +π^0 $ over different invariant variables in frame of the non-resonant mechanism, has been derived. The nucleon polarization is expressed in terms of six invariant complex amplitudes, assuming the conservation of the hadron electromagnetic currents and the P-invariance of the hadron electromagnetic interaction. An inclusive experimental setup when the proton (or the antiproton) and the pion are detected in coincidence is considered. Numerical estimations were performed for the so called normal polarization in the energy range from threshold up to $s=16$ GeV$^2$, using a specific parametrization of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors and taking into account the unpolarized differential cross section of the non-resonant mechanism, as previously calculated.

## Key claim

The nucleon normal polarization in the reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar{N} + \pi^0\) is derived within the non-resonant mechanism only, and the numerical predictions depend radically on the choice of nucleon electromagnetic form factor parametrization, meaning that the polarization measurements could probe the phase difference between electric and magnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors derived the hadron tensor for a polarized nucleon in the reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar{N} + \pi^0\) assuming conservation of hadron electromagnetic currents and P-invariance, expressing the nucleon polarization in terms of six invariant complex amplitudes. They performed analytical integration over two of the four invariant variables (\(s_1, s_2, t_1, t_2\)) to obtain double and single differential distributions for the normal polarization, using a specific parametrization of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (labeled the "old" version) and the unpolarized differential cross section of the non-resonant mechanism previously calculated in Gakh (2022).

## Key numbers

- The energy range considered is from threshold up to \(s = 16\) GeV\(^2\).
- Numerical calculations were performed at \(s = 5, 6, 10, 16\) GeV\(^2\) for both \(\pi^0 p \bar{p}\) and \(\pi^0 n \bar{n}\) channels.
- The normal polarization \(P^N\) changes sign near \(s = 8\) GeV\(^2\) for the \(\pi^0 p \bar{p}\) channel but does not change sign for the \(\pi^0 n \bar{n}\) channel.
- The spin-dependent part of the cross section \(d\sigma^N/dx_{12}\) is positive for the \(n \bar{n} \pi^0\) channel and changes sign at \(s \approx 8\) GeV\(^2\) for the \(p \bar{p} \pi^0\) channel.
- The polarization magnitude for the \(n \bar{n} \pi^0\) channel is larger than for the \(p \bar{p} \pi^0\) channel, except for anomalously large proton polarization at \(s = 16\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Gakh (2022) for the unpolarized reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to N + \bar{N} + \pi^0\) to polarization observables, specifically the normal nucleon polarization. The reaction is currently accessible at BESIII and is related to crossing-symmetry reactions studied at PANDA (FAIR) and HADES. The results enable experimental determination of the phase difference between electric and magnetic nucleon form factors (\(Arg[G_E G_M^*]\)) in the timelike region, complementing the spacelike measurements from Jefferson Laboratory (JLab-GEP collaboration) that revealed different electric and magnetic distributions in the proton.
