# Charmonium in electromagnetic and vortical fields

> Due to larger mass and earlier production, heavy quark(quarkonium) can be sensitive probes to investigate the fast decaying electromagnetic and vortical fields produced in heavy-ion collisions. The non-relativistic Schroedinger-like equation for heavy quarks under strong electromagnetic fields in the rotating frame is deduced and used to construct the two-body equation for the charmonium system. The effective potential between charm and anti-charm becomes anisotropic in electromagnetic and vortical fields, especially along the direction of the Lorentz force. The vorticity will affect this asymmetry property largely and catalyze the transition from strong interaction dominant bound state to electromagnetic and vortical interaction controlled anisotropic bound state. It is possible to be realized in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charmonium bound states in heavy-ion collisions can transition from a strong-interaction-dominated state to an anisotropic state controlled by electromagnetic and vortical interactions, but this transition is conditional on the magnetic field being strong enough to form a new potential well and the vorticity acting as a perturbation that deepens or shallows that well.

## Method

The authors derived a non-relativistic Schrödinger-like equation for heavy quarks under strong electromagnetic fields in a rotating frame, starting from the Dirac equation in curved spacetime. They constructed a two-body Schrödinger equation for the charmonium system, separated the Hamiltonian into an electromagnetic main part and a rotational perturbation, and used standard quantum mechanical perturbation theory to treat the vortical terms, taking the average center-of-mass coordinate as an input.

## Key numbers

- The maximum magnetic field in semi-central Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy reaches \(5 m_\pi^2\) and in semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies reaches almost \(70 m_\pi^2\), where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
- The average vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma reaches \(\omega \sim 10^{21}/\text{s}\).
- The formation time for charm quarks is \(\tau_c \sim 1/2m_c \approx 0.06\) fm/c and for bottom quarks \(\tau_b \approx 0.02\) fm/c.
- The strength of the magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can reach \(eB \approx 70 m_\pi^2\), which is much larger than the vortical field \(m_Q \omega \approx m_\pi^2\) for charm quarks.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quarkonium in electromagnetic fields (Marasinghe 2011, Alford 2013, Cho 2014, Iwasaki 2021) and in vortical fields (Chen 2020) by combining both effects in a unified non-relativistic framework. It builds on the potential model derived from pNRQCD (Brambilla 1999) and the observation of global polarization of \(\Lambda\) hyperons by the STAR Collaboration (2017). The results enable future studies of the dynamic dissociation of anisotropic charmonium states in the quark-gluon plasma.
