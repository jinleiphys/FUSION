# Mixing and Screening of Photons and Gluons in a Color Superconductor

> We calculate the Debye and Meissner masses of photons and gluons for a spin-one color superconductor in the polar and color-spin-locked phases as well as for a spin-zero color superconductor in the 2SC and color-flavor-locked phases. A general derivation for photon-gluon mixing is provided in terms of the QCD partition function. We show qualitatively and quantitatively which of the gauge bosons attain a mass via the Anderson-Higgs mechanism. Contrary to the spin-zero phases, both spin-one phases exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, contrary to spin-zero color-superconducting phases (2SC and CFL), both spin-one phases (polar and color-spin-locked) exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect, but this result depends on the specific symmetry breaking pattern of the order parameter in each phase and the resulting mixing between photons and gluons.

## Method

The authors compute the Debye and Meissner masses of photons and gluons from the polarization tensors in the zero-energy, low-momentum limit using the QCD partition function with a diquark source term to account for color superconductivity. They derive a general formalism for photon-gluon mixing and apply it to four phases: 2SC, color-flavor-locked (CFL), polar, and color-spin-locked (CSL), using a group-theoretical argument based on the invariance of the gap matrix under subgroups of SU(3)_c x U(1)_em to determine mixing angles and screening properties.

## Key numbers

- For the 2SC phase: the mixing angle is given by cos^2(theta) = Pi_88/(Pi_88 + Pi_gg) with eta = -1/sqrt(3) for the new charge generator Q_tilde = Q + eta T_8
- For the CFL phase: the mixing angle coefficient is eta = 2/sqrt(3) for Q_tilde = Q + eta T_8
- For the polar phase: the mixing angle coefficient is eta = -2*sqrt(3)*q where q is the electric charge of the quark
- For the CSL phase: all coefficients kappa_1 through kappa_8 and kappa vanish, indicating no unbroken residual symmetry

## Context

This work extends previous studies of photon-gluon mixing in color superconductors by Litim and Manuel (2001) and by the authors' own earlier work on spin-one pairing (Schmitt, 2002). It provides a unified treatment of screening in both spin-zero phases (2SC and CFL, studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek, 1999) and spin-one phases (polar and CSL, analogous to phases in superfluid Helium-3 as described by Vollhardt and Wolfle, 1990). The results enable future calculations of transport properties and electromagnetic signatures of color-superconducting matter in neutron star interiors.
