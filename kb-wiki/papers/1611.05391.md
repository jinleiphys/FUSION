# From gluon topology to chiral anomaly: Emergent phenomena in quark-gluon plasma

> Heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and the LHC have found a new emergent phase of QCD, a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) that is distinctively different from either the low temperature hadron phase or the very high temperature weakly coupled plasma phase. Highly nontrivial emergent phenomena occur in such sQGP and two examples will be discussed in this contribution: the magnetic component of sQGP that stems from topologically nontrivial configurations in the gluon sector; and the anomalous chiral transport that arises as macroscopic manifestation of microscopic chiral anomaly in the quark sector. For both examples, their important roles in explaining pertinent heavy-ion data will be emphasized.

## Key claim

The strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) found at RHIC and the LHC, spanning temperatures of about 1 to 3 times the transition temperature Tc ~ 165 MeV, exhibits two emergent phenomena: a magnetic component from chromo-magnetic monopoles in the gluon sector and anomalous chiral transport from the microscopic chiral anomaly in the quark sector; both are claimed to be essential for explaining specific heavy-ion collision data, though the quantitative predictions for the chiral magnetic effect still carry theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The paper reviews two theoretical frameworks. For the magnetic component, the author describes the "magnetic scenario" where chromo-magnetic monopoles are abundant in the near-Tc plasma, and presents the CUJET3.0 simulation framework that implements nonperturbative near-Tc enhancement of jet-medium coupling by introducing monopole components and semi-QGP suppression, constrained by lattice QCD data. For anomalous chiral transport, the author describes the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) simulation that solves anomalous hydrodynamic equations with second-order viscous terms for chiral fermion currents in QGP with magnetic fields, built on top of the VISHNew bulk hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key numbers

- The sQGP temperature range is about 1 to 3 times the parton/hadron transition temperature Tc ~ 165 MeV.
- The normalized entropy density s/T^3 increases by almost an order of magnitude in a narrow window just around Tc, while the Polyakov loop stays well below unity even at 2Tc.
- CUJET3.0 with only one essential parameter successfully describes experimental data for seven sets of single hadron observables, including light hadrons' R_AA and v_2 at RHIC (Au+Au sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) and LHC (Pb+Pb sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV), D meson R_AA and v_2 at LHC, and B meson R_AA at LHC.
- The CUJET3.0 results show a prominent peak for the normalized jet transport coefficient hat{q}/T^3 and a strong minimum for eta/s, both located in the near-Tc regime.
- The AVFD simulations show that chiral magnetic effect predictions are quantitatively in the right ballpark of observed correlations from STAR data, with green bands reflecting current theoretical uncertainty in the initial axial charge generated by gluonic field fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the "semi-QGP" concept by Pisarski (2009) and the magnetic monopole scenario first conjectured by Liao and Shuryak (2006, 2008), building on lattice evidence for monopoles in pure gauge theories by Chernodub (2006), D'Alessandro (2007), and Bonati (2013). The anomalous chiral transport discussion builds on the anomalous hydrodynamic framework by Son (2009) and the chiral magnetic effect reviews by Kharzeev (2015) and Liao (2014). The CUJET3.0 framework enables systematic comparison of jet quenching observables with data from RHIC and LHC, while the AVFD framework enables quantitative predictions for the chiral magnetic effect that can be tested against STAR measurements and future isobaric collision experiments.
