# Two and Three Nucleon Forces

> Chiral symmetry allows two and three nucleon forces to be treated in a single theoretical framework. We discuss two new features of this research programme at $\cO(q^4)$ and the consistency of the overall chiral picture.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry provides a unified theoretical framework for two- and three-nucleon forces, but the expected chiral hierarchy (where higher-order terms should be smaller) is violated in the isoscalar central channel: the $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$ central potential $V_C^+$ is about 10 times larger than the $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ $V_C^-$ at 2.5 fm, and the $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$ drift potential can be comparable to $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$ spin-orbit terms in the isospin-even channel.

## Method

The paper reviews the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) expansion of two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces up to $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$, comparing heavy-baryon and covariant formulations. For the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP), three families of diagrams are classified: family I (minimal chiral symmetry, beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$), family II (pion-pion correlations, $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$), and family III (dependent on low-energy constants, beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$). For the three-nucleon force, the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential (TPE-3NP) is constructed from $\pi N$ subamplitudes $D^\pm$ and $B^\pm$, with strength coefficients determined from known parameters and LECs extracted from $\pi N$ subthreshold coefficients. Drift effects arising from the motion of the two-body center of mass within a three-body system are derived from covariant amplitudes in the linear gradient approximation.

## Key numbers

- The $\mathcal{O}(q^3)+\mathcal{O}(q^4)$ strength coefficients for the TPE-3NP are: $C_1^+ = 0.794$, $C_2^+ = -2.118$, $C_3^+ = 0.011$, $C_1^- = 0.691$, $C_2^- = -0.025$, $C_3^- = 0.021$, using $\sigma_{CD} = 60$ MeV and LECs from $d_{01}^+ = 1.14 \, m_\pi^{-3}$ and $b_{00}^- = 10.36 \, m_\pi^{-2}$.
- The ratio between $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$ and $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$ contributions to $V_C^+$ is larger than 0.5 for distances smaller than 2.5 fm.
- $V_C^+$ is about 10 times larger than $V_C^-$ at 2.5 fm, despite $V_C^-$ beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ and $V_C^+$ at $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$.
- The chiral expansion scale is set by $q << 1$ GeV, with the free $\pi N$ amplitude beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q)$ and the OPEP beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q^0)$.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory program for nuclear forces developed by groups using both heavy-baryon (FetM, HB) and covariant (BL, HR, HRR) approaches, and compares with earlier three-nucleon force results from TM and CDR. The paper identifies a persistent violation of chiral hierarchy in isoscalar channels, traced to large low-energy constants dynamically generated by delta intermediate states, suggesting that explicit inclusion of delta degrees of freedom in a covariant calculation (as studied by delta) could resolve the puzzle. The drift effects derived here (Rdrift) provide a new model-independent $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$ correction to two-body interactions within three-body systems, enhancing the role of $P$ waves in trinuclei.
