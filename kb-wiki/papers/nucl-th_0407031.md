# To what extent does the self-consistent mean-field exist?

> A non-convergent difficulty near level-repulsive region is discussed within the self-consistent mean-field theory. It is shown by numerical and analytic studies that the mean-field is not realized in the many-fermion system when quantum fluctuations coming from two-body residual interaction and quadrupole deformation are larger than an energy difference between two avoided crossing orbits. An analytic condition indicating a limitation of the mean-field concept is derived for the first time.

## Key claim

The self-consistent mean-field (Hartree-Fock) description of many-fermion systems breaks down near level-repulsive regions when the quantum fluctuation from two-body residual interaction and quadrupole deformation exceeds the energy difference between two avoided-crossing single-particle orbits; an analytic condition for this breakdown is derived for the first time.

## Method

The authors solve the quadratic constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) equation with the Gogny D1S interaction for the nucleus ⁶⁶Se using a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis. They analyze the non-convergent behavior of the CHF iteration near level crossing by constructing a 2×2 truncated Hamiltonian in the representation of the two interacting single-particle orbits and deriving recurrence relations for the off-diagonal matrix elements between successive iterations.

## Key numbers

- The missing region in the potential energy surface of ⁶⁶Se appears near quadrupole deformation ⟨Q̂⟩ = q ≈ 178 fm², where the CHF calculation becomes non-convergent.
- The non-convergent case studied in detail uses a constraint parameter μ = 150 fm².
- The edge point of convergence is at q₀ = 178 fm².
- The ratio h^{(2)}_{A,A+1}/h^{(1)}_{A,A+1} (off-diagonal Hamiltonian between first and second iterations) is positive in the convergent region, reaches zero at the edge point, and becomes negative in the non-convergent region.

## Context

This work extends numerical observations of non-convergence in constrained mean-field calculations (Dobaczewski, 2000) by providing the first analytic condition for the breakdown of the mean-field concept near level repulsion. The result clarifies why the competition between one-body potential and two-body residual interaction determines the applicability of the CHF method, and it opens the question of whether introducing "diabatic" orbits (Struczinski, 1977; Dobaczewski, 2000) or using dynamical constrained operators from the self-consistent collective coordinate method (SCC) can remedy the difficulty.
