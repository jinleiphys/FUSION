# Deuteron-${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ scattering using nucleon-${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ optical potentials fitted to four-body amplitudes

> Deuteron-${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ reactions in the 15 to 40 MeV range are studied using a three-body model where the constructed nonlocal optical potentials rely on rigorous nucleon-${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ scattering calculations. The differential cross section for the elastic scattering and neutron transfer reaction is predicted quite well up to 90 deg scattering angles. The importance of the Pauli term in complex potentials is demonstrated.

# Deuteron-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) scattering using nucleon-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) optical potentials fitted to four-body amplitudes

## Key claim

The authors construct nonlocal nucleon-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) optical potentials from rigorous four-body scattering calculations and use them in a three-body model to predict deuteron-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) elastic scattering and neutron transfer cross sections, finding that including a Pauli-repulsive term in the optical potential is essential for reproducing the shape of angular distributions at intermediate and backward angles, though discrepancies remain beyond 90 degrees.

## Method

The authors first solve the four-nucleon Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for nucleon-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) elastic scattering amplitudes using the inside-nonlocal outside-Yukawa (INOY04) two-nucleon potential. They then fit nonlocal optical potentials of Gaussian form, with partial-wave-dependent parameters, to reproduce those amplitudes over 13–30 MeV. These potentials are inserted into three-body AGS equations to compute deuteron-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) elastic scattering, the neutron transfer reaction \(^{3}\mathrm{He}(d,p)^{4}\mathrm{He}\), and deuteron breakup, with and without an additional Pauli-repulsive term in the \(S\)-wave channels.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energies studied: 14.6, 19.7, 24.9, 30.0, 34.9, and 39.9 MeV, compared with experimental data from Berkeley laboratory (Ref. [d3he1540]).
- The nucleon-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) optical potentials are fitted over the energy range 13 to 30 MeV.
- The Pauli-repulsive term strength is set to \(\Gamma = 1\) GeV; results become independent of \(\Gamma\) above a few hundred MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for the \(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) bound state overlap from microscopic calculations with INOY04 equals 1.65.
- Two choices for the neutron-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) binding potential in the \(^{1}S_0\) wave are used: \(V_1 = -2V_2 = -132.848\) MeV and \(V_1 = -V_2 = -156.113\) MeV, both with radii \(R_1 = 2R_2 = 1.7\) fm.
- Partial waves with orbital angular momentum \(L > 3\) are neglected as they are safely negligible up to 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Deltuva (2013, 2014) who performed rigorous four-body calculations of nucleon-\(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) scattering, and of Diego et al. who developed effective neutron-\(^{3}\mathrm{H}\) potentials with strong partial-wave dependence for a three-body \(^{3}\mathrm{H}+n+n\) model. The present study is the first to include a Pauli-repulsive term in a complex optical potential, going beyond the real low-energy nucleon-\(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) potentials of Thompson (2000). The results enable three-body calculations of deuteron-induced reactions on \(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) without fitting to experimental deuteron scattering data, and the authors suggest that the remaining large-angle discrepancies may indicate the need to include two-nucleon transfer mechanisms not captured by their model.
