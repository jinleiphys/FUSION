# Charge Symmetry Breaking in the Nucleon and Parity Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> The basic facts of charge symmetry breaking (CSB) phenomena are reviewed. The relevance of CSB to parity-violating electron-proton scattering experiments that seek to extract strange elastic form factors is discussed. Experimentalists have stated and written that the current uncertainty in our knowledge of CSB limits the ability to push further on the strange form factors. I discuss recent calculations using relativistic chiral perturbation theory and realistic values of strong coupling constants which show that the uncertainties due to lack of knowledge of CSB are at least ten times smaller than present experimental uncertainties. Estimates of CSB effects are made for the JLab Qweak and Mainz P2 experiments.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effects in the nucleon are at least ten times smaller than current experimental uncertainties in parity-violating electron-proton scattering experiments, contradicting earlier claims that CSB uncertainty limits the extraction of strange quark form factors.

## Method

The author uses relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory (RBχPT) to compute the CSB contributions to the neutral weak form factors of the proton, including pion loop diagrams and ρ-ω mixing contributions. The strong coupling constants are constrained by experimental data from nucleon-nucleon scattering and the ³He-³H binding energy difference, rather than using the larger values from dispersion analyses employed in prior work by Kubis and Lewis (2006). The resulting CSB form factors are then used to estimate the effect on the parity-violating asymmetry for the JLab Qweak and Mainz P2 experiments.

## Key numbers

- The CSB magnetic moment prediction is G_M^CSB(0) = 0.021 ± 0.01 ± 0.008, compared to current experimental bounds on the strangeness magnetic form factor G_M^s = 0.33 ± 0.4 at Q² = 0.1 GeV²
- The CSB electric form factor satisfies |G_E^CSB| < 0.005 for Q² < 0.3 GeV², compared to the strangeness electric form factor G_E^s = 0.006 ± 0.02 at Q² = 0.1 GeV²
- For the Qweak experiment, the CSB contribution to the parity-violating asymmetry is |δA^PV_CSB| < 1 ppb, negligible compared to the observed 279 ppb
- For the Mainz P2 experiment, the CSB contribution is even smaller: |δA^PV_CSB| < 0.01 ppb
- The RBχPT loop contribution to G_M^CSB(0) is 0.014, agreeing with the leading-order heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) loop contribution to better than 95%
- The G_E^CSB(Q²) rises monotonically to a maximum of about 0.001 ± 0.0005 at Q² = 0.3 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Kubis and Lewis (2006), who used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with resonance saturation and found G_M^CSB(0) = 0.025 ± 0.02 using a large ω-nucleon coupling constant g_ω ≈ 42 from dispersion analyses. The present work constrains g_ω to be less than about 19 ± 5 using experimental measurements of the ³He-³H binding energy difference, yielding G_M^CSB(0) = 0.031 ± 0.01 at next-to-leading order. The results enable continued extraction of strange quark form factors from parity-violating electron-proton scattering experiments without concern that CSB uncertainties are a limiting factor, and demonstrate consistency between relativistic and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory frameworks.
