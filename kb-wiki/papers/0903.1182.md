# Isospin mixing in nuclei within the nuclear density functional theory

> We present the self-consistent, non-perturbative analysis of isospin mixing using the nuclear density functional approach and the rediagonalization of the Coulomb interaction in the good-isospin basis. The largest isospin-breaking effects are predicted for N = Z nuclei and they quickly fall with the neutron excess. The unphysical isospin violation on the mean-field level, caused by the neutron excess, is eliminated by the proposed method. We find a significant dependence of the magnitude of isospin breaking on the parametrization of the nuclear interaction term. A rough correlation has been found between the isospin mixing parameter and the difference of proton and neutron rms radii. The theoretical framework described in this study is well suited to describe a variety of phenomena associated with isospin violation in nuclei, in particular the isospin symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decays.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first self-consistent, non-perturbative analysis of isospin mixing within modern energy-density-functional (EDF) methods, but the final values of the isospin-mixing parameter remain uncertain due to imperfect determination of the nuclear EDF, with a significant dependence on the parametrization of the nuclear interaction term.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the SLy4 EDF parameterization and the HFODD solver, which allows for arbitrary spatial deformations. They generated good-isospin basis states by isospin projection from the mean-field (MF) states, then rediagonalized the total Hamiltonian (strong interaction plus full Coulomb interaction with physical charge) in this basis to obtain the isospin-mixed ground state. Both direct and exchange Coulomb terms were calculated exactly.

## Key numbers

- In $^{40}$Ca, the isospin-mixing parameter after rediagonalization (AR) is 0.9%, which is about 50% larger than the before-rediagonalization (BR) value
- In $^{42}$Ca (a $T_z=1$ nucleus), the AR isospin mixing drops to 0.2%, then decreases exponentially to about 0.01% in $^{60}$Ca
- For $N=Z$ nuclei, the difference between BR and AR total energies rapidly increases with mass number, attaining about 2 MeV in $A=100$
- The AR energies are within 90 keV of the HF energies $E_{\text{MF}}$
- For $^{100}$Sn, the difference between extreme AR values obtained for SkO and SkP parameterizations is as large as 1.5%, about 30% of the isospin mixing value in that nucleus

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field approaches to isospin mixing (Engel 1970, Brink 1970, Caurier 1980, Caurier 1982, Auerbach 1983) by eliminating the spurious isospin mixing caused by neutron excess that had hampered calculations beyond $N=Z$ systems. The method is designed to provide improved isospin symmetry-breaking corrections for superallowed Fermi beta decays, which require high-precision calculations following new data from Penning-trap measurements (Hardy 2005, Towner 2008, Miller 2008). The framework can also be applied to Coulomb energy differences and properties of analogue states.
