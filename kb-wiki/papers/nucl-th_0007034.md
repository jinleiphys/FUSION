# Annual Report for the Department of Energy: 1999-2000, Nuclear Theory Group at the University of Washington

## Key claim

The Nuclear Theory Group at the University of Washington reports progress in developing effective field theories (EFTs) for multi-nucleon systems, with the claim that theoretical uncertainties in key low-energy observables can be reduced to the 1% level, but this reduction depends on fixing undetermined low-energy constants from either precise experimental measurements or complementary theoretical calculations (e.g., tritium beta-decay).

## Method

The group applied chiral perturbation theory and effective field theory to two- and three-nucleon systems, computing cross sections and scattering amplitudes order-by-order in the chiral expansion. They developed a Z-parameterization fitting procedure that reproduces the tail of the deuteron wave function at next-to-leading order, improving convergence of the EFT expansion for elastic and inelastic deuteron processes. For neutrino-deuteron interactions, they included a local, gauge-invariant axial current operator at next-to-leading order with coefficient L1,A, which can be fixed by either low-energy neutrino experiments or tritium beta-decay calculations.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical uncertainty in the $np\rightarrow d\gamma$ cross section reduced from ~5% (Burles, Nollet, Truran and Turner estimate) to ~3% (Chen and Savage), then further to ~1% (Rupak), eliminating this process as a significant source of uncertainty in Big-Bang nucleosynthesis codes
- In light-front nuclear matter calculations, nucleons carry at least 84% of the nuclear plus-momentum when correlations are included (Brueckner theory), compared to 65% in mean field theory and a desired value of ~90%
- Compressibility of nuclear matter obtained from light-front Brueckner theory: 180 MeV
- Nuclear medium enhances the average number of pions per nucleon by about 5%
- For coherent pion diffraction into two jets on nuclear targets, the cross section varies with nucleon number A as A^1.55±0.05 (Fermilab E791 data on 195Pt and 12C targets), compared to the color transparency prediction of approximately A^2 for forward cross section
- Neutrino-nucleon emission rates for $NN \rightarrow NN \nu \bar{\nu}$ and $NN \rightarrow NNa$: the one-pion-exchange approximation overestimated these rates by a factor of 4-5
- For two extra "gravity-only" dimensions, consistency with SN1987A observations requires these dimensions to have a radius less than 1 micron

## Context

This annual report summarizes the work of the Nuclear Theory Group at the University of Washington during 1999-2000, extending Weinberg's (1990) pioneering efforts to describe nuclear systems with effective field theory. The work on $np\rightarrow d\gamma$ directly addresses the uncertainty identified by Burles, Nollet, Truran and Turner in Big-Bang nucleosynthesis. The neutrino-deuteron calculations clarify ambiguities in existing potential model calculations by Ying, Haxton and Henley (YHH) and by Kubodera and Nozawa (KN), which disagreed at the 5% level due to different treatments of meson exchange currents. The light-front nuclear physics program aims to provide a relativistic treatment for interpreting experiments at Jefferson Laboratory and HERA, including the recent HERMES discovery of enhanced $\sigma_L/\sigma_T$ ratios in nuclear deep inelastic scattering. The color transparency work extends predictions from Frankfurt, Miller and Strikman (1993) on coherent pion diffraction, now tested by Fermilab E791. The astrophysics results on neutrino and axion radiation from neutron-star cooling will impact understanding of supernova explosions and axion mass bounds from SN1987A.
