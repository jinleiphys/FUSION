# The Skyrme model in the BPS limit

> In this review, we summarise the main features of the BPS Skyrme model which provides a physically well-motivated idealisation of atomic nuclei and nuclear matter: 1) it leads to zero binding energies for classical solitons (while realistic binding energies emerge owing to the semiclassical corrections, the Coulomb interaction and isospin breaking); 2) it describes a perfect non-barotropic fluid already at the microscopic (field theoretical) level which allows to study thermodynamics beyond the mean-field limit. These properties allow for an approximate but analytical calculation of binding energies of the most abundant nuclei, for a determination of the equation of state of skyrmionic matter (both in the full field theory and in a mean-field approximation) as well as the description of neutron stars as Skyrme solitons with a very good agreement with available observational data. All these results suggest that the proper low energy effective model of QCD should be close to the BPS Skyrme model in a certain sense (a "near-BPS Skyrme model"), with a prominent role played by the BPS part.

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model provides a physically well-motivated idealisation of atomic nuclei and nuclear matter, but the authors explicitly state it is an approximation to the proper low-energy effective field theory, which should be a "near-BPS Skyrme model" where the BPS part dominates while a small non-BPS (usual Skyrme) part is added perturbatively.

## Method

The authors review the BPS Skyrme model, which consists of a sextic term (the square of the baryon current) and a potential term, taken as the dominant contribution to the full near-BPS Skyrme Lagrangian. They derive the Bogomolny bound and BPS equation for static configurations, demonstrate the perfect fluid nature of the energy-momentum tensor, and compute thermodynamic quantities at zero temperature including the equation of state, compression modulus, and mean-field limits using target space averaging techniques.

## Key numbers

- The compression modulus of nuclear matter at nuclear saturation density (equilibrium P=0) is known to be about **250 MeV**; the BPS Skyrme model gives a zero compression modulus for realistic potentials (e.g., those with near-vacuum behaviour \(\mathcal{U} \sim \xi^\alpha\) for \(\alpha \ge 2\))
- For the quartic potential \(\mathcal{U} = \mathcal{U}_\pi^2\), the energy and volume are expressed in terms of generalized hypergeometric functions, with the high-density behaviour \(P \sim V^{-2}\) and the speed of sound approaching the maximum value \(\bar v_s = 1\) for nonzero pressure
- The mean-field chemical potential is given by \(\bar{\boldsymbol{\mu}} = 2\pi^2 \lambda \mu \left< \sqrt{\mathcal{U} + \tilde P} \right>_{\mathbb{S}^3}\), and in the high-density limit the mean-field energy density approaches the universal relation \(\bar \varepsilon \propto \bar{\boldsymbol{\mu}}^2\)

## Context

This review extends the Skyrme model originally proposed by Skyrme (1961), which successfully describes light nuclei but suffers from too high binding energies and a crystalline state of matter for heavy nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. The BPS Skyrme model addresses both issues by introducing a dominant sextic term, building on prior work by the same authors and others (Adam et al., 2010; Marleau, 2012; Speight, 2010). The model enables analytical calculation of binding energies for the most abundant nuclei, determination of the equation of state of skyrmionic matter, and description of neutron stars as Skyrme solitons, with the liquid-gas phase transition exactly reproducing the conjectured phase diagram of QCD at zero temperature near nuclear saturation as described by Fukuda and Hatsuda (2004).
