# Probing of nucleon mesonic structure by means of quasi-elastic knock-out processes like $p+e\to e'+π+n$

> The momentum distributions of pions and $ρ$-mesons in the nucleon are extracted from experiment. Perspectives of the quark microscopic theory of mesonic cloud are outlined.

## Key claim

The momentum distributions of pions and rho-mesons inside the nucleon can be extracted from experimental data on quasi-elastic knockout processes, but only under the condition that the pion-in-flight diagram dominates the longitudinal cross section at sufficiently high momentum transfer (Q² ≳ 1–2 GeV²/c²), and the extracted distributions depend on the choice of the cutoff parameter Λ_π in the πNN vertex form factor.

## Method

The authors analyze the electroproduction process p(e,e'π⁺)n using the pion-in-flight mechanism, where the longitudinal cross section σ_L factorizes into a product of the pion momentum distribution in the nucleon and a Q²-dependent pion form factor. They extract the pion momentum distribution from experimental σ_L data at Q² = 0.7 and 3.3 GeV²/c², using a πNN vertex form factor with a monopole cutoff Λ_π. For the transverse cross section σ_T, they consider the contribution from a virtual ρ-meson pole diagram. They also apply the microscopic ³P₀ constituent quark model to predict momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors for πB channels with B = N, Δ, N*(1535), and N**(1440).

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal cross section data analyzed at Q² = 0.7 GeV²/c² and Q² = 3.3 GeV²/c² from the experiment of Ref. [b]
- Pion form factor parametrized as F_π(Q²) = (1 + Q²/0.51)⁻¹ (Q² in GeV²/c²)
- Cutoff parameter Λ_π tested at values of 0.7 GeV/c and 1.2 GeV/c in the πNN vertex form factor F_πNN(k²) = Λ_π²/(Λ_π² + k²)
- Constituent quark model parameters: b_N = 0.6 fm (quark radius of nucleon), b_π = 0.3 fm (quark radius of pion)
- Threshold for ρ-meson dominance in transverse cross section: Q² ≳ 2–3 GeV²/c²

## Context

This work extends the nuclear physics technique of nucleon knockout from nuclei to the study of mesonic structure within the nucleon, following the earlier suggestion by Bebek et al. (1976) that the pion-in-flight diagram dominates the longitudinal cross section at high Q². The authors build on their own previous work (Neudatchin et al., 1998; Neudatchin et al., 2000) and the light-cone dynamics analysis by Strikman and Frankfurt (1981). The extracted momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors provide direct experimental constraints on the meson cloud of the nucleon, and the ³P₀ quark model predictions (based on the work of Ferretti et al., 1998; Dover et al., 1992) offer a microscopic framework for interpreting future coincidence experiments with higher precision.
