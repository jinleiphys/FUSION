# Soft photon and two hard jets forward production in proton-nucleus collisions

> We calculate the cross section for production of a soft photon and two hard jets in the forward rapidity region in proton-nucleus collisions at high energies. The calculation is performed within the hybrid formalism. The hardness of the final particles is defined with respect to the saturation scale of the nucleus. We consider both the correlation limit of small momentum imbalance and the dilute target limit where the momentum imbalance is of the order of the hardness of the jets. The results depend on the first two transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) gluon distributions of the nucleus.

## Key claim
The cross section for soft photon plus two hard jet production in forward p-A collisions is calculated within the hybrid formalism, where the photon transverse momentum is smaller than or of order the saturation scale Q_s, while the jet transverse momenta are much larger than Q_s; the calculation is restricted to the case where the photon is emitted before the hard quark-gluon splitting, and contributions from collinear two-parton densities in the incoming proton are not included.

## Method
The dressed quark state (including photon and gluon radiation) was constructed in the Color Glass Condensate wave function approach, Fourier transformed to mixed longitudinal-momentum/transverse-position space, and then eikonally scattered through the target color field. The partonic cross section was obtained by taking the expectation value of the number operator for the final-state photon, gluon, and quark in the outgoing wave function, averaging over target field configurations, and simplifying the Wilson line structures using Fierz identities and the relation between adjoint and fundamental representations.

## Key numbers
- The cross section depends on the first two transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) gluon distributions of the nucleus.
- The photon transverse momentum is smaller than or of order Q_s (the saturation scale of the nucleus), while the two jet transverse momenta are much larger than Q_s.
- The result contains factors (1+ξ̄₁²) and (1+ξ̄₂²) from the squared splitting amplitudes, where ξ₁ = k₁⁺/p⁺ is the photon longitudinal momentum fraction and ξ₂ = k₂⁺/(p⁺-k₁⁺) is the gluon longitudinal momentum fraction relative to the intermediate quark.

## Context
This work extends the hybrid formalism for forward particle production in p-A collisions, previously applied to light hadron production (Elastic vs Inelastic, 2015; nlohybridpart, 2015) and heavy hadron production (Altinoluk, 2015), to the case of photon plus two jet final states. It provides NLO corrections that are leading in the specific kinematic regime of a soft photon and two hard jets, complementing the central-rapidity calculation of photon plus two jet production (Benic, 2017) and the forward photon production calculation (Gelis, 2002). The results enable future experimental measurements at LHCb and ALICE to probe saturation effects and TMD gluon distributions at small x.
