# Neutron halo and the impact-parameter dependence of the $π^+ - π^-$ asymmetry in heavy ion collisions

> The theoretical nuclear physics predictions and experimental results on antiprotonic atoms lead to different proton and neutron spatial distributions. At CERN SPS energies production of positive and negative pions differs for $pp$, $pn$, $np$ and $nn$ scattering. These two facts lead to an impact parameter dependence of the $π^+$ to $π^-$ ratio in $^{208}Pb + ^{208}Pb$ collisions. A recent experiment at CERN seems to confirm qualitatively these predictions. The results for two models considered are almost identical. It may open a new possibility for determination of neutron density distribution in nuclei.

## Key claim

The impact-parameter dependence of the π⁺ to π⁻ ratio in ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies can be explained by combining the asymmetry of elementary nucleon-nucleon pion production cross sections with different proton and neutron spatial distributions, but the authors caution that the experimental data can only be used as an indication of the effect and no quantitative comparison is yet possible.

## Method

The authors calculate the π⁺/π⁻ ratio as a function of impact parameter b and Feynman variable xF using two models: a binary collision model and a wounded nucleon model, both generalized to distinguish proton and neutron contributions. Elementary pion production cross sections for pp, pn, np, and nn collisions are taken from the HIJING model, while proton and neutron density distributions for ²⁰⁸Pb are obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Skyrme interaction SLy4. The number of binary collisions of each type is computed from the overlap of proton and neutron thickness functions at a given impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used for normalization is σ_ine^NN = 30 mb, relevant for CERN SPS energies.
- The results are considered reliable up to xF ≈ 0.2, beyond which the HIJING model does not describe the π⁺-π⁻ asymmetry in elementary collisions.
- The ratio R_{+/-} shows a clear dependence on impact parameter b, with central and peripheral collisions differing significantly in the preliminary NA49 data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron distributions using antiprotonic atoms (Trzcińska, 2001) and radiochemical methods, which suggested a halo-type neutron density profile. It also builds on the observation by Rybicki (2003) of an impact-parameter dependence of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio in NA49 data. The authors propose that detailed comparison of their model with well-defined (xF, b) experimental data could provide a new method for determining neutron density profiles, complementary to existing techniques such as proton-nucleus elastic scattering (Satchler, 1994; Cooper, 2003), antiprotonic X-ray spectroscopy, and parity-violating electron scattering (Donnelly, 1989; Horowitz, 2001).
