# Second excited state of ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ tetramer

> The four-boson universality suggests the existence of the second excited tetramer state in a system of cold ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ atoms. It is not bound but could be seen as a resonance in the atom-trimer scattering. This process is rigorously calculated using the momentum-space transition operator framework with two realistic interatomic potentials. The $S$-wave phase shift and cross section show a resonant behavior below the excited trimer threshold, but there are sizable nonresonant contributions from $P$ and $D$ waves as well. The position and width of the resonant state is determined, and for the latter significant finite-range effects are found.

## Key claim

The second excited state of the ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ tetramer, predicted by four-boson universality, is not a bound state but exists as a resonance in atom-trimer scattering; however, its observation is partially masked by sizable nonresonant contributions from $P$ and $D$ waves, and the resonance width shows significant finite-range effects compared to universal predictions.

## Method

The author solved the Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using two realistic interatomic potentials (LM2M2 and PCJS). The "softening and extrapolation" method from Deltuva (2022) was employed to handle the strong short-range repulsion of the van der Waals potentials. The $S$-wave phase shift and cross section were calculated as functions of energy near the excited trimer threshold, and the resonant state parameters were extracted by fitting the phase shift to a sum of background and resonant contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the LM2M2 potential: resonance position $B_4^{**}/B_3^* = 1.82(2)$ and width $\Gamma/B_3^* = 0.010(1)$
- For the PCJS potential: resonance position $B_4^{**}/B_3^* = 1.79(2)$ and width $\Gamma/B_3^* = 0.009(1)$
- Universal (zero-range) prediction for the same $B_3^*/B_2$ ratio as LM2M2: $B_4^{**}/B_3^* = 1.888(1)$ and $\Gamma/B_3^* = 0.0048(1)$
- Absolute values for LM2M2: $B_4^{**} = 4.14(4)$ mK and $\Gamma = 0.023(2)$ mK
- Absolute values for PCJS: $B_4^{**} = 4.74(5)$ mK and $\Gamma = 0.024(2)$ mK
- At the $J=0$ resonance peak: $\delta_0 = 90.0(1)$ deg, $\sigma_0 = 830(1)$ $\mathrm{\AA}^2$; $J=1$ contributes $\sigma_1 = 1076(3)$ $\mathrm{\AA}^2$; $J=2$ contributes $\sigma_2 = 253(2)$ $\mathrm{\AA}^2$

## Context

This work extends previous bound-state calculations of ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ tetramers by Blume (2000), Lazauskas (2006), Hiyama (2012), and Deltuva (2022), which predicted two bound tetramer states associated with the trimer ground state. The universal Efimov scenario, studied by Hammer (2007) and Stecher (2009) using separable potentials, predicts a second excited tetramer associated with the excited trimer state, but this state had never been calculated for realistic ${}^4\mathrm{He}$ potentials. The results enable experimental searches for this resonance in cold atom-trimer collisions and quantify the deviation from universal predictions due to finite-range effects.
