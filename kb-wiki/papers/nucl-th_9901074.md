# Resonances in the three-neutron system

> A study of 3-body resonances has been performed in the framework of configuration space Faddeev equations. The importance of keeping a sufficient number of terms in the asymptotic expansion of the resonance wave function is pointed out. We investigated three neutrons interacting in selected force components taken from realistic nn forces.

## Key claim

The paper investigates three-neutron resonances using configuration space Faddeev equations with realistic neutron-neutron forces, but the final pole positions corresponding to actual force strengths could not be found due to the onset of numerical instabilities; the numerically reliable results achieved make it likely that three-neutron resonance energies will have large imaginary parts and therefore be physically not interesting.

## Method

The authors solved the configuration space Faddeev equations (Kowalski-Noyes equations) for three neutrons, using cubic spline interpolation on grids in hyperradius and hyperangle coordinates, with the asymptotic boundary condition approximated by including multiple terms in the expansion of the resonance wave function. They artificially enhanced selected force components from realistic nn potentials (Gogny and Reid93) in the \(^1S_0\), \(^3P_0\), and \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) states to create bound three-neutron states, then reduced the enhancement factors to trace resonance pole trajectories into the second energy sheet. They also attempted complex scaling and smooth-exterior complex scaling methods but encountered numerical problems with realistic nuclear forces.

## Key numbers

* For the \(J^\pi = 3/2^-\) state with the Gogny potential, the last reliable resonance position reached was \(E = (5.30 - i\,4.67)\) MeV at enhancement factors \((\lambda_{^1S_0}, \lambda_{^3P_0}, \lambda_{^3P_2-^3F_2}) = (1.0, 1.0, 3.6)\), requiring at least 4 terms in the asymptotic boundary condition.
* For the \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\) state with the Gogny potential, a relatively narrow resonance at \(E = (2.88 - i\,0.23)\) MeV was found at enhancement factors \((1.0, 5.3, 4.6)\).
* For the \(J^\pi = 1/2^-\) state with the Gogny potential, the three-neutron bound state disappeared into the second sheet when enhancement factors reached \((1.0, 7.5, 4.75)\).
* For the \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\) state with the Gogny potential, the three-neutron bound state disappeared into the second sheet when enhancement factors reached \((1.0, 5.5, 4.8)\).
* For the \(J^\pi = 3/2^-\) state with the Gogny potential, the three-neutron bound state disappeared when enhancement factors reached \((1.0, 1.0, 4.35)\).
* For the 2N system using the Reid93 potential in the \(^3P_0\) state with enhancement factor \(\lambda_V = 5.5\), a resonance at \(E = (1.42 - i\,0.75)\) MeV was found.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of three-nucleon resonances, which had produced conflicting results: Sperinde (1970) and Williams (1969) pointed to resonances in three-neutron and three-proton systems, while a variational study by Csoto (1996) using complex scaling with simplified forces predicted a three-neutron resonance at \(E = (14 - i\,13)\) MeV for a \(J^\pi = 3/2^+\) state. The present study is the first to attempt rigorous solutions of Faddeev equations with realistic NN forces for three-neutron resonances, but the numerical instabilities encountered prevent definitive conclusions; the results suggest that any three-neutron resonances will be too broad to be experimentally observable, which would be consistent with the experimental finding by Yuly (1997) of no evidence for a three-neutron resonance in the \(^3He(\pi^-,\pi^+)3n\) reaction.
