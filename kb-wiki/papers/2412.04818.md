# Neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering in presence of non-standard interactions: cross sections and nucleon polarizations

> New physics beyond the Standard Model (SM) may appear in the form of non-standard neutrino interactions (NSI). We have studied neutral current (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering in presence of NSI. We obtain that in this scenario, nucleon matrix elements depend not only on the isovector axial nucleon form factor but also on the isoscalar one. For the axial form factors we consequently rely on the quark flavor decomposition performed by QCD simulations in the lattice (LQCD). We have examined cross sections and polarization observables. For the current bounds on diagonal muon flavor NSI couplings we find substantial deviations from the SM predictions in cross sections and transverse polarizations of the outgoing nucleons. In view of the progress in the precision of LQCD determinations of nucleon properties, modern measurements of neutral current (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering will be in the position to discover or significantly constrain NSI.

## Key claim

For neutral current (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering with non-standard interactions (NSI), the nucleon matrix elements depend not only on the isovector axial form factor but also on the isoscalar axial form factor, which is not probed by Standard Model electroweak interactions; using current 90% confidence level bounds on diagonal muon flavor NSI couplings, the authors find substantial deviations from Standard Model predictions in cross sections and transverse polarizations of outgoing nucleons.

## Method

The authors derived the nucleon matrix elements for neutral current (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering in the presence of NSI, expressing them in terms of vector and axial-vector form factors that depend on isovector and isoscalar contributions. They adopted lattice QCD determinations of the flavor-decomposed axial form factors from the Extended Twisted Mass (ETM) Collaboration for the isoscalar axial form factor, and used standard parametrizations for electromagnetic form factors and strange form factors. Cross sections and final-nucleon polarization observables (longitudinal and transverse components) were computed for muon (anti)neutrinos on proton and neutron targets, using the 90% confidence level intervals for diagonal NSI couplings from atmospheric neutrino oscillation and NuTeV data.

## Key numbers

* At a (anti)neutrino energy of 5 GeV, the maximal variation in the total cross section for the neutron target is around 20% relative to the Standard Model prediction.
* At a (anti)neutrino energy of 5 GeV, the maximal variation in the total cross section for the proton target goes as high as 35% relative to the Standard Model prediction.
* The 90% confidence level intervals for the NSI couplings used are: epsilon^{uV}_{mu mu} in [-0.044, 0.044], epsilon^{dV}_{mu mu} in [-0.042, 0.042], epsilon^{uA}_{mu mu} in [-0.094, 0.14], and epsilon^{dA}_{mu mu} in [-0.072, 0.057].
* The axial form factor flavor decomposition parameters from the ETM Collaboration are: g_A^u = 0.859(18) with m_{A u} = 1.187(65) GeV, g_A^d = -0.423(17) with m_{A d} = 1.168(54) GeV, and g_A^s = -0.044(8) with m_{A s} = 0.992(164) GeV.
* The strange vector form factor parameters from the ETM Collaboration are: mu_s = -0.017(4), <r_M^2>^s = -0.015(9) fm^2, and <r_E^2>^s = -0.0048(6) fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the study of Papoulias (2016) on NSI effects in neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering by using lattice QCD input for the axial form factors instead of ad-hoc Q^2-dependent form factors, and by including the isoscalar axial form factor that was not considered in that earlier work. It generalizes the formalism of Bilenky (2013) for nucleon polarization in neutral current elastic scattering to the NSI scenario, and explores polarization observables for the first time in the neutral current case. The results enable future experimental studies of neutrino-nucleon interactions, such as those from MINERvA and upcoming experiments, to discover or significantly constrain NSI, provided that lattice QCD determinations of nucleon properties continue to improve in precision.
