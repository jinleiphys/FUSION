# Symmetry classification of uniform states in spin-2 Bose-Einstein condensates and neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids

> We clarify a relation between the Gross-Pitaevskii energy functional for spin-2 spinor Bose-Einstein condensates and the Ginzburg-Landau theory for neutron $^3P_2$ superfluidis (spin-triplet $P$-wave pairing with total angular momentum two). We then classify all uniform states with nontrivial unbroken symmetries, with the help of geometric invariant theory.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a precise mapping between the Gross-Pitaevskii energy functional for spin-2 spinor Bose-Einstein condensates and the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids, showing that both describe \(J=2\) condensates but differ in gradient terms, higher-order interactions, and magnetic field couplings, which leads to differences beyond topology such as stability and dynamics.

## Method

The authors use geometric invariant theory to express the configuration space \(\mathbb{C}^5\) of the five-component order parameter in terms of six independent \(U(1) \times SO(3)\) invariants: \(\rho\), \(\mathbf{S}^2\), \(|\Psi_{20}|^2\), \(|\Psi_{30}|^2\), \(|\Phi_{30}|^2\), and \(\Gamma_4\). They rewrite the spin-2 BEC Hamiltonian in terms of a \(3 \times 3\) traceless symmetric tensor \(A\) and the neutron \(^3P_2\) GL free energy in terms of five-component condensates \(\psi\), then classify all uniform states with nontrivial unbroken symmetries as strata in the orbit space.

## Key numbers

- The configuration space has \(10\) real dimensions (five complex components), and after factoring out \(U(1) \times SO(3)\) there are \(6\) independent invariants.
- The orbit space \(\mathcal{M}_{\rho=1}\) for fixed density is five-dimensional, parametrized by \(\mathbf{S}^2\), \(|\Psi_{20}|^2\), \(|\Psi_{30}|^2\), \(|\Phi_{30}|^2\), and \(\Gamma_4\).
- There are \(9\) candidate ground or metastable states (strata) for \(\mathbf{B}=0\) and \(15\) for \(\mathbf{B} \neq 0\).
- The space \(\mathcal{S}\) of states with nontrivial unbroken symmetry has dimension \(\dim[\mathcal{S}] = 2\).
- The ferromagnetic state is characterized by \(\mathbf{S}^2 = 4\rho^2\) and \(|\Psi_{20}|^2 = |\Psi_{30}|^2 = |\Phi_{30}|^2 = \Gamma_4 = 0\).
- The canted ferromagnetic state has \(\mathbf{S}^2 = \rho^2\) and \(|\Psi_{20}|^2 = |\Psi_{30}|^2 = |\Phi_{30}|^2 = \Gamma_4 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the symmetry classification of spin-2 BECs, previously studied using three invariants by Barnett (2006), Uchino (2010), and Kawaguchi (2011), by introducing three additional invariants (\(|\Psi_{30}|^2\), \(|\Phi_{30}|^2\), \(\Gamma_4\)) that are absent in the low-energy Hamiltonian of spin-2 BECs but required for the GL theory of neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids. It connects the GP theory for spinor BECs and the GL theory for neutron superfluids, which had been explored independently, and enables unified study of topological defects such as half-quantized non-Abelian vortices found in both systems by Borgh (2016) and Masuda (2016).
