# Dynamics of the linear-chain alpha cluster in microscopic time-dependent relativistic density functional theory

> The time-dependent covariant density functional theory in 3D lattice space has been developed and applied to investigate the microscopic dynamics of the linear-chain cluster states for carbon isotopes in the reactions $^4$He$+^8$Be and $^4$He$+^{10}$Be without any symmetry assumptions. By examining the density distribution and its time evolutions, the structure and dynamics of the linear-chain states are analyzed, and the quasiperiodic oscillations of the clusters are revealed. For $^4$He$+^8$Be, the linear-chain states evolve to a triangular configuration and then to a more compact shape. In contrast, for $^4$He$+^{10}$Be, the lifetime of the linear-chain states is much more prolonged due to the dynamical isospin effects by the valence neutrons which slow down the longitudinal oscillations of the clusters and persist the linear-chain states. The dependence of the linear chain survival time and dynamical isospin effects on impact parameters have been illustrated as well.

## Key claim

The time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) in full 3D lattice space reveals that the linear-chain cluster state in \({}^{14}\)C formed via \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{10}\)Be persists much longer than the analogous state in \({}^{12}\)C from \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{8}\)Be, specifically because the two valence neutrons introduce dynamical isospin effects that slow down longitudinal cluster oscillations, but only for head-on collisions at the specific center-of-mass energy of 2 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Dirac equation for nucleons in 3D lattice space without symmetry restrictions, using the relativistic density functional DD-ME2. Initial ground-state wavefunctions of \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{8}\)Be, and \({}^{10}\)Be were obtained from static covariant DFT calculations, then the nuclei were placed 12 fm apart along the \(z\) axis and Lorentz-boosted to a center-of-mass energy of 2 MeV. The time evolution was propagated with a predictor-corrector method up to 5000 fm/\(c\), and an absorbing imaginary potential removed emitted particles.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{8}\)Be at \(E_{\rm c.m.}=2\) MeV and zero impact parameter, the linear-chain configuration is retained up to about 3100 fm/\(c\), then bends into a triangular shape for approximately 1000 fm/\(c\) before collapsing to a compact shape.
* For \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{10}\)Be at \(E_{\rm c.m.}=2\) MeV and zero impact parameter, the linear-chain configuration persists to at least 5000 fm/\(c\) (the end of the simulation).
* The quadrupole deformation \(\beta_{20}\) for the linear-chain states in both collisions agrees with static CDFT predictions from Zhao (2015).
* Increasing the initial energy to 8 MeV changes the oscillation period times by no more than 10%.
* At impact parameter \(b=0.5\) fm, the linear-chain survival time for \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{10}\)Be is longer than for \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{8}\)Be; the lifetime does not drop to zero but stays around 500 fm/\(c\) with increasing impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic time-dependent Hartree-Fock study of the \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{8}\)Be reaction by Umar (2010) into the relativistic framework and, for the first time, provides a microscopic dynamical treatment of the linear-chain state in neutron-rich \({}^{14}\)C from \({}^{4}\)He+\({}^{10}\)Be. The results confirm the static CDFT predictions of Zhao (2015) for the deformations of linear-chain states in \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{14}\)C, and they support the experimental indications from Yamaguchi (2017) that linear-chain states exist in \({}^{14}\)C. The finding that valence neutrons in \(\pi\) orbits help stabilize the chain complements the earlier nonrelativistic TDHF work of Maruhn (2010) on \({}^{16}\)C and \({}^{20}\)C, where \(\sigma\) orbits were found important. The approach enables future TDCDFT studies of linear-chain dynamics in heavier neutron-rich systems and can guide resonant-scattering experiments with rare isotope beams.
