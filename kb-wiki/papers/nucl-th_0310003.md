# Hadron Multiplicity in Semi-Inclusive Lepton-Nucleon and Lepton-Nucleus Scattering

> We discuss multi-hadron production in both inelastic neutrino-nucleon interactions in the current fragmentation region and neutrino-nucleus collisions in the target fragmentation region. Our analysis, carried out within the framework of the quark-gluon string model, is mainly focused on the difference between these two processes. We show that the $Q^2$ dependence of hadron multiplicity in the current and target fragmentation regions is indeed completely different. The study of inelastic $ν-A$ scattering in the target fragmentation region also provides new information on nuclear structure at small $N-N$ distances. The results of the proposed approach are in satisfactory agreement with the data recently obtained at CERN by the NOMAD Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $Q^2$ dependence of hadron multiplicity in the current and target fragmentation regions is completely different, and that the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) describes the NOMAD data at low and moderate $Q^2$ where conventional perturbative QCD calculations fail.

## Method

The authors analyze inelastic neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus scattering within the Quark-Gluon String Model, using planar (one-Reggeon exchange) and cylindrical (one-Pomeron exchange) graphs to describe hadron production. For neutrino-nucleus interactions in the target fragmentation region, they employ an impulse approximation with a convolution formula that uses nucleon spectral functions from nuclear many-body theory, including a high-momentum tail from strongly correlated nucleons. The fragmentation functions are obtained from a recursive cascade model satisfying Regge asymptotics, and the calculations are compared to NOMAD collaboration data for $\nu+p$ and $\nu+C^{12}$ scattering.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum of a quark in a nucleon is $\langle k_t\rangle \simeq 0.25$ GeV/c, with $B=1/\langle k_t\rangle \simeq 4$ (GeV/c)$^{-1}$
- The QCD fit for charged multiplicity uses parameters $a=2.257$, $b=0.094$, $c=1.775$ and $Q_0=1$ GeV/c
- The QCD fit fails to reproduce NOMAD data on charged multiplicity at $Q < 5$ GeV/c
- The cylindrical graph dominates pion multiplicity in $\nu-C$ scattering at $Q^2>10$ (GeV/c)$^2$
- Negative pions were detected with momenta $0.35<p_\pi<0.8$ GeV/c in the backward hemisphere
- The high momentum tail of the nucleon distribution corresponds to $p>0.4$ GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the Quark-Gluon String Model, previously applied to hadronic reactions by Kaidalov (1981, 1982) and to lepton-nucleon processes by Benhar, Fantoni, Lykasov and Sukhatme (2002), to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The analysis contradicts the applicability of conventional perturbative QCD calculations at low $Q^2$, which fail to describe NOMAD data, while the QGSM approach provides satisfactory agreement. The study enables new investigations of nuclear structure at small nucleon-nucleon distances through the high-momentum tail of the nucleon distribution, building on the nonrelativistic spectral function calculations of Benhar, Fabrocini and Fantoni (1989).
