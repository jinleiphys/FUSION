# Status of some P-wave baryon resonances and importance of inelastic channels

> We analyze the current status of three P-wave baryon states $N(1710){1/ 2}^+$, $N(1900){3/2}^+$, and $Δ(1600){3/2}^+$ as given in the Review of Particles Physics (RPP). Since the evidence for a particle's existence is linked to its RPP "star" rating, we discuss its subjective present definition. We also present the accumulating evidence supporting these states and give our new "star" rating recommendations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three P-wave baryon resonances currently rated as three-star ("likely" to exist) in the Review of Particle Physics should be upgraded to four-star ("certain") status, provided that multi-channel analyses including inelastic channels are considered definitive rather than single-channel analyses of elastic pion-nucleon scattering alone.

## Method

The authors review and compare multiple partial-wave analysis (PWA) approaches for the N(1710)1/2+, N(1900)3/2+, and Delta(1600)3/2+ states, separating analyses that used mainly piN elastic data (Karlsruhe-Helsinki, Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley, George Washington University DAC) from those that included several inelastic channels (Kent State University, Zagreb, Argonne-Pittsburgh, Bonn-Gatchina, Julich, ANL-Osaka, Giessen). They evaluate how the Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) technique can distinguish true resonance poles from complex branch-point effects associated with inelastic channel openings, particularly the rhoN channel.

## Key numbers

- N(1710)1/2+: The rhoN channel opens at W = 1708 - i 70 MeV, which can mimic a resonance in single-channel analyses
- Delta(1600)3/2+: Breit-Wigner mass varies from 1510 +/- 20 MeV (BnGa analysis) to 1706 +/- 10 MeV (Kent State analysis); width varies from about 200 MeV to 500 MeV across different analyses
- N(1900)3/2+: L+P analysis of KH80 amplitudes finds pole at (1928 +/- 18 +/- 2) + i (152 +/- 40 +/- 9) MeV; KA84 analysis finds (1920 +/- 17 +/- 1) + i (215 +/- 37 +/- 2) MeV
- N(1900)3/2+: Decays via Delta(1232)pi with branching ratio of about 65% in gamma p -> p pi0 pi0 reactions

## Context

This work extends the discussion from the 2014 ECT* Workshop "Exciting Baryons" in Trento, where experts evaluated the status of three-star resonances in the Review of Particle Physics. The paper directly addresses the historical demotion of N(1710)1/2+ from four-star to three-star status between the 1982 and 1984 RPP editions, and the Delta(1600)3/2+ which dropped from three-star to two-star before being restored. The analysis builds on the Laurent+Pietarinen technique introduced by Svarc (2013) showing that single-channel analyses cannot distinguish resonances from complex branch-point effects, and demonstrates that multi-channel analyses from groups such as BnGa, Julich, and Kent State have now resolved these ambiguities. The findings enable a path forward for evaluating newly discovered baryon states using photoproduction data from CLAS and other facilities, particularly through channels producing K, eta, eta', and omega mesons.
