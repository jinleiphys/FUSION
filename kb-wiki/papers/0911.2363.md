# Momentum distributions in medium and heavy exotic nuclei

> We study nucleon momentum distributions of even-even isotopes of Ni, Kr, and Sn in the framework of deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS method, as well as of theoretical correlation methods based on light-front dynamics and local density approximation. The isotopic sensitivities of the calculated neutron and proton momentum distributions are investigated together with the effects of pairing and nucleon-nucleon correlations. The role of deformation on the momentum distributions in even-even Kr isotopes is discussed. For comparison, the results for the momentum distribution in nuclear matter are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nucleon momentum distributions in medium and heavy exotic nuclei (Ni, Kr, Sn isotopes) are sensitive to isotopic changes at low momenta and to nucleon-nucleon correlations at high momenta (above 1.5 fm\(^{-1}\)), but the specific correlation method used (light-front dynamics vs. local density approximation) affects the magnitude of the high-momentum tail, with the light-front dynamics method predicting systematically higher components above 3 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors calculated neutron and proton momentum distributions for even-even isotopes of Ni, Kr, and Sn using a deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS method (DDHF+BCS) with the SLy4 Skyrme force. They then incorporated nucleon-nucleon correlations beyond the mean field using two approaches: a light-front dynamics (LFD) method that relates the high-momentum tail to the deuteron momentum distribution with a strength parameter \(\beta = 5.0\), and a local density approximation (LDA) method using a Jastrow correlation function with parameter \(\gamma = 1.1\) fm\(^{-1}\). The mean-field wave functions were expanded in a deformed harmonic-oscillator basis with 12 major shells.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(\beta\) in the LFD method was set to 5.0, chosen because it yields results close to empirical data from \(y\)-scaling analyses and because the constant \(C^A\) from the ratio of nuclear to deuteron momentum distributions ranges from 4.0 for \(^{12}\)C to 4.9 for nuclear matter.
- The correlation parameter \(\gamma\) in the LDA method was set to 1.1 fm\(^{-1}\), taken from microscopic nuclear matter calculations.
- The high-momentum region where correlations become important is \(k > 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), with the LFD method predicting systematically higher components than LDA for \(k > 3\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the Sn isotopes \(^{100}\)Sn, \(^{120}\)Sn, and \(^{136}\)Sn, the total momentum distributions show appreciable differences at low momenta but no sensitivity at \(k > 1\) fm\(^{-1}\) on the linear scale shown.
- For \(^{84}\)Kr, the effect of pairing correlations on momentum distributions is small, with slightly more pronounced tails at high \(k\) when BCS correlations are included.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charge densities, radii, form factors, halo, and skin in the same isotopic chains of Ni, Kr, and Sn (Antonov et al. 2005, Sarriguren et al. 2007). It also builds on earlier calculations of nucleon momentum distributions in stable and light exotic nuclei using the natural-orbital representation (Gaidarov et al. 1996) and on the observation that high-momentum components are similar across nuclei due to nucleon-nucleon correlations (Ciofi degli Atti and Simula 1996). The results enable future comparisons with experimental momentum distributions extracted from knockout reactions with radioactive beams and with scaling analyses of inclusive electron scattering.
