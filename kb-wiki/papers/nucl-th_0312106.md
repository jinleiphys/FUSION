# Temperature dependence of modified CNO nuclear reaction rates in dense stellar plasmas

> We study the dependence of the CNO nuclear reaction rates on temperature, in the range of $10^7÷10^8$ K, the typical range of temperature evolution from a Sun-like star towards a white dwarf. We show that the temperature dependence of the CNO nuclear reaction rates is strongly affected by the presence of non-extensive statistical effects in the dense stellar core. A very small deviation from the Maxwell-Boltzmann particle distribution implies a relevant enhancement of the CNO reaction rate and could explain the presence of heavier elements (e.g. Fe, Mg) in the final composition of a white dwarf core. Such a behavior is consistent with the recent experimental upper limit to the fraction of energy that the Sun produces via the CNO fusion cycle.

## Key claim

A small deviation from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution (quantified by a non-extensive deformation parameter \(|\delta| \lesssim 0.0045\)) can significantly enhance CNO reaction rates in dense stellar plasmas, which may explain the presence of heavier elements like iron and magnesium in white dwarf cores, while remaining consistent with the experimental upper limit on the Sun's CNO luminosity fraction.

## Method

The authors modified the standard Maxwellian reaction rate formula for CNO nuclear reactions by replacing the Maxwell-Boltzmann energy distribution with a non-extensive (Tsallis) distribution characterized by the deformation parameter \(\delta = (1-q)/2\). They then computed the resulting non-Maxwellian reaction rates, luminosity contributions from the pp chain and CNO cycle, and the equilibrium chemical composition of CNO nuclei as functions of temperature in the range \(10^7\) to \(10^8\) K. The deformation parameter was constrained using the experimental upper limit on the solar CNO neutrino flux.

## Key numbers

- The non-extensive correction to CNO luminosity ranges from 37% to more than 53% for \(|\delta| = 0.0045\).
- Above \(T \approx 2 \times 10^7\) K, the luminosity is mainly due to the CNO cycle.
- The experimental upper limit on the CNO-to-pp luminosity ratio in the Sun is \((L_{CNO}/L_{\odot}) \simeq 7.3\%\).
- The classical maximum CNO neutrino flux is \((\Phi_{CNO}^{max})_M \simeq 2.49 \times 10^9\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\).
- The deformation parameter is constrained to \(|\delta| \simeq 0.0045\), corresponding to \(0.991 \lesssim q \lesssim 1.009\).
- The corrective factor to the \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O concentrations due to deformed statistics is of order 10%; the correction to \(^{14}\)N is about one order of magnitude smaller.

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment of stellar nuclear reaction rates based on Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (Rolfs and Clayton) by applying non-extensive statistical mechanics (Tsallis formalism) to the CNO cycle in dense stellar plasmas. The results are consistent with solar neutrino constraints from Bahcall (2002) and suggest that non-extensive effects could resolve the puzzle of white dwarfs with iron cores, which have been observed to have smaller radii than predicted by standard carbon-oxygen composition models (White Dwarf, 2003). The deformation parameter value also agrees with that derived from kinetic equations in the presence of a random microfield distribution (Lavagno et al., 2002).
