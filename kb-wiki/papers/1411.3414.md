# Determining Compositeness of Hadronic Resonances: the $Λ(1405)$ Radiative Decay and the $a_{0} (980)$-$f_{0} (980)$ Mixing

> Recently the concept of compositeness has been developed so as to distinguish whether interested hadrons are hadronic molecules or not. Here, in terms of compositeness, we investigate $\bar{K} N$ molecular structure of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance with the $Λ(1405)$ radiative decay and $K \bar{K}$ molecular structure of the $a_{0} (980)$ and $f_{0} (980)$ resonances with the $a_{0} (980)$-$f_{0} (980)$ mixing.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the compositeness (the fraction of the hadronic wave function corresponding to a two-hadron molecular state) of the $\Lambda(1405)$, $a_0(980)$, and $f_0(980)$ resonances can be constrained from experimental observables, but the extracted values are model-dependent and, for the $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$, the experimental mixing intensity excludes the possibility that both are simultaneously pure $K\bar{K}$ molecules.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach to describe meson-baryon and meson-meson scattering, where resonances appear as poles in the scattering amplitude. They relate the compositeness $X$ to the pole position and coupling constant via $X = -g^2 dG/ds|_{s=s_{\text{pole}}}$, where $G$ is the two-body loop function. For $\Lambda(1405)$, they compute the radiative decay width $\Lambda(1405) \to \Lambda\gamma$ and $\Sigma^0\gamma$ from Feynman diagrams with meson-baryon loops, treating the $\bar{K}N$ coupling constant as a free parameter linked to the compositeness. For $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$, they compute the $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing intensity from diagrams involving $K\bar{K}$ intermediate states and constrain the $K\bar{K}$ compositeness of each resonance from the experimental mixing intensity.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda(1405)$ mass $M_{\Lambda(1405)} = 1405\ \text{MeV}$ and width $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1405)} = 50\ \text{MeV}$ (from PDG).
- Experimental radiative decay widths (from isobar model fit of $K^-p$ atom decays): $\Gamma_{\Lambda\gamma} = 27 \pm 8\ \text{keV}$, $\Gamma_{\Sigma^0\gamma} = 10 \pm 4\ \text{keV}$ or $23 \pm 7\ \text{keV}$.
- From $\Gamma_{\Lambda\gamma} = 27 \pm 8\ \text{keV}$, the $\bar{K}N$ compositeness is estimated as $|X_{\bar{K}N}| = 0.5 \pm 0.2$.
- From $\Gamma_{\Sigma^0\gamma} = 10 \pm 4\ \text{keV}$, the constraint is $|X_{\bar{K}N}| > 0.5$.
- $a_0(980)$-$f_0(980)$ mixing intensity from BESIII: $\xi_{fa} = 0.60 \pm 0.20\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.12\ (\text{sys}) \pm 0.26\ (\text{para})\ \%$ with upper limit $\xi_{fa}|_{\text{upper limit}} = 1.1\ \%$ (90% C.L.).
- For the mixing analysis, masses are fixed to $M_a = M_f = 980\ \text{MeV}$, widths $\Gamma_a = 100\ \text{MeV}$, $\Gamma_f = 50\ \text{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of compositeness developed by Hyodo (2011), Aceti (2012), and Sekihara (2014) to connect with specific experimental observables. For $\Lambda(1405)$, it builds on earlier studies of its spatial size by Sekihara (2008, 2010, 2012) and the radiative decay calculation by Geng (2007). For the scalar mesons, it addresses the long-standing question of whether $a_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ are $K\bar{K}$ molecules, as proposed by Weinstein (1982), or compact tetraquark states as suggested by Jaffe (1976). The results enable future experimental measurements of the $\Lambda(1405)$ radiative decay and the $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing to directly constrain the molecular content of these resonances.
