# Exploring robust correlations between fermionic dark matter model parameters and neutron star properties: A two-fluid perspective

> The current observational properties of neutron stars have not definitively ruled out the possibility of dark matter. In this study, we primarily focus on exploring correlations between the dark matter model parameters and different neutron star properties using a rich set of EOSs. We adopt a two-fluid approach to calculate the properties of neutron stars. For the nuclear matter EOS, we employ several realistic EOS derived from the relativistic mean field model (RMF), each exhibiting varying stiffness and composition. In parallel, we look into the dark matter EOS, considering fermionic matter with repulsive interaction described by a relativistic mean field Lagrangian. A reasonable range of parameters is sampled meticulously. Interestingly, our results reveal a promising correlation between the dark matter model parameters and stellar properties, particularly when we ignore the uncertainties in the nuclear matter EOS. However, when introducing uncertainties in the nuclear sector, the correlation weakens, suggesting that the task of conclusively constraining any particular dark matter model might be challenging using global properties alone, such as mass, radius, and tidal deformability. Notably, we find that dark-matter admixed stars tend to have higher central baryonic density, potentially allowing for non-nucleonic degrees of freedom or direct Urca processes in stars with lower masses. There is also a tantalizing hint regarding the detection of stars with the same mass but different surface temperatures, which may indicate the presence of dark matter. With our robust and extensive dataset, we delve deeper and demonstrate that even in the presence of dark matter, the semi-universal C-Love relation remains intact.

# Exploring robust correlations between fermionic dark matter model parameters and neutron star properties: A two-fluid perspective

## Key claim
When uncertainties in the nuclear matter equation of state are ignored, strong correlations (approximately 0.9) exist between dark matter mass fraction and neutron star properties such as maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability; however, when nuclear sector uncertainties are introduced by considering multiple nuclear equations of state simultaneously, these correlations disappear, making it challenging to constrain dark matter models using global neutron star properties alone.

## Method
The authors employed a two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff formalism treating baryonic and dark matter as separate fluids interacting only gravitationally. For nuclear matter, four relativistic mean field equations of state (EOS1 through EOS4) with varying stiffness were used, while fermionic dark matter was described by a relativistic mean field Lagrangian with a single fermionic component and a dark vector meson. A total of 50,000 dark matter parameter combinations (dark matter mass, self-interaction coupling, and mass fraction) were sampled and solved for each nuclear EOS, yielding 200,000 mass-radius calculations, which were then filtered to require neutron star masses above 1.9 solar masses and non-halo configurations.

## Key numbers
- Dark matter mass range sampled: 0.5 GeV to 4.5 GeV
- Dark matter self-interaction coupling range: 0.1 fm to 5 fm
- Dark matter mass fraction range: 0% to 25%
- Total of 200,000 mass-radius calculations performed (50,000 dark matter EOSs for each of 4 nuclear EOSs)
- After filtering, remaining samples: 25,000 for EOS1, 14,000 for EOS2, 10,000 for EOS3, and 20,000 for EOS4
- Maximum masses of the four nuclear EOSs without dark matter range from 2.10 to 2.74 solar masses
- Radii for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars without dark matter range from 12.55 km to 13.78 km
- Tidal deformability for 1.4 solar mass neutron stars without dark matter ranges from 462 to 844
- The softest EOS (EOS3) can sustain only up to approximately 10% dark matter fraction, while EOS1 can sustain approximately 24%, EOS2 approximately 14%, and EOS4 approximately 22%
- Strong negative correlation of approximately 0.9 between dark matter mass fraction and maximum gravitational mass when using a single nuclear EOS (EOS1)
- Strong correlation of approximately 0.9 between dark matter mass fraction and radius and tidal deformability at masses 1.4, 1.6, and 2.0 solar masses for EOS1

## Context
This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars by Das (2020) and Karkevandi (2021) by systematically examining correlations between dark matter parameters and neutron star observables using a large ensemble of equations of state. The study builds on the relativistic mean field framework for nuclear matter developed by Malik (2023) and applies Kendall rank correlation analysis, a non-parametric method suitable for non-linear relationships. The finding that the semi-universal C-Love relation remains intact even in the presence of dark matter distinguishes dark matter effects from those of modified gravity, where such correlations break as shown by Nobleson (2023). The results suggest that future constraints on dark matter models will require additional observables beyond mass, radius, and tidal deformability, such as neutron star cooling rates or direct Urca process thresholds.
