# Ab initio computations from $^{78}$Ni towards $^{70}$Ca along neutron number $N=50$

> We present coupled-cluster computations of nuclei with neutron number $N=50$ "south" of $^{78}$Ni using nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. We find an erosion of the magic number $N=50$ toward $^{70}$Ca manifesting itself by an onset of deformation and increased complexity in the ground states. For $^{78}$Ni, we predict a low-lying rotational band consistent with recent data, which up until now has been a challenge for ab initio nuclear models. Ground states are deformed in $^{76}$Fe, $^{74}$Cr, and $^{72}$Ti, although the spherical states are too close in energy to unambiguously identify the shape of the ground state within the uncertainty estimates. In $^{70}$Ca, the potential energy landscape from quadrupole-constrained Hartree-Fock computations flattens, and the deformation becomes less rigid. We also compute the low-lying spectra and $B({\rm E2})$ values for these neutron-rich $N=50$ nuclei.

## Key claim
The magic neutron number N=50 erodes south of 78Ni, with an onset of deformation appearing in ground states, but the spherical and deformed states in 76Fe, 74Cr, and 72Ti are too close in energy to unambiguously identify the ground-state shape within the uncertainty estimates, and 70Ca exhibits no well-defined minima.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-cluster computations using nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory (the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction). They employed axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock calculations with a quadrupole constraint to map potential energy surfaces, then applied coupled-cluster with singles and doubles (CCSD) and angular-momentum projection to compute spectra and B(E2) transition strengths for N=50 isotones from 78Ni to 70Ca.

## Key numbers
- 78Ni: prolate minimum has deformation parameter beta2 approximately 0.28; spherical configuration has the lowest energy in model spaces Nmax=10-12 with hbar-omega=12-16 MeV
- 70Ca: prolate minimum has deformation parameter beta2 approximately 0.16; potential energy landscape flattens with no well-defined minima
- 76Fe: larger prolate minimum has beta2 approximately 0.22
- 74Cr: larger prolate minimum has beta2 approximately 0.25
- 72Ti: larger prolate minimum has beta2 approximately 0.22
- 78Ni: first excited 2+ state measured at 2.6 MeV (Taniuchi 2019); a state at 2.9 MeV tentatively assigned to a deformed 2+ state
- 78Ni: shell model predicted the low-lying 0+2 state associated with the deformed band at about 2.6 MeV (Nowacki 2016)
- Hartree-Fock computations employed eleven major oscillator shells (Nmax=10) with oscillator frequency hbar-omega=12 MeV

## Context
This work extends ab initio coupled-cluster computations from doubly magic 78Ni into more neutron-rich N=50 nuclei, building on prior shell-model calculations by Nowacki (2016) that identified a "fifth" island of inversion and on experimental work by Taniuchi (2019) that established shape coexistence in 78Ni. The results are compared with large-scale shell model (Nowacki 2016), Monte Carlo shell model (Taniuchi 2019), valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) calculations, and valence-space density matrix renormalization group (VS-DMRG) computations by Tichai (2024). The findings enable future studies of the erosion of magic numbers and shape coexistence in neutron-rich nuclei, though the authors note that credible computations of 70Ca would require generator coordinate methods and coupling to the continuum.
