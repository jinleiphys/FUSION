# Unified Parametrization of Quark and Lepton Mixing Matrices

> We present a unified parametrization of quark and lepton mixing matrices. By using some simple relations between the mixing angles of quarks and leptons, i.e., the quark-lepton complementarity, we parametrize the lepton mixing matrix with the Wolfenstein parameters $λ$ and $A$ of the quark mixing matrix. It is shown that the Wolfenstein parameter $λ$ can measure both the deviation of the quark mixing matrix from the unit matrix, and the deviation of the lepton mixing matrix from the exactly bimaximal mixing pattern.

# Unified Parametrization of Quark and Lepton Mixing Matrices

## Key claim
The quark (CKM) and lepton (PMNS) mixing matrices can be parametrized with a single set of parameters by assuming the quark-lepton complementarity relations between their mixing angles, specifically that the sum of the Cabibbo angle and the solar neutrino mixing angle equals 45 degrees, and the sum of the atmospheric mixing angle and the corresponding quark angle also equals 45 degrees. The Wolfenstein parameter λ from the CKM matrix then simultaneously quantifies the deviation of the CKM matrix from the unit matrix and the deviation of the PMNS matrix from the exactly bimaximal mixing pattern, but only if these numerical relations hold to good accuracy.

## Method
The authors start from the standard parametrization of a 3×3 mixing matrix with three mixing angles and one CP-violating phase. For the CKM matrix, they adopt the Wolfenstein parametrization in powers of λ = sinθ_C = 0.2243. Using the quark-lepton complementarity relations (θ_12 + θ'_12 = π/4, θ_23 + θ'_23 = π/4, and θ_31 ∼ θ'_31), they express the PMNS matrix mixing angles in terms of the CKM Wolfenstein parameters λ and A. They then expand the PMNS matrix as a power series in λ around the bimaximal mixing pattern, introducing two new parameters ζ and ξ (or ζ' and ξ') to describe the CP-violating sector and the U_e3 element, and consider two cases depending on whether U_e3 scales as λ^3 or λ^2.

## Key numbers
- λ = sinθ_C = 0.2243 ± 0.0016 (Wolfenstein parameter from CKM matrix)
- Best fit CKM angles: θ_12 = 12.9°, θ_23 = 2.4°, θ_31 = 0.2°
- Best fit CKM parameters: A = 0.82, ρ = 0.20, η = 0.33
- Best fit lepton mixing angles: θ_atm = 45.0° ± 6.5°, θ_sol = 32.6° ± 1.6°, θ_chz = 0° ± 7.4°
- θ_C + θ_sol = 45.5° at best fit points (supporting quark-lepton complementarity)
- sin²2θ_atm = 1.00 ± 0.05, tan²θ_sol = 0.41 ± 0.05, sin²2θ_chz = 0 ± 0.065
- For Case 1 (U_e3 ∼ λ^3): Jarlskog parameter J = (1/4)Aλ³ξ(1 − 2λ²) = 0.0022 ξ, with √(ζ²+ξ²) ∼ 8.2
- For Case 2 (U_e3 ∼ λ^2): J = (1/4)Aλ²ξ'(1 − 2λ²) = 0.0099 ξ', with √(ζ'²+ξ'²) ∼ 1.8
- Best fit point of sin²θ'_31 is 0.006 (preliminary estimate)
- Rescaled λ (dividing by √2) gives 0.1586, consistent with the range 0.08 < λ < 0.17 from prior work by Li (2005)

## Context
This work extends the quark-lepton complementarity idea proposed by Raidal (2004) and Smirnov (2004), which suggests simple numerical relations between quark and lepton mixing angles as evidence for grand unification or quark-lepton symmetry. The authors unify previous separate parametrizations of the PMNS matrix based on the bimaximal mixing pattern by Rodejohann (2002) and Li (2005), showing that those parametrizations emerge naturally as the leading and next-to-leading orders of this unified expansion. The work enables future determination of the PMNS matrix structure and lepton CP-violation parameters (ζ, ξ) once more precise measurements of |U_e3| and the Jarlskog parameter J become available from neutrino oscillation experiments and neutrinoless double beta decay searches.
