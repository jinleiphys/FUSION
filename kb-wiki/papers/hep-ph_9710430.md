# New developments in threshold pion photo- and electroproduction

> Photoproduction of neutral and charged pions off nucleons and deuterium has been precisely calculated in baryon chiral perturbation theory. I review the predictions with the accurate data that have become available over the last few years. Some progress in the description of neutral pion electroproduction off protons is also discussed.

# New developments in threshold pion photo- and electroproduction

## Key claim
Baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) calculations at order q⁴ for S-waves and q³ for P-waves can describe threshold pion photo- and electroproduction data, but the convergence for neutral pion photoproduction off protons is slow, making the electric dipole amplitude E₀₊ an unreliable test of chiral dynamics; the charged pion channels show better convergence and allow extraction of πN scattering lengths.

## Method
Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) was used to calculate threshold pion photo- and electroproduction amplitudes to order q⁴ for S-waves and q³ for P-waves, treating nucleons as static sources and expanding in external momenta, quark masses, and inverse nucleon mass. Low-energy constants (LECs) were determined by fitting to πN observables or estimated via resonance saturation from vector, axial vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar meson multiplets. For deuterium, the kernel was chirally expanded and matrix elements evaluated using phenomenological NN potentials (Argonne V18, Reid soft core, Nijmegen, Paris).

## Key numbers
- E₀₊ threshold for π⁺n: 28.2 ± 0.6 × 10⁻³/Mπ (CHPT prediction), compared to experimental values 27.9 ± 0.5, 28.8 ± 0.7, and 27.6 ± 0.3 × 10⁻³/Mπ
- E₀₊ threshold for π⁻p: -32.7 ± 0.6 × 10⁻³/Mπ (CHPT prediction), compared to experimental values -31.4 ± 1.3, -32.2 ± 1.2, and -31.5 ± 0.8 × 10⁻³/Mπ
- Isovector πN scattering length -b₁: 87.3 ± 2.4 × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹ from E₀₊ via Panofsky ratio, compared to 92 ± 4 from direct CHPT πN calculation
- E₀₊ at π⁰p threshold: -1.16 × 10⁻³/Mπ (CHPT), -1.22 (dispersion relations), -1.31 ± 0.8 and -1.32 ± 0.05 ± 0.06 (experiment)
- E₀₊ at π⁺n threshold from neutral channel: -0.44 × 10⁻³/Mπ (CHPT), approximately -0.4 (both dispersion relations and experiment)
- Reduced P-wave multipole p₁ for π⁰p: 10.33 (CHPT), 10.52 (dispersion relations), 10.02 ± 0.15 and 10.26 ± 0.10 (experiment), in units of |q||k| 10⁻³/Mπ⁺³
- Reduced P-wave multipole p₂₃ for π⁰p: 10.85 (dispersion relations), 11.44 ± 0.09 and 11.62 ± 0.08 (experiment), in units of |q||k| 10⁻³/Mπ⁺³
- Deuteron electric dipole amplitude E_d: -1.8 ± 0.2 × 10⁻³/Mπ⁺ (CHPT), compared to reanalysis of Saclay data giving -1.7 ± 0.2 and preliminary Saskatoon result -1.45 ± 0.09
- Photon asymmetry Σ at Eγ = 155 MeV and θ = 80°-100°: preliminary measured value 8 ± 5%, CHPT prediction 10%
- Panofsky ratio P = 1.543 ± 0.008

## Context
This review extends the HBCHPT calculations reported at the MIT workshop (1996) by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (BKM collaboration) for neutral pion photoproduction, and by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) for charged channels. The work contradicts the earlier low-energy theorem (LET) from 1970 for E₀₊ in γp → π⁰p, showing it is only a "low energy guess" due to non-analytic contributions from triangle diagrams. The deuterium calculation by Beane, Bernard, Lee, and Meißner (1997) enables extraction of the elementary neutron amplitude γn → π⁰n, which is experimentally inaccessible directly. The electroproduction results at k² = -0.1 GeV² from NIKHEF and Mainz provide constraints on new LECs a₃ and a₄, with a soft-pion theorem by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) relating the counterterms for E and L multipoles.
