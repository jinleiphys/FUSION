# Thermal Dileptons from Coarse-Grained Transport as Fireball Probes at SIS Energies

> Utilizing a coarse-graining method to convert hadronic transport simulations of Au+Au collisions at SIS energies into local temperature, baryon and pion densities, we compute the pertinent radiation of thermal dileptons based on an in-medium $ρ$ spectral function that describes available spectra at ultrarelativistic collision energies. In particular, we analyze how far the resulting yields and slopes of the invariant-mass spectra can probe the lifetime and temperatures of the fireball. We find that dilepton radiation sets in after the initial overlap phase of the colliding nuclei of about 7 fm/c, and lasts for about 13 fm/c. This duration closely coincides with the development of the transverse collectivity of the baryons, thus establishing a direct correlation between hadronic collective effects and thermal EM radiation, and supporting a near local equilibration of the system. This fireball "lifetime" is substantially smaller than the typical 20-30 fm/c that naive considerations of the density evolution alone would suggest. We furthermore find that the total dilepton yield radiated into the invariant-mass window of $M=0.3-0.7$ GeV/$c^{2}$, normalized to the number of charged pions, follows a relation to the lifetime found earlier in the (ultra-) relativistic regime of heavy-ion collisions, and thus corroborates the versatility of this tool. The spectral slopes of the invariant-mass spectra above the $ϕ$ mass provide a thermometer of the hottest phases of the collision, and agree well with the maximal temperatures extracted from the coarse-grained hadron spectra.

## Key claim

The authors find that thermal dilepton radiation in Au+Au collisions at SIS energies (1.23 AGeV) is emitted over a "radiation window" of about 13 fm/c, which is substantially shorter than the 20-30 fm/c that naive density evolution would suggest, and that this window closely coincides with the development of transverse collectivity in the baryons, supporting near local equilibration.

## Method

The authors applied a coarse-graining method to UrQMD hadronic transport simulations of central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV beam energy, averaging particle distributions over 21^3 spatial cells of 1 fm^3 volume in 1 fm/c time steps to extract local temperatures (from pion transverse-mass spectra), baryon densities, and pion chemical potentials. They then computed thermal dilepton emission using an in-medium ρ-meson spectral function from hadronic many-body theory, including a fugacity factor with an effective pion number κ=1.12 determined from the dominant ρ production channels in UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- The fireball "radiation window" lasts approximately 13 fm/c (from τ≈8-21 fm/c to produce 85% of the emission), compared to the 20-25 fm/c the inner cube spends above nuclear saturation density
- The total dilepton yield in the invariant-mass window M=0.3-0.7 GeV/c^2 normalized to charged pions is N_{e+e-}/N_{π±}=17.0×10^{-6} (with N_{e+e-}=2.9×10^{-4} and N_{π±}=17.1 in central Au+Au at 1.23 AGeV)
- The extracted fireball lifetime from the normalized dilepton yield is approximately 12-14 fm/c
- Maximal temperature of 75-85 MeV is maintained for approximately 10 fm/c around the maximal compression phase
- The average number of pions creating a ρ meson is κ=1.12, with ππ annihilation contributing ~15% and baryon resonance decays contributing ~85% of ρ production
- Using nucleon temperatures instead of pion temperatures increases the dilepton yield by about 25%

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach previously applied at SPS energies by Huovinen (2002) and more recently at SIS and SPS energies by Endres (2014, 2015), improving upon those studies through quantitative temperature extraction with error estimates and an improved determination of the pion fugacity factor. The authors establish a direct correlation between hadronic collective effects and thermal electromagnetic radiation at SIS energies, and demonstrate that the relation between dilepton yield and fireball lifetime proposed by Rapp (2014) for ultrarelativistic collisions also holds at these lower energies when normalizing to charged pions rather than charged particles. This enables using thermal dileptons as quantitative probes of fireball lifetime and temperature across a wide range of collision energies.
