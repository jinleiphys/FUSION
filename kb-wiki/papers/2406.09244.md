# Final-state interactions in neutrino-induced proton knockout from argon in MicroBooNE

> Neutrino event generators make use of intranuclear cascade models (INCs), to predict the kinematics of hadrons produced in neutrino-nucleus interactions. We perform a consistent comparison of different INCs, by using the same set of events as input to the NEUT, NuWro, Achilles and INCL INCs. The inputs correspond to calculations of the fully differential single-proton knockout cross section, either in the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) or plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), both including realistic nuclear hole spectral functions. We compare the INC results to DWIA calculations with an optical potential, used extensively in the analysis of (e,e'p) experiments. We point out a systematic discrepancy between both approaches. We apply the INC results to recent MicroBooNE data. We assess the influence of the choice of spectral function, finding that large variations in realistic spectral functions are indistinguishable with present data. The data is underpredicted, with strength missing in the region where two-nucleon knockout and resonance production contribute. However, the data is underpredicted also in regions of low transverse missing momentum, where one-nucleon knockout dominates. The inclusion of the interference with two-body currents could lead to additional strength in this region.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that different intranuclear cascade models (INCs) produce systematically different predictions for neutrino-induced proton knockout from argon, and that all tested INCs underpredict MicroBooNE data even in kinematic regions where single-nucleon knockout dominates, suggesting that interference with two-body currents may be needed to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method
The authors generated fully differential single-proton knockout cross sections for argon using both the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) and plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with realistic nuclear hole spectral functions. These events were then used as identical inputs to four different intranuclear cascade models (NEUT, NuWro, Achilles, and INCL) to model inelastic final-state interactions. The INC results were compared to RDWIA calculations with an optical potential (the standard approach in electron scattering analyses) and to recent MicroBooNE data.

## Key numbers
- Elastic FSI effects (RDWIA vs RPWIA) produce a 10% effect on observables compared to plane-wave calculations
- The titanium spectral function yields approximately 20.3 neutrons instead of 22, as seen by the sum of occupation numbers
- Shell model states have reduced occupation numbers of the order of 20-40%
- Short-range correlations account for approximately 20% of missing strength
- The 1f7/2 shell in argon has an occupation number of 0.205 (fraction of completely filled orbital)
- The 1d5/2 shell has a separation energy of 15.46 MeV with a Gaussian width of 4 MeV
- The 2s1/2 shell has a separation energy of 12.21 MeV with a Gaussian width of 2 MeV

## Context
This work extends comparisons of intranuclear cascade models for neutrino-nucleus interactions (Franco-Patino 2023, Gonzalez-Jimenez 2021) by using consistent inputs across multiple INCs and benchmarking against optical potential calculations used in electron scattering analyses (Cooper 1993). The approach combines fully differential RDWIA calculations with realistic spectral functions and INCs, representing a step forward in modeling neutrino-induced nucleon knockout. The results enable improved interpretation of MicroBooNE data and highlight the need for including two-body current interference effects, as suggested by Franco-Munoz (2022) and Lovato (2023).
