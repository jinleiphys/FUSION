# Regarding the possibility to observe the LHCb hidden-charm strange pentaquark $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ in antikaon-induced $J/ψ$ meson production on protons and nuclei near the $J/ψ$$Λ$ production threshold

> We study the near-threshold $J/ψ$ meson production on protons and nuclei by considering incoherent direct non-resonant $K^-p \to {J/ψ}Λ$ and two-step resonant ${K^-}p \to P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to {J/ψ}Λ$ charmonium production processes. We calculate the absolute excitation functions, energy and momentum distributions for the non-resonant, resonant and for the combined (non-resonant plus resonant) production of $J/ψ$ mesons off protons as well as off carbon and tungsten target nuclei at near-threshold incident antikaon energies by considering these elementary production channels as well as by assuming the spin-parity assignment of the hidden-charm resonance $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ with strangeness as $J^P=(3/2)^-$ within six different scenarios for the branching ratio $Br[P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to {J/ψ}Λ]$ of the decay $P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to {J/ψ}Λ$. We show that the combined observables considered reveal definite sensitivity to these scenarios, which means that they may be an important tool to provide further evidence for the existence of the pentaquark $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ resonance and to get valuable information on its decay rate to the ${J/ψ}Λ$ final state. Their measurements could be performed in the future at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hidden-charm strange pentaquark $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ could be observed in antikaon-induced $J/\psi$ production on protons and nuclei near the $J/\psi\Lambda$ production threshold, but only if the combined (non-resonant plus resonant) observables show sufficient sensitivity to different branching ratio scenarios for the decay $P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to J/\psi\Lambda$, which remains experimentally unmeasured.

## Method

The author calculated the absolute excitation functions, energy and momentum distributions for $J/\psi$ meson production off protons, carbon, and tungsten targets by considering both the incoherent direct non-resonant $K^-p \to J/\psi\Lambda$ process and the two-step resonant $K^-p \to P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to J/\psi\Lambda$ process. The non-resonant background was estimated using a phenomenological model based on available experimental data for $K^-p \to \phi X$ and $K^-p \to J/\psi X$ cross sections, assuming the same $J/\psi/\phi$ cross section ratio near threshold as at high excess energies. The resonant contribution was modeled using a standard Breit-Wigner prescription with the spin-parity assignment $J^P = (3/2)^-$ for the $P_{cs}(4459)^0$, and calculations were performed for six different scenarios for the branching ratio $Br[P_{cs}(4459)^0 \to J/\psi\Lambda]$.

## Key numbers

- The near-threshold center-of-mass energy region studied is 4.2126 GeV $\le \sqrt{s} \le$ 4.6126 GeV, corresponding to laboratory incident $K^-$ beam momenta 8.844 GeV/c $\le p_{K^-} \le$ 10.728 GeV/c.
- The threshold center-of-mass energy for $J/\psi\Lambda$ production is $\sqrt{s_{\rm th}} = m_{J/\psi} + m_{\Lambda} = 4.2126$ GeV.
- The $J/\psi$-nucleon absorption cross section used is $\sigma_{J/\psi N} = 3.5$ mb, motivated by SLAC photoproduction experiments.
- The $K^-N$ total cross section used is $\sigma_{K^-N}^{\rm tot} = 22$ mb for beam momenta of interest.
- The slope parameter for the $J/\psi$ differential cross section is $b_{J/\psi} = 2.1$ GeV$^{-2}$, derived from the $K^-p \to \phi\Lambda$ reaction at ${\tilde p}_{K^-} = 2.24$ GeV/c.
- The non-resonant total cross section for $K^-p \to J/\psi\Lambda$ near the resonant energy $W = 4.4588$ GeV is approximately 1 nb.
- At $p_{K^-} = 9$ GeV/c, the maximum $J/\psi$ laboratory production angle is $\theta_{J/\psi}^{\rm max} = 2.089^\circ$; at $p_{K^-} = 10$ GeV/c, it is $\theta_{J/\psi}^{\rm max} = 5.452^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the model developed by Paryev (2016) for studying the $P_c(4450)^+$ pentaquark in $J/\psi$ photoproduction on nuclei to the strange pentaquark $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ discovered by LHCb (2021) in $\Xi_b^- \to K^- J/\psi\Lambda$ decays. The calculations provide predictions for $J/\psi$ production in $K^-p$, $K^-{}^{12}{\rm C}$, and $K^-{}^{184}{\rm W}$ collisions that could be tested at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility, specifically at the planned K10 beam line. The non-resonant background estimates differ from the effective Lagrangian (Model I) and Regge (Model II) approaches of Clymton (2021) by factors of approximately 16 and 4, respectively, at the resonant energy.
