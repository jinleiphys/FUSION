# Proton shape fluctuations and its relation to DIS

> We review the recent progress in extracting the proton fluctuating substructure by studying exclusive processes at HERA, and the applications of these developments in the interpretation of the LHC heavy ion data. The possibilities to extract the proton geometry directly from the LHC high-multiplicity proton-nucleus and proton-proton collision data is also discussed.

## Key claim

The proton's fluctuating substructure, constrained by exclusive J/Psi production data from HERA, is necessary to describe both the incoherent diffractive cross section at HERA and the elliptic flow observed in proton-lead collisions at the LHC; however, the proton transverse area preferred by Bayesian analyses of LHC data is much larger than that extracted from the vector meson production data, and it is not a priori clear that these two quantities are the same.

## Method

The author reviews the extraction of the proton's fluctuating geometry by fitting the HERA exclusive J/Psi production data using a model where the proton density profile is parametrized by the width of the distribution for constituent quark positions (B_qc) and the width of the Gaussian gluon density around each quark (B_q), with additional log-normal fluctuations of the overall density of each hot spot. The resulting Wilson lines are then used as input for IP-Glasma simulations of proton-nucleus collisions, where the classical Yang-Mills equations are solved to obtain the pre-thermal energy-momentum tensor, which initializes hydrodynamical evolution followed by hadronization via the UrQMD afterburner.

## Key numbers

* The exclusive J/Psi photoproduction cross section at W = 75 GeV is compared with H1 data (Alexa 2013), showing that large geometry fluctuations are necessary to describe the incoherent cross section.
* The elliptic flow v2 in proton-lead collisions computed with fluctuating protons reproduces the CMS measurement (Chatrchyan 2013), whereas simulations with round protons produce too small v2.
* In ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions, the incoherent J/Psi production cross section at large momentum transfer is vastly different when nucleon substructure fluctuations are included versus when they are not.
* The ALICE collaboration reported that the incoherent J/Psi cross section becomes unobservable at the highest photon-proton center-of-mass energies, with the highest energy bin corresponding to W ~ 800 GeV.
* The Bayesian analysis of Ref. Moreland (2017) prefers initial entropy deposition similar to the IP-Glasma framework, and the case of a single constituent (no substructure) is strongly disfavored.

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke 2012) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuations of the proton's geometric shape, which were absent in earlier simulations that produced too small elliptic flow compared to CMS data (Schenke 2014). The proton substructure constrained by HERA exclusive J/Psi data (Mantysaari 2016) enables hydrodynamical simulations to reproduce LHC collective flow measurements in proton-lead collisions, and opens the possibility to study the x-dependence of proton shape fluctuations via ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC. Alternative approaches include purely initial-state explanations of flow-like behavior (Dusling 2017, Mace 2018) and Bayesian extraction of proton profiles from LHC data (Moreland 2017).
