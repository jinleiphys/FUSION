# Possibly stable configurations of $Θ^+_5$ in the flux-tube model

> In this work, following the literature, we investigate the semi-classical picture where various spatial configurations for $Θ^+_5$ were suggested by several authors. We use the flux tube model where the effective Column coefficient and string tension are extracted from the lattice QCD calculations, to obtain the interactions among the constituent quarks, which is later to be taken as an effective potential. It is reasonable to assume that keeping the simple spatial shape, only the breathing mode is considered, thus the whole system is much simplified and described by only one variable. Substituting the potential into the Schrödinger equation, we evaluate the binding energy of the system. We find that each of the configuration has higher symmetries and corresponds to a local minimum of energy and one of them results in a mass which is smaller than the others. It is interesting to notice that with such a very simplified semi-classical picture the minimal value corresponds to a mass of 1530 to 1554 MeV for $Θ^+_5$. The good agreement with the LEPS' data may indicate that such simplified picture indeed reflects physical reality in some sense.

## Key claim

The authors find that among several semi-classical flux-tube configurations for the pentaquark \(\Theta^+_5\), the planar double Y-shape where two quarks at the ends of the shorter edges form color-\(\bar 3\) states yields the lowest mass, in good agreement with the LEPS data; however, this result depends on the strong simplifying assumption that only the breathing mode is considered and the shape of each configuration remains invariant.

## Method

The authors use the flux-tube model with a non-relativistic Hamiltonian consisting of a kinetic term, a one-gluon-exchange Coulomb potential, and a confining potential proportional to the minimal total length of the flux tubes. The Coulomb coefficient \(A_{5q}=0.1366\) and string tension \(\sigma_{5q}=0.165\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) are taken from lattice QCD calculations by Okiharu et al., and the zero-point energy \(C_{5q}=-3.002\ \mathrm{GeV}\) is fixed by requiring the model to reproduce the nucleon mass. The Schrödinger equation is solved for four distinct spatial configurations (planar double Y, tetrahedral, twisted non-planar, and a second planar double Y) under the constraint that only the breathing mode is active, reducing each system to a single radial variable.

## Key numbers

- The mass of \(\Theta^+_5\) for the configuration in Fig. 1(e) (planar double Y with short-edge color-\(\bar 3\) states) is \(1.537\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The masses for the other configurations are: Fig. 1(a) \(1.743\ \mathrm{GeV}\), Fig. 1(c) \(1.756\ \mathrm{GeV}\), Fig. 1(d) \(1.754\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The minimal mass obtained by varying the light quark mass \(m_q\) is \(1.523\ \mathrm{GeV}\) at \(m_q = 0.29\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (with \(m_{\bar{s}} = 0.42\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
- The LEPS experimental value is \(M = 1.54 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with \(\Gamma < 25\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The string tension is \(\sigma_{5q} = 0.165\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and the Coulomb coefficient is \(A_{5q} = 0.1366\), both extracted from lattice QCD by Okiharu et al.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model calculations of Okiharu et al. (2003) and Alexandrou et al. (2003) by quantitatively comparing the masses of several proposed semi-classical configurations for the \(\Theta^+_5\) pentaquark, including the tetrahedral structure suggested by Song et al. (2003). The result that the planar double Y configuration with short-edge color-\(\bar 3\) states gives the lowest mass and agrees with the LEPS data (2003) suggests that this simplified picture may capture essential physics, but the authors note that a more realistic calculation including the structure and width of the pentaquark is needed as a next step.
