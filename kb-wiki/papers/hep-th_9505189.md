# Unique Closed-Form Quantization Via Generalized Path Integrals or by Natural Extension of the Standard Canonical Recipe

> The Feynman-Garrod path integral representation for time evolution is extended to arbitrary one-parameter continuous canonical transformations. One thereupon obtains a generalized Kerner-Sutcliffe formula for the unique quantum representation of the transformation generator, which can be an arbitrary classical dynamical variable. This closed-form quantization procedure is shown to be equivalent to a natural extension of the standard canonical quantization recipe -- an extension that resolves the operator-ordering ambiguity in favor of the Born-Jordan rule.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Feynman-Garrod path integral representation, originally developed for time evolution, can be extended to arbitrary one-parameter continuous canonical transformations, yielding a unique closed-form quantization procedure that resolves the operator-ordering ambiguity in favor of the Born-Jordan rule. This result is conditional on the exact treatment of the integrated q-averaging between neighboring path-integral nodes, which the author argues is often crudely approximated or ignored in textbook treatments.

## Method

The author extends the Feynman-Garrod path integral to general one-parameter continuous canonical transformations, where the generating function G(q,p) is an arbitrary real classical dynamical variable. From this path integral, a generalized Kerner-Sutcliffe formula is derived for the quantum representation of G in coordinate representation, yielding an explicit Hermitian matrix element. The author then shows this formula is equivalent to a natural extension of the standard canonical quantization recipe, where the quantum representation of the Poisson bracket of a function of q with a function of p equals (iв„Џ)^{-1} times the commutator of their individual quantum representations.

## Context

This work extends the Feynman-Garrod path integral (Feynman 1948, Garrod) and the Kerner-Sutcliffe quantization formula (Kerner) to arbitrary classical dynamical variables, not just Hamiltonians. It resolves the operator-ordering ambiguity in canonical quantization in favor of the Born-Jordan rule (Born, Cohen 1966), contrasting with inexact prescriptions such as the "mid-point prescription" (Das). The results enable a unique quantization procedure for field theories via an extension of the canonical commutation relations to arbitrary functionals of fields and their conjugate momenta.
