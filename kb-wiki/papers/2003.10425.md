# Centrality dependence of chemical freeze-out parameters and strangeness equilibration in RHIC and LHC energies

> We have estimated centrality variation of chemical freeze-out parameters from yield data at mid-rapidity of $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$ and $p$, $\bar{p}$ for collision energies of RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider), Beam Energy Scan (RHIC-BES) program, and LHC (Large Hadron Collider). We have considered a simple hadron resonance gas model and employed a formalism involving conserved charges ($B, Q, S$) of QCD for parameterization. Along with temperature and three chemical potentials ($T,μ_B,μ_Q,μ_S$), a strangeness under-saturation factor ($γ_S$) has been used to incorporate the partial equilibration in the strange sector. Our obtained freeze-out temperature does not vary much with centrality, whereas chemical potentials and $γ_S$ seem to have a significant dependence. The strange hadrons are found to deviate from a complete chemical equilibrium at freeze-out at the peripheral collisions. This deviation appears to be more prominent as the collision energy decreases at lower RHIC-BES energies. We have also shown that this departure from equilibrium reduces towards central collisions, and strange particle equilibration may happen after a threshold number of participants in $A$-$A$ collision.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strangeness equilibration in heavy-ion collisions depends on centrality, with strange hadrons deviating from complete chemical equilibrium at freeze-out in peripheral collisions, and that this deviation reduces towards central collisions, potentially reaching equilibration after a threshold number of participating nucleons (around \(N_{part}=150\)), though the freeze-out temperature shows little variation with centrality.

## Method

The authors used a hadron resonance gas model within a grand canonical ensemble framework, incorporating conserved charge constraints (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) and a strangeness under-saturation factor \(\gamma_S\). They solved a system of five equations, including strangeness neutrality, net-charge to net-baryon ratio, net-baryon to total-baryon ratio, net-baryon to total-hadron yield ratio, and a kaon-to-pion ratio condition, to extract the five parameters (\(T, \mu_B, \mu_Q, \mu_S, \gamma_S\)) from mid-rapidity yield data of \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), and \(p,\bar{p}\) across multiple centrality bins for collision energies from RHIC-BES (7.7 GeV) to LHC (2.76 TeV).

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature \(T_{CFO}\) saturates around 160 MeV near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39\) GeV for most central collisions, consistent with the Hagedorn limit.
- Baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) increases with centrality (higher \(N_{part}\)) and decreases with collision energy; at LHC energies \(\mu_B\) approaches zero.
- Strangeness under-saturation factor \(\gamma_S\) increases from peripheral to central collisions and saturates around \(N_{part}=150\); central values remain below 1 (around 0.9) even at high RHIC and LHC energies.
- For LHC (2.76 TeV) and RHIC (200 GeV), \(\gamma_S\) shows an increasing trend from lower peripheral to central collisions, with saturation near \(N_{part}=150\).
- At lower RHIC-BES energies (e.g., 7.7 GeV), the difference between peripheral and central \(\gamma_S\) values is larger than at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the conserved-charge-dependent parameter extraction method introduced by Bhattacharyya (2019) to study centrality dependence, building on prior statistical hadronization model analyses by Cleymans (1998, 2004), Andronic (2005, 2008), and Manninen (2008). It contradicts the assumption of full chemical equilibrium in peripheral collisions and supports the idea that strangeness equilibration requires a minimum system size, enabling future studies of the QCD phase diagram and the search for the critical point in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
