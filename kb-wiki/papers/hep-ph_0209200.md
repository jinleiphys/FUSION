# Modified Paschos-Wolfenstein relation and extraction of weak mixing angle sin^2 theta_W

> The NuTeV collaboration reported anomalously large weak mixing angle sin^2 theta_W in comparison with the standard model prediction. Neutrino and antineutrino charged- and neutral-current events are analyzed for extracting sin^2 theta_W. Although the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation is not directly used in the analysis, it plays an important role in the determination. Noting that the target nucleus, iron, is not an isoscalar nucleus, we derive a leading-order expression for a modified Paschos-Wolfenstein relation for nuclei, which may have neutron excess. Then, using charge and baryon-number conservations for nuclei, we discuss a nuclear correction in the sin^2 theta_W determination. It is noteworthy that nuclear modifications are different between valence up- and down-quark distributions. We show this difference effect on the NuTeV sin^2 theta_W deviation.

## Key claim

The NuTeV collaboration’s anomalously large weak mixing angle sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst) may be partially explained by a nuclear correction arising from the difference between valence up- and down-quark modifications in the iron target, but this correction is not large enough to account for the entire deviation under the two specific descriptions considered.

## Method

The author derives a leading-order modified Paschos-Wolfenstein (PW) relation for a general nuclear target with neutron excess, using charge and baryon-number conservation to constrain the valence-quark distributions. Two prescriptions for the correction factor ε_v (which quantifies the difference between valence up- and down-quark modifications) are then evaluated numerically: one from an approximate solution to the conservation equations, and one from a global χ² analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs). The correction term is averaged over the NuTeV kinematic range (0.01 < x < 0.75, 1 < Q² < 140 GeV²) and also weighted by NuTeV functional forms to estimate its effect on the extracted sin²θ_W.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV weak mixing angle: sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), compared to the standard model value 0.2227 ± 0.0004 from a global analysis excluding neutrino-nucleus data.
- Neutron excess for iron: ε̂_n = 4/56.
- Average NuTeV kinematics: ⟨E⟩ = 116 GeV, ⟨Q²⟩ = 25.6 GeV² (neutrinos) and 15.4 GeV² (antineutrinos), ⟨x⟩ = 0.22 (neutrinos) and 0.18 (antineutrinos).
- At Q² = 20 GeV², the ε_v correction term from the first prescription is comparable in magnitude to the NuTeV deviation (0.0050) at some x values, but after averaging over the NuTeV kinematics the effect becomes significantly smaller.

## Context

This work extends the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation (Paschos and Wolfenstein, 1973) to non-isoscalar nuclear targets, addressing a possible nuclear correction to the NuTeV result (Zeller, McFarland et al., 2002). It contrasts with earlier suggestions by Miller and Thomas (2002) that shadowing differences could explain the anomaly, and with the NuTeV collaboration’s own nuclear corrections (Zeller, 2002). The paper enables future investigations of valence-quark modifications in nuclei, which could be tested at a neutrino factory or the NuMI project, and it notes that the missing ε_v effect cannot be accounted for by electron/muon scattering data alone.
