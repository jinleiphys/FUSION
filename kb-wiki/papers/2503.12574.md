# Gluon gravitational form factors of the proton in a light-front spectator model

> We calculate the gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the proton using a light-front spectator model based on soft-wall AdS/QCD, where the active parton is a gluon. The model parameters are determined by fitting the unpolarized gluon distribution function to the NNPDF3.0nlo dataset. Subsequently, we predict the polarized gluon distribution, finding consistency with global analyses. Our predictions for the gluon GFFs show good agreement with recent lattice QCD simulations and experimental extractions. Using these gluon GFFs, we compute the contribution of the gluon to the proton mass and mechanical radii. We also analyze the gluonic contribution to the two-dimensional Galilean densities, including energy density, radial and tangential pressure, isotropic pressure, and pressure anisotropy of the proton.

## Key claim

The authors calculate gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the proton using a light-front spectator model with soft-wall AdS/QCD wave functions, finding that using a gluon-specific scale parameter \(\kappa = 2.62\) GeV (rather than the quark-determined value of 0.4 GeV) yields agreement with lattice QCD and experimental extractions, while the model's prediction for the gluon \(D\)-term at zero momentum transfer, \(D_g(0) = -8.61^{+0.71}_{-0.75}\), is comparable to some lattice results but strongly differs from others.

## Method

The authors model the proton as a two-particle Fock state of an active gluon and a spin-1/2 spectator representing the three valence quarks, using light-front wave functions derived from soft-wall AdS/QCD. Model parameters (including the scale \(\kappa = 2.62\) GeV, and parameters \(a = 3.880 \pm 0.223\), \(b = -0.530 \pm 0.007\)) are determined by fitting the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function to the NNPDF3.0nlo dataset at \(\mu_0 = 2\) GeV. The gluon GFFs \(A_g(Q^2)\), \(B_g(Q^2)\), \(D_g(Q^2)\), and \(\overline{C}_g(Q^2)\) are then extracted from the matrix elements of the gluonic energy-momentum tensor using the light-front wave function overlap representation.

## Key numbers

*   Gluon average longitudinal momentum fraction: \(\langle x \rangle_g = 0.404^{+0.037}_{-0.039}\), consistent with the global fit value \(0.414 \pm 0.008\) from Hou (2019).
*   Gluon spin contribution to the proton spin: \(\Delta G = 0.42^{+0.04}_{-0.04}\) for \(x_g \in [0.001, 1]\) at \(\mu_0 = 2\) GeV.
*   Gluon \(D\)-term at zero momentum transfer: \(D_g(0) = -8.61^{+0.71}_{-0.75}\), compared to lattice QCD values of \(-10(3)\) from Shanahan (2018) and \(-2.57(84)\) from Hackett (2023).
*   Gluon total angular momentum at zero momentum transfer: \(J_g = 0.206 \pm 0.013\), compared to lattice QCD values \(0.231(11)(22)\) from Wang (2021) and \(0.255(13)\) from Hackett (2023).
*   Forward limit of the \(\overline{C}_g\) form factor: \(\overline{C}_g(0) = -0.030 \pm 0.010\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front spectator model approach, previously applied to quark GFFs, to the gluonic sector of the proton, addressing the poorly constrained gluon contributions to the energy-momentum tensor. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations from Hackett (2023) and Pefkou (2021), experimental extractions from Duran (2022) and Guo (2021) using near-threshold \(J/\psi\) production, and a perturbative calculation for a quark dressed with a gluon by More (2023). The model enables predictions for two-dimensional light-front gluon densities (energy, pressure, shear) and mechanical radii, which can be tested by future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider.
