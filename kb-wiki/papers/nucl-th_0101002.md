# Off-Mass-Shell $π$N Scattering and $pp \to pp π^0$

> We adapt the off-shell $π$N amplitude of the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force to the half-off-shell amplitude of the pion rescattering contribution to $pp \to pp π^0$ near threshold. This {\em pion} rescattering contribution, together with the impulse term, provides a good description of the data when the half-off-shell amplitude is linked to the phenomenological invariant amplitudes obtained from meson factory $π$N scattering data.

# Off-Mass-Shell πN Scattering and pp → pp π⁰

## Key claim
The off-mass-shell πN amplitude from the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force, when adapted to the half-off-shell amplitude for pion rescattering in pp → pp π⁰ near threshold, provides a good description of the data when linked to phenomenological invariant amplitudes from meson factory πN scattering data. However, the calculation requires retaining the c₁ν² term (dropped in the three-body force context) and placing the produced pion on its mass shell, which are the only changes from the TM three-nucleon force amplitude structure.

## Method
The authors calculate in momentum space the nonrelativistic impulse term plus half-off-shell pion rescattering term for pp → pp π⁰ near threshold. They adapt the Tucson-Melbourne off-shell πN amplitude, originally developed for two-pion-exchange three-body forces, to the half-off-shell kinematics of pion rescattering by retaining the c₁ν² term (which is of order μ⁴/m² and dropped in the three-body force) and placing the produced pion on its mass shell. The T-matrix incorporates the TM Z-graph contribution and the non-spin flip even current algebra πN amplitude, with parameters fixed from a recent interior dispersion analysis of the SP98 phase shift solution to meson factory data.

## Key numbers
- The pion-nucleon sigma term divided by the pion decay constant squared: σ/f_π² ≈ 1.35 μ⁻¹
- The on-shell invariant amplitude: F⁺(0, μ², μ², μ²) ≈ -0.08 μ⁻¹
- The c₁ parameter: c₁ ≈ +1.23 μ⁻³
- The pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 93 MeV
- The monopole π⁰NN vertex function reproduces the 2% Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy

## Context
This work extends the earlier qualitative estimate of Hachenberg and Pirner (1978) and subsequent calculations by Efrosinin et al. (1985, 1990) and Hernandez and Oset (1995), which found enhancement of the cross section via s-wave pion rescattering. It contrasts with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Park et al. (1996) and Cohen et al. (1996), which decreased the theoretical cross section below the data. The approach enables a calibration of the πN scattering amplitude F⁺ underlying two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces using precise pp → pp π⁰ measurements from IUCF (Meyer et al., 1992) and Celsius (Bondar et al., 1995), complementary to standard on-mass-shell πN data constraints and chiral symmetry implementation.
