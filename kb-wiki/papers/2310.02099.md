# Re-evaluation of the $^{22}$Ne($p$,$γ$)$^{23}$Na reaction rate: $R-$matrix analysis of the non-resonant capture and effect of the 8945 keV (${7/2}^{-}$) resonance strength

> The $^{22}$Ne($p,γ$)$^{23}$Na capture reaction is a key member of the Ne-Na cycle of hydrogen burning. The rate of this reaction is critical in classical novae nucleosynthesis and hot bottom burning processes (HBB) in asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stars. Despite its astrophysical importance, significant uncertainty remains in the reaction rate due to several narrow low energy resonances lying near the Gamow window. The present work revisits this reaction by examining the contribution of the 8664 keV subthreshold state and the 151 keV doublet resonance state of 7/2$^-$ configuration in $^{23}$Na. Finite range distorted-wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) analyses of existing $^{22}$Ne($^3$He,$d$)$^{23}$Na transfer reaction data were carried out to extract the peripheral asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) of the 8664 keV state. The ANC value obtained in the present work is $\sim 25\%$ higher compared to the previous work by Santra et al.~\cite{SA20}. Systematic $R$-matrix calculations were performed to obtain the non-resonant astrophysical $S$-factor utilizing the enhanced ANC value. The resonance strengths of the 8945 keV doublets were deduced from shell model calculations. The total reaction rate is found to be $\sim 15\%$ higher at temperatures relevant for the HBB processes, compared to the recent rate measured by Williams et al.~\cite{WI20}, and matches the rate by Williams et al.~\cite{WI20} at temperatures of interest for classical novae nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The total reaction rate for the 22Ne(p,γ)23Na reaction is found to be approximately 15% higher at temperatures relevant for hot bottom burning processes in AGB stars compared to the recent rate measured by Williams et al. (2020), but matches that rate at temperatures of interest for classical novae nucleosynthesis; this result depends on a 25% higher asymptotic normalization coefficient for the 8664 keV subthreshold state compared to the prior work by Santra et al. (2020).

## Method

Finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) analyses were performed on existing 22Ne(3He,d)23Na transfer reaction data at 15 MeV to extract peripheral asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the 8664 keV subthreshold state and for the 7080, 7449, and 7890 keV bound excited states. Systematic R-matrix calculations using the code AZURE2 were then carried out to obtain the non-resonant astrophysical S-factor, constrained by the enhanced ANC values. Shell model calculations using the NUSHELLX code with the sdpfmu interaction in a truncated sdpf model space were performed to deduce the proton spectroscopic factor and resonance strength for the 7/2- state of the 8945 keV doublet.

## Key numbers

- ANC for the 8664 keV state: 179.5 ± 19.7 fm-1/2 from the 15 MeV data, approximately 25% higher than the value of 143.7 ± 15.2 fm-1/2 from Santra et al. (2020)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 8664 keV state: 0.50 ± 0.05 (present work), compared to 0.32 ± 0.05 from Santra et al. (2020)
- Shell model spectroscopic factor for the 7/2- state at 8944 keV: 0.0104, substantially higher than the upper limit of 1.08×10-3 from Hale et al. (2001) and the value of (3.94±0.9)×10-4 from Santra et al. (2020)
- Shell model resonance strength for the 7/2- state: 9.97×10-8 eV, compared to the Wigner limit upper bound of 1.89×10-10 eV and the experimental upper limit of ≤9.7×10-8 eV from Kelly et al. (2017)
- Shell model spectroscopic factor for the 3/2+ state at 8945 keV: 1×10-4, consistent with the experimental value of (5.54±1.41)×10-4 from Santra et al. (2020)
- Total reaction rate is approximately 15% higher at T9 = 0.08-0.1 compared to the rate of Williams et al. (2020)
- Non-resonant rate from the present work is approximately 30% higher at T9 = 0.05-0.1 compared to earlier rates of Depalo et al. (2016) and Kelly et al. (2017)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Santra et al. (2020) by extracting peripheral ANCs that satisfy necessary peripherality conditions, which the prior ANC value of 144 fm-1/2 did not, and by including contributions from the previously neglected bound excited states at 7080, 7449, and 7890 keV. The results contradict the earlier spectroscopic factor for the 7/2- state from Hale et al. (2001) and Santra et al. (2020), finding a substantially larger value from shell model calculations. The work enables improved reaction rate inputs for stellar models of hot bottom burning in AGB stars and classical novae nucleosynthesis, and it omits the resonances at Ex = 8862, 8894, and 9000 keV that were recently ruled out by inelastic-scattering measurements at the Q3D magnetic spectrometer in Munich (Adachi 2023, Cavanna 2023).
