# Pi N sigma-term and chiral-odd twist-3 distribution function e(x) of the nucleon in the chiral quark soliton model

> The isosinglet combination of the chiral-odd twist-3 distribution function $e^u(x)+e^d(x)$ of the nucleon has outstanding properties that its first moment is proportional to the well-known $πN$ sigma-term and that it contains a $δ$-function singularity at $x=0$. These two features are inseparably connected in that the above sum rule would be violated, if there is no such a singularity in $e^u(x)+e^d(x)$. Very recently, we found that the physical origin of this $δ$-function singularity can be traced back to the long-range quark-quark correlation of scalar type, which signals the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking of the QCD vacuum. The main purpose of the present paper is to give complete theoretical predictions for the chiral-odd twist-3 distribution function $e^a(x)$ of each flavor $a$ on the basis of the chiral quark soliton model, without recourse to the derivative expansion type approximation. These theoretical predictions are then compared with the empirical information extracted from the CLAS data of the semi-inclusive DIS processes by assuming the Collins mechanism only. A good agreement with the CLAS data is indicative of a sizable violation of the $πN$ sigma-term sum rule, or equivalently, the existence of a $δ$-function singularity in $e^u(x) + e^d(x)$.

## Key claim

The isosinglet combination of the chiral-odd twist-3 distribution function \(e^u(x)+e^d(x)\) contains a \(\delta\)-function singularity at \(x=0\) whose physical origin is the long-range quark-quark correlation of scalar type signaling spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking; the authors provide complete theoretical predictions for each flavor \(e^a(x)\) using the chiral quark soliton model without derivative expansion approximations, and find good agreement with CLAS data only if the \(\pi N\) sigma-term sum rule is violated, equivalently if the \(\delta\)-function singularity exists.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral-odd twist-3 distribution function \(e^a(x)\) for each flavor within the chiral quark soliton model (CQSM) by evaluating nucleon matrix elements of bilocal quark operators in the nucleon rest frame, using a stationary hedgehog pion field configuration and a \(1/N_c\) expansion in the collective rotational velocity \(\Omega\). The isosinglet distribution \(e^{(T=0)}(x)\) is obtained at leading order in \(\Omega\) using the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme with double subtraction, while the isovector distribution \(e^{(T=1)}(x)\) is obtained at next-to-leading order in \(\Omega\) with careful cancellation of singular terms. The model predictions at the scale \(Q^2 \simeq 0.30\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) are evolved to higher \(Q^2\) for comparison with empirical information extracted from CLAS semi-inclusive DIS data assuming the Collins mechanism only.

## Key numbers

- The first moment of \(e^{(T=0)}(x)\) is proportional to the \(\pi N\) sigma-term: \(\int_{-1}^1 e^{(T=0)}(x) dx = \sigma_{\pi N} / m_0\).
- The second Mellin moment of \(e^{(T=0)}(x)\) is given by \(\int_{-1}^1 x e^{(T=0)}(x) dx = (m_0 / M_N) \cdot N_c\) with \(N_c = 3\).
- The empirical estimate for the first moment of \(e^{(T=1)}(x)\) is \(\int_{-1}^1 e^{(T=1)}(x) dx \simeq 0.41 \pm 0.06\), obtained from \((M_n-M_p)_{QCD} \simeq (2.05 \pm 0.30)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_d - m_u \simeq 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The model energy scale is around \(600\ \mathrm{MeV}\) or \(Q^2 \simeq 0.30\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(e(x)\) by Efremov et al. (2001, 2002) using the CQSM within the valence-quark-only approximation, and by Schweitzer (2003) and the present authors (2003) using gradient expansion approximations that confirmed the \(\delta\)-function singularity. The authors provide exact numerical calculations without derivative expansion, including both isoscalar and isovector combinations, which were left untouched in Schweitzer (2003). The predictions enable comparison with CLAS data on azimuthal asymmetry \(A_{LU}\) in electroproduction of pions, and test the \(\pi N\) sigma-term sum rule and the existence of the \(\delta\)-function singularity at \(x=0\).
