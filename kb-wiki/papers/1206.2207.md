# Octet baryon electromagnetic form factors in nuclear medium

> We study the octet baryon electromagnetic form factors in nuclear matter using the covariant spectator quark model extended to the nuclear matter regime. The parameters of the model in vacuum are fixed by the study of the octet baryon electromagnetic form factors. In nuclear matter the changes in hadron properties are calculated by including the relevant hadron masses and the modification of the pion-baryon coupling constants calculated in the quark-meson coupling model. In nuclear matter the magnetic form factors of the octet baryons are enhanced in the low $Q^2$ region, while the electric form factors show a more rapid variation with $Q^2$. The results are compared with the modification of the bound proton electromagnetic form factors observed at Jefferson Lab. In addition, the corresponding changes for the bound neutron are predicted.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, extended to nuclear matter using in-medium hadron masses and modified pion-baryon coupling constants from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, predicts that the magnetic form factors of all octet baryons are enhanced at low momentum transfer \(Q^2\) in nuclear matter, while the electric form factors exhibit a more rapid variation with \(Q^2\); these medium modifications are calculated without including final-state interactions or meson-exchange currents, so the results represent only the valence-quark-structure contribution to the observed effects.

## Method

The authors extended the covariant spectator quark model, previously calibrated to octet baryon electromagnetic form factors in vacuum and on the lattice, to symmetric nuclear matter by replacing vacuum baryon and meson masses with their in-medium values from the QMC model and by scaling the pion-baryon coupling constants according to the QMC predictions. The valence-quark (bare) contributions were computed using S-wave quark-diquark wavefunctions with vector-meson-dominance parametrizations of the quark current, while the pion-cloud dressing was included via one-pion-loop diagrams with the pion-mass dependence explicitly built in to reproduce leading-order chiral behavior. The model parameters were constrained using experimental proton, neutron, and \(\Sigma^-\) electric and magnetic square radii in addition to the octet magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The neutron electric charge square radius in vacuum was improved from \(-0.029\ \text{fm}^2\) (previous model) to a value consistent with the experimental \(-0.116\ \text{fm}^2\) after including new constraints and explicit pion-mass dependence.
- The pion-cloud coupling coefficients for the octet baryons are \(\beta_N = 1\), \(\beta_\Lambda = 0.48\), \(\beta_\Sigma = 0.64\), and \(\beta_\Xi = 0.04\) (using \(\alpha = 0.6\) from \(SU(6)\)).
- The quark current parameters include \(\lambda_q = 1.21\), \(c_+ = 4.160\), \(c_- = 1.160\), \(d_+ = d_- = -0.686\), \(c_0 = 4.427\), and \(d_0 = -1.860\).
- The effective heavy-meson mass used in the quark-current parametrization is \(M_h = 2 M_N\).
- The in-medium magnetic form factors \(G_{MB}^\ast\) are enhanced at low \(Q^2\) because the effective nucleon mass satisfies \(M_N^\ast < M_N\), leading to \(|\mu_N^\ast| > |\mu_N|\).

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to octet baryons in vacuum (Ramalho, 2012) and on the lattice (Ramalho, 2012), to the nuclear-matter regime using the QMC model (Guichon, 1988; Saito, 2007) for in-medium masses and coupling constants. The results are compared with Jefferson Lab measurements of bound-proton form-factor modification from polarized (\( \vec{e}, e' \vec{p} \)) scattering on \(^4\)He (Strauch, 2003; Paolone, 2010; Malace, 2011) and with predictions for the bound neutron (Cloet, 2009). The approach enables predictions for the medium modification of all octet baryon electromagnetic form factors, including those of the \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Xi\) hyperons, which can be tested in future experiments on hypernuclei.
