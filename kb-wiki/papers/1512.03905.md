# Compositeness of Near Threshold Quasi-Bound States

> We study the compositeness of unstable hadrons which lie near the two-hadron threshold. In the framework of the effective field theory, we derive the relation between the compositeness of stable bound states with observables. We then extend this relation for the quasi-bound states with finite decay width. A prescription to interpret the complex value of the compositeness is presented. With this method, we show that Lambda(1405) is dominated by the Kbar N composite component and a_0(980) have only small Kbar K fraction.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized weak-binding relation that allows model-independent determination of the compositeness (probability of being a two-hadron composite state) for unstable quasi-bound states with finite decay width, provided the eigenenergy is sufficiently small relative to the interaction range and the decay channel is sufficiently separated in energy. The abstract's claim that Lambda(1405) is "dominated by the Kbar N composite component" and a_0(980) has "only small Kbar K fraction" is hedged by the condition that these conclusions hold only when the uncertainty measure U is small, which is satisfied for the analyzed data sets.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interactions for a two-body system, first for a single channel with a stable bound state, then for two channels where one channel contains a quasi-bound state with complex eigenenergy. They derive the scattering amplitude and express the compositeness X in terms of the effective potential and loop function, then expand the scattering length in powers of 1/R (where R is the length scale from the binding energy) to obtain the weak-binding relation. For quasi-bound states, they introduce a prescription to convert the complex compositeness into real quantities (tilde-X, tilde-Z, and uncertainty U) that admit probabilistic interpretation when U is small.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent weak-binding relation of Weinberg (1965), originally derived for stable bound states like the deuteron, to unstable quasi-bound states with finite decay width. It enables determination of the internal structure (compositeness versus elementariness) of near-threshold hadronic resonances directly from experimental observables without relying on specific models. The results support earlier model-dependent studies of Lambda(1405) compositeness by Sekihara (2014) based on chiral unitary models, and are consistent with lattice calculations by Hall (2014) and potential model analyses by Miyahara (2015). For f_0(980), the scattered values of tilde-X_KbarK across different experimental analyses indicate that more accurate input data are needed for a conclusive interpretation.
