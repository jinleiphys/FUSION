# Dispersive spherical optical model of neutron scattering from Al27 up to 250 MeV

> A spherical optical model potential (OMP) containing a dispersive term is used to fit the available experimental database of angular distribution and total cross section data for n + Al27 covering the energy range 0.1- 250 MeV using relativistic kinematics and a relativistic extension of the Schroedinger equation. A dispersive OMP with parameters that show a smooth energy dependence and energy independent geometry are determined from fits to the entire data set. A very good overall agreement between experimental data and predictions is achieved up to 150 MeV. Inclusion of nonlocality effects in the absorptive volume potential allows to achieve an excellent agreement up to 250 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors construct a dispersive optical model potential (OMP) for neutron scattering from Al27 that achieves excellent agreement with experimental total and differential cross section data up to 250 MeV, but only when relativistic kinematics and nonlocality corrections to the volume absorptive potential are included; without these corrections, the model systematically underestimates total cross sections above 130 MeV.

## Method

A spherical optical model potential containing a dispersive term was fitted to the available experimental database of angular distributions and total cross sections for n+Al27 from 0.1 to 250 MeV, using relativistic kinematics and a relativistic extension of the Schroedinger equation. The imaginary volume and surface potentials were parametrized with symmetric forms about the Fermi energy, then modified by asymmetric nonlocality corrections for the volume term at high energies. A global chi-squared optimization combined with grid searches was performed to determine the Hartree-Fock potential parameters, the absorptive potential strengths, and energy-independent geometry parameters.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section data from Finlay et al. (1991, 1993) covering 5.3 to 250 MeV were used, with experimental uncertainty of about plus or minus 1 percent.
- The Fermi energy for Al27 was derived from mass differences as -10.392 MeV; the average energy of single-particle states Ep was -5.66 MeV.
- The nonrelativistic DOM systematically underestimated total cross sections above 130 MeV; inclusion of relativistic and nonlocality corrections resolved this discrepancy up to 250 MeV.
- The dispersive correction from the asymmetric (nonlocal) volume absorption starts to increase significantly for energies above 50 MeV.
- The Madland global OMP overestimates experimental total cross sections by almost 20 percent above 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends dispersive optical model analyses, previously applied mainly to closed-shell nuclei like Ca40, Zr90, and Pb208, to a light nucleus (A less than 30) for the first time in a comprehensive study. It builds on prior phenomenological OMPs for Al27 by Petler et al. (1985), Lee et al. (1999), and Koning and Delaroche (2001), and on the total cross section measurements of Finlay et al. (1991, 1993). The resulting potential enables improved nuclear data evaluations for aluminium, which is an important structural material for accelerator-driven systems.
