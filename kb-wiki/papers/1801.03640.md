# Complete and Consistent Chiral Transport from Wigner Function Formalism

> Recently there has been significant interest in understanding the macroscopic quantum transport in a many-body system of chiral fermions. A natural framework for describing such a system which is generally out of equilibrium, is the transport equation for its phase space distribution function. In this paper, we obtain a complete solution of the covariant chiral transport for massless fermions, by starting from the general Wigner function formalism and carrying out a complete and consistent semiclassical expansion up to $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ order. In particular, we clarify certain subtle and confusing issues surrounding the Lorentz non-invariance and frame dependence associated with the 3D chiral kinetic theory. We prove that such frame dependence is uniquely and completely fixed by an unambiguous definition of the $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ correction to the distribution function in each reference frame.

## Key claim

The paper obtains a complete and consistent covariant chiral transport theory for massless fermions up to $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ order using the Wigner function formalism, and proves that the apparent Lorentz non-invariance and frame dependence of the 3D chiral kinetic theory is uniquely and completely fixed by an unambiguous definition of the $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ correction to the distribution function in each reference frame.

## Method

The authors start from the general Wigner function formalism for a collisionless system of massless fermions in external electromagnetic fields, decompose the Wigner function into its 16 Clifford algebra components, and focus on the coupled equations for the vector and axial-vector components. They perform a systematic semiclassical expansion in powers of $\hbar$ for both the operators and the chiral currents, solving the constraint equations at zeroth and first order to obtain the general form of the chiral current $\mathscr{J}^{\mu}_\chi$ up to $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$. The frame dependence is analyzed by examining how the decomposition of the first-order correction into a part parallel to the momentum and an orthogonal part transforms under Lorentz boosts.

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical expansion of the operators gives $\pi^{\mu}=p^{\mu}-\frac{1}{12}Q\hbar^2\triangle F^{\mu\nu}\partial^{p}_{\nu}+\mathbf{O}(\hbar^4)$ and $\triangledown^{\mu}=\partial^{\mu}-QF^{\mu\nu}\partial^{p}_{\nu}+\frac{1}{24}Q\hbar^2\triangle^2 F^{\mu\nu}\partial^{p}_{\nu}+\mathbf{O}(\hbar^4)$, showing the truncation at $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ order.
* The zeroth-order chiral current is $\mathscr{J}_{\mu,\chi}^{(0)}=p_{\mu}f^{(0)}_{\chi}\delta(p^2)$, with the classical on-shell condition enforced by the delta-function.
* The first-order chiral current is $\mathscr{J}^{\mu}_\chi=p^{\mu}f_{\chi}\delta(p^{2})+\hbar\chi Q\widetilde{F}^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}f^{(0)}_{\chi}\delta^{'}(p^{2})-\hbar\frac{\chi}{2p\cdot n}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\lambda\rho}n_{\nu}p_{\lambda}\left(\triangledown_{\rho}f^{(0)}_{\chi}\right)\delta(p^2)$, where $n^\mu$ is an auxiliary time-like unit vector.
* In the rest frame with $n^\mu=(1,0,0,0)$, the orthogonal part of the first-order correction becomes $\mathscr{K}_{\mu} = \left ( 0, \frac{\chi}{2|\mathbf{p}|} \mathbf{p} \times (\vec{\triangledown}f^{(0)}_{\chi}) \right )$.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function approach to chiral transport developed by Vasak (1987) and Zhuang (1995, 1998) by providing a complete and consistent solution up to $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ order. It clarifies the Lorentz invariance and frame dependence issues that were previously addressed in the 3D formulation of chiral kinetic theory by Chen (2014, 2015) and Hidaka (2016), proving that the frame dependence arises from the light-like nature of the momentum and is uniquely fixed by the definition of the $\hat{\mathbf{O}}(\hbar)$ distribution function correction. The results enable a fully covariant description of anomalous chiral transport phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect and chiral vortical effect in out-of-equilibrium systems, and provide a foundation for further phenomenological studies in heavy ion collisions and Weyl semimetals.
