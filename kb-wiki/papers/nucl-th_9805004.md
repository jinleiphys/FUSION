# On the determination of the pion effective mass in nuclei from pionic atoms

> The binding energies of the deeply bound 1s and 2p states in pionic atoms of $^{207}$Pb, recently established experimentally in the $^{208}$Pb(d,$^3$He) reaction, have been used by several groups to derive the pion effective mass in nuclear matter. We show that these binding energies are fully consistent with `normal' pionic atoms and that the real part of the pion-nucleus potential at the center of $^{207}$Pb is 28$\pm$3 MeV and not 20 MeV as suggested previously.

# On the determination of the pion effective mass in nuclei from pionic atoms

## Key claim
The binding energies of the deeply bound 1s and 2p states in pionic atoms of 207Pb are fully consistent with normal pionic atom data, and the real part of the pion-nucleus potential at the center of 207Pb is 28±3 MeV, not 20 MeV as previously suggested, provided that the low density limit is imposed on the s-wave part of the potential and that a real part of the two-nucleon absorption term (ReB0) is included.

## Method
The authors performed fits to a data base of 54 normal pionic atom states (1s to 4f, from oxygen to uranium) using the standard pion-nucleus optical potential with s-wave and p-wave terms, including a real part for the two-nucleon absorption parameter B0. They then compared the calculated binding energies for the deeply bound 1s and 2p states in 207Pb with experimental values from the 208Pb(d,3He) reaction, while systematically varying ReB0 and the s-wave parameters b0 and b1, and imposing constraints from the free pion-nucleon scattering lengths determined from pionic hydrogen.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of deeply bound states in 207Pb: B1s = 7.1±0.2 MeV, B2p = 5.31±0.09 MeV (from Yamazaki et al. 1997)
- Real part of the s-wave potential at the center of 207Pb: VS = 28±3 MeV (repulsive)
- ReB0 constrained to between -0.02 and -0.08 mπ^-4 from fits respecting the low density limit
- ImB0 = 0.056±0.003 mπ^-4 from the constrained fit
- Pion effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at ρ0 = 0.17 fm^-3: mπ(ρ0) = 167±3.5 MeV
- Pion effective mass at density ρ in 207Pb: mπ(ρ) = 170.4±3.6 MeV
- Calculated widths for deeply bound states from acceptable fits: 2p width 0.31-0.33 MeV, 1s width 0.45-0.50 MeV
- Free πN scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen (Sigg 1996): b0 = -0.0077±0.0072 mπ^-1, b1 = -0.0962±0.0071 mπ^-1

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Friedman and Soff (1985), who first predicted that deeply bound 1s and 2p states in heavy pionic atoms would be narrow enough to be observable, and of Toki and Yamazaki (1988, 1989), who proposed methods to populate such states. The paper directly contradicts the conclusions of Weise, Borchert, and Weise (1997) and Yamazaki et al. (1997), who derived a smaller potential (about 20 MeV) and a different pion effective mass by fitting only the deeply bound states without checking consistency with normal pionic atom data. The results confirm the persistence of the "missing" s-wave repulsion problem identified by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997), and demonstrate that the deeply bound states do not provide new information beyond what is already contained in normal pionic atom data. The analysis enables a more reliable determination of the pion effective mass in nuclear matter, with implications for chiral perturbation theory and the understanding of the pion-nucleus interaction at zero energy.
