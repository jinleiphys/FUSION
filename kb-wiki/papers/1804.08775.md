# New charm resonances and $J/ψ$ interactions in a hadron gas

> In relativistic heavy ion collisions, after the quark gluon plasma (QGP) phase there is a hadron gas (HG) phase. In both phases $J/ψ$ may be formed and destroyed. In this note we study the $J/ψ$ interactions with other mesons in the hadron gas phase. Making use of effective field Lagrangians we obtain the cross sections for the production and absorption processes. With respect to the existing calculations, the improvements introduced here are the inclusion of $K$ and $K^*$'s in the effective Lagrangian approach and the inclusion of processes involving the new exotic charmonium states $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4025)$. We conclude that the interactions between $J/ψ$ and all the considered mesons reduce the original $J/ψ$ abundance ( determined at the end of the quark gluon plasma phase ) by 20 \% and 24 \% in RHIC and LHC collisions respectively. Consequently, any really significant change in the $J/ψ$ abundance comes from dissociation and regeneration processes in the QGP phase.

# New charm resonances and J/psi interactions in a hadron gas

## Key claim

The interactions between J/psi and all considered mesons (pi, rho, K, K*, plus processes involving the exotic states Zc(3900) and Zc(4025)) reduce the original J/psi abundance by only 20% at RHIC and 24% at LHC, meaning that any really significant change in J/psi abundance must come from dissociation and regeneration processes in the quark gluon plasma phase rather than from the hadron gas phase.

## Method

The authors used SU(4) effective field Lagrangians with pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector, vector-vector-vector, and anomalous parity terms to compute cross sections for J/psi production and absorption processes with pi, rho, K, and K* mesons. They included s-channel exchanges of the exotic charmonium states Zc(3900) and Zc(4025) in the reactions J/psi + pi -> D + Dbar* and J/psi + rho -> D* + Dbar*, respectively, and solved a momentum-integrated rate equation with boost-invariant Bjorken flow and accelerated transverse expansion to evolve the J/psi abundance over time.

## Key numbers

- J/psi abundance reduction of 20% in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC)
- J/psi abundance reduction of 24% in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5 TeV (LHC)
- Critical temperature for QGP to hadronic matter transition: T_C = 175 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_F = 125 MeV
- Cut-off mass parameter for form factors: Lambda = 2 GeV
- Zc(3900) mass: 3881.2 MeV (with uncertainties +-4.2 +-52.7 MeV) and width 51.8 MeV (with uncertainties +-4.6 +-36 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Lee et al. and by the authors' previous study (Abreu et al., 2017) by including K and K* mesons in the effective Lagrangian approach and by incorporating the exotic charmonium states Zc(3900) and Zc(4025) as intermediate resonances, following the suggestion by Brizzi et al. that new charmonium states open additional channels for J/psi-light meson reactions. The results show that hadron gas interactions alone cannot account for large J/psi suppression, directing future work toward understanding dissociation and regeneration mechanisms within the quark gluon plasma phase itself.
