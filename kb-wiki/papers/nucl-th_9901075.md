# Parametrization of the octupole degrees of freedom

> A simple parametrization for the octupole collective variables is proposed and the symmetries of the wave functions are discussed in terms of the solutions corresponding to the vibrational limit. [PACS: 21.60Ev, 21.60.Fw, 21.10.Re]

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple parametrization of the octupole collective variables that guarantees the inertia tensor is diagonal in the intrinsic system, and discusses the symmetries of the wave functions in the vibrational limit. The authors note that this parametrization is one possible choice among several, and that the octupole case is considerably more complicated than the quadrupole or pairing collective descriptions previously studied.

## Method

The authors follow the standard Bohr collective model approach: they write the kinetic energy in terms of the octupole collective variables and their time derivatives, then define the intrinsic system by requiring that the off-diagonal components of the inertia tensor vanish. For the octupole (lambda=3) case, this yields three nonlinear conditions that reduce the seven original variables to four intrinsic parameters (X, Y, Z, and gamma). The Pauli prescription is used to quantize the quadratic Hamiltonian, and the symmetry properties of the wave functions under the 24 transformations between equivalent intrinsic systems are analyzed using the operators R1, R2, and R3.

## Key numbers

- The octupole collective variables have 7 components (alpha_{3mu}) in the laboratory system, reduced to 4 intrinsic parameters (X, Y, Z, gamma) after imposing the diagonal inertia tensor condition
- The diagonal inertia tensor components for the octupole case are given by J_kappa = B{4 rho^2 - 2*sqrt(21)*eta*cos(gamma_kappa)} where gamma_kappa = gamma - pi*kappa/3 for kappa = 1,2,3
- For the axially symmetric rigid rotor with gamma=0, the inertia tensor components are J_1 = J_2 = 3(X^2+Y^2)+30Z^2 and J_3 = 18(X^2+Y^2)
- The rotational energy for the axially symmetric case is given by T_rot = [I(I+1) + (2/3)K^2(1 - (9/4)sin^2(mu))/sin^2(mu)] / (2J_1)
- The basic scalars (invariants) under rotations are I^(2) = rho^2, I^(4) = (1/sqrt(5))eta^2, I^(6) = -sqrt(2/35)eta^3*cos(3*gamma), plus a tenth-order invariant identified by J. Blomqvist

## Context

This work extends the Bohr collective model approach (Bohr 1952, Bohr 1953) from the quadrupole case to the octupole degrees of freedom, following the same recipe used for quadrupole oscillations (Kumar and Baranger 1967) and pairing collective descriptions. The parametrization differs from earlier proposals by Rohozinski (based on O_h group symmetries) and Hamamoto (based on irreducible representations of the octahedron group A_1, F_1(k), F_2(k)), and provides a systematic framework for describing octupole deformations in nuclei such as those in the Ce-Ba and Rn-Th regions. The work enables future calculations of octupole collective spectra and the study of rotational bands with K=0,3,6,... that are more complex than the quadrupole case.
