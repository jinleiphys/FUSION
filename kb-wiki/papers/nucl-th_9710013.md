# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of the neutron drip-line in light nuclei

> The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory is applied in the mean-field approximation to the description of properties of light nuclei with large neutron excess. Pairing correlations and the coupling to particle continuum states are described by finite range two-body forces. Using standard parameter sets for the mean-field Lagrangian and the pairing interaction of Gogny-type, self-consistent solutions in coordinate space are calculated for the ground states of a number of neutron-rich nuclei. The model predicts the location of the neutron drip-line, reduction of the spin-orbit interaction, $rms$ radii, changes in surface properties, the formation of neutron skin and of neutron halo.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory predicts the location of the neutron drip-line for light nuclei from Carbon to Magnesium, but the exact drip-line position depends on the choice of effective interaction (NL1, NL3, or NL-SH) and on the proton number, with the neutron drip found at N=18 or N=20 for lighter elements (C, N, O, F) and at N=32 for Ne.

## Method

The authors solved the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently in coordinate space using finite element discretization, with the mean-field described by the NL1, NL3, and NL-SH parameter sets and pairing correlations treated by a finite-range Gogny-type interaction (D1S parameter set). The coupled system of Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov integro-differential eigenvalue equations and Klein-Gordon equations for the meson fields were solved for spherical ground states of neutron-rich C, N, O, F, Ne, Na, and Mg isotopes. The self-consistent quasi-particle eigenspectrum was transformed into the canonical basis to determine single-particle energies and occupation probabilities.

## Key numbers

* For Ne isotopes, the last bound isotope is \(^{40}\)Ne; for N \(\geq\) 32 (A \(\geq\) 42) the neutron Fermi level becomes positive and heavier isotopes are not bound.
* For Carbon, the N=20 isotope is unbound; for Oxygen, the neutron drip is at N=18 or N=20 depending on the effective force used.
* For N=20 isotones, the surface thickness decreases from 2.8 fm for \(^{28}\)O to 2.33 fm for \(^{32}\)Mg, and the diffuseness parameter decreases from 0.64 fm to 0.55 fm, as calculated with the NL3 effective force.
* The neutron \(rms\) radii for Na isotopes calculated with NL3 and D1S reproduce experimental data from Suz. (1995), with the possible exception of N=11.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RHB studies of Ne and C isotopes (Lalazissis et al., 1997; Lalazissis et al., 1997) to a systematic survey of the neutron drip-line for elements C through Mg. The results show that the neutron drip-line location is determined by the triplet of neutron states 1f\(_{7/2}\), 2p\(_{3/2}\), and 2p\(_{1/2}\), and that the spin-orbit interaction is strongly reduced near the drip-line, leading to more diffuse surfaces and the formation of neutron skin or halo. The calculations enable comparison with experimental data from radioactive beam experiments and provide predictions for the r-process nucleosynthesis.
