# Spectroscopic factors for bound s-wave states derived from neutron scattering lengths

> A simple and model-independent method is described to derive neutron single-particle spectroscopic factors of bound s-wave states in $^{A+1}Z = ^{A}Z \otimes n$ nuclei from neutron scattering lengths. Spectroscopic factors for the nuclei ^{13}C, ^{14}C, ^{16}N, ^{17}O, ^{19}O, ^{23}Ne, ^{37}Ar, and ^{41}Ar are compared to results derived from transfer experiments using the well-known DWBA analysis and to shell model calculations. The scattering length of ^{14}C is calculated from the ^{15}C_{g.s.} spectroscopic factor.

## Key claim

A model-independent method to extract neutron single-particle spectroscopic factors for bound s-wave states from free neutron scattering lengths is presented, with the hedge that the method applies only to light and intermediate nuclei that have either a single bound s-wave state or a strong s-wave state close to the neutron separation threshold.

## Method

The authors calculate the single-particle scattering length and width using a Woods-Saxon or folding potential adjusted to reproduce the binding energy of the subthreshold state. The experimental width is derived from the experimentally determined scattering length using the relation between scattering phase shift, scattering length, and resonance width at thermal energy (25 meV). The spectroscopic factor is then obtained as the ratio of the experimental width to the calculated single-particle width.

## Context

This work extends a previous model-independent method developed by the same group (Beer et al., 1996) that extracted spectroscopic factors from thermal neutron capture cross sections, which worked well only for bound p-waves. The new method provides spectroscopic factors for bound s-waves that can serve as benchmarks for results from DWBA analysis of transfer reactions (which have uncertainties up to 20% and often show systematic deviations) and from shell model calculations using interactions such as WBN, ZBMI, and USD. The derived spectroscopic factors are important for calculating neutron capture cross sections at astrophysically relevant energies (several keV) where p-wave to s-wave and d-wave transitions become significant.
