# Two-pion interferometry for partially coherent sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a multi-phase transport model

> We perform two-pion Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry for the partially coherent pion-emitting sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. A longitudinal coherent emission length, as well as a transverse coherent emission length, are introduced to the pion generation coordinates in calculating the HBT correlation functions of the partially coherent sources. We compare the model results with and without coherent emission conditions with experimental data in Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV, and in Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$2.76 TeV, and find that the HBT results of the partially coherent sources are closer to the experimental data than those of chaotic sources.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing momentum-dependent longitudinal and transverse coherent emission lengths (based on pion de Broglie wavelength) into the AMPT model produces two-pion HBT correlation functions that are closer to experimental data than those from completely chaotic sources, but only for specific coherent-length parameters (a_Z=0.5, a_T=1.8 for Au-Au; a_Z=0.8, a_T=2.5 for Pb-Pb) that are determined by fitting to the same experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model to generate pion freeze-out and generation coordinates in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They introduced momentum-dependent longitudinal and transverse coherent emission lengths L_CZ and L_CT, proportional to the pion de Broglie wavelength via parameters a_Z and a_T, and assumed that two pions with longitudinal and transverse generation coordinate differences smaller than these lengths are emitted coherently (no HBT correlation). The two-pion correlation functions were calculated and fitted with a Gaussian form in Bertsch-Pratt variables to extract HBT radii (R_out, R_side, R_long) and chaoticity parameter lambda, then compared with experimental data from STAR (2005) and ALICE (2016a).

## Key numbers

- In Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the coherent fraction CF for all pions ranges from 0.350 (K_T<200 MeV/c) to 0.384 (600<=K_T<1000 MeV/c), with the largest CF=0.494 for quark-coalescence pions at 400<=K_T<500 MeV/c.
- In Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the coherent fraction CF for all pions ranges from 0.316 (K_T<250 MeV/c) to 0.277 (850<=K_T<1200 MeV/c), with the largest CF=0.382 for quark-coalescence pions at 250<=K_T<350 MeV/c.
- The coherent-length parameters used were a_Z=0.5 and a_T=1.8 for Au-Au collisions, and a_Z=0.8 and a_T=2.5 for Pb-Pb collisions.
- The coherent fraction results for Pb-Pb collisions are consistent with experimental values of 0.23+/-0.08 from three-pion HBT correlations (ALICE, 2014) and 0.32+/-0.03(stat)+/-0.09(syst) from four-pion HBT correlations (ALICE, 2016).
- The total number of events was 3x10^4 for Au-Au and 6x10^3 for Pb-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Wang and Zhang (2022) that introduced a constant longitudinal coherent emission length in the AMPT model, by adding a momentum-dependent transverse coherent emission length and using generation coordinates rather than freeze-out coordinates. The results contradict the assumption of completely chaotic sources and support the interpretation that pion-emitting sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are partially coherent, as suggested by experimental observations of suppressed two-pion correlation functions at zero relative momentum from STAR (2005) and ALICE (2016a). The approach enables future studies of the relationship between coherent-length parameters and the thermodynamical properties of the particle production environment.
