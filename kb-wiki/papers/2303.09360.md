# Nuclear neutron radius and weak mixing angle measurements from latest COHERENT CsI and atomic parity violation Cs data

> The COHERENT collaboration observed coherent elastic neutrino nucleus scattering using a 14.6 kg cesium-iodide (CsI) detector in 2017 and recently published the updated results before decommissioning the detector. Here, we present the legacy determination of the weak mixing angle and of the average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\mathrm{Cs}$ and $^{127}\mathrm{I}$ obtained with the full CsI dataset, also exploiting the combination with the atomic parity violation (APV) experimental result, that allows us to achieve a precision as low as $\sim$4.5% and to disentangle the contributions of the $^{133}\mathrm{Cs}$ and $^{127}\mathrm{I}$ nuclei. Interestingly, we show that the COHERENT CsI data show a 6$σ$ evidence of the nuclear structure suppression of the full coherence. Moreover, we derive a data-driven APV+COHERENT measurement of the low-energy weak mixing angle with a percent uncertainty, independent of the value of the average neutron rms radius of $^{133}\mathrm{Cs}$ and $^{127}\mathrm{I}$, that is allowed to vary freely in the fit. Additionally, we extensively discuss the impact of using two different determinations of the theoretical parity non-conserving amplitude in the APV fit. Our findings show that the particular choice can make a significant difference, up to 6.5% on $R_n$(Cs) and 11% on the weak mixing angle. Finally, in light of the recent announcement of a future deployment of a 10 kg and a $\sim$700 kg cryogenic CsI detectors, we provide future prospects for these measurements, comparing them with other competitive experiments that are foreseen in the near future.

# Nuclear neutron radius and weak mixing angle measurements from latest COHERENT CsI and atomic parity violation Cs data

## Key claim

The combination of COHERENT CsI coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data with atomic parity violation (APV) data on cesium can determine the weak mixing angle with percent-level precision and the average neutron rms radius of CsI to approximately 4.5% precision, but the choice of theoretical parity non-conserving amplitude used in the APV fit can shift the extracted neutron radius by up to 6.5% and the weak mixing angle by up to 11%.

## Method

The authors performed a combined statistical analysis of the full COHERENT CsI dataset (14.6 kg detector, 2021 data release with refined quenching factor) using a Poissonian chi-squared function that includes neutrino time arrival information, and the APV experimental result on cesium using a Gaussian chi-squared function. They extracted the weak mixing angle and neutron rms radii for Cs-133 and I-127 by fitting the COHERENT data alone and in combination with APV, using two different theoretical determinations of the parity non-conserving amplitude: one from Dzuba (2012) and one from Sahoo (2021). The neutron form factors were parameterized using a symmetrized Fermi distribution with fixed diffuseness, while proton radii were taken from muonic atom spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

- Weak mixing angle from COHERENT CsI alone: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.231 +0.027 -0.024 (1 sigma)
- Weak mixing angle from APV using Dzuba (2012) amplitude: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.2375 +/- 0.0019 (1 sigma)
- Weak mixing angle from APV using Sahoo (2021) amplitude: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.2399 +/- 0.0016 (1 sigma)
- Average CsI neutron rms radius from COHERENT CsI alone: R_n(CsI) = 5.47 +/- 0.38 fm (1 sigma)
- Average CsI neutron rms radius from APV using Dzuba (2012) amplitude: R_n(CsI) = 5.29 +0.33 -0.34 fm (1 sigma)
- Average CsI neutron rms radius from APV using Sahoo (2021) amplitude: R_n(CsI) = 4.86 +0.28 -0.29 fm (1 sigma)
- Combined APV (Dzuba 2012) + COHERENT CsI: R_n(CsI) = 5.35 +0.25 -0.26 fm (1 sigma), precision approximately 4.5%
- Combined APV (Sahoo 2021) + COHERENT CsI: R_n(CsI) = 5.04 +0.23 -0.24 fm (1 sigma)
- COHERENT CsI data show a 6 sigma evidence of nuclear structure suppression of full coherence
- The two different PNC amplitude choices produce differences up to 6.5% on R_n(Cs) and 11% on the weak mixing angle

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Cadeddu et al. (2021) and the COHERENT collaboration (2021) by using the full CsI dataset with improved quenching factor and a Poissonian fitting procedure that exploits neutrino time arrival information. The combined APV+COHERENT approach was first demonstrated by Cadeddu et al. (2018, 2021) to simultaneously constrain weak mixing angle and nuclear parameters. The results are compared with the PREX-II measurement of the neutron skin of Pb-208 (2021) and the CREX measurement of Ca-48 (2022), which used parity-violating electron scattering. The analysis enables future comparisons with upcoming experiments including a planned 10 kg and approximately 700 kg cryogenic CsI detectors at the Spallation Neutron Source, as well as other competitive CEvNS experiments.
