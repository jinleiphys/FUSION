# The sixth order cumulant of net-proton number in Binomial distribution at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 200 GeV

> It is proposed that ratios of the sixth order to the second order cumulant ($C_6/C_2$) of conserved quantities are sensitive to the chiral crossover transition. Recently, the negative $C_6/C_2$ was obtained both in theoretical Lattice QCD and experiments at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 200 GeV. In this study, we investigate the behavior of net-proton $C_6/C_2$ in statistical Binomial distribution (BD) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $ 200 GeV in Au + Au collisions. With the BD parameters extracted from RHIC/STAR, it is found that $C_6/C_2$ can be negative. Furthermore, the obtained $C_6/C_2$ becomes smaller when applying the same magnitude of experimental statistics and calculation method to simulations. In 0-10\% centrality, there is a significant difference between the simulated result and theoretical expectation. Based on the extracted parameters and experimentally collected statistics, the baseline of net-proton $C_6/C_2$ in BD is presented.

# The sixth order cumulant of net-proton number in Binomial distribution at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} =\) 200 GeV

## Key claim

The negative \(C_6/C_2\) signal observed in both Lattice QCD and RHIC/STAR experiments at 200 GeV can be reproduced by a pure statistical Binomial distribution (BD) with no phase transition physics, provided the BD parameters are extracted from experimental data. Therefore, a negative \(C_6/C_2\) alone is not sufficient evidence for a chiral crossover transition; the signal may arise from statistical baselines and limited event statistics.

## Method

The authors model net-proton number as the difference of two independent Binomial distributions for protons and anti-protons, with parameters \(\mu_p\), \(\mu_{\bar{p}}\), and \(\varepsilon = \sigma^2/\mu\) extracted from RHIC/STAR data at mid-rapidity (\(|y|<0.5\)) and \(0.4 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\). They perform toy Monte Carlo simulations using the UrQMD model to obtain centrality-dependent input parameters, then generate event-by-event proton and anti-proton numbers from BD in each Refmult3 bin. The centrality bin width correction (CBWC) method is applied to compute \(C_6/C_2\) as a function of centrality, and the statistical dependence is studied by varying the number of minimum bias (MB) events from 100M to 1000M.

## Key numbers

- With \(\varepsilon\) between 0.94 and 0.99 (the experimentally measured range), the theoretical BD expectation for \(C_6/C_2\) ranges from \(-0.31\) to \(+0.71\).
- For \(\varepsilon = 0.97\), the theoretical BD expectation of \(C_6/C_2\) is 0.233.
- In 0-10% centrality with 300M MB events and \(\varepsilon = 0.99\), the simulated \(\left<C_6/C_2\right>\) is negative, while the theoretical expectation is 0.71.
- With 1000M MB events and \(\varepsilon = 0.97\) in 0-10% centrality, the simulated \(\left<C_6/C_2\right>\) is consistent with zero, while the theoretical expectation is 0.233.
- STAR has approximately 850M total events for analysis at 200 GeV, comprising 420M MB events (2010), 110M central trigger events (2010), and 320M MB events (2011).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of cumulants in statistical distributions up to fourth order (e.g., BD/NBD baselines for net-charge, net-kaon, and net-\(\Lambda\) by Gary (2016) and Xiaofeng (2017)) to the sixth order cumulant \(C_6/C_2\). It directly addresses the interpretation of negative \(C_6/C_2\) signals reported by the STAR Collaboration (2019, 2020) and Lattice QCD calculations by Karsch and collaborators (2011-2014). The authors show that the BD baseline, combined with finite-statistics effects from the CBWC method, can produce negative \(C_6/C_2\) values in central collisions without any phase transition physics. This enables a more careful separation of non-phase-transition contributions before comparing experimental results to Lattice QCD predictions for the chiral crossover.
