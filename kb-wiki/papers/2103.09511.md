# Three-body description of $^9$C: Role of low-lying resonances in breakup reactions

> The $^9$C nucleus and related capture reaction, ${^8\mathrm{B}}(p,γ){^9\mathrm{C}}$, have been intensively studied with an astrophysical interest. Due to the weakly-bound nature of $^9$C, its structure is likely to be described as the three-body (${^7\mathrm{Be}}+p+p$). Its continuum structure is also important to describe reaction processes of $^9$C, with which the reaction rate of the ${^8\mathrm{B}}(p,γ){^9\mathrm{C}}$ process have been extracted indirectly. We perform three-body calculations on $^9$C and discuss properties of its ground and low-lying states via breakup reactions. We employ the three-body model of $^9$C using the Gaussian-expansion method combined with the complex-scaling method. This model is implemented in the four-body version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method, by which breakup reactions of $^9$C are studied. The intrinsic spin of $^7$Be is disregarded. By tuning a three-body interaction in the Hamiltonian of $^9$C, we obtain the low-lying $2^+$ state with the resonant energy 0.781 MeV and the decay width 0.137 MeV, which is consistent with the available experimental information and a relatively high-lying second $2^+$ wider resonant state. Our calculation predicts also sole $0^+$ and three $1^-$ resonant states. We discuss the role of these resonances in the elastic breakup cross section of $^9$C on $^{208}$Pb at 65 and 160 MeV/A. The low-lying 2$^+$ state is probed as a sharp peak of the breakup cross section, while the 1$^-$ states enhance the cross section around 3 MeV. Our calculations will further support the future and ongoing experimental campaigns for extracting astrophysical information and evaluating the two-proton removal cross-sections.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a three-body (⁷Be + p + p) model of ⁹C, using a tuned three-body force and disregarding the intrinsic spin of ⁷Be, can reproduce the low-lying 2⁺ resonance energy and width consistently with experiment, and that the resulting resonances produce distinct signatures in breakup cross sections on ²⁰⁸Pb at 65 and 160 MeV/nucleon. The hedge is that the 3BF strength must be optimized separately for the 2⁺ states (V₀ = 5.320 MeV) versus the 0⁺ and 1⁻ states (V₀ = 3.172 MeV), and that the spinless-core approximation prevents reproduction of the 1⁺, 0⁺, and 3⁺ states of ⁸B.

## Method

The authors describe ⁹C as a ⁷Be + p + p three-body system using the Gaussian-expansion method (GEM) combined with the complex-scaling method (CSM) to identify resonances. They then implement these three-body wave functions in a four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) to calculate elastic breakup cross sections of ⁹C on a ²⁰⁸Pb target at 65 and 160 MeV/nucleon. The intrinsic spin of ⁷Be is disregarded, and a phenomenological three-body force is tuned to reproduce the ground-state two-proton separation energy and the low-lying 2⁺ resonance.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state two-proton separation energy S₂ₚ = 1.437 MeV, reproduced by including a repulsive three-body force with strength V₀ = 3.172 MeV for the 0⁺ and 1⁻ states.
* Low-lying 2⁺ resonance: resonant energy 0.781 MeV, decay width 0.137 MeV, obtained with a separately optimized three-body force strength V₀ = 5.320 MeV.
* High-lying second 2⁺ resonance: resonant energy 3.680 MeV, decay width 2.088 MeV.
* Three 1⁻ resonances: resonant energies (decay widths) of 3.129 (0.719), 4.187 (2.088), and 4.537 (2.293) MeV.
* One 0⁺ resonance: resonant energy 5.488 MeV, decay width 4.428 MeV.
* At 65 MeV/nucleon on ²⁰⁸Pb, the 1⁻ contribution is about 58.4% of the total breakup cross section, the 2⁺ contribution about 26.0% (with the narrow 2⁺ resonance contributing about 19.0%), and the 0⁺ contribution about 15.6%.
* Convergence of the calculated elastic breakup cross section achieved within about 3.5% fluctuation with respect to GEM parameters.

## Context

This work extends three-body models of ⁹C previously studied by Timofeyuk (2008) using hyperspherical methods, and by Arai (2001) using a four-body (α+³He+p+p) calculation. It also builds on CDCC studies of ⁹C breakup by Fukui (2012) and transfer reactions by Fukui (2015). The calculations are designed to support ongoing experimental campaigns (Chilug 2019, 2020) aiming to extract the astrophysical S-factor of ⁸B(p,γ)⁹C and to evaluate two-proton removal cross sections. The four-body CDCC implementation for a proton-rich nucleus is reported as the first such application.
