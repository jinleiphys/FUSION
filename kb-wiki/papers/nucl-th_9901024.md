# Empirical Formalism For Projectile Fragmentation and Production of New Neutron-rich Nuclei with RIBs

> A new empirical parametrization for the estimation of cross-sections of projectile fragments has been prescribed for studying the advantages and limitations of high energy RI beams for the production of new exotic nuclei. The parametrization reproduces the experimental data for the production of fragments from neutron rich projectiles accurately in contrast to the existing parametrization which tends to overestimate the cross-section of neutron rich fragments in most cases. The modified formalism has been used to compute the cross-sections of neutron-rich species produced by fragmentation of radioactive projectiles (RIBs). It has been found that, given any limit of production cross -section, the exoticity of the fragment increases rather slowly and shows a saturation tendency as the projectile is made more and more exotic.

# Empirical Formalism For Projectile Fragmentation and Production of New Neutron-rich Nuclei with RIBs

## Key claim

The new empirical parametrization reproduces experimental data for fragment production from neutron-rich projectiles accurately, whereas the existing formalism of Summerer et al (1990) tends to overestimate cross-sections of neutron-rich fragments; however, the modified formalism shows that as the projectile is made more exotic, the exoticity of the fragment increases slowly and saturates, which limits the utility of very neutron-rich radioactive beams for producing new neutron-rich exotic species.

## Method

The authors modified the charge-dispersion part of the Summerer et al (1990) empirical parametrization for projectile fragmentation cross-sections, introducing exoticity parameters for both projectile and fragment (defined as (A - A_beta)/Z) to account for the memory effect, and adding a (A_P - A_F)^(1/3) dependence to the width parameter R of the charge distribution. The mass-yield expression from Abu-Magd, Friedman and Hufner (1986) was retained unchanged. The formalism was tested against experimental data for reactions including 48Ca + 181Ta at 70 A MeV, 50Ti + 181Ta and 50Ti + 9Be at 80 A MeV, 78Kr + 58Ni at 75 A MeV, 86Kr + 27Al at 70 A MeV, and 86Kr + 9Be at 500 A MeV.

## Key numbers

- For production of 35Si and 40Si from 50Ca projectile, the ratio sigma_old/sigma_new increases from a factor of 3 for 35Si to a factor of 35.5 for the more exotic fragment 40Si.
- For production of 40Si, the ratio sigma_old/sigma_new increases from 35.5 with 50Ca projectile to about 52.2 with the more exotic projectile 58Ca.
- The formalism predicts cross-sections of 7 pb, 2.2 pb, and 180 pb for production of 78Ni by fragmentation of 86Kr, 98Kr, and 86Ge respectively.
- For Si fragments, the neutron drip line (rho_F = 0.71 for Si) can be reached with cross-sections in the range 0.001 to 0.01 mb for projectile exoticities greater than 0.65, requiring 56Ca or more exotic Ca projectiles.
- At energies below 100 A MeV, the exponent U of the charge distribution is set to 1.57 for neutron-rich fragments; at 500 A MeV, U = 1.4 is needed for reasonable agreement.

## Context

This work extends the empirical parametrization of Summerer et al (1990), which was developed for stable projectiles and was found to overestimate cross-sections for neutron-rich fragments from neutron-rich projectiles such as 48Ca. The modified formalism enables more reliable predictions for production yields of new neutron-rich nuclei using radioactive ion beams (RIBs) at projectile fragment separator facilities. The saturation of fragment exoticity with increasing projectile exoticity, driven by the new width parameter dependence and the drip-line cutoff factor xi^d, provides guidance for planning experiments at RIB facilities and suggests diminishing returns from using extremely neutron-rich projectiles.
