# Constraining the neutron-neutron scattering length with \eftnopi

> We compute a model-independent correlation between the difference of neutron-neutron and proton-proton scattering lengths |a(nn)-a^C(pp)| and the splitting in binding energies between Helium-3 and tritium nuclei. We use the effective field theory without explicit pions to show that this correlation relies only on the existence of large scattering lengths in the NN system. Our leading-order calculation, taken together with experimental values for binding energies and a^C(pp), yields a(nn)=-22.9 \pm 4.1 fm.

# Constraining the neutron-neutron scattering length with EFT(π̸)

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a model-independent correlation between the difference of the neutron-neutron and proton-proton scattering lengths |a(nn) - a^C(pp)| and the binding-energy splitting between Helium-3 and tritium, using leading-order effective field theory without explicit pions (EFT(π̸)). This correlation relies only on the existence of large scattering lengths in the NN system and yields a constraint on a(nn) that is independent of the conflicting direct experimental extractions.

## Method

The authors employ leading-order EFT(π̸) with a Gaussian regulator, including Coulomb interactions nonperturbatively and explicit charge-symmetry-breaking contact terms to distinguish nn, pp, and np channels. They solve the Schrodinger equation for two-body scattering and three-body bound states using a variational Resonating Group Method (RGM) in Jacobi-coordinate space, fitting five low-energy constants to the np singlet and triplet scattering lengths, the deuteron binding energy, the pp scattering length a^C(pp), and the triton and Helium-3 binding energies. The correlation between Δ(3) = B(t) - B(^3He) and Δ(a) = a(nn) - a^C(pp) is mapped by varying the nn contact interaction C^{nn}_S while keeping all other parameters fixed.

## Key numbers

- a(nn) = -22.9 ± 4.1 fm: the leading-order EFT(π̸) prediction obtained from combining the computed correlation with experimental binding energies and a^C(pp)
- Δ(3) = B(t) - B(^3He) = 764 keV: the experimental trinucleon binding-energy difference used as input
- a^C(pp) = -7.8063 ± 0.0026 fm: the proton-proton scattering length used as input
- a(np,s) = -23.748 ± 0.009 fm: the neutron-proton singlet scattering length used as input
- a(np,t) = 5.4194 ± 0.002 fm: the neutron-proton triplet scattering length used as input
- B(d) = 2.224575 ± 0.000009 MeV: the deuteron binding energy used as input
- B(t) = 8.481855 ± 0.000013 MeV: the triton binding energy used as input
- B(^3He) = 7.718109 ± 0.000010 MeV: the Helium-3 binding energy used as input
- Λ = 400, 800, 1600 MeV: three regulator cutoff values used to assess short-range theoretical uncertainty
- Extrapolated limit Λ → ∞ yields a(nn) ≈ -20.8 fm from the correlation line intersection with experimental Δ(3)
- Variational uncertainty in the RGM calculation is ±1.5 keV for the binding-energy difference

## Context

This work extends a long history of model-based studies correlating the nn scattering length with trinucleon binding energies, previously explored using potentials such as AV18+UIX (Pudliner 1997) and AV18 (Machleidt and Muther 2001), which predicted Δ(3) values of 756(1) keV and 762(9) keV respectively. The EFT(π̸) approach provides a model-independent framework that relies only on the existence of large scattering lengths, in contrast to the phenomenological potential models. The result enables a constraint on a(nn) that is independent of the conflicting direct experimental extractions from nd breakup reactions, which currently yield a(nn) = -16.1 ± 0.4 fm (Howell 1998, 2000) and a(nn) = -18.7 ± 0.7 fm (Gonzalez 1999, 2006). The analysis also builds on prior EFT(π̸) calculations of few-nucleon systems by Bedaque (1999), Platter (2004), and Kirscher (2010), and on the dibaryon formalism treatment of Δ(3) by Ando and Birse (2010) which predicted Δ(3) = 0.82 MeV.
