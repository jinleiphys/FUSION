# Bayesian truncation errors in equations of state of nuclear matter with chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials

> The truncation errors in equations of state (EOSs) of nuclear matter derived from the chiral nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials at different expansion orders are analyzed by a Bayesian model. These EOSs are expanded as functions of a dimensionless parameter, $Q$, which is determined by Fermi momentum, $k_F$ and breakdown scale, $Λ_b$. The degree-of-belief (DoB) intervals predicted by the chiral effective field theory are calculated within the corresponding expansion coefficients and the specific prior probability distribution functions in terms of Bayes theorem. The truncation errors of EOSs, generated by the DoB intervals, exhibit good order-by-order convergences with different chiral expansion order potentials. When DoB is considered as $1σ$ credibility, i.e., $68.27\%$ confidence interval, the truncation errors of binding energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are consistent with the results given by a simple error analysis method proposed by Epelbaum, {\it et al.} Finally, the reasonable values of the breakdown scale $Λ_b$ in nuclear matter are discussed through consistency checks of Bayesian method based on the calculations of success rate.

## Key claim

The Bayesian model shows that truncation errors in nuclear matter equations of state exhibit good order-by-order convergence with different chiral expansion order potentials, and when the degree-of-belief is taken as the 1σ (68.27%) confidence interval, the truncation errors are consistent with the simpler error analysis method of Epelbaum et al., but only for orders beyond NLO.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from the EKM group (Epelbaum et al.) to calculate the binding energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) order-by-order from NLO to N⁴LO. They extracted the dimensionless expansion coefficients cₙ from the EOSs and applied a Bayesian model with a log-normal prior for the coefficient size parameter c̄ and a step-function prior for the coefficients, using a hyperparameter δ = 1 and h = 10 higher-order terms, to compute degree-of-belief intervals for the truncation errors at each chiral order.

## Key numbers

- The expansion coefficients cₙ for PNM are located in the region [-2, 2] across all densities.
- For SNM, the empirical saturation properties are ρ = 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³ and E/A = -16 ± 1 MeV, which are not satisfied by the two-body chiral potentials even after including Bayesian truncation errors.
- The consistency checks for PNM used N = 24 data points at densities ρ = 0.05, 0.10, …, 0.40 fm⁻³.
- For PNM, breakdown scales Λ_b = 660 MeV (λ = 1.1) and Λ_b = 720 MeV (λ = 1.2) fall completely within the 2σ confidence interval of the ideal success rate.
- For SNM, the consistency checks favor a breakdown scale Λ_b = 480 MeV (λ = 0.8).

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian truncation error analysis previously applied to NN scattering observables by Furnstahl et al. (2015) and Melendez et al. (2017) to the case of nuclear matter equations of state, which were previously studied with a simpler error analysis method by Hu et al. (2017) using the same EKM chiral potentials. The results enable systematic uncertainty quantification for nuclear matter calculations from chiral effective field theory and suggest that a larger breakdown scale than the 600 MeV value from NN scattering may be more appropriate for nuclear matter, particularly for pure neutron matter.
