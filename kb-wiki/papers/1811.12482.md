# First results on the scalar WIMP-pion coupling, using the XENON1T experiment

> We present first results on the scalar WIMP-pion coupling from 1 t$\times$yr of exposure with the XENON1T experiment. This interaction is generated when the WIMP couples to a virtual pion exchanged between the nucleons in a nucleus. In contrast to most non-relativistic operators, these pion-exchange currents can be coherently enhanced by the total number of nucleons, and therefore may dominate in scenarios where spin-independent WIMP-nucleon interactions are suppressed. Moreover, for natural values of the couplings, they dominate over the spin-dependent channel due to their coherence in the nucleus. Using the signal model of this new WIMP-pion channel, no significant excess is found, leading to an upper limit cross section of $6.4\times10^{-46}$ cm$^2$ (90 % confidence level) at 30 GeV/c$^2$ WIMP mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first experimental search for a scalar WIMP-pion coupling, where the WIMP interacts with a virtual pion exchanged between nucleons in a nucleus, rather than directly with a single nucleon. No significant excess is found, leading to an upper limit cross section of 6.4 x 10^-46 cm^2 (90% confidence level) at 30 GeV/c^2 WIMP mass, but this result is conditional on the assumption that the standard spin-independent and spin-dependent WIMP-nucleon couplings are set to zero.

## Method

The XENON1T dual-phase liquid xenon time projection chamber (TPC) with 2.0 t target mass was used, analyzing the combined SR0+SR1 data set totaling 1 t x yr of exposure (32.1 days plus 246.7 days live time). The signal model for the scalar WIMP-pion coupling was derived from chiral effective field theory, replacing the standard spin-independent nuclear form factor F_+^M(q^2) with the novel pion-exchange form factor F_pi(q^2) in the differential recoil spectrum. The analysis used the same event selection, detector response calibration, background models (electronic recoils, radiogenic and cosmogenic neutrons, coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, accidental coincidences, and surface events), and profile likelihood ratio method as the XENON1T spin-independent search, with only the signal model changed.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on scalar WIMP-pion cross section: 6.4 x 10^-46 cm^2 (90% confidence level) at 30 GeV/c^2 WIMP mass
- Lowest local discovery p-value: 0.14, observed for WIMP masses above approximately 200 GeV/c^2
- Hierarchy of nuclear structure factors for xenon isotopes 129Xe and 131Xe: 1.7 x 10^4 (spin-independent) >> 1.1 x 10^3 (WIMP-pion) >> 0.34, 0.13 (spin-dependent)
- Chiral EFT scaling estimate: A^2 >> 4 (M_pi/Lambda_chi)^6 (m_N/M_pi)^2 A^2 >> (4/3)(J+1/J) <S_n/p>^2, with Lambda_chi ~ 500-600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the XENON1T spin-independent WIMP-nucleon search (Aprile 2018) to a new interaction channel motivated by chiral effective field theory calculations of two-body currents (Hoferichter 2015, 2016, 2018). The WIMP-pion coupling is predicted to be subleading to spin-independent scattering but dominant over spin-dependent scattering due to its coherent enhancement by the total number of nucleons, and it becomes relevant in scenarios where spin-independent couplings are suppressed, such as in heavy-WIMP effective field theory (Hill 2013) or blind spots in the minimal supersymmetric standard model (Cheung 2012, Huang 2014, Crivellin 2015). This analysis enables future comprehensive studies of WIMP-nucleus interactions beyond the standard spin-independent and spin-dependent channels, and the same methodology can be applied to other coherent two-body current operators.
