# Relativistic fluid dynamics of spin-polarized systems of particles

> We review basic ingredients of the recently introduced perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations for particles with spin one-half. For a quasi-realistic setup, first numerical solutions for various hydrodynamic variables including the spin polarization tensor are presented. Our results indicate that the initial spin polarization of the hottest region may play a dominant role in the polarization of particles at freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical solutions of perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations for spin-1/2 particles, showing that the initial spin polarization of the hottest region may play a dominant role in the polarization of particles at freeze-out, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the numerical implementation is available only for the original formulation of the theory, which may be superseded by more recent work employing an asymmetric energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors solve the conservation equations for energy-momentum and spin tensors for a perfect fluid of spin-1/2 particles with classical (Boltzmann) statistics, using equilibrium distribution functions that depend on temperature T, baryon chemical potential μ, and spin chemical potential Ω. They initialize a Gaussian temperature profile with parameters from the GLISSANDO Glauber model for Au+Au collisions at 20-30% centrality, impose a stationary vortex flow pattern, and evolve the system numerically to track the temperature, flow, and spin polarization tensor components in the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature T₀ = 200 MeV
- Initial central baryon chemical potential μ₀ = 200 MeV
- Particle mass set to m = 1 GeV (mass scale of the Λ hyperon)
- Gaussian widths: σ_y = 2.6 fm, σ_z = 2 fm (from GLISSANDO for Au+Au, centrality 20-30%), σ_x = 1 fm
- Initial spin chemical potential ratio: 2Ωᵢ/Tᵢ = 0.03
- Flow parameter r₀ = 1 fm
- Temperature and flow profiles shown at times t = 0.1, 4, and 8 fm
- Vortex cylinder radius constraint: R < 1/Ω̃ (where Ω̃ is angular velocity)

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles introduced by Florkowski (2017) and builds on the demonstration by Becattini (2007) that spin polarization couples to thermal vorticity in global thermal equilibrium. The numerical solutions enable future studies of spin polarization evolution in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that more recent work by Florkowski (2018) suggests modifications using an asymmetric energy-momentum tensor that would change the spin tensor evolution equation.
