# Transition balance in QCD nucleation

> As an extended and more complete version of the primary QCD nucleation model presented in Ref. [1], the new model introduces explicitly the transition balance and formulates it in both macroscopic and microscopic descriptions. The microscopic description of the transition balance in QCD nucleation is implemented in a kinetic parton cascade model and tested for a first-order phase transition from gluons to pions in a one-dimensional expansion with Bjorken boost invariance.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous QCD nucleation model (Feng, 2016) by explicitly introducing "transition balance", simultaneous forward and reverse transitions between partons and hadrons, to describe both bubble growth in expansion and bubble shrinkage in compression, under the condition that the system is not too far from thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors formulate transition balance in both macroscopic (hydrodynamic) and microscopic (kinetic) descriptions, deriving volume changes for parton and hadron phases from energy balance equations that include shear pressure and particle flow. The microscopic scheme is implemented in the Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS) kinetic parton cascade model, using stochastic transition probabilities for processes \(g+g \leftrightarrow \pi+\pi\) and \(g+g \leftrightarrow \pi+\pi+\pi\) (and their inverses), with momentum amplification to account for latent heat. The model is tested for a first-order phase transition from gluons to pions in a one-dimensional Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature: \(T_c = 0.2227\ \text{GeV}\).
- Bag constant: \(B^{1/4} = 0.23\ \text{GeV}\).
- Initial time: \(\tau_0 = 0.5\ \text{fm/c}\) with gluon temperature \(T_g = 0.3\ \text{GeV}\).
- Time when nucleation begins: \(\tau_c = 1.415\ \text{fm/c}\).
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at \(\tau_c\): \(\eta_g/s_g = 0.0406\).
- Gluon degeneracy factor: \(d_g = 16\); pion degeneracy factor: \(d_\pi = 3\).
- Cross section for gluonic scatterings: \(\sigma_g = 40\ \text{mb}\); test particle number: \(N_{test} = 20000\).
- Ratio of gained pions to lost gluons in conversion: \(1 < r < 61/48\) (approximately 1.27).

## Context

This work extends the primary QCD nucleation model of Feng (2016) by incorporating simultaneous forward and reverse transitions, enabling description of both expansion (partons to hadrons) and compression (hadrons to partons) scenarios, which the earlier model could not handle. The new framework enables future studies of first-order phase transition dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including the development of hydrodynamic and kinetic transport models for separate phases, and can be extended to include quarks and multiple hadron species.
