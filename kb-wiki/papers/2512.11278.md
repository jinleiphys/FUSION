# Structure and Formation of the Deeply Bound $\bar{p}$ atoms

> We study theoretically the structure and formation of the deeply bound $\bar{p}$ atoms. We find that the widths of the atomic states are narrower than the level spacing even for deeply bound states so that the well-isolated deeply bound $\bar{p}$ atoms are expected to exist. We also find the $\bar{p}$-nuclear states with huge widths. For the observation of the deep $ {\bar p}$-atomic states, we investigate theoretically the $(\bar{p}, p)$ reactions for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{31}$P target nuclei. We find that the momentum transfer of the $( {\bar p},p)$ reaction is small and the formation of the $ {\bar p}$-atomic states can be observed as the discrete peak structures in the $( {\bar p},p)$ spectrum. We conclude that the $(\bar{p}, p)$ reactions are very much suited for the $\bar{p}$ atom formation and the spectra of the reaction are expected to provide new valuable information on the $ {\bar p}$ atoms and $ {\bar p}$-nucleus interaction.

## Key claim

The authors claim that deeply bound antiprotonic atomic states exist as discrete, well-separated levels with widths narrower than the level spacing, but this holds only for atomic states (not nuclear states), and the observation of these states via the (p-bar, p) reaction depends on matching the momentum transfer to the angular momentum transfer of the specific final-state configuration.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation with a phenomenological p-bar-nucleus optical potential (Eq. 1) using a complex effective scattering length b0 = (2.5 ± 0.3) + i(3.4 ± 0.3) fm from Batty (1997), including finite-size Coulomb potential and leading-order vacuum polarization effects. They calculate formation cross sections for the (p-bar, p) reaction on 12C, 16O, and 31P targets at p_lab = 1 GeV/c using the effective number approach with eikonal distorted waves, summing over all combinations of p-bar bound states and proton-hole states in the final nucleus.

## Key numbers

- b0 = (2.5 ± 0.3) + i(3.4 ± 0.3) fm for the p-bar-nucleus optical potential
- For p-bar-11B atomic states: 1s binding energy 268.9 keV with width 57.9 keV; 2s binding energy 94.3 keV with width 12.4 keV; level spacing between atomic 1s and 2s is 174.6 keV
- For p-bar-11B nuclear states: 1s binding energy 109.2 MeV with width 722.8 MeV; 2s binding energy 12.4 MeV with width 504.7 MeV
- For p-bar-30Si atomic 1s state: binding energy 1100.8 keV with width 323.1 keV
- Momentum transfer at theta_lab = 0 degrees is approximately 22 MeV/c for 12C target at p_lab = 1 GeV/c
- Momentum transfer at theta_lab = 5 degrees is approximately 89 MeV/c for 12C target at p_lab = 1 GeV/c
- Occupation probability of the 2s1/2 state in 31P is 0.40 from Wesseling (1992)
- Proton separation energy for 2s1/2 state in 31P is 7.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Gibbs and Kaufmann (1984) and Baltz et al. (1985) who investigated deeply bound p-bar atomic states and their formation cross sections, and by Friedman (1999) who provided simple estimations of p-bar atom formation. The authors use the phenomenological optical potential from Batty (1997) and apply the effective number approach previously used for pionic atom formation by Yamazaki (2012) and for kaonic systems by Yamagata (2005). The paper enables future experimental searches for deeply bound p-bar atoms using the (p-bar, p) reaction, particularly identifying 31P as a suitable target for observing the deepest 1s atomic state.
