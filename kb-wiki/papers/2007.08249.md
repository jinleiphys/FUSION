# Exact equilibrium distributions in statistical quantum field theory with rotation and acceleration: scalar field

> We derive a general exact form of the phase space distribution function and the thermal expectation values of local operators for the free quantum scalar field at equilibrium with rotation and acceleration in flat space-time without solving field equations in curvilinear coordinates. After factorizing the density operator with group theoretical methods, we obtain the exact form of the phase space distribution function as a formal series in thermal vorticity through an iterative method and we calculate thermal expectation values by means of analytic continuation techniques. We separately discuss the cases of pure rotation and pure acceleration and derive analytic results for the stress-energy tensor of the massless field. The expressions found agree with the exact analytic solutions obtained by solving the field equation in suitable curvilinear coordinates for the two cases at stake and already - or implicitly - known in literature. In order to extract finite values for the pure acceleration case we introduce the concept of analytic distillation of a complex function. For the massless field, the obtained expressions of the currents are polynomials in the acceleration/temperature ratios which vanish at $2π$, in full accordance with the Unruh effect.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact form of the phase-space distribution function for a free quantum scalar field in global thermodynamic equilibrium with rotation and acceleration, without solving field equations in curvilinear coordinates; the method works for imaginary thermal vorticity first and then extracts the physical solution via analytic continuation, with the caveat that the series solution is only valid after integration over momentum and that non-analytic homogeneous solutions may exist.

## Method

The authors factorize the density operator using Poincaré group relations, writing it as a product of a translation operator and a Lorentz transformation operator for imaginary thermal vorticity. They then derive an iterative solution for the thermal expectation value of one creation and one annihilation operator, obtaining a series of Dirac delta functions with exponential factors. The physical results for real thermal vorticity are obtained by extracting the analytic part of the series (a procedure called "analytic distillation") and then performing analytic continuation.

## Key numbers

* For the massless field in pure acceleration, the obtained expressions of the currents are polynomials in the acceleration/temperature ratios which vanish at \(2\pi\), in full accordance with the Unruh effect.
* The series solution for \(\langle \hat{a}^\dagger(p) \hat{a}(p') \rangle\) is given as \(2\varepsilon' \sum_{n=1}^\infty \delta^3(\Lambda^n \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}') \exp[-n \tilde{b}(-n\varpi) \cdot p]\), where \(\Lambda = \exp[\varpi : J/2]\) and \(\tilde{b}\) is defined by a series expansion in powers of \(\varpi\).
* In the limit \(\varpi \to 0\), the expression reduces to the standard Bose-Einstein distribution: \(\langle \hat{a}^\dagger(p) \hat{a}(p') \rangle = 2\varepsilon' \delta^3(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}') / (e^{b \cdot p} - 1)\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of quantum corrections due to thermal vorticity (Becattini 2015, Buzzegoli 2018, Prokhorov 2019) to an exact, non-perturbative treatment for the scalar field. It agrees with exact solutions obtained by solving field equations in curvilinear coordinates for pure rotation (Ambrus) and pure acceleration (Becattini 2017, Becattini 2019). The method enables calculation of thermal expectation values in previously unexplored cases where both acceleration and vorticity are non-vanishing, and is a first step toward deriving exact solutions for the Dirac field, which is relevant for fermion polarization in relativistic fluids.
