# Resonances and continuum states of drip-line nuclei using the complex scaling method

> Resonances and continuum states of He isotopes are investigated using the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) with the complex scaling method (CSM). We discuss the following subjects: 1) Spectroscopic factors of the unbound nucleus 7He into the 6He-n components and their relation to the one-neutron removal strengths of 7He. The importance of the 6He(2+) resonance is shown. 2) Structure of five-body 0+ resonance of 8He from the viewpoint of the two-neutron pair coupling. The monopole strengths into five-body unbound states are also investigated. It is found that the sequential breakup process of 8He \to 7He+n \to 6He+n+n is dominant in the monopole excitation, while the contribution of 8He(0+_2) is negligible.

# Resonances and continuum states of drip-line nuclei using the complex scaling method

## Key claim
The complex scaling method combined with the cluster orbital shell model can describe four- and five-body resonances of He isotopes under correct boundary conditions for all decay channels, but the predicted $0^+_2$ resonance of $^8$He at 6.29 MeV excitation energy contributes negligibly to monopole strength, requiring other observables for experimental confirmation.

## Method
The authors employ the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) for $^4$He+$N_{\rm v}n$ systems, using the KKNN potential for $\alpha$-$n$ interactions and the Minnesota potential for $n$-$n$ interactions, with valence neutron orbits taken up to $d$-waves expanded via Gaussian basis functions. The complex scaling method (CSM) transforms relative coordinates by a scaling angle $\theta$, converting Gamow resonance boundary conditions into damping behavior at asymptotic regions, allowing bound-state techniques to be used for resonances. The extended completeness relation (ECR) constructed from complex-scaled eigenstates enables calculation of strength functions via the complex-scaled Green's function.

## Key numbers
- $^7$He ground state lies 0.40 MeV above the $^6$He ground state (experimental values: 0.44 MeV and 0.36 MeV from Skaza et al.)
- $^8$He ground state binding energy: 3.22 MeV from $^4$He+$4n$ threshold (experimental: 3.11 MeV)
- $^8$He $0^+_2$ state: excitation energy 6.29 MeV, decay width 3.19 MeV
- $^6$He matter radius: 2.37 fm (experimental range: 2.30-2.37 fm); charge radius: 2.01 fm (experimental: 2.068(11) fm)
- $^8$He matter radius: 2.52 fm (experimental range: 2.45-2.49 fm); charge radius: 1.92 fm (experimental: 1.929(26) fm)
- $^7$He($3/2^-_1$) spectroscopic factor for $^6$He($0^+_1$)-$n$: $0.64 + i0.06$ (real part matches experimental $0.64\pm0.09$ from Beck et al.)
- $^7$He($3/2^-_1$) spectroscopic factor for $^6$He($2^+_1$)-$n$: $1.55 - i0.31$
- $^7$He($1/2^-$) spectroscopic factor for $^6$He($0^+_1$)-$n(p_{1/2})$: $1.00 - i0.13$
- $^8$He ground state $(p_{3/2})^4$ configuration mixing: 86.0%
- $^8$He $0^+_2$ state $(p_{3/2})^2(p_{1/2})^2$ configuration mixing: 96.9%
- Monopole transition matrix elements from $^8$He ground state to $0^+_2$: $1.78-i0.38$ fm$^4$ (isoscalar) and $-0.003+i0.018$ fm$^4$ (isovector)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous COSM+CSM calculations for $^{5,6}$He (Myo 2007, 2009, 2010) to four- and five-body resonances of $^7$He and $^8$He, addressing the difficulty of observing excited states in He isotopes due to multiparticle decays noted by Skaza et al. (2006, 2007). The method enables calculation of spectroscopic factors and strength functions including non-resonant continuum contributions, similar to prior three-body Coulomb breakup analyses of halo nuclei (Myo 2001, 2007). The predicted $0^+_2$ state of $^8$He and its negligible monopole strength motivate experimental searches via two-neutron transfer reactions such as $^6$He($t$,$p$)$^8$He reported by Golovkov et al. (2009) without spin assignment.
