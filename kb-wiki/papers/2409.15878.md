# Second-order chiral kinetic theory from Wigner function approach

> We present a systematic method to derive the chiral kinetic theory from the Wigner equations based on quantum field theory order by order. We take special effort to derive the chiral kinetic theory in seven-dimensional phase space from the eight-dimensional chiral kinetic theory. We give the seven-dimensional chiral kinetic theory up to second order of semiclassical expansion. We find some new second-order contributions compared with other approaches.

## Key claim

The authors derive a second-order chiral kinetic equation in seven-dimensional phase space from quantum field theory using the Wigner function approach, finding new second-order contributions that were not obtained in previous work, specifically terms associated with higher derivatives of the distribution function that originate from the uncertainty principle.

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner equations for chiral fermions under a constant external electromagnetic field, decompose the chiral Wigner function using a time-like constant 4-vector, and perform a semiclassical expansion in powers of \(\hbar\) up to second order. They integrate out the time-like component of momentum to reduce the eight-dimensional chiral kinetic theory to seven-dimensional phase space, systematically organizing the Wigner function and kinetic equations order by order.

## Key numbers

- The first-order correction to the invariant phase space is \(\sqrt{\omega}^{(1)} = -\frac{s\lambda(B\cdot \bar p)}{2|\bar p|^3}\), where \(s\) is helicity, \(\lambda\) is energy sign, \(B\) is magnetic field, and \(\bar p\) is the spatial momentum.
- The first-order correction to the particle energy is \(\varepsilon^{(1)} = \frac{s\lambda(B\cdot \bar p)}{2|\bar p|^2}\).
- The second-order correction to the invariant phase space is \(\sqrt{\omega}^{(2)} = -\frac{1}{2|\bar p|^5}\bar\epsilon^{\rho\mu\nu}\bar p_\rho E_\mu B_\nu -\frac{\lambda}{4} \bar\nabla^\mu \bar\nabla^\nu \frac{\bar p_\mu \bar p_{\nu} -\bar p^2 \Delta_{\mu\nu}}{|\bar p|^4}\), where \(E\) is electric field.
- The second-order correction to the energy is \(\varepsilon^{(2)} = \frac{1}{8|\bar p|^5} \left[(B\cdot \bar p)^2 + B^2 \bar p^2 + (E\cdot \bar p)^2 -\bar p^2 E^2 \right] + \frac{\lambda}{4|\bar p|^4} \bar \epsilon^{\mu\rho\sigma} E_\mu B_\rho \bar p_\sigma + \text{(operator terms)}\).
- The second-order correction to the four-velocity \(\dot{x}^{(2)\mu}\) is given as an operator expression involving terms up to \(\frac{1}{|\bar p|^6}\).

## Context

This work extends previous first-order chiral kinetic theory (Gao 2012, Stephanov 2012, Son 2012) to second order in the semiclassical expansion, including both electromagnetic field and spacetime gradient contributions. It differs from Gorbar (2017), which derived second-order chiral kinetic theory from quantum mechanics including only second order in electromagnetic field, and from Hayata (2020) and Mameda (2023), which derived second-order equations in eight-dimensional phase space. The present result enables systematic study of second-order chiral effects such as those discussed by Kharzeev (2011) and others, and provides a complete quantum field theory derivation of the seven-dimensional second-order chiral kinetic equation.
