# Charmed Mesons and Charmonia: Three-Meson Strong Couplings

> 5 pages, 3 figures, contributed to "QCD@Work 2016 - International Workshop on Quantum Chromodynamics: Theory and Experiment" (27 - 30 June 2016, Martina Franca, Italy)

# Charmed Mesons and Charmonia: Three-Meson Strong Couplings

## Key claim

The relativistic constituent-quark model predictions for strong three-meson couplings involving charmonia and charmed mesons are systematically larger by roughly a factor of two than the corresponding results from QCD sum rules, revealing a clear disaccord between the two theoretical approaches.

## Method

The strong couplings were extracted from the residues of poles in transition form factors for timelike momentum transfer, computed using a relativistic dispersion approach based on a constituent-quark model with Gaussian wave functions. The transition form factors were calculated at low momentum transfer via double dispersion integrals of spectral densities derived from one-loop Feynman graphs, then interpolated using a three-parameter ansatz and extrapolated to the resonance region to obtain the pole residues.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\eta_c\eta_c\psi} = 25.8 \pm 1.7$ (dimensionless coupling for $\eta_c\eta_cJ/\psi$)
- $g_{\eta_c\psi\psi} = (10.6 \pm 1.5)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (coupling for $\eta_cJ/\psi J/\psi$)
- $g_{DD\psi} = 26.04 \pm 1.43$ (quark model) versus $11.6 \pm 1.8$ (QCD sum rules, Mat05)
- $g_{DD^*\psi} = (10.7 \pm 0.4)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (quark model) versus $(4.0 \pm 0.6)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (QCD sum rules, Mat05)
- $g_{D_sD_s\psi} = 23.83 \pm 0.78$ (quark model) versus $11.96 \pm 1.34$ (QCD sum rules, Bra14)
- $g_{D_sD_s^*\psi} = (9.6 \pm 0.8)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (quark model) versus $(4.30 \pm 1.53)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ (QCD sum rules, Bra15)
- $g_{DD^*\eta_c} = 15.51 \pm 0.45$
- $g_{D^*D^*\eta_c} = (9.76 \pm 0.32)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$
- $g_{D_sD_s^*\eta_c} = 14.15 \pm 0.52$
- $g_{D_s^*D_s^*\eta_c} = (8.27 \pm 0.37)\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$
- Constituent-quark masses: $m_u = m_d = 0.23\ \text{GeV}$, $m_s = 0.35\ \text{GeV}$, $m_c = 1.45\ \text{GeV}$
- SU(3)-breaking effects from replacing $d$ by $s$ quarks change the strong couplings by roughly 10%

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent-quark model approach of Lucha, Melikhov, and Sazdjian (2015) to compute strong couplings among charmonia and between charmonia and charmed mesons. The results are compared with QCD sum-rule calculations by Mat05, Bra14, and Bra15, revealing a systematic discrepancy of approximately a factor of two between the two methods. The findings enable future experimental searches for these couplings at facilities studying charm physics and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations of three-meson strong couplings.
