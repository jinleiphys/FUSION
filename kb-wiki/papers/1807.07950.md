# On Bulk Viscosity at Weak and Strong 't Hooft Couplings

> Bulk viscosity is an important transport coefficient that exists in the hydrodynamical limit only when the underlying theory is non-conformal. One example being thermal QCD with large number of colors. We study bulk viscosity in such a theory at low energies and at weak and strong 't Hooft couplings when the temperature is above the deconfinement temperature. The weak coupling analysis is based on Boltzmann equation from kinetic theory whereas the strong coupling analysis uses non-conformal holographic techniques from string and M-theories. Using these, many properties associated with bulk viscosity may be explicitly derived. This is a shortened companion paper that summarizes some of the results of our longer paper arXiv:1807.04713.

## Key claim

The paper derives bounds on the ratio of bulk viscosity to shear viscosity at both weak and strong 't Hooft couplings, but the strong-coupling results depend on the specific holographic dual (weak or strong string coupling) and the presence of a non-conformality parameter \(\epsilon = 3g_s M^2/(2\pi N)\); the abstract omits that the strong-coupling analysis requires a resolved warped-deformed conifold background and that the bounds differ between the weak-string and strong-string limits.

## Method

At weak 't Hooft coupling, the authors solve the Boltzmann equation for pure gluodynamics using a variational method, matching the kinetic theory to planar ladder diagrams in the \((r,a)\) basis of real-time finite-temperature field theory. At strong 't Hooft coupling, they use two holographic approaches: a weak-string-coupling type IIB dual on a resolved warped-deformed conifold with fluxes, and a strong-string-coupling M-theory uplift (the MQGP limit) involving a triple T-duality and a \(G_2\)-structure manifold. In the MQGP limit, they construct a gauge-invariant scalar perturbation \(Z_s(u)\) and solve its equation of motion near the horizon to extract the sound speed and attenuation constant.

## Key numbers

- At weak 't Hooft coupling: \(\frac{1}{3} - c_s^2 = \frac{55}{3456\pi^4} \lambda^2\) for the deviation of the squared sound speed from its conformal value.
- At weak 't Hooft coupling: \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} \propto \left(1/3 - c_s^2\right)^2\).
- At weak string coupling (strong 't Hooft): \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} > \frac{405 d_1}{16}\left(\frac{1}{3} - c_s^2\right)\), with \(d_1\) in the range \(\frac{32}{405} \le d_1 < \frac{13b r_c^4}{(13b + 640|c_2|) r_h^4}\).
- At strong string coupling (MQGP limit): \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} > \frac{91}{5}\left(\frac{1}{3} - c_s^2\right)\).
- In the MQGP limit, the sound speed is \(c_s = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{32\pi}\left(\frac{g_s M^2}{N}\right) \mathcal{C}_{21}(1) - \frac{g_s M^2}{6\sqrt{2} N}(c_1 + c_2 \log r_h)\).
- In the MQGP limit, the attenuation constant is \(\Gamma = \frac{1}{\pi T}\left[\frac{1}{6} + \frac{3 g_s M^2}{64\pi N}\left(\mathcal{C}_{21}(1) + \frac{8\sqrt{6}\pi}{27}(c_1 + c_2 \log r_h)\right)\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the weak-coupling kinetic-theory calculation of bulk viscosity by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2006) to a diagrammatic justification via planar ladder diagrams, and it contrasts with the lattice QCD sum-rule approach of Karsch, Kharzeev, and Tuchin (2007) and Romatschke and Son (2009), which the authors argue yields too weak constraints. The strong-coupling holographic results build on the Klebanov-Strassler (2000) and Ouyang (2004) conifold constructions and the MQGP limit of Dasgupta, Misra, and collaborators (2014); they provide explicit bounds on \(\zeta/\eta\) that can be compared to the Buchel bound (2008) and enable future studies of bulk viscosity in thermal QCD-like theories with large numbers of colors.
