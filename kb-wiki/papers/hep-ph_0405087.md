# Baryonium with a phenomenological skyrmion-type potential

> In this paper, we investigate the nucleon-antinucleon static energies in the Skyrme model with the product {\it Anzatz}. The calculation shows that in the ungroomed $S\bar S$ (skyrmion and antiskyrmion) channel, which leads to rapid annihilation, there exists a quasi-stable bound state which may give a natural explanation for the near-threshold enhancement in the proton-antiproton $(p\bar{p})$ mass spectrum reported by the BES Collaboration and the Belle Collaboration. Similar to the phenomenological well potential of the deuteron, we construct a phenomenological skyrmion-type potential to study this narrow $p\bar{p}$-resonance in $ J/ψ\to γp\bar{p}$. By this potential model, a $p\bar{p}$ baryonium with small binding energies is suggested and the decay width of this state is calculated by WKB approximation. In this picture the decay is attributed to quantum tunnelling and $p\bar{p}$ annihilation. Prediction on the decay mode from the baryonium annihilation at rest is also pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the near-threshold enhancement in the proton-antiproton mass spectrum observed by BES and Belle can be explained as a quasi-stable baryonium bound state, but the authors caution that the product Ansatz used is only exact when the separation between skyrmion and antiskyrmion is infinite, and that the classical binding energy from the Skyrme model (approximately 10 MeV) should be taken only as a rough guide for the phenomenological potential.

## Method

The authors first compute the static energy of a skyrmion-antiskyrmion system in the SU(2) Skyrme model using the product Ansatz with the identity rotation (the ungroomed S-bar-S channel), obtaining a classical binding energy from the minimum of the energy as a function of separation. They then construct a phenomenological square-well-plus-barrier potential inspired by this Skyrme result, with parameters guided by the deuteron potential model and QCD-inspired considerations, and solve the Schrodinger equation for S-wave bound states. The decay width of the resulting bound state is calculated using the WKB approximation for quantum tunneling through the barrier, attributing the decay to nucleon-antinucleon annihilation.

## Key numbers

* The classical binding energy of the skyrmion-antiskyrmion system is approximately 10 MeV at a separation of about 2.5 fm (from the Skyrme model calculation).
* The classical nucleon mass used in the Skyrme model is 867 MeV.
* The phenomenological square well potential has depth V_{p-bar-p} = 73 MeV and width a_{p-bar-p} = 2.0 fm, with a barrier height h = m_p/4 and barrier width lambda = 0.1 fm.
* The fitted binding energy of the baryonium state is E_2 = -17.2 MeV.
* The calculated decay width from the WKB approximation is Gamma = 15.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach previously applied to the deuteron by Braaten and Carson (1990) and to the nucleon-antinucleon static potential by Lu and Amado (1990), using the product Ansatz to study the skyrmion-antiskyrmion system. The paper provides a potential-model explanation for the near-threshold p-bar-p enhancement observed by BES in J/psi -> gamma p-bar-p and by Belle in B+ -> K+ p-bar-p and anti-B0 -> D0 p-bar-p, complementing alternative explanations such as final-state interactions (Zou, 2003) or gluonic states (Rosner, 2003). The model predicts that baryonium decay should favor final states with 4 to 7 pions over those with 2 or 3 pions, a testable prediction for future experiments.
