# Chiral condensate in nuclear matter with vacuum corrections

> Within the relativistic Hartree approach using a Lagrangian with density-dependent parameters respecting the chiral limit, it is found that the vacuum corrections from the nucleon Dirac sea soften the equation of state and favor the chiral symmetry restoration at high densities.

## Key claim

Within the relativistic Hartree approach using a Lagrangian with density-dependent parameters respecting the chiral limit, the vacuum corrections from the nucleon Dirac sea soften the equation of state and favor the chiral symmetry restoration at high densities, but the specific parameter set RSL2 is required to bring the predicted pressure closer to the experimental constraint and to make the in-medium chiral condensate decrease to zero by coincidence.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic Hartree model with density-dependent meson masses and coupling constants following the Brown-Rho scaling law, and renormalized the scalar self-energy and energy density using the Chin procedure to include finite vacuum corrections from the Dirac sea. The in-medium chiral condensate was then evaluated by applying the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to the nuclear matter energy density, using phenomenological sigma terms for the nucleon, vector mesons, and scalar meson to relate the current quark mass derivative of hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Incompressibility at saturation: \(\kappa = 230\ \text{MeV}\) for all parameter sets (RSL1, RSL2, SL1)
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: \(M^*/M = 0.788\) for RSL1, 0.810 for RSL2, 0.679 for SL1
- Free-space chiral condensate: \(<\bar{q}q>_0 = -(225 \pm 25\ \text{MeV})^3\)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_N = 45 \pm 7\ \text{MeV}\) (central value 45 MeV used)
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\); pion mass: \(m_\pi = 138\ \text{MeV}\)
- Scalar meson mass: \(m_\sigma = 590\ \text{MeV}\) (obtained from fitting ground-state properties of nuclei)
- Scaling function coefficient for meson masses: \(y \approx 0.1\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the in-medium chiral condensate using the Dirac-Brueckner approach (Li 1994, Brown 1996), relativistic mean-field models (de Jong 1995, de Jong 1996, Ma 1997, Baca 2005), quark-meson coupling models (Saito 1995, Wang 2003), and effective field theory (Pleiner 2008, Kaiser 2008), all of which showed hindrances toward chiral symmetry restoration at high densities. The authors' new contribution is to include vacuum corrections from the Dirac sea, which had received little attention in previous effective-model calculations, and to show that these corrections provide a mechanism favoring the reduction of the in-medium chiral condensate. The results enable further constraints on the in-medium scaling of the vector coupling constant within the Brown-Rho scaling framework and suggest that high-order correlation contributions may also help bring the condensate down at high densities.
