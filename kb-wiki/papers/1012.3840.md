# Quark mass variation constraints from Big Bang nucleosynthesis

> We study the impact on the primordial abundances of light elements created by a variation of the quark masses at the time of Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). In order to navigate through the particle and nuclear physics required to connect quark masses to binding energies and reaction rates in a model-independent way, we use lattice QCD data and a hierarchy of effective field theories. We find that the measured Helium-4 abundances put a bound of -1 % <~ d m_q/m_q <~ 0.7 % on a possible variation of quark masses. The effect of quark mass variations on the deuterium abundances can be largely compensated by changes of the baryon-to-photon ratio eta. Including bounds on the variation of eta coming from WMAP results and adding some additional assumptions further narrows the range of allowed values of d m_q/m_q.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the measured Helium-4 abundances constrain quark mass variations to approximately -1% < δm_q/m_q < 0.7%, but this bound depends on the baryon-to-photon ratio η; the deuterium abundance constraint is largely degenerate with η and therefore cannot independently limit quark mass variations without additional assumptions from WMAP results.

## Method

The authors connect quark mass variations to BBN observables using a two-tier effective field theory approach: chiral effective field theory (χEFT) provides the quark mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths and the deuteron binding energy, which are then used as inputs to pionless EFT to compute binding energies of ²H, ³H, ³He, and ⁴He, as well as the n+p→d+γ reaction rate. These modified nuclear inputs, along with the quark-mass-dependent neutron lifetime and weak interaction phase space parameter Q, are fed into a standard BBN code using a stochastic procedure with 300 random parameter sets per quark mass variation value to account for theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass variation bound from ⁴He abundances: -1% < δm_q/m_q < 0.7%
- Deuteron binding energy variation slope: (m_q/B₂)(δB₂/δm_q) = -0.085 ± 0.027, extracted from Epelbaum (2002)
- Spin singlet scattering length variation: δa_s/δm_π = (-1.4 ± 1.4) fm/MeV
- Neutron lifetime variation with quark mass: (m_q/Γ)(dΓ/dm_q) = 10.6 ± 1.5
- Weak interaction phase space parameter Q variation: (m_q/Q)(dQ/dm_q) = 1.59 ± 0.23
- Baryon-to-photon ratio η₁₀ from WMAP (Dunkley 2008): 6.23 ± 0.17
- BBN-preferred η₁₀ range at 95% confidence: 5.1 < η₁₀ < 6.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fundamental constant variation during BBN by Kneller (2003), Dmitriev (2003), Coc (2006), and others, but avoids model-dependent approximations by using effective field theories supplemented with lattice QCD data. The approach enables a more systematic connection between quark masses and nuclear binding energies than earlier work that relied on changing pion mass scaling alone. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations at physical quark masses to refine these constraints, and the methodology can be applied to other fundamental constant variations in BBN.
