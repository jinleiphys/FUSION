# Comment on "Canonical and Mircocanonical Calculations for Fermi Systems"

> In the context of nuclear physics Pratt recently investigated noninteracting Fermi systems described by the microcanonical and canonical ensemble. As will be shown his discussion of the model of equally spaced levels contains a flaw and a statement which is at least confusing.

# Comment on "Canonical and Mircocanonical Calculations for Fermi Systems"

## Key claim

Pratt's discussion of the equally-spaced-level model for noninteracting Fermi systems contains a flaw: the number of states at excitation energy \(gr\) is not directly connected to the number of partitions of \(r\) into distinct parts, but rather to the unrestricted partition problem where the same integer can be used repeatedly. Additionally, the data shown in Pratt's Fig. 3 for the microcanonical occupation probability were accidentally obtained for a model of randomly placed levels, not for the equally-spaced-level model as claimed.

## Method

The authors re-examine Pratt's recursive relations for the canonical partition function \(Z_A(\beta)\) and the microcanonical occupation probability \(\langle n_k \rangle_{\text{mc}}\) for noninteracting fermions in a model with equally spaced nondegenerate levels (spacing \(g\)). They compare the mapping of the fermionic system to the unrestricted integer partition problem (allowing repeated integers) versus Pratt's implied mapping to partitions into distinct parts, and compute the correct microcanonical occupation probability for excitation energy \(r=20\) using three different algorithms from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The \(A\)-particle groundstate energy is \(E_0(A) = g A(A+1)/2\) and the total energy is \(E_r(A) = E_0(A) + gr\), with \(r = 0,1,2,\ldots\).
- For \(r \leq A\), the number of states \(N_{A,E_r}\) is given by the number of partitions \(p(r)\) of the integer \(r\), independent of \(A\).
- For \(r > A\), the mapping to the partition problem breaks down and \(N_{A,E_r} < p(r)\).
- The correct microcanonical occupation probability for the equally-spaced-level model is shown for excitation energy \(20g\) and different system sizes; for comparison, the Fermi function at temperature \(\tau \equiv k_B T / g = 3.49\) is shown as a solid line.

## Context

This comment corrects a specific error in Pratt (2000) regarding the mapping between the equally-spaced-level Fermi model and integer partitions, and clarifies that the correct mapping is to the unrestricted partition problem as established by earlier work (Katsura and Vertogen, 1968; Arnaud, 1994). The corrected microcanonical occupation probability satisfies a symmetry relation \(\langle n_k \rangle_{\text{mc}} + \langle n_{A-k+1} \rangle_{\text{mc}} = 1\) and is monotonic in \(k\) for \(r \leq A\), which is essential for understanding how the grand canonical Fermi function is approached in the thermodynamic limit. The comment also notes that Pratt's new recursive formulas remain applicable for \(r > A\), where the earlier methods of Katsura and Vertogen (1968) and Arnaud (1994) cannot be used.
