# Multiplicity distributions in QCD jets and jet topics

> We evaluate the Koba-Nielsen-Olesen (KNO) scaling functions for quark- and gluon-initiated jets by incorporating energy conservation into the Double Logarithmic Approximation (DLA). The resulting modified DLA (MDLA) expressions differ substantially from the DLA predictions and qualitatively align with the recently proposed QCD-inspired expressions, albeit with some quantitative differences. By fixing the two parameters in the MDLA expressions, we show that the inclusive charged-particle multiplicity distributions of the two leading jets in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV, measured by ATLAS over a wide jet $p_T$ range of $0.1$-$2.5$ TeV, are well described within experimental uncertainties and consistent with PYTHIA simulations. This conclusion is further supported by direct comparisons with quark- and gluon-initiated jet distributions extracted via jet topics, though the propagated uncertainties from experimental data remain sizable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating energy conservation into the Double Logarithmic Approximation (DLA) yields modified KNO scaling functions that describe ATLAS charged-particle multiplicity distributions for the two leading jets in pp collisions at 13 TeV within experimental uncertainties, though the agreement with quark- and gluon-initiated jet distributions extracted via jet topics carries sizable propagated uncertainties from experimental data.

## Method

The authors evaluate KNO scaling functions for quark- and gluon-initiated jets by imposing energy conservation on the DLA evolution equations for generating functions, producing modified DLA (MDLA) expressions. They compute the MDLA scaling functions using Laguerre polynomial expansions up to order N=40, then combine these with leading-order partonic cross sections and N3LO mean parton multiplicities to predict inclusive charged-particle multiplicity distributions. The theoretical predictions are compared with ATLAS data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV over a jet pT range of 0.1-2.5 TeV and with PYTHIA simulations using the A14 tune, and additionally validated against quark/gluon jet distributions extracted via jet topics.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS data covers jet pT range 0.1-2.5 TeV at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV for pp collisions
- MDLA parameters fitted: gamma_0 = 0.43 and c_q = 0.8, obtained by fitting PYTHIA results over the entire pT range for gluon jets first, then quark jets
- The MDLA KNO functions are well approximated by negative binomial distributions with k = 5.4 for quark jets and k = 8.5 for gluon jets
- Using LHAPDF with scale Q = R*pT, gamma_0 values range from 0.41-0.51 across the plotted pT interval
- The two-loop anomalous dimension gamma(alpha_s) yields values in the range 0.36-0.43 for n_f = 5

## Context

This work extends the treatment of gluon jets in Dokshitzer (1993) by generalizing the MDLA to quark-initiated jets and provides an alternative calculation to the recent QCD-inspired expressions in Dokshitzer (2025). The results enable direct comparison with ATLAS measurements (2019) of charged-particle multiplicity distributions in jets, and demonstrate that jet topics (Metodiev 2018, Komiske 2018) can be used to extract quark and gluon jet multiplicity distributions without additional theoretical input, though with sizable experimental uncertainties.
