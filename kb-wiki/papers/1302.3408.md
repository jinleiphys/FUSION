# A twisted emission geometry in non-central Pb+Pb collisions measurable via azimuthally sensitive HBT

> We use the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions. In the freeze out geometry of non-central Pb+Pb collisions we observe a tilt of the particle emission zone in the collision plane away from the beam axis. We find that the magnitude of this tilt depends on the scale at which the distribution is measured. We quantify this "twisting" behavior with a parameterization and propose to measure it experimentally via azimuthally sensitive Hanbury-Brown Twiss correlations. Additionally we show that the twist is related to the emission of particles from different times during the evolution of the source. A systematic comparison between the theoretically observed twist in the freeze out position distribution and a mock experimental analysis of the model calculations via HBT correlations is shown.

# A twisted emission geometry in non-central Pb+Pb collisions measurable via azimuthally sensitive HBT

## Key claim
The freeze-out emission zone in non-central Pb+Pb collisions exhibits a scale-dependent tilt (a "twist") in the reaction plane, where the tilt angle varies with the radial distance from the collision center; this twist is in principle measurable experimentally via momentum-differential azimuthally sensitive Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) correlations, providing access to the time-evolution of the source geometry.

## Method
The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) hadronic transport model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at a beam energy of 8 AGeV. They extracted the spatial pion freeze-out distribution and fitted it with a three-dimensional Gaussian ellipsoid parameterized by a tilt angle that depends on the radial distance from the collision center. To make the twist experimentally accessible, they performed a mock azimuthally sensitive HBT analysis on the same UrQMD events, subdividing momentum space into eight azimuthal bins and fitting correlation functions in separate momentum-difference shells to extract a scale-dependent tilt angle from the HBT radii.

## Key numbers
- The system studied is Pb+Pb at $E_{lab} = 8$ AGeV, impact parameter $b = 3.4 - 6.8$ fm, with pions selected at midrapidity $|y| < 0.5$ and transverse momentum $p_\perp < 400$ MeV.
- The spatial freeze-out distribution was analyzed using equidistant 2 fm rings in the x-z plane to extract the radius-dependent tilt angle $\theta_S(r)$.
- The tilt angle $\theta_S(r)$ is parameterized as $\theta_S(r) = \theta_0 + \theta_{Mag} \exp(-r^2 / (2\sigma_{twist}^2))$, where the parameters were obtained from a full three-dimensional fit to the UrQMD freeze-out distribution.
- The HBT analysis used eight $45^\circ$-wide bins in azimuthal angle $\phi$ and momentum-difference shells to extract $\theta_S(q)$, where $q$ is the pair momentum difference; the results show a qualitative similarity between $\theta_S(r)$ versus $r$ and $\theta_S(q)$ versus $1/q$.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of tilted emission zones in non-central heavy-ion collisions, which had been related to "antiflow" (Brachmann 1999, Lisa 2000) and a "third component" of flow (Csernai 1999, 2011). The authors build on previous azimuthally sensitive HBT methodology (Lisa 2000, Mount 2010) and on earlier UrQMD HBT results (Li 2007, 2008, 2012). The key advance is demonstrating that the scale-dependent twist, arising from the interplay between early-time spectator shadowing and later-time pressure-gradient-driven expansion, can be extracted experimentally via momentum-differential HBT, enabling new constraints on the space-time evolution of anisotropic structures in heavy-ion collisions and potentially improving understanding of directed flow ($v_1$) measurements.
