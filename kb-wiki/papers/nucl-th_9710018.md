# Liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei

> In a finite temperature Thomas-Fermi framework, we calculate density distributions of hot nuclei enclosed in a freeze-out volume of few times the normal nuclear volume and then construct the caloric curve, with and without inclusion of radial collective flow. In both cases, the calculated specific heats $C_v$ show a peaked structure signalling a liquid-gas phase transition. Without flow, the caloric curve indicates a continuous phase transition whereas with inclusion of flow, the transition is very sharp. In the latter case, the nucleus undergoes a shape change to a bubble from a diffuse sphere at the transition temperature.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei can be either continuous or first-order depending on the presence of collective radial flow, with the transition temperature shifting from approximately 10 MeV (without flow) to approximately 5 MeV (with flow energy of about 1.3 MeV per nucleon).

## Method
The authors used a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) framework with a Seyler-Blanchard type momentum- and density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate self-consistent density distributions of hot nuclei confined in a freeze-out volume of 8 times the normal nuclear volume. They constructed caloric curves (excitation energy versus temperature) and computed the specific heat at constant volume \(C_v\) both with and without the inclusion of collective radial flow, which was simulated by adding a negative external pressure \(P_0\) to the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{150}\)Sm without flow, the specific heat \(C_v\) shows a sharp peak at a temperature \(T \simeq 10\) MeV, above which \(C_v\) falls to the classical value of 3/2.
- For \(^{150}\)Sm with flow pressure \(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) (corresponding to an average flow energy per nucleon of \(\simeq 1.3\) MeV), the caloric curve shows a plateau at \(T \simeq 5\) MeV over an excitation energy range of 5–10 MeV.
- With flow at \(P_0 = -0.1\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), the density distribution undergoes a shape transition from a diffuse sphere to a bubble within a temperature interval of \(\Delta T \approx 0.1\) MeV (from T = 5.2 MeV to T = 5.3 MeV).
- For \(^{85}\)Kr with freeze-out volume 8 times its normal volume, the transition temperature is shifted up by \(\approx 0.5\) MeV compared to \(^{150}\)Sm, and the specific heat peak is broader.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of liquid-gas phase equilibrium in nuclei (BSSD, LB) and limiting temperatures (NAT) by providing a self-consistent calculation of density profiles and caloric curves in finite nuclei. It contrasts with the microcanonical multifragmentation calculations of Gross (GRO) and the canonical calculations of the Copenhagen school (BON), which showed peaked specific heats but did not allow detailed study of density changes at criticality. The results offer a qualitative explanation for the different caloric curves observed experimentally in Au+Au collisions at 600A MeV (POC) and Au+C collisions at 1A GeV (HAU), suggesting that the presence or absence of radial flow determines whether the phase transition appears first-order or continuous.
