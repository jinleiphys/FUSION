# Density Dependence of the Symmetry energy and the Equation of State of Isospin Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy in the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter is studied using the isoscaling of the fragment yields and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamic calculation. It is observed that the experimental data at low densities are consistent with the form of symmetry energy,E$_{sym}$ $\approx$ 31.6 ($ρ/ρ_{\circ})^{0.69}$, in close agreement with those predicted by the results of variational many-body calculation. A comparison of the present result with those reported recently using the NSCL-MSU data suggests that the heavy ion studies favor a dependence of the form, E$_{sym}$ $\approx$ 31.6 ($ρ/ρ_{\circ})^γ$, where $γ$ = 0.6 - 1.05. This constraints the form of the density dependence of the symmetry energy at higher densities, ruling out an extremely " stiff " and " soft " dependences.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the density dependence of the symmetry energy in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, extracted from isoscaling of fragment yields, follows the form E$_{sym}$ ≈ 31.6 (ρ/ρ₀)^γ with γ = 0.69 at low densities, but the abstract's quoted range γ = 0.6–1.05 reflects a model-dependent comparison with other heavy-ion data (NSCL-MSU isospin diffusion) that probes different density regions.

## Method
The authors measured fragment yields from collisions of ⁴⁰Ar, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁸Fe, and ⁵⁸Ni beams on ⁵⁸Fe and ⁵⁸Ni targets at beam energies from 25 to 53 MeV/nucleon at the Texas A&M K500 Superconducting Cyclotron. They extracted the isoscaling parameter α from the fragment yields and compared it to antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations using two forms of the symmetry energy: a "soft" Gogny interaction and a "stiff" Gogny-AS interaction. The slope of α versus the difference in fragment isospin asymmetry (Z/A)² was used to infer the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy at low densities is consistent with E$_{sym}$ ≈ 31.6 (ρ/ρ₀)^0.69, where 31.6 is in MeV and ρ₀ is normal nuclear density.
- The range of the exponent γ from combining the present isoscaling data with NSCL-MSU isospin diffusion data is γ = 0.6 – 1.05.
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear density is taken as about 31 MeV, consistent with the variational calculations of Akmal and Pandharipande (1997, 1998) which give E$_{sym}$(ρ₀) = 32 MeV and γ = 0.6.
- Secondary de-excitation effects on α are estimated at 10–15% from statistical model studies, and can be as large as 50% according to Ono et al. (2005).

## Context
This work extends the isoscaling approach of Ono et al. (2003) to experimental data, favoring a stiff density dependence (Gogny-AS interaction) over a soft one (Gogny interaction). It complements the isospin diffusion analysis of Chen et al. (2005) using the IBUU04 transport model, which found γ = 1.05 from NSCL-MSU data. The results are consistent with the variational many-body calculations of Akmal and Pandharipande (1997, 1998) and with the neutron star studies of Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (2000). The combined heavy-ion data constrain the symmetry energy at supranormal densities, ruling out both extremely stiff and extremely soft forms, which is relevant for neutron star structure and supernova dynamics.
