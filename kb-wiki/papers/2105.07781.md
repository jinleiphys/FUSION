# Shell-model description for the first-forbidden $β^-$ decay of $^{207}$Hg into the one-proton-hole nucleus $^{207}$Tl

> In this work, we have performed large-scale shell-model calculations for the first-forbidden $β^{-}$ decay of $^{207}$Hg into the one-proton-hole nucleus $^{207}$Tl corresponding to the recently available experimental data from ISOLDE-CERN [T. A. Berry et al., Phys. Rev. C 101, 054311 (2020)]. We have used the one-particle one-hole ($1p$-$1h$) truncation for both protons and neutrons simultaneously across the doubly-shell closure at $^{208}$Pb in the final states of $^{207}$Tl. In our calculations, we have also considered the effect of mesonic enhancement $ε_{\mathrm{mec}}=2.01\pm 0.05$ in the rank-0 for the axial-charge matrix element $γ_{5}$. Here, we have calculated the $\log ft$ values from the ground-state of $^{207}$Hg to the several excited states of $^{207}$Tl and obtained a good agreement between the calculated and the experimental data. In the experimental data spin and parity for some states are not yet confirmed, thus based on the shell-model results for the $\log ft$ values we have given the prediction for these states. This is the first theoretical calculation for the $\log ft$ values for these transitions.

## Key claim

The authors present the first theoretical shell-model calculation of log ft values for the first-forbidden beta-minus decay of 207Hg into 207Tl, achieving good agreement with experimental data from ISOLDE-CERN (Berry et al., 2020) only after incorporating a specific mesonic enhancement factor of epsilon_mec = 2.01 ± 0.05 and heavily quenched coupling constants (gA/gA_free = 0.38, gV/gV_free = 0.51) from prior work by Zhi et al. (2013).

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the KHH7B interaction within a model space covering Z=58-114 and N=100-164 around the 208Pb core, employing one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) truncation for both protons and neutrons simultaneously in the final states of 207Tl. They calculated one-body transition densities (OBTDs) using the OXBASH code and computed log ft values by integrating the shape factor over the electron energy spectrum, incorporating the mesonic enhancement factor in the rank-0 axial-charge matrix element gamma_5 for Delta J = 0 transitions. The calculations used the experimental Q value of 4.550 MeV from Wang (2017) and the quenching factors from Zhi et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- Mesonic enhancement factor: epsilon_mec = 2.01 ± 0.05, applied to the rank-0 axial-charge matrix element gamma_5
- Quenching factors adopted: gA/gA_free = 0.38 and gV/gV_free = 0.51, from Zhi et al. (2013)
- Experimental Q value for the 207Hg beta decay: 4.550 MeV, from Wang (2017)
- Calculated log ft for the 11/2- state at 1.348 MeV: 8.19, compared to experimental value 7.2(4)
- Calculated log ft for the 9/2_2- state at 2.927 MeV: 4.86, compared to experimental value 5.42(7)
- Calculated log ft for the 9/2_3- state at 3.222 MeV: 5.59, compared to experimental value 5.58(8)
- Calculated log ft for the 9/2_4- state at 3.304 MeV: 5.90, compared to experimental value 5.95(7)
- Calculated log ft for the 9/2_1- state at 2.756 MeV: 6.62, compared to experimental value 6.3(2)
- Calculated log ft for the 5/2_1- state at 3.264 MeV: 9.12, compared to experimental value >8.7
- Calculated log ft for the 5/2_2- state at 3.520 MeV: 9.86, compared to experimental value >9.5

## Context

This work extends the shell-model studies of first-forbidden beta decay in the lead region initiated by Warburton (1991), who extracted the mesonic enhancement factor epsilon_mec = 2.01 ± 0.05 from systematic studies of A=205-212 nuclei around 208Pb, and by Zhi et al. (2013), who calculated quenching factors for first-forbidden transitions at N=82 and 126. The calculations directly respond to the experimental data from Berry et al. (2020) at ISOLDE-CERN, which provided new beta feeding branching ratios and log ft values for 207Hg decay but lacked theoretical shell-model calculations for these transitions. The results enable future experiments to confirm spin and parity assignments for several unconfirmed states in 207Tl at excitation energies 3.800, 3.850, and 3.940 MeV, and suggest that further theoretical work with larger model spaces (such as KHM3Y interaction with 24 orbitals) is needed to better reproduce octupole collective states.
