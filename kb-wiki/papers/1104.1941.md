# Equilibration in the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach probed with the Wigner distribution function

> Calculating the Wigner distribution function in the reaction plane, we are able to probe the phase-space behavior in time-dependent Hartree-Fock during a heavy-ion collision. We compare the Wigner distribution function with the smoothed Husimi distribution function. Observables are defined to give a quantitative measure for local and global equilibration. We present different reaction scenarios by analyzing central and non-central $^{16}O+$$^{16}O$ and $^{96}Zr+$$^{132}Zn$ collisions. It is shown that the initial phase-space volumes of the fragments barely merge. The mean values of the observables are conserved in fusion reactions and indicate a "memory effect" in time-dependent Hartree-Fock. We observe strong dissipation but no evidence for complete equilibration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations show strong one-body dissipation in heavy-ion collisions but no evidence for complete equilibration, with the initial phase-space volumes of the fragments barely merging even at the compound stage.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional TDHF calculations using the Skyrme parametrization SkI3 on a Cartesian coordinate-space grid with 1 fm mesh spacing, solving the equations via a sixth-order Taylor-series expansion of the time-evolution operator with a time step of 0.2 fm/c. They computed the one-body Wigner distribution function from the TDHF density matrix and defined compact observables including the global eccentricity of the momentum distribution, the intrinsic kinetic energy (using Thomas-Fermi-Weizsäcker approximation), and the ratio of fourth to second moments of the local momentum distribution. Central and non-central collisions were analyzed for ¹⁶O+¹⁶O at various energies and for ⁹⁶Zr+¹³²Sn.

## Key numbers

- Central ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collision at E_c.m. = 100 MeV: maximum intrinsic kinetic energy E_int ≈ 86 MeV at the compound stage (t ≈ 75 fm/c), saturating at about half that value asymptotically
- ¹⁶O+¹⁶O non-central collisions at b = 2 fm with E_c.m. = 20 MeV and E_c.m. = 50 MeV: both cases produce fused compound states with oscillations persisting up to t = 4000 fm/c
- ⁹⁶Zr+¹³²Sn fusion at E_c.m. = 250 MeV and b = 2 fm: final eccentricity much smaller than for ¹⁶O+¹⁶O, with intrinsic energy showing two stages of growth (fast initial rise then slower growth up to 1000 fm/c)
- Mesh spacing: dx = dy = dz = 1 fm; time step: 0.2 fm/c
- Reference values for Gaussian momentum distribution ratio R^(n): R^(1) = 3, R^(2) = 2, R^(3) = 5/3

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of heavy-ion collisions (Bonche 1976, Maruhn 2006) by introducing the Wigner distribution function as a detailed phase-space probe of equilibration, complementing earlier global measures of dissipation such as comparing initial and final kinetic energies (Maruhn 2006). The analysis builds on the concept of single-particle dissipation (Swiatecki) and the window and wall pictures (Blocki, Randrup). The results enable future studies of thermalization in mean-field dynamics and provide benchmarks for comparing TDHF with approaches that include two-body correlations (Bertsch, Bonasera, Abe).
