# Two-proton radioactivity and three-body decay. III. Integral formulae for decay widths in a simplified semianalytical approach

> Three-body decays of resonant states are studied using integral formulae for decay widths. Theoretical approach with a simplified Hamiltonian allows semianalytical treatment of the problem. The model is applied to decays of the first excited $3/2^{-}$ state of $^{17}$Ne and the $3/2^{-}$ ground state of $^{45}$Fe. The convergence of three-body hyperspherical model calculations to the exact result for widths and energy distributions are studied. The theoretical results for $^{17}$Ne and $^{45}$Fe decays are updated and uncertainties of the derived values are discussed in detail. Correlations for the decay of $^{17}$Ne $3/2^-$ state are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper develops integral formulae for three-body decay widths that allow a semianalytical treatment using a simplified Hamiltonian, claiming that the "corrected" width obtained via the Green's function method should equal the width from the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method, but only under the condition that the HH convergence is fast in the internal region and the procedure is exact under the barrier.

## Method

The authors extend the two-body integral formalism for decay widths to the three-body case by introducing an auxiliary Hamiltonian with different short-range nuclear interactions, then generating a "corrected" continuum wave function using the three-body Green's function and a transition potential (the difference between the original and auxiliary potentials). They apply this simplified model to the first excited 3/2⁻ state of ¹⁷Ne and the 3/2⁻ ground state of ⁴⁵Fe, using Woods-Saxon three-body potentials with specific radii (5 fm for ¹⁷Ne, 6 fm for ⁴⁵Fe) and small diffuseness parameter 0.4 fm, studying both one final state interaction (OFSI) and two final state interaction (TFSI) approximations.

## Key numbers

- ¹⁷Ne 3/2⁻ state three-body energy: \(E_{3r} = 0.344\) MeV
- ⁴⁵Fe 3/2⁻ ground state three-body energy: \(E_{3r} = 1.154\) MeV
- Three-body potential radius for ¹⁷Ne: \(\rho_0 = 5\) fm
- Three-body potential radius for ⁴⁵Fe: \(\rho_0 = 6\) fm
- Three-body potential diffuseness parameter: \(a_\rho = 0.4\) fm
- Experimental position of the s-wave two-body resonance in ¹⁷Ne simplified model: \(E_{2r} = 0.535\) KeV

## Context

This work extends the integral formalism for width calculations previously applied to two-body decays (e.g., alpha-decay in Harada (1968) and Kadmensky (1971), and proton emission from deformed states in Bugrov (1989) and Davydov (1998)) to the three-body case, building on the authors' own three-body cluster model developed in Grigorenko (2000, 2001, 2003). The simplified semianalytical approach enables calibration of full three-body hyperspherical calculations and provides a framework for studying two-proton radioactivity in systems like ¹⁷Ne and ⁴⁵Fe, with potential applications to astrophysical two-proton capture rates relevant to the rp-process waiting points.
