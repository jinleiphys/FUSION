# Electric and magnetic response of hot QCD matter

> We study the electric conductivity as well as the magnetic response of hot QCD matter at various temperatures $T$ and chemical potentials $μ_q$ within the off-shell Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach for interacting partonic systems in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The response of the strongly-interacting system in equilibrium to an external electric field defines the electric conductivity $σ_0$ whereas the response to a moderate external magnetic field defines the induced diamagnetic moment $μ_L$ ($T, μ_q$) as well as the spin susceptibility $χ_S(T, μ_q)$. We find a sizeable temperature dependence of the dimensionless ratio $σ_0/T$ well in line with calculations in a relaxation time approach for $T_c \! < \! T < \! 2.5 \!\, T_c$ as well as an increase of $σ_0$ with $μ_q^2/T^2$. Furthermore, the frequency dependence of the electric conductivity $σ(Ω)$ shows a simple functional form well in line with results from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM). The spin susceptibility $χ_S(T,μ_q)$ is found to increase with temperature $T$ and to rise $\sim μ_q ^2/T^2$, too. The actual values for the magnetic response of the QGP in the temperature range below 250 MeV show that the QGP should respond diamagnetically in actual ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions since the maximal magnetic fields created in these collisions are smaller than $B_c(T)$ which defines a boundary between diamagnetism and paramagnetism.

## Key claim

The authors use the off-shell Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach to compute the electric conductivity and magnetic response of hot QCD matter, finding that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) responds diamagnetically in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions because the maximal magnetic fields created in those collisions are smaller than a temperature-dependent critical field \(B_c(T)\) that separates diamagnetic from paramagnetic behavior.

## Method

The authors simulate infinite partonic matter in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions (volume \(V = 9^3\) fm\(^3\)) using the PHSD transport approach, which propagates off-shell quasiparticles with broad spectral functions. An external electric field \(E_z\) is applied to extract the stationary electric conductivity \(\sigma_0(T,\mu_q)\) from the ratio of the equilibrium current density to the field strength; for periodic fields the frequency-dependent conductivity \(\sigma(\Omega)\) is obtained from the phase shift and amplitude of the oscillating current. The magnetic response is studied by applying a moderate external magnetic field and separately computing the diamagnetic contribution (from the induced angular momentum via the Lorentz force) and the paramagnetic contribution (from spin precession, with spin-flip probabilities chosen to enforce the equilibrium Boltzmann factor \(\exp(-q e B / E T)\)), yielding the spin susceptibility \(\chi_S\) and the critical field \(B_c(T)\).

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless ratio \(\sigma_0/T\) at \(\mu_q=0\) shows a minimum near the critical temperature \(T_c\) followed by an approximate linear rise up to \(2 T_c\), compatible with lattice QCD results from Aarts (2013) and others.
- For \(T = 200\) MeV, the electric conductivity increases with chemical potential as \(\sigma_0(T,\mu_q)/T = [\sigma_0(T,0)/T] (1 + a(T) \mu_q^2)\) with \(a(T) \approx 11.6\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\); the best-fit coefficient for the \(\mu_q^2/T^2\) term is \(c_{\sigma_0} \approx 0.46\) over \(170\ \text{MeV} \leq T \leq 250\ \text{MeV}\).
- For periodic electric fields at \(T = 190\) MeV, the current amplitude \(A(\Omega) = 1/\sqrt{1+\Omega^2/\Gamma_q^2}\) and phase shift \(\delta = \arctan(\Omega/\Gamma_q)\) follow the DQPM prediction; at the top RHIC energy the frequency \(\Omega \approx 22\ \text{c}/\text{fm}\) suppresses the conductivity by more than a factor of 100 relative to its equilibrium value.
- The diamagnetic energy shift \(\Delta E_{mag,L}(T)\) for \(\mu_q=0\) is fitted by \(\Delta E_{mag,L}(T) = 0.3 \cdot (T-96)^{2.82}\ \text{MeV}\) in the interval \(170\ \text{MeV} \leq T \leq 250\ \text{MeV}\).
- The spin susceptibility \(\chi_S(T)\) for \(\mu_q=0\) is fitted by \(\chi_S(T) = 0.017 - 2.39/T\) (with \(T\) in MeV) over the same temperature range.
- The critical magnetic field \(B_c(T)\) separating diamagnetic from paramagnetic response is given by \(B_c(T) = \sqrt{\Delta E_{mag,L} / (e^2 \chi_S V)}\); for temperatures below 250 MeV the QGP is diamagnetic because the maximal fields in heavy-ion collisions are smaller than \(B_c(T)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the electric conductivity \(\sigma_0(T)\) for infinite parton matter at zero chemical potential (Steinert and Cassing 2013; Marty 2013) to finite quark chemical potential \(\mu_q\), and adds a systematic calculation of the magnetic response. The results for \(\sigma_0/T\) are compared to lattice QCD calculations by Aarts (2013) and others, and the frequency-dependent conductivity is shown to match the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM). The magnetic susceptibility and critical field results provide input for understanding the Chiral Magnetic Effect in peripheral heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007; others) and for determining photon production from the QGP; the approach enables future studies of the electromagnetic response in non-equilibrium heavy-ion collision simulations.
