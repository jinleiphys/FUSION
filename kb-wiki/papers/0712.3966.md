# Influence of the hexadecapole deformation on the two neutrino double-$\be ta $ decay

> The two neutrino double beta $(β^{-}β^{-})_{2ν}$ decay of $ ^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd nuclei for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition is studied in the PHFB model in conjunction with the pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole plus hexadecapole-hexadecapole effective two-body interaction and the effect of the latter is investigated on the calculation of nuclear transition matrix elements $M_{2ν}$. The reliability of the intrinsic wave functions of parent and daughter nuclei involved in the $(β^{-}β^{-})_{2ν}$ decay of above mentioned nuclei is established by obtaining an overall agreement between a number of theoretically calculated spectroscopic properties, namely the yrast spectra, reduced $B(E2$:$0^{+}\to 2^{+})$ transition probabilities, static quadrupole moments $Q(2^{+})$ and $g$-factors $g(2^{+})$ and the available experimental data. The effect of deformation on $M_{2ν}$ is also investigated to inveterate its inverse relation with nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a hexadecapole-hexadecapole (HH) term to the pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (PQQ) effective two-body interaction in the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model does not significantly alter the calculated nuclear transition matrix elements (NTMEs) for two-neutrino double-beta decay, thereby establishing the stability of previous PHFB results against changes in the effective interaction, while also confirming an inverse correlation between nuclear deformation and the NTME \(M_{2\nu}\).

## Method

The authors employed the PHFB model with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole plus hexadecapole-hexadecapole (PQQHH) effective two-body interaction to calculate the NTMEs \(M_{2\nu}\) for the \(0^+ \to 0^+\) transition in nine nuclei: \(^{94,96}\)Zr, \(^{98,100}\)Mo, \(^{104}\)Ru, \(^{110}\)Pd, \(^{128,130}\)Te, and \(^{150}\)Nd. The reliability of the intrinsic HFB wave functions was validated by comparing calculated spectroscopic properties (yrast spectra, \(B(E2:0^+ \to 2^+)\) transition probabilities, static quadrupole moments \(Q(2^+)\), and \(g\)-factors \(g(2^+)\)) with experimental data. The summation method was used to evaluate \(M_{2\nu}\) without explicit summation over intermediate \(1^+\) states, and the strength of the QQHH interaction was varied to study the effect of deformation on the NTMEs.

## Key numbers

- Phase space factors \(G_{2\nu}\) for \(g_A = 1.25\) range from \(9.709 \times 10^{-29}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (for \(^{104}\)Ru) to \(1.189 \times 10^{-16}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (for \(^{150}\)Nd).
- For \(^{94}\)Zr, the calculated half-life in the PHFB model is \(1.086 \times 10^{23}\) yr for \(g_A = 1.25\) and \(2.652 \times 10^{23}\) yr for \(g_A = 1.0\); the experimental limit is \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu} > 1.1 \times 10^{17}\) yr (Arnold et al., 1999).
- The extracted NTMEs \(M_{2\nu}\) from experimental half-lives vary from \(0.0152 \pm 0.0008\) (for \(^{130}\)Te) to \(0.1222 \pm 0.0034\) (for \(^{100}\)Mo) for \(g_A = 1.25\), and from \(0.0238 \pm 0.0013\) to \(0.1909 \pm 0.0053\) for \(g_A = 1.00\).
- The pairing strengths used are \(G_p = 30/A\) MeV and \(G_n = 20/A\) MeV for most nuclei, with exceptions: \(G_n = 18/A\) MeV for \(^{94}\)Zr, \(G_n = 22/A\) MeV for \(^{96}\)Zr, and \(G_p = G_n = 35/A\) MeV for \(^{128,130}\)Te, \(^{128,130}\)Xe, \(^{150}\)Nd, and \(^{150}\)Sm.
- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strengths are \(\chi_{2pp} = \chi_{2nn} = 0.0105\) MeV b\(^{-4}\), and the hexadecapole-hexadecapole strength is related by \(\chi_4 = 0.4884 \chi_2 A^{-2/3} b^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends previous PHFB model calculations by Chandra et al. (2005) and Singh et al. (2007) that used only the PQQ interaction, by adding a hexadecapole-hexadecapole term to test the stability of the results. The study confirms the inverse correlation between nuclear deformation and \(M_{2\nu}\) predicted earlier by Auerbach et al. (1993) and Troltenier et al. (1996), and aligns with deformed QRPA approaches by Alvarez-Rodriguez et al. (2004) and Pacearescu et al. (2004). The validated wave functions can be used for future calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, which are needed to extract lepton-number-violating parameters from experimental half-life limits.
