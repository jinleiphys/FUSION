# α-clustering effects in dissipative 12C+12C reactions at 95 MeV

> Dissipative 12C+12C reactions at 95 MeV are fully detected in charge with the GARFIELD and RCo apparatuses at LNL. A comparison to a dedicated Hauser-Feshbach calculation allows to select events which correspond, to a large extent, to the statistical evaporation of highly excited 24Mg, as well as to extract information on the isotopic distribution of the evaporation residues in coincidence with their complete evaporation chain. Residual deviations from a statistical behaviour are observed in αyields and attributed to the persistence of cluster correlations well above the 24Mg threshold for 6 α's decay.

## Key claim
Residual deviations from statistical Hauser-Feshbach predictions are observed in alpha-particle yields from dissipative 12C+12C reactions at 95 MeV, specifically an excess of (2α,16O*) events where the oxygen residue is excited above its neutron emission threshold. This excess is attributed to the persistence of cluster correlations in the 24Mg compound nucleus well above its threshold for breakup into six alpha particles.

## Method
Dissipative 12C+12C reactions at 95 MeV were measured using the GARFIELD and Ring-Counter (RCo) detectors at LNL, providing nearly 4π coverage and full charge identification of reaction products. Events with total detected charge Z_det=12 were selected and compared to a dedicated Monte Carlo Hauser-Feshbach calculation for the statistical evaporation of 24Mg at E*_fus=61.4 MeV, with transmission coefficients and level densities optimized for the A≈20 region. The comparison focused on energy spectra, branching ratios, and Q-value correlations to isolate deviations from statistical behavior.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 95 MeV for 12C+12C reactions
- Compound nucleus excitation energy: E*_fus = 61.4 MeV for complete fusion of 24Mg
- Maximum angular momentum assumed for fused system: J0_max = 12 ħ
- Experimental percentage of (2α, A O) events in the Q> region (low dissipation): (37±5)%, compared to (9±1)% from model predictions
- Experimental percentage of dissipative decays with α and oxygen fragment absorbed by (2α,16O*) channels: (63±5)%, compared to (10±1)% from theoretical predictions
- Q-value bands observed in (E_res, ΣE_α) plane: Q_kin = (1.27±1.40) MeV and (-5.35±1.70) MeV
- Energy resolution for triple coincidence measurements: ΔE/E ≲ 4%

## Context
This work extends studies of α-clustering in light nuclei, following theoretical predictions of cluster correlations from ab-initio calculations and experimental identification of rotational bands consistent with α-cluster structures (Freer, various years). It specifically addresses whether cluster correlations observed in resonant 12C+12C reactions at lower energies (Takashina, various years; Bremner, various years) persist in more dissipative channels typically associated with compound nucleus formation. The results enable future discrimination between two hypotheses, failure of Hauser-Feshbach theory for highly excited 24Mg versus breakdown of the compound nucleus decoupling assumption, through new data on 14N+10B at 80 MeV currently under analysis.
