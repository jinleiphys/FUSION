# Canonical transformation path to gauge theories of gravity II --- Spacetime coupling of spin-0 and spin-1 particle fields

> The generic form of spacetime dynamics as a classical gauge field theory has recently been derived, based on only the action principle and on the Principle of General Relativity. It was thus shown that Einstein's General Relativity is the special case where (i) the Hilbert Lagrangian (essentially the Ricci scalar) is supposed to describe the dynamics of the "free" (uncoupled) gravitational field, and (ii) the energy-momentum tensor is that of scalar fields representing real or complex structureless (spin-$0$) particles. It followed that all other source fields---such as vector fields representing massive and non-massive spin-$1$ particles---need careful scrutiny of the appropriate source tensor. This is the subject of our actual paper: we discuss in detail the coupling of the gravitational field with (i) a massive complex scalar field, (ii) a massive real vector field, and (iii) a massless vector field. We show that different couplings emerge for massive and non-massive vector fields. The \emph{massive} vector field has the \emph{canonical} energy-momentum tensor as the appropriate source term---which embraces also the energy density furnished by the internal spin. In this case, the vector fields are shown to generate a torsion of spacetime. In contrast, the system of a \emph{massless} and charged vector field is associated with the \emph{metric} (Hilbert) energy-momentum tensor due to its additional $\mathrm{U}(1)$ symmetry. Moreover, such vector fields do not generate a torsion of spacetime. The respective sources of gravitation apply for all models of the dynamics of the `free' (uncoupled) gravitational field---which do not follow from the gauge formalism but must be specified based on separate physical reasoning.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the canonical gauge theory of gravity uniquely determines the appropriate energy-momentum tensor for different source fields: massive vector fields require the canonical energy-momentum tensor as the source term and generate spacetime torsion, while massless vector fields with U(1) symmetry use the metric (Hilbert) energy-momentum tensor and do not generate torsion, with these distinctions holding for any model of the free gravitational field dynamics.

## Method

The authors apply a covariant canonical gauge formalism based on extended canonical transformations to derive field equations for scalar and vector fields coupled to a dynamical spacetime geometry with metric and connection as independent variables. They restrict to metric compatibility and analyze three specific systems: a complex Klein-Gordon scalar field, a massive Proca vector field, and a massless vector field with U(1) symmetry, deriving the appropriate source tensors from the consistency conditions of the field equations.

## Context

This work extends the canonical gauge theory of gravity developed by Struckmeier et al. (2017) from scalar fields to vector fields, showing that Einstein's General Relativity emerges only for scalar fields with the Hilbert Lagrangian. The paper contradicts the common practice of using the Belinfante-Rosenfeld symmetrization (Belinfante, 1939; Rosenfeld, 1940) to replace canonical energy-momentum tensors with metric ones, arguing this is not admissible for massive vector fields. The results enable reinvestigation of compact astrophysical objects like neutron stars and binary neutron star mergers using the correct canonical energy-momentum terms for vector repulsion effective field theory, with implications for white dwarfs, neutron stars, and ultra-high energy cosmic ray events to be published in a forthcoming paper.
