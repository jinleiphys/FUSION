# Anomalous thresholds in $B\to (P,V)γ^*$ form factors

> We study the effects of anomalous thresholds on the non-local form factors describing the hadronization of the light-quark contribution to $B\to(P,V)γ^*$ transitions. Starting from a comprehensive discussion of anomalous thresholds in the triangle loop function for different mass configurations, we detail how the dispersion relation for $ππ$ intermediate states is affected by contour deformations mandated by the anomalous branch points. Phenomenological estimates of the size of the anomalous contributions to the form factors are provided with couplings determined from measured branching fractions and Dalitz plot distributions. Our key finding is that anomalous effects are suppressed on the $ρ(770)$ resonance, while off-peak the effects can become as large as $\mathcal{O}(10\%)$ of the full (light-quark-loop induced) non-local form factors. We comment on future generalizations towards higher intermediate states and the charm loop, outlining how the dispersive framework established in this work could help improve the non-local form factors needed as input for a robust interpretation of $B\to (P,V)\ell^+\ell^-$ decays.

## Key claim

Anomalous thresholds in the triangle loop function for \(B\to (P,V)\gamma^*\) form factors are suppressed on the \(\rho(770)\) resonance, but off-peak the effects can become as large as \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\) of the full light-quark-loop induced non-local form factors, contradicting the common assumption that such effects might significantly distort the dispersive analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive analytic study of anomalous thresholds in the scalar triangle loop function \(C_0(t)\) using Landau equations and the Coleman-Norton theorem, classifying all possible mass configurations relevant for \(B\)-meson decays. They then implemented a dispersive framework for the light-quark contribution to \(B\to (P,V)\gamma^*\) form factors, decomposing the non-local matrix elements into Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) structures and solving the unitarity relations using Muskhelishvili-Omnes (MO) techniques with contour deformations to account for anomalous branch cuts. Phenomenological estimates were obtained by determining couplings from measured branching fractions and Dalitz plot distributions.

## Key numbers

- The triangle singularity \(t_+\) for the \(B^\pm \to K^{*\pm}\gamma^*\) configuration with \(\pi\pi\) intermediate states lies at \(-57.8\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case A), \(0.48-4.19i\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case B), or \(18.6\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case C), depending on the mass configuration.
- For \(B^\pm \to \rho^\pm\gamma^*\) with \(\pi\pi\) intermediate states, \(t_+\) lies at \(-859\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case A), \(0.73-4.81i\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case B), or \(26.4\ \text{GeV}^2\) (Case C).
- The two-particle threshold for the triangle diagram is \(t_\text{thr} = (m_2+m_3)^2\), and the pseudothreshold is \(t_\text{ps} = (\sqrt{p_1^2} - \sqrt{p_3^2})^2\).
- Anomalous effects are suppressed on the \(\rho(770)\) resonance but can reach \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\) of the full light-quark-loop induced non-local form factors off-peak.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach for \(B\to (P,V)\gamma^*\) form factors developed by Bobeth et al. (2017), Gubernari et al. (2020, 2022, 2023), which previously did not include anomalous threshold effects. It addresses concerns raised by Ciuchini et al. (2022) and Ladisa et al. (2022) about possible distortion of analytic structure by anomalous thresholds. The framework enables future generalizations to higher intermediate states, isoscalar contributions, \(B_s\) decays, and the charm loop, potentially improving the non-local form factors needed for robust interpretation of \(B\to (P,V)\ell^+\ell^-\) decays.
