# Study the exotic $Θ^+$ with polarized photon beams

> We carry out an analysis of the pentaquark $Θ^+$ photoproduction with polarized photon beams. Kinematical and dynamical aspects are examined for the purpose of determining $Θ^+$'s spin and parity. It shows that the polarized photon beam asymmetry in association with certain dynamical properties of the production mechanism would provide further information on its quantum numbers. Facilities at SPring-8, JLab, ELSA, and ESRF will have access to them.

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarized photon beam asymmetries in $\Theta^+$ photoproduction can provide additional information to determine the pentaquark's spin and parity, but the interpretation depends on the unknown dynamical properties of the production mechanism and the lack of knowledge about the $\Theta^+$ form factor and total width.

## Method

The author analyzes the photoproduction reaction $\gamma n \to K^- \Theta^+$ using effective Lagrangians for three possible $\Theta^+$ spin-parity configurations ($1/2^-$, $1/2^+$, and $3/2^+$) and derives the density matrix elements for $\Theta^+$ decay into $K^+ n$. The polarized beam asymmetries are then computed in the Born approximation limit, with the $\Theta^+$ magnetic moment estimated from the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model, and compared for the different quantum number assignments.

## Key numbers

- The $\Theta^+$ mass is 1.54 GeV and its width is less than 25 MeV, as reported by experimental collaborations (SPring-8, DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR).
- The Skyrme model predicts a $\Theta^+$ mass of 1.53 GeV with a narrow width of about 9 MeV.
- For the $1/2^+$ configuration, the coupling constant $g_{\Theta NK}=2.96$ is adopted for $\Gamma_{\Theta^+\to K^+ n}=10$ MeV, with a range of $g_{\Theta NK}=2.09$ to 4.68 for widths from 5 to 25 MeV.
- The $\Theta^+$ magnetic moment in the Jaffe-Wilczek picture is estimated as $\mu_\theta \simeq 0.094 (e_0/2M_\Theta)$ with $a^2=b^2=1/2$, corresponding to an anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa_\theta \simeq -0.87$.
- Differential cross sections are presented at center-of-mass energies $W=2.1$ and 2.5 GeV, showing forward peaking behavior.

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts to determine the $\Theta^+$ quantum numbers following its discovery by the SPring-8, DIANA, CLAS, and SAPHIR collaborations (2003). It contrasts the Skyrme model prediction of $J^P=1/2^+$ from Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) with quark model predictions of $1/2^-$ from Stancu and Riska (2003) and Jaffe and Wilczek (2003), as well as QCD sum rule and lattice QCD calculations favoring $1/2^-$. The analysis builds on prior photoproduction studies by Nam, Oh, and Kim (2003) and Liu and Ko (2003), and provides a framework for experimental tests at SPring-8, JLab, ELSA, and ESRF.
