# Nuclear Fragmentation at Intermediate Energies in the DCM-QGSM-SMM Model

> The development of nucleus-nucleus interaction models is a rapidly developing area of heavy-ion physics. Recently, a new model DCM-QGSM-SMM, developed at JINR and oriented toward use within the NICA project at energies of several GeV/nucleon, became available. However, the mechanisms of nuclear interactions used in this model can potentially operate effectively at lower energies. In this paper, the model predictions are compared with the FRAGM and FIRST/GSI experimental data in the energy range of nucleus-nucleus interactions starting from 300 MeV/nucleon, as well as with the predictions of other models used in this energy range.

# Nuclear Fragmentation at Intermediate Energies in the DCM-QGSM-SMM Model

## Key claim
The DCM-QGSM-SMM model, originally developed for energies above several GeV/nucleon for the NICA project, can describe nuclear fragmentation data down to 300 MeV/nucleon with accuracy comparable to models specifically designed for intermediate energies, though it systematically shifts fragmentation peaks toward higher momenta and underestimates their widths for heavier fragments.

## Method
The authors compared predictions of four models (DCM-QGSM, DCM-QGSM-SMM, Binary Cascade, and Liege Intranuclear Cascade) against experimental double-differential cross sections from the FRAGM setup at TWAC for carbon fragmentation on beryllium at 300 and 600 MeV/nucleon, integrated yields from 300 to 3200 MeV/nucleon, charged pion yields at 3.2 GeV/nucleon, and angular distributions from the FIRST/GSI experiment for carbon on gold at 400 MeV/nucleon. The DCM-QGSM-SMM model combines the Dubna intranuclear cascade with the quark-gluon string model for high-energy binary collisions and the statistical multifragmentation model for decay of thermalized residual nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Energy range tested: 300 to 3200 MeV/nucleon for carbon fragmentation on beryllium target
- Fragment momentum spectra measured with step of 50-100 MeV/c in the magneto-optical channel
- At 300 and 600 MeV/nucleon, double-differential cross sections d²σ/(dpdΩ) measured for fragments from protons to ⁷Be
- Proton background suppression of approximately 10³ achieved using Cherenkov counter for pion identification at 3.2 GeV/nucleon
- Angular range for FIRST/GSI data: 0.2° to 5° for carbon fragmentation on gold target at 400 MeV/nucleon
- Fragment detection limited by maximum rigidity of magneto-optical channel of 6 GeV/c at energies above 1 GeV/nucleon
- DCM-QGSM-SMM underestimates yields of ²H and ³He at 950 MeV/nucleon
- BC model shows best agreement with π⁻ momentum spectrum; DCM-QGSM-SMM describes spectrum up to 2.5 GeV/c
- Models predict Coulomb effect maximum in π⁻/π⁺ ratio near 600 MeV/c for 3.2 GeV/nucleon carbon fragmentation

## Context
This work extends testing of the DCM-QGSM-SMM model, developed at JINR for the NICA project (BM@N and MPD facilities), into the intermediate energy regime where the Binary Cascade and Liege Intranuclear Cascade models have been previously validated, particularly for carbon fragmentation. The comparison with FRAGM data partially extends an earlier comparison at 300 MeV/nucleon by adding ⁶Li and ⁷Be fragments and the DCM-QGSM model. The Coulomb effect in pion ratios, first observed at Bevalac for ²⁰Ne and ⁴⁰Ar at 400-500 MeV/nucleon and later at SPS for Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV/c, is predicted by all models for carbon at 3.2 GeV/nucleon but lacks experimental verification. The results enable further testing of these models with heavier fragments and nuclei to evaluate their generality.
