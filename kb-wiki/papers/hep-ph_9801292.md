# Nuclear Shadowing Effects on Prompt Photons at RHIC and LHC

> The transverse momentum distribution of prompt photons coming from the very early phase of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions for the RHIC and LHC energies is calculated by means of perturbative QCD. We calculate the single photon cross section ($A+B\to γ+X$) by taking into account the partonic sub processes $q+ \bar{q}\to γ+g$ and $q+g\toγ+q$ as well as the Bremsstrahlung corrections to those processes. We choose a lower momentum cut-off $k_0 =2$ GeV separating the soft physics from perturbative QCD. We compare the results for those primary collision with the photons produced in reactions of the thermalized secondary particles, which are calculated within scaling hydrodynamics. The QCD processes are taken in leading order. Nuclear shadowing corrections, which alter the involved nuclear structure functions are explicitly taken into account and compared to unshadowed results. Employing the GRV parton distribution parametrizations we find that at RHIC prompt QCD-photons dominate over the thermal radiation down to transverse momenta $k_T \approx 2$ GeV. At LHC, however, thermal radiation from the QGP dominates for photon transverse momenta $k_T \leq 5$ GeV, if nuclear shadowing effects on prompt photon production are taken into account.

# Nuclear Shadowing Effects on Prompt Photons at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

Prompt QCD photons dominate over thermal radiation from the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC down to transverse momenta of approximately 2 GeV, but at LHC thermal radiation dominates for photon transverse momenta up to 5 GeV *if* nuclear shadowing corrections to the parton distribution functions are taken into account; without shadowing, the crossover point occurs at approximately 3.75 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed the inclusive single-photon cross section for nucleus-nucleus collisions at midrapidity using leading-order perturbative QCD, including the partonic subprocesses q+anti-q → γ+g and q+g → γ+q, plus Bremsstrahlung corrections up to O(α_s²) simulated via a K-factor. Nuclear parton distributions were obtained by multiplying the GRV proton parton distributions with a parametrized shadowing ratio R_F₂(x,Q²) at the input scale Q₀=2 GeV, using the nuclear overlap function T_AA(b=0) to convert nucleon-nucleon cross sections to central nucleus-nucleus multiplicities. Thermal photon spectra were calculated within a three-dimensional scaling hydrodynamic model with two sets of initial conditions (rapid thermalization with high initial temperature, and slower thermalization with lower initial temperature) and a first-order phase transition described by the MIT bag model.

## Key numbers

- Lower momentum cut-off separating soft physics from perturbative QCD: k₀ = 2 GeV
- RHIC center-of-mass energy: √s = 200 AGeV; LHC center-of-mass energy: √s = 5.5 ATeV
- Nuclear overlap function for Pb+Pb: T_PbPb = 32/mb; for Au+Au: T_AuAu = 29/mb
- RHIC hydrodynamic initial conditions (rapid thermalization): T_i = 533 MeV, τ_i = 0.1236 fm/c, resulting dN^π/dy ≈ 1460
- LHC hydrodynamic initial conditions (rapid thermalization): T_i = 880 MeV, τ_i = 0.1 fm/c, resulting dN^π/dy ≈ 5300
- RHIC slower thermalization: T_i = 300 MeV, τ_i = 0.5 fm/c, dN^π/dy ≈ 1050
- LHC slower thermalization: T_i = 650 MeV, τ_i = 0.25 fm/c, dN^π/dy ≈ 5350
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 100 MeV
- K-factor: K = 1.5 for RHIC, K = 1.0 for LHC
- Nuclear shadowing correction at LHC yields suppression up to a factor of approximately 3.5
- Inverse slope of prompt QCD photons: approximately 500-1000 MeV at RHIC and 500-1100 MeV at LHC in the interval k_T = 2-5 GeV
- Inverse slope of thermal photons: at most 500 MeV at RHIC and 700 MeV at LHC
- Crossover point with shadowing at LHC: k_T ≈ 5 GeV; without shadowing: k_T ≈ 3.75 GeV
- Bremsstrahlung dominates over QCD Compton process by a factor of approximately 1.6[ln(1/x_T) - 1.1]

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of prompt photon production in heavy-ion collisions by Alam et al. (using Duke/Owens set I parton distributions) and by Eskola et al. (minijet calculations with shadowing), finding that the choice of parton distributions significantly affects the relative importance of Bremsstrahlung versus Compton contributions. The results establish that at LHC energies, nuclear shadowing corrections are essential for correctly predicting the background from initial nucleon-nucleon collisions against which thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma must be identified, particularly in the transverse momentum range 2-5 GeV where the QGP signal is expected to be most accessible.
