# Study of $β^- $ decays for sd model space with Shell model

> This paper investigates the beta decay of nuclei within the sd model space, encompassing the $0d_{3/2}$, $0d_{5/2}$, and $1s_{1/2}$ shells. We comprehensively analyze their decay characteristics, including the half-life, $logft$ value, Q value, branching ratio, B(GT) value, M(GT), and R(GT) value. These results are compared with experimental data from Z = 8-19 nuclei, employing the nuclear shell model framework. The calculations are performed using the USDB\cite{usdb} interaction, which is well-suited for nuclei with active valence nucleons in the $0d_{3/2}$, $0d_{5/2}$, and $1s_{1/2}$ shells. The study offers valuable insights into the beta decay properties of these nuclei and contributes to our understanding of the nuclear shell structure.}

## Key claim

The paper presents shell-model calculations of beta-decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei with proton number Z = 8-17 and neutron number N >= 11 within the sd model space, claiming that the USDB interaction provides a reasonable description of experimental data, though the abstract does not highlight that many theoretical predictions for branching ratios and log ft values show significant deviations from experiment, particularly for weaker decay branches and for nuclei near the neutron drip line where no experimental data exist.

## Method

The author performed nuclear shell-model calculations using the USDB effective interaction within the sd model space (0d3/2, 0d5/2, and 1s1/2 shells) using the NuShellX computational tool. For each parent nucleus, the theoretical beta-decay Q value was computed from the difference in shell-model binding energies plus Coulomb energies between parent and daughter, and partial half-lives were calculated from the ft-value formula using g_A = 1.260, with total half-lives obtained by summing over all allowed transitions to states within the Q-window. The calculated energy levels, branching ratios, log ft values, and B(GT) values were tabulated for each transition and compared with available experimental data from the literature.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of beta decay in the sd shell by using the USDB interaction of Richter et al. (2008) to systematically calculate decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei from Z=8 to Z=17, including several nuclei near the neutron drip line (such as 24O, 26F, 28Ne, 29Ne, 30Ne, 31Na) that had not been previously studied in terms of their beta-decay properties. The calculations enable future experimental investigations of these exotic nuclei by providing theoretical predictions for decay energies, branching ratios, and Gamow-Teller strength distributions, and they allow comparison with charge-exchange reaction data through the isospin symmetry relation between beta decay and (p,n) or (3He,t) reactions.
