# Lattice QCD at finite T and μ

> Recent results of lattice QCD at finite temperature and density are reviewed. At vanishing density the transition temperature, the equation of state and hadron properties are discussed both for the pure gauge theory and for dynamical staggered, Wilson and overlap fermions. The second part deals with finite density. There are recent results for full QCD at finite temperature and moderate density, while at larger densities QCD-like models are studied.

# Lattice QCD at finite T and μ

## Key claim

The paper reviews lattice QCD results at finite temperature and density, arguing that while continuum-extrapolated predictions for full QCD are becoming close to reality at vanishing density, results at finite chemical potential remain restricted to moderate densities (μ ≲ T) due to the sign problem of the fermion determinant, with larger densities only accessible via QCD-like models.

## Method

The review surveys lattice QCD simulations using multiple fermion discretizations (staggered, Wilson, overlap) and improved actions, comparing results for the transition temperature, equation of state, and phase diagram. At finite chemical potential, three approaches are described: multiparameter reweighting in β, m, and μ; Taylor expansion of the fermion determinant ratio in powers of μ; and analytic continuation from simulations at imaginary chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Quenched critical temperature: T_c/√σ ≈ 0.630–0.650 (continuum extrapolated, pure SU(3) gauge theory)
- Full QCD transition temperature: T_c ≈ 150 MeV (two flavors) and 170 MeV (three flavors) on N_t=4 lattices with p4 staggered fermions in the chiral limit
- CP-PACS Wilson fermion result: T_c = 171(4) MeV (chirally extrapolated, N_t=4)
- MILC collaboration result with ASQTAD action: T_c ≈ 180 MeV on 16³×8 lattices (2+1 flavors)
- Critical endpoint location (N_f=2+1 staggered, N_t=4): T_E = 160 ± 3.5 MeV, μ_B_E = 725 ± 35 MeV
- Critical pseudoscalar mass for N_f=3: m_PSc ≈ 290 MeV (standard staggered) and m_PSc ≈ 67 MeV (improved p4 fermions), both on N_t=4
- Phase diagram curvature coefficients: a₂ = -0.0056 (N_f=2), -0.011 (N_f=4), -0.0068 (N_f=3) from imaginary μ simulations
- Critical mass dependence for N_f=3: m_c(μ)/m_c(0) = 1 + 0.84(36)(μ/πT)²
- Overlap measure scaling: μ_{1/2} ∝ V^{-γ} with γ = 0.2–0.3 from 6³×4 to 12³×4 lattices

## Context

This review summarizes the state of lattice QCD thermodynamics circa 2003, extending earlier quenched results by Boyd (1996) and Beinlich (1997) to dynamical fermion simulations with staggered, Wilson, and overlap actions. The finite-density methods described, multiparameter reweighting by Fodor and Katz (2001), Taylor expansion by Allton (2002), and imaginary μ simulations by de Forcrand and D'Elia (2002), enabled the first lattice determinations of the QCD phase diagram at nonzero chemical potential, including the critical endpoint. The review also discusses QCD-like models (two-color QCD, isospin chemical potential, NJL model) as tools for studying the high-density, low-temperature region inaccessible to direct lattice simulations, and identifies the need for continuum extrapolations and larger volumes as key open issues.
