# Recent developments in nuclear structure theory: an outlook on the muonic atom program

> The discovery of the proton-radius puzzle and the subsequent deuteron-radius puzzle is fueling an on-going debate on possible explanations for the difference in the observed radii obtained from muonic atoms and from electron-nucleus systems. Atomic nuclei have a complex internal structure that must be taken into account when analyzing experimental spectroscopic results. Ab initio nuclear structure theory provided the so far most precise estimates of important corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic atoms and is well poised to also investigate nuclear structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic atoms. Independently on whether the puzzle is due to beyond-the-standard-model physics or not, nuclear structure corrections are a necessary theoretical input to any experimental extraction of electric and magnetic radii from precise muonic atom measurements. Here, we review the status of the calculations performed by the TRIUMF-Hebrew University group, focusing on the deuteron, and discuss preliminary results on magnetic sum rules calculated with two-body currents at next-to-leading order. Two-body currents will be an important ingredient in future calculations of nuclear structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic atoms.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic atoms, focusing on the deuteron, and presents preliminary calculations of magnetic sum rules with two-body currents at next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral effective field theory; the authors note that while the effect of two-body currents on the Lamb shift is small (a 20% enhancement of a 0.007 meV term), these currents are expected to be more important for the hyperfine splitting in muonic atoms.

## Method

The authors use chiral effective field theory and phenomenological potentials (AV18, N3LO chiral potential) combined with few-body calculational tools (harmonic oscillator basis diagonalization) to compute nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift and magnetic sum rules in the deuteron. For the magnetic sum rules, they implement the magnetic dipole operator at leading order (one-body) and at next-to-leading order (including seagull and pion-in-flight two-body currents), then compute the sum rules \(m_{-1}\) and \(m_0\) of the magnetic response function.

## Key numbers

- For muonic deuterium, the total two-photon exchange correction to the Lamb shift is \(\delta_{\rm TPE} = -1.727(20)\) meV, with nuclear Zemach term \(-0.424(3)\) meV and nuclear polarization term \(-1.245(19)\) meV.
- For muonic \(^3\)He\(^+\), \(\delta_{\rm TPE} = -15.46(39)\) meV; for muonic \(^4\)He\(^+\), \(\delta_{\rm TPE} = -9.58(38)\) meV.
- The experimental error in the Lamb shift measurement for \(\mu\)-D is 3.4 \(\mu\)eV, while the theoretical error in the TPE calculation is 20 \(\mu\)eV (ratio \(\approx 6\)).
- For the deuteron magnetic sum rules with the AV18 potential and one-body operator: \(m_{-1} = 13.9\) fm\(^3\), \(m_0 = 0.245\) fm\(^2\).
- With the N3LO chiral potential, adding NLO two-body currents changes the sum rules from LO values of \(m_{-1} = 14.0\) fm\(^3\) and \(m_0 = 0.245\) fm\(^2\) to LO+NLO values of \(m_{-1} = 15.1\) fm\(^3\) and \(m_0 = 0.277\) fm\(^2\) (effects of 5% and 11%, respectively).
- The magnetic contribution to the Lamb shift in the deuteron increases from 0.007 meV (LO) to 0.009 meV (LO+NLO), a 20% enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the TRIUMF-Hebrew University group's previous calculations of two-photon exchange corrections for muonic deuterium (Hernandez 2014), muonic \(^3\)He\(^+\) and \(^3\)H (Nevo 2016), and muonic \(^4\)He\(^+\) (Ji 2013, Nevo 2014), which used chiral effective field theory and phenomenological potentials. The results have been used by the CREMA collaboration to extract charge radii from muonic atom measurements, revealing the deuteron-radius puzzle (a 7\(\sigma\) deviation from CODATA-2010 and 3.5\(\sigma\) from ordinary deuterium spectroscopy). The new magnetic sum rule calculations with two-body currents prepare the way for future calculations of nuclear structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic atoms, which are planned for \(\mu\)-D and \(\mu\)-\(^3\)He\(^+\).
