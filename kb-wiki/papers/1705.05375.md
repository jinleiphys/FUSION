# The Energy Distribution of Subjets and the Jet Shape

> We present a framework that describes the energy distribution of subjets of radius $r$ within a jet of radius $R$. We consider both an inclusive sample of subjets as well as subjets centered around a predetermined axis, from which the jet shape can be obtained. For $r \ll R$ we factorize the physics at angular scales $r$ and $R$ to resum the logarithms of $r/R$. For central subjets, we consider both the standard jet axis and the winner-take-all axis, which involve double and single logarithms of $r/R$, respectively. All relevant one-loop matching coefficients are given, and an inconsistency in some previous results for cone jets is resolved. Our results for the standard jet shape differ from previous calculations at next-to-leading logarithmic order, because we account for the recoil of the standard jet axis due to soft radiation. Numerical results are presented for an inclusive subjet sample for $pp \to {\rm jet}+X$ at next-to-leading order plus leading logarithmic order.

## Key claim

The paper presents a factorization framework for the energy distribution of subjets within a jet, enabling resummation of logarithms of the ratio of subjet radius to jet radius (\(r/R\)), but the specific resummation structure and logarithmic order depend crucially on whether the subjet axis is the standard jet axis (double logarithms) or the winner-take-all axis (single logarithms), and the authors note that their results for the standard jet shape differ from previous calculations at next-to-leading logarithmic order because they account for the recoil of the standard jet axis due to soft radiation.

## Method

The authors develop a factorization theorem in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the cross section of inclusive jet production \(pp \to \text{jet}+X\), factorizing the dynamics at the jet scale \(R\) and subjet scale \(r\) into hard functions, parton distribution functions, and subjet functions. For \(r \ll R\), they further refactorize the subjet function onto semi-inclusive jet functions for the subjet, enabling DGLAP-type resummation of logarithms of \(r/R\). They calculate all relevant one-loop matching coefficients for both the anti-\(k_T\) and cone jet algorithms, and resolve an inconsistency in previous cone jet results by correcting the phase-space boundaries for two-parton configurations.

## Key numbers

* The semi-inclusive jet function for cone jets is corrected by an additional term \(\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} 2[P_{qq}(z)+P_{gq}(z)][\theta(z>1/2)\ln z + \theta(z<1/2)\ln(1-z)]\) for quarks, and an analogous term for gluons, resolving a momentum sum rule violation.
* The one-loop quark subjet function for anti-\(k_T\) jets contains a term \(\delta(1-z) L_{r/R} [P_{qq}(z_r)+P_{gq}(z_r)]\) where \(L_{r/R} = \ln(4\mu^2/(\omega_R^2 r^2)) - \ln(4\mu^2/(\omega_R^2 R^2))\).
* The one-loop gluon subjet function for anti-\(k_T\) jets contains a term \(\delta(1-z) L_{r/R} [P_{gg}(z_r)+2n_f P_{qg}(z_r)]\).
* The cone-in-cone quark subjet function includes a constant term \(\delta(1-z)\delta(1-z_r) C_F(7/2 + 3\ln 2 - \pi^2/3)\) at one loop.
* For the cone-in-anti-\(k_T\) quark subjet function, when \(r > R/2\), there is an additional term \(\delta(1-z)\delta(1-z_r) C_F [-\frac12 L_{r/R}^2 + \frac32 L_{r/R} - 2L_{r/R}\ln(1-r/R) + 4\text{Li}_2(1-r/R) + 1/2 - 2\pi^2/3 + 6r/R]\).

## Context

This work extends the framework for jet substructure observables introduced by Dai et al. (2016) for the subjet energy distribution, and connects to both the standard jet shape studied by Ellis et al. (1992), Seymour (1997), Li et al. (2011), and Chien et al. (2014), as well as the jet fragmentation function studied by Procura et al. (2009), Jain et al. (2011), and others. The corrected cone jet results resolve an inconsistency in earlier calculations by Aversa et al. (1988, 1989), Jager et al. (2004), Kaufmann et al. (2015), and Kang et al. (2016). The framework enables resummation of logarithms of the jet radius \(R\) and the ratio \(r/R\) for subjet observables, and provides a theoretical basis for jet substructure studies in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions, as well as for boosted object tagging in searches for beyond Standard Model physics.
