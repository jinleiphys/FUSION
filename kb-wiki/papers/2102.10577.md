# Self-consistent calculations of electron-capture decays in Z=118, 119, and 120 superheavy isotopes

> Weak decays in superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z = 118 - 120 and neutron numbers N = 175 - 184 are studied within a microscopic formalism based on deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations with pairing correlations. The half-lives of beta+ decay and electron capture are compared with alpha-decay half-lives obtained from phenomenological formulas. The sensitivity of the half-lives to the unknown Q-energies is studied by comparing the results obtained from different approaches for the masses. It is shown that alpha-decay is always dominant in this mass region. The competition between alpha and beta+/EC decay modes is studied in seven alpha-decay chains starting at different isotopes of Z=118, 119, and 120.

## Key claim

Alpha-decay is always dominant over beta+/electron-capture decay in superheavy nuclei with Z=118-120 and N=175-184, with beta+/EC half-lives systematically three to seven orders of magnitude longer than alpha-decay half-lives, except possibly at the ends of some alpha-decay chains where the ratio approaches unity.

## Method

The authors performed deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing correlations (using the SLy4 interaction) to obtain single-particle energies, wave functions, and occupation probabilities for superheavy isotopes. Beta+/EC half-lives were then calculated using a proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) approach, summing allowed Gamow-Teller transition strengths weighted by phase-space factors. Alpha-decay half-lives were obtained from five different phenomenological formulas (PS, Royer, VS1, VS2, DZR) using Q-alpha values from seven different mass models, and the competition between decay modes was studied in seven alpha-decay chains.

## Key numbers

- Q_EC energies for Z=118-120 isotopes are typically distributed around 2 MeV, with values ranging from approximately 0.16 MeV (for 271Sg) to 6.15 MeV (for 295120)
- For the chain starting at 295Og: T_alpha = 7.1x10^-3 s for 295Og, T_beta/EC = 2.3x10^3 s, ratio R = 3.2x10^5
- For the chain starting at 295120: T_alpha = 1.7x10^-5 s for 295120, T_beta/EC = 4.6x10^2 s, ratio R = 2.7x10^7
- For the chain starting at 298120: T_alpha = 2.0x10^-5 s for 298120, T_beta/EC = 2.0x10^2 s, ratio R = 9.8x10^6
- At the end of some chains, ratios approach unity: 267Db has R = 2.2x10^-1, 263Lr has R = 1.6x10^0
- Uncertainties from Q_alpha energies can vary T_alpha predictions by up to seven orders of magnitude
- Beta+/EC half-lives from deformed prolate shapes (beta_2 approx 0.5) are much lower than from spherical ground states

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Sarriguren 2019, 2020) on weak decays in superheavy nuclei, using the same pnQRPA formalism based on deformed Skyrme HF+BCS calculations. It addresses the competition between alpha and beta+/EC decay modes in the region of Z=118-120, which are the next elements targeted for discovery at laboratories such as GSI and JINR-Dubna. The results contradict earlier phenomenological approaches (Karpov 2012, Zagrebaev 2012) that used constant nuclear matrix elements, and instead show that nuclear structure effects, particularly deformation and parity selection rules, are crucial for determining beta+/EC half-lives. The work enables experimental planning by identifying which nuclei in alpha-decay chains might show branching between alpha and beta+/EC decay modes.
