# Relativistic Spin Magnetohydrodynamics

> Starting from kinetic theory description of massive spin-1/2 particles in presence of magnetic field, equations for relativistic dissipative non-resistive magnetohydrodynamics are obtained in the small polarization limit. We use a relaxation time approximation for the collision kernel in the relativistic Boltzmann equation and calculate non-equilibrium corrections to the phase-space distribution function of spin-polarizable particles. We demonstrate that our framework naturally leads to emergence of the well known Einstein-de Hass and Barnett effects. We obtain multiple transport coefficients and show, for the first time, that the coupling between spin and magnetic field appear at gradient order in hydrodynamic equations.

# Relativistic Spin Magnetohydrodynamics

## Key claim
The authors derive, for the first time, a relativistic dissipative non-resistive magnetohydrodynamic framework for spin-1/2 particles in the small polarization limit, showing that the coupling between spin polarization and magnetic field appears at first order in gradients of hydrodynamic quantities. The framework naturally reproduces the Einstein-de Haas and Barnett effects, but the conversion of spin polarization to thermal vorticity occurs only through coupling with the electromagnetic field.

## Method
Starting from the relativistic Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel, the authors compute non-equilibrium corrections to the phase-space distribution function of massive spin-1/2 particles in the presence of a magnetic field, keeping terms up to linear order in the spin polarization tensor. They take moments of the Boltzmann equation to obtain constitutive relations for the dissipative particle diffusion, bulk viscous pressure, shear viscous tensor, and spin current, and identify which gradient terms are dissipative by requiring positive entropy production via the H-theorem.

## Key numbers
- Spin-1/2 particles: the Casimir operator eigenvalue for the spin vector length is spin^2 = 3/4 (from the definition s^2 = 1/2 * (1 + 1/2) = 3/4)
- The equilibrium magnetization tensor M^{mu nu}_{eq} is linear in the spin polarization tensor omega^{mu nu}, taking the form M^{mu nu}_{eq} = a_1 omega^{mu nu} + a_2 u^{[mu} u_gamma omega^{nu]gamma}
- The dissipative transport coefficients are expressed as: zeta = -tau_eq * beta_{Pi Pi}, kappa^{mu alpha} = tau_eq * beta_{nn}^{<mu>alpha}, eta^{mu nu alpha beta} = tau_eq * beta_{pi pi}^{<mu nu>alpha beta}, and Sigma^{lambda mu nu alpha beta gamma} = tau_eq * B_Sigma^{lambda,[mu nu]alpha beta gamma}

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous formulations of dissipative spin-hydrodynamics (Bhadury 2020) and relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (Florkowski 2018) into a unified framework. It builds on the kinetic theory approach to spin using the classical analogue of particle spin (Florkowski 2018) and the relaxation-time approximation (Anderson 1974). The framework enables future simulations of heavy-ion collisions that may explain the observed difference in Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization at low collision energies (STAR 2017), which cannot be explained by global angular momentum alone and may require coupling between magnetic field and intrinsic magnetic moments.
