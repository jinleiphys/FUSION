# Delocalization of the axial charge in the chiral limit

> The nucleon's axial vector charge, g_A, becomes delocalized in the chiral limit. When m_π= 0, and SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R is exact, 1/3 of the nucleon's axial charge is to be found at infinite distance from the nucleon. For finite m_πthis result is approached smoothly as m_π-> 0. We illustrate this effect by considering the lepton-proton spin-spin interaction arising from Z^0 exchange as a function of m_π. Delocalization may have implications for lattice calculations of g_A and in nuclei.

## Key claim

When the pion mass is exactly zero and SU(2)L × SU(2)R is exact, exactly 1/3 of the nucleon's axial vector charge gA resides at infinite distance from the nucleon; for finite but small mπ this contribution is found at distances of order 1/mπ, so that any finite-volume calculation (including lattice simulations) will capture only 2/3 of the full gA.

## Method

The author analyzes the spatial distribution of the nucleon's axial current matrix element by separating it into a short-range contribution inside a sphere of radius R (much larger than the QCD scale) and a long-range contribution from the pion field outside that sphere. Using the Goldberger-Treiman relation and the explicit form of the pion pole contribution to the induced pseudoscalar form factor, the Fourier transform of the current is evaluated in the limit q→0, and the surface term at radius R is shown to contribute exactly gA/3. The analysis is then repeated for nonzero mπ, and the result is illustrated by computing the effective s-wave spin-spin interaction from Z0 exchange in a hydrogenic atom for two regimes: (a) mπ ≫ α mℓ and (b) α mℓ ≫ mπ.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit (mπ = 0), exactly 1/3 of the nucleon's axial charge gA is found at infinite distance from the nucleon; the remaining 2/3 is at typical hadronic distances.
- For finite mπ, the long-range contribution to gA from outside radius R is (1/3)(gπN fπ / M)(1 + mπ R) e^{-mπ R}, which approaches gA/3 as mπ → 0 for any fixed R.
- The radial density of axial charge from the pion tail at large r is dgA/dr = (1/3)(gπN fπ / M) mπ² r e^{-mπ r}.
- In the hydrogenic gedanken experiment, when mπ aℓ → 0 (pion range much larger than the atom), the effective spin-spin interaction is proportional to (2/3) gA; when mπ aℓ → ∞ (pion range much smaller than the atom), the full gA is recovered.

## Context

This work extends earlier observations in chiral quark models (Jaffe, 1979 Erice School) and in the Skyrme model (Adkins, Nappi and Witten, 1983; Cohen and Broniowski, 1986) by showing that the delocalization of the axial charge is a general consequence of chiral symmetry and the pion pole, independent of the short-distance structure. The result directly addresses the long-standing puzzle that lattice QCD calculations of gA extrapolated to the chiral limit consistently yield values below the physical gA ≈ 1.26 (Fukugita et al., 1995; Dong et al., 1995; Liu et al., 1994; Gockeler et al., 1996; Capitani et al., 1999; Gusken et al., 1999). It also suggests that medium-dependent modifications of gA in nuclei may arise from this delocalization rather than from partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The paper enables refined lattice extrapolation strategies (Cohen, 2001) and clarifies why the tensor charge gT and twist-two pseudotensor operators are not subject to the same subtlety.
