# Exploration of the phase diagram and the thermodynamic properties of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential with the PNJL effective model

> The QCD transition from a hadronic to a quark-gluon plasma phase is a cross-over at vanishing/small baryo-chemical potential, while at higher chemical potentials it is argued that it becomes of first order, ending with a critical end-point. The present goal is the determination of the critical line and, possibly, the recognition of the critical endpoint. For this purpose, the effective model (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov Loop) with 2+1 flavours is used. In Mean Field Approximation it is possible to obtain all thermodynamic quantities (pressure, entropy, energy and quark density) and their fluctuations, in particular, the generalized quark-number susceptibilities. We use the net baryon-number fluctuations and net-strangeness fluctuations to identify the critical line and the order of the phase transition in the chemical potential - temperature plane.

# Exploration of the phase diagram and the thermodynamic properties of QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential with the PNJL effective model

## Key claim
The PNJL effective model in Mean Field Approximation can locate the critical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram by using net baryon-number fluctuations and net strangeness fluctuations, with the critical endpoint found at a baryo-chemical potential of 862 MeV and temperature of 122 MeV, though the model shows deconfinement occurring at a lower temperature than the chiral transition, indicating it is only a semi-quantitative guide.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop (PNJL) in 2+1 flavors, solved in Mean Field Approximation to obtain the thermodynamic potential. From this potential they derive all thermodynamic quantities (pressure, entropy, energy, quark density) and their fluctuations, specifically the generalized quark-number susceptibilities (cumulants of conserved charges). They compute the kurtosis (kappa sigma^2 = chi_4/chi_2) and skewness (gamma sigma^3/M = chi_3/chi_1) of net baryon-number and net strangeness fluctuations as functions of temperature and baryo-chemical potential under three different scenarios for the chemical potentials: symmetric chemical potential (mu_u = mu_d = mu_s = mu_B/3), quasi-neutral strangeness (mu_s = 0), and heavy ion collision conditions (n_Q/n_B = 0.4, n_s = 0).

## Key numbers
- Critical endpoint located at mu_B = 862 MeV and T = 122 MeV, where fluctuations show an essential discontinuity
- Kurtosis (kappa sigma^2) at zero chemical potential approaches 1 at low temperature and 1/9 above the transition, consistent with baryon charge carried by baryons (charge 1) versus quarks (charge 1/3)
- Kurtosis and skewness computed at mu_B = 540 MeV, 810 MeV, 862 MeV, and 900 MeV as functions of temperature
- Kurtosis and skewness computed as functions of baryo-chemical potential at T = 142.9 MeV, 132.9 MeV, and 122.9 MeV

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD simulations (Lattice, year not specified) which provide results at zero or small chemical potential but cannot easily access the high chemical potential region relevant for heavy ion collisions and neutron star physics. The PNJL model approach builds on the NJL model (Kl, year not specified) and the Polyakov loop potential (Ratti, year not specified), and the fluctuation analysis follows the review by Asakawa (year not specified) on using conserved charge fluctuations as probes of microscopic degrees of freedom. The results enable future study of phenomenological quantities for heavy ion collisions such as speed of sound, heat capacity, and fluctuations of conserved charges.
