# Canonical form of Hamiltonian matrices

> On the basis of shell model simulations, it is conjectured that the Lanczos construction at fixed quantum numbers defines---within fluctuations and behaviour very near the origin---smooth canonical matrices whose forms depend on the rank of the Hamiltonian, dimensionality of the vector space, and second and third moments. A framework emerges that amounts to a general Anderson model capable of dealing with ground state properties and strength functions. The smooth forms imply binomial level densities. A simplified approach to canonical thermodynamics is proposed.

# Canonical form of Hamiltonian matrices

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that Lanczos tridiagonal matrices for nuclear shell-model Hamiltonians at fixed quantum numbers converge to smooth canonical forms (depending only on dimensionality, second and third moments, and interaction rank) within fluctuations and except for behavior very near the origin, rather than requiring the full exact diagonalization.

## Method

The Lanczos algorithm is applied to shell-model Hamiltonians at fixed angular momentum J, using a pivot chosen to exhaust sum rules for transition operators (e.g., Gamow-Teller strength). The resulting tridiagonal matrix elements are compared to analytic forms: constant or logarithmic diagonals and inverse binomial off-diagonals, with parameters determined by matching the second and third moments of the Hamiltonian. Exact diagonalizations for ⁴⁸Ca and (fpgd)⁸ configurations serve as the data for validating the conjectured forms.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca (J=4): dimensionality d=1755, N=10.78, σ=7.85 MeV, γ₁=-0.01
- For (fpgd)⁸ (J=6): dimensionality d=6579, N=12.68, σ'=2.14 MeV, σ=2.18 MeV, γ'₁=-0.246, γ₁=-0.296
- For ⁴⁸Sc Gamow-Teller strength in the pf shell: the JT=13 space has dimension d=8590, requiring 700 Lanczos iterations for exact S(E)
- The number of iterations needed to reach the smooth canonical lines is approximately N/2 iterations

## Context

This work extends the Two Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) or Embedded GOE (EGOE) approach by providing explicit analytic forms for Lanczos matrix elements, going beyond Wigner's Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) which applies only to full-rank Hamiltonians. The canonical forms imply binomial level densities at fixed quantum numbers, which the authors contrast with Bethe's formula from Bohr and Mottelson (1969). The framework enables simplified canonical thermodynamics via Eq. (4) for the partition function, and suggests that no low-energy thermal phase transitions occur in nuclei based on the binomial behavior of level densities. The authors note that the centroid and variance as functions of J deviate from Bethe's expression, requiring further study.
