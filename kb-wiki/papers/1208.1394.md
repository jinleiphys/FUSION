# Direct T-Matrix Approach to Determination of the Electric Polarizabilities of Quantum Bound Systems

> A new general formalism for determining the electric multipole polarizabilities of quantum (atomic and nuclear) bound systems based on the use of the transition matrix in momentum space has been developed. As distinct from the conventional approach with the application of the spectral expansion of the total Green's function, our approach does not require preliminary determination of the entire unperturbated spectrum; instead, it makes possible to calculate the polarizability of a few-body bound complex directly based on solving integral equations for the wave function of the ground bound state and the transition matrix at negative energy, both of them being real functions of momenta. A formula for the multipole polarizabilities of a two-body bound complex formed by a central interaction potential has been derived and studied. To test, the developed $T$-matrix formalism has been applied to the calculation of the dipole, quadrupole and octupole polarizabilities of the hydrogen atom.

## Key claim
The paper develops a direct T-matrix approach (DTMA) for calculating electric multipole polarizabilities of quantum bound systems that avoids the need to compute the entire unperturbed spectrum, instead requiring only the ground-state wave function and the transition matrix at negative energy; the method is tested on the hydrogen atom and reproduces the exact known polarizabilities, but the author notes that for three-body nuclei like 3He, consistent calculations using rigorous Faddeev formalism have not yet been performed, leaving a discrepancy between direct experimental and sum-rule-derived values unresolved.

## Method
The formalism expresses the electric multipole polarizability of an N-particle bound complex in terms of the ground-state wave function and the "truncated" Green's operator at the bound-state energy, which is rewritten using the free propagator and the transition operator T(E) via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. For a two-body bound complex with central interaction, the polarizability formula reduces to an expression involving derivatives of the ground-state wave function and the partial component of the transition matrix with orbital angular momentum l = λ, which satisfies an integral equation. The method is applied to the hydrogen atom by inserting analytical partial components of the Coulomb transition matrix into the derived formula and separating the result into free propagation, single-scattering (Born), and multiple-scattering contributions.

- Electric octupole polarizability of hydrogen: α_{E3}(H) = 131.250000 a.u., compared to exact value 525/4 = 131.25 a.u.

## Context
This work extends the Dalgarno-Lewis method (Dalgarno and Lewis, 1955) for calculating polarizabilities without spectral summation, but replaces the differential equation approach with integral equations for the transition matrix, making it applicable to few-body nuclear systems where the full spectrum is inaccessible. The author notes a discrepancy between the direct experimental result for the electric dipole polarizability of 3He (from scattering on 208Pb) and the value deduced from photoabsorption cross-section sum rules, and suggests that the new formalism, combined with Faddeev equations, could resolve this issue. The approach enables calculation of polarizabilities for systems like the deuteron, triton, and lambda hypertriton, building on prior work by the author (Kharchenko, 2011, 2012) using separable interactions.
