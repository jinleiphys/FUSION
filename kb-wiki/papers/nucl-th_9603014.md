# Low-energy M1 strength in the Li-7(p,gamma_0)Be-8 reaction

> The Li-7(p,gamma_0)Be-8 reaction is studied in a microscopic cluster model. All relevant subsystem properties are well reproduced. The calculated astrophysical S factor is in good agreement with the experimental data, although some M1 strength is missing in the 1+;1 resonance region. There is no contribution from the 1+;0 state. We estimate an M1 strength of 3.5% at 80 keV. By assuming the experimental resonance parameters and channel spin ratio for the first 1+ state that M1 strength is 6.3%, in good agreement with an R matrix analysis. The large (20%) M1 strength determined from a transition matrix element analysis may be caused by assuming a vanishing 0p3/2 spectroscopic factor, which is not supported by shell model calculations.

# Low-energy M1 strength in the Li-7(p,gamma_0)Be-8 reaction

## Key claim

The calculated astrophysical S factor from a microscopic cluster model agrees well with experimental data, but some M1 strength is missing in the 1+;1 resonance region; the authors estimate an M1 strength of 3.5% at 80 keV, which rises to 6.3% when assuming experimental resonance parameters and a channel spin ratio of 3.2 for I=2/I=1, contradicting the 20% M1 strength previously reported from a transition matrix element analysis.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic multi-cluster Resonating Group Method (RGM) approach to the eight-nucleon system, including He-4+He-3+proton, He-4+He-3+neutron, and alpha+alpha configurations, with the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction. They calculated E1, E2, and M1 cross sections perturbatively for capture to the Be-8 ground state, using variational methods for bound states and Kohn-Hulthén variational method for scattering states, with the exchange mixture parameter of the NN interaction decreased by 0.4% to reproduce the 386 keV 1+;1 resonance.

## Key numbers

- M1 strength at 80 keV: 3.5% from the microscopic model
- M1 strength at 80 keV: 6.3% when assuming experimental resonance parameters and I=2/I=1 channel spin ratio of 3.2
- First 1+ resonance: E_cm = 386 keV, width = 13 keV (model) compared to experimental width = 11 keV
- Second 1+ (T=0) state: E_cm = 1050 keV, width = 342 keV in the model (experimental: 897 keV, 138 keV)
- Spectroscopic amplitudes for 1+;1 state (Zheng interaction): S_I=1 = 0.3499, S_I=2 = 0.2774
- Spectroscopic amplitudes for 1+;1 state (Cohen-Kurath interaction): S_I=1 = 0.2644, S_I=2 = 0.3910
- (LS) component weights in Be-8: (1,0) = 83.4%, (0,1) = 0.03%, (1,1) = 13.3%, (2,1) = 3.2%

## Context

This work extends the microscopic cluster model previously applied to Be-7(p,gamma)B-8 (Csoto, 1993; Csoto et al., 1995) to the Li-7(p,gamma_0)Be-8 reaction, addressing discrepancies between the 20% M1 strength reported by Chasteler et al. (1994) from a transition matrix element analysis and the 5-6% M1 strength from Barker's (1995) R-matrix analysis. The results support the smaller M1 strength and suggest that the larger value arose from assuming a vanishing 0p3/2 spectroscopic factor, which is not supported by shell model calculations using the Zheng et al. (1995) interaction. The work enables more reliable extrapolation of the Be-7(p,gamma)B-8 S factor for solar neutrino physics, as the analogous reaction in Be-8 shows no evidence for large low-energy M1 strength.
