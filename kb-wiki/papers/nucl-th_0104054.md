# Elliptic flow at collider energies and cascade string models: The role of hard processes and multi-Pomeron exchanges

> Centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum dependence of hadron elliptic flow is studied in Au+Au collisions at BNL RHIC energies within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The QGSM predictions coincide well with the experimental data at $\sqrt{s}=130$ AGeV. Further investigations reveal that multi-Pomeron exchanges and hard gluon-gluon scattering in primary collisions, accompanied by the rescattering of hadrons in spatially anisotropic system, are the key processes needed for an adequate description of the data. These processes become essentially important for heavy-ion collisions at full RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon string model (QGSM) can quantitatively reproduce experimental elliptic flow data at RHIC energies, but only when multi-Pomeron exchanges and hard gluon-gluon scattering in primary collisions are included alongside rescattering of hadrons; without these processes the model cannot describe the rise of elliptic flow for peripheral events and the magnitude of the flow cannot exceed 3.5%.

## Method

The authors employed the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), which is based on Gribov-Regge theory, to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies. The model includes subprocesses with colour exchange connected to one or more Pomeron exchanges, hard gluon-gluon scattering with large momentum transfer, and semihard processes with quark and gluon scattering, with the inelastic hadron-hadron cross section calculated via the eikonal formalism using the Abramovskii-Gribov-Kancheli (AGK) cutting rules. The cascade (rescattering of secondaries) is introduced, and the model allows valence quarks and diquarks to interact promptly while newly produced hadrons can scatter only after a formation time related to string breaking.

## Key numbers

- Mean elliptic flow of charged particles at midrapidity ($|\eta| < 1.3$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ AGeV: $v_2^{ch} = 4.16 \pm 0.5\%$, compared to the STAR Collaboration measured value of $4.5 \pm 0.4\%$.
- Predicted elliptic flow of charged particles at midrapidity ($|\eta| < 1.3$) at full RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV: $v_2^{ch} = 4.9 \pm 0.5\%$.
- Without many-string processes, the elliptic flow at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV cannot exceed the limit of 3-3.5%.
- Elliptic flow saturates at impact parameter $b \approx 8$ fm for both energies.
- Elliptic flow saturates at transverse momentum $p_t \geq 1.8$ GeV/$c$, in agreement with experimental results.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flow in the QGSM at AGS and SPS energies (Am. 1991, Br. 1995, flow) to RHIC energies, and addresses the discrepancy where microscopic transport models like UrQMD (urqmd) and RQMD (Sor. 1999) predicted too weak elliptic flow while hydrodynamic models overpredicted it by 20-50% (ellST00). The results enable further comparison between string-based and hydrodynamic approaches to distinguish signals of quark-gluon plasma formation, and show that microscopic models remain viable for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
