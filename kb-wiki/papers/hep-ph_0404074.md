# Color Superconductivity of QCD at High Baryon Density

> At sufficiently high baryon density, a quark matter is expected to become a color superconductor because of the pairing forces mediated by gluons. The theoretical aspect of this novel phase of the strong interaction is reviewed with the emphasis on the perturbation theory of QCD at high chemical potential and low temperature. The derivation of the scaling formula of the superconducting transition temperature at weak coupling is explained in detail. The Ginzburg-Landau theory of the color superconductivity is also discussed.

## Key claim

At sufficiently high baryon density, quark matter becomes a color superconductor due to gluon-mediated pairing forces; the perturbative treatment is rigorously valid only at chemical potentials much higher than the QCD scale, a condition that may not be realized in nature, but these results serve as a proof of existence for the color superconducting phase.

## Method

The paper reviews the perturbative QCD approach at high chemical potential and low temperature, deriving the scaling formula for the superconducting transition temperature by analyzing the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the proper four-quark vertex function, decomposed into partial waves and color channels. The analysis incorporates hard dense loop resummation of the gluon propagator to regulate infrared divergences, and solves the resulting integral equation for the largest eigenvalue of the kernel to determine the pairing instability temperature. The Ginzburg-Landau theory of color superconductivity is also discussed.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement phase transition from lattice QCD occurs at \(T\simeq 150\) MeV for small chemical potential.
- The running coupling constant at high density is given by \(g = \frac{24\pi^2}{(11N_c - 2N_f)\ln(\mu/\Lambda_{\rm QCD})}\) with \(\mu \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).
- The non-BCS exponent in the transition temperature formula is \(\kappa = \sqrt{\frac{6N_c}{N_c+1}}\pi^2\).
- The pre-exponential factor in the transition temperature formula includes \(c = 1024\sqrt{2}\pi^3 N_f^{-5/2}\), \(c' = 2e^\gamma\) (with \(\gamma\) the Euler constant), and \(c'' = \exp\left[-\frac{1}{16}(\pi^2+4)(N_c-1)\right]\).
- The transition temperature scales as \(k_B T_{\rm pair}^{(J)} = c c' c'' c_J \frac{\mu}{g^5} e^{-\kappa/g} [1+O(g\ln g)]\).
- For two-flavor pairing, the ratio \(\Delta_0 / (k_B T_c) = \pi e^{-\gamma}\); for color-flavor locking with \(N_c = N_f = 3\), \(\Delta_0 / (k_B T_c) = 2^{-1/3} \pi e^{-\gamma}\).
- The Debye mass is \(m_D^2 \simeq \frac{N_f g^2 \mu^2}{2\pi^2}\).

## Context

This review extends the perturbative QCD treatment of color superconductivity initiated by earlier works such as Bailin and Love (1984) and developed by groups including Son (1999), Schafer and Wilczek (1999), and Pisarski and Rischke (1999). The paper explains the derivation of the non-BCS scaling formula for the transition temperature, which differs fundamentally from conventional BCS theory due to the long-range magnetic gluon interactions. The results enable first-principle calculations of color superconducting properties at asymptotically high densities, though the moderate density regime relevant for neutron star interiors remains inaccessible to perturbative methods and requires effective models such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approaches.
