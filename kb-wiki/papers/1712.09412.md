# Semigroup approach to the sign problem in quantum Monte Carlo simulations

> We propose a framework based on the concept of the semigroup to understand the fermion sign problem. By using properties of contraction semigroups, we obtain sufficient conditions for quantum lattice fermion models to be sign-problem-free. Many previous results can be considered as special cases of our new results. As a direct application of our new results, we construct a class of sign-problem-free fermion lattice models, which cannot be understood by previous frameworks. This framework also provides an interesting aspect in understanding related quantum many-body systems. We establish a series of inequalities for all the sign-problem-free fermion lattice models that satisfy our sufficient conditions.

## Key claim

The paper provides sufficient conditions for fermion lattice models to be sign-problem-free in auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) simulations, based on the mathematical structure of contraction semigroups; the key hedge is that these conditions are sufficient but not necessary, and the framework requires that the coefficient matrices satisfy both a real-structure condition and a contraction inequality involving two anticommuting real orthogonal matrices.

## Method

The author expresses the AFQMC sampling weight as a trace of products of exponentials of fermionic quadratic forms in the Majorana fermion basis, which forms a semigroup under multiplication. By requiring that the skew-symmetric coefficient matrices \(A_i\) satisfy an antilinear symmetry condition \(J_1^T A J_1 = \bar{A}\) and a contraction condition \(i(J_2 A - \bar{A} J_2) \leq 0\) with anticommuting real orthogonal matrices \(J_1\) and \(J_2\), the author proves that the sampling weight is real and non-negative. The framework unifies previous sign-problem-free results as special cases and constructs new models by allowing parameters to live in the full semigroup rather than only its maximal subgroup.

## Key numbers

* The sampling weight for each configuration of auxiliary fields is given by \(p = \mathrm{tr}(e^{h_1} e^{h_2} \cdots e^{h_k}) = \det(I + e^{A_1} e^{A_2} \cdots e^{A_k})^{1/2}\), where \(h_i = \gamma^T A_i \gamma / 4\) and \(A_i\) are \(2N \times 2N\) skew-symmetric complex matrices.
* The contraction condition requires \(i(J_2 A - \bar{A} J_2) \leq 0\), with \(J_2\) a skew-symmetric real orthogonal matrix satisfying \(J_2^2 = -I_{2N}\).
* The anticommutation relation \(\{J_1, J_2\} = 0\) is required for the invariant cone to contain strict contraction elements; the cone is trivial when \([J_1, J_2] = 0\).
* For the new class of models with skew-symmetric \(J_1\), the pairing term satisfies \(R \geq 0\) and \(S \geq 0\) for Hermitian matrices \(R\) and \(S\) in the complex fermion basis.
* An example model on a square lattice includes parameters \(t\), \(t_\perp\), \(U \geq 0\), and complex \(\Delta\) for \(d\)-wave BCS pairing.

## Context

This work extends and unifies previous frameworks for sign-problem-free fermion models, including those based on Kramers time-reversal invariance (Wu 2005), the fermion bag approach (Chandrasekharan 2010), the split orthogonal group (Wang 2015), Majorana reflection positivity (Wei 2016), and Majorana time-reversal symmetries (Li 2016). The semigroup approach shows that all these prior results correspond to the maximal subgroup of the contraction semigroup, while the new construction allows parameters in the full semigroup, enabling sign-problem-free simulations of models with pairing terms that could not be treated before. This framework enables the study of proximity effects between superconductivity and antiferromagnetism in correlated topological systems, and establishes a series of trace inequalities for expectation values of observables.
