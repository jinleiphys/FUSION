# The Kaon Form Factor in the Light-Cone Quark Model

> The electromagnetic form factor of the kaon meson is calculated in the light-cone formalism of the relativistic constituent quark model. The calculated $K^+$ form factor is consistent with almost all of the available experimental data at low energy scale, while other properties of kaon could also be interrelated in this representation with reasonable parameters. Predictions of the form factors for the charged and neutral kaons at higher energy scale are also given, and we find non-zero $K^0$ form factor at $Q^2 \ne 0$ due to the mass difference between the strange and down quarks inside $K^0$.

## Key claim

The light-cone constituent quark model, with parameters fixed by the weak decay constant and charged mean square radii, reproduces the available low-energy $K^+$ form factor data and predicts a non-zero $K^0$ form factor at $Q^2 \ne 0$ due to the mass difference between the strange and down quarks; the authors state the model should be considered valid only for $Q^2 \leq 2$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Method

The authors constructed a light-cone wave function for the kaon by applying a Melosh rotation to the instant-form SU(6) spin wave function and adopting a harmonic oscillator spatial wave function in the rest frame, transformed to the light-cone via the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) prescription. The four parameters (strange quark mass $m_s$, light quark mass $m_u=m_d$, harmonic scale $\beta$, and normalization $A_0$) were fixed by four constraints: the normalization condition, the weak decay constant $f_K = 113.4$ MeV, the charged mean square radius of $K^+$, and the charged mean square radius of $K^0$. The electromagnetic form factors for $K^+$ and $K^0$ were then calculated using the Drell-Yan-West formula in the $q^+ = 0$ frame.

## Key numbers

- Weak decay constant: $f_K = 113.3$ MeV (reproduced from input $f_K = 113.4$ MeV).
- Charged mean square radius of $K^+$: $\langle r_{K^+}^2 \rangle = 0.30$ fm$^2$ (experimental value $0.34 \pm 0.05$ fm$^2$).
- Charged mean square radius of $K^0$: $\langle r_{K^0}^2 \rangle = -0.055$ fm$^2$ (experimental value $-0.054 \pm 0.026$ fm$^2$).
- Constituent quark masses: $m_s = 500$ MeV, $m_u = m_d = 250$ MeV.
- Harmonic oscillator scale: $\beta = 393$ MeV.
- Normalization constant: $A_0 = 0.0742$.
- The model is stated to be valid for $Q^2 \leq 2$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone constituent quark model previously applied to the pion by Ma (1993, 1995) and Cao (1997) to the kaon system. The authors note that similar calculations for the kaon form factor have been performed by Choi (1998) and Simula. The results provide predictions for the $K^0$ form factor, which is non-zero due to the strange-down quark mass difference, and the authors suggest this could be tested experimentally to reveal the different contributions from strange and down quarks inside $K^0$.
