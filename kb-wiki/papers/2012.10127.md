# New equation of state involving Bose-Einstein condensate of antikaon for supernova and neutron star merger simulations

> We compute a new equation of state table including Bose-Einstein condensate of $K^{-}$ mesons for core collapse supernova and neutron star merger simulations. Nuclei and interacting nucleons in non-uniform matter is described in an extended version of the nuclear statistical equilibrium model including excluded volume effects whereas the uniform matter at higher densities is treated in the relativistic hadron field theory with density dependent couplings. The equation of state table is generated for a wide range of density ($10^{-12}$ to $\sim 1$ fm$^{-3}$), positive charge fraction (0.01 to 0.60) and temperature (0.1 to 158.48 MeV). The impact of antikaon condensate is investigated on different thermodynamic quantities for example free energy per baryon, entropy per baryon, pressure as well as compositions of matter. Furthermore, critical temperatures of antikaon condensation and the phase diagram of matter are also studied in this article.

# New equation of state involving Bose-Einstein condensate of antikaon for supernova and neutron star merger simulations

## Key claim

The authors construct the first equation of state (EoS) table that includes a Bose-Einstein condensate of K⁻ mesons for use in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations, using an average antikaon optical potential depth of U_K⁻ = -120 MeV. The antikaon condensate makes the EoS softer than the purely nucleonic HS(DD2) EoS, resulting in a lower maximum neutron star mass, but the effect is only present at high densities and depends strongly on temperature and positive charge fraction.

## Method

The inhomogeneous (sub-saturation) matter is described using an extended nuclear statistical equilibrium model with excluded volume effects, following the Hempel-Schaffner (HS) approach. The uniform dense matter at higher densities is treated in a relativistic hadron field theory with density-dependent couplings (DD2 parameter set), where nucleon-nucleon interactions are mediated by σ, ω, and ρ mesons. Kaon-nucleon interactions are included via minimal coupling, with kaon-vector meson couplings determined from the quark model and isospin counting rule (g_ωK = 1/3 g_ωN, g_ρK = g_ρN), and the scalar coupling fixed to produce U_K⁻ = -120 MeV at saturation density. The non-uniform and uniform EoS tables are merged by minimizing the free energy per baryon at fixed baryon density, temperature, and positive charge fraction.

## Key numbers

- EoS table covers baryon density range: 10⁻¹² to ~1 fm⁻³
- Temperature range: 0.1 to 158.48 MeV
- Positive charge fraction range: 0.01 to 0.60
- Grid: 301 baryon density points (Δlog₁₀(n_B) = 0.04), 81 temperatures (Δlog₁₀(T) = 0.04), 60 positive charge fractions (ΔY_q = 0.01)
- Antikaon optical potential depth used: U_K⁻ = -120 MeV
- Saturation density of the DD2 parameter set: n₀ = 0.149065 fm⁻³
- Maximum neutron star mass for HS(DD2) nucleonic EoS: 2.42 M_⊙ with radius 11.95 km
- Maximum neutron star mass for HS(DD2)K⁻ EoS with antikaon condensate: 2.19 M_⊙ with radius 12.14 km
- Smooth transition from nuclear to npK⁻ matter observed for temperatures below 80 MeV
- Threshold for including thermal antikaons in non-uniform matter: n_K^T > 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the widely used Hempel-Schaffner (HS) nucleonic EoS (Hempel and Schaffner, 2014) by adding antikaon degrees of freedom, and is the first EoS table with Bose-Einstein condensate of antikaons suitable for supernova and merger simulations. It builds on prior work by Banik, Hempel, and Bandyopadhyay (2014) who constructed a hyperonic EoS (BHBΛφ) compatible with 2 M_⊙ neutron stars, and on earlier studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and Knorren et al. (1995). The resulting EoS enables simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers with exotic matter in the form of antikaon condensates, and the authors plan to include hyperons alongside antikaons in future work.
