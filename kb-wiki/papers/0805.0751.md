# CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 Event Generators for the MCNP6, MCNPX, and MARS15 Transport Codes

> A description of the IntraNuclear Cascade (INC), preequilibrium, evaporation, fission, coalescence, and Fermi breakup models used by the latest versions of our CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators is presented, with a focus on our most recent developments of these models. The recently developed "S" and "G" versions of our codes, that consider multifragmentation of nuclei formed after the preequilibrium stage of reactions when their excitation energy is above 2A MeV using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) code by Botvina et al. ("S" stands for SMM) and the fission-like binary-decay model GEMINI by Charity ("G" stands for GEMINI), respectively, are briefly described as well. Examples of benchmarking our models against a large variety of experimental data on particle-particle, particle-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus reactions are presented. Open questions on reaction mechanisms and future necessary work are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper describes the physics models and recent improvements in the CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators, but notes that CEM does not consider the "trawling" effect (depletion of target nucleons during a cascade) and therefore does not describe reactions on very light nuclei like carbon at incident energies above about 1 GeV, nor on actinide nuclei above about 5 GeV, requiring a switch to LAQGSM at higher energies.

## Method

The authors present the IntraNuclear Cascade (INC), preequilibrium, evaporation, fission, coalescence, and Fermi breakup models used in CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03, with the INC based on the standard Dubna cascade model using a Fermi distribution for nuclear matter density and a three-dimensional Monte Carlo simulation of successive quasi-free collisions. The codes treat reactions as three-stage processes: INC, followed by preequilibrium emission using the Modified Exciton Model, followed by evaporation/fission or Fermi breakup for light residual nuclei with A ≤ 12. The "S" and "G" versions incorporate the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) by Botvina et al. and the fission-like binary-decay model GEMINI by Charity, respectively, for multifragmentation of highly excited nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi breakup model is used for excited nuclei with A ≤ 12 after the cascade stage
- The nuclear matter density uses a Fermi distribution with parameters c = 1.07 A^(1/3) fm and a = 0.545 fm
- For incident nucleons, the effective real potential V_N(r) = T_F(r) + ε, where T_F(r) is the local Fermi energy and ε is the binding energy
- For pions, a square-well nuclear potential with depth V_π ≈ 25 MeV is used, independent of nucleus and pion energy
- The INC is applicable when the DeBroglie wavelength is less than the average inter-nucleon distance Δ ~ 10^(-13) cm, requiring kinetic energy T > several tens of MeV
- The time between consecutive intranuclear collisions Δt = l/c ≳ 3·10^(-22)/σ (mb) sec
- The Dubna INC considers photonuclear reactions at energies above the Giant Dipole Resonance region (T_γ = 10–40 MeV)
- The Levinger model for photon absorption uses L ≈ 10 for the quasi-deuteron mechanism
- The Dubna INC does not consider three or more pion production, limiting photon energy applicability to T_γ ≲ 1.5 GeV
- New approximations were developed for 34 different types of elementary cross sections induced by nucleons, pions, and gammas

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) originally proposed by Gudima, Mashnik, and Toneev at JINR Dubna, and the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM), which itself improves upon the earlier QGSM. The paper builds on the International Code Comparison for Intermediate Energy Nuclear Data (1993–1994) which showed that CEM95 had one of the best predictive powers for nucleon-induced reactions above about 150 MeV. The codes are incorporated into transport codes including MCNP6, MCNPX, MARS15, GEANT4, and others, enabling simulations for Accelerator Transmutation of nuclear Wastes, Spallation Neutron Source, and other applications requiring intermediate- and high-energy nuclear data.
