# Microscopic formulation of the interacting boson-fermion model using the nuclear energy density functional

> Microscopic modeling of low-energy spectroscopy in medium-heavy and heavy odd-$A$ nuclei is an outstanding open problem in nuclear physics. We propose a novel spectra-generating collective model for odd-$A$ nuclei constructed by means of the nuclear energy density functional theory and the interacting boson-fermion model. The bosonic Hamiltonian for an even-even nucleus, which is treated as a core, and the strength parameters for the interactions between the core and an odd nucleon are completely determined by using as microscopic inputs the potential energy curves and deformed single-particle spectra obtained from the self-consistent mean-field calculations. In applications to odd-$A$ Eu, Sm, La, and Ba isotopes, we demonstrate the validity of the proposed method in reproducing reasonably the observed low-energy spectra and shape phase transitions in the general cases of the quadrupole collective states, that is, nearly spherical, strongly deformed, and $γ$-soft shapes, in the presence of an odd nucleon in a single-$j$ orbit.

# Microscopic formulation of the interacting boson-fermion model using the nuclear energy density functional

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to completely determine all parameters of the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian from self-consistent mean-field calculations based on nuclear energy density functional theory, without any adjustment to experimental data. However, for well-deformed nuclei with equilibrium deformation β_e ≥ 0.2, a separate scale factor C_BF for the boson-fermion coupling part must be introduced because the simplified analytical form of the single-particle energy formula does not adequately reproduce the HFB results at large deformations.

## Method

The authors perform constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SkM* Skyrme energy density functional to obtain potential energy curves for even-even core nuclei and deformed single-particle spectra for neighboring odd-A nuclei. The IBM Hamiltonian parameters for the even-even core are determined by mapping the HFB potential energy curve onto the IBM energy surface in the coherent state basis, while the boson-fermion interaction strengths (Γ₀, Λ₀, A₀) are fixed by matching the IBFM deformed single-particle energies λ_K(β;η) to the HFB single-particle energies ε_K(β) near the equilibrium deformation. The resulting IBFM Hamiltonian is then diagonalized numerically to obtain energy levels and wave functions for electromagnetic transition calculations.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium deformation β_e = 0.10 for ¹⁴⁸Sm (weakly deformed prolate shape)
- For odd-A Eu and Sm nuclei with N ≥ 90, β_e ≥ 0.2 (strongly deformed systems)
- Occupation probability v²_πh₁₁/₂ = 0.177 for ¹⁴⁹Eu, decreasing to 0.145 for ¹⁵⁹Eu
- Occupation probability v²_νi₁₃/₂ = 0.011 for ¹⁴⁹Sm, increasing to 0.192 for ¹⁵⁹Sm
- Occupation probability v²_πh₁₁/₂ = 0.081 for ¹²⁹La, decreasing to 0.077 for ¹³⁵La
- Occupation probability v²_νh₁₁/₂ = 0.376 for ¹²⁷Ba, increasing to 0.667 for ¹³³Ba
- Boson numbers N_B = 8 to 13 for even-even ¹⁴⁸-¹⁵⁸Sm isotopes
- Boson numbers N_B = 8, 7, 6, 5 for even-even ¹²⁸-¹³⁴Ba isotopes (neutrons are hole-like)
- The χ parameter for Sm isotopes approaches the SU(3) limit value χ = -√7/2 ≈ -1.32 near N = 94

## Context

This work extends the earlier proof-of-principle study by Homma and Nomura (2025) on axially deformed ¹⁴⁹-¹⁵⁹Eu isotopes to a more systematic investigation including odd-N Sm isotopes and γ-soft La and Ba isotopes in the A ≈ 130 mass region. The method contrasts with the projected EDF-GCM approach used by Bally (2014), Borrajo (2016), and Zhou (2024), which is computationally prohibitive for heavy odd-A nuclei, and with ab-initio GCM descriptions by Li (2025) that are limited to nuclei heavier than Mg. The results enable parameter-free predictions of low-energy spectroscopy and shape phase transitions in odd-A nuclei across different mass regions, including both axially deformed and γ-soft systems.
