# Rho and Sigma Mesons in Unitarized Thermal ChPT

> We present our recent results for the rho and sigma mesons considered as resonances in pion-pion scattering in a thermal bath. We use chiral perturbation theory to fourth order in p for the low energy behaviour, then extend the analysis via the unitarization method of the Inverse Amplitude into the resonance region. The width of the rho broadens about twice the amount required by phase space considerations alone, its mass staying practically constant up to temperatures of order 150 MeV. The sigma meson behaves in accordance to chiral symmetry restoration expectations.

# Rho and Sigma Mesons in Unitarized Thermal ChPT

## Key claim

The rho meson width broadens about twice as much as expected from phase-space increase alone, while its mass remains practically constant up to temperatures around 150 MeV; the sigma meson shows a decreasing mass and a width that initially increases then drops sharply near the two-pion threshold, consistent with chiral symmetry restoration expectations.

## Method

The authors compute the thermal pion-pion scattering amplitude to one loop in chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at order p⁴, using Matsubara sums in the imaginary-time formalism to incorporate temperature dependence. They then extend the amplitude into the resonance region via the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), a [1,1] Padé approximant that enforces exact unitarity while approximating the real part from the χPT expansion. The resonance poles are tracked in the complex plane on the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson width broadens about twice the amount required by phase-space considerations alone at temperatures up to 150 MeV.
- The rho meson mass stays practically constant up to temperatures of order 150 MeV.
- The thermal phase-space factor includes a term 2/(exp(β|E|/2)-1) modifying the zero-temperature factor √(1-4m_π²/E²).
- The chiral expansion corrections scale at least as the fourth power of the small quantity M_π/(4π f_π).

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984) combined with the Inverse Amplitude Method (Pelaez et al., prior work) to finite temperature, providing model-independent predictions for the ρ and σ resonances in a thermal bath. The results agree at low temperatures with an older calculation by Song (1995) and with the ρ mass behavior reported by Ko (1995) and by a fourth reference (1995), both of which address the dilepton excess below M_ρ. The approach does not directly access the chiral phase transition but constrains models that do, by providing the low-temperature behavior of resonance parameters.
