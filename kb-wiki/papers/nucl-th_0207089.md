# Nuclear forces in the chiral limit

> We investigate the behaviour of the nuclear forces as a function of the light quark masses (or, equivalently, pion mass) in the framework of chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order. The nucleon-nucleon force is described in terms of one- and two-pion exchange and local short distance operators, which depend explicitly and implicitly on the quark masses. The pion propagator becomes Coulomb-like in the chiral limit and thus one has significant scattering in all partial waves. The pion-nucleon coupling depends implicitly on the quark masses and we find that it becomes stronger in the chiral limit. There is a further quark mass dependence in the S-wave four--nucleon couplings, which can be estimated by means of dimensional analysis. We find that nuclear physics in the chiral limit becomes natural. There are no new bound states, the deuteron binding energy is B_D^{CL} = 9.6 +/- 1.9^+1.8_-1.0 MeV, and the S-wave scattering lengths take values of a few fm, a_{CL} (^1S_0) = -4.1 +/- 1.6^+0.0_-0.4 fm and a_{CL} (^3S_1) = 1.5 +/- 0.4^+0.2_-0.3 fm. We also discuss the extrapolation to larger pion masses pertinent for the extraction of these quantities from lattice simulations.

## Key claim
The authors perform a complete next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral effective field theory calculation of nucleon-nucleon forces in the chiral limit (pion mass \(M_\pi \to 0\)), finding that nuclear physics becomes "natural", there are no new bound states, the deuteron remains bound with binding energy \(B_D^{\rm CL} = 9.6 \pm 1.9^{+1.8}_{-1.0}\) MeV, and S-wave scattering lengths are of order a few fm, contradicting earlier suggestions that the deuteron might become unbound.

## Method
The nucleon-nucleon potential was constructed at NLO in chiral effective field theory using a modified Weinberg power counting, including one-pion exchange (OPE), leading two-pion exchange (TPE), and local short-range contact interactions. The explicit and implicit quark-mass dependences of all contributions were systematically worked out, and the potential was fully renormalized using dimensional regularization and the method of unitary transformation, with low-energy constants taken from analyses of \(\pi N\) and \(\pi N \to \pi \pi N\) processes. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation was then solved numerically to obtain phase shifts, binding energies, and scattering lengths for pion masses in the range \(0 < M_\pi < 300\) MeV.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy in the chiral limit: \(B_D^{\rm CL} = 9.6 \pm 1.9^{+1.8}_{-1.0}\) MeV.
- S-wave scattering length in the \(^1S_0\) channel in the chiral limit: \(a_{\rm CL}(^1S_0) = -4.1 \pm 1.6^{+0.0}_{-0.4}\) fm.
- S-wave scattering length in the \(^3S_1\) channel in the chiral limit: \(a_{\rm CL}(^3S_1) = 1.5 \pm 0.4^{+0.2}_{-0.3}\) fm.
- No new bound states appear in the chiral limit.

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis of Bulgac et al. (1997), who used only OPE and first-order perturbation theory to predict a 50% reduction in deuteron binding energy, and Beane et al. (2001), who found the deuteron could become unbound for \(M_\pi < 100\) MeV when including implicit quark-mass dependence via leading chiral logarithms. By performing a complete NLO calculation with full renormalization and using empirical low-energy constants rather than assuming dominance of chiral logarithms, the authors resolve the controversy and provide a reliable prediction for the chiral limit. The results enable extrapolation of lattice QCD data, which are typically obtained at larger pion masses, down to the physical and chiral limits.
