# Kaon-antikaon nuclear optical potentials and the kappa meson in the nuclear medium

> We study the properties of the kappa meson in the nuclear medium, starting from a chiral unitary model of S-wave, I=1/2 K-pi scattering, which describes the elastic K-pi phase shifts and generates a pole in the amplitude. Medium effects are considered by including pion and kaon selfenergies. We explore the changes in the kappa pole position and in the K-pi scattering amplitude at finite density, with two different models for the kaon selfenergy.

# Kaon-antikaon nuclear optical potentials and the kappa meson in the nuclear medium

## Key claim

The kappa meson pole mass and width are significantly modified in nuclear matter, with the pole splitting into separate Kπ and anti-Kπ branches due to the asymmetric self-energies of kaons and antikaons; however, the kappa does not become narrow enough to provide a clear experimental signal, although the accumulation of strength at lower Kπ invariant masses around 650 MeV might be observable.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral unitary model of S-wave, I=1/2 Kπ scattering, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the tree-level amplitude from lowest-order chiral perturbation theory and a cutoff regularization (q_max = 850 MeV). Medium effects are incorporated by dressing the pion and kaon propagators with self-energies: the pion self-energy includes P-wave ph and Δh excitations with a Landau-Migdal parameter, while two different models for the kaon self-energy are used (a chirally motivated shallow potential, model A, and a deep phenomenological potential, model B). The kappa pole is searched for in the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane using spectral representations of the dressed meson propagators.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum kappa pole position: M_κ + i Γ_κ/2 = 825 + 460i MeV
- At ρ = 1.5 ρ_0 with in-medium pions and free kaons: M_κ reduced from 825 MeV to 650 MeV, with Γ_κ reduced by 300 MeV
- Mass splitting between Kπ and anti-Kπ branches at normal nuclear density: about 50 MeV in model B
- Kaon self-energy parameter C: 0.13 for model A, 0.114 for model B (in units of m_K² ρ/ρ_0)
- Antikaon self-energy in model B at zero momentum and normal density: -200 MeV depth
- Cutoff used in the loop function: q_max = 850 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the sigma meson in nuclear matter (Vicente Vacas and Oset, 2002) to the kappa meson, using the same framework as Oller and Oset (1997) for meson-meson scattering. The study is motivated by the different behavior of kaon and antikaon optical potentials in the medium, where shallow potentials (Ramos and Oset, 1999; Lutz, 1997) and deep potentials (Sibirtsev, 1998; Friedman, 1998) both fit kaonic atom data but predict different medium modifications. The results enable experimental tests via Kπ invariant mass distributions in reactions with pions and kaons in the final state, and could help discriminate between competing models of the antikaon-nucleus optical potential.
