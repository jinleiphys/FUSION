# Statistical Physics in a Finite Volume with Absolute Conservation Laws

> Recursion relations are used to exactly calculate the partition function of a canonical ensemble in which all additive charges as well as the total isospin are strictly conserved. The ensemble can consist of particles that obey either classical or quantum statistics. Recursion relations are also employed to compute observables such as multiplicity distributions and isospin fluctuations.

## Key claim

Recursion relations can exactly compute the partition function of a canonical ensemble that strictly conserves all additive charges and total isospin simultaneously, for both classical and quantum statistics; the method avoids summing over the prohibitive number of partitions that would otherwise be required for large systems.

## Method

The partition function is rewritten as a recursion relation by inserting a factor of \(1/A \sum_j a_j \nu_j = 1\) and splitting the sum over isospin configurations using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. For quantum statistics, the isospin-conserving partition function is obtained from the projection-conserving partition function via \(\Omega_{A,I,M,Q} = Z_{A,M,Q} - Z_{A,M+1,Q}\). Recursion relations for multiplicity distributions and factorial moments are derived analogously, and isospin fluctuations are computed by rewriting the fluctuation operator in terms of spherical tensor components and applying the Wigner-Eckart theorem.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of 170 MeV used for all sample calculations (pion systems).
- Total number of pions fixed to \(A=20\) for multiplicity distribution and isospin fluctuation calculations.
- Isospin fluctuations divided by \(2A\) (classical) or \(2\langle A \rangle\) (Bose-Einstein) to highlight deviation from an independent pion gas.
- For classical particles with fixed \(A=20\), isospin fluctuation does not depend on volume; for Bose-Einstein particles at small volume, fluctuations are enhanced.
- When pion number is not fixed, isospin fluctuations converge to the independent-pion-gas value as system size increases.

## Context

This work extends recursion-relation techniques for canonical ensembles that conserve additive charges, previously applied to classical particles by Pratt (1999) and to Fermi systems by Pratt (1999), by adding exact conservation of total isospin. The method enables studies of isospin fluctuations relevant to CENTAURO events and provides a foundation for Monte Carlo algorithms that generate particles with exact charge and isospin conservation in hydrodynamical models of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where grand-canonical approaches are currently standard.
