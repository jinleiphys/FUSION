# Investigating the Transverse Single Spin Asymmetry in the Inelastic $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $p^\uparrow p$ and $p^\uparrow A$ collisions

> In this paper we propose to investigate the transverse single spin asymmetry in the inelastic $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $p^\uparrow p$ and $p^\uparrow A$ collisions at RHIC energies. At leading order this process probes the gluon Sivers function. We predict large values for the cross sections, which indicates that its experimental analysis is, in principle, feasible. The rapidity dependence of the single spin asymmetry is presented. We obtain that the asymmetry is strongly dependent on the model used for the gluon Sivers function and that it can be probed by the analysis of the $J/Ψ$ production at forward rapidities. Our results indicate that a future experimental analysis of this process can be useful to constrain the gluon Sivers function.

## Key claim

The transverse single spin asymmetry in inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑p and p↑A collisions at RHIC energies is strongly dependent on the model used for the gluon Sivers function, and its experimental analysis is feasible because the predicted cross sections are large; however, the numerical values for the asymmetry presented should be considered illustrative due to the current large uncertainty in the gluon Sivers function parameters.

## Method

The author calculates the cross section for inelastic J/psi photoproduction using the Color Evaporation Model (CEM), where the photon from an unpolarized proton or nucleus interacts with a transversely polarized proton. The hadronic cross section is factorized into the equivalent photon flux of the unpolarized hadron and the gamma-p cross section, with the gluon distribution in the polarized proton expressed as the sum of an unpolarized TMD gluon distribution and the gluon Sivers function. The single spin asymmetry A_N(Y) is computed as the ratio of the difference to the sum of cross sections for opposite transverse polarizations, using four different models for the gluon Sivers function (SIDIS1, SIDIS2, BV-a, and BV-b).

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: 0.932 nb
- Total cross section for inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑p collisions at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV: 1.245 nb
- Total cross section for inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: 380.0 nb
- Total cross section for inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV: 1664.5 nb
- The maximum and minimum values of A_N are almost independent of energy, with the peak occurring at larger rapidities as energy increases
- The predictions for A_N change by less than 5% if NRQCD formalism is used instead of CEM
- The transverse momentum of the vector meson is integrated in the range 0 to 1.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the gluon Sivers function using quarkonium production in proton-proton collisions (sivers_pp) and electron-proton collisions (Godbole, Asmita) to the case of photon-induced interactions in p↑p and p↑A collisions at RHIC. The analysis complements prior work on photon-induced interactions in unpolarized hadron collisions (Bert, Vicmag, Brunoall, Brunorun2, Vicdiego, Roman) by including a transversely polarized proton beam. The results indicate that the inelastic J/psi photoproduction in p↑A collisions is expected to be more easily performed experimentally than in p↑p collisions because the rapidity distribution magnitude is almost three orders of magnitude larger, and the study should also be possible at the AFTER@LHC experiment.
