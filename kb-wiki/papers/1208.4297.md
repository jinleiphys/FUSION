# New applications of Renormalization Group methods to nuclear matter

> We give an overview of recent results for the nuclear equation of state and properties of neutron stars based on microscopic two- and three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT). It is demonstrated that the application of Renormalization Group (RG) transformations allows efficient, controlled and simplified calculations of nuclear matter.

# New applications of Renormalization Group methods to nuclear matter

## Key claim
Renormalization Group (RG) transformations, specifically the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG), enable efficient, controlled, and simplified calculations of nuclear matter by decoupling high-momentum from low-momentum physics in nuclear interactions, but the approach requires consistent evolution of both the Hamiltonian and all operators (including many-body forces) to preserve physical predictions.

## Method
The author applies SRG unitary transformations to chiral effective field theory (EFT) two- and three-nucleon interactions, evolving them to lower resolution scales to suppress off-diagonal matrix elements. For symmetric nuclear matter, only NN interactions are evolved via RG while short-range 3N force parameters are fixed from few-body fits at the low-momentum scale. For neutron matter, chiral EFT interactions up to N3LO (including NN, 3N, and 4N forces) are used directly without RG evolution. Astrophysical constraints are imposed via a piecewise polytropic ansatz for the equation of state at high densities, with causality and observed neutron star mass limits as constraints.

## Key numbers
- For a typical neutron star of mass M = 1.4 solar masses, the radius range is R = 10.0–13.7 km for the minimum mass constraint M_min = 1.97 solar masses, and R = 11.6–13.7 km for M_min = 2.4 solar masses.
- The heaviest confirmed observed neutron star mass used as a constraint is M_min = 1.97 solar masses.
- The SRG evolution to a resolution scale of lambda = 1.5 fm^{-1} substantially softens the three-nucleon potential, suppressing off-diagonal couplings.
- For symmetric nuclear matter, even at a cutoff Lambda = 2.8 fm^{-1} (similar to lower cutoffs in chiral EFT potentials), saturation occurs at too high density without inclusion of three-nucleon forces.

## Context
This work extends the application of SRG methods, previously demonstrated for nucleon-nucleon interactions by Bogner (2007) and for three-nucleon forces by Hebeler (2011), to nuclear matter calculations and neutron star properties. It enables controlled calculations of the nuclear equation of state with theoretical error estimates, connecting microscopic chiral EFT interactions to astrophysical observables such as neutron star radii. The approach contrasts with conventional non-perturbative nuclear potentials and provides a framework for systematically evolving operators, as shown in recent work by Anderson (2012) on operator evolution.
