# Loop Corrections and Naturalness in a Chiral Effective Field Theory

> The loop expansion is applied to a chiral effective hadronic lagrangian; with the techniques of Infrared Regularization, it is possible to separate out the short-range contributions and to write them as local products of fields that are already present in our lagrangian. (The appropriate field variables must be re-defined at each order in loops.) The corresponding parameters implicitly include short-range effects to all orders in the interaction, so these effects need not be calculated explicitly. The remaining (long-range) contributions that must be calculated are nonlocal and resemble those in conventional nuclear-structure calculations. Nonlinear isoscalar scalar $(σ)$ and vector $(ω)$ meson interactions are included, which incorporate many-nucleon forces and nucleon substructure. Calculations are carried out at the two-loop level to illustrate these techniques at finite nuclear densities and to verify that the coupling parameters remain natural when fitted to the empirical properties of equilibrium nuclear matter. Contributions from the $ωN$ tensor coupling are also discussed.

## Key claim

The loop expansion in chiral effective field theory can be carried out to two-loop order while maintaining natural coupling parameters, provided that short-range contributions are absorbed into redefined local terms in the Lagrangian and only long-range (exchange) contributions are calculated explicitly.

## Method

The authors applied the loop expansion to a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian that includes nonlinear isoscalar scalar (σ) and vector (ω) meson self-interactions, as well as ωN tensor coupling and scalar-vector mixing terms. Using dimensional regularization and techniques analogous to Infrared Regularization, they separated two-loop baryon and mesonic loop integrals into short-range contributions (Lamb-shift and vacuum-fluctuation terms) that are absorbed into existing Lagrangian parameters, and long-range exchange terms that are evaluated explicitly. The resulting energy density was fitted to empirical properties of equilibrium nuclear matter to test the naturalness of the coupling parameters.

## Context

This work extends earlier two-loop calculations in QHD by McIntire et al. (2007) that included only rudimentary couplings, now incorporating nonlinear meson self-interactions and tensor terms that incorporate many-nucleon forces and nucleon substructure. The approach builds on the density functional theory framework for nuclear structure developed by Furnstahl, Serot, and others (1997, 1998, 2004) and uses Infrared Regularization techniques from Becher and Leutwyler (1999, 2000) to separate short-range and long-range dynamics. The results enable systematic inclusion of many-body correlations in nuclear matter calculations while preserving the naturalness assumption that dimensionless coupling coefficients remain of order unity.
