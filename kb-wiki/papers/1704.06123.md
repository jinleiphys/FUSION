# Scale Invariance in Heavy Hadron Molecules

> We discuss a scenario in which the $P_c(4450)^{+}$ heavy pentaquark is a $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$-$Λ_{c}(2595) \bar{D}$ molecule. The $Λ_{c1} \bar{D} \to Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ transition is mediated by the exchange of a pion almost on the mass shell that generates a long-range $1/r^2$ potential. This is analogous to the effective force that is responsible for the Efimov spectrum in three-boson systems interacting through short-range forces. The equations describing this molecule exhibit approximate scale invariance, which is anomalous and broken by the solutions. If the $1/r^2$ potential is strong enough this symmetry survives in the form of discrete scale invariance, opening the prospect of an Efimov-like geometrical spectrum in two-hadron systems. For a molecular pentaquark with quantum numbers $\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ the attraction is not enough to exhibit discrete scale invariance, but this prospect might very well be realized in a $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ pentaquark or in other hadron molecules involving transitions between particle channels with opposite intrinsic parity and a pion near the mass shell. A very good candidate is the $Λ_{c}(2595) \barΞ_b - Σ_c \barΞ_b'$ molecule. Independently of this, the $1/r^2$ force is expected to play a very important role in the formation of this type of hadron molecule, which points to the existence of $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ $Σ_c D^*$-$Λ_{c}(2595) D$ and $1^+$ $Λ_{c}(2595) Ξ_b - Σ_c Ξ_b'$ molecules and $0^{+}$/$1^{-}$ $Λ_{c}(2595) \barΞ_b - Σ_c \barΞ_b'$ baryonia.

## Key claim

The $P_c(4450)^{+}$ heavy pentaquark, if it is a $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*$-$\Lambda_{c}(2595) \bar{D}$ molecule, may exhibit approximate scale invariance due to a long-range $1/r^2$ potential generated by pion exchange near the mass shell; however, for the $\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ quantum numbers the attraction is not strong enough to produce discrete scale invariance, though this could be realized in a $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ pentaquark or in other hadron molecules such as the $\Lambda_{c}(2595) \bar{\Xi}_b - \Sigma_c \bar{\Xi}_b'$ system.

## Method

The authors derive the one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential for the $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^* \to \Lambda_{c1} \bar{D}$ transition using the heavy baryon chiral Lagrangian, where the pion is nearly on the mass shell, generating a $1/r^2$ vector force. They then solve the coupled-channel reduced Schrödinger equation at zero energy for various partial-wave bases, diagonalizing the matrix of effective $1/r^2$ potentials to find eigenvalues that determine whether discrete scale invariance (and a geometric bound-state spectrum) can occur. The strength parameter $g$ is evaluated using known axial couplings and decay constants, and compared with the critical threshold required for discrete scale invariance.

## Key numbers

- For the $P_c^*$ molecule with $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^{-}$, the coupling $g = 0.60^{+0.10}_{-0.10}\,h_2$; discrete scale invariance requires $|g| > 5/(4\sqrt{3}) \simeq 0.7217$, which is not met with $h_2 = 0.60 \pm 0.07$ (from CDF) or $h_2 = 0.63 \pm 0.07$ (from Cheng 2015).
- For a $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ pentaquark, the critical condition is $|g| > \sqrt{3}/4 \simeq 0.4330$, requiring $|h_2| > 0.73^{+0.11}_{-0.06}$, which overlaps with the current estimation $h_2 = 0.63 \pm 0.07$.
- For the $\Lambda_{c1} \bar{\Xi}_b - \Sigma_c \bar{\Xi}_b'$ baryonium, $g = 1.12^{+0.03}_{-0.05}\,h_2$; discrete scale invariance requires $|g| > 3/4$, i.e. $|h_2| > 0.67^{+0.03}_{-0.02}$, which is within the error of $h_2 = 0.63 \pm 0.07$.
- The binding radius for the $P_c^*(\frac{3}{2}^{-})$ is $0.94\,{\rm fm}$; for the $0^{-}$ and $1^{-}$ baryonia it is $0.40\,{\rm fm}$ and $0.84\,{\rm fm}$ respectively; for the $1^{+}$ $\Sigma_c \Xi_b'$-$\Lambda_{c1} \Xi_b$ molecule it is $0.87\,{\rm fm}$; for the $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ $\Sigma_c D^*$-$\Lambda_c D$ system it is $0.92\,{\rm fm}$; for the $0^{+}$ baryonium it is $0.86\,{\rm fm}$.
- The scale-invariant window for $P_c^*$-like molecules is $1/(R_s \mu_{\pi}) \sim 10-20$, with $|\mu_{\pi}| \simeq 5-35\,{\rm MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970) and discrete scale invariance from three-boson systems to two-hadron molecules, building on the known analogy between the $1/r^2$ potential and the Efimov effect (Braaten 2004, Hammer 2005). It specifically addresses the molecular interpretation of the $P_c(4450)^{+}$ pentaquark discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2015), following the proposal by Burns (2015) that the $\Sigma_c \bar{D}^* - \Lambda_{c1} \bar{D}$ system is analogous to the $X(3872)$. The paper enables predictions of new hadron molecules, including $\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ $\Sigma_c D^*$-$\Lambda_{c}(2595) D$ and $1^{+}$ $\Lambda_{c}(2595) \Xi_b - \Sigma_c \Xi_b'$ molecules, and $0^{+}/1^{-}$ $\Lambda_{c}(2595) \bar{\Xi}_b - \Sigma_c \bar{\Xi}_b'$ baryonia, and suggests that lattice QCD could realize the fine-tuning needed to observe a geometric spectrum.
