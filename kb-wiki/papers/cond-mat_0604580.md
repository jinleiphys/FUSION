# Superfluidity in a Three-flavor Fermi Gas with SU(3) Symmetry

> We investigate the superfluidity and the associated Nambu-Goldstone modes in a three-flavor atomic Fermi gas with SU(3) global symmetry. The s-wave pairing occurs in flavor anti-triplet channel due to the Pauli principle, and the superfluid state contains both gapped and gapless fermionic excitations. Corresponding to the spontaneous breaking of the SU(3) symmetry to a SU(2) symmetry with five broken generators, there are only three Nambu-Goldstone modes, one is with linear dispersion law and two are with quadratic dispersion law. The other two expected Nambu-Goldstone modes become massive with a mass gap of the order of the fermion energy gap in a wide coupling range. The abnormal number of Nambu-Goldstone modes, the quadratic dispersion law and the mass gap have significant effect on the low temperature thermodynamics of the matter.

## Key claim

In a three-flavor Fermi gas with SU(3) symmetry, spontaneous symmetry breaking to a SU(2) subgroup with five broken generators yields only three Nambu-Goldstone modes (one linear, two quadratic) rather than five, because the condition \(\langle[Q_i,Q_j]\rangle=0\) is not satisfied due to the density imbalance between paired and unpaired fermions; the other two expected modes acquire a mass gap of order the fermion energy gap in a wide coupling range.

## Method

The authors model the system using a Lagrangian with three fermion flavors and an attractive s-wave interaction in the flavor anti-triplet channel, then perform a Stratonovich-Hubbard transformation to introduce pair fields. They apply the mean-field (saddle-point) approximation to obtain the thermodynamic potential, solve the coupled gap equation and number equation at zero temperature, and evaluate the pair fluctuation effective action to quadratic order to identify collective modes and their dispersion laws.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(\alpha = (n_1+n_2-2n_3)/n\) is always positive in the superfluid phase, ranging from very small in the BCS limit to \(\alpha=1\) in the BEC limit (at \(T=0\)).
* The mass gap \(m_1\) of the two massive collective modes is of the order of the fermion energy gap \(\Delta\) in a wide coupling range \(-1 < (p_F a_s)^{-1} < 1\) (at \(T=0\)).
* The two gapless Nambu-Goldstone modes from the \(I=1,2\) sector have quadratic dispersion \(q_0 = {\bf q}^2/(2m)\), where \(m\) is the fermion mass.
* The single Nambu-Goldstone mode from the \(I=3\) sector has linear dispersion \(q_0 = v_s |{\bf q}|\) with velocity \(v_s = \sqrt{A(D+E)/(B^2-AC)}\).

## Context

This work extends studies of superfluidity in two-flavor atomic Fermi gases (e.g., \(^6\)Li experiments using Feshbach resonance) to a three-flavor system with SU(3) symmetry, building on earlier theoretical investigations of SU(3) Fermi gases (SU3-1, SU3-2, SU3-3). The abnormal number of Nambu-Goldstone modes and quadratic dispersion laws were previously discussed in relativistic field theory at finite density (Tomas, AG1, AG2) and in two-flavor color superconductivity in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL1), but in that context the massive modes had a very small mass gap compared to the quark energy gap, whereas here the mass gap is of order \(\Delta\) and thus has observable thermodynamic consequences. The results enable experimental tests in cold atomic gases such as \(^6\)Li or \(^{40}\)K with three degenerate hyperfine states.
