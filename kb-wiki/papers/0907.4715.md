# Bremsstrahlung emission from quark stars

> We calculate numerically the emissivity and surface flux of electron-electron bremsstrahlung radiation from the surface of a bare quark star. The restricted electronic phase space due to the presence of an effective photon mass results in a strong suppression. The emissivity and surface flux are found to be substantially smaller than those found in previous work, to the point where electron-positron pair production would remain the dominant mechanism at all temperatures in the relativistic regime. As a consequence, pair production remains a dominant process even at low surface temperatures as originally suggested by Usov in arXiv:astro-ph/9712304.

# Bremsstrahlung emission from quark stars

## Key claim

Electron-electron bremsstrahlung radiation from the electrosphere of a bare quark star is severely suppressed by the restricted electronic phase space due to an effective photon mass (plasma frequency), making it a negligible radiation mechanism compared to electron-positron pair production at all temperatures in the relativistic regime, contrary to the conclusion of Jaikumar et al. (2006).

## Method

The authors performed numerical calculations of the emissivity and surface flux of electron-electron bremsstrahlung using an exact ultra-relativistic cross-section formula, integrating over the full kinematic phase space with constraints imposed by the plasma frequency acting as an effective photon mass. The electron energies were integrated from μ + m_e to infinity using Fermi distributions, with the photon energy integrated from the plasma frequency ω_p to the kinematic limit, and the results were integrated along the radial direction of the electrosphere using a chemical potential profile from Glendenning (1997).

## Key numbers

- Plasma frequency: ω_p = √(4αμ²/3π + 4απT²/9), where μ is the electron chemical potential and T is the temperature
- Typical electron chemical potential used: μ₀ = 10 MeV
- Electrosphere thickness: approximately 1000 fm
- The suppression factor from phase space constraints is exponential, of the form exp(-ω_p/T), where typically ω_p ≫ T
- The cross-section enhancement from using the exact formula instead of Low's theorem gives a factor of approximately m²/μ², which is much weaker than the exponential suppression
- Surface flux is suppressed relative to electron-positron pair production at all temperatures below a few × 10¹⁰ K

## Context

This work re-examines the calculation of electron-electron bremsstrahlung from quark stars originally performed by Jaikumar et al. (2006), who argued that bremsstrahlung dominates over electron-positron pair production at temperatures between 10⁸ K and 10⁹ K. The authors show that Jaikumar et al. only partially included the effects of the plasma frequency, neglecting additional restrictions on electron energies and scattering angles. The result supports the original suggestion by Usov (1998) that electron-positron pair production remains the dominant radiation mechanism even at low surface temperatures around 10⁹ K. This enables the use of hard X-ray emission from pair annihilation as a distinguishing observational signature for bare quark stars versus neutron stars.
