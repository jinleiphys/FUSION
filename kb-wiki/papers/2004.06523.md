# Mass spectrum of pseudo-scalar glueballs from a Bethe-Salpeter approach with the rainbow-ladder truncation

> We suggest a framework based on the rainbow approximation to the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations with effective parameters adjusted to lattice QCD data to calculate the masses of the ground and excited states of pseudo-scalar glueballs. The structure of the truncated Bethe-Salpeter equation with the gluon and ghost propagators as solutions of the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations is analysed in Landau gauge. Both, the Bete-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger equations, are solved numerically within the same rainbow-ladder truncation with the same effective parameters which ensure consistency of the approach. We found that with a set of parameters, which provides a good description of the lattice data within the Dyson-Schwinger approach, the solutions of the Bethe Salpeter equation for the pseudo-scalar glueballs exhibit a rich mass spectrum which also includes the ground and excited states predicted by lattice calculations. The obtained mass spectrum contains also several intermediate excitations beyond the lattice approaches. The partial Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of the pseudo-scalar glueballs are presented as well.

# Mass spectrum of pseudo-scalar glueballs from a Bethe-Salpeter approach with the rainbow-ladder truncation

## Key claim

The authors claim that a self-consistent rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with effective parameters re-fitted to simultaneously describe lattice gluon propagator data and glueball bound states, produces a pseudo-scalar glueball mass spectrum that includes the ground and excited states predicted by lattice QCD, along with several intermediate excitations not seen in lattice calculations. The key hedge is that this result depends on a specific set of effective parameters that is not unique, and the approach is limited to the decoupling solution family for the gluon and ghost dressing functions.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations (tDSE) for gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge within the rainbow approximation, using Gaussian effective form factors with parameters re-fitted to lattice SU(2) data. They then analytically continue these propagators into the complex Euclidean momentum plane along the parabolic domain required by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and solve the truncated Bethe-Salpeter equation (tBSE) for pseudo-scalar glueballs using the same effective interaction kernel. The partial Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are decomposed over Gegenbauer polynomials, and the angular integrations are performed analytically to reduce the problem to one-dimensional integral equations.

## Key numbers

- Gluon dressing function parameters: ω₁₁ = 1.095 GeV, ω₁₂ = 2.15 GeV, D₁₁ = 0.465 GeV², D₁₂ = 0.116 GeV² for the 3-gluon loop
- Ghost loop parameters: ω₂ = 0.58 GeV, D₂ = 7.7 GeV²
- Gluon-ghost loop parameters: ω₂₁ = 2/3 × ω₁₁, ω₂₂ = ω₁₂, D₂₁ = 0.4π GeV², D₂₂ = 0.1π GeV²
- The tBS equation is solved for glueball masses M_gg with the gluon propagator evaluated at complex momenta given by p₁₂² = -M_gg²/4 + p² ± i M_gg p, defining a parabola in the complex plane
- The tBS wave function is expected to decrease sufficiently by p_max ∼ 3-4 GeV/c
- Lattice glueball states below 5 GeV are referenced as the target range for comparison

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter formalism (Kaptari and Kampfer, 2019; earlier works on quarkonia) from quark-antiquark bound states to pure gluonic bound states. It builds on the rainbow-ladder truncation approach previously applied to mesons by Alkofer and collaborators (the AWW kernel) and Maris and Roberts (2003), and on lattice QCD calculations of glueball masses by Morningstar and Peardon, Chen, and others. The approach enables future investigation of glueball-meson mixing phenomena and decay properties within a consistent framework, and could be extended to scalar glueballs once the more complicated ghost coupling diagrams are included.
