# Tomography of Ultra-relativistic Nuclei with Polarized Photon-gluon Collisions

> A linearly polarized photon can be quantized from the Lorentz-boosted electromagnetic field of a nucleus traveling at ultra-relativistic speed. When two relativistic heavy nuclei pass one another at a distance of a few nuclear radii, the photon from one nucleus may interact through a virtual quark-antiquark pair with gluons from the other nucleus forming a short-lived vector meson (e.g. ${ρ^0}$). In this experiment, the polarization was utilized in diffractive photoproduction to observe a unique spin interference pattern in the angular distribution of ${ρ^0\rightarrowπ^+π^-}$ decays. The observed interference is a result of an overlap of two wave functions at a distance an order of magnitude larger than the ${ρ^0}$ travel distance within its lifetime. The strong-interaction nuclear radii were extracted from these diffractive interactions, and found to be $6.53\pm 0.06$ fm ($^{197} {\rm Au }$) and $7.29\pm 0.08$ fm ($^{238} {\rm U}$), larger than the nuclear charge radii. The observable is demonstrated to be sensitive to the nuclear geometry and quantum interference of non-identical particles.

## Key claim
The observed spin interference pattern in the angular distribution of rho0 to pi+ pi- decays arises from the overlap of two wave functions at a distance an order of magnitude larger than the rho0 travel distance within its lifetime, and this effect is demonstrated to be sensitive to nuclear geometry and quantum interference of non-identical particles.

## Method
The STAR experiment at RHIC measured exclusive diffractive photoproduction of rho0 mesons in ultra-peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV, using the linear polarization of quasi-real photons from the Lorentz-boosted electromagnetic field of one nucleus interacting with gluons from the other nucleus. The rho0 mesons were reconstructed via their decay to pi+ pi- pairs, and the angular distribution of the decay products was analyzed as a function of the transverse momentum transfer |t| and the azimuthal angle phi between the rho0 transverse momentum and the daughter pion momentum. The strong-interaction nuclear radii were extracted from the diffractive interactions by studying the |t| distribution as a function of the polarization angle phi, with the phi=90 degree bin isolating the Pomeron contribution by minimizing photon momentum, polarization, and interference effects.

## Key numbers
- Strong-interaction nuclear radius for Au-197: 6.53 +/- 0.06 fm
- Strong-interaction nuclear radius for U-238: 7.29 +/- 0.08 fm
- Both extracted radii are larger than the nuclear charge radii
- The rho0 lifetime is about 1 fm, while the average impact parameter between nuclei is about 20 fm
- The equivalent incident photon energy in the rest frame of the target nuclei is in the range of 50-100 GeV at RHIC
- The rho0 mass is approximately 770 MeV
- Candidate pi+ pi- pairs were selected with invariant mass between 650 and 900 MeV
- The transverse momentum selection for the pi+ pi- pairs was PT < 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous ultra-peripheral collision measurements at RHIC and LHC (STAR Collaboration 2017, ALICE Collaboration 2020) that studied coherent photoproduction of vector mesons but could not reliably extract the gluon distribution due to uncertainties in the photon source and modeling. The measurement demonstrates that the previously observed anomalously large nuclear mass radii (more than 1 fm larger than charge radii) resulted from neglecting the quantum interference effect in the |t| distribution. The technique provides a path toward 3D gluon imaging similar to what is planned at the future electron-ion collider (EIC Accardi 2012), and the polarization-dependent observable enables separation of coherent from incoherent production more effectively than cross section measurements alone.
