# High-Spin Doublet Band Structures in odd-odd $^{194-200}$Tl isotopes

> The basis space in the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach is generalized for odd-odd nuclei to include two-neutron and two-proton configurations on the basic one-neutron coupled to one-proton quasiparticle state. The generalization allows to investigate odd-odd nuclei beyond the band crossing region and as a first application of this development, high-spin band structures recently observed in odd-odd $^{194-200}$Tl isotopes are investigated. In some of these isotopes, the doublet band structures observed after the band crossing have been conjectured to arise from the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry. The driving configuration of the chiral symmetry in these odd-odd isotopes is one-proton and three-neutrons rather than the basic one-proton and one-neutron as already observed in many other nuclei. It is demonstrated using the TPSM approach that energy differences of the doublet bands in $^{194}$Tl and $^{198}$Tl are, indeed, small. However, the differences in the calculated transition probabilities are somewhat larger than what is expected in the chiral symmetry limit. Experimental data on the transition probabilities is needed to shed light on the chiral nature of the doublet bands.

# High-Spin Doublet Band Structures in odd-odd $^{194-200}$Tl isotopes

## Key claim
The doublet band structures observed after band crossing in odd-odd $^{194-200}$Tl isotopes may arise from chiral symmetry breaking, but with a driving configuration of one-proton and three-neutrons rather than the usual one-proton and one-neutron configuration. However, while energy differences of the doublet bands in $^{194}$Tl and $^{198}$Tl are small, the calculated transition probability differences are somewhat larger than expected in the chiral symmetry limit, and experimental transition probability data is needed to confirm the chiral nature.

## Method
The authors generalized the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) basis space for odd-odd nuclei to include two-neutron and two-proton quasiparticle configurations coupled to the basic one-neutron plus one-proton configuration, enabling study beyond the band crossing region. They performed three-dimensional angular momentum projection from triaxial Nilsson plus BCS quasiparticle states, then diagonalized the shell model Hamiltonian (including quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions) in the projected basis for approximately 40 bands per nucleus. Transition probabilities B(E2) and B(M1) were calculated using the resulting wavefunctions with effective charges of 1.6e for protons and 0.6e for neutrons, and free gyromagnetic factors damped by 0.85 for the spin contributions.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters used: $\beta = 0.178$, $\gamma = 30^\circ$ for $^{194}$Tl; $\beta = 0.168$, $\gamma = 29^\circ$ for $^{196}$Tl; $\beta = 0.157$, $\gamma = 31^\circ$ for $^{198}$Tl; $\beta = 0.157$, $\gamma = 34^\circ$ for $^{200}$Tl
- For $^{194}$Tl, the deviation for the highest observed I=24 state is only about 100 keV between TPSM calculations and experiment
- For $^{196}$Tl, the maximum deviation for the I=20 state is 235 keV between TPSM calculations and experiment
- Band crossing is observed around 0.3 $\hbar\omega$ in all studied Tl isotopes, due to alignment of two neutrons
- Pairing strength parameters used: $G_1 = 20.12$ and $G_2 = 13.13$; quadrupole pairing strength $G_Q$ is 0.16 times $G_M$
- The ground state band for $^{194}$Tl has K=4 from one-proton coupled to one-neutron quasiparticle configuration with quasiparticle energy of 1.9 MeV, crossed at I=16 by a two-neutron aligned state with quasiparticle energy of 3.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the TPSM approach previously applied to odd-odd nuclei (Jehangir et al., 2012) by expanding the basis space to include multi-quasiparticle configurations, enabling study of high-spin states beyond the first band crossing. The paper investigates chiral symmetry breaking in the A~190 region, where chiral bands have been extensively studied in triaxial deformed nuclei (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997; Starosta et al., 2001). The authors build on experimental work by Singh et al. (2017), Paul et al. (2013), and others who observed doublet bands in $^{194-200}$Tl and speculated on chiral symmetry breaking with a $\pi h_{9/2} \otimes \nu i^{-3}_{13/2}$ configuration. The results suggest $^{194}$Tl and possibly $^{198}$Tl as chiral symmetry candidates, while ruling out $^{196}$Tl and $^{200}$Tl based on calculated energy differences. The work calls for experimental transition probability measurements to confirm the chiral nature, and notes that a third negative parity dipole band recently reported in $^{194}$Tl (Masiteng et al.) may represent a second chiral pair.
