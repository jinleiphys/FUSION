# In-medium modified $π$-$ρ$-$ω$ mesonic Lagrangian and properties of nuclear matter

> We investigate the bulk properties of symmetric nuclear matter within the framework of an in-medium modified chiral solitonic model with $π$, $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. We consider the modification of meson degrees of freedom in nuclear matter, based on phenomenology of pion-nucleus scattering and the empirical nuclear mass formula. We discuss the results of the density dependence of the volume term in the mass formula and the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, comparing them with relativistic mean-field models. The mass dropping of the $ρ$ meson in nuclear matter is also obtained and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an in-medium modified chiral soliton model with π, ρ, and ω mesons can simultaneously describe the bulk properties of symmetric nuclear matter and the mass dropping of vector mesons, but the quantitative results depend on whether the ρ and ω meson masses remain degenerate in medium (Model I) or only the ρ meson mass changes while the ω meson stays intact (Model II). The authors note that the nucleon mass from this model is overestimated by about 40%, so they use a rescaled volume-term coefficient of 26 MeV rather than the experimental 16 MeV.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral Lagrangian with π, ρ, and ω meson degrees of freedom, where the nucleon arises as a topological soliton. They introduce medium functionals α_p(ρ), α_s(ρ), ζ_ρ(ρ), and ζ_ω(ρ) that modify the pion kinetic and mass terms based on pion-nucleus scattering phenomenology, and the vector meson couplings based on an analogy with the medium-modified Skyrme term. Using the hedgehog Ansatz for the meson profiles, they solve the coupled nonlinear differential equations for the profile functions F(r), G(r), and ω(r) at a given nuclear matter density, then compute the classical soliton mass and the moment of inertia via collective quantization to obtain the effective nucleon and Δ isobar masses.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Volume-term coefficient at saturation: a₀ = 26.75 MeV (Model I) and 26.38 MeV (Model II)
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: K₀ = 110 MeV (Model I) and 233 MeV (Model II); the Model II value agrees with the empirical value K₀ = 235 ± 14 MeV from giant monopole resonance data
- Third-derivative coefficient: Q₀ = −1591 MeV (Model I) and −1213 MeV (Model II)
- Ratio of ρ meson mass at normal nuclear matter density to vacuum mass: m_ρ*/m_ρ = 0.70 (Model I) and 0.72 (Model II), corresponding to a mass dropping of about 27–30%
- Input parameters: f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, g = 5.85, m_V = m_ρ = m_ω = 770 MeV in free space
- Empirical parameters for medium functionals: b₀ = −0.024 m_π⁻¹, c₀ = 0.09 m_π⁻³, g₀' = 0.7

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral soliton approach of Rakhimov (1996) and Yakhshiev (2010), which previously used a Skyrme term for stabilization, by replacing the Skyrme term with explicit ρ and ω vector meson degrees of freedom following the free-space model of Meissner (1986, 1988). The results are compared with relativistic mean-field models such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1996), FSU (Todd-Rutel 2005), and the hybrid model of Piekarewicz (2008), as well as with the QCD sum rule predictions of Hatsuda and Lee (1991) and the KEK-PS E325 experiment (Naruki 2005). The approach enables future extension to asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star studies.
