# Double inclusive small-x gluon production and their azimuthal correlations in a biased ensemble

> We consider double $gg\rightarrow g$ production in the presence of a bias on the unintegrated gluon distribution of the colliding hadron or nuclei. Such bias could be due to the selection of configurations with a greater number of gluons or higher mean transverse momentum squared or, more generally, due to a modified spectral shape of the gluon distribution in the hadrons. Hence, we consider reweighted functional averages over the stochastic ensemble of small-x gluons. We evaluate explicitly the double inclusive gluon transverse momentum spectrum in high-energy collisions, and their azimuthal correlations, for a few simple examples of biases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that azimuthal correlations of two high-\(p_T\) gluons produced in high-energy collisions can be modified by a functional bias on the unintegrated gluon distribution of the colliding hadrons or nuclei, but only when the bias introduces an anisotropic momentum dependence or when the momentum scale \(Q^2\) of the bias is much smaller than the produced gluon momenta; for a constant isotropic boost of the gluon density over a wide momentum range, the angular moments remain unchanged relative to the unbiased ensemble.

## Method

The author computes the double inclusive gluon production cross section in the dilute, high-\(p_T\) limit using the "glasma graphs" \(k_T\)-factorization formalism, where the unintegrated gluon distribution \(\Phi(k)\) is modified by a multiplicative factor \(\eta(\vec k)\) representing the bias. The bias is implemented by reweighting the functional average over the stochastic ensemble of small-\(x\) gluons, shifting the most likely gluon distribution from \(X_s(k)\) to \(X_{s,b}(\vec k) = \eta(\vec k) X_s(k)\). Explicit analytic expressions for the disconnected (\(\mathcal{A}\)), Bose enhancement (\(\mathcal{B}\)), and HBT (\(\mathcal{C}\)) diagrams are derived for general \(\eta(\vec k)\), and then evaluated for specific isotropic and anisotropic bias functions using leading-logarithmic approximations for the Coulomb propagator integrals.

## Key numbers

* For the unbiased ensemble, the even angular moments for \(n \ge 1\) are \(\langle e^{2n i \phi}\rangle_{\text{unb.}} \approx \frac{\pi}{(N_c^2-1)A_\perp \Lambda^2} \frac{\Lambda^2}{|p^2-q^2|} \frac{q_{<}^{2n}}{q_{>}^{2n}} \frac{ \left(\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac{p^2}{q^2}\right) \log \frac{\min(p^2,q^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2} + 4\frac{q^2}{p^2}\log\frac{\min(p^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2}+ 4\frac{p^2}{q^2}\log\frac{\min(q^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2}} {\log\frac{p^2}{\Lambda^2} \log\frac{q^2}{\Lambda^2}}\), where \(\ell^2 = p^2+q^2-2pq\) and \(q_{<}^2=\min(p^2,q^2)\), \(q_{>}^2=\max(p^2,q^2)\).

* For a constant boost \(\eta(\vec k)=1+\eta_0\) between \(\Lambda^2\) and \(Q^2\) with \(Q^2 \ll p^2,q^2\), the even angular moments become \(\langle e^{2n i \phi}\rangle_{\text{bias}} \approx \frac{\pi}{(N_c^2-1)A_\perp \Lambda^2} \frac{\Lambda^2}{|p^2-q^2|} \frac{q_{<}^{2n}}{q_{>}^{2n}} \frac{ \left(\frac{q^2}{p^2}+\frac{p^2}{q^2}\right)\left( \log\frac{\min(p^2,q^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2} + \left(\frac{5\eta_0}{2}+\frac{\eta_0^2}{4}\right) \log\frac{Q^2}{\Lambda^2}\right) +4\frac{q^2}{p^2}\log\frac{\min(p^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2}+4\frac{p^2}{q^2}\log\frac{\min(q^2,\ell^2)}{\Lambda^2}} {\left(\log\frac{p^2}{\Lambda^2}+ \frac{\eta_0}{2}\log\frac{Q^2}{\Lambda^2}\right) \left(\log\frac{q^2}{\Lambda^2}+ \frac{\eta_0}{2}\log\frac{Q^2}{\Lambda^2} \right)}\).

* The penalty action for a constant boost \(\eta_0\) between \(\Lambda^2\) and \(Q^2\) is \(V_{\text{eff}} = \frac{1}{8\pi} N_c^2 A_\perp \Lambda^2 \frac{Q^2-\Lambda^2}{\Lambda^2} [\eta_0 - \log(1+\eta_0)]\), and the gluon number excess is \(\Delta N_g = \frac{1}{8\pi} N_c^2 A_\perp g^4 \mu^2 \eta_0 \log\frac{Q^2}{\Lambda^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of biased ensembles in the Color Glass Condensate framework, previously applied to single-inclusive gluon production in Dumitru (2018), to double inclusive gluon production and azimuthal correlations. It generalizes the constant rescaling of the color charge density considered by Mace (2018) for multiplicity dependence of azimuthal moments in p+Pb collisions, and the enhanced saturation scale approach of Dusling (2012) for high multiplicity p+p and p+Pb events, by introducing a functional bias on the full momentum-dependent gluon distribution. The results enable future studies of how event selection criteria (e.g., high multiplicity or high transverse energy) affect angular correlations in small-\(x\) gluon production, and can be tested against experimental data from the LHC.
