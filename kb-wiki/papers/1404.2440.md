# Electromagnetic rho-meson form factors in point-form relativistic quantum mechanics

> The relativistic point-form formalism that we proposed for the study of the electroweak structure of few-body bound states is applied to calculate the elastic form factors of spin-1 mesons, such as the rho meson, within constituent-quark models. We treat electron-meson scattering as a Poincare-invariant coupled-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator and extract the meson current from the resulting invariant 1-photon-exchange amplitude. Wrong cluster properties inherent in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework are seen to cause spurious contributions in the current. These contributions, however, can be separated unambiguously from the physical ones and we end up with a meson current with all required properties. Numerical results for the rho-meson form factors are presented assuming a simple harmonic-oscillator bound-state wave function. The comparison with other approaches reveals a remarkable agreement of our results with those obtained within the covariant light-front scheme proposed by Carbonell et al. [Phys. Rep. 300, 215 (1998)].

## Key claim

The point-form relativistic quantum mechanics formalism, applied to elastic electron-meson scattering as a Poincaré-invariant coupled-channel problem, can extract a microscopic rho-meson current with all required properties despite wrong cluster properties inherent in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework; the spurious contributions from cluster-separability violation can be separated unambiguously from the physical ones, and the resulting form factors show remarkable agreement with the covariant light-front scheme of Carbonell et al. (1998).

## Method

The authors treat electron-meson scattering as a two-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas mass operator, coupling the electron-quark-antiquark and electron-quark-antiquark-photon Hilbert spaces via a field-theoretical vertex interaction adapted from spinor quantum electrodynamics. They extract the invariant one-photon-exchange amplitude from the optical potential and identify the meson current from its structure, using velocity states as a basis and assuming a simple harmonic-oscillator bound-state wave function for the confined quark-antiquark pair. The spurious contributions arising from the violation of cluster separability in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework are identified and removed to obtain a physical current with correct covariance, conservation, and charge properties.

## Key numbers

* The rho-meson form factors are calculated for a constituent-quark model with equal quark masses \(m_q = m_{\bar q}\), using a harmonic-oscillator bound-state wave function; the results are compared with the covariant light-front scheme of Carbonell et al. (1998) and show "remarkable agreement."
* The four-momentum transfer squared is defined as \(Q^2 = -\underline{q}_\mu \underline{q}^\mu\), where \(\underline{q}^\mu = (\underline{k}_\alpha' - \underline{k}_\alpha)^\mu\) is the four-momentum transferred between electron and meson.
* The invariant mass of the free quark-antiquark pair is given by \(m_{q\bar q} = \omega_{\tilde k_q} + \omega_{\tilde k_{\bar q}} = \sqrt{(\omega_{k_q} + \omega_{k_{\bar q}})^2 - (\vec k_q + \vec k_{\bar q})^2}\).
* The Mandelstam \(s\) for the electron-meson system is \(s = (p_\alpha + p_e)^2 = \left(\sqrt{m_\pi^2 + \underline{\vec k}_\alpha^2} + \sqrt{m_e^2 + \underline{\vec k}_\alpha^2}\right)^2\), and the magnitude of particle momenta in the electron-meson rest frame is \(k = |\underline{\vec k}_\alpha'| = |\vec k_e'| = |\underline{\vec k}_\alpha| = |\vec k_e|\).

## Context

This work extends the point-form relativistic quantum mechanics formalism previously applied to the pion (Biernat et al., 2009) and to heavy-light mesons (Gomez-Rocha et al., 2012) to the spin-1 case of vector mesons like the rho meson. It addresses the known cluster-separability problem in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework (Sokolov, 1977; Coester, 1982; Keister, 1991) by identifying and removing spurious contributions in the electromagnetic current, rather than applying the formal Sokolov construction. The results enable comparison with the covariant light-front approach of Carbonell et al. (1998) and Karmanov (1994), and provide a measure of cluster-separability violation for strongly bound systems like confined quark-antiquark pairs.
