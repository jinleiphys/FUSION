# Shear viscosity and electric conductivity of a hot and dense QGP with a chiral phase transition

> We calculate two transport coefficients -- the shear viscosity over entropy ratio $η/s$ and the ratio of the electric conductivity to the temperature $σ_0/T$ -- of strongly interacting quark matter within the extended $N_f=3$ Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model along the crossover transition line for moderate values of baryon chemical potential $0 \leq μ_B \leq 0.9$ GeV as well as in the vicinity of the critical endpoint (CEP) and at large baryon chemical potential $μ_B=1.2$ GeV, where the first-order phase transition takes place. The evaluation of the transport coefficients is performed on the basis of the effective Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. We employ two different methods for the calculation of the quark relaxation times: i) using the averaged transition rate defined via thermal averaged quark-quark and quark-antiquark PNJL cross sections and ii) using the 'weighted' thermal averaged quark-quark and quark-antiquark PNJL cross sections. The $η/s$ and $σ_0/T$ transport coefficients have a similar temperature and chemical potential behavior when approaching the chiral phase transition for the both methods for the quark relaxation time, however, the differences grow with increasing temperature. We demonstrate the effect of the first-order phase transition and of the CEP on the transport coefficients in the deconfined QCD medium.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity over entropy ratio and electric conductivity over temperature of strongly interacting quark matter, calculated within the PNJL model using the relaxation time approximation, show similar temperature and chemical potential behavior for two different methods of computing quark relaxation times when approaching the chiral phase transition, but the differences between the two methods grow with increasing temperature.

## Method

The authors solved the effective Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for N_f=3 quark matter within the extended Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, which includes mesonic contributions to the grand potential and a temperature-dependent rescaling of the Polyakov potential to match lattice QCD results. They computed quark relaxation times using two methods: (i) the averaged transition rate defined via thermal averaged quark-quark and quark-antiquark PNJL cross sections, and (ii) the weighted thermal averaged quark-quark and quark-antiquark PNJL cross sections. The transport coefficients were evaluated along the crossover transition line for moderate baryon chemical potentials, near the critical endpoint (CEP), and at large baryon chemical potential where the first-order phase transition occurs.

## Key numbers

* The critical endpoint (CEP) is located at (T^CEP, mu_q^CEP) = (0.11, 0.32) GeV.
* The baryon chemical potential range studied is 0 <= mu_B <= 0.9 GeV for the crossover region, with additional calculations at mu_B = 1.2 GeV for the first-order phase transition region.
* The PNJL model parameters include bare quark masses m_u = 0.005 GeV and m_s = 0.134 GeV, with a cutoff Lambda = 0.569 GeV.
* The coupling constants are G = 2.3/Lambda^2 and K = 11.0/Lambda^5.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients within the NJL model at mu_B=0 by Marty (2013) to finite baryon chemical potential using the Polyakov-extended version of the model. The results are compared with those from the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) by Moreau (2019) and Soloveva (2019), which describes the QGP using strongly interacting quasiparticles with broad spectral functions rather than the on-shell quarks and resonant mesons of the PNJL approach. The study enables exploration of how the nature of degrees-of-freedom and the presence of a critical endpoint or first-order phase transition affect transport properties of the QGP, which is relevant for interpreting results from the Beam Energy Scan programs at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
