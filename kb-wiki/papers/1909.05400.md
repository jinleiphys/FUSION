# Role of vector channel in different classes of (non) magnetized neutron stars

> We study how the magnetic field and non-standard vector channels affect hadronic, quarkionic and hybrid stars. In the hadronic phase, we use the QHD model, within its standard $σωρ$ mesons, and compare the results with the ones obtained with the inclusion of the strangeness hidden $ϕ$ meson. In the quark phase, we use the standard SU(3) NJL and compare the results with the version that takes into account the vector channel $G_v(\barψγ^μψ)$. Magnetic fields are taken into account via chaotic magnetic field approximation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the strangeness-hidden $\phi$ meson in the hadronic phase or the vector channel $G_v$ in the quark phase stiffens the equation of state and increases the maximum mass of neutron stars, but the chaotic magnetic field approximation yields only a small (1–2%) increase in maximum mass regardless of the free parameter $\gamma$, which the authors present as a feature that makes the magnetic field effects "free-parameter" independent.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model with a modified GM1 parametrization to describe hadronic matter, including hyperons with coupling constants fixed by SU(6) symmetry, and compared results with and without the $\phi$ meson. For quark matter, they used the SU(3) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and compared the standard version with one including the vector channel $G_v(\bar{\psi}\gamma^\mu\psi)$. Magnetic fields were incorporated via the chaotic magnetic field approximation, which yields an isotropic pressure contribution of $p = B^2/6$, with a density-dependent magnetic field profile $B(\epsilon) = B_0 (\epsilon_M/\epsilon_c)^\gamma + B_{surf}$ using $\gamma = 4$ and $\gamma = 6$, and $B_0 = 3 \times 10^{18}$ G.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass without magnetic field and without $\phi$ meson: 1.95 $M_\odot$, which is "slightly below the limit of the PSR J0348+0432"
- Maximum neutron star mass with magnetic field ($B_0 = 3 \times 10^{18}$ G) but without $\phi$ meson: 1.98 $M_\odot$ (for both $\gamma = 4$ and $\gamma = 6$)
- Maximum neutron star mass without magnetic field but with $\phi$ meson: 2.06 $M_\odot$
- Maximum neutron star mass with both magnetic field and $\phi$ meson: 2.07 $M_\odot$ ($\gamma = 4$) and 2.08 $M_\odot$ ($\gamma = 6$)
- Radius of canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ star: 13.63 km (zero field), 13.62 km ($\gamma = 4$), 13.58 km ($\gamma = 6$), all with $\phi$ meson
- Central energy density for maximum mass star with $\phi$ meson: 1122 MeV/fm$^3$ (zero field), 998 MeV/fm$^3$ ($\gamma = 4$), 1060 MeV/fm$^3$ ($\gamma = 6$)
- Central magnetic field for maximum mass star with $\phi$ meson: 2.66 $\times 10^{18}$ G ($\gamma = 4$) and 2.83 $\times 10^{18}$ G ($\gamma = 6$)
- Hyperon potential depth used: $U_\Lambda = -28$ MeV
- Modified GM1 slope of symmetry energy: 88 MeV (reduced from original 94 MeV); symmetry energy: 30.5 MeV (reduced from 32.5 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetars using the chaotic magnetic field approximation (Zeldovich 1960s; Lopes 2015) and the role of the $\phi$ meson in hyperonic stars (Lopes 2013; Kapusta). It compares results with more complex calculations using the LORENE code (Dex 2018; Ro 2017) and finds that the chaotic approximation yields similar small increases in maximum mass. The paper addresses the tension between the PSR J0348+0432 mass measurement (Antoniadis) and the GM1 model without $\phi$ meson, and notes that the even more massive pulsar MSP J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019) may require new formalism if its mass exceeds 2.1 $M_\odot$. The work enables future studies of hybrid stars and comparisons between hadronic and quark matter descriptions of magnetars.
