# The strangeness form factors of the proton within nonrelativistic constituent quark model revisited

> We reexamine, within the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM), a recent claim that the current data on the strangeness form factors indicates that the uuds\bar{s} component in the proton is such that the uuds subsystem has the mixed spatial symmetry [31]_X and flavor spin symmetry [4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S, with \bar{s} in S state (configuration I). We find this claim to be invalid if corrected expressions for the contributions of the transition current to G_A^s and G_E^s are used. We show that, instead, it is the lowest-lying uuds\bar{s} configuration with uuds subsystem of completely symmetric spatial symmetry [4]_X and flavor spin symmetry [4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S, with \bar{s} in P state (configuration II), which could account for the empirical signs of all form factors G_E^s, G_M^s, and G_A^s. Further, we find that removing the center-of-mass motion of the clusters will considerably enhance the contributions of the transition current. We also demonstrate that it is possible to give a reasonable description of the existing form factors data with a tiny probability P_{s\bar{s}}=0.025% for the uuds\bar{s} component. We further see that with a small admixture of configuration I, the agreement of our prediction with data for G_A^s at low-q^2 region can be markedly improved. We find that without removing CM motion, P_{s\bar{s}} would be overestimated by about a factor of four in the case when transition current dominates. We also explore the consequence of a recent estimate reached from analyzing existing data on \bar{d} -\bar{u}, s +\bar{s}, and \bar{u} + \bar{d} - s -\bar{s}, that P_{s\bar{s}} lies between 2.4-2.9%. It would lead to a large size for the five-quark system and a small bump in both G_E^s+ηG_M^s and G_E^s in the region of q^2<=0.1 GeV^2 within the considered model.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a previous assertion (Riska and Zou, 2005; Riska and Zou, 2006) that the dominant five-quark configuration in the proton has the uuds subsystem in a P state with the anti-strange quark in an S state (configuration I) is invalid when corrected expressions for the transition current contributions to the axial and electric strangeness form factors are used. Instead, they argue that the lowest-lying configuration with uuds in a completely symmetric S state and the anti-strange quark in a P state (configuration II) can account for the empirical signs of all strangeness form factors, but only if the center-of-mass motion of the quark clusters is properly removed.

## Method

The authors performed calculations within the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for the three-quark (uud) and five-quark (uuds\bar{s}) components of the proton. They evaluated both diagonal matrix elements (within the five-quark component) and non-diagonal transition matrix elements (between three-quark and five-quark components) for the strangeness electromagnetic and axial-vector form factors, with and without removal of center-of-mass motion of the quark clusters. The model parameters (oscillator parameter ω₅ and probability P_{s\bar{s}}) were varied to fit experimental data on G_E^s + η G_M^s from the G0 collaboration and G_A^s as extracted by Pate et al. (2008).

## Key numbers

- Best fit for configuration II alone: P_{s\bar{s}} = 0.025%, ω₅ = 0.469 GeV, relative phase δ_P = +1
- Best fit for admixture of configurations I and II (admixture A): P_{s\bar{s}} = 0.058%, with 8% S-state (configuration I) and 92% P-state (configuration II) admixture
- Strangeness magnetic moment μ_s = 0.066 μ_N (in units of nuclear magneton) for pure P-state configuration with P_{s\bar{s}} = 0.025%
- Strangeness contribution to proton spin ΔS = G_A^s(0) = -0.062 for pure P-state configuration with P_{s\bar{s}} = 0.025%
- Size of the uuds\bar{s} component r_{5q} = 0.5 fm for the pure P-state and admixture A fits
- For the larger P_{s\bar{s}} = 2.4% scenario (admixture B): ω₅ = 0.108 GeV, r_{5q} = 2.16 fm, with 15% S-state and 85% P-state admixture
- Without removing center-of-mass motion, P_{s\bar{s}} would be overestimated by about a factor of four when transition current dominates

## Context

This work extends the earlier constituent quark model calculations of Riska and Zou (2005) and Riska and Zou (2006) on the strangeness form factors of the proton, correcting errors in the transition current contributions and incorporating center-of-mass motion removal that was absent in those prior studies. The authors also compare with lattice QCD results from Leinweber et al. (2005, 2006) and with the phenomenological analysis of Chang and Peng (2011) who estimated P_{s\bar{s}} between 2.4-2.9% from parton distribution data. The results enable future experimental searches for a small bump in G_E^s + η G_M^s and G_E^s at very low momentum transfer (q² ≤ 0.1 GeV²), which would be a signature of a larger five-quark component.
