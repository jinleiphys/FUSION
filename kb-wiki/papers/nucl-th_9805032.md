# Hyperon properties in finite nuclei using realistic $YN$ interactions

> Single-particle energies of the $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperons are obtained from the relevant self-energies. The latter are constructed within the framework of a perturbative many-body approach employing present realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions such as the models of the Juelich and Nijmegen groups. The effects of the non-locality and energy dependence of the self-energy on the bound states are investi- gated. It is also shown that, although the single-particle hyperon energies are well reproduced by local Woods-Saxon hyperon-nucleus potentials, the wave functions from the non-local self-energy are far more extended. Implications of this behavior on the mesonic weak decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei are discused.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that single-particle energies of Lambda and Sigma hyperons in finite nuclei can be reliably obtained from a perturbative many-body approach using realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions, but only when second-order corrections are included to account for the proper energy dependence and Pauli blocking in the finite nucleus; the lowest-order Hartree-Fock results using a nuclear matter G-matrix depend strongly on the chosen starting energy and density.

## Method

The authors first solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation in nuclear matter to obtain a G-matrix from the Nijmegen soft-core and Julich B hyperon-nucleon potentials. They then construct the hyperon self-energy in finite nuclei by expanding the finite-nucleus G-matrix to second order in the nuclear matter G-matrix, including a Hartree-Fock term and a two-particle-one-hole (2p1h) correction that accounts for the difference between finite-nucleus and nuclear-matter propagators. The self-energy is inserted into a Schrodinger equation, which is solved by diagonalization in a spherical box basis to obtain bound-state energies and wave functions for various hypernuclei from Oxygen-17 to Lead-209.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda binding energy in nuclear matter from the Nijmegen interaction is -24.3 MeV at a self-consistent starting energy of -74.3 MeV; from the Julich B interaction it is -30.2 MeV at a starting energy of -80.2 MeV
- The Sigma binding energy in nuclear matter from the Nijmegen interaction is -11.7 MeV at a starting energy of 15.8 MeV; from the Julich B interaction it is -63.5 MeV at a starting energy of -36.0 MeV
- The Lambda binding energy varies at most by 10 MeV over a starting energy range of 80 MeV in nuclear matter, while the Sigma binding energy varies by twice as much
- A Woods-Saxon potential with depth V0 = -30.7 MeV, radius parameter r0 = 1.1 fm, and fixed diffusivity a reproduces the experimental Lambda single-particle energy systematics

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach of Hjorth-Jensen (1996), who derived the Lambda self-energy in Oxygen-17, to a systematic study of Lambda hypernuclei from Oxygen-17 to Lead-209 and also examines Sigma hypernuclei. The method provides an alternative to earlier calculations by Yamamoto (1985, 1990, 1992, 1994), who used Gaussian parametrizations of nuclear matter G-matrices at an average density, and by Halderson (1993), who calculated the G-matrix directly in finite nuclei. The results show that the Julich B interaction overbinds the Sigma hyperon unrealistically in nuclear matter, pointing to a need for readjustment of the interaction parameters, and that the Nijmegen soft-core interaction gives a Lambda binding energy in nuclear matter about 7 MeV weaker than the Julich model. The finding that wave functions from the non-local self-energy are more extended than those from local Woods-Saxon potentials has implications for calculations of the mesonic weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei.
