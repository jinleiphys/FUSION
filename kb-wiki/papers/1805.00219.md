# Tidal Deformability of Neutron Stars with Realistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals

> We investigate the constraints on the mass and radius of neutron stars by considering the tidal deformability in the merge of neutron star binaries. In order to extract the most reliable range of uncertainty from theory, we employ models based upon the Skyrme force and density functional theory and select models that are consistent with empirical data of finite nuclei, measured properties of nuclear matter around the saturation density, and observation of the maximum mass of neutron stars. From the selected models, we calculate the Love number $k_2$, dimensionless tidal deformability $Λ$, and mass-weighted deformability $\tildeΛ$ in the binary system. We find that all the models considered in this work give $\tildeΛ$ less than 800 which is the upper limit obtained from the measurement of GW170817. However, the model dependence of tidal deformability is manifest such that our results on the tidal deformability exhibit critical sensitivity to the size of neutron stars.

# Tidal Deformability of Neutron Stars with Realistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals

## Key claim

All five nuclear energy density functional models considered in this work, which satisfy constraints from finite nuclei, nuclear matter properties, and the maximum mass of neutron stars, predict a mass-weighted tidal deformability $\tilde{\Lambda}$ less than 800 (the upper limit from GW170817), but the model dependence is manifest such that results exhibit critical sensitivity to the size of neutron stars, with the predicted range of $\tilde{\Lambda} = 360$ to $700$ being marginal compared to the lower limit of $\tilde{\Lambda} \geq 400$ estimated from AT2017gfo.

## Method

The authors selected five nuclear energy density functional models (SkI4, SGI, SLy4, GSkI, and KIDS) based on the Skyrme force and density functional theory, applying three criteria: consistency with empirical data of finite nuclei, measured properties of nuclear matter around saturation density, and the observed maximum mass of neutron stars ($\geq 1.93 M_\odot$). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations simultaneously with the second-order differential equation for the $l=2$ tidal perturbation to compute the tidal Love number $k_2$, dimensionless tidal deformability $\Lambda$, and mass-weighted deformability $\tilde{\Lambda}$ for binary systems.

## Key numbers

- For a single neutron star with $1.4 M_\odot$, the five models predict radii in the range $11.8\ \mathrm{km} \leq R_{1.4M_\odot} \leq 12.8\ \mathrm{km}$ and dimensionless tidal deformability $308 \leq \Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) \leq 583$
- The mass-weighted tidal deformability for the binary system (fixed chirp mass $M_{\mathrm{chirp}} = 1.188 M_\odot$ and mass ratio $0.7 \leq q \leq 1.0$) is predicted in the range $360 \leq \tilde{\Lambda} \leq 700$
- Individual $\Lambda(1.4 M_\odot)$ values for the five models: GSkI = 337.2, SLy4 = 312.9, SkI4 = 490.9, SGI = 583.0, KIDS = 307.5
- Individual radii for $1.4 M_\odot$: GSkI = 11.94 km, SLy4 = 11.82 km, SkI4 = 12.46 km, SGI = 12.77 km, KIDS = 11.79 km
- The soft models (GSkI, SLy4, KIDS) predict $\Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) < 400$ while the stiff models (SkI4, SGI) predict $\Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) > 400$
- Only 16 out of 240 Skyrme force models satisfy all 11 experimental/empirical constraints on nuclear matter properties (denoted CSkP)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tidal deformability constraints from the GW170817 neutron star merger event by applying multiple nuclear physics constraints simultaneously. The authors compare their results with several recent studies: Annala et al. (2018) who constructed over 20,000 EoSs using tri-tropic and quadru-tropic functions and found $R_{1.4M_\odot}$ between 11.1 km and 13.4 km; Fattoyev et al. (2018) who used relativistic mean field models and found $\Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) < 800$ limits $R_{1.4M_\odot} < 13.76$ km; Krastev et al. (2018) who used momentum-dependent interaction models and obtained $300 \leq \tilde{\Lambda} \leq 690$; and Lim et al. (2018) who used chiral effective field theory constraints and found $375.51 \leq \Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) \leq 538.43$ at 95% confidence. The results enable tighter constraints on the equation of state of dense nuclear matter by combining gravitational wave observations with nuclear physics data.
