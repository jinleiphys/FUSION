# Prompt Photon and Inclusive $π^0$ Production at RHIC and LHC

> We present results for prompt photon and inclusive $π^0$ production in p-p and A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We include the full next-to-leading order radiative corrections and nuclear effects, such as nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss. We find the next-to-leading order corrections to be large and $p_T$ dependent. We show how measurements of $π^0$ production at RHIC and LHC, at large $p_T$, can provide valuable information about the nature of parton energy loss. We calculate the ratio of prompt photons to neutral pions and show that at RHIC energies this ratio increases with $p_T$ approaching one at $p_T \sim 10$ GeV, due to the large suppression of $π^0$ production. We show that at the LHC, this ratio has steep $p_T$ dependence and approaches 10% effect at $p_T \sim 20$ GeV.

# Prompt Photon and Inclusive π⁰ Production at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The ratio of prompt photons to neutral pions at RHIC energies increases with transverse momentum and approaches one at pT ~ 10 GeV, but this strong rise depends on the assumption of energy-dependent parton energy loss (ε = κE with κ = 0.06); under constant energy loss the ratio increases only slowly with pT, similar to the proton-proton case.

## Method

The authors calculated inclusive π⁰ and prompt photon production cross sections in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at next-to-leading order (O(αs³)) in perturbative QCD, using MRS99 parton distributions and BKK pion fragmentation functions. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, they modified the parton distributions to include nuclear shadowing effects and modified the fragmentation functions to incorporate parton energy loss, considering both constant energy loss and energy-dependent energy loss (ε = κE). They computed the nuclear modification factor RAA(pT) and the γ/π⁰ ratio as functions of transverse momentum at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), the γ/π⁰ ratio approaches one at pT ~ 10 GeV for energy-dependent energy loss with κ = 0.06.
- At the LHC, the γ/π⁰ ratio approaches 0.2 at pT = 35 GeV for energy-dependent energy loss with κ = 0.06.
- At the LHC with constant energy loss ε = 1 GeV, π⁰ suppression decreases from 80% at pT = 5 GeV to 20% at pT = 40 GeV.
- At the LHC with energy-dependent loss ε = 0.06E, π⁰ suppression increases from 70% at pT = 5 GeV to 80% at pT = 40 GeV.
- Bremsstrahlung processes contribute 24% to the prompt photon cross section at pT = 3 GeV and 6% at pT = 12 GeV at RHIC.
- At LHC energy, bremsstrahlung processes contribute 60% to prompt photon production.
- Nuclear shadowing effects are less than 10% at both RHIC and LHC.

## Context

This work extends next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations of prompt photon and pion production to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, incorporating nuclear shadowing (using the EKS98 parameterization) and parton energy loss. The results address the challenge posed by PHENIX data (2002) showing stronger-than-expected suppression of π⁰ production at large pT at RHIC, and provide predictions for separating different sources of photon production in heavy-ion collisions. The calculations enable future measurements at RHIC and LHC to distinguish between constant and energy-dependent parton energy loss mechanisms, and to identify quark-gluon plasma signatures via direct photon measurements.
