# The meson-exchange model for the $Λ\barΛ$ interaction

> In the present work, we apply the one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) model to investigate the possibility of Y(2175) and $η(2225)$ as bound states of $Λ\barΛ(^3S_1)$ and $Λ\barΛ(^1S_0)$ respectively. We consider the effective potential from the pseudoscalar $η$-exchange and $η^{'}$-exchange, the scalar $σ$-exchange, and the vector $ω$-exchange and $ϕ$-exchange. The $η$ and $η^{'}$ meson exchange potential is repulsive force for the state $^1S_0$ and attractive for $^3S_1$. The results depend very sensitively on the cutoff parameter of the $ω$-exchange ($Λ_ω$) and least sensitively on that of the $ϕ$-exchange ($Λ_ϕ$). Our result suggests the possible interpretation of Y(2175) and $η(2225)$ as the bound states of $Λ\barΛ(^3S_1)$ and $Λ\barΛ(^1S_0)$ respectively.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the experimentally observed Y(2175) and η(2225) resonances can be interpreted as S-wave bound states of a Λ and an anti-Λ (Λ\bar{Λ}) in the one-boson-exchange potential model, but only if the cutoff parameters for the exchanged mesons are tuned within specific ranges; the interpretation is conditional on the choice of these cutoff parameters, particularly the ω-exchange cutoff.

## Method

The authors applied the one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) model, previously successful for the deuteron, to the Λ\bar{Λ} system. They derived the effective potential from pseudoscalar η and η' exchange, scalar σ exchange, and vector ω and φ exchange, using coupling constants obtained from SU(3)-flavor symmetry relations to the well-known nucleon-nucleon-meson couplings. The Schrödinger equation was solved numerically using a variational method with Laguerre polynomial basis functions, introducing monopole form factors at each vertex with adjustable cutoff parameters Λ_α for each exchanged meson.

## Key numbers

- The Λ mass is taken as 1115.7 MeV from the Particle Data Group.
- The threshold for Λ\bar{Λ} is 2231.3 MeV.
- For the state Λ\bar{Λ}(^1S_0) (candidate for η(2225)), binding energies range from -7.624 MeV to -13.290 MeV with cutoffs Λ_σ=900 MeV, Λ_ω between 925-950 MeV, and Λ_φ between 1700-1800 MeV.
- For the state Λ\bar{Λ}(^3S_1) (candidate for Y(2175)), binding energies range from -50.389 MeV to -82.744 MeV with the same cutoff sets.
- The experimental binding energies would be -6.37 MeV for η(2225) and -56.37 MeV for Y(2175), both lying within the calculated ranges.
- The difference in binding energy between the two states is 43-69 MeV, consistent with the mass difference between Y(2175) and η(2225).
- The coupling constants squared over 4π are: g²_{ηΛΛ}/4π = 4.473, g²_{η'ΛΛ}/4π = 9.831, g²_{σΛΛ}/4π = 3.459, g²_{ωΛΛ}/4π = 8.889, g²_{φΛΛ}/4π = 2.222.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange framework previously applied to the deuteron by Machleidt (1987) and to heavy meson-antimeson systems by Liu (2009) to the Λ\bar{Λ} baryon-antibaryon system. It builds on an earlier study by Dover (1977) who investigated Λ\bar{Λ} bound states with orbital angular momentum L≥1. The results suggest that Y(2175) and η(2225) may be molecular Λ\bar{Λ} states rather than conventional s\bar{s} mesons, and the authors note that the terms related to the Fourier transform function ℱ₄, which were omitted in a related study of Λ_c\bar{Λ}_c by Lee (2011), significantly deepen the binding for the spin-triplet state.
