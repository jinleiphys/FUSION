# Comprehensive Study of Multi-scale Jet-medium Interaction

> We explore jet-medium interactions at various scales in high-energy heavy-ion collisions using the JETSCAPE framework. The physics of the multi-stage modeling and the coherence effect at high virtuality is discussed through the results of multiple jet and high-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$ particle observables, compared with experimental data. Furthermore, we investigate the jet-medium interaction involved in the hadronization process.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE framework, with a new virtuality-dependent jet quenching strength \(\hat{q}(Q^2)\) that incorporates coherence effects, is crucial for simultaneously describing the nuclear modification factor for inclusive jets and leading hadrons across RHIC and LHC energies; the abstract omits that this coherence effect is implemented via a specific parametrized modulation factor \(f(Q^2)\) that suppresses energy loss at high virtuality.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE multi-stage Monte Carlo framework, where the MATTER energy loss module handles high-virtuality partons and the LBT module treats nearly on-shell partons, with a switching virtuality \(Q^2_\mathrm{sw}\) between them. The effective jet quenching strength was given a virtuality dependence via \(\hat{q} = \hat{q}_\mathrm{HTL} f(Q^2)\) with \(f(Q^2)\) parametrized as a ratio of logarithmic polynomials. The medium evolution was modeled using (2+1)-D freestreaming initial conditions followed by (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNU) with TRENTo initial conditions, and hadronization was studied using the Hybrid Hadronization module that interpolates between string fragmentation and quark recombination.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor \(R_\mathrm{AA}\) for reconstructed jets and single particles was computed for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV, and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, with comparisons to ATLAS, CMS, STAR, and PHENIX data.
- The virtuality-dependent modulation factor is parametrized as \(f(Q^2) = [1+10\ln^2(Q^2_\mathrm{sw}) + 100\ln^4(Q^2_\mathrm{sw})] / [1+10\ln^2(Q^2) + 100\ln^4(Q^2)]\).
- Groomed jet observables (\(z_g\) and \(r_g\) distributions) for charged jets in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV were compared to ALICE data.
- Jet fragmentation function modification in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV was compared to ATLAS data.
- Nuclear modification factors for \(D^0\) mesons and inclusive charged hadrons were compared to CMS and ALICE data.
- For the hadronization study, a jet shower with \(E=100\) GeV in a brick medium at temperature \(T=0.3\) GeV was used, with path lengths \(L\) varied and flow velocities \(\bm{v}=(0,0,0)\) and \(\bm{v}=(0.8,0,0)\).

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework (JETSCAPE Collaboration, 2017-2022) by implementing a virtuality-dependent jet quenching strength based on the resolution scale evolution of jets (Kumar, 2019). The results demonstrate that coherence effects are significant for describing high-\(p_\mathrm{T}\) single particle data at 5.02 TeV, and that the low-virtuality phase dominates charm quark energy loss compared to light flavor partons. The hadronization study using Hybrid Hadronization (Fries, 2019) shows that longer path lengths in the medium produce more baryons due to interactions with thermal partons, and that medium flow shifts the \(\Lambda\)-to-\(K\) ratio peak. This enables future systematic studies of multi-scale jet-medium interactions across different collision systems and energies.
