# Nuclear coalescence from correlation functions

> We derive a simple formula relating the cross section for light cluster production (defined via a coalescence factor) to the two-proton correlation function measured in heavy-ion collisions. The formula generalises earlier coalescence-correlation relations found by Scheibl & Heinz and by Mrowczynski for Gaussian source models. It motivates joint experimental analyses of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) and cluster yield measurements in existing and future data sets.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general relation between the coalescence factor for light nuclear clusters and the two-proton correlation function measured in heavy-ion collisions, without requiring a detailed model of the particle emission source. The relation generalises earlier work by Scheibl and Heinz (1998) and Mrowczynski (1987-2004) that was limited to Gaussian source models, and the authors show that the earlier Gaussian-source result emerges as a special case of their more general formalism.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic quantum mechanics to express the deuteron formation probability density as an integral over the two-particle reduced density matrix of the high-excitation state produced in the collision, then relate this to the two-proton correlation function via the Wigner density of the deuteron. They convert the result to Lorentz-invariant quantities using the Cooper-Frye prescription for the freeze-out surface, obtaining a formula that expresses the coalescence factor as an integral of the correlation function weighted by the deuteron probability density in momentum space. The derivation is then extended to clusters with mass number A greater than 2 using a Gaussian approximation for the cluster wave function.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron wave function Gaussian width: d = 3.2 fm, used for the analytic comparison with Scheibl and Heinz (1998)
- Nucleon mass: m ≈ 0.94 GeV, used in the coalescence factor formula
- The ratio B_3(p-p)/B_3(Pb-Pb) ≈ 4 × 10^3, predicted from the HBT radius difference (R ~ 1 fm in p-p versus R ~ 4 fm in Pb-Pb) and subsequently confirmed by ALICE measurements
- The approximation of neglecting ±q inside the Wigner functions is stated to be valid to ~10% accuracy for Pb-Pb collisions
- The correlation function and deuteron Wigner density both cut off at |q| ~ 0.1 m, where m is the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the coalescence-correlation relation previously derived by Scheibl and Heinz (1998) for Gaussian source models, and connects to a series of papers by Mrowczynski (1987-2004) who proposed a quantum mechanical sum rule relating the deuteron coalescence factor to the neutron-proton correlation function. The authors note that their result is less ambitious than Mrowczynski's sum rule because the correlation function in their formula must be corrected for final-state interactions, but it avoids the large-q contributions that plagued the earlier sum rule approach. The work enables joint experimental analyses of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) and cluster yield measurements in existing and future data sets from the LHC, and provides a theoretical basis for understanding why the simple Gaussian-source formula appears to work across systems ranging from Pb-Pb to p-p collisions.
