# Hadron resonance gas and nonperturbative QCD vacuum at finite temperature

> We study the nonperturbative QCD vacuum with two light quarks at finite temperature in the framework of hadron resonance gas. Temperature dependence of the quark and gluon condensates in the confined phase are obtained. We demonstrate that the quark condensate and one half (chromo-electric component) of gluon condensate evaporate at the same temperature, which corresponds to the temperature of quark-hadron phase transition. Critical temperature is T_c~190 MeV when temperature shift of hadron masses is taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the hadron resonance gas model for QCD with two light quarks, the quark condensate and exactly one half of the gluon condensate (the chromo-electric component) vanish at the same temperature, identified as the quark-hadron phase transition temperature, but only when the temperature shift of hadron masses is included; without this shift, both condensates evaporate at a higher temperature of about 215 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the confined phase as a hadron resonance gas, computing the total pressure from a sum over relativistic Bose and Fermi gases of all known hadron states with masses below 2.5 GeV for mesons and 3.0 GeV for baryons (2078 states total). They use the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and a renormalization-group-derived formula to extract the temperature dependence of the quark condensate and gluon condensate from the pressure. Hadron masses are treated as functions of the pion mass using a lattice-inspired five-parameter formula, and a temperature-dependent mass shift is incorporated via a dilaton field scaling factor.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature with temperature shift of hadron masses: \(T_c \simeq 190\) MeV.
- Critical temperature without temperature shift of hadron masses: \(T \sim 215\) MeV.
- Energy density at the phase transition: \(\varepsilon_h(T_c) \sim 1.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The temperature at which energy density reaches 1 GeV/fm\(^3\): \(T \simeq 175\) MeV.
- Pion decay constant used: \(F_{\pi} = 93\) MeV.
- String tension used: \(\sigma = (0.42\ \text{GeV})^2\).
- Zero-temperature gluon condensate range considered: \(\langle G^2 \rangle_0 = 0.5 \div 1.0\) GeV\(^4\).
- Temperature shift of hadron masses used in calculations: 16% (\(\chi_T/\chi_0 = 0.84\)).

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model, originally proposed by Hagedorn, to study the nonperturbative QCD vacuum, building on lattice results from Karsch (2000) and Nakamura (2003) that place the critical temperature for two-flavor QCD at 175–190 MeV. It confirms the "evaporation model" predictions of Simonov and the effective dilaton Lagrangian approach of Agasian (1999), showing that only the chromo-electric component of the gluon condensate vanishes at the phase transition while the chromo-magnetic component persists, consistent with lattice findings by D'Elia (2002) and the magnetic confinement picture. The results enable a unified quantitative description of the quark condensate, gluon condensate, and energy density at the phase transition within a single thermodynamic framework.
