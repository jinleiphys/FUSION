# Strong interactions: From methods to structures

> These are the proceedings of the workshop on "Strong interactions: From methods to structures" held at the Physikzentrum Bad Honnef of the Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft, Bad Honnef, Germany from February 12 to 16, 2011. The workshop concentrated on physics of cold atoms, chiral perturbation theory for mesons and baryons, chiral dynamics in few-baryon systems and effective field theories for systems with heavy quarks. Included are a short contribution per talk.

## Key claim

This paper presents the proceedings of a workshop on effective field theory methods for strongly interacting systems, covering cold atoms, chiral perturbation theory, few-baryon systems, and heavy quark physics; as a conference proceedings it summarizes presented talks rather than advancing a single novel claim.

## Method

The workshop brought together approximately 72 participants, with 47 presenting 35-minute talks covering cold-atom physics, chiral perturbation theory for mesons and baryons, chiral dynamics in few-baryon systems, effective field theories for heavy quarks, and some lattice QCD developments. The proceedings include a short abstract per talk with references, following the format of previous workshops in this series held in 1988, 1991, 1993, 1996, 1998, 2001, 2004, and 2006.

- The chemical potential for the unitary Fermi gas was measured as mu/epsilon_F = 0.429(7), independent of Delta mu within uncertainties for Delta mu/epsilon_F < 0.2.
- For the spin-balanced gas at T = T_c, the preliminary contact value was C(T_c) = 0.1102(11) epsilon_F^2 V = 3.26(3) k_F N.

## Context

This workshop continued a series of meetings on effective field theory methods for strongly interacting systems, following previous workshops in Ringberg (1988), Dobogoko (1991), Karrebaeksminde (1993), Trento (1996), and Bad Honnef (1998, 2001, 2004, 2006). The proceedings cover work extending approaches such as the operator product expansion applied to fermionic systems by Braaten and Platter (2008) and bosonic systems by Braaten, Kang, and Platter (2011), as well as functional renormalization group studies of few-body systems by Birse, Krippa, and Walet (2011) and Floerchinger, Schmidt, Moroz, and Wetterich (2009). The meeting enabled cross-fertilization between cold-atom, nuclear, and particle physics communities using effective field theory methods.
