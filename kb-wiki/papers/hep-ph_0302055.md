# Dynamical renormalization group approach to relaxation in quantum field theory

> The real time evolution and relaxation of expectation values of quantum fields and of quantum states are computed as initial value problems by implementing the dynamical renormalization group (DRG).Linear response is invoked to set up the renormalized initial value problem to study the dynamics of the expectation value of quantum fields. The perturbative solution of the equations of motion for the field expectation values of quantum fields as well as the evolution of quantum states features secular terms, namely terms that grow in time and invalidate the perturbative expansion for late times. The DRG provides a consistent framework to resum these secular terms and yields a uniform asymptotic expansion at long times. Several relevant cases are studied in detail, including those of threshold infrared divergences which appear in gauge theories at finite temperature and lead to anomalous relaxation. In these cases the DRG is shown to provide a resummation akin to Bloch-Nordsieck but directly in real time and that goes beyond the scope of Bloch-Nordsieck and Dyson resummations. The nature of the resummation program is discussed in several examples. The DRG provides a framework that is consistent, systematic and easy to implement to study the non-equilibrium relaxational dynamics directly in real time that does not rely on the concept of quasiparticle widths.

## Key claim

The dynamical renormalization group (DRG) provides a consistent, systematic framework to resum secular terms (terms that grow in time and invalidate perturbative expansions at late times) in the real-time evolution of quantum field expectation values and quantum states, yielding a uniform asymptotic expansion at long times; this approach goes beyond the scope of Bloch-Nordsieck and Dyson resummations, particularly for threshold infrared divergences in gauge theories at finite temperature where those standard methods fail.

## Method

The authors formulate the real-time evolution of the expectation value of a quantum field as a fully renormalized initial value problem using linear response and the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism. They solve the resulting integro-differential equation of motion (which includes the retarded self-energy) via Laplace transform, obtaining a non-perturbative Dyson-resummed solution. The perturbative expansion of this solution is then examined for late times, revealing secular (logarithmically growing) terms; the dynamical renormalization group is applied to resum these secular terms order by order, extracting the asymptotic long-time behavior directly in real time.

## Key numbers

* For a stable particle with a production threshold at \(\omega_{th}\), the cut contribution to the field expectation value decays as \(1/t^{\alpha+1}\) at late times, where \(\alpha\) is the threshold exponent (e.g., \(\alpha = 1/2\) for two massive bosonic particles).
* For a resonance (quasiparticle) in weak coupling, the field expectation value decays exponentially as \(e^{-\Gamma_k t}\) with width \(\Gamma_k = \pi \rho_k(\omega_k)/(2\omega_k) + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)\).
* For a model with a massless particle emission (threshold infrared divergence), the Bloch-Nordsieck resummation yields asymptotic power-law relaxation \(\phi_k(t) \sim \phi_k(0) (2/(\omega_k t))^\lambda \cos(\omega_k t + \pi\lambda/2)/\Gamma(1-\lambda)\) for \(mt \gg 1\), where \(\lambda = g^2/(2\pi m)^2\) is the dimensionless coupling.
* The perturbative expansion of this power-law result gives secular terms of the form \(\phi_k(t) = \phi_k(0)\cos(\omega_k t)[1 - \lambda \ln(\omega_k t) + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)]\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical renormalization group approach previously introduced by the same authors (Boyanovsky and de Vega, 2002) for studying relaxation in high-temperature gauge theories, including electrical conductivity in QED. It provides a real-time alternative to standard kinetic equations based on quasiparticle widths and to the Bloch-Nordsieck resummation of propagators (Weinberg, 1965), which fails for the more severe infrared divergences that appear at finite temperature (Blaizot and Iancu, 1997). The DRG method enables systematic treatment of non-exponential relaxation in gauge theories, condensed matter systems (mesoscopic transport, X-ray edge singularities), and cold dense QCD.
