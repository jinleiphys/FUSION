# Study on the $ϕ$-meson photoproduction off the proton target with the pentaquark-like $K^*Σ$ bound state $P_s$

> We utilize the effective Lagrangian method within the tree-level Born approximation to explore $ϕ$-meson photoproduction, i.e., $γp \to ϕp$. Our analysis encompasses contributions from various sources, including the Pomeron, $f_1$-Regge, pseudoscalar particles ($π$, $η$), scalar particles ($a_0$, $f_0$), protons, and three-nucleon resonance states. In addition, we consider a possible pentaquark-like $K^* Σ$-bound state $P_s$. The findings indicate that, apart from the region near the threshold, contributions other than the Pomeron generally have a limited impact on the total cross section. However, at specific angles, alternative contributions become crucial, particularly at smaller values of ${\rm cos}\,θ$. The incorporation of $P_s$ and other nucleon resonances proves essential to elucidate the bump observed near $W \sim 2.15$ GeV at very forward angles and behaviors within the range of $W=(2.0-2.3)$ GeV. Furthermore, in the region with $W\ge 2.5$ GeV, where nucleon resonances become negligible, contributions from the t-channel mesons become pivotal. Our calculations for spin density matrix components, examined in various frames, exhibit improvement when considering all contributions. This comprehensive approach successfully reproduces the observed bump by including $P_s$. We also briefly estimate the $P_s$ production via $ϕ$-meson photoproduction in the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), resulting in the luminosity of 10 fb$^{-1}$ per month.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a pentaquark-like $K^*\Sigma$ bound state $P_s$ with $J^P=3/2^-$ and mass 2.21 GeV, together with two nucleon resonances, is essential to reproduce the bump structure observed near $W \sim 2.15$ GeV in $\phi$-meson photoproduction, though the Pomeron exchange alone explains most of the total cross section except near threshold.

## Method

The authors employed the effective Lagrangian method within the tree-level Born approximation to compute the invariant amplitude for $\gamma p \to \phi p$, including contributions from Pomeron exchange, Reggeized $f_1(1285)$ axial-vector meson exchange, pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $\eta$) and scalar ($a_0(980)$, $f_0(980)$) meson exchanges, direct $\phi$-radiation via the proton, and three nucleon resonance states: $N^*(2000,5/2^+)$, $N^*(2300,1/2^+)$, and the pentaquark-like $K^*\Sigma$ bound state $P_s$ with $J^P=3/2^-$. The model parameters were determined by fitting to experimental total and differential cross section data from CLAS and other experiments, as well as spin-density matrix elements (SDMEs).

## Key numbers

- Mass of $P_s$: 2.21 GeV, total width: 28 MeV (compared to original coupled-channel values of 2.071 GeV and 14 MeV)
- Pomeron strength factor $C_\mathbb{P} = 6.5$ (set to account for high-energy cross section behavior)
- Strong coupling constant for $P_s \to \phi N$: $g_{P_s \phi N} = 0.14 + i\,0.2$
- Electromagnetic coupling $eh_1$ for $P_s$: $0.00858 + i\,0.01226$
- Decay width of $N^*(2000)$: 200 MeV; $N^*(2300)$: 300 MeV
- Coupling constants for meson exchanges: $g_{f_1 \gamma \phi}=0.17$ GeV$^{-2}$, $g_{\pi \gamma \phi}=-0.14$, $g_{\eta \gamma \phi}=-0.71$, $g_{a_0 \gamma \phi}=-0.77$, $g_{f_0 \gamma \phi}=-2.44$
- Estimated luminosity for $P_s$ production at the future Electron-Ion Collider: 10 fb$^{-1}$ per month

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of $\phi$-meson photoproduction that considered nucleon resonances with masses near the $K^*\Sigma$ threshold (Kiswandhi 2010, Kiswandhi 2011) but treated couplings as free parameters. It builds on the prediction of a $K^*\Sigma$ molecular bound state from a unitarized coupled-channel meson-baryon interaction model (Khemchandani 2011) and on previous hidden-strangeness production studies (Nam 2021). The results enable future experimental searches for the $P_s$ pentaquark-like state at the Electron-Ion Collider and provide a framework for understanding the bump structure near $W \sim 2.15$ GeV observed by the CLAS Collaboration (Dey 2014).
