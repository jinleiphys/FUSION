# Gamow-Teller (GT$\pm$) strength distributions of $^{56}Ni$ for ground and excited states

> Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions play an important and consequential role in many astrophysical phenomena. These include, but are not limited to, electron and positron capture rates which determine the fate of massive stars and play an intricate role in the dynamics of core collapse. These $GT_{\pm}$ transitions rates are the significant inputs in the description of supernova explosions. $GT_{\pm}$ strength function values are sensitive to the $^{56}Ni$ core excitation in the middle \textit{pf}-shell region and to the size of the model space as well. We used the pn-QRPA theory for extracting the GT strength for ground and excited states of $^{56}Ni$. We then used these GT strength distributions to calculate the electron \textit{and} positron capture rates which show differences with the earlier calculations. One curious finding of this paper is our enhanced electron capture rates on $^{56}Ni$ at presupernova temperatures. These differences need to be taken into account for the modeling of the early stages of Type II supernova evolution.

# Gamow-Teller (GT±) Strength Distributions of ⁵⁶Ni for Ground and Excited States

## Key claim

The authors report that their pn-QRPA calculations yield enhanced electron capture rates on ⁵⁶Ni at presupernova temperatures compared to earlier shell-model calculations, primarily because they include microscopic GT strength distributions from 30 excited states of the parent nucleus rather than only from the ground state.

## Method

The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with a Nilsson model basis and BCS pairing to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for both GT+ and GT- transitions from the ground state and 30 excited states of ⁵⁶Ni up to 10 MeV excitation energy. They used these strength distributions to perform state-by-state calculations of electron and positron capture rates, summing over all parent and daughter states (approximately 200 states in each daughter nucleus, ⁵⁶Co and ⁵⁶Cu, up to 45 MeV excitation) until convergence was achieved. The calculations assumed only allowed Gamow-Teller and superallowed Fermi transitions, with electrons and positrons treated as a Fermi gas and neutrino escape assumed to be free.

## Key numbers

- Total GT+ strength for the ground state of ⁵⁶Ni: 8.9, compared to 10.1 from Langanke and Martinez-Pineda (2003) and 9.8 ± 4 from shell model Monte Carlo calculations.
- Total GT- strength for the ground state of ⁵⁶Ni: 7.4, compared to 11.4 from a prior calculation by Nabi and Rahman (2005).
- GT+ centroid energy for the ground state of ⁵⁶Ni: approximately 5.7 MeV in daughter ⁵⁶Co; for the first excited state: approximately 7.9 MeV; for the second excited state: approximately 11.4 MeV.
- GT- centroid energy for the ground state of ⁵⁶Ni: approximately 4.7 MeV in daughter ⁵⁶Cu; for the first excited state: approximately 7.6 MeV; for the second excited state: approximately 8.6 MeV.
- Electron capture rates converge to approximately 500 s⁻¹ at T₉ = 30 for densities in the range 10⁰·⁵ to 10⁸ g/cm³.
- At densities near 10¹¹ g/cm³, electron capture rates remain constant until the core temperature approaches log T = 10 K.
- Positron capture rates decline by 3 orders of magnitude when density shifts from 10⁷ to 10¹¹ g/cm³ at log T = 10.5.
- The pn-QRPA model space extended up to 7ħω; the particle-hole force constant χ = 0.5 MeV and particle-particle force constant κ = 0.065 MeV were used for ⁵⁶Ni.

## Context

This work extends earlier pn-QRPA calculations of electron capture rates on ⁵⁶Ni by Nabi and Rahman (2005) and provides the first presentation of GT strength distributions from both parent and excited states of ⁵⁶Ni. The results differ from the parameterized rates of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) (1982), who placed the GT+ resonance at 3.8 MeV in ⁵⁶Co, and from the large-scale shell model calculations of Langanke and Martinez-Pineda (2003), who found the GT+ centroid around 2.5-3.0 MeV. Aufderheide et al. (1994) ranked ⁵⁶Ni among the top ten nuclei affecting the lepton-to-baryon ratio via electron capture, and the authors suggest that their enhanced rates could influence core-collapse supernova simulations, potentially favoring a prompt explosion mechanism.
