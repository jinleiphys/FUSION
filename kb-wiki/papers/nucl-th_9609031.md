# Elimination of the vacuum instability for finite nuclei in the relativistic $σ$-$ω$ model

> The $σ$-$ω$ model of nuclei is studied at leading order in the $1/N$ expansion thereby introducing the self consistent Hartree approximation, the Dirac sea corrections and the one fermion loop meson self energies in a unified way. For simplicity, the Dirac sea is further treated within a semiclassical expansion to all orders. The well-known Landau pole vacuum instability appearing in this kind of theories is removed by means of a scheme recently proposed in this context. The effect of such removal on the low momentum effective parameters of the model, relevant to describe nuclear matter and finite nuclei, is analyzed. The one fermion loop meson self energies are found to have a sizeable contribution to these parameters. However, such contribution turns out to come mostly from the Landau poles and is thus spurious. We conclude that the fermionic loop can only be introduced consistently in the $σ$-$ω$ nuclear model if the Landau pole problem is dealt with properly.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the vacuum instability (Landau ghost) in the relativistic σ-ω model can be removed using Redmond's prescription without altering the perturbative content of the theory, and that the one-fermion-loop meson self-energies contribute sizeably to low-momentum effective parameters, but this contribution is mostly spurious because it originates from the Landau poles rather than from physically meaningful physics.

## Method

The authors study the σ-ω model at leading order in the 1/N expansion, which unifies the self-consistent Hartree approximation, Dirac sea corrections, and one-fermion-loop meson self-energies. The Dirac sea is treated within a semiclassical (gradient) expansion to all orders, and the Landau pole vacuum instability is removed using Redmond's prescription, which adds a non-local counterterm to the effective action that cancels the ghost term in the meson propagator. The effect of this removal on the low-momentum effective parameters is analyzed by expanding the ghost-subtraction kernel in a power series in momentum squared around zero, keeping only the zeroth- and second-order terms, which are then used to reparameterize the zero-momentum masses and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The Landau ghost instability occurs at a length scale of 0.2 fm, comparable to the nucleon size and mass.
- The ghost residue R_G scales as R_G ~ g^{-2} and the ghost mass squared M_G^2 scales as M_G^2 ~ 4M^2 exp(4π^2/g^2) in the weak coupling regime, where M is the nucleon mass and g is the fermion-meson coupling constant.
- The fourth and higher orders in the gradient expansion of the ghost-subtraction energy ΔE are numerically negligible compared to zeroth- and second-order terms for typical mesonic profiles, with squared gradients suppressed by a factor (R M_G)^{-2}, where R is the nuclear radius.
- The zero-momentum masses and coupling constants after ghost removal are given by: ŵ{m}^2 = m^2 M_G^2 (M_G^2 - m^2 R_G) / (M_G^4 + m^4 R_G) and ŵ{g}^2 = g^2 (M_G^2 - m^2 R_G)^2 / (M_G^4 + m^4 R_G).

## Context

This work extends the earlier application of Redmond's prescription to the σ-ω model for nuclear matter by Tanaka (1990, 1991) and Tanaka and Bentz (1992), and applies it for the first time to finite nuclei within a self-consistent mean-field description including Dirac sea effects. The paper builds on the semiclassical expansion of the Dirac sea energy developed by Caro et al. (1996) and addresses the vacuum instability problem identified by Perry (1987) and Cohen (1987). The results enable a consistent treatment of the fermionic loop in the σ-ω model for finite nuclei, removing the spurious contributions from Landau poles and allowing meaningful calculations of nuclear binding energies and charge radii.
