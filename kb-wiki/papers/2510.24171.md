# Diffractive deep inelastic scattering in the dipole picture: the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution in exact kinematics

> We compute the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution to the diffractive structure functions in high-energy deep inelastic scattering. The obtained result corresponds to a finite part of the next-to-leading-order contribution to the diffractive cross section. Previous phenomenological applications have included this contribution only in the high-$Q^2$ or high-$M_X^2$ limits in the case of a soft gluon, and we numerically demonstrate that these existing estimates do not provide a good approximation for the full $q\bar{q}g$ contribution. Furthermore, we demonstrate that in addition to the soft gluon contribution, there is an equally important soft quark contribution to the diffractive structure functions at high $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical implementation of the $q\bar{q}g$ contribution to the diffractive cross section in exact eikonal kinematics, demonstrating that previous phenomenological estimates using the high-$Q^2$ or high-$M_X^2$ limits do not provide a good approximation for the full result, and that a soft quark contribution is equally important as the previously considered soft gluon contribution at high $Q^2$.

## Method

The authors compute the "trip" contribution to the diffractive structure functions, where the $q\bar{q}g$ system interacts with the target shockwave, using the NLO light-cone wavefunctions derived in Beuf (2022) and Beuf (2024). The calculation involves a high-dimensional phase space integral over the transverse coordinates of the three partons in both the amplitude and conjugate amplitude, with the constraint that the invariant mass of the final state is fixed, using a simplified GBW model for the dipole-target scattering amplitude with a fixed saturation scale and a hard-sphere impact parameter profile.

## Key numbers

* Saturation scale used in the GBW model: $Q_s^2 = 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
* Target transverse size parameter: $R_A = \sqrt{2B}$ with $B = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}$
* The soft gluon (Wusthoff) result is derived in the limit where the gluon carries lightcone momentum fraction $z_3 \lesssim Q_s^2/Q^2$
* The soft quark contribution is derived in the limit where the quark carries lightcone momentum fraction $z_1 \sim Q_s^2/Q^2 \ll 1$

## Context

This work extends the NLO CGC calculations of diffractive DIS by Beuf (2022) and Beuf (2024) from formal derivations to numerical implementation, and directly tests the accuracy of the approximate Wusthoff result used in phenomenology by Kowalski (2008), Marquet (2007), and others. The numerical framework developed here enables future comparisons with HERA diffractive structure function data and predictions for the EIC, and represents a step toward full NLO CGC phenomenology for diffractive processes.
