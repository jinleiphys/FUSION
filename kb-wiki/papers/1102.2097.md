# Quark-anti-quark potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes on lattice

> Quark--anti-quark (anti-q-q) potentials with finite quark masses are studied from the anti-q-q Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions in quenched lattice QCD. With the use of a method which has been recently developed in the derivation of nuclear forces from lattice QCD, we derive the anti-q-q potentials from the NBS wave functions. We calculate the anti-q-q NBS wave functions in pseudo-scalar and vector channels for several quark masses. The derived potentials at each quark mass in both channels show linear plus Coulomb form. We also discuss the quark-mass and channel dependence of the anti-q-q potentials.

## Key claim

The paper derives quark-antiquark potentials for finite quark masses from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions in quenched lattice QCD, assuming that the equal-time NBS amplitudes satisfy a Schrodinger-type equation with constant quark masses taken as half the vector meson mass, rather than using a rigorous reduction formula which is inapplicable due to the absence of asymptotic fields for confined quarks.

## Method

The authors computed equal-time NBS wave functions for quark-antiquark systems in pseudo-scalar and vector channels on a 32^3 x 48 quenched lattice at beta = 6.0 (lattice spacing 0.104 fm) using Wilson fermions with four hopping parameters. They extracted the leading-order local potential from the Schrodinger-type equation V(r) = (1/(2 mu)) (nabla^2 phi(r) / phi(r)) + E, where the NBS wave function phi(r) was obtained from the spatial correlation of four-point functions after projection onto S-wave states using the cubic rotational group. The quark mass was set to half the vector meson mass for each hopping parameter.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.104 fm, volume (3.3 fm)^3
- Four hopping parameters: kappa = 0.1520, 0.1480, 0.1420, 0.1320
- Corresponding pseudo-scalar meson masses: 0.94, 1.27, 1.77, 2.53 GeV
- Corresponding vector meson masses: 1.04, 1.35, 1.81, 2.55 GeV
- String tension from individual fits for heaviest quark (kappa=0.1320): 819 (47) MeV/fm in pseudo-scalar channel, 825 (48) MeV/fm in vector channel
- String tension from universal fit (mass-independent): 723 (30) MeV/fm
- Coulomb coefficient A for heaviest quark (kappa=0.1320): 215 (7) MeV*fm in pseudo-scalar channel, 195 (7) MeV*fm in vector channel
- Coulomb coefficient A for lightest quark (kappa=0.1520): 443 (5) MeV*fm in pseudo-scalar channel, 291 (13) MeV*fm in vector channel
- Root mean square radius for heaviest quark system: 0.3550 (14) fm

## Context

This work extends the method developed for nuclear forces from lattice QCD by Ishii (2007) and Aoki (2010) to quark-antiquark systems with finite quark masses, in contrast to the static quark-antiquark potential obtained from Wilson loops by Bali (2001) and TTT (2000) which assumes infinitely heavy quarks. The derived potentials show the same linear-plus-Coulomb form as the static potential, and the results enable future studies of the energy dependence of the local potential to check convergence of the velocity expansion, as well as applications to systems with lighter quarks where relativistic effects become important.
