# Trial wave functions with long-range Coulomb correlations for two-dimensional N-electron systems in high magnetic fields

> A new class of analytic wave functions is derived for two dimensional N-electron (2 <= N < infinity) systems in high magnetic fields. These functions are constructed through breaking (at the Hartree-Fock level) and subsequent restoration (via post-Hartree-Fock methods) of the circular symmetry. They are suitable for describing long-range Coulomb correlations, while the Laughlin and composite-fermion functions describe Jastrow correlations associated with a short-range repulsion. Underlying our approach is a collectively-rotating-electron-molecule picture, yielding for all N an oscillatory radial electron density.

# Trial wave functions with long-range Coulomb correlations for two-dimensional N-electron systems in high magnetic fields

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic wave functions that capture long-range Coulomb correlations in two-dimensional electron systems under high magnetic fields, producing oscillatory radial electron densities that agree with exact diagonalization results. However, the approach is specifically constructed for the limit where the confining potential can be neglected compared to the confinement induced by the magnetic field, and the wave functions are nonzero only for special values of the total angular momentum determined by the ring structure of the electron molecule.

## Method

The authors break circular symmetry at the unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF) level to obtain single-determinantal wave functions representing electron molecules arranged in concentric rings, then restore rotational symmetry via projection techniques to produce multi-determinantal wave functions. The UHF orbitals are approximated by analytic Gaussian functions centered at positions forming concentric regular polygons, and the final symmetry-preserving wave functions are expressed as sums over Slater determinants with coefficients given by products of sine functions of the single-particle angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- The minimum angular momentum for an N-electron system is L0 = N(N-1)/2, with special allowed values given by L = N(N-1)/2 + Σ n_q k_q for ring configuration (n_1, n_2, ..., n_r), where k_q = 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
- For N=6 and L=20, the underlying ring structure is (1,5) with L 5 units above L0=15, producing an electron density with a maximum at the origin followed by an outer hump
- For N=6 and L=21, the underlying structure is (0,6) with L 6 units above L0, producing a dip at the origin and a single outer hump
- For N=6, L=75 and N=6, L=105, the fractional fillings are 1/5 and 1/7 respectively
- For N=12, L=123, the ring structure is (3,9); for N=16, L=205, the ring structure is (1,5,10)
- The largest number of electrons for which the ring structure has been determined is N=230 with arrangement (1,6,12,18,23,25,34,37,37,37)

## Context

This work extends the rotating-electron-molecule (REM) picture previously developed by Yannouleas and Landman (2000, 2002) for quantum dots over the whole magnetic-field range. It directly addresses the failure of Laughlin (1983) and composite-fermion (Jain, 1990) wave functions to capture oscillatory radial electron densities found in exact diagonalization studies by Goldman and Tsiper (2001) for fractional quantum Hall systems at filling factor ν=1/3. The REM wave functions provide an alternative interpretation of the fractional quantum Hall effect hierarchy as originating from magic angular momenta of rotating electron molecules, and can be generalized to any number of electrons and fractional fillings.
