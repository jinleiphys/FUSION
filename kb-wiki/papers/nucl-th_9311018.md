# Pion Properties in a Hot $ΠN Δ$ Gas

> Based on a recent meson-exchange model for the vacuum $ππ$ interaction we compute selfconsistently the in-medium $ππ$ scattering amplitude and pion selfenergy in a hot $πNΔ$ gas. The contributions to the pion selfenergy are calculated from the $ππ$ T-matrix as well as from p-wave interaction with nucleons and thermally abundant $Δ$'s. Results are presented for two scenarios believed to be realized in the relativistic heavy ion collisions performed at the GSI-SIS and the CERN-SpS. Possible implications for the observed soft pion enhancement at both SIS and SpS are indicated.

## Key claim

The paper computes self-consistent in-medium modifications of pion properties in a hot gas of pions, nucleons, and Delta resonances, but the results are conditional on the quasiparticle approximation (QPA) whose validity is limited to small pion widths and may break down at baryon densities above approximately 1.5 times nuclear saturation density or at high pion chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors start from a meson-exchange model for the vacuum pi-pi interaction (Lohse et al.) and solve a self-consistent Brueckner-type problem by iterating the in-medium pi-pi T-matrix equation (Lippmann-Schwinger type with Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction) together with the pion selfenergy. The pion selfenergy receives two contributions: one from the pi-pi T-matrix in the pion gas and one from p-wave interactions with nucleons and Delta resonances, the latter computed using finite-temperature Lindhard functions with short-range spin-isospin correlations (Migdal parameters). The self-consistency involves the quasiparticle dispersion relation and the in-medium two-pion propagator, which includes Bose enhancement factors.

## Key numbers

* At T=100 MeV in chemical equilibrium, every fourth nucleon is excited into a Delta resonance.
* For temperatures T >= 170 MeV, Delta resonances are already in the majority relative to nucleons.
* The free-space Delta width is 115 MeV at the resonant momentum k_res = 297 MeV/c.
* Under SIS conditions (T=75 MeV, rho_B = 2 rho_0 with rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}), the pion density is 0.01 fm^{-3} for mu_pi = 0.
* Under SpS conditions (T=150 MeV, rho_B = 0.5 rho_0), increasing the pion chemical potential from 0 to 100 MeV increases the pion density from 0.12 fm^{-3} to 0.27 fm^{-3}.
* The rho-resonance shows an upward shift of about 70-90 MeV under SIS conditions.
* The in-medium pion mass decreases by nearly 20 MeV at SpS conditions with mu_pi = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent analysis of a thermal pion gas (Rapp and Wambach, 1993) by including nucleons and thermally excited Delta resonances, motivated by the "soft pion puzzle" observed in heavy-ion collisions at GSI-SIS and CERN-SpS. The approach builds on the meson-exchange model for pi-pi interaction by Lohse et al. and on the finite-temperature particle-hole model of Ericson and Weise (1988). The results enable quantitative studies of pion spectra and resonance modifications in hot hadronic matter, and the authors note that the attraction in the pion mean field from Delta N^{-1} excitations is a promising candidate to explain the low-p_T enhancement at SIS, as pointed out by Koch and Bertsch.
