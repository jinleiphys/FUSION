# Reexamination of $^{6}$Li scattering as a Probe to Investigate the Isoscalar Giant Resonances in Nuclei

> Inelastic ${}^{6}$Li scattering at 100 MeV/u on ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{93}$Nb have been measured with the high-resolution magnetic spectrometer Grand Raiden. The magnetic-rigidity settings of the spectrometer covered excitation energies from 10 to 40 MeV and scattering angles in the range $0^\circ < θ_{\text{lab.}}< 2^\circ$. The isoscalar giant monopole resonance was selectively excited in the present data. Measurements free of instrumental background and the very favorable resonance-to-continuum ratio of ${}^{6}$Li scattering allowed for precise determination of the $E0$ strengths in ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{93}$Nb. It was found that the monopole strength in ${}^{12}$C exhausts $52 \pm 3^\text{(stat.)} \pm 8 ^\text{(sys.)}$\% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), which is considerably higher than results from previous $α$-scattering experiments. The monopole strength in ${}^{93}$Nb exhausts $92 \pm 4^\text{(stat.)} \pm 10 ^\text{(sys.)}$\% of the EWSR, and it is consistent with measurements of nuclei with mass number of $A\approx90$. Such comparison indicates that the isoscalar giant monopole resonance distributions in these nuclei are very similar, and no influence due to nuclear structure was observed.

## Key claim

Inelastic scattering of 100 MeV/u \(^{6}\)Li on \(^{12}\)C and \(^{93}\)Nb, free of instrumental background and with a favorable resonance-to-continuum ratio, enables a precise extraction of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) strength; the monopole strength in \(^{12}\)C is found to be considerably higher than previous \(\alpha\)-scattering results, and the \(^{93}\)Nb results confirm that nuclear structure does not significantly affect the ISGMR centroid energy in the \(A\approx90\) region.

## Method

Inelastic \(^{6}\)Li scattering at 100 MeV/u on \(^{12}\)C and \(^{93}\)Nb targets was measured using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka University, covering excitation energies from 10 to 40 MeV and laboratory scattering angles from \(0^\circ\) to \(2^\circ\). A multipole-decomposition analysis (MDA) was performed using distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with the code CHUCK3, employing a double-folding formalism with the M3Y-Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction and a density-dependent term (BDM3Y1). The isovector giant dipole resonance contribution (below 10% of the total cross section) was subtracted before the MDA, and the instrumental background was absent in the measurements, eliminating the need for side-band analysis or background parameterization.

## Key numbers

*   Monopole strength in \(^{12}\)C exhausts \(52 \pm 3^\text{(stat.)} \pm 8^\text{(sys.)}\)% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in the energy range 10–30 MeV.
*   Monopole strength in \(^{12}\)C from 14 to 30 MeV exhausts \(49 \pm 3^\text{(stat.)} \pm 8^\text{(sys.)}\)% of the EWSR, which is 22% larger than the 27(5)% reported from a previous \(\alpha\)-scattering experiment (PhysRevC.68.014305).
*   The maximum of the \(E0\) distribution in \(^{12}\)C from a Lorentzian fit over 14–22 MeV is \(E_\text{m} = 20.47(6)\) MeV with a width \(\Gamma = 3.3(2)\) MeV.
*   Monopole strength in \(^{93}\)Nb exhausts \(92 \pm 4^\text{(stat.)} \pm 10^\text{(sys.)}\)% of the EWSR in the energy range 10–32 MeV.
*   The Lorentzian fit centroid for \(^{93}\)Nb over 11–24 MeV is \(16.81(7)\) MeV with a width \(\Gamma = 7.1(2)\) MeV.
*   The nuclear incompressibility extracted from the \(\sqrt{m_3/m_1}\) centroid for \(^{93}\)Nb is \(K_A = 178(2)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the ISGMR using \(\alpha\)-scattering (e.g., PhysRevC.68.014305 for \(^{12}\)C; Gupta (2016) for \(^{92}\)Mo) and earlier \(^{6}\)Li experiments (PhysRevC.52.3195). The results for \(^{12}\)C show that previous \(\alpha\)-scattering measurements significantly underestimated the monopole strength due to background subtraction methods. The \(^{93}\)Nb data settle a debate about nuclear-structure effects on the ISGMR centroid in the \(A\approx90\) region (PhysRevC.88.021301; Gupta (2016)), confirming that no significant influence is observed even for an odd-even nucleus. The work enables future studies of giant resonances in unstable nuclei using inverse-kinematics \(^{6}\)Li scattering.
