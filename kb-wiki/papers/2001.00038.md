# Randall-Sundrum Model with a Dilaton Field at Finite Temperature

> We find exact finite temperature solutions to $d=5$ Einstein-dilaton gravity with black branes and a Randall--Sundrum 3-brane. We show that there exists a unique generating superpotential for these models. The location of the black brane and the associated Hawking temperature depend on the value of the the 3-brane tension while other parameters are held fixed. The thermodynamics of these solutions are presented, from which we show that the entropy satisfies $S<\text{Vol}(\mathbb{R}^3)/4G_5$. We demonstrate that in a certain limit the gauge dual of this theory effectively reduces to $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM at finite temperature on $S^1\times\mathbb{R}^3$

## Key claim

The authors construct exact finite-temperature solutions to 5D Einstein-dilaton gravity with a Randall–Sundrum 3-brane, showing that a black brane can form only when the brane tension satisfies the bound ω > 1, and that the entropy is strictly bounded above by Vol(ℝ³)/4G₅, with the bound saturated only in the limit of vanishing brane tension.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled nonlinear equations of motion for a 5D Einstein-dilaton system with a bulk RS 3-brane at the ℝ/ℤ₂ orbifold fixed point, using a domain wall ansatz and a superpotential method to generate the scalar potential. They impose junction conditions at the brane and require the blackening function f(z) to have a zero at a finite horizon position z_h. The thermodynamics are derived from the Hawking temperature via Euclidean periodicity and the Bekenstein–Hawking area law.

## Key numbers

- Black brane formation requires ω = (3/4π)(k/σG₅) > 1, where σ is the brane tension, k² is proportional to the bulk cosmological constant, and G₅ is the bulk gravitational constant.
- The Hawking temperature is T = (k/4π)(4 − λ²)((ω² − 1)/ω)(ω²/(ω² − 1))^(3/(4−λ²)).
- The entropy density is s = 8πM³((ω² − 1)/ω²)^(3/(4−λ²)), with the upper bound s < 8πM³ (corresponding to S < Vol(ℝ³)/4G₅).
- For |λ| = 1 (tachyonic dilaton), a minimum temperature T_min = 3k/4π exists below which no black brane solution emerges.
- In the low temperature limit, the entropy grows as s ∼ M³(T/k)^(3/(1−λ²)).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature analysis of Bartz (2018) and Meyer (2004) on scalar glueball mass spectra by incorporating finite temperature and backreaction of the dilaton field. It provides an analytically tractable holographic model that reduces to the pure Randall–Sundrum model when λ = 0 and to Type IIB string theory on AdS₅×S⁵ (dual to N=4 SYM at finite temperature on S¹×ℝ³) in the limit of vanishing brane tension and cosmological constant. The results enable further studies of holographic QCD, beyond-standard-model physics, and early-universe cosmology.
