# The X17 boson and the d(p,e+ e-)3He and d(n,e+ e-)3H processes: a theoretical analysis

> The present work deals with the e+ e- pair production in the d(p,e+e-)3He and d(n,e+ e-)3H processes, in order to evidentiate possible effects due to the exchange of a hypothetical low-mass boson, the so-called X17. These processes are studied for energies of the incident beams in the range 18-30 MeV, in order to have a sufficient energy to produce such a boson, whose mass is estimated to be around 17 MeV. We first analyze them as a purely electromagnetic processes, in the context of a state-of-the-art approach to nuclear strong-interaction dynamics and nuclear electromagnetic currents, derived from chiral effective field theory chiEFT. Next, we examine how the exchange of a hypothetical low-mass boson would impact the cross sections for such processes. We consider several possibilities, that this boson is either a scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, or axial particle. The main aim of the study is to exploit the specular structure of the 3He and 3H nuclei to investigate the isospin dependency of the X17-nucleon interaction, as the alleged "proto-phobicity".

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the hypothetical X17 boson (mass ≈ 17 MeV) could produce observable signatures in the angular distributions of electron-positron pairs from the d(p,e⁺e⁻)³He and d(n,e⁺e⁻)³H reactions, but only for incident beam energies above 17.3 MeV (proton) or 16.1 MeV (neutron), where the boson production becomes kinematically allowed; the study explicitly hedges that the X17 contribution is only sizeable in the narrow kinematic region where Q² ≈ M_X², and that the nuclear dynamics must be fully taken into account rather than relying on a simple two-step resonance model.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio calculations of the d(p,e⁺e⁻)³He and d(n,e⁺e⁻)³H reactions using the hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) method to describe both the initial N+d scattering states and the final trinucleon bound states, with nuclear interactions from the Argonne v₁₈ (AV18) plus Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon force, and separately from chiral effective field theory (χEFT) Norfolk V_Ia plus 3N forces. Electromagnetic currents were derived from χEFT with low-energy constants fixed to reproduce trinucleon magnetic moments. The X17 exchange amplitude was added at first-order perturbation theory for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial boson cases, using one-body nuclear currents derived from χEFT Lagrangians, with hadron-X17 coupling constants taken from previous fits to the p(³H,e⁺e⁻)⁴He data.

## Key numbers

- Minimum beam energy for X17 production: 17.3 MeV for p+d and 16.1 MeV for n+d (assuming M_X = 17 MeV)
- X17 mass estimated around 17 MeV
- Beam energy range studied: 18–30 MeV
- Lower bound on X17-electron coupling: |ε_e| > 2 × 10⁻⁴ from SLAC E141 experiment
- Upper bound on X17-electron coupling: |ε_e| < 2 × 10⁻³ from KLOE-2 experiment
- Assumed value for ε_e in calculations: 10⁻³
- Q-value difference B₃ − B₂ ≈ 5 MeV for these reactions
- Minimum CM kinetic energy for X17 production: 10.7 MeV for n+d and 11.5 MeV for p+d

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous full-dynamics study of the p(³H,e⁺e⁻)⁴He reaction (Viviani 2021) to the mirror reactions d(p,e⁺e⁻)³He and d(n,e⁺e⁻)³H, motivated by the ATOMKI anomalies reported by Krasznahorkay (2015, 2019, 2021, 2022) and confirmed by Anh (2024) for ⁸Be. The study aims to exploit the specular structure of ³He and ³H to probe the isospin dependence (proto-phobicity) of the X17-nucleon interaction, and enables future experimental searches at facilities such as those proposed by Gustavino (2024) and the Montreal X-17 Project (Azuelos 2022).
