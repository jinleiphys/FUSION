# Formation of hypermatter and hypernuclei within transport models in relativistic ion collisions

> Within a combined approach we investigate the main features of the production of hyper-fragments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The formation of hyperons is modelled within the UrQMD and HSD transport codes. To describe the hyperon capture by nucleons and nuclear residues a coalescence of baryons (CB) model was developed. We demonstrate that the origin of hypernuclei of various masses can be explained by typical baryon interactions, and that it is similar to processes leading to the production of conventional nuclei. At high beam energies we predict a saturation of the yields of all hyper-fragments, therefore, this kind of reactions can be studied with high yields even at the accelerators of moderate relativistic energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the production of hypernuclei of various masses in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be explained by typical baryon interactions and coalescence mechanisms similar to those producing conventional nuclei, with the important qualifier that at high beam energies (above 5–10 GeV per nucleon) the yields of all hyper-fragments saturate, meaning such reactions can be studied with high yields even at accelerators of moderate relativistic energies.

## Method

The authors combined two transport models, UrQMD and HSD, to simulate the dynamical stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the production of hyperons. After the transport stage, they applied a coalescence of baryons (CB) model that forms clusters by requiring that baryons (nucleons and hyperons) have relative velocities below a coalescence parameter \(v_c\) and satisfy a coordinate-space proximity criterion. The CB model was applied to carbon-on-carbon collisions at beam energies from 2 to 10 GeV per nucleon and gold-on-gold collisions at 15 GeV per nucleon, integrating over all impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence velocity parameter \(v_c = 0.22c\) was used as the primary value, with a smaller value \(v_c = 0.1c\) also tested for light clusters.
- For carbon-on-carbon collisions at 2 and 10 GeV per nucleon, the yield of hyper-fragments saturates at beam energies above 5–10 GeV per nucleon.
- The coordinate-space proximity criterion used a radius parameter \(r_0 = 2\) fm, justified by multifragmentation studies.
- The transition time from transport to coalescence was 20 fm/c after maximum overlap for carbon nuclei and 40 fm/c for gold nuclei.
- The rapidity range for identifying spectator nucleons was \(\Delta y = 0.267\), associated with Fermi momenta in the initial nucleus.
- In gold-on-gold collisions at 15 GeV per nucleon, the difference between UrQMD and HSD predictions for light hyper-fragments was about a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used the Dubna cascade model (DCM) for hypernucleus formation (Botvina 2011, 2013) and the UrQMD model for light cluster production in central collisions (Steinheimer 2012). The combined transport-plus-coalescence approach provides a unified framework for predicting hypernucleus yields across all rapidities and mass numbers, enabling experimental planning at facilities such as FAIR, RHIC, and NICA. The results are consistent with earlier DCM calculations when the coalescence parameter is chosen between 0.1c and 0.22c.
