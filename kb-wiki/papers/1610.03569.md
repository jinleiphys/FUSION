# Nuclear axial current operators to fourth order in chiral effective field theory

> We present the complete derivation of the nuclear axial charge and current operators as well as the pseudoscalar operators to fourth order in the chiral expansion relative to the dominant one-body contribution using the method of unitary transformation. We demonstrate that the unitary ambiguity in the resulting operators can be eliminated by the requirement of renormalizability and by matching of the pion-pole contributions to the nuclear forces. We give expressions for the renormalized single-, two- and three-nucleon contributions to the charge and current operators and pseudoscalar operators including the relevant relativistic corrections. We also verify explicitly the validity of the continuity equation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the complete derivation of nuclear axial charge and current operators to fourth order in chiral effective field theory, demonstrating that the unitary ambiguity in these operators can be eliminated by requiring renormalizability and matching pion-pole contributions to nuclear forces, though the authors note that the resulting operators are not uniquely defined and depend on the choice of basis in Fock space.

## Method

The authors employed the method of unitary transformation (MUT) within heavy-baryon chiral effective field theory, starting from the effective Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, and external sources, then deriving the Hamiltonian via canonical formalism after eliminating higher-order time derivatives through field redefinitions. They solved the Okubo decoupling equation perturbatively to integrate out pion fields and separate the purely nucleonic subspace, then applied additional unitary transformations on the nucleonic subspace to achieve renormalizability and match pion-pole contributions to nuclear forces. The derivation was carried out to fourth order in the chiral expansion relative to the dominant one-body contribution, corresponding to leading two-loop, one-loop, and tree-level order for one-nucleon, two-nucleon, and three-nucleon terms respectively.

## Key numbers

* The unitary ambiguity in the resulting axial operators is parametrized by 33 continuously varying parameters α_i^{ax} plus an additional phase β_1^{ax}
* The breakdown scale of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces is estimated to be Λ_b ∼ 600 MeV
* The chiral dimension ν of nuclear charge and current operators is given by ν = -3 + Σ_i V_i κ_i, where κ is the inverse mass dimension of coupling constants
* The leading-order one-pion exchange two-nucleon potential V^(Q^0) and subleading contributions V^(Q^2) are constructed from the lowest-order πN coupling proportional to g_A from L_πN^(1)

## Context

This work extends the systematic derivation of nuclear forces and currents within chiral EFT by the Bochum-Bonn group, which previously derived electromagnetic currents (Kolling 2009, 2011) and nuclear forces up to fifth order (Epelbaum 2014, Entem 2014). The paper addresses discrepancies with the JLab-Pisa group's results (Baroni 2015, Pastore 2008-2011) for axial current operators, which arise from different treatments of unitary ambiguity and renormalization. The derived operators enable precision calculations of electroweak reactions in few-nucleon systems, including muon capture on deuteron (relevant for the MuSun experiment at PSI) and solar proton-proton fusion, and allow bridging between different reactions through the same low-energy constant that appears in the leading three-nucleon force and the short-range part of the two-nucleon weak current.
