# Probing strange stars and color superconductivity by r-mode instabilities in millisecond pulsars

> R-mode instabilities in rapidly rotating quark matter stars (strange stars) lead to specific signatures in the evolution of pulsars with periods below 2.5 msec, and may explain the apparent lack of very rapid pulsars. Existing data seem consistent with pulsars being strange stars with a normal quark matter phase surrounded by an insulating nuclear crust. In contrast, quark stars in a color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase are ruled out. Two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) is marginally inconsistent with pulsar data.

## Key claim

Existing pulsar data are consistent with millisecond pulsars being strange stars with a normal (non-superfluid) quark matter phase and an insulating nuclear crust, but only if the crust keeps the core temperature roughly two orders of magnitude above the observed surface temperature; completely bare strange stars or those in a color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase are ruled out, while a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase is marginally inconsistent.

## Method

The paper calculates the r-mode instability window for strange stars by solving the equation balancing gravitational wave driving against damping from shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and surface rubbing at the crust-fluid boundary. It uses a polytropic equation of state (n=1 and n=0) for the star, adopts analytic expressions for the viscous time scales from prior work (Madsen 1992, Lindblom et al. 1999), and compares the resulting critical spin frequencies as a function of core temperature against the observed positions of low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs) and the two fastest known millisecond pulsars. The analysis is repeated for quark matter in a CFL phase (with an exponential suppression of viscosities due to an energy gap) and for a 2SC phase (where only 4 of 9 quark color-flavor states are gapped).

## Key numbers

- The two fastest known millisecond pulsars have rotation periods of 1.5578 msec and 1.6074 msec, corresponding to spin frequencies of approximately 642 Hz and 622 Hz.
- For a strange star with a normal quark phase, the r-mode instability is active for core temperatures in the range of a few × 10^5 K to a few × 10^7 K for rotation periods below 2.5–3 msec.
- The bulk viscous damping time in the low-temperature limit is τ_bv^low = 0.886 s (πGρ̄/Ω^2) T_9^{-2} m_{100}^{-4}, where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K and m_{100} is the strange quark mass in units of 100 MeV.
- The shear viscous damping time is τ_sv = 5.37 × 10^8 s (α_S/0.1)^{5/3} T_9^{5/3} for an n=1 polytrope.
- The braking index during r-mode spin-down is N ≈ 9, derived from the cooling law t ∝ T_9^{-4} and the instability condition Ω ∝ T^{1/2}.
- For a CFL phase with an energy gap Δ = 1 MeV, the electron shear time scale is τ_sv^{ee} ≈ 2.95 × 10^7 s (μ_e/μ_q)^{-14/3} T_9^{5/3}, and the surface rubbing time scale for a maximal crust is τ_sr ≈ 1.42 × 10^8 s T_9 (ν/1 kHz)^{-1/2}.
- The observed upper limits on surface temperature for the two fastest pulsars are about 6 × 10^5 K and 9 × 10^5 K.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of r-mode instabilities in strange stars from Madsen (1998) by incorporating the reevaluated bulk viscosity damping times from Lindblom, Mendell, and Owen (1999) and the surface rubbing effect for neutron stars identified by Bildsten and Ushomirsky (1999). It applies the instability formalism to test the hypothesis that millisecond pulsars are strange stars, and uses pulsar data to constrain the phase of quark matter, specifically ruling out the CFL phase and marginally excluding the 2SC phase. The results enable future observational tests: rapid pulsars entering the instability window should exhibit a braking index near 9, and the predicted pile-up of periods near 2.5–3 msec can be compared with larger pulsar samples.
