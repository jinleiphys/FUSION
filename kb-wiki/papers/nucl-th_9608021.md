# A new effective Lagrangian for nuclear matter

> The relativistic mean field model, the Zimányi - Moszkowski (ZM) Lagrangian describes nuclear matter and stable finite nuclei even in the non-relativistic limit. It fails, however, to predict the correct non-relativistic spin-orbit (SO) coupling. In this paper we improve on this matter by an additional tensor coupling analogous to the anomalous gyromagnetic ratio. It can be adjusted to describe the SO-term without changing the mean field solution of the ZM-Lagrangian for nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a tensor coupling term to the Zimányi-Moszkowski (ZM) Lagrangian that enhances the spin-orbit coupling in the non-relativistic limit without altering the mean field solution for unpolarized nuclear matter, thereby correcting the ZM model's too-weak spin-orbit potential.

## Method

The authors add a tensor term coupling the antisymmetric spin current density \(\overline{\psi} \sigma_{\mu\nu} \psi\) to the field strength \(F^{\mu\nu}\) of the vector meson field in the ZM Lagrangian. They then derive the non-relativistic Hamiltonian from the Dirac equation using a \(1/M_*\) expansion and gradient expansion for the mean fields, isolating the spin-orbit coupling term as a function of the new parameter \(\lambda\). The mean field equations remain unchanged for unpolarized media because the tensor term's expectation value vanishes in that case.

## Key numbers

- The vector mean field \(V\) and scalar mean field \(S\) from the ZM Lagrangian are \(V = 82\) MeV and \(S = 140\) MeV.
- The spin-orbit potential in the improved model is \(V_{SO} = (1+2\lambda)V + S\).
- Comparing the spin-orbit strength to the Walecka model (where \(V+S = 785\) MeV) yields \(\lambda = 3.5\).
- The effective nucleon mass is defined as \(M_* = M / (1 + g_s \sigma / M)\), with \(M\) the bare nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the Zimányi-Moszkowski (ZM) Lagrangian (Zimányi and Moszkowski, recent), which describes nuclear matter and stable finite nuclei but fails to produce the correct non-relativistic spin-orbit coupling. The improvement is analogous to adding an anomalous gyromagnetic ratio term, and the authors note that Reinhard and collaborators (year not given) considered a similar tensor coupling in the Walecka model but found it negligible. The new Lagrangian enables consistent relativistic descriptions of finite nuclei for use in quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
