# Shell-model study of $\log ft$ values for $^{139,140,141}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{139,140,141}$La transitions

> In the present work, beta-decay properties such as $\log ft$ values and half-lives have been systematically studied corresponding to Ba isotopes using large-scale shell-model calculations. An extensive comparison of beta decay results corresponding to $^{141}$Ba$\rightarrow$ $^{141}$La using shell-model calculations is made with the recently available experimental data. In addition, we have also calculated the nuclear and beta decay properties corresponding to $^{139}$Ba$\rightarrow$ $^{139}$La and $^{140}$Ba$\rightarrow$ $^{140}$La transitions. The model-space considered here is $Z=50-82$ and $N=82-126$ with $^{132}$Sn core, and the interaction employed here is jj56pnb interaction. The beta decay results using shell-model calculations for all the mentioned isotopes are compared with the available experimental data. This is the first theoretical interpretation corresponding to recent experimental data.

# Shell-model study of $\log ft$ values for $^{139,140,141}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{139,140,141}$La transitions

## Key claim

This work provides the first theoretical interpretation of recent experimental beta-decay data for $^{141}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{141}$La transitions, but the shell-model calculations require a quenched axial-vector coupling constant ($g_A^{eff}=0.66\pm 0.03$) and a mesonic enhancement factor ($\epsilon_{MEC}=2.0$) to achieve reasonable agreement with experimental $\log ft$ values, and the agreement varies significantly across different final states.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the jj56pnb effective interaction in a model space of $Z=50-82$ and $N=82-126$ with a $^{132}$Sn core, employing the NuShellX and KSHELL diagonalization codes. Beta-decay properties including $\log ft$ values, average shape factors, and half-lives were calculated for allowed, first-forbidden unique, and first-forbidden non-unique transitions using both bare and effective weak coupling constants, with the effective axial-vector coupling constant taken as $g_A^{eff}=0.66\pm 0.03$ from Kostensalo (2018). The nuclear structure properties (energy spectra, magnetic moments, quadrupole moments, B(E2) and B(M1) values) were first calculated to validate the wave functions before computing beta-decay observables.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation between experimental and shell-model energy levels: 0.164 MeV for $^{139}$La, 0.0733 MeV for $^{140}$La, and 0.558 MeV for $^{141}$La
* For $^{139}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{139}$La ground state transition ($7/2^- \rightarrow 7/2^+$): shell-model $\log ft = 6.774$ compared to experimental $\log ft = 6.845(3)$
* For $^{140}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{140}$La transition to $1^-_1$ state at 43.8132(18) keV: shell-model $\log ft = 6.634$ compared to experimental $\log ft = 8.03(11)$
* For $^{141}$Ba $\rightarrow$ $^{141}$La transition to $3/2^+_1$ state at 467.37(8) keV: shell-model $\log ft = 6.035$ compared to experimental $\log ft = 7.05(3)$
* Shell-model magnetic moment for $^{139}$La($7/2^+_1$): 1.760 $\mu_N$ versus experimental 2.7830455(9) $\mu_N$
* Shell-model quadrupole moment for $^{139}$La($7/2^+_1$): +0.127 $eb$ versus experimental +0.200(6) $eb$
* Shell-model B(M1) for $^{141}$La($5/2^+_1 \rightarrow 7/2^+_1$): 0.005 $\mu_N^2$ versus experimental 0.00381$^{+34}_{-18}$ $\mu_N^2$

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of beta decay in the $^{132}$Sn region by the same group, following their calculations for $^{135,137}$Te (Sharma 2023) and earlier work in the $^{208}$Pb region (Sharma 2022, Srivastava 2022). The study is motivated by recent high-sensitivity gamma-ray measurements of $^{141}$Ba beta decay at Argonne National Laboratory using the Gammasphere array (Rufino 2022), which improved upon earlier measurements by Greenwood (1997) and Faller (1986). The calculations use the quenching approach established by Brown (1985) and Suhonen (2017), and the mesonic enhancement factor from Towner (1992) and Kostensalo (2018). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data for additional transitions in this mass region and may help assign spin-parity to tentative experimental states, such as proposing the $7/2^+_2$ state at 1420.491(8) keV in $^{139}$La based on closer $\log ft$ agreement.
