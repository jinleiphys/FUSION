# Centrality dependence of $ψ\prime$ over $J/ψ$ : a signal of QGP

> If nuclear collisions lead to QGP formation then the ratio of $ψ\prime$ over $J/ψ$ will remain constant with $E_T$ as both $J/ψ$ and $ψ\prime$ are melted in the QGP. On the other hand, if hot hadronic matter is produced, the ratio will continually fall with $E_T$, as $ψ\prime$'s are more suppressed in hadronic matter than the $J/ψ$'s. From the existing NA50 Pb+Pb data, we have constructed the ratio $σ(ψ\prime)/σ(J/ψ)$, as a function of $E_T$. The ratio give indication of QGP formation, but definite conclusion can not be reached. We have also given the prediction for the ratio at RHIC energy. If QGP is formed at RHIC, it could be detected from the ratio.

## Key claim

If a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is formed in nuclear collisions, the ratio of psi-prime to J/psi will remain constant with transverse energy (E_T) because both charmonium states are melted in the QGP; if only hot hadronic matter is produced, the ratio will continually fall with E_T. The existing NA50 Pb+Pb data show a tendency toward saturation in this ratio, but a definite conclusion about QGP formation cannot be reached from the SPS data alone.

## Method

The author constructed the ratio sigma(psi-prime)/sigma(J/psi) as a function of E_T from existing NA50 Pb+Pb data at 158 GeV/c and NA38 S+U data at 200 GeV/c. A QCD-based nuclear absorption model was used to describe J/psi and psi-prime production, with additional suppression from either comover interactions or QGP formation (parameterized by a threshold density above which psi-prime is dissolved). The model was then used to predict the ratio at RHIC energy for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- In S+U collisions, from peripheral to central collisions, J/psi are suppressed by a factor of approximately 1.3, while psi-prime are suppressed by a factor of approximately 4.
- In Pb+Pb collisions, psi-prime are suppressed by a factor of 8, while J/psi are suppressed by a factor of 3.
- For pA collisions, NA50 obtained alpha_{J/psi} = 0.933 +/- 0.105 and alpha_{psi-prime} = 0.906 +/- 0.022, indicating similar absorption cross sections for both states.
- In the QCD-based nuclear absorption model, the parameter epsilon^2 = 0.1875 GeV^2/fm was used to describe the gain in relative 4-square momentum per unit length.
- For the comover scenario, the comover absorption cross section sigma_co = 8 mb was obtained from fitting S+U data.
- For the QGP scenario in Pb+Pb collisions, the threshold density n_c = 2.8 fm^{-2} was used.
- For S+U collisions in the QGP scenario, the threshold density was n_c = 1.8 fm^{-2}, which the author notes is too low for QGP formation.
- At RHIC, the model predicts that with nuclear+QGP suppression, the ratio psi-prime/J/psi remains constant for E_T greater than 70 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Gupta and Satz (1992) that the centrality dependence of the psi-prime to J/psi ratio can serve as a thermometer for the deconfinement temperature. It contrasts with earlier studies of J/psi suppression alone, which could be explained by multiple models with or without QGP formation (Blaizot, Dinh, and Ollitrault 2000; Capella, Ferreiro, and Kaidalov 2000; Chaudhuri 2001, 2002). The analysis shows that while the SPS data are inconclusive, the ratio at RHIC energy could distinguish between comover and QGP suppression scenarios, enabling a more definitive test of deconfinement.
