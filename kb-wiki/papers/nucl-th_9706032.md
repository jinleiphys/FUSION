# Covariant and Light-Front Approaches to the Rho-Meson Electromagnetic Form Factors

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that among several light-front prescriptions for extracting electromagnetic form factors of a spin-1 composite particle, the Grach-Kondratyuk (GK) prescription yields results most consistent with the fully covariant calculation for the rho-meson, provided the model parameters are tuned to reproduce realistic size and quadrupole properties. The authors find that the light-front integration in k-minus, taking only the spectator quark pole, is not exact for the spin-1 case, leading to violation of the angular condition and prescription-dependent results.

## Method

The authors compute the rho-meson electromagnetic form factors from the Feynman one-loop triangle diagram for the J+ component of the current, using a constituent quark model with a Dirac pointlike quark and a covariant dipole regulator. They perform two calculations: a fully covariant integration in four-dimensional momentum space, and a non-covariant light-front calculation where the k-minus integration is performed analytically taking only the spectator quark pole, followed by numerical integration in k-plus and transverse momentum. The form factors are extracted using four different prescriptions from the literature (GK, CCKP, FFS, BH) and compared with the covariant results.

## Key numbers

- Rho-meson mass: 0.77 GeV
- Constituent quark mass (m): 0.43 GeV
- Regulator mass (m_R): 1.8 GeV
- Covariant mean square radius: 0.37 fm^2
- Covariant magnetic moment: 2.14 (compared to non-relativistic value of 2)
- Covariant quadrupole moment: 0.052 fm^2
- Light-front mean square radius values range from 0.37 to 0.40 fm^2 (at most 10% higher than covariant)
- Light-front magnetic moment values show a spread of 15% above the covariant result
- Light-front quadrupole moment values within 10% to 15% of covariant result
- Charge form factor zero crossing around 3 GeV^2
- At higher q^2, the BH prescription gives results about 30% below the covariant calculation for G_0

## Context

This work extends the discussion of rotational symmetry breakdown in light-front calculations of spin-1 electromagnetic form factors, following earlier work by Grach and Kondratyuk (1984), Chung, Coester, Keister, and Polizou (1988), and Brodsky and Hiller (1992). The authors compare their results with a QCD-inspired model by Cardarelli, Grach, Narodetskii, Pace, Salme, and Simula (1995) that incorporates one-gluon exchange at short distances and linear confinement at large distances. The paper enables practitioners to select the appropriate light-front prescription when calculating rho-meson form factors with null-plane wave functions, and demonstrates that the GK prescription is the most reliable among the tested schemes.
