# Potential dangers when phase shifts are used as a link between experiment and QCD

> Luscher has shown that in single channel problem (elastic region below first inelastic threshold) there exists a direct link between the discrete value of the energy in a finite QCD volume and the scattering phase shift at the same energy. However, when the extension of the theorem is made to the baryon resonance sector (multi-channel situation in the inelastic region above first inelastic threshold), eigenphases (diagonal multi-channel quantities) replace phase shifts (single channel quantities). It is necessary to stress that the renowned pi/2 resonance criterion is formulated for eigenphases and not for phase shifts, so the resonance extracting procedure has to be applied with utmost care. The potential instability of extracting eigenphases from experimental data which occurs if insufficient number of channels is used can be reduced if a trace function which explicitly takes multi-channel aspect of the problem into account is used instead of single-channel phase shifts.

## Key claim

Luscher's theorem, which directly links finite-volume QCD energy levels to scattering phase shifts in the single-channel elastic regime, cannot be applied naively to the multi-channel inelastic region relevant for baryon resonances: eigenphases (diagonal multi-channel quantities) replace single-channel phase shifts, and the standard pi/2 resonance criterion applies only to eigenphases, not to phase shifts.

## Method

The paper reviews the formal distinction between single-channel phase shifts and multi-channel eigenphases in the context of Luscher's theorem and its extensions to inelastic scattering. It examines the mathematical relationships among phase shifts, eigenphases, K-matrix poles, and T-matrix poles as resonance criteria, and discusses the instability of eigenphase extraction when an insufficient number of channels is used. The author proposes using a trace function (sum of eigenphases) as a multi-channel quantity that avoids the unphysical branch cuts present in individual eigenphases.

## Key numbers

- The baryon resonance energy range where new measurements are needed is 1.5 GeV to 2.5 GeV.
- The Bonn-Julich-Valencia UCHPT model successfully reproduced pi pi/pi eta and K anti-K data up to 1200 MeV.
- Only two 2-body channels are currently fairly well known: pi N elastic scattering and eta production.

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of Luscher's theorem (Luscher 1991) from single-channel elastic scattering to multi-channel inelastic scattering, as previously generalized by Liu (2006), Lage (2009), Bernard (2011), and others using non-relativistic effective field theory. The author specifically criticizes the approach of Durr (2008) for applying the pi/2 criterion directly to phase shifts rather than eigenphases. The work enables proper extraction of baryon resonance parameters from lattice QCD by emphasizing that trace functions (Hazi 1979) or direct T-matrix pole determination must replace naive phase-shift analysis, and it highlights the urgent need for new experimental data in inelastic channels, endorsing a proposed J-PARC experiment (Hicks 2012).
