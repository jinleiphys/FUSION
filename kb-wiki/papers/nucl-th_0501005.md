# Dynamical coupled-channel study of K+ Lambda photoproduction

> Results for the reaction gamma p --> K+ Lambda, studied within a constituent quark model and a dynamical coupled-channel approach, are presented and compared with recent data. Issues related to the search for missing baryon resonances are briefly discussed and the role played by a third S_{11} resonance is underlined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined constituent quark model (CQM) and dynamical coupled-channel (CC) approach, including intermediate and final state interactions, is necessary to reproduce the 920 measured differential cross sections for \(\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda\), and confirms that a third \(S_{11}\) resonance (mass \(\approx\) 1.780 GeV, width \(\approx\) 100 MeV) contributes significantly to the reaction, especially at backward angles around 1.8 GeV.

## Method

The direct \(K^+ \Lambda\) photoproduction amplitude was calculated using a chiral constituent quark model with \(SU(6) \otimes O(3)\) symmetry and configuration mixing from gluon exchange, including all known nucleon and hyperon resonances. This direct channel was then embedded in a dynamical coupled-channel model that accounts for intermediate state interactions (\(\pi N \to KY\) and \(KY \to KY\)) and final state interactions (\(KY \to KY\)), with channels \(\pi N\), \(K\Lambda\), and \(K\Sigma\). The free parameters (one symmetry-breaking parameter per nucleon resonance with mass \(\leq\) 2 GeV) were fitted to 920 CLAS differential cross sections using the CERN MINUIT package, while parameters for the meson-baryon interactions were taken from a prior model (model B in Ref. CC-04).

## Key numbers

- The full model (CQM + CC) reproduces 920 measured differential cross sections from the CLAS Collaboration.
- Coupled-channel effects induce up to 20% changes on total cross sections, as reported in an earlier paper (CC-01).
- The third \(S_{11}\) resonance has extracted mass \(M \approx 1.780\) GeV and width \(\Gamma \approx 100\) MeV.
- The total center-of-mass energy range studied is from threshold up to \(W \approx 2.3\) GeV.
- Significant contributions from the third \(S_{11}\) resonance are observed around 1.8 GeV, especially at the most backward angle (123 degrees).

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel studies (CC-01, CC-04) by replacing a simple effective Lagrangian model (with only two baryon resonances) with a comprehensive chiral constituent quark model (CQM) that includes all known baryon resonances. The results confirm the need for a third \(S_{11}\) resonance previously reported in the literature (S11) and in the authors' own prior CQM work (CQM). The approach enables future searches for missing baryon resonances and can be applied to polarization data from CLAS, SAPHIR, LEPS, and GRAAL to resolve discrepancies between experimental datasets.
