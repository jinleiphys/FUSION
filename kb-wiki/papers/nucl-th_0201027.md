# Charmonium-hadron interactions from QCD

> The heavy quark system is an excellent probe to learn about the QCD dynamics at finite density. First, we discuss the properties of the $J/ψ$ and $D$ meson at finite nucleon density. We discuss why their properties should change at finite density and then introduce an exact QCD relation among these hadron properties and the energy momentum tensor of the medium. Second, we discuss attempts to calculate charmonium-hadron total cross section using effective hadronic models and perturbative QCD. We emphasize a recent calculation, where the cross section is derived using QCD factorization theorem. We conclude by discussing some challenges for SIS 200.

# Charmonium-hadron interactions from QCD

## Key claim

The paper argues that changes in the properties of charmonium and open-charm mesons in nuclear matter, specifically the mass shift of the J/ψ (predicted to be -4 to -7 MeV at nuclear matter density) and the D meson (predicted around -50 MeV), can serve as quantitative probes of QCD dynamics at finite density, but these predictions carry significant model dependence and the J/ψ mass shift is so small that its observability is uncertain.

## Method

The authors derive an exact QCD sum rule relating the first moment of the spectral density for the heavy vector current to the gluon condensate and the energy-momentum tensor of the medium, then apply it to constrain changes in the J/ψ coupling and the D meson threshold. Separately, they re-derive the leading-order perturbative QCD result for J/ψ-hadron total cross sections using the QCD factorization theorem, with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the Coulombic bound state in the non-relativistic limit, and evaluate the cross section by convolving the J/ψ-gluon scattering cross section with the gluon distribution function of the hadron.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ mass shift at nuclear matter density: -4 to -7 MeV (potential model and QCD sum rule approaches)
- D meson average mass shift at normal nuclear matter density: around -50 MeV (quark-meson coupling model and QCD sum rule approach)
- Operator product expansion contributions at finite density: -1/12 Δ⟨α_s/π G²⟩ ≈ 6.25 × 10⁻⁵ × x GeV⁴, and 8/(16+3n_f) Δ⟨T_00⟩ ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁴ × x GeV⁴, where x = ρ/ρ_n.m. with ρ_n.m. = 0.17/(fm)³
- Relation between J/ψ suppression and D meson mass shift: 10% suppression in f_V² corresponds to 28 MeV decrease of the D meson mass in nuclear matter
- Typical cross sections used in hadronic final state interaction models: σ_{J/ψ+N} ∼ 5 mb and σ_{J/ψ+comover} ∼ 1 mb
- For the charmonium system in the pQCD formalism: α_s = 0.84 and separation scale ε_0 = 0.78 GeV

## Context

This work extends the heavy-quark probe approach of Peskin (1979) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979), who first applied perturbative QCD to heavy quarkonium-light hadron interactions using operator product expansion. The authors re-derive the leading-order result using the QCD factorization theorem, establishing equivalence between the two approaches for bound state scattering. The exact sum rule generalizes the finite-temperature/density sum rules introduced for light hadrons by Hatsuda and Lee (1995). The paper enables future experimental programs at SIS 200 and antiproton-nucleus facilities to measure J/ψ and D meson properties in nuclear matter, which would constrain higher-twist gluonic effects in the nucleon and provide a test of competing models, including the meson exchange model, quark exchange model of Wong and Barna (2001), and various perturbative QCD calculations, that currently disagree by orders of magnitude in their predictions for the J/ψ-nucleon cross section near threshold.
