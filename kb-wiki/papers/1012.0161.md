# Testing factorization in pA collisions at the LHC

> Global perturbative QCD analyses, based on large data sets from e-p and hadron collider experiments, provide tight constraints on the parton distribution function (PDF) in the proton. The extension of these analyses to nuclear parton distributions (nPDF) has attracted much interest in recent years. nPDFs are needed as benchmarks for the characterization of hot QCD matter in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and attract further interest since they may show novel signatures of non-linear density-dependent QCD evolution. However, it is not known from first principles whether the factorization of long-range phenomena into process-independent parton distribution, which underlies global PDF extractions for the proton, extends to nuclear effects. As a consequence, assessing the reliability of nPDFs for benchmark calculations goes beyond testing the numerical accuracy of their extraction and requires phenomenological tests of the factorization assumption. Here we argue that a proton-nucleus collision programme at the LHC, including a rapidity scan, would provide a set of measurements allowing for unprecedented tests of the factorization assumption underlying global nPDF fits.

## Key claim
The authors argue that a proton-nucleus (pA) collision programme at the LHC, including a rapidity scan, would provide unprecedented tests of whether collinear factorization (the assumption that nuclear effects can be fully absorbed into process-independent nuclear parton distribution functions) holds for hadron-nucleus collisions, which is not known from first principles.

## Method
The authors performed leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD calculations of single inclusive neutral pion production in proton-nucleus collisions, using CTEQ6L proton PDFs with EPS09LO nuclear modifications and KKP fragmentation functions. They computed the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}^{h}\) as a function of transverse momentum \(p_T\) and rapidity \(y\) for RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV deuteron-gold) and LHC energies (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 8.8\) TeV proton-lead), and compared the predictions with existing RHIC data and with alternative models (initial-state parton multiple scattering and non-linear low-\(x\) QCD evolution).

## Key numbers
- At RHIC mid-rapidity, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{dAu}^{\pi^0}\) shows an enhancement around \(p_T \simeq 4\) GeV, consistent with the anti-shadowing region of nPDFs.
- At LHC mid-rapidity (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 8.8\) TeV), the maximum of \(R_{pPb}^{\pi^0}\) shifts to much higher \(p_T\) values (approximately 40 times larger than at RHIC for the same momentum fraction \(x\)), reflecting the factor of 40 increase in center-of-mass energy.
- The rapidity scan from \(y = -4\) to \(y = +4\) at LHC energies allows scanning the shadowing (S), anti-shadowing (AS), and EMC regimes of nuclear PDFs.
- The authors estimate that within one month of running at nominal LHC luminosity, more than \(N_{\rm events} = 1000\) per GeV-bin can be accessed for the rapidity-dependent \(p_T\) range shown.

## Context
This work extends global nPDF analyses such as EPS09 (Eskola et al., 2009) and HKN07 (Hirai et al., 2007), which are based on fixed-target nuclear DIS and Drell-Yan data plus RHIC inclusive hadron data, but suffer from large uncertainties especially for the gluon distribution. The paper shows that LHC pA measurements can distinguish collinear factorization from alternative explanations of nuclear effects such as initial-state parton multiple scattering (Zhang et al., 2001) and non-linear low-\(x\) QCD evolution (Albacete, 2010), and can resolve qualitative differences between existing nPDF sets. This would enable improved nPDF benchmarks for characterizing hot QCD matter in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC.
