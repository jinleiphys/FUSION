# Unintegrated parton distributions and pion production in $pp$ collisions at SPS and RHIC energies

> Inclusive cross sections for pion production in $pp$ collisions are calculated for the first time fully based on unintegrated gluon, quark and antiquark distributions (uPDF). We use recently developed Kwieciński uPDF's and different phenomenological fragmentation functions (FF) from the literature. In addition to the $gg \to g$ diagram we include also $g q \to q$ and $q g \to q$ diagrams for inclusive parton production. The cross section for pions is obtained then by convoluting the inclusive parton distributions and FFs. Applications for SPS and RHIC are shown.

## Key claim

Inclusive cross sections for pion production in $pp$ collisions are calculated for the first time fully based on unintegrated gluon, quark and antiquark distributions (uPDF), using Kwiecinski uPDFs and phenomenological fragmentation functions, with the condition that the nonperturbative form factor parameter must be limited to $b_0 > 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$ to avoid exceeding experimental data in the perturbative regime.

## Method

The author calculates inclusive parton production using three leading-order diagrams ($gg \to g$, $gq \to q$, and $qg \to q$) within the $k_t$-factorization approach, working in impact parameter space with Kwiecinski unintegrated parton distributions. Nonperturbative effects are included via a factorizable Gaussian form factor in impact parameter space. The cross section for pions is obtained by convoluting the inclusive parton distributions with Kretzer fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energy W = 27.4 GeV, the nonperturbative form factor parameter must satisfy $b_0 > 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$ to keep results below experimental data for $p_t > 2$ GeV
- At RHIC energy W = 200 GeV, results for negative pions at pseudorapidities $\eta = 2.2$ and $\eta = 3.2$ are compatible with BRAHMS data for negative hadrons
- At RHIC energy W = 200 GeV, there is missing strength at central pseudorapidities $\eta = 0.0$ and $\eta = 1.0$ compared to BRAHMS data for charged hadrons

## Context

This work extends the approach of unintegrated gluon distributions to particle production by including unintegrated quark and antiquark distributions, building on the CCFM equations solved by Kwiecinski and collaborators (2003). The new contributions are comparable to the purely gluonic one at midrapidities and dominate in the fragmentation region, enabling the calculation of $\pi^+ - \pi^-$ asymmetry without introducing K-factors or initial transverse momenta by hand, in contrast to the standard collinear approach used by Owens, Field, Wang (2000), and Levai.
