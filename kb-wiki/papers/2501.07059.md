# Test for universality of short-range correlations in pion-induced Drell-Yan Process

> We investigate nuclear modification and the universality of short-range correlation (SRC) in pion-induced Drell-Yan process. Employing nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and pion PDFs, the ratio of differential cross sections of different nuclei relative to the free nucleon is presented. A kind of universal modification function was proposed which would provide nontrivial tests of SRC universality on the platform of pion-induced Drell-Yan. This work improves our understanding of nuclear structure and strong interactions.

# Test for universality of short-range correlations in pion-induced Drell-Yan Process

## Key claim

The authors propose that a universal modification function, constructed by normalizing the Drell-Yan cross-section ratio by the SRC scaling factor, can test the universality of short-range correlations in pion-induced Drell-Yan processes, but only in the region 0.3 ≤ x₂ ≤ 0.7 where the EMC effect is dominated by SRC; a higher-order correction term proportional to the proton-neutron asymmetry violates this universality but is approximately one order of magnitude smaller than the leading contribution.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio of differential cross sections for pion-induced Drell-Yan lepton-pair production on various nuclei (⁴He, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, ⁵⁶Fe, ¹⁹⁷Au, ²⁰⁸Pb) relative to a free nucleon target, using nuclear parton distribution functions from the EPPS21 parameterization and pion PDFs from the xFitter group global fits. They then define a universal modification function R_M that normalizes this ratio by the SRC scaling factor a₂^A and the nuclear geometry factor 2Z_A/A_A, and test whether this function collapses to a common curve across different nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The ratio factor R(dσ^DY; x₂, Q²) decreases from roughly 1.0 to 0.8 in the region 0.3 ≤ x₂ ≤ 0.6, depending on the nucleus type.
- After applying the universal modification, the values of R_M shrink to a range of 0.01 to -0.03 across all nuclei studied.
- The kinematics are fixed at Q² = 25 GeV² and center-of-mass energy √s = 21.8 GeV, corresponding to the E615 experiment beam energy.
- The lower integration limit for x_π is x_π,min ≈ 0.0526, determined by the kinematic constraint x_π x₂ = Q²/s for x₂,max = 1.

## Context

This work extends the study of the relationship between the EMC effect and short-range correlations, previously investigated in lepton deep inelastic scattering and neutrino DIS by Yang (2023) and Huang (2021), to the pion-induced Drell-Yan process. The authors build on the observation from the CLAS collaboration (2019) that nuclear modifications in the EMC region may originate from SRC pairs, and use SRC scaling factors compiled by Weinstein (2010) and Arrington (2012). The proposed universal modification function can be tested at future experiments such as the Electron-Ion Collider and the AMBER upgrade at COMPASS, which will use pion beams on nuclear targets to study nucleon structure.
