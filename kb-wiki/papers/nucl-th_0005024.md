# Mapping the proton drip line from Z=31 to Z=49

> The structure of proton drip line nuclei in the 60 < A < 100 mass range is studied with the Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model. For the elements which determine the astrophysical rapid proton capture process path, the RHB model predicts the location of the proton drip-line, the ground-state quadrupole deformations and one-proton separation energies at and beyond the drip-line. The results of the present theoretical investigation are compared with available experimental data. For possible odd-Z ground state proton emitters, the calculated deformed single-particle orbitals occupied by the odd valence proton and the corresponding spectroscopic factors are compared with predictions of the macroscopic-microscopic mass model.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model predicts the location of the proton drip line for odd-Z nuclei in the 60 < A < 100 mass range in excellent agreement with available experimental data, but for even-Z nuclei the drip line is predicted at extremely low isospin values (T_z = -3 or even T_z = -7/2) that are virtually impossible to produce experimentally and play no role in the rp-process.

## Method

The authors applied the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction for the mean-field Lagrangian and the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S for the pairing channel, solving the equations self-consistently for axially symmetric deformed shapes. They calculated ground-state quadrupole deformations, one-proton separation energies, deformed single-particle orbitals, and spectroscopic factors for nuclei along the proton drip line from Z=31 to Z=49 in the mass range 60 < A < 100. A simple blocking procedure was used for odd-proton and odd-neutron systems without breaking time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For Z=31 and Z=33, the calculated drip line nuclei are ⁶¹Ga and ⁶⁵As, respectively, in agreement with experimental data
- For Z=35, the RHB calculation predicts ⁷⁰Br as the last proton bound isotope; ⁶⁹Br is calculated to be proton unbound
- For Z=37, the calculation confirms ⁷⁴Rb as the last proton bound nucleus, in agreement with experiment
- For Z=39, the RHB model predicts ⁷⁷Y as the last proton bound nucleus (T_z = -1/2), in contrast to other mass models
- For even-Z nuclei, the proton drip line is predicted at T_z = -3 or T_z = -7/2, with the exception of ⁸⁴Ru at T_z = -2
- The energy window for observable ground state proton emission is calculated as 0.2 - 0.3 MeV for Z=30 and 0.35 - 0.5 MeV for Z=40
- For ⁷³Rb, the RHB model predicts an oblate shape (β₂ = -0.34) while the FRDM predicts a prolate shape (β₂ = 0.37)

## Context

This work extends previous RHB model studies of proton-rich nuclei by Lalazissis, Vretenar, and Ring (1998-1999) to the mass region 60 < A < 100, and provides input for the astrophysical rapid proton capture (rp) process path calculations as studied by Schatz (1998). The results are compared with the macroscopic-microscopic finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller, Nix, and collaborators (1995, 1997), and with experimental data from Mohar (1991), Winger (1993), Blank (1995), and others. The predictions for possible ground state proton emitters below Z=50 can guide future experiments in a region where no ground state proton radioactivity has been discovered so far.
