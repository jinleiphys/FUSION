# Phase Separation and an upper bound for $Δ$ for Fermi fluids in the Phase Separation and an upper bound for $Δ$ for Fermi fluids in the unitary regime

> An upper bound is derived for $Δ$ for a cold dilute fluid of equal amounts of two species of fermion in the unitary regime $k_f a \to \infty$ (where $k_f$ is the Fermi momentum and $a$ the scattering length, and $Δ$ is a pairing energy: the difference in energy per particle between adding to the system a macroscopic number (but infinitesimal fraction) of particles of one species compared to adding equal numbers of both. The bound is $δ\leq {5/3} (2 (2 ξ)^{2/5} - (2 ξ))$ where $ξ=ε/ε_{\rm FG}$, $δ= 2 Δ/ε_{\rm FG}$; $ε$ is the energy per particle and $ε_{\rm FG}$ is the energy per particle of a noninteracting Fermi gas. If the bound is saturated, then systems with unequal densities of the two species will separate spatially into a superfluid phase with equal numbers of the two species and a normal phase with the excess. If the bound is not saturated then $Δ$ is the usual superfluid gap. If the superfluid gap exceeds the maximum allowed by the inequality phase separation occurs.

## Key claim

An upper bound is derived for the pairing energy Δ in a cold dilute Fermi fluid with equal densities of two species in the unitary regime (k_f a → ∞), but the bound depends on the unknown universal constant ξ (the ratio of the actual energy per particle to that of a noninteracting Fermi gas). The bound is rigorous, but whether it is saturated depends on whether the system phase separates when the two species have unequal densities, which remains an open question.

## Method

The author derives the bound by considering the ground state energy density E(n_a, n_b) as a function of the densities of the two fermion species, parameterized as E(n_a, n_b) = α n_a^(5/3) f(n_b/n_a) with f(0)=1 and f(1)=2ξ. The requirement that the system cannot lower its energy by phase separation imposes that the curvature of E in the density plane must be nonnegative, which yields a differential inequality on f(x). Solving the saturated version of this inequality with the boundary conditions gives an upper bound f_max(x), from which the bound on δ = 2Δ/ε_FG follows via the relation between Δ and the derivative of f at x=1.

## Key numbers

- The bound is δ ≤ (5/3)[2(2ξ)^(2/5) - (2ξ)], where ξ = ε/ε_FG and δ = 2Δ/ε_FG.
- For the numerically extracted value ξ ≈ 0.42 ± 0.01, the critical value for δ_SF (the superfluid gap analog) that separates phase-separated from homogeneous behavior is approximately 1.70.
- The numerically extracted value for δ_SF is 1.68 ± 0.1, which is not accurate enough to determine whether phase separation occurs.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bedaque, Caldas and Rupak (2003), who argued on the basis of a generalized BCS ansatz that phase separation into a paired superfluid and a normal phase occurs for unequal densities, though their analysis was strictly valid only in weak coupling. The bound provides a rigorous constraint on numerical Monte Carlo calculations of the universal parameters ξ and δ, such as those by Carlson et al. (2003) and others, and shows that the numerically extracted values are surprisingly close to the critical value for phase separation, leaving open the possibility that they are exactly equal.
