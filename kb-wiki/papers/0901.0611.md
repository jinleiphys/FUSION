# Continuum Coupling and Pair Correlation in Weakly Bound Deformed Nuclei

> We formulate a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method applicable to weakly bound deformed nuclei using the coordinate-space Green's function technique. An emphasis is put on treatment of quasiparticle states in the continuum, on which we impose the correct boundary condition of the asymptotic out-going wave. We illustrate this method with numerical examples.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method for weakly bound deformed nuclei using the coordinate-space Green's function technique, with the key technical advance being the imposition of the correct asymptotic out-going wave boundary condition on continuum quasiparticle states. The abstract glosses over the fact that the method is demonstrated only for a simplified model using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential for the particle-hole field rather than a fully self-consistent mean-field, and that the numerical example is limited to a single nucleus (³⁸Mg) with a fixed deformation parameter.

## Method

The authors construct the exact quasiparticle Green's function for deformed HFB mean-fields using a coupled-channel representation with axial symmetry assumed. The HFB equation is written as coupled-channel Schrödinger equations for radial wave functions expanded in partial waves, and the Green's function is built from linear combinations of regular solutions (satisfying boundary conditions at the origin) and out-going wave solutions (connected to asymptotic Hankel functions). The generalized density matrix is then calculated via a contour integral of the HFB Green's function, incorporating contributions from both discrete and continuum quasiparticle states, and the HFB ground state is obtained through iterative self-consistency.

## Key numbers

- The calculation uses a deformation parameter β = 0.3 for ³⁸Mg.
- The radial integration interval is r = [0, 15 fm] with a step size Δr = 0.2 fm.
- The pairing interaction parameter η = 0.76 is chosen to produce a neutron pairing gap around Δ ∼ 1.5 MeV.
- The cutoff in quasiparticle energy is 60 MeV, and the maximum Ω (j_z) quantum number is Ω_max = 21/2.
- The Fermi energy for neutrons in the numerical example is λ = −889 keV.
- The smoothing parameter for the occupation number density is ε = 25 keV (giving artificial FWHM = 50 keV for a discrete state).
- The lowest-energy resonance corresponds to the [310]½ orbit in the deformed Woods-Saxon potential, which without pairing has a single-particle energy e = −798 keV.
- When the Woods-Saxon depth is shifted by +2 MeV, the single-particle energy and Fermi energy change to e = −40 keV and λ = −88 keV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the spherical continuum HFB theory of Belyaev (1987) and Dobaczewski (1996) to deformed nuclei, and contrasts with the approach of Michel (2008) using the Pöschl-Teller-Ginocchio basis and the coupled-channel analysis of Hamamoto (2007) for quasiparticle motion in deformed Woods-Saxon potentials. The method enables future application to continuum QRPA calculations for deformed neutron-rich nuclei, particularly in the region Z = 10–20 and N ≥ 20 accessible at new-generation RI-beam facilities. The numerical results suggest that the effective pairing gap of weakly bound orbits may not be reduced as much as claimed by Hamamoto (2007), provided the pairing interaction has surface enhancement.
