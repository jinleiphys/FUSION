# Kinetic theory and the speed of sound in dense matter

> We discuss a constraint on the speed of sound, $c_s^2$, derived from relativistic kinetic theory and show how it can be expressed in terms of the average sound speed, $\langle c_s^2 \rangle$. This reformulation highlights the interplay between instantaneous and integrated stiffness of the equation of state and allows the kinetic-theory bound to be visualized as a restriction in the $c_s^2$-$\langle c_s^2 \rangle $ plane.

# Kinetic theory and the speed of sound in dense matter

## Key claim
The kinetic-theory bound on the speed of sound, which requires positivity of the relaxation time in causal transport equations, can be reformulated in terms of the average sound speed ⟨c_s²⟩ = p/ε, showing that even moderate values of the average sound speed place nontrivial restrictions on the maximum locally admissible c_s². This bound is not equivalent to the causal limit c_s² ≤ 1 and becomes progressively more restrictive as the average stiffness increases.

## Method
The author rewrites the kinetic-theory bound c_s² ≤ (ε - p/3)/(ε + p) using the identity ⟨c_s²⟩ = p/ε to obtain c_s² ≤ (1 - ⟨c_s²⟩/3)/(1 + ⟨c_s²⟩). This reformulation is then applied to a statistical ensemble of equations of state constructed from a piecewise-linear speed-of-sound parametrization, requiring consistency with chiral effective field theory at low density and perturbative QCD at high density. The ensemble is visualized in the c_s²–⟨c_s²⟩ plane to identify which EOSs are excluded by the kinetic-theory constraint.

## Key numbers
- The kinetic-theory bound is given by c_s² ≤ (ε - p/3)/(ε + p), which depends explicitly on the thermodynamic state through the ratio p/ε.
- In terms of the average sound speed, the bound becomes c_s² ≤ (1 - ⟨c_s²⟩/3)/(1 + ⟨c_s²⟩).
- The conformal limit corresponds to c_s² = ⟨c_s²⟩ = 1/3, marked by dashed vertical and horizontal lines in the analysis.
- The gray band in the figure shows the perturbative QCD constraint, and threshold values at d_c = 0.2, β = 0, and γ = 1.75 are shown for comparison with the kinetic-theory bound.

## Context
This work extends prior analyses of the kinetic-theory bound originally derived by Olson (2000) and builds on the author's own recent work (Marczenko 2024, 2025) introducing the average sound speed as an integrated stiffness measure. The reformulation enables geometric visualization of the bound in the c_s²–⟨c_s²⟩ plane, which has been used in model-independent EOS constructions (Marczenko 2025) and applied to neutron-star EOSs by Moustakidis (2016), Margaritis (2019), and Laskos-Patkos (2024). The approach enables future fully statistical analyses of EOS ensembles incorporating the kinetic-theory constraint as an independent consistency requirement beyond causality and thermodynamic stability.
