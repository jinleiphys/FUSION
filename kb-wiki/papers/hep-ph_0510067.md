# Information theory point of view on multiparticle production processes

> Based on talks given at the 6th International Workshop on Very High Multiplicity Physics, JINR Dubna, 16-17 April 2005 (by. O.V.U.) and at the International Conference New Trends In High-Energy Physics, Yalta, Crimea, Ukraine, September 10-17, 2005 (G.W); 6 pages, 1 figure

## Key claim

The paper argues that multiparticle production data can be described using Information Theory (IT) with minimal assumptions, and that the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) in Tsallis entropy quantifies dynamical fluctuations present in the hadronizing system, but the method fails to fit \(e^+e^-\) data properly even with \(q<1\).

## Method

The authors apply the principle of maximum entropy using both Shannon entropy and Tsallis entropy to derive least-biased probability distributions for single-particle spectra. They impose constraints from energy conservation and normalization, with the inelasticity parameter \(K\) and the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) as fitted quantities. The resulting distributions are compared to experimental data for \(pp\), \(\bar{p}p\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) collisions, as well as \(e^+e^-\) annihilation.

## Key numbers

- For rapidity distributions, the longitudinal nonextensivity parameter is typically \(q_L \sim 1.2\), while the transverse parameter is \(q_T \sim 1.02\).
- The transverse parameter \(q_T\) is related to temperature fluctuations via \(q_T = 1 + \frac{\langle (1/\Lambda)^2 \rangle - \langle 1/\Lambda \rangle^2}{\langle 1/\Lambda \rangle^2}\).
- The longitudinal parameter \(q_L\) is related to the Negative Binomial multiplicity distribution via \(q_L = 1 + 1/k\), where \(k\) characterizes the distribution together with mean multiplicity \(\bar{n}\).

## Context

This work extends the original IT approach to multiparticle production by Chao (year not given in text) by incorporating nonextensive Tsallis entropy alongside Shannon entropy. The paper connects the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) to fluctuations in temperature (via superstatistics, Beck) and to the Negative Binomial multiplicity distribution (Kq). The approach enables a systematic way to identify minimal necessary assumptions for modeling hadronization, and suggests that failures to fit data (as in \(e^+e^-\) collisions) indicate the need for additional dynamical constraints or a true dynamical description.
