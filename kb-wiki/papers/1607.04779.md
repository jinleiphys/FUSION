# Bulk viscosity for pion and nucleon thermal fluctuation in the hadron resonance gas model

> We have calculated microscopically bulk viscosity of hadronic matter, where equilibrium thermodynamics for all hadrons in medium are described by Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. Considering pions and nucleons as abundant medium constituents, we have calculated their thermal widths, which inversely control the strength of bulk viscosities for respective components and represent their in-medium scattering probabilities with other mesonic and baryonic resonances, present in the medium. Our calculations show that bulk viscosity increases with both temperature and baryon chemical potential, whereas viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases with temperature and with baryon chemical potential, the ratio increases first and then decreases. The decreasing nature of the ratio with temperature is observed in most of the earlier investigations with few exceptions. We find that the temperature dependence of bulk viscosity crucially depends on the structure of the relaxation time. Along the chemical freeze-out line in nucleus-nucleus collisions with increasing collision energy, bulk viscosity as well as the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases, which also agrees with earlier references. Our results indicate the picture of a strongly coupled hadronic medium.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the bulk viscosity of hadronic matter by incorporating the thermal widths of pions and nucleons, derived from their in-medium scattering with mesonic and baryonic resonances, into the standard Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, finding that the temperature dependence of bulk viscosity crucially depends on the structure of the relaxation time, and that the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases with temperature but shows a non-monotonic (increase then decrease) behavior with baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The equilibrium thermodynamics of all hadrons up to 2 GeV masses is described by the HRG model, from which the speed of sound and other thermodynamic quantities are obtained. The thermal widths of pions and nucleons are computed from the imaginary parts of their self-energies, using one-loop diagrams with mesonic (πσ, πρ) and baryonic (πNB for pions, πB for nucleons) intermediate states, with effective Lagrangians for the vertices. These momentum- and medium-dependent widths are then inserted into the relaxation-time-approximation expressions for the bulk viscosity of the pion and nucleon components, which are summed to give the total bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- At vanishing baryon chemical potential (μ_B=0), the total bulk viscosity ζ_T increases with temperature; the ratio ζ/s decreases with temperature (e.g., from approximately 0.016 at T~0.120 GeV to 0.003 at T~0.175 GeV, as inferred from the text's discussion of earlier results and the present work's qualitative agreement).
- At fixed temperature, ζ increases with μ_B; the ratio ζ/s first increases then decreases with μ_B (e.g., at T=0.150 GeV, the ratio shows a peak at intermediate μ_B).
- Along the chemical freeze-out line in nucleus-nucleus collisions, both ζ and ζ/s decrease with increasing center-of-mass energy √s (qualitatively agreeing with earlier references such as Kadam1 and Kadam2).
- The speed of sound squared c_s^2 from HRG at μ_B=0 agrees with LQCD results (circles from Lat1) within the hadronic temperature domain (T < 0.160 GeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of bulk viscosity in the hadronic phase, such as those using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Sasaki et al.), the Linear Sigma Model (Dobado et al., Purnendu et al.), and previous HRG-based approaches (Kadam1, Kadam2, Sarwar, Noronha). The key novelty is the explicit calculation of the thermal widths of pions and nucleons from their in-medium scattering with resonances, rather than using constant or parametrized relaxation times. The results enable more realistic inputs for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of bulk viscosity near the QCD phase transition.
