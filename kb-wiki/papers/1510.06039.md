# Roy-Steiner-equation analysis of pion-nucleon scattering

> We review the structure of Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering, the solution for the partial waves of the t-channel process $ππ\to \bar N N$, as well as the high-accuracy extraction of the pion-nucleon S-wave scattering lengths from data on pionic hydrogen and deuterium. We then proceed to construct solutions for the lowest partial waves of the s-channel process $πN\to πN$ and demonstrate that accurate solutions can be found if the scattering lengths are imposed as constraints. Detailed error estimates of all input quantities in the solution procedure are performed and explicit parameterizations for the resulting low-energy phase shifts as well as results for subthreshold parameters and higher threshold parameters are presented. Furthermore, we discuss the extraction of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term via the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem, including the role of isospin-breaking corrections, to obtain a precision determination consistent with all constraints from analyticity, unitarity, crossing symmetry, and pionic-atom data. We perform the matching to chiral perturbation theory in the subthreshold region and detail the consequences for the chiral convergence of the threshold parameters and the nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that accurate solutions for the lowest partial waves of the s-channel process πN → πN can be found using Roy-Steiner equations if the scattering lengths extracted from pionic hydrogen and deuterium data are imposed as constraints, enabling a precision determination of the pion-nucleon σ-term consistent with all constraints from analyticity, unitarity, crossing symmetry, and pionic-atom data.

## Method

The authors construct Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering derived from hyperbolic dispersion relations, which relate the s-channel process πN → πN and the t-channel process ππ → N̅N. They solve the t-channel partial waves for ππ → N̅N, then use these as input to solve the s-channel equations for the lowest partial waves, imposing the scattering lengths from pionic atoms as constraints. Detailed error estimates of all input quantities are performed, and the resulting low-energy phase shifts are matched to chiral perturbation theory in the subthreshold region.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave scattering lengths extracted from pionic hydrogen and deuterium data are constrained at percent-level accuracy (explicit numerical values not given in the truncated text)
- The range of validity of the Roy equations from axiomatic field theory is smax = 60 Mπ² = (1.08 GeV)²
- Under Mandelstam analyticity assumptions, the range extends to smax = 68 Mπ² = (1.15 GeV)²
- The ππ scattering lengths from the combined Roy-equation and ChPT analysis are a0⁰ = 0.220 ± 0.005 and a0² = -0.0444 ± 0.0010

## Context

This work extends the Roy-equation methodology previously applied to ππ scattering (Roy, 1971; Ananthanarayan et al., 2000) and πK scattering (Buettiker et al., 2003) to the more complex πN system. The analysis resolves contradictions between the Karlsruhe-Helsinki and GWU/SAID partial-wave analyses by providing a representation consistent with all fundamental constraints. The results enable precision determination of the πN σ-term via the Cheng-Dashen low-energy theorem, which is crucial for interpreting dark-matter direct-detection experiments, searches for lepton-flavor violation, and nuclear-matter applications, as well as for determining low-energy constants that govern the long-range part of the nucleon-nucleon potential in nuclear chiral effective field theory.
