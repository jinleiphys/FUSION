# Exclusive scalar glueball $f_0(1500)$ production for FAIR and J-PARC energy range

> We evaluate differential distributions for exclusive scalar $f_0(1500)$ production for $p \bar p \to N_1 N_2 f_0$ (FAIR@GSI) and $p p \to p p f_0$ (J-PARC@Tokai). Both QCD diffractive and pion-pion meson exchange current (MEC) components are included. Rather large cross sections are predicted. The pion-pion component, never discussed in the literature, dominates close to the threshold while the diffractive component takes over for larger energies. The diffractive component is calculated based on two-gluon impact factors as well as in the framework of Khoze-Martin-Ryskin approach proposed for diffractive Higgs boson production. Different unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs) from the literature are used. The production of $f_0(1500)$ close to threshold could limit the so-called $πNN$ form factor in the region of larger pion virtualities.

# Exclusive scalar glueball $f_0(1500)$ production for FAIR and J-PARC energy range

## Key claim
The pion-pion meson exchange current (MEC) component, never discussed in the literature for this process, dominates exclusive $f_0(1500)$ production close to threshold, while the QCD diffractive component takes over at higher energies; the predicted cross sections are rather large but depend strongly on the assumed $\pi NN$ form factor cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ and on the unintegrated gluon distribution functions used.

## Method
The authors calculate differential cross sections for exclusive $f_0(1500)$ production in $p \bar p \to N_1 N_2 f_0$ (for FAIR) and $p p \to p p f_0$ (for J-PARC) using two separate mechanisms: a QCD diffractive component based on two-gluon impact factors and the Khoze-Martin-Ryskin (KMR) approach originally developed for diffractive Higgs production, and a pion-pion meson exchange current (MEC) component evaluated in the framework of meson exchange theory with monopole form factors. Different unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs) from the literature are employed, including Kharzeev-Levin, Kimber-Martin-Ryskin, and a mixed prescription combining small-x UGDFs with Gaussian smearing for the larger-x gluon.

## Key numbers
- Branching ratio $BR(f_0(1500) \to \pi \pi) = 0.349$ (34.9 $\pm$ 2.3 %) from PDG, used to normalize the pion-pion MEC amplitude.
- The $\pi NN$ form factor cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ is taken in the range 0.8 GeV to 1.2 GeV, producing the uncertainty band for the pion-pion component.
- The pion-pion MEC cross section for $p \bar p \to p \bar p f_0(1500)$ peaks at center-of-mass energy $W \approx$ 6-7 GeV, then drops with increasing energy.
- At $W = 10$ GeV, the gluonic contribution (Kharzeev-Levin UGDF) is about one order of magnitude smaller than the pion-pion component.
- The maximal energy planned for HESR at FAIR is $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ GeV.
- Close to threshold ($W = 2 m_N + m_{f_0(1500)}$), the average transferred four-momentum squared is about 1.5 GeV$^2$.
- The KMR approach uses a slope parameter $b_0 = 2$ GeV$^{-2}$ for the t-dependent form factor.
- The two-pion decay channel fraction is 34.9 $\pm$ 2.3 % of the total width.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological model of Close and Kirk (1997) for central exclusive $f_0(1500)$ production, which was fitted to unnormalized WA102 data at $\sqrt{s} \approx 30$ GeV and provided no absolute normalization or energy dependence. The present QCD-inspired approach provides absolute normalization, energy dependence, and rapidity dependence, unlike the earlier parametrization. The paper enables predictions for the PANDA experiment at FAIR and the J-PARC facility, where the pion-pion MEC mechanism close to threshold could constrain the $\pi NN$ form factor at larger pion virtualities, a region not previously studied. The diffractive component calculation follows the Khoze-Martin-Ryskin (2001) approach for exclusive Higgs production, previously applied to $\chi_c(0^+)$ production by Pasechnik, Szczurek, and Teryaev (2007) and to $\eta'$ production by Szczurek, Pasechnik, and Teryaev (2007).
