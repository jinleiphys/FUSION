# Neutral Kaon System in Dense Matter and Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Above a critical matter density the propagating modes of the neutral kaon system are essentially eigenstates of strangeness, but below it they are almost complete eigenstates of CP. We estimate the real and imaginary parts of the energies of these modes and their mixing at all densities up to nuclear matter density $2\times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$. In a heavy ion collision the strong interactions create eigenstates of strangeness, and these propagate adiabatically until the density has fallen to the critical value, whereupon the system undergoes a sudden transition to (near) eigenstates of CP. We estimate the critical density to be 20 g/cm$^3$, and that this density will be reached about $2\times 10^5$ fm/c after the end of the collision.

## Key claim

Above a critical matter density of about 20 g/cm³, the propagating modes of the neutral kaon system are essentially eigenstates of strangeness, but below that density they become almost complete eigenstates of CP; the transition between these regimes in a heavy-ion collision is sudden rather than adiabatic, contrary to what one might expect from a naive extrapolation.

## Method

The authors compute the real and imaginary parts of the neutral kaon self-energy in dense matter using two approaches: a low-density virial expansion based on kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, and an effective Lagrangian method incorporating the kaon-nucleon sigma term and vector density. They then solve a two-level Schrödinger equation with a time-dependent complex potential to model the evolution of the kaon system as the nuclear matter expands and dilutes after a heavy-ion collision, evaluating the adiabaticity of the transition from strangeness eigenstates to CP eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for transition between strangeness and CP eigenstates: \(\rho_c = 1.1 \times 10^{-14}\) nucleons/fm³, or about 19 g/cm³ (stated as 20 g/cm³ in the abstract).
- Time after the end of the collision when critical density is reached: about \(2 \times 10^5\) fm/c.
- Nuclear matter density: \(2 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm³ (0.155 nucleons/fm³).
- Vacuum kaon mass: \(m_K = 497.67\) MeV.
- Kaon mass difference: \(\Delta m = 3.52 \times 10^{-12}\) MeV.
- Short-lived kaon decay width: \(\Gamma_S = 7.38 \times 10^{-12}\) MeV.
- Long-lived kaon decay width: \(\Gamma_L = 1.27 \times 10^{-14}\) MeV.
- Ratio \(\Delta m / \Delta \Gamma = 0.478 \pm 0.003\).
- Difference in forward scattering amplitudes (Eberhard-Uchiyama estimate): \(\langle \bar{f} - f \rangle \approx 0.3(1+i)\) fm.
- Difference in forward scattering amplitudes (Shuryak-Thorsson estimate): \(\mathrm{Re}\, \langle \bar{f} - f \rangle \approx 1.5\) fm.
- Real part of \(F - \bar{F}\) from effective Lagrangian: \((260\ \mathrm{MeV})^2 (\omega/m_K) (\rho_N / 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3})\).
- Dimensionless ratio \(\chi\) at density 0.1 nucleons/fm³: \(\chi = 6.3 \times 10^{12}\).
- s-wave scattering length difference: \(\bar{f}_0 - f_0 = 0.1 + 0.6i\) fm.
- Time to pass through the transition region: about \(3 \times 10^5\) fm/c.
- Natural oscillation time of neutral kaons: \(1/\Delta m = 5.6 \times 10^{13}\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon regeneration in ordinary matter (Eberhard and Uchiyama, 1989) to the regime of dense nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions, and contrasts with the vacuum behavior where weak interactions dominate. It enables predictions for the ratio of long-lived to short-lived kaons observed in heavy-ion experiments at the AGS (Brookhaven, 11 GeV/nucleon Au-Au, ratio 6) and SPS (CERN, 160 GeV/nucleon Pb-Pb, ratio 2), showing that contrary to all other high-energy experiments, the ratio could be far from unity if the evolution were adiabatic, but the sudden transition found here predicts a ratio of 1.
