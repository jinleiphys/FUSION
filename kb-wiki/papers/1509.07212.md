# Heavy Quarkonium in a Holographic Basis

> We study the heavy quarkonium within the basis light-front quantization approach. We implement the one-gluon exchange interaction and a confining potential inspired by light-front holography. We adopt the holographic light-front wavefunction (LFWF) as our basis function and solve the non-perturbative dynamics by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix. We obtain the mass spectrum for charmonium and bottomonium. With the obtained LFWFs, we also compute the decay constants and the charge form factors for selected eigenstates. The results are compared with the experimental measurements and with other established methods.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that combining a holographic confining potential with a one-gluon exchange interaction within the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) framework yields heavy quarkonium mass spectra, decay constants, and charge form factors in reasonable agreement with experimental data and other theoretical approaches, though the decay constants are systematically larger than PDG data and the charge radii are systematically smaller than lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger results.

## Method

The authors construct an effective light-front Hamiltonian consisting of a holographic soft-wall confining potential (modified to include quark masses and longitudinal dynamics via a new longitudinal confinement term) plus a one-gluon exchange interaction with a counterterm to remove ultraviolet divergences. They expand the light-front wavefunctions in a basis of 2D harmonic oscillator functions (transverse) and Jacobi polynomials (longitudinal), truncate the basis by parameters N_max and L_max, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian matrix numerically using LAPACK to obtain mass eigenvalues and eigenfunctions for charmonium and bottomonium.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium coupling α_s = 0.3595, bottomonium coupling α_s = 0.25 (fixed via leading-order pQCD evolution)
- Fitted confining strength κ = 0.950 GeV (charmonium) and 1.491 GeV (bottomonium) at N_max = L_max = 16
- Fitted quark mass m_c = 1.510 GeV, m_b = 4.761 GeV at N_max = L_max = 16
- Root-mean-square deviation from experimental masses below D̄D threshold: 64 MeV (8 charmonium states, m_J=0) and 56 MeV (14 bottomonium states, m_J=0) at N_max = L_max = 16
- Gluon mass regulator μ_g = 0.02 GeV (taken much smaller than κ)
- Mean squared radius of J/ψ: 0.0441(8) fm²; of η_c: 0.038(5) fm²; of Υ: 0.0149(5) fm²; of η_b: 0.0146(8) fm² (extrapolated from N_max = 8, 16, 24)

## Context

This work extends the light-front holographic QCD approach of Brodsky and de Téramond (2009) by adding a realistic one-gluon exchange interaction, improving upon the pure holographic confinement model. It builds on previous BLFQ applications to positronium by Wiecki et al. (2015) and Vary et al. (2015), and on the one-gluon exchange formulation from Kaluza (1991), Krautgartner (1992), and Trittmann (1997). The results enable future extensions to higher Fock sectors (including sea quarks and gluons) and provide a computational platform for relativistic many-body bound-state problems in QCD.
