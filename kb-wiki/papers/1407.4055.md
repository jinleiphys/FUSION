# Nuclear Pasta Matter for Different Proton Fractions

> Nuclear matter under astrophysical conditions is explored with time-dependent and static Hartree-Fock calculations. The focus is in a regime of densities where matter segregates into liquid and gaseous phases unfolding a rich scenario of geometries, often called nuclear pasta shapes (e.g. spaghetti, lasagna). Particularly the appearance of the different phases depending on the proton fraction and the transition to uniform matter are investigated. In this context the neutron background density is of special interest, because it plays a crucial role for the type of pasta shape which is built. The study is performed in two dynamical ranges, one for hot matter and one at temperature zero to investigate the effect of cooling.

## Key claim

The paper finds a clear distinction between low proton fractions (X_p ≤ 0.13) and higher ones (X_p = 0.2–0.5), with the low-proton-fraction regime showing compressed density ranges for pasta shapes and unbound homogeneous matter, while the higher-proton-fraction regime behaves robustly and similarly across a wide range of proton fractions.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional, symmetry-unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Skyrme force SLy6, starting from stochastic distributions of α-particles and excess neutrons in a 16×16×16 fm³ periodic box. They generated thermal states with temperatures in the 2–7 MeV range by dynamical evolution, then cooled selected configurations to zero temperature via static Hartree-Fock calculations. The resulting pasta shapes were classified by geometry (sphere, rod, slab, bubble, etc.) and characterized by liquid density, gas (neutron background) density, and volume fraction.

## Key numbers

- The critical proton fraction for binding is X_p ≈ 0.13; below this value homogeneous matter is unbound, above it binding sets on quickly.
- Equilibrium densities for bound homogeneous matter saturate around ρ_nm = 0.12–0.16 fm⁻³ for X_p ≥ 0.13.
- Temperatures in the dynamical simulations lie between 2 and 7 MeV, with lower X_p and larger ρ associated with lower temperatures.
- The border between rod and rod(2) shapes occurs above ρ = 0.05 fm⁻³ for X_p = 0.1 and moves to about 0.04 fm⁻³ for symmetric matter (X_p = 0.5).
- For X_p > 0.29, the neutron background density vanishes completely in zero-temperature calculations; for X_p = 0.33, neutron Fermi energies are slightly below zero, indicating no neutron gas.
- Liquid phase densities for X_p = 0.1 are lower than for X_p ≥ 0.2, consistent with the equilibrium densities from the equation of state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TDHF study at X_p = 1/3 (Schuetrumpf 2013) to arbitrary proton fractions, providing a systematic map of pasta shapes across density and proton fraction. It complements classical molecular dynamics studies (Dorso 2012), the TDHF-like DYWAN model (Sebille 2011), and Thomas-Fermi calculations with relativistic mean-field models (Okamoto 2012). The results enable improved modeling of nuclear pasta in core-collapse supernova and proto-neutron star environments, particularly for understanding how deleptonization and cooling affect pasta geometry and neutron gas content.
