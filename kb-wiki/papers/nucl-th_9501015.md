# Another Look at ``soft'' lepton pair production in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung

> We compare different formalisms for the calculation of lepton pair emission in hadron-hadron collisions and discuss the consequences of the approximations inherent to each of them. Using a Lorentz-covariant and gauge-invariant formalism proposed by Lichard, we calculate lepton pair emission via bremsstrahlung in proton-proton and neutron-proton reactions at energies between 1 and 5 GeV to leading order in the virtual photon four-momentum. These new results suggest that some previous bremsstrahlung calculations based on varieties of the soft-photon approximation might have somewhat overestimated the DLS low-mass dilepton cross sections. We find that the respective intensities of dilepton production through $p p$ and $n p$ bremsstrahlung are energy-dependent and become comparable at $E_{\rm kin} \agt $ 2 GeV. We also calculate the polar angle anisotropy of the lepton spectrum.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Lorentz-covariant and gauge-invariant formalism for lepton pair bremsstrahlung, when filtered through the DLS experimental acceptance, reduces the predicted low-mass dielectron yields by a factor of 2-3 for neutron-proton and 2-5 for proton-proton collisions at 4.9 GeV compared to previous soft-photon approximation calculations, but that the uncorrected cross sections differ only by a factor of 2/3 at small invariant masses.

## Method

The authors calculate lepton pair emission via bremsstrahlung in proton-proton and neutron-proton reactions at kinetic energies between 1 and 5 GeV using a Lorentz-covariant and gauge-invariant formalism proposed by Lichard (1994), working to leading order in the virtual photon four-momentum. They compare results obtained with the full virtual-photon electromagnetic current against those using the real-photon current (Eq. 5) combined with Ruckl's formula, and also investigate the effect of exact treatment of many-body phase space versus the approximate phase-space correction factor. The calculations use parametrizations of elastic nucleon-nucleon differential cross sections from Lawson (1993) and Richard-Serre et al. (1980) and are filtered through the DLS experimental acceptance.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of cross sections using the virtual-photon current versus the real-photon current equals 2/3 for small invariant masses M ≤ 10 MeV for both pp and np processes.
- At 4.9 GeV kinetic energy, after DLS acceptance filtering, previous calculations overestimated low-mass dielectron yields by a factor of 2-3 for np bremsstrahlung and a factor of 2-5 for pp bremsstrahlung.
- The ratio R = (dσ/dM²)_pp / (dσ/dM²)_pn at small invariant masses is nearly 0.5 at 2 GeV kinetic energy and becomes larger than 1 at 4.9 GeV.
- At large invariant masses M > 0.5 GeV, previous bremsstrahlung calculations underestimate the dilepton yields by at most a factor of 2 even at 4.9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lichard (1994) on the proper Lorentz-covariant formulation of soft virtual photon bremsstrahlung, applying it to nucleon-nucleon collisions in the 1-5 GeV energy range relevant to DLS measurements at Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory. The results contradict earlier expectations based on classical multipole arguments that np bremsstrahlung should dominate over pp bremsstrahlung, confirming the finding of Haglin (1994) that the two processes are comparable at 4.9 GeV. The paper enables more reliable calculations of bremsstrahlung contributions to dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions, which must be disentangled from other sources such as η and Δ Dalitz decays and ππ annihilation.
