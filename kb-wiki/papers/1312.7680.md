# Kadanoff-Baym approach to the thermal resonant leptogenesis

> Using the non-equilibrium Green function method (Kadanoff-Baym equations) in the expanding universe, we investigate evolution of the lepton number asymmetry when the right-handed (RH) neutrinos have almost degenerate masses $|M_i^2-M_j^2| \ll M_i^2$. # Kadanoff-Baym approach to the thermal resonant leptogenesis

## Key claim
Using the non-equilibrium Green function method (Kadanoff-Baym equations) in an expanding universe, the authors derive the resonantly enhanced CP-violating parameter for right-handed neutrino decay and find that the regulator in the enhancement factor is \(R_{ij}=M_i \Gamma_i + M_j \Gamma_j\), consistent with Garny et al. (2011) in a constant background, but differing from the regulators \(R_{ij}=M_i \Gamma_j\) obtained by Pilaftsis (2003) and \(R_{ij}=|M_i \Gamma_i - M_j \Gamma_j|\) obtained by Buchmuller and Plumacher (1997).

## Method
The authors apply the Kadanoff-Baym equations, derived from the closed-time-path formalism and the 2PI effective action, to compute the evolution of lepton number asymmetry in a spatially flat expanding universe with scale factor \(a(t)\). They focus on the simplest 2PI diagram involving Yukawa interactions between right-handed neutrinos, SM lepton doublets, and the Higgs doublet, which captures the dominant decay and inverse-decay processes in the resonant regime. The CP-violating parameter \(\varepsilon_i\) is extracted directly from the evolution equation for lepton number by analyzing the off-diagonal Wightman functions of the right-handed neutrinos, with particular attention to how quantum flavor oscillations and deviations from thermal equilibrium affect the regulator.

## Key numbers
- The CP-violating parameter \(\varepsilon_i\) is proportional to the enhancement factor \((M_i^2 - M_j^2) M_i \Gamma_j / ((M_i^2 - M_j^2)^2 + R_{ij}^2)\), where the regulator is \(R_{ij} = M_i \Gamma_i + M_j \Gamma_j\).
- The decay width of the right-handed neutrino is \(\Gamma_i \simeq (h^\dagger h)_{ii} M_i / 8\pi\).
- The Hubble parameter in the radiation-dominated universe is \(H(t) = 1.66 \sqrt{g_*} \, T^2 / M_{pl} \sim T^2 / 10^{18}\,\text{GeV}\).
- The Davidson-Ibarra bound requires \(M \gtrsim 10^9\,\text{GeV}\) for hierarchical masses, but resonant leptogenesis can work for right-handed neutrino masses as light as the TeV scale.

## Context
This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym approach to leptogenesis previously applied to hierarchical masses by Buchmuller and Fredenhagen (2000) and to resonant leptogenesis in flat space-time by De Simone and Riotto (2007). It resolves a long-standing discrepancy among regulators: Pilaftsis (2003) obtained \(R_{ij}=M_i \Gamma_j\) from resummed propagator methods, while Buchmuller and Plumacher (1997) obtained \(R_{ij}=|M_i \Gamma_i - M_j \Gamma_j|\). The result \(R_{ij}=M_i \Gamma_i + M_j \Gamma_j\) matches the finding of Garny et al. (2011) in a constant background with an out-of-equilibrium initial state. The authors show that neglecting off-shell contributions would erroneously yield \(R_{ij}=M_i \Gamma_i - M_j \Gamma_j\). This work enables precise predictions for TeV-scale resonant leptogenesis, which is relevant for models such as the classically conformal \(U(1)_{B-L}\) extension of the Standard Model proposed by Iso, Okada, and Orikasa (2009).
