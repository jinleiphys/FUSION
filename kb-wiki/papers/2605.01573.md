# The atomic nucleus as a bound system of $3A$ quarks

> The atomic nucleus, viewed as a system of bound quarks, should, in principle, be described within an effective theory of low-energy quantum chromodynamics. This paper provides an overview of recently developed models that embody essential features of the desired effective theory. The Fermi gas model helps explain why the number of $d$ quarks is approximately equal to that of $u$ quarks in stable light nuclei up to ${\rm {}^{40}_{20}Ca}$. A modified bag model accounts for the deviation from this rule in heavier nuclei. With this model, the static properties of a wide range of stable nuclei can be described with reasonable accuracy. To make the most of the modified bag model, it is useful to invoke gauge/gravity duality. A refined version of duality states: ``The dynamics inside an extremal black hole in ${\rm AdS}_5$ is mapped onto the corresponding dynamics of a stable subnuclear system in ${\mathbb R}_{1,3}$''. This version of duality allows one to predict the primary decay channel of the lightest glueball. Another implication is that this framework explains why the periodic table contains a finite number of stable elements. Duality makes it possible to calculate the maximum allowed charge $Z_{\rm max}$ of stable heavy nuclei: $Z_{\rm max}\approx 82$, which is the charge of the ${\rm {}^{208}_{82}Pb}$ nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Yukawa meson-exchange picture of nuclear binding has a very limited range of applicability and that a modified bag model, combined with gauge/gravity duality, can describe stable nuclei as bound systems of \(3A\) quarks, with the claim that the maximum allowed charge of stable heavy nuclei is \(Z_{\rm max}\approx 82\) (the charge of \({}^{208}_{82}{\rm Pb}\)), though this result depends on the specific refined version of duality and the modified bag model parameters.

## Method

The authors develop a modified bag model where a single quark in a nucleus is described by a Dirac Hamiltonian with vector and scalar mean-field potentials, subject to a pseudospin symmetry condition that converts the bare quark mass into a dressed mass and produces an effective potential with a singularity at a finite radius, identified as the nuclear radius. They solve the resulting Schrodinger-like equation numerically with parameters \(\alpha_s=0.7\), \(\sigma=0.1\ {\rm GeV}^2\), and \(m=0.33\ {\rm GeV}\), and compare the average mean-field energy density with the degeneracy pressure energy density from a Fermi gas model to analyze the balance of forces in stable nuclei from \({}^{40}_{20}{\rm Ca}\) to \({}^{208}_{82}{\rm Pb}\). Gauge/gravity duality is invoked to calculate the maximum allowed charge and predict the primary decay channel of the lightest glueball.

## Key numbers

* The maximum allowed charge of stable heavy nuclei is \(Z_{\rm max}\approx 82\), which is the charge of the \({}^{208}_{82}{\rm Pb}\) nucleus.
* The coefficient \(\gamma\) in the Weizsacker formula for the term associated with the Pauli exclusion principle is 32 times greater than the coefficient \(\delta\) for the Coulomb interproton repulsion term.
* For stable light nuclei up to \({}^{40}_{20}{\rm Ca}\), the number of \(d\) quarks is approximately equal to the number of \(u\) quarks, with deviations such as \(n_d/n_u\approx 1.077\) for \({}^{9}_{4}{\rm Be}\), \(n_d/n_u\approx 1.036\) for \({}^{19}_{9}{\rm F}\), \(n_d/n_u\approx 1.029\) for \({}^{23}_{11}{\rm Na}\), \(n_d/n_u\approx 1.025\) for \({}^{27}_{13}{\rm Al}\), and \(n_d/n_u\approx 1.022\) for \({}^{31}_{15}{\rm P}\).
* For nuclei with \(Z\) in the range \(20\le Z\le 82\), the neutron excess \(\Delta N\) varies approximately linearly with \(Z\) as \(\Delta N=0.71(Z-20)\).
* The cavity radii \(r_\ast\) scale as \(R_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(R_0\approx 1\ {\rm fm}\), in agreement with the empirical relation \(R=R_0 A^{1/3}\).
* The calculated magnetic dipole moments agree with most experimental data within \(\sim 10\%\) (with a few \(\sim 20\%\) outliers) for a large set of stable isotopes.
* The parameters used in the numerical solution are \(\alpha_s=0.7\), \(\sigma=0.1\ {\rm GeV}^2\), and \(m=0.33\ {\rm GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the bag model approach from hadrons (Chodos 1974, DeTar 1983) to nuclei, addressing limitations of the Yukawa meson-exchange paradigm (Iwanenko 1932, Heisenberg 1932, Yukawa 1935) and chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1990, Epelbaum 2009, Machleidt 2011) which fail to explain the composition of stable nuclei, the absence of spontaneous pion emission, and the finite number of stable elements. The modified bag model builds on earlier quark-based nuclear models (Petry 1985, Maltman 1992) and the Cornell potential (Eichten 1975), while the gauge/gravity duality refinement (Kosyakov 2020-2025) enables predictions for the lightest glueball decay channel and the maximum stable nuclear charge, explaining why the periodic table ends at \({}^{208}_{82}{\rm Pb}\).
