# Isoscaling as a measure of Symmetry Energy in the Lattice Gas Model

> The energetic properties of nuclear clusters inside a low-density, finite-temperature medium are studied with a Lattice Gas Model including isospin dependence and Coulomb forces. Important deviations are observed respect to the Fisher approximation of an ideal gas of non-interacting clusters, but the global energetics can still be approximately expressed in terms of a simple modified energy-density functional. The multi-fragmentation regime appears dominated by combinatorial effects in this model, but the isoscaling of the largest fragment in low energy collisions appears a promising observable for the experimental measurement of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Lattice Gas Model, the symmetry energy extracted from standard isoscaling formulas applied to light fragments is dominated by combinatorial effects and does not reflect the true symmetry energy of the system; however, the isotopic distribution of the largest fragment produced in each event provides a much better measure of the symmetry energy, except near the transition temperature where large energy fluctuations occur.

## Method

The authors simulate a system of 150 nucleons (with varying isospin asymmetry from δ = -1/3 to δ = 1/3) on a cubic lattice of 8000 cells using a Hamiltonian with nearest-neighbor isospin-dependent interactions, Coulomb repulsion between protons, and kinetic energy. They sample the isobar canonical ensemble via Metropolis Monte Carlo at pressure P/P_c = 0.3 and temperatures from T/T_t = 0.88 to T/T_t = 1.1, then fit the average interaction energy to a liquid-drop inspired macroscopic parametrization with density-dependent volume, surface, symmetry, and Coulomb terms. They also compute isotopic yields and apply isoscaling formulas to extract symmetry energy estimates from both light fragments and the heaviest cluster.

## Key numbers

- The lattice spacing is r_0 = 1.8 fm, chosen so that full occupation gives saturation density ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- The nearest-neighbor coupling between protons and neutrons is ε_{1,-1} = 5.5 MeV; the Coulomb coupling constant is I_c = 1.44 MeV/fm.
- Eleven systems of mass number A = 150 with isospin ratios from δ = -1/3 to δ = 1/3 are simulated.
- The average volume varies between ρ/ρ_t = 0.7 and ρ/ρ_t = 1.8 over the temperature range T/T_t = 0.88 to T/T_t = 1.1.
- The global chi-squared per degree of freedom for the liquid-drop fit is χ²/N_dof = 5.
- The isoscaling parameter α is averaged over fragments from Z = 2 to Z = 7 for the symmetry energy extraction.

## Context

This work extends Lattice Gas Model studies of nuclear fragmentation (Das 2005, Campi 2005) by including isospin dependence and Coulomb forces, and it tests the validity of the Fisher approximation of non-interacting clusters. The paper directly addresses the controversy over whether isoscaling can reliably extract the symmetry energy, as proposed by Botvina (2002), and shows that the standard formula fails due to combinatorial effects, consistent with findings by Dasgupta (2005) and Claudio (2007). The result that the largest fragment's isotopic distribution tracks the symmetry energy better than light fragments suggests a new experimental approach, which is being pursued at Texas AandM with the MARS separator (Souliotis 2007) and at GANIL with VAMOS and INDRA (Chbihi 2007).
