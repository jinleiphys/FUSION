# Phase Transition in the chiral $σ$-$ω$ model with dilatons

> We investigate the properties of different modifications to the linear $σ$-model (including a dilaton field associated with broken scale invariance) at finite baryon density $ρ$ and nonzero temperature $T$. The explicit breaking of chiral symmetry and the way the vector meson mass is generated are significant for the appearance of a phase of nearly vanishing nucleon mass besides the solution describing normal nuclear matter. The elimination of the abnormal solution prohibits the onset of a chiral phase transition but allows to lower the compressibility to a reasonable range. The repulsive contributions from the vector mesons are responsible for the wide range of stability of the normal phase in the $(μ,T)$-plane. The abnormal solution becomes not only energetically preferable to the normal state at high temperature or density, but also mechanically stable due to the inclusion of dilatons.

## Key claim

The existence of a chiral phase transition (a phase of nearly vanishing nucleon mass) in the linear sigma-omega model depends critically on how the vector meson mass is generated and on the inclusion of explicit chiral symmetry breaking; the abnormal solution can be eliminated entirely by using a pure sigma-omega coupling for the vector meson mass or by including a logarithmic dilaton potential, which then prevents the chiral phase transition but allows the nuclear matter compressibility to be lowered to a physically reasonable range.

## Method

The authors extended the linear sigma model to include an isoscalar vector omega meson and a scalar dilaton field chi, with the vector meson mass generated dynamically by coupling to sigma and chi fields via a mixing parameter r. They applied the mean-field approximation at finite baryon density and temperature, solving the coupled equations of motion for the sigma, chi, and omega fields to compute the thermodynamic potential, free energy, and pressure. The model parameters were fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy -16 MeV at density 0.15 fm^-3), and the phase structure was mapped in the chemical potential-temperature plane for different parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: E_B = -16 MeV
- Pion mass fixed at m_pi = 138 MeV
- Vector meson mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Compressibility K for parameter set I: 1464 MeV; set II: 1403 MeV; set III: 669 MeV; set IV: 387 MeV; set V: 356 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation for set I: m_N*/m_N = 0.66; set II: 0.67; set III: 0.78; set IV: 0.84; set V: 0.80
- In the minimal model (set I), a chiral phase transition occurs at T = 168 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0
- The abnormal minimum corresponds to nucleon effective mass approximately 0.02 m_N
- The normal minimum corresponds to nucleon effective mass approximately 0.6-0.7 m_N
- Lower bound on lambda in the minimal model to prevent abnormal minimum at ground state density: lambda = 150

## Context

This work extends the chiral sigma-omega model studies of Mishustin, Bondorf, and Rho (1993) and Heide, Rudaz, and Ellis (1994) to finite temperature, investigating the phase structure in the chemical potential-temperature plane. It contrasts with the earlier work of Glendenning (1986), who found no region of chiral symmetry restoration because the abnormal phase was mechanically unstable; the inclusion of dilatons in the present model makes the abnormal phase mechanically stable. The paper shows that the repulsive omega-meson coupling leads to an open phase boundary, unlike the closed boundary found by Wakamatsu and Hayashi (1980) in the linear sigma model without omega mesons or dilatons. The results enable future studies of whether the predicted abnormal phase can be reached in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and motivate extensions to SU(3) chiral models.
