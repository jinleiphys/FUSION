# Microscopic Calculation of in-Medium Nucleon-Nucleon Cross Ssections

> We derive in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections in a microscopic model. Our calculations are based upon the Bonn NN potential and the Dirac-Brueckner approach for nuclear matter. We consider energies up to 300 MeV (in the laboratory frame) and densities up to twice nuclear matter density. Our results deviate substantially from cross section parametrizations that are commonly used in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim
In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections derived from the Bonn potential and Dirac-Brueckner approach deviate substantially from the commonly used parametrizations of Cugnon et al., particularly in their density dependence and anisotropy, with the effect that the Cugnon parametrization underestimates the anisotropy of in-medium np differential cross sections and either underestimates or overestimates total cross sections depending on energy and density.

## Method
The authors solved the in-medium Thompson equation (relativistic Bethe-Goldstone equation) for the G-matrix using the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential, with the Pauli operator, dispersion effects, and medium-modified Dirac spinors (replacing the free nucleon mass m by an effective mass m̃ = m + U_S). The scalar and vector fields U_S and U_V were determined self-consistently through iteration between the Dirac equation and the G-matrix equation. In-medium phase shifts were extracted from partial-wave G-matrix elements and used to compute differential and total cross sections for laboratory energies up to 300 MeV and densities up to twice nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.18 fm⁻³).

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density used: ρ₀ = 0.18 fm⁻³
- Energies considered: up to 300 MeV in the laboratory frame
- Densities considered: up to 2ρ₀
- Bonn A potential deuteron D-state probability: P_D = 4.47%
- In-medium np total cross sections decrease substantially with increasing density, particularly at low incident energies (e.g., below 150 MeV)
- At 300 MeV laboratory energy, the microscopic differential cross section retains anisotropy at all densities considered, unlike the isotropic Cugnon parametrization
- A parametrization for the total np cross section is given: σ_np(E_lab, ρ) = (31.5 + 0.092·abs(20.2 − E_lab^0.53)^2.9) · (1.0 + 0.0034·E_lab^1.51·ρ²) / (1.0 + 21.55·ρ^1.34), with E_lab in MeV and ρ in fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Shakin and co-workers (1980s) to compute in-medium NN cross sections, contrasting with earlier non-relativistic Brueckner calculations by Faessler and co-workers (1980s) which found an enhancement of cross sections below 150 MeV. The results disagree with the density-independent parametrization of Cugnon et al. (1980s) commonly used in transport models like BUU and QMD. The calculations enable more accurate treatment of two-body collisions in relativistic transport models (RBUU, RQMD) for intermediate-energy heavy-ion reactions, and provide a data file for interpolation of differential cross sections over densities 0–3ρ₀ and energies 0–300 MeV.
