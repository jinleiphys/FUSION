# Scaling and universality in two dimensions: three-body bound states with short-ranged interactions

> The momentum space zero-range model is used to investigate universal properties of three interacting particles confined to two dimensions. The pertinent equations are first formulated for a system of two identical and one distinct particle and the two different two-body subsystems are characterized by two-body energies and masses. The three-body energy in units of one of the two-body energies is a universal function of the other two-body energy and the mass ratio. We derive convenient analytical formulae for calculations of the three-body energy as function of these two independent parameters and exhibit the results as universal curves. In particular, we show that the three-body system can have any number of stable bound states. When the mass ratio of the distinct to identical particles is greater than 0.22 we find that at most two stable bound states exist, while for two heavy and one light mass an increasing number of bound states is possible. The specific number of stable bound states depends on the ratio of two-body bound state energies and on the mass ratio and we map out an energy-mass phase-diagram of the number of stable bound states. Realizable systems of both fermions and bosons are discussed in this framework.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a three-body system in two dimensions can have any number of stable bound states depending on the mass ratio and the ratio of two-body binding energies, but this universality is conditional: when the mass ratio of the distinct particle to the identical particles is greater than 0.22, at most two stable bound states exist, while for two heavy and one light particle an increasing number of bound states becomes possible.

## Method

The authors used a momentum-space zero-range model to formulate Faddeev equations for a system of two identical particles (A) and one distinct particle (B) confined to two dimensions. They solved the coupled homogeneous integral equations numerically to obtain the three-body binding energy as a function of the scaled two-body energy ratio (E_AA/E_AB) and the mass ratio (m_B/m_A), producing universal curves and an energy-mass phase diagram of the number of stable bound states.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons (m=1, epsilon_AA=1), the ground state scaled three-body energy is epsilon_3^(0)=16.52 and the first excited state is epsilon_3^(1)=1.27.
- For m=1, the first excited state exists only when the scaled two-body energy is in the interval 1.00x10^(-5) <= epsilon_AA <= 2.36.
- The threshold mass ratio for at most two stable bound states is m_B/m_A > 0.22.
- For the asymptotic large-epsilon_AA limit, the function G_1(m) takes values: G_1(0.1) ~ 4657, G_1(1.0) ~ 5751, G_1(10.0) ~ 12332.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of three-body bound states in two dimensions, which had established that for three equal-mass particles only two bound states exist (Nielsen et al., 1997; Tjon, 1979). The paper generalizes these results to arbitrary mass ratios and two-body interaction energies, showing a much richer structure than previously considered. The findings enable predictions for ultracold atomic gas experiments in quasi-2D setups, including heteronuclear mixtures such as 40K-87Rb, and provide a framework for understanding three-body states in systems with long-range interactions across multi-layer configurations.
