# Resonances in the solar neutrino capture cross-section for $ ^{76}Ge $ nuclei

> The calculation results of solar neutrino capture cross-section on the $^{76}Ge$ nucleus are presented. The charge-exchange strength function $S(E)$ was obtained from the experiment data in the $ ^{76}Ge(^{3}He, t)^{76}As $ reaction. The calculation includes the effect of the resonant structure of the strength function $S(E)$ on the calculated cross section $σ(E_ν)$. It is shown that only the giant Gamow-Teller resonance contributes about 20%, and an even greater contribution is made by excited states located lower in the continuous part of the spectrum. These contributions should be taken into account in the calculation of background events in experiments on double beta decay of the GERDA type (for example LEGEND).

## Key claim

The giant Gamow-Teller resonance contributes only about 20% to the solar neutrino capture cross-section on \(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\), while excited states in the continuous part of the spectrum below the neutron separation energy provide an even larger contribution; accounting for these continuous states increases the total capture rate by up to 50% relative to calculations using only discrete levels.

## Method

The charge-exchange strength function \(S(E)\) for the \(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}(^{3}\mathrm{He}, t)^{76}\mathrm{As}\) reaction was taken from experimental data with 30 keV resolution obtained at the University of Osaka cyclotron. The total neutrino capture cross-section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{total}}(E_\nu)\) was computed as the sum of a discrete part (using tabulated excited states up to 5 MeV) and a resonant part (integrating the strength function from 5 MeV to the neutron separation energy \(S_n = 7.3285\) MeV). The Gamow-Teller resonance was parameterized with a Breit-Wigner form using parameters \(\omega_i = 11.3\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 3.3\) MeV, and the capture rate was obtained by convolving the cross-section with solar neutrino fluxes from the BS05(OP) model.

## Key numbers

* Total capture rate of solar neutrinos by \(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\): 23.58 SNU (1 SNU = \(10^{-36}\) (nucleon·s)\(^{-1}\)).
* Capture rate from discrete levels only (below 5 MeV): 15.9 SNU, in agreement with the prior result of 15.6 SNU from reference [10].
* Capture rate from discrete levels plus the Gamow-Teller resonance tail (5 MeV to \(S_n\)): 19.85 SNU.
* Contribution of the \(^{8}\mathrm{B}\) neutrino channel to the total capture rate: 21.17 SNU.
* Neutron separation energy of \(^{76}\mathrm{As}\): \(S_n = 7.3285\) MeV.
* Threshold energy for neutrino capture: \(Q_{EC} = 921.51\) keV.
* Gamow-Teller resonance energy: \(E_{\mathrm{GTR}} \approx 11.3\) MeV; resonance width: \(\Gamma = 3.3\) MeV.
* Isobaric analog state energy: \(E_{\mathrm{IAS}} = 8.308\) MeV.
* Sum-rule normalization: \(\sum_i M_i^2 = 3\cdot(N-Z) = 36\).
* Parameter \(\alpha\) for the width function: \(\alpha \approx 0.018\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
* Parameter \(\beta\) for the width function: \(\beta = 6.9 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of solar neutrino capture on \(^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\) (e.g., reference [10], which reported 15.6 SNU for discrete transitions) by including the resonant structure of the continuous excitation spectrum for the first time. The results are directly relevant to background estimation in double-beta decay experiments such as GERDA and the next-generation LEGEND experiment, where neutrino-induced events can mimic the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) signal at 2039 keV. The methodology can be applied to other target nuclei and to the secondary process \(^{76}\mathrm{As} \rightarrow ^{76}\mathrm{Se} + e^{-} + \widetilde{\nu_e}\) in future work.
