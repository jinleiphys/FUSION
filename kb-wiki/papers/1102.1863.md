# First indications of the existence of a 38 MeV light scalar boson

> We present evidence for the existence of a light scalar particle that most probably couples exclusively to gluons and quarks. Theoretical and phenomenological arguments are presented to support the existence of a light scalar boson for confinement and quark-pair creation. Previously observed interference effects allow to set a narrow window for the scalar's mass and also for its flavor-mass-dependent coupling to quarks. Here, in order to find a direct signal indicating its production, we study published BABAR data on leptonic bottomonium decays. We observe a clear excess signal in the invariant-mass projections of electron-positron and muon-antimuon, which may be due to the emission of a so far unobserved scalar particle with a mass of about 38 MeV. In the process of our analysis, we also find an indication of the existence of a bottomonium-like hybrid state at about 10.061 GeV. Further signals might be interpreted as replicas of the light scalar, with masses roughly two and three times as large.

## Key claim

The authors present evidence for a light scalar particle with a mass of about 38 MeV, which they interpret as a glueball-like boson related to confinement and quark-pair creation, based on excess signals in published BABAR data on leptonic bottomonium decays; however, they acknowledge that each individual signal is much too small to make firm claims, and the overall significance is limited.

## Method

The authors analyzed published BABAR data (347.5 fb⁻¹) on the reactions e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻Υ(1,2³S₁) → π⁺π⁻ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ (ℓ = e, μ), studying the invariant-mass distributions of the lepton pairs. They subtracted a Gaussian distribution around the Υ(1³S₁) peak to isolate excess and deficit signals, and examined these residuals for structure at multiples of approximately 38 MeV. They also compared their findings with independent BABAR residual data presented by E. Guido (arXiv:0910.0423).

## Key numbers

- The proposed scalar boson mass is about 38 MeV, inferred from excess signals in the invariant-mass distributions of e⁺e⁻ and μ⁺μ⁻ pairs.
- In the μ⁺μ⁻ invariant-mass distribution for Υ(2³S₁) → π⁺π⁻Υ(1³S₁) → π⁺π⁻μ⁺μ⁻, deficit enhancements appear at approximately 38, 76, and 114 MeV above the Υ(1³S₁) mass (i.e., at about 9.50, 9.54, and 9.57 GeV).
- In the same reaction, the excess signal sets in at about 40 MeV below the Υ(1³S₁) mass, i.e., at about 9.42 GeV.
- For the reaction Υ(3³S₁) → π⁺π⁻Υ(1³S₁) → π⁺π⁻μ⁺μ⁻ (19 MeV bins), two connected enhancements are observed at 38 and 76 MeV below the Υ(1³S₁) mass, and two negative enhancements at 38 and 76 MeV above.
- For Υ(3³S₁) → π⁺π⁻Υ(2³S₁) → π⁺π⁻μ⁺μ⁻ (38 MeV bins), two connected enhancements appear at 38 and 76 MeV below the Υ(2³S₁) mass, and three at 38, 76, and 114 MeV above.
- An independent BABAR residual plot (E. Guido, arXiv:0910.0423) shows three minima in the data deficit above the Υ(1³S₁) mass at about 40, 70, and 115 MeV.
- A possible hybrid state (b̄bg) is indicated at about 10.061 GeV, corresponding to M_Υ(2³S₁) + 38 MeV.
- The universal quarkonium oscillation frequency used in the model is ω = 190 MeV.
- The flavor-independent mass scale M from the ³P₀ pair-creation mechanism is estimated at 30–40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of interference effects in annihilation data (van Beveren and Rupp, 2010, arXiv:1009.5191) and their conformally symmetric model for strong interactions (van Beveren, 1980, Nuovo Cim. A). It also relates to BABAR searches for light scalar bosons in radiative Υ decays (BABAR Collaboration, 2009, Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 081803), which found no evidence for such particles in the mass range 0.212–9.3 GeV. The proposed 38 MeV scalar is distinct from the electroweak Higgs and is interpreted as a light scalar glueball, potentially enabling further searches for hybrid b̄bg states and tests of lepton universality in Υ decays.
