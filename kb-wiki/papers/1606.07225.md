# Quark-Pauli effects in three octet-baryons

> To sustain a neutron star with about two times the solar mass, multi baryons including hyperons are expected to produce repulsive effects in the interior of its high baryon-density region. To examine possible quark-Pauli repulsion among the baryons, we solve the eigenvalue problem of the quark antisymmetrizer for three octet-baryons that are described by most compact spatial configurations. We find that the Pauli blocking effect is weak in the $Λnn$ system, while it is strong in the $Σ^-nn$ system. The appearance of the $Σ^-$ hyperon is suppressed in the neutron star interior but no quark-Pauli repulsion effectively works for the $Λ$ hyperon.

## Key claim

The quark-Pauli blocking effect is weak in the Λnn system but strong in the Σ⁻nn system, implying that Σ⁻ hyperon appearance is suppressed in neutron star interiors while Λ hyperons experience no effective quark-Pauli repulsion. This conclusion is conditional on the assumption that all three baryons occupy the most compact spatial configuration (the (0s)⁹ harmonic-oscillator state).

## Method

The authors constructed fully antisymmetric 9-quark wave functions for three octet-baryons by combining spin-flavor functions in the flavor SU(3) basis with a totally symmetric (0s)⁹ orbital configuration and a color wave function. They then solved the eigenvalue problem of the 9-quark antisymmetrizer to identify Pauli-forbidden or almost Pauli-forbidden states, which correspond to configurations where three baryons cannot occupy the same spatial region without experiencing quark-Pauli repulsion.

## Key numbers

- The Λnn system (Y=2, I=0, S=1/2) shows weak Pauli blocking effect
- The Σ⁻nn system (Y=2, I=1, S=1/2) shows strong Pauli blocking effect
- For the ΣNN system with S=1/2, I=1, there are four possible S'a' values but only two independent antisymmetric functions can be constructed
- For the NNN system (Y=3, I=1/2, S=1/2), the state belongs to the |14⟩₁-|14⟩₂ representation
- For the ΣNN system (I=2, S=1/2), the state belongs to the |41⟩₁-|41⟩₂ representation

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark-Pauli effects in two-baryon systems (OSY 1987; central 1995) and in multi-baryon systems composed of nucleons and a single hyperon (TSH 1982; SHT 1982; TS 1986; Suzuki 1984; Maltman 1985). The study is motivated by the need to explain the observation of neutron stars with about two solar masses (2SM 2010; 2SM 2013), which requires additional repulsion beyond what standard hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions provide. The results enable future calculations of three-baryon forces from quark-Pauli effects that could stiffen the neutron star equation of state, complementing other proposed mechanisms such as vector-meson exchange (vector 2012; Oertel 2015), repulsive Λnn three-body forces (Lonardoni 2015), and universal three-baryon repulsion (Takatsuka 2008; Yamamoto 2014).
