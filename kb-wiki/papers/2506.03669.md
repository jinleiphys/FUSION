# Off-shell ambiguities in correlation functions: strategies to minimize them

> We face here the problem of uncertainties in correlation functions due to the freedom in the off-shell dependence of the wave functions, or equivalently, off-shell ambiguities in the scattering matrices. We make the study for the case of meson baryon interaction, choosing the $KΛ$, $K Σ$, $ηN$ coupled channels, which are related to the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. We find that using realistic interactions based on chiral dynamics the uncertainties are small, of the order of $2-3$~\%, but could be much bigger if other methods are used. However, our study shows the way to optimally overcome these uncertainties in the analysis of correlation functions, and we provide a series of recommendations for any general analysis.

# Off-shell ambiguities in correlation functions: strategies to minimize them

## Key claim
Using realistic interactions based on chiral dynamics, the uncertainties in correlation functions due to off-shell ambiguities are small (2–3%), but the paper shows that these uncertainties can be much larger if other models are used, and provides recommendations to minimize them in any general analysis.

## Method
The authors study the meson-baryon coupled-channel system (KΛ, KΣ, ηN) related to the N*(1535) resonance, using a momentum-space formalism based on the Koonin-Pratt approach. They construct separable interaction potentials with a cutoff regulator q_max, compute the scattering T-matrices via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the chiral unitary approach, and evaluate correlation functions for the four coupled channels. The off-shell dependence is varied by changing the cutoff q_max between 630 and 1000 MeV, and the source radius R is also varied to study compensation effects.

## Key numbers
- Uncertainties in the K⁰Σ⁺ correlation function from changing q_max from 630 MeV to 1000 MeV are about 2% at small momenta and 1% at larger momenta (with R = 1 fm)
- Changing the source radius R by 10% (from 1.1 fm to 0.9 fm) produces changes of about 6% at small p and about 2% at p ~ 300 MeV/c
- For large source size R = 5 fm, correlation functions for q_max = 630 MeV and q_max = 1000 MeV are indistinguishable
- The recommended strategy of fitting the low-momentum part (below 50 MeV/c) reduces discrepancies at large p (~300 MeV/c) to about 1%, while fitting the tail gives discrepancies at small p of about 3%

## Context
This work extends the discussion of off-shell ambiguities in correlation functions initiated by Epelbaum (2023), who showed that the source function is not universal and depends on the off-shell form of the wave functions. The authors apply this analysis to meson-baryon systems, complementing earlier studies of nucleon-nucleon correlation functions where Kievsky (2023) found differences smaller than 5% using different NN models. The work builds on the chiral unitary approach of Oset and collaborators (Oller, 1997) and the correlation function formalism of Koonin (1977) and Pratt (1986). The results enable improved extraction of scattering observables (scattering lengths, effective ranges) from correlation function data, particularly for channels where scattering experiments are not possible, and support the inverse problem approach developed by Ikeno (2023), Albertus (2023), and others.
