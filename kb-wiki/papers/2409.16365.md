# Perturbative treatment of nonlocal chiral interactions in auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations

> Nuclear many-body systems, ranging from nuclei to neutron stars, are some of the most interesting physical phenomena in our universe, and Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) approaches are among the most accurate many-body methods currently available to study them. In recent decades, interactions derived from chiral effective field theory (EFT) have been widely adopted in the study of nuclear many-body systems. One drawback of the QMC approach is the requirement that the nuclear interactions need to be local, whereas chiral EFT interactions usually contain nonlocalities. In this work, we leverage the capability of computing second-order perturbative corrections to the ground-state energy in order to develop a self-consistent approach to including nonlocal operators in QMC calculations. We investigate both the deuteron and the neutron-matter equation of state in order to show the robustness of our technique, and pave the way for future QMC calculations at higher orders in the EFT, where nonlocal operators cannot be avoided.

## Key claim

The authors develop a self-consistent approach to include nonlocal chiral EFT operators in auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations by treating the nonlocal terms as second-order perturbative corrections to the ground-state energy, rather than including them directly in the imaginary-time propagator. The method is demonstrated for NLO interactions where only one nonlocal operator is present, but the approach is intended to enable future QMC calculations at N3LO and beyond where nonlocal operators cannot be avoided.

## Method

The authors construct an NLO chiral EFT interaction where all but one contact operator are local, replacing the local \(q^2 \bm{\sigma}_1\cdot\bm{\sigma}_2\) operator with the nonlocal \(k^2 \bm{\sigma}_1\cdot\bm{\sigma}_2\) operator. They perform AFDMC calculations using only the local parts of the interaction in the imaginary-time propagator, then compute the first- and second-order perturbative corrections from the nonlocal operator using a recently developed formalism that evaluates the integral \(I(\mathcal{T}) = \int_0^{\mathcal{T}} d\tau \langle \phi_0^{(0)} | V' e^{-[H_0-E_0^{(0)}]\tau} V' | \phi_0^{(0)} \rangle\) within the QMC framework. The method is tested on the deuteron and on neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The LO and NLO low-energy constants (LECs) are fit to neutron-proton phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis (NPWA) up to a lab energy of 150 MeV, for the \(^1\)S\(_0\), \(^3\)S\(_1\), \(\epsilon_1\), \(^1\)P\(_1\), \(^3\)P\(_0\), \(^3\)P\(_1\), and \(^3\)P\(_2\) partial waves.
- The coordinate-space cutoff \(R_0\) parameterizes the local regulator, with smaller values of \(R_0\) leading to harder interactions.
- The AFDMC method uses 15 auxiliary fields per nucleon to handle spin-isospin dependent operators via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation.

## Context

This work extends the local chiral EFT interactions developed by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) and Tews et al. (2016), which are limited to N2LO because nonlocal operators become unavoidable at N3LO. It builds on the second-order perturbation theory formalism for QMC introduced by Curry et al. (2023). The approach enables future AFDMC calculations using maximally local N3LO interactions from Somasundaram et al. (2024) and Tews et al. (2024), which contain nonlocal operators that cannot be treated with existing QMC methods.
