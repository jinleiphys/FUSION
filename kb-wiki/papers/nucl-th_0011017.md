# Proton fraction in neutron stars

> The proton fraction in ${\sl β}$-stable neutron stars is investigated within the framework of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock theory using the extended Skyrme effective interaction for the first time. The calculated results show that the proton fraction disappears at high density, which implies that the pure neutron matter may exist in the interior of neutron stars. The incompressibility of the nuclear equation of state is shown to be more important to determine the proton fraction. Meanwhile, it is indicated that the addition of muons in neutron stars will change the proton fraction. It is also found that the higher-order terms of the nuclear symmetry energy have obvious effects on the proton fraction and the parabolic law of the nuclear symmetry energy is not enough to determine the proton fraction.

## Key claim

The proton fraction in beta-stable neutron stars may disappear entirely at high density (pure neutron matter may exist in the interior), but this result depends on the choice of Skyrme parameter set and is not universal; for the SkI5 parameter set, the proton fraction does not disappear up to 1.5 fm\(^{-3}\).

## Method

The authors applied the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock theory with an extended Skyrme effective interaction to compute the energy per nucleon of asymmetric nuclear matter as a function of density and neutron excess. They imposed beta-equilibrium conditions (chemical potential equalities for neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) together with charge neutrality to solve numerically for the proton fraction as a function of baryon density, using three different Skyrme parameter sets (SII, SIII, SkI5) that differ in incompressibility and effective mass.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0\) = 0.1484 fm\(^{-3}\) (SII), 0.1453 fm\(^{-3}\) (SIII), 0.1558 fm\(^{-3}\) (SkI5)
- Incompressibility \(K\) = 341.38 MeV (SII), 355.35 MeV (SIII), 255.78 MeV (SkI5)
- Effective mass \(m^{*}/m\) = 0.580 (SII), 0.763 (SIII), 0.579 (SkI5)
- Symmetry energy coefficient \(C\) = 34.26 MeV (SII), 28.25 MeV (SIII), 36.75 MeV (SkI5)
- Proton fraction disappears at \(\rho = 0.48\) fm\(^{-3}\) for SII and at \(\rho = 0.32\) fm\(^{-3}\) for SIII
- For SkI5, the proton fraction reaches the critical Urca threshold \(x^{Urca} = 11\%\) at baryon density 0.2 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Causality condition fails for SII above 1.35 fm\(^{-3}\) and for SkI5 above 1.1 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Muons appear only above approximately 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\) for SIII

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies by Zhang (1996) and Zhang and Ge (1998) using an extended Skyrme interaction, and it contrasts with results from Brueckner theory, variational many-body theory (Wiringa, 1988), and relativistic mean field theory (Serot, 1986). The finding that pure neutron matter may exist at high density had also been observed in variational many-body calculations (Pandharipande, 1972). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling rates and neutrino emission, particularly by identifying which equations of state allow the direct Urca process (Lattimer, 1991) to operate.
