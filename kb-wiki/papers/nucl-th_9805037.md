# Deconfinement in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

> The Quark Meson Coupling Model which describes nuclear matter as a collection of non-overlapping MIT bags interacting by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons is used to study nuclear matter at finite temperature. In its modified version, the density dependence of the bag constant is introduced by a direct coupling between the bag constant and the scalar mean field. In the present work, the coupling of the scalar mean field with the constituent quarks is considered exactly through the solution of the Dirac equation. Our results show that a phase transition takes place at a critical temperature around 200 MeV in which the scalar mean field takes a nonzero value at zero baryon density. Furthermore it is found that the bag constant decreases significantly when the temperature increases above this critical temperature indicating the onset of quark deconfinement.

# Deconfinement in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim
A phase transition occurs at a critical temperature around 200 MeV in which the scalar mean field takes a nonzero value at zero baryon density, and the bag constant decreases significantly when the temperature increases above this critical temperature, indicating the onset of quark deconfinement. The coupling of the scalar mean field with the constituent quarks is treated exactly through the solution of the Dirac equation, rather than through the approximate density-dependent bag constant used in earlier modified versions.

## Method
The Quark Meson Coupling Model is used to describe nuclear matter as a collection of non-overlapping MIT bags interacting by self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons at finite temperature. The coupling of the scalar mean field with the constituent quarks is solved exactly via the Dirac equation, and the density dependence of the bag constant is introduced by a direct coupling between the bag constant and the scalar mean field. The authors compute the equation of state (pressure versus baryon density) and the scalar mean field as functions of temperature and baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for the phase transition: around 200 MeV, at which the scalar mean field takes a nonzero value at zero baryon density.
- Pressure isotherms are computed for temperatures of 25, 100, 150, 200, 220, and 240 MeV, shown as functions of baryon density up to approximately 0.8 fm^{-3}.
- The scalar mean field sigma is computed for temperatures of 5, 50, 100, and 150 MeV, shown as functions of baryon density up to approximately 0.5 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends the Quark Meson Coupling Model by treating the quark-scalar field coupling exactly via the Dirac equation, going beyond earlier modified versions that introduced a density-dependent bag constant through an approximate coupling. The results indicate a deconfinement phase transition at high temperature, which enables further studies of the quark-hadron phase transition in hot nuclear matter. The approach builds on the MIT bag model framework for describing nuclear matter as non-overlapping bags interacting via meson exchange.
