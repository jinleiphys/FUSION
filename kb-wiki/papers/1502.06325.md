# Cluster states and isoscalar monopole transitions of $^{24}$Mg

> We investigate structure of the excited $0^+$ states of $^{24}$Mg populated by isoscalar monopole transition from the ground state on the basis of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculation with Gogny D1S interaction. The calculated isoscalar monopole strength function shows reasonable agreement with experiment and is consistent with other theoretical calculation. The structure of the excited $0^+$ states with pronounced isoscalar monopole transitions are analyzed. It is found that the $0^+_2$, $0^+_3$ and $0^+_5$ states have mixed nature of mean-field and cluster, and that the $0^+_8$ state is dominated by $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster configuration. In addition, it is predicted that $5α$-pentagon+$α$ states appear around 23 MeV.

## Key claim

The excited $0^+$ states of $^{24}$Mg that appear as prominent peaks in the calculated isoscalar monopole strength function are associated with $^{20}$Ne+$\alpha$, $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, and $5\alpha$-pentagon+$\alpha$ cluster configurations, with the $0^+_8$ state at 15.3 MeV being dominated by the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster configuration and possibly corresponding to the experimentally observed peak at the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C cluster threshold energy.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with the Gogny D1S effective interaction, using three sets of basis wave functions: those constrained on quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta$ and $\gamma$, those constrained on harmonic oscillator quanta $\Delta N$ (up to $8\hbar\omega$ excitation), and those generated by applying the isoscalar monopole operator to the quadrupole-constrained wave functions. These 524 basis wave functions were projected to $J^\pi=0^+$ and superposed via the generator coordinate method (GCM) to obtain the ground and excited $0^+$ states and their isoscalar monopole transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- The ground state binding energy increases from 198.3 MeV to 199.2 MeV (0.9 MeV additional binding) when cluster configurations from $\Phi_{\Delta N}$ are included.
- The $0^+_2$ state at 9.3 MeV has $B(IS0) = 122$ fm$^4$ and $E_x B(IS0) = 6.1\%$ of EWSR (experiment: $B(IS0) = 180 \pm 20$ fm$^4$, $E_x B(IS0) = 6.4 \pm 0.7\%$).
- The $0^+_8$ state at 15.3 MeV has $B(IS0) = 77.8$ fm$^4$ and $E_x B(IS0) = 6.4\%$ of EWSR.
- The $0^+_{15}$ and $0^+_{24}$ states at 21.8 and 24.9 MeV exhaust 2.5% and 1.2% of EWSR, respectively.
- The full calculation (basis set c) yields $m_1^* = 90$ (fraction of EWSR), centroid energy $m_1^*/m_0^* = 22.2$ MeV, and $\sqrt{m_3^*/m_1^*} = 25.2$ MeV, compared to experimental values of $82 \pm 9$, $21.9^{+0.3}_{-0.2}$ MeV, and $24.7^{+0.5}_{-0.3}$ MeV, respectively.
- The $^{20}$Ne+$\alpha$ cluster configurations have inter-cluster distances of 3.0 fm ($\Delta N=4$) and 3.3 fm ($\Delta N=8$); the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C configurations have distances of 3.5 fm ($\Delta N=6$) and 4.0 fm ($\Delta N=8$).

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscalar monopole transitions as a probe for cluster states, previously established in $p$-shell nuclei such as $^{11}$B (Kawabata, 2007; Kanada-En'yo, 2007), $^{12}$C (Ajzenberg-Selove, 1990), and $^{16}$O (Ajzenberg-Selove, 1987), to the mid $sd$-shell nucleus $^{24}$Mg. The calculation is consistent with quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) results using the same Gogny D1S interaction (Peru, 2007) for the giant monopole resonance, but reveals cluster states not captured by QRPA. The results enable experimental identification of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C molecular resonances of astrophysical interest and suggest that exotic $\alpha$-cluster states can be observed via their decay modes, even when embedded in the giant monopole resonance region.
