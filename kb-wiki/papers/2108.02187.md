# Transient fluid dynamics with general matching conditions: a first study from the method of moments

> Recent works have revealed that matching conditions play a major role on general consistency properties of relativistic fluid dynamics such as causality, stability and wellposedness of the equations of motion. In this paper we derive transient fluid dynamics from kinetic theory, using the method of moments as proposed by Israel and Stewart, without imposing an specific matching condition. We then investigate how the equations of motion and their corresponding transport coefficients are affected by the choice of matching condition.

## Key claim

The paper derives transient relativistic fluid dynamics from kinetic theory using the method of moments without imposing a specific matching condition, showing that the equations of motion and transport coefficients depend on the choice of matching condition; this dependence is explored for general matching conditions parameterized by three exponents (q, s, z) that define which moments of the non-equilibrium distribution function are set to zero.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation for classical particles with elastic collisions and use the method of moments to derive an infinite tower of coupled differential equations for irreducible moments of the single-particle distribution function. They then truncate this expansion using a generalized 19-moment approximation (extending the standard 14-moment approximation of Israel and Stewart) that is consistent with arbitrary matching conditions, where the matching conditions are expressed as constraints on specific moments ρ_q, ρ_s, and ρ_z^μ. The final equations of motion for the fluid-dynamical variables are obtained in the massless limit, and a concrete example is given for a restricted class of matching conditions labeled "Exotic Eckart conditions."

## Key numbers

- The standard Landau matching conditions correspond to the parameter choice (q, s, z) = (1, 2, 0), while Eckart matching conditions correspond to (q, s, z) = (1, 2, 1)
- The maximum number of degrees of freedom in fluid-dynamical theories with arbitrary matching conditions is 19, compared to the usual 14 in Israel-Stewart theory
- For the general 19-moment truncation, the truncation parameters are set to (N_0, N_1) = (4, 2), while for Landau matching conditions the truncation uses (N_0, N_1) = (2, 1)
- The shear-stress expansion coefficient is given by Φ^{μν}_0 = π^{μν} / (2 I_{42}), where I_{42} is a thermodynamic integral

## Context

This work extends the method-of-moments derivation of Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979) to accommodate general matching conditions, motivated by recent findings that matching conditions affect causality, stability, and well-posedness of relativistic fluid dynamics. The paper builds on the work of Bemfica, Disconzi, and Noronha (2020), who showed that first-order theories can be causal and stable with appropriate matching conditions, and on a phenomenological derivation by Noronha (2021) that explored the dependence of Israel-Stewart equations on matching conditions. The results enable systematic investigation of how different matching conditions affect transient fluid dynamics derived from kinetic theory, with potential applications to heavy ion collisions and neutron star mergers.
