# Universal stress-tensor correlation functions of strongly coupled conformal fluids

> We have shown in two accompanying papers that, for Einstein gravity, the graviton multi-point functions are universal in a particular kinematic region and depend only on the (generalized) Mandelstam variable s. The effects of the leading corrections to Einstein gravity were shown to be similarly universal, inducing a specific difference in the angular dependence. Here we show, relying on the gauge-gravity duality, that the stress-tensor correlation functions of conformal fluids whose gravitational dual is either Einstein gravity or its leading correction are also universal. We discuss the possible significance of these results to multi-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions. We show that, to test our ideas, the stress-energy correlation functions have to measured rather than the standard multiplicity correlation functions. We then discuss schematically how stress-energy correlations in heavy-ion collisions can be used to test our findings. We argue that, if these correlations can be measured precisely enough, they will provide a unique way to probe the existence of a gravitational dual to the quark-gluon plasma and to determine its universality class.

# Universal stress-tensor correlation functions of strongly coupled conformal fluids

## Key claim
The stress-tensor correlation functions of conformal fluids with a gravitational dual are universal in a high-momentum kinematic region, depending only on the generalized Mandelstam variable s, and the leading corrections from Gauss-Bonnet gravity induce a specific difference in the angular dependence; however, these results apply only in the restricted kinematic regime where 1 << sqrt(s) L << pi L T, and the experimental test requires measuring stress-energy correlation functions rather than the standard multiplicity correlation functions.

## Method
The authors use the gauge-gravity duality (AdS/CFT) to compute connected n-point correlation functions of the stress-energy tensor for strongly coupled conformal fluids. They start from previously calculated one-particle irreducible (1PI) on-shell graviton amplitudes in a 5D AdS black brane background for both Einstein gravity and its leading-order Gauss-Bonnet correction, then apply holographic renormalization to translate these bulk graviton amplitudes into boundary stress-tensor correlation functions. The calculation is restricted to tensor graviton modes (h_xy) in a high-momentum kinematic regime where k^2, omega^2, s >> 1/L^2 but sqrt(s) << T, with the boundary theory defined on a sphere of radius R ~ L.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density for Einstein gravity: eta/s = 1/(4 pi)
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density with Gauss-Bonnet corrections: eta/s = (1/(4 pi))[1 - 8 epsilon], where epsilon ~ l_s^2/L^2 << 1 is the dimensionless parameter measuring the relative strength of corrections
- The renormalized four-point stress-tensor correlation function for Einstein gravity: (9/4) s times the product of four T_xy factors
- The renormalized six-point stress-tensor correlation function for Einstein gravity: (75/8) s times the product of six T_xy factors
- The two-point stress-tensor correlation function for Gauss-Bonnet gravity: [1 - 8 epsilon] k_1^2 times the product of two T_xy factors
- The four-point stress-tensor correlation function for Gauss-Bonnet gravity: (3/4)[3s + epsilon s^2] times the product of four T_xy factors
- The six-point stress-tensor correlation function for Gauss-Bonnet gravity: (15/8)[5s + 21 epsilon s(s+v)] times the product of six T_xy factors
- For the QGP at LHC: T ~ 400 MeV, R ~ 7 fm, pi T R ~ 15, giving a range in s of about 200
- The parameter combination epsilon (pi R T)^2 ~ 25(1 - 4 pi eta/s) for the QGP

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations of graviton multi-point functions (Brustein and Medved, 2011) to the boundary stress-tensor correlation functions via holographic renormalization. It builds on the foundational AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena, 1998; Witten, 1998) and the well-known result for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (Policastro, Son, and Starinets, 2001; Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). The paper enables experimental tests of whether the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC has a gravitational dual, and if so, whether that dual is Einstein gravity or a corrected theory such as Gauss-Bonnet gravity. The authors argue that multi-point stress-energy correlation functions provide additional constraints beyond the single parameter eta/s, potentially distinguishing between different universality classes of gravitational duals.
