# Kaon production in the HADES experiment in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV

> Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model by including the kaon reaction channels, we study the kaon prodution in heavy-ion collisions (HICs) at SIS (Darmstadt Schwerionen Synchrotron, GSI) energies. Based on simulations of a centrality of 0-40% Au + Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV, a typical reaction that has been carried out by the HADES Collaboration, we confirm that the medium modification of kaon masses plays a vital role in studying the kaon productions in HICs, and is also unavoidable for the successful interpretation of the HADES data on kaon rapidity distributions and transverse mass spectra. Moreover, it is shown that the directed flows of kaons are affected significantly by the kaon potential and slightly affected by the medium modification of kaon masses. Also, the rapidity-dependent inverse slope parameter $T_{B}$ of the kaon transverse mass spectra is shown to be affected considerably by both the kaon potential and medium modification of kaon masses. However, through checking the simulations of related reactions in FOPI and/or KaoS experiments, some of these regular effects do not seem to be obvious and appear to be the reaction system and/or beam energy dependent. Nevertheless, it can be confirmed that the medium modification of kaon masses is favored by observations from the inverse slope parameter $T_{B}$ and transverse mass spectra of kaons in both HADES Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV and FOPI Ni + Ni collisions at 1.93\textit{A} GeV. Therefore, measurements of the inverse slope parameter $T_{B}$ of kaon transverse mass spectra and the kaon directed flows in HADES Au + Au collisions would be great benefit to detection of the kaon potential and the corresponding medium effects on kaon masses.

# Kaon production in the HADES experiment in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV

## Key claim

The medium modification of kaon masses is confirmed as vital for interpreting HADES kaon rapidity distributions and transverse mass spectra, but the effects of kaon potential scenarios and mass modification on observables such as directed flows and inverse slope parameters are reaction-system and beam-energy dependent, being less obvious in lighter systems like FOPI Ni+Ni and KaoS reactions than in HADES Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model including kaon production, annihilation, and elastic channels, with two kaon potential scenarios: the empirical scattering length (ESL) scenario based on kaon-nucleon scattering length and the chiral Lagrangian (chL) scenario based on a scalar-vector kaon potential. They simulated 0-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV and compared results with and without medium modification of kaon masses against HADES data, also performing simulations of FOPI Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93$A$ GeV and KaoS Au+Au collisions at 1.5$A$ GeV for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Maximum compression density reached in 0-40% Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV: approximately 2.56-2.58 times saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ for all cases
- Kaon yield: about 0.03 kaons per event on average in the HADES reaction
- Repulsive kaon potential at saturation density: 30 MeV for both ESL and chL scenarios
- Kaon-nucleon scattering length: $\alpha_{KN} \approx -0.255$ fm (ESL scenario)
- Chiral Lagrangian parameter: $b_K = 3/(8f_\pi^2) \approx 0.333$ GeV fm$^3$; $a_K \approx 0.173$ GeV$^2$ fm$^3$
- Inverse slope parameter $T_B$ at midrapidity ($y=0$) for Au+Au: ESL without mass mod. = $100.03 \pm 5.4$ MeV; ESL with mass mod. = $120.76 \pm 10.1$ MeV; chL without mass mod. = $120.44 \pm 7.4$ MeV; chL with mass mod. = $137.46 \pm 12.5$ MeV
- Inverse slope parameter $T_B$ at midrapidity for Ni+Ni at 1.93$A$ GeV: ESL without mass mod. = $97 \pm 3.1$ MeV; ESL with mass mod. = $123 \pm 5.9$ MeV; chL without mass mod. = $101 \pm 2.9$ MeV; chL with mass mod. = $121 \pm 6.5$ MeV
- Kaon number $\Delta N$ at $y=0$ (rapidity interval 0.1) for Au+Au: ESL without mass mod. = $0.0278 \pm 0.00002$; ESL with mass mod. = $0.0174 \pm 0.00003$; chL without mass mod. = $0.0249 \pm 0.00002$; chL with mass mod. = $0.0176 \pm 0.00003$

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of kaon production at SIS energies (1-2$A$ GeV) by Li et al. (1994-1999), Cassing et al. (1997), and Pal et al. (2000), who investigated kaon potentials and medium effects using the ART and AMPT models. It directly addresses the HADES Collaboration data from Song et al. (2021) and HADES (2018, 2019), and compares with FOPI data from FOPI-Kaon (1997) and KaoS data from AB05. The study enables future measurements of the inverse slope parameter $T_B$ and kaon directed flows in HADES Au+Au collisions to discriminate between kaon potential scenarios and quantify medium effects on kaon masses.
