# Rate of photon production from hot hadronic matter

> Thermal photon emission rates from hot hadronic matter are studied to order $e^{2}g^{4}$, where $g$ indicates a strong-interaction coupling constant. Radiative decay of mesons, Compton and annihilation processes for hadrons, and bremsstrahlung reactions are all considered. Compared to the standard rates from the literature, one finds two orders of magnitude increase for low photon energies stemming mainly from bremsstrahlung and then a modest increase (factor of 2) for intermediate and high energy photons owing to radiative decays for a variety of mesons and from other reactions involving strangeness. These results could have important consequences for electromagnetic radiation studies at RHIC.

## Key claim

Thermal photon emission rates from hot hadronic matter, computed to order \(e^{2}g^{4}\) (next-to-next-to-leading order in the strong coupling), are two orders of magnitude larger at low photon energies and a factor of about 2 larger at intermediate and high energies compared to the standard rates from the literature; however, the precise increase at low energies is not quantitatively determined because multiple scattering interferences (Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect) have not yet been included.

## Method

The author computed thermal photon production rates by summing contributions from three classes of processes: radiative decays of mesons (one-loop self-energy, order \(e^{2}\)), Compton and annihilation processes for hadrons (two-loop self-energy, order \(e^{2}g^{2}\)), and bremsstrahlung from elastic meson scattering (three-loop self-energy, order \(e^{2}g^{4}\)). Radiative decay rates were computed using empirical decay widths from the Particle Data Group where available, while Compton and bremsstrahlung rates were modeled using an SU(3) chiral Lagrangian with coupling constants fitted to hadronic phenomenology; bremsstrahlung was treated in a soft-photon approximation.

## Key numbers

- Radiative decay widths used for low-lying resonances include: \(\rho(770)\to\pi\gamma\) at 117.9 keV; \(\omega(783)\to\pi^{0}\gamma\) at 734.2 keV; \(K^{*}(892)\to K\gamma\) at 83.5 keV; \(\phi(1020)\to\eta\gamma\) at 55.3 keV; \(b_{1}(1235)\to\pi\gamma\) at 227.2 keV; \(a_{1}(1260)\to\pi\gamma\) at 640\(\pm\)240 keV; \(a_{2}(1320)\to\pi\gamma\) at 286 keV; \(K_{1}(1270)\to K\gamma\) at 70 keV (estimated via vector meson dominance).
- At a temperature of \(T = 200\) MeV, the new rate (including radiative decays, strangeness reactions, and bremsstrahlung) shows a factor of 2 increase over the conventional hadronic rate for intermediate and high photon energies up to 3 GeV.
- The increase approaches two orders of magnitude around 200 MeV photon energy.
- The soft-photon approximation for bremsstrahlung is expected to yield no more than a factor of 2–4 suppression for photon energies \(E_{\gamma} \sim 0.5\) GeV due to missing multiple scattering interferences.

## Context

This work extends the standard hadronic photon production rates from Kapusta et al. (1991) and the parameterization by Nadeau et al., which included only \(\omega\to\pi^{0}\gamma\), \(\pi\pi\to\rho\gamma\), \(\rho\to\pi\pi\gamma\), and \(\pi\rho\to\pi\gamma\) to order \(e^{2}g^{2}\). The new calculation adds radiative decays of additional mesons (including strange mesons), strangeness-bearing Compton processes, and bremsstrahlung from elastic meson scattering, motivated by parallel advances in QCD photon rates computed to three-loop order by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001). The results enable more accurate modeling of the hadronic background for photon measurements at RHIC, which is a prerequisite for using photons as a quark-gluon plasma diagnostic.
