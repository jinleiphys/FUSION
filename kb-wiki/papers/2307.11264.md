# Electron-positron plasma in BBN: damped-dynamic screening

> We characterize in detail the very dense $e^- e^+ γ$ plasma present during the Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) and explore how it is perturbed electromagnetically by \lq\lq impurities, {\it i.e.\/}, spatially dispersed protons and light nuclei undergoing thermal motion. The internuclear electromagnetic screened potential is obtained (analytically) using the linear response approach, allowing for the dynamic motion of the electromagnetic field sources and the damping effects due to plasma component scattering. We discuss the limits of the linear response method and suggest additional work needed to improve BBN reaction rates in the primordial Universe. Our theoretical methods to describe the potential between charged dust particles align with previous studies on planetary and space dusty plasma and could have significant impact on interpretation of standard cosmological model results.

## Key claim

The authors show that during Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), the electron-positron plasma is so dense that scattering damping (collisions) dominates over the screening mass, meaning electromagnetic perturbations are overdamped; this damped-dynamic screening modifies the internuclear Coulomb potential in a way that could enhance nuclear reaction rates and potentially resolve the lithium abundance discrepancy, but the linear response method used has limits when the potential becomes large compared to thermal energy.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Vlasov-Boltzmann equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision term in the linear response approximation to obtain the longitudinal polarization tensor for a non-relativistic electron-positron plasma. They compute the relaxation rate (inverse mean free time) by averaging the cross sections for Moller, Bhabha, and inverse Compton scattering, using a temperature-dependent photon mass to regularize infrared divergences. The screened internuclear potential is then obtained by Fourier-transforming the Poisson equation with the longitudinal permittivity, treating light nuclei as moving charged impurities (dust) in the plasma.

## Key numbers

- During the BBN temperature range (86 keV > T > 50 keV), the average relaxation rate is approximately constant at κ = 10–12 keV.
- At T = 70 keV, the electron and positron density is about n_{e±} ≈ 10^7 n_B (baryon density).
- The electron chemical potential during BBN is very small, μ_e/T ≈ 10^{-6} to 10^{-7}.
- The Debye mass m_D is given by m_D^2 = 4πα (2mT/π)^{3/2} e^{-m/T} / (2T), with m_e c^2 = 511 keV.
- The BBN temperature interval is 86 keV > T_{BBN} > 50 keV.
- Positrons effectively disappear below T_split ≈ 20.3 keV.

## Context

This work extends the dynamic screening approach of Hwang et al. (2021) by including collisional damping via the BGK collision term, connecting BBN plasma physics to dusty plasma theory developed by Stenflo (1973) and others for planetary and space plasmas. The results suggest that standard BBN reaction rates may need revision due to overdamped screening, which could help resolve the discrepancy in predicted ^7Li abundances without invoking exotic physics such as variation of the fine-structure constant. The authors note that a self-consistent calculation where damping is included in the reaction rates themselves is needed as a next step.
