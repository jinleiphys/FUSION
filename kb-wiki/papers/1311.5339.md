# Constraining models of initial conditions with elliptic and triangular flow data

> We carry out a combined analysis of elliptic and triangular flow data using viscous relativistic hydrodynamics. We show that these data allow to put tight constraints on models of the early dynamics of a nucleus-nucleus collision. Specifically, the rms values of the initial ellipticity $\varepsilon_2$ and the initial triangularity $\varepsilon_3$ are constrained to lie within a narrow band for each centrality. We use these constraints as a filter for existing Monte-Carlo models of initial state, and provide a simple test that can be performed on any candidate model to determine its compatibility with data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combined analysis of elliptic and triangular flow data, using viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, constrains the root-mean-square values of initial ellipticity ε₂ and triangularity ε₃ to lie within a narrow band for each centrality, but this constraint is conditional on the linear-response approximation and the specific hydrodynamic parameters chosen (viscosity, initial time, freeze-out treatment).

## Method

The authors used viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with boost-invariant initial conditions to compute the hydrodynamic response coefficients (v_n/ε_n)_h for n=2 and 3, assuming linear response. They varied the shear viscosity over entropy ratio η/s from 0 to 0.24, initial thermalization time t₀ between 0.5 and 1 fm/c, and used both linear and quadratic freeze-out ansätze to estimate uncertainties. The rms values of ε₂ and ε₃ were extracted from experimental v₂ and v₃ data using Eq. (rms), and the resulting allowed region in the (ε₂, ε₃) plane was compared to predictions from Monte-Carlo models of initial conditions (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, MC-rcBK, IP-Glasma, DIPSY).

## Key numbers

- The power-law exponent k relating ε₂ and ε₃ is k=0.6 at LHC and k=0.5 at RHIC, from Eq. (powerlaw)
- For 0-5% central Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV, the allowed ratio √⟨ε₂²⟩/√⟨ε₃²⟩^0.6 ranges from 0.40 (minimum) to 0.43 (maximum)
- For 0-10% central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN=0.2 TeV, the allowed ratio √⟨ε₂²⟩/√⟨ε₃²⟩^0.5 ranges from 0.36 (minimum) to 0.41 (maximum)
- MC-KLN model is excluded at all centralities at both RHIC and LHC; for 0-10% RHIC its ratio is 0.49 (outside allowed band 0.36-0.41)
- DIPSY and IP-Glasma fall within the allowed region for all centralities; DIPSY gives 0.39 for 0-10% RHIC and 0.40 for 0-5% LHC
- Doubling the source width σ from 0.4 to 0.8 fm changes the ratio by only 2% for ε₂ and 1% for ε₃ in central collisions

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by the ALICE collaboration (2011) that placed constraints on initial eccentricity for very central collisions at LHC without hydrodynamic calculations, and by Alver et al. (2010) and Adare et al. (2011) who noted that some initial-state models could be ruled out by combined v₂ and v₃ data. The paper provides a systematic filter for initial-state models that can be applied without performing full hydrodynamic simulations, and enables future work to constrain the granularity of initial conditions using more detailed correlation data as suggested by Gardim et al. (2012).
