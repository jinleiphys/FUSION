# Lattice QCD and the Neutron Electric Dipole Moment

> The recent lattice QCD calculations of the neutron and proton electric dipole moments (EDMs) and the CP-violating $π{\rm NN}$ coupling constant due to the $θ$ term are reviewed. Progress towards nucleon EDM calculations, including the Weinberg three-gluon operator, and the quark chromoelectric dipole moment operator and their renormalization, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent lattice QCD calculations of the neutron and proton electric dipole moments (EDMs) from the QCD theta term, showing that when the CP-violating phase of the nucleon state is properly included, the results are consistent with zero within one sigma, contrary to earlier calculations that omitted this term.

## Method

The review summarizes lattice QCD calculations that extract the electric dipole form factor F3 from three-point and two-point correlation functions, using the Taylor expansion in the theta parameter to first order. The calculations employ various fermion actions including clover fermions, twisted mass fermions, highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ), domain-wall fermions (DWF), and overlap fermions, with the topological charge operator defined through gradient flow, the anomalous Ward identity, or the overlap Dirac operator. A cluster decomposition error reduction technique (CDER) is applied to reduce variance from the disconnected insertion of the topological charge by summing only local charge contributions within a finite 4-D radius.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM from clover fermions: d_n = -0.00152(71) theta-bar e·fm (lattice spacing 0.0907 fm, pion masses 410, 568, 699 MeV)
- Neutron EDM from twisted mass fermions: |d_n| = 0.0009(24) theta-bar e·fm (lattice spacing 0.08 fm, pion mass 139 MeV)
- Neutron EDM from clover+HISQ: d_n = -0.003(7)(20) theta-bar e·fm (lattice spacings 0.0570-0.1207 fm, pion masses 135-320 MeV)
- Neutron EDM from overlap+DWF: d_n = -0.00148(14)(31) theta-bar e·fm (lattice spacing 0.0114 fm, pion masses 339, 432, 560 MeV)
- Proton EDM from overlap+DWF: d_p = 0.0038(11)(8) theta-bar e·fm
- The current experimental upper limit on nEDM is d_n = (0.0 ± 1.1_stat ± 0.2_sys) × 10^{-26} e·cm from ultracold neutron experiments (Abel 2020)
- The CKM matrix contribution to nEDM is estimated at 1 × 10^{-32} e·cm < |d_n(CKM)| < 6 × 10^{-32} e·cm (Seng 2014)

## Context

This review updates the previous review by Shindler (2021) on lattice calculations of nucleon EDMs, focusing on the theta term and the CP-violating pi-NN coupling constant. The calculations extend earlier work by Shintani (2005), Berruto (2005), Guo (2015), and Alexandrou (2015) that did not include the CP phase of the nucleon state, and now show results consistent with zero. The lattice results provide the hadronic matrix elements needed to connect experimental EDM searches to beyond-standard-model physics, complementing ongoing experimental efforts including nEDM at LANL, TUCAN, PanEDM, PNPI, and beam nEDM experiments that aim to improve sensitivity by a factor of 10 to 100 by 2030.
