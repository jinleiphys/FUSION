# Chemical Equilibration and the Hadron-QGP Phase Transition

> We discuss recent experimental results on hadron multiplicities in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. The data for central collisions are in quantitative agreement with predictions of a thermal model assuming full chemical equilibration. It is argued that this provides strong, albeit indirect, evidence for the formation of a partonic phase in the collision prior to hadron production.

# Chemical Equilibration and the Hadron-QGP Phase Transition

## Key claim
The thermal model assuming full chemical equilibration quantitatively describes hadron multiplicities in central ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, providing strong but indirect evidence for the formation of a partonic phase prior to hadron production. The author argues that the closeness of chemical freeze-out parameters to the expected phase boundary between quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas is the key to understanding apparent chemical equilibration, rather than purely hadronic processes.

## Method
The author applies a grand canonical thermal model with temperature T and baryochemical potential μB as the two independent parameters, fixing the fireball volume, strangeness chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential via conservation laws for baryon number, strangeness, and charge. Hadron yields are computed using the partition function with an excluded volume correction for hadronic interactions, and the model parameters are determined solely from pion/baryon and anti-nucleon/nucleon ratios without using strange particle yields as input. The resulting predictions are compared to measured particle ratios from AGS and SPS experiments.

## Key numbers
- Temperature T = 168 MeV and baryochemical potential μB = 266 MeV from thermal model fits to SPS data
- Enhancement factors of more than one order of magnitude observed for multi-strange baryons compared to nucleon-nucleon collisions
- Strangeness suppression factor γs = 0.67 in e+e- annihilation, implying Omega production reduced by more than a factor of 3 compared to full chemical equilibrium in Pb-Pb collisions
- Hadronic system lifetimes of order 50 fm/c or more needed for full chemical equilibration, versus upper limits of about 10 fm/c from interferometry analyses
- Critical temperature at zero baryon density approximately 170 MeV from lattice QCD calculations

## Context
This work extends thermal model analyses previously applied at SIS, AGS, and SPS energies (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999; Braun-Munzinger et al., 1998) and contradicts purely hadronic event generator interpretations that cannot reproduce multi-strange hadron enhancement. The finding that chemical freeze-out occurs near the QCD phase boundary enables predictions for hadron production at RHIC and LHC (Stachel, 2000; Braun-Munzinger, 2000) and supports the interpretation that the system crosses the phase boundary from the quark-gluon plasma side. The results contrast with e+e- annihilation studies (Becattini, 1999) where strangeness suppression indicates non-thermal hadronization without gluonic contributions.
