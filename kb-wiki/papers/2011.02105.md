# Constrained extrapolation problem and order-dependent mappings

> We consider the problem of extrapolating the perturbation series for the dilute Fermi gas in three dimensions to the unitary limit of infinite scattering length and into the BEC region, using the available strong-coupling information to constrain the extrapolation problem. In this constrained extrapolation problem (CEP) the goal is to find classes of approximants that give well converged results already for low perturbative truncation orders. First, we show that standard Padé and Borel methods are too restrictive to give satisfactory results for this CEP. A generalization of Borel extrapolation is given by the so-called Maximum Entropy extrapolation method (MaxEnt). However, we show that MaxEnt requires extensive elaborations to be applicable to the dilute Fermi gas and is thus not practical for the CEP in this case. Instead, we propose order-dependent-mapping extrapolation (ODME) as a simple, practical, and general method for the CEP. We find that the ODME approximants for the ground-state energy of the dilute Fermi gas are robust with respect to changes of the mapping choice and agree with results from quantum Monte Carlo simulations within uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper proposes order-dependent-mapping extrapolation (ODME) as a simple, practical, and general method for the constrained extrapolation problem (CEP), where the goal is to find approximants that converge well at low perturbative orders by incorporating strong-coupling information. The authors show that standard Pade and Borel methods are too restrictive for the dilute Fermi gas CEP, and that Maximum Entropy extrapolation requires extensive elaborations that make it impractical for this case.

## Method

The authors consider the ground-state energy of an unpolarized spin-1/2 Fermi gas with short-range interactions, characterized by the variable x = k_F a_s, where k_F is the Fermi momentum and a_s is the s-wave scattering length. They construct order-dependent-mapping extrapolation (ODME) approximants that reproduce the perturbation series to order N and incorporate the leading strong-coupling coefficients d_1 and d_2, using the unitary limit Bertsch parameter xi as a constraint. The ODME method improves on the order-dependent mapping approach of Seznec and Zinn-Justin (1979) by building in information on the leading strong-coupling coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Perturbation coefficients for the dilute Fermi gas ground-state energy: c_1 = 10/(9 pi), c_2 = (44-8 ln 2)/(21 pi^2), c_3 = 0.0303089(0), c_4 = -0.0708(1)
- Bertsch parameter from experiment: xi = 0.376(4) (Ku et al., 2012)
- Bertsch parameter from quantum Monte Carlo: xi = 0.372(5) (Carlson et al., 2011)
- Leading strong-coupling coefficients: d_1 approximately -0.9, d_2 approximately -0.8 (Carlson et al., 2011)

## Context

This work extends the constrained extrapolation problem approach previously applied to the dilute Fermi gas by Wellenhofer et al. (2018, 2020), and addresses limitations of standard Pade and Borel methods for this system. The ODME method enables reliable extrapolation of the perturbation series from weak coupling to the unitary limit and into the BEC region, with results that agree with quantum Monte Carlo simulations within uncertainties. The approach is motivated by the need for practical resummation methods when only low-order perturbation coefficients (N less than or similar to 4-6) are available, as is typical in many nuclear-physics problems.
