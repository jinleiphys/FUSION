# Kaon form factor in holographic QCD

> The kaon form factor in the spacelike region is calculated using a holographic QCD model with the "bottom-up" approach. We found that our result for the kaon form factor in low $Q^2$ has a remarkable agreement with the existing data, where $Q^2$ is the four-momentum transfer squared. The charge radius of the kaon, as well as the kaon decay constant, are found to be in a good agreement with the experiment data. We then predict the kaon form factor in the asymptotic region (larger $Q^2$) showing $1/Q^2$ behavior, which is consistent with the perturbative QCD prediction.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the kaon form factor using a "bottom-up" holographic QCD model and find remarkable agreement with existing low-$Q^2$ data, but the agreement is explicitly limited to the low-momentum-transfer regime; the prediction for larger $Q^2$ shows a $1/Q^2$ behavior consistent with perturbative QCD, but no experimental data exist at those momenta to confirm it.

## Method
The authors employ a hard-wall AdS/QCD model with $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ flavor symmetry, where the 5D metric is truncated at an infrared cutoff $z_0 = 1/\Lambda_{QCD}$. They solve the equations of motion for the vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar bulk fields, and extract the kaon electromagnetic form factor from the three-point function of two axial-vector currents and one vector current via the AdS/CFT correspondence. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the pion mass, pion decay constant, and kaon mass.

## Key numbers
- Kaon mass $M^a_{K,1} = 495.7$ MeV (fitted value for $a=4,5,6,7$)
- Kaon decay constant $f_{K^{+}} = 104$ MeV
- Kaon charge radius $r_{K^{+}} = 0.56$ fm
- Strange current quark mass $m_s = 51.96$ MeV (model parameter before redefinition) or $m_s = 188.5$ MeV (after redefinition)
- Light current quark mass $m_q = 2.29$ MeV (before redefinition) or $m_q = 8.31$ MeV (after redefinition)
- Quark condensate $\sigma_q = (328.3\ \text{MeV})^3$ (before redefinition) or $\sigma = (213.7\ \text{MeV})^3$ (after redefinition)
- Hard-wall cutoff $z_0 = (322.5\ \text{MeV})^{-1}$
- $K^*$ meson mass $m_{K^*} = 791$ MeV
- $K_1$ meson mass $m_{K_1} = 1458$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the hard-wall holographic QCD approach previously applied to pion and nucleon form factors (Erlich et al. 2005, Karch et al. 2006, Grigoryan and Radyushkin 2007) to the kaon, which contains a strange quark. The results are compared to the experimental data of Amendolia et al. (1986) at low $Q^2$ and to the perturbative QCD prediction of Lepage and Brodsky (1980) at high $Q^2$. The authors contrast their approach with the light-front holographic method of Ahmady et al. (2018), noting that their own model yields a slightly smaller charge radius ($0.56$ fm versus $0.615$ fm). This work enables future calculations of form factors for heavier mesons containing bottom and charm quarks.
