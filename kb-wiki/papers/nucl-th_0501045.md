# Resonance and virtual bound state solutions of the radial Schrödinger equation

> The formulation of the eigenvalue problem for the Schrödinger equation is studied, for the numerical solution a new approach is applied. With the usual exponentially rising free-state asymptotical behavior, and also with a first order correction to it, the lower half of the $k$ plane is systematically explored. Note that no other method, including the complex rotation one, is suitable for calculating virtual bound states far from the origin. Various phenomena are studied about how the bound and the virtual bound states are organized into a system. Even for short range potentials, the free state asymptotical condition proves to be inadequate at some distance from the real $k$ axis; the first order correction doesn't solve this problem. Therefore, the structure of the space spanned by the mathematically possible solutions is studied and the notion of minimal solutions is introduced. Such solutions provide the natural boundary condition for bound states, their analytical continuation to the lower half plane does the same for resonances and virtual bound states. The eigenvalues coincide with the poles of the S-matrix. The possibility of continuation based on numerical data is also demonstrated. The proposed scheme is suitable for the definition of the in- and outgoing solutions for positive energies; therefore, also for the calculation of the so called distorted S-matrix in case of long range interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the usual free-state exponentially rising boundary condition is inadequate for calculating resonances and virtual bound states far from the real k-axis, and that the correct boundary condition is provided by the analytical continuation of minimal solutions to the lower half of the complex wave-number plane, with the eigenvalues coinciding with the poles of the S-matrix.

## Method

The author introduces a "reference method" where the radial Schrödinger equation solution is split into a fixed reference function and a correction function, producing an inhomogeneous differential equation that is integrated inward from a large radius. The notion of minimal solutions (solutions that decrease faster at infinity than any other solution) is used to define the natural boundary condition for bound states, and their analytical continuation to the lower half-plane provides the boundary condition for resonances and virtual bound states. The method is applied to the Saxon-Woods potential with parameters R_o=7.5 fm, μ=1.4 fm^{-1}, and V_o=-2.0 fm^{-2}, using a matching procedure at R_m=R_o with R_M=30.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- The stability region for the free-state reference function integration is Im(k) > -μ/2, where μ=1.4 fm^{-1}
- The iterated boundary condition extends the stability region to Im(k) > -μ (i.e., Im(k) > -1.4 fm^{-1})
- At k=-i·0.6 fm^{-1}, the difference between free-state and iterated boundary condition wavefunctions is only 4%
- The range singularity occurs at k=-iμ/2 = -i·0.7 fm^{-1}
- For L=4, the ground state is at i·0.904 fm^{-1} (not presented in the eigenvalue figure)
- An example eigenvalue for L=4 is at k=0.74 - i·0.37 fm^{-1}
- The unphysical truncation eigenvalues form a horizontal row with spacing inversely proportional to R_M (tested at R_M=25, 27.5, 32.5, and 35.0 fm)

## Context

This work extends the earlier reference method proposed by Borbély (1995) and addresses limitations of the complex rotation method, which according to Sert (2001) cannot give virtual states. The paper systematically explores the lower half of the k-plane for the Saxon-Woods potential, demonstrating that the free-state boundary condition generates unphysical "truncation eigenvalues" that depend on the matching radius R_M. The proposed scheme enables calculation of virtual bound states far from the origin and provides a framework for defining in- and outgoing solutions for long-range potentials, enabling calculation of the distorted S-matrix.
