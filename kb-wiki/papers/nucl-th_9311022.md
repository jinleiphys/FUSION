# Three-body resonances by complex scaling

> It is demonstrated that the complex scaling method can be used in practical calculations to localize three-body resonances. Our model example emphasizes the fact that in three-body systems several essentially different asymptotic behaviors can appear. We show that the possibility of these different asymptotic configurations can lead to an apparent, resonance like structure in the three-body continuum.

## Key claim

The complex scaling method can be used in practical calculations to localize three-body resonances, but the presence of multiple distinct asymptotic behaviors (such as resonance+scattering configurations) can produce apparent, resonance-like structures in the three-body continuum that are not genuine three-body resonances.

## Method

The author applied the complex scaling method to a three-body model with separable interactions between three particles (masses m1=m2=2, m3=4) using harmonic oscillator basis expansions in different Jacobi coordinate systems. Three trial wave functions were constructed: one using the (12)3 Jacobi system (Psi1), one using the (23)1 system (Psi2), and a unified combination of both (Psi3). The complex scaling transformation was applied uniformly to all relative coordinates, and the resulting complex-scaled Hamiltonian was diagonalized to identify resonant states as isolated complex eigenvalues that appear consistently across all Jacobi coordinate representations.

## Key numbers

- Two-body subsystem resonance in (1,2) at energy E = 1.5 - i0.5 (arbitrary units), produced by potential strength lambda12 = 0.6377 + i0.0697
- Two-body subsystem resonance in (1,3) and (2,3) at energy 1.7553 - i0.2438, produced by potential strengths lambda13 = lambda23 = 1.0
- Three-body resonance identified at energy 4.128 - i0.337, appearing in all three trial wave function calculations
- Oscillator size parameters: b = 1.0 for potentials, bar b = 2.0 for wave functions
- Complex rotation angle theta = 0.4 rad used in all three-body calculations

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously applied to two-body resonances and bound states (Ho 1983), to three-body systems where multiple asymptotic configurations can coexist. The study was motivated by contradictory theoretical results regarding the existence of a soft dipole resonance in the neutron halo nucleus 6He (Suzuki 1991; Danilin 1993). The author demonstrates that apparent structures in the three-body continuum can arise from resonance+scattering type asymptotic behaviors rather than genuine three-body resonances, and suggests that the claimed soft dipole resonance in 6He may be such an artifact. The method enables future realistic calculations of three-body resonances in nuclear systems like 6He, which the author states is in progress.
