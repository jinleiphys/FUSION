# Spin and Isospin Effects in the NN -> NK Lambda Reaction Near Threshold

> The spin and isospin structure of the amplitudes and observables for K+Lambda production in nucleon-nucleon collisions in the near-threshold region is analysed. It is shown that, with reasonable values for the relative strengths of the pi and rho terms in a meson-exchange model, one expects production on the neutron to be significantly stronger than that on the proton. Negative values of the spin-transfer coefficient D_NN are also predicted due to pi-rho interference.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a modified form of chiral perturbation theory (MChPT), which suppresses chiral logarithms above a certain energy scale via an additional parameter, provides a more suitable extrapolation of lattice QCD pseudoscalar decay constant data to the physical quark mass than standard dimensionally regularized ChPT, because it has fewer fit parameters and better matches the intermediate-mass behavior seen in lattice simulations.

## Method

The author presents results from UKQCD simulations using 2-flavor O(a)-improved Wilson quarks on 16^3 x 32 lattices at beta=5.2, with three quark masses corresponding to kappa values 0.1358, 0.1355, and 0.1350. The pseudoscalar decay constant f_PS is extracted using the order-a improved expression (1+b_A m_PS)(a f_A + c_A a f_P), and the results are compared with JLQCD data, standard ChPT predictions, Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations, and a proposed modified ChPT form that replaces the chiral logarithm's mass scale with a term containing an additional parameter to suppress the logarithm at higher masses.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar decay constant raw lattice values (a f_A, a f_P) at kappa=0.1358: 0.0829(26) and 0.1457(78); at kappa=0.1355: 0.1055(14) and 0.1835(44); at kappa=0.1350: 0.1336(11) and 0.2468(33)
* The modified ChPT fit parameter Lambda = 0.93 +/- 0.42 GeV, compared to the standard ChPT parameter Lambda_4 = 1.25 (+0.15/-0.13) GeV from Colangelo (2003)
* The modified ChPT form has three fit parameters (f_pi(0), Lambda, and X) versus seven parameters in the O(p^6) standard ChPT expansion
* Finite volume condition: m_PS * L is approximately 4 for all three simulated quark masses

## Context

This work extends the UKQCD collaboration's earlier results (Allton, 2004) on pseudoscalar decay constants and compares them with JLQCD data (Aoki, 2002) and Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations (Tandy, 2003; Maris, 1999). The paper critiques the JLQCD approach for omitting the chiral logarithm term and proposes a modified ChPT form that builds on earlier work by the Adelaide group (Leinweber, 1999; Leinweber, 2001; Young, 2002) to extend the radius of convergence of ChPT while maintaining correct chiral behavior near the limit. The approach enables more reliable extrapolation of lattice data to physical quark masses, though the author notes that continuum extrapolation and the difference between lattice mesons (equal-mass quarks) and physical mesons (e.g., K mesons with strange and light quarks) remain open issues.
