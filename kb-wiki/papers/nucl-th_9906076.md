# Elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions near the balance energy

> The proton elliptic flow in collisions of Ca on Ca at energies from 30 to 100 MeV/nucleon is studied in an isospin-dependent transport model. With increasing incident energy, the elliptic flow shows a transition from positive to negative flow. Its magnitude depends on both the nuclear equation of state (EOS) and the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section. Different elliptic flows are obtained for a stiff EOS with free nucleon-nucleon cross sections and a soft EOS with reduced nucleon-nucleon cross sections, although both lead to vanishing in-plane transverse flow at the same balance energy. The study of both in-plane and elliptic flows at intermediate energies thus provides a means to extract simultaneously the information on the nuclear equation of state and the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section in medium.

## Key claim

The proton elliptic flow in 48Ca+48Ca collisions at intermediate energies (30–100 MeV/nucleon) can distinguish between different combinations of nuclear equation of state (EOS) and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections that yield the same balance energy (approximately 65.5 MeV/nucleon), specifically showing that a stiff EOS with free cross sections gives negative elliptic flow while a soft EOS with 12% reduced cross sections gives positive elliptic flow at that energy.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate 48Ca+48Ca collisions at an impact parameter of 2 fm for beam energies from 30 to 100 MeV/nucleon. They compared two nuclear equations of state, a stiff EOS with compressibility 380 MeV and a soft EOS with compressibility 200 MeV, and varied the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section (free versus 12% reduced). The elliptic flow v2 was computed as the average of (p_x^2 - p_y^2)/(p_x^2 + p_y^2) for protons.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy: approximately 65.5 MeV/nucleon for both the stiff EOS with free nucleon-nucleon cross sections and the soft EOS with 0.88σ_NN (12% reduced cross sections).
- Compressibility values: 380 MeV for the stiff EOS; 200 MeV for the soft EOS.
- Impact parameter: 2 fm for all simulations.
- Energy range studied: 30 to 100 MeV/nucleon.
- Asymmetry potential strength: C = 32 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective flow in heavy ion collisions, particularly the use of balance energy (where in-plane transverse flow vanishes) to probe nuclear matter properties. Earlier work by Klaak (1993) and Li (1996) had shown that the same balance energy could be obtained with different EOS and cross section combinations, making it ambiguous. The present study builds on a suggestion by one of the authors (Li, 1999) that differential transverse flow could break this degeneracy, and demonstrates that elliptic flow provides an alternative observable to simultaneously extract the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
