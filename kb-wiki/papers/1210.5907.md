# Baryon Number Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The cross-over region of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy ion collisions is influenced by the nearby deconfinement, chiral and baryon condensation phase transitions. A characteristic signature of the deconfinement transition in this region can be infered using the flux tube model, which is dual to the Polyakov loop description and which offers a visual picture of what happens during the transition. The three-point (anti)vertices of a flux tube network lead to formation of (anti)baryons upon hadronisation. Since there is no fundamental interaction associated with the baryon number, correlations in the baryon number distribution at the last scattering surface directly reflect the preceding pattern of the flux tube vertices in the QGP. An alternating pattern of vertices and antivertices would lead to an oscillatory signal in the two-point baryon number correlations, under the experimental conditions prevalent in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The strength of the oscillations is a measure of the flexibility of the QGP.

# Baryon Number Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The deconfinement phase transition leaves a characteristic oscillatory signature in two-point baryon number correlations at the kinetic freeze-out stage of heavy ion collisions, provided the system has a non-zero chemical potential and is not in equilibrium. The strength of the oscillations is a measure of how tightly or softly the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is packed, reflecting the extent to which the nearby deconfinement transition influences QGP properties in the cross-over region.

## Method
The author formulates a flux tube model on a lattice with spacing a, where flux tubes live on links and quarks/vertices live on sites, with variables taking values 0, ±1 depending on flux direction. The grand canonical partition function is expressed as a 3-dimensional XY spin model with ordinary and Z3-symmetric magnetic fields, which can be simulated without a fermion sign problem at finite chemical potential. The equilibrium correlations between flux tube vertices in 3-dimensional position space are calculated and projected onto the observed surface of the fireball, then expanded in partial Fourier series based on rotation symmetry around the beam axis and parity.

## Key numbers
- The QGP fireball in central collisions has energy density ε ≃ 1 GeV/fm³ and temperature T_cr ≃ 175 MeV.
- For N_f = 3 QCD at small chemical potentials, the first-order deconfinement phase transition ends in a critical line around m = 1.5 GeV.
- The expected inter-baryon separation scale for the oscillatory signal is ∼ 2 fm.
- Heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC produce O(1000) hadrons, including O(100) baryons and antibaryons.

## Context
This work extends the flux tube model of deconfinement (Patel 2012) to predict a specific two-point baryon number correlation signal in heavy ion collisions, analogous to the study of temperature fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background radiation (Weinberg). The approach is dual to the Polyakov loop description of deconfinement and builds on lattice QCD results showing flux tube configurations between static quark sources (Leinweber). The formalism enables comparison with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, where the challenge is to extract baryon number correlations from detected protons and anti-protons, relying on isospin symmetry to characterize the total baryon number distribution.
