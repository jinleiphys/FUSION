# Systematic study of the $α$ decay preformation factor of nuclei around the $\boldsymbol{Z=82}$, $\boldsymbol{N=126}$ shell closures within a generalized liquid drop model

> In this work, we systematically study the $α$ decay preformation factors $P_α$ and $α$ decay half-lives of 152 nuclei around $Z$ = 82, $N$ = 126 closed shells based on a generalized liquid drop model while $P_α$ is extracted from the ratio of the calculated $α$ decay half-life to the experimental one. The results show that there is an obvious linear relationship between $P_α$ and the product of valance protons (holes) $N_p$ and valance neutrons (holes) $N_n$. At the same time, we extract the $$P_α$ values of even-even nuclei around $\emph{Z}$ = 82, $\emph{N}$ = 126 closed shells from the work of Sun \textit{et al.} [\href {https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6471/aac981} {J. Phys. G: Nucl. Part. Phys. $\bm{45}$, 075106 (2018)}], in which the $P_α$ can be calculated by two different microscopic formulas. We find that the $P_α$ are also related to $N_pN_n$. Combining with our previous works [Sun \textit{et al.}, \href {https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevC.94.024338} {Phys. Rev. C $\bm{94}$, 024338 (2016)}; Deng \textit{et al.}, \href {https://doi.org/10.1103/ PhysRevC 96.024318} {ibid. $\bm{96}$, 024318 (2017)}; Deng \textit{et al.}, \href {https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevC.97.044322} {ibid. $\bm{97}$, 044322 (2018)}] and the work of Seif \textit{et al.} [\href {http://dx.doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevC.84.064608}{Phys. Rev. C $\bm{84}$, 064608 (2011)}], we suspect that this phenomenon of linear relationship for the nuclei around those closed shells is model independent. It may be caused by the effect of the valence protons (holes) and valence neutrons (holes) around the shell closures. Finally, using the formula obtained by fitting the $P_α$ calculated by the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM), we calculate the $α$ decay half-lives of these nuclei. The calculated results are agree with the experimental data well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the linear relationship between the alpha decay preformation factor \(P_{\alpha}\) and the product of valence protons (holes) \(N_p\) and valence neutrons (holes) \(N_n\) for nuclei around the \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) shell closures is likely model independent, as it is observed using the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) in addition to previous microscopic and phenomenological approaches, and may be caused by the valence proton-neutron interaction near shell closures.

## Method

The authors used the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) to calculate alpha decay half-lives for 152 nuclei (including 47 even-even, 73 odd-A, and 32 doubly-odd nuclei) around the \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) shell closures. The alpha preformation factor \(P_{\alpha}\) was extracted from the ratio of the calculated GLDM half-life to the experimental half-life taken from NUBASE2016. The extracted \(P_{\alpha}\) values were then plotted against the scaled product \(\frac{N_p N_n}{Z_0 + N_0}\) to examine the linear relationship, and linear fits were performed to obtain parameters for predicting half-lives.

## Key numbers

- 152 nuclei around \(Z=82\), \(N=126\) shell closures were studied, comprising 47 even-even, 73 odd-A, and 32 doubly-odd nuclei.
- For even-even nuclei in Region I, the extracted \(P_{\alpha}\) values range from 0.031 (for \(^{208}\)Po) to 0.210 (for \(^{204}\)Ra).
- For even-even nuclei in Regions II and III, the extracted \(P_{\alpha}\) values range from 0.018 (for \(^{210}\)Po) to 0.604 (for \(^{220}\)Th).
- The standard deviation \(\sigma_2\) between the calculated half-lives (using GLDM with fitted \(P_{\alpha}\)) and experimental data is at most 0.40 for all nucleus types.
- For odd-A favored decay in Region I, \(P_{\alpha}\) values include 0.014 for \(^{211}\)At and 0.322 for \(^{195}\)Rn.

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Seif et al. (2011) and the authors' previous studies (Sun et al., 2016; Deng et al., 2017; Deng et al., 2018) which showed a linear relationship between \(P_{\alpha}\) and \(N_p N_n\) for nuclei around shell closures using different models (e.g., two-potential approach and cluster-formation model). By confirming this linear trend with the GLDM, the authors argue the relationship is model independent and likely due to valence proton-neutron interactions. This enables the use of a simple linear formula to predict alpha decay half-lives for nuclei in this region, with calculated half-lives agreeing well with experimental data (standard deviations below 0.40).
