# Radiative and Collisional Jet Energy Loss in the Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC

> We calculate and compare bremsstrahlung and collisional energy loss of hard partons traversing a quark-gluon plasma. Our treatment of both processes is complete at leading order in the coupling and accounts for the probabilistic nature of the jet energy loss. We find that the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for neutral $π^0$ production in heavy ion collisions is sensitive to the inclusion of collisional and radiative energy loss contributions while the averaged energy loss only slightly increases if collisional energy loss is included for parent parton energies $E\gg T$. These results are important for the understanding of jet quenching in Au+Au collisions at $200 {\rm AGeV}$ at RHIC. Comparison with data is performed applying the energy loss calculation to a relativistic ideal (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic description of the thermalized medium formed at RHIC.

## Key claim
The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for neutral pions is sensitive to the inclusion of both collisional and radiative energy loss, even though the average energy loss of hard partons increases only slightly when collisional loss is added for parent parton energies \(E \gg T\). The full parton distribution evolution, not just the mean energy loss, must be accounted for to capture this sensitivity.

## Method
The authors evolve hard parton momentum distributions through a quark-gluon plasma using coupled Fokker-Planck type rate equations. Radiative energy loss is treated with the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) formalism, which handles the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect correctly for all gluon energies above \(g_s T\). Collisional energy loss is incorporated via a drag term and a diffusion term that reproduce the leading-order mean energy loss rate and satisfy detailed balance. The medium is described by a relativistic ideal (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model constrained by soft observables at RHIC, and the final hadron spectra are obtained by vacuum fragmentation of the evolved jet distributions.

## Key numbers
- Mean collisional energy loss rates for a light hard parton scattering off a soft parton are given by expressions such as \(\left.\frac{dE}{dt}\right|_{qq} = \frac29 n_f \pi \alpha_s^2 T^2 \left[\ln \frac{ET}{m_g^2} + c_f + \frac{23}{12} + c_s \right]\), where \(m_g^2 = 2 \pi \alpha_s T^2 (1 + n_f/6)\) and \(c_s \approx -1.66246\).
- In a static infinite medium with \(T = 400\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\alpha_s = 1/3\), an initial quark jet of energy \(E_i = 16\ \mathrm{GeV}\) shows a larger mean energy loss from radiative processes than from elastic collisions alone.
- For the full hydrodynamic calculation at RHIC, the strong coupling constant is set to \(\alpha_s = 0.27\) to reproduce the most central data; this is adjusted from \(\alpha_s = 0.33\) used in a prior radiative-only study (Qin 2007).
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is computed for central (\(b = 2.4\ \mathrm{fm}\)) and mid-central (\(b = 7.5\ \mathrm{fm}\)) Au+Au collisions at \(200\ \mathrm{AGeV}\) and compared to PHENIX data for 0-5% and 20-30% centrality.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of radiative jet energy loss using the AMY formalism (Arnold 2001) and a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic medium (Qin 2007) by consistently including collisional energy loss. It contrasts with earlier estimates that found collisional loss to be negligible (Zakharov 2007; Renk 2007) and with studies that argued for a significant role of elastic collisions (Mustafa 2003; Mustafa 2004; Adil 2006; Wicks 2007). The results enable more complete comparisons of jet quenching models with RHIC data and emphasize the need to couple energy loss calculations to well-tested dynamical medium descriptions rather than simple average-loss approximations.
