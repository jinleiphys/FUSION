# Whole fusion-fission process with Langevin approach and compared with analytical solution for barrier passage

> We investigate time-dependent probability for a Brownian particle passing over the barrier to stay at a metastable potential pocket against escaping over the barrier. This is related to whole fusion-fission dynamical process and can be called the reverse Kramers problem. By the passing probability over the saddle point of inverse harmonic potential multiplying the exponential decay factor of a particle in the metastable potential, we present an approximate expression for the modified passing probability over the barrier, in which the effect of reflection boundary of potential is taken into account. Our analytical result and Langevin Monte-Carlo simulation show that the probability passing and against escaping over the barrier is a non-monotonous function of time and its maximal value is less than the stationary result of passing probability over the saddle point of inverse harmonic potential.

# Whole fusion-fission process with Langevin approach and compared with analytical solution for barrier passage

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the fusion probability in heavy-ion reactions should be identified with the maximal value of a time-dependent modified passing probability over the barrier of a metastable potential, rather than the stationary passing probability over an inverse harmonic potential alone. This modified probability is non-monotonous in time and its maximum is less than the stationary result, with the approximation becoming accurate only when the fission barrier height exceeds the temperature.

## Method
The authors model the fusion-fission process as a Brownian particle in a metastable potential consisting of an inverse harmonic potential at the saddle point smoothly linked to a harmonic potential at the ground state. They derive an approximate analytical expression for the time-dependent modified passing probability by multiplying the passing probability over the saddle point of an inverse harmonic potential (obtained from the phase-space distribution function) by an exponential decay factor exp(-r_e t), where r_e is the steady escape rate from the Kramers theory or the inverse of the mean first passage time (MFPT). The analytical results are compared with Langevin Monte-Carlo simulations of the full Langevin equation with Gaussian white noise.

## Key numbers
- Barrier height U_b = 1.0, temperature T = 0.4, damping coefficient γ = 1.0, initial mean velocity v̄₀ = -5.0, initial mean position x̄₀ = 0.2 (dimensionless units, with m = k_B = 1.0), used in the low-temperature case where U_b > T.
- Barrier height U_b = 0.25, temperature T = 2.0, used in the high-temperature case where T > U_b.
- Barrier height U_b = 1.0, temperature T = 2.0, damping coefficient γ = 2.0, used in another high-temperature comparison.
- Initial temperature of thermalization T₀ values tested: 0.0, 0.4, and 2.0, showing that the stable value of the time-dependent passing probability decreases with increasing T₀.

## Context
This work extends the Kramers problem (Kramers 1940) to the "reverse Kramers problem" relevant to heavy-ion fusion, where a Brownian particle with initial velocity passes over the saddle point into a metastable well and subsequently escapes. It builds on the fusion-by-diffusion model of Abe (2000), which used the stationary passing probability over an inverse harmonic potential as the fusion probability, and on prior work by Bao and collaborators (2003) on initial distribution effects. The paper enables a more accurate treatment of fusion probability for super-heavy element synthesis, where the fission barrier is low and the reflection boundary of the metastable potential significantly reduces the passing probability compared to the inverse harmonic approximation.
