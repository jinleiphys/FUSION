# Bohr Hamiltonian with Hulthen plus ring-shaped potential for triaxial nuclei with deformation-dependent mass term

> In this work, we present a new version of the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for triaxial nuclei within Deformation-Dependent Mass formalism (DDM) using the Hulthén potential. We shall call the developed model Z(5)-HD. Analytical expressions for energy spectra are derived by means of the recent version of the Asymptotic Iteration Method. The calculated numerical results of energies and B(E2) transition rates are compared with the experimental data, and several theoretical results from Z(5) model, the model Z(5)-H using the Hulthén potential without DDM formalism as well as theoretical predictions of Z(5)-DD model with Davidson potential using DDM formalism. The obtained results show an overall agreement with experimental data and an important improvement in respect to the other models.

## Key claim

The Z(5)-HD model, which combines the Hulthen potential with a deformation-dependent mass (DDM) formalism for triaxial nuclei, yields improved agreement with experimental energy spectra and B(E2) transition rates compared to models using the Hulthen potential without DDM or the Davidson potential with DDM, but only for isotopes where the fitted mass deformation parameter \(a\) is nonzero; for several Xe isotopes the fit yields \(a=0\), indicating they behave as pure vibrators.

## Method

The authors solved the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for triaxial nuclei (Z(5) critical point symmetry) by assuming a separable potential of the form \(v(\beta,\gamma)=u(\beta)+f^2 w(\gamma)/\beta^2\), with the Hulthen potential \(u(\beta)=-1/(e^{\tau\beta}-1)\) and a ring-shaped potential \(w(\gamma)=(c+s\cos^2(3\gamma))/\sin^2(3\gamma)\). The mass was taken as deformation-dependent via \(B(\beta)=B_0/(1+a\beta^2)^2\). The Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) was used to derive analytical expressions for the energy eigenvalues and wave functions. The model was applied to eight isotopes (\({}^{126,128,130,132,134}\)Xe and \({}^{192,194,196}\)Pt), and the parameters \(\tau\), \(c\), \(s\), and \(a\) were fitted to experimental data using a least-squares procedure.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{126}\)Xe, the fitted mass deformation parameter is \(a=0.0025\); for \({}^{128,130,132,134}\)Xe, the fit yields \(a=0.0000\).
* For \({}^{192,194,196}\)Pt, the fitted mass deformation parameters are \(a=0.0010\), \(0.0030\), and \(0.0059\), respectively, increasing with mass number.
* The root-mean-square deviation \(\sigma\) was evaluated using the formula \(\sigma=\sqrt{\sum_i (E_i(exp)-E_i(th))^2 / [(m-1)E(2_1^+)^2]}\), where \(m\) is the number of states fitted (ranging from 7 for \({}^{132,134}\)Xe to 16 for \({}^{126}\)Xe).
* The signature of triaxial rigid rotor \(\Delta E = |E(2_g^+)+E(2_\gamma^+)-E(3_\gamma^+)|\) yields values (in keV) of 49, 17, 26, 162, 379, 8, 28, and 29 for \({}^{126,128,130,132,134}\)Xe and \({}^{192,194,196}\)Pt, respectively.
* The correlation coefficient between the mass deformation parameter \(a\) from Z(5)-HD and Z(5)-DD models for \({}^{126}\)Xe and \({}^{192,194,196}\)Pt is \(\rho=0.982\).

## Context

This work extends the Z(5) critical point symmetry model (Iachello 2001) by incorporating both the Hulthen potential and deformation-dependent mass (DDM) formalism, building on prior studies that used the Davidson potential with DDM (Z(5)-DD model, Bonatsos et al. 2015) and the Hulthen potential without DDM (Z(5)-H model, Choudhury et al. 2018). The results confirm the vibrational nature of \({}^{128,130,132,134}\)Xe, consistent with earlier findings, and show that the mass deformation parameter \(a\) acts as a structural parameter correlated across different potential choices. The model enables more precise calculations of collective excited states and B(E2) transition rates for triaxial nuclei, particularly for Pt isotopes where DDM effects are significant.
