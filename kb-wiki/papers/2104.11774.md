# White dwarfs and generalized uncertainty principle

> This work is motivated by the sign problem in a logarithmic parameter of black hole entropy and the existing more massive white dwarfs than the Chandrasekhar mass limit. We examine the quadratic, linear, and linear-quadratic generalized uncertainty principle (GUP) models within the virtue of recent masses and radii of white dwarfs. We consider the modification generated by introducing the minimal length proposal on the degenerate Fermi gas equation of state (EOS) and on the hydrostatic equation. For the latter, we applied Verlinde's proposal regarding entropic gravity to derived the quantum corrected Newtonian gravity which in turn responsible for modifying the hydrostatic equation. Through the chi-square analysis of the models, we have found that the observation data favor the quadratic dan linear GUP models without mass limit. However, for the quadratic-linear GUP model, we can obtain the positive value of the free parameter $γ_0$ as well as we can get mass limit more massive than the Chandrasekhar mass limit. In the linear-quadratic GUP model, the formation of stable massive white dwarfs than the Chandrasekhar limit is possible only if both parameters are not equal.

## Key claim

The paper claims that certain generalized uncertainty principle (GUP) models can produce white dwarf masses exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit, but only under specific conditions: the quadratic-linear GUP model yields a positive free parameter gamma_0 and a super-Chandrasekhar mass limit, while the linear-quadratic GUP model permits stable massive white dwarfs only if its two parameters are not equal.

## Method

The authors introduced minimal-length modifications from three GUP models (quadratic, linear, and linear-quadratic) into both the degenerate Fermi gas equation of state and the hydrostatic equation of white dwarfs. For the hydrostatic equation, they applied Verlinde's entropic gravity proposal to derive quantum-corrected Newtonian gravity from modified Bekenstein-Hawking entropy-area relations. They then performed a chi-square analysis comparing the resulting mass-radius relations to observational white dwarf data from Holberg (2012).

## Key numbers

- The quadratic GUP model predicts a saturation number density of n(infinity) = pi^2 / [2 (2pi)^3 hbar^3 gamma^(3/2)] when Fermi momentum goes to infinity
- The average distance between Fermi particles at large Fermi momentum is approximately the Planck length: d-bar ~ l_P ~ 1.616 x 10^(-35) m
- The quadratic GUP model predicts pressure scaling as P proportional to p_F (instead of the standard p_F^4) at very large Fermi momentum
- Observed super-Chandrasekhar white dwarfs have masses in the range 2.1 - 2.8 solar masses (M_sun)

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Rashidi (2016), Ong (2018), and Mathew (2018, 2020) who examined GUP effects on white dwarf mass-radius relations but did not include the deformation of Newtonian gravity from minimal length. The paper also addresses the observational puzzle of super-Chandrasekhar white dwarfs (e.g., SN2003fg, SN2006gz, SN2007if, SN2009dc) which standard theory cannot explain. The results enable future tests of GUP models using white dwarf observations and may help constrain the free parameters of quantum gravity theories.
