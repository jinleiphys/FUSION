# Algebraic Model for scattering in three-s-cluster systems. I. Theoretical Background

> A framework to calculate two-particle matrix elements for fully antisymmetrized three-cluster configurations is presented. The theory is developed for a scattering situation described in terms of the Algebraic Model. This means that the nuclear many-particle state and its asymptotic behaviour are expanded in terms of oscillator states of the intra-cluster coordinates. The Generating Function technique is used to optimize the calculation of matrix elements. In order to derive the dynamical equations, a multichannel version of the Algebraic Model is presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for calculating fully antisymmetrized three-cluster scattering states within the Algebraic Model, but the asymptotic solutions rely on a decoupling approximation that assumes off-diagonal matrix elements of the effective charge are sufficiently small, and the validity of this approximation must be checked for any specific three-cluster system.

## Method

The authors expand the nuclear many-particle wave function for three s-cluster systems in a harmonic oscillator basis using Hyperspherical Harmonics with Zernike-Brinkman classification, characterized by quantum numbers including hypermomentum K, hyperradial excitation n, and partial angular momenta l1 and l2. The Generating Function technique is employed to compute matrix elements of kinetic and potential energy operators for fully antisymmetrized three-cluster configurations. A multichannel version of the Algebraic Model is formulated where individual channels are characterized by K-values, and the Schrodinger equation is reduced to a system of coupled matrix equations that are solved for residual expansion coefficients and S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The hypermomentum K satisfies K = l1 + l2, l1 + l2 + 2, l1 + l2 + 4, ... with the constraint that l1 + l2 cannot exceed K
- Oscillator shells with N quanta are characterized by the constraint N = 2n + K
- For normal parity states (pi = (-1)^L) the minimal hypermomentum is K_min = L, while for abnormal parity states (pi = (-1)^(L+1)) K_min = L + 1
- The asymptotic hyperradial solutions for neutral clusters involve Hankel functions H_(K+2)^(1) and H_(K+2)^(2) with argument k*rho, where k = sqrt(2mE/hbar^2)
- For charged clusters, the Whittaker function parameter mu = K + 2 and the Sommerfeld parameter eta = (m/hbar^2)*(Z_eff/k)
- The asymptotic regime condition is given by k^2/rho_n^2 << 1, where rho_n = sqrt(4n + 2K + 6)

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Model, previously applied to binary cluster configurations by Filippov and collaborators (1980s) and later to binary clusters coupled to collective channels, to fully antisymmetrized three-cluster systems. The paper provides the theoretical foundation for subsequent applications to the three-cluster configurations alpha+n+n for He-6 and alpha+p+p for Be-6 (presented in Part II). The approach enables rigorous treatment of the Pauli principle in three-cluster scattering, addressing a gap in prior calculations by Descouvemont (1994), Varga and collaborators (1996), and Csoto and Lang (1999) where the continuum was approximated through sets of two-cluster configurations.
