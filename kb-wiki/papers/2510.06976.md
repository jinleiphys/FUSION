# Scattering of charged particles within Efros method utilizing oscillator series expansion of wave functions

> We apply the version of the Efros method utilizing oscillator expansion of wave functions to the Coulomb scattering problem using our recent developments of the HORSE formalism. The approach yields accurate phase shifts and cross sections with significantly reduced computational cost compared to the full HORSE method, while maintaining agreement with exact solutions. These results demonstrate the efficiency of the Efros method and its prospect for applications in ab initio nuclear reaction calculations.

## Key claim

The Efros method using oscillator expansion of wave functions, combined with the authors' recent HORSE formalism developments for Coulomb interactions, yields accurate phase shifts and cross sections for charged-particle scattering even when only a small number of short-range functions (SRFs) is used, provided the number of SRFs is at least 4 for energies up to about 15 MeV.

## Method

The authors expand scattering wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis and treat the Coulomb interaction using a modified kinetic energy operator that incorporates an additional diagonal term at large oscillator quantum numbers, following their earlier HORSE work (Yanikov 2025). They then apply the Efros variational method (Efros 2012; Sharaf 2025) using either eigenstates of the truncated Hamiltonian or oscillator functions as short-range basis functions, with the last basis function chosen as the first oscillator function outside the truncation boundary. The resulting linear system is solved for the phase shift and expansion coefficients, and the method is tested against exact numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation using the Numerov method.

## Key numbers

- For \(p{-}\alpha\) scattering in the \(p_{3/2}\) wave with \(\hbar\omega = 27\) MeV and truncation boundary \(N=10\), the Efros method with \(v=4\) SRFs yields phase shifts that converge to exact values; with \(v=2\) SRFs, results become unsatisfactory for energies above approximately 15 MeV.
- The second eigenstate energy in the \(p_{3/2}\) case is 10.25 MeV, explaining the deviation of \(v=2\) results starting near 11 MeV.
- For \(p{-}\alpha\) scattering in the \(p_{1/2}\) wave with \(\hbar\omega = 22\) MeV and \(N=10\), the second eigenstate energy is 10.99 MeV, and \(v=2\) results deviate at this energy.
- The Woods–Saxon potential parameters used for \(p{-}\alpha\) scattering are \(V_0 = -43.0\) MeV, \(R_0 = 2.0\) fm, \(\alpha_0 = 0.70\) fm, \(V_{ls} = 40.0\) MeV·fm², \(R_1 = 1.50\) fm, and \(\alpha_1 = 0.35\) fm.
- For \(p{-}^{15}N\) scattering in the \(s_{1/2}\) wave, the Woods–Saxon parameters are \(V_0 = -55.91\) MeV, \(R_0 = 3.083\) fm, \(\alpha_0 = 0.53\) fm, \(V_{ls} = 0.9\) MeV·fm², \(R_1 = 3.083\) fm, and \(\alpha_1 = 0.53\) fm, with \(\hbar\omega = 18\) MeV.
- Convergence of the Efros method with eigenfunction SRFs is observed for \(\hbar\omega\) in the range from approximately 25 MeV to approximately 50 MeV when \(v \gtrsim 4\).

## Context

This work extends the Efros method (Efros 2012), previously applied to non-Coulomb scattering by Sharaf (2025), to charged-particle scattering by incorporating the Coulomb treatment developed within the HORSE formalism by Yanikov (2025). The approach addresses the prohibitive computational cost of the full HORSE method when combined with the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) for ab initio nuclear reaction calculations, as the HORSE requires extremely large numbers of eigenstates even for light nuclei. The results enable future applications of the Efros method combined with NCSM for ab initio studies of reactions involving charged particles.
