# Event-by-event hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions

> We compare v2/ε2 and v3/ε3 from single-shot and event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic calculations and discuss the validity of using single-shot calculations as substitutes for event-by-event calculations. Further we present a proof- of-concept calculation demonstrating that v2 and v3 together can be used to strongly reduce initial condition ambiguities.

## Key claim

For a viscous fluid with sufficiently large specific shear viscosity (η/s > O(0.2)), single-shot hydrodynamic evolution of smooth averaged initial profiles can substitute for event-by-event evolution of fluctuating initial conditions when calculating v2/ε2 and v3/ε3 for unidentified charged hadrons, but this substitution may fail for identified heavy hadrons and for ideal fluids.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 A GeV, comparing two approaches: single-shot evolution of smooth ensemble-averaged initial profiles and event-by-event evolution of fluctuating initial entropy density profiles generated from Monte Carlo Glauber (MC-Glb.) and Monte Carlo KLN (MC-KLN) models. They computed eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow (v2/ε2) and triangular flow (v3/ε3) for thermal pions, kaons, and protons under both ideal and viscous (η/s = 0.2) hydrodynamic evolution, then performed a proof-of-concept study using deformed Gaussian initial conditions with MC-Glauber-like and MC-KLN-like eccentricities to test whether simultaneous analysis of v2 and v3 can distinguish between initial condition models.

## Key numbers

- For ideal fluid event-by-event hydrodynamics, the difference in v2/ε2 between single-shot and event-by-event approaches is about 5% for pions and increases to about 10% for protons
- For viscous hydrodynamics with η/s = 0.2, single-shot and event-by-event approaches produce almost identical v2/ε2 and v3/ε3 regardless of hadron mass
- η/s = 0.2 suppresses v3/ε3 by approximately 50% compared to ideal fluid, while v2/ε2 is suppressed by only about 25%
- The MC-KLN model has approximately 20% larger ε2 than the MC-Glauber model, while both models have similar ε3
- To obtain the same v2(pT) for MC-KLN-like and MC-Glauber-like initial conditions, the fluid must be twice as viscous for the former than for the latter

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Qiu and Heinz, 2011) on extracting η/s from eccentricity-scaled flow coefficients, and addresses the practical question raised by Luzum (2008) and Song (2010) about whether single-shot hydrodynamics can replace event-by-event calculations. The paper demonstrates that simultaneous analysis of v2 and v3 can resolve initial condition ambiguities that previously left large uncertainties in η/s extraction, and enables discrimination between competing initial state models such as MC-Glauber and MC-KLN. The conclusions agree qualitatively with statements made by Alver (2010) and Adare (2011).
