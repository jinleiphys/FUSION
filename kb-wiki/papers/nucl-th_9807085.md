# Meson exchange and nucleon polarizabilities in the quark model

> Modifications to the nucleon electric polarizability induced by pion and sigma exchange in the q-q potentials are studied by means of sum rule techniques within a non-relativistic quark model. Contributions from meson exchange interactions are found to be small and in general reduce the quark core polarizability for a number of hybrid and one-boson-exchange q-q models. These results can be explained by the constraints that the baryonic spectrum impose on the short range behavior of the mesonic interactions.

## Key claim

Modifications to the nucleon electric polarizability from pion and sigma exchange in the quark-quark potential are small and generally reduce the quark core polarizability, contrary to the expectation that meson cloud contributions would substantially increase it. The results are explained by the constraints that the baryonic spectrum imposes on the short-range behavior of the mesonic interactions, meaning the nucleon wave function is sensitive only to the regularized short-range part of the OPE potential, not its long-range tail.

## Method

The authors used sum rule techniques within a non-relativistic constituent quark model to calculate lower and upper bounds on the nucleon electric polarizability. They evaluated the energy-weighted sum rule m1(Dz) and the non-energy-weighted sum rule m0(Dz) for the dipole operator, incorporating one-pion-exchange (OPE) and one-sigma-exchange (OSE) terms into the quark-quark potential, along with the associated two-body charge densities required by current conservation. Calculations were performed for the potential model of Valcarce et al. (1993) and compared with the hybrid model of Dziembowski et al. and the OBE model of Glozman et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

- Experimental proton static polarizability: α^p = (7.0 ± 2.2 ± 1.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Experimental neutron static polarizability: α^n = (12.0 ± 1.5 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Quark core estimate (CQM including OGE only): 3.10 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ ≤ α^(p,n) ≤ 3.76 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Enhancement factor κ from OPE in the Valcarce model: κ = 0.088 (positive, producing a ~10% reduction of the lower bound)
- Final bounds including OPE and OSE two-body charge densities: 2.60 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ ≤ α^p ≤ 3.59 × 10⁻⁴ fm³; 2.43 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ ≤ α^n ≤ 3.48 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Upper bound for α^p in the Dziembowski et al. model: 4.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Upper bound for α^p in the Glozman et al. model: 1.49 × 10⁻⁴ fm³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon polarizabilities in non-relativistic quark models (Schoberl and Leeb, 1986; Traini and Leonardi, 1994; Biasioli et al., 1999) which found that quark core models cannot simultaneously reproduce the baryon spectrum and the experimental polarizability values. The authors compare their results to the nuclear case where OPE produces a positive enhancement factor K (Orlandini, 1991), showing that the nucleon case differs because the quark wave function is sensitive only to the short-range regularized part of the OPE potential. The work enables future investigation of intrinsic quark polarizabilities or explicit qqq-q̄q components in the wave function, as the meson exchange terms in the potential are insufficient to explain the experimental polarizability values.
