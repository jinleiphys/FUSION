# Nuclear forces from chiral Lagrangians using the method of unitary transformation II: The two-nucleon system

> We employ the chiral nucleon-nucleon potential derived in ref.[1] to study bound and scattering states in the two-nucleon system. At next-to-leading order, this potential is the sum of renormalized one-pion and two-pion exchange and contact interactions. At next-to-next-to-leading order, we have additional chiral two-pion exchange with low-energy constants determined from pion-nucleon scattering. Alternatively, we consider the Δ(1232) as an explicit degree of freedom in the effective field theory. The nine parameters related to the contact interactions can be determined by a fit to the np S- and P-waves and the mixing parameter ε_1 for laboratory energies below 100 MeV. The predicted phase shifts and mixing parameters for higher energies and higher angular momenta are mostly well described for energies below 300 MeV. The S-waves are described as precisely as in modern phenomenological potentials. We find a good description of the deuteron properties.

## Key claim

The chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), when regularized with a cutoff and iterated in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, describes two-nucleon phase shifts and deuteron properties with an accuracy approaching that of modern phenomenological potentials, but only for laboratory energies below 300 MeV and with the caveat that the S-waves are described as precisely as in those potentials while higher partial waves are mostly well described.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral nucleon-nucleon potential using the method of unitary transformation, producing an energy-independent potential at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and NNLO. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space with a sharp or exponential cutoff regulator, fitting nine contact-term parameters to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis for np S- and P-waves and the mixing parameter ε₁ at laboratory energies below 100 MeV. At NNLO, they either used low-energy constants from pion-nucleon scattering or included the Δ(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: fπ = 93 MeV; pion mass: Mπ = 138.03 MeV; axial-vector coupling: gA = 1.26
- Low-energy constants c₁, c₃, c₄ from subthreshold amplitudes: c₁ = −0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = −4.70 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.40 GeV⁻¹
- At NLO, the sharp cutoff Λ = 500 MeV was used; at NNLO, Λ = 875 MeV
- The ΔNπ coupling constant determined from the SU(4) relation: gΔNπ = 27.2
- Nine contact-term parameters were fitted: two for each S-wave (¹S₀ and ³S₁), one for each of four P-waves (¹P₁, ³P₁, ³P₂, ³P₀), and one for the mixing parameter ε₁
- The potential describes phase shifts for laboratory energies below 300 MeV; the fit to determine contact terms used data below 100 MeV (NLO) or below 50–100 MeV (NNLO)

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces originally proposed by Weinberg (1990s) and developed by van Kolck and collaborators (e.g., 1998), who constructed a two-nucleon potential at NNLO using time-ordered perturbation theory with 26 parameters. The present paper improves on that by using the unitary transformation method to obtain an energy-independent potential and by fitting only nine parameters, achieving results comparable to modern phenomenological potentials such as the Bonn-Jülich, Nijmegen, Argonne, or CD-Bonn models. The work enables systematic, controlled calculations of few-nucleon systems and external probes within chiral effective field theory, and it provides a foundation for extending the approach to three-nucleon forces and higher orders.
