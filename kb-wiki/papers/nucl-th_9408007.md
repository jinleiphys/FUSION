# Exotic Atom Formation in External Electric Fields

> Validity of the coalescence model of exotic atom formation in the ion collisions is studied . An estimate is given for the rates of P state production in the electric fields of the colliding ions . Possibilities of quantum beat effects are indicated . The results may be of interest in the experimental search for pionium.

## Key claim

The coalescence model of exotic atom formation is valid at high energies, but the rate of P-state atom production via direct coalescence is very unlikely; instead, P-state atoms can be formed through Stark mixing in the external electric field of colliding ions after the pair is produced in an S-wave, though this mechanism yields rather small production rates.

## Method

The authors model the formation of particle-antiparticle pairs (e.g., pionium) in the electric field of colliding nuclei using a nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation, where the pair is created at short range in an S-wave and the external Coulomb potential of the nuclei induces transitions to higher angular momentum states. They expand the wave function into discrete atomic states and derive a system of first-order differential equations for the radial amplitudes in the high-energy (MeV region) approximation, using a completeness approximation to treat the continuum via an optical potential. The system is solved numerically for up to 21 atomic states, with convergence tested for the first three states.

## Key numbers

- The 1S state of pionium decays in about 10^{-13} seconds due to strong interactions.
- The effective dipole transition potential V_{2120}(x) is given by an analytic expression: V_{2120}(x) = (-24 + (24 + 48x + 48x^2 + 32x^3 + 16x^4) e^{-2x}) / x^2.
- The scale parameter governing the energy dependence is Z/pB, where Z is the total charge of the colliding nuclei, p is the pair momentum, and B is the Bohr radius.
- Calculations were performed for positronium formed in a strong field with Z=170.
- The system of differential equations was solved for up to K=21 states, with good convergence of probabilities only for the first three states.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model of exotic atom formation (Wycech and Green, 1993) by considering a mechanism for P-state production via Stark mixing in external electric fields, which is relevant to experimental searches for pionium at facilities such as CELSIUS, CERN, COSY, and Indiana UCL. The results enable estimates of P-state formation rates and indicate quantum beat effects that may be related to correlated electron-positron pairs observed in heavy ion collisions at GSI (Schweppe et al., 1983).
