# Quarks in Finite Nuclei

> We describe the development of a theoretical description of the structure of finite nuclei based on a relativistic quark model of the structure of the bound nucleons which interact through the (self-consistent) exchange of scalar and vector mesons.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling model, which allows mesons to couple to confined quarks rather than point-like nucleons, yields an acceptable nuclear incompressibility once the coupling constants are chosen to reproduce saturation properties, representing a significant improvement over Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) at the same level of sophistication. However, the extension to finite nuclei requires the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and a phenomenological center-of-mass correction, and the initial results for \({}^{16}\)O required a 10% adjustment of the slope parameter \(a\) (above the bag model calculation) to match the experimental charge density.

## Method

The authors solved the MIT bag equations for three quarks in the instantaneous rest frame of the nucleon, with the bag radius and quark wavefunctions adjusted to minimize energy in the presence of constant scalar and vector fields at the nucleon's center. They then used first-order perturbation theory to correct for the variation of these fields across the bag, and quantized the nucleon motion either through a non-relativistic expansion or via a Dirac equation with a scalar-field-dependent coupling constant \(g_\sigma(\hat{\sigma})\). The resulting mean-field equations for the \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) mesons were solved self-consistently for the finite nucleus \({}^{16}\)O using techniques from Walecka and Serot (1992).

## Key numbers

- Free bag radius: 0.8 fm, which shrinks by about 2% at nuclear matter density.
- The scalar charge \(C(\overline{\sigma})\) decreases by between 10% and 20% from free space to normal nuclear matter density.
- The isovector nucleon magnetic moment is 4.7 nuclear magnetons, leading to an anomalous coupling ratio \(f_{\rho}/g_{\rho} = 3.7\) (the isovector anomalous magnetic moment).
- The nucleon effective mass is estimated to be of the order of 600 MeV at 2.5 times normal nuclear matter density (\(2.5\rho_0\)).
- The \(\rho\) meson effective mass is also estimated to be around 600 MeV at the same density.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model, originally proposed by Guichon (1995), from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei, addressing the limitations of earlier local-density approximation studies of the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and super-allowed Fermi \(\beta\)-decay. The model improves upon Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) as formulated by Walecka (1974) and Serot (1992) by incorporating nucleon internal structure, and it enables future quantitative studies of hypernuclei (where the \(\Lambda\) spin-orbit force is naturally suppressed), heavier nuclei, and the density dependence of vector meson masses relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
