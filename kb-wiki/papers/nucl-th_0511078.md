# Neutron structure effects in the deuteron and one neutron halos

> Although the neutron (n) does not carry a total electric charge, its charge and magnetization distributions represented in momentum space by the electromagnetic form factors, $F_1^{(n)} (q^2)$ and $F_2^{(n)} (q^2)$, lead to an electromagnetic potential of the neutron. Using this fact, we calculate the electromagnetic corrections to the binding energy, $B_d$, of the deuteron and a one neutron halo nucleus (11Be), by evaluating the neutron-proton and the neutron-charged core (10Be) potential, respectively. The correction to $B_d$ (~9 keV) is comparable to that arising due to the inclusion of the $Δ$-isobar component in the deuteron wave function. In the case of the more loosely bound halo nucleus, 11Be, the correction is close to about 2 keV.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic correction to the deuteron binding energy due to neutron structure is approximately 9 keV, which is comparable to the correction from including a Delta-isobar component in the deuteron wave function; for the more loosely bound one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be, the correction is about 2 keV, but this value depends significantly on the parameterization of the neutron form factors and on whether the nuclear form factor of the 10Be core is included.

## Method

The authors derived an electromagnetic potential between a neutron and a charged particle by starting from the Feynman diagram for neutron scattering and expanding the scattering amplitude in 1/c^2 terms, generalizing the Breit equation to include the neutron electromagnetic form factors F1 and F2. They then evaluated the perturbative correction to the binding energy by integrating this potential with the unperturbed wave functions: for the deuteron, using the Paris potential wave function parameterization, and for 11Be, using a coupled-channel wave function from a neutron-10Be core model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy correction: total of 8.81 keV using the parameterization of Walcher (2003) and Glazier (2005) for the neutron form factors
- Deuteron binding energy correction: ranges from 7.9 keV to 11.8 keV depending on the choice of parameters a and b in the Galster-type parameterization of G_E^n
- 11Be binding energy correction: 1.85 keV using the Walcher (2003) and Glazier (2005) parameterization with the 10Be nuclear form factor included
- 11Be binding energy correction: 14 keV when assuming a point-like charged 10Be core (F_10Be = 4) instead of including the nuclear form factor
- The leading term from the F1^n F1^p combination contributes -1.25 keV to the deuteron correction
- The largest single contribution to the deuteron correction comes from the F2^n F2^p spin-independent term at 6.56 keV

## Context

This work extends the study of nucleon substructure effects in nuclear binding, motivated by renewed interest in nucleon form factors from Jefferson Laboratory experiments (Jones, 2000) and advances in radioactive beam experiments on halo nuclei (Tanihata, 1985). The calculated electromagnetic corrections are comparable to the Delta-isobar contributions to the deuteron binding energy found by Holinde (1980), and the authors note that the 10 keV scale of the deuteron correction is significant compared to the 25 eV Doppler broadening uncertainty in the experimental deuteron binding energy measurement (Greene, 1996). The results enable more precise calculations of primordial nucleosynthesis yields, where the alpha-dependence of the deuteron binding energy affects the coefficient relating deuterium abundance variations to changes in the fine structure constant.
