# Transverse momentum dependence of directed particle flow at 160 AGeV

> The transverse momentum ($p_t$) dependence of hadron flow at SPS energies is studied. In particular, the nucleon and pion flow in S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV is investigated. For simulations the microscopic quark-gluon string model (QGSM) is applied. It is found that the directed flow of pions $v_1(y, Δp_t)$ changes sign from a negative slope in the low-$p_t$ region to a positive slope at $p_t \geq 0.6$ GeV/c as recently also observed experimentally. The change of the flow behaviour can be explained by early emission times for high-$p_t$ pions. We further found that a substantial amount of high-$p_t$ pions are produced in the very first primary NN collisions at the surface region of the touching nuclei. Thus, at SPS energies high-$p_t$ nucleons seem to be a better probe for the hot and dense early phase of nuclear collisions than high-$p_t$ pions. Both, in the light and in the heavy system the pion directed flow $v_1(p_t, Δy)$ exhibits large negative values when the transverse momentum approaches zero, as also seen experimentally in Pb+Pb collisions. It is found that this effect is caused by nuclear shadowing. The proton flow, in contrary, shows the typical linear increase with rising $p_t$.

## Key claim

Using the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), the authors find that the directed flow of pions at SPS energies changes sign from negative (antiflow) at low transverse momentum to positive (normal flow) at p_t >= 0.6 GeV/c, and they argue that high-p_t nucleons are a better probe of the early dense phase than high-p_t pions because a substantial fraction of high-p_t pions are produced in primary NN collisions at the nuclear surface rather than from the thermalized source.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic simulations of S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV using the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), which treats hadronic interactions via Gribov-Regge theory with string excitation, fragmentation, resonance formation, and hadron rescattering. They computed the directed flow coefficient v_1 as a function of rapidity in different transverse momentum intervals and as a function of p_t in different rapidity windows, analyzing the freeze-out times of particles to correlate emission time with transverse momentum. The results were compared to experimental data from the NA49 and WA98 Collaborations, and a thermal model fit was applied to the low-p_t proton flow.

## Key numbers

- The directed flow of pions changes sign from negative slope at low p_t to positive slope at p_t >= 0.6 GeV/c in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV.
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV, the mean number of interactions per hadron is 9; for central S+S collisions it is 2.
- Pionic freeze-out distributions peak at t approximately 8 fm/c, while nucleon distributions peak at t approximately 15 fm/c in central Pb+Pb collisions.
- The thermal fit to proton v_1(p_t) in the rapidity interval 4 < y < 5 for p_t <= 0.4 GeV/c yields parameters: beta_a = 0.1c, T = 140 MeV, and beta_0 = 0.60c for S+S and beta_0 = 0.40c for Pb+Pb collisions.
- High-p_t nucleons emitted within the first 2 fm/c constitute a small fraction of the total high-p_t nucleon yield; high-p_t pions are produced primarily in primary NN collisions.
- In Pb+Pb collisions, the nucleon directed flow shows a linear increase with p_t, while the NA49 Collaboration observes almost zero proton flow signal in the interval 0.1 <= p_t <= 0.9 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow at SPS energies, particularly the experimental observations of the NA49 Collaboration (1998) and the thermal model explanation of negative v_1 at low p_t proposed by Voloshin (1997). The QGSM results are compared to RQMD model calculations by Liu, Panitkin, and Xu (1999) and to experimental data from the E877 Collaboration at AGS and the WA98 Collaboration. The paper enables future investigations into whether the discrepancy between the QGSM prediction of a linear rise in proton v_1(p_t) and the weak signal observed by NA49 indicates new dynamical features not included in the model, and it calls for comparison with forthcoming S+S data at the same energy.
