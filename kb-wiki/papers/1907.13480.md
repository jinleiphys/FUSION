# Relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller Equation of State for White Dwarfs in presence of Magnetic Field

> The relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller treatment of compressed atoms is extended to treat magnetized matter. Each atomic configuration is confined by a Wigner-Seitz cell and is characterized by a positive electron Fermi energy which varies insignificantly with the magnetic field. In the relativistic treatment the limiting configuration is reached when the Wigner-Seitz cell radius equals the radius of the nucleus with a maximum value of the electron Fermi energy which can not be attained in presence of magnetic field due to the effect of Landau quantization of electrons within the Wigner-Seitz cell. This treatment is implemented to develop the Equation of State for magnetized White Dwarf stars in presence of Coulomb screening. The mass-radius relations for magnetized White Dwarfs are obtained by solving the general relativistic hydrostatic equilibrium equations using Schwarzschild metric description suitable for non rotating and slowly rotating stars. The explicit effects of the magnetic energy density and pressure contributed by a density-dependent magnetic field are included to find the stable configurations of magnetized Super-Chandrasekhar White Dwarfs.

# Relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller Equation of State for White Dwarfs in presence of Magnetic Field

## Key claim

The relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller treatment of compressed atoms is extended to include magnetic fields, but the positive electron Fermi energy characterizing each Wigner-Seitz cell varies only insignificantly with the magnetic field, and the limiting configuration (where the cell radius equals the nuclear radius) cannot be attained in the presence of a magnetic field due to Landau quantization effects within the cell.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller equations for compressed atoms confined in Wigner-Seitz cells, incorporating Landau quantization of electrons in a magnetic field and Coulomb screening. The Poisson equation is solved with boundary conditions ensuring charge neutrality and positive Fermi energy, yielding the electron density distribution and electrostatic potential. The resulting equation of state is then used in the general relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with the Schwarzschild metric to compute mass-radius relations for non-rotating and slowly rotating magnetized white dwarfs, including explicit contributions from magnetic energy density and pressure with a density-dependent magnetic field profile.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for onset of inverse beta decay for $^4$He: $1.368 \times 10^{11}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (at $B_D = 5B_c$)
- Critical density for onset of inverse beta decay for $^{12}$C: $3.812 \times 10^{10}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (at $B_D = 5B_c$)
- Critical density for onset of inverse beta decay for $^{16}$O: $1.785 \times 10^{10}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (at $B_D = 5B_c$)
- Critical density for onset of inverse beta decay for $^{56}$Fe: $1.139 \times 10^{9}$ g cm$^{-3}$ (at $B_D = 5B_c$)
- The critical magnetic field $B_c = m_e^2 c^3 / (e \hbar) = 4.414 \times 10^{13}$ gauss
- Maximum central magnetic field used in calculations: $10 B_c = 4.414 \times 10^{14}$ gauss
- Surface magnetic field $B_s \sim 10^9$ gauss (from observations)
- Inverse beta decay threshold energy for $^4$He: 20.596 MeV
- Inverse beta decay threshold energy for $^{12}$C: 13.370 MeV
- Inverse beta decay threshold energy for $^{16}$O: 10.419 MeV
- Inverse beta decay threshold energy for $^{56}$Fe: 3.695 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller formalism for compressed atoms at zero temperature (Roy 2011) and finite temperature (Carpio 2014) to include magnetic fields, building on prior studies of magnetized white dwarfs that used free, relativistic, Landau-quantized electron gases (Das 2012, Das 2013, Nityananda 2014, Mukhopadhyay 2017). Previous calculations using the non-relativistic Lane-Emden equation with constant magnetic fields yielded masses up to 2.3-2.6 $M_\odot$ (Das 2012), and a mass limit of 2.58 $M_\odot$ was obtained under polytropic approximations (Das 2013, Dutta 2014). The present work provides exact equations of state for helium, carbon, oxygen, and iron white dwarfs without polytropic fitting, enabling more accurate general relativistic calculations of super-Chandrasekhar white dwarf configurations that may explain overluminous Type Ia supernovae such as SN2006gz, SN2007if, SN2009dc, and SN2003fg.
