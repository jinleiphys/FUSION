# Mean Field Calculation of Thermal Properties of Simple Nucleon Matter on a Lattice

> Thermal properties of single species nucleon matter are investigated assuming a simple form of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The nucleons are placed on a cubic lattice, hopping from site to site and interacting through a spin-dependent force, as in the extended, attractive Hubbard model. A mean field calculation in the Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov approximation suggests that the superfluid ground state generated by strong nucleon pairing undergoes a second-order phase transition to a normal state as the temperature increases. The calculation is shown to lead to a promising description of the thermal properties of low-density neutron matter. A possibility of a density wave phase is also examined.

## Key claim

The mean-field Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculation on a cubic lattice suggests that single-species nucleon matter undergoes a second-order phase transition from a superfluid to a normal state as temperature increases, but the authors caution that the results are only semi-quantitative because the Hamiltonian is not fully realistic and the interaction parameter values are uncertain, making the results not quite comparable to neutron matter except perhaps at very low density.

## Method

The authors place single-species nucleons on a cubic lattice with torus boundary conditions, using a Hamiltonian that includes on-site and next-neighbor interactions (an extended, attractive Hubbard model). They apply the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field approximation to decouple the interaction terms, diagonalize the Hamiltonian via the Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation in momentum space, and solve the resulting gap equations self-consistently for the gap parameter Δ and chemical potential μ. All numerical results are computed in the thermodynamic limit (N → ∞) by replacing momentum sums with integrals over the first Brillouin zone.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 0.66 t (or 4.0 MeV) for n = 1.0 (half filling); T_c = 0.55 t (or 3.3 MeV) for n = 0.5 and n = 1.5
- Zero-temperature gap Δ(T = 0) ≈ (2 – 2.5) t ≈ 12 – 15 MeV
- Ratio Δ(T=0) ≈ 3.6 T_c, compared to the weak-coupling BCS value of 3.54
- Critical exponent β ≈ 0.45 for the gap near T_c, compared to the mean-field BCS value of 1/2
- Discontinuity in heat capacity at T_c: ΔC_v / C_v(normal) ≈ 1.74 for n = 1.0; ≈ 1.43 for n = 0.5 and n = 1.5 (the BCS mean-field value is 12/7ζ(3) ≈ 1.43)
- Lattice spacing a = 1.842 fm; hopping parameter t = 6.11 MeV; on-site interaction U = -24.74 MeV; next-neighbor interaction V = 0.89 MeV
- Density at half filling (n = 1.0): ρ = 1/a³ ≈ 0.160 fm⁻³; corresponding Fermi momentum p_F ≈ 1.68 fm⁻¹, compared to π/a ≈ 1.71 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends a previous Monte Carlo lattice calculation by Seki and collaborators (MKSV) that showed evidence of a phase transition around 15 MeV for nuclear matter but suffered from statistical fluctuations (a sign problem) at low temperatures for single-species matter. The mean-field results here serve as a reference for a more extensive Monte Carlo calculation the authors are currently carrying out. The approach is intended to enable a better understanding of thermal properties of low-density neutron matter relevant to supernova and neutron star physics, building on earlier statistical models and lattice gas models as well as traditional nuclear many-body theories such as those by Dean (2003), the Argonne group, and others.
