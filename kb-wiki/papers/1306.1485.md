# HBT interferometry relative to the triangular flow plane in heavy-ion collisions

> The PHENIX Collaboration has reported third-order harmonic oscillations of the source radius parameters when measuring the Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlation function for charged hadrons relative to the triangular flow angle. We explore possible origins of such third-order oscillations with a simple Gaussian source featuring both a triangular geometric deformation and triangular flow. Third-order oscillations of the HBT radii can arise from a purely geometric triangular deformation superimposed on an azimuthally symmetric radial flow, or from a radially symmetry spatial distribution which expands anisotropically with a triangular component in the flow velocity profile. In both cases the final particle momentum distribution features triangular flow. We show that the two alternatives can be distinguished experimentally through the phase of the azimuthal oscillations of the HBT radii relative to the triangular flow plane.

## Key claim
Third-order harmonic oscillations of HBT radii can arise either from a purely geometric triangular deformation of the source combined with azimuthally symmetric radial flow, or from a radially symmetric spatial distribution expanding with a triangular component in the flow velocity profile; these two alternatives can be distinguished experimentally through the phase of the azimuthal oscillations of the HBT radii relative to the triangular flow plane.

## Method
The authors constructed a simple Gaussian source model with a triangular geometric deformation and triangular flow, generalizing the hydrodynamically expanding Gaussian source model used in prior work (Csorgo 1994, Chapman 1994, Wiedemann 1997, Heinz 2002, Retiere 2003). They performed a double Fourier expansion of the emission function in both the spatial angle and the momentum angle relative to the triangular flow plane, then derived analytic expressions for the Fourier coefficients of the HBT radius oscillations. The model was used to explore the origin of third-order harmonic oscillations of the sideward and outward HBT radii relative to the triangular flow angle.

## Key numbers
- Mean emission time: tau_f = 10 fm/c, emission duration: Delta_tau = 1 fm/c
- Gaussian source radius at freeze-out: R = 4.5 fm
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_0 = 120 MeV
- Average transverse flow rapidity: eta_f = 0.6 at r = R
- Width of freeze-out space-time rapidity distribution: Delta_eta = 2.3
- Pion mass: m_pi = 139 MeV
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at 0-10% centrality

## Context
This work extends the theoretical formalism of azimuthally sensitive HBT interferometry to higher-order harmonic oscillations, building on prior studies of elliptic flow HBT oscillations (Lisa 2000, Heinz 2002) which showed that first and second order harmonic oscillations are dominated by geometric deformations. The study was motivated by the PHENIX Collaboration's report (Niida 2013) of third-order harmonic oscillations of HBT radii relative to the triangular flow plane in central Au+Au collisions. The authors show that, unlike the elliptic case where a simple relation between the oscillation amplitude and final source ellipticity exists, no such relation can hold for triangular freeze-out eccentricity, and they demonstrate how experimental data can distinguish between geometric and flow-driven origins of the triangular oscillations.
