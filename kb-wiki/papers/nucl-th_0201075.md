# 9Be: A gateway nucleus into heavier nuclei?

> Level structure of 9Be is investigated calculating poles of the alpha alphar n three-body scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane. We find 10 levels, which make rotational bands in both positive and negative parities. All of them, except 7/2^+, have been observed experimentally and the corresponding positions and widths reasonably well matched. The 7/2^+ has a large width, and therefore has not been found, but may cause a noise in the experiment. No other levels are needed to explain the existing experimental observables below 11.28 MeV, where the 9th excited state exists. We definitely assign it 9/2^-.

## Key claim

The paper claims that all experimentally observed levels of 9Be below 11.28 MeV can be explained within an alpha-alpha-neutron three-body model, and that no additional "second levels" of low spin states are needed. The 7/2+ state, which has not been observed experimentally, is predicted to have a very large width (6.32 MeV) and may appear only as a broad background noise rather than a distinct resonance.

## Method

The author solved homogeneous Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the alpha-alpha-neutron three-body system, searching for poles of the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane using contour deformation methods. Separable potentials were used for the alpha-alpha interaction (Ali-Bodmer, Chien-Brown, and UIM potentials) and Yamaguchi-type separable potentials for the alpha-neutron interaction. The Coulomb interaction between alpha particles was neglected and treated as a constant energy shift of approximately 1.8 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 9Be calculated with UIM potential: -3.32 MeV; after adding 1.8 MeV Coulomb shift, agrees with experiment
- 5/2- level: excitation energy 2.33 MeV, width less than 10^-3 MeV (consistent with experimental width 0.00077 MeV)
- 1/2- level: excitation energy 2.82 MeV, width 0.50 MeV (experimental: 2.780 MeV, 1.08 MeV)
- 5/2+ level: excitation energy 3.91 MeV, width 0.35 MeV (experimental: 3.049 MeV, 0.282 MeV)
- 3/2+ level: excitation energy 4.95 MeV, width 1.95 MeV (experimental: 4.704 MeV, 0.743 MeV)
- 7/2- level: excitation energy 6.68 MeV, width 1.98 MeV (experimental from NM: 6.38 MeV, 1.21 MeV)
- 9/2+ level: excitation energy 6.65 MeV, width 2.46 MeV (experimental from NM: 6.76 MeV, 1.33 MeV)
- 7/2+ level: excitation energy 8.13 MeV, width 6.32 MeV (not observed experimentally)
- 9/2- level: excitation energy 10.36 MeV, width 1.14 MeV (identified with experimental 11.28 MeV resonance)

## Context

This work extends the three-body Faddeev approach previously applied to the six-nucleon systems (6He and 6Li) by Lehman and Rajan (1982) and Eskandarian and Afnan (1992) to the nine-nucleon system. It directly addresses the experimental contradictions between the standard compilation by Ajzenberg-Selove (1988) and new measurements by Dixit et al. (1991) and Glickman et al. (1991) regarding levels around 6.7 MeV. The most successful prior theoretical work by Arai et al. (1996) used a microscopic three-body model with complex scaling but did not resolve the confusion about higher-lying states. The paper enables a systematic understanding of 9Be resonances as three-body threshold effects and provides a foundation for subsequent work on 9LambdaBe hypernuclei.
