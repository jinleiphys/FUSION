# Dawning of the N=32 shell closure seen through precision mass measurements of neutron-rich titanium isotopes

> A precision mass investigation of the neutron-rich titanium isotopes $^{51-55}$Ti was performed at TRIUMF's Ion Trap for Atomic and Nuclear science (TITAN). The range of the measurements covers the $N=32$ shell closure and the overall uncertainties of the $^{52-55}$Ti mass values were significantly reduced. Our results confirm the existence of a weak shell effect at $N=32$, establishing the abrupt onset of this shell closure. Our data were compared with state-of-the-art \textit{ab-initio} shell model calculations which, despite very successfully describing where the $N=32$ shell gap is strong, overpredict its strength and extent in titanium and heavier isotones. These measurements also represent the first scientific results of TITAN using the newly commissioned Multiple-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Spectrometer (MR-TOF-MS), substantiated by independent measurements from TITAN's Penning trap mass spectrometer.

## Key claim

Precision mass measurements of neutron-rich titanium isotopes 51-55 conclusively establish the existence of a weak shell effect at N=32 in the titanium chain, ruling out a no-shell hypothesis by over 50 sigma, but state-of-the-art ab initio shell model calculations overpredict the strength and extent of this shell closure in titanium and heavier isotones.

## Method

Mass measurements of 51-55Ti were performed at TRIUMF's Ion Trap for Atomic and Nuclear science (TITAN) using the newly commissioned Multiple-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Spectrometer (MR-TOF-MS), with independent validation from TITAN's Penning trap mass spectrometer (MPET) for 51-53Ti and the calibrant isotopes 51V and 52-54Cr. The neutron-rich titanium isotopes were produced via spallation reactions using a 480 MeV proton beam on a tantalum target, selectively ionized with TRIUMF's Laser Ionization Source (TRILIS), and identified by comparing spectra with lasers on and off.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 54Ti measured as -45,744 (16) keV/c^2, with the empirical neutron-shell gap at 54Ti changing from 2.45(17) MeV to 2.70(12) MeV
- Mass excess of 55Ti measured as -41,832 (29) keV/c^2
- Mass excess of 51Ti measured as -49,731.3 (2.1) keV/c^2 (TITAN combined value)
- Mass excess of 52Ti measured as -49,478.7 (3.0) keV/c^2 (TITAN combined value)
- Mass excess of 53Ti measured as -46,881.3 (2.9) keV/c^2 (TITAN combined value)
- MR-TOF-MS achieved mass resolving power of approximately 220,000 with peak width of about 17 ns after times-of-flight of about 7.4 ms
- Systematic uncertainty of MR-TOF-MS evaluated to 3 x 10^-7
- MPET systematic effects evaluated to smaller than 1.5 x 10^-8 among the masses of interest

## Context

This work extends precision mass measurements of the N=32 shell closure from the potassium (Rosenbusch 2015), calcium (Gallant 2012, Wienholtz 2013), and scandium (Xu 2015) isotopic chains into the titanium chain, where previous data had uncertainties of hundreds of keV. The results confirm that titanium sits at a transition point between vanadium (which shows no N=32 shell signature) and the strong shell closure seen in scandium and calcium, enabling improved understanding of shell evolution and providing benchmarks for ab initio calculations of nuclear structure in this region.
