# J/Ψproduction, $χ$ polarization and Color Fluctuations

> The hard contributions to the heavy quarkonium-nucleon cross sections are calculated based on the QCD factorization theorem and the nonrelativistic quarkonium model. We evaluate the nonperturbative part of these cross sections which dominates at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 20$ GeV at the Cern Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and becomes a correction at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 6$ TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). $\J$ production at the CERN SPS is well described by hard QCD, when the larger absorption cross sections of the $χ$ states predicted by QCD are taken into account. We predict an $A$-dependent polarization of the $χ$ states. The expansion of small wave packets is discussed.

# J/Ψ production, χ polarization and Color Fluctuations

## Key claim

The observed suppression of J/Ψ in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS energies can be explained by hard QCD when the larger absorption cross sections of the χ states predicted by QCD are taken into account, rather than by the "preresonance" hypothesis or other QCD-motivated models that do not use the QCD factorization theorem.

## Method

The authors calculate hard contributions to heavy quarkonium-nucleon cross sections using the QCD factorization theorem and a nonrelativistic quarkonium model with a Cornell confining potential. They evaluate the nonperturbative QCD contribution by interpolating between known cross sections using a parametrization based on the transverse size of the quark-gluon configuration, with reference points from the φ-N, π-N, and open charm meson cross sections. The suppression factors for charmonium states in nuclear matter are computed using a semiclassical Glauber-type approximation, including formation length effects for the evolution of small wave packets.

## Key numbers

- The calculated J/Ψ-nucleon cross section is 3.6 mb, in agreement with SLAC data of 3.5 ± 0.8 mb at incident energy ~20 GeV.
- The nonperturbative part of the charmonium-nucleon cross section dominates at √sNN ≈ 20 GeV (SPS) and becomes a correction at √sNN ≈ 6 TeV (LHC).
- The formation length of J/Ψ at SPS energies (Elab = 200 AGeV) is lf ≈ 3 fm; for Ψ' it is lf ≈ 6 fm.
- The ratio of directly produced J/Ψ to those from Ψ' decays in pp collisions is J/Ψ : Ψ' = 0.92 : 0.08.
- The fraction of J/Ψ from χ decays is taken as 0.4, and the fraction from direct production plus Ψ' decays is 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization approach to charmonium-nucleon interactions, contrasting with earlier QCD-motivated models by Kharzeev (1996) and Kopeliovich (1995) that did not use the factorization theorem. The authors explain J/Ψ suppression observed in AB collisions as a consequence of large absorption of χ states, rather than the "preresonance" hypothesis suggested by Kharzeev (1996). The paper predicts a new QCD effect: A-dependent polarization of χ states due to longitudinally directed nuclear color filtering, which could be used to measure interactions of charmonium states with comoving hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The Υ-nucleon interaction cross section is calculated for the first time in this paper.
