# Pairing correlations in exotic nuclei

> The BCS and HFB theories which can accommodate the pairing correlations in the ground states of atomic nuclei are presented. As an application of the pairing theories, we investigate the spatial extension of weakly bound Ne and C isotopes by taking into account the pairing correlation with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method and a 3-body model, respectively. We show that the odd-even staggering in the reaction cross sections of $^{30,31,32}$Ne and $^{14,15,16}$C are successfully reproduced, and thus the staggering can be attributed to the unique role of pairing correlations in nuclei far from the stability line. A correlation between a one-neutron separation energy and the anti-halo effect is demonstrated for $s$- and p-waves using the HFB wave functions. We also propose effective density-dependent pairing interactions which reproduce both the neutron-neutron ($nn$) scattering length at zero density and the neutron pairing gap in uniform matter. Then, we apply these interactions to study pairing gaps in semi-magic finite nuclei, such as Ca, Ni, Sn and Pb isotopic chains.

# Pairing correlations in exotic nuclei

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering observed in reaction cross sections of neutron-rich Ne and C isotopes can be attributed to the pairing anti-halo effect, but this interpretation depends on the valence neutron occupying a low-angular-momentum orbital (2p₃/₂ for ³¹Ne or 2s₁/₂ for ¹⁵C) with a small separation energy; the staggering is only reproduced when the separation energy is small (e.g., Sₙ = 0.32 MeV for ³¹Ne) and not when it is large (Sₙ = 1.49 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction of surface type, adjusted to reproduce the empirical neutron pairing gap for ³⁰Ne. Reaction cross sections were then computed using the Glauber theory with the resulting HF and HFB densities, employing a two-dimensional Fourier transform technique for the phase-shift function. For ¹⁶C, a three-body model was used instead of HFB.

## Key numbers

- The mean square radius of the 2s₁/₂ wave function diverges as 1/|ε| in the HF limit |ε| → 0, while the HFB wave function radius remains finite, approaching ħ²/(2mΔ) where Δ is the pairing gap.
- For ³¹Ne, reaction cross sections were calculated with two assumed separation energies: Sₙ = 0.32 MeV and Sₙ = 1.49 MeV; clear odd-even staggering matching experiment was seen only for the smaller value.
- The empirical separation energy of ³¹Ne is Sₙ = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV.
- For the C isotopes, the rms radii were estimated as 2.53 fm for ¹⁴C, 2.90 fm for ¹⁵C, and 2.81 fm for ¹⁶C.
- The Woods-Saxon potential for ³¹Ne used a diffuseness parameter a = 0.75 fm.
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter used for the pairing interaction is ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the pairing anti-halo effect originally discussed by Bennaceur (2000) and applies it to explain experimental odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections measured by Takechi (2010) for Ne isotopes and by Fang (2004) for C isotopes. The authors contrast their pairing-based explanation with deformation calculations by Minomo (2011) which found large deformation (β₂ ≈ 0.42) in ³¹Ne but no significant odd-even staggering. The proposed density-dependent pairing interactions build on the work of Margueron (2007) and are tested against empirical pairing gaps in Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains, showing that an isovector term (IS+IV Bare) is necessary to reproduce the neutron number dependence of pairing gaps in neutron-rich nuclei.
