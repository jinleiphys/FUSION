# Phenomenological impact of the resummation of logs of alpha in heavy quarkonium

> Here we would like to review recent progress on the resummation on logarithms of $α_s$ in heavy quarkonium. We will mainly focus on the phenomenological relevance of these achievements. Determinations of the $η_b(1S)$ mass, $B_c(1S)$ hyperfine splitting, inclusive electromagnetic decays and implications for $t$-$\bar t$ production near threshold.

# Phenomenological impact of the resummation of logs of alpha in heavy quarkonium

## Key claim
The resummation of large logarithms of the heavy quark velocity in the non-relativistic renormalization group (NRG) framework of pNRQCD yields improved perturbative predictions for heavy quarkonium observables, but the convergence of the logarithmic expansion is not uniform across all observables: it works well for hyperfine splittings and spin ratios, while the spin-independent part of production/annihilation rates shows slower convergence and possible sensitivity to ultrasoft contributions and renormalon effects.

## Method
The author reviews the application of the non-relativistic renormalization group (NRG) within potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) to resum logarithms of the strong coupling constant alpha_s that arise from the hierarchy of scales m (hard), mv (soft), and mv^2 (ultrasoft) in heavy quarkonium. The analysis compares fixed-order (LO, NLO, NNLO) with logarithmic-ordered (LL, NLL, NNLL) approximations for hyperfine splittings and electromagnetic decay/production ratios, using the scale dependence of the results to assess convergence and theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers
- Predicted mass of eta_b(1S): 9421 +/- 11 (th) +9/-8 (delta_alpha_s) MeV, using the experimental Upsilon(1S) mass as input.
- Hyperfine splitting of bottomonium 1S ground state: E_hfs = 39 +/- 8 MeV (NLL result at local maximum).
- Hyperfine splitting of B_c(1S): M(B_c*) - M(B_c) = 65 +/- 24 (th) +19/-16 (delta_alpha_s) MeV.
- Predicted Gamma(eta_b(1S) -> gamma gamma) = 0.659 +/- 0.089 (th) +0.019/-0.018 (alpha_s) +/- 0.015 (exp) keV, using the experimental Gamma(Upsilon(1S) -> e+e-) = 1.314 +/- 0.029 keV.
- Ratio of cross sections for toponium: sigma_res(e+e- -> t tbar) / sigma_res(gamma gamma -> t tbar) = (1/(3 Q_t^2)) (1 - 0.132 - 0.018) at scale nu = 20.2 GeV.
- Ratio of decay rates for bottomonium: Gamma(Upsilon(1S) -> e+e-) / Gamma(eta_b(1S) -> gamma gamma) = (1/(3 Q_b^2)) (1 - 0.302 - 0.111) at scale nu = 1.295 GeV.
- Ratio of decay rates for charmonium: Gamma(J/Psi(1S) -> e+e-) / Gamma(eta_c(1S) -> gamma gamma) = (1/(3 Q_c^2)) (1 - 0.513 - 0.326) at scale nu = 0.645 GeV.
- Hyperfine splitting ratio for bottomonium excited states: E_hfs(2S)/E_hfs(1S) = 0.25.
- Hyperfine splitting ratio for charmonium excited states: E_hfs(2S)/E_hfs(1S) = 0.37 (perturbative estimate), compared to experimental 0.41 +/- 0.03.

## Context
This review extends the program of logarithmic resummation in pNRQCD pioneered by the author and collaborators (Kniehl, Penin, Smirnov, Steinhauser) to phenomenological applications. The hyperfine splitting results for bottomonium (KPPSS, 2004) and B_c (Penin, 2004) provide predictions that can be compared with lattice calculations (Eichenberger, 2004), potential models (Godfrey and Rosner, 2002), and sum rules (Narison, 2004). The charmonium hyperfine splitting analysis shows that NRG-improved perturbation theory brings the prediction closer to the experimental value of 117.7 +/- 1.3 MeV, where unquenched lattice results undershoot by about 20% (Pierro, 2003). The work on production/annihilation rates (Penin, 2004) provides NNLL results for spin ratios and highlights the need for complete NNLL calculations for top-antitop threshold production at a future linear collider, where the top mass and Higgs-top coupling determinations are at stake (Martinez and Miquel, 2003). The analysis also questions the accuracy of existing alpha_s determinations from Upsilon decay ratios, suggesting the theoretical uncertainty should be increased by a factor of two.
