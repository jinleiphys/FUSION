# Adding crust to BPS Skyrme neutron stars

> The Skyrme model and its generalisations provide a conceptually appealing field-theory basis for the description of nuclear matter and, after its coupling to gravity, also of neutron stars. In particular, a specific Skyrme submodel, the so-called Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) Skyrme model, allows both for an exact field-theoretic and a mean-field treatment of neutron stars, as a consequence of its perfect fluid property. A pure BPS Skyrme model description of neutron stars, however, only describes the neutron star core, by construction. Here we consider different possibilities to extrapolate a BPS Skyrme neutron star at high baryon density to a description valid at lower densities. In the exact field-theoretic case, a simple effective description of the neutron star crust can be used, because the exact BPS Skyrme neutron star solutions formally extend to sufficiently low densities. In the mean-field case, on the other hand, the BPS Skyrme neutron star solutions always remain above the nuclear saturation density and, therefore, must be joined to a different nuclear physics equation of state already for the outer core. We study the resulting neutron stars in both cases, facilitating an even more complete comparison between Skyrmionic neutron stars and neutron stars obtained from other approaches, as well as with observations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that BPS Skyrme neutron stars, which by construction only describe the high-density core, can be completed at lower densities using two distinct approaches: an effective crust description for exact field-theoretic solutions (which formally extend to low densities but become unreliable), and a matching to a standard nuclear physics equation of state for mean-field solutions (which remain above nuclear saturation density).

## Method

The authors apply the effective crust description of Zdunik, Fortin and Haensel (2017) to exact BPS Skyrme neutron star solutions, using the core-crust transition chemical potential of 955 MeV and the surface chemical potential of iron (approximately 930 MeV) to determine crust properties. For the mean-field case, they join the BPS Skyrme equation of state at a density above saturation to the BCPM equation of state based on Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body calculations from Vinas et al. (2017), which describes both the outer core and crust. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved for both approaches to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.160 fm^-3
* Energy per nucleon of infinite nuclear matter: E_inm = 922.9 MeV (nucleon mass 938.9 MeV minus binding energy 16.0 MeV per nucleon)
* Chemical potential at core-crust interface: mu_cc = 955 MeV, giving ratio mu_cc/mu_0 = 1.027
* Surface chemical potential (energy per baryon of iron): mu_0 ~ 930 MeV
* Core-crust transition density: n_cc ~ (1/2) n0
* Newton's constant: G_N = 1.322 x 10^-42 fm MeV^-1
* Maximum neutron star mass from standard Skyrme model: M_max > 2 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the BPS Skyrme model neutron star studies of Adam et al. (2015) and Adam et al. (2016) by adding low-density completions that were previously absent. The effective crust description follows the method of Zdunik, Fortin and Haensel (2017), while the mean-field matching uses the BCPM equation of state from Vinas et al. (2017). The paper enables more complete comparisons between Skyrmionic neutron stars and both neutron stars from other approaches (such as relativistic mean field theories like the Walecka model) and observational constraints, particularly for the maximum mass exceeding 2 solar masses that recent observations favor.
