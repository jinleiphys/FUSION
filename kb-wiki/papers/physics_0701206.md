# Theory of nuclear excitation by electron capture for heavy ions

> We investigate the resonant process of nuclear excitation by electron capture, in which a continuum electron is captured into a bound state of an ion with the simultaneous excitation of the nucleus. In order to derive the cross section a Feshbach projection operator formalism is introduced. Nuclear states and transitions are described by a nuclear collective model and making use of experimental data. Transition rates and total cross sections for NEEC followed by the radiative decay of the excited nucleus are calculated for various heavy ion collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives a theoretical formalism for nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC) using a Feshbach projection operator approach, but the cross sections and resonance strengths calculated depend on the specific nuclear collective model chosen and on experimental nuclear data, meaning the results are model-dependent and subject to the uncertainties of the input nuclear transition probabilities.

## Method

The authors extended a Feshbach projection operator formalism, originally developed for dielectronic recombination, to treat the coupled electron-nucleus system in NEEC. They described nuclear states using a collective vibrational model and electronic states with relativistic Dirac wave functions, then calculated transition rates for electric (E2) and magnetic (M1) multipole transitions by evaluating the matrix elements of the Coulomb and magnetic electron-nucleus interaction Hamiltonians. The total cross section for NEEC followed by radiative nuclear decay was obtained by summing over magnetic substates and integrating over photon emission angles, with the resonance strength expressed in terms of the electron capture rate, the radiative transition rate, and the total width of the intermediate state.

- For magnetic M1 transitions, the NEEC rate formula (Eq. 16) contains the reduced magnetic transition probability \( B\uparrow(ML, I_i \to I_d) \) and a sum over electronic matrix elements \( T^+_{di,\nu}, T^0_{di,\nu}, T^-_{di,\nu} \).
- The natural width \( \Gamma_d \) of the nuclear excited state is of the order of \( 10^{-6} \) eV.
- The resonance strength is given by \( S_d = \frac{2\pi^2}{p^2} \frac{A_r^{d \to f} Y_n^{i \to d}}{\Gamma_d} \), where \( p \) is the electron momentum, \( A_r^{d \to f} \) is the radiative transition rate, and \( Y_n^{i \to d} \) is the electron capture rate.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of NEEC beyond earlier scaling approaches used by Cue (1989) and Kimball (1991) by providing a fully quantum-mechanical derivation using Feshbach projectors and relativistic electronic wave functions. It also addresses discrepancies between previous non-relativistic calculations by Kimball (1991) and earlier estimates by Cue (1989) and Goldanskii (1981). The formalism enables future experimental searches for NEEC in heavy-ion storage rings or electron-beam ion traps, as suggested by Schippers (2004) and Wolf (2005), and could help determine nuclear transition energies and study atomic vacancy effects on nuclear lifetimes.
