# Extrapolation to infinite model space of no-core shell model calculations using machine learning

> An ensemble of neural networks is employed to extrapolate no-core shell model (NCSM) results to infinite model space for light nuclei. We present a review of our neural network extrapolations of the NCSM results obtained with the Daejeon16 NN interaction in different model spaces and with different values of the NCSM basis parameter $\hbarΩ$ for energies of nuclear states and root-mean-square (rms) radii of proton, neutron and matter distributions in light nuclei. The method yields convergent predictions with quantifiable uncertainties. Ground-state energies for $^{6}$Li, $^{6}$He, and the unbound $^{6}$Be, as well as the excited $(3^{+},0)$ and $(0^{+},1)$ states of $^{6}$Li, are obtained within a few hundred keV of experiment. The extrapolated radii of bound states converge well. In contrast, radii of unbound states in $^{6}$Be and $^{6}$Li do not stabilize.

# Extrapolation to infinite model space of no-core shell model calculations using machine learning

## Key claim

An ensemble of neural networks can extrapolate no-core shell model (NCSM) results to infinite model space for light nuclei, yielding energies within a few hundred keV of experiment for bound and narrow resonant states, but the method fails to produce convergent radius extrapolations for unbound states such as the ground state of $^{6}$Be and the $(3^{+},0)$ state of $^{6}$Li.

## Method

The authors employ an ensemble of 1024 fully connected feedforward neural networks with a 2-10-10-10-1 architecture, taking $N_{\max}$ and $\hbar\Omega$ as inputs and outputting energies or root-mean-square radii. Networks are trained on NCSM calculations using the Daejeon16 $NN$ interaction in model spaces up to $N_{\max}=18$, with data selection following the variational minimum for energies and a practical $\hbar\Omega$ cutoff of 12.5 to 40 MeV for radii. A multi-stage filtering process enforces a soft variational principle, $\hbar\Omega$-stability at a final model space of $N_{\max}^{f}=300$, and convergence criteria; the median of the surviving ensemble distribution is taken as the final estimate with the interquartile range as uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{6}$Li: $-32.036(3)$ MeV, compared to experimental $-31.995$ MeV (overbinding of 41 keV)
- Ground-state energy of $^{6}$He: $-29.429^{+0.007}_{-0.005}$ MeV, compared to experimental $-29.269(10)$ MeV (overbinding of 160 keV)
- Ground-state energy of $^{6}$Be: $-27.18^{+0.02}_{-0.01}$ MeV, compared to experimental $-26.924(5)$ MeV (overbinding of 256 keV)
- Excited state $(3^{+},0)$ of $^{6}$Li energy: $-30.129^{+0.004}_{-0.003}$ MeV, compared to experimental $-29.809(2)$ MeV (overbinding of 320 keV)
- Excited state $(0^{+},1)$ of $^{6}$Li energy: $-28.552^{+0.008}_{-0.005}$ MeV, compared to experimental $-28.434(1)$ MeV (overbinding of 118 keV)
- Predicted excitation energies of $(3^{+},0)$ and $(0^{+},1)$ states of $^{6}$Li: 1.907 MeV and 3.484 MeV, respectively (experimental: 2.186 MeV and 3.563 MeV)
- Largest relative energy uncertainty at $N_{\max}^{u}=18$ is for $^{6}$Be and does not exceed approximately 0.01%
- Point-proton radius $r_p$ of $^{6}$Li ground state: 2.51(2) fm (experimental: 2.38(3) fm)
- Point-proton radius $r_p$ of $^{6}$He ground state: 1.930(2) fm (experimental: 1.925(12) fm)
- Matter radius $r_m$ of $^{6}$He ground state: $2.500^{+0.03}_{-0.04}$ fm (experimental: 2.50(5) fm)
- Relative uncertainty of radius extrapolations for bound states is of order 1% at $N_{\max}^{u}=18$

## Context

This work extends the neural network extrapolation approach first investigated by Negoita et al. (2018, 2019) (the ISU approach) and later developed by the TUDa group (Knoll et al., 2023; Wolfgruber et al., 2024), using a more sophisticated network topology, careful training dataset construction, and strict network selection criteria. The method is applied to NCSM calculations with the Daejeon16 $NN$ interaction (Shirokov et al., 2016) and compared against the phenomenological Extrapolation B (Maris et al., 2009) and the Twisted Tape Extrapolation method (Rodkin et al., 2022). The results demonstrate that machine learning extrapolations can reliably predict energies of both bound and narrow resonant states (widths up to 100 keV), while radius extrapolations converge only for bound states, enabling systematic uncertainty quantification for ab initio nuclear structure calculations.
