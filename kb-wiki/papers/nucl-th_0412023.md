# Suppression of modes in the random phase approximation

> A general but simple method is proposed to eliminate the quantum fluctuations generated by selected one-body operators in the excitation spectrum of a discrete RPA Hamiltonian. This method provides an outstanding tool for the removal of the contaminating spurious effects originated from symmetry violations. It can be also applied as a mode filter for analysising RPA response functions.

# Suppression of modes in the random phase approximation

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to eliminate quantum fluctuations generated by selected one-body operators in the RPA excitation spectrum by adding an oscillator-like restoring force term κF² to the RPA Hamiltonian; in the limit κ → ∞, the fluctuations of the chosen operator F become completely suppressed, but the method works in practice for any finite κ sufficiently large to achieve convergence.

## Method

The method adds a supplementary term κ Σᵣ [P(r)² + Q(r)²] to the bosonic RPA Hamiltonian H(b,b⁺), where P(r) and Q(r) are selected momentum-like and coordinate-like one-body operators. By scaling the Hamiltonian as H'' = H'/κ and taking the limit κ → ∞, the original Hamiltonian term vanishes asymptotically, leaving only the supplementary term. This separates the eigenvalue spectrum into three parts: exactly zero eigenvalues, eigenvalues shifted to infinity (both from the supplementary term), and the normal vibrational spectrum cleaned of the selected modes, with the result that all vibrational transition amplitudes for the selected operators vanish.

## Key numbers

- The stepwise variation of κ up to κₘₐₓ = 10⁴ leads to rapid convergence of vanishing fluctuations together with converging RPA eigenvalues, tested for random RPA matrices up to dimension n = 1000.
- The method is illustrated for a nuclear system with equal proton and neutron numbers N_π = N_ν = 20, using a spherical Nilsson potential with dipole plus octupole residual interaction.
- A second illustration shows mode suppression effects in a schematic RPA calculation of the M1 response function for the nucleus ¹⁵⁶Gd, using a rotating axially deformed Nilsson plus pairing potential with spin plus quadrupole residual interaction.

## Context

This work extends the known idea of pushing out unwanted excitation modes, previously proposed for eliminating center-of-mass motion to restore Galilean invariance (Thouless, 1961) and for rotational spurious motion (Marshalek, 1977), but which had not been pursued consistently. The method is exact compared to approximate symmetry restoration approaches (Pjatov, 1982) that cannot satisfy the complete suppression condition. It enables new applications including defining clean body-fixed or angular-momentum-fixed reference systems for rotating deformed nuclei, performing RPA calculations based on two-quasiparticle excited states, and using simplified RPA with Strutinsky shell correction mean fields.
