# Two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for chiral and wobbling modes

> A two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (2DCH) on both azimuth and polar motions in triaxial nuclei is proposed to investigate the chiral and wobbling modes. In the 2DCH, the collective potential and the mass parameters are determined from three-dimensional tilted axis cranking (TAC) calculations. The broken chiral and signature symmetries in the TAC solutions are restored by the 2DCH. The validity of the 2DCH is illustrated with a triaxial rotor ($γ=-30^\circ$) coupling to one $h_{11/2}$ proton particle and one $h_{11/2}$ neutron hole. By diagonalizing the 2DCH, the angular momenta and energy spectra are obtained. These results agree with the exact solutions of the particle rotor model (PRM) at high rotational frequencies. However, at low frequencies, the energies given by the 2DCH are larger than those by the PRM due to the underestimation of the mass parameters. In addition, with increasing angular momentum, the transitions from the chiral vibration to chiral rotation and further to longitudinal wobbling motion have been presented in the 2DCH.

## Key claim

The two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (2DCH) restores the chiral and signature symmetries broken in tilted axis cranking (TAC) solutions and reproduces particle rotor model (PRM) results at high rotational frequencies, but at low frequencies the 2DCH energies are larger than PRM energies because the mass parameters are underestimated when the vibrational frequency is neglected.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian using the azimuth angle φ and polar angle θ as collective coordinates for a triaxial rotor (γ = -30°) coupled to one h₁₁/₂ proton particle and one h₁₁/₂ neutron hole. The collective potential V(θ,φ) was obtained from three-dimensional tilted axis cranking (TAC) calculations of the total Routhian surface, and the mass parameters Bθθ, Bθφ, Bφθ, and Bφφ were calculated using a cranking formula that neglects residual interactions. The quantized 2DCH was diagonalized in a Fourier basis with four symmetry subspaces defined by parity under φ → -φ and θ → π-θ transformations.

## Key numbers

- Coupling parameters: Cπ = 0.25 MeV for the proton particle, Cν = -0.25 MeV for the neutron hole
- Moment of inertia parameter: J₀ = 40 ħ²/MeV
- Triaxial deformation parameter: γ = -30°
- Rotational frequencies studied: ħω = 0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50, 0.70, and 0.90 MeV
- The minima in the potential energy surfaces change from φ = 0° to φ ≠ 0° at ħω ≈ 0.20 MeV, and to θ = 90°, φ = ±90° at ħω ≈ 0.70 MeV
- Mass parameters Bθθ and Bφφ at the potential minimum change by about two orders of magnitude from ħω = 0.10 MeV to ħω = 0.90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous one-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (1DCH) of Chen et al. (2013) that only considered φ as a dynamical variable, by including both θ and φ as collective coordinates. The 2DCH approach goes beyond the TAC mean-field approximation and the TAC+RPA method (Mukhopadhyay 2007, Almehed 2011), which is restricted to small-amplitude motions. The method enables the description of transitions from chiral vibration to chiral rotation and further to longitudinal wobbling motion with increasing angular momentum, and naturally classifies wobbling modes into φ-wobbling and θ-wobbling analogous to β and γ bands in the Bohr Hamiltonian.
