# Lattice calculation of hadronic tensor of the nucleon

> We report an attempt to calculate the deep inelastic scattering structure functions from the hadronic tensor calculated on the lattice. We used the Backus-Gilbert reconstruction method to address the inverse Laplace transformation for the analytic continuation from the Euclidean to the Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Backus-Gilbert reconstruction method can extract the Minkowski-space hadronic tensor from Euclidean lattice data via an inverse Laplace transform, but their preliminary lattice setup does not yet reach the physical deep inelastic scattering region because the kinematics yield negative $Q^2$ values.

## Method

The Euclidean hadronic tensor $\tilde{W}_{11}(\vec{p},\vec{q},\tau)$ was computed on a $12^3\times 128$ anisotropic clover lattice using four-point and two-point correlation functions in the path-integral formalism, focusing on the connected sea anti-parton and valence plus connected sea diagrams. The Backus-Gilbert method was applied to perform the inverse Laplace transform from Euclidean time $\tau$ to Minkowski energy $\nu$, using a linear combination of kernel functions to approximate a delta function and reconstruct the spectral density. Toy model tests with known spectral peaks and a continuum spectrum validated the reconstruction before application to lattice data.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: $12^3\times 128$ with spatial lattice spacing $a_s = 0.1785(53)$ fm and anisotropy $\xi = 5$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi \sim 640$ MeV
- Number of configurations: 500
- Toy model test: three masses $m_i = 0.2, 0.6, 1.2$ with amplitudes $A_i = 20, 40, 60$; reconstructed amplitudes were $21, 39, 61$ respectively
- Toy model with continuum spectrum: signal-to-noise ratio of 100; SVD cutoff of $10^{-6}$; reconstructed continuum density value of $50$ compared to expected $A/\delta\nu = 50$
- For the $\vec{p}=0$ case, the quasi-elastic peak tapers off at $\nu \sim 1$ (approximately 5.5 GeV on this lattice), while $|\vec{q}| = 3$ corresponds to 1.7 GeV, yielding $Q^2 < 0$

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean path-integral formulation of the hadronic tensor developed by Liu (1993, 1998, 1999) and the extended evolution equations for connected and disconnected sea partons from Liu (2017). It applies the Backus-Gilbert reconstruction method, suggested by Hansen (2017), as an alternative to the maximum entropy method of Asakawa (2000) for the inverse Laplace transform. The results enable future calculations on finer lattices (e.g., JLab clover lattices) with larger momentum transfers to access the physical DIS region with $x$ values from 0.07 to 0.45.
