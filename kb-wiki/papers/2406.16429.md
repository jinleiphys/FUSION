# Revisiting the nuclear magnetic octupole moment

> The nuclear magnetic octupole moment is revisited as a potentially useful observable for nuclear structure studies. The magnetic octupole moment, $Ω$, is examined in terms of the nuclear collective model including weak and strong coupling. Single-particle formulation is additionally considered in the overall comparison of theoretical predictions with available experimental data. Mirror nuclei symmetry is examined in terms of the magnetic octupole moment isoscalar and isovector terms. A full list of predictions for $Ω$ of odd-proton and odd-neutron nuclei in medium-heavy mass regimes of the nuclear chart is produced aiming at providing starting values for future experimental endeavors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear magnetic octupole moment, while historically neglected due to experimental difficulty, can be described by the collective model (weak or strong coupling) for most nuclei with spin \(I=l+1/2\), and that the effective weak coupling case appears more successful than the strong coupling case when compared with the most recent experimental data, challenging the earlier conclusion of Suekane and Yamaguchi (1957).

## Method

The authors derived expressions for the magnetic octupole moment \(\Omega\) using the extreme single-particle model (with both bare and effective \(g\)-factors) and the collective model in both the strong and weak coupling limits, following the formalism of Bohr and Mottelson. They compiled all available experimental \(\Omega\) values from the literature (21 isotopes) and compared them to the model predictions, grouping nuclei by the \(R_{4/2}\) ratio of their even-even neighbors to classify them as single-particle, weak-coupling, or strong-coupling regimes. They also extended the isospin formalism to express \(\Omega\) as isoscalar and isovector terms for mirror nuclei, and produced a full list of predicted \(\Omega\) values for odd-proton and odd-neutron nuclei in medium-heavy mass regions.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic octupole moment \(\Omega\) has been measured for only 21 isotopes total.
* For the collective model calculations, the authors used \(\langle r^2\rangle = 3/5 R_0^2\), \(R_0 = 1.35 A^{1/3}\) fm, and coupling constant \(k = 40\) MeV.
* The surface tension parameter was taken as \(4\pi R_0^2 S = 15.4 A^{2/3}\) MeV.
* The effective weak coupling case correctly predicted the order of magnitude for 7 isotopes, while the effective strong coupling case did so for only 2 isotopes.
* For the strong coupling case with \(I=3/2\), the octupole moment is expressed as \(\Omega \simeq \frac{19}{35}\Omega_{sp} + \frac{1}{35}\Omega_0\).
* The core contribution in the simplified weak coupling case is \(\Omega_c = -\frac{45}{224 \pi} g_R R_0^2 \frac{k^2}{\hbar \omega C} \cdot \frac{(2I-1)(I-1)}{I(I+1)^2}\).
* The effective \(g\)-factors used were \(g_s^{eff} = 0.7 g_s\) and \(g_l^{eff} = g_l + \delta g_l\) with \(\delta g_l = +0.1\) for an unpaired proton and \(\delta g_l = 0.0\) for an unpaired neutron.

## Context

This work extends the only previous systematic comparison of \(\Omega\) with nuclear models, that of Suekane and Yamaguchi (1957), by incorporating 21 measured isotopes (including recent measurements by de Groote et al. (2021, 2022) and others) and testing both strong and weak coupling collective models alongside single-particle models. The paper provides the first complete compilation of \(\Omega\) measurements and a set of predictions for over 100 unmeasured nuclei, intended as starting values for future experiments at radioactive beam facilities. It also opens the possibility of using mirror-nuclei symmetry to predict \(\Omega\) values, analogous to established systematics for magnetic dipole moments (Buck et al., 1983; Mertzimekis, 2016), though no mirror-pair data currently exist to test this.
