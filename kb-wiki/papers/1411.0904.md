# Hard Excluseive $Φ$ and $J/Ψ$ Photoproduction off a Proton

> We study the photoproduction of the vector mesons $Φ$ and $J/Ψ$ off a proton in the kinematical regime of large energies and scattering angles within the framework of perturbative QCD. Our investigations are based on the hard scattering approach. This means that the hadrons are replaced by their valence Fock states and scattering on the partonic level is described by tree graphs in which the large transferred momentum is redistributed between the valence partons via the exchange of hard gluons. We find that the unpolarized photoproduction cross sections are dominated by Compton-scattering-like graphs in which the photon couples to the proton, whereas vector-meson-dominance-like graphs, in which the photon fluctuates into the heavy quark-antiquark pair which then exchanges two gluons with the proton, play a minor role. We give explicit predictions for unpolarized scattering cross sections and compare them with experimental data where possible.

## Key claim

The hard scattering approach (HSA) predicts that unpolarized differential cross sections for exclusive Φ and J/Ψ photoproduction off a proton are dominated by Compton-scattering-like graphs (Class II) rather than vector-meson-dominance-like graphs (Class I), but this leading-order HSA description is insufficient for J/Ψ production at accessible momentum transfers because both |t| and the J/Ψ mass still play important roles as large scales.

## Method

The authors applied the hard scattering approach (HSA) of perturbative QCD, replacing each hadron by its valence Fock state and evaluating tree-level Feynman diagrams where hard gluons redistribute the large transferred momentum among valence partons. They used the asymptotic distribution amplitude for the proton (ϕ_p ∝ x₁x₂x₃) and a non-relativistic delta-function distribution amplitude for the vector mesons (ϕ_V ∝ δ(z₁ − 1/2)), with the charm-quark mass set to half the J/Ψ mass for J/Ψ production. The convolution integral over partonic momentum fractions was evaluated numerically after treating propagator singularities via partial fractioning and the iϵ prescription, using a one-loop running strong coupling α_S with a cutoff (0.7 for Φ, 0.5 for J/Ψ) to avoid non-perturbative values.

## Key numbers

- For Φ photoproduction at photon lab energy p_γ^(lab) = 9.3 GeV (s = 18.33 GeV²), the HSA prediction reproduces the angular dependence and order of magnitude of experimental data from SLAC, Daresbury, and JLab.
- For J/Ψ photoproduction at p_γ^(lab) = 150 GeV, the full HSA calculation (including transversely polarized J/Ψ) overshoots FNAL data by nearly two orders of magnitude.
- For J/Ψ photoproduction at p_γ^(lab) = 20 GeV, inclusion of the charm-quark mass significantly increases the cross section for longitudinally polarized J/Ψ, and transversely polarized J/Ψ give the dominant contribution.
- The strong coupling α_S cutoff was applied at 0.7 for Φ production and 0.5 for J/Ψ production to extrapolate to small |t| values.

## Context

This work extends the hard scattering approach (introduced by Efremov and Radyushkin, and by Brodsky and Lepage) to exclusive vector meson photoproduction, specifically for Φ and J/Ψ channels where competing mechanisms like vector-meson dominance and handbag-type mechanisms are suppressed due to the need to produce a heavy quark-antiquark pair. The results contradict the folklore picture (Laget) that Φ photoproduction proceeds via vector-meson dominance, instead finding Compton-scattering-like diagrams dominate. The 12 GeV upgrade of CEBAF at JLab will enable experimental tests of whether perturbative production mechanisms contribute substantially to the γp → Φp differential cross section at momentum transfers of a few GeV.
