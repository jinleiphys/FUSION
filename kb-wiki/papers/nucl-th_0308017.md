# Theory of fusion hindrance and synthesis of the superheavy elements

> The two-step model for fusion reactions of massive systems is briefly reviewed.By the use of fusion probabilities obtained by the model and of survival probabilities obtained by the new statistical code, we predict residue cross sections for 48Ca+actinide systems leading to superheavy elements with Z=114, 116 and 118.

# Theory of fusion hindrance and synthesis of the superheavy elements

## Key claim
The two-step model for fusion of massive systems, combining a surface friction model for the sticking probability with Langevin dynamics for shape evolution over the conditional saddle point, can reproduce experimental fusion excitation functions and residue cross sections for superheavy element synthesis without adjustable parameters, except for the choice of the neck parameter and the use of reduced shell correction energies from Møller et al.

## Method
The fusion probability is factorized into a sticking probability (calculated via an extended surface friction model with fluctuation forces up to the contact point) and a formation probability (calculated by solving Langevin equations for shape evolution from the contact point over the conditional saddle point using the two-center parameterization with frozen neck parameter epsilon = 0.8, liquid drop model potential, and one-body wall-and-window friction). Survival probabilities for the compound nucleus are computed with the new statistical code KEWPIE, which includes level densities from Tøke and Swiatecki's formula, Ignatyuk's shell correction prescription, Cohen-Plasil-Swiatecki fission barriers with Dahlinger et al. systematics for superheavy elements, and Kramers dynamical fission width with reduced friction 5 × 10^20 /sec.

## Key numbers
- The sticking probability shows an effective barrier shift of about 10 MeV higher than the original Coulomb barrier, providing an explanation of the extra-push energy.
- For the 48Ca+244Pu system, the formation probability remains very small over a wide range of excitation energy, consistent with observed fusion hindrance.
- The reduced friction for Kramers dynamical fission width is taken as 5 × 10^20 /sec, consistent with the one-body wall-and-window formula.
- The damping energy for Ignatyuk's shell correction prescription is taken as 18.5 MeV.
- The potential frequency energy quantum at both spherical shape and saddle point is taken as 1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the two-step fusion model previously reported by the authors (Abe et al., earlier publications) and applies it to 48Ca-induced reactions on actinide targets for superheavy element synthesis. The model contrasts with single-step approaches such as the surface friction model of Gross and Kalinowski for deep-inelastic collisions, and incorporates dissipation mechanisms inferred from studies of anomalous neutron and gamma multiplicities prior to fission (using the one-body wall-and-window formula). The predictions for Z=114, 116, and 118 provide guidance for ongoing experiments at Dubna, GSI, GANIL, RIKEN, and JAERI, and the authors state that systematic studies with other theoretical mass predictions are underway.
