# Small-$x$ Helicity Evolution: an Operator Treatment

> We rederive the small-$x$ evolution equations governing quark helicity distribution in a proton using solely an operator-based approach. In our previous works on the subject, the evolution equations were derived using a mix of diagrammatic and operator-based methods. In this work, we re-derive the double-logarithmic small-$x$ evolution equations for quark helicity in terms of the "polarized Wilson lines", the operators consisting of light-cone Wilson lines with one or two non-eikonal local operator insertions which bring in helicity dependence. For the first time we give explicit and complete expressions for the quark and gluon polarized Wilson line operators, including insertions of both the gluon and quark sub-eikonal operators. We show that the double-logarithmic small-$x$ evolution of the "polarized dipole amplitude" operators, made out of regular light-cone Wilson lines along with the polarized ones constructed here, reproduces the equations derived in our earlier works. The method we present here can be used as a template for determining the small-$x$ asymptotics of any transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) quark (or gluon) parton distribution functions (PDFs), and is not limited to helicity.

## Key claim

The authors rederive the double-logarithmic small-x evolution equations for quark helicity using a purely operator-based approach, showing that the previously derived equations (which used a mix of diagrammatic and operator methods) are reproduced exactly. The key advance is the explicit construction of complete polarized Wilson line operators for both quarks and gluons, including both gluon and quark sub-eikonal operator insertions, which had not been fully specified in prior work.

## Method

The authors start from the standard operator definition of the quark helicity transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMD) and evaluate it at small x using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC)/saturation framework in the A- = 0 light-cone gauge. They construct explicit operator expressions for polarized quark Wilson lines (involving sub-eikonal F12 field strength insertions between light-cone Wilson lines) and polarized gluon Wilson lines, then derive the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) evolution equations for "polarized dipole amplitude" operators made from regular and polarized Wilson lines. The evolution equations are obtained in both the large-Nc and large-Nc & Nf limits.

## Key numbers

* Quark helicity PDF small-x asymptotics: Δq(x, Q²) ~ (1/x)^(4/√3 * √(α_s N_c / (2π))) in the large-Nc limit with small 't Hooft coupling α_s N_c ≪ 1
* Gluon helicity PDF small-x asymptotics: ΔG(x, Q²) ~ (1/x)^(13/(4√3) * √(α_s N_c / (2π))) in the large-Nc limit with small 't Hooft coupling α_s N_c ≪ 1
* The resummation parameter is α_s ln²(1/x), referred to as the Double Logarithmic Approximation (DLA), originally introduced by Kirschner and Lipatov (1983)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivations of helicity evolution equations (Kovchegov 2015, 2016, 2017) by providing a complete operator-based formalism that cross-checks those results. The operator approach developed here can serve as a template for determining the small-x asymptotics of any transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMD), including transversity and the Sivers function, complementing efforts by Balitsky (2015, 2016) and others. The method opens the possibility to systematically derive small-x asymptotics for all TMDs in perturbative QCD, with the authors noting they will apply this formalism to quark transversity in a separate work.
