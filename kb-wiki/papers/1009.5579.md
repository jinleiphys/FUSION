# Neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{150}$Nd with account for deformation

> A microscopic state-of-the-art calculation of the nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{150}$Nd with an account for nuclear deformation is performed. The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a realistic residual interaction [the Brueckner $G$ matrix derived from the charge-depending Bonn (Bonn-CD) nucleon-nucleon potential] is used as the underlying nuclear structure model. The present calculated matrix element is suppressed by about 40% as compared with our previous QRPA result for $^{150}$Nd obtained with neglect of deformation. By making use of this newest nuclear matrix element, one may conclude that neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{150}$Nd, to be measured soon by the SNO+ collaboration, provides one of the best probes of the Majorana neutrino mass.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double beta decay of 150Nd, calculated within the proton-neutron QRPA with a realistic residual interaction and accounting for nuclear deformation, is suppressed by about 40% compared to the previous QRPA result that neglected deformation; this suppression still allows the SNO+ experiment to approach the inverse hierarchy region of the neutrino mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors performed a QRPA calculation in the intrinsic coordinate system of the axially deformed nucleus, using the adiabatic Bohr-Mottelson approximation. The single-particle basis was constructed from a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field with quadrupole deformation parameters β2=0.240 for 150Nd and β2=0.153 for 150Sm, fitted to reproduce experimental B(E2) values. The residual two-body interaction was the Brueckner G-matrix derived from the charge-dependent Bonn (Bonn-CD) nucleon-nucleon potential, with the particle-hole strength gph=0.90 fixed by the Gamow-Teller giant resonance position and the particle-particle strength gpp fitted to reproduce the experimental 2νββ-decay matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The calculated neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element M0ν for 150Nd with deformation is 3.16, compared to 5.78 in the spherical limit of the same calculation and 5.17 in the earlier spherical QRPA result of Rodin (2005).
- The corresponding half-life T0ν1/2 for an assumed effective Majorana neutrino mass ⟨mββ⟩ = 50 meV is 4.60 × 1025 years with deformation, versus 1.38 × 1025 years in the spherical limit.
- The experimental deformation parameters are β=0.29 for 150Nd and β=0.19 for 150Sm, extracted from B(E2) values.
- The fitted Woods-Saxon deformation parameters are β2=0.240 for 150Nd and β2=0.153 for 150Sm.
- The BCS overlap factor between initial and final BCS vacua is 0.52 with deformation and 0.85 in the spherical limit.
- The fitted particle-particle strength parameter gpp is 1.05 with deformation and 1.01 in the spherical limit.
- The Q-value for the decay is Qββ=3.37 MeV, and the phase-space factor is about 33 times larger than that for 76Ge.
- The SNO+ experiment plans 43.6 kg of 150Nd isotope and expects a sensitivity of T0ν1/2 ≈ 5 × 1024 years after one year.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA approach for deformed nuclei previously developed for 2νββ-decay by Simkovic (2003, 2004) and Sarriguren (2009) to the calculation of 0νββ-decay matrix elements. The result is significantly larger than the matrix elements from the pseudo-SU(3) model of Hirsch (1995) (1.57), the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach of Rath (2008) (1.61), and the interacting boson model of Barea (2009) (2.32), but smaller than the earlier spherical QRPA result of Rodin (2005) (5.17). The calculation enables a more reliable interpretation of the upcoming SNO+ experiment's sensitivity to the Majorana neutrino mass, suggesting that the experiment may still probe the inverse hierarchy region despite the suppression from deformation.
