# A doubly-periodic structure for the study of inhomogeneous bulk fermion matter with spatial localizations

> We present a method that offers perspectives to perform fully antisymmetrized simulations for inhomogeneous bulk fermion matter. The technique bears resemblance to classical periodic boundary conditions, using localized single-particle states. Such localized states are an ideal tool to discuss phenomena where spatial localization plays an important role. The antisymmetrisation is obtained introducing a doubly-periodic structure in the many-body fermion wave functions. This results in circulant matrices for the evaluation of expectation values, leading to a computationally tractable formalism to study fully antisymmetrized bulk fermion matter. We show that the proposed technique is able to reproduce essential fermion features in an elegant and computationally advantageous manner.

## Key claim
The paper presents a method for fully antisymmetrized simulations of inhomogeneous bulk fermion matter using localized single-particle states, but the key hedge is that the technique works by imposing a doubly-periodic structure on the many-body wave function that truncates the antisymmetry operator to a finite range, then exploits the resulting circulant matrix structure to recover the infinite-volume limit via integration over the first Brillouin zone.

## Method
The authors introduce a doubly-periodic structure: the simulation volume is periodically replicated on a lattice, and the single-particle wave packets themselves are made periodic with a larger period (Born-von Karman boundary conditions). This yields a nested block-circulant overlap matrix whose blocks correspond to lattice vectors connecting cells. The inverse overlap matrix is obtained by Fourier transforming to reciprocal space, integrating over the first Brillouin zone, and then transforming back. Expectation values of one- and two-body operators are computed as integrals over the Brillouin zone, reducing the infinite-volume problem to a finite calculation per unit cell.

## Key numbers
- For a one-dimensional test with one Gaussian per unit cell of size ℓ, the ratio √a/ℓ = 0.2 (small overlap) yields distinguishable-particle behavior, while √a/ℓ = 1 (large overlap) shows fermionic behavior approaching a free Fermi gas.
- For the two-dimensional test with N=25 Gaussian wave packets randomly placed in a square unit cell of length ℓ, the wave packets have variance √a = 0.2ℓ and zero mean momentum; the Fermi momentum of a free system with the same density is k_F = √(4πN)/ℓ.
- The computational order for expectation values is N^4 for two-body interactions, the same as for finite systems, but with an extra integration over the Brillouin zone.

## Context
This work extends antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) and fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) techniques (Feldmeier 2000, Ono 2004) to bulk fermion systems with full antisymmetrization, which had previously been missing. It contrasts with approaches using Bloch-Floquet states or twist-averaged boundary conditions (TinkaGammel 1993, Lin 2001) that require delocalized states and large configuration spaces to describe spatial localizations. The method enables computationally tractable simulations of inhomogeneous bulk fermion matter, such as neutron star crusts with nuclear pastas, using localized Gaussian wave packets, and can be directly applied to AMD and FMD frameworks.
