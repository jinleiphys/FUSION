# Note on the Orthogonal Set in Six Kinds of Boson Operators --In Relation to the su(1,1)- and its Relevant Algebras--

> Following the basic idea proposed by the present authors in recent paper, a possible form of the orthogonal set for many-body system consisting of six kinds of boson operators is developed. In contrast to the recent paper, in which the su(2)-algebra plays a central role, in this paper, the su(1,1)-algebra is in a central position. The orthogonal set obtained in this paper is expressed in terms of monomial with respect to the state generating operators.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an orthogonal basis for a many-body system of six boson operators, this time with the su(1,1)-algebra as the central algebraic structure rather than the su(2)-algebra used in the authors' prior work (I). The construction is limited to the case where the su(1,1)-spin quantum number t takes values +3/2, +2, 5/2, ... (the positive branch), and the basis states are expressed as monomials in state-generating operators.

## Method

The authors define six boson operators (â_i, â_i^*) and (b̂_i, b̂_i^*) for i=1,2,3 and construct the su(1,1)-algebra generators T̂_±,0 as sums over i of the Schwinger boson representation. They also introduce two independent su(3)-algebras (Ŝ and R̂ generators) and two su(2)-algebras (M̂ and L̂ spins) that commute with the su(1,1) generators. The intrinsic state |(γ);t⟩ is built by applying spherical tensor operators Ŝ_+(k,k₀) and R̂_+(l,l₀) to the vacuum, combined with Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, and then the full orthogonal set is expressed as monomials in the raising operators M̂_+, L̂_+, and P̂_+ acting on a reference state.

## Key numbers

- The su(1,1)-spin quantum number t takes values t = +3/2, +2, 5/2, ... (positive branch), with t₀ = t, t+1, t+2, ...
- The eigenvalue of the Casimir operator T̂² is t(t-1).
- The eigenvalue of the su(3) Casimir operator Ŝ² is (1/3)·(2t-3-l)(2t-l) + l(l+1).
- The quantum numbers satisfy the inequality k+l+m ≤ 2t-3, where k, l, m are su(3) and su(2) quantum numbers.
- The coupling rule for three su(1,1)-spins gives t ≥ |t₁₂-1| + |t₃-1/2| + 3/2 for the positive branch, and t ≥ |t₁₂| + |t₃-1/2| + 3/2 for the negative branch.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (I) (Kuriyama et al., 2003), which treated the su(2)-algebra case for six boson operators, to the su(1,1)-algebra case with (M=2, N=1) in the extended (M+1)(N+1)-dimensional algebra framework. The formalism builds on the Schwinger boson representation of su(1,1) and the authors' earlier deformed boson scheme (Kuriyama et al., 2003). The resulting orthogonal set enables the analysis of more realistic many-body systems than those treated in the deformed boson scheme alone, and the explicit monomial form of the basis states facilitates practical calculations.
