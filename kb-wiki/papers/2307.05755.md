# QCD on Rotating Lattice with Staggered Fermions

> We investigate the finite-temperature quantum chromodynamics (QCD) on a rotating lattice with $N_f=2+1$ staggered fermions and the projective plane boundary condition. We observe a negative rotational rigidity (defined in the main text) and a negative quark spin susceptibility associated with the chiral vortical effect. In contrast to most of the effective model predictions, we find that the chiral condensate decreases and the Polyakov loop increases with imaginary rotation, implying a rotational catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking and confinement by real rotation. We determine the phase boundaries for both chiral and confinement-deconfinement phase transitions on the $Ω_I$-$T$ plane, where $Ω_I$ is the imaginary angular velocity.

## Key claim
The authors find that, contrary to most effective model predictions, real uniform rotation at finite temperature *catalyzes* chiral symmetry breaking and confinement in QCD, based on lattice simulations using imaginary angular velocity and analytic continuation. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the validity of analytic continuation from imaginary to real rotation, which the authors test for the Polyakov loop in the quenched limit.

## Method
The authors simulate QCD with \(N_f=2+1\) staggered fermions on a rotating lattice by implementing a rest state in a rotating frame with an imaginary angular velocity \(\Omega_I\), using a projective plane boundary condition in the transverse plane to preserve causality. They measure the rotational rigidity (angular momentum response) and quark spin susceptibility, as well as the Polyakov loop and chiral condensate as functions of \(\Omega_I\) and temperature, and determine phase boundaries from the peaks of the disconnected susceptibilities.

## Key numbers
- Space-averaged light quark spin susceptibility: \(\bar\xi_l = -0.0063(1) a^{-2}\) (lattice units), nearly independent of \(\Omega_I\) and \(T\) over \(T \approx 100\)–\(300\) MeV.
- Space-averaged strange quark spin susceptibility: \(\bar\xi_s = -0.0068(1) a^{-2}\).
- Reinterpreted axial CVE conductivity: \(2\bar\xi_f \approx -0.472(5) T^2\) (unrenormalized), insensitive to flavor.
- For the Polyakov loop at quenched limit (\(\beta=5.7\), \(12^3\times 4\) lattice), Taylor expansion coefficients: \(c_0 = 0.10498(3)\), \(c_2 = (-3.0 \pm 1.1) \times 10^2\), indicating suppression by real rotation.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of rotation from pure gluodynamics (Braguta 2020, Braguta 2021) and quenched Wilson fermions (Yamamoto 2013) to full dynamical staggered fermions. It sharply contradicts effective model predictions that real rotation suppresses the chiral condensate (Chen 2015, Jiang 2016, Chernodub 2016, Wang 2018, Zhang 2020, Sadooghi 2021, Chen 2022, Chen 2023) and deconfinement temperature (Chen 2020, Braga 2022, Yadav 2022, Zhao 2022, Fujimoto 2021, Chen 2022). The results enable future tests using functional renormalization group or Dyson-Schwinger methods, and raise questions about the validity of analytic continuation for rotation.
