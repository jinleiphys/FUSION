# Decoding the structure near the $π^+π^-$ mass threshold in $ψ(3686) \rightarrow J/ψπ^+π^-$ decays

> In light of recent high-precision data taken by the BESIII Collaboration, we reconsider the dipion transition $ψ(3686) \rightarrow J/ψπ^+π^-$. The strong pion-pion final-state interactions are taken into account model-independently by using dispersion theory. We find that we can reproduce the substructure near the $π^+π^-$ threshold observed experimentally without introducing an extra resonance state. While a helicity-flip amplitude plays an important role for the formation of the dip in the invariant-mass distribution, the virtual exchange of the charmoniumlike exotic $Z_c(3900)$ state improves the fit quality only slightly.

# Decoding the structure near the π⁺π⁻ mass threshold in ψ(3686) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻ decays

## Key claim

The resonancelike structure near the π⁺π⁻ mass threshold observed by BESIII can be reproduced without introducing a new resonance state, using only chiral contact terms and dispersively treated pion-pion final-state interactions. The virtual exchange of the charmoniumlike exotic Zc(3900) state improves the fit quality only slightly, and the structure previously parametrized as a Breit-Wigner resonance with mass 282.6 MeV and width 17.3 MeV is not required by the data.

## Method

The authors use dispersion theory with single-channel Omnès functions to treat ππ final-state interactions model-independently, incorporating S-wave and D-wave ππ isoscalar phase shifts from the literature. They perform simultaneous fits to the BESIII ππ invariant-mass distributions and helicity angular distributions for ψ(3686) → J/ψπ⁺π⁻, including chiral contact terms at leading order in the chiral and heavy-quark nonrelativistic expansions, with optional addition of a Zc(3900)-exchange amplitude. The fits are performed both for the full unfolded spectra and for the folded low-energy region (0.28–0.32 GeV).

## Key numbers

- The BESIII Breit-Wigner parametrization of the threshold structure gives mass = (282.6 ± 0.4_stat ± 2.5_sys) MeV and width = (17.3 ± 0.8_stat ± 0.4_sys) MeV, which the authors argue is not a genuine resonance.
- Fit I (chiral contact terms only) yields χ²/dof = 13.5 (1105+479 over 117 degrees of freedom) for the full spectra.
- Fit II (with Zc exchange, Zc mass fixed at 3.880 GeV and width at 0.036 GeV) yields χ²/dof = 12.3 (1095+327 over 116 degrees of freedom).
- Fit III (Zc mass and width free within ranges M_Zc ∈ 3.880(24) GeV, Γ_Zc ∈ 0.036(17) GeV) yields χ²/dof = 12.1 (1086+289 over 114 degrees of freedom), with best-fit M_Zc = 3.873(2) GeV and Γ_Zc = 0.019(1) GeV.
- For the folded low-energy region (0.28–0.32 GeV), Fits Ib, IIb, and IIIb give χ²/dof = 1.04, 1.03, and 1.08 respectively.
- The D-wave amplitudes are about two orders of magnitude smaller than the S-wave amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive studies of dipion transitions in heavy quarkonia, including Chen (2015, 2016, 2019) for Υ decays and Dong (2021) for ψ(3686) decays. It directly addresses the BESIII (2025) observation of a threshold structure that was interpreted as a possible new resonance, contradicting the earlier chiral unitary approach of Guo (2004) which BESIII claimed failed to reproduce the enhancement. The result enables a model-independent framework for analyzing threshold structures in charmonium transitions without invoking exotic resonances, and clarifies that the Zc(3900) exchange, while improving the helicity angular distribution, is not necessary to explain the invariant-mass peak. The authors note that a concurrent study by Wang (2025) addresses the same problem but relies on a mechanism the authors argue is inconsistent with coupled-channel analyses of the I=0 S-wave (Kaiser, 1998; Garcia-Martin, 2011; Briceno, 2017).
