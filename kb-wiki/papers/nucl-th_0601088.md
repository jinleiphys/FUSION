# A Note on the Two-Level Pairing Model Obeying the su(2)*su(2)-Algebra

> The two-level pairing model obeying the su(2)*su(2)-algebra, which was discussed in the previous paper, is re-formed in the framework of the su(1,1)*su(1,1)-algebra in the Schwinger boson representation. With the aid of MYT mapping method, the Schwinger boson representation is reduced to the Holstein-Primakoff boson representation, which is expressed in terms of two kinds of bosons. Further, with the aid of the squeezed coherent state, the classical counterpart is obtained.

## Key claim

The two-level pairing model, previously formulated in an su(2)*su(2)-algebra Schwinger boson representation, is re-formed in an su(1,1)*su(1,1)-algebra framework and then reduced to a Holstein-Primakoff boson representation using the MYT mapping method, with the classical counterpart obtained via a squeezed coherent state; the equivalence to the earlier one-boson disguised form is demonstrated, but the ordering of terms in the classical Hamiltonian matters for consistency with the quantized version.

## Method

The authors start from the su(1,1)*su(1,1)-algebra generators expressed in terms of four Schwinger boson operators (a_+, a_-, b_+, b_-) and construct an orthogonal basis labeled by eigenvalues of the Casimir-related operators T(σ) and K(σ). Using the MYT mapping method, they map this Schwinger boson representation onto a Holstein-Primakoff representation involving two kinds of bosons (c_+, c_-), obtaining a mapped Hamiltonian. They then construct a squeezed coherent state parameterized by angle-action variables (φ_σ, T(σ)) and canonical boson variables (c_σ, c_σ*), compute its expectation value of the Hamiltonian to obtain the classical counterpart, and finally reduce the two-boson system to a one-boson form to show equivalence with the earlier work.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalues of the operators K(σ) and T(σ) are ħk_σ and ħt_σ, with t_+, t_- = 1/2, 1, 3/2, ... and k_+, k_- = 0, 1, 2, ... (Section 2, Eq. 8b).
- The classical Hamiltonian H^0 (Eq. 22) is identical in form to the quantized Holstein-Primakoff Hamiltonian ħ (Eq. 13) under the correspondence ĉ_σ ~ c_σ, ĉ_σ* ~ c_σ* (Eq. 23).
- The mapping from the two-boson state |k_+, k_-⟩ to the one-boson state |k⟩ uses the relation k_+ = k, k_- = 2m - k, where m = 0, 1/2, 1, ... and k = 0, 1, ..., 2m (Eqs. 24b, 25).

## Context

This work extends the previous paper (referred to as (A)) on the two-level pairing model in the su(2)*su(2)-algebra Schwinger boson representation by re-forming it in the su(1,1)*su(1,1)-algebra and deriving a Holstein-Primakoff representation with two bosons. The MYT mapping method (attributed to the authors' earlier work) is used to connect the Schwinger and Holstein-Primakoff representations. The squeezed coherent state approach provides a classical counterpart, and the reduction to one boson shows equivalence to the disguised form in (A), enabling future applications of either representation depending on convenience.
