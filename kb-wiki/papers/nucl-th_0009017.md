# To the nature of intermediate- and short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Instead of the Yukawa mechanism for intermediate- and short-range interaction some new approach based on formation of the symmetric six-quark bag in the state $|(0s)^6[6]_X,L=0>$ dressed due to strong coupling to $π$, $σ$ and $ρ$ fields are suggested. This new mechanism offers both a strong intermediate-range attraction which replaces the effective $σ$-exchange (or excitation of two isobars in the intermediate state) in traditional force models and also short-range repulsion. Simple illustrative model is developed which demonstrates clearly how well the suggested new mechanism can reproduce $NN$ data.

# To the nature of intermediate- and short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction

## Key claim

The authors propose that intermediate-range attraction and short-range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction arise not from Yukawa t-channel meson exchange but from s-channel formation of a symmetric six-quark bag in the state |(0s)^6[6]_X, L=0⟩ dressed by strong coupling to π, σ, and ρ fields. This mechanism is presented as an alternative to effective σ-exchange or two-isobar excitation in traditional force models, but the authors acknowledge that traditional Yukawa mechanisms for π-, 2π-, and ρ-meson exchanges between isolated nucleons remain essential at separations beyond the six-quark bag (>1 fm) and in high partial waves (L>3).

## Method

The authors develop a quark-meson microscopic model in which the NN transition amplitude proceeds through six-quark + 2π intermediate states in the s-channel, with virtual emission and absorption of two correlated pions by diquark pairs or by 1p-shell quarks jumping between 1p and 0s orbits. The transition amplitude is calculated using the quark-pair-creation model (QPCM), with the two-pion pair forming scalar σ and vector ρ mesons that dress the symmetric six-quark bag. The resulting NN interaction takes the form of a separable energy-dependent potential matrix with nodal form factors, derived from the orthogonality between the proper NN channel and the six-quark part of the intermediate bag state.

## Key numbers

- The effective pole position E_0 for the dressed bag propagator is approximately 600 MeV (E_0 ~ 600 MeV), corresponding to the sum of the six-quark bag energy and the effective σ-meson mass inside the bag.
- The model fits NN phase shifts in the energy range 0 to 600 MeV, and for the ^1S_0 channel up to 1200 MeV.
- The soft cut-off parameter Λ_πNN used in the model is 0.594 GeV (0.594 GeV), in contrast to values around 0.8 GeV typical of one-boson-exchange models.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is f^2_πNN/(4π) = 0.075.
- The cut-off parameter Λ_πNN is varied between 0.60 and 0.73 GeV in the calculations.
- The transition form factors for L=0 and L=2 partial waves contain coefficients a_L=0 = 1/3 and a_L=2 = -2/3 in the exponential expression.
- The effective strength constants g_L for the transition NN → d_0+σ are given by C_L values: for L=0, C_0 = -617/(144 × 2020√5); for L=2, C_2 = 11/(144 × 81).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the Moscow potential model (Kukulin 1999, PRC99) and the six-quark symmetry approach (Kus91, PRC99), and it directly challenges the traditional Yukawa-based NN force models reviewed by Machleidt (1987). The proposed s-channel dressed-bag mechanism provides a microscopic foundation for semi-phenomenological models such as the Moscow NN potential, the Tabakin separable potential, and the quark-compound-bag (QCB) model by Simonov. The model predicts strong three-nucleon and four-nucleon forces mediated by 2π and ρ exchanges, which the authors argue could resolve the long-standing puzzle of the analyzing power A_y in N-d scattering (Foster 1999) and the binding energies of light nuclei.
