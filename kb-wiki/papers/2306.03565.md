# Lattice study on a tetra-quark state $T_{bb}$ in the HAL QCD method

> We study a doubly-bottomed tetra-quark state $(bb\bar{u}\bar{d})$ with quantum number $I(J^P)=0(1^+)$, denoted by $T_{bb}$, in lattice QCD with the Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) quark action for $b$ quarks. Employing $(2+1)$-flavor gauge configurations at $a \approx 0.09$ {fm} on $32^3\times 64$ lattices, we have extracted the coupled channel potential between $\bar{B}\bar{B}^*$ and $\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*$ in the HAL QCD method, which predicts an existence of a bound $T_{bb}$ below the $\bar{B}\bar{B}^*$ threshold. By extrapolating results at $m_π\approx 410,\, 570,\, 700$ {MeV} to the physical pion mass $m_π\approx140$ {MeV}, we obtain a biding energy with its statistical error as $E_{\rm binding}^{\rm (single)} = 155(17)$ MeV and $E_{\rm binding}^{\rm (coupled)} = 83(10)$ MeV, where ``coupled" means that effects due to virtual $\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*$ states are included through the coupled channel potential, while only a potential for a single $\bar{B}\bar{B}^*$ channel is used in the analysis for ``single". A comparison shows that the effect from virtual $\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*$ states is quite sizable to the binding energy of $T_{bb}$. We estimate systematic errors to be $\pm 20$ MeV at most, which are mainly caused by the NRQCD approximation for $b$ quarks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the doubly-bottomed tetra-quark state \(T_{bb}\) exists as a bound state below the \(\bar{B}\bar{B}^*\) threshold, with a binding energy that depends strongly on whether the coupled-channel effect from virtual \(\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*\) states is included. The abstract's claim of a bound state is qualified by the finding that the single-channel analysis gives a binding energy nearly twice as large as the coupled-channel result, and the systematic error from the NRQCD approximation for \(b\) quarks is estimated at \(\pm 20\) MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using \((2+1)\)-flavor gauge configurations at a lattice spacing \(a \approx 0.09\) fm on \(32^3 \times 64\) lattices, with the Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) action for \(b\) quarks. They extracted the coupled-channel potential between \(\bar{B}\bar{B}^*\) and \(\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*\) using the HAL QCD method, employing a time-dependent method to avoid ground-state saturation issues, and then solved the Schrödinger equation to obtain binding energies at three pion masses (\(m_\pi \approx 410, 570, 700\) MeV) before extrapolating to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy from single-channel analysis (only \(\bar{B}\bar{B}^*\) potential): \(E_{\rm binding}^{\rm (single)} = 155(17)\) MeV at the physical pion mass.
- Binding energy from coupled-channel analysis (including \(\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*\) virtual states): \(E_{\rm binding}^{\rm (coupled)} = 83(10)\) MeV at the physical pion mass.
- Systematic error estimate: \(\pm 20\) MeV at most, mainly from the NRQCD approximation for \(b\) quarks.
- Lattice spacing: \(a \approx 0.0907(13)\) fm; spatial lattice size: \(L_s \approx 2.902(42)\) fm.
- Pion masses used for extrapolation: \(m_\pi \approx 701(1), 571(0), 416(1)\) MeV on the three ensembles (PACS-CS-A, B, C).
- Spin-averaged \(\bar{B}\) meson mass on the ensembles: approximately 5440(174), 5382(269), 5332(220) MeV.
- Energy splitting \(\Delta E_{\bar{B}\bar{B}^*}\) (difference between \(\bar{B}^*\) and \(\bar{B}\) energies at rest): 49.4(2.6), 44.9(1.6), 42.7(3.9) MeV on the three ensembles.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of \(T_{bb}\) that used either static quark approximations or NRQCD with single-channel analyses, such as Bicudo (2015, 2016), Francis (2016), Junnarkar (2018), Leskovec (2019), Mohanta (2020), and Hudspith (2023). Those studies reported binding energies ranging from about 59 to 186 MeV depending on the treatment of \(b\) quarks and the number of channels. The present paper is the first to combine NRQCD \(b\) quarks with a coupled-channel HAL QCD analysis, demonstrating that virtual \(\bar{B}^* \bar{B}^*\) states reduce the binding energy substantially compared to single-channel NRQCD results. This enables more precise theoretical predictions for the as-yet-unobserved \(T_{bb}\) state and provides a methodology applicable to other heavy tetra-quark systems.
