# Hard jet substructure in a multistage approach

> We present predictions and postdictions for a wide variety of hard jet-substructure observables using a multistage model within the JETSCAPE framework. The details of the multistage model and the various parameter choices are described in [A. Kumar et al., arXiv:2204.01163]. A novel feature of this model is the presence of two stages of jet modification: a high virtuality phase [modeled using the modular all twist transverse-scattering elastic-drag and radiation model (MATTER)], where modified coherence effects diminish medium-induced radiation, and a lower virtuality phase [modeled using the linear Boltzmann transport model (LBT)], where parton splits are fully resolved by the medium as they endure multiple scattering induced energy loss. Energy-loss calculations are carried out on event-by-event viscous fluid dynamic backgrounds constrained by experimental data. The uniform and consistent descriptions of multiple experimental observables demonstrate the essential role of modified coherence effects and the multistage modeling of jet evolution. Using the best choice of parameters from [A. Kumar et al., arXiv:2204.01163], and with no further tuning, we present calculations for the medium modified jet fragmentation function, the groomed jet momentum fraction $z_g$ and angular separation $r_g$ distributions, as well as the nuclear modification factor of groomed jets. These calculations provide accurate descriptions of published data from experiments at the Large Hadron Collider. Furthermore, we provide predictions from the multistage model for future measurements at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multistage model combining high-virtuality (MATTER) and low-virtuality (LBT) modules within the JETSCAPE framework, using parameters previously tuned to single-hadron and jet suppression data with no further adjustment, can simultaneously describe multiple jet substructure observables from LHC experiments, thereby confirming the essential role of modified coherence effects in jet-medium interactions.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of heavy-ion collisions using the JETSCAPE framework with a two-stage in-medium parton shower: high-virtuality partons evolved via the MATTER module (which incorporates modified coherence effects that suppress medium-induced radiation at high virtuality), and low-virtuality partons evolved via the LBT module (where scattering-driven energy loss dominates). The medium background was generated using event-by-event (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics with TRENTO initial conditions, with parameters from Bayesian calibration for LHC energies. Jet substructure observables were calculated from the same simulation events previously used to study nuclear modification factors, without any retuning of parameters.

## Key numbers

- The switching virtuality between MATTER and LBT modules was set to \(Q^2_{\mathrm{sw}} = 4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- The modulation factor for modified coherence effects decreases sharply with virtuality: \(f(Q^2=(5\ \mathrm{GeV})^2) \approx 0.0359\), \(f(Q^2=(10\ \mathrm{GeV})^2) \approx 0.00862\), and \(f(Q^2=(50\ \mathrm{GeV})^2) \approx 0.00104\)
- The cutoff virtuality for hadronization was set to \(Q_{\mathrm{min}}^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)
- The transition temperature for partons leaving the dense medium was \(T_c = 0.16\ \mathrm{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of the MATTER+LBT multistage model previously applied to nuclear modification factors for jets, high-\(p_T\) charged particles, and D mesons (Kumar et al., 2023, Phys. Rev. C 107, 034911). By analyzing jet fragmentation functions, groomed jet momentum fraction \(z_g\) and angular separation \(r_g\) distributions, and the nuclear modification factor of groomed jets, the authors demonstrate that modified coherence effects are essential for describing internal jet structure, not just inclusive yields. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming RHIC measurements and motivate further inclusion of hydrodynamic medium response and nonperturbative hadronization effects.
