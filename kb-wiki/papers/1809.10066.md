# Inhomogeneous chiral phases away from the chiral limit

> The effect of explicit chiral-symmetry breaking on inhomogeneous chiral phases is studied within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with nonzero current quark mass. Generalizing an earlier result obtained in the chiral limit, we show within a Ginzburg-Landau analysis that the critical endpoint of the first-order chiral phase boundary between two homogeneous phases gets replaced by a "pseudo-Lifshitz point" when the possibility of inhomogeneous order parameters is considered. Performing a stability analysis we also show that the unstable mode along the phase boundary is in the scalar but not in the pseudoscalar channel, suggesting that modulations which contain pseudoscalar condensates, like a generalized dual chiral density wave, are disfavored against purely scalar ones. Numerically we find that the inhomogeneous phase shrinks as one moves away from the chiral limit, but survives even at significantly large values of the current quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with nonzero current quark mass, the critical endpoint (CEP) of the first-order chiral phase boundary between homogeneous phases exactly coincides with the "pseudo-Lifshitz point" (PLP) where the inhomogeneous phase terminates, generalizing the chiral-limit result that the tricritical point coincides with the Lifshitz point.

## Method

The authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the thermodynamic potential about a spatially constant constituent quark mass \(M_0\), keeping terms up to fourth order in the fluctuating field \(\delta M(\mathbf{x})\) and including gradient terms. They compute the GL coefficients \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_{4,a}, \alpha_{4,b}\) from the NJL model's mean-field thermodynamic potential using a gradient expansion, and evaluate the inverse scalar and pseudoscalar correlation functions \(\Gamma_S^{-1}(\mathbf{q}^2)\) and \(\Gamma_P^{-1}(\mathbf{q}^2)\) to identify instabilities toward inhomogeneous modes. The analysis is performed for two-flavor NJL with Pauli-Villars regularization, with parameters fitted to a vacuum constituent quark mass of 300 MeV and pion decay constant of 88 MeV in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- For current quark mass \(m = 10\) MeV (corresponding to vacuum pion mass \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV), the inverse scalar correlation function \(\Gamma_S^{-1}\) touches zero at \(T = 10\) MeV and \(\mu = 344\) MeV, indicating the phase boundary.
- The relation between current quark mass and vacuum pion mass is given: \((m/\mathrm{MeV}, m_\pi/\mathrm{MeV}) = (0,0), (1,43), (5,96), (10,135), (20,191), (30,235), (40,271), (50,303)\).
- The temperature of the PLP/CEP decreases strongly for small \(m\) but levels off at larger masses; the paper reports that the inhomogeneous phase survives "even at significantly large values of the current quark mass."

## Context

This work extends the chiral-limit analysis of Nickel (2009), who showed that the Lifshitz point coincides with the tricritical point in the NJL model, and the numerical evidence of Nickel (2009) that the inhomogeneous phase persists for nonzero quark mass. It contrasts with results in the quark-meson model by Andersen (2018), who reported that the inhomogeneous phase disappears already at a pion mass of 37 MeV. The finding that the PLP coincides with the CEP implies that the existence of an inhomogeneous phase is as plausible as the existence of a CEP in the NJL model, and it enables future studies of fluctuation effects beyond mean field, as well as extensions to the quark-meson model and QCD via Dyson-Schwinger approaches.
