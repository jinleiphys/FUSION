# Flow and the equation of state of nuclear matter

> 16 pages, 21 figures, talk given at the 7th Int. Conf. on Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions, NN2000, Strasbourg, France, July 3-7, 2000

# Flow and the equation of state of nuclear matter

## Key claim
Collective flow observables in heavy-ion collisions can constrain the equation of state (EOS) of hadronic matter, but the inference requires disentangling the EOS from momentum-dependent mean fields and transport properties; some level of softening in the EOS may occur at beam energies between 2 and 4 GeV/nucleon, corresponding to compression of 3-4 times normal nuclear density, but the evidence is not conclusive and depends on which flow observable is examined.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental measurements of radial, sideward, and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions across beam energies from below 100 MeV/nucleon to above 100 GeV/nucleon, and compares them to transport-model simulations based on relativistic Landau theory with nucleon, pion, delta, and N* degrees of freedom. The simulations solve Boltzmann equations with different parametrizations of the energy functional that yield soft EOS, hard EOS, and soft EOS with a high-density phase transition, with and without momentum-dependent mean fields. The elliptic flow excitation function is used to assess the pace of expansion of participant matter relative to spectator passage time.

## Key numbers
- Sideward flow is maximal between 0.1 and 10 GeV/nucleon beam energy in central collisions of heavy nuclei.
- In 2 GeV/nucleon Au + Au collisions at impact parameter b=8 fm, participant matter gets compressed to less than 3 times normal nuclear density (rho/rho_0 less than 3) for a hard EOS with momentum-dependent mean fields.
- At 400 MeV/nucleon Au + Au collisions, simulations compare incompressibility values K=380 MeV (stiff) and K=210 MeV (soft) for momentum-independent and momentum-dependent mean fields.
- The elliptic flow data at 2 GeV/nucleon show expansion as rapid as for the hard EOS with momentum dependence; at higher energies the expansion becomes more like the soft EOS.
- The ratio of out-of-plane to in-plane proton yields R_N = (1-2v_2)/(1+2v_2) is measured for 400 MeV/nucleon Bi + Bi reactions as a function of transverse momentum.
- The optical potential in nuclear matter from DBHF calculations with Bonn-A interaction is compared to transport-model parametrizations at different densities.

## Context
This review extends the program of constraining the nuclear EOS from collective flow, building on earlier work by Siemens and Rasmussen (1979) on radial flow parametrization, and by Ollitrault (1992) on the competition between squeeze-out and in-plane emission in elliptic flow. The paper synthesizes data from the FOPI Collaboration (Poggi et al., 1995), NA44 Collaboration (Bearden et al., 1997), and KaoS measurements (Brinkmann et al., 1996), among others. The results enable future differential studies of flow as a function of transverse momentum, rapidity, and impact parameter to disentangle EOS effects from momentum-dependent mean fields, and to constrain microscopic theories such as Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The deduced pressure-density boundaries for symmetric nuclear matter at T=0 complement constraints from neutron star properties, which generally pertain to lower densities.
