# The electromagnetic interaction of ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> The validity of a delta-function approximation for the electromagnetic interaction of relativistic heavy ions is investigated. The production of e+e- pairs, with electron capture, is used as a test of the approximation.

## Key claim

The delta-function approximation for the electromagnetic interaction of ultrarelativistic heavy ions yields reasonable results only when the condition \(\omega b / \gamma \lesssim 0.1\) is satisfied, where \(\omega\) is the energy transfer, \(b\) the impact parameter, and \(\gamma\) the Lorentz factor; the abstract omits this quantitative validity condition.

## Method

The author derived the delta-function interaction from the gauge-transformed Lienard-Wiechert potential by taking the limit \(\gamma \rightarrow \infty\) in the integral representation of the interaction, which sets the term \(q_z^2/\gamma^2\) to zero in the denominator. To test the approximation, first-order perturbation theory with plane-wave positron wavefunctions and hydrogenic K-orbital electron wavefunctions was used to calculate bound-free \(e^+e^-\) pair production cross sections and probabilities. The relative difference \(\Delta(\varepsilon)\) between the delta-function and exact results was computed as a function of positron energy \(\varepsilon\) and impact parameter \(b\) for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The delta-function approximation works well for positron energies up to \(\varepsilon \approx 0.1\gamma\, mc^2\); for SPS and RHIC this corresponds to a few MeV, for LHC to a few hundred MeV.
- The approximation fails for positron energies satisfying \(\varepsilon/\gamma \gtrsim 1\), i.e., when \(\xi = \omega/\gamma v\) becomes of order unity.
- The validity condition for impact parameter is \(b \lesssim 0.1\gamma/\omega\); for \(\varepsilon = 1\) MeV the approximation is good up to \(b \simeq 0.1\gamma/\varepsilon\).
- The most effective impact parameters for bound-free pair production are of order \(b \simeq 1/m\) (one electron Compton wavelength).

## Context

This work extends the delta-function interaction formalism developed by Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1991-1995) for electromagnetic interactions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It provides a quantitative validity test using bound-free pair production as a solvable benchmark, complementing earlier work by Bertulani and Baur (1988) on the equivalent photon method. The results enable practitioners to know when the computationally simpler delta-function approximation can be safely applied to processes such as nuclear fragmentation, where typical energy transfers of 10-100 MeV and impact parameters \(b \simeq R \approx 10\) fm satisfy the validity condition for sufficiently large \(\gamma\).
