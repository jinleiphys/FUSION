# The interparticle interaction and noncommutativity of conjugate operators in quantum mechanics. $H$-like atoms

> A quantum mechanical model for the systems consisting of interacting bodies is considered. The model takes into account the noncommutativity of the space and impulse operators and the correlation equations for the indeterminacy of these quantities. The noncommutativity of the operators is here a result of the action of the interparticle forces and represents a natural generalization of the conventional commutation relation for the space and impulse operators for a single particle. The efficiency of the model is demonstrated by specific calculations concerning several well-known atomic systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the coordinate and momentum operators for different interacting particles are noncommutative, with the noncommutativity arising from interparticle forces rather than being an intrinsic property of single particles; the model's validity depends on the precise value of the Rydberg constant, and would be discarded if that constant were only 0.0003 eV larger.

## Method

The authors formulate the NOCE (noncommutativity of operators and correlation equations) model, introducing commutation parameters βkl and correlation factors γkl that modify the standard commutation relations for different particles. For Hydrogen-like atoms, they derive a fifth-degree algebraic equation for the matrix element of the force fnl, which is solved to obtain modified particle masses and energy levels without iteration. The model is applied to calculate energy levels of the 12C H-like atom using experimental ground state energy to determine the correlation factor γ12.

- For 12C H-like atom ground state: experimental energy E10 = −489.9933 eV, yielding γ12 ≈ 0.640255, with ε2 = 11177.292 MeV
- The root x1 = 7.763419586802 × 10−7 for Hydrogen with γ12 = 2.44463 × 10−4, giving f10(1) = 1.027321949956 × 10−9 MeV/fm and Δme c2 ≈ 0.387924 × 10−9 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier ideas from Refs. [1] and [2] (referenced in the paper) about noncommutativity of coordinate and momentum operators for different particles, providing a physical substantiation and introducing correlation equations that enable specific calculations. The model produces energy levels for H-like atoms that depend on both principal quantum number n and orbital quantum number l, in contrast to non-relativistic quantum mechanics where energy depends only on n, and differs from the Dirac theory which depends on total spin j. The approach enables calculation of energy spectra for H-like atoms such as 12C, 24Mg, 40Ar, 52Cr, 64Zn, 84Kr, 98Mo, and 238U, using experimental data from Refs. [15]–[20].
