# Hydrodynamics with spin --- recent developments

> Recent theoretical developments in hydrodynamics of particles with spin 1/2 are briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent theoretical developments in hydrodynamics of spin-1/2 particles, arguing that the spin polarization tensor can be independent of the thermal vorticity, contrary to earlier assumptions that directly connected spin polarization to thermal vorticity.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of a perfect-fluid hydrodynamic framework for spin-1/2 particles based on a generalized local equilibrium density operator that includes the spin polarization tensor as an additional hydrodynamic variable. The framework uses spin-dependent phase-space distribution functions to define the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor, and compares two formulations: the canonical (Noether) form and the GLW (de Groot, van Leeuwen, van Weert) form, showing they are connected by a pseudo-gauge transformation. A semiclassical kinetic equation for Wigner functions is analyzed to derive the hydrodynamic equations, and a classical treatment of spin using an extended phase space with spin four-vectors is also presented.

## Key numbers

- The spin length squared in the particle rest frame is taken as the Casimir operator value for spin-1/2: spin^2 = 3/4.
- The integration measure for the classical spin phase space is normalized so that ∫ dS = 2 for spin-1/2 particles.
- For large spin polarization (P >> 1), the Pauli-Lubanski three-vector in the particle rest frame saturates at |π_*| = spin = √(3/4).
- For small spin polarization (P << 1), the Pauli-Lubanski three-vector in the particle rest frame gives |π_*| = spin^2 * P/3 = P/4.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of spin pioneered by Weyssenhoff (1940s) and builds on the spin polarization measurements of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons by the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2018). The approach was first proposed by Florkowski (2017) and developed in subsequent works, and contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic models by Karpenko (2016) that directly connected spin polarization to thermal vorticity. The review enables future development of viscous hydrodynamics with spin, including dissipation effects and kinetic coefficients related to spin observables, as well as potential application of anisotropic hydrodynamics methods for systems with large spin polarization.
