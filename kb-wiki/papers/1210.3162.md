# The Slater approximation for Coulomb exchange effects in nuclear covariant density functional theory

> The relativistic local density approximation (LDA) for the Coulomb exchange functional in nuclear systems is presented. This approximation is composed of the well-known Slater approximation in the non-relativistic scheme and the corrections due to the relativistic effects. Its validity in finite nuclei is examined by comparing with the exact treatment of the Coulomb exchange term in the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. The relativistic effects are found to be important and the exact Coulomb exchange energies can be reproduced by the relativistic LDA within 5% demonstrated by the semi-magic Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes from proton drip line to neutron drip line.

## Key claim

The relativistic local density approximation (RLDA) for the Coulomb exchange term, which adds relativistic corrections to the traditional non-relativistic Slater approximation, can reproduce exact Coulomb exchange energies from relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory within 5% for semi-magic Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes across the entire neutron range from proton drip line to neutron drip line.

## Method

The authors derived a relativistic LDA for the Coulomb exchange energy functional by starting from the relativistic homogeneous nuclear matter model, obtaining analytic expressions for the exchange energy density that include corrections up to order beta^2 (where beta = (3 pi^2 n_p)^(1/3)/M). They tested this RLDA by comparing its predictions for Coulomb exchange energies and single-particle energies against exact RHFB calculations using the PKA1 effective interaction for the particle-hole channel and D1S for pairing, solving the equations on a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis in a spherical box of radius 20 fm.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic correction improvements range from 4.5% in Pb-182 to 3.5% in Pb-266 when comparing the NR LDA (Slater approximation) to the exact RHFB results
- The relative deviations Delta E_Cex from the relativistic LDA decrease from 3.6% in Pb-182 to 1.7% in Pb-266
- For Pb-208, the RLDA shifts occupied proton states upward by approximately +400 keV for deeply bound states and +100 keV for states near the Fermi surface, while unoccupied bound states shift downward by -200 keV
- The total energy difference between RLDA and RHFB calculations for Pb-208 is -0.97 MeV, mainly due to the Coulomb exchange energy difference of -0.99 MeV
- For the mirror pair O-17 and F-17, the experimental binding energy difference of 3.54 MeV is reproduced as 3.47 MeV by RHFB and 3.33 MeV by RLDA
- For the mirror pair Ni-48 and Ca-48, the RHFB and RLDA calculations give almost identical binding energy differences of 63.44 and 63.38 MeV, respectively, compared to the experimental value of 66.95(53) MeV
- The relativistic corrections to the LDA improve agreement with exact results by 3 to 5%

## Context

This work extends the Slater approximation (Slater 1951), which has been widely used in non-relativistic nuclear DFT calculations including Skyrme (Titin-Schnaider 1974, Skalski 2001, LeBloas 2011) and Gogny (Anguiano 2001) approaches, to the relativistic domain for covariant density functional theory. The study builds on the recent development of density-dependent RHFB theory (Long 2010a, Long 2010b) which treats the Coulomb exchange term exactly, and on earlier work on relativistic LDA for electronic systems (Rajagopal 1973, MacDonald 1979, Engel 2011). The results enable implementation of Coulomb exchange effects in the simpler relativistic Hartree (RMF) framework while preserving its locality, which is important for applications such as isospin symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed beta decays (Liang 2009) and Coulomb displacement energies of mirror nuclei.
