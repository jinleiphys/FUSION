# Kaon flow as a probe of the kaon potential in nuclear medium

> The flow of kaons, i.e., the average kaon transverse momentum as a function of rapidity, and the associated flow parameter in heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies is investigated in the relativistic transport model. It is found that the pattern of kaon flow at the final stage is sensitive to the kaon potential used in the model and is thus a useful probe of the kaon potential in a nuclear medium.

# Kaon flow as a probe of the kaon potential in nuclear medium

## Key claim
The pattern of kaon flow (average transverse momentum versus rapidity) and the associated flow parameter in heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies are sensitive to the kaon potential used in the relativistic transport model, but this sensitivity manifests primarily during the expansion stage of the collision (15–22 fm/c) when the central density is still above normal nuclear matter density, not during the compression stage.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport model (Ko 1987) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 3 fm. Kaon production was treated perturbatively using test particles, with kaon motions governed by the classical equations of motion under three different kaon potentials: a weak repulsive potential from mean-field chiral Lagrangian (Eq. disp1), a medium repulsive potential from impulse approximation using the kaon-nucleon scattering length (Eq. disp2), and a strong repulsive potential from the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model where higher-order corrections cancel the scalar attraction (Eq. disp3). Kaon-nucleon collisions were included with a total cross section of about 10 mb.

## Key numbers
- Kaon flow parameter without kaon potential: approximately 52 MeV (positive, same direction as nucleons)
- Kaon flow parameter with weak repulsive potential (mean-field chiral Lagrangian): approximately 17 MeV (positive, factor of 3 smaller than no-potential case)
- Kaon flow parameter with medium repulsive potential (impulse approximation): approximately -9 MeV (negative, antiflow)
- Kaon flow parameter with strong repulsive potential (NJL model): approximately -86 MeV (negative, large antiflow)
- Central baryon density during the expansion stage when flow changes: from about 2.6 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0) to about 0.8 rho_0
- Kaon-nucleon sigma term used: Sigma_KN approximately 350 MeV
- Kaon decay constant: f_K approximately 93 MeV (assumed equal to pion decay constant f_pi)
- Isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length: a_KN approximately -0.255 fm

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nucleon flow (Danielewicz 1985, 1993; Bass 1993; Li 1994) and pion flow (Bass 1993; Li 1994) in heavy-ion collisions to the case of kaons, which have a large mean free path and are thus less affected by stochastic two-body collisions than pions or antiprotons. The study builds on previous findings (Fang 1994) that an attractive kaon scalar potential is needed to explain subthreshold kaon production yields from the Kaos collaboration at GSI, and that neglecting scalar attraction would underestimate kaon yield by about a factor of three. The results enable experimental flow analysis of kaon data from heavy-ion collisions to distinguish between competing theoretical predictions for the kaon potential in dense nuclear matter, including the mean-field chiral Lagrangian approach (Kaplan and Nelson 1986; Brown 1987; Prakash 1991), the impulse approximation (Brown 1988; Chen 1992), and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Weise 1992).
