# $T$-matrix Analysis of Static Wilson Line Correlators from Lattice QCD at Finite Temperature

> We utilize a previously constructed thermodynamic $T$-matrix approach to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to calculate Wilson line correlators (WLCs) of a static quark-antiquark pair and apply them to the results from 2+1-flavor lattice-QCD (lQCD) computations with realistic pion mass. The self-consistent $T$-matrix results, which include constraints from the lQCD equation of state in the light-parton sector, can describe the lQCD data for WLCs fairly well once refinements of the input parameters are implemented. In particular, the input potential requires less screening than used in previous $T$-matrix analyses. Pertinent predictions for the spectral and transport properties of the QGP are discussed, including the spatial diffusion coefficient for heavy quarks which turns out to have a rather weak temperature dependence, in approximate agreement with recent lQCD results.

## Key claim

The self-consistent thermodynamic \(T\)-matrix approach, when constrained by lattice QCD data for both the equation of state and static Wilson line correlators, can describe the correlator data fairly well, but only after the input potential is refined to exhibit less screening than used in previous \(T\)-matrix analyses, particularly for the confining interaction at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors employed a thermodynamic \(T\)-matrix approach that self-consistently resums ladder diagrams to compute one- and two-body correlation functions for partons in the quark-gluon plasma. They calculated the first cumulant of the static Wilson line correlator (the effective mass \(m_1\)) from the \(T\)-matrix spectral functions and fitted its \(\tau\)-dependence to 2+1-flavor lattice QCD results, iterating the fit with constraints from the lattice QCD equation of state to refine the in-medium screening parameters of the color-Coulomb and confining potentials.

## Key numbers

* The string tension in vacuum was fixed to \(\sigma = 0.225\ \text{GeV}^2\) and the coupling constant to \(\alpha_s = 0.27\), fitted to vacuum lattice QCD free energy data.
* The Debye mass for the confining interaction, \(m_s\), was held constant at \(0.2\ \text{GeV}\) across all temperatures, unlike previous work where it increased with temperature.
* The mixing coefficient for the Lorentz structure of the confining potential was set to \(\chi = 0.8\) (compared to 0.6 in a prior study and 1 in an earlier one).
* The two-body interaction (Luttinger-Ward functional) became the leading contribution to the pressure at \(T = 0.195\ \text{GeV}\), indicating a transition in degrees of freedom.
* The effective light-quark mass was approximately \(0.5\ \text{GeV}\), while collective modes in the light-quark spectral functions appeared near \(\omega \simeq 0.1\ \text{GeV}\) at zero momentum.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic \(T\)-matrix approach of Liu (2017) and Tang (2023) by applying it to static Wilson line correlators from lattice QCD calculations by Bala (2021) and Bazavov (2023), which had previously shown marked disagreement with hard-thermal loop perturbation theory. The refined potential, with weaker screening of the confining interaction, enables improved microscopic predictions for heavy-quark spectral and transport properties, including a spatial diffusion coefficient with weak temperature dependence that approximately agrees with recent lattice QCD results from Altenkort (2023).
