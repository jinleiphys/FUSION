# Femtoscopy of $DN$ and $\bar{D}N$ systems

> The capability of the ALICE@LHC and STAR@RHIC experiments to reconstruct $D$ mesons has enabled femtoscopic correlation measurements of open-charm mesons in both small and large systems. In this work, we present a theoretical calculation of the correlation functions of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with nucleons, based on the Koonin-Pratt formalism. We employ an effective Lagrangian to model the interaction between charmed mesons and baryons and apply the TROY formalism to obtain the off-shell $T$-matrix in coupled channels, incorporating the effect of the Coulomb interaction when the pair involves two charged particles. The resulting full coupled-channel wave function is inserted into the Koonin-Pratt equation with channel weights derived from a thermal model. Additionally, we compute the correlation functions using the Lednický-Lyuboshitz approximation with low-energy scattering parameters extracted from the unitarized amplitudes. We compare these two approaches and provide predictions for different correlated pairs. Our results can be tested against current and future experimental data from the ALICE and STAR collaborations in both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical predictions for femtoscopic correlation functions of D mesons with nucleons, claiming these can be tested against current and future ALICE and STAR experimental data, but the results depend significantly on the underlying interaction model and the approximation used (Lednicky-Lyuboshitz versus full off-shell TROY formalism), with the LL approximation failing to account for coupled-channel effects.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian with vector meson exchange to model the strong interaction between charmed mesons and baryons, using both Weinberg-Tomozawa and Zero-Range approximations for the interaction kernel. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain the off-shell T-matrix in coupled channels, incorporating Coulomb interactions for charged pairs, and then insert the resulting wave function into the Koonin-Pratt equation with channel weights derived from a thermal model at T=171 MeV. For comparison, they also compute correlation functions using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz approximation with low-energy scattering parameters extracted from the unitarized amplitudes, using a Gaussian source profile with radius r0=1 fm for small systems.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff values: kmax=674 MeV for Zero-Range interaction with g=6.0, kmax=752 MeV for Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction with f=1.15f_pi (f_pi=92 MeV)
- DN I=0 scattering length: -0.58 fm (WT), -0.63 fm (ZR); DN I=1 scattering length: -1.55+i0.75 fm (WT), -1.79+i0.32 fm (ZR)
- DbarN I=0 scattering length: 0 fm (WT), 0.02 fm (ZR); DbarN I=1 scattering length: -0.29 fm (WT), -0.34 fm (ZR)
- Thermal model temperature: T=171 MeV for high-multiplicity pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV
- Source radius for small system calculations: r0=1 fm
- Adjusted cutoff for TROY calculations: kmax=630 MeV to reproduce Lambda_c(2595) mass

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of open-charm hadron interactions, following previous analyses of D-pi correlations by Torres-Rincon (2023) and building on the vector meson-exchange model of Hofmann (2005) in the zero-range approximation of Jimenez-Tejero (2009). The results can be compared with experimental data from ALICE (2022) on D-p and D+ antiproton correlations in pp collisions at 13 TeV, and with theoretical predictions from other groups including Haidenbauer (2010), Garcia-Recio (2008), and Yamaguchi (2011). The TROY formalism enables future studies of larger systems as those studied by the STAR collaboration.
