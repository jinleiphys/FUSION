# Heavy pseudoscalar-meson decay constants with strangeness from the extended nonlocal chiral-quark model

> We study the weak-decay constants for the heavy pseudoscalar mesons, D, Ds, B, and Bs. For this purpose, we employ the extended nonlocal chiral-quark model (ExNLChQM), motivated by the heavy-quark effective field theory as well as the instanton-vacuum configuration. In addition to the heavy-quark symmetry and the nonlocal interactions between quarks and pseudoscalar mesons in ExNLChQM, a correction for the strange-quark content inside Ds and Bs is also taken into account and found to be crucial to reproduce the empirical values. From those numerical results, we obtain f_{D,Ds,B,Bs}=(207.53, 262.56, 208.13, 262.39) MeV, which are in good agreement with experimental data and other theoretical estimations. Using those numerical results, we compute the CKM matrix elements and the Cabibbo angle, using various mesonic and leptonic heavy-meson decay channels, resulting in (|V_{cd}|,|V_{cs}|,|V_{ub}|,|V_{td}|/|V_{ts}|)=(0.224,0.968,<5.395*10^{-3},0.215) and theta_C=12.36^o which are well compatible with available data.

## Key claim

The extended nonlocal chiral-quark model (ExNLChQM), which combines instanton-vacuum effects with heavy-quark effective field theory, can reproduce the weak-decay constants of heavy pseudoscalar mesons including strangeness, but only if a phenomenological correction factor for the strange-quark content (derived from the empirical kaon decay constant) is introduced to compensate for omitted nonlocal and meson-loop contributions.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian in Minkowski space for the SU(5) flavor sector (u,d,s,c,b) by extending the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM) with heavy-quark effective field theory (HQEFT). The heavy-meson weak-decay constants are computed via an external-field method using a momentum-dependent effective quark mass parameterization, with the heavy-quark renormalization scale \(\Lambda_Q\) fitted to reproduce the non-strange heavy-meson data. A phenomenological factor \(\mathcal{N}_s = 1.71\), calibrated from the empirical ratio \(f_K/f_\pi\), is applied to account for strangeness effects that would otherwise require explicit nonlocal contributions and \(1/N_c\) meson-loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- \(f_D = 207.53\) MeV, \(f_B = 208.13\) MeV, computed with \(\Lambda_q = 600\) MeV, \(\Lambda_Q = 1000\) MeV, and \(\mathcal{N}_{u,d} = 1\).
- \(f_{D_s} = 262.56\) MeV, \(f_{B_s} = 262.39\) MeV, computed with the same parameters but \(\mathcal{N}_s = 1.71\).
- Ratio \(f_{D_s}/f_D = 1.27\) and \(f_{B_s}/f_B = 1.26\).
- Extracted CKM matrix elements: \(|V_{cd}| = 0.224\), \(|V_{cs}| = 0.968\), \(|V_{ub}| < 5.395 \times 10^{-3}\), \(|V_{td}|/|V_{ts}| = 0.215\), and Cabibbo angle \(\theta_C = 12.36^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous ExNLChQM study (Nam 2011) from non-strange heavy mesons (\(D\), \(B\)) to include strange heavy mesons (\(D_s\), \(B_s\)) for the SU(5) flavor sector. The results are compared with numerous theoretical approaches including lattice QCD (Becirevic 1998, ElKhadra 1997, Aoki 1997, Blossier 2009), light-cone formalism (Hwang 2010), light-front quark model (Choi 2007), field-correlator method (Badalian 2007), Bethe-Salpeter method (Cvetic 2004, Wang 2005), and relativistic quark model (Ebert 2006). The computed decay constants enable extraction of CKM matrix elements, and the author notes that future experimental measurements of \(f_{B_s}\) could discriminate between models, as the present result (262.39 MeV) is higher than most other theoretical predictions.
