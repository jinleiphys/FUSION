# Extracting the Omega- electric quadrupole moment from lattice QCD data

> The Omega- has an extremely long lifetime, and is the most stable of the baryons with spin 3/2. Therefore the Omega- magnetic moment is very accurately known. Nevertheless, its electric quadrupole moment was never measured, although estimates exist in different formalisms. In principle, lattice QCD simulations provide at present the most appropriate way to estimate the Omega- form factors, as function of the square of the transferred four-momentum, Q2, since it describes baryon systems at the physical mass for the strange quark. However, lattice QCD form factors, and in particular GE2, are determined at finite Q2 only, and the extraction of the electric quadrupole moment, Q_Omega= GE2(0) e/(2 M_Omega), involves an extrapolation of the numerical lattice results. In this work we reproduce the lattice QCD data with a covariant spectator quark model for Omega- which includes a mixture of S and two D states for the relative quark-diquark motion. Once the model is calibrated, it is used to determine Q_Omega. Our prediction is Q_Omega= (0.96 +/- 0.02)*10^(-2) efm2 [GE2(0)=0.680 +/- 0.012].

# Extracting the Omega- electric quadrupole moment from lattice QCD data

## Key claim

The Omega- electric quadrupole moment is predicted to be Q_Omega = (0.96 +/- 0.02) x 10^(-2) e fm^2 [G_E2(0) = 0.680 +/- 0.012], but this extraction depends on an extrapolation of lattice QCD data from finite Q^2 down to Q^2 = 0 using a covariant spectator quark model that assumes meson cloud dressing is not significant for the Omega- system.

## Method

The authors used a covariant spectator quark model for the Omega- that includes a mixture of an S state and two D states (D3 with core spin 3/2 and D1 with core spin 1/2) for the relative quark-diquark motion. They calibrated the five adjustable model parameters (momentum scale parameters alpha_S, alpha_D3, alpha_D1 and admixture coefficients a, b) by fitting to unquenched lattice QCD data from Alexandrou et al. (2010) for G_E0, G_M1, and G_E2 form factors, plus a single quenched lattice data point for G_M3 from Boinepalli et al. (2009). The calibrated model was then used to determine the form factors at Q^2 = 0.

## Key numbers

- Q_Omega = (0.96 +/- 0.02) x 10^(-2) e fm^2 [G_E2(0) = 0.680 +/- 0.012], the predicted electric quadrupole moment of the Omega- extracted from lattice QCD data via the covariant spectator quark model
- D-state admixture coefficients from the fit: a = 0.0341 (D3 state, 0.11%) and b = 0.2666 (D1 state, 6.6%), with momentum scale parameters alpha_S = 0.1793, alpha_D3 = 0.5394, alpha_D1 = 0.4674
- Chi-squared values from the fit: chi^2(G_E0) = 5.28, chi^2(G_M1) = 2.66, chi^2(G_E2) = 0.203, chi^2(G_M3) = 2.49, chi^2(total) = 2.58
- The magnetic octupole moment was estimated as O_Omega = (1.27 +/- 0.04) x 10^(-2) e fm^3
- The directly extracted value from lattice QCD data assuming an exponential dependence (Alexandrou et al., 2010) corresponds to (1.18 +/- 0.12) x 10^(-2) e fm^2, compared to the present result of (0.96 +/- 0.02) x 10^(-2) e fm^2

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to spin 1/2 baryons and the Delta baryon (Ramalho and Pena, earlier works) to include D-state admixtures for the Omega-, enabling extraction of the electric quadrupole form factor at Q^2 = 0. The result is consistent with the existing range of predictions (0.4 to 4.0) x 10^(-2) e fm^2 from various formalisms including quark models (Gershtein 1981, Isgur 1982), chiral perturbation theory (Butler 1994, Geng 2009), and large-N_c limit (Buchmann 2002, 2007). The method provides an alternative to simple analytical extrapolation forms (dipole, tripole, exponential) and yields information about the microscopic wavefunction structure, including D-state admixture percentages. Future lattice QCD simulations for G_M3 are needed to confirm or disprove the predicted large D1 state mixture of 6.6%.
