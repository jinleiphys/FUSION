# Thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter with three-body forces

> We calculate thermodynamic quantities in symmetric nuclear matter within the self-consistent Green's functions method including three-body forces. The thermodynamic potential is computed directly from a diagrammatic expansion, implemented with the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials and the Urbana three-body forces. We present results for entropy and pressure up to temperatures of 20 MeV and densities of 0.32 fm^-3. While the pressure is sensitive to the inclusion of three-body forces, the entropy is not. The unstable spinodal region is identified and the critical temperature associated to the liquid-gas phase transition is determined. When three-body forces are added we find a strong reduction of the critical temperature, obtaining T_c ~ 12 MeV.

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-body forces in self-consistent Green's functions calculations of symmetric nuclear matter strongly reduces the critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition, from approximately 18-20.5 MeV to 11.5-12.5 MeV, while leaving the entropy largely unaffected.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled set of T-matrix ladder-diagram equations for the nucleon self-energy and single-particle Green's function, using the CD-Bonn and Nijmegen nucleon-nucleon potentials with and without the Urbana three-nucleon potential. Three-body forces were included via an effective two-body interaction obtained by averaging over the third particle, with two parameters fixed to reproduce the correct saturation density and binding energy. The grand canonical potential was computed directly from its diagrammatic expansion, and the pressure was obtained without numerical derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for CD-Bonn potential alone: T_c = 18 MeV; with three-body forces: T_c = 12.5 MeV
- Critical temperature for Nijmegen potential alone: T_c = 20.5 MeV; with three-body forces: T_c = 11.5 MeV
- Critical density for CD-Bonn alone: rho_c = 0.107 fm^-3; with three-body forces: rho_c = 0.096 fm^-3
- Critical density for Nijmegen alone: rho_c = 0.094 fm^-3; with three-body forces: rho_c = 0.088 fm^-3
- Critical pressure for CD-Bonn alone: P_c = 0.43 MeV fm^-3; with three-body forces: P_c = 0.14 MeV fm^-3
- Critical pressure for Nijmegen alone: P_c = 0.50 MeV fm^-3; with three-body forces: P_c = 0.15 MeV fm^-3
- Dimensionless parameter P_c/(rho_c T_c) for CD-Bonn alone: 0.22; with three-body forces: 0.12
- Dimensionless parameter P_c/(rho_c T_c) for Nijmegen alone: 0.26; with three-body forces: 0.14
- Calculations performed up to temperatures of 20 MeV and densities of 0.32 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent Green's functions calculations (Soma 2006, Soma 2008) by systematically including three-body forces and mapping the liquid-gas phase transition. The results with two-body forces alone agree with other microscopic calculations: Rios (2008) found T_c = 18.5 MeV for CD-Bonn using a similar Green's functions approach, while Baldo and Ferreira (1998) obtained T_c approximately 21 MeV for Argonne V18 using the Bloch-de Dominicis formalism. However, in contrast to the present work, Baldo and Ferreira found that three-body forces did not strongly affect the critical temperature (reducing it only to approximately 20 MeV). The strong reduction of T_c found here is closer to values from other approaches (9-12 MeV from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and Bloch-de Dominicis calculations). The results enable more reliable extrapolations of the nuclear equation of state to the densities and temperatures relevant for core-collapse supernovae and heavy-ion collisions.
