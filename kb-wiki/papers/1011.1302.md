# Axial and electromagnetic observables of hyperons in 2-flavor chiral perturbation theory

> Two-flavor chiral expansions provide a useful perturbative framework to study hadron properties. Such expansions should exhibit marked improvement over the conventional three-flavor chiral expansion. Although in principle one can formulate two-flavor theories for the various hyperon multiplets, the nearness of kaon thresholds can seriously undermine the effectiveness of such two-flavor theories in practice. We investigate the importance of virtual kaon thresholds on hyperon properties, specifically their isovector axial charges and electromagnetic observables. In particular we uncover the underlying expansion parameter governing the description of virtual kaon thresholds. For spin-half hyperons, this expansion parameter is under theoretical control. As a result, the virtual kaon contributions are well described in the two-flavor theory by terms analytic in the pion mass-squared. For spin three-half hyperons, however, one is closer to the kaon production threshold, and the expansion parameter is not as small. Breakdown of $SU(2)$ chiral perturbation theory is shown to arise from a pole in the expansion parameter associated with the kaon threshold. We find that, despite the fact that higher-order corrections to the expansion parameter is necessary to ascertain whether the two-flavor theory of spin three-half hyperons remains perturbative, there is a useful perturbative expansion for isovector axial charges and magnetic moments of both spin-half and spin three-half hyperons.

## Key claim
Two-flavor chiral perturbation theory provides a useful perturbative expansion for isovector axial charges and magnetic moments of both spin-half and spin three-half hyperons, despite the fact that for spin three-half hyperons the expansion parameter associated with virtual kaon thresholds is not as small and higher-order corrections are necessary to ascertain whether the theory remains perturbative.

## Method
The authors formulate a two-flavor (SU(2)) chiral perturbation theory for hyperons by expanding about the SU(2) chiral limit, where strange quark mass dependence is absorbed into low-energy constants or appears as power-law suppressed terms. They derive the expansion parameter governing virtual kaon threshold contributions, ε_{BB'} = (½ m_π²) / (½ m_{η_s}² - δ_{BB'}²), and evaluate its size for various strangeness-changing baryon transitions. They then compute the non-analytic functions arising from kaon loop diagrams contributing to hyperon isovector axial charges and electromagnetic observables, and compare the SU(2) expansion of these functions to the full SU(3) results.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter ε_{SU(2)} = m_π² / m_{η_s}² ≈ 0.04, where m_{η_s} = 0.688 GeV is the mass of the quark basis s̄s meson.
- For ΔS = -1 virtual transitions, the expansion parameters include: ε_{NΣ*} = 0.24, ε_{ΛΞ*} = 0.15, ε_{ΞΩ} = 0.08, ε_{ΣΞ*} = 0.08, ε_{NΣ} = 0.05, ε_{ΛΞ} = 0.05, ε_{NΛ} = 0.04, ε_{ΔΣ*} = 0.04, ε_{Σ*Ξ*} = 0.04, ε_{Ξ*Ω} = 0.04, and ε_{ΣΞ} = 0.04.
- For ΔS = 1 virtual transitions: ε_{ΛΔ} = 0.04, ε_{ΞΣ*} = 0.04, and ε_{ΣΔ} = 0.04.
- Using lattice data and phenomenology, the worst-case expansion parameters are bounded as 0.31 ≤ ε_{NΣ*} ≤ 0.37 and 0.16 ≤ ε_{ΛΞ*} ≤ 0.18.
- The isovector axial charges from lattice data are approximately g_A ≈ 1.2, g_{ΣΣ} ≈ 0.8, and g_{ΞΞ} ≈ 0.2.

## Context
This work extends the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory framework for hyperons developed by Tiburzi and Walker-Loud (2008, 2009) and Jiang, Tiburzi, and Walker-Loud (2009, 2010) to systematically assess the impact of virtual kaon thresholds. It contrasts with the conventional three-flavor SU(3) chiral expansion, which has a generic expansion parameter ε ~ m_η / M_B ≈ 0.5. The results enable reliable pion mass extrapolations of lattice QCD data for hyperon observables computed at the physical strange quark mass, as demonstrated by comparison with lattice data from Lin et al. (2007). The paper also notes a parallel with Ledwig et al. (2010) regarding singularities in moments and radii of resonances near threshold.
