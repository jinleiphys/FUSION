# No-Core Monte Carlo Shell Model Calculations with Unitary Correlation Operator Method and Similarity Renormalization Group

> The unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) and the similarity renormalization group theory (SRG) are compared and discussed in the framework of no-core Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) calculations for $^{3}$H and $^{4}$He. The treatment of spurious center-of-mass motion by Lawson's prescription is performed in the MCSM calculations. These results with both transformed interactions show good suppression of spurious center-of-mass motion with proper Lawson's prescription parameter $β_{\rm c.m.}$ values. The UCOM potentials obtains faster convergence of total energy for the ground state than that of SRG potentials in the MCSM calculations, which differs from the cases in the no-core shell model calculations (NCSM). This differences are discussed and analyzed in terms of the truncation scheme in the MCSM and NCSM, as well as the properties of potentials of SRG and UCOM.

## Key claim

In no-core Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) calculations for ³H and ⁴He, the UCOM-transformed potential yields faster convergence of the ground-state total energy than the SRG-transformed potential, which is the opposite of the trend observed in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations; this difference arises from the different truncation schemes used in MCSM (major-shell truncation) versus NCSM (single-particle excitation energy truncation).

## Method

The authors performed no-core MCSM calculations for the ground states of ³H and ⁴He using both SRG-transformed and UCOM-transformed two-nucleon interactions derived from the N³LO potential, within a harmonic oscillator basis truncated at e_max = 3. Spurious center-of-mass motion was suppressed using Lawson's prescription with a parameter β_cm, and the total intrinsic ground-state energies were computed as functions of the oscillator parameter ħω for model spaces e_max = 1, 2, and 3.

## Key numbers

- For ³H with e_max = 3 and ħω = 28 MeV, the expectation value E_cm of the center-of-mass Hamiltonian falls below 50 keV for both V_SRG and V_UCOM when β_cm > 20.
- For ⁴He with e_max = 3 and ħω = 36 MeV, the expectation value E_cm falls below 30 keV for both potentials when β_cm > 20.
- For ³H, the ground-state energy variation across ħω from 16 to 52 MeV is about 4 MeV for both potentials.
- For ⁴He, the ground-state energy variation across ħω from 16 to 52 MeV is about 5 MeV for both potentials.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of SRG and UCOM potentials from NCSM calculations (Roth 2008) into the MCSM framework, using the same N³LO starting interaction. The finding that UCOM converges faster than SRG in MCSM, opposite to the NCSM result, is explained by the different truncation schemes: MCSM uses major-shell truncation, which better captures the correlations present in UCOM, while NCSM uses excitation-energy truncation, which favors the pre-diagonalized structure of SRG. This enables future MCSM calculations to choose the more suitable transformed interaction for their truncation scheme.
