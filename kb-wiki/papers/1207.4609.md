# Resumming soft and collinear contributions in deeply virtual Compton scattering

> We calculate the quark coefficient function Tq(x,xi) that enters the factorized amplitude for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) at all order in a soft and collinear gluon approximation, focusing on the leading double logarithmic behavior in x +/- xi, where x +/- xi is the light cone momentum fraction of the incoming/outgoing quarks. We show that the dominant part of the known one loop result can be understood in an axial gauge as the result of a semi-eikonal approximation to the box diagram. We then derive an all order result for the leading contribution of the ladder diagrams and deduce a resummation formula valid in the vicinity of the boundaries of the regions defining the energy flows of the incoming/outcoming quarks, i.e. x = +/- xi. The resummed series results in a simple closed expression.

## Key claim

The authors show that the dominant double-logarithmic contributions to the DVCS quark coefficient function near the boundaries \(x = \pm \xi\) can be resummed to all orders in \(\alpha_s\), yielding a simple closed-form expression; this resummation is valid only in the vicinity of these boundaries where the Mandelstam variables become parametrically small compared to \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors work in a light-like axial gauge (\(p_1 \cdot A = 0\)) and analyze the quark coefficient function \(T^q(x,\xi)\) for deeply virtual Compton scattering. They identify that in the limit \(x \to \xi\), the dominant contributions arise from ladder diagrams with strong ordering of longitudinal and transverse momenta, and they extract the leading double-logarithmic behavior \(\alpha_s^n \ln^{2n}(\xi-x)/(x-\xi)\) using a semi-eikonal approximation. The analysis proceeds by examining one-loop diagrams via Ward identities to isolate the box diagram as the source of the double logarithm, then generalizes to \(n\)-loop ladder diagrams to derive a resummation formula.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop coefficient function contains terms of order \([\log^2(\xi \pm x)]/(x \pm \xi)\) in the vicinity of the boundaries \(x = \pm \xi\), as shown in Eq. (C1) where the leading double-logarithmic term is \(\frac{e_q^2 \alpha_s C_F}{4\pi (x-\xi+i\varepsilon)} \log^2\!\left(\frac{\xi-x}{2\xi}-i\varepsilon\right)\).
* The resummed series for the leading contribution of ladder diagrams results in a simple closed expression (not explicitly given in the truncated text), with the one-loop finite part from the gluonic pole contribution yielding \(I^{\rm fin.}_{\rm E.L.V.,\,g} = -4\frac{2\pi i}{x-\xi}\frac{1}{2!}\log^2(\xi-x)\).
* The kinematic ordering for the dominant contributions requires \(x \sim \xi \gg |\beta_1| \sim |x-\xi| \gg |\beta_2| \gg \cdots\) and \(|k_{\perp 1}^2| \ll |k_{\perp 2}^2| \ll \cdots \ll |k_{\perp n}^2| \ll s \sim Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the resummation techniques previously applied to threshold singularities in deep inelastic scattering (Sterman 1986, Catani 1989) to the non-forward kinematics of DVCS, where the presence of two distinct momentum fractions \(x \pm \xi\) creates a two-scale problem near the boundaries between the ERBL and DGLAP regions. The resummed coefficient function enables more precise extraction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from future high-precision DVCS data expected from JLab 12 GeV and COMPASS-II, going beyond the leading-logarithmic treatment of both evolution equations and coefficient functions.
