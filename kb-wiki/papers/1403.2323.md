# Renormalization of the jet-quenching parameter

> We study the radiative processes that affect the propagation of a high energy gluon in a dense medium, such as a quark-gluon plasma. In particular, we investigate the role of the large double logarithms corrections, $\simα_s \ln^2 L/τ_0$, that were recently identified in the study of $p_\perp$-broadening by Liou, Mueller and Wu. We show that these large corrections can be reabsorbed in a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter controlling both momentum broadening and energy loss. We argue that the probabilistic description of these phenomena remains valid, in spite of the large non-locality in time of the radiative corrections. The renormalized jet-quenching parameter is enhanced compared to its standard perturbative estimate. As a particular consequence, the radiative energy loss scales with medium size $L$ as $L^{2+γ}$, with $γ=2\sqrt{α_s N_c /π}$, as compared to the standard scaling in $L^2$.

## Key claim

The large double logarithmic corrections to momentum broadening, ∼α_s ln²(L/τ₀), can be reabsorbed into a renormalization of the jet-quenching parameter that controls both momentum broadening and energy loss, with the renormalized parameter enhanced compared to its standard perturbative estimate, provided the probabilistic description remains valid despite the large non-locality in time of the radiative corrections.

## Method

The authors calculate the dominant radiative correction to the 2-point function describing gluon propagation through a dense medium modeled as a random color field, using the eikonal approximation and a path integral formalism. They evaluate the correction to the dipole cross-section and the jet-quenching parameter from soft gluon emission, then verify that the same renormalized parameter appears in the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum and the splitting kernel for the in-medium QCD cascade in the large-Nc limit.

## Key numbers

* The radiative energy loss scales with medium size L as L^(2+γ), where γ = 2√(α_s N_c/π), compared to the standard scaling in L².
* The double logarithmic corrections are of order ∼α_s ln²(L/τ₀), where L is the medium length and τ₀ ∼ 1/T in a quark-gluon plasma.
* The jet-quenching parameter in the independent multiple scattering approximation is given by q̂(p²) ≃ 4π α_s² N_c n ln(p²/m_D²), where m_D is the Debye screening mass.
* The typical branching time for medium-induced gluon radiation is τ_br ≡ √(ω/q̂), and the formation time is τ_f ∼ ω/k_⊥².
* The mean energy lost through radiation is parametrically ⟨ω⟩ ≃ α_s C_R q̂ L².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Liou, Mueller and Wu (2013) who identified large double logarithmic contributions to p⊥-broadening, and builds upon the authors' previous paper Blaizot and Mehtar-Tani (2013) where double logarithmic corrections emerged in the transport equation describing parton propagation. The paper demonstrates that the renormalization of the jet-quenching parameter is universal, applying equally to momentum broadening and energy loss, and enables resummation of leading radiative corrections for phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collisions. A possible evolution of the jet-quenching parameter was first discussed by Casalderrey-Solana (2007).
