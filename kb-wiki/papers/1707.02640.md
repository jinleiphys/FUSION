# Rapidity dependence of proton cumulants and correlation functions

> The dependence of multi-proton correlation functions and cumulants on the acceptance in rapidity and transverse momentum is studied. We find that the preliminary data of various cumulant ratios are consistent, within errors, with rapidity and transverse momentum independent correlation functions. However, rapidity correlations which moderately increase with rapidity separation between protons are slightly favored. We propose to further explore the rapidity dependence of multi-particle correlation functions by measuring the dependence of the integrated reduced correlation functions as a function of the size of the rapidity window.

## Key claim

The preliminary STAR data on proton cumulant ratios at √s = 7.7 GeV are consistent with rapidity- and transverse-momentum-independent correlation functions within experimental errors, but rapidity correlations that moderately increase with rapidity separation between protons are slightly favored over constant correlations.

## Method

The authors analyze preliminary STAR data on proton cumulants K₂, K₃, and K₄ in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 7.7 GeV, converting cumulants into integrated multi-particle correlation functions (couplings c₂, c₃, c₄). They test whether the data are consistent with constant reduced correlation functions across rapidity and transverse momentum, and then explore a quadratic ansatz for the rapidity dependence of the correlation functions, cₙ(y₁,...,yₙ) = cₙ⁰ + γₙ × (mean squared rapidity difference), to constrain the coefficients γ₂, γ₃, and γ₄.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 7.7 GeV, the two-proton reduced correlation function from STAR preliminary data is approximately described by c₂(y₁−y₂) = c₂⁰ + γ₂(y₁−y₂)² with γ₂ ∼ 2×10⁻².
- From the constant correlation fit to STAR data at |y|<0.5 and 0.4<pₜ<2 GeV, the extracted couplings are c₂⁰ ≈ −1.1×10⁻³, c₃⁰ ≈ −1.7×10⁻⁴, and c₄⁰ ≈ 7.3×10⁻⁵.
- For the quadratic rapidity-dependent fits, favored values are γ₂ = 10⁻² (with c₂⁰ ≈ −2.8×10⁻³), γ₃ = 10⁻³ (with c₃⁰ ≈ −3.4×10⁻⁴), and γ₄ = 2×10⁻⁴ (with c₄⁰ ≈ 3.9×10⁻⁵).
- In the full-acceptance limit (all baryons detected), the couplings are determined solely by total baryon number B: c₂ = −1/B, c₃ = 2/B², c₄ = −6/B³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bzdak and Koch (2016) on extracting multi-proton correlations from cumulant measurements, and responds to preliminary STAR observations (Luo 2015, STAR presentations 2017) of a rising two-proton correlation function with rapidity separation. The paper enables future experimental measurements of the integrated reduced correlation functions cₙ(Δy) as a function of rapidity window size, which would directly constrain the rapidity dependence of three- and four-proton correlations and help resolve whether the observed anti-correlations in rapidity are consistent with cluster formation scenarios or other dynamical effects.
