# Analysis of the 16C(d,p)17C reaction from microscopic 17C wave functions

> We present a semi-microscopic study of the 16C(d,p)17C transfer reaction. The 17C overlap integrals and spectroscopic factors are obtained from a microscopic cluster model, involving many 16C+n configurations. This microscopic model provides a fair description of the 17C bound-state energies. The 16C+d scattering wave functions are defined in the CDCC method, where the deuteron breakup is simulated by pseudostates. The transfer cross sections are in good agreement with recent data. We confirm the 16C(2+)+n structure of the ground state, and show that deuteron breakup effects have a significant influence on the cross sections. We study the 17C(p,d)16C reverse reaction and suggest that the cross section to the 2+ state should be large. A measurement of the ground-state cross section would provide a strong test of the microscopic wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the 16C(d,p)17C reaction cross section to the 17C ground state is small due to its dominant 16C(2+)+n structure, but the authors hedge that a specific measurement of this ground-state cross section would provide a strong test of the microscopic wave functions, and they suggest the reverse 17C(p,d)16C reaction as an alternative probe.

## Method

The authors performed a semi-microscopic study of the 16C(d,p)17C transfer reaction using 17C overlap integrals and spectroscopic factors obtained from a microscopic cluster model (Resonating Group Method, RGM) with many 16C+n configurations. The 16C+d scattering wave functions were defined using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, where deuteron breakup was simulated by pseudostates. Transfer cross sections were computed without adjustable parameters, using the post representation for the transfer scattering matrix.

## Key numbers

- 17C ground state (3/2+) binding energy: -0.734 MeV; spectroscopic factor in the 16C(0+)+n channel: S = 5.2 × 10^-3; rms radius: 2.61 fm
- 17C first excited state (1/2+) binding energy: -0.517 MeV; spectroscopic factor in the 16C(0+)+n channel: S = 0.942; rms radius: 2.75 fm
- 17C second excited state (5/2+) binding energy: -0.402 MeV; spectroscopic factor in the 16C(0+)+n channel: S = 0.562; rms radius: 2.60 fm
- Ground-state spectroscopic factor in the dominant (I,ℓ) = (3/2,2) channel of the 16C(2+)+n configuration: S = 0.972
- 16C core rms radius: 2.42 fm (from shell model with oscillator parameter b = 1.6 fm)
- Experimental 16C+d elastic scattering energy: 24 MeV/nucleon (center-of-mass energy 42.67 MeV)
- Experimental 16C(d,p)17C reaction center-of-mass energy: 30.58 MeV
- Predicted ground-state 16C(d,p)17C cross section around θ = 0°: approximately 0.1 mb, compared to 10-20 mb for excited states
- Predicted 17C(p,d)16C(2+) cross section at center-of-mass energy 29.1 MeV: approximately 10 mb at small angles

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RGM description of 17C from Descouvemont (2000) and Thompson and Descouvemont (2010) to analyze recent 16C(d,p)17C experimental data from Pereira-Lopez et al. (2020). The semi-microscopic approach, developed in Descouvemont (2022), improves upon standard DWBA methods (Satchler, 1983) by using fully antisymmetric microscopic wave functions without adjustable spectroscopic factors. The results enable future measurements of the reverse 17C(p,d)16C reaction to test the microscopic wave functions, particularly for the small 16C(0+)+n component of the ground state.
