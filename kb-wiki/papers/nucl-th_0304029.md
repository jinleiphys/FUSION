# The alpha decay of deformed superheavy elements

> The interaction potential between the alpha particle and the deformed parent nucleus was used for description of the decay of superheavy nuclei. It consists of centrifugal, nuclear and Coulomb parts suitably modified for deformed nuclei. The significant effect of various shapes of barriers obtained for deformed parent nuclei on calculated alpha half-life times were shown. The finally calculated half-life times due to the spontaneous alpha decay for superheavy elements were compared with the results obtained from other models and the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the deformation of the parent nucleus in the alpha decay model significantly affects the calculated half-lives of superheavy elements, and that the best agreement with experimental data is obtained when deformation is taken into account, though the results for some isotopes (e.g., Z=114) still show substantial discrepancies.

## Method

The authors constructed a total interaction potential between the alpha particle and the deformed parent nucleus, consisting of Coulomb, nuclear, and centrifugal terms modified for axially symmetric deformation. The Coulomb potential was calculated using a multipole expansion for deformed charge distributions, the nuclear potential was computed using the proximity approximation with a universal function, and the barrier penetration probability was obtained via the WKB action integral. Half-lives were calculated as a function of the emission angle theta and then averaged over the nuclear surface using a surface weighting function.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus $^{264}Hs_{156}$, the difference in barrier maxima between the lowest and highest orientations is $\Delta V_{MAX} = 1.7$ MeV; a difference of only $\Delta V_{MAX} = 0.5$ MeV gives approximately a factor of 10 difference in half-life.
- The alpha decay energy for $^{264}Hs_{156}$ is $Q_{\alpha} = 10.57$ MeV.
- The largest difference in entrance energy (sticking configuration) as a function of angle is $\Delta V_{ENT} = 2.5$ MeV.
- The energy of the first excited 2+ state for $^{264}Hs_{156}$ is $E_{2+} = 46.6$ keV.
- For the isotope $^{289}114$, the calculated half-life logarithm is $\log(T_{1/2}) = -1.30$ s, while the Viola-Seaborg formula gives $\log(T_{1/2}) = 4.86$ s and experimental data indicate $\log(T_{1/2}) = 1.32$ s.
- For the isotope $^{288}114$, the calculated half-life logarithm is $\log(T_{1/2}) = -2.86$ s, the Viola-Seaborg formula gives $\log(T_{1/2}) = 2.80$ s, and the experimental value is $\log(T_{1/2}) = 0.26$ s.
- The preformation probability (spectroscopic factor) for the alpha particle is taken as $S_{\alpha} = 6.3 \times 10^{-3}$ from prior work by Blendowske, Fliessbach and Walliser (1996).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of deformed superheavy nuclei near doubly magic $^{270}Hs$ by Sobiczewski et al. (1991) and uses equilibrium deformations from Moller (1995). The calculated half-lives are compared with the phenomenological Viola-Seaborg formula (Viola and Seaborg, 1966; Moller, 1997), the Generalized Liquid Drop Model (GLDM), and experimental data from Oganessian (2002) and others. The results show that including deformation significantly increases half-lives compared to spherical calculations, and the method enables systematic predictions for superheavy elements Z=108-114 without relying on phenomenological formulas.
