# Form Factor Effects in the Direct Detection of Isospin-Violating Dark Matter

> Isospin-violating dark matter (IVDM) provides a possible mechanism to ameliorate the tension among recent direct detection experiments. For IVDM, we demonstrate that the results of direct detection experiments based on neutron-rich target nuclei may depend strongly on the density dependence of the symmetry energy which is presently largely unknown and controls the neutron skin thickness that reflects the relative difference of neutron and proton form factors in the neutron-rich nuclei. In particular, using the neutron and proton form factors obtained from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by varying the symmetry energy within the uncertainty region set by the latest model-independent measurement of the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from PREX experiment at JLab, we find that, for IVDM with neutron-to-proton coupling ratio fixed to $f_n/f_p=-0.7$, the form factor effect may enhance the sensitivity of Xe-based detectors (e.g., XENON100 and LUX) to the DM-proton cross section by a factor of $3$ in the DM mass region constrained by CMDS-II(Si) and even by more than an order of magnitude for heavy DM with mass larger than $80$ GeV, compared with the results using the empirical Helm form factor. Our results further indicate that the form factor effect can significantly modify the recoil spectrum of Xe-based detectors for heavy IVDM with $f_n/f_p=-0.7$.

## Key claim

For isospin-violating dark matter (IVDM) with neutron-to-proton coupling ratio fixed to \(f_n/f_p = -0.7\), the form factor effect from realistic neutron and proton distributions, controlled by the largely unknown density dependence of the symmetry energy, can enhance the sensitivity of Xe-based detectors to the DM-proton cross section by a factor of 3 in the DM mass region constrained by CDMS-II(Si), and by more than an order of magnitude for heavy DM with mass larger than 80 GeV, compared to results using the empirical Helm form factor.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for finite nuclei, varying the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L(\rho_c)\) at subsaturation density \(\rho_c \approx 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\) to produce three parameter sets (Lc40, Lc92, Lc134) that span the uncertainty region of the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb measured by the PREX experiment at JLab. They then computed proton and neutron form factors from the resulting density distributions and used the standard formalism for spin-independent direct detection, including the effective form factor that weights proton and neutron contributions by the isospin-violation factor \(g_{\rm np} = f_n/f_p\), to evaluate the degradation factor relating the DM-nucleon cross section to the DM-proton cross section for Si and Xe targets.

## Key numbers

- The PREX measurement gives a neutron skin thickness for \(^{208}\)Pb of \(\Delta r_{\rm np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm, assuming a point-proton rms radius of 5.45 fm.
- The SHF parameter set MSL1 with \(L(\rho_c) = 92.4^{+41.8}_{-51.9}\) MeV predicts \(\Delta r_{\rm np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm for \(^{208}\)Pb and \(\Delta r_{\rm np} = 0.28^{+0.12}_{-0.14}\) fm for \(^{132}\)Xe.
- For IVDM with \(f_n/f_p = -0.7\), the form factor effect enhances the sensitivity of Xe-based detectors to the DM-proton cross section by a factor of 3 in the DM mass region constrained by CDMS-II(Si) and by more than an order of magnitude for DM mass larger than 80 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isospin-violating dark matter (IVDM) by incorporating realistic nuclear form factors from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, going beyond the empirical Helm form factor commonly used in direct detection analyses (Helm, 1956; Lewin and Smith, 1996). It builds on the finding by Frandsen et al. (2013) that IVDM with \(f_n/f_p = -0.7\) can reconcile the tension between CDMS-II(Si) and XENON100, and on the work of Chen et al. (2011) and Co' et al. (2012) showing that using separate proton and neutron distributions can significantly affect DM-nucleus scattering. The results demonstrate that the poorly known density dependence of the symmetry energy, which controls neutron skin thickness, introduces a substantial uncertainty in the interpretation of direct detection experiments for neutron-rich targets, and that future measurements of neutron distributions (e.g., from PREX-II) can help reduce this uncertainty.
