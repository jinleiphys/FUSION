# Study of Neutron Star Properties under the Two-Flavor Quark NJL Model

> The Equation of State (EOS) of matter within neutron stars is a central topic in nuclear physics and astrophysics.This study investigates hadron-quark hybrid stars by integrating the density-dependent DDME2 relativistic mean-field model for hadronic matter with a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter.A quintic polynomial interpolation is employed to construct a smooth ($C^2$ continuity) and thermodynamically consistent crossover between the phases.We systematically explore the parameter space to reconcile the tension between the high stiffness required by massive pulsars and the softness demanded by tidal deformability and radius constraints.Our analysis demonstrates that to simultaneously satisfy the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 and the compact radius constraints from NICER (e.g., PSR J0437-4715), the hadron-quark crossover must initiate in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density.This result suggests that the early percolation of quark degrees of freedom is a necessary feature to accommodate current multi-messenger observations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that to simultaneously satisfy the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (approximately 2.08 solar masses) and the compact radius constraints from NICER (e.g., PSR J0437-4715), the hadron-quark crossover in hybrid stars must initiate in the vicinity of nuclear saturation density, implying that early percolation of quark degrees of freedom is a necessary feature for current multi-messenger observations.

## Method
The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by combining the density-dependent DDME2 relativistic mean-field model for hadronic matter with a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, including scalar and vector interaction channels. A quintic polynomial interpolation with C2 continuity is used to create a smooth crossover between the hadronic and quark phases, with boundary conditions ensuring continuity of pressure, baryon number density, and baryon number susceptibility. The parameter space of the quark vector coupling constant (G_V), the phase transition endpoint (BU), and the scalar coupling factor (G_S Lambda^2) is systematically explored to satisfy constraints from massive pulsars, tidal deformability from GW170817, and NICER radius measurements.

## Key numbers
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: M approximately 2.08 solar masses, used as a high-mass constraint requiring stiff EOS
- Tidal deformability constraint: Lambda_1.4 less than or equal to 800 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, from GW170817
- NICER radius range: approximately 11-13 km for stars near 1.4 solar masses, from observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0437-4715
- DDME2 saturation density: n_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3}, used as reference unit for crossover boundaries
- DDME2 nuclear saturation properties: binding energy E/A = -16.14 MeV, incompressibility K = 251 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.3 MeV
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass m_u = m_d = 5.50 MeV, three-momentum cutoff Lambda = 664.3 MeV, fiducial scalar coupling G_S Lambda^2 = 1.970 (corresponding to constituent quark mass M approximately 335 MeV in vacuum)

## Context
This work extends the "three-window" approach to hybrid star construction from Baym (2018) and Kojo (2015), using a smooth crossover rather than a first-order Maxwell construction to avoid EOS softening that prevents supporting massive neutron stars. The study builds on the DDME2 model from Lalazissis (2005) and the NJL model framework from Buballa (2005), and it directly addresses the tension between high-density stiffness required by massive pulsars like PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019, Salmi 2024) and intermediate-density softness demanded by tidal deformability from GW170817 (Abbott 2017, 2018, 2019) and compact radii from NICER (Riley 2019, Bogdanov 2019). The results enable future work on constraining the onset density of quark matter and the role of vector interactions in hybrid star EOS.
