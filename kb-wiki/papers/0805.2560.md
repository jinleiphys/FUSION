# Triaxiality in the interacting boson model

> The signature splitting of the $γ$-vibrational band of several Ru, Pd, Xe, Ba, Os and Pt isotopes is analyzed in the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM). The nuclei studied are close to the $γ$-unstable SO(6) limit of the IBM and have well-known $γ$ bands. It is shown that in most nuclei the signature splitting is better reproduced by the inclusion of a three-body interaction between the $d$ bosons. In none of the nuclei evidence for a stable, triaxial ground-state shape is found.

# Triaxiality in the interacting boson model

## Key claim

The signature splitting of the γ-vibrational band in near-SO(6) nuclei is better reproduced by including a three-body interaction between d bosons in the IBM-1 Hamiltonian, but in none of the 16 nuclei studied is evidence found for a stable, triaxial ground-state shape, the triaxial minimum that develops in the potential energy surface exists only precariously for χ=0 and quickly disappears when χ acquires non-zero values.

## Method

The authors performed least-squares fits of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian with up to two-body interactions (using the extended consistent-Q formalism) to the ground-state and γ-band energies of 16 Ru, Pd, Xe, Ba, Os, and Pt isotopes, then added a three-body d-boson interaction (primarily the L=3 term B̂₃†·B̃₃) while keeping the χ parameter fixed from E2 transition data. The signature splitting S(J) was computed from the γ-band energies to quantify the even-odd staggering, and the classical limit of each fitted Hamiltonian was obtained via coherent-state formalism to extract the potential energy surface V(β,γ) and assess triaxiality.

## Key numbers

- For ¹¹⁰Ru: rms deviation σ improved from 39 keV (two-body only) to 20 keV (with v₃ = -28.1 keV)
- For ¹¹²Ru: rms deviation σ improved from 55 keV (two-body only) to 38 keV (with v₃ = -46.8 keV)
- For ¹¹⁴Pd: rms deviation σ improved from 91 keV (two-body only) to 61 keV (with v₃ = -94.6 keV)
- For ¹¹⁶Pd: rms deviation σ improved from 85 keV (two-body only) to 56 keV (with v₃ = -84.3 keV)
- For ¹⁰⁸Ru: rms deviation σ increased from 23 keV (two-body only) to 45 keV when adding the L=3 term, but improved to 19 keV when using the L=2 term (v₂ = +30.5 keV)
- The three-body interaction strength v₃ = -50 keV was used in schematic SO(6) calculations with N=10 bosons, κ = -50 keV, κ' = 5 keV, εd = λd = 0
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β ≈ 1.2 was found for Ru isotopes from the classical limit

## Context

This work extends the analysis of γ-band staggering in near-SO(6) nuclei previously studied by Casten and von Brentano (1985) for Xe and Ba isotopes in the A~130 region, and by Stefanescu et al. (2007) for Ru isotopes. The authors systematically test whether the three-body d-boson interaction (first proposed by Heyde (1984) and applied by Casten (1985) to Xe/Ba and ¹⁹⁶Pt) can reproduce the observed signature splitting patterns without introducing stable triaxial deformation. The results enable future studies to distinguish between γ-soft and rigid-triaxial interpretations of γ-band data, and establish that the L=3 cubic term provides a partial dynamical symmetry that leaves ground-state and β bands solvable while modifying γ-band energies.
