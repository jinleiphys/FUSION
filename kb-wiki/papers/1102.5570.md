# Nuclear mass systematics and nucleon-removal thresholds; application to 17-Na

> A survey of known threshold excitations of mirror systems suggests a means to estimate masses of nuclear systems that are uncertain or not known, as does a trend in the relative energies of isobaric ground states. Using both studies and known mirror-pair energy differences, we estimate the mass of the nucleus 17-Na and its energy relative to the p+16-Ne threshold. This model-free estimate of the latter is larger than that suggested by recent structure models.

# Nuclear mass systematics and nucleon-removal thresholds; application to 17-Na

## Key claim

Using two model-free systematics, threshold excitation energies of mirror systems and trends in isobaric ground-state energy differences, the authors estimate that the ground state of 17-Na lies 3.66 ± 0.5 MeV above the p+16-Ne threshold, which is larger than the 2.4–2.7 MeV predicted by recent structure models (Timofeyuk and Descouvemont 2010; Fortune, Lacaze, and Sherr 2010).

## Method

The authors compiled nucleon-removal threshold energies (Th(nX) and Th(pY)) for mirror pairs using the Ame2003 mass compilation, plotting them as a function of core mass number A for isospins T ≤ 2. They also applied a semi-empirical formula for ground-state gap energies ε(Z,A) of light-mass isobars (masses 6–20) relative to a stable base nucleus, using that formula to extrapolate the mass of 17-Na from the trend in mirror ground-state energy differences D_T.

## Key numbers

- The n+16-C threshold lies 0.728 MeV below the 17-C ground state; combining this with a Δ(Th) estimate of ~4 MeV for the 17-C/17-Na mirror pair yields a 17-Na ground state ~3.3 MeV above the p+16-Ne threshold.
- The ground-state gap energy ε(11,17) for 17-Na is estimated as 25.5 ± 0.5 MeV above the 17-O ground state, leading to an atomic mass-energy of 17.03752 ± 0.00054 u.
- The resulting p+16-Ne threshold energy relative to the 17-Na ground state is 3.66 ± 0.5 MeV.
- Previous structure models predicted centroid energies of 2.4 MeV (width 1.36 MeV) by Timofeyuk and Descouvemont (2010) and 2.7 MeV (width 2.2 MeV) by Fortune, Lacaze, and Sherr (2010).
- An earlier mass formula (Kelson 1966) gave a mass excess of 35.61 MeV for 17-Na, corresponding to a p+16-Ne threshold of 3.65 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of mirror-pair threshold energies and isobaric ground-state energy differences previously studied by Kelson (1966) and Anagnostatos (1985), who gave isovector Coulomb energies for A=17 of ~4 MeV. The results provide a model-free cross-check on microscopic structure calculations of the proton-unstable nucleus 17-Na by Timofeyuk and Descouvemont (2010) and Fortune, Lacaze, and Sherr (2010), suggesting those models may underestimate the ground-state energy relative to the proton emission threshold. The estimated mass enables future experimental searches for 17-Na resonances in p+16-Ne scattering, analogous to studies of 15-F via 14-O-p scattering (Goldberg 2004; Guo 2005).
