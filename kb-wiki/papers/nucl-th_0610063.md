# Freeze Out Process with In-Medium Nucleon Mass

> We investigate the kinetic freeze out scenario of a nucleon gas through a finite layer. The in-medium mass modification of nucleons and it's impact on the freeze out process is studied. A considerable modification of the thermodynamical parameters temperature, flow-velocity, energy density and particle density has been found in comparison with evaluations which use a constant vacuum nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating a density- and temperature-dependent in-medium nucleon mass (rather than a constant vacuum mass) substantially modifies the thermodynamical parameters of the kinetic freeze-out process in a nucleon gas, causing a faster freeze-out for all quantities. This result is conditional on the specific QCD sum-rule parameterization of the nucleon mass shift and the assumption of immediate re-thermalization within the finite freeze-out layer.

## Method

The authors model kinetic freeze-out through a finite time-like layer using a hydrodynamical approach, solving coupled differential equations for temperature, flow velocity, energy density, and particle density. The in-medium nucleon pole mass is computed from QCD sum rules at finite density and temperature, using the scalar and vector self-energy contributions parameterized via in-medium condensates in a Fermi gas approximation. The equation of state for the nucleon gas incorporates this in-medium mass, and the system is evolved from pre-freeze-out conditions (T = 150 MeV, n = 1.5 n0, v = 0.5 c) through the layer until the interacting component vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Pre-freeze-out starting conditions: temperature T = 150 MeV, nucleon density n = 1.5 n0 (where n0 = 0.17 fm^-3), flow velocity v = 0.5 c.
- Vacuum nucleon pole mass M_N(0) = 939 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density n0 and zero temperature: scalar self-energy Re Σ_S = -400 MeV, vector self-energy Re Σ_V = +300 MeV (from QCD sum rules); the authors' parameterization yields Re Σ_S = -390 MeV and Re Σ_V = 315 MeV.
- Nuclear binding energy E0 = 16 MeV; compressibility K ≈ 235 MeV.
- Chiral condensate ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ = (-0.250 GeV)^3; nucleon sigma term σ_N ≈ 50 MeV; averaged light quark mass m_q ≈ 5 MeV.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_c ≈ 175 MeV is cited as the upper bound for the freeze-out regime.

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic and hydrodynamical freeze-out models (e.g., the finite-layer approach of Csernai and collaborators, as in the "timelike" and "spacelike" layer formulations) by incorporating in-medium hadron mass modifications, which had not been considered in earlier evaluations. The results suggest that for collision scenarios with high baryonic densities, such as those at the AGS at BNL or the SIS at GSI, in-medium mass shifts should be included to accurately describe the freeze-out process; the authors note that even stronger effects may arise if in-medium modifications are also applied during the preceding hadronization stage.
