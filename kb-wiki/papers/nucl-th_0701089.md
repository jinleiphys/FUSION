# Neutrino Properties, Cosmology

## Key claim
This review summarizes the known properties of neutrinos (masses, mixing angles, mass-squared differences) and their implications for cosmology, but explicitly neglects sterile neutrinos and the LSND anomaly. The author notes that while neutrino oscillations are established, the absolute mass scale, mass hierarchy (normal, inverted, or quasi-degenerate), the value of the mixing angle θ₁₃, and the Dirac or Majorana nature of neutrinos remain undetermined.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental results from solar neutrino experiments (SNO, KamLAND), atmospheric neutrino data, and reactor experiments (CHOOZ) to extract the neutrino mass-squared differences and mixing angles in a two-flavor approximation, then extends to the full 3×3 Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata (PMNS) mixing matrix including CP-violating phases. It discusses the MSW matter effect in the Sun, neutrinoless double beta decay as a probe of Majorana masses, very high energy cosmic-ray neutrinos and the GZK cutoff, and the leptogenesis mechanism for baryogenesis via the seesaw model with heavy right-handed neutrinos.

## Key numbers
- Solar neutrino mass-squared difference: |Δm₁₂²| = (7.9 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁵ eV²
- Solar mixing angle: sin²θ₁₂ = 0.30 ± 0.08 (θ₁₂ ≈ 36.9°)
- Atmospheric neutrino mass-squared difference: |Δm₂₃²| = (2.6 ± 0.5) × 10⁻³ eV²
- Atmospheric mixing angle: sin²θ₂₃ = 0.52 ± 0.2 (2θ₂₃ ≈ 90°)
- CHOOZ limit on θ₁₃: sin²θ₁₃ ≤ 0.006 +0.03 −0.006
- Tritium beta decay limit on electron neutrino mass: m_νe ≤ 2.2 eV
- WMAP + Galactic Redshift Survey sum of neutrino masses: Σ m_i ≤ 0.70 eV
- Moscow-Heidelberg limit on neutrinoless double beta decay half-life for ⁷⁶Ge: T₁/₂ ≥ 1.9 × 10²⁵ y, corresponding to m_ν(e) ≤ 0.35 eV
- Subgroup of Moscow-Heidelberg collaboration measured half-life (0.7–4.2) × 10²⁵ y, implying m_ee = (0.2–0.6) eV
- GZK cutoff energy for protons: ~4 × 10¹⁹ eV
- Seesaw model estimate: m_ν ~ v²/M_R ~ 10⁻² eV for M_R ~ 10¹⁵ GeV and v ~ 100 GeV
- Observed baryon asymmetry: (B − B̄)/n_γ ~ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰

## Context
This review summarizes the state of neutrino physics as of 2007, extending the work of SNO (2001–2002) and KamLAND (2002) that established m₂ > m₁, and the atmospheric neutrino results from Super-Kamiokande. It discusses the CHOOZ limit on θ₁₃ and the need for next-generation experiments (Double Chooz, T2KK, MINOS, OPERA) to measure it precisely. The paper connects neutrino properties to cosmology via the WMAP bound on the sum of neutrino masses, the GZK cutoff from Greisen (1966) and Zatsepin and Kuzmin (1966), and the leptogenesis mechanism proposed by Fukugita and Yanagida (1986) as an explanation for the baryon asymmetry of the universe. Open questions include the absolute neutrino mass scale, the mass hierarchy, the value of θ₁₃, CP violation in the neutrino sector, the nature of very high energy cosmic rays above the GZK limit, and the confirmation of neutrinoless double beta decay.
