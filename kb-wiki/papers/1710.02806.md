# X(3872): propagating in a dense medium

> In cold nuclear matter, the shifts in the mass and current-meson coupling as well the vector self energy of the exotic $X(3872)$ are calculated using the diquark-antidiquark current within the framework of the in-medium two-point QCD sum rule. At the rest frame of the medium, the three momentum of the considered particle is fixed to remove the contributions of the particles with negative energy. In the calculations, we include the in-medium condensates of quark-quark, gluon-gluon and quark-gluon. It is observed that, the shift due to the nuclear matter is negative and is about $25\%$ when the saturation density is used. Such shift is considerably large and comparable with the nucleon' mass shift due to the nuclear medium. The negative shift in the current-meson coupling due to nuclear matter is approximately $10\%$. At the saturation density, the vector self energy of the exotic $X(3872)$ state is found to be $Σ_\upsilon=1.31$ GeV. It is shown that the mass, current-meson coupling and vector self energy of $X(3872)$ strongly depend on the density of cold nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The mass of the exotic X(3872) state in cold nuclear matter experiences a negative shift of about 25% at saturation density, a shift comparable to the nucleon mass shift in nuclear medium, based on calculations using a diquark-antidiquark interpolating current within the in-medium two-point QCD sum rule framework at fixed three-momentum of 0.27 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium two-point correlation function for the X(3872) state using a diquark-antidiquark interpolating current with quantum numbers JPC=1++. They performed the calculation in the rest frame of the nuclear medium at fixed three-momentum |p|=0.27 GeV to remove negative-energy particle contributions, and included in-medium quark-quark, gluon-gluon, and quark-gluon condensates. The hadronic and OPE representations were matched through dispersion relations, with Borel transformation and continuum subtraction applied to obtain sum rules for the mass, current-meson coupling, and vector self energy.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift of X(3872) due to cold nuclear matter at saturation density: negative 25%
- Shift in current-meson coupling due to nuclear matter: negative approximately 10%
- Vector self energy of X(3872) at saturation density: Sigma_v = 1.31 GeV
- Saturation nuclear matter density used: rho_N^sat = 0.11^3 GeV^3
- Borel mass parameter working interval: 3 GeV^2 <= M^2 <= 5 GeV^2
- In-medium continuum threshold working interval: 4.1^2 GeV^2 <= s_0^* <= 4.3^2 GeV^2
- Average pole contribution: 56% of total contribution

## Context

This work extends the study of exotic hadron properties from vacuum to cold nuclear matter, representing the first attempt to calculate in-medium properties of non-conventional particles. It builds on the diquark-antidiquark picture of X(3872) proposed by Maiani (2004) and the in-medium QCD sum rule methodology developed by Cohen (1994) and applied to standard hadrons by Azizi (2014) and others. The results are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments and for planned measurements at PANDA at FAIR and NICA collaborations, which aim to study in-medium properties of both standard and exotic hadrons.
