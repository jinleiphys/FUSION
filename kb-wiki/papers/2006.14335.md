# Thermal radiation and inclusive production in the running coupling $k_T$ -- factorization approach

> The characteristics of the thermal radiation are investigated using a two - component model, with the hard component being described by the Color Glass Condensate formalism. The inclusive transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons produced in proton - proton and proton - nucleus collisions at LHC energies and large - $p_T$ are estimated using the running coupling $k_T$ - factorization formula and the solution of the Balitsky - Kovchegov equation. Our results indicate that the thermal term is necessary to describe the experimental data and that the effective thermal temperature has an energy dependence similar to the saturation scale. We demonstrate that the enhancement of the thermal temperature in $pPb$ collisions is consistent with that predicted by the saturation scale.

## Key claim

The thermal component is necessary to describe the inclusive transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons in proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at LHC energies, and the effective thermal temperature exhibits an energy dependence similar to the saturation scale, with the enhancement in pPb collisions consistent with the saturation scale prediction.

## Method

The authors used a two-component model combining a thermal (exponential) term with a hard component calculated using the running coupling k_T-factorization formula and the solution of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, employing the "g1.101" unintegrated gluon distribution set. They fitted the model to LHC data for charged hadron p_T spectra from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations at energies from 0.9 TeV to 13 TeV for pp collisions, and at 5.02 TeV for pPb collisions, with the K-factor as the only free parameter in the CGC component.

## Key numbers

- Effective thermal temperature T_th for pp collisions at 7 TeV: 0.1012(67) GeV (ALICE), 0.1013(37) GeV (ATLAS), 0.1021(55) GeV (CMS)
- Effective thermal temperature T_th for pp collisions at 13 TeV: 0.1075(45) GeV (ALICE), 0.1085(30) GeV (ATLAS)
- Effective thermal temperature T_th for pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV: 0.1053(64) GeV
- Energy dependence fit parameters for T_th(s) = T_0 [sqrt(s/s_0)]^n: T_0 = (5.2 +/- 1.3) x 10^{-2} GeV, n = 0.076 +/- 0.029, with chi^2/nu = 0.566 for nu = 9
- K-factor values range from 7.47(15) at 0.9 TeV (CMS) down to 2.125(19) at 13 TeV (ATLAS)
- p_T^* (where thermal and CGC contributions are equal) for pp collisions at 7 TeV: 0.507 GeV (ALICE), 0.530 GeV (ATLAS), 0.519 GeV (CMS)
- p_T^* for pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV: 0.491 GeV

## Context

This work extends the two-component model approach of Bylinkin (2014), Baker (2017), and Feal (2018) by replacing the phenomenological parametrization of the hard component with a first-principles CGC calculation using the running coupling k_T-factorization formula. It confirms the findings of Kharzeev (2005) that the thermal temperature is proportional to the saturation scale, and supports the results of Gotsman (2019) that a thermal radiation term is necessary even when using CGC-based descriptions. The analysis enables more quantitative studies of thermal radiation in cosmic ray interactions, where p_T^* is estimated to reach about 0.61 GeV at ultrahigh energies.
