# Photoproduction of K* meson off the proton target with the Regge contributions

> We investigate the K* Lambda(1116) photoproduction off the proton target, employing the tree-level Born approximation within the effective Lagrangian approach. We take into account the kappa, K, and K* exchanges in the t channel in addition to the ground-state nucleon- and hyperon-pole contributions in the s and u channels with the gauge-invariant form-factor scheme. The nucleon and hyperon resonances, D_13(2080) and Sigma*(1385) are also included. We take into account the Regge trajectories of the strange-mesons for the t-channel contributions in such a way that the Feynman and Regge propagators are interpolated smoothly for the relevant photon energy region. We provide numerical results for the differential cross sections, which result in qualitatively good agreement with the presently available experimental data from CLAS collaboration at Jefferson laboratory. It turns out that the Feynman-Regge interpolation for the meson exchanges play a critical role in reproducing the data simultaneously for the low and relatively high energy regions. We also compute the total cross sections, momentum-transfer dependence of the differential cross sections in the t-channel, and the photon-beam asymmetry as useful guides for future experiments.

## Key claim

The Feynman-Regge interpolation for strange-meson exchanges in the t channel is critical for simultaneously reproducing the differential cross section data for K*Λ photoproduction across both low and relatively high photon energies (Eγ = 2.15–2.65 GeV), but the model shows sizable underestimates at backward angles and overshoots in the forward-scattering region at high energy.

## Method

The authors employed a tree-level Born approximation within an effective Lagrangian approach, including s-channel nucleon and u-channel hyperon pole contributions (ground-state proton, Λ, Σ, Σ*) plus the nucleon resonance D13(2080) and hyperon resonance Σ*(1385). In the t channel, they included scalar κ(800), pseudoscalar K(495), and vector K*(892) exchanges, using a gauge-invariant form-factor scheme and interpolating between Feynman and Regge propagators for the strange-meson exchanges via a mixing parameter R.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range studied: Eγ = 2.16 to 2.65 GeV for differential cross sections as a function of cosθ
- Cutoff mass for form factors: Λ = 500–550 MeV
- Mixing parameters for Regge interpolation: R_{S,P,V} = 0.45–0.6 (with R_c = 0.0)
- Averaged parameters used: Λ = 525 MeV, R_{S,P,V} = 0.53
- Strong couplings from NSC97a/NSC97f models: g_{KNΛ} = -13.42 (-17.42), g^V_{K*NΛ} = -4.26 (-6.11), g^T_{K*NΛ} = -11.31 (-14.85), g_{κNΛ} = -8.31 (-10.01)
- Magnetic-dipole moment of K*: μ_{K*+} = 2.5
- D13(2080) resonance width: Γ_{D13} ≈ 350 MeV
- Regge trajectory slopes: α'_{t_S} = 0.7/GeV², α'_{t_P} = 0.7/GeV², α'_{t_V} = 0.85/GeV²
- D13 resonance peaks in total cross section at Eγ = 1.8–1.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of K* photoproduction by Zhao (2001), Nam (2005), Oh (2006), and Ozaki (2009), and responds to recent experimental capabilities at LEPS2 (SPring-8) and CLAS12 (Jefferson Laboratory). The authors build on the Regge trajectory approach of Vanderhaeghen (1997) and Corthals (2005, 2006), and apply the Feynman-Regge interpolation prescription previously developed by Nam (2010, 2011) for hyperon photoproduction. The results provide predictions for total cross sections, t-channel momentum-transfer dependence dσ/dt, and photon-beam asymmetry Σ as guides for ongoing and future experiments.
