# Parton and Hadron Correlations in Jets

> Correlation between shower partons is first studied in high $p_T$ jets. Then in the framework of parton recombination the correlation between pions in heavy-ion collisions is investigated. Since thermal partons play very different roles in central and peripheral collisions, it is found that the correlation functions of the produced hadrons behave very differently at different centralities, especially at intermediate $p_T$. The correlation function that can best exhibit the distinctive features is suggested. There is not a great deal of overlap between what we can calculate and what has been measured. Nevertheless, some aspects of our results compare favorably with experimental data.

## Key claim

The correlation between pions in heavy-ion collisions is driven primarily by kinematical correlations among shower partons within a single jet, under the working hypothesis that shower partons are emitted independently except for the momentum constraint that the sum of their momentum fractions cannot exceed 1; the resulting hadronic correlation functions behave very differently at different centralities, especially at intermediate transverse momentum, but the authors note that "there is not a great deal of overlap between what we can calculate and what has been measured."

## Method

The authors use a one-dimensional parton recombination model to compute single-pion and di-pion distributions in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, decomposing the parton distributions into thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), and shower-shower (SS) components. Shower parton distributions are determined from fragmentation functions under the assumption of dynamical independence except for momentum conservation, and the hard parton distribution is scaled by the average number of binary collisions for non-central events. The two-pion correlation function is then constructed from the four-parton recombination formula, with the correlation arising from the nonfactorizability of the shower parton distributions that all originate from the same hard parton.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope parameter for thermal partons: T = 0.305 GeV/c, showing no significant dependence on centrality
- Suppression factor ξ increases from 0.09 at 0-10% centrality to 0.97 at 80-92% centrality
- Thermal normalization C decreases from 21.8 (GeV/c)^(-1) at 0-10% to 2.3 (GeV/c)^(-1) at 80-92% centrality
- Hard parton momentum integration range: k_min = 3 GeV/c to upper limit of 40 GeV/c
- The TS component dominates in the intermediate p_T range 3 < p_T < 9 GeV/c for central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions
- For peripheral (80-92%) collisions, the SS component dominates for all p_T > 3 GeV/c
- The normalized correlation function G_2 shows a dip at p = 2 GeV/c with negative values for 0-10% centrality, but decreases monotonically for 80-92% centrality

## Context

This work extends the parton recombination model previously applied to single-particle inclusive distributions in Au+Au collisions by Hwa and Yang (2003-2004) and to d+Au collisions, now applied to dihadron correlations. The approach differs from the correlation study of Fries, Bass, and Muller (2003) by explicitly breaking hard partons into shower partons rather than treating soft-hard recombination directly. The results enable future differentiation of dynamical correlations from the kinematical baseline established here, and suggest that the function G_2 (similar to Pearson's correlation) is the best quantity for revealing centrality-dependent features of dihadron correlations, though direct comparison with experimental data remains limited due to lack of particle identification in most measurements.
