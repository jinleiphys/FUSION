# Investigation of light scalar resonances at COSY

> The $a_0$(980) and $f_0$(980) resonances are two well established states in the excited meson spectrum. We review the most prominent theoretical models which try to explain the structure of these states. It is discussed whether data from COSY on $a_0$ and $f_0$ production in $pp$, $pn$, $pd$ and $dd$ collisions allow to distinguish between the different approaches. Very promising in this respect seems to be the measurement of the reaction $dd \to (\mathrm{^4He} a_0^0 \to) \mathrm{^4He} π^0η$ which violates isospin conservation and can be related to $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing.

# Investigation of light scalar resonances at COSY

## Key claim
The paper reviews theoretical models for the \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) resonances and argues that measurements at COSY, particularly the isospin-violating reaction \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,\pi^0\eta\), may distinguish between competing interpretations, but only if the cross sections are large enough to be measured, and no key observable has yet been identified to discriminate mesonic molecules, chiral unitarity effects, and \((qq)(\bar{q}\bar{q})\) states.

## Method
The authors review six classes of theoretical models for the light scalar mesons: \(q\bar{q}\) states, tetraquark \((qq)(\bar{q}\bar{q})\) states, mesonic molecules, the chiral unitary approach, semi-bound states, mixing schemes with a scalar glueball, and vacuum scalars. They then examine existing COSY data on \(K^+K^-\) production in \(pp\) and \(pd\) collisions from the COSY-11, MOMO, and ANKE experiments, and provide cross-section estimates for the proposed \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,a_0^0\) reaction based on scaling from measured \(pd \to {}^3\mathrm{He}\,K^+K^-\) data and known branching ratios.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for \(pp \to pp\,K^+K^-\) at \(Q = 17\) MeV: \(1.8 \pm 0.27^{+0.26}_{-0.35}\) nb (COSY-11).
- Total cross section for \(pd \to {}^3\mathrm{He}\,K^+K^-\) at \(Q = 40\) MeV: \(9.6 \pm 1.0\) nb (MOMO).
- Total cross section for \(pd \to {}^3\mathrm{He}\,K^+K^-\) at \(Q = 56\) MeV: \(17.5 \pm 1.8\) nb (MOMO).
- Total cross section for \(pp \to d\,K^+\bar{K}^0\) at \(Q = 44\) MeV: \(45 \pm 6 \pm 16\) nb (ANKE, preliminary), with \(\sim 70\%\) resonant contribution via \(a_0^0/f_0\).
- Estimated cross section for \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,K^+K^-\) at \(Q = 40\) MeV: \(\simeq 0.4\) nb.
- Estimated cross section for \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,f_0 \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,\pi^+\pi^-\): \(1\) to \(4\) nb.
- Estimated cross section for \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,a_0^0\): \(0.05\) to \(0.2\) nb, assuming mixing intensity \(|\xi|^2 \simeq 0.05\).
- Mixing intensity from WA102 data: \(|\xi|^2 = 8 \pm 3\%\).

## Context
This paper extends the long-standing debate on the nature of the \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) resonances, reviewing models from Weinstein (mesonic molecules), Lohse (compact \(K\bar{K}\) states), Achasov (four-quark states), and Close and Kirk (glueball mixing). It builds on earlier proposals by Achasov and others that \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing near the \(K\bar{K}\) threshold can be probed via isospin-violating reactions. The work enables future measurements at the ANKE spectrometer at COSY, with planned upgrades including a frozen-pellet target and a large-acceptance photon detector to study the \(dd \to {}^4\mathrm{He}\,\pi^0\eta\) reaction.
