# Third-order particle-hole ring diagrams with contact-interactions and one-pion exchange

> The third-order particle-hole ring diagrams are evaluated for a NN-contact interaction of the Skyrme type. The pertinent four-loop coefficients in the energy per particle $\bar E(k_f) \sim k_f^{5+2n}$ are reduced to double-integrals over cubic expressions in euclidean polarization functions. Dimensional regularization of divergent integrals is performed by subtracting power-divergences and the validity of this method is checked against the known analytical results at second-order. The complete ${\cal O}(p^2)$ NN-contact interaction is obtained by adding two tensor terms and their third-order ring contributions are also calculated in detail. The third-order ring energy arising from long-range $1π$-exchange is computed and it is found that direct and exchange contributions are all attractive. The very large size of the pion-ring energy, $\bar E(k_{f0})\simeq -92\,$MeV at saturation density, is however in no way representative for that of realistic chiral NN-potentials. Moreover, the third-order (particle-particle and hole-hole) ladder diagrams are evaluated with the full ${\cal O}(p^2)$ contact interaction and the simplest three-ring contributions to the isospin-asymmetry energy $A(k_f)\sim k_f^5$ are studied.

## Key claim

The third-order particle-hole ring energy from one-pion exchange is computed to be very large (approximately -92 MeV at saturation density), but the author explicitly states this result is "in no way representative" of realistic chiral NN-potentials, serving instead as a semi-analytical benchmark for more complex numerical calculations.

## Method

The author evaluates third-order particle-hole ring diagrams for NN-contact interactions of the Skyrme type and for one-pion exchange, reducing the four-loop coefficients in the energy per particle to double-integrals over cubic expressions in euclidean polarization functions. Dimensional regularization is performed by subtracting power-divergences, and its validity is checked against known analytical results at second order. The complete O(p^2) NN-contact interaction is obtained by adding two tensor terms, and their third-order ring contributions are calculated in detail, along with third-order ladder diagrams and three-ring contributions to the isospin-asymmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of third-order particle-hole ring diagrams beyond the simple s-wave contact interaction studied in low-density expansions, applying it to the full O(p^2) Skyrme-type contact interaction and one-pion exchange. Previous work by the same author (Kaiser, 2012) calculated second-order contributions from Skyrme interactions, and ref. [phhole] found that third-order ring diagrams contribute about 1-2 MeV in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density when computed from low-resolution chiral NN-potentials. The semi-analytical results presented here enable benchmarking of more complex numerical calculations that involve multiple partial-wave sums and momentum-space integrations, as recently performed by ref. [jeremy].
