# Stability of the linear chain structure for $^{12}$C in covariant density functional theory on a 3D lattice

> The stability of the linear chain structure of three $α$ clusters for $^{12}$C against the bending and fission is investigated in the cranking covariant density functional theory, in which the equation of motion is solved on a 3D lattice with the inverse Hamiltonian and the Fourier spectral methods. Starting from a twisted three $α$ initial configuration, it is found that the linear chain structure is stable when the rotational frequency is within the range of $\sim$2.0 MeV to $\sim$2.5 MeV. Beyond this range, the final states are not stable against fission. By examining the density distributions and the occupation of single-particle levels, however, these fissions are found to arise from the occupation of unphysical continuum with large angular momenta. To properly remove these unphysical continuum, a damping function for the cranking term is introduced. Eventually, the stable linear chain structure could survive up to the rotational frequency $\sim$3.5 MeV, but the fission still occurs when the rotational frequency approaches to $\sim$4.0 MeV.

## Key claim

The linear chain structure of three alpha clusters in $^{12}$C is stable against bending and fission only within a specific range of rotational frequencies, and the apparent fissions seen at higher frequencies in the undamped calculation arise from occupation of unphysical continuum states rather than genuine nuclear fission.

## Method

The authors solved the cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) equations on a 3D lattice using the inverse Hamiltonian method to avoid variational collapse and the Fourier spectral method to circumvent the Fermion doubling problem. Starting from a twisted three-alpha initial configuration, they performed self-consistent imaginary-time evolution at various rotational frequencies, and introduced a Fermi-type damping function for the cranking term to remove unphysical continuum states with large angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- The linear chain structure is stable for rotational frequencies $\hbar\omega$ in the range of approximately 2.0 MeV to 2.5 MeV without damping, and up to approximately 3.5 MeV with the damping function.
- Fission occurs when the rotational frequency approaches approximately 4.0 MeV, even after removing unphysical continuum.
- The ground state energy of $^{12}$C obtained is $-87.8$ MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value of $-92.2$ MeV.
- The moment of inertia for the linear chain state rotational band is $2.87\ (\text{MeV})^{-1}\hbar^2$ from the 3D lattice calculation, corresponding to $\hbar^2/2\Im = 0.174$ MeV.
- The damping parameters adopted are $r_D = 9$ fm and $a_D = 0.2$ fm for the main calculations.
- The grid spacing is 0.8 fm, with 24 grid points along the $x$ and $y$ axes and 34 along the $z$ axis.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of linear chain states in light nuclei using density functional theories, particularly the nonrelativistic DFT calculations for $^{16}$O by Ichikawa (2011) and for $^{24}$Mg by Iwata (2015), which found similar stabilization by rotation. It also builds on the harmonic oscillator basis CDFT calculations for carbon isotopes by Zhao (2015). The results enable comparison with experimental rotational bands in $^{14}$C reported by Oertzen (2004), Freer (2014), and Yamaguchi (2017), where moments of inertia of $\hbar^2/2\Im = 0.12$ MeV and $0.19$ MeV have been observed.
