# Universal four-component Fermi gas in one dimension

> A four-component Fermi gas in one dimension with a short-range four-body interaction is shown to exhibit a one-dimensional analog of the BCS-BEC crossover. Its low-energy physics is governed by a Tomonaga-Luttinger liquid with three spin gaps. The spin gaps are exponentially small in the weak coupling (BCS) limit where they arise from the charge-density-wave instability, and become large in the strong coupling (BEC) limit because of the formation of tightly-bound tetramers. We investigate the ground-state energy, the sound velocity, and the gap spectrum in the BCS-BEC crossover and discuss exact relationships valid in our system. We also show that a one-dimensional analog of the Efimov effect occurs for five bosons while it is absent for fermions. Our work opens up a very rich new field of universal few-body and many-body physics in one dimension.

## Key claim

A four-component Fermi gas in one dimension with a short-range four-body interaction exhibits a one-dimensional analog of the BCS-BEC crossover, but the authors caution that this terminology should not be taken literally because there is no spontaneous symmetry breaking in one dimension.

## Method

The authors start with a lattice model that realizes the BCS-BEC crossover in one dimension and derive a continuum field-theoretical description in the universal regime near a four-body resonance. They use bosonization to analyze the low-energy effective theory in the BCS limit and the Lieb-Liniger model for the BEC limit of tightly-bound tetramers, and they compute the anomalous dimensions of five-body composite operators to study the Efimov effect.

## Key numbers

- The four-body resonance occurs at \(U/t = 32\pi^3 / \Gamma(1/4)^4 \approx 5.742\) on the lattice.
- The tetramer binding energy in the universal regime is \(E_0 = -\hbar^2/(2ma^2)\).
- In the BCS limit, the spin gaps are \(\Delta_f \propto v_F k_F e^{-\pi^2/(8k_F|a|)} \sin(f\pi/4)\) for \(f=1,2,3\).
- In the BEC limit, the spin gaps are \(\Delta_f \to f/(8ma^2)\) for \(f=1,2,3\).
- For five bosons with four-component resonant interaction, the discrete scaling factor for pentamer bound states is \((E_n/E_{n+1}) = (71.8)^2\).
- For five identical bosons, the scaling factor is \((E_n/E_{n+1}) = (12.4)^2\).
- The Tomonaga-Luttinger parameter in the BCS limit is \(K \to 1 + 6k_F|a|/\pi^2\).
- The sound velocity in the BCS limit is \(v_s \to v_F(1 - 6k_F|a|/\pi^2)\).
- The Tomonaga-Luttinger parameter in the BEC limit is \(K \to 4(1 + \eta \cdot 2k_F a/\pi)\).
- The sound velocity in the BEC limit is \(v_s \to (v_F/4)(1 - \eta \cdot 2k_F a/\pi)\).
- The contact density in the BCS limit is \(\mathcal{C}/k_F^2 \to 16k_F^2 a^2 / \pi^2\).
- The contact density in the BEC limit is \(\mathcal{C}/k_F^2 \to 4/(k_F a) - \eta k_F^2 a^2/(3\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of universal BCS-BEC crossover, previously studied for two-component Fermi gases in three dimensions by Eagles (1969), Leggett (1980), and Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985), to a four-component Fermi gas in one dimension with four-body interactions. It also demonstrates a one-dimensional analog of the Efimov effect for five bosons, which contrasts with the ordinary Efimov effect that occurs only in spatial dimensions between 2.30 and 3.76 as shown by Nielsen (2001). The results enable future studies of universal few-body and many-body physics in one dimension, including determination of the one-dimensional Bertsch parameter and the tetramer-tetramer scattering length.
