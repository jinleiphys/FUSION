# Color neutrality effects in the phase diagram of the PNJL model

> The phase diagram of a two-flavor Polyakov loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is analyzed imposing the constraint of color charge neutrality. Main effects of this constraint are a shrinking of the chiral symmetry breaking (chiSB) domain in the T-mu plane, a shift of the critical point to lower temperatures and a coexistence of chiSB and two-flavor superconducting phases. The effects can be understood in view of the presence of a nonvanishing color chemical potential mu_8, which is necessary to compensate the color charge density rho_8 induced by the nonvanishing Polyakov-loop mean field phi_3.

## Key claim

Imposing color charge neutrality in the two-flavor Polyakov loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model leads to a coexistence region of chiral symmetry breaking (χSB) and two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phases, a shrinking of the χSB domain in the temperature-chemical potential plane, and a shift of the critical endpoint to lower temperatures; these effects are driven by a nonvanishing color chemical potential μ₈ that compensates the color charge density induced by the Polyakov-loop mean field φ₃.

## Method

The authors start from the Euclidean action of the two-flavor PNJL model, including quark-antiquark and quark-quark (diquark) interactions, and couple the fermions to a background SU(3) color gauge field via the Polyakov loop. They perform a standard bosonization and apply the mean-field approximation, keeping nonzero vacuum expectation values for the chiral condensate σ̄, the diquark condensate Δ̄, and the Polyakov-loop variables φ₃ and φ₈. Color neutrality is enforced by introducing color chemical potentials μ₃ and μ₈ and solving the coupled gap equations together with the conditions that the color charge densities ρ₃ and ρ₈ vanish; the analysis assumes φ₈ = 0 and μ₃ = 0, leaving μ₈ as the only nonvanishing color chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Quark current mass m̄₀ = 5.5 MeV, coupling constant G = 10.1 GeV⁻², cutoff Λ = 650 MeV (standard parameter set from Ratti (2005) and Roessner (2006)).
- At T = 0 and low chemical potential, the constituent quark mass is M₀ = 324.1 MeV.
- The critical chemical potential for the first-order chiral restoration transition at T = 0 is μ_c(T=0) = 342 MeV.
- The critical endpoint for the non-superconducting case is located at about (μ_E, T_E) = (333 MeV, 64 MeV) when color neutrality is imposed; without color neutrality, the first-order line extends up to about T_E = 100 MeV.
- At μ = 0, the phase transition temperature is about 255 MeV.
- The diquark gap Δ̄ is of the order of a hundred MeV for most of the temperature range, decreasing rapidly to zero near the phase border at T ∼ 150 MeV.
- The ratio H/G is taken around 0.75 (Fierz value), with additional cases H/G = 0.7 and 0.8 explored.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model analyses of Ratti (2005), Roessner (2006), Sasaki (2006), and Fukushima (2008) by incorporating the constraint of color charge neutrality, which had been neglected in most previous PNJL studies. It builds on the earlier work of Abuki (2008) that considered neutrality constraints only at a fixed chemical potential μ = 500 MeV with massless quarks. The results enable further investigation of the quarkyonic phase (coexistence of confinement and chiral symmetry restoration) proposed by McLerran (2007) for large N_c QCD, and suggest possible observable consequences in heavy-ion collision experiments at future facilities such as FAIR-CBM, NICA-MPD, and J-PARC, including enhanced low-mass dilepton production from diquark-antidiquark annihilation and a BEC-BCS crossover in quark matter.
