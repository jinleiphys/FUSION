# Lattice-based QCD equation of state at finite baryon density: Cluster Expansion Model

> The QCD equation of state at finite baryon density is studied in the framework of a Cluster Expansion Model (CEM), which is based on the fugacity expansion of the net baryon density. The CEM uses the two leading Fourier coefficients, obtained from lattice simulations at imaginary $μ_B$, as the only model input and permits a closed analytic form. Excellent description of the available lattice data at both $μ_B = 0$ and at imaginary $μ_B$ is obtained. We also demonstrate how the Fourier coefficients can be reconstructed from baryon number susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The Cluster Expansion Model (CEM) provides an analytic equation of state for QCD at finite baryon density that has no critical point, meaning any unambiguous signal of the QCD critical point must appear as a deviation from CEM predictions; the model assumes that higher-order Fourier coefficients of the net baryon density can be expressed in terms of the two leading coefficients using Stefan-Boltzmann ratios, neglecting connected three-baryon correlations.

## Method

The authors constructed the CEM by taking the two leading Fourier coefficients \(b_1(T)\) and \(b_2(T)\) from lattice QCD simulations at imaginary chemical potential as input, then setting all higher-order coefficients \(b_k\) for \(k \geq 3\) using the relation \(b_k(T) = \alpha_k^{\rm SB} [b_2(T)]^{k-1} / [b_1(T)]^{k-2}\), where \(\alpha_k^{\rm SB}\) are the Stefan-Boltzmann limit values. The fugacity expansion of the net baryon density was then summed analytically into a closed form involving polylogarithms. The model was tested by comparing its predictions for baryon number susceptibilities \(\chi_2^B\), \(\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B\), \(\chi_6^B/\chi_2^B\), and \(\chi_8^B\) against lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations.

## Key numbers

* The CEM uses the two leading Fourier coefficients \(b_1(T)\) and \(b_2(T)\) from lattice QCD as the only input; these were obtained from simulations at imaginary \(\mu_B\) for temperatures above 135 MeV.
* The Stefan-Boltzmann limit values for the ratios are given by \(\alpha_k^{\rm SB} = 8^{k-1} (3+4\pi^2)^{k-2} / (3+16\pi^2)^{k-1} \cdot (3+4\pi^2 k^2) / k^3\) for \(k = 3,4,\ldots\)
* The CEM predictions for \(\chi_2^B\) and \(\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B\) show quantitative agreement with lattice QCD data from both the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations across the temperature range shown.
* The ratio \(\chi_6^B/\chi_2^B\) exhibits a negative dip in its temperature dependence, which the CEM reproduces despite having no critical point.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fourier coefficients from imaginary \(\mu_B\) lattice QCD simulations reported by Vovchenko (2017) and provides an analytic model that can be used for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density. The CEM contradicts interpretations that the negative dip in \(\chi_6^B/\chi_2^B\) is an unambiguous signature of chiral criticality, as previously suggested by Friman (2011), since the same behavior appears in a model with no critical point. The model enables reconstruction of the leading Fourier coefficients from baryon number susceptibility data, allowing consistency checks between different lattice QCD collaborations (Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD).
