# Relativistic quantum transport coefficients for second-order viscous hydrodynamics

> We express the transport coefficients appearing in the second-order evolution equations for bulk viscous pressure and shear stress tensor using Bose-Einstein, Boltzmann, and Fermi-Dirac statistics for the equilibrium distribution function and Grad's 14-moment approximation as well as the method of Chapman-Enskog expansion for the non-equilibrium part. Specializing to the case of transversally homogeneous and boost-invariant longitudinal expansion of the viscous medium, we compare the results obtained using the above methods with those obtained from the exact solution of the massive 0+1d relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation. We show that compared to the 14-moment approximation, the hydrodynamic transport coefficients obtained by employing the Chapman-Enskog method leads to better agreement with the exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for massive gases with Bose-Einstein, Boltzmann, and Fermi-Dirac statistics, the Chapman-Enskog method yields second-order hydrodynamic transport coefficients that show better agreement with the exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation than the Grad's 14-moment approximation, particularly for bulk viscous pressure evolution.

## Method

The authors derived second-order evolution equations for bulk viscous pressure and shear stress tensor by coarse-graining the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation, using both Grad's 14-moment approximation and the Chapman-Enskog iterative method to determine the non-equilibrium phase-space distribution function. They expressed the resulting transport coefficients in terms of thermodynamic integrals involving modified Bessel functions for Bose-Einstein (a=-1), Boltzmann (a=0), and Fermi-Dirac (a=1) statistics. The transport coefficients were then tested against the exact solution of the massive 0+1d relativistic Boltzmann equation for a transversally homogeneous, boost-invariant longitudinally expanding system.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 300 MeV at initial proper time τ0 = 0.5 fm/c, with particle mass m = 300 MeV and relaxation times τeq = τΠ = τπ = 0.25 fm/c, corresponding to initial η/s = 1/4π
- For the ratio ζ/η scaled by (1/3 - cs²)² in the small-z approximation: Γ = 75 for Boltzmann distribution and Γ = 48 for Fermi-Dirac distribution
- For Bose-Einstein distribution, the scaled ratio ζ/η diverges as ~1/(1+a) in the small-z limit
- Two initial anisotropy configurations were tested: ξ0 = 0 (isotropic, with π0 = Π0 = 0) and ξ0 = 100 (highly oblate, with π0 = 2.86 GeV/fm³ and Π0 = 0.138 GeV/fm³)

## Context

This work extends previous derivations of second-order transport coefficients for massive gases with classical Boltzmann statistics (Denicol 2014, Jaiswal 2014) to include quantum Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics. It builds on the comparison between Grad's 14-moment approximation and the Chapman-Enskog method that was previously demonstrated for classical statistics (Jaiswal 2013, 2014), and uses the exact solution of the 0+1d RTA Boltzmann equation (Florkowski 2014) as a benchmark. The results enable more accurate hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions by providing quantum-statistically correct transport coefficients, particularly for bulk viscous evolution which has been less understood than shear viscous transport.
