# Zemach moments of 3He and 4He

> We use the world data on elastic electron scattering on 3He and 4He to determine the Zemach moments <r>_(2) and <r^3>_(2). These quantities are required to interpret the Lamb shift and HFS data of muonic Helium presently being measured at PSI by the CREMA collaboration. The rms-radii are determined as well.

## Key claim

The paper determines Zemach moments and rms-radii for ³He and ⁴He from world electron scattering data, providing the corrections needed to interpret muonic Helium Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting measurements, but notes that for ³He the spin-1/2 nature and less precise data increase uncertainties compared to ⁴He.

## Method

The author performed Sum-Of-Gaussians (SOG) fits to the world data on elastic electron scattering from ⁴He and ³He, including recently published high-q data for ⁴He. For ³He, both charge (monopole) and magnetic (dipole) form factors were separated using forward- and backward-angle data, with Coulomb distortion corrections applied where needed. The large-radius tail of the density was constrained using the proton separation energy, and model dependence was checked by comparing results from GFMC, Faddeev, and Woods-Saxon potential calculations.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He charge rms-radius: 1.681 ± 0.004 fm
- ⁴He third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩_(2): 16.73 ± 0.10 fm³
- ⁴He fourth moment ⟨r⁴⟩: 14.35 ± 0.11 fm⁴
- ³He first Zemach moment ⟨r⟩_(2): 2.528 ± 0.016 fm
- ³He third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩_(2): 28.15 ± 0.70 fm³
- ³He charge rms-radius: 1.973 ± 0.014 fm
- ³He magnetic rms-radius: 1.976 ± 0.047 fm
- ³He fourth charge moment ⟨r⁴_ch⟩: 32.9 ± 1.60 fm⁴
- ³He–⁴He isotope shift δ⟨r²⟩: 1.066 ± 0.06 fm²

## Context

This work extends the author's previous SOG analysis for ⁴He (Sick 2008) and provides the Zemach moments needed by the CREMA collaboration at PSI for interpreting muonic Helium Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting measurements (Antognini 2011). The results enable comparison with ab-initio calculations for light nuclei (Brida 2011, Sauer 2000) and address the discrepancy between electron scattering and muonic atom radii that was highlighted by the proton radius puzzle (Pohl 2010, Sick 2012). The isotope shift from electron scattering agrees with atomic Helium measurements (Shiner 1995, Van Rooij 2011, Cancio Pastor 2012) but is not precise enough to resolve the several-standard-deviation differences among those atomic values.
