# Deuteron Dipole Polarizabilities and Sum Rules

> The scalar, vector, and tensor components of the (generalized) deuteron electric polarizability are calculated, as well as their logarithmic modifications. Several of these quantities arise in the treatment of the nuclear corrections to the deuterium Lamb shift and the deuterium hyperfine structure. A variety of second-generation potential models are used and a (subjective) error is assigned to the calculations. The zero-range approximation is used to analyze a subset of the results, and a simple relativistic version of this approximation is developed.

## Key claim

The scalar, vector, and tensor components of the deuteron electric polarizability and their logarithmic modifications are calculated using second-generation potential models, with the caveat that the results are non-relativistic and use the impulse approximation for the dipole operator, and that the assigned errors are subjective estimates based on scaling to experimental asymptotic normalization constants.

## Method

The authors compute the twelve polarizabilities and sum rules (scalar, vector, and tensor components of both the electric polarizability tensor and the D-tensor, plus their logarithmic modifications) using Podolsky's method for second-order perturbation theory, solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation in partial waves. Seven second-generation potential models are used: Argonne V18, Reid Soft Core 1993, and five Nijmegen models. The zero-range approximation is employed to analyze a subset of results analytically, and a simple relativistic version of this approximation is developed using the Relativistic Schrödinger Equation.

## Key numbers

- Scalar electric polarizability α_E = 0.6330(13) fm³
- Scalar D-tensor component s (multiplied by 10) = 0.1882(4) fm²
- Scalar α_E × log(2Ē/m_e) = 1.8750(36) fm³
- Scalar D-tensor × log(2Ē/m_e) (multiplied by 10) = 0.6327(12) fm²
- Vector polarizability σ = −0.00092(5) fm³
- Tensor polarizability τ = 0.0317(3) fm³
- Tensor D-tensor component t (multiplied by 10) = 0.01322(10) fm²
- Zero-range result for α_E = 0.6378(12) fm³, using asymptotic S-wave normalization constant A_S = 0.8845(8) fm⁻¹/²
- Zero-range result for D^{αα} = 0.01916(4) fm²
- Experimental asymptotic D/S amplitude ratio η = 0.0253(2)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of deuteron polarizabilities by Friar and collaborators (2004) using modern second-generation potentials, where only three of the twelve quantities had been previously calculated and only two with modern potentials. The scalar polarizabilities had been calculated before and have not changed significantly; the tensor result is nearly 10% smaller than the older calculation by Tauber (1956), reflecting a smaller modern value of η. The vector components of the sum rule play a role in ordinary deuterium hyperfine structure, while the tensor components determine part of the deuterium quadrupole hyperfine structure, for which calculations of this effect have not yet been performed. The results enable improved treatment of nuclear corrections to the deuterium Lamb shift and hyperfine structure.
