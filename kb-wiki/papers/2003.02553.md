# On microscopic theory of pygmy- and giant resonances

> The Green function formalism with a consistent account for phonon coupling (PC), based on the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi-systems, is applied for pygmy- and giant multipole resonances in magic nuclei with the aim to consider particle-hole (ph) and complex 1p1h phonon configurations. A new equation for the effective field, which describes nuclear polarizability, is obtained. It contains new PC contributions which are of interest in the energy region under consideration. They are due to: i)the tadpole effect in the standard ph-propagator, ii)two new induced interactions (caused by the exchange of ph-phonon) in the second ph-channel and in the particle-particle channels, and iii) the first and second variations of the effective interaction in the phonon field. The general expressions for energies and probabilities of transitions between the ground and excited states are obtained. The qualitative analysis and discussion of the new terms are performed.

# On microscopic theory of pygmy- and giant resonances

## Key claim

The paper derives a new equation for the effective field describing nuclear polarizability that includes phonon coupling corrections beyond those previously incorporated only into the particle-hole propagator, specifically the tadpole effect in the standard ph-propagator, two new induced interactions in the second ph-channel and particle-particle channels, and first and second variations of the effective interaction in the phonon field; however, the analysis remains qualitative and no numerical calculations are performed, so the actual importance of these new terms for describing pygmy and giant resonances remains to be tested.

## Method

The authors apply the Green function formalism based on the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) to magic nuclei, considering particle-hole (ph) and complex 1p1h⊗phonon configurations at the g² level of phonon coupling corrections, where g is the phonon creation amplitude. They obtain a new equation for the effective field Ṽ by taking first and second variations of the standard TFFS vertex equation in the phonon field, then iterating to include all g² corrections. The resulting equation contains 12 classes of diagrammatic terms, which are expressed analytically using single-particle Green functions, phonon Green functions, the effective interaction F, and its variations δF and δ²F.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously developed by the Kurchatov Institute group for ground and low-lying states (Khodel and Saperstein, 1982; Sapozhnikov and Tolokonnikov, 2016) into the pygmy dipole resonance and giant multipole resonance energy region, aiming to close the gap between GF approaches applied to low-lying states and those applied to higher-energy resonances. It contrasts with earlier GF approaches for PDR and GMR (Kamerdzhiev, 1983; Tselyaev, 1989; Tselyaev, 2007) that included phonon coupling only into the ph-propagator, and with the time blocking approximation (TBA) and quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA) methods that did not include the tadpole effect in the generalized propagator. The new terms derived here may help explain the observed PDR fine structure in ²⁰⁸Pb that could not be fully reproduced within the latest self-consistent Skyrme functional approach with PC (Lyutorovich, 2018).
