# 6$^+$ isomers in neutron-rich Sn-isotopes beyond N$=82$ and effective interaction

> Recent observation of the 6$^+$ seniority isomers and measurements of the B${(E2)}$ values in the $^{134-138}$Sn isotopes lying close to the neutron drip line have raised some questions about the validity of the currently used effective interactions in the neutron-rich region. Simpson $\it{et}$ $\it{al.}$ [Phys. Rev. Lett. $\textbf{113}$, 132502 (2014)] had to modify the diagonal and non-diagonal $νf_{7/2}^2$ two body matrix elements of the "${Vlk}$" interaction by ${\sim150}$ keV in their shell model calculations in order to explain the data of $^{136}$Sn. In contrast, we are able to explain the observed energy levels and the B${(E2)}$ values after marginal reduction of the same set of matrix elements by ${25}$ keV in the "RCDB" (Renormalized CD-Bonn) interaction. The observed mismatch in reproducing the data of $^{136}$Sn is due to the seniority mixing. Further, we do not find it necessary to consider the core excitations, and the "RCDB" interaction seems better suited to explain the data beyond N$=82$ magic number.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the "RCDB" (Renormalized CD-Bonn) effective interaction, with only a 25 keV reduction of the diagonal and non-diagonal νf²₇/₂ two-body matrix elements, can explain the observed energy levels and B(E2) values in neutron-rich ¹³⁴⁻¹³⁸Sn isotopes, in contrast to the ~150 keV reduction required by Simpson et al. (2014) for the "Vlk" interaction.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed using the Nushell code with the "RCDB" interaction, assuming a doubly-closed ¹³²Sn core (Z=50, N=82) and a valence space of six neutron orbitals [2f₇/₂, 3p₃/₂, 1h₉/₂, 3p₁/₂, 2f₅/₂, 1i₁₃/₂]. The diagonal and non-diagonal νf²₇/₂ matrix elements of the "RCDB" interaction were reduced by 25 keV to create a modified interaction ("RCDBMO"), and the resulting level energies and B(E2) values for the yrast 6⁺ → 4₁⁺ transitions were compared with experimental data for ¹³⁴Sn, ¹³⁶Sn, and ¹³⁸Sn.

## Key numbers

- The νf²₇/₂ matrix elements were reduced by 25 keV in the "RCDB" interaction, compared to the ~150 keV reduction used by Simpson et al. (2014) for the "Vlk" interaction.
- For ¹³⁶Sn, the calculated B(E2; 6⁺ → 4₁⁺) value increased from 14.5 e²fm⁴ to 22.0 e²fm⁴ with the "RCDBMO" interaction, close to the experimental value of 24(4) e²fm⁴.
- For ¹³⁶Sn, the calculated B(E2; 4₁⁺ → 2⁺) value increased from 28.0 e²fm⁴ to 95.3 e²fm⁴ with the "RCDBMO" interaction.
- For ¹³⁸Sn, the calculated B(E2; 6⁺ → 4₁⁺) value increased from 12.3 e²fm⁴ to 16.8 e²fm⁴ with the "RCDBMO" interaction, close to the experimental value of 16(4) e²fm⁴.
- The second 4₂⁺ state in ¹³⁶Sn came down in energy by 41 keV with the "RCDBMO" interaction.

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy reported by Simpson et al. (2014) in Physical Review Letters, who found that the "Vlk" interaction required a ~150 keV modification to reproduce the B(E2) value in ¹³⁶Sn, which otherwise differed from experiment by a factor greater than 10. The present study shows that the "RCDB" interaction (also known as "CWG") requires only a marginal 25 keV reduction, and that the mismatch in ¹³⁶Sn arises from seniority mixing between v=2 and v=4 states rather than from core excitations. This work enables further refinement of effective interactions in the N=82-126 region and suggests that measuring additional B(E2) values in ¹³⁴⁻¹³⁸Sn would provide further tests of these interactions.
