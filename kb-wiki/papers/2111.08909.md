# Hadron-quark Pasta Phase in Massive Neutron Stars

> The structured hadron-quark mixed phase, known as the pasta phase, is expected to appear in the core of massive neutron stars. Motivated by the recent advances in astrophysical observations, we explore the possibility of the appearance of quarks inside neutron stars and check its compatibility with current constraints. We investigate the properties of the hadron-quark pasta phases and their influences on the equation of state (EOS) for neutron stars. In this work, we extend the energy minimization (EM) method to describe the hadron-quark pasta phase, where the surface and Coulomb contributions are included in the minimization procedure. By allowing different electron densities in the hadronic and quark matter phases, the total electron chemical potential with the electric potential remains constant, and local ? equilibrium is achieved inside the Wigner-Seitz cell. The mixed phase described in the EM method shows the features lying between the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions, which is helpful for understanding the transition from the Gibbs construction (GC) to the Maxwell construction (MC) with increasing surface tension. We employ the relativistic mean-field model to describe the hadronic matter, while the quark matter is described by the MIT bag model with vector interactions. It is found that the vector interactions among quarks can significantly stiffen the EOS at high densities and help enhance the maximum mass of neutron stars. Other parameters like the bag constant can also affect the deconfinement phase transition in neutron stars. Our results show that hadron-quark pasta phases may appear in the core of massive neutron stars that can be compatible with current observational constraints.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark pasta phase, a structured mixed phase with geometric configurations, may appear in the core of massive neutron stars and be compatible with current observational constraints, but only when the vector interactions among quarks are sufficiently strong to stiffen the equation of state and when the surface tension is moderate (around 40 MeV fm\(^{-2}\)), as the mixed phase described by the energy minimization method lies between the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions.

## Method

The authors extended the energy minimization (EM) method to describe the hadron-quark pasta phase within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, where the hadronic and quark phases are separated by a sharp interface and particle densities in each phase are taken as uniform. The hadronic matter was described by the relativistic mean-field model with the BigApple parameterization, while the quark matter was described by the MIT bag model with vector interactions (vMIT). By allowing different electron densities in the hadronic and quark phases, the total electron chemical potential including the electric potential remains constant, and local \(\beta\) equilibrium is achieved inside the Wigner-Seitz cell; the equilibrium conditions were derived by minimizing the total energy density under constraints of baryon number conservation and global charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- The BigApple model predicts a maximum neutron-star mass of \(M_{\rm{max}}=2.6 M_\odot\) (saturation density \(n_0=0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\), energy per nucleon \(E_0=-16.34\) MeV, incompressibility \(K=227\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{\rm{sym}}=31.3\) MeV, slope of symmetry energy \(L=39.8\) MeV).
- For the vMIT model with \(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV and vector coupling \(G_V=0.2\) fm\(^2\), the EM method gives onset densities for droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble, and pure quark phases of 0.451, 0.535, 0.594, 0.700, 0.736, and 0.770 fm\(^{-3}\), respectively.
- For the same parameters (\(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV, \(G_V=0.2\) fm\(^2\)), the EM method yields a maximum neutron-star mass of \(2.32 M_\odot\) with a central baryon density of 0.69 fm\(^{-3}\) and a total radius of 13.10 km.
- With \(B^{1/4}=170\) MeV and \(G_V=0.2\) fm\(^2\), the EM method gives a maximum mass of \(2.08 M_\odot\) and a total radius of 12.65 km.
- The surface tension used in the EM calculations is \(\sigma=40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\).
- The vector coupling \(G_V\) was varied in the range 0–0.3 fm\(^2\); the bag constant values used were \(B^{1/4}=170\) MeV and 180 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the energy minimization method previously developed by the same group (Wu et al., 2019; Ju et al., 2021) to include charge screening effects by allowing different electron densities in the hadronic and quark phases, which helps understand the transition from the Gibbs construction (zero surface tension) to the Maxwell construction (large surface tension). The results are compared with the coexisting phases (CP) method of Wu et al. (2019) and with earlier Thomas-Fermi calculations by Endo et al. (2006), Maruyama et al. (2007), and Yasutake et al. (2014). The findings enable more realistic modeling of the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars, allowing future work to test compatibility with multimessenger constraints from GW170817, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the high-mass compact object in GW190814.
