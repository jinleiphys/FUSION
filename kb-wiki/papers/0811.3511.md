# A solvable model which has X(5) as a limiting symmetry and removes some inherent drawbacks

> Solvable Hamiltonians for the $β$ and $γ$ intrinsic shape coordinates are proposed. The eigenfunctions of the $γ$ Hamiltonian are spheroidal periodic functions, while the Hamiltonian for the $β$ degree of freedom involves the Davidson's potential and admits eigenfunctions which can be expressed in terms of the generalized Legendre polynomials. The proposed model goes to X(5) in the limit of $|γ|$-small. Some drawbacks of the X(5) model, as are the eigenfunction periodicity and the $γ$ Hamiltonian hermiticity, are absent in the present approach. Results of numerical applications to $^{150}$Nd, $^{154}$Gd and $^{192}$Os are in good agreement to the experimental data. Comparison with X(5) calculations suggests that the present approach provides a quantitative better description of the data. This is especially true for the excitation energies in the gamma band.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a solvable model for the β and γ intrinsic shape coordinates that reproduces the X(5) critical-point symmetry in the limit of small |γ|, while restoring the periodicity of the γ eigenfunctions and the hermiticity of the γ Hamiltonian, two properties that are violated in the standard X(5) approximation.

## Method

The authors separate the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian by choosing a potential of the form V(β,γ) = V(β) + U(γ)/β², which avoids replacing β² by its expectation value. The β Hamiltonian uses a Davidson potential (β² + β₀⁴/β²), whose eigenfunctions are expressed in terms of generalized Laguerre polynomials. The γ Hamiltonian, after including a singular term 9K²/(4 sin²(3γ)) to cancel the coupling with the rotational degrees of freedom, is transformed via x = cos(3γ) into the differential equation for spheroidal oblate functions, whose solutions are periodic spheroidal functions. Numerical applications are performed for ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵⁴Gd, and ¹⁹²Os.

## Key numbers

- For the Davidson potential in β, the excitation energies are given by E_{n,τ} = 2n + 1 + [(τ + 3/2)² + β₀⁴]^{1/2}, where β₀⁴ is a free parameter determined variationally for each angular momentum.
- For the spheroidal γ Hamiltonian with u₁ = 0, the energy E_{nm} is related to the spheroidal eigenvalue λ_{nm} by E_{nm} = 9 λ_{nm}, with m = K/2 and c² = u₂/9.
- For small c, the asymptotic expansion of the spheroidal energy is E_{nm} ≈ 9n(n+1) − [2(n(n+1)+m²−1)/((2n−1)(2n+3))] u₂ + higher-order terms in u₂².
- For large c, the asymptotic expansion for the spheroidal energy is E_{nm} ≈ −u₂ + 3q√u₂ + 9(m² − (q²+5)/8) − (27q/(64√u₂))(11+q²−32m²), where q = 2(n−m)+1.
- The characteristic values cₙ⁺(0) = n² for the Mathieu equation (K=1 case) at q=0 determine the energy via Eₙ⁺ − u = cₙ⁺ − (10/9)q.

## Context

This work extends the X(5) critical-point symmetry introduced by Iachello (2001) for the U(5)–SU(3) phase transition, which used Bessel functions and an approximate γ Hamiltonian that lost periodicity and hermiticity. The present model builds on earlier solvable treatments of the γ degree of freedom using spheroidal functions (Fortunato, 2005) and on the use of the Davidson potential for β by Bonatsos et al. (2004). The formalism enables a consistent quantum-mechanical description of the γ variable that preserves the symmetries of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian, and it provides a framework for testing whether the X(5) approximation's violations of periodicity and hermiticity cause significant quantitative corrections to energies and E2 transitions.
