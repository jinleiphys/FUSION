# Lorentz contraction, geometry and range in antiproton-proton annihilation into two pions

> We present a geometric interpretation of the so-called annihilation range in reactions of the type $\bar pp \to$ {\em two light mesons} based upon Lorentz effects in the highly relativistic final states ($γ=E_{\mathrm{cm}}/2mc^2\simeq 6.8-8.0$). Lorentz-boosted meson wave functions, within the framework of the constituent quark model, result in a richer angular dependence of the annihilation amplitudes and thus in higher partial wave contributions ($J>1$) than usually obtained. This approach sheds some light on what could be a "{\em short}" annihilation range and how it is influenced by the angular distribution of the final states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the annihilation range in antiproton-proton annihilation into two pions is not a fixed, short distance (like the nucleon Compton wavelength of ~0.1 fm) but instead depends on the emission angle of the pions, because Lorentz contraction of the highly relativistic final-state mesons (γ ≈ 6.8–8.0) introduces a strong angular dependence into the annihilation amplitudes.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator confinement, where the initial antiproton and proton and the final pions are described by Gaussian wave packets. They compute annihilation amplitudes for the ³P₀ and ³S₁ quark-line diagrams, but crucially they Lorentz-boost the pion wave functions from the pion rest frame to the center-of-mass frame, applying the equal-time condition for the quark and antiquark. The resulting amplitudes acquire new relativistic terms that depend on γ and on the angle between the pion momentum and the boost direction, which translates into the center-of-mass scattering angle.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic factor for the final-state pions is γ = E_cm / (2 m_π c²) ≈ 6.8–8.0, corresponding to center-of-mass energies √s = 1.9–2.2 GeV from the LEAR data of Hasan et al. (2005).
- The pion size parameter β is chosen to give the pion a radius r_π = 0.48 fm.
- The annihilation amplitudes from the Lorentz-boosted wave functions can be up to two orders of magnitude larger than the non-relativistic terms, depending on the γ value.
- Partial wave analyses of the LEAR and pre-LEAR data consistently require partial waves with total angular momentum J up to 3 or 4, whereas baryon exchange models give vanishing contributions for J ≥ 1.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the antiproton-proton annihilation range, where baryon exchange models (Shapiro, 1978; Moussallam, 1984; Mull, 1990; Yan, 1996) predict a very short range (~0.1 fm) but fail to reproduce the LEAR data of Hasan et al. (2005) and earlier pre-LEAR experiments (Eisenhandler, 1975; Carter, 1978; Tanimori, 1990). The authors contrast their geometric, Lorentz-contraction-based interpretation with earlier geometric approaches using bag models (Alberg, 1991), which found annihilation peaking at 0.7–0.95 fm, and with the Povh-Walcher method (Povh, 1992) applied by the Bonn group (Haidenbauer, 1992), which gave ranges of 0.5 fm (Paris potential) to 1.1 fm (Bonn potential). The paper enables future work to incorporate relativistic wave-function boosting into quark-model calculations of annihilation observables, and suggests that a single fixed annihilation range is an oversimplification.
