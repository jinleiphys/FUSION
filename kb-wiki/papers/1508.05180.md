# Small-x, Diffraction and Vector Mesons

> This talk discusses recent progress in some topics relevant for deep inelastic scattering at small x. We discuss first differences and similarities between conventional collinear factorization and the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering. Many of the recent theoretical advances at small x are related to taking calculations in the nonlinear saturation regime to next-to-leading order accuracy in the QCD coupling. On the experimental side significant recent progress has been made in exclusive and diffractive processes, in particular in ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

This talk argues that while the dipole picture and collinear factorization are both valid frameworks for describing deep inelastic scattering at small x, the dipole picture is more naturally suited for understanding gluon saturation and provides a more direct relation between inclusive and diffractive cross sections, though it is only applicable at small x with Q^2 not too large but large enough for weak coupling.

## Method

The paper compares the infinite momentum frame (collinear factorization) and target rest frame (dipole picture) descriptions of DIS, discussing how each organizes perturbative QCD calculations. It reviews recent theoretical progress in next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations for the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) and JIMWLK evolution equations, forward particle production in proton-nucleus collisions, and connections between the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework and transverse momentum dependent (TMD) gluon distributions. The experimental discussion focuses on exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s is defined by the condition π R_p^2 Q_s^2 ~ α_s xG(x,Q_s^2), where R_p is the proton radius and xG is the gluon distribution
- For nuclear targets, coherent events dominate for momentum transfers |t| ~ 1/R_A^2, where R_A is the nuclear radius, while incoherent events dominate for |t| ~ 1/R_p^2
- The incoherent cross section for nuclear targets is suppressed by a factor ~3 compared to A times the nucleon cross section (Lappi, 2010)
- The ALICE collaboration measured the Ψ(2S) to J/Ψ photoproduction cross section ratio in γ-nucleus collisions to be approximately twice as large as in γ-proton collisions (Adam, 2015)

## Context

This talk extends the discussion of small-x physics from leading-order BK and JIMWLK evolution to NLO accuracy, building on work by Balitsky (2008), Kovchegov (1999), and Jalilian-Marian (1997). It connects the CGC formalism to TMD gluon distributions following work by Dominguez (2011) and Kovchegov (2013), and discusses how exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral collisions (ALICE, LHCb, CMS) provides constraints on nuclear gluon distributions and fluctuating nuclear geometry. The next steps include resolving the issue of large negative NLO corrections in forward particle production (Stasto, 2013) and developing resummation methods for the NLO BK equation (Iancu, 2015).
