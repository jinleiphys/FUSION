# An anlaysis on $J/ψ\toπ^0γ^*$ within resonance chiral theory

> In this study, we analyze the first measurement of the electron-positron invariant mass spectrum in $J/ψ\to π^0 e^+e^-$ by BESIII, using the framework of resonance chiral theory. Our results indicate that both strong interaction and electromagnetic transition are essential to accurately describe the data. We obtain the $π^0$ transition form factor for $J/ψ\to π^0γ^*$ and the corresponding decay branching ratios for $J/ψ\to π^0 l^+l^-$. The decay process $J/ψ\to π^0 V$ is also examined. It is found that $J/ψ\to π^0 ρ^0$ is dominated by the strong interaction, while the other two channels, $J/ψ\to π^0 ω$ and $π^0 ϕ$, arise primarily from electromagnetic transitions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the BESIII data on the electron-positron invariant mass spectrum in $J/\psi \to \pi^0 e^+e^-$ can be accurately described within resonance chiral theory (RChT) using only three free parameters, but only if both strong interaction and electromagnetic transition contributions are included, with the caveat that the pion transition form factor must be extended beyond its previously validated range using a pQCD-inspired continuation scheme.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian within the framework of U(3) resonance chiral theory that includes pseudoscalar mesons, vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$), the $J/\psi$ charmonium state, and the electromagnetic current. They compute the transition form factor $G_{\psi\pi^0\gamma^*}(q^2)$ as a sum of leading-order strong interaction, electromagnetic transition, and charmonium contributions, then fit the three unknown parameters $g^\psi_{12}$, $h^\psi_{12}$, and $\beta_{\psi\pi^0\gamma^*}'$ to the BESIII $e^+e^-$ invariant mass spectrum data using MINUIT, with the pion transition form factor taken from previous work and extended to high virtuality via a pQCD-motivated continuation.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2_{\mathrm{d.o.f.}} = 0.91$ for the fit to the BESIII $e^+e^-$ invariant mass spectrum data with three free parameters
- $g^\psi_{12} = (-2.6 \pm 3.1) \times 10^{-6}$ (dimensionless coupling parameter)
- $h^\psi_{12} = (-29.5 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-6}$ (dimensionless coupling parameter)
- $\beta_{\psi\pi^0\gamma^*}' = 0.9 \pm 0.7$ (dimensionless parameter for heavier vector resonance contribution)
- $\mathrm{Br}[J/\psi \to \pi^0\gamma] = (3.42 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-5}$, consistent with the PDG value $(3.39 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-5}$
- $\mathrm{Br}[J/\psi \to \pi^0 e^+e^-] = (7.70 \pm 0.57) \times 10^{-7}$, consistent with the BESIII measurement $(8.06 \pm 0.49) \times 10^{-7}$
- $\mathrm{Br}[J/\psi \to \pi^0 \mu^+\mu^-] = (4.80 \pm 0.38) \times 10^{-7}$ (prediction)
- $f_\psi = (0.294 \pm 0.001)$ GeV, fixed from the PDG branching ratio $\mathrm{Br}_{J/\psi\to e^+e^-} = (5.971 \pm 0.032)\%$
- Cutoff scale $s_0 = 2.3^2$ GeV$^2$ for the pion transition form factor continuation

## Context

This work extends previous RChT analyses of charmonium decays by Chen (2014) and Yan (2023) to the $J/\psi \to \pi^0 \gamma^*$ process, using the first BESIII measurement of the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass spectrum. It also builds on the authors' own previous determination of the pion transition form factor (Zhang, 2025). The analysis addresses the long-standing puzzle of $\psi(nS) \to \rho\pi$ decays dating back to Franklin (1983), where recent work by Yan (2023) and Kivel (2023) attributed the observed patterns to destructive interference between strong and electromagnetic amplitudes. The results enable predictions for $J/\psi \to \pi^0 \mu^+\mu^-$ that can be tested in future experiments, and clarify that $J/\psi \to \pi^0 \rho^0$ is dominated by strong interaction while $J/\psi \to \pi^0 \omega$ and $\pi^0 \phi$ arise primarily from electromagnetic transitions.
