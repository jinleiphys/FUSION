# Soft-collinear effective theory for hadronic and nuclear collisions: The evolution of jet quenching from RHIC to the highest LHC energies

> In the framework of soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons, results and predictions for inclusive hadron suppression, based upon in-medium parton shower evolution, are presented for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV and $\sqrt{s}=2.76, \, 5.1$ ATeV, respectively. The $\rm SCET_G$ medium-induced splitting kernels are further implemented to evaluate the attenuation of reconstructed jet cross in such reactions and to examine their centrality and radius $R$ dependence. Building upon a previously developed method to systematically resum the jet shape at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, a quantitative understanding of the jet shape modification measurement in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ ATeV at the LHC can be achieved. Predictions for photon-tagged jet cross sections and shapes, that can shed light on the parton flavor dependence of in-medium parton shower modification, are also given.

## Key claim

Within the soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET_G) framework, the medium-induced splitting kernels can unify the treatment of vacuum and medium-induced parton showers, enabling quantitative predictions for inclusive hadron suppression, jet cross section attenuation, and jet shape modification from RHIC to the highest LHC energies, with the caveat that the coupling between the jet and the medium is constrained to approximately 5% uncertainty from the comparison of full DGLAP evolution to traditional energy loss approaches.

## Method

The author derived medium-modified DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions using SCET_G-based splitting kernels, which are the sum of vacuum and medium-induced components. These evolution equations were solved to obtain medium-evolved fragmentation functions, which were then implemented in phenomenological calculations of hadron suppression in heavy ion collisions. For jet observables, the medium-induced splitting functions were used to generalize the concept of energy loss for jets with reconstruction parameter R beyond the soft gluon approximation, and the jet energy functions were computed including both vacuum and medium contributions.

- Charged hadron R_AA calculated in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV and compared with ATLAS experimental data

- Jet shape modification presented as ratio rho(PbPb)/rho(pp) for inclusive jets with R=0.3 at sqrt(s)=2.76 ATeV, compared to CMS data
- Predictions for charged hadron R_AA in central and mid-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.1 ATeV
- Predictions for differential jet shape modification for inclusive jets (green band) and photon-tagged jets (blue band) with R=0.5 in central Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the soft-collinear effective theory framework (Bauer 2000) by incorporating Glauber gluon modes (Idilbi 2008) to describe jet evolution in strongly-interacting matter, building upon the derivation of O(alpha_s) 1-to-2 medium-induced splitting kernels (Ovanesyan 2011). The approach enables going beyond the traditional energy loss approximation to parton propagation in matter (Kang 2014) and provides quantitative control over uncertainties in the in-medium modification of hadron production cross sections. The predictions for photon-tagged jet cross sections and shapes at LHC Run II energies can shed light on the parton flavor dependence of in-medium parton shower modification.
