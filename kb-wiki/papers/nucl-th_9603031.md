# Sidewise dispersion relations and the structure of the nucleon vertex

> We revisit sidewise dispersion relations as a method to relate the nucleon off-shell form factor to observable quantities, namely the meson-nucleon scattering phase shifts. It is shown how for meson-nucleon scattering a redefinition of the intermediate fields leaves the scattering amplitude invariant, but changes the behavior of the off-shell form factor as expressed through dispersion relations, thus showing representation dependence. We also employ a coupled-channel unitary model to test the validity of approximations concerning the influence of inelastic channels in the sidewise dispersion relation method.

## Key claim

Sidewise dispersion relations cannot uniquely determine the half-off-shell $\pi NN$ form factor from observable meson-nucleon phase shifts, because the off-shell form factor is representation-dependent under field redefinitions that leave on-shell scattering amplitudes invariant.

## Method

The authors revisit the sidewise dispersion relation formalism for the nucleon vertex, showing how a redefinition of the intermediate fields (e.g., the Dyson transformation $\psi \rightarrow \exp(i\beta\gamma_5\phi)\psi$) changes the off-shell behavior of the vertex function $K(q^2,w)$ while leaving the on-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitude unchanged. They then employ a coupled-channel unitary model with $\pi N$ and $\eta N$ channels to test two approximations for the phase of the form factor above inelastic thresholds: the "threshold" approximation (ignoring inelastic contributions) and the "Goldberger-Treiman" approximation (setting $\Re A = 0$ for the inelastic term).

## Key numbers

* The on-shell $\pi NN$ coupling constant is $g$, with the tree-level vertex $\Gamma^5(p',p)_{ps} = g \gamma_5$ in the pseudoscalar representation.
* Under the Dyson transformation with $\beta = -g/2m$, the resulting $\pi NN$ coupling becomes purely pseudovector, with the half-off-shell vertex function $\tilde{K}(q^2, \pm w') = g + \beta(m \mp w')$.
* The inelastic threshold for the $\eta N$ channel is at $w_T$, above which the two-channel unitarity equations involve the $\pi N$ and $\eta N$ T-matrix elements $T_{\pi\pi}$, $T_{\eta\eta}$, and $T_{\pi\eta}$.
* The phase of the form factor below the inelastic threshold is given by $\phi_{\pi} = \delta^l_{\pi}$, the elastic $\pi N$ phase shift, while above threshold the threshold approximation implies $\rho_l^2 = 1$ (unrealistic above $\pi\pi N$ threshold).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of off-shell vertices by Bincer (1960) and Epstein (1970), who proposed using sidewise dispersion relations to relate the nucleon off-shell form factor to meson-nucleon phase shifts. It also connects to studies by Rudy (1994) and Stefan (1994) in chiral perturbation theory, which demonstrated representation dependence of off-shell pion electromagnetic form factors under unitary field transformations. The paper shows that the sidewise dispersion relation method cannot uniquely determine the off-shell form factor, in contrast to the successful use of dispersion relations for the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude at the non-physical point by Hohler (1979) for determining the pion-nucleon sigma term.
