# New parameterization of Skyrme's interaction for regularized multi-reference energy density functional calculations

> [Background] Symmetry restoration and configuration mixing in the spirit of the generator coordinate method based on energy density functionals have become widely used techniques in low-energy nuclear structure physics. Recently, it has been pointed out that these techniques are ill-defined for standard Skyrme functionals, and a regularization procedure has been proposed to remove the resulting spuriosities from such calculations. This procedure imposes an integer power of the density for the density dependent terms of the functional. At present, only dated parameterizations of the Skyrme interaction fulfill this condition. [Purpose] To construct a set of parameterizations of the Skyrme energy density functional for multi-reference energy density functional calculations with regularization using the state-of-the-art fitting protocols. [Method] The parameterizations were adjusted to reproduce ground state properties of a selected set of doubly magic nuclei and properties of nuclear matter. Subsequently, these parameter sets were validated against properties of spherical and deformed nuclei. [Results] Our parameter sets successfully reproduce the experimental binding energies and charge radii for a wide range of singly-magic nuclei. Compared to the widely used SLy5 and to the SIII parameterization that has integer powers of the density, a significant improvement of the reproduction of the data is observed. Similarly, a good description of the deformation properties at $A\sim 80$ was obtained. [Conclusions] We have constructed new Skyrme parameterizations with integer powers of the density and validated them against a broad set of experimental data for spherical and deformed nuclei. These parameterizations are tailor-made for regularized multi-reference energy density functional calculations and can be used to study correlations beyond the mean-field in atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper constructs new Skyrme energy density functional parameterizations (SLyIII.xx) that have an integer power of the density (α=1), making them suitable for regularized multi-reference calculations, while achieving significantly better reproduction of experimental binding energies and charge radii than the widely used SLy5 and the older SIII parameterization that also has integer density powers.

## Method

The authors adjusted the Skyrme functional parameters using a merit function that minimizes weighted squared residuals against nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy per nucleon, symmetry energy, Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule enhancement factor), the neutron matter equation of state from Wiringa et al., binding energies and charge radii of six doubly-magic nuclei, and spin-orbit splittings in 208Pb. They constructed four parameter sets with isoscalar effective masses m*0/m ranging from 0.7 to 1.0, then validated these against binding energies and charge radii of singly-magic spherical nuclei and deformation properties of nuclei around A∼80.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.153 fm−3 for all four SLyIII.xx parameterizations (compared to 0.145 fm−3 for SIII and 0.160 fm−3 for SLy5)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ ranges from 361.3 MeV (SLyIII.0.7) to 379.4 MeV (SLyIII.1.0), compared to 355.4 MeV for SIII and 229.9 MeV for SLy5
- Binding energy mean deviation E_dev = 1.46 MeV for SLyIII.0.8, compared to 2.49 MeV for SLy5 and 1.88 MeV for SIII
- Charge radius mean deviation rc_dev = 0.020 fm for SLyIII.0.8, compared to 0.012 fm for SLy5 and 0.051 fm for SIII
- χ² values for binding energies: 4.33 (SLyIII.0.8), 14.80 (SLy5), 63.99 (SIII)
- χ² values for charge radii: 0.74 (SLyIII.0.8), 1.74 (SLy5), 7.79 (SIII)
- Pairing strength V0 = 987 MeV·fm³ for SLyIII.0.8, fitted to the neutron spectral pairing gap of 1.393 MeV in 120Sn

## Context

This work extends the fitting protocol developed by Chabanat et al. (1997, 1998) for the SLy parameterizations, but imposes the integer density power α=1 required by the regularization procedure proposed by Lacroix et al. (2009) and Duguet et al. (2009) for multi-reference energy density functional calculations. The new parameterizations are designed for beyond-mean-field calculations (symmetry restoration and generator coordinate method) where standard Skyrme functionals with non-integer density powers become ill-defined. Unlike the approach of Kortelainen et al. (2010, 2011) which incorporates correlation energy into the functional at the single-reference level, these parameterizations are intended for explicit calculation of correlations in a multi-reference framework, enabling direct calculation of spectra and transition probabilities in the laboratory frame.
