# Hadron and hadron-cluster production in a hydrodynamical model including particle evaporation

> We discuss the evolution of the mixed phase at RHIC and SPS within boostinvariant hydrodynamics. In addition to the hydrodynamical expansion, we also consider evaporation of particles off the surface of the fluid. The back-reaction of the evaporation process on the dynamics of the fluid shortens the lifetime of the mixed phase. In our model this lifetime of the mixed phase is <12 fm/c in Au+Au at RHIC and <6.5 fm/c in Pb+Pb at SPS, even in the limit of vanishing transverse expansion velocity. Strangeness separation occurs, especially in events (or at rapidities) with relatively high initial net baryon and strangeness number, enhancing the multiplicity of MEMOs (multiply strange nuclear clusters). If antiquarks and antibaryons reach saturation in the course of the pure QGP or mixed phase, we find that at RHIC the ratio of antideuterons to deuterons may exceed 0.3 and even anti-helium to helium>0.1. Due to fluctuations, at RHIC even negative baryon number at midrapidity is possible in individual events, so that the antibaryon and antibaryon-cluster yields exceed those of the corresponding baryons and clusters.

## Key claim

The mixed phase in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and SPS has a shorter lifetime than previously estimated when particle evaporation from the fluid surface and its back-reaction on the hydrodynamics are included; the authors find that even in the limit of vanishing transverse expansion velocity, the mixed phase lifetime is ≤12 fm/c in Au+Au at RHIC and ≤6.5 fm/c in Pb+Pb at SPS.

## Method

The authors model the mixed phase evolution using boost-invariant longitudinal hydrodynamics combined with particle evaporation from the surface of an expanding cylindrical fluid element. They employ a MIT bag model equation of state (bag parameter B=380 MeV/fm³, strange quark mass m_s=150 MeV) for the quark-gluon plasma and a hadron gas including all well-established hadrons up to 2 GeV with Hagedorn eigenvolume corrections. The evaporation rates are computed from the hadron gas densities and mean transverse velocities, and the back-reaction on entropy, baryon number, strangeness, and energy is tracked via coupled rate equations until the QGP fraction vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Mixed phase lifetime ≤12 fm/c in Au+Au at RHIC (initial time τ_M=5 fm/c, hadronization time τ_H=16.6 fm/c)
- Mixed phase lifetime ≤6.5 fm/c in Pb+Pb at SPS (initial time τ_M=3 fm/c)
- Mixed phase temperature T_C ≈ 160 MeV (at small μ_q and μ_s)
- For standard RHIC initial conditions (S/N_B=200, N_B=25, N_s=0): π multiplicity 830, K multiplicity 100, anti-K multiplicity 92.6, N multiplicity 44.4, anti-N multiplicity 25.8, Λ multiplicity 13.2, anti-Λ multiplicity 8.67 (total including final breakup)
- Antideuteron/deuteron ratio >0.3 at RHIC (0.0355/0.104 = 0.34 from table 1)
- Anti-helium/helium ratio >0.1 at RHIC (1.43×10⁻⁷/1.20×10⁻⁶ = 0.12 from table 1)
- For S+Au at SPS: anti-N/N ≈ 0.1
- For Pb+Pb at SPS (S/N_B=45, N_B=80): π multiplicity 570, N multiplicity 68.1, anti-N multiplicity 7.48, d multiplicity 0.373, anti-d multiplicity 4.57×10⁻³
- Mixed phase lifetime in S+Au at SPS: 4.3 fm/c (including evaporation and expansion)
- Without evaporation, mixed phase lifetime would be 19 fm/c at RHIC (τ_H-τ_M)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangeness distillation and hadron evaporation in heavy-ion collisions by the same group (CSCG, 1995) which assumed spherical symmetry without boost-invariant expansion. The model bridges between the isochronous freeze-out scenario of Braun-Munzinger (1995) and the pure evaporation model of Barz et al. (1990). The results enable predictions for antinuclei and multiply strange nuclear cluster (MEMO) production at RHIC, and suggest that event-by-event fluctuations in net baryon number could produce events with negative baryon number at midrapidity, dramatically enhancing antibaryon cluster yields.
