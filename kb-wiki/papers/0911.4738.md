# Three and two-hadron correlations in \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We compare the azimuthal correlations arising from three and two hadron production in high energy proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV, using the leading order matrix elements for two-to-three and two-to-two parton-processes in perturbative QCD. We first compute the two and three hadron production cross sections in mid-rapidity proton-proton collisions. Then we consider Au + Au collisions including parton energy loss using the modified fragmentation function approach. By examining the geometrical paths the hard partons follow through the medium, we show that the two away-side partons produced in two-to-three processes have in average a smaller and a greater path length than the average path length of the away-side parton in two-to-two processes. Therefore there is a large probability that in the former processes one of the particles escapes while the other gets absorbed. This effect leads to an enhancement in the azimuthal correlations of the two-to-three with respect to the two-to-two parton-processes when comparing to the same processes in proton-proton collisions since in average the particle with the shortest path length looses less energy with respect to the away side particle in two-to-two processes. We argue that this phenomenon may be responsible for the shape of the away-side in azimuthal correlations observed in mid-rapidity Au + Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The suppression of two-to-three hadron production relative to two-to-two hadron production is smaller in Au+Au collisions than in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, due to the different average path lengths traveled by three versus two final-state partons through the medium. This geometrical effect may explain the double-hump structure observed in the away-side azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD matrix elements for two-to-three and two-to-two parton processes to obtain differential cross sections for three-hadron and two-hadron production at mid-rapidity in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. For Au+Au collisions, they incorporated parton energy loss using a modified fragmentation function approach (following the method of Ref. zoww), integrating over the location of hard scatterings in the nuclear overlap area and dividing by the overlap area. The path-length distributions for away-side partons were computed from a Glauber-model nuclear geometry with a Woods-Saxon profile.

## Key numbers

- Three-hadron production cross section in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, with each hadron carrying transverse momentum h_t = 10 GeV/c, shows a peak at Δφ = 2π/3 radians (left panel of Fig. 1).
- The ratio of the three-hadron differential cross section in Au+Au to that in p+p is approximately constant ~0.7 as a function of azimuthal angle φ.
- The ratio of the two-to-three to two-to-two cross section in Au+Au divided by the same ratio in p+p is approximately constant ~2.26 as a function of φ.
- All calculations are performed at mid-rapidity (all rapidities set to zero) with √S = 200 GeV for p+p and √S_NN = 200 GeV for Au+Au.

## Context

This work extends the study of azimuthal correlations beyond two-hadron production (previously studied in Ref. zoww) to three-hadron production using complete LO matrix elements for the first time in A+A collisions. It addresses the observation of a double-hump or broadened away-side peak in Au+Au collisions reported by PHENIX and STAR, which has been attributed to collective phenomena in hydrodynamic approaches (Takahashi, 2009) and other models (Casalderrey, Miklos, Majmunder, Chiu, Chaudhuri). The authors argue that the geometrical enhancement of two-to-three processes relative to two-to-two processes in nuclear collisions may contribute to this structure without invoking collective flow, and suggest that the signal strength is within reach of current experiments.
