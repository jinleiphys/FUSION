# Isoscalar, isovector and orbital contributions in $M1$ transitions from analogous $M1$ and Gamow-Teller transitions in $T=\frac{1}{2}$ mirror nuclei

> The isoscalar and isovector components and their contributions to $M1$ transitions are discussed in the odd-$A$, $T=1/2$ mirror nuclei with mass number ranging from $A=23$ to 37. The orbital contributions in various $M1$ transitions and ground state magnetic moments are calculated by comparing analogous $M1$ and Gamow-Teller transitions between mirror pairs. The orbital contributions in different $M1$ transitions are explained on the basis of configurations of the initial and final states involved. In magnetic moments, the orbital contributions are found to be dependent on the deformation and single-particle nature of the states. All the $T=1/2$ mirror pairs are studied using isospin non-conserving interaction. The results are also compared with predictions from \textit{ab initio} effective interaction derived from realistic nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The isoscalar and isovector components of M1 transitions in T=1/2 mirror nuclei from A=23 to 37 are extracted by comparing analogous M1 and Gamow-Teller transitions, with the orbital contributions found to depend strongly on the deformation and single-particle configurations of the states involved, though the analysis relies on an assumed average value of R_MEC = 1.25 for sd-shell nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the isospin-non-conserving USDC interaction within the sd model space, and compared results with an ab initio coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) derived from chiral NN and NNN forces. They decomposed M1 transition strengths into isoscalar and isovector components using the formalism of Fujita et al., and extracted orbital contributions (R_oc) by comparing B_IV(M1) with B(GT) for analogous transitions between mirror pairs, using effective g-factors and a quenching factor q=0.76 for USDC and q=0.78 for CCEI.

## Key numbers

- For the 23Na/23Mg pair (g.s. magnetic moments): experimental μ = 2.218 μ_N (23Na) and -0.536 μ_N (23Mg), with USDC giving 2.162 μ_N and -0.396 μ_N respectively
- For the 25Mg/25Al pair (g.s. magnetic moments): experimental μ = -0.855 μ_N (25Mg) and 3.645 μ_N (25Al), with USDC giving -0.861 μ_N and 3.757 μ_N respectively
- For the 27Al/27Si pair (g.s. magnetic moments): experimental μ = 3.642 μ_N (27Al) and -0.865 μ_N (27Si), with USDC giving 3.576 μ_N and -0.710 μ_N respectively
- For the 33S/33Cl pair (g.s. magnetic moments): USDC gives μ = 0.664 μ_N (33S) and 0.841 μ_N (33Cl), with R_oc = 0.02 indicating negligible orbital contribution
- For the 27Al/27Si 3/2^+_1 transition: experimental B(M1) = 0.015(1) μ_N^2 (27Al) and 0.019(1) μ_N^2 (27Si), with R_oc = 51.3(80) indicating very large orbital contribution
- The R_MEC parameter is taken as 1.25 for all sd-shell nuclei, and g_s^IV = 4.706 is used throughout

## Context

This work extends the experimental analyses of Fujita et al. (2011, 2014, 2015) on 23Na/23Mg, 25Mg/25Al, and 27Al/27Si mirror pairs to a systematic study of all T=1/2 mirror nuclei from A=23 to 37 using the isospin-non-conserving USDC interaction. It enables future experimental measurements of M1 transitions in the less-studied mirror partners (particularly 29P, 31S, 33Cl, 35Ar, 37K) by providing theoretical predictions, and tests the ab initio CCEI approach against phenomenological shell-model results for electromagnetic and weak transition strengths.
