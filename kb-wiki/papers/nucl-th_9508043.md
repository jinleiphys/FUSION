# Decay Anisotropy of $e^+e^-$ Sources from $pN$ and $pd$ collisions

> A full calculation of lepton-pair angular characteristics is carried out for $e^+e^-$ pairs created in $pp$, $pn$ and $pd$ collisions at intermediate energies. It is demonstrated that the proposed new observable, the dilepton decay anisotropy, quite sensitively changes for different sources and may be useful for their disentangling. The relevance of the dilepton decay anisotropy is shown in the context of a puzzling energy behavior for the ratio of the lepton yield from $pd$ to $pp$ reactions as observed at the BEVALAC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dilepton decay anisotropy coefficient \(B\) is sensitive to the specific production channel of \(e^+e^-\) pairs in \(pp\), \(pn\), and \(pd\) collisions, and may serve as an additional observable to disentangle dilepton sources; however, the anisotropy coefficient for the \(\eta\)-Dalitz decay channel depends on both the initial collision energy and the dilepton invariant mass, approaching 1 only near threshold or for \(M \to 0\), and dropping to about \(0.1 \div 0.25\) for \(M\) close to \(m_\eta\) at bombarding energies from 1.26 to 5 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for dilepton production in the form \(S_i(M,\theta) = A_i (1 + B_i \cos^2\theta)\) for each channel \(i\) (bremsstrahlung, \(\Delta\)-Dalitz decay, and \(\eta\)-Dalitz decay) using a microscopic One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) model for the bremsstrahlung and \(\Delta\) channels, and a phase-space oriented expression with a parametrized production matrix element for the \(\eta\) channel. The total anisotropy coefficient \(B^{pN}\) for proton-nucleon collisions is obtained as a weighted sum of the individual channel coefficients, including interference between bremsstrahlung and \(\Delta\) channels. For \(pd\) collisions, the anisotropy coefficient is computed by summing contributions from \(pp\) and \(pn\) interactions, with the \(\eta\)-channel contribution calculated using the cross section from TKB95 and the Paris deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy coefficient \(B_\eta\) for \(\eta\)-Dalitz decay approaches 1 only close to threshold or for \(M \rightarrow 0\).
- For invariant mass \(M\) close to the kinematical limit (\(M \to m_\eta\)), \(B_\eta\) approaches a lower limit of about \(0.1 \div 0.25\) for bombarding energies from 1.26 to 5 GeV.
- The \(\eta\)-production threshold in \(pN\) interactions is about 1.26 GeV; below this energy, the \(\eta\)-Dalitz decay contribution is significant for \(pd\) but negligible for \(pp\).
- The ratio of anisotropy coefficients \(R_B = B^{pd}/B^{pp}\) is shown at energies of 1.26 and 2.1 GeV, with solid curves (including \(\eta\)-Dalitz decay) and dashed curves (without \(\eta\)-channel) illustrating the relative importance of the \(\eta\)-decay contribution.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Bratkovskaya et al. 1994, BSCMTT 1994) on using lepton-pair angular distributions to distinguish dilepton sources, and addresses the puzzling beam-energy dependence of the \(pd/pp\) dielectron ratio measured at the BEVALAC (W93). The results support the interpretation that subthreshold \(\eta\)-meson production (TKB95) contributes to the \(pd/pp\) ratio, and the calculated anisotropy coefficients provide a new observable to experimentally discriminate dilepton sources in \(pN\) and \(pd\) reactions.
