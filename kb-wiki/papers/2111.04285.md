# Dineutron in the $2^+_1$ state of $^6$He

> We investigate the dineutron in the $2^+_1$ state of $^6$He via analysis of its decay mode by using the complex scaling method. In this letter, we propose the cross section for the resonant state to distinguish the resonant contributions from the nonresonant ones. As the results, it is found that the shoulder peak appears in the cross section for the resonant state as a function of $\varepsilon_{n\text{-}n}$. Furthermore, we show that the $S$ = 0 component of the cross section, where $S$ is the total spin of the valence two neutrons, has a peak around the shoulder peak, which comes from the dineutron configuration in the $2^+_1$ state. Thus we conclude that the shoulder peak is expected to indicate the existence of the dineutron in the $2^+_1$ state.

## Key claim

The shoulder peak in the breakup cross section as a function of the two-neutron relative energy, previously suggested to indicate a dineutron in the \(2^+_1\) state of \(^6\)He, is confirmed to originate from the resonant state itself rather than from nonresonant contributions, by using a method that isolates the resonant contribution via the complex scaling method.

## Method

The authors analyzed the \(^6\)He + \(^{12}\)C reaction at 240 MeV/nucleon as a four-body (\(n + n + \alpha + ^{12}\)C) system using the continuum discretized coupled channels method (CDCC) combined with the complex-scaled Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS). They defined a "resonant cross section" by reconstructing the transition matrix using the extended completeness relation in the complex scaling method (CSM), which allows complete separation of the resonant \(2^+_1\) state from nonresonant continuum states. The double-differential breakup cross section (DDBUX) with respect to the two-neutron relative energy \(\varepsilon_{n-n}\) and the energy between the two-neutron center-of-mass and the alpha particle \(\varepsilon_{nn-\alpha}\) was then calculated for the resonant state only.

## Key numbers

* The \(2^+_1\) resonant state of \(^6\)He has a resonant energy \(\varepsilon_r = 0.823\) MeV and a decay width \(\Gamma = 0.121\) MeV, obtained from the complex scaling method.
* The ground state energy of \(^6\)He is calculated as \(-0.972\) MeV.
* The shoulder peak in the resonant cross section as a function of \(\varepsilon_{n-n}\) appears around 0.7 MeV, while a clear peak appears around 0.2 MeV.
* The angular density of the \(2^+_1\) state takes its maximum value in the region \(\theta_{12} = 60^\circ\) to \(80^\circ\), where \(\theta_{12}\) is the opening angle between the two valence neutrons.
* The convergence of the calculated cross section is achieved within about 5% fluctuation.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Kikuchi et al. (2013), who investigated the \(2^+_1\) state of \(^6\)He via the same reaction and suggested that a shoulder peak in the gated cross section indicates a dineutron, but could not completely exclude nonresonant contributions. The present paper provides stronger evidence by isolating the resonant contribution using the CSM, confirming that the shoulder peak comes from the \(2^+_1\) state and specifically from its \(S=0\) (total spin of valence neutrons) component. This method enables future analysis of resonant states in other two-neutron halo nuclei such as \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{14}\)Be, and \(^{22}\)C.
