# Anomalous Production of Omega and AntiOmega is Evidence for the Formation of Disoriented Chiral Condensates?

> No conventional picture of nucleus-nucleus collisions has yet been able to explain the abundance of Omega and AntiOmega hadrons in central collisions between Pb nuclei at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS. We argue that this is evidence that they are produced from topological defects in the form of skyrmions arising from the formation of disoriented chiral condensates. It is shown that the excess Omega or AntiOmega produced could not be easily washed out in the hadronic medium and therefore would survive in the final state.

## Key claim
The observed excess of Omega and AntiOmega hadrons in central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV at the CERN SPS, which cannot be explained by conventional hadronic models, may be evidence for production from skyrmions arising from disoriented chiral condensates (DCC), provided that the DCC domains are small enough that the non-observation of DCC via pion ratios does not contradict the proposal.

## Method
The authors estimate the probability per unit volume for skyrmion/anti-skyrmion formation from DCC domains using a triangulated spatial domain approach on the order parameter space S^3, then extrapolate from WA97 and NA49 collaboration data on Omega, AntiOmega, Xi, and AntiXi yields to estimate the total number of baryons and antibaryons from DCC per central collision. They also calculate destruction timescales for Omega via hadronic reactions (π + Ω → K + Ξ and K + Ω → π + Ξ) using thermal averaged cross-sections parameterized from prior work (Kapusta, Mekjian, and others) to determine whether the DCC-produced Omega could survive the hadronic medium.

## Key numbers
- WA97 measured Ω+AntiΩ = 0.410 ± 0.08 per central collision within one unit around y=0, and AntiΩ/Ω = 0.383 ± 0.081.
- Extrapolation to full momentum space yields approximately 0.5 AntiΩ per central collision at the SPS.
- The probability per unit volume for skyrmion/anti-skyrmion pair formation is estimated as 0.08 ξ^{-3}, where ξ is the domain correlation length.
- The domain size ξ is estimated phenomenologically as approximately 2 fm, consistent with theoretical estimates of 1.5 fm (Rajagopal and Wilczek) and 3-4 fm (Gavin and Muller).
- Destruction timescales for Omega at T=150 MeV are 500 fm/c for π + Ω → K + Ξ and 488 fm/c for K + Ω → π + Ξ; at T=170 MeV they are 370 fm/c and 290 fm/c respectively; at T=200 MeV they are 257 fm/c and 152 fm/c respectively.

## Context
This work extends the puzzle of Omega and AntiOmega yields at the SPS, which could not be reproduced by the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model (URQMD) without unphysical parameter adjustments, and which caused a tenfold increase in chi-squared when included in thermal fits (Rapp and others, 2000). The proposal that DCC formation is the source of the anomaly is orthogonal to the usual DCC observable (neutral-to-total pion ratio), which requires large domains; the small domain size (approximately 2 fm) explains why DCC have not been observed via pions. The paper enables future experimental searches for DCC via rare hyperon yields rather than pion ratios, and suggests that the Omega slope anomaly in m_T spectra (Hama and others) may be explained by the low flow velocity of DCC-produced baryons.
