# Fierz-complete NJL model study: fixed points and phase structure at finite temperature and density

> Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models are frequently employed as low-energy models in various research fields. With respect to the theory of the strong interaction, this class of models is indeed often used to analyze the structure of the phase diagram at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. The predictions from such models for the phase structure at finite quark chemical potential are of particular interest as this regime is difficult to access with lattice Monte Carlo approaches. In this work, we consider a Fierz-complete version of a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. By studying its renormalization group flow, we analyze in detail how Fierz-incomplete approximations affect the predictive power of such model studies. In particular, we investigate the curvature of the phase boundary at small chemical potential, the critical value of the chemical potential above which no spontaneous symmetry breaking occurs, and the possible interpretation of the underlying dynamics in terms of difermion-type degrees of freedom. We find that the inclusion of four-fermion channels other than the conventional scalar-pseudoscalar channel is not only important at large chemical potential but also leaves a significant imprint on the dynamics at small chemical potential as measured by the curvature of the finite-temperature phase boundary.

## Key claim

The inclusion of four-fermion interaction channels beyond the conventional scalar-pseudoscalar channel (specifically vector channels) significantly alters the predicted phase structure of the NJL model not only at large chemical potential but also at small chemical potential, as measured by the curvature of the finite-temperature phase boundary.

## Method

The authors studied a Fierz-complete NJL model with a single fermion species at leading order of the derivative expansion of the effective action, using the Wetterich renormalization group equation. They analyzed the RG flow of the four-fermion couplings (scalar-pseudoscalar and vector channels) at zero and finite temperature and chemical potential, employing a four-dimensional regularization scheme that preserves Poincare invariance in the vacuum limit. The onset of spontaneous symmetry breaking was identified by the divergence of four-fermion couplings at a critical RG scale.

## Key numbers

* The non-Gaussian fixed-point for the scalar-pseudoscalar coupling in the one-channel approximation is at lambda_sigma* = 8 pi^2, with critical exponent Theta = 2.
* The Fierz-complete system has three fixed points: a Gaussian fixed-point at (0,0) and two non-Gaussian fixed-points at (3 pi^2, pi^2) and (-32 pi^2, 16 pi^2).
* The critical scale for spontaneous symmetry breaking in the one-channel approximation is given by k_cr = Lambda * ((lambda_sigma^(UV) - lambda_sigma*)/lambda_sigma^(UV))^(1/Theta) for lambda_sigma^(UV) > lambda_sigma*.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies (Klevansky 1992, Buballa 2003, Fukushima 2011) by addressing the Fierz ambiguity problem identified by Jaeckel (2002), where mean-field approximations produce results that depend on an unphysical parameter related to the choice of mean field. The authors demonstrate that Fierz-incomplete approximations (considering only the scalar-pseudoscalar channel) miss significant dynamical effects even at small chemical potential. The study enables more reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at finite density, a regime difficult to access with lattice Monte Carlo approaches.
