# Temperature dependence of dimension 6 gluon operators and their effects on Charmonium

> Starting from an earlier representation of the independent dimension 6 gluon operators in terms of color electric and magnetic fields, we estimate their changes near the critical temperature $T_c$ using the temperature dependence of the dimension 4 electric and magnetic condensates extracted from pure gauge theory on the lattice. We then improve the previous QCD sum rules for the $J/ψ$ mass near $T_c$ based on dimension 4 operators, by including the contribution of the dimension 6 operators to the OPE. We find an enhanced stability in the sum rule and confirm that the $J/ψ$ will undergo an abrupt change in the property across $T_c$.

# Temperature dependence of dimension 6 gluon operators and their effects on Charmonium

## Key claim

The inclusion of dimension 6 gluon operators in the QCD sum rule for the J/ψ mass near the critical temperature Tc improves the stability of the sum rule up to slightly above Tc (1.05 Tc), confirming that the J/ψ undergoes an abrupt change in its property across Tc rather than melting immediately at Tc.

## Method

The authors estimated the temperature dependence of dimension 6 gluon operators by expressing them in terms of color electric (E) and magnetic (B) fields and using the temperature dependence of dimension 4 electric and magnetic condensates extracted from pure gauge lattice calculations. They assumed isotropic fields with negligible angular correlations, approximating the triple scalar products BEE and BBB from the lower-dimensional condensates, and used the vacuum value ⟨g³fG³⟩₀ = (0.6 GeV)⁶ from Nikolaev (1982). They then incorporated these dimension 6 condensates into the operator product expansion (OPE) of the vector current correlation function for charmonium and applied QCD moment sum rules to extract the J/ψ mass at temperatures from 0.8 Tc to 1.2 Tc.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum value of the dimension 6 gluon condensate: ⟨g³fG³⟩₀ = (0.6 GeV)⁶, used to determine ⟨αₛ³/²BEE⟩₀ = −⟨αₛ³/²BBB⟩₀ = (0.6 GeV)⁶ / [4(4π)³/²]
- Thermal gluon mass near Tc: mG = 0.6 GeV, used to estimate the ratio G₄/G₂ for the dimension 6 twist-2 operator
- Critical temperature for the pure gauge theory: Tc = 260 MeV, used as the reference for thermal momentum averaging
- The J/ψ mass shows a stable plateau at T = 1.05 Tc when dimension 6 operators are included, whereas the sum rule with only dimension 4 operators becomes unstable at that temperature
- At T = 1.2 Tc, the dimension 6 corrections become twice as large as the vacuum value with opposite sign, signaling the onset of OPE breakdown

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of J/ψ near Tc by Morita and Lee (2007, 2008, 2009) that used only dimension 4 gluon condensates and found instability above 1.05 Tc. The authors build on their own renormalization of dimension 6 gluon operators (Kim and Lee, 2015) and on lattice extractions of dimension 4 electric and magnetic condensates by Lee (2008). The improved stability enables confirmation that J/ψ survives up to slightly above Tc, consistent with later Maximum Entropy method analyses by Gubler (2011) that found melting at slightly higher temperature. The approach also connects to the quasiparticle picture of Morita (2010) for estimating the higher moment G₄.
