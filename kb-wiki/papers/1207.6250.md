# Existence of exotic torus configuration in high-spin excited states of $^{40}$Ca

> We investigate the possibility of the existence of the exotic torus configuration in the high-spin excited states of $^{40}$Ca. We here consider the spin alignments about the symmetry axis. To this end, we use a three-dimensional cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock method and search for stable single-particle configurations. We find one stable state with the torus configuration at the total angular momentum $J=$ 60 $\hbar$ and an excitation energy of about 170 MeV in all calculations using various Skyrme interactions. The total angular momentum J=60 $\hbar$ consists of aligned 12 nucleons with the orbital angular momenta $Λ=+4$, +5, and +6 for spin up-down neutrons and protons. The obtained results strongly suggest that a macroscopic amount of circulating current breaking the time-reversal symmetry emerges in the high-spin excited state of $^{40}$Ca.

## Key claim
Using a three-dimensional cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock method, the authors find exactly one stable torus configuration in $^{40}$Ca at total angular momentum $J = 60\ \hbar$ and excitation energy around 170 MeV, but only within a specific range of the rotational frequency parameter $\hbar\omega$ (1.2–1.7 MeV for SLy6 and SkI3; 1.2–1.8 MeV for SkI4); states at $J = 20\ \hbar$ and $100\ \hbar$ are unstable.

## Method
The authors performed three-dimensional cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with a Lagrange multiplier $\omega$ to enforce alignment of single-particle angular momenta along the symmetry axis ($z$-axis). They used a Cartesian grid (32 × 32 × 24 points, spacing 1.0 fm) and three different Skyrme interactions (SLy6, SkI3, SkI4), starting from an initial ring configuration of 10 $\alpha$ particles placed on the $x$-$y$ plane. The calculations scanned a range of rotational frequencies $\hbar\omega$ from 0.5 to 2.0 MeV, iterating 15000 times to search for converged stable single-particle configurations.

## Key numbers
- The stable torus configuration appears at total angular momentum $J = 60\ \hbar$ for all three Skyrme interactions.
- Excitation energies: 170.45 MeV (SLy6), 174.22 MeV (SkI3), and 172.53 MeV (SkI4).
- The fitted density distribution parameters for SLy6: $\rho_0 = 0.13\ \text{fm}^{-3}$, $r_0 = 6.07\ \text{fm}$, $\sigma = 1.61\ \text{fm}$.
- The stable region of $\hbar\omega$ is 1.2–1.7 MeV for SLy6 and SkI3, and 1.2–1.8 MeV for SkI4.
- The total angular momentum $J = 60\ \hbar$ consists of 12 aligned nucleons (6 protons and 6 neutrons) with orbital angular momenta $\Lambda = +4$, $+5$, and $+6$.
- The classical rigid-body moment of inertia for the torus configuration with SLy6 is $\mathscr{T}_\perp = 20.97\ \hbar^2\cdot\text{MeV}^{-1}$.
- The estimated magnetic moment is $\mu = 30\ \mu_N$.

## Context
This work extends the study of high-spin oblate isomers and yrast traps, such as those observed in $^{152}$Dy (Khoo 1978) and $^{158}$Er (Simpson 1994), to the extreme limit of a torus shape. It builds on earlier theoretical work by Wong (1972, 1973, 1978) using the macroscopic-microscopic model for torus configurations in heavy-mass systems, and on constrained Hartree-Fock calculations by Warda (2007) and Staszczak (2009). The finding of a stable torus state in $^{40}$Ca at $J = 60\ \hbar$ suggests a new form of nuclear matter with macroscopic circulating current and broken time-reversal symmetry, and opens questions about how such a "femto-scale magnet" would rotate and whether it could be observed experimentally despite competition from fission.
