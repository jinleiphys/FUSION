# Study of np-scattering for S, P and D Waves using Deng-Fan Potential by Phase Function Method

> In this paper, the np - scattering phase shifts and cross section for S,P and D partial waves have been obtained for energies below the pion threshold, by considering Deng-Fan potential as model of interaction. The radial time independent Schrödinger equation has been analytically solved using Nikiforov - Uvarov method to obtain the energy expression for ground state of np system. Utilising this, the scattering phase shifts for $^3S_1$ have been obtained using phase function method. The phase equations for various scattering states $^1S_0$, $^1P_1$, $^3P_{0,1,2}$, $^1D_2$, and $^3D_{1,2,3}$ have been numerically solved for obtaining corresponding scattering phase shifts and their respective partial cross section. The total scattering cross sections computed at various energies are found to be closely matching with experimental data. The low energy scattering parameters determined from scattering phase shifts of $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ are reasonably close to experimental ones. Hence, Deng-Fan potential is a good phenomenological potential to understand the np - scattering system.

# Study of np-scattering for S, P and D Waves using Deng-Fan Potential by Phase Function Method

## Key claim
The Deng-Fan potential, when fitted separately for each partial wave channel, produces scattering phase shifts and total cross sections for np scattering that match experimental data up to 350 MeV laboratory energy, with discrepancies of 2-3 degrees in S-wave phase shifts at 300-350 MeV. The authors claim this demonstrates the potential is a suitable phenomenological model for np interaction, contrary to a prior claim by Saha et al. that the phase function method gives inferior results compared to the Jost function method.

## Method
The radial time-independent Schrödinger equation for the Deng-Fan potential was solved analytically using the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method to obtain the ground state energy expression for the np system. Scattering phase shifts for the $^3S_1$, $^1S_0$, $^1P_1$, $^3P_{0,1,2}$, $^1D_2$, and $^3D_{1,2,3}$ channels were obtained by numerically solving the first-order Riccati-type phase equations using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta method, with model parameters optimized separately for each partial wave against experimental phase shift data from Perez et al. (Granada group) up to 350 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy used as constraint for $^3S_1$: -2.224549 MeV
- For $^1S_0$ state: scattering length = -24.016 fm (experimental: -23.749(8) fm); effective range = 2.49 fm (experimental: 2.81(5) fm)
- For $^3S_1$ state: scattering length = 5.477 fm (experimental: 5.424(3) fm); effective range = 1.89 fm (experimental: 1.76(5) fm)
- Total cross section at 0.5 MeV: simulated = 6.083 b (experimental = 6.135 b)
- Total cross section at 10 MeV: simulated = 0.9533 b (experimental = 0.9455 b)
- Total cross section at 350 MeV: simulated = 0.01629 b (experimental = 0.03411 b)
- At 350 MeV, P-wave contributes 86% of total cross section, D-wave contributes 13%, S-wave contributes 1%
- Model parameters for $^3S_1$: V1 = 728.3840 MeV, V2 = 1443.0481 MeV, alpha = 2.0429 fm^-1
- Model parameters for $^1S_0$: V1 = 450.3160 MeV, V2 = 918.3518 MeV, alpha = 1.9291 fm^-1

## Context
This work extends the phase function method analysis of np scattering using the Deng-Fan potential beyond the 50 MeV limit of Saha et al., who used both Jost function and phase function methods but only for S-waves up to 50 MeV. The authors previously studied np scattering using the Morse function (a limiting case of Deng-Fan) and deuteron structure. The Deng-Fan potential belongs to the exponential-type potential class, offering analytical solutions unlike the Yukawa potential (which matched data only up to 50 MeV) or the Malfliet-Tjon potential. This work enables future application of the Deng-Fan potential to other scattering systems such as n-D, p-D, n-alpha, and p-alpha scattering, as well as cross-checking deuteron properties.
