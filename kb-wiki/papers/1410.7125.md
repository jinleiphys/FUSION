# Two-nucleon Correlations in the Decay of Unbound Nuclei beyond the Drip Lines

> We discuss the two-neutron emission decay of $^{26}$O nucleus from a viewpoint of dineutron correlations between the valence neutrons. We first discuss how the dineutron correlation is realized both in the coordinate space and in the momentum space by employing a three-body model. We then discuss the decay energy spectrum as well as the angular distribution of emitted neutrons. We show that an emission of two neutrons in back-to-back directions is enhanced, which we interpret as a clear manifestation of the dineutron correlation between the valence neutrons.

# Two-nucleon Correlations in the Decay of Unbound Nuclei beyond the Drip Lines

## Key claim

The two-neutron emission decay of the unbound nucleus ²⁶O shows an enhanced probability for neutrons to be emitted in back-to-back directions (opening angle near 180°), which the authors interpret as a clear manifestation of dineutron correlation between the valence neutrons, but only if the angular distribution is measured experimentally, as it has not yet been observed.

## Method

The authors employ a three-body model of ²⁴O + n + n, using a Woods-Saxon potential between each valence neutron and the ²⁴O core (parameters chosen to reproduce the d₃/₂ resonance state of ²⁵O at 770 keV) and a density-dependent contact pairing interaction between the valence neutrons (parameters adjusted to reproduce the ground state energy of ²⁷F). The decay energy spectrum and angular distribution are computed using the Green's function technique to fully account for continuum couplings.

## Key numbers

- The d₃/₂ resonance state of ²⁵O is at 770 keV.
- The uncorrelated two-neutron decay energy spectrum for ²⁶O peaks at 1.54 MeV (twice the single-particle resonance energy).
- With the pairing interaction, the 0⁺ state peak shifts to 0.148 MeV (energy shift ΔE = -1.392 MeV) and the 2⁺ state peak shifts to 1.354 MeV (ΔE = -0.186 MeV).
- The ratio of energy shifts ΔE_{I=0}/ΔE_{I=2} is 7.48 in the three-body model, compared to a simple single-j model prediction of 5.
- For a pairing interaction yielding a ground state energy of 5 keV rather than 148 keV, the 2⁺ state appears at 1.338 MeV.
- The average opening angle between emitted neutrons is ⟨θ₁₂⟩ = 115.3° for the correlated case.
- The extracted mean opening angles from Coulomb breakup for comparison are ⟨θ₁₂⟩ = 65.2 ± 12.2 degrees for ¹¹Li and 74.5 ± 12.1 degrees for ⁶He (from Hagino and Sagawa 2007).

## Context

This work extends three-body model studies of dineutron correlations in Borromean nuclei (Matsuo 2005, 2006; Hagino and Sagawa 2005) to the direct two-neutron emission decay of unbound nuclei beyond the drip line, specifically ²⁶O. The calculations build on the Green's function technique originally developed for continuum dipole excitations of ¹¹Li by Bertsch and Esbensen (1992). The results enable experimental tests at new-generation RI beam facilities such as the SAMURAI facility at RIKEN, where the predicted angular distribution and the 2⁺ state energy (around 1.3 MeV) could be measured to confirm the dineutron correlation interpretation.
