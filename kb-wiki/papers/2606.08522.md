# Post-Merger Gravitational-Wave Uncertainties of Binary Neutron Stars under Multi-Messenger EOS Constraints

> The high-frequency gravitational waves emitted by a binary neutron star merger remnant carry information on matter at densities and temperatures beyond those reached in isolated neutron stars. We quantify how tightly current multi-messenger constraints already determine the dominant post-merger frequency $f_{2,\rm mean}$. Adopting a set of cold equations of state (EOSs) constrained jointly by gravitational-wave tidal deformability, NICER mass--radius measurements, massive-pulsar masses, chiral effective field theory at low density, and perturbative QCD at asymptotically high density, for each binary mass we select the softest and stiffest models of the multi-messenger posterior and follow their coalescence with fully general-relativistic hydrodynamics simulations. Together with a broad set of EOSs drawn from the literature ($82$ models in total), these simulations show that, once the binary mass and a single measure of the stellar compactness ($Λ$ or $R$) are held fixed, the residual spread of $f_{2,\rm mean}$ is only $\sim 100\,{\rm Hz}$, a factor of several below the $\gtrsim 500\,{\rm Hz}$ range spanned by an EOSs set including those already disfavored by the data. This tight calibration of the cold-matter prediction implies that a future high-frequency detection departing from it would point directly to additional physics, such as a hadron--quark transition occurring at finite temperature. We further confirm the quasi-universal relation $(f_1+f_3)/2 \approx f_{2,\rm mean}$ to within $\sim 116\,{\rm Hz}$, which provides a model-independent estimate of $f_{2,\rm mean}$ from the secondary spectral peaks.

## Key claim

The dominant post-merger gravitational-wave frequency \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) is predicted to within a residual spread of only \(\sim 100\,{\rm Hz}\) once the binary mass and a single measure of stellar compactness (tidal deformability \(\Lambda\) or radius \(R\)) are held fixed, but only when the cold equation of state (EOS) is constrained by current multi-messenger data; this residual is a factor of several below the \(\gtrsim 500\,{\rm Hz}\) range spanned by EOSs already disfavored by observations, implying that a future detection departing from this tight calibration would point to additional finite-temperature physics such as a hadron-quark transition.

## Method

The authors generated quasi-equilibrium irrotational binary initial data using the pseudo-spectral code Lorene with an initial separation of \(45\,{\rm km}\) and evolved the general-relativistic hydrodynamics with WhiskyTHC within the Einstein Toolkit, using a finite-volume scheme with fifth-order reconstruction and the Z4c formulation for spacetime. They constructed two classes of non-parametric statistical EOSs: one using a feed-forward neural network method (Han23 models) retaining only those within the 90% credible region of multi-messenger constraints, and another (updated models) connecting chiral-effective-field-theory at low density to perturbative QCD at high density, incorporating recent NICER measurements for PSR J0437-4715 and PSR J0614-3329 using the 68% highest posterior density interval. For each binary mass between \(1.25\,M_\odot\) and \(1.40\,M_\odot\), they selected the softest and stiffest models based on central density and evolved them with thermal indices \(\Gamma_{\rm th}=1.6\) and \(2.0\), then combined these with 82 total EOS models from the literature to fit quasi-universal relations for \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) as a function of mass and compactness.

## Key numbers

- Residual spread of \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) after fixing binary mass and compactness: \(\sigma_{f_2} \approx 100\,{\rm Hz}\) (specifically \(85.7\,{\rm Hz}\) for the Han23 baseline \(M\)–\(\ln\Lambda\) fit and \(102.3\,{\rm Hz}\) for the updated baseline \(M\)–\(\ln\Lambda\) fit)
- Full spread of \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) across EOSs including those disfavored by data: \(\gtrsim 500\,{\rm Hz}\)
- Frequency shift induced by varying thermal index \(\Gamma_{\rm th}\) from \(1.6\) to \(2.0\): \(\sim 100\)–\(120\,{\rm Hz}\) at \(M_{\rm NS}=1.30\)–\(1.35\,M_\odot\)
- Quasi-universal relation \((f_1+f_3)/2 \approx f_{2,\rm mean}\) confirmed to within \(\sim 116\,{\rm Hz}\)
- Best-fit \(M\)–\(\ln\Lambda\) relation for updated baseline: \(f_2^{\rm fit} = 3487.4 - 2982.0\,(M-1.4) - 871.0\,\ln(\Lambda/272)\) with \(\sigma_{f_2}=102.3\,{\rm Hz}\)
- Best-fit \(M\)–\(R\) relation for updated baseline: \(f_2^{\rm fit} = 3406.7 + 1555.3\,(M-1.4) - 556.2\,(R-11.55)\) with \(\sigma_{f_2}=119.2\,{\rm Hz}\)
- Stiff updated model \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) values range from \(3253\) to \(3375\,{\rm Hz}\) over \(M_{\rm NS}=1.30\)–\(1.40\,M_\odot\) (with \(\Gamma_{\rm th}=2.0\))
- Soft updated model \(f_{2,\rm mean}\) values: \(3649\,{\rm Hz}\) (M125-softG20) and \(3920\,{\rm Hz}\) (M130-softG20)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bauswein (2012), Bernuzzi (2015), and Rezzolla (2016) by using a non-parametric Bayesian inference framework for the EOS rather than a limited set of phenomenological models, and it quantifies the post-merger frequency uncertainty under the latest multi-messenger constraints from gravitational-wave tidal deformability, NICER mass-radius measurements, and massive-pulsar masses. The finding that the residual uncertainty is comparable to the thermal shift from varying \(\Gamma_{\rm th}\) enables future detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer to distinguish finite-temperature effects from cold EOS uncertainties, probing matter at densities and temperatures inaccessible to inspiral and pulsar observations of cold stable neutron stars.
