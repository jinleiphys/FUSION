# Quantum mechanical ab-initio simulation of the electron screening effect in metal deuteride crystals

> In antecedent experiments the electron screening energies of the d+d reactions in metallic environments have been determined to be enhanced by an order of magnitude in comparison to the case of gaseous deuterium targets. The analytical models describing averaged material properties have not been able to explain the experimental results so far. Therefore, a first effort has been undertaken to simulate the dynamics of reacting deuterons in a metallic lattice by means of an ab-initio Hartree-Fock calculation of the total electrostatic force between the lattice and the successively approaching deuterons via path integration. The calculations have been performed for Li and Ta, clearly showing a migration of electrons from host metallic to the deuterium atoms. However, in order to avoid more of the necessary simplifications in the model the utilization of a massive parallel supercomputer would be required.

# Quantum mechanical ab-initio simulation of the electron screening effect in metal deuteride crystals

## Key claim

The authors performed a first-principles Hartree-Fock simulation of the d+d reaction in Li and Ta metal deuteride crystals, obtaining electron screening energies far below the experimentally observed values (e.g., -43.39 eV for Li versus experimental values of approximately 150 eV or more). The discrepancy is attributed to necessary simplifications in the model, particularly the inability to include time-dependent effects and adequate basis sets for conduction band electrons, given the limitations of workstation-class computing.

## Method

The authors used the analytic Hartree-Fock method (Roothaan-Hall approach) with Gaussian-type orbital basis sets to compute the electrostatic force between two approaching deuterons embedded in small crystalline clusters of LiH and Ta2H, treating the impact as quasi-static via the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The relative electronic force was integrated along the projectile trajectory to obtain the molecular screening energy, following the path-integration method of Shoppa (1996). Calculations were performed with the Gaussian quantum chemistry package using basis sets ranging from STO-3G to 6-31G(d,p) and, for Ta, LanL2DZ with effective core potentials.

## Key numbers

- Molecular screening energy for LiH (molecule lih-li1, basis 6-31G(d,p)): -43.39 eV, compared to experimental values of approximately 150 eV or more for Li.
- Molecular screening energy for Ta2H (smallest molecule, simplest basis): 39.72 eV, below the smallest Li value.
- Charge transfer to the central hydrogen atom in LiH (basis 6-31G(d,p)): -0.455479 e (Mulliken population analysis), nearly ten times larger than with STO-3G (-0.054858 e).
- Net computation time: 1.6 years; single points on the trajectory took one week.
- Lattice constant for LiH: a = 4.085 angstrom (cubic space centered).
- Lattice constants for Ta2H: a = 3.38 angstrom, b = 3.41 angstrom (tetragonal).

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental observations by the same group (Voloshin 1998, Czerski 2001) and others (Yuki 1998, Kasagi 2002, Rolfs 2002-2004) that d+d reaction screening energies in metals are an order of magnitude larger than in D2 gas targets (Greife 1995). The authors' previous analytical model based on dielectric function theory (Czerski 2004, 2006) gave screening energies a factor of 2 below experiment, while the Debye-Huckel model proposed by Rolfs (2003) was deemed inapplicable to condensed matter. The present ab-initio approach, though yielding values far below experiment, demonstrates electron migration from host metal atoms to hydrogen and identifies the need for time-dependent Hartree-Fock methods on massively parallel computers to capture collective effects such as plasmons and Fermi surface interactions.
