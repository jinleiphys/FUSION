# Quadrupole dominance in the light Sn and in the Cd isotopes

> [Background] The BE2 rates of the Sn isotopes for $N\le 64$ exhibit enhancements hitherto unexplained. The same is true for the Cd isotopes. [Purpose] Describe the electromagnetic properties of the Sn and Cd isotopes [Method] Shell model calculations with a minimally renormalized realistic interaction, supplemented by Quasi and Pseudo-SU3 symmetries and Nilsson-SU3 selfconsistent calculations. [Results for $N\le 64$] Shell model calculations with the neutron effective charge as single free parameter describe well the BE2(2>0) and BE2(4>2) rates for $N\le 64$ in the Cd and Sn isotopes. The former exhibit weak permanent deformation corroborating the prediction of a Pseudo-SU3 symmetry, which remains of heuristic value in the latter, where the pairing force erodes the quadrupole dominance. Calculations in $10^7$ and $10^{10}$-dimensional spaces exhibit almost identical patterns: A vindication of the shell model. [Results for $N\ge 64$] Nilsson-SU3 calculations describe BE2 patterns in [112-120]Cd and [116-118]Sn isotopes having sizable quadrupole moment of non-rotational origin denoted as q-vibrations. No calculations are proposed for the heavier species, for which the conventional seniority dscription is assumed for Sn, while in Cd the quadrupole moments change sign. [Conclusion] A radical reexamination of traditional interpretations in the region has been shown to be necessary, in which quadrupole dominance plays a major role. What emerges is a bumpy but coherent view.

# Quadrupole dominance in the light Sn and in the Cd isotopes

## Key claim
Quadrupole dominance, rather than seniority-based pairing, is responsible for the B(E2) enhancement patterns in light Sn (N ≤ 64) and Cd isotopes, but in Sn the pairing force erodes the quadrupole dominance such that pseudo-SU3 symmetry remains only of heuristic value, whereas in Cd the symmetry produces weak permanent deformation that corroborates the pseudo-SU3 prediction.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed using a minimally renormalized realistic interaction (N3LO-based, with quadrupole and pairing components adjusted by factors q×10% and p×10%, respectively, and an overall 1.1 scaling) in truncated spaces defined by proton and neutron excitations within the g₉/₂ and r₄ orbits, supplemented by quasi-SU3 and pseudo-SU3 symmetry analyses and Nilsson-SU3 self-consistent calculations. The neutron effective charge was treated as the single free parameter, with the proton effective charge fixed at 1.4, and the monopole part of the Hamiltonian was replaced by single-particle energies from the GEMO prescription.

## Context
This work extends the pseudo-SU3 symmetry framework previously applied to rare-earth nuclei (Zuker, 1994) and N=Z nuclei (Zuker, 2018) to the Sn and Cd isotopic chains. It contradicts the traditional seniority-based interpretation of the Sn region, where a parabolic B(E2) trend was taken as evidence of pairing dominance, and instead shows that quadrupole coherence produces the observed plateau for N ≤ 64. The calculations reproduce and explain the B(E2) enhancements first observed by Banu et al. (2005) in ¹⁰⁸Sn and subsequent measurements by Vaman et al. (2007), Cederkäll et al. (2007), and others. For N ≥ 64, the work introduces "q-vibrational" states to describe Cd isotopes where the B(E2: 4⁺→2⁺)/B(E2: 2⁺→0⁺) ratio ≈ 2, a long-standing puzzle since Tamura and Udagawa (1966) observed large static quadrupole moments in vibrational nuclei. The results enable future studies of the transition to seniority-dominated behavior in heavier Sn isotopes and the oblate-prolate competition in Cd near N=72.
