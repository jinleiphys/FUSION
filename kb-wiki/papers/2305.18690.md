# Numerical indication that center vortices drive dynamical mass generation in QCD

> The first calculation of the response of the momentum space quark propagator to center vortices in the ground state fields of QCD is presented. Center vortices are identified on 2+1-flavour dynamical gauge fields with $m_π\simeq 156$ MeV to obtain the vortex-removed and vortex-only quark propagator. Dynamical mass generation is found to vanish upon vortex removal, while the vortex-only field is able to generate dynamical mass. These new signatures strengthen the lattice QCD evidence indicating that center vortices underpin both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement.

## Key claim

The first calculation of the momentum-space quark propagator's response to center vortices in full dynamical QCD shows that dynamical mass generation vanishes upon vortex removal, while vortex-only fields can generate dynamical mass; this result strengthens the evidence that center vortices underpin both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and quark confinement, though the vortex-only mass function exceeds the untouched value in the infrared rather than matching it quantitatively.

## Method

Center vortices were identified on 2+1-flavour dynamical gauge fields from the PACS-CS Collaboration (32^3 x 64 lattice, m_pi = 156 MeV, a = 0.0933 fm) using maximal center gauge fixing and center projection. The overlap quark propagator was calculated in Landau gauge on untouched, vortex-removed, and vortex-only ensembles at a bare quark mass of 6 MeV; the vortex-only field required centrifuge preconditioning and annealed smoothing before the overlap fermion action could be applied. The quark mass function M(p) and renormalized dressing function Z(p) were extracted from the momentum-space propagator using a MOM scheme with renormalization scale nu = 6.8 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the dynamical gauge fields: m_pi = 156 MeV, close to the physical value
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0933 fm, set by the Sommer parameter r_0 = 0.49 fm
- Bare quark mass for valence propagator: m_q = 6 MeV
- Renormalization scale: nu = 6.8 GeV
- Untouched mass function peaks at approximately 350 MeV in the infrared
- Vortex-only mass function peaks at approximately 600 MeV, about 250 MeV above the untouched peak
- Vortex-removed mass function is essentially identical to the tree-level value at all momenta, indicating elimination of dynamical mass generation
- 30 gauge configurations used for untouched and vortex-removed calculations; 60 configurations for vortex-only calculations
- 80 lowest-lying eigenmodes explicitly projected for untouched/vortex-removed; 150 eigenmodes for vortex-only

## Context

This work extends previous pure-gauge SU(3) studies by Trewartha et al. (2015), which found residual dynamical mass (~100 MeV) upon vortex removal in the absence of dynamical fermions, and by Biddle et al. (2022) on vortex-modified string tension in full QCD. It also builds on SU(2) staggered fermion results by Bowman et al. (2008) showing similar vortex-driven mass generation. The results enable future investigations of the vortex-removed hadron spectrum in dynamical QCD and deeper understanding of how dynamical fermions modify center vortex structure compared to pure gauge theory.
