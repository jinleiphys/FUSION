# Effects of the initial-state geometry on D-meson production in pp and pPb collisions

> Going from lower to higher multiplicity events in proton-proton and proton-lead collisions, the data show a stronger than linear growth of the D-meson normalized yields. In this contribution we try to understand this behavior using a Monte Carlo event generator which implements the $k_T$-factorization formalism. We use different spatial distribution of matter in the proton and in the lead nucleus at the initial stage of these collisions. We find that, with all the tested spatial distributions, the model reproduces well the behavior seen in the data. We conclude that this observable is not appropriate for a detailed study of the spatial distribution of matter in the proton.

## Key claim
The observed stronger-than-linear growth of D-meson normalized yields with event multiplicity in pp and pPb collisions can be reproduced by a Monte Carlo event generator using any of the tested spatial distributions of matter in the proton (hard-sphere, Gaussian, analytical baryon junction, or numerical baryon junction), meaning this observable cannot discriminate between those distributions.

## Method
The authors used the MC-KLN event generator, which implements the kT-factorization formalism with KLN unintegrated gluon distributions, to simulate gluon production in pp and pPb collisions at midrapidity. Four different nucleon spatial configurations (hard-sphere, Gaussian, analytical baryon junction BJ1, and numerical baryon junction BJ2) were inserted through thickness functions and overlap functions, with intrinsic fluctuations in the saturation scale parametrized by a log-normal distribution with width sigma. D-meson yields were obtained by convoluting the gluon cross section with the g to D KKKS08 fragmentation function at factorization scale mu = mD = 1.8 GeV, while charged particle multiplicities were computed assuming parton-hadron duality.

## Key numbers
- D-meson relative yields were computed in two transverse momentum ranges: 1 < pT < 2 GeV and 2 < pT < 4 GeV for both pp and pPb collisions.
- The saturation scale parameter used was bar-lambda = 0.23 in the formula Qs^2(x, r_perp) = T(r_perp) * (2 GeV^2 / 1.53 fm^-2) * (0.01/x)^bar-lambda.
- The theoretical curves begin to split at dN/deta / <dN/deta> approximately equal to 3, with the high-multiplicity region defined as dN/deta / <dN/deta> greater than or approximately equal to 4.
- The D-meson mass used as factorization scale was mD = 1.8 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the simulations of Terra, Giannini, and Navarra (2025) on multiplicity distributions in pp and pPb collisions to the study of D-meson relative yields, following earlier work by the same authors on J/psi relative yields using baryon junction initial conditions. The results contradict the expectation that the D-meson relative yield observable could distinguish between different proton spatial configurations (hard-sphere, Gaussian, or baryon junction models from Glazek (2020), Deb (2020), and Schenke), and instead show that all models reproduce the ALICE data within current experimental precision. The paper enables future studies to focus on other observables or await higher-statistics LHC data to constrain the proton matter distribution.
