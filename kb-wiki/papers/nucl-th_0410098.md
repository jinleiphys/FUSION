# Some aspects of $Θ^+$ parity determination in the reaction $γN\to Θ^+ \bar{K}\to N K \bar{K}$

> We analyze the problem of how to determine the parity of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark in the reaction $γN\to KΘ\to NK\bar{K}$, where $N=n,p$. Our model calculations indicate that the contribution of the non-resonant background of the reaction $γN\to NK\bar{K}$ cannot be neglected, and that suggestions to determine the parity based solely on the initial-stage process $γN\to KΘ$ cannot be implemented cleanly. We discuss the various mechanisms that contribute to the background, and we identify some spin observables which are sensitive.

## Key claim

The parity of the Θ⁺ pentaquark cannot be determined cleanly from the reaction γN → Θ⁺ K̅ → N K K̅ using only the initial-stage process, because the non-resonant background from γN → N K K̅ contributes significantly and modifies the spin observables; however, some spin observables remain sensitive mostly to the Θ⁺ parity rather than to the details of the production mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed model calculations using an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level diagrams for the resonant process γN → Θ⁺ K̅ (including s-, u-, t-channel, and contact terms) and for the non-resonant background (including vector meson ρ, ω, φ, scalar σ, and tensor a₂, f₂ meson exchanges). They computed unpolarized and spin observables for both γp → p K⁰ K̅⁰ and γn → n K⁺ K⁻ reactions at a photon energy of Eγ ≈ 2 GeV, with the Θ⁺ mass fixed at M₀ = 1540 MeV, and compared predictions for positive (Jᴾ = 1/2⁺) and negative (Jᴾ = 1/2⁻) parity assignments.

## Key numbers

- The observed ratio of the resonance peak to the non-resonant continuum in photoproduction experiments is about 1.5–2, meaning the difference between resonant and non-resonant amplitudes is a factor of two.
- At Eγ ≈ 2 GeV, the K K̅ invariant mass is distributed in the region 1 GeV ≲ M_{KK̅} ≲ 1.2 GeV, which straddles the φ meson mass.
- The coupling constants for tensor meson decays are g_{a₂KK} = 4.9 and g_{f₂KK} = 7.4, derived from known decay widths.
- The estimated coupling constants for tensor meson photoproduction are e g_{a₂ωγ} = 0.29 and e g_{f₂ργ} = 0.14.
- The Θ⁺ mass is taken as M₀ = 1540 MeV; all experiments give only upper limits of the decay width (about 25 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Ejiri (2004), Rekalo and Tomassi-Gustafsson (2004), and Nakayama and Love (2004) for using "model-independent" triple spin observables to determine the Θ⁺ parity in γN → Θ⁺ K̅, but shows that these strict predictions lose definiteness when applied to the full three-body final state γN → N K K̅ where the Θ⁺ decays. The analysis builds on effective Lagrangian models of Θ⁺ photoproduction by Hosaka (2003), Oh, Ko, and Lee (2003), and others, and incorporates vector meson photoproduction models from Titov, Lee, and others (1999, 2002). The results enable future experimental analyses to identify kinematic regions where the parity dependence is clear despite background contamination.
