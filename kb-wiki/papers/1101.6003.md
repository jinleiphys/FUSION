# Role of quantum fluctuations in a system with strong fields

> In this work we study how quantum fluctuations modify the quantum evolution of an initially classical field theory. We consider a scalar $ϕ^4$ theory coupled to an external source as a toy model for the Color Glass Condensate description of the early time dynamics of heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that quantum fluctuations considerably modify the time evolution driving the system to evolve in accordance with ideal hydrodynamics. We attempt to understand the mechanism behind this relaxation to ideal hydrodynamics by using modified initial spectra and studying the particle content of the theory.

# Role of quantum fluctuations in a system with strong fields

## Key claim
Quantum fluctuations modify the time evolution of an initially classical scalar φ⁴ field theory, driving the system to evolve in accordance with ideal hydrodynamics, but this relaxation to a well-defined equation of state (prethermalization) does not necessarily imply thermal equilibrium; the system may not be in thermal equilibrium even after the equation of state becomes time-independent.

## Method
The author considers a scalar φ⁴ theory coupled to an external source that initializes a classical field with occupation number ~1/g², then evolves the system with quantum fluctuations superimposed. For the homogeneous case, analytic solutions using Jacobi elliptic functions are obtained and ensemble averages over Gaussian fluctuations are performed. For the full 3D case with space-dependent fluctuations, the author performs semi-classical simulations on a 12³ lattice with an ensemble of 1000 configurations, using the quantum field theory power spectrum as initial conditions, and also studies modified spectra to isolate the role of different momentum modes.

## Key numbers
- Model parameters: φ₀ = 12, φ̇₀ = 0, g = 0.5 (with a factor of 4! in the potential)
- For the homogeneous system, the relaxation time is τ_relax ≈ 2/(gσ) ≈ 20 for σ = 0.4
- The zero-mode effective mass is m² ≡ (gφ₀)²/2 = 18, with oscillation period T ≈ 3
- The resonance band at t = 0 lies between k_res ≈ 3 and k_res ≈ 3.22
- For the homogeneous zero-mode fluctuations, the relaxation time from the power spectrum is τ_relax ≈ 180 for g = 0.5
- The final number density at late times follows n_k ~ ω_k^(-s) with s = 1.45, which does not match the classical thermal equilibrium expectation of 1/ω_k
- The 3D simulation relaxes about 3–4 times faster than the homogeneous estimate
- For fixed energy density with φ₀ ~ 1/g^(1/2), the relaxation time scales as τ_relax ~ 1/g^(3/4)

## Context
This work extends the resummation approach for secular divergences first presented in Dusling (2010) to a scalar φ⁴ toy model of the Color Glass Condensate description of heavy-ion collisions. The rapid establishment of an equation of state via phase decoherence (prethermalization) was previously studied in a linear σ-model using the 2PI effective action by Berges (2004). The resummation of secular divergences is qualitatively similar to the resummation of leading logarithms (g² ln(1/x₁,₂))ⁿ used for inclusive quantities in the CGC framework by Gelis, Lappi, and Venugopalan. Considerable related work exists for φ⁴ theory in the context of reheating after inflation by Prokopec, Frolov, and Felder, and for thermalization in heavy-ion collisions using N-particle irreducible effective actions by Berges, Aarts, and collaborators. The methods shown here are suggested to be extendable to classical Yang-Mills theories, with work in progress by Dusling, Gelis, and Venugopalan.
