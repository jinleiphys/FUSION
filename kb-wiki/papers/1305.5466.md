# Relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions in charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering at ArgoNeuT kinematics

> The analysis of the recent charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections measured by the ArgoNeuT Collaboration requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final-state interactions. In this work, we evaluate differential neutrino-nucleus cross sections with the relativistic Green's function model, where final-state interactions are described in the inclusive scattering consistently with the exclusive scattering using a complex optical potential. The sensitivity to the parameterization adopted for the phenomenological optical potential is discussed. The predictions of the relativistic Green's function model are compared with the results of different descriptions of final-state interactions.

# Relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions in charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering at ArgoNeuT kinematics

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which uses a complex optical potential to describe final-state interactions, produces larger charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections on 40Ar than the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) or the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach, but all three models generally underpredict the ArgoNeuT experimental data, particularly at low muon momentum and for muon scattering angles between 5 and 20 degrees. The authors note that models based on the impulse approximation may neglect important contributions from processes beyond quasielastic scattering at the few-GeV energies of the ArgoNeuT flux.

## Method

The authors evaluate flux-averaged differential cross sections for charged-current muon neutrino scattering on 40Ar using three relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions: RPWIA (where final-state interactions are neglected), RMF (where the same real relativistic mean field potential is used for both bound and scattering states), and RGF (where a complex optical potential with real and imaginary parts accounts for final-state interactions consistently in inclusive and exclusive scattering). For the RGF calculations, three parameterizations of the phenomenological optical potential are used (EDAD1, DEM, and UNDEM), and the bound nucleon states are taken as self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean field approach with medium-dependent meson-nucleon vertices. The cross sections are integrated over the ArgoNeuT muon neutrino flux with an average energy of 4.3 GeV and energies up to 50 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Average neutrino energy of the ArgoNeuT flux: ⟨Eν⟩ = 4.3 GeV, with energies up to 50 GeV.
- In the peak region at muon scattering angle θ ≈ 5-7 degrees, the RGF-DEM and RGF-EDAD1 cross sections differ by about 20% to 30%.
- The standard value of the axial mass used in all calculations is MA = 1.03 GeV/c².
- The first two measurements in the low energy bins of muon momentum Pμ are underestimated by all models by a factor of 2.
- Numerical uncertainties on the second term of the hadron tensor in the RGF calculation are estimated at most within 10%.

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons of relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering (Meucci 2009) and for charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering on 12C (Meucci 2011) to the ArgoNeuT kinematics on 40Ar. The RGF model had previously been shown to describe both charged-current quasielastic and neutral-current elastic MiniBooNE data without increasing the axial mass MA above the standard value (Meucci 2011, Meucci 2011). The results indicate that impulse-approximation-based models, while reproducing the shape and order of magnitude of the ArgoNeuT data, generally underpredict the experimental cross sections, suggesting that a careful evaluation of multinucleon emission, non-nucleonic contributions such as Delta excitations, and a better determination of the relativistic optical potential are needed to reduce theoretical uncertainties and recover missing inclusive strength.
