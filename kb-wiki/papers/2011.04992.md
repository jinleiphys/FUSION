# Three-body structure of $^{19}$B: Finite-range effects in two-neutron halo nuclei

> The structure and $B(E1)$ transition strength of $^{19}$B are investigated in a $^{17}\text{B}+n+n$ model, triggered by a recent experiment showing that $^{19}$B exhibits a well pronounced two-neutron halo structure. Preliminary analysis of the experimental data was performed by employing contact $n$-$n$ interactions, which are known to underestimate the $s$-wave content in other halo nuclei such as $^{11}$Li. In the present work, the three-body hyperspherical formalism with finite-range two-body interactions is used to describe $^{19}$B. In particular, two different finite-range $n$-$n$ interactions will be used, as well as a simple central Gaussian potential whose range is progressively reduced. The purpose is to determine the main properties of the nucleus and investigate how they change when using contact-like $n$-$n$ potentials. Special attention is also paid to the dependence on the prescription used to account for three-body effects, i.e., a three-body force or a density-dependent $n$-$n$ potential. We have found that the three-body model plus finite-range potentials provide a description of $^{19}$B consistent with the experimental data. The results are essentially independent of the short-distance details of the two-body potentials, giving rise to an $(s_{1/2})^2$ content of about 55%, clearly larger than the initial estimates. Very little dependence has been found as well on the prescription used for the three-body effects. The total computed $B(E1)$ strength is compatible with the experimental result, although we slightly overestimate the data around the low-energy peak of the $dB(E1)/d\varepsilon$ distribution. Finally, we show that a reduction of the $n$-$n$ interaction range produces a significant reduction of the $s$-wave contribution, which then should be expected in calculations using contact interactions.

## Key claim

The three-body model with finite-range two-body interactions provides a description of \(^{19}\)B consistent with experimental data, yielding an \((s_{1/2})^2\) content of about 55%, clearly larger than the initial estimates from contact interactions, and the results are essentially independent of the short-distance details of the two-body potentials and of the prescription used for three-body effects.

## Method

The authors describe \(^{19}\)B as a \(^{17}\text{B}+n+n\) three-body system using the hyperspherical formalism, expanding the wave function in hyperspherical harmonics up to \(K_{\text{max}}=30\) and solving the hyperradial equations via the pseudostate method with a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator basis. Two finite-range \(n\)-\(n\) interactions (GPT and GFJ potentials) and a series of central Gaussian potentials with progressively reduced range are employed, with the two-neutron separation energy fixed to \(S_{2n}=0.5\) MeV either by a Gaussian three-body force or by a density-dependent term in the \(n\)-\(n\) potential.

## Key numbers

- \((s_{1/2})^2\) content of the \(^{19}\)B ground state: 53.2% (GPT + three-body force), 56.3% (GFJ + three-body force), 54.7% (GPT + density-dependent term), 57.5% (GFJ + density-dependent term).
- \((d_{5/2})^2\) content of the \(^{19}\)B ground state: 39.2% (GPT + three-body force), 35.6% (GFJ + three-body force), 38.2% (GPT + density-dependent term), 34.7% (GFJ + density-dependent term).
- For the narrowest Gaussian potential (range \(b=0.4\) fm), \((s_{1/2})^2\) content drops to 21.5% (with three-body force) and 24.1% (with density-dependent term).
- Root-mean-square distances with GPT interaction: \(\langle r_x^2\rangle^{1/2}=7.28\) fm, \(\langle r_y^2\rangle^{1/2}=5.01\) fm; with contact-like Gaussian 1: 5.96 fm and 3.90 fm, respectively.
- Total \(B(E1)\) strength up to 6 MeV with GPT interaction: \(1.53\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\), compatible with the experimental value \(1.64\pm0.06(\text{stat})\pm0.12(\text{sys})\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).
- With Gaussian 2 potential (36.8% \(s\)-wave content), \(B(E1)\) strength up to 6 MeV is \(1.26\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the three-body description of two-neutron halo nuclei to \(^{19}\)B, following the experimental observation of its halo structure by Cook (2020). It directly addresses the known underestimation of \(s\)-wave content in contact-interaction calculations, previously noted for \(^{11}\)Li by Esbensen (1997) and later confirmed by Zinser (1997), Garrido (1997), Casal (2017a), and Gomez-Ramos (2017a). The results show that finite-range \(n\)-\(n\) potentials are necessary to obtain the correct \(s\)-wave fraction and \(B(E1)\) strength, and that contact-like interactions produce overly compact wave functions and underestimate the dipole response. This enables more reliable predictions for the structure of other two-neutron halo nuclei and clarifies the role of interaction range in three-body models.
