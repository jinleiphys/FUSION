# Exchange assisted tunneling and positron annihilation on inner atomic shells

> It is known for a long time that the long range asymptotic behavior of the Hartree-Fock orbitals is different from that of the orbitals in the local potential. However, there is no consensus about observable physical effects associated with this asymptotics. Here we argue that weaker decrease of the Hartree-Fock orbitals at large distances is responsible for the positron annihilation on the inner shell electrons, which is observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the exchange interaction between inner and outer electrons changes the long-range asymptotic behavior of inner-shell Hartree-Fock orbitals, making them decay with the ionization potential of the atom rather than with the individual orbital energy, and that this effect is observable in positron annihilation on inner atomic shells.

## Method

The authors derive the asymptotic form of one-particle orbitals from the many-electron wave function using a projection onto eigenstates of the (N-1)-electron ion, obtaining coupled equations for the amplitudes. They show that at large distances the exchange interaction couples inner orbitals to the outermost orbital, leading to an asymptotic decay governed by the outermost orbital's energy. They then apply this formalism to positron annihilation, estimating the classical turning point for a thermal positron and computing orbital ratios from relativistic Hartree-Fock-Dirac calculations for Be and Xe atoms.

## Key numbers

- For Be in the configuration 1s²2p², the positron turning point is estimated as r_t ≈ 4.2 a.u.; at this distance the 1s orbital with exchange is about one order of magnitude larger than without exchange, enhancing the annihilation rate (proportional to probability density) by approximately two orders of magnitude.
- For Be in 1s²2p², the ratio of 2p to 1s orbitals at r ≈ 4 a.u. is on the order of 10², leading to an estimated 1s contribution to the annihilation rate of about 10⁻⁴, compared to 10⁻⁶ without exchange assisted tunneling.
- For Xe, the total probability of annihilation on the inner shells 4d, 4p, and 4s was found experimentally to be 2.4%; the classical turning point for the positron is r_t ≈ 3.1 a.u., where the ratio of 5p to 4d orbitals is close to 10.
- The annihilation linewidth is given by Γ ≈ √(|ε_i| m_e c²) ≈ 3.7 × √(|ε_i|) keV, where ε_i is the binding energy of the electron.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Hartree-Fock asymptotic behavior first noted by Handy, Marron, and Silverstone (1969) and later analyzed by Drukarev, Fedorov, and Safronova (1982) and Flambaum (2009). It contradicts the assumption that inner orbitals decay with their own binding energy, as would follow from scattering theory. The paper enables interpretation of positron annihilation experiments on noble gases by Ivanov et al. (1997) and coincidence measurements by Eijndhoven et al. (2002), and suggests that exchange-assisted tunneling is more observable in positron annihilation than in field ionization proposed by Amusia (2009).
