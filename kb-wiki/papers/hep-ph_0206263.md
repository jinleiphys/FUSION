# Tetraquark bound states in a constituent quark model and the nature of the a_0(980) and f_0(980)

> In this work we study tetraquark bound states in the framework of the constituent quark model of Ref. [2], which has been used for the description of non-strange two- and three-baryon systems and later on applied to the hadron spectra.

# Tetraquark bound states in a constituent quark model and the nature of the a_0(980) and f_0(980)

## Key claim

The constituent quark model predicts stable tetraquark bound states in the light-heavy sector, but the scalar mesons a_0(980) and f_0(980) can only be described as tetraquarks if the confinement strength is artificially reduced by a factor of 0.765 relative to the meson sector, indicating that the model's two-body confinement ansatz is insufficient for these systems.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for tetraquark systems using a variational method with a trial wave function expressed as a linear combination of gaussians in the Jacobi coordinates of the four-body system. The calculation employed the full color-space wave function including both the {3̅3} and {6̅6} color singlet components, and the interaction was based on a constituent quark model with gluon and Goldstone boson exchanges, with parameters previously fixed from the NN interaction and baryon and meson spectra.

## Key numbers

- For light-heavy tetraquarks [(qq)(b̅b̅)] with (I,S)=(0,1): total energy E_T = 10072 MeV, binding energy ΔE = -509 MeV (the most bound system found)
- For [(qq)(c̅c̅)] with (I,S)=(0,1): total energy E_T = 3556 MeV, binding energy ΔE = -254 MeV
- For [(qq)(b̅b̅)] with (I,S)=(1,0): total energy E_T = 10493 MeV, binding energy ΔE = -64 MeV
- For [(qq)(b̅b̅)] with (I,S)=(1,2): total energy E_T = 10509 MeV, binding energy ΔE = -96 MeV
- For [(qq)(b̅b̅)] with (I,S)=(0,2): total energy E_T = 11251 MeV, binding energy ΔE = +646 MeV (unbound)
- For the scalar meson candidates with the standard confinement: E(I=0) = 1282 MeV and E(I=1) = 1275 MeV, both too high to match the experimental a_0(980) and f_0(980)
- With reduced confinement (a_c^Tetraquark = 0.765 * a_c^Meson): E(I=0) = 987 MeV and E(I=1) = 981 MeV, nearly degenerate and matching the experimental masses
- Spatial exchange terms contributed less than 0.5% to the binding energy in all cases tested

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Fernandez and Valcarce (previous work), originally developed for non-strange two- and three-baryon systems, to tetraquark bound states. It builds on the suggestion by Jaffe (1977) that q²q̅² configurations could explain the light scalar mesons without orbital excitation. The results contradict earlier studies by Stancu and collaborators that neglected the {6̅6} color component and spatial exchange terms, which had incorrectly forbidden certain (I,S) channels. The finding that the a_0(980) and f_0(980) can be described as tetraquarks only with an ad hoc reduction of the confinement strength opens the question of whether three- and four-body confinement terms, not included in the model, are essential for a consistent description.
