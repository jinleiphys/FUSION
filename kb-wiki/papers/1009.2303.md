# Superfluid dynamics in neutron star crusts

> A simple description of superfluid hydrodynamics in the inner crust of a neutron star is given. Particular attention is paid to the effect of the lattice of nuclei on the properties of the superfluid neutrons, and the effects of entrainment, the fact that some fraction of the neutrons are locked to the motion of the protons in nuclei.

# Superfluid dynamics in neutron star crusts

## Key claim
The paper provides a simple hydrodynamic description of superfluid neutrons in the inner crust of a neutron star, emphasizing that entrainment, where some fraction of neutrons are locked to the motion of protons in nuclei, modifies the effective mass of nuclei and couples lattice vibrations to superfluid sound waves. The authors derive that an appropriately defined neutron superfluid density, not simply the density of neutrons between nuclei, governs both the effective inertia of nuclei and the strength of vector coupling between modes, as a consequence of Galilean invariance.

## Method
The authors formulate linearized hydrodynamic equations for the coupled system of superfluid neutrons and a crystalline lattice of nuclei, treating the nucleons as nonrelativistic and neglecting gravitational potential variations across the crust. They derive conservation laws for neutron and proton number, momentum conservation including elastic contributions from the lattice, and the Josephson relation for the superfluid phase, then solve for the dispersion relations of long-wavelength collective modes. The analysis uses a coarse-grained average phase for the superfluid and expresses the neutron current density in terms of a superfluid density \(n_n^s\) and a bound-neutron density \(n_n^b = n_n - n_n^s\).

## Key numbers
- The superfluid coherence length is given by \(\xi \sim \hbar v_{Fn}/\Delta_n\), where \(v_{Fn}\) is the neutron Fermi velocity and \(\Delta_n\) the neutron superfluid energy gap.
- The transverse sound velocity in the crust is \(v_t^2 = S / [(n_p + n_n^b)m]\), where \(S\) is the shear modulus, \(n_p\) the proton density, \(n_n^b\) the bound-neutron density, and \(m\) the nucleon mass.
- The longitudinal mode dispersion relation is a quadratic coupling equation involving \(n_n^s\), \(n_n^b\), the bulk modulus contribution \(\tilde K\), the shear modulus \(S\), the coupling constant \(L = n_p \partial \mu_n / \partial n_p\), and the interaction \(E_{nn} = \partial \mu_n / \partial n_n\).
- The effective mass of nuclei includes a factor \(1 + n_n^b / n_p\), so the mass density of nuclei is \(m(n_p + n_n^b)/n_I\).
- The neutron effective mass \(m_n^{\text{eff}}\) can be as large as \(\sim 15m\) in some treatments, but only the combination \(n_n^{\text{eff}} / m_n^{\text{eff}}\) enters the hydrodynamics.

## Context
This work extends the two-fluid model for superfluid helium-4 (Landau and Lifshitz) to the neutron star crust, incorporating the periodic lattice of nuclei and entrainment effects previously studied in the liquid core (Sauls, 1989). It builds on recent work by Chamel and collaborators (CCH2) who identified the importance of neutron entrainment by protons in the crust. The formalism enables calculation of torsional modes relevant to quasiperiodic oscillations in giant flares (Duncan, 1998; Strohmayer, 1991; Andersson, 2007) and thermal transport by superfluid neutrons (Aguilera, 2009), with detailed numerical applications promised in future work.
