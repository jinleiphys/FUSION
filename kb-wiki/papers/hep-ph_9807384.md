# On the absence of fifth-order contributions to the nucleon mass in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory

> (New version with some expanded discussion; figures and minor typos corrected.) We have calculated the contribution proportional to the fifth power of the pion mass in the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass in two flavour HBCPT. Only one irreducible two-loop integral enters, and this vanishes. All other corrections in the heavy-baryon limit can be absorbed in the physical pion-nucleon coupling constant which enters in the third order term, and so there are no contributions at fifth order. Including finite nucleon mass corrections, the only contribution agrees with the expansion of the relativistic one-loop graph in powers of the ration of the pion and nucleon masses, and is only 0.3% of the third order term. This is an encouraging result for the convergence of two-flavour heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The authors show that in two-flavour heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT), the fifth-order (proportional to the fifth power of the pion mass) contribution to the nucleon mass vanishes in the heavy-baryon limit, with all corrections absorbed into the physical pion-nucleon coupling constant that enters at third order; the only non-zero fifth-order piece comes from finite nucleon mass corrections and amounts to only 0.3% of the third-order term.

## Method

The authors calculated all Feynman diagrams contributing to the nucleon self-energy at order \(q^5\) in two-flavour HBCPT, including two-loop diagrams, one-loop diagrams with insertions from \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(3)}\) and \({\cal L}_{\pi\pi}^{(4)}\), and diagrams with two insertions from \({\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\). They evaluated the irreducible two-loop integral using Feynman parameters and dimensional regularisation, and accounted for renormalisation of the bare pion mass and pion-nucleon coupling constant to their physical values. The mass shift was obtained by solving the pole condition for the heavy-baryon propagator expanded to fifth order.

## Key numbers

* The fifth-order contribution from finite nucleon mass corrections is only 0.3% of the third-order term.
* The third-order contribution to the nucleon mass shift is \(\delta m^{(3)} = -3 g_A^2 M_\pi^3 / (32 \pi F_\pi^2)\).
* The combined third- and fifth-order result is \(\delta m^{(3)} + \delta m^{(5)} = -3 g_{\pi NN}^2 M_\pi^3 / (32 \pi m_N^2) \left(1 - M_\pi^2 / (8 m_N^2)\right)\).
* The pion-nucleon sigma commutator is estimated from scattering data to be \(45 \pm 8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass in two-flavour HBCPT, following earlier calculations to third order by Meissner et al. (1995, 1998) and Mojzis (1998). The vanishing of the irreducible two-loop integral is described as essentially accidental, and the authors note that Borasoy and Meissner had conjectured that fifth-order contributions would come mainly from one-loop diagrams rather than genuine two-loop diagrams. The result enables a clearer assessment of convergence of the chiral expansion for the nucleon mass, though the fourth-order piece (calculated by Stein et al., 1998) still involves unknown low-energy constants that must be determined from pion-nucleon scattering data.
