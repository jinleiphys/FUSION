# K+/pi+ probes of jet quenching in AA collisions

> Non-abelian energy loss in quark gluon plasma is shown to lead to novel hadron ratio suppression patterns in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. We apply recent (GLV) estimates for the gluon radiative energy loss, which increases linearly with the jet energy up to E<20 GeV and depends quadratically on the nuclear radius, R. The K+/π+ ratio is found to be most sensitive to the initial density of the plasma.

# K+/pi+ Probes of Jet Quenching in AA Collisions

## Key claim
The K+/pi+ ratio in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies is predicted to show a factor of 2-3 enhancement in the pT range of 3-6 GeV for an effective static opacity of n-bar = 4, but this enhancement depends on the specific GLV energy loss model which gives an approximately constant fractional energy loss Delta E/E, unlike earlier models that predicted a stronger pT-dependent suppression.

## Method
The authors applied leading-order perturbative QCD (pQCD) with parton distribution functions (MRST98) and fragmentation functions (BKK95) to compute hadron production in p+p and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV. They incorporated the GLV radiative energy loss, which depends linearly on jet energy up to E < 20 GeV and quadratically on nuclear radius R, by shifting the jet energy before fragmentation using the relation p_c* = p_c - Delta E(E,L) and modifying the fragmentation function scale accordingly. The calculation used slab geometry with average screening scale mu = 0.5 GeV, strong coupling alpha_s = 0.3, and gluon mean free path lambda_g = 1 fm, varying the effective opacity n-bar = L/lambda_g.

## Key numbers
- The GLV energy loss Delta E(E,L) scales as approximately E * (L/6 fm)^2 for jet energies up to E < 20 GeV
- The K+/pi+ ratio shows a factor of 2-3 enhancement in the pT range of 3-6 GeV for effective opacity n-bar = 4
- The analysis covers the transverse momentum range 2 < pT < 10 GeV at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV
- The impact parameter b_max = 4.7 fm for the 10% most central Au+Au collisions
- The recently reported pi0 suppression corresponds to an effective static opacity in the range n-bar approximately 3-4

## Context
This work extends the GLV energy loss formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2001) to hadron ratios, building on earlier estimates by Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and BDMS (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Schiff, 1997) that predicted different energy loss dependencies on plasma thickness. The authors note that earlier analyses of Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 20 AGeV (Wang, 1998; Gyulassy, Levai, 1994) indicated negligible energy loss at that energy, while the present work enables quantitative predictions for RHIC experiments to distinguish between different jet quenching mechanisms by measuring the K+/pi+ ratio. The approach contrasts with the energy-dependent fractional energy loss model of Wang (1998) which used Delta E/E = (0.5 GeV/E)(L/fm), and the results suggest that fluctuations in energy loss simply renormalize the effective opacity needed to obtain a fixed quenching factor.
