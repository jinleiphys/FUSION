# Heavy-Flavour Pentaquarks in a Chiral Constituent Quark Model

> Within the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska, we discuss the stability of heavy pentaquarks, i.e. hadrons containing four light quarks and a heavy antiquark. The spin-dependent part of the Hamiltonian is dominated by the short-range part of the Goldstone-boson-exchange interaction. We find that these systems are not bound, having an energy above the lowest dissociation threshold into a baryon and a meson.

## Key claim

Within the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska, heavy-flavour pentaquarks (four light quarks and a heavy antiquark) are not bound as compact objects, having an energy several hundred MeV above the lowest dissociation threshold into a baryon and a meson; however, the authors note that the Goldstone-boson-exchange interaction also generates a long-range attraction that could favour a deuteron-size molecular-type bound state, which is not studied in this work.

## Method

The authors use a Hamiltonian with a linear confining interaction and a spin-spin component of the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) interaction in its SU_F(3) form, with parameters from Glozman, Papp and Plessas (1996) that reproduce the light-quark baryon spectrum. They assume a symmetric [4]_O orbital wave function for the four-light-quark subsystem in a colour [211]_C state, and perform variational calculations with a trial wave function using Jacobi coordinates to obtain pentaquark masses for systems with strangeness S=0,-1,-2,-3 and heavy antiquark Q=c or b. The heavy-quark mass is adjusted to reproduce the experimental average mass of D or B mesons.

## Key numbers

- For uudd\bar{c}: energy 3.607 GeV, threshold (N + \bar{D}) 2.978 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.629 GeV
- For uudd\bar{b}: energy 6.889 GeV, threshold (N + \bar{B}) 6.272 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.617 GeV
- For uuds\bar{c}: energy 3.545 GeV, threshold (N + \bar{D}_s) 3.057 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.488 GeV
- For uuds\bar{b}: energy 6.827 GeV, threshold (N + \bar{B}_s) 6.349 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.478 GeV
- For udss\bar{c}: energy 3.630 GeV, threshold (\Lambda + \bar{D}_s) 3.253 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.377 GeV
- For udss\bar{b}: energy 6.911 GeV, threshold (\Lambda + \bar{B}_s) 6.544 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.367 GeV
- For usss\bar{c}: energy 3.940 GeV, threshold (\Xi + \bar{D}_s) 3.464 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.476 GeV
- For usss\bar{b}: energy 7.223 GeV, threshold (\Xi + \bar{B}_s) 6.756 GeV, giving E - E_T = 0.467 GeV
- The lowering of E - E_T when replacing charm by bottom is small, of order 10 MeV
- The constant C_chi in the simplified GBE interaction is about 30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of heavy-flavour tetraquarks within the chiral constituent quark model (Pepin, Stancu, Genovese and Richard, 1997), where the GBE interaction was found to strongly bind QQ\bar{q}\bar{q} systems, at variance with models based on one-gluon exchange. The present results differ from earlier predictions of stable compact pentaquarks based on one-gluon exchange (Gignoux et al., 1987; Lipkin, 1987) and from calculations using instanton or Skyrme models that indicated possible bound states or near-threshold resonances. The authors suggest that future work should investigate deuteron-like baryon-meson molecular states using dynamical few-body approaches such as the resonating group method, and note that including orbital states with two quanta of excitation or the quark-antiquark interaction could lower the pentaquark energy.
