# Non-perturbative heavy quark diffusion coefficients in arbitrarily magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> Heavy quark (HQ) momentum ($κ$) and spatial diffusion ($D_s$) coefficients are computed in a non-perturbative thermal QCD medium in the presence of a background magnetic field of arbitrary strength. Both perturbative and non-perturbative effects are incorporated via the in-medium HQ potential, obtained from the resummed gluon propagator. We find that the momentum diffusion coefficients become anisotropic even in the static heavy quark limit, with the magnetic field direction defining the axis of anisotropy. This anisotropy originates from restrictions on longitudinal momentum diffusion in the gluon spectral function, and naturally leads to two spatial diffusion coefficients ($D_s^L$, $D_s^T$). Non-perturbative effects are found to be dominant at low temperatures. These results provide a more consistent input for Langevin based calculations of the heavy quark directed flow at RHIC and LHC energies.

# Non-perturbative heavy quark diffusion coefficients in arbitrarily magnetized quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficients become anisotropic even in the static (zero-velocity) limit when a background magnetic field of arbitrary strength is present, contrary to the weak-field regime where isotropy is preserved. This anisotropy originates from a delta-function term $\delta(q_z)$ in the imaginary part of the gluon self-energy form factor, which suppresses longitudinal momentum diffusion from a portion of the spectral function while the entire spectral function contributes to transverse diffusion.

## Method

The in-medium heavy quark potential was derived from a resummed gluon propagator whose temporal component was parametrized as the sum of a perturbative (Yukawa) part and a non-perturbative (string) part, with the non-perturbative ansatz constrained to reproduce the confining term of the Cornell potential in vacuum. The potential was used as a proxy for the gluon propagator in the heavy quark self-energy, from which the scattering rate was evaluated using Weldon's formula; the momentum diffusion coefficients $\kappa_L$ and $\kappa_T$ were then obtained by integrating the squared momentum transfer weighted by the differential scattering rate, with a UV cutoff $q_{max}=3.1Tg^{1/3}$ applied to the perturbative integrals.

## Key numbers

- The string-tension-related constant $m_G^2$ is related to the vacuum string tension $\sigma$ by $\sigma = (2/3)\alpha_s m_G^2$, with temperature-dependent spatial string tension taken from Bala (2025).
- The UV cutoff for perturbative integrals is $q_{max} = 3.1\,T\,g^{1/3}$, following Beraudo (2009).
- The string-part integral $I_q^s = \int_0^\infty dq \, q^3/(q^2+m_D'^2)^3 = 1/(4m_D'^2)$ is UV finite.
- The scaled spatial diffusion coefficients $2\pi T D_s^L$ and $2\pi T D_s^T$ are shown as functions of $T/T_c$ at $eB = 0.1\,m_\pi^2$, with the longitudinal coefficient slightly larger than the transverse coefficient across the temperature range shown.
- The ratio of string to Yukawa contributions to $\kappa$ is isotropic: $\kappa_L^s/\kappa_L^Y = \kappa_T^s/\kappa_T^Y$, with non-perturbative effects dominant at low temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in magnetized QCD matter, which were limited to either the weak-field ($eB \ll T^2$) or strong-field ($eB \gg T^2$) regimes, by using the Landau quantization framework valid for arbitrary magnetic field strength (Bandyopadhyay, 2023). The results provide more consistent inputs for Langevin-based calculations of heavy quark directed flow $v_1$ at RHIC and LHC energies, where the interplay between magnetic field and non-perturbative medium effects is expected to be important; the computed spatial diffusion coefficients show broad qualitative agreement with lattice QCD results from Banerjee (2012), Francis (2015), Ding (2012), Brambilla (2020), and Altenkort (2024), though those lattice results were obtained at zero magnetic field.
