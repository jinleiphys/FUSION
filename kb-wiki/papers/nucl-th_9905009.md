# The Two-Nucleon Sector with Effective Field Theory

> I present the results obtained for several observables in the two-nucleon sector using effective field theory with KSW power-counting and dimensional regularization. In addition to the phase shifts for nucleon-nucleon scattering, several deuteron observables are discussed, including electromagnetic form factors, polarizabilities, gamma+d -> gamma+d Compton scattering, and n+p -> d+gamma. A detailed comparison between the effective field theory with pions, the theory without pions, and effective range theory is made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that effective field theory with KSW power-counting and dimensional regularization provides a systematic expansion for two-nucleon observables, but the convergence of perturbative pion exchange remains an open question, as the subleading contribution from potential pion exchange is found to be smaller than that from higher-derivative four-nucleon operators for deuteron observables.

## Method

The author computed nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts in the spin-singlet (¹S₀) and spin-triplet (³S₁-³D₁) channels using KSW power-counting, treating potential pion exchange perturbatively and including local four-nucleon operators up to next-to-next-to-leading order. Deuteron electromagnetic form factors, polarizabilities, Compton scattering, and radiative capture np→dγ were calculated by evaluating three-point correlation functions and applying LSZ reduction, with counterterms fixed by scattering data or the deuteron magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths: a^(¹S₀) = -23.7 fm and a^(³S₁) = 5.4 fm
- Deuteron charge radius: theoretical value √⟨r²⟩_th = 1.89 fm (LO+NLO contributions: 1.53 fm + 0.36 fm) compared to experimental value 2.11 fm
- Effective range theory charge radius: √⟨r²⟩_ER = 1.98 fm, close to the matter radius r_m = 1.967 ± 0.002 fm
- Quadrupole moment: μ_Q,th = 0.40 fm² (NLO) compared to experimental value 0.2859 fm²
- Magnetic moment: μ_M = 0.88 - 0.02 (fit) = 0.86 nuclear magnetons (NLO)
- Electric polarizabilities: α_E0 = 0.595 fm³ and α_E2 = -0.062 fm³ (NLO)
- Radiative capture cross section at LO: σ^LO = 297.2 mb, compared to experimental σ^expt = 334.2 ± 0.5 mb for neutrons at |v| = 2200 m/s
- Mixing parameter coefficient: C_SDzeroone = -4.57 fm⁴ (fit to Nijmegen phase shift analysis)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Weinberg (1990) and Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) by computing a comprehensive set of two-nucleon observables under KSW power-counting, including comparisons with effective range theory of Bethe and Longmire (1950) and Noyes (1965). The results enable future calculations of three-nucleon systems and provide a framework for extracting neutron polarizabilities from gamma-deuteron Compton scattering data, with potential model calculations by Friar (1975) and others serving as benchmarks.
