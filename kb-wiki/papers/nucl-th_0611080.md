# Semiclassical Distorted Wave Model Analysis of the $(π^-,K^+)$ $Σ$ Formation Inclusive Spectrum

> $(π^-,K^+)$ hyperon production inclusive spectra with $p_π=1.2$ GeV/c measured at KEK on $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si are analyzed by the semiclassical distorted wave model. Single-particle wave functions of the target nucleus are treated using Wigner transformation. This method is able to account for the energy and angular dependences of the elementary process in nuclear medium without introducing the factorization approximation frequently employed. Calculations of the $(π^+,K^+)$ $Λ$ formation process, for which there is no free parameter since the $Λ$ s.p. potential is known, demonstrate that the present model is useful to describe inclusive spectra. It is shown that in order to account for the experimental data of the $Σ^-$ formation spectra a repulsive $Σ$-nucleus potential is necessary whose magnitude is not so strong as around 100 MeV previously suggested.

## Key claim

The analysis shows that the KEK $(\pi^-,K^+)$ $\Sigma^-$ formation inclusive spectra on $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si require a repulsive $\Sigma$-nucleus potential, but the magnitude is not as strong as the approximately 100 MeV previously suggested by DWIA analyses in Ref. (KEK, 2005) and Ref. (Saha, 2005), instead favoring a weaker repulsive strength.

## Method

The authors applied the semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model to the $(\pi^-,K^+)$ inclusive reaction, using Wigner transformation of the target nuclear density matrix to treat single-particle wave functions explicitly rather than employing the Thomas-Fermi approximation used in their preparatory work (Kohno, 2005). The elementary $\pi^- + p \rightarrow K^+ + \Sigma^-$ amplitude was parameterized with Legendre polynomial coefficients fitted to experimental data from Good and Kofler (1970), Gou (1964), Dahl (1967), and Doy (1968), and the unobserved hyperon was described by a Woods-Saxon optical potential with a Lorentz-type smearing to simulate inelastic channels.

## Key numbers

- The $\Sigma$ single-particle potential strength favored by the data is repulsive with $U_\Sigma^0$ in the range of approximately 10 to 50 MeV, not the approximately 100 MeV previously suggested by DWIA analyses in Ref. (KEK, 2005).
- For the $\Lambda$ formation test case, the standard $\Lambda$ potential of $V_\Lambda^0 = -30$ MeV reproduces the energy dependence of the inclusive spectra, though absolute values are short by about 35 percent.
- The $\pi^- + p \rightarrow K^+ + \Sigma^-$ total cross section at $\sqrt{s}=1.79$ GeV (corresponding to $p_\pi = 1.2$ GeV/c) yields a forward-angle differential cross section of about 130 $\mu$b in the laboratory frame.
- The half-width for smearing the $\Sigma$ spectra is parameterized as $\Gamma_\Sigma(E)/2 = 10 + 10/(1+(20/E)^2)$ with $E$ in MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SCDW model, originally developed for intermediate energy nucleon reactions (SCDW1, 1995; SCDW2, 1997; SCDW3, 1999), to hyperon production reactions, providing an independent calculational scheme from the DWIA analyses of the KEK data by Ref. (KEK, 2005) and Harada and Hirabayashi (2005). The results contradict the strongly repulsive $\Sigma$ potential of approximately 100 MeV from those earlier analyses and are more consistent with the repulsive potential of about 20 MeV from the analysis of $(K,\pi)$ spectra on $^9$Be by Dabrowski (1998) and the nuclear matter calculations with the SU(6) quark model potential FSS by Kohno (2005), which gives about 20 MeV repulsion at normal density. The method enables future inclusion of multi-step contributions and center-of-mass corrections to improve quantitative reliability.
