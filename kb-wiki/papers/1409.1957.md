# Causal Baryon Diffusion and Colored Noise for Heavy Ion Collisions

> We construct a model for baryon diffusion which has the desired properties of analyticity and causality. The model also has the desired property that the noise correlation function is not a Dirac delta function in space and time. The model depends on three time constants in addition to the diffusion constant. This description can be incorporated into 2nd order viscous hydrodynamical models of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a model for baryon diffusion that ensures both analyticity and causality, and produces a noise correlation function that is not a Dirac delta function in space and time, but the model requires at least third-order dissipative fluid dynamics (the Gurtin-Pipkin equation) to achieve finite correlation lengths and times for noise, whereas second-order (Cattaneo) only gives finite propagation speed.

## Method

The authors generalize the diffusion equation to the Gurtin-Pipkin equation, which includes three time constants (τ₁, τ₂, τ₃) in addition to the diffusion constant D, and derive the corresponding dissipative baryon current. They then apply the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to compute the baryon density response function, the density correlation function, and the noise correlation function for both the Cattaneo equation (second-order) and the Gurtin-Pipkin equation (third-order), analyzing the pole structures and group velocities to verify causality.

## Key numbers

- The critical wave number for the Cattaneo equation is k_c = 1/(4 τ₁ D), below which the density correlator has a pair of imaginary poles and above which it has a pair of complex poles.
- The asymptotic group velocity for the Cattaneo equation is v₀ = √(D/τ₁).
- The Cattaneo noise correlator is ⟨I^i I^j (t, x)⟩ = (σ T/τ₁) δ(x) exp(-|t|/τ₁) δ_{ij}, giving finite range in time but a delta function in space.
- For the Gurtin-Pipkin equation, all three poles lie in the lower half plane if τ₂² < τ₁ τ₃', and the asymptotic group velocity is v₀ = √(τ₃' D/τ₂²).
- The critical wave number for the Gurtin-Pipkin equation when τ₁ > 2τ₂ is k_c² = (τ₁²/τ₂² - 4)/(4 τ₃ D).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hydrodynamic fluctuations in the one-dimensional Bjorken model by Kapusta (2011) and Young (2013), addressing the problem that white noise (a Dirac delta function in space and time) creates severe problems in nonperturbative numerical fluid dynamics with small grid sizes. The model can be incorporated into second-order viscous hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that baryon transport and noise may play a crucial role near a critical point, though microscopic calculations are needed to compute the time constants as functions of temperature and density.
