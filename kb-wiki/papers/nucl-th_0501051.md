# Dirac's inspired point form and hadron form factors

> Noticing that the point-form approach referred to in many recent works implies physics described on hyperplanes, an approach inspired from Dirac's one, which involves a hyperboloid surface, is presented. A few features pertinent to this new approach are emphasized. Consequences as for the calculation of form factors are discussed.

# Dirac's inspired point form and hadron form factors

## Key claim
The paper presents a new point-form approach inspired by Dirac's original formulation, which uses a hyperboloid surface rather than the hyperplanes implied by the "point-form" approach used in many recent works. This new approach improves the asymptotic behavior of form factors for strongly-bound systems compared to the earlier point form, but still shares some deficiencies with instant and front forms using parallel kinematics.

## Method
The author develops a relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) approach based on a hyperboloid-type surface, where the off-energy-shell constraint for a two-body system takes the Lorentz scalar form (p₁+p₂-P)²=0. A wave equation is derived from the square of the momentum operator P^μ, incorporating a Lorentz-type transformation adapted from the Bakamjian-Thomas one, and the interaction is assumed to fulfill constraints that account for higher-order meson-exchange contributions. The approach introduces a new degree of freedom appearing in the definition of the norm and in the velocity operator, which differs from earlier point-form expressions.

## Key numbers
- At high Q², the new point form (D.P.F.) shows a Q⁻⁴ behavior for the pion charge form factor, while the earlier point form ("P.F.") provides a Q⁻⁸ behavior.
- The violation of Poincaré space-time translation invariance at the matrix-element level is a factor of 2-3 for the nucleon and roughly 6 for the pion in the point-form approach.
- The charge squared radius in the new point form scales like the inverse of the squared mass of the system, similar to instant and front forms with parallel kinematics.

## Context
This work extends the earlier "point-form" approach used for nucleon form factors by Wagenbrunn (2000) and for pion form factors by Desplanques (2001, 2002, 2003), which failed to reproduce the asymptotic behavior and showed too fast a drop-off at small Q² for strongly-bound systems. The author notes that the earlier "point form" was identified by Bakamjian (1961) as an "instant form... which displays the symmetry properties inherently present in the point form" and by Sokolov (1985) as involving "hyperplanes orthogonal... to the 4-velocity... of the system." The new approach enables comparison of form factor calculations across different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, revealing that Poincaré space-time translation invariance may be more important than Lorentz invariance for validating approaches, and that the intrinsic Lorentz covariance of the point form is less advantageous than previously expected.
