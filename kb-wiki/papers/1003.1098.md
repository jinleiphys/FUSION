# Study of the $γd\to K^{+}K^{-}np$ reaction and an alternative explanation for the "$Θ^{+}(1540)$ pentaquark" peak

> We present a calculation of the $γd \to K^+ K^- n p $ reaction with the aim of seeing if the experimental peak observed in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass around 1526 MeV, from where evidence for the existence of the $Θ^+$ has been claimed, can be obtained without this resonance as a consequence of the particular dynamics of the process and the cuts applied in the experimental set up. We find that a combination of facts leads indeed to a peak around 1530 MeV for the invariant mass of $K^+ n$ without the need to invoke any new resonance around this energy. This, together with statistical fluctuations that we prove to be large with the statistics of the experiment, is likely to produce the narrower peak observed there.

## Key claim

The peak observed in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass around 1526 MeV, previously claimed as evidence for the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark, can be reproduced without invoking any new resonance, arising instead from a combination of the reaction dynamics, kaon rescattering, and the specific experimental cuts applied at LEPS, together with statistical fluctuations that are large given the experiment's statistics.

## Method

The authors performed a theoretical simulation of the $\gamma d \to K^+ K^- n p$ reaction, modeling $\phi$ production as the dominant mechanism and including rescattering of the $K^+$ or $K^-$ with the spectator nucleon using realistic deuteron wave functions. The $KN$ and $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes were constructed using chiral unitary dynamics, and the $\Lambda(1520)$ production was also included. The cross section was evaluated via Monte Carlo integration in the center-of-mass frame, implementing the same kinematic cuts used in the LEPS experiment to reconstruct the $K^+ n$ invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- The experimental peak observed in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass is around 1526 MeV; the simulation produces a peak around 1530 MeV without any $\Theta^+$ term in the amplitude.
- The $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance has $J^P = 3/2^-$, with mass $M_{\Lambda(1520)}$ and width $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1520)}$ used in its propagator $\mathcal{D}_{\Lambda} = 1/(M_{K^- p} - M_{\Lambda(1520)} + i\Gamma_{\Lambda(1520)}/2)$.
- The $\phi$ meson propagator is given by $1/((k_1+k_2)^2 - M_\phi^2 + i M_\phi \Gamma_\phi)$, with $M_\phi$ the $\phi$ mass and $\Gamma_\phi$ its width.

## Context

This work directly addresses the controversy surrounding the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark, following the LEPS experiment (Nakano et al., 2009) which reported a peak in $\gamma d \to K^+ K^- n p$ at 1526 MeV, while the CLAS experiment (Kinnon et al., 2009) with ten times more statistics and complete kinematics failed to observe the peak. The authors extend earlier theoretical studies of $\phi$ production and kaon rescattering, using chiral unitary dynamics for $KN$ and $\bar{K}N$ interactions (Ramos et al., Bennhold et al., Meissner et al., Magas et al., Felipe et al.) to show that the observed structure is a kinematic artifact rather than a genuine resonance. This work enables a reinterpretation of the LEPS data and suggests that future experiments with higher statistics and full kinematic reconstruction are needed to resolve the pentaquark question.
