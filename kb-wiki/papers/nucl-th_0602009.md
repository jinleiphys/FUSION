# Anisotropic flow at RHIC: How unique is the number-of-constituent-quark scaling?

> The transverse momentum dependence of the anisotropic flow $v_2$ for $π$, $K$, nucleon, $Λ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ is studied for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV within two independent string-hadron transport approaches (RQMD and UrQMD). Although both models reach only 60% of the absolute magnitude of the measured $v_2$, they both predict the particle type dependence of $v_2$, as observed by the RHIC experiments: $v_2$ exhibits a hadron-mass hierarchy (HMH) in the low $p_T$ region and a number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) dependence in the intermediate $p_T$ region. The failure of the hadronic models to reproduce the absolute magnitude of the observed $v_2$ indicates that transport calculations of heavy ion collisions at RHIC must incorporate interactions among quarks and gluons in the early, hot and dense phase. The presence of an NCQ scaling in the string-hadron model results suggests that the particle-type dependencies observed in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate $p_T$ might be related to the hadronic cross sections in vacuum rather than to the hadronization process itself.

## Key claim

The observed number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow \(v_2\) at intermediate transverse momentum is not a unique signature of quark coalescence or deconfinement; hadronic transport models that include only hadronic cross sections (scaling roughly with the number of constituent quarks via the additive quark model) also reproduce the NCQ scaling pattern, though they reach only 60% of the absolute magnitude of the measured \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors used two independent hadron-string transport models, RQMD(v2.4) and UrQMD(v2.2), to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV. They calculated the elliptic flow \(v_2\) as a function of transverse momentum \(p_T\) for pions, kaons, nucleons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi\), and \(\Omega\) hadrons, and compared the results to experimental data from the STAR collaboration. The time evolution of \(v_2\) and the dependence on collision centrality were also analyzed.

## Key numbers

* Both RQMD and UrQMD reach only about **60%** of the absolute magnitude of the measured charged hadron \(v_2\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV.
* The hadron-mass hierarchy (HMH) is observed for \(p_T \le 1.5\) GeV/c, with heavier hadrons exhibiting smaller \(v_2\) values than lighter hadrons.
* The crossing between meson and baryon \(v_2\) occurs at about \(p_T \sim 1.2\) GeV/c in minimum bias collisions.
* NCQ scaling is observed in the intermediate \(p_T\) region (\(p_T > 1\) GeV/c) for all hadrons except pions; the scaling uses \(n_q=2\) for mesons and \(n_q=3\) for baryons.
* In the high \(p_T\) region (\(p_T \ge 2.5\) GeV/c), all \(v_2\) values start to decrease, indicating deviation from ideal hydrodynamic behavior.
* An ordering of elliptic flow values is observed: \(v_2({\rm N}) > v_2(\Lambda) > v_2(\Xi) > v_2(\Omega)\) at fixed \(p_T\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow in hadronic transport models (Bleicher 2000) and directly challenges the interpretation that NCQ scaling observed at RHIC (Voloshin 2002, Fries 2004, Molnar 2004) is a unique signature of partonic coalescence or deconfinement. The finding that hadronic rescattering alone can produce the same scaling pattern implies that detailed comparisons between data and theory are necessary to disentangle hadronic and partonic effects at intermediate \(p_T\) (1.5–5 GeV/c). The authors suggest that high-precision \(v_2\) measurements of resonance hadrons like \(K^*\), \(\rho\), \(\Delta\), \(\Lambda^*\), and \(\Xi^*\) could provide further tests of the deconfinement-plus-recombination hypothesis.
