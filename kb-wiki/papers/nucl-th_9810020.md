# The weak strangeness production reaction $pn \to pΛ$ in a one-boson-exchange model

> The weak production of Lambdas in nucleon-nucleon scattering is studied in a meson-exchange framework. The weak transition operator for the $NN \to N Λ$ reaction is identical to a previously developed weak strangeness-changing transition potential $ΛN \to NN$ that describes the nonmesonic decay of hypernuclei. The initial $NN$ and final $YN$ state interaction has been included by using realistic baryon-baryon forces that describe the available elastic scattering data. The total and differential cross sections as well as the parity-violating asymmetry are studied for the reaction $pn \to pΛ$. These observables are found to be sensitive to the choice of the strong interaction potential and the structure of the weak transition potential.

## Key claim

The weak strangeness production reaction \(pn \to p\Lambda\) has a total cross section on the order of \(10^{-12}\) mb, making it at the borderline of feasibility for existing experimental facilities, and the predictions are highly sensitive to the choice of the strong hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction model used to distort the final-state wave functions.

## Method

The authors employed a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) formalism with a one-boson-exchange weak transition potential, including six mesons (\(\pi, \eta, K, \rho, \omega, K^*\)), identical to the potential previously developed for the nonmesonic decay of hypernuclei (\(\Lambda N \to NN\)). Initial-state \(NN\) and final-state \(YN\) distorted waves were generated from realistic strong potentials: the Nijmegen 93 and Bonn B models for \(NN\), and the Nijmegen soft-core and Jülich A/B models for \(YN\). Total and differential cross sections and the parity-violating asymmetry were computed for the reaction \(pn \to p\Lambda\) as a function of proton lab momentum.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for \(pn \to p\Lambda\) is of the order of \(10^{-12}\) mb, as expected for a weak process.
* The Fermi coupling constant used is \(G_F m_\pi^2 = 2.21 \times 10^{-7}\).
* The relative momentum of the \(pn\) pair ranges from 415 MeV/c to 525 MeV/c over the explored proton lab momenta.
* The opening of the \(\Sigma N\) channel, coupled to the \(\Lambda N\) channel through the strong interaction, appears as a step in the cross section at a proton lab momentum around 1140 MeV/c.
* Including the \(\rho\) meson in the weak potential reduces the pion-only cross section by a factor of 2 or more.
* The differential cross section at a proton lab momentum of 1137 MeV/c shows that adding the \(\rho\) meson to the pion reduces the result by a factor slightly more than 2.

## Context

This work extends the one-boson-exchange weak transition potential previously developed for the nonmesonic decay of hypernuclei (\(\Lambda N \to NN\)) by Parreño, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997) to the inverse free-space reaction \(pn \to p\Lambda\). It provides predictions for an experimentally challenging process that, if measured, could probe the weak strangeness-changing baryon-baryon interaction more directly than hypernuclear decay, which probes only one kinematic setting. The results highlight that current YN scattering data are insufficient to constrain the strong YN interaction models, making it difficult to extract the weak four-fermion interaction from this reaction without additional YN scattering data, especially differential cross sections and polarization observables.
