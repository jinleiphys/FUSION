# D-meson diffusion in hadronic matter

> We present effective-field-theory results with unitarized interactions on the D-meson transport coefficients in a gas populated by light mesons and baryons at finite temperature and baryochemical potential. The Fokker-Planck equation is used to compute the drag force, the relaxation time and the diffusion coefficients of D mesons for collisions at FAIR. At finite baryochemical potential, the combined effect of net baryonic density and sizable meson-baryon interaction makes the D mesons to relax more efficiently than in the case at zero baryochemical potential. We also describe the connection with the quark-gluon plasma phase in adiabatic trajectories on the phase diagram at both zero and finite baryochemical potential.

# D-meson diffusion in hadronic matter

## Key claim
The authors compute D-meson transport coefficients in hadronic matter using effective field theory with unitarized interactions, finding that at finite baryochemical potential (relevant for FAIR collisions) the presence of baryons makes D mesons relax more efficiently than at zero baryochemical potential, though the relaxation times remain longer than the typical fireball lifetime of ~10 fm, meaning D mesons cannot fully equilibrate.

## Method
The authors use the Fokker-Planck kinetic equation to compute the drag force, relaxation time, and diffusion coefficients for D mesons interacting with a thermal bath of light mesons (pions, kaons, antikaons, eta) and baryons (nucleons and Delta baryons). The interaction is described by effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy-quark-spin symmetries, with the scattering amplitudes unitarized via a Bethe-Salpeter equation to dynamically generate resonant states such as the D0(2400), Sigma_c(2800), and Lambda_c(2595). For finite baryochemical potential, the authors assume adiabatic trajectories with constant entropy per baryon consistent with FAIR collision energies.

## Key numbers
- Drag force F(p=100 MeV) for a D meson at mu_B=0 is shown as a function of temperature; pions give the dominant contribution, with kaons, antikaons, and eta contributing appreciably only at T>100 MeV, and baryon (N+Delta) contribution negligible at mu_B=0.
- The position-space diffusion coefficient D_x normalized by the thermal wavelength 2pi T is computed at mu_B=0 and compared to QGP results from Rapp and Hees (2009) and lattice-QCD from Banerjee, Datta, Gavai, and Majumdar (2011).
- Three adiabatic trajectories are considered for FAIR collisions corresponding to fixed entropy per baryon of 10-30, with initial collision energies sqrt(s) approximately 5-40 A GeV.
- Relaxation times tau_R for D mesons are computed for each adiabatic trajectory and for mu_B=0; all relaxation times are greater than the fireball lifetime t_f ~ 10 fm, confirming D mesons cannot totally relax through collisions.
- The diffusion coefficient 2pi T D_x shows a possible minimum at both the crossover temperature (at mu_B=0) and at the first-order transition temperature (at FAIR collisions).

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of D-meson transport coefficients by Abreu et al. (2011) and Tolos et al. (2013) to include finite baryochemical potential relevant for FAIR physics, and complements similar studies for B mesons by Abreu et al. (2012). The hadronic-phase results are connected to QGP-phase calculations from Rapp and Hees (2009) and lattice-QCD from Banerjee, Datta, Gavai, and Majumdar (2011), as well as perturbative QGP results at finite quark chemical potential from Moore (2004). The work enables improved modeling of heavy-meson energy loss and flow harmonics (v_n) and nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR energies, where net baryon density is significant.
