# J/psi elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The J/psi elliptic flow in high energy nuclear collisions is calculated in a transport model. While the flow is very small at SPS and RHIC energies, it is strongly enhanced at LHC energy due to the dominance of the regeneration mechanism.

## Key claim

The J/psi elliptic flow at LHC energy is predicted to be strongly enhanced (up to about 10%) compared to SPS and RHIC energies, but this enhancement is conditional on the dominance of the regeneration mechanism from thermalized charm quarks and only applies for transverse momenta below about 4 GeV.

## Method

The authors solved a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium distribution functions in transverse phase space at mid-rapidity, coupled with 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the partonic medium evolution. The dissociation and regeneration rates were calculated from gluon dissociation and its reverse process, with charm quarks assumed to be kinetically thermalized in the hot medium. The calculation was performed for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy and compared to results at SPS and RHIC energies at impact parameter b = 7.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy, the maximum J/psi elliptic flow reaches about 10% at transverse momentum p_t ~ 4 GeV for semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter b = 7.8 fm
- At RHIC energy, the regeneration contribution to total J/psi production is around 50% in central Au+Au collisions, but the elliptic flow remains very weak for semi-central collisions
- At SPS energy, the J/psi elliptic flow is extremely small due to negligible regeneration
- The J/psi production with p_t < 4 GeV at LHC is dominated by regeneration

## Context

This work extends the charmonium transport model of Zhuang (2009) to calculate elliptic flow, building on earlier discussions of J/psi regeneration and its competition with primordial production in models by Thews (2001), Braun-Munzinger (2004), and Rapp (2003). The calculation is motivated by the large uncertainty in the J/psi elliptic flow observed at RHIC by PHENIX (2007) and the measured single electron elliptic flow indicating charm quark thermalization at RHIC. The results enable experimental tests at LHC to distinguish between production mechanisms and provide a signature of charm quark collectivity in the quark-gluon plasma.
