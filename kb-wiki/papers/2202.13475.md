# The Equation of State of Neutron-Rich Matter at Fourth Order of Chiral Effective Field Theory and the Radius of a Medium-Mass Neutron Star

> We report neutron star predictions based on our most recent equations of state. These are derived from chiral effective field theory, which allows for a systematic development of nuclear forces, order by order. We utilize high-quality two-nucleon interactions and include all three-nucleon forces up to fourth order in the chiral expansion. Our ab initio predictions are restricted to the domain of applicability of chiral effective field theory. However, stellar matter in the interior of neutron stars can be up to several times denser than normal nuclear matter at saturation, and its composition is essentially unknown. Following established practices, we extend our microscopic predictions to higher densities matching piecewise polytropes. The radius of the average-size neutron star, about 1.4 solar masses, is sensitive to the pressure at normal densities, and thus it is suitable to constrain ab initio theories of the equation of state. For this reason, we focus on the radius of medium-mass stars. We compare our results with other theoretical predictions and recent constraints.

## Key claim

The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star predicted from chiral effective field theory at fourth order, including all subleading three-nucleon forces, is 11.96 km with a combined uncertainty of ±0.80 km, but this result depends on piecewise polytropic extrapolations to high densities beyond the domain where chiral effective field theory is reliable (estimated as about twice saturation density).

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state for neutron-rich matter using chiral effective field theory two-nucleon forces at fifth order and all three-nucleon forces up to fourth order (N³LO), with a momentum-space cutoff Λ = 450 MeV. They solved the particle-particle ladder in the hole-line expansion via a non-perturbative G-matrix calculation with self-consistent single-particle potentials, then extended the microscopic predictions to higher densities by matching piecewise polytropes at two matching densities (ρ₁ = 0.277 fm⁻³ and ρ₂ = 0.506 fm⁻³) with adiabatic indices Γ ranging from 1.5 to 4.5, applying causality and maximum mass constraints.

## Key numbers

- Radius of 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: R₁.₄ = (11.96 ± 0.80) km, combining truncation error (±0.54 km) and extrapolation uncertainty (±0.58 km standard deviation from polytrope combinations)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³: e_sym(ρ₀) = 31.3 ± 0.8 MeV
- Slope parameter L at saturation density: L(ρ₀) = 52.6 ± 4.0 MeV
- Proton fraction at normal density at N³LO: 0.046 ± 0.0035, far below the direct Urca threshold of approximately 11%
- Radius values for individual polytrope combinations range from 10.05 km (Γ₁=1.5, Γ₂=4.5) to 12.39 km (Γ₁=4.5, any Γ₂)
- Central densities for 1.4 M⊙ stars range from 0.39 fm⁻³ to 0.82 fm⁻³ across acceptable polytrope combinations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT calculations (Sammarruca and Millerson, 2021) by including all subleading three-nucleon forces at fourth order and applying them to neutron star structure predictions. The result of 11.96 km falls within the LIGO/Virgo constraint range of 11.1 to 13.4 km from the GW170817 event (Abbott et al., 2017, 2018) and is consistent with the range of 10 to 14 km generally found from chiral EFT-based equations of state (Drischler et al., 2021; Hebeler et al., 2010). The findings disagree with the implications of the PREX II experiment for neutron star properties. The work enables more precise comparisons between ab initio nuclear theory and multi-messenger astrophysical observations, particularly for constraining the equation of state at normal densities through the canonical-mass neutron star radius.
