# Infrared Exponents and Running Coupling of SU(N) Yang-Mills Theories

> We present approximate solutions for the gluon and ghost propagators as well as the running coupling in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theories. We solve the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger equations in flat Euclidean space-time without any angular approximation. This supplements recently obtained results employing a four-torus, i.e. a compact space-time manifold, as infrared regulator. We confirm previous findings deduced from an extrapolation with tori of different volumes: the gluon propagator is weakly vanishing in the infrared and the ghost propagator is highly singular. For non-vanishing momenta our propagators are in remarkable agreement with recent lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The authors present approximate numerical solutions for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theories, solving the Dyson-Schwinger equations in flat Euclidean space-time without angular approximations. They confirm that the gluon propagator is weakly vanishing in the infrared and the ghost propagator is highly singular, but note that the intermediate momentum regime is where the omission of two-loop diagrams causes deficiencies in their solutions.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the ghost and gluon dressing functions in Landau gauge, using bare vertices and omitting all diagrams involving four-gluon vertices. They perform the angular integrals numerically without approximation, splitting the radial loop integral into three momentum regions and expanding the dressing functions in Chebychev polynomials for the angular integrations. A MOM regularization scheme is applied, with the ghost equation subtracted at zero momentum and the gluon equation at a perturbative scale, and the momentum scale is fixed by requiring the experimental value of the running coupling at the Z-boson mass.

## Key numbers

- Infrared exponent for the gluon propagator: \(\kappa \approx 0.595\), giving gluon propagator behavior \(D_{gl}(k^2) \sim (k^2)^{2\kappa -1}\) and ghost propagator behavior \(D_{gh}(k^2) \sim (k^2)^{-\kappa -1}\)
- Infrared fixed point of the running coupling for SU(3): \(\alpha(0) \approx 2.972\)
- Subleading infrared power solutions: \(\rho_{(2)} = 0.58377\) and \(\rho_{(3)} = 1.20300\)
- Infrared matching point: \(\epsilon^2 = (0.55\ \text{MeV})^2\)
- Numerical cutoff: \(x_{UV} = (177\ \text{GeV})^2\), with perturbative scale \(s = (174\ \text{GeV})^2\)
- Running coupling fixed to \(\alpha(x) = 0.118\) at \(x = M_Z^2 = (91.187\ \text{GeV})^2\)
- For projector \(\zeta = 5\), the infrared exponent changes to \(\kappa = 0.4610\); for \(\zeta = 3.9\), \(\kappa = 0.5038\)

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger studies by von Smekal (1997), Atkinson (1998), and Zwanziger (2001) that employed angular approximations or focused only on the infrared limit, and supplements the authors' own prior results on a four-torus (Fischer, 2002). The solutions agree remarkably well with lattice calculations by Langfeld (2002) for two colours, and the combined evidence points toward an infrared vanishing gluon propagator and an infrared singular ghost propagator in Landau gauge, consistent with the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion. The numerical fits provided can serve as input for phenomenological calculations in hadron physics.
