# Elastic and radiative heavy quark interactions in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Elastic and radiative heavy quark interactions with light partons are studied with the partonic transport description BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings). After calculating the cross section of radiative processes for finite masses in the improved Gunion-Bertsch approximation and verifying this calculation by comparing to the exact result, we study elastic and radiative heavy quark energy loss in a static medium of quarks and gluons. Furthermore, the full 3+1D space-time evolution of gluons, light quarks, and heavy quarks in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are calculated with BAMPS including elastic and radiative heavy flavor interactions. Treating light and heavy particles on the same footing in the same framework, we find that the experimentally measured nuclear modification factor of charged hadrons and D mesons at the LHC can be simultaneously described. In addition, we calculate the heavy flavor evolution with an improved screening procedure from hard-thermal-loop calculations and confront the results with experimental data of the nuclear modification factor and the elliptic flow of heavy flavor particles at RHIC and LHC.

# Elastic and radiative heavy quark interactions in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a unified pQCD-based transport description (BAMPS) treating light and heavy partons on the same footing can simultaneously describe the experimentally measured nuclear modification factor of charged hadrons and D mesons at the LHC, but only when both elastic and radiative heavy quark interactions are included and the LPM effect is implemented via an effective cut-off parameter.

## Method

The authors implement radiative (bremsstrahlung) processes for heavy quarks in the BAMPS parton cascade by generalizing the improved Gunion-Bertsch approximation to finite quark masses, factorizing the 2-to-3 matrix element into an elastic 2-to-2 part times a gluon emission factor. They compare this approximation to exact matrix element calculations for the process qQ -> qQg and verify good agreement. The LPM effect is implemented via an effective constraint that only allows gluon emission if the mean free path exceeds a parameter X_LPM times the formation time, with the formation time generalized to include the heavy quark mass. The screening mass is determined by matching hard-thermal-loop results for heavy quark energy loss in a static medium.

## Key numbers

- The screening mass is set to mu^2 = kappa_t * m_D^2 with kappa_t = 1/(2e) ≈ 0.2, matched to hard thermal loop calculations
- For the comparison of Gunion-Bertsch to exact cross sections, calculations are performed at T = 400 MeV with alpha_s = 0.3 and s - M^2 = 18 T^2 ≈ 2.88 GeV^2
- The Debye mass for n_f = 3 at T = 400 MeV and alpha_s = 0.3 is m_D^2 = 8 alpha_s (3+n_f) T^2 / pi ≈ 0.73 GeV^2
- The dead cone suppression factor from Dokshitzer and Kharzeev is D_DK = 1/(1 + theta_D^2/theta^2)^2 with theta_D = M/E, which the authors show is a limiting case of their more general expression

## Context

This work extends previous BAMPS heavy flavor calculations (Uphoff 2011, 2012, 2013) that included only elastic heavy quark interactions, by adding radiative processes. It builds on the improved Gunion-Bertsch approximation for light partons (Fochler 2013) and generalizes it to finite masses. The approach contrasts with other pQCD-based models that treat elastic and radiative processes separately (Moore 2004, Zhang 2005, Molnar 2006 for elastic; Abir 2012 for radiative) or combine them (Mustafa 2004, Armesto 2005, Djordjevic 2005, Cao 2011). The results enable simultaneous description of light and heavy flavor observables within a single transport framework, and the improved screening procedure from hard-thermal-loop calculations provides a path to confront both R_AA and v_2 of heavy flavor particles at RHIC and LHC with experimental data.
