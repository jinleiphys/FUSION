# Relativistic BGK hydrodynamics

> Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision kernel is employed in the Boltzmann equation to formulate relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics. In this formulation, we find that there remains freedom of choosing a matching condition that affects the scalar transport in the system. We also propose a new collision kernel which, unlike BGK collision kernel, is valid in the limit of zero chemical potential and derive relativistic first-order dissipative hydrodynamics using it. We study the effects of this new formulation on the coefficient of bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The authors find that the BGK collision kernel leaves a free parameter in the matching condition, which affects only the scalar transport (bulk viscosity) and allows it to be specified independently of shear viscosity, unlike the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) where both are fixed by a single relaxation time.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic Boltzmann equation with the BGK collision kernel and propose a modified BGK (MBGK) kernel that remains well-defined at zero chemical potential. They solve the MBGK Boltzmann equation to first order in gradients to obtain the non-equilibrium correction to the distribution function, then derive the first-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations and expressions for transport coefficients, including a free parameter \(b_0\) that arises from the undetermined second matching condition.

## Key numbers

* The coefficient of bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) depends on the free parameter \(b_0\) as \(\zeta = -\tau_{\rm R}\big[ \frac{\chi_b}{\beta} (\epsilon_0 + P_0) - \frac{5 \beta I_{32}^+}{3} -\frac{\chi_a n_0}{\beta} + \frac{(\chi_a+b_0)}{\beta} ( \beta P_0 - \chi_b \epsilon_0 + \chi_a n_0 ) \big]\).
* The shear viscosity \(\eta = \tau_{\rm R}\, \beta\, I_{32}^+\) and the conductivity \(\kappa = \tau_{\rm R} \big[I_{11}^+ - \frac{n_0^2}{\beta(\epsilon_0+P_0)} \big]\) are independent of \(b_0\) and match the RTA results.
* The squared speed of sound at zero chemical potential is \(c_s^2 = \frac{(\epsilon_0 + P_0)}{3 \epsilon_0 + (3 + z^2) P_0}\), where \(z \equiv m/T\).
* The RTA matching conditions are recovered for the particular choice \(b_0 = -\chi_a\), which gives \(n = n_0\) and \(\epsilon = \epsilon_0\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic BGK collision kernel approach to dissipative hydrodynamics, which has been less explored than the RTA framework (Denicol 2012, Jaiswal 2014, Florkowski 2013). The BGK kernel conserves net particle four-current by construction but is ill-defined at zero chemical potential, a regime relevant for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The proposed MBGK kernel enables studies of bulk viscosity independently of shear viscosity, and the freedom in the matching condition allows access to a class of hydrodynamic theories, with RTA as a special case.
