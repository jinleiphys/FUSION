# First determination of the one-proton induced Non-Mesonic Weak Decay width of p-shell Λ-Hypernuclei

> Previous studies of proton and neutron spectra from Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of eight Lambda-Hypernuclei (A = 5-16) have been revisited. New values of the ratio of the two-nucleon and the one-proton induced decay widths, Gamma_2N/Gamma_p, are obtained from single proton spectra, Gamma_2N/Gamma_p = 0.50 +/- 0.24, and from neutron and proton coincidence spectra, Gamma_2N/Gamma_p = 0.36 +/- 0.14stat +0.05sys -0.04sys , in full agreement with previously published ones. With these values, a method is developed to extract the one-proton induced decay width in units of the free Lambda decay width, Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda, without resorting to Intra Nuclear Cascade models but by exploiting only experimental data, under the assumption of a linear dependence on A of the Final State Interaction contribution. This is the first systematic determination ever done and it agrees within the errors with recent theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic determination of the one-proton induced non-mesonic weak decay width in units of the free Lambda decay width, Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda, for eight p-shell hypernuclei (A=5-16), obtained without using Intra Nuclear Cascade models but by exploiting only experimental data under the assumption of a linear dependence on A of the Final State Interaction contribution.

## Method

The FINUDA Collaboration revisited proton and neutron spectra from the Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of eight Lambda-Hypernuclei (A=5-16) produced at the DAΦNE collider. They fitted the proton kinetic energy spectra above 70 MeV to Gaussians, extracted the ratio of two-nucleon to one-proton induced decay widths (Gamma_2N/Gamma_p) from both single proton spectra and neutron-proton coincidence spectra, and then developed a method to determine Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda by parametrizing Final State Interaction effects through a coefficient alpha that scales linearly with mass number A, calibrated using indirect determinations for 5-Lambda-He and 12-Lambda-C.

## Key numbers

- Gamma_2N/Gamma_p = 0.50 +/- 0.24 (stat) +/- 0.04 (sys) from single proton spectra
- Gamma_2N/Gamma_p = 0.36 +0.14 (stat) +0.05 (sys) -0.04 (sys) from neutron and proton coincidence spectra
- Gamma_2N/Gamma_NMWD = 0.25 +/- 0.12 (stat) +/- 0.02 (sys) from single proton spectra
- Gamma_2N/Gamma_NMWD = 0.20 +0.08 (stat) +0.04 (sys) -0.03 (sys) from coincidence spectra
- Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda for 5-Lambda-He = 0.22 +/- 0.05 (this work), compared to 0.21 +/- 0.07 from Szymanski et al.
- Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda for 12-Lambda-C = 0.65 +/- 0.19 (this work), compared to 0.31 +/- 0.07 from Noumi et al. and 0.45 +/- 0.10 from Bhang et al.
- Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda for 16-Lambda-O = 0.44 +/- 0.12 (this work)
- The FSI coefficient alpha_A = (0.215 +/- 0.031) * A, indicating 35% of primary protons lost to FSI in 5-Lambda-He and 63% in 16-Lambda-O

## Context

This work extends the FINUDA Collaboration's previous analyses of proton spectra from NMWD (published in 2009 and 2010) and the neutron-proton coincidence analysis (published in 2012), providing refined values of Gamma_2N/Gamma_p that agree with earlier results and with the KEK SKS Collaboration measurement (Kim et al., Gamma_2N/Gamma_NMWD = 0.29 +/- 0.13). The new Gamma_p/Gamma_Lambda determinations agree within errors with recent theoretical calculations by Motoba et al. and enable systematic comparison of one-proton induced decay widths across the p-shell hypernuclei mass range without relying on Intra Nuclear Cascade models.
