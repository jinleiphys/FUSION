# On "the authentic damping mechanism" of the phonon damping model

## Key claim
The phonon damping model (PDM) fits to giant dipole resonance (GDR) photoabsorption cross sections have no physical content because the model's Breit-Wigner line shape is an *ad hoc* input forced by the assumption of equal coupling strength to all 1p1h states, not a result of microscopic dynamics; the fitted strength parameter $f_1$ decreases with increasing configuration space size $N$ as $f_1 \sim 1/\sqrt{N}$, contradicting the physical expectation that collectivity should increase with basis size.

## Method
The author performed PDM calculations with random $E_{\alpha}$ spectra (0 to 50 MeV) to reproduce a Lorentzian GDR line with centroid $E_0 = 15.0$ MeV and width $\Gamma = 4.3$ MeV, fitting the PDM parameters $f_1$ and $\omega$ with a smearing parameter $\varepsilon = 0.5$ MeV (as in the original work by Dang et al.). The convergence of the PDM result to the Lorentzian shape was tested for increasing numbers $N$ of 1p1h states and repeated with multiple random spectra. The physical meaning of the fitted $f_1$ parameter was then analyzed using the exact second-moment relation $W_2 = (f_1)^2 \cdot N$.

## Key numbers
- The PDM calculations with random $E_{\alpha}$ spectra aimed to reproduce a Lorentzian GDR with $E_0 = 15.0$ MeV and $\Gamma = 4.3$ MeV.
- The smearing parameter used was $\varepsilon = 0.5$ MeV, matching the value used by Dang et al. for the oxygen chain.
- For $^{48}$Ca, the parameter $f_1$ was renormalized by 34% relative to $^{40}$Ca (fitted to four digits).
- From $^{18}$O to $^{16}$O, $f_1$ was reduced by approximately 25%.
- For the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in $^{40}$Ca, the PDM predicts 0.3% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), while the experimental value (excluding the $[2^+_1 \times 3^-_1]_{1^-}$ two-phonon candidate at 6.950 MeV) is about 0.007%, a discrepancy factor of 40.

## Context
This Comment critiques the phonon damping model as applied by Dang et al. (2001) to GDR and PDR in oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. It extends earlier textbook treatments of phonon fragmentation (Bohr and Mottelson, 1969) and prior microscopic discussions of the PDR as arising from lowest 1p1h $1^-$ configurations (Enders et al., 2000; Hartmann et al., 2000; Catara et al., 1997; Oros et al., 1998; Vretenar et al., 2001; Colò et al., 2001). The work enables a critical re-evaluation of whether phenomenological fits with adjustable parameters can claim to describe the damping mechanism of giant resonances, and it calls for independent data tests (such as PDR fine structure) rather than selective comparison.
