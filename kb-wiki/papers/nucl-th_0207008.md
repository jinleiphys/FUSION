# Constraints on proton-proton fusion from helioseismology

> The proton-proton ($pp$) fusion cross-section found at the heart of solar models is unconstrained experimentally and relies solely on theoretical calculations. Effective field theory provides an opportunity to constrain the $pp$ cross-section experimentally, however, this method is complicated by the appearance of two-nucleon effects in the form of an unknown parameter $L_{1,A}$. We present a method to constrain $L_{1,A}$ using the Standard Solar Model and helioseismology. Using this method, we determine a value of $L_{1,A}$ = 7.0 fm$^{3}$ with a range of 1.6 to 12.4 fm$^{3}$. These results are consistent with theoretical estimates of $L_{1,A} \approx$ 6 fm$^{3}$.

## Key claim

The unknown two-nucleon parameter \(L_{1,A}\) in the effective field theory description of proton-proton fusion can be constrained using helioseismology and the Standard Solar Model, yielding a value consistent with theoretical estimates; the method assumes that the uncertainty in the model-computed p-mode frequencies is comparable to or less than the observational error bars, an assumption that requires further formal uncertainty analysis.

## Method

The authors used the Yale stellar evolution code (YREC) to construct Standard Solar Models, varying the \(S_{pp}(0)\) cross-section according to the EFT relation between \(L_{1,A}\) and \(\Lambda\). For each variation, they computed low-\(l\) p-mode oscillation frequencies using a nonradial, nonadiabatic stellar oscillation program, then compared the small frequency spacings \(\delta\nu(n,l)\) to observed BiSON data. The allowed range of \(L_{1,A}\) was determined by finding where the average \(\delta\nu(n,l)\) difference between model and observations fell within the \(\pm 0.066\ \mu\)Hz average observed error in the 2500–3000 \(\mu\)Hz frequency range.

## Key numbers

- \(L_{1,A} = 7.0\ \text{fm}^3\) with a range of 1.6 to 12.4 fm\(^3\), determined from helioseismology.
- The theoretical estimate from dimensional analysis gives \(L_{1,A} \approx 6\ \text{fm}^3\).
- The reference Standard Solar Model yields a theoretical total \(^8\)B neutrino flux of \(4.93 \times 10^6\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).
- The observed total \(^8\)B neutrino flux from SNO is \((5.09^{+0.44}_{-0.43}(\text{stat})^{+0.46}_{-0.43}(\text{syst})) \times 10^6\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).
- The average observed error in the small spacing frequencies within the 2500–3000 \(\mu\)Hz range is \(\pm 0.066\ \mu\)Hz.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to constrain the \(pp\) cross-section using simplified solar models (Antia, year not given) by employing a fully-developed stellar evolution code and helioseismic data. The result for \(L_{1,A}\) is consistent with theoretical dimensional analysis (Butler and Chen, year not given) and with other evaluations of \(5.6 \pm 2.0\ \text{fm}^3\) (authors not named) and \(6.5 \pm 2.4\ \text{fm}^3\) (Schiavilla et al., 1998). The constrained \(L_{1,A}\) can be used to predict cross-sections for neutrino-deuteron breakup reactions relevant to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory.
