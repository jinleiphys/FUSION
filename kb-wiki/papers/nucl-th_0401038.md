# Random interactions in nuclei and extension of $0^+$ dominance in ground states to irreps of group symmetries

> Random one plus two-body hamiltonians invariant with respect to $O({\cal N}_1) \oplus O({\cal N}_2)$ symmetry in the group-subgroup chains $U({\cal N}) \supset U({\cal N}_1) \oplus U({\cal N}_2) \supset O({\cal N}_1) \oplus O({\cal N}_2)$ and $U({\cal N}) \supset O({\cal N}) \supset O({\cal N}_1) \oplus O({\cal N}_2)$ chains of a variety of interacting boson models are used to investigate the probability of occurrence of a given $(ω_1 ω_2)$ irreducible representation (irrep) to be the ground state in even-even nuclei; $[ω_1]$ and $[ω_2]$ are symmetric irreps of $O({\cal N}_1)$ and $O({\cal N}_2)$ respectively. Numerical results obtained for ${\cal N}_1 \geq 3, {\cal N}_2=1$ and ${\cal N}_1, {\cal N}_2 \geq 3$ situations are well explained by an extended Hartree-Bose mean-field analysis.

## Key claim

Random one-plus-two-body Hamiltonians with \(O(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus O(\mathcal{N}_2)\) symmetry produce ground-state irreducible representations (irreps) with probabilities that are well explained by an extended Hartree-Bose mean-field analysis, but the results depend on whether \(\mathcal{N}_2 = 1\) or \(\mathcal{N}_2 \geq 3\) and on whether the boson number \(N\) is even or odd.

## Method

The author constructed random one-plus-two-body Hamiltonians that are scalars under \(O(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus O(\mathcal{N}_2)\) symmetry, using Casimir operators of the group chains \(U(\mathcal{N}) \supset U(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus U(\mathcal{N}_2) \supset O(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus O(\mathcal{N}_2)\) and \(U(\mathcal{N}) \supset O(\mathcal{N}) \supset O(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus O(\mathcal{N}_2)\). For boson numbers \(N = 10\) to \(25\), a 500-member random matrix ensemble was diagonalized in the basis of symmetric \(U(\mathcal{N})\) irreps \(\{N\}\) to compute the probability that each \((\omega_1 \omega_2)\) irrep is the ground state. An extended Hartree-Bose mean-field analysis using coherent states and cranking was then applied to explain the numerical probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For \(\mathcal{N}_1 \geq 3, \mathcal{N}_2 = 1\) (case I) with even \(N\): \(\omega_1 = 0\) occurs as ground state with \(\sim 65\)-\(74\%\) probability and \(\omega_1 = N\) with \(\sim 25\)-\(32\%\) probability.
- For case I with odd \(N\): \(\omega_1 = 0\) occurs with \(\sim 46\)-\(50\%\), \(\omega_1 = 1\) with \(\sim 18\)-\(24\%\), and \(\omega_1 = N\) with \(\sim 24\)-\(30\%\) probability.
- For \(\mathcal{N}_1, \mathcal{N}_2 \geq 3\) (case II) with even \(N\): \((\omega_1 \omega_2) = (00)\) occurs as ground state with \(\sim 50\)-\(55\%\), and \((N0)\) and \((0N)\) each with \(\sim 20\)-\(24\%\) probability.
- For the \(U(7)\) model with \((\mathcal{N}_1, \mathcal{N}_2) = (4,3)\) and \(N=10\): \((00)\) probability = \(55.4\%\), \((N0) = 21.4\%\), \((0N) = 20.5\%\).
- For the \(sdg\)IBM with \((\mathcal{N}_1, \mathcal{N}_2) = (10,5)\) and \(N=10\): \((00)\) probability = \(55.3\%\), \((N0) = 22.9\%\), \((0N) = 19.2\%\).
- For IBM-4 with \((\mathcal{N}_1, \mathcal{N}_2) = (3,3)\) and \(N=10\): \((00)\) probability = \(49.2\%\), \((N0) = 22.8\%\), \((0N) = 22.8\%\).

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Bertsch et al. (1998) that random interactions produce \(0^+\) ground states in even-even nuclei with high probability, and the interacting-boson-model studies by Bijker and Frank (2000) showing that random interactions generate vibrational and rotational structures. It generalizes these results to \(O(\mathcal{N}_1) \oplus O(\mathcal{N}_2)\) group symmetries relevant to many interacting boson models (e.g., \(sp\)IBM, \(sd\)IBM, \(sdg\)IBM, IBM-3, IBM-4). The findings enable future studies of geometric chaos and regularities from random interactions in other group-subgroup chains for both boson and fermion systems, and suggest that in models like IBM-4 a regular part must be added to enhance the physical \((ST)=(00)\) ground state.
