# Octupole correlations in $^{220,222,224,226}$Rn

> The octupole correlations in $^{220,222,224,226}$Rn are investigated by using multi-dimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory. The ground-state properties and potential energy surfaces are analyzed, revealing that octupole deformation appears in $^{222,224}$Rn, but not in $^{220,226}$Rn. The relationship between pairing correlations and octupole deformation is examined, showing that the neutron pairing energy decreases as octupole deformation develops, whereas the proton pairing energy shows the opposite behavior. The microscopic origin of octupole correlations in these radon isotopes are explored based on an examination of the single-particle levels near the Fermi surface and a schematic two-level model. Experiments have indicated that these isotopes undergo octupole vibrations and the present prediction of octupole deformation in $^{222,224}$Rn awaits further confirmation.

## Key claim

The authors predict that static octupole deformation exists in the ground states of \(^{222}\)Rn and \(^{224}\)Rn but not in \(^{220}\)Rn or \(^{226}\)Rn, based on multi-dimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory calculations. This prediction awaits experimental confirmation, as experiments to date have only observed octupole vibrational bands in these isotopes.

## Method

The authors used multi-dimensionally constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) theory with nonlinear point-coupling functionals (PC-PK1, DD-PC1, and DD-ME2) and a separable pairing force of finite range with two parameter sets (S-D1 and S-D1S). They calculated potential energy surfaces in the \(\beta_{20}-\beta_{30}\) plane for \(^{220,222,224,226}\)Rn under the assumption of axial symmetry, with basis truncation at \(N_F=20\) fermionic shells and \(N_B=26\) bosonic shells. The BCS method was used to treat pairing correlations, and the microscopic origin of octupole deformation was analyzed using single-particle level diagrams and a schematic two-level model.

## Key numbers

- \(^{222}\)Rn: octupole deformation \(\beta_{30} \sim 0.1\) across all parameter sets; energy gain from octupole deformation \(\Delta E_{\text{oct}} = 0.08\) to \(0.78\) MeV depending on the functional and pairing parameters
- \(^{224}\)Rn: octupole deformation \(\beta_{30} \sim 0.1\)–\(0.14\) across all parameter sets; energy gain \(\Delta E_{\text{oct}} = 0.12\) to \(0.79\) MeV depending on the functional and pairing parameters
- \(^{220}\)Rn and \(^{226}\)Rn: \(\beta_{30} = 0.000\) (no octupole deformation) for all parameter sets
- Binding energies for all four isotopes calculated with all parameter sets are within approximately 1–2 MeV of experimental values (e.g., \(^{220}\)Rn: calculated 1696.52–1698.04 MeV vs. experimental 1697.80 MeV; \(^{226}\)Rn: calculated 1726.06–1727.05 MeV vs. experimental 1728.09 MeV)
- Quadrupole deformations \(\beta_{20}\) range from 0.119 to 0.125 for \(^{220}\)Rn, 0.154 to 0.162 for \(^{222}\)Rn, 0.183 to 0.204 for \(^{224}\)Rn, and 0.194 to 0.200 for \(^{226}\)Rn

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on octupole correlations in radon isotopes, which have shown conflicting predictions: macro-microscopic models with Woods-Saxon potential predicted octupole deformation in \(^{222,224}\)Rn (Nazarewicz, 1984), finite-range liquid-drop model calculations predicted all four isotopes exhibit octupole deformation (Moller, 2016), and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with Gogny interaction predicted octupole deformation in \(^{220}\)Rn at the mean-field level (Robledo, 2012; Robledo, 2013). The present MDC-RMF results contradict the latter two predictions and agree with the macro-microscopic model. The work enables future beyond-mean-field studies using the generator coordinate method and calls for experimental confirmation of static octupole deformation in \(^{222,224}\)Rn, where only octupole vibrational bands have been observed to date (Nature, 2013; Nature Communications, 2019).
