# Two-Step Contribution to Intermediate Energy (p,p') and (p,n) Reactions

> We calculate the two-step contribution to (p,p') and (p,n) reactions at intermediate energy. We describe the motion of the incident nucleon with plane wave and compare the contribution from the two-step processes with that from the one-step processes. To describe the two-step processes, we extende the response functions into the nondiagonal ones with respect to the momentum transfer q. We performed a numerical calculation for the cross sections of the $^{12}$C, $^{40}$Ca(p,p') scatterings and the spin longitudinal and the spin transverse cross sections of the $^{12}$C,$^{40}$Ca(p,n) reactions at 346 MeV and 494 MeV. We found that the two-step contribution is appreciable in comparison with the one-step processes in higher energy transfer region for the spin longitudinal and the spin transverse (p,n) reactions. We also found that the two-step processes give larger contribution to the spin transverse (p,n) reaction than to the spin longitudinal reaction. This finding is very encouraging to interpret the discrepancy between the DWIA calculation and the experimental results of the spin longitudinal and the spin transverse cross sections.

## Key claim

The two-step contribution to (p,p') and (p,n) reactions at intermediate energy is appreciable compared to one-step processes in the higher-energy transfer region, and the two-step processes give larger contributions to the spin transverse (p,n) reaction than to the spin longitudinal reaction, which may help interpret the discrepancy between DWIA calculations and experimental results for the spin longitudinal and spin transverse cross sections.

## Method

The authors developed a formalism for two-step reactions within the plane-wave approximation, extending the response functions to nondiagonal forms with respect to momentum transfer q. They applied the Tamura-Udagawa-Lenske (TUL) statistical approximation and the spectator assumption to calculate the two-step T-matrix, treating the spin degree of freedom carefully by accounting for the difference between the direction of total momentum transfer and that in each step. Numerical calculations were performed for the cross sections of 12C and 40Ca(p,p') scattering and the spin longitudinal and spin transverse cross sections of 12C and 40Ca(p,n) reactions at 346 MeV and 494 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies used: 346 MeV and 494 MeV for both (p,p') and (p,n) reactions on 12C and 40Ca targets.
- The mean field U0 was set to -3.5 MeV for K_lab = 346 MeV and 5.5 MeV for K_lab = 494 MeV, based on values from Mottelson and Jones respectively.
- The two-step contribution was found to be appreciable compared to one-step processes in the higher-energy transfer region for both spin longitudinal and spin transverse (p,n) reactions.

## Context

This work extends the multi-step direct reaction framework developed by Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (FKK) and by Tamura, Udagawa, and Lenske (TUL), who studied multi-step processes but rarely investigated spin dependence. The authors address the known discrepancy between DWIA calculations and experimental results for spin longitudinal and spin transverse cross sections in (p,n) reactions, where the ratio R_L/R_T is experimentally found to be less than 1, contrary to RPA predictions. The calculation enables a more complete understanding of the spin dependence in multi-step processes and provides a path toward resolving the R_L/R_T problem.
