# Chiral perturbation theory for the Wilson lattice action

> We extend chiral perturbation theory to include linear dependence on the lattice spacing $a$ for the Wilson action. The perturbation theory is written as a double expansion in the small quark mass $m_q$ and lattice spacing $a$. We present formulae for the mass and decay constant of a flavor-non-singlet meson in this scheme to order $a$ and $m_q^2$. The extension to the partially quenched theory is also described.

# Chiral perturbation theory for the Wilson lattice action

## Key claim

The paper extends continuum chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to include linear dependence on the lattice spacing $a$ for the Wilson action, constructing a double expansion in the small quark mass $m_q$ and $a$. The authors note that this expansion is valid only when the hierarchy $\epsilon \gg \delta$ holds (where $\epsilon \sim p^2/\Lambda_\chi^2 \sim \chi/\Lambda_\chi^2$ and $\delta \sim \rho/\Lambda_\chi^2$), meaning the quark mass effects must dominate over the lattice spacing effects for the perturbative ordering they adopt.

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian for the Wilson lattice action by treating the Pauli term $a C_{SW} \bar\psi \sigma_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu}\psi$ (the only new dimension-5 operator at $\mathcal{O}(a)$) as a symmetry-breaking spurion on equal footing with the quark mass matrix. They write the leading-order (LO) Lagrangian at $\mathcal{O}(\epsilon,\delta)$ and the next-to-leading order (NLO) Lagrangian at $\mathcal{O}(\epsilon^2,\epsilon\delta)$, then compute the mass and decay constant of a flavor-non-singlet meson through NLO using standard chiral perturbation theory techniques with loop diagrams and tree-level counterterms. The extension to partially quenched theories is described by promoting traces to super-traces with an extended flavor structure including valence quarks, sea quarks, and ghosts.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is taken as $\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}$, the scale of the rho meson mass where the EFT breaks down.
- The LO meson mass squared is given by $M_{AB}^2 = \mu_{AB}$ where $\mu = \chi + \rho$, with $\chi = 2B_0 m_q$ and $\rho = 2W_0 a C_{SW}$.
- The NLO loop contribution to the mass contains a factor $1/(48 f^2 \pi^2)$ multiplying $\mu_{AB} \sum_x R_x^{AB} \mu_x \log \mu_x$.
- The NLO tree-level mass contribution involves the low-energy constants $L_4, L_5, L_6, L_8$ and their Wilson counterparts $W_4, W_5, W_6, W_8$, with coefficients such as $24/f^2$, $8/f^2$, and $48/f^2$.
- The LO decay constant is $f_{AB} = f$.
- The NLO loop contribution to the decay constant contains terms with $1/(64\pi^2 f)$ and $1/(192\pi^2 f)$.
- At $\mathcal{O}(a^2)$, the only correction to meson masses is an additional term of the form $\omega a^2$ with $\omega$ an unknown constant of mass-dimension 4; the decay constant receives no corrections at this order.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Sharpe and Singleton (1998), who first used a similar method to investigate the phase diagram for Wilson fermions in two-flavor QCD. The paper provides the explicit NLO formulae for meson masses and decay constants needed to extract the Gasser-Leutwyler coefficients of ordinary continuum ChPT from lattice simulations at finite lattice spacing, and also generalizes the formalism to partially quenched theories following the work of Sharpe and Shoresh (2000, 2001). The results enable lattice practitioners to perform controlled continuum extrapolations of Wilson fermion data using the full $\mathcal{O}(a)$ chiral behavior, including non-trivial logarithms that entangle $a$ and $m_q$, rather than relying on naive linear extrapolations.
