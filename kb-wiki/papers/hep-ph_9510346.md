# The Pion Structure Function in a Constituent Model

> Using the recent relatively precise experimental results on the pion structure function, obtained from Drell--Yan processes, we quantitatively test an old model where the structure function of any hadron is determined by that of its constituent quarks. In this model the pion structure function can be predicted from the known nucleon structure function. We find that the data support the model, at least as a good first approximation.

## Key claim

The constituent-with-structure model, where the pion structure function is predicted from the known nucleon structure function via a convolution with constituent quark distributions, provides a reasonably accurate but not perfect description of the pion structure function data from Drell-Yan processes, with the model requiring a somewhat larger K' factor (1.3-1.6) than the model-independent fit (1.1-1.3).

## Method

The authors took the proton parton densities from the MRS(G) fit at Q₀² = 4 GeV² and deconvoluted them using the SU(6)W ⊗ O(3) constituent wave function from Altarelli et al. (1974) to extract the parton densities in a U constituent quark. They then convoluted these constituent parton densities with a pion constituent distribution function (parameterized with β̃ = 0.1) to predict the pion structure function, and compared the predictions to Drell-Yan data as compiled by Sutton et al. (1995), using the K factor formalism to account for QCD corrections.

## Key numbers

- Second moment of valence in the proton at Q₀² = 4 GeV²: V_p^(2) = 0.39 ± 0.02
- Second moment of valence in the pion at Q₀² = 4 GeV² from model: V_π^(2) = 0.39 ± 0.02 (same as proton)
- Second moment of valence in the pion at Q₀² = 4 GeV² from model-independent fit: V_π^(2) = 0.46 ± 0.04
- Total momentum carried by sea and gluons in the proton at Q₀² = 4 GeV²: 0.61 ± 0.02
- Total momentum carried by sea and gluons in the pion at Q₀² = 4 GeV²: 0.54 ± 0.04
- K' factor range for the model: 1.3-1.6
- K' factor range for model-independent fit: 1.1-1.3
- Lattice QCD (quenched) result for second moment of valence in pion at 49 GeV²: V_π^(2) = 0.46 ± 0.07, corresponding to V_π^(2)(4 GeV²) = 0.55 ± 0.08

## Context

This work extends the constituent-with-structure model originally proposed by Altarelli et al. (1974) for nucleons, applying it to the pion using improved Drell-Yan data from Sutton et al. (1995). The model predicts that the total momentum carried by gluons, sea, and valence should be the same in the nucleon and pion at the same Q², which is supported by the data within uncertainties. The results enable predictions for structure functions of other hadrons where no data exist, and the authors note that quenched lattice QCD calculations by Sachrajda et al. give larger second moments than both the model and the experimental fit, suggesting the quenched approximation is poor for hadronic structure functions.
