# Dual oscillators and Quantum Pendulums: spectrum and correlators

> We outline the nonlinear transformation in the path integral representation for partition function of O(N) symmetric oscillator systems bringing their duality to certain one-dimensional oscillators with unstable potential shapes. This duality transformation realizes the equivalence between spectra of a Hermitian and a non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. It is exploited to calculate exactly the spectra of quantum rotators . The zero-temperature limit is considered and the dual representation of n-point correlators of coordinate operators is obtained.

# Dual oscillators and Quantum Pendulums: spectrum and correlators

## Key claim
The paper establishes a duality transformation (the "equivalent oscillators" or EO-transformation) that maps the partition function of an O(N)-symmetric oscillator system with a real, bounded-below potential onto a one-dimensional quantum system with a complex, unstable potential, provided the complex variable u(τ) satisfies Re u(τ) > 0 and the relation −\dot u + u^2 = ω^2 + iσ(τ) holds; the equivalence is exact for the spectrum but the dual representation involves a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian and requires careful handling of the ordering problem and contour deformation to avoid poles.

## Method
The EO-transformation is derived using a phase-space-like path integral: a nonlinear change of variable σ(τ) → u(τ) via ω^2 + iσ(τ) = −\dot u + u^2 is performed on the functional determinant of the operator −∂_τ^2 + ω^2 + iσ(τ). The Jacobian of this transformation is computed explicitly, yielding a local expression for the partition function in terms of ∫_0^β dτ u(τ). For anharmonic potentials, an auxiliary Lagrange multiplier σ(τ) is introduced to enforce the constraint γ(τ) = \vec q^2(τ), and after applying the EO-transformation the path integral is decomposed into angular-momentum sectors with degeneracy weights a_l^{(N)}. The resulting dual action is then evaluated either in radial coordinates (for N ≥ 2) or by integrating out γ(τ) for the quartic anharmonicity case.

## Key numbers
- Partition function for the harmonic oscillator: Z_β(0) = (2 sinh(ωβ/2))^{-N}
- Jacobian of the change of variables \vec q → \vec q_1: ||δ\vec q_1/δ\vec q|| = [sinh(½∫_0^β dτ u(τ)) / sinh(½ωβ)]^N
- Jacobian of the transformation σ → u: ||−i δσ/δu|| = \tilde C · 2 sinh(∫_0^β dτ u(τ))
- Degeneracy weights for N > 1: a_l^{(N)} = (N + 2l − 2) (N + l − 3)! / [(N − 2)! l!]; for N = 1: a_0^{(1)} = a_1^{(1)} = 1, a_l^{(1)} = 0 for l ≥ 2
- Rigid rotator energy spectrum (constant radius R_0): E_l = (N + 2l − 2)^2 / (16 R_0^2)
- For N = 2 quantum pendulum in polar-angle coordinate: E_l = l^2 / (4 R_0^2)

## Context
This work extends the equivalent-oscillator transformation previously developed by Andrianov (AAA) for relating Hermitian and non-Hermitian oscillator systems, and connects to studies of PT-symmetric non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with real spectra by Bender (2002), Jones (2005), and others. The duality is shown to reproduce the standard radial Schrödinger equation for O(N)-symmetric potentials, and for the quartic anharmonicity it yields a dual action with complex potential unbounded from below, matching perturbative results found by Zinn-Justin (1981) and Avron (1981) for the singlet sector. The method enables exact calculation of quantum pendulum spectra and provides a dual representation of n-point correlators, establishing a potential link to ODE/IM correspondence and integrable models as discussed by Dorey and Tateo (1999).
