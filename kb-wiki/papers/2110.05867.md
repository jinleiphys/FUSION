# Hydrodynamic analyses of nuclear collisions in Landau and Eckart frames

> Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic model at finite density is a promising tool for analyzing the dense QCD matter created in the beam energy scan experiments. The hydrodynamic frame can be chosen in the direction of energy flow, which is called the Landau frame, or conserved charge flow, which is called the Eckart frame. In this study, I investigate the stability and causality of full second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equations in the two frames. Then the effects of frame choice on hydrodynamic variables and experimental observables are estimated numerically and are found to be visible on the flow but limited on the rapidity distributions.

## Key claim
The study finds that the choice between Landau and Eckart frames in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics has visible effects on the hydrodynamic flow but only limited effects on rapidity distributions of particles, with the caveat that these conclusions are drawn from (1+1)-dimensional simulations with specific parameter choices and without shear or bulk viscosities.

## Method
The author performs linear mode analysis of full second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equations in both Landau and Eckart frames to examine stability and causality conditions. Numerical (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of 17.3 GeV Pb+Pb collisions are then conducted using parametric initial conditions at τ_th = 3 fm/c with the neos B equation of state, comparing ideal, baryon diffusive, and energy dissipative evolutions in both frames.

## Key numbers
- Initial thermalization time: τ_th = 3 fm/c
- Simulation time for comparison: τ = 20 fm/c
- Beam energy: 17.3 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS
- Transport coefficients used in Eckart frame: κ_W = 10(e+P), τ_W = 2κ_W/(e+P)T
- Causality condition in Landau frame: κ_V ≥ 0 and τ_V ≥ 0 in long wavelength limit
- Causality condition in Eckart frame: κ_W ≥ 0 and τ_W - κ_W/(e+P)T ≥ 0 in long wavelength limit

## Context
This work extends the relativistic hydrodynamic framework for dense QCD matter created in beam energy scan experiments, building on the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1979) and earlier work by Monnai (2019). The study addresses the long-standing issue of hydrodynamic frame fixing in systems with non-vanishing conserved currents, comparing the Landau frame (Landau 1959) and Eckart frame (Eckart 1940). The results enable future (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulations with shear and bulk viscosities, and analyses of boosted systems.
