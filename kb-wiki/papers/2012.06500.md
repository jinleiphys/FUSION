# Microscopic origin of reflection-asymmetric nuclear shapes

> Background: The presence of nuclear ground states with stable reflection-asymmetric shapes is supported by rich experimental evidence. Theoretical surveys of odd-multipolarity deformations predict the existence of pear-shaped isotopes in several fairly localized regions of the nuclear landscape in the vicinity of near-lying single-particle shells with $Δ\ell=Δj=3$. Purpose: We analyze the role of isoscalar, isovector, neutron-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-proton multipole interaction energies in inducing the onset of reflection-asymmetric ground-state deformations. Methods: The calculations are performed in the framework of axial reflection-asymmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using two Skyrme energy density functionals and density-dependent pairing force. Results: We show that reflection-asymmetric ground-state shapes of atomic nuclei are driven by the odd-multipolarity neutron-proton (or isoscalar) part of the nuclear interaction energy. This result is consistent with the particle-vibration picture, in which the main driver of octupole instability is the isoscalar octupole-octupole interaction giving rise to large $E3$ polarizability.

## Key claim

Reflection-asymmetric (pear-shaped) nuclear ground states are driven primarily by the odd-multipolarity neutron-proton (isoscalar) part of the nuclear interaction energy, but the total reflection-asymmetric deformation energy results from dramatic cancellations between even- and odd-multipolarity components, making the final outcome highly sensitive to small variations in individual contributions.

## Method

The authors performed axial reflection-asymmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the code hfbtho (v3.00) with two Skyrme energy density functionals (SLy4 and UNEDF2) and density-dependent pairing forces. They decomposed the total HFB energy into multipole components (monopole, dipole, quadrupole, octupole, and higher) and further separated these into isoscalar, isovector, neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton contributions. Calculations were carried out for even-even barium, radium, uranium, and ytterbium isotopes by constraining the octupole deformation β₃ from 0.0 to 0.25 at fixed equilibrium quadrupole deformation β₂⁽⁰⁾.

## Key numbers

- For ²²⁴Ra at β₃=0.15, the multipole expansion summed up to λ=9 converges to within about 20 keV of the total energy, while the monopole component alone is off by about 150 MeV.
- The ratio of coupling constants for the octupole-octupole interaction is estimated as κ̃ₙₙ/κ̃ₙₚ ≈ κ̃ₚₚ/κ̃ₙₚ ≈ 0.27, based on average octupole polarizabilities χ₃,₀ ≈ 2 and χ₃,₁ ≈ -0.4.
- For the isoscalar octupole polarizability, the simple estimate gives χ₃,₀(Δ𝒩=1)=3 and χ₃,₀(Δ𝒩=3)=1/3; for the isovector case, χ₃,₁(Δ𝒩=1)=-0.78 and χ₃,₁(Δ𝒩=3)=-0.54.
- The deformation energy ΔE = E(β₃=0.05) - E(β₃=0) was used as a measure of octupole deformability; negative values of ΔE delineate regions where reflection-asymmetric deformations set in for Ba, Ra, and U isotopes.
- Pairing strengths used: for SLy4, Vₙ = -325.25 MeV and Vₚ = -340.06 MeV; for UNEDF2, Vₙ = -231.30 MeV and Vₚ = -255.04 MeV (adjusted to reproduce the average experimental neutron pairing gap for ¹²⁰Sn, Δₙ = 1.245 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the quadrupole-energy analysis of Dobaczewski (1998) and Werner (1994) to odd-multipolarity shapes, providing a microscopic decomposition of the deformation energy that had previously been studied only phenomenologically. The results are consistent with the particle-vibration coupling picture of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Bes (1975), where the isoscalar octupole-octupole interaction drives octupole instability. The analysis enables future studies to understand why certain localized regions of pear-shaped nuclei appear near single-particle shells with Δℓ=Δj=3, as surveyed by Cao (2020), and why the deformation energies are so small compared to individual multipole contributions.
