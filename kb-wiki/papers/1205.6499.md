# Spin Response and Neutrino Emissivity of Dense Neutron Matter

> We study the spin response of cold dense neutron matter in the limit of zero momentum transfer, and show that the frequency dependence of the long-wavelength spin response is well constrained by sum-rules and the asymptotic behavior of the two-particle response at high frequency. The sum-rules are calculated using Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo technique and the high frequency two-particle response is calculated for several nucleon-nucleon potentials. At nuclear saturation density, the sum-rules suggest that the strength of the spin response peaks at $ω\simeq$ 40--60 MeV, decays rapidly for $ω\geq $100 MeV, and has a sizable strength below 40 MeV. This strength at relatively low energy may lead to enhanced neutrino production rates in dense neutron-rich matter at temperatures of relevance to core-collapse supernova.

## Key claim

The spin response of cold dense neutron matter at zero momentum transfer is well constrained by combining sum-rules (calculated via Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo) with the asymptotic high-frequency behavior of the two-particle response, but the reconstruction is not unique and depends on the chosen parametrization; the resulting low-energy strength may lead to enhanced neutrino production rates in core-collapse supernova conditions, though three-nucleon forces are expected to introduce 10–20% corrections to the sum-rules.

## Method

The authors used Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) with the Argonne AV8' two-body potential to compute three sum-rules of the spin response function at zero momentum transfer: the inverse-energy-weighted sum-rule \(S_\sigma^{-1}\) (related to spin susceptibility), the static sum-rule \(S_\sigma^{0}\), and the energy-weighted sum-rule \(S_\sigma^{+1}\). They supplemented these sum-rules with the asymptotic high-frequency behavior of the two-particle response, obtained by solving the two-neutron Schrödinger equation with the same Hamiltonian in a spherical cavity. Two different parametrizations of the response function were then fitted to satisfy the sum-rule constraints and the high-frequency tail.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear saturation density (\(n = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the sum-rules give \(S_\sigma^{-1} = 0.0044(7)\) MeV\(^{-1}\), \(S_\sigma^{0} = 0.20(1)\), and \(S_\sigma^{+1} = 11(1)\) MeV.
* The average excitation energies at saturation density are \(\bar{\omega}_0 = 46(11)\) MeV and \(\bar{\omega}_1 = 55(8)\) MeV, indicating a peak in the spin response near 40–60 MeV.
* The spin susceptibility relative to the free Fermi gas is \(\chi / \chi_F = 0.34\) at \(n = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The high-frequency asymptotic behavior follows \(S_\sigma^{\text{high}}(\omega) \propto (\omega_c / \omega)^9\) with \(\omega_c \simeq 100\)–150 MeV for the densities considered.
* The low-frequency relaxation time parameter \(C_\sigma\) at nuclear density is \(\simeq 0.22\) MeV\(^{-1}\) for one-pion exchange and \(\simeq 0.08\) MeV\(^{-1}\) for realistic potentials (Bacca 2009).

## Context

This work extends earlier sum-rule estimates by Sigl (1996) and Olsson (2004) by computing all three lowest-order sum-rules simultaneously and combining them with a direct calculation of the two-particle high-frequency response. It provides a non-perturbative constraint on the spin structure function that can be used to compute neutrino emissivity via the bremsstrahlung process \(n+n \rightarrow n+n+\nu+\bar{\nu}\), improving upon perturbative approaches such as those of Friman and Maxwell (1979) and Hanhart et al. (2001). The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino production rates in core-collapse supernova simulations, though the method does not yet include three-nucleon forces or superfluid pairing effects relevant for cold neutron stars.
