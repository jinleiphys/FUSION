# Time-dependent mean field determination of the excitation energy in transfer reactions: application to the reaction $^{238}$U on $^{12}$C at 6.14 MeV/A

> The internal excitation of nuclei after multi-nucleon transfer is estimated by using the time-dependent mean-field theory. Transfer probabilities for each channel as well as the energy loss after re-separation are calculated. By combining these two informations, we show that the excitation energy distribution of the transfer fragments can be obtained separately for the different transfer channels. The method is applied to the reaction involving a $^{238}$U beam on a $^{12}$C target, which has recently been measured at GANIL. It is shown that the excitation energy calculated with the microscopic theory compares well with the experimental observation, provided that the competition with fusion is properly taken into account. The reliability of the excitation energy is further confirmed by the comparison with the phenomenological HIPSE model at higher center of mass energies.

# Time-dependent mean field determination of the excitation energy in transfer reactions: application to the reaction $^{238}$U on $^{12}$C at 6.14 MeV/A

## Key claim
The time-dependent mean-field theory can determine the excitation energy distribution of transfer fragments separately for each transfer channel, and these calculated excitation energies compare well with experimental observations, but only when the competition with fusion is properly taken into account and after applying a 3 MeV shift to correct for the systematic overestimation at low center-of-mass energies due to the poor treatment of light systems like $^{12}$C in the mean-field approach.

## Method
The authors performed TDHF+BCS calculations for the $^{238}$U + $^{12}$C reaction at 6.14 MeV/A using the Skyrme force Sly4d with surface pairing interaction, with calculations done at zero impact parameter and two orientations of the deformed $^{238}$U nucleus (parallel and perpendicular). The impact parameter dependence was incorporated effectively using a formula relating the center-of-mass energy to the distance of closest approach, and the excitation energy was extracted from the energy loss between entrance and exit channels. Transfer probabilities for each channel were obtained using a projection operator technique, and the final transfer-induced fission cross sections were computed by combining the pure transfer probabilities with phenomenological fusion and fission probabilities, then averaging over all orientations of the uranium nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The dynamical fusion barriers for $^{238}$U+$^{12}$C were estimated to be 57.6 MeV for parallel orientation ($\theta=0$) and 63.9 MeV for perpendicular orientation ($\theta=\pi/2$).
- The excitation energy as a function of center-of-mass energy was parametrized as $E^* = \exp[a(E_{\rm cm}' - B_{\rm fus}(\theta)) + c]$ with $a=0.75$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $c=1.84$.
- The experimental fission probability for $^{240}$Pu was parametrized with parameters $f_1=0.4384$, $f_2=0.3034$, $f_3=0.6554$ MeV$^{-1}$, $f_4=3.233$, $f_5=0.07403$, $f_6=0.9167$ MeV$^{-1}$, and $f_7=11.94$.
- The TDHF+BCS results overestimated experimental excitation energies at low center-of-mass energies by approximately 3 MeV, requiring a shift of $\langle E^* \rangle_{\rm cor}(E_{\rm cm}) = \langle E^* \rangle(E_{\rm cm} - 3\ {\rm MeV})$.
- The deformation parameter of $^{238}$U was found to be $\beta_2 = 0.244$.
- HIPSE simulations were performed at beam energies of 5.6, 5.7, 5.8, 6.0, and 6.14 MeV/A with $8 \times 10^6$ events per energy and impact parameters from 0 to 4 fm.

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent mean-field approach for transfer reactions previously developed by Scamps et al. (2013) and the projection technique of Simenel (2011) to extract channel-by-channel excitation energies, applied to the $^{238}$U+$^{12}$C reaction measured at GANIL by Rodriguez-Tajes et al. (2014, 2016). The method enables comparison with experimental transfer-induced fission data and is further validated against the phenomenological HIPSE model of Lacroix et al. (2004), which was originally designed for heavy-ion reactions around the Fermi energy. The results demonstrate that mean-field theory can provide chemical and energetic information on transfer reactions channel-by-channel, despite being an average theory, and suggest future improvements using TDHFB theory with Skyrme or Gogny effective interactions.
