# Phase transition to the state with nonzero average helicity in dense neutron matter

> The possibility of the appearance of the states with a nonzero average helicity in neutron matter is studied in the model with the Skyrme effective interaction. By providing the analysis of the self-consistent equations at zero temperature, it is shown that neutron matter with the Skyrme BSk18 effective force undergoes at high densities a phase transition to the state in which the degeneracy with respect to helicity of neutrons is spontaneously removed.

## Key claim

Neutron matter described by the Skyrme BSk18 effective interaction undergoes a spontaneous phase transition at high densities to a state with nonzero average helicity, where the degeneracy with respect to neutron helicity is removed. This transition occurs specifically because the first Landau parameter \(G_1\) becomes sufficiently negative (\(G_1 < -3\)) while the zeroth parameter \(G_0\) remains above \(-1\), conditions that the BSk18 force satisfies even though it avoids ferromagnetic instability.

## Method

The authors formulated self-consistent equations for the normal distribution function of neutrons at zero temperature using the Skyrme effective interaction with the BSk18 parametrization, which includes additional density-dependent terms beyond the standard Skyrme force. They derived expressions for the single-particle energy with a helicity-dependent splitting \(\Delta(p)\) and solved the coupled equations for the renormalized chemical potential and the parameter \(\nu\) that determines \(\Delta(p)\). The energy density of the helicity-ordered state was then compared to that of the normal state to establish thermodynamic preference.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for the phase transition: \(\varrho \approx 5.67\,\varrho_0\), where \(\varrho_0 = 0.1586\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for the BSk18 force.
- Saturation density where average helicity reaches \(\lambda = 1\) (all neutron spins aligned along their momenta): \(\varrho \approx 6.93\,\varrho_0\).
- The energy splitting \(\Delta(p_F)\) between neutron spectra with different helicities becomes comparable to the neutron Fermi energy \(\varepsilon_F\) at high densities.
- The difference between energy densities of the helicity-ordered state and the normal state is negative for all densities where nontrivial solutions exist, confirming thermodynamic preference.

## Context

This work extends the study of spontaneous spin polarization in nuclear matter, which had previously been predicted by various Skyrme parametrizations at densities from \(\varrho_0\) to \(4\varrho_0\) but contradicted by realistic NN interactions (Pandharipande et al., 1972; Bombaci et al., 2006). The BSk18 force, proposed by Chamel, Goriely, and Pearson (2009), was designed to avoid ferromagnetic instability while preserving fits to nuclear masses and microscopic neutron matter results. The present analysis shows that even without ferromagnetic instability, a helicity-ordered state can appear at high densities, which may affect neutrino opacities and the thermal evolution of neutron stars. Future work could examine the influence of strong magnetic fields characteristic of pulsars and magnetars on the average helicity.
