# Delta electroproduction in a chiral bag model approach

> We study the γ^* N -> Δtransition amplitudes in a recoil corrected cloudy bag model approach. A modified Peierls-Thouless projection method is used to construct the Galilean invariant baryon states. The pionic contribution is found to be significant. The effect of the recoil correction is to reduce the magnitude of the transition amplitudes at small momentum transfer and to enhance them at modest momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The gamma* N -> Delta transition amplitudes are calculated in a recoil corrected cloudy bag model, where the pionic contribution is found to be significant and the recoil correction reduces the magnitude of the transition amplitudes at small momentum transfer while enhancing them at modest momentum transfers. The authors note that with a small bag radius of 0.7 fm they are able to reproduce the experimental helicity amplitude, but the results depend on the bag radius and the simplified Peierls-Thouless projection method used.

## Method

The authors used a modified Peierls-Thouless projection method to construct Galilean invariant baryon states from the cloudy bag model, correcting for spurious center-of-mass motion. They calculated the helicity amplitudes for virtual photoproduction of the Delta resonance, including contributions from the bare quark core (Fig. 1a), pion cloud corrections to the baryon states (Fig. 1b), and the photon-pion-pion coupling vertex (Fig. 1c), with intermediate baryons restricted to N and Delta states.

## Key numbers

- The bare transition amplitude for the real photon limit (Q^2 -> 0) increases with bag radius, with the PT recoil correction reducing bare transition amplitudes by 5 to 10 percent for Q^2 less than 0.5 GeV^2 within a reasonable range of bag radii.
- For bag radius R = 0.7 fm with PT projection, the total helicity amplitude A_{3/2} is -249 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (compared to the Particle Data Group estimate of -258 +/- 6 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}).
- The static (no recoil correction) total helicity amplitude A_{3/2} for R = 0.7 fm is -260 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, while for R = 1.0 fm it is -205 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.
- The renormalized pi NN coupling constant used is f^{NN} approximately 3.03, corresponding to f^2_{pi NN} approximately 0.081.
- The PT projection reduces the transition moment by about 5 to 8 percent overall.

## Context

This work extends earlier cloudy bag model calculations of delta photoproduction by Ke and by Bermuth et al., which neglected the recoil correction. The authors note that in similar calculations using constituent quark models (nonrelativistic and relativized quark models), the helicity amplitudes are usually significantly underpredicted. The results enable more accurate predictions for the gamma* N -> Delta transition form factors, which can be tested against anticipated high-quality data from new generation accelerators.
