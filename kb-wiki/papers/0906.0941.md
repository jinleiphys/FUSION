# On an exact hydrodynamic solution for the elliptic flow

> Looking for the underlying hydrodynamic mechanisms determining the elliptic flow we show that for an expanding relativistic perfect fluid the transverse flow may derive from a solvable hydrodynamic potential, if the entropy is transversally conserved and the corresponding expansion "quasi-stationary", that is mainly governed by the temperature cooling. Exact solutions for the velocity flow coefficients $v_2$ and the temperature dependence of the spatial and momentum anisotropy are obtained and shown to be in agreement with the elliptic flow features of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an exact analytic solution for elliptic flow can be derived from a hydrodynamic potential if the transverse flow is assumed to be both transversally isentropic and "quasi-stationary" (driven mainly by temperature cooling), with the caveat that these approximations are valid only in the supersonic regime and at not-too-short times.

## Method

The authors assume a relativistic perfect fluid with constant speed of sound, restrict to the central rapidity region, and impose transverse entropy conservation together with a quasi-stationary condition where the transverse flow evolves only through temperature changes. Using the Khalatnikov-Kamenshchik (KK) potential method and a hodograph transformation from spatial coordinates to temperature and azimuthal angle, they derive a linear partial differential equation for the potential and obtain exact solutions in terms of hypergeometric and Meijer functions. The elliptic flow coefficient v2 and spatial eccentricity are then expressed analytically as functions of temperature through Fourier expansion of the potential.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound is taken as the reference value c_s = 1/√3 (approximately 0.577) throughout the main calculations
- The geometrical anisotropy parameter ρ is set to 0.8 for the temperature dependence curves shown in Fig. 1
- The supersonic bound temperature T_s is given by T_s = T_0 √(1 - c_s^2), where T_0 is the Bernoulli constant temperature
- The initial temperature T_I is constrained to satisfy T_I ≤ T_s, with the fluid always in the supersonic regime v_⊥ ≥ c_s
- The ratio v2/ε is plotted as a function of rescaled time θ for c_s^2 values of 1/3, 1/5, 1/7, and 1/10 (Fig. 3, lower panel)

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic potential method of Khalatnikov and Kamenshchik (KK) for stationary transverse flow to a quasi-stationary regime applicable to heavy-ion collisions, building on the longitudinal (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic approach of Landau and Bjorken. The analytic solutions reproduce qualitative features observed in numerical hydrodynamic simulations by Hirano and others, including the temperature dependence of spatial and momentum anisotropies, the linear dependence of v2 on centrality, and the centrality and speed-of-sound dependence of v2/ε. The results enable analytic study of the hydrodynamic mechanisms underlying elliptic flow and may facilitate connections to gauge/gravity dual approaches, though quantitative agreement would require more refined initial conditions and inclusion of weak viscosity.
