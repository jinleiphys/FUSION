# Shape fluctuations in a Fermi system with nonlinear dissipativity

> The contribution of thermal fluctuations to the widths of isoscalar giant multipole resonances (GMR) in heated nuclei is studied. Starting from the collisional kinetic equation, it is shown that an additional contribution to the nuclear friction and the corresponding GMR widths arises due to the nonlinear dissipativity effect. It is also shown that the magnitude of the contributions of the thermal fluctuations to the nuclear friction coefficient and the GMR widths do not exceed 20%.

# Shape fluctuations in a Fermi system with nonlinear dissipativity

## Key claim
The contribution of thermal fluctuations to the widths of isoscalar giant multipole resonances (GMR) in heated nuclei arises from nonlinear dissipativity effects in the collision integral, but the magnitude of these contributions to the nuclear friction coefficient and the GMR widths does not exceed approximately 20%.

## Method
Starting from the collisional kinetic equation with a random force, the authors derived hydrodynamic-like equations of motion for particle density, velocity field, and pressure tensor using p-moment techniques with Fermi-surface distortion restricted to multipolarity l=2. They performed a high-order expansion of the collision integral up to third order in the variation of the distribution function, then carried out ensemble averaging to obtain the macroscopic response function and strength function for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in a nucleus with A=224.

## Key numbers
- The maximum value of the relaxation parameter ℏ/τ′ does not exceed 0.07 MeV for the nucleus with A=224.
- The maximum value of the relaxation parameter ℏ/τ′′ does not exceed 0.49 MeV for the nucleus with A=224.
- The ratio τ₂/τ′′ at zero temperature is 1.16 for the nucleus with A=224.
- The ratio Δγ/γ₀ reaches a maximum value that does not exceed approximately 0.2 (20%) as a function of temperature for the GQR in the nucleus with A=224.
- The phenomenological parametrization of the GQR energy used is E_R = 60·A^{-1/3} MeV.
- The nuclear radius parameter used is r₀ = 1.12 fm, with Fermi energy ε_F = 39 MeV.
- The surface energy coefficient b_S = 17.2 MeV and Coulomb energy coefficient b_C = 0.7 MeV were used for the liquid drop stiffness coefficient.
- The in-medium scattering parameter α = 9.2 MeV was used, corresponding to σ_in ≈ σ_free/2 with σ_free ≈ 40 mb.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic theory approach to nuclear collective motion, building on prior semiclassical treatments of collisional damping in cold nuclei by Bertsch (1977), Brink and collaborators, and Kolomietz (1990). It addresses the open problem of the temperature dependence of GMR widths in hot nuclei, where previous work by Orlandi and collaborators (1990) and Blatt and collaborators considered thermal shape fluctuations, while Brussaard and collaborators (1990) and Vinh Mau and collaborators studied thermal contributions from increased nucleon-nucleon collision rates. The nonlinear dissipativity effect identified here provides an additional mechanism beyond the linear-order variation of the collision integral, where thermal fluctuations do not contribute to dissipation. This enables improved predictions for the temperature-dependent width of giant resonances in heated nuclei, particularly for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance.
