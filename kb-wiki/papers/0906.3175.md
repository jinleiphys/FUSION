# A lattice test of strong coupling behaviour in QCD at finite temperature

> We propose a set of lattice measurements which could test whether the deconfined, quark-gluon plasma, phase of QCD shows strong coupling aspects at temperatures a few times the critical temperature for deconfinement, in the region where the conformal anomaly becomes unimportant. The measurements refer to twist-two operators which are not protected by symmetries and which in a strong-coupling scenario would develop large, negative, anomalous dimensions, resulting in a strong suppression of the respective lattice expectation values in the continuum limit. Special emphasis is put on the respective operator with lowest spin (the spin-2 operator orthogonal to the energy-momentum tensor within the renormalization flow) and on the case of quenched QCD, where this operator is known for arbitrary values of the coupling: this is the quark energy-momentum tensor. The proposed lattice measurements could also test whether the plasma constituents are pointlike (as expected at weak coupling), or not.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that lattice measurements of the expectation value of the spin-2 quark energy-momentum tensor (the unprotected leading-twist operator orthogonal to the total energy-momentum tensor) in quenched QCD can test whether the quark-gluon plasma at temperatures a few times the critical temperature exhibits strong-coupling behavior, by checking whether this expectation value is strongly suppressed in the continuum limit (indicating strong coupling) or only slowly evolving from ideal gas expectations (indicating weak coupling).

## Method

The authors propose computing the thermal expectation value of the fermionic twist-2 operator $\mathcal{O}^{\mu\nu}_f = \bar q \gamma^\mu iD^\nu q$ (minus trace) in quenched QCD on the lattice, where this operator is known for arbitrary coupling and represents the unique spin-2 operator orthogonal to the conserved energy-momentum tensor. The rate at which $\langle \mathcal{O}^{\mu\nu}_f \rangle_T$ approaches zero as the lattice spacing decreases (i.e., as the resolution scale $Q^2 \to \infty$ at fixed temperature $T$) distinguishes weak from strong coupling: slow logarithmic suppression at weak coupling versus rapid suppression from large negative anomalous dimensions at strong coupling.

## Key numbers

- For $T \simeq 3T_c$ and inverse lattice spacing $a^{-1} \equiv Q \simeq 4$ GeV, the deviation of $\langle \mathcal{O}^{00}_f \rangle_T$ from the ideal gas value should not exceed 30% at weak coupling (lowest-order perturbative QCD estimate).
- At strong coupling, the expectation value $\langle \mathcal{O}^{00}_f \rangle_T$ would be reduced by a factor of 5 or more compared to the ideal gas value.
- The conformal anomaly $(\epsilon - 3p)/\epsilon$ becomes smaller than 10% for temperatures $T \gtrsim 2T_c \simeq 400$ MeV, based on lattice studies by Cheng et al. (2007).
- The relevant temperature range for the proposed test is $2T_c \lesssim T \lesssim 5T_c$.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between weak-coupling (HTL-resummed perturbation theory) and strong-coupling (AdS/CFT from ${\mathcal N}=4$ SYM) approaches to the quark-gluon plasma, motivated by RHIC results on elliptic flow and jet quenching. It builds on lattice studies by Cheng et al. (2007) showing the conformal anomaly becomes small above $2T_c$, and on calculations of anomalous dimensions in ${\mathcal N}=4$ SYM by Gubser et al. (2002), Kotikov et al. (2004), and Brower et al. (2006). The proposed test would complement existing lattice measurements of charge fluctuations by Cheng et al. (2008) that support a quasiparticle picture, by directly probing whether plasma constituents are pointlike (weak coupling) or composite (strong coupling).
