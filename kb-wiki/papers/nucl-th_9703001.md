# Broken SU(3) symmetry in deformed even-even nuclei

> A collective vector-boson model with broken SU(3) symmetry, in which the ground state band and the lowest $γ$ band belong to the same irreducible representation but are non-degenerate, is applied to several deformed even-even nuclei. The model description of ground and $γ$ bands together with the corresponding B(E2) transition probabilities is investigated within a broad range of SU(3) irreducible representations $(λ,μ)$. The calculations show that the $(λ,μ)$ characteristics of rotational nuclei depend to a great extent on the magnitude of the SU(3) splitting between the ground and $γ$ bands. It is found that for weakly split spectra, the ground--$γ$ band coupling scheme is realized relevantly within narrow regions of ``favored'' $(λ,μ)$ multiplets, while in the cases of strong splitting a description in which the ground band is situated alone in an irreducible representation is favored. The obtained results are analyzed in terms of the bandmixing interactions. The possibility for a transition between the different collective SU(3) schemes is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the SU(3) irreducible representation (λ,μ) characteristics of deformed even-even nuclei depend strongly on the magnitude of the energy splitting between the ground state band and the first γ band; for weakly split spectra the ground-γ band coupling scheme is realized within narrow regions of "favored" (λ,μ) multiplets, while for strong splitting a description with the ground band alone in an irreducible representation is favored.

## Method

The authors applied a collective vector-boson model with broken SU(3) symmetry, in which the ground state band and lowest γ band belong to the same irreducible representation but are non-degenerate, to eight rare earth nuclei (164Dy, 164-168Er, 168,172Yb, 176,178Hf) and one actinide nucleus (238U). They performed simultaneous χ² minimization fits of the Hamiltonian parameters g₁, g₂, g₃ to experimental low-lying ground and γ-band energy levels (up to L=8-10) and B(E2) transition ratios, scanning SU(3) irreps within the range 10 ≤ λ ≤ 160 and 2 ≤ μ ≤ 8. The model Hamiltonian included L², L·Q·L, and A⁺A terms, with matrix elements evaluated in the Bargmann-Moshinsky basis.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) irrep range scanned was 10 ≤ λ ≤ 160 and 2 ≤ μ ≤ 8.
- For 168Er, the model reproduced the interband ratio R₁(2)=1.81, R₁(4)=5.34, R₁(6)=10.31, compared to experimental values R₁(2)=1.78, R₁(4)=4.81, R₁(6)=10.6.
- The energy rms deviation σ_E and transition ratio rms deviation σ_B were used as quality measures, with n_E equal to the number of levels and n_B the number of ratios in each fit.
- For 238U, only intraband ratios were considered due to insufficient experimental interband transition data, with the ground band fitted up to L=18 and the γ-band up to L=5.

## Context

This work extends the vector-boson model with broken SU(3) symmetry previously developed by the same group (Minkov et al., 1995-1996) to a systematic survey across multiple deformed nuclei, going beyond the earlier restriction to (λ,2) irreps. It contrasts with the pseudo-SU(3) model of Casten et al. (1990), which used only energy fits and did not reproduce interband B(E2) ratios accurately (e.g., for 168Er giving R₁(4)=3.0 versus experimental 4.81), and with the SU(3) limit of the Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello, 1978) where the ground band sits alone in an irrep and the γ and β bands are degenerate. The results enable systematic determination of favored SU(3) irreps directly from experimental data and clarify the transition between different collective SU(3) coupling schemes as a function of ground-γ band splitting.
