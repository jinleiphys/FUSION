# Spin-isospin Response in Finite Nuclei from an Extended Skyrme Interaction

> The magnetic dipole (M1) and the Gamow-Teller (GT) excitations of finite nuclei have been studied in a fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) approach by using a Skyrme energy density functional with spin and spin-isospin densities. To this end, we adopt the extended SLy5st interaction which includes spin-density dependent terms and stabilize nuclear matter with respect to spin instabilities. The effect of the spin-density dependent terms is examined in both the mean field and the spin-flip excited state calculations. The numerical results show that those terms give appreciable repulsive contributions to the M1 and GT response functions of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The spin-density dependent terms added to the standard SLy5 Skyrme interaction (creating the extended SLy5st interaction) give repulsive contributions to the M1 and GT response functions of finite nuclei, shifting the excitation energies upward, but the improvement in agreement with experimental data is marginal and a global refitting of all parameters is needed for better quantitative results.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for the nuclei ⁹⁰Zr and ²⁰⁸Pb using the standard SLy5 Skyrme parameter set and the extended SLy5st set which includes spin-density and spin-isospin density dependent terms. The spin-density dependent terms were switched on and off in both the HF mean field and the RPA residual interaction calculations to isolate their effect on the M1 and GT response functions.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless Landau parameter G₀' increases from -0.14 (SLy5) to 0.15 (SLy5st) when spin-density dependent terms are included.
- The dimensionless Landau parameter G₀ increases from 1.12 (SLy5) to 1.19 (SLy5st).
- For GT excitations in ⁹⁰Zr, the low-lying centroid energy shifts from 5.23 MeV to 5.53 MeV (shift of 0.3 MeV) and the high-lying centroid shifts from 16.26 MeV to 16.68 MeV (shift of 0.42 MeV) when including spin-density dependent terms.
- For GT excitations in ²⁰⁸Pb, the low-lying centroid energy shifts from 9.87 MeV to 10.92 MeV (shift of 1.05 MeV) and the high-lying centroid shifts from 18.13 MeV to 19.07 MeV (shift of 0.94 MeV).
- The RPA collective GT state in ²⁰⁸Pb at 19.07 MeV (with spin-density terms) is close to the experimental GT excitation energy of 19.2 ± 0.2 MeV.
- For M1 excitations in ²⁰⁸Pb, peak energies are 7.6 MeV (lower) and 9.3 MeV (higher) with spin-density terms, compared to 7.4 MeV and 9.1 MeV without them.
- For M1 excitations in ⁹⁰Zr, the peak energy is 9.4 MeV with spin-density terms and 9.1 MeV without them.

## Context

This work extends the earlier development of the extended Skyrme interaction by Margueron et al. (2012), which added spin-density dependent terms to improve spin and spin-isospin properties of nuclear matter based on microscopic G-matrix calculations. The study applies this interaction to finite nuclei for the first time, examining M1 and GT excitations in ⁹⁰Zr and ²⁰⁸Pb. The results show that the spin-density terms can modify spin-flip response functions without altering ground-state properties of even-even nuclei, but the authors note that a global refitting of both spin-independent and spin-dependent density terms is needed, and future work should also include tensor forces as studied by Cao et al. (2011) and Bai et al. (2011).
