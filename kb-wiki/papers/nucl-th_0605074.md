# Dimer scattering in the epsilon expansion

> The atom-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering lengths are analytically calculated in an expansion around four spatial dimensions for fermions with a large 2-body scattering length 'a'. We find the atom-dimer scattering length a_ad/a = 4/3-2/9 epsilon and the dimer-dimer scattering length a_dd/a = epsilon -0.344 epsilon^2, where epsilon = 4-d and d is the number of spatial dimensions. These ratios a_ad/a = 1.11 and a_dd/a = 0.656 at epsilon = 1 are to be compared with the non-perturbatively calculated numerical results 1.18 and 0.6 respectively. The neutron-deuteron scattering length in the quartet channel a_nD = 4.78 fm is in reasonable agreement with the experimental value 6.35 fm, considering 2-body effective range corrections r_0/a = 0.4 were excluded. The deuteron-deuteron scattering length a_DD = 3.15 fm in the spin-2 channel.

## Key claim

The atom-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering lengths are analytically calculated to next-to-leading order in an epsilon expansion around four spatial dimensions for fermions with a large but finite two-body scattering length, with the caveat that the results at epsilon=1 (three spatial dimensions) are perturbatively close to but not identical to non-perturbatively calculated numerical values, and that two-body effective range corrections were excluded.

## Method

The author formulates an epsilon expansion in d=4-epsilon spatial dimensions for a system of spin-1/2 fermions with a large two-body S-wave scattering length, using a Lagrangian density with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a dimer field. The coupling constant is chosen so that the dimer wavefunction renormalization is of order 1 plus O(epsilon), making the epsilon expansion equivalent to a loop expansion, and the atom-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering amplitudes are calculated to next-to-leading order in epsilon.

## Key numbers

- Atom-dimer scattering length ratio: a_ad/a = 4/3 - (2/9)epsilon + O(epsilon^2), giving a_ad/a = 1.11 at epsilon=1, compared to the non-perturbative numerical result 1.18
- Dimer-dimer scattering length ratio: a_dd/a = epsilon - 0.344 epsilon^2 + O(epsilon^3), giving a_dd/a = 0.656 at epsilon=1, compared to the non-perturbative numerical result 0.6
- Neutron-deuteron scattering length in the quartet channel: a_nD = 4.78 fm, compared to the experimental value a_exp_nD = 6.35 +/- 0.02 fm
- Deuteron-deuteron scattering length in the spin-2 channel: a_DD = 3.15 fm
- The ratio T_ad/T_aa = 1 - epsilon/6 + O(epsilon^2) = 0.83 at epsilon=1, compared to the non-perturbative value 0.885 (corresponding to a_ad = 1.18 a)
- The ratio T_dd/T_aa = (1/2)epsilon - 0.172 epsilon^2 + O(epsilon^3) = 0.328 at epsilon=1

## Context

This work extends the epsilon expansion technique developed by Nishida and Son (2006) for thermodynamic properties of dilute Fermi gases to few-body scattering problems, specifically atom-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering. The epsilon expansion provides a perturbative alternative to the non-perturbative resummation methods used in effective field theory by Bedaque, Kaplan, and others (1997-1999) for three-dimensional systems with large scattering lengths, and to the partial resummation of dimer-dimer diagrams by Pieri and Strinati (2000). The results enable perturbative study of many-body properties at finite scattering length, such as Bose-Einstein condensation in atomic systems near Feshbach resonances, and can be generalized to deuteron scattering in nuclear physics spin channels where all nucleon spins are aligned.
