# Hadron transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis normalized and unnormalized statistics

> The exact analytical formulas for the transverse momentum distributions of the Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac and Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics of particles with nonzero mass in the framework of the Tsallis normalized and Tsallis unnormalized (also known as Tsallis-1 and Tsallis-2) statistics have been consistently derived. The final exact results were expressed in terms of the series expansions in the integral representation. The zeroth term approximation to both quantum and classical statistics of particles has been introduced. We have revealed that the phenomenological classical Tsallis distribution (widely used in high energy physics) is equal to the distribution of the Tsallis unnormalized statistics in the zeroth term approximation, but the phenomenological quantum Tsallis distributions (introduced by definition on the basis of the generalized entropy of the ideal gas) do not correspond to the distributions of the Tsallis statistics. We have found that in the ranges of the entropic parameter relevant to the processes of high-energy physics ($q<1$ for Tsallis-1 and $q>1$ for Tsallis-2) the Tsallis statistics is divergent. Therefore, to obtain physical results, we have regularized the Tsallis statistics by introducing an upper cut-off in the series expansion. The exact numerical results for the Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac and Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics of particles in the Tsallis normalized and unnormalized statistics have been obtained. We observed that the exact results of the Tsallis statistics strongly enhanced the production of high-$p_{T}$ hadrons in comparison with the usual phenomenological Tsallis distribution function at the same values of $q$. The $q$-duality of the Tsallis normalized and unnormalized statistics for the massive particles was studied.

## Key claim

The phenomenological classical Tsallis distribution widely used in high-energy physics is exactly equal to the zeroth term approximation of the Tsallis unnormalized (Tsallis-2) statistics for massive particles, but the phenomenological quantum Tsallis distributions for Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics do not correspond to any distribution derived from the Tsallis statistics. The exact Tsallis statistics strongly enhances high-\(p_{T}\) hadron production compared to the phenomenological Tsallis distribution at the same \(q\) values, and the Tsallis statistics diverges in the entropic parameter ranges relevant to high-energy physics (\(q<1\) for Tsallis-1 and \(q>1\) for Tsallis-2), requiring regularization via an upper cut-off in the series expansion.

## Method

The authors derived exact analytical formulas for transverse momentum distributions of Bose-Einstein, Fermi-Dirac, and Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics of massive particles in the grand canonical ensemble for both Tsallis normalized (Tsallis-1) and Tsallis unnormalized (Tsallis-2) statistics, expressing results as series expansions in integral representation using the Hilhorst formula. They introduced the zeroth term approximation for both quantum and classical statistics, and regularized the divergent series by introducing an upper cut-off determined from the minima of the characteristic function. The exact numerical results were obtained for massive pions with mass \(m = 139.57\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Temperature \(T = 82\) MeV, chemical potential \(\mu = 0\), radius \(R = 4\) fm, and pion mass \(m = 139.57\) MeV were used for numerical calculations of the Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics.
- The zeroth term approximation for Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics yields the distribution \(\frac{d^{2}N}{dp_{T}dy} = \frac{gV}{(2\pi)^{2}} p_{T} m_{T} \cosh y \left[1+ \frac{1-q}{q} \frac{m_{T} \cosh y-\mu}{T} \right]^{\frac{1}{q-1}}\), which under the transformation \(q \to 1/q\) recovers the phenomenological Tsallis distribution of Cleymans et al. (2012).
- The norm function \(\Lambda\) in the zeroth term approximation of Tsallis-1 statistics equals zero (\(\Lambda = 0\)).

## Context

This work generalizes the earlier ultrarelativistic (massless) results of Parvan (2017) to massive particles for all three statistics (Maxwell-Boltzmann, Fermi-Dirac, Bose-Einstein), and extends the exact Tsallis formalism previously applied to massless particles in Parvan et al. (2016). The paper clarifies the relationship between the phenomenological Tsallis-like distribution used by Cleymans et al. (2012) and the fundamental Tsallis statistics, showing that the phenomenological quantum distributions do not correspond to the exact Tsallis statistics. The results enable proper fitting of experimental hadron transverse momentum spectra from LHC and RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, CMS, ATLAS) using the exact Tsallis formalism rather than the approximate phenomenological form, particularly improving description at low \(p_{T}\) for light hadrons at low collision energies as noted in comparisons with NA61/SHINE data.
