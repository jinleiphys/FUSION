# Simulation of Heavy Ion Reactions with Nonlocal Kinetic Equations

> The incorporation of realistic trajectories of two-particle scattering into existing BUU or QMD codes meets considerable difficulties. We propose a method of replacing the scattering event by a tractable non-local scenario reproducing the correct asymptotics. The first principle derivation justifies the use of corresponding nonlocal kinetic equations. The nonlocal shifts are necessary if the system should approach an equation of state with second quantum virial coefficient. The modifications of QMD and BUU codes are discussed and comparison are presented with resent data. We find a pre-equilibrium production of particles leading to higher energetic distributions of protons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that incorporating nonlocal spatial and time shifts into the collision term of BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) and QMD (Quantum Molecular Dynamics) simulations, derived from the phase shifts of the scattering T-matrix, reproduces the correct asymptotic trajectories of two-particle scattering and is necessary for the system to approach an equation of state with the second quantum virial coefficient, with the hedge that the selfconsistent evaluation of all shifts for all collisions is too demanding and requires additional approximations.

## Method

The authors derive a nonlocal kinetic equation (NKE) from quantum statistics, where the nonlocal shifts (spatial displacement Δ, time delay Δ_t, energy shift Δ_E, and momentum shift Δ_K) are given as derivatives of the total phase shift of the scattering T-matrix. They then implement an "instant approximation" that replaces the non-instant scattering event with a sudden jump at the point of closest approach, using effective final coordinates and momenta that asymptotically match the correct trajectories, and incorporate these shifts into a QMD code for heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Central collision of 129Xe → 119Sn at 50 MeV/A was simulated.
- The nonlocal corrections bring the proton spectrum towards experimental values from INDRA experiments, while leaving cluster spectra (Figs. b–f) almost unchanged.
- The number of collisions per time in the BUU simulation is enhanced at the pre-equilibrium stage (early times) when nonlocal corrections are included, compared to the local approximation.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical efforts to correct the inherent contradiction in the Boltzmann equation, that a collision with finite diameter is treated as instantaneous and local in space, by building on the Enskog equation for hard sphere gases (Enskog, via Chapman and Cowling 1990) and prior nuclear matter treatments by Malfliet (1983) and Halpert (1981), as well as resonance scattering work by Pratt and Danielewicz (1996). The authors' own prior derivations of the nonlocal kinetic equation (Morawetz et al. 1996, Lipavsky et al. 1997) provide the first-principles foundation. The results enable improved simulations of heavy ion reactions that better reproduce experimental proton spectra and may play a vital role in extracting the nuclear equation of state, with the nonlocal corrections being easily incorporated into existing BUU and QMD codes without increasing computational time.
