# Nonmesonic Weak Decay of $Λ$ Hypernuclei within a Nuclear Matter Formalism

> The nonmesonic weak decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei using nonrelativistic nuclear matter is studied. As the basic building block we use the Polarization Propagator Method developed by Oset and Salcedo. It is shown that the exact calculation of exchange terms is required. Using the Local Density Approximation we evaluate the nonmesonic decay width for $^{12}_ΛC$ and compare the result with a finite nucleus calculation, obtaining a qualitative agreement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that exact calculation of exchange terms in the nonmesonic weak decay of \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei within a nuclear matter formalism is required, and that approximate treatments of these terms (such as those used in prior work by Jido, Oset and Palomar (2001)) lead to significantly different results for the neutron-to-proton decay ratio \(\Gamma_{n/p}\).

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic nuclear matter formalism based on the Polarization Propagator Method (PPM) originally developed by Oset and Salcedo (1985). They construct a full one-meson exchange (OME) transition potential including the pseudoscalar and vector meson octet (\(\pi, \eta, K, \rho, \omega, K^*\)) with short-range correlations, and evaluate both direct and exchange contributions to the nonmesonic decay width. The Local Density Approximation (LDA) is used to obtain results for the finite hypernucleus \(^{12}_{\Lambda}C\), with the \(\Lambda\) hyperon taken in the \(1s_{1/2}\) harmonic oscillator orbit.

## Key numbers

* The weak coupling constant is \(G_F m_{\pi}^2 = 2.21 \times 10^{-7}\), with parity violating coupling \(A_{\pi}=1.05\) and parity conserving coupling \(B_{\pi}=-7.15\) for the \(\Lambda N \pi\) vertex.
* The strong coupling constant for the \(NN\pi\) vertex is \(g_{NN \pi}=13.3\), and the monopole form factor cutoff for the pion is \(\Lambda_{\pi}=1.3\) GeV.
* For the \(\rho\)-meson, the vector and tensor strong couplings are \(g_{NN \rho}^V=3.16\) and \(g_{NN \rho}^T=13.3\), with cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rho}=1.4\) GeV.
* The short-range correlation parameter used is \(q_c = 780\) MeV.
* The experimental ratio for the neutron-to-proton decay is \(\Gamma_{n/p}^{exp}= 1.17^{+0.09+0.22}_{-0.08-0.18}\) from Hasegawa (2002), while the total nonmesonic decay width is \(\Gamma_{NM}^{exp}\sim\Gamma_0\) from Moine (1974).

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter approach to nonmesonic weak decay of \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei by explicitly including exact exchange terms, which were either neglected or approximated in prior nuclear matter studies such as those by Oset and Salcedo (1985) and Jido, Oset and Palomar (2001). The results are compared with finite nucleus calculations by Barbero et al. (2002) and Parreno et al. (1997), showing qualitative agreement for most mesons except \(\omega\) and \(K^*\). The formalism is intended to enable future studies of RPA correlations and the two-nucleon stimulated decay process \(\Lambda NN \rightarrow NNN\), which are identified as missing ingredients needed to resolve the persistent discrepancy between theory and experiment for the \(\Gamma_{n/p}\) ratio.
