# Nucleon Polarizability Contribution to the Hydrogen Lamb Shift and Hydrogen -- Deuterium Isotope Shift

> The correction to the hydrogen Lamb shift due to the proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities is expressed analytically through their static values, which are known from experiment. The numerical value of the correction to the hydrogen 1S state is $ - 71 \pm 11 \pm 7$ Hz. Correction to the H-D 1S-2S -- isotope shift due to the proton and neutron polarizabilities is estimated as $ 53 \pm 9 \pm 11$ Hz.

# Nucleon Polarizability Contribution to the Hydrogen Lamb Shift and Hydrogen – Deuterium Isotope Shift

## Key claim
The correction to the hydrogen Lamb shift from proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities is expressed analytically using only their static values, within a logarithmic approximation where the typical proton excitation energy (~300 MeV) makes the logarithm ln(Ē/mₑ) genuinely large (about 6–7). The numerical result for the 1S state is −71 ± 11 ± 7 Hz, but the authors note this correction is too small to be detected experimentally.

## Method
The authors follow the approach of Khriplovich (prior work), using the gauge A₀ = 0 for virtual photons and computing the electron-proton forward scattering amplitude. They perform a Wick rotation, transform the integral over Euclidean ω, and integrate with logarithmic accuracy, taking the polarizabilities at ω = 0 and k² = 0. The resulting effective operator is a contact interaction proportional to [5α(0) − β(0)] ln(Ē/mₑ) δ(r). For the hydrogen–deuterium isotope shift, they assume deuteron polarizability is the sum of relative-motion and internal nucleon polarizabilities, with proton contributions canceling, leaving only the neutron contribution.

## Key numbers
- Proton polarizability sum: αₚ(0) + βₚ(0) = (14.2 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton polarizability difference: αₚ(0) − βₚ(0) = (10.0 ± 1.5 ± 0.9) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Combined coefficient: 5αₚ(0) − βₚ(0) = (58.4 ± 5.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton polarizability correction to hydrogen 1S Lamb shift: −71 ± 11 ± 7 Hz (first error: 15% logarithmic approximation; second: polarizability uncertainties)
- Neutron electric polarizability: αₙ(0) = (9.8 +1.9/−2.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron contribution to ground-state Lamb shift difference (H–D): 61 ± 10 ± 12 Hz
- Nucleon polarizability contribution to H–D 1S-2S isotope shift: 53 ± 9 ± 11 Hz

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of deuteron polarizability contributions to the deuterium Lamb shift by Pachucki (prior work) and Friar (prior work), and agrees numerically with a result by Starr (prior work) for hydrogen. The authors note their formula differs from an earlier result by Ericson (prior work) by the inclusion of the magnetic polarizability term, and from Pachucki (prior work) by a factor of 5/2 for the electric term and the inclusion of β. The hydrogen correction is too small for detection, but the H–D isotope shift correction (53 ± 9 ± 11 Hz) is comparable to the experimental accuracy of 150 Hz and to the theoretical precision of 70 Hz for the deuteron relative-motion polarizability contribution from Friar (prior work). The result enables improved comparison between theory and experiment for the hydrogen–deuterium 1S-2S isotope shift.
