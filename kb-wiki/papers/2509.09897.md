# Gluon splitting rates in an anisotropic plasma in the AMY formalism

> We introduce a novel numerical method to obtain the gluon splitting rates in an anisotropic QCD plasma in the AMY formalism, suitable for an anisotropic collision kernel. The method extends previous works by decomposing the additional angular information into Fourier modes, resulting in a significantly larger system of differential equations to solve numerically. It is then tested by calculating the rates for a simple anisotropic model for the collision kernel, which is compared to a thermal system. Remarkably, the obtained rates can be well-approximated by the rates calculated from an angular-averaged collision kernel, while still deviating significantly from equilibrium. An isotropic model for the collision kernel that is commonly used in QCD kinetic theory simulations, and which relies on the infrared temperature $T_*$ and an effective Debye mass, leads to rates that significantly deviate from the nonequilibrium rate, particularly at smaller parton energies.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a numerical method to compute gluon splitting rates in an anisotropic QCD plasma within the AMY formalism, but the method is tested only on a simple anisotropic model for the collision kernel where the Debye mass acquires an angular dependence; the obtained rates can be well-approximated by rates calculated from an angular-averaged collision kernel, while still deviating significantly from equilibrium.

## Method

The integral equation for the gluon splitting rate in impact parameter space is decomposed into Fourier modes in the angular coordinate, yielding a coupled system of second-order differential equations for the Fourier coefficients. The system is solved by integrating from small to large impact parameter using a Runge-Kutta method, with boundary conditions imposed at small b from the analytic vacuum solution and at large b by requiring regularity. The method is tested on an anisotropic collision kernel model where the Debye mass depends on the azimuthal angle as \(m_D^2(\phi) = (1 - 2\xi/3 + \xi\cos^2\phi)\bar m_D^2\), with the anisotropy parameter \(\xi\) characterizing a squeezed thermal distribution.

## Key numbers

- For the anisotropic model with \(\xi = 0.5, 1.0, 1.35\), the rate obtained from the angular-averaged kernel differs from the full anisotropic rate by less than \(0.5\%\) for all considered parton energies and splitting fractions \(z = 0.1\) and \(z = 0.5\).
- The isotropic approximation using the infrared temperature \(T_*\) and effective Debye mass \(m_D\) deviates from the nonequilibrium rate by more than \(10\%\), with the largest deviations at smaller parton energies around the scale \(T\).
- The numerical method uses a coupling value of \(g = 0.1\) for all calculations.
- The Fourier decomposition requires \(n_{\mathrm{fourier}} = 7\) (corresponding to \(n_{\mathrm{max}} = 3\)) to obtain sufficient accuracy for the parameter range considered.

## Context

This work extends the numerical approach of Aurenche (2002) and Moore (2021) for solving the AMY splitting rate equation from isotropic to anisotropic collision kernels, and generalizes the perturbative treatment of small anisotropies by Hauksson (2023). The method enables calculation of gluon splitting rates for arbitrary anisotropic collision kernels, and a companion paper by Altenburger (2025) applies it to kernels obtained from QCD kinetic theory simulations of the initial stages in heavy-ion collisions. The results question the accuracy of the isotropic approximation using \(T_*\) and \(m_D\) that is commonly used in QCD kinetic theory simulations by Kurkela (2014-2018) and others.
