# Effect of isospin dependent cluster recognition on the observables in heavy ion collisions

> We introduce isospin dependence in the cluster recognition algorithms used in the Quantum Molecular Dynamics model to describe fragment formation in heavy ion collisions. This change reduces the yields of emitted nucleons and enhances the yields of fragments, especially heavier fragments. The enhancement of neutron-rich lighter fragments mainly occurs at mid-rapidity. Consequently, isospin dependent observables, such as isotope distributions, yield ratios of $n/p$, $t/^3He$, and isoscaling parameters are affected. We also investigate how equilibration in heavy ion collisions is affected by this change.

## Key claim

Introducing isospin-dependent cluster recognition in the Quantum Molecular Dynamics model by using different spatial coalescence radii for proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton pairs changes fragment yields and isospin-sensitive observables, but the effects are relatively small and only partially close the gap between simulations and experimental data.

## Method

The authors modified the minimum spanning tree (MST) cluster recognition algorithm in the ImQMD05 transport code by assigning different coalescence radii: \(R_0^{nn}=R_0^{np}=6\) fm and \(R_0^{pp}=3\) fm, instead of the standard uniform value \(R_0=3.5\) fm. They simulated central collisions of \(^{124}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) and \(^{112}\mathrm{Sn}+^{112}\mathrm{Sn}\) at \(E/A=50\) MeV with impact parameter \(b=2\) fm, using the asy-soft symmetry energy (\(\gamma_i=0.5\)) and isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. They compared fragment charge distributions, rapidity distributions of neutrons, protons, tritons, and \(^3\mathrm{He}\), isotope yields, double ratios, isoscaling parameters, and the equilibration observable \(\sigma_{\mathrm{trans}}^2/\sigma_{\mathrm{long}}^2\) between the standard MST and the isospin-dependent iso-MST methods.

## Key numbers

- Z=1 particle multiplicities reduced by about 16% when using iso-MST compared to MST for \(^{124}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) at \(E/A=50\) MeV, \(b=2\) fm.
- Fragments with \(Z>12\) are strongly enhanced; fragments with \(Z=2-12\) show multiplicities larger by about 3%.
- \(R(n/p)\) for \(^{124}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) with kinetic energy \(E_k<40\) MeV: \(2.131\pm0.005\) (MST) vs. \(2.302\pm0.006\) (iso-MST).
- \(DR(n/p)\) for \(E_k<40\) MeV: \(1.514\pm0.005\) (MST) vs. \(1.641\pm0.006\) (iso-MST), an increase of about 8.3%.
- \(R(t/^3\mathrm{He})\) for \(^{124}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\): \(3.031\pm0.026\) (MST) vs. \(3.497\pm0.028\) (iso-MST).
- \(DR(t/^3\mathrm{He})\) for \(E_k/A<40\) MeV: \(1.57\pm0.02\) (MST) vs. \(1.64\pm0.02\) (iso-MST).
- Isoscaling parameter \(\alpha\) for fragments with \(Z=3-8\): 0.19 (MST) vs. 0.22 (iso-MST); experimental value is \(0.36\pm0.04\) from Tsang (2001).
- Equilibration observable \(\sigma_{\mathrm{trans}}^2/\sigma_{\mathrm{long}}^2\) for \(Z=1-6\) particles: 0.58 (MST) vs. 0.62 (iso-MST).
- \(Y(t)/Y(^3\mathrm{He})\) at mid-rapidity reaches about 4.0 with iso-MST.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to improve cluster recognition in QMD models, such as the early cluster recognition algorithm (ECRA) by Dorso (1993), the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA) by Goss (1997) and Puri (2000), and the MST with binding energy (MSTB) by Goyal (2011), none of which addressed isospin dependence. The results show that including isospin-dependent coalescence radii, motivated by neutron skin and halo effects and the Coulomb repulsion between protons, moves simulated observables (e.g., isoscaling parameter \(\alpha\), double ratios, and stopping power) toward better agreement with experimental data from Tsang (2001) and Kohley (2011). The authors note that sequential decay calculations, which are model-dependent as discussed by Tsang (2006) and Maria (2008), may further modify the results, and that the iso-MST method should be included in transport models to improve comparisons with data.
