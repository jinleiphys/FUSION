# Nuclear moments for the neutrinoless double beta decay II

> The recently developed formalism for the evaluation of nuclear form factors in neutrinoless double beta decay is applied to $^{48}Ca$, $^{76}Ge$, $^{82}Se$, $^{100}Mo$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$ nuclei. Explicit analytical expressions that follows from this theoretical development, in the single mode model for the decay of $^{48}Ca$, have been worked out. They are useful both for testing the full numerical calculations, and for analytically checking the consistency with other formalisms. Large configuration space calculations are compared with previous studies, where alternative formulations were used. Yet, besides using the G-matrix as residual interaction, we here use a simple $δ$-force. Attention is paid to the connected effects of the short range nuclear correlations and the finite nucleon size. Constraints on lepton number violating terms in the weak Hamiltonian (effective neutrino Majorana mass and effective right-handed current coupling strengths) are deduced.

## Key claim

The paper applies a recently developed Fourier-Bessel multipole expansion formalism for neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements to six candidate nuclei, finding that while the overall formalism is equivalent to previous approaches, the numerical results for the recoil and tensor matrix elements show significant discrepancies with earlier calculations by Pantis and Vergados (1990) and Muto, Bender and Klapdor (1989), particularly for the recoil matrix element M_R where the combined effects of finite nucleon size and short-range correlations produce values that are not simply the product of individual corrections.

## Method

The authors performed large configuration space QRPA calculations using a delta-force residual interaction (rather than the G-matrix used in prior work) for six nuclei: Ca-48, Ge-76, Se-82, Mo-100, Te-128, and Te-130. For Ca-48, they also carried out single-mode model calculations with harmonic oscillator wave functions and compared analytical expressions with shell model results from Pantis and Vergados (1990). Short-range correlations were included via a correlation function f_SRC(r)=1-j_0(k_c r) with k_c=3.93 fm^{-1}, and finite nucleon size effects were introduced through dipole form factors with Lambda=850 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For Ca-48 in the single-mode model with FNS+SRC: M_GT = -0.947, M_F = 0.130, M_R = -0.796, M_T = 0.309 (bare axial vector coupling g_A=1.254)
* For Ge-76 in QRPA with FNS+SRC: M_GT = -2.845, M_F = 0.749, M_R = -4.863, M_T = -0.065

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism development (Barbero et al., 1998) for neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, applying it to full numerical calculations for the first time beyond the neutrino mass term. The results are compared with two prior QRPA evaluations: Muto, Bender and Klapdor (1989) using the formalism of Doi, Kotani and Takasugi (1985), and Pantis, Simkovic, Vergados and Faessler (1996) using the formalism of Vergados (1990). The paper enables extraction of constraints on lepton number violating parameters (effective Majorana mass and right-handed current couplings) from experimental half-life limits, and provides analytical expressions for Ca-48 that can test consistency between different theoretical formulations.
