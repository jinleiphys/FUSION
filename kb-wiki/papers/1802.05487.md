# Shannon entropy and particle decays

> We deploy Shannon's information entropy to the distribution of branching fractions in a particle decay. This serves to quantify how important a given new reported decay channel is, from the point of view of the information that it adds to the already known ones. Because the entropy is additive, one can subdivide the set of channels and discuss, for example, how much information the discovery of a new decay branching would add; or subdivide the decay distribution down to the level of individual quantum states (which can be quickly counted by the phase space). We illustrate the concept with some examples of experimentally known particle decay distributions.

## Key claim

Shannon entropy applied to particle decay branching fractions provides a quantitative measure of how much information a newly discovered decay channel adds to the already known ones, but the entropy saturates when additional channels have small branching fractions, making their contribution negligible.

## Method

The authors compute the Shannon entropy S = -Σ BR_j log BR_j for experimentally known meson and gauge boson decays using branching fraction data from the Particle Data Group (Patrignani 2016). They examine how entropy changes as decay channels are added sequentially in order of decreasing branching fraction, and they propose two derived quantities (normalized entropy increment and degree of likeness) to quantify the information value of discovering a new channel. They also explore entropy additivity by comparing primary decay chains with secondary decays that include subsequent decays of intermediate particles.

## Key numbers

- The maximum entropy for a decay distribution with N channels is log N, reached when all branching fractions equal 1/N
- The minimum entropy is 0, reached when one channel dominates with BR_1 ≈ 1
- For the f_2(1270) meson, the ππ decay channel dominates at 85% branching fraction
- For the Z boson, channels 5, 6, and 7 contribute less to the normalized entropy increment than channels 1 through 4
- The entropy of charmonia (J/ψ and χ_c1) grows approximately linearly with the number of channels when many channels have commensurable branching fractions, before showing the characteristic log N behavior

## Context

This work extends the application of Shannon entropy from heavy ion collisions (Ma 2014, 2018) and parton splitting (Brogueira 1996, Cao 1996) to particle decay distributions. It builds on d'Enterria's (2012) observation that the Higgs boson sits at maximum entropy of its decay distribution, and on the maximum entropy principle proposed by Alves, Dias and da Silva (2014, 2017) for predicting axion masses. The paper enables systematic comparison of decay distributions across different particles and provides tools to quantify the information value of new decay channel discoveries, which is relevant for the Particle Data Group's ongoing compilation of decay data.
