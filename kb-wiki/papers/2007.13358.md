# Low-energy cluster vibrations in N = Z nuclei

> Significant transition strength in light $α$-conjugate nuclei at low energy, typically below 10 MeV, has been observed in many experiments. In this work the isoscalar low-energy response of N=Z nuclei is explored using the Finite Amplitude Method (FAM) based on the microscopic framework of nuclear energy density functionals. Depending on the multipolarity of the excitation and the equilibrium deformation of a particular isotope, the low-energy strength functions display prominent peaks that can be attributed to vibration of cluster structures: $α$+$^{12}$C+$α$ and $α$+$^{16}$O in $^{20}$Ne, $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C in $^{24}$Mg, 4$α$+$^{12}$C in $^{28}$Si, etc. Such cluster excitations are favored in light nuclei with large deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-energy isoscalar multipole strength in light N=Z nuclei arises from vibrations of cluster structures (such as α+¹²C+α in ²⁰Ne), but this interpretation is conditional: the prominent low-energy peaks appear only for nuclei with large equilibrium prolate deformation, and the strength is attributed to cluster vibrations based on analysis of transition densities and 2-quasiparticle configurations, not direct experimental observation.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Quasiparticle Finite Amplitude Method (QFAM) based on the DD-PC1 energy density functional to compute isoscalar multipole (λ=0,1,2,3) strength functions for N=Z nuclei from ¹²C to ⁵⁶Ni. Calculations were performed in an axially-deformed harmonic oscillator basis with N_sh=14 major shells, and the strength functions were folded with a Lorentzian of width Γ=2γ to avoid divergences near QRPA states. The analysis included deformation-constrained calculations varying the axial quadrupole deformation β₂, and decomposition of excitation modes into 2-quasiparticle contributions using contour integration in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium deformation of ²⁰Ne is β₂ ≈ 0.5 (prolate), with ground-state density peaks of ≈ 0.2 fm⁻³ at the outer ends of the symmetry axis
- Low-energy cluster vibration peaks appear at ℏω ≈ 6.75 MeV for the monopole mode and ℏω ≈ 7.65 MeV for the octupole mode (K=0 component) in ²⁰Ne
- The centroids of the monopole strength function in ²⁰Ne converge to E_low = 18.1 MeV and E_high = 27.3 MeV for N_sh=14 (calculated in energy intervals 10-22.5 MeV and 22.5-35 MeV respectively)
- Significant low-energy monopole strength begins to appear at β₂ ∼ 0.2 in ²⁰Ne, with the leading 2-qp contribution increasing from 25% to more than 40% of total strength between β₂=0.4 and β₂=0.5
- In ²⁴Mg, two low-energy monopole peaks are obtained at ∼ 7 MeV and ∼ 10 MeV, with the higher mode at ℏω = 10.03 MeV corresponding to ¹²C+¹²C cluster oscillations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cluster excitations in light α-conjugate nuclei that used cluster models (Tomoda 1978, Uegaki 1979) or Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics combined with Generator Coordinate Method (Kimura 2004, Chiba 2015, Kanada-En'yo 2016). It provides a microscopic description using relativistic energy density functionals, complementing earlier multi-reference GCM calculations based on the same functionals (Marević 2018, 2019). The QFAM approach enables systematic calculation of strength functions across multiple nuclei and deformations, and the results enable future comparisons with experimental data on low-energy E0, E1, E2, and E3 strength in light N=Z nuclei.
