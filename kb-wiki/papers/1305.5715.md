# Polarization observables in low-energy antiproton-proton scattering

> We investigate the polarization parameters in low-energy antiproton-proton elastic ($\bar{p}p\rightarrow\bar{p}p$) and charge-exchange ($\bar{p}p\rightarrow\bar{n}n$) scattering. The predictions for unmeasured observables are based on our new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/$c$ antiproton laboratory momentum, which gives an optimal description of the existing database. Sizable and remarkable spin effects are observed, in particular for charge-exchange scattering. These result from the spin dependence of the long- and medium-range one- and two-pion exchange antinucleon-nucleon potential and the state dependence of the parametrized short-range interaction. We study the possibility of polarizing a circulating antiproton beam with a polarized proton target by exploiting the spin dependence of the $\bar{p}p$ total cross section. It appears feasible to achieve a significant transverse polarization of an antiproton beam within a reasonable time.

## Key claim

The authors predict that a significant transverse polarization of a circulating antiproton beam can be achieved within a reasonable time by exploiting the spin dependence of the antiproton-proton total cross section via a polarized proton target, based on their new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors performed an energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of all antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c antiproton laboratory momentum, using one-pion and two-pion exchange interactions derived from the effective chiral Lagrangian of QCD for the long-range part and a complex, energy-dependent boundary condition at r=1.2 fm for the short-range part. They then used the resulting scattering amplitudes to calculate predictions for unmeasured spin observables in elastic and charge-exchange scattering, and applied the formalism of spin filtering to estimate the polarization buildup of an antiproton beam circulating through a polarized proton target.

## Key numbers

- The PWA describes the existing antiproton-proton database of 3749 scattering data with chi-squared_min/N_df = 1.048, where N_df = 3578 is the number of degrees of freedom.
- For a target with transverse polarization, the maximal beam polarization is around -15%, depending on acceptance angle; at momenta around 350 MeV/c, the polarization can reach about -20% for acceptance angle theta_acc_lab = 30 mrad.
- For a target with longitudinal polarization, the maximal beam polarization is around -2% at low momenta and 5% at higher momenta; at momenta around 1000 MeV/c, polarizations are between 5% and 6% for acceptance angles between 5 and 30 mrad.
- The optimal time t_0 for polarization buildup is of the order of several tens of hours for momenta from 100 to 1000 MeV/c and acceptance angles of 5, 10, 20, and 30 mrad, assuming target areal density n=10^14 cm^-2 and revolution frequency f=10^6 s^-1.
- The statistical uncertainty in the predictions for the polarizations P_perp and P_parallel is less than 2% in the momentum range considered.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous partial-wave analysis (Zhou and Timmermans, 2012) of antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c to provide predictions for unmeasured spin observables, similar in spirit to the qualitative discussion of spin observables by Dover and collaborators (1982) using a simple optical-potential model before LEAR came into operation. The results enable future experiments to test these predictions and potentially improve the PWA solution, and they support the feasibility of producing a polarized antiproton beam via spin filtering, as previously demonstrated for protons by the FILTEX experiment at the Test Storage Ring in Heidelberg (Rathmann et al., 1993) and confirmed by the PAX Collaboration at COSY (Augustyniak et al., 2012).
