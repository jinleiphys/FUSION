# Quantum entropy and QCD factorization for low-$Q^2$ $ν$DIS

> Deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) is an essential process for exploring the structure of visible matter and testing the standard model. At the same time, the theoretical interpretation of DIS measurements depends on QCD factorization theorems whose validity deteriorates at the lower values of $Q^2$ and $W^2$ typical of neutrino DIS in accelerator-based oscillation searches. For this reason, progress in understanding the origin and limits of QCD factorization is invaluable to the accuracy and precision of predictions for these upcoming neutrino experiments. In these short proceedings, we introduce a novel approach based on the quantum entropy associated with continuous distributions in QCD, using it to characterize the limits of factorization theorems relevant for the description of neutrino DIS. This work suggests an additional avenue for dissecting factorization-breaking dynamics through the quantum entropy, which could also play a role in quantum simulations of related systems.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel approach based on quantum entropy (differential entropy) associated with continuous distributions in QCD to characterize the limits of factorization theorems for neutrino DIS, particularly at the lower values of \(Q^2\) and \(W^2\) where factorization validity deteriorates. The authors suggest that factorization-breaking effects imprint themselves on the entropy, but present this as a preview demonstration within a simplified spectator model, with a longer study to appear soon.

## Method

The authors use the spectator model of Moffat et al. (2017), which allows DIS structure functions to be computed both exactly (including factorization-breaking effects) and under the explicit assumption of QCD factorization. They compute \(k_T\)-dependent structure functions \(f_1(x, k_T, Q^2)\) and define a differential entropy \(H_{F_1}(x,Q^2)\) and its \(k_T\)-dependent integrand \(\mathcal{H}_{F_1}(x, k_T, Q^2)\) from these distributions. The entropy is evaluated at kinematical points relevant to neutrino scattering, specifically at the threshold of the soft DIS region, and the exact and factorized calculations are compared via ratios.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed at \(Q = 2\) GeV and \(x = 0.6\), corresponding to \((Q, W) = (2, 1.9)\) GeV.
* The constituent quark mass \(m_q\) is taken as 0.3 or 0.5 GeV, and the scalar spectator mass \(m_s\) is taken as 0.67 to 0.75 GeV.
* The virtuality is set to \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) for the results shown in Fig. 2.
* The ratio of the \(k_T\)-integrated differential entropy (exact over factorized) begins to diverge as \(x_{Bj}\) gets larger, signifying growing factorization-breaking effects as \(W\) decreases toward the shallow-inelastic scattering region.

## Context

This work extends the spectator model approach of Moffat et al. (2017), which formulated the interacting nucleon as a struck quark and recoiling spectator diquark, to study factorization-breaking effects in DIS. It builds on prior work by Hagiwara et al. (2018) that formulated entropies of entanglement from quantities related to the multi-dimensional wave function of the proton, including the Wehrl entropy assessed on the Wigner function. The results suggest a connection between quantum mechanical entropies and factorization-breaking dynamics, which could enable new insights from quantum simulations of QCD bound states as discussed by Khor et al. (2023).
