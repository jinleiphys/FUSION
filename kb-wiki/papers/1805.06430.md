# New Perspective on Hybrid Mesons

> It is thought that strong interactions within the Standard Model can generate bound-states in which non-Abelian gauge-bosons play a dual role, serving both as force and matter fields. In this context we introduce a novel approach to the hybrid-meson (valence-gluon+quark+antiquark) bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory. Exploiting the existence of strong two-body correlations in the gluon-quark, $q_g=[gq]$, and gluon-antiquark, $\bar q_g=[g\bar q]$ channels, we argue that a sound description of hybrid properties can be obtained by solving a coupled-pair of effectively two-body equations; and, consequently, that hybrids may be viewed as highly-correlated $q_g \bar q \leftrightarrow q \bar q_g$ bound-states. Analogies may be drawn between this picture of hybrid structure and that of baryons, in which quark+quark (diquark) correlations play a key role. The potential of this formulation is illustrated by calculating the spectrum of light-quark isovector hybrid mesons.

## Key claim

The authors propose that hybrid mesons (valence-gluon+quark+antiquark bound states) can be described by solving a coupled pair of effectively two-body equations, treating them as highly-correlated $q_g \bar q \leftrightarrow q \bar q_g$ bound states, analogous to how diquark correlations simplify the baryon three-body problem. The approach reproduces the lattice-QCD hybrid spectrum only after enhancing the anomalous chromomagnetic moment of the gluon-quark correlation beyond what the leading-order rainbow-ladder truncation provides.

## Method

The authors formulate a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the $gq\bar q$ three-body system, assuming strong two-body correlations exist in the gluon-quark ($[gq]$) and gluon-antiquark ($[g\bar q]$) channels. They first solve a homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $[gq]$ correlation amplitude in rainbow-ladder truncation, then use these amplitudes together with a simplified propagator ansatz for the $[gq]$ correlation to solve the resulting effectively two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation for hybrid meson masses. The kernel is completed with dressed quark and gluon propagators determined from fits to $\pi$- and $\rho$-meson observables.

## Key numbers

- $[gq]$ correlation mass: $m_{gq} = 1.0$ GeV (with $\pm 10\%$ variations in $\omega_{3g}$ and $m_g$ giving $m_{gq} \to (1 \pm 0.1) m_{gq}$)
- Rainbow-ladder (RL) direct hybrid masses (GeV): $0^{-+}$: 1.28(9), $1^{-+}$: 1.80(4), $1^{--}$: 1.64(10), $0^{+-}$: 1.73(13), $0^{--}$: 1.74(3)
- Anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM) improved hybrid masses (GeV): $0^{-+}$: 1.62(6), $1^{-+}$: 1.75(8), $1^{--}$: 1.86(10), $0^{+-}$: 1.87(14), $0^{--}$: 1.90(3)
- Rescaled lattice QCD results (20$^3$ volume, GeV): $0^{-+}$: 1.69(2), $1^{-+}$: 1.72(2), $1^{--}$: 1.77(6), $0^{+-}$: 1.99(2)
- Parameters used for ACM improvement: $\kappa_{gq} = 2.4$, $\mathpzc{s}_V = (0.70\ \text{GeV})^2$, $\mathpzc{s}_S = (0.63\ \text{GeV})^2$
- Infrared gluon mass scale: $m_g = 0.6$ GeV; dressed light-quark infrared mass-scale: $M \simeq 0.35$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the diquark-correlation approach to baryons (Cahill 1988, Maris 2002, Maris 2004) to the hybrid meson three-body problem, providing a continuum alternative to lattice QCD calculations of hybrid spectra (Dudek 2010, Dudek 2011). The approach corrects defects of earlier two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation studies of exotic mesons (Qin 2011, Fischer 2014, Hilger 2016) which produced unrealistically light exotic states. The formulation enables future predictions of hybrid decay properties and other processes to guide experimental searches for hybrid candidates below 2 GeV.
