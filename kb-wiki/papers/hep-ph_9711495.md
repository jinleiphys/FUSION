# Gluonia in QCD with massless quarks

> I briefly review the estimate of the gluonia masses, decay and mixings in QCD with massless quarks from QCD spectral sum rules and some low-energy theorems. The data in the $0^{++}$ channel can be explained with some maximal gluonium-quarkonium mixing schemes, which then suggest a large violation of the OZI rule, similar to the case of the $η'$ in the $U(1)_A$ sector.

# Gluonia in QCD with massless quarks

## Key claim

QCD spectral sum rules and low-energy theorems can provide a plausible explanation of the complex gluonia spectra, but the consistency of different sum rules in the scalar sector requires the existence of an additional lower-mass, broad σ_B meson that is strongly coupled both to gluons and to pairs of Goldstone bosons, which can be missed in a one-resonance parametrization or in lattice quenched approximations.

## Method

The author applies QCD spectral sum rules (SVZ Laplace sum rules) to two-point correlators built from gauge-invariant two-gluon currents with quantum numbers 0^{++}, 2^{++}, and 0^{-+}, including non-perturbative contributions from the dimension-four gluonic condensate in the Wilson expansion. The sum rules are analyzed using a duality ansatz parametrization of the spectral function, with stability criteria in the continuum threshold t_c and sum rule variable τ, and the results are combined with low-energy theorems and vertex sum rules (Goldberger-Treiman-like relations) to estimate hadronic widths and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- Gluonic condensate value: ⟨α_s G²⟩ ≈ (0.07 ± 0.01) GeV⁴, estimated from e⁺e⁻ data and heavy-quark mass splittings
- QCD scale for three active flavours: Λ = (375 ± 125) MeV
- Unmixed scalar gluonium G mass: 1.5 ± 0.2 GeV (upper bound 2.16 ± 0.22 GeV), with decay constant f_G = 390 ± 145 MeV
- Unmixed tensor gluonium T mass: 2.0 ± 0.1 GeV (upper bound 2.7 ± 0.4 GeV), with f_T = 80 ± 14 MeV
- Unmixed pseudoscalar gluonium P mass: 2.05 ± 0.19 GeV (upper bound 2.34 ± 0.42 GeV), with f_P = 8–17 MeV
- Scalar gluonium G decay width into ηη: 1–2 MeV; into ηη': 5–10 MeV; into (4π)_S: 60–138 MeV; γγ width: 0.2–1.8 keV
- σ_B meson (broad, input mass 0.75–1.0 GeV) π⁺π⁻ width: 0.2–0.5 GeV; γγ width: 0.2–0.3 keV
- Maximal mixing angle between σ_B and S₂ (light quarkonium): θ_S ≈ (40–45)°
- f_0(0.98) γγ width: ≈ 0.3 keV (experimental)
- f_J(1.71) K⁺K⁻ width: 112 ± 50 MeV; γγ width: 1.1 ± 0.5 keV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCD spectral sum rule analyses (Narison, 1996, 1997) and addresses the long-standing problem of gluonium (glueball) identification in the scalar, tensor, and pseudoscalar channels. The results suggest a large violation of the OZI rule in the scalar sector, analogous to the η' case in the U(1)_A sector, and provide a framework for interpreting experimental candidates such as the G(1.5–1.6) from GAMS, the f_0(0.98), f_0(1.37), f_0(1.5), and f_J(1.71). The mixing schemes proposed enable future experimental searches for radial excitations in the 2.0–2.2 GeV region and further tests of the gluonium-quarkonium mixing hypothesis.
