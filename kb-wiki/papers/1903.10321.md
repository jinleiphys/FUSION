# Interwoven limit cycles in the spectra of mass imbalanced many-boson system

> The independence between few-body scales beyond the van der Waals universality is demonstrated for the extreme mass-imbalanced case of a specific many-boson system. This finding generalizes the scaling properties of universal tetramers to a broader class of heterogeneous few-boson systems. We assume two heavy atoms interacting with $(N-2)-$lighter ones at the unitary limit, using a particular case where no interactions are active between identical particles, by investigating the interwoven spectra of this many-body system for an arbitrary number of light bosons. A large mass-ratio between the particles allows us to treat this $N-$body system analytically, by solving an effective inverse-squared long-range interaction which is stablished for the two heavy bosons. For a cluster with $N-2$ light bosons ($N\ge 4$), we discuss the implications of the corresponding long-range potentials associated with different subsystem thresholds, implying in independent interwoven limit cycles for the correlation between the energies of excited $N-$body system. Our study with extreme mass-imbalanced few-boson bound states provides a fundamental understanding of the scaling behavior of their interwoven spectra. The novel insights enlarge the well-known Efimov physics paradigm and show the existence of different limit cycles, which could be probed by new experiments.

# Interwoven limit cycles in the spectra of mass imbalanced many-boson system

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for an extreme mass-imbalanced many-boson system (two heavy bosons interacting with N-2 light bosons at the unitary limit, with no interactions between identical particles), independent few-body scales exist beyond the van der Waals universality, generalizing the scaling properties of universal tetramers to a broader class of heterogeneous few-boson systems. The authors show that the interwoven limit cycles for the N-body system emerge from different subsystem thresholds, but this result is derived within the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and is strictly valid only for large mass ratios where the heavy particles move much slower than the light ones.

## Method

The authors extend the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) adiabatic approach, previously applied to two-heavy and one-light particle systems, to an N-body system with two identical heavy bosons and (N-2) identical light bosons (N >= 4), where only heavy-light interactions are nonzero. By solving the effective inverse-squared long-range interaction that emerges for the two heavy bosons in the unitary limit (scattering length a -> infinity), they obtain the bound-state spectrum from modified Bessel functions of the second kind with pure imaginary order i s_N, where s_N is the scaling factor for the N-body system. The short-range cutoff for the N-body system is associated with the size of the (N-1)-body subsystem, providing a physically motivated boundary condition.

## Key numbers

* For mass ratio A = 0.01 (light-to-heavy mass ratio), the three-body energy ratio between successive states is e^(2 pi / s_0) = 4.70, while the four-body energy ratio is e^(2 pi / s_4) = 2.97 (using non-adiabatic three-body scaling factor s_0 = 4.061).
* For mass ratio A = 0.04, the three-body energy ratio is 19.5, the four-body ratio is 7.95, the five-body ratio is 5.39, and the six-body ratio is 4.29.
* For mass ratio A = 0.001, the three-body energy ratio is 1.64, the four-body ratio is 1.42, the five-body ratio is 1.33, and the six-body ratio is 1.28.
* The adiabatic scaling factor s_3 for A = 0.01 is 3.9891, compared to the exact non-adiabatic value s_0 = 4.0612; for A = 0.001, s_3 = 12.675 compared to s_0 = 12.698.
* The constant gamma in the effective potential is gamma = 0.5671433, defined by gamma = e^(-gamma).
* The fitting constant epsilon for the kappa(R) approximation is epsilon = 0.185.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Hadizadeh et al. (2011) on universal four-boson correlations and the existence of a four-body scale beyond the Efimov three-body parameter, as well as the earlier work of Adhikari et al. (1995) and Yamashita et al. (2006) on additional scales in few-body systems. The paper generalizes these results to systems with more than four particles by showing that each new boson added introduces an independent short-range scale, leading to interwoven limit cycles with different geometric ratios. The results enable experimental tests using ultracold heteronuclear mixtures such as Lithium-Caesium (Li-6 and Cs-133), where narrow Feshbach resonances may allow disentangling the independent few-body scales, and support the possibility of observing multiple limit cycles in recombination experiments.
