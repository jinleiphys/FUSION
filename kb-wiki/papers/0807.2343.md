# Unified description of equation of state and transport properties of nuclear matter

> Correlated basis function perturbation theory and the formalism of cluster expansions have been recently employed to obtain an effective interaction from a state-of-the-art nucleon nucleon potential model. The approach based on the effective interaction allows for a consistent description of the nuclear matter ground state and nucleon-nucleon scattering in the nuclear medium. This paper reports the the results of numerical calculations of different properties of nuclear and neutron matter, including the equation of state and the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity transport coefficients, carried out using the effective interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective interaction derived from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential using correlated basis function perturbation theory can provide a unified description of both the equation of state and transport coefficients of nuclear and neutron matter, but the results for shear viscosity and thermal conductivity depend strongly on whether medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section are included.

## Method

The authors derived an effective interaction from the Argonne \(v_{18}\) nucleon-nucleon potential using correlated basis function perturbation theory and cluster expansion techniques, including the effects of many-nucleon forces through a density-dependent modification of the two-body potential. They computed the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using first-order perturbation theory with a Fermi gas basis, then calculated the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of pure neutron matter within Landau theory of normal Fermi liquids, using both free-space and medium-modified nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections obtained from the effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter at equilibrium density (\(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the effective mass ratio is \(m^\star(p_F)/m = 0.65\), in close agreement with the lowest order correlated basis function result of Fabrocini and Fantoni (1993)
- The in-medium neutron-neutron cross section at \(E_{cm}=100\) MeV shows appreciable suppression compared to free space, with results shown at \(\rho = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\rho = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Inclusion of medium modifications increases the shear viscosity \(\eta T^2\) by a factor of approximately 2.5 at half nuclear matter density and by a factor of approximately 10 at \(\rho = 2\rho_0\)
- The thermal conductivity \(\kappa T\) shows a difference exceeding one order of magnitude at \(\rho = 2\rho_0\) when comparing medium-modified results to those of Baiko, Haensel and Yakovlev (2001) obtained from free-space cross sections
- The correction factor between the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov approximation and the exact Brooker-Sykes solution for viscosity lies in the range \(0.750 < (\eta/\eta_{AK}) < 0.925\)

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function effective interaction approach of Shannon and Benhar (2007) by including the effects of many-nucleon forces, which become dominant at high densities relevant to neutron star studies. The results enable improved calculations of neutron star oscillation damping and cooling, as the large medium-induced increases in viscosity and thermal conductivity are expected to produce appreciable effects on gravitational wave emission and neutron star thermal evolution, with the impact on neutron star cooling currently under investigation by Fiorilla (2008).
