# Spectral Distribution Studies with Modified Kuo-Brown Interaction in the Upper Half of the fp Shell

> The spectral distribution method with modified Kuo-Brown interaction is extended to the study of the upper half as well as to odd-A nuclei, of the fp shell. The calculations show similar success to that obtained for the lower half.

# Spectral Distribution Studies with Modified Kuo-Brown Interaction in the Upper Half of the fp Shell

## Key claim

The spectral distribution method using the modified Kuo-Brown (KB3) interaction, previously successful for lighter fp-shell nuclei, is extended to the upper half of the fp-shell and to odd-A nuclei, achieving improved agreement with experimental binding energies, particularly for nuclei with A > 70, after incorporating corrections from nonzero skewness (γ₁) and excess (γ₂) via the Cornish-Fisher expansion. However, the authors note that KB3 was originally constructed to improve spectroscopy at the beginning of the fp-shell, and its success for heavier fp-shell nuclei remains uncertain in the absence of full diagonalization.

## Method

The authors apply spectral distribution theory using the KB3 interaction in the four fp-shell orbits (f₇/₂, f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂), partitioning the shell model space by configuration and isospin (m̃,T). Ground state energies are obtained via the Ratcliff procedure, integrating Gaussian configuration densities until the area equals half the ground state degeneracy, with corrections from nonzero skewness (γ₁) and excess (γ₂) applied through the Cornish-Fisher expansion. For γ₂, a phenomenological correction term γ₂ = γ₂(m) + am + bm² is used, with parameters a and b determined by best fit separately for odd-A, odd-odd, and even-even nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For odd-A nuclei, best predictions use a = 0.006 and b = -0.00029, with RMS deviation of calculated binding energies from experimental values of 1.22 MeV.
- For odd-odd nuclei, best predictions use a = 0.005 and b = -0.00028, with RMS deviation of 1.34 MeV.
- For even-even nuclei, best predictions use a = 0.007 and b = -0.00031, with RMS deviation of 1.77 MeV.
- For m = 24, centroids for T = 0 and T = 8 are -243.89 MeV and -193.21 MeV for KB3, compared to -229.80 MeV and -186.49 MeV for MHW2.
- For m = 28, centroids for T = 0 and T = 6 are -304.12 MeV and -274.55 MeV for KB3, compared to -285.25 MeV and -259.98 MeV for MHW2.
- For m = 24 and T = 0, the width for KB3 is 14.89 MeV, while for MHW2 it is 13.09 MeV.
- The correlation coefficient between KB3 and MHW2 for m = 28 varies between 0.992 and 0.989.
- Inclusion of the correlation term in Gamow-Teller sum rule strengths produces a decrease of less than 3%.

## Context

This work extends the spectral distribution method studies previously performed in the lower half of the fp-shell with the KB3 interaction by Choubey and Kar (1997) and earlier sd-shell studies by French and collaborators (1985). The results show considerable improvement over earlier spectral distribution calculations by Haq and Parikh (1985), who used the MHW2 interaction with scalar isospin moments and excited state corrections. The authors note that for nuclei near neutron number 40, excitations to the 1g₉/₂ orbit may become important, and future studies for A = 70 and beyond should include this orbit. The Gamow-Teller sum rule estimates are noted as useful for calculating electron capture and beta decay rates for supernova and presupernova evolution, as well as r- and s-process nucleosynthesis.
