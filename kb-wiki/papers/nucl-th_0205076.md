# Unitarity and the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> We investigate the relation between different three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation and the analytic structure of the resultant amplitudes in the energy plane. This correlation is studied for both the $ϕ^2σ$ interaction Lagrangian and the $πN$ system with $s$-, $u$-, and $t$-channel pole diagrams as driving terms. We observe that the equal-time equation, which includes some of the three-body unitarity cuts, gives the best agreement with the Bethe-Salpeter result. This is followed by other 3-D approximations that have less of the analytic structure.

## Key claim

The paper investigates which three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation best preserves the analytic structure and unitarity content of the full four-dimensional equation, finding that the equal-time (Klein) equation, which includes some three-body unitarity cuts, gives the best agreement with the Bethe-Salpeter result, but only for the specific models and approximations considered (ladder approximation with undressed propagators and vertices).

## Method

The authors performed a pinch analysis on the relative-energy integration contour in the kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to identify which unitarity thresholds are preserved by different three-dimensional reductions. They compared the analytic structure of the equal-time (Klein), Cohen, instantaneous, and Blankenbecler-Sugar equations for both a scalar \(\phi^2\sigma\) model and a \(\pi N\) system with \(s\)-, \(u\)-, and \(t\)-channel pole diagrams as driving terms, then computed numerical phase shifts to quantify the differences.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\phi^2\sigma\) model emulating the \(NN\) system: \(\phi\) mass = 1 GeV, \(\sigma\) mass = 0.15 GeV; the \(\phi\phi\) system has a bound state with binding energy 2.2 MeV (corresponding to the deuteron).
- The Bethe-Salpeter equation generates thresholds at \(\sqrt{s} = 2m + n\mu\) for \(n = 1, 2, \dots\) in the \(\phi^2\sigma\) model, where \(m\) is the \(\phi\) mass and \(\mu\) is the \(\sigma\) mass.
- For \(\pi N\) scattering with \(t\)-channel exchange, the three-body threshold is at \(\sqrt{s} = m_N + \mu_\pi + \mu_H\), where \(\mu_H\) is the mass of the exchanged meson.
- For \(\pi N\) scattering with \(u\)-channel exchange, the three-body thresholds are at \(\sqrt{s} = 2m_N + m_H\) and \(\sqrt{s} = 2\mu_\pi + m_H\), where \(m_H\) is the mass of the exchanged baryon.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation previously studied by Gross (1982), who proposed the one-body limit as a criterion, and by Pascalutsa and Tjon (2000), who used the instantaneous approximation for \(\pi N\) scattering. The paper establishes that the equal-time equation, originally suggested by Klein (1953) and revisited for the \(\phi^2\sigma\) model by Lahiff and Afnan (1997) and for nucleon-nucleon scattering by Phillips and Wallace (1996), preserves more of the analytic structure than the Cohen (1970), instantaneous (1952), or Blankenbecler-Sugar (1966) approximations. The results enable systematic improvement of three-dimensional equations by identifying which unitarity cuts must be included to approach the full Bethe-Salpeter result, particularly important for \(\pi N\) scattering near the pion production threshold where the \(N^*(1440)\) and \(N^*(1535)\) resonances appear.
