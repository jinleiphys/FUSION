# Bootstrap technique to study correlation between neutron skin thickness and the slope of symmetry energy in atomic nuclei

> We present a new statistical tool based on random sampling to assess the confidence interval of Pearson's and Spearman's correlation coefficients. These estimators are then used to quantify the statistical correlations among the neutron skin thickness of atomic nuclei and the slope of the symmetry energy in the infinite nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Non-Parametric Bootstrap (NPB) method provides robust confidence intervals for Pearson and Spearman correlation coefficients between neutron skin thickness and the slope of the symmetry energy, without assuming Gaussian errors. The correlation is strong for the neutron-rich nucleus \(^{132}\)Sn but absent for the proton-rich \(^{100}\)Sn, though the authors caution that statistical correlation does not establish a physical causal link.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) for \(^{100}\)Sn and \(^{132}\)Sn using 60 different nuclear energy density functionals (Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic), spanning a wide range of the slope of the symmetry energy \(L_0\). They applied the Non-Parametric Bootstrap method, performing 30,000 resampling simulations of the data set to build empirical distributions of the Pearson (\(r\)) and Spearman (\(\rho\)) correlation coefficients, from which 95% confidence intervals were extracted.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{132}\)Sn: Pearson coefficient \(r = 0.91_{-0.06}^{+0.05}\) and Spearman coefficient \(\rho = 0.85_{-0.11}^{+0.08}\), indicating a strong linear and monotonic correlation between neutron skin thickness and \(L_0\).
- For \(^{100}\)Sn: Pearson coefficient \(r = 0.03_{-0.51}^{+0.47}\) and Spearman coefficient \(\rho = -0.17_{-0.36}^{+0.38}\), showing no significant correlation.
- The bootstrap analysis used 30,000 simulations to derive the 95% confidence limits for each estimator.
- The symmetry energy slope \(L_0\) was extracted at saturation density \(\rho_0 \sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Centelles et al. (2009) and Roca-Maza et al. (2011) that suggested a correlation between neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei and the slope of the symmetry energy, which is a key ingredient for the equation of state of neutron stars. The NPB method provides a more rigorous statistical assessment than previous analyses, which assumed Gaussian error distributions. The results enable future application of this technique to a broader set of nuclei to study how the correlation evolves with isospin asymmetry, and may help refine experimental campaigns measuring neutron skins, such as those by Abrahamyan et al. (2012) and Tarbert et al. (2014).
