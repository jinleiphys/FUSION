# On the Experimental Identification of Spin-Parities and Single-Particle Configurations in $^{257}$No and Its $α$-Decay Daughter $^{253}$Fm

> Recently measured lifetimes of the favored $α$ decays from $^{257}$No have been calculated using the quantum mechanical tunneling within WKB approximation using microscopic nuclear potentials. Results obtained assuming previously assigned (ambiguous) parent spin of $\frac{7^+}{2}$ and newly assigned configuration $\frac{3^+}{2}$ [622] have been compared. Hindrance factors for the favored decays have also been compared with the calculated hindrances using higher angular momenta transfers. Although the calculations substantiate the findings, yet it makes the spin-parity assignment of $\frac{3^+}{2}$ for the ground state of $^{257}$No less definite.

# On the Experimental Identification of Spin-Parities and Single-Particle Configurations in $^{257}$No and Its $\alpha$-Decay Daughter $^{253}$Fm

## Key claim

Although the calculations substantiate the experimental findings of Asai (2005), the spin-parity assignment of $\frac{3^+}{2}$ for the ground state of $^{257}$No is made less definite because the measured $\alpha$-decay half-life of 24.5(5) s cannot rule out the possibility of the previously assigned $\frac{7^+}{2}$ spin-parity.

## Method

The authors calculated $Q_{\alpha}$ values from measured $\alpha$ particle kinetic energies using standard recoil correction and electron shielding correction following Perlman and Rasmussen (1957), and from atomic mass excesses using the experimental mass table of Audi, Wapstra and Thibault (2003). They then computed $\alpha$-decay lifetimes using quantum mechanical tunneling within the WKB approximation with microscopic nuclear potentials obtained by double folding density distribution functions with density-dependent M3Y effective interaction, including Coulomb and centrifugal barrier contributions.

## Key numbers

- Measured $\alpha$-decay half-life of $^{257}$No: 24.5(5) s
- Calculated half-life for $\frac{3^+}{2}$ parent to $\frac{3^+}{2}$ daughter (124.1 keV state, l=0): 15.68(72) s
- Calculated half-life for $\frac{3^+}{2}$ parent to $(\frac{3^+}{2})$ daughter (22.3 keV state, l=0): 7.28(38) s
- Calculated half-life for $(\frac{7^+}{2})$ parent to $\frac{1^+}{2}$ daughter (ground state, l=4): 69.78 s using Audi (2003) masses
- Calculated half-life for $\frac{3^+}{2}$ parent to $\frac{1^+}{2}$ daughter (ground state, l=2): 16.84 s using Audi (2003) masses
- Calculated half-life for $(\frac{7^+}{2})$ parent to $\frac{3^+}{2}$ daughter (124.1 keV state, l=2): 28.94(133) s
- Measured $E_{\alpha}$ for transition to 22.3 keV state: 8.323(7) MeV, yielding derived $Q_{\alpha}$ = 8.496(7) MeV
- Measured $E_{\alpha}$ for transition to 124.1 keV state: 8.222(6) MeV, yielding derived $Q_{\alpha}$ = 8.394(6) MeV
- $Q_{\alpha}$ for ground-state to ground-state transition from mass excesses: 8.466 MeV (Audi 2003)

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Asai (2005), who measured favored $\alpha$-decay lifetimes from $^{257}$No and claimed spin-parity identification of excited states in $^{253}$Fm based on internal conversion coefficients. The calculations test both the newly assigned $\frac{3^+}{2}$[622] configuration from Asai (2005) and the earlier less definite $\frac{7^+}{2}$ assignment from Firestone and Shirley (1999). The results show that the measured half-life of 24.5(5) s is consistent with either assignment, making the $\frac{3^+}{2}$ ground-state spin-parity less definite than claimed. This work enables further experimental clarification of the ground-state configuration of $^{257}$No, which is important for understanding single-particle structure in the heavy actinide region.
