# An experimental measurement of the coexistence curve and critical temperature, density and pressure of bulk nuclear matter

> Infinite, neutron-proton symmetric, neutral nuclear matter has a critical temperature of 17.9+-0.4 MeV, a critical density of 0.06+-0.01 nucleons per cubic fermi and a critical pressure of 0.31+-0.07 MeV per cubic fermi. These values have been obtained from our analysis of data from six different reactions studied in three different experiments: two "compound nuclear" reactions: 58Ni+12C-->70Se and 64Ni+12C-->76Se (both performed at the LBNL 88" Cyclotron) and four "multifragmentation" reactions: 1 GeV/c pi+197Au (performed by the ISiS collaboration), 1 AGeV 197Au+C, 1 AGeV 139La+12C and 1 AGeV 84Kr+12C (all performed by the EOS collaboration). The charge yields of all reactions as a function of excitation energy were fit with a version of Fisher's droplet model modified to account for the dual components of the fluid (i.e. protons and neutrons), Coulomb effects, finite size effects and angular momentum arising from the nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have determined the critical point of infinite, neutron-proton symmetric, neutral nuclear matter from experimental data, but this determination relies on fitting a modified Fisher droplet model to fragment yields from six different nuclear reactions, with the critical temperature defined as the point where the surface free energy vanishes in the model, not directly measured.

## Method

Fragment charge yields as a function of excitation energy from two compound nuclear reactions (58Ni+12C→70Se and 64Ni+12C→76Se at the LBNL 88" Cyclotron) and four multifragmentation reactions (1 GeV/c π+197Au from ISiS; 1 AGeV 197Au+12C, 1 AGeV 139La+12C, and 1 AGeV 84Kr+12C from EOS) were fit with a version of Fisher's droplet model. The model was modified to account for finite size effects, Coulomb effects, isospin asymmetry, angular momentum, and secondary decay of fragments, using the complement method to relate the free energy change upon fragment emission to the properties of bulk nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: Tc = 17.9 ± 0.4 MeV
- Critical density: ρc = 0.06 ± 0.01 nucleons per cubic fermi
- Critical pressure: pc = 0.31 ± 0.07 MeV per cubic fermi
- Surface energy coefficient from liquid drop expansion: as = 17.8048 (used in determining Tc = as/bs)
- Critical exponents set to three-dimensional Ising class values: σ = 0.63946 ± 0.0008, τ = 2.209 ± 0.006
- Lower charge cutoff of Z ≈ 6 used in analysis to select rarely-emitted fragments

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to characterize the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, which had examined critical exponents (Finn 1982, Gilkes 1994, Elliott 2000, 2002), caloric curves (Pochodzalla 1995), and negative heat capacities (D'Agostino 2000), but suffered from lack of knowledge of the system's location in pressure-density-temperature space. The analysis makes no assumptions about the system's location in (p, ρ, T) space and accounts for Coulomb force and angular momentum, unlike prior critical exponent studies that assumed systems were at coexistence with surface energy as the dominant factor. The results enable future work to place caloric curves and heat capacity measurements in the context of a known phase diagram.
