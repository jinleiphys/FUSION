# Identified particles from viscous hydrodynamics

> Identified particle observables from viscous hydrodynamics are sensitive to the fluid-to-particle conversion. Instead of the commonly assumed "democratic" Grad ansatz for phase space corrections $δf$, we utilize corrections calculated from linearized covariant transport theory. Estimates based on a pion-proton system with binary collisions indicate that protons are much closer to equilibrium than pions, significantly affecting the dissipative reduction of differential elliptic flow in Au+Au at RHIC. In addition, we test linear response against fully nonlinear transport for a two-component massless system in a Bjorken scenario. Strikingly, we find that, while linear response accounts well for the dynamical sharing of shear stress, the momentum dependence of phase space corrections is best described by Grad's quadratic ansatz, and not the linear response solution.

# Identified particles from viscous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The commonly used "democratic" Grad ansatz for converting viscous fluid to particles is inconsistent with scattering rates in a hadron gas; protons are much closer to equilibrium than pions in a pion-proton system with binary collisions, and this significantly affects the dissipative reduction of differential elliptic flow in Au+Au at RHIC. However, while linear response accounts well for the dynamical sharing of shear stress between species, the momentum dependence of phase space corrections is best described by Grad's quadratic ansatz, not the linear response solution.

## Method

The author uses linearized covariant transport theory to compute dissipative phase space corrections for a pion-proton system with binary 2-to-2 collisions, solving the integral equation variationally to obtain species-dependent corrections. The linear response results are then tested against fully nonlinear 2-to-2 Boltzmann transport for a two-component massless system (A+B) undergoing a 0+1D Bjorken expansion, with isotropic cross sections scaled as σ ∝ τ^(2/3) to keep η/s approximately constant.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at √s = 200A GeV, b = 7 fm, with η/s = 0.1 and freezeout at T = 165 MeV, the "democratic" prescription oversuppresses proton elliptic flow by 20-30% compared to the dynamical approach at moderate p_T ~ 1-2 GeV.
- Effective scattering cross sections used: σ_ππ = 30 mb, σ_πp = 20 mb, σ_pp = 50 mb, at T ~ 120-165 MeV.
- For the two-component massless system, initial inverse Knudsen numbers are K_A(τ_0) = 2 and K_B(τ_0) = 1 (species A scatters twice as often as B), with isotropic cross section ratio σ_AA : σ_AB : σ_BB = 4 : 2 : 1.
- Grad's quadratic ansatz reproduces transport spectra to within about 1% for times up to 10τ_0 and momenta up to 5-6 times the temperature.
- The linear response calculation yields a momentum dependence δf ∝ p^(1.47) for isotropic 2-to-2 processes.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Teaney (2009) by computing dissipative phase space corrections from linearized covariant transport theory for a hadron gas, rather than assuming the "democratic" Grad ansatz used in viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Luzum and Romatschke, Heinz and Song, and Schenke. The results bring into question the accuracy of all previous viscous hydrodynamic calculations of identified particle observables, and enable future refinements with realistic cross sections, real viscous hydrodynamics, quantum statistics, and more species.
