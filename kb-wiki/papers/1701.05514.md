# Forward two-photon exchange in elastic lepton-proton scattering and hyperfine-splitting correction

> We relate the forward two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes to integrals of the inclusive lepton-proton scattering cross sections. These relations yield an alternative way for the evaluation of the TPE correction to hyperfine-splitting (HFS) in the hydrogen-like atoms with an equivalent to the standard approach (Iddings, Drell and Sullivan) result implying the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule. For evaluation of the individual effects (e.g., elastic contribution) our approach yields a distinct result. We compare both methods numerically on examples of the elastic contribution and the full TPE correction to HFS in electronic and muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The paper derives an alternative method for evaluating the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to hyperfine splitting (HFS) in hydrogen-like atoms using dispersion relations for forward lepton-proton scattering amplitudes, which yields results equivalent to the standard approach (Iddings, Drell, and Sullivan) only after imposing the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule; for individual contributions (e.g., elastic), the two methods give distinct numerical results.

## Method

The author writes dispersion relations for the forward elastic lepton-proton scattering amplitudes at leading order in the fine-structure constant, relating the real parts of the spin-dependent TPE amplitudes to integrals of inclusive lepton-proton scattering cross sections via the optical theorem. These inclusive cross sections are then expressed in terms of the proton spin structure functions \(g_1\) and \(g_2\), and the TPE amplitudes at threshold (\(\omega = m\)) are evaluated as double integrals over photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and photon energy \(\nu_\gamma\). The resulting HFS correction is compared numerically with the standard approach for both electronic and muonic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

* The proton magnetic moment is \(\mu_P \approx 2.793\) (dimensionless, used in the one-photon exchange amplitude).
* The elastic threshold for the virtual photon energy is \(\nu_{\mathrm{thr}} = 0\); the inelastic threshold is \(\nu^{\mathrm{inel}}_{\mathrm{thr}} = m_\pi + (m_\pi^2 + Q^2)/(2M)\), where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
* The leading-order nS-level HFS (Fermi energy) is given by \(E^{\mathrm{HFS},0}_{\mathrm{nS}} = \frac{8}{3} \frac{m_r^3 \alpha^4}{M m} \frac{\mu_P}{n^3}\), with \(m_r\) the reduced mass of the lepton-proton bound state.
* The TPE correction to the Lamb shift for nS states is expressed as \(\Delta E_{\mathrm{nS}} = -\frac{8 m_r^4 \alpha^5}{\pi n^3} \int_0^\infty \frac{\mathrm{d} Q^2}{Q^5} \left( G_E^2(Q^2) - 2 G_E'(0) Q^2 - 1 \right)\), reproducing the third Zemach moment.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Iddings (1959, 1965), Drell and Sullivan (1966), and Faustov (1966, 1970) for TPE corrections to HFS by providing an alternative derivation based on dispersion relations for lepton-proton scattering amplitudes rather than the standard forward Compton scattering approach. The method enables a direct comparison of individual channel contributions (elastic vs. inelastic) between the two approaches, which differ numerically for the elastic part. The results are relevant for interpreting high-precision spectroscopy measurements of muonic hydrogen by the CREMA Collaboration at PSI and for constraining proton structure in the context of the proton radius puzzle.
