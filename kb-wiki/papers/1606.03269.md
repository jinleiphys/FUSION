# Collinearly improved JIMWLK evolution in Langevin form

> The high-energy evolution of Wilson line operators, which at leading order is described by the Balitsky-JIMWLK equations, receives large radiative corrections enhanced by single and double collinear logarithms at next-to-leading order and beyond. We propose a method for resumming such logarithmic corrections to all orders, at the level of the Langevin formulation of the JIMWLK equation. The ensuing, collinearly-improved Langevin equation features generalized Wilson line operators, which depend not only upon rapidity (the logarithm of the longitudinal momentum), but also upon the transverse size of the color neutral projectile to which the Wilson lines belong. This additional scale dependence is built up during the evolution, via the condition that the successive emissions of soft gluons be ordered in time. The presence of this transverse scale in the Langevin equation furthermore allows for the resummation of the one-loop running coupling corrections.

## Key claim
The authors propose a method to resum double and single collinear logarithms to all orders in the JIMWLK evolution by introducing generalized Wilson line operators that depend on the transverse size of the parent projectile, enforced through time-ordering constraints on successive soft gluon emissions, thereby extending previous collinear-improvement schemes beyond the large-\(N_c\) limit of the BK equation.

## Method
The authors construct a non-local (in rapidity) Langevin equation for Wilson line operators by enforcing time-ordering of successive soft gluon emissions via kinematical constraints involving the transverse size of the projectile. This introduces an additional scale dependence in the Wilson lines, beyond the usual transverse coordinate and rapidity, which is built up dynamically during the evolution. The resulting collinearly-improved Langevin equation is presented in two versions: one with time-ordering and running coupling, and a second that additionally resums single collinear logarithms, both formulated as functional stochastic equations on the SU\((N_c)\) group manifold.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order JIMWLK evolution resums radiative corrections of type \((\alpha_s Y)^n\) to all orders, where \(Y \equiv \ln(1/x)\) is the rapidity.
- The dipole kernel \(\mathcal{M}_{\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}\mathbf{z}} = (\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y})^2 / [(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{z})^2(\mathbf{z}-\mathbf{y})^2]\) appears in the Balitsky hierarchy for the dipole \(S\)-matrix.
- The noise correlator is \(\langle \nu_{m,\mathbf{x}}^{ia} \nu_{n,\mathbf{y}}^{jb} \rangle = (1/\epsilon) \delta^{ij} \delta^{ab} \delta_{mn} \delta_{\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}}\), with \(\epsilon\) the rapidity step size.
- The running coupling resummation is enabled by the transverse size scale \(r\) of the projectile, which sets the scale for collinear logs.

## Context
This work extends the collinear-improvement methods previously developed for the BK equation by Beuf (2014) and Iancu et al. (2015) to the full JIMWLK hierarchy, going beyond the large-\(N_c\) limit. The authors address the instability of NLO Balitsky-JIMWLK equations caused by large double and single collinear logarithms, which had rendered the NLLA approximation impractical as shown by Lappi and Mantysaari (2015). The resulting Langevin formulation enables numerical simulations of finite-\(N_c\) effects and more complicated scattering operators such as the color quadrupole, and may also provide inspiration for collinear improvement in the BMS equation for non-global logarithms.
