# Light Front Dynamics and The Binding Correction

## Key claim

The nuclear binding correction in deep inelastic lepton scattering is essentially the same in light front and instant form representations, contrary to claims in several earlier papers that the binding correction almost vanishes in the light front formulation. The equivalence holds when the full four-momenta of interacting nucleons are properly accounted for, including the excitation and kinetic energies of the residual nucleus.

## Method

The author derives the nuclear structure function in the light front representation using the convolution form with a nucleon momentum distribution function, then expands it in moments to extract the binding correction. By explicitly calculating the average light front momentum fraction ⟨z⟩ from the conservation of light front momenta and the binding energies of the initial and residual nuclei, the author shows that the resulting separation energy term matches the instant form result. The analysis also examines the momentum sum rule used in contradicting papers and demonstrates that it is violated by meson field contributions.

## Key numbers

- For heavy nuclei, the nucleon separation energy is approximately 40 MeV, giving ⟨z⟩ ≈ 0.96.
- The moment I₂ = E_sep / m_N, where E_sep is the separation energy and m_N is the nucleon mass.
- For the deuteron, the binding correction from the instant form and light front approaches are similar because ⟨E_n⟩ ≈ 0.

## Context

This work extends the earlier binding correction explanation of the nuclear EMC effect by Akulinichev, Shlomo, and collaborators (We1, We2) and defends it against later claims by authors including Benhar and Ciofi degli Atti (BC) and Jung and Kim (JK) that the binding correction vanishes in light front dynamics. The paper clarifies that the momentum sum rule used in those contradicting papers omits meson contributions, analogous to how the gluon contribution is needed for the momentum sum rule in the one-nucleon quark-gluon case. This enables a consistent treatment of nuclear binding effects across different dynamical representations.
