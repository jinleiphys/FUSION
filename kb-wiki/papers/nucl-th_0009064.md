# Continuum QCD and light mesons

> The ladder-rainbow truncation of the set of Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to study light mesons. The parameters in the effective interaction are constrained by the chiral condensate and f_π; the current quark masses are fitted to m_πand m_K. The dressed quark propagators are in qualitative agreement with recent lattice-QCD results at low q^2 while having the correct perturbative behavior at large q^2. The resulting vector meson masses are within 5% of the experimental values. The obtained electromagnetic form factors and strong and electroweak coupling constants are also in good agreement with the data. At finite temperature, this truncation leads to a mean-field chiral phase transition. The spatial pion mass is almost constant below this transition, but rises with T close to and above T_c. The mass of its chiral partner, an idealized sigma meson, decreases with T until T_c, where it becomes degenerate with the pion.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, with an effective interaction constrained by the chiral condensate and pion decay constant, reproduces light meson properties to within 5% of experimental values, but the scalar meson results are strictly those of an idealized chiral partner because the truncation omits couplings to the dominant ππ decay mode and yields degenerate isoscalar and isovector bound states.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved in rainbow truncation, and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states was solved in ladder truncation, using an effective quark-antiquark interaction that combines an infrared Gaussian term with the perturbative QCD running coupling at large momenta. The model parameters (ω = 0.4 GeV, D = 0.93 GeV²) and current quark masses were fitted to the chiral condensate, mπ, mK, and fπ at zero temperature. At finite temperature, the Matsubara formalism was employed with the same effective interaction extended minimally, and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations were solved for the lowest external Matsubara mode to obtain spatial meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Vector meson masses within 5% of experimental values: mρ = 0.742 GeV (expt. 0.770 GeV), mK* = 0.936 GeV (expt. 0.892 GeV), mφ = 1.072 GeV (expt. 1.020 GeV)
- Pion mass mπ = 0.138 GeV, kaon mass mK = 0.496 GeV, with decay constants fπ = 0.092 GeV and fK = 0.109 GeV
- Chiral condensate of about (240 GeV)³ used to constrain the effective interaction
- Critical temperature Tc ∼ 0.15 GeV for the chiral phase transition, with mean-field critical exponent β = 1/2
- At T=0, the σ meson mass mσ = 0.56 GeV in the chiral limit and 0.59 GeV at current quark mass m̂ = 5.7 MeV
- Pion charge radius r²π = 0.45 fm² (expt. 0.44 fm²), kaon charge radii r²K+ = 0.38 fm² (expt. 0.34 fm²), r²K0 = -0.086 fm² (expt. -0.054 fm²)
- Coupling constants: gπγγ = 0.50 (expt. 0.50), gρπγ = 0.54 (expt. 0.57), gρππ = 4.85 (expt. 6.02), gφKK = 4.63 (expt. 4.64)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Roberts (2000) and Alkofer (2000) to systematically calculate light meson masses, decay constants, form factors, and coupling constants within a single consistent truncation. The dressed quark propagators show qualitative agreement with lattice-QCD results from Skullerud (2000) at low momentum while reproducing perturbative behavior at high momentum. The finite-temperature results enable comparison with lattice simulations of chiral symmetry restoration and provide predictions for how pion and sigma meson properties evolve through the phase transition, though the rainbow-ladder truncation requires future improvements beyond ladder truncation to describe observed scalar mesons and include ππ coupling effects as noted by Pennington (2000).
