# Low-lying quadrupole collective states of the light and medium Xenon isotopes

> Collective low lying levels of light and medium Xenon isotopes are deduced from the Generalized Bohr Hamiltonian (GBH). The microscopic seven functions entering into the GBH are built from a deformed mean field of the Woods-Saxon type. Theoretical spectra are found to be close to the ones of the experimental data taking into account that the calculations are completely microscopic, that is to say, without any fitting of parameters.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Generalized Bohr Hamiltonian (GBH), with all seven functions derived microscopically from a Woods-Saxon mean field without any parameter fitting, produces theoretical spectra "quite close" to experimental data for light and medium Xenon isotopes (A=112-126). However, the hedge is that while the energy scales and low-lying levels (2₁⁺, 4₁⁺) are well reproduced, the theoretical spectra systematically belong to the anharmonic vibrator type, whereas most experimental spectra exhibit doublets characteristic of the Wilets-Jean γ-soft model, indicating the shell correction is not strong enough to produce a sharp deformed minimum.

## Method

The authors solved the single-particle Schrödinger equation using a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field with universal parameters from the literature, then computed the collective potential energy via the macroscopic-microscopic method (liquid drop plus Strutinsky shell correction using a semiclassical level density, plus BCS pairing corrections). The six inertial functions (three mass parameters B_ββ, B_βγ, B_γγ and three moments of inertia ℑ₁, ℑ₂, ℑ₃) were calculated using the Inglis-Belyaev cranking approximation. The resulting GBH was diagonalized numerically using the FORTRAN code of Libert to obtain collective energy levels for even-even ¹¹²⁻¹²⁶Xe isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation β for ¹¹⁸⁻¹²²Xe is about 0.26–0.29, corresponding to the neutron mid-shell region (N=64–66)
- Experimental quadrupole β values for all studied isotopes lie in the interval 0.19 < β < 0.29
- Experimental energy ratio R_exp = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) is about 2.3–2.5 for all isotopes, placing them between the vibrational limit (R=2) and the γ-unstable limit (R=2.5)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for ¹¹⁸Xe₆₄: neutron potential depth V₀ = -45.99 MeV, proton potential depth V₀ = -53.22 MeV; spin-orbit coupling κ = 17.74 MeV·fm² (neutrons) and 21.13 MeV·fm² (protons)
- Liquid drop parameters: r₀ ≈ 1.275 fm, ζ = 52.8(1 − 2.84I²) with I = (N−Z)/(N+Z)

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic GBH calculations that required ad-hoc corrections, such as introducing pairing vibrations (Ref. 14) or artificially reducing pairing strengths by 20% (Ref. 14a), to fix the "stretching" of theoretical spectra. The authors show that using a Woods-Saxon mean field with universal parameters yields correct mass parameter magnitudes and avoids the stretching problem without such corrections. The results enable further studies to improve the shell correction strength so that the theoretical potential energy develops a sharper minimum at deformed shapes, which would bring the spectra from the anharmonic vibrator type toward the Wilets-Jean γ-soft type observed experimentally.
