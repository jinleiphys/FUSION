# Abnormal dilepton yield from local parity breaking in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a novel explanation for the dilepton excess observed in heavy ion collisions at invariant masses below 1 GeV. We argue that the presence of local parity breaking due to a time-dependent isosinglet and/or isotriplet pseudoscalar condensate substantially modifies the dispersion relation of photons and vector mesons propagating in such a medium, changing the ρ~ spectral function and resulting in a potentially large excess of e+e- and μ+μ- with respect to the theoretical predictions based in a 'cocktail' of known hadronic processes. Possible signatures to prove or disprove this effect are discussed.

# Abnormal dilepton yield from local parity breaking in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed excess of dilepton pairs (e⁺e⁻ and μ⁺μ⁻) at invariant masses below 1 GeV in central and semi-central heavy-ion collisions may be explained by local parity breaking (LPB) due to a time-dependent pseudoscalar condensate, rather than by conventional in-medium modifications of meson properties. The mechanism requires a pseudoscalar background parameter ζ of order 1–2 MeV, and the authors note that the presence of LPB does not preclude other many-body or in-medium corrections as long as they do not represent double counting.

## Method

The authors employ a Vector Dominance Model (VDM) framework with SU(2) flavor symmetry, coupling photons, ρ mesons, and ω mesons to quarks, and introduce a parity-odd Chern-Simons term arising from a time-dependent pseudoscalar background field a(t). The mass-shell equations for vector mesons are solved for an isosinglet pseudoscalar background, yielding modified dispersion relations where the transverse polarizations split into two branches with masses m²_{V,±} = m²_V ± 360 ζ |k|. The dilepton production rate is computed using a modified ρ propagator with these mass-shifted states, incorporating a thermal average with effective temperature and normalizing to the standard hadronic "cocktail" used by NA60 and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson mass splitting for isosinglet background: m²_{V,±} = m²_V ± 360 ζ |k|, where ζ is the time derivative of the pseudoscalar field
- The vacuum stability condition requires ζ < m_V/180 ≈ 4.3 MeV
- Best fit to NA60 μ⁺μ⁻ data for central collisions uses ζ ≈ 2 MeV with effective temperature T ≈ 300 MeV
- Best fit to PHENIX e⁺e⁻ data for minimum bias uses ζ ≈ 1 MeV with effective temperature T ≈ 110 MeV
- The ρ/ω ratio enhancement factor is 1.6 for NA60 data and approximately 1.8 for PHENIX data
- The ρ meson mean free path is 1.3 fm, compared to fireball size L = 5–10 fm
- The φ meson mean free path (~40 fm) makes it insensitive to medium effects

## Context

This work proposes a novel explanation for the dilepton excess observed by CERES, HELIOS/3, NA60 (CERN SPS) and PHENIX (BNL RHIC) at invariant masses below 1 GeV, which has remained unexplained by conventional hadronic processes. The mechanism extends the idea that a pseudoscalar field slowly evolving in time can modify electromagnetic properties of the vacuum (Axion, 2008) and is related to topological charge fluctuations studied in the context of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev, 2008; lattice QCD simulations, 2009). The approach contrasts with previous explanations based on dropping ρ mass or broadening resonance (Brown, Rapp, Cassing, Zahed, 1990s–2000s) and proton-neutron scattering (Lapidus, 2009), which have been unable to account for the factor 4–7 enhancement observed at RHIC and SPS energies. The results enable experimental searches for parity violation through dilepton polarization asymmetries and could be tested at LHC heavy-ion collisions.
