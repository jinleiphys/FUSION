# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of $A=9,10$ nuclei

> We report on quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the ground and low-lying excited states of $A=9,10$ nuclei using realistic Hamiltonians containing the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon potential alone or with one of several three-nucleon potentials, including Urbana IX and three of the new Illinois models. The calculations begin with correlated many-body wave functions that have an $α$-like core and multiple p-shell nucleons, $LS$-coupled to the appropriate $(J^π;T)$ quantum numbers for the state of interest. After optimization, these variational trial functions are used as input to a Green's function Monte Carlo calculation of the energy, using a constrained path algorithm. We find that the Hamiltonians that include Illinois three-nucleon potentials reproduce ten states in $^9$Li, $^9$Be, $^{10}$Be, and $^{10}$B with an rms deviation as little as 900 keV. In particular, we obtain the correct 3$^+$ ground state for $^{10}$B, whereas the Argonne $v_{18}$ alone or with Urbana IX predicts a 1$^+$ ground state. In addition, we calculate isovector and isotensor energy differences, electromagnetic moments, and one- and two-body density distributions.

## Key claim

The paper reports that Hamiltonians including the new Illinois three-nucleon potentials (IL2, IL3, IL4) reproduce ten states in A=9,10 nuclei with an rms deviation as low as 900 keV, and correctly predict a 3+ ground state for 10B, whereas the Argonne v18 potential alone or with the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential wrongly predicts a 1+ ground state.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) to construct correlated many-body wave functions with an alpha-like core and multiple p-shell nucleons, LS-coupled to the appropriate (J^pi;T) quantum numbers, then optimized these trial functions. These were used as input to Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations with a constrained path algorithm to project out the exact lowest-energy state for each set of quantum numbers. The Hamiltonians included the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential alone or with one of several three-nucleon potentials (Urbana IX, IL2, IL3, IL4), and the calculations were performed for ten stable or narrow natural-parity states in 9Li, 9Be, 10Be, and 10B.

## Key numbers

- The AV18/IL2 Hamiltonian reproduces ten states in 9Li, 9Be, 10Be, and 10B with an rms deviation of 900 keV from experimental energies
- The AV18/IL3 Hamiltonian gives an rms deviation of 1100 keV for the same ten states
- The AV18/IL4 Hamiltonian gives an rms deviation of 1700 keV for the same ten states
- For comparison, the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian gives an rms deviation of 2.3 MeV for 17 narrow states in A <= 8 nuclei, while AV18 alone gives 7.7 MeV
- The first excited state of 9Li is at 2.691 MeV excitation, believed to be a (1/2-;3/2) state
- The 9Be second excitation is a narrow (5/2-;1/2) state at 2.429 MeV with width less than 1 keV
- The 10Be ground state decays by beta- emission with half-life 1.51 x 10^6 years
- The 10Be first excited state is a (2+;1) state at 3.368 MeV
- The 10B ground state is (3+;0), with (1+;0) excited states at 0.718 MeV and 2.154 MeV, a (2+;0) state at 3.587 MeV, and a (0+;1) state at 1.740 MeV
- The 9He resonance is observed at approximately 1.2 MeV above the 8He+n threshold with width 100 keV
- The 10He resonance is observed at approximately 1.1 MeV above the 8He+n+n threshold with width <= 300 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum Monte Carlo calculations from A <= 8 nuclei (Pieper et al., 1995, 1997; Wiringa et al., 2000) to A=9,10 systems, using the same realistic Hamiltonians. The new Illinois three-nucleon potentials (IL2-IL4) were previously shown to reproduce 17 narrow states in A <= 8 nuclei with rms deviation of approximately 400 keV (Pieper et al., 2001), significantly improving over the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian. The calculations enable future work on unnatural parity intruder states and second excited states in these nuclei, as well as extension to heavier p-shell systems.
