# Nuclear Physics from QCD

## Key claim
Direct contact between QCD and simple nuclear systems can be made by exploiting effective field theory (EFT) to control the quark-mass dependence of few-nucleon observables, but the quark-mass dependence of the deuteron binding energy and scattering lengths in the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel remains uncertain due to unknown low-energy constants, particularly $D_2$ and $\overline{d}_{16}$.

## Method
The quark-mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering is computed using effective field theory with two different power-counting schemes: Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) counting for the $^1S_0$ channel, and Beane-Bedaque-Savage-van Kolck (BBSvK) counting for the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ coupled channels, which resums the chiral limit of one-pion exchange. For the $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ system, the potential is derived at leading order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT) by evaluating box and crossed-box two-pion exchange diagrams, with the $\Sigma_Q^{(*)}$ mass splitting treated as $\overline\Delta \sim m_\pi$.

## Key numbers
- In the $^1S_0$ channel, the scattering length as a function of pion mass is given by an analytic expression containing a LO constant $\gamma$ and a combination $D_2(\mu)$ of $m_q$-dependent operator coefficients; $D_2$ is presently unknown and estimated via naive dimensional analysis with parameter $\eta = 1/5$ or $1/15$.
- In the $^3S_1$ channel, the allowed range for $\overline{d}_{16}$ is $-2.61\ {\rm GeV}^{-2} < \overline{d}_{16} < -0.17\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}$ and $\overline{d}_{18} = -0.51\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}$; the deuteron may be bound or unbound in the chiral limit.
- The $^3S_1$ scattering length relaxes to natural values of $\sim 1\ {\rm fm}$ when the pion mass exceeds $\sim 200\ {\rm MeV}$.
- The $\Sigma_c-\Lambda_c$ mass splitting is $167.1\ {\rm MeV}$, the $\Sigma_c^*-\Lambda_c$ splitting is $232.7\ {\rm MeV}$, and the spin-weighted average is $\overline\Delta \sim 211\ {\rm MeV}$.
- The asymptotic $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ potential in QCD falls as $V^{\rm QCD}(r) \rightarrow - (3 g_3^4 m_\pi^{9/2})/(16 \pi^{5/2} r^{5/2} \overline\Delta^2 f_\pi^4) e^{-2 m_\pi r}$ at large distances.
- The leading hairpin contribution to the $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ potential in PQQCD falls as $V^{\rm HP}(r) \rightarrow - c_1^2 (m_{SS}^2 - m_\pi^2)^2 m_\pi^{5/2} / (64 \pi^{5/2} \Lambda_\chi^6) e^{-2 m_\pi r} / \sqrt{r}$ at asymptotically large distances.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to NN interactions developed by Weinberg (1990), Kaplan-Savage-Wise (1998), and Beane-Bedaque-Savage-van Kolck (BBSvK) by computing the explicit quark-mass dependence of scattering lengths and binding energies. It also extends the $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ potential calculation of Arndt-Beane-Savage (2003) to partially-quenched QCD. The results enable constraints on time variation of the Higgs vacuum expectation value through Big-Bang nucleosynthesis, as studied by Yoo and Scherrer (2003), and provide the chiral extrapolation formulas needed for lattice QCD simulations of two-nucleon systems performed at unphysically large quark masses.
