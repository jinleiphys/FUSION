# Influence of orthogonalization procedure on astrophysical S-factor for the direct $α+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $γ$ capture process in a three-body model

> The astrophysical S-factor for the direct $ α(d,γ)^{6}{\rm Li}$ capture reaction is calculated in a three-body model based on the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method. A sensitivity of the E1 and E2 astrophysical S-factors to the orthogonalization method of Pauli forbidden states in the three-body system is studied. It is found that the method of orthogonalising pseudopotentials (OPP) yields larger isotriplet ($T=1$) components than the supersymmetric transformation (SUSY) procedure. The E1 astrophysical S-factor shows the same energy dependence in both cases, but strongly different absolute values. At the same time, the E2 S-factor does not depend on the orthogonalization procedure. As a result, the OPP method yields a very good description of the direct data of the LUNA collaboration at low energies, while the SUSY transformation strongly underestimates the LUNA data. \keywords{three-body model; orthogonalization method; astrophysical S factor.

# Influence of orthogonalization procedure on astrophysical S-factor for the direct α+d → ⁶Li + γ capture process in a three-body model

## Key claim
The method of orthogonalising pseudopotentials (OPP) yields a very good description of the direct LUNA collaboration data for the astrophysical S-factor at low energies, while the supersymmetric transformation (SUSY) procedure strongly underestimates the same data; the E1 S-factor shows the same energy dependence in both cases but strongly different absolute values, whereas the E2 S-factor does not depend on the orthogonalization procedure.

## Method
The authors calculated the astrophysical S-factor for the direct α(d,γ)⁶Li capture reaction using a three-body model based on the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method. They treated Pauli forbidden states in the three-body α+p+n system using two different orthogonalization procedures: the method of orthogonalising pseudopotentials (OPP) and the supersymmetric transformation (SUSY) method. The final ⁶Li(1+) ground-state wave function was computed with the Minnesota NN-potential and Voronchev et al. α-N potentials, and the scattering wave function of α-d relative motion was calculated with a deep Dubovichenko potential.

## Key numbers
- The ⁶Li ground state energy converges at E = -3.70 MeV with maximal hypermomentum K_max = 24 for both OPP and SUSY methods.
- The isotriplet (T=1) component of the ⁶Li ground state wave function has a norm square of 5.27 × 10⁻³ in the OPP method, compared to 1.10 × 10⁻⁴ in the SUSY method.
- The deuteron ground-state energy from the Minnesota potential is E_d = -2.202 MeV.
- The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the ⁶Li(1+) ground state is C_αd = 2.31 fm⁻¹/².
- The threshold energy for the reaction is E_th = 1.474 MeV.
- The α-N potential was renormalized by a scaling factor of 1.014 to reproduce the experimental binding energy of E_b = 3.70 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous three-body model calculations by Tursunov and collaborators (2016, 2018) and by Baye and Tursunov (2018), which used the OPP method and reproduced LUNA collaboration data. The study directly compares OPP and SUSY orthogonalization procedures, motivated by the finding that the exact mass prescription used in earlier literature (Typel, 1997; Mukhamedzhanov, 2011; Tursunov, 2015; Nollett, 2001; Tombrello, 1991) has no microscopic basis. The results enable more reliable reaction rate evaluations for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis models addressing the ⁶Li/⁷Li abundance ratio discrepancy, and show that the choice of orthogonalization method critically affects the E1 contribution while leaving the E2 contribution unchanged.
