# Vector meson photoproduction studied in its radiative decay channel

> We provide an analysis of vector meson photoproduction in the channel of the vector meson decaying into a pseudoscalar meson plus a photon, i.e. $V\to Pγ$. It is shown that non-trivial kinematic correlations arise from the measurement of the $Pγ$ angular distributions in the overall c.m. system in comparison with those in the vector-meson-rest frame. In terms of the vector meson density matrix elements, the implication of such kinematic correlations in the measurement of polarization observables is discussed. For the $ω$ meson production, due to its relatively large branching ratios for $ω\toπ^0γ$, additional events from this channel may enrich the information about the reaction mechanism and improve the statistics of the recent measurement of polarized beam asymmetries by the GRAAL Collaboration. For $ϕ\to ηγ$, $ρ\to πγ$, and $K^*\to Kγ$, we expect that additional information about the spin structure of the vector meson production vertex can be derived.

## Key claim

The paper shows that measuring vector meson photoproduction via the radiative decay channel \(V \to P\gamma\) introduces non-trivial kinematic correlations in the overall center-of-mass system that are absent in the vector-meson-rest frame, and these correlations must be accounted for when extracting polarization observables, particularly for heavier pseudoscalar mesons or higher reaction energies.

## Method

The authors derived the angular distribution formalism for vector meson decay into a pseudoscalar meson plus a photon (\(V \to P\gamma\)) in the overall \(\gamma N\) center-of-mass system, using the effective Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L}_{VP\gamma} = (e g_V / M_V) \epsilon_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta} \partial^\alpha A^\beta \partial^\gamma V^\delta P\). They expressed the decay distributions in terms of vector meson density matrix elements and compared them with the standard formalism for decays into spinless particles, analyzing how Lorentz transformation from the vector-meson-rest frame to the overall c.m. system introduces kinematic factors \(\mathcal{F}_1(\theta_c)\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2(\theta_c)\) that modify the angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for \(\omega \to \pi^0\gamma\) is \((8.5 \pm 0.5)\%\) (Particle Data Group 2004), providing additional statistics for \(\omega\) meson photoproduction studies.
- Branching ratio for \(\phi \to \eta\gamma\) is \((1.295 \pm 0.025)\%\) (Particle Data Group 2004), making this channel a significant source for \(\phi\) meson photoproduction near threshold.
- For \(\omega \to \eta\gamma\), the ratio \(M_\pi^2 / |\mathbf{r}_\pi|^2 = 0.13\); for \(\omega \to \pi^0\gamma\), the ratio \(M_\eta^2 / |\mathbf{r}_\eta|^2 = 7.56\), demonstrating that kinematic correlations become more significant for heavier pseudoscalar decay channels.
- The kinematic correlation factor \(W_b/W_a = 2 + \frac{1}{2} \frac{|\mathbf{q}|^2}{M_V^2} \left(1 + \frac{3}{2} \frac{M_P^2}{|\mathbf{r}|^2}\right)\) shows explicit dependence on vector meson momentum and pseudoscalar meson mass.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Schilling, Seyboth, and Wolf (1970) for vector meson decays into spinless particles to the radiative decay channel \(V \to P\gamma\), which had not been previously analyzed. It builds on studies of density matrix elements and polarization observables by Pichowsky, Savkli, and Tabakin (1996) and inequality constraints by Kloet et al. (1998). The analysis enables experimental facilities at GRAAL (ESRF), LEPS (SPring-8), and Crystal Ball (ELSA) to extract additional spin structure information from \(\omega \to \pi^0\gamma\), \(\phi \to \eta\gamma\), \(\rho \to \pi\gamma\), and \(K^* \to K\gamma\) decays, particularly for studying nucleon resonance excitations in vector meson photoproduction.
