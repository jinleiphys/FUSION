# Pion $β$ decay and $τ\toππν_τ$ beyond leading logarithms

> The consistent matching of short-distance contributions and hadronic matrix elements is crucial for precise predictions of weak processes involving hadrons. In this Letter, we address this point for charged-current processes involving two pions -- pion $β$ decay $π^\pm\toπ^0 e^\pmν_e$ and hadronic $τ$ decays $τ^\pm\toπ^\pmπ^0ν_τ$ -- whose decay rates depend on the so-called $γW$ box correction. Using recent results from lattice QCD, we show how to formulate the matching beyond leading-logarithmic accuracy, in particular, how to cancel the dependence on the scheme choice for evanescent operators. As main results, we obtain a prediction for the decay rate of pion $β$ decay with theory uncertainties improved by a factor of three, which renders theory uncertainties negligible for future determinations of $V_{ud}$ even beyond the reach of the PIONEER experiment, and an evaluation of isospin-breaking corrections to $τ\toππν_τ$ with negligible uncertainty from the short-distance matching, as necessary for a future $τ$-based determination of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates how to formulate the matching of short-distance contributions and hadronic matrix elements for charged-current two-pion processes beyond leading-logarithmic accuracy, showing that the scheme dependence from evanescent operators cancels explicitly between Wilson coefficients and matrix elements, but only when the matching is performed at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) order rather than at leading-logarithmic (LL) order.

## Method
The authors match the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT) to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at NLL accuracy, using both a spurion method to derive expressions for individual low-energy constants and a direct amplitude-matching approach that exploits Ward identities and current algebra. They combine these matching relations with lattice QCD results for the nonperturbative γW box correction from Feng (2020) and Yoo (2023), and with renormalization-group evolution at NLL and NLL_s accuracy, to obtain scheme-independent predictions for the decay rates.

## Context
This work extends the EFT-based matching framework developed for neutron decay by Cirigliano (2023) to the meson sector, specifically pion beta decay and hadronic tau decays. It supersedes the leading-logarithmic analysis of Descotes-Genon (2005) by incorporating NLL and NLL_s resummation, and uses lattice QCD results from Feng (2020) and Yoo (2023) for the nonperturbative γW box correction. The improved radiative corrections render theory uncertainties negligible for future V_ud determinations from pion beta decay at the PIONEER experiment, and enable a tau-based determination of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with negligible short-distance matching uncertainty.
