# Classical chromo-dynamics of relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions produce thousands of particles, and it is sometimes difficult to believe that these processes allow for a theoretical description directly in terms of the underlying theory - QCD. However once the parton densities are sufficiently large, an essential simplification occurs - the dynamics becomes semi-classical. As a result, a simple ab initio approach to the nucleus--nucleus collision dynamics may be justified. In these lectures, we describe the application of these ideas to the description of multi--particle production in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We also discuss the role of semi-classical fields in the QCD vacuum in hadron interactions at low and high energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at sufficiently high parton densities, the dynamics of relativistic heavy ion collisions becomes semi-classical, allowing an *ab initio* description directly from QCD; however, this simplification holds only when the occupation numbers of gluon fields become large, so that the weak-coupling limit (small \(\alpha_s\)) is equivalent to the classical limit (\(\hbar \to 0\)), and the non-linear structure of QCD can be treated systematically.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical framework based on classical chromo-dynamics, starting from the QCD Lagrangian and re-scaling the gluon fields to isolate the coupling constant dependence. The saturation scale \(Q_s\) is derived from the condition that non-linear interactions among gluons become important when \(\partial_\mu \tilde{A}_\mu \sim \tilde{A}_\mu^2\), leading to a classical field description where the number of gluons scales as \(1/\alpha_s\). The approach is applied to multi-particle production by treating the initial nuclear color fields as a classical "color glass condensate," and the spectral density of the energy-momentum tensor is used to connect perturbative and non-perturbative regimes via scale anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The infrared frozen value of the strong coupling is estimated as \(\langle \alpha_s \rangle_{IR} \simeq 0.56\) for QCD with \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=2\).
- The non-perturbative contribution to the intercept of the "soft" Pomeron is \(\Delta = 0.08 \div 0.1\), derived from a matching scale \(M_0^2 = 4 \div 6\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The saturation scale satisfies \(Q_s^2 \sim \alpha_s\ \frac{x G_A(x,Q_s^2)}{\pi R_A^2}\), with the gluon number scaling as \(x G_A(x,Q_s^2) \sim \frac{\pi}{\alpha_s(Q_s^2)} Q_s^2 R_A^2\).
- The long-range potential between two heavy quarkonia at large distances decays as \(V^{\pi \pi}(R) \rightarrow -\left(\bar d_2 \frac{a_0^2}{\epsilon_0}\right)^2 \left( \frac{4 \pi^2}{b} \right)^2 \frac{3}{2} (2 m_\pi)^4 \frac{m_\pi^{1/2}}{(4\pi R)^{5/2}} e^{-2 m_\pi R}\).
- The perturbative potential at short distances is \(V_\theta(R) = - g^4 \left( \bar d_2 \frac{a_0^2}{\epsilon_0}\right)^2 \frac{15}{8\pi^3} \frac{1}{R^7}\) (with a factor of 23 instead of 15 when the tensor \(2^{++}\) contribution is included).

## Context

This work extends the classical approach to QCD in the high-density regime, building on the concepts of parton saturation (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin 1983; Mueller and Qiu 1986; Blaizot and Mueller 1987) and the color glass condensate (Iancu, Leonidov, McLerran; Mueller; Venugopalan). It also connects to the BFKL resummation (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov) for high-energy scattering and to the low-energy theorems for the QCD energy-momentum tensor (Voloshin and Zakharov 1980; Novikov and Shifman 1981). The results enable a unified description of both low-energy heavy quarkonium scattering and high-energy hadron interactions in terms of the QCD vacuum structure, and provide a framework for predicting centrality-dependent multiplicity in heavy ion collisions.
