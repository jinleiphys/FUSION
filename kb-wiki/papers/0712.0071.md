# Continuum Coupling Effects in Spectra of Mirror Nuclei and Binding Systematics

> Continuum-coupling correction to binding energies in neutron rich oxygen and fluorine isotopes and to excitation energies of 2+ states in 36Ca and 36S mirror nuclei are studied using the real-energy continuum shell model.

# Continuum Coupling Effects in Spectra of Mirror Nuclei and Binding Systematics

## Key claim

Continuum coupling corrections to binding energies and excitation energies, computed using the real-energy continuum shell model (SMEC), can explain the Thomas-Ehrmann shift in mirror nuclei and the anti-odd-even staggering (anti-OES) in neutron-rich oxygen and fluorine isotopes, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the quenching factor used to account for intruder configurations and on the ratio of neutron-proton to neutron-neutron continuum coupling strengths, which must be reduced near the neutron drip line.

## Method

The authors employ the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), which couples the standard shell model (CQS) Hilbert space of bound states to the scattering continuum of decay channels via a two-body residual interaction. The energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian in the Q-subspace is diagonalized to obtain complex eigenvalues, and the continuum-coupling energy correction is computed as an incoherent sum of contributions from couplings to all states in the daughter (A-1) nucleus. For the mirror nuclei study, a quenching factor is introduced to account for admixtures of intruder configurations from the fp shell, and calculations are performed using WBT and IOKIN effective Hamiltonians in sdfp and psdfp model spaces.

## Key numbers

- The experimental excitation energy of the 2₁⁺ state in ³⁶Ca is 3.05 ± 0.05 MeV, while in ³⁶S it is 3.2909 MeV, giving a difference of approximately 240 keV.
- The SMEC calculation with both neutron and proton continua yields 2₁⁺ excitation energies of 3.403 MeV for ³⁶Ca and 3.690 MeV for ³⁶S, compared to the SM value of 2.641 MeV for both nuclei.
- With only proton continuum couplings for ³⁶Ca (³⁵K + p), the 2₁⁺ energy is 3.019 MeV; with only neutron continuum couplings for ³⁶Ca (³⁵Ca + n), it is 3.514 MeV.
- With only neutron continuum couplings for ³⁶S (³⁵S + n), the 2₁⁺ energy is 3.024 MeV; with only proton continuum couplings for ³⁶S (³⁵P + p), it is 3.822 MeV.
- For a quenching factor Q_f = 0.651, the SMEC excitation energy of the 2₁⁺ state in ³⁶S reproduces the experimental value exactly.
- The fraction of the total continuum-coupling energy correction coming from coupling to the ground state of the daughter nucleus exceeds 60% for ²⁴O, ²⁶O, and ²⁸O, but is less than approximately 20% in fluorine isotopes.
- The optimal ratio V₀^(np)/V₀^(nn) near the neutron drip line is approximately 1/2, whereas for nuclei near the valley of beta-stability the standard choice is approximately 2.
- The one-proton separation energy in ³⁶Ca is 2.56 MeV; the one-neutron separation energy in ³⁶S is 9.89 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system (OQS) formulation of nuclear structure, building on the Thomas-Ehrmann shift concept (Thomas, 1950s) and the Gamow-state formalism for weakly-bound nuclei. The SMEC approach, developed by the authors and collaborators (Karim, 2000s; Okołowicz, 2003), embeds the standard shell model in the continuum of decay channels. The study of anti-OES in fluorine isotopes connects to the BCS description of pairing correlations and to the work of Satula (1990s) on deformed mean-field effects, while the mirror-symmetry breaking analysis for ³⁶Ca-³⁶S follows the experimental measurement by Buerger (2000s). The results enable future investigations of the qualitative difference between weakly-bound systems at the proton drip line (where the Coulomb barrier suppresses continuum coupling) versus the neutron drip line, and motivate further study of the three-component origin of odd-even staggering in binding energies.
