# Kinetic theory for massive spin-1 particles

> We calculate the Wigner function for charged spin-1 particles in inhomogeneous classical electromagnetic fields, going to first order in a power series in $\hbar$. The Boltzmann equation for the scalar distribution function obtained from this formalism agrees with previous calculations for spin-1/2 particles. In particular, we recover a Mathisson force of twice the magnitude, correctly reflecting the higher dipole moment of vector mesons. Evolution equations for vector and tensor degrees of freedom are obtained, and global equilibrium is discussed.

# Kinetic theory for massive spin-1 particles

## Key claim
The authors derive a quantum kinetic theory for massive spin-1 particles in classical electromagnetic fields to first order in ℏ, recovering a Mathisson force of twice the magnitude compared to spin-1/2 particles, which correctly reflects the higher dipole moment of vector mesons. The Boltzmann equation for the scalar distribution function agrees with previous calculations for spin-1/2 particles, but the tensor polarization degrees of freedom are a genuine spin-1 property absent for spin-1/2 particles.

## Method
The authors start from a complex Proca field Lagrangian with an additional coupling term that sets the gyromagnetic ratio to exactly two. They define the Wigner function for charged vector particles in the presence of classical electromagnetic fields, including a gauge link, and derive exact equations of motion for it. The Wigner function is decomposed into scalar, antisymmetric tensor, and symmetric traceless tensor components, and the equations are solved to first order in ℏ using a power series expansion, yielding on-shell Boltzmann-Vlasov-like equations for the scalar distribution function V, the spin tensor Σ^{μν}, and the tensor polarization t^{μν}.

## Key numbers
- The Mathisson force term in the Boltzmann equation for spin-1 particles differs by a factor of two compared to the spin-1/2 case, consistent with the spin magnitude of vector mesons being twice as large.
- The Proca field Lagrangian includes a term −i q F_{μν} V^μ V^{†ν} that enhances the coupling such that the gyromagnetic ratio is exactly equal to two.
- The Wigner function is defined with a prefactor of −2/[(2πℏ)^4 ℏ] in the integral over the gauge-invariant two-point function.
- The equilibrium distribution function is parametrized as f^e = [exp(g^e) − 1]^{−1}, where g^e = β·k − eα, with e ∈ {+,−} distinguishing particles and antiparticles.

## Context
This work extends the quantum kinetic theory approach previously developed for spin-1/2 particles by Weickgenannt (2019) to massive spin-1 particles, motivated by the unexpectedly large tensor polarization of vector mesons observed in heavy-ion collisions by ALICE (2019), Mohanty (2021), and STAR (2022). The formalism recovers the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi (BMT) equation for the classical spin vector at zeroth order in ℏ, and the tensor polarization components obey BMT equations for their eigenvectors. The global equilibrium analysis reveals that the thermal vorticity term encodes the massive analogue of the axial chiral vortical effect, while the electromagnetic field terms determine the chiral separation effect, building on work by Kharzeev (2015), Avkhadiev (2017), Yamamoto (2017), and Huang (2020). Away from equilibrium, a nonvanishing tensor polarization is induced by the shear-stress tensor of the medium, as shown by Wagner (2022).
