# Nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations: 2 modes

> Extending our earlier study of nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations (canonical transformations for fermions) for one fermionic mode, in the present paper we perform a thorough study of general (nonlinear) canonical transformations for two fermionic modes. We find that the Bogolyubov-Valatin group for n=2 fermionic modes which can be implemented by means of unitary SU(2^n = 4) transformations is isomorphic to SO(6;R)/Z_2. The investigation touches on a number of subjects. As a novelty from a mathematical point of view, we study the structure of nonlinear basis transformations in a Clifford algebra [specifically, in the Clifford algebra C(0,4)] entailing (supersymmetric) transformations among multivectors of different grades. A prominent algebraic role in this context is being played by biparavectors (linear combinations of products of Dirac matrices, quadriquaternions, sedenions) and spin bivectors (antisymmetric complex matrices). The studied biparavectors are equivalent to Eddington's E-numbers and can be understood in terms of the tensor product of two commuting copies of the division algebra of quaternions H. From a physical point of view, we present a method to diagonalize any arbitrary two-fermion Hamiltonian. Relying on Jordan-Wigner transformations for two-spin-1/2 and single-spin-3/2 systems, we also study nonlinear spin transformations and the related problem of diagonalizing arbitrary two-spin-1/2 and single-spin-3/2 Hamiltonians. Finally, from a calculational point of view, we pay due attention to explicit parametrizations of SU(4) and SO(6;R) matrices (of respective sizes 4x4 and 6x6) and their mutual relation.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the group of general (nonlinear) Bogolyubov-Valatin canonical transformations for two fermionic modes, implementable by unitary SU(4) transformations, is isomorphic to SO(6;R)/Z_2, extending the authors' earlier one-mode analysis and providing the widest possible generalization of the original linear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations for two modes.

## Method

The authors formulate the most general ansatz for nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations as unitary SU(4) transformations acting on the 16-dimensional operator basis of the two-fermion Hilbert space, expressed in terms of fermion creation and annihilation operators and their products. They map the problem onto nonlinear basis transformations in the Clifford algebra C(0,4), study the algebraic structure of biparavectors (equivalent to Eddington's E-numbers and the tensor product of two quaternion algebras), and derive the group structure as SO(6;R)/Z_2. They then apply this framework to diagonalize arbitrary two-fermion Hamiltonians and, via Jordan-Wigner transformations, two-spin-1/2 and single-spin-3/2 Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

* The Bogolyubov-Valatin group for n=2 fermionic modes is isomorphic to SO(6;R)/Z_2, with SU(2^n = 4) as the implementing unitary group.
* The Clifford algebra C(0,4) is generated by 4 antihermitian operators c_k (k=1,...,4) satisfying {c_k, c_l} = -2 delta_{kl} 1_4.
* The biparavector space is 15-dimensional, spanned by antisymmetric objects c_{m1 m2} with m1,m2 = -1,0,1,...,4.
* The trace condition for any annihilation operator b_k yields: 4 lambda^{(0|0)}_k + 2 lambda^{(1|1)}_k + 2 lambda^{(2|2)}_k - lambda^{(1,2|1,2)}_k = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier systematic study of nonlinear Bogolyubov-Valatin transformations for one fermionic mode (van Holten and Scharnhorst, 2008) to the two-mode case, generalizing the original linear transformations introduced by Bogolyubov (1958) and Valatin (1958) for superconductivity. The results connect to the mathematics of Clifford algebras, Eddington's E-numbers, and the tensor product of quaternions, and enable the diagonalization of arbitrary two-fermion and two-spin-1/2 Hamiltonians, with applications to quantum information theory and nuclear magnetic resonance product operator formalism.
