# Vertical Structure of Neutrino Dominated Accretion Disks and Neutrino Transport in the disks

> We investigate the vertical structure of neutrino dominated accretion disks by self-consistently considering the detailed microphysics, such as the neutrino transport, vertical hydrostatic equilibrium, the conservation of lepton number, as well as the balance between neutrino cooling, advection cooling and viscosity heating. After obtaining the emitting spectra of neutrinos and antineutrinos by solving the one dimensional Boltzmann equation of neutrino and antineutrino transport in the disk, we calculate the neutrino/antineutrino luminosity and their annihilation luminosity. We find that the total neutrino and antineutrino luminosity is about $10^{54}$ ergs/s and their annihilation luminosity is about $5\times10^{51}$ ergs/s with an extreme accretion rate $10 M_{\rm {sun}}$/s and an alpha viscosity $α=0.1$. In addition, we find that the annihilation luminosity is sensitive to the accretion rate and will not exceed $10^{50}$ ergs/s which is not sufficient to power the most fireball of GRBs, if the accretion rate is lower than $1 M_{\rm {sun}}$/s. Therefore, the effects of the spin of black hole or/and the magnetic field in the accretion flow might be introduced to power the central engine of GRBs.

# Vertical Structure of Neutrino Dominated Accretion Disks and Neutrino Transport in the Disks

## Key claim

The authors find that neutrino pair annihilation luminosity from neutrino dominated accretion flows (NDAFs) is about $5\times10^{51}$ ergs/s for an extreme accretion rate of $10 M_{\odot}$/s, which can roughly satisfy the energy demand of the most energetic gamma-ray bursts (GRBs); however, for accretion rates below $1 M_{\odot}$/s, the annihilation luminosity will not exceed $10^{50}$ ergs/s, which is insufficient to power the most energetic GRB fireballs, suggesting that black hole spin or magnetic fields may be needed to explain the central engine of GRBs.

## Method

The authors construct a steady, vertically resolved model of a neutrino dominated accretion disk around a $3.3 M_{\odot}$ black hole, solving the one-dimensional Boltzmann equation for neutrino and antineutrino transport in the vertical direction using a two-stream approximation. They self-consistently enforce vertical hydrostatic equilibrium, lepton number conservation, and thermal balance between neutrino cooling, advection cooling, and alpha viscosity heating, using an exact equation of state based on Fermi-Dirac integrals for protons, neutrons, electron pairs, neutrinos, and photons. After obtaining the convergent disk structure, they solve the full Boltzmann equation to compute the energy-dependent and direction-dependent neutrino spectra, then calculate the neutrino pair annihilation luminosity by integrating the annihilation rate over the entire disk surface and correcting for the finite disk thickness.

## Key numbers

- Total neutrino and antineutrino luminosity: $L_{\nu} = L_{\bar\nu} = 5.2 \times 10^{53}$ ergs/s for accretion rate $\dot M = 10 M_{\odot}$/s and alpha viscosity $\alpha = 0.1$
- Neutrino pair annihilation luminosity: $L_{\nu\bar\nu} = 5 \times 10^{51}$ ergs/s for $\dot M = 10 M_{\odot}$/s and $\alpha = 0.1$, after correction for disk thickness
- Annihilation efficiency: $\eta = L_{\nu\bar\nu} / (L_{\nu} + L_{\bar\nu}) = 0.48\%$ for the extreme accretion rate case
- For accretion rates lower than $1 M_{\odot}$/s, the annihilation luminosity will not exceed $10^{50}$ ergs/s
- Temperature in the inner part of the disk at radius $r = 10 M$: approximately $T \approx 8$ MeV
- Mass density in the inner part of the disk at $r = 10 M$: approximately $\rho \sim 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$
- Inner boundary of the disk: $r_{\rm in} = 6 M$; outer boundary: $r_{\rm out} = 100 M$
- Black hole mass: $M = 3.3 M_{\odot}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino dominated accretion flows by self-consistently solving the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport rather than using the gray body spectra assumption of Janiuk (2007) or the Eddington approximation of Reynoso (2007). It improves upon the simplified neutrino transport model of Di Matteo (2002) by treating neutrino and antineutrino transport separately, and addresses the unrealistic annihilation efficiencies (up to 100%) found in Liu (2010) due to oversimplified assumptions about the equation of state and annihilation geometry. The results enable more accurate predictions of whether NDAFs alone can power GRB central engines, and motivate future work incorporating black hole spin and magnetic fields into the accretion flow model.
