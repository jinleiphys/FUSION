# Anomalous transport from holography: Part II

> This is a second study of chiral anomaly induced transport within a holographic model consisting of anomalous $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ Maxwell theory in Schwarzschild-$AdS_5$ spacetime. In the first part, chiral magnetic/separation effects (CME/CSE) are considered in presence of a static spatially-inhomogeneous external magnetic field. Gradient corrections to CME/CSE are analytically evaluated up to third order in the derivative expansion. Some of the third order gradient corrections lead to an anomaly-induced negative $B^2$-correction to the diffusion constant. We also find non-linear in $B$ modifications to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW). In the second part, we focus on the experimentally interesting case of the axial chemical potential being induced dynamically by a constant magnetic and time-dependent electric fields. Constitutive relations for the vector/axial currents are computed employing two different approximations: (a) derivative expansion (up to third order) but fully nonlinear in the external fields, and (b) weak electric field limit but resuming all orders in the derivative expansion. A non-vanishing non-linear axial current (CSE) is found in the first case. Dependence on magnetic field and frequency of linear transport coefficient functions (TCFs) is explored in the second.

## Key claim

The paper derives gradient corrections to chiral magnetic and separation effects (CME/CSE) up to third order in derivative expansion, finding that some third-order terms produce an anomaly-induced negative \(B^2\) correction to the diffusion constant, which violates the universal form of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2008); the authors also compute nonlinear modifications to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) speed and decay rate.

## Method

The authors use a holographic model of anomalous \(U(1)_V \times U(1)_A\) Maxwell theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\) spacetime, working in the probe limit where gauge fields do not backreact on the geometry. They solve the bulk dynamical equations for the gauge fields by promoting static homogeneous solutions to functions of boundary coordinates, then compute boundary currents via the fluid/gravity correspondence. In the first part, they evaluate gradient corrections to CME/CSE up to third order for a static spatially-inhomogeneous magnetic field; in the second part, they study a constant magnetic field with time-dependent electric field using two approximations: derivative expansion up to third order (fully nonlinear in external fields) and weak electric field limit with all-order resummation of derivatives.

## Key numbers

* Diffusion constant gets a negative correction: \(\mathcal{D}_0 = \frac{1}{2} - 18(2\log 2 - 1) \kappa^2 B^2\), where \(\kappa\) is the anomaly coefficient and \(B\) is the magnetic field strength.
* Dispersion relation for the chiral magnetic wave: \(\omega = [\mp 1 + 36(1-2\log 2)\kappa^2 \mathbf{B}^2] 6\kappa \vec{q}\cdot \vec{\mathbf{B}} - [\frac{1}{2} + 18(1-2\log 2)\kappa^2 \mathbf{B}^2] i q^2 - \frac{i}{8} q^4 \log 2 + \cdots\), where \(\mathbf{B}\) is a constant magnetic field and \(q\) is momentum.
* The Hawking temperature is normalized to \(\pi T = 1\) throughout the calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study Bu, Lublinsky, and Sharon (2016) on anomalous transport from holography, going beyond the weak external field approximation to include nonlinear effects. It contrasts with studies by other groups (e.g., 2014, 2015, 2016) that focused on weak electric fields where the axial current vanishes, whereas this work finds a non-vanishing nonlinear axial current (CSE) in the derivative expansion case. The results enable future exploration of frequency-dependent transport coefficient functions and the separation of anomaly-induced effects from non-anomalous ones by varying the relative angle between electric and magnetic fields.
