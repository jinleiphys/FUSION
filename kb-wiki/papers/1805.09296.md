# Bose enhancement, the Liouville effective action and the high multiplicity tail in p-A collisions

> In the framework of dense-dilute CGC approach we study fluctuations in the multiplicity of produced particles in p-A collisions. We show that the leading effect that drives the fluctuations is the Bose enhancement of gluons in the proton wave function. We explicitly calculate the moment generating function that resums the effects of Bose enhancement. We show that it can be understood in terms of the Liouville effective action for the composite field which is identified with the fluctuating density, or saturation momentum of the proton. The resulting probability distribution turns out to be very close to the gamma-distribution. We also calculate the first correction to this distribution which is due to pairwise Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlations of produced gluons.

## Key claim

The leading contribution to multiplicity fluctuations in p-A collisions arises from Bose enhancement of gluons in the projectile (proton) wave function, and this effect is not suppressed by the area of the projectile; the resulting probability distribution is very close to a gamma-distribution, though the authors note that the treatment of infrared divergences differs from prior work by Gelis et al. (2009), leading to different cumulant structure.

## Method

The authors work within the dense-dilute Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for the projectile and assuming a dense target to employ factorizable averages of Wilson lines. They calculate the moment generating function for gluon multiplicity by first integrating over projectile color charge configurations, then performing the target average using a dipole approximation for Wilson line correlators. The leading Bose enhancement contributions are resummed exactly, while Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations are treated as a subleading correction.

## Key numbers

- The mean number of produced particles (first cumulant) is given by κ₁ = (1/8π)(N_c² − 1) S_⟂ μ_p² 𝔇 ln(k²_min/Λ²), where S_⟂ is the projectile area, μ_p² is the MV model parameter, 𝔇 is the integrated dipole operator, k_min is the minimum momentum of produced gluons, and Λ is the infrared cutoff scale.
- Higher cumulants for n ≥ 2 are κ_n = (n−2)! (N_c²−1) S_⟂ Λ² / (8π) × (μ_p² 𝔇 / Λ²)^n.
- The double inclusive cross section from Bose enhancement scales as (N_c²−1) S_⟂ times an integral over q of |μ_p²(q)|²/q⁴, giving an area dependence proportional to S_⟂², the same as the square of the single inclusive cross section.
- The HBT contribution to double inclusive production is subleading, lacking the quadratic infrared divergence that gives the extra factor of area.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of multiplicity fluctuations in p-A collisions within the CGC framework, building on the approach of Gelis et al. (2009) but with a dense target rather than a dilute one, which suppresses half of the contributions considered leading in that earlier work. The authors also relate their result for the moment generating function to the Liouville effective action for a composite field identified with the fluctuating density or saturation momentum of the proton, as discussed in Dumitru et al. (2017). The calculation enables a systematic understanding of the high-multiplicity tail in small-system collisions and provides a basis for including HBT corrections and other subleading effects.
