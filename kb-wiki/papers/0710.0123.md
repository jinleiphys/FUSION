# Bottom Baryons

> Recently CDF and D0 collaborations observed several bottom baryons. In this work we perform a systematic study of the masses of bottom baryons up to $1/m_Q$ in the framework of heavy quark effective field theory (HQET) using the QCD sum rule approach. The extracted chromo-magnetic splitting between the bottom baryon heavy doublet agrees well with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors use QCD sum rules in the heavy quark effective field theory (HQET) framework, including corrections up to order \(1/m_Q\), to predict the masses of bottom baryons; the extracted chromo-magnetic splitting between the bottom baryon heavy doublet agrees well with experimental data, but the same formalism applied to charmed baryons yields a chromo-magnetic splitting for \(\Omega_c^*\) that is much larger than the experimental value, indicating the heavy quark expansion does not work well for charmed hadrons.

## Method

The authors derive mass sum rules for S-wave bottom baryons (\(\Xi_b\), \(\Xi_b'\), \(\Xi_b^*\), \(\Omega_b\), \(\Omega_b^*\)) using interpolating currents in HQET, including finite strange quark mass corrections. They compute the leading-order binding energy \(\bar{\Lambda}_i\) and the \(1/m_Q\) corrections from kinetic and chromo-magnetic operators, then extract the baryon masses and the mass splittings between the spin-\(\frac{1}{2}^+\) and spin-\(\frac{3}{2}^+\) members of the \(\mathbf{6_F}\) doublet. The analysis uses Borel transformed sum rules with a single-pole plus continuum ansatz, and the working region for the Borel parameter is chosen as \(0.4 < T < 0.6\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- \(\bar{\Lambda}_{\Sigma_b} = 950^{+78}_{-74}\) MeV; \(\bar{\Lambda}_{\Xi_b'} = 1042^{+76}_{-74}\) MeV; \(\bar{\Lambda}_{\Omega_b} = 1169 \pm 74\) MeV; \(\bar{\Lambda}_{\Lambda_b} = 773^{+68}_{-59}\) MeV; \(\bar{\Lambda}_{\Xi_b} = 908^{+72}_{-67}\) MeV (extracted at \(T=0.5\) GeV with continuum thresholds \(\omega_i\) of 1.3, 1.4, 1.55, 1.1, and 1.25 GeV respectively).
- Mass splitting \(m_{\Sigma_b^*} - m_{\Sigma_b} = 26 \pm 1\) MeV (this work) vs. experimental value 21 MeV from CDF.
- Mass splitting \(m_{\Xi_b^*} - m_{\Xi_b'} = 26 \pm 1\) MeV (this work).
- Mass splitting \(m_{\Omega_b^*} - m_{\Omega_b} = 28^{+8}_{-2}\) MeV (this work).
- Predicted masses: \(\Sigma_b = 5809^{+82}_{-76}\) MeV; \(\Xi_b' = 5903^{+81}_{-79}\) MeV; \(\Omega_b = 6036 \pm 81\) MeV; \(\Sigma_b^* = 5835^{+82}_{-77}\) MeV; \(\Xi_b^* = 5929^{+83}_{-79}\) MeV; \(\Omega_b^* = 6063^{+83}_{-82}\) MeV; \(\Lambda_b = 5637^{+68}_{-56}\) MeV; \(\Xi_b = 5780^{+73}_{-68}\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of heavy baryons in HQET, such as those by Dai et al. (2003) who calculated \(1/m_Q\) corrections for \(\Lambda_Q\) and \(\Sigma_Q^{(*)}\), and by Wang (2007) who studied \(\Omega_{c,b}^*\). It also complements phenomenological quark model calculations by Capstick and Isgur (1986), Roncaglia et al. (1995), and Jenkins (1996), as well as lattice QCD predictions by Mathur et al. (2002). The results provide predictions for the yet-unobserved \(\Xi_b'\), \(\Xi_b^*\), \(\Omega_b\), and \(\Omega_b^*\) that can be tested at Tevatron, while the poor agreement for charmed baryons (e.g., predicted \(\Omega_c^*\) splitting \(\sim 133\) MeV vs. experimental 70.8 MeV) highlights the limitations of the \(1/m_Q\) expansion for charm quarks.
