# J/psi production in relativistic heavy ion collisions from a multi-phase transport model

> Using A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, we study J/psi production from interactions between charm and anti-charm quarks in initial parton phase and between D and Dbar mesons in final hadron phase of relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). Including also the inverse reactions of J/psi absorption by gluons and light mesons, we find that the net number of J/psi from the parton and hadron phases is smaller than that expected from the superposition of initial nucleon-nucleon collisions, contrary to the J/psi enhancement predicted by the kinetic formation model. The production of J/psi is further suppressed if one includes the color screening effect in the parton phase. We have also studied the dependence of J/psi production on the charm quark mass and the effective charm meson mass.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with a more realistic space-time evolution than the kinetic formation model, the net number of J/psi from parton and hadron phases is smaller than that expected from the superposition of initial nucleon-nucleon collisions, contrary to the J/psi enhancement predicted by the kinetic formation model of Thews (2001).

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model, a hybrid of HIJING for initial conditions, ZPC for parton evolution, and ART for hadron evolution, to simulate central (b=0) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV. They included J/psi production from c+cbar -> J/psi+g in the parton phase and D+Dbar -> J/psi+M in the hadron phase, along with the inverse absorption reactions, and treated charm particles perturbatively due to their rarity. Color screening was modeled via a critical radius in the transverse plane where the parton energy density exceeds the critical density for J/psi dissociation.

## Key numbers

- Charm-anti-charm pair production cross section in nucleon-nucleon collisions: 350 microbarns.
- Rapidity density of charm quark pairs at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV: about 1.5 per unit rapidity.
- J/psi production cross section per unit rapidity in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV: 0.63 microbarns.
- Nuclear overlap function T_{Au+Au}(b=0): 30 mb^{-1}, giving 0.019 J/psi per unit rapidity expected from primary nucleon-nucleon collisions.
- Final net J/psi number per event per unit rapidity (|y|<1) with color screening: 0.0007 for m_D=1.70 GeV, 0.0019 for m_D=1.87 GeV, 0.0053 for m_D=2.01 GeV.
- Final net J/psi number per event per unit rapidity without color screening: 0.0010 for m_D=1.70 GeV, 0.0024 for m_D=1.87 GeV, 0.0057 for m_D=2.01 GeV.
- J/psi number from parton phase only without color screening: about 0.0014 per event.
- J/psi number from parton phase only with color screening: about 0.0007 per event.
- J/psi number increases by 72% when charm quark mass increases from 1.35 GeV to 1.5 GeV, and by about a factor of 4 when charm quark mass is 1.8 GeV compared to 1.35 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic formation model of Thews (2001) by using a dynamical transport model (AMPT) instead of assuming an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma with 1-D Bjorken hydrodynamics, and it contradicts that model's prediction of J/psi enhancement at RHIC. The study also extends earlier AMPT work on J/psi suppression (Zhang 2002) by including secondary J/psi production from charm-anti-charm interactions and D meson interactions. The results enable future studies to test whether the suppression or enhancement scenario is correct, and to investigate the role of gluon shadowing and feeddown from higher charmonium states.
