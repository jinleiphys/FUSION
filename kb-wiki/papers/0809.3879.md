# The low-lying Scalar Mesons and Related Topics

> After presenting the motivations to explore the low-lying scalar mesons such as the $σ$, $a_0$ and $κ$ in the unquenched as well as quenched lattice QCD, we review the works done by our collaboration (SCALAR Collaboration) with a what-to-do-next list. We briefly mention the imporatance to explore the $N_c$ dependence of and possible effects of the $U_A(1)$ anomaly to the properties the low-lying scalar mesons.

## Key claim

The SCALAR Collaboration's lattice QCD simulations suggest that a low-lying sigma meson exists as a pole in QCD, but the authors caution that the lattice used is small and coarse, so the masses obtained should be considered upper limits rather than definitive values.

## Method

The collaboration performed full QCD simulations with dynamical Wilson fermions and the plaquette gauge action on an 8^3 × 16 lattice at β = 4.8, using a point source and sink. The sigma meson propagator was computed including both connected and disconnected diagrams, with the disconnected part calculated using the Z2 noise method and the vacuum expectation value subtracted. For the kappa meson, a quenched approximation was employed on a 20^3 × 24 lattice with a finer lattice spacing of a = 0.1038(33) fm.

## Key numbers

- Sigma-to-rho mass ratio m_σ/m_ρ = 1.583 ± 0.098 at κ = 0.1846, decreasing to 1.112 ± 0.060 at κ = 0.1891
- Connected-part-only mass ratio m_con./m_ρ = 2.400 ± 0.018 at κ = 0.1846, increasing to 2.481 ± 0.031 at κ = 0.1891 (identified with the a_0 meson)
- Lattice spacing a = 0.207(9) fm for the full QCD simulation at β = 4.8
- Critical hopping parameter κ_c = 0.195(3)
- Kappa meson mass from quenched approximation ∼ 1.7 GeV, compared to experimental candidates in the range 660–800 MeV
- Linear fits in lattice units: m_ρ a = 0.3767 × (m_π a)^2 + 0.8099 and m_σ a = 1.6818 × (m_π a)^2 + 0.3219

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological analyses that identified a sigma meson pole in the π-π S-matrix, such as the analysis by Caprini (2005) giving M_σ = 441 − i 272 MeV, and the tetraquark proposal by Jaffe (1976). The results contradict the simple constituent quark model expectation that scalar mesons should lie in the 1.2–1.6 GeV region, and instead support the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model picture where the sigma emerges from chiral symmetry breaking. The findings enable future lattice studies using larger lattices, chiral fermions, variational methods with multiple interpolating operators including tetraquark operators, and exploration of N_c dependence to clarify whether the sigma is a tetraquark, hybrid, or collective q-anti-q state.
