# Correlation and isospin dynamics of participant-spectator matter in neutron-rich colliding nuclei at 50 MeV/nucleon

> The sensitivities of isospin asymmetry and collision geometry dependencies of participant (overlapping region)- spectator (quasiprojectile and quasitarget region) matter towards the symmetry energy using the isospin quantum molecular dynamical model are explored. Particularly, the difference of the number of nucleons in the overlapping zone to the quasi-projectile-target matter is found to be quite sensitive to the symmetry energy at semiperipheral geometries compared to the individual yield. It gives us a clue that this quantity can be used as a measure of isospin migration. Further, the yield of neutrons (charge of the second-largest fragment) is provided as a tool for overlapping region (quasi-projectile-target) matter to check the sensitivity of the above-mentioned observable towards the symmetry energy experimentally.

## Key claim

The difference in the number of nucleons between the overlapping region (OR) and the quasi-projectile plus quasi-target (QP+QT) matter is sensitive to the slope of the symmetry energy at semi-peripheral geometries, but only weakly dependent on the isospin asymmetry of the system; this observable is proposed as a measure of isospin migration rather than a direct probe of the symmetry energy magnitude.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model with a static soft equation of state and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections to simulate collisions of Sn isotopes (112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, and 132Sn+132Sn) at 50 MeV/nucleon across the full range of impact parameters. Nucleons that experienced at least one collision were assigned to the overlapping region (OR) matter, while the remainder constituted the QP+QT matter, following the fireball model definition. The sensitivity of the normalized difference (NOR - NQP+QT)/NTot to the symmetry energy was examined for soft (γi = 0.5) and stiff (γi = 1.5) density dependencies, and correlations with fragment yields were analyzed via power-law fitting.

## Key numbers

- The compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is given as κ0 = 235 ± 14 MeV.
- The symmetry energy parameters used are Cs,k = 25 MeV and Cs,p = 35.2 MeV, with γi = 0.5 (soft) and γi = 1.5 (stiff).
- For the correlation between OR nucleons and light charged particles, the power-law exponent τ is 0.605 for neutrons, 0.546 for protons, 0.815 for 2H, 0.831 for 3H, 1.022 for 3He, and 1.180 for 4He.
- For the correlation between QP+QT nucleons and light charged particles, τ is -0.317 for neutrons, -0.288 for protons, -0.423 for 2H, -0.418 for 3H, -0.524 for 3He, and -0.599 for 4He.
- The slope parameter for Zmax-1/AProjectile with QP+QT nucleons is 0.58.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly following the Symmetry Energy Project (SEP) at Michigan State University (2011) which called for new observables to parametrize the sub-saturation density dependence of symmetry energy. The authors build on prior work by Sood (2004) and Saks (2012) on participant-spectator matter at balance energy, and by Rizzo (2008) on isospin migration driven by the symmetry energy slope. The results suggest that the yield of neutrons and the charge of the second largest fragment (Zmax-1/AProjectile) can serve as experimental proxies for the OR and QP+QT nucleon numbers, enabling experimental tests of isospin migration at facilities such as NSCL.
