# Rapidly rotating strange stars for a new equation of state of strange quark matter

> For a new equation of state of strange quark matter, we construct equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating strange stars in general relativity. The sequences are the normal and supramassive evolutionary sequences of constant rest mass. We also calculate equilibrium sequences for a constant value of $Ω$ corresponding to the most rapidly rotating pulsar PSR 1937 + 21. In addition to this, we calculate the radius of the marginally stable orbit and its dependence on $Ω$, relevant for modeling of kilo-Hertz quasi-periodic oscillations in X-ray binaries.

## Key claim

For a new equation of state (EOS) of strange quark matter based on a density-dependent confinement model, the mass and radius of the compact object in 4U 1728-34 would be bounded above by 1.15 solar masses and 9.3 km if it is a strange star, but the paper's abstract omits that this bound is conditional on the star being a strange star and that the two EOS models considered are both consistent with the source.

## Method

The authors solved the Einstein field equations for a general axisymmetric and stationary spacetime assuming a perfect fluid configuration, using a Green's function approach to iteratively compute metric coefficients. They constructed equilibrium sequences of rapidly rotating strange stars for two parameterizations of a new EOS (SS1 and SS2) based on a density-dependent scalar potential for quark confinement, and compared results with the MIT bag model EOS (B90_0). They calculated constant rest mass sequences, constant angular velocity sequences, and the radius of the marginally stable orbit for each EOS.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for the non-rotating SS1 model: 2.2 solar masses (rest mass sequence)
- Upper bound for mass of the compact object in 4U 1728-34 if it is a strange star: 1.15 solar masses
- Upper bound for radius of the compact object in 4U 1728-34 if it is a strange star: 9.3 km
- Rotational frequency used for 4U 1728-34 calculations: 364 Hz
- Boundary layer thickness between the surface and innermost stable orbit for the maximum central density listed: approximately 5 km
- Normal nuclear matter density used in the EOS: 0.16 fm^-3
- Parameter values for the two EOS models: SS1 uses nu = 0.333, SS2 uses nu = 0.286

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of rotating strange stars by Gourgoulhon et al. (1999), Stergioulas et al. (1999), and Zdunik et al. (2000), which all used the schematic MIT bag model EOS. The new EOS from Dey et al. (1998) incorporates density-dependent quark masses and asymptotic freedom, unlike the simpler MIT bag model. The results enable more accurate modeling of kilo-Hertz quasi-periodic oscillations in X-ray binaries and provide constraints for identifying strange star candidates such as 4U 1728-34, SAX J1808.4-3658, and 4U 1820-30.
