# Simulating nonequilibrium quantum fields with stochastic quantization techniques

> We present lattice simulations of nonequilibrium quantum fields in Minkowskian space-time. Starting from a non-thermal initial state, the real-time quantum ensemble in 3+1 dimensions is constructed by a stochastic process in an additional (5th) ``Langevin-time''. For the example of a self-interacting scalar field we show how to resolve apparent unstable Langevin dynamics, and compare our quantum results with those obtained in classical field theory. Such a direct simulation method is crucial for our understanding of collision experiments of heavy nuclei or other nonequilibrium phenomena in strongly coupled quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that stochastic quantization with a complex Langevin equation can be used for first-principles lattice simulations of nonequilibrium quantum field dynamics in Minkowski space-time, provided that unstable Langevin dynamics are resolved through techniques such as back-stepping or regularization with a small imaginary mass.

## Method
The authors simulate a self-interacting scalar field theory on a 3+1 dimensional space-time lattice with anisotropic discretization, using a Langevin equation in an additional fifth "Langevin-time" direction to construct the quantum ensemble. Starting from a non-thermal initial state specified by classical field configurations, they evolve the complex field via a discretized Langevin equation with Gaussian noise, averaging over Langevin trajectories to compute nonequilibrium correlation functions. They compare the quantum results to classical field theory simulations under identical initial conditions and lattice regularization.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: $8^3 \times 20$ with anisotropy parameter $\gamma = 4$ (where $\gamma = a / a_t$)
- Langevin step size: $\epsilon = 10^{-5}$ (in units of $a^2$), with $10^6$ updatings requiring approximately 2 hours of vector processor time
- Free field mass: $\hat{m} = 2.315$ (in lattice units), showing correct period $2\pi\gamma/\hat{m}$ for the correlator
- Interacting theory: coupling $\lambda = 1$ and $\hat{m} = 0$, with connected correlator $G(\hat{t})$ exhibiting finite damping time
- Regularization: small imaginary mass of about $10^{-4}/a$ used to suppress unstable Langevin dynamics

## Context
This work extends stochastic quantization techniques, previously applied to Euclidean field theories with non-real actions by Ambjorn (1986) and Karsch (1985), to nonequilibrium quantum field theory in Minkowski space-time for the first time. It builds on analytical advances using n-particle irreducible functional integral techniques by Berges (2004) and provides a direct numerical method to test those approximations. The approach enables first-principles simulations of far-from-equilibrium dynamics relevant to heavy-ion collisions in QCD, ultra-cold quantum gases, and other strongly coupled quantum many-body systems, with potential extensions to non-Abelian gauge theories and two-component scalar fields for Bose condensates.
