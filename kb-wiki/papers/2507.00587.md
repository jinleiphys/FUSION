# Optimized binning for response function reconstruction via Chebyshev expansions

> We propose an optimized histogram binning strategy to reconstruct nuclear response functions via the Chebyshev expansion bound-state method. Our approach employs a stochastic regularization of the density of states to define adaptive, equal-area bins. Using the deuteron solved in a harmonic-oscillator basis with a chiral interaction, we benchmark on dipole and longitudinal responses, obtaining excellent agreement with exact theory and experiment. This general framework readily extends to other many-body systems and opens the door to new ab initio calculations of lepton-nucleus cross sections in medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a binning strategy for reconstructing nuclear response functions via Chebyshev expansions that uses a stochastic regularization of the density of states to define adaptive, equal-area bins, but the method is benchmarked only on the deuteron (a two-body system) and the authors note that the approach requires careful tuning of the kernel resolution parameter \(\lambda\) and that computational constraints may limit resolution in heavier systems.

## Method

The authors compute the Gaussian integral transform (GIT) of the response function using Chebyshev polynomial expansions of the Hamiltonian, then reconstruct a histogram of the response by binning the GIT. To determine optimal bin edges, they estimate a regularized density of states (DOS) via a stochastic sampling approach: they draw random pivot states from a Rademacher distribution (each coefficient \(\xi_j = \pm 1\) with equal probability), compute Chebyshev moments for these randomized states, and reconstruct the DOS from the averaged moments. Bin edges are placed at local minima of this regularized DOS, or by equal-area partitioning when minima are unresolved.

## Key numbers

* Kernel resolution \(\lambda = 0.025\) MeV used for the E1 response reconstruction with \(N_{\text{mom}} = 6000\) moments.
* Kernel resolution \(\lambda = 0.05\) MeV used for the high-\(q\) longitudinal response (\(q = 441\) MeV) with \(N_{\text{mom}} = 6000\) moments.
* For the longitudinal response at \(q = 20\) MeV, multipoles 0 through 4 were included; at \(q = 441\) MeV, multipoles 0 through 6 were included.
* For the E1 response, model spaces with \(N_{\text{max}} = 100\) and \(200\) and oscillator frequencies \(\hbar\Omega = 8\) and \(15\) MeV were tested; the binning reconstruction reproduced experimental data with "negligibly small" reconstruction uncertainties.
* The regularized DOS estimation used \(\lambda = 0.5\) MeV and \(N_{\text{draw}} = 2000\) Rademacher samples for the binning demonstration in Fig. 2.

## Context

This work extends the Chebyshev expansion histogram method introduced by Sobczyk et al. (2022) for reconstructing response functions without ill-posed integral transform inversion. It provides a systematic binning strategy to replace ad hoc bin choices, benchmarked on the deuteron where full diagonalization is feasible. The approach is designed to be compatible with many-body methods such as the no-core shell model, coupled-cluster theory, in-medium similarity renormalization group, and self-consistent Green's function methods, and aims to enable ab initio calculations of lepton-nucleus cross sections in medium-mass nuclei.
