# Calculation of fission observables through event-by-event simulation

> The increased interest in more exclusive fission observables has demanded more detailed models. We present here a new computational model, FREYA, that aims to meet this need by producing large samples of complete fission events from which any observable of interest can then be extracted consistently, including arbitrary correlations. The various model assumptions are described and the potential utility of the model is illustrated by means of several novel correlation observables.

## Key claim

FREYA (Fission Reaction Event Yield Algorithm) is a new Monte Carlo model that generates complete fission events event-by-event, enabling the consistent extraction of any observable including arbitrary correlations, but the authors explicitly state that the present preliminary version involves "a number of simplifying approximations" and results "cannot be expected to be numerically accurate."

## Method

FREYA first selects the mass and charge partition of the fissioning nucleus from empirical five-Gaussian fits to product mass distributions and a normal charge distribution, then determines the scission configuration using two coaxial spheroidal prefragments with a specified tip separation. The total excitation energy is partitioned between the two fragments according to their Fermi-gas level densities, with thermal fluctuations sampled from normal distributions, and the Coulomb repulsion between deformed prefragments is converted entirely into relative kinetic energy. Post-fission de-excitation proceeds by iterating statistical neutron evaporation using a Weisskopf spectrum until no further neutron emission is energetically possible, followed by statistical photon emission with a minimum energy threshold of 200 keV.

## Key numbers

- For thermal-neutron induced fission of ²³⁵U: mean total neutron multiplicity = 2.47; for ²³⁹Pu: mean total neutron multiplicity = 2.96
- Correlation coefficient between neutron multiplicities from light and heavy fragments: C_LH = -0.12 for ²³⁵U, C_LH = -0.19 for ²³⁹Pu
- Mean kinetic energy of neutrons from either fragment for ²³⁹Pu: 1.98 MeV (all multiplicities); for ²³⁵U: 1.84 MeV (all multiplicities)
- Charge dispersion σ_Z used: 0.40 for ²³⁶U(n,f) and 0.50 for ²³⁹Pu(n,f), from Reisdorf et al.
- Photon energy cutoff threshold: ε_γ^min = 200 keV
- Mean neutron kinetic energy from Weisskopf spectrum: ⟨ε_n⟩ = 2T_f^max with variance 2(T_f^max)²
- Mean photon energy from statistical emission: ⟨ε_γ⟩ = 3T_f with variance 3T_f²

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo fission simulation approach of Lemaire et al. (2005), who simulated statistical decay of fission fragments from ²⁵²Cf spontaneous fission and thermal ²³⁵U fission, by providing a more complete framework that includes both neutron and photon emission from arbitrary fissioning nuclei at specified excitation energies. FREYA enables the study of novel correlation observables that could not be addressed with earlier codes focused on more inclusive quantities, such as the anticorrelation between neutron multiplicities from light and heavy fragments and the dependence of fragment kinetic energy on neutron multiplicity. The model is currently limited to lower energies (such as thermal fission) because it ignores pre-fission neutron emission (n-th chance fission), and the authors note that well-tuned existing codes remain more reliable for common observables like average multiplicities and spectra.
