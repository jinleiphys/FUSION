# Relativistic spin hydrodynamics with momentum and spin-dependent relaxation time

> Using the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) along with the theory of semi-classical spin, we develop a framework of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics such that the relaxation time can depend on the momenta and spin of the constituent spin-1/2 particles. We also consider a general definition of the fluid four-velocity allowing the theory to be valid in a general frame and matching conditions. Consequently, we construct the frame-invariant bulk, shear, particle diffusion, and spin transport coefficients, showing that the evolution of fluid remains unaffected by spin in the limit of small polarization as was the case where the relaxation time was independent of spin or momentum.

## Key claim

Using the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) with a semi-classical treatment of spin, the paper develops a relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics where the relaxation time can depend on both momentum and spin of spin-1/2 particles; the framework shows that the evolution of the fluid remains unaffected by spin in the limit of small polarization, even when the relaxation time depends on spin or momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation using the Chapman-Enskog-like iterative expansion to first order in spacetime gradients, employing the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) with a relaxation time that can depend on spacetime, momentum, and spin. They adopt a general definition of the fluid four-velocity without specifying frame or matching conditions a priori, and construct the out-of-equilibrium distribution function for spin-1/2 particles in an extended phase-space that includes spin degrees of freedom. The frame-invariant bulk, shear, particle diffusion, and spin transport coefficients are then determined from the entropy production.

## Key numbers

- The spin integral measure includes the Casimir operator eigenvalue \(\mathfrak{s}^2 = 3/4\) for spin-1/2 particles.
- The equilibrium distribution is expanded to linear order in the spin polarization tensor \(\omega^{\mu\nu}\), keeping terms only up to \(\mathcal{O}(\omega^2)\).
- The relaxation time is parameterized as \(\tau_{\rm R}(x,p,s) = \tau_{\rm eq}(x,p) (u\cdot s)^{2\ell}\) where \(\ell\) is a positive integer.
- The equilibrium number density is given by \(n_0 = 2 I_{10}^-\), the energy density by \(\varepsilon_0 = 2 I_{20}^+\), and the pressure by \(P_0 = -2 I_{21}^+\).

## Context

This work extends prior formulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics from kinetic theory (Florkowski 2018, Bhadury 2020) by incorporating momentum and spin dependence of the relaxation time, which earlier treatments treated as independent of such particle properties. It builds on the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) developed by Dash (2021) and Biswas (2022) to resolve inconsistencies in the Anderson-Witting approximation when relaxation time depends on momentum. The framework enables computation of frame-invariant spin transport coefficients for the first time, and addresses unresolved issues in heavy-ion collision phenomenology such as the "spin sign puzzle" of \(\Lambda\)-hyperon longitudinal polarization and the global polarization of vector mesons.
