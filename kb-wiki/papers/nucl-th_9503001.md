# GCM study of hexadecapole correlations in superdeformed $^{194}$Hg

> The role of hexadecapole correlations in the lowest superdeformed band of $^{194}$Hg is studied by self consistent mean field methods. The generator coordinate method with particle number projection has been applied using Hartree-Fock wave functions defined along three different hexadecapole paths. In all cases, the ground state is not significantly affected by hexadecapole correlations and the energies of the corresponding first excited hexadecapole vibrational states lie high in energy. The effect of rotation is investigated with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov method and a zero range density-dependent pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper finds that hexadecapole correlations in the superdeformed band of 194Hg are very weak, with the ground state not significantly affected and the first excited hexadecapole vibrational states lying high in energy (above 3 MeV), contrary to the hypothesis that such correlations could explain the observed ΔI=4 staggering in the dynamical moment of inertia.

## Method

The authors performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations with particle number projection using Hartree-Fock wave functions constrained along three different hexadecapole paths (Q44z, Q44x, and Q44x with constrained quadrupole moment). They also performed cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction to investigate the effect of rotation at spin I=42.

## Key numbers

- The HFBCS SD state energy is 1519.41 MeV, with hexadecapole moments Q44z = 0 fm4 and Q44x = 20000 fm4
- The gain in energy due to dynamical correlations for the collective lowest state is less than 1.0 MeV in all cases
- The first excited GCM state energies are: 4.02 MeV (Q44z path), 3.21 MeV (Q44x path), and 4.19 MeV (Q44x path with constrained quadrupole)
- The mass quadrupole moment Q20 does not vary by more than 1 barn in the whole considered range of Q44z values
- Overlaps between wave functions at the SD minimum and extreme hexadecapole deformations (E* ~ 4 MeV) vary between 0.1 and 0.3
- The shift in Q40z from approximately 38000 fm4 (I=0) to 35300 fm4 (I=42) is consistent with the shift in equilibrium Q44x

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean field studies of superdeformed nuclei in the A=190 mass region, specifically addressing the ΔI=4 staggering phenomenon observed experimentally in 194Hg by Cederwall (1994) and in other nuclei by Flibotte (1993). The results contradict phenomenological models by Hamamoto and Mottelson (1994) and others that assumed hexadecapole correlations could explain the staggering, and are consistent with earlier shell correction calculations by Ragnarsson (1994) and tilted axis cranking calculations by Frauendorf (1994) that found hexadecapole effects to be extremely small. The study enables future investigations of whether hexadecapole correlations might be enhanced by direct coupling between quadrupole and hexadecapole modes.
