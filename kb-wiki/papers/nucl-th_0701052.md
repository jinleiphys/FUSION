# Short-range correlations and neutrinoless double beta decay

> In this work we report on the effects of short-range correlations upon the matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay. We focus on the calculation of the matrix elements of the neutrino-mass mode of neutrinoless double beta decays of 48Ca and 76Ge. The nuclear-structure components of the calculation, that is the participant nuclear wave functions, have been calculated in the shell-model scheme for 48Ca and in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) scheme for 76Ge. We compare the traditional approach of using the Jastrow correlation function with the more complete scheme of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Our results indicate that the Jastrow method vastly exaggerates the effects of short-range correlations on the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim

The Jastrow correlation function method, as traditionally used in neutrinoless double beta decay calculations, vastly exaggerates the effects of short-range correlations on the nuclear matrix elements, reducing them by 30%–40%, whereas the more complete unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) yields only a 7%–16% reduction, suggesting that earlier calculations using the Jastrow method have considerably overestimated these effects.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear matrix elements for the neutrino-mass mode of neutrinoless double beta decay of ⁴⁸Ca using the nuclear shell model (OXBASH code with the FPBP two-body interaction in the pf shell) and for ⁷⁶Ge using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a model space of 1p-0f-2s-1d-0g-0h₁₁/₂ orbitals and the Bonn-A G-matrix interaction. Short-range correlations were incorporated by two methods: the traditional Jastrow correlation function and the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) with parameter sets derived from the Bonn-A and Argonne V18 potentials.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca, the bare total Gamow-Teller matrix element M_GT^(0ν) = -1.278; with Jastrow correlations it becomes -0.835 (≈35% reduction); with UCOM Bonn-A it becomes -1.150 (≈10% reduction); with UCOM AV18 it becomes -1.101 (≈14% reduction).
- For ⁴⁸Ca, the bare total Fermi matrix element M_F^(0ν) = 0.367; with Jastrow correlations it becomes 0.221 (≈40% reduction); with UCOM Bonn-A it becomes 0.324 (≈12% reduction); with UCOM AV18 it becomes 0.308 (≈16% reduction).
- For ⁷⁶Ge, the bare total matrix element (M_GT^(0ν) - (g_V/g_A)² M_F^(0ν)) = -8.328; with Jastrow correlations it becomes -5.811 (≈30% reduction); with UCOM Bonn-A it becomes -7.734 (≈7% reduction).
- For the 7⁺ intermediate state in ⁴⁸Ca, the Jastrow method reduces the Gamow-Teller matrix element from -0.150 to -0.029 (roughly a factor of 5 reduction), while UCOM produces only a 20%–30% reduction.

## Context

This work extends the debate on short-range correlations in neutrinoless double beta decay, contrasting the traditional Jastrow approach used by the Tuebingen group (2000s) and earlier work by Tomoda (1991) with the more modern UCOM method developed by Feldmeier (1998) and Neff (2003). The results contradict the large corrections reported in earlier Jastrow-based calculations (Tomoda 1991, Tuebingen group) and align with the smaller corrections found using the Horie-Sasaki method by Krmpotic (1992) and Barbero (1999). The study enables more reliable extraction of neutrino masses from experimental half-life limits by providing improved nuclear matrix elements, and the authors note that study of UCOM effects on heavier nuclei is in progress.
