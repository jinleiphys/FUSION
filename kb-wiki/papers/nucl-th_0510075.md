# Sigma Terms of Light-Quark Hadrons

> A calculation of the current-quark mass dependence of hadron masses can help in using observational data to place constraints on the variation of nature's fundamental parameters. A hadron's sigma-term is a measure of this dependence. The connection between a hadron's sigma-term and the Feynman-Hellmann theorem is illustrated with an explicit calculation for the pion using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations: in the vicinity of the chiral limit sigma_pi = m_pi/2. This truncation also provides a decent estimate of sigma_rho because the two dominant self-energy corrections to the rho-meson's mass largely cancel in their contribution to sigma_rho. The truncation is less accurate for the omega, however, because there is little to compete with an omega->rho+pi self-energy contribution that magnifies the value of sigma_omega by ~25%. A Poincare' covariant Faddeev equation, which describes baryons as composites of confined-quarks and -nonpointlike-diquarks, is solved to obtain the current-quark mass dependence of the masses of the nucleon and Delta, and thereby sigma_N and sigma_Delta. This "quark-core" piece is augmented by the "pion cloud" contribution, which is positive. The analysis yields sigma_N~60MeV and sigma_Delta~50MeV.

## Key claim

The sigma-terms of light-quark hadrons, which measure the current-quark mass dependence of hadron masses, are calculated using Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev equations; the analysis yields sigma_N approximately 60 MeV and sigma_Delta approximately 50 MeV, but these values include a positive "pion cloud" contribution added to a "quark-core" piece, and the truncation used is less accurate for the omega meson because a self-energy contribution magnifies sigma_omega by about 25 percent.

## Method

The pion sigma-term was calculated explicitly using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, illustrating the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. For the rho and omega mesons, meson-loop self-energy corrections (rho to pi pi, rho to omega pi, and omega to rho pi) were estimated and added to the rainbow-ladder results. For baryons, a Poincare covariant Faddeev equation describing nucleons and Deltas as composites of confined quarks and nonpointlike diquarks was solved to obtain the current-quark mass dependence of their masses, and a "pion cloud" contribution was then added to the quark-core result.

## Key numbers

- sigma_pi = 0.069 GeV from direct rainbow-ladder calculation (or 0.071 GeV from chiral-limit estimate using f_pi^0 = 0.088 GeV and condensate = (-0.241 GeV)^3 at zeta=1 GeV)
- sigma_rho^RL = 0.025 GeV from rainbow-ladder truncation; after including pi pi and omega pi self-energy corrections, sigma_rho^{RL+pi pi+omega pi} = 0.022 GeV
- sigma_omega^{RL+rho pi} = 0.032 GeV = 0.043 m_omega, an increase of less than 25 percent over the rainbow-ladder result
- Constituent-quark sigma-term sigma_Q = 6.2 MeV for the parametrisation used, with Euclidean constituent-quark mass M_{u,d}^E = 0.33 GeV and current-quark mass m = 5.08 MeV
- sigma_N approximately 60 MeV and sigma_Delta approximately 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of meson properties (Maris and Tandy, 1999; Maris and Roberts, 1997) and Faddeev equation models for baryons (Oettel et al., 2000) to compute sigma-terms needed for interpreting searches for variation of fundamental constants. The results enable constraints on variations of quark masses from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, the Oklo natural nuclear reactor data, quasar absorption spectra, and atomic clock measurements (Flambaum et al., 2004; Dmitriev et al., 2004; Shuryak, 2003). The analysis shows that the pion's sigma-term is approximately half its mass, reflecting its Goldstone boson nature, while the rho and omega mesons have much smaller sigma-term to mass ratios.
