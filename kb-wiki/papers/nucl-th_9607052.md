# Role of anharmonicities and non-linearities in heavy ion collisions. A microscopic approach

> Using a microscopic approach beyond RPA to treat anharmonicities, we mix two-phonon states among themselves and with one-phonon states. We also introduce non-linear terms in the external field. These non-linear terms and the anharmonicities are not taken into account in the "standard" multiphonon picture. Within this framework we calculate Coulomb excitation of 208Pb and 40Ca by a 208Pb nucleus at 641 and 1000MeV/A. We show with different examples the importance of the non-linearities and anharmonicities for the excitation cross section. We find an increase of 10 % for 208Pb and 20 % for 40Ca of the excitation cross section corresponding to the energy region of the double giant dipole resonance with respect to the "standard" calculation. We also find important effects in the low energy region. The predicted cross section in the DGDR region is found to be rather close to the experimental observation.

## Key claim

Using a microscopic approach beyond RPA that mixes two-phonon states among themselves and with one-phonon states, and includes non-linear terms in the external field, the authors find that the excitation cross section in the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) region increases by 10% for 208Pb and 20% for 40Ca compared to the "standard" multiphonon calculation, bringing theoretical predictions closer to experimental observations.

## Method

The authors start from a self-consistent RPA calculation with the SGII Skyrme interaction to define one-phonon states for each nucleus, selecting all natural-parity phonons with multipolarity lower than 4 that exhaust at least 5% of the energy-weighted sum rule. They then construct the residual interaction in the space of one- and two-phonon states using boson mapping techniques, diagonalize the internal Hamiltonian to obtain mixed eigenstates, and solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation in this subspace under a relativistic Coulomb excitation field to compute excitation probabilities and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb excited by 208Pb at 641 MeV/A, the excitation cross section in the DGDR energy region increases by 10% relative to the "standard" calculation.
- For 40Ca excited by 208Pb at 1000 MeV/A, the excitation cross section in the DGDR energy region increases by 20% relative to the "standard" calculation.
- The one-phonon basis for 208Pb includes states such as GDR_1 at 12.435 MeV (63% EWSR), GDR_2 at 16.662 MeV (17% EWSR), the low-lying 2+ state at 5.545 MeV (15% EWSR), and the low-lying 3- state at 3.464 MeV (21% EWSR).
- Energy shifts from anharmonicities for dipole 1- states range from -207 keV (GDR_1 ⊗ 2+) to +195 keV (3- ⊗ 2+), with typical shifts of a few hundred keV.
- The mixing coefficients of one-phonon components in two-phonon-dominated states are typically around 0.05, with a maximum around 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the independent multiphonon picture used in earlier calculations (e.g., Bertsch and Esbensen, 1996) by including both anharmonicities in the nuclear Hamiltonian and non-linear terms in the excitation operator, which were previously neglected. The approach addresses discrepancies between experimental data and standard calculations for Coulomb excitation of nuclei such as 136Xe on 208Pb (Schmidt et al., 1993) and 208Pb (Stroth and collaborators, 1995), where the disagreement ranged from about 10% to 60%. The results enable more accurate predictions for heavy-ion Coulomb excitation cross sections and provide a framework for studying multiphonon states beyond the harmonic approximation.
