# Magnetic catalysis and inverse catalysis for heavy pions

> We investigate the QCD phase diagram for nonzero background magnetic fields using first-principles lattice simulations. At the physical point (in terms of quark masses), the thermodynamics of this system is controlled by two opposing effects: magnetic catalysis (enhancement of the quark condensate) at low temperature and inverse magnetic catalysis (reduction of the condensate) in the transition region. While the former is known to be robust and independent of the details of the interactions, inverse catalysis arises as a result of a delicate competition, effective only for light quarks. By performing simulations at different quark masses, we determine the pion mass above which inverse catalysis does not take place in the transition region anymore. Even for pions heavier than this limiting value - where the quark condensate undergoes magnetic catalysis - our results are consistent with the notion that the transition temperature is reduced by the magnetic field. These findings will be useful to guide low-energy models and effective theories of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper determines the pion mass threshold above which inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) of the quark condensate no longer occurs in the transition region, finding that even for pions heavier than this limiting value, where the condensate undergoes magnetic catalysis, the transition temperature remains reduced by the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors performed first-principles lattice QCD simulations using tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action with three flavors of stout improved rooted staggered quarks on \(N_s^3 \times N_t\) lattices with \(N_s=24\) and \(N_t=6\). They varied the light quark mass from its physical value up to the strange quark mass, fixed the strange quark mass to physical, and computed the change in the renormalized light quark condensate \(\Delta\Sigma(B,T,m)\) at a fixed magnetic field \(eB_0=0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) along the pseudo-critical temperature trajectory \(T_c(m,B=0)\), interpolating in both magnetic field and temperature to correct for flux quantization.

## Key numbers

- The limiting quark mass at which IMC turns into MC is \(\widetilde{m} = 14.07(55)\, m_{\mathrm{phys}}\), corresponding to a limiting pion mass \(\widetilde{M}_\pi = 497(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At the physical point on \(N_t=6\) lattices, the pseudo-critical temperature at zero magnetic field is \(T_c(m_{\mathrm{phys}},B=0) = 149.9(9)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The chiral limit extrapolation gives \(T_c(m=0) = 138(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The magnetic field used throughout is \(eB_0 = 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The squared pion mass follows \(M_\pi^2 = M_0^2 \cdot (m/m_{\mathrm{phys}})\) with \(M_0 = 132.62(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of DElia et al. (2018) by using a larger set of pion masses and a different mass-independent scale-setting procedure to assess robustness of the qualitative picture. The results provide a quantitative benchmark for low-energy models and effective theories of QCD that attempt to reproduce inverse magnetic catalysis, such as those by Farias et al. (2014), Ayala et al. (2014), and others that required tuning model parameters as functions of the magnetic field.
