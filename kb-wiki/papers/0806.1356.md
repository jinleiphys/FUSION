# From Glasma to Quark Gluon Plasma in heavy ion collisions

> When two sheets of Color Glass Condensate collide in a high energy heavy ion collision, they form matter with very high energy densities called the Glasma. We describe how this matter is formed, its remarkable properties and its relevance for understanding thermalization of the Quark Gluon Plasma in heavy ion collisions. Long range rapidity correlations contained in the near side ridge measured in heavy ion collisions may allow one to directly infer the properties of the Glasma.

## Key claim

The Glasma, a coherent classical field formed immediately after the collision of two sheets of Color Glass Condensate (CGC), has properties that may allow direct inference from long-range rapidity correlations (the near side ridge) measured in heavy ion collisions, and its dynamics including plasma instabilities are essential for understanding the subsequent thermalization into a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP).

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, where classical fields with high occupation numbers couple stochastically to static light cone color sources. It describes the leading order (LO) computation of multi-particle production in the Glasma by solving classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations with initial conditions determined by matching solutions in the backward light cone, and discusses how next-to-leading order (NLO) quantum fluctuations and plasma instabilities (such as the Weibel instability) are incorporated via a factorization theorem that resums leading logarithms and leading temporal divergences.

## Key numbers

- Energy densities in the Glasma are estimated as ε ∼ 20–40 GeV/fm³ for saturation scales Q_S obtained from fits to HERA and fixed target e+A data.
- The saturation scale at RHIC is Q_S² ∼ 1–1.4 GeV²; estimates for the LHC are Q_S² ∼ 2.6–3.9 GeV².
- CGC model extrapolations to the LHC give dN/dη|_(η∼0) ≈ 1000–1400 charged particles.
- The initial transverse energy E_T ∼ Q_S is about 3 times larger than the final measured E_T.
- The nuclear saturation scale extracted from A+A multiplicity at RHIC agrees to ∼10% with the value extracted from fits to HERA data.
- The ridge spans Δη ∼ 2 units in pseudo-rapidity at STAR and Δη ∼ 3–4 units at PHOBOS.
- Unstable fluctuations grow as α_S exp(√(Q_S τ)), becoming comparable to the diluting longitudinal fields on time scales of order (1/Q_S) ln²(C/α_S).

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework (McLerran-Venugopalan model, 1994) and the JIMWLK/Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equations to describe the early-time dynamics of heavy ion collisions. It builds on prior work by Gelis, Lappi, and Venugopalan (2007-2008) on factorization in the Glasma and by Romatschke and Venugopalan (2006) on plasma instabilities. The paper enables quantitative connections between initial conditions from the CGC and subsequent hydrodynamic evolution, and provides a dynamical model for the near side ridge observed at RHIC (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS collaborations) that is distinct from other models (Hwa, 2008). Open questions include the complete NLO computation of gluon production, the resummation of instabilities to determine the spectrum Z̃[a(u)], and the matching of the Glasma stress-energy tensor to kinetic theory or hydrodynamics at late times.
