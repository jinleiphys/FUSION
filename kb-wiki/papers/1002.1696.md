# Pion in a Box

> The residual mass of the pion in a finite spatial box at vanishing quark masses, the mass gap, is computed with two flavors of dynamical clover fermions. The result is compared with predictions of chiral perturbation theory in the delta regime.

## Key claim

The residual pion mass (mass gap) in a finite spatial box at vanishing quark masses, computed with two flavors of dynamical clover fermions, agrees with the next-to-next-to-leading (NNL) order prediction of chiral perturbation theory in the delta regime, even though the lattice volumes studied barely enter the delta regime condition \(m_\pi L \leq 1\).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the Wilson gauge action and two flavors of mass-degenerate \(O(a)\) improved Wilson fermions (clover fermions) at two gauge couplings (\(\beta=5.29\) and \(5.40\)) on a range of lattice volumes from \(16^3\times 32\) to \(40^3\times 64\) with periodic (antiperiodic) boundary conditions in the spatial (temporal) direction. Pion masses were measured on each volume and fitted with an interpolating formula that connects the delta-regime mass gap to the \(O(p^4)\) chiral expansion in the \(p\) regime, allowing extraction of the residual pion mass in the chiral limit. The results were compared to the analytic predictions of chiral effective theory at leading (L), next-to-leading (NL), and next-to-next-to-leading (NNL) order from Leutwyler (1987), Hasenfratz and Niedermayer, and Hasenfratz (2009).

## Key numbers

* Residual pion mass \(m_\pi^{\rm res} = 172(9)\) MeV on the \(L=1.61\) fm lattice at \(\beta=5.40\) with \(L/a=24\)
* Residual pion mass \(m_\pi^{\rm res} = 144(15)\) MeV on the \(L=1.81\) fm lattice at \(\beta=5.29\) with \(L/a=24\)
* Residual pion mass \(m_\pi^{\rm res} = 119(12)\) MeV on the \(L=2.15\) fm lattice at \(\beta=5.40\) with \(L/a=32\)
* Residual pion mass \(m_\pi^{\rm res} = 54(14)\) MeV on the \(L=3.01\) fm lattice at \(\beta=5.29\) with \(L/a=40\)
* Pion decay constant in the chiral limit extracted from the largest lattice: \(F \equiv F_\pi|_{m_\pi=0}=78^{+14}_{-10}\) MeV
* Low-energy constant \(\bar{l}_3 = 4.2(2)\) from the average of fits across all volumes
* Lattice spacings: \(a=0.075\) fm at \(\beta=5.29\) and \(a=0.067\) fm at \(\beta=5.40\), set using \(r_0=0.467\) fm from the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Leutwyler (1987) and Hasenfratz and Niedermayer, who computed the energy levels of the chiral rotator in the delta regime, by providing the first direct lattice QCD computation of the mass gap with dynamical fermions. The results confirm the NNL order chiral perturbation theory prediction of Hasenfratz (2009) for volumes ranging from \(L/r_0 \approx 3.5\) to \(6.5\). The study enables future precise determinations of the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) without requiring renormalization of the axial vector current, though the authors note that the current precision is insufficient to reliably extract the low-energy constants \(\bar{l}_1\) and \(\bar{l}_2\).
