# Towards a new paradigm for heavy-light meson spectroscopy

> Since 2003 many new hadrons, including the lowest-lying positive-parity charm-strange mesons ${D_{s0}^*(2317)}$ and ${D_{s1}(2460)}$, were observed that do not conform with quark model expectations. It was recently demonstrated that various puzzles in the charm meson spectrum find a natural resolution, if the SU(3) multiplets for the lightest scalar and axial-vector states, amongst them the ${D_{s0}^*(2317)}$ and the ${D_{s1}(2460)}$, owe their existence to the nonperturbative dynamics of Goldstone-Boson scattering off $D_{(s)}$ and $D^*_{(s)}$ mesons. Most importantly the ordering of the lightest strange and nonstrange scalars becomes natural. In this work we demonstrate for the first time that this mechanism is strongly supported by the recent high quality data on the ${B^-\to D^+π^-π^- }$ provided by the LHCb experiment. This implies that the lowest quark-model positive-parity charm mesons, together with their bottom counterparts, if realized in nature, do not form the ground-state multiplet. This is similar to the pattern that has been established for the scalar mesons made from light up, down and strange quarks, where the lowest multiplet is considered to be made of states not described by the quark model. In a broader view, the hadron spectrum must be viewed as more than a collection of quark model states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nonperturbative dynamics of Goldstone-Boson scattering off charm mesons, which generates the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ as hadronic molecules, is strongly supported by recent high-quality LHCb data on $B^-\to D^+\pi^-\pi^-$, implying that the lowest quark-model positive-parity charm mesons do not form the ground-state multiplet if they exist in nature.

## Method

The authors employ unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UChPT) at next-to-leading order for coupled-channel Goldstone-Boson–charm-meson scattering, with parameters fixed to fully dynamical lattice QCD scattering lengths. They then construct the $S$-wave decay amplitude for $B^-\to D^+\pi^-\pi^-$ incorporating chiral symmetry constraints and coupled-channel unitarity, and fit the resulting angular moments to LHCb data using two free parameters for the $S$-wave plus phase parameters for known $P$- and $D$-wave resonances. The same framework is applied to $B_s^0 \to \bar{D}^0 K^- \pi^+$ data with one additional free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Predicted $D_{s0}^*$ mass: $2315^{+18}_{-28}$ MeV, compared to RPP value $2317.7\pm0.6$ MeV and LQCD value $2348^{+7}_{-4}$ MeV (Bali 2017)
- Predicted $D_{s1}$ mass: $2456^{+15}_{-21}$ MeV, compared to RPP value $2459.5\pm0.6$ MeV and LQCD value $2451\pm4$ MeV (Bali 2017)
- Predicted $B_{s0}^*$ mass: $5720^{+16}_{-23}$ MeV, consistent with LQCD value $5711\pm23$ MeV (Lang 2015)
- Predicted $B_{s1}$ mass: $5772^{+15}_{-21}$ MeV, consistent with LQCD value $5750\pm25$ MeV (Lang 2015)
- Lower $D_0^*$ pole: $(2105^{+6}_{-8}, 102^{+10}_{-11})$ MeV (mass, half-width); higher $D_0^*$ pole: $(2451^{+35}_{-26}, 134^{+7}_{-8})$ MeV; RPP lists $(2318\pm29, 134\pm20)$ MeV
- Lower $D_1$ pole: $(2247^{+5}_{-6}, 107^{+11}_{-10})$ MeV; higher $D_1$ pole: $(2555^{+47}_{-30}, 203^{+8}_{-9})$ MeV; RPP lists $(2427\pm40, 192^{+65}_{-55})$ MeV
- Best fit to $B^-\to D^+\pi^-\pi^-$ data yields $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}=1.7$ with $B/A = -3.6\pm 0.1$ and subtraction constant $a_A = 1.0\pm 0.1$
- Best fit to $B_s^0 \to \bar{D}^0 K^- \pi^+$ data yields $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}=1.6$ with $C/A=4.8^{+3.4}_{-1.7}$
- The $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ molecular component is larger than 70% based on the Weinberg compositeness criterion applied to the $DK$ scattering length from Liu (2012) and lattice QCD (Mohler 2013)

## Context

This work extends the UChPT framework developed by Liu (2012) and Albaladejo (2016), which resolved three longstanding puzzles in charm-meson spectroscopy: the low masses of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ relative to quark model predictions, the near-equality of their mass difference with the $D^*-D$ hyperfine splitting, and the inverted ordering of strange versus nonstrange scalar masses. The analysis contradicts the conventional quark model picture of Godfrey (1985) and instead parallels the pattern established for light scalar mesons ($f_0(500)$, etc.), where the lowest multiplet is not described by the quark model. The results enable future tests including lattice QCD studies at SU(3)-symmetric quark masses to detect the predicted sextet pole, and experimental searches for bottom-sector analogues at LHCb and Belle-II.
