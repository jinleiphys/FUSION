# Lattice QCD at finite temperature: some aspects related to chiral symmetry

> Out of the many exciting results obtained with the lattice approach to QCD under extreme conditions, I discuss a few selected items related to chiral symmetry: the chiral condensate as an approximate order parameter, meson screening masses, and masses of baryons and mesons, including D(s) mesons, when approaching the crossover from the hadronic side.

# Lattice QCD at finite temperature: some aspects related to chiral symmetry

## Key claim

The chiral condensate, meson screening masses, and hadron masses (including baryons and D(s) mesons) all show precursors to chiral symmetry restoration as the thermal crossover is approached from the hadronic side, but the results for meson groundstate masses rely on the assumption that spectral broadening can be ignored at low temperature, which may not be justified especially in the axial-vector channel.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results using staggered quarks at the physical point with continuum extrapolation for thermodynamics (Budapest-Wuppertal-Houston and HotQCD collaborations), and anisotropic Wilson-clover quark simulations with N_f=2+1 flavours, an anisotropy a_s/a_tau ~ 3.45, and a lattice cutoff a_tau^{-1} ~ 6 GeV for spectroscopy (FASTSUM collaboration). Screening masses are computed on thermodynamic lattices by integrating over Euclidean time, while hadron groundstate masses are extracted from temporal correlators on anisotropic lattices using the assumption of narrow spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature from chiral susceptibility: T_pc^chi = 158.0(6) MeV (Budapest-Wuppertal-Houston, 2020)
- Pseudocritical temperature from chiral susceptibility: T_pc^chi = 156.5(1.5) MeV (HotQCD, 2018)
- Extrapolation using Wilson-type quarks only yields T_pc = 158(3) MeV at the physical point (FASTSUM, 2020)
- FASTSUM Generation 2 ensembles: m_pi = 384(4) MeV, transition temperature 170(4) MeV
- FASTSUM Generation 2L ensembles: m_pi = 236(2) MeV, transition temperature 165(3) MeV
- Inflection-point temperatures for parity-doubling parameter R in lighter pion ensembles lie between 157 and 160 MeV

## Context

This plenary talk from Quark Matter 2022 reviews lattice QCD results extending the thermodynamic studies of Borsanyi (2010), HotQCD (2014), and others. The work on baryon parity doubling and meson masses by the FASTSUM collaboration (Aarts, 2015-2022) provides benchmarks for effective parity-doublet models used at finite density, such as those by Marczenko (2018), and for model predictions of thermal behaviour in mesonic channels, e.g. Jung (2016). The results enable comparison with effective model descriptions that are extended to regions of the QCD phase diagram where lattice QCD is not directly applicable, such as nonzero baryon density.
