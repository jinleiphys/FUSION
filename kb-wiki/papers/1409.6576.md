# Systematics of intermediate-energy single-nucleon removal cross sections

> There is now a large and increasing body of experimental data and theoretical analyses for reactions that remove a single nucleon from an intermediate-energy beam of neutron- or proton-rich nuclei. In each such measurement, one obtains the inclusive cross section for the population of all bound final states of the mass A-1 reaction residue. These data, from different regions of the nuclear chart, and that involve weakly- and strongly-bound nucleons, are compared with theoretical expectations. These calculations include an approximate treatment of the reaction dynamics and shell-model descriptions of the projectile initial state, the bound final states of the residues, and the single-particle strengths computed from their overlap functions. The results are discussed in the light of recent data, more exclusive tests of the eikonal dynamical description, and calculations that take input from more microscopic nuclear structure models.

# Systematics of intermediate-energy single-nucleon removal cross sections

## Key claim

The ratio of experimental to theoretical inclusive one-nucleon removal cross sections, \(R_s = \sigma_\text{exp}/\sigma_\text{th}\), shows a systematic dependence on the neutron-proton separation energy asymmetry parameter \(\Delta S\), with strongly-bound nucleon removal yielding suppressed \(R_s\) values as low as 0.24(3) and weakly-bound nucleon removal giving \(R_s\) values near unity; however, the theoretical cross sections depend on multiple inputs beyond spectroscopic factors, so the ratio does not directly probe any single nuclear structure quantity.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental inclusive single-nucleon removal cross sections from intermediate-energy beams (mostly 80-100 MeV/nucleon on a \(^9\)Be target, with some data at higher energies on carbon targets) and compared them to theoretical cross sections calculated using the eikonal reaction model with shell-model spectroscopic factors, Hartree-Fock constrained radial overlaps and optical potentials, and summed over all bound final states of the mass \(A-1\) residue. The comparison was quantified by the ratio \(R_s = \sigma_\text{exp}/\sigma_\text{th}\), plotted against the asymmetry parameter \(\Delta S\) (the difference between the effective separation energies of the removed and the other nucleon species).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{32}\)Ar neutron removal (\(S_n \approx 22\) MeV, \(\Delta S \approx +20\) MeV): \(R_s = 0.24(3)\).
- For \(^{15}\)C neutron removal (\(S_n = 1.22\) MeV, \(\Delta S \approx -20\) MeV): \(R_s\) near unity.
- For \(^{14}\)O proton removal on a carbon target at 305 MeV/nucleon: \(\sigma_\text{sp} = 27.76\) mb, \(\sigma_\text{exp} = 35(5)\) mb, \(C^2S = 1.55\), yielding \(R_s = 0.76(11)\).
- For \(^{10}\)C neutron removal at 1.6 GeV/nucleon (\(S_n = 21.28\) MeV): \(\sigma_\text{exp} = 21.4(17)\) mb, calculated \(\sigma_\text{sp} = 22.8\) mb, \(R_s = 0.49(4)\), consistent with the 120 MeV/nucleon value of 0.49(2) from Gribble (2012).
- For \(^{10}\)Be neutron removal and \(^{10}\)C neutron removal using variational Monte Carlo structure input: \(R_s^\text{VMC} = 1.00(5)\) and 0.75(4), respectively, compared to shell-model values of 0.69(4) and 0.49(2).

## Context

This work updates the earlier systematic compilation of Gade (2008) with new data from multiple facilities (NSCL, RIKEN, HIRFL, Berkeley) for nuclei including \(^{57}\)Ni, \(^{22}\)Mg, \(^{9}\)Li, \(^{9}\)C, \(^{10}\)Be, \(^{10}\)C, \(^{36}\)Ca, \(^{19,20}\)C, \(^{36,38,40}\)Si, \(^{28}\)Mg, and \(^{14}\)O. The observed suppression of \(R_s\) for well-bound nucleon removal extends the pattern seen in electron-induced proton knockout studies reviewed by Dickhoff (2004), while the near-unity \(R_s\) for weakly-bound nucleons suggests reduced correlation effects beyond the shell model. The results motivate further theoretical work using more microscopic structure models such as variational Monte Carlo (Pieper 2001) and coupled-cluster calculations (Jensen 2011), and call for higher-energy measurements (above 200 MeV/nucleon) to test the kinematic cutoff effects on the most strongly-bound removal cases.
