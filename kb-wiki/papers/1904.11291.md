# Neutrino-13C Cross Sections at Supernova Neutrino Energies

> We present neutrino capture cross sections on 13C at supernova neutrino energies, up to 50 MeV. For both charged-current and neutral-current reactions partial cross sections are calculated using statistical Hauser-Feschbach method. Coherent elastic neutrino scattering cross section for a 13C target is also provided.

## Key claim

The authors present neutrino-13C cross sections up to 50 MeV using the SFO shell-model Hamiltonian combined with the statistical Hauser-Feschbach method, extending their previous calculations that were limited to reactor energies. The cross sections are calculated with a 5% quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant and include all particle emission channels, but the results depend on the specific choice of the SFO Hamiltonian and its treatment of tensor forces.

## Method

The authors used the SFO (Suzuki-Fujimoto-Otsuka) shell-model Hamiltonian in a configuration space including up to 2ħω excitations with g_A^eff/g_A = 0.95 to calculate nuclear wave functions and transition strengths for 13C. Neutrino-nucleus reaction cross sections were evaluated using the multipole expansion of weak hadronic currents, and partial cross sections for photon and particle emission channels were calculated using the statistical Hauser-Feschbach method with branching ratios for neutron, proton, deuteron, α, 3He, 3H, and γ decay channels. Particle transmission coefficients were calculated by the optical model, and γ transmission coefficients were calculated with the Brink formula using E1 and M1 parameters from the RIPL-2 database.

## Key numbers

- Total charged-current cross section for ν_e + 13C at 50 MeV: 2.84 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (all channels included)
- Total charged-current cross section for ν_e + 13C at 10 MeV: 3.10 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- Proton knock-out cross section (dominant CC channel) at 50 MeV: 2.05 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- Proton knock-out cross section at 10 MeV: 1.14 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- Total coherent elastic scattering cross section for ν + 13C at 50 MeV: 3.74 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- Total coherent elastic scattering cross section at 10 MeV: 1.77 × 10^{-41} cm^2
- Neutral-current neutron emission cross section for ν + 13C at 50 MeV: 1.80532 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- Neutral-current neutron emission cross section for ν̄ + 13C at 50 MeV: 1.39820 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- Charged-current neutron emission threshold for 13C: 22.284 MeV (S_n = 20.064 MeV, Q = 2.220 MeV)
- Neutral-current neutron emission threshold for 13C: 4.946 MeV (equal to neutron separation energy)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of neutrino-13C cross sections at reactor energies (Suzuki 2012) to supernova-relevant energies up to 50 MeV, using the SFO Hamiltonian that improves upon the earlier Cohen-Kurath (1965) and Millener-Kurath (1975) Hamiltonians by properly including tensor force components. The results are relevant for carbon-based scintillator detectors such as Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz, BOREXINO, JUNO, and the proposed LENA experiment, where the 1.07% natural abundance of 13C becomes non-negligible in precision measurements. The calculated Standard Model cross sections for the 13C(ν̄, ν̄' n)12C* reaction leading to the 4.44 MeV state in 12C provide a baseline for investigating proposed beyond-Standard-Model explanations of the 5-7 MeV spectral distortion observed in short-baseline reactor experiments (Berryman 2018).
