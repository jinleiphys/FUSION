# Gluon correlations in the glasma

> The physics of the initial conditions of heavy ion collisions is dominated by the nonlinear gluonic interactions of QCD. These lead to the concepts of parton saturation and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). We discuss recent progress in calculating multi-gluon correlations in this framework, prompted by the observation that these correlations are in fact easier to compute in a dense system (nucleus-nucleus) than a dilute one (proton-proton).

## Key claim

Multi-gluon correlations in the glasma are easier to compute in a dense system (nucleus-nucleus) than in a dilute one (proton-proton), because the dominant contributions come from averaging over classical color charge configurations rather than from quantum loop effects, with the caveat that these formally classical correlations are physically quantum effects where the weak coupling is compensated by a large logarithm of the energy resummed into the probability distribution.

## Method

The paper derives multi-gluon correlations by first computing the single-gluon spectrum from the classical field configuration for a fixed configuration of color charges, then averaging over the probability distribution W_y[ρ] of those charges. For the multiplicity distribution, the calculation uses the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model Gaussian probability distribution and works in the linearized approximation where sources are parametrically strong in g but treated to lowest nontrivial order in ρ, reducing the problem to counting pairwise contractions of sources. The rapidity dependence is obtained from the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution, formulated as a Langevin equation in the space of Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- The gluon multiplicity is N ~ 1/α_s, where α_s is the strong coupling constant
- The parameter k in the negative binomial distribution is given by k ≈ (N_c^2 - 1) Q_s^2 S_⊥ / (2π), where N_c is the number of colors, Q_s is the saturation scale, and S_⊥ is the transverse area
- The mean multiplicity is n̄ = f_N (1/α_s) Q_s^2 S_⊥
- The k parameter increases with energy as k ~ Q_s^2 ~ √s^λ, observed in heavy ion data from √s = 62 GeV to 200 GeV by PHENIX
- The two-gluon correlation function C(p,q) is proportional to the product of four unintegrated gluon distributions, with three evaluated at the rapidity of one produced gluon and one at the other
- The ridge in Cu+Cu collisions was observed at √s = 62.4 GeV and in Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV, with the PHOBOS experiment observing correlations up to Δη ~ 4, later extended to Δη ~ 5
- The CMS ridge in proton-proton collisions vanishes for p_⊥, q_⊥ ≲ 1 GeV and p_⊥, q_⊥ ≳ 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework for initial conditions of heavy ion collisions, building on the glasma flux tube picture of Dumitru (2008) and Gavin (2008). The calculation of the negative binomial distribution parameter k from first principles provides a theoretical foundation for the phenomenological NBD fits used by UA5 (Alner, 1985; Ansorge, 1988) and ALICE (Aamodt, 2010). The rapidity-dependent correlation calculation enables comparison with PHOBOS data on the ridge in nucleus-nucleus collisions and with the recent CMS observation of a ridge in proton-proton collisions (Collaboration, 2010), suggesting that ridge-like effects are present already in the initial scattering process rather than arising solely from transverse flow.
