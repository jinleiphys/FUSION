# Chemical potentials of light hadrons and quarks from yield ratios of negative to positive particles in high energy $pp$ collisions

> We describe the transverse momentum spectra of $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ produced in proton-proton ($pp$) collisions at different collision energies by using a Tsallis-Pareto-type function, and obtain the yield ratios, $k_π$, $k_{K}$, and $k_{p}$, of negative to positive particles from the fitting results of transverse momentum spectra and the extracted normalization constants. The transverse momentum dependent and energy dependent chemical potentials of light hadrons ($π$, $K$, and $p$) and quarks ($u$, $d$, and $s$) in $pp$ collisions are then extracted successively from the yield ratios. The six types of transverse momentum dependent chemical potentials show the trend of being close to zero in low-$p_T$ region and away from zero in high-$p_T$ region. Meanwhile, the energy dependent chemical potentials seem to decrease slightly with the increase of the collision energy, and the limiting values of the six types of chemical potentials are zero at very high energy in $pp$ collisions, which confirms that the partonic interactions possibly play an important role at RHIC and LHC, especially at LHC.

## Key claim

The chemical potentials of light hadrons (pi, K, p) and quarks (u, d, s) extracted from yield ratios of negative to positive particles in pp collisions approach zero at high transverse momentum and at high collision energies, which the authors interpret as evidence that partonic interactions become dominant at LHC energies, though the extracted values show significant fluctuations and the method assumes thermal and chemical equilibrium in a system where such equilibrium is not guaranteed.

## Method

The authors fitted transverse momentum spectra of pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, and antiproton from pp collisions at six energies (62.4 GeV to 13 TeV) using a Tsallis-Pareto-type function to extract normalization constants. From these normalization constants they obtained yield ratios of negative to positive particles, then used thermal and statistical model relations together with a parametrized chemical freeze-out temperature to extract both transverse-momentum-dependent and center-of-mass-energy-dependent chemical potentials for hadrons and quarks.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature parametrized as T_ch = T_lim / (1 + exp[2.60 - ln(sqrt(s_NN))/0.45]) with T_lim = 0.164 GeV
- Most values of mu_pi, mu_K, mu_p, mu_u, mu_d, and mu_s are within +/- 50 MeV across all energies studied
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, all six chemical potentials focus around zero in the whole transverse momentum region
- Energy-dependent chemical potentials fitted with power-law functions: mu_pi = (5 +/- 2)(sqrt(s))^(-0.162 +/- 0.070) MeV, mu_K = (27 +/- 1)(sqrt(s))^(-0.352 +/- 0.054) MeV, mu_p = (198 +/- 2)(sqrt(s))^(-0.454 +/- 0.059) MeV, with sqrt(s) in GeV
- Quark chemical potential fits: mu_u = (61 +/- 2)(sqrt(s))^(-0.412 +/- 0.056) MeV, mu_d = (131 +/- 2)(sqrt(s))^(-0.668 +/- 0.116) MeV, mu_s = (37 +/- 4)(sqrt(s))^(-0.538 +/- 0.178) MeV
- Fitted temperature parameter T ranges from 0.117 GeV (pi+ at 200 GeV) to 0.243 GeV (p at 7 TeV)
- Normalization constant N0 for pi+ ranges from 1.042 (62.4 GeV) to 5.914 (7 TeV)

## Context

This work extends the study of chemical potentials from heavy-ion collisions (Au-Au, Pb-Pb) to the simpler pp collision system, using a method based on yield ratios previously applied by the same group (He, 2015; Wei, 2016). The results show similarity to those in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting universality in particle production as previously noted by Sarkisyan (2016, 2017) for multiplicity distributions. The approach avoids the sign problem in lattice QCD calculations for non-zero chemical potentials and provides a phenomenological method to study the QCD phase diagram across energies from RHIC to LHC.
