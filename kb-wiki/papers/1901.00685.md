# R-matrix method and the nonlocal nucleon optical potential

> The calculable R-matrix method is applied to solve the Schrödinger equation in the optical model (OM) analysis of the elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering using a nonlocal nucleon optical potential (OP). The phenomenological nonlocal nucleon OP proposed by Perey and Buck (PB), and the two recent versions of the PB parametrization were used in the present OM study of the elastic nucleon scattering on $^{27}$Al, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb targets at different energies. The comparison of the OM results given by the calculable $R$-matrix method with those given by other methods confirms that the calculable $R$-matrix method is an efficient tool for the OM study of the elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering using a nonlocal nucleon OP.

## Key claim

The calculable R-matrix method is shown to be an efficient and reliable alternative for solving the Schrodinger equation with a nonlocal nucleon optical potential in optical model analyses of elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering, producing results indistinguishable from those of three other established methods (NLOM, NLAT, DWBA98) across multiple target nuclei and energies up to 40 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied the calculable R-matrix method with a channel radius of 15 fm, using Lagrange-mesh basis functions and Gauss-Legendre quadrature to directly evaluate both local and nonlocal matrix elements without iterative procedures. They solved the radial integro-differential equation for elastic nucleon scattering on 27Al, 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb targets at energies from 14.6 to 40 MeV, using the nonlocal Perey-Buck (PB) potential, the Tian-Pang-Ma (TPM) potential, and their energy-dependent versions (PB-E and TPM-E). The OM results were compared with those from the NLOM, NLAT, and DWBA98 codes.

## Key numbers

- Channel radius a = 15 fm was chosen for all R-matrix calculations
- Elastic neutron scattering on 208Pb was studied at 14.6, 20, 26, 30.3, and 40 MeV using the PB potential
- Elastic neutron scattering on 27Al was studied at 18 and 26 MeV using PB, TPM, PB-E, and TPM-E potentials
- Elastic neutron scattering on 40Ca at 16.9 MeV, 48Ca at 16.8 MeV, and 90Zr at 24 MeV were analyzed
- Elastic proton scattering on 40Ca was studied at 21, 30, and 40 MeV using TPM and TPM-E potentials
- The PB potential parameters were originally fitted to neutron elastic scattering on 208Pb at 7.0 and 14.5 MeV
- The TPM potential was fitted to data on 32S, 56Fe, 120Sn, and 208Pb at energies of 8 to 30 MeV
- The energy-dependent PB-E and TPM-E potentials were fitted to neutron data on 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb at energies of 5 to 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the calculable R-matrix method, originally developed for nuclear resonant scattering with local potentials (Descouvemont, 2010), to nonlocal nucleon optical potentials in elastic scattering. It validates the method against three existing approaches: the iterative method used in NLOM (Perey and Buck, 1962) and NLAT (Titus et al., 2016), and the direct discretization method in DWBA98 (Raynal, 1998). The results enable future use of the R-matrix method with microscopic nonlocal folding-model potentials and application to nucleon radiative capture at astrophysical energies.
