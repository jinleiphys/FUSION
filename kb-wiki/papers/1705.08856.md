# Absence of jet quenching in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Medium effects on the production of high-$p_{\rm T}$ particles in nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions are generally quantified by the nuclear modification factor ($R_{\rm AA}$), defined to be unity in absence of nuclear effects. Modeling particle production including a nucleon-nucleon impact parameter dependence, we demonstrate that $R_{\rm AA}$ at midrapidity in peripheral AA collisions can be significantly affected by event selection and geometry biases. Even without jet quenching and shadowing, these biases cause an apparent suppression for $R_{\rm AA}$ in peripheral collisions, and are relevant for all types of hard probes and all collision energies. Our studies indicate that calculations of jet quenching in peripheral AA collisions should account for the biases, or else they will overestimate the relevance of parton energy loss. Similarly, expectations of parton energy loss in light-heavy collision systems based on comparison with apparent suppression seen in peripheral $R_{\rm AA}$ should be revised. Our interpretation of the peripheral $R_{\rm AA}$ data would unify observations for lighter collision systems or lower energies where significant values of elliptic flow are observed despite the absence of strong jet quenching.

## Key claim

Even in the complete absence of jet quenching and shadowing, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions can be significantly below unity due to event selection and geometry biases, meaning that observed suppression in peripheral collisions does not necessarily indicate parton energy loss.

## Method

The authors used two Monte Carlo models: HIJING (v1.383) and a custom toy model called HG-PYTHIA (Hpythia) that combines the HIJING Glauber initial state with PYTHIA (v6.28, Perugia 2011 tune) for particle production. Both models include a nucleon-nucleon impact parameter dependence for hard scatterings but turn off jet quenching and shadowing. The models were run for AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=0.2\) TeV and PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, with centrality selection based on charged particle multiplicity in pseudorapidity ranges matching the experimental data (3<|η|<4 for RHIC, 2.5<|η|<5 for LHC).

## Key numbers

- PHENIX data for 80-92% central AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=0.2\) TeV: constant fit to \(R_{\rm AA}\) between 3-17 GeV/\(c\) yields \(0.80\pm0.03\) with reduced \(\chi^2<1\)
- CMS data for 70-90% central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV: constant fit to \(R_{\rm AA}\) between 10-100 GeV/\(c\) yields \(0.74\pm0.02\) with reduced \(\chi^2<1\)
- For 50-70% centrality PbPb at 5.02 TeV, \(R_{\rm AA}\) exhibits a significant slope of about 0.003 GeV\(^2\)/\(c^2\), reaching \(R_{\rm AA}\sim1\) at around 125 GeV/\(c\)
- In HIJING, the average number of hard scatterings per NN collision is \(\langle N_{\rm hard}^{\rm NN}\rangle=1.77\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV and 0.28 at 0.2 TeV
- The geometry bias alone reaches 10-20% for most peripheral collisions; adding the selection bias reaches up to 75% (at 0.2 TeV) and 40% (at 5.02 TeV) for the most peripheral collisions
- Average number of binary collisions in the models for the peripheral classes: \(\langle N_{\rm coll}\rangle\approx4.1\) for AuAu at 0.2 TeV and 8.6 for PbPb at 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of selection biases previously studied in pPb collisions at the LHC by Adam (2014) to peripheral AA collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. The authors argue that calculations of parton energy loss in peripheral collisions, such as the transport coefficient extraction by Dainese (2004) which changes from 0.1 GeV\(^2\)/fm to 1 GeV\(^2\)/fm between peripheral and mid-central AuAu collisions, overestimate the suppression caused by parton energy loss if biases are not accounted for. The findings also imply that expectations for parton energy loss in light-heavy collision systems from Zhang (2013), Tywoniuk (2014), and Shen (2016) should be revisited. The interpretation unifies observations of large elliptic flow in peripheral AA collisions with the absence of strong jet quenching, consistent with low-energy heavy-ion results from Adamczyk (2016).
