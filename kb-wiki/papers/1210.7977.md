# Scalar susceptibilities and four-quark condensates in the meson gas within Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We analyze the properties of four-quark condensates and scalar susceptibilities in the meson gas, within finite temperature Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT). The breaking of the factorization hypothesis does not allow for a finite four-quark condensate and its use as an order parameter, except in the chiral limit. This is rigorously obtained within ChPT and is therefore a model-independent result. Factorization only holds formally in the large $N_c$ limit and breaks up at finite temperature even in the chiral limit. Nevertheless, the factorization breaking terms are precisely those needed to yield a finite scalar susceptibility, deeply connected to chiral symmetry restoration. Actually, we provide the full result for the SU(3) quark condensate to NNLO in ChPT, thus extending previous results to include kaon and eta interactions. This allows to check the effect of those corrections compared to previous approaches and the uncertainties due to low-energy constants. We provide a detailed analysis of scalar susceptibilities in the SU(3) meson gas, including a comparison between the pure ChPT approach and the virial expansion, where the unitarization of pion scattering is crucial to achieve a more reliable prediction. Through the analysis of the interactions within this approach, we have found that the role of the $σ$ resonance is largely canceled with the scalar isospin two channel interaction, leaving the $ρ(770)$ as the main contribution. Special attention is paid to the evolution towards chiral restoration, as well as to the comparison with recent lattice analysis.

## Key claim

The four-quark condensate cannot be used as an order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature because factorization breaking terms are divergent, except in the chiral limit or the large \(N_c\) limit; this is a model-independent result rigorously obtained within Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Method

The authors computed the four-quark scalar correlators and two-quark condensates in the meson gas within finite-temperature Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) for SU(3) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), including all relevant meson interactions (\(\pi, K, \eta\)). They then tested the factorization hypothesis by comparing the four-quark condensate to the square of the two-quark condensate, and derived the scalar susceptibilities from both the four-quark correlators and the mass derivatives of the condensates, also performing a virial expansion with unitarized pion scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The four-quark condensate factorization breaking term at NNLO in SU(3) is given by \(\frac{2}{9F^4}\left(3G^T_\pi(0)^2+4G^T_K(0)^2+G^T_\eta(0)^2\right)\) relative to the square of the two-quark condensate, where the thermal propagators \(G^T_i(0)\) contain divergent contributions.
* For the light four-quark condensate \(\langle (\bar q q)_l^2 \rangle_T\), the factorization breaking term is \(\frac{1}{2F^4}\left(3G^T_\pi(0)^2+G^T_K(0)^2+\frac{1}{9}G^T_\eta(0)^2\right)\) relative to \(\langle (\bar q q)_l \rangle_T^2\).
* The scalar susceptibility \(\chi(T)\) to \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) in ChPT counting is \(\chi(T) = B_0^2\left[ 24(12L^r_6(\mu)+2L^r_8(\mu)+H^r_2(\mu)) -12\nu_\pi -16\nu_K -4\nu_\eta + 6g_2(M_\pi,T) + 8g_2(M_K,T) + 2g_2(M_\eta,T) \right]\), where the \(g_2\) functions encode finite-temperature contributions.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature analysis of four-quark condensate factorization by Gomez Nicola, Pelaez, and Ruiz de Elvira (2010) to finite temperature, showing that the factorization breaking persists and is divergent, contradicting assumptions made in QCD sum rules (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1979) and in proposals for order parameters by Leupold (2005, 2006). It also provides the full SU(3) thermal quark condensate to NNLO, extending earlier ChPT calculations by Gerber and Leutwyler (1988) that treated kaons and etas as free fields. The results enable a rigorous comparison with lattice QCD analyses of chiral restoration and provide a baseline for testing the role of the \(\rho(770)\) resonance versus the \(\sigma\) resonance in scalar susceptibilities.
