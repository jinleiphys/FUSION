# RHIC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV hadron yields and the isospin dependent equation of state

> The statistical hadronization model has been successful in extracting information at chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions. At RHIC, with a collision energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, many different ion species have been used for $A$+$A$ collisions. This allows for a scan across the charge fraction $Y_Q=Z/A$, where $Z$ is the proton number and $A$ is the baryon number. We first make predictions for $A$+$A$ collisions that do not yet have published experimental data on hadron yield ratios (O+O, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr). We then use both the experimental and predicted yield ratios to perform thermal fits across $Y_Q$, enabling us to extract $s/n_B$ and other thermodynamic information at chemical freeze-out. Using the relation between $s/n_B$ and $Y_Q$, we can calculate a new constraint on the finite temperature equation of state at finite densities. We discuss implications of this constraint and propose future runs that can help connect to the equation of state relevant for neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
Using existing and predicted hadron yield ratios across six collision systems at RHIC, the authors extract the second derivative of entropy per baryon number with respect to isospin asymmetry at fixed temperature, obtaining a negative value that provides a new constraint on the finite-temperature equation of state relevant for neutron star mergers. The result is limited by the fact that the symmetric nuclear matter point (O+O) relies on predicted rather than experimental yield ratios.

## Method
The authors use the Hadron Resonance Gas model within the Thermal-FIST package to perform minimum chi-squared fits to hadron yield ratios (pi-/pi+, K-/K+, antiproton/proton, K-/pi-, antiproton/pi-) from Cu+Cu, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. They then fit polynomial functions to the Y_Q dependence of these ratios to predict yield ratios for O+O, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr, and use all six systems to extract freeze-out parameters and thermodynamic quantities including s/n_B as a function of charge fraction Y_Q.

## Key numbers
- The second derivative of s/n_B with respect to isospin asymmetry delta at delta=0 and T approximately 145 MeV is -0.183_{-0.166}^{+0.128} x 10^5
- Freeze-out temperature is approximately 145 MeV across all ion species at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The charge fraction Y_Q range covered is [0.387, 0.5] across the six collision systems (U+U: 0.387, Au+Au: 0.399, Zr+Zr: 0.417, Ru+Ru: 0.458, Cu+Cu: 0.460, O+O: 0.500)
- The saturation density used for baryon density normalization is n_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the approach of Mroczek (2024) who showed that the dense matter equation of state can be explored through an expansion in temperature and isospin asymmetry, and is consistent with theoretical calculations from Alford (2022) in the regime relevant to neutron star mergers. The results demonstrate that experimental hadron yield data for O+O collisions would provide the most important constraints for taking the delta to 0 limit, and the authors propose future runs at CBM at FAIR and at the LHC using different ion species to map the derivative across a wider region of the phase diagram.
