# Shell-model calculations for p-shell hypernuclei

> The interpretation of hypernuclear gamma-ray data for p-shell hypernuclei in terms of shell-model calculations that include the coupling of Lambda- and Sigma-hypernuclear states is briefly reviewed. Next, Lambda 8Li, Lambda 8Be, and Lambda 9Li are considered, both to exhibit features of Lambda-Sigma coupling and as possible source of observed, but unassigned, hypernuclear gamma rays. Then, the feasibility of measuring the ground-state doublet spacing of Lambda 10Be, which, like Lambda 9Li, could be studied via the (K-,pi0 gamma) reaction, is investigated. Structural information relevant to the population of states in these hypernuclei in recent (e,e'K+) studies is also given. Finally, the extension of the shell-model calculations to sd-shell hypernuclei is briefly considered.

# Shell-model calculations for p-shell hypernuclei

## Key claim

The shell-model framework that includes explicit coupling between Lambda- and Sigma-hypernuclear states can correlate a large body of hypernuclear gamma-ray data with relatively few YN parameters, but the need for different Lambda-N spin-spin interaction strengths at either end of the p-shell and a stronger enhancement of nuclear vector interaction terms near mid-shell remain unresolved problems.

## Method

Shell-model calculations start from a weak-coupling basis where a hyperon (Lambda or Sigma) is coupled in angular momentum and isospin to eigenstates of a p-shell Hamiltonian for the core nucleus, using empirical core energies from experiment where available. The Lambda-N effective interaction is parametrized by five radial integrals (overline{V}, Delta, S_Lambda, S_N, and T) corresponding to central, spin-spin, symmetric spin-orbit, antisymmetric spin-orbit, and tensor components, with the Lambda-Sigma coupling interaction treated via analogous parameters that connect core states through Fermi and Gamow-Teller type matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state doublet spacing in Lambda-12-C: 161 keV, established from direct observation of the 161 keV gamma-ray and from transitions from an excited 1- state at 2832 keV
- Ground-state doublet spacing in Lambda-10-B: less than 100 keV (experimental limit)
- Lambda-7-Li ground-state doublet: calculated 693 keV, experimental 692 keV
- Lambda-7-Li excited-state doublet (7/2+ to 5/2+): calculated 494 keV, experimental 471 keV
- Lambda-9-Be doublet (3/2+ to 5/2+): calculated 44 keV, experimental 43 keV
- Lambda-11-B ground-state doublet (7/2+ to 5/2+): calculated 267 keV, experimental 264 keV
- Lambda-11-B excited-state doublet (3/2+ to 1/2+): calculated 475 keV, experimental 505 keV
- Lambda-12-C ground-state doublet (2- to 1-): calculated 153 keV, experimental 161 keV
- Lambda-15-N excited-state doublet (3/2+_2 to 1/2+_2): calculated 507 keV, experimental 481 keV
- Lambda-16-O ground-state doublet (1- to 0-): calculated 23 keV, experimental 26 keV
- Lambda-16-O excited-state doublet (2- to 1-_2): calculated 248 keV, experimental 224 keV
- Lambda-8-Li ground-state doublet predicted spacing: approximately 442 keV (tentatively associated with a 442.1(21) keV observed gamma-ray)
- Lambda-8-Li 1- ground state energy shift from Lambda-Sigma coupling: 160 keV
- Lambda-8-He ground-state doublet predicted spacing: 101 keV, with Lambda-Sigma coupling contributions of 154 and 182 keV to the 1- and 2- states respectively
- Lambda-5-He s_Lambda single-particle energy: 3.12 MeV (used as reference for binding energy calculations)
- Lambda-19-F predicted doublet spacings (without Lambda-Sigma coupling): 305 keV for state based on lowest 1+ core state, 196 keV for state based on lowest 3+ core state

## Context

This work extends the shell-model interpretation of gamma-ray transitions in p-shell hypernuclei previously reviewed by Millener (2008) and Tamura (2008), which covered 22 gamma-ray transitions in hypernuclei from A=7 to A=16. New results from KEK E566 using the Hyperball2 detector array (reported by Tamura and Ma at the Hyp-X conference) established the Lambda-12-C ground-state doublet spacing as 161 keV. The calculations use refined p-shell interactions building on the Cohen and Kurath (1965) interactions, with explicit Lambda-Sigma coupling following the approach of Gal, Soper, and Dalitz (1978) and using G-matrix calculations by Akaishi (2000) for the NSC97e,f interactions (Rijken 1999). The work enables interpretation of ongoing and planned experiments including (e,e'K+) studies at JLab (Cusanno 2010, Tang 2012) and (K-,pi0 gamma) studies at J-PARC (Tamura 2012), and provides a foundation for extending shell-model calculations to sd-shell hypernuclei such as Lambda-19-F.
