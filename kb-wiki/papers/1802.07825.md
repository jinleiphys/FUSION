# Gauge Invariant Noether's Theorem in Yang-Mills Theory

> The gauge invariant definition of the spin dependent gluon distribution function from first principle is necessary to study the proton spin crisis at high energy colliders. In this paper we derive the gauge invariant Noether's theorem in Yang-Mills theory by using combined Lorentz transformation plus local non-abelian gauge transformation. We find that the definition of the gauge invariant spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field does not exist in Yang-Mills theory although the definition of the gauge invariant spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the quark exists. We show that the gauge invariant definition of the spin angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field in the literature is not correct because of the non-vanishing boundary surface term in Yang-Mills theory. We also find that the Belinfante-Rosenfeld tensor in Yang-Mills theory is not required to obtain the symmetric and gauge invariant energy-momentum tensor of the quark and the Yang-Mills field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a gauge-invariant definition of the spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field does not exist in Yang-Mills theory, contrary to some claims in the literature, because of a non-vanishing boundary surface term; however, a gauge-invariant definition of the spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the quark does exist.

## Method

The author derives a gauge-invariant Noether's theorem in Yang-Mills theory by applying a combined Lorentz transformation plus a local non-abelian gauge transformation, rather than using the Lorentz transformation alone. This combined transformation is applied to the Yang-Mills field and to the quark field separately, and the resulting conserved currents are used to extract the gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor and angular momentum tensor for each sector. The derivation explicitly shows that the gauge-invariant spin angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field vanishes because the functional differential of the field becomes proportional to the field strength tensor, leaving no spin term.

## Key numbers

* The combined experimental data predict that the sum of quark, antiquark, and gluon spins inside the proton contributes about 50% of the proton spin, i.e., \(S_q + S_{\bar q} + S_g \sim \frac{1}{4}\) (where the proton spin is \(\frac{1}{2}\)).
* The gauge-invariant angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field is given by \(\vec{J}_g = \int d^3x \, \vec{r} \times [\vec{E}^b(x) \times \vec{B}^b(x)]\), with no separate spin or orbital term.
* The gauge-invariant angular momentum of the quark plus Yang-Mills field is \(\vec{J}_{q+g} = \int d^3x \, [\vec{r} \times (\vec{E}^b \times \vec{B}^b) + \vec{r} \times (\psi^\dagger [-i\overrightarrow{D} + i\overleftarrow{D}] \psi) + \psi^\dagger \vec{\Sigma} \psi]\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivation of a gauge-invariant Noether's theorem in Dirac-Maxwell theory (Nayak 2018) to the non-abelian case of Yang-Mills theory. It directly contradicts prior attempts to define a gauge-invariant gluon spin angular momentum, such as those reviewed by Jaffe (1996) and others, by showing that the boundary surface term in Yang-Mills theory prevents such a definition. The result enables a first-principles approach to the proton spin crisis by clarifying that only the total angular momentum of the Yang-Mills field (not its spin or orbital parts separately) is gauge invariant, and that the Belinfante-Rosenfeld tensor is unnecessary for obtaining a symmetric, gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor.
