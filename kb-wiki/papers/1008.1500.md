# The observed charmed hadron $Λ_c(2940)^+$ and the $D^*N$ interaction

> In this work, we systematically study the interaction of $D^*$ and nucleon, which is stimulated by the observation of $Λ_c(2940)^+$ close to the threshold of $D^*p$. Our numerical result obtained by the dynamical investigation indicates the existence of the $D^*N$ systems with $J^P=1/2^\pm,\,3/2^\pm$, which not only provides valuable information to understand the underlying structure of $Λ_c(2940)^+$ but also improves our knowledge of the interaction of $D^*$ and nucleon. Additionally, the bottom partners of the $D^*N$ systems are predicted, which might be as one of the tasks in LHCb experiment.

## Key claim

The dynamical one-boson-exchange (OBE) model study of the $D^*N$ system supports assigning the observed $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ as a $D^*N$ molecular state with either $I(J^P)=0(1/2^+)$ or $0(3/2^-)$, provided the cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ is taken as 1.17 GeV or 1.09 GeV respectively to reproduce the approximately -6 MeV binding energy relative to the $D^*p$ threshold.

## Method

The authors derived the effective potential for $D^*N$ scattering using the OBE model with $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ exchanges, employing effective Lagrangians from heavy quark effective theory and chiral symmetry. They constructed coupled-channel wave functions for $D^*N$ systems with $J^P = 1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm$ and solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation using the FESSDE program to find bound state solutions. The same formalism was then extended to the $\bar{B}^*N$ system as the bottom partner.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ mass: $2939.8 \pm 1.3 (\text{stat}) \pm 1.0 (\text{syst})$ MeV/c$^2$ and width $\Gamma = 17.5 \pm 5.2 (\text{stat}) \pm 5.9 (\text{syst})$ MeV from BaBar (Aubert 2006)
- $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ mass: $2938.0 \pm 1.3^{+2.0}_{-4.0}$ MeV/c$^2$ and width $\Gamma = 13^{+8+27}_{-5-7}$ MeV from Belle (Abe 2006)
- The $D^*p$ threshold lies approximately 6 MeV above the $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ mass
- Bound state solutions with binding energy less than 10 MeV were found for four $D^*N$ systems: $I(J^P)=0(3/2^-), 0(1/2^+), 1(1/2^-), 1(3/2^+)$
- For $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ as a $D^*N$ molecular state with -6 MeV binding energy, the required cutoff is $\Lambda = 1.17$ GeV for $I(J^P)=0(1/2^+)$ and $\Lambda = 1.09$ GeV for $I(J^P)=0(3/2^-)$
- The cutoff $\Lambda$ was varied in the region $0.8 \leq \Lambda \leq 1.2$ GeV
- Coupling constants used: $g = 0.59$, $\beta = 0.9$, $\lambda = 0.56$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g_s = g_\pi/(2\sqrt{6})$ with $g_\pi = 3.73$, $g^2_{\pi NN}/(4\pi) = 13.6$, $g^2_{\rho NN}/(4\pi) = 0.84$, $g^2_{\omega NN}/(4\pi) = 20$, $g^2_{\sigma NN}/(4\pi) = 5.69$, $\kappa = 6.1$ for $\rho$ and $\kappa = 0$ for $\omega$

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ previously proposed by He (2006) as an S-wave $D^{*0}p$ molecular state with $J^P=1/2^-$, and by Dong (2009) who studied strong decays and suggested $J^P=1/2^+$. The dynamical OBE calculation provides a systematic search across all $J^P$ channels up to $5/2^\pm$, finding that only $0(1/2^+)$ and $0(3/2^-)$ assignments can reproduce the observed binding energy. The prediction of bottom partners ($\bar{B}^*N$ systems) for LHCb searches follows from heavy quark symmetry, analogous to how the study of charmed baryon excitations by Capstick (1986) and Copley (1979) in potential models provided conventional baryon assignments.
