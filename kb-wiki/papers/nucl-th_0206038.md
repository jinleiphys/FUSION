# Interaction of $η$ mesons with a three-nucleon system

> A study of the $η$-3N interaction in the energy region near the $η$-^3H elastic scattering threshold is presented. The calculational scheme is based on the four-body scattering formalism. A manageable form of the integral equations is achieved by using a separable ansatz for the driving two-body $ηN$- and NN-forces as well as for the subamplitudes appearing in the (1+3) and (2+2) partitions of the $η$-3N system. Results presented for the $η$-^3H scattering length point to the existence of a virtual (antibound) s-wave $η$-^3H state. As a consequence, a large enhancement of the cross section for $η$-production from three-body nuclei is predicted.

# Interaction of η mesons with a three-nucleon system

## Key claim

The η-3N interaction is not strong enough to bind the system, contrary to some earlier claims; instead, a virtual (antibound) s-wave η-³H state exists near zero energy, which produces a large enhancement of η-production cross sections from three-body nuclei but does not constitute a true bound state.

## Method

The authors employed the four-body scattering formalism, reducing the integral equations to two-body form via the quasiparticle approach. Separable rank-one parametrizations with Hulthen form factors were used for the ηN and NN amplitudes, with only the S11(1535) resonance included in the ηN channel and only ¹S0 and ³S1 states in the NN sector. The Hilbert-Schmidt method provided separable representations for the integral kernels in the (1+3) and (2+2) partitions of the η-3N system.

## Context

This work extends the four-body scattering formalism to η-3N interactions, contradicting earlier finite-rank-approximation results (Rakitin 1996) and simple optical model calculations (Wilkin 1993, Trs97) that predicted bound states for modest ηN scattering lengths. The results agree qualitatively with experimental data from May (1996) showing enhanced η-production near threshold, but the calculation fails to reproduce the ³He(π⁻,η)³H cross section data from Peng (1989), attributed to oversimplified treatment of the nuclear wave function with a Yamaguchi NN-potential that has too much attraction at high momenta. The work enables future refinements using more realistic NN-potentials that include short-range repulsion.
