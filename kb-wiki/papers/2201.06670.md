# Family of New Exact Solutions for Longitudinally Expanding Ideal Fluids

> We report on the discovery of new analytical solutions of the equations of relativistic ideal hydrodynamics. In this solution, the fluid expands in the longitudinal direction and contains a plateau structure that extends over a finite range in rapidity and can be either symmetric or asymmetric in that variable. We further calculate the corresponding pseudo-rapidity distribution of hadron yields, and find decent agreement with experimental measurements in high-energy Pb+Pb, Au+Au, p+Pb, and d+Au collisions.

## Key claim
The paper reports a new family of exact analytical solutions to 1+1D relativistic ideal hydrodynamics that are not boost-invariant, can be either symmetric or asymmetric in rapidity, and produce pseudorapidity distributions that agree reasonably well with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions, but only after applying a Gaussian smearing kernel to account for resonance decay and hadron scattering effects not included in the ideal-fluid calculation.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations in Milne coordinates for a longitudinally expanding fluid with no transverse flow, using a conformal equation of state \(p = c_s^2 \varepsilon\). They impose the ansatz that the fluid rapidity \(\xi\) and the logarithm of the energy density \(\ln\varepsilon\) each separate into a sum of a function of \(x^+\) and a function of \(x^-\) (light-cone coordinates), which corresponds to separating target and projectile contributions. They then derive the family of solutions (Eqs. 1–3) parameterized by \(t_0\) (controlling rapidity width), \(a\) (controlling asymmetry), and \(\tau_0\) (time scale). The hadron pseudorapidity distribution is computed via the Cooper–Frye freeze-out formula on an isothermal hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- The solution reduces to the Bjorken flow when \(a=1\) and \(t_0=0\).
- The asymmetry parameter \(a\) is constrained to the range \(\sqrt{(1-c_s^2)/(1+c_s^2)} \leq a \leq \sqrt{(1+c_s^2)/(1-c_s^2)}\) to ensure convergence of the energy density.
- For comparisons with experimental data, the authors use \(\tau_\mathrm{ini} = 0.4\ \mathrm{fm}/c\), \(T_f = 145\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(t_0 = 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}/c\).
- The ratio \(T_\mathrm{ini}/T_f\) is set to 2.0 (p+Pb), 2.0 (d+Au), 2.2 (Pb+Pb), and 1.9 (Au+Au).
- The asymmetry parameter is set to \(a=1.05\) for asymmetric systems and \(a=1.00\) for symmetric systems.
- The pseudorapidity distribution width is given by \(w \approx 2\ln(\tau_\mathrm{ini}/t_0) + 6\ln(T_\mathrm{ini}/T_f)\).
- The slope at midrapidity is \(\mathrm{d}^2 N_{ch}/\mathrm{dy}_p^2|_{\mathrm{y}_p=0} \approx [(1-a)/2] \cdot \mathrm{d}N_{ch}/\mathrm{dy}_p|_{\mathrm{y}_p=0}\).

## Context
This work extends the family of analytical solutions for longitudinally expanding ideal fluids beyond the boost-invariant Bjorken flow (Bjorken 1983) and the symmetric rapidity-profile solutions of Csörgő, Nagy, and Csanád (2006). It also generalizes the Bjorken solution by introducing a finite time shift \(t_0\) that produces a rapidity-dependent profile, analogous to a finite beam-energy effect. The solution enables phenomenological modeling of the longitudinal initial condition in heavy-ion collisions and can serve as a benchmark for calibrating numerical hydrodynamic codes, as noted by Marrochio et al. (2013). The separation of target and projectile contributions in the energy density is motivated by Glasma-based models (Romatschke 2017, Lappi 2006) where the energy deposition factorizes into saturation scales of the two nuclei.
