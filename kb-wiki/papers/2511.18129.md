# Nuclear structure study with two- and three-nucleon contact interactions derived within low-energy EFT

> We present the results of the application of a nuclear potential consisting of two- and three-nucleon contact interactions in nuclear structure investigations. The nuclear Hamiltonian has been derived for a very low-energy regime within the framework of the effective field theory, its low-energy constants have been fitted to a few low-energy nucleon-nucleon experimental observables and the deuteron and 3H binding energies. Our goal is to validate the ability of this Hamiltonian to reproduce some important features of open-shell nuclei, and to this end we derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians for nuclei in the p- and sd-shell mass regions. The results of shell-model calculations with these effective Hamiltonians are then compared with experiment, and also with those obtained with a nuclear Hamiltonian derived within chiral perturbation theory, that includes also terms with one- and two-pion exchanges.

## Key claim
The authors test whether a nuclear Hamiltonian built solely from two- and three-nucleon contact interactions (pionless EFT) can reproduce shell evolution in open-shell nuclei, but find that it provides poor agreement with experiment for most systems studied, with several level inversions and incorrect single-particle sequences.

## Method
The authors derived effective shell-model Hamiltonians via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion using many-body perturbation theory, starting from a pionless EFT potential with two-nucleon forces at LO and N3LO and a three-nucleon force at LO. They included one- and two-body Goldstone diagrams up to third order for two-nucleon vertices and up to first order for three-nucleon vertices, plus density-dependent two-body contributions from three-body diagrams at second order. Shell-model calculations were performed for nuclei in the p-shell (6Li, 10B, 12C) and sd-shell (oxygen isotopes 17O, 18-24O) mass regions.

## Key numbers
- The N3LO LEEFT 2NF reproduces NN scattering data up to 25 MeV laboratory energy (the LO 2NF reproduces singlet np scattering length and deuteron binding energy, the NLO up to 15 MeV).
- For 17O, the LO LEEFT Heff gives degenerate 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 single-particle energies; the N3LO LEEFT Heff gives a single-particle spacing of about 3 MeV for both proton and neutron orbitals.
- The calculated yrast 2+ excitation energy in 18O from LEEFT Heffs is almost twice the observed value.
- The yrast 2+ states for even-mass oxygen isotopes from A=18 to 24 are compared with experiment and ChPT results.

## Context
This work extends the pionless EFT approach of Schiavilla (2021) to shell-model calculations in the p- and sd-shell regions, following the realistic shell-model methodology of Coraggio (2020). The results are compared with those from the chiral perturbation theory Hamiltonian of Entem and Machleidt (N3LO 2NF plus N2LO 3NF) as used by Fukui (2018) and Lyu (2025). The study confirms the earlier finding of Coraggio (2015) that as the energy scale of the nuclear Hamiltonian decreases, many-body forces become increasingly important for reproducing shell evolution. The authors conclude that the current LEEFT Hamiltonian, with only a LO three-nucleon contact term, is insufficient to balance the strong attractive 2NF and correctly describe low-lying spectra, and that higher-order many-body forces (3NF, 4NF, etc.) will be needed for heavier systems.
