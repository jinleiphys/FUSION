# Two-nucleon scattering in a modified Weinberg approach with a symmetry-preserving regularization

> We consider the nucleon-nucleon scattering problem by applying time-ordered perturbation theory to the Lorentz invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. Using a symmetry preserving higher derivative form of the effective Lagrangian, we exploit the freedom of the choice of the renormalization condition and obtain an integral equation for the scattering amplitude with an improved ultraviolet behavior. The resulting formulation is used to quantify finite regulator artifacts in two-nucleon phase shifts as well as in the chiral extrapolations of the S-wave scattering lengths and the deuteron binding energy. This approach can be straightforwardly extended to analyze few-nucleon systems and processes involving external electroweak sources.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a symmetry-preserving higher-derivative regularization, applied within a Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory using time-ordered perturbation theory, allows for a finite cutoff that can be varied over a wide range while maintaining renormalizability at leading order, but the approach reveals that cutoff variation alone is insufficient to estimate theoretical uncertainty because it is insensitive to neglected long-range interactions.

## Method

The authors employ time-ordered perturbation theory applied to a Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian, adding higher-derivative terms to the nucleon propagator to improve ultraviolet behavior while preserving chiral symmetry. They define an effective potential as the sum of two-particle irreducible time-ordered diagrams and solve the resulting integral equation for the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude, treating the leading-order potential non-perturbatively. The regularization introduces a cutoff parameter Lambda in the nucleon propagator, and the authors vary this parameter to quantify finite-regulator artifacts in phase shifts and chiral extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass M_pi = 138.0 MeV, pion decay constant F_pi = 92.2 MeV, nucleon mass m = 938.9 MeV, and axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.285 (effective value accounting for Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy)
- Regulator values Lambda = 600, 800, 1000, and 1200 MeV were used for phase shift calculations
- Low-energy constants C_S + C_V and C_AV + 2C_T were adjusted to reproduce ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 phase shifts from Nijmegen partial wave analysis below E_lab = 50 MeV
- For the ^3P_0 channel, an infinitely large repulsive counter term is required already at Lambda approximately 566 MeV to reproduce low-energy behavior
- The regulator range studied was 600 MeV < Lambda < infinity

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz-invariant chiral EFT approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering developed by Epelbaum (2012) by introducing a symmetry-preserving higher-derivative regularization that allows non-perturbative treatment of higher-order corrections, unlike the earlier formulation where corrections had to be treated perturbatively to maintain renormalizability. The results are compared with the nonrelativistic chiral NN potentials of Epelbaum (2014) that used a semi-local regularization with coordinate-space regulator R = 0.9 fm, and the cutoff variation provides a test of the uncertainty quantification approach from that work. The approach can be extended to few-nucleon systems and processes involving external electroweak sources.
