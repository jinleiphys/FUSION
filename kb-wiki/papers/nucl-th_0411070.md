# Nuclear interactions from the renormalization group

> We discuss how the renormalization group can be used to derive effective nuclear interactions. Starting from the model-independent low-momentum interaction V_{low k}, we successively integrate out high-lying particle and hole states from momentum shells around the Fermi surface as proposed by Shankar. The renormalization group approach allows for a systematic calculation of induced interactions and yields similar contributions to the scattering amplitude as the two-body parquet equations. We review results for the 1S0 and 3P2 superfluid pairing gaps as well as the spin dependence of effective interactions in neutron matter. Implications for the cooling of neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim

The renormalization group (RG) can systematically derive effective nuclear interactions by successively integrating out high-lying particle and hole states from momentum shells around the Fermi surface, yielding contributions to the scattering amplitude similar to the two-body parquet equations, but the approach is currently limited to one-loop truncation and treats pairing correlations explicitly in weak-coupling BCS theory after the RG is evolved to the Fermi surface.

## Method

Starting from the model-independent low-momentum interaction V_{low k} (which has momentum components below a cutoff Λ and preserves all low-energy two-body observables), the author applies Shankar's RG approach to neutron matter by restricting the effective interaction to low-lying states near the Fermi surface. The RG equation for the effective four-point vertex is solved iteratively at one loop, integrating out successive momentum shells, with the initial condition a(Λ=k_F) = V_{low k} including only scalar and spin-spin interactions. Pairing correlations are then treated in weak-coupling BCS theory after the RG evolution to the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- For Λ ≲ 2.0 fm⁻¹, all NN potentials that fit scattering data and include the same long-distance pion physics lead to the same "universal" low-momentum interaction V_{low k}.
- The mean-field BCS ^1S_0 pairing gap Δ₀ ≈ 3.3 MeV is suppressed to Δ ≈ 0.8 MeV at maximum when polarization effects (spin fluctuations) are included via the RG.
- The universal extreme low-density limit gives Δ/Δ₀ = (4e)^{-1/3} ≈ 0.45 for the suppression factor due to particle-hole polarization effects.
- For the ^3P_2 superfluid gap, consistency with neutron star cooling data requires Δ ≲ 30 keV (or Δ ≲ 10 keV in a more stringent analysis), compared to free-space gaps Δ₀ ∼ 0.1 MeV.
- The second-order particle-particle contributions to the scalar Landau function F(q') and spin-spin Landau function G(q') are shown for k_F = 1.0 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the RG approach to interacting Fermi systems proposed by Shankar (1994), which is widely used in condensed matter physics for the 2d Hubbard model, to nuclear neutron matter. The results for the ^1S_0 pairing gap are similar to those of Wambach et al., and the mean-field V_{low k} weak-coupling gap agrees well with BCS results. The suppression of the ^3P_2 superfluid gap due to screening of the spin-orbit interaction has implications for neutron star cooling simulations, where previous work (e.g., by Baldo et al.) used free-space interactions. The approach enables future extensions to non-central interactions, asymmetric matter including neutron/proton pairing, and consistent renormalization of currents/operators for effective valence shell-model interactions in heavy nuclei.
