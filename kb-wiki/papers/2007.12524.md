# On feasibility of azimuthal flow studies with Principal Component Analysis

> It is shown that the Principal Component Analysis applied to azimuthal single-particle distributions allows to perform flow analysis in ways that are analogous to the traditional approaches based on multi-particle correlations. In particular, symmetric cumulants are considered. It is demonstrated also that statistical fluctuations due to a finite number of particles per event practically do not play a role for higher order PCA-based cumulants.

# On feasibility of azimuthal flow studies with Principal Component Analysis

## Key claim
Principal Component Analysis (PCA) applied to event-by-event azimuthal single-particle distributions can reproduce traditional multi-particle cumulant flow observables, including symmetric cumulants, with the important condition that statistical fluctuations from finite particle multiplicity are negligible for higher-order PCA-based cumulants when the flow magnitude is approximately 0.1 and the number of particles per event is at least 100.

## Method
The author applied PCA to normalized single-particle azimuthal distributions from simulated Pb-Pb collisions (AMPT generator, 2.8 million events), obtaining orthonormal eigenvectors and decomposition coefficients. These PCA coefficients were then combined into expressions analogous to two-particle cumulants, four-particle cumulants, and symmetric cumulants, with corrections derived for statistical noise contributions from finite particle multiplicity. The PCA-based results were compared directly to values obtained from traditional multi-particle correlation methods.

## Key numbers
- Pb-Pb collisions at 5 TeV simulated with AMPT generator: 2.8 × 10^6 events analyzed
- Charged particles selected with pseudorapidity |η| < 0.8 and transverse momentum range 0.2–5 GeV/c for v₂ studies; |η| < 2.4 for symmetric cumulant studies
- Number of φ bins used for PCA: M = 48
- Statistical noise correction for v₂{2} is more pronounced in peripheral events (lower multiplicity); for v₂{4}, correction is almost irrelevant when flow magnitude v_n ∼ 0.1 and number of particles is ≳ 100
- PCA-extracted v₂{2} and v₂{4} values match traditional cumulant method results across centrality classes
- Symmetric cumulants SC(3,2) and SC(4,2) from PCA match multi-particle correlation calculations, with slight deviations for SC(4,2) in peripheral centrality classes

## Context
This work extends the application of PCA to heavy-ion flow analysis, building on the suggestion by Ollitrault (2015) to apply PCA to two-particle azimuthal correlations and on the author's own prior work (Altsybeev, 2019) showing PCA reveals Fourier harmonics from single-particle distributions. It directly addresses a previous attempt by Liu (2019) to study symmetric cumulants with PCA, where distorted PCA basis vectors prevented matching truth values. The paper demonstrates that PCA-based flow analysis can reproduce results from the traditional multi-particle cumulant framework of Bilandzic (2014) and Ollitrault (2001), enabling future flow studies using PCA as an alternative or complementary method that may reveal hidden patterns in collective behavior.
