# QCD-like phase diagram with Efimov trimers and Cooper pairs in resonantly interacting SU(3) Fermi gases

> We investigate color superfluidity and trimer formation in resonantly interacting SU(3) Fermi gases with a finite interaction range. The finite range is crucial to avoid the Thomas collapse and treat the Efimov effect occurring in this system. Using the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation with medium effects, we show the effects of the atomic Fermi distribution on the Efimov trimer energy at finite temperature. We show the critical temperature of color superfluidity within the non-selfconsistent $T$-matrix approximation (TMA). In this way, we can provide a first insight into the phase diagram as a function of the temperature $T$ and the chemical potential $μ$. This phase diagram consists of trimer, normal, and color-superfluid phases, and is similar to that of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite density and temperature, indicating a possibility to simulate the properties of such extremely dense matter in the laboratory.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first insight into the phase diagram of resonantly interacting SU(3) Fermi gases with a finite interaction range, showing that the phase diagram consists of trimer, normal, and color-superfluid phases and is similar to that of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite density and temperature. However, the authors note that their in-medium three-body equation cannot be justified near the critical temperature where molecular Bose-Einstein distributions increase, and the region near the crossing of the trimer threshold and superfluid transition is expected to be dominated by strong multi-body correlations not captured by their treatment.

## Method

The authors used the non-selfconsistent T-matrix approximation (TMA) within the Matsubara formalism to determine thermodynamic properties and the critical temperature of color superfluidity, solving the number equation for the chemical potential at fixed density. They combined this with the in-medium Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation, which exactly describes Efimov physics in three-body problems, incorporating Pauli-blocking effects through the Fermi-Dirac distribution function in the statistical factor. The finite interaction range was modeled using a two-channel Feshbach resonance model with a range parameter R* to avoid the Thomas collapse and properly treat Efimov trimers.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum trimer energy: E3^V = -0.01385/(mR*^2), where R* is the range parameter
- Critical temperature in the high-density limit: Tc^HDL = 0.133 TF, obtained from the Bose-Einstein condensation temperature of diatomic molecules
- Maximum pseudogap size: Δpg ≈ 0.55 εF at μmR*^2 = 0, where εF is the Fermi energy of an ideal Fermi gas
- The trimer energy E3^M is obtained as a dimensionless function E3^M = (1/mR*^2) X(R*/λT, μ/T), where λT = √(2π/mT) is the thermal de Broglie wavelength

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superfluidity in three-component Fermi gases (He, Rapp, Paananen, Bedaque, Kantian, Catelani, Martikainen, Ozawa) that used zero-range interactions, which lead to the Thomas collapse and cannot properly treat Efimov trimers. It also extends the zero-temperature study of finite-range effects by Nishida (2012) to finite temperature. The phase diagram is analogous to the phenomenological QCD phase diagram consisting of hadron, deconfined quark, and color superconducting phases (Fukushima), suggesting that ultracold SU(3) Fermi gases could serve as quantum simulators for QCD matter. The work enables future self-consistent treatments of two-body and three-body correlations in the regime near μ=T=0 where trimer formation and color superfluidity compete.
