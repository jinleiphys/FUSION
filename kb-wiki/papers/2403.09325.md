# Improved Constraints on the Variation of the Weak Scale from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We present an improved calculation of the light element abundances in the framework of Big Bang nucleosynthesis as a function of the Higgs vacuum expectation value $v$. We compare the methods of our calculation to previous literature including the recently published work of Burns et al. [1]. The PDG result for the $^4$He abundance can be explained within $2σ$ by $-0.014 \leq δv / v \leq 0.026$, for deuterium we find the constraint $-0.005 \leq δv / v \leq -0.001$. These bounds are more stringent than what was found earlier.

## Key claim

The paper presents improved constraints on the variation of the Higgs vacuum expectation value (VEV) from Big Bang nucleosynthesis, finding that the PDG helium-4 abundance can be explained within 2σ by a relative VEV variation between -0.014 and 0.026, while deuterium gives a more stringent constraint between -0.005 and -0.001. The authors note that these bounds are more stringent than found in earlier work by Burns et al. (2024), but the deuterium constraint depends sensitively on the treatment of the n+p→d+γ reaction rate.

## Method

The authors performed BBN simulations using the PRyMordial code, incorporating updated calculations of the neutron-proton mass difference from Cottingham sum rule results, lattice QCD data for the pion mass dependence of the nucleon mass and axial-vector coupling, and low-energy theorems combined with lattice QCD data for the deuteron binding energy and nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths. They recalculated the weak n↔p rates including variations in the electron mass, Fermi constant, and axial-vector coupling, and used pionless effective field theory to compute the n+p→d+γ reaction rate as a function of the Higgs VEV.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton mass difference QCD contribution: 1.87 ± 0.16 MeV, QED contribution: -0.58 ± 0.16 MeV
- For helium-4 (PDG data, 2σ): -0.014 ≤ δv/v ≤ 0.026
- For deuterium (PDG data, 2σ): -0.005 ≤ δv/v ≤ -0.001
- For helium-4 (EMPRESS data, 2σ): 0.011 ≤ δv/v ≤ 0.055
- Logarithmic derivative of deuterium abundance with respect to VEV: d ln Y_d / d ln v = -11.17
- Logarithmic derivative of helium-4 abundance with respect to VEV: d ln Y_P / d ln v = -1.20
- Physical neutron lifetime at δv=0: 878.4 s
- Physical average pion mass: 138.03 MeV
- For |δv/v| ≤ 0.1, the pion mass varies between 129 MeV and 147 MeV

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Burns et al. (2024) by using lattice QCD data and low-energy theorems for nucleon-nucleon scattering instead of one-boson-exchange models for the deuteron binding energy, and by including temperature-dependent effects in the weak n↔p rates beyond the linear approximation used by Berengut et al. (2013). The improved constraints enable future combined analyses of variations in the Higgs VEV and the electromagnetic fine-structure constant from all light element abundances, and could be extended to study possible variations of Yukawa couplings.
