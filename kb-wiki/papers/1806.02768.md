# Combined few-body and mean-field model for nuclei

> The challenging nuclear many-body problem is discussed along with classifications and qualitative descriptions of existing methods and models. We present detailed derivations of a new method where cluster correlations co-exist with an underlying mean-field described core-structure. The variation of an antisymmetrized product of cluster and core wave functions and a given nuclear interaction, provide sets of self-consistent equations of motion. After the applications on dripline nuclei we discuss perspectives with improvements and applications. In the conclusion we summarize while emphasizing the merits of consistently treating both short- and large-distance properties, few- and many-body correlations, ordinary nuclear structure, and concepts of halos and Efimov states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method that combines few-body and mean-field treatments to describe cluster correlations coexisting with an underlying mean-field core structure, but the method's applicability is conditional on the choice of effective interactions and the restriction of Hilbert space, requiring renormalization of valence interactions (e.g., fitting the neutron-neutron interaction to the experimental scattering length in vacuum) to ensure correct asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors derive coupled sets of self-consistent equations of motion by varying an antisymmetrized product of cluster and core wave functions with a given nuclear interaction. They apply the method to three dripline nuclei: 26O described as 24O plus two neutrons, 72Ca described as 70Ca plus two neutrons, and 70Kr described as 68Se plus two protons. Skyrme effective interactions are used between all pairs of nucleons, with the valence neutron-neutron interaction modified to fit the experimental scattering length in vacuum for the 26O case, and the Skyrme interaction strength continuously varied for the 72Ca case to fine-tune the approach to the dripline.

## Key numbers

- 26O: considered as 24O core surrounded by two valence neutrons, with the core being double magic and spherical
- 72Ca: described as 70Ca plus two neutrons; the s-state near the unbound threshold provides conditions for halo structures and possibly Efimov states, but Efimov state formation is prevented because the neutron-neutron scattering length is comparable to the spatial extension of a possible Efimov state, and scaling would place the next state outside our galaxy
- 70Kr: described as 68Se plus two protons; no 1- resonance is found and therefore no significant E1 transition, consistent with the long waiting time since both E2 and background transitions are very slow
- The nucleon mean-free path at low energy within nuclei is roughly 5 fm, comparable to the radii of medium heavy nuclei
- Nuclear densities fall off from maximum to zero over surface widths of about 2 fm
- Intrinsic nucleonic excitations occur at 140 MeV when the pion can be created
- The number of nucleons considered ranges from 2 to 300 for ordinary nuclei, up to about 1000 for exotic toroidal systems, and 1055 for neutron stars

## Context

This work extends the tradition of combining few-body and many-body methods in nuclear physics, building on prior developments in self-consistent mean-field calculations (Vautherin 1972), the shell model (Mayer 1950), and cluster models. The method addresses the long-standing challenge of simultaneously describing short-distance properties (mean-field structure) and large-distance properties (halos and Efimov states) that has limited models like the no-core shell model (Barrett 2013) and traditional few-body cluster approaches. The applications to dripline nuclei enable future studies of weakly bound systems, halo formation, and astrophysical rp-process waiting points, with the authors noting that full consistency between core and cluster interactions remains an open challenge for improvement.
