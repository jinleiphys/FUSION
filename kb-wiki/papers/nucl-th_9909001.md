# Systematic Study of the Kaon to Pion Multiplicity Ratios in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a systematic study of the kaon to pion multiplicity ratios (K+/pi+ and K-/pi-) in heavy-ion collisions from AGS to RHIC energy using the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) model. The model satisfactorily describes the available experimental data on K+/pi+ and K-/pi-. Within the model, we find that the strong increase of the ratios with the number of participants is mainly due to hadronic rescattering of produced mesons with ingoing baryons and their resonances. The enhancement of K/pi in heavy-ion collisions with respect to elementary p+p interactions is larger at AGS energy than SPS energy, and decreases smoothly with bombarding energy. The total multiplicity ratios at RHIC energy are predicted by RQMD to be K+/pi+ = 0.19 and K-/pi- = 0.15.

# Systematic Study of the Kaon to Pion Multiplicity Ratios in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The strong increase of the K/pi ratios with the number of participants in heavy-ion collisions is mainly due to hadronic rescattering of produced mesons with ingoing baryons and their resonances, not to rope formation or direct QGP signatures. The enhancement over p+p interactions is larger at AGS energy than SPS energy and decreases smoothly with bombarding energy, with the model predicting K+/pi+ = 0.19 and K-/pi- = 0.15 at RHIC energy.

## Method
The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) model in cascade mode (version 2.3 for AGS and SPS energies, version 2.4 for RHIC) to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions from AGS to RHIC energies. They systematically compared results with and without particle rescattering (meson-induced interactions) and with and without rope formation to isolate the underlying mechanisms. The model was first validated against p+p data from AGS to SPS energies.

## Key numbers
- Predicted total multiplicity ratios at RHIC energy (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV): K+/pi+ = 0.19, K-/pi- = 0.15
- Predicted midrapidity multiplicity ratios at RHIC energy: K+/pi+ = 0.19, K-/pi- = 0.17
- At AGS energy, meson-induced rescattering increases kaon multiplicity by 300% while changing pion multiplicity by -20%
- At SPS energy, rescattering changes kaon multiplicity by 20-30% and pion multiplicity by -10-0%
- At RHIC energy, rescattering changes kaon multiplicity by 60% and pion multiplicity by +20%
- The eta-decay contribution to charged pion multiplicities is 2-4% in the model
- The phi-decay contributions are a couple to 10% in K+ and 7-15% in K- multiplicity
- Fitted enhancement factor for overall K+/pi+: 1.8 + 11.2 GeV/sqrt(s)
- Fitted enhancement factor for overall K-/pi-: 1.4 + 6.4 GeV/(sqrt(s)-2.87 GeV)^2

## Context
This work extends earlier RQMD studies by Mat89, Sor91:meson, and Sor95:flavor that showed rescattering effects on strangeness production at AGS energies, and provides a systematic baseline from AGS through SPS to RHIC energies. The results challenge the original QGP signature interpretation of K/pi enhancement proposed by Egg91 and Kap86, supporting instead the hadronic rescattering explanation. The paper enables comparison with upcoming RHIC data and provides a hadronic baseline against which possible QGP signatures can be identified, complementing thermal model approaches by Gaz and transport calculations by the Giessen group (Cassing) which found strongest deviations from data at AGS energy.
