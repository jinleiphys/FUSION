# Quasiparticle Resonances in the BCS Approach

> We present a simple method for calculating the energies and the widths of quasiparticle resonant states. The method is based on BCS equations solved in the Berggren representation. In this representation the quasiparticle resonances are associated to the Gamow states of the mean field. The method is illustrated for the case of neutron-rich nuclei $^{20-22}$O and $^{84}$Ni. It is shown that the contribution of the continuum coupling to the pairing correlations is small and largely dominated by a few resonant states close to the continuum threshold.

## Key claim

The authors present a method for calculating the energies and widths of quasiparticle resonant states using BCS equations solved in the Berggren representation, where the contribution of the continuum coupling to pairing correlations is small and largely dominated by a few resonant states close to the continuum threshold.

## Method

The BCS equations are solved in the Berggren representation, which includes bound states, Gamow resonances, and a continuous set of scattering states of complex energy. The pairing field and chemical potential are first calculated by solving the BCS equations using a real energy representation (with scattering states on the real energy axis), then the pairing gaps associated to Gamow states are obtained by integrating the pairing field with the square of Gamow states, and finally the quasiparticle energies are computed using the standard BCS formula with these complex quantities.

- For $^{84}$Ni: average gap = 1.493 MeV (cBCS), pairing energy = -0.644 MeV, rms radius = 4.650 fm

- Single-particle resonance $1f_{7/2}$ in $^{17}$O: energy = 7.45 - i 1.53 MeV

- Quasiparticle resonance $1d_{3/2}$ in $^{22}$O: energy = 4.082 - i 0.075 MeV, occupation probability = 0.013 - i 0.003
- Quasiparticle resonance $2d_{3/2}$ in $^{84}$Ni: energy = 1.050 - i 0.183 MeV, occupation probability = 0.171 - i 0.030

## Context

This work extends previous approaches to continuum treatment in nuclear structure, including the continuum shell model (Zel), continuum-RPA (Bertsch, Giai), and resonant-RPA (Ver, Cur). It also builds on earlier BCS treatments of resonant continuum by Kruppa and Clv, and by Ni1, Ni2, Ni3, which used scattering states in the vicinity of single-particle resonance energies. The method enables direct calculation of quasiparticle resonance widths in odd-even nuclei near the drip line, treating each resonance as an individual state rather than a continuous set of scattering states.
