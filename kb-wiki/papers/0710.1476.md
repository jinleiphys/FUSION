# MACE -- Mach cones in heavy ion collisions

> We study the propagation of sound-like perturbations created by a jet moving with supersonic velocity through the quark-gluon-plasma created in heavy-ion reactions within the model MACE (MAch Cone Evolution). Predictions for heavy-ion reactions at RHIC energies (Au+Au-collisions) and for Pb+Pb reactions at the LHC ($\sqrt s=5.5A$ TeV) are presented and potential observations by the STAR, PHENIX and ALICE experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The MACE model shows that sound-like perturbations from a supersonic jet propagating through quark-gluon plasma produce double-peaked two-particle and four-peaked three-particle correlation structures, but these peaks are systematically shifted by about 0.2 radians away from the expected "perfect Mach positions" (π ± cos⁻¹ c_S), which could feign the presence of hadron gas instead of the actually present quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors developed the MACE (MAch Cone Evolution) model, which takes an existing 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamical evolution (calculated using the Particle in Cell code with an ideal gas equation of state, P = ε/3) as input and propagates sound waves through the resulting velocity field. A jet is introduced at a random position proportional to energy density, moving at the speed of light in a straight line, and at each timestep it creates spherical elementary sound waves represented by "wave markers" that propagate with the speed of sound (c_S = 1/√3) relative to the local fluid flow. The wave front surface is reconstructed using "wave lines" distributed azimuthally around the jet direction, and signals are inserted perpendicular to these lines at fixed intervals to generate correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound used in the model: c_S = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 (from ideal gas EoS P = ε/3)
- For RHIC Au+Au collisions: √s_NN = 130 GeV, with jet acceptance cut at |η| < 0.7 (STAR-TPC)
- For LHC Pb+Pb collisions: √s_NN = 5.5 TeV, with jet acceptance cut at |η| = 0.9 (ALICE-TPC)
- Expected "perfect Mach positions" for two-particle correlations: Δφ₁ = 2.2 and Δφ₂ = 4.1 (π ± cos⁻¹ c_S)
- Systematic shift of peak positions: δΔφ ≈ 0.2 radians away from the perfect Mach positions
- Central jet started from the center of the collision zone shows peaks at Δφ ≈ π ± 0.96 (the expected positions)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Mach cones in heavy-ion collisions originally proposed by Scheid and Greiner (1974) and later developed by Hofmann, Baumgardt, Stoecker and collaborators (1975-1980), and revived by Stoecker and Casalderrey-Solana (2004). The MACE model is the first to propagate sound waves through a realistic, non-static, inhomogeneous hydrodynamical velocity field without assuming a perfect cone shape, enabling predictions for both RHIC (STAR, PHENIX) and LHC (ALICE) experiments. The results show that collective flow does not wash out the cone structure but shifts the peak positions, and that the "wide-peak" structure observed at RHIC is not reproduced, suggesting non-linear effects near the jet or jet slowing may be responsible for that experimental feature.
