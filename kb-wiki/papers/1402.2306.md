# Percolation Approach to Initial Stage Effects in High Energy Collisions

> Possible phase transition of strongly interacting matter from hadron to a quark-gluon plasma state have in the past received considerable interest. The clustering of color sources provides a framework of the the partonic interactions in the initial stage of the collisions. The onset of de-confinement transition is identified by the spanning percolation cluster in 2D percolation. In this talk results are presented both for the multiplicity and the elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies. The thermodynamic quantities temperature, equation of state and transport coefficient are obtained in the framework of clustering of color sources. It is shown that the results are in excellent agreement with the recent lattice QCD calculations(LQCD).

# Percolation Approach to Initial Stage Effects in High Energy Collisions

## Key claim
The Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) reproduces thermodynamic quantities (temperature, equation of state, and shear viscosity) in agreement with lattice QCD calculations, but only when the percolation density parameter ξ exceeds the critical value of 1.2 that marks the onset of deconfinement.

## Method
The paper applies 2D percolation theory to color strings stretched between projectile and target in high-energy nuclear collisions. Strings are treated as small discs in transverse space; when they overlap at sufficient density, they form clusters that behave as single color sources with vector-summed color charges. The model uses the percolation density parameter ξ and the color suppression factor F(ξ) to compute multiplicity, elliptic flow, temperature, energy density, and shear viscosity, with F(ξ) extracted from experimental transverse momentum spectra.

## Key numbers
- Critical percolation density for spanning cluster: ξc ≥ 1.2
- Energy density relation for 1.2 < ξ < 2.88: εi = 0.788 ξ GeV/fm³
- η/s for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV: 2.5 times the KSS bound
- η/s for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: 3.3 times the KSS bound
- Minimum η/s = 0.20 at T/Tc = 1.15, corresponding to trace anomaly peak ∼ 5
- Longitudinal extension of color source: L = 1 fm

## Context
This work extends the Color String Percolation Model developed by Pajares and collaborators (Pajares, 2005; Pajares, 2007) and applies it to RHIC and LHC data. The model is presented as a saturation approach similar to the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations from the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov, 2012) and with the AdS/CFT lower bound for η/s from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005). The paper shows that the inverse of η/s is equivalent to the trace anomaly (ε-3P)/T⁴ across a wide temperature range.
