# Quantum Fields far from Equilibrium and Thermalization

> I review the use of the 2PI effective action in nonequilibrium quantum field theory. The approach enables one to find approximation schemes which circumvent long-standing problems of non-thermal or secular (unbounded) late-time evolutions encountered in standard loop or 1/N expansions of the 1PI effective action. It is shown that late-time thermalization can be described from a numerical solution of the three-loop 2PI effective action for a scalar $ϕ^4$--theory in 1+1 dimensions (with Jurgen Cox, hep-ph/0006160). Quantitative results far from equilibrium beyond the weak coupling expansion can be obtained from the 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action at next-to-leading order (NLO), calculated for a scalar O(N) symmetric quantum field theory (hep-ph/0105311). Extending recent calculations in classical field theory by Aarts et al. (hep-ph/0007357) and by Blagoev et al. (hep-ph/0106195) to $N>1$ we show that the NLO approximation converges to exact (MC) results already for moderate values of $N$ (with Gert Aarts, hep-ph/0107129). I comment on characteristic time scales in scalar quantum field theory and the applicability of classical field theory for sufficiently high initial occupation numbers.

## Key claim

The 2PI effective action, combined with a 1/N expansion at next-to-leading order (NLO), provides a controlled nonperturbative approximation scheme for far-from-equilibrium quantum field dynamics that avoids the secular (unbounded) late-time behavior of standard 1PI expansions, and can describe late-time thermalization even when the system is far from equilibrium and couplings are not weak.

## Method

The author solves the stationarity condition δΓ[G]/δG = 0 for the 2PI effective action Γ[G] of a scalar O(N) symmetric φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions, truncating Γ₂[G] at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion. The resulting evolution equations for the statistical two-point function F and the spectral function ρ are integrated numerically without further approximations, using the decomposition G(x,y) = F(x,y) - (i/2) ρ(x,y) sign_C(x⁰-y⁰). Initial conditions are chosen either as a quench from a high-temperature thermal distribution or as a Gaussian distribution peaked around a narrow momentum range.

## Key numbers

- Damping rate γ^(damp)_0 = 0.016 M_I for a quench from T₀ = 2 M_I, M₀² = 2 M_I², with λ/6N = 0.083 M_I² and N=10.
- Initial oscillation frequency ε₀/2π = 0.17 M_I for the same quench parameters.
- Damping time scales ∼ N for large N at strong coupling λ/M_I² = 30.
- Power-law exponent ∼ 1.75 for the equal-time mode F(x⁰,x⁰;p) at intermediate times for a Gaussian initial distribution peaked at p_ts = 2.5 M_I with T₀ = 4 M_I and λ/6N = 0.1 M_I², N=10.
- Ratio of damping rate to thermalization rate γ^(damp)/γ^(therm) ∼ O(10) for the same parameters.
- Equilibrium inverse slope T_eq = 4.7 M_I for the quantum evolution with the Gaussian initial condition.

## Context

This work extends the three-loop 2PI calculations of Berges (2000) and Aarts et al. (2001) to a systematic 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action, which provides a small nonperturbative expansion parameter not restricted to weak couplings. It contrasts with leading-order large-N or mean-field approximations (which neglect scattering and fail to describe thermalization) and with standard 1/N expansions of the 1PI effective action (which become secular at late times). The NLO 1/N approximation is shown to converge to exact Monte Carlo results for the classical statistical field theory already for moderate N, extending the classical field theory comparisons of Aarts et al. (2001) and Blagoev et al. (2001) to N>1. The approach enables quantitative studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics including thermalization, and the author identifies extension to 3+1 dimensions as the most pressing next step for applications to heavy-ion collisions and early-universe reheating.
