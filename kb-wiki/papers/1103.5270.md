# Shape changes and large-amplitude collective dynamics in neutron-rich Cr isotopes

> Shape-phase transition in neutron-rich Cr isotopes around N=40 is investigated by employing the collective Hamiltonian approach. The inertial functions for large-amplitude vibration and rotation are evaluated by the local normal modes along the axial quadrupole collective coordinate using the Skyrme and pairing energy density functionals. The time-odd components of the mean fields are fully included in the derived masses. The low-lying spectra obtained by requantizing the collective Hamiltonian show an excellent agreement with the recent experimental data. Gradual change from spherical to axially deformed shapes in between N=34 and 38 is well described.

## Key claim

The shape-phase transition in neutron-rich Cr isotopes around N=40 is described using a collective Hamiltonian approach that fully includes the time-odd components of the mean field in the inertial functions, showing that ⁶⁰Cr is located close to the critical point of the shape-phase transition rather than being simply spherical or deformed.

## Method

The authors constructed a (1+2)-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (axial vibration in β plus rotation about two perpendicular axes) by solving constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) equations with Skyrme SkM* and volume-type pairing energy density functionals in coordinate space. The vibrational mass and rotational moment of inertia were evaluated by solving the local quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (LQRPA) equations on top of approximately 20 CHFB states, fully incorporating time-odd mean-field components. The collective Schrödinger equation was then requantized and solved to obtain low-lying spectra and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Cr, the D1S-Gogny EDF calculation from Gau09 gave E(2₁⁺)=0.82 MeV, R₄/₂=2.2, Q(2₁⁺)=-34 e·fm², and B(E2↑)=1515 e²·fm⁴.
- The LQRPA calculation used 256 CPUs per nucleus, taking about 185 CPU hours for inertial functions and about 3700 CPU hours total per nucleus.
- The CHFB equations used a mesh size of Δρ=Δz=0.5 fm with box boundary conditions at (ρ_max, z_max)=(12.25, 12.00) fm.
- Quasiparticle states were truncated at E_α ≤ 60 MeV, and two-quasiparticle states at E_α+E_β ≤ 60 MeV.
- For a harmonic oscillator test case with B=50 MeV⁻¹ and C=200 MeV, the (1+2)D model gave E(2₁⁺)=2.00 MeV and E(4₁⁺)=4.26 MeV, while the exact values are 2 MeV and 4 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the energy-density-functional (EDF) based collective Hamiltonian approach previously applied with Gogny interactions (Lib99, Gau09), relativistic Lagrangians (Nik09, Li10), and Skyrme interactions (Pro09), but improves upon those by fully including time-odd mean-field components rather than using the cranking approximation. The results are compared with spherical shell-model calculations using pairing-plus-multipole forces with monopole correction (Kan08) and the LNPS interaction (Len10), as well as with experimental data from Bur05, Zhu06, Aoi09, and Gad10. The framework enables systematic investigation of low-lying collective states across the nuclear chart using massively-parallel supercomputers, and future comparison with the generator-coordinate method using β-constrained HFB states is suggested.
