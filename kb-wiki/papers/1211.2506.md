# Formation of eta-prime(958)-mesic nuclei by (p,d) reaction

> We calculate theoretically the formation spectra of eta'(958)-nucleus systems in the (p,d) reaction for the investigation of the in-medium modification of the eta' mass. We show the comprehensive numerical calculations based on a simple form of the eta' optical potential in nuclei with various potential depths. We conclude that one finds an evidence of possible attractive interaction between eta' and nucleus as peak structure appearing around the eta' threshold in light nuclei such as 11C when the attractive potential is stronger than 100 MeV and the absorption width is of order of 40 MeV or less. Spectroscopy of the (p,d) reaction is expected to be performed experimentally at existing facilities, such as GSI. We also estimate the contributions from the omega and phi mesons, which have masses close to the eta' meson, concluding that the observation of the peak structure of the eta'-mesic nuclei is not disturbed although their contributions may not be small.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that a clear peak structure in the (p,d) reaction spectrum around the eta' production threshold can serve as evidence of an attractive eta'-nucleus interaction, but only when the attractive potential is stronger than 100 MeV and the absorption width is 40 MeV or less; the peak may arise from either genuine bound states or threshold enhancement, both indicating attraction.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of eta'-mesic nuclei formation by Nagahiro (2004) and provides comprehensive calculations to support the planned experimental search at GSI described by Itahashi (2012). The study is motivated by theoretical work by Jido (2011) suggesting that partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter can induce a mass reduction of the eta' meson of about 100 MeV, and by NJL model calculations by Costa (2002) suggesting around 150 MeV mass reduction at saturation density. The results enable experimentalists to design coincidence measurements of decay particles from eta' bound states, which could reduce the large background estimated in the experimental proposal.
