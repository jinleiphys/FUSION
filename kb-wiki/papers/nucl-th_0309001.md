# Light-front time picture of few-body systems

> The projection of the four-dimensional two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation at the light-front hypersurface is discussed. Elimination of the relative time is performed using a quasi-potential expansion, which is shown to be equivalent to an infinite set of coupled equations for the Green's functions of the different light-front Fock-states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quasi-potential reduction of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation on the light-front hypersurface yields an infinite hierarchy of coupled equations for light-front Green's functions with different numbers of particles, but this hierarchy excludes complete crossed-ladder diagrams and self-energy terms even in the covariant ladder approximation.

## Method

The authors perform a quasi-potential reduction of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) by integrating out the relative light-front time variable, using the method of Woloshyn and Jackson (1973). They derive coupled equations for the light-front Green's functions of Fock-states with increasing particle number, first for a bosonic model with interaction Lagrangian \( \mathcal{L}^B_I = g_S\phi_1^\dagger\phi_1\sigma + g_S\phi_2^\dagger\phi_2\sigma \) and then for a fermionic Yukawa model with \( \mathcal{L}^F_I = g_S\overline{\Psi}\Psi\sigma \). The hierarchy is constructed by expanding the effective interaction \( \nu(K) \) in terms of the elementary vertex \( v \) and the free N-body light-front Green's functions \( g_0^{(N)}(K) \).

## Key numbers

- The free two-body light-front Green's function is given by \( g_0(K) = i\widehat\Omega^{-1}g_0^{(2)}(K)\widehat\Omega^{-1} \), where \( \widehat\Omega = \sqrt{\widehat k_1^+(K^+-\widehat k_1^+)} \) and \( K^+ > 0 \).
- The free N-body light-front Green's function is \( g_0^{(N)}(K) = \left[\prod_{j=1}^N \theta(\widehat k_j^+)\theta(K^+-\widehat k_j^+)\right]\left(K^- - \widehat K_0^{(N)-} + i o\right)^{-1} \), with \( \widehat K_0^{(N)-} = \sum_{j=1}^N \widehat{k}_{j\ on}^- \).
- For the bosonic model, the vertex matrix element is \( \langle q k_\sigma |v|k\rangle = -2(2\pi)^3\delta(q+k_\sigma-k) \frac{g_S}{\sqrt{q^+k_\sigma^+ k^+}} \theta(k_\sigma^+) \).
- For the fermionic Yukawa model, the vertex matrix element for emission of one sigma boson is \( \langle (q,s^\prime) k_\sigma |v|(k,s)\rangle = -2m(2\pi)^3\delta(q+k_\sigma-k) \frac{g_S}{\sqrt{q^+k_\sigma^+ k^+}} \theta(k_\sigma^+) \overline{u}(q,s^\prime)u(k,s) \).

## Context

This work extends the light-front treatment of the two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation previously developed by Sales, Frederico, Carlson, and Sauer (2000, 2001) by explicitly constructing the hierarchy of coupled Green's function equations for both bosonic and fermionic systems. The hierarchy resembles the iterated resolvent method described by Brodsky, Pauli, and Pinsky (1998) and provides a framework for truncating the light-front Fock-space that goes beyond the quasi-potential expansion in second order. The approach enables future studies of renormalization in light-front dynamics and can be applied to bound-state calculations of hadrons and few-nucleon systems at short distances, as discussed by Carbonell, Desplanques, Karmanov, and Mathiot (1998).
