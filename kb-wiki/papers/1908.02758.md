# Fractionalized Degrees of Freedom at Infinite Coupling in large Nf QED in 2+1 dimensions

> I consider quantum electrodynamics with many electrons in 2+1 space-time dimensions at finite temperature. The relevant dimensionless interaction parameter for this theory is the fine structure constant divided by the temperature. The theory is solvable at any value of the coupling, in particular for very weak (high temperature) and infinitely strong coupling (corresponding to the zero temperature limit). Concentrating on the photon, each of its physical degrees of freedom at infinite coupling only contributes half of the free-theory value to the entropy. These fractional degrees of freedom are reminiscent of what has been observed in other strongly coupled systems (such as N=4 SYM), and bear similarity to the fractional Quantum Hall effect, potentially suggesting connections between these phenomena. The results found for QED3 are fully consistent with the expectations from particle-vortex duality.

# Fractionalized Degrees of Freedom at Infinite Coupling in large Nf QED in 2+1 dimensions

## Key claim
At infinite coupling (zero temperature limit) in large Nf QED in 2+1 dimensions, each physical photon degree of freedom contributes exactly half of its free-theory value to the entropy, yet the total photon-plus-ghost contribution to the entropy vanishes, making the theory at strong coupling appear as Nf emergent non-interacting Dirac fermions.

## Method
The author computes the partition function and free energy density for QED3 with Nf fermions in the large Nf limit at finite temperature, using dimensional regularization and Matsubara frequency sums. The photon polarization tensor is evaluated in the zero-temperature limit (infinite coupling) and used to extract the entropy density contributions from the photon degrees of freedom and Faddeev-Popov ghosts separately.

## Key numbers
- Each photon degree of freedom at infinite coupling contributes s_strong = 3ζ(3)T^2/(4π) to the entropy, which is exactly half of the free-theory value s_free = 3ζ(3)T^2/(2π)
- The total entropy density at strong coupling is s_QED3,strong = 9 Nf ζ(3) T^2/(2π) + O(Nf^{-1}), with the photon-plus-ghost contribution vanishing at leading order
- The fermion entropy density is s_fermion = 9 Nf ζ(3) T^2/(2π)
- The ghost entropy density is s_gh = -3ζ(3)T^2/π

## Context
This work extends the study of fractionalized degrees of freedom in strongly coupled quantum field theories, previously observed in N=4 super Yang-Mills theory (Gubser 1998) where the total entropy at infinite coupling is 3/4 of the free theory value, and in the bosonic O(N) model in 2+1 dimensions (Sachdev 1993, Drummond 1997, Romatschke 2019) where a ratio of 4/5 was found. The results are fully consistent with expectations from particle-vortex duality for QED3 (Son 2015, Karch 2016). The author suggests potential connections to the fractional quantum Hall effect, though further work is needed to illuminate this possible link.
