# Double pion production in $NN$ and $\bar{N}N$ collisions

> With an effective Lagrangian approach, we give a full analysis on the $NN \to NNππ$ and $\bar{N}N\to \bar{N}Nππ$ reactions by exploring the roles of various resonances with mass up to 1.72 GeV. We find large contributions from $Δ$, $N^*(1440)$, $Δ(1600)$ and $Δ(1620)$ resonances. Our calculations also indicate sizeable contributions from nucleon poles for the energies close to the threshold. A good description to the existing data of different isospin channels of $NN\to NNππ$ and $\bar{N}N\to \bar{N}Nππ$ for beam energies up to 2.2 GeV is reached. Our results provide important implications to the ABC effect and guildlines to the future experimental projects at COSY, HADES and HIRFL-CSR. We point out that the PANDA at FAIR could be an essential place for studying the properties of baryon resonances and the data with baryon and anti-baryon in final states are worth analyzing.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a good description of existing data for double pion production in both nucleon-nucleon and antinucleon-nucleon collisions up to beam energies of 2.2 GeV by including contributions from multiple baryon resonances, but they note that their model fails to reproduce the ππ invariant mass spectra for beam energies above 1.0 GeV and the level-off behavior around 1.0 GeV in the total cross section of the pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ channel.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with a Lorentz covariant orbital-spin coupling scheme, including all well-established N* and Δ* resonances with masses below 1.72 GeV. Coupling constants were determined from empirical partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group, and adjustable cutoff parameters in form factors at vertices and resonances were fitted to data, using the pp → nnπ⁺π⁺ channel to constrain Δ and Δ* contributions because it has negligible N* contribution.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies for data considered range from threshold up to 2.2 GeV, with the overall system invariant mass about 2.8 GeV for Tp = 2.2 GeV
- Resonances included have masses up to 1.72 GeV, with significant contributions from Δ(1232), N*(1440), Δ(1600), and Δ(1620)
- The total cross section of pp → ppπ⁰π⁰ should be a factor of about four larger than that of pp → nnπ⁺π⁺ if double-Δ mechanism were dominant, but new measurements indicate approximately equal values
- The PANDA facility is expected to provide antiproton beams with kinetic energy ranging from 1 to 15 GeV and luminosity of about 10³¹ cm⁻² s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the Valencia model (more than 10 years old) and the one-pion exchange models (more than 45 years old) by including additional resonances beyond Δ(1232) and N*(1440), specifically Δ(1600) and Δ(1620), and by applying the analysis to antinucleon-nucleon collisions for the first time. The results provide guidelines for future experiments at COSY, HADES, HIRFL-CSR, and PANDA at FAIR, and offer implications for understanding the ABC effect in pn → dπ⁰π⁰ reactions, suggesting that N*(1440) and nucleon poles must be carefully considered before reaching conclusions.
