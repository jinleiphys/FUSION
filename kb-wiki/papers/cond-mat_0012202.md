# Multiply charged metal cluster anions

> Formation, stability patterns, and decay channels of silver dianionic and gold trianionic clusters are investigated with Penning-trap experiments and a shell-correction method including shape deformations. The theoretical predictions pertaining to the appearance sizes and electronic shell effects are in remarkable agreement with the experiments. Decay of the multiply anionic clusters occurs predominantly by electron tunneling through a Coulomb barrier, rather than via fission, leading to appearance sizes unrelated to those of multiply cationic clusters.

# Multiply charged metal cluster anions

## Key claim

Multiply anionic metal clusters (Ag_N^{2-} and Au_N^{3-}) decay predominantly by electron autodetachment through a repulsive Coulomb barrier rather than by fission, and their appearance sizes are unrelated to those of multiply cationic clusters; the theoretical predictions agree remarkably with experiment only when electronic shell effects from ellipsoidal shape deformations are included.

## Method

The authors combined Penning-trap experiments with a finite-temperature shell-correction method (SCM) that incorporates triaxial shape deformations and a modified Nilsson hamiltonian for the valence electrons. Silver dianions (Ag_N^{2-}) and gold trianions (Au_N^{3-}) were produced by electron attachment in a Penning trap, and their relative abundances were measured via time-of-flight mass spectrometry. The theoretical multiple electron affinities were calculated by separating the free energy into a smooth liquid-drop-model part and a Strutinsky-type shell-correction term, with thermal shape fluctuations evaluated via Boltzmann averaging.

## Key numbers

- Appearance size for silver dianions: n_a^{2-}(Ag) = 27 (theoretical); smallest experimental observation at N=24 but yields very small for N=24-27.
- Appearance size for gold trianions: n_a^{3-}(Au) = 54 (theoretical and experimental).
- Second electron affinity of Ag_24^{2-}: A_2 = 0.04 eV, forming a weak island of stability.
- Gold trianions with N <= 53 and 56 <= N <= 62 have A_3 < 0 (unstable against electron emission).
- Exothermic fission of Au_N^{3-} occurs only for N <= 25; for larger clusters fission is endothermic.
- Work function values used: W(Au) = 5.31 eV, W(Ag) = 4.26 eV.
- Wigner-Seitz radius for gold: r_s = 3.01 a.u. (at T=0 K).
- Electron beam energy for charging: about 40 eV.
- Magnetic field of Penning trap: 5 T.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Yannouleas and Landman (1993) that first suggested electron autodetachment through a Coulomb barrier as a decay channel for multiply anionic clusters, and it builds on experimental observations of dianionic gold clusters by Schweikhard et al. (1999) and trianionic metal clusters by Herlert et al. (2000). The results contrast sharply with multiply cationic clusters, which decay via fission as studied by Brechignac et al. (1990) and Barnett et al. (1991). The findings enable future studies of multiply charged metal cluster anions via collisional or laser activation, as well as investigations of their chemical properties.
