# Large violation of the flavour SU(3) symmetry in $η$MAID2018 isobar model

> We demonstrate that the explanation of the neutron anomaly around $W\sim 1685$MeV in $γN\to ηN$ reactions provided by the $η$MAID2018 isobar model is based on large violation of the flavour SU(3) symmetry in hadron interactions. This is yet another example of how conventional explanation (without invoking exotic narrow nucleon resonance) of the neutron anomaly metamorphoses into unconventional physics picture of hadron interactions. A possibility to mend the flavour SU(3) symmetry for some of resonances in $η$MAID model is discussed.

## Key claim

The ηMAID2018 isobar model’s explanation of the neutron anomaly (a narrow peak in the γn → ηn cross section around W ∼ 1685 MeV) requires a large violation of flavour SU(3) symmetry in the coupling constants of the N(1650) and N(1710) resonances, unless one allows strong non-octet components in the amplitude or revises the common understanding of SU(3) in hadron interactions.

## Method

The authors extracted the absolute values of the πN, ηN, KΛ, and KΣ coupling constants for the N(1535), N(1650), and N(1710) resonances from Table IV and Eq. (50) of the ηMAID2018 fit (Tiator 2018). They then compared these values to the predictions of the standard octet SU(3) symmetry relations, which relate the couplings via a single parameter α = F/(D+F). For each resonance, they solved the SU(3) relations for α using the reported |gπN| and |gηN|, and then computed the predicted |gKΛ| and |gKΣ| to check consistency with the ηMAID2018 values.

## Key numbers

* N(1650, 1/2⁻) in ηMAID2018: |gπN| = 0.36, |gηN| = 0.28, |gKΛ| = 0.20, |gKΣ| = 1.21; SU(3) predictions for α = 0.83 give |gKΛ| = 0.32 and |gKΣ| = 0.24, and for α = -0.33 give |gKΛ| = 0.04 and |gKΣ| = 0.60, both in acute contradiction with the ηMAID2018 value |gKΣ| = 1.21.
* N(1710, 1/2⁺) in ηMAID2018: |gπN| = 0.10, |gηN| = 0.22, |gKΛ| = 0.52, |gKΣ| = 0; SU(3) predictions for α = 1.9 give |gKΛ| = 0.16 and |gKΣ| = 0.28, and for α = -1.40 give |gKΛ| = 0.06 and |gKΣ| = 0.38, both in acute contradiction with the ηMAID2018 values.
* N(1535, 1/2⁻) in ηMAID2018: |gπN| = 0.45, |gηN| = 0.62; the SU(3) solution α = -0.78 agrees with the global SU(3) analysis value α = -0.53 ± 0.15 from SU31 and the SU(6) quark model value α = -1/2.
* The ratio of photocouplings for N(1710) in ηMAID2018 is |A₁/₂ᵖ / A₁/₂ⁿ| ≈ 0.13, matching the prediction for the anti-decuplet nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ earlier critique (Kuznetsov and Polyakov 2014) of the Bonn-Gatchina analysis of the neutron anomaly, which required the N(1650) to be a cryptoexotic pentaquark. Here they show that the ηMAID2018 isobar model (Tiator 2018) similarly violates flavour SU(3) symmetry, and they suggest that the N(1710) in ηMAID2018 may actually represent two states: a conventional octet N(1710) and a narrow anti-decuplet nucleon, whose existence was predicted by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). This hypothesis could resolve both the SU(3) violation and the need for an extraordinarily large KΣ coupling in N(1650).
