# Bayesian Inference of the Dense Matter Equation of State built upon Covariant Density Functionals

> A modified version of the density dependent covariant density functional model proposed in [T. Malik, M. Ferreira, B. K. Agrawal and C. Providência, ApJ 930, 17 (2022)] is employed in a Bayesian analysis to determine the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter with nucleonic degrees of freedom. Various constraints from nuclear physics and microscopic calculations of pure neutron matter (PNM) along with a lower bound on the maximum mass of neutron stars (NSs) are imposed on the EOS models to investigate the effectiveness of progressive incorporation of the constraints, their compatibility as well as correlations among parameters of nuclear matter and properties of NSs. Our results include the different roles played by pressure and energy per particle of PNM in constraining the isovector behavior of nuclear matter; tension with the values of Dirac effective mass extracted from spin-orbit splitting; correlations between the radius of the canonical mass NS and second and third order coefficients in the Taylor expansion of energy per particle as a function of density; correlation between the central pressure of the maximum mass configuration and Dirac effective mass of the nucleon at saturation. For some of our models the tail of the NS maximum mass reaches $2.7~\mathrm{M}_{\odot}$, which means that the secondary object in GW190814 could have been a NS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a modified density-dependent covariant density functional model, when constrained by both pressure and energy per particle of pure neutron matter from chiral effective field theory, can produce neutron star maximum masses reaching 2.7 solar masses, suggesting the secondary object in GW190814 could have been a neutron star. However, this result depends on the specific set of constraints applied and is not universal across all model runs; the authors find tension between the Dirac effective mass values extracted from spin-orbit splitting and those preferred by their Bayesian posteriors.

## Method

The authors employ a Bayesian inference framework using a modified version of the density-dependent covariant density functional model from Malik et al. (2022), with six free parameters describing the density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings for sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They perform Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling using the emcee package, imposing constraints from symmetric nuclear matter properties (saturation density, energy, compressibility, symmetry energy), chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter energy per particle and pressure at three subsaturation densities, and a lower bound on the maximum neutron star mass. Six different runs are conducted with varying combinations of constraints to investigate their individual and combined effectiveness.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density constraint: 0.153 fm^-3 with standard deviation 0.005 fm^-3
- Saturation energy: -16.1 MeV with standard deviation 0.2 MeV
- Compressibility modulus: 230 MeV with standard deviation 40 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32.5 MeV with standard deviation 1.8 MeV
- Dirac effective mass at saturation: 0.55 m_N with standard deviation 0.05 m_N
- Pure neutron matter energy per particle constraints at 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 fm^-3: 9.50 ± 0.52 MeV, 12.68 ± 1.20 MeV, and 16.31 ± 2.13 MeV respectively
- Pure neutron matter pressure constraints at 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16 fm^-3: 0.509 ± 0.093, 1.238 ± 0.302, and 2.482 ± 0.687 MeV/fm^3 respectively
- For some models the tail of the neutron star maximum mass reaches 2.7 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of Malik et al. (2022) by incorporating both pressure and energy per particle constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter by Hebeler et al. (2013), rather than pressure alone. It also investigates the role of Dirac effective mass constraints from spin-orbit splitting data (Typel 2005) and tests sensitivity to the maximum mass lower bound (2.0 vs 2.2 solar masses). The results enable future studies of correlations between nuclear matter parameters and neutron star properties, and suggest that the secondary object in GW190814 could be a neutron star if certain equation of state models are realized in nature.
