# Two-Neutron Halo State of ${}^{15}$B Around $3.48$ MeV By A Three-Body Model

> We investigate low-lying bound states of the neutron-rich nucleus ${}^{15}$B by assuming it is a three-body system made of an inert core ${}^{13}$B and two valence neutrons. The three-body wave functions are obtained using the Faddeev formalism. Special attention is paid to the excited state at $3.48(6)$ MeV observed in the ${}^{13}\text{C}({}^{14}\text{C},{}^{12}\text{N}){}^{15}\text{B}$ reaction, whose properties are less clear theoretically. In our three-body model, besides the ground state $3/2_1^-$, a second $3/2_2^-$ state is discovered at around $3.61$ MeV, which might be identified with the excited state observed at $3.48(6)$ MeV. We study this $3/2_2^-$ state in detail, which turns out to be a two-neutron halo state with a large matter radius $r_\text{m}\approx 4.770$ fm.

# Two-Neutron Halo State of 15B Around 3.48 MeV By A Three-Body Model

## Key claim
The excited state of 15B observed experimentally at 3.48(6) MeV is identified with a calculated 3/2_2^- state at around 3.61 MeV excitation energy, which exhibits a two-neutron halo structure. This identification is conditional: the authors note a 0.13 MeV discrepancy between their calculated excitation energy and the experimental value, which they attribute to possible unresolved experimental errors or theoretical defects in model building.

## Method
The authors model 15B as a three-body system consisting of an inert 13B core plus two valence neutrons, solving the Faddeev equations in hyperspherical coordinates with a hyperspherical harmonics expansion truncated at K_max = 22. The neutron-neutron interaction uses the Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) potential, while the neutron-core interaction employs a Gaussian form with spin-dependent terms following the Garrido-Fedorov-Jensen (GFJ) ansatz, with parameters fitted to reproduce known properties of 13B and 14B. Three different three-body interaction parameter sets (V3BA, V3BB, V3BC) are introduced to account for core deformation and excitation effects, each tuned to reproduce the experimental ground-state energy of 15B exactly.

## Key numbers
- The calculated 3/2_2^- excited state has an excitation energy of 3.598–3.615 MeV (depending on the three-body parameter set), compared to the experimental value of 3.48(6) MeV
- The matter radius of the 3/2_2^- state is r_m* ≈ 4.770 fm (V3BA), 4.763 fm (V3BB), or 4.739 fm (V3BC), corresponding to the size of a stable nucleus with A ≈ 58
- The ground state 3/2_1^- has a matter radius of 3.085–3.104 fm, consistent with a stable nucleus of A ≈ 16
- The RMS distance between the two valence neutrons in the excited state is r_nn* ≈ 15.26 fm (V3BA), and the RMS distance from the 13B core to the valence neutron pair is r_c,nn* ≈ 8.20 fm (V3BA)
- The ground state energy is fixed to the experimental value of -3.747(21) MeV relative to the 13B + n + n threshold
- The excited state energy relative to the same threshold is -0.1293 to -0.1481 MeV (calculated) versus -0.267(64) MeV (experimental)
- The dominant occupation probability for the excited state is (1s_1/2)^2 at 86.32–86.36%, with (0d_5/2)^2 at 7.21–7.23% and (0d_3/2)^2 at 4.81–4.82%

## Context
This work extends three-body Faddeev calculations previously applied to other two-neutron halo nuclei such as 6He, 11Li (Zhukov 1993), 12Be (Nunes 1996), 14Be (Tarutina 2004), 17B (Ren 1990), 22C (Horiuchi 2006), and 23N (Zhang 2014) to the neutron-rich boron isotope 15B. The identification of the 3/2_2^- state as a two-neutron halo is consistent with the WBT and WBT* shell model predictions (Warburton 1992, Stanoiu 2004), which also assign 3/2^- to the third excited state, but contradicts some other shell-model and AMD calculations (Kanada-Enyo 1995) that give different spin/parity assignments. The results enable future experimental studies of halo structure in 15B through reaction cross-section measurements on different targets, as recently performed for 13B, 14B, and 15B by Estrade (2014) and Tanaka (2017).
