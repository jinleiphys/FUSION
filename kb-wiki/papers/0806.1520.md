# A Realistic Formalism for 4N Bound State in a Three-Dimensional Yakubovsky Scheme

> A spin-isospin dependent Three-Dimensional formalism based on the momentum vectors for the four-nucleon bound state is presented. The four-nucleon Yakubovsky equations with two- and three-nucleon interactions are formulated as a function of the vector Jacobi momenta. Our formalism, according to the number of spin-isospin states that one takes into account, leads to only a strictly finite number of the coupled three-dimensional integral equations to be solved. The evaluation of the transition and permutation operators as well as the coordinate transformations due to considering the continuous angle variables instead of the discrete angular momentum quantum numbers are less complicated in comparison with partial wave representation. With respect to partial wave the present formalism with the smaller number of equations leads to higher dimensionality of the integral equations. We have concluded that Three-Dimensional formalism is less cumbersome for considering the three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The paper presents a spin-isospin dependent Three-Dimensional (3D) formalism for the four-nucleon bound state that replaces discrete angular momentum quantum numbers with continuous angle variables, leading to a strictly finite number of coupled integral equations that are energy independent, in contrast to the partial wave approach where the number of equations grows with energy.

## Method

The authors formulate the four-nucleon Yakubovsky equations with two- and three-nucleon interactions directly in terms of vector Jacobi momenta and spin-isospin quantum numbers, avoiding partial wave decomposition. They introduce two sets of 4N basis states corresponding to the "3+1" (123,4) and "2+2" (12,34) partitions, and evaluate the matrix elements of the NN t-matrix, permutation operators P, P-tilde, and P34, as well as the coordinate transformations between different Jacobi coordinate sets, all expressed through continuous angle variables and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The 3D formalism for the 3N bound state (3H and 3He) leads to only 24 coupled equations for the fully charge dependent case
- The 3H binding energy calculated with the Bonn-B potential using the 3D Faddeev approach yields -8.152 MeV, in good agreement with partial wave based methods
- The free 4N propagator is given by G0 = (E - H0)^{-1}, with the energy denominator expressed in terms of Jacobi momenta as E - u1^2/m - 3u2^2/(4m) - 2u3^2/(3m) for the "3+1" partition and E - v1^2/m - v2^2/(2m) - v3^2/m for the "2+2" partition

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous spin-isospin dependent 3D approach for the three-nucleon bound state (Bayegan, 2008) to the four-nucleon system, building on the Yakubovsky scheme developed by Nogga (2000). The formalism enables future numerical investigations of the alpha-particle binding energy with realistic two- and three-nucleon forces, including the Tucson-Melbourne two-pion exchange 3NF, and is intended to be more efficient than partial wave methods for higher-energy scattering problems.
