# Realistic Implementation of Chiral Magnetic Wave in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) is a gapless collective excitation of chiral charges along the direction of magnetic field in the Quark-Gluon Plasma that arises from the triangle anomaly of QCD. We perform reliable study of the CMW in the realistic simulation of heavy ion collisions, and find that the CMW contributions to the charge dependent elliptic flow of pions, $Δv_2 = v_2(π^-)-v_2(π^+)$, linearly depending on the net charge asymmetry $A_\pm\equiv (N_+-N_-)/(N_++N_-)$ with a positive slope $r$, is comparable to the recent experimental results from RHIC. We identify "Freezeout Hole Effect", which is a direct consequence of the propagation of CMW during the realistic evolution of fireball, as the dominant physics effect responsible for a sizable contribution from the CMW to the slope parameter $r$, and emphasize that a proper treatment of the freeze out condition is crucial in any reliable computation of the CMW contribution to the slope parameter $r$. We also implement chiral phase transition effect in our study, which illustrates the sensitivity of the results to chiral phase transition temperature, and suggest that the CMW can be an important probe of QCD chiral phase transition. Our results on the impact parameter dependence compare well with the RHIC experiments. We also give predictions for the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) contribution to the charge-dependent elliptic flow slope parameter \(r\) is comparable to (though somewhat less than) the experimental value from RHIC, but this result is highly sensitive to the freeze-out treatment and the chiral phase transition temperature; the "Freezeout Hole Effect" is identified as the dominant physics mechanism, and a proper freeze-out condition is crucial for any reliable computation.

## Method

The authors performed a 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic simulation (using the Romatschke code with Glauber initial conditions) of the quark-gluon plasma fireball, treating the charge asymmetry as a linear perturbation. They tracked chiral charge flow using co-moving fluid cell trajectories determined by the effective velocity \(\tilde{u}^\mu_{L,R} = u^\mu \mp u^\mu_\chi\), where \(u^\mu_\chi\) is the CMW velocity derived from lattice QCD susceptibilities, and applied a Cooper-Frye type freeze-out at constant temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV to compute the final pion momentum distributions. The magnetic field was modeled as a time-varying homogeneous field \(eB(\tau) = (eB)_{\rm max} / (1 + (\tau/\tau_B)^2)\), and a sharp chiral phase transition boundary was implemented where the CMW velocity drops to zero.

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter \(r\) (defined by \(\Delta v_2 = r A_\pm\)) receives a CMW contribution comparable to the experimental RHIC value, though the result is reduced by up to a factor of 5 when the chiral phase transition temperature \(T_\chi\) is varied from 150 MeV to 165 MeV.
* The magnetic field lifetime parameter \(\tau_B\) and the maximum field strength \((eB)_{\rm max}\) are used as inputs; the field profile is \(eB(\tau) = (eB)_{\rm max} / (1 + (\tau/\tau_B)^2)\).
* The freeze-out temperature is set to \(T_f = 120\) MeV, guided by Shen (2011).
* The initial time for the hydrodynamic simulation is \(\tau_i = 0.4\) fm.
* The CMW velocity is given by \(v_\chi = eN_c B / (2\pi^2 \chi_V)\), with \(\chi_V/T^2\) parametrized from lattice data as \(\chi_V/T^2 = 0.609 (1 - 8.29 e^{-T/0.048})\) (temperature \(T\) in GeV).
* The entropy density \(s/T^3\) is parametrized as \(s/T^3 = (-17.4 - 0.185/T^2 + 62.7 T^{0.33} - 46.2 T^{1.43})\) (temperature \(T\) in GeV).
* The ratio \(\kappa = r / \bar{v}_2\) is introduced as a convenient observable; predictions for the LHC energy show a different impact parameter dependence compared to RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the earlier CMW simulations of Burnier (2011, 2012), which used a static plasma, simplified freeze-out, and a blast wave approximation, by implementing a realistic 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic background with lattice QCD equation of state and susceptibility. The results contrast with the recent simulation of Hongo (2013), which claimed a negligibly small CMW contribution to \(r\); the authors attribute this discrepancy to Hongo (2013)'s crude freeze-out treatment (fixed-time surface) and neglect of the chiral phase transition effect. The work also relates to the study of Taghavi (2013), which discussed the smallness of snapshot charge magnitudes but did not address the integrated charge flow across the freeze-out surface that determines \(r\). The findings suggest that the CMW can serve as a probe of the QCD chiral phase transition temperature, and the predicted LHC impact parameter dependence of \(\kappa\) offers a testable signature.
