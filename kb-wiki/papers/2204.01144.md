# Spin and polarization: a new direction in relativistic heavy ion physics

> Since the first evidence of a global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in relativistic nuclear collisions in 2017, spin has opened a new window in the field, both at experimental and theoretical level, and an exciting perspective. The current state of the field is reviewed with regard to the theoretical understanding of the data, reporting on the most recent achievements and envisioning possible developments. The intriguing connections of spin physics in relativistic matter with fundamental questions in quantum field theory and applications in the non-relativistic domain are discussed.

## Key claim

Spin polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions, first observed in 2017 for Lambda hyperons, is now understood to arise from a combination of thermal vorticity and a newly identified quantum local equilibrium term (thermal shear tensor), which resolves the long-standing discrepancy between theory and the measured momentum-dependent polarization data at top RHIC energy.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of spin polarization in relativistic fluids, deriving the mean spin vector from local equilibrium conditions using the four-temperature vector beta = u/T and its gradients. The spin polarization vector is expressed as the sum of two leading-order terms: one proportional to the thermal vorticity (antisymmetric derivative of beta) and one proportional to the thermal shear tensor (symmetric derivative of beta), with the latter being a recently discovered quantum correction that is non-dissipative and vanishes at global equilibrium. The formalism is applied to the Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in relativistic nuclear collisions, where the hydrodynamic model provides the temperature, four-velocity, and chemical potential fields on the hadronization hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Lambda hyperons was first observed in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (STAR collaboration, 2017)
- The lowest energy polarization measurement by STAR reached sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV
- The spin polarization magnitude is of the order of a few percent, making the factor (2S_h+1) in the momentum spectrum a good approximation
- The new thermal shear term (equation 13) can increase the global polarization magnitude by almost a factor of 2 at low energy where baryon chemical potential is large, according to a recent study (Yiyin, 2022)

## Context

This review extends the work of Becattini et al. (2013-2017) who derived the thermal vorticity formula for spin polarization and successfully predicted the global polarization magnitude, but whose predictions failed for the momentum-dependent polarization measured by STAR (2019). The new thermal shear term, independently derived by Becattini et al. (2021) and Yiyin (2021), resolves this discrepancy at top RHIC energy when temperature gradients are removed assuming an isothermal hadronization hypersurface. The field now enables using spin polarization as a probe of initial hydrodynamic conditions and vorticity fields, with open questions including the role of dissipative corrections, the applicability of hydrodynamics at very low energies, and the splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda polarization possibly due to electromagnetic fields.
