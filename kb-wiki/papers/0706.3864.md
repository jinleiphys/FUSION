# Isospin Effects on Astrophysical S-Factors

> We estimate the ratios of bare astrophysical S-factors at zero incident energy for proton and deuteron induced reactions in a model which assumes a compound nucleus formation probability plus a statistical decay. The obtained ratios agree well with available experimental values, as far as the reactions which have dominant s-wave entrance channel components are investigated. Due to its simplicity the model could be used as a guidance for predictions on reactions which have not been investigated yet.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the bare astrophysical S-factor at zero incident energy can be estimated from a two-step compound-nucleus model (formation probability plus statistical decay), and that ratios of S-factors for reactions with identical entrance channels can be predicted without fitting parameters, provided the reactions have dominant s-wave entrance channel components.

## Method

The authors model low-energy fusion as compound nucleus (CN) formation with a tunneling probability modified by nuclear effects, followed by statistical decay using the Weisskopf model. They take the zero-energy limit, retain only the l=0 partial wave, and compute S-factor ratios for reactions with identical entrance channels (where screening effects cancel) using analytical expressions involving Q-values, nuclear radii, and level densities. The nuclear radius is parameterized as R_N = α_n × 1.2 × (A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3}) fm, with α_n as a free parameter.

## Context

This work extends the compound-nucleus approach to astrophysical S-factors, building on the R-matrix theory of Barker (various years) and the Weisskopf statistical model (1937). The model is simpler than full R-matrix calculations and is intended as a guidance for reactions not yet measured experimentally. The ratio method cancels electron screening effects, which are discussed in the context of screening potentials by Strieder et al. (various years) and others. The approach enables predictions for unmeasured reactions and can be extended to include l≥1 partial waves in future work.
