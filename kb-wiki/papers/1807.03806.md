# Constraining unintegrated gluon distributions from inclusive photon production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> We compute the leading order (LO) $qg\to q γ$ and next-to-leading order (NLO) $gg\to q{\bar q} γ$ contributions to inclusive photon production in proton-proton (p+p) collisions at the LHC. These channels provide the dominant contribution at LO and NLO for photon transverse momenta $k_{γ\perp}$ corresponding to momentum fractions of $x\leq 0.01$ in the colliding protons. Our computations, performed in the dilute-dense framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (CGC EFT), show that the NLO contribution dominates at small-$x$ because it is sensitive to $k_\perp$-dependent unintegrated gluon distributions in both of the protons. We predict a maximal $10\%$ modification of the cross section at low $k_{γ\perp}$ as a direct consequence of the violation of $k_\perp$-factorization. The coherence effects responsible for this modification are enhanced in nuclei and can be identified from inclusive photon measurements in proton-nucleus collisions. We provide numerical results for the isolated inclusive photon cross section for $k_{γ\perp}\leq 20$ GeV in p+p collisions that can be tested in the future at the LHC.

## Key claim

The NLO $gg\to q\bar q\gamma$ channel dominates inclusive photon production at LHC energies for photon transverse momenta $k_{\gamma\perp}\leq 20$ GeV, but the authors hedge that this dominance is conditional on probing momentum fractions $x\leq 0.01$ in the colliding protons; at RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV) the NLO contribution is only $\sim 10\%$, and the computation's systematic uncertainties increase outside the preferred kinematic regime where the dilute-dense approximation is robust.

## Method

The authors computed the LO $qg\to q\gamma$ and NLO $gg\to q\bar q\gamma$ contributions to inclusive photon production in p+p collisions within the dilute-dense framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, using the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for dipole evolution with McLerran-Venugopalan initial conditions at $x_0=0.01$. They performed 10-dimensional Monte Carlo integrations for the full CGC expression and 8-dimensional integrations for the $k_\perp$-factorized approximation, applying an isolation cut $R=0.4$ to separate direct photons from fragmentation contributions, and normalized their results to ATLAS data using a $K$-factor of $2.4$.

## Key numbers

- NLO fraction of the inclusive photon cross-section at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV (RHIC) is $\sim 10\%$ for $\eta_\gamma=1.0$
- At $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, the NLO contribution exceeds $60\%$ for all $k_{\gamma\perp}$ shown
- At $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and $13$ TeV, the NLO contribution exceeds $\sim 90\%$ for $k_{\gamma\perp}\leq 20$ GeV
- Maximal $10\%$ modification of the cross section at low $k_{\gamma\perp}$ due to violation of $k_\perp$-factorization
- $K$-factor of $2.4$ required to match the lowest ATLAS data point at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV
- Quark masses used: $m_u=m_d=0.005$ GeV, $m_s=0.095$ GeV, $m_c=1.3$ GeV, $m_b=4.5$ GeV
- Proton radius $R_p=0.48$ fm, corresponding to $S_\perp=7.24$ mb
- Systematic error band of $15\%$ included in comparisons to data

## Context

This work extends prior CGC calculations of inclusive photon production that considered only LO $qg\to q\gamma$ contributions (Kopeliovich 1998, Gelis 2002, Baier 2004, Kopeliovich 2007) and the authors' own NLO computation of the $gg\to q\bar q\gamma$ channel (Benic 2016). The results enable future constraints on unintegrated gluon distributions from LHC data, particularly from ALICE and forward LHC upgrades (LHCf, ALICE FoCal), and provide a foundation for predictions in proton-nucleus collisions and high-multiplicity p+p and p+A collisions. The framework has previously been applied successfully to heavy quarkonium production in p+p and p+A collisions (Ma 2014, Ma 2015, Ma 2017).
