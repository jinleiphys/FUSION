# Probing internucleus potential with large-angle quasi-elastic scattering

> Recent measurements for fusion cross section at energies around the Coulomb barrier have systematically indicated a significant deviation of fusion cross sections from a prediction of double-folding model. It has been argued that the deviation can be accounted for if one uses a large value of surface diffuseness parameter for a Woods-Saxon internuclear potential. We investigate this problem using large-angle quasi-elastic scattering, that is a good counterpart of fusion reaction. Towards a reconciliation of the apparent anomaly in the diffuseness parameter for fusion reactions, we also discuss possible ingredients which are missing in present nuclear reaction models. Those include a deviation from the frozen density approximation, the effect of antisymmetrization and the role of coordinate dependent moment of inertia for the relative motion between the colliding nuclei.

# Probing internucleus potential with large-angle quasi-elastic scattering

## Key claim

Large-angle quasi-elastic scattering at deep sub-barrier energies can probe the surface diffuseness parameter of the internucleus potential in a transparent way, but the extracted diffuseness depends on whether the colliding nuclei are spherical or deformed. For spherical systems the diffuseness parameter is around 0.60 fm (consistent with the double folding model), while for deformed systems it is significantly larger (0.79–1.14 fm), though the deformed-system results are more model-dependent and may be affected by normalization choices in the experimental data.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental large-angle quasi-elastic scattering data at deep sub-barrier energies (where the ratio of quasi-elastic to Rutherford cross sections exceeds 0.94) for several heavy-ion systems. For spherical systems (³²S + ¹⁹⁷Au, ³⁴S + ¹⁹⁷Au, ³²,³⁴S + ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb), they used a one-dimensional optical potential with Woods-Saxon form, varying the surface diffuseness parameter while keeping the barrier height fixed at the Akyüz-Winther value. For deformed systems (¹⁶O + ¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁶O + ¹⁸⁶W), they included the effect of target deformation in coupled-channels calculations and fitted data both below and around the lowest barrier.

## Key numbers

- For ³²S + ¹⁹⁷Au: best-fit surface diffuseness parameter a = 0.57 ± 0.04 fm
- For ³⁴S + ¹⁹⁷Au: best-fit surface diffuseness parameter a = 0.53 ± 0.03 fm
- For ¹⁶O + ¹⁵⁴Sm: best-fit surface diffuseness parameter a = 1.14 ± 0.03 fm
- For ¹⁶O + ¹⁸⁶W: best-fit surface diffuseness parameter a = 0.79 ± 0.04 fm
- The conventional surface diffuseness parameter used for elastic and inelastic scattering is around 0.63 fm
- Fusion reaction data require a larger diffuseness parameter ranging between 0.75 and 1.5 fm (citing Newton (2004))

## Context

This work extends the debate over the "diffuseness anomaly" in fusion reactions, where fusion cross sections require larger surface diffuseness parameters (0.75–1.5 fm) than the conventional value of ~0.63 fm used in elastic and inelastic scattering (Broglia and Winther (1991), Christensen (1976)). The authors show that large-angle quasi-elastic scattering at deep sub-barrier energies gives diffuseness values consistent with the double folding model for spherical systems, suggesting the anomaly originates from dynamics inside the Coulomb barrier rather than the surface region. The paper discusses two missing ingredients in present reaction models: the role of antisymmetrization (beyond the knock-on exchange term in the double folding model) and the possibility of a coordinate-dependent moment of inertia for relative motion in an adiabatic treatment, which could introduce strong angular momentum dependence in the internucleus potential.
