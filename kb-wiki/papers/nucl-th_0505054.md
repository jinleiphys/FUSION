# Inhomogeneities in the freeze-out of relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS

> We study the role of temperature and density inhomogeneities on the freeze-out of relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS. Especially the impact on the particle abundancies is investigated. The quality of the fits to the measured particle ratios in 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions significantly improves as compared to a homogeneous model.

## Key claim
Allowing for Gaussian-distributed temperature and baryon-chemical-potential inhomogeneities on the freeze-out surface significantly improves the fit to measured particle ratios in 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions compared to a homogeneous model, but the extracted mean temperature and chemical potential do not directly correspond to the actual emission temperatures of the particles.

## Method
The author models an inhomogeneous fireball at chemical decoupling by assuming that the intensive variables temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B are distributed according to a Gaussian. The average density of each hadron species is computed by folding the ideal-gas local density with these Gaussian distributions, including feeding from strong and weak decays. A χ² fit is performed to midrapidity and 4π particle multiplicities measured by the NA49 collaboration in √s_NN = 17.3 GeV Pb+Pb collisions, comparing the homogeneous case (δT = δμ = 0) with the inhomogeneous case where δT and δμ are free parameters.

## Key numbers
- For midrapidity data: χ²/dof = 40.4/8 for the homogeneous fit (T̄ = 155 ± 5 MeV, μ̄_B = 200 ± 10 MeV) versus χ²/dof = 11.2/6 for the inhomogeneous fit (T̄ = 105 ± 5 MeV, μ̄_B = 230 ± 15 MeV, δT = 35 ± 5 MeV, δμ_B = 80 ± 40 MeV).
- For 4π data: χ²/dof = 40.0/11 for the homogeneous fit (T̄ = 145 ± 5 MeV, μ̄_B = 210 ± 15 MeV) versus χ²/dof = 5.7/9 for the inhomogeneous fit (T̄ = 100 ± 5 MeV, μ̄_B = 260 ± 15 MeV, δT = 30 ± 5 MeV, δμ_B = 190 ± 35 MeV).
- Mean emission temperatures for the inhomogeneous 4π fit range from 136 MeV (protons) to 165 MeV (anti-Omega); mean emission chemical potentials range from 22 MeV (anti-protons) to 487 MeV (protons).

## Context
This work extends homogeneous thermal-model analyses of particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (e.g., the compilation by Becattini (2003)) by introducing a simple Gaussian parametrization of freeze-out inhomogeneities, following the suggestion by Dumitru (2005) that such inhomogeneities arise from first-order phase transitions in non-equilibrium hydrodynamical simulations (Paech, 2003). The results suggest that the decoupling surface may not be well stirred and that inhomogeneities could serve as a variable connecting measured particle abundances to the expansion dynamics; the author notes that investigations at lower SPS and RHIC energies are underway.
