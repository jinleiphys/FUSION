# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic form factors in light nuclei

> We present Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic form factors in $A=6-10$ nuclei, based on Norfolk two- and three-nucleon interactions, and associated one- and two-body electromagnetic currents. Agreement with the available experimental data for $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^9$Be and $^{10}$B up to values of momentum transfer $q\sim 3$ fm$^{-1}$ is achieved when two-nucleon currents are accounted for. We present a set of predictions for the magnetic form factors of $^7$Be, $^8$Li, $^9$Li, and $^9$C. In these systems, two-body currents account for $\sim40-60\%$ of the total magnetic strength. Measurements in any of these radioactive systems would provide valuable insights on the nuclear magnetic structure emerging from the underlying many-nucleon dynamics. A particularly interesting case is that of $^7$Be, as it would enable investigations of the magnetic structure of mirror nuclei.

# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic form factors in light nuclei

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic form factors for A=6-10 nuclei, using chiral effective field theory based Norfolk two- and three-nucleon interactions with consistent one- and two-body electromagnetic currents, achieve agreement with experimental data for ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, and ¹⁰B up to momentum transfer q ~ 3 fm⁻¹, but only when two-nucleon currents are included. The authors note that for ⁶Li specifically, the agreement is less satisfactory than for the other nuclei, possibly due to deficiencies in the adopted many-body nuclear model.

## Method
The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of magnetic form factors for nuclei with A=6-10, using the Norfolk NV2+3-IIb* chiral interactions (derived up to N3LO in chiral effective field theory with explicit pions, nucleons, and intermediate Delta resonances) and consistent one- and two-body electromagnetic currents evaluated up to N3LO. The magnetic form factors were extracted from reduced matrix elements of magnetic multipole operators M1, M3, and M5, computed from the electromagnetic current operator matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- Two-body currents account for approximately 40-60% of the total magnetic strength in ⁷Be, ⁸Li, ⁹Li, and ⁹C in the momentum transfer region 0.5 ≤ q ≤ 3 fm⁻¹
- Agreement with experimental data is achieved up to momentum transfer q ~ 3 fm⁻¹ for ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, and ¹⁰B
- The theoretical uncertainty band reflects contributions from terms beyond N3LO in the currents, estimated using the approach of Epelbaum (2014)
- For ¹⁰B, the M5 multipole fills the diffraction minimum, confirming a hypothesis from Donnelly (1984)
- In ⁷Be, ⁸Li, ⁹Li, and ⁹C, the M1 diffraction minimum is filled by the M3 multipole, with an inversion of M1 and M3 multipole strengths observed between mirror nuclei pairs (⁹Li-⁹C, ⁷Li-⁷Be, ⁹Be-⁹B)

## Context
This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of magnetic form factors in A=2 and A=3 nuclei (Carlson 1997, Phillips 2006, Piarulli 2012, Gnech 2022) and the VMC study of ⁶Li using Argonne AV18+UIX interactions (Wiringa 1998) to the A=6-10 mass region using a chiral effective field theory framework. It provides the first microscopic calculations of magnetic form factors for ⁷Be, ⁸Li, ⁹Li, and ⁹C, for which no experimental data currently exist. The predictions are relevant to experimental programs at RIKEN and other facilities developing techniques for electron scattering from radioactive nuclei (Tsukada 2023), and would enable investigations of magnetic structure in mirror nuclei, particularly the ⁷Li-⁷Be system.
