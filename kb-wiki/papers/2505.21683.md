# Quantum correlation dynamics and in-medium 3$\leftrightarrow$3 collisions of fermions

> In this study we aim for quantifying the role of in-medium 3$\leftrightarrow$3 collisions for systems of fermions which initially are out-off equilibrium. The formulation of the 3-body dynamics is based on the equations of motion method for identical fermions -- also denoted as quantum correlation dynamics -- and presented in detail. The on-shell 2-body collision integral is briefly reviewed and the on-shell 3-body collision integral is derived on the basis of the same two-body interaction in leading order. The resulting equations obey particle number as well as energy-momentum conservation. For a quantification of the relative impact of 3-body interactions we employ a model study for a homogeneous system in space in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. We address spin-isospin symmetric nuclear matter systems with momentum distributions that are given by shifted Fermi spheres (without overlap) as encountered in the initial phase of nucleus-nucleus collisions after contact. The results for the relaxation times -- employing an effective 2-body interaction -- are compared to Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport calculations in the continuum limit for the same bombarding energies and are found to agree on the level of a few percent. We find that the additional 3-body interactions reduce the relaxation times up to a factor of 3 at 130 A$\cdot$MeV. Furthermore, it is shown in BUU transport calculations that an enhanced stopping by 3$\leftrightarrow$3 collisions shows up in the angular distribution of energetic nucleons ($>$ 60 MeV) e.g. in central $^{97}_{45}Rh$ collisions at 40 A$\cdot$MeV that lead to the formation of a compound nucleus. The angular distribution of the energetic nucleons changes from a slightly forward peaked angular distribution to a slightly sidewards peaked angular distribution which might be controlled experimentally.

# Quantum correlation dynamics and in-medium 3↔3 collisions of fermions

## Key claim

The paper claims that in-medium 3↔3 collisions reduce relaxation times by up to a factor of 3 at 130 A·MeV for spin-isospin symmetric nuclear matter, but this result is obtained using a model study with a homogeneous system in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions and an effective 2-body interaction, not from experimental data or a full realistic nuclear interaction.

## Method

The author derives the on-shell 3-body collision integral from the equations of motion method for identical fermions (quantum correlation dynamics) using the same two-body interaction in leading order, then solves the resulting equations for a homogeneous system in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The momentum distributions are given by shifted Fermi spheres without overlap, mimicking the initial phase of nucleus-nucleus collisions after contact. The relaxation times from the full quantum correlation dynamics are compared to Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport calculations in the continuum limit for the same bombarding energies.

## Key numbers

- The additional 3-body interactions reduce relaxation times up to a factor of 3 at 130 A·MeV for spin-isospin symmetric nuclear matter.
- The BUU transport calculations agree with the quantum correlation dynamics results on the level of a few percent for the relaxation times.
- The angular distribution of energetic nucleons (above 60 MeV) in central ⁹⁷₄₅Rh collisions at 40 A·MeV changes from slightly forward peaked to slightly sidewards peaked when 3↔3 collisions are included.
- The study addresses bombarding energies below about 130 A·MeV where the degrees of freedom are just identical fermions (nucleons).

## Context

This work extends the BBGKY hierarchy approach and quantum correlation dynamics methods previously developed by Cassing and collaborators (Cassing 1987, 1988, 1990, 1992) to include explicit 3↔3 collision terms for identical fermions. It goes beyond standard Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport models (Bertsch 1984, Aichelin 1985, Bertsch 1988) and Time-Dependent-Density-Matrix (TDDM) approaches (Tohyama 1989, Gong 1990) by quantifying the role of three-body interactions in the energy domain below 130 A·MeV where only elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering is available. The results enable improved transport simulations for low-energy heavy-ion collisions and suggest that experimental measurements of angular distributions of energetic nucleons could reveal the presence of 3-body scattering effects.
