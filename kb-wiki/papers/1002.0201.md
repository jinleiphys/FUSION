# Baryon resonance production in the $π+d$ reaction and search for $η$-mesic nuclei at J-PARC

> A double-scattering reaction $π^+ + d\to p + p + η$, where an $η$ meson is rescattered, may provide new information on the $ηN\to ηN$ scattering. An idea to measure this reaction is proposed. Moreover, experimental search for $η$-mesic nuclei by the same $(π, N)$ reaction is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a double-scattering reaction π⁺ + d → p + p + η, where an η meson is rescattered off a proton, may provide new information on ηN → ηN scattering, but notes that the ηN scattering length has a large ambiguity (real part between 0.270 fm and 1.050 fm, imaginary part between 0.190 fm and 0.399 fm), and that a new calculation for the π⁺ + d reaction based on models for π⁺ + p and p + p reactions is awaited.

## Method

The authors propose using the (π, N) reaction on nuclear targets at the J-PARC facility, specifically at the K1.8BR beamline, to study two processes: (1) the double-scattering reaction π⁺ + d → p + p + η, where the quasi-free reaction π⁺ + n → p + η is followed by η + p → η + p rescattering, with the goal of detecting a slow proton (∼186 MeV/c) from S₁₁(1535) decay to separate double-scattering from quasi-free events; (2) the (π⁻, n) reaction on ⁷Li and ¹²C targets to search for η-mesic nuclei, using missing-mass spectroscopy with detection of back-to-back Nπ pairs from N*(1535) decay, under recoilless conditions (proton scattered at ∼0°).

## Key numbers

- ηN scattering length real part: between 0.270 fm and 1.050 fm; imaginary part: between 0.190 fm and 0.399 fm (Haider 2002)
- S₁₁(1535) resonance lies about 50 MeV above the ηN threshold, with decay branching ratio into η+N of 45–60% (PDG 2008)
- Proton from S₁₁(1535) decay (mass 1.535 GeV/c²) has momentum of ∼186 MeV/c
- Optimal beam momentum for the double-scattering reaction: around 0.8–1.1 GeV/c
- For η-mesic nuclei search, the magic momentum for η energy ω_η = m_η − 50 MeV is 777 MeV/c, and for ω_η = m_η is 950 MeV/c
- TAPS/MAMI experiment on γ + ³He → η + X: binding energy −4.4 ± 4.2 MeV, width 25.6 ± 6.1 MeV (Pfeiffer 2004)
- COSY-GEM experiment on ²⁷Al(p, ³He) reaction: binding energy −13.13 ± 1.64 MeV, width 10.22 ± 2.98 MeV for ²⁵Mg ⊗ η system (COSY-GEM 2009)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of η-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986), who found that bound states may exist for mass number A ≥ 12 using scattering lengths of (0.28+0.29i) fm or (0.27+0.22i) fm. The paper revisits the BNL experiment by Chrien (1988) that searched for η-mesic nuclei using the (π⁺, p) reaction on lithium, carbon, oxygen, and aluminum targets but found no peak structure, proposing improvements by using recoilless kinematics and detecting Nπ decay pairs in coincidence. The proposed experiments at J-PARC could constrain theoretical models of in-medium ηN interaction and the S₁₁(1535) resonance, potentially distinguishing between the chiral doublet model (where level crossing between N* and N modes may occur) and the chiral unitary model (predicting a shallow bound state similar to Garcia-Recio 2002).
