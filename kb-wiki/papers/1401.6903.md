# Elliptic Flow and Shear Viscosity of the Shattered Color Glass Condensate

> In this talk, we report on our results about the computation of the elliptic flow of the quark-gluon-plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, simulating the expansion of the fireball by solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation for the parton distribution function tuned at a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$. We emphasize the role of saturation in the initial gluon spectrum modelling the shattering of the color glass condensate, causing the initial distribution to be out of equilibrium. We find that the saturation reduces the efficiency in building-up the elliptic flow, even if the thermalization process is quite fast $τ_{therm} \approx 0.8 \,\rm fm/c$. and the pressure isotropization even faster $τ_{isotr} \approx 0.3 \,\rm fm/c$. The impact of the initial non-equilibrium manifests for non-central collisions and can modify the estimate of the viscosity respect to the assumption of full thermalization in $p_T$-space.

# Elliptic Flow and Shear Viscosity of the Shattered Color Glass Condensate

## Key claim

The initial non-equilibrium momentum distribution with a saturation scale, as produced by the shattering of the color glass condensate, reduces the efficiency of converting spatial eccentricity into elliptic flow compared to thermalized initial conditions, even though thermalization occurs rapidly (τ_therm ≈ 0.8 fm/c) and pressure isotropization even faster (τ_isotr ≈ 0.3 fm/c). This effect modifies the estimate of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s by about a factor of two relative to the assumption of full thermalization in p_T-space, and is most pronounced for semi-peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for the parton distribution function to simulate the expansion of the fireball produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. They fix the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s cell-by-cell in coordinate space using the Chapmann-Enskog relation between cross section and viscosity, rather than computing microscopic scattering processes. Three initial conditions are compared: a thermalized Glauber model (Th-Glauber), a thermalized factorized-KLN model (Th-fKLN) where only the spatial distribution follows the KLN model, and a full fKLN initialization where both the coordinate-space distribution and the momentum-space spectrum are given by the KLN model with saturation below Q_s.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time τ_therm ≈ 0.8 fm/c for the fKLN initial condition at both RHIC and LHC energies
- Pressure isotropization time τ_isotr ≈ 0.3 fm/c for 4πη/s = 1
- Initial time τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c for RHIC runs and τ_0 = 0.3 fm/c for LHC runs in the thermalized cases; τ_0 = 0.2 fm/c for the fKLN initialization
- Maximum temperature T_0 = 0.34 GeV for Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy (√s = 200 A GeV) and T_0 = 0.51 GeV for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy
- Average saturation scale ⟨Q_s⟩_{y=0} ≈ 1 GeV for most central collisions at longitudinal momentum fraction x = 0.01
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC with b = 7.5 fm, the Th-Glauber initialization with 4πη/s = 1 gives the same v_2(p_T) as the Th-fKLN initialization with 4πη/s ≈ 2
- The full fKLN initialization with 4πη/s = 1 produces v_2(p_T) similar to Th-Glauber with 4πη/s = 1, despite the larger initial eccentricity of the fKLN spatial distribution

## Context

This work extends viscous hydrodynamical studies of elliptic flow and η/s determination (Luzum 2008, Alver 2010, Song 2011, Adare 2011) by using kinetic theory at fixed η/s, which can handle initial conditions far from equilibrium unlike hydrodynamics. The authors implement the factorized-KLN model (Kharzeev 2004, Drescher 2006, Hirano 2009) for the shattered color glass condensate initial condition, including the saturation feature in momentum space that previous hydrodynamical implementations discarded. The results show that the initial momentum-space distribution with saturation reduces v_2 relative to thermalized initial conditions with the same spatial eccentricity, implying that η/s estimates from hydrodynamics that assume thermalized p_T spectra may need revision by about a factor of two. This work enables future studies of higher-order flow harmonics v_n using fluctuating initial conditions combined with kinetic theory, relaxing the assumption of sudden thermalization used in hybrid approaches combining classical Yang-Mills dynamics with hydrodynamics (Gale 2012).
