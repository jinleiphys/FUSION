# Chiral effective field theory on the lattice at next-to-leading order

> We study nucleon-nucleon scattering on the lattice at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. We determine phase shifts and mixing angles from the properties of two-nucleon standing waves induced by a hard spherical wall in the center-of-mass frame. At fixed lattice spacing we test model independence of the low-energy effective theory by computing next-to-leading-order corrections for two different leading-order lattice actions. The first leading-order action includes instantaneous one-pion exchange and same-site contact interactions. The second leading-order action includes instantaneous one-pion exchange and Gaussian-smeared interactions. We find that in each case the results at next-to-leading order are accurate up to corrections expected at higher order.

## Key claim

At next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory on the lattice, nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and mixing angles computed using two different leading-order lattice actions (same-site contact interactions versus Gaussian-smeared interactions) agree up to corrections expected at higher order, provided the cutoff momentum is set low enough (approximately 314 MeV) to avoid sign/phase problems in Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and mixing angles on a three-dimensional spatial lattice with spacing a = (100 MeV)^{-1} and temporal spacing a_t = (70 MeV)^{-1}, using the spherical wall method to impose a hard spherical boundary on the relative separation between two nucleons in the center-of-mass frame. They constructed two different leading-order lattice actions (LO1 with same-site contact interactions and LO2 with Gaussian-smeared interactions), fitted the leading-order contact coefficients to S-wave scattering data, and then added the same set of seven next-to-leading-order contact interactions with two derivatives to both actions. Phase shifts were extracted from the energies of spherical standing waves vanishing at the wall boundary, with the spin-triplet case requiring simultaneous solution for coupled partial waves and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = (100 MeV)^{-1}, temporal spacing: a_t = (70 MeV)^{-1}
- Cutoff momentum: Lambda = 314 MeV, approximately 2.3 times the pion mass (m_pi = 138.08 MeV)
- Leading-order contact coefficients for LO1: C^{I=1} = -5.021 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}, C^{I=0} = -5.714 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}
- Leading-order contact coefficients for LO2: C^{I=1} = -3.414 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}, C^{I=0} = -4.780 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}
- Physical constants used: nucleon mass m = 938.92 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138.08 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, axial charge g_A = 1.26

## Context

This work extends the lattice chiral effective field theory formalism developed by Borasoy et al. (2006) from leading order to next-to-leading order, and is the first of a pair of papers on NLO lattice chiral EFT. The coarse-lattice approach (Lambda approximately 2.3 m_pi) is similar in motivation to the continuum low-momentum potential V_low k derived using the renormalization group by Bogner et al. (2001, 2003). The companion paper uses the resulting lattice action to perform Monte Carlo simulations of dilute neutron matter, and future work is planned to study different lattice spacings and functional forms for quasi-local operators.
