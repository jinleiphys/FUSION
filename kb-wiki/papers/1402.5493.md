# Residue Coulomb interaction among isobars and its influence in symmetry energy of neutron-rich fragment

> The residue Coulomb interaction (RCI), which affects the results of symmetry energy of neutron-rich nucleus in isobaric yield ratio (IYR) methods, is difficult to be determined. Four RCI approximations are investigated: (1) the M1--RCI adopting the $a_{c}/T$ (the ratio of Coulomb energy coefficient to temperature) determined from the IYR of mirror-nuclei fragment; (2) the M2--RCI by fitting the difference between IYRs; (3) the M3--RCI by adopting the standard Coulomb energy at a temperature $T=2$MeV; and (4) neglecting the RCI among the three isobars. The M1--, M2-- and M3--RCI is found to no larger than 0.4. In particular, the M2--RCI is very close to zero. The effects of RCI in the $a_{sym}/T$ of fragment are also studied. The M1-- and M4--$a_{sym}/T$ are found to be the lower and upper limitations of $a_{sym}/T$, respectively. The M2--$a_{sym}/T$ overlaps the M4--$a_{sym}/T$, which indicates that the M2--RCI is negligible, at the same time the RCI among the three isobars can be neglected. A relative consistent low values of M3--$a_{sym}/T$ ($7.5\pm2.5$) are found in very neutron-rich isobars.

## Key claim

The residue Coulomb interaction (RCI) among three isobars in the isobaric yield ratio (IYR) method is found to be negligible, with the M2-RCI approximation (obtained by fitting the difference between IYRs) being very close to zero, and the omission of RCI among three isobars is verified to be reasonable for determining the symmetry energy of neutron-rich fragments.

## Method

Four approximations for the residue Coulomb interaction (RCI) were investigated using fragment yields from the 140A MeV 58Ni + 9Be reaction (measured at NSCL) and the 1A GeV 124,136Xe + Pb reactions (measured at GSI). The M1-RCI adopts the ac/T ratio determined from the IYR of mirror-nuclei fragments; the M2-RCI is obtained by fitting the difference between IYRs using the correlation between (ΔlnR-Δ21)/A and 1/A2; the M3-RCI uses the standard Coulomb energy calculated at T=2 MeV; and the M4-RCI neglects the RCI among the three isobars entirely.

## Key numbers

- The M1-, M2-, and M3-RCI values are all no larger than 0.4.
- The M2-RCI is very close to zero, indicating the RCI among three isobars can be neglected.
- The M3-asym/T yields a relative consistent low value of 7.5 ± 2.5 in very neutron-rich isobars.
- For the M1 approximation, ac/T values of 0.55, 0.71, and 0.52 are used for the 58Ni, 124Xe, and 136Xe reactions, respectively.
- The difference between RCIs obtained from two fitting methods for mirror nuclei is no larger than 0.2.

## Context

This work extends previous IYR methods for determining symmetry energy of neutron-rich fragments, which were developed by Huang (2010), Ma (2011, 2012), and Marini (2012). The study addresses the difficulty in separating temperature and Coulomb energy contributions to free energy, which was noted in earlier works by Mei (2010) and Ma (2012). The finding that RCI among three isobars can be neglected enables simpler determination of asym/T for neutron-rich fragments using Eq. (asym3) without requiring Coulomb corrections, and the consistent low values of M3-asym/T (7.5 ± 2.5) in very neutron-rich isobars support the conclusion that symmetry energy coefficients become similar for fragments with large neutron excess, consistent with earlier observations by Ma (2012, 2013).
