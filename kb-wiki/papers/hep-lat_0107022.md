# Quenched QCD at finite temperature with chiral Fermions

> We study physics at temperatures just above the QCD phase transition (Tc) using chiral (overlap) Fermions in the quenched approximation of lattice QCD. Exact zero modes of the overlap Dirac operator are localized and their frequency of occurrence drops with temperature. This is closely related to axial U(1) symmetry, which remains broken up to 2Tc. After subtracting the effects of these zero modes, chiral symmetry is restored, as indicated by the behavior of the chiral condensate. The pseudoscalar and vector screening masses are close to ideal gas values.

## Key claim

Using chiral (overlap) fermions in quenched lattice QCD, the authors show that axial U(1) symmetry remains broken up to 2Tc, and that after subtracting the effects of localized zero modes of the overlap Dirac operator, chiral symmetry is restored and screening masses approach ideal gas values.

## Method

The authors generated quenched QCD configurations at T/Tc = 1.25, 1.5, and 2 on 4×8³ and 4×12³ lattices, using overlap fermions defined via the Wilson-Dirac operator. They computed quark propagators for 10 quark masses from ma=0.001 to 0.5 using a multimass inversion, identified exact zero and near-zero modes of the overlap Dirac operator, and subtracted their contributions to the chiral condensate and screening correlators. The Ginsparg-Wilson relation was verified to an accuracy of 10⁻⁹, and chiral Ward identities were satisfied to better than 1 part in 10⁵.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: T/Tc = 1.25, 1.5, and 2.0, corresponding to lattice couplings β = 5.8, 5.8941, and 6.0625 respectively
- Zero modes had eigenvalues μ² < 10⁻⁷ at T=1.5Tc and 2Tc, with localization measure σ ≈ (3-8)×10⁻³ on 4×12³ lattices
- Non-zero modes had eigenvalues 0.1 ≲ μ² and localization measure σ < 10⁻³
- Pseudoscalar screening masses M_PS are within 10% of ideal gas values for all T ≥ 1.25Tc and ma < 0.1
- The ratio m_PS(T)/m_V(T) is within 10% of unity for T ≥ 1.25Tc

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of screening masses above Tc using staggered quarks (DeTar, 1987; Laermann et al., 1986) and Wilson quarks (Iwasaki et al., 1996), which had shown strong deviations from ideal gas behavior in the scalar and pseudoscalar channels. By using overlap fermions that preserve chiral symmetry on the lattice, the authors resolve this puzzle and show that the anomalous behavior was due to zero mode contributions. The results enable future studies of whether near-zero modes at T ≤ 1.25Tc are quenched artifacts or persist in full QCD with dynamical fermions.
