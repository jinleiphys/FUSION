The long distance behaviour, related to the deuteron bound state properties, is evaluated using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with one particle on mass shell. # Relativistic deuteron structure function at large Q^2

## Key claim

The deuteron structure function F_2^D calculated using the Bethe-Salpeter formalism with one nucleon on mass shell shows important deviations from other convolution models in the region of large x (x_N > 0.3), and the extraction of the neutron structure function from combined deuteron and proton data is model dependent and particularly sensitive to the features of the deuteron wave function, including the presence of P states.

## Method

The deuteron deep inelastic unpolarized structure function F_2^D was calculated using the Wilson operator product expansion method, with the long distance behaviour evaluated from the Bethe-Salpeter equation with one particle on mass shell. The deuteron Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes were obtained from a quasi-potential equation using a nucleon-nucleon interaction involving meson exchange (sigma, omega, pion, rho), with two different couplings for the pion: a pseudovector coupling (lambda=0, model BS0) and a mixed pseudoscalar-pseudovector coupling (lambda=0.4, model BS04). The ratio F_2^D/2F_2^N was compared with results from a simpler Bethe-Salpeter model (BSsm), a non-relativistic calculation (NR), and a light-cone calculation (LC).

## Key numbers

- The momentum fraction carried by mesons (delta_N) is 6.3 x 10^{-3} for the BS0 model and 7.2 x 10^{-3} for the BS04 model, compared to 5.0 x 10^{-3} for the NR calculation, 3.9 x 10^{-3} for the BSsm model, and 0 for the LC calculation
- For the BS04 model with lambda=0.4, the percentage of P states in the deuteron wave function is 0.45 percent
- The discrepancy between models becomes significant for x_N > 0.3
- The discrepancy with experimental data at large x is of order approximately 25 percent and exceeds experimental error bars

## Context

This work extends the operator product expansion method described in Refs. [a10,a11] to the deuteron, using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with one particle on mass shell following the Gross prescription (Ref. [a15]). The results show that binding effects produce important deviations from simpler convolution models, particularly the light-cone calculation (Ref. [a19]) and non-relativistic calculations (Ref. [a10]), and that the presence of deuteron P states (only in the BS04 model) causes deviations even at small x. This work enables a more consistent extraction of the neutron structure function from combined deuteron and proton data, addressing the model dependence noted in Ref. [a14] where additional corrections were shown to be important in the deuteron.
