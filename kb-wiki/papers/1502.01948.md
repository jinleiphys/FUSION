# Vector Meson Spectral Functions in a Coarse-Graining Approach

> Dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at top SPS energy is investigated within a coarse-graining approach that combines an underlying microscopic evolution of the nuclear reaction with the application of medium-modified spectral functions. Extracting local energy and baryon density for a grid of small space-time cells and going to each cell's rest frame enables to determine local temperature and chemical potential by application of an equation of state. This allows for the calculation of thermal dilepton emission. We apply and compare two different spectral functions for the $ρ$: A hadronic many-body calculation and an approach that uses empirical scattering amplitudes. Quantitatively good agreement of the model calculations with the data from the NA60 collaboration is achieved for both spectral functions, but in detail the hadronic many-body approach leads to a better description, especially of the broadening around the pole mass of the $ρ$ and for the low-mass excess. We further show that the presence of a pion chemical potential significantly influences the dilepton yield.

## Key claim

The coarse-graining approach, which combines a microscopic transport simulation (UrQMD) with medium-modified spectral functions, achieves quantitatively good agreement with NA60 dilepton data for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV, but only when a finite pion chemical potential is included; without it, the low-mass yield (0.2–0.4 GeV/c²) is underestimated by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors used 1000 UrQMD events for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV, dividing the space-time into cells of 0.8 fm per side and time steps of 0.2 fm/c. For each cell, they extracted local energy and baryon density, boosted to the local rest frame, and determined temperature and baryochemical potential using a hadron-gas equation of state matched to a lattice QCD equation of state above 170 MeV. They then calculated thermal dilepton emission rates using two different in-medium ρ spectral functions: a hadronic many-body calculation (Rapp SF) and an approach using empirical scattering amplitudes (Eletsky SF), and compared the resulting invariant-mass spectra to NA60 data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum energy density in the central cell reaches 100 times ground-state density; maximum baryon density reaches 20 times ρ₀.
- Maximum temperature in the central cell is roughly 300 MeV, reached about 1 fm/c after the start of the collision.
- Critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV used for the transition from hadron-gas to lattice equation of state.
- Without a pion chemical potential, the dilepton yield in the mass range 0.2–0.4 GeV/c² is underestimated by a factor of two compared to the data.
- The coarse-graining grid used cell dimensions Δx = Δy = Δz = 0.8 fm and time step Δt = 0.2 fm/c.
- The ensemble used 1000 UrQMD events with impact parameters from 0 to 8.5 fm, corresponding to ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ = 119.

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach proposed by Huovinen (2002) and detailed by Endres (2014) to top SPS energy, combining microscopic transport from the UrQMD model with in-medium spectral functions. It compares two spectral function approaches: the hadronic many-body calculation by Rapp (1999) and the empirical scattering amplitude method by Eletsky (2001), finding that the Rapp SF gives a better description of the low-mass excess and ρ broadening. The results are consistent with fireball-model calculations by van Hees (2006) that also found a finite pion chemical potential to be crucial, but the coarse-graining approach provides a more realistic space-time evolution than simplified fireball parametrizations. The work enables further application of the method to other collision energies from SIS to LHC.
