# Gamow-Teller transitions and the spin EMC effect: the Bjorken sum-rule in medium

> Gamow-Teller transitions in nuclei tell us that the nucleon's axial charge $g_A^{(3)}$ is quenched in large nuclei by about 20%. This result tells us that the spin structure of the nucleon is modified in nuclei and disfavours models of the medium dependence of parton structure based only on nucleon short range correlations in nuclei. For polarized photoproduction the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral is expected to be strongly enhanced in medium.

## Key claim

Gamow-Teller transitions in nuclei indicate that the nucleon's isovector axial charge \(g_A^{(3)}\) is quenched by about 20% in large nuclei, which implies that the spin structure of the nucleon is modified in the nuclear medium and disfavors models of the EMC nuclear effect that rely solely on nucleon short-range correlations.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from Gamow-Teller transitions (beta decays of large nuclei) to extract the effective axial charge in medium, \(g_A^{*(3)}\), and connects this to the Bjorken sum-rule for polarized deep inelastic scattering. It then compares predictions from mean-field models (e.g., quark-meson coupling model, NJL model) and short-range correlation models for the spin-dependent structure function \(g_1\) in nuclei, using the quenching of \(g_A^{(3)}\) as a constraint. The analysis extends to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum-rule for polarized photoproduction, estimating medium enhancements based on changes in nucleon and \(\Delta\) resonance masses and magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- The effective axial charge in medium, \(g_A^{*(3)}\), is quenched by about 20% in large nuclei (close to nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)), yielding \(g_A^{*(3)} \simeq 1\).
- For free protons, \(g_A^{(3)} = 1.270 \pm 0.003\) from neutron beta decays.
- In the QMC model, \(g_A^{*(3)}\) is reduced by about 10% at \(\rho_0\); including OGE and pion cloud effects gives about 14% reduction.
- NJL model calculations give \(g_A^{*(3)}\) reduced by about 20% at \(\rho_0\) for large nuclei, and by about 6% in \(^7\)Li.
- The Bjorken sum-rule for free protons is confirmed at the level of 5% in polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments.
- For the GDH sum-rule, the QMC model predicts an enhancement factor of 2.1 at \(\rho_0\); an independent estimate using antiproton effective mass reduction gives an enhancement factor of 2.2 at \(\rho_0\).
- The effective Regge intercept for the \(a_1\) trajectory is \(\alpha_{a_1} = 0.31 \pm 0.04\).
- The GDH sum-rule for free protons predicts 205 \(\mu\)b; the current experimental value is \(211 \pm 13\) \(\mu\)b.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the EMC nuclear effect (Cloet 2019) to the spin-dependent case, using the quenching of \(g_A^{(3)}\) from Gamow-Teller transitions (Ericson 1998, Ericson 1973) as a phenomenological constraint. It contrasts mean-field models (e.g., QMC by Saito 2005, NJL by Cloet 2006) that predict a significant spin EMC effect with short-range correlation models (Thomas 2018) that predict negligible spin dependence. The analysis enables future experiments at Jefferson Laboratory with a polarized \(^7\)Li target to search for the spin EMC effect in the range \(0.06 < x < 0.8\), and suggests that studies of the GDH sum-rule in medium would complement deep inelastic measurements.
