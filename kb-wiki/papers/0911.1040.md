# Analyzing Non-Extensivity of $η$-spectra in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> The transverse momentum spectra of secondary $η$ particles produced in $P+P$, $D+Au$ and $Au+Au$ interactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at different centralities have been studied in the light of a non-extensive thermodynamical approach. The results and the possible thermodynamical insights, thus obtained, about the hadronizing process have also been discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The Tsallis non-extensive thermodynamic approach provides a moderately satisfactory description of eta meson transverse momentum spectra across different collision systems (P+P, D+Au, Au+Au) at the same center-of-mass energy, but the quality of the fit depends on applying specific constraints linking temperature fluctuations and multiplicity fluctuations for nucleus-nucleus collisions; the non-extensivity parameter q does not appear to approach unity even for the largest participant numbers, suggesting genuine non-extensive behavior rather than a simulated microcanonical effect.

## Method

The authors fitted the experimental transverse momentum spectra of eta mesons using a Tsallis non-extensive distribution function derived from the generalized grand canonical partition function, with the working formula expressed in terms of transverse mass. For P+P collisions, the parameters (effective Hagedorn temperature T_eff and non-extensivity parameter q) were left free; for D+Au and Au+Au collisions, the parameters were constrained by relationships linking T_eff to a baseline temperature T_0 and linking the average multiplicity to the number of participant nucleons N_part, following the approach of Wilk and Wlodarczyk.

## Key numbers

- Baseline temperature T_0 = 155 MeV (average value from fits across all collision systems)
- Constraint parameter c = 1.88 (average value from fits)
- For comparison: T_0 for charged pions was 220 MeV and for proton-antiprotons was 360 MeV (from prior work by Wilk et al. on the authors' previous analysis); T_0 for pion production in e+e- collisions was 131 MeV with c = 1.83
- The analysis covers eta mesons produced at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (De et al., 2009) of charged pion and proton/antiproton spectra using the same non-extensive approach, but now applies the constraints of equations (22)-(23) that were not used in that earlier study. The results contrast with those of Biro et al. (2005, 2006), who analyzed eta meson spectra only for minimum bias Au+Au collisions in the central rapidity region, whereas the present work covers the full longitudinal momentum range, multiple collision systems (P+P, D+Au, Au+Au), and centrality-dependent windows. The findings enable future re-analysis of pion, kaon, and proton/antiproton spectra with the constrained non-extensive approach to resolve discrepancies in the extracted T_0 and c values.
