# N-Body Nuclear Forces at Short Distances in Holographic QCD

> We provide a calculation of N-body (N>2) nucleon interactions at short distances in holographic QCD. In the Sakai-Sugimoto model of large N_c massless QCD, N baryons are described by N Yang-Mills instantons in 5 spacetime dimensions. We compute a classical short distance interaction hamiltonian for N 'tHooft instantons. This corresponds to N baryons sharing identical classical spins and isospins. We find that genuine N-body nuclear forces turn out to vanish for N>2, at the leading order. This suggests that classical N-body forces are always suppressed compared with 2-body forces.

## Key claim

The authors compute classical short-distance N-body nuclear forces (N≥3) in the Sakai-Sugimoto model of holographic QCD and find that genuine N-body forces vanish at leading order for N>2, suggesting that classical N-body forces are always suppressed compared with 2-body forces. This result is obtained for baryons sharing identical classical spins and isospins, using 'tHooft instanton solutions rather than generic instanton configurations.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto model, where baryons are identified with Yang-Mills instantons in 5-dimensional spacetime. They compute the classical short-distance interaction Hamiltonian for N 'tHooft instantons, which correspond to N baryons with identical classical spins and isospins, using the ADHM construction and Osborn's formula for the instanton density. The calculation evaluates two contributions to the Hamiltonian: a U(1) potential term and an SU(2) potential term, with the kinetic term suppressed by 1/N_c. For the SU(2) term, the authors show it reduces to a sum over individual instantons with no inter-nucleon distance dependence, leaving only the U(1) term to contribute to N-body forces.

## Key numbers

- The scale parameter M_KK = 949 MeV, fixed by fitting the rho meson mass.
- The classical instanton size is (ρ_cl)^2 = 1/(8π^2 a) √(6/5), where a = 1/(216π^3).
- The 2-body force scales as O(N_c/(λ r^2)) in the original coordinates.
- The 3-body force at leading order scales as O(N_c/(λ^3 r^4)) after the leading term vanishes.
- The N-body force at leading order scales as O(N_c/(λ^(N-1) r^(2N-2))) for N≥3.
- The ratio of (N+1)-body to N-body forces is V^(N+1)/V^(N) ~ 1/(λ r^2) ≪ 1 for N≥3, in units where M_KK=1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of short-range 2-body nuclear forces in holographic QCD (Hashimoto 2009) to N-body forces for N≥3. The results contrast with chiral soliton models like the Skyrme model, where multi-soliton solutions are difficult to obtain. The suppression of many-body forces relative to 2-body forces is consistent with empirical knowledge from nuclear physics, where three-body interactions are known to be weaker than two-body interactions, as seen in the Fujita-Miyazawa force and in calculations of light nuclei binding energies and nuclear matter saturation density. The work enables further study of short-range many-body interactions relevant for high-density nuclear matter in neutron stars and supernovae.
