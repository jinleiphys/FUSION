# Energy conservation and scaling violations in particle production

> We use a simple Colour Glass Condensate/String Percolation Model argument to show the existence, due to energy conservation, of bounds to the violation of Feynman scaling and limiting fragmentation.

## Key claim

Using a Colour Glass Condensate/String Percolation Model argument, the authors show that energy conservation imposes a bound on the exponential violation of Feynman scaling, but that limiting fragmentation can be violated only if the particle density at fixed rapidity difference decreases with increasing beam energy.

## Method

The authors derive an energy conservation inequality for a fixed particle species in either the Feynman scaling region or the limiting fragmentation region, using the relation from saturation models that transverse momentum squared is proportional to the rapidity density. They then test the consistency of assumed scaling violations against this inequality, and analyze a specific evolution equation for the rapidity distribution whose parameters are fitted to RHIC data.

## Key numbers

- The bound on the exponential violation of Feynman scaling is \(\lambda \leq 2/3\), where \(\rho(y,Y) \sim e^{\lambda Y}\).
- The phenomenologically extracted value \(\lambda \simeq 0.288\) from Golec-Biernat (1998) lies within this bound.
- A fit to RHIC data yields parameter values: \(\lambda = 0.247\), \(\alpha = 0.269\), and \(\delta = 0.67\) for the evolution equation solution.
- The experimental rapidity range available for limiting fragmentation studies is from \(Y = 3\) to \(Y = 5.3\).

## Context

This work extends the Colour Glass Condensate approach of McLerran and collaborators (1993-2001) and the String Percolation Model of Armesto (1996) and Nardi (1998) by incorporating energy conservation constraints on scaling violations. The authors use the evolution equation from Dias de Deus (2007) and the RHIC data analysis of Brogueira (2006) to test their bounds. The results enable predictions for multiplicity distributions at LHC energies, as carried out by Abreu (2007) for Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions.
