# Effects of ground-state correlations on damping of giant dipole resonaces in $LS$ closed shell nuclei

> The effects of ground-state correlations on the damping of isovector giant dipole resonances in $LS$ closed shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are studied using extended random-phase-approximation (RPA) approaches derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. It is pointed out that unconventional two-body amplitudes of one particle--three hole and three particle--one hole types which are neglected in most extended RPA theories play an important role in the fragmentation of isovector dipole strength.

# Effects of ground-state correlations on damping of giant dipole resonances in $LS$ closed shell nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unconventional two-body amplitudes of one particle–three hole (1p–3h) and three particle–one hole (3p–1h) types, which are neglected in most extended RPA theories, play an important role in the fragmentation of isovector dipole strength in $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, specifically because the ground states of these nuclei are highly correlated with occupation probabilities deviating from Hartree-Fock values by close to 10%.

## Method

The author uses extended random-phase-approximation (ERPA) approaches derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), where the ground state is obtained as a stationary solution of the TDDM equations including the correlated part of the two-body density matrix $C_2$ and an approximate treatment of the three-body correlation $C_3$ via a Coupled-Cluster-Doubles-like truncation. The ERPA equations couple one-body amplitudes $x^\mu_{\alpha\alpha'}$ and two-body amplitudes $X^\mu_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}$, where the two-body amplitudes include 1p–3h and 3p–1h configurations enabled by fractional occupation probabilities. For $^{40}$Ca, a simpler small-amplitude limit of TDDM (STDDM) is used that neglects $C_3$ and keeps only 2p–2h and 2h–2p components of $C_2$, with a reduction factor $f=0.66$ applied to the residual interaction in the one-body channels to correct for the inconsistent effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- Occupation probability for the $1p_{3/2}$ state in $^{16}$O: 0.913 (proton) and 0.913 (neutron) from TDDM, compared to 0.910 from exact diagonalization (EDA), showing ~9% deviation from the Hartree-Fock value of 1.
- Occupation probability for the $1d_{5/2}$ state in $^{16}$O: 0.095 (proton) and 0.096 (neutron) from TDDM, compared to 0.099 from EDA, showing ~10% occupation of a particle state.
- The RPA peak for the isovector giant dipole resonance in $^{16}$O is at 21.9 MeV, consisting of $1p_{3/2} \rightarrow 1d_{5/2}$ transitions.
- The RPA peak for the isovector giant dipole resonance in $^{40}$Ca is at 17.7 MeV, consisting of $1d_{5/2} \rightarrow 1f_{7/2}$ transitions.
- In the $^{16}$O calculation, the numbers of matrix elements are: about 8500 for $C_2$, $1.7 \times 10^6$ for $C_3$, and 8300 for $X^\mu_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}$, while EDA uses about $4.6 \times 10^4$ configurations.
- For $^{40}$Ca in the $J$ scheme, the number of $C_2$ matrix elements is 336, and the number of $X^\mu_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}$ matrix elements is 2048.
- Occupation probabilities for $^{40}$Ca from TDDM: $1d_{5/2}$ at 0.923 (proton) and 0.924 (neutron); $2s_{1/2}$ at 0.846 (both); $1f_{7/2}$ at 0.154 (both).
- The reduction factor $f=0.66$ is used for the residual interaction in the one-body channels for the large-space $^{40}$Ca calculation.
- The strength distributions for $^{40}$Ca are smoothed with an artificial width $\Gamma=0.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous extended RPA theories (such as second RPA and particle-vibration coupling models) by including 1p–3h and 3p–1h amplitudes that are usually neglected, building on the time-dependent density-matrix theory framework developed by the author in earlier work (Tohyama, 2007, 2018, 2020). The results for $^{16}$O are benchmarked against exact diagonalization (EDA) calculations, showing good agreement. For $^{40}$Ca, the STDDM results are compared with experimental photoabsorption cross-section data from Ahrens (1975) and with other theoretical approaches such as particle-phonon coupling models (Tsel, Egoro) and large-scale SRPA calculations (Gamb1, Gamb2). The work suggests that future extended RPA studies of giant dipole resonances in these nuclei should include both conventional 2p–2h configurations and the unconventional 1p–3h and 3p–1h configurations.
