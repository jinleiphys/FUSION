# Pearcey integrals, Stokes lines and exact baryonic layers in the low energy limit of QCD

> The first analytic solutions representing baryonic layers living at finite baryon density within a constant magnetic field in the gauged Skyrme model are constructed. A remarkable feature of these configurations is that, if the Skyrme term is neglected, then these baryonic layers in the constant magnetic background cannot be found analytically and their energies grow very fast with the magnetic field. On the other hand, if the Skyrme term is taken into account, the field equations can be solved analytically and the corresponding solutions have a smooth limit for large magnetic fields. Thus, the Skyrme term discloses the universal character of these configurations living at finite Baryon density in a constant magnetic field. The classical gran-canonical partition function of these configurations can be expressed explicitly in terms of the Pearcey integral. This fact allows us to determine analytically the Stokes lines of the partition function and the corresponding dependence on the baryonic chemical potential as well as on the external magnetic field. In this way, we can determine various critical curves in the ($μ_B-B_{ext}$) plane which separates different physical behaviors. These families of inhomogeneous baryonic condensates can be also dressed with chiral conformal excitations of the solutions representing modulations of the layers themselves. Some physical consequences are analyzed.

## Key claim

The authors construct the first analytic solutions for baryonic layers (lasagna-type nuclear pasta) at finite baryon density in a constant external magnetic field within the gauged Skyrme model, but only when the Skyrme term is included; without it, the non-linear sigma model equations lose coercivity for large magnetic fields and no analytic solutions exist.

## Method

The authors employ the generalized Euler parametrization of the SU(2) Skyrme field, imposing orthogonality conditions on the angular functions and a linear ansatz for the external U(1) gauge potential that yields a constant magnetic field. For the Skyrme model (with the Skyrme term present), they impose the additional condition p/L_phi^2 = q/L_theta^2, which reduces the field equations to a single ordinary differential equation for the radial profile chi(r) that admits an analytic linear solution. The classical grand-canonical partition function for these configurations is expressed in terms of the Pearcey integral, allowing analytic determination of Stokes lines in the (mu_B, B_ext) plane.

## Key numbers

* The universal solution for the baryonic layer profile is chi(r) = sqrt(4 L_r^2 / lambda) r + chi_0, with the boundary conditions chi(0)=0 and chi(2pi)=pi requiring lambda / L_r^2 = 16 for a single linear segment.
* The baryonic charge takes the form B = 2 I eta ||c||^2, where I = p q n, with p, q, n integers.
* The energy density for the NLsigmaM case is rho_E = (K/2) ||c||^2 { 8p^2/L_phi^2 + chi'^2/L_r^2 + q^2/L_theta^2 + 4 P(r) sin^2(chi/2) }, where P(r) is a second-order polynomial in r determined by the external magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the analytic construction of baryonic layers (lasagna states) from the ungauged Skyrme model (Canfora et al., 2018; NostroI, NostroII) to the case of a strong external constant magnetic field, relevant for magnetars (Esposito; Mereghetti) and heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev; d'Enterria). The results contrast with the non-linear sigma model (lambda=0), where the equations lose coercivity for large fields, and enable analytic computation of critical curves in the chemical potential-magnetic field plane, circumventing the sign problem that plagues lattice QCD at finite baryon chemical potential.
