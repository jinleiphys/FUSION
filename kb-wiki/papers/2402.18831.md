# A direct probe of $Λ$ potential in nuclear medium

> Using the Liège intranuclear-cascade model together with the ablation model ABLA, an investigation is conducted into the effects of $Λ$ potential in $Λ$-nucleus and $Λ$-hypernucleus-nucleus collisions across various beam energies. The findings show that the angle and transverse-momentum distributions of scattered $Λ$ hyperon, the scattering cross section of the $Λ$ hyperon in $Λ$-nucleus collisions as well as the rapidity distribution of $Λ$ hyperon in $Λ$-hypernucleus-nucleus collisions are significantly influenced by the strength of the $Λ$ potential in these scattering reactions across various beam energies. These demonstrations, unhindered by the uncertainties of $Λ$ and hypernuclei productions in nuclear medium, allow for a direct investigation of the $Λ$ potential, especially its momentum dependence. The criticality of probing the $Λ$ potential is closely associated with the resolution of the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that angle distributions, transverse-momentum distributions, scattering cross sections of Λ hyperons in Λ-nucleus collisions, and rapidity distributions in Λ-hypernucleus-nucleus collisions are significantly influenced by the strength of the Λ potential, and that these observables allow a direct investigation of the Λ potential (especially its momentum dependence) without the uncertainties from Λ and hypernuclei production in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The Liège intranuclear-cascade (INCL) model, version INCL++6.32, was used to simulate the collision stage of Λ-nucleus and Λ-hypernucleus-nucleus reactions, treating successive relativistic binary hadron-hadron collisions with a Woods-Saxon density distribution and an isospin-dependent constant hyperon potential. The subsequent deexcitation stage of hot remnants was described by the ABLA07 model, which was extended to include hyperon emission and cold hypernuclei formation. The Λ potential value was varied from -50 MeV to -20 MeV at normal density to illustrate its effects across beam energies from 25 MeV to 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Λ potential values were varied from -50 MeV to -20 MeV at saturation density for illustrative purposes, compared to an initial estimate of -30 MeV.
- In Λ+⁴⁰Ca scatterings, the influence of the Λ potential on Λ emissions is pronounced at polar angles θ > 60°.
- In central ¹⁶ΛO+¹⁶O collisions at 300 MeV/nucleon, dense matter approximately twice the saturation density is achieved.
- The total cross sections for Λ scatterings in Λ+⁴⁰Ca reactions increase with beam energy, while the total cross sections for hypernucleus ⁴⁰ΛCa production show an inverse trend with increasing beam energy.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the INCL model to investigate Λ-nucleon interactions in nuclear medium (Yong 2023), and builds on earlier calculations of hyperon potentials using extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism (1998). The study aims to directly probe the Λ potential in nuclear medium to help resolve the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars, where theoretical models predict hyperons would soften the equation of state, contradicting observations of high-mass neutron stars. The proposed reactions could be conducted at JLab/CLAS (2019), BEPCII/BESIII, and GSI/FAIR using a dedicated HYDRA time-projection chamber prototype (2023).
