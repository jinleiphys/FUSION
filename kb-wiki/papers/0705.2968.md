# Production of hypernuclei in multifragmentation of nuclear spectator matter

> In peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy ions highly excited spectators containing Lambda-hyperons can be produced. Such strange spectator matter may undergo a break-up into many fragments (multifragmentation) as it is well established for ordinary nuclear systems. We generalize the statistical multifragmentation model, previously successfully used for the description of experimental data, for the case of hypernuclear systems. We predict relative yields of hypernuclei and the main characteristics of such a break-up. We point at a connection of this phenomenon with a liquid-gas phase transition in hypermatter.

# Production of hypernuclei in multifragmentation of nuclear spectator matter

## Key claim
The statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), previously successful for ordinary nuclear systems, can be generalized to describe the break-up of highly excited spectator matter containing Lambda hyperons produced in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions; the relative yields of hypernuclei are sensitive to the choice of hypernuclear mass formula, and the process is connected to a liquid-gas phase transition in hypermatter.

## Method
The authors extended the grand-canonical version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) by adding a hyper-energy term to the free energy of nuclear fragments, using either the Samanta formula or a liquid-drop parametrization for the binding energy of hypernuclei. They solved balance equations for baryon number, charge, and strangeness to calculate average yields of fragments with zero, one, or two Lambda hyperons for spectator sources with masses A0=50, 100, and 200, charges Z0=20–80, and hyperon numbers H0=0–2, at temperatures from 3 to 10 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³
- Free volume parameter: κ ≈ 2, giving Vf = κV0
- Inverse volume level density parameter: ε0 ≈ 16 MeV
- Liquid-drop model parameters: w0 = 16 MeV, β0 = 18 MeV, Tc = 18 MeV, γ = 25 MeV
- For single hypernuclei with A > 8, the average deviation of liquid-drop masses from experimental masses is 1.8 MeV, while the Samanta formula gives 2.2 MeV
- Multifragmentation occurs at temperatures T ≈ 4–6 MeV (liquid-gas coexistence region), with complete disintegration into light particles above T > 8–9 MeV
- The freeze-out volume corresponds to densities ρ ≈ 0.1–0.3 ρ0 and temperatures T ≈ 3–8 MeV

## Context
This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) developed by the authors and collaborators (Botvina et al., 1995; EOS collaboration) to include strangeness, building on experimental observations of Lambda hyperon capture by spectators in GeV-range heavy-ion collisions (Lopez; Dubna). The approach is motivated by UrQMD calculations of hypernucleus production via coalescence (hyphi) and by prior studies of multifragmentation as a liquid-gas phase transition (Pochodzalla, 1995). The results enable future experimental tests of hypernuclear mass formulas and open the possibility of studying the phase diagram of hyper-nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities, with potential applications to neutron star crusts.
