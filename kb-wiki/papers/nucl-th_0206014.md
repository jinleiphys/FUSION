# Violation of energy-per-hadron scaling in a resonance matter

> Yields of hadrons, their average masses and energies per hadron at the stage of chemical freeze-out in (ultra)relativistic heavy-ion collisions are analyzed within the statistical model. The violation of the scaling <E>/<N> = 1 GeV observed in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 130 AGeV is linked to the formation of resonance-rich matter with a considerable fraction of baryons and antibaryons. The rise of the energy-per-hadron ratio in baryon-dominated matter is discussed. A violation of the scaling condition is predicted for a very central zone of heavy-ion collisions at energies around 40 AGeV.

## Key claim
The scaling law ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ ≈ 1 GeV at chemical freeze-out, previously observed across a broad energy range from SIS to SPS, is violated in Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 AGeV (where it rises to about 1.1 GeV) and is predicted to also rise in baryon-dominated matter formed at energies around 40 AGeV, due to the formation of resonance-rich matter with a significant fraction of baryons and antibaryons.

## Method
The authors used a conventional statistical model (SM) of an ideal hadron gas, employing series expansions of Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distribution functions to compute particle number densities, energy densities, and partial pressures at given temperature T, baryon chemical potential μB, and strangeness chemical potential μS. They also applied a generalization to two thermal sources (TSM) that accounts for possible inhomogeneities of net baryon charge and net strangeness distribution inside the reaction volume, fitting the models to experimental data on hadron yields and ratios from AGS (11.6 AGeV) through SPS (40 and 80 AGeV) to RHIC (130 AGeV).

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 AGeV (RHIC), the energy per hadron at chemical freeze-out is ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = 1.12 GeV, with average hadron mass ⟨M⟩ = 0.77 GeV, obtained from a fit with T = 176 MeV and μB = 39.8 MeV.
- At SPS energies (40 AGeV), the TSM gives a central core temperature of T = 166 ± 5 MeV and baryon chemical potential μB = 413 ± 15 MeV, with net baryon density ρB = 0.430 ± 0.03 fm⁻³ (about 2.5 times normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³).
- The baryon fraction in the hadron yield decreases from 64.5% (TSM) at AGS (11.6 AGeV) to 13.7% at RHIC, while the antibaryon fraction rises from zero to 9.2%.
- Average baryon and meson masses in the central zone are 1.22 GeV and 432 MeV at 40 AGeV, and 1.38 GeV and 585 MeV at √s = 130 AGeV.
- The predicted saturation values at zero baryo-chemical potential are ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ ≈ 1.1 GeV and ⟨M⟩ ≈ 770 MeV, provided the chemical freeze-out temperature does not exceed 180 MeV.
- In baryon-dominated matter with densities of 2-3 ρ₀, the energy per hadron ratio is estimated to rise to about 1.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier observation by Cleymans and Redlich (1999) of the ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ ≈ 1 GeV scaling law across SIS to SPS energies, and explains its violation at RHIC as reported by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001). The two-source model (TSM) was previously proposed by the same authors to account for inhomogeneous baryon charge distributions observed at SPS by the NA52 collaboration. The results enable predictions for the planned GSI accelerator at energies between 10 and 40 AGeV, where baryon-dominated resonance matter with elevated energy-per-hadron ratios should be formed and can be probed by measuring hadron yields in a narrow midrapidity window.
