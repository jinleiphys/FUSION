# Thermalization of Heavy Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Charm- and bottom-quark rescattering in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is investigated with the objective of assessing the approach towards thermalization. Employing a Fokker-Planck equation to approximate the collision integral of the Boltzmann equation we augment earlier studies based on perturbative parton cross sections by introducing resonant heavy-light quark interactions. The latter are motivated by recent QCD lattice calculations which indicate the presence of "hadronic" states in the QGP. We model these states by colorless (pseudo-) scalar and (axial-) vector D- and B-mesons within a heavy-quark effective theory framework. We find that the presence of these states at moderate QGP temperatures substantially accelerates the kinetic equilibration of c-quarks as compared to using perturbative interactions. We also comment on consequences for $D$-meson observables in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The presence of resonant heavy-light quark interactions (modeled as "D-" and "B-meson" states above the quark-antiquark threshold) in the Quark-Gluon Plasma at moderate temperatures substantially accelerates the kinetic equilibration of charm quarks compared to using only perturbative QCD cross sections, with the caveat that this acceleration depends on the assumed resonance masses and widths, and the very existence of such resonances above T_c remains an assumption.

## Method

The authors model charm- and bottom-quark rescattering in a Quark-Gluon Plasma using a Fokker-Planck equation to approximate the collision integral of the Boltzmann equation. They augment earlier perturbative QCD calculations by introducing resonant heavy-light quark interactions via effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy-quark symmetry, with D- and B-meson propagators evaluated using either dimensional renormalization or dipole form-factor regularization. The drag and diffusion coefficients are computed from the scattering matrix elements and then used to evolve charm-quark transverse-momentum spectra within an expanding fireball model simulating central Au-Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Charm-quark equilibration time scale with resonant interactions: about a few fm/c at RHIC temperatures, compared to significantly longer times without resonances
- For bottom quarks, the kinetic equilibration time with resonances is around 10 fm/c or more
- Drag coefficient A and diffusion coefficient B0 for resonant rescattering are increased by a factor of approximately 3 over pQCD results at T=200 MeV
- Peak cross section for resonance case: about 10 mb, compared to an almost constant 4 mb for pQCD
- D-meson resonance widths varied between 300-500 MeV to cover the range suggested by effective quark models
- Charm-quark mass set to m_c=1.5 GeV; physical D-meson mass set to 2 GeV
- Bottom-quark mass set to m_b=4.5 GeV; B-meson mass set to 5 GeV
- Strong coupling constant varied over the range alpha_s=0.3-0.5
- Dissipation-fluctuation theorem satisfied within 3% for pQCD cross sections and within 11% for resonance cross sections in the renormalization scheme

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charm-quark thermalization by Svetitsky (1988) based on elastic perturbative QCD cross sections, and addresses the finding by Mustafa and Thoma (1997) that more moderate values of alpha_s render charm-quark thermalization under RHIC conditions unlikely. The resonant interaction mechanism is motivated by lattice QCD calculations indicating hadronic states in the QGP (Asakawa and Hatsuda, 2003; Karsch and Laermann, 2003) and by Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations (Grigoryan and Klevansky, 1992; Blaschke et al., 2002, 2003). The results enable more realistic predictions for D-meson elliptic flow and transverse-momentum spectra in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and suggest that rapid thermalization of charm quarks could explain preliminary RHIC data showing D-meson v2 comparable to light hadrons.
