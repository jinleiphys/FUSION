# Radiative corrections and parity nonconservation in heavy atoms

> The self-energy and the vertex radiative corrections to the effect of parity nonconservation in heavy atoms are calculated analytically in orders Z alpha^2 and Z^2 alpha^3 ln(lambda_C/r_0), where lambda_C and r_0 being the Compton wavelength and the nuclear radius, respectively. The value of the radiative correction is -0.85% for Cs and -1.41% for Tl. Using these results we have performed analysis of the experimental data on atomic parity nonconservation. The obtained values of the nuclear weak charge, Q_W=-72.90(28)_{exp}(35)_{theor} for Cs, and Q_W=-116.7(1.2)_{exp}(3.4)_{theor} for Tl, agree with predictions of the standard model. As an application of our approach we have also calculated analytically dependence of the Lamb shift on the finite nuclear size.

# Radiative corrections and parity nonconservation in heavy atoms

## Key claim
The self-energy and vertex radiative corrections to atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) in heavy atoms are calculated analytically in orders \(Z\alpha^2\) and \(Z^2\alpha^3\ln(\lambda_C/r_0)\), yielding corrections of \(-0.85\%\) for Cs and \(-1.41\%\) for Tl; after including these corrections, the extracted nuclear weak charges agree with the standard model within \(0.5\sigma\), resolving a previously reported \(2.5\sigma\) deviation.

## Method
The authors computed the self-energy and vertex radiative corrections to the PNC matrix element between \(p_{1/2}\) and \(s_{1/2}\) states in heavy atoms using analytic expansions in the Coulomb field of the nucleus. They employed the Fried-Yennie gauge for the non-logarithmic \(\alpha(Z\alpha)\) term and the Feynman gauge for the logarithmic \(\alpha(Z\alpha)^2\ln(\lambda_C/r_0)\) term, with the electron mass and binding energy neglected in the momentum range \(m \ll p \ll 1/r_0\) where logarithmic contributions arise. The total radiative correction was then combined with previously known Breit interaction, neutron skin, and renormalization corrections to reanalyze experimental PNC data for cesium and thallium.

## Key numbers
- Radiative correction to PNC effect: \(-0.85\%\) for Cs, \(-1.41\%\) for Tl.
- Nuclear weak charge for Cs: \(Q_W = -72.90 \pm 0.28_{\text{exp}} \pm 0.35_{\text{theor}}\); standard model prediction is \(-73.09 \pm 0.03\).
- Nuclear weak charge for Tl: \(Q_W = -116.7 \pm 1.2_{\text{exp}} \pm 3.4_{\text{theor}}\); standard model prediction is \(-116.7 \pm 0.1\).
- Relativistic enhancement factor \(R \approx 3\) for Cs and \(R \approx 9\) for Tl, Pb, and Bi.
- Non-logarithmic self-energy and vertex correction coefficient: \(B = -\alpha(Z\alpha)(7/12 + 2\ln 2)\).
- Logarithmic coefficient for self-energy and vertex correction: \(A = -\alpha(Z\alpha)^2/\pi \times (15/4 - \pi^2/6)\).
- Correction due to renormalization from \(q=0\) to \(q=1/r_0\) (diagrams c and d): \(\delta_{cd} \sim -0.1\%\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior analytic calculation of the Uehling-potential (vacuum polarization) correction to PNC (Milstein and Sushkov, 2001) by providing the first analytic calculation of the self-energy and vertex radiative corrections in heavy atoms. It resolves a \(2.5\sigma\) discrepancy between the measured weak charge in Cs and the standard model prediction reported by the Cs PNC experiment (Wood et al., 1997). The analysis incorporates the Breit interaction correction from Derevianko (2000) and Dzuba et al. (1998), neutron skin corrections from Derevianko (2000), and many-body calculations from Dzuba et al. (1989, 1997), Blundell et al. (1992), Kozlov et al. (2001), and Derevianko et al. (2001). The approach also yields an analytic expression for the finite-nuclear-size contribution to the Lamb shift that agrees with numerical computations by Cheng et al. (1993) and Johnson and Sapirstein (1994).
