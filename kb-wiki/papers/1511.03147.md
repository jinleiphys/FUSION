# Electromagnetic recombination spectra at the quark-hadron phase transition

> When quarks hadronize, they accelerate. Because they carry electric charge, they must radiate light as they accelerate and hadronize. This is true not only in jets but also in heavy ion collisions, where a thermalized plasma of quarks and gluons cools into a gas of hadrons. First, direct emission of photons from two quarks coalescing into pions is calculated using the quark-meson model. The yield of final-state photons to pions is found to be about $e^2/g^2_{πqq}$, which is on the order of a percent. Second, the yield of photons from the decay of highly excited color singlets, which may exist ephemerally during hadronizaton, is estimated. Because these contributions occur late in the reaction, they should carry significant elliptic flow, which may help explain the large observed flow of direct photons at RHIC by the PHENIX Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The enhanced emission also helps explain PHENIX's surprisingly large observed $γ/π$ ratio.

## Key claim

The paper argues that photon emission during the quark-hadron phase transition, from both direct quark coalescence into pions and from decays of excited color-singlet states, may contribute significantly to the observed direct photon yield and elliptic flow at RHIC, but the authors hedge that their estimates "fall short of dominating in any range of transverse momentum" and that "significant uncertainty exists" due to rapidly changing model parameters near the transition.

## Method

The authors calculate photon production from quark-antiquark coalescence into pions using the quark-meson model at tree level, computing thermal rates for the processes \(q\bar{q}\to\pi\gamma\) and \(q\bar{q}\to\pi\pi\) at \(T=175\) MeV. They then estimate the production of excited mesonic states by modeling hadronization as an adiabatic transition of quarks confined in a spherical bag that shrinks from \(R=1.3\) fm to \(R=0.8\) fm, using solutions to the Dirac equation in a confining potential. Finally, they compute electromagnetic decay rates of these excited states in the dipole approximation using the same valence quark model.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of photon to pion yields from direct coalescence is estimated as \(e^2/g^2_{\pi qq}\), which is "on the order of a percent"; the simplest estimate gives approximately \(0.7\%\).
- At \(T=175\) MeV, the average distance between a quark and its partner antiquark at hadronization is \(1.3\) fm, corresponding to confining spheres containing on average 9 quarks and 9 antiquarks.
- The bag radius shrinks from \(1.3\) fm to approximately \(0.8\) fm during hadronization.
- Radiative decay rates for transitions among confined quark states (e.g., \(j=3/2, n=0 \to j=1/2, n=0\)) are on the order of \(0.001\) fm\(^{-1}\) (specifically \(0.003178/q^2\) fm\(^{-1}\) for that transition), giving branching fractions into photons of roughly \(0.1\%\).
- The photon yield from decays of excited states at \(p_T=2.5\) GeV is estimated to constitute "a few tenths" of the direct photon production near that momentum.
- The entropy density at \(T=175\) MeV from lattice calculations is \(8\) fm\(^{-3}\), implying approximately \(0.3\) positive pions per cubic fm in the final state.

## Context

This work extends calculations of thermal photon production in heavy ion collisions (Kapusta 1991, Dion 2011) by adding a neglected source: photons emitted during hadronization itself. It addresses the discrepancy between early predictions of low photon elliptic flow and the large \(v_2\) measured by PHENIX (Petti 2013), as well as the unexpectedly large \(\gamma/\pi\) ratio. The direct coalescence calculation differs from Campbell (2015) by focusing on radiation from charged quarks rather than gluonic degrees of freedom. The results suggest that future work should incorporate these hadronization-era photons into hydrodynamic models and improve the mapping of excited states to the measured hadronic spectrum.
