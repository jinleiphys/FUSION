# Anisotropic flow in sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> The results on elliptic flow in sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) reported by the ALICE collaboration are remarkably similar to those for sqrt(s)=200 GeV gold-gold collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). This result is surprising, given the expected longer lifetime of the system at the higher collision energies. We show that it is nevertheless consistent with 3+1 dimensional viscous event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations, and demonstrate that elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC is built up mostly within the first 5 fm/c of the evolution. We conclude that an "almost perfect liquid" is produced in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. Furthermore, we present predictions for triangular flow as a function of transverse momentum for different centralities.

## Key claim
The similarity of elliptic flow between LHC Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and RHIC Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is not surprising but rather a natural consequence of the self-quenching of elliptic flow when the spatial eccentricity drops below 0.1, with flow built up mostly within the first 5 fm/c at both energies.

## Method
The authors performed 3+1 dimensional viscous event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with the Israel-Stewart formalism, initializing energy density via a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions. They used the s95p-v1 equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas, with a Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure including resonance decays, and compared results to ALICE experimental data for elliptic flow and presented predictions for triangular flow.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density at center: 175 GeV/fm^3 for ideal case, 158 GeV/fm^3 for eta/s=0.08
- Thermalization time: tau_0=0.3 fm/c for LHC (compared to 0.4 fm/c for RHIC)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb: R=6.62 fm, d=0.546 fm
- Gaussian width for wounded nucleon energy density contribution: sigma_0=0.3 fm
- Longitudinal profile parameters: eta_flat=10, sigma_eta=0.5
- Freeze-out temperature used: T_FO=135 MeV
- Elliptic flow built up almost entirely within first 5-6 fm/c at both RHIC and LHC
- QGP phase lifetime approximately 40% longer at LHC than RHIC

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic calculations at RHIC energies (Schenke 2010) to LHC energies, confirming that the "almost perfect liquid" description applies at both facilities. The results contradict naive expectations that higher pressure and longer QGP lifetime at LHC would produce much larger elliptic flow, consistent with earlier 2+1D ideal hydrodynamic predictions by Kestin (2008) showing small decreases in v_2 at higher energy densities. The findings enable future combined comparisons of experimental spectra and higher harmonics to determine initial conditions and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.
