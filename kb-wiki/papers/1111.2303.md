# On the interaction between two point electric charges

> The general formula for the interaction potential between two point electric charges which contains the lowest order corrections to the vacuum polarization is derived and investigated. Analytical derivation of this formula is based on the closed analytical expression for the Uehling potential obtained earlier. Our analytical formula has the correct asymtotic behaviour at small and large distances between two interacting electric charges. We also discuss a number of problems which are of great interest in applications, e.g., vacuum polarization corrections to the Coulomb scattering and cusp value between two electrically charged particles. An original algebraic procedure developed in this study allows one to determine consequtive corrections for vacuum polarization for Coulomb systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula for the interaction potential between two point electric charges that includes both the Uehling potential and the Wichmann-Kroll potential, claiming that this sum has correct asymptotic behavior at both small and large interparticle distances, unlike the Uehling potential alone which decays too rapidly (exponentially) at large distances.

## Method

The author starts from the closed analytical expression for the Uehling potential derived in earlier work, then adds the Wichmann-Kroll potential obtained from a Lagrangian formulation of the vacuum polarization correction to the electric field. The Wichmann-Kroll potential is regularized by replacing the singular form with a regularized expression \(W_K(r) = -\frac{2 Q^3 \alpha^{7}}{225 \pi r (r^2 + \alpha^2)^2}\). The total potential is then used to derive Fourier spatial resolutions, scattering amplitudes via the variable phase method, and modified cusp values for Coulomb systems.

## Key numbers

- The Uehling potential short-range asymptotic: \(\phi(r)\mid_{r \rightarrow 0} \simeq \frac{Q}{r} \left\{ 1 + \frac{\alpha}{3 \pi} \left[ -\frac53 - 2 \gamma + 2 \ln \alpha - 2 \ln r \right] \right\}\) where \(\gamma \approx 0.5772156649\) is the Euler constant and \(\ln \alpha \approx -4.92024365857\)
- The Uehling potential long-range asymptotic: \(\phi(r)\mid_{r \rightarrow \infty} \simeq \frac{Q}{r} \left\{ 1 + \frac{\alpha^{\frac52}}{4 \sqrt{\pi} r^{\frac32}} \exp(-\frac{2}{\alpha} r) \right\}\), decaying as \(\sim r^{-\frac32} \exp(-274 r)\) in atomic units
- The Wichmann-Kroll potential in atomic units: \(W_K(r) = -\frac{2 Q^3 \alpha^{7}}{225 \pi r (r^2 + \alpha^2)^2}\)
- The Fourier spatial resolution of the Wichmann-Kroll potential: \(w_K(k) = - \frac{2 \pi Q^3 \alpha^{6}}{225} \exp(-k \alpha)\)
- The expected Uehling correction to the pure Coulomb cusp is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-3}\) a.u. (0.1%), while the Wichmann-Kroll contribution is approximately \(1 \times 10^{-7}\) a.u. for light atoms with nuclear charge \(Q \le 5\)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier studies (Frolov, earlier papers) on the Uehling potential by incorporating the Wichmann-Kroll correction originally derived by Wichmann and Kroll (Wichmann and Kroll, 1956) to fix the incorrect exponential decay of the Uehling potential at large distances. The paper also references the independent derivation of the Uehling potential formula by Pauli and Rose (Pauli and Rose, 1936). The results enable more accurate calculations of vacuum polarization effects in atomic systems, including corrections to Coulomb scattering cross-sections and modified cusp values that can be tested in highly accurate numerical computations.
