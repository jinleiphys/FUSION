# Hard Probe of Geometry and Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=$ 0.2, 2.76, and 5.5 TeV

> [Background:] A significant quenching of high energy jets was observed in the heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) facility, and is now confirmed at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) facility. The RHIC plus LHC era provides a unique opportunity to study the jet-medium interaction that leads to the jet quenching, and the medium itself at different collision energies (medium temperatures). [Purpose:] We study the azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching, to seek constraints on different models featuring distinct path-length and density dependences for jet energy loss, and to gain a better understanding of the medium. [Methods:] The models are fixed by using the RHIC data, and then applied to study the LHC case. A set of harmonic (Fourier) coefficients $v_{n}$ are extracted from the jet azimuthal anisotropy on a event-by-event basis. [Results:] The second harmonics $v_{2}$, mostly driven by the medium's geometry, can be used to differentiate jet quenching models. Other harmonics are also compared with the LHC (2.76 TeV) data. The predictions for future LHC (5.5 TeV) run are presented. [Conclusions:] We find that a too strong path-length dependence (e.g., cubic) is ruled out by the LHC $v_{2}$ data, while the model with a strong near-$T_c$ enhancement for the jet-medium interaction describes the data very well. It is worth pointing out that the latter model expects a less color-opaque medium at LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching, specifically the second harmonic coefficient \(v_2\) of high-\(p_t\) hadrons, can discriminate between different jet energy loss models; a model with strong near-\(T_c\) enhancement of jet-medium interaction describes data at both RHIC and LHC energies, while a model with cubic path-length dependence is ruled out by LHC \(v_2\) data.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event Monte Carlo Glauber simulations to generate fluctuating initial conditions for heavy ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.2\), 2.76, and 5.5 TeV. They implemented three geometric jet quenching models with different path-length and matter-density dependences: a near-\(T_c\) enhancement (NTcE) model with quadratic path-length dependence and a density-dependent interaction strength, a model with quadratic path-length dependence and constant interaction strength (\(\mathrm{L}^2\)), and a model with cubic path-length dependence and constant interaction strength (\(\mathrm{L}^3\)). The overall opaqueness parameter for each model was fixed by requiring \(R_{AA} \approx 0.18\) in the 0-5% most central collisions at RHIC \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, and then the models were applied to predict observables at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) in the most central collisions at RHIC \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV was used to calibrate all models to \(R_{AA} \approx 0.18\).
* The reference p-p spectrum exponent \(n\) used in the analysis was \(n \approx 8.1\) for \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV, \(n \approx 6.0\) for \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, and \(n \approx 5.4\) for \(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV.
* The NN inelastic cross section \(\sigma_{inel}\) was set to 42 mb for \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) TeV, 62 mb for \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, and 66 mb for \(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV.
* The initial entropy density at equilibrium time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c was scaled with participant density at RHIC and with a mixture \((1-\delta)/2 \times \rho_p + \delta \times \rho_c\) at LHC, with \(\delta = 0.118\) for both 2.76 and 5.5 TeV cases.
* The near-\(T_c\) enhancement model parameters were \(\xi = 6\), \(s_c = 7/\mathrm{fm}^3\), and \(s_w = 2/\mathrm{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the geometric tomography approach to jet quenching, building on prior studies of high-\(p_t\) azimuthal anisotropy at RHIC by Shuryak (2001) and Liao (2008), and on systematic model comparisons by Renk (2011). The study provides predictions for future LHC runs at 5.5 TeV and demonstrates that the near-\(T_c\) enhancement model, motivated by the emergence of magnetic monopoles near \(T_c\) as proposed by Liao (2006), can simultaneously describe \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data at both RHIC and LHC energies, while ruling out models with too strong path-length dependence.
