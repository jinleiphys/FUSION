# Final state interactions in $^{4}$He(e,e$^\prime$p)$^{3}$H at large proton energy

> At large proton energy, the final state interactions of the knocked out nucleon in $\eep$ reactions off nuclear targets can be described within the eikonal approximation, treating the spectator particles as a collection of fixed scattering centers. We use a generalization of this approach, suitable to take into account the possible occurrence of color transparency, to carry out an accurate calculation of the missing momentum distribution of the process $^{4}He(e,e^\prime p)^{3}H$. The pattern of final state interaction effect is analyzed for different kinematical setups in the domain corresponding to $2 \le Q^2 \le 20$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that final state interaction (FSI) effects in the exclusive reaction \(^{4}\text{He}(e,e^\prime p)^{3}\text{H}\) can be calculated using a coupled-channel multiple scattering approach that includes color transparency, and that the resulting distortions of the missing momentum distribution provide a signature of color transparency already at moderate \(Q^2 \sim 2.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), not only at asymptotically high momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors computed the missing momentum distribution \(W_{\mathbf{p}}(\mathbf{p}_m)\) for the exclusive \(^{4}\text{He}(e,e^\prime p)^{3}\text{H}\) reaction using a generalization of the Glauber eikonal approximation. The scattering operator was constructed to include both diagonal and off-diagonal rescattering processes among hadronic states, with the compact state produced at the electromagnetic vertex modeled by a Gaussian wave function. The overlap \(\chi_0(\mathbf{X})\) between the \(^{4}\text{He}\) ground state and the \(^{3}\text{H}\) ground state was taken from Variational Monte Carlo calculations using the Argonne v14 two-nucleon interaction and the Urbana VII three-body potential, and the averaged scattering operator was evaluated by Monte Carlo integration over configurations of the \(^{4}\text{He}\) wave function.

## Key numbers

* At \(Q^2 = 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) and in the absence of color transparency, the missing momentum distribution at \(p_{m,\perp} = p_{m,z} = 0\) is quenched by 18% due to final state interactions.
* The calculations cover the range \(2 \le Q^2 \le 20\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
* The quark-diquark oscillation frequency used to model the proton excited states was \(\omega_0 = 0.35\) GeV.
* The parameter \(C\) in the ejectile wave function \(\langle \rho | E \rangle \propto e^{-C \rho^2 Q^2}\) was set to \(C = 1\), with the note that results are insensitive to \(C\) as long as \(C \ge 0.05\).
* At \(Q^2 \sim 2.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), off-diagonal rescatterings are responsible for more than 60% of the calculated forward-backward asymmetry for all kinematical windows considered.
* Six intermediate hadronic states were included in all numerical calculations, with the first two to three excited states saturating the off-diagonal contribution at missing momentum below 300 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel multiple scattering approach developed by the same authors for semi-inclusive \((e,e^\prime p)\) and \((p,2p)\) reactions (Benhar et al., 1998; Nikolaev et al., 1998) to the fully exclusive case where the final state of the recoiling nucleus is specified. The calculation uses the overlap function \(\chi_0(\mathbf{X})\) computed by Schiavilla et al. (1998) with realistic wave functions. The results are compared to earlier semi-inclusive calculations for \(^{4}\text{He}\) using a Jastrow model (Benhar et al., 1992) and a four-body wave function with tensor correlations (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1999), finding a similar pattern of FSI effects on the S-wave contribution. The paper enables experimental searches for color transparency in exclusive \((e,e^\prime p)\) reactions on few-nucleon systems at existing facilities like TJNAF, where the predicted asymmetry shows a significant CT signal already at moderate \(Q^2\).
