# Nucleon Structure from Basis Light-Front Quantization : Status and Prospects

> We review recent advancements in understanding nucleon structure within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework--a fully relativistic, nonperturbative approach to solving quantum field theories. In its initial phase, we start with the leading Fock sector $|qqq\rangle$ and an effective light-front Hamiltonian incorporating confinement and one-gluon exchange within which BLFQ can already successfully describe key nucleon observables. The framework has since been extended to include the next-to-leading Fock sector $|qqqg\rangle$, enabling studies of gluonic contributions to the nucleon's internal structure, including gluon helicity, orbital angular momentum, and three-dimensional imaging through generalized and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (GPDs and TMDs). Most recently, BLFQ has achieved a significant milestone by computing nucleon light-front wavefunctions as eigenstates of the QCD Hamiltonian without an explicit confining potential. These calculations, including Fock sectors up to $|qqqq\bar{q}\rangle$, further develop the path to first-principles predictions of quark and gluon matter densities, helicity and transversity distributions, and spin observables, showing qualitative agreement with experimental and phenomenological results. Together, these developments highlight BLFQ's growing capacity to provide an increasingly complete and realistic picture of nucleon structure grounded in QCD.

# Nucleon Structure from Basis Light-Front Quantization: Status and Prospects

## Key claim
The Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework has achieved a significant milestone by computing nucleon light-front wavefunctions as eigenstates of the QCD Hamiltonian without an explicit confining potential, including Fock sectors up to |qqqqq̅⟩, showing qualitative agreement with experimental and phenomenological results, though the agreement remains qualitative rather than quantitative, and the calculations are still truncated in Fock space.

## Method
The authors solve the light-front Schrödinger equation for nucleon mass eigenstates by expanding the state vector in a Fock-space basis of parton states (quarks, antiquarks, and gluons) and diagonalizing the light-front QCD Hamiltonian. Starting with the leading |qqq⟩ sector using an effective Hamiltonian with confinement and one-gluon exchange, the framework was extended to include the |qqqg⟩ sector for gluonic contributions, and most recently to include |qqqqq̅⟩ sectors without an explicit confining potential. The light-front gauge A⁺=0 is adopted, and the Fock space is truncated using the Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (LFTDA) for practical computation.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of proton electric to magnetic Sachs form factors from double-polarization experiments decreases almost linearly with increasing momentum transfer for Q² > 0.5 GeV², while Rosenbluth separation results remain nearly constant in the spacelike region.
- Lattice QCD simulations of axial form factors have been performed for pion masses in the range 0.2–0.6 GeV.
- For the down quark, the ratio of polarized to unpolarized PDFs remains negative across the experimentally accessible range x ≲ 0.6, with no observed sign reversal.

## Context
This review extends the BLFQ approach developed by Vary (2009) and Zhao (2014) for nucleon structure, building on earlier work using the leading Fock sector |qqq⟩ by Mondal (2019) and Xu (2021). The framework now competes with lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou (2013, 2020) and the PACS Collaboration (2018), as well as Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches by Roberts (2013) and Bashir (2012). The results enable future comparisons with experimental programs at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), the Large Hadron-Electron Collider (LHeC), and the 22 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab, and provide a path toward first-principles predictions of quark and gluon distributions, helicity and transversity distributions, and spin observables.
