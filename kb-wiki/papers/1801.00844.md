# Towards a relativistic formulation of baryon-baryon interactions in chiral perturbation theory

> In this talk, we report on two recent studies of relativistic nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions in covariant chiral perturbation theory, where they are constructed up to leading order. The relevant unknown low energy constants are fixed by fitting to the nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering data. It is shown that these interactions can describe the scattering data with a quality similar to their next-to-leading order non-relativistic counterparts. These studies show that it is technically feasible to construct relativist baryon-baryon interactions, and in addition, after further refinements, these interactions may provide important inputs to {\it ab initio} relativistic nuclear structure and reaction studies and help improve our understanding of low energy strong interactions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that relativistic (covariant) chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions constructed at leading order (LO) can describe scattering data with a quality similar to next-to-leading order (NLO) non-relativistic chiral interactions, but they note that a simultaneous fit of NN and YN systems is impossible without properly accounting for SU(3) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors constructed LO relativistic baryon-baryon potentials using covariant chiral perturbation theory, retaining the full Dirac spinor structure rather than expanding in inverse nucleon mass. The potentials consist of four-baryon contact terms and one-pion (or one-pseudoscalar-meson) exchange, with low-energy constants (LECs) determined by fitting to NN phase shifts from the Nijmegen93 partial wave analysis (for laboratory kinetic energy up to 100 MeV) and to YN scattering data (35 cross sections plus a Σ⁻p inelastic capture ratio at rest). The scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving the Kadyshevsky three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with a separable Gaussian cutoff function used for regularization.

## Key numbers

- Best fit for NN interaction achieved at cutoff Λ = 750 MeV with χ²/d.o.f. ~ 2.0, fitting neutron-proton phase shifts from Nijmegen93 with laboratory kinetic energy E_lab ≤ 100 MeV
- Best fit for YN interaction achieved at Λ = 600 MeV with χ² = 16.1, fitting 35 cross sections and one inelastic capture ratio
- Pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV and axial vector coupling g_A = 1.267 used in the Lagrangians
- Cutoff variation range for NN: Λ from 500 MeV to 1000 MeV; for YN: Λ from 500 MeV to 850 MeV
- The relativistic LO YN interaction yields χ² less sensitive to cutoff variation compared to the LO non-relativistic heavy baryon approach, and comparable to the NLO non-relativistic approach

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic chiral perturbation theory approach to baryon-baryon interactions pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and developed further by Epelbaum (1999), Entem and Machleidt (2003), and others for NN, and by Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2007, 2013) for YN interactions. The relativistic formulation is motivated by the success of covariant chiral perturbation theory in the one-baryon sector (Geng 2008, 2009, 2011) and by the demand for relativistic potentials in ab initio nuclear structure studies (Shen 2016, 2017). The results enable future construction of higher-order relativistic chiral forces and their application to relativistic nuclear structure and reaction studies, as well as to hypernuclear physics and neutron star properties.
