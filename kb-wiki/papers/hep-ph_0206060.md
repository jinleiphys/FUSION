# Black Hole Production at LHC: String Balls and Black Holes from pp and Lead-lead Collisions

> If the fundamental planck scale is near a TeV, then parton collisions with high enough center-of-mass energy should produce black holes. The production rate for such black holes at LHC has been extensively studied for the case of a proton-proton collision. In this paper, we extend this analysis to a lead-lead collision at LHC. We find that the cross section for small black holes which may in principle be produced in such a collision is either enhanced or suppressed, depending upon the black hole mass. For example, for black holes with a mass around 3 TeV we find that the differential black hole production cross section, dσ/dM, in a typical lead-lead collision is up to 90 times larger than that for black holes produced in a typical proton-proton collision. We also discuss the cross-sections for `string ball' production in these collisions. For string balls of mass about 1 (2) TeV, we find that the differential production cross section in a typical lead-lead collision may be enhanced by a factor up to 3300 (850) times that of a proton-proton collision at LHC.

## Key claim

If the fundamental Planck scale is near a TeV, then parton collisions with sufficient center-of-mass energy should produce black holes; the cross section for small black holes in lead-lead collisions at LHC is either enhanced or suppressed relative to proton-proton collisions, depending on the black hole mass, and the enhancement factors quoted assume unshadowed parton distribution functions and neglect saturation effects.

## Method

The authors compute the differential production cross sections dσ/dM for black holes and string balls in both pp collisions at √s = 14 TeV and PbPb collisions at √s^NN = 5.5 TeV at LHC, using the parton-level cross sections from Dimopoulos and Landsberg (2001) and Dimopoulos and Emparan (2001). They convolute these with the CTEQ6M parton distribution functions, taking the factorization scale Q² = M², and assume unshadowed nuclear parton distributions (R_A = 1) because the minimum x probed is 0.033, where shadowing is negligible. For black holes they assume n = 4 extra spatial dimensions and a fundamental Planck scale M_P = 1 TeV; for string balls they take string coupling g_s = 0.3 and consider string mass scales M_s = 1, 2, 3 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For black holes of mass 3 TeV, the differential cross section dσ/dM in a PbPb collision is up to 90 times larger than in a pp collision at LHC.
- For string balls of mass 1 TeV, the differential cross section in a PbPb collision is up to 3300 times larger than in a pp collision at LHC.
- For string balls of mass 2 TeV, the differential cross section in a PbPb collision is up to 850 times larger than in a pp collision at LHC.
- For string balls of mass 3 TeV, the differential cross section in a PbPb collision is up to 90 times larger than in a pp collision at LHC.
- The total cross section for producing black holes with mass above 1 TeV is about 14 nb in pp collisions and about 28000 nb in PbPb collisions at LHC.
- The minimum x probed for black hole production in PbPb collisions is x_min = 0.033, derived from M²/s = 1/(5.5 × 5.5).

## Context

This work extends the black hole production calculations for pp collisions at LHC, previously studied by Dimopoulos and Landsberg (2001) and Giddings and Thomas (2002), to heavy-ion (lead-lead) collisions. It also applies the string ball formalism of Dimopoulos and Emparan (2001) to both collision systems. The results enable experimental searches at LHC to consider heavy-ion runs as a potential source of enhanced black hole and string ball production, particularly for masses near 1-3 TeV, and may constrain cosmological models with a runaway dilaton if no black holes are observed.
