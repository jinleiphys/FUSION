# Dynamical coupled channel calculation of pion and omega meson production

> A dynamical coupled channel approach is used to study $π$ and $ω$--meson production induced by pions and photons scattering from the proton. Six intermediate channels including $πN$, $ηN$, $πΔ$, $σN$, $ρN$ and $ωN$ are employed to describe unpolarized and polarized data. Bare parameters in an effective hadronic Lagrangian are determined in a fit to data for $πN \to πN$, $γN \to πN$, $π^- p \to ωn$, and $γp \to ωp$ reactions at center-of-mass energies from threshold to $W < 2.0$ GeV. The $T$ matrix determined in these fits is used to calculate the photon beam asymmetry for $ω$-meson production and the $ωN \toωN$ total cross section and $ωN$ scattering lengths.

## Key claim

A dynamical coupled channel model with six intermediate channels (πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN, ωN) can describe existing π and ω meson production data from threshold to W < 2.0 GeV, but the model's predictive power for unfitted observables (such as photon beam asymmetry for ω production) is limited by the large number of bare parameters required and the simplifications made in the ωN channel interactions.

## Method

The T matrix for pion-induced and photon-induced meson production reactions was decomposed into non-resonant (Born) and resonant contributions, with the non-resonant part solved via coupled-channel integral equations in a partial-wave basis. Bare parameters in an effective hadronic Lagrangian were determined by fitting to πN → πN partial wave amplitudes from Arndt et al. (2006), γN → πN data, π⁻p → ωn data from Danburg (1971) and Karami (1979), and γp → ωp data from the SAPHIR and GRAAL collaborations. The resulting T matrix was then used to calculate the photon beam asymmetry for ω production and the ωN → ωN total cross section and scattering lengths without refitting.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range: threshold to W < 2.0 GeV
- Six intermediate channels included: πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN, ωN
- 74 interaction mechanisms among the channels πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN, ωN, and γN
- ω meson width Γ_ω = 8.5(1) MeV (neglected in the calculation)
- Partial wave contributions included up to L=3 (F-wave) for most terms, with all partial waves required for convergence of t-channel pion exchange
- Momentum grid of 25 Gauss-Legendre points used for numerical integration, with convergence checked up to 45 points
- Approximately 10³-10⁴ χ² evaluations required for optimization using the MINUIT package
- Single χ² evaluation requires approximately 100 node-minutes of computation

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled channel approach of Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (2007) by adding the ωN channel to the previous five-channel model (πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN). It is comparable to the model treatments of Krehl et al. (1999) and Chen et al. (2007), but should be contrasted with coupled channel calculations from the Giessen group (Shklyar et al., 2004) and KVI (Usov et al., 2005) that neglect off-shell contributions. The results enable future applications to ρ and φ production, pion and ω meson electroproduction, and reactions on nuclear targets, as well as providing insight into the role of vector mesons in dense nuclear matter relevant to neutron-rich stellar environments.
