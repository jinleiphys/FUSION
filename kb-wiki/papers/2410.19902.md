# A Universal Bound on QCD Axions from Supernovae

> We identify a new production channel for QCD axions in supernova environments that contributes to axion emissivity for all models solving the strong CP problem. This channel arises at tree-level from a shift-symmetry-breaking operator constructed at next-to-leading order in Chiral Perturbation Theory. In scenarios where model-dependent derivative couplings to nucleons are absent, this sets the strongest model-independent constraint on the axion mass, improving on existing bounds by two orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new tree-level production channel for QCD axions in supernova environments, arising from a shift-symmetry-breaking operator at next-to-leading order in Chiral Perturbation Theory. This channel provides the strongest model-independent constraint on the axion mass in scenarios where model-dependent derivative couplings to nucleons are absent, improving on existing bounds by two orders of magnitude, but the bound depends on the specific supernova model used.

## Method

The authors constructed the Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) Lagrangian for two light quark flavors up to next-to-leading order, focusing on the isospin-breaking term proportional to the low-energy constant $\hat{c}_5$. They calculated the squared matrix elements for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung processes ($NN \to NNa$) at tree level, including both identical nucleon ($nn$, $pp$) and mixed nucleon ($np$) cases, and integrated these over supernova simulation data to obtain axion luminosities.

## Key numbers

- Axion decay constant bound: $f_a > 1.1^{+0.4}_{-0.6} \times 10^8 \, \mathrm{GeV}$ at 68% C.L.
- Axion mass bound: $m_a < 5.3^{+7.3}_{-1.3} \times 10^{-2} \, \mathrm{eV}$ at 68% C.L.
- The axion luminosity from the new channel exceeds the previous estimate from Lucente (2022) by a factor of approximately $10^3$ using the same supernova data
- Neutrino luminosity at $t=1\,\text{s}$ used for the bound: $L_\nu=5.5\times10^{52}\,\text{erg}\,\text{s}^{-1}$
- Low-energy constant $\hat{c}_5 = -0.09(1) \, \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$
- Axion-nucleon-photon coupling magnitude: $|C_{a N \gamma}| \simeq 10^{-3}$
- Alternative process ($\pi N \to a N$) yields a bound of $f_a \gtrsim 4 \times 10^7~\mathrm{GeV}$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of axion production in supernovae beyond the one-loop suppressed nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) operator studied by Lucente (2022). The new tree-level channel arises from the same operator that generates the nEDM at one-loop, previously examined by Crewther (1979) and Pospelov (1999). The result establishes a model-independent bound that even astrophobic axion models, such as those proposed by Di Luzio (2017), cannot evade, and demonstrates the effectiveness of systematic ChPT approaches for constraining QCD axion parameter space.
