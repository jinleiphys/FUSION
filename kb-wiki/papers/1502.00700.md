# Ab initio effective interactions for sd-shell valence nucleons

> We perform \textit{ab initio} no-core shell-model calculations for $A=18$ and $19$ nuclei in a $4\hbarΩ$, or $N_{\rm max}=4$, model space by using the effective JISP16 and chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials and transform the many-body effective Hamiltonians into the $0\hbarΩ$ model space to construct the $A$-body effective Hamiltonians in the $sd$-shell. We separate the $A$-body effective Hamiltonians with $A=18$ and $A=19$ into inert core, one-, and two-body components. Then, we use these core, one-, and two-body components to perform standard shell-model calculations for the $A=18$ and $A=19$ systems with valence nucleons restricted to the $sd$ shell. Finally, we compare the standard shell-model results in the $0\hbarΩ$ model space with the exact no-core shell model results in the $4\hbarΩ$ model space for the $A=18$ and $A=19$ systems and find good agreement.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that *ab initio* no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations in a \(4\hbar\Omega\) model space can be used to derive effective two-body valence interactions for the \(sd\)-shell, and that standard shell-model (SSM) calculations using these derived interactions reproduce the NCSM results for \(A=18\) and \(A=19\) systems, but only for cases with at most one valence proton and without including three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors first performed NCSM calculations for \(A=18\) and \(A=19\) nuclei in a \(4\hbar\Omega\) (\(N_{\rm max}=4\)) model space using the JISP16 and chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials, applying the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation at the two-body cluster level to obtain a primary effective Hamiltonian. They then performed a second unitary transformation to project the many-body effective Hamiltonian into the \(0\hbar\Omega\) model space (the \(sd\)-shell valence space), and separated the resulting \(A\)-body effective Hamiltonian into inert core, one-body, and two-body components by performing separate NCSM calculations for \(^{16}\)O, \(^{17}\)O, and \(^{17}\)F. Finally, they used these core, one-body, and two-body components in standard shell-model calculations for \(^{18}\)F and \(^{19}\)F and compared the results with the original NCSM calculations.

## Key numbers

- The NCSM ground-state energy of \(^{18}\)F (\(1^+_1\), \(T=0\)) in the \(4\hbar\Omega\) model space with \(\hbar\Omega=14\) MeV is \(-122.742\) MeV for JISP16 and \(-126.964\) MeV for chiral N3LO.
- The core energy (\(E_{\rm core}\)) for \(^{16}\)O derived from JISP16 is \(-115.529\) MeV for \(A=18\) and \(-115.319\) MeV for \(A=19\); for chiral N3LO it is \(-118.469\) MeV for \(A=18\) and \(-118.306\) MeV for \(A=19\).
- Neutron single-particle energies for JISP16 at \(A=18\) are \(-3.068\) MeV (\(j=1/2\)), \(-2.270\) MeV (\(j=5/2\)), and \(6.262\) MeV (\(j=3/2\)); proton single-particle energies are \(0.603\) MeV (\(j=1/2\)), \(1.398\) MeV (\(j=5/2\)), and \(9.748\) MeV (\(j=3/2\)).
- Neutron single-particle energies for chiral N3LO at \(A=18\) are \(-3.638\) MeV (\(j=1/2\)), \(-3.042\) MeV (\(j=5/2\)), and \(3.763\) MeV (\(j=3/2\)); proton single-particle energies are \(0.044\) MeV (\(j=1/2\)), \(0.690\) MeV (\(j=5/2\)), and \(7.299\) MeV (\(j=3/2\)).
- The NCSM calculation for \(^{18}\)F produced 28 low-lying states whose eigenvalues are reproduced by the SSM calculation using the derived effective interaction.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Navratil (1997) and Lisetskiy (2008), who derived effective interactions for \(p\)-shell nuclei using NCSM calculations, into the \(sd\)-shell region. The method provides an *ab initio* alternative to phenomenological \(sd\)-shell interactions and is complementary to recent approaches based on the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Bogner, 2014) and the coupled-cluster method (Jansen, 2014). The results enable future standard shell-model calculations for heavier \(sd\)-shell nuclei that are beyond the reach of full NCSM calculations, though the present work is limited to systems with at most one valence proton and does not include three-nucleon forces.
