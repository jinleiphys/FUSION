# Probing spherical outbursts of neutron star mergers from an equation of state with composite nucleons

> We consider a variational 'crystalline' equation of state (EOS) that follows from nucleons that are chiral solitons with deep relativistic quark bound states. In this model conventional quark matter does not occur till a high density threshold at which the quark bound states in the nucleons get compressed and merge with the continuum. Once the barrier at this threshold is overcome, roughly when, $n_B \sim 1/fm^3$, we expect the 'crystalline' nuclear matter to make a sudden transition into quark matter when the EOS becomes soft through a decompression. The sudden increase in density triggers a collapse releasing large amount of gravitational potential energy that can generate a spherical outburst (or kilonova) of ejected matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a sudden phase transition from a stiff "crystalline" nuclear matter state to soft quark matter, occurring at a threshold baryon density of roughly \(n_B \sim 1/\text{fm}^3\), can release enough gravitational potential energy to produce the nearly spherical outburst (kilonova) observed in the GW170817 neutron star merger, as reported by Sneppen (2023). The hedge is that the calculations are approximate and heuristic, and the APR 98 equation of state is used as a substitute for the author's own model up to the threshold density.

## Method

The author uses a variational "crystalline" equation of state derived from nucleons modeled as chiral solitons with deep relativistic quark bound states, based on a relativistic Dirac band-structure calculation of a cubic lattice of solitonic composite nucleons (Banerjee, Glendenning, and Soni, 1985). To estimate the gravitational energy release from the phase transition, the author substitutes the APR 98 nuclear equation of state for the crystalline state up to the threshold density and performs a Maxwell construction to find the matching density in the quark matter phase at equal pressure. A Newtonian estimate of the gravitational potential energy difference is then computed for a uniform-density 2 solar mass star undergoing a density change from \(\rho_1 \sim 1.6 \times 10^{15}\) g/cc to \(\rho_2 \sim 2.53 \times 10^{15}\) g/cc.

## Key numbers

- Threshold baryon density for the transition from crystalline nuclear matter to quark matter: \(n_B \sim 1/\text{fm}^3\) (roughly).
- Transition density used in the APR 98 substitute calculation: \(n_B \sim 0.95/\text{fm}^3\), with pressure \(P \sim 600~\text{MeV}/\text{fm}^3\) in the nuclear phase.
- Matching density in the quark matter phase at equal pressure: \(n_B \sim 1.5/\text{fm}^3\), with energy per baryon \(EB \sim 1260\) MeV.
- Corresponding mass densities: \(\rho_1 \sim 1.6 \times 10^{15}\) g/cc (at \(n_{B1} \sim 0.95/\text{fm}^3\)) and \(\rho_2 \sim 2.53 \times 10^{15}\) g/cc (at \(n_{B2} \sim 1.5/\text{fm}^3\)).
- Estimated gravitational energy release for a 2 solar mass star (\(M \sim 4 \times 10^{33}\) g): \(\Delta E_G \sim 0.7 \times 10^{53}\) ergs.
- Energy injection required for spherical outburst according to Sneppen (2023): more than about 30 MeV per baryon.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier model (Soni, 2019) and the crystalline equation of state of Banerjee, Glendenning, and Soni (1985) to explain the spherical outburst observed in the GW170817 neutron star merger, as analyzed by Sneppen (2023). It contrasts with hybrid crossover models (Baym, Hatsuda, Kojo, Powell, Song, and Takatsuka, 2018) that lack a density discontinuity, and with the APR 98 equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) which allows a mixed phase before transitioning to quark matter. The model enables a new scenario for neutron star mergers where a central, isotropic explosion from a sudden phase transition can produce the observed spherical ejecta, unlike anisotropic mechanisms such as rotational or magnetic energy dissipation.
