# Ground State Magnetic Dipole Moment of $^{40}$Sc

> The hyperfine coupling constants of the proton dripline odd-odd $^{40}$Sc nucleus were deduced from the hyperfine spectrum of the $3d4s\,^3\text{D}_2$\,$\leftrightarrow$\,$3d4p\,^3\text{F}^\circ_3$ transition in Sc II, measured by the bunched beam collinear laser spectroscopy technique. The ground state magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments were determined for the first time as $μ$\,=\,$+5.57(4)(2)\,μ_N$ and $Q$\,=\,$+42(38)(28)\,e^2\;{\rm fm^2}$, respectively. The magnetic moment is well reproduced by the additivity rule with magnetic moments of neighboring odd-even nuclei in the vicinity of the doubly-magic $^{40}$Ca nucleus. An ab-initio multishell valence-space Hamiltonian was also employed to calculate the magnetic moment of $^{40}$Sc, which spans across the $sd$ and $fp$ nuclear shells, where we obtained good agreements.

## Key claim

The ground state magnetic dipole moment of the proton dripline nucleus $^{40}$Sc was measured for the first time, and the additivity rule using magnetic moments of neighboring odd-even nuclei ($^{41}$Sc and $^{39}$Ca) reproduces the experimental value well, provided that configuration mixing and mesonic effects are accounted for via an empirical correction.

## Method

The hyperfine spectrum of the $3d4s\,^3\text{D}_2\leftrightarrow3d4p\,^3\text{F}^\circ_3$ transition in Sc II was measured using bunched beam collinear laser spectroscopy at the BECOLA facility. Radioactive $^{40}$Sc ions were produced via a one-proton pickup and one-neutron removal reaction of a 140 MeV/A $^{40}$Ca beam on a natural Be target, separated in the A1900 fragment separator, thermalized in a gas cell, and extracted at 30 keV. The hyperfine coupling constants were extracted from fits to the spectra, and the magnetic moment was deduced by comparison to the reference isotope $^{45}$Sc using the ratio of hyperfine A constants and nuclear spins.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole moment of $^{40}$Sc: $\mu = +5.57(4)(2)\,\mu_N$ (first measurement)
- Electric quadrupole moment of $^{40}$Sc: $Q = +42(38)(28)\,e\,\text{fm}^2$ (first measurement, large uncertainty)
- Hyperfine A constant for $^{40}$Sc $^3\text{D}_2$ state: $A^{\text{hf}} = +520.1(39)(18)\,\text{MHz}$
- Hyperfine B constant for $^{40}$Sc $^3\text{D}_2$ state: $B^{\text{hf}} = +58(52)(39)\,\text{MHz}$
- Magnetic moment of reference $^{45}$Sc: $\mu = +4.75631(48)\,\mu_N$ (re-evaluated from NMR data)
- Magnetic moment of $^{41}$Sc: $+5.4376(80)(06)\,\mu_N$ (this work)
- Magnetic moment of $^{39}$Ca: $(+)1.0217(1)\,\mu_N$ (from literature, Minamisono 1976)

## Context

This work extends measurements of ground state magnetic moments to the proton dripline odd-odd nucleus $^{40}$Sc, which lies near the doubly-magic $^{40}$Ca and requires cross-shell excitations spanning the $sd$ and $fp$ major shells. The experimental result is compared to the additivity rule using known moments of $^{41}$Sc (Minamisono 1990) and $^{39}$Ca (Minamisono 1976), and to ab-initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions. The good agreement with the empirically corrected additivity rule and with IMSRG calculations benchmarks these theoretical approaches for odd-odd nuclei requiring cross-shell excitations, and enables future studies of nuclear structure near the proton drip line.
