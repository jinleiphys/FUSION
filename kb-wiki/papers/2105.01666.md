# A dispersive estimate of scalar contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering

> We consider the contribution of scalar resonances to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. While the $f_0(500)$ has already been addressed in previous work using dispersion relations, heavier scalar resonances have only been estimated in hadronic models so far. Here, we compare an implementation of the $f_0(980)$ resonance in terms of the coupled-channel $S$-waves for $γ^*γ^*\to ππ/\bar K K$ to a narrow-width approximation, which indicates $a_μ^{\text{HLbL}}[f_0(980)]=-0.2(2)\times 10^{-11}$. With a similar estimate for the $a_0(980)$, the combined effect is thus well below $1\times 10^{-11}$ in absolute value. We also estimate the contribution of heavier scalar resonances. In view of the very uncertain situation concerning their two-photon couplings we suggest to treat them together with other resonances of similar mass when imposing the matching to short-distance constraints. Our final result is a refined estimate of the $S$-wave rescattering effects in the $ππ$ and $\bar K K$ channel up to about $1.3$ GeV and including a narrow-width evaluation of the $a_0(980)$: $a_μ^\text{HLbL}[\text{scalars}]=-9(1)\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim
The paper provides a refined dispersive estimate of scalar resonance contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon g-2, finding that the combined scalar contribution is small (well below 1 × 10⁻¹¹ in absolute value for the f₀(980) and a₀(980)), but the result depends on the chosen basis for the hadronic light-by-light tensor decomposition and individual narrow-width estimates are not basis-independent.

## Method
The authors use a coupled-channel Muskhelishvili–Omnès formalism for γ*γ* → ππ/KK̅ S-wave helicity amplitudes, incorporating data-driven hadronic Omnès functions from an N/D analysis fitted to Roy and Roy–Steiner results for ππ → ππ and ππ → KK̅. They compare this dispersive rescattering approach to a narrow-width approximation for scalar resonances, using transition form factors from a quark model that interpolates between the normalization at zero virtualities and short-distance constraints from light-cone expansion. The sum rule that ensures basis independence is monitored to assess uncertainties from the high-energy continuation.

## Key numbers
- a_μ^HLbL[S-wave rescattering] = −8.7(1.0) × 10⁻¹¹ (combined S-wave rescattering including I=0 and I=2 contributions up to about 1.3 GeV)
- a_μ^HLbL[f₀(980)]|_rescattering = −0.2(1) × 10⁻¹¹ (from integrating the deficit in the spectral function around the f₀(980) region)
- a_μ^HLbL[f₀(980)]|_NWA = −0.37(6) × 10⁻¹¹ (narrow-width approximation using M_f₀(980) = 0.99 GeV and Γ_γγ[f₀(980)] = 0.31(5) keV)
- a_μ^HLbL[f₀(980)] = −0.2(2) × 10⁻¹¹ (combined estimate from rescattering and narrow-width approaches)
- a_μ^HLbL[a₀(980)] = −(0.6_−0.1⁺⁰·²) × 10⁻¹¹ (from coupled-channel analysis with Γ_γγ[a₀(980)] = 0.5⁺⁰·²_−0.1 keV)
- a_μ^HLbL[scalars] = −9(1) × 10⁻¹¹ (final refined estimate including S-wave rescattering in ππ and KK̅ channels up to 1.3 GeV and narrow-width evaluation of a₀(980))
- Sum rule violation for S-wave I=0 rescattering: 5% remainder (indicating approximate basis independence)

## Context
This work extends the dispersive treatment of scalar contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering beyond the f₀(500) studied in Colangelo et al. (2017) using ππ phase shifts, by including coupled-channel effects with KK̅ intermediate states to address the f₀(980) region. The results contradict earlier model-dependent estimates from Pauk et al. (2014) and Knecht et al. (2018) that used Lagrangian definitions without proper treatment of basis dependence. The analysis enables a consistent combination of scalar contributions with other intermediate states (pseudoscalars, axial-vectors, pion and kaon boxes) within the dispersive framework, and suggests that heavier scalar resonances above 1 GeV should be included in the matching to short-distance constraints rather than treated as separate narrow resonances.
