# Uncertainty quantified three-body model applied to the two-neutron halo $^{22}$C

> Two-neutron halo nuclei offer a fascinating probe into the behaviour of quantum few-body systems at the limits of binding. Although few nuclei have already been clearly identified, many of their properties remain poorly constrained. $^{22}$C, one of the heaviest, still lacks a precise identification of its static and dynamic properties, such as its mass and dipole strength in the continuum. One main difficulty is that properties of two-neutron halo nuclei are inferred from experimental data using a theoretical model. Therefore, accurately determining the characteristics of two-neutron halo nuclei requires an accurate theoretical model and careful quantification of the uncertainties. In this work, we examine $^{22}$C with a three-body model, seeing $^{22}$C as a $^{20}$C core and two halo neutrons, and quantify for the first time the uncertainties associated with the $^{20}$C-$n$ interaction using a Bayesian approach. We propagate these uncertainties to properties of bound and scattering states of $^{22}$C, as well as its dipole strength. The comparison of our prediction for the matter radius to experimentally-derived values suggests that $^{22}$C is bound by less than 0.35~MeV and is dominated by a $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration. Our analysis of the dipole strength shows that final-state interaction needs to be included for an accurate description, the uncertainties on the strength function are about 50\% and are mostly influenced by uncertainties on the ground-state properties, and partial-wave occupation of $^{22}$C depends on the scattering length and the $d_{3/2}$ resonance energy of the $^{20}$C-$n$ unbound system. Such sensitivity of the dipole strength to the properties of both $^{21}$C and $^{22}$C properties motivates a precise measurement of the $^{22}$C dipole strength function, that will allow to precisely and accurately resolve the spectroscopy of these nuclei.

# Uncertainty quantified three-body model applied to the two-neutron halo $^{22}$C

## Key claim

The paper suggests that $^{22}$C is bound by less than 0.35 MeV and dominated by a $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration, but this conclusion depends on comparing the predicted matter radius to experimentally-derived values that likely have underestimated uncertainties because they do not include contributions from theoretical modelling in the analysis of the reaction data.

## Method

The authors model $^{22}$C as a three-body system consisting of a $^{20}$C core and two halo neutrons using a hyperspherical harmonics framework with an R-matrix approach that treats bound and scattering states consistently. They calibrate the $^{20}$C-$n$ Woods-Saxon potential parameters (depths $V_s$, $V_d$, spin-orbit strength $V_{ls}$, and radius $R$) to available information on the $^{21}$C low-lying spectrum using a Bayesian approach with Monte-Carlo sampling, then propagate the posterior uncertainties to $^{22}$C observables by performing three-body calculations for 315 samples.

## Key numbers

- The $s$-wave scattering length used for the virtual state in $^{21}$C is $-2.8 \pm 1.4$ fm, using the mean value from Mosby (2013) with a conservative large error assigned
- The $d_{5/2}$ resonance in $^{21}$C is at $1.5 \pm 0.1$ MeV from Leblond (2015); the $d_{3/2}$ resonance is assumed not to be seen below 2.5 MeV
- The two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ is tested at five values: 0.1, 0.2, 0.35, 0.5, and 1.0 MeV
- The matter radius derived from interaction cross sections on a hydrogen target is $5.4 \pm 0.9$ fm (Tanaka 2010) and on a carbon target is $3.4 \pm 0.08$ fm (Togano 2016); these correspond to hyperradii of $22 \pm 5$ fm and $9.1 \pm 0.7$ fm respectively
- The $^{20}$C core rms radius is $2.98 \pm 0.05$ fm (Ozawa 2001)
- The uncertainties on the dipole strength function are about 50% and are mostly influenced by uncertainties on the $^{22}$C ground-state properties

## Context

This work extends previous three-body model calculations of two-neutron halo nuclei (Nunes 1996, Tostevin 2001, Thompson 2004, Lovell 2017, Pinilla 2016, Ershov 2012, Horiuchi 2006) by adding Bayesian uncertainty quantification for the core-neutron interaction, which had not been done before for such systems. The results contradict the larger matter radius derived by Tanaka (2010) and are only consistent with the smaller value from Togano (2016) for $s$-wave dominated configurations with $S_{2n} \lesssim 0.35$ MeV. The analysis enables future precise measurements of the $^{22}$C dipole strength function to resolve the spectroscopy of both $^{21}$C and $^{22}$C, and the framework can be applied to other two-neutron halo systems.
