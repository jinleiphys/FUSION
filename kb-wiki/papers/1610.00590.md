# Systematic Extraction of QGP Properties

> We investigate the collision energy dependence of shear viscosity over the entropy density ratio $η/s$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6, 39$, and $62.4$ GeV, using Bayesian statistical analysis and Gaussian process emulators to explore the full input parameter space of a transport+hydrodynamics hybrid model. The ratio is found to decrease as a function of collision energy, supporting the results from previous studies performed with the same hybrid model.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s in Au+Au collisions decreases with increasing collision energy, but the posterior distributions show that no parameter values are fully excluded given the present uncertainties, so the median and peak values shift toward lower η/s at higher energies rather than being definitively constrained.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining UrQMD hadron+strings cascade for the initial state and (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with a constant η/s input, transitioning back to transport at a switching energy density ε_SW. They performed Bayesian statistical analysis with Gaussian process emulators and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to explore the full input parameter space, calibrating against STAR data for charged particle elliptic flow v₂{2} and K/π ratios at √s_NN = 19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- η/s posterior median and peak values move toward lower values at higher collision energies across √s_NN = 19.6, 39, and 62.4 GeV
- 100 random parameter combinations were drawn from the posterior for verification, with full model simulations run at the median values
- Charged particle elliptic flow v₂{2} was compared at √s_NN = 19.6 and 39 GeV, with simulation results using posterior median values shown with 10% relative error bars
- Transverse momentum distributions for π⁻ and K⁺ at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV for (0-15)% centrality were compared to PHOBOS data

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian statistical analysis previously applied to Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC by Bernhard (2016) to Au+Au collisions at RHIC beam energy scan energies. The results confirm the collision energy dependence of η/s found in earlier studies by Karpenko (2015) using the same hybrid model. The analysis enables future simultaneous fitting of the full beam energy scan range once η/s parametrizations include both temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B dependence, and provides a model-agnostic framework for quantitative comparisons between different theoretical approaches.
