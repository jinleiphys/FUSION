# Ab initio lattice results for Fermi polarons in two dimensions

> We investigate the attractive Fermi polaron problem in two dimensions using non-perturbative Monte Carlo simulations. We introduce a new Monte Carlo algorithm called the impurity lattice Monte Carlo method. This algorithm samples the path integral in a computationally efficient manner and has only small sign oscillations for systems with a single impurity. As a benchmark of the method, we calculate the universal polaron energy in three dimensions in the scale-invariant unitarity limit and find agreement with published results. We then present the first fully non-perturbative calculations of the polaron energy in two dimensions and density correlations between the impurity and majority particles in the limit of zero range interactions. We find evidence for a smooth crossover transition from fermionic quasiparticle to molecular state as a function of interaction strength.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully non-perturbative *ab initio* lattice calculations of the Fermi polaron energy and density correlations in two dimensions, finding evidence for a smooth crossover transition from fermionic quasiparticle to molecular state as a function of interaction strength, rather than a sharp phase transition.

## Method

The authors introduce a new impurity lattice Monte Carlo (ILMC) method that hybridizes worldline and auxiliary-field Monte Carlo algorithms: the spacetime worldlines of each impurity are sampled explicitly while all other particles are handled using the auxiliary-field formalism. They simulate a system of N up-spin fermions (the majority bath) and one down-spin impurity on periodic spatial lattices of size L^2 with temporal extent L_t, using the lattice Hamiltonian with zero-range attractive interactions tuned to reproduce two-body bound state energies. The ground state energy is extracted from Euclidean time projection amplitudes, and density-density correlations between impurity and majority particles are measured directly from the path integral.

## Key numbers

- 3D unitarity limit polaron energy ratio: theta = epsilon_p / epsilon_F = -0.622(9), in agreement with diagrammatic Monte Carlo result theta = -0.618 from Prokof'ev (2008) and experimental values theta = -0.58(5) from Shin (2008) and theta = -0.64(7) from Sommer (2012)
- 2D simulations performed for binding momenta kappa_B = sqrt(m|epsilon_B|) equal to 0.22, 0.31, 0.43, 0.53, and 0.62 in units of hbar/a, with lattice sizes ranging from 20^2 x 100 to 80^2 x 700
- Crossover region identified in the dimensionless parameter eta = (1/2) ln(2 epsilon_F / |epsilon_B|) between eta = -0.90 and eta = -0.75, consistent with diag MC transition at eta = -0.95 +/- 0.15 from Vlietinck (2013) and eta = -1.1 +/- 0.2 from Kroiss (2014), and with experimental result eta = -0.88(20) from Kohl (2012) after conversion to pure 2D
- Density correlations measured for N = 8, 14, 20 up-spin particles on L^2 = 40^2 lattice at eta = 1.5, 0.5, -0.8, -1.0, showing central peak width approximately hbar/k_F independent of interaction strength

## Context

This work extends prior diagrammatic Monte Carlo studies of 2D Fermi polarons by Vlietinck (2013) and Kroiss (2014), and variational calculations by Parish (2013) and Parish (2014), by providing the first fully non-perturbative *ab initio* lattice calculation that does not require separate treatments of polaron and molecular states. The ILMC method enables direct observation of the smooth crossover between these states through both energy calculations and density-density correlations, and the authors note the method can be applied to other impurity problems including alpha particles in dilute neutron gases and Lambda hyperons in nuclear matter.
