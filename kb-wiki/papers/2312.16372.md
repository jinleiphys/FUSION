# Convergence of Ginzburg-Landau expansions: superconductivity in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory and chiral symmetry breaking in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the convergence of the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion in the context of the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory for superconductivity and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for chiral symmetry breaking at finite temperature $T$ and chemical potential $μ$. We present derivations of the all-order formulas for the coefficients of the GL expansions in both systems under the mean-field approximation. We show that the convergence radii for the BCS gap $Δ$ and dynamical quark mass $M$ are given by $Δ_\text{conv} = πT$ and $M_\text{conv} = \sqrt{μ^2 + (πT)^2}$, respectively. We also discuss the implications of these results and the quantitative reliability of the GL expansion near the first-order chiral phase transition.

## Key claim
The Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion for the BCS gap Δ and the NJL dynamical quark mass M has a finite radius of convergence under the mean-field approximation, given by Δ_conv = πT and M_conv = √(μ² + (πT)²) respectively, meaning that adding more terms to the expansion improves results only when the order parameter lies within these radii.

## Method
The authors derive all-order formulas for the GL expansion coefficients in both the BCS theory and the NJL model by expanding the free energy in powers of the order parameter (Δ or M) using a Matsubara frequency representation. For the BCS case, they compute the coefficients from the integral J₀ involving the density of states near the Fermi surface, taking the Debye cutoff to infinity for terms that remain finite. For the NJL model, they compute coefficients from the integral J₂ with a momentum cutoff Λ, using polygamma functions and polylogarithms to obtain closed-form expressions for the nth-order coefficients.

## Key numbers
- Convergence radius for BCS gap: Δ_conv = πT
- Convergence radius for NJL dynamical quark mass: M_conv = √(μ² + (πT)²)
- Fourth-order GL coefficient in BCS: α₄ = (7ζ(3)/16) × ρ/(πT)², where ρ is the density of states at the Fermi surface
- For BCS with ρG = 0.18, the GL solution converges when T/T_c ≥ 0.53
- The zero-temperature BCS gap: Δ₀ = 2ω_D e^{-1/(ρG)}
- The critical temperature in BCS: T_c = e^γ Δ₀/π

## Context
This work extends the study of convergence properties of effective field theory expansions, previously investigated for hydrodynamic derivative expansions in holographic plasmas (Grozdanov 2019) and for perturbative expansions in quantum field theory (Dyson 1952, 't Hooft 1977). The authors provide the first explicit formulas for generic higher-order GL coefficients in the NJL model in 3+1 dimensions and the first determination of convergence radii for both systems. The results enable quantitative assessment of when truncating the GL expansion at finite order is reliable, particularly near the first-order chiral phase transition in the NJL model, and clarify the physical origin of the different convergence radii between BCS and NJL systems.
