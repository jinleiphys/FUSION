# Electromagnetic production of electron and positron in collisions of heavy nuclei

> We consider the cross-section of electromagnetic production of $e^+ e^-$ pair in adiabatic scattering of heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The cross-section for electromagnetic production of \(e^+ e^-\) pairs in adiabatic collisions of heavy nuclei (specifically uranium, \(Z \simeq 92\), at relative velocity \(v \simeq 0.1\)) is comparable to the previously estimated nonperturbative positron production cross-section, despite being a perturbative QED effect.

## Method

The author calculates the cross-section for \(e^+ e^-\) pair production via two virtual photons exchanged between the colliding heavy nuclei, treating the nuclei classically as point charges moving along straight-line trajectories (small-angle scattering). The cross-section is derived from a Feynman diagram with double lines for heavy nuclei, single lines for electrons/positrons, and wavy lines for photons, then estimated by averaging over the orientation of the total pair momentum and using dimensional analysis to obtain a closed-form expression.

## Key numbers

- The estimated cross-section for electromagnetic \(e^+ e^-\) production in uranium-uranium collisions is \(\sigma \sim 0.4 \cdot 10^{-25} \; \mathrm{cm}^2\).
- The typical momenta of produced electrons and positrons are about 15 MeV, derived from the bound \((\bar{p}_{-}+\bar{p}_{+})^2 > 12\,m^2/v^2\) with \(v \simeq 0.1\).
- The cross-section formula contains the factor \(32 (Z\alpha)^4 v^3 / \pi^4\), with \(Z \simeq 92\), \(\alpha = 1/137\), and \(v \simeq 0.1\).
- The result is on the same order as the earlier estimate \(\sigma_P \sim 10^{-25} \; \mathrm{cm}^2\) for positron production with electron capture from Popov (1970s).

## Context

This work extends the study of electron-positron pair creation in heavy-ion collisions, previously considered for superheavy nuclei near the critical charge \(Z_c \simeq 170\) (Pomeranchuk and Smorodinsky, 1945; Zeldovich and Popov, 1970s), where nonperturbative effects dominate. The author shows that for subcritical nuclei like uranium (\(Z=92\)), a perturbative QED mechanism yields a cross-section comparable to the nonperturbative estimate of Popov (1970s), enabling experimental searches for pair production in collisions of common heavy nuclei without requiring superheavy elements.
