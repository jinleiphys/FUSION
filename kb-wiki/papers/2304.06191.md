# A generalized hydrodynamizing initial stage for Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present an extended Bayesian analysis using Trajectum where the initial condition can now include binary scaling. For the far-from-equilibrium evolution before hydrodynamics we introduce an interpolation between free streaming and a holographically inspired evolution that exhibits fast hydrodynamization. We find strong evidence that binary scaling is incompatible with experimental data and find evidence that the holographic far-from-equilibrium evolution is preferred. We end with a discussion on several changes and improvements in the Bayesian framework.

## Key claim

The paper finds strong evidence that binary scaling energy deposition (proportional to the product of thickness functions) is incompatible with experimental heavy-ion collision data, and that a holographically inspired far-from-equilibrium evolution is preferred over free streaming, though the preference depends on the hydrodynamization time and the set of observables used.

## Method

The authors extend the Bayesian analysis framework Trajectum by introducing a generalized T R ENTo initial condition with an extra parameter \(q\) that interpolates between energy density and entropy density interpretations, and by adding a parameter \(r_\text{hyd}\) that linearly interpolates between free-streaming and holographically inspired pre-hydrodynamic evolution. They perform Markov Chain Monte Carlo fits to 653 experimental data points from PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, including both integrated and \(p_T\)-differential observables, and compare the posterior distributions for the new parameters.

## Key numbers

- The posterior for the new \(q\) parameter is \(q = 1.34^{+0.14}_{-0.18}\) at 90% confidence, ruling out binary scaling (\(q=2\)) and consistent with \(\rho \propto (\mathcal{T}_A\mathcal{T}_B)^{2/3}\).
- The nucleon width is constrained to \(w = 0.55^{+0.13}_{-0.14}\) fm, based on the inclusion of the total nuclear cross section \(\sigma_{AA}\).
- The reference energy scale is set to \(E_\text{ref} = 0.2\) GeV to minimize correlation between \(N\) and \(q\).
- The saturation scale used in IP-Glasma comparisons is \(Q_{s,0} = 0.794\) GeV.
- IP-Glasma coarse-grained results at a lattice spacing of 0.94 fm (factor 16) yield best-fit \(q\) values consistent with the posterior.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analyses of Bernhard (2019) and Nijs et al. (2020, 2021) by generalizing the T R ENTo initial condition and the pre-hydrodynamic stage. It directly tests the binary scaling model (Bialas 1976, Shor 1988) and the free-streaming assumption used in prior Trajectum studies, finding both disfavored. The results enable future analyses to use a physically motivated initial condition with \(p=0\) and a holography-inspired pre-equilibrium stage, and they provide a benchmark for comparing microscopic models such as IP-Glasma and EKRT against experimental data.
