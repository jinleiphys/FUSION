# New fixed points of the renormalisation group for two-body scattering

> We outline a separable matrix ansatz for the potentials in effective field theories of nonrelativistic two-body systems with short-range interactions. We use this ansatz to construct new fixed points of the renormalisation-group equation for these potentials. New fixed points indicate a much richer structure than previously recognized in the RG flows of simple short-range potentials.

# New fixed points of the renormalisation group for two-body scattering

## Key claim
The paper constructs an infinite number of new fixed points of the renormalisation-group equation for two-body scattering by short-range forces, using a separable matrix ansatz for the potential. The authors show that each new fixed point has at least two unstable directions, meaning a physical system described by one would require fine-tuning of at least two parameters, making such points unlikely to be realised in practice.

## Method
The authors start from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for S-wave two-body scattering and derive an RG equation for the potential by demanding cutoff independence of the K-matrix. They introduce a separable matrix ansatz where the potential is written as a product of a column vector of powers of off-shell momenta, a matrix of energy-dependent coefficients, and a row vector. This ansatz reduces the nonlinear RG equation to a set of uncoupled linear equations for the inverse of the coefficient matrix, which can be integrated analytically to find fixed-point solutions and the renormalised trajectories connecting them.

## Key numbers
- The unitary fixed point (N=1, S_1={0}) has one relevant perturbation, with the parameter α related to the scattering length by α = π/(2a).
- The separable fixed point with S_1={1} has three relevant perturbations, with scaling dimensions corresponding to Λ^(-5), Λ^(-3), and Λ^(-1).
- The two-term separable fixed point with S_2={0,1} has six relevant perturbations, with scaling dimensions corresponding to Λ^(-1), Λ^(-3), Λ^(-5), and combinations involving p^2.
- For the two-term fixed point, when αγ-β^2 is nonzero, the determinant of the coefficient matrix behaves as Λ^6/(αγ-β^2) + O(Λ^7) as Λ→0.

## Context
This work extends the RG analysis of two-body scattering by short-range forces, which began with Weinberg's observation of a nontrivial fixed point and was developed further by Gegelia (1998), Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), van Kolck (1998), and Birse (1998). The existence of additional fixed points had been conjectured by Cohen (1999) and hints were seen in a functional RG analysis by Harada and Kubo (2006), but no explicit forms were found. The new fixed points and their renormalised trajectories provide a systematic framework for power counting in effective field theories expanded around these points, though the requirement of multiple fine-tuned parameters makes them unlikely to describe natural physical systems.
