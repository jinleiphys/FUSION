# Trap anharmonicity and sloshing mode of a Fermi gas

> For a gas trapped in a harmonic potential, the sloshing (or Kohn) mode is undamped and its frequency coincides with the trap frequency, independently of the statistics, interaction and temperature of the gas. However, experimental trap potentials have usually Gaussian shape and anharmonicity effects appear as the temperature and, in the case of Fermions, the filling of the trap are increased. We study the sloshing mode of a degenerate Fermi gas in an anharmonic trap within the Boltzmann equation, including in-medium effects in both the transport and collision terms. The calculated frequency shifts and damping rates of the sloshing mode due to the trap anharmonicity are in satisfactory agreement with the available experimental data. We also discuss higher-order dipole, octupole, and bending modes and show that the damping of the sloshing mode is caused by its coupling to these modes.

# Trap anharmonicity and sloshing mode of a Fermi gas

## Key claim

The sloshing (Kohn) mode of a degenerate Fermi gas in an anharmonic trap exhibits frequency shifts and damping rates that can be described by solving the Boltzmann equation with in-medium effects, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the assumed value of the harmonic trap frequency; the damping arises from coupling of the center-of-mass motion to higher-order collective modes (radial dipole, octupole, and bending modes) that are damped by collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for a two-component Fermi gas in the normal-fluid phase, including a mean-field potential from ladder-approximation self-energy and in-medium modified cross sections. They use the phase-space moments method with an ansatz containing first-order terms (x and p_x) and an extended 18-term third-order ansatz that includes all monomials of third order in position and momentum satisfying the mode symmetries. The trap potential is modeled as a sixth-order expansion of a Gaussian optical dipole trap with additional magnetic confinement, using parameters from the Innsbruck experiment (Riedl 2008). Mode frequencies and damping rates are extracted by fitting the response function's strength peak with a single Lorentzian.

## Key numbers

- Trap parameters from Riedl (2008): radial trap frequency ω_rad/(2π) = 1100 Hz, axial trap frequency ω_z/(2π) = 26 Hz, trap depth V_0 = 19 μK, atom number N = 6×10^5 of ^6Li in the unitary limit (1/(k_F a) = 0)
- Fermi energy E_F = (3N ω_rad^2 ω_z)^(1/3), giving E_F/V_0 ≈ 0.1
- At T/T_F = 0.4, the sloshing mode survives for several hundreds of oscillation periods; at T/T_F = 1.2, its amplitude decreases by a factor of 1/e after approximately 6 oscillations
- The frequency shift from the harmonic trap frequency ω_x is below ω_x for all temperatures studied, with the shift increasing with temperature
- The first-order result without mean field gives a larger frequency shift than the full calculation; the mean field reduces the shift most strongly at low temperature
- Comparison with Wu and Zhang (2012) using trap parameters ω_rad/(2π) = 1800 Hz, ω_z/(2π) = 32 Hz, V_0 = 50 μK shows agreement above T/T_F ~ 0.5, but the present calculation gives slightly stronger frequency shifts at lower temperatures

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-equation approach of Chiacchiera (2009, 2011) to the sloshing mode in anharmonic traps, building on the Kohn theorem (Kohn 1961, Brey 1989) which guarantees undamped oscillation at the trap frequency in harmonic potentials. The study addresses the ad-hoc corrections used in Riedl (2008) and Wu and Zhang (2012) by providing a rigorous theoretical description of anharmonicity effects. The results enable more precise interpretation of collective-mode experiments in optical dipole traps, particularly for determining trap frequencies and correcting damping rates of other modes. The formalism can be extended to study other collective modes and to include superfluid phases.
