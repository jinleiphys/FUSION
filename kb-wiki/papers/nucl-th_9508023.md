# Precise solution of few-body problems with stochastic variational method on correlated Gaussian basis

> Precise variational solutions are given for problems involving diverse fermionic and bosonic $N=2-7$-body systems. The trial wave functions are chosen to be combinations of correlated Gaussians, which are constructed from products of the single-particle Gaussian wave packets through an integral transformation, thereby facilitating fully analytical calculations of the matrix elements. The nonlinear parameters of the trial function are chosen by a stochastic technique. The method has proved very efficient, virtually exact, and it seems feasible for any few-body bound-state problems emerging in nuclear or atomic physics.

## Key claim

The stochastic variational method (SVM) on a correlated Gaussian basis provides a virtually exact, fully analytical variational solution for few-body bound-state problems with N=2-7 particles, but the claim of "virtually exact" is conditioned on the stochastic selection of nonlinear parameters being repeated until the energy converges, with the rate of convergence controlled by dynamically decreasing a preset energy-gain threshold.

## Method

The trial wave function is expanded as a linear combination of correlated Gaussian basis functions, each containing a positive-definite symmetric matrix A of nonlinear parameters and a global angular function defined by a single solid spherical harmonic of a linear combination of Jacobi coordinates. The basis is built stepwise by randomly generating candidate parameter sets, evaluating the energy gained by including each candidate via a reduced eigenvalue problem, and keeping only those candidates whose energy gain exceeds a dynamically decreasing threshold. Hamiltonian matrix elements are computed fully analytically by first evaluating Slater determinants of single-particle Gaussian wave packets in generator coordinates, then transforming to relative coordinates to eliminate center-of-mass motion, and finally performing an integral transformation to the correlated Gaussian basis.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method previously applied to multicluster descriptions of light exotic nuclei (Varga and Suzuki, 1994) by generalizing it to encompass diverse fermionic and bosonic systems with nuclear or Coulomb interactions. It provides an alternative to Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods (Payne, 1990; Faddeev, 1965; Glockle, 1983), conventional variational approaches (ATMS, hyperspherical, CRC-BGV), variational Monte Carlo (VMC), and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods, which have been most successful for systems beyond four nucleons (Pieper, 1995). The method enables fully analytical calculation of matrix elements for up to 7 particles, avoiding the Monte Carlo integration required by other approaches, and produces wave functions in compact analytical form suitable for calculating physical properties.
