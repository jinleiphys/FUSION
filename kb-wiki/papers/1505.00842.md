# Total Reaction Cross Sections in CEM and MCNP6 at Intermediate Energies

> Accurate total reaction cross section models are important to achieving reliable predictions from spallation and transport codes. The latest version of the Cascade Exciton Model (CEM) as incorporated in the code CEM03.03, and the Monte Carlo N-Particle transport code (MCNP6), both developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL), each use such cross sections. Having accurate total reaction cross section models in the intermediate energy region ($\sim$50 MeV to $\sim$5 GeV) is very important for different applications, including analysis of space environments, use in medical physics, and accelerator design, to name just a few. The current inverse cross sections used in the preequilibrium and evaporation stages of CEM are based on the Dostrovsky {\it et al.} model, published in 1959. Better cross section models are available now. Implementing better cross section models in CEM and MCNP6 should yield improved predictions for particle spectra and total production cross sections, among other results. Our current results indicate this is, in fact, the case.

## Key claim

Implementing the NASA total reaction cross section model (with Kalbach systematics for low-energy neutrons) as the inverse cross section in the preequilibrium stage of CEM03.03F yields improved predictions for particle spectra and total production cross sections compared to the older Dostrovsky et al. model from 1959, though the improvement is preliminary and the evaporation stage still uses the older model.

## Method

The authors replaced the Dostrovsky et al. inverse cross section model used in the preequilibrium stage of CEM03.03 with the NASA model (Tripathi et al.), supplemented by Kalbach systematics for low-energy neutrons below approximately 24 MeV. They implemented this change in a new FORTRAN routine called gamagu3, which uses 8-point Gaussian quadrature for low exciton numbers (15 or fewer) and 8-point Gauss-Laguerre quadrature for higher exciton numbers, and compared the resulting total reaction cross sections and emission probabilities against experimental data and calculations from MCNP6 and the Barashenkov and Polanski (B&P) systematics.

## Key numbers

- The intermediate energy region of interest is approximately 50 MeV to 5 GeV.
- The switchpoint from Kalbach systematics to the NASA model for neutron-induced reactions is at approximately 24 MeV.
- For the n + 208Pb reaction, the 8-point Gaussian quadrature sampling points include 3.84 MeV, 19.7 MeV, 45.9 MeV, 79.0 MeV, 114 MeV, 148 MeV, 174 MeV, and 190 MeV; the 8-point Gauss-Laguerre sampling points include 0.428 MeV, 2.27 MeV, 5.66 MeV, 10.7 MeV, 17.7 MeV, 27.1 MeV, 39.6 MeV, and 57.5 MeV.
- For the 12C + 12C reaction, total charge-changing (TCC) cross sections are stated to be 5% to 10% less than total reaction cross sections.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of total reaction cross section models at Los Alamos National Laboratory by Mashnik et al. (2002) and Prael et al., which found the NASA model superior to the Dostrovsky et al., Tsang et al., and Barashenkov and Polanski systematics. The paper directly addresses a recommendation from the 2008-2010 IAEA Benchmark of Spallation Models to improve CEM's ability to predict energetic light fragments. The new inverse cross sections are intended for eventual implementation into MCNP6, which currently uses an updated version of the Barashenkov and Polanski model for mean-free path calculations.
