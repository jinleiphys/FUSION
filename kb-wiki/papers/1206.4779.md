# CP violating Tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing

> In view of the new data from the Daya Bay and RENO collaborations, King has presented a very natural deformation of tri-bimaximal mixing. Here we show that L/E flatness of the e-like event ratio in the atmospheric neutrino data, when coupled with King's observation that the smallest neutrino mixing angle, θ_{13}, seems to be related to the largest quark mixing angle (the Cabibbo angle θ_C), leads to a CP violating tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing. King's tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing follows as a leading order approximation from our result.

# CP violating Tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing

## Key claim
The paper shows that combining the L/E flatness of the e-like event ratio in atmospheric neutrino data with King's observation that θ₁₃ relates to the Cabibbo angle leads to a CP-violating neutrino mixing matrix, with King's tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing emerging as a leading-order approximation. The result depends on the specific choices θ₂₃ = π/4 and δ = ±π/2, which are motivated by the atmospheric data but remain consistent with current global fits.

## Method
The author fixes the neutrino mixing angles to θ₁₂ = arcsin(1/√3) (tri-bimaximal value), θ₂₃ = π/4, and θ₁₃ = arcsin(sin θ_C/√2) using the Wolfenstein parameter λ = 0.2253, then constructs the full 3×3 mixing matrix U^± with δ = ±π/2. The CP-violating asymmetries A_CP are computed analytically from the resulting matrix to leading order in λ.

## Key numbers
- Wolfenstein parameter λ = 0.2253 ± 0.0007, used to relate θ₁₃ to the Cabibbo angle via θ₁₃ = arcsin(λ/√2)
- CP asymmetry magnitude: |A_CP^(μe)| = |A_CP^(τe)| = |A_CP^(τμ)| ≈ 0.146 × (sum of three sine terms involving Δm² and L/2p)
- For Earth, gravitational potential Φ^⊕_grav ≈ 6.95 × 10⁻¹⁰; for neutron stars, Φ^NS_grav ≈ 0.2, implying ~20% modification to A_CP in supernova environments
- Global fit values cited: sin²θ₂₃ = 0.49⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·₀₅ (normal hierarchy) and 0.53⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·₀₇ (inverted hierarchy); δ = (0.83⁺⁰·⁵⁴₋₀·₆₄)π (normal hierarchy)

## Context
This work extends King's tri-bimaximal-Cabibbo mixing proposal (King, 2012) by incorporating the L/E flatness condition from atmospheric neutrino data (Fukuda, 1998) and the earlier observation linking that flatness to θ₂₃ = π/4 and δ = ±π/2 (Ahluwalia, 2002). The resulting CP-violating matrix generalizes King's leading-order result and provides a testable framework for CP violation in neutrino oscillations, with implications for supernova modeling where gravitational redshift modifies the asymmetries at the ~20% level.
