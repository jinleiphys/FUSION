# Strange mesons in nuclear matter at finite temperature

> We study the properties of $K$ and $\bar K$ mesons in nuclear matter at finite temperature from a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels which incorporates the $s$- and p-waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction. The in-medium solution accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on all the baryons involved, and $π$ and kaon self-energies. We calculate $K$ and $\bar K$ (off-shell) spectral functions and single particle properties. The $\bar K$ effective mass gets lowered by about -50 MeV in cold nuclear matter at saturation density and by half this reduction at T=100 MeV. The p-wave contribution to the ${\bar K}$ optical potential, due to $Λ$, $Σ$ and $Σ^*$ excitations, becomes significant for momenta larger than 200 MeV/c and reduces the attraction felt by the $\bar K$ in the nuclear medium.The $\bar K$ spectral function spreads over a wide range of energies, reflecting the melting of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the contribution of $YN^{-1}$ components at finite temperature. In the $KN$ sector, we find that the low-density theorem is a good approximation for the $K$ self-energy close to saturation density due to the absence of resonance-hole excitations. The $K$ potential shows a moderate repulsive behavior, whereas the quasi-particle peak is considerably broadened with increasing density and temperature. We discuss the implications for the decay of the $ϕ$ meson at SIS/GSI energies as well as in the future FAIR/GSI project.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $\bar K$ effective mass is lowered by about -50 MeV in cold nuclear matter at saturation density, but this reduction is only half as large at T=100 MeV, indicating a strong temperature dependence that the abstract's single number does not capture.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels for the $s$-wave kaon-nucleon interaction, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell amplitudes. Medium effects are incorporated by modifying intermediate meson-baryon states through Pauli blocking, mean-field binding potentials for baryons from a Walecka-type $\sigma$-$\omega$ model, and meson self-energies for pions and kaons, with the kaon self-energy determined self-consistently. The $p$-wave contribution to the $\bar K$ self-energy is computed from hyperon-hole ($Yh$) excitations using finite-temperature Lindhard functions, including $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma^*$ pole terms.

## Key numbers

- $\bar K$ effective mass lowered by about -50 MeV in cold nuclear matter at saturation density ($\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$) and T=0 MeV
- $\bar K$ effective mass reduction is half the cold value at T=100 MeV (i.e., about -25 MeV)
- $p$-wave contribution to the $\bar K$ optical potential becomes significant for momenta larger than 200 MeV/c
- Nucleon effective mass $M_N^* = 579$ MeV at $\rho_0$ and T=0 MeV, increasing to 634 MeV at T=100 MeV
- Hyperon attraction at $\rho_0$ and T=0 MeV is about -50 MeV
- Cut-off momentum for regularization of meson-baryon loop functions: $q_{\rm max} = 630$ MeV/c
- Hadronic form factor cut-off for $p$-wave vertices: $\Lambda_K = 1050$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature studies of $\bar K$ in nuclear matter by Ramos et al. (1999) and Tolos et al. (2006) to finite temperature, using the Imaginary Time Formalism to maintain proper analytical properties of retarded Green's functions. The results are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision data from SIS/GSI energies and future FAIR experiments, particularly for understanding $\phi$ meson decay in hot and dense matter, where the $\phi$ predominantly decays into $\bar K K$ pairs and is highly sensitive to kaon effective mass changes.
