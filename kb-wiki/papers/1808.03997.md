# Precision Mass Measurement of $^{58-63}$Cr: Nuclear Collectivity towards the \emph{N}=40 Island of Inversion

> The neutron-rich isotopes $^{58-63}$Cr were produced for the first time at the ISOLDE facility and their masses were measured with the ISOLTRAP spectrometer. The new values are up to 300 times more precise than those in the literature and indicate significantly different nuclear structure from the new mass-surface trend. A gradual onset of deformation is found in this proton and neutron mid-shell region, which is a gateway to the second island of inversion around \emph{N}=40. In addition to comparisons with density-functional theory and large-scale shell-model calculations, we present predictions from the valence-space formulation of the \emph{ab initio} in-medium similarity renormalization group, the first such results for open-shell chromium isotopes.

## Key claim

The new mass measurements of neutron-rich chromium isotopes (⁵⁸⁻⁶³Cr) are up to 300 times more precise than previous literature values and reveal a gradual onset of ground-state collectivity toward N=40, rather than the sudden onset previously suggested by less precise data.

## Method

The chromium isotopes were produced at ISOLDE/CERN via 1.4-GeV proton-induced fission of a UCx target, with selective ionization using a resonance ionization laser ion source (RILIS). Masses of ⁵⁸⁻⁶²Cr were measured using the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance (ToF-ICR) technique in ISOLTRAP's precision Penning trap, while ⁶³Cr was measured using the multi-reflection time-of-flight mass separator (MR-ToF MS) as a mass spectrometer due to its low production yield. The mass values were compared to density-functional theory (UNEDF0), large-scale shell-model calculations (GXPF1A, LNPS, LNPS′), and ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of ⁵⁹Cr: -48,115.9(0.7) keV, compared to AME2016 value of -48,090(220) keV (factor of ~300 improvement in precision)
- Mass excess of ⁶⁰Cr: -46,908.5(1.1) keV, compared to AME2016 value of -46,670(190) keV
- Mass excess of ⁶¹Cr: -42,496.5(1.8) keV, compared to AME2016 value of -42,480(100) keV
- Mass excess of ⁶²Cr: -40,852.6(3.5) keV, compared to AME2016 value of -40,890(150) keV
- Mass excess of ⁶³Cr: -36,178(73) keV, compared to AME2016 value of -36,010(360) keV
- Production yields ranged from 3×10⁵ ions/s for ⁵⁹Cr down to 3×10¹ ions/s for ⁶³Cr
- The δ*₂ₙ values for Cr isotopes at N>36 are smaller than those for Fe isotopes, indicating stronger flattening of S₂ₙ and faster correlation-energy gain

## Context

This work extends precision mass measurements into the chromium isotopic chain, which lies between the previously studied calcium and nickel regions where shell closures at N=32, 34, and 40 had been observed (Steppenbeck 2013, Wienholtz 2013, Bernas 1982). The results provide the first ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations for open-shell chromium isotopes, revealing that current VS-IMSRG calculations (like the GXPF1A shell-model interaction) miss degrees of freedom needed to describe the observed collectivity, likely due to neglected three- or higher-body operators. The new mass values constrain models of neutron-star crust heating and cooling, particularly for the electron-capture sequence ⁶⁴Cr→⁶⁴V→⁶⁴Ti identified as a main heating source in the outer crust (Schatz 2006, PhysRevC.93.035805).
