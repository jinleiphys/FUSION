# Light front field theory of relativistic quark matter

> Light-front quantization to many-particle systems of finite temperature and density provides a novel approach towards a relativistic description of quark matter and allows us to calculate the perturbative as well as the non-perturbative regime of QCD. Utilizing a Dyson expansion of light-front many-body Green functions we have so far calculated three-quark, quark-quark, and quark-antiquark correlations that lead to the chiral phase transition, the formation of hadrons and color superconductivity in a hot and/or dense environment. Presently, we use an effective zero-range interaction, to compare our results with the more traditional instant form approach where applicable.

## Key claim
Light-front quantization can be extended to finite-temperature and finite-density many-particle systems, enabling a unified treatment of both perturbative and non-perturbative QCD regimes. The authors use an effective zero-range interaction as a lowest-order approximation, and the results are compared with the more traditional instant-form approach where applicable.

## Method
The authors utilize a Dyson expansion of light-front many-body Green functions to derive relativistic in-medium few-body equations. They first solve the mean-field problem (neglecting the retardation or memory term) using a zero-range interaction, which is considered the lowest-order effective interaction in a 1/Nc expansion of QCD. The grand canonical partition function is expressed in terms of light-front operators, and Fermi distribution functions are derived for a grand canonical ensemble in equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- Isolated constituent quark mass taken as m = 336 MeV.
- Coupling strength and cut-off Λ are determined to reproduce the nucleon mass m_N = 938 MeV.
- Three-body mass M_3(μ,T) is calculated as a function of chemical potential for different temperatures with a cut-off parameter Λ = 8m.
- Mott transition condition for the three-quark continuum: M_3(μ_Mott, T_Mott) = 3m(μ_Mott, T_Mott).
- Mott transition condition for the quark-diquark continuum: M_3(μ_Mott, T_Mott) = m(μ_Mott, T_Mott) + M_2(μ_Mott, T_Mott).
- Mott lines are shown for two extreme cases: Λ = 4m (with di-quark substate) and Λ = 8m (Borromean nucleon).

## Context
This work extends the light-front quantization approach to finite-temperature field theory, building on prior developments by Dirac (1949) and Brodsky (1997). It generalizes earlier work by Beyer (2001) and Strauss (2004) on the NJL model, where spontaneous symmetry breaking and chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature were demonstrated. The approach enables future studies of how quark matter changes to nucleonic matter at lower temperatures, and provides tools to investigate three-quark correlations in the context of color superconductivity, which has not been explored previously.
