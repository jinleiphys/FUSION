# Strange Particle Production Via The Weak Interaction

> The differential cross sections for the neutrino-induced weak charged current production of strange particles in the threshold energy region are presented. The general representation of the weak hadronic current is newly developed in terms of eighteen unknown invariant amplitudes to parametrize the hadron vertex. The Born term approximation is used for the numerical calculations in the framework of the Cabibbo theory and SU(3) symmetry. For unpolarized octet baryons four processes are investigated, whereas in the case of polarized baryons only one process is chosen to study the sensitivity of the differential cross section to the various polarizations of the initial state nucleon and the final state hyperon.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new model-independent general representation of the weak hadronic current for strange particle production, expressed in terms of eighteen unknown invariant amplitudes, and uses the Born term approximation within Cabibbo theory and SU(3) symmetry to perform numerical calculations near threshold energy, with the caveat that the results depend on the specific model chosen to extract the eighteen structure functions.

## Method

The authors derived the most general Lorentz-covariant form of the weak hadronic current for neutrino-induced charged-current associated production of strange particles (K mesons and hyperons) by expanding the current operator in terms of bilinear covariant basis elements and all available independent four-momenta, yielding eighteen invariant amplitudes. They then applied the Born term approximation, summing contributions from s-channel, t-channel, and u-channel exchange diagrams with parametrized form factors determined from the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis, SU(3) symmetry predictions for strong coupling constants, and the nucleon axial form factor. The differential cross section was computed as a contraction between the leptonic tensor (derived via Casimir's trick) and the hadronic tensor, for four unpolarized octet baryon reactions and one polarized reaction.

## Key numbers

- Fermi constant: \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5} \,\text{GeV}^{-2}\)
- Nucleon axial form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(g_A(0) = +1.26\)
- Axial form factor parameterization: \(g_A(Q^2) = g_A(0) / [1 + 3.31 \tau(Q^2)]^2\), where \(\tau(Q^2) = Q^2 / (4M^2)\)
- Masses used for exchange particles: proton (939 MeV), neutron (939 MeV), K\(^0\) (498 MeV), K\(^+\) (494 MeV), \(\Sigma^+\) (1189 MeV), \(\Sigma^0\) (1192 MeV), \(\Sigma^-\) (1197 MeV), \(\Lambda\) (1116 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical studies of strange particle production via weak interactions by Shrock (1975), Mecklenburg (1976), and Dewan (1980), who employed Cabibbo theory with SU(3) symmetry and decomposed the matrix element in terms of helicity amplitudes from the Born diagram. The new general representation of the hadronic current in terms of eighteen invariant amplitudes allows the formalism to be applied to various reactions and models without recalculating the differential cross section, and is intended to support future experiments such as Miner\(\nu\)a in studying the strange-quark content of the nucleon and the structure of the hadronic weak current.
