# Fission dynamics of $^{252}$Cf

> The time-dependent generator coordinate method with the gaussian overlap approximation (TDGCM+GOA) formalism is applied to describe the fission of $^{252}$Cf. We perform analysis of fission from the initial states laying in the energetic range from the ground state to the state located 4 MeV above the fission barrier. The fission fragment mass distributions, obtained for different parity, energy of levels and types of mixed states, are calculated and compared with experimental data. The impact of the total time of wave packet propagation on the final results is studied as well. The weak dependence of obtained mass yields on the initial conditions is shown.

## Key claim

The TDGCM+GOA formalism applied to fission of 252Cf shows a weak dependence of the obtained mass yields on the initial conditions, including excitation energy and parity of the initial state, but the calculated mass distributions are narrower than experimental data and the most probable mass ratio (140/112) is slightly smaller than the measured one (142/110).

## Method

The authors performed static calculations of the potential energy surface (PES) using the Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov model with D1S Gogny-type interactions, with constraints on quadrupole Q20 and octupole Q30 moments. Dynamic calculations were done within the TDGCM+GOA formalism, solving the collective Hamiltonian eigenproblem in a two-dimensional ground state well to generate initial wave functions, then propagating them through the nuclear potential. The probability flux along the pre-scission line was computed, and the random neck rupture (rnr) mechanism was applied to obtain final fragment mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Fission barrier of 252Cf calculated within HFB with Gogny D1S forces: Bf = 9.71 MeV
- Eigenstates investigated in the energy range from the ground state to Bf + 4 MeV
- Propagation time Tpropag = 2.6 x 10^-23 s (arbitrarily chosen)
- For states with En > Bf + 2 MeV, tunneling probability saturates near 1 at t = 50 x 10^-21 s
- Lowest states have tunneling probability below 1% after Tpropag
- Most probable fragment mass ratio from calculations: 140/112; experimental: 142/110
- Two eigenstates (g34+ at E34 = 11.82 MeV; g35+ at E35 = 11.86 MeV) show divergent behavior, with fission probability changing by only 0.03-0.05 when propagation time is doubled
- Mixed state peaks shifted by 4 mass units compared to experimental data

## Context

This work extends the TDGCM+GOA approach previously applied by Goutte et al. (2005) and others to study fission dynamics, specifically examining how initial conditions affect mass yields for 252Cf. The results are compared with experimental data from CF and CFn, and the random neck rupture mechanism follows the approach of B83, B88, B90, and WZ15. The study enables further investigation of whether non-perturbative mass parameters would modify the mass yields, though the authors note that perturbative and non-perturbative masses produce fairly similar collective parameters at large elongations (bn22).
