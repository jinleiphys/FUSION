# Production of Charm Quarks in a Parton Cascade Model for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm {NN}}}$= 200 GeV

> We study the production and dynamics of heavy quarks in the parton cascade model for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The model is motivated by the QCD parton picture and describes the dynamics of an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision in terms of cascading partons which undergo scattering and multiplication while propagating. We focus on the dynamics of charm quark production and evolution in p+p and Au+Au collisions for several different interaction scenarios, i.e. collisions only between primary partons without radiation of gluons, multiple collisions without radiation of gluons and multiple collisions with radiation of gluons, allowing us to isolate the contributions of parton rescattering and radiation to charm production. We also discuss results of an eikonal approximation of the collision which provides a valuable comparison with mini-jet calculations and clearly brings out the importance of multiple collisions.

# Production of Charm Quarks in a Parton Cascade Model for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV

## Key claim

The parton cascade model (PCM) can describe charm quark production in p+p and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, but only for the early, pre-equilibrium stages prior to quark-gluon plasma formation at $\tau_0 \approx 1$ fm/c; the model is not intended to describe the full thermalized QGP evolution, and its omission of parton fusion processes (violating detailed balance) is justified only because high-momentum heavy quarks are not expected to fully thermalize.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo parton cascade model VNI/BMS, based on a Boltzmann transport description with pQCD matrix elements for 2-to-2 parton-parton interactions and time-like branchings (2-to-3 processes) including gluon splitting into heavy quark pairs. They simulated charm quark production in p+p and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV under three interaction scenarios: (1) collisions only between primary partons without gluon radiation, (2) multiple collisions without radiation, and (3) multiple collisions with gluon radiation via parton showers. An eikonal approximation was used for comparison with minijet calculations using GRV-HO parton distribution functions with fixed $\alpha_s = 0.3$ and $Q_0^2 = 0.589$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- The leading process $gg \rightarrow c\bar{c}$ is about 50% more likely in the eikonal approximation compared to the case when only primary-primary collisions are allowed in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV.
- The number of collisions in p+p increases by about 20% when going from the eikonal approximation to proper multiple scatterings, and by about 60% compared to primary-primary collisions only.
- The momentum cut-off $p_0$ used to regularize 2-to-2 pQCD matrix elements corresponds to $Q_0^2 = 0.589$ GeV$^2$.
- The cut-off for terminating parton shower splittings is $\mu_0 \geq 1$ GeV, with $\mu_0^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ for gluons and $\mu_0^2 = m_q^2 + 1$ GeV$^2$ for quarks.
- The fragmentation ratio for $c \rightarrow D^0$ used for comparison with STAR data is approximately 0.565.
- Results for p+p charm $p_T$ spectra are shown only for $p_T \geq 2$ GeV due to the momentum cut-off.

## Context

This work extends earlier parton cascade model studies of heavy quark dynamics (Geiger 1993, Bass 2002) and provides a pQCD-based baseline for charm production at RHIC energies, complementing hybrid approaches that couple heavy quark transport to hydrodynamic medium evolution (Moore 2004, Cao 2013). The authors compare their results to minijet calculations using the formalism of Mangano (1991) and to STAR $D^0$ data (Ye 2014). The study enables future investigation of whether pQCD-based approaches can describe high-$p_T$ heavy quark data at RHIC and LHC, and helps disentangle initial-state effects and early non-equilibrium contributions from later QGP medium modifications.
