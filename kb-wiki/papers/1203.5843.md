# The total nucleon-nucleon cross section at large N_c

> It is shown that at sufficiently large $N_c$ for incident momenta which are much larger than the QCD, the total nucleon-nucleon cross section is independent of incident momentum and given by $σ^{\rm total}=2 π\log^2(N_c) / (m^2_π)$. This result is valid in the extreme large $N_c$ regime of $\log(N_c) \gg 1$ and has corrections of relative order $\log (\log(N_c))/\log(N_c)$. A possible connection of this result to the Froissart-Martin bound is discussed.

## Key claim

At sufficiently large \(N_c\) and for incident momenta much larger than \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\), the total nucleon-nucleon cross section becomes independent of incident momentum and is given by \(\sigma^{\text{total}} = 2\pi \log^2(N_c) / m_\pi^2\). This result is valid only in the extreme large \(N_c\) regime where \(\log(N_c) \gg 1\), and has corrections of relative order \(\log(\log(N_c)) / \log(N_c)\).

## Method

The author first analyzes a toy model of nonrelativistic potential scattering with a control parameter \(\lambda\) that scales mass, potential strength, and momentum linearly, mirroring the \(N_c\) scaling of nucleon-nucleon scattering. Using a partial-wave decomposition and semi-classical methods, the total cross section is shown to be dominated by large impact parameters where the longest-range Yukawa interaction (analogous to one-pion exchange) controls the phase shifts. The result is then extended to the full QCD case by arguing that the S-matrix elements for nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering scale as \(\log(S) \sim N_c\), and that at large angular momentum the real part of the phase shift from one-pion exchange dominates over the imaginary part, leading to the same \(\log^2(N_c)\) behavior.

## Key numbers

- Total nucleon-nucleon cross section at large \(N_c\): \(\sigma^{\text{total}} = 2\pi \log^2(N_c) / m_\pi^2\), with corrections of relative order \(\log(\log(N_c)) / \log(N_c)\).
- For the physical world of \(N_c = 3\), the formula predicts \(\sigma^{\text{total}} \approx 150\) mb, which is significantly larger than the empirical value of approximately 40 mb.
- Over three orders of magnitude in \(\sqrt{s}\) from 1.5 GeV to 1200 GeV, the proton-proton cross section varies by only about 25%.
- The Froissart-Martin bound gives \(\sigma^{\text{total}} \le (4\pi / m_\pi^2) \log^2(N_c)\) at large \(N_c\); the result satisfies this inequality by exactly a factor of 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the large \(N_c\) analysis of QCD pioneered by 't Hooft and Witten (1979) to the total nucleon-nucleon cross section, a quantity that had received little prior attention except for a recent study of its spin-flavor dependence by Cohen and Gelman (2012). The result is derived from the scaling behavior of the NN interaction (strength \(\sim N_c\), range \(\sim N_c^0\)) and the dominance of one-pion exchange at long range. The \(\log^2(N_c)\) form is strikingly similar to the Froissart-Martin bound, and the author notes that the prediction is not phenomenologically relevant for \(N_c = 3\) because \(\log(3) \gg 1\) is not satisfied, but the result is of theoretical interest for understanding QCD in the large \(N_c\) limit.
