# Possibility of ferromagnetic neutron matter

> We study ferromagnetism at high density of neutrons in the QCD hadron phase, by using the simplest chiral effective model incorporating magnetic fields and the chiral anomaly. Under the assumption of spatial homogeneity, we calculate the energy density as a function of neutron density, with a magnetization and a neutral pion condensation a la Dautry and Neyman. We find that at a high density the energy of the ferromagnetic order is lower than that of the ordinary neutron matter, and the reduction effect is enhanced by the anomaly. Compared to the inhomogeneous phase with the alternating layer structure, our ferromagnetic phase turns out to be unfavored. However, once an axial vector meson condensation is taken into account in our simplest model, the ferromagnetic energy density is lowered significantly, which still leaves some room for a possible realization of a QCD ferromagnetic phase and ferromagnetic magnetars.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of spatial homogeneity, the simplest chiral effective model of dense neutrons with neutral pion condensation and magnetic fields shows that ferromagnetic order becomes energetically favored over ordinary neutron matter at densities above approximately 5 times the standard nuclear density, but this ferromagnetic phase is unfavored compared to the inhomogeneous alternating layer structure (ALS) phase; however, inclusion of axial vector meson condensation significantly lowers the ferromagnetic energy density, leaving open the possibility of a QCD ferromagnetic phase in magnetars.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral effective Lagrangian for neutrons coupled to neutral pions and magnetic fields, including the QCD chiral anomaly term, and assumes a neutral pion condensation of the form \(\Pi_0(x) = \mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{x}\) following Dautry and Neyman (1979). Under the assumption of spatial homogeneity, the energy density is calculated as a function of neutron density, with the magnetization and pion condensation treated as variational parameters, and minimized to find the equilibrium configuration. The ferromagnetic phase energy is then compared to that of the ALS inhomogeneous phase from Tamagaki (1976) and Takatsuka (1977, 1978, 1993), and the effect of axial vector meson condensation is studied using the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key numbers

- The ferromagnetic phase is favored over ordinary neutron matter at densities \(\rho > 5\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0\) is the standard nuclear density.
- The generated magnetic field in the ferromagnetic phase is \(\sim 40\) MeV, corresponding to \(\sim \mathcal{O}(10^{17})\) G.
- The threshold density for complete spin polarization in the toy model is \(\rho_2 = \frac{3^2 \pi^4}{2^4} \frac{1}{m^3 \alpha^6}\).
- The lower critical density for onset of ferromagnetism in the toy model is \(\rho_1 = \frac{\pi^4}{3} \frac{1}{m^2 \alpha^6}\).
- The thermodynamic stability upper bound for the fully polarized ferromagnetic phase is \(\rho_3 = \frac{2^2 \pi^4}{3} \frac{1}{m^3 \alpha^6}\), which is larger than \(\rho_2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Eto, Hatsuda, and Hashimoto (2012) for a ferromagnetic phase in high-density neutron matter using neutral pion domain walls coupled to magnetic fields via the chiral anomaly, generalizing it to a dense system with back-reaction from Fermi energy. It directly compares with the ALS (alternating layer structure) pion condensation phase studied by Tamagaki (1976) and Takatsuka (1977, 1978, 1993), finding the ALS phase energetically favored by several times. The results suggest that realistic nuclear forces, inclusion of protons and electrons, and axial vector meson condensation (studied via AdS/CFT) are necessary next steps to determine whether a QCD ferromagnetic phase can be realized in neutron stars.
