# Renormalization of QCD under longitudinal rescaling

> The form of the quantum Yang-Mills action, under a longitudinal rescaling is determined using a Wilsonian renormalization group. The high-energy limit, is the extreme limit of such a rescaling. We compute the anomalous dimensions and discuss the validity of the high-energy limit. This thesis is an expanded version of joint work with P. Orland, which appeared in arXiv:0901.2955.

## Key claim

Under a longitudinal rescaling of coordinates \(x^{0,3} \rightarrow \lambda x^{0,3}\) with \(\lambda \ll 1\), the quantum Yang-Mills action acquires anomalous powers of \(\lambda\) at one loop, but the perturbative renormalization group breaks down when \(|\ln \lambda| \gg 1\) due to uncontrollable fluctuations of the longitudinal chromomagnetic field, so the result is valid only under the condition \(|\ln \lambda| \ll 1\).

## Method

The author uses a Wilsonian renormalization-group procedure with a sharp momentum cutoff, integrating out high-momentum components of the gauge field from a spherical momentum shell to an ellipsoidal shell (with longitudinal momenta cut off at a smaller scale than transverse momenta), then performing a longitudinal scale transformation to restore an isotropic cutoff. The integration over fast gauge fields and associated ghost fields is carried out to one-loop order using the connected-graph expansion, and counterterms are included to restore gauge invariance broken by the sharp cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal rescaling parameter \(\lambda\) is related to the center-of-mass energy squared \(s\) by \(\lambda \sim s^{-1/2}\).
- The condition for validity of perturbation theory is \(|\ln \lambda| \ll 1\); if \(|\ln \lambda| \gg 1\) the perturbative renormalization group breaks down.
- The longitudinal momenta are cut off at a scale \(2\Lambda/\sqrt{\tilde b}\) where \(\tilde b = \lambda^{-2}\), while transverse momenta are cut off at \(2\Lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the classical longitudinal rescaling approach of Verlinde and Verlinde (1993) to the quantum level, showing that the effective action acquires anomalous dimensions not present in the classical treatment. The results are relevant to the color-glass condensate picture of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and to the BFKL approach of Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, and Lipatov (1975); the author argues that contrary to common belief, the color-glass condensate is actually a strongly-coupled quantum theory because quantum fluctuations of the longitudinal magnetic field become large. The anisotropic renormalization group was discussed earlier by Aref'eva and Volovich (1983) but was not applied to Yang-Mills theories.
