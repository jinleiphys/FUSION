# Evidence for BCS Diquark Condensation in the 3+1d Lattice NJL Model

> We present results of numerical simulations of the 3+1d Nambu - Jona-Lasinio model with a non-zero baryon chemical potential mu, with particular emphasis on the superfluid diquark condensate and associated susceptibilities. The results, when extrapolated to the zero diquark source limit, are consistent with the existence of a non-zero BCS condensate at high baryon density. The nature of the infinite volume and zero temperature limits are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that the 3+1d lattice Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at high baryon chemical potential exhibits a BCS-type diquark condensate, but this conclusion is provisional because it depends on discarding data with diquark source strengths below a threshold of j < 0.3, where the data deviate sharply from the trends observed at higher source strengths.

## Method

The authors simulate the 3+1d NJL model on Euclidean lattices using a hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with staggered fermions, treating the diquark source terms in the partially quenched approximation (sources set to zero during the bosonic field update but non-zero during measurement of diquark observables). They measure the diquark condensate and the susceptibility ratio R = -χ+/χ- as functions of the diquark source strength j for various chemical potentials μ, extrapolating results to the zero-temperature limit via linear fits in L_t^{-1} and to the thermodynamic limit via linear extrapolation in V^{-1}. The lattice parameters are chosen to match low-energy QCD phenomenology in the large-N_c Hartree approximation, yielding a lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1076 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m* = 350 MeV (set to match phenomenology)
- Pion mass m_π = 138 MeV (obtained with bare quark mass am_0 = 0.002)
- Inverse coupling β = a^2/g^2 = 0.565 (derived from the parameter matching)
- Lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1076 MeV, or a ≈ 0.2 fm
- At μ = 1 (in lattice units), the diquark condensate ⟨qq+⟩ a^3 ≈ m* a ≈ 0.2 - 0.3, comparable in magnitude to the vacuum chiral condensate
- The susceptibility ratio at μ = 1 extrapolates to lim_{j→0} R ≈ 0, indicating broken U(1)_B symmetry
- The chiral condensate and onset scale μ_o from lattice data are about 30% smaller than the large-N_c Hartree prediction

## Context

This work extends the lattice analysis of the NJL model from 2+1 dimensions (Hands and Walters, 2001) to 3+1 dimensions, motivated by the possibility of genuine BCS condensation in the higher-dimensional case. The results contrast sharply with the 2+1d NJL model, where the diquark condensate vanishes as a power of j and the susceptibility ratio R is approximately constant, indicating a critical phase with unbroken U(1)_B symmetry at high density. The study also contrasts with simulations of Two Colour QCD (Hands et al., 2000; Kogut et al., 2000), where U(1)_B breaking proceeds via Bose-Einstein condensation without a Fermi surface. The findings enable future work to determine the BCS gap Δ directly from the fermion dispersion relation and to explore whether the chiral transition becomes first order for a constituent quark mass of 400 MeV, as suggested by variational studies (Amore et al., 2002).
