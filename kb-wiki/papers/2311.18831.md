# Schwinger-Dyson equation on the complex plane -- A four-fermion interaction model at finite temperature --

> We extend the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) on the complex plane, which was treated in our previous research, to finite temperature. As a simple example, we solve the SDE for a model with four-fermion interactions in the (1+1) space-time dimensions at strong coupling region. We investigate the properties of the effective mass and energy for the fermions, especially near the phase transition temperature.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) method on the complex energy plane to finite temperature, solving it for a four-fermion interaction model in (1+1) dimensions. They find that below the phase transition temperature, the poles of the effective propagator lie on the real axis even though the effective mass has an imaginary part, which differs from the situation in strongly coupled QED and QCD where poles are not on the real axis.

## Method

The authors formulate the SDE for the effective mass in the imaginary-time formalism, replacing the energy integration with a sum over Matsubara frequencies for fermions. They extend both the effective mass and energy to complex values from the outset, then rewrite the Matsubara sum as contour integrals on the complex energy plane using the residue theorem. The real and imaginary parts of the SDE are solved simultaneously by an iterative method with initial input values of M_R(0) = M_I(0) = 0.01Λ, using a UV cutoff Λ and IR cutoff δ/Λ = 10^{-3}.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition temperature ranges: 0.90 < T_C/Λ < 0.93 for coupling λ = 0.3; 1.86 < T_C/Λ < 1.89 for λ = 0.6; 2.79 < T_C/Λ < 2.82 for λ = 0.9
- The approximate relation between phase transition temperature and coupling constant is T_C/Λ ≃ πλ, yielding T_C/Λ ≃ 0.94, 1.88, 2.83 for λ = 0.3, 0.6, 0.9 respectively
- The infrared cutoff is set to δ/Λ = 10^{-3} and ε = 10^{-5}Λ^2 for all calculations
- For T > T_C, the energy at q=0 takes values E_R(T,0) = √(ε/2) and E_I(T,0) = -√(ε/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study (Tanaka and Sasagawa, 2023) of the SDE on the complex energy plane to finite temperature, using the same four-fermion contact interaction model in (1+1) dimensions. The method differs from other approaches by defining the SDE with complex mass and complex energy from the beginning without assuming a spectral representation, unlike studies using spectral representations (Refs. 28-33 in the paper) or the Padé approximation analysis of lattice data (Ref. 34). The results enable future investigation of the temperature dependence of propagator pole positions on the complex energy plane in gauge theories such as QED and QCD.
