# Triple Charmonium Production in pQCD

> We study the role of $1\rightarrow2$ and $1\rightarrow3$ processes in triple charmonium production. We see that the ratio of effective cross sections of TPS and DPS only moderately depends on charmonium transverse momenta, but the total DPS and TPS cross sections each separately may have rather strong dependence on charmonia transverse momenta in the central kinematics that can be studied experimentally.

## Key claim

The ratio of effective cross sections for triple parton scattering (TPS) and double parton scattering (DPS) depends only moderately on charmonium transverse momenta, but the total DPS and TPS cross sections each separately may have a rather strong dependence on charmonia transverse momenta in the central kinematics region, in contrast to the mean field approximation where these effective cross sections are model independent.

## Method

The authors compute the contributions of \(1\rightarrow 2\) and \(1\rightarrow 3\) parton splitting processes to triple charmonium production in the leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) of pQCD. They decompose the three-parton generalized parton distribution (\({}_3GPD\)) into mean field (\(3\rightarrow 3\)), \(2\rightarrow 3\), and \(1\rightarrow 3\) components, then evaluate the geometric integrals using a Gaussian two-gluon form factor with slope parameter \(B_g\). The perturbative splitting distributions \(\phantom{}_{[1\rightarrow 2]}D\) and \(\phantom{}_{[1\rightarrow 3]}D\) are computed via DGLAP evolution, and the resulting effective cross sections and their ratios are evaluated numerically for LHC kinematics with center-of-mass energy \(s = 1.96 \times 10^8\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

- The mean field ratio \(k = \sigma_{eff;3+3}^{TPS} / \sigma_{eff;2+2}^{DPS} \approx 0.86\), consistent with the value \(\sigma_{eff;TPS}/\sigma_{eff;DPS} \sim 0.85\) from prior mean field calculations.
- The geometric factor for the \(R\) ratio (TPS \(2\rightarrow 3\) contribution) is \(6 \times 12/5 = 14.4\), compared to the DPS \(1\rightarrow 2\) geometric factor of \(2 \times 7/3 \approx 4.6\), meaning the enhancement from parton splitting for TPS is more than 3 times that for DPS.
- The geometric factor for the \(R'\) ratio (TPS \(1\rightarrow 3\) contribution) is \(2 \times 3 \times 12/3 = 24\).
- The charmonium virtuality is taken as \(Q^2 \sim p_t^2 + 4 m_c^2\) with \(m_c = 1.5\ \text{GeV}\); transverse momenta considered are \(\ge 3.5-6\ \text{GeV}\) and less than \(10\ \text{GeV}\).
- The ratio \(\sigma_{eff;TPS}/\sigma_{eff;DPS}\) changes from the no-correlation value of \(k \sim 0.85\) to values of order 1 (up to 15 percent) when all processes are included.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD formalism for multiparton interactions developed by Blok, Strikman, and collaborators (Blok 2011, 2012, 2014) and by Diehl and others (Diehl 2016), which introduced two-particle generalized parton distributions (\({}_2GPD\)s) and the role of \(1\rightarrow 2\) processes in DPS. It directly addresses the recent observation of triple charmonium production by CMS (2023), which could only be explained by significant TPS contributions. Prior theoretical studies of TPS charmonium production (d'Enterria 2017, Shao 2019) used only the mean field approximation with a fixed ratio \(\sigma_{eff;TPS}/\sigma_{eff;DPS} \sim 0.85\) independent of transverse momenta. The present work enables experimental tests of whether the effective cross sections for DPS and TPS indeed depend on charmonium transverse momentum, which would distinguish the pQCD splitting mechanism from the mean field picture.
