# Modeling quasielastic interactions of monoenergetic kaon decay-at-rest neutrinos

> Monoenergetic muon neutrinos at 236 MeV are readily produced in intense medium-energy proton facilities ($\gtrsim$2-3~GeV) when a positive kaon decays at rest (KDAR; $K^+ \rightarrow μ^+ ν_μ$). These neutrinos provide a unique opportunity to both study the neutrino interaction and probe the nucleus with a monoenergetic weak-interaction-only tool. We present cross section calculations for quasielastic scattering of these 236~MeV neutrinos off $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ar, paying special attention to low-energy aspects of the scattering process. Our model takes the description of the nucleus in a mean-field (MF) approach as the starting point, where we solve Hartree-Fock (HF) equations using a Skyrme type nucleon-nucleon interaction. Thereby, we introduce long-range nuclear correlations by means of a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) framework where we solve the CRPA equations using a Green's function method. The model successfully describes ($e,e'$) data on $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ca in the kinematic region that overlaps with the KDAR $ν_μ$ phase space. In addition to these results, we present future prospects for precision KDAR cross section measurements and applications of our calculations in current and future experiments that will utilize these neutrinos.

## Key claim

The authors present cross section calculations for quasielastic scattering of 236 MeV monoenergetic kaon decay-at-rest (KDAR) neutrinos off carbon-12 and argon-40, claiming that their continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) model successfully describes electron scattering data in the overlapping kinematic region, but the comparison to the only existing KDAR neutrino data (from MiniBooNE) is inconclusive due to the coarseness of the measurement, with the CRPA model yielding a Δχ² of 3.20 (χ² probability 0.36) in a shape-only comparison.

## Method

The authors start from a Hartree-Fock (HF) mean-field description of the nuclear ground state using a Skyrme-type nucleon-nucleon interaction (SkE2 force), then introduce long-range nuclear correlations through a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) framework solved via a Green's function method. The outgoing nucleon wavefunctions are determined as solutions of the positive-energy Schrödinger equation using the same nuclear potential as the ground state, naturally including Fermi motion, binding, Pauli blocking, and elastic final state interactions. The residual interaction strength is constrained using a dipole form factor with a cut-off mass of 455 MeV fitted to electron scattering data, and the nuclear responses are folded with a Lorentzian of width 3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The KDAR neutrino energy is exactly 236 MeV, produced via the decay K⁺ → μ⁺ ν_μ
- The energy transfer range extends up to E_ν - m_μ ≈ 120 MeV
- For carbon-12, the Fermi momentum used in the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) comparison is k_F = 228 MeV, with an average binding energy E_s = 25 MeV
- The CRPA model total cross section for carbon-12 is 1.58 × 10⁻³⁹ cm², compared to the MiniBooNE measured value of (2.7 ± 1.2) × 10⁻³⁹ cm²
- The dipole cut-off mass in the residual interaction is 455 MeV, determined from a global fit to (e,e') data
- The Lorentzian folding width for single-particle excitations is 3 MeV
- The lowest single-particle energy in carbon within the CRPA model is approximately 17 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CRPA formalism previously developed by the same group (Ryckebusch 1988, 1989; Jachowicz 1999, 2002, 2004, 2006; Pandey 2014, 2015, 2016; Van Dessel 2018) to the specific case of 236 MeV KDAR neutrinos, which occupy a kinematic region between low-energy nuclear excitations and the genuine quasielastic regime. The calculations are relevant for current experiments including MiniBooNE (which reported the first KDAR neutrino measurement on carbon-12, Aguilar-Arevalo 2018), the COHERENT collaboration, and future experiments such as JSNS2, the Short-Baseline Neutrino program, and DUNE, where precise KDAR cross section measurements could help constrain neutrino interaction models and reduce uncertainties in oscillation analyses. The authors note that RPA calculations by Nieves (2004) have shown that including collective effects is essential for describing inclusive cross sections in the low-Q² region, and that KDAR data can provide constraints in this challenging kinematic regime.
