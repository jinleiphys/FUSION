# Universality of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations

> Short-range correlations between nucleon pairs in different spin-isospin channels are investigated for light nuclei using the Argonne V8' interaction. At distances below 1 fm a universal behavior is found for the deuteron, 3H, 3He and for ground and first excited states in 4He. This behavior in coordinate space is reflected by a universal behavior for the high-momentum components in momentum space. The universality indicates that a pairwise renormalization is possible in order to obtain a universal effective two-body interaction that does not scatter to high momentum states. The exact two-body densities are compared with those obtained using the unitary correlation operator method with simple trial wave functions. The effect of three-body correlations due to the tensor force on the two-body densities is discussed.

## Key claim

Short-range central and tensor correlations between nucleon pairs in light nuclei (deuteron, 3H, 3He, 4He ground and first excited state) exhibit a universal behavior at distances below 1 fm and at relative momenta above about 3 fm⁻¹, independent of the specific many-body state, when the Argonne V8' interaction is used.

## Method

The authors solved the few-body Schrödinger equation exactly for A=2, 3, and 4 nuclei using a correlated Gaussian basis expanded via the stochastic variational method with the Argonne V8' (AV8') potential. They computed one-body densities in coordinate and momentum space and two-body densities in each spin-isospin channel (S=1,T=0; S=0,T=1; S=1,T=1; S=0,T=0) as functions of relative distance and relative momentum, integrating over the center-of-mass coordinate of the pair.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factors C^N_{S,T} for the S=1,T=0 channel: 61.35 fm³ (deuteron), 31.25 fm³ (3H), 31.75 fm³ (3He), 13.23 fm³ (4He ground state), 31.06 fm³ (4He excited 0⁺ state at 20.21 MeV)
- Normalization factors C^N_{S,T} for the S=0,T=1 channel: 49.02 fm³ (3H), 50.76 fm³ (3He), 18.55 fm³ (4He ground state), 51.55 fm³ (4He excited 0⁺ state)
- Number of pairs in 4He ground state: 2.992 in (S=1,T=0), 2.572 in (S=0,T=1), 0.428 in (S=1,T=1), 0.008 in (S=0,T=0)
- Binding energies: deuteron -2.24 MeV, 3H -7.76 MeV, 3He -7.10 MeV, 4He -25.09 MeV, 4He excited state -7.16 MeV
- Matter point radii: deuteron 1.96 fm, 3H 1.75 fm, 3He 1.79 fm, 4He 1.49 fm, 4He excited state 3.94 fm
- Universality in coordinate space holds below about 1 fm; universality in momentum space holds above about 3 fm⁻¹
- High-momentum components in S=1,T=0 channel are larger by a factor of 2-2.5 compared to S=0,T=1 channel

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Suzuki et al. (2009) on internucleon correlation functions and confirms the universality of short-range correlations previously noted by Forest et al. (1996) using Green's function Monte Carlo methods. The universal behavior suggests that a pairwise renormalization can produce a universal effective two-body interaction that does not scatter to high momentum states, enabling the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) to describe short-range correlations with simple trial wave functions. The results also show that three-body correlations induced by the tensor force in the S=1,T=0 channel cause depopulation of the S=0,T=1 channel in favor of the S=1,T=1 channel, an effect that cannot be explained by two-body correlations alone.
