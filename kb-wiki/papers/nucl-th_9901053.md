# Backward hadron production in neutrino-nucleus interactions

> The production of backward pions in lepton-nucleus collisions is analyzed. We show that a large yield of high momentum backward pions can be explained by the Regge asymptotic of the distribution of nucleons carrying a large momentum fraction in the nuclear target. The calculated spectra of pions emitted in the neutrino + Ne --> muon + pion + X reaction are in satisfactory agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The large yield of high-momentum backward pions observed in neutrino-nucleus collisions can be explained by the Regge asymptotic of the nucleon momentum distribution at large momentum fraction, provided the nonperturbative Q^2-dependence of the quark distribution in the nucleus is taken into account; the approach works only in the region of small Q^2 and large W_X where the cylindrical (Pomeron-exchange) graph dominates.

## Method

The authors computed the semi-inclusive spectrum of backward pions produced in neutrino-neon interactions using the impulse approximation, factorizing the nuclear vertex (described by a distribution function f_A(y,Q^2) built from Regge asymptotics) and the elementary neutrino-nucleon scattering amplitude (described by planar and cylindrical Regge graphs). The nuclear distribution function at large y > 1.7 was constructed from the overlap of relativistic phase space for quarks in correlated nucleons, extending beyond the range of nonrelativistic spectral functions. The elementary spectrum was evaluated using the cylindrical (one-Pomeron exchange) graph only, since the planar (Reggeon exchange) contribution is negligible at the large W_X values dominating the integral over x and Q^2.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic spectral function P(k) covers momenta |k| < 0.7 GeV/c and removal energies (m - k_0) < 0.6 GeV, limiting its use to y < 1.7.
- The Reggeon intercept is alpha_R(0) = 0.5; the average baryonic trajectory intercept is tilde_alpha_B(0) = -0.5.
- The supercritical Pomeron intercept satisfies Delta = tilde_alpha_P(0) - 1 = 0.08.
- The parameter s_0 = 1 GeV^2 is used in the Regge energy factor (W_X / s_0).
- The fragmentation function parameter a_0 = 0.65 and lambda = 0.5.
- The calculated spectrum differs significantly from the spectral-function calculation for pion momenta p_pi > 0.4 GeV/c, where nucleon momenta larger than about 0.8 GeV/c dominate.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Benhar, Fantoni, Lykasov, and Slavin (1997) for backward proton production in electron-nucleus scattering to backward pion production in neutrino-nucleus interactions, and compares with data from the WA59 collaboration (1989). The method, originally developed by Efremov, Kaidalov, Kim, Lykasov, and Slavin (1988, 1994) and Lykasov (1993) for hadron-nucleus processes, relates lepton-induced and hadron-induced emission of fast backward hadrons through the same Regge-based nuclear distribution function. The results enable quantitative investigation of target fragmentation into hadrons and suggest that short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations described by overlapping three-quark distributions govern backward hadron production across different probes.
