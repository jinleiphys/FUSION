# EoS for hot neutron stars

> We review the equation of state (EoS) models covering a large range of temperatures, baryon number densities and electron fractions presently available on the \textsc{CompOSE} database. These models are intended to be directly usable within numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, binary neutron star mergers and proto-neutron star evolution. We discuss their compliance with existing constraints from astrophysical observations and nuclear data. For a selection of purely nucleonic models in reasonable agreement with the above constraints, after discussing the properties of cold matter, we review thermal properties for thermodynamic conditions relevant for core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. We find that the latter are strongly influenced by the density dependence of the nucleon effective mass. The selected bunch of models is used to investigate the EoS dependence of hot star properties, where entropy per baryon and electron fraction profiles are inspired from proto-neutron star evolution. The $Γ$-law analytical thermal EoS used in many simulations is found not to describe well these thermal properties of the EoS. However, it may offer a fair description of the structure of hot stars whenever thermal effects on the baryonic part are small, as shown here for proto-neutron stars starting from several seconds after bounce.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the finite-temperature equation of state (EoS) models available on the CompOSE database for use in simulations of core-collapse supernovae, binary neutron star mergers, and proto-neutron star evolution, finding that the Γ-law analytical thermal EoS commonly used in simulations does not accurately describe thermal properties of the EoS, though it may provide a fair description of hot star structure when thermal effects on the baryonic part are small, such as for proto-neutron stars starting from several seconds after bounce.

## Method

The authors surveyed all general-purpose EoS tables on the CompOSE database (as of August 2021), categorizing them by treatment of inhomogeneous matter (single-nucleus approximation vs. nuclear statistical equilibrium), effective interaction type (Skyrme, covariant density-functional, chiral mean-field, or ab initio-based), and particle content (nucleons only, or including hyperons, pions, or quarks). For a selected subset of purely nucleonic models in reasonable agreement with astrophysical and nuclear constraints, they computed cold matter properties and then analyzed thermal properties for thermodynamic conditions relevant to core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, focusing on the density dependence of the nucleon effective mass. They also used the selected models to investigate the EoS dependence of hot star properties with entropy per baryon and electron fraction profiles inspired from proto-neutron star evolution.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational masses of cold β-equilibrated neutron stars range from 1.91 M⊙ (GROM(LS220L) with Λ-hyperons) to 2.16 M⊙ (SRO(APR) with pion condensate)
- Radius of canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star ranges from 11.3 km (SRO(APR)) to 12.7 km (LS(LS220) and SRO(LS220))
- Radius of a 2.072 M⊙ neutron star ranges from 10.9 km (SRO(APR)) to 13.69 km (upper bound from NICER for PSR J0740+6620)
- Combined tidal deformability \(\tilde \Lambda\) for GW170817 ranges from 272 (SRO(APR), q=1) to 664 (LS(LS220), q=0.73)
- The EoS tables cover baryon number densities from \(10^{-15}\) to 1–10 fm\(^{-3}\), temperatures from 0.1 to 100 MeV, and electron fractions from 0.01 to 0.6
- The transition from inhomogeneous to homogeneous matter in the Gulminelli and Raduta model occurs at \(n_t = 10^{-1}\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of cold neutron star EoS (e.g., Fortin 2016, Margueron 2018, Li 2019) to finite-temperature conditions relevant for dynamic astrophysical environments. It builds on theoretical developments by Constantinou (2014, 2015) and numerical simulations by Schneider (2019, 2020), Yasin (2020), Raithel (2021), and Andersen (2021) that demonstrated the sensitivity of hot matter properties to the nucleon effective mass. The paper enables future multimessenger analyses of binary neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernovae by providing a comprehensive comparison of available EoS models and their compliance with constraints from pulsar mass measurements (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, MSP J0740+6620), NICER radius measurements (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620), and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint.
