# Measurement of the Transverse-Longitudinal Cross Sections in the p (e,e'p)pi0 Reaction in the Delta Region

> Accurate measurements of the p(e,e?p)pi0 reaction were performed at Q^2=0.127(GeV/c)^2 in the Delta resonance energy region. The experiments at the MIT-Bates Linear Accelerator used an 820 MeV polarized electron beam with the out of plane magnetic spectrometer system (OOPS). In this paper we report the first simultaneous determination of both the TL and TL? (``fifth" or polarized) cross sections at low Q^{2} where the pion cloud contribution dominates the quadrupole amplitudes (E2 and C2). The real and imaginary parts of the transverse-longitudinal cross section provide both a sensitive determination of the Coulomb quadrupole amplitude and a test of reaction calculations. Comparisons with model calculations are presented. The empirical MAID calculation gives the best overall agreement with this accurate data. The parameters of this model for the values of the resonant multipoles are |M_{1+}(I=3/2)|= (40.9 \pm 0.3)10^{-3}/m_pi, CMR= C2/M1= -6.5 \pm 0.3%, EMR=E2/M1=-2.2 \pm 0.9%, where the errors are due to the experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first simultaneous determination of both the real (σ_TL) and imaginary (σ_TL') transverse-longitudinal interference cross sections for the p(e,e'p)π⁰ reaction at low Q², where the pion cloud contribution is predicted to dominate the quadrupole amplitudes. The empirical MAID calculation gives the best overall agreement with the data, but the authors note that only one model is in reasonable agreement, so the previous approach of estimating model errors from differences between models can no longer be used.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the MIT-Bates Linear Accelerator using an 820 MeV polarized electron beam with 37% polarization and 6 μA average current, incident on a liquid hydrogen target. The scattered electrons were detected in the One Hundred Inch Proton Spectrometer (OHIPS) and coincident protons in two out-of-plane magnetic spectrometers (OOPS) deployed at fixed laboratory angles relative to the momentum transfer q, with out-of-plane angles φ = 225° and 315°. Cross sections were extracted from phase-space matched regions in four dimensions (W, Q², θ, φ) using Monte Carlo simulations for normalization and radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- Q² = 0.127 (GeV/c)² for all measurements, in the Δ resonance energy region
- Measurements performed at center-of-mass energies W = 1232 MeV and W = 1170 MeV
- Resonant multipole parameters extracted from MAID fit: |M₁₊(I=3/2)| = (40.9 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³/m_π
- CMR = C2/M1 = -6.5 ± 0.3%
- EMR = E2/M1 = -2.2 ± 0.9%
- Electron beam energy: 820 MeV with 0.85% duty factor

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the p(e,e'p)π⁰ reaction by the same collaboration (Mertz, 2001) and provides the first simultaneous determination of both TL and TL' cross sections at low Q² where the pion cloud contribution is predicted to dominate. The results test dynamical models including Sato-Lee (Sato and Lee, 2001), DMT (Kamalov and Yang, 1999), and dispersion relation calculations (Aznauryan, 1999), finding that only the empirical MAID model agrees with the data, while the more fundamental dynamical models show significant disagreements. This enables future model-independent multipole analyses when combined with new double-polarization experiments at the same kinematics (Sparveris, 2003; van Buuren, 2003).
