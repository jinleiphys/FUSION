# Analytical solutions for the Bohr Hamiltonian with the Woods-Saxon potential

> Approximate analytical solutions in closed form are obtained for the 5-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian with the Woods-Saxon potential, taking advantage of the Pekeris approximation and the exactly soluble one-dimensional extended Woods-Saxon potential with a dip near its surface. Comparison to the data for several gamma-unstable and prolate deformed nuclei indicates that the potential can describe well the ground state and gamma-1 bands of many prolate deformed nuclei corresponding to large enough "well size" and diffuseness, while it fails in describing the beta-1 bands, due to its lack of a hard core, as well as in describing gamma-unstable nuclei, because of the small "well size" and diffuseness they exhibit.

## Key claim

The Woods-Saxon potential, when used in the 5-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian, can describe well the ground state and gamma-1 bands of many prolate deformed nuclei with sufficiently large "well size" and diffuseness, but fails for beta-1 bands due to its lack of a hard core and for gamma-unstable nuclei because of their small "well size" and diffuseness.

## Method

The authors applied the Pekeris approximation to rewrite the centrifugal barrier term in the Woods-Saxon potential using exponentials, allowing them to map the problem onto the exactly soluble one-dimensional modified Woods-Saxon potential with a surface dip. They then solved the Bohr Hamiltonian for both gamma-unstable nuclei (using O(5) symmetry) and prolate deformed nuclei (using a gamma-dependent potential and separation of variables), fitting the resulting energy expressions to experimental spectra of 63 nuclei with R4/2 > 2.9.

## Key numbers

- For prolate deformed nuclei, good fits were obtained only when the "well size" parameter satisfied β̃₀ ≥ 1.9 and the diffuseness parameter satisfied ã₀ ≥ 0.44; below these thresholds (β̃₀ ≤ 1.8 and/or ã₀ ≤ 0.43) the Pekeris approximation breaks down
- The depth parameter V₀ showed a clear minimum near the middle of the neutron shell for Yb isotopes, with maximum deformation at ¹⁷⁴Yb₁₀₄
- The condition for allowed quantum numbers in the gamma-unstable case restricts n ≤ ½(-1 + √(1 + 192(τ+1)(τ+2)ã⁴/β₀⁴)), which for real nuclei only permits n=0
- For prolate deformed nuclei, the analogous condition n ≤ ½(-1 + √(1 + 192(L(L+1)/3 + λ + 2)ã⁴/β₀⁴)) similarly restricts solutions to n=0 only

## Context

This work extends the family of analytical Bohr Hamiltonian solutions beyond the Davidson (Dav), Kratzer (Kratzer), and Morse (Morse) potentials, which all possess hard cores, to the Woods-Saxon potential which lacks one. The authors compare their results to the critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello, 2000) and X(5) (Iachello, 2001), as well as to prior numerical calculations using the Rotation Vibration Model (Faessler, Sheline) and the Algebraic Collective Model (Rowe, Caprio). The failure to describe beta-1 bands and gamma-unstable nuclei identifies limitations of the Woods-Saxon potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian context, suggesting that potentials with hard cores may be necessary for those nuclear features.
