# Correlation studies of the $^{7}$He excited states

> The unbound nucleus $^{7}$He was recently studied in the $^2$H($^{6}$He,$^1$H)$^{7}$He reaction at 29 $A\,$MeV beam energy in Ref.[M.S. Golovkov et al., Phys. Rev. C 109, L061602 (2024)]. The excitation spectrum of $^{7}$He was measured up to $E_T=8$ MeV ($E_T$ is energy above the $^{6}$He-$n$ threshold). Angular distribution for the $^{6}$He-$n$ decay of the $^{7}$He $3/2^-$ ground state can be explained by a strong spin alignment induced by a reaction mechanism. The correlation information for the higher-lying $^{7}$He excitations is available as backward-forward asymmetry for the $^{6}$He-$n$ decay in the $^{7}$He frame. The asymmetry function has an expressed energy profile which may be explained by using quite restrictive assumptions about structure of $^{7}$He excitations or/and peculiarities of the reaction mechanism. In the analysis of [M.S. Golovkov et al., Phys. Rev. C 109, L061602 (2024)] the observation the $s_{1/2}$ state in $^{7}$He is declared with $E_r \approx 2.0$ MeV. Our work is based on the same $^{7}$He data. However, the data analysis was improved and also the data interpretation is substantiated with the detailed PWBA reaction studies and coupled-channel calculations of the $^{7}$He continuous spectrum. The idea of the $s_{1/2}$ resonant state with $E_r \approx 2.0$ is rejected. In addition, the position of the $1/2^-$ state in $^{7}$He is confined to the interval $E_r=2.2-3.0$ MeV, with ``preferred'' value 2.6 MeV. There is indication on the second $3/2^-$ state in the data with $E_r \sim 4.5$ MeV and with the lower resonance energy limit $E_r \gtrsim 3.5$ MeV. Importance and prospects of more detailed correlation studies of $^{7}$He continuum are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors reject the previously claimed \(s_{1/2}\) resonant state in \(^{7}\)He at \(E_r \approx 2.0\) MeV from Golovkov (2024), arguing that the on-shell R-matrix parameterization used in that work is not applicable for reaction studies where the off-shell T-matrix should be applied; instead, they confine the \(1/2^-\) state to \(E_r = 2.2-3.0\) MeV and find indication of a second \(3/2^-\) state at \(E_r \sim 4.5\) MeV.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed data from the \(^{2}\)H(\(^{6}\)He,\(^{1}\)H)\(^{7}\)He reaction at 29 \(A\,\)MeV beam energy using improved analysis procedures, including a "combined mass method" that uses neutron coincidence data for complete kinematics reconstruction with about four times better energy resolution than the missing mass method. They substantiated their interpretation with detailed plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) reaction studies and coupled-channel calculations of the \(^{7}\)He continuous spectrum, examining the backward-forward asymmetry of the \(^{6}\)He-\(n\) decay in the \(^{7}\)He frame.

## Key numbers

- Ground state resonance energy \(E_r = 0.41(2)\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.14(5)\) MeV from combined mass spectrum analysis
- The \(1/2^-\) state position confined to the interval \(E_r = 2.2-3.0\) MeV, with "preferred" value 2.6 MeV
- Indication of a second \(3/2^-\) state at \(E_r \sim 4.5\) MeV with lower resonance energy limit \(E_r \gtrsim 3.5\) MeV
- Missing mass energy resolution of \(\sim 600\) keV FWHM for \(0 < E_T < 2\) MeV and 700 keV FWHM for \(6 < E_T < 8\) MeV
- Combined mass method provides \(\sim 150\) keV FWHM energy resolution for the \(^{7}\)He ground state
- 60-75% of the total cross section with \(E_T \lesssim 6\) MeV is connected with population of excited states

## Context

This work extends the study of \(^{7}\)He by Golovkov (2024) and Bezbakh (2024) using the same experimental data but with improved analysis and more extensive theoretical modeling. The results contradict the claim of a low-lying \(s_{1/2}\) state in \(^{7}\)He from Golovkov (2024) and address the long-standing disagreement in the field, which Fortune (2018) split into two "camps": those supporting a \(1/2^-\) state below 2 MeV (Markenroth 2001, Meister 2002, Skaza 2006) and those favoring \(E_T \gtrsim 2\) MeV (Wuosmaa 2005, Baroni 2013, Pieper 2002). The correlation methods developed here enable more detailed studies of the \(^{7}\)He continuum and could be applied to improve precision of ground state width measurements to \(\Delta\Gamma \sim 10-15\) keV in future experiments.
