# Lattice study of correlators for quarkonium decay

> While there has been a lot of progress in developing a formalism for the study of quarkonia in QGP, a nonperturbative study is still difficult. For bottomonia, where the system size is much less than the inverse temperature, the interaction of the system with the medium can be approximated by a dipole interaction with the color electric field. The decay of the quarkonia can be connected to a correlation function of the color electric field. We present preliminary results from a lattice study of the relevant color electric field correlator. The structure of the correlator, and its difference from the corresponding correlator studied for heavy quark diffusion, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary lattice results for the color electric field correlator relevant to bottomonium decay in a quark-gluon plasma, showing that the nonperturbative correlator is not symmetric about $\tau T = 1/2$, unlike the correlator used for heavy quark diffusion, and that at very high temperatures ($10^4 T_c$) the lattice result agrees well with next-to-leading order perturbative calculations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of the Euclidean color electric field correlator $G_{EE}(\tau)$ in a gluonic plasma at various temperatures, using the potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) formalism where bottomonium decay is described by a singlet-to-octet transition. The correlator was renormalized by multiplying with an exponential factor involving the adjoint Polyakov loop to remove the mass divergence from the adjoint Wilson line. Continuum extrapolations were performed using results at different lattice spacings $a = 1/N_\tau T$, and the results were compared to the correlator $G_F^E(\tau)$ used for heavy quark diffusion studies.

## Key numbers

- At $1.5 T_c$, the renormalized correlator $G_r^E(\tau;a)$ shows mild cutoff effects in the range $\tau T \in [0.3, 0.8]$, with continuum extrapolation performed.
- At $10^4 T_c$, the nonperturbative lattice result agrees very well with the NLO perturbative result from Ref. [tumpert].
- The octet-octet correlator $G_{oct}^E(\tau)$ obeys the NLO scaling relation $\frac{G_{oct}^E(\tau;T)}{G_F^E(\tau;T)} \approx 2 \frac{N_c^2-4}{N_c^2-1}$ within errors.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative lattice study of color electric field correlators for quarkonium decay in a quark-gluon plasma, building on the pNRQCD formalism developed by Brambilla and collaborators and the open quantum system approach of Akamatsu. The correlator studied differs from the one used for heavy quark diffusion by Larsen et al. (CLM) and by the HotQCD collaboration, as it uses adjoint rather than fundamental Wilson lines, leading to different symmetry properties. The results enable future extraction of the transport coefficient $\kappa$ from the $\omega \to 0$ limit of the real-time correlator, which is needed for Markovian descriptions of bottomonium suppression in heavy ion collisions.
