# Exact two-particle eigenstates in partially reduced QED

> We consider a reformulation of QED in which covariant Green functions are used to solve for the electromagnetic field in terms of the fermion fields. It is shown that exact few-fermion eigenstates of the resulting Hamiltonian can be obtained in the canonical equal-time formalism for the case where there are no free photons. These eigenstates lead to two- and three-body Dirac-like equations with electromagnetic interactions. Perturbative and some numerical solutions of the two-body equations are presented for positronium and muonium-like systems, for various strengths of the coupling.

## Key claim

The authors show that exact few-fermion eigenstates of a partially reduced QED Hamiltonian can be obtained in the canonical equal-time formalism, but only when the terms involving free (physical) photons are omitted and an unconventional "empty" vacuum state is used; the resulting two-body equation is the Breit equation in the Coulomb gauge or the Eddington-Gaunt equation in the Lorentz gauge.

## Method

The authors reformulate QED by using covariant Green functions to solve for the electromagnetic field in terms of the fermion fields, then truncate the Hamiltonian to exclude states with free photons. They work in the Schrodinger picture with equal-time quantization, impose an unconventional empty vacuum annihilated by the full fermion field operators, and construct exact one-, two-, and three-fermion eigenstates of the normal-ordered Hamiltonian. The two-body equation is reduced to radial form for states of given total angular momentum and parity, yielding sets of coupled first-order differential equations that are solved perturbatively and numerically.

## Key numbers

- For positronium and muonium-like systems, perturbative O(alpha^4) corrections to the Rydberg spectrum of J=0 states are obtained (section 8).
- The Coulomb-gauge Green function components are G_00(r) = 1/(4 pi r), G_0i = 0, and G_ij(r) = -1/(8 pi r) (delta_ij + x_i x_j / r^2) (equation 4.4).
- The Lorentz-gauge Green function is G(r) = 1/(4 pi r) (equation 5.6), with the first retardation correction given by G_1(r) = r/(4 pi) (equation 5.7).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier approach to scalar Yukawa (Wick-Cutkosky) theory (Darewych 1998, Darewych and Byl 1998) and the Coulomb-QED model (Darewych and Duviryak 1996) to full QED with both Coulomb and transverse photon exchange. The resulting two-body equations are the Breit equation (Breit 1929) in the Coulomb gauge and the Eddington-Gaunt equation (Eddington 1929, Gaunt 1929) in the Lorentz gauge; the paper shows how the Breit equation can be recovered in the Lorentz gauge when lowest-order retardation effects are included. The formalism enables systematic perturbative and numerical studies of two-fermion bound states such as positronium and muonium, and provides a framework for extending to three-body systems.
