# On Double-Beta Decay Half-Life Time Systematics

> Recommended 2 $β$ (2 $ν$) half-life values and their systematics were analyzed in the frame- work of a simple empirical approach. T$^{2 ν}$$_{1/2}$ ~ 1/E$^{8}$ trend has been observed for $^{128,130}$Te recommended values. This trend was used to predict T$^{2 ν}$$_{1/2}$ for all isotopes of interest. Current results were compared with other theoretical and experimental works.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two-neutrino double-beta decay half-lives for 0⁺→0⁺ transitions follow an empirical trend of T^(2ν)_(1/2) ~ 1/E⁸, but this trend is qualified as being derived primarily from the specific case of ¹²⁸,¹³⁰Te and then extended to other isotopes with additional dependence on Coulomb energy and nuclear deformation.

## Method

The author analyzed NNDC-recommended 2β(2ν) half-life values for 0⁺→0⁺ transitions, focusing first on the tellurium isotopes ¹²⁸Te and ¹³⁰Te to extract an energy-dependent scaling law. This trend was then combined with the dimensionless Coulomb energy parameter ξ ≈ ZA⁻¹/³ and nuclear deformation parameters β₂ from Raman et al. (2001) to construct an empirical formula. The resulting rule was fitted to experimental data and used to predict half-lives for all isotopes of interest, with comparisons to recommended and experimental values.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²⁸,¹³⁰Te, the half-life ratio T^(2ν)_(1/2)(¹²⁸Te) / T^(2ν)_(1/2)(¹³⁰Te) ≈ 5.7 × 10³ ~ (E₁₃₀/E₁₂₈)^(8.1), establishing the ~1/E⁸ trend.
- The empirical rule is T^(2ν)_(1/2)(0⁺→0⁺) ≈ (1±0.5)×10²⁴ / (E⁸ ξ² β₂²), where T_(1/2) is in years and E in MeV.
- Recommended half-life for ¹²⁸Te (combined 0ν+2ν): (3.5±2.0)×10²⁴ y; for ¹³⁰Te (2ν only): (6.1±4.8)×10²⁰ y.
- Recommended half-life for ¹⁰⁰Mo (2ν, ground state): (7.0±0.4)×10¹⁸ y; for ¹⁵⁰Nd (2ν, ground state): (7.9±0.7)×10¹⁸ y.
- Predicted half-life for ¹³⁶Xe: (4.50±2.25)×10²⁰ y, which disagrees with the experimental lower limit of ≥8.5×10²¹ y; using an alternative β₂ from Edvardson and Norlin (1975) gives ≈ (9.0±4.5)×10²⁰ y.

## Context

This work extends the Grodzins (1962) approach to double-beta decay systematics and agrees with the theoretical phase-space calculation of Primakoff and Rosen (1969), who predicted an energy dependence between the eighth and eleventh power. The empirical rule enables predictions for all 2β(2ν)-decay candidates, highlighting the strong sensitivity to nuclear deformation parameters from Raman et al. (2001) and identifying discrepancies for ¹³⁶Xe, ²³⁸U, ⁹⁶Zr, ¹⁰⁰Mo, and ¹¹⁶Cd that may indicate either model limitations or the need for improved deformation measurements. The analysis also suggests a high probability for 2β(2ν)-decay in the strongly deformed nucleus ¹⁶⁰Gd, motivating future charge-exchange reaction measurements.
