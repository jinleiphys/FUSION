# Covariant separable interaction for the neutron-proton system in $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial-wave state

> Within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach a rank-six separable neutron-proton interaction kernel for the triplet coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial-wave state is constructed. Two different methods of a relativistic generalization of initially nonrelativistic form factors parametrizing the kernel are considered. The model parameters are determined by fitting the elastic $^3S_1$ and $^3D_1$ phase shifts and the triplet scattering length as well as the asymptotic $D/S$ ratio of the deuteron wave functions and the deuteron binding energy. The $D$-state probability constraints 4-7\% are taken into account. The deuteron magnetic moment is calculated. The half-off-shell properties are further demonstrated by the Noyes-Kowalski functions. The first test of the constructed kernel is performed by calculating the deuteron electrodisintegration at three different kinematic conditions.

## Key claim

Within a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, a rank-six separable neutron-proton interaction kernel for the triplet coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial-wave state is constructed, but the mixing parameter $\varepsilon_1$ is not well described and is excluded from the fit, as the authors found that simultaneously reproducing phase shifts, low-energy parameters, and the mixing parameter is not possible with a rank-six kernel.

## Method

The authors constructed a rank-six separable interaction kernel for the Bethe-Salpeter equation describing the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial-wave state of the neutron-proton system. Two relativistic generalizations of nonrelativistic Yamaguchi-type form factors were considered, denoted MY6 (using $\bp^2 \to -p^2$) and MYQ6 (using $\bp^2 \to Q^2$). Model parameters were determined by fitting elastic $^3S_1$ and $^3D_1$ phase shifts from the SAID program, the triplet scattering length, the asymptotic $D/S$ ratio of the deuteron wave function, and the deuteron binding energy, while the $D$-state probability was constrained to 4-7% and the deuteron magnetic moment was calculated without being included in the fit.

## Key numbers

- Triplet scattering length: MY6 gives 5.42 fm, MYQ6 gives 5.42 fm, experimental value is 5.424(4) fm
- Effective range: MY6 gives 1.800 fm, MYQ6 gives 1.768 fm, experimental value is 1.759(5) fm
- Deuteron binding energy: both MY6 and MYQ6 give 2.2246 MeV, experimental value is 2.224644(46) MeV
- $D$-state probability: MY6 gives 4.92%, MYQ6 gives 4.65%, experimental constraint is 4-7%
- Asymptotic $D/S$ ratio: MY6 gives 0.0255, MYQ6 gives 0.0259, experimental value is 0.0256(4)
- Deuteron magnetic moment: MY6 gives 0.8500 $e/2m$, MYQ6 gives 0.8535 $e/2m$, experimental value is 0.8574 $e/2m$
- The models describe phase shifts for laboratory energies up to 3 GeV, whereas the nonrelativistic Graz II model works only for $T_{\rm Lab} \leq 0.4$ GeV and CD-Bonn was constructed for $T_{\rm Lab} \leq 350$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous separable kernel construction for uncoupled partial-wave states (Bondarenko 2008) to the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channel, and improves upon the relativistic Graz II model (Rupp 1989) which had nonintegrability problems at higher energies. The constructed kernel enables calculations of deuteron reactions such as electrodisintegration without resorting to quasipotential or nonrelativistic approximations, and the authors plan to include final state interactions in future work. The model is compared with the Paris potential (Lacombe 1980), CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt 2000), and the empirical SP07 SAID solution (Arndt 2007).
