# Baryon Resonance Dynamics in pi N \to N V Reactions Near Threshold

> The rho - omega interference in the exclusive reaction pi N \to N' e+ e- is studied within a schematic model including the established baryon resonances up to 1720 MeV. Near threshold the interference can be used to separate the isoscalar part of the electromagnetic current. The role of various baryon resonances is highlighted.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\rho-\omega$ interference pattern in the exclusive reactions $\pi^- p \to n e^+ e^-$ and $\pi^+ n \to p e^+ e^-$ near threshold can be used to isolate the isoscalar part of the electromagnetic current, but this sensitivity is conditional on the specific energy region below $\sqrt{s} = 1.72$ GeV where the destructive interference between $\rho$ and $\omega$ contributions makes the two reactions' cross sections differ significantly.

## Method

The authors employ a schematic Born-term model with only $s$- and $u$-channel diagrams, including established baryon resonances up to 1720 MeV in both isoscalar and isovector channels. The isoscalar channel includes the nucleon and eight resonances ($P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{15}(1675)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $P_{13}(1720)$), while the isovector channel adds four $\Delta$ states ($P_{33}(1232)$, $P_{33}(1600)$, $S_{31}(1620)$, $D_{33}(1700)$). The subprocess $\pi N \to N' V$ with $V = \rho, \omega$ and subsequent $V \to e^+ e^-$ decay is calculated, with interaction Lagrangians and parameters taken from Riska and Brown (no year given).

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for the change in interference behavior is $\sqrt{s} = 1.72$ GeV; below this energy the differential cross sections of $\pi^- p \to n e^+ e^-$ and $\pi^+ n \to p e^+ e^-$ are quite different, while above threshold they become similar.
- Differential cross sections are presented at a fixed polar angle of $\theta = 30^\circ$ in the $\pi N$ center-of-mass system.
- The energy dependence of differential cross sections is shown at a fixed invariant mass of $M_{e^+ e^-} = 0.6$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approaches of Friman (no year given) and Penner and Mosel (no year given) by focusing on the narrow energy region near threshold for the specific reaction $\pi N \to N' e^+ e^-$, using a simpler schematic model. The results are directly relevant to the HADES spectrometer at GSI, which can measure these elementary reactions with pion beams on hydrogen or deuterium targets. The paper also connects to studies of the OZI rule in $\omega$ and $\phi$ production, where the authors previously found (Kampfer, Titov, and Reznik, no year given) that baryon resonance contributions can explain deviations from the OZI rule without invoking strangeness content in the nucleon.
