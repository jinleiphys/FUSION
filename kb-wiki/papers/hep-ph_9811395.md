# CP Violation in the Semileptonic $B_{l4}$ (B->D πl ν) Decays: A Model Independent Analysis

> CP violation from physics beyond the Standard Model is investigated in $B_{l4}$ decays: $B\to Dπl\barν_l$. The semileptonic $B$-meson decay to a $D$-meson with an emission of single pion is analyzed with heavy quark effective theory and chiral perturbation theory. In the decay process, we include various excited states as intermediate states decaying to the final hadrons, $D+π$. The CP violation is implemented in a model independent way, in which we extend leptonic current by including complex couplings of the scalar sector and those of the vector sector in extensions of the Standard Model. With these complex couplings, we calculate the CP-odd rate asymmetry and the optimal asymmetry. We find that the optimal asymmetry is sizable and can be detected at $1σ$ level with about $10^6$-$10^7$ $B$-meson pairs, for some reference values of new physics effects.

## Key claim

CP violation from physics beyond the Standard Model in $B_{l4}$ decays ($B \to D\pi l\bar{\nu}_l$) can be sizable enough to be detected at $1\sigma$ level with about $10^6$-$10^7$ $B$-meson pairs, but only for some reference values of new physics effects (specifically, for $|\Im(\zeta)| = 0.5$ or $2.0$) and only when using the optimal asymmetry rather than the simple rate asymmetry.

## Method

The authors analyzed $B_{l4}$ decays using heavy quark effective theory (HQET) and chiral perturbation theory (chPT) in the soft-pion limit, including intermediate excited states ($D^*$, $D_0$, $D_1$, and the first radially excited $D'^*$) that decay to $D+\pi$. They extended the leptonic current by introducing complex couplings for both scalar and vector sectors beyond the Standard Model, parametrized by complex parameters $\chi$ and $\eta$ (or $\zeta = \eta/(1+\chi)$), and calculated the CP-odd rate asymmetry and the optimal asymmetry using a weight function $w_{\rm opt} = \mathcal{D}/\mathcal{S}$.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction for $B^- \to D^+\pi^-\tau\bar{\nu}_\tau$: $0.045\%$ (using $\tau_{B^\pm} = 1.65 \times 10^{-12}$ s)
- Branching fraction for $B^- \to D^0\pi^0\tau\bar{\nu}_\tau$: $1.37\%$
- Decay rate for $B^- \to D^+\pi^-\tau\bar{\nu}_\tau$: $1.79 \times 10^{-16}$ GeV
- Decay rate for $B^- \to D^0\pi^0\tau\bar{\nu}_\tau$: $5.56 \times 10^{-15}$ GeV
- Soft-pion limit restriction: $s_M \le 6.5$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to pion momentum less than about $0.6$ GeV
- Number of $B$-meson pairs needed for $1\sigma$ detection with optimal asymmetry: $10^6$-$10^7$ for $|\Im(\zeta)| = 0.5$ and $2.0$
- Coupling constants used: $g = 0.5$, $\alpha_1 = -1.43$, $\alpha_2 = -0.14$, $\alpha_3 = 0.69$
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 93$ MeV
- Mass differences for resonances: $\delta m_{M_0} = 0.39$ GeV, $\delta m_{M_1} = 0.71$ GeV, $\delta m_{M^{\prime *}} = 0.56$ GeV
- Widths for resonances: $\Gamma_{M_0} = 1040$ MeV, $\Gamma_{M_1} = 405$ MeV, $\Gamma_{M^{\prime *}} = 191$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $B_{l4}$ decays by Goity and Roberts (1997), who studied $B \to D\pi l\nu$ including higher excited intermediate states, by incorporating CP violation from beyond-Standard-Model physics in a model-independent way. The authors also consider tau leptons in the final state, unlike previous studies that considered only light leptons ($e$ and $\mu$), because CP-violating effects from scalar exchange are proportional to lepton mass. This analysis enables future searches for new physics at $B$-meson factories by providing predictions for both the rate asymmetry and the more powerful optimal asymmetry, with specific estimates of the number of $B$-meson pairs needed for detection.
