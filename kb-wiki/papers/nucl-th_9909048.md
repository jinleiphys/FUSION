# On the search for a narrow penta-quark Z$^+$ baryon in NN interactions

> The possibility for an observation of a narrow penta-quark $Z^+$ baryon in $NN$ reactions is discussed. It is shown that the $pp{\to}nΣ^+K^+$ reaction at excess energies around 100 MeV above threshold provides optimal conditions for $Z^+$ baryon detection by an analysis of the $nK^+$ invariant mass spectrum, if the $Z^+$ mass is located around 1.5 GeV involving a rather narrow width.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $pp{\to}n\Sigma^+K^+$ reaction at excess energies around 100 MeV above threshold provides optimal conditions for detecting a narrow penta-quark $Z^+$ baryon, but only if the $Z^+$ mass is located around 1.5 GeV with a width of about 5 MeV; the signal becomes almost invisible at higher excess energies or if the resonance has a wider width or higher mass.

## Method

The authors calculated the $pp{\to}n\Sigma^+K^+$ reaction cross section including contributions from the $Z^+$ baryon via $K$-meson exchange, using a Breit-Wigner form for the $Z^+$ resonance and a $N{\Sigma}K$ coupling constant of $g_{N{\Sigma}K}=3.86$ from SU(3). They compared the $Z^+$ signal to the background from the intermediate $\Delta^{++}(1920)$ resonance and final state interactions, analyzing the $nK^+$ invariant mass spectra at excess energies of 100 MeV and 200 MeV, both with and without kinematic cuts on the $\Sigma^+K^+$ and $n\Sigma^+$ invariant masses.

## Key numbers

- The $Z^+$ baryon mass predicted by the chiral quark-soliton model is around 1.5 GeV with a width $\Gamma_Z \leq 15$ MeV, with the most favorable width about 5 MeV.
- The $pp{\to}n\Sigma^+K^+$ total cross section at $\epsilon=100$ MeV is 2 $\mu$b, with the $Z^+$ contribution around 80 nb; at $\epsilon=200$ MeV the total cross section is 12.7 $\mu$b with the $Z^+$ contribution around 120 nb.
- The threshold for $Z^+$ production at its pole for $M_Z=1.5$ GeV is $\epsilon \simeq 66$ MeV.
- The $N{\Sigma}K$ coupling constant used is $g_{N{\Sigma}K}=3.86$, with the range from data analysis being $3.5 < g_{N{\Sigma}K} < 6.4$.
- The $Z^+$ signal might be detected with an experimental statistics of about 200 events at $\epsilon \simeq 100$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for strangeness $S=+1$ baryons beyond the mass range of 1.74 to 2.16 GeV previously investigated in $KN$ elastic and inelastic scattering, as summarized by the PDG. The paper specifically addresses the predictions from the chiral quark-soliton model (Maxim, 1999) suggesting a low-mass $Z^+$ around 1.5 GeV with narrow width, in contrast to the MIT bag model prediction of 1.7 GeV and the Skyrme model result of 1.7 GeV (Walliser, 1999). The study enables experimental searches at the COoler SYchlotron (COSY) in Jülich, proposing that the $pp{\to}n\Sigma^+K^+$ reaction with appropriate kinematic cuts can separate the $Z^+$ signal from background processes dominated by the $\Delta^{++}(1920)$ resonance and final state interactions.
