# Charmonium Production in gamma-A, p-A and A-A

> The data for the production of the $ψ'$ meson in pA collisions at 450 GeV at CERN-SPS (of the NA50-collaboration) yields $σ(ψ' N)\approx 8$ mb under the assumption that the $ψ'$ is produced as a result of the space-time evolution of a point-like $c\bar c$ pair which expands with time to the full size of the charmonium state. However, much higher values of $σ(ψ' N)$ are not ruled out by the data. We show that recent CERN data confirm the suggestion that color fluctuations are the major source of suppression of the $J/ψ$ yield as observed at CERN in both pA and AA collisions.

# Charmonium Production in gamma-A, p-A and A-A

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the NA50 pA data at 450 GeV can be fitted with a psi'-nucleon cross section of approximately 8 mb under the assumption of point-like c-cbar production with time-dependent expansion, but a much larger cross section of 20 mb is not ruled out by the data. The author argues that color fluctuations, rather than other mechanisms, are the dominant source of J/psi suppression observed at CERN in both pA and AA collisions.

## Method
The author applies a semiclassical Glauber approximation with a time-dependent cross section that evolves linearly from a point-like configuration to the full charmonium size during the formation time. For photoproduction, the Generalized Vector Dominance Model (GVDM) is used, which includes non-diagonal transitions between J/psi and psi' states. The suppression factor for J/psi production is calculated as a weighted sum of the suppression factors for J/psi, psi', and chi states, accounting for the decay of higher resonances into J/psi after they leave the target nucleus.

## Key numbers
- sigma(psi' N) approximately 8 mb from fitting NA50 pA data at 450 GeV, under the point-like production assumption with formation time t_f = 0.45 fm
- sigma(psi' N) = 20 mb with t_f = 0.6 fm also fits the data and is favored by nucleus-nucleus data
- J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section of 3.5 mb found at SLAC
- J/psi formation length l_f approximately 3 fm at SPS energies (p = 30 GeV, E_lab = 200 AGeV), corresponding to proper formation time tau_f = 0.3 fm
- psi' radius r = 0.45 fm from nonrelativistic charmonium models
- Mixing angle theta = 19 +/- 2 degrees between 2S and 1D charmonium states, determined from e+e- decay widths
- Predicted universal ratio sigma(psi'')/sigma(psi') approximately 0.1 from point-like production
- GVDM yields approximately 8 +/- 2 mb for psi'-nucleon cross section at SPS energies
- Inelastic pi-nucleon cross section at these energies is 20 mb

## Context
This work extends the author's previous model (Gerland, ref. ger) that described J/psi production in pA collisions by including production and decays of higher resonances (chi, psi') into J/psi, and applies it to new NA50 data. The approach contrasts with Arleo (ref. arleo), who introduced a color octet state with an eigen lifetime of 0.06 fm that the author argues is inconsistent with QCD. The paper also references the work of Spieles (ref. spieles) showing additional suppression in AA collisions from final state interactions with newly produced particles. The results confirm that the strength of hadron-hadron interactions depends on the volume occupied by color, as predicted by pQCD (ref. sigma). The author notes that future measurements at HERA B (E_lab = 920 GeV) will cover a larger x_F range, especially negative x_F where formation time effects are less important, allowing measurement of genuine cross sections.
