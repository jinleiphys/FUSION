# Effect of $K^*$ meson magnetic dipole moment on the $e^+e^- \to K^+ K^-π^0 π^0 $ cross section

> We explore the sensitivity of the $e^{+} e^{-} \to K^+ K^- 2 π^0$ cross section to the magnetic dipole moment (MDM) of the $K^*$ vector meson. We describe the $γ^* \to 2K2π$ vertex using a vector meson dominance model, including the intermediate resonant contributions relevant for energies below 2.4 GeV. Using BaBar data for this process, we show that this observable is indeed sensitive to the MDM of the $K^*$; we obtain a central value for the MDM of $μ_{K^*}=4.5$ and an upper bound of $\barμ_{K^*} = 6.3$, in units of $e/2 m_{K^*}$. We emphasize the need for higher precision data to provide a first data-driven determination of this parameter to confront it with theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The cross section of \(e^+e^- \to K^+K^-\pi^0\pi^0\) is sensitive to the magnetic dipole moment (MDM) of the \(K^*\) meson, but due to limited precision of the available BaBar data, only an upper bound can be extracted rather than a definitive measurement; the authors obtain a central value of \(\mu_{K^*}=4.5\) and an upper bound of \(\bar{\mu}_{K^*} = 6.3\) in units of \(e/2 m_{K^*}\).

## Method

The authors model the \(\gamma^* \to 2K2\pi\) vertex using a vector meson dominance (VMD) approach, including intermediate resonant contributions from \(\phi\), \(K^*\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(\sigma\) mesons relevant for energies below 2.4 GeV. They construct gauge-invariant amplitudes for channels involving \(K^*\) radiative vertices (channels A, B, C) and scalar-mediated channels (channel D), then perform a fit to BaBar cross section data using the Minuit minimization subroutine, treating the \(K^*\) MDM (\(\beta\)), scalar meson parameters, and relative phases as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Central value of the \(K^*\) MDM: \(\mu_{K^*} = 4.5\) in units of \(e/2 m_{K^*}\)
- Upper bound of the \(K^*\) MDM: \(\bar{\mu}_{K^*} = 6.3\) in units of \(e/2 m_{K^*}\)
- Fit quality: \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.6\) for the cross section data below 2.4 GeV
- Fitted \(\beta\) (magnetic dipole moment parameter): \(1.5^{+1.2}\) (no lower bound quoted)
- \(\sigma\)-meson mass from fit: \(m_\sigma = 0.71 \pm 0.02\) GeV
- \(\sigma\)-meson width from fit: \(\Gamma_\sigma = 0.13^{+0.13}_{-0.08}\) GeV
- Coupling ratio \(g_{\phi^\prime\phi f_0}/g_{\phi^\prime} = 1.15^{+0.07}_{-0.15}\)
- Coupling ratio \(g_{\phi^\prime\phi \sigma}/g_{\phi^\prime} = 0.32 \pm 0.06\)

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to the \(\rho\) meson by Rojas (2024), where the MDM of the \(\rho\) was extracted from \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-2\pi^0\) data, to the strange vector meson \(K^*\) using the analogous process \(e^+e^- \to K^+K^-2\pi^0\) measured by the BaBar Collaboration (2011). The result provides a first data-driven constraint on the \(K^*\) MDM, enabling comparison with theoretical predictions from lattice QCD (Lee, 2008), QCD sum rules (Aliev, 2009), Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches (Bhagwat, 2006; Hawes, 1998), and relativistic Hamiltonian models (Badalian, 2012), which predict values in the range \(\mu_{K^*} = [2, 2.68]\); the extracted central value of 4.5 lies above these predictions, but the authors emphasize that higher-precision data are needed for a definitive determination.
