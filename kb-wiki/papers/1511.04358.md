# Coexistence of nuclear shapes: self-consistent mean-field and beyond

> A quantitative analysis of the evolution of nuclear shapes and shape phase transitions, including regions of short-lived nuclei that are becoming accessible in experiments at radioactive-beam facilities, necessitate accurate modeling of the underlying nucleonic dynamics. Important theoretical advances have recently been made in studies of complex shapes and the corresponding excitation spectra and electromagnetic decay patterns, especially in the "beyond mean-field" framework based on nuclear density functionals. Interesting applications include studies of shape evolution and coexistence in N = 28 isotones, the structure of lowest $0^+$ excitations in deformed N $\approx$ 90 rare-earth nuclei, and quadrupole and octupole shape transitions in thorium isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs), extended beyond the mean-field approximation to include collective correlations through symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, provide a universal microscopic framework for describing shape coexistence and shape transitions across the nuclear chart, but the quantitative accuracy for specific observables (such as the excitation energy of the \(0_2^+\) state in \(^{44}\)S) depends on the degree of configuration mixing included in the model.

## Method

The authors employ relativistic energy density functionals (specifically the DD-PC1 functional) to perform constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations of deformation energy surfaces. They then extend the framework beyond mean-field by constructing a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole degrees of freedom (or a two-dimensional Hamiltonian for quadrupole-octupole degrees of freedom), with deformation-dependent mass parameters, moments of inertia, and collective potentials determined from the microscopic mean-field solutions. The collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities, which are compared to experimental data for \(N=28\) isotones, \(N\approx 90\) rare-earth nuclei, and thorium isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The spherical \(N=28\) shell gap decreases from 4.73 MeV in \(^{48}\)Ca to 3.86 MeV in \(^{44}\)S.
- In \(^{44}\)S, the theoretical excitation energy of the \(0_2^+\) state is much higher than the experimental value; the experimental \(0_2^+\) state lies at 1.365 MeV.
- The experimental monopole transition strength in \(^{44}\)S is \(\rho^2(E0;0_2^+ \to 0_1^+)=8.7(7)\times 10^{-3}\).
- For the three lowest \(2^+\) states in \(^{44}\)S, the spectroscopic quadrupole moments are: \(2_1^+\): \(-10.8\) \(e\)fm\(^2\); \(2_2^+\): \(+8.2\) \(e\)fm\(^2\); \(2_3^+\): \(-7.3\) \(e\)fm\(^2\).
- The \(2_1^+\) state in \(^{44}\)S has 89% \(K=0\) component and 11% \(K=2\) component; the \(2_2^+\) state has 21% \(K=0\) and 79% \(K=2\); the \(2_3^+\) state has 78% \(K=0\) and 22% \(K=2\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of relativistic energy density functionals (specifically the DD-PC1 functional, introduced by Niksic et al. 2008) to shape coexistence phenomena, building on prior constrained mean-field studies of \(N=28\) isotones by Delaroche et al. (2010) and Rodriguez and Egido (2011). The paper also continues a series of studies by the same group (Nikolic et al. 2007, Li et al. 2009) on shape phase transitions in rare-earth nuclei. The results enable future comparisons with shell model calculations, such as those by Chevrier et al. (2014) for \(^{44}\)S, and provide a framework for interpreting data from radioactive-beam facilities on short-lived nuclei.
