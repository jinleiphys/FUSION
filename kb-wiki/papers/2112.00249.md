# Role of the effective range in the weak-binding relation

> We study the range correction in the weak-binding relation, which relates the internal structure of hadrons with the scattering length and the binding energy. Utilizing the effective field theories, we show that the effective range originates from the derivative coupling interaction as well as from the channel coupling to the bare state, and that the different contributions are not distinguishable. By examining the compositeness in the effective field theories, it is demonstrated that the effective range induces the finite range correction for the weak-binding relation in addition to the previously known contributions. We thus propose to include the range correction in the uncertainty terms of the weak-binding relation.

## Key claim

The effective range introduces a finite range correction to the weak-binding relation that does not vanish even in the zero-range limit, and this correction should be included in the uncertainty terms of the relation; the origin of the effective range (derivative coupling versus channel coupling to a bare state) is not distinguishable from observables.

## Method

The authors constructed three nonrelativistic effective field theories: a zero-range model with only a contact interaction, an effective range model with an additional derivative coupling term, and a resonance model with a bare field coupled to the scattering state. They computed the two-body scattering amplitudes and bound-state compositeness in each model, then compared the exact compositeness values with those extracted from Weinberg's weak-binding relation to identify discrepancies arising from the effective range.

## Key numbers

* In the effective range model, the scattering amplitude is \( f_{\rm ER}(k) = \left(-\frac{1}{a_0} + \frac{r_e}{2}k^2 - ik\right)^{-1} \), where the effective range \( r_e \) is always negative, consistent with the Wigner causality bound.
* The relation between scattering length \( a_0 \), binding length \( R = 1/\sqrt{2\mu B} \), and effective range in the effective range model is \( a_0 = R \frac{2}{2 - r_e/R} \), which reduces to \( a_0 = R \) only when \( r_e \to 0 \).
* The LHCb collaboration reported an upper bound on the magnitude of the effective range for \( T_{cc} \) of 11 fm; an analysis of \( X(3872) \) suggests \( r_e \sim -5 \) fm.

## Context

This work extends the weak-binding relation originally formulated by Weinberg (1965) and later generalized in hadron physics by Kamiya, Hyodo, and collaborators (2015, 2016). The paper resolves a discrepancy in the effective range model where the weak-binding relation gave \( X < 1 \) while the exact compositeness was \( X = 1 \). The proposed range correction, defining \( R_{\rm typ} = \max\{R_{\rm int}, R_{\rm eff}\} \) with \( R_{\rm eff} \) from the effective range expansion, enables more reliable compositeness extraction for systems with large effective ranges such as \( T_{cc} \) and \( X(3872) \).
