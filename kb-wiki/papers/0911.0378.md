# Response of spin polarized neutron matter under the presence of a strong magnetic field with Skyrme interactions

> The effects of a strong magnetic field in the dynamical response of a pure neutron system to a weak neutrino probe are studied within the framework of the Landau Fermi Liquid Theory in the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation. We use Skyrme forces to parametrize the partially magnetized nuclear interacting plasma and describe its collective modes. We study the vector and vector-axial matter response functions and the neutrino mean free path in this two-component fermion system with net spin polarization and density dependent in-medium correlations. We find a decrease in the neutrino opacity of magnetized matter with respect to the non-magnetized case for fields $log_{10} B(G) \gtrsim 17$.

## Key claim
The neutrino opacity of magnetized neutron matter decreases relative to the non-magnetized case for magnetic fields with log10 B(G) greater than about 17, but the effect is density-dependent and only becomes substantial (approximately 10% or more) at densities near three times saturation density and at the maximum field strength considered, log10 B(G) = 18.

## Method
The author used Landau Fermi Liquid Theory in the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation with Skyrme effective interactions (SLy4 and SLy7 parametrizations) to model a two-component pure neutron system with spin polarization induced by a strong external magnetic field. The vector and vector-axial response functions were computed in the random phase approximation (RPA) including both monopolar (l=0) and dipolar (l=1) terms in the quasiparticle interaction, and the neutrino mean free path was then derived from these response functions.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strengths considered range from B=0 G up to a maximum internal value of B ≈ 10^18 G, the latter allowed by the scalar virial theorem.
- At saturation density ρ0 (0.158 fm^-3 for SLy4, 0.160 fm^-3 for SLy7) and B=10^17 G, the induced spin polarization is very mild, with Δ ≈ -1%.
- The collective spin zero sound modes at saturation density, B=10^17 G, and q=0.25 fm^-1 are approximately ω_+ ≈ ω_- ≈ 33.0 MeV.
- At ρ/ρ0 = 3.4, B=10^17 G, and q=0.25 fm^-1, the antiparallel collective mode energy is reduced by about -11% relative to the B=0 case.
- For the SLy4 parametrization at ρ/ρ0 = 3 and B=5×10^17 G, the spin-down component shows a shift to lower energies of approximately 10%.
- At ρ/ρ0 = 3 and incoming neutrino energy E_ν = 15 MeV, the SLy4 parametrization gives a decrease in neutrino opacity of about 4% relative to the non-magnetized case; for SLy7 at the same density and maximum field, the decrease is about 10% in the dipolar response.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of matter response functions in pure neutron systems without magnetic fields (e.g., works by the author and collaborators in 2008 and earlier studies cited as "pnm1, pnm2, pnm3") and builds on the author's prior study of magnetization in neutron plasmas using Skyrme and Gogny forces (Perez-Garcia 2008). It also connects to studies of ferromagnetic transitions in nuclear systems (Jerome 2000) and to the role of neutrinos in proto-neutron star cooling. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutrino transport in magnetized neutron star interiors, particularly in magnetars, and point toward the need for future work including finite temperature, beta-equilibrated matter, and proton polarization.
