# Bulk viscosity of superfluid hyperon stars

> We calculated bulk viscosity due to non-equilibrium weak processes in superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter of neutron stars. For that, the dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics, formulated in paper [1] for superfluid mixtures, was extended to the case when both nucleons and hyperons are superfluid. It was demonstrated that in the most general case (when neutrons, protons, Lambda, and Sigma^{-} hyperons are superfluid), non-equilibrium weak processes generate sixteen bulk viscosity coefficients, with only three of them being independent. In addition, we corrected an inaccuracy in a widely used formula for the bulk viscosity of non-superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter.

# Bulk viscosity of superfluid hyperon stars

## Key claim

The paper calculates the bulk viscosity generated by non-equilibrium weak processes in superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter of neutron stars, demonstrating that in the most general case (when neutrons, protons, Λ, and Σ⁻ hyperons are all superfluid) there are sixteen bulk viscosity coefficients, with only three of them being independent. The authors also correct an inaccuracy in a widely used formula for the bulk viscosity of non-superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter, though they note that this correction typically changes results by only about 10–30%.

## Method

The authors extended the dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics for superfluid mixtures, previously formulated by Gusakov (2007) for npe matter, to the case where both nucleons and hyperons (Λ and Σ⁻) are superfluid. They derived the entropy generation equation and used the Onsager symmetry principle to reduce the number of independent bulk viscosity coefficients from sixteen to ten, then further to three independent coefficients. For the non-superfluid case, they solved the linearized continuity equations for baryons and hyperons with source terms from non-leptonic weak reactions (n+n ↔ p+Σ⁻, n+p ↔ p+Λ, n+n ↔ n+Λ, n+Λ ↔ Λ+Λ), accounting for the fast strong reaction n+Λ ↔ p+Σ⁻ which previous work had mishandled.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity was calculated at temperature T = 3×10⁹ K and oscillation frequency ω = 10⁴ s⁻¹ for non-superfluid matter, using the third equation of state of Glendenning (1985)
- The correction to the Lindblom and Owen (2002) formula changes the bulk viscosity by typically ∼ (10–30)%
- Typical perturbation frequencies considered are 10³–10⁴ s⁻¹ for radial modes or r-modes of rapidly rotating neutron stars
- The baryon number density range considered extends above n_b ≳ (0.3–0.6) fm⁻³, where non-relativistic approximations become poorly justified

## Context

This work extends the dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics of Gusakov (2007), which calculated four bulk viscosity coefficients for superfluid npe matter, to the more complex case of nucleon-hyperon matter containing Λ and Σ⁻ hyperons. It corrects an inaccuracy in the bulk viscosity formula of Lindblom and Owen (2002) that was subsequently used by many authors (van Dalen 2004, Drago 2005, Mohit 2006, Debarati 2006, Pan 2006, Debarati 2007); the error arose from neglecting the neutron source term from the fast strong reaction n+Λ ↔ p+Σ⁻. The results enable more accurate calculations of damping times for gravitational-wave instabilities in neutron stars containing hyperonic matter, and provide the hydrodynamic framework needed to incorporate superfluidity of multiple baryon species simultaneously.
