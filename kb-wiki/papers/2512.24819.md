# Causality constraints and anisotropic states

> We investigate the effects of anisotropy on dispersion relations and convergence in relativistic hydrodynamics. In particular, we show that for dispersion relations with a branch point at the origin (such as sound modes), there exists a continuum of collisions between hydrodynamic modes at complex wavevector. These collisions are then explicitly demonstrated to be present in a holographic plasma. We lay out a criterion for when the continuum of collisions affects the convergence of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion. Finally, the radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations in anisotropic systems is bounded from above on the basis of compatibility with microscopic causality.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for hydrodynamic dispersion relations with a branch point at the origin (such as sound modes), there exists a continuum of collisions between hydrodynamic modes at complex wavevector, and that the radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations in anisotropic systems is bounded from above by compatibility with microscopic causality.

## Method

The author analyzes dispersion relations in relativistic hydrodynamics using the framework of several complex variables, studying how anisotropy affects convergence properties. The analysis is performed on first-order hydrodynamic theories (shear diffusion and sound modes in boosted equilibrium states, and Alfvén waves in magnetohydrodynamics) and on holographic plasmas dual to asymptotically AdS₅ black branes. The radius of convergence is determined by finding critical surfaces where the discriminant of the spectral curve vanishes, and causality constraints are derived by demanding that dispersion relations come from singularities of retarded two-point functions that decay rapidly enough at infinity.

## Key numbers

- For the boosted shear mode with \(v_0 = 1/\sqrt{3}\) and \(D = 4/(2\pi T_0)\), the radius of convergence as a function of angle \(\theta\) between \(\mathbf{k}\) and \(\mathbf{v}_0\) is given by \(R(\theta) = \frac{\sqrt{\gamma^2 \sin^2(\theta) + 2 \cos^2(\theta) - \sqrt{2 (1 + \gamma^2 + (1- \gamma^2) \cos(2\theta))} \cos(\theta)}}{2 \gamma D |v_0| \sin^2(\theta)}\).
- For the boosted sound mode with \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\), \(v_0 = 1/\sqrt{2}\), and \(\Gamma = 4/(2\pi T_0)\), the expansion of \(\mathfrak{w}\) about \(r=0\) (where \(r^2 = \mathfrak{q}_z^2 + \mathfrak{q}_x^2\)) yields \(\mathfrak{w}_\pm = \frac{2\sqrt{2}\cos(\theta) \pm \sqrt{3\cos^2(\theta) + 5\sin^2(\theta)}}{5} r + \mathcal{O}(r^2)\).
- The discriminant for the boosted sound mode at the origin vanishes: \(\Delta_\mathfrak{w}(0,0) = 0\).
- The critical surface for the boosted shear mode is given analytically by \(|k_z| = \frac{1}{4} \sqrt{ \frac{(1 - 4 D^2 |k_x|^2 v_0^2)^2}{D^2 v_0^2 (1-v_0^2)}}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of convergence in hydrodynamic derivative expansions from isotropic systems (studied by Heller (2013), Grozdanov (2019), and others) to anisotropic systems, where the wavevector has multiple independent complex components. The paper generalizes the causality constraints on transport coefficients derived by Heller (2022) to systems with a single anisotropy. The results enable future work on bounding transport coefficients in anisotropic systems such as magnetohydrodynamics and on understanding the convergence properties of hydrodynamic expansions in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers.
