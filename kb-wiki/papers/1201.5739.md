# Partial wave expansion for photoproduction of two pseudoscalars on a nucleon

> The amplitudes for photoproduction of two pseudoscalars on a nucleon are expanded in the overall c.m. frame in a model independent way with respect to the contribution of the final state partial wave of total angular momentum $J$ and its projection on the normal to the plane spanned by the momenta of the final particles. The expansion coefficients which are analogues to the multipole amplitudes for single meson photoproduction contain the complete information about the reaction dynamics. Results of an explicit evaluation are presented for the moments $W_{jm}$ of the inclusive angular distribution of an incident photon beam with respect to the c.m.\ coordinate system defined by the final particles taking photoproduction of $π^0π^0$ and $π^0η$ as an example.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent partial wave expansion for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon, where the expansion coefficients (analogous to multipole amplitudes in single meson photoproduction) contain the complete dynamical information, but the method is explicitly limited to the unpolarized differential cross section and does not allow a unique determination of the amplitudes without additional observables.

## Method

The authors perform a partial wave decomposition of the transition amplitude in the overall center-of-mass frame, expanding the final state in terms of total angular momentum J and its projection M onto the normal to the plane spanned by the final three particles. The formalism uses a "rigid body" coordinate system K_fs attached to the final state plane, with the z-axis normal to that plane, and expresses the T-matrix elements in terms of reduced multipole amplitudes. The differential cross section is then expanded in spherical harmonics Y_jm of the photon angles, with coefficients W_jm that are functionals of the partial wave amplitudes t^{JM}_{νλμ}.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section σ_0 is given by σ_0 = π c(W) ∫ dω₁ dω₂ Σ_{νλμJM} (1/(2J+1)) |t^{JM}_{νλμ}(ω₁,ω₂)|²
- The kinematical factor c(W) = M_N² / [4(2π)⁴ (W² - M_N²)]
- For γp → π⁰π⁰p, only waves with J ≤ 5/2 are important below E_γ = 1 GeV
- The moments W_jm for j=3,4 are small for γp → π⁰π⁰p in the model calculation
- For γp → π⁰ηp, the J^P = 3/2⁻ wave dominates from threshold to about E_γ = 1.7 GeV
- The selection rule W_jm = 0 for j+m = odd is derived from parity invariance

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis techniques used for single meson photoproduction to the three-body final state case, addressing the lack of a general recipe for reactions with three particles in the final state that has hindered rigorous analysis. The method contrasts with isobar model approaches used by Oset, Laget, Ochi, Mokeev, and others, which depend on assumptions about the production mechanism and suffer from non-uniqueness of solutions. The formalism enables model-independent extraction of moments W_jm from experimental data, which can then be compared with predictions from models such as those of Fix and Arenhoevel (2011) for γp → π⁰π⁰p and Fix, Kamalov, and Lee (2005) for γp → π⁰ηp, potentially resolving open questions about the role of resonances like D₁₃(1520), D₃₃(1700), and P₁₁(1440).
