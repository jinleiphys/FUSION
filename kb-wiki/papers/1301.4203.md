# Nuclear matrix elements for double-ő≤ decay

> Background: Direct determination of the neutrino mass through double-$ő≤$ decay is at the present time one of the most important areas of experimental and theoretical research in nuclear and particle physics. Purpose: We calculate nuclear matrix elements for the extraction of the average neutrino mass in neutrinoless double-$ő≤$ decay. Methods: The microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) is used. Results: Nuclear matrix elements in the closure approximation are calculated for $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{110}$Pd, $^{116}$Cd, $^{124}$Sn, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, $^{148}$Nd, $^{150}$Nd, $^{154}$Sm, $^{160}$Gd, and $^{198}$Pt decay. Conclusions: Realistic predictions for the expected half-lives in neutrinoless double-$ő≤$ decay with light and heavy neutrino exchange in terms of neutrino masses are made and limits are set from current experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay calculated within the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) using the closure approximation, but the authors note that the IBM-2 description is only approximate for nuclei with A less than about 70, and that the results for calcium-48 decay in particular should be considered a rough estimate.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) to calculate nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless and two-neutrino double-beta decay in the closure approximation, mapping the fermion transition operator onto a boson space at leading order. The matrix elements were evaluated with realistic IBM-2 wave functions taken from the literature or obtained from fits to experimental energies and electromagnetic properties, using the formulation of Simkovic et al. for the transition operator with Jastrow Miller-Spencer short-range correlations and free values of g_V=1 and g_A=1.269.

## Key numbers

* For germanium-76 decay to selenium-76, the dimensionless nuclear matrix element M^(0nu) to the ground state is 5.42 in IBM-2, compared to 4.68 in QRPA-Tu and 2.22 in the interacting shell model (ISM).
* For xenon-136 decay to barium-136, the dimensionless M^(0nu) to the ground state is 3.33 in IBM-2, compared to 2.22 in QRPA-Tu and 1.70 in the ISM.
* For tellurium-128 decay to xenon-128, the dimensionless M^(0nu) to the ground state is 4.48 in IBM-2, compared to 3.77 in QRPA-Tu and 2.26 in the ISM.
* For calcium-48 decay to titanium-48, the dimensionless M^(0nu) to the ground state is 1.98 in IBM-2, but the authors note this should be more appropriately quoted as 1.33 to 1.98 if the Fermi matrix element is set to zero.
* The ratio of matrix elements for tellurium-128 to tellurium-130 is 1.11 from IBM-2, matching 1.11 from the ISM and 1.13 from QRPA-Tu.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program for systematic evaluation of double-beta decay quantities, following their calculation of phase-space factors reported in Kotila (2012). The IBM-2 results are compared with quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations from the Tubingen group (Simkovic et al.) and the Jyvaskyla group (Suhonen), as well as interacting shell model (ISM) calculations from Poves et al. and Menendez et al. The paper enables extraction of limits on the effective neutrino mass from current experiments and provides a consistent set of matrix elements across all candidate nuclei within a single model framework.
