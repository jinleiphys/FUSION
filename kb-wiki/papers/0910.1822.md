# Separable potential model for $K^{-}N$ interactions at low energies

> The effective separable meson-baryon potentials are constructed to match the equivalent chiral amplitudes up to the second order in external meson momenta. We fit the model parameters (low energy constants) to the threshold and low energy $K^{-}p$ data. In the process, the $K^{-}$-proton bound state problem is solved exactly in the momentum space and the 1s level characteristics of the kaonic hydrogen are computed simultaneously with the available low energy $K^{-}p$ cross sections. The model is also used to describe the $πΣ$ mass spectrum and the energy dependence of the $K^{-}n$ amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors construct separable meson-baryon potentials that match chiral amplitudes up to second order in external meson momenta, and they fit model parameters to simultaneously reproduce the 1s level characteristics of kaonic hydrogen (computed exactly in momentum space, not via the approximate Deser-Trueman relation) and available low-energy \(K^{-}p\) cross sections and threshold branching ratios, with the caveat that the fits are not strongly affected by inclusion of the \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectrum or by enforcing the Cayley-Hamilton identity on the second-order couplings.

## Method

The authors construct a separable potential matrix for ten coupled meson-baryon channels (\(\pi\Lambda\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(K^{-}p\), \(\bar{K}^{0}n\), \(\eta\Lambda\), \(\eta\Sigma^{0}\), \(K^{0}\Xi^{0}\), \(K^{+}\Xi^{-}\)) based on a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^{2})\). They solve the coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations algebraically for s-wave T-matrices and compute the \(K^{-}p\) bound state exactly in momentum space via the Jost matrix method, searching for zeros of its determinant with the Mueller algorithm. Model parameters (five inverse-range radii \(\alpha_{j}\), the meson-baryon chiral coupling \(f\), the ratio \(r_{KN/\pi\Sigma}\), and five second-order low-energy constants \(d_{D}, d_{F}, d_{0}, d_{1}, d_{2}\) with \(d_{2}=0\) enforced) are fitted to 16 data points: three threshold branching ratios, cross sections at \(p_{LAB}=110\) and 200 MeV, the \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectrum peak at 1395 MeV, and the DEAR kaonic hydrogen shift and width.

## Key numbers

*   Kaonic hydrogen 1s level strong-interaction shift: \(\Delta E_{N}(1s) = 193 \pm 43\) eV (DEAR experimental value used as fit target).
*   Kaonic hydrogen 1s level width: \(\Gamma(1s) = 249 \pm 150\) eV (DEAR experimental value used as fit target).
*   Threshold branching ratio \(\gamma = \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}) / \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \pi^{-}\Sigma^{+}) = 2.36 \pm 0.04\) (experimental value from Martin (1981)).
*   Threshold branching ratio \(R_{c} = \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \text{charged particles}) / \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \text{all}) = 0.664 \pm 0.011\) (experimental value from Martin (1981)).
*   Threshold branching ratio \(R_{n} = \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \pi^{0}\Lambda) / \sigma(K^{-}p \rightarrow \text{all neutral states}) = 0.189 \pm 0.015\) (experimental value from Martin (1981)).
*   Best-fit \(\chi^{2}/N\) values range from 1.29 to 1.37 for \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) between 20 and 50 MeV.
*   Fitted meson-baryon chiral coupling \(f\) ranges from 107.6 to 114.5 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   Fitted inverse-range parameter \(\alpha_{KN}\) ranges from 625 to 639 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   Fitted inverse-range parameter \(\alpha_{\pi\Sigma}\) ranges from 579 to 640 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   Fitted inverse-range parameter \(\alpha_{\pi\Lambda}\) ranges from 219 to 345 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   Fitted inverse-range parameter \(\alpha_{\eta\Lambda/\Sigma}\) ranges from 568 to 936 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   Fitted inverse-range parameter \(\alpha_{K\Xi}\) ranges from 151 to 260 MeV across the four \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) options.
*   The point-like Coulomb potential reproduces the Bohr energy of the 1s level with precision better than 0.1 eV; inclusion of finite-size and vacuum polarization effects gives an attractive shift \(\Delta E_{FS+VP}(1s) = -20.2\) eV.

## Context

This work extends the separable-potential approach of Cieply and Smejkal (2007) by adding constraints from the \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectrum peak and the Cayley-Hamilton identity on the second-order couplings, and by restricting inverse-range parameters to physically motivated intervals to avoid unphysical poles. It contrasts with the on-shell N/D scheme of Oset and collaborators (2001) by using off-shell form factors parameterized by inverse-range radii rather than subtraction constants. The model enables future Faddeev-type calculations of the \(\bar{K}NN - \pi\Sigma N\) system, as noted by Shevchenko et al. (2007), and is relevant for the SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic deuterium.
