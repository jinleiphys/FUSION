# Anisotropic flow and jet quenching in ultra-relativistic U+U collisions

> Full-overlap U+U collisions provide significantly larger initial energy densities at comparable spatial deformation, and significantly larger deformation and volume at comparable energy density, than semicentral Au+Au collisions. We show quantitatively that this provides a long lever arm for studying the hydrodynamic behavior of elliptic flow in much larger and denser collision systems and the predicted non-linear path-length dependence of radiative parton energy loss.

# Anisotropic flow and jet quenching in ultra-relativistic U+U collisions

## Key claim

Full-overlap U+U collisions at top RHIC energy can increase the initial entropy density per unit transverse area by about 55% compared to semicentral Au+Au collisions, reaching values around 40/fm², and can more than double both the absolute radiative energy loss and its difference between in-plane and out-of-plane directions, but these advantages depend on selecting events with essentially no spectator nucleons and on the specific collision orientation (side-on-side vs. edge-on-edge).

## Method

The authors used a Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon density distribution for uranium nuclei (radius parameters R_∥ = 7.94 fm, R_⊥ = 6.17 fm, ratio 1.29) to compute initial entropy density in the transverse plane, with a fraction α = 0.75 scaling with wounded nucleon density and the remainder with binary collision density, fitted to reproduce Au+Au charged particle multiplicity at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They considered only b = 0 collisions with full nuclear overlap, controlled by the polar angle Φ between the beam direction and the nuclear symmetry axis, and estimated radiative parton energy loss using a figure of merit t proportional to the integrated parton density along the parton trajectory, both with and without dilution from longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- Peak entropy density in Φ = 0 (side-on-side) U+U collisions: s₀^UU = 167 fm⁻³ at proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Central entropy density in b = 0 Au+Au collisions: s₀ = 117 fm⁻³ at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Maximum peak energy density in central U+U is about 62% larger than in most central Au+Au collisions
- Initial eccentricity ε_x ranges from 0 (edge-on-edge) to 0.25 (side-on-side) in full-overlap U+U; average over all angles ⟨ε_x⟩ ≈ 0.13
- Particle production in full-overlap U+U varies by almost 15% between side-on-side and edge-on-edge configurations
- The quantity (1/S)(dN_ch/dy) ∼ s₀ increases by ≈ 55% in full-overlap U+U edge-on-edge geometry, to values around 40/fm²
- In Au+Au collisions, the energy loss decreases with increasing ε_x by 35% (in-plane) and 55% (out-of-plane); in U+U, the difference in energy loss between out-of-plane and in-plane emission is more than twice that achievable in Au+Au
- Total energy loss in full-overlap U+U is larger by up to a factor of 2 compared to Au+Au

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals for U+U collisions by Li (2000), Shuryak (2000), and Kolb et al. (2000) by providing quantitative calculations of multiplicity distributions, spatial eccentricity, and semiquantitative estimates of parton energy loss. The paper addresses two open questions from RHIC Au+Au data: whether the ratio v₂/ε_x approaches the ideal fluid dynamical limit at high entropy density (where data from the NA49 collaboration at the SPS appeared to cross the hydrodynamic curve near 25/fm²), and whether the non-linear path-length dependence of radiative parton energy loss predicted by Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) theory can be distinguished experimentally. The results enable a dedicated experimental program at RHIC using zero-degree calorimeters to select full-overlap U+U events, providing a longer lever arm for testing hydrodynamic behavior and jet quenching mechanisms than is possible with Au+Au collisions alone.
