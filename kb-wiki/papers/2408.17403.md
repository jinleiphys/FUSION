# How do mirror charge radii constrain density dependence of the symmetry energy?

> It has recently been suggested that differences in the charge radii of mirror nuclei ($Î”R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$) are strongly correlated with the neutron-skin thickness ($R_{\rm skin}$) of neutron-rich nuclei and with the slope of the symmetry energy ($L$). To test this assumption, we present ab initio calculations of $R_{\rm skin}$ in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, $Î”R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ in $^{36}$Ca$-^{36}$S, $^{38}$Ca$-^{38}$Ar, $^{41}$Sc$-^{41}$Ca, $^{48}$Ni$-^{48}$Ca, $^{52}$Ni$-^{52}$Cr, and $^{54}$Ni$-^{54}$Fe mirror pairs, and $L$. Employing the recently developed 34 chiral interaction samples, identified by the history matching approach, we conduct rigorous statistical analysis of correlations among $Î”R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$, $R_{\rm skin}$ and $L$, accounting for quantified uncertainties from low-energy constants of chiral interaction, chiral effective field theory truncation and many-body method approximation. The ab initio results reveal an appreciable $Î”R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}-L$ correlation in $fp$-shell mirror pairs. However, contrary to previous studies, the present calculation finds that the studied $sd$-shell mirror pairs do not exhibit any $Î”R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}-L$ correlation.

## Key claim
The paper tests the assumption that differences in charge radii of mirror nuclei ($\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$) are strongly correlated with the slope of the symmetry energy ($L$), finding that this correlation holds for $fp$-shell mirror pairs but is absent for $sd$-shell mirror pairs, contrary to previous studies.

## Method
The author performed ab initio calculations using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) approach with 34 chiral NN+3N interaction samples identified by history matching. The calculations were conducted for neutron-skin thickness ($R_{\rm skin}$) in $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ in six mirror pairs, and the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy. A Bayesian sampling/importance resampling method was employed to generate posterior predictive distributions, incorporating quantified uncertainties from chiral effective field theory truncation and many-body method approximations.

## Key numbers
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{36}$Ca-$^{36}$S: experimental value 0.150$\pm$0.004 fm, posterior predictive distribution (PPD) median $0.161^{+0.018}_{-0.018}$ fm
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{38}$Ca-$^{38}$Ar: experimental value 0.063$\pm$0.003 fm, PPD median $0.068^{+0.009}_{-0.009}$ fm
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{54}$Ni-$^{54}$Fe: experimental value 0.049$\pm$0.004 fm, PPD median $0.053^{+0.010}_{-0.010}$ fm
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{48}$Ni-$^{48}$Ca: PPD median $0.266^{+0.031}_{-0.032}$ fm
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{52}$Ni-$^{52}$Cr: PPD median $0.112^{+0.015}_{-0.015}$ fm
- $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ for $^{41}$Sc-$^{41}$Ca: PPD median $0.043^{+0.007}_{-0.007}$ fm
- Coefficient of determination (CoD) for $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}-L$ correlation in $fp$-shell mirror pairs: between 0.38 and 0.55
- CoD for $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}-L$ correlation in $sd$-shell mirror pairs ($A=36$, $A=38$): approximately zero
- CoD for $^{208}$Pb $R_{\rm skin}-L$ correlation: 0.89
- CoD for $^{48}$Ca $R_{\rm skin}-L$ correlation: 0.64

## Context
This work extends ab initio studies of the symmetry energy slope $L$ and its correlation with nuclear observables, following the history-matching approach of Hu (2022) that identified 34 chiral interaction samples. It directly tests the suggestion from PhysRevLett.119.122502 and PhysRevC.97.014314 that $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}$ is proportional to $L$, and contrasts with the MR-IMSRG results of PhysRevResearch.2.022035 using EM family interactions, which found a narrow range for both quantities. The finding that $sd$-shell mirror pairs show no $\Delta R^{\rm mirr}_{\rm ch}-L$ correlation contradicts previous mean-field studies and indicates that quantum many-body effects significantly influence the correlation, meaning mirror charge radii cannot universally constrain $L$ as previously assumed.
