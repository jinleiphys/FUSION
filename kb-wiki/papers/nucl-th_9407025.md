# Quantum Effects in the Stochastic Behaviour of Nuclear Matter at Finite Excitations\FOOTNOTE

> We examine the dynamics of statistical fluctuations in nuclear matter. Linear response functions for the average phase space density are derived within Landau theory. Properties of the stochastic forces are deduced from the quantal fluctuation dissipation theorem, with a suitable generalization to unstable modes. Sizable quantum effects are found both inside and outside the spinodal regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that sizable quantum effects in the stochastic forces governing fluctuations in nuclear matter persist up to temperatures of about 12 MeV, both inside and outside the spinodal regime, contrary to the expectation that such effects would be negligible at finite temperature. The authors note that their treatment of unstable modes via a generalized fluctuation-dissipation theorem requires a lower temperature bound of approximately 1.43 MeV for validity, and they caution that results near this limit should not be overinterpreted.

## Method

The authors derive linear response functions for the average phase space density from a linearized Landau-Vlasov equation with a relaxation-time collision term, using a low-temperature approximation that replaces the energy derivative of the Fermi function with a delta function at the Fermi surface. They then compute the equilibrium fluctuations from the quantal fluctuation-dissipation theorem, generalizing the integration contour to handle unstable modes by deforming it above all poles of the response function. The diffusion coefficients for the stochastic forces are obtained from the stationary limit of the fluctuation evolution equations, using a Drude regularization with cutoff frequency 100 MeV to handle divergences in the quantum regime.

## Key numbers

- Quantum effects in the diffusion coefficient persist up to T ≈ 10–12 MeV, where at T = 12 MeV the quantal and classical diffusion coefficients still differ by a factor of about 1.5.
- The lower temperature bound for the treatment of unstable modes is T₀ ≈ 1.43 MeV, occurring at density ρ = 0.045 fm⁻³.
- The Drude regularization cutoff ω_D = 100 MeV was used; varying ω_D between 30 and 200 MeV changes the diffusion coefficient by less than about 20%.
- The parameter β = 50 MeV·fm⁵ was used for the q² term in the Landau force to reproduce the spinodal region.

## Context

This work extends the Landau theory approach of Heiselberg, Pethick, and Ravenhall (1993) for average dynamics to include quantal statistical fluctuations, generalizing the method of Hofmann, Kiderlen, and Tsekhmistrenko (1992) to unstable modes. The technique is analogous to the treatment of dissipative tunneling for a single collective variable by Hofmann, Thoma, and Ingold (to be published) and Hofmann and Ingold (1991), where the same contour-deformation method was validated against exact functional-integral results. The results enable future studies of spinodal instabilities in heavy-ion collisions with quantum fluctuations included, and the authors note that a forthcoming paper by Kiderlen (1994) will provide more details on the comparison with the Boltzmann-equation approach of Randrup and Remaud (1990).
