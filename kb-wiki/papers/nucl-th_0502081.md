# On Parametric Resonance in Quantum Many-Body System

> The dynamics governed by a requantized collective Hamiltonian in the coupled Lipkin model is investigated in the time-dependent variational approach with squeezed state. It is pointed out that there is a possibility of the parametric resonance mechanism which leads to amplifying the amplitude of quantum fluctuation around the collective mode in this model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the coupled Lipkin model, a collective oscillation around a static configuration inevitably leads to amplification of the quantum fluctuation mode through a parametric resonance mechanism, but this resonance is weak because it corresponds to the n=4 case in the Mathieu equation, meaning the damping time of the collective motion may be rather long.

## Method

The authors first specify the collective submanifold of the coupled Lipkin model using the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock method, obtaining a requantized collective Hamiltonian with a double-well potential. They then apply a time-dependent variational approach with a squeezed state to this Hamiltonian, deriving coupled equations of motion for the collective coordinate Q and the quantum fluctuation variable eta. By expanding around static solutions and taking the semiclassical limit (hbar approaching 0), they reduce the fluctuation dynamics to a generalized Mathieu equation.

## Key numbers

* For the model parameters epsilon_1=1.5, epsilon_2=2.0, V_1=0.05 chi, V_2=0.1 chi, V_3=0.075 chi, and Omega=5, the collective potential coefficients c_0, c_1, and c_2 are tabulated for chi values from 1.5 to 5.0; for example, at chi=3.0, c_0=-0.747, c_1=1.40, c_2=-0.002.
* The ratio |(1/6)c_2|/|(1/4)c_1| is at most 0.3278 (at chi=1.5) and as low as 9.5 x 10^{-4} (at chi=3.0), justifying truncation of the collective potential at Q^4 order near the phase transition.
* The static solutions in the semiclassical limit are Q_0^2 = -c_0/c_1 (positive) and eta_0^4 = -1/(8c_0) (positive).
* The Mathieu equation parameters are omega_0^2 = -8c_0, h = (3/4) sigma sqrt(-c_1/c_0), and gamma = sqrt(-2c_0), with the relation omega_0 = 2 gamma corresponding to the n=4 resonance case.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous adiabatic TDHF study of the coupled Lipkin model (Kuriyama, Yamamura, and Iida, 1984) by investigating the time-dependent dynamics of quantum fluctuations around the collective mode, an aspect left untouched in that earlier work. The parametric resonance mechanism is analogous to that found by Tsue (2002) in the O(4) linear sigma model for disoriented chiral condensate formation, where the chiral condensate oscillation amplifies quantum pion modes. The results suggest that if additional intrinsic modes exist beyond the collective mode and its quantum fluctuation, stronger instabilities (such as n=1 in the Mathieu equation) may appear, potentially leading to dissipation of collective motion.
