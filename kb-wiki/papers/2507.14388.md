# On the Impossibility of Obtaining Time-Independent, Three-Dimensional, Spherically-Symmetric Densities of Confined Systems of Relativistically Moving Constituents

> The quantum mechanical definition of probability, the uncertainty principle and Poincare invariance provide strong basic restrictions on the ability to define spatial densities associated with form factors describing the properties of confined systems of relativistically moving constituents. Despite this, many papers ignore one or more of these restrictions. Here I show how to obtain time-independent, two-dimensional densities that are consistent with the stated restrictions. This is done using the light-front, infinite momentum frame formalism. Two-dimensional density interpretations of the axial-vector form factor and all three gravitational form factors are obtained. Additionally, an expression of a two-dimensional mass density related to the trace of the energy momentum tensor is obtained. I also show that all known methods for finding three-dimensional densities: using the Breit frame, Abel transformations, Wigner distributions and spherically-symmetric wave packets with vanishing spatial extent violate the basic restrictions in different manners. Furthermore, the use of the latter leads to densities that vanish almost everywhere in space as time increases from an initial value.

## Key claim

The paper argues that it is fundamentally impossible to obtain legitimate time-independent, three-dimensional, spherically-symmetric spatial densities for confined systems of relativistically moving constituents (such as nucleons), because the quantum mechanical definition of probability, the uncertainty principle, and Poincaré invariance together forbid such constructions. The author shows that only two-dimensional, time-independent densities can be consistently defined, using the light-front infinite-momentum-frame formalism with the Drell-Yan condition \(\Delta^+ = 0\).

## Method

The author begins from the postulate that probability density is given by \(|\Psi|^2\) and that for Poincaré-invariant systems the wave function does not factorize into internal and center-of-mass parts as it does for Galilean-invariant systems. Using light-front coordinates with \(x^\pm = (x^0 \pm x^3)/\sqrt{2}\) and imposing the Drell-Yan condition \(\Delta^+ = 0\), the author constructs wave packets that are plane waves in \(P^+\) and Gaussian in transverse momentum, then takes the infinite-momentum limit \(P^+ \to \infty\) to eliminate time (\(x^+\)) dependence. The resulting two-dimensional densities are obtained as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of form factors. The author then applies this formalism to the axial-vector form factor and all three gravitational form factors (\(A(t), J(t), D(t)\)), and also to the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. Separately, the author examines four existing methods for extracting three-dimensional densities, Breit frame interpretation, Abel transformations, Wigner distributions, and spherically-symmetric wave packets with vanishing spatial extent, and demonstrates how each violates the basic restrictions.

## Key numbers

- Axial form factor dipole parameters: \(G_A(0) = 1.2723\), \(M_A = 1.014\) GeV, used to compute the transverse axial charge density \(\rho_A(x_\perp) = G_A(0) M_A^2 \frac{M_A x_\perp K_1(M_A x_\perp)}{4\pi}\).
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor is modeled via a generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation: \(G_P(\Delta^2) = \frac{4M^2 G_A(\Delta^2)}{-\Delta^2 + m_\pi^2}\), with \(m_\pi\) the pion mass.
- The mean-squared transverse radius is given by \(\langle \mathbf{x}_\perp^2 \rangle_{\mathcal{O}} = -\frac{1}{4} \frac{d}{d \boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2} F_{\mathcal{O}}(\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2)\), where the transverse squared radii are two-thirds of the oft-used spherical radii.
- The integral of the two-dimensional mass density \(m(x_\perp)\) satisfies \(\int d^2 x_\perp \, m(x_\perp) = 2M^2\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing critique of Breit-frame density interpretations that began with Yennie, Lévy, and Ravenhall (1957) and continues through more recent work by Miller (2018, 2019) and Freese (2021, 2025). The paper directly contradicts the widespread practice of interpreting form factors as three-dimensional Fourier transforms of spherically-symmetric densities, a practice rooted in Hofstadter's non-relativistic treatment of nuclear charge densities. The author shows that the Breit frame fails because it does not satisfy the property that density is the absolute square of a wave function, that Abel transformations fail due to length contraction effects for moving systems, that Wigner distributions suffer from infinite fluctuations in both position and momentum, and that spherically-symmetric wave packets with vanishing spatial extent produce densities that vanish almost everywhere as time increases. The two-dimensional light-front densities derived here enable consistent interpretations of axial-vector and gravitational form factors, and the formalism provides a foundation for computing transverse densities from lattice QCD and experimental data from Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
