# Nuclear corrections of parton distribution functions

> We report global analysis results of experimental data for nuclear structure-function ratios F_2^A/F_2^{A'} and proton-nucleus Drell-Yan cross-section ratios sigma_{DY}^{pA}/sigma_{DY}^{pA'} in order to determine optimum parton distribution functions (PDFs) in nuclei. An important point of this analysis is to show uncertainties of the distributions by the Hessian method. The results indicate that the uncertainties are large for gluon distributions in the whole x region and for antiquark distributions at x>0.2. We provide a code for calculating any nuclear PDFs at given x and Q^2 for general users. They can be used for calculating high-energy nuclear reactions including neutrino-nucleus interactions, which are discussed at this workshop.

## Key claim
The authors determine optimum nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) by a global chi-squared analysis of nuclear structure-function ratios and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios, but they explicitly note that the uncertainties remain large for gluon distributions across the entire x region and for antiquark distributions at x > 0.2, meaning the extracted NPDFs are not reliably constrained in those sectors.

## Method
The authors performed a leading-order chi-squared fit to 951 data points consisting of nuclear structure-function ratios F2^A/F2^A' (899 points) and proton-nucleus Drell-Yan cross-section ratios sigma_DY^pA/sigma_DY^pA' (52 points) for targets from deuteron to lead. The nuclear PDFs are parametrized at Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 as the free-nucleon PDF multiplied by a weight function w_i(x,A,Z) that depends on A, Z, and x through a cubic polynomial divided by (1-x)^beta_i, with parameters determined by MINUIT and uncertainties estimated via the Hessian method with Delta chi^2 = 10.427 for one-sigma errors.

## Key numbers
- Total chi^2 = 1489.8 for 951 data points at the optimum fit.
- 606 data points for F2^A/F2^D ratios, 293 for F2^A1/F2^A2 ratios (A' not deuteron), and 52 for Drell-Yan ratios.
- The NPDFs are provided for Q^2 from 1 GeV^2 to 10^8 GeV^2 and x from 10^-9 to 1, though the smallest experimental x is 0.0055 and the maximum experimental Q^2 is 173 GeV^2.
- The uncertainty estimation uses Delta chi^2 = 10.427, corresponding to nine free parameters.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier NPDF analysis presented at the NuInt01 and NuInt02 workshops (Hirai, Kumano, Nagai 2001, 2002) by adding Drell-Yan and HERMES data, including charm-quark distributions, and providing uncertainty estimates via the Hessian method. The results enable calculations of nuclear corrections for high-energy neutrino-nucleus interactions relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments, though the authors note that an extension from the deep inelastic scattering region to the resonance region (as done for the nucleon by Bodek and Yang in 2002) is needed for medium-energy neutrino experiments.
