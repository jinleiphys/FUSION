# Nonabelian density functional theory

> Given a vector space of microscopic quantum observables, density functional theory is formulated on its dual space. A generalized Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and the existence of the universal energy functional in the dual space are proven. In this context ordinary density functional theory corresponds to the space of one-body multiplication operators. When the operators close under commutation to form a Lie algebra, the energy functional defines a Hamiltonian dynamical system on the coadjoint orbits in the algebra's dual space. The enhanced density functional theory provides a new method for deriving the group theoretic Hamiltonian on the coadjoint orbits from the exact microscopic Hamiltonian.

# Nonabelian density functional theory

## Key claim

The paper generalizes conventional density functional theory to any vector space of microscopic quantum observables, proving that a Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and universal energy functional exist on the dual space of that vector space. When the observables form a Lie algebra under commutation, the energy functional defines a Hamiltonian dynamical system on the coadjoint orbits in the algebra's dual space, enabling derivation of group-theoretic Hamiltonians from the exact microscopic Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors formulate density functional theory on the dual space of an arbitrary vector space of hermitian operators, defining a moment map from the projective Hilbert space to the dual space. They prove a generalized Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (Theorem 1) showing the moment map is injective on states that are absolute nondegenerate minima of shifted energy functionals, and establish a Levy constrained-search formulation (Theorem 2) that extends minimization to all N-representable densities. When the operator space is a Lie algebra, they construct the coadjoint action of the corresponding Lie group on the dual space, show each coadjoint orbit is a symplectic manifold, and define a model Hamiltonian h on the orbit via the symplectic form and the derivative of the restricted energy functional.

## Key numbers

- The vector space of operators for the cranked Nilsson model is eight-dimensional, corresponding to the rotational model Lie algebra rot(3), formed from linear combinations of angular momentum J and quadrupole operator Q^(2)_mu.
- The full Nilsson model expands the operator space to a 10-dimensional space that includes spin-orbit interaction and the square of orbital angular momentum.
- The paper provides no other explicit numerical results; it is a formal theoretical development.

## Context

This work extends the original Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (1964) and Levy's constrained-search formulation (1979, 1982) from the specific case of one-body multiplication operators to arbitrary operator spaces. It unifies several existing algebraic approaches to nuclear structure, including the Elliott su(3) algebra, the Ginocchio so(8) fermion algebra, the general collective model algebra gcm(3), and the symplectic model algebra sp(3,R). The formalism enables a systematic derivation of mean-field approximations such as Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov from the exact microscopic Hamiltonian, and provides a framework for constructing group-theoretic models for nuclear collective motion where no external potential dominates the dynamics.
