# Radial oscillations in neutron stars from QCD

> We study the stability against infinitesimal radial oscillations of neutron stars generated by a set of equations of state obtained from first-principle calculations in cold and dense QCD and constrained by observational data. We consider mild and large violations of the conformal bound, $c_{s} = 1/\sqrt{3}$, in stars that can possibly contain a quark matter core. Some neutron star families in the mass-radius diagram become dynamically unstable due to large oscillation amplitudes near the core.

## Key claim

The paper shows that some neutron star families in the mass-radius diagram, generated by equations of state that allow large violations of the conformal bound on the speed of sound, are dynamically unstable not because their fundamental-mode frequency becomes imaginary, but because the infinitesimal radial displacement amplitude becomes large (around 30) near the core, which in full general relativity leads to non-linear destabilization.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for a set of tabulated equations of state from Annala et al. (2019) and Kurkela et al. (2014), then used the first-order formalism of Gondek et al. (1997) to compute the fundamental-mode frequencies and the Lagrangian radial displacement profiles for infinitesimal radial oscillations. They classified stability by checking both the sign of the squared fundamental-mode frequency and the magnitude of the relative radial displacement throughout the star.

## Key numbers

- The three representative equations of state from Kurkela et al. (2014) produce maximum squared speeds of sound of 0.95 (Case III), with Case III yielding fundamental-mode frequencies up to about 4.5 kHz.
- The critical energy density for the deconfinement transition in the speed-of-sound interpolation method of Annala et al. (2019) is approximately 400–700 MeV/fm^3.
- For Cases 3 and 4 from Annala et al. (2019), the relative radial displacement amplitude reaches values around 20–40 in the interior region near the core (r less than about R/2).
- The maximum mass of neutron stars is constrained to the range 2.1 to 2.3 solar masses from Rezzolla et al. (2017) and Ruiz et al. (2017).

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of neutron stars with quark matter cores beyond the simple static criterion (dM/d epsilon_c greater than or equal to 0) used in the constrained equation-of-state bands of Annala et al. (2019) and Kurkela et al. (2014). It builds on earlier radial oscillation studies of hybrid stars by Gupta et al. (2002) and Pereira et al. (2017), and uses the non-linear general-relativistic results of Gourgoulhon (1995) to interpret large oscillation amplitudes as dynamical instability. The findings enable future work to incorporate linear stability analysis as a systematic constraint in equation-of-state interpolations, and suggest that tidal deformabilities from gravitational-wave observations could be mapped to fundamental-mode frequencies for different neutron star masses.
