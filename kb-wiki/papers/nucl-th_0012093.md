# Signatures of the quark gluon plasma: a personal overview

> We first discuss, on a no-QGP basis, the two prominent indications of (a) enhanced strangeness production and of (b) anomalous $J/ψ$-suppression: We elaborate in particular on a recent idea of antihyperon production solely by multi-mesonic reactions. As a possible source for an enhanced dissociation of $c \bar{c}$ pairs we summarize the findings within the 'early'-comover absorption scenario of prehadronic string excitations. As an exotic candidate we then finally adress the stochastic formation of so called disoriented chiral condensates: An experimentally feasible DCC, if it does exist, has to be a rare event following an unusual and nontrivial distribution on an event by event basis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two prominent QGP signatures, enhanced strangeness production and anomalous J/ψ suppression, can be explained by hadronic mechanisms without invoking a deconfined phase, and that disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs), if they exist, must be rare events following a non-Poissonian distribution.

## Method

The author reviews multi-mesonic back-reactions (n pions + n_Y kaons ↔ antihyperon + nucleon) as a hadronic production mechanism for antihyperons, using a master equation with an equilibration timescale proportional to inverse baryon density. For J/ψ suppression, a microscopic transport calculation tracks c̄c pairs through the collision dynamics, including dissociation by nucleons (3–6 mb cross-section) and by prehadronic string excitations, where strings are treated as completely "black" for c̄c states with a string radius R_s as the single phenomenological parameter. For DCCs, stochastic Langevin equations for chiral order parameters in an expanding volume are solved to sample possible trajectories and compute pion number distributions.

## Key numbers

- Antihyperon equilibration timescale: 1–3 fm/c, assuming initial baryon density 1–2 times normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ and annihilation cross-section σ ≈ 50 mb.
- String tension σ ≈ 1 GeV/fm; for string radius R_s ≈ 0.3 fm, local color electric energy density ≈ 4 GeV/fm³.
- For p+U collisions with R_s = 0.4 fm, only 2% of J/ψ's are absorbed by strings; for central Pb+Pb, R_s ≈ 0.2–0.3 fm gives best fit to data, with 40% of absorbed J/ψ's dissociated by strings.
- For D=3 expansion, individual DCC fluctuations of 50–200 pions may occur, while average ⟨n_π⟩ is only 5–20 pions.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing debate on QGP signatures by offering hadronic alternatives to the deconfined-phase interpretation. For strangeness, it counters the QGP-based enhancement argument of Koch, Müller, and Rafelski (1986) by proposing multi-mesonic reactions as in Greiner and Leupold (2000). For J/ψ suppression, it builds on the Matsui and Satz (1986) screening proposal and the comover absorption scenario, specifically the "early"-comover string dissociation model of Geiss et al. For DCCs, it follows the original ideas of Anselm, Bjorken, and Rajagopal and Wilczek, and extends the author's own prior work with Xu (2000) on stochastic formation. The work enables future experimental searches at RHIC (e.g., STAR TPC) using higher-order factorial cumulants to identify rare DCC events.
