# Transverse momentum distribution of vector mesons produced in ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the transverse momentum distribution of vector mesons produced in ultraperipheral relativistic heavy ion collisions (UPCs). In UPCs there is no strong interaction between the nuclei and the vector mesons are produced in photon-nucleus collisions where the (quasireal) photon is emitted from the other nucleus. Exchanging the role of both ions leads to interference effects. A detailed study of the transverse momentum distribution which is determined by the transverse momentum of the emitted photon, the production process on the target and the interference effect is done. We study the total unrestricted cross section and those, where an additional electromagnetic excitation of one or both of the ions takes place in addition to the vector meson production, in the latter case small impact parameters are emphasized.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum distribution of vector mesons produced in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions is more complex than previously treated, particularly in the intermediate transverse momentum region, due to interference effects between the two possible photon-emission diagrams that depend sensitively on impact parameter.

## Method

The authors derive the vector meson production amplitude using a semiclassical approximation with impact-parameter-dependent amplitudes, factorizing the process into nuclear absorption, electromagnetic excitation, and vector meson production. They model the vector meson production via the vector dominance model (VDM) with a Gaussian elastic scattering amplitude, and compute the cross section by integrating over impact parameter with and without tagging conditions for electromagnetic excitation of the ions. An alternative derivation from eikonal/Glauber theory is also presented, showing equivalence in the sudden limit.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius used: R = 7 fm
- Electromagnetic excitation probability parameter: S ≈ 5.45 × 10⁻⁵ Z³ N A⁻²/³ fm²
- Form factor radius: R_γ ≈ 2.2 fm
- Vector meson scattering radius: R_V ≈ 2.2 fm
- Total cross section for ρ⁰ production at RHIC (Y=0): dσ/dY_ρ(RHIC) = 70 mb
- Total cross section for J/Ψ production at LHC (Y=0): dσ/dY_J/Ψ(LHC) = 0.75 mb
- Lorentz factor for RHIC Au-Au collisions: γ = 108
- Lorentz factor for LHC Pb-Pb collisions: γ = 3000

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson production in ultraperipheral collisions by Klein (1999) and Nystrand (2002), providing a more rigorous theoretical treatment of the transverse momentum distribution and interference effects. The results are directly applicable to analyzing experimental data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, and enable predictions for future LHC measurements of J/Ψ and Υ production. The formalism can be extended to more realistic descriptions beyond the Gaussian approximation for the elastic scattering amplitude.
