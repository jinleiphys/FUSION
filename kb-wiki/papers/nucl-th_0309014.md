# A model of binary collisions dependence of jet quenching in nuclear collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> We describe a model of jet quenching in nuclear collisions at RHIC energies. In the model, jet quenching is to be caused by the interruption of jet formation by nucleons arriving at the position of jet formation in a time shorter than the jet formation time. Our mechanism predicts suppression of high-pt spectra also in d+Au reactions.

## Key claim

The model proposes that jet quenching in nuclear collisions at RHIC energies is caused by the interruption of jet formation by subsequent nucleons arriving at the jet formation site in a time shorter than the jet formation time, based on the uncertainty principle; this mechanism predicts suppression of high-pt spectra also in d+Au reactions, which the authors acknowledge differs from the observed enhancement in d+Au data at intermediate pt.

## Method

The authors formulate a Glauber model in the nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass system with uniform nuclear density, where the yield of jets in nuclear collisions relative to nucleon-nucleon collisions is calculated by integrating over tube-on-tube collisions with a suppression factor. The suppression factor accounts for jet destruction by subsequent incoming nucleons using a time-dependent absorption cross-section sigma_a(pt,t,t') = sigma_0 * (1/(1+(pt*(t'-t))^2))^2, where t is the time of the hard interaction and t' is the time when the destroying nucleon arrives at the jet position. The absorption cross-section parameter sigma_0 is tuned to 8 mb, and the total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section is taken as 40 mb.

## Key numbers

- Mean-free path of an incident nucleon in the nucleon-nucleon CMS at RHIC: lambda approx 0.025 fm
- Limit on processes not influenced by the mechanism: Delta p_L greater than or approximately 8 GeV/c
- Nuclear density: rho_0 = 0.138 fm^{-3}
- Total inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section: sigma_nn = 40 mb = 4 fm^2
- Absorption cross-section parameter: sigma_0 = 8 mb = 0.8 fm^2
- In the old model (LPPT1), p_T0 = 8 GeV/c for collisions at RHIC
- Forbidding nucleons within a nucleus (at rest) to be closer than 1 fm makes the suppression weaker by 5-15%, depending on pt
- The new model's suppression factor F goes like exp(-const/p_T^2) for high pt, while the old model approaches exp(-const/pt^4)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Lietava et al., 2003) by introducing a time-dependent absorption cross-section that improves the Lorentz invariance of the formalism, while the previous version used a simpler pt-dependent cross-section. The model differs from the perturbative-QCD-improved parton model of Zhang and Papp (2002) and the parton saturation mechanism of Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2003), as those approaches do not explain the observed suppression pattern. The authors note that their model cannot describe the Cronin effect observed in d+Au data from PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, and BRAHMS (2003), but suggest it may become relevant for pt above approximately 9 GeV/c where the Cronin effect may cease; they plan to combine the Cronin effect with the present model in future work.
