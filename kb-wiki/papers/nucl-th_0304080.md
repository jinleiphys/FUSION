# Hadrons in Nuclei

> Changes of hadronic properties in dense nuclear matter as predicted by theory have usually been investigated by means of relativistic heavy-ion reactions. In this talk I show that observable consequences of such changes can also be seen in more elementary reactions on nuclei. Particular emphasis is put on a discussion of photonuclear reactions; examples are the dilepton production at $\approx 1$ GeV and the hadron production in nuclei at 10 - 20 GeV virtual photon energies. The observable effects are expected to be as large as in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and can be more directly related to the underlying hadronic changes.

# Hadrons in Nuclei

## Key claim
Observable consequences of in-medium changes of hadronic properties can be seen in elementary photonuclear reactions on normal nuclei, not only in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The expected sensitivity of dilepton spectra in photonuclear reactions at 1–2 GeV is as large as that in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the results can be more directly related to the underlying hadronic changes because the spectator matter remains close to equilibrium.

## Method
The author combines coherent initial-state interactions (leading to shadowing at photon energies above about 1 GeV) with incoherent final-state interactions described by a coupled-channel transport theory. Vector mesons are produced and transported with their correct spectral functions, rather than being produced at pole mass with spectral functions folded in only at decay. For hadron production at high energies (10–20 GeV), a novel expression for incoherent particle production on the nucleus is derived to separate coherent initial-state and incoherent final-state interactions.

## Key numbers
- Dilepton production in photonuclear reactions is studied at photon energies of 1–2 GeV on lead-208.
- In relativistic heavy-ion reactions at CERES (40 AGeV Pb+Au), about 1/2 of all dileptons come from densities lower than 2 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is nuclear matter equilibrium density).
- For hadron production at HERMES, photon energies are of order 10–20 GeV with Q² ≈ 2 GeV.
- Hadron multiplicities can be described only with formation times greater than approximately 0.3 fm; larger formation times yield nearly identical curves due to finite target nucleus size.
- Calculations for leading hadron interactions during formation use a cross section of 0.25 σ_h, where σ_h is the normal hadronic interaction cross section.

## Context
This work extends the study of in-medium vector meson changes from heavy-ion reactions (e.g., the CERES experiment, which found a dilepton excess in the 300–700 MeV invariant mass range) to photonuclear reactions on normal nuclei. The author notes that explanations of the CERES excess remain ambiguous, with both dropping-mass and modified-spectral-function models fitting the data, and even free-radiation calculations being compatible within uncertainties (Koch). The photonuclear approach provides a cleaner testing ground because the nuclear matter is at rest and in equilibrium, unlike the far-from-equilibrium conditions in heavy-ion collisions. This work enables direct experimental tests of in-medium hadron changes at facilities such as JLAB (experiment underway, Weygand) and provides a hadronic baseline for interpreting claims of quark-gluon plasma signals in heavy-ion data.
