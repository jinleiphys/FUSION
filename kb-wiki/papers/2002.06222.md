# Projective Cooling for the transverse Ising model

> We demonstrate the feasibility of ground state preparation for the transverse Ising model using projective cooling, and show that the algorithm can effectively construct the ground state in the disordered (paramagnetic) phase. On the other hand, significant temperature effects are encountered in the ordered (ferromagnetic) phase requiring larger lattices to accurately simulate.

## Key claim
Projective cooling can effectively prepare the ground state of the transverse Ising model in the disordered (paramagnetic) phase, but significant thermal effects appear in the ordered (ferromagnetic) phase, requiring larger lattices to accurately simulate.

## Method
The projective cooling algorithm was applied to the transverse Ising model on one-dimensional lattices. A small region \(R_s\) containing \(N_s\) sites was embedded symmetrically within a larger system \(R_b\) containing \(N_b\) sites; an initial state with all spins up was time-evolved under the larger Hamiltonian, then excitations outside \(R_s\) were projected away. The time evolution operator was implemented using a Suzuki-Trotter approximation with time step \(\delta t = 0.01 / J\). Two different Hamiltonian formulations were used depending on the phase: in the disordered phase (\(J < h_T\)) the transverse field was diagonal, while in the ordered phase (\(J > h_T\)) the nearest-neighbor coupling was diagonal.

## Key numbers
- \(h_T = 1\) throughout, placing the phase transition at \(J = 1\).
- The longitudinal field was set to \(h = N_s^{-15/8}\) to ensure perturbative effects.
- \(N_b\) ranged from 6 to 14 sites; \(N_s\) ranged from 4 to 9 sites.
- The Suzuki-Trotter time step was \(\delta t = 0.01 / J\).
- For the disordered phase at \(J=0.4\) and \(N_s=5\), plateaus in energy per site were observed for \(N_b = 11\) and \(13\), but not for \(N_b = 7\).
- For the ordered phase at \(J=1.4\) and \(N_s=6\), the minimum energy density approached the exact value as \(N_b\) increased.
- Infinite volume extrapolation used the ansatz \(E(N_b) = A e^{-B N_b} + E_{\infty}\); results showed excellent overlap with exact diagonalization away from \(J=1\), with substantial deviation near the phase transition when \(N_s > 5\).
- Finite-size scaling of magnetic susceptibility broke down for ratios \(N_s/N_b > 5/9\).

## Context
This work extends the projective cooling algorithm introduced by Lee (2019), which was previously applied only to models conserving particle number, to the transverse Ising model, a system with effective pair creation and annihilation. The transverse Ising model was chosen as a stepping stone toward lattice computations for QCD, following the roadmap used in lattice gauge theory (RevModPhys.55.775, RevModPhys.51.659). The results enable future applications to bound state extraction in attractive interacting models such as Ising-like models with both \(\sigma^z\sigma^z\) and \(\sigma^x\sigma^x\) interactions, as well as extensions to the Schwinger or O(N) models.
