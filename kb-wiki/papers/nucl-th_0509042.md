# Can the meson cloud explain the nucleon strangeness?

> We use the meson cloud model, including the kaon and the $K^*$ contributions, to estimate the electric and magnetic strange form factors of the nucleon. We compare our results with the recent measurements of the strange quark contribution to parity-violating asymmetries in the forward G0 electron-proton scattering experiment. We conclude that it is not possible to explain the data using this model.

## Key claim

The meson cloud model, including both kaon and K* contributions, cannot explain the G0 Collaboration data for the combination of strange form factors \(G_E^s + \eta G_M^s\); the model yields a negative and decreasing value with \(Q^2\) while the data are consistent with zero, and increasing the K* cutoff worsens the agreement.

## Method

The authors computed the strange electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon using a hadronic one-loop model with effective Lagrangians for the KNY and K*NY vertices (Y = Lambda, Sigma). They included kaon loops, K* loops, and K/K* transition contributions, regulated by monopole form factors with cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_K = 0.9\) GeV and \(\Lambda_{K^*}\) varied between 0.9 and 1.1 GeV, and compared the resulting combination \(G_E^s + \eta G_M^s\) to the parity-violating asymmetry data from the G0 experiment at TJNAF over \(0.12 \leq Q^2 \leq 1.0\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The kaon mass used is \(m_K = 495\) MeV and the K* mass is \(m_{K^*} = 895\) MeV.
- At \(Q^2 \sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) with \(\Lambda_K = \Lambda_{K^*} = 0.9\) GeV, the model gives \(G_E^s = 0.0053\) and \(G_M^s = -0.11\).
- Using an adjusted coupling \(F_{KK^*}^{(s)}(0) = 8.0\) and \(\Lambda_{K^*} = 1.1\) GeV, the model yields at \(Q^2 \sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\): \(G_E^s = -0.023\) and \(G_M^s = 0.24\).
- The HAPPEX Collaboration extracted \(G_E^s = -0.01 \pm 0.03\) and \(G_M^s = 0.55 \pm 0.28\) at \(Q^2 \sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\); the SAMPLE result for \(G_M^s\) at the same \(Q^2\) is \(0.37 \pm 0.22\).

## Context

This work extends earlier meson cloud model calculations of nucleon strangeness (Barz et al., 1998; Forkel, Navarra, and Nielsen, 2000) by confronting them with the new G0 Collaboration data (Aniol et al., 2005). The results contrast with the light-cone chiral cloud model of Melnitchouk and Malheiro (1999), which gives a positive value for the combination \(G_E^s + \eta G_M^s\). The paper highlights that measuring the individual electric and magnetic strange form factors, rather than only their combination, is essential for understanding the nucleon's strangeness content.
