# Mainz "measurement" of the $E2/M1$ ratio in the $N-Δ$ transition

> We comment on the recent Mainz "measurement" (PRL78 (1997) 606) of the $E2/M1$ ratio in the $N-Δ$ electromagnetic transition. Analyzing the Mainz data using an effective Lagrangian approach, we find this ratio to be (-3.19$\pm$0.24)%.

## Key claim

The E2/M1 ratio in the N-Δ transition is not directly measured by Beck et al. (1997) but is an inferred quantity requiring theoretical modeling; using an effective Lagrangian approach without the approximations of Beck et al. yields a substantially different value of -(3.19±0.24)% rather than the reported -(2.5±0.2±0.2)%.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the Mainz data on differential cross sections and polarized photon asymmetries for the reaction γp → pπ⁰ in the energy region 270 to 420 MeV, using an effective Lagrangian approach that retains partial waves beyond s and p and avoids the approximations made by Beck et al. (1997). They corrected an error in the expression for the coefficient A∥ and showed that identifying the quantity R = C∥/(12A∥) with the E2/M1 ratio REM is imprecise because it requires neglecting the M1−, E0+, and isospin 1/2 components of M1+ and E1+, as well as neglecting E1+ in A∥ entirely.

## Key numbers

- REM = -(3.19±0.24)% at the K-matrix pole (338.4±0.5 MeV), extracted from the Mainz data using the effective Lagrangian approach.
- M1 = 282.5±1.3 and E2 = -9.00±0.66, both in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², at the K-matrix pole.
- At 340 MeV, REM = -(3.09±0.24)% from the same analysis.
- The quantity R at 340 MeV is -(2.69±0.17)%, consistent with the result of Beck et al. (1997).
- Correcting the Beck et al. value of R for the isospin 3/2 piece of the E1+ amplitude gives REM ≈ -(2.9±0.23)%.

## Context

This work comments on the analysis of Beck et al. (1997) from the Mainz facility, which reported a precise measurement of the E2/M1 ratio in the N-Δ transition, and also compares with earlier data from the Brookhaven LEGS facility (Andy et al.). The authors show that the approximations used by Beck et al. (1997) introduce a systematic error in the extracted ratio, and that their own effective Lagrangian approach yields a value consistent with the LEGS data when properly analyzed. This enables a more accurate determination of the N-Δ transition multipoles and clarifies the role of isospin 1/2 contributions and neglected multipoles in such extractions.
