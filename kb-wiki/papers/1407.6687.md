# Geometrical Scaling and the Dependence of the Average Transverse Momentum on the Multiplicity and Energy for the ALICE Experiment

> We review the recent ALICE data on charged particle multiplicity in p-p collisions, and show that it exhibits Geometrical Scaling (GS) with energy dependence given with characteristic exponent $λ=0.22$. Next, starting from the GS hypothesis and using results of the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, we calculate $< p_{\text{T}}>$ as a function $N_{\rm ch}$ including dependence on the scattering energy $W$. We show that $< p_{\text{T}}>$ both in p-p and p-Pb collisions scales in terms of scaling variable $(W/W_{0})^{λ/(2+λ)}% \sqrt{N_{\mathrm{ch}}/S_{\bot}}$ where $S_{\bot}$ is multiplicity dependent interaction area in the transverse plane. Furthermore, we discuss how the behavior of the interaction radius $R$ at large multiplicities affects the mean $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ dependence on $N_{\rm ch}$, and make a prediction that $< p_{\text{T}}>$ at high multiplicity should reach an energy independent limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ALICE p-p and p-Pb data on average transverse momentum as a function of charged particle multiplicity exhibit geometrical scaling, provided one includes an energy-dependent rescaling of the interaction radius; the scaling is not perfect at the highest multiplicities, where the interaction radius is predicted to saturate to an energy-independent limit.

## Method

The authors first test geometrical scaling (GS) of charged particle multiplicity spectra in p-p collisions at 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV by constructing ratios of spectra at different energies as functions of the scaling variable τ = pT²/Qs²(x) and minimizing χ² to extract the exponent λ. Using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, they then derive an expression for the mean transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ as a function of Nch and scattering energy W, incorporating a multiplicity-dependent interaction radius R(Nch) from Bzdak (2013). They fit the 7 TeV p-p data to obtain parameters α, β, and γ, then predict ⟨pT⟩ at other energies using the energy scaling factor (W/W0)^(λ/(2+λ)) applied to the radius.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic exponent λ = 0.22 for geometrical scaling of ALICE p-p multiplicity spectra (determined by χ² minimization over the largest τ interval).
- λ/(2+λ) ≈ 0.099, giving the power-law energy dependence of the average saturation scale Q̅s(W) ∝ W^0.099.
- Fit parameters for p-p at 7 TeV: α = 0.268 GeV, β = 0.153 GeV·fm, γ = 1.138.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV: α_PbPb = 0.43 GeV, β_PbPb = 0.11 GeV.
- Interaction radius parametrization for p-p at 7 TeV: R_pp(x) = 0.387 + 0.0335x + 0.274x² - 0.0542x³ [fm] for x < 3.4, saturating at 1.538 fm for x ≥ 3.4, where x = (γ Nch)^(1/3).
- Interaction radius parametrization for p-Pb at 5.02 TeV: R_pPb(x) = 0.21 + 0.47x [fm] for x < 3.5, with saturation at 2.394 fm for x ≥ 5.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical scaling hypothesis, previously applied to DIS at HERA (Stasto, Golec-Biernat, Kwiecinski, 2000) and to CMS p-p data (McLerran and Praszalowicz, 2010), to the ALICE p-p and p-Pb data. It contradicts the earlier ALICE claim (Abelev, 2013) that scaling breaks down at larger multiplicities, by showing that including the energy dependence of the interaction radius restores scaling. The results enable predictions for ⟨pT⟩ at higher LHC energies and for p-Pb collisions, and suggest that the interaction radius extracted from such data can provide nontrivial information on the geometry of high-energy hadronic collisions.
