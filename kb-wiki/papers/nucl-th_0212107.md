# Incoherent rho^0 electroproduction off nuclei

> In the present paper we investigate incoherent rho^0 electroproduction off complex nuclei. We derive a novel, simple expression for the incoherent electroproduction cross section in which one can clearly separate the final state interactions of the reaction products from the 'initial state interactions' of the photon that give rise to nuclear shadowing. In the special case of purely absorptive final state interactions we deduce from our expression the known Glauber result. A more realistic treatment of the final state interactions within a transport model is then used to compare our predictions with experimental data from the HERMES experiment.

# Incoherent $\rho^0$ electroproduction off nuclei

## Key claim

The paper derives a novel expression for the incoherent electroproduction cross section that separates final state interactions from the initial state interactions (shadowing) of the photon, and finds that for $^{14}$N the HERMES data is compatible with the assumption that the diffractively produced $\rho^0$ starts interacting with a hadronic cross section right after production, with no evidence for a finite formation time or color transparency in this kinematic regime.

## Method

The authors use a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to treat the final state interactions of reaction products in a coupled-channel framework, accounting for Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and finite lifetimes of unstable particles. The initial state interactions of the photon (shadowing) are described via a vector meson dominance (VMD) approach where the photon fluctuates into vector meson components that propagate through the nucleus before interacting, with the PYTHIA event generator used to model the elementary $\gamma^*N$ interaction. The transparency ratio $T_A = \sigma_{\gamma^*A\rightarrow hX} / (A\sigma_{\gamma^*N\rightarrow hX})$ is calculated for exclusive $\rho^0$ production off $^{14}$N and $^{84}$Kr and compared with HERMES experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: $\nu \approx 10-20$ GeV, photon virtuality $Q^2 \approx 0.5-5$ GeV$^2$ (HERMES kinematic regime)
- Invariant mass $W = 5$ GeV used for PYTHIA cross section decomposition
- $\rho^0 N$ total cross section: $\sigma_{\rho^0} = 25$ mb; elastic part: $\sigma_{el} = 3$ mb
- Four-momentum transfer cut: $|t-t_{max}| < 0.4$ GeV$^2$ for hydrogen; $|t-t_{max}| > 0.09$ GeV$^2$ for nuclear targets (to remove coherent production)
- Exclusivity measure: $\Delta E < 0.4$ GeV for hydrogen; $-2$ GeV $< \Delta E < 0.6$ GeV for nuclear targets
- Two-body correlation parameter: $q_c = 0.78$ GeV
- At $|t-t_{max}| \approx 0.05$ GeV$^2$, the differential cross section is about 25% smaller when including Fermi motion and Pauli blocking compared to calculations without them
- For $^{84}$Kr, about 10% of the difference between transport and Glauber calculations arises from $\rho^0$ decay into two pions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for shadowing in photoproduction (Falter 2002) to electron-induced reactions, and provides a derivation that shows equivalence to the Glauber result of Huefner et al. (1996) in the limit of purely absorptive final state interactions. The paper contrasts with Kopeliovich et al. (2001), who used a light-cone QCD formalism and found potential for color transparency effects; the present authors find no evidence for color transparency in the $^{14}$N data, attributing this to the specific correlation between $Q^2$ and coherence length in the HERMES data. The results enable future searches for color transparency in $^{84}$Kr data, where the authors predict deviations from simple Glauber theory due to finite $\rho^0$ lifetime and elastic scattering out of the kinematic acceptance window.
