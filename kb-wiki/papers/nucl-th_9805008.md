# About the Equivalence of Cut-off and Renormalised Effective Field Theories

> The equivalence of subtractively renormalized and cut-off effective field theories is demonstrated for the example of very low energy effective field theory for the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

# About the Equivalence of Cut-off and Renormalised Effective Field Theories

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that cut-off effective field theory can reproduce the scattering amplitude calculated using subtractively renormalised theory up to the order of accuracy of the considered approximation, provided the cut-off parameter is taken of the order of the mass of the lightest particle integrated out. The equivalence holds only within the range of momenta for which Weinberg's power counting is valid, and the difference between the two approaches is of higher order in the small expansion parameter.

## Method

The author considers a very low energy effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering in the ¹S₀ channel, where pions have been integrated out. The effective Lagrangian contains contact interactions with increasing numbers of derivatives. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved for the T-matrix using both subtractive renormalisation (with off-mass-shell subtractions at p² = -μ²) and sharp cut-off regularisation (with cut-off parameter l). The equivalence is demonstrated analytically at p² order and p⁴ order by matching both approaches to the effective range expansion and showing that the resulting inverse T-matrices are equal up to the included order. Numerical phase shifts are compared for various values of the subtraction point μ and cut-off parameter l.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length a = -1/(8.4 MeV) and effective range rₑ = 0.0137 MeV⁻¹ are used as input parameters for numerical calculations.
- For p² order with cut-off l = 130 MeV and μ = 100 MeV, cut-off phase shifts agree with subtractively renormalised phase shifts for momenta up to approximately 80 MeV.
- For p² order with cut-off l = 130 MeV, the cut-off coupling constants are found to be C̄ ≈ -1/(78.2 MeV)² and C̄₂ ≈ 1/(155.5 MeV)⁴.
- For p⁴ order with cut-off l = 130 MeV, the cut-off coupling constants are C̄ ≈ -1/(76.8 MeV)², C̄₂ ≈ 1/(135.2 MeV)⁴, and B̄₁ ≈ -1/(124.6 MeV)⁶.
- For cut-offs around m_π (approximately 140 MeV), phase shifts are cut-off independent up to momenta of about 50 MeV at p² order and about 60 MeV at p⁴ order.

## Context

This work addresses the ongoing debate about whether cut-off regularisation in effective field theories for nucleon-nucleon scattering is systematic and consistent, a question raised by Phillips et al. (1997), Epelbaum et al. (1997), and others. The paper extends the approach of Weinberg (1990, 1991) for chiral power counting and builds on the subtractive renormalisation method used by Kaplan et al. (1996, 1997) with Power Divergent Subtractions, while noting that the scheme presented here differs from that approach when pions are included explicitly. The demonstration that cut-off theory can reproduce subtractively renormalised results up to the order of approximation enables the use of cut-off regularisation in practical calculations, following the original cut-off chiral perturbation theory work of Ordonez et al. (1994), and provides a justification for the systematic character of such calculations.
