# Photon induced K$Λ$ production on the proton in the low energy region

> The associated photoproduction of $KΛ$ from the proton in the low energy region is studied using an isobar model in which the non-resonant contributions are obtained from the non-linear sigma model with chiral SU(3) symmetry which predicts, in a natural way, the contact term with its coupling strength along with the coupling strengths of the various Born terms predicted by the non-linear sigma model. The present model is an extension of the non-linear sigma model with chiral SU(2) symmetry, used earlier to study the photo, electro, and neutrino productions of pions. In the resonance sector, the contributions from the well established nucleon resonances ($R$) in the $s$ channel, the hyperon resonances ($Y^{*}$) in the $u$ channel, and the kaon resonances ($K^{*}$ and $K_{1}$) in the $t$ channel having spin $\le \frac{3}{2}$ and mass $<2$ GeV with a significant branching ratio in $KΛ$ decay mode, have been considered. The strong and electromagnetic couplings of the $s$ channel nucleon resonances are taken from experiments while the couplings for the resonances in the $t$ and $u$ channels are fitted to reproduce the current data on the associated photoproduction of $KΛ$ in this energy region. The numerical results are presented for the total and differential cross sections and are compared with the available experimental data from CLAS and SAPHIR as well as with some of the recent theoretical models.

## Key claim

The authors present an isobar model for photon-induced \(K\Lambda\) production on the proton where the non-resonant contributions are derived from the non-linear sigma model with chiral SU(3) symmetry, which predicts the contact term and Born-term coupling strengths without free parameters, while the couplings for \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel resonances are fitted to reproduce current experimental data; the model is intended as a benchmark for future weak production of strange particles by (anti)neutrinos, and the total cross sections at higher energies (\(W > 1.72\) GeV) as well as angular distributions are predictions of the model rather than fits.

## Method

The hadronic current is separated into background terms (non-resonant \(s\), \(t\), \(u\) channel Born diagrams, a contact term, and \(t\)-channel kaon resonances \(K^*(892)\) and \(K_1(1270)\) plus \(u\)-channel hyperon resonances \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1800)\)) and resonance terms from \(s\)-channel nucleon resonances with spin \(\le 3/2\) and mass \(<2\) GeV having a branching ratio \(>4-5\%\) in \(K\Lambda\) decay. The non-resonant terms are obtained from the non-linear sigma model with chiral SU(3) symmetry using the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and axial couplings \(D=0.804\), \(F=0.463\); the strong and electromagnetic couplings of the \(s\)-channel nucleon resonances are taken from experimental helicity amplitudes and PDG partial decay widths, while the couplings of the \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel resonances are fitted to reproduce the CLAS and SAPHIR data. A common dipole form factor with cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_B\) is used for all background vertices, and a separate common cut-off \(\Lambda_R\) for the \(s\)-channel resonance vertices; the effect of unitarity is examined using energy-dependent decay widths of the nucleon resonances.

## Key numbers

* The threshold for \(K\Lambda\) production is 1.61 GeV (photon energy in the laboratory frame).
* The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
* The axial vector coupling constants for the baryon octet are \(D = 0.804\) and \(F = 0.463\).
* The fine-structure constant is taken as \(\alpha = 1/137\).
* The nucleon resonances included in the \(s\) channel have masses in the range 1.6–1.9 GeV and spin \(\le 3/2\); specifically, the resonances are \(S_{11}(1650)\), \(P_{11}(1710)\), \(P_{13}(1720)\), \(P_{11}(1880)\), \(S_{11}(1895)\), and \(P_{13}(1900)\).
* The \(t\)-channel kaon resonances are \(K^*(892)\) and \(K_1(1270)\); the \(u\)-channel hyperon resonances are \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1800)\).
* The model results are compared with experimental data from SAPHIR (1998, 2004), CLAS (2006, 2010), and MAMI-C, and with theoretical models including Saclay-Lyon (SL), Kaon-MAID (KM), Ghent, BS1, BS3, Bonn-Gatchina, Bonn-Julich, and KSU.

## Context

This work extends the non-linear sigma model with chiral SU(2) symmetry, previously used for photo-, electro-, and neutrino-induced pion production (Hernandez 2007, Alam 2015), to chiral SU(3) symmetry for kaon-hyperon photoproduction. It differs from earlier isobar models such as Saclay-Lyon (SL, SLA), Kaon-MAID (KM2), Ghent (Jan01A), BS1 (Skoupil 2016), and BS3 (Skoupil 2018) by using a chiral SU(3) framework that predicts the contact term and Born couplings without free parameters, and by fitting only the \(t\)- and \(u\)-channel resonance couplings. The model is intended to provide a benchmark for calculating weak production of strange particles by (anti)neutrinos in the \(\Delta S=0\) sector, complementing current efforts to model neutrino-nucleon cross sections in the few-GeV region for neutrino oscillation experiments.
