# Isospin dependence of projectile-like fragment production at intermediate energies

> The cross sections of fragments produced in 140 $A$ MeV $^{40,48}$Ca + $^9$Be and $^{58,64}$Ni + $^9$Be reactions are calculated by the statistical abration-ablation(SAA) model and compared to the experimental results measured at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL) at Michigan State University. The fragment isotopic and isotonic cross section distributions of $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni, $^{40}$Ca and $^{58}$Ni, and $^{48}$Ca and $^{64}$Ni are compared and the isospin dependence of the projectile fragmentation is studied. It is found that the isospin dependence decreases and disappears in the central collisions. The shapes of the fragment isotopic and isotonic cross section distributions are found to be very similar for symmetric projectile nuclei. The shapes of the fragment isotopic and isotonic distributions of different asymmetric projectiles produced in peripheral reactions are found very similar. The similarity of the distributions are related to the similar proton and neutron density distributions inside the nucleus in framework of the SAA model.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of projectile-like fragment production decreases and disappears in central collisions, while the shapes of fragment isotopic and isotonic cross section distributions are very similar for symmetric projectile nuclei and also for different asymmetric projectiles produced in peripheral reactions, with these similarities related to similar proton and neutron density distributions inside the nucleus in the framework of the statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model.

## Method

The authors calculated fragment cross sections for 140 A MeV 40,48Ca + 9Be and 58,64Ni + 9Be reactions using the statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model, which describes peripheral nuclear collisions as a two-step process: first, a Glauber-type abrasion stage where nucleons in the overlapping region between projectile and target interact, followed by an evaporation stage where the excited spectator deexcites via light particle emission. The calculated fragment isotopic and isotonic cross section distributions were compared to experimental data measured at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL) at Michigan State University, with comparisons made between 40Ca and 48Ca, 58Ni and 64Ni, 40Ca and 58Ni, and 48Ca and 64Ni to study the isospin dependence.

## Key numbers

* Fragment cross sections were calculated for reactions at 140 A MeV for four projectile-target systems: 40Ca + 9Be, 48Ca + 9Be, 58Ni + 9Be, and 64Ni + 9Be.
* The excitation energy of the projectile spectator was estimated as E* = 13.3 <ΔA(b)> MeV, where 13.3 is the mean excitation energy per abraded nucleon.
* For 58Ni (N/Z = 1.071) and 64Ni (N/Z = 1.286), the fragment isotopic and isotonic distributions overlap when ΔZ of isotopes and ΔN of isotones are more than 9, providing direct evidence of the disappearance of isospin dependence.
* For 48Ca (N/Z = 1.4) and 64Ni (N/Z = 1.286), the ΔN < 3 fragment isotonic distributions of 48Ca and 64Ni overlap, revealing similar proton density distributions in the surface regions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Fang et al. (2000) on the isospin effect in projectile fragmentation at 60 A MeV for 16,18O + 9Be, 36,40Ar + 9Be, and 40,48Ca + 9Be reactions, where a shift in isotopic distribution peak positions was observed. The study uses the comprehensive fragment cross section data from Mocko et al. (2006, 2008) for 40Ca, 48Ca, 58Ni, and 64Ni projectiles at 140 A MeV, which had previously been analyzed with the EPAX code, the HIPSE model, and the AMD model. The findings enable estimation of fragment production in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions based on the similarity of fragment isotopic and isotonic distributions for nuclei with similar proton and neutron density distributions.
