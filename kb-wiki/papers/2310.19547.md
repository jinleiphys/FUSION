# Expansion of one-, two- and three-body matrix elements on a generic spherical basis for nuclear ab initio calculations

> Ab initio studies of atomic nuclei are based on Hamiltonians including one-, two- and three-body operators with very complicated structures. Traditionally, matrix elements of such operators are expanded on a Harmonic Oscillator single-particle basis, which allows for a simple separation of the center-of-mass motion from the intrinsic one. A few recent investigations have showed that the use of different single-particle bases can bring significant advantages to numerical nuclear structure computations. In this work, the complete analytical expression of the Hamiltonian matrix elements expanded on a generic spherical basis is presented for the first time. This will allow systematic studies aimed at the determination of optimal nuclear bases.

## Key claim

This paper presents, for the first time, the complete analytical expressions for one-, two-, and three-body Hamiltonian matrix elements expanded on a generic spherical single-particle basis, not limited to the Harmonic Oscillator basis. The authors provide the formalism using Wong-Clement coefficients in place of Moshinsky brackets, enabling systematic studies of optimal nuclear bases for ab initio calculations, though the work is purely formal and does not test any specific alternative basis.

## Method

The authors derive the matrix elements of the intrinsic nuclear Hamiltonian (kinetic energy, two-body potential, and three-body potential) in a generic spherical single-particle basis with spin and isospin degrees of freedom, using J-coupled T-decoupled scheme. The transformation from Jacobi-coordinate matrix elements to the generic basis is accomplished through Wong-Clement coefficients, which generalize the Moshinsky brackets used for Harmonic Oscillator bases. The kinetic energy center-of-mass correction is expressed in both momentum-space and coordinate-space formulations, with the two-body operator \(\vec{p}_1\cdot\vec{p}_2\) factorized into products of one-body reduced matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic kinetic energy is expressed as \(T = \frac{\hat{A}-1}{\hat{A}}\sum_i\frac{\vec{p}_i^2}{2m_N} - \frac{1}{m_N\hat{A}}\sum_{i<j}\vec{p}_i\cdot\vec{p}_j\), where \(\hat{A}\) is the particle-number operator and \(m_N\) is the average nucleon mass.
- The one-body kinetic energy matrix element in momentum space is \(t_{n_a n_b}^{l \tau} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_N} \int_0^{+\infty} dp\,p^4\, \tilde\phi_{n_a l \tau}(p) \, \tilde\phi_{n_b l \tau}(p)\).
- The reduced matrix element for the spherical harmonic \(Y_1\) acting on spin-1/2 fermions is \(\braket{(l_a s_a) j_a || Y_1 || (l_c s_c) j_c} \equiv \sqrt{\frac{3}{4\pi}} \frac{\hat{j}_c}{\hat{j}_a} (-1)^{j_a-j_c+1}\braket{j_c\frac{1}{2}10 | j_a \frac{1}{2}}\pi(l_c, 1, l_a)\), where \(\pi(l_1,l_2,\dots) \equiv \frac{1}{2}[1+(-1)^{l_1+l_2+\dots}]\) enforces parity constraints.
- The two-body matrix element of \(\vec{p}_1\cdot\vec{p}_2\) in J-coupled scheme is \(\braket{ab; J | \vec{p}_1\cdot\vec{p}_2 | cd; J} = (-1)^{j_b+j_c+J}\, \hat{j}_a \hat{j}_b \, \begin{Bmatrix} j_a & j_b & J\\ j_d & j_c & 1 \end{Bmatrix} \braket{a||\vec{p}_1||c}\braket{b||\vec{p}_2||d}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Moshinsky (1959) for Harmonic Oscillator bases to generic spherical bases using Wong-Clement coefficients (1969), building on recent investigations showing advantages of alternative bases for nuclear structure computations. The paper provides the necessary analytical framework to systematically study bases beyond the HO, such as the Optimised Reference State (OpRS) basis used in SCGF methods (Barbieri, 2009; Barbieri, 2022) and natural orbitals (NAT) from correlated density matrices, enabling future work on optimal basis selection for weakly bound nuclei and neutron-rich exotic systems where HO wave functions have incorrect asymptotic behavior.
