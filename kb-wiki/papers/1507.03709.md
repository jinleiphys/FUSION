# Microscopic derivation of the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian for shape coexistence/mixing dynamics

> Assuming that the time-evolution of the self-consistent mean field is determined by five pairs of collective coordinate and collective momentum, we microscopically derive the collective Hamiltonian for low-frequency quadrupole modes of excitation. We show that the five-dimensional collective Schrödinger equation is capable of describing large-amplitude quadrupole shape dynamics seen as shape coexistence/mixing phenomena. We focus on basic ideas and recent advances of the approaches based on the time-dependent mean-field theory, but relations to other time-independent approaches are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the five-dimensional (5D) quadrupole collective Hamiltonian for describing large-amplitude shape dynamics (shape coexistence/mixing) can be microscopically derived from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) theory, but only under the assumption that the time-evolution of the self-consistent mean field is determined by five pairs of collective coordinate and collective momentum, and with the practical approximation of solving the local QRPA (LQRPA) equations rather than the fully self-consistent adiabatic SCC equations.

## Method

The authors start from the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, which solves the TDHFB equations by expanding in collective momenta up to second order, yielding moving-frame HFB and QRPA equations at every point of the collective coordinate. For practical derivation of the 5D quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, they employ the local QRPA (LQRPA) approximation, which solves the constrained HFB equation for quadrupole deformation parameters (β,γ) and then solves the moving-frame QRPA equations without curvature terms to obtain the vibrational inertial masses and moments of inertia. The resulting collective Hamiltonian is quantized using the Pauli prescription in curvilinear coordinates, and the collective Schrödinger equation is solved for the vibrational wave functions expanded in terms of the Wigner D-functions.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation parameters are defined by β cos γ = η D⁺₂₀ and (1/√2) β sin γ = η D⁺₂₂, where η is a scaling factor.
- The moments of inertia are given by J_k(β,γ) = 4β² D_k(β,γ) sin²(γ - 2πk/3) for k = 1, 2, 3.
- The vibrational kinetic energy operator in the quantized form contains the factor -ħ²/2√(WR) where W = D_ββ D_γγ - D_βγ² and R = D₁ D₂ D₃.
- The rotational wave functions include the normalization factor √[(2I+1)/(16π²(1+δ_K0))].

## Context

This work extends the concepts of the random-phase approximation (RPA) and quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) for small-amplitude vibrations to large-amplitude collective motion (LACM) by building on the self-consistent collective coordinate (SCC) method developed by Marumori (1977) and Rowe and Bassermann (1976). The adiabatic SCC (ASCC) method was previously proposed by Matsuo, Nakatsukasa, and Matsuyanagi (2000) for treating shape coexistence where multiple local minima compete. The paper enables microscopic calculations of shape coexistence/mixing phenomena across the nuclear chart, going beyond the constrained HFB approach by self-consistently determining the optimal collective operators at each deformation point, and it provides a framework to study quantum phase transitions from spherical to deformed shapes.
