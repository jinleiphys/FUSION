# Wobbling motion in the multi-bands crossing region

> The backbending in the A=180 mass region is expected to be caused by multi-bands crossing between low-K (g- and s-bands) and high-K bands. % We analyze a mechanism of coupling of these bands in terms of a dynamical treatment for nuclear rotations, i.e., the wobbling motion. The wobbling states are produced through the generator coordinate method after angular momentum projection, in which the intrinsic states are constructed through the 2d-cranked HFB calculations.

## Key claim

The backbending in the A=180 mass region is expected to be caused by multi-bands crossing between low-K (g- and s-bands) and high-K bands, and the authors analyze this coupling mechanism through the wobbling motion as a dynamical treatment of nuclear rotations, using the generator coordinate method after angular momentum projection on 2d-cranked HFB states.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional cranked HFB (2d-CHFB) calculations for 182Os to generate intrinsic states with different tilt angles of the rotation axis, representing both principal-axis rotation (PAR) for low-K bands and tilted-axis rotation (TAR) for high-K bands. They then applied exact angular momentum projection (AMP) to these 2d-CHFB states and used the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the tilt angle as the generator coordinate, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to construct wobbling states as superpositions of projected states with different tilt angles. The calculations used five angular points (θ = 0°, ±7°, and ±20°) with a fixed angular momentum constraint of J = 14ħ.

## Key numbers

- The backbending region occurs at critical spin Ic ≈ 14ħ
- The intrinsic states of 182,184Os have prolate shape with γ < 3° and β ≈ 0.275 in the backbending region
- The integration limit for tilt angles was set to |θmax| = 20°
- The net dimension of the Hill-Wheeler equation was {5 × (2I+1)} for a given angular momentum I
- The cut-off dimension was set to ncut = 11 for all states except the I = 17 yrare state (ncut = 10)
- The calculated signature splitting was roughly 500 keV while the experimental value is about 100 keV
- The high-K bands begin with even spin (I = 8ħ or 10ħ)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of backbending beyond the two-band crossing (g- and s-bands) mechanism established by Stephens and Simon (1972) for light rare-earth nuclei, addressing the three-band crossing (g-, s-, and t-bands) observed in the A=180 mass region by Walker et al. (1993) and others. The authors improve upon their previous GCM calculations without angular momentum projection (Horibata, Onishi, and Oi, 1995) which suffered from convergence problems in the Hill-Wheeler equation, and they show that AMP provides stabilization of eigenvalues. The work enables future studies of the complete three-band crossing by using CHFB states with different J values, and identifies the need for number projection to eliminate "slope" artifacts in the energy spectrum.
