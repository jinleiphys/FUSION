# Forces inside a strongly-coupled scalar nucleon

> We investigate the gravitational form factors of a strongly coupled scalar theory that mimic the interaction between the nucleon and the pion. The non-perturbative calculation is based on the light-front Hamiltonian formalism. We renormalize the energy-momentum tensor with a Fock sector dependent scheme. We also systematically analyze the Lorentz structure of the energy-momentum tensor and identify the suitable hadron matrix elements to extract the form factors, avoiding the contamination of spurious contributions. We verify that the extracted form factors obey momentum conservation as well as the mechanical stability condition. From the gravitational form factors, we compute the energy and pressure distributions of the system. Furthermore, we show that utilizing the Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation, the off-diagonal Fock sector contributions from the interaction term can be converted to diagonal Fock sector contributions, yielding a systematic non-perturbative light-front wave function representation of the energies and forces inside the system.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that gravitational form factors (GFFs) of a strongly-coupled scalar theory can be extracted non-perturbatively using light-front Hamiltonian methods with Fock sector dependent renormalization, and that the off-diagonal Fock sector contributions from the interaction term in the energy-momentum tensor can be converted to diagonal Fock sector contributions via the Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation, yielding a systematic light-front wave function representation of energies and forces inside the system, but only within the quenched approximation (neglecting nucleon-antinucleon loops) and with truncation to three-body Fock sectors.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Schrödinger equation for a scalar Yukawa theory (complex scalar "mock nucleon" coupled to real scalar "mock pion") using Fock sector dependent renormalization, obtaining eigenstates up to four particles (one nucleon plus three pions) with three-body truncation. They compute hadron matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor in the Drell-Yan frame ($q^+=0$) using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory diagrams, then extract the gravitational form factors $A(q^2)$ and $D(q^2)$ by analyzing the Lorentz structure with covariant light-front dynamics to remove spurious contributions from broken dynamical symmetries.

## Key numbers

- Physical masses: mock nucleon mass $m = 0.94$ GeV, mock pion mass $\mu = 0.14$ GeV
- Dimensionless coupling defined as $\alpha = g^2/(16\pi m^2)$, where $g$ is the physical coupling
- Fock sector expansion shown to converge up to three-body truncations for field strength renormalization constant and electromagnetic form factor at non-perturbative couplings (based on prior work Li (2015) and Karmanov (2016))
- The forward limit ($q=0$) yields $t^{++} = 2(P^+)^2$ and $t^{+-} = 2(m^2+P_\perp^2)$, independent of counterterms, using the normalization condition $I_1 + I_2 + I_3 = 1$ for the three-body truncation

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative calculations of gravitational form factors beyond perturbative QCD (Tong (2022)) and phenomenological models such as holography (Mamo (2022), Fujita (2022)), light-front quark-diquark models (Chakrabarti (2020)), and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Xing (2022)). It addresses the challenge that prior calculations (Chakrabarti (2020)) reported unphysical divergences in the $D$ term due to improper renormalization of the stress component of the energy-momentum tensor. The scalar theory serves as a simplified model for nucleon-pion interactions, enabling systematic study of how forces inside the mock nucleon evolve with coupling strength, and provides a foundation for extending the light-front wave function representation of the $D$ term to more realistic theories.
