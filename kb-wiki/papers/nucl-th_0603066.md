# Collisional Energy Loss in a Finite Size QCD Matter

> Computation of collisional energy loss in a finite size QCD medium has become crucial to obtain reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We here compute this energy loss up to the zeroth order in opacity. Our approach consistently treats both soft and hard contributions to the collisional energy loss. Consequently, it removes the unphysical energy gain in a region of lower momenta obtained by previous computations. Most importantly, we show that for characteristic QCD medium scales, finite size effects on the collisional energy loss are not significant.

## Key claim

The paper computes collisional energy loss in a finite size QCD medium up to zeroth order in opacity, and claims that for characteristic QCD medium scales, finite size effects on the collisional energy loss are not significant, contrary to the earlier suggestion by Peigne et al. that finite size effects lead to a significant reduction.

## Method

The author computes the collisional energy loss from the one-loop Hard-Thermal Loop (HTL) gluon propagator diagram (zeroth order in opacity) for a jet produced in a finite static QCD medium of size L, using Coulomb gauge and treating both soft and hard contributions consistently. The finite size is introduced via a theta function limiting the interaction time to L/v, and the energy loss is obtained by integrating the squared matrix element over the phase space of the medium partons with equilibrium momentum distributions. The infinite medium case is recovered by taking the limit where the jet is produced at x0 = -∞, and the results are compared to previous calculations by Braaten and Thoma (1991) and Thoma and Gyulassy (1991).

## Key numbers

- Debye mass μ_D ≈ 0.5 GeV, computed from μ_D² = g² T² (1 + N_f/6) with T = 0.225 GeV, N_f = 2.5, and α = 0.3.
- Charm quark mass taken as M = 1.2 GeV; bottom quark mass taken as M = 4.75 GeV; light quark mass taken as M = μ_D/√6.
- For bottom quarks, the unphysical negative energy loss in the Braaten and Thoma (1991) computation persists up to 5 GeV, and in the Thoma and Gyulassy (1991) computation up to 2 GeV.
- Assumed medium thickness L = 5 fm for the comparison figures; mean free path λ = 1.2 fm for the radiative energy loss comparison.
- In the lower momentum range p < 10 GeV, collisional energy loss dominates the radiative energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the collisional energy loss calculations of Braaten and Thoma (1991) and Thoma and Gyulassy (1991) for infinite QCD media, and directly contradicts the finite-size results of Peigne et al., who found significant reduction of collisional energy loss in finite media. The paper shows that Peigne et al. did not properly separate collisional from radiative energy loss, and that the finite size effects are not significant. The results enable more reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly for the "single electron puzzle" at RHIC where collisional energy loss is shown to be comparable to or larger than radiative energy loss in the relevant momentum range.
