# Form Factors in the Algebraic Cluster Model

> I present a derivation of form factors in the Algebraic Cluster Model for an arbitrary number of identical clusters. The form factors correspond to representation matrix elements which are derived in closed form for the harmonic oscillator and deformed oscillator limits. These results are relevant for applications in nuclear, molecular and hadronic physics.

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form expressions for electromagnetic transition form factors in the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) for an arbitrary number of identical clusters, but only for two specific dynamical symmetry limits: the harmonic oscillator (U(3k-3) limit) and the deformed oscillator (SO(3k-2) limit). The results are valid for finite boson number N and in the large-N limit, with the caveat that the derivation assumes the clusters have no internal structure.

## Method

The author uses the Algebraic Cluster Model, which is based on a U(3k-2) spectrum generating algebra constructed from bosonic quantization of the k-1 relative Jacobi coordinates for a system of k identical clusters. Transition form factors are obtained by evaluating matrix elements of the operator exp(i ε D_{k-1,0}), where D_{k-1,0} is a dipole operator expressed in terms of boson creation and annihilation operators, and these matrix elements are derived in closed form for the U(3k-3) and SO(3k-2) dynamical symmetry limits using the transformation properties of a single boson. The derivation is carried out both for finite boson number N and in the large-N limit, with the charge distribution of each cluster smeared by a Gaussian.

## Key numbers

- Elastic form factor in the U(3k-3) limit (harmonic oscillator) for finite N: U_el(ε) = (cos ε)^N, with ε = -qβ/X_D and X_D = √(3N)
- Elastic form factor in the large-N limit of the U(3k-3) limit: U_el → exp(-q²β²/6)
- Elastic form factor in the SO(3k-2) limit (deformed oscillator) for finite N: U_el(ε) = [N!(3k-5)!/(N+3k-5)!] C_N^{(3k-4)/2}(cos ε), with X_D = √(N(N+3k-4)/(k-1))
- Elastic form factor in the large-N limit for k even: U_el → (3k-5)!! j_{(3k-6)/2}(qβ√(k-1))/(qβ√(k-1))^{(3k-6)/2}
- Elastic form factor in the large-N limit for k odd: U_el → (3k-5)!! J_{(3k-5)/2}(qβ√(k-1))/(qβ√(k-1))^{(3k-5)/2}
- Root-mean-square radius relation: ⟨r²⟩ = β² + 3/(2γ), where γ is the Gaussian smearing parameter and β = 2.50 fm in the example calculations
- Excitation probability in the U(3k-3) limit for finite N: P_n(ε) = C(N,n) (sin²ε)^n (cos²ε)^{N-n}
- Excitation probability in the large-N limit of the U(3k-3) limit: P_n → (1/n!)(q²β²/3)^n exp(-q²β²/3)
- For k=4 clusters in the U(3k-3) limit, symmetric states are multiplied by factors: 1 for n=0, 1/3 for n=2, 2/9 for n=3; the n=1 shell contains no symmetric state
- For k=4 clusters in the SO(3k-2) limit, symmetric states are multiplied by factors: 1 for τ=0, 1/4 for τ=2, 11/36 for τ=3; the τ=1 multiplet contains no symmetric states

## Context

This work extends previous derivations of representation matrix elements and form factors for systems dominated by a single multipole (Ref. BG) to the case of coupled oscillators relevant for alpha-cluster nuclei, generalizing the U(4) vibron model for two-body problems (vibron, cluster, mesons), the U(7) model for three-body clusters (BIL, BDL, ACM, C12), and the recently introduced U(10) model for four-body clusters (RB, O16). The results enable future applications of the ACM to four-body systems in molecular physics (X4 molecules), nuclear physics (16O as a cluster of four alpha particles), and hadronic physics (q⁴-q̄ multiquark configurations), and can be extended to nonidentical particles and other geometric configurations (BL).
