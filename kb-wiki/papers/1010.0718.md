# Holography and Anomaly Matching for Resonances

> We derive a universal relation for the transverse part of triangle anomalies within a class of theories whose gravity dual is described by the Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory. This relation provides a set of sum rules involving the masses, decay constants and couplings between resonances, and leads to the formulas for the matrix elements of the vector and axial currents in the presence of the soft electromagnetic field. We also discuss that this relation is valid in real QCD at least approximately. This may be regarded as the anomaly matching for resonances as an analogue of that for the massless excitations in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives a universal relation for the transverse part of triangle anomalies that holds for any momentum transfer \(Q^2\) in a class of holographic QCD models, and argues that this relation is valid at least approximately in real QCD at both small and large \(Q^2\), though the authors note the uncertainty in real QCD should be resolved in the future.

## Method

The authors consider five-dimensional Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory with a U(\(N_f\)) gauge group, where chiral symmetry is broken by boundary conditions in the infrared. They compute the correlation function of vector and axial currents in a weak electromagnetic background field by substituting the classical solution into the Chern-Simons action, using the Wronskian of the mode functions to relate the transverse anomaly \(w_T(Q^2)\) to the difference between axial and vector current correlators. The resulting relation is then expanded in terms of infinite towers of vector and axial-vector meson resonances to derive sum rules.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal anomaly is fixed as \(w_L(Q^2) = 2N_c / Q^2\), matching the QCD result with \(\kappa = N_c/(24\pi^2)\).
* The transverse anomaly relation is \(w_T(Q^2) = N_c/Q^2 - (N_c/f_\pi^2)[\Pi_A(Q^2) - \Pi_V(Q^2)]\) for any \(Q^2\).
* The pion decay constant satisfies \(4/f_\pi^2 = \int_{-z_0}^{z_0} dz / f^2(z)\).
* The longitudinal sum rule is \(\sum_i g_{\gamma V_i \pi} g_{V_i} / m_{V_i}^2 = N_c/(2\pi^2 f_\pi)\).
* The transverse sum rules are \(\sum_j g_{\gamma V_i A_j} g_{A_j} / (m_{A_j}^2 - m_{V_i}^2) = -N_c g_{V_i}/(4\pi^2 f_\pi^2)\) for each vector meson \(i\) and \(\sum_i g_{\gamma V_i A_j} g_{V_i} / (m_{A_j}^2 - m_{V_i}^2) = -N_c g_{A_j}/(4\pi^2 f_\pi^2)\) for each axial-vector meson \(j\).
* For the "cosh" model example, meson masses are \(m_n^2 = n(n+1)\Lambda^2\) and decay constants are \(g_n = \sqrt{2n(n+1)(2n+1)}\Lambda^2/g_5\).
* The predicted coupling \(g_{\gamma \rho f_1} \approx -9.2\) for \(N_c=3\) is compared to the experimental value \(|g_{\gamma \rho f_1}| = 7.6 \pm 1.1\) from the decay rate \(\Gamma_{\rm exp}(f_1 \rightarrow \rho^0 + \gamma) = 1.34 \pm 0.38\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the transverse part of triangle anomalies initiated by Vainshtein (2002) and further studied by Czarnecki (2002) and Knecht (2003), who found that the transverse part receives nonperturbative corrections unlike the longitudinal part. The holographic approach encompasses both the "bottom-up" AdS/QCD model of Son and Stephanov (2004) and the "top-down" Sakai-Sugimoto model (2005). The derived sum rules and matrix elements provide constraints on resonance parameters that can be tested against experimental data, and the results are relevant for theoretical estimates of hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
