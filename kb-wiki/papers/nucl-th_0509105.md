# Reconstructing $ρ^0$ and $ω$ mesons from non-leptonic decays in C+C at 2AGeV

> We predict transverse and longitudinal momentum spectra and yields of $ρ^0$ and $ω$ mesons reconstructed from hadron correlations in C+C reactions at 2~AGeV. The rapidity and $p_T$ distributions for reconstructable $ρ^0$ mesons differs strongly from the primary distribution, while the $ω$'s distributions are only weakly modified. We discuss the temporal and spatial distributions of the particles emitted in the hadron channel. Finally, we report on the mass shift of the $ρ^0$ due to its coupling to the $N^*(1520)$, which is observable in both the di-lepton and $ππ$ channel. Our calculations can be tested with the Hades experiment at GSI, Darmstadt.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of ρ⁰ mesons reconstructed from hadronic (ππ) decays in C+C collisions at 2 AGeV differ strongly from the primary (di-lepton-like) distributions, while ω meson distributions are only weakly modified; this difference is attributed to rescattering of daughter pions rather than to in-medium modifications of the meson properties.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model, a non-equilibrium approach based on covariant propagation of hadrons and strings with cross sections calculated by detailed balance or fitted to data, to simulate more than 8 × 10⁶ minimum-bias C+C events at 2 AGeV. They identified "reconstructable" resonances by tracking each resonance's decay products: if any daughter hadron rescatters, the resonance signal is considered lost; otherwise it is counted as reconstructable via hadron correlations. "All decayed" resonances (those reconstructable via di-leptons after multiplying by the branching ratio) were also recorded for comparison.

## Key numbers

- At midrapidity, the ρ⁰ yield drops from 3.5% (di-lepton channel) to 0.8% (hadronic channel) of all decayed ρ⁰ mesons.
- At midrapidity, 8 × 10⁻³ ρ⁰ mesons per event can be reconstructed from pion correlations.
- The peak emission time for reconstructable ρ⁰ mesons is 7.5 fm/c, compared to 5.5 fm/c for all decayed ρ⁰ mesons (a shift of 2 fm/c).
- The peak emission time for reconstructable ω mesons is 13 fm/c, compared to 7.5 fm/c for all decayed ω mesons.
- The peak transverse emission radius for reconstructable ρ⁰ mesons is r_T = 1.8 fm, compared to r_T = 1.3 fm for all decayed ρ⁰ mesons.
- The decay N*(1520) → p + ρ⁰ contributes 75% to the reconstructable ρ⁰ mass spectrum below 600 MeV, kinematically restricting the ρ⁰ mass to m_ρ ≤ m_N*(1520) - m_p ≈ 580 MeV.
- The ρ⁰ mass distribution shows a double peak structure with maxima at the ρ pole mass (770 MeV) and around 500–600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of hadron resonance reconstruction in heavy-ion collisions, following experimental efforts at SPS and RHIC energies (Markert, 2004; Adams, 2004) and prior theoretical transport-model studies (Bleicher, 2002; Torrieri, 2004). The predictions are directly testable with the HADES experiment at GSI, Darmstadt, which measures di-lepton yields at moderate energies; the hadronic reconstruction provides complementary information on the late-stage dynamics of the collision and allows direct tests of hadronic transport models without di-lepton "after burners." The observed double-peak structure in the ρ⁰ mass spectrum is attributed to decay kinematics of the N*(1520) production channel rather than to in-medium modifications, contrasting with earlier interpretations (Brown, 1991; Rapp, 1999; Cassing, 1997).
