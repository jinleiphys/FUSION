# A chiral bag model approach to delta electroproduction

> Helicity amplitudes for the $γ^* N \to Δ$ transition are calculated using the cloudy bag model. A correction for center-of-mass motion is carried out using a modified Peierls-Thouless projection method. This reduces the magnitude of the transition amplitudes at small momentum transfer and enhances them at modest momentum transfers. Our calculation shows that the pion cloud contributes substantially to the transition helicity amplitudes, with the final result giving reasonable agreement with the corresponding experimental values.

# A chiral bag model approach to delta electroproduction

## Key claim

The cloudy bag model, when including a Peierls-Thouless center-of-mass correction and perturbative pion cloud contributions, yields helicity amplitudes for the gamma* N to Delta transition that agree reasonably well with experimental values, but only for a limited range of momentum transfer (Q^2 less than about 1.6 GeV^2) and with a strong dependence on the chosen bag radius.

## Method

The authors calculated the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and A_{3/2} for virtual photon electroproduction of the Delta resonance using the cloudy bag model, which couples a perturbative pion field to MIT bag quarks to restore chiral symmetry. Center-of-mass motion was corrected using a simplified Peierls-Thouless projection method that constructs Galilean-invariant baryon states from the product of individual quark wave functions. The total transition amplitude was computed as the sum of three contributions: the bare quark core term, a term from the gamma-qq interaction with one pion in the air, and a term from the gamma-pi-pi coupling vertex, with the pion loop integrals evaluated using time-ordered perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* At the real photon limit (Q^2 = 0), the total helicity amplitude A_{3/2} for bag radius R = 0.7 fm is -249 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (with Peierls-Thouless projection), compared to the Particle Data Group estimate of -258 +/- 6 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
* The center-of-mass correction reduces the bare transition amplitude by 5 to 10 percent for Q^2 less than 0.5 GeV^2, and flips sign to increase the amplitude for larger momentum transfers
* The pion cloud contribution accounts for approximately two-thirds of the total amplitude at Q^2 = 0 for bag radius R = 0.8 fm
* The E2/M1 ratio from the gamma-pi-pi diagram is approximately -0.3 x 10^{-3}, about two orders of magnitude smaller than the Particle Data Group estimate of -0.015 +/- 0.004
* The recoil function K(r) cuts down contributions from quark wave functions near the bag boundary by up to 50 percent

## Context

This work extends earlier cloudy bag model calculations by Kalbermann and Eisenberg (1983) and Bermuth et al. (1986), who calculated photoproduction of Delta and Roper resonances but neglected recoil effects and were limited to real photons at the Delta resonance peak. The authors note that similar constituent quark models, such as the nonrelativistic quark model of Isgur, Karl, and Koniuk (1982) and the relativized quark model of Warns et al. (1990), typically underpredict the helicity amplitudes significantly. The calculation enables future work incorporating Lorentz boosts, as suggested by the Licht-Pagnamenta prescription, and exploration of SU(6)-violating admixtures in baryon wave functions to improve agreement with experimental data at higher momentum transfers.
