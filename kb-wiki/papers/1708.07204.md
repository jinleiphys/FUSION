# Enhancement of the Triple Alpha Rate in a Hot Dense Medium

> In a sufficiently hot and dense astrophysical environment the rate of the triple-alpha (3alpha) reaction can increase greatly over the value appropriate for helium burning stars owing to hadronically induced de-excitation of the Hoyle state. In this paper we use a statistical model to evaluate the enhancement as a function of temperature and density. For a density of $10^6 gm cm^{-3}$ enhancements can exceed a factor of one-hundred. In high temperature/density situations, the enhanced 3alpha rate is a better estimate of this rate and should be used in these circumstances. We then examine the effect of these enhancements on production of $^{12}$C in the neutrino wind following a supernova explosion and in an x-ray burster.

## Key claim

The triple-alpha reaction rate can be enhanced by a factor exceeding one hundred at a density of 10^6 g cm^-3 due to hadronically induced de-excitation of the Hoyle state, but the enhancement factors are uncertain by about a factor of two to three based on comparisons between Hauser-Feshbach calculations and available experimental cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model implemented in the TALYS code (version 1.8) to calculate inelastic scattering cross sections for neutrons, protons, and alpha particles on 12C, for transitions from the ground state and the 4.44 MeV 2+ state to the 7.65 MeV Hoyle state. They then computed the inverse reaction rates for de-excitation of the Hoyle state by integrating these cross sections over a Maxwellian velocity distribution, and compared the resulting particle-induced de-excitation rates to the known radiative width of the Hoyle state (tau_gamma = 1.710 x 10^-13 sec). For proton scattering to the Hoyle state, experimental data were used up to 2.30 MeV instead of the TALYS calculations due to strong resonances not reproduced by the model.

## Key numbers

- At a density of 10^6 g cm^-3 and T_9 = 1.0, the total neutron-induced enhancement factor is 115 (sum of contributions from ground state and 4.44 MeV state transitions), with an estimated uncertainty range of 38 to 345.
- The radiative lifetime of the Hoyle state used in calculations is tau_gamma = 1.710 x 10^-13 sec.
- The Q-values for excitation of the Hoyle state are -7.654 MeV from the ground state and -3.215 MeV from the 4.44 MeV 2+ state.
- The multiplying constants for the rate equation are k_n = 6.557 x 10^-6, k_p = 6.565 x 10^-6, and k_alpha = 9.200 x 10^-7.
- For an x-ray burster model, the enhancement reaches a maximum of 30% during the onset of the burst.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Shaw and Clayton (1967), who examined electromagnetic enhancement effects and found them unimportant below 10^9 g cm^-3, and by Truran and Kozlovsky (1969), who considered nuclear induced processes before reliable cross sections were available. The paper also builds on experimental studies of inelastic proton scattering by Davids and Bonner (1971) and alpha scattering by Morgan (1970), but addresses the previously missing neutron-induced cross sections and transitions from the 2+ state to the Hoyle state. The results enable improved modeling of 12C production in neutrino-driven winds from core-collapse supernovae and in x-ray bursters, where the enhanced triple-alpha rate may compete with the alpha-alpha-n process and affect the robustness of the r-process.
