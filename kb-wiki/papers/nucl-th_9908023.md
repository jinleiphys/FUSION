# A next-to-next-to-leading order pp -> ppπ^0 transition operator in chiral perturbation theory

> We discuss the results of a systematic calculation of the next-to-next-to-leading order amplitude for the pp -> ppπ^0 S-wave production at the threshold in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. We find six new diagrams, two of which can be viewed as vertex corrections, of 15 - 20 %, to the pion-exchange graph, whereas the rest are much larger, one exceeding 900 %. We discuss the reasons for this enhancement, as well as the steps necessary to be taken before the final comparison with experiment.

# A next-to-next-to-leading order \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) transition operator in chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The next-to-next-to-leading order calculation in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory reveals that six new one-loop diagrams contribute to the threshold S-wave \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) amplitude, but the large size of several contributions (one exceeding 900% of the leading pion-exchange graph) indicates the theory is being used outside its natural range of applicability, rather than representing a simple convergence of the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) transition operator at next-to-next-to-leading order, identifying 19 topologically distinct one-loop diagrams and one recoil correction. Isospin selection rules and the S-wave character of the outgoing pion reduced these to six non-vanishing one-loop diagram types plus one recoil correction (type VII). The relative sizes of these contributions were evaluated in the plane-wave approximation for initial and final proton states, using three parameter sets (A, B, C) for the low-energy constants \(c_{1,2,3}\).

## Key numbers

- Type IV diagram gives the largest contribution, with relative size \(R_K = 9.5\) (parameter set A), 5.1 (set B), and 7.2 (set C) compared to the leading pion rescattering diagram.
- Types II and III cancel to within 1% at threshold, with values of +6.7 and -6.7 (set A), +3.6 and -3.6 (set B), and +5.1 and -5.1 (set C), respectively.
- Vertex correction types V and VI give small contributions: type V at 0.18 (set A), 0.10 (set B), 0.14 (set C); type VI at 0.14 (set A), 0.08 (set B), 0.11 (set C).
- The recoil correction (type VII) has relative size 2.6 (set A), 1.9 (set B), and 2.0 (set C).
- The enhancement factor for two-pion exchange diagrams is estimated as \(k^2 f_\pi^{-2} = - m_\pi m_N f_\pi^{-2} \simeq -15\).
- The three-momentum transfer at threshold is \(\sqrt{\vec{k}^2} \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi m_N} \simeq \sqrt{7} m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory treatment of \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) beyond the leading-order Born and pion rescattering diagrams (Fig. 1), which had been found by Lee and Riska (1993) to underestimate the measured cross sections from Bloomington and Uppsala by roughly a factor of five. The large loop contributions found here can be interpreted as an effective \(\sigma\)-meson exchange, connecting to alternative meson-exchange models by Lee and Riska (1993). The results highlight the need for distorted wave calculations, which the authors note are complicated by the non-square-integrable nature of the production operator and significant contributions from momenta up to 2 GeV, beyond the chiral perturbation theory regime.
