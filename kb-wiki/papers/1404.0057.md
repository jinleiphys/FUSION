# Inhomogeneous phases in the quark-meson model with vacuum fluctuations

> Inhomogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking phases at non-vanishing chemical potential and temperature are studied within a two-flavor quark-meson model in the chiral limit. The analysis is performed beyond the standard mean-field approximation by taking into account the Dirac-sea contributions of the quarks. Compared with the case where the Dirac sea is neglected, we find that the inhomogeneous phase shrinks, but in general does not disappear. It is shown within a Ginzburg-Landau analysis that the Lifshitz point of the inhomogeneous phase coincides with the tricritical point if the ratio between sigma-meson and constituent quark mass in vacuum is chosen to be $m_σ/M = 2$, corresponding to the fixed mass ratio in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. In the present model, however, this ratio can be varied, offering the possibility to separate the two points. This is confirmed by our numerical calculations, which demonstrate a strong sensitivity of the size of the inhomogeneous phase on $m_σ$. Finally, we uncover a general instability of the model with respect to large wave numbers of the chiral modulations, which calls for further improvements beyond the present approximation.

## Key claim

Including Dirac-sea vacuum fluctuations in the quark-meson model shrinks the inhomogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking phase compared to the standard mean-field approximation, but the inhomogeneous phase generally does not disappear; the Lifshitz point coincides with the tricritical point only when the sigma-meson to constituent quark mass ratio is exactly \(m_\sigma/M = 2\), and varying this ratio can separate the two points.

## Method

The authors studied inhomogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking in a two-flavor quark-meson model in the chiral limit, going beyond the standard mean-field approximation by including the renormalized Dirac-sea contributions of the quarks. They performed a Ginzburg-Landau analysis to locate the Lifshitz point relative to the tricritical point, and numerically computed the phase diagram for one-dimensional spatial modulations of the chiral order parameter using Pauli-Villars regularization. The model parameters were fitted to vacuum properties (constituent quark mass \(M = 300\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 88\) MeV, and variable sigma-meson mass \(m_\sigma\)).

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass in vacuum was fixed to \(M = 300\) MeV and the pion decay constant to its chiral-limit value \(f_\pi = 88\) MeV.
- For the NJL-model relation \(m_\sigma = 2M = 600\) MeV, the Lifshitz point coincides with the tricritical point.
- With Pauli-Villars regularization at \(\Lambda = 5\) GeV (interpreted as the "renormalized" results), the phase boundaries become cutoff-independent; a cross-check at \(\Lambda = 10\) GeV confirmed stability.
- For homogeneous phases, including the Dirac sea with \(\Lambda = 300\) MeV already produces a tricritical point separating first-order from second-order transitions, whereas the standard mean-field approximation exhibits a first-order transition along the entire phase boundary even at vanishing chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous phases in the quark-meson model by Nickel (2009) that neglected quark vacuum contributions, and addresses regularization ambiguities present in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model where similar inhomogeneous phases have been studied by Sadzikowski (2000), Nakano (2004), Nickel (2009), Carignano (2010, 2011, 2012), and Tatsumi (2013). The results show that including vacuum fluctuations shrinks the inhomogeneous phase but does not eliminate it, and the model exhibits an instability toward large wave numbers that calls for further improvements beyond the present approximation.
