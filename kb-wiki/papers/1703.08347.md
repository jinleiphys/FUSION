# Microscopic studies on nuclear spin-isospin properties---a personal perspective on covariant density functional theory

> Spin and isospin are essential degrees of freedom in nuclear systems, and the relevant studies on their properties play important roles not only in nuclear physics but also in nuclear astrophysics, particle physics, and so on. In this presentation for the IUPAP Young Scientist Prize 2016, I would like to introduce the microscopic studies on nuclear spin-isospin properties in the framework of covariant density functional theory (DFT), by taking a few works that I have been joining in as examples. It is seen that the covariant scheme plays an important role in describing the spin properties in a consistent way, such as the spin-orbit splitting, the pseudospin symmetry, etc. Meanwhile, the Fock terms play important roles in describing the isospin properties fully self-consistently, such as the Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole resonances, the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to nuclear superallowed $β$ transitions, etc. To connect the covariant DFT to more fundamental theories, an \textit{ab initio} relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory for finite nuclei is introduced, and a personal perspective for the coming decade is also illustrated.

# Microscopic studies on nuclear spin-isospin properties, a personal perspective on covariant density functional theory

## Key claim
Covariant density functional theory (DFT), particularly when including both Hartree and Fock terms, can describe nuclear spin-isospin properties such as Gamow-Teller resonances, spin-dipole excitations, and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections in a fully self-consistent way; however, the unitarity of the CKM matrix remains an open question because DFT-based isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections yield a less clear conclusion than shell-model results.

## Method
The author employed covariant density functional theory based on a relativistic Lagrangian with meson-exchange interactions (sigma, omega, rho, pi) and photons, solving the Dirac equation for nucleons. For spin-isospin excitations, random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations were performed on top of relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) self-consistent ground states, including both local Hartree and non-local Fock terms. For the CKM matrix unitarity test, isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections delta_c were computed systematically for nine superallowed beta transitions using the same RHF+RPA framework. Additionally, a relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory for finite nuclei was developed by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently in the two-body frame.

## Key numbers
- Gamow-Teller resonances in Pb-208: strength distributions reproduced by self-consistent RHF+RPA, with isoscalar mesons playing essential roles via Fock terms (no explicit numerical peak positions given in text).
- Spin-dipole excitations in O-16: three components distinguished experimentally in 2011 at RCNP using a polarized proton beam, reproduced in detail by RHF+RPA.
- Isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections delta_c: computed for nine superallowed beta transitions; values are substantially different from shell-model results, as noted by the Particle Data Group.
- V_ud values: RHF+RPA result from Liang (2009) and projected DFT results from Satula et al. (2011, 2012) differ substantially from shell-model results; the unitarity condition V^2_ud + V^2_us + V^2_ub is satisfied within error bars using shell-model delta_c but less clear using DFT delta_c.
- RBHF for O-16: energy per particle and charge radius calculated with Bonn A interaction; spin-orbit splitting well reproduced from bare nucleon-nucleon interaction; RBHF capability extended to Ca-48 (Shen 2017).
- R-process: a remarkable speeding up of r-matter flow leads to enhanced abundances of elements with A >= 140 (Niu 2013).

## Context
This work extends the covariant DFT framework developed since the Walecka model (1974) and summarized in the volume "Relativistic Density Functional for Nuclear Structure" (Meng 2016). It addresses limitations of Hartree-only approaches in spin-isospin channels and tensor effects, as noted by Liang (2012). The pseudospin symmetry analysis builds on Hecht and Adler (1969) and Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969). The CKM unitarity test connects to Particle Data Group reviews and shell-model calculations, with alternative DFT results from Satula et al. (2011, 2012). The RBHF development for finite nuclei (Shen 2016, 2017) provides a benchmark for earlier effective density approximation and local density approximation calculations, and aims to link covariant DFT to lattice QCD simulations from the HAL QCD collaboration. The perspective envisions a new-generation DFT oriented by quantum field theories using renormalization group flow equations and effective field theory power counting.
