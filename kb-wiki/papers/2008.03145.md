# Exclusive vector meson production in electron-ion collisions at the EIC, LHeC and FCC-$eh$

> The exclusive vector meson production in electron-ion collisions for the energies of the future colliders is investigated. We present predictions for the coherent and incoherent $ϕ$ and $J/ψ$ production in $eAu$ collisions considering the possible states of nucleon configurations in the nuclear wave function and taking into account of the non-linear corrections to the QCD dynamics. The cross sections and transverse momentum distributions are estimated assuming the energies of the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), Large Hadron Electron Collider (LHeC) and Future Circular Collider (FCC-$eh$). Our results indicate that a future experimental analysis of these processes can shed light on the modeling of the gluon saturation effects and constrain the description of the QCD dynamics at high energies.

# Exclusive vector meson production in electron-ion collisions at the EIC, LHeC and FCC-$eh$

## Key claim

The exclusive coherent and incoherent production of $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ mesons in electron-gold collisions at future colliders can discriminate between QCD models with and without gluon saturation effects, but only if the final state is a light meson ($\phi$) at low photon virtuality ($Q^2 \le 1$ GeV$^2$); for $J/\psi$ production the predictions of the b-Sat and b-Non Sat models are similar, making that channel less discriminating.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent cross sections for exclusive $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ photoproduction and electroproduction in $eAu$ collisions using the color dipole formalism. The dipole-nucleus cross section is treated via the Glauber-Gribov formalism with a b-Sat (IP-SAT) model that includes non-linear saturation effects, and compared to a b-Non Sat linearized approximation. The nuclear wave function is sampled over 500 distinct nucleon configurations drawn from a Woods-Saxon profile with Gaussian nucleon substructure of width $B_G = 4.0$ GeV$^{-2}$, using the Sartre event generator. Predictions are made for three collider configurations: EIC (21 GeV electron on 100 GeV/nucleon gold), LHeC (60 GeV on 2800 GeV/nucleon), and FCC-$eh$ (60 GeV on 7885 GeV/nucleon).

## Key numbers

- For coherent $J/\psi$ production at EIC with $Q^2 \le 1.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 521.8 nb, b-Non Sat = 716.9 nb.
- For coherent $\phi$ production at EIC with $Q^2 \le 1.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 24544.4 nb, b-Non Sat = 304383.0 nb (factor > 10 difference).
- For incoherent $\phi$ production at EIC with $Q^2 \le 1.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 4938.3 nb, b-Non Sat = 73192.1 nb.
- For coherent $J/\psi$ production at LHeC with $Q^2 \le 1.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 1095.5 nb, b-Non Sat = 1563.0 nb.
- For coherent $\phi$ production at FCC-$eh$ with $Q^2 \le 1.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 39870.0 nb, b-Non Sat = 462055.0 nb.
- For coherent $\phi$ production at EIC with $5.0 \le Q^2 \le 10.0$ GeV$^2$: b-Sat = 16.3 nb, b-Non Sat = 48.5 nb (difference shrinks at higher $Q^2$).
- All cross sections are integrated over $|t| \le 0.08$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of coherent and incoherent vector meson production in electron-ion and ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (Toll, 2012; Mantysaari, 2016; Cepila, 2018) by providing the first predictions for exclusive $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ production at LHeC and FCC-$eh$ energies. The results confirm that the $\phi$ meson, due to its larger dipole size, is far more sensitive to saturation effects than the $J/\psi$, as anticipated in earlier dipole model studies (Kowalski, 2003; Run 2, 2013). The findings enable future experimental programs at the EIC, LHeC, and FCC-$eh$ to design measurements of the transverse momentum distribution of $\phi$ production as a probe of the transition from linear to non-linear QCD dynamics.
