# Relationship of field-theory based single boson exchange potentials to current ones

> It is shown that field-theory based single boson exchange potentials cannot be identified to those of the Yukawa or Coulomb type that are currently inserted in the Schrödinger equation. The potential which is obtained rather corresponds to this current single boson exchange potential corrected for the probability that the system under consideration is in a two-body component, therefore missing contributions due to the interaction of these two bodies while bosons are exchanged. The role of these contributions, which involve at least two boson exchanges, is examined. The conditions that allow one to recover the usual single boson exchange potential are given. It is shown that the present results have some relation: i) to the failure of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in reproducing the Dirac or Klein-Gordon equations in the limit where one of the constituent has a large mass, ii) to the absence of corrections of relative order alpha log(1/alpha) to a full calculation of the binding energy in the case of neutral massless bosons or iii) to large corrections of wave-functions calculated perturbatively in some light-front approaches.

# Relationship of field-theory based single boson exchange potentials to current ones

## Key claim
Field-theory based single boson exchange potentials cannot be directly identified with the Yukawa or Coulomb type potentials used in the Schrödinger equation; the correct potential corresponds to the usual single boson exchange potential corrected by a factor accounting for the probability that the system is in a two-body component, and the usual form is recovered only when contributions from two-boson exchanges (especially crossed diagrams) are included.

## Method
The authors start from a field-theory motivated, energy-dependent single boson exchange interaction derived from time-ordered diagrams. They expand this interaction in powers of 1/ω_k and perform a transformation analogous to the Foldy-Wouthuysen procedure to obtain an energy-independent effective potential. This effective potential is then compared to the standard single boson exchange potential V_0, and the correction term involving V_1 (related to the probability of finding the system with a boson in flight) is identified. The authors compute perturbative corrections to deuteron wave functions using the Bonn-Q and Bonn-QB models, and also analyze the Wick-Cutkosky model for scalar boson exchange.

## Key numbers
- The correction to the deuteron S-wave wave function from the term ½{V₁,V₀} is a strong reduction, more than obtained in earlier works by CARB2 and FRED; the D-wave is less changed, resulting in a relative enhancement of the D-wave with respect to the S-wave.
- Perturbative calculations using current deuteron wave functions as zeroth order require a large renormalization of the first order wave function by about 20%.
- Renormalization effects related to the meson in flight content of the deuteron state have been found to be of the order of 3-4% (BONN, DESP1).
- The ratio of deuteron asymptotic normalizations A_D/A_S and the deuteron quadrupole moment both exceed experiment by 20-25%.
- In the Wick-Cutkosky model (two scalar particles exchanging a massless spin-0 particle), corrections to the binding energy are of order m α³ log(1/α) (relative order α log(1/α) with respect to the binding energy).
- For the Wick-Cutkosky model in the light-front approach, part of the wave function correction can be accounted for by a renormalization of the coupling constant: α → α (1 - (2/π) α log(1/α)).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the Bethe-Salpeter equation's failure to reproduce the Dirac or Klein-Gordon equations in the limit where one constituent has a large mass (ITZY, FRIA1), and addresses the presence of α³ log(1/α) corrections in the Wick-Cutkosky model (FELD, FRED, DESP2, CARB1) that are absent in the Coulomb interaction case. The paper also addresses the large renormalization (about 20%) found in perturbative light-front calculations of the deuteron wave function (FRED, CARB2), which is much larger than conventional relativistic corrections of order (v/c)². The results enable a better understanding of how to construct appropriate zeroth-order approximations for perturbative calculations using energy-dependent interaction models, and clarify the relationship between field-theory based potentials and the phenomenological potentials used in nuclear physics.
