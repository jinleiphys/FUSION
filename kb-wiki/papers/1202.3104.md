# Equation of state at non-zero baryon density based on lattice QCD

> We employ the lattice QCD data on Taylor expansion coefficients to extend our previous parametrization of the equation of state to finite baryon density. When we take into account lattice spacing and quark mass dependence of the hadron masses, the Taylor coefficients at low temperature are equal to those of hadron resonance gas. Parametrized lattice equation of state can thus be smoothly connected to the hadron resonance gas equation of state at low temperatures.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD Taylor expansion coefficients at low temperature can be made to match hadron resonance gas (HRG) predictions by applying a 30 MeV temperature shift to the lattice data to account for discretisation effects on hadron masses, enabling a smooth connection between the parametrized lattice equation of state and the HRG equation of state at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD data on Taylor expansion coefficients of pressure with respect to baryon and strangeness chemical potentials from the p4 action (Miao 2008, Cheng 2008) and applied a 30 MeV temperature shift to account for lattice spacing and quark mass effects. They parametrized the shifted coefficients as inverse polynomials of temperature, matched them to HRG values at T=155 MeV with continuity of the coefficient and its first three derivatives, and fixed remaining parameters by chi-squared fits. The resulting equation of state was then used to compute thermodynamic quantities and elliptic flow in hydrodynamic models.

## Key numbers

- Temperature shift of 30 MeV applied to lattice data points below 206 MeV, with the 209 MeV point shifted by 15 MeV
- Matching temperature T_SW = 155 MeV where parametrization connects to HRG values
- Second order coefficients required to reach 95% of their Stefan-Boltzmann value at 800 MeV temperature
- Entropy per baryon values s/n_B = 400, 65, and 40 correspond to collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 200, 39, and 17 GeV respectively
- At full SPS energy sqrt(s_NN) = 17 GeV, the p_T-differential elliptic flow is almost insensitive to the equation of state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parametrization of the equation of state at zero chemical potential (Huovinen 2009) to finite baryon density using lattice QCD Taylor coefficients from Miao (2008) and Cheng (2008). The approach enables hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, relevant for the Beam Energy Scan program, though the authors note that the insensitivity of elliptic flow to the equation of state at SPS energies is problematic for experimental searches of the QCD critical point.
