# Initial Parton Distribution just after Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the initial distribution of a parton system which is formed just after relativistic heavy ion collision by the elastic scattering among the constituent partons in details and analyze the baryon and strangeness contents of the primary parton system. We present the rapidity and energy distributions of the system.

# Initial Parton Distribution just after Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The initial parton system formed just after a relativistic heavy ion collision can be described by elastic scattering among constituent partons using a factorization method, but this approach only yields relatively high transverse momentum partons and does not reproduce soft partons, making it less reliable at RHIC energy than at higher energies. The authors extend this method to include baryon and strangeness contents at RHIC and LHC energies, which prior approaches based on color glass condensate or Schwinger-like radiation could not provide.

## Method
The authors calculate the production of partons (gluons, quarks, and antiquarks) from elastic scattering between constituents of projectile and target nuclei using leading-order perturbative QCD cross sections, with a K-factor of 2 to account for higher-order diagrams. They use CTEQ4, GRV98, and an empirical Hirano distribution for parton distribution functions, combined with the EKS98 nuclear ratio function, and consider a head-on collision (impact parameter b=0) with a running strong coupling constant and a minimum momentum transfer cutoff Q0.

## Key numbers
- Total available CM energy at RHIC: 39.4 TeV for head-on Au-Au collision at 200 GeV per nucleon pair
- Total available CM energy at LHC: 1140 TeV for Pb-Pb collision at 5.5 TeV per nucleon pair
- At RHIC, for 70% liberated energy into minijets, Q0 is estimated between 1.7 and 1.9 GeV/c; for 70-80% liberated energy, Q0 is about 1.7 GeV/c
- At LHC, for 70-80% liberated energy, Q0 is 3.5-3.7 GeV/c for CTEQ distribution and 3.7-3.9 GeV/c for GRV model
- Total number of produced partons at RHIC with CTEQ at Q0 = 1.7 GeV/c: about 3700
- Total number of produced partons at LHC with CTEQ at Q0 = 3.5 GeV/c: about 11700
- At RHIC (Q0 = 1.7 GeV): 3062 gluons (average energy 7.01 GeV), 230 u quarks (13.9 GeV), 255 d quarks (14.5 GeV), 33 s quarks (7.9 GeV), 62 ubar antiquarks (8.1 GeV), 61 dbar antiquarks (8.2 GeV)
- At LHC (Q0 = 3.5 GeV): 10380 gluons (average energy 66.8 GeV), 356 u quarks (235.7 GeV), 384 d quarks (253.3 GeV), 122 s quarks (79.9 GeV), 170 ubar antiquarks (90.2 GeV), 168 dbar antiquarks (92.0 GeV)
- Average energy per gluon at central rapidity: about 2.3 GeV at RHIC, about 5.7 GeV at LHC
- Total number of initial partons from the Krasnitz et al. (2003) CGC-based distribution: about 5400 for Qs = 1.7 GeV/c and 4200 for Qs = 1.5 GeV/c at RHIC

## Context
This work extends the factorization method previously used by Eskola (1996), Hammon et al. (1999), Nayak et al. (2002), and Cooper et al. (2002) to calculate minijet production, now including baryon and strangeness contents at RHIC and LHC energies. It contrasts with the color glass condensate approach of Krasnitz et al. (2003) and Lappi (2003), which applies only to central rapidity and cannot calculate quark or antiquark production, and with the Schwinger-like radiation model of Khachatryan (2006) that provides fast thermalization but also lacks baryon content. The resulting distributions can serve as initial conditions for partonic transport theory or hydrodynamic equations of motion to study the time evolution of the system.
