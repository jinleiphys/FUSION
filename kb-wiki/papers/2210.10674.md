# Odd-odd neutron-rich rhodium isotopes studied with the double Penning trap JYFLTRAP

> Precision mass measurements of neutron-rich rhodium isotopes have been performed at the JYFLTRAP Penning trap mass spectrometer at the Ion Guide Isotope Separator On-Line (IGISOL) facility. We report results on ground- and isomeric-state masses in $^{110,112,114,116,118}$Rh and the very first mass measurement of $^{120}$Rh. The isomeric states were separated and measured for the first time using the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique. For $^{112}$Rh, we also report new half-lives for both the ground state and the isomer. The results are compared to theoretical predictions using the BSkG1 mass model and discussed in terms of triaxial deformation.

## Key claim

Precision mass measurements of neutron-rich odd-odd rhodium isotopes resolve ground and isomeric states for the first time using the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique, revealing that previously reported excitation energies for the isomer in $^{112}$Rh were significantly overestimated (340(70) keV in NUBASE20 versus 38.4(19) keV from this work), and that the low-spin state is confirmed as the ground state in $^{112}$Rh.

## Method

The masses of neutron-rich $^{110,112,114,116,118,120}$Rh isotopes were measured using the double Penning trap mass spectrometer JYFLTRAP at the IGISOL facility, with fission fragments produced by 25-MeV protons on a natural uranium target. Ground and isomeric states in $^{110,112,114,116,118}$Rh were separated and measured for the first time using the PI-ICR technique, while $^{120}$Rh was measured with the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance (TOF-ICR) technique using a 10-30-10 ms Ramsey excitation pattern. For $^{112}$Rh, the low-spin ground state was produced exclusively via in-trap beta decay of $^{112}$Ru, and half-lives of both states were determined from decay curves measured with a silicon detector after the Penning trap.

## Key numbers

* $^{110}$Rh ground state mass excess: -82702.4(23) keV; isomer excitation energy: 38.0(15) keV
* $^{112}$Rh ground state mass excess: -79603.7(17) keV; isomer excitation energy: 38.4(19) keV (weighted mean)
* $^{112}$Rh ground state half-life: 2.22(13) s; isomer half-life: 6.96(8) s
* $^{114}$Rh ground state mass excess: -75662.7(26) keV; isomer excitation energy: 110.9(32) keV
* $^{116}$Rh ground state mass excess: -70729(2) keV; isomer excitation energy: 120.8(19) keV
* $^{118}$Rh ground state mass excess: -64994(5) keV; isomer excitation energy: 189(6) keV
* $^{120}$Rh ground state mass excess: -58614(58) keV (first measurement)

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich Rh isotopes at JYFLTRAP by Hager et al. (2007) and Kolhinen et al. (2003), which could not resolve ground and isomeric states due to the techniques then available. The results provide the first direct determinations of isomeric excitation energies in $^{110,114,116,118}$Rh and the first mass measurement of $^{120}$Rh, enabling improved benchmarking of nuclear mass models such as BSkG1 (Scamps et al., 2021) and testing predictions of triaxial deformation in this region. The new data also resolve a long-standing discrepancy in the $^{112}$Rh isomer excitation energy and confirm the ordering of low-spin and high-spin states.
