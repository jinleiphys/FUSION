# Exclusive $D \bar D$ meson pair production in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> The cross sections for exclusive $D^+D^-$ and $D^0 \bar {D^0}$ meson pair production in peripheral nucleus - nucleus collisions are calculated and several differential distributions are presented. The calculation of the elementary $γγ\to D \bar D$ cross section is done within the heavy-quark approximation and in the Brodsky- Lapage formalism with distribution amplitudes describing recent CLEO data on leptonic $D^+$ decay. Realistic (Fourier transform of charge density) charge form factors of nuclei are used to generate photon flux factors. Absorption effects are discussed and quantified. The cross sections of a few nb are predicted for RHIC and of a few hundreds of nb for LHC with details depending on the approximation made in calculating elementary $γγ\to D \bar D$ cross sections.

## Key claim

The cross sections for exclusive \(D^+D^-\) and \(D^0\bar{D}^0\) meson pair production in peripheral heavy-ion collisions are predicted to be a few nb at RHIC and a few hundred nb at LHC, but these values depend strongly on whether the heavy-quark approximation or the Brodsky-Lepage formalism with realistic distribution amplitudes is used for the elementary \(\gamma\gamma\to D\bar{D}\) cross section.

## Method

The authors calculate the elementary \(\gamma\gamma\to D\bar{D}\) cross section using two approaches: (1) the heavy-quark approximation of Choi (1994) with Dirac delta-like distribution amplitudes, and (2) the Brodsky-Lepage formalism with distribution amplitudes extracted from CLEO data on leptonic \(D^+\) decay. The nuclear cross section for \(AA\to AA D\bar{D}\) is then obtained via the equivalent photon approximation in impact-parameter space, using realistic charge form factors (Fourier transforms of measured charge densities) for gold (Au) and lead (Pb) nuclei, with an absorption factor \(\theta(b-2R_A)\) to enforce peripheral collisions. A cut on the D-meson transverse momentum \(p_t > 1\) GeV is imposed.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at RHIC (\(W_{NN}=200\) GeV) with the heavy-quark approximation: \(\sigma_{tot}(D^+D^-) = 12.44\) nb, \(\sigma_{tot}(D^0\bar{D}^0) = 21.2\) nb.
- For Au+Au at RHIC with the Brodsky-Lepage formalism (Huang distribution amplitude): \(\sigma_{tot}(D^+D^-) = 0.08\ \mu\)b, \(\sigma_{tot}(D^0\bar{D}^0) = 5.06\) nb.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC (\(W_{NN}=5.5\) TeV) with the heavy-quark approximation: \(\sigma_{tot}(D^+D^-) = 1.68\ \mu\)b, \(\sigma_{tot}(D^0\bar{D}^0) = 4.09\ \mu\)b.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC with the Brodsky-Lepage formalism: \(\sigma_{tot}(D^+D^-) = 1.92\ \mu\)b, \(\sigma_{tot}(D^0\bar{D}^0) = 0.28\ \mu\)b.
- The elementary cross section for \(\gamma\gamma\to D^0\bar{D}^0\) is larger than that for \(D^+D^-\) near threshold but falls faster with increasing \(W_{\gamma\gamma}\).
- The distribution in \(D\bar{D}\) invariant mass shows a steep fall-off, with the dominant contribution coming from the region of very small invariant masses close to threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of exclusive \(\mu^+\mu^-\) (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010), \(\rho^0\rho^0\) (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2009), and \(c\bar{c}\) and \(b\bar{b}\) (Klusek-Gawenda et al., 2010) production in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. It is the first calculation of exclusive \(D\bar{D}\) meson pair production in \(AA\) collisions, using both the heavy-quark approximation of Choi (1994) and the Brodsky-Lepage formalism with distribution amplitudes from Huang (2007) that describe CLEO data. The results enable experimental feasibility studies at RHIC and LHC, and the strong dependence on theoretical details motivates experimental verification.
