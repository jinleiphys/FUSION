# From Hypernuclei to Hypermatter: a Quantum Monte Carlo Study of Strangeness in Nuclear Structure and Nuclear Astrophysics

> The work presents the recent developments in Quantum Monte Carlo calculations for nuclear systems including strange degrees of freedom. The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm has been extended to the strange sector by the inclusion of the lightest among the hyperons, the $Λ$ particle. This allows to perform detailed calculations for $Λ$ hypernuclei, providing a microscopic framework for the study of the hyperon-nucleon interaction in connection with the available experimental information. The extension of the method for strange neutron matter, put the basis for the first Diffusion Monte Carlo analysis of the hypernuclear medium, with the derivation of neutron star observables of great astrophysical interest.

## Key claim
The Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) algorithm has been extended to include the $\Lambda$ hyperon, enabling microscopic calculations of $\Lambda$ hypernuclei and, for the first time, a Diffusion Monte Carlo analysis of strange neutron matter. The work provides a framework to study the hyperon-nucleon interaction and derive neutron star observables, but the extension to include a $\Lambda N$ space-exchange potential via an extended isospin formalism is noted as not feasible for the phenomenological interactions used, due to the violation of strangeness conservation.

## Method
The AFDMC algorithm was extended to the strange sector by including the $\Lambda$ particle. For finite systems, the trial wave function includes single-particle orbitals referred to the center of mass, Jastrow correlation functions for $\Lambda N$, $NN$, and $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairs, and Slater determinants for nucleons and hyperons. The method was also extended to treat strange neutron matter, providing a basis for studying the hypernuclear medium.

## Key numbers
- The $\Lambda$ hyperon mass is 1115.683(6) MeV.
- The $\Lambda$ hyperon mean life is 2.63(2) $\times 10^{-10}$ s.
- The hyperon separation energy $B_\Lambda$ for $^3_\Lambda$H is 0.13(5) MeV.
- The standard set of hyperon-nucleon scattering data comprises 35 selected $\Lambda p$ low energy scattering data and some $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ data at higher energies, for a total of 52 $YN$ scattering data.

## Context
This work extends Quantum Monte Carlo techniques, previously applied to nucleonic systems, into the strange sector. It builds upon the AFDMC method and aims to connect microscopic calculations of hypernuclei to the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars, where the appearance of hyperons softens the equation of state. The study of $\Lambda$ hypernuclei is essential because the instability of hyperons in vacuum prevents direct hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon scattering experiments, making hypernuclei the primary source of information on the strange sector, as noted in reviews by Hashimoto (2006) and Davis (2005).
