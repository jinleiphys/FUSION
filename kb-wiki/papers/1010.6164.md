# Faddeev fixed-center approximation to the $N\bar{K}K$ system and the signature of a $N^*(1920)(1/2^+)$ state

> We perform a calculation for the three body $N \bar{K} K$ scattering amplitude by using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, taking the interaction between $N$ and $\bar{K}$, $N$ and $K$, and $\bar{K}$ and $K$ from the chiral unitary approach. The resonant structures show up in the modulus squared of the three body scattering amplitude and suggest that a $N\bar{K}K$ hadron state can be formed. Our results are in agreement with others obtained in previous theoretical works, which claim a new $N^*$ resonance around 1920 MeV with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$. The existence of these previous works allows us to test the accuracy of the fixed center approximation in the present problem and sets the grounds for possible application in similar problems, as an explorative tool to determine bound or quasibound three hadron systems.

# Faddeev fixed-center approximation to the $N\bar{K}K$ system and the signature of a $N^*(1920)(1/2^+)$ state

## Key claim

The three-body $N\bar{K}K$ scattering amplitude, calculated using the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations with chiral unitary two-body interactions, shows a resonant structure around 1920 MeV that is consistent with a $N^*$ state of spin-parity $J^P=1/2^+$, but the primary value of this work is to test the reliability of the fixed-center approximation rather than to provide new evidence for the resonance itself.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations using the fixed-center approximation for two configurations: $N$ scattering from a $\bar{K}K$ cluster (forming $f_0(980)$ or $a_0(980)$) and $K$ scattering from a $\bar{K}N$ cluster (forming $\Lambda(1405)$). The two-body $\bar{K}N$, $KN$, and $\bar{K}K$ scattering amplitudes were taken from the chiral unitary approach, and the form factors for the two-body clusters were computed from the same framework. The total three-body scattering amplitude $T$ was obtained by summing single-scattering and double-scattering contributions with the appropriate isospin projections for total isospin $I=1/2$.

## Key numbers

- Peak in $|T|^2$ for $Na_0 \to Na_0$ scattering appears around **1915 MeV** with a width of about **80 MeV** (using the standard form factor extrapolation below threshold gives a peak at **1925 MeV** with width around **30 MeV**)
- Peak in $|T|^2$ for $K\Lambda(1405) \to K\Lambda(1405)$ scattering appears around **1925 MeV**
- The $K\Lambda(1405)$ configuration dominates over the $Na_0$ configuration by a factor of approximately **4** in $|T|^2$ when properly normalized (specifically $|(M_{a_0(980)}/m_K) T_{K\Lambda(1405)}|^2 \simeq 4 |T_{Na_0 \to Na_0}|^2$)
- Cutoff values used: $\Lambda = 1000$ MeV for $f_0/a_0(980)$ and $\Lambda = 630$ MeV for $\Lambda(1405)$
- The bound state masses used are **980 MeV** for $f_0/a_0(980)$ and **1420 MeV** for $\Lambda(1405)$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $N\bar{K}K$ system by Jido (2009) using nonrelativistic variational calculations and by Martinez Torres et al. (2009-2010) using full Faddeev coupled-channel equations, both of which found a bound state around 1920 MeV with $J^P=1/2^+$. The present calculation tests the fixed-center approximation as a simpler tool for three-hadron systems, finding it reliable for this problem and thus enabling its application to other three-hadron systems where bound or quasibound states may exist.
