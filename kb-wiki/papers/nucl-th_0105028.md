# Covariant axial form factor of the nucleon in a chiral constituent quark model

> The axial form factor G_A of the nucleon is investigated for the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model using the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics. The results, being covariant, show large contributions from relativistic boost effects. The predictions are obtained directly from the quark-model wave functions, without any further input such as vertex or constituent-quark form factors, and fall remarkably close to the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The axial form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) of the nucleon, calculated from the Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) constituent quark model using the point-form spectator approximation (PFSA), falls remarkably close to experimental data without any additional parameters such as vertex cutoffs or constituent-quark form factors, but the predicted axial constant \(g_A \equiv G_A(0) = 1.15\) is lower than the experimental value of \(1.255 \pm 0.006\).

## Method

The authors used the point-form approach to relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics (Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics) to compute the nucleon axial form factor. They employed the GBE constituent quark model wave functions from a semirelativistic framework as the sole input, assuming point-like constituent quarks with a quark axial charge \(g_A^q = 1\), and applied the point-form spectator approximation (PFSA) to calculate the reduced matrix elements of the axial current in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- Axial constant predicted: \(g_A \equiv G_A(0) = 1.15\), compared to the experimental value \(g_A = 1.255 \pm 0.006\) from beta-decay experiments.
- Axial radius predicted: \(\langle r_A^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.520\ \text{fm}\), compared to experimental values of \((0.635 \pm 0.023)\ \text{fm}\) from pion electroproduction and \((0.65 \pm 0.07)\ \text{fm}\) from neutrino experiments.
- Nonrelativistic result for the axial constant: \(g_A = 1.65\), close to the SU(6) harmonic oscillator prediction of \(5/3\).
- Experimental axial mass values used for comparison: \(M_A = 1.069 \pm 0.016\ \text{GeV}\) (world average from charged-pion electroproduction), \(M_A = 1.077 \pm 0.039\ \text{GeV}\) (Mainz p(e,e'pi+)n experiment), and \(M_A = 1.032 \pm 0.036\ \text{GeV}\) (world average from neutrino/antineutrino scattering).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (GrazPV) using the same GBE constituent quark model and point-form approach, demonstrating that the same wave functions also successfully describe the axial structure of the nucleon. The results contradict the need for additional phenomenological parameters such as vertex form factors or constituent-quark form factors that were required in earlier approaches (e.g., Riska). The paper enables further tests of the GBE quark model wave functions through calculations of other observables and opens the question of whether two-body current contributions, which were neglected in the PFSA, remain small.
