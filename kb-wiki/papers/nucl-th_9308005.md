# Inclusive scattering of electrons from nuclear matter in the few GeV range

> We study Final State Interaction effects (FSI) in inclusive electron scattering of several GeV from nuclear matter. We consider separately the cases of 'elastic' and 'inelastic' knockout of respectively a nucleon and a non-nucleonic particle. For the former category FSI have been calculated, using the first cumulant approximation of a relativistic generalization of the theory of Gersch et al, including intermediate NN states states with produced hadrons. We also discuss a formal expression for the first cumulant approximation on the basis of the PWIA. FSI between an inelasticlly knocked-out particle and core nucleons are neglected. We present two scaling analyses of the data, from which the above 'inelastic' contributions have been been removed. The data expressed in a relativistic West variable show scaling of a much better quality than the same in the IA scaling variable. Predictions for inclusive cross sections in the two first cumulant versions are presented and discussed. We make a critical comparison of the above with the IA approach of Benhar et al and their results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that final state interactions (FSI) in inclusive electron scattering from nuclear matter at several GeV can be treated using a first cumulant approximation of a relativistic generalization of the non-relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory, but only for the "elastic" knockout of a nucleon; FSI for "inelastic" knockout of non-nucleonic particles are neglected entirely, and the approach relies on the assumption that relativity enters primarily through kinematics of the knocked-out particle while spectator nucleons remain non-relativistic.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic generalization of the GRS theory for the nuclear response, using a first cumulant approximation that retains only binary NN collisions in the final state interactions. They replaced the static potential V in the non-relativistic theory with an effective interaction derived from on-shell pN scattering amplitudes, parameterized by total and reactive cross sections and slope parameters. Two versions of the first cumulant were constructed: one based on the GRS scaling variable y_W^r (relativistic West variable) and another based on the IA scaling variable y_0^r, with the latter omitting the second phase term from the GRS formalism. The "inelastic" contributions from non-nucleonic knockout were treated only in the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) without FSI corrections.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: 0.5 ≤ q (GeV) ≤ 2.4 and energy losses ω ≤ 2.0 GeV
- The non-relativistic Fermi momentum is stated as the typical upper bound for nucleon momenta in the target, with ⟨p⟩ ≤ p_F ≪ m
- The paper reports that data expressed in the relativistic West variable y_W^r show scaling of "much better quality" than the same data expressed in the IA scaling variable y_0^r
- The first cumulant FSI factor (Eq. 3.25) contains the total pN cross section σ_q^{tot} and the total reactive cross section σ_q^{reac}, with the profile function A_q(b) having a Gaussian form with width parameter Q_q^0

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory (Gersch et al., 1973) for the nuclear response to the few-GeV range, incorporating relativistic kinematics and particle production channels through the use of on-shell pN scattering amplitudes. The authors make a critical comparison with the impulse approximation (IA) approach of Benhar et al. (1991), who postulated a different form for FSI. The paper also references earlier work by Butler and Koonin on particle-hole scattering contributions, and by Frankfurt et al. on ratios of inclusive cross sections for finite nuclei. The results enable future studies of color transparency effects and provide a framework for calculating inclusive cross sections that can be tested against nuclear matter quasi-data extracted from finite nuclei.
