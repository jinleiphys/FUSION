# Coherent Contributions of Nuclear Mesons to Electroproduction and the HERMES Effect

> We show that nuclear sigma, omega, and pi mesons can contribute coherently to enhance the electroproduction cross section on nuclei for longitudinal virtual photons at low Q^2 while depleting the cross section for transverse photons. We are able to describe recent HERMES inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering data at low Q^2 and small x using photon-meson and meson-nucleus couplings which are consistent with (but not determined by) existing constraints from meson decay widths, nuclear structure, deep inelastic scattering, and lepton pair production data. We find that while nuclear-coherent pion currents are not important for the present data, they could be observed at different kinematics. Our model for coherent meson electroproduction requires the assumption of mesonic currents and couplings which can be verified in separate experiments. The observation of nuclear-coherent mesons in the final state would verify our theory and allow the identification of a specific dynamical mechanism for higher-twist processes.

# Coherent Contributions of Nuclear Mesons to Electroproduction and the HERMES Effect

## Key claim

Nuclear sigma, omega, and pi mesons can contribute coherently to enhance the electroproduction cross section on nuclei for longitudinal virtual photons at low Q^2 while depleting the cross section for transverse photons, but the coupling constants g and g_{\rho\gamma\sigma} have never been measured and their magnitudes could turn out to be small, so the authors cannot rule out the HERMES data using their theory.

## Method

The authors compute contributions to the nuclear hadronic tensor from coherent meson fields using effective Lagrangians for photon-meson interactions, with nuclear meson fields described by mean-field theory parameters. For the longitudinal cross section, they evaluate the process where a virtual photon interacts with a nuclear omega meson to produce a sigma meson in the final state; for the transverse cross section, they consider destructive interference between the dominant vector meson dominance process and a process where the virtual photon converts a nuclear sigma meson into a vector meson. The calculations use Fermi-form nuclear densities for nitrogen-14 and simple rescaling for helium-3, with form factors and coupling constants chosen to reproduce HERMES data while remaining consistent with constraints from meson decay widths, nuclear structure, deep inelastic scattering, and Drell-Yan data.

## Key numbers

- For nitrogen-14 at Q^2 ~ 0.5 GeV^2 and x ~ 0.01, R^N/R^D (nitrogen vs. deuterium) is approximately 5, resulting from a nuclear enhancement of the longitudinal cross section by about a factor of 2 and a reduction of the transverse cross section by about a factor of 2.5
- The central value of the nuclear omega field V^0 is taken as 20 MeV, within the expected range 13 < V^0 < 28 MeV from mean-field theories
- The upper limit for the gamma-omega-sigma coupling g is set by g^2_UL = 2, derived from the omega -> sigma gamma decay constraint with m_sigma = 600 MeV
- The pion contribution to sigma_L(A)/sigma_L(D) at x = 0.0125 is estimated as approximately 0.01, using f_{\pi/A}(x) ~ 0.01 from infinite nuclear matter calculations
- The coupling g_{\rho\gamma\sigma} needed to describe the strong shadowing is 2.9, compared to a natural expectation of approximately 0.5; if g_{\rho\gamma\sigma} = 0.5, sigma_T(A)/sigma_T(D) ~ 0.95 for x ~ 0.01
- The nuclear sigma field central value is taken as sigma(0) = -36 MeV, between the QHD1 value of -43 MeV and the nonlinear model value of -29 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of meson exchange in nuclear physics, building on mean-field models such as QHD1 (Serot and Walecka) and the nonlinear sigma model (Furnstahl, Price, and Walker 1987), to explain the anomalously strong nuclear dependence of R^A observed by the HERMES collaboration (Ackerstaff et al. 1999) at low Q^2 and small x. The authors show that coherent meson contributions can produce effects much larger than typical shadowing effects (less than 20%) and previous estimates of nuclear enhancements of R^A (Rae and others). The work enables experimental tests through exclusive final-state measurements such as gamma* He-3 -> pi+ H-3, gamma* N-14 -> pi- O-14, and gamma* N-14 -> pi+ C-14, which could identify specific dynamical mechanisms for higher-twist processes in electroproduction.
