# Jet quenching with $T$-dependent running coupling

> We perform an analysis of jet quenching in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies with the temperature dependent running QCD coupling. Our results show that the $T$-dependent QCD coupling largely eliminates the difference between the optimal values of $α_s$ for the RHIC and LHC energies. It may be viewed as direct evidence of the increase of the thermal suppression of $α_s$ with rising temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using a temperature-dependent running QCD coupling in jet quenching calculations largely eliminates the previously observed difference between the optimal values of the coupling constant needed to simultaneously describe RHIC and LHC data, which may be viewed as direct evidence of increased thermal suppression of the coupling with rising temperature.

## Method

The author adapts the light-cone path integral (LCPI) formalism for induced gluon emission to incorporate a temperature-dependent running coupling, using a parametrization of alpha_s(Q,T) motivated by lattice calculations and functional renormalization group results. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed for central rapidity region using a Bjorken 1+1D expanding QGP fireball, and chi-squared fits are performed to determine the optimal parameter kappa (defining the scale Q_fr = kappa T where the coupling freezes) for RHIC 0.2 TeV Au+Au and LHC 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb data separately.

## Key numbers

* For the T-dependent coupling fit to PHENIX Au+Au 0.2 TeV data (p_T > 9 GeV), optimal kappa = 2.65 with chi^2/d.p. = 0.167
* For the T-dependent coupling fit to all LHC Pb+Pb 5.02 TeV data (9 < p_T < 120 GeV), optimal kappa = 3.33 with chi^2/d.p. = 1.04
* For the T-independent coupling fit to PHENIX data, optimal alpha_s^fr = 0.698 with chi^2/d.p. = 0.157
* For the T-independent coupling fit to all LHC data, optimal alpha_s^fr = 0.451 with chi^2/d.p. = 0.93
* Using the LHC-optimized T-dependent kappa = 3.28 for RHIC gives chi^2/d.p. = 0.71-0.81, while using the LHC-optimized T-independent alpha_s^fr = 0.455 for RHIC gives chi^2/d.p. = 4.4-4.8
* Average initial temperature: T_0 ~ 320 MeV for 0.2 TeV Au+Au and T_0 ~ 420 MeV for 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb central collisions

## Context

This work extends previous jet quenching analyses using the LCPI approach (Zakharov 2004, 2008, 2013, 2020) that found a discrepancy between the optimal frozen coupling values for RHIC and LHC data, with a similar difference reported in terms of the transport coefficient q-hat by the JET Collaboration and Salgado et al. The study is motivated by lattice calculations of the effective in-medium coupling (Bazavov et al.) and functional renormalization group results (RG1). The T-dependent coupling largely resolves the tension between RHIC and LHC data, enabling future work to extract the temperature dependence of the QCD coupling from jet quenching measurements and to explore possible enhancements near T_c from color-magnetic monopole effects.
