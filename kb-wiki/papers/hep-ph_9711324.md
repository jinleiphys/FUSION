# Formation and Evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Imposing an equilibrium between the thermal pressure of deconfined quarks and gluons and the dynamical compression pressure exercised by in-flowing nuclear matter, we study the initial thermal conditions reached in a quark-gluon plasma fireball formed in a relativistic heavy ion collision. We show that entropy is produced primarily in the pre-equilibrium stage of the reaction. We test our approach, comparing our results with the S-W/Pb collision results at 200 GeV A and find a surprising degree of agreement assuming about 50% stopping. We apply our method to a determination of the conditions in collisions of Au-Au at 11 GeV A and Pb-Pb at 157 GeV A, assuming full stopping of momentum, energy and baryon number. Our detailed results directly determine the spectral shape and abundance of (strange) hadrons and electromagnetic probes (photons, dileptons) produced in the collision, and we explore specific experimental consequences.

## Key claim

By imposing a pressure balance between the thermal pressure of deconfined quarks and gluons and the dynamical compression pressure from in-flowing nuclear matter, the authors claim they can determine the initial thermal conditions of a quark-gluon plasma fireball ab initio, but this relies on assuming specific stopping fractions (about 50% for S→W/Pb at 200 GeV A, and full stopping for Au→Au at 11 GeV A and Pb→Pb at 157 GeV A).

## Method

The authors equate the dynamical pressure from in-flowing nuclear matter (derived from the energy-momentum tensor using the incident CM momentum per nucleon, the nuclear density, and a momentum stopping fraction) with the thermal pressure of the QGP, computed from a perturbative QCD equation of state that includes thermal masses, chemical non-equilibrium occupancy factors for gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks, and a vacuum pressure term. They then solve for temperature and baryochemical potential at three stages: the initial thermalization time (t_th), the chemical equilibration time for light quarks and gluons (t_ch), and after strangeness production (t_s), using conservation of energy per baryon and the pressure balance condition.

## Key numbers

- For S→W/Pb at 200 GeV A (E_CM = 8.8 GeV, stopping η = 0.5): the observed source temperature T_0 = 233 MeV (with γ_s = 0.8), compared to the experimental value of 232 ± 5 MeV from transverse mass spectra.
- For Au→Au at 11 GeV A (E_CM = 2.6 GeV, full stopping): the initial thermal temperature T_th = 0.260 GeV, the chemical equilibration temperature T_ch = 0.212 GeV, and the temperature after strangeness production T_0 = 0.190 GeV.
- For Pb→Pb at 157 GeV A (E_CM = 8.6 GeV, full stopping): the predicted observable temperature T_0 = 270 MeV, with baryon density ρ/ρ_0 = 1.81 and specific entropy S/B = 41.7.
- The dynamical pressure benchmark values: P_dyn = 0.5 GeV/fm^3 at γ_CM = 3.4 (corresponding to 23.6 GeV/c^2 A projectile) and P_dyn = 1 GeV/fm^3 at γ_CM = 6.4 (86.3 GeV/c^2 A), using normal nuclear density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The strange-quark fugacity for Si→Au at 14.6 GeV A from earlier analysis: λ_s = 1.72 ± 0.19, distinctly different from unity.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Letessier, Rafelski, Tounsi 1993, 1994) that used experimental data to infer QGP properties, by now deriving those properties from first principles using a pressure balance condition. It contradicts the hadron gas (Hagedorn gas) scenario by showing that the entropy content per baryon (S/B ≈ 40-50 for 200 GeV A collisions) is nearly twice as large as what a confined hadronic state would produce, supporting the QGP interpretation. The results enable predictions for the upcoming Pb→Pb collisions at 157 GeV A and provide a framework for calculating electromagnetic probe yields (dileptons, photons) from the evolving QGP fireball, building on earlier dilepton calculations by Kämpfer and collaborators (1992).
