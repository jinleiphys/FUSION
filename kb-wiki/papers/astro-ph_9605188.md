# Impact of medium effects on the cooling of non-superfluid and superfluid neutron stars

> Neutrino emission from the dense hadronic component in neutron stars is subject to strong modifications due to collective effects in the nuclear medium. We implement new estimates of the neutrino emissivities of two processes operating in the nuclear medium into numerical cooling simulations of neutron stars. The first process is the modified Urca process, for which the softening of the pion exchange mode and other polarization effects as well as the neutrino emission arising from the intermediate reaction states are taken into account. The second process concerns neutrino emission through superfluid pair breaking and formation processes. It is found that the medium effects on the emissivity of the modified Urca process result in a strong density dependence, which gives a smooth crossover from the standard to the nonstandard cooling scenario for increasing star masses. For superfluid stars, the superfluid pair breaking and formation processes accelerate mildly both the standard and the nonstandard cooling scenario. This leads to a good agreement between the theoretical cooling tracks and the rather low temperatures observed for objects like PSRs 0833-45 (Vela), 0656+14, and 0630+18 (Geminga). The robustness of our findings against variations in both the underlying equation of state of baryonic matter and the used fast cooling processes is demonstrated. Hence we conclude that the two recalculated neutrino emissivities studied here enable one to reproduce theoretically most of the observed pulsar temperatures by varying the masses of neutron star models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including medium-modified neutrino emissivities for the modified Urca process (MU-VS86) and superfluid pair-breaking/formation processes (NPBF/PPBF) in neutron star cooling simulations produces a smooth, mass-dependent transition from standard to nonstandard cooling scenarios, enabling theoretical cooling tracks to match both hotter and cooler observed pulsar temperatures; however, this agreement depends on the specific parametrizations of the effective pion gap and nucleon-nucleon correlations, which are not well constrained at high densities.

## Method

The authors implemented new density-dependent neutrino emissivity formulas for the modified Urca process (incorporating pion mode softening, polarization effects, and intermediate-state neutrino emission) and for superfluid neutron and proton pair-breaking/formation processes into a general relativistic numerical cooling code. They computed cooling curves for neutron star models using four different equations of state (non-relativistic variational UV14+UVII, relativistic Hartree HV and G300, and relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock RBHF(B)+HFV), with and without superfluidity, and with and without fast cooling processes (direct Urca and pion Urca), then compared the resulting surface temperatures to observed X-ray data from pulsars including PSRs 0833-45 (Vela), 0656+14, and 0630+18 (Geminga).

## Key numbers

- The medium-modified Urca emissivity (MU-VS86) is given by approximately 2.4 x 10^24 erg cm^-3 s^-1 times a density-dependent factor (n/n0)^(10/3) times (m*_N/m_N)^4 times [m_pi/(alpha * tilde-omega)]^8 times Gamma^8 times T_9^8, where T_9 = T/10^9 K and n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is nuclear saturation density.
- The neutron pair-breaking/formation (NPBF) emissivity is approximately 6.6 x 10^28 erg cm^-3 s^-1 times (n/n0)^(1/3) times (m*_N/m_N) times (Delta_n/MeV)^7 times I(Delta_n/T) for T below the neutron critical temperature.
- The proton pair-breaking/formation (PPBF) emissivity is approximately 1.7 x 10^28 erg cm^-3 s^-1 times (n/n0)^(1/3) times (m*_N/m_N) times (Delta_p/MeV)^7 times I(Delta_p/T) for T below the proton critical temperature.
- The direct Urca emissivity is approximately 4 x 10^27 erg cm^-3 s^-1 times (n_e/n0)^(1/3) times (m*_N/m_N)^2 times Gamma^2 times T_9^6, with superfluid suppression factors.
- The pion Urca emissivity is approximately 1.5 x 10^27 erg cm^-3 s^-1 times (p_Fn/m_pi) times (m*_N/m_N)^2 times Gamma^4 times T_9^6 times sin^2(theta) for densities above the pion condensation threshold.
- The effective pion gap at saturation density is tilde-omega^2(n0) approximately 0.8-0.95 m_pi^2, and the vertex correlation factor Gamma(n0) is approximately 0.4.
- The transition density between crust and core is assumed to be rho_tr = 1.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3.
- Superfluid regions: neutrons in 1S0 state from 7 x 10^11 to 2 x 10^14 g cm^-3, protons in 1S0 state from 2 x 10^14 to 4 x 10^14 g cm^-3, neutrons in 3P2 state from 2 x 10^14 to 5 x 10^15 g cm^-3.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino emissivities by Friman and Maxwell (1979) for the modified Urca process (MU-FM79) and by Flowers et al. (1976) for pair-breaking processes, incorporating medium modifications from Voskresensky and Senatorov (1984, 1986, 1987) that produce a strong density dependence absent in the free one-pion-exchange approximation. The results enable a unified description of both slowly cooling pulsars (consistent with standard modified Urca cooling) and rapidly cooling objects like Vela and Geminga, which previously required either fast cooling processes (direct Urca, pion Urca) that tended to over-cool, or ad-hoc mass variations; the medium-modified rates provide a smooth mass-dependent transition between these regimes. The robustness of the conclusions is tested against variations in the equation of state (using models from Wiringa et al. 1988, Weber and Weigel 1989, Glendenning 1989, and Huber et al. 1994) and against uncertainties in superfluid gap parameters from Ainsworth et al. (1989), Amundsen and Ostgaard (1985), and Wambach et al. (1991).
