# Fast partons as a source of energy and momentum in a perturbative quark-gluon plasma

> I derive the space-time distribution of energy and momentum deposited by a fast parton traversing a perturbative quark-gluon plasma by considering the fast parton as the source of an external color field interacting with the medium. I include the medium's response to screen the fast parton's color field by incorporating dielectric functions and compare to the unscreened result.

## Key claim

The paper derives the space-time distribution of energy and momentum deposited by a fast parton traversing a perturbative quark-gluon plasma, treating the parton as an external color field source and including the medium's screening response via dielectric functions; the derivation assumes a weakly coupled plasma at asymptotically high temperature and that the resulting source term can be coupled to hydrodynamic equations at distance scales of order \((g^4 T)^{-1}\).

## Method

The author treats the fast parton as the source of an external color field interacting with a perturbative QGP through the collisionless Boltzmann equation, obtaining the microscopic equation of motion and then taking moments via the Chapman-Enskog approach under the assumption of local thermal equilibrium. The color fields generated by the fast parton are expressed in Fourier space using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation with dielectric functions \(\epsilon_L\) and \(\epsilon_T\) that encode the medium's screening response. The resulting source term \(J^\nu\) is evaluated explicitly both with and without dielectric screening by performing angular integrations over the directional velocity of medium particles and reducing the problem to twelve two-dimensional integrals in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass for gluons in HTL approximation: \(m_{\rm D} = \sqrt{N_c/3}\, gT\); with flavor included: \(m_{\rm D} = \sqrt{(2 N_c + N_f)/6}\, gT\).
- For the unscreened case, the total energy deposited per unit length is \(-\frac{dE}{dx} = \frac{\alpha_s ({Q_p^a})^2 m_{\rm D}^2}{2} \ln \left[\frac{\rho_{\rm max}}{\rho_{\rm min}}\right]\left(1-\frac{y}{\gamma^2 u}\right)\), where \(y = \cosh^{-1}(\gamma)\) is the rapidity.
- The unscreened source term for energy density deposited per unit time is \(J^0(\rho,z,t) = d(\rho,z,t)\gamma u^2 \times\left(1 - \frac{z_{-}}{(z_{-}^2 + \rho^2)}\left(z_{-} + \frac{\gamma u \rho^2}{\sqrt{\rho^2 + z_{-}^2 \gamma^2}}\right)\right)\), where \(z_{-} = (z - u t)\) and \(d(\rho,z,t) = \frac{\alpha_s ({Q_p^a})^2 m_{\rm D}^2}{8\pi(\rho^2 + \gamma^2(z-ut)^2)^{3/2}}\).
- The unscreened transverse momentum density deposited per unit time is given by \({\bf J}_\text{v}(\rho,z,t) = \left(\bald{x} - \bald{u} t\right)\frac{\alpha_s ({Q_p^a})^2 m_{\rm D}^2}{8\pi \left(z_{-}^2+\rho^2\right)^2} \times\left(\frac{u^4 \rho^4+\left(z_{-}^2 \gamma ^2+\rho ^2\right) \left(2 z_{-}^2+\frac{\left(u^2+2\right)\rho^2}{\gamma^2}\right)}{\left(z_{-}^2 \gamma^2+\rho ^2\right)^2} -\frac{2 u z_{-}}{\gamma  \sqrt{z_{-}^2 \gamma^2+\rho ^2}}\right)\).
- The plots are scaled by \(m_{\rm D}^5 ({Q_p^a})^2 \approx 20.4 \text{ GeV}/\text{fm}^4\) for a gluon moving in a QGP at 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the schematic derivation by Neufeld et al. (2008) that gave a hydrodynamic source term for a fast parton in the absence of in-medium screening, finding a propagating Mach cone in the resulting linearized hydrodynamic evolution. It addresses the open question raised by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2004) and Chaudhuri (2005) regarding the explicit form of the energy-momentum source term generated by a supersonic parton, which is needed to predict the spectrum and shape of sound waves excited in the medium. The derived source term enables future work coupling this first-principles distribution to hydrodynamic simulations of the QGP, with the author noting that a calculation of the source at intermediate scales will be presented in a future publication.
