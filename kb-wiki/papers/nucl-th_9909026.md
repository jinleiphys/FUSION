# Effective density dependent pairing forces in the T=1 and T=0 channels

> Effective density dependent pairing forces of zero range are adjusted on gap values in T=0,1 channels calculated with the Paris force in symmetric nuclear matter. General discussions on the pairing force are presented. In conjunction with the effective k-mass the nuclear pairing force seems to need very little renormalization in the T=1 channel. The situation in the T=0 channel is also discussed.

# Effective density dependent pairing forces in the T=1 and T=0 channels

## Key claim

The authors argue that, at least as a first guess, the bare nucleon-nucleon potential should be used as the pairing force in the gap equation, and that this strategy is not necessarily orthogonal to the use of density dependent zero range forces with a cutoff. However, they caution that in the T=0 channel the situation may be more subtle because the tensor force, which makes this channel more attractive, may be more affected by screening than the rest of the force.

## Method

The authors solve the gap equation for homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter using the bare Paris force, and compare results with those from the Gogny force (D1 and D1S parameterizations). They then adjust a density dependent zero range pairing force of the form proposed by Bertsch and Esbensen (1991) to reproduce the gap values obtained from the Paris force in both the T=1 and T=0 channels, using a cutoff energy and including a density dependent effective mass taken from the Gogny force.

## Key numbers

- For the T=1 channel fit with effective mass: cutoff energy epsilon_C = 60 MeV, eta = 0.45, alpha = 0.47, v0 = 481 MeV fm^3
- For the T=1 channel fit with bare mass (m*/m = 1): cutoff energy epsilon_C = 60 MeV, eta = 0.70, alpha = 0.45, v0 = 481 MeV fm^3
- The Gogny D1S scattering length is a_D1S = 12.16 fm, compared to the experimental neutron-neutron scattering length a = 18.5 fm
- The T=0 channel gap values reach more than a factor of two larger than in the T=1 channel when using the bare Paris force
- For the T=0 channel fit, v0 = -1.05 (hbar^2/m) (2 pi^2 / k_C)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bertsch and Esbensen (1991) for constructing density dependent zero range pairing forces, refining their arguments and extending them to the T=0 proton-neutron pairing channel. The authors argue against the widespread practice of using G-matrices (such as Skyrme forces) in the gap equation, noting that this leads to double counting, and demonstrate that the Gogny force (Decharge and Gogny, 1980) acts very nearly like a bare force in the T=1 pairing channel. The resulting parametrizations are intended for use in finite nuclei calculations via the local density approximation, following the work of Kucharek et al. (1989). The T=0 channel results are based on the earlier calculations of Baldo, Lombardo and Schuck (1995).
