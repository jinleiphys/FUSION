# Constraints on neutron star radii based on chiral effective field theory interactions

> We show that microscopic calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions constrain the properties of neutron-rich matter below nuclear densities to a much higher degree than is reflected in commonly used equations of state. Combined with observed neutron star masses, our results lead to a radius R = 9.7 - 13.9 km for a 1.4 M_{solar} star, where the theoretical range is due, in about equal amounts, to uncertainties in many-body forces and to the extrapolation to high densities.

## Key claim

Microscopic calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions constrain the pressure of neutron-rich matter below nuclear densities to within ±25%, leading to a radius R = 9.7 - 13.9 km for a 1.4 M_sun star, where the theoretical range is due in about equal amounts to uncertainties in many-body forces and to the extrapolation to high densities.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of neutron matter using chiral low-momentum two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, with the 3N coupling constants c1 and c3 varied over ranges determined from extractions based on the subleading two-pion-exchange NN interaction. They extended these results to matter in beta equilibrium using a parametrization involving the symmetry energy, and then extended the equation of state to higher densities using piecewise polytropes with indices Γ1 and Γ2 and transition density ρ12, constrained by causality and the requirement that the EOS support a 1.65 M_sun star. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for non-rotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter energy per particle at saturation density ρ0 = 2.7 × 10^14 g cm^-3: E_n(ρ0)/N = 16.3 ± 2.2 MeV (including 3N forces), compared to E^NN_n(ρ0)/N = 10.4 MeV (NN-only)
- Symmetry energy parameters: S2(ρ0) = 30.1 MeV with γ = 0.5 for low-pressure limit (c1 = -0.7 GeV^-1, c3 = -2.2 GeV^-1); S2(ρ0) = 34.4 MeV with γ = 0.6 for high-pressure limit (c1 = -1.4 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.8 GeV^-1)
- Pressure at saturation density: P(ρ0) = 1.4 - 2.1 MeV fm^-3
- Radius for a 1.4 M_sun star: R = 9.7 - 13.9 km (final result including crust effects)
- Radius for a 1.4 M_sun star without crust correction: R = 9.3 - 13.5 km
- Radius for a 1.4 M_sun star using NN-only interactions: R^NN = 8.8 - 11.0 km
- If a 2.0 M_sun star were observed, radius range reduces to R = 10.5 - 13.3 km
- Neutron skin thickness for ^208Pb: δR = 0.14 - 0.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the microscopic neutron matter calculations of Hebeler et al. (2009) by improving the range of the 3N coupling c3 and applying the results to neutron star structure. It contradicts the much wider spread of radii found in commonly used equations of state such as the Shen EOS, which gives R ≈ 15 km, and reduces the pressure spread at nuclear densities from a factor of 6 to a factor of 1.5 compared to the representative set of EOS compiled by Lattimer and Prakash (2001). The results enable more rigorous constraints on neutron star radii for upcoming observations, including gravitational wave signals from merging binary neutron stars and the PREX parity-violating electron scattering experiment on ^208Pb.
