# Final state interaction effects in exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron

> The exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron is considered within the Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable interaction kernel. The relativistic kernel of nucleon-nucleon interaction is obtained considering the phase shifts in the elastic neutron-proton scattering and properties of the deuteron. The differential cross section is calculated within the impulse approximation under several kinematic conditions of the Bonn experiment. Final state interactions between the outgoing nucleons are taken into account. Partial-wave states of the neutron-proton pair with total angular momentum J=0,1 are considered.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable interaction kernel can describe final state interaction effects in exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration, but the agreement with Bonn experimental data is limited to specific kinematic ranges; for example, under kinematic condition set II the calculation with FSI describes data only up to about 2.3 GeV in the np pair invariant mass, and deviations appear above that.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon-nucleon T matrix using a separable ansatz for the interaction kernel, with parameters fitted to elastic np phase shifts and deuteron properties. The exclusive three-differential cross section for the d(e,e'p)n reaction is computed in the relativistic impulse approximation, first in the plane-wave approximation (PWA) and then including final state interactions (FSI) via the T matrix. Partial-wave states of the np pair with total angular momentum J=0,1 are considered, using the rank-six MY6 potential for the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel and multirank separable potentials for the 1S0, 1P1, 3P0, and 3P1 channels.

## Key numbers

- Under kinematic condition set II of the Bonn experiment (Breuker 1985), the FSI calculation describes data for np pair invariant mass sqrt(s) from 2.15 GeV to 2.3 GeV, but deviates from data above 2.3 GeV.
- The kinematic conditions span electron beam energies E_e from 1.2 GeV to 1.569 GeV, with scattered electron energies E_e' ranging from 0.800 GeV to 1.175 GeV.
- The momentum transfer squared Q^2 ranges from 0.106 (GeV/c)^2 to 0.257 (GeV/c)^2 across the five kinematic sets.
- The np pair invariant mass sqrt(s) ranges from 1.9675 GeV to 2.3325 GeV across all kinematic conditions.
- The missing mass sqrt(s)-2m ranges from 0.090 GeV to 0.455 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach with separable kernels developed by Bondarenko et al. (2008, 2010) to exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration, where previous nonrelativistic models (Forest 1983, Arenhovel 1982) required FSI and two-body currents for agreement at higher energies. The calculations are compared with Bonn experimental data from Breuker (1985) and Boden (1992). The results show that FSI effects increase the cross section and become more important at higher np pair energies, but the model requires further investigation of non-nucleon degrees of freedom at high s and inelasticity effects, which the authors state will be addressed in a separate paper.
