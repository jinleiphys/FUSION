# Finite-Volume Electromagnetic Corrections to the Masses of Mesons, Baryons and Nuclei

> Now that Lattice QCD calculations are beginning to include QED, it is important to better understand how hadronic properties are modified by finite-volume QED effects. They are known to exhibit power-law scaling with volume, in contrast to the exponential behavior of finite-volume strong interaction effects. We use non-relativistic effective field theories describing the low-momentum behavior of hadrons to determine the finite-volume QED corrections to the masses of mesons, baryons and nuclei out to O(1/L^4) in a volume expansion, where L is the spatial extent of the cubic volume. This generalizes the previously determined expansion for mesons, and extends it by two orders in 1/L to include contributions from the charge radius, magnetic moment and polarizabilities of the hadron. We make an observation about direct calculations of the muon g-2 in a finite volume.

## Key claim

The paper uses non-relativistic effective field theories (NREFTs) to determine finite-volume QED corrections to hadron masses out to O(1/L^4), extending previous work by two orders in 1/L to include contributions from charge radius, magnetic moment, and polarizabilities, but the results are valid only in the continuum limit of lattice QCD calculations and neglect exponentially suppressed strong-interaction effects of order e^{-m_pi L}.

## Method

The authors construct non-relativistic effective field theories (scalar NRQED for mesons and J=0 nuclei, and NRQED for baryons and J=1/2 nuclei) that describe the low-momentum behavior of hadrons coupled to electromagnetic fields in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions and the photon zero modes removed. They compute one-loop Feynman diagrams in these EFTs to extract the finite-volume QED mass shifts order by order in a 1/L expansion, matching the EFT coefficients to experimentally known hadronic properties such as charge radii, magnetic moments, and polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

* Leading-order (LO) finite-volume QED mass shift for a charged scalar particle: delta m_phi^(LO) = (alpha_e Q^2)/(2 L) * c_1, where c_1 = -2.83729
* Next-to-leading order (NLO) recoil correction: delta m_phi^(NLO) = (alpha_e Q^2)/(m_phi L^2) * c_1
* Charge-radius contribution at N^2LO: delta m_phi^(N^2LO) = + (2 pi alpha_e Q)/(3 L^3) * <r^2>_phi
* For a volume with L = 4 fm, the finite-volume QED mass shift of a charged meson is approximately 0.5 MeV
* Charged pion charge radius: sqrt(<r^2>)_(pi+) = 0.672 +/- 0.008 fm
* Charged kaon charge radius: sqrt(<r^2>)_(K+) = 0.560 +/- 0.031 fm
* Proton anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_p = 1.792847356(23)
* Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_n = -1.9130427(5) * M_n/M_p
* Proton charge radius: <r^2>_p = 0.768 +/- 0.012 fm^2
* Baldin sum rule for proton: alpha_E^(p) + beta_M^(p) = (13.69 +/- 0.14) x 10^(-4) fm^3
* Baldin sum rule for neutron: alpha_E^(n) + beta_M^(n) = (15.2 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3

## Context

This work generalizes and extends previous finite-volume QED calculations for mesons by Hayakawa (2008) and others, which had determined corrections only out to O(1/L^2) using chiral perturbation theory. The NREFT approach provides a systematic framework that can be applied to baryons and nuclei, and the results enable lattice QCD practitioners to more accurately correct for finite-volume QED effects when extracting infinite-volume hadron masses, particularly important as lattice calculations at physical pion mass with m_pi L ~ 4 are now including QED. The authors also make an observation about direct calculations of the muon g-2 in a finite volume.
