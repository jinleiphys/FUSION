# Constraints on Two-Body Axial Currents from Reactor Antineutrino-Deuteron Breakup Reactions

> We discuss how to reduce theoretical uncertainties in the neutrino-deuteron breakup cross-sections crucial to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory's efforts to measure the solar neutrino flux. In effective field theory, the dominant uncertainties in all neutrino-deuteron reactions can be expressed through a single, common, isovector axial two-body current parameterized by $L_{1,A}$. After briefly reviewing the status of fixing $L_{1,A}$ experimentally, we present a constraint on $L_{1,A}$ imposed by existing reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup data. This constraint alone leads to an uncertainty of 6-7% at 7 MeV neutrino energy in the cross-sections relevant to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory. However, more significantly for the Sudbury experiment, the constraint implies an uncertainty of only 0.7% in the ratio of charged to neutral current cross-sections used to verify the existence of neutrino oscillations, at the same energy. This is the only direct experimental constraint from the two-body system, to date, of the uncertainty in these cross-sections.

## Key claim

The dominant theoretical uncertainties in all neutrino-deuteron breakup reactions relevant to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory can be expressed through a single isovector axial two-body current parameter \(L_{1,A}\) in effective field theory; existing reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup data constrain this parameter to \(\overline{L}_{1,A}^{\text{reactor}} = 3.6 \pm 5.5\ \text{fm}^3\), which translates to a 6-7% uncertainty in the individual charged-current and neutral-current cross sections at 7 MeV but only a 0.7% uncertainty in their ratio.

## Method

The authors apply pionless effective field theory (EFT(\(\pi\hskip-0.6em/\))) to compute the charged-current (\(\overline{\nu}_e d \rightarrow e^+ nn\)) and neutral-current (\(\overline{\nu}_x d \rightarrow \overline{\nu}_x np\)) cross sections for reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They parameterize each cross section as \(\sigma(E) = a(E) + b(E) L_{1,A}\) using tabulated coefficients from prior work, update the axial coupling \(g_A\) from 1.26 to 1.267, and include electromagnetic radiative corrections (1.5% for NC, 3.5% for CC). They then extract \(L_{1,A}\) by comparing the energy-integrated cross sections (in cm\(^2\)/fission) from three reactor experiments (Rovno, Krasnoyarsk, Bugey) with the EFT predictions, averaging the six independent measurements (three CC, three NC) to obtain a combined constraint.

## Key numbers

- Combined reactor constraint: \(\overline{L}_{1,A}^{\text{reactor}} = 3.6 \pm 5.5\ \text{fm}^3\) (statistical uncertainty \(\pm 4.6\ \text{fm}^3\) from six measurements, systematic uncertainty \(\lesssim 3\ \text{fm}^3\) from higher-order corrections, added in quadrature).
- Individual CC cross sections (in \(10^{-44}\ \text{cm}^2/\text{fission}\)): Rovno \(1.17 \pm 0.16\), Krasnoyarsk \(1.05 \pm 0.12\), Bugey \(0.95 \pm 0.20\).
- Individual NC cross sections (in \(10^{-44}\ \text{cm}^2/\text{fission}\)): Rovno \(2.71 \pm 0.47\), Krasnoyarsk \(3.09 \pm 0.30\), Bugey \(3.15 \pm 0.40\).
- Inferred \(L_{1,A}\) values (in fm\(^3\)): CC Rovno \(17.4 \pm 13.9\), NC Rovno \(-2.0 \pm 13.8\), CC Krasnoyarsk \(-1.3 \pm 9.5\), NC Krasnoyarsk \(1.8 \pm 8.1\), CC Bugey \(-1.5 \pm 17.2\), NC Bugey \(11.1 \pm 11.7\).
- At 7 MeV neutrino energy: individual CC and NC cross-section uncertainty 6-7%; CC/NC ratio uncertainty 0.7%.
- Tritium beta decay constraint (NNLO): \(L_{1,A}^{\text{Schiavilla et al.}} = 4.2 \pm 3.7\ \text{fm}^3\).
- Helioseismology constraint (NNLO): \(L_{1,A} = 4.8 \pm 6.7\ \text{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of neutrino-deuteron reactions by Butler, Chen, and Kong (2001) by providing the first direct experimental constraint on the two-body axial current \(L_{1,A}\) from the two-body system itself, using reactor antineutrino data from Rovno, Krasnoyarsk, and Bugey. The result is consistent with independent constraints from tritium beta decay (Schiavilla et al.) and helioseismology, but is totally independent of both. The constraint enables the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory to reduce theoretical uncertainties in its solar neutrino flux measurements, particularly in the CC/NC ratio used to verify neutrino oscillations, while also motivating future higher-order EFT calculations and more precise reactor experiments.
