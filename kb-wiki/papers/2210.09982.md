# Extended superscaling with two-particle emission in electron and neutrino scattering

> An extended superscaling analysis of quasielastic electron scattering data is proposed by parametrizing the scaling function as the sum of a symmetric function corresponding to the emission of a single particle plus a contribution from the phase space of two-particle emission. The phase space of two-particle emission (2p2h) is multiplied by a q-dependent parameter that has been fitted to describe the tail behavior of the scaling function. This approach allows for an alternative description of the quasielastic electron scattering data, incorporating the contributions from both single-particle and two-particle emission processes induced by the one-body current and explaining the asymmetry of the scaling function. In a factorized schematic model based on the independent-pair approximation, the 2p2h parameter is related to the high-momentum distribution of the pair averaged over 2p-2h excitations. However, in the phenomenological fitting approach undertaken here, this coefficient includes other contributions such as interference with two-body currents and effects of the final-state interactions. We present predictions for the inclusive two-nucleon emission cross section induced by electrons and neutrinos.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the high-energy tail of the quasielastic scaling function, which is asymmetric and extends beyond the relativistic Fermi gas prediction, can be parametrized as a sum of a symmetric 1p1h component plus a 2p2h contribution proportional to the two-particle phase space, with the 2p2h coefficient fitted to data but not uniquely attributable to short-range correlations because it also encodes interference with two-body currents and final-state interaction effects.

## Method

The authors perform a superscaling analysis of inclusive electron scattering data using the SuSAM* (superscaling with relativistic effective mass) framework, where the experimental cross section is divided by the single-nucleon response to obtain the phenomenological scaling function. They parametrize this scaling function as the sum of a symmetric function (representing 1p1h emission) plus a term proportional to the 2p2h phase space integral multiplied by a q-dependent coefficient fitted to reproduce the tail behavior for high energy transfer. The same 2p2h parameters extracted from electron scattering are then applied to predict charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections by replacing electromagnetic single-nucleon responses with weak ones.

## Key numbers

- The 2p2h contribution to the quasielastic cross section is about 20% of the total cross section (cited from prior work, not original to this paper)
- The analysis focuses on intermediate momentum transfer q ≤ 1 GeV/c, which is the region of interest for neutrino experiments
- The semiempirical formula for 2p2h responses includes a factor of 1/(m_N^2 m_pi^4) to make the 2p2h parameters c_K^{NN'}(q) dimensionless, where m_pi is the pion mass
- The phase space function F_{NN'}(q,omega) is defined for pp, pn, and nn correlated pairs with separate Fermi momenta for protons and neutrons

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* superscaling analysis of Amaro et al. (2017, 2018) and the MEC 2p2h subtraction approach of Martinez-Consentino et al. (2021), where the 2p2h meson-exchange current contribution was removed from data to obtain a cleaner scaling function. The new parametrization is alternative and complementary to the search for short-range correlation pairs from inclusive electron scattering data at high momentum transfer q > 1.5 GeV/c by Weiss et al. (2021). The results enable predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the 2p2h channel induced by the one-body current, complementing the existing MEC-based 2p2h predictions for neutrino scattering from Martinez-Consentino et al. (2021b).
