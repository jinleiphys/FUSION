# Spectral functions of isoscalar scalar and isovector electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon at two-loop order

> We calculate the imaginary parts of the isoscalar scalar and isovector electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon up to two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory. Particular attention is paid on the correct behavior of Im $σ_N(t)$ and Im $G_{E,M}^V(t)$ at the two-pion threshold $t_0=4 m_π^2$ in connection with the non-relativistic 1/M-expansion. We recover the well-known strong enhancement near threshold originating from the nearby anomalous singularity at $t_c = 4m_π^2-m_π^4/M^2 = 3.98 m_π^2$. In the case of the scalar spectral function Im $σ_N(t)$ one finds a significant improvement in comparison to the lowest order one-loop result. Higher order $ππ$-rescattering effects are however still necessary to close a remaining 20%-gap to the empirical scalar spectral function. The isovector electric and magnetic spectral functions Im $G_{E,M}^V(t)$ get additionally enhanced near threshold by the two-pion-loop contributions. After supplementing their two-loop results by a phenomenological $ρ$-meson exchange term one can reproduce the empirical isovector electric and magnetic spectral functions fairly well.

## Key claim

The two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation recovers the correct threshold behavior of the isovector electromagnetic spectral functions (Im \(G_{E,M}^V(t) \sim (t-4m_\pi^2)^{3/2}\)) and the strong enhancement near threshold from the anomalous singularity, but a 20% gap remains between the two-loop result for the isoscalar scalar spectral function and the empirical one, indicating that higher-order \(\pi\pi\)-rescattering effects are still needed.

## Method

The imaginary parts of the isoscalar scalar form factor \(\sigma_N(t)\) and the isovector electric and magnetic (Sachs) form factors \(G_{E,M}^V(t)\) of the nucleon were calculated up to two-loop order in chiral perturbation theory, using a non-relativistic \(1/M\)-expansion that preserves the correct analytical structure at the two-pion threshold \(t_0 = 4m_\pi^2\). The contributions were grouped into four classes: one-loop diagrams with leading-order relativistic \(\pi N\) vertices, one-loop diagrams with second-order chiral \(\pi\pi NN\)-contact vertices (proportional to low-energy constants \(c_{1,2,3,4}\)), two-loop diagrams representing \(\pi N\)-scattering and \(\pi\pi\)-rescattering, and factorizable two-loop diagrams involving the one-loop scalar or charge form factor of the pion.

## Key numbers

* The anomalous singularity lies at \(t_c = 4m_\pi^2 - m_\pi^4/M^2 = 3.98\,m_\pi^2\), close to the two-pion threshold \(t_0 = 4m_\pi^2\).
* The one-loop result for Im \(\sigma_N(t)\) is given by eq.(5) with parameters \(g_A = 1.3\), \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.57\) MeV, and \(M = 938.27\) MeV.
* The low-energy constants used are: \(c_1 = -1.4\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_2 = 3.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -6.0\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 3.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(\bar d_1 + \bar d_2 = 3.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\bar d_3 = -3.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\bar d_5 = 0.1\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The mean square charge radius of the pion is \(\langle r^2_{ch} \rangle_\pi = (0.44 \pm 0.01)\) fm\(^2\); the mean square scalar radius of the pion is \(\langle r^2_S \rangle_\pi = (0.61 \pm 0.04)\) fm\(^2\).
* The two-loop result for Im \(\sigma_N(t)\) still underestimates the empirical peak at \(t \simeq 5 m_\pi^2\) by about 20%.
* The \(\rho(770)\)-meson mass is taken as \(m_\rho = 769.3\) MeV with coupling \(g_{\rho\pi} = 6.03\) (from \(\Gamma_\rho(m_\rho^2) = 150.2\) MeV); the parameters \(b_E = 1.0\) and \(b_M = 3.5\) are adjusted to the empirical \(\rho\)-resonance peak at \(t \simeq 28 m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculations of the isovector electromagnetic form factors in baryon chiral perturbation theory by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) and the heavy-baryon one-loop results of the spectral functions by the same author (2000), correcting the threshold behavior that was formally incorrect in the heavy nucleon limit. It also builds on the dispersion relation analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Hohler and Pietarinen (1975) and the more recent refinements by the Bonn-Mainz group (Mergell, Meissner, and Hammer). The two-loop results enable a more precise comparison with the empirical spectral functions, but the remaining 20% gap in the scalar channel and the need for a phenomenological \(\rho(770)\)-meson term to reproduce the isovector spectral functions point to the necessity of including higher-order \(\pi\pi\)-rescattering effects and a more complete treatment of the \(\rho\)-resonance.
