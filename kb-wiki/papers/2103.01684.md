# Reevaluating reactor antineutrino spectra with new measurements of the ratio between $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu $β$ spectra

> We report a reanalysis of the reactor antineutrino energy spectra based on the new relative measurements of the ratio $R=\!^{e}S_5/^{e}S_9$ between cumulative $β$ spectra from $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu, performed at a research reactor in National Research Centre Kurchatov Institute (KI). A discrepancy with the $β$ spectra measured at Institut Laue-Langevin (ILL) was observed, indicating a steady excess of the ILL ratio by the factor of $1.054\pm0.002$. We find a value of the ratio between inverse beta decay cross section per fission for $^{235}$U and $^{239}$Pu: $(^5σ_f/^9σ_f)_{KI} = 1.45 \pm 0.03$, and then we reevaluate the converted antineutrino spectra for $^{235}$U and $^{238}$U. We conclude that the new predictions are consistent with the results of Daya Bay and STEREO experiments.

## Key claim

The new relative measurements of the ratio between cumulative beta spectra from 235U and 239Pu at Kurchatov Institute show a steady excess of the Institut Laue-Langevin ratio by a factor of 1.054 ± 0.002, indicating that the ILL beta spectrum for 235U was overestimated in absolute normalization, and the corrected antineutrino spectra are consistent with Daya Bay and STEREO results.

## Method

The authors performed relative measurements of the ratio R = eS5/eS9 between cumulative beta spectra from 235U and 239Pu simultaneously at the IR-8 research reactor at Kurchatov Institute, using high-purity foils with known isotopic fractions and thermal neutron flux. They compared these measurements with the ILL beta spectra data republished by Haag et al. (2014), fitted the constant excess factor using weighted least squares, and then reevaluated the converted antineutrino spectra for 235U and 238U by applying the correction factor to the Huber-Mueller conversion method.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of ILL to KI beta spectra ratio: excess factor k = 1.054 ± 0.002
- Ratio of inverse beta decay cross sections per fission for 235U and 239Pu: (5σf/9σf)KI = 1.45 ± 0.03
- Combined Daya Bay and RENO result for comparison: (5σf/9σf) = 1.44 ± 0.10
- Reevaluated IBD yields: 235σf = (6.27 ± 0.13) × 10−43 cm2/fission; 239σf = (4.33 ± 0.11) × 10−43 cm2/fission; 238σf = (9.34 ± 0.47) × 10−43 cm2/fission; 241σf = (6.01 ± 0.13) × 10−43 cm2/fission
- Daya Bay average IBD yield: ⟨σf⟩ = (5.94 ± 0.09) × 10−43 cm2/fission, corresponding to 1.007 ± 0.031 of the prediction
- STEREO 235U IBD yield: 235σf = (6.34 ± 0.22) × 10−43 cm2/fission, corresponding to 1.012 ± 0.043 of the prediction
- Systematic error of the KI ratio measurement: 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the Huber-Mueller model (Huber 2011, Mueller 2011) for reactor antineutrino spectra by correcting the absolute normalization of the ILL beta spectra for 235U, which were originally measured by Schreckenbach et al. (1981, 1985) and von Feilitzsch et al. (1982). The results address the Reactor Antineutrino Anomaly (Mention 2011) by showing that the deficit observed in Daya Bay (2017, 2019) and STEREO (2020) experiments can be explained by an overestimation of the 235U beta spectrum normalization, rather than requiring sterile neutrino oscillations. The reevaluated spectra enable improved predictions for reactor antineutrino experiments and ongoing searches for sterile neutrinos.
