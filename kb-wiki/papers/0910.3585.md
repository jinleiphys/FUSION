# Fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from the Glauber models

> In the first part of the talk we discuss the role of the two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations on signatures of the heavy-ion collisions which are a priori expected to be sensitive to these effects. We find that while the fluctuations of the number of produced particles are indeed affected, other quantities (v_2 fluctuations, size fluctuations) are insensitive to the presence of the NN correlations in the nucleon distributions. In the second part we show that the fluctuations of the transverse size of the initial source cause, after a suitable hydrodynamic evolution, fluctuations of the transverse flow velocity at hadronic freeze-out. This in turn yields the event-by-event fluctuations of the transverse momentum of the produced particles, p_T. Our results demonstrate that practically all of the observed event-by-event p_T fluctuations may be explained this way.

## Key claim
The paper claims that event-by-event fluctuations of the average transverse momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be explained almost entirely by fluctuations in the initial transverse size of the system, which arise from the random distribution of wounded nucleons in the Glauber model. The authors hedge that this mechanism works for the wounded-nucleon model but overshoots the data by about 20% in the more realistic mixed model.

## Method
The authors used the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo code to generate initial nucleon distributions for lead-lead and gold-gold collisions, including or excluding two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations from Alvioli et al. (2009). They computed the scaled standard deviation of the transverse size parameter at fixed numbers of wounded nucleons, then used a hydrodynamic model with statistical hadronization (THERMINATOR) to convert size fluctuations into transverse momentum fluctuations via a linear expansion around the mean size. The derivative of average transverse momentum with respect to size was obtained by running two hydrodynamic simulations at each centrality with initial profiles squeezed or stretched by 5%.

## Key numbers
- The scaled standard deviation of the transverse size parameter is about 2-3% for central gold-gold collisions and grows toward peripheral collisions approximately as 1/sqrt(N_W).
- The derivative (langle r rangle / langle langle p_T rangle rangle) * (d bar p_T / d langle r rangle) is approximately -0.4, independent of centrality.
- This yields an average pressure-to-energy-density ratio P/epsilon of about 0.2.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are 32 mb (SPS), 42 mb (RHIC), and 63 mb (LHC).
- The experimental cuts applied are 0.2 GeV < p_T < 2 GeV (STAR detector cuts).

## Context
This work extends the Glauber model treatment of heavy-ion collisions by incorporating realistic two-body nucleon-nucleon correlations from Alvioli et al. (2009) and by providing a hydrodynamic mechanism for transverse momentum fluctuations. It contradicts earlier assumptions that nucleon-nucleon correlations significantly affect elliptic flow fluctuations, finding instead that only multiplicity fluctuations are sensitive to these correlations. The results enable future studies to use p_T fluctuations as a probe of the equation of state, specifically the average P/epsilon ratio, and provide a baseline against which other fluctuation sources (clusters, minijets, core-corona effects) must be compared. The approach builds on prior work by Ollitrault (1991) relating size fluctuations to p_T fluctuations via P/epsilon, and on hydrodynamic models by Florkowski et al. (2001) and Broniowski et al. (2001).
