# $B(E2;2^{+}_{1}\rightarrow0^{+}_{1})$ anomaly in $^{166}$Os

> Recently, the very small $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow0_{1}^{+})$ value was found in $^{166}$Os, which is 7(4) W.u. \textbf{and} much smaller than the values of adjacent nuclei $^{168,170}$Os, 74(13) W.u. and 97(9) W.u.. With the help of a new technique ``SU(3) analysis" and a new explanatory framework of the B(E2) anomaly, it was found that, the $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow0_{1}^{+})$ anomaly really can exist. In this paper, we discuss the $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow0_{1}^{+})$ anomaly in $^{166}$Os for the first time. Four results are used to fit the experimental data in $^{166,168,170}$Os successfully. This implies that the level-crossing or level-anticrossing explanation is more reasonable.

# Wiki Page: B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) Anomaly in ¹⁶⁶Os

## Key claim

The very small B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) value of 7(4) W.u. in ¹⁶⁶Os, which is nearly ten times smaller than the values in adjacent nuclei ¹⁶⁸Os and ¹⁷⁰Os, can be explained by level-crossing or level-anticrossing mechanisms within the SU3-IBM framework. The authors claim that four different parameter sets from three distinct mechanisms successfully fit the experimental data, implying that the level-crossing or level-anticrossing explanation is more reasonable than alternative interpretations.

## Method

The authors employed the SU(3) analysis technique within the interacting boson model extended with SU(3) third-order interactions (SU3-IBM), using a Hamiltonian containing five fitting parameters: the d-boson energy ε_d, the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength κ, the third-order interactions ζ[Q×Q×Q]⁰ and η[L×Q×L]⁰, and the rotational term fL². They performed SU(3) analysis for boson numbers N=7, 8, and 9 corresponding to ¹⁶⁶Os, ¹⁶⁸Os, and ¹⁷⁰Os respectively, and fitted four distinct parameter sets to reproduce the experimental B(E2) values and low-lying energy levels.

## Key numbers

- ¹⁶⁶Os B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) = 7(4) W.u. (experimental), compared to 74(13) W.u. in ¹⁶⁸Os and 97(9) W.u. in ¹⁷⁰Os
- ¹⁶⁶Os B(E2;8⁺₁→6⁺₁) = 1.4(5) W.u. (experimental), fitted values range from 1.0 to 2.8 W.u. across the four results
- In the SU(3) analysis with N=7, the ratio of B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) values for N=7:8:9 is 23.8:30.4:37.8 (or 61:78:97 with different normalization)
- The level-crossing point for the 4⁺ states in the SU(3) symmetry limit occurs at η = -15.20 keV; the 2⁺ state crossover occurs at η = -30.72 keV
- For ¹⁷⁰Os, the fitted B₄/₂ ratio (B(E2;4⁺₁→2⁺₁)/B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁)) = 0.39, matching the experimental value
- The average B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) value for 22 nuclei with N=7 (excluding ¹⁶⁶Os and ¹⁶²W) is 50.2 W.u.

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously developed by Wang (2020) to explain the B(E2) anomaly in ¹⁷⁰Os, and applies it for the first time to the more extreme case of ¹⁶⁶Os. The study builds on the level-crossing mechanism proposed by Wang (2020) and the general explanatory framework developed by Li (2025), which combines level-crossing and level-anticrossing mechanisms. The experimental B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) value for ¹⁶⁶Os was measured by Stolze (2021), and the authors compare their results with the B(E2) anomalies previously observed in ¹⁶⁸,¹⁷⁰Os by Grahn (2016) and Goasduff (2019), as well as in ¹⁶⁶W by Joss (2017). The successful fitting of four different parameter sets suggests that the level-crossing or level-anticrossing explanation is more convincing than alternative mechanisms, though the authors note that fourth-order interactions should be included in future work to improve the energy level fits, particularly for the 8⁺₁ state.
