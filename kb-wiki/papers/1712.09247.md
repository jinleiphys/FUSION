# On the power divergence in quasi gluon distribution function

> Recent perturbative calculation of quasi gluon distribution function at one-loop level shows the existence of extra linear ultraviolet divergences in the cut-off scheme. We employ the auxiliary field approach, and study the renormalization of gluon operators. The non-local gluon operator can mix with new operators under renormalization, and the linear divergences in quasi distribution function can be into the newly introduced operators. After including the mixing, we find the improved quasi gluon distribution functions contain only logarithmic divergences, and thus can be used to extract the gluon distribution in large momentum effective theory.

## Key claim

The linear ultraviolet divergences in the quasi gluon distribution function, which cannot be absorbed into Wilson line renormalization alone, can be systematically removed by including operator mixing with new operators and a mass counterterm for the Wilson line within the auxiliary field formalism.

## Method

The authors employed the auxiliary field (Z-field) method to convert the non-local gluon operator into local operators, enabling a standard renormalization analysis. They performed one-loop perturbative calculations of the quasi gluon distribution in the cut-off scheme, separating contributions from diagrams without Wilson lines, with one Wilson line, and with Wilson line self-energy. The renormalization was carried out by considering mixing between the gluon field strength operator and two additional operators (Ω^(2) and Ω^(3)) allowed by BRST symmetry, plus a mass counterterm δm_z for the Wilson line.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculation of quasi gluon distribution by Wang (2017) which first identified that linear divergences appear even in diagrams without Wilson lines, unlike the quasi quark case. The auxiliary field approach follows the formalism of Gervais (1979) and Dorn (1986), and the operator mixing analysis builds on the renormalization framework for non-local operators. The results enable the extraction of gluon distribution functions in large momentum effective theory (LaMET) by showing that the improved quasi gluon distribution contains only logarithmic divergences after including the mixing and mass counterterm, making it suitable for lattice QCD calculations.
