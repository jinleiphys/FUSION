# Fission Dynamics of Compound Nuclei

> Collisions between $^{248}$Cm and $^{48}$Ca are systematically investigated by time-dependent density functional calculations with evaporation prescription. Depending on the incident energy and impact parameter, fusion, deep-inelastic and quasi-fission events are expected to appear. In this paper, possible fission dynamics of compound nuclei is presented.

# Fission Dynamics of Compound Nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents a method to simulate fission dynamics of excited compound nuclei by combining time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) with an evaporation prescription, but the authors acknowledge that the pure TDDFT results give an unrealistically high fusion cross-section compared to experiments, and the additional survival probability against fission must be included to obtain comparable results.

## Method
Time-dependent density functional calculations with a Skyrme interaction (SLy6) were performed for collisions between ²⁴⁸Cm and ⁴⁸Ca in a spatial box of 48×48×24 fm³ with periodic boundary conditions, using unit spatial spacing of 1.0 fm and unit time spacing of 2/3×10⁻²⁴ s. For fission dynamics, an initial configuration of two fission fragments at a distance R₀ was prepared such that the excitation energy matched that of the compound nucleus found in the collision, with the initial many-body wave function given as a single Slater determinant and orthogonalized via Gram-Schmidt method before starting TDDFT calculations. An evaporation prescription for neutron and alpha-particle emissions was then applied to the TDDFT final products to account for thermal effects.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier for ²⁴⁸Cm + ⁴⁸Ca collisions: 209.0 MeV
- Incident energy used in systematic calculations: 268 MeV in the centre-of-mass frame
- Impact parameters studied: b = 4, 6, 8, and 10 fm
- For symmetric fission of ²⁹⁶Lv at b = 4 fm, the distance R₀ deduced from excitation energy: 11.8 fm
- Spatial box dimensions: 48×48×24 fm³
- Unit spatial spacing: 1.0 fm
- Unit time spacing: 2/3×10⁻²⁴ s
- Typical time-scale of low-energy heavy-ion reactions: 1000 fm/c (several 10⁻²¹ s)
- Typical time-scale of thermal effects (collision-fission): several 10⁻¹⁹ s
- N/Z ratios: ²⁴⁸Cm = 1.58, ⁴⁸Ca = 1.40, ²⁹⁶Lv = 1.55
- Initial ²⁴⁸Cm diameters: 19 fm (x), 19 fm (y), 18 fm (z)

## Context
This work extends time-dependent density functional theory approaches to fission dynamics of superheavy compound nuclei, addressing a long-standing problem in nuclear theory previously tackled by Negele and others. The method enables self-consistent treatment of fission dynamics for excited compound nuclei formed in heavy-ion collisions, with the key innovation being the determination of initial conditions from the excitation energy of the compound nucleus. The results suggest that symmetric fission of ²⁹⁶Lv into two ¹⁴⁸Ce fragments is possible in ²⁴⁸Cm + ⁴⁸Ca collisions at 268 MeV, but the total duration time for the fission process remains an open question for future investigation.
