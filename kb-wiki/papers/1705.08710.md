# From cold Fermi fluids to the hot QGP

> Strongly coupled quantum fluids are found in different forms, including ultracold Fermi gases or tiny droplets of extremely hot Quark-Gluon Plasma. Although the systems differ in temperature by many orders of magnitude, they exhibit a similar almost inviscid fluid dynamical behavior. In this work, we summarize some of the recent theoretical developments toward better understanding this property in cold Fermi gases at and near unitarity.

## Key claim

Cold Fermi gases at and near unitarity and the hot Quark-Gluon Plasma, despite differing in temperature by many orders of magnitude, exhibit a similar almost inviscid fluid dynamical behavior; the paper summarizes recent theoretical developments toward understanding this property, with the hedge that the work focuses on cold Fermi gases and does not present new experimental results for the QGP.

## Method

The authors developed an anisotropic fluid dynamics framework derived from moments of the Boltzmann equation, using an anisotropic distribution function motivated by exact solutions in the free streaming limit for a harmonic trap potential. They implemented a numerical code based on the PPMLR scheme to solve the evolution equations for the density, fluid velocity, energy, and anisotropic energy components in the unitarity limit. The framework was confronted with expansion data from Cao et al. (2011) for temperatures in the range \(0.79 \leq T/T_F \leq 1.54\) to extract the shear viscosity, and a fugacity expansion was used to study the scattering length dependence of the shear viscosity near unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity best fit in the high-temperature limit: \(\eta = 0.282 (mT)^{3/2}\), which agrees with the kinetic theory expectation \(\eta_{\rm CE} \simeq 0.269 (mT)^{3/2}\) from Bruun and Smith (2005, 2006) and Schaefer (2014).
- Analyzed expansion data from Cao et al. (2011) for temperatures in the range \(0.79 \leq T/T_F \leq 1.54\) with Fermi temperature \(T_F\).
- Superfluid transition temperature: \(T_c \simeq 0.23 T_F\).
- Fugacity expansion coefficients for shear viscosity near unitarity: \(c_0 = 1/(4\pi)\), \(c_1 \simeq -0.03325\), with \(\eta = \eta_\infty (1 + c_0 (\lambda/a)^2 + c_1 (z\lambda/a) + \dots)\).
- Bulk to shear viscosity ratio in the high-temperature limit: \(\zeta/\eta \sim (z\lambda/a)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of nearly inviscid flow in strongly coupled quantum fluids, building on the observation of similar behavior in cold Fermi gases (O'Hara et al., 2002) and the Quark-Gluon Plasma (Romatschke and Romatschke, 2007). It enables the reliable extraction of the temperature and density dependence of shear viscosity from expansion measurements, and provides a microscopic explanation for the anomalous shift of the minimal shear viscosity away from unitarity observed by Elliott et al. (2014). The anisotropic fluid dynamics framework developed here can be compared to the relativistic anisotropic fluid dynamics used for heavy-ion collisions by Martinez and Strickland (2010), Florkowski and Ryblewski (2011), and Strickland (2014).
