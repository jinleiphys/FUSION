# Completeness of the Coulomb scattering wave functions

> Completeness of the eigenfunctions of a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, which is the basic ingredient of quantum mechanics, plays an important role in nuclear reaction and nuclear structure theory. However, until now, there was no a formal proof of the completeness of the eigenfunctions of the two-body Hamiltonian with the Coulomb interaction. Here we present the first formal proof of the completeness of the two-body Coulomb scattering wave functions for repulsive unscreened Coulomb potential. To prove the completeness we use the Newton's method [R. Newton, J. Math Phys., 1, 319 (1960)]. The proof allows us to claim that the eigenfunctions of the two-body Hamiltonian with the potential given by the sum of the repulsive Coulomb plus short-range (nuclear) potentials also form a complete set. It also allows one to extend the Berggren's approach of modification of the complete set of the eigenfunctions by including the resonances for charged particles. We also demonstrate that the resonant Gamow functions with the Coulomb tail can be regularized using Zel'dovich's regularization method.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first formal proof that the eigenfunctions of the two-body Hamiltonian with repulsive unscreened Coulomb potential form a complete set, a result that was previously assumed but never rigorously demonstrated. The proof relies on Newton's method (Newton, 1960) and requires careful handling of the singular point at k=0, where the Coulomb Jost solutions have a branch cut that short-range potentials lack.

## Method

The authors apply Newton's contour integral method to the partial-wave Coulomb Green's function in coordinate space, splitting the closed integration contour in the complex momentum plane into three parts: the large semicircle at infinity, the real axis, and a small semicircle around k=0. They evaluate the asymptotic behavior of the Coulomb Jost solutions and regular solutions at large |k| to show the large-semicircle contribution gives π h(r), and use the analytic properties of the Whittaker functions to prove the small-semicircle contribution vanishes as its radius goes to zero. The real-axis integral is then reduced to an expression involving the physical Coulomb scattering wave functions, yielding the completeness relation.

## Context

This work extends Newton's completeness proof (Newton, 1960) from short-range potentials to the repulsive Coulomb potential, filling a long-standing gap in the literature. The result validates the application of Berggren's method (Berggren, 1968) for including resonant states in the basis for charged-particle systems, as used in the Gamow shell model (Michel, 2002). It also demonstrates that Zel'dovich's regularization method (Zel'dovich, 1960) can normalize resonant Gamow functions with Coulomb tails, enabling the extension of Berggren's approach to charged-particle nuclear reaction theory.
