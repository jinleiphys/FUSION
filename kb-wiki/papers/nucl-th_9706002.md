# Parton cascade description of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies ?

> We examine Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energy 158 A GeV, by employing the earlier developed and recently refined parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model and its Monte Carlo implementation. This space-time model involves the dynamical interplay of perturbative QCD parton production and evolution, with non-perturbative parton-cluster formation and hadron production through cluster decays. Using computer simulations, we are able to follow the entwined time-evolution of parton and hadron degrees of freedom in both position and momentum space, from the instant of nuclear overlap to the final yield of particles. We present and discuss results for the multiplicity distributions, which agree well with the measured data from the CERN SPS, including those for K mesons. The transverse momentum distributions of the produced hadrons are also found to be in good agreement with the preliminary data measured by the NA49 and the WA98 collaboration for the collision of lead nuclei at the CERN SPS. The analysis of the time evolution of transverse energy deposited in the collision zone and the energy density suggests an existence of partonic matter for a time of more than 5 fm.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a perturbative QCD parton-cascade model (PCM), with no fine-tuning to heavy-ion data, can describe the gross particle production properties of Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energy 158 A GeV, despite the widespread belief that such collisions are dominated by "soft" physics. The hedge is that this success comes as a surprise to the authors themselves, and that the model's parameters were fixed by e+e- and pp data, not by heavy-ion data.

## Method

The authors used the parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model (PCM) and its Monte Carlo implementation VNI to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. The simulation traces the space-time evolution of a mixed system of partons, pre-hadronic clusters, and hadrons from nuclear overlap to final particle yields, using perturbative QCD cross-sections for parton interactions and a phenomenological cluster-decay model for hadronization. The initial state is constructed from nucleon structure functions and elastic form-factors, and the time evolution is followed in small time steps (about 10^-3 fm) in 7-dimensional phase-space.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 158 A GeV (sqrt(s)/A = 17 GeV) for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS.
- The maximum energy density in the partonic phase reaches greater than about 5 GeV/fm^3 in the central region.
- The energy density drops below a "critical" density of about 2 GeV/fm^3 only after about 2 fm.
- Partonic matter exists for a period of almost 7 fm.
- The energy density contained by hadronic matter never exceeds 0.5 GeV/fm^3.
- The transverse energy dE_T/dy at y=0 is reduced by about 10% when multiple scatterings among partons are switched off, implying up to 20% of the energy deposited in partonic collisions originates from multiple scatterings.
- The dynamical regularization cut-off p_0 is set to 1.1 GeV, with alpha_s(p_0^2) approximately 0.45.
- Beam remnants account for about 50% of the transverse energy at central rapidities.

## Context

This work extends the parton-cascade/cluster-hadronization model (PCM), previously applied to RHIC and LHC energies, down to CERN SPS energies, challenging the prevailing view that SPS collisions are dominated by "soft" physics as modeled by string-based event generators like FRITIOF, VENUS, RQMD, and DPM. The authors argue that their pQCD-based approach provides a more microscopic understanding than these phenomenological models, and that the success at SPS gives confidence in PCM projections for RHIC and LHC. The work enables future studies of nuclear collisions with other beams and energies, and suggests that at RHIC the conditions for a baryon-free quark-gluon plasma in the central rapidity region may be very favorable.
