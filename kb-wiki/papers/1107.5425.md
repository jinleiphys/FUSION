# Models for quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Models developed for the exclusive and inclusive quasielastic (QE) electron-nucleus scattering have been extended to QE neutrino-nucleus scattering. Different descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) are compared. For the inclusive electron scattering the relativistic Green's function model (RGF) is compared with a model based on the use of relativistic purely real mean field (RMF) potentials in the final state. Both approaches lead to a redistribution of the strength but conserving the total flux. Results for electron and neutrino scattering are presented and discussed in different conditions and kinematics. The results of the RGF and RMF models are compared with the double-differential charged-current QE neutrino cross sections recently measured by the MiniBooNE collaboration using a carbon target.

# Models for quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which uses a complex energy-dependent optical potential to describe final-state interactions (FSI), can produce charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino-nucleus cross sections of the same magnitude as MiniBooNE data without increasing the nucleon axial mass above its standard value of 1.03 GeV/c^2, whereas the relativistic mean field (RMF) model generally underpredicts the data. However, the choice of optical potential parameterization significantly affects the RGF results, and contributions beyond the quasielastic peak (such as meson-exchange currents and Delta effects) have not been included.

## Method
The authors compare two relativistic approaches for inclusive quasielastic electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering: the RGF model, which uses a complex energy-dependent optical potential whose imaginary part accounts for inelastic channels, and the RMF model, which uses a purely real energy-independent mean-field potential for both bound and scattering states. For exclusive and semi-inclusive processes, the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) is employed with phenomenological optical potentials. The models are applied to calculate cross sections for 12C targets and compared with MiniBooNE CCQE double-differential data.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon axial mass standard value: M_A = 1.03 GeV/c^2 (used in all calculations)
- At q = 1000 MeV/c for 12C(e,e'), the RGF1 cross section at the peak overshoots the RMF cross section by up to 40%
- For 12C(nu_mu, mu-) at E_nu = 1000 MeV and q = 500 MeV/c, RGF and RMF yield similar predictions within a few percent
- For 12C(nu_mu, mu-) at E_nu = 1000 MeV and q = 1000 MeV/c, RGF yields increasingly larger cross sections than RMF
- MiniBooNE flux-averaged double-differential cross sections are shown for cos(theta) bins: 0.4-0.5, 0.7-0.8, 0.8-0.9, and T_mu bins: 0.2-0.3 GeV and 0.6-0.7 GeV
- The RFG model requires M_A = 1.35 GeV/c^2 to reproduce MiniBooNE data, compared to the world average of 1.03 GeV/c^2

## Context
This work extends models previously developed and tested for exclusive and inclusive electron-nucleus scattering (Giusti, Meucci, and collaborators over the preceding decade) to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The RGF model builds on the Green's function approach of Capuzzi et al. (1991, 2005) for inclusive electron scattering, while the RMF model follows the approach of Caballero et al. (2005) from the Madrid-Sevilla group. The comparison with MiniBooNE data (2010) addresses the ongoing debate about whether the nucleon axial mass must be increased to explain neutrino cross sections, or whether improved treatment of nuclear effects (FSI, correlations, meson-exchange currents) can resolve the discrepancy. The results enable further investigation of optical potential parameterizations and motivate consistent inclusion of additional contributions such as those from Amaro et al. (2011), Martini et al. (2011), and Nieves et al. (2011).
