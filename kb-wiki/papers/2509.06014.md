# Perfect spin hydrodynamics at all orders in spin polarization

> We compare two recently developed frameworks of perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-$1/2$ particles, based respectively on classical kinetic theory and the Wigner function. We show that the conserved currents in both approaches have the same form at each order of the expansion in the components of the spin polarization tensor $ω$. The only difference is a relative multiplicative factor, which is equal to 1 at the lowest nontrivial order and increases monotonically with the expansion order.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the conserved currents in classical and quantum perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles have the same functional form at every order of expansion in the spin polarization tensor components, differing only by a multiplicative factor that equals 1 at lowest nontrivial order and increases monotonically with expansion order; this correspondence holds despite the usual expectation that classical descriptions require large quantum numbers.

## Method

The author compared the generating functions for conserved currents in two frameworks: classical kinetic theory using Mathisson's spin description with Boltzmann-distributed particles in an extended phase space, and quantum theory based on a novel Wigner function. By expanding both generating functions in powers of the spin polarization tensor ωμν and evaluating the spin-space integrals in the classical case using a recursive relation for products of spin four-vectors, the author derived explicit expressions for the scalar densities at each order and computed the ratio between classical and quantum contributions.

## Key numbers

* The classical spin normalization constant spin = √(3/4) makes the baryon current Nμ and energy-momentum tensor Tμν agree with the quantum description exactly at second order in ω, and the spin tensor Sλ,μν agree at first order in ω.
* At the 2n-th order in ω, the contribution to Nμ and Tμν (or (2n-1)-th order for Sλ,μν) is 3n/(2n+1) times larger in the classical picture than in the quantum picture.
* The effective classical spin normalization that would make the two descriptions match at order 2n is spin2n = (2n+1)1/(2n)/2, which equals √(3/4) at n=1 and approaches 1/2 in the limit n → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between classical spin hydrodynamics (Drogosz 2024, Florkowski 2024) and the recently developed quantum Wigner function approach (Bhadury 2025, Kar 2025), which was previously shown to agree only at low spin polarizations. The result explains the similarity in application range formulas derived earlier (Drogosz 2025) and provides a precise quantification of when the classical description breaks down. The findings enable systematic higher-order calculations in spin hydrodynamics and support the use of either framework for sufficiently low values of the spin polarization tensor components.
