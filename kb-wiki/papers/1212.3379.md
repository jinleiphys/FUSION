# Pygmy and Giant Dipole Resonances by Coulomb Excitation using a Quantum Molecular Dynamics model

> Pygmy and Giant Dipole Resonance (PDR and GDR) in Ni isotopes have been investigated by Coulomb excitation in the framework of the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (IQMD). The spectra of $γ$ rays are calculated and the peak energy, the strength and Full Width at Half Maximum (FWHM) of GDR and PDR have been extracted. Their sensitivities to nuclear equation of state, especially to its symmetry energy term are also explored. By a comparison with the other mean-field calculations, we obtain the reasonable values for symmetry energy and its slope parameter at saturation, which gives an important constrain for IQMD model. In addition, we also studied the neutron excess dependence of GDR and PDR parameters for Ni isotopes and found that the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) $PDR_{m_1}/GDR_{m_1}%$ increases linearly with the neutron excess.

## Key claim

The Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model can reproduce the experimental PDR peak energy for \({}^{68}\)Ni, but the calculated FWHM is somewhat larger than the data; the extracted symmetry energy and its slope parameter at saturation are constrained by comparing the IQMD energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) fraction with mean-field calculations, yielding a range rather than a single value.

## Method

The authors used the IQMD model to simulate Coulomb excitation of Ni projectiles on a \({}^{197}\)Au target, extracting the time-dependent dipole moment for both GDR (all neutrons vs. all protons) and PDR (valence neutrons vs. isospin-symmetric core). The \(\gamma\)-ray emission probability was obtained via Fourier transformation of the second time derivative of the dipole moment, and the peak energy, strength, and FWHM were extracted by Gaussian fitting. The calculations were performed for different incident energies, equations of state (soft/hard with/without momentum-dependent interaction), symmetry energy coefficients \(C_{sym}\), and for a chain of even-even Ni isotopes from \({}^{62}\)Ni to \({}^{78}\)Ni.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{68}\)Ni + \({}^{197}\)Au at 600 MeV/nucleon, impact parameter 24 fm, soft EOS without MDI, and \(C_{sym} = 32\) MeV: the calculated PDR peak energy agrees with experimental data from Ref. [nidat], though the calculated FWHM is "a little larger."
* With increasing \(C_{sym}\) from 16 to 64 MeV, the GDR peak energy increases by about 25%, while the PDR peak energy decreases by only about 4%.
* By fitting the EWSR fraction \(PDR_{m_1}/(PDR_{m_1}+GDR_{m_1})\%\) versus the slope parameter \(L_s\) and assuming 15% uncertainty, the authors obtain a reasonable range for \(L_s\) of 60–81 MeV, corresponding to \(C_{sym} \approx 23.3\)–37.3 MeV and symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) \approx 24.2\)–31.2 MeV.
* For Ni isotopes from \({}^{62}\)Ni to \({}^{78}\)Ni at 100 MeV/nucleon, impact parameter 24 fm, soft EOS without MDI, and \(C_{sym} = 32\) MeV: the GDR peak is around 12 MeV and the PDR peak around 10 MeV; the EWSR fraction \(PDR_{m_1}/GDR_{m_1}\%\) increases linearly with mass number (neutron excess).

## Context

This work extends the IQMD model, previously applied to GDR calculations, to also describe PDR in Ni isotopes, complementing mean-field and RPA approaches such as those by Ma et al. (2012) and Baran et al. The results confirm the relative insensitivity of PDR to the symmetry energy, consistent with earlier findings from relativistic RPA (Ma et al., 2012) and the semiclassical Landau-Vlasov approach (Baran et al.). The extracted symmetry energy parameters provide a constraint for the IQMD model and can inform future studies of neutron-rich nuclei and their role in nucleosynthesis and neutron-capture rates.
