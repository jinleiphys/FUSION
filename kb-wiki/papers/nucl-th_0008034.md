# The Role of Meson Retardation in Deuteron Photodisintegration above Pion Threshold

> Photodisintegration of the deuteron above $π$ threshold is studied in a coupled channel approach including $N Δ$ and $πd$ channels with consideration of pion retardation in potentials and exchange currents. A much improved description of total and differential cross sections in the energy region between $π$ threshold and 400-450 MeV is achieved. With respect to polarization observables, the description of the linear photon asymmetry and the proton polarization remains problematic.

## Key claim

Including full meson retardation in the coupled-channel treatment of deuteron photodisintegration above pion threshold yields a much improved description of total and differential cross sections up to 400-450 MeV, but the description of the linear photon asymmetry and the proton polarization remains problematic even with this refinement.

## Method

The authors constructed a hadronic interaction model in the two-nucleon system that includes nucleon, Delta, and pion degrees of freedom, with full meson retardation in potentials and exchange currents, using the Elster potential as the basic NN interaction. They fixed the gamma-N-Delta coupling by simultaneously fitting the M1+^(3/2) multipole in pion photoproduction on the nucleon and the P33 partial wave of pion-nucleon scattering, then applied this framework to compute photodisintegration observables. The effective current operator was split into nucleonic and resonant one-body and two-body (meson exchange current) contributions, with retarded pion and gamma-pi-rho/omega currents treated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized gamma-N-Delta coupling constant G_M1^(Delta N) was fitted to -3.91, with the Born amplitude parameter b = 0.84.
- The gamma-pi-rho and gamma-pi-omega electromagnetic coupling constants used were g_(gamma pi rho) = 0.41 and g_(gamma pi omega) = 2.02.
- The Delta resonance mass used was M_Delta^res = 1232 MeV.
- The normalization constant N_d for the deuteron in the retarded approach was 0.992 for the Elster potential.
- The effective gamma-N-Delta coupling modulus shows weak energy dependence, while the static coupling of Wilhelm et al. is considerably smaller at low energies, contributing to underestimation of the total cross section.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier coupled-channel model (Schwamb and Arenhoevel, 1999) that incorporated meson retardation in NN scattering, applying it now to deuteron photodisintegration. It addresses the failure of static interaction models to describe experimental data in the Delta resonance region, and directly compares its effective gamma-N-Delta coupling to the parameterization of Wilhelm et al. (1992, 1993), which used an onshell prescription and a different fit to the data of Berends and Donnachie (1975). The results enable future studies of electromagnetic reactions in the two-nucleon system with consistent treatment of retardation, though polarization observables remain a challenge.
