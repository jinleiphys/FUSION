# Hadron spectra of finite-density QC$_2$D

> We investigate the chemical-potential dependence of hadron spectra in two-color QCD using first-principles lattice simulations. We compute two-point correlation functions for all allowed hadronic operators by newly including the contributions from disconnected diagrams, and extract the corresponding effective masses. In the meson sector, the mass hierarchy in the hadronic phase (normal vacuum) is found to be $m_π\lesssim m_η < m_σ\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_ρ\sim m_ω\ll m_{a_1}$, which is similar to that in three-color QCD. In the superfluid phase, this hierarchy is modified, and with increasing density it changes to $m_σ\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_{a_1} < m_ρ< m_π\sim m_η \mathrm{(noisy)} \ll m_ω \mathrm{(noisy)}$. In the diquark sector, the ordering remains as $m_{NG} \lesssim m_{I=0, S} < m_{I=1, AV} < m_{I=0, PS} \lesssim m_{I=0, V}$ in both phases, and the Nambu--Goldstone mode associated with spontaneous breaking of $U(1)_B$ is confirmed to be nearly massless. Furthermore, by comparing correlators for chiral partners, we find indications of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice calculation of hadron spectra in two-color QCD that includes contributions from disconnected diagrams, finding that the mass hierarchy in the meson sector changes from the hadronic phase to the superfluid phase, with the ordering $m_\pi\lesssim m_\eta < m_\sigma\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_\rho\sim m_\omega\ll m_{a_1}$ in the vacuum shifting to $m_\sigma\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_{a_1} < m_\rho< m_\pi\sim m_\eta \mathrm{(noisy)} \ll m_\omega \mathrm{(noisy)}$ at high density, though the authors note that the $\sigma$, $\eta$, and $\omega$ masses are noisy and the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing with relatively heavy quark masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermions using the Iwasaki gauge action, computing two-point correlation functions for all allowed meson and diquark operators including disconnected diagram contributions. They extracted effective masses from the exponential decay of these correlators at zero temperature across a range of chemical potentials spanning the hadronic phase, the BEC-type superfluid phase, and the BCS-type superfluid phase. The diquark source term was introduced to explicitly break $U(1)_B$ symmetry and control the direction of spontaneous symmetry breaking, with the extended fermion matrix method used to incorporate this term into the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass in the hadronic phase is approximately $m_\pi \approx 0.6$ in lattice units (from Fig. 3 in the paper), with the hierarchy $m_\pi\lesssim m_\eta < m_\sigma\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_\rho\sim m_\omega \ll m_{a_1}$.
- In the superfluid phase at the highest chemical potential studied, the hierarchy becomes $m_\sigma\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_{a_1} < m_\rho< m_\pi\sim m_\eta \mathrm{(noisy)} \ll m_\omega \mathrm{(noisy)}$, with the pion mass increasing by roughly a factor of 2 relative to its vacuum value.
- The Nambu-Goldstone mode associated with $U(1)_B$ breaking is confirmed to be nearly massless, with its mass consistent with zero within statistical errors across all chemical potentials in the superfluid phase.
- The diquark sector ordering remains $m_{NG} \lesssim m_{I=0, S} < m_{I=1, AV} < m_{I=0, PS} \lesssim m_{I=0, V}$ in both phases, with the iso-singlet scalar diquark being the lightest massive state after the NG mode.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of hadron spectroscopy in dense two-color QCD by Muroya et al. (2002) and Hands et al. (2007), which did not fully incorporate disconnected diagram contributions, and complements effective field theory approaches such as chiral perturbation theory by Kogut et al. (2000) and the sigma model by Suenaga et al. (2022, 2025). The results provide first-principles input for understanding how hadron masses change in dense matter, relevant to interpreting neutron-star observations and experimental searches for in-medium modifications of vector mesons at KEK-PS E325 and J-PARC E16, as well as the $\eta'$-mesic nuclei experiment at GSI. The observed degeneracy of chiral partner correlators at high density indicates chiral symmetry restoration, though the authors note that the relatively heavy quark mass and finite lattice spacing limit quantitative conclusions.
