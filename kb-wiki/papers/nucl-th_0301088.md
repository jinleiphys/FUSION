# On the Boson Number Operator in the Deformed Boson Scheme

> Concerning the energy of harmonic oscillator, a prescription is proposed for making the original form unchanged even after q-deformation. Applicability of the prescription is limited, but, it can be applied to various cases which are well known.

# On the Boson Number Operator in the Deformed Boson Scheme

## Key claim

The authors propose a prescription for constructing a deformed boson number operator that preserves the original harmonic oscillator form of the Hamiltonian after q-deformation, but the prescription is limited to cases where the inverse relation between the deformed commutator and the original number operator can be solved.

## Method

The authors start from a boson system with operators (c, c*) and define deformed operators (γ, γ*) = (F(N)c, c*F(N)) where F(N) is a function of the boson number operator N = c*c. They introduce an operator M = c*c + G(b) - G(0), where b = (1/2)([c, c*] - 1) is a null operator, and then deform it to M_q = γ*γ + G(β) - G(0), where β = (1/2)([γ, γ*] - 1). By imposing the condition that γ*γ + G(β) = N, they determine G(β) such that M_q reduces to c*c minus a constant, preserving the harmonic oscillator form.

## Key numbers

- For the su(2) and su(1,1) Holstein-Primakoff representations with F(N) = √(1 ∓ N/n₀): β = ∓ N/n₀, and M_q = c*c (no constant shift).
- For the second Holstein-Primakoff representation of su(1,1) with F(N) = √(N/n₀ - 1): β = N/n₀ - 1, and M_q = c*c - (n₀ + 1), where N runs in the region (n₀+1), (n₀+2), (n₀+3), ...
- For the q-deformed case with φ(n) = √([1 - q^{-2(n+1)}]/[1 - q^{-2}]): β = -(1/2)(1 - q^{-2N}), and M_q = c*c.
- For the case φ(n) = 1: β = -(1/2)Q₀ (projection operator rejecting the vacuum), and M_q = c*c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deformed boson scheme (Kuriyama et al., 2002) which was related to generalized boson coherent states and built upon the ideas of Penson and Solomon (1999). The prescription addresses a limitation in earlier treatments where special devices were needed for su(2)- and su(1,1)-algebraic models to maintain the harmonic oscillator form of the Hamiltonian. The results enable systematic deformation of interaction terms H^(1) in many-body boson systems while preserving the free harmonic oscillator part of the Hamiltonian.
