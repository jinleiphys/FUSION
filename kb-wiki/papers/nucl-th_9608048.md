# On the $m_\perp$-dependence of Bose-Einstein correlation radii

> Within a theoretical study using both HYLANDER and RQMD, we revisit Bose-Einstein correlation measurements of 200AGeV S+S and 160AGeV Pb+Pb. Transverse flow does not show up in the Bose-Einstein measurements. The decrease of the effective transverse radius parameters with increasing transverse average momentum of the particle pair is largely due to the effect of resonance decays.

## Key claim

The decrease of effective transverse Bose-Einstein correlation radii with increasing transverse average momentum of the particle pair is largely due to resonance decays, not transverse flow; the authors argue that no simple $m_\perp$-scaling behavior is observed in either the hydrodynamical model (HYLANDER) or the cascade model (RQMD) when calculations are performed at midrapidity.

## Method

The authors used two realistic source models: the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic one-fluid-dynamics model HYLANDER and the transport model RQMD (v2.2). For 200AGeV S+S and 160AGeV Pb+Pb central collisions, they calculated Bose-Einstein correlation functions for negative pions and kaons, including resonance decays, and fitted the results to a Gaussian form to extract effective radii $R_\parallel$, $R_{side}$, and $R_{out}$ as functions of the pair transverse momentum $K_\perp$ and transverse mass $m_\perp$. Detector acceptances were accounted for to enable comparison with NA35 and NA49 data.

## Key numbers

- For 200AGeV S+S, the maximum transverse velocity from HYLANDER is $u_\perp^{max}(S)=0.43$; for 160AGeV Pb+Pb, it is $u_\perp^{max}(Pb)=0.61$.
- In the HYLANDER calculations for S+S, effective radii were evaluated at pair rapidity $y_K=4.0-y_{cm} \approx 1.0$; for Pb+Pb, at $y_K=4.5-y_{cm} \approx 1.6$.
- For the RQMD calculations of 160AGeV Pb+Pb at midrapidity $y_K=0.0$, a freeze-out temperature of $T_f = 140$ MeV was used.
- The averaged transverse flow velocity at midrapidity in RQMD for Pb+Pb central collisions is about 40% of the speed of light.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sinyukov (1987) and by Csorgo and collaborators (mid-1990s) who proposed $m_\perp$-scaling of Bose-Einstein correlation radii, and contradicts the claim that transverse radii follow the same scaling law. The authors build on their own prior hydrodynamical calculations (Schlei et al., several papers from 1992-1995) that included resonance decays, and compare with experimental data from the NA35 and NA49 Collaborations. The results enable a more careful interpretation of Bose-Einstein correlation measurements, showing that resonance decays, not transverse flow, dominate the $K_\perp$-dependence of transverse radii, and that experimental acceptance effects (as seen in NA44 data) can produce apparent $m_\perp$-scaling.
