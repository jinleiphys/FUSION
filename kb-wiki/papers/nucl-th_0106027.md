# Recent developments in the dynamical and unitary isobar models for pion electromagnetic production

> gamma N->Delta transition form factors and threshold pi^0 photo- and electroproduction are studied with the new version of MAID and a dynamical model. By re-analyzing the recent Jlab data on p(e,e'p) pi^0 at Q^2 = 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)^2, we find that the hadronic helicity conservation is not yet observed in this region of Q^2. The extracted R_{EM}, starting from a small and negative value at the real photon point, actually exhibits a clear tendency to cross zero and change sign as Q^2 increases, while the absolute value of R_{SM} is strongly increasing. Our analysis indicates that A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}, but not A_{3/2}, starts exhibiting the pQCD scaling behavior at about Q^2 = 2.5 (GeV/c)^2. For the pi^0 photo- and electroproduction near threshold, results obtained within the dynamical model with the use of a meson-exchange pi N model for the final state interaction are in as good agreement with the data as ChPT.

## Key claim

By re-analyzing recent Jlab data on p(e,e'p)π⁰ at Q² = 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)², the authors find that hadronic helicity conservation is not yet observed in this region of Q², and that the extracted REM exhibits a clear tendency to cross zero and change sign as Q² increases, contrary to previous analyses which concluded REM would stay negative and become more negative.

## Method

The authors employ two theoretical frameworks: the new version of the unitary isobar model (MAID) and the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model, both of which describe pion photo- and electroproduction via a t-matrix formalism that separates background and resonant contributions. For the Δ(1232) resonance region, they parametrize the electric, magnetic, and Coulomb amplitudes with a form factor F(Q²) = (1+βQ²)e^{-γQ²}G_D(Q²) and extract the Q² dependence of the scaling-violation parameters X_E^Δ and X_S^Δ by fitting to Jlab data at Q² = 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)². For threshold π⁰ production, they include final state interactions via a meson-exchange πN model solved with coupled-channels equations and compare with a K-matrix approximation for the cusp effect.

## Key numbers

- REM at Q² = 0 is between -2.5% and -3.0% from experiments (Beck97)
- X_E^Δ(MAID) = 1 - Q²/3.7, X_E^Δ(DMT) = 1 + Q⁴/2.4, extracted from Jlab data at Q² = 2.8 and 4.0 (GeV/c)²
- X_S^Δ(MAID) = 1 + Q⁶/61, X_S^Δ(DMT) = 1 - Q²/0.1, extracted from the same Jlab data
- The pQCD scaling behavior for A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} starts at about Q² ≥ 2.5 (GeV/c)²
- The pion charge exchange amplitude a_{πN} = 0.124/m_{π⁺}
- The cusp effect approximation yields differences around 10% between coupled-channels and K-matrix approaches near π⁰p threshold
- At Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)², the DMT model predictions for E_{0+} and L_{0+} multipoles are compared with data from NIKHEF and Mainz (Distler)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model developed in Yang (1999) (the DMT model) and the unitary isobar model MAID from Drechsel and Tiator (1998) to analyze the γ*NΔ transition form factors and threshold π⁰ production. The results contradict the original analysis of Frolov (1999) which concluded REM would stay negative with increasing Q², and instead suggest that scaling behavior may set in earlier than hadronic helicity conservation. The threshold π⁰ results provide a comparison between the dynamical model approach and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as developed by Bernard and others, showing that the dynamical model with meson-exchange πN final state interactions achieves agreement with data comparable to ChPT. This enables future studies of the transition from non-perturbative to perturbative QCD regimes in nucleon structure.
