# The Neutrino Response of Low-Density Neutron Matter from the Virial Expansion

> We generalize our virial approach to study spin-polarized neutron matter and the consistent neutrino response at low densities. In the long-wavelength limit, the virial expansion makes model-independent predictions for the density and spin response, based only on nucleon-nucleon scattering data. Our results for the neutrino response provide constraints for random-phase approximation or other model calculations, and we compare the virial vector and axial response to response functions used in supernova simulations. The virial expansion is suitable to describe matter near the supernova neutrinosphere, and this work extends the virial equation of state to predict neutrino interactions in neutron matter.

# The Neutrino Response of Low-Density Neutron Matter from the Virial Expansion

## Key claim

The virial expansion, truncated at second order in the fugacity, provides model-independent predictions for the long-wavelength vector and axial neutrino response of low-density neutron matter, based solely on experimental nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts; however, these predictions are strictly valid only for densities where the fugacity z < 1/2, corresponding to densities n less than approximately 4 x 10^11 (T/MeV)^(3/2) g/cm^3, and the axial response approximation neglects possible contributions from multi-pair states in the time-like region.

## Method

The authors generalized the virial equation of state to spin-polarized neutron matter by expanding the grand-canonical partition function in powers of the fugacity for spin-up and spin-down components separately, with second virial coefficients b_n,1 (like spins) and b_n,0 (opposite spins) computed from sums of elastic scattering phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis including all partial waves with L ≤ 6. The long-wavelength vector response S_v(q=0) was derived from the density derivative of the pressure, and the axial response S_a(q=0) from the spin susceptibility, both expressed in terms of the virial coefficients and fugacity. The virial results were compared to Brueckner calculations of Bombaci et al. (2005) and to random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations following Burrows and Sawyer (1998).

## Key numbers

- The virial expansion is valid for densities n ≤ 4 x 10^11 (T/MeV)^(3/2) g/cm^3, corresponding to fugacity z < 1/2.
- At T = 4 MeV, the second virial coefficient for spin-symmetric neutron matter is b_n = 0.306 (without charge-independence breaking) and the axial virial coefficient is b_a = -0.652.
- At T = 5 MeV, the virial coefficients obtained only from the scattering length a_np = -23.768 fm are b_n = 0.44 and b_a = -0.80; in the unitary limit (|a_s| = infinity) they are b_n = 0.53 and b_a = -0.88.
- Charge-independence breaking (CIB) effects using a_nn = -18.5 fm reduce the virial coefficients by up to 10% for T < 5 MeV.
- At T = 4 MeV and density n = 0.0016 fm^(-3) = n_0/100, the total neutrino response (ratio of in-medium to free cross section) is 0.72, which is 10% larger than the RPA response of Burrows and Sawyer (1998).
- The RPA interaction parameters used for comparison are f_0 = 1.76 x 10^(-5) MeV^(-2) for the density response and g_0 = 4.50 x 10^(-5) MeV^(-2) for the spin response.

## Context

This work extends the virial equation of state for low-density nucleonic matter previously developed by the authors (Horowitz and Schwenk, 2005; 2006) to predict neutrino interactions in neutron matter, providing a model-independent benchmark for supernova simulations. The virial results disagree with the RPA response of Burrows and Sawyer (1998) because the latter uses Landau parameters from symmetric nuclear matter (F_0 = -0.28, F_0' = 0.95) that yield an incorrect repulsive vector response for pure neutron matter, whereas the virial expansion predicts an attractive vector response (S_v > 1) consistent with the attractive neutron-neutron interaction. The virial pressure and entropy agree well with Brueckner calculations of Bombaci et al. (2005), and the long-wavelength response provides strong constraints for all relevant momentum transfers since the RPA momentum dependence is found to be very weak.
