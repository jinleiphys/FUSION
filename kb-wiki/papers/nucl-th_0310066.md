# Superfluid LDA (SLDA): Local Density Approximation for Systems with Superfluid Correlations

> We present a concise account of our development of the first genuine Local Density Approximation (LDA) to the Energy Density Functional (EDF) for fermionic systems with superfluid correlations, with a particular emphasis to nuclear systems.

# Superfluid LDA (SLDA): Local Density Approximation for Systems with Superfluid Correlations

## Key claim

The authors present the first genuinely local extension of the Kohn-Sham Local Density Approximation (LDA) to fermionic systems with superfluid correlations, applicable to both nuclear and atomic systems. The approach is claimed to be theoretically consistent and physically meaningful despite the inherent short-range/ultraviolet divergence characteristic of any local pairing field, though the authors acknowledge that gradient corrections remain the largest source of uncertainty and that the density dependence of the pairing coupling constant could not be identified in their nuclear calculations.

## Method

The authors construct a Superfluid LDA (SLDA) energy density functional (EDF) by separating it into a normal part (taken from existing Skyrme-type interactions such as SLy4 or Fayans' FaNDF^0) and a superfluid part quadratic in the anomalous density. They solve self-consistent Bogoliubov-de Gennes-type equations with a position-dependent renormalized coupling constant that removes dependence on the energy cutoff (chosen approximately 1.5 times the Fermi energy or larger), using a single density-independent bare coupling constant for both neutrons and protons in T=1 pairing channels.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff energy E_c should be chosen approximately 1.5 times the value of the Fermi energy or larger to eliminate dependence on the cutoff value.
- Maximum pairing gaps from infinite matter HFB/BCS calculations are of order 3 MeV for k_F ≈ 1 fm^{-1}, with essentially vanishing pairing gaps at nuclear saturation densities.
- Correlation effects reduce the maximum pairing gap to about 1 MeV, a value considered reasonable by many researchers.
- In very dilute systems, the BCS pairing gap is incorrect and the actual value is smaller by a factor of about 2.2 (Gorkov, Heiselberg).
- The average value of (N-Z)/A over all measured nuclear masses with A ≥ 8 used in Goriely et al. is 0.1473.
- The ratio f(ρ_n,ρ_p)/g(ρ_n,ρ_p) ≈ -0.39 for a charge-symmetric superfluid EDF that reproduces the average stronger proton pairing.
- The SLDA has been applied to more than 200 nuclei with surprisingly high accuracy for single-nucleon and two-nucleon separation energies.
- Pairing gaps in systems studied span almost twenty orders of magnitude across different applications.

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham LDA framework (originally developed for normal Fermi systems) to superfluid systems, addressing a gap left by earlier attempts such as Gross et al. where the pairing field remained nonlocal. The authors contrast their approach with the standard HFB/BCS approximation, which they argue fails in the low-density regime where SLDA works, and with the naive local density approximation for pairing gaps criticized by Grasso and Urban (2003). The formalism enables fully self-consistent treatment of finite nuclei with exact continuum treatment, and has been applied to vortex states in neutron matter and dilute atomic Fermi gases, as well as two-dimensional quantum dots. The authors note that Goriely et al. (Samyn et al.) independently arrived at the same conclusion of a density-independent pairing coupling constant in a fully self-consistent analysis of all known nuclear masses.
