# Strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs and the total lepton number violation

> The influence of a neutrinoless electron to positron conversion on a cooling of strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs is studied.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless electron-to-positron conversion on iron nuclei in strongly magnetized iron white dwarfs could produce a detectable change in the white dwarf's luminosity, but only at very low luminosities (Log(L/L_sun) around -7.5) and only if the cooling theory for such magnetized dwarfs is sufficiently well understood to distinguish this effect from standard cooling.

## Method

The authors model a strongly magnetized iron white dwarf with the magnetic field strength set so that only the ground Landau level is occupied, using a polytropic equation of state with index n=1. They calculate the (e-,e+) conversion rate on 56Fe using the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with a model space of 0-4 hbar-omega oscillator shells and a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential, deriving two-body G-matrix elements from the Charge Dependent Bonn potential. The reaction rate is proportional to the square of the effective Majorana neutrino mass, and the energy released per event is estimated from the two-photon positron-electron annihilation probability.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for the reaction e- + 56Fe -> 56Cr + e+ is Delta = 6.33 MeV.
- The white dwarf mass considered is M_WD = 2 M_sun.
- For Fermi energy epsilon_F = 200, the ratio of the change in luminosity to solar luminosity is Log(Delta L/L_sun) = -7.9 for |<m_nu>| = 0.4 eV and -7.3 for |<m_nu>| = 0.8 eV.
- The energy released per single reaction ranges from 9.1 MeV (at epsilon_F = 20) to 104.4 MeV (at epsilon_F = 200).
- The electron number density at epsilon_F = 200 is 3.519 x 10^36 per cm^3, and the matter density is 1.25 x 10^13 g/cm^3.
- The magnetic field parameter gamma = B/B_c ranges from 200 (at epsilon_F = 20) to 20000 (at epsilon_F = 200), where B_c = 4.414 x 10^13 G.

## Context

This work extends the idea of using white dwarf cooling as a laboratory for particle physics, previously applied to axion searches by Isern et al., to the case of lepton number violation via a process analogous to neutrinoless double beta decay. The authors note that detailed cooling models for strongly magnetized white dwarfs have not yet been elaborated, and their simple Mestel-type model gives unreliable results compared to the more detailed iron-core white dwarf cooling curves of Althaus et al. The paper enables future work to incorporate this lepton-number-violating energy source into proper magnetized white dwarf cooling codes once those are developed.
