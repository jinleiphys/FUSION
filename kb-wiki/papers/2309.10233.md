# Quark phases in neutron stars consistent with implications of NICER

> The analyses for the NICER data imply $R_{2.0M_\odot}=12.41^{+1.00}_{-1.10}$ km and $R_{1.4M_\odot}=12.56^{+1.00}_{-1.07}$ km, indicating the lack of significant variation of the radii from $1.4 M_\odot$ to $2.0 M_\odot$. This feature cannot be reproduced by the hadronic matter due to the softening of equation of state (EoS) by hyperon mixing, indicating the possible existence of quark phases in neutron-star interiors. % Two models are used for quark phases: In the quark-hadron transition (QHT) model, quark deconfinement phase transitions from a hadronic-matter EoS are taken into account so as to give reasonable mass-radius ($MR$) curves by adjusting the quark-quark repulsions and the density dependence of effective quark mass. % In the quarkyonic model, the degrees of freedom inside the Fermi sea are treated as quarks and neutrons exist at the surface of the Fermi sea, where $MR$ curves are controlled mainly by the thickness of neutron Fermi layer. % The QHT and quarkyonic EoSs can be adjusted so as to reproduce radii, tidal deformabilities, pressure and central densities inferred from the NICER analysis better than the nucleonic matter EoS, demonstrating the clear impacts of quark phases. Then, the maximum mass for the quakyonic-matter EoS is considerably larger than that for the QHT-matter EoS.

## Key claim

The NICER data imply that neutron-star radii are nearly constant (≈12.4–12.6 km) from 1.4 M⊙ to 2.0 M⊙, a feature that cannot be reproduced by hadronic matter with hyperon mixing; the authors show that this feature can be reproduced by two different models of quark phases (quark-hadron transition and quarkyonic matter) provided the quark-quark repulsion is made sufficiently strong and the transition density is lowered to about 2 ρ₀.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid neutron-star equations of state (EoS) using two approaches: (1) a quark-hadron transition (QHT) model in which a hadronic-matter EoS (ESC baryon-baryon interactions with MPb three-baryon repulsions) is joined to a quark-matter EoS derived from two-body quark-quark potentials (including meson-exchange, instanton-exchange, one-gluon-exchange, and multi-pomeron-exchange) via a replacement interpolation method; (2) a quarkyonic model in which quarks fill the Fermi sea and neutrons exist only at the Fermi surface, with the thickness of the neutron layer controlled by parameters Λ and κ. Both models are combined with a β-stable nucleonic-matter EoS and a crust EoS, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius (MR) curves.

## Key numbers

- NICER-inferred radii used as criteria: R₂.₀M⊙ = 12.41⁺¹.⁰⁰₋₁.₁₀ km and R₁.₄M⊙ = 12.56⁺¹.⁰⁰₋₁.₀₇ km, with maximum mass M_max/M⊙ = 2.21⁺⁰.³¹₋₀.₂₁ (Legred 2021)
- For the QHT model with Q4 (α_S = 0.69, ρ_c = 7.5ρ₀, B₀ = 10.0), the quark-hadron transition density is ≈2ρ₀ and the maximum mass is 2.25 M⊙ with R₁.₄M⊙ = 12.5 km
- For the quarkyonic model with Λ = 380 MeV and κ = 0.3 (using B1 nuclear interactions), transition densities are 0.28–0.38 fm⁻³ (1.6ρ₀–2.2ρ₀)
- The QHT model with Q2 (α_S = 0.25) gives R₂.₀M⊙ = 12.0 km, smaller than the NICER criterion of 12.41 km

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hybrid stars with quark phases (Baldo 2006, Ozel, Weissenborn, Klahn, Bonanno, Lastowiecki 2012, Shahrbaf 2020, KBH 2022, YYR 2022) by applying the stricter NICER radius constraints (Miller 2021, Riley 2021, Legred 2021) that require R₂M⊙ ≈ R₁.₄M⊙. It contrasts with the quark-hadron crossover (QHC) framework of KBH (2022), which uses phenomenological interpolation, by employing explicit quark-quark potentials in a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework. The results enable comparison of two distinct quark-phase mechanisms (first-order-like QHT versus second-order quarkyonic transition) and show that the quarkyonic model produces a peak in sound speed similar to the QHC model, while the QHT model does not.
