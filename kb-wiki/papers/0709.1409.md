# The effect of "pre-formed" hadron potentials on the dynamics of heavy ion collisions and the HBT puzzle

> The nuclear stopping, the elliptic flow, and the HBT interferometry are calculated by the UrQMD transport model, in which potentials for "pre-formed" particles (string fragments) from color fluxtube fragmentation as well as for confined particles are considered. This description provides stronger pressure at the early stage and describes these observables better than the default cascade mode (where the "pre-formed" particles from string fragmentation are treated to be free-streaming). It should be stressed that the inclusion of potential interactions pushes down the calculated HBT radius $R_O$ and pulls up the $R_S$ so that the HBT time-related puzzle disappears throughout the energies from AGS, SPS, to RHIC.

## Key claim

Including density-dependent (Skyrme-like) potentials for "pre-formed" particles (string fragments) from color fluxtube fragmentation, in addition to potentials for confined baryons, reduces the calculated HBT radius \(R_O\) and increases \(R_S\) so that the \(R_O/R_S\) ratio matches experimental data across AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, thereby resolving the HBT time-related puzzle; however, the authors note that this approach is "only semi-realistic" and that the compressibility provided by the currently employed potential may be too strong at RHIC.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics) hadron-string transport model, adding a density-dependent (Skyrme-like) potential for "pre-formed" particles from string fragmentation (with a reduction factor of 2/3 for mesons due to quark-number difference) and a soft equation of state with momentum dependence (SM-EoS) for confined baryons. They calculated the rapidity distributions of net protons and pions, the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of charged hadrons, and the HBT radii \(R_L\), \(R_O\), and \(R_S\) (via the CRAB v3.0\(\beta\) correlation after-burner) for central heavy-ion collisions at AGS (2–8A GeV), SPS (20–158A GeV), and RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130\) and 200 GeV) energies, comparing results with and without these potentials.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_b=158\)A GeV for central Pb+Pb collisions, the total pion multiplicity changes by less than 5% when potentials for "pre-formed" particles are added.
- At RHIC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the elliptic flow \(v_2\) from the cascade mode is only about 55% of the experimental four-particle cumulant data; potentials for confined baryons contribute about 4% additional flow, while potentials for "pre-formed" hadrons increase the flow further in more central collisions.
- The \(R_O/R_S\) ratio at small \(k_T\) (150\(\pm\)50 MeV/\(c\) at AGS and SPS; 200\(\pm\)50 MeV/\(c\) at RHIC) is reduced from cascade-mode values to approximately unity at RHIC when potentials for both "pre-formed" and confined particles are included, compared to data.
- At \(E_b=2\)A GeV, the two-body mesonic Coulomb potential affects HBT radii only very weakly at \(k_T<100\) MeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Li et al., 2007) that found the HBT time-related puzzle present at almost all energies from AGS to RHIC in cascade-mode UrQMD calculations. It contrasts with the approach of Cramer et al. (2004), who used a single-pion optical potential to simulate interactions with the dense medium, whereas here microscopic "nuclear" potentials are applied to "pre-formed" hadrons from string fragmentation. The results enable further exploration of early-stage dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note that a more complete description including (in)elastic scatterings of confined hadrons and deconfined partons is needed, and that ideally the equation of state for the new phase should be based on first-principle lattice QCD calculations (Bluhm et al., 2007).
