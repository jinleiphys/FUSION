# Pairing in Nuclei

> Simple generic aspects of nuclear pairing in homogeneous medium as well as in finite nuclei are discussed. It is argued that low-energy nuclear structure is not sensitive enough to resolve fine details of nuclear nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in general and pairing NN interaction in particular what allows for regularization of the ultraviolet (high-momentum) divergences and a consistent formulation of effective superfluid local theory. Some aspects of (dis)entanglement of pairing with various other effects as well as forefront ideas concerning isoscalar pairing are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Low-energy nuclear structure is not sensitive enough to resolve fine details of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in general and pairing NN interaction in particular, which allows for regularization of ultraviolet divergences and a consistent formulation of an effective superfluid local theory; however, the paper notes that this insensitivity holds only because nucleonic Cooper pairs are spatially extended objects with a coherence length much larger than the interaction range.

## Method

The paper reviews the independence of the $^1$S$_0$ pairing gap in homogeneous nuclear matter with respect to details of the bare NN interaction, comparing results from CD-Bonn and Nijmegen potentials. It then presents regularization schemes for local superfluid theory, including the Papenbrock-Bertsch effective theory approach that uses the scattering length as a counter-term, and the Bulgac-Yu superfluid local density approximation (SLDA) that introduces a running coupling constant to remove cutoff dependence. The discussion also covers analysis of odd-even staggering (OES) in superdeformed bands and high-spin isomers in $N=83$ nuclei, as well as shell-model and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations of band-terminating states in the $A\sim50$ mass region to search for isoscalar pairing signatures.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length $\xi \sim 50$ fm for nuclear matter, derived from $\xi \sim \frac{(\hbar c)^2 k_F}{(mc^2)\Delta}$, exceeding the typical interaction range $r_o \sim 1/k_F$
- The phase-shift approximation for the $^1$S$_0$ gap works surprisingly well up to Fermi momentum $k_F\sim 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$, while the effective range approximation works only at low densities up to $k_F\sim 0.6$ fm$^{-1}$
- The finite-range Gogny force automatically discriminates states above $E_c\sim 40$ MeV, based on $r_o p_c \sim \hbar$ with $r_o\sim 1$ fm
- Inclusion of shape and pairing polarization effects in high-spin isomers requires approximately 10% larger pairing strength $G$ compared to the traditional average gap method to reproduce experimental OES
- Shell-model and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations of energy differences $\Delta E = E(d_{3/2}^{-1} f_{7/2}^{n+1}) - E(f_{7/2}^n)$ in $A\sim50$ nuclei show a constant offset of approximately 475 keV relative to experimental data

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on nuclear pairing by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958), Belyayev (1959), and Nilsson and Prior (1961), who established the BCS approach with constant matrix element pairing interactions. The paper discusses how the Gogny force (parameter set D1S from Berger et al., 1984) and local density dependent delta interactions can reproduce pairing gaps calculated from bare NN potentials, as shown by Garrido et al. (1999). The regularization schemes build on the effective theory approach of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1999) and the SLDA method of Bulgac and Yu (2002). The discussion of isoscalar pairing addresses open questions about the Wigner energy in $N=Z$ nuclei, which shell-model studies (e.g., Brendel et al., 1990; Satula and Wyss, 1997) attribute predominantly to $T=0$ interaction, though the structure is complex and not dominated by $L=0,S=1,T=0$ or $J=1,T=0$ isoscalar pairs.
