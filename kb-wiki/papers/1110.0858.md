# Hadronic Parity Violation in Effective Field Theory

> An effective field theory program to analyze and interpret hadronic parity violation in two-, three-, and few-nucleon systems is described. Observables can be parameterized in terms of five low-energy constants, which have to be determined from experimental input. Results for parity-violating observables in the two- and three-nucleon sectors are presented, including a discussion of the relevance of parity-violating three-nucleon interactions.

# Hadronic Parity Violation in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Hadronic parity violation in two-, three-, and few-nucleon systems can be systematically parameterized in terms of five low-energy constants (LECs) within a pionless effective field theory (EFT) framework, but the abstract's claim that these LECs "have to be determined from experimental input" glosses over the fact that currently insufficient experimental data exist from the two- and three-nucleon sectors to fully determine them, making predictions contingent on ongoing experimental efforts.

## Method
The author employs a pionless effective field theory (EFT($\slashed{\pi}$)) containing only nucleons as dynamical degrees of freedom, organized by a power counting that predicts the relative size of contributions to observables. The parity-violating Lagrangian is written in terms of five partial-wave transition operators using auxiliary dibaryon fields, corresponding to the field-theoretic equivalent of Danilov amplitudes. Leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculations are performed for parity-violating observables in two-nucleon systems ($\vec{p}p$ scattering, $\vec{n}p$ spin rotation, $np\leftrightarrow d\gamma$) and three-nucleon systems ($\vec{n}d$ spin rotation), with analysis of whether parity-violating three-nucleon interactions are required for renormalization.

## Key numbers
- The parity-violating component of nucleon-nucleon interactions is suppressed by about $10^{-7}-10^{-6}$ compared to the parity-conserving part.
- For $\vec{p}p$ scattering at $E=13.6\,\text{MeV}$, the longitudinal asymmetry $A_L^{pp}$ is proportional to $4pM \sqrt{\rSing/\pi}\,\left[ \dbR + \dcR + \ddR \right]$, where $p$ is center-of-mass momentum, $M$ the nucleon mass, and $\rSing$ the effective range in the singlet channel.
- The estimated $\vec{n}p$ spin rotation angle magnitude is $|\text{d}\phi_\text{PV}^{np}/\text{d}l| \approx (10^{-7} - 10^{-6})\,\text{rad}/\text{m}$ for a target density $\rho\approx10^{23}\,\text{cm}^{-3}$.
- The estimated $\vec{n}d$ spin rotation angle magnitude is $|\text{d}\phi_\text{PV}^{nd}/\text{d}l| \approx (10^{-7} - 10^{-6})\,\text{rad}/\text{m}$, of the same size as the hydrogen target estimate.
- Theoretical error estimates from higher-order contributions are roughly $10\%$ for NLO calculations.

## Context
This work extends the Danilov amplitude approach (Danilov, 1965) and the meson-exchange model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1979) by providing a systematic, model-independent effective field theory framework with theoretical error estimates. The pionless EFT approach builds on prior work by Bedaque and van Kolck (2002) for parity-conserving few-nucleon systems and by Zhu et al. (2004), Girlanda et al. (2008), and Phillips et al. (2008) for parity-violating operators. The analysis confirms the finding of Griesshammer et al. (2010) that no parity-violating three-nucleon interaction is required for renormalization up to NLO, ensuring that the five two-nucleon LECs suffice for calculations with approximately 10% accuracy. The results enable future predictions of parity-violating observables in three- and few-nucleon systems once sufficient experimental constraints become available from ongoing efforts at the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory and NIST.
