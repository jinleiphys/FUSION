# Unraveling the Energy-Energy Correlators for Heavy Flavor Tagged Jets in pp, p+Pb and Pb+Pb Collisions

> In this study, energy-energy correlator (EEC) distributions for the $\rm D^0$, $\rm B^0$-tagged, inclusive, and the PYTHIA generated pure quark jets are computed in pp, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at \sqrts =5.02 TeV for a same jet transverse momentum interval 15-30 GeV. We find the number of particles per jet determines the height of the EEC distribution in pp baseline. The averaged energy weight distribution resulted in a shift of the originally larger angular distributed particle distribution to a smaller RL, thereby obtaining an EEC distribution. The EEC distributions for all quark-tagged jets in A+A exhibit a noticeable shift towards larger RL region, suggesting that the jets will be more widely distributed compared to those in pp collisions. The jet quenching effect will cause the pair angular distribution to shift towards larger values and increase the number of particles per jet. This redistribution of energy within the jets suggests that the already reduced jet energy is redistributed among a larger number of particles, leading to a reduced energy weight per pair. The enhancement of the number of particles per jet and the reduced averaged energy weight interplay with each other to form the medium modification of EEC.

## Key claim

The paper decomposes the energy-energy correlator (EEC) observable into three separate factors, number of particles per jet, normalized pair-angular distribution, and averaged energy weight, to show that the medium modification of EEC in heavy-ion collisions arises from an interplay between an increased number of particles per jet and a reduced average energy weight per pair, rather than from a simple shift in angular scale alone.

## Method

The authors use PYTHIA 8 with the Monash 2013 tune to simulate pp, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, reconstructing anti-k_T jets with radius R = 0.4 for jet transverse momentum 15–30 GeV and pseudorapidity |eta_jet| < 0.5. For p+Pb collisions, nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) are implemented via the EPPS21 parameterization. For Pb+Pb collisions, the in-medium evolution of partons is simulated using the SHELL model, which combines Langevin dynamics for elastic scattering with a Higher-Twist approach for medium-induced gluon radiation, coupled to the CLVisc hydrodynamic background. The EEC is then rewritten as a product of three factors: the average number of particle pairs per jet, the normalized angular distribution of pairs, and the average energy weight per pair.

## Key numbers

*   The average number of particle pairs per jet in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV is 1.3751 for B0-tagged jets, 7.4908 for D0-tagged jets, 13.2147 for light quark-initiated jets, and 19.2787 for inclusive charged jets.
*   In Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the average number of particle pairs per jet increases to 1.8902 for B0-tagged jets, 8.9286 for D0-tagged jets, 17.6994 for light quark-initiated jets, and 21.7357 for inclusive charged jets, giving PbPb/pp ratios of 1.3746, 1.1919, 1.3393, and 1.1274 respectively.
*   In p+Pb collisions, the nPDF-induced suppression of the EEC ratio R_pA reaches a maximum of approximately 0.85 for B0-tagged jets in the angular range R_L = 0.007–0.02.
*   The EEC distributions for all quark-tagged jets in Pb+Pb show a shift toward larger R_L, with light quark-initiated jets exhibiting the strongest enhancement in the R_L region 0.15–0.7, while B0-tagged jets show maximum enhancement around R_L = 0.3–0.4.

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions, building on the ALICE measurement of EEC for D0-tagged jets at 13 TeV (ALICE 2024) and the authors' prior work on inclusive charged jets (Chen 2024). It provides a decomposition of the EEC observable that enables direct comparison of the angular structure of heavy-flavor tagged jets (B0, D0) with light quark and inclusive jets, revealing a mass-dependent peak shift in the rescaled EEC that reflects the dead-cone effect. The analysis enables future experimental measurements to separate the contributions of particle multiplicity and energy redistribution to the medium modification of jet substructure observables.
