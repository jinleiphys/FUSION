# Dark-matter-nucleus scattering in chiral effective field theory

> Chiral effective field theory allows one to calculate the response of few-nucleon systems to external currents, both for currents that can be probed in the Standard Model and ones that only exist in Standard-Model extensions. In combination with state-of-the-art many-body methods, the constraints from chiral symmetry can then be implemented in nuclear structure factors that describe the response of atomic nuclei in direct-detection searches for dark matter. We review the present status of this approach, including the role of coherently enhanced two-body currents, the discrimination of dark matter candidates based on the nuclear response functions, and limits on Higgs-portal dark matter.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic framework for calculating nuclear response functions in dark-matter direct detection, but the approach currently relies on the nuclear shell model for heavy nuclei like xenon because ab initio methods consistent with chiral interactions are not yet available for such heavy systems.

## Method

The authors use chiral EFT to derive one- and two-body currents for WIMP-nucleus scattering, including scalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor operators, up to one-loop order in the chiral expansion. These currents are then embedded in nuclear structure factors calculated using the large-scale nuclear shell model for nuclei relevant to direct-detection experiments, with two-body contact operators renormalized to the nuclear binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Typical momentum transfer in WIMP-nucleus scattering is q ≤ 2 μ_{Nχ} v ∼ 200 MeV, with reduced mass μ_{Nχ} ∼ 100 GeV and relative velocity v ∼ 10^{-3}
- The scalar single-nucleon matrix element f_N = f_N^{1b} + f_N^{2b} = 0.308(18), combining one- and two-body contributions for Higgs-portal dark matter
- The standard SI single-nucleon cross section relation: σ_{χN}^{SI} = μ_N^2 |c_+^M|^2 / π, where μ_N is the reduced WIMP-nucleon mass
- Discrimination study uses interaction strength σ_0 = 10^{-47} cm^2 for a WIMP mass m_χ = 100 GeV in a DARWIN-like detector setting

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of WIMP-nucleus scattering by Prezeau (2003), Cirigliano (2012), Menendez (2012), and Klos (2013), among others, providing a comprehensive review of the formalism including coherently enhanced two-body currents. The results enable improved interpretation of direct-detection limits from experiments such as XENON1T, LUX, and PandaX-II, and allow discrimination of dark matter candidates based on nuclear response functions. The publicly available Python notebook provides structure factors for fluorine, silicon, argon, germanium, and xenon, facilitating future comparisons with experimental data.
