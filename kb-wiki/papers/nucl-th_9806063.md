# The reaction $πN \to ππN$ above threshold in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We study the reaction $π^\pm p \to π^\pm π^+ n$ and $π^- p \to π^0 π^0 n$ in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory of chiral order three from threshold up to pion laboratory kinetic energies of 400 MeV. We find that the contributions from amplitudes of chiral order three are large and play an essential role in reproducing the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors find that contributions from amplitudes of chiral order three are large and essential for reproducing experimental data, but note that the slow convergence of the chiral expansion (with O(q³) contributions much larger than lower-order terms) indicates a potential problem for the theory, and that results are sensitive to the unknown low-energy constants (LECs) which introduce significant uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute total cross sections for the reactions π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n and π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to chiral order O(q³). They evaluate the transition amplitude from the effective chiral Lagrangian, including one-loop diagrams and counterterms, and perform the phase-space integration numerically using a Monte-Carlo routine. The unknown LECs (b₄, b₁₁, b₁₂, b₁₃, b₁₄, b₁₇) are treated as free parameters, varied over the range -10 to 10 to study sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed for pion laboratory kinetic energies from threshold up to 400 MeV, with results presented separately for the ranges threshold to 200 MeV and threshold to 400 MeV.
- Three sets of calculations for each reaction: all unknown LECs set to 0, set to 10, and set to -10.
- The threshold amplitudes D₁ and D₂ are listed in Tables 3 and 4 (numerical values not provided in the truncated text).
- The leading order amplitudes "consistently underestimate the experimental results by a large amount over most of the kinematic range."

## Context

This work extends the threshold HBChPT calculation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) to energies above threshold up to 400 MeV, and contrasts with tree-level chiral Lagrangian models that include resonances explicitly (Oset 1985, Jensen 1997) which are not consistent with chiral power counting. The results enable future systematic determination of the unknown LECs by fitting total and differential cross-section data, and highlight the need to address the slow convergence of HBChPT observed also in other processes.
