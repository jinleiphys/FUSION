# Toward a bridge between relativistic and nonrelativistic density functional theories for nuclei

> The nonrelativistic reduction of the self-consistent covariant density functional theory is realized for the first time with the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method. The reduced nonrelativistic Hamiltonian and densities are calculated by solving the corresponding flow equations with a novel expansion in terms of the inverse of the Dirac effective mass. The efficiency and accuracy of this newly proposed framework have been demonstrated for several typical spherical nuclei. It is found that the exact solutions of the total energies, traces of vector and scalar densities, and the root-mean-square radii are reproduced quite well for all nuclei. This allows one to directly compare and bridge the relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear energy density functional theories in the future.

## Key claim

The authors achieve the first self-consistent nonrelativistic reduction of covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, but the accuracy for total energies and single-particle spectra depends on including higher-order terms (up to at least the \(p^6\) term) in the expansion in the inverse Dirac effective mass, and the radii are not improved by that term.

## Method

The Dirac Hamiltonian and the vector- and scalar-density operators are transformed via SRG flow equations, using a novel perturbative expansion in powers of \(1/\tilde{M}\), where \(\tilde{M}=M+S\) is the Dirac effective mass (the bare nucleon mass plus the scalar potential). The resulting block-diagonal (even) Hamiltonian and density operators are solved self-consistently for spherical nuclei using the point-coupling functional PC-PK1 in a harmonic-oscillator basis. Results are compared to exact CDFT solutions obtained by the shooting method.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{208}\)Pb, the SRG method (without the \(p^6\) term) gives a total energy \(E_{\rm tot} = -1652.87\) MeV, compared to the exact value \(-1637.45\) MeV; including the \(p^6\) term improves this to \(-1640.77\) MeV.
* For \(^{208}\)Pb, the trace of the scalar density \(\text{Tr}(\rho_s)\) is 199.50 (SRG) vs. 199.63 (exact); with the \(p^6\) term it becomes 199.60.
* For \(^{208}\)Pb, the root-mean-square radius \(R_m\) is 5.70 fm (SRG) and 5.71 fm (SRG with \(p^6\) term), vs. the exact 5.62 fm.
* After including the \(p^6\) term, the mean deviation of the total energy per particle \(E_{\rm tot}/A\) across seven nuclei (\(^{16}\)O, \(^{40,48}\)Ca, \(^{100,120,132}\)Sn, \(^{208}\)Pb) is reduced to 0.035 MeV.
* The Kohn-Sham potential \(V(r)+S(r)\) from the SRG method is shallower by about 3 MeV for \(r<5\) fm and slightly deeper for \(r>6\) fm compared to the exact potential for \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The total vector and scalar densities for \(^{208}\)Pb are underestimated by about 0.007 fm\(^{-3}\) in the interior (\(r<5\) fm) and slightly overestimated in the surface (\(r>6\) fm).

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach previously applied to fixed potentials (Guo 2012, Guo 2014, Zhao 2014) and the improved \(1/\tilde{M}\) expansion (Ren 2019) to fully self-consistent CDFT calculations. It provides a direct bridge between relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear density functional theories, enabling future comparisons of the role of specific terms in each framework. The method contrasts with earlier nonrelativistic reductions based on Schrodinger-like equations (Reinhard 1989) or the reconstituted Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation (Guo 2019, Guo 2020), and it preserves the Hermiticity of the reduced Hamiltonian at each order.
