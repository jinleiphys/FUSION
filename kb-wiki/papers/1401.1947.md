# Poles of Karlsruhe-Helsinki KH80 and KA84 solutions extracted by using Laurent+Pietarinen method

> Poles of partial wave scattering matrices have also in hadron spectroscopy recently been established as a sole link between experiment and QCD theories and models [1-4]. We have, however, noticed the fact that Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH) partial wave analyses, one of the two partial wave analyses which have been "above the line" in PDG [1] for over three decades, does calculate and PDG compiles the abundance of Breit-Wigner (BW) parameters obtained by local BW fit, but has produced only a limited number of pole information obtained by using speed plot (SP) method [5]. We stress the fact that in KH method only Mandelstam analyticity is used as a theoretical constraint, so KH partial wave solutions are as model independent as possible. Therefore, they represent a very confident and valuable input, so it is of extreme importance to extract as many resonance pole parameters from them as possible. In addition, the troubling fact is that BW parameters given in PDG have been obtained from KH80 solution, while pole parameters have been obtained from KA84 version of KH solution. To remedy this, we have used newly developed Laurent+Pietarinen expansion method [6,7] for obtaining pole positions for all partial waves for KH80 and KA84 solutions, compared them, and shown that the differences in pole parameters are, with very few discussed exceptions, negligible for all partial waves. We finally give a full set of pole parameters for both solutions for all partial waves; results which are until now unpublished.

## Key claim

The authors use the Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) method to extract pole parameters from both the KH80 and KA84 Karlsruhe-Helsinki partial wave solutions, and show that differences between the two sets of pole parameters are negligible for most partial waves, with very few exceptions, thereby providing a complete set of pole parameters for both solutions that was previously unpublished.

## Method

The authors apply the Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) expansion method, which combines a Laurent (Mittag-Leffler) expansion for resonant poles with Pietarinen series expansions to represent background contributions from branch cuts, to fit the KH80 and KA84 partial wave amplitudes on the real energy axis. They use three Pietarinen functions (one for left-hand cuts, two for physical inelastic channels) combined with a minimal number of poles, and determine all parameters by minimizing a discrepancy parameter \(D_{dp}\) using MINUIT. The systematic uncertainty is estimated by varying the third physical branch point \(x_R\) among thresholds of dominant inelastic channels and by releasing it as a free parameter, then averaging results and computing standard deviations.

## Key numbers

* For the \(S_{11}\) partial wave, the \(N(1535) 1/2^{-}\) pole from KH80 L+P is at \(1509 \pm 4 \pm 2\) MeV (real part) with width \(-2\,\text{Im}\,W_p = 118 \pm 9 \pm 2\) MeV; from KA84 L+P it is at \(1505 \pm 3 \pm 1\) MeV with width \(103 \pm 7 \pm 3\) MeV.
* For the \(P_{11}\) partial wave, the \(N(1440) 1/2^{+}\) pole from KH80 L+P is at \(1363 \pm 2 \pm 2\) MeV with width \(180 \pm 4 \pm 5\) MeV; from KA84 L+P it is at \(1365 \pm 2 \pm 4\) MeV with width \(187 \pm 4 \pm 10\) MeV.
* For the \(D_{13}\) partial wave, the \(N(1520) 3/2^{-}\) pole from KH80 L+P is at \(1506 \pm 1 \pm 1\) MeV with width \(115 \pm 2 \pm 1\) MeV; from KA84 L+P it is at \(1506 \pm 1 \pm 1\) MeV with width \(116 \pm 2 \pm 2\) MeV.
* For the \(F_{15}\) partial wave, the \(N(1680) 5/2^{+}\) pole from KH80 L+P is at \(1674 \pm 2 \pm 1\) MeV with width \(129 \pm 3 \pm 1\) MeV; from KA84 L+P it is at \(1672 \pm 2 \pm 1\) MeV with width \(132 \pm 4 \pm 1\) MeV.
* For the \(G_{17}\) partial wave, the \(N(2190) 7/2^{+}\) pole from KH80 L+P is at \(2079 \pm 4 \pm 9\) MeV with width \(509 \pm 7 \pm 16\) MeV; from KA84 L+P it is at \(2065 \pm 3 \pm 11\) MeV with width \(526 \pm 7 \pm 2\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Karlsruhe-Helsinki partial wave analyses (KH80 and KA84) by Hohler et al. (1984) and Koch et al. (1985), which previously provided only limited pole information obtained via the speed plot method of Hohler (1993). The authors use the recently developed Laurent+Pietarinen method (Svarc et al., 2012, 2013) to extract a complete set of pole parameters for all partial waves, addressing the inconsistency that Breit-Wigner parameters in the Review of Particle Physics came from KH80 while pole parameters came from KA84. This enables future comparisons with other analyses such as the Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley (CMB) analysis by Cutkosky et al. (1979, 1980), and provides a more complete link between experimental scattering data and QCD-inspired models.
