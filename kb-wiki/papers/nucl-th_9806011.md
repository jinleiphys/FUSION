# Mesons as qbar-q Bound States from Euclidean 2-Point Correlators in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach

> We investigate the 2-point correlation function for the vector current. The gluons provide dressings for both the quark self energy as well as the vector vertex function, which are described consistently by the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation and the inhomogeneous ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation. The form of the gluon propagator at low momenta is modeled by a 2-parameter ansatz fitting the weak pion decay constant. The quarks are confined in the sense that the quark propagator does not have a pole at timelike momenta. We determine the ground state mass in the vector channel from the Euclidean time Fourier transform of the correlator, which has an exponential falloff at large times. The ground state mass lies around 590 MeV and is almost independent of the model form for the gluon propagator. This method allows us to stay in Euclidean space and to avoid analytic continuation of the quark or gluon propagators into the timelike region.

## Key claim

The ground state mass of the vector meson extracted from the Euclidean-time Fourier transform of the 2-point correlator lies around 590 MeV and is almost independent of the model form for the gluon propagator, though this value is lower than the phenomenological rho meson mass of 780 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator and the inhomogeneous ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed vector vertex function, using a common model gluon propagator as input. The gluon propagator is modeled by a two-parameter ansatz (an infrared-regularized Mandelstam 1/q^4 singularity plus an asymptotic ultraviolet tail) with parameters fixed to reproduce the weak pion decay constant f_pi = 88 MeV in the chiral limit. The vector 2-point correlation function is computed entirely in Euclidean space, and its Fourier transform with respect to Euclidean time is evaluated to extract the ground state mass from the exponential falloff at large times.

## Key numbers

- Ground state vector meson mass m_0 ≈ 590 MeV, extracted from the exponential falloff of the Fourier-transformed correlator for all three parameter sets
- Weak pion decay constant in the chiral limit f_pi = 88 MeV, used to fix the gluon propagator parameters
- Three parameter sets used: Set 1 (Delta = 1.0e-4 GeV^4, chi = 1.02 GeV), Set 2 (Delta = 5.0e-4 GeV^4, chi = 1.12 GeV), Set 3 (Delta = 1.0e-3 GeV^4, chi = 1.18 GeV)
- QCD scale Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV used in the ultraviolet tail of the gluon propagator
- Comparison values: phenomenological vector meson mass 780 MeV; instanton liquid model gives 950 MeV (Shuryak); quenched lattice QCD gives 720 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach (Roberts and Williams; Tandy) to higher mass meson states beyond Goldstone bosons, using a method that avoids analytic continuation of propagators into the timelike region. It contrasts with earlier approaches that solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation on shell (Frank) and with QCD sum rules that use the Borel transform (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov). The method enables future study of other meson channels (e.g., scalar, axial-vector) and provides a Euclidean-space framework analogous to lattice QCD calculations (Cucchieri et al.) and instanton models (Shuryak).
