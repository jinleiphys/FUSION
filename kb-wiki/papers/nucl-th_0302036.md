# Spin effects and relative momentum spectrum of two protons in deuteron charge-exchange breakup

> The relation between the differential cross section of the charge-exchange breakup of a fast deuteron d+p -> (pp)+n and the differential cross section of the charge transfer process n+p -> p+n is discussed taking into account the effects of the proton identity and of the Coulomb and strong interactions of protons in the final state. The distribution of the relative momenta of the protons is found in the framework of the impulse approach. It is shown that the use of polarized initial deuterons and protons allows one to separate two spin-dependent terms in the amplitude of the charge transfer reaction n+p -> p+n at zero angle and to determine their phase difference. The influence of the deuteron d-wave state is investigated.

# Spin effects and relative momentum spectrum of two protons in deuteron charge-exchange breakup

## Key claim

The differential cross section of the forward charge-exchange breakup of a fast deuteron on a proton target is determined only by the spin-flip part of the amplitude of the charge transfer reaction n+p -> p+n at zero angle, provided the momentum transfer is close to zero; the study of this process with polarized deuterons and a polarized proton target allows separation of two spin-dependent terms in that amplitude and determination of their phase difference, though the accuracy of the soft part of the proton momentum spectrum is estimated at the level of several percent and Glauber rescattering corrections may reach about 20% for relative momenta above 100 MeV/c.

## Method

The authors employed the impulse approach to relate the differential cross section of the deuteron charge-exchange breakup d+p -> (pp)+n to the differential cross section of the charge transfer reaction n+p -> p+n, accounting for proton identity (Fermi statistics) and final-state Coulomb and strong interactions via exact solutions of the Schroedinger equation for the two-proton system. The deuteron wave function was modeled using both the Hulthen expression and a square-well potential, while the two-proton s-wave radial function was calculated using a combined Coulomb and strong square-well potential with parameters fitted to low-energy pp-scattering data (scattering length f0 = 7.8 fm, effective radius d0 = 2.8 fm). The contribution of the deuteron d-wave state was included using a wave function with s-wave and d-wave amplitudes beta^2 = 0.98 and gamma^2 = 0.2.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron radius rho = 4.3 fm, effective radius d = 1.7 fm, alpha = 6.25 (Hulthen wave function parameters)
- Square-well potential parameters for deuteron: width b = 2.06 fm, depth V = 34 MeV, binding energy epsilon = 2.3 MeV
- Square-well potential parameters for pp-scattering: V = 11.7 MeV, b = 2.78 fm, scattering length f0 = 7.8 fm, effective radius d0 = 2.8 fm
- Bohr radius of the two-proton system a_B = 57.5 fm
- Dimensionless variable x = k*rho, with k = 45.8*x MeV/c
- The difference between the Hulthen and square-well calculations of the momentum spectrum functions Q(x) and x^2 Q(x) does not exceed a few percent
- At small relative proton momenta (k < 50 MeV/c), Glauber rescattering corrections do not exceed several percent; at k > 100 MeV/c they can be essential (about 20%)
- Deuteron s-wave and d-wave amplitudes: beta = 0.98, gamma = 0.2, with beta^2 + gamma^2 = 1

## Context

This work extends earlier impulse-approach studies of deuteron charge-exchange breakup by Lednicky and collaborators (2001, 2002) which considered only unpolarized initial particles and treated final-state interactions approximately with tens-of-percent accuracy. The present paper improves the calculation of the proton relative momentum spectrum to the percent level using exact solutions of the Schroedinger equation, and incorporates the deuteron d-wave state and polarization effects. The results enable experimental separation of the two spin-dependent amplitudes a and b in the n+p -> p+n charge transfer reaction at zero angle, and determination of their phase difference, using polarized deuteron beams on polarized proton targets. The authors note that Glauber rescattering corrections were not included and should be accounted for at higher relative momenta.
