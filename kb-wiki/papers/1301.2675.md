# Dense QCD and phenomenology of compact stars

> I discuss three topics in physics of massive (two solar-mass and larger) neutron stars containing deconfined quark matter: (i) the equation of state of deconfined dense quark matter and its color superconducting phases, (ii) the thermal evolution of stars with quark cores, (iii) color-magnetic flux tubes in type-II superconducting quark matter and their dynamics driven by Aharonov-Bohm interactions with unpaired fermions.

## Key claim
Massive neutron stars (around two solar masses) can contain deconfined quark matter in color-superconducting phases while remaining consistent with the observational lower bound on maximum mass, but only for specific combinations of high vector coupling in the quark equation of state and high transition density from hadronic to quark matter.

## Method
The author constructs hybrid star models by combining a relativistic mean-field nuclear equation of state (using NL3 and GM3 parameterizations fitted to bulk nuclear and hypernuclear data) with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian for quark matter that includes the t' Hooft interaction and vector interactions with coupling G_V. The thermal evolution of these stars is then computed using a cooling code that incorporates neutrino emissivities from paired and unpaired quark phases, with the pairing gaps and transition temperatures treated as free parameters. The dynamics of color-magnetic flux tubes in the two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase are analyzed via the Aharonov-Bohm scattering cross section of unpaired fermions off flux tubes.

## Key numbers
- The recent discovery of a compact star with mass 1.97 M_sun sets an observationally clean lower bound on the maximum mass via Shapiro delay (Demorest 2010).
- Pulse phase-resolved X-ray spectroscopy of PSR J0437-4715 sets a lower limit on the radius of a 1.76 M_sun star: R > 11 km within 3-sigma error (Bogdanov, in Radii).
- For the cooling model of Cas A, the transition temperature T* from the perfect 2SC phase to a crystalline color superconducting state is approximately 10 MeV in an NJL-based equilibrium model.
- The lower critical field H_c1 for formation of Abrikosov flux tubes containing color-magnetic flux in the 2SC phase is of order 10^17 G.
- The timescale for expulsion of color-magnetic flux tubes from a 2SC core is estimated to be of order 10^10 years (Alford 2010).
- The critical temperature for pairing of blue quarks T_cb = 0.1 MeV was used in the Cas A cooling simulations.

## Context
This work extends the modeling of hybrid stars with color-superconducting quark cores (Bonanno 2011) to include thermal evolution and magnetic flux tube dynamics, building on earlier cooling studies (Alford 2004) and flux tube analyses (Alford 2010). The results enable future studies of rapid rotations and oscillations in massive compact objects, as well as improved modeling of weak interaction rates and transport coefficients (such as thermal conductivity) needed for thermal and magnetic evolution simulations.
