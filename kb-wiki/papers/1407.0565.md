# Remarkable Features of Decaying Hagedorn States

> Hagedorn states (HS) are a tool to model the hadronization process which occurs in the phase transition phase between the quark gluon plasma (QGP) and the hadron resonance gas (HRG). Their abundance is believed to appear near the Hagedorn temperature $T_H$ which in our understanding equals the critical temperature $T_c$. These hadron-like resonances are characterized by being very massive and by not being limited to quantum numbers of known hadrons. To generate a whole zoo of such new states we solve the covariantly formulated bootstrap equation by regarding energy conservation and conservation of the baryon number $B$, strangeness $S$ and electric charge $Q$. To investigate their decay properties decay chain calculations of HS were conducted. One single (heavy) HS with certain quantum numbers decays by various two-body decay channels subsequently into final stable hadrons. Multiplicities of these stable hadrons, their ratios and their energy distributions are presented. Strikingly the final energy spectra of resulting hadrons show a thermal-like distribution with the characteristic Hagedorn temperature $T_H$. All hadronic properties like masses, spectral functions etc. are taken from the hadronic transport model Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD).

## Key claim

The final energy spectra of hadrons produced by the cascade decay of a single heavy Hagedorn state, without any reinteractions, follow a thermal Boltzmann distribution whose temperature exactly equals the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\) obtained from fitting the exponential part of the Hagedorn mass spectrum, but only for the specific case of radius \(R = 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Method

The authors solved a covariantly formulated bootstrap equation with conservation of energy, baryon number \(B\), strangeness \(S\), and electric charge \(Q\) to generate Hagedorn state mass spectra for different quantum number combinations and radii. Using these spectra and the principle of detailed balance, they computed total and partial two-body decay widths and branching ratios, then performed cascade decay simulations of single initial Hagedorn states down to stable hadrons, with all hadronic properties taken from the UrQMD transport model.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature \(T_H = 0.145\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for radius \(R = 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(T_H = 0.162\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for \(R = 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}\), both nearly independent of quantum number combinations.
- The thermal temperature of final hadron spectra from cascade decays equals \(T_{th} = 0.162\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for \(R = 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}\), matching the fitted Hagedorn temperature.
- For an initial Hagedorn state mass of \(4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with \(B=S=Q=0\), the calculated multiplicity ratios include \(K^-/\pi^- = 0.187\), \(\overline p/\pi^- = 0.043\), \(\Lambda/\pi^- = 0.021\), \(\Lambda/\overline p = 0.494\), \(\Xi^-/\pi^- = 0.0023\), and \(\Omega^-/\pi^- \cdot 10^{-3} = 0.086\).
- For an initial Hagedorn state mass of \(8\ \mathrm{GeV}\) with \(B=S=Q=0\), the calculated multiplicity ratios include \(K^-/\pi^- = 0.210\), \(\overline p/\pi^- = 0.066\), \(\Lambda/\pi^- = 0.038\), \(\Lambda/\overline p = 0.579\), \(\Xi^-/\pi^- = 0.0066\), and \(\Omega^-/\pi^- \cdot 10^{-3} = 0.560\).
- The total decay width of Hagedorn states tends to a constant value for large masses, depending only on the radius \(R\).
- The probability for a Hagedorn state to decay into two particles is \(69\%\), into three particles \(24\%\), etc., giving an accepted error of about \(30\%\) from the two-body approximation.

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model originally proposed by Hagedorn (1965) and later developed by Frautschi (1971) and Hamer (1971), providing a covariant formulation with explicit quantum number conservation. The finding that cascade decays of Hagedorn states produce thermal spectra with temperature equal to the Hagedorn temperature offers a microscopic mechanism for thermal-like hadronization in ultrarelativistic collisions, complementing earlier work by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2007, 2009, 2010) on Hagedorn states as catalysts for chemical equilibration of strange baryons. The results enable future implementation of Hagedorn states into the UrQMD transport model via \(2 \leftrightarrow 1\) processes, allowing investigation of thermalization, chemical equilibration, and transport properties near the deconfinement transition.
