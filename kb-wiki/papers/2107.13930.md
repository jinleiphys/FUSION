# Triply-heavy tetraquarks in an extended relativized quark model

> In this paper, we adopt an extended relativized quark model to investigate the triply-heavy tetrquarks systematically. The mass spectra are obtained by solving the four-body relativized Hamiltonian including the Coulomb potential, confining potential, spin-spin interactions, and relativistic corrections. We find that all of the triply-heavy tetraquarks lie above the corresponding meson-meson thresholds, and thus no stable one exists. In particular, besides the spin-spin interactions, the Coulomb and confining potentials also contribute to the mass splittings in the $cb\bar c \bar q$ and $cb\bar b \bar q$ systems. Moreover, the whole mass spectra for triply heavy tetraquarks show quite similar patterns, which preserve the light flavor SU(3) symmetry and heavy quark symmetry well. Through the fall-apart mechanism, the triply-heavy tetraquarks may easily decay into the heavy quarkonium plus heavy-light mesons, which are good candidates for investigation in future experiments.

## Key claim

All triply-heavy tetraquarks studied lie above their corresponding meson-meson thresholds, meaning no stable bound state exists; the authors qualify that this result depends on the specific extended relativized quark model used, which includes relativistic corrections and a confining potential alongside Coulomb and spin-spin interactions.

## Method

The authors solved a four-body relativized Hamiltonian for S-wave triply-heavy tetraquarks (Q1Q2Q3q4) using the Gaussian expansion method with 1000 Gaussian bases. The Hamiltonian included relativistic kinetic energy, a one-gluon-exchange potential with smeared Coulomb and spin-spin terms, and a confining potential, with all parameters taken from the original Godfrey-Isgur relativized quark model (Godfrey 1985). Color, flavor, spin, and spatial wave functions were constructed for each system, and mixing between different configurations with the same J^P was included by diagonalizing the mass matrix.

## Key numbers

- For the cc c n system: masses range from 5400 MeV (1+ state) to 5506 MeV (2+ state); the 0+ states are at 5403 MeV and 5533 MeV.
- For the cc c s system: masses range from 5476 MeV (0+ state) to 5599 MeV (0+ state); the 2+ state is at 5584 MeV.
- For the cc b n system: masses range from 8658 MeV (0+ state) to 8761 MeV (0+ state); the 2+ state is at 8755 MeV.
- For the cc b s system: masses range from 8751 MeV (0+ state) to 8845 MeV (0+ state); the 2+ state is at 8840 MeV.
- Mass splittings for cc c n and cc c s systems are 133 MeV and 123 MeV respectively, comparable to D and Ds meson splittings.
- For the bb c n system: masses are around 12 GeV with a mass splitting of 140 MeV.
- For the bb c s system: mass splitting is 117 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of singly-, doubly-, and fully-heavy tetraquarks (Lu 2020) using the same extended relativized quark model framework. It contradicts earlier predictions from the chromomagnetic interaction model and QCD sum rule (Chen 2016, Jiang 2017) which found weakly bound triply-heavy tetraquarks, while being consistent with the nonrelativistic quark model study (Silvestre-Brac 1993) that found no bound states. The results provide mass predictions and decay channels for future experimental searches, particularly in heavy quarkonium plus heavy-light meson final states via the fall-apart mechanism.
