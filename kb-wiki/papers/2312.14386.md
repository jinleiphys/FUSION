# Systematic study of the rotational bands in the $A\sim 250$ mass region

> For the first time, we conducted a comprehensive analysis of the rotational bands in the \( A\sim250 \) mass region. Utilizing a variety of rotational energy models and formulas, we have extracted free parameters for 36 rotational bands within this mass region, encompassing neutron numbers from N = 148 to 152. A significant enhancement has been made to the vibrational distortion model, leading to an exceptional match with the experimental data on dynamic moment of inertia across most of the rotational bands studied. Through the application of this revised model, we have categorized the dynamic moment of inertia into three distinct groups. The implications of the modified vibrational distortion term and how it evolves with changes in rotational frequency is also investigated. Our systematic investigation brings to light the peculiar behavior of Pu isotopes, especially in ground state bands of \( ^{242,244}\text{Pu}\), within this mass region. We provide a thorough discussion on various aspects such as the role of shape-fluctuation energy, the softness parameter, band-head and average moment of inertia, the prominence of the anti-pairing effect, and the transition from pairing to anti-pairing effects in Pu isotopes. Moreover, these findings are compared with other isotones, offering a comprehensive understanding of their unique characteristics.

## Key claim

For the first time, a comprehensive analysis of rotational bands in the A~250 mass region was conducted, extracting free parameters for 36 rotational bands across neutron numbers N=148 to 152. The authors claim that a significant enhancement to the vibrational distortion model yields an exceptional match with experimental dynamic moment of inertia data, but this match is qualified as applying to "most of the rotational bands studied" rather than all.

## Method

The authors applied five rotational energy models (vibrational distortion model, shape fluctuation model, nuclear softness formula, semiclassical particle rotor model, and variable moment of inertia inspired interacting boson model) to fit experimental gamma-ray transition energies from 36 rotational bands in N=148 to 152 isotones. Free parameters were extracted via least-squares fitting for each band, and the dynamic moment of inertia was calculated and categorized into three distinct groups based on the fitted vibrational distortion parameters. The modified vibrational distortion model introduced a frequency-dependent term of the form Im^(2) = Im^(2)_c ± Im^(2)_vib[(ω_max - ω)/ω_max]^2.

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of rotational bands in the A~250 mass region, building on the particle-number conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) approach of He and collaborators (2009, 2015) which has been successful in characterizing this mass region. The study also references the vibrational distortion model introduced by Roy and collaborators, the shape fluctuation model of Sathpathy, and the nuclear softness formula of Gupta and Brentano et al. The findings enable future comparisons with self-consistent approaches such as Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov and relativistic mean-field theory predictions for superheavy element stability, and provide systematic parameter sets for testing theoretical models of nuclear rotation and pairing in the heaviest nuclei.
