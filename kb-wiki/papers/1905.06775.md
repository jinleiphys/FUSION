# The onset of $ΛΛ$ hypernuclear binding

> Binding energies of light, $A\leq 6$, $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei are calculated using the stochastic variational method in a pionless effective field theory (EFT) approach at leading order with the purpose of assessing critically the onset of binding in the strangeness S=-2 hadronic sector. The EFT input in this sector consists of (i) a $ΛΛ$ contact term constrained by the $ΛΛ$ scattering length $a_{ΛΛ}$, using a range of values compatible with $ΛΛ$ correlations observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and (ii) a $ΛΛN$ contact term constrained by the only available $A\leq 6$ $ΛΛ$ hypernucler binding energy datum of $^{6}_{ΛΛ}$He. The recently debated neutral three-body and four-body systems $^{3}_{ΛΛ}$n and $^{4}_{ΛΛ}$n are found unbound by a wide margin. A relatively large value of $|a_{ΛΛ}| \gtrsim 1.5$ fm is needed to bind $^{4}_{ΛΛ}$H, thereby questioning its particle stability. In contrast, the particle stability of the $A=5$ $ΛΛ$ hypernuclear isodoublet $^{5}_{ΛΛ} $H--$^{5}_{ΛΛ}$He is robust, with $Λ$ separation energy of order 1 MeV.

## Key claim

The onset of ΛΛ hypernuclear binding for A ≤ 6 is assessed using leading-order pionless effective field theory, finding that the neutral three-body and four-body systems ³ΛΛn and ⁴ΛΛn are unbound by a wide margin, while the particle stability of the A=5 isodoublet ⁵ΛΛH–⁵ΛΛHe is robust; however, the particle stability of ⁴ΛΛH requires a ΛΛ scattering length magnitude |aΛΛ| ≳ 1.5 fm, which the authors argue is unlikely based on most theoretical constructions.

## Method

The authors solved the A-body Schrödinger equation variationally using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis functions, employing a leading-order pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) Lagrangian. The two-body contact interactions were regularized with a Gaussian cutoff λ and fitted to low-energy scattering lengths, including a ΛΛ contact term constrained by the ΛΛ scattering length aΛΛ over a range from −0.5 fm to −1.9 fm, and a ΛΛN three-body contact term fitted to the ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV datum from the Nagara event. The ΛN interaction terms used the Alexander[B] model as the primary input, with comparisons to χLO, χNLO, and NSC97f models.

## Key numbers

- ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = BΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) − 2BΛ(⁵ΛHe) = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV, used as the constraint for the ΛΛN three-body contact term.
- The ΛΛ scattering length aΛΛ was varied over the range −0.5 fm to −1.9 fm, based on analyses of ΛΛ correlations from relativistic heavy ion collisions.
- A minimum value of |aΛΛ| ≳ 1.5 fm is required to bind ⁴ΛΛH, for cutoff λ ≳ 4 fm⁻¹ in the renormalization scale invariance limit.
- For aΛΛ = −0.8 fm and cutoff λ = 4 fm⁻¹, the Λ separation energy BΛ(⁵ΛΛH) = 1.21 MeV when constrained by ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) = 0.67 MeV.
- The extrapolated Λ separation energy for ⁵ΛΛH in the λ → ∞ limit is BΛ(∞) = 1.14 ± 0.01⁺⁰·⁴⁴₋₀·₂₆ MeV.
- The Λ momentum scale in ⁶ΛΛHe is approximately pΛ ≈ 83 MeV/c, yielding an EFT expansion parameter (Q/2mπ) ≈ 0.3 and LO accuracy of order (Q/2mπ)² ≈ 0.09.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach previously applied to single-Λ hypernuclei by Contessi et al. (2018) to the strangeness S = −2 sector, providing the first comprehensive EFT application to light ΛΛ hypernuclei. It contradicts the particle stability claimed for ⁴ΛΛn by some earlier few-body calculations (e.g., Pochodzalla 2019) and supports the J-PARC proposal P75 to produce ⁵ΛΛH. The results enable future studies of multi-Λ hypernuclei and strange hadronic matter, and call for next-to-leading-order calculations incorporating ΛΛ–ΞN–ΣΣ coupling and effective-range contributions.
