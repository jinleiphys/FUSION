# Isospin-Breaking Vacuum-to-Pi^0,Eta Pseudoscalar Matrix Elements at Next-to-Leading Order in the Chiral Expansion

> We employ Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to evaluate the complete set of pseudoscalar matrix elements, <0|P_f|pi^0,eta>, with P_f any of the flavor-diagonal pseudoscalar currents (f=u,d,s), to order (m_d-m_u), and to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. These matrix elements represent the basic input to a QCD sum rule analysis of isospin breaking in the pi NN couplings using the three-point-function method. We discuss also how one could use the results to construct a one parameter family of interpolating fields for the $π^0$, all of whose members have zero vacuum-to-$η$ matrix element, and explain how this could in principle be used to provide non-trivial tests of the reliability of the assumptions underlying the use of the three-point-function method. It is shown that the isospin-breaking mixing parameters required for this construction receive significant corrections beyond leading order in the chiral expansion.

# Isospin-Breaking Vacuum-to-π⁰,η Pseudoscalar Matrix Elements at Next-to-Leading Order in the Chiral Expansion

## Key claim

The complete set of vacuum-to-π⁰,η matrix elements of flavor-diagonal pseudoscalar currents (P_u, P_d, P_s) is evaluated to order (m_d − m_u) and to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, revealing that the isospin-breaking mixing parameters required to construct "pure" π⁰ interpolating fields receive significant corrections beyond leading order, approximately 21–30% enhancements depending on the specific choice of interpolating field parameter α.

## Method

The authors employ Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) with the effective Lagrangian up to fourth order in the chiral counting, including the Gasser–Leutwyler low-energy constants L₁–L₈ and H₂. They compute the pseudoscalar current matrix elements by expanding the currents in terms of renormalized physical π⁰ and η fields using 1-loop wavefunction renormalization and mixing angles, then evaluate the contributing graphs to next-to-leading order. The results are expressed in terms of the physical pion decay constant f_π, pion mass m_π, and the average light quark mass ̂m, with the leading-order π⁰–η mixing angle θ₀ = (√3/4)(m_d − m_u)/(m_s − ̂m) serving as the isospin-breaking expansion parameter.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order π⁰–η mixing angle is θ₀ = (√3/4)(m_d − m_u)/(m_s − ̂m), where ̂m = (m_u + m_d)/2.
- The vacuum-to-π⁰ matrix element of the I=1 current is <0|P_u − P_d|π⁰> = m_π² f_π / ̂m, exact to O(m_d − m_u) at next-to-leading order.
- The mixing parameter ε(α) for the family of π⁰ interpolating fields is given by ε(α) = −√3 θ₀ [1.299 + 0.0894 α] at next-to-leading order, compared to the leading-order value of −√3 θ₀.
- For the MH choice (α = −1/3), the next-to-leading order enhancement factor is 1.27; for the choice with no P_s content (α = −1), the enhancement factor is 1.21.
- The choice α = −4/3 removes all LEC contributions from the expression for ε(α).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of Meissner and Henley (1995), who first attempted a QCD sum rule treatment of isospin-breaking πNN couplings using the three-point-function method but relied on a leading-order π⁰ interpolating field. The results provide the essential input for constructing a continuous family of π⁰ interpolating fields with zero vacuum-to-η matrix element, enabling future tests of the reliability of the three-point-function method, particularly regarding the assumption of pion pole dominance at Q² ∼ −1 GeV² and the potential contamination from higher resonances such as the η′. The calculations build on the ChPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) and the 1-loop mixing angle formalism of Maltman (1993, 1995).
