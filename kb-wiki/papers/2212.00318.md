# ASTROPHYSICAL S(0)-FACTORS FOR THE $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$, $^{3}{\rm H}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ and $^{7}{\rm Be}(p, γ)^{8}{\rm B}$ DIRECT CAPTURE PROCESSES IN A POTENTIAL MODEL

> Astrophysical S-factors at zero energy for the direct nuclear capture reactions $^{3}{\rm He}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$, $^{3}{\rm H}(α, γ)^{7}{\rm Li}$ and $^{7}{\rm Be}(p, γ)^{8}{\rm B}$ are estimated within the framework of two-body potential cluster model on the basis of extranuclear capture approximation of D. Baye and E. Brainis. The values of S(0)-factors have been calculated using two different potential models for each process, which were adjusted to the binding energies and empirical values of the asymptotical normalization coefficients from the literature. New values of S(0)-factors have been obtained.

# Astrophysical S(0)-Factors for Direct Capture Processes in a Potential Model

## Key claim
The authors obtain new theoretical estimates of the zero-energy astrophysical S-factors for three direct capture reactions using a two-body potential cluster model with the extranuclear capture approximation, but note that the method is only methodical for the $d(\alpha,\gamma)^6$Li reaction because its isospin-forbidden E1 transition requires a three-body model for realistic estimates.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation with two-body central Gaussian nuclear potentials and point-like Coulomb potentials for the entrance and exit channels. They use the extranuclear capture approximation of Baye and Brainis (1998, 2020) to compute the zero-energy overlap integrals, employing rescaled Coulomb functions that remain finite at zero energy. For each reaction, two different potential models are adjusted to reproduce the binding energies and empirical asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from the literature.

## Key numbers
- $^{3}{\rm He}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$: S(0) = 0.56 keV b (model V$_{\rm D}^{\rm n}$) and 0.58 keV b (model V$_{\rm M1}^{\rm n}$), consistent with Solar Fusion II value of 0.56 $\pm$ 0.04 keV b
- $^{3}{\rm H}(\alpha, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Li}$: S(0) = 0.10 keV b (model V$_{\rm D}^{\rm n}$) and 0.09 keV b (model V$_{\rm M1}^{\rm n}$), consistent with NACRE value of 0.10 $\pm$ 0.02 keV b
- $^{7}{\rm Be}(p, \gamma)^{8}{\rm B}$: S(0) = 18.32 eV b (model V$_{\rm D}$) and 19.61 eV b (model V$_{\rm M}$), consistent with Solar Fusion II value of 20.8 $\pm$ 2.10 eV b
- ANC for $^{3}{\rm He}+\alpha \to ^{7}{\rm Be}$ ground state: 4.34 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm D}^{\rm n}$) and 4.79 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm M1}^{\rm n}$)
- ANC for $^{3}{\rm H}+\alpha \to ^{7}{\rm Li}$ ground state: 3.72 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm D}^{\rm n}$) and 4.10 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm M1}^{\rm n}$)
- ANC for $p+^{7}{\rm Be} \to ^{8}{\rm B}$: 0.70 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm D}$) and 0.73 fm$^{-1/2}$ (V$_{\rm M}$)

## Context
This work extends the theoretical model previously developed by Turakulov and Tursunov (2015, 2018, 2020, 2021) for determining zero-energy astrophysical S-factors using the extranuclear capture approximation. The results are compared with data from the Solar Fusion II Collaboration (Adelberger et al., 2011) and the NACRE Collaboration (1999). The method can be extended to the $d(\alpha,\gamma)^6$Li reaction, but the authors note that realistic estimates of its isospin-forbidden E1 S-factor require a three-body model as shown by Belyaeva and Tursunov (2018). The calculated S(0) values are relevant for Standard Solar Model neutrino flux predictions and Big Bang nucleosynthesis models, particularly the cosmological lithium problem.
