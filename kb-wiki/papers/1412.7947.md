# Theoretical investigation of the decay of the $N(2120)$ resonance to nucleon resonances near 1.7 GeV

> Background: Until now the knowledge about nucleon resonances with a mass higher than 2 GeV has been scarce. Huge amounts of experimental data of the multipion photoproduction have been accumulated, and more can be expected in the future in facilities such as JLab 12 GeV. It makes it possible to investigate the decay of a nucleon resonance into another nucleon resonance. Purpose: The possibility to research the decay of the $N(2120)$ to nucleon resonances near 1.7 GeV in the three-pion photoproduction will be explored to provide useful information for future experimental study. Method: The pion and radiative decay amplitudes of nucleon resonances are studied within the constituent quark model, which is used to calculate the couplings constants, especially for the decay of a nucleon resonance near 2.1 GeV to another nucleon resonance near 1.7 GeV. The three-pion photoproduction off the neutron target, i.e.,$γn \toπ^{-}π^{-}Δ^{++}\to π^{-}π^{-}π^+ p$, is investigated based on the effective Lagrangian method with the coupling constant obtained form the decay amplitudes. Results: The resonance contribution with a state $N(^2P_M)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ near 2.1 GeV decaying to a state $N(^4P_M)\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ near 1.7 GeV, i.\ e., $N(2120)\to N(1675)π$ is dominant in the process considered. The total cross section from the resonance contribution is at the order of 1 $μ$b and can be easily distinguished from the background. Conclusions: Our results suggest it is practicable to research the decay of the $N(2120)$ to the $N(1675)$ in experiment.

# Theoretical investigation of the decay of the $N(2120)$ resonance to nucleon resonances near 1.7 GeV

## Key claim

The decay of the $N(2120)$ resonance (specifically the $N(^2P_M)3/2^-$ state near 2.1 GeV) to the $N(1675)$ resonance (the $N(^4P_M)5/2^-$ state near 1.7 GeV) via pion emission is predicted to dominate the three-pion photoproduction process $\gamma n \to \pi^-\pi^-\Delta^{++}$, with a total cross section at the order of 1 $\mu$b that can be distinguished from background, suggesting this decay channel is experimentally accessible at facilities such as JLab. The claim is conditional on the constituent quark model calculations and the specific choice of the $N(^2P_M)3/2^-$ state as the $N(2120)$.

## Method

The authors used the constituent quark model under SU(6) $\otimes$ O(3) symmetry to calculate helicity amplitudes for radiative and strong decays of nucleon resonances, from which coupling constants were extracted. These coupling constants were then employed in an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the cross section for the reaction $\gamma n \to \pi^-\pi^-\Delta^{++}$, including both resonance contributions (cascade decays of a high-mass nucleon resonance to a lower nucleon resonance and then to $\Delta^{++}$) and background contributions from $t$-channel pion exchange and contact terms. The cross sections were calculated using the CERNLIB code FOWL, with form factors and cut-off parameters varied to assess uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section from the dominant resonance contribution ($N(2120) \to N(1675)\pi$) is at the order of 1 $\mu$b for the process $\gamma n \to \pi^-\pi^-\Delta^{++}$.
* The decay width for $N(^2P_M)3/2^- \to N(^4P_M)5/2^-\pi^-$ (i.e., $N(2120) \to N(1675)\pi$) is 220.18 MeV, the largest among all considered cascade channels.
* The radiative decay width of the $N(^2P_M)3/2^-$ state (candidate for $N(2120)$) to $n\gamma$ is 0.180 MeV, with helicity amplitudes $A_{3/2}^n = -i0.057$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $A_{1/2}^n = i0.015$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
* The strong decay width of the $N(^4P_M)5/2^-$ state (candidate for $N(1675)$) to $\pi^-\Delta^{++}$ is 43.4 MeV, with helicity amplitudes $A^\pi_{3/2} = -i1.182$ and $A^\pi_{1/2} = -i0.847$.
* The coupling constants for the dominant channel $N(^2P_M)3/2^- \to N(^4P_M)5/2^-\pi^-$ are $g_1 = 0.130$ and $g_2 = 6.929$.

## Context

This work extends the study of high-mass nucleon resonances (above 2 GeV), which are poorly known compared to lower-mass states, by proposing a specific decay channel that could be observed in multipion photoproduction experiments. It builds on the constituent quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1990s) for the third $N3/2^-$ state near 2.1 GeV, and on previous studies of $\Lambda(1520)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ photoproduction by He et al. (2012-2014) that suggested the importance of this resonance. The results enable future experimental searches at CLAS12 and GlueX at JLab, where the predicted cross section of about 1 $\mu$b is large enough to be measured, and the small background contribution makes the $N(2120)$ signal distinguishable.
