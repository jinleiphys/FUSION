# Interpretation of normal-deformed bands in 167Lu

> With the aim to get a general understanding of rotational bands in the deformed rare-earth region or in deformed nuclei in general, the observed normal-deformed rotational structures in $^{167}$Lu are interpreted within the unpaired and paired cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky formalisms, CNS and CNSB. Particular attention is devoted to the band crossings. For this nucleus with the Fermi surface high up in the $h_{11/2}$ shell, we conclude that except for the paired AB and BC crossings in configurations with an even and odd number of $i_{13/2}$ neutrons, respectively, the observed band crossings can be understood within the unpaired formalism. Especially, it means that above the AB and BC crossings, the evolution with spin is described as a gradual alignment of the spin vectors of the particles outside closed shells. Consequently, the configurations can be characterized by the number of particles occupying open $j$-shells or groups of $j$-shells. In the present study, we revise the interpretation of some experimental bands and also the nature of the crossings while some previous configuration assignments are confirmed.

# Interpretation of normal-deformed bands in 167Lu

## Key claim

The observed band crossings in 167Lu can be understood within the unpaired cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky formalism, except for the paired AB and BC crossings in configurations with an even and odd number of i13/2 neutrons, respectively; above these crossings, the evolution with spin is described as a gradual alignment of spin vectors rather than as discrete alignment steps.

## Method

The authors applied the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) formalism without pairing and the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky-Bogoliubov (CNSB) formalism with monopole pairing to interpret the normal-deformed rotational bands in 167Lu. Calculations were carried out in a quadrupole and hexadecapole deformation mesh (ε2, γ, ε4) using the StA150 Nilsson model parameters, which are an arithmetic average of the standard and A=150 parameter sets. Two-band-mixing calculations were performed for interacting bands to extract interaction matrix elements and construct smooth unperturbed crossing bands.

## Key numbers

- Interaction matrix element of 38 keV (30 keV) for the crossing between bands 1 and 10 for signature α=−1/2 (α=1/2), with crossing at I≈29 for both signatures
- Interaction matrix element of 24 keV for the crossing between band 9a and the TSD1 band (band 11) in the spin range I=26.5–36.5
- All observed states in the spin range I=23.5–33.5 (I=24.5–34.5) for α=−1/2 (α=1/2) bands reproduced within ±7 keV (±5 keV) in the two-band-mixing fit
- The [402]5/2 band lies approximately 200 keV excited in energy relative to the [404]7/2 band

## Context

This work extends the CNS and CNSB formalisms, previously applied to terminating bands in lighter nuclei such as 161Lu (Ma 2014) and to superdeformed bands in the 152Dy region (Ragnarsson 1993), to a nucleus in the middle of the deformed rare-earth region where no terminating bands are expected. The study revises some configuration assignments from the experimental work of Roux (2015) on 167Lu, particularly for the positive-parity bands based on N=4 proton orbitals, and provides a framework for understanding rotational bands in deformed rare-earth nuclei generally. The results enable future experiments to test the predicted configuration assignments and band-crossing patterns.
