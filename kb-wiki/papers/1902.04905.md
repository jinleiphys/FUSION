# Dynamically assisted nuclear fusion

> We consider the prototypical deuterium-tritium fusion reaction. At intermediate initial kinetic energies (in the keV regime), a major bottle-neck of this reaction is the Coulomb barrier between the nuclei, which is overcome by tunneling. Here, we study whether the tunneling probability can be enhanced by an additional electromagnetic field, such as an x-ray free electron laser (XFEL). We find that this dynamical assistance should be feasible with present-day or near future technology.

## Key claim

The tunneling probability for deuterium-tritium fusion can be enhanced by an additional electromagnetic field such as an XFEL, but the effect depends strongly on the temporal profile of the field and requires field strengths of order 10^13 V/m or higher for keV-scale frequencies; the authors find this "should be feasible with present-day or near future technology" but note that perturbative treatments break down at the required field strengths.

## Method

The authors model the D-T fusion reaction as non-relativistic point particles with a Lagrangian including the Coulomb repulsion, nuclear attraction, and an external vector potential representing the XFEL field. They apply a Floquet ansatz to treat the oscillating field perturbatively, computing the first-order side-band wave functions to obtain the enhanced tunneling exponent. They also develop a Büttiker-Landauer approach using a WKB eikonal (Hamilton-Jacobi) equation to go beyond lowest-order perturbation theory, analyzing the imaginary part of the action to determine the enhancement for different pulse shapes (sinusoidal, Gaussian, Sauter).

## Key numbers

- For initial energy ℰ = 1 keV, the classical turning point r_ℰ is around 1.4 pm, and the Coulomb field strength at that point is around 7×10^14 V/m.
- The WKB tunneling probability without assistance is P ∼ 10^{-15} for ℰ = 1 keV and P ∼ 10^{-5} for ℰ = 10 keV.
- For a constant electric field of 35×10^14 V/m, the exponent factor π is replaced by 8/3 ≈ 2.7, changing P from ∼10^{-15} to ∼10^{-13}.
- With ℰ = 1 keV and an XFEL frequency ℏω = 10 keV, the tunneling exponent from the Floquet approach is enhanced by ten orders of magnitude.
- For ℰ = ω = 1 keV, the Büttiker-Landauer approach requires field strengths of order 10^13 V/m for dynamical assistance to set in.
- For ω = 2 keV, the required field strength drops to 10^10 V/m.
- The reduced mass μ ≈ 1.12 GeV; the effective charge q_eff ≈ q/5.
- The maximum imaginary turning time for ℰ = 1 keV gives ℰ|τ|/ℏ ≈ 8.6.

## Context

This work extends the concept of dynamically assisted tunneling from the Sauter-Schwinger effect (electron-positron pair creation) to nuclear fusion, drawing on the Franz-Keldysh effect (Franz 1958, Keldysh 1958) and the Büttiker-Landauer traversal time (Büttiker 1982). The authors explicitly compare their results to the dynamically assisted Schwinger effect studied by Linder (2014) and others, noting the same qualitative dependence on pulse shape. The paper enables experimental tests at XFEL facilities (citing Mimura 2018) and suggests that the mechanism should also work for other fusion reactions such as deuterium-deuterium fusion, though with additional spatial-dependence complications.
