# Studying $Λ^*$ resonances in the $p \bar p \rightarrow Λ\barΛη$ reaction

> In this work, we make a theoretical study on the $p \bar p \rightarrow Λ\barΛη$ reaction for antiproton beam energy from threshold to 4GeV within an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model. By assuming this reaction is dominated by the excitation of $Λ$ and $\bar Λ$ resonances in intermediate states, we calculate the total cross sections and give the predictions of the angular distribution and invariant mass spectrum of final particles. In particular, we discuss the possibility to verify the existence of a narrow $Λ$ resonance found in the process of $K^- p\to ηΛ$ in the present reaction. It shows that the $p \bar p \rightarrow \bar ΛΛη$ reaction can provide us with valuable information about the $Λ$ resonances having significant couplings to $\bar K N$ and $Λη$ channels. Thus the experimental data of this reaction will be a good supplement to the $\bar K N\toηΛ$ scattering data for studying $Λ$ resonances.

## Key claim

The $p \bar p \rightarrow \Lambda \bar\Lambda \eta$ reaction, for antiproton beam energies from threshold to 4 GeV, can serve as a sensitive probe for a narrow $\Lambda$ resonance (mass near 1668.5 MeV, width near 1.5 MeV) previously suggested in $K^- p \to \eta \Lambda$ data, because the large threshold momentum of the $p \bar p$ reaction enhances the contribution of such a resonance relative to the dominant $\Lambda(1670)$.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model, assuming the reaction proceeds via $K$ and $K^*$ meson exchange between the initial proton and antiproton, exciting intermediate $\Lambda$ and $\bar\Lambda$ resonances that decay to $\Lambda\eta$ or $\bar\Lambda\eta$. They considered three scenarios: (I) including the known $\Lambda(1670)$ plus a narrow $D_{03}$ resonance ($\Lambda^*_D$); (II) including $\Lambda(1670)$ plus a narrow $P_{03}$ resonance ($\Lambda^*_P$); and (III) including only $\Lambda(1670)$. Model parameters (coupling constants, masses, widths, relative phases) were determined by fitting the total and differential cross sections of the $K^- p \to \eta \Lambda$ reaction. Initial-state interactions were treated phenomenologically using parameters from prior work on $p\bar p \to \bar\Lambda_c^- \Lambda_c^+$.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section of $p \bar p \rightarrow \Lambda \bar\Lambda \eta$ is predicted to be roughly at the order of $0.1$ to $10$ nb for antiproton laboratory momenta $P_{lab} = 3.1$ to $4$ GeV.
* In Scenario I, the narrow $\Lambda^*_D$ resonance has mass $1668.5 \pm 0.5$ MeV and width $1.5 \pm 0.5$ MeV; the product of its coupling constants is $f_{\Lambda_D^*\bar KN} f_{\Lambda_D^*\Lambda\eta} = 28.2 \pm 2.4$.
* In Scenario II, the narrow $\Lambda^*_P$ resonance has mass $1663.6 \pm 0.5$ MeV and width $11.0 \pm 1.4$ MeV; the product of its coupling constants is $f_{\Lambda_P^*\bar KN} f_{\Lambda_P^*\Lambda\eta} = 2.98 \pm 0.08$.
* The $\Lambda(1670)$ parameters in Scenario I are mass $1672.5 \pm 1.0$ MeV, width $24.5 \pm 2.7$ MeV, and product $g_{\Lambda(1670)\Lambda\eta} g_{\Lambda(1670) {\bar K}N} = 0.30 \pm 0.03$.
* The $\Lambda(1670)$ parameters in Scenario II are mass $1672.2 \pm 0.8$ MeV, width $27.6 \pm 1.0$ MeV, and product $g_{\Lambda(1670)\Lambda\eta} g_{\Lambda(1670) {\bar K}N} = 0.32 \pm 0.03$.
* The $\Lambda(1670)$ parameters in Scenario III (no narrow resonance) are mass $1671.5 \pm 0.2$ MeV, width $23.2 \pm 0.2$ MeV, and product $g_{\Lambda(1670)\Lambda\eta} g_{\Lambda(1670) {\bar K}N} = 0.28 \pm 0.02$.
* The threshold momentum for the $p \bar p$ reaction is a factor of 4 larger than that for the $K^- p \to \eta \Lambda$ reaction, enhancing the D-wave $\Lambda^*_D$ contribution.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $\Lambda$ resonances from the $K^- p \to \eta \Lambda$ reaction (studied by Liu et al. (2015) and by Kamano et al. (2015)) to the $p \bar p$ production channel, motivated by the upcoming $\bar{\text{P}}$ANDA experiment at FAIR. It builds on theoretical studies of baryon spectroscopy in $N \bar N$ collisions by He, Haidenbauer, Shyam, and others, which focused on charmed hadron production. The calculations predict that the $p \bar p \rightarrow \Lambda \bar\Lambda \eta$ reaction can distinguish between the $D_{03}$ and $P_{03}$ assignments for the proposed narrow resonance through the shape of the $M_{\Lambda\eta}$ invariant mass spectrum and the Dalitz plot, enabling future experimental verification.
