# Microscopic nature of the photon strength function: stable and unstable Ni and Sn isotopes

> The pygmy-dipole resonances and photon strength functions in stable and unstable Ni and Sn isotopes are calculated within the microscopic self-consistent version of the extended theory of finite fermi systems which includes the QRPA and phonon coupling effects and uses the known Skyrme forces SLy4. The pygmy dipole resonance in $^{72}Ni$ is predicted with the mean energy of 12.4 MeV and the energy-weighted sum rule exhausting 25.6\% of the total strength. The microscopically obtained photon E1 strength functions are used to calculate nuclear reaction properties, i.e the radiative neutron capture cross section, gamma-ray spectra, and average radiative widths. Our main conclusion is that in all these quantities it is necessary to take the phonon coupling effects into account.

## Key claim

The paper claims that phonon coupling (PC) effects must be included in microscopic self-consistent calculations of photon strength functions and pygmy dipole resonances to achieve agreement with experiment, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the calculations use only the SLy4 Skyrme force and a spherical approximation, and the agreement is demonstrated for only a limited set of stable and unstable Ni and Sn isotopes.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent extended theory of finite Fermi systems (ETFFS) in the quasiparticle time blocking approximation (QTBA), which combines the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with phonon coupling effects, employing the SLy4 Skyrme interaction. Ground states were calculated with the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) method using the spherical code HFBRAD, and the residual interaction was derived as the second derivative of the Skyrme functional. The resulting E1 photon strength functions were then used as input to calculate radiative neutron capture cross sections, gamma-ray spectra, and average radiative widths for various Ni and Sn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The pygmy dipole resonance in $^{72}$Ni is predicted at a mean energy of 12.4 MeV, exhausting 25.7% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in the QTBA calculation.
- For $^{68}$Ni, the QTBA calculation gives a PDR mean energy of 10.8 MeV exhausting 8.7% of the EWSR in the (7-13) MeV interval, compared to the experimental value of about 5% of the EWSR.
- For $^{58}$Ni, the QTBA calculation gives a PDR mean energy of 14.0 MeV exhausting 11.7% of the EWSR in the (8-14) MeV interval.
- The quantity $2\pi\Gamma_{\gamma}/D_0$ for $^{120}$Sn is calculated as $7.10\times10^{-3}$ with QTBA, compared to the experimental value of $6.98\times10^{-3}$.
- For $^{62}$Ni, the QTBA calculation gives $2\pi\Gamma_{\gamma}/D_0 = 4.33\times10^{-3}$, compared to the experimental value of $5.98\times10^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic self-consistent approach to photon strength functions beyond the HFB+QRPA method previously included in the Reference Input Parameters Libraries (RIPL2 and RIPL3) and used in reaction codes like EMPIRE and TALYS. The authors show that the HFB+QRPA approach, while necessary, is insufficient and must be complemented by phonon coupling effects, as suggested by experiments on $^{68}$Ni by Wieland (2009) and Rossi (2013), and on Sn isotopes by Utsunomiya (2011) and Toft (2011). The results enable improved predictions for nuclear reaction properties in exotic nuclei relevant to nuclear astrophysics, where phenomenological Lorentzian-based models lack predictive power.
