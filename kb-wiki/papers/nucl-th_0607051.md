# Recent developments in chiral dynamics of hadrons and hadrons in a nuclear medium

> In this talk I present recent developments in chiral dynamics of hadrons and hadrons in a medium addressing the following points: interaction of the octet of pseudoscalar mesons with the octet of baryons of the nucleon, showing recent experimental evidence on the existence of two $Λ(1405)$ states, the interaction of the octet of pseudoscalar mesons with the decuplet of baryons of the $Δ$, with particular emphasis on the $Λ(1520)$ resonance, dynamically generated by this interaction. Then I review the interaction of kaons in a nuclear medium and briefly discuss the situation around the claims of deeply bound states in nuclei. The large renormalization of the $Λ(1520)$ in the nuclear medium is shown as another example of successful application of the chiral unitary techniques.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach predicts two distinct poles for the Λ(1405) resonance, one from a singlet and one from an octet branch, which couple differently to \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) channels and therefore produce different observable shapes depending on the reaction mechanism, rather than a single resonance state.

## Method

The authors apply the N/D method to unitarize coupled-channel scattering amplitudes derived from chiral Lagrangians for meson-baryon interactions, factorizing the kernel on-shell to reduce the Bethe-Salpeter equations to algebraic equations. They extrapolate the T-matrix to the complex second Riemann sheet to search for poles, and study the trajectories of these poles as SU(3) symmetry is gradually broken from a symmetric situation to the real world. For the Λ(1520), they include the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) decay channels and fine-tune subtraction constants to match the resonance position and width, then compute cross sections for \(K^-p \to \pi^0\pi^0\Lambda\) and \(K^-p \to \pi^+\pi^-\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- Two poles exist in the region of the Λ(1405): one from the original singlet and one from one branch of the original octet, with the narrower pole coupling strongly to \(\bar{K}N\) and the wider one to \(\pi\Sigma\).
- The \(\bar{K}\) optical potential at normal nuclear matter has a strength of about 50 MeV attraction and an imaginary part of about 50 MeV, leading to deeply bound states with about 30 MeV binding and widths of around 100 MeV.
- The \(\Lambda(1520)\) free width is 14 MeV; in the nuclear medium the width from the \(\pi\Sigma(1385)\) channel alone already exceeds this free width.
- For \(K^-p \to \pi^0\pi^0\Lambda\), the mechanism based on the strong coupling of the \(\Lambda(1520)\) to the \(\pi\Sigma(1385)\) channel is largely dominant up to 575 MeV/c of kaon momentum.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Oller and collaborators (2000) for meson-baryon systems and by Jido et al. (2003) for the two-pole structure of the Λ(1405). It provides experimental support from the \(K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0\) experiment by Prakhov et al. and from the \(K^-p \to \pi^+\pi^-\Lambda\) data by Mast et al. (1973). The results contradict claims by Akaishi et al. (2005) of deeply bound kaonic states with binding energies of about 200 MeV, showing instead that peaks attributed to such states arise from conventional \(K^-\) absorption by nucleon pairs followed by rescattering, as confirmed by the FINUDA collaboration (2006). The work enables further studies of hadron renormalization in nuclear medium, particularly the large medium modification of the \(\Lambda(1520)\) width.
