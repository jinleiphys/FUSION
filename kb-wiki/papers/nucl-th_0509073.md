# Potential energy surfaces for cluster emitting nuclei

> Potential energy surfaces are calculated by using the most advanced asymmetric two-center shell model allowing to obtain shell and pairing corrections which are added to the Yukawa-plus-exponential model deformation energy. Shell effects are of crucial importance for experimental observation of spontaneous disintegration by heavy ion emission. Results for 222Ra, 232U, 236Pu and 242Cm illustrate the main ideas and show for the first time for a cluster emitter a potential barrier obtained by using the macroscopic-microscopic method.

## Key claim

The macroscopic-microscopic method, combining Yukawa-plus-exponential model deformation energy with shell and pairing corrections from the asymmetric two-center shell model, yields potential energy surfaces for cluster-emitting nuclei that show for the first time a potential barrier for a cluster emitter, providing a qualitative justification for the barrier shape assumed in the analytical superasymmetric fission (ASAF) model, though the deepest valley on the total deformation energy is the cold fission valley (due to doubly magic 132Sn) rather than the cluster-decay valley (due to 208Pb), because the latter lies on the Businaro-Gallone mountain of the macroscopic energy.

## Method

The macroscopic deformation energy was calculated using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model (Y+EM) with parameters from Moller et al., while shell corrections were obtained via Strutinsky's method and pairing corrections via the BCS formalism, both using single-particle levels from the asymmetric two-center shell model. The total deformation energy was computed as the sum of the Y+EM energy and the shell-plus-pairing corrections for a grid of separation distances R and mass asymmetries eta, with the shape parametrized by two intersecting spheres. Potential energy surfaces were generated for four nuclei (222Ra, 232U, 236Pu, 242Cm) by cutting the PES at fixed asymmetry to obtain one-dimensional potential barriers for cluster emission.

## Key numbers

- 222Ra: three valleys on the shell-plus-pairing corrections PES at eta ~ 0.8, 0.3, and 0.1; the 14C radioactivity valley corresponds to eta_A = 0.874 and eta_Z = 0.864, with daughter 208Pb (magic N=126, Z=82) and light fragment 14C (magic N=8).
- 232U: three valleys on the shell-plus-pairing corrections PES at eta ~ 0.8, 0.3, and 0.15; the 24Ne radioactivity valley corresponds to eta_A = 0.793 and eta_Z = 0.783, with daughter 208Pb; the cold fission valley (132Sn + 100Mo) is at eta_A = 0.138 and eta_Z = 0.087.
- 236Pu: three valleys on the shell-plus-pairing corrections PES at eta ~ 0.75, 0.28, and <0.1; the 28Mg radioactivity valley corresponds to eta_A = 0.763 and eta_Z = 0.745, with daughter 208Pb; the cold fission valley (132Sn + 104Ru) is at eta_A = 0.119 and eta_Z = 0.064.
- 242Cm: four valleys on the shell-plus-pairing corrections PES at eta ~ 0.7, 0.4, 0.3, and 0.1; the 34Si radioactivity valley corresponds to eta_A = 0.719 and eta_Z = 0.708, with daughter 208Pb and light fragment 34Si (magic N=20); the cold fission valley (132Sn + 110Pd) is at eta_A = 0.091 and eta_Z = 0.042.

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of cluster radioactivity by Poenaru et al. (2002) using the analytical superasymmetric fission (ASAF) model, by providing microscopic potential energy surfaces that confirm the importance of shell effects from doubly magic fragments (208Pb and 132Sn) for cluster emission and cold fission. The results support the barrier shape assumed in the ASAF model, which was previously compared to the Myers-Swiatecki liquid-drop model barrier (Myers and Swiatecki, 1966), and enable future studies of cluster decay half-lives and the identification of new candidates for experimental observation. The approach also connects to the search for superheavy elements motivated by Walter Greiner in the 1960s, as the same cold valleys on the PES are relevant for both superheavy nuclei production and cluster radioactivity.
