# Spin asymmetry for proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with tensor-polarized deuteron

> Tensor structure of the deuteron can be studied by deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan process to understand it in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom. It probes interesting features in the deuteron including a D-wave contribution. In the charged-lepton DIS, twist-two structure functions $b_1$ and $b_2$ are expressed by tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs). We note that the HERMES experimental measurement of $b_1$ seems to be much different from a conventional theoretical prediction. This puzzling situation should be significantly improved in the near future by an approved experiment to measure $b_1$ at JLab. On the other hand, the tensor-polarized PDFs, especially antiquark distributions, could be measured by spin asymmetries in the Drell-Yan process with a tensor-polarized deuteron at Fermilab. In this work, we estimate tensor-polarization asymmetries for the Fermilab Drell-Yan experiment by using a parametrization for the tensor-polarized PDFs to explain the HERMES $b_1$ data. Obtained spin asymmetries are typically a few percent order and they could be measured by the Fermilab-E1039 experiment. Since the tensor-polarized antiquark distributions will play an important role to solve the puzzle, further theoretical and experimental efforts are needed toward the Drell-Yan experiment at Fermilab and other hadron facilities.

## Key claim

The tensor-polarized spin asymmetries for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process are estimated to be of order a few percent, and these asymmetries could be measured by the Fermilab-E1039 experiment; however, the reliability of the estimate depends on using a parametrization (set 2) that includes finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions to explain the HERMES \(b_1\) data, whereas a parametrization without such antiquark distributions (set 1) gives different results at small \(x_2\).

## Method

The authors calculate the spin asymmetry \(A_Q\) (defined as twice the asymmetry \(A_{UQ_0}\)) for the Drell-Yan process with an unpolarized 120 GeV proton beam and a tensor-polarized deuteron target, using the parton model expression that relates the asymmetry to tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs). Unpolarized PDFs are taken from the MSTW code at leading order, and tensor-polarized PDFs are taken from a parameterization (Kumano, 2010) based on HERMES data, with two sets: set 1 has no tensor-polarized antiquark distributions at the initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), while set 2 includes them. The distributions are evolved to the typical Drell-Yan scale \(Q^2 = 30\) GeV\(^2\) using DGLAP evolution equations, and error bands are estimated using the Hessian method with \(\Delta \chi^2 = 1\).

## Key numbers

- The HERMES measurement of the integrated \(b_1\) structure function over \(x\) from 0.002 to 0.85 gives \(\int dx\, b_1(x) = [1.05 \pm 0.34 \pm 0.35] \times 10^{-2}\) (in the measured kinematic range) and \([0.35 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.18] \times 10^{-2}\) (with \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\)).
- The initial scale for tensor-polarized PDFs is \(Q_0^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), and the typical scale for the Fermilab Drell-Yan experiment is \(Q^2 = 30\) GeV\(^2\).
- The spin asymmetries \(A_Q(x_1, x_2)\) are a few percent for both set 1 and set 2 at typical momentum fractions \(x_1 = 0.2\), \(x_1 = 0.4\), and \(x_1 = 0.6\); the differences between the two sets are large at very small \(x_2\).

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of the deuteron tensor structure, which began with the HERMES measurement of \(b_1\) (Airapetian, 2005) that showed a much larger value than the conventional convolution-model prediction (Hoodbhoy, 1988; Jaffe, 1988; Khan, 1991). The authors provide the first theoretical estimate of tensor-polarized spin asymmetries for the Fermilab-E1039 Drell-Yan experiment, which is needed to design the experiment and to compare with future data. The results enable direct measurement of tensor-polarized antiquark distributions, which could resolve the puzzle of the HERMES \(b_1\) data, and the approach can be applied at other hadron facilities such as BNL-RHIC, CERN-COMPASS, J-PARC, GSI-FAIR, and IHEP in Russia.
