# The pion-nucleon sigma term from Lattice QCD

> We summarize recent evidence, both from lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory, that suggests that larger-than-expected excited-state contamination could be the reason for the tension between phenomenological determinations and previous direct lattice-QCD calculations of the pion--nucleon sigma term $σ_{πN}$. In addition, we extend the $χ$PT analysis by calculating the corrections due to including the $Δ(1232)$ resonance as an explicit degree of freedom. This correction is found to be small, thereby corroborating the excited-state effects found in the $Δ$-less calculation and the result for $σ_{πN}$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the persistent tension between phenomenological determinations of the pion-nucleon sigma term (around 60 MeV) and direct lattice-QCD calculations (around 40 MeV) can be resolved by larger-than-expected excited-state contamination from multihadron states, and that including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in chiral perturbation theory produces only small corrections, corroborating the Delta-less analysis.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using Wilson-clover fermions on six 2+1+1-flavor ensembles generated with the highly improved staggered quark action by the MILC collaboration, at pion masses of approximately 315, 230, and 138 MeV. They constructed Euclidean correlation functions and performed simultaneous fits to nucleon two-point and three-point functions using spectral decomposition, keeping four states in the two-point function and three states in the three-point function. They extended a previous chiral perturbation theory analysis to next-to-next-to-leading order including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom, calculating the corrections to the scalar charge from diagrams involving the Delta.

## Key numbers

- The standard fit to lattice data gives a pion-nucleon sigma term around 40 MeV, while imposing multihadron excited-state contamination gives approximately 60 MeV, removing the tension with the phenomenological value.
- The next-to-leading-order and next-to-next-to-leading-order chiral perturbation theory contributions each generate up to -10 MeV of excited-state contamination at source-sink separations t = tau/2 around 0.5-0.7 fm.
- The combined effect of the chiral perturbation theory corrections reduces the approximately 60 MeV value on the physical mass ensemble to approximately 40 MeV.
- The Delta(1232) resonance corrections to the chiral perturbation theory analysis are found to be small, much smaller than the shift between next-to-leading-order and next-to-next-to-leading-order results.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Gupta (2021) by including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in chiral perturbation theory, assessing the stability of the chiral expansion. The paper addresses the tension between phenomenological determinations from pion-nucleon scattering data (Gasser 1991, Pavan 2002, Alarcon 2011, Hoferichter 2015, Ruiz de Elvira 2017) and direct lattice QCD calculations (JLQCD 2018, chiQCD 2015, BMW 2015, ETM 2019, BMW 2020). The findings enable a resolution of this tension by demonstrating that excited-state contamination from multihadron states, previously neglected in direct lattice calculations, can reconcile the two approaches.
