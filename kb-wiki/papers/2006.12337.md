# Light nuclei production in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7-80 GeV from UrQMD model

> Light nuclei production in relativistic $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions from 7.7 to 80 GeV is investigated within the Ultra-relativistic-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics model (UrQMD) with a naive coalescence approach. The results of the production of light nuclei at midrapidity can essentially match up the experimental data and a slight enhancement of combined ratio of ${N_{p}N_{t}}/{N_{d}^{2}}$ where $N_p, N_d$ and $N_t$ represent respectively the yields of proton, deuteron and triton, which is sensitive to the neutron density fluctuations, occurs around 20 GeV. However, this enhanced ${N_{p}N_{t}}/{N_{d}^{2}}$ ratio should not be over-understood considering that the present UrQMD model is a cascade version without equation of state (EoS), i.e. there is an absence of critical end point mechanism. Furthermore, within different rapidity regions, the kinetic temperatures of different light nuclei are extracted by the Blast-wave model analysis and ratios among different light nuclei are also discussed.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model with a naive coalescence approach can essentially reproduce experimental light-nuclei yields at midrapidity, and a slight enhancement of the combined ratio \(N_{p}N_{t}/N_{d}^{2}\) occurs around 20 GeV; however, this enhancement should not be over-interpreted as a sign of the critical end point because the present UrQMD version is a cascade model without an equation of state.

## Method

Central (b = 0–3 fm) \(^{197}\)Au + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 7.7–80 GeV were simulated with the UrQMD-3.3p1 cascade model. Light nuclei were identified via a minimum spanning tree (MST) coalescence algorithm using coordinate and momentum cuts (\(\Delta r < 3.575\) fm, \(\Delta p < 0.285\) GeV/\(c\)) in the two-particle rest frame at a stopping time of 65 fm/\(c\). Kinetic temperatures were extracted by fitting transverse-momentum spectra with the Blast-wave model.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron yield \(dN/dY\) at midrapidity (\(|Y|<0.3\)) matches STAR data; for \(|Y|<0.1\) the deuteron yield is lower than data, while triton yield agrees within \(|Y|<0.1\) but is lower within \(|Y|<0.3\).
* The combined ratio \(O_1 = N_p N_t / N_d^2\) shows a slight enhancement around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 20\) GeV and a broad peak near 60 GeV for small midrapidity cuts.
* Kinetic temperatures for deuteron, triton, and \(^3\)He in the \(|Y|<1.0\) and range1 regions are about 80 MeV, lower than the proton kinetic temperature in the same regions.
* The ratio \(N_n/N_p\) for all midrapidity cuts decreases with increasing \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\); at low energies it is affected by the initial isospin asymmetry of \(^{197}\)Au (118/79 \(\approx\) 1.49).
* In the spectator region (\(|Y|>Y_1\)), \(N_n/N_p\) tends to 1.4 at high energies, close to the initial isospin asymmetry.
* Ratios \(O_2\), \(O_3\), and \(O_4\) (involving \(^4\)He) are around 1–2 below 20 GeV and tend to zero at higher energies within midrapidity cuts.

## Context

This work extends the UrQMD-based study of light-nuclei production to a wide energy range (7.7–80 GeV) and to multiple rapidity regions, following the proposal by Sun et al. (2018) and preliminary STAR Beam Energy Scan results that the ratio \(N_t N_p / N_d^2\) may be sensitive to neutron density fluctuations near the QCD critical end point. The authors caution that the observed enhancement around 20 GeV cannot be taken as evidence for the critical end point because the cascade UrQMD lacks phase-transition mechanisms, in line with the baryon-density study by Xu et al. (2020) using the same model. The results motivate further work with models that include a realistic equation of state or critical end point mechanism, and they highlight that rapidity-dependent measurements of light-nuclei ratios may provide additional information beyond midrapidity.
