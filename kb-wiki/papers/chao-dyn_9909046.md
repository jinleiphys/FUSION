# The Statistics of Chaotic Tunnelling

> We discuss the statistics of tunnelling rates in the presence of chaotic classical dynamics. This applies to resonance widths in chaotic metastable wells and to tunnelling splittings in chaotic symmetric double wells. The theory is based on using the properties of a semiclassical tunnelling operator together with random matrix theory arguments about wave function overlaps. The resulting distribution depends on the stability of a specific tunnelling orbit and is therefore not universal. However it does reduce to the universal Porter-Thomas form as the orbit becomes very unstable. For some choices of system parameters there are systematic deviations which we explain in terms of scarring of certain real periodic orbits. The theory is tested in a model symmetric double well problem and possible experimental realisations are discussed.

## Key claim

The distribution of tunnelling rates in chaotic systems depends on the stability of a specific tunnelling orbit (the instanton) and is therefore not universal, though it reduces to the universal Porter-Thomas form when that orbit becomes very unstable. The authors also find systematic deviations from their predicted distribution when the real extension of the instanton orbit is periodic, which they attribute to scarring effects.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical tunnelling operator from the Green's function and treat its eigenvalues as exponentially decaying with a parameter lambda (the inverse stability of the instanton orbit). They then make a random matrix theory ansatz that the overlaps between the tunnelling operator eigenstates and the chaotic eigenstates are Gaussian random variables, leading to a closed-form distribution for the rescaled tunnelling rate y. The theory is tested numerically by computing many tunnelling splittings in a model symmetric double-well potential with chaotic classical dynamics, using fixed-energy quantisation.

## Key numbers

- The second moment of the rescaled tunnelling rate is given by ⟨y²⟩ = 1 + (2/β)(1-λ)/(1+λ), where β=1 for GOE and β=2 for GUE statistics.
- For small λ, the distribution reduces to Porter-Thomas form: exp(-y/2)/√(2πy) for β=1 and exp(-y) for β=2.
- For λ close to unity, the distribution approaches a Gaussian with variance σ² ≈ a/β, where a = 1-λ.
- For large y (y ≫ λ), the asymptotic forms are P(y) ≈ exp(-y/2a)/√(2πay) for GOE and P(y) ≈ exp(-y/a)/a for GUE.
- Near the saddle, λ ≈ exp(-2πω_y/ω_x) where ω_x and ω_y are the curvatures of the potential saddle along and transverse to the instanton.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous results (Creagh and Whelan, 1996, 1999) on average tunnelling rates in chaotic systems to the full statistical distribution of fluctuations. It contrasts with the chaos-assisted tunnelling regime studied by Tomsovic and Ullmo (1994) and others, where the distribution has power-law decays rather than exponential decays. The theory enables predictions for resonance widths in chaotic metastable wells and tunnelling splittings in chaotic symmetric double wells, with applications to hydrogen atoms in parallel electric and magnetic fields (Cacciani et al., 1986), dissociative decays of excited nuclei and molecules, and conductance peak statistics in quantum dots (Jalabert, Stone and Alhassid, 1992; Chang et al., 1996; Folk et al., 1996).
