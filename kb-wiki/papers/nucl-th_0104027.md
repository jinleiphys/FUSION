# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering in a Three-Dimensional Approach

> The nucleon-nucleon t-matrix is calculated directly as function of two vector momenta for different realistic NN potentials. The angular and momentum dependence of the full amplitude is studied and NN observables are calculated.

## Key claim

The authors formulate and numerically illustrate a three-dimensional (3D) approach to nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering that works directly with momentum vectors and a helicity representation for spin, avoiding the conventional partial-wave decomposition; the approach is demonstrated to be feasible for realistic NN potentials, but the paper emphasizes that the scheme is algebraically simple only when potentials are given in an operator form, as is the case for field-theoretic interactions.

## Method

The Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the NN t-matrix is formulated using momentum-helicity basis states, where the total spin of the two nucleons is represented via helicity eigenvalues with respect to the relative momentum. For the singlet case (S=0) a single integral equation per parity is solved, while for the triplet case (S=1) a set of two coupled two-dimensional integral equations per parity and initial helicity state is solved. The t-matrix is computed directly as a function of two vector momenta (magnitudes and the angle between them) for the Argonne AV18 and Bonn-B potentials, and NN observables are derived from the resulting amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The half-shell t-matrix \(T^{101}_{00}(q,q_0,\theta)\) is displayed for \(q_0 = 375\) MeV/c, corresponding to \(E_{lab} = 300\) MeV, with amplitudes given in units of \(10^{-7}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).
- At \(E_{lab} = 300\) MeV, partial waves up to at least \(j_{max} = 12\) are needed to obtain a converged result for every on-shell t-matrix element.
- For the differential cross section at \(E_{lab} = 300\) MeV, a sum up to \(j_{max} = 16\) is required to achieve convergence within 1%, with convergence especially slow at backward angles.
- For the analyzing power \(A_y\) at \(E_{lab} = 300\) MeV, a partial wave sum up to \(j_{max} = 10\) is needed to reach similar convergence.
- The on-shell amplitudes \(|\langle ++|T|--\rangle|^2\) and \(|\langle ++|T|++\rangle|^2\) are shown in units of \(10^{-14}\) MeV\(^{-4}\) as a function of \(\cos \theta\) for \(q_0 = 375\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the conventional partial-wave decomposition approach to NN scattering by introducing a 3D momentum-space formulation that avoids the need for many angular momentum states at intermediate energies (a few hundred MeV). The method is applied to two realistic potentials, Argonne AV18 (Wiringa et al., 1995) and Bonn-B (Machleidt et al., 1987), and the results are compared to standard partial-wave sums. The authors intend this scheme as a starting point for treating three-nucleon scattering in a similar 3D fashion, which would be more efficient than the partial-wave approach for few-body systems at intermediate energies.
