# Transport Synthetic Acceleration for the Solution of the One-Speed Nonclassical Spectral S$_N$ Equations in Slab Geometry

> The nonclassical transport equation models particle transport processes in which the particle flux does not decrease as an exponential function of the particle's free-path. Recently, a spectral approach was developed to generate nonclassical spectral S$_N$ equations, which can be numerically solved in a deterministic fashion using classical numerical techniques. This paper introduces a transport synthetic acceleration procedure to speed up the iteration scheme for the solution of the monoenergetic slab-geometry nonclassical spectral S$_N$ equations. We present numerical results that confirm the benefit of the acceleration procedure for this class of problems.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a transport synthetic acceleration (specifically S$_2$SA) procedure that speeds up the source iteration scheme for solving the one-speed nonclassical spectral S$_N$ equations in slab geometry, and presents numerical results confirming the benefit; however, the spectral radii for the accelerated scheme are not exactly consistent with those found when applying corresponding techniques to the classical S$_N$ transport equation, which the authors attribute to the nonclassical equation containing an altogether different scattering term that depends on the free-path.

## Method

The authors first derive the nonclassical spectral S$_N$ equations in slab geometry by expanding the nonclassical angular flux as a truncated series of Laguerre polynomials in the free-path variable $s$, then applying the discrete ordinates (S$_N$) formulation. They then implement a two-stage transport synthetic acceleration scheme: a single transport sweep to obtain a half-iterate solution estimate, followed by an error-correction stage using an S$_2$ approximation (N=2) of the error equation to estimate and correct the error at each iteration. The spatial variable is discretized into 200 elements using the linear discontinuous Galerkin finite element method, and the stopping criterion is that relative deviations between consecutive estimates of the classical scalar flux must be $\leq 10^{-6}$.

## Key numbers

- For exponential $p(s)$ with $\sigma_t = 1.0$, $X=200$, $M=50$, and $c=0.999$: stand-alone SI required 6439 iterations with spectral radius 0.9989, while S$_2$SA required 6 iterations with spectral radius 0.1685.
- For exponential $p(s)$ with $c=0.8$: SI required 56 iterations (spectral radius 0.7997), S$_2$SA required 6 iterations (spectral radius 0.1328).
- For nonexponential $p(s)$ with $\sigma_t = 1.0$, $X=200$, $M=50$, and $c=0.999$: SI required 6443 iterations (spectral radius 0.9989), S$_2$SA required 6 iterations (spectral radius 0.1802).
- For nonexponential $p(s)$ with $c=0.8$: SI required 56 iterations (spectral radius 0.7997), S$_2$SA required 6 iterations (spectral radius 0.1538).
- All calculations used Gauss-Legendre angular quadrature with $N=16$ for the transport equation and $N=2$ for the error equation, with spatial discretization into 200 elements.

## Context

This work extends the spectral approach for nonclassical transport developed in a prior paper (JCP, 2020), which represented the nonclassical flux as a series of Laguerre polynomials and solved it using conventional discrete ordinates (S$_N$) formulation with source iteration. The paper introduces transport synthetic acceleration techniques (S$_2$SA) to this class of nonclassical spectral problems for the first time. The nonclassical transport equation itself was originally introduced to describe photon transport in Earth's cloudy atmosphere (Davis and Marshak, 2010; Knyazikhin and Beres, 2013) and has found applications in nuclear engineering (Vasques and Larsen, 2009; Vasques, 2013) and computer graphics. The results enable efficient solution of nonclassical transport problems in highly scattering systems where the spectral radius of the source iteration scheme can get arbitrarily close to unity.
