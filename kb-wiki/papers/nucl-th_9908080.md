# Neutron-proton radiative capture, photo-magnetic and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

> The cross sections for the M1-capture n + p -> D + gamma, the photo-magnetic and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron are evaluated in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD). The cross section for M1-capture is evaluated by taking into account the contributions of chiral one-meson loop corrections and the Delta(1232) resonance. The cross sections for the photo-magnetic and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron are evaluated by accounting for final-state interaction of the nucleon pair in the spinsinglet S-wave state. The amplitudes of low-energy elastic np and nn scattering contributing to these processes are obtained in terms of the S-wave scattering lengths and the effective ranges. This relaxes substantially the statement by Bahcall and Kamionkowski (Nucl. Phys. A625 (1997) 893) that the RFMD is unable to describe a non-zero effective range for low-energy elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering. The cross sections for the anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron averaged over the anti-neutrino energy spectrum agree good with experimental data.

## Key claim

The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD), when augmented with chiral one-meson loop corrections and the Delta(1232) resonance contribution, can describe the M1-capture cross section n + p -> D + gamma to within about 14% of the experimental value, and the cross sections for photo-magnetic and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron agree with experimental data when final-state interactions are included via S-wave scattering lengths and effective ranges, thereby relaxing the earlier claim by Bahcall and Kamionkowski that the RFMD cannot describe a non-zero effective range for low-energy elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors evaluated the M1-capture cross section n + p -> D + gamma by computing chiral one-meson loop corrections using Chiral Perturbation Theory at the quark level with a linear realization of chiral U(3)xU(3) symmetry, then adding the contribution of the Delta(1232) resonance via effective Lagrangians for pi N Delta and gamma N Delta interactions. For photo-magnetic and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron, they summed an infinite series of one-nucleon loop diagrams in the large N_C expansion to account for final-state interactions of the nucleon pair in the spin-singlet S-wave state, expressing the amplitudes in terms of S-wave scattering lengths and effective ranges.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level M1-capture cross section in RFMD: 276 mb, compared to the experimental value (334.2 +/- 0.5) mb and the potential model approach value (302.5 +/- 4) mb
- M1-capture cross section after including chiral one-meson loop corrections: 287.2 mb
- Effective coupling constant G_piNN = 3.27 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-2}, derived from g_piNN = 13.4, M_pi = 135 MeV, M_N = 940 MeV, and np S-wave scattering length a_np = (-23.75 +/- 0.01) fm
- Momentum integrals for chiral loop corrections: J_pi a1 N = 0.017 M_pi^{-2} and J_pi VN = 0.024 M_pi^{-2}
- Coupling constants relative to g_piNN: g_pi N Delta = 2.12 g_piNN and g_gamma N Delta = 0.32 g_piNN

## Context

This work extends the RFMD framework previously developed by Ivanov and collaborators (1996-1998) by incorporating chiral perturbation theory and Delta(1232) resonance contributions, addressing the criticism by Bahcall and Kamionkowski (1997) that the RFMD could not describe non-zero effective ranges in low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering. The results enable improved calculations of solar pp fusion and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron, with the averaged cross sections for anti-neutrino disintegration agreeing with experimental data from the Reines experimental group (1997).
