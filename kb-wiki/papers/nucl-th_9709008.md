# A Microscopic Calculation of Photoabsorption Cross Sections on Protons and Nuclei

> A recently developed model for $ρ$-meson propagation in dense hadronic matter is applied to total photoabsorption cross sections in $γ$-proton and $γ$-nucleus reactions. Within the vector dominance model the photon coupling to the virtual pion cloud of the nucleon, two-body meson-exchange currents, as well as $γ$-nucleon resonances are included. Whereas the $γp$ reaction is determined by the low-density limit of the model, higher orders in the nuclear density are important to correctly account for the experimental spectra observed on both light and heavy nuclei over a wide range of photon energies, including the region below the pion threshold. In connection with soft dilepton spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions we emphasize the importance of photoabsorption to further constrain the parameters of the model.

# A Microscopic Calculation of Photoabsorption Cross Sections on Protons and Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors show that their previously developed model for ρ-meson propagation in dense hadronic matter, when extended to the photon point (M²=0) and including improved treatment of two-pion selfenergy and resonant ρN contributions, can describe total photoabsorption cross sections on both protons and nuclei with a limited number of parameters, but only if the naive vector dominance model is corrected by allowing independent adjustment of the BNγ coupling (transition magnetic moment) for each resonance.

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross section is computed from the imaginary part of the transverse ρ-meson propagator in nuclear matter via the vector dominance model, using a selfenergy that includes two-pion loop contributions (modified in-medium by pion interactions with nucleons) and direct ρN resonance couplings from s-channel pole graphs for baryon resonances (N(939), Δ(1232), N(1520), N(1720), Δ(1620), Δ(1700), Δ(1905)). The calculations are performed in infinite nuclear matter at an average density of 0.8ρ₀, with short-range correlation effects parameterized by Migdal parameters and in-medium corrections to resonance widths, using an improved VDM that replaces the naive ρ-meson propagator with a transition form factor allowing independent photon coupling strengths.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff parameter for pion form factor: Λπ = 550 MeV
- Cutoff parameter for ρ-meson form factor: Λρ = 600 MeV (ΛρNN = 1 GeV)
- Ratio of photon coupling to naive VDM value: rB = 0.5 (common value for all resonances)
- Average nuclear density used for calculations: ρ̄N = 0.8 ρ₀
- Landau-Migdal parameters: g'NN = 0.6, g'αβ = 0.25 for all other transitions, g's-wave ≡ 0
- In-medium width correction for N(1520): ΓmedN(1520) = 250 MeV
- Photoabsorption calculations cover incident photon energies up to about 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model for ρ-meson propagation in hadronic matter (Rapp et al., 1997) to the photon point (M²=0), providing an important constraint on model parameters used to describe dilepton enhancement in heavy-ion collisions observed by CERES and HELIOS-3. The approach builds on earlier work by Friman and Pirner (1989) on ρN resonance couplings and by Klingl, Lenz, and Weise on improved vector dominance, and enables more reliable transport-theoretical calculations of dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at CERN.
