# Multireference Covariant Density Functional Theory with Stochastic Basis

> Multireference density functional theory (MR-DFT) provides a pivotal microscopic framework for the description of the ground state properties, low-lying nuclear spectra and transition properties of atomic nuclei. Conventionally, practical implementations of MR-DFT rely on empirically chosen generator coordinates, which may omit relevant collective degrees of freedom and thus fail to capture sufficient collective correlations. Here we introduce the stochastic-basis multireference density functional theory (MR-SDFT). This is an extended scheme that augments the MR-DFT toolkit by (i) generating a diverse ensemble of mean-field reference configurations via a stochastic external field and (ii) selecting a compact subspace with Projection-Selection method. The chosen reference configurations are then linearly superposed within the MR-DFT framework to yield spectroscopic observables. Applying this framework to \nuclide[20]{Ne}, \nuclide[24]{Mg} and \nuclide[28]{Si} with the covariant density functional theory (CDFT), it is demonstrated that the MR-SCDFT leads to lower ground-state energies, smaller point-proton rms radius, and a softer ground-state band compared to the conventional MR-CDFT.

## Key claim

The stochastic-basis multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-SCDFT) generates a more diverse ensemble of mean-field reference configurations than conventional MR-CDFT by using a stochastic external field, leading to lower ground-state energies, smaller point-proton rms radii, and softer ground-state bands for the sd-shell nuclei 20Ne, 24Mg, and 28Si, but the method still misses triaxial degrees of freedom and overestimates some excitation energies.

## Method

The authors replace the conventional deformation-constraining external field in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a stochastic external field composed of a Gaussian-smoothed random perturbation confined by a mask function near the nuclear interior. They generate 140 mean-field states for each nucleus using the PC-F1 point-coupling functional, discard outliers with excessive deformation, and then apply the Projection-Selection (PS) method to select a compact subspace of approximately 11 configurations based on projected energies and orthogonality criteria. The selected configurations are then superposed via the generator coordinate method with quantum-number projections (parity, proton number, neutron number, and angular momentum) to solve the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation for spectroscopic observables.

## Key numbers

- For 20Ne: MR-SCDFT ground-state energy = -160.511 MeV, compared to MR-CDFT = -159.452 MeV and experimental = -160.645 MeV
- For 20Ne: MR-SCDFT point-proton rms radius = 2.931 fm, compared to MR-CDFT = 3.000 fm and experimental = 2.889(2) fm
- For 20Ne: MR-SCDFT R4/2 ratio = 2.825, compared to MR-CDFT = 3.217 and experimental = 2.600
- For 24Mg: MR-SCDFT ground-state energy = -198.684 MeV, compared to MR-CDFT = -197.273 MeV and experimental = -198.257 MeV
- For 24Mg: MR-SCDFT point-proton rms radius = 3.010 fm, compared to MR-CDFT = 3.032 fm and experimental = 2.941(2) fm
- For 28Si: MR-SCDFT ground-state energy = -236.256 MeV, compared to MR-CDFT = -235.223 MeV and experimental = -236.537 MeV
- For 28Si: MR-SCDFT R4/2 ratio = 2.606, compared to MR-CDFT = 3.184 and experimental = 2.596
- The PS method used a cutoff parameter Lc = 0.83 and Lc_min = 0.01 for configuration selection
- 140 mean-field states were generated, with 114 retained after outlier rejection for 20Ne

## Context

This work extends the optimized-basis generator coordinate method developed by Matsumoto (2023, 2025) and the stochastic external field approach introduced by Hizawa (2023) from quantum chemistry into nuclear structure physics. The method addresses the limitation of conventional MR-DFT that relies on empirically chosen generator coordinates, which may omit relevant collective degrees of freedom. The results enable future applications to heavier nuclei and suggest that including triaxial degrees of freedom (as argued by Kanada-En'yo, 2021, for 24Mg) and gamma deformation (as suggested by Frycz, 2024, for 28Si) would further improve agreement with experiment.
