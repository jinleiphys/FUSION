# Multicomponent second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics with binary reactive collisions

> We derive the multi-component second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamic equations using the moment-expansion method. By computing the transport coefficients using hard-sphere interactions, we investigate the role of multiple components and the reactive collisions. We find that both of these factors increase the effective cross-section and hence decrease the transport coefficients and relaxation times. We further compute such transport properties using leading-order perturbative QCD cross-sections. For both types of cross-sections, we find that the ratio between vector current relaxation time and conductivity for a multi-component fluid is notably different from that for a single-component fluid. Therefore, the current study provides a more applicable guideline for such a ratio in phenomenological hydrodynamics simulations.

## Key claim

The paper derives multicomponent second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamic equations using the moment-expansion method, finding that both multiple components and reactive collisions increase the effective cross-section and thereby decrease transport coefficients and relaxation times; the ratio between vector current relaxation time and conductivity for a multicomponent fluid is notably different from that for a single-component fluid, providing a more applicable guideline for phenomenological hydrodynamics simulations.

## Method

The authors employed the moment-expansion method (Denicol, 2012) starting from the relativistic Boltzmann equation with binary two-to-two scatterings, including both elastic collisions between different components and reactive collisions that change particle identity (e.g., quark+antiquark to gluon+gluon). They computed transport coefficients by diagonalizing the evolution matrix for scalar, vector, and tensor moments, first using hard-sphere interactions and then using leading-order perturbative QCD cross-sections. The matching conditions (Landau frame) were implemented to constrain the moments, and the slowest dynamical eigenmodes were isolated to obtain the Navier-Stokes limit and relaxation times.

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart (1979) second-order relativistic hydrodynamics from single-component to multicomponent systems with reactive collisions, building on prior multicomponent viscous hydrodynamics derivations by Prakash (1993), Monnai (2010), and the relaxation time approximation approach of Bhadury (2020). The results enable more realistic phenomenological simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly at high baryon density where isospin chemical potential effects become important, such as at future FAIR and NICA facilities.
