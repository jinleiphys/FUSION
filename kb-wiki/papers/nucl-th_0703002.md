# The Delta N -> NN transition in finite nuclei

> We perform a direct finite nucleus calculation of the partial width of a bound Delta isobar decaying through the non-mesonic decay mode, Delta N -> NN. This transition is modeled by the exchange of the long ranged pi meson and the shorter ranged rho meson. The contribution of this decay channel is found to be approximately 60 % of the decay width of the Delta particle in free space. Considering the additional pionic decay mode, we conclude that the total decay width of a bound Delta resonance in nuclei is of the order of 100 MeV and, consequently, no narrow Delta nuclear states exist, contrary to recent claims in the literature. Our results are in complete agreement with microscopic many-body calculations and phenomenological approaches performed in nuclear matter.

# The Delta N -> NN transition in finite nuclei

## Key claim

The total decay width of a bound Delta resonance in nuclei is approximately 100 MeV, meaning no narrow Delta nuclear states exist, contrary to recent claims in the literature. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the Delta is bound in a 1s orbital with a binding energy of about 25 MeV, and the calculation is performed specifically for the system of a Delta coupled to a carbon-12 core.

## Method

The authors perform a direct finite-nucleus calculation of the partial width for the non-mesonic decay mode Delta N -> NN, modeling the transition via one-pion and one-rho meson exchange. The initial bound Delta-nucleus system is described using a shell model with the Delta in a 1s orbital weakly coupled to a carbon-12 core, while the final two-nucleon state is treated with distorted waves from a T-matrix scattering equation using the Nijmegen NSC97f potential. Short-range Delta N correlations are included phenomenologically via a correlation function, and the calculation employs Moshinsky brackets to transform the two-body wave functions into relative and center-of-mass coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The non-mesonic decay width from the combined pi+rho exchange with initial and final state interactions is 0.58 times the free Delta width of 120 MeV, corresponding to approximately 70 MeV
- The total decay width of a bound Delta resonance in nuclei is of the order of 100 MeV when including the additional pionic decay mode
- The Delta binding energy is assumed to be approximately 25 MeV, taken from the s-shell energy of the mean-field model used in Bartsch (1999)
- The harmonic oscillator parameters are b_Delta = 1.59 fm and b_N = 1.64 fm for the Delta and nucleon respectively
- The pi-exchange contribution dominates, giving 0.61 times the free width with ISI+FSI, while rho-exchange contributes only 0.83 x 10^-3 times the free width
- The tensor transition potential dominates over the central term by two orders of magnitude (0.58 vs 0.16 x 10^-2 in units of free width for the s+p shell contribution)
- The representative relative NN momentum used in the analysis is 510 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic many-body calculations in nuclear matter by Oset (1979, 1981, 1987) and Weise (1977) to a direct finite-nucleus calculation, and contradicts claims of narrow Delta nuclear states made by Bartsch (1999) and Walcher (2001). The results agree with the phenomenological Delta-hole model analyses of Hirata (1977, 1978) and Horikawa (1980), which established an imaginary Delta potential of about -40 MeV at normal nuclear matter density. The calculation enables a definitive assessment of whether bound Delta states can be narrow, concluding that the in-medium Delta width remains of the same order as the free width and no narrow states exist.
