# Shear Viscosity of Collider-Produced QCD Matter I: AMY Formalism vs. A Modified Relaxation Time Approximation in 0-flavor SU(3) Theory

> The AMY formalism is widely used to describe the transport coefficients of asymptotically hot and dense QCD matter, such as shear viscosity $η$. In literature prior to AMY, the viscosity of an asymptotically hot QCD plasma was expressed by a $q^2$ momentum transfer-weighted relaxation time approximation. Recent studies that compared numerical transport calculations and analytical expressions for $η$ demonstrated that asymptotically high temperatures and densities induce anisotropic scatterings, which are exhibited in the quark-gluon plasma produced by relativistic heavy ion collisions. In these studies, the QGP was treated as a Maxwell-Boltzmann-distributed gluon gas with added (anti-)quark degrees of freedom. One such method used in the comparison was the ``modified'' $q^2$ transport-weighted RTA. In this study, a comparison between the AMY formalism (both numerical calculations and next-leading-log expression) and the modified RTA expression for $η$ is made in 0-flavor SU(3) theory for collider-produced QGP. The comparison between numerical AMY calculations and the modified RTA method shows perfect agreement under the temperatures relevant for collider-produced QGP. Additionally, AMY is compared with the Chapman-Enskog method, which is well understood to better describe anisotropic collider-produced QGP.

## Key claim

The AMY formalism (both numerical basis-function calculations and the next-leading-log expression) and the modified RTA method show perfect agreement for shear viscosity under the temperatures relevant for collider-produced QGP, specifically in 0-flavor SU(3) theory where the QGP is treated as a pure gluon gas.

## Method

The author compared three approaches for calculating shear viscosity in 0-flavor SU(3) theory: the AMY formalism (using both numerical basis-function calculations and the next-leading-log expression), a modified relaxation time approximation (RTA*) that uses the transport cross section weighted by momentum transfer, and the Chapman-Enskog method. All methods used the same perturbative QCD gluon-gluon scattering amplitude with Debye screening mass, and the running gauge coupling was expressed as a function of temperature. The comparison was made by plotting the dimensionless quantity η×g⁴/T³ against the ratio m_D/T across temperatures relevant for collider-produced QGP.

## Key numbers

* For 0-flavor SU(3) theory, the next-leading-log expression for shear viscosity is η^NLL = (T³/g⁴) × [η₁ / ln(μ*/m_D)], where η₁ = 27.126 and μ*/T = 2.765.
* The Debye screening mass for 0-flavor SU(3) is m_D = gT, and the running coupling is defined by 1/g²(T) = (9/8π²) ln(T/Λ_T) + (4/9π²) ln[2 ln(T/Λ_T)] with Λ_T = 30 MeV.
* The collider-produced QGP temperature window is on average between T = 550 MeV and T = 150 MeV, corresponding to m_D/T values in the range [1.56, 2.06].
* The NLL expression diverges as m_D/T approaches μ*/T = 2.765, corresponding to a threshold temperature of T = 74.5 MeV, which is below the Hagedorn temperature T_H ≈ 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior comparisons between numerical transport calculations and analytical expressions for shear viscosity, specifically building on studies by Plumari (2012) and MacKay (2022) that compared the Chapman-Enskog method with the modified RTA approach. The study addresses a question raised by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003) about whether the AMY formalism exhibits inherent RTA* behavior in certain temperature regions. The results enable future work using the AMY formalism for collider-produced QGP temperatures where the next-leading-log expression begins to diverge from exact numerical calculations.
