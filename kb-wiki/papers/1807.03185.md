# On strangeness in NA61/SHINE

> NA61/SHINE is a fixed target experiment at the CERN Super-Proton- Synchrotron. The main goals of the experiment are to discover the critical point of strongly interacting matter and to study the properties of the onset of deconfinement. In order to reach these goals, a study of hadron production properties is performed in nucleus-nucleus, proton-proton and proton-nucleus interactions as a function of collision energy and size of the colliding nuclei. In this paper, I will review recent results on strangeness production in p+p, Be+Be and Ar+Sc collisions in the SPS energy range. Kinematic spectra and mean multiplicities of kaons obtained with various analysis methods will be shown. An overview of strangeness production and its dependence on system size in the vicinity of the phase transition will be presented as well.

## Key claim

The paper reviews preliminary NA61/SHINE results showing that strangeness production in Be+Be collisions closely resembles p+p interactions, while Ar+Sc collisions approach Pb+Pb behavior, suggesting a possible "onset of fireball" threshold in system size that is not predicted by current theoretical models.

## Method

The NA61/SHINE fixed-target spectrometer at the CERN SPS measured hadron production in p+p, Be+Be, and Ar+Sc collisions across SPS energies. Transverse mass spectra of kaons were fitted with exponential functions to extract inverse slope parameters at mid-rapidity, and mean multiplicities of kaons and pions were used to compute K/pi ratios in both mid-rapidity and 4pi acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Kaon mass M_K approximately 500 MeV; strange quark mass m_s approximately 100 MeV; critical temperature of hadron matter estimated around 160 MeV
- Be+Be inverse slope parameters for kaons fall very close to p+p results, showing a step structure similar to Pb+Pb but at a lower level
- The K+/pi+ ratio (4pi acceptance) for Ar+Sc lies between p+p and heavy-ion (Pb+Pb, Au+Au) results, approaching Pb+Pb values at higher collision energies
- Be+Be K+/pi+ ratio at mid-rapidity is only slightly higher than p+p values

## Context

This work extends the NA49 measurements of Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions (NA49 Collaboration, 2002) and p+p reference data (NA61/SHINE, 2016) by providing the first intermediate-system results from Be+Be and Ar+Sc. The data challenge the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES), which predicts canonical strangeness suppression should already be negligible for Be+Be, and the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics model (PHSD), which predicts decreasing strangeness with increasing system size at high energies. These results enable mapping of a possible "onset of fireball" threshold in system size, with more intermediate-system data from NA61/SHINE expected to test this hypothesis.
