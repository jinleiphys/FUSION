# Self-consistent continuum random phase approximation calculations of $^4$He electromagnetic responses

> We study the electromagnetic responses of $^4$He within the framework of the self-consistent continuum random phase approximation theory. In this approach the ground state properties are described by a Hartree-Fock calculation. The single particle basis constructed in this manner is used in the calculations of the continuum responses of the system. Finite-range interactions are considered in the calculations. We compare our results with photon absorption cross sections and electron scattering quasi-elastic data. From this comparison, and also from the comparison with the results of microscopic calculations, we deduce that our approach describes well the continuum excitation.

## Key claim
Self-consistent continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) calculations, using finite-range effective interactions, describe the continuum excitation of $^4$He well for both photoabsorption and quasi-elastic electron scattering, despite the mean-field approach being unreliable for such a light nucleus. The authors hedge that the good performance is because complex excitations beyond one-particle one-hole are almost absent in $^4$He, unlike in medium-heavy nuclei where a spreading width must be added.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations to construct the single-particle basis for $^4$He, using three finite-range effective interactions: the Gogny D1S and D1M parameterizations, and the Brink-Boeker B1 interaction. They then solved the CRPA equations without approximations for continuum excitations, computing total photoabsorption cross sections (summing 1$^-$ and 2$^+$ multipoles) and longitudinal and transverse quasi-elastic electron scattering responses at various momentum transfers, considering multipole excitations up to $J=6$ or $J=8$ for convergence.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies from HF: D1S gives 30.28 MeV, D1M gives 29.54 MeV, B1 gives 28.48 MeV, compared to experimental 28.29 MeV.
- Proton separation energies: D1S gives 19.39 MeV, D1M gives 18.25 MeV, B1 gives 26.00 MeV, compared to experimental 19.81 MeV.
- Neutron separation energies: D1S gives 20.09 MeV, D1M gives 18.96 MeV, B1 gives 26.00 MeV, compared to experimental 20.58 MeV.
- RMS charge radii: D1S gives 2.04 fm, D1M gives 2.02 fm, B1 gives 1.92 fm, compared to experimental 1.68 fm.
- Nuclear matter symmetry energies: D1M gives 29.45 MeV, D1S gives 31.77 MeV, B1 gives 58.55 MeV.
- The 2$^+$ excitation contributes only about 2% to the total photoabsorption cross section.
- Quasi-elastic responses were calculated at momentum transfers of 200, 300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, with multipole excitations up to $J=6$ for 200 MeV/c and up to $J=8$ for 500 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CRPA technique (De Donno 2011), which was applied to medium-heavy nuclei (oxygen and calcium isotopes) and produced satisfactory giant resonance peak positions but overly narrow widths. The $^4$He results are compared to fully microscopic Lorentz Inverse Transform (LIT) calculations (Gazit 2006; Bacca 2007, 2009a, 2009b), which solve the Schrodinger equation without approximations. The authors find that CRPA works better for $^4$He than for medium-heavy nuclei because the missing spreading width from complex excitations is negligible in this light system, enabling future studies of final state interactions in few-body systems using effective theories.
