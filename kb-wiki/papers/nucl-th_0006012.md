# Charmonium + Light Hadron Cross Sections

> In this contribution we summarize experimental information and theoretical results for the dissociation cross sections of charmonium by light hadrons. Theoretical predictions for these RHIC-related processes differ by orders of magnitude over the physically relevant energy range. The results found by the author and collaborators using a constituent interchange model, which predicts cross sections in the mb region near threshold, are discussed in more detail.

# Charmonium + Light Hadron Cross Sections

## Key claim
The dissociation cross sections of charmonia by light hadrons are crucial for interpreting J/ψ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature, but theoretical predictions differ by orders of magnitude over the physically relevant energy range. The authors' constituent interchange model predicts cross sections in the millibarn region near threshold, which is intermediate between the nanobarn-scale pQCD predictions and the tens-of-millibarn predictions from meson exchange models with pointlike vertices.

## Method
The authors review experimental constraints from J/ψ photoproduction and nuclear absorption data, then compare three theoretical approaches: color-dipole pQCD (Kharzeev and Satz), t-channel charmed meson exchange, and constituent interchange quark model calculations. Their own calculations use numerically determined Coulomb-plus-linear-plus-hyperfine quark potential model wavefunctions, evaluating Born-order meson-meson scattering amplitudes via nine-dimensional Monte Carlo integration of the interquark Hamiltonian matrix elements with quark interchange diagrams.

## Key numbers
- Experimental J/ψ+N cross section inferred from SLAC photoproduction (1977): 3.5 ± 0.8 mb at Eγ ≈ 17 GeV (√s ≈ 6 GeV)
- Experimental J/ψ+N absorption cross section from nuclear production data: ≈ 6 mb at √s ≈ 10 GeV (Capella et al., 1997)
- Experimental J/ψ+N absorption cross section from E866 with negative xF selection: 2.8 ± 0.3 mb at √s ≈ 10 GeV (He, Hüfner, Kopeliovich, 1996)
- Experimental ψ'+N absorption cross section from same E866 analysis: 10.5 ± 3.6 mb at √s ≈ 10 GeV
- Kharzeev-Satz pQCD J/ψ+N cross section formula: σ = 2.5 mb × (1 − λ₀/λ)^6.5, giving nanobarn-scale cross sections 500 MeV above threshold
- Matinyan-Müller meson exchange J/ψ+π and J/ψ+ρ cross sections: 0.5 to 1 mb range at 500 MeV above threshold
- Sibirtsev-Tsushima-Thomas meson exchange J/ψ+N → Λc+¯D peak cross section: ≈ 2 mb near √s = 4.6 GeV
- Haglin meson exchange J/ψ+N → Λc+¯D peak cross section: ≈ 7 mb near √s = 4.3 GeV
- Martins-Blaschke-Quack constituent interchange J/ψ+π total cross section maximum: ≈ 7 mb at √s ≈ 4.1 GeV
- Authors' constituent interchange J/ψ+π cross section peak: ≈ 1 mb at √s ≈ 4.0 GeV
- Authors' constituent interchange ψ'+π cross section maximum: ≈ 5 mb
- Quark model parameters used: αs = 0.6, string tension b = 0.18 GeV², light quark mass mu,d = 0.33 GeV, charm quark mass mc = 1.6 GeV, OGE contact hyperfine smearing distance = 0.25 fm

## Context
This work reviews and extends calculations motivated by the Matsui and Satz (1986) proposal that J/ψ suppression could signal quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors contrast three theoretical approaches: the color-dipole pQCD method of Peskin and Bhanot (1979) as applied by Kharzeev and Satz (1994), which gives very small cross sections but uses Coulombic wavefunctions inappropriate for charmonium; t-channel meson exchange calculations by Matinyan and Müller (1998), Sibirtsev, Tsushima and Thomas (2000), and Haglin (2000); and constituent interchange calculations by Martins, Blaschke and Quack (1995) and the authors' own work (Wong et al., 1999). The authors note that the Isgur-Maltman (1984) argument against vector meson exchange for short-range interactions applies to charmed meson exchange as well, since the exchange range of about 0.1 fm is smaller than hadron sizes. The wide spread of predictions (six orders of magnitude between pQCD and meson exchange near threshold) presents an opportunity to distinguish between hadron-hadron scattering models experimentally.
