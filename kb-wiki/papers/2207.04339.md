# Pion scalar, vector and tensor form factors from a contact interaction

> The pion scalar, vector and tensor form factors are calculated within a symmetry-preserving contact interaction model (CI) of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), encompassed within a Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations approach. In addition to the traditional rainbow-ladder truncation, a modified interaction kernel for the Bethe-Salpeter equation is adopted. The implemented kernel preserves the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities, while also providing additional freedom. Consequently, new tensor structures are generated in the corresponding interaction vertices, shifting the location of the mass poles appearing in the quark-photon and quark tensor vertex and yielding a notorious improvement in the final results. Despite the simplicity of the CI, the computed form factors and radii are compatible with recent lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a modified rainbow-ladder (MRL) truncation of the contact interaction (CI) model, which preserves vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities while introducing additional tensor structures in interaction vertices, yields pion form factors and radii compatible with recent lattice QCD simulations, despite the inherent simplicity of the contact interaction approach.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving contact interaction model within the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations framework, extending the traditional rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation with a modified kernel that includes non-ladder structures (identity, gamma5, and sigma-mu-nu matrices). They compute the quark scalar, vector, and tensor vertices by solving inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations, then evaluate the pion scalar, vector, and tensor form factors using the impulse approximation. For the scalar vertex, a monopole Ansatz is used instead of the direct solution due to the overly hard behavior of the computed dressing function.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_pi = 0.139 GeV, decay constant: f_pi = 0.103 GeV, with current quark mass m = 0.007 GeV.
* Rho meson mass: m_rho = 0.771 GeV (CI-MRL), compared to 0.929-0.953 GeV in CI-RL.
* Scalar meson mass: m_sigma = 0.815 GeV; tensor resonance mass m_T equals m_rho.
* Scalar charge radius: r_S^pi = (0.434 fm)^2.
* Vector charge radius: r_V^pi = (0.558 fm)^2, compared to experimental value 0.659(4) fm.
* Tensor charge radius: r_T^pi = (0.583 fm)^2.
* Model parameters: m_G = 0.127 GeV, tau_uv = 1/0.905 GeV^-1, tau_ir = 1/0.24 GeV^-1, xi = 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the CI-MRL truncation previously applied to vector mesons by Xing et al. (2021) to the calculation of pion scalar, vector, and tensor form factors. The results are compared with recent lattice QCD simulations by Alexandrou et al. (2021), showing improved agreement over traditional CI-RL calculations (Roberts et al., 2011; Wilson et al., 2011; Raya et al., 2021). The computed vector charge radius lies closer to the experimental value than earlier CI calculations, and the pattern r_S < r_V < r_T follows the inverse mass ordering of the corresponding meson poles. The approach enables future extensions to axial-vector meson properties and provides a framework for studying pion structure with higher-spin probes relevant to beyond Standard Model physics.
