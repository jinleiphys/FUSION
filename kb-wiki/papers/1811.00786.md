# Hyperonic stars and the symmetry energy

> In the present study we analyse the effect of the density dependence of the symmetry energy on the hyperonic content of neutron stars within a relativistic mean field description of stellar matter. For the $Λ$-hyperon, we consider parametrizations calibrated to $Λ$-hypernuclei. For the $Σ$ and $Ξ$-hyperons uncertainties that reflect the present lack of experimental information on $Σ$ and $Ξ$-hypernuclei are taken into account. We perform our study considering nuclear equations of state that predict two solar mass stars, and satisfy other well settled nuclear matter properties. The effect of the presence of hyperons on the radius, the direct Urca processes, and the cooling of accreting neutron stars are discussed. We show that some star properties are affected in a similar way by the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the hyperon content of the star. To disentangle these two effects it is essential to have a good knowledge of the equation of state at supra-saturation densities. The density dependence of the symmetry energy affects the order of appearance of the different hyperons, which may have direct implications on the neutron star cooling as different hyperonic neutrino processes processes may operate at the center of massive stars. For models which allow for the direct Urca process to operate, hyperonic and purely nucleonic ones are shown to have a similar luminosity when hyperons are included in agreement with modern experimental data. It is shown that for a density dependent hadronic model constrained by experimental, theoretical and observational data, the low-luminosity of SAX J$1808.4-3658$ can only be modelled for a hyperonic NS, suggesting that hyperons could be present in its core.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy and the hyperon content of neutron stars affect star properties in similar ways, making it essential to have good knowledge of the equation of state at supra-saturation densities to disentangle these two effects; for a density-dependent hadronic model constrained by experimental, theoretical and observational data, the low-luminosity of SAX J1808.4-3658 can only be modelled for a hyperonic neutron star, suggesting hyperons could be present in its core.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach to describe stellar matter, considering both models with constant couplings (FSU2, FSU2R, FSU2H, NL3, TM1 and their variants) and density-dependent couplings (DD2, DDME2). For the Lambda hyperon, they used parametrizations calibrated to reproduce binding energies of single and double Lambda-hypernuclei, while for Sigma and Xi hyperons they considered uncertainties in the hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter (U_Sigma = 0 or +30 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV). The inner crust equation of state was calculated within the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- The FSU2R model with hyperons does not describe two solar mass stars (not even 1.9 M_sun as indicated by the most recent measurements of PSR J1614-2230)
- For the Xi potential, U_Xi(n_0) = -18 MeV is taken, compatible with experimental data from the reaction 12C(K-,K+)12_Xi-Be which gives U_Xi(n_0) ~ -14 to -18 MeV
- The Lambda-potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation ranges from -30 MeV (FSU2-a) to -38 MeV (FSU2H-b)
- The crust-core transition density n_t ranges from 0.054 fm^-3 (FSU2) to 0.087 fm^-3 (FSU2H)
- For the TM1wrho family, the direct Urca process sets in at densities of order 2n_0 or below for L >= 75 MeV, corresponding to stars with mass equal to 1 M_sun or below

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Cavagnoli (2011) and Providencia (2013) on the interplay between symmetry energy and hyperon content, but with the improvement of using hyperonic models calibrated to existing experimental hypernuclei data as developed by Fortin (2017). It also follows the approach of using unified inner crust-core equations of state, since Fortin (2016) showed that non-unified equations of state give large uncertainties on the star radius. The paper addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by the discovery of two solar mass pulsars (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432), which many hyperonic models cannot reproduce, and discusses how this can be overcome by using sufficiently hard equations of state at high densities or by going beyond simple SU(6) symmetry for the isoscalar vector meson couplings, as explored by Weissenborn (2012, 2013).
