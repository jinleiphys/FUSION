# How well do we know the neutron-matter equation of state at the densities inside neutron stars? A Bayesian approach with correlated uncertainties

> We introduce a new framework for quantifying correlated uncertainties of the infinite-matter equation of state derived from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). Bayesian machine learning via Gaussian processes with physics-based hyperparameters allows us to efficiently quantify and propagate theoretical uncertainties of the equation of state, such as $χ$EFT truncation errors, to derived quantities. We apply this framework to state-of-the-art many-body perturbation theory calculations with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions up to fourth order in the $χ$EFT expansion. This produces the first statistically robust uncertainty estimates for key quantities of neutron stars. We give results up to twice nuclear saturation density for the energy per particle, pressure, and speed of sound of neutron matter, as well as for the nuclear symmetry energy and its derivative. At nuclear saturation density the predicted symmetry energy and its slope are consistent with experimental constraints.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a Bayesian framework using Gaussian processes to quantify correlated uncertainties in the chiral effective field theory (χEFT) equation of state for neutron matter, producing the first statistically robust uncertainty estimates for key neutron-star quantities up to twice nuclear saturation density, but the results are conditional on the specific choice of momentum cutoff (Λ = 500 MeV) and the particular many-body perturbation theory calculations used.

## Method

The authors performed many-body perturbation theory calculations with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions up to fourth order in the χEFT expansion (next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, N3LO). They trained Gaussian processes with physics-based hyperparameters on the order-by-order predictions of the energy per particle for pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, learning the magnitude of truncation errors and their correlations across densities and between observables. The framework then propagated these correlated uncertainties to derived quantities including pressure, speed of sound, symmetry energy, and its density derivative.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: S_v = 31.7 ± 1.1 MeV (1σ level)
- Slope parameter L at saturation density: L = 59.8 ± 4.1 MeV (1σ level)
- Speed of sound squared at 2n0: c_s²(2n0) ≈ 0.14 ± 0.08 (N2LO) and c_s²(2n0) ≈ 0.10 ± 0.07 (N3LO)
- EFT breakdown scale Λ_b consistent with 600 MeV
- Correlation length for pure neutron matter: ℓ = 0.97 fm⁻¹; for symmetric nuclear matter: ℓ = 0.48 fm⁻¹
- Correlation between truncation errors of pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter: ρ = 0.94
- Interpolation uncertainty set to 0.1% of total energy per particle (but ≥ 20 keV)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian truncation-error model developed by Melendez et al. (2019) to infinite nuclear matter, providing the first statistically rigorous uncertainty quantification for χEFT predictions of neutron-star matter. The results are consistent with experimental constraints from heavy-ion collisions, neutron-skin measurements, giant dipole resonances, and dipole polarizabilities compiled by Lattimer et al. (2014, 2012), and the predicted S_v–L correlation agrees with the joint experimental constraint. The framework enables future comparisons with observational data from NICER and LIGO-Virgo, and can be extended to arbitrary isospin asymmetry and finite temperature.
