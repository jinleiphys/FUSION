# Minimizing Selection Bias in Inclusive Jets in Heavy-Ion Collisions with Energy Correlators

> The first-ever measurement of energy correlators within inclusive jets produced in heavy-ion collisions, revealed by the CMS Collaboration, shows a clear enhancement at large angles relative to the proton-proton (p-p) baseline. However, interpreting this enhancement is complicated due to selection bias from energy loss, which also distorts the energy correlator heavy-ion to p-p ratio in the hadronization region, hindering our understanding of parton/hadron dynamics in a colored medium. In this Letter, we introduce a new ratio of energy correlator observables that removes the leading effects of selection bias from the two-point energy correlator spectrum (E2C). Pythia and Herwig simulations show that the impact of selection bias in the E2C is reduced by an order of magnitude, while sensitivity to any other medium modifications is retained. This quantity can be obtained directly from the experimental measurements presented by CMS, as illustrated in the accompanying note.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new ratio of energy correlator observables that removes the leading effects of selection bias from the two-point energy correlator spectrum (E2C) in heavy-ion inclusive jet samples, reducing the impact of selection bias by an order of magnitude while retaining sensitivity to other medium modifications. The method works under the assumption that energy loss is small relative to the jet transverse momentum and that medium modifications are restricted to angles larger than the hadronization scale.

## Method

The authors derive an "unbiasing function" C₂ from the ratio of heavy-ion to proton-proton cumulative distribution functions of the E2C, using the known scaling behavior of the E2C in both the perturbative and free-hadron-gas regimes. They validate the method using Pythia 8.230 and Herwig 7.2.2 Monte Carlo simulations of proton-proton collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, modeling energy loss as a constant shift in the jet transverse momentum spectrum by comparing jet samples with different pT ranges (e.g., 120-140 GeV versus 125-145 GeV for a 5 GeV shift, and 120-140 GeV versus 135-155 GeV for a 15 GeV shift).

## Key numbers

- Selection bias of ε = 5 GeV on ~120 GeV jets: the E2C ratio shows features up to approximately 10% magnitude; after applying C₂, these features are reduced to less than 1%.
- Selection bias of ε = 15 GeV on ~120 GeV jets: the E2C ratio shows deviations up to 20%; after applying C₂, the effect is reduced to around 1% at large angles and approximately 2% at smaller angles.
- The accuracy of the key approximation (Eq. 9 in the paper) was 10% or better for perturbatively large values of R_L and between 10% and 20% in the non-perturbative region, validated over the experimentally measured range 0 < R_L < 0.4.
- The simulations used approximately 44 million events for Pythia (tune CP5) and approximately 7 million events for Herwig (tune CH3), with jets clustered using the anti-kT algorithm with jet radius R = 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the first-ever measurement of energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions by the CMS Collaboration (CMS-PAS-HIN-23-004), which showed an enhancement at large angles relative to proton-proton baseline but was complicated by selection bias from energy loss. The method draws parallels with the αs-extraction strategy used in proton-proton collisions by Chen (2020), Komiske (2022), and Lee (2022), where specific correlator ratios eliminate classical dynamics from the spectrum. Prior approaches to mitigate selection bias include using γ/Z + jet events (Brewer, 2021; CMS, 2024), which are much rarer than inclusive jet events; this work enables the continued use of high-statistics inclusive jet samples to probe the quark-gluon plasma.
