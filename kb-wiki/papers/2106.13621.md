# Equivalence of dissipative and dissipationless dynamics of interacting quantum systems with its application to the unitary Fermi gas

> We analytically study quantum dissipative dynamics described by the Caldirola-Kanai model with inter-particle interactions. We have found that the dissipative quantum dynamics of the Caldirola-Kanai model can be exactly mapped to a dissipationless quantum dynamics under a negative external harmonic potential, even when the particles are strongly interacting. In particular, we show that the mapping is valid for the unitary Fermi gas, which is relevant for cold atoms and nuclear matters.

## Key claim

The authors show that the dissipative quantum dynamics of the Caldirola-Kanai model with inter-particle interactions can be exactly mapped to a dissipationless quantum dynamics under a negative external harmonic potential, but this mapping holds exactly only for two special cases: non-interacting systems and unitary Fermi gases where the s-wave scattering length is infinite.

## Method

The authors start from the classical equation of motion with a damping force and introduce a coordinate transformation x(t) = e^{-γt/2} y(t) that maps the dissipative Caldirola-Kanai model to a dissipationless system with an inverted harmonic potential. They extend this mapping from single-particle to many-particle quantum systems, and then apply it to the unitary Fermi gas by modeling the inter-particle interactions using either the Huang-Yang pseudo-potential or the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition, showing that the mapping holds when the s-wave scattering lengths satisfy a specific time-dependent relation that becomes trivial at unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The coordinate transformation relating dissipative and dissipationless systems is x(t) = e^{-γt/2} y(t), where γ is the dissipation rate
- The inverted harmonic potential term in the dissipationless Hamiltonian is -mγ²x²/8
- The s-wave scattering lengths of the two systems must satisfy a_ij^(H)(t) = e^{γt/2} a_ij^(CK)(t) for the mapping to hold
- For the unitary gas, the mapping is valid when a_ij^(H) = a_ij^(CK) = ±∞

## Context

This work extends the single-particle mapping previously discussed by Kerner (1958), Baskoutas, Jannussis, and Mignani (1994), and Huang and Wu (1998) to many-particle systems with strong interactions. The mapping is particularly relevant for the unitary Fermi gas, which has been realized in ultracold atoms and is important for understanding neutron matter and nuclear phenomena. The result enables analytical study of dissipative quantum dynamics in strongly interacting systems relevant to cold atoms and nuclear reactions, where dissipation plays essential roles in non-equilibrium dynamics.
