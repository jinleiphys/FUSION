# Excited Baryons and Chiral Symmetry Breaking of QCD

## Key claim
The mass splitting within the same parity pair of excited baryons is directly controlled by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking of QCD, as demonstrated by the fact that reducing the quark condensate toward zero causes the positive- and negative-parity states to become degenerate. This link is established through parity-projected QCD sum rules where the term responsible for the splitting involves only dimension-odd condensates such as the quark condensate.

## Method
The author computed N* masses in the spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 sectors using two non-perturbative methods: lattice QCD and QCD sum rules. In lattice QCD, parity projection was applied to correlation functions by taking traces with (1±γ₄)/4 to isolate positive- and negative-parity states, and spin projection was used to separate spin-3/2 from spin-1/2 contributions in the spin-3/2 interpolating field. In QCD sum rules, a Monte Carlo-based analysis explored the predictive ability of the method, and parity-projected sum rules were derived that separate the contributions of positive- and negative-parity states through the sign of the B term, which contains only dimension-odd condensates.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the spin-3/2+ N* mass to the nucleon ground state mass was extracted from lattice correlation functions as a function of (π/ρ)², with preliminary results heading in the direction of experimental values where available.
- The standard value of the quark condensate parameter used in the QCD sum rule analysis is a = -(2π)² ⟨q̄q⟩ = 0.52 GeV³.
- As the quark condensate is decreased from its standard value, the mass of N(1/2⁻) falls faster than N(1/2⁺), and in the limit ⟨q̄q⟩ = 0 the two states become degenerate.

## Context
This work extends prior lattice QCD studies of the N* spectrum by Lee (1999, 2000) and others (Derek 1995, Sas 2000, DGR 2000, 2001) that focused primarily on the spin-1/2 sector, by calculating excited baryon states in the spin-3/2 sector. The parity-projected sum rule approach builds on earlier work by Jido (1996, 1997) and Lee (2002) and establishes a direct connection between parity splitting and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking that was previously only suggested. The results enable systematic study of the baryon spectrum from first principles using both lattice QCD and QCD sum rules, with the ability to dial quark masses and dissect the degrees of freedom responsible for the observed mass splittings.
