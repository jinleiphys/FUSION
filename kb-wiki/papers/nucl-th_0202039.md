# Chiral 2pi exchange at order four and peripheral NN scattering

> We calculate the impact of the complete set of two-pion exchange contributions at chiral order four (also known as next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, N3LO) on peripheral partial waves of nucleon-nucleon scattering. Our calculations are based upon the analytical studies by Kaiser. It turns out that the contribution of order four is substantially smaller than the one of order three, indicating convergence of the chiral expansion. We compare the prediction from chiral pion-exchange with the corresponding one from conventional meson-theory as represented by the Bonn Full Model and find, in general, good agreement. Our calculations provide a sound basis for investigating the issue whether the low-energy constants determined from pi-N lead to reasonable predictions for NN.

## Key claim

The chiral two-pion exchange contribution at order four (N3LO) is substantially smaller than the order-three (NNLO) contribution, indicating convergence of the chiral expansion for peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering, though the authors note that the order-three contribution was itself several times larger than the order-two contribution, raising prior questions about convergence.

## Method

The authors applied the analytic expressions for two-pion exchange at chiral order four derived by Kaiser using covariant perturbation theory and dimensional regularization, and computed the resulting on-shell NN amplitudes for peripheral partial waves (orbital angular momentum L ≥ 3). They included one-loop diagrams with c_i^2, c_i/M_N, and 1/M_N^2 corrections, as well as two-loop contributions, and combined these with one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange to calculate phase shifts via partial-wave decomposition in a helicity-state basis.

## Key numbers

- The combined effect from the numerically integrated two-loop contributions W_C^(b), V_T^(b), and V_S^(b) on NN phase shifts is smaller than 0.1 deg in F and G waves and smaller than 0.01 deg in H waves at T_lab = 300 MeV.
- The average pion mass used for irreducible 2π exchange contributions is m_π = 138.039 MeV.
- The charged and neutral pion masses used for charge-dependent OPE are m_π± = 139.5702 MeV and m_π0 = 134.9766 MeV.
- The nucleon mass used in the iterated OPE is M_N = 938.9182 MeV (twice the reduced mass of proton and neutron).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to the NN interaction initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordóñez (1992), van Kolck (1994, 1999), and others, by adding the complete two-pion exchange contributions at order four to the existing order-three (NNLO) calculations of Kaiser et al. (1997, 1998, 1999, 2001). The results provide a sound basis for investigating whether low-energy constants determined from πN scattering lead to reasonable predictions for NN scattering, and enable future development of quantitative chiral NN potentials that include contact terms of order four consistently with the two-pion exchange contributions.
