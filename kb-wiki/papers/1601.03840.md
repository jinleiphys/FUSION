# Photoproduction of $KΛ$ on the proton

> Kaon photoproduction on the proton is studied in the resonance region using an isobar model. The higher-spin nucleon (3/2 and 5/2) and hyperon (3/2) resonances were included in the model utilizing the consistent formalism by Pascalutsa and they were found to play an important role in data description. The spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 hyperon resonances in combination with the Born terms contribute significantly to the background part of the amplitude. Various forms of the hadron form factor were considered in the construction and the dipole and multidipole forms were selected as those most suitable for the data description. Model parameters were fitted to new experimental data from CLAS, LEPS, and GRAAL collaborations and two versions of the model, BS1 and BS2, were chosen. Both models provide a good overall description of the data for the c.m. energies from the threshold up to 2.4 GeV. Predicted cross sections of the models at very small kaon angles being consistent with results of the Saclay-Lyon model indicate that the models could be also successful in predicting the hypernucleus production cross sections. Although kaon photoproduction takes place in the third-resonance region with many resonant states, the total number of included resonances, 15 and 16, is quite moderate and it is comparable with numbers of resonances in other models. The set of chosen nucleon resonances overlaps well with the set of the most probable contributing states determined in the Bayesian analysis with the Regge-plus-resonance model. Particularly, we confirm that the missing resonances $P_{13}(1900)$ and $D_{13}(1875)$ do play important role in the description of data. However, the spin-1/2 state $P_{11}(1880)$ included in the Bayesian analysis was replaced in our analysis with the near-mass spin-5/2 state $N^*(1860)$, recently considered by the Particle Data Group.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their new isobar model, using the consistent Pascalutsa formalism for higher-spin resonances, provides a good overall description of kaon photoproduction data from threshold up to 2.4 GeV center-of-mass energy, but they qualify that the model requires a moderate number of resonances (15 or 16) and that the choice of hadron form factor (dipole or multidipole) is critical to achieving this description.

## Method

The authors constructed a single-channel isobar model for the reaction γp → K⁺Λ, including Born terms and s-channel nucleon resonances, t-channel kaon resonances (K*(892) and K₁(1270)), and u-channel hyperon resonances with spins 1/2 and 3/2. Higher-spin nucleon resonances (3/2 and 5/2) and hyperon resonances (3/2) were treated using the consistent gauge-invariant formalism of Pascalutsa, which removes nonphysical spin-1/2 components from the Rarita-Schwinger propagator. Model parameters were fitted to experimental data from CLAS, LEPS, and GRAAL collaborations, and two versions (BS1 and BS2) were selected after testing dipole, multidipole, Gauss, and multidipole Gauss hadron form factors.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range covered: from threshold up to 2.4 GeV
- Total number of included resonances: 15 (BS1) and 16 (BS2)
- Cut-off parameter values for hadron form factors lie in the range approximately 0.7 to 3.0 GeV
- Specific resonances included: N3 (S₁₁(1535), mass 1535 MeV, width 150 MeV), N4 (S₁₁(1650), mass 1655 MeV, width 150 MeV), N7 (P₁₃(1720), mass 1720 MeV, width 270 MeV), N9 (F₁₅(1680), mass 1685 MeV, width 130 MeV), P1 (P₁₁(1880), mass 1870 MeV, width 235 MeV), P2 (P₁₃(1900), mass 1900 MeV, width 500 MeV), P3 (F₁₅(2000), mass 2050 MeV, width 198 MeV), P4 (D₁₃(1875), mass 1875 MeV, width 220 MeV), P5 (F₁₅(1860), mass 1860 MeV, width 270 MeV)
- Hyperon resonances included: Λ(1405) mass 1405 MeV width 50 MeV, Λ(1520) mass 1519.54 MeV width 15.6 MeV, Λ(1600) mass 1600 MeV width 150 MeV, Λ(1670) mass 1670 MeV width 35 MeV, Λ(1690) mass 1690 MeV width 60 MeV, Λ(1800) mass 1800 MeV width 300 MeV, Λ(1810) mass 1810 MeV width 150 MeV, Λ(1890) mass 1890 MeV width 100 MeV, Σ(1660) mass 1660 MeV width 100 MeV, Σ(1670) mass 1670 MeV width 60 MeV, Σ(1750) mass 1750 MeV width 90 MeV, Σ(1940) mass 1940 MeV width 220 MeV
- Kaon resonances: K*(892) mass 891.66 MeV width 50.8 MeV, K₁(1270) mass 1272 MeV width 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Saclay-Lyon (SL) and Gent isobar models by incorporating the consistent Pascalutsa formalism for higher-spin baryon resonances, which previously had been applied mainly in the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model by the Gent group (RPR-2011A). The authors confirm the importance of the missing resonances P₁₃(1900) and D₁₃(1875) identified in the Bayesian analysis of the RPR model, but replace the spin-1/2 state P₁₁(1880) with the near-mass spin-5/2 state N*(1860) recently considered by the Particle Data Group. The predicted cross sections at very small kaon angles are consistent with the Saclay-Lyon model, suggesting the models could be used to predict hypernucleus production cross sections.
