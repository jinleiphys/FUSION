# Hard thermal loops for soft or collinear external momenta

> We consider finite temperature 1-loop diagrams with hard loop momenta and an arbitrary number of external gauge fields when the external momenta are either soft, or near the light cone and nearly collinear with the loop momentum. We obtain a recursion relation for these diagrams which we translate into an equation for their generating functional. By integrating out the soft fields while keeping two collinear ones we find an integral equation, originally due to Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe, which sums the bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation contribution to the thermal photon production rate.

## Key claim

The paper derives an integral equation for the photon polarization tensor that sums the bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation contributions to the thermal photon production rate, but only under the condition that the external momenta are either soft (of order \(gT\)) or near the light cone and nearly collinear with the loop momentum. The derivation avoids including the infrared-divergent thermal width in quark propagators, using only the asymptotic mass, and relies on the approximation that the connected part of the gluon-field average vanishes at leading order.

## Method

The authors work in the imaginary time formalism at finite temperature, considering 1-loop diagrams with hard loop momenta (of order \(T\)) and an arbitrary number of external gauge fields. They approximate scalar propagators by keeping only the pole that gives a collinear enhancement, then partial-fraction the product of two such propagators to obtain a recursion relation linking an \(n\)-point diagram to the difference of two \((n-1)\)-point diagrams. This recursion is translated into an equation for the generating functional (the induced current), which is then specialized by distinguishing soft gluon fields from collinear photon fields. After iterating the integral equation once and integrating out the soft gluons using their HTL-resummed propagators, a closed integral equation for the reduced polarization tensor is obtained.

## Key numbers

* The asymptotic mass for scalars and fermions in representation \(r\) is \(m^2 = \frac{1}{4} C_2(r) g^2 T^2\), with the quadratic Casimir \(C_2(r) = (N^2 - 1)/(2N)\) for an SU(\(N\)) gauge theory.
* The Debye mass is \(m_{\rm D}^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{6} (2N + N_s + N_f)\) for an SU(\(N\)) theory with \(N_s\) complex scalars and \(N_f\) Dirac fermions.
* The integral over the gluon propagator yields \(\frac{i}{4} T \left[ -\frac{1}{\vec{q}_\perp^2} + \frac{1}{\vec{q}_\perp^2 + m_{\rm D}^2} \right]\) after performing the Matsubara sum and the integral over \(q_\parallel\).
* The final integral equation for the reduced polarization tensor \(\widehat{\Pi}_\nu\) contains a factor \(i C_2(r) g^2 T\) multiplying the transverse-momentum integral.

## Context

This work extends the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective theory approach (Blaizot and Iancu, 1993) to the case of collinear external momenta, where the standard Vlasov equation is replaced by a non-local equation. The resulting integral equation reproduces the one previously obtained by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) for the photon production rate, which sums the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect in bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation. The derivation provides a systematic field-theoretic framework for integrating out hard modes in a soft and collinear background, enabling future calculations of other observables such as dilepton production.
