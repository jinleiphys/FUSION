# Fractal structure of hadrons and non-extensive statistics

> The role played by non-extensive thermodynamics in physical systems has been under intense debate for the last decades. Some possible mechanisms that could give rise to non-extensive statistics have been formulated along the last few years, in particular the existence of a fractal structure in thermodynamic functions for hadronic systems. We investigate the properties of such fractal thermodynamical systems, in particular the fractal scale invariance is discussed in terms of the Callan-Symanzik~equation. Finally, we propose a diagrammatic method for calculations of relevant quantities.

# Fractal structure of hadrons and non-extensive statistics

## Key claim
The fractal structure of thermodynamic systems (thermofractals) naturally leads to non-extensive Tsallis statistics, but this derivation depends on the self-similarity condition that the probability distribution at one hierarchical level is proportional to that at the next level. The entropic index q emerges from the fractal dimension and the effective number of constituent subsystems, not from an arbitrary parameter.

## Method
The authors model hadronic systems as thermofractals: self-similar thermodynamic objects where each subsystem is itself a thermofractal with kinetic and internal energy components. They impose scale invariance and self-similarity conditions on the energy distributions across hierarchical levels, then solve the resulting integral equations to derive the Tsallis-type distribution. They further derive a Callan-Symanzik equation for the vertex function of thermofractals and propose a diagrammatic method for computing partition functions and related quantities.

## Key numbers
- Fractal dimension of hadrons: D = 0.69, obtained from thermofractal analysis and close to values from intermittence analysis by Ghosh (1998).
- The relation between the entropic index q and the fractal parameters: q - 1 = 2(1 - ν)/(3N), where ν is a scaling exponent and N is the number of constituent subsystems.
- The scale factor between hierarchical levels: λ_n = E^(n)/E^(0) = (1/N)^(n/(1-D)).
- The anomalous dimension for thermofractals: d = 1 - D.

## Context
This work extends the thermofractal formalism developed by Deppman (2016) and connects it to the Tsallis non-extensive statistics framework introduced by Tsallis (1987). The authors apply the formalism to the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) approach for QCD thermodynamics in the confined phase, following earlier work by Megias (2015) and Menezes (2014). The results enable a field-theoretical treatment of thermofractals, with a planned detailed study by Deppman (in preparation) that will develop the connection to quantum field theory scaling properties.
