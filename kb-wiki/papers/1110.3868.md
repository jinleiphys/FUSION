# Effects of Fock term, tensor coupling and baryon structure variation on a neutron star

> The equation of state for neutron matter is calculated within relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation. The tensor couplings of vector mesons to baryons are included, and the change of baryon internal structure in matter is also considered using the quark-meson coupling model. We obtain the maximum neutron-star mass of $\sim 2.0 M_\odot$, which is consistent with the recently observed, precise mass, $1.97\pm0.04 M_{\odot}$. The Fock contribution is very important and, in particular, the inclusion of tensor coupling is vital to obtain such large mass. The baryon structure variation in matter also enhances the mass of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that including the Fock term, tensor couplings of vector mesons, and baryon structure variation in matter can produce a maximum neutron-star mass of approximately 2.0 solar masses, consistent with the observed mass of 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses, but only when all three effects are included together; the tensor coupling in the Fock term is specifically identified as vital for reaching such a large mass.

## Method

The equation of state for neutron matter was calculated within relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, including the tensor couplings of vector mesons (omega and rho) to baryons and the exchange of pions in the Fock term. The change of baryon internal structure in matter was incorporated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and its chiral extension (CQMC), which introduces a density-dependent sigma-baryon coupling constant reflecting the scalar polarizability of the baryon. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was then solved to obtain neutron-star masses and radii, with the coupling constants adjusted to reproduce nuclear saturation properties and hyperon potentials from hypernuclear data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass of approximately 2.0 solar masses, consistent with the observed mass 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses
- In the Hartree-Fock calculation with hyperons (npY), the maximum masses are: QHD+NL(HF) = 1.92 solar masses, QMC(HF) = 1.95 solar masses, CQMC(HF) = 2.02 solar masses
- In the Hartree-Fock calculation without hyperons (np), the maximum masses are: QHD+NL(HF) = 2.15 solar masses, QMC(HF) = 2.11 solar masses, CQMC(HF) = 2.23 solar masses
- The Xi- hyperon first appears at a density of approximately 0.49 fm^-3 in the QMC Hartree-Fock calculation
- In symmetric nuclear matter at normal density (0.15 fm^-3), the nucleon effective mass ratio M_N*/M_N is 0.73 for QMC(HF) and 0.70 for CQMC(HF)
- The symmetry energy at saturation is 36.9 MeV for QMC(HF) and 37.4 MeV for CQMC(HF)

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations of neutron matter in Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) by Huber et al., which ignored baryon structure variation, and in the QMC model by Rikovska-Stone et al. (2006), which missed the tensor coupling and the space component of baryon self-energy. The results enable reconciliation of the observed 1.97 solar mass pulsar (Demorest et al., 2010) with theoretical predictions that include hyperons, which typically soften the equation of state and reduce maximum masses. The study opens the door to future inclusion of Delta isobar degrees of freedom, K-meson exchange, meson condensates, and quark matter.
