# Long standing problem of 210Bi and the realistic neutron-proton effective interaction

> The odd-odd nucleus 210Bi is studied within the framework of the shell model using effective two-body matrix elements derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The experimental energies of the proton-neutron multiplet ph9/2 ng9/2 are remarkably well reproduced by the theory, which accounts for the 1- state being the ground state instead of the 0- predicted by the Nordheim strong coupling rule. It is shown that the core-polarization effects are crucial to produce this inversion. The similarity between neutron-proton multiplets in the 132Sn and 208Pb regions is discussed in connection with the effective interaction.

## Key claim

The shell-model effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, with no adjustable parameters, reproduces the correct ordering of the 0- and 1- states in 210Bi, solving a long-standing problem that earlier realistic calculations (Herling and Kuo, 1972) had failed to resolve. The key condition is that core-polarization effects from 1p1h excitations, computed with a sufficiently large number of intermediate states, are crucial to produce the inversion.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for 210Bi assuming a 208Pb closed core, with valence protons occupying the six levels of the 82-126 shell and valence neutrons the seven levels of the 126-184 shell. The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the Vlow-k approach (cutoff momentum 2.2 fm-1), followed by a Q-box folded-diagrams expansion including one- and two-body diagrams up to second order, with the Lee-Suzuki iteration method summing folded diagrams to all orders. Single-particle energies were taken from experiment, and the OXBASH shell-model code was used for the calculations.

## Key numbers

- Calculated ground-state binding energy of 210Bi: 8.347 ± 0.003 MeV, compared to experimental value 8.404 ± 0.002 MeV (Audi, 2003)
- Discrepancies between calculated and experimental excitation energies for the πh9/2 νg9/2 multiplet are less than 50 keV for all states except the 9- state, which is predicted 90 keV above experiment
- The πh9/2 νg9/2 configuration dominates the yrast state wave functions with 96% to 100% purity, except for the 1- state where it is 91%
- The oscillator parameter used was 6.88 MeV, obtained from ℏω = 45 A-1/3 - 25 A-2/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful realistic shell-model study of 134Sb (Coraggio, 2006) to the 208Pb region, where the πh9/2 νg9/2 multiplet in 210Bi is the counterpart of the πg7/2 νf7/2 multiplet in 134Sb. It resolves a problem that had persisted since the 1960s, where early empirical calculations (Kim, 1963; Mello, 1963; Hughes, 1966) required tensor-force components, and the realistic calculation by Herling and Kuo (1972) using the Hamada-Johnston potential failed to reproduce the 0-1- inversion. The results enable further tests of realistic effective interactions in heavy nuclei and demonstrate the similarity between neutron-proton multiplets in the 132Sn and 208Pb regions.
