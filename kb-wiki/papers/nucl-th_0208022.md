# Off-shell omega production in proton-proton collisions near threshold

> The omega production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is described through the decay of intermediate nuclear resonances. Close to threshold the pp --> pp omega cross section is dominated by the off-shell production of omega mesons with masses far below the physical omega mass of 782 MeV. A crucial role plays thereby the N^*(1535) resonance. Due to a strong N omega decay mode this resonance leads to off-shell contributions which are in the vicinity of the threshold about one order of magnitude larger than the experimentally measurable contribution from only the omega peak. After a subtraction of the theoretical "background" from off-shell omega production the available data are accurately reproduced over the entire energy range from very close to threshold up to several GeV above threshold. The scenario of a weaker N^*(1535) --> N omega decay mode which is still consistent with electro- and photo-production data is discussed as well. In the latter case the off-shell contributions to the pp --> pp omega cross section are substantially reduced but the description of the experimental cross section is poor above threshold.

## Key claim

The near-threshold pp → ppω cross section is dominated by off-shell ω meson production with masses far below 782 MeV, mediated primarily by the N*(1535) resonance; the experimentally measurable ω peak contribution is only obtained after subtracting this theoretical off-shell background, and the strong N*(1535)Nω coupling scenario reproduces data well while a weaker coupling consistent with electro- and photoproduction data gives a poor description above threshold.

## Method

The authors describe ω production in pp collisions via a two-step mechanism: NN → NR followed by R → Nω, where R are nucleon resonances. The RNω coupling strengths are determined from an extended Vector Meson Dominance (eVMD) model fitted to radiative and mesonic decay data, and the resonance production cross sections in pp collisions are taken from fits to pion, double-pion, η, and ρ production data. The cross section is computed by integrating over the off-shell masses of both the intermediate resonance and the ω meson, using Breit-Wigner distributions and energy-dependent widths, with the NN final-state interaction effectively included in the phenomenological resonance production matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The physical ω meson pole mass is 782 MeV; the ω width at this mass is 8.4 MeV.
- At excess energy ε = 3.8 MeV, the differential cross section shows no clear ω peak structure; at ε = 19.6 and 30.1 MeV the peak becomes visible, and at ε = 173 MeV the off-shell strength is moderate.
- The N*(1535) resonance lies 167 MeV below the ω pole mass; its off-shell partial Nω width reaches 16 GeV above the ω threshold for the strong coupling scenario.
- The off-shell contribution from the N*(1535) is about one order of magnitude larger than the experimentally measurable ω peak contribution near threshold.
- The ω decay width parameterization uses exponent a = 8 for M < mω and a = 10 for M > mω.

## Context

This work extends resonance-model approaches previously applied to ρ meson production and dilepton production in pp reactions (Friman 1997, Post 2001, Krivoruchenko 2002, Faessler 2000) to ω production, using the same eVMD framework. The calculations are compared to SATURNE data (Hibou 1999) at excess energies 4–30 MeV and COSY-TOF data (COSY-TOF Collaboration 2001) at 92 and 173 MeV. The results challenge the earlier on-shell treatment by Nakayama (1998) which omitted off-shell effects, and enable future studies of in-medium ω meson modifications in heavy-ion collisions by providing a baseline understanding of the elementary production mechanism.
