# Central Nucleon-Nucleon Potential and Chiral Scalar Form Factor

> The central two-pion exchange NN potential at large distances is studied in the framework of relativistic chiral symmetry and related directly to the nucleon scalar form factor, which describes the mass density of its pion cloud. This relationship is well supported by phenomenology and allows the dependence of the asymptotic potential on the nucleon mass to be assessed. Results in the heavy baryon limit are about 25% larger than those corresponding to the empirical nucleon mass in the region of physical interest. This indicates that it is very important to keep this mass finite in precise descriptions of the NN system and supports the efficacy of the relativistic chiral framework. One also estimates the contribution of subleading effects and presents a simple discussions of the role of the quark condensate in this problem.

## Key claim
The central two-pion exchange NN potential at large distances can be directly related to the nucleon scalar form factor within a relativistic chiral framework, but the heavy baryon limit yields results about 25% larger than those using the empirical nucleon mass in the region of physical interest, indicating that finite nucleon mass is essential for precise NN descriptions.

## Method
The author derived the asymptotic central two-pion exchange potential from the isoscalar NN scattering amplitude containing two intermediate pions, subtracting the iterated one-pion exchange to avoid double counting. The potential was expressed in terms of the nucleon scalar form factor and subthreshold coefficients from pion-nucleon scattering, then evaluated both in the relativistic framework and in the heavy baryon (infinite nucleon mass) limit for comparison. Fourier transforms were performed to obtain coordinate-space potentials, and the leading chiral contribution was compared with the Argonne phenomenological potential.

## Key numbers
- Heavy baryon limit results are about 25% larger than those using the empirical nucleon mass (938.28 MeV) in the asymptotic region of the coordinate-space potential.
- In momentum space, the heavy baryon limit deviates from the relativistic result by about 30% for the empirical nucleon mass.
- For nucleon masses of 10 times the empirical mass, deviations from the heavy baryon limit are about 10%; for 100 times, about 2%; for 1000 times, less than 1%.
- The ratio of the leading chiral potential to the Argonne phenomenological central potential shows typical discrepancies of about 20% in the range 1 fm to 4 fm.
- Empirical subthreshold coefficients from three analyses: a00+ = -1.46 ± 0.10 μ⁻¹ (H83), -1.30 ± 0.02 μ⁻¹ (KH), -1.27 ± 0.03 μ⁻¹ (PA); a01+ = 1.14 ± 0.02 μ⁻³ (H83), 1.35 ± 0.14 μ⁻³ (KH), 1.27 ± 0.03 μ⁻³ (PA).
- The scalar form factor at the Cheng-Dashen point σ(2μ²) is 64 ± 8 MeV (H83), 88 ± 15 MeV (KH), 90 ± 8 MeV (PA).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic chiral approach to two-pion exchange potentials developed by Robilotta (1999) and contrasts with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997) and by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1996). The author shows that the relativistic framework of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) automatically yields accurate leading contributions, whereas heavy baryon calculations would require extension to higher orders to match the same precision. The comparison with the Argonne potential by Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995) demonstrates that the simple scalar form factor mechanism accounts for the leading central potential, with remaining gaps of order expected from chiral corrections.
