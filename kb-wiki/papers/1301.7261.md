# Exotic hadrons and hadron-hadron interactions in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the exotic hadron structure and hadron-hadron interactions in view of heavy ion collisions. First, we demonstrate that a hadronic molecule with a large spatial size would be produced more abundantly in the coalescence model compared with the statistical model result. Secondly, we constrain the Lambda-Lambda interaction by using the recently measured Lambda-Lambda correlation data. We find that the RHIC-STAR data favor the Lambda-Lambda scattering parameters in the range 1/a_0 <= -0.8 fm^{-1} and r_{eff} >= 3 fm.

## Key claim

The paper argues that heavy ion collisions can serve as a "hadron size ruler" because the coalescence model predicts enhanced production yields for spatially extended hadronic molecules compared to compact multi-quark states, relative to the statistical model baseline. The authors also constrain the Lambda-Lambda interaction from RHIC-STAR correlation data, finding that the data favor scattering parameters in the range 1/a_0 <= -0.8 fm^{-1} and r_eff >= 3 fm, but note these results are obtained in a simple setup without full treatment of feeddown, flow, or coupled-channel effects.

## Method

The authors compare hadron yields from the coalescence model and the statistical model at RHIC and LHC energies, using harmonic oscillator wave functions with frequency parameters omega=550 MeV for u,d quarks and fitted values omega_s=519 MeV, omega_c=385 MeV, omega_b=338 MeV for strange, charm, and bottom quarks respectively. For the Lambda-Lambda interaction, they compute two-particle correlation functions from a Gaussian source profile using several interaction models (Nijmegen models, quark model fss2, Ehime potential) and compare with RHIC-STAR preliminary data, including a simple prescription for Sigma^0 decay feeddown effects.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence/statistical model ratio R^CS_h for normal hadrons lies in the range 0.2 < R^CS_h < 2 (gray band).
- For compact multi-quark states, adding an s-wave, p-wave, or d-quark gives suppression factors of 0.36, 0.093, or 0.029 respectively relative to normal hadrons.
- The optimal oscillator frequency for two-body s-wave coalescence at T=170 MeV, mu=500 MeV, R=5 fm is hbar*omega=16 MeV, much smaller than normal hadron values of hbar*omega=300-600 MeV.
- The Lambda-Lambda bond energy in double hypernucleus ^6_{Lambda Lambda}He is Delta B_{Lambda Lambda} approx 0.6 MeV.
- Scattering parameters from the Nagara event: (a_0, r_eff)=(-0.77 fm, 6.59 fm) from Filikhin and Gal, or (-0.575 fm, 6.45 fm) from Hiyama et al.
- The RHIC-STAR data favor Lambda-Lambda scattering parameters in the range 1/a_0 <= -0.8 fm^{-1} and r_eff >= 3 fm.
- The apparent source size of Lambda from correlation data is around R ~ 2 fm without flow, or 3 fm <= R <= 4 fm with flow parameter eta_f=0.5.
- The Sigma^0 decay feeddown effect can be simulated by multiplying C(q)-1 by a factor of 0.39.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Chen et al. (2004) for using coalescence models in heavy ion collisions and the earlier suggestion by Greiner and Muller (1989) that resonance parameters can be fixed from correlation measurements. The Lambda-Lambda interaction analysis builds on the Nagara event observation of double Lambda hypernucleus ^6_{Lambda Lambda}He and prior work by Filikhin and Gal and Hiyama et al. that extracted scattering parameters from the bond energy. The results constrain the long-standing search for the H dibaryon originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) and enable future studies combining feeddown, flow, and coupled-channel effects for more precise determination of the Lambda-Lambda interaction.
