# Fluctuation dynamics in a relativistic fluid with a critical point

> To describe dynamics of bulk and fluctuations near the QCD critical point we develop general relativistic fluctuation formalism for a fluid carrying baryon charge. Feedback of fluctuations modifies hydrodynamic coefficients including bulk viscosity and conductivity and introduces nonlocal and non-instantaneous terms in constitutive equations. We perform necessary ultraviolet (short-distance) renormalization to obtain cutoff independent deterministic equations suitable for numerical implementation. We use the equations to calculate the universal non-analytic small-frequency dependence of transport coefficients due to fluctuations (long-time tails). Focusing on the critical mode we show how this general formalism matches existing Hydro+ description of fluctuations near the QCD critical point and nontrivially extends it inside and outside of the critical region.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general relativistic fluctuation formalism for a fluid carrying baryon charge, showing that the feedback of fluctuations modifies hydrodynamic coefficients and introduces nonlocal, non-instantaneous terms in constitutive equations; the formalism extends the Hydro+ description of fluctuations near the QCD critical point and can be extended further to a proposed "Hydro++" approach that remains valid closer to the critical point than Hydro+.

## Method

The authors convert stochastic hydrodynamic equations into a hierarchy of equations for equal-time correlation functions, truncated at two-point correlators, with the truncation controlled by the same expansion parameter as the gradient expansion in hydrodynamics. They perform ultraviolet renormalization by subtracting short-distance singularities to obtain cutoff-independent deterministic equations for the Wigner functions of fluctuations, and then compare the resulting equations to known results for conformal fluids, nonrelativistic fluids, and the Hydro+ formalism.

## Key numbers

- The physically relevant magnitude of fluctuations, obtained by averaging over scales \(\ell_* = 1/q_*\), scales as \(q_*^{3/2} \sim k^{3/4}\), where \(k\) is the background wave-number.
- The contribution of \(n\)-point functions due to higher-order nonlinearities comes at order \(k^{3n/4}\).
- For a conformal fluid, \(\dot T = c_s^2 = 1/3\), \(\eps_m = \alpha_p = \dot c_s = 0\), and \(\dot c_p = 1\).
- For a nonrelativistic ideal gas with \(T \ll M\), \(c_s^2 = \gamma T/M\) and \(\dot T = \gamma - 1\).
- Near the critical point, the relaxation rate of the slowest non-hydrodynamic mode scales as \(\Gamma_\xi \sim \xi^{-3}\), and the next-to-slowest mode relaxation rate scales as \(\Gamma_\xi' \sim \xi^{-2}\).
- The Kawasaki function \(K(x) = \frac{3}{4x^2}[1 + x^2 + (x^3 - x^{-1})\arctan x]\) describes the critical contribution to the relaxation rate of \(N_{mm}\).
- The \(q\)-dependent relaxation rate is \(\Gamma(q) = 2\left(\frac{\kappa_0}{c_p} + \frac{T}{6\pi\eta\xi}K(q\xi)\right) q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the deterministic fluctuation hydrodynamics method developed by An, Basar, Stephanov, and Yee (2019) for chargeless fluids to fluids carrying a conserved baryon charge, relevant for QCD critical point searches. The formalism reproduces the Hydro+ description of Stephanov and Yin (2018) near the critical point and extends it by including the next-to-slowest modes (\(N_{m\T{i}}\) and \(N_{\T{i}\T{j}}\)), allowing validity up to \(k \sim \xi^{-1}\) rather than the \(k \sim \xi^{-2}\) limit of Hydro+. The results also match the nonrelativistic fluctuation hydrodynamics of Andreev (1978) and Andreev (1980) in the appropriate limit, while correcting omissions of off-equilibrium correlator components in those works.
