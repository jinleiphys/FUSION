# A Realistic Description of Nucleon-Nucleon and Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions in the SU_6 Quark Model

> We upgrade a SU_6 quark-model description for the nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions by improving the effective meson-exchange potentials acting between quarks. For the scalar- and vector-meson exchanges, the momentum-dependent higher-order term is incorporated to reduce the attractive effect of the central interaction at higher energies. The single-particle potentials of the nucleon and Lambda, predicted by the G-matrix calculation, now have proper repulsive behavior in the momentum region q_1=5 - 20 fm^-1. A moderate contribution of the spin-orbit interaction from the scalar-meson exchange is also included. As to the vector mesons, a dominant contribution is the quadratic spin-orbit force generated from the rho-meson exchange. The nucleon-nucleon phase shifts at the non-relativistic energies up to T_lab=350 MeV are greatly improved especially for the 3E states. The low-energy observables of the nucleon-nucleon and the hyperon-nucleon interactions are also reexamined. The isospin symmetry breaking and the Coulomb effect are properly incorporated in the particle basis. The essential feature of the Lambda N - Sigma N coupling is qualitatively similar to that obtained from the previous models. The nuclear saturation properties and the single-particle potentials of the nucleon, Lambda and Sigma are reexamined through the G-matrix calculation. The single-particle potential of the Sigma hyperon is weakly repulsive in symmetric nuclear matter. The single-particle spin-orbit strength for the Lambda particle is very small, in comparison with that of the nucleons, due to the strong antisymmetric spin-orbit force generated from the Fermi-Breit interaction.

## Key claim

The upgraded SU_6 quark model (fss2) achieves a significantly improved description of nucleon-nucleon phase shifts up to T_lab=350 MeV, particularly for the triplet-even (3E) states, by incorporating momentum-dependent Bryan-Scott terms and spin-orbit contributions from scalar-meson exchange, while the abstract's claim of "greatly improved" phase shifts is qualified by the fact that the model still does not account for inelastic channels and shows deviations at higher energies up to T_lab=800 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a quark-quark interaction Hamiltonian combining a confinement potential, the Fermi-Breit interaction, and effective meson-exchange potentials (EMEP) for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons, with the new feature being momentum-dependent higher-order terms and quadratic spin-orbit forces from rho-meson exchange. They solved the resonating-group method (RGM) equation in the Lippmann-Schwinger formalism (LS-RGM) for nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering, and extended this to G-matrix calculations for nuclear matter properties. Model parameters were determined by fitting the SP99 phase shift analysis for np scattering with partial waves J ≤ 2 at energies T_lab ≤ 350 MeV, constrained by the deuteron binding energy and 1S0 NN scattering length, plus low-energy YN total cross section data.

## Key numbers

- The averaged deviation of calculated phase shifts from empirical values is sqrt(χ²)=0.655° for np scattering, compared to sqrt(χ²) ~ 3° in the previous FSS model
- Nucleon-nucleon phase shifts were fitted for incident energies up to T_lab=350 MeV
- The single-particle potentials of the nucleon and Lambda now show proper repulsive behavior in the momentum region q_1=5-20 fm^{-1}
- The harmonic oscillator width parameter b was reduced from ≥0.6 fm to approximately 0.56 fm
- The 3D_2 phase shift in the previous FSS model was more attractive than experiment by 10° around T_lab ~ 300 MeV
- The cut-off mass for the pion form factor in OBEP is approximately 800-900 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU_6 quark model studies (Fujiwara et al., FSS model, 1996) by incorporating vector meson exchange and momentum-dependent terms motivated by the Bryan-Scott approach (Bryan and Scott, 1967). The improvements address deficiencies identified in G-matrix calculations (Fujiwara et al., 1998) where single-particle potentials showed unrealistic attraction. The model enables more reliable predictions for hypernuclear properties, including the single-particle spin-orbit strength for Lambda particles, and provides a framework for examining charge symmetry breaking effects in hypernuclei previously studied with the Nijmegen potential (Nagels et al., 1973).
