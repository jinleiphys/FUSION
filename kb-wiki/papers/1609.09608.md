# The effect of neutron skin on inclusive prompt photon production in Pb~+~Pb collisions at the LHC

> Recent experiments on lead (\textrm{$Pb_{82}^{208}$}) nuclei have observed the celebrated phenomenon of neutron skin-thickness of low energy nuclear physics. The skin-thickness provides a measure of extension of spatial distribution of neutrons inside the atomic nucleus than protons. We have studied the effect of neutron skin-thickness on inclusive prompt photon production in Pb~+~Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies. We have calculated the \textquoteleft central-to-peripheral ratio\textquoteright ($R_\textrm{cp}$) of prompt photon production with and without accounting for neutron skin effect. The neutron skin causes a characteristic enhancement in the ratio, in particular at forward rapidity, which is distinguishable in our calculation. However a very precise direct photon measurement up to large transverse momenta would be necessary to constrain the feature in experiment.

## Key claim

The neutron skin of Pb nuclei causes a characteristic enhancement in the central-to-peripheral ratio (R_cp) of inclusive prompt photon production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, particularly at forward rapidity; however, a very precise direct photon measurement up to large transverse momenta would be necessary to constrain this feature experimentally.

## Method

The author used next-to-leading order perturbative QCD via the INCNLO numerical program with CTEQ5M1 parton distribution functions and BFG-II photon fragmentation functions to calculate prompt photon production cross-sections. The optical Glauber model was extended to separate proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and mixed nucleon-nucleon scattering contributions using separate Woods-Saxon distributions for protons (R_p = 6.680 fm, c_p = 0.447 fm) and neutrons (R_n = 6.70 ± 0.03 fm, c_n = 0.55 ± 0.01 fm) in Pb nuclei, from which effective proton and neutron numbers were derived for each centrality class.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin parameters for Pb: R_n = 6.70 ± 0.03 fm, c_n = 0.55 ± 0.01 fm; proton parameters: R_p = 6.680 fm, c_p = 0.447 fm
- At forward rapidity (y = 2.0), neutron skin effect causes a rise in R_cp by approximately 7% for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and by approximately 5% at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV
- At mid-rapidity (y = 0.0), the enhancement from neutron skin is at most 2-3% for both collision energies
- The momentum fraction probed at mid-rapidity lies in the range 0.01 less than x less than 0.06; at forward rapidity (y = 2.0), the range is 0.05 less than x less than 0.5
- The effective proton-to-neutron number ratio for central collisions is approximately 0.65 (Z/N value for Pb)

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron skin effects, previously measured in low-energy nuclear physics experiments via parity-violating electron scattering (PRL 2012) and coherent photo-production of pions (PRL 2014), into the high-energy heavy-ion collision regime at the LHC. The author notes that a closely related article by Helenius et al. appeared on the preprint server during this work, which also found that neutron skin enhances direct photon production in Pb+Pb collisions using a different approach. The results suggest that isolated prompt photon measurements by CMS and ATLAS Collaborations at forward rapidity could provide experimental constraints on the neutron skin effect, though the isolation criterion necessarily reduces the fragmentation contribution to the inclusive cross-section.
