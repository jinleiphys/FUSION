# Giant dipole resonance in $^{201}$Tl at low temperature

> The thermal pairing gap obtained by embedding the exact solutions of the pairing problem into the canonical ensemble is employed to calculate the width and strength function of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) within the phonon damping model. The results of calculations describe reasonably well the data for the GDR width as well as the GDR linearized strength function, recently obtained for $^{201}$Tl in the temperature region between 0.8 and 1.2 MeV, which other approaches that neglect the effect of non-vanishing thermal pairing fail to describe.

## Key claim

The phonon damping model (PDM) combined with exact thermal pairing gaps from the canonical ensemble (CE) can describe the GDR width and strength function in the open-shell nucleus \(^{201}\)Tl at low temperatures (0.8–1.2 MeV), a regime where approaches neglecting non-vanishing thermal pairing fail.

## Method

The authors calculated the GDR width and strength function using the phonon damping model (PDM), which treats the damping of the GDR phonon via coupling to noncollective particle-hole (ph) and particle-particle/hole-hole (pp/hh) configurations. Thermal pairing was incorporated by embedding exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian into the canonical ensemble to obtain temperature-dependent pairing gaps for neutrons and protons separately, which were then used to compute quasiparticle energies and occupation numbers within the PDM's quasiparticle representation. The single-particle levels were obtained from axially deformed Woods-Saxon potentials, and the exact CE pairing gaps were computed for 14 doubly folded levels around the Fermi surface for each nucleon species.

## Key numbers

* GDR width in \(^{201}\)Tl at \(T = 0.8\) MeV: 3.58 MeV (theory) vs. \(3.4 \pm 0.45\) MeV (experiment at \(T = 0.82\) MeV).
* GDR width in \(^{201}\)Tl at \(T = 1.0\) MeV: 3.96 MeV (theory) vs. \(3.8 \pm 0.45\) MeV (experiment at \(T = 0.97\) MeV).
* GDR width in \(^{201}\)Tl at \(T = 1.2\) MeV: 4.5 MeV (theory) vs. \(4.5 \pm 0.45\) MeV (experiment at \(T = 1.12\) MeV).
* GDR peak energy \(E_{GDR}\) fixed at 13.8 MeV, independent of temperature.
* Exact CE neutron pairing gap at \(T = 1\) MeV: 0.34 MeV; proton pairing gap at \(T = 1\) MeV: approximately 1 MeV.
* Pairing parameters: \(G_N = 0.182\) MeV (neutrons), \(G_Z = 0.37\) MeV (protons), chosen to reproduce empirical gaps \(\Delta_{N,Z} = 1\) MeV at \(T=0\).

## Context

This work extends the PDM framework, previously applied to \(^{120}\)Sn (Dang and Arima, 2002) and \(^{208}\)Pb (Dang and Arima, 1999), to the open-shell nucleus \(^{201}\)Tl, where thermal pairing is essential. It directly addresses the failure of the thermal shape fluctuation model (TSFM) to describe the low-temperature GDR width in this nucleus, as noted in the experimental study by Banerjee et al. (2012). The results enable future microscopic calculations of GDR damping in open-shell nuclei at low temperature and low angular momentum, and confirm that thermal pairing effects must be included up to at least \(T \sim 1.5\) MeV.
