# A mass-dependent beta-function

> Threshold effects related to fermion masses are considered for an all-order beta-function based on a background field momentum subtraction scheme. Far away from all thresholds, the suggested beta-function reduces to the conjectured all-order form inspired by the Novikov-Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov beta-function of N=1 supersymmetric gauge theories with a fixed integer number of fermion flavours. At (formally) infinite masses the corresponding pure Yang--Mills beta-function is recovered. We discuss applications to the phase diagram of non-Abelian field theories.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a mass-dependent all-order beta-function for non-Abelian gauge theories that smoothly interpolates between the massless fixed-flavour limit and the pure Yang-Mills limit, but the construction is not unique: any factor of \(1+O(g^2)\) introduced in the mass-dependent anomalous dimension or the modified \(\bar{\beta}_0'\) would still reproduce the two-loop background-field momentum-subtraction result, and such a factor would only affect the scheme at third loop order.

## Method

The author starts from the two-loop beta-function coefficients computed in the background field momentum subtraction scheme, which naturally incorporate smooth decoupling of massive fermions via functions \(b_0(x)\) and \(b_1(x)\) of \(x = -\mu^2/(4m^2)\). He then constructs a mass-dependent all-order beta-function by replacing the universal two-loop coefficients \(\beta_0, \beta_1\) and the anomalous dimension \(\gamma\) in the conjectured all-order form of Ryttov and Sannino (2008) with their mass-dependent counterparts \(\bar{\beta}_0, \bar{\beta}_1, \bar{\gamma}\), while adjusting the denominator to ensure the correct massless and infinite-mass limits. The resulting expression is applied to determine the (quasi)conformal window for gauge theories with fermions in various representations of \(SU(N_c)\), \(Sp(2N_c)\), and \(SO(N_c)\) groups.

## Key numbers

* The two-loop coefficients in the background field momentum subtraction scheme are \(\bar{\beta}_0 = \frac{11}{3}C_2(G) - \frac{4}{3}T(R)\sum_{j=1}^{N_f} b_0(x_j)\) and \(\bar{\beta}_1 = \frac{34}{3}C_2(G)^2 - T(R)\sum_{j=1}^{N_f} b_1(x_j)\), where \(x_j = -\mu^2/(4m_j^2)\).
* The massless all-order beta-function of Ryttov and Sannino (2008) is \(\beta(g) = -\frac{g^3}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{\beta_0 - \frac{2}{3}T(R)N_f\gamma(g^2)}{1 - \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2}C_2(G)(1+2\beta_0'/\beta_0)}\) with \(\beta_0' = C_2(G) - T(R)N_f\).
* For the minimal walking technicolour model with two techniflavours in the adjoint representation of \(SU(2)\), the zone in which the flavours are gradually switched off spans four orders of magnitude in energy scale (see Fig. 4).
* The dynamically generated techniquark mass in the \(\mathcal{L}_m\) channel is estimated as \(\Sigma(0) \approx 2\pi F_\pi / \sqrt{d_\mathrm{R}}\), where for two techniflavours \(F_\pi = \Lambda_\mathrm{ew} = 246\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The universal formula for the lower bound of the conformal window from the worldline formalism is \(1 = \kappa\, 2N_f\, T(R)/C_2(G)\) with \(\kappa \approx 1/4\); from the massless all-order beta-function one finds \(\kappa = (2+\gamma)/11\), which would give \(\gamma \approx 3/4\) if the two results are combined.

## Context

This work extends the conjectured all-order beta-function of Ryttov and Sannino (2008), itself inspired by the Novikov–Shifman–Vainshtein–Zakharov beta-function of \(\mathcal{N}=1\) supersymmetric gauge theories, by incorporating threshold effects from fermion masses via the background field momentum subtraction scheme of Jegerlehner (1998). It enables quantitative studies of the (quasi)conformal window in non-Abelian gauge theories with massive fermions, relevant for walking technicolour models (Dietrich, Sannino, and collaborators, 2005–2006) and for comparison with lattice simulations (Hietanen et al., 2008; Catterall et al., 2007). The paper also discusses how explicit fermion mass terms of different flavour-symmetry-breaking patterns can affect the phase diagram, particularly for technicolour models with (pseudo)real representations.
