# Nuclear viscosity estimated by dynamics of neck formation in the early stage of nuclear collision

> The very early stage of the coalescence of two nuclei is studied and used to estimate the nuclear viscosity. The time evolution of the neck region has been simulated by the unified Langevin equation method, which is used in the analysis of heavy-ion collisions from the approaching stage to the fusion-fission stage. It is found that the transition from viscous to inertial coalescence that appeared in the neck growth of macroscopic drops can also be seen in the present simulation in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The dynamics of neck growth is analyzed in terms of the hydrodynamical formula and the viscosity coefficient of nuclear matter is estimated using the analogy of macroscopic drops.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transition from viscous to inertial coalescence, previously observed in macroscopic liquid drops, also occurs in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and that the nuclear viscosity coefficient can be estimated from the linear growth of the neck radius in the early stage of coalescence. The extracted viscosity values depend on the incident energy and angular momentum, with the result for high orbital angular momentum (0.02 ± 0.01 TP) being consistent with values derived from giant dipole resonance experiments.

## Method

The authors simulated the early stage of coalescence for the 136Xe + 136Xe reaction using a unified Langevin equation method with three collective coordinates (distance between centers, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) in a two-center parametrization. They calculated the time evolution of the neck radius from 20000 trajectory trials at three center-of-mass energies (E_c.m./V_bass = 1.01, 1.10, and 1.20) with zero orbital angular momentum, and additionally for L = 210ℏ at the highest energy. The neck growth was analyzed using hydrodynamical scaling laws: linear in time for the viscous regime (R_neck ∝ C₀σt/η) and square-root in time for the inertial regime (R_neck ∝ D₀(σR₀/ρ)^(1/4)√t).

## Key numbers

- Viscosity coefficients extracted from linear neck growth at zero angular momentum: 0.0092 TP, 0.0090 TP, and 0.0086 TP for E_c.m./V_bass = 1.01, 1.10, and 1.20, respectively
- For L = 210ℏ at E_c.m./V_bass = 1.20, the extracted viscosity coefficient is η = 0.02 ± 0.01 TP
- The crossover neck radii R_c are 0.6 fm, 1.6 fm, and 1.87 fm for the three incident energies (E_c.m./V_bass = 1.01, 1.10, and 1.20)
- The Bass barrier energy for the system is V_bass = 299.56 MeV
- Relative velocities of the two colliding nuclei are 29, 31, and 32 fm/zs for the three incident energies
- The correction factors Δr for the inertial regime are 0.54 fm and 0.74 fm for E_c.m./V_bass = 1.10 and 1.20
- The relaxation time for the diabatic-to-adiabatic potential transition is τ = 0.1 zs
- The characteristic relaxation time of the neck parameter is t₀ = 0.35 zs with variance Δ_ε = 0.1 zs
- The nucleus density is assumed to be ρ = 2.5 × 10¹⁷ kg/m³

## Context

This work extends the study of neck formation dynamics from macroscopic liquid drops (as studied experimentally by Thoroddsen et al. (2005) and Aarts et al. (2005)) to nuclear systems, using the liquid drop model analogy that has been foundational in nuclear physics since Weizsäcker (1935) and Bohr and Wheeler (1939). The estimated viscosity values are compared with those derived from giant dipole resonance experiments (reported as scattered around 0.025 TP) and from fission analyses by Koonin and Nix (1977) and others (range 0.04 TP to 0.08 TP). The method enables a new approach to determining nuclear viscosity from the dynamics of neck formation in the contact stage, which could help clarify the uncertainty of viscosity or friction force parameters used in analyses of deep inelastic collisions, quasifission, and fusion-fission processes.
