# On the structure of large N cancellations in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We show how to compute loop graphs in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory including the full functional dependence on the ratio of the Delta--nucleon mass difference to the pion mass, while at the same time automatically incorporating the 1/N cancellations that follow from the large-N spin-flavor symmetry of baryons in QCD. The one-loop renormalization of the baryon axial vector current is studied to demonstrate the procedure. A new cancellation is identified in the one-loop contribution to the baryon axial vector current. We show that loop corrections to the axial vector currents are exceptionally sensitive to deviations of the ratios of baryon-pion axial couplings from SU(6) values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a calculational scheme that simultaneously retains the full functional dependence of loop graphs on the ratio of the Delta-nucleon mass difference to the pion mass, while automatically incorporating the 1/N_c cancellations that follow from large-N_c spin-flavor symmetry; the authors identify a new cancellation in the one-loop contribution to the baryon axial vector current and show that loop corrections are exceptionally sensitive to deviations of baryon-pion axial coupling ratios from SU(6) values.

## Method

The authors combine heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with the 1/N_c expansion, computing one-loop corrections to the baryon axial vector current using velocity-dependent baryon fields and including both spin-1/2 octet and spin-3/2 decuplet intermediate baryon states. The loop integrals are evaluated with full dependence on the Delta-nucleon mass difference Delta and the pion mass m_pi, using dimensional regularization in the MS-bar scheme. The large-N_c cancellations are made manifest by expanding the axial current operator A^{ia} in terms of SU(6) spin-flavor operators G^{ia}, J^i, and T^a, and analyzing the commutator structure of the loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- The physical value of m_pi/Delta is approximately 0.5 in the real world.
- The pion decay constant f is proportional to sqrt(N_c), and the axial coupling g_A is proportional to N_c, so the pion-baryon vertex is of order sqrt(N_c).
- The one-loop correction to the axial current is of order 1/N_c times the tree-level value, rather than N_c times the tree-level value, due to large-N_c cancellations.
- The Delta-nucleon mass difference Delta = M_Delta - M_N is proportional to 1/N_c, approaching zero in the large-N_c limit.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV.
- The function F^{(1)}(m_b,0,mu) in the degeneracy limit equals -1/(16 pi^2 f^2) * m_b^2 * (11/3 + ln(m_b^2/mu^2)).

## Context

This work extends the earlier heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory analysis of baryon axial vector currents by Jenkins and Manohar (1991), which found large cancellations between loop graphs with intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states. The large-N_c cancellations were later proven by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1993, 1994) using the 1/N_c expansion, showing that baryon axial coupling ratios should have SU(6) values with F/D = 2/3 up to corrections of order 1/N_c^2 for pions. The present paper provides a practical calculational scheme that retains the full m_pi/Delta dependence while making the 1/N_c cancellations manifest, enabling systematic higher-order calculations without the misleading large-N_c growth that would occur if Delta states were not explicitly included.
