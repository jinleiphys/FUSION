# Roles of the tensor and pairing correlations on the halo formation in 11Li

> We study the roles of the tensor and pairing correlations on the halo formation in 11Li with an extended 9Li+n+n model. We first solve the ground state of 9Li in the shell model basis by taking 2p-2h states using the Gaussian functions with variational size parameters to take into account the tensor correlation fully. In 11Li, the tensor and pairing correlations in 9Li are Pauli-blocked by additional two neutrons, which work coherently to make the configurations containing the 0p1/2 state pushed up and close to those containing the 1s1/2 state. Hence, the pairing interaction works efficiently to mix the two configurations by equal amount and develop the halo structure in 11Li. For 10Li, the inversion phenomenon of s- and p-states is reproduced in the same framework. Our model furthermore explains the recently observed Coulomb breakup strength and charge radius for 11Li.

## Key claim

The tensor and pairing correlations in the \(^9\)Li core are Pauli-blocked by additional neutrons in \(^{11}\)Li, and this blocking works coherently to push the \(0p_{1/2}\) configuration up in energy until it becomes nearly degenerate with the \(1s_{1/2}\) configuration, allowing the pairing interaction to mix them equally and produce the halo structure.

## Method

The authors solve the ground state of \(^9\)Li using a tensor-optimized shell model with up to \(2p\)-\(2h\) excitations within the \(0p\) shell, using Gaussian basis functions with variational size parameters to fully incorporate the tensor correlation. For \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{10}\)Li, they employ a coupled \(^9\)Li+\(n\)+\(n\) and \(^9\)Li+\(n\) orthogonality condition model (OCM), where the last neutrons are treated in a few-body approach and the Pauli-blocking effect dynamically couples the \(^9\)Li configurations to the relative motion of the valence neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The \((1s)^2\) probability for the last two neutrons in \(^{11}\)Li is 46.9%, with \((p_{1/2})^2\) at 42.7%, \((p_{3/2})^2\) at 2.5%, \((d_{5/2})^2\) at 4.1%, and \((d_{3/2})^2\) at 1.9%.
- The matter radius of \(^{11}\)Li is 3.41 fm.
- The charge radius of \(^{11}\)Li is 2.44 fm, compared to 2.23 fm for \(^9\)Li (experimental values: 2.467\(\pm\)0.037 fm for \(^{11}\)Li and 2.217\(\pm\)0.035 fm for \(^9\)Li).
- The root-mean-square distance between the core and the center of mass of the two neutrons (\(\sqrt{\langle R^2_{\rm c-2n}\rangle}\)) is 5.69 fm; the distance between the last two neutrons is 7.33 fm.
- The two-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Li is reproduced as 0.31 MeV.
- The relative energy difference \(\Delta E\) between the \((1s)^2\) and \((0p)^2\) configurations in the present model is \(-0.1\) MeV (i.e., degenerate).

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body models of \(^{11}\)Li (e.g., Thompson (1994), Esbensen (1992), Myo (2002)) that had to phenomenologically lower the \(1s_{1/2}\) state relative to the \(0p_{1/2}\) state without a microscopic explanation. By explicitly including the tensor force in the \(^9\)Li core via the tensor-optimized shell model, the authors show that Pauli-blocking of the tensor and pairing correlations naturally produces the \(s\)-\(p\) shell-gap disappearance. The model also reproduces the inversion of \(s\)- and \(p\)-states in \(^{10}\)Li and explains the recently observed Coulomb breakup strength and charge radius of \(^{11}\)Li, enabling future studies of halo nuclei where tensor correlations are expected to be important.
