# Isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decay: Radial excitations

> Based on an exact formalism, we study the effects of isospin-symmetry-breaking interactions on superallowed 0+ -> 0+ transitions. We calculate the second-order renormalization of the Fermi matrix element due to radial contributions and show that radial excitations neglected in the treatment of Towner and Hardy are significant. These are estimated to decrease the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections. Our results provide a correction term that can be included in existing approaches.

## Key claim

Radial excitations neglected in the Towner and Hardy (2008) treatment of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decay are significant at second order in isospin-symmetry-breaking interactions and are estimated to decrease the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections, potentially reducing the extracted value of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element \(V_{ud}\).

## Method

The authors start from an exact formalism (Miller and Schwenk, 2008) that expresses the correct isospin raising operator \(\tau_+\) as a sum of the Towner-Hardy operator \(\tau_+^{\rm TH}\) and a correction operator \(\delta\tau_+\) that accounts for radial excitations (states with different radial quantum numbers \(n\) and \(n'\)). They evaluate both the Towner-Hardy term and the radial-excitation correction term to second order in isospin-symmetry-breaking interactions, using a single-particle basis with occupation probabilities and closure approximations for the energy denominators.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry-limit Fermi matrix element is \(M_0 = \sqrt{2}\) for \(T=1\) parent and daughter states.
* The radial-excitation correction term \(\delta M_F\) is estimated to cancel the Towner-Hardy contribution \(\delta_{C2}\) at second order when the radial excitation energy is \(\widetilde{E}_\alpha-\widetilde{E}_{\alpha'} = 2\hbar\omega\) and the closure energy denominator is \(|\Delta E_i| = 4\hbar\omega\).
* The energy denominator \(|\Delta E_i|\) for the isovector monopole state ranges between \(3\) and \(4\hbar\omega\) (Auerbach, 2009).

## Context

This work extends the exact formalism of Miller and Schwenk (2008) to provide numerical estimates of the radial-excitation corrections that were neglected in the standard treatment of Towner and Hardy (2008). The results offer a possible explanation for the smaller isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections found by Auerbach (2009) and by Liang et al. using relativistic density-functional calculations. The correction term derived here can be included in future numerical calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections that follow the Towner-Hardy approach or make a similar truncation of basis states.
