# The $d^*(2380)$ in neutron stars - a new degree of freedom?

> Elucidating the appropriate microscopic degrees of freedom within neutron stars remains an open question which impacts nuclear physics, particle physics and astrophysics. The recent discovery of the first non-trivial dibaryon, the $d^*(2380)$, provides a new candidate for an exotic degree of freedom in the nuclear equation of state at high matter densities. In this paper a first calculation of the role of the $d^*(2380)$ in neutron stars is performed, based on a relativistic mean field description of the nucleonic degrees of freedom supplemented by a free boson gas of $d^*(2380)$. The calculations indicate that the $d^*(2380)$ would appear at densities around three times normal nuclear matter saturation density, influencing the upper mass limit for a stable neutron star and the neutron and proton fractions. New possibilities for neutron star cooling mechanisms arising from the $d^*(2380)$ are also predicted.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first calculation of the role of the recently discovered dibaryon \(d^*(2380)\) in neutron stars, finding that it would appear at densities around three times normal nuclear matter saturation density and could comprise around 20% of the matter in the center of heavy stars, but the calculation treats the \(d^*(2380)\) as a free boson gas with no explicit interactions with nucleons or mesons, and the authors note that future work must include such interactions when experimental information becomes available.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic mean field description of nucleonic degrees of freedom using the GM1 parametrization of the Glendenning-Moszkowski model, supplemented by a free boson gas of pointlike \(d^*(2380)\) particles. They solve the Tolmann–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations to obtain the equation of state and mass-radius relation for neutron stars, varying the in-medium \(d^*(2380)\) mass by \(\pm 100\) MeV to explore sensitivity. They also consider a model that additionally includes the \(\Delta\) quartet as an explicit degree of freedom to study competition between the \(d^*(2380)\) and other postulated constituents.

## Key numbers

* The \(d^*(2380)\) appears at densities between 2.7 and 3.2 times normal nuclear matter saturation density (\(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
* For the nominal \(d^*(2380)\) mass of 2380 MeV, the maximum neutron star mass is reduced from 2.36 \(M_\odot\) (pure nucleonic) to 2.05 \(M_\odot\).
* The \(d^*\) fraction at the center of the maximum-mass star is 21% for the nominal mass, ranging from 20% (for \(m_{d^*}=2480\) MeV) to 22% (for \(m_{d^*}=2280\) MeV).
* The maximum star radius increases from 11.8 km (pure nucleonic) to 13.06 km (nominal \(d^*\) mass), and the central density decreases from 5.80 \(\rho_0\) to 5.03 \(\rho_0\).
* The dimensionless tidal deformability parameter \(\Lambda_{M_{NS}=1.4M_{\odot}} = 740\) is consistent with LIGO constraints.
* The \(d^*(2380)\) resonance has mass \(M \approx 2380\) MeV and width \(\Gamma \approx 70\) MeV, with a resonance pole at \((2380\pm 10)-i(40\pm 5)\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends generic theoretical studies of dibaryons in neutron stars (e.g., Refs. NM1, NM2, NM3, NM4) by using the first experimentally identified non-trivial dibaryon, the \(d^*(2380)\) discovered by the Wasa-at-Cosy collaboration. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" (EPJA16) and "\(\Delta\) puzzle" (Drago2) by showing that the \(d^*(2380)\) may compete with other exotic phases and could help reconcile neutron star mass observations with the presence of exotic degrees of freedom. The results enable future studies of \(d^*(2380)\) interactions in medium, its role in neutron star cooling via weak processes like \(nn \to d^* e^- \bar{\nu}_e\), and its potential signatures in neutron star mergers and gamma-ray bursts.
