# Time singularities of correlators from Dirichlet conditions in AdS/CFT

> Within AdS/CFT, we establish a general procedure for obtaining the leading singularity of two-point correlators involving operator insertions at different times. The procedure obtained is applied to operators dual to a scalar field which satisfies Dirichlet boundary conditions on an arbitrary time-like surface in the bulk. We determine how the Dirichlet boundary conditions influence the singularity structure of the field theory correlation functions. New singularities appear at boundary points connected by null geodesics bouncing between the Dirichlet surface and the boundary. We propose that their appearance can be interpreted as due to a non-local double trace deformation of the dual field theory, in which the two insertions of the operator are separated in time. The procedure developed in this paper provides a technical tool which may prove useful in view of describing holographic thermalization using gravitational collapse in AdS space.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a general procedure for determining the leading singularity structure of unequal-time two-point correlators in AdS/CFT when a scalar field satisfies Dirichlet boundary conditions on an arbitrary time-like surface in the bulk, but the analysis is limited to a simplified setup where the reflective surface (mirror) does not backreact on the geometry and serves as a crude approximation to a collapsing shell before horizon formation.

## Method

The authors introduce a potential barrier \(V = V_0 \delta(z - f(t))\) into the Klein-Gordon equation for a massless scalar field in AdS\({}_{d+1}\) and take the \(V_0 \to \infty\) limit to impose Dirichlet boundary conditions at the mirror trajectory \(z = f(t)\). They derive recursion relations for the coefficients of singularities in the bulk-boundary propagator by matching the most singular parts of the free propagator \(G_0\) and the convolution of the bulk-bulk propagator \(G_{bb}\) with the leading-order singular propagator \(G_{LO}\) along the mirror trajectory. The singularities are then propagated to the boundary to obtain the two-point correlator singularities.

## Key numbers

* For a static mirror at constant radial coordinate \(z_s\), the singular times are \(t_m = t' + (2m+1)z_s\) and \(\tilde{t}_m = t' + 2m z_s\), with the ratio of consecutive residues \(|R_m| \equiv 1\) for \(m > 0\).
* For a mirror on a scaling trajectory \(z = t / u_0\) with \(u_0 > 1\), the singular times are \(t_m = t' \frac{u_0}{u_0-1} \left( \frac{u_0+1}{u_0-1} \right)^m\) and \(\tilde{t}_m = t' \left( \frac{u_0+1}{u_0-1} \right)^m\), with the ratio of consecutive residues \(|R_m| = \left( \frac{u_0+1}{u_0-1} \right)^c\) where \(c = (d+1)/2\).
* The initial conditions for the recursion are given by \(g_0 = \frac{\Gamma(\frac{d+1}{2})\Gamma(\frac{1}{2})}{\pi \Gamma(\frac{d}{2})} \frac{c}{A_4} \frac{z_s^{c+1}}{(2i)^c t_0^{c+1}}\) for the static mirror and \(g_0 = \frac{\Gamma(\frac{d+1}{2})\Gamma(\frac{1}{2})}{\pi \Gamma(\frac{d}{2})} \frac{c}{A_4} \frac{1}{(2i)^c u_0 (u_0-1)^c}\) for the scaling mirror.

## Context

This work extends the bulk-cone singularity conjecture of Hatta, Lin, and Rey (2008) by providing a method to determine not just the location but the precise form and coefficients of singularities in boundary correlators due to a Dirichlet surface in the bulk. It builds on previous studies of static mirrors by Amado and Hoyos (2008) and scaling mirrors by Erdmenger, Lin, and Ngo (2011), and the authors propose that the technique will be useful for studying holographic thermalization via gravitational collapse models, where the Dirichlet surface approximates a collapsing shell before black hole formation.
