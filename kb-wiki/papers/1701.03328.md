# Enhanced effect of CP-violating nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment in HfF$^+$ molecule

> HfF$^+$ cation is a very promising system to search for the electron electric dipole moment (EDM), and corresponding experiment is carried out by E. Cornell group. Here we theoretically investigate the cation to search for another T,P-odd effect -- the nuclear magnetic quadrpole moment (MQM) interaction with electrons. We report the first accurate ab initio relativistic electronic structure calculations of the molecular parameter $W_M$=0.494 $\frac{10^{33}\mathrm{Hz}}{e~{\rm cm}^2}$ that is required to interpret the experimental data in terms of the MQM of Hf nucleus. For this we have implemented and applied the combined Dirac-Coulomb(-Gaunt) and relativistic effective core potential approaches to treat electron correlation effects from all of the electrons and to take into account high-order correlation effects using the coupled cluster method with single, double, triple and noniterative quadruple cluster amplitudes, CCSDT(Q). We discuss interpretation of the MQM effect in terms of the strength constants of T,P-odd nuclear forces, proton and neutron EDM, QCD parameter $θ$ and quark chromo-EDM.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first accurate *ab initio* relativistic calculation of the molecular parameter \(W_M = 0.494 \times 10^{33} \, \text{Hz} / (e \cdot \text{cm}^2)\) for HfF\(^+\), which is required to interpret experimental data in terms of the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the Hf nucleus; the abstract glosses over the fact that the accuracy is estimated to be better than 4%, with the main uncertainty due to omitting the interference of the Gaunt interaction and correlation effects.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic electronic structure calculations of the \(W_M\) parameter for the first excited \(^3\Delta_1\) state of HfF\(^+\) using a combined Dirac-Coulomb(-Gaunt) and relativistic effective core potential (GRECP) approach. They treated electron correlation effects from all electrons using the coupled cluster method with single, double, triple, and noniterative quadruple cluster amplitudes [CCSDT(Q)], and included corrections for inner-core correlation, Gaunt interaction, high-order correlation effects, and basis set saturation. The molecular parameter \(W_M\) was computed from the matrix element of the MQM Hamiltonian, and the results were used to express the MQM energy shift in terms of fundamental CP-violating quantities such as the QCD parameter \(\tilde{\theta}\) and quark chromo-EDM.

## Key numbers

- \(W_M = 0.494 \times 10^{33} \, \text{Hz} / (e \cdot \text{cm}^2)\) for the \(^3\Delta_1\) state of HfF\(^+\) at an internuclear distance of 3.41 Bohr.
- Inner-core contribution to \(W_M\) is about 3%; Gaunt contribution is about -1.6%; high-order correlation effects give -0.3%.
- For \(^{177}\text{HfF}^+\) (\(I = 7/2\)) and the ground rotational level \(J = 1\), the MQM energy shifts \(|\delta(J,F)|\) are \(0.107 \, W_M M\), \(0.143 \, W_M M\), and \(0.05 \, W_M M\) for \(F = 5/2, 7/2, 9/2\), respectively.
- The MQM in the laboratory frame for \(^{177}\text{Hf}\) is expressed as \(M = (1.5 \eta_p - 1.1 \eta_n) \times 10^{-33} \, (e \cdot \text{cm}^2) - (4.0 d_p + 2.9 d_n) \times 10^{-13} \, \text{cm}\).
- The MQM in terms of the QCD parameter \(\tilde{\theta}\) is \(M(\theta) = -7 \times 10^{-28} \, \tilde{\theta} \cdot e \cdot \text{cm}^2\).
- The MQM in terms of quark chromo-EDM is \(M(\tilde{d}) = -4 \times 10^{-11} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d) \cdot e \cdot \text{cm}\).
- Current limits on \(d_p\) (\(|d_p| < 2.0 \times 10^{-25} \, e \cdot \text{cm}\)), \(|\tilde{\theta}| < 1.5 \times 10^{-10}\), and \(|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 5.7 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{cm}\) correspond to energy shifts \(|0.143 \, W_M M| < 6 \, \mu\text{Hz}\), \(7 \, \mu\text{Hz}\), and \(16 \, \mu\text{Hz}\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of HfF\(^+\) for the electron electric dipole moment (EDM) by Petrov (2007, 2009) and Fleig (2013), and applies the combined Dirac-Coulomb and GRECP scheme developed by Skripnikov (2016) to the nuclear MQM problem. The calculated \(W_M\) value is close to that in ThF\(^+\) (Skripnikov, 2015) and slightly smaller than in TaN (Skripnikov, 2015; Fleig, 2016). The result enables the interpretation of ongoing experiments by the Cornell group on HfF\(^+\) to search for the nuclear MQM and related CP-violating effects, including the QCD parameter \(\tilde{\theta}\) and quark chromo-EDM.
