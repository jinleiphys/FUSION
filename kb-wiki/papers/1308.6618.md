# Long-range rapidity correlations in high energy AA collisions in Monte Carlo model with string fusion

> The magnitude of long-range correlations between observables in two separated rapidity windows, proposed as a signature of the string fusion and percolation phenomenon, is studied in the framework of non-Glauber Monte Carlo string-parton model, based on the picture of elementary collisions of color dipoles. The predictions, obtained with and without string fusion, demonstrate effects of color string fusion on the observables in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC: decrease of n-n correlation coefficient with centrality and negative pt-n correlations, if the sufficiently effective centrality estimator is applied. In general case it is shown that the values of n-n and pt-n correlation coefficients strongly depend on the method of collision centrality fixation. In contrast, the predictions obtained for pt-pt correlation have almost no effect of centrality determination method and the corresponding experimental data would produce the strong limitation on the transverse radius of a string.

## Key claim

The magnitude of long-range correlations between observables in two separated rapidity windows, proposed as a signature of string fusion and percolation, is studied in a non-Glauber Monte Carlo string-parton model; the values of n-n and pt-n correlation coefficients strongly depend on the method of collision centrality fixation, while pt-pt correlations are almost unaffected by the centrality determination method and would produce a strong limitation on the transverse radius of a string.

## Method

The authors used a non-Glauber Monte Carlo string-parton model based on elementary collisions of color dipoles, where nucleons are composed of quark-diquark and quark-antiquark pairs forming dipoles whose interaction probability depends on transverse coordinates. Strings stretched between projectile and target partons are allowed to fuse in the transverse plane using a cellular version of the string fusion model, with the main effect being modification of mean transverse momentum and multiplicity according to scaling laws with the number of overlapping strings. Centrality was estimated using two approaches: number of participant nucleons (Npart) and a multiplicity estimator (``vzero'') covering rapidity windows close to the ALICE V0 detector acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV were simulated with rapidity windows of width 0.8
- Both pt-n and pt-pt correlations vanish at a rapidity gap of about 10 units, while n-n correlations remain present
- String radius values from 0.2 fm to 0.4 fm were tested for pt-pt correlation sensitivity
- The fit function for correlation coefficient dependence on forward window width is b = Δy_F / (Δy_F + ϰ)
- Centrality bins narrower than 5% gave consistent results for pt-pt correlations

## Context

This work extends the study of long-range rapidity correlations proposed by Amelin as a signature of string fusion and percolation, one of the collectivity effects described by Braun and Pajares. The decrease of n-n correlations with centrality in the model with string fusion contradicts predictions of the CGC model by McLerran and collaborators. The results enable experimental constraints on the transverse radius of a string through measurement of pt-pt correlations, which are robust against centrality determination issues that plague other correlation observables.
