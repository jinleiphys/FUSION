# Improved (e,e') response functions at intermediate momentum transfers: the 3He case

> A possibility of extending the applicability range of non-relativistic calculations of electronuclear response functions in the quasielasic peak region is studied. We show that adopting a particular model for determining the kinematical inputs of the non-relativistic calculations can extend this range considerably, almost eliminating the reference frame dependence of the results. We also show that there exists one reference frame, where essentially the same result can be obtained with no need of adopting the particular kinematical model. The calculation is carried out with the Argonne V18 potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. A comparison of these improved calculations with experimental data shows a very good agreement for the quasielastic peak positions at $q=500,$ 600, 700 MeV/c and for the peak heights at the two lower $q$--values, while for the peak height at $q=700$ MeV/c one finds differences of about 20%.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the frame dependence of non-relativistic calculations of the longitudinal (e,e') response function for 3He at intermediate momentum transfers (500-700 MeV/c) can be drastically reduced by adopting a two-body breakup model with relativistic kinematics to determine the input to the non-relativistic dynamics calculation, though at q=700 MeV/c a discrepancy of about 15-25% in peak height remains.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic calculations of the longitudinal response function R_L for 3He using the Argonne V18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, with the charge operator supplemented by first-order relativistic corrections (Darwin-Foldy and spin-orbit terms). They computed R_L in four different reference frames (laboratory, anti-lab, Breit, and active nucleon Breit) at momentum transfers q=500, 600, and 700 MeV/c, then introduced a procedure where the final-state relative energy input to the non-relativistic calculation is determined from a relativistically correct two-body kinematics relation for the knocked-out nucleon and residual system.

## Key numbers

- At q=500 MeV/c, differences between the two extreme frames (ANB and LAB) were 6 MeV in peak position and 13% in peak height; after the relativistic kinematics procedure, frame dependence reduced to maximally 4% in peak height.
- At q=600 MeV/c, differences between ANB and LAB frames were 11 MeV in peak position and 19% in peak height; after the procedure, frame dependence reduced to maximally 6% in peak height.
- At q=700 MeV/c, differences between ANB and LAB frames were 20 MeV in peak position and 24% in peak height; after the procedure, frame dependence reduced to maximally 9% in peak height, but the theoretical peak height overestimated experiment by about 20-30%.
- Using the proton form factor fit from MMD instead of the dipole fit reduced the peak height by about 4% at q=500 MeV/c, 6% at q=600 MeV/c, and 7% at q=700 MeV/c, bringing the discrepancy at 700 MeV/c to about 15%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Efros et al., 2004) of longitudinal response functions for three-nucleon systems at momentum transfers up to 500 MeV/c, where frame dependence was already noted as non-negligible above 400 MeV/c. The frame dependence problem had also been observed in deuteron electrodisintegration by Arenhoevel and collaborators (e.g., Fabian and Arenhoevel, 1978). The paper demonstrates that the active nucleon Breit frame, where nucleon momenta are limited to about q/2, yields results nearly identical to those from the proposed relativistic kinematics procedure, enabling reliable non-relativistic calculations at higher momentum transfers than previously possible.
