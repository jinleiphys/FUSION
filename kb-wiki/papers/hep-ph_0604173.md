# Proton and anti-proton production in the forward region of d+Au collisions at RHIC from the color glass condensate

> The power-law tail of high-pt pi^\pm spectra observed in forward d+Au collisions at RHIC can be attributed to the power-law decrease of the dipole forward scattering amplitude appearing in the color glass condensate (CGC) approach. Forward particle production probes the small-x gluon distribution of the target nucleus where its anomalous dimension is rather flat (gamma = 0.6 \sim 0.8) for moderately high pt (\lsim 5 GeV), and where the leading-twist DGLAP approximation is not valid. In the same framework, we examine p and \bar{p} production using baryon fragmentation functions parameterized in the Lund fragmentation scheme. This provides a good description of the forward \bar{p} spectrum while it underestimates the p data by as much as a factor of 2 \sim 3 at pt \lsim 4 GeV. Part of this anomalous baryon excess can be attributed to surviving constituent diquarks from the deuteron projectile. Thus, the contribution from diquark scattering may play an essential role for forward baryon formation.

## Key claim

The color glass condensate (CGC) framework, which successfully describes forward pion spectra via the power-law decrease of the dipole scattering amplitude, can also describe forward antiproton production in d+Au collisions at RHIC, but it underestimates proton data by a factor of 2-3 at transverse momenta below 4 GeV. This anomalous proton excess can be partially attributed to surviving constituent diquarks from the deuteron projectile that fragment into baryons.

## Method

The authors used a collinearly factorized CGC formalism with an asymmetric DGLAP-proj ⊗ BFKL-targ scheme, incorporating mass corrections for hadrons. They employed the KKT model parameterization for the dipole forward scattering amplitude with an anomalous dimension γ = 0.6-0.8, LO CTEQ5 parton distribution functions, and baryon fragmentation functions from the Lund string model (JETSET) rescaled to match the Q²-dependence of KKP or AKK fragmentation functions. For the diquark contribution, they used the Stockholm diquark model with a dipole form-factor and modeled diquark fragmentation using standard fragmentation functions multiplied by a breakup probability.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension γ of the target gluon distribution is γ = 0.6-0.8 for moderately high p_t (≲ 5 GeV) at forward rapidity
- The CGC formalism with standard PDFs and FFs underestimates forward proton data by a factor of 2-3 at p_t ≲ 4 GeV
- The saturation scale at b=0 is given by Q_s²(y_A,b=0) = A^{1/3} Q_0² (x_0/x_A)^λ with Q_0 ≈ 1 GeV, λ ≈ 0.3, and x_0 ≈ 3.0×10⁻⁴
- The average saturation scale for minimum bias collisions is ⟨Q_s²(b)⟩ = (2/3) Q_s²(y_A,b=0)
- The diquark form-factor uses M² = 10 GeV², corresponding to a diquark radius of about 0.2 fm
- The KKP NLO FFs are smaller than AKK NLO FFs by a factor of 5-12 in the region 1.5 ≲ p_t ≲ 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism previously applied to charged pion production in forward d+Au collisions at RHIC (Hayashigaki, 2006) to proton and antiproton production. It addresses the baryon excess puzzle observed by BRAHMS at forward rapidity (y ∼ 3), where the p/π⁺ ratio ∼ 0.8 is substantially larger than in p+p collisions, and where NLO pQCD calculations with standard fragmentation functions cannot reproduce the data (GSV, 2005). The diquark mechanism proposed here builds on earlier diquark models that successfully described FNAL data near midrapidity (diquark_pict, 2004) and the Stockholm diquark parameterization of the proton structure function (diquark_param, 1998). This work enables future quantitative studies of forward baryon production in p(d)+A collisions and its relation to saturation physics, particularly for understanding the transition from string breaking to projectile fragmentation regions.
