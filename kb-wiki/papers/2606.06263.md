# Spin-orbit correlation of quarks within quarkonium

> The spin-orbit correlation (SOC) provides a unique probe into the internal spin structure of hadrons. Defined via the parity-odd (P-odd) energy-momentum tensor (EMT), this observable can remain non-vanishing even in systems where the total angular momentum is zero. In this study, we connect the formal field-theoretical definition of the SOC to a non-perturbative quantum many-body framework utilizing light-front dynamics. Furthermore, we show how the SOC can be extracted directly from the hadronic matrix elements of the P-odd EMT, establishing a pathway to access this observable within the partonic picture. As a practical application, we compute the transverse and longitudinal SOC distributions for charmonium and $B_c$ mesons. While our findings align with rough estimates based on the Clebsch-Gordan decomposition, we demonstrate that these observables yield rich, non-trivial information regarding partonic dynamics.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit correlation (SOC) of quarks within quarkonium, defined via the parity-odd energy-momentum tensor, can remain non-vanishing even in spin-0 hadrons where total angular momentum is zero, and the authors compute this observable for charmonium and B_c mesons using light-front wave functions, finding results that align with non-relativistic quark model estimates but also reveal non-trivial relativistic effects such as non-zero SOC in S-wave states.

## Method

The authors connected the field-theoretical definition of the SOC via the parity-odd energy-momentum tensor to a light-front dynamics framework, deriving expressions for the SOC in terms of light-front wave functions. They then computed the transverse and longitudinal SOC distributions for charmonium and B_c mesons using wave functions obtained from basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) within the valence Fock sector, with basis truncation parameters N_max=8 for charmonium and N_max=32 for B_c systems.

## Context

This work extends the concept of spin-orbit correlation, first articulated by Lorce (2014) for the nucleon and subsequently extended to spin-0 systems by Lorce and Song (2025), to heavy quarkonia as pristine environments for investigating quantum many-body dynamics. The calculations using BLFQ provide quantitative predictions that can be compared with future lattice QCD calculations by Dudek (2011) and effective field theory approaches such as potential nonrelativistic QCD by Brambilla (2004), while also addressing the ongoing debate surrounding spin-orbit correlations in spin-0 hadrons.
