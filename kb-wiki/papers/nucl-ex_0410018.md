# Critical Behavior in Light Nuclear Systems: Experimental Aspects

> An extensive experimental survey of the features of the disassembly of a small quasi-projectile system with $A \sim$ 36, produced in the reactions of 47 MeV/nucleon $^{40}$Ar + $^{27}$Al, $^{48}$Ti and $^{58}$Ni, has been carried out. Nuclei in the excitation energy range of 1-9 MeV/u have been investigated employing a new method to reconstruct the quasi-projectile source. At an excitation energy $\sim$ 5.6 MeV/nucleon many observables indicate the presence of maximal fluctuations in the de-excitation processes. The fragment topological structure shows that the rank sorted fragments obey Zipf's law at the point of largest fluctuations providing another indication of a liquid gas phase transition. The caloric curve for this system shows a monotonic increase of temperature with excitation energy and no apparent plateau. The temperature at the point of maximal fluctuations is $8.3 \pm 0.5$ MeV. Taking this temperature as the critical temperature and employing the caloric curve information we have extracted the critical exponents $β$, $γ$ and $σ$ from the data. Their values are also consistent with the values of the universality class of the liquid gas phase transition. Taken together, this body of evidence strongly suggests a phase change in an equilibrated mesoscopic system at, or extremely close to, the critical point.

## Key claim

The data strongly suggest a phase change in an equilibrated mesoscopic system at, or extremely close to, the critical point, but the authors note that these observations do not guarantee critical behavior has been reached, and in contrast to heavier systems the caloric curve for A ~ 36 does not exhibit a plateau at the point of maximum fluctuations.

## Method

The authors studied quasi-projectiles with A ~ 36 produced in 47 MeV/nucleon 40Ar + 27Al, 48Ti, and 58Ni reactions, reconstructing the quasi-projectile source event-by-event using a new method combining three-source fits and Monte Carlo sampling to assign light charged particles to emission sources. They then analyzed the reconstructed quasi-projectile events across nine excitation energy windows from 1 to 9 MeV/nucleon using Fisher droplet model fits, Campi plots, normalized variances of order parameters, Zipf's law analysis, and extraction of critical exponents from scaling relations.

## Key numbers

* Maximum fluctuations in the disassembly of A ~ 36 occur at an excitation energy of 5.6 ± 0.5 MeV/nucleon.
* The temperature at the point of maximal fluctuations is 8.3 ± 0.5 MeV.
* The effective Fisher-law exponent τeff reaches a minimum of ~ 2.3 in the excitation energy range of 5 to 6 MeV/nucleon, close to the critical exponent of the liquid-gas phase transition universality class.
* The average quasi-projectile has a mass of 36 and a charge of 16.
* The highest excitation energy of the quasi-projectile can reach 9 MeV/nucleon.
* For the 40Ar + 58Ni reaction, 28000 good quasi-projectile events were accumulated; for 40Ar + 48Ti, 54000 events; for 40Ar + 27Al, 56000 events.
* The accepted good events in the violent collision bins account for 4.3% of total events for the 40Ar + 58Ni reaction.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear systems to the smallest system (A ~ 36) for which such extensive analysis has been attempted, reducing Coulomb contributions compared to heavier systems studied by the EOS collaboration (Hauger, 1998) and the ISiS collaboration (Beaulieu, Bauer). The results are consistent with earlier Fisher droplet model analyses by Elliott (2002, 2003) that extracted critical parameters for heavier systems, but the absence of a caloric curve plateau contrasts with results for heavier systems reported by Natowitz (2000). The findings enable further theoretical work on finite-size effects in phase transitions and raise questions about the role of Coulomb interactions, as discussed by Bonche, Song, Richert, and DasGupta.
