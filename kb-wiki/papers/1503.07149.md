# Linearized nonequilibrium dynamics in nonconformal plasma

> We investigate the behaviour of the lowest nonhydrodynamic modes in a class of holographic models which exhibit an equation of state closely mimicking the one determined from lattice QCD. We calculate the lowest quasinormal mode frequencies for a range of scalar self-interaction potentials and find that the damping of the quasinormal modes at the phase transition/crossover falls off by a factor of around two from conformality after factoring out standard conformal temperature dependence. The damping encoded in the imaginary part of the frequencies turns out to be correlated with the speed of sound and is basically independent of the UV details of the model. We also find that the dynamics of the nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom remains ultralocal, even to a higher degree, as we deviate from conformality. These results indicate that the role of nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom in the vicinity of the crossover transition may be enhanced.

## Key claim

The damping of the lowest nonhydrodynamic quasinormal modes in holographic models mimicking the QCD equation of state decreases by a factor of around two from conformality at the phase transition/crossover, after factoring out standard conformal temperature dependence, and this damping is correlated with the speed of sound and independent of the UV details of the model.

## Method

The authors studied a family of holographic bottom-up models coupling five-dimensional gravity to a single scalar field with a self-interaction potential chosen to reproduce the QCD equation of state from lattice data. They constructed black hole backgrounds in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates and solved the wave equation for an external massless scalar field to compute the lowest quasinormal mode frequencies as a function of temperature and momentum, using a Chebyshev spectral method to solve the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- The conformal limit value of the imaginary part of the lowest quasinormal mode frequency at zero momentum is Im ω_conf / (2π T) = -1.373.
- For potentials mimicking the QCD equation of state, the damping decreases by a factor of around two close to the crossover temperature T_c relative to the conformal high-temperature limit.
- The phenomenological fit parameters for the damping deviation from conformality are γ = -3.729 and γ' = 0.452 for potential V_2.
- The curvature of the damping at k=0, Im ω''(k=0) / (2π T), follows the speed of sound squared c_s^2 with proportionality constant α ≈ 1.114 for potential V_2.
- For the broader class of potentials (V_6 through V_11), the damping decreases by a factor of 2 to 7 relative to the conformal theory at high temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom from the conformal N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2004) to nonconformal holographic models that exhibit a QCD-like equation of state. The authors use the bottom-up approach of Gubser (2008) and Gubser et al. (2008) to construct backgrounds with a scalar potential, building on prior calculations of second-order hydrodynamic transport coefficients by Noronha (2014). The results indicate that nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom become more important near the QCD crossover, and the ultralocality property observed by Heller (2014) for conformal theories persists and even strengthens in nonconformal plasmas.
