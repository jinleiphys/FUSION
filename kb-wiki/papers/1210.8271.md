# High-p_T and Jets. A Summary of Results from Quark Matter 2012

> A broad range of new experimental data and theoretical results on the properties of hadronic matter under extreme conditions have been reported at Quark Matter 2012 conference. At this conference the scientific community was presented with a variety of measurements from the 2011 lead-lead LHC run using hard probe observables. Many measurements, such as boson-jet correlations, production rates of the b-jets, high precision jet fragmentation and others were shown for the first time. The new data from the LHC was matched by new techniques and analyses coming from RHIC experiments. This proceeding summarises the new measurements with high-p_T particles and jets and attempts to provide a theoretical explanation for the novel results presented at the conference

## Key claim

This conference summary reports that the wealth of new high-pT and jet measurements from the 2011 LHC lead-lead run and RHIC experiments, shown at Quark Matter 2012, provide detailed constraints on jet-medium interactions but that a quantitative description of the data requires additional tuning of models, and that the relative importance of radiative versus collisional energy loss processes remains unsettled.

## Method

The authors compiled and compared experimental results on inclusive hadron suppression (RAA, RCP), fully reconstructed jet suppression, di-jet momentum imbalance, boson-jet correlations, jet azimuthal anisotropy (v2, v3), jet radius dependence of suppression, and jet fragmentation functions from the ATLAS, CMS, ALICE, STAR, and PHENIX collaborations. They then evaluated these data against several theoretical models of parton energy loss (e.g., Buzzatti, Renk, Vitev, and others) that incorporate both radiative and collisional mechanisms with different angular structures and time-ordering assumptions.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron RCP at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 30-40 GeV reaches 0.2 at pT = 7 GeV at the highest RHIC energy.
- At pT > 80 GeV, charged hadron RAA at LHC reaches ~0.6 and does not grow with increasing pT.
- Jet RCP for fully reconstructed calorimetric jets from ATLAS and CMS agree within uncertainties; ALICE track-jets agree above pT = 70 GeV.
- RCP of muons associated with jets reaches approximately 0.5 in the most central events and is almost independent of pT.
- The v2 harmonic of charged particles remains significantly larger than zero at all measured pT; v3 vanishes at pT > 30 GeV.
- The average ratio of sub-leading to leading jet momentum shows significantly stronger imbalance compared to simulation at all measured pT, with the difference independent of leading jet pT.
- The double ratio of jet RCP with different radius R shows lesser suppression of jets with large R for pT < 100 GeV.
- The fragmentation function ratio in Pb+Pb relative to pp is consistent with unity for z -> 1, reaches a minimum just below z ~ 0.1 (xi ~ 3), and grows above unity towards small z.

## Context

This paper summarizes the experimental and theoretical results presented at the Quark Matter 2012 conference, extending the earlier measurements from the first LHC Pb+Pb run (2010) and the RHIC energy scan. It reports first results on boson-jet correlations, b-jet suppression, high-precision jet fragmentation, and fully reconstructed jet anisotropy. The new data enable discrimination between models of parton energy loss (e.g., Buzzatti, Renk, Vitev, and others) and highlight the need for theoretical advances in understanding the time structure of vacuum fragmentation and interference effects between multiple color charges in the medium, as discussed by Yacine and others.
