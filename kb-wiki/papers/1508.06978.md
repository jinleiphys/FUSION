# The contribution of NLO and LPM corrections to thermal dilepton emission in heavy ion collisions

> Recently lots of efforts have been made to obtain the next to leading order and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal corrections to the thermal dilepton emission rate in perturbative QCD. Here we apply these results to the plasma created in heavy ion collisions and see wether these corrections improve the comparison between theoretical calculations and experimental results for the invariant mass dependence of the dilepton emission rate. In particular, we simulate the quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and LHC using a 2+1-dimensional viscous hydro model. We compare our results to STAR experiment and comment on the need for a non-perturbative determination of the dilepton rate at low invariant mass.

## Key claim

Applying NLO and LPM corrections to the thermal dilepton emission rate in perturbative QCD does not uniformly improve agreement with STAR data: the NLO+LPM rate overshoots the experimental data at small invariant mass, while the NLO alone fits best but still overshoots for M < 0.5 GeV, indicating that a non-perturbative determination is needed in that region.

## Method

The authors simulate the quark-gluon plasma produced in Au-Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV) using the 2+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code SONIC, with Bjorken longitudinal expansion and a freeze-out temperature Tc=0.17 GeV. They integrate the LO, NLO, and NLO+LPM dilepton emission rates (tabulated from Ghisoiu and Laine (2014) and Ghiglieri and Moore (2014)) over the hydrodynamic evolution, boosting the lab-frame momenta to the local rest frame of each fluid cell. The resulting invariant mass spectra are averaged over impact parameter to match STAR centrality bins and compared to experimental data for |y|<1 and pT in [0.2,15] GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: T=0.37 GeV for RHIC, T=0.47 GeV for LHC
- Critical temperature for QGP phase boundary: Tc=0.17 GeV
- Shear viscosity over entropy ratio: eta/s=1/(4 pi)
- Starting proper time for hydrodynamics: tau_start=0.5 fm
- Transverse momentum integration range for RHIC: pT in [0.2,15] GeV
- Transverse momentum integration range for LHC: pT in [0,15] GeV
- Rapidity integration range for RHIC comparison: |y|<1
- Impact parameter range for 0-80% centrality in Au-Au: b=0-13 fm

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of the thermal dilepton rate by Ghisoiu and Laine (2014) (NLO and LPM to LO) and Ghiglieri and Moore (2014) (LPM at NLO) from the rate level to full heavy-ion collision observables using hydrodynamic simulations. It directly compares to STAR experimental data (2014) and finds that the LPM corrections, while suppressing the rate at very small M, enhance it at M~0.5 GeV too much to match experiment, suggesting that lattice QCD or other non-perturbative methods are needed for M<0.5 GeV. The predictions for LHC provide a testable benchmark for future experimental data that could help resolve the tension between higher-order calculations and STAR results.
