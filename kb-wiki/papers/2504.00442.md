# Transition magnetic moments for $Δ\rightarrow p$ transition in asymmetric nuclear matter

> In the present work we calculate the transition magnetic moments for the radiative decays of $Δ$ baryon to proton $(Δ\rightarrow p)$ in isospin asymmetric nuclear medium at finite temperature using chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. Within the framework of chiral SU(3) mean field model, the properties of baryons in asymmetric medium are modified through the exchange of scalar fields $(σ, ζ, δ)$ and vector fields $(ω, ρ)$. The isospin asymmetry of medium is taken into account via scalar-isovector field $δ$ and vector iso-vector field $ρ$. We calculate the in-medium masses of quarks, proton and $Δ$ baryon in asymmetric matter within the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model and use these as input in the chiral constituent quark ($χ$CQM) model to calculate the in-medium transition magnetic moments for $(Δ\rightarrow p)$ transition for different values of isospin asymmetry of hot and dense medium. For calculating the magnetic moments of baryons, contributions of valence quarks, quark sea and orbital angular momentum of quark sea are considered in these calculations.

## Key claim

The transition magnetic moment for the Δ⁺ → p γ decay decreases with increasing baryon density in nuclear matter, but the magnitude of this decrease is slightly moderated when the medium has finite isospin asymmetry (Iₐ > 0), especially at high density.

## Method

The authors combine two models: the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model, which provides in-medium quark and baryon masses by coupling quarks to scalar (σ, ζ, δ) and vector (ω, ρ) meson fields in a mean-field approximation, and the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), which computes the transition magnetic moment from valence quarks, sea quarks, and orbital angular momentum of the sea. The in-medium effective quark masses and baryon masses from the CQMF model are used as input to the χCQM, and the coupled mean-field equations are solved for various baryon densities, temperatures, and isospin asymmetry parameters Iₐ.

## Key numbers

- In symmetric nuclear matter (Iₐ = 0) at T = 0 MeV, the transition magnetic moment μ*(Δ → p) decreases by 35.71% when baryon density increases from vacuum to ρ₀, and by 65.77% when density increases to 3ρ₀.
- At Iₐ = 0.5 and T = 0 MeV, the decrease at ρ₀ is 34.82% and at 3ρ₀ is 63.69%.
- At T = 100 MeV and Iₐ = 0, the decrease from vacuum to ρ₀ is 30.65% and to 3ρ₀ is 61.31%.
- At T = 100 MeV and Iₐ = 0.5, the decrease from vacuum to ρ₀ is 30.36% and to 3ρ₀ is 60.42%.
- The strange quark vacuum mass offset is m_q0 = 77 MeV for the s quark.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon magnetic moments in nuclear matter using the CQMF model (Singh 2016, Singh 2017, Singh 2020) and the χCQM in free space (Dahiya 2018) to the specific case of the Δ → p transition in isospin-asymmetric, finite-temperature matter. The results enable future calculations of other decuplet-to-octet baryon transition magnetic moments in asymmetric nuclear matter, which are relevant for understanding the internal structure and deformation of baryons in dense environments probed by heavy-ion collision experiments such as those at FAIR and NICA.
