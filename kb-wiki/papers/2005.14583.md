# Accurate nuclear symmetry energy at finite temperature within a BHF approach

> We compute the free energy of asymmetric nuclear matter in a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach at finite temperature, paying particular attention to the dependence on isospin asymmetry. The first- and second-order symmetry energies are determined as functions of density and temperature and useful parametrizations are provided. We find small deviations from the quadratic isospin dependence and very small corresponding effects on (proto)neutron star structure.

# Accurate nuclear symmetry energy at finite temperature within a BHF approach

## Key claim

The quadratic (parabolic) dependence of the free energy on isospin asymmetry remains an excellent approximation for neutron star structure even at finite temperature up to 50 MeV, with second-order corrections producing effects that are nearly invisible on mass-radius relations.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy of asymmetric nuclear matter using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach at finite temperature, employing the Argonne V18 and Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potentials supplemented with compatible microscopic three-body forces. They extract the first- and second-order symmetry energies by evaluating the free energy at three isospin asymmetries (symmetric matter, asymmetric matter with proton fraction 0.2, and pure neutron matter) and provide analytical parametrizations valid for densities 0.05 to 1.0 fm⁻³ and temperatures 5 to 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of the quadratic β² fit for the free energy per nucleon: 0.47 MeV (V18 EOS) and 0.54 MeV (BOB EOS)
- RMS deviation of the linear β² fit: 1.51 MeV (V18) and 1.77 MeV (BOB)
- First-order symmetry energy at saturation density and zero temperature: J₂(0) = 31.0 MeV (V18) and 32.7 MeV (BOB)
- Slope parameter at saturation density and zero temperature: L₂(0) = 58.5 MeV (V18) and 64.2 MeV (BOB)
- Second-order symmetry energy at saturation density and zero temperature: J₄(0) = 0.41 MeV (V18) and 0.93 MeV (BOB)
- Fit parameters provided for densities 0.05 to 1.0 fm⁻³ and temperatures 5 to 50 MeV, with RMS deviations better than 0.3 MeV for all equations of state

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature BHF calculations of the symmetry energy (Bombaci 1991, Zuo 1999) to finite temperature, and goes beyond the parabolic approximation that becomes less reliable at high temperature (Zuo 2004, Tian 2013, Wellenhofer 2015). The results are compared with phenomenological equations of state (SFHo by Steiner 2013, Shen 1998) and with chiral effective field theory calculations (Wellenhofer 2015). The parametrizations enable practical use in simulations of hot neutron stars and neutron star mergers, where temperatures of several tens of MeV are relevant.
