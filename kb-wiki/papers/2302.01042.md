# Implementation of chiral two-nucleon forces to nuclear many-body methods with Gaussian-wave packets

> Many-body methods that use Gaussian-wave packets to describe nucleon-spatial distribution have been widely employed for depicting various phenomena in nuclear systems, in particular clustering. So far, however, the chiral effective field theory, a state-of-the-art theory of nuclear force, has not been applied to such methods. In this paper, we give the formalism to calculate the two-body matrix elements of the chiral two-nucleon forces using the Gaussian-wave packets. We also visualize the matrix elements and investigate the contributions of the central and tensor forces. This work is a foothold towards an \textit{ab initio} description of various cluster phenomena in view of nucleons, pions, and many-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

This paper provides the formalism to calculate two-body matrix elements of chiral two-nucleon forces (up to N3LO) for use in nuclear many-body methods that employ Gaussian-wave packets, such as antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), which have previously lacked a connection to chiral effective field theory. The work is presented as a "foothold" toward an ab initio description of cluster phenomena, meaning the actual numerical implementation and application to specific nuclei is not yet performed.

## Method

The author derives the multipole-expansion functions needed to express the chiral potential, originally given as a function of transferred momentum q and average momentum Q, in terms of the relative momenta p and p' of the initial and final two-nucleon states. These functions are then used to compute the two-body matrix elements of the chiral two-nucleon force within the Gaussian-wave-packet basis, separating contributions into central, spin-orbit, and tensor components for both contact terms and pion-exchange terms at each chiral order (LO, NLO, N2LO, N3LO). The formalism also incorporates nonlocal regularization with a cutoff momentum Lambda and includes the necessary parity and angular-momentum projection operators for constructing many-body states via the generator-coordinate method.

## Key numbers

- The chiral potential is expanded up to N3LO (chiral-expansion power n_chi = 4), with contributions at LO (n_chi=0), NLO (n_chi=2), N2LO (n_chi=3), and N3LO (n_chi=4).
- The nonlocal regulator is given by u_n(p,p',Lambda) = exp[-(p/Lambda)^(2n) - (p'/Lambda)^(2n)], where Lambda is the cutoff momentum and n is an integer.
- The one-pion exchange potential includes the factor (g_A/(2f_pi))^2, where g_A is the axial vector coupling constant and f_pi is the pion-decay constant.
- The center-of-mass kinetic energy expectation value for a Slater determinant is given as 3 hbar^2 nu nu' / (m_N (nu+nu')), where nu and nu' are the Gaussian width parameters and m_N is the average nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian-wave-packet-based many-body methods, such as antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) developed by Brink (1966) and others, by providing the formalism to incorporate chiral effective field theory forces that have become the state-of-the-art in nuclear physics. It enables future ab initio calculations of cluster phenomena (e.g., alpha clustering, molecular-like states in light nuclei) using realistic nuclear forces derived from quantum chromodynamics, rather than phenomenological interactions. The paper builds upon the chiral potentials developed by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Machleidt (2011) at N3LO, and the formalism is designed to be compatible with the generator-coordinate method and parity/angular-momentum projection techniques already used in AMD.
