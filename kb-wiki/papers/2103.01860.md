# Mapping topology to nuclear dilaton-HLS effective field theory for dense baryonic matter

> We describe the on-going effort to formulate the baryon-quark continuity in terms of a topology change in the equation of state (EoS) of dense baryonic matter in analogy to -- and inspired by -- the mapping of the characteristics of Chern-Simon topological field theory to Kohn-Sham density functional theory in the fractional quantized Hall effect (FQHE). This is done by translating the density-dependent characteristics of the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition formulated in the presence of hidden local symmetry and (hidden) scale symmetry to the density-dependent parameters of a renormalization-group approach to Fermi-liquid fixed point theory. Predictions made in finite nuclei and infinite compact-star matter are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change in dense baryonic matter, analogous to the mapping of Chern-Simons topological field theory to Kohn-Sham density functional theory in the fractional quantum Hall effect, can be encoded into an effective field theory (G$n$EFT) that successfully describes nuclear matter from low density up to compact-star densities, but the approach remains "mystifying" and "unorthodox" with the precise justification still incomplete.

## Method

The authors translate the density-dependent characteristics of the skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition, obtained from skyrmion crystal simulations using a Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry (HLS) and hidden scale symmetry (HSS), into density-dependent parameters of a renormalization-group approach to Fermi-liquid fixed point theory. The effective Lagrangian ${\cal L}_{{\psi\chi}{\rm HLS}}$ couples baryons to pions, vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$), and a dilaton field $\chi$ (identified with $f_0(500)$ as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of broken scale symmetry), with the topology change at density $n_{1/2}$ imposing constraints such as the vector manifestation fixed point, parity doubling, and the dilaton-limit fixed point.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Topology change (skyrmion-half-skyrmion transition) density range: $2 \lesssim n_{1/2}/n_0 \lesssim 4$
- Cutoff scale for standard chiral effective field theory ($s$ChEFT): $\Lambda_{sChEFT} \sim (400-500)$ MeV
- Vector meson mass scale for HLS: $m_V \sim 700$ MeV
- Dilaton mass in vacuum: $\sim 500$ MeV
- Chiral-invariant nucleon mass from parity doubling: $m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N$
- Vector manifestation fixed point density: $n_{\rm VM} \gtrsim 25 n_0$
- Dilaton-limit fixed point constraint: $g_A \to g_A^{\rm DL} = 1$
- Conformal sound velocity: $v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3$ (emerging in massive compact stars)

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the "genuine dilaton scenario" of Crewther and Tunstall (2015) to dense baryonic matter, building on the skyrmion crystal studies of Park, Vento, and collaborators (2010-2018). It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory ($s$ChEFT) approaches that break down above $\sim 2n_0$, and with phenomenological relativistic mean-field theories that lack direct connection to QCD topology. The approach enables predictions for the equation of state of compact stars, including the sound velocity and maximum mass, and suggests a resolution to the long-standing quenched $g_A$ problem in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions via the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point.
