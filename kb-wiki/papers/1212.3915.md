# Role of the cluster structure of $^7$Li in the dynamics of fragment capture

> Exclusive measurements of prompt $γ$-rays from the heavy-residues with various light charged particles in the $^7$Li + $^{198}$Pt system, at an energy near the Coulomb barrier (E/$V_b$ $\sim$ 1.6) are reported. Recent dynamic classical trajectory calculations, constrained by the measured fusion, $α$ and $t$ capture cross-sections have been used to explain the excitation energy dependence of the residue cross-sections. These calculations distinctly illustrate a two step process, breakup followed by fusion in case of the capture of $t$ and $α$ clusters; whereas for $^{6}$He + $p$ and $^{5}$He + $d$ configurations, massive transfer is inferred to be the dominant mechanism. The present work clearly demonstrates the role played by the cluster structures of $^7$Li in understanding the reaction dynamics at energies around the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dominant mechanism for capture of weakly bound clusters ($t$ and $\alpha$) from $^7$Li is breakup followed by fusion, while for more strongly bound clusters ($^6$He+$p$ and $^5$He+$d$) massive transfer is inferred to be the dominant mechanism, based on comparisons between exclusive particle-gamma coincidence data and classical dynamical model calculations.

## Method

Exclusive measurements of prompt $\gamma$-rays from heavy residues were performed in coincidence with light charged particles ($\alpha$, $t$, $d$, $p$) for the $^7$Li + $^{198}$Pt system at 45 MeV beam energy (E/$V_b$ ~ 1.6). The measured integrated cross-sections for complete fusion, $\alpha$-capture, and $t$-capture were used to constrain the three-body classical dynamical model implemented in the PLATYPUS code, which treats breakup stochastically and follows fragment time evolution. The differential cross-sections from PLATYPUS were then fed into the statistical model code PACE2 to calculate evaporation residue cross-sections for comparison with the experimental yields.

## Key numbers

- $\alpha$-capture cross-section at 45 MeV: 75 $\pm$ 5 mb (experiment), 77 mb (PLATYPUS calculation)
- $t$-capture cross-section at 45 MeV: 440 $\pm$ 10 mb (experiment), 366 mb (PLATYPUS calculation)
- Complete fusion cross-section at 45 MeV: 1004 $\pm$ 50 mb (experiment), 958 mb (PLATYPUS calculation)
- Breakup threshold for $\alpha$ + $t$ in $^7$Li: 2.47 MeV
- Breakup thresholds for $^6$He + $p$ and $^5$He + $d$ in $^7$Li: 9.98 MeV and 9.52 MeV, respectively
- Ground-state Q-values: $\alpha$ + $^{201}$Au = +8.9 MeV, $t$ + $^{202}$Hg = -2.6 MeV, $d$ + $^{203}$Hg = -2.8 MeV, $p$ + $^{204}$Hg = +2.4 MeV
- Average excitation energies from PLATYPUS: 28.0 MeV ($\alpha$-capture), 25.5 MeV ($t$-capture), 38.0 MeV ($^5$He capture), 42.0 MeV ($^6$He capture)
- Breakup probability function parameters: amplitude $A = 1.68 \times 10^4$, slope $\beta = 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of breakup fusion for weakly bound nuclei such as $^{6,7}$Li (Casten 1978, Utsunomiya 1983) by using the three-dimensional classical trajectory model of Diaz-Torres (2007, 2012) to interpret exclusive particle-gamma coincidence data for the first time. The results enable future studies of nuclear structure in nuclei formed as $^5$He+target or $^6$He+target using $^7$Li beams, and have implications for predicting production cross-sections of exotic nuclei with radioactive ion beams for high-spin spectroscopy. The authors note that no existing quantum approaches can quantify these measurements, making the development of such approaches a desirable theoretical challenge.
