# Formation of antideuterons in heavy ion collisions

> The antideuteron production rate at high-energy heavy ions collisions is calculated basing on the concept of anti-d formation by antinucleons which move in the mean field of the fireball constituents (mainly pions). The explicit formula is presented for the coalescence parameter B_2 in terms of deuteron binding energy and fireball volume.

# Formation of antideuterons in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The antideuteron coalescence parameter B₂ can be calculated from the deuteron binding energy and fireball volume by treating antideuteron formation as a fusion reaction of antinucleons moving in the mean field of fireball constituents, under the condition that a balance between antideuteron formation and disintegration by pion collisions is established. The approach assumes that the fireball is at a "dense gas" stage intermediate between chemical and thermal freeze-out, and the results are estimated to have about 50% uncertainty.

## Method
The authors model antideuteron production via the fusion reaction \(\bar{p} + \bar{n} \to \bar{d}\) occurring in the dense hadronic gas stage of fireball evolution, where particles are not on mass shell due to interactions with the medium. They use kinetic equations with Breit-Wigner mass distributions for antinucleons and antideuterons, incorporating the effective pnd coupling constant from Landau and the Bethe-Peierls theory of low-energy pn scattering. The coalescence parameter B₂ is derived from the balance between the formation rate and the disintegration rate from collisions with pions, expressed in terms of the deuteron binding energy ε, the antinucleon width Γ, and the fireball volume V.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy ε = 2.2 MeV, used as input parameter.
- Antinucleon width Γ ≈ 300 MeV at SPS energies (Pb+Pb at √s = 17 A GeV), with pion contribution about 75% and nucleon contribution about 25%.
- Theoretical B₂ for SPS (NA44): B₂^th = 3.0 × 10⁻⁴ GeV², compared with experimental B₂^exp = (4.4 ± 1.3) × 10⁻⁴ GeV² for the most central 10% events.
- Theoretical B₂ for RHIC (STAR, Au+Au at √s = 130 A GeV): B₂^th = 3.8 × 10⁻⁴ GeV², compared with experimental B₂^exp = (4.5 ± 0.3 ± 1.0) × 10⁻⁴ GeV².
- Theoretical B₂ for AGS (E864, Au+Pt at √s = 4.8 A GeV): B₂^th ∼ 1.5 × 10⁻³ GeV², compared with experimental B₂^exp = (4.1 ± 2.9 ± 2.3) × 10⁻³ GeV².
- Fireball volume at SPS: V = 6.2 × 10³ fm³; at RHIC: V = 7.2 × 10³ fm³; at AGS: V = 2.8 × 10³ fm³.
- Balance condition: 2Γ(m_N/E_p) ≈ 2.0 fm⁻¹, much larger than the fireball expansion rate estimate of about 0.25 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical approaches to antideuteron production in heavy ion collisions, including statistical models (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999; Cleymans et al., 1999), the model of antinucleon sources in the fireball (Mrazek, 2001), and the Wigner function approach (Gyulassy et al., 1983; Nagle et al., 1996). Unlike those earlier models, this calculation accounts for the interaction of antinucleons forming antideuterons with fireball constituents and for antideuteron disintegration by pion collisions. The results enable comparison with experimental data from NA44 at SPS, STAR at RHIC, and E864 at AGS to probe the fireball evolution stage between chemical and thermal freeze-out, and to test models of fireball evolution through the extracted parameter V⁻¹(\(\overline{n^2}_{\bar{p}}/\bar{n}^2_{\bar{p}}\)).
