# Exchange interaction radically changes behavior of a quantum particle in a classically forbidden region: simple model

> Exchange interaction strongly influences the long-range behavior of localized electron orbitals and quantum tunneling amplitudes.In the Hartree-Fock approximation the exchange produces a power-law decay instead of the usual exponential decrease at large distances. To show that this effect is real (i.e. not a result of the approximation) we consider a simple model where different effects may be accurately analyzed. Applications include huge enhancement of inner electron ionization by a static electric field or laser field considered in Ref. M. Ya. Amusia, arxiv:0904.4395

# Exchange interaction radically changes behavior of a quantum particle in a classically forbidden region: simple model

## Key claim
The exchange interaction between identical particles can produce a power-law decay of localized electron orbitals and quantum tunneling amplitudes instead of the usual exponential decrease at large distances, and this effect is real rather than an artifact of the Hartree-Fock approximation, as demonstrated in a simple two-well model where correlation effects can be accurately analyzed.

## Method
The author constructs a model of resonance tunneling between two potential wells with two identical particles: one in a ground state orbital (1) and one in a higher state (2) with energy close to the top of the barrier. The amplitude for the ground state particle to appear in the second well is derived from the Coulomb exchange integral G(2,1a;1b,2) and the Hartree-Fock equation, showing that the two-step process of particle 2 tunneling followed by exchange interaction transfers particle 1 without requiring its own exponentially small tunneling amplitude. The analysis is extended to cases with strong Coulomb repulsion and to the ionization of inner atomic electrons by an external electric field.

## Key numbers
- For inner orbitals inside molecules, the exchange-induced decay is proportional to r^{-2}.
- For macroscopic systems, the decay is proportional to cos(k_f r) r^{-nu}, where nu = 3 for 1D, nu = 3.5 for 2D, and nu = 4 for 3D crystals.
- In one example from Amusia (2009), the enhancement factor for ionization probability by an external electric field was 10^{39}.
- The exchange integral expansion starts from 1/|a-b|^2 when the distance between wells |a-b| is much larger than the orbital size r_1.
- For strong Coulomb repulsion, the effective mixing amplitude is t_eff ~ t_2^2 G(2b,1a;1b,2a)/Q^2, where Q = Q_aa - Q_ab is the difference in Coulomb energies.

## Context
This work extends the results of Flambaum (2009), DFS, Froese, and Handy on exchange-induced power-law decay in the Hartree-Fock approximation by providing a simple exactly analyzable model that confirms the effect is not an artifact of the approximation. The paper directly addresses the mechanism proposed by Amusia (2009) for huge enhancement of inner electron ionization by electric fields, and contrasts it with the atomic antenna mechanism of Kuchiev and Corkum. The results enable further study of coherent enhancement from multiple external electrons in many-electron atoms and clusters, as observed in ionization experiments by Borisov et al.
