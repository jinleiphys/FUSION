# The mass of charged pions in neutron star matter

> We examine the behavior of charged pions in neutron-rich matter using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. This study is motivated by the prospect that pions, or pion-like excitations, may be relevant in neutron-rich matter encountered in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. We find, as previously expected, that the $π^-$ mass increases with density and precludes s-wave condensation at $n_B\lesssim n_{\text{sat}}$, where $n_{\text{sat}} \simeq 0.16 \text{fm}^{-3}$ is the nuclear saturation density, and the mass of the $π^+$ mode decreases with density. The uncertainty in these predictions increases rapidly for $n_B \gtrsim n_{\text{sat}} $ because low energy constants associated with the two-pion-two-nucleon operators in chiral perturbation theory are poorly constrained. We find that these uncertainties are especially large in symmetric nuclear matter and should be included in the analysis of pion-nucleus interactions at low energy and pionic atoms. In neutron-rich matter, accounting for the self-energy difference between neutrons and protons related to the nuclear symmetry energy has several effects. It alters the power counting of certain higher-order contributions to the pion self-energy. Previously unimportant but attractive diagrams are enhanced and result in a modest reduction of the pion masses. Furthermore, in the low-wavelength limit, a collective mode with the quantum numbers of the $π^+$ appears.

## Key claim

The authors find that the π⁻ mass increases with density and precludes s-wave condensation at baryon densities n_B ≲ n_sat (where n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³), but the uncertainty in these predictions increases rapidly for n_B ≳ n_sat because low-energy constants associated with two-pion-two-nucleon operators in chiral perturbation theory are poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors compute the charged pion self-energy in neutron-rich matter using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) up to O(q⁶), including both single-nucleon and two-nucleon contributions. They augment the calculation with a model for the nucleon self-energy that incorporates energy shifts of neutrons and protons in dense nuclear medium, and they include previously neglected two-loop diagrams involving p-wave interactions in the intermediate state. The nucleon self-energy is fit to dense matter data, and uncertainties are estimated by examining contributions from lowest-order ππNNNN operators with unknown coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Pion mass in vacuum: m_π = 139 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.27
- Chemical potential for negative charge in outer core of neutron star: μ̂ ≈ 100 MeV, possibly up to 300 MeV in inner core
- Empirical value of isovector scattering amplitude at threshold: T⁻(m_π) = 1.85 fm
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_N ≈ 45 ± 15 MeV
- Nucleon Fermi momentum at saturation: k_F ∼ 300 MeV
- Scale Λ_NN = (16π f_π² / g_A² M) ≈ 300 MeV

## Context

This work revisits calculations of pion condensation in dense matter first proposed by Sawyer and Scalapino (1972) and Migdal (1973), extending earlier model-dependent analyses from the 1970s and 1980s. The calculation improves upon previous χPT calculations of the pion self-energy in asymmetric matter by Kaiser (2001) and Kolomeitsev (2002) by including additional diagrams and elucidating the role of pion coupling to two-nucleon currents. The results are relevant for understanding the ground state and response properties of dense nuclear matter at finite temperatures in neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernovae.
