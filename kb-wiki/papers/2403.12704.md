# $J/ψ$ production within a jet in high-energy proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Within the framework of leading power factorization formalism of nonrelativistic quantum chromodynamics, we calculate the jet fragmentation function for $J/ψ$ production in proton-proton (pp) collisions ranging from $\sqrt{s}=500$ GeV to $13$ TeV. The reasonable agreements between theory and experimental data indicate that $J/ψ$ production within a jet is mainly dominated by gluon fragmentation. Such a mechanism can be further tested by the predicted jet transverse momentum and radius dependence of jet fragmentation function. Based on the satisfying description of pp baseline, we carry out the first theoretical investigation on medium modification on $J/ψ$ production within jet in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, using a linear Boltzmann transport model combined with hydrodynamics for the simulation of jet-medium interaction. The consistency with the experimental measurement on nuclear modification factor $R_{\text{AA}}$ by CMS collaboration reveals that the gluon jet quenching is the driving force for the suppression of $J/ψ$ production in jet. Furthermore, we make predictions for the dependence of $R_{\text{AA}}$ on the jet transverse momentum and jet radius $R$, which can be tested in future measurements to further constrain the flavor dependence of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The paper claims that J/ψ production within a jet in both proton-proton and lead-lead collisions is dominated by gluon fragmentation (over 85% of the yield), and that the suppression observed in heavy-ion collisions is driven by gluon jet quenching rather than by charm quark energy loss, contrary to intuitive expectations.

## Method

The authors calculate the jet fragmentation function for J/ψ production using leading-power NRQCD factorization, convolving hard QCD partonic cross sections generated by MadGraph and showered by PYTHIA with leading-order NRQCD fragmentation functions for gluon and charm channels. For heavy-ion collisions, they simulate jet-medium interactions using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model combined with 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamics for the bulk medium evolution, with the strong coupling fixed to α_s = 0.18.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions, gluon fragmentation contributes >85% of J/ψ production within jets across a wide range of z_J/ψ at all three collision energies studied (500 GeV, 5.02 TeV, 13 TeV).
- The normalized z_J/ψ distributions are compared with experimental data from LHCb at √s = 13 TeV (jet R=0.5, p_T^jet > 20 GeV/c), CMS at √s = 5.02 TeV (jet R=0.3, 30 < p_T^jet < 40 GeV/c), and STAR at √s = 500 GeV (jet R=0.4, p_T^jet > 10 GeV/c).
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA(z_J/ψ) at √s = 5.02 TeV shows overall suppression and a slight rising trend with increasing z_J/ψ, consistent with CMS measurements.
- Predictions show mild p_T^jet dependence of R_AA but strong jet radius R dependence: with R=0.6 the R_AA shows a downward trend at large z_J/ψ, while with R=0.3 it shows a rising trend.

## Context

This work extends the fragmenting jet function approach of Bain (2016, 2017) and the gluon fragmentation improved PYTHIA method to heavy-ion collisions, providing the first theoretical description of the CMS measurement of J/ψ production within jets in PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The results contradict the expectation that nuclear modification for J/ψ production is driven by charm quark energy loss, as seen in D^0 measurements by Li (2022), and instead demonstrate that gluon jet quenching is the dominant mechanism. The predictions for p_T^jet and jet radius dependence of R_AA can be tested in future LHC measurements to further constrain the flavor dependence of jet quenching and the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
