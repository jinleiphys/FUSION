# L-Particle and Kaluza-Klein World

> We present some arguments in favour of that the Kaluza-Klein picture of the world has been confirmed in the experiments at very low energies where the nucleon-nucleon dynamics has been studied. Early predicted ${\cal L}$-particle is related to the new scale of strong nucleon-nucleon forces in the spirit of Kaluza-Klein approach. It is shown that KK excitations remarkably describe the experimentally observed mass spectrum of diproton system.

# L-Particle and Kaluza-Klein World

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Kaluza-Klein picture of extra dimensions has already been confirmed in low-energy nucleon-nucleon experiments, specifically through the mass spectrum of the diproton system. The claim is hedged by the assumption that the protons can be treated as scalar particles in the simplest model, and that the KK tower corresponds to either a one-dimensional compact extra space or a d-dimensional torus with a specific Diophantine constraint on the mode numbers.

## Method

The author fits a theoretical formula for the proton-proton total cross section to existing accelerator and cosmic ray data, extracting a new threshold energy near the elastic threshold. This threshold is interpreted as the mass of a new particle (the L-particle) at 1.833 MeV above twice the proton mass. Using the Kaluza-Klein mass formula for KK excitations, the size of the compact extra dimension is calculated from the L-particle mass, and a KK tower of diproton states is constructed and compared to experimentally observed diproton resonance masses.

## Key numbers

- L-particle mass: m_L = 1.833 MeV, extracted from the threshold sqrt(s_thr) = 3.5283 GeV^2
- Compact extra dimension size: R = 24.1 GeV^{-1} = 4.75 x 10^{-13} cm, corresponding to 1/R = 41.481 MeV
- Fundamental mass scale for d=6 extra dimensions: M ~ 5 TeV
- First KK excitation mass (n=1): M_1 = 1878.38 MeV, compared to experimental diproton mass 1877.5 ± 0.5 MeV
- Estimated partial width for KK boson decay: Gamma_n ~ n · 0.4 MeV
- Fitted parameters for total cross section formula: c_1 = (192.85 ± 1.68) GeV^{-2}, c_2 = (186.02 ± 1.67) GeV^{-2}, s_thr = (3.5283 ± 0.0052) GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Kaluza-Klein scenario to low-energy nuclear physics, claiming that the diproton resonance spectrum observed in proton-proton scattering (with resonances documented by multiple experimental groups cited as [6], [7], [8], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14], [15], [16]) can be described by a KK tower of excitations. The author contrasts this with the standard view that KK excitations would only be observable at super-high energies above 1 TeV, and notes that the brane world scenario (referenced to review articles [1,2]) was proposed to allow large extra dimensions. The work enables a reinterpretation of existing low-energy nuclear data as evidence for extra dimensions, and suggests that a suitable compact manifold could provide a global solution to the spectral problem of all particles and nuclei.
