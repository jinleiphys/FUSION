# Shape transitions in proton-neutron systems

> We explore the possibility of shape transitions in proton-neutron systems driven by deformation differences between proton and neutron fluids. Within the framework of the proton-neutron interacting boson model, we show that such dynamic shape transitions cannot occur in well deformed nuclei but are a possibility in transitional nuclei. Likely candidates in the Os-Pt isotopes are discussed and predictions of the model are compared with the existing data.

# Shape transitions in proton-neutron systems

## Key claim
Within the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2), dynamic shape transitions driven by deformation differences between proton and neutron fluids cannot occur in well deformed nuclei but are a possibility in transitional nuclei, provided that the F-spin vector parameters N_v and χ_v have the same sign (for prolate starting shapes) and that the boson number is sufficiently high to avoid early cutoff effects.

## Method
The authors employed the 1/N expansion method (angular momentum projected mean field theory) for analytic study of the energy surface, and exact numerical diagonalization using the NPBOS code for quantitative predictions in transitional nuclei. They used the simplest IBM-2 Hamiltonian with quadrupole-quadrupole and Majorana interactions, and calculated yrast spectroscopic quadrupole moments as the signature for shape change (negative for prolate, positive for oblate). Systematic variation of the F-spin vector parameters (N_v = N_π - N_ν, χ_v, ε_v) was performed to identify conditions for a prolate-oblate shape transition.

## Key numbers
- In the deformed test case, the prolate minimum energy was E_min = -4.83 MeV with an energy difference ΔE = 2.23 MeV to the oblate minimum, with no possibility of crossover at higher spin.
- In the transitional test case, the energy difference between minima was ΔE = 0.85 MeV, with the possibility of crossover at some critical spin.
- For the favorable transitional case (N=12, N_v=4, χ_v=2, ε_v=0), the quadrupole moment Q(L) changed sign between spins L=16-18.
- Including ε_v = -0.4 MeV reduced the energy difference ΔE from 0.85 MeV to 0.68 MeV in the transitional case.
- For 192Os (N=8), the calculated quadrupole moments did not actually change sign, attributed to the low boson number causing early cutoff effects.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of shape transitions in the sdg boson model (Kuyucak, 1991) and addresses the possibility of dynamic shape transitions in two-fluid (proton-neutron) systems. It builds on measurements of neutron deformation using pion beams (Knudsen, 1991) and g-factor anomalies in Os-Pt isotopes (Kuyucak, 1995). The predictions for 192Os are compared with the pairing-plus-quadrupole model calculations (Kumar, 1972) that first predicted static prolate-oblate shape-phase transitions in this region. The authors note that more precise quadrupole moment measurements with new detector systems (Euroball, Gamma-Sphere) are needed to test the prediction, and that observation of such transitions would provide an independent test of the F-spin breaking mechanism used to explain M1 properties in IBM-2.
