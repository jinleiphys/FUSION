# Light-cone Distribution Amplitudes of the Nucleon and Negative Parity Nucleon Resonances from Lattice QCD

> We present the results of a lattice study of light-cone distribution amplitudes (DAs) of the nucleon and negative parity nucleon resonances using two flavors of dynamical (clover) fermions on lattices of different volumes and pion masses down to m_pi = 150 MeV. We find that the three valence quarks in the proton share their momentum in the proportion 37% : 31% : 31%, where the larger fraction corresponds to the u-quark that carries proton helicity, and determine the value of the wave function at the origin in position space, which turns out to be small compared to the existing estimates based on QCD sum rules. Higher-order moments are constrained by our data and are all compatible with zero within our uncertainties. We also calculate the normalization constants of the higher-twist DAs that are related to the distribution of quark angular momentum. Furthermore, we use the variational method and customized parity projection operators to study the states with negative parity. In this way we are able to separate the contributions of the two lowest states that, as we argue, possibly correspond to N*(1535) and a mixture of N*(1650) and the pion-nucleon continuum, respectively. It turns out that the state that we identify with N*(1535) has a very different DA as compared to both the second observed negative parity state and the nucleon, which may explain the difference in the decay patterns of N*(1535) and N*(1650) observed in experiment.

## Key claim

The three valence quarks in the proton share their momentum in the proportion 37% : 31% : 31%, where the larger fraction corresponds to the u-quark that carries proton helicity; the state identified with N*(1535) has a qualitatively different distribution amplitude compared to both the second observed negative parity state and the nucleon, which may explain the difference in the decay patterns of N*(1535) and N*(1650) observed in experiment.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using two flavors of dynamical (clover) fermions on lattices of different volumes and pion masses down to 150 MeV. They calculated moments of light-cone distribution amplitudes (DAs) by relating them to matrix elements of local three-quark operators with covariant derivatives, classified according to irreducible representations of the spinorial hypercubic group to control operator mixing. The variational method and customized parity projection operators were used to separate the contributions of the two lowest negative parity states.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon coupling f_N = 2.84(1)(33) x 10^{-3} GeV^2 at scale mu^2 = 4 GeV^2, approximately 30% smaller than existing QCD sum rule estimates
* Nucleon couplings lambda_1 = -4.13(2)(20) x 10^{-2} GeV^2 and lambda_2 = 8.19(5)(39) x 10^{-2} GeV^2 at scale mu^2 = 4 GeV^2
* Momentum fractions for valence quarks in the proton: <x_1> = 0.372(7), <x_2> = 0.314(3), <x_3> = 0.314(7), where the first number corresponds to the u-quark carrying proton helicity
* Pion masses as low as m_pi approximately 150 MeV were used in the simulations

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations by the QCDSF collaboration (Gockeler 2008, Braun 2008) and an exploratory study of negative parity nucleon resonances (Braun 2009) by using a much larger set of lattices with different volumes, lattice spacings, and pion masses down to 150 MeV. The results enable first reliable chiral and infinite volume extrapolations to the physical limit, and are important for forthcoming studies of electroproduction of nucleon resonances at large momentum transfers at the 12 GeV upgrade of the Jefferson Lab accelerator facility. The finding that the N*(1535) has a very different DA compared to the nucleon and the second negative parity state may explain experimentally observed differences in their decay patterns.
