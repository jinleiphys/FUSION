# Constraints on Variation of the Weak Scale from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> Recently, the EMPRESS collaboration has included new data in the extraction of the primordial $^4$He abundance from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), resulting in a determination that differs from the previous value and from theoretical expectations. There have been several studies attempting to explain this anomaly which involve variation of fundamental constants between the time of BBN and the present. Since the Higgs vacuum expectation value (vev) is the only dimensionful parameter in the Standard Model and it is already known to vary during the electroweak phase transition, we consider the possibility that the vev is slightly different during BBN compared to its present value. A modification of the vev changes not only particle masses but also affects, through mass thresholds, the QCD confinement scale. We use the recently developed PRyMordial program to study this variation and its impact on the $^4$He and deuterium abundances. We find that bounds on $|{δv}/{v}|$ are approximately $0.01$, and that the EMPRESS result can be explained within $2σ$ if $0.008 < {δv}/{v}< 0.02$, but at the cost of worsening the current $2σ$ discrepancy in the deuterium abundance to over $3σ$.

## Key claim
The Higgs vacuum expectation value (vev) could have been slightly different during Big Bang Nucleosynthesis compared to its present value, with bounds on |δv/v| of approximately 0.01; the EMPRESS ⁴He anomaly can be explained within 2σ if 0.008 < δv/v < 0.02, but this worsens the existing 2σ discrepancy in deuterium abundance to over 3σ.

## Method
The authors used the PRyMordial BBN code to compute primordial ⁴He and deuterium abundances as functions of the Higgs vev, incorporating the vev's effects on the neutron-proton mass difference, the deuteron binding energy (modeled via π, σ, and ω meson exchange potentials), and the QCD confinement scale Λ_QCD (which scales as v^0.25 due to quark mass threshold effects). They scaled the electron mass and W boson mass with the vev, and assumed the 12 key thermonuclear reaction rates vary as Λ_QCD. The network of Boltzmann equations was evolved from neutron freeze-out through deuterium photo-disassociation down to keV temperatures.

## Key numbers
- Bounds on |δv/v| are approximately 0.01 from combined BBN constraints.
- The EMPRESS ⁴He result can be explained within 2σ if 0.008 < δv/v < 0.02.
- For negative δv/v, the 2σ upper bound on the magnitude is approximately 0.001 from ⁴He (PDG value) and approximately 0.025 from deuterium.
- For positive δv/v using the PDG ⁴He result, the magnitude must be less than 0.008 at 2σ.
- The deuterium abundance theoretical uncertainty is 0.05, similar to the experimental error.
- Λ_QCD scales as (δv/v)^0.25, with the exponent varying from 0.245 to 0.255 over the possible range.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of Higgs vev variation during BBN by Dixit and Sher (1988) and others (Yoo 2002, Chamoun 2005, Gassner 2006, Landau 2008), which did not include the effect of vev variation on Λ_QCD through quark mass thresholds. It also builds on the anthropic studies of Agrawal et al. (1997, 1998) and Jeltema and Sher (1999) that considered this threshold effect. The use of the modern PRyMordial code enables more accurate calculations than previous work. The results show that vev variation cannot simultaneously resolve both the EMPRESS ⁴He anomaly and the deuterium discrepancy, motivating further experimental and theoretical work on both abundances.
