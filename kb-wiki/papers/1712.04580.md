# A flow paradigm in heavy-ion collisions

> The success of hydrodynamics in high energy heavy-ion collisions leads to a flow paradigm, to understand the observed features of harmonic flow in terms of the medium collective expansion regarding initial state geometrical properties. In this review, we present some essential ingredients in the flow paradigm, including the hydrodynamic modeling, the characterization of initial state geometry and the medium response relations. The extension of the flow paradigm to small colliding systems is also discussed.

## Key claim

The flow paradigm posits that observed harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions can be understood as a medium response to initial-state geometrical properties, but this framework relies on effective parameterizations of the initial state and transport properties that introduce substantial uncertainties, and its validity must be re-examined for small colliding systems where the onset of hydrodynamics is less certain.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of the flow paradigm, which uses viscous hydrodynamics (typically truncated at second order in the gradient expansion, following the Israel-Stewart formulation) to model the collective expansion of the quark-gluon plasma. Initial-state geometry is characterized by eccentricities \(\mathcal{E}_n\) defined from the azimuthal moments of the energy density profile, and the medium response relations between these eccentricities and harmonic flow coefficients \(V_n\) are established empirically from hydrodynamic simulations. The review also discusses how event-by-event fluctuations in the initial state, captured by Monte Carlo models such as MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and IP-Glasma, lead to fluctuations and correlations in the observed flow harmonics.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density in QGP is close to the lower bound \(\eta/s = O(\hbar/(4\pi k_B))\) from gauge-gravity duality.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic simulations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is \(\tau_0 \sim O(1)\) fm/c; for smaller colliding systems, some simulations suggest \(\tau_0 \sim O(0.1)\) fm/c.
- Over 99% of total particle yields in heavy-ion experiments are soft particles (small transverse momentum), whose correlations are described by the flow paradigm.
- The elliptic flow \(V_2\) is the most dominant flow signature, with the reaction-plane ellipticity defined as \(\varepsilon_2^{RP} = \{x^2 - y^2\} / \{x^2 + y^2\}\), bounded by unity.

## Context

This review extends the flow paradigm established from hydrodynamic modeling of nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC, building on earlier work by Ollitrault (1992) linking elliptic flow to almond-shaped initial geometry and by Alver (2010) on triangular flow. The paper discusses how the paradigm is now being generalized to small colliding systems such as proton-lead, deuteron-gold, and high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions, where the validity of viscous hydrodynamics must be re-examined and where thermal fluctuations (described by the fluctuation-dissipation relation) may become significant. The framework enables quantitative constraints on the extraction of \(\eta/s\) while minimizing dependence on effective parameterizations of the initial state.
