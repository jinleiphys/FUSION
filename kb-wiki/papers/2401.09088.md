# Limiting attractors in heavy-ion collisions -- the interplay between bottom-up and hydrodynamic attractors

> In this contribution to the Quark Matter 2023 proceedings, we study the hydrodynamization process in heavy-ion collisions using QCD kinetic theory and introduce the new concept of limiting attractors. They are defined via an extrapolation of observables to vanishing and infinite couplings. We find that the pressure ratio exhibits both a hydrodynamic and a bottom-up limiting attractor, while the ratios of hard probes transport coefficients $\hat q^{zz}/\hat q^{yy}$ and $κ_T/κ_z$ are better described in terms of the new bottom-up limiting attractor.

## Key claim
The paper introduces the concept of "limiting attractors" defined by extrapolating observables to vanishing and infinite coupling at fixed rescaled time, and shows that while the pressure ratio exhibits both a hydrodynamic and a bottom-up limiting attractor, the ratios of hard probe transport coefficients are better described by the bottom-up limiting attractor.

## Method
The authors performed QCD kinetic theory simulations of the Boltzmann equation for a gluon plasma, evolving the distribution function in a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous geometry. They varied the coupling over the range 0.5 ≤ λ ≤ 20 and used different initial conditions with varying initial anisotropy parameter ξ. For each observable, they extrapolated results to λ → 0 (bottom-up attractor) and λ → ∞ (hydrodynamic attractor) at fixed rescaled times using the bottom-up thermalization time τ_BMSS and the relaxation time τ_R respectively.

## Key numbers
- Coupling range studied: 0.5 ≤ λ ≤ 20
- Bottom-up time scale: τ_BMSS = α_s^{-13/5} / Q_s
- Relaxation time scale: τ_R = 4π(η/s) / T
- Initial condition mean transverse momentum: ⟨p_T⟩ = 1.8 Q_s
- Parametrization coefficients for bottom-up limiting attractor of jet quenching ratio: c_1^q̂ = 0.12, c_2^q̂ = 3.45
- Parametrization coefficients for bottom-up limiting attractor of heavy quark diffusion ratio: c_1^κ = 0.093, c_2^κ = 1.33
- Parametrization valid for τ ≳ 0.01 τ_BMSS

## Context
This work extends the concept of hydrodynamic attractors (Heller 2015) and earlier kinetic theory attractor studies at fixed coupling (Almaalol 2020) and with varying couplings (Kurkela 2018) by introducing limiting attractors that interpolate between weak and strong coupling. The results enable a universal description of hard probe transport coefficients (jet quenching parameter q̂ and heavy quark diffusion coefficient κ) during the pre-hydrodynamic stage, building on recent work on these coefficients in non-equilibrium plasmas (Boguslavski 2023).
