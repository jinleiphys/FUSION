# Spectator isobar production in the $A\left( {γ,πNN} \right)B$ reaction

> We present an analysis of the spectator mechanism of $Δ$-isobar production in the pion photoproduction on nuclei with the emission of two nucleons. The reaction mechanism is studied within the framework of the $ΔN$-correlation model, which considers the isobar and nucleon of the $ΔN$-system produced in the nucleus at the virtual $NN \to ΔN$ transition, to be in a dynamic relationship. The two-particle transition operator for nuclei is obtained by the \textit{S}-matrix approach. We consider the properties of the spectator mechanism of isobar production using the example of the reaction $^{16}$O$ (γ,\ π^{ - }pn)^{14}$O. Numerical estimates of the cross section are obtained in the kinematic region, where it is possible to expect the manifestation of bound isobar-nuclear states.

## Key claim

The spectator mechanism of Δ-isobar production in the A(γ,πNN)B reaction can mimic the signature of bound Δ-nuclear states, meaning that experimental signals previously interpreted as evidence for Δ-nuclei may instead arise from this spectator process under certain kinematic conditions.

## Method

The authors developed a ΔN-correlation model in which the wave function of the initial nucleus includes both nucleon and isobar configurations, with the ΔN-system produced via a virtual NN→ΔN transition. They derived the two-particle transition operator tγπ for nuclei using an S-matrix approach in relativistic formalism, then applied the model to the specific reaction 16O(γ,π−pn)14O in the plane wave approximation. The cross sections were calculated for kinematic regions where bound isobar-nuclear states might be expected, and compared with contributions from direct reaction mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy: Eγ = 500 MeV for all calculations
- Pion momentum: pπ = 170 MeV/c
- Proton momentum: pp = 470 MeV/c (with additional calculations at 570 MeV/c and 620 MeV/c)
- The cross section for Δ0-isobar production dominates over Δ−-isobar production at proton emission angles of 90° and 180°, with the Δ− process suppressed due to the extremely small Fourier component of bound proton wave functions at momentum p̃n1 = pn1 − pγ
- The Δ0-isobar production cross section is maximal when the opening angle between the pion and proton is 180°
- The excitation energy Eex = M15OΔ − M15O distribution from the spectator mechanism overlaps with empirical data for Δ-nuclei excitation energies from four prior experiments (Bartsch, Glavanakov 2008, Liang, Argan)

## Context

This work extends the ΔN-correlation model previously developed by Glavanakov (2013) for direct isobar knockout to the spectator mechanism of isobar production, which was proposed in Glavanakov (2017). The spectator mechanism can produce angular distributions of pion-nucleon pairs similar to those expected from bound Δ-nuclei, complicating the interpretation of data from experiments at MAMI in Mainz (Bartsch) and the Tomsk synchrotron (Glavanakov 2008) that were interpreted as evidence for Δ-nuclear states. The model enables future experimental discrimination between spectator isobar production and genuine Δ-nucleus formation by examining the momentum dependence of the cross section, which differs significantly between the two processes.
