# Constraints on Regge models from perturbation theory

> We study the constraints that the operator product expansion imposes on large $N_c$ inspired QCD models for current-current correlators. We focus on the constraints obtained by going beyond the leading-order parton computation. We explicitly show that, assumed a given mass spectrum: linear Regge behavior in $n$ (the principal quantum number) plus corrections in $1/n$, we can obtain the logarithmic (and constant) behavior in $n$ of the decay constants within a systematic expansion in $1/n$. Our example shows that it is possible to have different large $n$ behavior for the vector and pseudo-vector mass spectrum and yet comply with all the constraints from the operator product expansion.

# Constraints on Regge models from perturbation theory

## Key claim

The operator product expansion (OPE) does not force the Regge slopes for vector and axial-vector channels to be equal, contrary to claims in some earlier literature; different large-\(n\) behavior for the vector and pseudo-vector mass spectra can be accommodated while still satisfying all OPE constraints, provided the decay constants acquire the appropriate logarithmic and power corrections in \(n\).

## Method

The authors work in the large-\(N_c\) limit with infinitely narrow resonances and assume a linear Regge mass spectrum for high excitations, \(M_V^2(n) = B_V n + A_V + C_V/n + \cdots\), plus a systematic expansion of the decay constants \(F_V^2(n)\) in powers of \(1/n\) and \(1/\ln n\). They match the hadronic Adler function (sum over resonances) to its OPE expression order by order in \(1/Q^2\), using the Euler-MacLaurin formula to convert sums into integrals and extract logarithmic terms. The matching at LO fixes the leading \(n\)-dependence of \(F_V^2(n)\) from perturbation theory; at NLO the vanishing of the \(1/Q^2\) condensate fixes the \(1/n\) correction; at NNLO the gluon condensate term fixes the \(1/n^2\) correction.

## Key numbers

- Regge slope for vector channel (model I): \(B_V = 1.525 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Regge slope for vector channel (model II): \(B_V = 1.128 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Regge slope for axial-vector channel: \(B_A = 1.278 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Intercept for vector (I): \(A_V = -1.038 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Intercept for vector (II): \(A_V = 0.353 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Intercept for axial-vector: \(A_A = -0.100 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- \(1/n\) coefficient for vector (I): \(C_V = 0.123 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- \(1/n\) coefficient for vector (II): \(C_V = -0.885 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- \(1/n\) coefficient for axial-vector: \(C_A = 0.349 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\)
- Leading-order decay constant for vector (I) at \(n=1\): \(F_V = 156\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (experimental: \(156 \pm 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\))
- Leading-order decay constant for axial-vector at \(n=1\): \(F_A = 123\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (experimental: \(122 \pm 24\ \mathrm{MeV}\))
- Gluon condensate used: \(\beta \langle G^2 \rangle = -(352\ \mathrm{MeV})^4\)
- Strong coupling at 1 GeV: \(\alpha_s(1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 0.5\)

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining hadronic models via the OPE, going beyond the leading-order parton analysis that was the focus of earlier studies such as Beane (2001) and Cohen (2002). It provides a systematic framework for incorporating power-like \(1/n\) corrections to the Regge spectrum, in contrast to Afonin (2004) which allowed only exponentially suppressed deviations. The results enable future studies of renormalon effects in the relation between decay constants and mass spectra, and support the possibility of different Regge slopes for vector and axial-vector channels as found in AdS/CFT analyses by Casero (2007).
