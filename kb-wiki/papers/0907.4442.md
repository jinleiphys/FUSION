# Compared analysis of proton electromagnetic form factors in space-like and time-like regions

> New possibilities of high precision measurements of hadron form factors in annihilation and scattering reactions over an unexplored kinematical region suggest a compared analysis, in view of a global description of the nucleon structure.

# Compared analysis of proton electromagnetic form factors in space-like and time-like regions

## Key claim
A unified description of proton electromagnetic form factors across space-like and time-like regions is possible, but the simple extrapolation of models and methods between regions must be taken very carefully due to unresolved discrepancies between polarized and unpolarized measurements in the space-like region and the potential role of two-photon exchange contributions.

## Method
The paper presents a formalism-based comparison of proton electromagnetic form factors extracted from elastic electron-proton scattering (space-like region) and from annihilation reactions such as antiproton-proton to lepton pairs (time-like region), assuming one-photon exchange. The analysis contrasts the Rosenbluth separation method for unpolarized cross sections with polarization transfer measurements in the space-like region, and examines angular distributions and asymmetries in the time-like region to assess the feasibility of individual electric and magnetic form factor extraction.

## Key numbers
- In space-like region, unpolarized cross section measurements determined $G_E$ and $G_M$ up to $Q^2 \simeq 8.8$ GeV$^2$, and $G_M$ alone up to $Q^2 \simeq 31$ GeV$^2$ under the assumption $G_E = 0$.
- The dipole form is approximated as $G_D(Q^2) = (1 + Q^2/0.71 \text{ GeV}^2)^{-2}$.
- Polarization transfer data give $\mu G_E/G_M = 1$ for $Q^2 < 0.4$ (GeV/c)$^2$ and $\mu G_E/G_M = 1.0587 - 0.14265$ for $Q^2 \le 6$ (GeV/c)$^2$, deviating from unity as $Q^2$ increases.
- For $\epsilon = 0.2$ in space-like region, the electric contribution to the reduced cross section becomes lower than 3% starting from 2 GeV$^2$.
- In time-like region, the relative contribution of the electric term to the total cross section is larger than 10% for $q^2 \le 15$ GeV$^2$.
- The PANDA experiment at FAIR will use antiproton beams up to 15 GeV kinetic energy, with each simulated data point corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 2 fb$^{-1}$ obtainable in four months of data taking.
- If two-photon exchange is the reason for the discrepancy between polarized and unpolarized measurements in space-like region, a contribution of 5% is necessary to bring data into agreement in the $|q^2|$ range between 1 and 6 (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing study of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, which began with Rosenbluth (1950) and the polarization method of Rekalo (1968). It directly addresses the discrepancy between unpolarized measurements (Andivahis et al., 1994; Arnold et al., 1986) and polarized measurements (Jones et al., 2000) in the space-like region, and the limited time-like data from PS170 at LEAR (Bardadin-Otwinowska et al., 1994) and BABAR (2006). The analysis enables future experiments at PANDA and Jefferson Laboratory to test asymptotic QCD predictions, including quark counting rules and the Phragmen-Lindelof theorem, and to search for two-photon exchange contributions at the 5% level.
