# Possible antimagnetic rotation bands in $^{100}$Pd: a particle-number conserving investigation

> The particle-number conserving method based on the cranked shell model is adopted to investigate the possible antimagnetic rotation bands in $^{100}$Pd. The experimental kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia, together with the $B(E2)$ values are reproduced quite well. The occupation probability of each neutron and proton orbital in the observed antimagnetic rotation band is analyzed and its configuration is confirmed. The contribution of each major shell to the total angular momentum alignment with rotational frequency in the lowest-lying positive and negative parity bands is analyzed. The level crossing mechanism of these bands is understood clearly. The possible antimagnetic rotation in the negative parity $α=0$ branch is predicted, which sensitively depends on the alignment of the neutron ($1g_{7/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$) pseudo-spin partners. The two-shears-like mechanism for this antimagnetic rotation is investigated by examining the closing of the proton hole angular momentum vector towards the neutron angular momentum vector.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed antimagnetic rotation (AMR) band in \(^{100}\)Pd is not a pure AMR band but is highly mixed with common collective rotation, and that a possible AMR band exists in the negative parity \(\alpha=0\) branch, which depends sensitively on the alignment of the neutron (\(1g_{7/2}\), \(2d_{5/2}\)) pseudo-spin partners.

## Method

The particle-number conserving (PNC) method based on the cranked shell model (CSM) was used, with monopole pairing and a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) truncation of 1000 configurations for both neutrons and protons. The Nilsson Hamiltonian was cranked about the \(x\)-axis, and the deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.12\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.02\) from FRDM(2012) were adopted. The occupation probabilities of single-particle orbitals and the contributions of each major shell to the total angular momentum alignment were analyzed to determine configurations and level crossing mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters used: \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.12\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.02\) (from FRDM 2012); a smaller deformation \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.083\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.013\) (from FRDM 1995) was also tested.
- Pairing strengths: \(G_{\rm n} = G_{\rm p} = 0.5\) MeV.
- An upbending in the ground state band is predicted at \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.65\) MeV, caused by neutron \(h_{11/2}\) orbitals.
- The first level crossing in the AMR band occurs around \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.4\) MeV, caused by \(g_{9/2}\) protons.
- The \(B(E2)\) values decrease with increasing rotational frequency, a key feature of AMR; the \(J^{(2)}/B(E2)\) ratios increase at \(\hbar\omega > 0.55\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach previously applied to AMR bands in \(^{105,106}\)Cd (Zhang 2013) and \(^{101,104}\)Pd (Zhang 2016, Zhang 2019) to the even-even nucleus \(^{100}\)Pd, where an AMR band was recently confirmed by lifetime measurement (Sihotra 2020). The study confirms the two-quasineutron configuration \(\nu^2 1/2^-[550]1/2^+[420]\) suggested by Perez (2001) and predicts a new AMR band in the signature \(\alpha=0\) branch, which could be tested by future experiments. The results also contrast with neighboring \(^{101}\)Pd, where rearrangement of proton \(g_{9/2}\) hole occupations plays a larger role in the two-shears-like mechanism.
