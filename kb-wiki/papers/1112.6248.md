# Experimental constraint on the $ρ-$ meson form factors in the time--like region

> The annihilation reaction $e^++e^-\rightarrow \bar ρ+ρ$ is considered. The constraint on time-like $ρ$-meson form factors from the measurement done by the BaBar collaboration at $\sqrt{s}=10.5$ GeV is analyzed.

# Experimental constraint on the ρ-meson form factors in the time-like region

## Key claim

The BaBar measurement of the $e^+e^- \to \rho^+\rho^-$ reaction at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV provides a constraint on the three time-like electromagnetic form factors of the $\rho$-meson, but the data allow two distinct parameter sets (I and II) that cannot be disentangled, and the extracted form factor ratios deviate from helicity-conserving QCD predictions.

## Method

The authors used a model-independent formalism for spin-one particles to relate the three $\rho$-meson electromagnetic form factors ($G_C$, $G_M$, $G_Q$) to the helicity amplitudes $F_{00}$, $F_{10}$, $F_{11}$ measured by BaBar. They employed a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) parametrization with three mass parameters ($m_C$, $m_M$, $m_Q$) and widths, originally fitted to space-like predictions from covariant and light-front constituent quark models, then analytically continued to the time-like region. The mass parameters were re-adjusted to reproduce the BaBar helicity amplitude ratios and total cross section at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section from BaBar at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV: $\sigma = (19.5 \pm 1.6\ (\text{stat}) \pm 3.21\ (\text{syst}))$ fb
- Ratio of helicity amplitude moduli squared at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV: $|F_{00}^B|^2 : |F_{10}^B|^2 : |F_{11}^B|^2 = 0.51 \pm 0.14\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.07\ (\text{syst}) : 0.10 \pm 0.04\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.01\ (\text{syst}) : 0.04 \pm 0.03\ (\text{stat}) \pm 0.01\ (\text{syst})$
- Overall rescaling factor of $0.011 \pm 0.002$ GeV$^2$ applied to amplitudes from the earlier parametrization of Adamuscin (2007), which gave $\sigma = 201$ fb
- Normalization condition: $|F_{00}^B|^2 + 4|F_{10}^B|^2 + 2|F_{11}^B|^2 = 1$
- Form factor normalizations at $q^2=0$: $G_C(0)=1$, $G_M(0)=\mu_\rho=2.14$, $G_Q(0)=-M^2 Q_\rho = -0.79$
- Extracted form factor moduli at $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV from the earlier parametrization: $|G_C|^2 = 1.017 \times 10^{-4}$, $|G_Q|^2 = 1.167 \times 10^{-7}$, $|G_M|^2 = 5.186 \times 10^{-7}$
- Parameter set (I): $m_C = 1.05$ GeV, $m_M = 1.28$ GeV, $m_Q = 0.97$ GeV
- Parameter set (II): $m_C = 0.77$ GeV, $m_M = 1.28$ GeV, $m_Q = 1.12$ GeV
- Form factor ratios at $q^2$ corresponding to $\sqrt{s}=10.58$ GeV: $G_C:G_M:G_Q = -63:8:-1$ for set (I) and $-10:5:-1$ for set (II)
- QCD helicity-conserving prediction for $\tau=46.53$: $G_C:G_M:G_Q = -30:2:-1$

## Context

This work extends the model-independent formalism for spin-one electromagnetic form factors developed by Gakh (2006) and the VMD parametrization of Adamuscin (2007) by confronting it with the first experimental data on $e^+e^- \to \rho^+\rho^-$ from the BaBar collaboration (Aubert, 2008). The results enable future measurements at other energies to test the $q^2$ dependence of the parametrization and to resolve the ambiguity between the two parameter sets. The deviation from helicity conservation predicted by Brodsky (1992) suggests that either helicity conservation does not apply or additional reaction mechanisms contribute in this kinematic range.
