# Nuclear Structure Study of Some Actinide Nuclei

> A modified version of the previously proposed exponential model with pairing attenuation for the well deformed even-even nuclei has been applied to predict the energy levels of doubly even actinide nuclei. Satisfactory results are obtained by that model as compared with the experimental results. The backbending phenomena are successfully described and discussed. A further comparison with the main previous models has been undertaken to confirm its validity in the heavy nuclei region.

## Key claim

The authors apply a modified exponential model with pairing attenuation, where the exponent's denominator is treated as a free parameter (nu), to predict yrast-band energies of doubly even actinide nuclei; they claim "satisfactory" agreement with experiment for both low-lying states and the backbending region, but the model's validity is demonstrated only for the specific set of Th, U, and Pu isotopes considered.

## Method

The authors fit a three-parameter exponential formula E(I) = (hbar^2 / 2phi_0) I(I+1) exp[Delta_0 (1 - I/I_c)^(1/nu)] to experimental ground-state-band energies of even-even Th, U, and Pu nuclei, using a least-squares procedure to determine phi_0, Delta_0, and nu for each nucleus. They compare the resulting level energies and backbending plots (moment of inertia versus rotational frequency squared) with predictions from the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model and the three-parameter Nuclear Softness (NS3) model, as well as with the original exponential model (nu fixed to 2). Experimental data are taken from published compilations and recent Nuclear Data Sheets.

## Key numbers

*   For 232Th, the modified exponential model (Expo2) predicts the 2+ state at 0.051554 MeV (experiment: 0.049369 MeV) and the 30+ state at 5.163 MeV (experiment: 5.162 MeV).
*   For 238U, Expo2 gives the 2+ state at 0.0463545 MeV (experiment: 0.044916 MeV) and the 28+ state at 4.51387 MeV (experiment: 4.517 MeV).
*   For 240Pu, Expo2 yields the 2+ state at 0.044081 MeV (experiment: 0.042824 MeV) and the 26+ state at 4.087 MeV (experiment: 4.088 MeV).
*   The fitted parameter nu for the modified exponential model ranges from 0.51292 (224Th) to 0.85624 (228Th).
*   The ratio R4 = E(4+)/E(2+) for the nuclei studied ranges from 2.896 (224Th) to 3.136 (226Th).

## Context

This work extends the exponential model with pairing attenuation originally developed by Sood and Jain (Sood, year not given in text) and later modified by Hamad (Hamad, year not given in text) to the actinide region, specifically for Th, U, and Pu isotopes. It compares its results against the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) model of Mariscotti (Mariscotti, year not given in text) and the Nuclear Softness (NS3) model of Gareer (Gareer, year not given in text), finding that the modified exponential model reproduces backbending behavior more successfully than the VMI model for several isotopes. The study enables further phenomenological testing of pairing-attenuation models in heavy deformed nuclei and suggests that the three-parameter exponential formula can serve as a simple alternative to more complex approaches like the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) for describing yrast bands up to high spin.
