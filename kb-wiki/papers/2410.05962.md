# 16O electroweak response functions from first principles

> We present calculations of various electroweak response functions for the 16O nucleus obtained using coupled-cluster theory in conjunction with the Lorentz integral transform method. We employ nuclear forces derived at next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory and perform a Bayesian analysis to assess uncertainties. Our results are in good agreement with available electron-scattering data at q~ 326 MeV/c. Additionally, we provide several predictions for the weak response functions in the quasi-elastic peak region at q= 300 and 400 MeV/c, which are critical for long-baseline neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The authors present first-principles calculations of electroweak response functions for 16O using coupled-cluster theory with the Lorentz integral transform method, finding good agreement with electron-scattering data at q ~ 326 MeV/c, but the agreement is conditional on the specific kinematic regime and the calculations omit two-body currents and triples excitations, which are expected to affect the transverse response at higher energy transfers.

## Method

The authors solve the 16O ground state using coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) starting from Hartree-Fock with chiral effective field theory forces at next-to-leading order (DeltaNLO_GO(450)) and next-to-next-to-leading order (DeltaNNLO_GO(450)). They compute response functions via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, which avoids explicit calculation of continuum excited states by transforming the response into a bound-state-like problem, then invert the transform to recover the physical response functions including full final state interactions. Uncertainty quantification combines a Gaussian Process model for chiral truncation errors with LIT inversion uncertainties from varying the Lorentzian width (Gamma = 5, 10 MeV) and basis parameters.

## Key numbers

- Electron-scattering cross section calculated at E = 537 MeV and theta = 37.1 degrees (q ~ 330 MeV/c), compared to data from O'Connell (1987) and Anghinolfi (1996)
- Proton separation energy computed as 10.6 MeV for NNLO_sat and 11.7 MeV for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), compared to experimental value of 12.1 MeV
- Weak response functions predicted at q = 300 MeV/c and q = 400 MeV/c using 8 and 11 multipoles respectively for convergence
- Threshold energy for 16O(nu_e,e)p15O channel computed as 12.5 MeV for NNLO_sat and 13.6 MeV for DeltaNNLO_GO(450), compared to experimental value of 14.4 MeV
- Model space of 15 major oscillator shells (N_max = 2n + l = 14) with harmonic oscillator frequency hbar-Omega = 14 MeV
- Chiral expansion parameters p ~ 250 MeV and Lambda_chi ~ 450 MeV used for uncertainty quantification

## Context

This work extends the LIT-CC method previously applied to photo-absorption and electron-nucleus reactions on lighter nuclei (bacca2013, bacca2014, miorelli2016, miorelli2018, simonis2019, Sobczyk 2021, Sobczyk 2023) to the A=16 system for the first time. It provides ab initio calculations of weak response functions critical for long-baseline neutrino experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, where oxygen is a key target material. The results enable future work on 40Ar and can serve as direct input to Monte Carlo event generators for neutrino oscillation analyses, while also providing benchmarks for the spectral function formalism (Sobczyk 2023) and Bayesian neural network predictions (Sobczyk ANN).
