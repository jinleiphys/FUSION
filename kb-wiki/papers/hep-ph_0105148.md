# Excited Heavy Baryons and Their Symmetries III: Phenomenology

> Phenomenological applications of an effective theory of low-lying excited states of charm and bottom isoscalar baryons are discussed at leading and next-to-leading order in the combined heavy quark and large $N_c$ expansion. The combined expansion is formulated in terms of the counting parameter $λ\sim 1/m_Q, 1/N_c$; the combined expansion is in powers of $λ^{1/2}$. We work up to next-to-leading order. We obtain model-independent predictions for the excitation energies, the semileptonic form factors and electromagnetic decay rates. The spin-averaged mass of the doublet of the first orbitally excited sate of $Λ_b$ is predicted to be approximately $5920 MeV$. It is shown that in the combined limit at leading and next-to-leading order there is only one independent form factor describing $Λ_b \to Λ_c \ell \barν$; similarly, $Λ_b \to Λ_{c}^{*} \ell \barν$ and $Λ_b \to Λ_{c1} \ell \barν$ decays are described by a single independent form factor. These form factors are calculated at leading and next-to-leading order in the combined expansion. The electromagnetic decay rates of the first excited states of $Λ_c$ and $Λ_b$ are determined at leading and next-to leading order. The ratio of radiative decay rates $Γ(Λ_{c}^{*} \to Λ_c γ) / Γ(Λ_{b1} \to Λ_b γ)$ is predicted to be approximately 0.2, greatly different from the heavy quark effective theory value of unity.

## Key claim

The paper presents model-independent predictions for excited heavy baryon phenomenology using a combined heavy quark and large \(N_c\) expansion, but explicitly notes that because \(N_c=3\) in the real world the expansion parameter \(\lambda^{1/2} \sim 1/\sqrt{3}\) is "not particularly small," so predictions even at next-to-leading order "may be relatively crude."

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian for low-lying excited states of \(\Lambda_c\) and \(\Lambda_b\) baryons using a combined expansion in powers of \(\lambda^{1/2}\), where \(\lambda \sim 1/m_Q, 1/N_c\). They work up to next-to-leading order (NLO), including terms of order \(\lambda\) in the Hamiltonian, and compute excitation energies, semileptonic form factors, and electromagnetic decay rates using harmonic oscillator wave functions derived from the effective theory. The two phenomenological constants \(\kappa\) and \(\alpha\) are determined from the excitation energies of \(\Lambda_c\) baryons and then used to predict observables in the bottom sector.

## Key numbers

- Spin-averaged mass of the first orbitally excited state doublet of \(\Lambda_b\) predicted to be approximately \(5920 \, \text{MeV}\) at leading order.
- Ratio of radiative decay rates \(\Gamma(\Lambda_{c}^{*} \to \Lambda_c \gamma) / \Gamma(\Lambda_{b1} \to \Lambda_b \gamma)\) predicted to be approximately \(0.2\), compared to the heavy quark effective theory value of unity.
- Value of the \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c \ell \bar{\nu}\) form factor at zero recoil predicted to be \(0.998\) at leading order.
- Spin-averaged mass of the \(\Lambda_{c1}\) and \(\Lambda_{c1}^{*}\) doublet given as approximately \(2610 \, \text{MeV}\).
- Spin-averaged meson masses: \(m_{\bar{D}} \approx 1980 \, \text{MeV}\), \(m_{\bar{B}} \approx 5310 \, \text{MeV}\).
- The relation \((m_{\Lambda_b} - m_{\Lambda_c}) - (m_{\bar{B}} - m_{\bar{D}}) = 10 \, \text{MeV}\) is satisfied in nature.
- The NLO correction to this relation yields \(60 \, \text{MeV}\), consistent with zero within theoretical uncertainty of up to \(140 \, \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This paper is the third in a series developing an effective theory of heavy baryons based on a contracted \(O(8)\) symmetry that emerges in the combined heavy quark and large \(N_c\) limit, extending earlier work by the same authors (Baccouche et al., previous papers in the series). The approach reproduces leading-order results of bound state models (such as those by the "bst" authors) but provides a model-independent framework that can be systematically extended to higher orders. The predictions differ from standard heavy quark effective theory (HQET) results: for example, the ratio of radiative decay rates is \(0.2\) rather than unity, and the flavor symmetry is broken already at order \(\lambda^{1/2}\) rather than at \(1/m_Q\). The work enables future experimental tests once excited \(\Lambda_b\) states are observed, and it provides a framework for combining heavy quark and large \(N_c\) expansions in baryon phenomenology.
