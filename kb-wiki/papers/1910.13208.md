# A New Theory Framework for the Electroweak Radiative Corrections in $K_{l3}$ Decays

> We propose a new theory framework to study the electroweak radiative corrections in $K_{l3}$ decays by combining the classic current algebra approach with the modern effective field theory. Under this framework, the most important $\mathcal{O}(G_Fα)$ radiative corrections are described by a single tensor $T^{μν}$ involving the time-ordered product between the charged weak current and the electromagnetic current, and all remaining pieces are calculable order-by-order in Chiral Perturbation Theory. We further point out a special advantage in the $K_{l3}^{0}$ channel that it suffers the least impact from the poorly-constrained low-energy constants. This finding may serve as a basis for a more precise extraction of the matrix element $V_{us}$ in the future.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new framework for electroweak radiative corrections in \(K_{l3}\) decays that combines current algebra with effective field theory, arguing that the existing Chiral Perturbation Theory treatment has conceptual issues including an incomplete resummation of higher-order terms leading to a relative systematic error of order \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-3})\) and uncontrolled uncertainties from low-energy constants; the authors claim the \(K_{l3}^{0}\) channel has a special advantage of minimal dependence on poorly-constrained low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors combine a classic current algebra approach from the late 1970s (Sirlin 1977) with modern effective field theory, expressing the dominant \(\mathcal{O}(G_F\alpha)\) radiative corrections through a single hadronic tensor \(T^{\mu\nu}\) involving the time-ordered product of the charged weak current and electromagnetic current. They develop a diagrammatic approach to calculate non-enhanced contributions order-by-order in Chiral Perturbation Theory, and split the \(\mathcal{O}(e^{2}p^{2})\) corrections into two-point and three-point functions to isolate the dependence on unknown low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The current PDG (2018) values are \(|V_{ud}| = 0.97420(21)\) and \(|V_{us}| = 0.2243(5)\), giving \(\Delta_{\mathrm{CKM}} = -0.0006(5)\)
- Updated \(V_{ud}\) from dispersion relation analysis (Seng 2018) gives \(|V_{ud}| = 0.97366(15)\), leading to \(\Delta_{\mathrm{CKM}} = -0.0017(4)\), a 4\(\sigma\) violation of unitarity
- \(K_{l2}\) extraction gives \(|V_{us}| = 0.2253(7)\) while \(K_{l3}\) gives \(|V_{us}| = 0.2231(8)\), disagreeing at \(\sim\)2\(\sigma\) level
- A recent lattice study (Bazavov 2018) gives \(|V_{us}| = 0.22333(61)\) from \(K_{l3}\), leading to \(\Delta_{\mathrm{CKM}} = -0.0021(4)\), a 5\(\sigma\) violation of unitarity
- The Fermi constant is \(G_F = 1.1663787(6) \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\)
- The parameter \(Z = (M_{\pi^{\pm}}^2 - M_{\pi^0}^2)/(8\pi\alpha F_0^2) \approx 0.8\)

## Context

This work extends the current algebra approach of Sirlin (1977) for treating electroweak radiative corrections in semileptonic decays, and addresses limitations in the existing Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations by Cirigliano (2001, 2008). The framework enables a more precise extraction of \(|V_{us}|\) from \(K_{l3}\) decays, which is critical for testing first-row CKM unitarity given the recent tension from updated \(V_{ud}\) determinations (Seng 2018, Gorchtein 2018). The method also provides a basis for future lattice QCD calculations of the \(\gamma W\)-box diagram (Seng 2019) and for incorporating improved neutrino scattering data from the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility.
