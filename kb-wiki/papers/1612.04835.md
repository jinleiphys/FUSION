# Process-independent strong running coupling

> We unify two widely different approaches to understanding the infrared behaviour of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), one essentially phenomenological, based on data, and the other computational, realised via quantum field equations in the continuum theory. Using the latter, we explain and calculate a process-independent running-coupling for QCD, a new type of effective charge that is an analogue of the Gell-Mann--Low effective coupling in quantum electrodynamics. The result is almost identical to the process-dependent effective charge defined via the Bjorken sum rule, which provides one of the most basic constraints on our knowledge of nucleon spin structure. This reveals the Bjorken sum to be a near direct means by which to gain empirical insight into QCD's Gell-Mann--Low effective charge.

# Process-independent strong running coupling

## Key claim

The authors claim to have defined and calculated a process-independent running coupling for QCD that is almost pointwise identical to the process-dependent effective charge defined via the Bjorken sum rule, but this agreement relies on the careful treatment of gluon-ghost scattering effects that contribute roughly 60% of the coupling's strength at infrared momenta near the gluon mass scale.

## Method

The authors combined the pinch technique with the background field method in Landau gauge to construct a modified gluon dressing function from which a renormalization-group-invariant running coupling can be computed, using the gluon vacuum polarisation to capture all renormalisation group features. They then combined self-consistent solutions of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the gluon and ghost two-point functions with lattice-QCD results, using a [1,2] Pade approximant to interpolate between infrared lattice data and ultraviolet perturbative constraints. The resulting process-independent coupling was compared with the process-dependent effective charge extracted from world data on the Bjorken sum rule.

## Key numbers

- The infrared fixed point of the process-independent coupling: $\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(k^2=0) = \alpha_0 \approx 0.9\pi$
- The gluon mass scale at infrared momenta: $m_0 := m_g(0) \approx m_p/2$ (approximately half the proton mass)
- Ghost-gluon interactions produce roughly 60% enhancement of $\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(k^2)$ for $k \simeq m_0$
- The subleading term in the perturbative expansion differs by only 4% between the two couplings: $\widehat{\alpha}_{\rm PI}(k^2) = \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) (1 + 1.09 \, \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) + \ldots)$ versus $\alpha_{g_1}(k^2) = \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) (1 + 1.14 \, \alpha_{\overline{\rm MS}}(k^2) + \ldots)$
- An inflection point in the coupling occurs at $\sqrt{k^2} = 0.7$ GeV

## Context

This work unifies two previously distinct approaches to understanding QCD's infrared behaviour: the phenomenological approach based on data from the Bjorken sum rule (pioneered by Grunberg (1982) for process-dependent effective charges) and the computational approach using continuum quantum field equations. The result extends earlier work by Binosi et al. (2014, 2016) on the renormalization-group-invariant interaction and by Aguilar et al. (2009) on the Taylor coupling, and it enables the Bjorken sum rule to be interpreted as a near-direct empirical window into QCD's analogue of the Gell-Mann-Low effective charge. The process-independent coupling is already known to unify a vast array of hadron observables including the pion mass and decay constant, the light meson spectrum, and meson parton distribution amplitudes and form factors.
