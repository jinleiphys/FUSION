# Analysis of flux-integrated semi-exclusive cross sections for charged current quasi-elastic neutrino scattering off 40Ar at energies available at the MicroBooNE experiment

> Flux-integrated semi-exclusive differential and integral cross sections for quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering on argon are analyzed. We calculate these cross sections using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation and compare with recent MicroBooNE data. We found that the measured cross sections can be described well within the experimental uncertainties with value of the nucleon axial mass $1 < M_A < 1.2$ GeV. The contribution of the exclusive channel $\boldmath{(ν_μ, μp)}$ to the flux-integrated inclusive cross sections is about 50\%.

## Key claim

The measured flux-integrated semi-exclusive cross sections for charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino scattering on argon can be described well within experimental uncertainties using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation, but only when the nucleon axial mass is constrained to the range 1 < M_A < 1.2 GeV; the agreement is notably worse at the highest muon scattering angle bin (low Q^2) and for the full phase space with M_A = 1.2 GeV.

## Method

The author calculated flux-integrated differential and integral cross sections for the CC1p0π (charged-current one-muon one-proton zero-pion) channel of νμ-40Ar scattering using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with a relativistic shell model for bound nucleon wave functions and an optical potential for final-state interactions. The calculations used the Booster Neutrino Beam energy spectrum at Fermilab and applied the MicroBooNE momentum thresholds (muon momentum > 0.1 GeV/c, proton momentum > 0.3 GeV/c). The results were compared to MicroBooNE data across multiple kinematic distributions, with χ^2/d.o.f. values computed for M_A = 1 GeV and 1.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon axial mass is constrained to the range 1 < M_A < 1.2 GeV from the comparison with MicroBooNE data.
- For the restricted phase space (cosθ < 0.8), χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.12 for M_A = 1 GeV and χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.36 for M_A = 1.2 GeV.
- For the full phase space (cosθ < 0.95), χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.26 for M_A = 1 GeV and χ^2/d.o.f. = 2.22 for M_A = 1.2 GeV.
- The measured integrated cross section for cosθ < 0.8 is (4.05 ± 1.4) × 10^{-38} cm^2; the RDWIA calculation gives 3.65 × 10^{-38} cm^2 (M_A = 1 GeV) and 4.48 × 10^{-38} cm^2 (M_A = 1.2 GeV).
- The measured integrated cross section for the full phase space is (4.93 ± 1.55) × 10^{-38} cm^2; the RDWIA calculation gives 5.24 × 10^{-38} cm^2 (M_A = 1 GeV) and 6.30 × 10^{-38} cm^2 (M_A = 1.2 GeV).
- The contribution of the exclusive (νμ, μp) channel with proton momentum > 300 MeV/c to the inclusive cross section is about 50% at muon momentum ≈ 1 GeV/c.
- The maximum of the double differential cross section lies in the range 0.9 < pμ < 1.1 GeV/c and 0.8 < cosθ < 0.96.
- The MicroBooNE data had 410 CC1p0π candidate events, with statistical uncertainty 15.9% and systematic uncertainty 26.2%.

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied by Butkevich (2007-2020) to electron and neutrino scattering on 12C, 16O, 40Ca, and 40Ar, and tests it against the first exclusive CCQE-like neutrino-argon cross section measurements from the MicroBooNE experiment (Abratenko 2020). The extracted axial mass range 1 < M_A < 1.2 GeV is consistent with best-fit values from CCQE-like analyses of MiniBooNE and MINERvA data (Wilkinson 2016, Butkevich 2018). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-argon interactions for the DUNE and SBN experiments, though the author notes that MicroBooNE's statistical and systematic precision is insufficient for current needs and that measurements with better than 20% uncertainty are required to constrain models and M_A values for precision oscillation analyses.
