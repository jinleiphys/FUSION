# A Chiral Effective Lagrangian for Nuclei

> An effective hadronic lagrangian consistent with the symmetries of quantum chromodynamics and intended for applications to finite-density systems is constructed. The degrees of freedom are (valence) nucleons, pions, and the low-lying non-Goldstone bosons, which account for the intermediate-range nucleon-nucleon interactions and conveniently describe the nonvanishing expectation values of nucleon bilinears. Chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly, with a light scalar meson included as a chiral singlet to describe the mid-range nucleon-nucleon attraction. The low-energy electromagnetic structure of the nucleon is described within the theory using vector-meson dominance, so that external form factors are not needed. The effective lagrangian is expanded in powers of the fields and their derivatives, with the terms organized using Georgi's ``naive dimensional analysis''. Results are presented for finite nuclei and nuclear matter at one-baryon-loop order, using the single-nucleon structure determined within the model. Parameters obtained from fits to nuclear properties show that naive dimensional analysis is a useful principle and that a truncation of the effective lagrangian at the first few powers of the fields and their derivatives is justified.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective hadronic Lagrangian for finite-density nuclear systems that incorporates chiral symmetry nonlinearly and includes a light scalar meson as a chiral singlet to describe intermediate-range nucleon-nucleon attraction, with the claim that naive dimensional analysis (NDA) provides a useful truncation scheme and that coefficients fitted to nuclear properties are "natural" (of order unity), though the authors acknowledge that at finite density the Fermi momentum introduces a new scale that complicates whether NDA and naturalness remain valid when higher-order many-body effects are absorbed into one-loop coefficients.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian with nucleons, pions, and low-lying non-Goldstone bosons (scalar, vector, and isovector mesons) using the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry following Callan, Coleman, Wess, and Zumino (CCWZ). They organized terms by powers of fields and derivatives using Georgi's naive dimensional analysis, included electromagnetic structure via vector-meson dominance without external form factors, and applied the Lagrangian to finite nuclei and nuclear matter at one-baryon-loop (Dirac-Hartree) order, fitting parameters to nuclear properties.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \( f_\pi \approx 93 \) MeV
- Axial coupling constant: \( g_{\rm A} \approx 1.26 \)
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: \( \lambda_{\rm p} = 1.793 \)
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: \( \lambda_{\rm n} = -1.913 \)
- Scalar meson mass range: 500 to 600 MeV (from correlated two-pion exchange strength distribution)
- Rho meson mass: \( m_\rho \) (value not specified in text, but used in KSRF relation \( g_{\rho\pi\pi} = m_\rho^2 / (2 g_\rho f_\pi^2) \))
- Typical resonance mass in isovector-vector channel: \( m_1 = 1450 \) MeV, with \( m_\rho^2 (m_1^2 - m_\rho^2) / m_1^4 \approx 1/5 \)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1995-1996) that explored incorporating QCD symmetries into effective hadronic models, and builds upon the successful phenomenology of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) as reviewed by Serot (1986, 1992). It contrasts with renormalizable QHD models that encountered difficulties from large vacuum loop effects (Perry 1987, Furnstahl 1988, 1989), and instead adopts the modern effective field theory approach of Lepage (1989), Polchinski (1992), Georgi (1994), and Weinberg (1995). The work enables a unified framework for nuclear structure calculations that incorporates single-nucleon electromagnetic structure without external form factors, and provides a test of whether naive dimensional analysis and naturalness are useful concepts for effective field theories of nuclei at finite density.
