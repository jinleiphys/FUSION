# Three-body antikaon-nucleon systems

> The paper contains a review of the exact or accurate results achieved in the field of the three-body antikaon-nucleon physics. Different states and processes in $\bar{K}NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ systems are considered. In particular, quasi-bound states in $K^- pp$ and $K^- K^- p$ systems were investigated together with antikaonic deuterium atom. Near-threshold scattering of antikaons on deuteron, including the $K^- d$ scattering length, and applications of the scattering amplitudes are also discussed. All exact three-body results were calculated using some form of Faddeev equations. Different versions of $\bar{K}N$, $ΣN$, $\bar{K}\bar{K}$, and $NN$ potentials, specially constructed for the calculations, allowed investigation of the dependence of the three-body results on the two-body input. Special attention is paid to the antikaon-nucleon interaction, being the most important for the three-body systems. Approximate calculations, performed additionally to the exact ones, demonstrate accuracy of the commonly used approaches.

## Key claim

This paper reviews exact or accurate three-body calculations for antikaon-nucleon systems (KNN and KKN), showing that despite using three different antikaon-nucleon potentials (one-pole, two-pole, and chirally motivated) that all reproduce two-body experimental data with equal accuracy, the three-body results cannot distinguish between the one-pole and two-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Method

Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations with coupled channels were solved for the KNN-pi Sigma N and KKN-K pi Sigma systems using separable potentials for the KN, Sigma N, KK, and NN interactions. Three different KN potentials (two phenomenological with one- or two-pole Lambda(1405) structure, and one chirally motivated) were fitted to low-energy K-p scattering data, threshold branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift and width. For kaonic deuterium, Faddeev-type equations with strong plus Coulomb interactions were solved for the first time for a hadronic atom where Coulomb plays the main role.

## Context

This review extends the author's series of calculations (Shevchenko 2012, 2013, 2015) on K-pp and K-K-p quasi-bound states, K-d scattering, and kaonic deuterium, following the initial claim by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) that deep narrow quasi-bound states exist in KNN systems. The work contradicts earlier approximate Deser-type formulas for kaonic hydrogen by showing they have about 10% error for the antikaon-nucleon system, and demonstrates that three-body calculations cannot resolve the two-pole versus one-pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The results enable future experimental comparisons with HADES, LEPS, J-PARC E15 and E27 experiments, and provide exact calculations of kaonic deuterium characteristics for the first time using Faddeev-type equations with Coulomb interactions.
