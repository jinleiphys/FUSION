# Exploring Lifetime Effects in Femtoscopy

> We investigate the role of lifetime effects from resonances and emission duration tails in femtoscopy at RHIC in two Blast-Wave models. We find the non-Gaussian components compare well with published source imaged data, but the value of R_out obtained from Gaussian fits is not insensitive to the non-Gaussian contributions when realistic acceptance cuts are applied to models.

## Key claim

The non-Gaussian tails observed in femtoscopic source images at RHIC are dominated by omega resonance decays, but there is sufficient room for contributions from extended emission duration (lifetime effects); however, the value of R_out obtained from Gaussian fits is not insensitive to these non-Gaussian contributions when realistic acceptance cuts are applied, contrary to expectations from models without such cuts.

## Method

The authors constructed two Blast-Wave models of the pion-emitting source: a baseline model with exponential time emission and Gaussian spatial profiles including omega resonance feeddown, and the Therminator program which includes a full resonance decay table. They generated two-particle correlation functions via Monte Carlo integration, applied PHENIX acceptance cuts (pseudorapidity |eta|<0.35 and transverse momentum 0.2<k_T<0.36 GeV), and compared the resulting source functions to published PHENIX source-imaged data and to Gaussian fits in Bertsch-Pratt coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Baseline model parameters: T=165 MeV, f=0.5 (core fraction), R_T=R_z=4 fm, tau_fo=10 fm/c, rho_0=0.6
- Omega resonance lifetime: tau_omega=23 fm/c, with 88.8% branching fraction to pi+ pi- pi0
- Gaussian fit results for baseline model (with cuts): lambda=0.676±0.010, R_side=4.36±0.05 fm, R_out=12.16±0.16 fm, R_long=4.45±0.05 fm
- Gaussian fit results for model without omega and without emission tail: lambda=0.998±0.006, R_side=3.99±0.02 fm, R_out=5.39±0.03 fm, R_long=4.06±0.02 fm
- R_out increases by ~60% when either omega or emission tail is added alone, and another ~40% when both are included
- Therminator model parameters: T=165.6 MeV, mu_b=28.5 MeV, tau=8.55 fm/c, rho_max=8.92 fm, v_T=0.311

## Context

This work extends the source imaging technique developed by the PHENIX collaboration (2005) which first observed non-Gaussian tails in 200 GeV central Au+Au collisions, and addresses the "HBT Puzzle" where the R_out/R_side ratio from hydrodynamic models (Huovinen 2001, Kolb 2003, Hirano 2002) disagrees with measurements. The analysis shows that omega resonance decays dominate the measured non-Gaussian tail, but lifetime effects contribute primarily in the outwards direction, and that three-dimensional source imaging is needed to disentangle these contributions. The results enable future studies using kaon femtoscopy as a more direct probe of primordial source lifetime.
