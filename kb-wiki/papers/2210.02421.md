# Densities and momentum distributions in A <=12 nuclei from chiral effective field theory interactions

> Current and future electron and neutrino scattering experiments will be greatly aided by a better understanding of the role played by short-range correlations in nuclei. Two-body physics, including nucleon-nucleon correlations and two-body electroweak currents, is required to explain the body of experimental data for both static and dynamical nuclear properties. In this work, we focus on examining nucleon-nucleon correlations from a chiral effective field theory perspective and provide a comprehensive set of new variational Monte Carlo calculations of one- and two-body densities and momentum distributions based on the Norfolk many-body nuclear Hamiltonians for A<=12 systems. Online access to detailed tables and figures is available.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive set of variational Monte Carlo calculations of one- and two-body densities and momentum distributions for A ≤ 12 nuclei using chiral effective field theory interactions, but the results depend on which specific Norfolk interaction model is used, with "soft" chiral models (NV2+3-Ia and NV2+3-Ia*) showing finite probability of finding two nucleons at short distances while "hard" models (NV2+3-IIb* and phenomenological AV18+UX) show this probability approaching zero.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo wave functions were constructed for nuclei from ²H to ¹²C using the Norfolk chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (NV2+3-Ia, NV2+3-Ia*, NV2+3-Ib*, NV2+3-IIa*, NV2+3-IIb*) and the phenomenological AV18+UX interaction. One- and two-body densities were evaluated as δ-function expectation values, while momentum distributions were obtained via Fourier transforms of nondiagonal density matrices using Monte Carlo integration with Gauss-Legendre points and random directional sampling. For two-body momentum distributions, short-range and long-range pair contributions were separated by a pair separation boundary.

## Key numbers

- The NV2+3-Ia and NV2+3-IIa class I (II) potentials fit approximately 2700 (3700) NN scattering data points with χ²/datum ≤ 1.1 (≤ 1.4)
- For ⁴He with NV2+3-Ia*, the number of np short-range pairs (r ≤ 2.0 fm) is 2.04 and long-range pairs is 1.96, while pp short-range pairs are 0.53 and long-range pairs are 0.47
- For ⁴He with AV18+UX, the number of np short-range pairs (r ≤ 2.0 fm) is 2.06 and long-range pairs is 1.94, while pp short-range pairs are 0.48 and long-range pairs are 0.52
- For ¹²C with AV18+UX and separation boundary a = 2.5 fm, the number of np short-range pairs is 12.9 and long-range pairs is 23.1, while pp short-range pairs are 4.3 and long-range pairs are 10.7
- In ⁴He, reaching momenta k ∼ 10 fm⁻¹ with good statistics required integrating to x_max = 20 fm using 200 Gauss-Legendre points
- All nuclei show a dramatic shift in slope of one-body momentum distributions at k = 2 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the previous library of variational Monte Carlo calculations using phenomenological Argonne v₁₈ (AV18) and Urbana X (UX) interactions from Wiringa (2013) to include results from the Norfolk chiral effective field theory interactions (NV2+3). The new calculations provide pair densities as functions of both pair separation and pair center-of-mass, and two-body momentum distributions differentiated by a pair separation boundary, which enables studies of many-body factorization and the Generalized Contact Formalism as reported by Cruz-Torres (2019). The results are made available online for the nuclear physics community to support interpretation of current and future electron and neutrino scattering experiments.
