# Simulation of Collective Neutrino Oscillations on a Quantum Computer

> In astrophysical scenarios with large neutrino density, like supernovae and the early universe, the presence of neutrino-neutrino interactions can give rise to collective flavor oscillations in the out-of-equilibrium collective dynamics of a neutrino cloud. The role of quantum correlations in these phenomena is not yet well understood, in large part due to complications in solving for the real-time evolution of the strongly coupled many-body system. Future fault-tolerant quantum computers hold the promise to overcome much of these limitations and provide direct access to the correlated neutrino dynamic. In this work, we present the first simulation of a small system of interacting neutrinos using current generation quantum devices. We introduce a strategy to overcome limitations in the natural connectivity of the qubits and use it to track the evolution of entanglement in real-time. The results show the critical importance of error-mitigation techniques to extract meaningful results for entanglement measures using noisy, near term, quantum devices.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first simulation of collective neutrino oscillations on a current-generation quantum computer, but the results are limited to a small system of four neutrinos and require significant error-mitigation techniques to extract meaningful entanglement measures from the noisy quantum device.

## Method

The authors map a two-flavor neutrino Hamiltonian with all-to-all spin-spin interactions onto a linear array of qubits using a swap network that implements all pair interactions with only nearest-neighbor gates. They perform a single Trotter-Suzuki time step for a system of four neutrinos on the IBMQ Vigo quantum processor, using 8192 measurement shots per data point and applying zero-noise extrapolation and maximum-likelihood state tomography to mitigate readout and gate errors.

## Key numbers

- System size: N=4 neutrino amplitudes (mapped to qubits 2,1,3,4 on the IBMQ Vigo processor)
- Measurement statistics: 8192 repetitions ("shots") per data point
- Circuit depth: up to 90 single-qubit rotations plus 18 CNOT gates for time evolution, plus 2 rotations for state preparation
- Approximation error from the pair propagator method: deviations in the range 3-7% from the exact symmetry for the inversion probability
- Time range explored: up to approximately ηt ≈ 6 in units of the two-body coupling η

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino many-body dynamics that used Matrix Product State methods (Roggero 2021a, Roggero 2021b) to treat systems where entanglement remains manageable, and earlier mean-field treatments (Duan 2006) that neglect quantum correlations entirely. The results demonstrate that current noisy quantum devices can begin to access correlated neutrino dynamics, but the substantial errors from single-qubit rotations (90 operations versus 14 in prior work by Roggero) highlight the need for improved error mitigation and future fault-tolerant quantum computers to reach larger system sizes and longer evolution times.
