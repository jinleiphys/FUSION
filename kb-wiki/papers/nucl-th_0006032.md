# Mean field theory for global binding systematics

> We review some possible improvements of mean field theory for application to nuclear binding systematics. Up to now, microscopic theory has been less successful than models starting from the liquid drop in describing accurately the global binding systematics. We believe that there are good prospects to develop a better global theory, using modern forms of energy density functionals and treating correlation energies systematically by the RPA.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the RPA correlation energy formula, combined with modern energy density functionals, offers a promising and computationally tractable path to develop a microscopic theory of nuclear binding systematics that could achieve RMS deviations below 1 MeV, a level currently only reached by phenomenological liquid-drop models with many free parameters.

## Method

The paper reviews the limitations of existing mean-field approaches (Skyrme and Gogny interactions) for global binding systematics and proposes using the RPA correlation energy formula \(E_{corr}= \frac{1}{2}(\sum_i\hbar\omega_i - Tr(A))\) to systematically include correlation effects from center-of-mass motion, quadrupole deformations, and pairing. The authors test the HF+RPA approach on simple model Hamiltonians (two-particle harmonic oscillator, three-level Lipkin model, two-level pairing model) and discuss computational strategies using separable interactions with Lanczos-type methods for efficient RPA solution.

## Key numbers

- The liquid drop model fit of Moller et al. (1995) achieves an RMS deviation of 0.67 MeV using 15 free parameters and a similar number of fixed parameters.
- Patyk et al. found that Skyrme interactions from the literature give RMS errors greater than 2 MeV for global binding.
- Brown (1998) obtained an RMS deviation of 0.8 MeV for 10 closed-shell nuclei using a new Skyrme fit.
- Fayans (1998) obtained an RMS binding error of 1.2 MeV for 100 spherical nuclei using a generalized energy functional.
- In the two-particle harmonic oscillator model, the RPA correction gives the exact energy \(\hbar\omega_0/\sqrt{2}\), while the center-of-mass subtraction prescription gives \(3\hbar\omega_0/4\).
- For the pairing model of oxygen isotopes, the deviation from BCS approximation is around 2 MeV for even-A systems and around 1.2 MeV for odd-A systems, varying within about 0.5 MeV along the isotopes.
- In the test of the Lanczos method with D=20 particle-hole states, the exact correlation energy is \(E_{corr} = -0.542 e_0\); the single-state approximation is off by 20%, while the two-state approximation has an accuracy of 1%.

## Context

This paper extends the work of Vautherin and Brink (1972) on Skyrme-based mean-field theory and addresses the paradox that microscopic theory has been less successful than liquid-drop models (Moller et al., 1995) for global binding systematics. The authors propose that the RPA correlation formula, first suggested for this purpose by Friedrich and Reinhard (1986), can systematically treat the correlation energies from broken symmetries (center-of-mass, deformation, pairing) that limit the accuracy of pure mean-field approaches. This work enables the development of a global mass theory using HF+RPA with separable interactions, with work in progress to tackle nuclear masses using this approach.
