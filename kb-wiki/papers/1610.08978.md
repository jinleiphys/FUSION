# Reconciling threshold and subthreshold expansions for pion-nucleon scattering

> Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at one loop fails in relating the pion-nucleon amplitude in the physical region and for subthreshold kinematics due to loop effects enhanced by large low-energy constants. Studying the chiral convergence of threshold and subthreshold parameters up to fourth order in the small-scale expansion, we address the question to what extent this tension can be mitigated by including the $Δ(1232)$ as an explicit degree of freedom and/or using a covariant formulation of baryon ChPT. We find that the inclusion of the $Δ$ indeed reduces the low-energy constants to more natural values and thereby improves consistency between threshold and subthreshold kinematics. In addition, even in the $Δ$-less theory the resummation of $1/m_N$ corrections in the covariant scheme improves the results markedly over the heavy-baryon formulation, in line with previous observations in the single-baryon sector of ChPT that so far have evaded a profound theoretical explanation.

## Key claim

Including the Δ(1232) as an explicit degree of freedom in chiral perturbation theory reduces the low-energy constants to more natural values and improves consistency between threshold and subthreshold kinematics for pion-nucleon scattering, but even without the Δ, resummation of 1/m_N corrections in a covariant formulation markedly improves results over the heavy-baryon formulation, although a rigorous power-counting argument for this improvement remains lacking.

## Method

The authors performed matching of πN subthreshold parameters determined by Roy-Steiner equations to chiral perturbation theory up to full one-loop order (ε^4) in the small-scale expansion, both in heavy-baryon and covariant formulations with and without explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom. Low-energy constants were extracted order-by-order from subthreshold parameters, and the resulting predictions for eight leading threshold parameters were compared to the Roy-Steiner benchmark values. The Δ-sector couplings h_A, g_1, and b_4,5 were estimated using large-N_c constraints and phenomenological input.

## Key numbers

- h_A = 1.40 ± 0.05 (axial coupling for nucleon-Δ transition)
- g_1 = 2.32 ± 0.26 (axial coupling for ΔΔ transition)
- b_4 + b_5 = (0 ± 5) GeV^{-1} and b_4 - b_5 = (0 ± 5) GeV^{-1} (Δ-sector low-energy constants)
- M_π = 139.57 MeV, F_π = 92.2 MeV, m_N = 938.27 MeV, m_Δ = 1232 MeV, g_A = 1.289
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: a_0+^+ = (-0.7 ± 6.6) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-1} (isoscalar s-wave scattering length)
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: a_0+^- = (83.4 ± 1.0) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-1} (isovector s-wave scattering length)
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: a_1+^+ = (132.7 ± 7.6) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-3} (isoscalar p-wave scattering volume)
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: a_1+^- = (-81.1 ± 2.1) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-3} (isovector p-wave scattering volume)
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: b_0+^+ = (-33.8 ± 6.6) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-3} (isoscalar effective range)
- At N^3LO in covariant scheme with Δ: b_0+^- = (2.8 ± 2.8) × 10^{-3} M_π^{-3} (isovector effective range)

## Context

This work extends previous matching of Roy-Steiner equations to heavy-baryon ChPT by Hoferichter et al. (2015), which found that the chiral representation was not accurate enough to relate threshold and subthreshold kinematics due to loop effects enhanced by large low-energy constants. The inclusion of the Δ(1232) and covariant formulation addresses this tension, with implications for multi-nucleon applications where subthreshold kinematics are relevant for two-pion exchange in nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results enable more reliable determination of low-energy constants for chiral effective field theory in the few-nucleon sector.
