# Quarkonium Suppression in the Open Quantum System Approach

> Quarkonium suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions has been studied experimentally for decades to probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. For this purpose, complete theoretical understanding of the time evolution of quarkonium inside the quark-gluon plasma is needed but challenging. Here I review recent progress in applying the open quantum system framework to describe the real-time dynamics of quarkonium, with a focus on the gauge-invariant chromoelectric field correlators of the plasma that control the dynamics.

# Quarkonium Suppression in the Open Quantum System Approach

## Key claim
The open quantum system framework provides a more complete theoretical description of quarkonium real-time dynamics inside the quark-gluon plasma than semiclassical transport equations, but its applicability depends on specific hierarchies of time scales (the quantum Brownian motion limit or the quantum optical limit) that determine which approximations to the Lindblad equation are valid.

## Method
The author reviews the derivation of Lindblad equations for the heavy quark-antiquark pair subsystem interacting with the thermal QGP environment, distinguishing two limiting cases: the quantum Brownian motion limit (valid when both the subsystem intrinsic time scale and the relaxation time are much larger than the environment correlation time) and the quantum optical limit (valid when the relaxation time is much larger than both the environment correlation time and the subsystem intrinsic time scale). The QGP properties relevant to quarkonium dynamics are encoded in gauge-invariant chromoelectric field correlators dressed with Wilson lines, which enter the non-Hermitian parts of the Lindblad equations and differ between the two limits in their frequency dependence.

## Key numbers
- The environment (QGP) correlation time is expected to be τ_E ~ 1/T, where T is the QGP temperature.
- In the quantum Brownian motion limit, the hierarchy τ_S ≫ τ_E leads to T ≫ H_{Q\bar{Q}}, meaning only the zero frequency limit of the chromoelectric field correlator G^>_E(q_0=0) contributes.
- In the quantum optical limit, no hierarchy exists between the QGP temperature and the binding energy, so only the finite frequency part of the chromoelectric field correlator matters in bound-unbound transitions.

## Context
This review extends the semiclassical transport approaches (Boltzmann and Langevin equations) used by Chen (2017), Du (2017), Yao (2017-2020), and Zhao (2021) by incorporating quantum coherence and decoherence effects that those methods neglect. The work builds on the open quantum system framework developed by Akamatsu (2014), Katz (2015), Brambilla (2016-2021), Kajimoto (2017), Blaizot (2017-2018), Yao (2018-2020), and Miura (2019). The chromoelectric field correlators discussed are similar to but distinct from the correlator used to define the heavy quark diffusion coefficient studied by Casalderrey-Solana (2006), differing in the Wilson line configurations; perturbative calculations by Burnier (2010) and Binder (2021) showed these correlators agree in the temperature-dependent part but differ by a finite constant at next-to-leading order. The framework enables future applications to dark matter bound state formation in the early universe and jet quenching in heavy ion collisions, and may benefit from quantum computing simulations as explored by De Jong (2020-2021).
