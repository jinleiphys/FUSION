# Interactions of Non-Abelian Global Strings

> Non-Abelian global strings are expected to form during the chiral phase transition. They have orientational zero modes in the internal space, associated with the vector-like symmetry SU(N)_{L+R} broken in the presence of strings. The interaction among two parallel non-Abelian global strings is derived for general relative orientational zero modes, giving a non-Abelian generalization of the Magnus force. It is shown that when the orientations of the strings are the same, the repulsive force reaches the maximum, whereas when the relative orientation becomes the maximum, no force exists between the strings. For the Abelian case we find a finite volume correction to the known result. The marginal instability of the previously known Abelian eta' strings is discussed.

## Key claim

The interaction force between two parallel non-Abelian global strings depends on their relative internal orientation, ranging from a maximum repulsive (or attractive) force when orientations are identical to zero force when the relative orientation is maximal (α = π); this generalizes the known Abelian Magnus force, and the result includes a finite-volume correction to the previously known Abelian case.

## Method

The authors construct two parallel non-Abelian global string solutions in the U(N)_L × U(N)_R linear σ model, parameterizing the relative internal orientation by an SU(2) rotation within the CP^{N-1} moduli space. Using the Abrikosov ansatz for the total configuration (Φ_tot = φ_1 φ_2) and approximating the fields by their asymptotic forms at large separation (a ≫ m^{-1}), they compute the interaction energy density by subtracting the individual string energies from the total gradient energy. The force per unit length is then obtained by differentiating the integrated energy with respect to the string separation.

## Key numbers

* The interaction energy per unit length for two strings separated by distance 2a with relative orientation α is E(a,α,L) = ± π (1 + cos α) [ -ln 4 - 2 ln a + ln(a² + L²) ], where L is an infrared cutoff.
* The force per unit length between the strings is f(a,α,L) = ± (1 + cos α) (π/a - π a/(a² + L²)), which for a ≪ L → ∞ reduces to f ≈ ± (1 + cos α) π/a.
* For the Abelian case (α = 0), the force reduces to the known Magnus force, with the term π a/(a² + L²) giving a finite-volume correction to the results of Vilenkin (1982) and Shellard (1987).
* For the maximum relative orientation (α = π), the force vanishes: f = 0.
* In a stability analysis for N = 3 and coupling κ = 0.2, the lowest eigenvalue for small perturbations is ω² = 1.9851, indicating dynamical stability.

## Context

This work extends the study of global strings from the well-known Abelian case (Vilenkin 1982; Shellard 1987; Perivolaropoulos 1991) to non-Abelian global strings that arise during the chiral phase transition in QCD, as proposed by Balachandran (2002). The non-Abelian strings carry orientational zero modes in CP^{N-1} internal space, and the derived orientation-dependent interaction generalizes the Magnus force. The results show that the previously known Abelian η' string is marginally unstable to decay into N non-Abelian strings with no binding energy, resolving the cosmological domain wall problem. This work enables future studies of string networks with internal degrees of freedom, interactions with Nambu-Goldstone bosons via non-Abelian tensor fields, and applications to color superconductors (reported separately by Nakano, Nitta, and Matsuura).
