# Search for Tetraneutron by Pion Double Charge Exchange Reaction at J-PARC

> Tetraneutron ($^4n$) has come back in the limelight, because of recent observation of a candidate resonant state at RIBF. We propose to investigate the pion double charge exchange (DCX) reaction, i.e. $^4\mathrm{He}(π^- , π^+)$, as an alternative way to populate tetraneutron. An intense $π^-$ beam with the kinetic energy of ~850 MeV, much higher than that in past experiments at LAMPF and TRIUMF, will open up a possibility to improve the experimental sensitivity of the formation cross section, which will be much smaller than hitherto known DCX cross sections such as $^9\mathrm{Be}(π^-, π^+)^9\mathrm{He}\ (g.s.)$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a pion double charge exchange (DCX) reaction on \(^4\mathrm{He}\) at an incident kinetic energy of approximately 850 MeV, much higher than past experiments at LAMPF and TRIUMF, can improve experimental sensitivity to a tetraneutron state, even though the formation cross section is expected to be much smaller than previously known DCX cross sections.

## Method

The paper proposes to measure the \(^4\mathrm{He}(\pi^-,\pi^+)\) reaction at the J-PARC Hadron Experimental Facility using an intense \(\pi^-\) beam with kinetic energy around 850 MeV. As a preparatory step, the authors plan to first study the analog transition \(^{18}\mathrm{O}(\pi^+,\pi^-)^{18}\mathrm{Ne}\) (g.s.) at the K1.8 beamline using the S-2S spectrometer to examine the energy dependence of the DCX cross section and establish experimental procedures. The tetraneutron search itself is planned for the future High-Intensity High-Resolution (HIHR) beamline, with a possible pilot measurement at the existing K1.8 beamline.

## Key numbers

- The candidate resonant tetraneutron state observed at RIBF has an energy of \(0.83 \pm 0.65\,\text{(stat.)} \pm 1.25\,\text{(syst.)}\,\mathrm{MeV}\) relative to the \(4n\) decay threshold, with an upper limit on the width of \(2.6\,\mathrm{MeV}\) in FWHM.
- The LAMPF experiment set an upper limit on the differential cross section for a bound tetraneutron of \(22\,\mathrm{nb/sr}\) at the \(1\sigma\) confidence level.
- The proposed measurement aims for a sensitivity of \(1\,\mathrm{nb/sr}\) at 850 MeV, which is three orders of magnitude smaller than the expected cross section of \(\sim 1\,\mathrm{\mu b/sr}\) for the \(^{18}\mathrm{O}(\pi^+,\pi^-)^{18}\mathrm{Ne}\) (g.s.) reaction.
- At the HIHR beamline, with a \(2.0\,\mathrm{g/cm^2}\)-thick liquid \(^4\mathrm{He}\) target, the yield of a tetraneutron state is estimated at \(97\,\text{events}/(2\,\text{weeks})/(\mathrm{nb/sr})\).
- The analog transition measurement at K1.8 is expected to yield approximately 400 counts per day with a \(10^7/\text{spill}\) \(\pi^+\) beam on a \(2\,\mathrm{g/cm^2}\)-thick \(^{18}\mathrm{O}\)-enriched water target.

## Context

This work extends the search for the tetraneutron, motivated by the recent observation of a candidate resonant state at RIBF by Kisamori et al. (2016), using a completely different reaction mechanism. It contrasts with past pion DCX experiments at LAMPF (Ungar et al., 1980s) and TRIUMF (Gorringe et al., 1980s) that used lower beam energies (165 and 80 MeV, respectively) and found no bound tetraneutron. The proposed higher-energy measurement at J-PARC could enable the first observation of a tetraneutron via pion DCX and, if successful, would provide an independent confirmation of the resonant state, with implications for few-body nuclear physics and the equation of state of neutron stars.
