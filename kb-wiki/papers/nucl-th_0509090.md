# Parity violation in $pp$ scattering and vector-meson weak-coupling constants

> We calculate the parity-nonconserving longitudinal asymmetry in the elastic $\vec{p} p$ scattering at the energies where experimental data are available. In addition to the standard one-meson exchange weak potential, the variation of the strong-coupling constants and the non-standard effects such as form factors and $2 π$-exchange description of the $ρ$-exchange potential are taken into account. With the extra effects, we investigate the compatibility of the experimental data and the presently-known range of the vector-meson weak-coupling constants.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether various non-standard effects (variation of strong-coupling constants, form factors, and a two-pion-exchange description of the rho-exchange potential) can reconcile the experimental parity-nonconserving asymmetry data in elastic proton-proton scattering with a negative omega-meson weak-coupling constant, but finds that none of these effects resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors calculate the parity-nonconserving longitudinal asymmetry in elastic polarized-proton-proton scattering at 13.6, 45, and 221 MeV using a one-meson exchange weak potential. They incorporate variations in the strong-coupling constants of the rho and omega mesons, monopole-type form factors at the strong vertices, a dispersion-relation-based two-pion-exchange description of the rho-meson potential including Delta and N* resonances, and a chiral-soliton-model correction at the isoscalar weak rho-nucleon-nucleon vertex.

## Key numbers

- DDH "best-guess" weak-coupling constants: h^pp_rho = -15.5 and h^pp_omega = -3.04
- Experimental asymmetry at 13.6 MeV: (-0.95 ± 0.15) × 10^(-7)
- Experimental asymmetry at 45 MeV: (-1.50 ± 0.23) × 10^(-7)
- Experimental asymmetry at 221 MeV: (0.84 ± 0.29) × 10^(-7)
- With strong-coupling set S1 and DDH best-guess weak couplings, the bare rho-exchange asymmetry at 13.6 MeV is -0.96 × 10^(-7); at 45 MeV is -1.73 × 10^(-7); at 221 MeV is 0.43 × 10^(-7)
- With strong-coupling set S2 and DDH best-guess weak couplings, the bare rho-exchange asymmetry at 13.6 MeV is -1.33 × 10^(-7); at 45 MeV is -2.39 × 10^(-7); at 221 MeV is 0.75 × 10^(-7)
- The two-pion-plus-N*-exchange contribution enhances the asymmetry by factors of 1.23 to 1.28 relative to the bare rho-exchange at all three energies
- With the chiral-soliton vertex correction (cutoff Lambda' = 0.771 GeV) and set S1, the asymmetry at 13.6 MeV becomes -1.69 × 10^(-7); at 45 MeV becomes -2.92 × 10^(-7); at 221 MeV becomes 0.67 × 10^(-7)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Carlson et al. (2002), who obtained a positive omega-nucleon-nucleon PNC coupling from the same experimental data, and contrasts with the DDH "best-guess" values from Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1980) and updated calculations that favor negative omega-nucleon-nucleon weak couplings. The paper shows that the considered non-standard effects cannot simultaneously satisfy all three experimental data points with a negative omega weak-coupling constant, suggesting either missing contributions to the PNC potential, large corrections to the single-meson exchange model, or possible issues with the 221 MeV experimental measurement.
