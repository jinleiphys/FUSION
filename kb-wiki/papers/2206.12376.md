# Thermalization of gluons in spatially homogeneous systems

> We investigate thermalization of gluons in spatially homogeneous systems using the Boltzmann equation in diffusion approximation. A complete picture on thermalization is obtained for both initially under- and over-populated systems. In an initially under-populated system, we find that its soft sector undergoes three stages: overheating, cooling/overcooling and reheating before full thermalization is achieved. In an initially over-populated system, we find that its soft sector only undergoes two stages towards full thermalization: overheating and cooling. The cooling stage is consistently driven by momentum broadening due to multiple elastic collisions, manifest as a non-thermal scaling solution.

# Thermalization of gluons in spatially homogeneous systems

## Key claim

The Boltzmann equation in diffusion approximation (BEDA) reproduces the same qualitative thermalization stages as the more complete effective kinetic theory (EKT) for both under- and over-populated gluon systems, provided the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) inelastic rate is included; the authors hedge that the BEDA and EKT differ in the typical momentum of soft gluons at very early times, but this does not change the overall thermalization picture.

## Method

The authors solved the QCD Boltzmann equation numerically in the diffusion approximation, including both elastic 2-to-2 scattering (dominated by small-angle collisions) and inelastic 1-to-2 splitting with the LPM effect. They used parametric estimates to identify distinct thermalization stages and verified these by numerically evolving the phase-space distribution for spatially homogeneous systems with initial step-function occupation up to momentum Q. The Debye mass, jet quenching parameter, and effective temperature were tracked as functions of time.

## Key numbers

- For an initially under-populated system with $f_0=0.01$ and $\alpha_s=0.1$, full thermalization is achieved at $Qt \gtrsim 200$.
- The transition from the overheating stage to the cooling stage in under-populated systems occurs at $t \sim \alpha_s^{-2} f_0 Q^{-1}$.
- The overcooling temperature minimum in under-populated systems scales as $T_*/T_{eq} \sim f_0^{1/12}$ at $Qt \sim \alpha_s^{-2} f_0^{-1/3}$.
- For over-populated systems, the scaling solution during the cooling stage gives $p \sim (\alpha_s f_0)^{2/7} (Qt)^{1/7} Q$ and $T_* \sim (\alpha_s f_0)^{1/7} \alpha_s^{-1} Q (Qt)^{-3/7}$.
- The transition to the scaling regime in over-populated systems begins at $t \sim (\alpha_s f_0)^{-2} Q^{-1}$.
- Full thermalization in over-populated systems occurs at $Qt \sim \alpha_s^{-7/4} (\alpha_s f_0)^{-1/4}$.

## Context

This work extends the parametric bottom-up thermalization picture of Baier (2000) by providing the first numerical solution of the BEDA including both elastic scattering and the LPM inelastic rate. It confirms the three-stage thermalization (overheating, cooling/overcooling, reheating) for under-populated systems and the two-stage process (overheating, cooling) for over-populated systems previously seen in EKT calculations by Kurkela (2014) and Du (2020). The universal scaling solution found in over-populated systems matches earlier results from classical statistical field simulations by Berges (2012) and Schlichting (2012). The work enables future studies of thermalization and hydrodynamization in 2+1D or 3+1D systems using the computationally cheaper BEDA approach.
