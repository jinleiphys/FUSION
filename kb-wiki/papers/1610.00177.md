# Implications for (d,p) reaction theory from nonlocal dispersive optical model analysis of $^{40}$Ca(d,p)$^{41}$Ca

> The nonlocal dispersive optical model (NLDOM) nucleon potentials are used for the first time in the adiabatic analysis of a (d,p) reaction to generate distorted waves both in the entrance and exit channels. These potentials were designed and fitted by Mahzoon $et \text{ } al.$ [Phys. Rev. Lett. 112, 162502 (2014)] to constrain relevant single-particle physics in a consistent way by imposing the fundamental properties, such as nonlocality, energy-dependence and dispersive relations, that follow from the complex nature of nuclei. However, the NLDOM prediction for the $^{40}$Ca(d,p)$^{41}$Ca cross sections at low energy, typical for some modern radioactive beam ISOL facilities, is about 70$\%$ higher than the experimental data despite being reduced by the NLDOM spectroscopic factor of 0.73. This overestimation comes most likely either from insufficient absorption or due to constructive interference between ingoing and outgoing waves. This indicates strongly that additional physics arising from many-body effects is missing in the widely used current versions of (d,p) reaction theories.

## Key claim

The NLDOM prediction for the \(^{40}\)Ca(d,p)\(^{41}\)Ca cross section at 11.8 MeV is about 70% higher than experimental data even after applying the NLDOM spectroscopic factor of 0.73, indicating that additional many-body physics is missing from current (d,p) reaction theories, with the overestimation likely arising from either insufficient absorption or constructive interference between ingoing and outgoing waves.

## Method

The authors used the nonlocal dispersive optical model (NLDOM) nucleon potentials, originally fitted by Mahzoon et al. (2014), to generate distorted waves in both the entrance and exit channels for an adiabatic analysis of the \(^{40}\)Ca(d,p)\(^{41}\)Ca reaction at 11.8 MeV. They constructed a local-equivalent deuteron-target adiabatic potential by extending the local scheme proposed by Timofeyuk (2013) to handle the seven different nonlocality parameters of the NLDOM, including first-order corrections and a generalized Perey factor. The NLDOM parameters were refit to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the \(0f_{7/2}\) neutron level in \(^{41}\)Ca (8.36 MeV), and the resulting cross sections were compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The NLDOM prediction for the \(^{40}\)Ca(d,p)\(^{41}\)Ca cross section at 11.8 MeV is about 70% higher than the experimental data.
- The NLDOM spectroscopic factor used to reduce the prediction is 0.73.
- The experimental binding energy of the \(0f_{7/2}\) neutron level in \(^{41}\)Ca is 8.36 MeV.
- The nonlocality parameters in the NLDOM range from 0.64 fm to 2.07 fm, with values including \(\beta_{vol_1} = 0.84\) fm, \(\beta_{vol_2} = 1.55\) fm, \(\beta_{wb} = 1.04\) fm, \(\beta_{sur+} = 0.94\) fm, \(\beta_{sur-} = 2.07\) fm, \(\beta_{vol+} = 0.64\) fm, and \(\beta_{vol-} = 0.81\) fm.
- The refit Hartree-Fock volume potential strength \(V_{HF}^0\) was changed from 100.06 MeV to 106.15 MeV, and the radius parameter \(r_{HF}\) from 1.10 fm to 1.14 fm.

## Context

This work extends the use of NLDOM potentials, previously applied to nucleon scattering and (p,d) reactions by Ross (2015) within DWBA, to the adiabatic analysis of (d,p) reactions for the first time. It follows the theoretical prescription of Johnson (2014) for choosing nucleon-nucleus potentials in the \(A+n+p\) three-body problem, which requires evaluating the potentials at half the deuteron incident energy plus about 57 MeV from the \(n-p\) kinetic energy. The results challenge current (d,p) reaction theories, including DWBA and ADWA, by showing that even with the sophisticated NLDOM potentials, the predicted cross sections significantly overestimate data, suggesting that many-body effects beyond the current framework are needed.
