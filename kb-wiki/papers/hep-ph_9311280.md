# Transverse Quark Distribution in Mesons - QCD Sum Rule Approach -

> QCD sum rules are used to compute the first few moments of the mesonic quark momentum. Transverse, longitudinal and mixed transverse-longitudinal components are examined. The transverse size of the pion is shown to be dictated by the gluon condensate, even though the mass and the longitudinal distribution are dominated by the quark condensate. The implications of our results for color transparency physics and finite temperature QCD are discussed.

## Key claim

The transverse size of the pion is dictated by the gluon condensate, even though the mass and longitudinal distribution are dominated by the quark condensate; the authors derive sum rules for mixed transverse-longitudinal moments of mesonic wave functions using QCD sum rules, with estimated uncertainties of plus or minus 50 percent for all moments.

## Method

The authors derived new Borel sum rules for the mixed moments ⟨ξ^(n-l) k_⊥^l⟩ of the pion (and rho meson) wave function by starting from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and expanding in longitudinal and transverse components. They performed the operator product expansion (OPE) for the relevant correlation function up to dimension-six operators, including perturbative, gluon condensate, and four-quark condensate contributions, then matched the OPE side to the phenomenological side via Borel transformation. The sum rules were analyzed by finding a Borel window where power corrections are less than 30 percent of the perturbative part and choosing the continuum threshold S_0 that minimizes dependence on the Borel mass M^2.

## Key numbers

* ⟨k_⊥^2⟩ (the n=0, l=2 moment) implies an average transverse momentum of (300 MeV)^2, consistent with a rough estimate of (323 MeV)^2 from Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1992).
* The vacuum condensates used are ⟨√α ūu⟩^2 ≃ 1.83 × 10^(-4) GeV^6 and ⟨(α/π) G^2⟩ ≃ 1.2 × 10^(-2) GeV^4.
* For the n=0, l=2 moment: moment value 0.36, M_min^2 = 0.8 GeV^2, S_0 = 0.5 GeV^2.
* For the n=2, l=2 moment: moment value 0.14, M_min^2 = 1.3 GeV^2, S_0 = 0.9 GeV^2.
* For the n=4, l=2 moment: moment value 0.09, M_min^2 = 1.9 GeV^2, S_0 = 1.4 GeV^2.
* For the n=0, l=4 moment: moment value 1.17, M_min^2 = 1.2 GeV^2, S_0 = 0.4 GeV^2.
* For the n=2, l=4 moment: moment value 0.30, M_min^2 = 1.5 GeV^2, S_0 = 0.5 GeV^2.
* For the n=4, l=4 moment: moment value 0.20, M_min^2 = 2.0 GeV^2, S_0 = 0.5 GeV^2.
* The quantity Δ ≡ [⟨k_⊥^4⟩ − ⟨k_⊥^2⟩^2] / ⟨k_⊥^2⟩^2 is approximately 8 from the sum rule results.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1982, 1984), who determined longitudinal moments ⟨ξ^(2n)⟩ of the pion distribution function, to include transverse momentum dependence for the first time. The results are relevant for color transparency physics, where the transverse size of the pion determines whether color transparency effects can be observed, and for finite temperature QCD, where the authors find that to lowest order in T^2/(6f_π^2) the transverse moments do not change. The authors note that Mikhailov and Radyushkin (1992) criticized the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky approach for neglecting non-local condensates, and they estimate a 50 percent uncertainty from this source. The results enable future work on factorization of longitudinal and transverse degrees of freedom in meson wave functions and on the temperature dependence of hadronic properties.
