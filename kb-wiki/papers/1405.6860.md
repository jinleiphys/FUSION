# Charge splitting of directed flow and space-time picture of pion emission from the electromagnetic interactions with spectators

> We estimate the effect of the spectator-induced electromagnetic interaction on the directed flow of charged pions. For intermediate centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$~GeV, we demonstrate that the electromagnetic interaction between spectator charges and final state pions results in charge splitting of positive and negative pion directed flow. Such a charge splitting is visible in the experimental data reported by the STAR Collaboration. The magnitude of this charge splitting appears to strongly depend on the actual distance between the pion emission site (pion at freeze-out) and the spectator system. As such, the above electromagnetic effect brings new, independent information on the space-time evolution of pion production in heavy ion collisions. From the comparison of our present analysis to our earlier studies made for pions produced at higher rapidity, we formulate conclusions on the rapidity dependence of the distance between the pion emission site and the spectator system. This distance appears to decrease with increasing pion rapidity, reflecting the longitudinal expansion of the strongly-interacting system responsible for pion emission. Thus for the first time, information on the space-time characteristics of the system is being provided by means of the spectator-induced electromagnetic interaction. The above electromagnetic effect being in fact a straight-forward consequence of the presence of spectator charges in the collision, we consider that it should be considered as a baseline for studies of other phenomena, like those related to the electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The spectator-induced electromagnetic interaction between spectator charges and final state pions is sufficient to explain the observed charge splitting of positive and negative pion directed flow in intermediate centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, with the magnitude of this splitting strongly depending on the distance between the pion emission site and the spectator system.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of charged pions propagating in the electromagnetic field generated by two spectator systems, modeled as homogeneously charged Lorentz-contracted spheres following their initial trajectories. Pions were assumed to be emitted from a single point in space and time, with initial (y, pT) distributions based on nucleon-nucleon collisions scaled by wounded nucleon counting and full azimuthal symmetry. The pions were numerically traced in the combined electric and magnetic fields from both spectators using the Lorentz force and relativistic equations of motion, with the reduced distance DE between the pion emission site and the spectator system as the single free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The mean number of participants for intermediate centrality (10-40%) Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV was about 166, with a geometrical impact parameter bgeom = 7.09 fm and average spectator charge Q = 45.7 elementary units.
- The effective impact parameter (distance of closest approach between the two sphere centers) was b = 11.1 fm.
- The electromagnetically-induced directed flow values v1π+,EM and v1π-,EM extracted from STAR data reached up to about 0.002.
- In the simulation, electromagnetically-induced directed flow reached absolute values up to 0.025 in the considered rapidity range, with the best fit to STAR data obtained for DE = 3 fm.
- For pions at higher rapidity (y/ybeam > 0.64), the optimal emission distance dE was found to be in the range 0-1 fm from earlier studies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of spectator-induced electromagnetic effects on pion directed flow in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 17.3 GeV (Rybicki 2013) and on π+/π- ratios at high rapidity (Rybicki 2012). The analysis uses STAR Collaboration data (STAR 2014) on charged pion directed flow in Au+Au collisions. The electromagnetic effect studied here provides a baseline for more complex phenomena such as those related to electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma discussed by Hirono, Hongo and Hirano (2012) and by Gursoy, Kharzeev and Rajagopal (2014). The results enable extraction of space-time information on the longitudinally expanding system created in heavy ion collisions, independent from femtoscopic analyses or blast-wave model fits.
