# Bremsstrahlung in alpha-decay: angular analysis of spectra

> A quantum mechanical method of calculation of bremsstrahlung spectra in alpha-decay of heavy nuclei with taking into account an angle between directions of the alpha-particle motion and the photon emission is presented. Dependence between the bremsstrahlung spectrum and the angle value is obtained in a simple analytical form. The method can be used for a comparative analysis of experimental data, obtained at different angles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum mechanical method for calculating bremsstrahlung spectra in alpha-decay that includes the angle between the alpha-particle direction and photon emission, obtaining the angular dependence in a simple analytical form; however, the method relies on the approximation that the photon emission does not change the direction of the alpha-particle motion, and the authors note that the model is primarily useful for comparative analysis of experimental data obtained at different angles rather than for absolute predictions.

## Method

The authors develop a multipolar quantum mechanical model based on quantum electrodynamics, using the probability of spontaneous photon emission expressed through wave functions of the initial and final states of the alpha-particle system. They apply a multipolar expansion of the vector potential of the electromagnetic field of the daughter nucleus, and derive expressions for the bremsstrahlung spectrum that include angular integrals involving vector spherical harmonics. The key simplification is the assumption that the direction of the alpha-particle motion is unchanged during photon emission, which allows expressing the angular dependence through a simple exponential factor exp(-ikr cosβ) and reduces the calculation to the E1 multipole contribution.

## Key numbers

* The model is applied to the nucleus ²¹⁰Po, for which experimental data exist at angles of 90° and 25°.
* The tunneling time of the alpha-particle through the decay barrier is estimated to be approximately 10⁻²⁰–10⁻²⁴ seconds (nuclear timescales).
* The bremsstrahlung spectrum probability is given by dW/dΩ_ν = (Z²_eff e² k_f w_fi)/((2π)⁴ m) |p(k_i, k_f)|², where k_i,f = (1/ℏ)√(2mE_i,f) and w_fi = E_i – E_f.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous multipolar quantum mechanical model (Maydanyuk, 2003) which first allowed calculation of bremsstrahlung spectra dependent on the angle between alpha-particle motion and photon emission, but suffered from convergence problems in numerical calculations. The new simplified approach addresses these limitations by making the angular dependence more explicit and computationally tractable. The method enables comparative analysis of experimental data from the Russian-Italian group (Eremin, 2000) at 90° and the Japanese group (Kasagi, 1997) at 25°, which show qualitative differences that may be explained by the angular dependence. The approach contrasts with earlier isotropic models (Kasagi, 1997; D'Arrigo, 1994; Papenbrock, 1998; Tkalya, 1999) and with the instant accelerated classical model (D'Arrigo, 1994) which required additional adjustable parameters.
