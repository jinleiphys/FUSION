# Flavor decomposition of the elastic nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> The u- and d-quark contributions to the elastic nucleon electromagnetic form factors have been determined using experimental data on GEn, GMn, GpE, and GpM. Such a flavor separation of the form factors became possible up to 3.4 GeV2 with recent data on GEn from Hall A at JLab. At a negative four-momentum transfer squared Q2 above 1 GeV2, for both the u- and d-quark components, the ratio of the Pauli form factor to the Dirac form factor, F2/F1, was found to be almost constant, and for each of F2 and F1 individually, the d-quark portions of both form factors drop continuously with increasing Q2.

## Key claim

Using recent data on the neutron electric form factor from Hall A at Jefferson Lab, the authors performed a flavor decomposition of the elastic nucleon electromagnetic form factors up to Q2 = 3.4 GeV2 and found that, for Q2 above 1 GeV2, the ratios F2u/F1u and F2d/F1d are almost constant, contrary to the more complex behavior of the proton and neutron ratios; the abstract glosses over the fact that this constancy is only observed for Q2 > 1 GeV2, while at lower Q2 these same ratios show significant variation.

## Method

The authors used experimental data on the proton electric and magnetic form factors (GEp, GMp) and the neutron electric and magnetic form factors (GEn, GMn) to extract the u- and d-quark contributions to the Dirac (F1) and Pauli (F2) form factors via the relations F1(2)u = 2 F1(2)p + F1(2)n and F1(2)d = 2 F1(2)n + F1(2)p, assuming charge symmetry. The neutron GEn/GMn data from Riordan et al. (2010) and other references were combined with the Kelly parameterization for the other form factors to produce the flavor-separated results.

## Key numbers

- Flavor decomposition was performed up to Q2 = 3.4 GeV2 using recent GEn data from Hall A at JLab.
- At Q2 = 0, the normalizations are F1u(0) = 2 and F1d(0) = 1; the anomalous magnetic moments are kappa_u = +1.67 and kappa_d = -2.03.
- At Q2 = 3.41 GeV2, the flavor-separated form factors are: F1u = 0.0851(12), F1d = 0.0131(24), F2u = 0.0370(12), F2d = -0.0337(24).
- The slope of S_d (Q2 F2d/F1d) with Q2 is about six times larger than that of S_u.
- Up to Q2 approximately 1 GeV2, there is a constant scaling factor of about 2.5 for F1 and about 0.75 for F2 between the u- and d-quark contributions.

## Context

This work extends the flavor decomposition of nucleon form factors to higher Q2 than previously possible, enabled by the GEn data from Riordan et al. (2010). The results contradict the expectation from the relativistic constituent quark model of Miller (2005), which deviates considerably from the data, and are in qualitative agreement with predictions for generalized parton distribution moments from Diehl et al. (2005). The findings enable future measurements of GEn/GMn up to Q2 = 10 GeV2 to explore the range over which the constant behavior of F2u/F1u and F2d/F1d persists.
