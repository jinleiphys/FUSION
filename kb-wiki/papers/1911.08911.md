# Time-evolution of net-baryon density fluctuations across the QCD critical region

> We investigate the role of a finite surface tension during the time-evolution of fluctuations in the net-baryon density. The systems in this study undergo a temperature evolution across the phase transition in the critical region of the QCD phase diagram. The occuring non-equilibrium effects are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a finite surface tension in the stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density fluctuations leads to larger non-equilibrium effects than in the simpler Gauss model, with the local variance at the critical temperature reduced by a factor of 2 compared to equilibrium even for the largest diffusion coefficient studied.

## Method

The authors numerically solve a stochastic diffusion equation for net-baryon density fluctuations in one spatial dimension, using a free energy functional that includes a Gaussian mass term, a kinetic energy term with surface tension K, and non-linear coupling terms. They compare three models: the Gauss model (K=0, λ_i=0), the Gauss+surface model (K≠0, λ_i=0), and the Ginzburg-Landau model (K≠0, λ_i≠0). The system undergoes a temperature evolution following T(τ)=T_0(τ_0/τ) from T_0=0.5 GeV at τ_0=1 fm/c, with the pseudo-critical temperature reached at τ-τ_0=2.33 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Critical density n_c = 1/(3 fm^3)
- System size L = 20 fm
- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.5 GeV at initial time τ_0 = 1 fm/c
- Pseudo-critical temperature reached at τ-τ_0 = 2.33 fm/c
- For the Gauss+surface model with D(τ_0)=1 fm, the maximum of the local variance σ^2 is reduced by a factor of 2 compared to the equilibrium value at T_c due to non-equilibrium effects
- Diffusion coefficients studied: D = 1, 0.1, and 0.01 fm at τ_0 = 1 fm/c
- Dimensionless couplings for the Ginzburg-Landau model: λ̃_3=1, λ̃_4=10, λ̃_6=3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of critical fluctuation dynamics in one spatial dimension by Nahrgang et al. (2017, 2018) that examined the Gauss model and Ginzburg-Landau model separately. The paper specifically contrasts the Gauss model (studied in Nahrgang 2017) with models including surface tension, showing that finite-range correlations require additional time to build up, leading to stronger non-equilibrium effects. The results enable future work incorporating Bjorken expansion and second-order relaxation equations for embedding in full fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
