# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay from Lattice QCD: The Short-Distance $ŽĆ^-\rightarrowŽĆ^+ e^- e^-$ Amplitude

> This work presents a determination of potential short-distance contributions to the unphysical $ŽĆ^-\rightarrowŽĆ^+ e^- e^-$ decay through lattice QCD calculations. The hadronic contributions to the transition amplitude are described by the pion matrix elements of five Standard Model Effective Field Theory operators, which are computed on five ensembles of domain-wall fermions with $N_f = 2 + 1$ quark flavors with a range of heavier-than-physical values of the light quark masses. The matrix elements are extrapolated to the continuum, physical light-quark mass, and infinite volume limit using a functional form derived in chiral Effective Field Theory ($Žć\mathrm{EFT}$). This extrapolation also yields the relevant low-energy constants of $Žć\mathrm{EFT}$, which are necessary input for $Žć\mathrm{EFT}$ calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay of nuclei.

## Key claim

This work presents a direct lattice QCD computation of the pion matrix elements of the five leading short-distance (dimension-9) operators that contribute to the unphysical $\pi^-\rightarrow\pi^+ e^- e^-$ decay, performed at zero electron mass and at threshold, with the results showing mild tension with a previous mixed-action lattice QCD calculation of the same matrix elements by the same research community.

## Method

The authors compute the bare pion matrix elements of five Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) operators on five ensembles of domain-wall fermions with $N_f = 2 + 1$ quark flavors at heavier-than-physical light quark masses, using wall-source propagators in Coulomb gauge. The bare matrix elements are extracted from three-point correlation functions via excited-state fits, then renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI/sMOM scheme and perturbatively matched to the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme. Finally, the renormalized matrix elements are extrapolated to the continuum, physical light-quark mass, and infinite volume limits using a functional form derived in chiral Effective Field Theory ($\chi$EFT), which also yields the relevant low-energy constants $\beta_k$ needed for $\chi$EFT calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay in nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses on the five ensembles range from 302.0(1.1) MeV to 432.2(1.4) MeV, with lattice spacings of 0.1106(3) fm (24I ensemble) and 0.0828(3) fm (32I ensemble)
- The physical pion decay constant used for normalization is $f_{\pi}^{(\mathrm{phys})} = 130.2\;\mathrm{MeV}$
- Fits of the effective matrix elements to the excited-state model yield $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof}$ values between 0.10 and 0.73
- The electroweak scale is $v = 247\;\mathrm{GeV}$, and the scale of chiral symmetry breaking is $\Lambda_{\chi}^2\equiv 8\pi^2 f_\pi^2$

## Context

This work extends the same collaboration's previous lattice QCD calculation of the long-distance $\pi^-\rightarrow\pi^+ e^- e^-$ amplitude mediated by light Majorana neutrino exchange (Detmold 2020) by now computing the short-distance contributions on the same ensembles, enabling a consistent comparison of both contributions within a single framework. The results show mild tension with an earlier mixed-action lattice QCD calculation of the same short-distance pion matrix elements (PhysRevLett.121.172501), which the authors suggest may be due to differences in the fermion action used. The extracted $\chi$EFT low-energy constants $\beta_k$ provide necessary input for $\chi$EFT calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay in nuclei such as $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, $^{76}\mathrm{Ge}$, and $^{136}\mathrm{Xe}$, which are currently studied by experiments constraining half-lives beyond $10^{26}$ years.
