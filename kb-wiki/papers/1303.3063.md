# Probing nuclear dynamics in jet production with a global event shape

> We study single jet production in electron-nucleus collisions e^- + N_A -> J + X, using the 1-jettiness (τ_1) global event shape. It inclusively quantifies the pattern of radiation in the final state, gives enhanced sensitivity to soft radiation at wide angles from the nuclear beam and final-state jet, and facilitates the resummation of large Sudakov logarithms associated with the veto on additional jets. Through their effect on the observed pattern of radiation, 1-jettiness can be a useful probe of nuclear PDFs and power corrections from dynamical effects in the nuclear medium. This formalism allows for the standard jet shape analysis while simultaneously providing sensitivity to soft radiation at wide angles from the jet. We use a factorization framework for cross-sections differential in $τ_1$ and the transverse momentum (P_{J_T}) and rapidity (y) of the jet, in the region τ_1<< P_{J_T}. The restriction $τ_1 << P_{J_T}$ allows only soft radiation between the nuclear beam and jet directions, thereby acting as a veto on additional jets. This region is also insensitive to the details of the jet algorithm, allowing for better theoretical control over resummation, while providing enhanced sensitivity to nuclear medium effects. We give numerical results at leading twist, with resummation at the next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) level of accuracy, for a variety of nuclear targets. Such studies would be ideal for the EIC and the LHeC proposals for a future electron-ion collider, where a range of nuclear targets are planned.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the 1-jettiness (τ₁) global event shape in single jet production from electron-nucleus collisions can serve as a probe of nuclear PDFs and power corrections from dynamical effects in the nuclear medium, provided the phase space is restricted to τ₁ ≪ P_J_T (the jet transverse momentum), which acts as a veto on additional jets and suppresses jet algorithm dependence.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization framework using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the differential cross-section d³σ/(dy dP_J_T dτ₁) in the region τ₁ ≪ P_J_T, separating the physics into hard, beam, jet, and soft functions. They perform resummation of Sudakov logarithms at the next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) level of accuracy for a variety of nuclear targets, using nuclear parton distribution functions at leading twist.

## Key numbers

- Cross-section differential in τ₁ and rapidity y shown for proton target at P_J_T = 20 GeV and center-of-mass energy of 90 GeV, with NNLL resummation
- Nuclear scale parameterization: Q_s²(A) ∼ A^α Λ_QCD², where α determines scaling with atomic weight A
- Power corrections scale as Q_s²(A)/(τ₁ P_J_T), increasing for heavier nuclear targets and smaller τ₁ and P_J_T values
- The small parameter λ² ∼ τ₁/P_J_T defines the SCET power counting in the resummation region

## Context

This work extends the 1-jettiness formalism previously applied to hadronic collisions (Stewart 2010) to electron-nucleus scattering, building on the factorization framework first derived for this observable in Kang (2012). It provides the first NNLL resummation for a DIS event shape, enabling future studies at the proposed EIC and LHeC facilities where a range of nuclear targets are planned. The approach complements traditional jet shape analyses by simultaneously providing sensitivity to wide-angle soft radiation that can reveal nuclear medium effects such as energy loss and induced gluon radiation.
