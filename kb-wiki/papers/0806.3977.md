# The neutron negative central charge density: an inclusive-exclusive connection

> Models of generalized parton distributions at zero skewness are used to relate the behavior of deep inelastic scattering quark distributions, evaluated at high x, to the transverse charge density evaluated at small distances. We obtain an interpretation of the recently obtained negative central charge density of the neutron. The d quarks dominate the neutron structure function for large values of Bjorken x, where the large momentum of the struck quark has a significant impact on determining the center of momentum, and thus the ``center'' of the nucleon in the transverse position plane.

## Key claim

Using models of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness, the negative central transverse charge density of the neutron arises naturally because d quarks dominate the neutron structure function at high Bjorken x, and the large momentum of the struck quark significantly affects the definition of the center of momentum in the transverse plane, localizing the negative contribution near b=0.

## Method

The authors relate deep inelastic scattering quark distributions at high x to transverse charge densities at small distances using GPDs at zero skewness. They compute the impact-parameter-dependent parton distributions ρ⊥^q(b,x) as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the GPD H_q(x,t), using three different GPD parameterizations (Diehl et al. 2005, Guidal et al. 2005, Ahmad et al. 2007) that incorporate fits to parton distributions and electromagnetic form factors. They separate the contributions to the neutron transverse charge density into different x-regions and also examine the distribution relative to spectator partons by rescaling to B_rel = b/(1-x).

## Key numbers

- The best-fit GPD parameters from Diehl et al. (2005) are: A_u = 1.26 GeV^{-2}, B_u = 0.59 GeV^{-2}, A_d = 3.82 GeV^{-2}, B_d = 0.32 GeV^{-2}
- For x ≈ 0.1, the half-maximum width of the proton transverse charge density is 0.5 fm; for x ≈ 0.8, it is 0.12 fm
- The four Δx regions (x<0.15, 0.15<x<0.3, 0.3<x<0.5, x>0.5) yield 58%, 25%, 14%, and 3% of the total proton charge, respectively
- Above x=0.5, d_v^n(x) is at least three times the size of u_v^n(x) in the neutron
- The separation between low and high x contributions to the neutron charge density uses a boundary at x=0.23

## Context

This work extends the model-independent transverse charge density formalism of Miller (2007) by using the inclusive-exclusive connection between deep inelastic scattering and elastic form factors via GPDs. It provides an interpretation of the negative central neutron charge density that contradicts the intuitive pion cloud picture (where a positive core with a negative tail would be expected). The analysis uses GPD parameterizations from Diehl et al. (2005), Guidal et al. (2005), and Ahmad et al. (2007), and relates to the Foldy term explanation for the difference between electric and magnetic radii in the proton from Miller (2007b). This work enables a deeper understanding of how parton distributions at different x values contribute to the spatial charge distribution of nucleons.
