# A novel solution to the hyperon-puzzle in neutron stars

> Neutron stars offer a great opportunity to study highly compressed hadronic matter experimentally and theoretically. However, the so-called hyperon-puzzle arises at neutron star densities. The hyperon coexistence with other particles in compressed matter softens the equation of state and many widely-accepted models fail to reproduce precise observations of large neutron star masses. Here, we propose a novel mechanism to retain the stiffness of the high density state with hyperons by considering the explicit momentum dependence of their in-medium potentials. Our approach modifies conventional strangeness threshold conditions and generates new threshold effects on hyperons in high-density matter. We demonstrate these effects within the Non-Linear Derivative model, which incorporates baryon momentum-dependent fields based on empirical and microscopic studies. It turns out that even soft momentum-dependent strangeness fields do prohibit their populations in neutron star matter. The generic momentum dependence of strangeness potentials, as modeled by the non-linear derivative approach, is crucial for resolving the long-standing hyperon-puzzle in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the explicit momentum dependence of hyperon in-medium potentials, as modeled by the Non-Linear Derivative (NLD) approach, can resolve the hyperon-puzzle in neutron stars by preventing hyperon population even when conventional threshold conditions are met at zero momentum.

## Method

The authors extend the NLD model, which incorporates baryon momentum-dependent fields via non-linear derivative operators, to β-equilibrated neutron star matter including Λ, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, and Σ⁻ hyperons. They solve the coupled set of non-linear equations for the σ-meson field, chemical equilibrium conditions, and charge neutrality constraints self-consistently, using hyperon potentials calibrated to chiral effective field theory (χ-EFT) results at saturation density. The momentum-dependent regulators in the NLD model modify the conventional strangeness threshold conditions by introducing an explicit momentum dependence into the hyperon selfenergies and in-medium energies.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass M ≈ 2.05 M⊙ is maintained with hyperons included in the NLD model, the same value as without hyperons.
- The Λ-hyperon potential shows a density-dependent transition from stiff to soft momentum-dependent behavior, becoming clearly visible at ρB = 5.5 ρsat (approximately 0.85 fm⁻³).
- NLD hyperon cutoff parameters are given in units of GeV: for Λ hyperon, Λσ = 0.76 GeV, Λω₁ = 0.95 GeV, Λω₂ = 0.79 GeV (first set) and Λω₂ = 0.75 GeV (second set); for Σ⁻ hyperon, Λσ = 0.85 GeV, Λω₁ = 0.95 GeV, Λω₂ = 0.79 GeV, Λρ = 0.6 GeV.
- The NLD model reproduces χ-EFT optical potentials at densities of ρB = 0.062, 0.155, and 0.2325 fm⁻³ for pure neutron matter.

## Context

This work extends the NLD model previously applied to nuclear matter systems (Gaitanos, 2013; Gaitanos, 2011; Gaitanos, 2015) to include strangeness degrees of freedom in β-equilibrated neutron star matter. It directly addresses the hyperon-puzzle, where conventional relativistic mean-field models such as the NLρ model (Ref. NLr) produce significant hyperon fractions that soften the equation of state and reduce maximum neutron star masses below observed values (Demorest, 2010; Antoniadis, 2013). Previous proposed solutions included modified vector repulsion and additional strangeness exchange mesons (Bednarek, 2012; Weissenborn, 2012; Oertel, 2015; Maslov, 2015), while microscopic approaches (Gerstung, 2020; Petschauer, 2016; Haidenbauer, 2023) predicted explicit momentum dependence of hyperon potentials. The NLD approach enables a consistent description of both nuclear matter properties and the suppression of hyperon populations at high densities, resolving the hyperon-puzzle without introducing additional mesonic degrees of freedom.
