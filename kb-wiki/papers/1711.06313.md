# Relativistic particle in a box: Klein-Gordon vs Dirac Equations

> The problem of a particle in a box is probably the simplest problem in quantum mechanics which allows for significant insight into the nature of quantum systems and thus is a cornerstone in the teaching of quantum mechanics. In relativistic quantum mechanics this problem allows also to highlight the implications of special relativity for quantum physics, namely the effect that spin has on the quantized energy spectra. To illustrate this point, we solve the problem of a spin zero relativistic particle in a one- and three-dimensional box using the Klein-Gordon equation in the Feshbach-Villars formalism. We compare the solutions and the energy spectra obtained with the corresponding ones from the Dirac equation for a spin one-half relativistic particle. We note the similarities and differences, in particular the spin effects in the relativistic energy spectrum. As expected, the non-relativistic limit is the same for both kinds of particles, since, for a particle in a box, the spin contribution to the energy is a relativistic effect.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a relativistic particle in a box, the spin of the particle affects the quantized energy spectrum only as a relativistic effect; in the non-relativistic limit (box size much larger than the Compton wavelength), the energy spectra for spin-0 (Klein-Gordon) and spin-1/2 (Dirac) particles become identical, despite the different boundary conditions required by each equation.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for a spin-0 particle in one- and three-dimensional infinite square wells using the Feshbach-Villars formalism, which rewrites the second-order Klein-Gordon equation as a Schrodinger-type equation linear in time. To avoid the Klein paradox and retain a one-particle description, they model the infinite well as a position-dependent mass that goes to infinity outside the box. They compare the resulting energy spectra with those previously obtained for the Dirac equation for a spin-1/2 particle in the same potentials, analyzing the differences in terms of dimensionless quantities scaled by the Compton wavelength.

## Key numbers

* For a one-dimensional box of length L, the Klein-Gordon energy is E_B(n) = sqrt(n^2 pi^2 hbar^2 c^2 / L^2 + m^2 c^4) with wave number k_n = n pi / L, while the Dirac energy uses the same formula but with k_n satisfying tan(k_n L) = - hbar k_n / (m c).
* For an electron, the Compton wavelength lambda_C = hbar/(m c) is approximately 3.86 x 10^{-13} m (3.86 x 10^{-3} Angstrom); for a charged pion pi^pm, lambda_C is approximately 1.41 fm (1.41 x 10^{-5} Angstrom).
* For a three-dimensional cubic box of side L, the Klein-Gordon energy is E_B(n) = sqrt( |k(n)|^2 hbar^2 c^2 + m^2 c^4 ) with k_i(n) = n_i pi / L, while the Dirac energy uses the same formula but with k_i(n) satisfying tan(k_i L_i) = -2(E_B + m c^2) hbar k_i c / (hbar^2 c^2 k_i^2 - (E_B + m c^2)^2).
* For box sizes much larger than the Compton wavelength (L_C = L/lambda_C >> 1), both Klein-Gordon and Dirac spectra approach the non-relativistic result, with the Dirac transcendental equation yielding x_n ~ n pi / L_C.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous solutions for the Dirac equation in a one-dimensional box (Alberto, Fiolhais, and Gil) and a three-dimensional box (Alberto, Das, and Vagenas) to spin-0 particles via the Klein-Gordon equation. It contrasts with textbook treatments such as Greiner, which only consider finite square wells for relativistic particles, and addresses the Klein paradox issue that prevents simply taking the well height to infinity. The results enable comparison of relativistic density of states for fermion and boson gases, and provide pedagogical material for teaching relativistic quantum mechanics, particularly the role of spin and boundary conditions in relativistic energy spectra.
