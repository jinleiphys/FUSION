# Freezeout properties of different light nuclei at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan

> We study the transverse momentum spectra of light nuclei (deuteron, anti-deuteron and triton) produced in Gold-Gold (Au-Au) collisions in different centrality bins by the blast wave model with Tsallis statistics. The model results are in agreement with the experimental data measured by STAR Collaboration in special transverse momentum ranges. We extracted the kinetic freezeout temperature, transverse flow velocity and kinetic freezeout volume. It is observed that kinetic freezeout temperature and transverse flow velocity increases initially, and then saturates from 14.5-39 GeV, while the kinetic freezeout volume increase initially up to 19.6 GeV but saturates from 19.6-39 GeV. This may indicate that the phase transition starts in part volume that ends in the whole volume at 39 GeV and the critical point may exists somewhere in the energy range of 14.5-39 GeV. The present work observed that the kinetic freezeout temperature, transverse flow velocity and kinetic freezeout volume has a decreasing trend from central to peripheral collisions. We found the freezeout volume of triton is smaller than those of deuteron and anti-deuteron, which shows that triton freezeout earlier than that of deuteron and anti-deuteron.

## Key claim

The kinetic freezeout temperature and transverse flow velocity extracted from light nuclei spectra in Au-Au collisions increase initially with collision energy and then saturate in the range 14.5–39 GeV, which the authors interpret as a possible indication that the phase transition from hadronic matter to QGP starts in part of the volume at 14.5 GeV and ends in the whole volume at 39 GeV, with the critical point possibly existing somewhere in that energy range; however, the authors explicitly note that this interpretation is "loose" and that the quality of some fits is insufficient to make a strong conclusion.

## Method

The authors fitted the transverse momentum spectra of deuteron, anti-deuteron, and triton measured by the STAR Collaboration in Au-Au collisions at eight beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) across multiple centrality bins using the blast-wave model with Tsallis statistics (TBW). The model replaces the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics with a Tsallis distribution characterized by an entropy index q, and simultaneously extracts the kinetic freezeout temperature T0, transverse flow velocity βT, and kinetic freezeout volume V from the fits.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron in 0–10% central Au-Au collisions, T0 increases from 0.112 ± 0.006 GeV at 7.7 GeV to 0.160 ± 0.006 GeV at 200 GeV; βT increases from 0.352 ± 0.009 c to 0.430 ± 0.009 c over the same energy range.
- For deuteron in 0–10% central collisions, the kinetic freezeout volume V increases from 3400 ± 165 fm³ at 7.7 GeV to 6800 ± 180 fm³ at 200 GeV.
- For triton in 0–10% central collisions, T0 increases from 0.125 ± 0.005 GeV at 7.7 GeV to 0.166 ± 0.006 GeV at 200 GeV; βT increases from 0.319 ± 0.010 c to 0.400 ± 0.012 c.
- For triton in 0–10% central collisions, V increases from 2600 ± 155 fm³ at 7.7 GeV to 5750 ± 180 fm³ at 200 GeV.
- The freezeout volume of triton is systematically smaller than that of deuteron and anti-deuteron at the same energy and centrality; for example at 7.7 GeV 0–10% central, triton V = 2600 ± 155 fm³ versus deuteron V = 3400 ± 165 fm³.
- T0 and βT saturate in the range 14.5–39 GeV for all species; for deuteron 0–10% central, T0 = 0.137–0.138 GeV and βT = 0.380–0.381 c across 14.5–39 GeV.
- All extracted parameters show a decreasing trend from central to peripheral collisions; for deuteron at 7.7 GeV, T0 drops from 0.112 ± 0.006 GeV (0–10%) to 0.096 ± 0.006 GeV (60–80%).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kinetic freezeout properties in heavy-ion collisions by applying the blast-wave model with Tsallis statistics specifically to light nuclei (deuteron, anti-deuteron, triton) across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. The observed saturation of T0 and βT in the 14.5–39 GeV range is discussed in relation to the QCD phase diagram and the search for the critical point, with the authors noting that their interpretation of a phase transition onset from these data is tentative and requires further investigation and comparison with other findings such as those from lattice QCD calculations and other phenomenological models. The centrality dependence results are consistent with several prior studies (e.g., those reporting decreasing T0 and βT from central to peripheral collisions) but disagree with others that found an increasing trend of T0 with decreasing centrality.
