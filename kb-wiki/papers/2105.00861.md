# Vector and Axial-Vector Mesons in Nuclear Matter

> As a first step towards a realistic phenomenological description of vector and axial-vector mesons in nuclear matter, we calculate the spectral functions of the $ρ$ and the $a_1$ meson in a chiral baryon-meson model as a low-energy effective realization of QCD, taking into account the effects of fluctuations from scalar mesons, nucleons, and vector mesons within the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach. The phase diagram of the effective hadronic theory exhibits a nuclear liquid-gas phase transition as well as a chiral phase transition at a higher baryon-chemical potential. The in-medium $ρ$ and $a_1$ spectral functions are calculated by using the previously introduced analytically-continued FRG (aFRG) method. Our results show strong modifications of the spectral functions in particular near the critical endpoints of both phase transitions which may well be of relevance for electromagnetic rates in heavy-ion collisions or neutrino emissivities in neutron-star merger events.

## Key claim

As a first step towards a realistic phenomenological description, the authors calculate spectral functions of the ρ and a₁ meson in nuclear matter using a chiral baryon-meson model with fluctuations from scalar mesons, nucleons, and vector mesons within the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) approach; the results show strong modifications of the spectral functions near the critical endpoints of both the nuclear liquid-gas and chiral phase transitions, which may be relevant for electromagnetic rates in heavy-ion collisions or neutrino emissivities in neutron-star merger events.

## Method

The authors use the parity-doublet model (PDM) extended with vector and axial-vector mesons, describing nucleons and their parity partners (N(938) and N*(1535)) with a chirally invariant bare nucleon mass m₀,ₙ. They solve the Wetterich equation for the effective average action within the local potential approximation (LPA), using three-dimensional regulator functions to allow analytic continuation via the analytically-continued FRG (aFRG) method. The flow equation for the effective potential is solved numerically using a grid method, and the in-medium ρ and a₁ spectral functions are calculated from the analytically-continued two-point functions.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition critical endpoint is located at baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 896 MeV and temperature T ≈ 10 MeV.
* The chiral phase transition critical endpoint is located at μ_B ≈ 925 MeV and T ≈ 33 MeV.
* The UV cutoff is set to Λ = 1000 MeV and the IR scale to k_IR = 40 MeV.
* The bare nucleon mass is m₀,ₙ = 800 MeV, chosen as a compromise to obtain phenomenologically reasonable values.
* The resulting vacuum masses are: pion mass m_π = 137 MeV, sigma mass m_σ = 474 MeV, nucleon mass m_N₁ = 938 MeV, and parity partner mass m_N₂ = 1533 MeV, with pion decay constant f_π = 92.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the parity-doublet model by Weyrich (2015) which confirmed the existence of two distinct first-order phase transitions in nuclear matter, and builds on the aFRG framework developed by Kamikado (2013) and Tripolt (2013, 2014) for calculating spectral functions. The inclusion of fluctuating vector and axial-vector mesons follows the formalism of Jung (2019) and allows, for example, the three-body resonance decay a₁ → ρπ → 3π to be accounted for. This enables future calculations of electromagnetic spectral functions and thermal dilepton rates in heavy-ion collisions, as well as neutrino emissivities in proto-neutron stars and binary neutron-star mergers.
