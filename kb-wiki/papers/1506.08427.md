# High $Q^2$ behavior of the electromagnetic form factors in the relativistic hypercentral CQM

> The ratio $R_p$ between the electric and magnetic proton form factors has been recently measured at JLab up to $Q^2=8.5 ~GeV^2$. We have extended the calculation of the nucleon form factors with the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model and compared them with the data on $R_p$ and on the $Q^2$ behavior of the ratio $Q^2 F_2/F_1$. In both cases the theoretical curves agree with the experimental points.

# High $Q^2$ behavior of the electromagnetic form factors in the relativistic hypercentral CQM

## Key claim
The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), when extended to high momentum transfer without any additional parameter fitting, reproduces the recent JLab data on the proton form factor ratio $R_p$ up to $Q^2 = 8.5$ GeV$^2$, showing a continued decrease with no indication of a zero crossing at finite $Q^2$, contrary to several earlier model predictions.

## Method
The authors extended the relativistic hCQM calculation of nucleon form factors from Santopinto et al. (2007) to higher $Q^2$ values up to 12 GeV$^2$, using the same formalism with boosted initial and final nucleon states to the Breit Frame and relativistic corrections to the non-relativistic current. Intrinsic quark form factors were included to account for dynamical aspects beyond the three-valence quark approach. The theoretical curves were compared with experimental data on $R_p = \mu_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)$ and the ratio $Q^2 F_2/F_1$.

## Key numbers
- The ratio $R_p$ was measured at JLab up to $Q^2 = 8.5$ GeV$^2$ (Puckett, 2010), with large error bars for the highest points.
- The theoretical calculation was extended up to $Q^2 = 12$ GeV$^2$ without any further parameter fitting.
- The Skyrme soliton model predicted a zero of the electric form factor at $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ (Holzwarth, 1996), later revised to $Q^2 \approx 16$ GeV$^2$ (Holzwarth, 1999).
- The light cone constituent quark model of Frank et al. (1995) predicted a zero at $Q^2 = 6$ GeV$^2$.
- The Rome group's Bethe-Salpeter/VMD approach predicted a zero at about $9$ GeV$^2$.
- The Iachello et al. VMD fit predicted a zero at $Q^2 = 8$ GeV$^2$.
- The Kelly fit predicted a zero at about $Q^2 \approx 10$ GeV$^2$.
- The Lomon fit predicted a zero between $13$ and $14$ GeV$^2$.
- The quark core size from the hCQM is about $0.5$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic hCQM calculation of Santopinto et al. (2007) to higher $Q^2$, comparing with new JLab polarization data from Puckett (2010) that extend to $Q^2 = 8.5$ GeV$^2$. The results contradict several earlier model predictions of a zero in the electric form factor at finite $Q^2$, including those from the Skyrme soliton model (Holzwarth, 1996), the light cone constituent quark model (Frank et al., 1995), and various VMD fits (Iachello et al., Kelly, Lomon). The agreement with data required intrinsic quark form factors, pointing to the necessity of going beyond the three-valence quark approach. The authors suggest that future experiments at the upgraded 12 GeV Jefferson Lab may clarify whether unquenching the CQM to include higher Fock components can generate a dip in the electric form factor through interference between valence and sea contributions.
