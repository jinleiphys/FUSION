# Probing anharmonic properties of nuclear surface vibration by heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Describing fusion reactions between ^{16}O and ^{154}Dy and, between ^{16}O and ^{144}Sm by the $sd-$ and $sdf-$ interacting boson model, we show that heavy-ion fusion reactions are strongly affected by anharmonic properties of nuclear surface vibrations and nuclear shape, and thus provide a powerful method to study details of nuclear structure and dynamics.

# Probing anharmonic properties of nuclear surface vibration by heavy-ion fusion reactions

## Key claim
Heavy-ion fusion reactions, particularly the fusion barrier distribution, are strongly affected by anharmonic properties of nuclear surface vibrations (especially reorientation effects from non-zero quadrupole moments of excited states), and can therefore serve as a probe of nuclear shape and structure. The puzzle of why double-phonon coupling spoils agreement for $^{16}$O + $^{144}$Sm but improves it for $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb can be resolved by including anharmonicities in the transition operators rather than in the energy spectrum.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for $^{16}$O + $^{154}$Dy and $^{16}$O + $^{144}$Sm fusion reactions using the interacting boson model (IBM), the $sd$-IBM for quadrupole vibrations and the $sdf$-IBM for including octupole vibrations. Anharmonicity was introduced via the $\chi$ parameters in the quadrupole and octupole operators, which generate non-zero static quadrupole moments in excited states, while the coupling form factors included both nuclear and Coulomb parts with full-order nuclear coupling for the $^{144}$Sm case.

## Key numbers
- For $^{154}$Dy: boson number $N=4$, quadrupole deformation $\beta=0.24$, $\chi=-2.36$, $d$-boson excitation energy $\epsilon_d=0.335$ MeV.
- For $^{144}$Sm: adopted boson number $N=2$, $\epsilon_d=1.66$ MeV, $\epsilon_f=1.81$ MeV; nuclear potential $V_0=105.1$ MeV, $R_0=8.54$ fm, $a=0.75$ fm, target radius $R_T=5.56$ fm.
- Optimal parameters from $\chi^2$ fit for $^{144}$Sm: $\beta_2=0.13$, $\beta_3=0.23$, $\chi_2=-3.30$, $\chi_{2f}=-2.48$, $\chi_3=2.87$.
- Extracted static quadrupole moments for $^{144}$Sm: first $2^+$ state = $-0.28$ b, first $3^-$ state = $-0.70$ b (using effective charge $e_B=0.16$ eb from experimental $B(E2;0\to 2^+_1)$).
- Harmonic-limit deformation parameters from electric transitions: $\beta_2=0.11$, $\beta_3=0.21$.

## Context
This work extends the study of heavy-ion fusion barrier distributions as a probe of nuclear structure, following earlier demonstrations that the sign of hexadecupole deformation can be determined from fusion barrier distributions (Leigh et al. 1993, Lemmon et al. 1995) and that double-phonon excitations play an important role in $^{58}$Ni + $^{60}$Ni fusion (Stefanini et al. 1995). The paper directly addresses a puzzle raised by Leigh, Dasgupta, and Hinde regarding the $^{16}$O + $^{144}$Sm system: single-phonon coupling gave good agreement with data (M. 1995), but adding double-phonon coupling in the harmonic limit destroyed that agreement, contrary to the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb case (D. 1997). The resolution shown here, that anharmonicity in the transition operators (not the energy spectrum) restores agreement, enables future determination of quadrupole moments and signs of deformation in spherical nuclei through subbarrier fusion reactions.
