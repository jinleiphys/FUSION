# Nuclear deformation and the two neutrino double-βdecay in ^{124,126}Xe,^{128,130}Te, ^{130,132}Ba and ^{150}Nd isotopes

> The two neutrino double beta decay of $^{124,126}$Xe,$^{128,130}$Te, $ ^{130,132}$Ba and $^{150}$Nd isotopes is studied in the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model. Theoretical 2$ν$ $β^{-}β^{-}$ half-lives of $^{128,130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd isotopes, and 2$νβ^{+}β^{+}$, 2$ν$ $β^{+}EC$ and 2$ν$ $ECEC$ for $ ^{124,126}$Xe and $^{130,132}$Ba nuclei are presented. Calculated quadrupolar transition probabilities B(E2: $0^+\to 2^+$), static quadrupole moments and $g$ factors in the parent and daughter nuclei reproduce the experimental information, validating the reliability of the model wave functions. The anticorrelation between nuclear deformation and the nuclear transition matrix element $M_{2ν} $ is confirmed.

# Nuclear deformation and the two neutrino double-β decay in 124,126Xe, 128,130Te, 130,132Ba and 150Nd isotopes

## Key claim

The anticorrelation between nuclear deformation and the nuclear transition matrix element M2ν for two-neutrino double-beta decay is confirmed across multiple isotopes in the A~130 and A~150 mass regions, using Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) wave functions that reproduce experimental electromagnetic properties. However, the model explicitly excludes spin-isospin interactions known to control the size of 2ν ββ decay matrix elements in QRPA calculations, and the summation method used relies on the assumption that the Gamow-Teller operator commutes with the effective two-body interaction.

## Method

The authors employ the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (PPQQ) effective two-body interaction to generate wave functions for parent and daughter nuclei, then restore rotational symmetry via angular momentum projection. The nuclear transition matrix elements M2ν for 0+ → 0+ transitions are calculated using the summation method, which avoids explicit summation over intermediate odd-odd nuclear states by assuming the Gamow-Teller operator commutes with the two-body interaction. The strength of the proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole interaction χpn is fixed by reproducing the experimental excitation energy of the first 2+ state in each nucleus, and all other parameters are kept fixed throughout the calculation.

## Key numbers

- For 128Te 2ν β−β− decay: predicted half-life T1/2 = (1.05-2.55) × 10^24 y for gA = (1.25 - 1.0), using phase space factor G2ν = 8.475 × 10^−22 y^−1
- For 130Te 2ν β−β− decay: predicted half-life T1/2 = (1.16-2.82) × 10^20 y for gA = (1.25 - 1.0), using phase space factor G2ν = 4.808 × 10^−18 y^−1
- For 150Nd 2ν β−β− decay: predicted half-life T1/2 = (7.89-19.27) × 10^18 y for gA = (1.25 - 1.0), using phase space factor G2ν = 1.189 × 10^−16 y^−1
- Pairing interaction strengths fixed as Gp = Gn = 35/A MeV
- Like-particle quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strengths: χpp = χnn = 0.0105 MeV b^−4
- Single particle energies used (in MeV): ε(1d5/2)=0.0, ε(2s1/2)=1.4, ε(1d3/2)=2.0, ε(0g7/2)=4.0, ε(0h11/2)=6.5 (4.8 for 150Nd and 150Sm)
- Calculated B(E2: 0+ → 2+) transition probabilities agree with experiment for all cases except 126Xe (differing by 0.015 e^2b^2) and 150Sm (differing by 0.466 e^2b^2)

## Context

This work extends the PHFB formalism previously applied to 2ν β−β− decay in the A~100 mass region by Chandra et al. (2005) and to 2ν e+DBD modes by Raina et al. (2006), now covering the A~130 and A~150 mass regions. The study confirms the anticorrelation between quadrupole deformation and the nuclear transition matrix element M2ν that was earlier demonstrated by Auerbach et al. (1993) and Troltenier et al. (1996), and provides a systematic comparison with results from QRPA (Engel et al., 1988; Staudt et al., 1990), pseudo-SU(3) (Castanos et al., 1994; Hirsch et al., 1995), SU(4)στ (Rumyantsev et al., 1998), and shell model (Caurier et al., 1999) calculations. The results enable future extensions of the PHFB model to include spin-isospin two-body terms and describe odd-odd intermediate nuclei, which would allow evaluation of ββ decay matrix elements without the summation method.
