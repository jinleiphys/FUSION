# Folding model approach to the elastic $p+^{12,13}$C scattering at low energies and radiative capture $^{12,13}$C$(p,γ)$ reactions

> The proton radiative capture $^{12,13}$C$(p,γ)$ reactions at astrophysical energies, key processes in the CNO cycle, are revisited in the potential model with the proton-nucleus potential for both the scattering and bound states obtained in the folding model, using a realistic density dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction. For the consistency, this same folding model is also used to calculate the optical potential of the elastic $p+^{12,13}$C scattering at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The folded $p+^{12,13}$C optical potentials are shown to account well for both the elastic $p+^{12,13}$C3 scattering and astrophysical $S$ factors of the radiative capture $^{12,13}$C$(p,γ)$ reactions.

## Key claim

The folding model using a density-dependent CDM3Y3 nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the rearrangement term included, can simultaneously describe both elastic p+12,13C scattering at low energies and the astrophysical S factors of the radiative capture 12,13C(p,γ) reactions, provided the folded potential is renormalized by factors of 1.20–1.33 to account for higher-order contributions beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The authors calculated the proton-nucleus optical potential using the folding model with the CDM3Y3 density-dependent interaction derived from the M3Y-Paris force, including the rearrangement term from the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem. This folded potential was used in the optical model analysis of elastic p+12C scattering at 2.39, 2.97, and 4.61 MeV and p+13C scattering at 1.548, 1.917, and 2.378 MeV, with a renormalization factor Ns adjusted to fit the data. The same folded potential, with separate renormalization factors for scattering (Ns) and bound (Nb) states, was then applied in the potential model to compute the astrophysical S factors for the 12C(p,γ)13N and 13C(p,γ)14N reactions, with spectroscopic factors SF adjusted to reproduce the measured resonance peaks.

## Key numbers

- For elastic p+12C scattering, the best-fit renormalization factor Ns = 1.32 at Ep = 2.39 MeV and Ns = 1.33 at Ep = 2.97 and 4.61 MeV.
- For elastic p+13C scattering, the best-fit renormalization factor Ns = 1.23, 1.21, and 1.25 at Ep = 1.55, 1.92, and 2.38 MeV, respectively.
- The 12C(p,γ)13N reaction has a 1/2+ resonance peak at ER ≈ 0.422 MeV, reproduced with Ns ≈ 1.317 and spectroscopic factor SF ≈ 0.40 for the resonance component.
- The calculated reduced transition probability B(E1;1/2+ → 1/2-) ≈ 20.31 e2 fm2 at the resonance peak, compared to the experimental value of 22.77 ± 0.25 e2 fm2 from Endt (1993).
- The final state ANC for 12C(p,γ)13N is AF2 ≈ 2.061 fm-1, compared to 2.045 fm-1 from the proton transfer reaction analysis by Yarmukhamedov (1997).
- The bound state of 13N required Nb ≈ 1.005 to reproduce the binding energy Eb ≈ 1.943 MeV of the valence 1p1/2 proton.

## Context

This work extends the folding model approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering by Khoa (2002) and Loan (2015, 2020) to the astrophysical context of radiative capture reactions in the CNO cycle. It provides a microscopic alternative to the phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential model used by Huang et al. (2010) and the NACRE II compilation, and to the R-matrix method used by Burjan (2008) and Mukhamedzhanov (2003). The results enable predictions of reaction rates for proton-induced reactions on unstable nuclei where phenomenological potentials are unavailable, and the renormalization factors quantify the importance of higher-order contributions beyond the mean-field approximation that need to be addressed in future folding model studies.
