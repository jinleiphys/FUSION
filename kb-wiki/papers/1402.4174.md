# Chiral Langevin theory for non-Abelian plasmas

> Charged plasmas with chirality imbalance are unstable and tend to reduce the imbalance. This chiral plasma instability is, however, not captured in (anomalous) hydrodynamics for high-temperature non-Abelian plasmas. We derive a Langevin-type classical effective theory with anomalous parity-violating effects for non-Abelian plasmas that describes the chiral plasma instability at the magnetic scale. We show that the time scale of the instability is of order $[g^4 T \ln(1/g)]^{-1}$ at weak coupling.

## Key claim

The authors derive a Langevin-type classical effective theory for non-Abelian plasmas with chirality imbalance that captures the chiral plasma instability at the magnetic scale (∼ g²T), which is missed by anomalous hydrodynamics because the instability occurs at length scales shorter than the mean free path ∼ (g⁴T)⁻¹.

## Method

The authors split gauge fields into soft modes (k ∼ g²T) and (semi)hard modes (k ≫ g²T), then integrate out the (semi)hard degrees of freedom from the chiral kinetic theory with Berry curvature corrections. The resulting effective theory is a Langevin equation for the soft gauge field, Eq. (chiral_Langevin), supplemented by an evolution equation for the chiral charge density n₅ via the axial anomaly relation, Eq. (eq:n_5). The noise term satisfies the fluctuation-dissipation theorem with the same color conductivity σc as in the parity-invariant case.

## Key numbers

* The time scale of the chiral plasma instability is of order [g⁴ T ln(1/g)]⁻¹ at weak coupling.
* The magnetic scale momentum is k ∼ g²T, with gauge field amplitude A ∼ gT and magnetic field B ∼ g³T².
* The color conductivity is σc = m_D²/(3γ) ∼ T/ln(1/g), where γ = Nc αg T ln(1/g) is the damping rate for hard thermal bosons.
* The anomalous conductivity is σanom = Nf g² μ₅/(4π²).
* The mean free path is l_mfp ∼ (g⁴T)⁻¹ (up to logarithmic corrections).
* The Debye screening mass is ∼ gT.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-type effective theory for soft gauge fields previously derived by Bodeker (1998) and Arnold, Son, and Yaffe (1998) to include anomalous parity-violating effects from a chirality imbalance. It addresses the chiral plasma instability identified in Akamatsu (2013) using chiral kinetic theory, which occurs at the magnetic scale where hydrodynamics (including the anomalous hydrodynamics of Son and Surowka (2009)) is inapplicable. The resulting framework enables numerical simulations of non-Abelian chiral plasma dynamics that are simpler than full chiral kinetic theory, and it incorporates both dissipation and thermal fluctuations systematically, going beyond earlier static treatments of Chern-Simons waves by Rubakov (1985, 1986).
