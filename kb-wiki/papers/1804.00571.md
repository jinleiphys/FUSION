# Event patterns from negative pion spectra in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS

> Rapidity-dependent transverse momentum spectra of negatively charged pions measured at different rapidities in proton-proton collisions at the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) at various energies within its Beam Energy Scan (BES) program are investigated by using one- and two-component standard distributions where the chemical potential and spin property of particles are implemented. The rapidity spectra are described by a double-Gaussian distribution. At the stage of kinetic freeze-out, the event patterns are structured by the scatter plots in the three-dimensional subspaces of velocity, momentum and rapidity. The results of the studies of the rapidity-independent transverse mass spectra measured at mid-rapidity in proton-proton collisions are compared with those based on the similar transverse mass spectra measured in the most central beryllium-beryllium, argon-scandium and lead-lead collisions from the SPS at its BES energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using rapidity-dependent (rather than rapidity-independent) transverse momentum spectra to construct event patterns yields "fine" scatter plots that are more accurate than previous non-fine patterns, though the qualitative shapes (spherical in velocity space, cylindrical in momentum space) remain unchanged for negatively charged pions produced in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS Beam Energy Scan energies.

## Method

The authors fit rapidity-dependent double-differential transverse momentum spectra of negatively charged pions measured by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration in proton-proton collisions at five beam momenta (20 to 158 GeV/c) using one- or two-component standard (Bose-Einstein) distributions that incorporate chemical potential and spin. Rapidity spectra are described by a double-Gaussian distribution. Using the fitted parameters, Monte Carlo sampling generates three-dimensional scatter plots in velocity, momentum, and rapidity subspaces to construct event patterns at kinetic freeze-out. For comparison, the same procedure is applied to rapidity-independent transverse mass spectra measured at mid-rapidity in proton-proton, central beryllium-beryllium, argon-scandium, and lead-lead collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Chen, Liu, Sarkisyan-Grinbaum, 2017) that used rapidity-independent transverse mass spectra to construct event patterns, which the current paper terms "non-fine" patterns. By using rapidity-dependent spectra from the NA61/SHINE Collaboration (2014) and NA49 Collaboration (2002, 2003), the authors produce "fine" event patterns that account for the variation of transverse momentum spectra with rapidity. The results enable more accurate modeling of the kinetic freeze-out stage in small (pp) and large (Pb-Pb) collision systems at SPS energies, and can be extended to other particle species and collision energies.
