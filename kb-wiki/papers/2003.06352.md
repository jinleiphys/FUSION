# Momentum Anisotropy of Leptons from Two Photon Processes in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the azimuthal angular correlation between the lepton transverse momentum $P_\perp$ and the impact parameter $b_\perp$ in non-central heavy-ion collisions, where the leptons are produced through two-photon scattering. Among the Fourier harmonic coefficients, a significant $v_4$ asymmetry is found for the typical kinematics at RHIC and LHC with a mild dependence on the $P_\perp$, whereas $v_2$ is power suppressed by the lepton mass over $P_\perp$. This unique prediction, if confirmed from the experiments, shall provide crucial information on the production mechanism for the dilepton in two-photon processes.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a significant $v_4$ azimuthal asymmetry for leptons produced via two-photon scattering in non-central heavy-ion collisions, while $v_2$ is power-suppressed by the lepton mass over $P_\perp$; this prediction is conditional on experimental confirmation and applies specifically when the total transverse momentum of the lepton pair is integrated up to about 100 MeV, where the electromagnetic contribution dominates over hadronic contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the impact-parameter-dependent photon flux for relativistic heavy nuclei using classical electrodynamics (Jackson method) and the nuclear charge form factor from the STARlight MC generator. They then derive the differential cross section for the Breit-Wheeler process $\gamma\gamma \to \ell^+\ell^-$ at lowest-order QED, integrating over the individual impact parameters $b_{1\perp}$ and $b_{2\perp}$ with the constraint $\vec{b}_\perp = \vec{b}_{1\perp} - \vec{b}_{2\perp}$ to obtain the azimuthal angular correlation between the lepton transverse momentum $\vec{P}_\perp$ and the collision impact parameter $\vec{b}_\perp$. The helicity-flip photon generalized parton distribution (GPD) $h_\gamma(x,b_{1\perp})$ is shown to equal the unpolarized photon GPD $f_\gamma(x,b_{1\perp})$ for coherent photons, leading to a $\cos(4\phi)$ modulation in the cross section.

## Key numbers

- The mean total transverse momentum of the lepton pair $q_\perp$ reaches about 100 MeV in the most central collisions at the LHC, compared to about 40 MeV for ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) (from ATLAS measurement, cited in text).
- The maximal value of the asymmetry $-2\langle \cos(4\phi) \rangle$ ranges from 26% to 34% depending on center-of-mass energy, lepton transverse momentum region, and collision species (Au-Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV).
- The asymmetry increases with impact parameter $b_\perp$ until it reaches a maximum when $b_\perp$ is slightly larger than $2R_A$, where $R_A = 1.1 A^{1/3}$ fm (with $a=0.7$ fm for the form factor).
- The asymmetries show mild dependence on lepton transverse momentum $P_\perp$ at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dilepton production in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) (Baur 2003, Baltz 2009, Klein 2018) to peripheral and central collisions, where the total transverse momentum of the lepton pair is small (below about 100 MeV). It differs from earlier studies by Zha (2018), Li (2019), and others that focused on azimuthal asymmetries depending on the total transverse momentum $q_\perp$; here the anisotropy is defined through the lepton momentum $P_\perp$ with respect to the impact parameter $b_\perp$. The predicted $v_4$ asymmetry, if confirmed experimentally, would help identify the production mechanism of lepton pairs at low total transverse momentum and could enable the use of lepton pairs to probe the electromagnetic properties of the quark-gluon plasma, in contrast to hadronic flow which arises from collective modes.
