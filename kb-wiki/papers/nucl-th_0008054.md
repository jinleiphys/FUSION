# Supersymmetric Transformations in Coupled-Channel Systems

> A transformation of supersymmetric quantum mechanics for N coupled channels is presented, which allows the introduction of up to N degenerate bound states without altering the remaining spectrum of the Hamiltonian. Phase equivalence of the Hamiltonian can be restored by two successive supersymmetric transformations at the same energy. The method is successfully applied to the 3S1-3D1 coupled channels of the nucleon-nucleon system and a set of Moscow-type potentials is thus generated.

# Supersymmetric Transformations in Coupled-Channel Systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a closed-form supersymmetric transformation that can introduce up to N degenerate bound states into an N-channel coupled system without altering the remaining spectrum, but the actual number of bound states added depends on the rank of the constant matrix Λ and on the singularity structure of the potential at the origin, with phase equivalence requiring two successive transformations at the same energy.

## Method
The authors construct a superpotential W(r) as an N×N matrix satisfying a Riccati-type differential equation, using a general solution of the Schrödinger equation at the transformation energy and a constant symmetric matrix Λ. For adding bound states, they choose the regular solution of the initial Hamiltonian with integration bound a=∞, and require Λ to be negative semidefinite to avoid singularities. Phase-equivalent potentials are obtained by performing two successive SUSY transformations at the same energy: first using asymptotically vanishing solutions, then using the transformation of Eq. (4) with the regular solution of the intermediate Hamiltonian.

- In the ℓ₁=0, ℓ₂=2 modified Gaussian system, the initial potential has a bound state at E=-29.45 MeV, and an additional bound state is introduced at E=200 MeV.
- The Reid soft core potential (RSC) for the ³S₁-³D₁ channel is used as starting point to generate Moscow-type potentials with an added bound state.

## Context
This work extends the SUSY transformation formalism from uncoupled channels (Baye 1987, Ancarani 1992) to coupled-channel systems, completing the approach of Sparenberg and Baye (1997) who had addressed removal of bound states and phase-equivalence but not the introduction of bound states. The method enables generation of phase-equivalent Moscow-type potentials from standard nucleon-nucleon interactions like the Reid soft core potential (Reid 1968), which is relevant for understanding Pauli-forbidden states in composite particle systems as discussed by Neudatchin et al. (1973) and Leeb et al. (1994). The transformation allows systematic construction of families of phase-equivalent coupled-channel potentials with controlled bound-state spectra, applicable to inverse scattering problems and effective interactions in nuclear physics.
