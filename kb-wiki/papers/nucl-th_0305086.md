# A new Fermi smearing approach for scattering of multi-GeV electrons by nuclei

> The cross section for electron scattering by nuclei at high momentum transfers is calculated within the Fermi smearing approximation (FSA), where binding effects on the struck nucleon are introduced via the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The model naturally preserves current conservation, since the response tensor for an off-shell nucleon conserves the same form that for a free one but with an effective mass. Different parameterizations for the inelastic nucleon structure function, are analyzed. The smearing at the Fermi surface is introduced through a momentum distribution obtained from a perturbative nuclear matter calculation. Recent CEBAF data on inclusive scattering of 4.05 GeV electrons on $^{56}$Fe are well reproduced for all measured geometries for the first time, as is evident from the comparison with previous calculations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a new Fermi smearing approach, using the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) to introduce binding effects and final-state interactions (FSI) via an effective nucleon mass, reproduces CEBAF inclusive electron scattering data on ⁵⁶Fe for all measured geometries for the first time, avoiding the factor 2–10 overestimation at low energy loss (x > 1) present in previous calculations such as those of Rinat and Taragin (2000).

## Method
The nuclear response tensor is calculated within a Fermi smearing approximation (FSA) where the struck nucleon is treated as off-shell but with a free-nucleon form for the response tensor, using an effective mass M* from the RHA to preserve current conservation. The nucleon momentum distribution n(p) is obtained from a perturbative nuclear matter calculation including 0p0h, 2p2h, and 4p4h configurations (second- and fourth-order corrections). The inelastic nucleon structure functions are taken from parameterizations by Bodek et al. and Whitlow, with the off-shell prescription w_off-shell(Q²,ν) = w_on-shell(Q²,ν*) where ν* = p*·q/M*.

## Key numbers
- Incident electron energy: 4.05 GeV on ⁵⁶Fe target.
- Momentum transfer range covered: 1 < Q² < 7 (GeV/c)².
- Bjorken x range covered: 0.2 ≲ x ≲ 4.2.
- Effective mass in RHA: M* = 0.74 M (compared to M* = 0.648 M in MFT).
- Fermi momentum for ⁵⁶Fe: p_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹.
- For the longitudinal response at q = 0.55 GeV: ω_qe = 0.182 GeV (Δω_qe = 0.302 GeV); at q = 1.14 GeV: ω_qe = 0.615 GeV (Δω_qe = 0.436 GeV).
- Scattering angles measured: θ = 15°, 23°, 30°, 37°, 45°, 55°, 74°.

## Context
This work extends the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) by incorporating binding effects and FSI via the RHA effective mass, improving upon earlier PWIA calculations by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991, 1994, 1996) and Benhar et al. (1991) that used optical potentials or Glauber theory. It directly addresses the overestimation of cross sections at x > 1 found by Rinat and Taragin (2000) in their relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith series approach. The model enables a consistent treatment of both elastic and inelastic nucleon response at multi-GeV energies while preserving gauge invariance, and suggests that further improvements could come from momentum-dependent effective masses or higher-order contributions to the momentum distribution.
