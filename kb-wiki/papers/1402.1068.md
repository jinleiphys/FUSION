# Towards an improved description of SiDIS by a polarized 3He target

> The possibility of improving the description of the semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off polarized 3He, that provides information on the neutron single spin asymmetries, is illustrated. In particular, the analysis at finite momentum transfers in a Poincare' covariant framework is outlined and a generalized eikonal approach to include final state interaction is presented.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a path to improve the extraction of neutron single-spin asymmetries from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off a polarized 3He target by incorporating both a Poincaré covariant description of nuclear dynamics and a generalized eikonal treatment of final-state interactions, but the actual numerical results shown are preliminary and the full combined framework is deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors extend a previous plane-wave impulse approximation analysis of SiDIS off polarized 3He (Scopetta et al.) by adopting a Light-Front Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics framework with Bakamjian-Thomas construction for Poincaré covariance, and by incorporating final-state interactions via the generalized eikonal approach. The GEA treats the propagation of the debris after photon absorption and subsequent hadronization through an eikonal S-matrix with an effective cross section that includes both nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon contributions. The spin-dependent spectral function of 3He is transformed from instant-form to light-front form using Melosh rotations, and the GEA overlaps are embedded into this covariant framework.

## Key numbers

* Effective neutron polarization in polarized 3He: p_n = 0.878; effective proton polarization: p_p = -0.023 (PWIA values)
* With FSI included, effective neutron polarization becomes p_n = 0.756 and proton polarization p_p = -0.027, representing about a 15-20% depolarization effect
* Average effective cross section used in GEA: ⟨σ_eff⟩ = 71 mb
* For beam energy 8.8 GeV and x_Bj = 0.21, the neutron dilution factor f_n changes from 0.304 (PWIA) to 0.353 (with FSI), while the product p_n × f_n remains nearly unchanged (0.266 vs 0.267)
* FSI effects become sizable for missing momentum |p_mis| ≥ 1 fm^{-1} in perpendicular kinematics

## Context

This work extends the earlier PWIA analysis of SiDIS off polarized 3He by Scopetta et al. for extracting neutron single-spin asymmetries, and builds upon prior applications of the generalized eikonal approach to unpolarized SiDIS by Cosyn et al. and by the same authors. The approach enables future extraction of neutron SSAs from 3He SSAs at JLab12 kinematics, with the encouraging finding that while FSI modifies individual dilution factors and polarizations by 15-20%, their product remains stable, suggesting the extraction procedure can be safely applied.
