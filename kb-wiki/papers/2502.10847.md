# Conformality Thresholds in Neutron Stars

> The dense-matter equation of state (EOS) under neutron-star conditions is investigated. We focus on the qualitative behavior of the speed of sound and its average. We characterize and compare the theoretical criteria for identifying nearly conformal matter. We find that the consistency of these criteria in determining the onset of conformal matter is dictated by the combined state-of-the-art theoretical and astrophysical constraints.

## Key claim
The consistency of different theoretical criteria for identifying nearly conformal matter in neutron stars is dictated by the combined state-of-the-art theoretical and astrophysical constraints, rather than any single criterion being universally applicable.

## Method
The authors investigated the dense-matter equation of state under neutron-star conditions by focusing on the qualitative behavior of the speed of sound and its average. They characterized and compared multiple theoretical criteria for identifying the onset of nearly conformal matter, evaluating how these criteria perform when subjected to combined theoretical and astrophysical constraints.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing investigation into the behavior of dense matter in neutron stars, particularly the transition to conformal behavior at high densities. It builds upon prior studies of the speed of sound in dense matter and the criteria for conformality, such as those discussed by Cherman et al. and other authors working on the equation of state of dense nuclear matter. The findings enable a more nuanced understanding of when and how matter in neutron stars becomes nearly conformal, which is relevant for interpreting neutron-star observations and constraining theoretical models.
