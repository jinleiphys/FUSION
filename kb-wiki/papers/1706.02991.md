# Waves in magnetized quark matter

> We study wave propagation in a non-relativistic cold quark-gluon plasma immersed in a constant magnetic field. Starting from the Euler equation we derive linear wave equations and investigate their stability and causality. We use a generic form for the equation of state, the EOS derived from the MIT bag model and also a variant of the this model which includes gluon degrees of freedom. The results of this analysis may be relevant for perturbations propagating through the quark matter phase in the core of compact stars and also for perturbations propagating in the low temperature quark-gluon plasma formed in low energy heavy ion collisions, to be carried out at FAIR and NICA.

# Waves in magnetized quark matter

## Key claim
Wave propagation in a non-relativistic cold quark-gluon plasma under a constant magnetic field is stable and causal for a generic equation of state, but for the MIT bag model and its improved variant (mQCD), causality may be violated for waves propagating along the direction of a very strong magnetic field, where the phase velocity can exceed the speed of light.

## Method
The authors start from the non-relativistic Euler equation with an external magnetic field, using a multifluid approach with separate equations for up, down, and strange quarks. They linearize the Euler and continuity equations around an equilibrium configuration, assume plane-wave perturbations, and derive the dispersion relation from the determinant of the resulting 4x4 matrix. The analysis is performed for three equations of state: a generic non-relativistic EOS with anisotropic sound speeds, the MIT bag model EOS, and an improved MIT bag model (mQCD) that includes gluonic degrees of freedom and non-perturbative effects.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses used: m_u = 2.2 MeV, m_d = 4.7 MeV, m_s = 96 MeV (from PDG values)
- Electron charge in natural units: Q_e = 0.08542
- Relevant strong magnetic fields are of order 10^19 G, corresponding to B Q_e ~ m_pi^2 ~ 0.02 GeV^2, with m_pi ~ 140 MeV and 1 GeV^2 = 1.44 x 10^19 G
- For very strong B field with the generic EOS, phase velocity v_p ~ (B Q_e)/(m_f |k|) ~ (m_pi^2)/(m_f |k|), and v_p < 1 when |k| > 1000 MeV
- Ordinary nuclear matter density: rho_N = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For the MIT bag model in the strong-field limit (n_max^f = 0), the effective sound speed squared is (V_s)^2 = (tilde{Q} mu)/(|Q_f| m_f) - (|Q_f| m_f)/(2 tilde{Q} mu), where tilde{Q} = sum |Q_f| over flavors

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of wave propagation in cold dense matter (Fogaca, Navarra, 2013) by adding the effects of a constant magnetic field. It also builds on multifluid approaches used in plasma physics (Azam, 2000; multifluid formalism) and on studies of magnetized quark matter equations of state (MIT bag model with magnetic field; mQCD model from Fogaca, Navarra, 2011, 2016). The results are relevant for perturbations in quark matter in compact star cores and in low-temperature quark-gluon plasma to be produced at FAIR and NICA facilities. The analysis identifies regimes where the non-relativistic hydrodynamic formalism breaks down (phase velocity exceeding light speed), pointing to the need for a relativistic treatment for waves along very strong magnetic fields.
