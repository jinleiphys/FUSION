# Collective flow in single-hit QCD kinetic theory

> Motivated by recent interest in collectivity in small systems, we calculate the harmonic flow response to initial geometry deformations within weakly coupled QCD kinetic theory using the first correction to the free-streaming background. We derive a parametric scaling formula that relates harmonic flow in systems of different sizes and different generic initial gluon distributions. We comment on similarities and differences between the full QCD effective kinetic theory and the toy models used previously. Finally we calculate the centrality dependence of the integrated elliptic flow $v_2$ in oxygen-oxygen, proton-lead and proton-proton collision systems.

# Collective flow in single-hit QCD kinetic theory

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the linear response of elliptic flow to initial geometry deformations in weakly coupled QCD kinetic theory can be described by a parametric scaling formula that depends on only two scaling variables, even though the full QCD effective kinetic theory has a more complex collision kernel than simplified models. The authors note that their results are not fully leading-order accurate because they push the effective kinetic theory beyond its strict applicability regime, representing instead a model calculation based on current best understanding of perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors solve the QCD effective kinetic theory Boltzmann equation in the single-hit approximation, treating the first correction to the free-streaming background as a perturbation linear in the number of scatterings. They compute the transverse-energy-weighted elliptic flow response by linearizing the elastic 2↔2 collision kernel around a symmetric background, using Monte-Carlo sampling of multidimensional phase-space integrals on a discrete time-radius grid. The initial conditions are taken from a Color-Glass-Condensate-inspired gluon distribution and a deformed thermal distribution, with small azimuthal perturbations of the form δf ∝ x⊥² cos(2φx) to generate elliptic flow.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collectivity in small systems using simplified kinetic theory models, particularly the isotropization time approximation (ITA) studied by Kurkela (2018-2020) and others (Heiselberg 1998, Kolb 2000, Borghini 2010, Romatschke 2018). The ITA approach found that flow response depends only on a single opacity parameter γ̂, while the full QCD effective kinetic theory of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2002) introduces additional dependence on occupancy and coupling through Bose enhancement and Debye screening. The paper enables systematic comparison between QCD kinetic theory and experimental data for oxygen-oxygen, proton-lead, and proton-proton collisions, providing a framework to study how hydrodynamization arises as a function of system size.
