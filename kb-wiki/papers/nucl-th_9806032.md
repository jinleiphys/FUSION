# \bar{p}p low energy parameters from annihilation cross section data

> The recent experimental data obtained by the OBELIX group on total $\bar{p}p$ annihilation cross section are analysed; the low energy spin averaged parameters of the $\bar{p}p$ scattering amplitude (the imaginary parts of the S-wave scattering length and P-wave scattering volume) are extracted from the data. Their values are found to be equal to $Im a_{sc} = - 0.69 \pm 0.01 (stat) \pm 0.03 (sys) fm, Im A_{sc} = - 0.76 \pm 0.05 (stat) \pm 0.04 (sys) fm^3$. The results are in very good agreement with existing atomic data.

# \bar{p}p low energy parameters from annihilation cross section data

## Key claim
The imaginary parts of the S-wave scattering length and P-wave scattering volume for the \bar{p}p system can be extracted from annihilation cross section data alone, but only as spin-averaged effective values rather than true statistical averages of singlet and triplet contributions, and the extraction requires accounting for Coulomb effects which modify the near-threshold behaviour from 1/v to 1/v^2.

## Method
The authors fitted the theoretical expression for the total \bar{p}p annihilation cross section, including both S-wave and P-wave contributions with Coulomb corrections, to OBELIX experimental data for antiproton momenta between 47 and 106 MeV/c. They fixed the real parts of the scattering amplitudes to 0.85 fm based on atomic data, leaving only the imaginary parts of the S-wave scattering length and P-wave scattering volume as free parameters, and performed the fit using the MINUIT program.

## Key numbers
- Im a_sc = -0.69 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.03 (sys) fm (imaginary part of S-wave scattering length)
- Im A_sc = -0.76 ± 0.05 (stat) ± 0.04 (sys) fm^3 (imaginary part of P-wave scattering volume)
- χ^2 = 0.2 per point for the fit including Coulomb effects
- χ^2 = 0.9 per point for a fit using the wrong formula without Coulomb interaction, which gave Im a_sc = -1.13 ± 0.01 fm
- The difference between cross sections with and without Coulomb interaction is approximately 100 mb at 60 MeV/c antiproton laboratory momentum

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Carbonell and Protasov (1993) by applying their Coulomb-corrected scattering length formalism to the first experimental data from the OBELIX collaboration at LEAR, which measured \bar{p}p annihilation cross sections down to 44 MeV/c. The results agree well with values extracted from atomic data on protonium (Batty 1990s, Carbonell and Protasov 1992), providing an independent and complementary determination. The analysis enables future experiments at the Antiproton Decelerator to improve precision, particularly for the P-wave scattering volume, and suggests that measurements of annihilation channels forbidden in S-wave (such as ηη or π^0π^0) could reveal the non-vanishing P-wave contribution at low energies due to Coulomb effects.
