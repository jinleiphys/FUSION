# Newly observed D_{sJ}(3040) and the radial excitations of P-wave charmed-strange mesons

> Inspired by the newly observed $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ state, in this work we systemically study the two-body strong decays of P-wave charmed-strange mesons with the first radial excitation. Under the assignment of $1^{+}(j^P=1/2^+)$, i.e. the first radial excitation of $D_{s1}(2460)^+$, we find that the width of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ is close to the lower limit of the BaBar measurement. This indicates that it is reasonable to interpret $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ as the first radial excitation of $D_{s1}(2460)^+$. Our calculation further predicts that $0^-+1^-$ channels e.g. $D^+K^{*0}$, $D^0 K^{*+}$ and $D_s^+ϕ$ are important for the search for $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$. To help future experiments finding the remaining three P-wave charmed-strange mesons with the first radial excitation, we present the predictions for the strong decays of these three P-wave charmed-strange mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the newly observed $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ state can be reasonably interpreted as the first radial excitation of $D_{s1}(2460)^+$ (the $1^+(j^P=1/2^+)$ state), but only under the condition that the calculated total decay width (approximately 204 MeV) reaches the lower limit of the BaBar measurement when the harmonic oscillator parameter $R_A$ is taken as 2.8 GeV$^{-1}$.

## Method

The authors used the $^3P_0$ (Quark Pair Creation) model to calculate the two-body strong decay widths of P-wave charmed-strange mesons with the first radial excitation. They employed simple harmonic oscillator wave functions for the meson spatial wavefunctions and took the mixing angle between the $2^1P_1$ and $2^3P_1$ basis states as $\theta' = -54.7^\circ$, the same as in the heavy quark limit for the ground-state P-wave mesons. The strength of quark pair creation from the vacuum was fixed at $\gamma = 6.3$, and the $R$ parameter in the harmonic oscillator wave function was chosen to reproduce the root mean square radius from solving the Schrodinger equation with a linear potential.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$: $M = 3044 \pm 8(\text{stat})^{+30}_{-5}(\text{syst})$ MeV (from BaBar experiment)
- Width of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$: $\Gamma = 239 \pm 35(\text{stat})^{+46}_{-42}(\text{syst})$ MeV (from BaBar experiment)
- Calculated total decay width of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ under $1^+(S^\star)$ assignment: approximately 204 MeV for $R_A = 2.8$ GeV$^{-1}$, which reaches the lower limit of the experimental width
- Ratio of partial widths for $1^+(S^\star)$: $\Gamma(1^+(S^\star) \to 0^-+1^-) / \Gamma(1^+(S^\star) \to 1^-+0^-) \sim 0.79$ for $R_A = 2.8$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Predicted mass of $0^+(S^\star)$ charmed-strange meson: 2.837 GeV (from Matsuki, Morii and Sudoh (2006))
- Calculated total decay width of $0^+(S^\star)$ charmed-strange meson: 108 MeV for $R_A = 2.8$ GeV$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the study of charmed-strange meson spectroscopy following the BaBar Collaboration's (2009) observation of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$ in the $D^*K$ invariant mass spectrum. The authors build upon the heavy quark limit classification of mesons into doublets (as used by Matsuki, Morii and Sudoh (2006) who predicted a $2^3P_1$ $c\bar{s}$ state mass of 3082 MeV) and apply the $^3P_0$ model, which was previously used by Close (2005), Zhang (2006), and Luo (2009) for related charmed-strange decays. The results enable future experimental searches for the remaining three radial excitations of P-wave charmed-strange states ($0^+(S^\star)$, $1^+(T^\star)$, and $2^+(T^\star)$) and suggest that $0^-+1^-$ channels such as $D^+K^{*0}$, $D^0 K^{*+}$, and $D_s^+\phi$ are important for further confirmation of $D_{sJ}(3040)^+$.
