# Novel observables for exploring QCD collective evolution and quantum entanglement within individual jets

> We postulate that non-perturbative QCD effects occurring during parton fragmentation can result in collective effects of a multi-parton system, reminiscent of those observed in high-energy hadronic or nuclear interactions with large final-state particle multiplicity. Proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider showed surprising signatures of a strongly interacting, thermalized quark-gluon plasma, which was thought only to form in collisions of large nuclear systems. Another puzzle observed earlier in $e^{+}e^{-}$ collisions is that production yields of various hadron species appear to follow a thermal-like distribution with a common temperature. We propose searches for thermal and collective properties resulting from parton fragmentation processes using high multiplicity jets in high-energy elementary collisions. Several novel observables are studied using the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator. Experimental observation of such collectivity will offer a new view of non-perturbative QCD dynamics of multi-parton systems at the smallest scales. Absence of any collective effects may offer new insights into the role of quantum entanglement in the observed thermal behavior of particle production in high energy collisions.

## Key claim

The authors postulate that non-perturbative QCD effects during parton fragmentation can produce collective, QGP-like behavior within individual high-multiplicity jets, but they explicitly hedge that this is a speculative alternative picture and that the absence of such signatures would instead highlight the role of quantum entanglement in thermal-like hadron production.

## Method

The authors propose a new analysis framework where particles inside a high-transverse-momentum jet are redefined in a jet-centric coordinate system (with the z-axis along the jet momentum), and then standard heavy-ion observables are applied to these particles as a function of the charged multiplicity within the jet. They demonstrate the feasibility of this approach using the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator for proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, studying particle multiplicity distributions, pseudorapidity densities, transverse momentum spectra relative to the jet axis, strange hadron yield ratios, and two-particle angular correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Jets with transverse momentum above 500 GeV and 800 GeV have most likely charged multiplicities around 25, with tails reaching up to 140 charged particles per jet (PYTHIA 8, pp at 13 TeV).
- For high-multiplicity jets (Nch^j > 100), the charged particle density dNch/deta* reaches values of nearly 70, comparable to the multiplicity regime where collective effects have been observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions.
- The PYTHIA 8 sample corresponds to an integrated luminosity of approximately 50 fb^-1, filtered to select events with minimum invariant transverse momentum (hat-pT) of 470 GeV.
- Two-particle correlation functions are studied for particles with 0.3 < jT < 3 GeV, using a pseudorapidity gap of |Delta eta*| > 2 to isolate long-range correlations.

## Context

This work extends the puzzle of thermal-like hadron production observed in e+e- collisions (Becattini, 1995; Castorina, 2007) and the discovery of long-range collective correlations in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC (Khachatryan, 2010) to the smallest possible system: a single jet. It contrasts with conventional jet substructure approaches that trim away soft particles (Asquith, 2018) and with null results from searches for ridge correlations in e+e- collisions (Badea, 2019) and e-p collisions (ZEUS, 2019). The proposed observables could enable a unified view of non-perturbative many-body QCD processes from e+e- to AA collisions, or alternatively provide evidence for quantum entanglement explanations of thermal behavior (Kharzeev, 2017; Berges, 2017).
