# Spin symmetry in Dirac negative energy spectrum in density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> The spin symmetry in the Dirac negative energy spectrum and its origin are investigated for the first time within the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory. Taking the nucleus $^{16}$O as an example, the spin symmetry in the negative energy spectrum is found to be a good approximation and the dominant components of the Dirac wave functions for the spin doublets are nearly identical. In comparison with the relativistic Hartree approximation where the origin of spin symmetry lies in the equality of the scalar and vector potentials, in DDRHF the cancellation between the Hartree and Fock terms is responsible for the better spin symmetry properties and determines the subtle spin-orbit splitting. These conclusions hold even in the case when significant deviations from the G-parity values of the meson-antinucleon couplings occur.

## Key claim

The spin symmetry in the Dirac negative energy spectrum (single anti-nucleon spectrum) is found to be a good approximation within density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory for the nucleus 16O, but the origin differs from the relativistic Hartree approximation: in DDRHF, cancellation between the Hartree and Fock terms is responsible for the better spin symmetry properties and determines the subtle spin-orbit splitting, rather than the equality of scalar and vector potentials as in RMF.

## Method

The authors solved the DDRHF equations self-consistently in coordinate space using the parameter set PKO1 for the nucleus 16O, obtaining the single-particle energies and wave functions for bound states in the Dirac sea. They then analyzed the effective potentials in the Schrodinger-type equation for the dominant component F(r), separating contributions from the centrifugal barrier, spin-orbit potential, and the Hartree versus Fock terms. The contributions from different channels to the single-particle energies and spin-orbit splittings were quantified by integrating the relevant potential terms weighted by the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For the 0p spin doublet in 16O: the single-particle energy of ν0p1/2 is -342.43 MeV and ν0p3/2 is -342.37 MeV, giving a spin-orbit splitting ΔE = 0.06 MeV
- For the 3p spin doublet in 16O: the single-particle energy of ν3p1/2 is -799.91 MeV and ν3p3/2 is -799.84 MeV, giving a spin-orbit splitting ΔE = 0.07 MeV
- The Fock contribution to the spin-orbit potential (V_SOP^E) for the 0p doublet is 4.53 MeV, while the Hartree contribution (V_SOP^D) is 0.79 MeV, showing Fock terms are dominant
- The Fock contribution to the spin-orbit potential (V_SOP^E) for the 3p doublet is 1.10 MeV, while the Hartree contribution (V_SOP^D) is 0.36 MeV
- When meson-antinucleon couplings were reduced to 0.3 times the standard values (g_iN̄N̄ = g_iN̄N = g_iNN̄ = 0.3 g_iNN), the spin symmetry remained well conserved

## Context

This work extends the investigation of spin symmetry in the Dirac negative energy spectrum from the relativistic Hartree approximation (Zhou 2003, He 2006) to the more complete density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory, which includes Fock terms and one-pion exchange. The DDRHF theory had previously succeeded in quantitative description of ground-state properties (Long 2006) and improved descriptions of nucleon effective mass and shell evolution (Long 2007, Long 2008). The findings enable future studies of relativistic symmetries with non-local potentials and suggest that spin symmetry in the anti-nucleon spectrum persists even when strong annihilation channels cause significant deviations from G-parity values of meson-antinucleon couplings.
