# Properties of chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N$^3$LO with high cutoffs studied by local projection

> The chiral nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction at high cutoffs has been plagued by the presence of spurious bound states. In this work, the chiral $NN$ interaction at N$^3$LO is studied by the local projection method as the cutoff increases. The evolution of short-range behaviors of pion-exchange interactions and contact interactions is intuitively demonstrated. The $P$-channel potentials toward high cutoffs appear to be erratic at short ranges to compromise with phase shifts, while such erratic behaviors can be avoided in $S$ and $D$ channels. Furthermore, a chiral $NN$ interaction at N$^3$LO is studied at a cutoff of 700 MeV. The properties of deuteron and triton are testified with this interaction. Such a hard interaction is expected to provide an alternative choice for studies of short-range correlations and high density nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon interactions at high cutoffs develop spurious deep bound states in $P$-waves (first appearing around 760-850 MeV cutoff), but these can be avoided in $S$ and $D$ channels up to 1 GeV, and a usable hard interaction at 700 MeV cutoff can be constructed by fitting phase shifts up to 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors apply the local projection method to the chiral N$^3$LO potential in momentum space, which integrates out non-localities to produce a coordinate-space representation that preserves the main features of the interaction. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain phase shifts and fit contact-term parameters to the GWU SP07 and Nijmegen PWA93 $np$ phase-shift analyses using a genetic algorithm, with cutoff $\Lambda$ varied from 500 to 1000 MeV to study the emergence of spurious bound states.

## Key numbers

- First spurious bound state in $^3P_0$ channel appears at cutoff $\Lambda \approx 760$ MeV; in $^1P_1$ at $\Lambda \approx 800$ MeV; in $^3P_1$ at $\Lambda \approx 850$ MeV
- At $\Lambda = 850$ MeV for $^3P_0$, the first eigenvalue before local projection is -44964 MeV and after local projection is -41180 MeV; at $\Lambda = 820$ MeV, eigenvalues are -40431 MeV and -37531 MeV respectively
- For the fitted 700 MeV cutoff interaction, the $pp$ scattering length with Coulomb force $a_{pp}^C = -7.82$ fm (empirical: -7.8196(26) fm); $np$ scattering length $a_{np} = -23.83$ fm (empirical: -23.740(20) fm); triplet scattering length $a_t = 5.424$ fm (empirical: 5.419(7) fm)
- The $^3P_0$ channel fit with $\Lambda = 700$ MeV and $T_{\rm lab}^{\rm cut} = 500$ MeV yields $\chi^2$/datum = 2.4

## Context

This work extends studies of spurious bound states in chiral interactions that were previously examined at LO by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and at N$^2$LO by Long and Yang (2012), now providing the first comprehensive analysis at N$^3$LO. The local projection method was previously applied to similarity renormalization group evolution by Wendt, Furnstahl, and Perry (2011). The resulting hard interaction at 700 MeV cutoff is intended as an alternative to soft chiral potentials like EMN500 from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) for studies of short-range correlations in electron scattering and high-density nuclear matter relevant to neutron star observations.
