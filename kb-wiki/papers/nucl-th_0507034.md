# Study of isomers in neutron-rich magic $^{132}$Sn region

> Shell model calculations have been done for interpreting two representative isomeric states in neutron rich $^{132}Sn$ region using SMN and SMPN Hamiltonians. They are, (i) 2.91 min isomer in $^{138}Cs$ and (ii) the 0.57 $μs$ isomer in $^{136}$Sb nuclei. The results are compared with those obtained with KH5082 and CW5082 Hamiltonians. How the results clearly distinguish the most appropriate interaction has been discussed.

## Key claim
Shell model calculations show that only the SMPN Hamiltonian, which differs from SMN by only four proton-proton two-body matrix elements, correctly reproduces the 2.91 min isomeric state in $^{138}$Cs, while the SMN, KH5082, and CW5082 Hamiltonians fail to predict the long-lived isomer.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code with a valence space consisting of proton orbitals $\pi(1g_{7/2}, 2d_{5/2}, 2d_{3/2}, 3s_{1/2}, 1h_{11/2})$ and neutron orbitals $\nu(1h_{9/2}, 2f_{7/2}, 2f_{5/2}, 3p_{3/2}, 3p_{1/2}, 1i_{13/2})$ above a $^{132}$Sn inert core. Four Hamiltonians were compared: KH5082 (scaled from the Pb-region), CW5082 (modified by Chou and Warburton), SMN (further modified with new A=134 data), and SMPN (SMN with four proton-proton tbmes adjusted to reproduce $^{134}$Te binding energy and low-lying levels). Half-lives of isomeric states were calculated from the resulting wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- $^{138}$Cs: measured isomeric state half-life is 2.91 min; SMPN calculation gives approximately 12 min for the $6^- \rightarrow 3^-$ M3 transition (using bare g-factors)
- $^{136}$Sb: measured isomeric state half-life is $0.565 \pm 0.050$ $\mu$s; deduced B(E2) = $4.11^{+0.40}_{-0.33}$ W.u. for the $6^-$ to $4^-$ transition with 47 keV transition energy
- $^{136}$Sb: effective charges derived to reproduce the experimental half-life are proton effective charge = 1.00 and neutron effective charge = $1.00^{+0.06}_{-0.05}$ (in units of e)
- $^{134}$Te: low-lying excited levels at 1279 keV ($2^+$), 1576 keV ($4^+$), and 1692 keV ($6^+$) used to modify the four proton-proton tbmes

## Context
This work extends shell model calculations in the neutron-rich $^{132}$Sn region, which resembles the $^{208}$Pb region but involves nuclei far from stability relevant to astrophysical r-process models. The study compares four Hamiltonians (KH5082, CW5082, SMN, SMPN) and shows that the choice of proton-proton two-body matrix elements sensitively affects predictions of isomeric half-lives. The results contradict the ground state spin assignment of $^{136}$Sb by Hoff et al., who argued for $1^-$ based on beta decay systematics, and instead favor a $2^-$ ground state. The work enables future experimental investigation of the $^{136}$Sb ground state spin and comparison of shell model predictions with beta-delayed neutron emission half-lives.
