# Ab initio many-body calculations of nucleon-nucleus scattering

> We develop a new ab initio many-body approach capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei, by combining the resonating-group method with the use of realistic interactions, and a microscopic and consistent description of the nucleon clusters. This approach preserves translational symmetry and Pauli principle. We outline technical details and present phase shift results for neutron scattering on 3H, 4He and 10Be and proton scattering on 3He and 4He, using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. Our A=4 scattering results are compared to earlier ab initio calculations. We find that the CD-Bonn NN potential in particular provides an excellent description of nucleon-4He S-wave phase shifts. On the contrary, the experimental nucleon-4He P-wave phase shifts are not well reproduced by any NN potential we use. We demonstrate that a proper treatment of the coupling to the n-10Be continuum is successful in explaining the parity-inverted ground state in 11Be.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new *ab initio* many-body approach that combines the resonating-group method (RGM) with the no-core shell model (NCSM) to describe both bound and scattering states in light nuclei using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, but the experimental nucleon-4He P-wave phase shifts are not well reproduced by any NN potential used, while the CD-Bonn NN potential provides an excellent description of nucleon-4He S-wave phase shifts.

## Method

The approach couples the RGM, a microscopic cluster technique using fully antisymmetric many-body wave functions with nucleons grouped into clusters, with the NCSM, an *ab initio* method for calculating ground and excited states of light nuclei using a harmonic oscillator basis. The cluster eigenstates are obtained by diagonalizing the intrinsic Hamiltonians in the NCSM model space, and the relative-motion wave functions are determined by solving the many-body Schrodinger equation in the Hilbert space spanned by binary-cluster channel states, with the Hamiltonian and norm kernels calculated using both Jacobi-coordinate and single-particle Slater-determinant basis expansions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron scattering on 3H, 4He and 10Be and proton scattering on 3He and 4He were calculated using realistic NN potentials, with phase shift results presented for A=4, A=5 and A=11 systems.
- The CD-Bonn NN potential provides an excellent description of nucleon-4He S-wave phase shifts, while experimental nucleon-4He P-wave phase shifts are not well reproduced by any NN potential used.
- A proper treatment of the coupling to the n-10Be continuum successfully explains the parity-inverted ground state in 11Be.

## Context

This work extends earlier *ab initio* calculations for A=4 scattering systems and builds upon the NCSM/RGM approach previously introduced by the authors (Quaglioni and Navratil, 2008). The method overcomes limitations of the standard NCSM, which gives an incorrect description of long-range correlations and lacks coupling to the continuum, and enables *ab initio* studies of low-energy nuclear reactions on light nuclei for systems with more than four nucleons, which had been a rare exception in prior work such as the Green's function Monte Carlo calculations for n-4He scattering.
