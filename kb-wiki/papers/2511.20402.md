# New framework for extracting GPDs from exclusive photon electroproduction

> Recently, a new framework for studying generic $2 \to 3$ hard exclusive reactions, referred to as single-diffractive hard exclusive processes, has been introduced to provide a cleaner separation of the underlying physical mechanisms. In this work, we expand this formalism to the case of exclusive real-photon electroproduction off a nucleon, $e(\ell) + N(p) \to e(\ell') + N(p') + γ(q')$, which represents the classical channel for accessing generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in nucleons and nuclei. This extension enables a more systematic and physically transparent formulation of the reaction dynamics, paving the way for improved extractions of GPDs from experimental data as compared to existing approaches.

## Key claim

The paper extends a recently introduced framework for single-diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs) to exclusive real-photon electroproduction off a nucleon, claiming that this new SDHEP frame provides a cleaner and more systematic separation of the Bethe-Heitler (BH) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) contributions than the conventional Breit-frame approach, thereby enabling improved extractions of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from experimental data.

## Method

The authors reformulate the exclusive photon electroproduction process $e(\ell) + N(p) \to e(\ell') + N(p') + \gamma(q')$ using a two-stage picture where the nucleon first diffractively emits a low-virtuality state $A^*$ with momentum $\Delta = p - p'$, which then undergoes a hard $2 \to 2$ scattering with the electron to produce the final-state photon and electron. They perform a leading-order (LO) amplitude calculation for both the BH and DVCS subprocesses within this SDHEP frame, deriving eight polarization asymmetries associated with well-defined azimuthal modulations. The approach is contrasted with the conventional Breit-frame formalism, where the BH contribution induces intricate azimuthal dependence that complicates GPD extraction.

## Key numbers

- The hard scale is set by $Q^2 = -q^2 \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$, where $q = \ell - \ell'$ is the virtual photon momentum from the electron.
- The soft scale is $t = \Delta^2 = (p-p')^2$, with the condition $\ell'_T \sim q'_T \gg \sqrt{-t}$ for the two-stage picture to be a good approximation.
- The BH amplitude contains a factor $1/t$ from the exchanged photon propagator.
- The DVCS amplitude contains a factor $1/Q^2$ from the virtual photon propagator.
- The leading-power interference of BH and DVCS yields eight polarization asymmetries with $\cos\phi$ or $\sin\phi$ modulations, corresponding to the eight real degrees of freedom of the GPD integrals.

## Context

This work extends the SDHEP framework introduced by Qiu, Sato, and Yu (2024) for generic $2 \to 3$ hard exclusive reactions to the specific case of photon electroproduction, which has traditionally been analyzed using the Breit frame following the factorization proofs of Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1996, 1998) and Ji (1998). The new framework enables a coherent description of both BH and DVCS subprocesses centered around the low-virtuality exchange state $A^*$, rather than the high-virtuality virtual photon of DVCS, which should facilitate more model-independent GPD extractions from experimental data at facilities such as Jefferson Lab, HERA, COMPASS, and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
