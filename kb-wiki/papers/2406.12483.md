# Spin-independent interactions of Dirac Fermionic Dark Matter in the composite Higgs models

> According to recent measurements, dark matter magnetic dipole moment is strongly constrained. In the composite Higgs models the magnetic dipole moment of the Dirac dark matter fermion and its mass can be suppressed by the approximate U(1) symmetry. We consider E_6 inspired composite Higgs model (E_6CHM) with U(1) symmetry violating operators, which give rise to dark matter's mass and coupling constant to Higgs boson. The dependence of the spin-independent dark matter-nucleon scattering cross section on the E_6CHM parameters is explored. We argue that there are regions of the parameter space which are still safe from all current constraints and may lead to spectacular LHC signatures.

# Spin-independent interactions of Dirac Fermionic Dark Matter in the composite Higgs models

## Key claim
The E6 inspired composite Higgs model (E6CHM) can suppress the magnetic dipole moment of Dirac dark matter fermions via an approximate U(1) symmetry, and there exist regions of parameter space where the spin-independent dark matter-nucleon scattering cross section remains below current experimental limits, provided the dark matter fermion constitutes only a fraction of the total dark matter density.

## Method
The authors consider the E6CHM with U(1) symmetry violating operators that generate the dark matter fermion mass and its coupling to the Higgs boson. They compute the spin-independent dark matter-nucleon scattering cross section mediated by t-channel exchange of the 125 GeV Higgs scalar, Z-boson, and photon, and compare the results to experimental bounds from direct detection experiments such as LUX-ZEPLIN, XENONnT, and PandaX-4T, accounting for the possibility that the lightest exotic fermion contributes only a fraction of the total dark matter density.

## Key numbers
- The compositeness scale f is required to be larger than 5-6 TeV from electroweak precision measurements in the E6CHM.
- The dark matter density in the Solar system is about 0.4 GeV/cm^3.
- For dark matter particles with masses around 100 GeV, about 10^13 particles cross each cubic meter on Earth per year.
- The thermally averaged annihilation cross section for weakly-interacting massive particles is approximately 2.3 × 10^{-26} cm^3/s.
- The scalar colour triplet T may be lighter than 2 TeV when f ≈ 5-6 TeV.
- If the dark matter fermion mass is below 800 GeV, ATLAS has excluded bottom squark masses below 1200-1270 GeV at 95% confidence level, with comparable limits from CMS.

## Context
This work extends the study of composite Higgs models, particularly the minimal composite Higgs model (MCHM) of Agashe (2004) and the E6CHM framework developed by Nevzorov (2015-2016). The analysis addresses the stringent constraints on dark matter magnetic dipole moments from direct detection experiments such as PICO (2022) and PandaX, which would otherwise require f ≳ 10^4 TeV. The paper enables future searches at the LHC for scalar colour triplets decaying into b-quarks and dark matter fermions, producing signatures with missing transverse energy.
