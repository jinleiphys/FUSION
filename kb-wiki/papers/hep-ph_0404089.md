# New symmetry current for massive spin-3/2 fields

> We present several new results which will be of value to theorists working with massive spin-3/2 vector-spinor fields as found, for example, in low and intermediate energy hadron physics and also linearized supergravity. The general lagrangian and propagator for a vector-spinor field in d-dimensions is given. It is shown that the observables of the theory are invariant under a novel continuous symmetry group which is also extended to an algebra. A new technique is developed for exploring the consequences of the symmetry and a previously unknown conserved vector current and charge are found. The current leads to new interactions involving spin-3/2 particles and may have important experimental consequences.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observables of a massive spin-3/2 Rarita-Schwinger field are invariant under a novel continuous symmetry group (point transformations) that redefines lower-spin auxiliary fields, and that this symmetry implies a previously unknown conserved vector current and charge; however, the classical action is not invariant under the transformation because it is non-unitary, so the symmetry is imposed on equivalence classes of classical actions that lead to the same quantum theory.

## Method

The author writes the most general Lagrangian for a massive vector-spinor field in d dimensions in terms of a complex parameter a, then shows that the parameter dependence can be absorbed into a "point transformation" θ^{μν}(a) = g^{μν} + (a/d) γ^μ γ^ν. The path integral is shown to be invariant under global point transformations because the functional determinant cancels. To apply Noether's method despite the non-unitary nature of the transformation, the author imposes that classical actions related by a circle-shift of the parameter are equivalent, then performs an infinitesimal local point transformation to derive a conserved current J^ρ.

## Key numbers

- The point transformation is defined as θ^{μν}(a) = g^{μν} + (a/d) γ^μ γ^ν, with the condition a ≠ -1 to avoid singularity.
- The group multiplication law is a ∘ b = a + b + ab, and the inverse parameter is ã = -a/(1+a).
- The conserved current is J^ρ = i (1+a) [ψ̄·γ ψ^ρ - ψ̄^ρ γ·ψ], and the conserved charge is Q = i (1+a) ∫ d^{d-1}x [χ₁† χ₂ - χ₂† χ₁], where χ₁ = γ·ψ and χ₂ = γ⁰ ψ⁰.
- The new interaction Lagrangian coupling to a vector field A^μ is L_int = i g (1+a) [ψ̄·γ ψ_μ - ψ̄_μ γ·ψ] A^μ, with coupling constant g.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of the Rarita-Schwinger field (Rarita and Schwinger, 1941) and the massive gravitino action in linearized supergravity (van Nieuwenhuizen, 1981) by providing the most general Lagrangian and propagator in d dimensions. The new symmetry current offers a potential approach to the long-standing inconsistency problems in spin-3/2 interactions identified by Johnson and Sudarshan (1961), Velo and Zwanziger (1969), and Deser and Waldron (2000). The results enable new calculations of higher-loop effects in dimensional regularization for processes involving the Δ(1232) resonance or other spin-3/2 particles, such as pion scattering amplitudes and anomalous magnetic moments.
