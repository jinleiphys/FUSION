# High-spin states with seniority v=4,4,6 in 119-126Sn

> The 119-126Sn nuclei have been produced as fission fragments in two reactions induced by heavy ions: 12C+238U at 90 MeV bombarding energy, 18O+208Pb at 85 MeV. Their level schemes have been built from gamma rays detected using the Euroball array. High-spin states located above the long-lived isomeric states of the even- and odd-A 120-126Sn nuclei have been identified. Moreover isomeric states lying around 4.5 MeV have been established in 120,122,124,126Sn from the delayed coincidences between the fission fragment detector SAPhIR and the Euroball array. The states located above 3-MeV excitation energy are ascribed to several broken pairs of neutrons occupying the nu h11/2 orbit. The maximum value of angular momentum available in such a high-j shell, i.e. for mid-occupation and the breaking of the three neutron pairs, has been identified. This process is observed for the first time in spherical nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper identifies high-spin states in the neutron-rich Sn isotopes 119-126, attributing them to the breaking of up to three neutron pairs in the νh11/2 orbital, and claims that the maximum angular momentum available from this configuration (for mid-occupation and three broken pairs) is observed for the first time in spherical nuclei, with the caveat that the assignments rely on systematic comparisons across isotopes and angular correlation analysis where statistics permit.

## Method

The Sn isotopes were produced as fission fragments in two heavy-ion reactions: 12C+238U at 90 MeV and 18O+208Pb at 85 MeV. Gamma rays were detected using the Euroball array, and new isomeric states around 4.5 MeV were identified via delayed coincidences between the SAPhIR fission-fragment detector and Euroball. Level schemes were built from multi-gated coincidence spectra and three-dimensional cubes analyzed with the Radware package, while spin and parity assignments were made using γ-γ angular correlations at three average relative angles (22°, 46°, and 75°) and by analogy with known level structures of neighboring isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Half-life of the 4890-keV state in 120Sn: T1/2 = 36(4) ns, measured from time distributions of the 1190- and 557-keV transitions.
* Half-life of the 4720-keV state in 122Sn: T1/2 = 146(15) ns, measured from the time distribution of the 1103-keV transition.
* Half-life of the isomeric state in 124Sn: T1/2 = 260(25) ns, measured from time distributions of the 1047- and 620-keV transitions.
* Excitation energy of the 12+ state in 120Sn: 4092.5 keV, fed by the 1190.3-keV transition from the 10+ state at 2902.2 keV.
* Excitation energy of the 12+ state in 122Sn: 3869.1 keV, fed by the 1103.5-keV transition from the 10+ state at 2765.6 keV.
* Excitation energy of the 12+ state in 124Sn: 3703.4 keV, fed by the 1046.8-keV transition from the 10+ state at 2656.6 keV.
* Excitation energy of the 15- state in 120Sn: 4890.1 keV, depopulated by the 241.1-keV transition to the 13- state at 4649.0 keV.
* Excitation energy of the 15- state in 122Sn: 4720.5 keV, depopulated by the 242.1-keV transition to the 13- state at 4478.4 keV.
* Excitation energy of the 15- state in 124Sn: 4551.8 keV, depopulated by the 228.5-keV transition to the 13- state at 4323.2 keV.

## Context

This work extends the known high-spin structures of heavy Sn isotopes (A ≥ 120) beyond the long-lived isomeric states (10+ and 7-) previously identified in deep-inelastic reactions by Broda (1992) and Zhang (2000). It also complements the recent report of a 15- isomeric state in 128Sn by Pietri (2011) from fragmentation of 136Xe. The results enable systematic studies of seniority-4, -5, and -6 configurations in spherical nuclei near the N=82 shell closure, and provide benchmarks for shell-model calculations of neutron excitations in the νh11/2 orbital.
