# Photoproduction of eta-prime-mesons from the Proton

> The presently available data for the reaction $γp{\to}η^\prime{p}$ are analyzed in terms of a model in which the dominant production mechanism is the exchange of the vector mesons $ω$ and $ρ$. To describe the data at photon energies close to the production threshold we introduce a resonance contribution due to the well established $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance. Finally we study the contributions due to nucleon exchange to the $η^\prime$ photoproduction and find, that those contributions can be seen at large angles in the differential cross section.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the available data for $\gamma p \to \eta^\prime p$ can be largely described by vector meson exchange ($\omega$ and $\rho$) in the $t$-channel, with a small additional contribution from the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance near threshold, but the authors hedge that the data are too limited to definitively rule out contributions from other baryon resonances or to extract a reliable value for the $g_{\eta^\prime NN}$ coupling constant.

## Method

The authors constructed a model using effective Lagrangians for vector meson exchange ($\omega$ and $\rho$) in the $t$-channel, with coupling constants taken from the Bonn potential model and form factors at the $V\eta^\prime\gamma$ vertices adjusted to an exponential form with a fitted cutoff parameter. They added a single $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance contribution in the $s$- and $u$-channels to describe data near threshold, and separately evaluated nucleon exchange contributions in the $s$- and $u$-channels to assess sensitivity to the $\eta^\prime NN$ coupling. The model was compared to differential cross section data from the SAPHIR collaboration at ELSA for photon energies $0.9 \le E_\gamma \le 2.6$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The slope of the $t$-dependence of the differential cross section at $\sqrt{s} > 2$ GeV is fitted as $b = 1.8 \pm 0.2$ GeV$^{-2}$, extracted from a parameterization $d\sigma/dt = A \exp(bt)$.
- The exponential form factor cutoff at the $V\eta^\prime\gamma$ vertices is set to $\tilde{\Lambda}_{V\eta^\prime\gamma} = 1.2$ GeV$^{-2}$, adjusted to reproduce the data at $E_\gamma \ge 1.9$ GeV.
- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance coupling to the $\eta^\prime$ meson is taken as $g_{\eta^\prime NR} = 3.4$, with a magnetic coupling $\kappa_R = 2.3$ and a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_R = 1$ GeV.
- The nucleon exchange calculation uses a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_N = 800$ MeV to preserve gauge invariance.
- The reaction threshold is at $\sqrt{s} = 1896$ MeV, corresponding to $t = -0.45$ GeV$^2$ at threshold.
- The vector meson coupling constants are $g_{\rho NN} = 3.9$, $g_{\omega NN} = 10.6$, with tensor-to-vector coupling ratio 6.1 for $\rho$ and zero for $\omega$.
- The radiative decay widths used are $\Gamma_{\rho \to \gamma\eta^\prime} = 60 \pm 5$ keV and $\Gamma_{\omega \to \gamma\eta^\prime} = 6.1 \pm 0.8$ keV, yielding $g_{\rho\eta^\prime\gamma} = 1.36$ and $g_{\omega\eta^\prime\gamma} = 0.4$.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of $\eta^\prime$ photoproduction by the SAPHIR collaboration (SAPHIR1, SAPHIR2) and the ABBHHM and AHHM collaborations, which provided total cross sections and angular spectra. The authors contrast their approach with isobar analyses that invoked two resonances ($S_{11}(1897)$ and $P_{11}(1986)$) and with models by Zhang et al. (2001), Li (1997), Borasoy et al. (2002), and Chiang et al. (2002) that considered multiple baryon resonances. The paper enables future extraction of the $g_{\eta^\prime NN}$ coupling constant from backward-angle measurements at higher photon energies, and motivates precision data at $2 < E_\gamma < 5$ GeV to better constrain $t$-channel exchange mechanisms and possible Reggeization.
