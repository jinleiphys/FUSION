# Strange and nonstrange baryon spectra in the relativistic interacting quark-diquark model with a Gürsey and Radicati-inspired exchange interaction

> The relativistic interacting quark-diquark model, constructed in the framework of point form dynamics, is extended to strange baryons. The strange and nonstrange baryon spectra are calculated and compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The relativistic interacting quark-diquark model, previously applied only to nonstrange baryons, is extended to strange baryons by replacing the earlier spin-isospin exchange interaction with a more general Gürsey-Radicati-inspired exchange interaction that includes a flavor-dependent term; the model reproduces the strange and nonstrange baryon spectra up to 2 GeV with a root-mean-square deviation of 119 MeV for a combined fit and 72 MeV for a fit focused on the strange sector, but the introduction of additional diquark types in the strange sector leads to 19 predicted missing resonances below 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic quark-diquark mass operator in point-form dynamics, containing a direct interaction (Coulomb-like with cutoff plus linear confinement) and an exchange interaction with spin, flavor, and isospin terms inspired by Gürsey and Radicati (1964). The mass operator is diagonalized numerically using a variational basis of 100 harmonic oscillator shells, without perturbative approximations, to compute baryon masses for nonstrange (N, Delta) and strange (Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega) resonances. Two parameter fits are performed: Fit 1 to both nonstrange and strange baryon data, and Fit 2 to the strange sector only.

## Key numbers

*   Root-mean-square deviation of 119 MeV for Fit 1 (combined nonstrange and strange baryons).
*   Root-mean-square deviation of 72 MeV for Fit 2 (strange baryons only).
*   Calculated nucleon mass (N(939) P11): 939 MeV (Fit 1), matching the experimental value of 939 MeV.
*   Calculated Delta(1232) P33 mass: 1247 MeV (Fit 1), compared to experimental range 1230-1234 MeV.
*   Calculated Lambda(1116) P01 mass: 1128 MeV (Fit 1), compared to experimental 1116 MeV.
*   Calculated Sigma(1193) P11 mass: 1134 MeV (Fit 1), compared to experimental range 1189-1197 MeV.
*   Calculated Xi(1318) P11 mass: 1343 MeV (Fit 1), compared to experimental range 1315-1322 MeV.
*   Diquark mass differences from Fit 2: M_{[n,n]} = 607 MeV, M_{[n,s]} = 856 MeV, M_{\{n,n\}} = 963 MeV, M_{\{n,s\}} = 1216 MeV, M_{\{s,s\}} = 1352 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic interacting quark-diquark model of Santopinto (2004) and Ferretti (2011) from nonstrange to strange baryons, using a Gürsey-Radicati-inspired exchange interaction instead of the earlier spin-isospin form. It addresses the long-standing problem of missing resonances in three-quark constituent quark models (e.g., Isgur 1979, Capstick 1986, Glozman-Riska, Loring 2001, Giannini 2001) by introducing the diquark as an effective degree of freedom, which reduces the state space. The model successfully reproduces the mass of the Lambda(1405) resonance, a known difficulty for three-quark models, and predicts 19 missing strange baryon resonances below 2 GeV that could be searched for at JLab, BES, ELSA, and Crystal Barrel/TAPS. The approach can be extended to charmed and bottomed baryons in future work.
