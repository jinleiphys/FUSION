# Effective theories of scattering with an attractive inverse-square potential and the three-body problem

> A distorted-wave version of the renormalisation group is applied to scattering by an inverse-square potential and to three-body systems. In attractive three-body systems, the short-distance wave function satisfies a Schroedinger equation with an attractive inverse-square potential, as shown by Efimov. The resulting oscillatory behaviour controls the renormalisation of the three-body interactions, with the renormalisation-group flow tending to a limit cycle as the cut-off is lowered. The approach used here leads to single-valued potentials with discontinuities as the bound states are cut off. The perturbations around the cycle start with a marginal term whose effect is simply to change the phase of the short-distance oscillations, or the self-adjoint extension of the singular Hamiltonian. The full power counting in terms of the energy and two-body scattering length is constructed for short-range three-body forces.

## Key claim

The distorted-wave renormalisation group (DWRG) analysis of the attractive inverse-square potential (ISP) that arises in three-body systems shows that the leading three-body interaction is marginal, with its renormalisation-group flow tending to a limit cycle as the cut-off is lowered, and that the full power counting in energy and two-body scattering length can be constructed for short-range three-body forces, confirming the power counting found by Bedaque et al. (2003) but in a more transparent way due to the clean separation of short- and long-range physics.

## Method

The authors apply a distorted-wave version of the renormalisation group (DWRG) to scattering by an attractive inverse-square potential and to three-body systems. They impose a cut-off on the distorted waves of the pairwise forces (rather than free waves) to separate short- and long-range physics, then derive an RG equation for the three-body potential by demanding that the scattering amplitude be independent of the cut-off. The analysis focuses on the lowest hyperangular-momentum channel (s0 ≈ 1.006) for three s-wave bosons, where the Hamiltonian becomes separable in hyperspherical coordinates and the short-distance wave functions exhibit logarithmic oscillatory behaviour.

## Key numbers

- The magnitude of the smallest hyperangular momentum is s0 ≈ 1.006, which determines the strength of the attractive inverse-square potential in the three-boson system.
- Bound-state energies form a geometric tower with spacing E_n = -(p_*^2/M) exp(2nπ/s0), where p_* is the scale introduced by the self-adjoint extension.
- The system is invariant under the transformation p_* → p_* exp(nπ/s0) for integer n, meaning all such values define the same self-adjoint extension.
- The leading (marginal) perturbation around the limit cycle is energy-independent; each additional power of energy (p̂^2) gives a term two orders higher in the cut-off Λ.

## Context

This work extends the distorted-wave RG method previously applied by Barford and Birse (2003) to Coulomb and repulsive inverse-square potentials, now applied to the attractive ISP relevant to three-body systems. It confirms the power-counting scheme for three-body forces found by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (2003) using the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation, but derives it within a full RG framework that exploits Efimov's (1970) separation of the three-body wave function. The approach enables a transparent derivation of the power counting and directly relates coefficients in the three-body potential to observables through a distorted-wave effective-range expansion.
