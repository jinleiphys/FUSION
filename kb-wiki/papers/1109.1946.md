# Finite-size and Particle-number Effects in an Ultracold Fermi Gas at Unitarity

> We investigate an ultracold Fermi gas at unitarity confined in a periodic box $V=L^3$ using renormalization group (RG) techniques. Within this approach we can quantitatively assess the long range bosonic order parameter fluctuations which dominate finite-size effects. We determine the finite-size and particle-number dependence of universal quantities, such as the Bertsch parameter and the fermion gap. Moreover, we analyze how these universal observables respond to the variation of an external pairing source. Our results indicate that the Bertsch parameter saturates rather quickly to its value in the thermodynamic limit as a function of increasing box size. On the other hand, we observe that the fermion gap shows a significantly stronger dependence on the box size, in particular for small values of the pairing source. Our results may contribute to a better understanding of finite-size and particle-number effects present in Monte-Carlo simulations of ultracold Fermi gases.

## Key claim
The Bertsch parameter saturates relatively quickly to its thermodynamic-limit value as box size increases, whereas the fermion gap shows significantly stronger dependence on box size, especially for small values of the external pairing source.

## Method
The authors employ a functional renormalization group (FRG) approach to study a spin-balanced two-component Fermi gas at unitarity confined in a periodic box of volume V = L³ at zero temperature. They include an external pairing source J to control U(1) symmetry breaking in the finite volume, and analyze both mean-field approximations (dropping order-parameter fluctuations) and beyond-mean-field calculations that incorporate bosonic Nambu-Goldstone mode fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- Bertsch parameter in the mean-field approximation in the thermodynamic limit: ξ ≈ 0.60 (for J → 0)
- Fermion gap in the mean-field approximation in the thermodynamic limit: Δ/ε_F ≈ 0.63 (for J → 0)
- The relative shift of the Bertsch parameter δξ as a function of density shift δn_J follows δξ = -(2/3)(δn_J) + (5/9)(δn_J)² + O((δn_J)³) in the thermodynamic limit

## Context
This work extends functional renormalization group techniques previously applied to finite-volume effects in QCD low-energy models (Braun 2004, Braun 2005, Braun 2010) to the unitary Fermi gas problem. The study aims to improve comparability between analytical approaches and lattice Monte-Carlo simulations (Wingate 2006, Goulko 2009, Forbes 2010, Lee 2010) which are performed in finite volumes, and may help quantify finite-size errors in Quantum Monte-Carlo studies of Tan relations (Drut 2010).
