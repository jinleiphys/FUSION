# Pre-Equilibrium Evolution of Conserved Charges with ICCING Initial Conditions

> Heavy-ion collisions can be well described through relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, but questions still remain when hydrodynamics is applicable because the initial state may begin very far-from-equilibrium. Thus, a pre-equilibrium evolution phase is used to bridge the gap between the initial state and hydrodynamics. K$ϕ$MP$ϕ$ST is one such pre-equilibrium model that propagates the energy-momentum tensor by decomposing it into the background and fluctuations around that background, whose evolution is captured by Green's functions. We extend this formalism to include conserved charges and calculate the corresponding non-equilibrium Green's functions in the relaxation time approximation. The ICCING algorithm initializes conserved charges in the initial state by sampling $g \rightarrow q\bar{q}$ splitting probabilities and is, thus, perfectly positioned to implement Green's functions for charge propagation. We show that this method alters the initial state charge geometries and is applicable in central to mid-central collisions.

## Key claim

The authors extend the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium formalism to include the evolution of conserved charges (baryon, strangeness, electric charge) by computing non-equilibrium Green's functions in the relaxation time approximation, and show that coupling these Green's functions to the ICCING initial condition algorithm alters the initial-state charge geometries in central to mid-central heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a longitudinally boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous background, then linearize around this background to obtain evolution equations for small perturbations of the distribution functions. They decompose the perturbations into Fourier modes in the transverse plane and expand the distribution functions in spherical harmonics to compute non-equilibrium Green's functions that propagate energy-momentum and charge perturbations from the initial time to the hydrodynamic starting time. These Green's functions are then applied to the ICCING initial conditions, which sample \(g \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) splitting probabilities to initialize conserved charge densities, treating the charge and energy differences from gluon splittings as small perturbations around the background.

## Key numbers

* The constant \(C_\infty \approx 0.9\) quantifies how efficiently initial energy is converted into thermal energy in the background evolution.
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is chosen as \(\tilde{\eta}/s = 1/(4\pi)\) in the calculations.
* The effective degeneracy factor for all partons is \(\nu_{\text{eff}} = \nu_g + \frac{7}{8} 2N_f \nu_q\) with \(\nu_g = 16\), \(\nu_q = 6\), and \(N_f = 3\).
* The relaxation time satisfies \(\tau_R T(\tau) = 5\,\tilde{\eta}T/(e+P) = \text{const}\) for the conformal system.

## Context

This work extends the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium formalism (Kurkela et al., 2018), which previously only propagated the energy-momentum tensor, to also evolve conserved charge densities. It builds on the ICCING algorithm (Carzon et al., 2019; Martinez et al., 2019) that initializes baryon, strangeness, and electric charge densities by sampling gluon splitting probabilities. The pre-equilibrium evolution is needed because moving directly from initial conditions to hydrodynamics produces a large fraction of acausal fluid cells (about 30% in Plumberg et al., 2021), and including a pre-equilibrium stage reduces this fraction. The method enables future coupling of ICCING initial conditions to 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics codes that simultaneously evolve all conserved currents.
