# Extracting Br(omega->pi^+ pi^-) from the Time-like Pion Form-factor

> We extract the G-parity-violating branching ratio Br(omega->pi^+ pi^-) from the effective rho-omega mixing matrix element Pi_{rho omega}(s), determined from e^+e^- -> pi^+ pi^- data. The omega->pi^+ pi^- partial width can be determined either from the time-like pion form factor or through the constraint that the mixed physical propagator D_{rho omega}^{mu nu}(s) possesses no poles. The two procedures are inequivalent in practice, and we show why the first is preferred, to find finally Br(omega->pi^+ pi^-) = 1.9 +/- 0.3%.

# Extracting Br(ω→π⁺π⁻) from the Time-like Pion Form-factor

## Key claim

The branching ratio Br(ω→π⁺π⁻) = 1.9 ± 0.3% is extracted from the effective ρ-ω mixing matrix element determined from e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data, but the result depends on which of two inequivalent procedures is used to relate the mixing matrix element to the partial width; the authors show that the method using the time-like pion form factor is preferred over the method requiring the mixed physical propagator to possess no poles.

## Method

The authors fit the time-like pion form factor F_π(s) in the ρ-ω interference region using e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ cross-section data, parametrizing the ρ⁰ contribution with a function F_ρ(s) that satisfies unitarity, analyticity, time-reversal invariance, and charge conservation. The effective ρ-ω mixing matrix element Π̃_{ρω}(s) is extracted as a fitting parameter, and the ω→π⁺π⁻ partial width is then computed from this mixing element using the relation between the effective coupling constant g_{ωππ}^{eff} and the pion form factor at the ω pole.

## Key numbers

- Br(ω→π⁺π⁻) = 1.9 ± 0.3%
- Effective ρ-ω mixing matrix element Π̃_{ρω}(m_ω²) = −3500 ± 300 MeV² (using SU(6) value f_{ωγ}/f_{ργ} = 1/3)
- After finite-width correction, Π̃_{ρω}(m_ω²) = −3900 ± 300 MeV²
- Im Π̃_{ρω}(m_ω²) = −300 ± 300 MeV²
- Π̃′_{ρω}(m_ω²) = 0.03 ± 0.04
- Γ(ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻) = 7.11 ± 0.08 ± 0.25 keV
- Br(ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻) = (4.63 ± 0.05 ± 0.07) × 10⁻⁵
- ρ pole mass m̄_ρ = 757.0 ± 1.1 MeV
- ρ pole width Γ̄_ρ = 141.3 ± 3.1 MeV
- Γ(ω → e⁺e⁻) = 0.60 ± 0.02 keV (from PDG)

## Context

This work extends earlier fits by Gardner and O'Connell (1998) to e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ data from Barkov et al. (1985) and resolves ambiguities in how to extract the ω→π⁺π⁻ branching ratio from the ρ-ω mixing matrix element. The result agrees closely with Bernicha et al. (1998) who found 1.85 ± 0.30% using a different method, and is consistent with but more precise than earlier measurements by Benaksas et al. (1972) and Quenzer et al. (1978). The analysis enables improved determination of isospin violation in the vector meson sector and clarifies the relationship between the effective mixing matrix element extracted from data and the physical ω→π⁺π⁻ decay width.
