# Exclusive final state hadron observables from neutrino-nucleus multi-nucleon knockout

> We present results of an updated calculation of the 2p2h (two particle two hole) contribution to the neutrino-induced charge-current cross section. We provide also some exclusive observables, interesting from the point of view of experimental studies, e.g. distributions of momenta of the outgoing nucleons and of available energy, which we compare with the results obtained within the NEUT generator. We also compute, and separate from the total, the contributions of 3p3h mechanisms. Finally, we discuss the differences between the present results and previous implementations of the model in MC event-generators, done at the level of inclusive cross sections, which might significantly influence the experimental analyses, particularly in the cases where the hadronic observables are considered.

## Key claim

The updated 2p2h calculation, which now explicitly evaluates the ΔΔ-diagram instead of using a pre-computed ImΣΔ parametrization, yields total cross sections that differ by only 5–10% from the previous implementation, but the new treatment is essential for obtaining realistic exclusive hadron observables rather than the isotropic phase-space distributions used in Monte Carlo generators like NEUT.

## Method

The authors compute the 2p2h and 3p3h contributions to the neutrino-nucleus charged-current cross section using a many-body formalism based on meson-exchange currents (pion and rho exchange) with in-medium modifications described by longitudinal and transverse Landau-Migdal parameters g′l and g′t, which are now treated as energy-transfer dependent. The Δ(1232) resonance is treated with a pure spin-3/2 propagator and an updated C5A form factor (MAΔ = 950 MeV), and the ΔΔ-diagram is evaluated explicitly to preserve information about outgoing nucleon kinematics, while the 3p3h contribution is still taken from the parametrization of Oset (1987). The exclusive hadron distributions are compared with those from the NEUT generator, which uses the same inclusive cross section but distributes outgoing nucleons uniformly in phase space before applying a cascade model.

## Key numbers

- The total 2p2h cross section on 12C shows a plateau above Eν ≈ 1.1 GeV due to a momentum-transfer cutoff at |q| < 1.2 GeV.
- The difference between the present 2p2h calculation and the previous implementation (Nieves 2011) is at the order of 5–10%, interpreted as theoretical uncertainty.
- The 3p3h contribution corresponds to around 20% of the total 2p2h cross section for neutrino energies above 1 GeV.
- For Eν = 1.5 GeV, the |q| < 1.2 GeV cutoff neglects around 10% of the available phase space.
- The Landau-Migdal parameters in the low-momentum limit give g′l = g′t = 0.63, the value used in previous RPA and 2p2h calculations (Nieves 2004, 2011).
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is f2πNN/4π = 0.08, with cutoff Λπ = 1200 MeV and mπ = 139 MeV; the rho meson mass is mρ = 770 MeV with Cρ = 2 and Λρ = 2500 MeV.
- The C5A form factor for the Δ-N transition uses MAΔ = 950 MeV with normalization 1.18 at q2 = 0.

## Context

This work extends the 2p2h model of Nieves (2011), which together with the approaches of Martini (2009, 2010) provided the first sensible theoretical explanation for the MiniBooNE axial mass puzzle (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010). The new calculation improves on the previous treatment by removing numerical approximations, updating the Δ propagator and form factor following Hernandez (2016), and treating the Landau-Migdal parameters as energy-dependent. The exclusive hadron distributions enable comparison with Monte Carlo generators like NEUT (Hayato 2009) and provide predictions for experiments such as Hyper-Kamiokande, where multi-nucleon knockout processes bias neutrino energy reconstruction if not properly modeled. The results also serve as a validation of the previous inclusive calculation and address tensions between theoretical approaches noted by Dolan (2019).
