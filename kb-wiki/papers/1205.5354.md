# Overlap of quasiparticle random-phase approximation states for nuclear matrix elements of the neutrino-less double beta decay

> Quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) is applied to two nuclei, and overlap of the QRPA excited states based on the different nuclei is calculated. The aim is to calculate the overlap of intermediate nuclear states of the double-beta decay. We use the like-particle QRPA after the closure approximation is applied to the nuclear matrix elements. The overlap is calculated rigorously by making use of the explicit equation of the QRPA ground state. The formulation of the overlap is shown, and a test calculation is performed. The effectiveness of the truncations used is shown.

## Key claim

The overlap of QRPA intermediate states for neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements can be calculated rigorously using the explicit QRPA ground-state wave function, and the test calculation shows that bold truncations of the two-quasiparticle space are allowed for the un-normalized overlap matrix elements, though the normalization factors require a less-truncated calculation.

## Method

The like-particle QRPA (quasiparticle random-phase approximation) is applied to the initial and final nuclei after the closure approximation is used for the nuclear matrix elements. The overlap of QRPA excited states based on different nuclei is calculated using the explicit equation of the QRPA ground state, expressed as an exponential of a four-quasiparticle operator. The overlap matrix elements are expanded up to second order in the ground-state generators, and the normalization factors are calculated up to fourth order, using the HFB ground states of the two nuclei connected by a unitary transformation of the quasiparticle basis.

## Key numbers

- The HFB ground state of $^{26}$Mg has proton quadrupole deformation $\beta_\mathrm{p} = -0.199$, proton pairing gap $\Delta_\mathrm{p} = 0.794$ MeV, neutron deformation $\beta_\mathrm{n} = -0.195$, and neutron pairing gap $\Delta_\mathrm{n} = 1.510$ MeV.
- The HFB ground state of $^{26}$Si has $\beta_\mathrm{p} = -0.224$, $\Delta_\mathrm{p} = 0.865$ MeV, $\beta_\mathrm{n} = -0.206$, and $\Delta_\mathrm{n} = 1.402$ MeV.
- The total dimension of the HFB space is approximately 330, including states with negative $j_z$.
- The cutoff of the quasiparticle energy is 20 MeV for the HFB equation.
- The truncation of the two-canonical-quasiparticle states at $\mathfrak{N}_F + \mathfrak{N}_I = 350$ is sufficient for convergence of the overlap matrix elements, compared to the total of approximately 3300 states without truncation.
- The contribution of the second-order terms $G^{FI2}_{mm^\prime}$ is negligible in the diagonal overlap matrix elements.
- The contribution of $(K_1\pi_1) \neq (0+)$ to the first-order terms $G^{FI1}_{mm^\prime}$ is very small for all states except the spurious state $m=1$.

## Context

This work extends the like-particle QRPA approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, following the suggestion by Engel and building on the QRPA formalism of Ring and Schuck (1980) and the explicit QRPA ground-state equation of Balian and Brezin (1969). The overlap calculation is performed with greater accuracy than in previous studies by Simkovic et al. (2004), Kamo et al. (1991), Civitarese et al. (1986), and Grotz and Klapdor (1985). The method enables more accurate calculation of the intermediate-state overlap for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay nuclear matrix elements, with the test on $^{26}$Mg and $^{26}$Si demonstrating that the explicit QRPA ground-state wave function is computationally tractable for modern parallel computers.
