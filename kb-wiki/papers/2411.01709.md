# Transitions To Door-way States And Nuclear Responses Against 2-body External Fields

> Nuclear microscopic structural models that treat two-body effective interactions self-consistently becomes available, one of which is second-random-phase-approximation (SRPA). SRPA can be used to study evolutions from 1 particle-1 hole (1p1h) to 2 particle-2 hole (2p2h) states from different point of view from reaction models. We studied nuclear excitations created by 1-body and 2-body external fields and discuss transitions between 1p1h and 2p2h states obtained by the SRPA approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the second-random-phase-approximation (SRPA) can describe transitions from 1 particle-1 hole (1p1h) to 2 particle-2 hole (2p2h) states in nuclear excitations, but the strength distributions are significantly smaller than standard 1p1h RPA because some matrix elements of the two-body force are already accounted for in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock mean field, requiring a subtraction method to correct this.

## Method

The author developed a new computational SRPA code using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method with the SkM* interaction and a box size of 16 fm, solving the SRPA equation for phonon creation operators that include both 1p1h and 2p2h configurations. The calculation was performed with single-particle and 2p2h excitation energies below 70 MeV, adopting the diagonal approximation that neglects transitions between 2p2h states. Transition strengths were computed for both 1-body external fields (isoscalar monopole operator) and 2-body external fields (double isoscalar monopole operator) for the nucleus 16O.

## Key numbers

- The most important 1p1h state for the monopole resonance in 16O at 16.75 MeV (RPA) is proton 0p1/2 → 2p5/2 with amplitude p_mi^(nu) ≈ 0.97 at 16.71 MeV
- For the SRPA low-lying resonance at 16.43 MeV, the same 1p1h state amplitude decreases to p_mi^(nu) ≈ 0.69, while neutron 0p1/2 → 2p5/2 contributes p_mi^(nu) ≈ 0.05
- For the SRPA high-lying resonance at 33.84 MeV, the most important configuration is neutron 0p1/2 → 1s1/2 and proton 0p3/2 → 2d5/2 with p_mnij^(nu) = 0.83
- The 2p2h level density exceeds the 1p1h level density at about 15 MeV for J^pi = 0+ in 16O
- For the double isoscalar monopole resonance at 42.99 MeV, the main configuration is proton (0p1/2)^2 → 0d5/2 3d5/2 with p_mnij^(nu) = 0.72
- Only resonances with transition strengths greater than 0.5 fm^4 are shown in the total amplitude analysis

## Context

This work extends the application of SRPA, previously used to study spreading widths of giant resonances and Gamow-Teller transitions (Gambacurta 2015, Yang 2021, Gambacurta 2022), to understand how 1p1h states evolve to 2p2h states from a structural perspective rather than the reaction-model approach of multi-step direct models (FKK, Tamura 1982, Nishioka 1988) or exciton models (Griffin 1966). The results enable future studies of meson-exchange current effects in muon capture reactions, where 2-body external fields directly create 2p2h initial configurations, and the author plans to include full 2p2h residual interactions beyond the diagonal approximation.
