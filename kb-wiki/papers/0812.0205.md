# Shell model study of single-particle and collective structure in neutron-rich Cr isotopes

> The structure of neutron-rich Cr isotopes is systematically investigated by using the spherical shell model. The calculations reproduce well the known energy levels for the even-even $^{52-62}$Cr and odd-mass $^{53-59}$Cr nuclei, and predict a lowering of excitation energies around neutron number N=40. The calculated $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\to 0_{1}^{+})$ systematics shows a pronounced collectivity around N=40; a similar characteristic behavior has been suggested for Zn and Ge isotopes. Causes for the sudden drop of the $9/2_{1}^{+}$ energy in $^{59}$Cr and the appearance of very low $0_{2}^{+}$ states around N=40 are discussed. We also predict a new band with strong collectivity built on the $0_{2}^{+}$ state in the N=40 isotope $^{64}$Cr.

## Key claim

The spherical shell model with a restricted $fpg$ model space (using a $^{48}$Ca core and including the $\nu g_{9/2}$ orbital) can reproduce the known energy levels of neutron-rich Cr isotopes and predict a pronounced collectivity around $N=40$, but the agreement depends on including specific monopole interactions and the $g_{9/2}$ orbital; the GXPF1A interaction in the smaller $fp$-shell space fails for isotopes beyond $N=34$.

## Method

Large-scale spherical shell model calculations were performed using the ANTOINE code with a Hamiltonian consisting of pairing plus multipole forces (including $J=0$ and $J=2$ pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and octupole-octupole terms) plus monopole corrections, in an $fpg$ model space comprising $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$ active proton orbitals and the same plus $0g_{9/2}$ neutron orbitals, with eight $f_{7/2}$ neutrons frozen relative to a $^{48}$Ca core. Single-particle energies were taken from $^{49}$Ca and $^{41}$Ca excitation energies, with $\varepsilon_{g9/2}=4.5$ MeV chosen to reproduce the $9/2_{1}^{+}$ energy in $^{53}$Cr; effective charges $e_\pi=1.5e$ and $e_\nu=0.5e$ were used for $B(E2)$ calculations.

## Key numbers

- The $9/2_{1}^{+}$ state energy drops from 2087 keV in $^{55}$Cr to 1057 keV in $^{57}$Cr and down to 503 keV in $^{59}$Cr, reproduced well by the calculation.
- Calculated $B(E2;2_{1}^{+}\rightarrow 0_{1}^{+})$ for $^{62}$Cr is 307 $e^2$fm$^4$ and for $^{64}$Cr is 329 $e^2$fm$^4$, consistent with Sorlin et al. values of 302 and 318 $e^2$fm$^4$ respectively.
- Deformation estimated from calculated $B(E2)$ around $N=40$ is $\beta_2 \sim 0.3$.
- For $^{59}$Cr, the deformation estimated from the $9/2_{1}^{+}$ band is $\beta_2 = 0.18$, consistent with Total Routhian Surface calculations.
- The $B(E2;13/2_1^{+}\rightarrow 9/2_1^{+})$ in the odd-mass band increases from 143 $e^2$fm$^4$ in $^{53}$Cr to 360 $e^2$fm$^4$ in $^{59}$Cr.

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of neutron-rich Cr isotopes beyond the $fp$-shell space used by Honma (GXPF1A interaction) and KB3G, which fail for $^{59}$Cr and $^{60}$Cr, and builds on earlier calculations by Sorlin et al. (2003) in the $fpg$-shell space with a $^{48}$Ca core. The results show that the $g_{9/2}$ orbital is essential for describing the sudden drop of the $9/2_{1}^{+}$ state and the development of collectivity around $N=40$, similar to behavior seen in Zn and Ge isotopes by Padilla and Walle. The predicted band built on the $0_{2}^{+}$ state in $^{64}$Cr enables future experimental searches for strong collectivity in this neutron-rich region, though inclusion of the $d_{5/2}$ orbital may be needed for nuclei beyond $N=40$.
