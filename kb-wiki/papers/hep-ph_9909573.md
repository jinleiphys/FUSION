# Probing the Space-Time evolution of Heavy Ion Collisions with Bremsstrahlung

> We examine the bremsstrahlung photons emitted in the central collisions of two gold nuclei at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. While the measurements of the final hadrons can reveal only the amount of stopping, they tell us very little about the space-time evolution of the bulk matter in the collisions. By using two extreme collision scenarios, we argue that the low and medium energy bremsstrahlung photons can, not only reveal the amount of stopping, but also the finer details of the space-time evolution of the charges.

# Probing the Space-Time evolution of Heavy Ion Collisions with Bremsstrahlung

## Key claim
Low and medium energy bremsstrahlung photons from central Au+Au collisions at RHIC can distinguish between different space-time evolution scenarios of the bulk charges, even when the final hadron rapidity distributions are identical. The authors argue this using two extreme collision scenarios (Bjorken-like and Landau-like) that produce the same final rapidity-transverse spatial distribution but different photon spectra.

## Method
The authors use the LEXUS model, which treats A-A collisions as rows of multiple nucleon-nucleon scatterings without parton language, to generate a realistic 2-D rapidity-transverse spatial distribution of net charge. They then compute the bremsstrahlung radiation amplitude from a time-varying current using the standard formula for radiation from accelerating charges, inserting two different acceleration profiles: a Bjorken-like scenario where nucleons progressively decelerate to final rapidities, and a Landau-like scenario where nucleons stop instantaneously then are driven to final rapidities by pressure.

## Key numbers
- The initial nuclei travel at velocity \(v_0 = \tanh y_0 \sim 1\) (ultrarelativistic) at RHIC energy of 100 GeV/nucleon in the center of mass frame.
- The bremsstrahlung intensity formula includes the factor \(\frac{\alpha Z^2}{4\pi^2}\) where \(\alpha\) is the fine structure constant and \(Z\) is the nuclear charge.
- For the Landau-like scenario, the photon spectra show oscillations and enhancements for deceleration times \(t_f = 20, 10, 2,\) and \(1\) fm/c at an angle \(\theta = 5^\circ\) from the beam axis.
- The Bjorken-like scenario shows almost no dependence on \(t_f\) and very slow variation with photon energy \(\omega\) at the same angle.

## Context
This work extends the idea of using bremsstrahlung to measure baryon stopping, previously illustrated by the same authors in Kapusta and Wong (1999), by adding sensitivity to the space-time evolution of charges. The LEXUS model was designed to interpret A-A collisions as nucleon clusters undergoing multiple scatterings, and if it cannot reproduce data, that would indicate new physics such as quark-gluon plasma formation. The method enables experimental distinction between different collision dynamics using only a few photon detectors placed at small angles to the beam pipe, rather than the many hadronic detectors needed for rapidity distribution measurements.
