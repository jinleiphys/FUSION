# Quenching of high-pT hadrons: Alternative scenario

> A new scenario, alternative to energy loss, for the observed suppression of high-pT hadrons observed at RHIC is proposed. In the limit of a very dense medium crated in nuclear collisions the mean free-path of the produced (pre)hadron vanishes, and and the nuclear suppression, R_{AA} is completely controlled by the production length. The RHIC data are well explained in a parameter free way, and predictions for LHC are provided.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) hadrons in nuclear collisions at RHIC can be explained without invoking energy loss in the medium, provided the produced medium is so dense that the mean free path of the pre-hadron vanishes; in that limit the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) is completely controlled by the production length of the hadron and can be predicted in a parameter-free way.

## Method

The authors compute the quark fragmentation function in Born approximation and include gluon radiation via the jet lag effect, which introduces a dependence on the coherence length \(l_c\). They then model central collisions of identical heavy nuclei with a uniform nuclear density and derive an analytic expression for \(R_{AA}\) that depends on the mean and mean-square production length. In the limit of a very dense medium, the absorption terms vanish and \(R_{AA}\) reduces to \(\langle l_c^2 \rangle / R_A^2\), which is evaluated using only vacuum properties of the fragmentation process.

## Key numbers

- For central \(Cu-Cu\) collisions, the parameter-free prediction gives \(R_{AA} \approx 0.42\), which is confirmed by data.

## Context

This work challenges the standard energy-loss scenario for high-\(p_T\) suppression, which assumes a long hadronization length inside the medium and which the authors note had no prior justification (Kopeliovich et al., earlier work). The alternative scenario extends the authors' previous treatment of the jet lag effect and the coherence-length formulation of fragmentation (Berger, 1980; Kopeliovich et al., jet lag paper). It enables a parameter-free prediction for \(R_{AA}\) at LHC energies and implies that the observed suppression may not carry detailed information about medium properties beyond indicating that the medium is very dense.
