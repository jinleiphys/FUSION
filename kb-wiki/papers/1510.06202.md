# Etaprime interactions with nucleons and nuclei

> We summarise recent progress in theory and experiment towards understanding etaprime meson interactions with nucleons and nuclei. Highlights include the production mechanism of etaprime mesons in proton-proton collisions close to threshold, the etaprime effective mass shift in nuclei and the determination of the etaprime-nucleon scattering length in free space.

## Key claim

The paper summarises recent progress in understanding $\eta'$ meson interactions with nucleons and nuclei, highlighting that the $\eta'$ experiences an effective mass shift in nuclei catalysed by its gluonic component, though the $\eta'$-nucleon interaction in free space is much weaker than the $\eta$-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors review experimental and theoretical studies of $\eta'$ production in proton-proton collisions close to threshold using the COSY-11 facility, $\eta'$ photoproduction experiments at ELSA and Jefferson Laboratory, and studies of the $\eta'$ in medium via photoproduction from nuclear targets at CBELSA/TAPS. They also discuss the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model calculations for in-medium mass shifts and the axial U(1) extended chiral Lagrangian for $\eta' \pi$ interactions.

## Key numbers

- $\eta'$ total width in free space: $\Gamma = 0.226 \pm 0.017 (stat.) \pm 0.014 (syst.)$ MeV, determined from mass distribution in COSY-11 experiments
- $\eta'$ effective mass shift in nuclei at nuclear matter density: $-37 \pm 10 (stat.) \pm 10 (syst.)$ MeV, measured from photoproduction on carbon targets with average $\eta'$ momentum of 1.1 GeV
- $\eta'$-nucleus optical potential imaginary part at nuclear matter density: $W(\rho_0) = -10 \pm 2.5$ MeV
- $\eta'$-proton scattering length in free space: $\mathrm{Re}(a_{\eta' p}) = 0 \pm 0.43$ fm, $\mathrm{Im}(a_{\eta' p}) = 0.37^{+0.40}_{-0.16}$ fm
- $\eta'$ production in proton-proton collisions is s-wave dominated for excess energy up to at least ${\cal E} = 11$ MeV
- $\eta' \pi^-$ production is enhanced relative to $\eta \pi^-$ production by a factor of 5-10 in exotic $L=1,3,5$ partial waves with quantum numbers $L^{-+}$ in COMPASS experiments at 191 GeV

## Context

This review extends the experimental programme on $\eta'$ interactions that began with COSY-11 studies of near-threshold production (Pawel Moskal and collaborators, 2000s) and photoproduction measurements at ELSA (CBELSA/TAPS collaboration) and Jefferson Laboratory. The results enable searches for $\eta'$ bound states in nuclei at ELSA, GSI and LEPS2, and provide constraints on the role of gluonic degrees of freedom in low-energy QCD, particularly through the QMC model predictions (Guichon and collaborators) and the axial U(1) extended chiral Lagrangian framework (Veneziano and collaborators).
