# Short range correlations in the extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> Short-range potential has been added into an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model. The RMS radius, binding energy and momentum distribution of $^{12}$C with different initial structures and short-range potential parameters have been checked. The separation energy of $^{12}$C(p,2p)$^{11}$B reaction is calculated and compared with the experimental data, it indicates that our modified EQMD model can be taken as a reliable tool for studying proton pair knock-out reaction. Furthermore, the short range correlation effects on emission time and momentum spectrum of two protons are discussed. Finally, the momentum correlation function of the emitted proton pair is calculated by the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz's analytical method. The result explains that short-range repulsion causes high momentum tail and weakens the momentum correlation function.

## Key claim

Adding a short-range repulsive potential to the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model produces a high-momentum tail in the nucleon momentum distribution and weakens the proton-pair momentum correlation function, but the authors note that the added potential is preliminary and that tensor force contributions, which are spin- and isospin-dependent, are not included.

## Method

The authors added a repulsive term resembling a Lennard-Jones potential to the two-body Skyrme interaction in the EQMD model, with the repulsive strength controlled by a parameter \(r_0\). They initialized \(^{12}\)C with either a spherical (Woods-Saxon) or a triangular three-\(\alpha\) cluster structure, cooled the system to a ground state, and then simulated the \(^{12}\)C(p,2p)\(^{11}\)B reaction at 250 MeV incident energy. The momentum correlation function of the emitted proton pair was calculated using the Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical method.

## Key numbers

- RMS radius of triangular cluster \(^{12}\)C without short-range correlation (SRC): 2.57 fm; experimental value: 2.47 fm.
- RMS radius of spherical \(^{12}\)C without SRC: 2.30 fm; experimental value: 2.47 fm.
- Binding energy of triangular cluster \(^{12}\)C without SRC: 7.26 MeV; experimental value: 7.68 MeV.
- Binding energy of spherical \(^{12}\)C without SRC: 8.71 MeV; experimental value: 7.68 MeV.
- For spherical \(^{12}\)C with SRC, RMS radius ranges from 2.32 fm (\(r_0 = 1.3\)) to 2.37 fm (\(r_0 = 1.1\)); binding energy ranges from 8.00 MeV (\(r_0 = 1.3\)) to 6.88 MeV (\(r_0 = 1.1\)).
- For triangular \(^{12}\)C with SRC, RMS radius ranges from 2.34 fm (\(r_0 = 1.3\)) to 2.37 fm (\(r_0 = 1.2\)); binding energy ranges from 6.87 MeV (\(r_0 = 1.3\)) to 6.12 MeV (\(r_0 = 1.1\)).
- The momentum correlation function shows a peak at relative momentum \(q = 20\) MeV/\(c\).
- Most protons are emitted before 100 fm/\(c\) in the knockout reaction.

## Context

This work extends the EQMD model, originally developed by Maruyama (1996) to describe light nuclei and cluster structures, by adding a short-range repulsive potential to study short-range correlations (SRC). It builds on experimental findings from Jefferson Laboratory (Hen et al., Subedi et al., Duer et al.) showing that neutron-proton pairs dominate SRC in nuclei from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{208}\)Pb, and on the high-energy inverse kinematics (p,2p) experiment on \(^{12}\)C reported in Nature Physics. The results enable future studies of SRC effects in proton-pair knockout reactions and suggest that including tensor force and spin-isospin degrees of freedom would improve the model.
