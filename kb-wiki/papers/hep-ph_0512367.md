# Baryon Inhomogeneity Generation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma Phase

> We discuss the possibility of generation of baryon inhomogeneities in a quark-gluon plasma phase due to moving Z(3) interfaces. By modeling the dependence of effective mass of the quarks on the Polyakov loop order parameter, we study the reflection of quarks from collapsing Z(3) interfaces and estimate resulting baryon inhomogeneities in the context of the early universe. We argue that in the context of certain low energy scale inflationary models, it is possible that large Z(3) walls arise at the end of the reheating stage. Collapse of such walls could lead to baryon inhomogeneities which may be separated by large distances near the QCD scale. Importantly, the generation of these inhomogeneities is insensitive to the order, or even the existence, of the quark-hadron phase transition. We also briefly discuss the possibility of formation of quark nuggets in this model, as well as baryon inhomogeneity generation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that baryon inhomogeneities can be generated in the quark-gluon plasma phase itself by collapsing Z(3) domain walls, independent of whether the quark-hadron phase transition is first order or a cross-over. The mechanism relies on the assumption that the effective mass of quarks depends on the Polyakov loop order parameter, leading to quark reflection from moving Z(3) interfaces, and requires specific low energy scale inflationary models to produce sufficiently large domain walls near the QCD scale.

## Method

The authors model the effective mass of quarks as a linear function of the Polyakov loop magnitude |l(x)|, with the mass increasing as |l(x)| decreases across a Z(3) domain wall. They approximate the resulting potential barrier as rectangular, using the minimum |l| value from numerically computed wall profiles at fixed temperatures (200 MeV and 300 MeV) to determine barrier height and width (0.5 fm at 300 MeV, 1 fm at 200 MeV). Transmission coefficients for quarks are calculated from the Dirac equation, and evolution equations for net baryon density inside and outside a collapsing spherical domain wall are solved, assuming rapid wall collapse at sound speed and neglecting cosmic expansion during collapse.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension of Z(3) walls: 0.34 GeV/fm^2 at T = 200 MeV and 2.61 GeV/fm^2 at T = 300 MeV
- Critical temperature T_c taken as approximately 182 MeV
- Wall width d = 0.5 fm for T = 300 MeV and d = 1 fm for T = 200 MeV
- Current quark masses used: m_u approximately m_d = 10 MeV, m_s approximately 140 MeV
- Constituent mass parameter m_0 taken as 300 MeV for the primary estimate
- Baryon number as large as about 10^44 within a size of order 1 meter for large collapsing walls
- Wall collapse velocity assumed to be c/sqrt(3) (speed of sound)

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of baryon inhomogeneity generation during a first order quark-hadron phase transition (Witten 1984, Fuller 1988) which required moving hadronic bubble walls and failed if the transition was a cross-over as suggested by lattice calculations. The authors propose an alternative mechanism using Z(3) domain walls in the quark-gluon plasma phase, building on earlier studies of Z(3) interface properties (Pisarski 2000, Dumitru 2001) and their topological strings (Layek 2005). The scenario enables new constraints on low energy scale inflationary models (such as those near the electroweak scale) through inhomogeneous nucleosynthesis calculations, and also suggests possible baryon fluctuation generation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions for future computer simulation studies.
