# Comment on "The third Zemach moment of the proton", by Cloet and Miller

> Cloet and Miller, in arXiv:1008.4345, state that "existing data rule out a value of the third Zemach moment large enough to explain the current puzzle with the proton charge radius determined from the Lamb shift in muonic Hydrogen. This is in contrast with the recent claim of De Rújula [arXiv:1008.3861]". To be more precise: it is not. It is, however, contrary to what they claim that I claim. Cloet and Miller have simply misinterpreted my claims.

# Comment on "The third Zemach moment of the proton", by Cloet and Miller

## Key claim

The author's main claim is that his model-independent algebraic extraction of the third Zemach moment from the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift and the CODATA proton charge radius yields a value of 3.32 ± 0.22 fm for [⟨r³⟩(2)]^(1/3), and that Cloet and Miller have misinterpreted his work by attributing to him a monopole model claim he did not make.

## Method

The author uses the model-independent theoretical expression for the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift as a function of ⟨r²⟩ and the third Zemach moment ⟨r³⟩(2), equates it to the measured Lamb shift value, and substitutes the CODATA value of ⟨r²⟩ to solve algebraically for ⟨r³⟩(2). He then contrasts this model-independent result with the model-dependent approach of Cloet and Miller, who extrapolate high-q² electron-proton scattering parameterizations (dipole, Kelly, Alberico) to low q² to extract the third Zemach moment.

## Key numbers

- CODATA proton charge radius: ⟨r²⟩ = (0.8768 ± 0.0069 fm)²
- Measured muonic hydrogen Lamb shift (2P_{3/2}^{F=2} → 2S_{1/2}^{F=1}): L_exp = 206.2949 ± 0.0032 meV
- Theoretical Lamb shift expression: L_th = 209.9779(49) − 5.2262 ⟨r²⟩ + 0.00913 ⟨r³⟩(2) (in meV with radii in fm)
- Extracted third Zemach moment: [⟨r³⟩(2)]^(1/3) = 3.32 ± 0.22 fm
- Single-pole approximation mass scale from this moment: m ≈ 261 MeV, compared to 2m_π± ≈ 278 MeV

## Context

This comment responds to Cloet and Miller (2010), who claimed that existing data rule out a third Zemach moment large enough to explain the proton radius puzzle, contradicting De Rújula's earlier claim. The author clarifies that Cloet and Miller misinterpreted his work: he did not claim a monopole model accounts for the puzzle, but rather used a monopole only to estimate the large-r slope of the charge distribution. The work extends the discussion of Sick, who emphasized the difficulty of extracting reliable information on ρ(r) from its Fourier transform G_E(q²). The result enables a model-independent benchmark for the third Zemach moment against which model-dependent extractions from electron-proton scattering data can be compared.
