# Intrinsic Parity of the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ Mesons

> Contrary to the usual belief, by carefully examining the operation of parity transformation on the $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ mesons in the generalized canonical representation, we establish that the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ meson-antimeson pair have {\it opposite} intrinsic parity. This opens up the possibility that while the particles without an internal structure may utilize one representation of the Lorentz group, phenomenologies of composite particles may exploit a different representation. As such (perhaps, only some of) the meson structures beyond the standard $q\,\overline {Q}$ may exploit the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ generalized canonical representation of the Lorentz group -- this would result in a meson and the associated antimeson to manifest themselves in {\it different} partial waves. %%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%% CONSTRUCTIVE COMMENTS WELCOME, AS ALWAYS D. ## Key claim
Contrary to the usual belief that the relative intrinsic parity of a particle-antiparticle pair is representation-independent, this paper establishes that for mesons described by the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ representation of the Lorentz group, the meson and antimeson have opposite intrinsic parity, whereas in the $(1/2,1/2)$ representation they have the same parity. The author notes that which representation is actually realized in nature for composite particles is an experimentally open question.

## Method
The author examines the parity transformation on the $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ matter field in the generalized canonical representation, using the spin-one Weinberg equation and the explicit form of the $6\times 6$ $\gamma_{\mu\nu}$ matrices. By requiring the parity-transformed wave function to satisfy the same field equation, the parity operator $S(\Lambda_P)$ is identified with $\gamma_{\circ\circ}$. The transformation properties of the particle and antiparticle creation operators are then derived from the action of $\gamma_{\circ\circ}$ on the explicit $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ spinors $u_\sigma(p)$ and $v_\sigma(p)$, yielding opposite signs for particles and antiparticles.

## Key numbers
- The $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ spinors $u_\sigma(p)$ and $v_\sigma(p)$ satisfy $\gamma_{\circ\circ}\, u_\sigma(p') = +\,u_\sigma(p)$ and $\gamma_{\circ\circ}\, v_\sigma(p') = -\,v_\sigma(p)$, where $p'$ is the parity-transformed momentum.
- The particle creation operators transform as $U(\Lambda_P)\,a^\dagger_\sigma(\vec p)\, U^{-1}(\Lambda_P) = +\,a^\dagger_\sigma(-\,\vec p)$.
- The antiparticle creation operators transform as $U(\Lambda_P)\,b^\dagger_\sigma(\vec p)\, U^{-1}(\Lambda_P) = -\,b^\dagger_\sigma(-\,\vec p)$.

## Context
This work extends the author's ab initio study based on Weinberg (1964) and Wigner (1939) to establish a relativistic phenomenology for high-spin hadronic resonances. It contradicts the usual belief, as stated in Weinberg (1989) and elsewhere, that relative intrinsic parity is representation-independent. The result opens the possibility that composite particles beyond standard $q\overline{Q}$ structures may exploit the $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ representation, which would cause mesons and antimesons to manifest in different partial waves, and this can be tested at facilities such as CEBAF, NIKHEF, RHIC, and a possible LAMPF upgrade.
