# Nuclear Corrections to Hyperfine Structure in Light Hydrogenic Atoms

> Hyperfine intervals in light hydrogenic atoms and ions are among the most accurately measured quantities in physics. The theory of QED corrections has recently advanced to the point that uncalculated terms for hydrogenic atoms and ions are probably smaller than 0.1 parts per million (ppm), and the experiments are even more accurate. The difference of the experiments and QED theory is interpreted as the effect on the hyperfine interaction of the (finite) nuclear charge and magnetization distributions, and this difference varies from tens to hundreds of ppm. We have calculated the dominant component of the 1s hyperfine interval for deuterium, tritium and singly ionized helium, using modern second-generation potentials to compute the nuclear component of the hyperfine splitting for the deuteron and the trinucleon systems. The calculated nuclear corrections are within 3% of the experimental values for deuterium and tritium, but are about 20% discrepant for singly ionized helium. The nuclear corrections for the trinucleon systems can be qualitatively understood by invoking SU(4) symmetry.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors evaluate the leading-order nuclear correction to the hyperfine splitting using a Low-moment formalism that expresses the energy shift as a correlation between the nuclear charge density and the nuclear current density. They decompose the nuclear current into convection, spin-magnetization, and meson-exchange parts, then separate the calculation into single-nucleon (Zemach) terms and two-nucleon correlation terms, evaluating the latter using modern second-generation nuclear potentials for the deuteron and trinucleon wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The proton Zemach moment is 1.086(12) fm, leading to a -58.2(6) kHz contribution to hydrogen hyperfine structure, which equals -41.0(5) ppm.
- The neutron Zemach moment is -0.042 fm.
- The calculated nuclear corrections are within 3% of experimental values for deuterium and tritium, but about 20% discrepant for singly ionized helium.
- Uncalculated QED terms for hydrogenic atoms are probably smaller than 0.1 ppm.
- The difference between experiment and QED theory (interpreted as nuclear effects) varies from tens to hundreds of ppm, with specific values for the 1s state: -33 ppm for hydrogen, 138 ppm for deuterium, -38 ppm for tritium, and -212 ppm for singly ionized helium.
- The intrinsic size of nuclear corrections is given by (-2 m_e alpha R) = -38 ppm [R/fm], where R is the Low moment in fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for nuclear corrections to hyperfine structure originally developed by Low and Bohr for the deuteron, and applies it to trinucleon systems (tritium and helium-3) for the first time using modern second-generation potentials. The calculations complement the recent QED advances summarized by Eides (2001) and Savely (2002), and the proton Zemach moment determination from electron-scattering data by Ingo (2004). The results enable more precise tests of nuclear structure models through hyperfine measurements, though the 20% discrepancy for singly ionized helium indicates that additional nuclear effects (such as meson-exchange currents or relativistic corrections) need to be included for three-nucleon systems.
