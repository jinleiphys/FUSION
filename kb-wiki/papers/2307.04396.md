# Diffusion and fluctuations of open charmed hadrons in an interacting hadronic medium

> Heavy quarks are excellent probes to understand the hot and dense medium formed in ultra-relativistic collisions. In a hadronic medium, studying the transport properties, e.g. the drag ($γ$), momentum diffusion ($B_{0}$), and spatial diffusion ($D_{s}$) coefficients of open charmed hadrons can provide useful information about the medium. Moreover, the fluctuations of charmed hadrons can help us to locate the onset of their deconfinement. In this work, we incorporate attractive and repulsive interactions in the well-established van der Waals hadron resonance gas model (VDWHRG) and study the diffusion and fluctuations of charmed hadrons. This study helps us understand the importance of interactions in the system, which affect both the diffusion and fluctuations of charmed hadrons.

## Key claim

The van der Waals hadron resonance gas model, which includes both attractive and repulsive interactions among hadrons, yields drag, momentum diffusion, and spatial diffusion coefficients for the $D^{0}$ meson that agree with several other phenomenological models, while also showing that interactions affect the magnitude of these transport coefficients compared to the ideal or excluded-volume HRG models. The study further claims that fluctuations of open charmed hadrons can help locate the onset of deconfinement, but the results are presented as comparisons with lattice QCD data rather than a definitive identification of the transition temperature.

## Method

The authors used the van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model, which incorporates attractive interactions via parameter $a = 0.926$ GeV fm$^3$ and repulsive interactions via hardcore radii $r_M = 0.2$ fm for mesons and $r_{B(\bar{B})} = 0.62$ fm for baryons/antibaryons, taken from Sarkar (2018). The drag coefficient $\gamma$ of the $D^{0}$ meson was computed using the relaxation time approximation, where the relaxation time $\tau^{-1} = \sum_j n_j \langle \sigma_j v_j \rangle$ was evaluated with elastic scattering cross sections of 10 mb for $Dm \rightarrow Dm$ and 15 mb for $DB(\bar{B}) \rightarrow DB(\bar{B})$ processes, following Ozvenchuk (2014) and He (2011). The momentum diffusion coefficient $B_0$ was obtained from the Einstein relation $B_0 = \gamma m_D T$, and the spatial diffusion coefficient $D_s$ from $D_s = T/(m_D \gamma)$. Fluctuations of open charmed hadrons were studied via susceptibilities $\chi_n^C$ computed from the VDWHRG partition function.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient $\gamma$ of $D^0$ meson increases with temperature; at $\mu_B = 0$ GeV, values from VDWHRG lie between those of ideal HRG (highest) and EVHRG (lowest), with VDWHRG slightly higher than EVHRG due to attractive interactions.
- Transverse momentum diffusion coefficient $B_0$ increases with temperature; at $\mu_B = 0$ GeV, VDWHRG results are consistent with Ghosh (2011) and Torres-Rincon (2021).
- Spatial diffusion coefficient $2\pi T D_s$ decreases with increasing temperature, approaching a minimum near the critical temperature; at $\mu_B = 0$ GeV, VDWHRG results align with Ozvenchuk (2014), He (2011), and Torres-Rincon (2021).
- For finite baryochemical potential, at $\mu_B = 0.630$ GeV, a non-monotonic behavior in $\gamma$, $B_0$, and $2\pi T D_s$ is observed at lower temperatures, attributed to the approach toward the liquid-gas phase transition with a critical point near $T \simeq 65$ MeV and $\mu_B \simeq 715$ MeV for the chosen VDW parameters.
- The ratio $\chi_4^C / \chi_2^C$ of net charm fluctuations is compared with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2014).

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals HRG model, previously applied to light-flavor thermodynamics by Vovchenko (2016) and Sarkar (2018), to the transport properties and fluctuations of open charmed hadrons. It complements prior studies of $D^0$ meson diffusion using the Fokker-Planck equation in ideal HRG by Ozvenchuk (2014), effective field theory by Ghosh (2011), and the Kadanoff-Baym approach by Torres-Rincon (2021), as well as lattice QCD calculations of charm fluctuations by Bazavov (2014). The results enable future comparisons between hadronic and partonic contributions to heavy-flavor observables in heavy-ion collisions, and may help constrain the charm hadron freeze-out temperature in a differential freeze-out scenario.
