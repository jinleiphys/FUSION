# From bare interactions, low--energy constants and unitary gas to nuclear density functionals without free parameters: application to neutron matter

> We further progress along the line of Ref. [Phys. Rev. {\bf A 94}, 043614 (2016)] where a functional for Fermi systems with anomalously large $s$-wave scattering length $a_s$ was proposed that has no free parameters. The functional is designed to correctly reproduce the unitary limit in Fermi gases together with the leading-order contributions in the s- and p-wave channels at low density. The functional is shown to be predictive up to densities $\sim0.01$ fm$^{-3}$ that is much higher densities compared to the Lee-Yang functional, valid for $ρ< 10^{-6}$ fm$^{-3}$. The form of the functional retained in this work is further motivated. It is shown that the new functional corresponds to an expansion of the energy in $(a_s k_F)$ and $(r_e k_F)$ to all orders, where $r_e$ is the effective range and $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum. One conclusion from the present work is that, except in the extremely low--density regime, nuclear systems can be treated perturbatively in $-(a_s k_F)^{-1}$ with respect to the unitary limit. Starting from the functional, we introduce density--dependent scales and show that scales associated to the bare interaction are strongly renormalized by medium effects. As a consequence, some of the scales at play around saturation are dominated by the unitary gas properties and not directly to low-energy constants. For instance, we show that the scale in the s-wave channel around saturation is proportional to the so-called Bertsch parameter $ξ_0$ and becomes independent of $a_s$. We also point out that these scales are of the same order of magnitude than those empirically obtained in the Skyrme energy density functional. We finally propose a slight modification of the functional such that it becomes accurate up to the saturation density $ρ\simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.

# From bare interactions, low-energy constants and unitary gas to nuclear density functionals without free parameters: application to neutron matter

## Key claim

The authors show that a parameter-free density functional for neutron matter, constrained by the unitary gas limit and low-density expansions, is predictive up to densities of about 0.01 fm$^{-3}$ and can be further modified to remain accurate up to saturation density (0.16 fm$^{-3}$), provided that nuclear systems are treated perturbatively in $-(a_s k_F)^{-1}$ with respect to the unitary limit except at extremely low densities.

## Method

The authors construct a functional for the energy ratio $E/E_{\rm FG}$ as a function of $a_s k_F$ and $r_e k_F$, where $a_s$ is the s-wave scattering length, $r_e$ is the effective range, and $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum. Five parameters in the functional are fixed by imposing the low-density Hartree-Fock expansion and the unitary gas limit, including the Bertsch parameter $\xi_0$ and the effective-range dependence parameters $\eta_e$ and $\delta_e$ from the literature. The functional is then compared to quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) results for neutron matter and to ab-initio calculations, and a modification is proposed to extend accuracy to saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter low-energy constants: $a_s = -18.9$ fm and $r_e = 2.7$ fm.
- The functional is predictive up to densities $\sim 0.01$ fm$^{-3}$, compared to the Lee-Yang functional which is valid only for $\rho < 10^{-6}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Bertsch parameter $\xi_0 = 0.376$, with effective-range parameters $\eta_e = 0.127$ and $\delta_e = -0.055$ from For12.
- The functional is modified to become accurate up to saturation density $\rho \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Average p-wave scattering volume for neutron matter: $a_p^3 \simeq 0.6$ fm$^3$ from experimental data, or $0.25$ fm$^3$ for the AV4 interaction used in QMC.

## Context

This work extends the parameter-free functional approach of Lacroix (2016) for Fermi systems with large s-wave scattering length, which was originally applied to cold atoms. The functional improves upon the Lee-Yang expansion (Lee and Yang, 1957; Bishop, 1973; Hammer, 2000) by incorporating the unitary gas limit, and it connects to the Skyrme energy density functional framework by showing that density-dependent scales around saturation are dominated by unitary gas properties rather than bare low-energy constants. The approach enables the construction of less empirical nuclear density functionals that can be applied to both infinite neutron matter and, in future work, finite nuclear systems.
