# J/psi suppression in heavy ion collisions by quark momentum diffusion

> The momentum diffusion effect of the quark pair due to the multiple scattering in a nuclear medium is studied to explain the observed J/psi yields in SPS experiments. The resulting suppression is found to be insufficient to reproduce the J/psi yield in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energy.

# J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions by quark momentum diffusion

## Key claim

The momentum diffusion of a quark-antiquark pair due to random multiple scattering in nuclear matter produces a power-law suppression of J/ψ yields that is insufficient to explain the Pb-Pb data at SPS energy, leaving that suppression anomalous; the model can describe pA and lighter AB collisions but fails for the most central Pb-Pb point.

## Method

The author modifies the Qiu-Vary-Zhang (QVZ) model by replacing the uniform shift of the c-cbar pair relative momentum (which gives exponential suppression from the open charm threshold) with a Gaussian smearing of the transition probability, representing classical three-dimensional random-walk momentum diffusion with variance ε²L. The J/ψ cross section is then computed using the factorized leading-order QCD formula with CTEQ5L parton distributions and the smeared transition probability, and compared to NA50 data for pA and AB collisions at SPS energy.

## Key numbers

- ε² = 0.185 GeV²/fm for the momentum diffusion model (solid curve), compared to ε² = 0.25 GeV²/fm for the original QVZ shifted-momentum model (dashed curve)
- The transition probability uses α_F = 1 and f_J/ψ = K_J/ψ N_J/ψ = 0.485, same as QVZ (2002)
- For asymptotically large L, the smeared transition probability falls as L^{-3/2} (three-dimensional diffusion), which is a power law rather than exponential
- The Pb-Pb data point lies far below the diffusion-model curve, while all other pA and AB data points are reasonably fitted

## Context

This work extends the QVZ model (Qiu, Vary, Zhang, 2002), which successfully explained J/ψ suppression from pA to AB collisions including Pb-Pb by a uniform momentum shift from coherent multiple scattering. The author shows that treating the multiple scattering as classical random walk (momentum diffusion) produces a qualitatively different, weaker suppression that cannot account for the Pb-Pb data, thus reinforcing the anomalous nature of that suppression first reported by the NA50 Collaboration (1997). The result suggests that the quantum coherence effects captured by the QVZ shift operator, rather than classical diffusion, are essential for explaining the Pb-Pb data, and that at RHIC and LHC energies a more complete quantum description of resonance production at parton level will be needed.
