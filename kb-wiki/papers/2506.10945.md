# Non-Abelian dynamics on a cube: improving quantum compilation through qudit-based simulations

> Recent developments in mapping lattice gauge theories relevant to the Standard Model onto digital quantum computers identify scalable paths with well-defined quantum compilation challenges toward the continuum. As an entry point to these challenges, we address the simulation of SU(2) lattice gauge theory. Using qudit registers to encode the digitized gauge field, we provide quantum resource estimates, in terms of elementary qudit gates, for arbitrarily high local gauge field truncations. We then demonstrate an end-to-end simulation of real-time, qutrit-digitized SU(2) dynamics on a cube. Through optimizing the simulation, we improved circuit decompositions for uniformly-controlled qudit rotations, an algorithmic primitive for general applications of quantum computing. The decompositions also apply to mixed-dimensional qudit systems, which we found advantageous for compiling lattice gauge theory simulations. Furthermore, we parallelize the evolution of opposite faces in anticipation of similar opportunities arising in three-dimensional lattice volumes. This work details an ambitious executable for future qudit hardware and attests to the value of codesign strategies between lattice gauge theory simulation and quantum compilation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates an end-to-end simulation of real-time SU(2) lattice gauge theory dynamics on a cube using qutrit (three-level) quantum registers, but the resource estimates and circuit optimizations are provided for arbitrarily high local gauge field truncations, with the explicit caveat that the full simulation is only demonstrated for the qutrit-digitized case.

## Method

The authors encode digitized SU(2) gauge fields on lattice links using qudit registers, construct the plaquette operator (the elementary building block of the lattice gauge theory Hamiltonian) in terms of qudit gates, and provide explicit circuit decompositions for uniformly-controlled qudit rotations. They then compile the full real-time evolution operator for a cubic lattice volume, parallelizing the evolution of opposite faces, and provide resource counts for the required elementary qudit gates (GCX gates, single-qudit rotations R_a, and X_{ij} gates) as functions of qudit dimension d and control qudit count k.

## Key numbers

* For the qutrit (d=3) plaquette operator, the full operator expression contains 217 distinct Pi*Gi terms with non-zero phase phi.
* For the ququart (d=4) plaquette operator, there are 2346 distinct Pi*Gi terms with non-zero phi.
* The number of distinct Gi terms under D4 symmetry grows as c(c+1)(c^2+c+2)/8 where c = d-1, giving 1 term for d=2, 6 for d=3, 21 for d=4, 55 for d=5, 120 for d=6, 231 for d=7, 406 for d=8, and 666 for d=9.
* The resource count for the uniformly-controlled rotation subroutine UCDBT uses d^k GCX gates when d is even, and d^k + (k-1) GCX gates when d is odd, plus d^k single-qudit rotation gates R_a.
* The input verification circuit for the plaquette operator with |s|=2 and k=3 uses 10 GCX gates and has depth 10.

## Context

This work extends the program of mapping lattice gauge theories onto quantum computers, building on the SU(2) digitization framework of Klco (2019) and Rahman (2021). It provides explicit quantum resource estimates and circuit decompositions that enable near-term simulation of non-Abelian gauge dynamics on qudit-based hardware, and the optimized uniformly-controlled rotation decompositions are applicable to general quantum computing tasks beyond lattice gauge theory. The paper enables future experimental implementations on qudit hardware and demonstrates the value of codesign between lattice gauge theory simulation and quantum compilation.
