# Large Rapidity Gap Processes in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> The cross sections for a variety of channels of proton-nucleus interaction associated with large gaps in rapidity are calculated within the Glauber-Gribov theory. We found inelastic shadowing corrections to be dramatically enhanced for such events. We employ the light-cone dipole formalism which allows to calculate the inelastic corrections to all orders of the multiple interaction. Although Gribov corrections are known to make nuclear matter more transparent, we demonstrate that in some instances they lead to an opaqueness. Numerical calculations are performed for the energies of the HERA-B experiment, and the RHIC-LHC colliders.

## Key claim

Inelastic shadowing corrections (Gribov corrections) are dramatically enhanced for large rapidity gap processes in proton-nucleus collisions, and contrary to the conventional wisdom that these corrections make nuclear matter more transparent, they can in some instances lead to increased opaqueness.

## Method

The authors employ the light-cone dipole formalism to calculate cross sections for various large rapidity gap channels in proton-nucleus interactions, incorporating inelastic shadowing corrections to all orders of multiple interaction within the Glauber-Gribov theory. They test four model combinations using two proton wave function models (quark-diquark and symmetric three-quark) and two dipole cross section models (quadratic in transverse size and saturated). Numerical calculations are performed for the energies of the HERA-B experiment (sqrt(s)=41.6 GeV), RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV), and LHC (sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of forward single diffractive to forward elastic cross section in pp collisions at HERA-B energy is R_sd = 0.065, extracted from experimental data as 5.5 mb/GeV^2 divided by 84.5 mb/GeV^2
- The triple-Pomeron coupling parameters from a triple-Regge analysis give G_3Pom(0) = G_PomPomReg(0) = 3.2 mb/GeV^2 and G_RegRegPom(0) = 13.2 mb/GeV^2
- The integrated single diffractive pp cross section at HERA-B energy is 3.27 mb, with the PomPomReg (valence quark excitation) part contributing 1.13 mb
- For Model III (quark-diquark proton with saturated cross section), the diffraction-to-elastic ratio R_sd = 0.13 at HERA-B energy, decreasing to 0.06 at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and 0.01 at LHC (sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV)
- For Model IV (symmetric proton with saturated cross section), R_sd = 0.07 at HERA-B energy, decreasing to 0.03 at RHIC and 0.0045 at LHC
- The mean transverse size of gluonic spots in the proton is fitted to be about 0.3 fm
- The pion-to-proton total cross section ratio at HERA-B energy is R_pi/p = 0.6, rising to 0.66 at LHC energy

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-Gribov theory of nuclear interactions to large rapidity gap processes, building on the light-cone dipole formalism developed by Kopeliovich and collaborators (ZKL). The authors use the saturated dipole cross section parametrization fitted to hadronic cross sections and low-Q^2 HERA data (KST2), which contrasts with the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (GBW) parametrization that cannot reproduce the correct energy dependence of hadronic cross sections. The results enable reliable calculations of the fraction of inelastic cross section missed by central-rapidity triggers at HERA-B, RHIC, and LHC experiments, and provide sensitive tests of the gluonic structure of the proton through diffractive processes.
