# J/$ψ$ suppression as a function of the energy of the zero degree calorimeter : can it discriminate between deconfining and comovers interaction models ?

> The ratio of $J/ψ$ over Drell-Yan cross-sections as a function of the energy of the zero degree calorimeter in $Pb$ $Pb$ collisions has been computed in a comovers interaction model and compared with the results of a deconfining model. The predictions of the two models are different both for peripheral events and for very central ones. These differences are analyzed and the results of the models confronted with available data -- not only for $Pb$ $Pb$ but also for $pA$ and $SU$ collisions.

# J/ψ suppression as a function of the energy of the zero degree calorimeter: can it discriminate between deconfining and comovers interaction models?

## Key claim
The ratio of J/ψ to Drell-Yan cross-sections as a function of the energy of the zero degree calorimeter (E_ZDC) can discriminate between deconfining and comovers interaction models, provided that the shape and absolute normalization of the data become better known; the present data favor the comovers model for peripheral events but do not yet allow a clear-cut discrimination for central events.

## Method
The authors computed the J/ψ to Drell-Yan ratio as a function of E_ZDC in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon using a comovers interaction model (with σ_abs = 4.5 mb and σ_co = 1 mb) and a deconfining model, both incorporating fluctuations in transverse energy (E_T). They derived the E_T-E_ZDC correlation from the Dual Parton Model, relating the number of participants and spectators to the measured calorimeter energies, and applied this correlation to convert the model predictions from E_T to E_ZDC. The results were compared with NA50 data for Pb-Pb, as well as with pA and S-U collisions.

## Key numbers
- Absorptive cross-section in the comovers model: σ_abs = 4.5 mb (compared to the NA50 nuclear absorption value of σ_abs = 6.4 ± 0.8 mb)
- Comover cross-section: σ_co = 1 mb
- Coefficient q relating E_T to charged multiplicity: q = 0.62 GeV (for 1998 data) and q = 0.69 GeV (for S-U at 200 GeV/c per nucleon)
- Contamination parameter α for secondaries in E_ZDC: α = 0.076
- Width parameter a for E_T fluctuations: a = 0.60 (1998 data) and a = 0.852 (1996 data)
- Absolute normalization of J/ψ to DY ratio: 46.8 for pp, pA, and S-U; 59.4 for Pb-Pb (a 27% difference, of which 9% is attributed to energy and isospin rescaling, leaving a 17% discrepancy)
- A-dependence exponent α for pA: α = 0.943 (compared to NA38 value α = 0.919 ± 0.015 and E866 value α = 0.955 ± 0.02 ± 1% systematics)
- Beam energy: E_in = 158 GeV

## Context
This work extends the debate between deconfining models (e.g., those requiring sharp thresholds as in the NA50 interpretation) and comovers interaction models (Capella et al., 2000) for explaining J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions. The NA50 collaboration had interpreted the data as showing a sudden onset of anomalous suppression at E_T ~ 40 GeV and a second change at E_T ~ 90-100 GeV, with nuclear absorption alone (σ_abs = 6.4 mb) describing pA, S-U, and peripheral Pb-Pb data. The present paper shows that the comovers model, which naturally produces a steeper suppression for peripheral events, is favored by the E_ZDC data in that region, while the deconfining model's stronger fall-off for central events cannot yet be ruled out. The analysis enables future discrimination with higher-statistics data (e.g., the 2000 NA50 run) and clarifies that the E_T-loss effect, which complicated comparisons using the minimum-bias analysis, is avoided when using E_ZDC.
