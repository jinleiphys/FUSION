# Quarkonium Physics at a Fixed-Target Experiment using the LHC Beams

> We outline the many quarkonium-physics opportunities offered by a multi-purpose fixed-target experiment using the p and Pb LHC beams extracted by a bent crystal. This provides an integrated luminosity of 0.5 fb-1 per year on a typical 1cm-long target. Such an extraction mode does not alter the performance of the collider experiments at the LHC. With such a high luminosity, one can analyse quarkonium production in great details in pp, pd and pA collisions at sqrt(sNN)~115 GeV and at sqrt(sNN)~72 GeV in PbA collisions. In a typical pp (pA) run, the obtained quarkonium yields per unit of rapidity are 2-3 orders of magnitude larger than those expected at RHIC and about respectively 10 (70) times larger than for ALICE. In PbA, they are comparable. By instrumenting the target-rapidity region, the large negative-xF domain can be accessed for the first time, greatly extending previous measurements by Hera-B and E866. Such analyses should help resolving the quarkonium-production controversies and clear the way for gluon PDF extraction via quarkonium studies. The nuclear target-species versatility provides a unique opportunity to study nuclear matter and the features of the hot and dense matter formed in PbA collisions. A polarised proton target allows the study of transverse-spin asymmetries in J/psi and Upsilon production, providing access to the gluon and charm Sivers functions.

## Key claim

A fixed-target experiment using bent-crystal-extracted LHC proton and lead beams can achieve quarkonium yields 2-3 orders of magnitude larger than at RHIC and about 10 (70) times larger than ALICE in pp (pA) runs, but only if the extraction mode does not alter LHC collider performance and the target-rapidity region is instrumented to access the large negative-xF domain for the first time.

## Method

The authors propose extracting 7 TeV proton and Pb LHC beams via a bent crystal to strike fixed targets (hydrogen, deuterium, nuclear targets, or polarised proton targets), achieving sqrt(sNN) approximately 115 GeV for pp/pA collisions and 72 GeV for PbA collisions. They estimate yields per unit of rapidity for J/psi and Upsilon production based on an integrated luminosity of 0.5 fb-1 per year on a 1 cm-long target, comparing these to existing and planned experiments at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity of 0.5 fb-1 per year on a typical 1 cm-long target
- Center-of-mass energy sqrt(sNN) approximately 115 GeV for pp and pA collisions
- Center-of-mass energy sqrt(sNN) approximately 72 GeV for PbA collisions
- J/psi yields per unit of rapidity at y=0: 5.2 x 10^8 per year on a 100 cm solid hydrogen target; 1.1 x 10^9 per year on a 1 cm tungsten target; 6.7 x 10^8 per year on a 1 cm lead target
- Upsilon yields per unit of rapidity at y=0: 1.0 x 10^6 per year on a 100 cm solid hydrogen target; 2.3 x 10^6 per year on a 1 cm tungsten target; 1.3 x 10^6 per year on a 1 cm lead target
- For comparison: RHIC pp at 200 GeV yields 4.8 x 10^5 J/psi and 1.2 x 10^3 Upsilon per year per unit rapidity; RHIC dAu at 62 GeV yields 1.2 x 10^4 J/psi and 1.8 x 10^1 Upsilon per year per unit rapidity
- LHC low-PT pp at 14 TeV yields 3.6 x 10^7 J/psi and 1.8 x 10^5 Upsilon per year per unit rapidity (for 0.05 fb-1 integrated luminosity)

## Context

This paper extends the fixed-target quarkonium program pioneered by experiments such as E866 (Leitch, 1999) and HERA-B (Abt, 2008), which were limited in rapidity coverage and statistical precision. The proposed AFTER experiment would bridge the energy gap between SPS and RHIC, enabling simultaneous charmonium and bottomonium measurements across a wide range of nuclear targets. This would help resolve the long-standing quarkonium production puzzles that have persisted since the CDF measurements (Abe, 1997) challenged the Colour-Singlet Model, and would enable gluon PDF extraction at mid and large x where current constraints from DIS experiments are poor.
