# Relativistic and Non-Relativistic Mean Field Investigation of the Superdeformed Bands in $^{62}$Zn

> Following the discovery of the superdeformed(SD) band in $^{62}$Zn, we calculate several low-lying SD bands in $^{62}$Zn using Relativistic Mean Field and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models. Both models predict similar results, but still we can see some qualitative differences in the results of these two models, which are coming from the difference of the detail of single-particle levels.

## Key claim
Both the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models identify the same configurations (D3 and D4) as the best candidates for the experimentally observed superdeformed band in ⁶²Zn, but the two models show qualitative differences in deformation parameters and single-particle level ordering that prevent a definitive conclusion about which model better describes these very high spin states.

## Method
The authors performed cranked Relativistic Mean Field calculations using the NL-SH parameter set and cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the SLy4 parameter set (with the HFODD code) for several low-lying superdeformed bands in ⁶²Zn. They solved the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields in a uniformly rotating frame using the tetrad formalism, with the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis cutoff at N_F=8 for nucleons and N_B=10 for mesons. The configurations considered were A (2 protons, 2 neutrons in N_osc=4 orbits), D (2 protons, 3 neutrons in N_osc=4 orbits, with four sub-configurations D1-D4), and C (2 protons, 4 neutrons in N_osc=4 orbits).

## Key numbers
- Experimental quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.45⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₇ for the superdeformed band in ⁶²Zn
- RMF calculation at Ω ≈ 1.3 MeV gives β₂ ≈ 0.37 (band A), 0.49 (band D3), and 0.46 (band D4)
- SHF calculation at Ω ≈ 1.3 MeV gives β₂ ≈ 0.43 (band A), 0.50 (band D3), and 0.48 (band D4)
- The SD states become yrast at I ≳ 24, corresponding to rotational frequency Ω ≳ 1.3 MeV, the highest frequency observed among SD bands at the time
- Both RMF and SHF calculations for ⁶⁰Zn give β₂ = 0.46 at Ω = 1.3 MeV for band A, consistent with the reference value β₂ = 0.47 (ε₂ = 0.41) from Ragnarsson (1990)

## Context
This work extends the application of Relativistic Mean Field theory to superdeformed bands in the A ≈ 60 mass region for the first time, following the experimental discovery of the SD band in ⁶²Zn by Svensson et al. (1997). The calculations are compared with the configuration-dependent shell correction approach of Svensson et al. (1997, 1998) and with previous RMF calculations by the Munich group (Koenig and Ring, 1989, 1990; Afanasjev et al., 1996) for SD bands in the A ≈ 150, 80, and 190 mass regions. The results enable future systematic investigations of many nuclei in this mass region using various parameter sets of both RMF and SHF models, and highlight the need for experimental parity and signature assignments to distinguish between the candidate configurations D3 and D4.
