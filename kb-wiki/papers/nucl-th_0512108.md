# $Λ(1520)$ at finite density

> We study the decay channels of the $Λ(1520)$ in a nuclear medium and find a sizable change - of the order of factor five - of the width of this hyperon at normal nuclear matter density. The mass shift of the $Λ(1520)$ is moderate.

## Key claim

The width of the Λ(1520) hyperon increases by approximately a factor of five at normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), while its mass shift is only moderate (δm ≈ −30 MeV). This dramatic broadening arises primarily because the decay channel Λ(1520) → π Σ*(1385), which is forbidden in free space for nominal masses, opens in the nuclear medium when the pion can excite particle-hole and Δ(1232)-hole states.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium self-energy of the Λ(1520) by including its d-wave decay channels (Λ(1520) → K̄N and Λ(1520) → π Σ) and the s-wave decay Λ(1520) → π Σ*(1385), which is forbidden in vacuum but opens in the medium. The pion and antikaon propagators in the loops are renormalized by coupling to particle-hole, Δ(1232)-hole, and hyperon-hole excitations, respectively, with intermediate baryons dressed using phenomenological optical potentials that account for nuclear binding, Pauli blocking, and short-range correlations of the Landau-Migdal type. The Σ*(1385) itself is renormalized in the medium through its π Σ and π Λ decay channels, with its width increasing by a factor of 2–3 at normal density.

## Key numbers

- Free width of Λ(1520): Γ_free ≈ 15.6 MeV
- Width of Λ(1520) at normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³): increases by factor ∼5 relative to free width
- Mass shift of Λ(1520) at normal density: δm ≈ −30 MeV
- Free width of Σ*(1385): Γ_free ≈ 35 MeV
- Width of Σ*(1385) at normal density: increases by factor ∼2–3 relative to free width
- Mass shift of Σ*(1385) at normal density: δm ≈ −40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Kaskulov and Oset (2005) for the in-medium renormalization of the Λ(1520), building on the chiral unitary approach where the Λ(1520) is dynamically generated as a quasibound state of the π Σ*(1385) system (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2004; Sarkar et al., 2005). The study parallels earlier work on the Δ(1232) in nuclear matter, where pion renormalization via ph and Δh excitations leads to medium modifications. The predicted large increase in width should be experimentally verifiable in reactions such as γ A → K⁺ Λ(1520) A′ and p A → p′ K⁺ Λ(1520) A′, where a sizable A-dependence linked to the in-medium width modification is expected.
