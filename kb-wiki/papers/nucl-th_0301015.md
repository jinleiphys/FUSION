# Systematic Regge theory analysis of omega photoproduction

> Systematic analysis of available data for $ω$-meson photoproduction is given in frame of Regge theory. At photon energies above 20 GeV the $γ{+}p{\to}ω{+}p$ reaction is entirely dominated by Pomeron exchange. However, it was found that Pomeron exchange model can not reproduce the $γ{+}p{\to}ρ{+}p$ and $γ{+}p{\to}ω{+}p$ data at high energies simultaneously with the same set of parameters. The comparison between $ρ$ and $ω$ data indicates a large room for meson exchange contribution to $ω$-meson photoproduction at low energies. It was found that at low energies the dominant contribution comes from $π$ and $f_2$-meson exchanges. There is smooth transition between the meson exchange model at low energies and Regge theory at high energies.

## Key claim

At photon energies above 20 GeV the reaction is entirely dominated by Pomeron exchange, but the Pomeron exchange model cannot reproduce the gamma + p -> rho + p and gamma + p -> omega + p data simultaneously with the same set of parameters, indicating that the Pomeron coupling to omega photoproduction differs from that for rho photoproduction.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic Regge theory analysis of available omega-meson photoproduction data, parameterizing the scattering amplitudes for soft Pomeron, hard Pomeron, f2-meson, and pi-meson exchanges. They fitted the normalization constants for each exchange channel to the differential and total cross section data across photon energies from 1.4 GeV to 225 GeV, and compared the resulting parameter sets with those previously established for rho-meson photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The soft Pomeron trajectory parameters are epsilon = 0.08 and alpha_prime_P1 = 0.25 GeV^{-2}, with the coupling constant readjusted to beta_0 = 2.35 GeV^{-1} for omega photoproduction (compared to beta_0 = 2.0 GeV^{-1} used for rho photoproduction)
- The phenomenological Pomeron exchange normalization constant for omega photoproduction is A_P1 = 0.02 fm/GeV, while for rho photoproduction it is A_P1 = 0.06 fm/GeV
- The hard Pomeron normalization constant for omega photoproduction is A_P0 = 1.2 x 10^{-4} fm/GeV, compared to A_P0 = 3.6 x 10^{-4} fm/GeV for rho photoproduction
- The f2-meson exchange parameter for omega photoproduction is A_f = 0.053 fm/GeV, scaled by a factor of 3 from the rho photoproduction value of A_f = 0.159 fm/GeV
- The pi-meson exchange parameter is A_pi = 0.1 fm/GeV, adjusted to reproduce the differential cross section at E_gamma = 4.7 GeV
- The ratio of gamma + p -> omega + p to gamma + p -> rho^0 + p cross sections at photon energies above 6 GeV is consistent with the SU(3) prediction of 1/9
- The gamma-omega coupling is 3.4 +/- 0.2 times smaller than the gamma-rho coupling based on radiative decay widths

## Context

This work extends the Regge theory analyses previously applied to rho-meson and phi-meson photoproduction by Donnachie and Landshoff (1990s) and by Laget (1990s), who had found that calculations with pi, f2, and Pomeron trajectories underestimated the omega photoproduction total cross section at E_gamma above 50 GeV. The analysis shows that while Pomeron exchange dominates above 20 GeV, the omega photoproduction parameters differ from those for rho photoproduction, and at low energies the reaction is dominated by pi and f2 exchanges. The results enable a unified description of omega photoproduction from low to high energies, with a smooth transition between the meson exchange model of Sibirtsev (2002) at low energies and Regge theory at high energies.
