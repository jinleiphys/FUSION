# About one possibility of relativistic description of polarized deutron fragmentation

> In the framework of the light-front quantum theory developed by Karmanov et al. an analysis of the experimental data on the tensor analyzing power of the nuclear fragmentation of relativistic deuterons with the large transversal momentum proton emission has been made. With the Karmanov's wave function taken in system in which z-axis directed along the deuteron beam we have managed to explain the existing data without invoking additional to nucleons degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
Using Karmanov's relativistic light-front deuteron wave function with the quantization axis (z-axis) directed along the deuteron beam, the authors can describe the experimental data on the tensor analyzing power of deuteron fragmentation without invoking additional degrees of freedom beyond nucleons, but only within the approximate calculations that exploit the dependence on the choice of the light-front surface orientation.

## Method
The authors analyzed the tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}\) of the reactions \(^{9}\text{Be}(d,p)X\) at 4.5 GeV/\(c\) and \(^{12}\text{C}(d,p)X\) at 9 GeV/\(c\) using the light-front dynamics formalism. They employed Karmanov's relativistic deuteron wave function, which depends on six invariant functions of the internal momentum \(k\) and the angle between \(\mathbf{k}\) and the light-front normal \(\mathbf{n}\), and compared the results to calculations using standard non-relativistic deuteron wave functions from the Bonn B and Paris potentials.

## Key numbers
- For the reaction \(^{9}\text{Be}(d,p)X\) at an initial deuteron momentum of 4.5 GeV/\(c\) and a proton emission angle of 80 mr, calculations with Karmanov's wave function qualitatively reproduce the experimental data, whereas standard wave functions (Bonn B, Paris) change sign at a proton momentum of approximately 3.2 GeV/\(c\).
- For the reaction \(^{12}\text{C}(d,p)X\) at an initial deuteron momentum of 9 GeV/\(c\) and a proton emission angle of 85 mr, the momentum dependence calculated with Karmanov's wave function is very close to the experimental points, while standard non-relativistic wave functions are in sharp contradiction with the data.
- The internal momenta of nucleons in the deuteron considered in this analysis reach values of \(k \sim 0.5 - 0.8\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context
This work extends the light-front dynamics approach of Karmanov et al. (Karmanov, 2000) to the description of polarization observables in deuteron fragmentation, specifically addressing the failure of standard non-relativistic deuteron wave functions (Bonn B, Paris) to reproduce the tensor analyzing power \(A_{yy}\) data from Saclay and Dubna experiments. The results enable a simpler interpretation of the deuteron as a two-nucleon system at short distances (internal momenta up to 0.8 GeV/\(c\)) without invoking exotic degrees of freedom, and suggest that relativistic effects in the fragmentation process can be accounted for via light-front dynamics.
