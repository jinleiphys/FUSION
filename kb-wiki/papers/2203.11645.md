# Confronting a set of Skyrme and $χ_{EFT}$ predictions for the crust of neutron stars

> With the improved accuracy of neutron star observational data, it is necessary to derive new equation of state where the crust and the core are consistently calculated within a unified approach. For this purpose we describe non-uniform matter in the crust of neutron stars employing a compressible liquid-drop model, where the bulk and the neutron fluid terms are given from the same model as the one describing uniform matter present in the core. We then generate a set of fifteen unified equations of state for cold catalyzed neutron stars built on realistic modelings of the nuclear interaction, which belongs to two main groups: the first one derives from the phenomenological Skyrme interaction and the second one from $χ_{EFT}$ Hamiltonians. The confrontation of these model predictions allows us to investigate the model dependence for the crust properties, and in particular the effect of neutron matter at low density. The new set of unified equations of state is available at the CompOSE repository.

## Key claim
The paper confronts fifteen unified equations of state for neutron stars built from two groups of nuclear interactions (phenomenological Skyrme and chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians) to evaluate model dependence in crust properties, finding that Skyrme models are better constrained for symmetric matter while chiral EFT models are better constrained for neutron matter at low density.

## Method
The authors employ a compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) to describe non-uniform matter in the neutron star crust, where the bulk and neutron fluid terms come from the same model used for the core, ensuring a unified approach. They generate fifteen unified equations of state by fitting a meta-model to predictions from seven Skyrme parametrizations and eight chiral EFT Hamiltonians, then optimize CLDM parameters (surface, curvature, Coulomb coefficients) against experimental nuclear masses from the 2016 Atomic Mass Evaluation for nuclei with A ≥ 12 and Z ≥ 6.

## Key numbers
- Fifteen unified equations of state generated for cold catalyzed neutron stars, available at the CompOSE repository
- Seven Skyrme parametrizations and eight chiral EFT Hamiltonians compared
- For Skyrme models: saturation energy E_sat ranges from -15.3 MeV (H3_mm) to -16.1 MeV (BSK16_mm); saturation density n_sat ranges from 0.158 fm^-3 (SGII_mm) to 0.162 fm^-3 (F0_mm)
- For chiral EFT models: E_sat ranges from -13.2 MeV (H7_mm) to -17.0 MeV (H1_mm); n_sat ranges from 0.139 fm^-3 (H7_mm) to 0.186 fm^-3 (H1_mm)
- Symmetry energy L_sym ranges from 32.4 MeV (RATP_mm) to 50.9 MeV (LNS5_mm) for Skyrme models, and from 36.5 MeV (H7_mm) to 58.9 MeV (DHS_L59_mm) for chiral EFT models
- χ_E fit quality for nuclear masses ranges from 2.59 MeV (SLy5_mm) to 5.32 MeV (H7_mm) for models with odd-even staggering correction
- Minimal nuclear binding energy for Skyrme models: -8.75 MeV (LNS5_mm, SGII_mm) at (68,30); for chiral EFT models: -10.23 MeV (H7_mm) at (56,26)
- PREX-II constraint gives E_sym = 38.1 ± 4.7 MeV and L_sym = 106 ± 37 MeV

## Context
This work extends the unified equation of state approach previously applied by Carreau (2019) and Grams (2021) to include a broader set of Skyrme interactions (BSK14, BSK16, F0, LNS5, RATP, SGII, SLy5) and chiral EFT Hamiltonians (H1-H7, DHS_L59, DHS_L69), building on the meta-model framework of Margueron (2018). The results enable systematic comparison of crust properties across different nuclear interaction models and provide ready-to-use equations of state for astrophysical simulations, addressing the 3-5% theoretical uncertainty in neutron star radius predictions from inconsistent crust-core treatments identified by Fortin (2016).
