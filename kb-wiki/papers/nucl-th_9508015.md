# Multifragmentation in alpha(5GeV/u) + Au Reactions

> We simulate the fragmentation processes in the \alAu collisions at the bombarding energy 5 GeV/u using the simplified RQMD approach. The statistical decay calculation is connected to obtain the final state. >From the simulation we find that the residual nucleus created by this collision has a annular eclipse shape, and then expands more strongly to the transverse direction than to the beam-direction. Then the angular-distribution of the intermediate mass fragments has a sideward peak; this result explains the experimental result.

# Multifragmentation in alpha(5GeV/u) + Au Reactions

## Key claim
The simplified Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD/S) simulation suggests that the experimentally observed sideward peak in intermediate mass fragment (IMF) angular distributions at θ_lab ≈ 70° arises from residual nuclei with an annular eclipse or partial eclipse shape that expand more strongly in the transverse direction than along the beam direction, rather than from isotropic thermal decay of a hot compound nucleus.

## Method
The authors used a simplified RQMD approach (RQMD/S) that avoids full Lorentz covariance by equalizing all baryon and meson time coordinates, enabling energy conservation during meson production and absorption. They simulated α + Au collisions at 5 GeV/u using a Skyrme-type mean-field with hard equation of state (incompressibility K = 380 MeV), Cugnon's parameterization for elastic baryon-baryon cross-sections, and Wolf's parameterization for inelastic channels including Δ, N*(1440), and N*(1535) resonances decaying into nucleons, pions, and η mesons. After the dynamical stage, each fragment was boosted to its rest frame, its excitation energy evaluated, and statistical decay calculated to obtain the final fragment distribution.

## Key numbers
- Bombarding energy: 5 GeV/u for α projectiles on Au targets
- Incompressibility of the Skyrme-type mean-field: K = 380 MeV (hard EOS)
- Twenty simulations performed at impact parameter b = 0 fm for the annular eclipse shape analysis
- Events restricted to impact parameter b < 6 fm for angular-distribution calculations
- IMF angular-distribution peak for fragments with 5 ≤ Z ≤ 20 appears at θ_lab = 80°
- Experimental data show IMF angular-distribution peaks at θ_lab = 70°
- Time step for orbital space projections: every 4 fm/c
- Annular eclipse shape appears at t = 20 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the QMD approach of Maruyama et al. (1994), which successfully reproduced heavy-ion collision data at several tens of MeV/u, to the relativistic energy regime using the RQMD framework developed by Sorge (1993) and Maruyama (1994). The study directly addresses experimental results from the KEK group for proton and alpha induced reactions at 12 GeV and 5 GeV/u respectively. The simplified RQMD approach enables investigation of multifragmentation as a probe of the nuclear equation of state and liquid-gas phase transition in low density nuclear matter, while avoiding the CPU time and energy conservation problems of full RQMD. The results suggest that selecting central collision events could provide detailed information about hot nuclei with annular eclipse shapes.
