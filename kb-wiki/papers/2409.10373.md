# Lévy walk of pions in heavy-ion collisions

> The process of Lévy walk, i.e., movement patterns described by heavy-tailed random walks, plays a role in various phenomena, from chemical and microbiological systems through marine predators to climate change. Recent experiments have suggested that this phenomenon also appears in heavy-ion collisions. However, the theoretical interpretation supporting such findings is still debated. In high-energy collisions of heavy nuclei, the strongly interacting Quark Gluon Plasma is created, which, similarly to the early Universe, undergoes a rapid expansion and transition back to hadronic matter. In the subsequent expanding hadron gas, particles interact until kinetic freeze-out, when their momenta stop changing, and they freely transition toward the detectors. Measuring spatial freeze-out distributions is a crucial tool in understanding the dynamics of the created matter and the interactions among its constituents. In this paper, we introduce a three-dimensional analysis of the spatial freeze-out distribution of pions (the most abundant particles in such collisions). Utilising Monte-Carlo simulations of high-energy collisions, we show that the chain of processes ending in a final state pion has a step length distribution leading to Lévy-stable distributions. Subsequently, we show that simulated pion freeze-out distributions indeed exhibit heavy tails and can be described by a three-dimensional elliptically contoured symmetric Lévy-stable distribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the spatial freeze-out distribution of pions in heavy-ion collisions can be described by a three-dimensional elliptically contoured symmetric Levy-stable distribution, but the Levy index extracted from EPOS simulations is significantly larger than experimental values from PHENIX, indicating that Levy walks alone may not fully explain the observed heavy tails in real collisions.

## Method

The authors analyzed 100 UrQMD-simulated gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to track the full interaction history of final-state pions and compute step length distributions for four process types (scattering, decay, coalescence, string fragmentation). They then simulated 200 GeV Au+Au collisions with the EPOS model and performed event-by-event fits of three-dimensional elliptically contoured Levy-stable distributions to the pair freeze-out distance distributions in the Bertsch-Pratt coordinate system, extracting the Levy index alpha and scale parameters R_out, R_side, and R_long as functions of pair transverse mass.

## Key numbers

- The tail of the total step-size distribution from UrQMD exhibits a power-law exponent of -1.69
- The fits to the step length distribution covered the range of 400 fm to 200,000 fm, spanning more than two orders of magnitude
- The maximum impact parameter for UrQMD collisions was set to 4.7 fm, corresponding to the 0-10% centrality class
- The EPOS simulations used 10% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Context

This work extends experimental observations from SPS (NA61/SHINE 2023), RHIC (PHENIX 2017, 2024), and LHC (CMS 2023) that found non-Gaussian freeze-out distributions characterized by Levy-stable distributions. The study provides a theoretical foundation for these observations by demonstrating that hadronic rescattering and decays naturally produce Levy-walk behavior, and it is the first to show that three-dimensional Levy-stable sources appear in event-by-event analyses rather than from averaging effects. The results enable more appropriate extraction of spatial particle distributions from femtoscopic data and point toward needed improvements in simulations, particularly regarding Coulomb interactions which have been shown by Kiselev (2021) to lead to Levy flight.
