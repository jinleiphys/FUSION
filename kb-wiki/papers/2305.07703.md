# The Hydrohedron: Bootstrapping Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> As an effective theory, relativistic hydrodynamics is fixed by symmetries up to a set of transport coefficients. A lot of effort has been devoted to explicit calculations of these coefficients. Here we propose a shift in perspective: we deploy bootstrap techniques to rule out theories that are inconsistent with microscopic causality. What remains is a universal convex geometry in the space of transport coefficients, which we call the hydrohedron. The landscape of all consistent theories necessarily lie inside or on the edges of the hydrohedron. We analytically construct cross-sections of the hydrohedron corresponding to bounds on transport coefficients that appear in sound and diffusion modes for theories without stochastic fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that imposing microscopic causality on relativistic hydrodynamics, via a condition on retarded Green's functions, carves out a universal convex geometry in the space of transport coefficients (the "hydrohedron") that all consistent theories must lie inside or on; the analysis is restricted to theories without stochastic fluctuations and uses only analyticity of the mode functions at zero wave-vector and the causality condition.

## Method

The authors impose the causality condition \(v_{\text{LC}} |\text{Im}\, k| - \text{Im}\, \omega(k) \geq 0\) on hydrodynamic dispersion relations \(\omega(k)\) expanded as Taylor series in the wave-vector \(k\) around \(k=0\), with a finite radius of convergence \(R\). They multiply this condition by non-negative periodic functions and integrate around a circle of radius \(r < R\), converting the problem into a moment problem that yields an infinite hierarchy of inequalities on the dimensionless transport coefficients \(R^{n-1}c_n\). The analysis is specialized to two cross-sections: a diffusive mode (odd powers of \(k\) vanish, even coefficients purely imaginary) and a sound mode (odd coefficients real, even coefficients purely imaginary).

## Key numbers

* For the diffusion cross-section, the first bound on the dimensionless diffusivity is \(-\frac{16}{3\pi} \leq R\beta_2 \leq 0\), where \(\beta_2\) is the diffusion constant (with \(D \equiv -\beta_2\)).
* For the diffusion cross-section, the second bound is \(-\frac{64}{15\pi} \leq R^3\beta_4 \leq \frac{256 - 15\pi R\beta_2 (8 + 3\pi R\beta_2)}{90\pi}\).
* For the sound cross-section, the speed of sound \(\alpha_1\) satisfies \(|\alpha_1| \leq 1\).
* For the sound cross-section, the bound on sound attenuation is \(-\frac{16}{3\pi} + \frac{\pi}{2}\alpha_1^2 \leq R\beta_2 \leq 0\), where \(\beta_2 = -\Gamma_s/2\) and \(\Gamma_s\) is the sound attenuation length.
* For a \(d\)-dimensional conformal field theory with \(|\alpha_1| = 1/\sqrt{d-1}\), the sound attenuation bound becomes \(R\Gamma_s \leq \frac{32}{3\pi} - \frac{\pi}{d-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous result (Heller 2022) that causality implies a finite radius of convergence \(R\) for hydrodynamic series and two-sided bounds on all \(R^{n-1}c_n\). It applies bootstrap techniques, analogous to the modern conformal bootstrap (Rattazzi 2008, Poland 2018) and S-matrix bootstrap (Arkani-Hamed 2020, Caron-Huot 2022), to hydrodynamics. The resulting hydrohedron provides universal constraints independent of spacetime dimension or microscopic theory, and the bounds are compared to the KSS bound (Kovtun 2004) and Planckian bounds (Hartnoll 2014, Blake 2016), which are not consequences of causality alone. The work enables future inclusion of stochastic fluctuations and application to other mode types such as Goldstone modes and quasinormal modes.
