# Nuclear tetrahedral configurations at spin zero

> The possibility of the existence of stable tetrahedral deformations at spin zero is investigated using the Skyrme-HFBCS approach and the generator coordinate method (GCM). The study is limited to nuclei in which the tetrahedral mode has been predicted to be favored on the basis of non self-consistent models. Our results indicate that a clear identification of tetrahedral deformations is unlikely as they are strongly mixed with the axial octupole mode. However, the excitation energies related to the tetrahedral mode are systematically lower than those of the axial octupole mode in all the nuclei included in this study.

# Nuclear tetrahedral configurations at spin zero

## Key claim
Using self-consistent Skyrme-HFBCS calculations and the generator coordinate method (GCM), the authors find that stable tetrahedral deformations at spin zero are unlikely to be clearly identifiable, because they are strongly mixed with the axial octupole mode. However, the excitation energies of the tetrahedral mode are systematically lower than those of the axial octupole mode across all nuclei studied.

## Method
The authors performed constrained Skyrme-HFBCS mean-field calculations with the axial octupole moment Q30 and the triaxial octupole moment Q32 (which generates tetrahedral deformations) as constraining operators, using three Skyrme parametrizations (SLy4, SkM*, SIII). They then carried out two-dimensional GCM calculations coupling the axial and tetrahedral octupole modes, with particle-number projection and parity projection applied to the mean-field wave functions, to study the dynamical coupling between these modes in the vicinity of the spherical configuration.

## Key numbers
- For 156Gd, a tetrahedral minimum with an energy gain of 500 keV relative to the spherical configuration was obtained at the mean-field level.
- For 110Zr with the SIII interaction, the tetrahedral minimum was 100 keV below the spherical point at the mean-field level.
- The correlation energy from octupole fluctuations and parity restoration ranged from 1.184 MeV (98Zr, SIII) to 3.303 MeV (110Zr, SLy4).
- The lowest negative-parity excitation energies from the 2D GCM ranged from 1.072 MeV (156Gd, SLy4) to 2.832 MeV (80Zr, SIII).
- The tetrahedral mode excitation energy was systematically lower than the axial octupole mode; the largest difference was 0.8 MeV for 110Zr and 0.5 MeV for 156Gd.
- The B(E3) ratio for transitions from the tetrahedral state to the ground state relative to the axial octupole state to the ground state ranged from 0.67 (110Zr) to 1.15 (156Gd).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent study of 80,98Zr (Zberecki et al., 2006, 2007) by including the dynamical coupling between axial and tetrahedral octupole modes. It contradicts predictions from non-self-consistent Woods-Saxon potential calculations (Dudek et al., 2002; Dudek et al., 2006) that identified "tetrahedral magic numbers" and suggested stable tetrahedral configurations. The results indicate that experimental detection of tetrahedral deformations at spin zero is unlikely, though the study does not rule out rotating tetrahedral configurations.
