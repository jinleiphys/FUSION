# Adiabatic quantum tunneling in heavy-ion sub-barrier fusion

> High precision measurements of the fusion excitation functions for the reactions ^{40}Ca + ^{194}Pt, ^{192}Os clearly demonstrate that projectile excitation significantly modifies the potential barrier distribution. In sharp contrast, fusion of ^{16}O + ^{144}Sm appears to show no influence of the projectile excitation on the shape of the barrier distribution. These apparently conflicting conclusions are reconciled in this work, using realistic coupled--channels calculations, which show that high energy states produce an adiabatic potential renormalisation. This result indicates that adiabatic effects restrict, in a natural way, the states which influence the shape of a fusion barrier distribution. The analysis of barrier distributions thus offers a criterion for the relevance of the `counter term' prescription in the Caldeira-Leggett approach.

## Key claim

High-energy intrinsic excitations (such as the 6.13 MeV octupole state in 16O) produce an adiabatic potential renormalisation that does not change the shape of the fusion barrier distribution, while lower-energy excitations (such as the 3.70 MeV octupole state in 40Ca) do modify the barrier shape; this resolves the apparent conflict between the 16O + 144Sm and 40Ca + 194Pt/192Os data.

## Method

Realistic coupled-channels calculations were performed for the reactions 16O + 144Sm and 40Ca + 194Pt, 192Os, treating couplings to all orders (i.e., without expanding the nuclear interaction in the deformation parameter) and numerically evaluating coupling matrix elements at each internuclear separation. The calculations used Woods-Saxon nuclear potentials with parameters fitted to reproduce high-energy fusion cross sections, and deformation parameters extracted from reduced transition probabilities. The incoming wave boundary condition was imposed to simulate strong absorption inside the fusion barrier.

## Key numbers

- For 16O + 144Sm: the octupole vibration of 16O has excitation energy 6.13 MeV and deformation parameter beta_3 = 0.733; the octupole vibration of 144Sm has excitation energy 1.81 MeV and beta_3 = 0.205.
- For 40Ca + 194Pt: the octupole vibration of 40Ca has excitation energy 3.70 MeV and beta_3 = 0.339; the target rotational band includes states up to 10+ with beta_2 = -0.154 and beta_4 = -0.045.
- For 40Ca + 192Os: the target rotational band has beta_2 = 0.167 and beta_4 = -0.043.
- The experimental barrier distribution for 16O + 144Sm shows a lower energy peak near 60 MeV and a higher energy peak near 65 MeV.
- Shifting the calculated barrier distribution for 16O + 144Sm by 2 MeV to higher energies reproduces the experimental data when all-order couplings are included.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of barrier distributions from fusion excitation functions, previously applied to surface vibrations (Dasgupta et al., 1994; Leigh et al., 1995), multi-phonon excitations (Stefanini et al., 1995), and rotational excitations (Leigh et al., 1995; Liang et al., 1993), by resolving conflicting conclusions about projectile excitation effects. The results show that simplified coupled-channels codes like CCFUS (Dasso and Landowne, 1987) are unreliable even for weak couplings, and that exact coupled-channels calculations are necessary for quantitative interpretation. The work also connects to the Caldeira-Leggett approach in condensed matter physics by demonstrating when the counter term prescription is relevant for avoiding double-counting of coupling effects in heavy-ion fusion.
