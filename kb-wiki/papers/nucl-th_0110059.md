# HFB Calculations Near the Drip Lines

> We present the first set of results of solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, which describe the self-consistent mean field theory with pairing interaction. Calculations for even-even nuclei are carried out on a two-dimensional axially symmetric lattice, in coordinate space. An important aspect of our method is the proper representation of the quasi-particle continuum wavefunctions, which are considered for energies up to 60 MeV. This stage is essential for a proper description of nuclei near the drip lines, due to the strong coupling between weakly bound states and the particle continuum for such nuclei. High accuracy is achieved by representing the operators and wavefunctions using the technique of basis-splines. Calculations for Sn isotopes are presented to demonstrate the reliability of the method.

## Key claim

The authors present the first set of results from solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a two-dimensional axially symmetric lattice in coordinate space, but the results are limited to even-even nuclei and the method's reliability is demonstrated only for Sn isotopes, with the caveat that the pairing gap shows sensitivity to the chosen box size.

## Method

The HFB equations were solved on a two-dimensional axially symmetric lattice in cylindrical coordinates (r,z) using basis-spline (B-spline) techniques for operator and wavefunction representation. Quasiparticle continuum wavefunctions were included for energies up to 60 MeV, and the Skyrme interaction was used for the particle Hamiltonian with a delta-function pairing interaction. The eigenvalue problem was solved by direct diagonalization with LAPACK, using an iterative self-consistent procedure starting from a HF+BCS calculation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for 120Sn: -1021 MeV (2-D calculation with SkM* interaction), compared to experimental value of -1020 MeV
- Binding energy for 150Sn: -1157 MeV (2-D calculation with SkM* interaction), compared to 1-D value of -1155 MeV
- Neutron Fermi energy for 120Sn: -8.39 MeV (2-D), compared to 1-D value of -7.94 MeV
- Neutron Fermi energy for 150Sn: -1.99 MeV (2-D), compared to 1-D value of -1.86 MeV
- Neutron pairing gap for 120Sn: 1.85 MeV (2-D), compared to 1-D value of 1.256 MeV and experimental value of 1.378 MeV
- Neutron pairing gap for 150Sn: 2.10 MeV (2-D), compared to 1-D value of 1.55 MeV
- Quasiparticle continuum wavefunctions considered for energies up to 60 MeV
- Box size of 20 fm used for 150Sn calculations with 17 points in r and 34 points in z direction
- Maximum Omega quantum number of 17/2 for 150Sn

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional HFB calculations of Dobaczewski (1996) to a two-dimensional axially symmetric framework, enabling treatment of deformed nuclei far from stability. The method addresses the strong coupling between weakly bound states and the particle continuum near the drip lines, which was identified as crucial for convergence. The approach enables future calculations of ground state observables such as total binding energy, charge radii, proton and neutron densities, separation energies, pairing gaps, and potential energy surfaces for exotic nuclei.
