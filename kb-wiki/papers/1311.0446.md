# Thermodynamic Treatment of High Energy Heavy Ion Collision

> The hadron production in heavy ion collision is treated in the framework of thermodynamic vision. Thermodynamic system formed during central collision of Pb-Pb at high energies is considered, through which binary collision is assumed among the valance quarks. The partition function of the system is calculated; accordingly the free available energy, the entropy and the chemical potential are calculated. The concept of string fragmentation and defragmentation are used to form the newly produced particles. The average multiplicity of the newly produced particles are calculated and compared with the recent experimental results.

## Key claim
The paper presents a thermodynamic model for hadron production in central Pb-Pb collisions, where the system is treated as a grand canonical ensemble of valence quarks undergoing binary collisions. The model claims fair agreement with experimental data, but the authors note that predictions exceed measured values by 11-15% at lower energies (20 and 30 A GeV) and underestimate pion and kaon yields at 158 A GeV, with the phi meson yield being double the experimental value due to the assumption that u, d, and s quark pair production are equally probable.

## Method
The authors model the participant zone of central Pb-Pb collisions as a thermodynamic system of valence quarks, using the Boltzmann equation with local equilibrium distribution functions and a 3-parameter quark-quark potential (Coulomb-like, string repulsive, and constant terms). The grand canonical partition function is calculated for binary interacting quarks with a separation-dependent expansion, from which the free energy, entropy, internal energy, and chemical potential are derived. A Monte Carlo simulation based on string fragmentation and defragmentation is then used to generate particle multiplicities, which are compared to experimental data from the NA49 collaboration at incident momenta of 40, 80, and 158 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers
- The effective quark radius used for all collisions is 0.4 fm.
- Calculations are performed for temperatures in the range T ≈ 120-200 MeV.
- The average number of participant nucleons in the central collision region (b ≤ 6 fm) is 250, corresponding to 750 quarks.
- The partition function is calculated for separation distances r_ij of 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 fm, and temperatures of 20, 40, 60, and 200 MeV.
- At energies of 20 and 30 A GeV, the thermodynamic model prediction exceeds measured values by 11-15%.
- At 158 A GeV, the calculated phi meson yield is double the experimental value.

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic treatment of heavy ion collisions previously developed by Hussein et al. (2000) and applies it to Pb-Pb collisions using a quark-level description with binary interactions. The model builds on the Glauber approach for participant nucleon calculation (Hegab et al., 1990) and the string fragmentation model (Hussein et al., 1995). The results are compared with experimental data from the NA49 collaboration (2008, 2012) and discussed in the context of recent STAR measurements on strange hadron production (Zhu, 2009, 2012). The authors note that the model's assumption of equal production probability for u, d, and s quark pairs needs revision, and they plan to study the quark-hadron phase transition through a temperature-chemical potential phase diagram in future work.
