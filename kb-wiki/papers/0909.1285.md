# Reply on "Comments on 'Time modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI experiments'"

> According to experimental data at GSI, the rates of the number of daughter ions, produced by the nuclear K-shell electron capture (EC) decays of the H-like 140Pr58+, 142Pm60+ and 122I52+ ions, are modulated in time with periods T_{EC} of the order of a few seconds, obeying an A-scaling T_{EC} = A/20 s, where A is the mass number of the mother nuclei, and with amplitudes a^{EC}_d ~ 0.21. In turn, the positron decay mode of the H-like 142Pm60+ ions showed no time modulation of the decay rates. As has been shown in Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502 (2009) and Phys. Rev. Lett. 101, 182501 (2008), these data can be explained by the interference of two neutrino mass-eigenstates. In this letter we give a reply on the comments on our paper Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 062502 (2009) by V. Flambaum (arXiv: 0908.2039 [nucl-th]).

# Wiki Page: Reply on “Comments on ‘Time modulation of the K-shell electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions at GSI experiments’”

## Key claim
The time modulation of K-shell electron capture (EC) decay rates observed at GSI for H-like heavy ions arises from interference of massive neutrino mass-eigenstates, and is not eliminated by the asymptotic orthogonality of final-state wave functions, contrary to the assertion by Flambaum (2009). The modulation persists because the time-differential detection of daughter ions, with a resolution much shorter than the modulation periods, introduces energy and momentum uncertainties that cause the wave functions of daughter ions from different decay channels to overlap.

## Method
The authors analyze the EC decay of H-like heavy ions (mother ion m to daughter ion d plus electron neutrino) as a superposition of decay channels m → dⱼ + νⱼ, where νⱼ are massive neutrino mass-eigenstates. They show that when daughter ions are detected with a finite time resolution τ_d, the energy and momentum uncertainties δE_d and |δq_d| satisfy δE_d ≫ |ωᵢⱼ| and |δq_d| ≫ |qᵢ − qⱼ|, where ωᵢⱼ = Δm²ᵢⱼ / (2M_m) are the energy differences between channels. This smearing leads to non-vanishing interference terms in the EC decay rate, producing a time-dependent rate λ_EC(t) = λ_EC (1 + 2 Σᵢ>ⱼ Re[U*ₑᵢ Uₑⱼ] cos(ωᵢⱼ t)). The calculation uses time-dependent perturbation theory and wave packets for the daughter ions, related to the density matrix description of unisolated quantum systems.

## Key numbers
- Modulation periods T_EC for EC decays of H-like ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺, ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺, and ¹²²I⁵²⁺ are of order a few seconds, obeying the scaling T_EC = A/20 s, where A is the mass number of the mother nucleus.
- Amplitudes of the time modulation a^EC_d are approximately 0.21.
- The positron decay mode of H-like ¹⁴²Pm⁶⁰⁺ showed no time modulation of the decay rates.
- The condition for observing modulation is τ_d ≪ T_ij, where T_ij = 2π/ω_ij are the modulation periods; for τ_d ≫ T_ij the modulation vanishes.

## Context
This reply addresses comments by Flambaum (2009) on the authors’ earlier work (Ivanov and Kienle, 2008; 2009) that explained the GSI experimental data (GSI collaboration, 2008) on time-modulated EC decay rates of H-like heavy ions. The GSI experiments observed modulation in EC decays but not in positron decays of the same ions. The authors’ model invokes interference of massive neutrino mass-eigenstates, using the standard 3×3 unitary mixing matrix U with mixing angles θ_ij, and the neutrino oscillation frequencies ω_ij = Δm²_ij / 2E. This work enables further theoretical and experimental investigation of neutrino mass-eigenstate interference in nuclear decay processes, and clarifies the role of detection resolution in observing quantum interference effects in weak decays.
