# The unreasonable weakness of r-process cosmic rays in the neutron-star-merger nucleosynthesis scenario

> We reach the robust conclusion that, by combining the observed cosmic rays of r-process elements with the fact that the velocity of the neutron-star-merger ejecta is much higher than that of the supernova ejecta, either (1) the reverse shock in the neutron-star-merger ejecta is a very inefficient accelerator that converts less than 0.003% of the ejecta kinetic energy to the cosmic-ray energy or (2) the neutron star merger is not the origin of the Galactic r-process elements. We also find that the acceleration efficiency should be less than 0.1% for the reverse shock of the supernova ejecta with the observed cosmic rays lighter than the iron.

## Key claim

By combining observed cosmic-ray composition with the fact that neutron-star-merger ejecta have much higher velocity than supernova ejecta, the authors conclude that either the reverse shock in neutron-star-merger ejecta converts less than 0.003% of ejecta kinetic energy to cosmic-ray energy, or neutron star mergers are not the dominant source of Galactic r-process elements.

## Method

The authors estimate the energy injection rate of r-process cosmic rays at the reverse shock for both supernova and neutron-star-merger scenarios, using the Galactic r-process production rate, event rates, ejecta masses, and ejecta velocities. They compare these estimates to observed cosmic-ray composition data, which show no selective enhancement of r-process elements, to derive upper limits on the acceleration efficiency at the reverse shock.

## Key numbers

- The acceleration efficiency at the reverse shock in neutron-star-merger ejecta must be less than 0.003% (epsilon_CR,r less than or equal to 0.003%) to avoid contradicting observed cosmic-ray composition.
- The acceleration efficiency at the reverse shock for supernova ejecta must be less than 0.1% (eta_r/f less than or equal to 0.1%) based on observed cosmic rays lighter than iron.
- The energy injection rate of r-process cosmic rays from neutron star mergers is approximately 100 times that from supernovae when using the same fiducial parameters (eta_r/f = 0.01, epsilon_CR = 0.1).
- The typical ejecta velocity for neutron star mergers is 0.2c, corresponding to kinetic energy of 3e50 erg, while supernova ejecta velocity is about 0.01c with kinetic energy of 1e51 erg.
- The Galactic r-process production rate is about 1e-6 solar masses per year.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Arnett and Schramm (1973) using cosmic-ray composition to identify nucleosynthesis sites, and builds on studies of neutron-star-merger ejecta evolution by Nakar and Piran (2011) and cosmic-ray acceleration by Kyutoku and Ioka (2013) and Takami and Ioka (2014). The results challenge the neutron-star-merger scenario for r-process nucleosynthesis, which has been supported by nuclear network calculations (Freiburghaus et al. 1999; Wanajo et al. 2014) and Galactic chemical evolution models (Hirai et al. 2015; Shen et al. 2015; van de Voort et al. 2015). The paper enables future work on precise cosmic-ray isotope measurements to separate r-process and s-process elements, and theoretical investigations of reverse-shock acceleration in r-process-dominated ejecta.
