# Holographic quarkyonic matter

> We point out a new configuration in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, allowing baryons in the pointlike approximation to coexist with fundamental quarks. The resulting phase is a holographic realization of quarkyonic matter, which is predicted to occur in QCD at a large number of colors, and possibly plays a role in real-world QCD as well. We find that holographic quarkyonic matter is chirally symmetric and that, for large baryon chemical potentials, it is energetically preferred over pure nuclear matter and over pure quark matter. The zero-temperature transition from nuclear matter to the quarkyonic phase is of first order in the chiral limit and for a realistic pion mass. For pion masses far beyond the physical point we observe a quark-hadron continuity due to the presence of quarkyonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new configuration in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, where pointlike baryons coexist with fundamental quarks, provides a holographic realization of quarkyonic matter; this phase is chirally symmetric and energetically preferred over pure nuclear matter and pure quark matter at large baryon chemical potentials, but only within the specific approximation of pointlike, non-interacting instantons and in the decompactified limit of the model.

## Method

The authors employ the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model in the deconfined geometry and decompactified limit, introducing baryons as pointlike instanton configurations on the flavor branes and quarks via string sources attached to the tip of connected flavor branes or via a nontrivial abelian gauge field on branes reaching the horizon. They solve the equations of motion derived from the Dirac-Born-Infeld action combined with contributions from string sources, pointlike baryons, and a quark mass term, then compare free energies of candidate phases (nuclear matter, quark matter, and quarkyonic matter) across temperature and chemical potential. The analysis includes both the chiral limit and finite pion masses, with the baryon location and densities determined dynamically.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the Hawking-Page transition is \(T_c = M_{\mathrm{KK}}/(2\pi)\), identified with the deconfinement temperature.
* The dimensionless temperature is related to the horizon location by \(t = (3/(4\pi)) \sqrt{u_T}\).
* The dimensionless constituent quark mass is \(M_q = u_c - u_T\).
* The dimensionless baryon mass is \(M_b = (u_b/3) \sqrt{f_T(u_b)}\).
* The pion decay constant in the model is given by \(f_\pi^2 = (32 \lambda N_c M_{\mathrm{KK}}^2)/(3\pi^2 \ell^3) (\Gamma[9/16]/\Gamma[1/16])^3 (\Gamma[11/16]/\Gamma[3/16])\).
* The zero-temperature transition from nuclear matter to the quarkyonic phase is of first order in the chiral limit and for a realistic pion mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quarkyonic matter in large-\(N_c\) QCD, originally proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007), and provides an explicit holographic construction within the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, going beyond earlier holographic approaches that either lacked quark degrees of freedom (Kaplunovsky 2012, 2013) or did not include baryons (Chen 2019). The results enable future improvements by incorporating nonzero instanton widths and interactions, and suggest a possible quark-hadron continuity at large pion masses via the quarkyonic phase, which was speculated about in earlier work by Bitaghsir Fadafan (2018).
