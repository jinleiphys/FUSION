# Magnetic field influence on the early time dynamics of heavy-ion collisions

> In high energy heavy-ion collisions the magnetic field is very strong right after the nuclei penetrate each other and a non-equilibrium system of quarks and gluons builds up. Even though quarks might not be very abundant initially, their dynamics must necessarily be influenced by the Lorentz force. Employing the 3+1d partonic cascade BAMPS we show that the circular Larmor movement of the quarks leads to a strong positive anisotropic flow of quarks at very soft transverse momenta. We explore the regions where the effect is visible, and explicitly show how collisions damp the effect. As a possible application we look at photon production from the flowing non-equilibrium medium.

## Key claim

The magnetic Lorentz force from the strong early-time magnetic field in non-central heavy-ion collisions can generate a large positive elliptic flow (v₂) of quarks at very soft transverse momenta (p_T ≲ 0.1 GeV), but this effect is strongly damped by collisions and is only present if the magnetic field acts on quarks before their formation time has elapsed.

## Method

The authors use the 3+1-dimensional partonic cascade BAMPS, which solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation via Monte-Carlo techniques for massless on-shell quarks and gluons, with an external force term for the electromagnetic Lorentz force. They initialize quarks and gluons with a power-law p_T distribution (exponent n = 2, 3, 4) and a constant rapidity distribution, using a smooth Glauber Monte Carlo geometry for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 8.5 fm. Two simple parametrizations of a homogeneous external magnetic field B_y are applied: a constant field eB_y = 4 m_π² that switches off at t = 0.3 fm/c (param 1), and a time-dependent field eB_y(t) = eB_y⁰ (1 + t²/t_c²)^(-3/2) with t_c = 0.065 fm/c (param 2, based on Deng (2012)).

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field parametrization 1: eB_y = 4 m_π² (constant for t < 0.3 fm/c)
- Magnetic field parametrization 2: eB_y(t) = eB_y⁰ (1 + t²/t_c²)^(-3/2) with t_c = 0.065 fm/c, corresponding to RHIC Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV, impact parameter ~8 fm (20-40% centrality)
- In the collisionless model with param 1, the differential v₂ of quarks reaches up to 80% at very low p_T (around 0.02-0.1 GeV) at forward rapidity
- In BAMPS with collisions (σ_tot = 10 mb) and param 1, the v₂ at p_T = 40-60 MeV is about 20% lower than the collisionless result; after t = 2 fm/c it is around 0.34
- With param 2 (more realistic), the maximal v₂ is about 30%, shifted to even lower p_T than with param 1
- The formation time Δt_f = cosh(y)/p_T, when applied, deletes all relevant magnetic field effects; at t = 0.3 fm/c the minimum momentum at midrapidity is p_T ≈ 0.66 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier analytic studies by Gursoy (2014) who found a strong magnetic (Hall) effect on directed flow at p_T ≲ 0.25 GeV for RHIC and LHC, and by Voronyuk (2011) who modeled a hadron gas under magnetic fields without strong effects. The authors provide an exploratory study of early-time non-equilibrium dynamics of deconfined quarks and gluons, showing that collisions damp the magnetic-field-induced flow. The results suggest that experimental detection of this effect would require access to ultrasoft transverse momenta (p_T ≲ 0.1 GeV), which is currently challenging; photon production from the flowing medium is proposed as a possible probe, though the photon v₂ is smaller than the quark v₂.
