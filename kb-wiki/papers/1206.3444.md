# Charmed baryon resonances with heavy-quark spin symmetry

> Charmed baryon resonances that are generated dynamically are studied within a unitary baryon-meson coupled-channel model, which incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. The extension of the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry plus a suitable symmetry breaking is used. The model produces resonances with negative parity from s-wave interaction of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ baryons. Some of our dynamically generated states can be readily assigned to resonances found experimentally, while others do not have a straightforward identification and require the compilation of more data and also refinements of the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many charmed baryon resonances can be dynamically generated as meson-baryon molecules in a coupled-channel model that respects heavy-quark spin symmetry, but the identification with experimental states is tentative and requires further data and model refinements, especially because the model does not include three-body decay channels that could affect the computed widths.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry, including pseudoscalar and vector mesons coupled to 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons in s-wave. They solve the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels to obtain scattering amplitudes, then extract resonance poles in the complex energy plane. The symmetry breaking follows the chain SU(8) ⊃ SU(6) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SU(2), implemented via deformed mass and decay constant parameters, allowing assignment of SU(8), SU(6), and SU(3) labels and identification of heavy-quark spin symmetry multiplets.

## Key numbers

- Λc(2595) identified with a pole at 2618.8 MeV (width smaller than experimental value, as three-body Λc π π decay not included)
- A second broad Λc resonance at 2617 MeV with large coupling to Σc π, showing the same two-pole pattern as Λ(1405)
- Spin-3/2 Λc resonance at (2666.6 − i 26.7) MeV, assigned to experimental Λc(2625)
- Three Σc resonances (C=1, S=0, I=1, J=1/2) at masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV with widths 0.8, 188.0, and 87.0 MeV respectively
- Spin-3/2 Σc bound state at 2568.4 MeV, thought to be charmed counterpart of Σ(1670)
- Second spin-3/2 Σc resonance at (2692.9 − i 33.5) MeV
- Ξc(2790) candidate identified with pole at (2804.8 − i 13.5) MeV, with large Ξc′ π coupling
- Two spin-3/2 Ξc resonances at (2819.7 − i 16.2) MeV and (2845.2 − i 22.0) MeV, one possibly identified with Ξc(2815)
- Three Ωc bound states (C=1, S=-2, I=0, J=1/2) at masses 2810.9, 2884.5, and 2941.6 MeV
- Two spin-3/2 Ωc bound states at 2814.3 and 2980.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SU(8) spin-flavor symmetric model of Garcia-Recio et al. (2008) and Gamermann et al. (2010) by implementing a symmetry-breaking pattern SU(8) ⊃ SU(6) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SU(2) that respects heavy-quark spin symmetry, unlike earlier coupled-channel approaches such as those of Tolos et al. (2004), Hofmann and Lutz (2005, 2006), and Jimenez-Tejero et al. (2009) which did not incorporate HQSS. The model enables identification of HQSS multiplets among dynamically generated resonances and provides predictions for charmed baryon states with C=2 and C=3 that await experimental confirmation, particularly from the PANDA and CBM experiments at FAIR.
