# Pairing gaps from nuclear mean-field models

> We discuss the pairing gap, a measure for nuclear pairing correlations, in chains of spherical, semi-magic nuclei in the framework of self-consistent nuclear mean-field models. The equations for the conventional BCS model and the approximate projection-before-variation Lipkin-Nogami method are formulated in terms of local density functionals for the effective interaction. We calculate the Lipkin-Nogami corrections of both the mean-field energy and the pairing energy. Various definitions of the pairing gap are discussed as three-point, four-point and five-point mass-difference formulae, averaged matrix elements of the pairing potential, and single-quasiparticle energies. Experimental values for the pairing gap are compared with calculations employing both a delta pairing force and a density-dependent delta interaction in the BCS and Lipkin-Nogami model. Odd-mass nuclei are calculated in the spherical blocking approximation which neglects part of the the core polarization in the odd nucleus. We find that the five-point mass difference formula gives a very robust description of the odd-even staggering, other approximations for the gap may differ from that up to 30% for certain nuclei.

## Key claim

The five-point mass difference formula provides the most robust description of odd-even mass staggering, while other approximations for the pairing gap can differ from it by up to 30% for certain nuclei, depending on the choice of pairing interaction and whether BCS or Lipkin-Nogami treatment is used.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkI4 parameterization for spherical, semi-magic nuclei, treating pairing correlations with both the conventional BCS model and the approximate projection-before-variation Lipkin-Nogami method. They employed two local zero-range pairing interactions (a simple delta force and a density-dependent delta interaction) and compared multiple definitions of the pairing gap: three-point, four-point, and five-point mass-difference formulae, averaged matrix elements of the pairing potential, and single-quasiparticle energies. Odd-mass nuclei were calculated using the spherical blocking approximation, which neglects part of the core polarization in the odd nucleus.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear pairing correlations using self-consistent mean-field models, particularly those employing local pairing energy functionals as developed by Dobaczewski et al. (1995, 1996) and Krieger et al. (1990). It systematically compares the Lipkin-Nogami method with conventional BCS for local pairing forces, building on earlier work by Reinhard et al. (1996) that considered mean-field corrections in the LN scheme. The findings enable more reliable fitting of pairing strengths for self-consistent calculations across the nuclear chart, especially for exotic nuclei where differences between gap definitions become pronounced, and highlight the need for properly adjusted spin and spin-isospin channels in effective interactions before core polarization effects in odd-mass nuclei can be treated quantitatively.
