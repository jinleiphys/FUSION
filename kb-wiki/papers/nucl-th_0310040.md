# Buda-Lund hydro model for ellipsoidally symmetric fireballs and the elliptic flow at RHIC

> The ellipsoidally symmetric extension of Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model is shown here to yield a natural description of the pseudorapidity dependence of the elliptic flow $v_2(η)$, as determined recently by the PHOBOS experiment for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ and 200 GeV. With the same set of parameters, the Buda-Lund model describes also the transverse momentum dependence of $v_2$ of identified particles at mid-rapidity. The results confirm the indication for quark deconfinement in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, obtained from a successful Buda-Lund hydro model fit to the single particle spectra and two-particle correlation data, as measured by the BRAHMS, PHOBOS, PHENIX and STAR collaborations.

## Key claim

The ellipsoidally symmetric extension of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model can simultaneously describe the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow \(v_2(\eta)\) measured by PHOBOS and the transverse momentum dependence of \(v_2\) of identified particles at mid-rapidity, but the model calculation falls 0.02 below the PHOBOS data point at mid-rapidity, which the authors attribute to a non-flow contribution rather than a failure of the model.

## Method

The authors extended the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model from axial to ellipsoidal symmetry by introducing a scaling variable \(s\) and a corresponding four-velocity distribution that satisfy \(u^\mu \partial_\mu s = 0\), with the flow characterized by direction-dependent expansion rates \((\dot X, \dot Y, \dot Z)\) and geometric scales \((X, Y, Z)\). They computed the invariant momentum distribution and elliptic flow using a saddle-point approximation in the integration, then determined \(v_2\) numerically by integrating over transverse momentum and angle, using parameter values previously obtained from fits to single-particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation data from BRAHMS, PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR.

## Key numbers

* Central temperature \(T_0 = 210\) MeV, surface temperature \(T_s \approx 105\) MeV, and critical temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV, with \(a = 1\) giving \(s_c = 0.235\) for the boundary of the region above \(T_c\).
* Principal axes of the "critical" ellipsoid (where \(T \ge T_c\)): \(X_c \approx 4.2\) fm, \(Y_c \approx 5.1\) fm, \(Z_c \approx 8.5\) fm, yielding a volume \(V_c \approx 753\) fm\(^3\).
* The model calculation of \(v_2(\eta=0)\) lies 0.02 below the published PHOBOS data point at mid-rapidity, consistent with the magnitude of non-flow contributions studied by STAR (order 0.01 for mid-rapidity minimum bias data).

## Context

This work extends the axially symmetric Buda-Lund model (Csanad et al., 2003; Csorgo et al., 2003) to ellipsoidal symmetry, providing the first successful description of the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow at RHIC, which previous models had not achieved. The results confirm the indication for quark deconfinement in Au+Au collisions at RHIC found in earlier Buda-Lund fits to single-particle spectra and HBT radii. The finite longitudinal size and three-dimensional Hubble flow distinguish this approach from numerical hydrodynamic calculations such as Hirano and Tsuda (2002), which used Bjorken-type initial conditions and a constant freeze-out temperature and produced a flatter \(v_2(\eta)\) near mid-rapidity.
