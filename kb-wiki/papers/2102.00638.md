# Successive variational approach with the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for the $^5$He nucleus

> We study $^5$He variationally as the first $p$-shell nucleus in the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) using the bare nucleon--nucleon interaction without any renormalization. In TOAMD, the central and tensor correlation operators promote the AMD's Gaussian wave function to a sophisticated many-body state including the short-range and tensor correlations with high-momentum nucleon pairs. We develop a successive approach by applying these operators successively with up to double correlation operators to get converging results. We obtain satisfactory results for $^5$He, not only for the ground state but also for the excited state, and discuss explicitly the correlated Hamiltonian components in each state. We also show the importance of the independent optimization of the correlation functions in the variation of the total energy beyond the condition assuming common correlation forms used in the Jastrow approach.

## Key claim

The TOAMD method, using up to double correlation operators, yields converging variational results for the ground and excited states of $^5$He with the bare AV6' interaction, but the ground state energy of -20.3 MeV remains above the 4He+n threshold by about 5.7 MeV due to the unbound nature of the system, and the method's independent optimization of correlation functions provides better energy convergence than the Jastrow approach.

## Method

The authors applied the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) method to 5He, starting from an AMD reference wave function with a 4He+n cluster configuration and a Gaussian range parameter nu = 0.15 fm^-2. They multiplied the AMD wave function by central (F_S) and tensor (F_D) correlation operators successively up to double products (F_S F_S, F_D F_S, F_D F_D), with each correlation function independently optimized via Gaussian expansion with 6 basis functions under the Ritz variational principle. The Hamiltonian used the bare AV6' potential without renormalization, and matrix elements were evaluated analytically using cluster expansion techniques. For the excited state, the authors extended the method by superposing three TOAMD basis states with different cluster distances (0.2 fm, 0.5 fm, 1.0 fm) in a generator coordinate method (GCM).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 5He in TOAMD with single AMD configuration: -20.33 MeV (full double correlation terms, AV6' potential)
- Ground state energy of 5He in TOAMD+GCM: -20.63 MeV (full double correlation terms, AV6' potential)
- Excited state energy of 5He in TOAMD+GCM: -11.32 MeV (full double correlation terms, AV6' potential)
- Energy of 3H in TOAMD: -7.89 MeV (nu=0.15 fm^-2) and -7.93 MeV (optimized nu=0.11 fm^-2), compared to GFMC value of -7.95(1) MeV
- Energy of 4He in TOAMD: -25.39 MeV (nu=0.15 fm^-2) and -26.01 MeV (optimized nu=0.23 fm^-2), compared to GFMC value of -26.85(2) MeV
- Ground state radius of 5He in TOAMD+GCM: 2.11 fm; excited state radius: 2.43 fm
- Hamiltonian components for 5He ground state in TOAMD+GCM: kinetic energy 99.67 MeV, central force -57.86 MeV, tensor force -62.46 MeV
- Hamiltonian components for 5He excited state in TOAMD+GCM: kinetic energy 98.50 MeV, central force -52.58 MeV, tensor force -57.24 MeV
- Energy loss when using common correlation functions (Jastrow-like approach) instead of independent optimization: about 4 MeV for 5He, compared to 2 MeV for 4He

## Context

This work extends the TOAMD method, previously successful for s-shell nuclei (Myo 2017a, 2017b, 2017c, 2017d), to the first p-shell nucleus 5He as a five-body problem. It contrasts with approaches that use renormalization techniques such as the no-core shell model with similarity renormalization group (Barrett 2013, Hergert 2016) or lattice simulations with chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2012), instead treating bare correlations explicitly. The results enable future application of TOAMD to heavier p-shell nuclei and allow direct analysis of correlated Hamiltonian components and nucleon momentum distributions without renormalization, with the possibility of adding triple correlation products for improved accuracy.
