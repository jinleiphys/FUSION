# Evolution of Nuclear Many-Body Forces with the Similarity Renormalization Group

> The first practical method to evolve many-body nuclear forces to softened form using the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) in a harmonic oscillator basis is demonstrated. When applied to He4 calculations, the two- and three-body oscillator matrix elements yield rapid convergence of the ground-state energy with a small net contribution of the induced four-body force.

## Key claim

The first practical method to evolve many-body nuclear forces to softened form using the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) in a harmonic oscillator basis is demonstrated, with the caveat that when applied to A ≥ 4 systems the induced four-body force is omitted and the transformations are only approximately unitary.

## Method

The SRG flow equation is solved in a discrete Jacobi coordinate harmonic oscillator basis for A=2 and A=3 subsystems, yielding evolved two-body and three-body matrix elements that can be applied unchanged to any nucleus. The two-body matrix elements are fixed by evolving in the A=2 subsystem, then the three-body matrix elements are isolated by evolving in the A=3 subsystem and subtracting the evolved two-body contributions. The evolved matrix elements are then used in No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations for ³H and ⁴He.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ³H with initial N³LO NN and N²LO NNN interactions: −8.473(4) MeV, compared to experimental −8.482 MeV
- Ground-state energy of ⁴He with initial N³LO NN and N²LO NNN interactions: −28.50(2) MeV, compared to experimental −28.296 MeV
- Net induced four-body contribution to ⁴He ground-state energy at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹: approximately 50 keV
- SRG evolution parameter λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ used as a compromise between improved convergence and growth of induced many-body interactions
- NCSM calculations for ³H used Nmax = 36 and ħΩ = 28 MeV, with convergence better than 10 keV
- ⁴He calculations used Nmax = 28 for evolution and Nmax = 18 for diagonalization, with ħΩ = 36 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach from two-body forces to consistent evolution of three-body forces, building on prior SRG developments by Bogner (2007) and Jurgenson (2008) and on the NCSM framework of Navratil (2009). The method enables the use of softened chiral interactions from Epelbaum (2008) in NCSM calculations with greatly accelerated convergence, and the harmonic oscillator matrix elements can also be used for coupled cluster and many-body perturbation theory calculations. The induced four-body contribution at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ (about 50 keV) is smaller than the estimated few hundred keV contribution from the long-ranged part of the N³LO four-nucleon force estimated by Rozpedzik (2006).
