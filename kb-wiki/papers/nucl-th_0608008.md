# Spin ordered phase transitions in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter

> The possibility of appearance of spin polarized states in nuclear matter is studied within the framework of a Fermi liquid theory with Skyrme effective forces in a wide range of isospin asymmetries and densities. There are a few possible scenarios of spin ordered phase transitions: (a) nuclear matter with SLy4 interaction undergoes at some critical density a phase transition to a spin polarized state with the oppositely directed spins of neutrons and protons; (b) in nuclear matter with SkI5 interaction a spin polarized state with the like-directed neutron and proton spins is formed; (c) nuclear matter with SkI3 interaction under increasing density, at first, undergoes a phase transition to the state with the opposite directions of neutron and proton spins, which goes over at larger density to the state with the same direction of nucleon spins. Spin polarized states at strong excess neutrons over protons are accompanied by the long tails in the density profiles of the neutron spin polarization parameter near the critical density, if the energy gain of the transition from the nonpolarized state to the polarized one is the decreasing function of isospin asymmetry (SLy4 force). In the opposite case, if the energy gain is increased with isospin asymmetry, there are no long tails in the density distribution of the neutron spin polarization parameter (SkI3, SkI5 forces).

## Key claim

The paper shows that the specific Skyrme force parametrization determines whether spin-polarized phase transitions in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter produce long tails in the neutron spin polarization density profiles near the critical density, with the presence of such tails depending on whether the energy gain of the transition decreases or increases with isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The author solves self-consistent Fermi liquid theory equations for the single-particle energy coefficients expanded in Pauli matrices in spin and isospin space, using the Skyrme effective interactions SLy4, SkI3, and SkI5. The numerical solutions yield the neutron and proton spin polarization parameters as functions of density at zero temperature for various isospin asymmetries, and the total energy per nucleon of spin-ordered versus nonpolarized states is compared.

## Key numbers

- For SLy4 force in symmetric nuclear matter, the critical density for antiferromagnetic spin ordering is approximately 0.33 fm$^{-3}$.
- For SLy4 force in neutron matter, the critical density for ferromagnetic instability is approximately 0.59 fm$^{-3}$.
- For SkI5 force in neutron matter, the critical density for ferromagnetic spin state is approximately 0.28 fm$^{-3}$.
- For SkI5 force in symmetric nuclear matter, the critical density for ferromagnetic spin ordering is approximately 0.43 fm$^{-3}$.
- For SkI3 force in symmetric nuclear matter, the critical density for ferromagnetic ordering is approximately 0.910 fm$^{-3}$ and for antiferromagnetic ordering approximately 0.917 fm$^{-3}$.
- For SkI3 force in neutron matter, the critical density of spin instability is approximately 0.37 fm$^{-3}$.
- The nuclear saturation density used as reference is $\varrho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the research of Isayev (2005) on spin polarizability of nuclear matter with SLy4 and SLy5 interactions to a wider domain of isospin asymmetries, including symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter as limiting cases, and introduces SkI3 and SkI5 parametrizations that were previously used for describing isotope shifts in neutron-rich nuclei by Reinhard and Flocard (1995). The results are relevant for understanding neutron star thermal and magnetic properties, and for constraining the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy in neutron star models as discussed by Steiner, Prakash, Lattimer, and Ellis (2005).
