# Equation of State and Heavy-Quark Free Energy at Finite Temperature and Density in Two Flavor Lattice QCD with Wilson Quark Action

> We study the equation of state at finite temperature and density in two-flavor QCD with the RG-improved gluon action and the clover-improved Wilson quark action on a $ 16^3 \times 4$ lattice. Along the lines of constant physics at $m_{\rm PS}/m_{\rm V} = 0.65$ and 0.80, we compute the second and forth derivatives of the grand canonical partition function with respect to the quark chemical potential $μ_q = (μ_u+μ_d)/2$ and the isospin chemical potential $μ_I = (μ_u-μ_d)/2$ at vanishing chemical potentials, and study the behaviors of thermodynamic quantities at finite $μ_q$ using these derivatives for the case $μ_I=0$. In particular, we study density fluctuations at none-zero temperature and density by calculating the quark number and isospin susceptibilities and their derivatives with respect to $μ_q$. To suppress statistical fluctuations, we also examine new techniques applicable at low densities. We find a large enhancement in the fluctuation of quark number when the density increased near the pseudo-critical temperature, suggesting a critical point at finite $μ_q$ terminating the first order transition line between hadronic and quark gluon plasma phases. This result agrees with the previous results using staggered-type quark actions qualitatively. Furthermore, we study heavy-quark free energies and Debye screening masses at finite density by measuring the first and second derivatives of these quantities for various color channels of heavy quark-quark and quark-anti-quark pairs. The results suggest that, to the leading order of $μ_q$, the interaction between two quarks becomes stronger at finite densities, while that between quark and anti-quark becomes weaker.

## Key claim

Using two-flavor lattice QCD with Wilson-type quarks, the authors find a large enhancement in quark number fluctuations when the density is increased near the pseudo-critical temperature, which suggests a critical point at finite quark chemical potential terminating the first-order transition line between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases, in qualitative agreement with previous results using staggered-type quark actions.

## Method

The authors performed simulations on a 16^3 x 4 lattice using the RG-improved Iwasaki gauge action and the clover-improved Wilson quark action for two flavors of dynamical quarks, along lines of constant physics defined by pseudo-scalar to vector meson mass ratios of 0.65 and 0.80. They computed the second and fourth derivatives of the grand canonical partition function with respect to the quark chemical potential at vanishing chemical potentials using the Taylor expansion method, and also studied heavy-quark free energies by measuring their first and second derivatives for various color channels of heavy quark-quark and quark-antiquark pairs.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature in the chiral limit: 171-180 MeV for N_t=4 and 160-184 MeV for N_t=6, estimated from O(4) scaling fits using the Polyakov loop susceptibility
- T_c r_0 approximately 0.40 for N_t=6, close to the MILC Collaboration value of 0.402(29) from 2+1 flavor QCD
- Simulations performed at thirteen values of T/T_pc from 0.82 to 4.0 for m_PS/m_V=0.65 and twelve values from 0.76 to 3.0 for m_PS/m_V=0.80
- T_pc/m_V approximately 0.22 (T_pc approximately 170 MeV) in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature QCD studies by the CP-PACS Collaboration (2003, 2004) using the same clover-improved Wilson quark action, which had shown that both the standard Wilson and clover-improved Wilson actions reproduce the expected O(4) scaling behavior around the chiral phase transition, unlike staggered-type quarks. The results qualitatively agree with the Bielefeld-Swansea Collaboration (2003, 2005) who found rapid increase in quark number fluctuations using p4-improved staggered quarks, and with 2+1 flavor staggered simulations by the MILC Collaboration (2007) and RBC-Bielefeld Collaboration (2008). The study enables systematic comparison between Wilson-type and staggered-type quark actions at finite density, and provides a foundation for future continuum extrapolations with Wilson quarks.
