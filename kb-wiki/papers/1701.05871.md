# Transverse densities of octet baryons from chiral effective field theory

> Transverse densities describe the distribution of charge and current at fixed light-front time and provide a frame-independent spatial representation of hadrons as relativistic systems. We calculate the transverse densities of the octet baryons at peripheral distances b = O(M_pi^{-1}) in an approach combining chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) and dispersion analysis. The densities are represented as dispersive integrals of the imaginary parts of the baryon electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region (spectral functions). The spectral functions on the two-pion cut at t > 4 M_pi^2 are computed using relativistic ChEFT with octet and decuplet baryons in the EOMS renormalization scheme. The calculations are extended into the rho-meson mass region, using a dispersive method that incorporates the timelike pion form-factor data. The approach allows us to construct densities at distances b > 1 fm with controlled uncertainties. Our results provide insight into the peripheral structure of nucleons and hyperons and can be compared with empirical densities and lattice-QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the peripheral transverse densities of octet baryons at distances \(b > 1\) fm using a combination of chiral effective field theory and dispersion analysis, with the hedge that the isovector spectral functions are computed at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) and extended into the \(\rho\)-meson mass region via a dispersive improvement that multiplies the \(\chi\)EFT result by the squared modulus of the timelike pion form factor.

## Method

The isovector spectral functions of the octet-baryon electromagnetic form factors on the two-pion cut at \(t > 4 M_\pi^2\) were computed using relativistic \(\chi\)EFT with octet and decuplet baryons in the EOMS renormalization scheme. The calculations were extended into the \(\rho\)-meson mass region by multiplying the \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) spectral functions by \(|F_\pi(t)|^2\), where \(|F_\pi(t)|^2\) is extracted from \(e^+e^- \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\) annihilation data. The transverse densities were then obtained via dispersive integrals of the spectral functions using modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The \(\chi\)EFT spectral functions are computed at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) for the two-pion cut starting at \(t = 4 M_\pi^2\).
- The improved \(\chi\)EFT results for the nucleon reproduce dispersion-theoretical spectral functions up to \(t \sim 16 M_\pi^2\) within errors.
- The approach constructs densities at distances \(b > 1\) fm with controlled uncertainties.
- The isoscalar spectral functions are parametrized by vector-meson poles (\(V = \omega, \phi\)) for \(t < 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- At large distances \(b > 3\) fm, the densities are dominated by the isovector component from two-pion exchange near threshold.
- The isoscalar component becomes comparable to the isovector at distances below \(b \sim 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of peripheral nucleon densities using SU(2) \(\chi\)EFT (Granados 2013, Granados 2015) to the full SU(3) flavor-octet baryons (\(N, \Sigma, \Xi, \Lambda\)). It combines chiral effective field theory with dispersion analysis to compute transverse densities down to \(\sim 1\) fm, enabling comparison with empirical densities and lattice-QCD calculations. The approach can be extended to decuplet baryons (especially the \(\Delta\) isobar) and octet-decuplet transition form factors such as \(N \rightarrow \Delta\).
