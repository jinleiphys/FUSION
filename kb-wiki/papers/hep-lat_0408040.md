# Finite volume effects using lattice chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim
Lattice regularization of chiral perturbation theory provides a numerically convenient method for computing finite-volume effects in pion observables, because loop diagrams become finite summations for any nonzero lattice spacing; the lattice spacing can be chosen small enough to be irrelevant for the volume effects studied, and the results agree with standard one-loop calculations.

## Method
The authors use a lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian for two quark flavors, with an isotropic spacetime lattice of spacing *a*, *N_s* spatial sites, and *N_t* temporal sites. Loop momenta are summed rather than integrated, yielding finite expressions for the pion mass, decay constant, form factor, and charge radius at one-loop order; the volume dependence is isolated by computing differences between finite volumes and an effectively infinite volume (L_s = 8 fm) with N_t >> N_s.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing chosen as *a* = 0.1 fm for numerical results.
- Infinite volume approximated by L_s = 8 fm.
- Temporal extent set to N_t = 5 N_s to study spatial volume effects on a lattice of effectively infinite temporal extent.
- The pion mass difference between finite and infinite volume is computed and shown to agree with standard one-loop results from Colangelo and Durr (2004).

## Context
This work extends the pioneering finite-volume chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1988) by using lattice regularization instead of dimensional regularization, making numerical evaluation of loop diagrams straightforward. It builds on the lattice-regularized chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian of Borasoy, Lewis, and Ouimet (2003) but for two flavors rather than three. The method enables inexpensive computation of volume dependences for the pion mass, decay constant, form factor, and charge radius, and the results for the decay constant agree with the one-loop calculation of Colangelo and Haefeli (2004).
