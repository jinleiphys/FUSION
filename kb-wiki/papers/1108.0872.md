# Perturbative Renormalizability of Chiral Two Pion Exchange in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering: P- and D-waves

> We study the perturbative renormalizability of chiral two pion exchange in nucleon-nucleon scattering for p- and d-waves within the effective field theory approach. The one pion exchange potential is fully iterated at the leading order in the expansion, a choice generating a consistent and well-defined power counting that we explore in detail. The results show that perturbative chiral two pion exchange reproduces the data up to a center-of-mass momentum of k_cm ~300 MeV at NNLO and that the effective field theory expansion convergences up to k_cm ~ 350 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) can be treated perturbatively in nucleon-nucleon scattering for P- and D-waves, provided that one-pion exchange (OPE) is fully iterated at leading order and that the counterterm structure is modified from the Weinberg counting to ensure renormalizability. The approach reproduces data up to a center-of-mass momentum of approximately 300 MeV at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO), with the expansion converging up to approximately 350 MeV.

## Method

The authors employ a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework in which the leading-order (LO) interaction consists of fully iterated one-pion exchange plus the necessary counterterms to renormalize attractive singular potentials, following the scheme of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005). Chiral two-pion exchange at NLO and N²LO is then included as a perturbative correction. The renormalization of the subleading corrections is analyzed by requiring that the perturbative phase shifts remain finite in the zero-cutoff limit, which determines the required number and scaling of contact operators for each partial wave.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative chiral TPE reproduces scattering data up to a center-of-mass momentum of \(k_{\rm cm} \sim 300\,{\rm MeV}\) at N²LO.
- The effective field theory expansion converges up to \(k_{\rm cm} \sim 350\,{\rm MeV}\).
- For the \(^1S_0\) channel, the hard scale governing the short-range interaction is \(\Lambda_{0,s} \simeq 270\,{\rm MeV}\), giving a convergence rate \(Q/\Lambda_0 \simeq m_\pi / \Lambda_{0,s} \simeq 0.5\).
- The residual cutoff dependence of the renormalized \(^3P_0\) LO phase shift scales as \(d\delta^{(0)}_{{}^3P_0}/dr_c \sim k^3 r_c^{3/2} a_3^{1/2}\), confirming the existence of the \(r_c \to 0\) limit.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-based power counting proposed by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) to subleading orders, providing a systematic framework for including chiral two-pion exchange as a perturbation. It addresses the non-renormalizability of the Weinberg counting at NLO and N²LO, which had been identified in earlier studies by Pavon Valderrama and collaborators (2005, 2009). The results are consistent with the perturbative renormalization analysis of Birse (2005) and with the momentum-space formulation of Long and Yang (2011) for the \(^3P_0\) partial wave. The approach enables a consistent power counting for nuclear EFT that avoids the "deuteron NLO disaster" and other pathologies of the fully iterated Weinberg scheme.
