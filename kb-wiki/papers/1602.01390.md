# Assessing the role of nuclear effects in the interpretation of the MiniBooNE low-energy anomaly

> We study the impact of the effect of multinucleon interactions in the reconstruction of the neutrino energy on the fit of the MiniBooNE data in terms of neutrino oscillations. We obtain some improvement of the fit of the MiniBooNE low-energy excess in the framework of two-neutrino oscillations and a shift of the allowed region in the $\sin^2 2\vartheta$--$Δ{m}^2$ plane towards smaller values of $\sin^2 2\vartheta$ and larger values of $Δ{m}^2$. However this effect is not enough to solve the problem of the appearance-disappearance tension in the global fit of short-baseline neutrino oscillation data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of multinucleon interactions in neutrino energy reconstruction improves the fit of the MiniBooNE low-energy excess in a two-neutrino oscillation framework, shifting the allowed region toward smaller values of sin²2θ and larger values of Δm², but this effect is insufficient to resolve the appearance-disappearance tension in global short-baseline neutrino oscillation data.

## Method

The authors used the MiniBooNE muon-to-electron neutrino and antineutrino Monte Carlo full transmutation events from the final data release, then statistically separated quasielastic events from misidentified pion production events using the nuclear model of Martini et al. (2009). They randomly divided the remaining events into true quasielastic and multinucleon interaction groups in proportion to probabilities calculated for different (Eν, Eν_rec) pairs, and for the multinucleon events they recalculated the reconstructed neutrino energy using the theoretical correlation between true and reconstructed energy from Martini et al. (2012), including detector energy resolution. The background from νe-induced events was similarly modified by rescaling event weights according to the ratio of νe and νμ fluxes and applying the same multinucleon treatment.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit oscillation parameters without multinucleon interactions: sin²2θ = 1.0, Δm² = 0.040 eV², with χ²_min = 37.3 for 36 degrees of freedom (41% goodness-of-fit)
- The best-fit oscillation parameters with multinucleon interactions: sin²2θ = 0.98, Δm² = 0.041 eV², with χ²_min = 36.6 for 36 degrees of freedom (44% goodness-of-fit)
- Without oscillations, the χ² value decreased from 56.0 to 54.7 when including multinucleon interactions (38 degrees of freedom)
- The misidentified pion events distribution is shifted horizontally by approximately ΔEν ≈ 300 MeV relative to the quasielastic distribution, corresponding to ΔM = (M²Δ - M²N)/(2MN) ≈ 338 MeV
- In antineutrino mode, only 3/4 of scatterings occur on carbon protons, reducing the multinucleon contribution

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Martini et al. (2012) that multinucleon interactions could explain part of the MiniBooNE low-energy anomaly, and builds on the nuclear model of Martini et al. (2009, 2010) that successfully reproduced MiniBooNE quasielastic-like cross section data without modifying the axial mass. The paper contradicts the MiniBooNE collaboration's conclusion (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2013) that multinucleon interactions do not significantly change the oscillation fit, finding instead a small improvement. This work enables more accurate treatment of nuclear effects in oscillation analyses for MiniBooNE, T2K, and future short-baseline experiments, though the authors note that the multinucleon effect alone cannot resolve the tension between appearance and disappearance data in global fits.
