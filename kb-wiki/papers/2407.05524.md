# Significance of tensor force in pseudo-spin symmetry

> We point out that the pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) of nuclei significantly depends on the proton ($Z$) and neutron numbers ($N$), sometimes giving rise to the characteristic structures. By using the non-relativistic spherical Hartree-Fock calculation with a realistic tensor force, we show that the tensor force may be deeply relevant to the $Z$- and $N$-dependence of the PSS. While the PSS has often been discussed in the context of the relativistic symmetry, the tensor-force effects on the PSS sometimes look analogous to the $Z$-and $N$-dependence of the PSS in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations without explicit tensor force. The observed variation of the $p0d_{3/2}$-$p1s_{1/2}$ levels from $^{40}$Ca to $^{34}$Si is consistent with the tensor-force-driven $Z$-dependence of the PSS, but not necessarily with the RMF result. Even though it is too early to be conclusive, this result elucidates the significance of the tensor force when discussing the PSS.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the tensor force may be deeply relevant to the proton-number (Z) and neutron-number (N) dependence of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) in nuclei, but the authors state it is too early to be conclusive whether the tensor force dominates this dependence over relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic spherical Hartree-Fock calculations using the M3Y-P6 semi-realistic interaction, which contains a realistic tensor force derived from a bare nucleonic interaction through the G-matrix. They compared single-particle energy spacings between PSS partner orbitals across different nuclei using M3Y-P6, the Gogny-D1S interaction (which has no explicit tensor force), M3Y-P6 with the tensor-force contribution subtracted, and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the DD-ME2 parameter-set. Experimental single-particle energies were extracted from low-lying levels adjacent to doubly-closed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The near degeneracy of n0f5/2 and n1p3/2 in Ni nuclei is a typical PSS example, while the magicity of N=32 near 52Ca occurs due to large spacing between these orbits, showing Z-dependence
- For the p0d3/2-p1s1/2 levels from 40Ca to 34Si, the experimental data is consistent with the tensor-force-driven picture but not with the RMF result using DD-ME2
- At 48Ca, the tensor-force effect is sizable due to occupation on n0f7/2, while small at 40Ca
- For the n0g7/2 and n1d5/2 PSS partners at N=50 nuclei, M3Y-P6 describes the experimental Z-dependence owing to the tensor force, while the level spacing seems too large at Sn with D1S and too small at Zr with DD-ME2
- For the n0h9/2 and n1f7/2 spacing from 140Ce to 132Sn, the tensor force suppresses the increase of n0h9/2 energy, keeping the PSS a good approximation

## Context

This work extends the discussion of PSS beyond the relativistic symmetry framework established by Ginocchio (1997), Meng (1998), and Liang, Meng, and Zhou (2015), who argued PSS is a relativistic symmetry. The paper connects PSS to the shell evolution driven by the tensor force, as previously studied by Otsuka et al. (2005), Lesinski et al. (2007), and Sorlin and Porquet (2014). The results enable future investigations of how the Z- and N-dependence of PSS arises within the relativistic Hartree-Fock scheme, which includes exchange terms and tensor coupling from meson-nucleon interactions.
