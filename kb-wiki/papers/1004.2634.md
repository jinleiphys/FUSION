# D mesic nuclei

> The energies and widths of several D^0 meson bound states for different nuclei are obtained using a D-meson selfenergy in the nuclear medium, which is evaluated in a selfconsistent manner using techniques of unitarized coupled-channel theory. The kernel of the meson-baryon interaction is based on a model that treats heavy pseudoscalar and heavy vector mesons on equal footing, as required by heavy quark symmetry. We find D^0 bound states in all studied nuclei, from 12C up to 208Pb. The inclusion of vector mesons is the keystone for obtaining an attractive D-nucleus interaction that leads to the existence of D^0-nucleus bound states, as compared to previous studies based on SU(4) flavor symmetry. In some cases, the half widths are smaller than the separation of the levels, what makes possible their experimental observation by means of a nuclear reaction. This can be of particular interest for the future PANDA@FAIR physics program. We also find a D^+ bound state in 12C, but it is too broad and will have a significant overlap with the energies of the continuum.

## Key claim
Using a self-consistent unitarized coupled-channel approach that treats heavy pseudoscalar and heavy vector mesons on equal footing (as required by heavy quark symmetry), the authors find D^0 bound states in all studied nuclei from ^12C to ^208Pb, but only when vector mesons are included; previous SU(4) flavor symmetry models without vector mesons produce a repulsive or insufficiently attractive D-nucleus interaction that does not support bound states.

## Method
The D-meson selfenergy in nuclear matter was evaluated self-consistently by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with up to 22 coupled channels (including D, D*, and baryon states) for each isospin and spin sector, using a SU(8) spin-flavor kernel that respects heavy quark symmetry. The in-medium selfenergy was then used to construct an energy-dependent local optical potential via the local density approximation, and the Schrodinger equation was solved in coordinate space to find bound-state energies and widths for various nuclei from ^12C to ^208Pb.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3), the threshold optical potential is (-25 - i14) MeV.
- For ^12C, the 1s D^0 bound state has binding energy B = -7.0 MeV and half-width Gamma/2 = 5.0 MeV.
- For ^208Pb, the 1s D^0 bound state has B = -11.5 MeV and Gamma/2 = 3.3 MeV; the 1p state has B = -10.2 MeV and Gamma/2 = 3.6 MeV; the 2p state has B = -5.9 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.9 MeV; the 1d state has B = -8.7 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.0 MeV; the 1f state has B = -6.9 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.6 MeV; the 2s state has B = -8.1 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.2 MeV.
- For ^40Ca, the 1s D^0 bound state has B = -10.7 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.5 MeV; the 1p state has B = -6.8 MeV and Gamma/2 = 5.4 MeV.
- For ^24Mg, the 1s D^0 bound state has B = -8.4 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.1 MeV; the 1p state has B = -3.5 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.9 MeV.
- For ^118Sn, the 1s D^0 bound state has B = -11.3 MeV and Gamma/2 = 3.6 MeV; the 1p state has B = -9.5 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.0 MeV; the 1d state has B = -7.2 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.7 MeV; the 2s state has B = -6.4 MeV and Gamma/2 = 4.8 MeV.
- For ^12C, the D^+ bound state has B = -4.6 MeV and Gamma/2 = 6.4 MeV (half-width larger than binding energy, thus not a clear bound state).
- In the SU(4) model (without vector mesons), the D selfenergy at threshold and rho = rho_0 is repulsive, and no D^0-nucleus bound states exist.
- The low-density limit (without self-consistency) gives imaginary parts about 60% larger than the self-consistent result at rho = rho_0 in the energy region -20 MeV <= q^0 - m_D <= 0.

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent unitarized coupled-channel approach previously applied to eta-nucleus bound states (GarciaRecio, 2002) and antikaon-nucleus bound states (Baca, 2000) to the charm sector. It contradicts earlier predictions by Tsushima (1998) based on a quark-meson coupling model, which found binding energies of about 100 MeV for D^0 in ^208Pb with no width, whereas the present model finds binding of about 10 MeV with widths of several MeV. The results enable experimental searches for D^0 mesic nuclei at the future PANDA@FAIR facility, with the best prospects for observation in medium-mass nuclei like ^24Mg where the 1s state has half-width about half the binding energy.
