# Effects of entrance channels on the deexcitation properties of the same compound nucleus formed by different pairs of collision partners

> The properties of deexcitation of the same $^{220}$Th compound nucleus (CN) formed by different mass (charge) asymmetric reactions are investigated. It is demonstrated that the effective fission barrier $<B_{\rm fis}>$ value being a function of the excitation energy $E^*_{\rm CN}$ is strongly sensitive to the various orbital angular momentum $L=\ell\hbar$ distributions of CN formed with the same excitation energy $E^*_{CN}$ by the very different entrance channels $^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb, $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf, $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba and $^{96}$Zr+$^{124}$Sn. Consequently, the competition between the fission and evaporation of light particles (neutron, proton, and $α$-particle) processes along the deexcitation cascade of CN depends on the orbital angular momentum distribution of CN. Therefore, the ratio between the evaporation residue cross sections obtained after emission of neutral and charged particles and neutrons only for the same CN with a given excitation energy $E^*_{CN}$ is sensitive to the mass (charge) asymmetry of reactants in the entrance channel.

## Key claim

The effective fission barrier of the same compound nucleus (220Th) formed at the same excitation energy is strongly sensitive to the orbital angular momentum distribution imparted by the entrance channel, which means that the deexcitation cascade and the competition between fission and light-particle evaporation depend on the mass (charge) asymmetry of the reactants even when the compound nucleus and its excitation energy are identical.

## Method

The authors calculated capture, complete fusion, quasifission, and fast-fission cross sections for four reactions (16O+204Pb, 40Ar+180Hf, 82Se+138Ba, and 96Zr+124Sn) using a dinuclear-system model that solves equations of relative motion with friction forces and accounts for orientation angles of deformed nuclei. They then computed the deexcitation cascade of the 220Th compound nucleus by evaluating neutron, proton, and alpha-particle emission widths and fission widths from the level densities of each intermediate excited nucleus, using damping functions for shell corrections that depend on both excitation energy and angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The mass asymmetry parameters for the four entrance channels are eta = 0.86 (16O+204Pb), 0.64 (40Ar+180Hf), 0.26 (82Se+138Ba), and 0.13 (96Zr+124Sn).
- Threshold excitation energies for compound-nucleus formation are about 27.8 MeV (16O+204Pb), 35.5 MeV (40Ar+180Hf), 12 MeV (82Se+138Ba), and 17.5 MeV (96Zr+124Sn).
- At E*_CN = 35.5 MeV, the effective fission barrier <B_fis> for 220Th* is 4.16 MeV (16O+204Pb), 5.44 MeV (40Ar+180Hf), 4.88 MeV (82Se+138Ba), and 3.72 MeV (96Zr+124Sn).
- At E*_CN = 61 MeV, <B_fis> for 220Th* is 1.52 MeV (16O+204Pb), 2.21 MeV (40Ar+180Hf), 4.53 MeV (82Se+138Ba), and 2.54 MeV (96Zr+124Sn).
- The ratio ER(Z<90)/ER(Z=90) at E*_CN = 35.5 MeV is 9.5 (16O+204Pb), 2.9 (40Ar+180Hf), 8.7 (82Se+138Ba), and 3.3 (96Zr+124Sn).
- The ratio ER(Z<90)/ER(Z=90) at E*_CN = 61 MeV is 10.7 (16O+204Pb), 15.2 (40Ar+180Hf), 13.3 (82Se+138Ba), and 19.4 (96Zr+124Sn).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Mandaglio et al., 2009; Mandaglio et al., 2012; Fazio et al., 2004) on the role of angular momentum in fusion and evaporation-residue formation, and it contradicts the implicit assumption that the same compound nucleus at the same excitation energy deexcites identically regardless of how it was formed. The results enable experimentalists to optimize detector setups for superheavy-element synthesis by showing that evaporation residues after charged-particle emission (with Z < Z_CN) are many times more abundant than residues after neutron emission only, so facilities should be arranged to detect residues with atomic numbers ranging from Z_CN-8 to Z_CN.
