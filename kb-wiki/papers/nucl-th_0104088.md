# Effective Range in Doublet S-wave Neutron-Deuteron Scattering

> The effective field theory approach is applied to the three-nucleon process of $S=1/2$ neutron-deuteron scattering in the S-wave, including the effective range parameters summed at all orders. This is achieved through a modification of the power counting, following a recent suggestion in the literature. It is shown that with this procedure, while the convergence of the loop integrals improves, one cannot meaningfully include a three-body effective term to describe low-energy data affected by the presence of the triton bound state.

# Effective Range in Doublet S-wave Neutron-Deuteron Scattering

## Key claim
Including effective range parameters for the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ and $^1\mathrm{S}_0$ nucleon-nucleon channels at all orders improves the ultraviolet convergence of the integral equations for $S=1/2$ neutron-deuteron scattering, but this improvement is insufficient to make the equations sensitive to a three-body interaction term, preventing the description of low-energy data affected by the triton bound state.

## Method
The author applies effective field theory with auxiliary dibaryon fields for the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ (deuteron) and $^1\mathrm{S}_0$ channels, following the power-counting suggestion of Beane and Savage (2001) that treats both the scattering length and effective range as order $Q^{-1}$. The coupled integral equations for the $d+N\rightarrow d+N$ and $d+N\rightarrow t+N$ amplitudes are solved numerically with the full propagators that resum the effective range parameters $\rho_d$ and $r_{0t}$, and the dependence on a three-body contact term $H$ is tested.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy $B = 2.225$ MeV, giving $\gamma_d = 45.7066$ MeV
- Singlet scattering length $a_t = -23.714$ fm, giving $\gamma_t = 1/a_t$
- Effective range in the $^3\mathrm{S}_1$ channel: $\rho_d = 1.765$ fm
- Effective range in the $^1\mathrm{S}_0$ channel: $r_{0t} = 2.73$ fm
- Experimental $S=1/2$ $nd$ scattering length: $a_3^{(1/2)} = (0.65 \pm 0.04)$ fm (Dilg et al., 1971)
- Three-body parameter fitted to the scattering length: $\Lambda_* = 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$
- $s_0 \approx 1.0064$ (asymptotic constant from the three-body equations)

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory treatment of three-nucleon systems by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999, 2000) to include the effective range resummation proposed by Beane and Savage (2001) for two-body processes. The $S=1/2$ neutron-deuteron channel is known to require a three-body force counterterm to remove cutoff dependence, as shown by Bedaque et al. (1999) and earlier by Danilov (1961). The present result shows that the improved power counting does not resolve the sensitivity problem, in contrast to its success in two-body systems, and suggests that explicit inclusion of the triton as a dynamical field may be necessary.
