# A Bethe-Salpeter study with the <A^2>-enhanced effective QCD coupling

> Dyson-Schwinger equations provide a prominent approach to physics of strong interactions. To reproduce the hadronic phenomenology well, the Dyson-Schwinger approach in the rainbow-ladder approximation must employ an effective interaction between quarks which is fairly strong at intermediate (Q^2 approx. 0.5 GeV^2) spacelike transferred momenta. We have recently proposed that such an interaction may originate from the dimension 2 gluon condensate <A^2> which has recently attracted much attention, and showed that the resulting effective running coupling leads to the sufficiently strong dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and successful phenomenology at least in the light sector of pseudoscalar mesons. In the present paper, we give a more detailed investigation of the parameter dependence of these results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the enhancement of the effective quark-gluon interaction at intermediate momenta (Q² ~ 0.5 GeV²), which is required for successful Dyson-Schwinger phenomenology in the rainbow-ladder approximation, may originate from the dimension-2 gluon condensate ⟨A²⟩; however, the proposed effective coupling α_eff(Q²) is justified only for relatively high Q² and is known to be unrealistic in the Q² → 0 limit, so its validity at low Q² is only justified a posteriori by the phenomenological results.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger (gap) equations for quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equations for pseudoscalar q\bar{q} bound states (u,d,s) in the rainbow-ladder approximation, using the same calculational procedures as in their previous studies and the Jain-Munczek approach, but replacing the effective coupling with the ⟨A²⟩-enhanced form α_eff(Q²) = α_pert(Q²) Z^{Npert}(Q²) G^{Npert}(Q²)². The nonperturbative parts Z^{Npert} and G^{Npert} depend on the dynamical gluon mass m_A (proportional to the ⟨A²⟩ condensate) and on parameters C_A and C_G, which were treated as free parameters to be fixed by phenomenology, with initial estimates from lattice and operator product expansion considerations.

## Key numbers

- The Landau-gauge lattice value for g²⟨A²⟩ is 2.76 GeV², yielding m_A = 0.845 GeV as an initial estimate.
- The initial estimate for C_A is (0.640 GeV)^4, based on the dimension-4 condensate ⟨F²⟩.
- The chiral-limit results with the parameter set C_A = C_G = (0.640 GeV)^4, m_A = 0.884 GeV give f_π ≈ 88.2 MeV and ⟨\bar{q}q⟩ ≈ (-214 MeV)^3.
- The chiral-limit results with the refitted parameter set C_A = C_G = (0.6060 GeV)^4, m_A = 0.8402 GeV give f_π ≈ 90.5 MeV and ⟨\bar{q}q⟩ ≈ (-217 MeV)^3.
- With the initial parameter set and JM bare masses (m̃_u = 3.1×10⁻³ GeV, m̃_s = 73×10⁻³ GeV), the pion mass is 136.70 MeV, the pion decay constant is 91.2 MeV, and the π⁰→γγ amplitude is 0.272×10⁻³ MeV⁻¹.
- With the refitted parameter set and adjusted bare masses, the kaon mass is 494.92 MeV and the kaon decay constant is 111.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Kekez and Klabucar, 2003) that the ⟨A²⟩ gluon condensate can provide the intermediate-momentum enhancement needed in phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger studies, by providing a detailed investigation of parameter dependence. It builds on the ab initio Dyson-Schwinger studies of Fischer and Alkofer (2003) and others, which found that ghost contributions are crucial for the intermediate-momentum enhancement of the running coupling, and on the operator product expansion results for gluon and ghost polarization functions from Lavelle and collaborators and from Kondo (2001). The results enable further phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger studies using a coupling with a theoretical motivation from the ⟨A²⟩ condensate, though the form remains unreliable at very low Q².
