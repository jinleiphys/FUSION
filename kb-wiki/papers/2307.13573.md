# Scalar propagator in a background gluon field beyond the eikonal approximation

> We investigate the path integral representation of the scalar propagator in a background gluon field, extending beyond the eikonal approximation by considering all gauge field components and incorporating its $x^-$ dependence. Utilizing the worldline formalism, we integrate the Schwinger proper time to express the scalar propagator in light-cone coordinates, facilitating a direct comparison with known results in the literature. The derived propagator captures the change of longitudinal momentum of the projectile within the medium. In the high-energy limit, our result simplifies to the effective gluon propagator employed in the BDMPS-Z formalism. Hence, we propose that our outcome serves as a foundational point for investigating corrections to the BDMPS-Z spectrum arising from the longitudinal momentum transfer of the radiated gluon with the medium, as well as for studying collisional energy loss phenomena. Lastly, by employing an expansion around the classical saddle point solution, we systematically derive an eikonal expansion in inverse powers of the boost parameter, encompassing corrections related to longitudinal momentum transfer and interactions of the projectile with the transverse component of the field.

## Key claim
The paper derives a path integral representation of the scalar propagator in a background gluon field that goes beyond the eikonal approximation by including all gauge field components and their \(x^-\) dependence, with the condition that the result is valid only when \(x^+ \ne y^+\) (the light-cone times of the endpoints are not equal).

## Method
Using the worldline formalism, the Feynman scalar propagator is expressed as a path integral in light-cone coordinates after integrating out the Schwinger proper time. The derivation fixes the \(z^+\) trajectory by integrating over \(p^-\) and reparametrizes the worldline to use light-cone proper time \(\tau^+\), yielding a configuration-space path integral where the Wilson line contains both the longitudinal component \(A^-\) and the transverse components \(\vec{A}\) of the gauge field. An expansion around the classical saddle point solution is then employed to systematically derive an eikonal expansion in inverse powers of the boost parameter.

## Key numbers
- The Schwinger proper time is expressed as \(T = (x^+ - y^+)/2\langle p^+\rangle\), where \(\langle p^+\rangle\) is the average longitudinal momentum over the particle path.
- The scalar propagator in configuration space (Eq. 4.2) contains a factor \(\Theta(\langle p^+\rangle)/(2\langle p^+\rangle)\) for the forward-propagating case, with \(\langle p^+\rangle = (1/(x^+-y^+))\int_{y^+}^{x^+} d\tau^+ p^+(\tau^+)\).
- The light-cone time trajectory is given by \(z^+(\tau^+) = y^+ + \int_{y^+}^{\tau^+} (p^+(\bar{\tau}^+)/\langle p^+\rangle) d\bar{\tau}^+\), with \(\dot{z}^+(\tau^+) = p^+(\tau^+)/\langle p^+\rangle\).

## Context
This work extends the non-eikonal corrections to the scalar propagator previously studied in the CGC framework by Altinoluk et al. (2014, 2016, 2021, 2022), who computed corrections from finite target width and transverse field components. The derived propagator simplifies in the high-energy limit to the effective gluon propagator used in the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996), and is proposed as a foundation for computing corrections to the BDMPS-Z spectrum from longitudinal momentum transfer and for studying collisional energy loss. The result also enables systematic calculation of next-to-eikonal corrections to scattering amplitudes in \(p\)A collisions and DIS at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider.
