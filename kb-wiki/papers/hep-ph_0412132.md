# The Roper and Radiative Decay of Pentaquarks

> Identifying the Roper N(1440) and N(1710) as the pentaquark octet and anti-decuplet, respectively, we analyze their main decay modes in the diquark picture. The ratio of the partial decay widths is largely consistent with the nearly ideal mixing of the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model, which then allows to predict the width of the radiative decay of N(1440), $Γ_{10}(N\to pγ)=1/4 Γ_{12}(N\to nγ)=0.25-0.31$ MeV. We then show that the three-body radiative decay of the pentaquark anti-decuplet is quite enhanced due to its mixing with the pentaquark octet. We find for the $J^P={1/2}^+$ pentaquark anti-decuplet $Γ(Θ^+\to K^+ n γ)=0.034 \sim 0.041$ MeV. The diquark picture of the pentaquark predicts $Γ(Θ^+\to K^+ n γ) =4 Γ(Θ^+\to K^0 p γ)$. Finally we show that the difference in the Theta+ mass in the K0 p and K+ n decay channels may be accounted for by the missing photons in the radiative decay.

## Key claim

Identifying the Roper N(1440) and N(1710) as the pentaquark octet and anti-decuplet, respectively, the ratio of their partial decay widths is largely consistent with the nearly ideal mixing of the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model, provided that the gross SU(3) violation seen in the partial widths is explained by the exponential dependence of the tunneling amplitude on SU(3) violating terms.

## Method

The author analyzed the dominant decay modes of N(1440) and N(1710) in the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark picture, using chiral perturbation theory to compute partial decay widths and WKB tunneling amplitudes for the diquark barrier. The radiative decay of N(1440) and the three-body radiative decay of the pentaquark anti-decuplet (Theta+) were calculated from effective Lagrangians, with couplings extracted from experimental partial widths and the tunneling amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Partial decay width of N(1440) to N pi: 210-245 MeV (from PDG).
- Partial decay width of N(1710) to Lambda K: 5-25 MeV (from PDG).
- Outgoing meson momentum for N(1440) to N pi: 396 MeV.
- Outgoing meson momentum for N(1710) to Lambda K: 268 MeV.
- Tunneling amplitude for N(1440) (WKB): e^{-S_0} = 0.26.
- Tunneling amplitude for N(1710) (WKB): e^{-S_0} = 0.16.
- Ratio of tunneling amplitudes: 1.7; ratio of couplings: 1.3-3.3.
- Predicted radiative decay width of N(1440) to p gamma: 0.25-0.31 MeV.
- Predicted three-body radiative decay width of Theta+ to K+ n gamma: 0.034-0.041 MeV.
- Predicted ratio of Theta+ radiative decay widths: Gamma(Theta+ to K+ n gamma) = 4 Gamma(Theta+ to K0 p gamma).
- Two-body decay width of Theta+ to K N: 0.24-0.78 MeV.
- Three-body radiative decay contributes 4.6-13.5% to the K+ n channel and 1.2-3.8% to the K0 p channel.

## Context

This work extends the Jaffe-Wilczek diquark model (Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003) for pentaquarks by analyzing the decay modes of the Roper N(1440) and N(1710), addressing criticisms by Cohen (2004) and Goeke et al. (2004) that such identification leads to gross SU(3) violation. The analysis enables predictions for radiative decays that can be tested experimentally, and suggests that the systematic mass difference of Theta+ in K0p and K+n decay channels, noted by Rossi (2004) and Hicks (2004), may be explained by missing photons in radiative decay.
