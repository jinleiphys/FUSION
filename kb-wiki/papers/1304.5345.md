# Interplay of total cross sections and ratios of real to imaginary parts of hadron amplitudes

> The impact of different assumptions about high energy behavior of the total cross section of proton-proton interactions on the ratio of the real to imaginary part of the forward elastic scattering amplitude is analyzed. It is shown how experimental data about this ratio at LHC energies can help in the proper choice of the asymptotic dependence of the total cross section.

# Interplay of total cross sections and ratios of real to imaginary parts of hadron amplitudes

## Key claim

The ratio of the real to imaginary part of the forward elastic scattering amplitude (ρ₀) at LHC energies can discriminate between competing asymptotic models of the total proton-proton cross section, but no single term from the three-component phenomenological fit (linear log, log², or power-law) separately satisfies both the measured total cross sections and the expected ρ₀ values; only a combination with a constant term appears satisfactory.

## Method

The author applies Kramers-Kronig dispersion relations to connect the total cross section σ_t(s) to the forward ratio ρ₀(s), using a local dispersion relation approximation where ρ₀ is primarily sensitive to the logarithmic derivative of σ_t. Three separate energy-dependent components for σ_t are considered: a₁ ln s, a₂ ln² s, and a₃ s^Δ, and the resulting analytic expressions for ρ₀ are derived and evaluated at LHC energies of 7 TeV and 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For a pure ln s dependence of σ_t (a₂ = a₃ = 0): ρ₀ = 0.0887 at 7 TeV and 0.0823 at 14 TeV.
- For a pure ln² s dependence of σ_t (a₁ = a₃ = 0): ρ₀ = 0.1774 at 7 TeV and 0.165 at 14 TeV.
- For a pure power-law dependence with Δ = 0.13: ρ₀ = 0.21 (constant with energy).
- For a pure power-law dependence with Δ = 0.08: ρ₀ = 0.131 (constant with energy).
- For a pure power-law dependence with Δ = 0.06: ρ₀ = 0.094 (constant with energy).
- For an intermediate ln^r s dependence with 1.3 ≤ r ≤ 1.52: ρ₀ between 0.115 and 0.135 at 7 TeV, and between 0.107 and 0.125 at 14 TeV.
- For a damped ln² s law with σ_t ∝ s^{-α} ln² s and α ≈ 0.02: ρ₀ = 3 × 10⁻⁴ at 7 TeV and ρ₀ = -2.6 × 10⁻² at 14 TeV.
- The recent fit from Bhal (2013) predicts σ_t = 95.4 ± 1.1 mb at 7 TeV, compared to the experimental value of 98.3 ± 2.9 mb.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of using dispersion relations to connect total cross sections and forward scattering ratios, following the approach of Dremin (2013) and earlier work by Bialas and Charny (1977) and Block and Cahn (1985). The analysis is motivated by the surprising rise of hadron total cross sections first observed in kaon-proton interactions at Protvino and later confirmed at ISR, SppS, and Tevatron. The results enable experimental discrimination at LHC between the Froissart-Martin bound (ln² s asymptotics), the hard Pomeron (power-law) scenario, and intermediate logarithmic behaviors, with the specific prediction that ρ₀ values below 0.13 would favor a smaller hard Pomeron intercept Δ and indicate a long approach to asymptopia.
