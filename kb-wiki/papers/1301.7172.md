# Two neutrino double-$β$ decay in the interacting boson-fermion model

> A calculation of the spectroscopic properties, energy levels and electromagnetic transitions and moments, of the ten nuclei $^{128,130}$Te, $^{128,130}$I, $^{128,130}$Xe, $^{129,131}$I, $^{127,129}$Te within the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM-2) and its extensions (IBFM-2 and IBFFM-2) is presented. The wave functions so obtained are used to calculate single-$β$ and $2νββ$ matrix elements for $^{128,130}$Te $\to$ $^{128,130}$I $\to$ $^{128,130}$Xe ecay. Use of the effective value of the axial vector coupling constant $g_{A,\text{eff},β}$ extracted from single-$β$ produces results for $2νββ$ in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The authors present a new approach to calculating two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements without the closure approximation, using the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2 and IBFFM-2). The agreement with experiment is achieved only after extracting an effective axial vector coupling constant \(g_{A,\text{eff}}\) from single-beta decay data, yielding a value of \(0.28 \pm 0.03\) that is significantly quenched relative to the free-nucleon value.

## Method

The authors first calculate spectroscopic properties (energy levels, electromagnetic transitions and moments) for the ten nuclei \({}^{128,130}\text{Te}\), \({}^{128,130}\text{I}\), \({}^{128,130}\text{Xe}\), \({}^{129,131}\text{I}\), and \({}^{127,129}\text{Te}\) using the interacting boson model (IBM-2) for even-even nuclei, the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2) for odd-even nuclei, and the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2) for odd-odd nuclei. They then use the resulting wave functions to compute single-beta and two-neutrino double-beta matrix elements for the decays \({}^{128,130}\text{Te} \to {}^{128,130}\text{I} \to {}^{128,130}\text{Xe}\), employing the Tomoda formulation for the double-beta half-life and extracting the effective axial vector coupling constant from a comparison of calculated and experimental \(\log ft\) values for single-beta decays.

## Key numbers

*   Extracted effective axial vector coupling constant: \(g^{\text{IBM}}_{A,\text{eff}} = 0.28 \pm 0.03\), obtained from the average of values from EC/\(\beta^{+}\) decay (0.313) and \(\beta^{-}\) decay (0.255) of \({}^{128}\text{I}\).
*   Hindrance factor for EC/\(\beta^{+}\) decay of \({}^{128}\text{I}\) (\(1^{+}_{1}\)) to \({}^{128}\text{Te}\) (\(0^{+}_{1}\)): \(h = 4.05\), derived from the ratio of IBM Gamow-Teller strength \(B(\text{GT})[\text{IBM}] = 1.676\) to experimental \(B(\text{GT})[\text{EC}] = 0.102 \pm 0.002\).
*   Hindrance factor for \(\beta^{-}\) decay of \({}^{128}\text{I}\) (\(1^{+}_{1}\)) to \({}^{128}\text{Xe}\) (\(0^{+}_{1}\)): \(h = 4.98\), derived from the ratio of IBM \(B(\text{GT})[\text{IBM}] = 0.248\) to experimental \(B(\text{GT})[\beta^{-}] = 0.0100 \pm 0.0001\).
*   Experimental \(\log ft\) for EC/\(\beta^{+}\) decay \({}^{128}\text{I}\) (\(1^{+}_{1}\)) \(\to\) \({}^{128}\text{Te}\) (\(0^{+}_{1}\)): \(5.049 \pm 0.007\).
*   Experimental \(\log ft\) for \(\beta^{-}\) decay \({}^{128}\text{I}\) (\(1^{+}_{1}\)) \(\to\) \({}^{128}\text{Xe}\) (\(0^{+}_{1}\)): \(6.061 \pm 0.005\).
*   Calculated magnetic moment of the \(5^{+}_{1}\) ground state of \({}^{130}\text{I}\): \(\mu = 3.12\ \mu_{\text{N}}\), compared to the experimental value \(3.349 \pm 0.007\ \mu_{\text{N}}\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of two-neutrino double-beta decay without the closure approximation, which had been performed within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) by Suhonen and the large-scale shell model (LSSM) by Caurier, by introducing a new approach based on the interacting boson model and its fermionic extensions. The calculation enables a systematic study of the mechanism of two-neutrino double-beta decay in tellurium isotopes, identifying which intermediate states in the odd-odd iodine nuclei contribute, and provides a framework for extracting the effective axial vector coupling constant in heavy nuclei where Gamow-Teller strength is strongly quenched.
