# Microscopic study on fusion reactions $^{40,48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ and the effect of tensor force

> We provide a microscopic description of the fusion reactions between $^{40,48}$Ca and $^{78}$Ni. The internuclear potentials are obtained using the density-constrained (DC) time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach and fusion cross sections are calculated via the incoming wave boundary condition method. By performing DC-TDHF calculations at several selected incident energies, the internuclear potentials for both systems are obtained and the energy-dependence of fusion barrier are revealed. The influence of tensor force on internuclear potentials of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ is more obvious than those of $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$. By comparing the calculated fusion cross sections between $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ and $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$, an interesting enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the former system is found, which can be explained by the narrow width of internuclear potential for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ while the barrier heights and positions are very close to each other. The tensor force suppresses the sub-barrier fusion cross sections of both two systems.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the sub-barrier fusion cross sections for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ are enhanced compared to $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$, despite the barrier heights and positions being very close, because the internuclear potential for the former system has a narrower width. The tensor force suppresses sub-barrier fusion cross sections for both systems, with a slightly larger effect for $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$.

## Method
The authors performed density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) calculations at incident energies of 70, 80, and 90 MeV to extract internuclear potentials for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ and $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$. The calculations used Skyrme effective interactions SIII, SIIIT (SIII plus tensor force), SLy5, and SLy5t (SLy5 plus tensor force) on a three-dimensional grid. Fusion cross sections were then obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the incoming wave boundary condition method using the transformed potentials.

## Key numbers
- For $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ with SLy5, the barrier height changes from 65.7 MeV at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=70$ MeV to 67.4 MeV at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=90$ MeV, and the barrier position changes from 11.7 fm to 11.3 fm.
- For $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ with SLy5, the barrier height is 66.4 MeV at 11.6 fm for $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=70$ MeV and 67.5 MeV at 11.3 fm for $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=90$ MeV.
- The barrier heights and positions for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ and $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ calculated with SLy5(t) and SIII(T) differ by about 1.5 MeV and 0.3 fm, respectively.
- At sub-barrier energies, the fusion cross sections of $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ are larger than those of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ by several times.

## Context
This work provides the first microscopic study of fusion reactions involving the doubly magic neutron-rich nucleus $^{78}$Ni, extending prior DC-TDHF studies on systems such as $^{40,48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ (Guo 2018) and $^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{40,48}\mathrm{Ca}$ (Oberacker 2013). The finding that the narrower potential width for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ enhances sub-barrier fusion is similar to the explanation for measurements of $^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$ versus $^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ (Kolata 2012). The results enable predictions for future experiments at radioactive-ion-beam facilities and demonstrate that the tensor force, which affects spin-unsaturated systems more strongly, suppresses sub-barrier fusion cross sections.
