# Understanding the cosmic abundance of $^{22}$Na: lifetime measurements in $^{23}$Mg

> Simulations of explosive nucleosynthesis in novae predict the production of $^{22}$Na, a key astronomical observable to constrain nova models. Its gamma-ray line at 1.275 MeV has not yet been observed by the gamma-ray space telescopes. The $^{20}$Ne/$^{22}$Ne ratio in presolar grains, a possible tool to identify nova grains, also depends on $^{22}$Na produced. Uncertainties on its yield in classical novae currently originate from the rate of the $^{22}$Na(p, $γ$)$^{23}$Mg reaction. At peak novae temperatures, this reaction is dominated by a resonance at E$_{\text{R}}$=0.204 MeV, corresponding to the $E_x$=7.785 MeV excited state in $^{23}$Mg. The resonance strengths measured so far disagree by one order of magnitude. An experiment has been performed at GANIL to measure the lifetime and the proton branching ratio of this key state, with a femtosecond resolution for the former. The reactions populating states in $^{23}$Mg have been studied with a high resolution detection set-up, i.e. the particle VAMOS, SPIDER and gamma tracking AGATA spectrometers, allowing the measurements of lifetimes and proton branchings. We present here a comparison between experimental results and shell-model calculations, that allowed us to assign the spin and parity of the key state. Rather small values obtained for reduced $M1$ matrix elements, $|M(M1)|\lesssim 0.5$ $μ_N$, and proton spectroscopic factors, $C^{2}S_{\text{p}}$<10$^{-2}$, seem to be beyond the accuracy of the shell model. With the reevaluated $^{22}$Na(p, $γ$)$^{23}$Mg rate, the $^{22}$Na detectability limit and its observation frequency from novae are found promising for the future space telescopes.

## Key claim

The paper resolves a factor-of-10 disagreement in the strength of the 0.204 MeV resonance in the \(^{22}\text{Na}(p,\gamma)^{23}\text{Mg}\) reaction by measuring the lifetime and proton branching ratio of the \(E_x = 7.785\) MeV state in \(^{23}\text{Mg}\), but the shell-model calculations show large relative uncertainties (up to a factor of 10) for the very small spectroscopic factors and M1 transition probabilities of this key state.

## Method

The \(E_x = 7.785\) MeV state in \(^{23}\text{Mg}\) was populated via the \(^{3}\text{He}(^{24}\text{Mg}, ^{4}\text{He})^{23}\text{Mg}^*\) reaction using a 4.6 MeV/u \(^{24}\text{Mg}\) beam on a \(^{3}\text{He}\)-implanted gold target at GANIL. The reaction products were measured with the VAMOS spectrometer for \(^{4}\text{He}\) identification and momenta, the AGATA \(\gamma\)-ray tracking spectrometer for \(\gamma\)-ray transitions and lifetimes, and the SPIDER silicon telescope for proton momenta, achieving femtosecond resolution for lifetimes. The measured lifetimes and proton branching ratios were compared with \(sd\)-shell model calculations using charge-dependent USDAcpn and USDBcpn interactions to assign the spin and parity of the key state.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the \(E_x = 7.785\) MeV state: \(\tau = 10(3)\) fs (from prior work Jenkins 2004), measured with femtosecond resolution in the present experiment.
- Resonance energy: \(E_{\text{R}} = 0.204\) MeV, corresponding to the \(E_x = 7.785\) MeV excited state in \(^{23}\text{Mg}\).
- Reduced M1 matrix elements for the key state: \(|M(M1)| \lesssim 0.5\ \mu_N\).
- Proton spectroscopic factor for the key state: \(C^{2}S_{\text{p}} < 10^{-2}\), with a measured value of approximately \(10^{-3}\).
- Gamma width of the key state: \(\Gamma_{\gamma} \lesssim 0.1\) eV.
- Gamma width of the IAS (\(E_x = 7.803\) MeV): 3.0(2) eV for the mirror state, with shell-model predictions of 2 eV (USDAcpn) and 3 eV (USDBcpn).
- Proton width of the IAS: \(\Gamma_{\text{p}} = 5(2)\) meV, with spectroscopic factor \(C^{2}S_{p} = 0.0056(20)\).
- log\(_{10}\)(ft) of the IAS: 3.305(23) (from Zhai 2007).
- RMS deviations for reduced M1 matrix elements: 0.322 \(\mu_N\) (USDAcpn) and 0.273 \(\mu_N\) (USDBcpn) for transitions in \(^{23}\text{Mg}\) and \(^{21,23}\text{Na}\).

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of the \(^{22}\text{Na}(p,\gamma)^{23}\text{Mg}\) reaction rate, which had a factor-of-10 uncertainty due to disagreements among direct measurements (Direct, Stegmuller 1996, Seuthe 1990) and a contradictory lifetime measurement by Jenkins (2004). The paper resolves the spin assignment of the key \(E_x = 7.785\) MeV state, previously proposed as \(7/2^+\) (Jenkins 2004) or \((3/2^+, 5/2^+)\) (Kwag 2021), assigning it as \(J^\pi = 7/2^+\) based on shell-model comparison and the absence of isospin mixing with the IAS. The reevaluated reaction rate enables predictions for the detectability of \(^{22}\text{Na}\) \(\gamma\)-ray emission from novae by future space telescopes COSI and e-ASTROGAM, with a conservative observation frequency of once every 20 years for COSI.
