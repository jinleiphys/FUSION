# Lattice QCD Calculation of Nuclear Parity Violation

> We present the first lattice QCD calculation of the leading-order momentum-independent parity violating coupling between pions and nucleons, $h_{π{NN}}^1$. The calculation performs measurements on dynamical anisotropic clover gauge configurations, with a spatial extent of $L\sim2.5$ fm, a spatial lattice spacing of $a_s\sim0.123$ fm, and a pion mass of $m_π\sim389$ MeV. While this first calculation does not include non-perturbative renormalization of the bare parity-violating operators, a chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, or contributions from disconnected (quark-loop) diagrams, these are expected to result in systematic errors within the quoted statistical error. We find a contribution from the `connected' diagrams of $h_{π{NN}}^{1,con}=(1.099\pm0.505^{+0.058}_{-0.064})\times10^{-7}$, consistent with current experimental bounds and previous model-dependent theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The first lattice QCD calculation of the leading-order momentum-independent parity violating coupling between pions and nucleons, \(h_{\pi{NN}}^{1}\), is presented, but it does not include non-perturbative renormalization, chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, or contributions from disconnected (quark-loop) diagrams; these omissions are expected to result in systematic errors within the quoted statistical error.

## Method

The calculation uses dynamical anisotropic clover gauge configurations with 2+1 quark flavors at a pion mass of 389 MeV, measuring three-point correlation functions with three-quark interpolating operators for proton and neutron-pion states. A ratio of three-point to two-point functions is formed to extract the matrix element, and the connected diagram contribution is isolated by performing separate quark propagator inversions at the source and at the weak operator insertion, with the energy injection term eliminated via an antisymmetric combination of forward and backward interactions.

## Key numbers

- Connected diagram contribution to \(h_{\pi{NN}}^{1}\): \(h_{\pi{NN}}^{1,con} = (1.099 \pm 0.505^{+0.058}_{-0.064}) \times 10^{-7}\), where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is systematic.
- Pion mass used: \(m_{\pi} \sim 389\) MeV.
- Spatial lattice spacing: \(a_s \sim 0.123\) fm; temporal lattice spacing: \(0.035\) fm.
- Spatial extent: \(L \sim 2.5\) fm; total lattice dimensions: \((2.5\ \text{fm})^3 \times 9\) fm.
- Total measurements: 100,871 of each smearing combination of \(H^{ij}\).
- Operator insertion timeslice: \(t' = 24\).

## Context

This work extends the model-dependent theoretical predictions of \(h_{\pi{NN}}^{1}\) from the quark model (Desplanques 1979), chiral solitons (Kaiser 1988, Kaiser 1989), and QCD sum rules (Henley 1995, Henley 1998, Lobov 2002) by providing the first direct calculation from QCD with quantifiable errors. The result is consistent with current experimental bounds and previous model estimates, and it enables future lattice QCD calculations at lighter pion masses with non-perturbative renormalization and inclusion of quark-loop diagrams, aiming to match the precision of the upcoming NPDGamma experiment at Oak Ridge.
