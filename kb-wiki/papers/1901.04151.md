# Impact of magnetic-field fluctuations on measurements of the chiral magnetic effect in collisions of isobaric nuclei

> We investigate the properties of electromagnetic fields in isobaric $_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}+\,_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}$ and $_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}+\,_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV by using a multiphase transport model, with special emphasis on the correlation between magnetic field direction and participant plane angle $Ψ_{2}$ (or spectator plane angle $Ψ_{2}^{\rm SP}$), i.e. $\langle{\rm cos}\ 2(Ψ_B - Ψ_{2})\rangle$ [or $\langle{\rm cos}\ 2(Ψ_B - Ψ_{2}^{\rm SP})\rangle$]. We confirm that the magnetic fields of $_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}+\,_{44}^{96}\textrm{Ru}$ collisions are stronger than those of $_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}+\,_{40}^{96}\textrm{Zr}$ collisions due to their larger proton fraction. We find that the deformation of nuclei has a non-negligible effect on $\langle{\rm cos}\ 2(Ψ_B - Ψ_{2})\rangle$ especially in peripheral events. Because the magnetic-field direction is more strongly correlated with $Ψ_{2}^{\rm SP}$ than with $Ψ_{2}$, the relative difference of the chiral magnetic effect observable with respect to $Ψ_{2}^{\rm SP}$ is expected to be able to reflect much cleaner information about the chiral magnetic effect with less influences of deformation.

## Key claim

The magnetic-field direction is more strongly correlated with the spectator plane angle than with the participant plane angle, so the relative difference of the chiral magnetic effect observable measured with respect to the spectator plane is expected to reflect cleaner information about the chiral magnetic effect with less influence from nuclear deformation, though the deformation of nuclei has a non-negligible effect on the correlation with the participant plane especially in peripheral events.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model to simulate collisions of Ru-96 and Zr-96 at a center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair. They calculated the initial electromagnetic fields at the field point r = (0,0,0) at time t = 0 using the Liénard-Wiechert potentials summed over all charged protons, and computed the participant plane from the spatial distribution of partons and the spectator plane from spectator neutrons of one projectile.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in magnetic field B between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions for case 1 is about 4% in central events and increases to 6% in peripheral events.
- The relative difference in magnetic field B for case 2 is about 4% in central events, gently decreases to 3% in mid-central events, then increases to 11.5% in peripheral events.
- The correlation ⟨cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2)⟩ has a maximum of about −0.5 for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at impact parameter b = 6–9 fm.
- The correlation ⟨cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2^SP)⟩ is around two times larger than the correlation with the participant plane, and in peripheral collisions it approaches one.
- For case 1, the relative ratio of ⟨cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2)⟩ between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr is about 5% in most central bins then decreases to −2% in most peripheral bins.
- For case 2, the relative ratio of ⟨cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2)⟩ is about 7% in most central bins and increases to about 27% in most peripheral bins.
- The relative ratio of ⟨(eB/m_π^2)^2 cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2)⟩ for case 1 is flat near 10%.
- The relative ratios of ⟨cos 2(Ψ_B − Ψ_2^SP)⟩ for both case 1 and case 2 are close to zero for noncentral collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the chiral magnetic effect in isobaric collisions at RHIC, following the proposal by Voloshin (2010) and calculations by Deng (2016) and Huang (2017). The authors use the AMPT model to systematically compare the magnetic field properties and their correlations with event planes in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, addressing the impact of nuclear deformation uncertainty (two cases of β2 values from Raman (1987) and Moller (1995)). The results enable experimental analyses at RHIC to use the spectator plane as a cleaner reference for isolating the chiral magnetic effect signal from background contributions.
