# K+ and K- potentials in hadronic matter are observable quantities

> The comparison of $K^+$ and $K^-$ spectra at low transverse momentum in light symmetric heavy ion reactions at energies around 2 AGeV allows for a direct experimental determination of the strength of the $K^+$ as well as of t he $K^-$ nucleus potential. Other little known or unknown input quantities like the production or rescattering cross sections of $K^+$ and $K^-$ mesons do not spoil this signal. This result, obtained by simulations of these reactio ns with the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model, may solve the longstanding question of the behaviour of the $K^-$ in hadronic matter and especially whether a $K^-$ condensate can be formed in heavy ion collisions.

# K+ and K- potentials in hadronic matter are observable quantities

## Key claim
The ratio of K+ and K- transverse momentum spectra at low momentum in light symmetric heavy ion reactions at energies around 2 AGeV allows a direct experimental determination of both the K+ and K- nucleus potential strengths, independent of uncertainties in production or rescattering cross sections. This claim is conditional on using light symmetric systems (specifically 12C+12C) and selecting the low transverse momentum region where the ratio is sensitive to the potential and insensitive to other poorly known input quantities.

## Method
The authors performed simulations of 12C+12C collisions at 2 AGeV incident energy with impact parameters 0-4 fm using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model. They separated K- mesons into two classes: those produced directly via BB → BB K+ K- reactions and those produced in secondary collisions (πY or BY → K-), then computed the ratio of K+ to K- transverse momentum spectra while varying the strength of the kaon-nucleus potential by multiplying the theoretical potential parameters αK+ = 0.07 and αK- = -0.22 by a factor α ranging from 0 to 2.

## Key numbers
- αK+ = 0.07 and αK- = -0.22 are the theoretical potential parameters from Korpa and Lutz (2004) for the linear density approximation ω(k=0,ρ) = m_K(ρ=0) (1 + α ρ/ρ0)
- The simulations use 12C+12C reactions at an incident energy of 2 AGeV with impact parameter range 0-4 fm
- For the K- production cross section: σ(NΔ) = 0.75 σ(NN) = 0.6 mb (E - E_thres), with energies measured in GeV in the hyperon rest frame
- The ratio of K+/K- spectra at low pT varies by approximately a factor of two when the K- potential is varied while keeping the K+ potential fixed at its theoretical value

## Context
This work addresses the longstanding controversy over K- behavior in hadronic matter, where theoretical predictions from different groups (Lutz 2001, Ramos 1999, Tolos 2006) differ substantially due to poorly known in-medium coupling constants and particle dressing. The authors extend prior IQMD simulations of strange meson production (Hartnack et al., "PR") by identifying an observable that isolates the potential dependence from other uncertain inputs. The approach enables experimental determination of the K- potential at normal nuclear matter density, which is relevant to the question of whether K- condensates can form in heavy ion collisions and whether the potential strength is compatible with observations from K- atoms.
