# Towards flavored bound states beyond rainbows and ladders

> We give a snapshot of recent progress in solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation with a beyond rainbow-ladder ansatz for the dressed quark-gluon vertex which includes ghost contributions. We discuss the motivations for this approach with regard to heavy-flavored bound states and form factors and briefly describe future steps to be taken.

## Key claim

The authors present a "beyond rainbow-ladder" ansatz for the dressed quark-gluon vertex that includes ghost contributions, but they acknowledge that the resulting dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is considerably weaker than in the best available phenomenological interaction models, and the vertex model must still pass the test of reproducing weak decay constants for heavy-light mesons.

## Method

The authors extract a momentum-dependent effective vertex function from lattice-QCD data on dressed quark propagators, gluon propagators, and ghost propagators by inverting the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation. They then define a "ghost-improved" Ball-Chiu vertex that satisfies an approximate Slavnov-Taylor identity, and use this vertex in the quark gap equation to compute mass functions for light and heavy quarks. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for heavy-light mesons is formulated with the fully dressed vertex, requiring quark propagator solutions at complex momenta.

## Key numbers

- The mass function for light u and d quarks at zero momentum is M(0) approximately 220 MeV using the extracted vertex, in accordance with lattice results from Bowman (2005) and Furui (2006)
- The phenomenological interaction model of Qin (2011) yields M(0) approximately 600 MeV for light quarks, indicating the extracted vertex produces considerably weaker dynamical chiral symmetry breaking
- For b quarks, the difference in mass function at zero momentum between the two models is Delta M_b(0) approximately 600 MeV
- The ratio of transition form factors measuring SU(3)_F breaking is F_+^{B to K}(0)/F_+^{B to pi}(0) = 1.23 and A_0^{B to K*}(0)/A_0^{B to rho}(0) = 1.25, from Ivanov (2007)
- Corrections to the heavy-quark limit in b to c transitions are approximately 20-30 percent, while in c to d transitions they can be as large as a factor of 2, from Ivanov (2007)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach to heavy-light mesons beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation, which Nguyen (2010) showed fails to adequately reproduce basic static observables like weak decay constants. The authors combine lattice-QCD data from Bowman (2005), Furui (2006), Bogolubsky (2009), and Oliveira (2012) with continuum methods, following the ghost-improved vertex approach of Aguilar (2010) and Fischer (2003). The next step is to compute the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and weak decay constants for D_(s) and B_(s) mesons, which will enable calculations of transition form factors needed for CKM matrix element determinations and studies of CP-violating observables in heavy meson decays.
