# Correlated-basis description of $α$-cluster and delocalized $0^+$ states in $^{16}$O

> A five-body calculation of $^{12}$C+$n$+$n$+$p$+$p$ is performed to take a step towards solving an outstanding problem in nuclear theory: The simultaneous and accurate description of the ground and first excited 0$^+$ states of $^{16}$O. The interactions between the constituent particles are chosen consistently with the energies of bound subsystems, especially $^{12}$C$+n$, $^{12}$C$+p$, and $α$-particle. The five-body dynamics is solved with the stochastic variational method on correlated Gaussian basis functions. No restriction is imposed on the four-nucleon configurations except the Pauli principle excluding the occupied orbits in $^{12}$C. The energies of both the ground and first excited states of $^{16}$O are obtained in excellent agreement with experiment. Analysis of the wave functions indicates spatially localized $α$-particle-like cluster structure for the excited state and shell-model-like delocalized structure for the ground state.

## Key claim

A five-body calculation of a $^{12}$C core plus four valence nucleons, without assuming a preformed $\alpha$-cluster, can simultaneously reproduce the energies of both the ground and first excited $0^+$ states of $^{16}$O in excellent agreement with experiment, provided the core-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions are chosen consistently with the energies of bound subsystems.

## Method

The five-body system $^{12}$C+$n$+$n$+$p$+$p$ is solved using the stochastic variational method on correlated Gaussian basis functions. The Minnesota potential (with strengths multiplied by 0.9814 to fine-tune the $\alpha$-particle binding energy) is used for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and parity-dependent Woods-Saxon potentials are fitted to reproduce the low-lying states of $^{13}$C and $^{13}$N. The Pauli principle is enforced by projecting out $0s_{1/2}$ and $0p_{3/2}$ harmonic-oscillator orbits of the core, using an orthogonality projection method with a pseudo-potential of $\lambda=10^4$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^{16}$O: $-$35.47 MeV from the $^{12}$C+$n$+$n$+$p$+$p$ threshold (experiment: $-$35.46 MeV)
- First excited $0^+$ state energy of $^{16}$O: $-$29.52 MeV from threshold (experiment: $-$29.41 MeV)
- Ground state energy of $^{16}$C: $-$18.47 MeV from threshold (experiment: $-$18.59 MeV)
- Excitation energy of the $0^+_2$ state in $^{16}$O: 6.05 MeV (experimental value)
- Point matter radius of $^{16}$O($0^+_1$): 2.47 fm (experiment: 2.57(2) fm)
- Point matter radius of $^{16}$O($0^+_2$): 3.03 fm
- Point matter radius of $^{16}$C($0^+_1$): 2.62 fm (experiment: 2.70(3) fm, 2.64(5) fm)
- $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ spectroscopic factor $S_\alpha$ for $^{16}$O($0^+_2$): 0.680
- $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ spectroscopic factor $S_\alpha$ for $^{16}$O($0^+_1$): 0.105
- Dimensionless reduced $\alpha$-width $\theta_\alpha^2$ for $^{16}$O($0^+_2$): 0.341 at $r=6$ fm
- Monopole matrix element $|\langle 0^+_2|r_p^2|0^+_1\rangle|$: 6.55 fm$^2$ (experiment: 3.55$\pm$0.21 fm$^2$)
- Average harmonic-oscillator quanta $M_Q$ for $^{16}$O($0^+_1$): 5.5; for $^{16}$O($0^+_2$): 14.3
- Standard deviation $\sigma_Q$ for $^{16}$O($0^+_1$): 2.9; for $^{16}$O($0^+_2$): 8.3

## Context

This work extends the semi-microscopic $^{12}$C+$\alpha$ two-cluster model of Suzuki (1976) toward a fully microscopic five-body description without assuming a preformed $\alpha$-cluster. It addresses the long-standing problem of shape coexistence in $^{16}$O, which has been studied with the no-core shell model (Maris, 2009), coupled-cluster theory (Wloch, 2005), and beyond-mean-field approaches (Bender, 2003; Shinohara, 2006), all of which struggled to reproduce the $0^+_2$ excitation energy. The results enable future studies including $^{12}$C core excitation and extension to heavier nuclei such as $^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ca, $^{44,52}$Ti, and $^{212}$Po to explore the universal role of $\alpha$-like correlation in shape coexistence and $\alpha$-decay.
