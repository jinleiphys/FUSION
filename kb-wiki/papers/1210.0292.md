# Charmonium dissociation and heavy quark transport in hot quenched lattice QCD

> We study the properties of charmonium states at finite temperature in quenched lattice QCD on large and fine isotropic lattices. We perform a detailed analysis of charmonium correlation and spectral functions both below and above Tc. Our analysis suggests that the S wave states disappear at about 1.5 Tc. The charm diffusion coefficient is estimated and found to be approximately 1/πT at 1.5Tc {\leq} T {\leq} 3Tc.

## Key claim
The analysis suggests that S-wave charmonium states (J/ψ and ηc) dissociate by 1.46 Tc, and the charm diffusion coefficient is estimated to be approximately 1/(πT) in the range 1.5 Tc ≤ T ≤ 3 Tc, though the authors note the estimate at 1.46 Tc is the most reliable due to better prior information.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the standard Wilson plaquette action for gluons and a non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson clover fermion action for valence charm quarks, with the vector meson mass tuned to nearly the physical J/ψ mass. They measured charmonium correlation functions on isotropic lattices of size 128³ × Nτ with Nτ = 96, 48, 32, 24, corresponding to temperatures 0.73, 1.46, 2.20, and 2.93 Tc, where Tc ≈ 270 MeV in quenched QCD. The spectral functions were extracted using the maximum entropy method (MEM), with default models informed by reconstructed correlator analyses and a transport peak ansatz for the vector channel.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.010 fm, inverse lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 18.974 GeV at β = 7.793
- Pseudoscalar ground state peak mass at 0.73 Tc: mηc = 3.31(4) GeV from Jackknife analysis
- At 1.46 Tc, the pseudoscalar peak shifts by about 0.8 GeV to around 4.1 GeV
- Ratio G(τ)/Grec(τ) deviates from unity at largest distances by about 5%, 8%, and 12% at 1.46, 2.20, and 2.93 Tc respectively in the pseudoscalar channel
- Ratio G(τ)/Grec(τ) deviates by about 16% at large distances in the vector channel
- From a one-parameter fit assuming complete bound state cancellation at 1.46 Tc: for M = 1.0 GeV, 2πTD ≈ 0.6; for M = 1.8 GeV, 2πTD ≈ 3.6
- Charm diffusion coefficient from MEM analysis: 2πTD ≈ 1.8–2.3 in the available temperature range

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of charmonium properties at finite temperature, following the conjecture of Matsui and Satz (1986) that heavy mesons may survive to higher temperatures due to Debye screening. The results are compared with perturbative next-to-leading order calculations by Caron-Huot and Moore (2007), AdS/CFT computations by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2003), Langevin dynamics by Moore and Teaney (2004), and heavy quark effective theory (HQET) results by Banerjee et al. (2011) and Francis et al. (2011), with which the diffusion coefficient estimates compare favorably. The findings enable improved modeling of J/ψ suppression and heavy quark flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
