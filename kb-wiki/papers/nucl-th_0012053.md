# Optical potentials for the antiproton nucleus interactions

> The nuclear interactions of atomic and low energy antiprotons are studied. Measurements of level shifts and widths in the lightest elements are analyzed and compared with new results obtained in heavy nuclei. Simple geometric properties of antiproton - nucleus interactions are demonstrated. Upon this background one finds some anomalies that indicate strong energy dependence in the subthreshold antiproton - nucleon interactions. The use of of antiproton in studies of the nuclear surface is briefly discussed.

# Optical potentials for the antiproton nucleus interactions

## Key claim
The nuclear interactions of atomic and low energy antiprotons can be studied through measurements of level shifts and widths, but the effective scattering length parameterization of the optical potential shows strong energy dependence in the subthreshold antiproton-nucleon interactions, particularly for the P-wave, which may indicate a resonance or quasi-bound state in the $^{13}P_{0}$ wave just below threshold.

## Method
The author analyzes atomic level shifts and widths from antiprotonic atoms across the periodic table, extracting effective scattering lengths $a$ from the optical potential $V^{opt}(R) = (2\pi/\mu_{NN}) a \rho(R)$. For light nuclei (p, D, $^{3}$He, $^{4}$He), the $\bar{p}N$ scattering parameters $a_0$ (S-wave scattering length) and $a_1$ (P-wave scattering volume) are extracted using the Trueman formula and multiple scattering series summation methods. For heavy nuclei, a best-fit phenomenological optical potential is constructed using Fermi densities and compared with 150 level shifts and widths from carbon to uranium.

## Key numbers
- The effective scattering length from early atomic experiments was $a \approx (-1.5 - i 2.5)$ fm, corresponding to a potential depth of $(-120 - i 200)$ MeV at the nuclear surface.
- For protonium ($p\bar{p}$), the S-wave scattering length is $a_0 = 0.83(1) - i0.69(3)$ fm and the P-wave scattering volume is $a_1 = -0.4(8) - i0.64(4)$ fm$^3$.
- For antiprotonic deuterium, the $\bar{p}D$ scattering length is $A_0^D = (0.706(17) - i0.39(27))$ fm and volume $A_1^D = (3.15(33) - i3.18(19))$ fm$^3$.
- The best-fit constant scattering length for light atoms is $a = (-1.10(5) - i1.85(5))$ fm, with $\chi^2/F = 1.19$ for $Z < 17$, rising to 3.20 for all Z.
- The $\bar{p}n / \bar{p}p$ capture ratio $\sigma_{n/p}$ from atomic level widths ranges from 0.96(9) for $^{96}$Zr lower level to 5.13(80) for $^{106}$Cd upper level.
- The $\bar{p}n / \bar{p}p$ capture ratio from single nucleon capture experiments ranges from 0.5(1) for $^{106}$Cd to 5.00(21) for $^{116}$Cd.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of antiprotonic atom data from the early measurements of Roberson (1977) and the recent CERN PS209 experiment (Trzcinska, 2000), which detected more transitions with improved precision and fine structure resolution. The paper builds on the Paris potential calculations for $\bar{N}N$ interactions (1994) and the multiple scattering series summation method (Wycech, 1985). The results enable comparison between X-ray measurements and radiochemical single-nucleon capture experiments (Lubinski, 1994), testing whether antiprotons can serve as a valid tool to study the distant nuclear surface, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei and nuclei with loosely bound protons.
