# Deeply bound meson-nuclear states: concepts and strategies

> This presentation reviews recent developments in the understanding of low-energy pion- and kaon-nucleon interactions as they relate to deeply bound pionic atoms and possible antikaon-nuclear bound states. A brief update is given on 1s states of pionic atoms, summarizing their theoretical interpretation based on in-medium chiral effective field theory. This is followed by a state-of-the-art discussion of low-energy $\bar{K}N$ interactions, with special emphasis on the subthreshold region relevant to the proposed kaon-nuclear systems.

# Deeply bound meson-nuclear states: concepts and strategies

## Key claim

The hypothesis that narrow, deeply bound $K^-$-nuclear states can exist depends on whether the attractive $\bar{K}N$ interaction remains active below the $\pi\Sigma$ threshold (around $\sqrt{s} \simeq 1.33$ GeV), where the dominant $I=0$ decay channel is turned off and the imaginary part of the amplitude drops; the present chiral SU(3) coupled-channels analysis provides estimates for the subthreshold $K^-$-nucleus potential but accounts for less than half of the attraction needed to explain the strong binding reported in $K^-$-tribaryon systems.

## Method

The paper combines in-medium chiral perturbation theory for pionic atoms with chiral SU(3) coupled-channels dynamics for antikaon-nucleon interactions. For pionic atoms, the energy-dependent pion self-energy is computed from two-loop in-medium chiral perturbation theory and inserted into a Klein-Gordon equation. For kaonic systems, the $K^-N$ forward scattering amplitudes are obtained by iterating driving terms from the next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon Lagrangian to all orders in coupled-channel integral equations, generating the $\Lambda(1405)$ dynamically as an $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$ quasibound state. These subthreshold amplitudes are then used to construct the local s-wave $K^-$-nucleus potential via the kaon self-energy in matter.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV (vacuum value)
- Effective density-dependent pion decay constant at nuclear matter density: $f_\pi^*(\rho_0) \simeq f_\pi\left(1 - 0.2\right)$, i.e., a decrease of about 20% at $\rho_0 \simeq 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- Nuclear matter density: $\rho_0 \simeq 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- Predicted $K^-$ effective mass at normal nuclear matter density drops to about three quarters of its vacuum value (from calculations in WKW96)
- Predicted $K^-$ in-medium width at normal nuclear matter density and beyond: reduced to 10 MeV or less
- Predicted $K^-$-nucleus s-wave potential depth at $\rho = \rho_0$: about -120 MeV
- Subthreshold $K^-p$ potential at $\omega = m_K - 100$ MeV: $U_p^{(0)} \simeq -(160 + 30\,i)$ MeV
- Subthreshold $K^-n$ potential at $\omega = m_K - 100$ MeV: $U_n^{(0)} \simeq -(65 + 15\,i)$ MeV
- Ratio of imaginary to real part of $K^-n$ subthreshold amplitude at about 100 MeV below threshold: $Im\,f(K^- n) / Re\,f(K^- n) \simeq 0.2$
- Estimated cross section for $\bar{p}p \rightarrow 2K^+2K^-$ near threshold: $\sigma \sim 10$ $\mu$b
- Threshold energy for $\bar{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^-K^+K^+$ in the lab frame: $T_{lab}(\bar{p}) \simeq 204$ MeV

## Context

This work reviews and extends the chiral dynamics approach to deeply bound meson-nuclear states, building on the Ericson-Ericson framework for pionic atoms (EE66) and the Akaishi-Yamazaki hypothesis (AY02) for kaon-nuclear bound states. The pionic atom analysis updates calculations from KKW03 and KW01, using data from Suz04. The kaon-nucleon analysis extends the chiral SU(3) coupled-channels work of KSW95 and KWW97 with the most recent update from BNW05, incorporating new kaonic hydrogen data from the DEAR experiment (Beer04). The paper enables future experimental searches for deeply bound kaonic systems, particularly proposing a reaction using antiproton annihilation on nuclear targets ($\bar{p}A \rightarrow K^+K^+ + [K^-K^-pp(A-3)]$) as a signature for strong binding of $K^-$ pairs, which could be studied at the future FAIR/GSI facility.
