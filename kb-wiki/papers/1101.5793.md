# Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics at Relativistic Collider Energies

> The novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach is applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies with respect to differential hadronic spectra in comparison to available data. The PHSD approach is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons (DQPM) matched to reproduce recent lattice-QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest group in thermodynamic equilibrium. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks), respectively, obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality as well as energy-momentum conservation. Our dynamical studies for heavy-ion collisions at relativistic collider energies are compared to earlier results from the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) approach - incorporating no explicit dynamical partonic phase - as well as to experimental data from the STAR, PHENIX, BRAHMS and PHOBOS collaborations for Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy of $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV. We find a reasonable reproduction of hadron rapidity distributions and transverse mass spectra and also a fair description of the elliptic flow of charged hadrons as a function of the centrality of the reaction and the transverse momentum $p_T$. Furthermore, an approximate quark-number scaling of the elliptic flow $v_2$ of hadrons is observed in the PHSD results, too.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport approach, when refitted to the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD results (critical temperature T_c ≈ 160 MeV and critical energy density ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³), provides a reasonable reproduction of hadron rapidity distributions, transverse mass spectra, and a fair description of elliptic flow for charged hadrons at top RHIC energy, though the authors note that alternative initial conditions (e.g., a color-glass condensate) might be necessary at RHIC or LHC energies.

## Method

The PHSD approach combines a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons (DQPM) with hadronic transport from the HSD model, using covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion to mesons and three-quark fusion to baryons, obeying flavor current conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation. The DQPM parameters were refitted to reproduce lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (Aoki et al., 2009), yielding a critical temperature T_c ≈ 160 MeV and critical energy density ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³. Initial conditions include string melting when local energy density exceeds ε_c, with partons assigned finite masses and widths from the DQPM spectral functions, and the system is evolved using off-shell transport equations with a scalar mean field derived from the DQPM potential energy density.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 160 MeV and critical energy density ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³ from the refitted DQPM, matched to lattice QCD results from Aoki et al. (2009)
- At t ≈ 2.5 fm/c in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, about 85% of the energy at midrapidity (|y| ≤ 1) is carried by partonic degrees of freedom
- The width parameter b for meson formation is fixed to b = 0.66 fm, corresponding to an average rms radius of mesons
- Hadronization of partons to hadrons occurs essentially within 6-8 fm/c for central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV
- The DQPM uses d_g = 16 transverse gluonic degrees of freedom and d_q = d_ȯ = 18 fermion degrees of freedom for three flavors (N_f = 3)

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach previously applied at SPS energies (40 to 160 A GeV) in Bratkovskaya et al. (2008, 2009) to top RHIC energy (√s = 200 GeV), comparing results to the HSD approach (which lacks explicit partonic degrees of freedom) and to experimental data from the STAR, PHENIX, BRAHMS, and PHOBOS collaborations. The refitted DQPM parameters now match the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD results (Aoki et al., 2009) rather than the earlier Cheng et al. (2008) lattice data, resulting in a lower critical temperature and energy density. The approach enables systematic comparison of differential hadronic observables at RHIC energies and provides a framework for studying the transition from partonic to hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions, with future applications to hard probes and LHC energies anticipated.
