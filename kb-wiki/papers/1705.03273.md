# Phase structure of NJL model with weak renormalization group

> We analyze the chiral phase structure of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature and density by using the functional renormalization group (FRG). The renormalization group (RG) equation for the fermionic effective potential $V(σ;t)$ is given as a partial differential equation, where $σ:=\bar ψψ$ and $t$ is a dimensionless RG scale. When the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (D$χ$SB) occurs at a certain scale $t_c$, $V(σ;t)$ has singularities originated from the phase transitions, and then one cannot follow RG flows after $t_c$. In this study, we introduce the weak solution method to the RG equation in order to follow the RG flows after the D$χ$SB and to evaluate the dynamical mass and the chiral condensate in low energy scales. It is shown that the weak solution of the RG equation correctly captures vacuum structures and critical phenomena within the pure fermionic system. We show the chiral phase diagram on temperature, chemical potential and the four-Fermi coupling constant.

## Key claim

The authors show that the weak solution method applied to the functional renormalization group (FRG) equation for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can follow RG flows after dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DχSB) occurs, without introducing auxiliary bosonic fields, provided the Rankine-Hugoniot condition is used to resolve the multi-valued mass function that arises at and after the critical scale.

## Method

The authors start from the Wegner-Houghton (sharp cutoff) FRG equation for the fermionic effective potential V(σ;t) of the NJL model with one flavor and Nc colors, at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ. They employ the large-Nc approximation and a uniform σ approximation, obtaining a partial differential equation for V. To handle the singularities that appear when the four-Fermi coupling diverges at the chiral symmetry breaking scale tc, they reformulate the RG equation as a "weak" equation by moving derivatives onto a test function. They then solve the system via the method of characteristics, obtaining ordinary differential equations for σ(t), M(t) = ∂σV, and V(t). When the mass function becomes multi-valued after tc, the physical single-valued solution is selected by imposing the Rankine-Hugoniot (equal-area) condition, which defines the discontinuity ("shock") position σ*(t).

## Key numbers

* The critical fixed point for the dimensionless rescaled four-Fermi coupling is G̃c = 1, where G̃ = GΛ²/2π².
* The critical scale at which the four-Fermi coupling diverges is tc = (1/2) log[G̃0/(G̃0 − G̃c)] for an initial coupling G̃0 > G̃c.
* The initial four-Fermi coupling used in the numerical examples is g0 := G0Λ0²/2π² = 2.01.
* The dynamical quark mass is defined as Mphys = limσ→0+ M(σ;∞) (the limit from positive σ at the infrared end).

## Context

This work extends the weak solution method previously introduced by Aoki (2014) for the NJL model at zero temperature and density to the case of finite temperature and chemical potential. It contrasts with the auxiliary field (Hubbard-Stratonovich) method used in earlier FRG studies by Aoki (1999) and others, which introduces bosonic degrees of freedom to avoid singularities but complicates the analysis. The present approach enables the study of both second-order and first-order chiral phase transitions within a pure fermionic FRG framework, without the need for bosonization, and yields the chiral phase diagram on the temperature-chemical potential-four-Fermi coupling parameter space.
