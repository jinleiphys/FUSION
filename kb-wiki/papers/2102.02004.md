# Role of dense matter in tidal deformations of inspiralling neutron stars and in gravitational waveform with unified equations of state

> The role of dense-matter properties in the tidal deformability of a cold nonaccreted neutron star is further investigated. Using the set of Brussels-Montreal unified equations of state, we have computed the gravitoelectric Love numbers $k_\ell$ and the gravitomagnetic Love numbers $j_\ell$ up to $\ell=5$. Their relative importance and their sensitivity to the symmetry energy and the neutron-matter stiffness are numerically assessed. Their impact on the phase of the gravitational-wave signal emitted by binary neutron star inspirals is also discussed.

## Key claim

Using the Brussels-Montreal unified equations of state, the authors computed gravitoelectric Love numbers \(k_\ell\) and gravitomagnetic Love numbers \(j_\ell\) up to \(\ell=5\) for cold nonaccreted neutron stars, finding that these higher-order tidal coefficients are mainly sensitive to the stiffness of the neutron-matter equation of state and only weakly to the symmetry energy, with the crust's influence on the Love numbers being almost exactly compensated by its effect on the stellar radius.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations simultaneously with the differential equations for static metric perturbations (gravitoelectric and gravitomagnetic) for each of the Brussels-Montreal unified equations of state (BSk19, BSk22, BSk24, BSk25, BSk26). They computed the Love numbers \(k_2\) through \(k_5\) and \(j_2\) through \(j_5\) using closed-form analytic expressions derived from the formalism of Damour and Nagar (2009) and Poisson (2015), with explicit formulas for \(k_5\), \(j_3\), \(j_4\), and \(j_5\) derived for the first time. The tidal corrections to the gravitational-wave phase were then evaluated using post-Newtonian expressions up to the relevant orders.

## Key numbers

- For a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star using the BSk24 equation of state, the value of \(k_5\) represents only 4% of the value of \(k_2\), and \(j_5\) represents 3% of \(j_2\).
- The gravitomagnetic tidal coefficients are about an order of magnitude smaller than their gravitoelectric counterparts.
- The tidal correction \(\Psi_3\) associated with \(k_3\) is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the leading tidal term \(\Psi_2\).
- The correction \(\widetilde{\Psi}_2\) induced by the gravitomagnetic Love number \(j_2\) lies between those associated with \(k_3\) and \(k_4\).
- The gravitomagnetic Love numbers \(j_\ell\) are positive for static fluids but negative for irrotational fluids, with comparable absolute magnitudes.
- The symmetry-energy coefficient \(J\) was varied among 29, 30, 31, and 32 MeV across the BSk22, BSk24, and BSk25 functionals, with the incompressibility coefficient \(K_v\) fixed to \(240\pm10\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Perot and Chamel, 2019, 2020) on the role of neutron-matter stiffness and symmetry energy in the gravitoelectric Love number \(k_2\) and tidal deformability, now considering higher-order multipoles up to \(\ell=5\) for both gravitoelectric and gravitomagnetic perturbations. It corrects earlier calculations by Kumar and others (2017) which used equations of state like NL3 that are incompatible with experimental nuclear data and which incorrectly computed the gravitomagnetic Love number \(j_2\). The results enable more realistic gravitational-waveform templates for upcoming third-generation detectors such as the Einstein telescope, potentially allowing measurements of gravitomagnetic effects that could discriminate between static and irrotational fluid interiors and provide additional tests of general relativity.
