# Solar Neutrinos from CNO Electron Capture

> The neutrino flux from the sun is predicted to have a CNO-cycle contribution as well as the known pp-chain component. Previously, only the fluxes from beta+ decays of 13N, 15O, and 17F have been calculated in detail. Another neutrino component that has not been widely considered is electron capture on these nuclei. We calculate the number of interactions in several solar neutrino detectors due to neutrinos from electron capture on 13N, 15O, and 17F, within the context of the Standard Solar Model. We also discuss possible non-standard models where the CNO flux is increased.

## Key claim
The neutrino flux from electron capture on CNO-cycle nuclei (13N, 15O, 17F) in the Sun has been calculated and is small relative to the pp-chain flux, but it introduces a model-dependent background to the SNO measurement of the total 8B flux at the level of about one event per year. The calculation assumes the Standard Solar Model (SSM) CNO contribution of 1.5% of solar luminosity, but the authors also consider non-standard models where the CNO flux is increased up to 99.95% of solar luminosity.

## Method
The authors calculated the electron capture neutrino flux from 13N, 15O, and 17F in the CNO cycle by relating the electron capture rate to the beta+ decay flux, including corrections for both bound and continuum electrons in the solar plasma. They evaluated the electron density ratios at a fixed point in the solar core (0.057 solar radius, where CNO fluxes peak) and integrated over the entire solar volume, then computed interaction rates for charged-current, neutral-current, and elastic scattering processes on various detector targets (deuterium, 7Li, 37Cl, 71Ga, and electrons) using published cross-sections.

## Key numbers
- SSM beta+ decay flux for 13N: 5.48 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (with +0.21%/-0.17% uncertainties)
- SSM beta+ decay flux for 15O: 4.80 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1 (with +0.25%/-0.19% uncertainties)
- SSM beta+ decay flux for 17F: 5.63 × 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1 (with +0.25%/-0.25% uncertainties)
- Electron capture flux for 13N: 4.33 × 10^5 cm^-2 s^-1
- Electron capture flux for 15O: 1.90 × 10^5 cm^-2 s^-1
- Electron capture flux for 17F: 3.32 × 10^3 cm^-2 s^-1
- Electron capture neutrino energies: 13N at 2.220 MeV, 15O at 2.754 MeV, 17F at 2.761 MeV
- In SNO, the 15O electron capture NC rate is about 0.4 events per year; below the 5.5 MeV analysis threshold there were about 13 events expected from this source
- In the 99.95% CNO model, the 15O beta+ decay flux is 3.41 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1 (70-fold increase over SSM), raising the 15O electron capture NC rate in SNO to 30 yr^-1
- The 8B electron-capture branch flux is 1.3 cm^-2 s^-1
- The CNO cycle contribution to solar luminosity is constrained to less than 7.3% at 3 sigma

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of CNO neutrino fluxes by Bahcall (who considered only beta+ decays and electron capture from free electrons, not bound states) to include electron capture from bound electrons in the solar plasma. The authors note that Bahcall (no year given) considered electron capture from free electrons but not bound state electrons. The calculation uses the Standard Solar Model of Bahcall and Pinsonneault (no year given) and provides interaction rates for existing detectors (SNO, BOREXINO, KamLAND, Homestake, Sage, Gallex, GNO) as well as future detectors like a proposed 7Li-based detector suggested by Haxton (no year given). The results enable precision measurements of CNO fluxes using the mono-energetic line spectrum from electron capture, and allow setting more stringent limits on the CNO fraction of solar luminosity.
