# Accurate Charge-Dependent Nucleon-Nucleon Potential at Fourth Order of Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We present the first nucleon-nucleon potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (fourth order) of chiral perturbation theory. Charge-dependence is included up to next-to-leading order of the isospin-violation scheme. The accuracy for the reproduction of the NN data below 290 MeV lab. energy is comparable to the one of phenomenological high-precision potentials. Since NN potentials of order three and less are known to be deficient in quantitative terms, the present work shows that the fourth order is necessary and sufficient for a reliable NN potential derived from chiral effective Lagrangians. The new potential provides a promising starting point for exact few-body calculations and microscopic nuclear structure theory (including chiral many-body forces derived on the same footing).

## Key claim

The paper presents the first nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential at fourth order (N3LO) of chiral perturbation theory that reproduces NN scattering data below 290 MeV laboratory energy with a chi-squared per datum comparable to phenomenological high-precision potentials, whereas potentials at third order and below are quantitatively deficient. The authors qualify that the fourth order is "necessary and sufficient" for a reliable NN potential from chiral effective Lagrangians, but note that three of the low-energy constants had to be treated as semi-free parameters in the fit rather than being fixed from pion-nucleon data alone.

## Method

The authors constructed a momentum-space NN potential by combining contact terms (24 at N3LO, plus 2 charge-dependent contacts) with irreducible pion-exchange diagrams up to two-pion exchange at N3LO, using analytic expressions from Kaiser (2001) and Entem and Machleidt (2002). All contributions were multiplied by an exponential regulator with cutoff Lambda = 0.5 GeV to enforce the low-momentum validity of chiral perturbation theory, and the potential was solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain phase shifts and observables. The 29 total parameters (26 contact parameters plus three low-energy constants c2, c3, c4 varied from their pion-nucleon values) were optimized by first fitting phase shifts and then minimizing chi-squared directly against the 1999 np and pp databases below 290 MeV.

## Key numbers

- np chi-squared per datum for 0-290 MeV: N3LO = 1.10 (2402 data), compared to NNLO = 10.1, NLO = 36.2, and AV18 = 1.04
- pp chi-squared per datum for 0-290 MeV: N3LO = 1.50 (2057 data), compared to NNLO = 35.4, NLO = 80.1, and AV18 = 1.38
- Regulator cutoff Lambda = 0.5 GeV used throughout
- Low-energy constants used: c1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c2 = 2.80 GeV^-1, c3 = -3.20 GeV^-1, c4 = 5.40 GeV^-1 (with c3 chosen on the low side and c4 substantially above their pion-nucleon determinations)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV and axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.29 used

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory program for nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and developed by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1996) at NNLO, and by Epelbaum et al. (1998, 2000) who constructed the first momentum-space NN potential at NNLO. The authors show that the N3LO potential achieves accuracy comparable to the phenomenological high-precision Argonne V18 potential of Wiringa et al. (1995), whereas earlier chiral potentials at NLO and NNLO had chi-squared per datum above 20 and were therefore unacceptable for quantitative few-body and nuclear structure calculations. The new potential enables exact few-body calculations and microscopic nuclear structure theory using consistent chiral two- and three-nucleon forces derived on the same footing.
