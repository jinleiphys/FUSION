# Probing cold nuclear matter effects with the productions of isolated-$γ$ and $γ$+jet in p$+$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 8.16 TeV

> We investigate the cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects on the productions of the isolated prompt photon and $γ+$jet in proton-lead collisions at $\rm 8.16$ TeV under the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative quantum chromodynamics calculations with four parametrizations for nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), i.e. DSSZ, EPPS16, nCTEQ15, nIMParton. Our theoretical calculations provide good descriptions of pp baseline in the ATLAS collaboration and make predictions for future experimental results at $\rm p$+$\rm Pb$ collisions. We calculate the dependence of the nuclear modification factor of isolated prompt photon on transverse momentum $\rm p_T^γ$ and pseudo-rapidity $η^γ$ at very forward and backward rapidity regions, and demonstrate that the forward-to-backward yield asymmetries $\rm Y_{\rm pPb}^{\rm asym}$ as a function of $\rm p_T^γ$ with different nPDFs parametrizations have diverse behaviors. Furthermore, the nuclear modification factor of isolated-$γ+$jet $\rm R_{\rm pPb}^{γJet}$ as a function of $γ+$jet's pseudo-rapidity $η_{γ\rm Jet}=\frac{1}{2}(η_γ+η_{\rm Jet})$ at different average transverse momentum $\rm p_T^{\rm avg}=\frac{1}{2}(\rm p_T^γ+\rm p_T^{\rm Jet})$ has been discussed, which can facilitate a tomography study of CNM effects with precise locations in a rather wide kinematic region by varying the transverse momenta and rapidities of both isolated photon and jet in p+A collisions.

# Probing cold nuclear matter effects with the productions of isolated-γ and γ+jet in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 8.16 TeV

## Key claim

The authors show that the nuclear modification factor for isolated prompt photon production in p+Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV is dominated by shadowing effects at forward rapidity (3 < η^γ < 4) and by EMC effects at backward rapidity (−4 < η^γ < −3), but the magnitude and p_T dependence of these effects vary significantly across the four nuclear PDF parametrizations (DSSZ, EPPS16, nCTEQ15, nIMParton), with the nIMParton set being applied for the first time to hard processes in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations using the JETPHOX program with CT14 free-proton PDFs, replacing the free-proton PDFs with four nuclear PDF parametrizations (DSSZ, EPPS16, nCTEQ15 at NLO; nIMParton at LO) to incorporate cold nuclear matter effects. They computed the nuclear modification factor R_pPb for isolated prompt photons as a function of p_T^γ and η^γ at forward (3 < η^γ < 4) and backward (−4 < η^γ < −3) rapidity, the forward-to-backward yield asymmetry Y_pPb^asym, and the nuclear modification factor for γ+jet production as a function of the combined pseudo-rapidity η_γJet = ½(η_γ + η_Jet) at different average transverse momentum p_T^avg = ½(p_T^γ + p_T^Jet) intervals.

## Key numbers

- The isolated photon cross section in p+p collisions at 8 TeV was calculated up to p_T^γ = 1 TeV and showed good agreement with ATLAS experimental data.
- At forward rapidity (3 < η^γ < 4), the average Bjorken ⟨x_Pb⟩ is lower than 0.055, indicating shadowing effect dominance.
- At backward rapidity (−4 < η^γ < −3), the average Bjorken ⟨x_Pb⟩ ranges from 0.25 to 0.8, corresponding to the EMC effect region.
- The nIMParton EMC minimum appears at p_T^γ = 150 GeV with corresponding ⟨x_Pb⟩ around 0.5, the lowest among the four parametrizations.
- For γ+jet production, the shadowing region corresponds to η_γJet > 1.6, anti-shadowing to −0.2 < η_γJet < 1.6, and EMC effects to η_γJet < −0.2 at the 150 < p_T^avg < 200 GeV interval.
- The γ+jet nuclear modification factor was computed at two p_T^avg intervals: 115 GeV < p_T^avg < 4000 GeV and 200 GeV < p_T^avg < 250 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold nuclear matter effects using prompt photon and photon-jet production in p+A collisions, building on the EPS09 framework (Eskola 2009) and its successor EPPS16 (Eskola 2016), as well as the DSSZ (de Florian 2011), nCTEQ15 (Kusina 2015), and nIMParton (Wang 2016) parametrizations. The calculations use the same JETPHOX NLO pQCD program and ATLAS isolation cuts (E_T^iso < 6 GeV, R_cone = 0.4) as previous ATLAS measurements at 8 TeV (Aaboud 2016). The work enables a tomographic study of cold nuclear matter effects by varying the transverse momenta and rapidities of both the isolated photon and jet, allowing precise kinematic localization of shadowing, anti-shadowing, and EMC effect regions in p+Pb collisions at LHC energies.
