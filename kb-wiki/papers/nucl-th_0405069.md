# $J$-pairing Interactions of Fermions in a Single-$j$ Shell

> In this talk I shall introduce our recent works on general pairing interactions and pair truncation approximations for fermions in a single-j shell, including the spin zero dominance, features of eigenvalues of fermion systems in a single-j shell interacting by a $J-$pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for fermions in a single-\(j\) shell interacting via a \(J\)-pairing interaction (only one \(G_J\) set to \(-1\) and all others zero), the low-lying states can be well approximated by wavefunctions built from pairs of spin \(J\), and that for \(n=3\) the eigenvalues are exactly given by a closed form in terms of six-\(j\) symbols; however, the approximation fails for some states when using pairs with angular momentum \(J_{\rm max}-2\) rather than \(J_{\rm max}\).

## Method

The author diagonalizes the Hamiltonian \(H_J = - A^{J\dagger} \cdot A^J\) for fermions in a single-\(j\) shell, where only one pairing strength \(G_J\) is set to \(-1\) and all others to zero. For \(n=3\), the basis is constructed from a fermion coupled to a \(J\)-pair, and the normalization and Hamiltonian matrix elements are evaluated analytically using six-\(j\) symbols, yielding eigenvalues \(E_I^{J(j)} = - N^{(I)}_{jJ; jJ}\). For \(n=4\) and higher, the \(J\)-pair truncation approximation is tested by comparing exact shell-model diagonalization results with those obtained using only states built from \(J\)-pairs.

## Key numbers

* For \(j=21/2\) and \(n=4\), the number of different \(G_J\) values giving a ground state with spin \(I=0\) is \(\mathcal{N}_0 = 5\), while \(\mathcal{N}_2 = \mathcal{N}_4 = \mathcal{N}_8 = \mathcal{N}_{20} = \mathcal{N}_{28} = \mathcal{N}_{36} = 1\); the total number of \(G_J\) values is \(N = (2j+1)/2\).
* For three fermions in a single-\(j\) shell with \(j=21/2\) and \(J_{\rm max}\) pairing, the eigenvalue of the \(I^{(3)}_{\rm max} (=3j-3)\) state is \(-\frac{59}{26} = -2.26923076923077\).
* For four fermions in a single-\(j\) shell with \(j=21/2\) and \(J_{\rm max}\) pairing, the eigenvalues \(\mathcal{E}_I\) for \(I=18\) through \(25\) range from \(-2.26923076925915\) (for \(I=18\)) to \(-2.26925701778767\) (for \(I=25\)), and the overlaps with states constructed from a fermion coupled to a three-fermion state are almost 1 within a precision of \(10^{-5}\).
* For \(n=3\), the non-zero eigenvalue for a given \(I\) and \(J\) is \(E_I^{J(j)} = -1 - 2(2J+1) \left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} J & j & I \\ J & j & j \end{array} \right\}\).

## Context

This work extends the discovery of \(0^+\) ground state dominance in the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) and its relation to generalized seniority by Johnson, Bertsch, Dean and Talmi (1999). It provides an empirical method to predict ground-state spin probabilities using only the single-\(j\) shell geometry, and introduces a \(J\)-pair truncation approximation that is exact for \(n=3\) and accurate for low-lying states of \(n=4,5,6\). The results enable future studies of pairing correlations in nuclear structure, particularly for systems with \(J_{\rm max}\) pairing where eigenvalues approach integers and where states of \(n=4\) can be traced back to \(n=3\) configurations.
