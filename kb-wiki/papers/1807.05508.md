# Anisotropic hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC

> In this proceedings contribution, we review 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP). Then, we show some recent phenomenological comparisons between the aHydroQP model and some experimental results. We show comparisons between aHydroQP and Pb-Pb 2.76 TeV collisions from the ALICE collaboration and Au-Au 200 GeV collisions from RHIC experiments. We show that the quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics model is able to describe the experimental results for Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions quite well for many observables such as the spectra, multiplicity, elliptic flow, and HBT radii in many centrality classes.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model can describe experimental results for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV quite well for many observables including spectra, multiplicity, elliptic flow, and HBT radii across many centrality classes, but the authors present only a small subset of observables due to space limitations and refer to original references for more details.

## Method

The authors take moments of the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time approximation (RTA) collisional kernel to obtain 3+1d dynamical equations for quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics, using a distribution function in the local rest frame that depends on anisotropy parameters and a temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass. They then compare the model predictions to experimental data from the ALICE collaboration for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and from PHOBOS and PHENIX collaborations for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, fitting parameters to the data.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature at proper time tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c: T_0 = 600 MeV for Pb-Pb 2.76 TeV collisions
- Initial temperature at proper time tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c: T_0 = 455 MeV for Au-Au 200 GeV collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 0.159 for Pb-Pb 2.76 TeV collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 0.179 for Au-Au 200 GeV collisions
- Freeze-out temperature fixed at T_FO = 130 MeV for both collision systems

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics approach, which was motivated by the fact that the quark-gluon plasma is highly momentum anisotropic at early times after nuclear passage, building on prior work by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010, 2012), Ryblewski (2012), Bazow (2013), and Nopoush (2014). The 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics model enables improved phenomenological comparisons with heavy-ion collision data from both RHIC and LHC energies, and the authors note that recent reviews of anisotropic hydrodynamics are available from Strickland (2014) and Alqahtani (2017).
