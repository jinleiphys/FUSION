# Geometric Phase in Quantum Billiards with a Pointlike Scatterer

> We examine the quantum energy levels of rectangular billiards with a pointlike scatterer in one and two dimensions. By varying the location and the strength of the scatterer, we systematically find diabolical degeneracies among various levels. The associated Berry phase is illustrated, and the existence of localized wave functions is pointed out. In one dimension, even the ground state is shown to display the sign reversal with a mechanism to circumvent the Sturm-Liouville theorem.

# Geometric Phase in Quantum Billiards with a Pointlike Scatterer

## Key claim
By varying the location and strength of a pointlike scatterer inside a rectangular billiard, diabolical degeneracies and associated Berry phase (sign reversal of wave functions) occur systematically, including in one dimension where the ground state can exhibit sign reversal despite the Sturm-Liouville theorem, because the diabolical point occurs at infinite scatterer strength where the theorem does not apply.

## Method
The authors solve the bound-state problem for a rectangular billiard with a pointlike scatterer using self-adjoint extension theory to handle the singularity. In one dimension, eigenvalues are obtained from the equation G(ω) − s = 0, where G(ω) is the diagonal free Green's function at the scatterer location. In two dimensions, a regularized Green's function is used to handle logarithmic divergence, and eigenvalues are found from the equation Ḡ(ω) − s̄ = 0. Level crossings are identified by examining energy spectra as functions of scatterer position and strength.

## Key numbers
- In one dimension, unperturbed eigenvalues are ε_n = π/4 · n² for n = 1, 2, ... (mass set to 2π)
- Diabolical points occur at scatterer positions x₀ = M/N (relative primes with N > M > 0) and strength s = 0 (infinite strength)
- In two dimensions, the rectangle has dimensions x ∈ [0, R] and y ∈ [0, 1/R] with R = π/e ≈ 1.1557273
- The formal inverse strength is set to s̄ = 1/10
- For the specific case R = π/e, diabolical points occur at (x₀, y₀) = (0.5R, 0.3177/R) involving the second and third states, and at (0.5R, 0.3875/R) for the tenth and eleventh states
- The unperturbed energy for the (2,1) state at the first diabolical point is ε_φ ≈ 3.4; for the (4,2) state at the second diabolical point it is ε_φ ≈ 13.6

## Context
This work extends the study of geometric (Berry) phase, discovered by Berry (1984) and further developed by Wilczek and Zee (1984), to the simple setting of billiards with pointlike scatterers. It provides a more transparent pedagogical example than earlier demonstrations in triangular billiards by Berry and Wilkinson (1984). The authors show that the diabolical crossings are not artifacts of the pointlike idealization, as similar sign reversal is observed in billiards with small finite-size obstacles (referencing Cheon and Cohen, 1989). The results enable systematic location of diabolical points in such systems and suggest potential applications in mesoscopic devices for precise obstacle placement and phase manipulation.
