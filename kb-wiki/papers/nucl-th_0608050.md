# Three-Particle Correlations from Parton Cascades in Au+Au Collisions

> We present a study of three-particle correlations among a trigger particle and two associated particles in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) with both partonic and hadronic interactions. We found that three-particle correlation densities in different angular directions with respect to the triggered particle (`center', `cone', `deflected', `near' and `near-away') increase with the number of participants. The ratio of `deflected' to `cone' density approaches to 1.0 with the increasing of number of participants, which indicates that partonic Mach-like shock waves can be produced by strong parton cascades in central Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

Three-particle correlation densities in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV from the AMPT model show a ratio of `deflected' to `cone` density approaching 1.0 with increasing number of participants, which the authors interpret as indicating that partonic Mach-like shock waves can be produced by strong parton cascades, but they note that in peripheral collisions a deflected jet mechanism also contributes.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT) with both partonic and hadronic interactions to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They applied an event-mixing technique to extract three-particle correlation signals among a trigger particle (2.5 < pTtrig < 4 GeV/c) and two associated particles (1.0 < pTassoc < 2.5 GeV/c) within |η| < 1.0, subtracting hard-soft, soft-soft, and random combinatorial backgrounds. The correlation densities were integrated over five defined azimuthal angular regions (`center`, `cone`, `deflected`, `near`, and `near-away`) and studied as functions of centrality (number of participants Npart).

## Key numbers

- Trigger particle pT window: 2.5 < pTtrig < 4 GeV/c; associated particle pT window: 1.0 < pTassoc < 2.5 GeV/c.
- Parton interaction cross section in AMPT model assumed to be 10 mb.
- Ratio of `center`/`deflected` and `center`/`cone` density ratios fall from above 2.0 in peripheral collisions to near 1.0 in central collisions with increasing Npart.
- Ratio of `deflected`/`cone` density approaches 1.0 in central collisions.
- Nuclear modification factor Rcp for 0-10%/40-60% centralities: Nbin = 939.4 (0-10%) and 93.7 (40-60%); Rcp from melting AMPT matches experimental data shape when scaled by factor 1.2.
- Rcp from default AMPT is independent of pT and inconsistent with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous di-hadron correlation study using the AMPT model (Ma et al., 2006) which found that hadronic rescattering alone cannot reproduce the observed amplitude of Mach-like structure on the away-side. The three-particle correlation method was proposed to distinguish between different emission scenarios (Mach-cone shock wave, Cherenkov gluon radiation, deflected jets, large angle gluon emission) that could produce the splitting of the away-side peak observed by STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The results suggest that strong parton cascades in the AMPT model produce hydrodynamic-like behavior that generates conic emission patterns, while a linearized hydrodynamical model by Chaudhuri and Heinz (2005) found no Mach-like structures in di-hadron correlations. The findings enable future quantitative comparisons with experimental three-particle correlation data from RHIC and provide constraints on the role of partonic versus hadronic mechanisms in jet-medium interactions.
