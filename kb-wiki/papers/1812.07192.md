# Unravelling High-Energy Hadron Structures with Lattice QCD

> Parton distribution functions are key quantities for us to understand the hadronic structures in high-energy scattering, but they are difficult to calculate from lattice QCD. Recent years have seen fast development of the large-momentum effective theory which allows extraction of the $x$-dependence of parton distribution functions from a quasi-parton distribution function that can be directly calculated on lattice. The extraction is based on a factorization formula for the quasi-parton distribution function that has been derived rigorously in perturbation theory. A systematic procedure that includes renormalization, perturbative matching, and power corrections has been established to calculate parton distribution functions. Latest progress from lattice QCD has shown promising signs that it will become an effective tool for calculating parton physics.

## Key claim

The large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) allows extraction of the Bjorken-x dependence of parton distribution functions (PDFs) from lattice QCD via a factorization formula for quasi-PDFs, but the extraction is valid only in the window where the hadron momentum \(P^z\) is much larger than the QCD scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) and the hadron mass \(M\), and the ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\) is much larger than \(P^z\); the quasi-PDF does not have a well-defined \(P^z \to \infty\) limit when the UV cutoff is taken first.

## Method

The author reviews the LaMET formalism, in which a quasi-PDF is defined from an equal-time correlator of a nonlocal quark bilinear operator with a spacelike Wilson line, calculable on a Euclidean lattice. The renormalization of the quasi-PDF is proven to be multiplicative in coordinate space, with a linear power divergence from the Wilson line self-energy subtracted nonperturbatively (e.g., via the RI/MOM scheme). The renormalized quasi-PDF is then matched to the light-cone PDF in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme via a perturbative matching kernel derived from the operator product expansion of the nonlocal operator, with power corrections of order \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2/(x^2 P_z^2)\) and \(M^2/P_z^2\).

## Key numbers

- The factorization formula for the quasi-PDF \(\tilde{q}(x, P^z, \mu)\) is given by \(\tilde{q}(x, P^z, \mu) = \int_{-1}^1 \frac{dy}{|y|} C(x/y, \mu/(|y|P^z)) q(y, \mu) + O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2/(x^2 P_z^2), M^2/P_z^2)\), where the matching kernel \(C\) is computed at one-loop order in \(\alpha_s\).
- The one-loop Wilson coefficient for the \(n\)-th moment in the OPE is \(C^q_n(\mu^2 z^2) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \left[ \left( \frac{3+2n}{2+3n+n^2} + 2H_n \right) \ln \frac{\mu^2 z^2 e^{2\gamma_E}}{4} + \frac{7+2n}{2+3n+n^2} + 2(1-H_n)H_n - 2H_n^{(2)} \right]\), with \(C_F = 4/3\).
- The renormalization factor in the RI/MOM scheme is defined at a subtraction point \(p^2 = -\mu_R^2\) and \(p_z = p^R_z\), with the condition that the renormalized off-shell quark matrix element equals its tree-level value; the window for the subtraction scales is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \ll \mu_R \ll a^{-1}\) and \(p_z^R \ll a^{-1}\).
- The \(\overline{\rm MS}\) renormalization constant for the quasi-PDF at one loop is \(Z_{\overline{\rm MS}}(\epsilon) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \frac{3}{2} \left[ \frac{1}{\epsilon} - \gamma_E + \ln(4\pi) \right] + O(\alpha_s^2)\).

## Context

This paper reviews the LaMET approach pioneered by Ji (2013, 2014), which enables lattice QCD calculations of the x-dependence of PDFs, a long-standing challenge because light-cone correlators cannot be directly computed on a Euclidean lattice. The work extends earlier efforts that only computed the lowest three Mellin moments of PDFs (Martinelli 1987, Detmold 2001, Dolgov 2002) and contrasts with alternative approaches such as the pseudo-PDF method (Radyushkin 2017) and the direct OPE of the Compton amplitude (Chambers 2017). The systematic procedure described, including nonperturbative renormalization, perturbative matching, and power corrections, enables future lattice calculations of unpolarized, helicity, and transversity PDFs at physical pion mass and large proton momentum, with recent results showing agreement with global fits in the moderate-x region.
