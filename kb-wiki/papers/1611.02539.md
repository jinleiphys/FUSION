# Confinement, Holonomy and Correlated Instanton-Dyon Ensemble I: SU(2) Yang-Mills Theory

> The mechanism of confinement in Yang-Mills theories remains a challenge to our understanding of nonperturbative gauge dynamics. While it is widely perceived that confinement may arise from chromo-magnetically charged gauge configurations with nontrivial topology, it is not clear what types of configurations could do that and how, in pure Yang-Mills and QCD-like (non-supersymmetric) theories. Recently a promising approach has emerged, based on statistical ensembles of dyons/anti-dyons that are constituents of instanton/anti-instanton solutions with nontrivial holonomy where the holonomy plays a vital role as an effective "Higgsing" mechanism. We report a thorough numerical investigation of the confinement dynamics in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory by constructing such a statistical ensemble of correlated instanton-dyons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a statistical ensemble of correlated instanton-dyons (constituents of KvBLL calorons with nontrivial holonomy) can drive confinement in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, but only when short-range repulsive correlations between dyons and anti-dyons are included; without such correlations, the ensemble alone cannot overcome the perturbative one-loop potential that favors trivial holonomy.

## Method

The authors construct a partition function for an ensemble of L-type and M-type dyons and their anti-dyons, using an approximate metric for the moduli space proposed by Diakonov and Petrov (2007) and a parametrized dyon-anti-dyon interaction potential with a repulsive core from gradient flow studies by Larsen et al. (2014, 2015). They perform Monte Carlo simulations using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm in a flat box with periodic boundary conditions, computing the holonomy potential via thermodynamic integration over an auxiliary parameter. The temperature dependence enters through the running coupling in the instanton action S(T) = (22/3) log(T/Λ), and the three key input parameters are the Debye screening mass M_D, the core potential strength V_c, and the core size ζ_j^c.

## Key numbers

- The critical action is determined at S_c = 7.22, which fixes the scale parameter Λ = 0.373 T_c.
- The spatial volume used for most results is V = 43.37 (dimensionless, scaled by T^3).
- The optimal dyon density range per species is n_D ∈ [0, 0.5], corresponding to N_D ∈ [0, 22] dyons.
- The three key parameters are chosen as Debye mass M_D = 2, core potential strength V_c = 20, and core size ζ_j^c = 2.
- The Polyakov loop order parameter ⟨L_∞⟩ follows the 3D Ising model critical exponents β ≈ 0.3265(3) and Δ ≈ 0.530(16) with a fit χ^2 = 1.44×10^{-4}, compared to a mean-field fit (β_mf = 1/2) with χ^2 = 0.13.
- The fitting parameters for the critical power law are b = 0.858(142) and d = -0.017(348).
- For S = 5–7, the free energy minimum lies at ν_min = 0.5 (confined phase); for S ≥ 7.5, the minimum shifts away from ν = 0.5 (deconfined phase).
- At S = 7.25, the minimum is found at ν_min ≈ 0.453.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-dyon approach pioneered by Diakonov and Petrov (2007, 2009) and Gerhold et al. (2006), who showed that uncorrelated dyon ensembles push the holonomy toward confinement but cannot overcome the one-loop perturbative potential. The authors build on the finding by Shuryak and collaborators (2013, 2014, 2015) that short-range repulsive dyon-anti-dyon correlations are necessary for confining holonomy at low temperature, and on effective Coulomb plasma models by Liu et al. (2015). The results enable quantitative comparison with lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory and provide a microscopic foundation for the "dual superconductor" picture of confinement, as well as inputs for chiral models incorporating Polyakov loop dynamics.
