# The sensitivity of R_pA to color recombination effects

> In hadronization models with color recombination, partons are allowed to regroup into color singlet structures that are different from those determined by the perturbative parton shower. This aims at modeling the possibility that soft interactions of partons with the underlying event can change color connections. If such an effect is at play in proton-proton collisions, it may be expected to be enhanced in proton-nucleus collisions due to the higher color charge density in the underlying event. Here, we provide a qualitative argument that color recombination effects could lead to a multiplicity dependent hardening of single inclusive hadron spectra that dies out very weakly with increasing transverse momentum. We present results of a (conservative) model implementation in the cluster hadronization model of the SHERPA event generator. In this model, we find that color recombination effects harden indeed the single inclusive hadron spectra without affecting the jet spectra, but that this effect does not depend significantly on underlying event activity. We explain this model feature and we argue why, in general, data on proton-nucleus collisions can help to constrain hadronization models used in proton-proton event generators.

## Key claim

Color recombination effects could lead to a multiplicity-dependent hardening of single inclusive hadron spectra that dies out very weakly (proportional to 1/p_perp) with increasing transverse momentum, but the specific conservative model implementation in SHERPA does not show a significant dependence on underlying event activity, meaning it cannot account quantitatively for the observed discrepancy between the hadron and jet nuclear modification factors in proton-lead collisions.

## Method

The authors implemented a color recombination model within the cluster hadronization framework of the SHERPA event generator, where pairs of color singlet clusters are allowed to swap color connections with a probability that favors decreasing the clusters' invariant mass. To mimic proton-nucleus collisions, they increased the underlying event activity in proton-proton collisions by approximately a factor of 3 while keeping all other settings (including the parton distribution function set) unchanged, then compared charged hadron and jet transverse momentum spectra with and without color recombination enabled.

## Key numbers

- In proton-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the CMS and ATLAS collaborations reported a charged hadron nuclear modification factor R_pPb that exceeds unity by 20-50% at transverse momenta between 20 GeV and 100 GeV
- The nuclear modification factor R_pPb for jets is compatible with unity for transverse energies above 50 GeV
- The color recombination probability parameter P_cr was set to 0.25 in the SHERPA implementation
- The parameter t_0 in the recombination probability formula is of order 1 GeV^2
- The ratio of typical minimum bias multiplicities between pPb and pp collisions is approximately a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends discussions of color reconnection effects that began with Sjostrand (1987) regarding the need to minimize color string length to describe the increase of average transverse momentum with multiplicity in minimum bias events. The study was motivated by the discrepancy between hadron and jet nuclear modification factors reported by CMS and ATLAS collaborations in proton-lead collisions, which could not be explained by nuclear modification of parton distribution functions as studied by Helenius (2015). The results show that the SHERPA cluster hadronization model's conservative color recombination implementation (allowing reconnections only within the same original color singlet system) does not produce the expected enhancement with underlying event activity, leaving open the possibility that less conservative models might show different behavior.
