# Coupling and Dissociation in Artificial Molecules

> We show that the spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock method, in conjunction with the companion step of the restoration of spin and space symmetries via Projection Techniques (when such symmetries are broken), is able to describe the full range of couplings in two-dimensional double quantum dots, from the strong-coupling regime exhibiting delocalized molecular orbitals to the weak-coupling and dissociation regimes associated with a Generalized Valence Bond combination of atomic-type orbitals localized on the individual dots. The weak-coupling regime is always accompanied by an antiferromagnetic ordering of the spins of the individual dots. The cases of dihydrogen (H$_2$, $2e$) and dilithium (Li$_2$, $6e$) quantum dot molecules are discussed in detail.

# Coupling and Dissociation in Artificial Molecules

## Key claim
The spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock (sS-UHF) method, combined with symmetry restoration via Projection Techniques, can describe the full range of couplings in two-dimensional double quantum dots from strong-coupling (delocalized molecular orbitals) to weak-coupling and dissociation (Generalized Valence Bond localized orbitals), but only when space symmetry is broken; the weak-coupling regime is always accompanied by antiferromagnetic spin ordering, contrary to the ferromagnetic ordering predicted by molecular-orbital-based methods such as local-spin-density (LSD) calculations.

## Method
The authors solved the spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock (sS-UHF) equations using the Pople-Nesbet-Roothaan formalism with an interdot-distance-adjustable basis formed from eigenfunctions of a two-center-oscillator (TCO) confining potential. For the H₂-QDM case, they applied Löwdin's spin projection operator to the sS-UHF single Slater determinant to restore broken spin symmetry, yielding a Generalized Valence Bond (GVB) wave function. They compared results from restricted Hartree-Fock (RHF), spin-unrestricted Hartree-Fock (s-UHF), sS-UHF, and GVB approaches for both Li₂-QDM (6 electrons) and H₂-QDM (2 electrons) systems.

## Key numbers
- For Li₂-QDM at d=70 nm, Vb=10 meV, κ=20: sS-UHF total energy for P=0 unpolarized state = 74.136 meV, which is 1.379 meV lower than the RHF P=0 energy of 75.515 meV; the sS-UHF unpolarized state is only 0.001 meV lower than the s-UHF P=2 polarized state at 74.137 meV.
- For H₂-QDM at d=30 nm, Vb=4.95 meV, κ=20: RHF singlet energy = 13.68 meV, sS-UHF singlet energy = 12.83 meV (gain of 0.85 meV), triplet energy = 13.01 meV.
- For H₂-QDM with κ=40 (weaker e-e repulsion), sS-UHF and GVB solutions collapse to the MO solution for interdot barrier Vb ≤ 2.8 meV.
- The extent of the 1s atomic orbital is l₀ = 28.50 nm for the chosen parameters (ħω₀ = 5 meV, m* = 0.067 mₑ, corresponding to GaAs).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior development of the sS-UHF method for single quantum dots (Yannouleas and Landman, 2000) and quantum dot molecules (Yannouleas and Landman, 1999) to explicitly demonstrate the failure of molecular-orbital-based approaches for weakly-coupled quantum dot molecules. It contradicts the spontaneous ferromagnetic ordering predicted for double quantum dots by local-spin-density (LSD) calculations of Nagaraja (1997) and Wensauer (2000), showing that such predictions are artifacts of the molecular-orbital structure implicit in those density-functional calculations. The work enables systematic treatment of quantum dot molecules across all coupling regimes using GVB wave functions, analogous to the Heitler-London approach in natural molecules.
