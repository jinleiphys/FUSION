# Effective Field Theory calculations of $NN\to NNπ$

> In this review we present the recent advances for calculations of the reactions $NN\to NNπ$ using chiral effective field theory. Discussed are the next-to-next-to leading order loop contributions with nucleon and Delta-isobar for near threshold s-wave pion production. Results of recent experimental pion-production data for energies close to the threshold are analyzed. Several particular applications are discussed: (i) it is shown how the measured charge symmetry violating pion-production reaction can be used to extract the strong-interaction contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference; (ii) the role of $NN\to NNπ$ for the extraction of the pion-nucleon scattering lengths from pionic atoms data is illuminated.

## Key claim
The paper reviews how chiral effective field theory with a modified momentum counting scheme (MCS), where the expansion parameter is \(\chi_{\rm MCS}= p/\Lambda_\chi \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi/m_N} \simeq 0.4\), can systematically describe near-threshold \(NN\to NN\pi\) reactions, resolving long-standing discrepancies between earlier phenomenological models and experimental data. The authors argue that the neutral channel \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) is unique in directly probing higher-order MCS contributions, as the dominant lower-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term does not contribute there.

## Method
The authors employ heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with a momentum counting scheme (MCS) that treats the initial nucleon three-momentum at threshold (\(p \simeq 360\) MeV) as the expansion scale, rather than the pion mass. They calculate the irreducible production operator \(\mathcal{A}_{\rm prod}\) for s-wave and p-wave pion production up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO), including explicit \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobar degrees of freedom, and then convolute this operator with non-perturbative \(NN\) wave functions obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation with a chiral \(NN\) potential.

## Key numbers
* The expansion parameter in the momentum counting scheme is \(\chi_{\rm MCS} \simeq \sqrt{m_\pi/m_N} \simeq 0.4\), where the initial nucleon three-momentum at threshold is \(p \simeq 360\) MeV.
* The initial nucleon three-momentum at threshold is \(p = |\vec p| \simeq \sqrt{m_N m_\pi} \sim 360\) MeV, significantly larger than the pion mass.
* The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.
* The typical hadronic scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 1\) GeV.

## Context
This review extends the earlier work of Hanhart (2004) and the tree-level calculations of Cohen (1996), Park (1996), and Sato (1997) by including N\(^2\)LO loop contributions with nucleons and \(\Delta\)-isobars. It resolves the factor-of-2 and factor-of-10 discrepancies between the phenomenological model of Koltun (1973) and experimental data for \(pp\to d\pi^+\) and \(pp\to pp\pi^0\), respectively, and provides a framework for extracting the strong-interaction contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference from charge symmetry breaking data (Filin, 2013) and for determining the low-energy constant \(d\) that connects pion production to weak processes like \(pp\) fusion and tritium beta decay (Park, 2003).
