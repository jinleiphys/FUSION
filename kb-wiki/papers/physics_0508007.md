# Nucleon deformation and atomic spectroscopy

> Recent ineleastic electron-proton scattering experiments have led to rather accurate values for the N->Delta transition quadrupole moment Q(N->Delta).The experimental results imply a prolate (cigar-shaped) intrinsic deformation of the nucleon. The nonsphericity of the proton's charge distribution might be seen in the spectrum of atomic hydrogen. The possibilities and limitations for determining the geometric shape of the nucleon in an atomic physics experiment are discussed.

# Nucleon deformation and atomic spectroscopy

## Key claim

The paper argues that the intrinsic quadrupole deformation of the nucleon, implied by recent measurements of the N→Δ transition quadrupole moment, might be detectable in atomic hydrogen spectroscopy, but only through isotope shift measurements rather than direct hyperfine splitting because the proton has spin 1/2 and therefore no spectroscopic quadrupole moment.

## Method

The author derives relations between the neutron charge radius, the N→Δ transition quadrupole moment, and the intrinsic quadrupole moment of the proton using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and a quark model with one-gluon exchange. The intrinsic quadrupole charge density is then Fourier-transformed to coordinate space using a Galster parametrization of the neutron charge form factor, and the deformation contribution to the hydrogen Lamb shift is estimated by inserting the neutron charge radius into the standard isotope shift formula for s-electrons.

## Key numbers

- The experimental N→Δ transition quadrupole moment is Q(p→Δ+)(exp) = -0.0846(33) fm² (Tia03) and Q(p→Δ+)(exp) = -0.108(9) fm² (Bla01)
- The neutron charge radius is r²_n = -0.113(3) fm² (Kop95)
- The quark model prediction Q(p→Δ+) = -0.08 fm² agrees with experiment
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment of the proton is Q_0^p = -r²_n = 0.113 fm², implying a prolate deformation
- The nucleon deformation parameter is δ_N = 0.11, corresponding to a major-to-minor axis ratio a/b = 1.11
- The Δ deformation parameter is δ_Δ = -0.09, with a/b = 0.91
- The estimated deformation contribution to the hydrogen 1s energy shift is δE_IS(def) = 157 kHz

## Context

This work extends the author's previous quark model calculations (Buc97, Hen01) relating the neutron charge radius to nucleon deformation, and connects to the historical discovery of nuclear quadrupole moments in atomic hyperfine spectra by Schüler and Schmidt (1934) and Casimir (1936). The paper suggests that increasingly accurate hydrogen isotope shift measurements (Hub97) might reveal discrepancies arising from nucleon deformation, and notes that the proton charge radius from atomic Lamb shift measurements may differ from that obtained in elastic electron-proton scattering due to different time scales of the measurements.
