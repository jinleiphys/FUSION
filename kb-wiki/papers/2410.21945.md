# Rapid cooling of the Cassiopeia A neutron star due to superfluid quantum criticality

> The rapid cooling of the neutron star in Cassiopeia A is speculated to arise from an enhanced neutrino emission caused by the onset of $^3P_2$-wave neutron superfluidity in the core. However, the neutrino emissivity due to Cooper-pair breaking and formation is in tension with the requirements for explaining the observed cooling rate. Here, we show that such a rapid cooling can be explained once the non-Fermi liquid behavior of the non-superfluid neutron liquid induced by superfluid quantum criticality is included into the theoretical description of neutron star cooling, without assuming the existence of additional energy loss processes. Our results indicate that the neutron star in Cassiopeia A remains in the thermal relaxation stage, which is greatly prolonged by the non-Fermi liquid behavior. The good agreement between our theoretical results and recent observational cooling data points to the pivotal role played by superfluid quantum criticality in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The rapid cooling of the Cassiopeia A neutron star can be explained by including non-Fermi liquid behavior induced by superfluid quantum criticality, without requiring additional energy loss processes beyond standard neutrino emission, provided that the neutron star is still in a thermal relaxation stage that is greatly prolonged by this non-Fermi liquid behavior.

## Method

The authors modified the publicly available NSCool code package to incorporate non-Fermi liquid corrections to the neutron specific heat and total neutrino emissivity, which take the form of T ln(T0/T) terms arising from superfluid quantum critical fluctuations. They solved the full general-relativistic energy balance and energy transport equations using the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall equation of state for a 1.6 solar mass neutron star with a carbon envelope, treating the coupling constant h as a single adjustable parameter while fixing the q-factor for Cooper-pair breaking and formation neutrino emissivity at theoretical values.

## Key numbers

- The observed surface temperature of the Cas A neutron star decreased by 4% (from 2.12 x 10^6 K to 2.04 x 10^6 K) during 2000-2009, later adjusted to a 2% cooling rate over the subsequent decade
- The standard cooling paradigm predicts a cooling rate of approximately 0.7%, much smaller than observed
- The best fit to observations was obtained with proton superconducting critical temperature T_Cp = 1.65 x 10^9 K and neutron superfluid critical temperature T_Cn = 1.00 x 10^9 K
- The upper critical density for 3P2-wave neutron superfluidity is approximately 4.63 times the nuclear saturation density rho_0
- The thermal relaxation time is prolonged to roughly 400 years due to non-Fermi liquid enhancement of the heat capacity
- The internal magnetic field of the Cas A neutron star is estimated to be (0.93-6.23) x 10^14 G

## Context

This work extends the minimal cooling paradigm of Page (2011) and Shternin (2011), which attributed the rapid cooling to enhanced neutrino emission from Cooper-pair breaking and formation processes but faced tension because the required suppression factor q (0.40-2.60) exceeded the theoretical upper bound of 0.79 from Leinson (2010). The authors show that including non-Fermi liquid corrections from superfluid quantum criticality, as developed in their earlier work Zhu (2025), resolves this tension and allows q values as low as 0.19. This enables future constraints on the coexistence of 1S0-wave and 3P2-wave neutron superfluids in neutron star cores, supporting the weak-competition scenario of Yasui (2020) over the first-order interface picture.
