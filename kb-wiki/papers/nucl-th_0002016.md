# Ground states of the Wick-Cutkosky model using light-front dynamics

> We consider the ground state in a model with scalar nucleons and a meson using the formalism of light-front dynamics. Light-front potentials for two-nucleon bound states are calculated using two approaches. First, light-front time-ordered perturbation theory is used to calculate one- and two-meson-exchange potentials. These potentials give results that agree well with the ladder and ladder plus crossed box Bethe-Salpeter spectra. Secondly, approximations that incorporate non-perturbative physics are used to calculate alternative one-meson-exchange potentials. These non-perturbative potentials give better agreement with the spectra of the full non-perturbative ground-state calculation than the perturbative potentials. For lightly-bound states, all of the approaches appear to agree with each other.

## Key claim

Light-front dynamics can reproduce the bound-state spectra of the Wick-Cutkosky model, but only when non-perturbative approximations to the one-meson-exchange potential are used do the results approach the full non-perturbative solution; for lightly-bound states all approaches agree, but for deeper binding the perturbative potentials systematically underestimate the binding energy compared to the full Feynman-Schwinger representation result.

## Method

The authors constructed light-front potentials for two-nucleon bound states in the massive Wick-Cutkosky model using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory (LFTOPT) to compute one-meson-exchange (OBE) and two-meson-exchange (TBE) potentials, including both ladder and crossed-box diagrams. They also developed four non-perturbative approximations to the OBE potential (symmetrized mass, instantaneous, retarded, and modified-Green's-function potentials) and solved the resulting light-front Schrodinger equation numerically to obtain the coupling constant versus bound-state mass curves. The results were compared to spectra from the ladder and ladder-plus-crossed-box Bethe-Salpeter equations and to the full Feynman-Schwinger representation (FSR) solution.

## Key numbers

* The bound-state mass E is related to the binding energy B by E = 2M - B, where M is the nucleon mass.
* The OBE potential for scattering states reduces to M^2/E_scat divided by ((k1-p1)^2 - mu^2), matching the usual equal-time OBE potential.
* The light-front Schrodinger equation in the center-of-momentum frame yields the eigenvalue condition (E^2 - (2k^0)^2) times the wavefunction equals the integrated potential term, where k^0 = sqrt(M^2 + k_ET^2).
* The paper reports that for lightly-bound states, all approaches (perturbative and non-perturbative) agree with each other, but no specific numerical binding energies or coupling constants are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the light-front dynamics approach to bound states in the Wick-Cutkosky model, building on prior studies by Sales et al. (1999) who computed bound states using OBE and TBE:SB approximations and compared to ladder Bethe-Salpeter results. The authors also consider crossed-graph contributions to the TBE potential and several non-perturbative approximations, comparing to the full FSR solution of Simonov (1993) and Nieuwenhuis (1996). The study enables future work on relativistic deuteron form factors at high momentum transfers, where light-front dynamics provides a natural framework for connecting to experimental observables such as the A(Q^2) structure function measured at Jefferson Lab.
