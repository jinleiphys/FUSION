# Non-contact interactions and the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2

> We summarise recent results for the quark loop part of the light-by-light scattering contribution to the muons anomalous magnetic moment. In particular we focus on the impact of a momentum dependent quark and quark-photon vertex. We compare the Dyson-Schwinger description with that of the extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (ENJL) and find important quantitative differences. In particular the transverse parts of the quark-photon-vertex, which serve as a dynamical extension of simple vector meson dominance models, do not yield the large suppression as found in the ENJL model.

# Non-contact interactions and the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2

## Key claim
The standard value for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment may be too small in its central value and too optimistic in its error estimate, because the large suppression from vector meson dominance found in the Extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model is an artefact of its contact interaction, which artificially restricts the relative quark momentum dependence.

## Method
The authors computed the quark-loop part of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon g-2 using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in the rainbow-ladder truncation, which satisfies the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. They compared these results with those from the ENJL model, which is a non-renormalisable effective theory with a contact interaction and a cutoff near 1 GeV. The comparison focused on the momentum dependence of the quark propagator (mass function and wave-function renormalisation) and the quark-photon vertex, including its transverse components.

## Key numbers
- The combined theory result for the muon anomalous magnetic moment stands at 116,591,827.0 (64) x 10^{-11}, compared to the experimental result of 116,592,089 (63) x 10^{-11}, giving a discrepancy at 3 sigma (possibly as high as 4.8 sigma).
- In the DSE approach, the average quark mass probed is of order 200 MeV, lighter than the constituent quark mass of approximately 300 MeV used in the ENJL model.
- Restricting the relative quark momentum to zero in the leading transverse part of the quark-photon vertex reduces the quark-loop contribution from approximately 100 x 10^{-11} down to approximately 40 x 10^{-11}, for both the ENJL model and the DSE approach.
- The DSE calculated quark-loop contribution with full relative momentum dependence is 96 x 10^{-11}; the DSE fit gives 105 x 10^{-11}; the ENJL model with a relative momentum factor f(k^2) gives 103 x 10^{-11}.
- The standard value used in current evaluations is a_mu^{LBL} = 105 (26) x 10^{-11}.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering developed by Fischer, Goecke, and Williams (2010-2013) and contrasts it with the ENJL model calculations of Bijnens (1995). The results challenge the standard evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light contribution used by Hagiwara (2011) and Benayoun (2012), suggesting that the central value may be underestimated and the error too small. The findings enable a re-evaluation of the muon g-2 discrepancy, which could otherwise be misinterpreted as a signal of beyond-standard-model physics.
