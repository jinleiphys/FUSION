# Binding in light nuclei: Statistical NN uncertainties vs Computational accuracy

> We analyse the impact of the statistical uncertainties of the the nucleon-nucleon interaction, based on the Granada-2013 np-pp database, on the binding energies of the triton and the alpha particle using a bootstrap method, by solving the Faddeev equations for $^3$H and the Yakubovsky equations for $^4$He respectively. We check that in practice about 30 samples prove enough for a reliable error estimate. An extrapolation of the well fulfilled Tjon-line correlation predicts the experimental binding of the alpha particle within uncertainties.

# Binding in light nuclei: Statistical NN uncertainties vs Computational accuracy

## Key claim
The statistical uncertainty from NN scattering data on the binding energies of triton and alpha particle is comparable to the current numerical accuracy of few-body calculations, meaning that further improvements in numerical precision are not warranted unless the statistical uncertainty is also reduced. The Tjon-line correlation extrapolation predicts the experimental alpha binding energy within uncertainties, but only when using a moderate sample of 30 Monte Carlo generated potentials.

## Method
The authors propagated statistical uncertainties from the Granada-2013 np-pp database (6713 data points, chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.04) using a bootstrap method with a gaussian potential that is statistically equivalent to the original delta-shells potential. They solved the Faddeev equations for triton and Yakubovsky equations for alpha particle, evaluating binding energies for multiple Monte Carlo generated parameter sets to obtain distributions and uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy from most likely parameters: E_t(p_0) = 7.666 ± 0.001 MeV (numerical error)
- Triton binding energy from Monte Carlo sample: E_t^th = -7.6669 ± 0.0124 MeV
- Experimental triton binding energy: E_t^exp = -8.4820 ± 0.0001 MeV
- Tjon-line slope: a = 4.7(1), intercept b = 11.4
- Statistical uncertainty extrapolated for alpha particle: ΔE_alpha^stat = 4.7(1) × ΔE_t^stat = 50(5) keV
- Numerical accuracy for triton: ΔE_t^num ~ 1 keV
- Numerical accuracy for alpha particle: ΔE_alpha^num = 20 keV
- Sample size used for Tjon analysis: M = 30
- Number of principal components needed for most of the uncertainty in E_t: approximately 10

## Context
This work extends the pioneering error propagation study of Adam (1993) using the Paris potential (chi^2/d.o.f. ~ 2) to the much more precise Granada-2013 database (chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.04). It confirms earlier findings using hyperspherical harmonics for A=3 (Perez 2014) and no-core full configuration shell model calculations (Perez 2015) that numerical accuracy is close to optimal given the statistical uncertainty. The results enable future inclusion of three-nucleon forces, which are needed to resolve the discrepancy between theoretical and experimental triton binding energies, and suggest that 3NF parameter variations will absorb most of the NN statistical uncertainty.
