# Electronic stopping in astrophysical fusion reactions

> The stopping power of protons and deuterons in low energy collisions with helium gas targets is investigated with the numerical solution of the time-dependent Schrödinger coupled-channels equations using molecular orbital wavefunctions. It is shown that at low projectile energies the energy loss is mainly due to nuclear stopping, charge exchange, and the excitation of low energy levels in the target. The second and third mechanisms, called electronic stopping, dominate for E>200 eV. At lower energies it is also shown that a threshold effect is responsible for a quick drop of the energy loss. This investigation sheds more light on the long standing electron screening problem in fusion reactions of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim
Electronic stopping (charge exchange and target excitation) dominates over nuclear stopping for projectile energies above 200 eV, but below that energy a threshold effect causes a rapid drop in energy loss that is not captured by standard extrapolations such as the Andersen-Ziegler tables.

## Method
The author solved the time-dependent Schrödinger coupled-channels equations using molecular orbital wavefunctions constructed from Slater-type orbitals, with a Hartree-Fock variational method to obtain the lowest energy states of the p+He and d+He systems. The nuclei were assumed to follow classical straight-line trajectories, and the dynamical evolution was calculated by solving the coupled equations for the expansion coefficients of the two-center states.

## Key numbers
- For p+He collisions, the threshold energy for electronic stopping is approximately 9 keV, derived from the 1s²(¹S₀) → 1s2s(³S) transition energy of 18.7 eV in helium.
- For d+³He collisions, the threshold deuteron energy is of the order of 18 keV.
- The nuclear stopping cross section for p+⁴He at E_p < 30 keV is given by S_n = S_0 * ln(1+1.1383ε) / (ε + 0.01321ε^0.21226 + 0.19593ε^0.5), with S_0 = 0.779 and ε = 5.99 E_p (E_p in keV), in units of 10⁻⁵ eV cm².
- For d+³He, the nuclear stopping coefficients are S_0 = 1.557 and ε = 4.491 E_d.
- The charge-exchange cross section for p+He shows a maximum at approximately 20 keV proton energy.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Bertulani and de Paula (2000), who studied energy loss of protons on hydrogen gas targets, to the more complicated case of helium targets. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between experimental fusion cross sections and theoretical predictions for the electron screening effect, as reported by Rolfs (2001) and others. The results suggest that the smaller-than-expected electronic stopping power at very low energies, including the threshold effect first reported by Formicola (2000), may explain why laboratory screening appears larger than theory predicts, and calls for improved theoretical and experimental studies of energy loss at extremely low energies.
