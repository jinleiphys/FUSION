# Toroidal resonance: relation to pygmy mode, vortical properties and anomalous deformation splitting

> We review a recent progress in investigation of the isoscalar toroidal dipole resonance (TDR). A possible relation of the TDR and low-energy dipole strength (also called a pygmy resonance) is analyzed. It is shown that the dipole strength in the pygmy region can by understood as a local manifestation of the collective vortical toroidal motion at the nuclear surface. Application of the TDR as a measure of the nuclear dipole vorticity is discussed. Finally, an anomalous splitting of the TDR in deformed nuclei is scrutinized.

# Toroidal resonance: relation to pygmy mode, vortical properties and anomalous deformation splitting

## Key claim

The low-energy dipole strength (pygmy dipole resonance, PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei can be understood as a local manifestation of the collective vortical toroidal dipole resonance (TDR) at the nuclear surface, despite the PDR and TDR representing fundamentally different irrotational and vortical kinds of nuclear motion. The authors propose that the toroidal strength, rather than the Ravenhall-Wambach prescription using the $j_+$ current component, provides a natural and robust measure of nuclear dipole vorticity.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase-Approximation (QRPA) calculations using the SLy6 Skyrme force, with exact residual interaction for spherical nuclei and separable residual interaction for axially deformed nuclei. They computed toroidal and compression E1(T=0) strength functions, transition densities, and current fields for the spherical nucleus $^{132}$Sn and the deformed nucleus $^{238}$U, analyzing the summed current fields over the 6-10 MeV energy interval covering the PDR and low-energy TDR peaks.

## Key numbers

- $^{132}$Sn: The PDR and low-energy TDR/CDR peaks share the same energy region (6-10 MeV), where the summed current fields show a clear isoscalar toroidal character
- $^{238}$U: The deformation parameter $\beta = 0.286$ was obtained by minimization of total energy, reproducing the experimental value $\beta_{\text{exp}} = 0.2863(24)$
- $^{238}$U: In the SRPA calculation, the main K=1 TDR peak lies significantly lower (5-10 MeV) than the main K=0 peak (10-17 MeV), showing anomalous deformation splitting opposite to the GDR order
- $^{238}$U: The two-quasiparticle (2qp) toroidal strength also exhibits the same anomalous K=1 < K=0 ordering, indicating the effect is not caused by residual interaction
- The calculations used a configuration space with particle-hole (two-quasiparticle) energies up to 70 MeV, with a constant averaging parameter $\Delta = 1$ MeV for deformed nuclei

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Nesterenko et al., 2011; Kvasil et al., 2011, 2013) on the isoscalar toroidal dipole resonance and its relation to the compression dipole resonance. It contradicts the Ravenhall-Wambach prescription (Ravenhall and Wambach, 1987) for measuring nuclear vorticity, showing that the $j_+$ current component fails for TDR and CDR cases. The analysis builds on earlier RPA calculations (Vretenar et al., 2001; De Gregorio et al., 2014) and Quasiparticle Phonon Model calculations (Ryczayeva et al., 2002) that observed toroidal flow in the PDR region but did not establish the relation between PDR and TDR. The work enables experimental discrimination of the TDR from other dipole modes via its anomalous deformation splitting, and provides a framework for understanding the PDR as a surface manifestation of toroidal motion, with implications for nuclear equation of state studies and astrophysical applications.
