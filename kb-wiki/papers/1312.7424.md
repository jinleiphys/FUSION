# Effective model approach to meson screening masses at finite temperature

> Temperature dependence of pion and sigma-meson screening masses is evaluated by the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the entanglement vertex. We propose a practical way of calculating meson screening masses in the NJL-type effective models. The method based on the Pauli-Villars regularization solves the well-known difficulty that the evaluaton of screening masses is not easy in the NJL-type effective models.The PNJL model with the entanglement vertex and the Pauli-Villars regularization well reproduces lattice QCD results on temperature dependence of the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop. The method is applied to analyze temperature dependence of pion screening mass calculated with state-of-the-art lattice simulations with success in reproducing the lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a practical method for calculating meson screening masses in NJL-type effective models, claiming that the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the entanglement vertex and Pauli-Villars regularization reproduces lattice QCD results for pion screening masses, but only after rescaling the lattice data by a factor of 154/196 to account for finite lattice-spacing effects in the reference simulations.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor entanglement-PNJL (EPNJL) model with Pauli-Villars regularization to compute meson screening masses from the pole of the mesonic correlation function in the complex momentum plane. They derive a new expression for the one-loop polarization function where vacuum and temperature cuts partially cancel, isolating the screening mass pole from the branch cuts and allowing determination of screening masses without performing the Fourier transform to coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant at vacuum: f_pi = 93.3 MeV
- Pion mass at vacuum: M_pi = 138 MeV (with original parameters m_0 = 6.3 MeV, G_s = 5.0 GeV^-2, Lambda = 0.768 GeV)
- For comparison with 2+1 flavor lattice QCD data, parameters refitted to m_0 = 10.3 MeV and T_0 = 156 MeV, yielding M_pi = 175 MeV at vacuum
- Chiral transition temperature: T_c = 154 MeV (rescaled from the lattice value of 196 MeV using factor 154/196)
- Entanglement parameters used for best fit to lattice data: alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.31
- Pure gauge Polyakov-loop potential parameter T_0 = 200 MeV with alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.20 reproduces two-flavor lattice QCD results

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to compute meson screening masses in NJL-type models by Florkowski (1997) and Kunihiro (1991), which suffered from unphysical oscillations due to three-dimensional momentum cutoff regularization and heavy numerical calculations from slowly-damping oscillatory integrals. The method enables quantitative comparison with state-of-the-art 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations by Cheng (2010), and the EPNJL model with entanglement vertex solves the problem that the standard PNJL model (Ratti, 2006) fails to reproduce the coincidence of chiral and deconfinement transitions seen in two-flavor lattice QCD.
