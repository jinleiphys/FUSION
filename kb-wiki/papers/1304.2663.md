# Spin instabilities of infinite nuclear matter and effective tensor interactions

> We study the effects of the tensor force, present in modern effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, in the spin instability of nuclear and neutron matter. Stability conditions of the system against certain very low energy excitation modes are expressed in terms of Landau parameters. It is shown that in the spin case, the stability conditions are equivalent to the condition derived from the spin susceptibility, which is obtained as the zero-frequency and long-wavelength limit of the spin response function calculated in the Random Phase Approximation. Zero-range forces of the Skyrme type and finite-range forces of M3Y and Gogny type are analyzed. It is shown that for the Skyrme forces considered, the tensor effects are sizeable, and tend to increase the spin instability which appears at smaller densities than in the case that the tensor is not taken into account. On the contrary, the tensor contribution of finite range forces to the spin susceptibility is small or negligible for both isospin channels of symmetric nuclear matter as well as for neutron matter. A comparison with the spin susceptibility provided by realistic interactions is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including tensor terms in Skyrme-type effective interactions systematically lowers the critical density for spin instabilities (ferromagnetic transitions) in nuclear and neutron matter, making the instability appear at smaller densities than when tensor forces are omitted. However, for finite-range interactions of the M3Y and Gogny type, the tensor contribution to the spin susceptibility is small or negligible, and does not drive such instabilities.

## Method

The authors calculate the static spin response (spin susceptibility) of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in the zero-frequency and long-wavelength limit using the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), fully including exchange terms. The particle-hole interaction is expanded in Legendre polynomials to obtain Landau parameters (central \(G_\ell\) and tensor \(H_\ell\)), and the spin susceptibility is derived from a closed algebraic system of coupled equations involving these parameters. The stability conditions against spin excitations are expressed as inequalities on the Landau parameters, and the critical density for ferromagnetic instability is identified as the density where the inverse spin susceptibility vanishes.

## Key numbers

- For Skyrme force SIII with added tensor terms, the inverse spin susceptibility for pure neutron matter crosses zero at a density lower than the case without tensor; the tensor effect is "sizeable" and the instability appears at "smaller densities" (no exact critical density given in the truncated text).
- For the T44 Skyrme parameterization, pure neutron matter shows a ferromagnetic transition around \(\rho \sim 0.3\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the D1M Gogny force supplemented with a tensor term, the \(\ell=1, J=0^-\) stability condition in symmetric nuclear matter signals an instability with a critical density close to \(0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the D1M Gogny force in the isovector channel of symmetric nuclear matter, the inverse spin susceptibility signals an instability at a density close to \(\rho = 0.45\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For neutron matter with finite-range forces (M3Y-P2, D1S, D1M), the minimum value of the stability conditions is about 0.75, "far from zero", and no instability is observed in the density range considered.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tensor force effects in effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, following studies by the Lyon group (Meyer et al., 2009, 2012) on RPA response functions and sum rules, and by Colo et al. (2010) on stability conditions for Skyrme forces. The paper contrasts the behavior of zero-range Skyrme forces (which show enhanced spin instability with tensor terms) with finite-range M3Y and Gogny forces (where tensor effects are negligible). The results enable a better understanding of whether spontaneous ferromagnetic transitions in neutron matter are a physical prediction or an artifact of the Skyrme interaction, and provide a benchmark for future energy density functionals.
