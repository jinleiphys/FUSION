# Comments on ISR method in modern experiment and influence of final state radiation

> We study the effect of final state radiation in the process $e^++e^- \to \bar p+ p$, in the kinematical conditions of BaBar and BESIII experiment. We show that this effect could be large, in particular in the low $x$ region ($x$ is the photon energy fraction) and should be taken into account.

## Key claim

Final state radiation (FSR) in the process \(e^+ + e^- \to \bar p + p + \gamma\) can be large enough to require correction, particularly at low photon energy fraction \(x\), but for the BaBar experiment the effect is negligible (below 1%) in the high-\(x\) region where most statistics lie, while for BESIII it reaches about 4% at \(x = 0.2\).

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross sections for initial state radiation (ISR) and final state radiation (FSR) in the reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to \bar p + p + \gamma\) using Born approximation, including proton electromagnetic form factors. They integrated over the final proton and antiproton angles in the full \(4\pi\) region and over the photon emission angle within the experimental angular ranges of BaBar (20 to 160 degrees) and BESIII (minimal detection angle 5 degrees). The ratio \(R = \sigma^{ISR} / (\sigma^{ISR} + \sigma^{FSR})\) was evaluated for both point-like protons and protons with form factors parametrized following the Iachello-Jackson-Lande model extended to the time-like region.

## Key numbers

- For BaBar conditions (\(\sqrt{s} = 111.6\) GeV\(^2\), \(E_\gamma > 3\) GeV, \(x > 0.8\), angular range 20–160 degrees), the FSR correction is less than 1% (from Fig. 1, solid line).
- For BESIII conditions (\(\sqrt{s} = 3\) GeV, minimal detection angle 5 degrees), the FSR correction is about 4% at \(x = 0.2\) (from Fig. 4, solid line).
- The BaBar detection requires photon energy \(E_\gamma > 3\) GeV, corresponding to \(x > 0.8\).
- The center-of-mass energy squared for BaBar is \(s = 111.6\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the ISR-based extraction of time-like proton form factors used by the BaBar collaboration (BaBar, based on the work of Benayoun and Moussallam) by quantifying the previously neglected FSR contribution. The authors show that the factorization of the cross section into a photon-dependent function and the annihilation cross section holds for ISR but not for FSR, and that for BESIII the FSR correction is sizable enough to require inclusion. The proton form factor parametrization follows Tomasi-Gustafsson (2005), which extends the Iachello-Jackson-Lande model (Iachello, 2003; Wan, 2004) to the time-like region.
