# Dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma using leading-order (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics

> Dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is computed using the leading-order (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. It is shown that high-energy dilepton spectrum is sensitive to the initial local-rest-frame momentum-space anisotropy of the QGP. Our findings suggest that it may be possible to constrain the early-time momentum-space anisotropy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using high-energy dilepton yields.

## Key claim

High-energy dilepton spectra from the quark-gluon plasma are sensitive to the initial local-rest-frame momentum-space anisotropy, suggesting that these observables may be used to experimentally constrain the early-time degree of anisotropy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided the effect is larger for shear viscosity to entropy density ratios above the AdS/CFT lower bound.

## Method

The authors compute dilepton production at leading order in the electromagnetic coupling from a quark-gluon plasma whose space-time evolution is described by (3+1)-dimensional leading-order spheroidal anisotropic hydrodynamics. The dilepton emission rate is obtained from relativistic kinetic theory using the Romatschke-Strickland ansatz for the quark and antiquark distribution functions, characterized by an anisotropy parameter ξ and a transverse momentum scale Λ. The hydrodynamic evolution equations are derived from moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation, and the resulting spectra are obtained by integrating the rate over the full (3+1)-dimensional space-time history of a minimum-bias lead-lead collision at LHC energy.

## Key numbers

- LHC beam energy for the simulated lead-lead collision: 2.76 TeV (per nucleon pair)
- Critical temperature for emission cutoff: 175 MeV
- Transverse momentum integration range: p⊥min = 1 GeV to p⊥max = 20 GeV
- Invariant mass integration range: Mmin = 1 GeV to Mmax = 20 GeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used for the AdS/CFT lower bound case: η/s = 1/(4π)
- The observed effects of initial anisotropy on the spectra are larger for η/s > 1/(4π)

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic probes from anisotropic plasmas, building on the kinetic theory calculations of dilepton rates in anisotropic media by Mauricio (2007) and Martinez (2008), and on the development of (3+1)D anisotropic hydrodynamics by Martinez, Florkowski, Ryblewski, and Strickland (2010-2013). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, and motivate inclusion of hadronic phase contributions and next-to-leading-order corrections to the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework as discussed by Bazow (2013, 2015) and Tinti (2013, 2014).
