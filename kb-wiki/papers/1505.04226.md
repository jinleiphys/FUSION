# Flow harmonics $v_n$ at finite density

> We generalize the Gubser solution of viscous hydrodynamics by including the finite density effect and analytically compute the flow harmonics $v_n$. We explicitly show how $v_n$ and their viscous corrections depend on the chemical potential. The difference in $v_n$ between particles and antiparticles is also analytically computed and shown to be proportional to various chemical potentials and the viscosity. Excellent agreement is obtained between the results and the available experimental data from the SPS, RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Gubser solution of viscous hydrodynamics to include finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials, and analytically computes flow harmonics \(v_n\) and their viscous corrections, finding that the difference in \(v_n\) between particles and antiparticles is proportional to the chemical potentials and the viscosity, with qualitative agreement to experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and the LHC.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations coupled to conserved currents under the assumptions of conformal symmetry, boost invariance, and Gubser flow, treating the baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) to all orders and a subleading isospin chemical potential \(\tilde{\mu}\) to linear order. They perturb the isotropic solution with azimuthal anisotropy proportional to \(\cos n\phi\) and compute the flow harmonics \(v_n\) analytically using the Cooper-Frye freezeout formula on a constant-energy-density hypersurface in the early-time regime \(\tau \ll L\), expanding to linear order in the eccentricity \(\epsilon_n\) and the shear viscosity \(\eta\).

## Key numbers

* The integrated flow harmonic ratio in the ideal case is \(v_n/\epsilon_n = \frac{9}{64}\frac{\Gamma(3n)}{\Gamma(4n)}\left(\frac{128}{B^3}\right)^n \Gamma^2\left(\frac{n}{2}\right) \frac{n^2(3n+2)^2(n-1)}{2(4n+1)}\), where \(B\) is related to the freezeout energy density \(\varepsilon_c = 1\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\).
* The viscous correction to \(v_n\) is given by \(v_n/\epsilon_n = \frac{9}{64}\frac{\Gamma(3n)}{\Gamma(4n)}\left(\frac{128}{B^3}\right)^n \Gamma^2\left(\frac{n}{2}\right) \left\{\frac{n^2(3n+2)^2(n-1)}{2(4n+1)} - \frac{3n^3(n-1)K}{16(3n-1)}(3n^2+3n+2)\right\}\), where the Knudsen number \(K = h(\alpha)\bar{\eta}(\alpha)B^2 / (f(\alpha)C)\).
* The particle-antiparticle difference in \(v_n\) from the viscous chemical potential effect is \(\delta v_n^1 / v_n^{\text{ideal}} = \frac{\gamma K}{4} \left(k - \frac{3f'}{4f}\right) \frac{n(n+2)(4n+1)}{(3n-1)(3n+2)^2}\), where \(\gamma(\alpha) = 2 / \left(\frac{4}{3\alpha} + \frac{4g'}{3g} - \frac{f'}{f}\right)\) and \(k = \pm 1\) for particles/antiparticles.
* The particle multiplicity is related to the parameter \(C\) by \(dN_i/dY \approx g_i (4C^3/\pi)\) for massless pions.

## Context

This work extends the analytical Gubser solution of viscous hydrodynamics (Gubser 2010) and the subsequent computation of flow harmonics at zero chemical potential (Hatta 2014) to include finite baryon density, relevant for the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC. It provides an analytical framework to understand the beam-energy dependence of \(v_n\) and the particle-antiparticle splitting of flow harmonics, complementing numerical simulations (Kolb 2000, Teaney 2001, Petersen 2009, Shen 2012, Karpenko 2015) and enabling analytic estimates for lower-energy collisions where the chemical potential is significant.
