# Another Decade of Strange Quark Matter (ASTRO) Physics

> We present a perspective of strange quark matter research in the 1991-2001 decade focused on astrophysics topics, with particular attention to open problems. We outline the basic concepts and developments in the field, paying attention to the established research and promising perspectives. An analysis of the whole literature (experimental searches, theory, astrophysical/cosmological) serves to point out some general trends, supported by a fairy complete statistical sample of published works.

# Another Decade of Strange Quark Matter (ASTRO) Physics

## Key claim
Strange quark matter (SQM) remains a completely speculative but vigorously pursued hypothesis after decades of research; the paper argues that despite the lack of definitive evidence, astrophysical observations from new-generation telescopes and neutrino observatories may soon allow falsification of whether SQM exists and whether it has ever been produced in the Universe.

## Method
The authors present a personal perspective review of SQM astrophysics research from 1991-2001, analyzing the published literature (390 papers directly dealing with SQM in that decade) and classifying contributions into three categories: stability and physical properties, laboratory searches, and astrophysical/cosmological studies. They focus on selected astrophysical problems including supernova explosions, compact star structure, gamma-ray bursts, and detection prospects, while discussing uncertainties and open questions.

## Key numbers
- The absolute number of SQM papers in 1992-2001 was 390, showing a smooth continuous growth of approximately 70% compared to the 223 papers in the previous decade (1969-1991)
- Astrophysical/cosmological papers grew from about 1/3 of total at the decade's beginning to more than one-half in later years, with a peak of approximately 2/3 in the year 2000
- A redshift of z = 0.35 was detected in the bursting source EXO 0748-676 spectrum by Cottam, Paerels and Mendez
- Mass and radius determinations for sources Her X-1 and SAX J1808.4-3658 yielded values around 1 solar mass and approximately 7 km
- The timescale for weak interactions to create net strangeness in deconfined matter is approximately 10^{-8} s
- The difference (m_n - E/n) at P=0 is not expected to be larger than a few tens of MeV, requiring model accuracy of about 1%
- The upper limit to the rate of accretion-induced collapse events is approximately 10^{-4} yr^{-1} galaxy^{-1}

## Context
This review extends the benchmark 1991 Aarhus Workshop on SQM in Physics and Astrophysics and the foundational work of Bodmer (1971), Chin and Kerman (1979), Terazawa (1979), Bjorken and McLerran (1979), and Witten (1984). It assesses progress since those early developments and identifies persistent open questions including whether SQM is the true ground state of hadronic matter, whether it has ever been produced in the Universe, and whether the deconfinement transition occurs inside neutron stars. The paper enables future work by cataloging the literature and highlighting priority tasks such as detailed calculations of SQM combustion in supernovae and systematic studies of the color-flavor locked state's implications for stellar structure.
