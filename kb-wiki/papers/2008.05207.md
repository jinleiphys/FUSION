# Thermal and viscous dissipation in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the effects of finite baryon density and temperature on the bulk properties of matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions within second-order dissipative hydrodynamics. The relativistic fluid evolution equations for heat flow and shear stress tensor are derived from kinetic theory by using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the single-particle phase-space distribution function. The new equations provide a number of additional terms associated with heat-shear couplings as compared to the existing derivations based on entropy principle. The dissipative equations are encoded in non-boost-invariant hydrodynamic model simulation and studied for the evolution of high baryon density matter encountered at the beam energy scan program at RHIC. We find that thermal dissipation dominates shear pressure in defining the bulk observables at the low energy but its effect diminishes at ultra-relativistic energies.

# Thermal and viscous dissipation in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors derive new second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations from kinetic theory using Grad's 14-moment approximation that include additional heat-shear coupling terms not present in previous entropy-principle derivations, and find that thermal dissipation dominates shear pressure in defining bulk observables at low collision energies (around $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ GeV) but its effect diminishes at ultra-relativistic energies (up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV).

## Method

The relativistic fluid evolution equations for heat flow and shear stress tensor were derived from kinetic theory using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the single-particle phase-space distribution function, with the linearized Boltzmann equation solved to obtain the out-of-equilibrium distribution function. The dissipative equations were encoded in a non-boost-invariant (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model simulation using the SHASTA-FCT algorithm, employing a (2+1)-flavor QCD equation of state with Taylor series expansion up to sixth order in baryon chemical potential matched to a hadron resonance gas equation of state at low temperature. The model was applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV), Pb+Pb collisions at SPS ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV), and Au+Au collisions at AGS ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ GeV), with initial conditions tuned to reproduce net-proton and charged pion rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at $\tau_0 = 0.4$ fm: $\epsilon_0 = 23.5$ GeV/fm$^3$ for RHIC (200 GeV), 16.2 GeV/fm$^3$ for SPS (17.3 GeV), and 9.0 GeV/fm$^3$ for AGS (5 GeV)
- Decoupling temperature: $T_{\rm dec} = 150$ MeV for RHIC, 142 MeV for SPS, and 135 MeV for AGS
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio fixed at $\eta_v/s = 0.08$ (equivalent to $1/4\pi$)
- Relaxation time for shear pressure: $\tau_\pi = 6\eta_v/w$ where $w = \epsilon + P$ is the enthalpy
- Relaxation time for heat flow: $\tau_q = 5\lambda T(\hat{h} - 3)/[w(\hat{h} - 5)^2]$ where $\hat{h} = w/nT$
- Lattice QCD crossover temperature at zero chemical potential: $T_c \approx 154$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous second-order relativistic hydrodynamic theories such as the Muller-Israel-Stewart formalism (MIS) and derivations based on entropy maximization by Muronga and Rischke, Betz, Huovinen, and Bouras, which lacked the microscopic dynamics found in kinetic theory or ignored heat-shear coupling. The new equations provide additional heat-shear coupling terms compared to these prior approaches, and the numerical implementation demonstrates that thermal conduction has noticeable effects in baryon-rich regions and at lower collision energies, unlike at ultra-relativistic energies where heat transport is typically ignored. The study enables more realistic hydrodynamic modeling of the beam energy scan program at RHIC and may be promising in the search for the critical point in the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density and temperature.
