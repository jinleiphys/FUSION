# Relativistic effects in neutron-deuteron elastic scattering

> We solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation including relativistic features such as relativistic kinematics, boost effects and Wigner spin rotations. As dynamical input a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction exactly on-shell equivalent to the AV18 potential has been used. The effects of Wigner rotations for elastic scattering observables were found to be small. The boost effects are significant at higher energies.They diminish the transition matrix elements at higher energies and lead in spite of the increased relativistic phase-space factor as compared to the nonrelativistic one to rather small effects in the cross section, which are mostly restricted to the backward angles.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in neutron-deuteron elastic scattering, including relativistic kinematics, boost effects, and Wigner spin rotations, produce only small changes in cross sections (mostly restricted to backward angles) despite significant boost-induced suppression of transition matrix elements at higher energies, because the increased relativistic phase-space factor partially compensates.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation in momentum-space partial-wave representation, incorporating relativistic kinetic energy, Lorentz-boosted two-nucleon t-matrices, and Wigner spin rotations. The boosted NN potential was approximated by a leading-order expansion in q/ω and v/ω, derived from the AV18 potential via an on-shell-equivalent transformation that preserves NN phase shifts. The coupled integral equations were solved by Neumann-series summation with Padé acceleration, using partial waves up to j_max=5 and total 3N angular momenta up to J=25/2.

## Key numbers

- Incoming neutron lab energies studied: 28, 65, 135, and 250 MeV
- Changes in cross section due to Wigner spin rotations: under 1%
- Changes in spin observables due to Wigner spin rotations: under 5% (except near zero crossings)
- Partial-wave convergence required 2N subsystem total angular momenta up to j_max=5 and 3N total angular momenta up to J=25/2, yielding up to 143 coupled integral equations per J and parity
- Boost approximation tested at deuteron binding energy: the leading-order expansion (Eq. 2apr) preserved the deuteron binding energy and D-state probability across all energies, while neglecting boost entirely or omitting the k-dependence of the first-order term produced significant deviations at higher energies

## Context

This work extends nonrelativistic three-nucleon Faddeev calculations (Witala 1988, Gloeckle 1996) to include relativistic effects, motivated by discrepancies between nonrelativistic theory and experimental nd cross-section data above approximately 100 MeV that could not be fully resolved by adding three-nucleon forces (Witala 1998, Sekiguchi 2002). The relativistic Hamiltonian approach follows the equal-time formulation of Keister and Polyzou (1991) and the boosted-potential method applied to the 3N bound state by Kamada (2002). This is the first calculation of relativistic effects in the 3N continuum, enabling future studies that incorporate full boost dynamics beyond the leading-order approximation and comparisons with precise nd scattering data at intermediate energies.
