# Calculation of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ to two loops in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> We present a calculation of the leading two-loop corrections to the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ in two covariant versions of two-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory. Taking the low-energy constants from a combined analysis of elastic and inelastic pion-nucleon scattering, we find that these corrections are rather moderate.

## Key claim

The leading two-loop corrections to the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in two covariant versions of two-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory (the extended-on-mass-shell approach and infrared regularization) are found to be rather moderate when the low-energy constants are taken from a combined analysis of elastic and inelastic pion-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors computed the leading two-loop diagrams of chiral order \(q^5\) contributing to \(g_A\) using both the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) approach and the method of infrared regularization (IR). The calculation involved 44 irreducible two-loop diagrams plus contributions from wave function renormalization, one-loop graphs with vertices from the third-order pion-nucleon Lagrangian, and counterterms; the loop integrals were evaluated using Feyncalc, TARCER, and HypExp, with master integrals decomposed into infrared, mixed, and regular parts.

## Key numbers

- The chiral expansion of \(g_A\) takes the form \(g_A = g_0 \{1 + \Delta^{(2)} + \Delta^{(3)} + \Delta^{(4)}\} + \mathcal{O}(M^5)\), where \(\Delta^{(2)}\) and \(\Delta^{(3)}\) are one-loop corrections and \(\Delta^{(4)}\) is the leading two-loop correction of chiral order \(q^5\).
- The one-loop coefficient \(\alpha_2 = -2 - 4g_0^2\) and \(\alpha_3 = (1/(24\pi F^2 m))(3+3g_0^2-4m c_3+8m c_4)\).
- The hard scale related to chiral symmetry breaking is estimated as \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_\pi \simeq 1.2\) GeV, with \(F_\pi \simeq 92\) MeV.
- The one-loop wave function renormalization constant \(Z_{\rm 1-loop}\) contains terms proportional to \(M^2\) with coefficients involving \(g_0^2\) and the pion mass \(M\).
- The counterterm contribution to \(g_A\) at two-loop order is parameterized as \(g_A^{\rm ct} = C M^4\), where \(C\) contains terms up to \(g_0^5\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of Bernard (2006), which used renormalization group methods in the heavy baryon approach and confronted two-loop results with lattice calculations at unphysical pion masses. The present calculation uses the operator basis and low-energy constants from Gasser (2002) and provides the first full two-loop calculation of \(g_A\) in covariant chiral perturbation theory, enabling more precise chiral extrapolations for lattice QCD simulations and improving the understanding of the convergence of the chiral expansion for this observable.
