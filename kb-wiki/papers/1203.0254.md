# The Electromagnetic Self-Energy Contribution to M_p - M_n and the Isovector Nucleon Magnetic Polarizability

> We update the determination of the isovector nucleon electromagnetic self-energy, valid to leading order in QED. A technical oversight in the literature concerning the elastic contribution to Cottingham's formula is corrected and modern knowledge of the structure functions is used to precisely determine the inelastic contribution. We find δM_{p-n}^γ= 1.30(03)(47) MeV. The largest uncertainty arises from a subtraction term required in the dispersive analysis, which can be related to the isovector magnetic polarizability. With plausible model assumptions, we can combine our calculation with additional input from lattice QCD to constrain this polarizability as: β_{p-n} = -0.87(85) x 10^{-4} fm^3.

# The Electromagnetic Self-Energy Contribution to M_p - M_n and the Isovector Nucleon Magnetic Polarizability

## Key claim

The isovector nucleon electromagnetic self-energy is determined to be δM_{p-n}^γ = 1.30(03)(47) MeV, but the largest uncertainty (0.47 MeV) arises from a subtraction term in the dispersive analysis that depends on the poorly known isovector magnetic polarizability. The central value is larger than the previous determination of 0.76 ± 0.30 MeV from Gasser (1974), primarily due to correcting a technical oversight in the elastic contribution to Cottingham's formula.

## Method

The authors apply Cottingham's sum rule, which relates the nucleon electromagnetic self-energy to measured elastic and inelastic electron scattering cross sections via dispersion relations. They correct an inconsistency in the literature where an unsubtracted dispersion relation was incorrectly applied to the elastic contribution in the t_1 parameterization, and instead perform a subtracted dispersive analysis of T_1 and t_1 with an unsubtracted analysis of T_2 and t_2. The elastic contribution is evaluated using the Kelly parameterization of nucleon form factors, the inelastic contribution uses the Bosted-Christy fits for the resonance region (Q^2 up to 8 GeV^2, W up to 3.1 GeV) extended with Regge parameterizations, and the subtraction term is modeled using elastic form factors plus a dipole-damped magnetic polarizability term.

## Key numbers

- δM_{p-n}^γ = 1.30(03)(47) MeV (total isovector electromagnetic self-energy)
- Elastic contribution: δM^{el}|_{p-n} = 1.39(02) MeV
- Inelastic contribution: δM^{inel}|_{p-n} = 0.057(16) MeV
- Elastic subtraction term: δM^{sub}_{el}|_{p-n} = -0.62(02) MeV
- Inelastic subtraction term: δM^{sub}_{inel}|_{p-n} = 0.47 ± 0.47 MeV (proportional to the isovector magnetic polarizability)
- β_{p-n} = -0.87(85) × 10^{-4} fm^3 (isovector magnetic polarizability, constrained by combining with lattice QCD)
- M_n - M_p = 1.29333217(42) MeV (experimental neutron-proton mass difference)
- Lattice QCD determination of m_d - m_u contribution: δM^{latt}_{m_d - m_u}|_{p-n} = -2.53(40) MeV (weighted mean of results from Beane (2006), Blum (2010), and deDivitiis (2011))
- Model-independent bound from combining experiment and lattice: δM_{p-n}^γ = 1.24(40) MeV

## Context

This work updates the classic determination by Gasser (1974) of the electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference using Cottingham's sum rule, correcting a technical oversight in the elastic contribution and incorporating modern structure function data. The improved precision of the elastic and inelastic contributions (reducing uncertainty from ±0.30 MeV to ±0.03 MeV) is offset by a larger uncertainty from the subtraction term, which is linked to the isovector magnetic polarizability. The results enable better separation of electromagnetic and quark mass difference contributions to isospin breaking, which is important for lattice QCD calculations of isospin-violating effects (Blum 2010, deDivitiis 2011) and for effective field theory treatments of charge asymmetric nuclear reactions (van Kolck 1996, Miller 2006). The authors note that upcoming experiments at MAX-Lab Lund and the HIGS facility at TUNL may improve knowledge of the isovector magnetic polarizability.
