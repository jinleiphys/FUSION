# Neutrino Emission from Helium White Dwarfs with Condensed Cores

> The possibility that ions in a helium white dwarf star are in a Bose-Einstein condensed state has been explored recently. In particular, it has been argued that the resulting novel quantum liquid has a new kind of quasiparticle excitation with a phonon-like dispersion relation. We investigate the neutrino emission rate due to this gapless state and the resulting impact on the total luminosity of helium white dwarf stars, as a possible observable way of detecting this exotic phase. If the condensation temperature for the quantum liquid state, which is currently not known very precisely, turns out to be high enough, our calculations indicate that neutrino emission due to the gapless mode would make a large contribution to the total luminosity of the helium white dwarf stars.

## Key claim
If the condensation temperature for the quantum liquid state in helium white dwarfs turns out to be high enough, neutrino emission from the gapless "half-sound" quasiparticle mode would make a large contribution to the total luminosity of the star; the condensation temperature is currently not known very precisely.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective field theory for the gapless half-sound mode that arises when helium ions in a white dwarf core undergo Bose-Einstein condensation. They performed a matching calculation from the Standard Model down to this effective theory to derive the coupling between half-sound quasiparticles and neutrinos via Z boson exchange, then computed the neutrino emissivity from the annihilation reaction HH → νν̄. The resulting emissivity was integrated over a model white dwarf density profile from the hydrostatic equilibrium equation, with the integration restricted to regions where the condensate is expected to exist.

## Key numbers
- The half-sound speed is c_H ∼ 0.001 c, or c_H ∼ 300 km/s, for a representative white dwarf with central density 5 × 10⁵ g/cm³ and T ≲ 3 × 10⁶ K.
- The neutrino emissivity from half-sound annihilation scales as Q ∝ c_H⁻⁷, giving Q ≈ 9.5 (G_F² sin⁴θ_W T¹¹)/(M² c_H⁷).
- The plasmon gap m_A ranges between 2.1 × 10⁶ K and 4.6 × 10⁶ K as the ratio a₀/l (Bohr radius to interparticle spacing) goes from 30 to 50.
- The electromagnetic luminosity follows L/L_⊙ ≈ (T/(7 × 10⁷ K))² (M/M_⊙) for a simple Mestel model.
- The specific heat of the condensed phase is ∼10⁻² times smaller than the classical plasma phase and ∼10⁻⁴ times smaller than an ion lattice.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Bedaque, 2011) which identified the gapless half-sound mode in the condensed phase of super-dense helium, and earlier proposals by Gabadadze (2008, 2009) that helium nuclei in white dwarfs could Bose-condense. The paper addresses the puzzle from observations by Strickler (2009) that the helium white dwarf sequence in globular cluster NGC 6397 terminates early, suggesting faster cooling than expected. The results indicate that if the condensation temperature is high (parameter C = 10, 100, or 1000 in the critical temperature formula), neutrino emission from half-sound annihilation could compete with electromagnetic radiation as a cooling mechanism, but definitive conclusions require more precise calculations of the critical temperature and finite-temperature effects.
