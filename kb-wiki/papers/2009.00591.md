# Equation of Motion Method to strongly correlated Fermi systems and Extended RPA approaches

> The status of different extensions of the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) is reviewed. The general framework is given within the Equation of Motion Method and the equivalent Green's function approach for the so-called Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA). The role of the Pauli principle is analyzed. A comparison among various approaches to include Pauli correlations, in particular, renormalized RPA (r-RPA), is performed. The thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter are studied with several cluster approximations for the self-energy of the single-particle Dyson equation. More particle RPA's are shortly discussed with a particular attention to the alpha-particle condensate. Results obtained concerning the Three-level Lipkin, Hubbard and Picket Fence Models, respectively, are outlined. Extended second RPA (ESRPA) is presented.

## Key claim

The paper reviews extensions of the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) for strongly correlated Fermi systems, arguing that the Equation of Motion method and Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) can be formulated to include Pauli-principle corrections and higher-order correlations, but the exact solution of the ground-state vacuum condition for the RPA operator is generally not possible without approximations, except in special model cases or when an extended operator is used.

## Method

The authors develop the Equation of Motion (EOM) method for fermion pair operators (e.g., particle-hole and particle-particle channels), deriving self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) equations from a generalized energy-weighted sum rule. They also present an equivalent Green's function formalism based on two-time cluster propagators, leading to a Dyson-type Bethe-Salpeter equation with a kernel depending on a single frequency. Applications are made to exactly solvable models (three-level Lipkin, Hubbard, Picket Fence) and to infinite nuclear matter, using cluster expansions for the single-particle self-energy and extended second RPA (ESRPA).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on RPA extensions pioneered by Rowe (1968) and later developed by Schuck, Dukelsky, and others (e.g., Dukelsky and Schuck 1990, 1999; Ropke et al. 1980, 1995, 2009). It contrasts with standard RPA (based on a Hartree-Fock ground state) by incorporating Pauli-principle corrections via renormalized RPA (r-RPA) and self-consistent RPA (SCRPA). The paper also connects to Coupled Cluster Doubles theory (Jemai and Schuck 2011, 2013) and to Green's function approaches for finite-temperature nuclear matter. It enables future work on cluster mean-field methods for quartetting (alpha-particle condensation) and on extended second RPA for damping of collective modes.
