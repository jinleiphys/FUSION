# Hyperon-rich Matter and Kaons in Matter

> We discuss the equation of state of neutron stars in the dense interior considering hyperons and the possible onset of kaon condensation within the relativistic mean field model. We find that hyperons are favoured in dense matter and that their appearance make the existence of a kaon condensed phase quite unlikely. Implications for the recent measurements of kaons in heavy ion collisions at subthreshold energy are also given.

# Hyperon-rich Matter and Kaons in Matter

## Key claim

Hyperons are strongly favored in dense neutron star matter, and their presence makes the onset of kaon condensation quite unlikely, contradicting earlier predictions from chiral perturbation theory that had not included hyperons. The authors find that at least 100 MeV are missing for the onset of kaon condensation across all parameter sets considered.

## Method

The authors use the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with SU(6) symmetry for vector coupling constants and fix scalar coupling constants to the empirical potential depths of hyperons in normal nuclear matter. They introduce additional meson fields (the scalar $f_0(975)$ and vector $\phi(1020)$) to account for the strongly attractive $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction, and determine the onset of s-wave kaon condensation from the condition $-\mu_e = \mu_{K^-} \equiv \omega_{K^-}(k=0)$. For heavy-ion collisions, they compute the density-dependent shift of threshold energies for kaon and antikaon production processes using both the RMF model and chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear at $2\rho_0$ (first $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^-$), with $\Xi^-$ populated at $3\rho_0$, where $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The potential depths used are $U_\Lambda^{(N)} = U_\Sigma^{(N)} = -30$ MeV and $U_\Xi^{(N)} = -28$ MeV; the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction parameters give $U^{(\Xi)}_\Xi \approx U^{(\Xi)}_\Lambda \approx 2U^{(\Lambda)}_\Xi \approx 2U^{(\Lambda)}_\Lambda \approx -40$ MeV.
- The proton fraction exceeds 11% in the density range $(2-4)\rho_0$, enabling the direct URCA process.
- The electrochemical potential never exceeds 160 MeV in the presence of hyperons, making antikaon condensation impossible (at least 100 MeV short of the required value).
- The vacuum threshold energy for NN$\to$N$\Lambda$K is $Q({\rm N}\Lambda{\rm K})\approx 671$ MeV; at $3\rho_0$ this rises to about 800 MeV (RMF) and about 860 MeV (chiral perturbation theory).
- The vacuum threshold for NN$\to$NNK$\bar K$ is $Q({\rm K}\bar K)\approx 988$ MeV; at $3\rho_0$ the RMF model gives a reduction of about $-140$ MeV, while chiral perturbation theory predicts a reduction of about $-56$ MeV at maximum.
- The kaon feels a repulsive potential of 29 MeV at low density (from the low-density theorem), while the $\Lambda$ sees an attractive potential of $-30$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the RMF treatment of hyperonic matter by Glendenning (1987) and addresses the earlier prediction from chiral perturbation theory by Brown and collaborators that antikaon condensation would occur at $\rho\approx (3-4)\rho_0$. The authors note that Prakash and collaborators found hyperons shift the critical density for kaon condensation to higher density, while their own prior work (Schaffner et al., 1996) suggested condensation becomes very unlikely due to strong hyperon-hyperon interactions. The results imply that neutron stars are more likely "giant hypernuclei" and that low-mass black holes from kaon condensation are disfavored. For heavy-ion collisions, the work shows that subthreshold K$^+$ production is mainly driven by rescattering and resonance decays rather than in-medium effects, while enhanced K$^-$ production in dense matter should be observable in antikaon flow or excitation functions.
