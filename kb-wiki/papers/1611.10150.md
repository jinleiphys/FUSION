# Event patterns extracted from top quark-related spectra in proton-proton collisions at 8 TeV

> We analyze the transverse momentum ($p_T$) and rapidity ($y$) spectra of top quark pairs, hadronic top quarks, and top quarks produced in proton-proton ($pp$) collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV. For $p_T$ spectra, we use the superposition of the inverse power-law suggested by the QCD (quantum chromodynamics) calculus and the Erlang distribution resulting from a multisource thermal model. For $y$ spectra, we use the two-component Gaussian function resulting from the revised Landau hydrodynamic model. The modelling results are in agreement with the experimental data measured at the detector level, in the fiducial phase-space, and in the full phase-space by the ATLAS Collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Based on the parameter values extracted from $p_T$ and $y$ spectra, the event patterns in three-dimensional velocity ($β_x$-$β_y$-$β_z$), momentum ($p_x$-$p_y$-$p_z$), and rapidity ($y_1$-$y_2$-$y$) spaces are obtained, and the probability distributions of these components are also obtained.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum and rapidity spectra of top quark pairs, hadronic top quarks, and top quarks in proton-proton collisions at 8 TeV can be described by a superposition of an inverse power-law (from QCD calculus) and an Erlang distribution (from a multisource thermal model) for pT, and a two-component Gaussian function (from a revised Landau hydrodynamic model) for y, but the modelling is only shown to agree with ATLAS data at the detector level, in the fiducial phase-space, and in the full phase-space under the specific condition that the rest mass values are taken from the same ATLAS reference.

## Method

The authors fit the pT spectra of top quark pairs, hadronic top quarks, and top quarks using a superposition of an inverse power-law (for the harder scattering process) and an Erlang distribution (for the hard scattering process), and fit the y spectra using a two-component Gaussian function symmetric about mid-rapidity. From the extracted parameters, they use a Monte Carlo method to generate 1000 discrete sets of (px, py, pz), (βx, βy, βz), and (y1, y2, y) values, which are then plotted as three-dimensional scatter plots (event patterns) and used to compute probability distributions and root-mean-square values of the components.

## Key numbers

- For t-tbar systems at the detector level, the inverse power-law parameters are p0 = 162.86 ± 4.20 GeV/c and n = 7.70 ± 0.40, with χ²/dof = 2.88.
- For hadronic top quarks at the detector level, the superposition parameters include k = 0.10 ± 0.02, ⟨pTi⟩ = 39.70 ± 0.50 GeV/c, and m = 3, with χ²/dof = 11.34.
- For t-tbar systems at the detector level, the two-component Gaussian rapidity parameters are yF = 0.36 ± 0.02 and σyF = 0.53 ± 0.02, with χ²/dof = 2.27.
- For hadronic top quarks at the detector level, the rapidity parameters are yF = 0.53 ± 0.02 and σyF = 0.68 ± 0.02, with χ²/dof = 2.21.
- For t-tbar systems at the detector level, the root-mean-square velocity components are √(βx²) = 0.11 ± 0.01 c, √(βy²) = 0.11 ± 0.01 c, and √(βz²) = 0.50 ± 0.01 c, with maximum |βz| = 0.96 c.
- For hadronic top quarks at the detector level, the root-mean-square velocity components are √(βx²) = 0.31 ± 0.01 c, √(βy²) = 0.32 ± 0.01 c, and √(βz²) = 0.59 ± 0.01 c, with maximum |βz| = 0.99 c.
- For t-tbar systems at the detector level, the root-mean-square momentum components are √(px²) = 63.2 ± 2.8 GeV/c, √(py²) = 63.9 ± 2.8 GeV/c, and √(pz²) = 373.4 ± 11.0 GeV/c, with maximum |pz| = 1545.0 GeV/c.
- For hadronic top quarks at the detector level, the root-mean-square momentum components are √(px²) = 97.0 ± 2.7 GeV/c, √(py²) = 98.8 ± 2.5 GeV/c, and √(pz²) = 279.1 ± 10.8 GeV/c, with maximum |pz| = 1347.2 GeV/c.
- The rest mass used for t-tbar systems at the detector level is m0 = 463.2 GeV, and for (hadronic) top quarks is m0 = 172.5 GeV, both from the ATLAS reference.

## Context

This work extends the event pattern method previously applied by the same group to net-baryons in Au-Au collisions at RHIC (Liu et al., 2015), charged particles in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (Liu et al., 2015), and Z bosons and quarkonium states at LHC energies (Liu et al., 2016). It enables the extraction of three-dimensional velocity, momentum, and rapidity distributions for top quark-related products, which can be used to compare production mechanisms of heavy versus light flavor particles and to test the applicability of the hybrid thermal-plus-QCD model to top quark spectra.
