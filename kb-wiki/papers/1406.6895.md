# Statistical uncertainties of a chiral interaction at next-to-next-to leading order

> We have quantified the statistical uncertainties of the low-energy coupling-constants (LECs) of an optimized nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). In addition, we have propagated the impact of the uncertainties of the LECs to two-nucleon scattering phase shifts, effective range parameters, and deuteron observables.

## Key claim

The statistical uncertainties of the low-energy coupling constants (LECs) of an optimized chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) are at the 1% level, with the exception of the $c_1$ and $C_{^1P_1}$ LECs which exhibit relatively larger errors; the analysis is conditional on the assumption that the residual errors in the objective function are approximately normally distributed and independent.

## Method

The authors optimized 13 LECs of an NNLO chiral two-nucleon potential (with cutoff $\Lambda=500$ MeV and spectral function renormalization cutoff $\Lambda_{\text{SFR}}=700$ MeV) by minimizing a $\chi^2$ objective function constructed from 1848 measured proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering cross sections below laboratory energy $T_{\text{LAB}} \leq 125$ MeV, using the derivative-free POUNDerS optimization algorithm. The covariance matrix of the LECs was obtained from the Hessian matrix of the $\chi^2$ function at the optimum, computed via bivariate spline interpolation, and the statistical uncertainties were propagated to deuteron observables, effective range parameters, and scattering phase shifts using a first-order Taylor expansion (error propagation by perturbation) and validated with Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the objective function is $\chi^2(\mathbf{x}_{\mu}) = 2243.5$, giving $\chi^2/N_{datum} = 1.15$ over the $T_{\text{LAB}}$ range 0-125 MeV.
- The dataset consists of $N_{obs}=1848$ measured data points, $N_{\nu}=108$ normalization constants, $N_{float}=11$ floated data sets, and $N_{par}=24$ total parameters, yielding $N_{df}=1921$ degrees of freedom.
- The relative statistical uncertainties ($1\sigma/x_{\mu}$) of the LECs range from 0.14% ($C_{^1S_0}$) to 5.88% ($c_1$); $c_1$ has a 1$\sigma$ uncertainty of $5.41 \times 10^{-2}$, while $c_3$ and $c_4$ have uncertainties of $1.68 \times 10^{-2}$ and $4.22 \times 10^{-2}$, respectively.
- The deuteron ground-state energy is predicted as $-2.222(8)$ MeV, with a relative theoretical error of 0.4%, compared to the experimental value of $2.22456627(46)$ MeV.
- The $np$ scattering length $a_{np}$ is $-23.74(17)$ fm, consistent with the experimental value $-23.740(20)$ fm.
- The $pp$ effective range $r^C_{pp}$ is $2.754(2)$ fm, compared to experimental values $2.790(14)$ fm (Bergervoet 1988) and $2.769(14)$ fm (Sanchez 1983).

## Context

This work extends the optimization methodology of Ekstrom (2013) by constructing an objective function based directly on experimental cross sections and their uncertainties rather than on phase shifts, enabling a rigorous statistical uncertainty analysis. The results are compared with the similar uncertainty analysis of Navarro (2014) on coarse-grained delta-shell interactions, finding qualitative agreement on the larger uncertainty of $c_1$ but with significantly smaller absolute uncertainties in the present work. The quantified LEC uncertainties enable future propagation to statistical uncertainties of many-body observables computed with ab initio models, providing a foundation for separating statistical and systematic uncertainties in chiral nuclear interactions.
