# Beam-Energy Dependence of Charge Balance Functions from Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> Balance functions have been measured in terms of relative pseudorapidity ($Δη$) for charged particle pairs at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV using the STAR detector. These results are compared with balance functions measured at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV by the ALICE Collaboration. The width of the balance function decreases as the collisions become more central and as the beam energy is increased. In contrast, the widths of the balance functions calculated using shuffled events show little dependence on centrality or beam energy and are larger than the observed widths. Balance function widths calculated using events generated by UrQMD are wider than the measured widths in central collisions and show little centrality dependence. The measured widths of the balance functions in central collisions are consistent with the delayed hadronization of a deconfined quark gluon plasma (QGP). The narrowing of the balance function in central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7 GeV implies that a QGP is still being created at this relatively low energy.

## Key claim
The narrowing of charge balance functions in central Au+Au collisions persists down to the lowest measured beam energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, which the authors interpret as evidence that a deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is still being created at this relatively low energy, contingent on the interpretation that narrow balance functions signal delayed hadronization of a deconfined system.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration measured balance functions in terms of relative pseudorapidity (Delta-eta) for charged particle pairs in Au+Au collisions at six beam energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV. The analysis used like-sign subtraction to isolate correlations from balancing charges, and the widths of the balance functions were extracted as a function of collision centrality and beam energy. The measured widths were compared to those from shuffled events (to remove physics correlations), from UrQMD model calculations, and from ALICE Pb+Pb data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Balance function widths were measured at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
- The width decreases as collisions become more central and as beam energy increases, with the narrowest widths observed in central collisions at the highest RHIC energy of 200 GeV.
- Balance function widths from shuffled events show little dependence on centrality or beam energy and are larger than the observed widths.
- UrQMD model calculations produce wider balance function widths than measured in central collisions and show little centrality dependence.
- The measured widths in central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV are consistent with the delayed hadronization interpretation, implying QGP creation at this energy.

## Context
This work extends previous balance function measurements by the STAR Collaboration at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV to a systematic beam-energy scan down to 7.7 GeV, and compares with ALICE results at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The results contradict expectations from hadronic models such as UrQMD, which fail to reproduce the narrowing trend. The findings enable mapping of the onset of deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions and provide experimental constraints for theoretical models of the QCD phase diagram, particularly in the region of the proposed critical point.
