# Predictions of the residue cross-sections for the elements Z=113 and Z=114

> An extremely good reproduction of experimental excitation function of the 1n reactions producing Z=110,Z=111 and Z=112 is obtained by the two-step model and the statistical decay code KEWPIE. Thus, an extension of the recipe permits us to predict reliable values of the residue cross-sections of the elements Z=113 and Z=114 which will be a useful guide for planning of experiments.

## Key claim

The two-step model combined with the statistical decay code KEWPIE reproduces experimental excitation functions for 1n reactions producing Z=110, Z=111, and Z=112 extremely well, but only after introducing a phenomenological reduction factor (0.36-0.4) for the shell correction energy to match absolute peak heights; predictions for Z=113 and Z=114 are therefore conditional on this same factor being valid for extrapolation.

## Method

The fusion probability was calculated using a two-step model: the approaching phase was described by a Langevin equation with friction and fluctuation forces for the relative motion, and the formation phase by a multi-dimensional Langevin equation for shape evolution from a pear-shaped sticking configuration to a spherical compound nucleus. The survival probability was computed with the statistical decay code KEWPIE, which solves the time-dependent Bateman equation. Calculations were performed using three different mass tables (Moller & Nix, Koura, Myers & Swiatecki), each with a constant reduction factor for the shell correction energy calibrated to the Z=110 system.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{64}Ni + ^{208}Pb \rightarrow ^{271}110 + 1n$, the shell correction energy reduction factor was 0.4 for Moller & Nix, 0.36 for Koura, and 0.4 for Myers & Swiatecki.
- The global phenomenological trend shows a decrease of 1n residue cross-sections by roughly a factor of 3 for each increase of one charge unit in the super-heavy element region.
- For the reaction $^{76}Ge + ^{208}Pb \rightarrow ^{283}114 + 1n$, the predicted residue cross-section is around a few tenths of a picobarn.
- The reduced friction coefficient used in the Kramers factor for KEWPIE was set to $\beta = 5 \times 10^{-21} s$ for all results.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to hot fusion reactions with $^{48}Ca$ plus actinide targets (She and Abe, 2002; Abe and Kosenko, 2002) to cold fusion paths producing elements Z=110-112, and then to predictions for Z=113 and Z=114. The model addresses the fusion-hindrance phenomenon observed experimentally by Quint (1993), Schmidt (1999), and Reisdorf (1985), and uses the surface friction model of Gross (1975) for the approaching phase. The predictions provide a guide for future experiments at RIKEN and GSI to produce new super-heavy elements via 1n evaporation channels.
