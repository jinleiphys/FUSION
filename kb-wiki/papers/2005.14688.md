# Helicity-dependent extension of the McLerran-Venugopalan model

> We construct a generalization of the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model including helicity effects for a longitudinally polarized target (a proton or a large nucleus). The extended MV model can serve as the initial condition for the helicity generalization of the JIMWLK evolution equation constructed in our previous paper, as well as for calculation of helicity-dependent observables in the quasi-classical approximation to QCD.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a generalization of the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model that includes helicity effects for a longitudinally polarized target, providing the inhomogeneous term (initial condition) for the helicity-JIMWLK evolution equation derived in the authors' previous work. The construction is explicitly performed for a large nucleus with atomic number A ≫ 1, though the authors express hope that it may also apply to a proton based on the success of the unpolarized MV model in proton phenomenology.

## Method

The authors start from the standard MV model formalism for a large nucleus, representing the target as A independent nucleons each containing "valence" partons that serve as classical sources for the gluon field. They rederive the Gaussian weight functional for the unpolarized case using coordinate-space position eigenstates and creation/annihilation operators, then extend this formalism to include sub-eikonal operators that depend on helicity. The helicity-dependent extension introduces a new field β(x) ≡ F₁₂(x) (the sub-eikonal gluon field strength) and quark fields ψ, ψ̄ into the weight functional, alongside the eikonal field α(x) ≡ A⁺(x). The final result for the initial condition functional W⁽⁰⁾ is given in Eq. (W_final) of the paper.

## Key numbers

- The atomic number condition A ≫ 1 is stated as the domain of validity for the construction, with the nucleon number density ρ_A normalized such that ∫ d²b db⁻ ρ_A(b⁻, b) = A
- The nucleon size is taken as r_p ∼ 1/Λ_QCD, with Λ_QCD the non-perturbative QCD scale
- The evolution equation resums powers of α_s ln²(1/x) at leading order, in contrast to the standard JIMWLK evolution which resums powers of α_s ln(1/x)
- The dimension of the adjoint representation is K_A = N_c² − 1 and of the fundamental representation is K_F = N_c

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran 1993, 1994) to include helicity dependence, building on the authors' previous derivation of the helicity-JIMWLK evolution equation (Cougoulic and Kovchegov 2019). The paper is part of a series aimed at constructing small-x asymptotics of quark and gluon helicity distributions and orbital angular momentum (Kovchegov 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019). The resulting initial condition enables calculation of helicity-dependent observables in the quasi-classical approximation to QCD and provides the necessary input for solving the flavor-singlet helicity-JIMWLK equation, which is relevant to addressing the proton spin puzzle.
