# Exploring the Macroscopic Properties and Nonradial Oscillations of Proto-Neutron Stars: Effects of Temperature, Entropy, and Lepton Fraction

> Neutron stars (NSs) have traditionally been viewed as cold, zero-temperature entities. However, recent progress in computational methods and theoretical modelling has opened up the exploration of finite temperature effects, marking a novel research frontier. This study examines Proto-Neutron Stars (PNSs) using the BigApple parameter set to investigate their macroscopic properties. Two approaches are employed: one with constant temperatures (10-50 MeV) and the other fixing entropy per baryon (S) at predefined levels (S = 1 and S = 2). Notably, S remains constant with increasing baryon density due to electron-positron pair formation at finite temperatures. Analysis of PNS mass-radius profiles, considering neutrino trapping and temperature effects, reveals flattened curves and expanded radii with increasing temperature, resulting in slightly higher masses compared to zero temperature. The influence of lepton fraction ($Y_l$) on maximum PNS mass is explored, indicating that higher $Y_l$ values lead to a softer Equation of State (EoS), reducing maximum mass and increasing the canonical radius ($R_{1.4}$). Further investigation of a constant entropy EoS demonstrates that higher entropy is associated with increased maximum PNS masses and flatter mass-radius curves. Central temperature versus maximum mass relationships suggest a correlation between NS mass and temperature. Lastly, we investigate the behaviour of $f$-mode frequencies in PNS. It reveals that the frequency of these modes decreases with increasing entropy and temperature, reflecting complex thermodynamic interactions within the stars.

## Key claim

The study shows that proto-neutron star macroscopic properties and f-mode oscillation frequencies depend on whether the equation of state is modeled with constant temperature (10-50 MeV) or constant entropy per baryon (S = 1 and S = 2), with the constant entropy approach argued to be more physically realistic for supernova and merger scenarios than the uniform-temperature assumption used in prior work.

## Method

The authors used the BigApple relativistic mean-field parameter set, extended to finite temperature, to compute the equation of state for proto-neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium with neutrino trapping. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium to obtain mass-radius relations, and calculated f-mode oscillation frequencies using the Cowling approximation, which neglects metric perturbations.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range explored: 10-50 MeV for the constant-temperature approach
- Entropy per baryon values fixed at S = 1 and S = 2 for the constant-entropy approach
- Lepton fraction values Y_l = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4 used in constant-entropy calculations
- BigApple parameter set reproduces the 2.6 solar mass neutron star constraint from GW190814 across the entire temperature range studied
- Canonical radius R_{1.4} increases with higher lepton fraction Y_l, while maximum mass decreases

## Context

This work extends finite-temperature studies of proto-neutron stars that previously assumed uniform temperature distributions, such as those by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) using the LS model and Shen et al. (2011) using relativistic mean-field theory with Thomas-Fermi approximation. The constant-entropy approach is argued to better represent conditions in core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers like GW170817. The results enable more accurate modeling of proto-neutron star evolution and gravitational wave signals from f-mode oscillations in hot, newly formed neutron stars.
