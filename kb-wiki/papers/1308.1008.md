# Density dependence of the symmetry energy from neutron skin thickness in finite nuclei

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy around saturation density, characterized by the slope parameter L, is studied using information provided by the neutron skin thickness in finite nuclei. An estimate for L is obtained from experimental data on neutron skins extracted from antiprotonic atoms. We also discuss the ability of parity-violating elastic electron scattering to obtain information on the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb and to constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The size and shape of the neutron density distribution of 208Pb predicted by mean-field models is briefly addressed. We conclude with a comparative overview of the L values predicted by several existing determinations.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy at saturation density can be constrained to a range of approximately 30 to 80 MeV by fitting the droplet model to neutron skin thickness data from antiprotonic atoms, after including a surface-width correction; the extracted range depends on the assumed parametrization of the surface-width contribution and on the choice of the empirical bulk symmetry energy coefficient \(J = 31.6\) MeV.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear droplet model (DM) to relate the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) of finite nuclei to the ratio \(J/Q\) (bulk symmetry energy over surface stiffness) and, via a linear correlation, to the slope parameter \(L\). They performed extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations of asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter to extract the surface widths \(b_n\) and \(b_p\) and the surface stiffness coefficient \(Q\) for several Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean-field models. The DM formula including a surface-width correction was fitted to the experimental \(\Delta r_{np}\) data of 26 stable nuclei (\(40 \leq A \leq 238\)) measured from antiprotonic atoms at CERN by Trzcińska et al. (2001, 2004). Separately, the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{\rm pv}\) in elastic electron scattering on \({}^{208}\)Pb was computed in the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) using self-consistent mean-field densities, and the result from the first PREX run was used to extract a \(\Delta r_{np}\) value.

## Key numbers

- The linear fit of \(\Delta r_{np}\) versus \(L\) for \({}^{208}\)Pb from mean-field models gives \(\Delta r_{np} \;{\rm (fm)} = 0.101 + 0.00147 L \;{\rm (MeV)}\) (correlation coefficient \(r = 0.979\)).
- Fitting the DM (with \(b_n = b_p\)) to antiprotonic-atom data yields \(L = 75 \pm 25\) MeV (corresponding to \(\gamma = 0.79 \pm 0.25\) in a power-law parametrization \(S(\rho) = J (\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma\) with \(J = 31.6\) MeV).
- Including the surface-width correction, the fits to antiprotonic-atom data give \(J/Q = 0.667 \pm 0.047\) (for \(c = 0.07\) fm) and \(J/Q = 0.791 \pm 0.049\) (for \(c = -0.05\) fm), corresponding to a total range \(0.62 \le J/Q \le 0.84\).
- Converting the \(J/Q\) range to \(L\) via the linear relation \(L = 139 J/Q - 52\) MeV to \(L = 150 J/Q - 57\) MeV gives \(30 \le L \le 80\) MeV.
- The first PREX run measured \(A_{\rm pv} = 0.656 \pm (0.060)_{\rm stat} \pm (0.014)_{\rm syst}\) ppm at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 0.0088 \pm 0.0001\) GeV\(^2\), from which \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\) fm (prexprl12) and \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.302 \pm (0.175)_{\rm exp} \pm (0.026)_{\rm model} \pm (0.005)_{\rm strange}\) fm (horowitz12) were extracted for \({}^{208}\)Pb.
- The experimental trend of \(\Delta r_{np}\) from antiprotonic atoms across the mass table is fitted by \(\Delta r_{np} = (0.90 \pm 0.15) I + (-0.03 \pm 0.02)\) fm, where \(I = (N-Z)/A\).

## Context

This work extends the droplet-model analysis of Centelles et al. (2009) and Warda et al. (2009) by incorporating a surface-width correction to the neutron skin formula and by comparing the resulting \(L\) constraints with those from the PREX parity-violating electron scattering experiment on \({}^{208}\)Pb. The extracted \(L\) range of 30–80 MeV overlaps with the common range of 45–70 MeV reported from other determinations (e.g., isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions by Li et al. (2005, 2008) and giant resonance studies by Roca-Maza et al. (2012, 2013)). The results support the need for improved precision in neutron skin measurements, such as the upcoming PREX-II and CREX experiments at Jefferson Lab, to further constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
