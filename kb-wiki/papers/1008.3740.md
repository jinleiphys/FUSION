# Medium effects of magnetic moments of baryons on neutron stars under strong magnetic fields

> We investigate medium effects due to density-dependent magnetic moments of baryons on neutron stars under strong magnetic fields. If we allow the variation of anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of baryons in dense matter under strong magnetic fields, AMMs of nucleons are enhanced to be larger than those of hyperons. The enhancement naturally affects the chemical potentials of baryons to be large and leads to the increase of a proton fraction. Consequently, it causes the suppression of hyperons, resulting in the stiffness of the equation of state. Under the presumed strong magnetic fields, we evaluate relevant particles' population, the equation of state and the maximum masses of neutron stars by including density-dependent AMMs and compare them with those obtained from AMMs in free space.

# Medium effects of magnetic moments of baryons on neutron stars under strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

If density-dependent anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of baryons are allowed in dense matter under strong magnetic fields, the AMMs of nucleons are enhanced to become larger than those of hyperons, which increases chemical potentials and proton fraction, suppresses hyperons, and stiffens the equation of state; however, the contribution of density-dependent AMMs to the maximum mass is at most about 0.1 solar masses, remaining subsidiary to the dominant effect of the magnetic field strength itself.

## Method

The authors employed the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model including octet baryons and leptons under a constant magnetic field along the z-axis, incorporating electromagnetic interactions, Landau quantization of charged particles, and anomalous magnetic moments of baryons. The density-dependent AMM values for baryon octets were taken from the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model, which accounts for the swollen effect of nucleons by increasing the bag radius about 20% at saturation density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved using the resulting equation of state for both slow and fast varying magnetic field profiles parameterized as a function of density.

## Key numbers

- AMM enhancement at saturation density: proton about 25%, Lambda about 10%, Xi about 5% (from MQMC model)
- Surface magnetic field taken as B_surf = 10^15 G from observations
- Critical magnetic field for electron: B_e^c = 4.414 x 10^13 G
- Three magnetic field strengths used: B_0^* = 1 x 10^5, 2 x 10^5, and 3 x 10^5 (in units of B_e^c)
- Increase of pressure P_m about 37 MeV fm^-3 at rho = 6 rho_0 for fast case with B_0^* = 3 x 10^5
- Maximum mass without magnetic fields: about 1.59 M_sun (hyperonic star)
- Maximum mass for fast varying field with B_0^* = 3 x 10^5: 2.96 M_sun (density-dependent AMM) versus 2.88 M_sun (constant AMM)
- Central density for fast case B_0^* = 3 x 10^5: 5.00 rho_0 (density-dependent AMM) versus 4.90 rho_0 (constant AMM)
- Central magnetic field for fast case B_0^* = 3 x 10^5: B_c^* = 2.75 x 10^5 (density-dependent AMM) versus 2.71 x 10^5 (constant AMM)
- Maximum mass increase due to density-dependent AMMs: about 0.07 M_sun for B_0^* = 3 x 10^5 in fast varying field
- Upper limit of magnetic fields inferred: B approximately 3 x 10^5 B_e^c G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars under strong magnetic fields by Bandyopadhyay (1997), Broderick (2000), and Shen (2009) that included electromagnetic interactions and Landau quantization but used constant AMM values. The authors apply density-dependent AMMs from their earlier MQMC model calculations (Ryu, 2009) to neutron star structure, finding that while magnetic field effects dominate, medium-dependent AMMs can increase maximum masses by about 0.1 M_sun. The results for fast varying magnetic fields satisfy observational constraints from XTE J1739-285 and can explain neutron star masses of 2-3 M_sun for hyperonic stars, whereas the slow varying field case does not satisfy the constraint. The authors note that the largest enhancement of nucleon AMMs in other models (Cheon, 1992) reaches about 40% at saturation density, which would produce larger but still subsidiary effects.
