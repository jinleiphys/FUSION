# On the Lorentz structure of the confinement potential

> We investigate the Lorentz structure of the confinement potential through a study of the meson spectrum using Salpeter's instantaneous approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The equivalence between Salpeter's and a random-phase-approximation (RPA) equation enables one to employ the same techniques developed by Thouless, in his study of nuclear collective excitations, to test the stability of the solutions. The stablity analysis reveals the existence of imaginary eigenvalues for a confining potential that transforms as a Lorentz scalar. Moreover, we argue that the instability persists even for very large values of the constituent quark mass. In contrast, we find no evidence of imaginary eigenvalues for a timelike vector potential --- even for very small values of the constituent mass.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a confining potential transforming as a Lorentz scalar leads to an instability (imaginary eigenvalues) in Salpeter's equation for pseudoscalar mesons, and that this instability persists even for very large constituent quark masses; in contrast, a timelike vector confining potential produces no such instability even for very small constituent masses.

## Method

The authors used Salpeter's instantaneous approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a quark-antiquark system, exploiting the algebraic equivalence between Salpeter's equation and a random-phase-approximation (RPA) equation. They applied Thouless's stability analysis from nuclear collective excitations to test for imaginary eigenvalues in the pseudoscalar channel, expanding the Salpeter amplitudes in a harmonic-oscillator basis and diagonalizing the resulting matrix equation for both scalar and timelike-vector Lorentz structures of a linear confining potential.

## Key numbers

- String tension: sigma = 0.29 GeV^2 used in numerical calculations.
- For a constituent quark mass of M = 300 MeV, scalar confinement produced imaginary eigenvalues (E^2 < 0) even with only five basis states; timelike confinement showed stable plateaus.
- For a constituent quark mass of M = 900 MeV, scalar confinement appeared stable with 5 or 15 basis states but became unstable when 25 basis states were included, revealing the instability.
- The critical ratio for stability of mixed scalar/timelike confinement was found at x >= 0.5, where x is the fraction of timelike structure in the potential.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Lagae (1992) and Gara, Durand, and Durand (1989), who concluded that scalar confinement was not correct within the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter framework, and by Archvadze, Chachkhunashvili, and Kopaleishvili (1993), who found no stable solutions for scalar confinement in the light-mass spectrum. The paper also agrees with Munz, Resag, Metsch, and Petry (1993), who found that reliable solutions for scalar confinement exist only for large quark masses and weak confinement strength. The stability analysis enables future work to determine the Lorentz structure of confinement by requiring stable solutions in relativistic quark models.
