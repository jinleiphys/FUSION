# Monopoles and string tension in SU(2) QCD

> Monopole and photon contributions to abelian Wilson loops are calculated using Monte-Carlo simulations of SU(2) QCD in the maximally abelian gauge. The string tension is well reproduced only by monopole contributions, whereas photons alone are responsible for the Coulomb coefficient of the abelian static potential.

## Key claim

The string tension in SU(2) QCD is well reproduced by monopole contributions alone, while photons alone are responsible for the Coulomb coefficient of the abelian static potential, but this result holds specifically after abelian projection in the maximally abelian (MA) gauge and depends on the use of extended monopoles of type-2 with a scale factor.

## Method

Monte-Carlo simulations of SU(2) QCD were performed on a 24^4 lattice at beta values from 2.4 to 2.7 using the Wilson action. After fixing to the maximally abelian gauge, the authors decomposed abelian link fields into photon and monopole contributions, then separately evaluated the expectation values of the photon part (W1) and monopole part (W2) of abelian Wilson loops. Extended monopoles of type-2 (2^3 and 3^3 cubes) were used in addition to the smallest 1^3 monopoles, and static potentials were extracted from Creutz ratios assuming a linear plus Coulomb plus constant form.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed on a 24^4 lattice at beta = 2.4, 2.5, 2.6, and 2.7
- 50 configurations were used for measurements, taken every 30 sweeps after 1500 thermalization sweeps
- Type-2 extended monopole loops with b greater than b_c ~ 5.2 x 10^(-3) (Lambda_L)^(-1) were found to condense, where b = n a(beta) for n^3 extended monopoles
- The scale parameter Lambda determined from photon Coulomb coefficients was Lambda ~ 48 Lambda_L, close to the value Lambda ~ 42 Lambda_L fixed from the monopole action
- String tensions from monopole contributions reproduced the full abelian string tensions for beta less than or equal to 2.7
- The photon part had almost vanishing string tensions across all beta values studied

## Context

This work extends the approach of Stack (1992), who showed that monopole contributions alone reproduce the string tension in compact QED, to the case of SU(2) QCD after abelian projection. The results support the 't Hooft conjecture (1981) that abelian monopole condensation is the confinement mechanism in QCD, building on earlier demonstrations of abelian dominance in the MA gauge by Suzuki and Yotsuyanagi (1990) and Kronfeld et al. (1987). The findings enable further studies of finite-temperature systems and extension to SU(3) QCD with or without dynamical quarks.
