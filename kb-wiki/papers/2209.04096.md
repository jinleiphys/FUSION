# Enhanced pion-to-proton ratio at the onset of the QCD phase transition

> The pion-to-proton ratio is identified as a potential signal for a non-equilibrium first-order chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, as the pion multiplicity is directly related to entropy production. To showcase this effect, a non-equilibrium Bjorken expansion starting from realistic initial conditions along a Taub adiabat is used to simulate the entropy production. Different dynamical criteria to determine the final entropy-per-baryon number are investigated and matched to a hadron resonance gas model along the chemical freeze out curve to obtain the final pion and proton numbers. We detect a strong enhancement of their multiplicity ratio at the energies where the system experiences a strong phase transition as compared to a smooth crossover which shows almost no enhancement.

## Key claim
The pion-to-proton multiplicity ratio is enhanced at low beam energies when a non-equilibrium first-order chiral phase transition occurs, compared to a smooth crossover scenario; this enhancement is qualitatively independent of the specific freeze-out criterion used and arises from additional entropy produced by the relaxational dynamics of the chiral order parameter.

## Method
A non-equilibrium Bjorken expansion was coupled to a Langevin equation for the chiral order parameter (sigma field) from a quark-meson model in mean-field approximation. Initial conditions were obtained from the Rankine-Hugoniot-Taub adiabat for low to intermediate beam energies, and the final entropy-per-baryon number was extracted using two freeze-out criteria (constant sigma field value of 70 MeV and vanishing second derivative of sigma with respect to proper time). The final S/A was then matched to two parametrized chemical freeze-out curves (freeze-out curve A from Vovchenko (2015) and freeze-out curve B from Cleymans (2005)) to compute pion and proton multiplicities via a hadron resonance gas model using the Thermal-FIST package.

## Key numbers
- Initial entropy-per-baryon number values of 3.9 and 11.96 were used as the smallest and largest initial conditions from the Taub adiabat.
- For sqrt(s_NN) <= 3.0 GeV, both freeze-out criteria yielded nearly the same final entropy-per-baryon number, with discrepancies of order ~10%.
- Freeze-out curve A parameters: a = 0.157 GeV, b = 0.087 GeV^-1, c = 0.092 GeV^-3.
- Freeze-out curve B parameters: a = 0.166 GeV, b = 0.139 GeV^-1, c = 0.053 GeV^-3.
- For freeze-out curve B without phase transition, the pion-to-proton ratio approached zero near sqrt(s_NN) ~ 2.2 GeV; with phase transition, the lowest ratio was 0.45.
- The quark-meson model parameters used: f_pi = 93 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, lambda^2 = 19.7.

## Context
This work extends the non-equilibrium chiral fluid dynamics approach of Herold (2018) by connecting entropy production from a first-order phase transition to experimentally accessible particle ratios, rather than focusing on higher-order cumulants or correlation functions. It contrasts with lattice QCD results (Karsch 2016, Bazavov 2020) that rule out a phase transition at small baryochemical potential, and with experimental beam energy scan programs at STAR, NA49/61, and HADES that primarily measure cumulants. The results suggest that a sudden increase in the pion-to-proton ratio in a beam energy scan at low sqrt(s_NN) could signal the onset of a first-order chiral phase transition, complementing flow measurements from STAR (2021) that favor a stiff hadronic equation of state at 3 GeV.
