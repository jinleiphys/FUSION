# Soft Electromagnetic Radiations from Heavy Ion Colisions

> The production of low mass dileptons and soft photons from thermalized Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) and hadronic matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions is evaluated. A boost invariant longitudinal and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of the systems created in central collision of lead nuclei at CERN SPS, BNL RHIC, and CERN LHC, and undergoing a first order phase transition to hadronic matter is considered. A large production of low mass ($M<$ 0.3 GeV) dileptons, and soft photons ($p_T<$ 0.4 GeV) is seen to emanate from the bremsstrahlung of quarks and pions. We find an increase by a factor of 2--4 in the low mass dilepton and soft photon yield as we move from SPS to RHIC energies, and an increase by an order of magnitude as we move from SPS to LHC energies. Most of the soft radiations are found to originate from pion driven processes at SPS and RHIC energies, while at the LHC energies the quark and the pion driven processes contribute by a similar amount. The study of the transverse mass distribution is seen to provide interesting details of the evolution. We also find a unique universal behaviour for the ratio of $M^2$ weighted transverse mass distribution for $M=$ 0.1 GeV to that for $M=$ 0.2 and 0.3 GeV, as a function of $M_T$, for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, in the absence of transverse expansion of the system. A deviation from this universal behaviour is seen as a clear indication of the flow.

## Key claim

The production of low-mass dileptons (M < 0.3 GeV) and soft photons (p_T < 0.4 GeV) from bremsstrahlung of quarks and pions in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter is evaluated, with the hedge that the soft photon approximation (SPA) is applied and that Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) suppression is assumed to be marginal for photon energies above 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors use the soft photon approximation (SPA) to compute the rates of soft real photon and dilepton emission from quark matter and hadronic matter, employing kinetic theory with a model Lagrangian for the hadronic strong cross-section and a prescription from Danei (1995) for the QGP elastic cross-section. These rates are then integrated over a boost-invariant, longitudinally and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of the system formed in central Pb-Pb collisions, assuming a first-order phase transition at T = 160 MeV and freeze-out at T = 140 MeV, with initial thermalization at proper time tau_i = 1 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Low-mass dileptons are defined as M < 0.3 GeV; soft photons are defined as p_T < 0.4 GeV.
- The yield of low-mass dileptons and soft photons increases by a factor of 2-4 from SPS to RHIC energies, and by an order of magnitude from SPS to LHC energies.
- The initial proper time for thermalized QGP formation in Pb-Pb collisions is tau_i = 1 fm/c.
- The first-order phase transition temperature is 160 MeV; the freeze-out temperature is 140 MeV.
- Photon energies considered are above 100 MeV to avoid substantial LPM suppression.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of photon and dilepton production from relativistic heavy ion collisions by focusing on the bremsstrahlung contributions from quarks and pions, which had been largely neglected in favor of quasi-particle scattering processes. It corrects discrepancies in earlier soft electromagnetic radiation estimates, as noted by Lichard (1995). The results enable the use of low-energy photons and dileptons as probes of the collective transverse flow in the late stages of the collision, with a universal behavior in the ratio of M^2-weighted transverse mass distributions predicted for SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies in the absence of transverse expansion.
