# Short-range and tensor correlations in the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pn) reaction

> The cross sections for electron induced two-nucleon knockout reactions are evaluated for the example of the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pn)$^{14}$N reaction leading to discrete states in the residual nucleus $^{14}$N. These calculations account for the effects of nucleon-nucleon correlations and include the contributions of two-body meson exchange currents as the pion seagull, pion in flight and the isobar current contribution. The effects of short-range as well as tensor correlations are calculated within the framework of the coupled cluster method employing the Argonne V14 potential as a model for a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The relative importance of correlation effects as compared to the contribution of the meson exchange currents depends on the final state of the residual nucleus. The cross section leading to specific states, like e.g. the ground state of $^{14}$N, is rather sensitive to the details of the correlated wave function.

# Short-range and tensor correlations in the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pn) reaction

## Key claim

The cross section for the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pn)$^{14}$N reaction is sensitive to the details of the correlated two-nucleon wave function, and the relative importance of correlation effects versus meson exchange currents depends on the specific final state of the residual nucleus; a simple Jastrow-type correlation function that neglects tensor correlations and state dependence gives dramatically different results from the coupled-cluster treatment using the Argonne V14 potential.

## Method

The authors evaluated the exclusive $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pn)$^{14}$N cross section in the one-photon exchange approximation using correlated two-body wave functions from the coupled cluster (exp(S)) method in the S2 approximation with the Argonne V14 potential. The nuclear current operator included one-body (convective and spin) currents and two-body meson exchange currents (pion seagull, pion-in-flight, and isobar Delta current), with final-state interactions treated via distorted waves from a complex phenomenological optical potential. Calculations were performed in super-parallel kinematics at energy transfer omega = 215 MeV and momentum transfer q = 316 MeV/c, corresponding to planned MAMI experiments.

## Key numbers

- Energy transfer: omega = 215 MeV; momentum transfer: q = 316 MeV/c; incident electron energy: E0 = 855 MeV; electron scattering angle: theta = 18 degrees
- Oscillator length for the uncorrelated model state: b = 1.72 fm
- Excitation energies predicted by configuration mixing: 1+_2 state at 4.54 MeV (experimental 3.95 MeV), 2+ state at 7.17 MeV (experimental 7.03 MeV)
- The (e,e$'$pn) cross sections are about an order of magnitude larger than corresponding (e,e$'$pp) cross sections
- For the 2+ state, the recoil-momentum distribution maximum occurs at p_B approximately 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to the $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp)$^{14}$C reaction (Giusti et al., 1998; Klasen et al., 1998) to the more challenging pn knockout channel, where tensor correlations and meson exchange currents both contribute significantly. The coupled cluster approach provides state-dependent correlated wave functions that go beyond the simple Jastrow-type correlation functions used in earlier work (Giusti et al., 1997). The results support the experimental program at MAMI (Mainz) for triple coincidence measurements including neutron detection, and suggest that comparing cross sections to different final states of $^{14}$N may help disentangle correlation effects from meson exchange current contributions.
