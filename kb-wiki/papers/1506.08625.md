# Electromagnetic form factors in the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> We discuss the recent experimental results on the ratio bertween the electric and magnetic proton form factors and how they can be described by the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model.

# Electromagnetic form factors in the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), when treated relativistically, can describe the experimentally observed decrease of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio, but the model cannot by itself predict the possible zero in the electric form factor; the quark form factors used to fit the data require that the pointlike relativistic calculation already provides a realistic description.

## Method

The authors used the hCQM with a hypercentral potential (linear plus Coulomb-like) and a hyperfine interaction treated as a perturbation. They boosted the three-quark nucleon states to the Breit frame and calculated the elastic nucleon form factors in a fully relativistic framework, including quark form factors to account for finite constituent quark size. The free parameters in the quark form factors were fitted to four sets of experimental data: the ratio R, the proton magnetic form factor G_M^p, and the neutron electric and magnetic form factors G_E^n and G_M^n.

## Key numbers

- The hypercentral potential parameters are tau = 4.59 and alpha = 1.61 fm^{-2}, with the hyperfine interaction strength set by the N-Delta mass difference.
- The non-relativistic hCQM gives a proton r.m.s. radius of about 0.5 fm.
- The non-relativistic calculation predicts R = 1; adding hyperfine interaction gives R = 0.99.
- The ratio R deviates strongly from 1 for Q^2 >= 1 (GeV/c)^2, with an almost linear decrease and a possible zero at Q^2 approximately 8 (GeV/c)^2.
- The quark size obtained from the fit is not larger than 0.3 fm, consistent with evidence from inelastic proton structure functions showing extended objects of size about 0.2-0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic hCQM calculations (De Sanctis et al., earlier work) and addresses the Jefferson Laboratory data (Puckett et al., Gayou et al., 2000s) showing the proton form factor ratio deviating from unity, contradicting the traditional dipole fit and Rosenbluth plot results. The relativistic hCQM results coincide with dispersion relation calculations from the Mainz group (Hammer et al.). The findings enable further experimental tests at higher Q^2 to confirm the possible zero in the proton electric form factor, which would challenge most theoretical models of proton structure.
