# Astrophysical implications on hyperon couplings and hyperon star properties with relativistic equations of states

> Hyperons are essential constituents in the neutron star interior. The poorly-known hyperonic interaction is a source of uncertainty for studying laboratory hypernuclei and neutron star observations. In this work, we perform Bayesian inference of phenomenological hyperon-nucleon interactions using the tidal-deformability measurement of the GW170817 binary neutron star merger as detected by LIGO/Virgo and the mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0541 and PSR J0740+6620 as detected by NICER. The analysis is based on a set of stiff relativistic neutron-star-matter equation of states with hyperons from the relativistic mean-field theory, naturally fulfilling the causality requirement and empirical nuclear matter properties. We specifically utilize the strong correlation recently deduced between the scalar and vector meson hyperon couplings, imposed by the measured $Λ$ separation energy in single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, and perform four different tests with or without the strong correlation. We find that the laboratory hypernuclear constraint ensures a large enough $Λ$-scalar-meson coupling to match the large vector coupling in hyperon star matter. When adopting the current most probable intervals of hyperon couplings from the joint analysis of laboratory and astrophysical data, we find the maximum mass of hyperon stars is at most $2.176^{+0.085}_{-0.202}M_{\odot}$ ($68\%$ credible interval) from the chosen set of stiff equation of states. The reduction of the stellar radius due to hyperons is quantified based on our analysis and various hyperon star properties are provided.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of hyperon stars is at most \(2.176^{+0.085}_{-0.202}M_{\odot}\) (68% credible interval) when using a set of stiff relativistic mean-field equations of state and the current most probable intervals of hyperon couplings from a joint analysis of laboratory hypernuclear data and astrophysical observations, but this result is conditional on the chosen set of stiff equations of state and the specific Bayesian inference framework.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference of phenomenological hyperon-nucleon interactions using 17 stiff relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state that can support a \(\geq 2.3 M_{\odot}\) pure neutron star. They incorporated the tidal deformability measurement from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger (LIGO/Virgo), mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0541 and PSR J0740+6620 (NICER), and a laboratory constraint from the measured \(\Lambda\) separation energy in single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei that imposes a strong correlation between the scalar (\(R_{\sigma\Lambda}\)) and vector (\(R_{\omega\Lambda}\)) hyperon couplings. Four different tests were performed with and without the hypernuclear constraint to determine posterior probability distributions for the coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hyperon stars: \(2.176^{+0.085}_{-0.202}M_{\odot}\) (68% credible interval) from the joint analysis including NICER, GW170817, and hypernuclear data
- For the NL3\(\omega\rho\) EOS with the joint analysis including hypernuclear data, the most probable intervals are \(R_{\sigma\Lambda}=0.712^{+0.157}_{-0.215}\) and \(R_{\omega\Lambda}=0.778^{+0.121}_{-0.183}\) (68% credible interval)
- The \(\Lambda\) single-particle potential at saturation density is \(U_{\Lambda}(\rho_0)=-30\) MeV, corresponding to \(R_{\sigma\Lambda}\sim0.61\) under SU(6) symmetry
- The scalar couplings for \(\Sigma\) and \(\Xi\) hyperons were fixed at \(R_{\sigma\Sigma}=0.443\) and \(R_{\sigma\Xi}=0.302\), reproducing potentials \(U_{\Sigma}(\rho_0)=+34\) MeV and \(U_{\Xi}(\rho_0)=-14\) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of nuclear matter parameters (Traversi et al. 2020, Ilyas et al. 2022, Miao et al. 2022) by focusing specifically on hypernuclear matter using a set of 18 RMF effective interactions. It builds on the linear relation between \(R_{\sigma\Lambda}\) and \(R_{\omega\Lambda}\) recently fitted from \(\Lambda\) separation energies in hypernuclei (Rong et al. 2021). The results enable improved predictions for hyperon star properties such as mass, radius, and tidal deformability, and provide constraints on hyperonic interactions that can be tested by future neutron star observations and hypernuclear experiments.
