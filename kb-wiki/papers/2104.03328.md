# The Time-reversal Odd Side of a Jet

> We re-examine the jet probes of the nucleon spin and flavor structures. We find for the first time the time-reversal odd (T-odd) component of a jet, conventionally thought to vanish, can survive due to the non-perturbative fragmentation and hadronization effects and could be testable. This additional contribution of a jet will lead to novel jet phenomena relevant for unlocking the access to several spin structures of the nucleon, which were thought to be impossible by using jets. As examples, we show how the T-odd constituent can couple to the proton transversity at the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) and can give rise to the anisotropy in the jet production in $e^+e^-$ annihilations. We expect the T-odd contribution of the jet to have broad applications in high energy nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the time-reversal odd (T-odd) component of a jet, conventionally assumed to vanish due to the time-reversal symmetry of perturbative QCD, can survive because of non-perturbative fragmentation and hadronization effects in the kinematic region where the jet transverse momentum is of order 1 GeV, which is the focus of nucleon spin studies.

## Method
The authors derive a QCD factorization theorem for jet production using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), focusing on the winner-take-all (WTA) jet scheme with radius R. They decompose the jet function matrix element into a conventional T-even part and a T-odd part, and show that the T-odd jet function couples to T-odd proton distributions such as transversity. They demonstrate the feasibility by computing the azimuthal asymmetry in transversely polarized deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and in e+e- annihilation, using Pythia8 simulations with the StringSpinnor plugin for the DIS case and parameterized non-perturbative models for the e+e- case.

## Key numbers
- The relevant kinematic region for T-odd jet effects is q_T < 3 GeV, where jets receive substantial non-perturbative contributions.
- For the e+e- annihilation prediction, the center-of-mass energy is sqrt(s) = sqrt(110) GeV.
- The non-perturbative Sudakov factor parameters used are g_2 = 0.84, g_h = 0.042 GeV^{-2}, Q_0 = 2.4 GeV^2, and g_c = 0.0236 GeV^2.
- The b_max parameter in the b*-prescription is 1.5 GeV^{-1}, with c_0 approximately 1.22.
- For jet charge selection, the fraction of u (anti-d) quark jets with Q_J > 0.25 is 52%, while for d (anti-u) quark jets it is 15%; for Q_J < -0.25, the fractions are 15% and 52% respectively.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet probes of nucleon spin structure, which previously could only access unpolarized TMDs and Sivers functions (Kang 2011, Liu 2018, Arratia 2020), by identifying a T-odd component that enables access to transversity and other T-odd distributions thought impossible with jets (Kang 2020). The mechanism is analogous to the Collins effect (Collins 1992) in semi-inclusive DIS, where final-state interactions induce spin-dependent asymmetries. The T-odd jet function is universal and can be constrained by e+e- annihilation data from BaBar and BELLE, enabling cleaner extractions of transversity at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC) and EicC compared to traditional hadron-based measurements.
