# The Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry Partners of the X(3872)

> We explore the consequences of heavy quark spin symmetry for the charmed meson-antimeson system in a contact-range (or pionless) effective field theory. As a trivial consequence, we theorize the existence of a heavy quark spin symmetry partner of the X(3872), with $J^{PC}=2^{++}$, which we call X(4012) in reference to its predicted mass. If we additionally assume that the X(3915) is a $0^{++}$ heavy spin symmetry partner of the X(3872), we end up predicting a total of six $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ molecular states. We also discuss the error induced by higher order effects such as finite heavy quark mass corrections, pion exchanges and coupled channels, allowing us to estimate the expected theoretical uncertainties in the position of these new states.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of a heavy quark spin symmetry partner of the X(3872) with quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=2^{++}\) and a mass around 4012 MeV, which they call X(4012). This prediction relies on the assumption that the X(3872) is a \(1^{++}\) molecular state and that heavy quark spin symmetry is approximately valid for charm quarks, with the caveat that finite heavy quark mass corrections, pion exchanges, and coupled channel effects introduce theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors construct a contact-range (pionless) effective field theory for the charmed meson-antimeson system, incorporating heavy quark spin symmetry constraints at lowest order. They use a gaussian regulator with cut-off values of 0.5 GeV and 1.0 GeV, and determine the two independent lowest-order counterterms \(C_{0a}\) and \(C_{0b}\) by fitting to the known masses of the X(3872) (assumed as a \(1^{++}\) \(D^*\bar{D}\) bound state) and the X(3915) (assumed as a \(0^{++}\) \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) molecule). They then solve the bound state equation for each \(J^{PC}\) channel to predict the masses of the remaining molecular states, and estimate uncertainties by varying the contact interaction strength by \(\pm 15\%\) to account for expected HQSS violations.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass: \(3871.57 \pm 0.25\) MeV (experimental input)
- X(3915) mass: \(3917.4\) MeV (experimental input, used as \(0^{++}\) \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) state)
- Predicted X(4012) (\(2^{++}\) \(D^*\bar{D}^*\)) mass: \(4012 \pm 3\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, \(4012^{+4}_{-9}\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV
- Predicted \(0^{++}\) \(D\bar{D}\) state (X(3710)) mass: \(3706 \pm 10\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, \(3712^{+13}_{-17}\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV
- Predicted \(1^{+-}\) \(D^*\bar{D}\) state (X(3820)) mass: \(3814 \pm 17\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, \(3819^{+24}_{-27}\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV
- Predicted \(1^{+-}\) \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) state (X(3955)) mass: \(3953 \pm 17\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV, \(3956^{+25}_{-28}\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV
- Binding energy of X(3872) used as input: \(B_X(1^{++}) \simeq 4.2\) MeV
- Binding energy of X(4012) predicted: \(\sim 5\) MeV
- Lowest-order couplings for \(\Lambda = 0.5\) GeV: \(C_{0a} = -3.53\) fm\(^2\), \(C_{0b} = 1.59\) fm\(^2\)
- Lowest-order couplings for \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV: \(C_{0a} = -1.06\) fm\(^2\), \(C_{0b} = 0.27\) fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to heavy meson molecules, building on the X-EFT framework of Fleming et al. (2007) for the X(3872) and the HQSS analysis of the bottom sector by Voloshin (2011) and Mehen (2011). The paper predicts a full multiplet of six \(D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}\) molecular states in the isoscalar charm sector, including the X(4012) as a \(2^{++}\) partner of the X(3872). The results enable experimental searches for these predicted states and provide a framework for testing the molecular interpretation of other XYZ states such as the X(3915) and X(3940).
