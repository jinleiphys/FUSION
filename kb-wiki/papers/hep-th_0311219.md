# Harmonic scaling laws and underlying structures

> Based on the effective field theory philosophy, a universal form of the scaling laws could be easily derived with the scaling anomalies naturally clarified as the decoupling effects of underlying physics. In the novel framework, the conventional renormalization group equations and Callan-Symanzik equations could be reproduced as special cases and a number of important and difficult issues around them could be clarified. The underlying theory point of view could envisage a harmonic scaling law that help to fix the form of the loop amplitudes through anomalies, and the heavy field decoupling can be incorporated in this underlying theory approach in a more unified manner.

# Harmonic scaling laws and underlying structures

## Key claim

Based on the effective field theory philosophy, a universal form of scaling laws can be derived by assuming the existence of underlying structures that make short-distance physics well-defined, with scaling anomalies naturally arising as the decoupling effects of these underlying structures rather than as intrinsic quantum mechanical breakdowns of scale invariance. The conventional renormalization group equations and Callan-Symanzik equations are reproduced as special cases of this more general framework, but their validity is restricted to the decoupling limit where no scale becomes comparable to the underlying scales.

## Method

The author postulates the existence of underlying structures for each effective field theory operator, which become visible at extremely short distances and render loop integrations finite. Using operator and Hilbert state vector formalism, the author derives canonical scaling laws for vertex functions that include contributions from both the effective field theory parameters and the underlying parameters (or their agent constants). The decoupling limit of the underlying structures' canonical scaling contributions is then expanded in terms of effective field theory operators, yielding the anomalous dimensions that appear in the renormalization group and Callan-Symanzik equations. The approach is illustrated on one-loop amplitudes in massless and massive QED.

## Key numbers

- Electron self-energy at one-loop in massless QED: $\Sigma^{(1)}(p,-p) = -i\frac{e^2 p\!\!\!/}{16\pi^2} \ln\frac{-p^2}{\bar{c}^2_{\psi;(1)}}$, with scaling anomaly $(p\cdot\partial_p - 1)\Sigma^{(1)} = -i\frac{e^2}{8\pi^2}p\!\!\!/$
- Vacuum polarization at one-loop in massless QED: $\Pi^{(1)}_{\mu\nu}(p,-p) = i\frac{e^2}{12\pi^2}(g_{\mu\nu}p^2 - p_\mu p_\nu)\ln\frac{-p^2}{\bar{c}_{A;(1)}^2}$, with scaling anomaly $(p\cdot\partial_p - 2)\Pi^{(1)}_{\mu\nu} = i\frac{e^2}{6\pi^2}(p^2 g_{\mu\nu} - p_\mu p_\nu)$
- Anomalous dimensions from one-loop QED: $\delta^{(1)}_\psi = \frac{e^2}{8\pi^2}$ for the electron field, $\delta^{(1)}_A = \frac{e^2}{6\pi^2}$ for the photon field
- Massive vacuum polarization agent constant determined by harmony: $C(m;\bar{c}_A) = -\ln\frac{\bar{c}_A^2}{m^2}$

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory philosophy as presented in the textbook by Weinberg and the renormalization group framework developed by Stueckelberg and Petermann, Gell-Mann and Low, and Callan and Symanzik. The author clarifies that conventional renormalization group equations and Callan-Symanzik equations are prescription-dependent and may fail in nonperturbative contexts, as previously noted by Yang (2002) and in studies of scheme dependence by various authors. The harmony notion introduced here provides a method for determining the unknown agent constants in loop amplitudes through scaling constraints, analogous to how gauge invariance constrains such constants in chiral anomaly calculations by 't Hooft. This framework enables a simplified formulation of renormalization without infinite subtractions, potentially useful for complicated effective field theories such as the Standard Model, particularly in its unstable sector and the CKM matrix.
