# Antiproton-deuteron hydrogenic states in optical models

> By solving the Faddeev equations for the ppn system, we compute the antiproton-deuteron level shifts and widths for the lowest hydrogenic states as well as the corresponding pd scattering lengths and volumes. The pd annihilation densities are obtained and compared to the nuclear density of deuterium. The validity of the Trueman relation for composite particles is studied. The strong part of NN interaction is described by two different optical models, including the pp-nn coupling and n-p mass difference, while for NN several realistic interactions are used.

## Key claim
By solving the Faddeev equations for the coupled \(\bar{p}pn\)-\(\bar{n}nn\) three-body system with realistic interactions, the authors compute antiproton-deuteron level shifts and widths for the lowest hydrogenic states; however, the results for P-waves are strongly model-dependent and can differ even in sign between the two optical models used, indicating that the theoretical uncertainties for those states are large.

## Method
The \(\bar{p}d\) system was treated as a three-body \(\bar{p}pn\)-\(\bar{n}nn\) problem solved via Faddeev equations in configuration space, extended to include \(\bar{p}p\)-\(\bar{n}n\) charge-exchange coupling and the neutron-proton mass difference. The strong part of the \(\bar{N}N\) interaction was described by two optical models (Kohno-Weise and Jülich chiral EFT), while several realistic NN interactions (AV18, INOY, N3LO, and the simpler MT13) were used for the nucleon-nucleon part. The partial-wave expansion included amplitudes up to \(l_x \leq 5, l_y \leq 5\), and the two-dimensional radial functions were expanded on Lagrange-mesh bases.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state S-wave (\(^2S_{1/2}\), n=1) complex energy shift with I-N3LO + KW: \(\Delta E = 2.209 - 1.0509i\) keV; the pointlike Coulomb energy is \(-\epsilon_1^{(0)} = 16.6662\) keV.
- For the same state with I-N3LO + Jülich: \(\Delta E = 2.108 - 1.085i\) keV.
- For the \(^2P_{1/2}\) state (n=2) with I-N3LO + KW: \(\Delta E = -58.5 - 244i\) meV; with I-N3LO + Jülich: \(\Delta E = 18.7 - 329i\) meV (sign change in the real part).
- The Rydberg constant for \(\bar{p}d\) is \(R_y(\bar{p}d) = 16.6662\) keV.
- The deuteron binding energy used is \(E_d = -2.2246\) MeV.
- The neutron-proton mass difference is \(\Delta m = 1.293\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier approximate calculations of \(\bar{p}d\) level shifts by Wycech et al. (1985), Lévai and Tahir (1990), and Yan et al. (2008), which used S-wave deuterons, neglected \(\bar{p}p\)-\(\bar{n}n\) coupling, or employed separable potentials. By solving the full three-body problem with realistic interactions and including the deuteron D-wave and charge-exchange coupling, the authors provide benchmark results for the PUMA project, which aims to use antiprotonic atoms to probe peripheral nucleon densities in short-lived isotopes. The strong model dependence found for P-waves highlights the need for improved \(\bar{N}N\) potentials and better experimental data on protonium P-states.
