# Radiative proton-antiproton annihilation and isospin mixing in protonium

> A detailed analysis of the radiative $p\bar p$ annihilation is made in the framework of a two-step formalism, the $p\bar p$ annihilates into meson channels containing a vector meson with a subsequent conversion into a photon via the vector dominance model (VDM). Both steps are derived from the underlying quark model. First, branching ratios for radiative protonium annihilation are calculated and compared with data. Then, details of the isospin interference are studied for different models of the initial protonium state and also for different kinematical form factors. The isospin interference is shown to be uniquely connected to the $p\bar p - n\bar n $ mixing in the protonium state. Values of the interference terms directly deduced from data are consistent with theoretical expectations, indicating a dominant $p\bar p$ component for the $^1S_0$ and a sizable $n\bar n$ component for the $^3S_1$ protonium state. The analysis is extended to the $p\bar p \to γΦ$ transition, where the large observed branching ratio remains unexplained in the VDM approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin interference terms in radiative protonium annihilation are uniquely connected to the $p\bar p - n\bar n$ mixing in the atomic wave function, with the extracted interference terms from data being consistent with theoretical expectations only when using the preferred kinematical form factor; the large observed branching ratio for $p\bar p \to \gamma\Phi$ remains unexplained within the vector dominance model approach.

## Method

The authors adopt a two-step formalism where $p\bar p$ annihilates into meson channels containing a vector meson ($\rho$ or $\omega$), followed by conversion of the vector meson into a photon via the vector dominance model (VDM), with both steps derived consistently from the A2 quark model. The strong transition amplitudes $p\bar p \to VM$ are calculated using the A2 model with initial state interaction coefficients ${\cal B}(I,J)$ that contain the distortion in the protonium state, and the VDM amplitudes $A_{V\gamma}$ are derived from the quark model with the phase structure determined by the flavor wave functions. Branching ratios for radiative decays from S-wave protonium are expressed in terms of the strong branching ratios $B(VM)$ and the interference terms $cos\beta_J$, which depend solely on the initial state interaction coefficients.

## Key numbers

- $A_{\rho\gamma} = e/f_{\rho} = 0.055$ and $A_{\omega\gamma} = (1/3)A_{\rho\gamma}$ for the VDM amplitudes in the limit $m_V \approx k^0$
- The kinematical form factor constant $A = 1.2\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ from a phenomenological fit to the momentum dependence of multipion final states in $p\bar p$ annihilation
- The range parameter $d_{A2} = 0.12\ \text{fm}^2$ (for KW and DR2 potentials) and $d_{A2} = 0.03\ \text{fm}^2$ (for DR1 potential) adjusted to match ratios of isospin probabilities
- The theoretically expected OZI rule ratio $R(X) = B(N\bar N \to \phi X)/B(N\bar N \to \omega X) = tan^2\alpha \approx 0.001 - 0.003$ for an ideally mixed vector meson nonet

## Context

This work extends the initial analysis by the Crystal Barrel collaboration (Amsler et al., 1993) which showed sizable and almost maximally destructive isospin interference for all radiative channels, and the formalism of Delcourt (1982) for radiative decays. The paper provides a theoretical framework connecting the interference terms to $p\bar p - n\bar n$ mixing in protonium wave functions calculated with three different $N\bar N$ potential models: Kohno-Weise (Kohno and Weise, 1986) and two versions of the Dover-Richard potentials (Dover and Richard, 1980; Richard, 1982). The analysis enables future extraction of the isospin dependence of the $N\bar N$ interaction from improved experimental data on radiative annihilation branching ratios.
