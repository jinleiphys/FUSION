# Vanishing Gamow-Teller Transition Rate for A=14 and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Medium

> The problem of the near vanishing of the Gamow-Teller transition ($GT$) in the A=14 system between the lowest $J=0^+~ T=1$ and $J=1^+~ T=0$ states is revisited. The model space is extended from the valence space $(p^{-2})$ to the valence space plus all 2$\hbar ω$ excitations. The question is addressed as to what features of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in the medium are required to obtain the vanishing $GT$ strength in this extended space. It turns out that a combination of a realistic strength of the tensor force combined with a spin-orbit interaction which is enhanced as compared to the free interaction yields a vanishing $GT$ strength. Such an interaction can be derived from a microscopic meson exchange potential if the enhancement of the small component of the Dirac spinors for the nucleons is taken into account.

# Vanishing Gamow-Teller Transition Rate for A=14 and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Medium

## Key claim
The near-vanishing Gamow-Teller transition strength in the A=14 system can be reproduced in an extended model space (including 2ħω excitations) only when the nucleon-nucleon interaction combines a realistic tensor force with a spin-orbit interaction enhanced by about 50% relative to the free-space value, consistent with relativistic Dirac phenomenology predictions of an effective Dirac mass m_D/m ≈ 2/3.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed for the A=14 system using both a phenomenological two-body interaction with variable spin-orbit (x) and tensor (y) strength parameters, and the Bonn A G-matrix interaction. The model space was extended from the valence p-shell (p^{-2}) to include all 2ħω excitations. Single-particle energies were self-consistently determined from the same interaction used for valence particles, and the Dirac effective mass m_D/m was varied to simulate relativistic medium modifications.

## Key numbers
- With the Bonn A interaction and m_D/m = 0.60 in the large space (0+2)ħω, B(GT) = 0.0018, compared to B(GT) = 2.335 with m_D/m = 1.0 in the same space
- In the small space (0ħω) with Bonn A, B(GT) = 5.294 for m_D/m = 1.0, dropping to 0.0978 for m_D/m = 0.60
- With the phenomenological (x,y) interaction in the large space, B(GT) vanishes near y = 0.75 when x = 1.5 (enhanced spin-orbit), but never vanishes for x = 1.0 regardless of y
- In the small space with x = 1.0, B(GT) vanishes near y = 0.5 (half the full tensor strength)
- With the Nijm II realistic interaction in the large space, B(GT) = 1.795, compared to 3.967 in the small space

## Context
This work revisits the classic problem first analyzed by Inglis (1953) and Jancovici and Talmi (1954), who showed that a tensor interaction is required to obtain the vanishing Gamow-Teller transition in A=14. The present study extends those calculations to include 2ħω excitations and demonstrates that the earlier small-space results are recovered only when the spin-orbit interaction is enhanced, as predicted by the Dirac phenomenology of Serot and Walecka (1986) and the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of Muther, Machleidt, and Brockman (1990). The results support the interpretation that medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, particularly the enhancement of the small component of Dirac spinors (reducing m_D/m to about 2/3), are essential for reproducing this sensitive observable. The work also connects to the debate between Brown and Rho (1990), who argue for weaker tensor interactions in the medium due to meson mass scaling, and the "self-weakening" mechanism proposed by Zamick, Zheng, and Fayache (1995, 1997), where higher-shell admixtures effectively reduce the apparent tensor strength.
