# Λ_{c} enhancement from strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> We propose the enhancement of $Λ_{c}$ as a novel QGP signal in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. Assuming a stable bound diquark state in the strongly coupled QGP near the critical temperature, we argue that the direct two-body collision between a $c$ quark and a $[ud]$ diquark would lead to an enhanced $Λ_{c}$ production in comparison with the normal three-body collision among independent $c$, $u$ and $d$ quarks. In the coalescence model, we find that the $Λ_{c}/D$ yield ratio is enhanced substantially due to the diquark correlation.

## Key claim

Assuming a stable bound diquark state exists in the strongly coupled QGP near the critical temperature, the direct two-body collision between a charm quark and a [ud] diquark would lead to an enhanced Λc production compared to the normal three-body collision among independent c, u, and d quarks; the enhancement is robust with respect to changes in the diquark binding energy and the size of Λc.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model to compute the yield of Λc from two separate channels: three-body coalescence of independent c, u, and d quarks, and two-body coalescence of a c quark with a [ud] diquark. The diquark mass was estimated using a constituent quark model with color-spin interaction, with values ranging from 0.455 GeV (maximum binding) to 0.6 GeV (threshold). The yields were calculated for an expanding fire-cylinder model of central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.5 TeV (LHC), using equilibrium quark numbers at the critical temperature TC = 0.175 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Λc/D0 yield ratio at TC = 0.175 GeV is approximately 0.11 without diquarks in QGP (dashed line)
- Λc/D0 yield ratio increases to approximately 0.44 with diquark mass m[ud] = 0.6 GeV (thin solid line)
- Λc/D0 yield ratio reaches approximately 0.89 with diquark mass m[ud] = 0.455 GeV (bold solid line)
- Diquark mass in vacuum estimated as m[ud] = 0.455 GeV for maximum binding, with binding energy 0.145 GeV
- Equilibrium diquark numbers at RHIC: N[ud] approximately 77 for m[ud] = 0.455 GeV and 44 for m[ud] = 0.6 GeV
- Equilibrium diquark numbers at LHC: N[ud] approximately 208 for m[ud] = 0.455 GeV and 119 for m[ud] = 0.6 GeV
- Charm quark numbers in one unit of midrapidity: Nc approximately 3 at RHIC and 20 at LHC
- Volume at hadronization: VC approximately 1000 fm3 for RHIC and 2700 fm3 for LHC
- Λb/B0 ratios: 0.098 (no diquark), 0.38 (m[ud] = 0.6 GeV), 0.82 (m[ud] = 0.455 GeV)
- Conversion time for Λcπ → ND in hadronic phase: τ approximately 17.8 fm, giving suppression factor e^{-tH/τ} approximately 0.57

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach previously applied to light hadrons and charmed mesons at RHIC (Greco et al., 2003; Fries et al., 2003) to include diquark correlations in the strongly coupled QGP, building on the suggestion by Shuryak (2003) that color non-singlet bound states may exist near TC. The predicted enhancement of Λc/D0 ratio (factor 4-8) provides a novel experimental signal for QGP formation that could be tested at RHIC and LHC, and the analysis can be straightforwardly extended to Λb production. The results contradict the simpler statistical hadronization model prediction of Λc/D0 approximately 0.24 at TC = 0.175 GeV (Lee et al., 2007) and the measured ratio of approximately 0.159 from p+p collisions by SELEX (Kushnirenko et al., 2000).
