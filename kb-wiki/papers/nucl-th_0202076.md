# Interplay of Three-Body Interactions in the EOS of Nuclear Matter

> The equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter has been investigated within Brueckner approach adopting the charge-dependent Argonne $V_{18}$ two-body force plus a microscopic three-body force based on a meson-exchange model. The effects on the equation of state of the individual processes giving rise to the three-body force are explored up to high baryonic density. It is found that the major role is played by the competition between the strongly repulsive $(σ, ω)$ exchange term with virtual nucleon-antinucleon excitation and the large attractive contribution due to $(σ, ω)$ exchange with $N^*(1440)$ resonance excitation. The net result is a repulsive term which shifts the saturation density corresponding to the only two-body force much closer to the empirical value, while keeping constant the saturation energy per particle. The contribution from $(π, ρ)$ exchange 3BF is shown to be attractive and rather small. The analysis of the separate three-body force contributions allows to make a comparison with the prediction of Dirac-Brueckner approach which is supposed to incorporate via the {\it dressed} Dirac spinors the same virtual nucleon-antinucleon excitations as in the present three-body force. The numerical results suggest that the three-body force components missing from the Dirac-Brueckner approach are not negligible, especially at high density. The calculation of the nuclear mean field and the effective mass shows that the three-body force affects to a limited extent such properties.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the net repulsive effect of the full microscopic three-body force (3BF) on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter arises from a competition between the strongly repulsive \((\sigma, \omega)\) exchange term with virtual nucleon-antinucleon excitation and the large attractive contribution from \((\sigma, \omega)\) exchange with \(N^*(1440)\) Roper resonance excitation; the net result shifts the saturation density closer to the empirical value while keeping the saturation energy per particle constant, but the authors note that the three-body force components missing from the Dirac-Brueckner approach are not negligible, especially at high density.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using the charge-dependent Argonne \(V_{18}\) two-body force plus a microscopic three-body force based on a meson-exchange model. The three-body force was reduced to an effective two-body force by averaging over the wave function of the third nucleon, including short-range correlations via the defect function \(\eta(r)\) in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) partial waves. The self-consistent BHF procedure with continuous choice for the auxiliary potential was used to solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation, and the individual contributions from different 3BF diagrams (including \(\sigma,\omega\) exchange with \(N\bar N\) pairs, \(\sigma,\omega\) exchange with Roper resonance, and \(\pi,\rho\) exchange) were computed separately.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density with full 3BF: \(\rho \simeq 0.198\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with energy per nucleon \(E \simeq -15.08\ \text{MeV}\), compared to \(\rho \simeq 0.265\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) from pure \(AV_{18}\) two-body force.
- Incompressibility at saturation: \(K \simeq 207\ \text{MeV}\), in agreement with the empirical value of \(210 \pm 30\ \text{MeV}\).
- The \(2\sigma-(N\bar N)\) 3BF energy shift follows approximately \(\Delta E/A \simeq 2.4 \cdot (\rho/\rho_0)^{8/3}\ \text{MeV}\); without correlations (\(\eta=0\)) the prefactor increases to 3.6.
- The \(2\sigma-(N\bar N)\) contribution and the Dirac-Brueckner relativistic correction differ by at most \(26\%\) at the highest density.
- The effective mass at the Fermi momentum saturates around \(m^*/m \simeq 0.72\) at high density (\(\rho \gtrsim 0.2\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)) when 3BF is included.

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner calculations with microscopic three-body forces by Lejeune, Lombardo, and Mathiot (2000) and by Baldo, Bombaci, and Burgio (1997), now using the updated charge-dependent Argonne \(V_{18}\) two-body potential. The results are compared with the Dirac-Brueckner approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Malfiet (1989), showing that the \(2\sigma-(N\bar N)\) 3BF reproduces the main relativistic correction but that other 3BF components (especially from the Roper resonance) are significant at high density, making the full equation of state much softer than Dirac-Brueckner predictions. The work enables improved modeling of high-density nuclear matter for astrophysical compact objects and heavy-ion collision simulations, and provides a benchmark for comparing phenomenological three-body forces (such as those used in variational calculations by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988)) with microscopic approaches.
