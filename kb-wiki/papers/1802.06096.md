# Rho-meson properties in medium

> Properties of $ρ$-meson in symmetric nuclear matter are investigated in a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM), using the in-medium inputs calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The LFCQM used in this study was already applied for the studies of the electromagnetic properties of $ρ$-meson in vacuum, namely, the charge~$G_0$, magnetic~$G_1$, and quadrupole~$G_2$ form factors, electromagnetic charge radius, and electromagnetic decay constant. We predict that the electromagnetic decay constant, charge radius, and quadrupole moment are enhanced as increasing the nuclear matter density, while the magnetic moment is slightly quenched. Furthermore, we predict that the value $Q^2_{\rm zero}$, which crosses zero of the charge form factor, $G_0(Q^2_{\rm zero})=0$ ($Q^2 = -q^2 > 0$ with $q$ being the four-momentum transfer), decreases as increasing the nuclear matter density.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the electromagnetic decay constant, charge radius, and quadrupole moment of the rho-meson are enhanced with increasing nuclear matter density, while the magnetic moment is slightly quenched; however, the two different treatments of the regulator mass in medium predict opposite density dependence for the electromagnetic decay constant, indicating this quantity requires further investigation.

## Method

The authors combine a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) with in-medium inputs calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which uses the MIT bag model to describe how the internal structure of hadrons is modified in symmetric nuclear matter through self-consistent exchange of scalar-isoscalar sigma and vector-isoscalar omega meson fields coupled directly to the confined light quarks. The LFCQM wave function is obtained from the plus-component of the electromagnetic current in the Drell-Yan condition, using a regulator function to make the loop integrals finite, with the regulator mass chosen to reproduce the experimental rho-meson decay constant in vacuum. Two regularization treatments are compared: one with density-independent regulator mass as in vacuum, and one assuming the same density dependence of the regulator mass as that of the in-medium constituent quark mass.

## Key numbers

- In vacuum (rho_N/rho_0 = 0), the rho-meson electromagnetic decay constant f_rho = 153.627 MeV, charge radius <r_rho^2> = 0.267 fm^2, magnetic moment mu_rho = 2.20 (in units of e/2m_rho), quadrupole moment Q_{2rho} = -0.0590 fm^2, and Q^2_zero = 2.96 GeV^2
- At nuclear matter density rho_N/rho_0 = 0.50, with density-dependent regulator mass: effective quark mass m_q* = 0.333 GeV, effective rho-meson mass m_rho* = 0.618 GeV, charge radius <r_rho*^2> = 0.560 fm^2, decay constant f_rho* = 108.720 MeV, magnetic moment mu_rho* = 2.18, quadrupole moment Q*_{2rho} = -0.0876 fm^2, Q^2_zero = 1.93 GeV^2
- At rho_N/rho_0 = 0.90, with density-dependent regulator mass: effective quark mass m_q* = 0.260 GeV, effective rho-meson mass m_rho* = 0.514 GeV, charge radius <r_rho*^2> = 2.013 fm^2, decay constant f_rho* = 58.696 MeV, magnetic moment mu_rho* = 2.10, quadrupole moment Q*_{2rho} = -0.1128 fm^2, Q^2_zero = 1.34 GeV^2
- The experimental value for the rho^0 to e+e- decay width in vacuum is Gamma_ee = 7.04 +/- 0.06 keV, corresponding to f_rho = 152 +/- 8 MeV
- The binding energy B* = m_q* + m_qbar* - m_rho* becomes negative beyond about rho/rho_0 = 0.90, indicating no bound state beyond this density

## Context

This work extends the authors' preliminary study of rho-meson properties in symmetric nuclear matter (de Melo 2016) by introducing a new regularization treatment where the regulator mass follows the same density dependence as the in-medium constituent quark mass. The approach builds on previous applications of the same LFCQM to study pion (Melo 2014), kaon (Yabusaki 2017), and nucleon (de Araujo 2017) properties in medium, and uses the QMC model originally developed by Guichon (1988) and Frederico (1989). The opposite density dependence predicted for the decay constant by the two regularization methods suggests that a proper in-medium regularization method needs to be developed, and the results should be tested with other models.
