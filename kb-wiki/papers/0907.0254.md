# Charge Symmetry Breaking in the n p --> d pi^0 reaction

> The asymmetry in the angular distribution of n p --> d pi^0 due to Charge Symmetry Breaking is calculated using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. Recent developments in power counting have proven successful in describing total cross sections, and we apply them to the asymmetry calculation. Reducibility in one of the leading order diagrams is examined. We compare the updated theory with experimental results for a range of physically reasonable parameters and find over-prediction for the entire range.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the forward/backward asymmetry in the angular distribution of the reaction \(np\rightarrow d\pi^0\) using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, including recent recoil corrections to the Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex, and find that the theory over-predicts the experimental asymmetry for the entire range of physically reasonable parameters considered.

## Method

The calculation uses the hybrid Weinberg approach, where a two-particle-irreducible operator is computed from HB\(\chi\)PT diagrams and then convolved with phenomenological NN wavefunctions from the Argonne V18 potential. The leading-order strong diagrams (impulse, rescattering with Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex, and Delta) are included along with their \(\nu=1\) recoil corrections, and the reducibility of the impulse diagram is handled by subtracting the static one-pion-exchange contributions already present in the wavefunctions. Charge symmetry breaking is introduced via vertices from the Lagrangian that encode the up-down quark mass difference and electromagnetic effects, with the leading CSB contribution coming from the rescattering diagram with a \(\nu=1\) CSB vertex.

## Key numbers

- The experimental energy corresponds to a lab-frame kinetic energy \(T_L = 279.5\) MeV, slightly above threshold at \(T_L = 275.1\) MeV.
- The dimensionless off-threshold parameter is \(\eta_\mathbf{C} = 0.169\), corresponding to a pion center-of-mass momentum \(q_\mathbf{C} = 22.86\) MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy is \(E_b = 2.224\) MeV.
- The scale for external momenta is \(\tilde{p} \equiv \sqrt{m_\pi M_N} = 356\) MeV.
- The expansion parameter is \(\chi \equiv \tilde{p}/M_N = \sqrt{m_\pi/M_N} = 0.40\).
- The three leading-order strong diagrams alone give a cross section \(\alpha \approx 55\ \mu\)b, while the experimental value near threshold is \(\alpha_{exp} \approx 90\ \mu\)b (Hutcheon 1989).
- The wavefunction correction to the impulse diagram is \(\sim 4\%\) of the total impulse amplitude at the experimental energy.
- For the p-wave observable \(\alpha_2/\alpha_0\), the best fit to data uses a cutoff \(\Lambda = 310\) MeV for the Delta diagram; for the analyzing power \(A_y\), the best fit uses \(\Lambda = 560\) MeV; the authors adopt the geometric mean \(\Lambda = 417\) MeV for displaying results.

## Context

This work extends the power-counting scheme of Lensky et al. (2005), which successfully described the total cross section for \(pp\rightarrow d\pi^+\), to the neutral pion production channel \(np\rightarrow d\pi^0\) and to the asymmetry calculation. It updates the earlier CSB asymmetry calculations of Niskanen (1998) and van Kolck (2000) by including the recoil corrections and the reducibility subtleties. The authors compare their results with the experimental asymmetry from Opper et al. (2003) and with a separate recent calculation by Filin et al. (2009). The over-prediction of the asymmetry indicates that further theoretical work is needed, possibly involving higher-order terms or a more refined treatment of the Delta resonance contributions.
