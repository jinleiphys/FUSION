# Connection of four-dimensional Langevin model and Hauser-Feshbach theory to describe statistical decay of fission fragments

> We developed a method superposing two different fission modes calculated in a four-dimensional Langevin model to obtain more accurate fission fragment yield and total kinetic energy (TKE). The two fission modes correspond to the standard I and standard II modes reported by Brosa et al., and parameters in the Langevin model and the superposing ratio were determined to reproduce the fission fragment yield of $^{240}$Pu of spontaneous fission. We also investigated the fission fragment yields and the TKEs of other Pu isotopes by using the same Langevin parameters and different superposing ratios, such as spontaneous fission of $^{238,242}$Pu and neutron-induced fission of $^{239}$Pu. The prompt fission observables, such as the neutron multiplicity, the prompt fission neutron spectrum, and the independent fission product yield were calculated in the Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay model implemented in a nuclear reaction code TALYS with $^{239}$Pu(n,f) in the incident energies ranging from thermal energy up to 5 MeV. The calculated fission observables qualitatively reproduce several known trends while calculated results have quantitative discrepancies between reported data. Although more improvements are needed for the most important nuclides, it turned out that our approach has the capability to provide prompt fission observables for difficult-to-measure nuclides.

## Key claim

The authors developed a method that superposes two four-dimensional Langevin calculations (corresponding to the standard I and standard II fission modes) to improve the accuracy of fission fragment yields and total kinetic energies, but the approach still shows quantitative discrepancies against experimental data for the most important nuclides, particularly overestimations at specific fragment masses and underestimations of TKE in certain mass regions.

## Method

The four-dimensional Langevin model simulated nuclear deformation from near the ground state up to scission using collective variables (elongation, fragment deformations, mass asymmetry, and a neck parameter) with a macro-microscopic potential. Two separate Langevin calculations were performed with different neck parameters and zero-point energies to represent the standard I (ST1) and standard II (ST2) fission modes, and these were superposed using a ratio ζ determined by fitting to experimental fission fragment yields of 240Pu spontaneous fission. The resulting fission fragment data (yields and average TKE) were then used as input for the Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay model implemented in TALYS to calculate prompt fission observables for 239Pu(n,f) at incident energies from thermal up to 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu(sf), the Langevin calculation gave an average TKE of 179.23 MeV, compared to experimental values of 178.5±0.1 MeV (Demattè et al.) and 179.4±0.1 MeV (Schillebeeckx et al.)
- For 238Pu(sf), the calculated average TKE was 176.47 MeV versus experimental 176.4±0.3 MeV (Demattè et al.) and 177.0±0.3 MeV (Schillebeeckx et al.)
- For 242Pu(sf), the calculated average TKE was 183.01 MeV versus experimental 180.5±0.1 MeV (Demattè et al.) and 180.7±0.1 MeV (Schillebeeckx et al.)
- For 239Pu(nth,f), the calculated average TKE was 176.88 MeV versus experimental 177.93±0.01 MeV (Schillebeeckx et al.)
- The calculated neutron multiplicity at thermal energy for 239Pu(n,f) was 2.874, compared to ENDF-B/VIII.0 (2.870) and JENDL-5 (2.870)
- The ST1 mode used parameters ε=0.65 and ℏωμ=(2,2,2,1); the ST2 mode used ε=0.25 and ℏωμ=(2,2,2,2.7)
- The deviation between calculated and experimental average TKE for Pu isotopes was approximately 2%

## Context

This work extends the four-dimensional Langevin model developed by Ishizuka (2017), Usang (2019), and Ishizuka (2020) by incorporating the superposition of two fission modes inspired by the Brosa model (1990), which classified fission paths into standard I, standard II, superlong, and supershort modes. The approach enables consistent calculation of prompt fission observables (neutron multiplicity, prompt fission neutron spectrum, independent fission product yield) using the Hauser-Feshbach theory in TALYS, building on prior work by Okumura (2022) on the energy-dependent anisothermal parameter RT. The method aims to provide evaluated fission data for difficult-to-measure nuclides, though improvements are still needed for the most important isotopes like 239Pu.
