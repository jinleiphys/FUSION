# New Results on Perturbative Color Transparency in Quasi-Exclusive Electroproduction

> We review the perturbative QCD formalism of hadronic electromagnetic form factors and the color transparency ratio for quasi-exclusive electroproduction of the proton and pion from nuclear targets. We have completed the first full calculations including all leading order quark subprocesses and integrations over distribution amplitudes, including Sudakov effects. For the case of the proton, the calculated result shows scaling beyond $Q^2=10$ GeV$^2$. The calculation incorporating filtering due to the nuclear medium is cleaner than the corresponding calculation in free space because of attenuation of large distance amplitudes. We find that the color transparency ratio is rather insensitive to theoretical uncertainties inherent in the perturbative formalism, such as the choice of the hadron distribution amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the color transparency ratio for quasi-exclusive electroproduction of protons and pions from nuclear targets is rather insensitive to theoretical uncertainties in the perturbative QCD formalism, such as the choice of the hadron distribution amplitude, but that uncertainties in nuclear spectral functions and correlations now dominate the theoretical error.

## Method

The authors performed the first full leading-order perturbative QCD calculations of hadronic electromagnetic form factors and the color transparency ratio, including all quark subprocesses, integrations over distribution amplitudes, and Sudakov effects. They incorporated nuclear filtering by modeling the attenuation of large-distance amplitudes in the nuclear medium using an effective inelastic cross section parametrized as \(k b^2\), where \(b\) is the hadron size, and adjusted the parameter \(k\) to fit experimental data. The calculations used the improved factorization scheme of Li and Sterman, retaining intrinsic transverse momentum dependence in the gluon propagator.

## Key numbers

- For the proton form factor, scaling is observed for \(Q^2\) larger than about 10 GeV\(^2\).
- For the pion transparency ratio using the CZ wave function, results change by less than 3% for \(Q^2\) larger than 10 GeV\(^2\) when comparing different wave functions.
- For the proton transparency ratio, a value of \(k=6\) in the attenuation cross section \(\sigma = k b^2\) gives a reasonable fit to experimental data, corresponding to a typical proton size of about 0.77 fm.
- A reasonable range of \(k\) values for the proton is \(k=5\) and \(k=6\), corresponding to \(b\) values of 0.85 fm and 0.77 fm respectively.
- For the pion, the attenuation cross-section is 25-30 mb for a pion size of about 0.8 fm, using \(k=4\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD formalism of Li and Sterman (1992) for hadronic form factors to nuclear targets, building on earlier ideas of color transparency by Brodsky and Mueller (1988) and nuclear filtering by Jain and Ralston (1992) and Bertsch and collaborators (1988). The calculations show that nuclear medium effects make the color transparency ratio cleaner and less sensitive to hadronic wave function uncertainties than free-space calculations, enabling future precision experiments at facilities like CEBAF and ELFE to discover color transparency by measuring the \(A\) dependence at fixed moderately large \(Q^2\).
