# Parton Interaction Rates and Limits on Perturbative QCD

> The relevance of parton interaction rates for observables of a quark- gluon plasma and for testing field theoretical methods is exhibited. Using the Braaten-Pisarski method the interaction rate as well as the transport rate beyond the leading logarithm approximation are estimated. Limts on the validity of perturbative QCD for relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.

# Parton Interaction Rates and Limits on Perturbative QCD

## Key claim
Using the Braaten-Pisarski resummation method, the elastic parton interaction rate and transport rate in a quark-gluon plasma can be estimated beyond the leading logarithm approximation, but perturbative QCD breaks down for realistic coupling constants (α_s ≳ 0.2) at thermal momenta, as the transport rate becomes negative.

## Method
The author computes elastic parton scattering rates (gg→gg, gq→gq, qq→qq) using the Braaten-Pisarski effective perturbation theory, which replaces bare gluon propagators with resummed propagators containing hard thermal loops. For the ordinary interaction rate, the quark self-energy with an effective gluon propagator is evaluated; for the transport rate, the Braaten-Yuan separation method splits the calculation into soft (q<q*) and hard (q>q*) momentum transfer contributions, using effective and bare propagators respectively, with the separation scale q* dropping out of the final result.

## Key numbers
- Quark interaction rate: Γ_q ≃ 1.3 α_s T
- Gluon interaction rate: Γ_g ≃ 3.0 α_s T
- Gluon thermalization time: τ_g ≃ 0.7 fm/c for α_s = 0.3 and T = 300 MeV
- Quark thermalization time: τ_q ≃ 1.7 fm/c for α_s = 0.3 and T = 300 MeV
- Gluon transport rate (N_f=2): Γ_{g,trans} ≃ 6.6 α_s^2 T ln(0.19/α_s)
- Quark transport rate (N_f=2): Γ_{q,trans} ≃ 2.5 α_s^2 T ln(0.21/α_s)
- Static color conductivity: σ_c ≃ 2.4 T
- Shear viscosity coefficient: η = T^3/α_s^2 [0.11/ln(0.19/α_s) + 0.37/ln(0.21/α_s)]
- Critical coupling for transport rate breakdown: α_s ≳ 0.2 (g ≳ 1.6) for thermal partons

## Context
This work extends the Braaten-Pisarski (1990) resummation method and the Braaten-Yuan (1991) separation technique to compute parton interaction rates beyond the leading logarithm approximation. The results contradict naive perturbation theory, which gives quadratically infrared-divergent rates, and show that simplified approximations using the Debye mass as an infrared regulator (e.g., Shuryak 1980) give similar numerical values. The paper enables estimates of thermalization times, shear viscosity, and color conductivity in the quark-gluon plasma, but establishes that leading-order perturbative QCD is unreliable for α_s ≳ 0.2 at thermal momenta, motivating next-to-leading order calculations.
