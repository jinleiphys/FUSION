# Relativistic nucleon optical potentials with isospin dependence in Dirac Brueckner Hartree-Fock approach

> The relativistic optical model potential (OMP) for nucleon-nucleus scattering is investigated in the framework of Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using the Bonn-B One-Boson- Exchange potential for the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. Both real and imaginary parts of isospin-dependent nucleon self-energies in nuclear medium are derived from the DBHF approach based on the projection techniques within the subtracted T -matrix representation. The Dirac potentials as well as the corresponding Schrodinger equivalent potentials are evaluated. An improved local density approximation is employed in this analysis, where a range parameter is included to account for a finite-range correction of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. As an example the total cross sections, differential elastic scattering cross sections, analyzing powers for n, p + 27Al at incident energy 100 keV < E < 250 MeV are calculated. The results derived from this microscopic approach of the OMP are compared to the experimental data, as well as the results obtained with a phenomenological OMP. A good agreement between the theoretical results and the measurements can be achieved for all incident energies using a constant value for the range parameter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic microscopic optical potential (RMOP) derived from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using the Bonn-B interaction can reproduce experimental nucleon-nucleus scattering data for n, p + 27Al across a wide energy range (100 keV to 250 MeV) with only a single adjustable range parameter (t = 1.4 fm) in the improved local density approximation, achieving agreement comparable to or better than the phenomenological Koning-Delaroche potential.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon self-energies in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using the DBHF approach with the Bonn-B one-boson-exchange potential, employing the subtracted T-matrix representation to resolve ambiguities from positive-energy-state projection. They extracted scalar and vector Dirac potentials from these self-energies and converted them to Schrodinger-equivalent central, spin-orbit, and Darwin potentials. An improved local density approximation with a Gaussian finite-range correction (range parameter t = 1.4 fm) was used to map nuclear-matter potentials onto finite nuclei, and the resulting optical potentials were applied to calculate elastic scattering observables for n, p + 27Al using the APMN code with Hauser-Feshbach compound-nucleus corrections at low energies.

## Key numbers

* Saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter: Fermi momentum k_F = 1.33 fm^-1 with binding energy E/A = -14.71 MeV, compared to empirical values k_F = 1.36 ± 0.06 fm^-1 and E/A = -16 ± 1 MeV.
* The single adjustable range parameter in the improved local density approximation was fixed to t = 1.4 fm for all calculations.
* Calculations covered incident energies from 100 keV to 250 MeV for n, p + 27Al elastic scattering.
* The asymmetry parameters for Al isotopes were taken as β = 0.037 for 27Al, 0.297 for 37Al, and 0.447 for 47Al.
* The real part of Σ_s - Σ_0 in symmetric nuclear matter at k_F = 1.35 fm^-1 is approximately constant at -60 MeV for large energies E, while the imaginary part is essentially constant around -20 MeV at larger energies.

## Context

This work extends the DBHF approach with projection techniques previously developed for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Dalen, Muther, and collaborators (2004, 2007, 2010) to construct a fully microscopic optical potential for finite nuclei. It improves upon earlier semi-phenomenological approaches such as Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean-field models by starting from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction without adjustable parameters for the nuclear interaction itself, and it resolves the failure of non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock to reproduce nuclear matter saturation without three-body forces. The resulting RMOP enables predictions for unstable neutron-rich nuclei like 37Al and 47Al where experimental data are scarce, and the authors indicate that a full parameterization of the potential for convenient use by other research groups will follow in subsequent work.
