# Bayesian analysis of nuclear polarizability corrections to the Lamb shift of muonic H-atoms and He-ions

> The extraction of nuclear charge radii from spectroscopy experiments in muonic atoms is currently limited by the large uncertainties associated with the theoretical evaluation of the nuclear polarizability effects. To facilitate calculations, these polarizability corrections are conventionally expressed as an expansion in a dimensionless parameter $η$, which has been argued in previous literature to hold an approximate value of 0.33 in light-nuclear systems. In this work, we check this claim by doing a Bayesian analysis of the nuclear-polarizability corrections to the Lamb shift in $μ^2$H and $μ^3$H atoms and in $μ^3$He$^+$ and $μ^4$He$^+$ ions at various orders in the $η$-expansion. Our analysis supports the claim that $η\ll 1$ in these systems and finds truncation uncertainties that are similar to the previous estimate, the only exception being the truncation uncertainties in the $μ^3$He$^+$ ion, which are found to be larger.

## Key claim

The paper tests the claim from prior literature that the dimensionless expansion parameter η has an approximate value of 0.33 in light muonic systems; the Bayesian analysis supports that η is indeed much smaller than 1, but finds that the most likely value is approximately 0.15 for most systems, with the exception of μ³He⁺ where the maximum-likelihood value is about 0.35, and there remains a 6% to 27% probability that η could be as large as 0.33 depending on the system and prior choices.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of the nuclear polarizability corrections to the Lamb shift in μ²H, μ³H, μ³He⁺, and μ⁴He⁺, using data from chiral effective field theory calculations with a Hamiltonian including two-body forces at N³LO and three-body forces at N²LO. They modeled the polarizability correction as an expansion in powers of η, assumed the dimensionless expansion coefficients are drawn from a common distribution with a scale parameter, and used Bayes' theorem to derive posterior probability distributions for η and for the truncation uncertainty of the expansion, marginalizing over nuisance parameters with different prior choices (uniform or Gaussian for coefficients, exponential or beta for η).

## Key numbers

- The most likely value of η is approximately 0.15 for μ²H, μ³H, and μ⁴He⁺ systems, and about 0.35 for the μ³He⁺ ion.
- The probability that η exceeds 0.33 ranges from a minimum of 6% (μ⁴He⁺ with prior choices A, α_η) to a maximum of 27% (μ³H with prior choices B, β_η).
- In the combined analysis of all four systems, the maximum-likelihood values of η are approximately 0.22 (prior A) and 0.20 (prior B).
- The 68% confidence interval for truncation uncertainties in μ³He⁺ is roughly a factor of 4 larger than previous estimates from Ji (2018).
- Relativistic corrections to the polarizability are small, amounting to 1.5% in μ²H, 3.7% in μ³H, 3.6% in μ³He⁺, and 6.5% in μ⁴He⁺, as reported in Ji (2018).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear polarizability corrections to muonic Lamb shifts previously calculated by Ji (2018) and others (Pachucki 2011, Friar 2013, Hernandez 2014, Nevo Dinur 2016, Muli 2019), who introduced the η-expansion and estimated η ≈ 0.33 from the uncertainty principle. The Bayesian approach, adapted from methods developed for truncation errors in nuclear effective field theories by Furnstahl (2015), provides a rigorous statistical framework for quantifying uncertainties in the η-expansion. The results enable more reliable extraction of nuclear charge radii from muonic atom spectroscopy, particularly by identifying that truncation uncertainties in μ³He⁺ are larger than previously assumed, motivating further theoretical work on that system.
