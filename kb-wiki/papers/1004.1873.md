# Multi-phonon $γ$-vibrational bands in odd-mass nuclei studied by triaxial projected shell model approach

> Inspired by the recent experimental data (Phys. Lett. B {\bf 675} (2009) 420), we extend the triaxial projected shell model approach to study the $γ$-band structure in odd-mass nuclei. As a first application of the new development, the $γ$-vibrational structure of $^{103}$Nb is investigated. It is demonstrated that the model describes the ground-state band and multi-phonon $γ$-vibrations quite satisfactorily, supporting the interpretation of the data as one of the few experimentally-known examples of simultaneous occurrence of one- and two-$γ$-phonon vibrational bands. This generalizes the well-known concept of the surface $γ$-oscillation in deformed nuclei built on the ground-state in even-even systems to $γ$-bands based on quasiparticle configurations in odd-mass systems.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, extended for the first time to odd-mass nuclei, reproduces the observed yrast and one-phonon γ-bands in ¹⁰³Nb quite well, but predicts a more harmonic γγ-band (with a bandhead energy ratio of 2.01) than the experimentally suggested anharmonic ratio of 1.87, indicating that the model's description of the two-phonon band may require a generator-coordinate treatment of triaxiality to fully match the data.

## Method

The authors extended the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) to odd-mass nuclei by constructing a basis of angular-momentum-projected one-quasiproton and three-quasiparticle (one-proton plus two-neutron) configurations from triaxially deformed Nilsson states. They performed three-dimensional angular-momentum projection and subsequent shell-model diagonalization in the laboratory frame using a quadrupole-quadrupole plus pairing Hamiltonian, with the triaxial deformation parameter ε' = 0.16 chosen to reproduce the experimental γ-bandhead energy. For ¹⁰³Nb, the calculation employed a quadrupole deformation ε = 0.3 and included 44 intrinsic states within a 3.5 MeV energy window around the Fermi surfaces.

## Key numbers

- The γ-bandhead energy is calculated to be 0.72 MeV with ε' = 0.16, compared to the experimental value of 0.73 MeV; with ε' = 0 (axial symmetry) the γ-bandhead is 1.63 MeV.
- The calculated γγ-bandhead is at 1.73 MeV, compared to the experimental γγ-bandhead; the TPSM prediction lies about 200 keV higher than the experimental γγ-band.
- The ratio of γγ- to γ-bandhead energies is E_γγ/E_γ = 2.01 in TPSM, versus the experimental ratio of 1.87.
- The lowest one-quasiproton state has an energy of 0.9812 MeV, with projection from K = 5/2 forming the main component of the ground-state band.
- The lowest three-quasiparticle intrinsic state has an excitation energy of 2.755 MeV, and the three-quasiparticle band crosses the ground-state band at I = 31/2.

## Context

This work extends the TPSM approach, previously applied to even-even nuclei (Sun 2000, Sheikh 2008, Sheikh 2009), to odd-mass systems for the first time, motivated by experimental data from Wang (2009) reporting γ- and γγ-bands in ¹⁰³Nb from spontaneous fission of ²⁵²Cf. The study addresses the long-standing debate between the quasiparticle-phonon nuclear model (Soloviev 1992), which argued that γγ-vibrational bands cannot exist due to Pauli blocking, and the multi-phonon method (Jolos and Piepenbring 1988), which predicted collective two-phonon states; the TPSM approach removes the angular-momentum non-conservation issues of these earlier models. The results enable future generator-coordinate-method calculations using triaxial deformation as a generator coordinate to better describe the observed anharmonicity in the γγ-band.
