# Diffractive mechanisms in $pp \to pp π^{0}$ reaction at high energies

> We present a study of exclusive production of $π^{0}$ meson in proton-proton collisions at high energies. Both diffractive bremsstrahlung (Drell-Hiida-Deck type model), photon-photon, photon-omega and photon-odderon exchange mechanisms are included in the calculation. The $π^{0}$-bremsstrahlung contribution dominates at large (forward, backward) pion rapidities and contributes at small $π^0 p$ invariant mass and could be therefore misinterpreted as the Roper resonance $N^{*}(1440)$. Large cross sections of the order of mb are predicted. We predict strong dependence of the slope in $t$ (squared four-momentum transfer between ingoing and outgoing proton) on the mass of the supplementary excited $π^{0} p$ system. At high energy and midrapidity, the photon-photon contribution dominates over the diffractive components, however, the corresponding cross section is rather small. The photon-odderon and odderon-photon contributions are included in addition and first estimates (upper limits) of their contributions are presented. We suggest a search for the odderon contribution at midrapidity and at $p_{\perp,π^{0}} \sim$ 0.5 GeV. Our predictions are ready for verification at LHC. The bremsstrahlung mechanisms discussed here contribute also to the $pp \to p(n π^{+})$ reaction. Both channels give a sizable contribution to the low-mass single diffractive cross section and must be included in extrapolating the measured experimental single diffractive cross section.

## Key claim

The diffractive bremsstrahlung (Drell-Hiida-Deck) mechanism for exclusive π⁰ production in pp collisions yields large cross sections of order mb, but the photon-odderon and odderon-photon contributions are only upper-limit estimates; the odderon signal may be separable at midrapidity and p⊥,π⁰ ∼ 0.5 GeV, but the corresponding cross section is less than 20 nb in |yπ⁰| < 2.5.

## Method

The authors calculate amplitudes for the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction at high energies including diffractive bremsstrahlung via pomeron exchange (pion-exchange, proton-exchange, and direct-production diagrams), photon-photon fusion, photon-omega (and omega-photon) exchange, and photon-odderon (and odderon-photon) exchange. Absorption effects are incorporated using a non-resonant model with hadronic form factors (cutoff parameters ΛN and Λπ). Differential cross sections in rapidity, transverse momentum, azimuthal angle between outgoing protons, and invariant mass of the π⁰p system are computed for ISR (√s = 45 GeV), RHIC (√s = 500 GeV), and LHC (√s = 14 TeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for the π⁰-bremsstrahlung mechanism are of the order of mb (no single precise number given, but stated as "order of mb").
- At √s = 14 TeV, the photon-odderon contribution yields a total cross section less than 20 nb in the rapidity region |yπ⁰| < 2.5, based on the HERA upper limit σγp→π⁰p < 49 nb.
- The Ewerz-Nachtmann estimate uses σγp→π⁰p = 6 nb for the odderon contribution at √s = 14 TeV.
- Absorptive corrections lower the π⁰-bremsstrahlung cross section by a factor of 2 to 3.
- The hadronic form factor cutoff parameters used are ΛN = Λπ = 1 GeV (with an alternative ΛN = 0.6 GeV, Λπ = 1 GeV for uncertainty estimates).
- The suggested odderon search region is at midrapidity and p⊥,π⁰ ∼ 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Drell-Hiida-Deck mechanism for diffractive πN production in NN collisions (Deck, 1960s) to exclusive π⁰ production at LHC energies, and introduces photon-omega and photon-odderon exchange mechanisms not previously discussed in the literature for this channel. The authors note that the WA102 Collaboration (1998) studied central π⁰ production at √s = 29.1 GeV but eliminated diffractive contributions. The results enable searches for odderon exchange at the LHC using forward detectors (ALFA in ATLAS, TOTEM in CMS) and zero-degree calorimeters, and show that the π⁰-bremsstrahlung mechanism contributes sizably to the low-mass single diffractive cross section, which must be accounted for when extrapolating measured single diffractive cross sections.
