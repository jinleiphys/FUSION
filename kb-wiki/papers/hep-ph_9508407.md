# $η\to π^0 γγ$ to ${\cal{O}}(p)^6$ in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The decay $η\to π^0 γγ$ is discussed in the framework of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. The process is dominated by the ${\cal{O}}(p^6)$ in the momentum expansion where tree-level amplitudes from the effective Lagrangian ${\cal{L}}^6$ enter together with one--loop contributions from ${ \cal{L}}^4$ and two--loop contributions from ${\cal{L}}^2$. We estimate the 6 independent ${\cal{L}}^6$ coupling constants by resonance saturation consistent with the pion production process $γγ\to π^0 π^0$ and calculate the pion--loop part of the one- and two--loop amplitude. Predictions for the total rate and spectrum of $η\to π^0 γγ$ are given together with a discussion of the uncertainties involved.

## Key claim

The decay \(\eta\to \pi^0\gamma\gamma\) is dominated by \({\cal{O}}(p^6)\) contributions in chiral perturbation theory, but a self-consistent quantitative description requires the full set of six independent \({\cal{L}}^6\) coupling constants rather than the reduced set of three used in earlier work; the paper estimates these constants via resonance saturation and finds that attempts with only three constants are troubled by incompatibility of the mass terms.

## Method

The author constructs the most general \({\cal{L}}^6\) Lagrangian for SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, identifying six independent low-energy constants \(d_1,\ldots,d_6\) that contribute to \(\eta\to \pi^0\gamma\gamma\) and \(\gamma\gamma\to \pi^0\pi^0\). These constants are determined by fitting the \({\cal{O}}(p^6)\) low-energy limit of a meson-exchange model (including vector, axial-vector, scalar, and tensor resonances) to the full resonance amplitudes. The pion-loop parts of the one-loop (\({\cal{L}}^4\)) and two-loop (\({\cal{L}}^2\)) contributions are calculated analytically, and the overlapping two-loop diagrams are evaluated using a Feynman-parameter method extended to handle the unstable \(\eta\) mass.

## Key numbers

* Total decay width from the full \({\cal{L}}^6\) tree-level amplitude alone: \(\Gamma^{L^6} = 0.652\) eV.
* Adding the \({\cal{O}}(p^4)\) one-loop amplitude (charged pion and kaon loops) increases the width to \(\Gamma^{L^6+O(p^4)} = 0.733\) eV.
* Including also the \({\cal{L}}^4\) one-loop contributions reduces the width to \(\Gamma^{L^6+O(p^4)+L^4} = 0.673\) eV.
* Adding factorizable two-pion-loop contributions gives \(\Gamma^{L^6+O(p^4)+L^4+{\rm fact.}} = 0.682\) eV.
* The linear combination \(d_1-3d_4 = -3.56\times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) from the \({\cal{O}}(p^6)\) fit (all-order fit: \(-4.81\times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
* The linear combination \(d_2-3d_5 = 9.59\times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) from the \({\cal{O}}(p^6)\) fit (all-order fit: \(12.4\times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
* The \({\cal{O}}(p^4)\) one-loop result for the decay width is \(3.89\times 10^{-3}\) eV (from Bij92), compared to the experimental value \(0.84\pm 0.2\) eV (from Data90).

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of \(\eta\to \pi^0\gamma\gamma\) that used only three \({\cal{L}}^6\) constants (Bij92; Belu95; Belk95) and found inconsistencies when simultaneously describing \(\gamma\gamma\to \pi^0\pi^0\). It builds on the two-loop calculation of \(\gamma\gamma\to \pi^0\pi^0\) by Gas94 and the general \({\cal{L}}^6\) Lagrangian of Sche94. The results enable future experimental measurements of the \(\eta\to \pi^0\gamma\gamma\) spectrum to discriminate between different sets of \({\cal{L}}^6\) coupling constants, and they show that a complete treatment of the two-loop amplitude is necessary for a reliable prediction.
