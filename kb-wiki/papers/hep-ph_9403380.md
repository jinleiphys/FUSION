# Pi- Eta Mixing from QCD Sum Rules

> The $q^2$ dependence of the $π$-$η$ mixing amplitude is examined with the use of QCD sum rules. The linear slope of the mixing function $θ(q^2)$ is found to be much smaller than that for $ρ$-$ω$ mixing. Thus the mixing amplitude is approximately the same in the space-like region as in the time-like one, and one may neglect the $q^2$ dependence of the mixing. A comparison between a hadron-meson, an effective chiral model, and the QCD sum rules approaches is made.

# π-η Mixing from QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim

The π-η mixing amplitude has a much weaker dependence on the squared momentum transfer q² than the ρ-ω mixing amplitude, such that the mixing function θ(q²) changes by only about 5% from q² = m²η to q² = -m²η, making it effectively constant for practical purposes. This conclusion depends on the choice of a Borel window between 1.0 GeV² and 1.5 GeV² and the assumption of SU(3) symmetry for the decay constants fπ = fη.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules with Borel transformations applied to the correlation function of axial vector currents for π⁰ and η⁰, keeping operators up to dimension 6 in the operator product expansion (OPE) and working to first order in αs and α. They extracted the mixing parameters gπ and gη from a system of sum rules that cancel the contributions of higher resonances (b₁(1235) and h₁(1170)) and the continuum, then constructed the mixing function θ(q²) = α q² + β using the relation between the mixing angle and the pole-saturated spectral function.

## Key numbers

- gπ = 114 MeV², gη = 117 MeV², with an estimated error of 10-20%
- α = 3.65 × 10⁻⁴ (dimensionless slope parameter)
- β = 3660 MeV² (intercept parameter)
- θ(q² = m²η) = 3800 MeV², slightly smaller than the 4200 MeV² obtained from charge-symmetry-breaking nuclear forces
- The slope α from QCD sum rules is about three times smaller than the chiral perturbation theory value of 1.08 × 10⁻³
- The ratio α/β from QCD sum rules is 9.45 × 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻², compared to 2.836 × 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻² from chiral perturbation theory and 4.4 × 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻² from the hadronic model

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to ρ-ω mixing by Hatsuda, Henley, Meissner and Krein (1994) to the case of π-η mixing, motivated by the observation of Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (1992) that meson mixing used in nucleon-nucleon charge-symmetry-violating potentials may have significant q² dependence. The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Maltman (1992) and a hadronic model by Piekarewicz (1993); the QCD sum rule slope is substantially smaller than both alternatives, supporting the conclusion that π-η mixing can be treated as approximately q²-independent. This enables simpler treatment of π-η mixing in calculations of charge-symmetry-violating nuclear forces, in contrast to the ρ-ω case where the q² dependence is strong and must be included.
