# Renormalization group and Fermi liquid theory for many-nucleon systems

> We discuss renormalization group approaches to strongly interacting Fermi systems, in the context of Landau's theory of Fermi liquids and functional methods, and their application to neutron matter.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Fermi liquid theory, truncated at the two-quasiparticle interaction level, is systematically controlled even for strongly interacting systems because three-quasiparticle and higher-body interactions are suppressed by powers of the ratio of excitation energy to chemical potential, not by the strength of the underlying interaction.

## Method

The paper develops the microscopic foundation of Fermi liquid theory for neutron matter using the one-body Green's function and the Dyson equation, showing how quasiparticle poles emerge from the spectral function. It then analyzes the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quasiparticle scattering in the zero-sound channel and demonstrates the scaling of n-quasiparticle interactions by expanding the energy variation in powers of the quasiparticle distribution δn, counting powers of Δ/μ where Δ is the excitation energy scale and μ the chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle lifetime scales as 1/τ_p ∼ (p − k_F)^2, while the quasiparticle energy scales linearly as ε_p − μ ∼ (p − k_F), so the condition |ε_p − μ| ≫ 1/τ_p holds for states close to the Fermi surface.
- The effective mass is given by m^*/m = 1 + F_1^s/3, where F_1^s is the dimensionless Landau parameter.
- The specific heat is c_V = (m^* k_F / 3) k_B^2 T.
- The incompressibility is κ = (3 k_F^2 / m^*) (1 + F_0^s).
- The spin susceptibility is χ_m = β^2 N(0)/(1 + F_0^a) for spin-1/2 fermions with magnetic moment β.
- The Pomeranchuk stability criterion is F_l^{s/a} > −(2l+1).
- Three-quasiparticle interactions are suppressed by Δ/μ compared to two-quasiparticle interactions, where Δ is the excitation energy scale and μ the chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the Landau Fermi liquid theory (Landau, 1956-1959) and its microscopic formulation by Migdal (1967) and Brown et al. (1970) to modern renormalization group approaches for nuclear forces. It builds on the RG methods for nuclear forces developed by Bogner, Furnstahl, and Schwenk (2003) and the non-perturbative RG approach for nucleonic matter (Schwenk, Friman, and Brown, 2003). The paper enables systematic calculations of Fermi liquid parameters for neutron matter using RG-evolved interactions, including the spin structure of induced interactions and three-body forces.
