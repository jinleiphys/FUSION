# Lattice theory for nonrelativistic fermions in one spatial dimension

> I derive a loop representation for the canonical and grand-canonical partition functions for an interacting four-component Fermi gas in one spatial dimension and an arbitrary external potential. The representation is free of the "sign problem" irrespective of population imbalance, mass imbalance, and to a degree, sign of the interaction strength. This property is in sharp contrast with the analogous three-dimensional two-component interacting Fermi gas, which exhibits a sign problem in the case of unequal masses, chemical potentials, and repulsive interactions. The one-dimensional system is believed to exhibit many phenomena in common with its three-dimensional counterpart, including an analog of the BCS-BEC crossover, and nonperturbative universal few- and many-body physics at scattering lengths much larger than the range of interaction, making the theory an interesting candidate for numerical study. Positivity of the probability measure for the partition function allows for a mean-field treatment of the model; here, I present such an analysis for the interacting Fermi gas in the SU(4) (unpolarized, mass-symmetric) limit, and demonstrate that there exists a phase in which a continuum limit may be defined.

## Key claim

The paper derives a loop representation for the partition function of a four-component Fermi gas in one spatial dimension that is free of the sign problem for any positive masses, any chemical potentials, and couplings \(g \ge -1\), in contrast to the three-dimensional two-component case which exhibits a sign problem under mass or population imbalance. The sign-problem-free representation holds only after explicitly summing over all space-like fermion bubble configurations, which eliminates the negative signs that would otherwise arise from such bubbles.

## Method

The author discretizes the continuum Euclidean action for a four-component Fermi gas with a four-fermion contact interaction on an \(N_\tau \times N_s\) lattice, then performs a hopping parameter expansion of the grand-canonical partition function. Nonvanishing Grassmann integrations yield configurations of self-avoiding closed fermion loops (wrapping the temporal direction) and space-like fermion bubbles; the bubbles are summed analytically using a recursion relation for the one-dimensional partition function \(z_n(m)\), leaving a representation in terms of bubble-free loop configurations only. The resulting expression is a sum over self-avoiding time-wrapping loops with positive weights for all \(m_\sigma > 0\), any \(\mu_\sigma\), and \(g \ge -1\).

## Key numbers

- The recursion relation for the domain partition function is \(z_n(m) = z_{n-1}(m)(1 + 1/m) - (1/(2m)^2) z_{n-2}(m)\), with \(z_0 = 1\) and \(z_1 = 1 + 1/m\).
- The analytic solution for \(z_n(m)\) is given by \(z_n(m) = \frac{1}{2^{n+1}\sqrt{1+2/m}} \left[ \left(1 + 1/m + \sqrt{1+2/m}\right)^{n+1} - \left(1 + 1/m - \sqrt{1+2/m}\right)^{n+1} \right]\), which is positive for all \(m > 0\) and any \(n \ge 0\).
- The infinite-volume ratio for boundary domains is \(R_\infty^\pm(m) = \left[ \frac{1}{2} \left( 1 + 1/m + \sqrt{1+2/m} \right) \right]^{\pm 1}\).
- The interaction energy expectation value is \(I = \frac{1}{N_\tau} \frac{g}{1+g} \langle \mathcal{B}_\tau(\cap_\sigma c_\sigma) \rangle\).
- The integrated contact density is \(\mathcal{C} = (mg)^2 I / g\) in the SU(4) symmetric limit.

## Context

This work extends the lattice approach of Chen and Kaplan (2003) for nonrelativistic fermions to a four-component one-dimensional system with a four-body interaction, building on the universal few- and many-body physics described by Nishida (2009). The sign-problem-free representation enables future numerical studies of the BCS-BEC crossover analog, the unitary limit at infinite scattering length, and universal Tan relations in one dimension, using local loop-updating schemes or the worm algorithm of Prokof'ev and Svistunov (2001).
