# Importance of core polarization in halo nuclei

> A comment on the importance of core polarization in halo nuclei. I point out that although core polarization is suppressed in neutron halos, it plays an essential role in their binding mechanism.

## Key claim

Core polarization is suppressed in neutron halo nuclei compared to stable systems, but it still plays an essential role in the binding mechanism of these nuclei; the effect is indirect, manifesting through a three-body excitation-deexcitation mechanism where the core is excited by one halo neutron and deexcited by the other.

## Method

The author studied the core polarization effect in a six-body, three-cluster (alpha+n+n) model of ^6He, first using a simple model where the alpha particle was not excitable, then including monopole alpha-particle excitations by allowing several harmonic oscillator size parameters for the alpha particle. The model reproduced experimental alpha+n and n+n phase shifts, and the two-neutron separation energy was computed for each version to isolate the contribution from core excitation.

## Key numbers

- In the simple model without alpha excitation, the two-neutron separation energy of ^6He was 0.64 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.975 MeV.
- Including monopole alpha-particle excitations increased the two-neutron separation energy to 0.74 MeV, a 15% gain over the simpler model.
- The experimental two-neutron separation energy of ^6He (0.975 MeV) was reproduced in a parameter-free model that also included the alpha-particle breakup into two ^3H particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body model studies of halo nuclei, such as those by Lehman (1982, 1984) which underbound A=6 nuclei, and by Kamimura (1991) which found ^11Li to be unbound in a rigorous three-body model. The author's earlier work (Csoto, 1993) showed that including core excitation-deexcitation and core breakup mechanisms can recover the missing binding energy, and the present comment argues that these effects are essential despite being suppressed in neutron halos. The results suggest that similar core polarization effects will play an even larger role in ^11Li because the ^9Li core is much softer than the alpha particle.
