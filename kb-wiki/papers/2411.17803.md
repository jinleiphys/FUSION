# Chiral effective theory of scalar and vector diquarks revisited

> Chiral effective theory of light diquarks is revisited. We construct an effective Lagrangian based on the linear representation of three-flavor chiral symmetry. Here, we focus on the effect of a chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetric term originated from an eight-point quark interaction. From this model, we obtain the mass formulas of scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector diquarks, which also describe the dependence of diquark masses on the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the $U(1)_A$ anomaly. We regard singly heavy baryons as two-body systems composed of one heavy quark and one diquark and then predict the fate of the mass spectrum and the strong decay widths under chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a chiral effective theory for light diquarks that includes a new chiral and U(1)_A symmetric term from an eight-point quark interaction (coefficient μ_0^2), which modifies the mass hierarchy of pseudoscalar diquarks and can eliminate the inverse mass ordering previously predicted when μ_0^2 is sufficiently large.

## Method
The authors build an effective Lagrangian for scalar/pseudoscalar (S/P) and axial-vector/vector (A/V) diquarks based on the linear representation of three-flavor chiral SU(3)_R × SU(3)_L symmetry, including Nambu-Goldstone boson fields. They determine the four Lagrangian parameters for S/P diquarks by fitting to diquark masses extracted from lattice QCD and from the masses of singly heavy baryons (Λ_c, Ξ_c) treated as heavy-quark–diquark two-body systems, with the strange P-diquark mass treated as a variable input. The chiral condensates are then scaled by a parameter x (from 1 in normal vacuum to 0 in the chiral symmetric vacuum) to study the x-dependence of diquark masses and the resulting heavy-baryon spectra and decay widths.

## Key numbers
- Nonstrange S-diquark mass M_ud(0^+) = 725 MeV, taken from lattice QCD simulations (ChinQCD).
- Nonstrange P-diquark mass M_ud(0^-) = 1406 MeV, obtained from a three-body constituent quark model (Yoshida) for the ρ-mode excited Λ_c(1/2^-).
- Strange S-diquark mass M_ns(0^+) = 942 MeV, determined to reproduce the ground-state Ξ_c mass of 2469 MeV.
- The ρ-mode excited Ξ_c mass M_0 ≡ M_ρ(Ξ_c;1/2^-) is varied in the range 2623 MeV to 3279 MeV, corresponding to strange P-diquark masses M_ns(0^-) from 1115 MeV to 1819 MeV.
- The crossing point x_AS where nonstrange A-diquark and S-diquark masses become equal ranges from 0.5661 to 0.8580 across the parameter cases, with x_AS = 0.6747 in Case:N.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous chiral effective theory of diquarks (Kim et al., 2021; Kim et al., 2022) by adding a new μ_0^2 term that is both chiral SU(3)_R × SU(3)_L and U(1)_A symmetric, which was not included in those earlier studies. The paper also corrects an error in the x-dependent mass formulas from those prior papers. The results enable predictions for how the masses of singly heavy baryons and their strong decay widths (e.g., Σ_Q → Λ_Q + π) change under partial chiral symmetry restoration in hot or dense matter, and they clarify the possible normal versus inverse mass ordering of pseudoscalar diquarks.
