# Semi-classical kinetic theory for massive spin-half fermions with leading-order spin effects

> We consider the quantum kinetic-theory description for interacting massive spin-half fermions using the Wigner function formalism. We derive a general kinetic theory description assuming that the spin effects appear at the classical and quantum level. To track the effect of such different contributions we use the semi-classical expansion method to obtain the generalized dynamical equations including spin, analogous to classical Boltzmann equation. This approach can be used to obtain a collision kernel involving local as well as non-local collisions among the microscopic constituent of the system and eventually, a framework of spin hydrodynamics ensuring the conservation of the energy-momentum tensor and total angular momentum tensor.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semi-classical kinetic theory for massive spin-half fermions where spin polarization effects are allowed to appear at both leading (zeroth) and next-to-leading (first) order in the \(\hbar\) expansion of the Wigner function, in contrast to prior work that assumed spin effects arise only at first order in \(\hbar\).

## Method

The authors start from the quantum kinetic equation for the Wigner function of interacting Dirac particles and decompose it into components using the Clifford algebra (scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor). They perform a systematic semi-classical expansion in powers of \(\hbar\) up to second order, derive mass-shell conditions at zeroth and first order, and obtain kinetic equations for the scalar and axial-vector components. The scalar and axial-vector coefficients are then combined into a single distribution function \(\mathfrak{f}(x,k,\mathfrak{s})\) that depends on an additional spin phase-space variable \(\mathfrak{s}^{\mu}\).

## Key numbers

* The mass-shell condition for all leading-order coefficients requires \(k^2 = m^2\), where \(k\) is the kinetic momentum.
* The leading-order pseudo-scalar component is \(\mathcal{P}^{(0)} = 0\).
* The spin measure is defined as \(\int \mathrm{dS}(k) \equiv \frac{1}{\pi}\sqrt{\frac{k^2}{3}} \int d^4\mathfrak{s} \, \delta(\mathfrak{s}\cdot \mathfrak{s}+3) \, \delta(k\cdot \mathfrak{s})\), and satisfies \(\int \mathrm{dS}(k) = 2\).
* The leading-order axial-vector coefficient satisfies the orthogonality condition \(k^{\mu}\mathcal{A}_{\mu}^{(0)} = 0\), while at first order \(k^{\mu}\mathcal{A}_{\mu}^{(1)} = \mathcal{C}_{\mathcal{P}}^{(0)}\) (non-zero in general).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory framework presented in Florkowski (2018) and the approach of Weickgenannt (2020, 2021) by allowing spin polarization to appear at the classical (zeroth-order in \(\hbar\)) level rather than only as a first-order quantum effect. The formalism enables future derivation of quantum kinetic equations with a phase-space extended to spin, and can be used to systematically include local and non-local collisions in the collision kernel for developing a spin hydrodynamic framework.
