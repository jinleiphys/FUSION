# Bayesian analysis of the shear modulus in the neutron-star crust

> The elastic properties of the neutron-star crust are important for the calculations of crustal modes. In particular, the ability of the crust to support shear stresses has been connected to observations of quasi-periodic oscillations and to crust deformations potentially emitting gravitational waves. In this work, we assess the uncertainties in the shear modulus and shear speed in the neutron-star outer and inner crust. To this aim, we performed a Bayesian analysis of the shear properties of the neutron-star crust at zero temperature starting from both a non-informative and a nuclear-physics-informed prior. For the treatment of inhomogeneous matter in the crust, we relied on the one-component plasma approximation, with a (semi-)classical treatment of the ions. We show that the use of a nuclear-physics-informed prior has a non-negligible impact on the prediction of the elastic properties of the crust. The frequency of the fundamental torsional crustal modes we obtain is compatible with the low-frequency range of observed quasi-periodic oscillations, our estimates lying in the interval $\approx 20 - 50$~Hz. Although the different considered priors lead to compatible results, the inclusion of nuclear-physics experimental information in the prior considerably reduces the uncertainties in the prediction of the elastic properties of the crust, potentially constraining the predicted frequency of the crustal modes.

## Key claim

The authors show that including nuclear-physics experimental information in the Bayesian prior considerably reduces the uncertainties in the predicted elastic properties of the neutron-star crust, although both the non-informative and nuclear-physics-informed priors lead to compatible results for the shear modulus and shear speed.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of the shear modulus and shear speed in the neutron-star outer and inner crust at zero temperature, using a compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) in the one-component plasma approximation with a (semi-)classical treatment of the ions. They sampled 17 nuclear-model parameters from both a uniform flat prior and a multivariate Gaussian prior informed by nuclear-structure observables, then applied filters including nuclear-mass fits, chiral effective field theory constraints, causality, thermodynamic stability, neutron-star maximum mass observations, tidal deformability from GW170817, and NICER mass-radius data.

## Key numbers

- The fundamental torsional crustal mode frequency lies in the interval approximately 20-50 Hz, compatible with the low-frequency range of observed quasi-periodic oscillations
- The shear modulus expression used is mu = 0.1194 * n_N (Z e)^2 / a, with a correction factor (1 - u^(5/3) / (2 - 4 u^(1/3) + 3 u)) for finite nuclear size
- The neutron drip density is approximately 2 x 10^(-4) fm^(-3), and the crust-core transition occurs at about 0.5 n_sat, where n_sat is approximately 0.16 fm^(-3)
- The saturation density n_sat is varied between 0.15 and 0.17 fm^(-3) in the parameter sampling
- The symmetry energy E_sym is varied between 26 and 38 MeV, and L_sym between 10 and 80 MeV
- The effective mass m_sat^star/m is varied between 0.6 and 1.2, and the effective mass isosplit Delta m_sat^star/m between 0.0 and 2.0

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the shear modulus in neutron-star crusts, particularly those by Strohmayer (1991) and Ogata (1990) for bcc Coulomb solids, and by Zemlyakov (2023) for finite-size corrections. It also builds on the CLDM approach of Baym (1971) and the meta-model of Margueron (2018), and uses the posterior distributions from Klausner (2025) for the nuclear-physics-informed prior. The results enable more reliable predictions of torsional crustal mode frequencies for comparison with quasi-periodic oscillation observations from magnetar giant flares, and can inform models of crustal deformations that may emit gravitational waves.
