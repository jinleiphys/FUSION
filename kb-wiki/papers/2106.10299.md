# The photon PDF within the CT18 global analysis

> Building upon the most recent CT18 global fit, we present a new calculation of the photon content of the proton based on an application of the LUX formalism. In this work, we explore two principal variations of the LUX ansatz. In one approach, which we designate "CT18lux," the photon PDF is calculated directly using the LUX formula for all scales, $μ$. In an alternative realization, "CT18qed," we instead initialize the photon PDF in terms of the LUX formulation at a lower scale, $μ\! \sim\! μ_0$, and evolve to higher scales with a combined QED+QCD kernel at $\mathcal{O}(α),~\mathcal{O}(αα_s)$ and $\mathcal{O}(α^2)$. While we find these two approaches generally agree, especially at intermediate $x$ ($10^{-3}\lesssim x\lesssim0.3$), we discuss some moderate discrepancies that can occur toward the end-point regions at very high or low $x$. We also study effects that follow from variations of the inputs to the LUX calculation originating outside the pure deeply-inelastic scattering (DIS) region, including from elastic form factors and other contributions to the photon PDF. Finally, we investigate the phenomenological implications of these photon PDFs for the LHC, including high-mass Drell-Yan, vector-boson pair, top-quark pair, and Higgs associated with vector-boson production.

## Key claim

The paper presents two new photon parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the proton, CT18lux and CT18qed, built on the CT18 global analysis framework using the LUX formalism, finding general agreement between the two approaches at intermediate momentum fraction x (10^{-3} ≲ x ≲ 0.3) but noting moderate discrepancies at very high or low x that depend on how the photon PDF is initialized and evolved.

## Method

The authors implemented two variations of the LUX formalism within the CT18 NNLO global analysis. In the "CT18lux" approach, the photon PDF is calculated directly using the LUX master formula at all scales μ, with quark and gluon PDFs taken unchanged from the CT18 NNLO fit. In the "CT18qed" approach, the photon PDF is initialized using the LUX formulation at a low starting scale μ ∼ μ₀ and evolved to higher scales using combined QED+QCD DGLAP evolution kernels at O(α), O(αα_s), and O(α²). The elastic and inelastic components of the photon PDF were treated separately, with the elastic contribution determined through proton electromagnetic form factors and the inelastic contribution through proton structure functions F₂ and F_L.

## Key numbers

- The photon momentum fraction carried by the proton is about 0.22% at μ = 1.3 GeV and grows to about 0.65% at μ = 1 TeV
- The two approaches (CT18lux and CT18qed) agree especially at intermediate x values in the range 10⁻³ ≲ x ≲ 0.3
- The photon PDF uncertainty from quark and gluon PDF variations is at the 1-2% level over a wide range of x, consistent with the LUXqed17 and MMHT2015qed determinations
- The CT18lux PDF set contains 1 central set and 58 Hessian error sets derived from the CT18 NNLO PDFs

## Context

This work extends the CT14QED analysis (Schmidt, 2015) by applying the LUX formalism (Manohar, 2016; Manohar, 2017) within the CT18 global fit framework (Hou, 2019). It builds on prior photon PDF determinations including LUXqed17, NNPDF3.1luxQED (Bertone, 2017), and MMHT2015qed (Harland-Lang, 2019), while introducing two distinct implementation strategies. The results enable more precise predictions for LHC processes sensitive to electroweak corrections, including high-mass Drell-Yan, vector-boson pair, top-quark pair, and Higgs associated with vector-boson production.
