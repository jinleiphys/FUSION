> A carefully motivated symmetric variant of the Poisson bracket in ordinary (not Grassmann) phase space variables is shown to satisfy identities which are in algebraic correspondence with the anticommutation postulates for quantized Fermion systems. # Non-Grassmann "Classicization" of Fermion Dynamics

## Key claim

A symmetric variant of the Poisson bracket defined in ordinary (non-Grassmann) phase space variables satisfies identities algebraically corresponding to the anticommutation postulates for quantized Fermion systems, but only when the generating functions are constrained to be constant or linear in each of the complex phase space variables, which forces the resulting "classical" Fermion dynamics to be linear and of Schroedinger type (e.g., the Dirac equation but not the Klein-Gordon or Maxwell equations).

## Method

The author constructs a "symmetric Poisson bracket" from the ordered Poisson bracket in complex dimensionless phase space variables a and a*, defined with the explicit requirement that hbar is nonzero. The symmetric Poisson bracket relations among the phase space components are shown to algebraically correspond to the anticommutation relations of Fermion creation and annihilation operators. Infinitesimal canonical transformations are then required to preserve these symmetric Poisson bracket relations, which imposes constraints on the second derivatives of the generating functions, forcing them to be at most linear in a and a*.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric Poisson bracket relations for the complex phase space components are: {a_i, a_j}_+ = 0 = {a_i*, a_j*}_+, and {a_i, a_j*}_+ = delta_{ij} = +{a_j*, a_i}_+ (Eq. 6).
- The constraint on Fermion system generating functions is: partial^2 G / (partial a_i partial a_j) = 0 = partial^2 G / (partial a_i* partial a_j*) (Eq. 12).
- For the discretized Klein-Gordon Hamiltonian in one spatial dimension, the second derivative q_s^2 partial^2 H / (partial q_i partial q_{i+2}) = -(c q_s/(2 Delta x))^2 is nonzero while partial^2 H / (partial p_i partial p_{i+2}) = 0, violating the constraint (Eq. 18).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Casalbuoni (1976) and Casalbuoni (1976) who attempted to "classicize" Fermion dynamics using Grassmann phase space variables, which the author argues are physically mismotivated by the hbar to 0 limit and theoretically ambiguous. The paper instead uses true c-number phase space variables and follows the symplectic geometry framework of Guillemin and Sternberg (1984) to derive the structure of "classical" Fermion dynamics. The result enables a non-Grassmann formulation of Fermion dynamics that excludes nonlinear theories such as the old four-Fermion theory of beta decay and non-Schroedinger-type equations such as Klein-Gordon and Maxwell.
