# Symplectic Effective Field Theory for Nuclear Structure Studies

> A Symplectic Effective Field Theory that unveils the observed emergence of symplectic symmetry in atomic nuclei is advanced. Specifically, starting from a simple extension of the harmonic-oscillator Lagrangian, an effective field theory applied against symplectic basis states is shown to yield a Hamiltonian system with one fitted parameter. The scale of the system can be determined self consistently as the ratio of the average volume of a nucleus assumed to be spherical to its volume as determined by the average number of oscillator quanta, which is stretched by the fact that the plane-wave solution satisfies the equations of motion at every order without the need for perturbative corrections. As an application of the theory, results for 20Ne, 22Ne and 22Mg are presented that yield energy spectra, B(E2) values, and matter radii in good agreement with experimentally measured results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that symplectic symmetry in atomic nuclei emerges naturally from a simple extension of the harmonic-oscillator Lagrangian, yielding a Hamiltonian system with only one fitted parameter, provided the coupling coefficient satisfies a specific condition that allows plane-wave solutions to satisfy the equations of motion at every order without perturbative corrections.

## Method

The authors construct a non-renormalizable effective field theory by extending the harmonic-oscillator Lagrangian density to n-th order with a mass term at each order, then discretize the fields into localized plane waves with periodic boundary conditions. They express the resulting Hamiltonian density in terms of symplectic Sp(3,R) generators, apply diagonal and off-diagonal coupling schemes to the spatial integrals, and reduce the system to a single energy mode at ℏΩ = 41𝒜⁻¹/³ MeV. The theory is applied to symplectic basis states to produce a quantum mechanical Hamiltonian for nuclear structure calculations.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator energy scale is set to ℏΩ = 41𝒜⁻¹/³ MeV, where 𝒜 is the mass number.
- The coupling coefficient α is constrained to scale as α ∼ 1/Nₐᵥ³/², where Nₐᵥ = √(N_f N_i) is the geometric average of boson numbers between initial and final Fock states.
- For ²⁰Ne, ²²Ne, and ²²Mg, the theory yields energy spectra, B(E2) values, and matter radii in good agreement with experimentally measured results (no specific numerical values for these observables are given in the truncated text).
- Single symplectic irreducible representations recover approximately 70%–80% of the probability distribution and account for nearly 90%–100% of observables such as energy spectra, B(E2) values, and radii.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological Sp(3,R) symplectic model, previously applied to nuclei from ¹⁶O (PhysRevLett.97.202501, 2006) through ²³⁸U (CASTANOS1991469, 1991), by providing a first-principles effective field theory foundation. It builds on results from the ab initio Symmetry-adapted No-Core Shell Model (SA-NCSM) (LAUNEY2016101, 2016; PhysRevLett.124.042501, 2020) which suggested symplectic symmetry arises naturally from realistic chiral interactions. The theory enables systematic nuclear structure calculations using symplectic basis states with a single fitted parameter, potentially connecting to continuum Schwinger function method results (Proceedings:2020fyd, 2020; Barabanov:2020jvn, 2020) that describe how the strong interaction emerges from QCD.
