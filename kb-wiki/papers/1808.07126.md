# Medium polarization effects in 3SD1 spin-triplet pairing

> Stimulated by the still puzzling competition between spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing in nuclei, the 3SD1 neutron-proton pairing is investigated in the framework of BCS theory of nuclear matter. The medium polarization effects are included in the single particle spectrum and also in the pairing interaction starting from the G-matrix, calculated in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. The vertex corrections due to spin and isospin collective excitations of the medium are determined from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the RPA limit, taking into account the tensor correlations. It is found that the self-energy corrections confine the superfluid state to very low-density, while remarkably quenching the magnitude of the energy gap, while the induced interaction has an attractive effect. The interplay between spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing is discussed in nuclear matter as well as in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors find that medium polarization effects, specifically self-energy corrections from the single-particle spectrum and Fermi surface depletion, dramatically quench the \(^3SD_1\) spin-triplet neutron-proton pairing gap and confine superfluidity to very low densities; however, the induced interaction from collective excitations is attractive and partially restores the gap magnitude, though the peak remains at low density, suggesting spin-triplet pairing is a surface phenomenon in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation for symmetric nuclear matter in the \(^3SD_1\) channel, using the Argonne AV18 two-body interaction plus a consistent three-body force. The bare interaction is replaced by the Brueckner \(G\)-matrix in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation to handle short-range correlations. Medium polarization effects are included via: (1) self-energy corrections to the quasi-particle spectrum using the effective mass approximation and Z-factors for Fermi surface depletion; and (2) vertex corrections to the pairing interaction from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the RPA (Landau) limit, which yields an induced interaction expressed in terms of Landau-Migdal parameters including tensor terms.

## Key numbers

* The \(^3SD_1\) energy gap with only the bare interaction peaks at approximately 12 MeV, compared to about 3 MeV for the \(^1S_0\) spin-singlet pairing.
* After including self-energy corrections (effective mass and Z-factor), the \(^3SD_1\) peak gap remains over two times larger than the \(^1S_0\) gap with the same self-energy approximation.
* The p-p induced interaction matrix element in the \(^3SD_1\) channel at density \(\rho = 0.035\) fm\(^{-3}\) (\(k_F = 0.80\) fm\(^{-1}\)) is \(-22.32\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) for the SS component, \(-0.10\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) for the SD component, and 0.00 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) for the DD component.
* For N=Z nuclei, the LDA-averaged gap with screening is 3.54 MeV for A=40, 3.75 MeV for A=100, and 4.00 MeV for A=200; without screening the corresponding values are 6.82 MeV, 8.18 MeV, and 9.38 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior BCS calculations of spin-triplet pairing in nuclear matter (Lombardo, Schulze) by including medium polarization effects that were previously studied only for spin-singlet pairing (Cao 2006, Zhang). The self-energy corrections follow the approach of Cugnon and others, while the induced interaction is derived from the RPA version of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Landau limit, following the formalism of Friman and Dickhoff. The results support the finding of Bertsch (2010) that spin-triplet pairing may dominate in heavy N=Z nuclei (A>140), but suggest that even for lighter nuclei the intrinsic pairing strength in the \(^3SD_1\) channel is larger than in the \(^1S_0\) channel, motivating future studies of competition with spin-orbit splitting in finite nuclei using tools such as those of Idini.
