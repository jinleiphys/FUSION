# Application of information entropy to nuclei

> Shannon's information entropies in position- and momentum- space and their sum $S$ are calculated for various $s$-$p$ and $s$-$d$ shell nuclei using a correlated one-body density matrix depending on the harmonic oscillator size $b_0$ and the short range correlation parameter $y$ which originates from a Jastrow correlation function. It is found that the information entropy sum for a nucleus depends only on the correlation parameter $y$ through the simple relation $S= s_{0A} + s_{1A} y^{-λ_{sA}}$, where $s_{0A}$, $s_{1A}$ and $λ_{sA}$ depend on the mass number $A$. A similar approximate expression is also valid for the root mean square radius of the nucleus as function of $y$ leading to an approximate expression which connects $S$ with the root mean square radius. Finally, we propose a method to determine the correlation parameter from the above property of $S$ as well as the linear dependence of $S$ on the logarithm of the number of nucleons.

# Application of Information Entropy to Nuclei

## Key claim

Shannon's information entropy sum S for s-p and s-d shell nuclei depends only on the short-range correlation parameter y (not on the harmonic oscillator size b0) through the relation S = s0A + s1A * y^(-λsA), where the coefficients depend on mass number A. This allows determination of the correlation parameter from entropy without fitting to experimental charge form factors for each nucleus individually, requiring only experimental data from two reference nuclei (⁴He and ⁴⁰Ca).

## Method

The author calculated correlated one-body density matrices for N=Z s-p and s-d shell nuclei (⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ²⁴Mg, ²⁸Si, ³²S, ³⁶Ar, ⁴⁰Ca) using the factor cluster expansion of Clark and co-workers with a Jastrow correlation function f(r)=1-exp[-y r_b²]. The position-space density ρ(r) and momentum-space density n(k) were computed from the correlated one-body density matrix, and Shannon information entropies Sr and Sk were evaluated numerically. The entropy sum S = Sr + Sk was fitted to a power-law function of 1/y, and a similar fit was performed for the root-mean-square radius. The correlation parameter y for each nucleus was then determined by combining the entropy-sum relation with the linear dependence of S on ln(A), using ⁴He and ⁴⁰Ca as calibration nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The information entropy sum S for all studied nuclei depends only on the correlation parameter y through S = s0A + s1A * y^(-λsA), with nucleus-specific coefficients s0A, s1A, and λsA given in Table I of the paper.
- For ⁴He and ⁴⁰Ca, the fitted parameters a and b in S = a + b ln A are a = 5.4029 and b = 0.9360, compared to a = 5.325 and b = 0.858 found previously with SkIII interaction (Massen 1999).
- The correlation parameter y values for closed-shell nuclei are systematically smaller (stronger correlations) than for neighboring open-shell nuclei; for example, y = 7.7658 for ³⁶Ar when treated as a 1d-2s shell nucleus versus smaller values for closed-shell ⁴⁰Ca.
- Introduction of short-range correlations increases the information entropy sum by 3% to 5% compared to the harmonic oscillator case (y = ∞).
- The lower bound for the entropy sum from the entropic uncertainty relation is S ≥ 3(1 + ln π) ≈ 6.434 (in units where ħ = 1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Massen and collaborators (Panos 1997, Massen 1998, Massen 2002) that found a linear dependence of S on ln(N) for nucleon densities in nuclei, valence electron densities in atomic clusters, and trapped Bose atoms. It also builds on the correlated density matrix formalism developed by Moustakidis (2000, 2001) using the factor cluster expansion of Ristig, Ter Low, and Clark (1970). The method enables determination of the short-range correlation parameter from both density and momentum distributions simultaneously, whereas that parameter is usually determined only from the density distribution. The approach also allows prediction of charge form factors for nuclei without experimental form factor data (as demonstrated for ³⁶Ar), requiring only experimental charge RMS radii and calibration from two reference nuclei.
