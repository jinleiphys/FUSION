# Using Baryonic Charge Balance Functions to Resolve Questions about the Baryo-Chemistry of the QGP

> Baryon annihilations during the hadronic stage of heavy-ion collisions affects final-state baryon and antibaryon yields and final-state correlations of baryons and antibaryons. Understanding annihilation is important for addressing questions about the chemistry at the beginning of the hadronic stage, and for interpreting charge-balance correlations involving baryons. Here, charge balance functions, using protons and antiprotons binned by relative momentum, rapidity and azimuthal angle, are shown to clarify the amount of annihilation in the hadronic stage. This enables a more accurate extraction of the baryo-chemistry at the beginning of the hadronic stage. Understanding annihilation is also crucial if charge balance correlations are to be used to infer the chemistry of the earliest stages of a heavy-ion collision. Calculations are presented based on microscopic simulations of the hadronic stage coupled to a hydrodynamic description of the earlier stage, along with a detailed modeling of correlations of protons and antiprotons, known as charge-balance functions.

## Key claim

Charge balance functions (BFs) binned by relative invariant momentum \(q_{\rm inv}\) can unambiguously quantify the amount of baryon annihilation in the hadronic stage of heavy-ion collisions, because the large annihilation cross section at low relative momentum produces a dip in the BF at \(q_{\rm inv} \lesssim 100\) MeV/\(c\) that is separable from other physics. This enables a more accurate extraction of the baryo-chemistry at the beginning of the hadronic stage, but the model calculations have significant uncertainty due to the phenomenological annihilation cross section being motivated by \(p\bar{p}\) data and possibly differing for other species.

## Method

The authors coupled a hydrodynamic description of the earlier stage (for 0-5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV) to a microscopic hadronic cascade simulation. Two-particle charge-charge correlations were sourced and propagated assuming local chemical equilibrium according to charge susceptibilities from lattice calculations, then projected onto hadronic degrees of freedom at the hadronization temperature \(T_H = 155\) MeV; these correlations (type-I) were combined with correlations sourced during the cascade (type-II), which included baryon annihilation and regeneration. Annihilation was implemented using a parameterized cross section \(\sigma_{\rm A} = 67 / P_{\rm lab}^{0.7}\) mb, and regeneration was accounted for by reducing the annihilation rate by a suppression factor \([1 - S_\mu(\vec{\mu},\vec{T}) S_T(\vec{\mu},\vec{T})]\) derived from effective chemical potentials and temperatures extracted from the cascade.

## Key numbers

- The annihilation cross section parameterization: \(\sigma_{\rm A} = 67 / P_{\rm lab}^{0.7}\) mb, where \(P_{\rm lab}\) is the momentum of a baryon in the rest frame of the other baryon in GeV/\(c\); for \(P_{\rm lab} < 200\) MeV/\(c\), cross sections exceed 200 mb.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, approximately 18% of baryons were annihilated if regeneration was neglected; after incorporating suppression factors, roughly 12% of baryons were annihilated during the cascade.
- The dip in the BF from annihilation is expected for \(q_{\rm inv} \lesssim 100\) MeV/\(c\).
- The hadronization temperature used was \(T_c = 155\) MeV.
- The calculation used 9600 cascade events per case, with cyclic boundary conditions at spatial rapidities \(\eta_{\rm min,max} = \mp 5\).
- The \(p/\pi\) ratio reported by ALICE falls by approximately 15-20% from semi-central to the most central collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of charge balance functions by Pratt et al. (2021) that omitted annihilation, and addresses the role of baryon annihilation in the hadronic stage, which had been estimated to vary from approximately 10% to 30% in models by Rapp (2000), Steinheimer (2012), Pan (2014), and Seifert (2017). The results enable more confident interpretation of proton-antiproton BFs binned by relative rapidity and azimuthal angle, which are used to infer the chemical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma and to extract the light-quark diffusivity from experiment. The authors note that final-state interactions distort BFs only for \(q_{\rm inv} < 50\) MeV/\(c\) (Pratt, 2022), making the annihilation contribution separable.
