# A neutrinoless double beta decay master formula from effective field theory

> We present a master formula describing the neutrinoless-double-beta decay ($0νββ$) rate induced by lepton-number-violating (LNV) operators up to dimension nine in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory. We provide an end-to-end framework connecting the possibly very high LNV scale to the nuclear scale, through a chain of effective field theories. Starting at the electroweak scale, we integrate out the heavy Standard Model degrees of freedom and we match to an $SU(3)_c\otimes U(1)_{\mathrm{em}}$ effective theory. After evolving the resulting effective Lagrangian to the QCD scale, we use chiral perturbation theory to derive the lepton-number-violating chiral Lagrangian. The chiral Lagrangian is used to derive the two-nucleon $0νββ$ transition operators to leading order in the chiral power counting. Based on renormalization arguments we show that in various cases short-range two-nucleon operators need to be enhanced to leading order. We show that all required nuclear matrix elements can be taken from existing calculations. Our final result is a master formula that describes the $0νββ$ rate in terms of phase-space factors, nuclear matrix elements, hadronic low-energy constants, QCD evolution factors, and high-energy LNV Wilson coefficients, including all the interference terms. Our master formula can be easily matched to any model where LNV originates at energy scales above the electroweak scale. As an explicit example, we match our formula to the minimal left-right-symmetric model in which contributions of operators of different dimension compete, and we discuss the resulting phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper presents a master formula for the neutrinoless double beta decay rate that connects lepton-number-violating operators up to dimension nine in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory to nuclear observables, but the derivation assumes that the lepton-number-violating scale is well above the electroweak scale (Λ ≫ m_W), and the chiral power counting has not been tested in the large nuclei of experimental interest.

## Method

The authors construct a chain of effective field theories starting from SU(3)_c × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y-invariant ΔL=2 operators at the electroweak scale, matching to an SU(3)_c × U(1)_em effective theory after electroweak symmetry breaking, evolving the resulting Lagrangian to the QCD scale via one-loop renormalization group equations, and then using chiral perturbation theory to derive the lepton-number-violating chiral Lagrangian. From this chiral Lagrangian, they derive the two-nucleon 0νββ transition operators at leading order in the chiral power counting, incorporating short-range two-nucleon operators that are enhanced to leading order based on renormalization arguments.

## Key numbers

* Current experimental limit: T^{0ν}_{1/2} > 1.07 × 10^{26} yr for ¹²⁶Xe (KamLAND-Zen, 2016)
* Higgs vacuum expectation value: v ≃ 246 GeV
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV
* The dimension-five operator scales as Λ⁻¹, dimension-seven as Λ⁻³, and dimension-nine as Λ⁻⁵, where Λ is the lepton-number-violation scale
* For left-right symmetric models, the dimension-five operator scales as y²/Λ with y ∼ m_e/v ∼ 10⁻⁶, making dimension-seven and -nine operators competitive for Λ in the 1-10 TeV range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective field theory framework (Cirigliano, 2017; Cirigliano, 2018) which was limited to dimension-five and -seven lepton-number-violating operators, now including dimension-nine operators. The framework generalizes and extends earlier work by Prezeau (2003) and Graesser (2016), and disagrees with the master formula of Pas (2000) which missed important hadronic physics, particularly couplings to ππ and πN, and underestimated certain four-nucleon operator low-energy constants by O(16π²). The master formula enables model-independent connections between any high-energy lepton-number-violating model and 0νββ decay rates, using nuclear matrix elements that can be taken from existing calculations.
