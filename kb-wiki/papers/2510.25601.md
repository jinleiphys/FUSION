# Out-of-equilibrium contributions to charm hadrons in a fluid-dynamic approach

> Building on previous studies that demonstrated the applicability of a fluid-dynamic description of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, the present work extends the framework by computing the out-of-equilibrium contributions to the distribution function of charm hadrons. The analysis includes corrections arising from the initial out-of-equilibrium distribution of charm quarks after a free-streaming phase, as well as from the freeze-out surface within fluid dynamics. These results enable the exact computation of integrated yields and transverse momentum distributions of charm hadrons for different values of the spatial diffusion coefficient, thereby providing the basis for a systematic determination of the charm transport coefficients. In addition, the limits of applicability of the approach are identified by determining the transverse-momentum region in which charm hadrons are described by a well-defined, positive distribution function.

## Key claim
The paper extends a fluid-dynamic description of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma by computing out-of-equilibrium corrections to charm-hadron distribution functions at both the initial stage and freeze-out, but the approach is valid only when the inverse Reynolds number for diffusion is much smaller than unity, and breaks down for transverse momenta above approximately 2 GeV/c where the total distribution function can become negative.

## Method
The authors initialize charm quarks using a FONLL pQCD production cross section with a free-streaming phase from formation time τ_i = 0.1 fm/c to hydrodynamic initialization time τ_0 = 0.4 fm/c, then evolve the total-charm diffusion current using a relaxation-type equation derived from a multi-species Fokker-Planck approach in the Hadron Resonance Gas of charm (HRGc). The out-of-equilibrium corrections at freeze-out are computed via the Cooper-Frye procedure using a first-order Navier-Stokes relation between each charm-hadron diffusion current and the total charm diffusion current, with transport coefficients expressed in terms of the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s.

## Key numbers
- Charm-anticharm production cross section at mid-rapidity: dσ^{Q\bar{Q}}/dy|_{y=0} = 0.463 mb, using CTEQ6.6 PDFs for PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, 0-10% centrality
- Inelastic proton-proton cross section: σ^{in} = 70 mb
- Formation time of charm quarks: τ_i = 0.1 fm/c; hydrodynamic initialization time: τ_0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Spatial diffusion coefficient value studied: D_s T = 0.24, representing the upper limit at T = 1.1 T_c from Altenkort (2023)
- The integrand of the D^0 meson momentum spectrum becomes predominantly negative for p_T ≳ 2 GeV/c when D_s T = 0.24

## Context
This work extends the fluid-dynamic framework for charm quarks developed in Capellino (2022, 2023) by including out-of-equilibrium corrections at both initial stage and freeze-out, which were previously neglected. The approach builds on the method of moments from Denicol (2012) and the multi-species treatment from Fotakis (2022), and addresses the unphysical dependence of charm multiplicities on D_s found in earlier work. The results enable future Bayesian analyses to constrain the charm spatial diffusion coefficient by comparing computed yields and transverse momentum spectra to experimental data from ALICE and CMS.
