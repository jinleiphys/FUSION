# Quark mean field model with pion and gluon corrections for $Λ,~Ξ^0$ hypernuclei and neutron stars

> Properties of $Λ,~Ξ^0$ hypernuclei and neutron stars are investigated in quark mean field model with pion and gluon corrections. Firstly, $u, ~d$, and $s$ quarks are confined by relativistic harmonic oscillator potentials to generate the baryons, like nucleon, $Λ, ~Σ$, and $Ξ$ hyperons. The effects of pion-quark coupling and one-gluon exchange are considered perturbatively. Then, the baryons interact with each other through exchanging $σ, ~ω,$ and $ρ$ mesons between quarks in hypernuclei and nuclear matter. The strengths of confinement potentials for $u, ~d,$ and $s$ quarks are determined by the masses and radii of free baryons. The coupling constants between the quarks and mesons are fixed by the ground-state properties of several nuclei and single-hyperon potentials at nuclear saturation density, which arises three parameter sets for the coupling constants between mesons and quarks, named QMF-NK1S, QMF-NK2S, and QMF-NK3S. Compared to the results of previous quark mean field model without pion and gluon corrections, it is found that properties of $Λ$ hypernuclei, i.e. the single $Λ$ energies, are more consistent with the experimental observables. Properties of $Ξ^0$ hypernuclei are also calculated and compared with the results in previous quark mean field model. With these three parameter sets, the neutron stars containing hyperons are investigated through solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation. Maximum masses of neutron stars approach $2.1M_\odot$ with hyperons and corresponding radii are around $13$ km.

## Key claim

The quark mean field model with perturbative pion and gluon corrections produces single-Λ hypernuclei energies that are more consistent with experimental observables than the previous QMF model without these corrections, but only when the coupling constants are also constrained by finite-nuclei properties (not solely by saturation-density hyperon potentials as in earlier work by Mishra et al.).

## Method

The authors confine u, d, and s quarks in relativistic harmonic oscillator potentials to generate baryons (nucleon, Λ, Σ, Ξ), then treat pion-quark coupling and one-gluon exchange perturbatively as corrections to the baryon mass. The baryons interact via σ, ω, and ρ meson exchange between quarks in hypernuclei and nuclear matter, with the meson-field equations solved self-consistently in the mean-field approximation. Three parameter sets (QMF-NK1S, QMF-NK2S, QMF-NK3S) are fixed by fitting ground-state properties of several normal nuclei and the empirical single-Λ and single-Ξ potentials at nuclear saturation density (UΛ = -30 MeV, UΞ = -12 MeV), then applied to Λ and Ξ0 hypernuclei and to neutron-star matter via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers

- Single-Λ binding energy in 1s1/2 state of ⁴⁰ΛCa: -17.76 MeV (QMF-NK3S) vs. experimental -18.7 ± 1.1 MeV
- Single-Λ binding energy in 1s1/2 state of ²⁰⁸ΛPb: -25.86 MeV (QMF-NK3S) vs. experimental -26.9 ± 0.8 MeV
- Single-Ξ0 binding energy in 1s1/2 state of ²⁰⁸Ξ0Pb: -9.41 MeV (QMF-NK3S)
- Maximum neutron-star mass with hyperons: approximately 2.1 M⊙, with corresponding radii around 13 km
- u-quark masses used: 250 MeV (set A/QMF-NK1S), 300 MeV (set B/QMF-NK2S), 350 MeV (set C/QMF-NK3S)
- s-quark masses: 330 MeV (set A), 380 MeV (set B), 430 MeV (set C)
- Free Λ mass from set A: 1124.803 MeV (experimental: 1115.683 ± 0.006 MeV)
- Free Ξ0 mass from set A: 1311.625 MeV (experimental: 1314.86 ± 0.20 MeV)
- Spin-orbit splittings for single-Λ hypernuclei are less than 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMF model without pion and gluon corrections (Hu et al., 2014) by incorporating chiral-symmetry (pion) and one-gluon-exchange effects following the perturbative scheme of Mishra et al. (2015, 2016), but with the crucial difference that coupling constants are also constrained by finite-nuclei properties rather than only by saturation-density hyperon potentials. The improved agreement with Λ hypernuclei data addresses a limitation of the Mishra et al. approach, and the predictions for Ξ0 hypernuclei provide guidance for future experiments at J-PARC, MAMI, JLab, and FAIR. The maximum neutron-star mass of about 2.1 M⊙ with hyperons contributes to the "hyperon puzzle" discussion, where many earlier models produced too-soft equations of state to support 2 M⊙ neutron stars.
