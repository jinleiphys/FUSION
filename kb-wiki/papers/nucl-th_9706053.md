# Incompleteness of Representation Theory: Hidden symmetries and Quantum Non-Integrability

## Key claim

Representation theory does not enumerate all integrable limits of quantum systems because inner automorphisms of the dynamical algebra, which are neglected in standard subalgebra classification, can generate additional exactly solvable Hamiltonians that appear to have strongly broken symmetry. The number of these hidden symmetries depends on the specific realization of the Hamiltonian and on physical constraints such as time-reversal invariance and Hermiticity.

## Method

The author examines bosonic realizations of Lie algebras (specifically U(4) and U(6)) and identifies inner automorphisms generated by gauge rotations of one boson type relative to others, which preserve the commutation relations but alter the form of S-subalgebra generators. These transformations produce conjugate embeddings of subalgebras that are equivalent from the perspective of representation theory but yield distinct dynamical symmetries when used to construct Hamiltonians from Casimir invariants. The resulting Hamiltonians are re-expressed as linear combinations of Casimir operators from multiple standard group chains, demonstrating that they are exactly solvable despite mixing invariants from different chains.

## Key numbers

- For the U(4) Vibron model, the conjugate O(4) embedding (denoted $\overline{O(4)}$) is generated by the transformation $\pi_\mu \rightarrow -i\pi_\mu$, $\sigma \rightarrow \sigma$, yielding generators $\{L_\mu, D_\nu^\prime\}$ where $D_\mu^\prime = i[\pi^\dagger \sigma - \sigma^\dagger \tilde \pi]_\mu^{(1)}$.
- The $\overline{O(4)}$ Hamiltonian $H(\overline{O4}) = \alpha C_2[\overline{O(4)}] + \beta C_2[O(3)]$ expands as $-\alpha C_2[O(4)] + 4\alpha (N-1)C_1[U(3)] - 4\alpha C_2[U(3)] + (2\alpha + \beta)C_2[O(3)] + 6\alpha N$, mixing Casimir operators from both U(3) and O(4) chains.
- For the U(6) Interacting Boson Model, the conjugate SU(3) embedding corresponds to gauge rotation angles $\phi_2 = 0, \pi$ (with $\pi/2, 3\pi/2$ excluded by time-reversal invariance), while for conjugate O(6) the allowed angle is $\phi_2 = 0, 3\pi/2$.
- The $\overline{SU(3)}$ Hamiltonian $H(\overline{SU(3)}) = \alpha Q^+ \cdot Q^+ + \beta L \cdot L$ expands into $5\alpha \hat n_d + \alpha \hat n_d^2 - \alpha Q^- \cdot Q^- - 2\alpha P^\dagger P - 6\alpha U \cdot U + (\beta - 7\alpha/20)L \cdot L + 2N(N+4)$, involving Casimir operators from all three standard IBM dynamical symmetries.

## Context

This work extends the standard group-theoretical approach to dynamical symmetries as formulated in the Interacting Boson Model of Iachello and Arima (1987) and the Vibron model of Iachello and Levine (1995), which assumed that the subalgebra embeddings classified by Dynkin (1952) uniquely enumerate all integrable limits. The paper contradicts the prevailing assumption that mixing Casimir operators from different group chains necessarily implies non-integrability, as argued in studies of quantum chaos by Zhang and Feng (1995) and Alhassid, Novoselsky, and Whelan (1990). The results enable the identification of new integrable regions in parameter spaces previously expected to be chaotic, as demonstrated in triatomic molecule studies using $U(4) \otimes U(4)$ by Champion (1996), and provide a framework for classifying hidden symmetries in models with multiple boson types such as the proton-neutron IBM and octupole-deformed nuclei.
