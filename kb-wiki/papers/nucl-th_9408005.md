# Pion interferometry with pion-source-medium interactions

> An extended pion source, which can be temporarily created by a high energy nuclear collision, will also absorb and distort the outgoing pions. We discuss how this effect alters the interferometric pattern of the two-pion momentum correlation function. In particular, we show that the two-pion correlation function decreases rapidly when the opening angle between the pions increases. The opening-angle dependence should serve as a new means of obtaining information about the pion source in the analysis of experimental data.

## Key claim

The two-pion momentum correlation function acquires a strong dependence on the opening angle between the pions when pion–source-medium interactions are included via a local optical potential, and this effect persists even for a Gaussian source distribution; the abstract's claim that the correlation function "decreases rapidly when the opening angle increases" is conditional on the presence of finite absorptive interactions (imaginary part of the optical potential), and the effect is most detectable at small opening angles where the kinematic minimum relative momentum is small.

## Method

The authors model the pion–source-medium interaction by adding a local optical potential \(U(x)\) to the Klein-Gordon equation for the pion field, then compute the distorted single-particle wave functions in the eikonal approximation. They assume a static, spherically symmetric source with either a Gaussian or uniform density profile, and parametrize the optical potential as a complex constant times the source density, with \(\sigma_R\) and \(\sigma_I\) representing the real and imaginary parts of the forward scattering amplitude. The two-pion correlation function \(C_2(\mathbf{k}_1,\mathbf{k}_2)\) is then evaluated from the overlap integral of the distorted wave functions, and its dependence on the opening angle \(\phi\) between the two pion momenta and on the total pair momentum \(P\) is studied numerically.

## Key numbers

* For a black-sphere source of radius \(R\), the correlation function for parallel pions (\(\phi=0\)) is \(C_2 = 1 + \frac{4}{q^2 R^2} \sin^2(qR/2)\), while for back-to-back pions (\(\phi=\pi\)) it is \(C_2 = 1\) for all \(q\).
* The kinematic minimum relative momentum for a given opening angle \(\phi\) and total momentum \(P\) is \(q_{\rm min} = P\left(\frac{1-\cos\phi}{1+\cos\phi}\right)^{1/2}\).
* For a source with central density \(\rho_o = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the representative optical potential parameters are \((\sigma_R,\sigma_I) = (\pm 2, -2)\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), corresponding to a characteristic absorption length of \((\sigma \rho_o)^{-1} = 1.5\ \mathrm{fm}\) in nuclear matter.
* For a uniform source of radius \(R_S = 10\ \mathrm{fm}\) and a Gaussian source of radius \(R_G = 5\ \mathrm{fm}\) (chosen to have the same rms radius and central density), the opening-angle-averaged correlation function at \(P=400\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(q=40\ \mathrm{MeV}\) shows significant suppression as \(\phi\) increases when \(\sigma_I \neq 0\).
* With \(\sigma_I = 0\) and \(\sigma_R \approx -1.5\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), the \(\phi\)-averaged correlation function for a uniform spherical source can reach values as large as 4.5 at \(q=100\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The E802 data for \(\pi^-\) correlations in \(14.6A\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\) \({}^{28}\mathrm{Si} + {}^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) collisions, fitted to a Gaussian form, give \(R_e = 3.42 \pm 0.26\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(\lambda = 0.65 \pm 0.07\); the authors' static Gaussian model with \(R_G \approx 5\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(c=10\) (i.e., \(\sigma_R,\sigma_I\) scaled by a factor of 10) reproduces the correlation function shape.

## Context

This work extends the standard Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) formalism for two-pion interferometry, as developed by Yano and Koonin (1978), Gyulassy, Kauffmann, and Wilson (1979), and Pratt (1984), by including final-state interactions of the pions with the source medium via an optical potential. The authors show that the usual assumption of weak final-state interactions, which leads to a correlation function depending only on the relative momentum \(q\), is violated when the source absorbs or distorts the outgoing pions. This effect provides an alternative explanation for the non-Gaussian shapes and reduced coherence parameters \(\lambda\) observed in experimental data from the AGS (e.g., E802 and E859) and elsewhere, and suggests that future analyses should explicitly study the opening-angle dependence rather than averaging over it.
