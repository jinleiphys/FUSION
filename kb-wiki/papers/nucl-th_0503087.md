# Quantum interferences in the $γN \to e^+e^- N$ reaction close to the vector meson production threshold

> The exclusive photoproduction of $e^+e^-$ pairs from nucleons close to the vector meson production threshold ($1.4<\sqrt s <1.8$ GeV) results from two main processes: the emission of Bethe-Heitler pairs and the photoproduction of $ρ^0$- and $ω$-mesons decaying into $e^+e^-$ pairs. The Bethe-Heitler amplitudes are purely electromagnetic and reflect mostly the nucleon magnetic structure. The $γN\to e^+e^- N$ amplitudes arising from vector meson production and decay are derived from $γN\to ρ^0 N$ and $γN \to ωN$ amplitudes supplemented by the Vector Meson Dominance assumption. The vector meson photoproduction amplitudes are calculated using a relativistic and unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering. They depend sensitively on the coupling of vector fields to baryon resonances. The $γN \to e^+e^- N$ differential cross sections display interference patterns. The interference of Bethe-Heitler pair production with vector meson $e^+e^-$ decay is quite small in the domain of validity of our model for all angles of the emitted $e^+e^-$ pair. The interference of $ρ^0$- and $ω$-mesons in the $e^+e^-$ channel can be large. It is constructive for the $γp \to e^+e^- p$ reaction and destructive for the $γn \to e^+e^- n$ reaction. We discuss the shape and magnitude of the $e^+e^-$ pair spectra produced in the $γp \to e^+e^- p$ and $γn \to e^+e^- n$ reactions as functions of the pair emission angle and of the total center of mass energy $\sqrt s$.

## Key claim

The interference of Bethe-Heitler pair production with vector meson \(e^+e^-\) decay is quite small in the domain of validity of the model for all angles of the emitted \(e^+e^-\) pair, while the interference of \(\rho^0\)- and \(\omega\)-mesons in the \(e^+e^-\) channel can be large; it is constructive for the \(\gamma p \to e^+e^- p\) reaction and destructive for the \(\gamma n \to e^+e^- n\) reaction.

## Method

The authors calculate the \(\gamma N \to e^+e^- N\) differential cross sections by summing Bethe-Heitler amplitudes (expressed in terms of nucleon Dirac and Pauli form factors) with amplitudes for \(\rho^0\)- and \(\omega\)-meson photoproduction and subsequent decay into \(e^+e^-\) pairs, using the Vector Meson Dominance assumption. The vector meson photoproduction amplitudes are obtained from a relativistic and unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering (including \(\pi N\), \(\pi \Delta\), \(\rho N\), \(\omega N\), \(K \Lambda\), \(K \Sigma\), \(\eta N\) and \(\gamma N\) channels) that generates baryon resonances dynamically in the S\(_{11}\), S\(_{31}\), D\(_{13}\) and D\(_{33}\) partial waves. The cross sections are projected onto forward and backward hemispheres of the lepton pair phase space to isolate the symmetric (incoherent) and antisymmetric (interference) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The total center of mass energy range studied is \(1.4 < \sqrt s < 1.8\) GeV.
- The coupling constants for the generalized Vector Meson Dominance transition tensor are \(g_{\omega,1}^{(+)} = 0.083\), \(g_{\rho,1}^{(+)} = 0.469\), \(g_{\omega,2}^{(+)} = 0.000\), and \(g_{\rho,2}^{(+)} = 0.241\).
- The dimensional coupling constants for the \(\gamma V\) interaction Lagrangian are \(|f_\rho| = 0.036\) GeV\(^2\) and \(|f_\omega| = 0.011\) GeV\(^2\).
- The proton and neutron magnetic moments used are \(\mu_p = 2.793\) and \(\mu_n = -1.913\).
- The nucleon electromagnetic form factors are parametrized with a dipole form using the scale \(0.71\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the same theoretical framework previously applied to the \(\pi N \to e^+e^- N\) reaction (Lutz, 2002) to the photoproduction channel, using the vector meson photoproduction amplitudes from a coupled-channel model (Lutz, 2002) that describes pion-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering and meson photoproduction data. The results provide predictions for upcoming experiments at JLab with the CLAS detector (G7 collaboration) and suggest that the vector meson contribution can be extracted from experimental \(e^+e^-\) spectra by subtracting the accurately calculable Bethe-Heitler term and neglecting the small Bethe-Heitler–vector meson interference.
