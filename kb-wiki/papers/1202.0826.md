# Boosting equal time bound states

> We present an explicit and exact boost of a relativistic bound state defined at equal time of the constituents in the Born approximation (lowest order in hbar). To this end, we construct the Poincaré generators of QED and QCD in D=1+1 dimensions, using Gauss' law to express A^0 in terms of the fermion fields in A^1=0 gauge. We determine the fermion-antifermion bound states in the Born approximation as eigenstates of the time and space translation generators P^0 and P^1. The boost operator is combined with a gauge transformation so as to maintain the gauge condition A^1=0 in the new frame. We verify that the boosted state remains an eigenstate of P^0 and P^1 with appropriately transformed eigenvalues and determine the transformation law of the equal-time, relativistic wave function. The shape of the wave function is independent of the CM momentum when expressed in terms of a variable, which is quadratically related to the distance x between the fermions. As a consequence, the Lorentz contraction of the wave function is proportional to 1/(E-V(x)) and thus depends on x via the linear potential V(x).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates an explicit and exact boost of a relativistic bound state defined at equal time of its constituents, but only in the Born approximation (lowest order in \(\hbar\)) and only in \(D=1+1\) dimensions where the interaction is instantaneous and Coulombic, with the gauge condition \(A^1=0\) maintained by combining the boost operator with a gauge transformation.

## Method

The authors construct the Poincaré generators \(P^0\), \(P^1\), and \(M^{01}\) for QED and QCD in \(D=1+1\) dimensions, using Gauss' law to eliminate \(A^0\) in favor of fermion fields in \(A^1=0\) gauge. They determine fermion-antifermion bound states as eigenstates of the translation generators in the Born approximation, using retarded propagators to avoid intermediate pair production. The boost operator is applied to these states, and the transformation law of the equal-time wave function is derived, verifying that the boosted state remains an eigenstate of \(P^0\) and \(P^1\) with appropriately transformed eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

* The linear potential in QED\(_2\) is \(V(x) = \frac{1}{2} e^2 |x|\), where \(x\) is the relative coordinate between fermions.
* The bound state rest mass \(M\) is defined by \(M \equiv \sqrt{E^2 - k^2}\), where \(E\) is the energy and \(k\) the center-of-mass momentum.
* The invariant variable \(s(x)\) is defined as \(s(x) \equiv \frac{1}{2} \int_0^x du [E - V(u)] = \frac{\epsilon(x)}{2e^2} [2EV(x) - V(x)^2] = \frac{\epsilon(x)}{2e^2} (M^2 - p^2)\), where \(\epsilon(x)\) is the sign function.
* The Lorentz contraction of the wave function is proportional to \(1/(E - V(x))\), depending on \(x\) through the linear potential.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Hoyer (1986) that bound states of two fermions interacting via a linear potential exhibit boost covariance in their bound state equation. The present paper derives this result from first principles by identifying the system with a relativistic two-fermion bound state of 1+1 dimensional QED (or QCD) in the Born approximation. The approach enables explicit transformation of equal-time wave functions between frames, which is essential for scattering amplitudes involving bound states as external particles, and suggests possible generalization to \(D=3+1\) dimensions by imposing boundary conditions on Gauss' law to generate a linear instantaneous potential.
