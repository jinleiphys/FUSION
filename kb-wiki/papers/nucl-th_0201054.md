# The $σ$ meson in a nuclear medium through two pion photoproduction

> We present theoretical results for $(γ, π^0 π^0)$ production on nucleons and nuclei in the kinematical region where the scalar isoscalar $ππ$ amplitude is influenced by the $σ$ pole. The final state interaction of the pions modified by the nuclear medium produces a spectacular shift of strength of the two pion invariant mass distribution induced by the moving of the $σ$ pole to lower masses and widths as the nuclear density increases.

# The σ meson in a nuclear medium through two pion photoproduction

## Key claim
The two-pion invariant mass distribution in (γ, π⁰π⁰) reactions on nuclei shows a shift of strength toward lower invariant masses due to the modification of the ππ interaction in the nuclear medium, which is interpreted as the σ meson pole moving to lower masses and narrower widths as nuclear density increases. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the use of a conventional many-body expansion based on standard chiral Lagrangians, and that alternative approaches based on chiral symmetry restoration from the dropping quark condensate could produce similar effects through different mechanisms.

## Method
The authors model the elementary γN → Nπ⁰π⁰ reaction using tree-level diagrams including nucleons, Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), and Δ(1700) resonances, following the approach of Nacher et al. (2001). The final state interaction of the two pions in the isoscalar (I=0) channel is implemented via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, factorizing the on-shell ππ scattering amplitude and the two-pion loop function. For nuclear targets, the cross section is calculated using the local density approximation and a semiclassical transport method that accounts for pion absorption and quasielastic scattering, with the in-medium ππ interaction taken from Chiang et al. (1998).

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range: Eγ = 400–460 MeV
- The improved loop calculation for the Δ Kroll-Ruderman term results in a 10 per cent reduction in the cross section
- Inclusion of baryon form factors leads to a further reduction of about 15 per cent in the final cross section
- The cut-off of the monopole form factor is changed from 1 GeV to 625 MeV to implement loop corrections
- The average nuclear density felt by the reaction is 35 per cent of normal nuclear density for ¹²C and 65 per cent for ²⁰⁸Pb
- The real part of the pion selfenergy produces a moderate increase of the cross section by 20 per cent in ¹²C
- The renormalization of the I=0 amplitude nearly doubles the cross section for the γp → π⁰π⁰p reaction

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of unitary extensions of chiral perturbation theory (UχPT) developed by Dobado et al. (1990, 1993), Oller et al. (1997, 1999), and others, which generate the σ meson dynamically as a ππ resonance through multiple scattering. It directly addresses the medium modification of the ππ interaction previously studied by Schuck et al. (1988), Rapp et al. (1996), and Chiang et al. (1998). The (γ, π⁰π⁰) reaction is argued to be better suited than the (π, ππ) reaction studied by Bonutti et al. (1996, 1998) and Starostin et al. (2000) because photons are not distorted and can probe larger nuclear densities. The results are compared with preliminary experimental data from the Mainz group (Metag), and the pole evolution of the σ meson with density is further analyzed in a companion work by Vacas et al. (2002).
