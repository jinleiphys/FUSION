# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for multinucleon transfer processes in $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reactions

> Multinucleon transfer processes in heavy-ion reactions at energies slightly above the Coulomb barrier are investigated in a fully microscopic framework of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. Transfer probabilities are calculated from the TDHF wave function after collision using the projection operator method which has recently been proposed by Simenel (C. Simenel, Phys. Rev. Lett. 105, 192701 (2010)). We show results of the TDHF calculations for transfer cross sections of the reactions of $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn at $E_{lab}=$ 170, 174 MeV, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb at $E_{lab}=$ 235, 249 MeV, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb at $E_{lab}=$ 328.4 MeV, for which measurements are available. We find the transfer processes show different behaviors depending on the $N/Z$ ratios of the projectile and the target, and the product of the charge numbers, $Z_P Z_T$. When the projectile and the target have different $N/Z$ ratios, fast transfer processes of a few nucleons towards the charge equilibrium of the initial system occur in reactions at large impact parameters. As the impact parameter decreases, a neck formation is responsible for the transfer. A number of nucleons are transferred by the neck breaking when two nuclei dissociate, leading to transfers of protons and neutrons in the same direction. Comparing cross sections by theory and measurements, we find the TDHF theory describes the transfer cross sections of a few nucleons reasonably. As the number of transferred nucleons increases, the agreement becomes less accurate. The TDHF calculation overestimates transfer cross sections accompanying a large number of neutrons when more than one proton are transferred. Comparing our results with those by other theories, we find the TDHF calculations give qualitatively similar results to those of direct reaction models such as GRAZING and Complex WKB.

# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for multinucleon transfer processes in 40,48Ca+124Sn, 40Ca+208Pb, and 58Ni+208Pb reactions

## Key claim

The TDHF theory describes multinucleon transfer cross sections for a few nucleons reasonably well, but the agreement becomes less accurate as the number of transferred nucleons increases; the TDHF calculation overestimates transfer cross sections accompanying a large number of neutrons when more than one proton are transferred, and the results are qualitatively similar to those of direct reaction models such as GRAZING and Complex WKB.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional with all time-odd terms (except the second derivative of spin densities) on a uniform Cartesian grid with 0.8 fm spacing. Transfer probabilities were extracted from the TDHF wave function after collision using the projection operator method of Simenel (2010), which employs a Fourier integral over an angle variable to compute the probability that a given number of protons and neutrons occupy the projectile-like fragment region. Transfer cross sections were obtained by integrating these probabilities over impact parameters from the fusion critical impact parameter up to 10 fm for each reaction system.

## Key numbers

- Fusion critical impact parameter for 40Ca+124Sn at Elab=170 MeV: bf = 3.95 fm
- Fusion critical impact parameter for 48Ca+124Sn at Elab=174 MeV: bf = 3.93 fm
- Coulomb barrier height for 40Ca+124Sn: VB ≈ 116.3 MeV (frozen-density approximation)
- Coulomb barrier height for 48Ca+124Sn: VB ≈ 115.1 MeV (frozen-density approximation)
- Coulomb rainbow angle for 40Ca+124Sn: 99 degrees (from TDHF trajectories)
- Coulomb rainbow angle for 48Ca+124Sn: 100 degrees (from TDHF trajectories)
- Grid spacing: 0.8 fm; time step: Δt = 0.2 fm/c; box size: 48 fm × 48 fm × 20.8 fm for collision calculations
- Product of charge numbers ZPZT: 1000 for 40,48Ca+124Sn, 1640 for 40Ca+208Pb, and 2296 for 58Ni+208Pb

## Context

This work extends the TDHF approach to multinucleon transfer reactions, building on the projection method introduced by Simenel (2010) for calculating transfer probabilities from TDHF wave functions. The authors compare their fully microscopic results with experimental measurements for four reaction systems previously studied by Corradi and collaborators, and with predictions from direct reaction models such as GRAZING and Complex WKB. The study enables systematic assessment of TDHF theory's capability to describe multinucleon transfer, revealing its strengths for few-nucleon transfer and its limitations for channels involving many transferred nucleons, particularly when accompanied by large neutron transfer.
