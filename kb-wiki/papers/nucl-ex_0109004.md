# The Nucleon Structure in the Resonance Region

> Recent results on inclusive and exclusive electroproduction experiments at Jefferson Lab are discussed. They include measurements of the spin responses for protons and neutrons in and above the resonance region, exclusive single and double pion production to measure resonance transition multipoles, and searches for predicted yet unobserved quark model states. A brief outlook into the new domain of generalized parton distribution is given as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that precise electroproduction data from Jefferson Lab in the resonance region are essential for bridging the gap between the deep inelastic regime (where quark momentum and spin distributions are measured) and the confinement regime of strong QCD, but many results presented are preliminary and qualitative features may change with final analysis.

## Method

The paper reviews inclusive double-polarization electroproduction experiments measuring spin asymmetries \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) for protons and neutrons, from which the spin structure function \(g_1(x,Q^2)\) and its first moment \(\Gamma_1(Q^2)\) are extracted. Exclusive single-pion (\(\pi^0\), \(\pi^+\)) and eta (\(\eta\)) electroproduction measurements are used to determine resonance transition multipoles, particularly for the \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(S_{11}(1535)\) states. Searches for missing quark model states are conducted through analysis of \(p\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(p\omega\) final states, and a first measurement of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) beam spin asymmetry is reported.

## Key numbers

- The first moment \(\Gamma_1^p(Q^2)\) for the proton shows a zero crossing near \(Q^2 = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).
- The asymmetry \(A_1+\eta A_2\) for protons reaches peak values of about 0.6 at \(Q^2=0.8\) GeV\(^2\) and W=1.5 GeV.
- The \(S_{11}(1535)\) transition form factor \(A_{1/2}(Q^2)\) drops by a factor of less than 2.5 over a range \(Q^2 < 3\) GeV\(^2\).
- CLAS data on \(R_{EM}\) and \(R_{SM}\) multipole ratios for the \(\gamma N\Delta(1232)\) transition cover \(Q^2\) from 0.3 to 4.0 GeV\(^2\).
- The DVCS beam spin asymmetry was measured at 4.3 GeV beam energy, \(Q^2=1.3\) GeV\(^2\), \(<t> = 0.11\) GeV\(^2\), \(x=0.21\).
- Total cross section data for \(\gamma^* p \rightarrow p\pi^+\pi^-\) are shown at \(Q^2=0.65\) GeV\(^2\), 0.95 GeV\(^2\), and 1.25 GeV\(^2\).
- The generalized GDH integral for neutrons is evaluated over the region from pion threshold to W = 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of nucleon spin structure from SLAC and the GDH sum rule constraints of Gerasimov (1965) and Drell (1966), and the Bjorken sum rule. It provides the first systematic data on the \(Q^2\) evolution of \(\Gamma_1\) in the transition region between the real photon point and the deep inelastic regime, testing Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations by Ji (1995) and pQCD evolutions. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of resonance contributions to spin structure function moments and open the door to hard baryon spectroscopy using generalized parton distributions.
