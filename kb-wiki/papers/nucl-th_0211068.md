# Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum for Binding Systematics in Neutron-Rich Isotopes of Oxygen and Fluor

> Continuum coupling correction to binding energies in the neutron rich oxygen and fluorine isotopes is studied using the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum. We discuss the importance of different effects, such as the position of one-neutron emission threshold, the effective interaction or the number of valence particles on the magnitude of this correction.

# Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum for Binding Systematics in Neutron-Rich Isotopes of Oxygen and Fluor

## Key claim

Continuum coupling corrections to binding energies in neutron-rich oxygen and fluorine isotopes are significant and depend strongly on the position of the one-neutron emission threshold, the isospin of the coupling, and the number of valence particles; however, the magnitude and sign of the odd-even staggering of these corrections can reverse depending on whether the threshold is calculated self-consistently or fixed artificially, meaning the effect is not a simple monotonic correction but is threshold-dependent and can either enhance or attenuate binding energy staggering.

## Method

The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) formalism was applied to oxygen and fluorine isotopes, using projection operators to separate bound (Q) and scattering (P) subspaces. The effective Hamiltonian included coupling between discrete shell-model states and one-neutron continuum channels, with radial wave functions generated self-consistently from a Woods-Saxon potential. Full sd-shell valence space was used for N<20 and full pf-shell for N>20, with USD and KB' effective interactions respectively, and all asymptotic channels composed of shell-model states were included (from 3 channels in 28O to 1837 in 31F).

## Key numbers

- The neutron-neutron continuum coupling strength was set to V0^(nn) = 414 MeV·fm^3 for the oxygen chain.
- In oxygen isotopes, the continuum correction E_corr shows an approximately quadratic dependence on the number of valence neutrons, with deviations characteristic of continuum coupling.
- For the oxygen chain, the T=1 monopole terms of the two-body interaction were modified by: δV(0d5/2,1s1/2)^(T=1) = -0.05 MeV, δV(1s1/2,0d3/2)^(T=1) = +0.25 MeV, δV(0d3/2,0d3/2)^(T=1) = -0.35 MeV.
- For fluorine isotopes, the np-continuum coupling strength V0^(np) varied from approximately 2V0^(nn) for well-bound isotopes to approximately (1/2)V0^(nn) near the neutron drip-line.
- For fluorine with V0^(np) = (1/2)V0^(nn), the T=0 two-body interaction was modified by: δV(0d5/2,0d3/2)^(T=0) = +0.6 MeV, δV(0d5/2,0f7/2)^(T=0) = +0.9 MeV.
- The continuum coupling effect on one- and two-neutron separation energies is mainly seen for S_n less than approximately 3 MeV, i.e., close to the one-neutron drip-line.

## Context

This work extends the SMEC formalism previously developed by Bennaceur, Nowacki, Okolowicz, and Ploszajczak (1999, 2000) to a systematic study of binding energies across entire isotopic chains from stability to the neutron drip-line. It addresses the observed disappearance of the N=20 shell closure and appearance of N=16 closure in neutron-rich nuclei (Ozawa, Kobayashi, Suzuki, Yoshida, and Tanihata, 2000), as well as the dramatic shift of the neutron drip-line from oxygen (unstable beyond N=16, as shown by Tarasov et al., 1997) to fluorine (stable up to 31F with N=22, as shown by Sakurai et al., 1999). The results enable quantitative estimation of how continuum coupling renormalizes the effective shell-model Hamiltonian near the drip-line, and demonstrate that the odd-even staggering indicator Δ^(3)(N) can be "falsified" by np-continuum coupling rather than reflecting genuine changes in neutron pairing correlations.
