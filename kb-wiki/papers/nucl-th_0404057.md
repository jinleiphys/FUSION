# Properties of the $ω$ Meson at Finite Temperature and Density

> The mass shift, width broadening, and spectral density for the $ω$ meson in a heat bath of nucleons and pions is calculated using a general formula which relates the self-energy to the forward scattering amplitude. We use experimental data to saturate the scattering amplitude at low energies with resonances and include a background Pomeron term, while at high energies a Regge parameterization is used. The peak of the spectral density is little shifted from its vacuum position, but the width is considerably increased due to collisional broadening. At normal nuclear matter density and a temperature of 150 MeV the spectral density of the $ω$ meson has a width of 140 MeV. Zero temperature nuclear matter is also discussed.

## Key claim

The mass shift of the omega meson in a heat bath of nucleons and pions is small (at most a few tens of MeV) and negligible compared to the vacuum mass, but the width is considerably increased due to collisional broadening; the authors qualify that the results are intermediate between the two extreme models used in their prior work (Martell and Ellis, 2003) and that the agreement with Rapp (2001) is satisfying.

## Method

The authors construct the forward scattering amplitude for omega mesons scattering from nucleons and pions using experimental data, saturating the low-energy region with Breit-Wigner resonances (including updated resonance data from Shklyar, Penner and Mosel (2004) for the omega-nucleon channel) plus a background Pomeron term, and using a Regge parameterization at high energies. They then compute the retarded self-energy on shell via a single integral formula, summing over target species (pions and nucleons) and including the vacuum contribution, to obtain the mass shift, width, and spectral density as functions of momentum, temperature, and nucleon density.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density (0.16 nucleons/fm³) and a temperature of 150 MeV, the spectral density of the omega meson has a width of 140 MeV (full width at half maximum).
- At a temperature of 150 MeV and nucleon density n_N = 1 (equilibrium nuclear matter density), the omega meson width is 130 MeV, an enhancement of the vacuum width (8.4 MeV) by a factor of 15.
- At zero-temperature nuclear matter, the mass shift is approximately +30 MeV (at momentum p = 0) and the width due to collisional broadening is 75 MeV.
- At zero nucleon density and T = 150 MeV, the calculated width is about 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior model-independent approach (Martell and Ellis, 2003) for vector meson self-energies by incorporating improved omega-nucleon resonance data from Shklyar, Penner and Mosel (2004), replacing the two extreme models (two-resonance and omega-to-rho) used previously. The results are consistent with the many-body approach of Rapp (2001) and with estimates from Post and Mosel (2004), Lutz, Wolf and Friman (1998), and Muhlich, Falter and Mosel (2003), though some of those find smaller widths; the calculated spectral density enables predictions for dilepton production from the omega meson channel in heavy ion collisions.
