# Theoretical Study on Rotational Bands and Shape Coexistence of $^{183,185,187}${Tl} in the Particle Triaxial-Rotor Model

> By taking the particle triaxial-rotor model with variable moment of inertia, we investigate the energy spectra, the deformations and the single particle configurations of the nuclei $^{183,185,187}$Tl systemically. The calculated energy spectra agree with experimental data quite well. The obtained results indicate that the aligned bands observed in $^{183,185,187}$Tl originate from the $[530]{1/2}^{-}$, $[532]{3/2}^{-}$, $[660]{1/2}^{+}$ proton configuration coupled to a prolate deformed core, respectively. Whereas, the negative parity bands built upon the ${9/2}^{-}$ isomeric states in $^{183,185,187}$Tl are formed by a proton with the $[505]{9/2}^{-}$ configuration coupled to a core with triaxial oblate deformation, and the positive parity band on the ${13/2}^{+}$ isomeric state in $^{187}$Tl is generated by a proton with configuration $[606]{13/2}^{+}$ coupled to a triaxial oblate core.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the aligned rotational bands observed in $^{183,185,187}$Tl originate from specific proton configurations ($[530]1/2^{-}$, $[532]3/2^{-}$, $[660]1/2^{+}$) coupled to a prolate deformed core, while the negative parity bands built on $9/2^{-}$ isomeric states arise from the $[505]9/2^{-}$ configuration coupled to a triaxial oblate core. The key hedge is that these assignments depend on best-fit deformation parameters obtained by minimizing $\chi^2$ between calculated and experimental energy spectra, with the band-head energy fixed artificially to the experimental value.

## Method

The authors employed the particle triaxial-rotor model with a variable moment of inertia for the even-even core, using a hydrodynamical relation for the three moments of inertia with a variable moment parameter $\Im_0(I) = \Im_0 \sqrt{1 + bI(I+1)}$ where $b = 0.013$. They diagonalized the total Hamiltonian in a symmetrically strong coupling basis, taking 13 single-particle orbitals near the Fermi surface for each nucleus, with Nilsson parameters $\kappa = 0.054$ and $\mu = 0.690$, pairing gap $\Delta = 12/\sqrt{A}$, and a Coriolis attenuation factor $\xi = 0.95$. Deformation parameters $\beta$ and $\gamma$ were fitted by minimizing $\chi^2 = \frac{1}{N}\sum_j (E^{cal}_j - E^{exp}_j)^2$ for each band, using values from Lane (1995) as initial trial parameters.

## Context

This work extends the experimental studies of Lane (1995) on rotational bands in $^{185,187}$Tl and the later work of Reviol (2000) and Muikku (2001) on $^{183}$Tl, providing the first systematic theoretical investigation of triaxial deformation in these light odd-A thallium isotopes. The particle triaxial-rotor model with variable moment of inertia had been previously applied to similar problems in other mass regions by authors such as Zhang and Shen (2002), Shen et al. (2004), and Chen et al. (2005). The results confirm the conjecture by Lane (1995) that the $[505]9/2^{-}$ band may involve triaxial oblate deformation, and enable future studies of shape coexistence and configuration mixing in the Z=82 region using this model.
