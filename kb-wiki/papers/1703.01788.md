# Are there any narrow $K^-$- nuclear states?

> We performed self-consistent calculations of $K^-$-nuclear quasi-bound states using a single-nucleon $K^-$ optical potential derived from chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models, supplemented by a phenomenological $K^-$ multinucleon potential introduced recently to achieve good fits to kaonic atom data [1]. Our calculations show that the effect of $K^-$ multinucleon interactions on $K^-$ widths in nuclei is decisive. The resulting widths are considerably larger than corresponding binding energies. Moreover, when the density dependence of the $K^-$-multinucleon interactions derived in the fits of kaonic atoms is extended to the nuclear interior, the only two models acceptable after imposing as additional constraint the single-nucleon fraction of $K^-$ absorption at rest do not yield any kaonic nuclear bound state in majority of considered nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when a phenomenological $K^-$ multinucleon potential fitted to kaonic atom data is extended to the nuclear interior (full density option), the only two chiral meson-baryon interaction models (Prague and Kyoto-Munich) that satisfy the additional constraint of single-nucleon absorption fractions at rest do not yield any kaonic nuclear bound state in the majority of nuclei considered; the resulting widths are considerably larger than the corresponding binding energies.

## Method

Self-consistent calculations of $K^-$-nuclear quasi-bound states were performed by solving the Klein-Gordon equation for $K^-$ in the medium, using a $K^-$ optical potential constructed from chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models (Prague and Kyoto-Munich) for the single-nucleon part, supplemented by a phenomenological multinucleon potential $V_{K^-}^{(2)} = -4\pi B (\rho/\rho_0)^\alpha \rho$ fitted to kaonic atom data. The energy- and density-dependent subthreshold energy shift $\delta\sqrt{s}$ was evaluated self-consistently, and two extrapolation scenarios for the multinucleon potential beyond half nuclear density were considered: full density (FD) and half-density limit (HD).

## Key numbers

* For the $^{208}$Pb$+K^-$ system with the KM1 model (FD option), the $1s$ binding energy $B_{K^-} = 33$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{K^-} = 273$ MeV.
* For the $^{208}$Pb$+K^-$ system with the P1 model (FD option), the $1s$ binding energy $B_{K^-} = 36$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{K^-} = 302$ MeV.
* For the $^{208}$Pb$+K^-$ system with the KM2 model (FD option), the $1s$ binding energy $B_{K^-} = 53$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{K^-} = 429$ MeV.
* For the $^{208}$Pb$+K^-$ system with the P2 model (FD option, with Im$V_{K^-}$ scaled by factor 0.8), the $1s$ binding energy $B_{K^-} = 47$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{K^-} = 412$ MeV.
* For the $^{16}$O nucleus, the KM1 model with FD option yields no bound state; the P1 model with FD option also yields no bound state.
* The subthreshold energy shifts in the nuclear center of $^{208}$Pb range from approximately $-35$ to $-115$ MeV across models.
* The parameters of the multinucleon potential for the P1 model: $\alpha = 1$, Re$B = -1.3 \pm 0.2$ fm, Im$B = 1.5 \pm 0.2$ fm; for KM1: $\alpha = 1$, Re$B = -0.9 \pm 0.2$ fm, Im$B = 1.4 \pm 0.2$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Friedman and Gal (2016), who fitted phenomenological $K^-$ multinucleon potentials to kaonic atom data for several chiral meson-baryon interaction models and imposed the additional constraint of single-nucleon absorption fractions from bubble chamber experiments. The present calculations show that the $K^-$ multinucleon interactions cause a decisive increase in $K^-$ widths, making identification of $K^-$-nuclear quasi-bound states in experiment seem impossible. The results contradict earlier expectations from calculations using only single-nucleon potentials (Cieply et al., 2011; Friedman and Gal, 2016) that suggested narrower widths, and they enable future exploration of $K^-$ multinucleon processes in few-body systems.
