# Stability and causality of rest frame modes in second-order spin hydrodynamics

> We analyze the low- and high-momentum rest frame modes in the second-order spin hydrodynamics and check the asymptotic causality of the theory. A truncation scheme of the Israel-Stewart formalism derived in our earlier work is proposed that extends the minimal causal formulation. It consists of altogether 40 interconnected relaxation-type dynamical equations -- 16 (24) of them correspond to the independent components of the energy-momentum (spin) tensor. Similarly to previous studies, we find that the stability of the perturbations and asymptotic causality require using the spin equation of state that satisfies the generalized Frenkel condition demanding that the ``electric'' and ``magnetic'' components of the spin density tensor have opposite signs. For low-momentum modes this behavior is similar to that found earlier for the first-order (Navier-Stokes) spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The stability of perturbations and asymptotic causality in second-order spin hydrodynamics require the spin equation of state to satisfy the generalized Frenkel condition, where the "electric" and "magnetic" components of the spin density tensor have opposite signs; this condition is essentially the same as that found earlier for first-order (Navier-Stokes) spin hydrodynamics, indicating that including more terms in the second-order theory is not crucial for stability.

## Method

The authors perform a linear mode analysis of the 40 relaxation-type dynamical equations (16 for the energy-momentum tensor and 24 for the spin tensor) derived from the Israel-Stewart formalism, truncating only the so-called mixed terms. They decompose perturbations into plane waves propagating in the z-direction, obtaining a 40x40 block-diagonal matrix whose determinant yields dispersion relations for sound channels, shear channels, and purely spin channels. The analysis examines both low-momentum and high-momentum regimes to determine stability conditions and asymptotic causality.

## Key numbers

- The formalism consists of 40 interconnected relaxation-type dynamical equations, with 16 corresponding to independent components of the energy-momentum tensor and 24 to those of the spin tensor.
- The sound channel dispersion relations include modes such as ω₁,₂ = ±cₛk_z - i(4η/3 + ζ)k_z²/[2(ε₀ + p₀)].
- The shear channel includes a mode ω₁ = -iηk_z²/(ε₀ + p₀).
- The stability conditions are χ_b ≡ ∂S⁰ⁱ/∂ω⁰ⁱ < 0 and χ_s ≡ ∂Sⁱʲ/∂ωⁱʲ > 0, leading to S₁ < 0 and S₂ > 0 in the fluid rest frame.

## Context

This work generalizes the results of Hattori (2019), Daher (2022), Sarwar (2022), and Xie (2023), extending the minimal causal spin hydrodynamics studied by Xie (2023) to include the full 40 relaxation-type equations derived by Biswas (2023). The findings agree with conventional relativistic hydrodynamics as studied by Hiscock (1985) and others, and the same conditions provide high-momentum stability and asymptotic causality provided relaxation times are sufficiently large. The analysis enables future work on stability and causality in boosted frames, potentially leveraging the information current approach introduced by Gavassino (2021, 2022) and recently applied in minimal spin hydrodynamics by Ren (2024).
