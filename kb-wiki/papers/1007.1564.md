# Dynamical properties of nuclear and stellar matter and the symmetry energy

> The effects of density dependence of the symmetry energy on the collective modes and dynamical instabilities of cold and warm nuclear and stellar matter are studied in the framework of relativistic mean-field hadron models. The existence of the collective isovector and possibly an isoscalar collective mode above saturation density is discussed. It is shown that soft equations of state do not allow for a high density isoscalar collective mode, however, if the symmetry energy is hard enough an isovector mode will not disappear at high densities. The crust-core transition density and pressure are obtained as a function of temperature for $β$-equilibrium matter with and without neutrino trapping. An estimation of the size of the clusters formed in the non-homogeneous phase as well as the corresponding growth rates and distillation effect is made. It is shown that cluster sizes increase with temperature, that the distillation effect close to the inner edge of the crust-core transition is very sensitive to the symmetry energy, and that, within a dynamical instability calculation, the pasta phase exists in warm compact stars up to 10 - 12 MeV.

## Key claim

The existence of high-density isoscalar and isovector collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter depends critically on both the stiffness of the equation of state and the density dependence of the symmetry energy: soft equations of state suppress the high-density isoscalar mode, while a sufficiently hard symmetry energy allows an isovector mode to persist at high densities.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Vlasov equation, a semiclassical limit of time-dependent Hartree-Fock, applied to the nonlinear Walecka model (NLWM) in the mean-field approximation to study collective modes and dynamical instabilities of asymmetric nuclear matter and beta-equilibrium stellar matter. They considered several relativistic mean-field parametrizations (NL3, TM1, FSU, and their variants with mixed isoscalar-isovector couplings) that differ in the density dependence of the symmetry energy and incompressibility, and calculated the dispersion relation for longitudinal modes to identify unstable regions and collective mode frequencies.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is 0.148 fm$^{-3}$ for all models except TM1, which has $\rho_0 = 0.145$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The symmetry energy at saturation ranges from 31.660 MeV (NL3$\omega\rho$ with $\Lambda_v = 0.03$) to 37.344 MeV (NL3).
- The symmetry energy slope $L$ at saturation ranges from 55.082 MeV (TM1$\omega\rho$ with $\Lambda_v = 0.03$) to 118.320 MeV (NL3).
- The incompressibility $K$ at saturation is 227.895 MeV for FSU, 269.937 MeV for NL3, and 279.514 MeV for TM1.
- The pasta phase in warm compact stars exists up to temperatures of 10 - 12 MeV.
- The crossing density $\rho_c$ (where the collective mode changes from isovector to isoscalar character) decreases with increasing proton fraction and is sensitive to the high-density symmetry energy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter previously studied by Greco et al. (2003) and Providencia et al. (2006) within relativistic models, and by Haensel (1978) and Matera (1994) in non-relativistic frameworks. It also builds on the Vlasov formalism developed by Nielsen et al. (1991, 1993) for symmetric matter and applied to asymmetric matter by Avancini et al. (2005). The dynamical instability calculations complement thermodynamic approaches used by Oyamatsu (2007), Avancini et al. (2008, 2009), and Xu et al. (2009) to determine crust-core transition densities, and enable predictions for the properties of warm neutron star crusts, including cluster sizes and the distillation effect, which are relevant for understanding neutron star cooling and seismic events.
