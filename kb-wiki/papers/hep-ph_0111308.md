# Chiral loops and VMD in the V->PPgamma decays

> We evaluate radiative decays of $ρ$ and $ω$ going to two neutral mesons, $π^{0} π^{0}$ and $π^{0} η$. We use the sequential vector decay mechanisms in addition to chiral loops and $ρ$-$ω$ mixing. The chiral loops are obtained using elements of $UχPT$ successfully applied in the study of meson-meson interactions up to 1.2 GeV. The chiral loops are found very important in the case of the $ρ\to π^{0} π^{0} γ$ decay and small in the other cases. A good agreement with present measurements of $ρ\to π^{0} π^{0} γ$ and $ω\to π^{0} π^{0}γ$ is obtained and predictions are made for the other decays where the rates obtained are rather small.

## Key claim

The chiral loop contribution is found to be very important for the ρ → π⁰π⁰γ decay (comparable to the sequential vector-meson-dominance mechanism), but small for the ω → π⁰π⁰γ, ρ → π⁰ηγ, and ω → π⁰ηγ decays, where the sequential mechanism dominates; the total branching ratios agree with existing measurements within experimental errors, but the authors estimate a realistic theoretical uncertainty of about 20%.

## Method

The authors evaluate the radiative decays ρ → π⁰π⁰γ, ω → π⁰π⁰γ, ρ → π⁰ηγ, and ω → π⁰ηγ by combining three mechanisms: (i) sequential vector meson decay (V → PV → PPγ) using the VMD model of Bramon (1992), (ii) chiral loop contributions with final-state meson-meson interactions described by unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UχPT) following Oller (1997), and (iii) ρ–ω mixing effects. The loop amplitudes are constructed from the chiral resonance Lagrangian of Ecker (1989) with couplings G_V = 69 MeV and F_V = 154 MeV, and the strong transition amplitudes t_{M₁M₂, M₁′M₂′} are taken from the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Oller (1997) regularized with a cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for ρ → π⁰π⁰γ: total = 4.2 × 10⁻⁵ (sequential: 1.5 × 10⁻⁵, loops: 1.5 × 10⁻⁵)
- Branching ratio for ω → π⁰π⁰γ: total = 4.7 × 10⁻⁵ (sequential: 4.3 × 10⁻⁵, loops: 4.3 × 10⁻⁷, with ρ–ω mixing: 4.8 × 10⁻⁵)
- Branching ratio for ρ → π⁰ηγ: total = 7.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ (sequential: 6.6 × 10⁻¹⁰, loops: 5.4 × 10⁻¹¹, with ρ–ω mixing: 7.5 × 10⁻¹⁰)
- Branching ratio for ω → π⁰ηγ: total = 3.3 × 10⁻⁷ (sequential: 3.0 × 10⁻⁷, loops: 2.2 × 10⁻⁹, with ρ–ω mixing: 3.4 × 10⁻⁷)
- Experimental branching ratio for ρ → π⁰π⁰γ from SND Collaboration: (4.8⁺³·⁴₋₁·₈ ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁵
- Experimental branching ratio for ω → π⁰π⁰γ from SND Collaboration: (7.8 ± 2.7 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁵; from GAMS Collaboration: (7.2 ± 2.5) × 10⁻⁵
- Coupling constants used: G_V = 69 MeV, F_V = 154 MeV; pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV
- ρ–ω mixing enhances the ω → π⁰π⁰γ branching ratio by 8% and the ω → π⁰ηγ branching ratio by 11%

## Context

This work extends the UχPT approach previously applied to φ radiative decays by Marco (1999) to the ρ and ω systems, and complements the VMD-only calculations of Bramon (1992) and the combined VMD-plus-loop analysis of Bramon (2001) by using the dynamically generated scalar resonances (σ, f₀(980), a₀(980)) from the unitarized chiral framework rather than explicit resonance-exchange models. The results provide a consistency check of the UχPT description of meson-meson interaction and enable future tests of the nature of scalar mesons through precise measurements of the ππ invariant mass distribution in ρ → π⁰π⁰γ.
