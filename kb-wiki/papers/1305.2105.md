# Gamow peak approximation near strong resonances

> We discuss the most effective energy range for charged particle induced reactions in a plasma environment at a given plasma temperature. The correspondence between the plasma temperature and the most effective energy should be modified from the one given by the Gamow peak energy, in the presence of a significant incident-energy dependence in the astrophysical S-factor as in the case of resonant reactions. The suggested modification of the effective energy range is important not only in thermonuclear reactions at high temperature in the stellar environment, e.g., in advanced burning stages of massive stars and in explosive stellar environment, as it has been already claimed, but also in the application of the nuclear reactions driven by ultra-intense laser pulse irradiations.

# Gamow peak approximation near strong resonances

## Key claim

The standard Gamow peak approximation for the most effective energy of charged-particle reactions in a plasma must be modified when the astrophysical S-factor has a significant energy dependence, as in resonant reactions; the authors demonstrate that this modification is relevant not only for stellar environments at high temperatures but also for nuclear reactions driven by ultra-intense laser pulse irradiations, where plasma temperatures can match those of explosive stellar environments.

## Method

The authors derive a modified condition for the most effective energy by including the logarithmic derivative of the energy-dependent S-factor in the saddle-point equation, extending the standard Gamow peak formula. They evaluate this condition numerically using experimentally determined S-factors for four reactions: 11B(p,α)8Be, 10B(p,α)7Be, 3H(d,n)4He, and 2H(d,n)3He, comparing the resulting effective energy windows with the conventional Gamow peak approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 11B(p,α)8Be, two low-energy resonances are considered: at E_r = 148 keV (width Γ = 5.2 keV) and at E_r = 581.3 keV (width Γ = 300 keV)
- For the reaction 10B(p,α)7Be, an s-wave resonance is included at E_r = 9.1 keV with width Γ = 16 keV
- For the reaction 3H(d,n)4He, a resonance is considered at E_r = 50 keV with width Γ = 177 keV
- Plasma temperatures in laser-cluster fusion experiments are reported from 8 keV to 30 keV
- Laser-accelerated protons via TNSA mechanism can reach temperatures as high as 5 MeV
- The Texas Petawatt laser experiment used a peak intensity of 2 × 10^18 W/cm^2 in 1.5 ps pulses
- The ABC laser facility delivers 50 J in 3 ns
- Deuteron acceleration as high as 170 MeV has been reported from ultra-thin (300 nm) foil targets at the TRIDENT laser

## Context

This work extends the known result that the effective energy window for thermonuclear reactions can differ significantly from the Gamow peak, previously discussed for (p,γ) reactions by Newton (2007) and for proton, alpha, and neutron induced reactions by Rauscher (2010) at high temperatures (around 1 GK, i.e., k_BT = 86 keV) relevant to advanced stellar burning stages. The authors show that the same effect applies to laser-induced plasma environments, where temperatures from the TNSA and BOA acceleration mechanisms match explosive stellar conditions, enabling improved interpretation of fusion yield measurements and determination of low-energy cross sections from laser-plasma experiments.
