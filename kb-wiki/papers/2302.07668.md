# Impact of vorticity and viscosity on the hydrodynamic evolution of hot QCD medium

> The strongly interacting transient state of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium created in ultra-relativistic collisions survives for a duration of a few fm/c. The spacetime evolution of QGP crucially depends on the equation of state (EoS), vorticity, viscosity, and external magnetic field. In the present study, we obtain the lifetime of a vortical QGP fluid within the ambit of relativistic second-order viscous hydrodynamics. We observe that the coupling of vorticity and viscosity significantly increases the lifetime of vortical QGP. The inclusion of a static magnetic field, vorticity, and viscosity makes the evolution slower. However, the static magnetic field slightly decreases the QGP lifetime by accelerating the evolution process for a non-rotating medium. We also report the rate of change of vorticity in the QGP, which will be helpful in studying the behavior of the medium in detail.

## Key claim

The coupling of vorticity and viscosity significantly increases the lifetime of a vortical quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fluid, but this effect is conditional on the initial conditions: the cooling rate becomes almost independent of vorticity when the fluid rotates close to the speed of light or when the initial temperature is very high, causing the non-viscous rotating fluid to behave like an ideal fluid.

## Method

The authors solved three coupled non-linear differential equations derived from relativistic second-order (Muller-Israel-Stewart) viscous hydrodynamics, describing the proper-time evolution of temperature, shear viscous term (Phi), and vorticity (omega) for a QGP medium with zero baryon chemical potential. They used an ideal gas equation of state for massless u, d, s quarks and gluons, introduced vorticity through a modified Euler thermodynamic relation with a rotation chemical potential, and included a static magnetic field as an extension. The coupled equations were solved numerically for multiple sets of initial conditions (T0, tau0, omega0, Phi0) to study the individual and combined effects of vorticity and viscosity on medium cooling.

## Key numbers

*   Initial vorticity of omega = (9 +/- 1) x 10^21 sec^{-1} was estimated from global Lambda hyperon polarization measurements at RHIC, making QGP the most vortical fluid found in nature.
*   For initial conditions T0 = 0.35 GeV, tau0 = 0.2 fm, omega0 = 5 fm^{-1}, the cooling rate of the rotating non-viscous fluid (T^omega_SO) was approximately equal to the ideal fluid cooling rate (T_Ideal) initially up to tau ~ 2 fm.
*   For initial conditions T0 = 0.35 GeV, tau0 = 0.6 fm, omega0 = 1.0 fm^{-1}, Phi0 = 0.13334 GeV^4, the large value of tau0 reduced Phi0, leading to faster cooling for the second-order viscous fluid (T_SO).
*   For initial conditions T0 = 0.550 GeV, tau0 = 0.2 fm, omega0 = 5 fm^{-1}, Phi0 = 1.4785 GeV^4, the high initial vorticity effect almost vanished at the relatively high initial temperature, and the cooling for the non-viscous rotating fluid behaved like an ideal fluid.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic studies of QGP evolution by Singh (2018) that used an ideal equation of state and estimated the time evolution of non-relativistic vorticity, showing vorticity decreases as the system evolves. It also builds on the framework of second-order viscous hydrodynamics from Muronga (2001, 2003) and the Muller-Israel-Stewart theory. The results enable more detailed modeling of spin polarization phenomena such as the chiral vortical effect and provide a foundation for studying how vorticity and viscosity together affect the QGP lifetime and cooling rate in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
