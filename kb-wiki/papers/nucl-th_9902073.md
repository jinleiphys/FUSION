# Integral representation of the RPA correlation energy

> Using the spectral function F'(z)/F(z) the RPA correlation energy and other properties of a finite system can be written as a contour integral in a compact way. This yields a transparent expression and reduces drastically the numerical efforts for obtaining reliable values. The method applied to pairing vibrations in rotating nuclei as an illustrative example.

## Key claim

The RPA correlation energy can be computed via a contour integral of the spectral function \(F'(z)/F(z)\), which drastically reduces the numerical effort (by a factor of \(10^2\) in integration points) compared to direct summation over the many closely spaced RPA roots, while also correctly accounting for spurious modes.

## Method

The authors express the RPA correlation energy as a contour integral in the complex plane using Cauchy's theorem applied to the spectral function \(S(z) = F'(z)/F(z)\), where \(F(z)\) is the determinant \( \det(1 - R^0(z)\chi) \) from the RPA response function. The integration contour encloses all positive RPA eigenfrequencies \(\omega_\nu\) and the two-quasiparticle poles \(E_\mu\), allowing the sum \(\frac{1}{2}(\sum \omega_\nu - \sum E_\mu)\) to be evaluated numerically along a rectangular path far from the poles, where the integrand is smooth. The method is demonstrated for pairing vibrations in the rotating nucleus \(^{178}\text{W}\) using the tilted axis cranking (TAC) model with a separable pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- The number of integration points is reduced by a factor of \(10^2\) without loss of precision, drastically reducing CPU time.
- For heavy nuclei (\(A \approx 150\)), the number of RPA roots is of order \(10^4\), none of which can be neglected.
- The method is applied to the g-band of \(^{178}\text{W}\), where the RPA correlation energy substantially lowers the TAC Routhian energy and improves the description compared to TAC with particle number projection.

## Context

This work extends the method of Shlomo (Year not given) for calculating the rotational dependence of the correlation energy, which did not treat spurious modes and required a dense grid for convergence. The contour integral approach remedies these deficiencies and provides a transparent expression applicable to finite quantum systems such as nuclei and metallic clusters. The method enables efficient calculation of RPA correlation energies and other integral characteristics (e.g., aligned angular momentum) for realistic systems, with a forthcoming paper by Almehed et al. planned for comprehensive analysis.
