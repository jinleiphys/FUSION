# Hadron widths in mixed-phase matter

> We derive classically an expression for a hadron width in a two-phase region of hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The presence of QGP gives hadrons larger widths than they would have in a pure hadron gas. We find that the $ϕ$ width observed in a central Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV/nucleon is a few MeV greater than the width in a pure hadron gas. The part of observed hadron widths due to QGP is approximately proportional to $(dN/dy)^{-1/3}$.

# Hadron widths in mixed-phase matter

## Key claim

The observed width of a hadron in a mixed-phase region of hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is larger than in a pure hadron gas, but the increase is modest: for the phi meson in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV/nucleon, the additional width is only a few MeV, and the result depends on assumptions about the droplet distribution (parameter y_d) and the critical temperature T_c.

## Method

The authors derive a classical expression for the hadron width in a static mixed-phase region by modeling the matter as spherical QGP droplets embedded in hadronic matter. They compute the hadron decay rate per unit four-volume and the hadron density per unit volume in the mixed phase, then obtain the width as their ratio. The QGP contribution is estimated using two different expressions for the phi decay rate in QGP: one from Pisarski (1993) treating the phi as an s-anti-s pair with thermal momenta, and another from the heavy quark damping rate limit. These are combined with a one-dimensional, boost-invariant hydrodynamic collision simulation to compute the time-integrated observed width.

## Key numbers

- For T_c = 150 MeV, the increase in phi width Gamma^m - Gamma^h is 1.1 MeV with y_d = 1 and 2.3 MeV with y_d = 0.1
- For T_c = 190 MeV, the increase in phi width Gamma^m - Gamma^h is 1.5 MeV with y_d = 1 and 3.1 MeV with y_d = 0.1
- Using the heavy quark damping rate expression for gamma^q(k), the increase is 1.1 MeV (y_d=1) and 2.4 MeV (y_d=0.1) for T_c=150 MeV, and 1.5 MeV (y_d=1) and 3.3 MeV (y_d=0.1) for T_c=190 MeV
- In the infinite gamma^q limit, the increase is 1.2 MeV (y_d=1) and 2.5 MeV (y_d=0.1) for T_c=150 MeV, and 1.6 MeV (y_d=1) and 3.4 MeV (y_d=0.1) for T_c=190 MeV
- The hadronic gas phi width is about 6 MeV at T=150 MeV and 9 MeV at T=190 MeV (from Ko and Seibert, in press)
- The QGP contribution to the hadron width scales approximately as (dN/dy)^{-1/3}

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of light vector mesons in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, particularly the use of rho and phi mesons to determine the transition temperature to QGP (Seibert 1992; Asakawa and Ko, in press; Ko and Seibert, in press). Unlike previous work that considered only hadronic matter effects, this paper is the first to include the effect of QGP droplets on observed hadron properties. The result enables experimental searches for the QGP contribution by comparing phi widths in events with different total rapidity densities dN/dy, using satellite vector meson peaks in the dilepton spectrum as proposed by Asakawa and Ko. The authors note that the increased width is not an unambiguous QGP signal, as any high-temperature phase with short hadron lifetimes would produce the same effect.
