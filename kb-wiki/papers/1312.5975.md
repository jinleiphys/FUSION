# Tables of E2 Transition Probabilities from the first $2^{+}$ States in Even-Even Nuclei

> Experimental results of E2 transition probabilities or B(E2) values for the known first 2$^{+}$ states in 447 even-even nuclei have been compiled and evaluated. The evaluation policies for the analysis of experimental data have been described and new results are discussed. The recommended B(E2) values have been compared with comprehensive shell model calculations for a selected set of nuclei, where such theoretical procedures are amenable. The present work was motivated by a rapid increase in the number of new B(E2) measurements for the first 2$^{+}$ states since the previous evaluation of such data by S. Raman {\it et al.} published in 2001. Future plans to investigate the systematics of B(E2)$\uparrow$ values, and intercomparison of different experimental techniques to obtain these data are outlined.

## Key claim

This paper compiles and evaluates experimental B(E2) transition probabilities for the first 2⁺ states in 447 even-even nuclei, updating the previous evaluation by Raman et al. (2001) with new measurements that have rapidly accumulated since that earlier work; the recommended values are compared with shell model calculations only for a selected set of nuclei where such theoretical procedures are amenable, not for the full dataset.

## Method

The authors searched the Nuclear Science References (NSR), Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF), and Experimental Unevaluated Nuclear Data List (XUNDL) databases for experimental B(E2), lifetime, and deformation parameter data, verifying results against the previous evaluation by Raman et al. (2001) and original experimental papers. They compiled the data into a table extending the earlier list, including target, beam, beam energy, and annotations for beam energies exceeding the Coulomb barrier, and applied evaluation policies to produce recommended values. The recommended B(E2) values were then compared with comprehensive shell model calculations for a selected subset of nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the compilation and evaluation of B(E2) values for first 2⁺ states in even-even nuclei originally begun by Stelson and Grodzins (1965) and continued by Raman et al. (1987, 2001), incorporating the rapid increase in new measurements from radioactive beam facilities worldwide over the last 10-15 years. The evaluated data provide essential input for nuclear reaction model codes such as EMPIRE and TALYS used in ENDF evaluations, and for the Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL); the comparison with shell model calculations tests theoretical descriptions of nuclear structure phenomena including shell closure evolution, the island of inversion, and the onset of collectivity near driplines. The authors outline future plans to investigate the systematics of B(E2)↑ values and to intercompare different experimental techniques for obtaining these data.
