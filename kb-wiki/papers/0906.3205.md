# Crossover to Cluster Plasma in the Gas of Quark-Gluon Bags

> We study a smooth crossover transition in the gas of quark-gluon bags. The equation of state at high temperature is that of the quark-gluon plasma. However, the system consists of the bags with finite volumes which are defined by the model parameters of the mass-volume bag spectrum. Possible structures in this {\it cluster} quark-gluon plasma are classified.

## Key claim

The paper shows that even when the gas of quark-gluon bags undergoes a smooth crossover (no phase transition) and its equation of state at high temperature matches that of an ideal quark-gluon plasma, the system can consist of finite-sized clusters rather than a uniform plasma, with the average bag volume remaining finite at high temperature depending on the model parameters gamma and delta.

## Method

The authors study the statistical mechanics of a gas of quark-gluon bags using the partition function with excluded volume effects, where the mass-volume spectrum of bags follows the bag model form with parameters gamma, delta, and bag constant B. They analyze the Laplace transform of the partition function to find the pressure from the farthest-right singularity, and classify the behavior of the system based on the values of gamma and delta in the mass-volume spectrum. The average bag volume at high temperature is calculated using steepest descent and asymptotic expansions of incomplete gamma functions.

## Key numbers

- The validity limits for the asymptotic mass-volume spectrum are estimated as V0 approximately 1 fm^3 and M0 approximately 2 GeV.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann constant sigma_Q = 95 pi^2 / 60 counts gluons and quarks (u, d, s flavors) inside the bag.
- The condition for phase transitions to occur is gamma + delta < -3 and delta < -7/4 (region 3c in the phase diagram).
- For cases 1a, 2a, 3a (delta > -7/4): Delta s(T) ~ ln T, and average volume approaches V0 at high temperature.
- For cases 1c (gamma+delta > -3, delta < -7/4): Delta s(T) ~ T^{(7+4delta)/a} and average volume ~ T^{-(7+4delta)/a}, going to infinity.
- For cases 2c (gamma+delta = -3, delta < -7/4): Delta s(T) ~ exp(-T^{-7-4delta}) and average volume ~ exp(T^{-7-4delta}), going to infinity.

## Context

This work extends the gas of quark-gluon bags model previously developed by the authors and others (e.g., Gorenstein, Greiner, and collaborators in the 1980s-2000s) which demonstrated first, second, and higher order phase transitions. It is inspired by the suggestion of Ferroni (2009) to model a smooth crossover transition with this approach. The results classify possible high-temperature states of the system when no phase transition occurs, showing that a "cluster quark-gluon plasma" with finite-sized bags can exist, which may have different transport properties (shear and bulk viscosity) compared to an ideal quark-gluon plasma, relevant for understanding matter produced at RHIC and LHC in the crossover region of the QCD phase diagram.
