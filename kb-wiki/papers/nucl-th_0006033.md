# Properties of superdeformed fission isomers in the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> The rotational and deformation properties of superdeformed fission isomers in the $A\sim 240$ mass region have been investigated within the framework of the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. The dependence of the results of the calculations on the parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian has been studied. The rotational properties are best described by the NL1 force.

## Key claim

The cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, using the NL1 force for the RMF Lagrangian, best describes the rotational properties of superdeformed fission isomers in the A∼240 mass region, but the uncertainties in experimental charge quadrupole moments prevent using that observable to select the best RMF force.

## Method

The authors applied the recently developed CRHB theory, solving cranked RMF equations at the Hartree-Bogoliubov level with the finite-range D1S Gogny force in the particle-particle channel and approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method (APNP(LN)). Calculations were performed in three-dimensional Cartesian coordinates for even-even nuclei 236,238U and 240Pu, considering only reflection-symmetric shapes. The dependence of results on the parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian was studied by comparing the NLSH, NL3, NL1, and NL-Z forces.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic moment of inertia J(1) for 240Pu at Ωx=0.01 MeV: NL1 with APNP(LN) gives 155.2 MeV−1, NL3 gives 144.4 MeV−1, experiment is 149.3 MeV−1
- For 236U at Ωx=0.01 MeV: NL1 gives J(1)=150.5 MeV−1, experiment is 149.25 MeV−1
- For 238U at Ωx=0.01 MeV: NL1 gives J(1)=154.7 MeV−1, experiment is 153.06 MeV−1
- Charge quadrupole moment Q0 for 240Pu: NL1 gives 38.2 e b, NL3 gives 35.5 e b
- Neutron pairing energy in 240Pu with NL1: −1.823 MeV without APNP(LN), −13.129 MeV with APNP(LN)
- Proton pairing energy in 240Pu with NL1: −10.030 MeV without APNP(LN), −13.603 MeV with APNP(LN)
- Rigid body moment of inertia for 240Pu: 211.7 MeV−1

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field studies of fission isomers in 226Ra, 232Th, and 240Pu by the Frankfurt group (Rutz et al., 1995; Bender et al., 1999), which used BCS approximation with constant gap parameters. The CRHB theory had previously been successful in describing superdeformed bands in the A∼190 region (Afanasjev et al., 1999) and rare-earth nuclei. The results enable systematic study of superdeformed fission isomer properties without adjustable parameters, using the well-established D1S Gogny force for pairing correlations.
