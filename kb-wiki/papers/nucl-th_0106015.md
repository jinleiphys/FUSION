# Extracting the spectral function of He-4 from a relativistic plane-wave treatment

> The spectral function of He-4 is extracted from a plane-wave approximation to the (e,e'p) reaction using a fully relativistic formalism. We take advantage of both an algebraic ``trick'' and a general relativistic formalism for quasifree processes developed earlier to arrive at transparent, analytical expressions for all quasifree (e,e'p) observables. An observable is identified for the clean and model-independent extraction of the spectral function. Our simple relativistic plane-wave calculations provide baseline predictions for the recently measured, but not yet fully analyzed, momentum distribution of He-4 by the A1-collaboration from Mainz. Yet in spite of its simplicity, our approach predicts momentum distributions for He-4 that rival some of the best nonrelativistic calculations to date. Finally, we highlight some of the challenges and opportunities that remain, both theoretically and experimentally, in the extraction of quasifree observables.

## Key claim
The spectral function of He-4 can be extracted from the longitudinal response in (e,e'p) reactions with much weaker dependence on off-shell ambiguities and negative-energy state contributions than the conventional factorization approach, though the method requires a Rosenbluth separation and remains a plane-wave approximation that neglects final-state distortions.

## Method
The authors employed a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) formalism for the (e,e'p) reaction on He-4, using a mean-field approximation to the Walecka model. They exploited an algebraic trick that casts the bound-state propagator into a form identical in structure to the free Feynman propagator, enabling analytic trace calculations of all quasifree observables via FeynCalc. Gauge invariance was imposed ad-hoc by adjusting the effective nucleon mass of the emitted proton by about 20 MeV to minimize the gauge-variance term.

## Key numbers
- The longitudinal response quenching in He-4 from quasielastic (e,e') measurements was reported as 14%, with a larger 20-40% quenching reported in the semi-exclusive (e,e'p) reaction at quasielastic kinematics.
- The conventional cc1 prescription of de Forest for the elementary cross section overestimates the proton momentum distribution, integrating to 3.6 instead of the expected 2 for the two protons in the He-4 shell.
- The momentum distribution extracted using the factorization approximation with the cc2 form integrates to 2.9 rather than 2, representing a discrepancy of 45 percent.
- Distortion effects are estimated at about 12% but cannot account for the full 50-60% discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental data.
- High-quality data for the momentum distribution of He-4 is available up to a missing momentum of about 200 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic formalism for quasifree processes developed by Gardner and Piekarewicz (1994) and provides baseline predictions for the A1-collaboration data from Mainz (Florizone 1998, 2001; Kozlov 2000, 2001a, 2001b). The results are compared to nonrelativistic variational calculations by Schiavilla and collaborators (1986) using Urbana and Argonne potentials, and to Wiringa and collaborators (1991, 2001) using the Argonne v18 potential with Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. The approach enables a more robust extraction of the spectral function that is insensitive to off-shell ambiguities and negative-energy state contributions, though it requires experimental Rosenbluth separation of the cross section.
