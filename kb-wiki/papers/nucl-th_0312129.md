# Energy averages over regular and chaotic states in the decay out of superdeformed bands

> We describe the decay out of a superdeformed band using the methods of reaction theory. Assuming that decay-out occurs due to equal coupling (on average) to a sea of equivalent chaotic normally deformed (ND) states, we calculate the average intraband decay intensity and show that it can be written as an ``optical'' background term plus a fluctuation term, in total analogy with average nuclear cross sections. We also calculate the variance in closed form. We investigate how these objects are modified when the decay to the ND states occurs via an ND doorway and the ND states' statistical properties are changed from chaotic to regular. We show that the average decay intensity depends on two dimensionless variables in the first case while in the second case, four variables enter the picture.

## Key claim

The average intraband decay intensity of a superdeformed band can be decomposed into an optical background term plus a fluctuation term, analogous to average nuclear cross sections; the average depends on two dimensionless variables when the normally deformed (ND) states are chaotic (GOE) and on four variables when the ND states are regular (Poisson) and decay proceeds via an ND doorway.

## Method

The authors apply reaction theory to model the decay out of a superdeformed (SD) band, assuming equal coupling on average of an SD state to a sea of equivalent chaotic normally deformed (ND) states described by the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE). They calculate the energy average of the intraband decay intensity and its variance in closed form, then extend the model to the case where the ND states obey Poisson statistics and the SD state couples to a single ND doorway state, deriving the average background decay intensity as a function of four dimensionless variables.

## Key numbers

* The average background contribution to the decay intensity for the GOE case is \(I_{\rm av}^{\rm GOE} = (1 + \Gamma^\downarrow / \Gamma_S)^{-1}\), where \(\Gamma^\downarrow\) is the spreading width for mixing of an SD state with ND states and \(\Gamma_S\) is the electromagnetic decay width of the SD state.
* The average fluctuation contribution to the decay intensity for the GOE case is \(\overline{I_{\rm fl}} = 2 (\pi \Gamma_N / D)^{-1} (\Gamma^\downarrow / \Gamma_S)^2 / (1 + \Gamma^\downarrow / \Gamma_S)^3\), where \(\Gamma_N\) is the electromagnetic decay width of ND states and \(D\) is the mean level spacing of ND states.
* The variance of the decay intensity for the GOE case is \(\overline{(\Delta I)^2} = \overline{I_{\rm fl}}^2 f_1(\xi) + 2 I_{\rm av} \overline{I_{\rm fl}} f_2(\xi)\), where \(\xi \equiv (\Gamma_S + \Gamma^\downarrow) / \Gamma_N\), \(f_1(\xi) = 1/(1+\xi) + \xi/(1+\xi)^2 + \xi^2/[2(1+\xi)^3]\), and \(f_2(\xi) = 1/[2(1+\xi)]\).
* In the Poisson limit with an ND doorway, the average background decay intensity \(I_{\rm av}^{\rm Poi}\) depends on four dimensionless variables: \(\Gamma^\downarrow / \Gamma_S\), \(\Gamma_N / D\), \(\Gamma_S / D\), and \(\Delta / D\), where \(\Delta = E_d - E_0\) is the energy separation between the doorway state and the SD state.

## Context

This work extends the reaction-theory approach to SD band decay-out developed by Gu and Weidenmüller (1999) and Sargeant et al. (2002), who treated the ND states as chaotic (GOE). The authors incorporate the possibility of regular (Poisson) ND states and an ND doorway, following the chaos-assisted tunneling model of Åberg (1999). The results enable experimental determination of both the spreading width \(\Gamma^\downarrow\) and the ND level spacing \(D\) from measurements of the average decay intensity and its variance, and provide a framework to test whether an order-chaos transition in the ND minimum can explain the sudden depopulation of SD bands.
