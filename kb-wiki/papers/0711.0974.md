# Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC - Last Call for Predictions

> This writeup is a compilation of the predictions for the forthcoming Heavy Ion Program at the Large Hadron Collider, as presented at the CERN Theory Institute 'Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC - Last Call for Predictions', held from May 14th to June 10th 2007.

## Key claim

The paper compiles pre-LHC predictions for heavy-ion collisions from multiple theoretical approaches, with the explicit caveat that these are "last call" estimates made before LHC data became available to test them.

## Method

The contributions employ a variety of theoretical frameworks: a logistic equation model for rapidity distributions (Abreu et al.), the DPMJET-III Monte Carlo with chain fusion (Bopp et al.), string percolation models for transverse momentum spectra (Cunqueiro et al.), pQCD+saturation+hydrodynamics (EKRT model) for multiplicities and elliptic flow (Eskola et al.), Color Glass Condensate formalism with running coupling for nuclear modification factors (Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos), and modified fragmentation functions for photon and pion production (Jeon et al.). Each group applies its framework to predict observables for Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb (0-10% central) at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV: predicted dN_ch/deta = 2570 from pQCD+saturation+hydro model (Eskola et al.)
- Pb-Pb (0-10% central) at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV: predicted total dN/dy = 4730, dE_T/dy = 4070 GeV (Eskola et al.)
- Pb-Pb (0-10% central) at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV: predicted net-baryon number dN_B/dy = 3.11, p/bar-p ratio = 0.98 (Eskola et al.)
- Saturation scale for central Pb+Pb at LHC: p_sat approximately 2 GeV (Eskola et al.)
- Logistic model parameters from RHIC Au-Au fit: lambda=0.247, alpha=0.269, delta=0.67 (Abreu et al.)
- p-Pb (0-20% central) at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 TeV: predicted rapidity shift y_c = -2.08 (Abreu et al.)
- String percolation baryon enhancement parameter: alpha=0.09 fixed from bar-p/pi dependence on N_part (Cunqueiro et al.)
- Running coupling saturation momentum parameters: Lambda=0.2 GeV, B=2.25, Y_0=4; initial Q_s^2(A,Y_0)=1.5 GeV^2 for nucleus, Q_s^2(p,Y_0)=0.25 GeV^2 for proton (Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos)
- Universal behavior extrapolation: predicted (dN_ch/deta)_0 = 6.5-6.9 per participant pair for 6% central Au-Au at LHC (Jeon et al.)
- Minimum bias v_2 of pions at LHC predicted to reach values as high as 0.2 (Eskola et al.)

## Context

This compilation extends the RHIC-era theoretical work to LHC energies, with many contributions building on models previously tested against RHIC data from experiments such as STAR and PHENIX. The EKRT model (Eskola et al.) had successfully predicted charged particle multiplicities at RHIC and now extends to LHC conditions. The string percolation approach (Cunqueiro et al.) and DPMJET-III (Bopp et al.) apply frameworks developed for lower energies to predict the new energy regime. The running coupling CGC analysis (Iancu and Triantafyllopoulos) predicts near-total shadowing at forward rapidities, contradicting fixed-coupling expectations. The universal behavior analysis (Jeon et al.) identifies two universal curves in RHIC data and extrapolates to LHC, providing a benchmark for testing limiting fragmentation at higher energies. These predictions enabled comparison with early LHC data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS when the heavy-ion program began.
