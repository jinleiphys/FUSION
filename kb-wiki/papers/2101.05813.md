# Remarks on nuclear matter: how an $ω_0$ condensate can spike the speed of sound, and a model of $Z(3)$ baryons

> I make two comments about nuclear matter. First, I consider the effects of a coupling between the $O(4)$ chiral field, $\vecϕ$, and the $ω_μ$ meson, $\sim + \, ω_μ^2 \, \vecϕ^{\, 2}$; for any net baryon density, a condensate for $ω_0$ is unavoidably generated. I assume that with increasing density, a decrease of the chiral condensate and the effective $ω_0$ mass gives a stiff equation of state (EoS). In order to match that onto a soft EoS for quarkyonic matter, I consider an $O(N)$ field at large $N$, where at nonzero temperature quantum fluctuations disorder any putative pion "condensate" into a quantum pion liquid (Q$π$L) (arXiv:2005.10259). In this paper I show that the Q$π$L persists at zero temperature. If valid qualitatively at $N=4$, the $ω_0$ mass goes up sharply and suppresses the $ω_0$ condensate. This could generate a spike in the speed of sound at high density, which is of relevance to neutron stars. Second, I propose a toy model of a $Z(3)$ gauge theory with three flavors of fermions, where $Z(3)$ vortices confine fermions into baryons. In $1+1$ dimensions this model can be studied numerically with present techniques, using either classical or quantum computers.

# Remarks on nuclear matter: how an ω₀ condensate can spike the speed of sound, and a model of Z(3) baryons

## Key claim

The paper proposes qualitatively that a condensate of the ω₀ meson, generated unavoidably at any nonzero baryon density, can stiffen the nuclear equation of state at intermediate densities, but that quantum fluctuations in a quantum pion liquid (QπL) phase can then sharply increase the ω₀ mass and suppress its condensate, producing a spike in the speed of sound at high density relevant to neutron stars. The second part suggests a toy Z(3) gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions where Z(3) vortices confine three flavors of fermions into baryons, which can be studied numerically with present techniques.

## Method

The author analyzes an O(N) chiral field φ coupled to the ω_μ meson via a term ∼ κ² ω_μ² φ², working in the large-N limit at zero temperature. The effective action is derived by introducing constraint fields and integrating out φ and ξ, then solving the stationary point equations for the effective mass and the ω₀ condensate. For the Z(3) model, a Lagrangian is constructed with an Abelian gauge field, a charge-3 scalar for spontaneous symmetry breaking, and three degenerate fermion flavors of unit charge, where Z(3) vortices in the vacuum confine fermions through Aharonov-Bohm phases.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum ω meson mass is given by m_ω² = m̃_ω² + κ² σ₀², where σ₀ = f_π ≈ 92 MeV; at tree level with massless pions, m̃_ω = 0 when κ² = m_ω/f_π ∼ 8.4.
- In the large-N solution as Z → −∞, the effective mass shift δm_eff is exponentially suppressed: δm_eff ∼ √(−Z) M exp(− (2^(3/2) π²)/(λ N) (−Z)^(3/2)).
- The ω₀ mass in the QπL phase becomes m_ω² = m̃_ω² + Z² (κ²/(4λ)) M², and the ω₀ condensate scales as ω̂₀ ∼ 1/Z².
- Drischler et al. (2020) find that for a 2.6 solar mass neutron star, at some baryon density the speed of sound squared c_s² ∼ 0.55, and the equation of state must be soft until n_B ∼ 1.5–1.8 n_sat.
- Leonhardt et al. (2019) find evidence for a spike in the speed of sound at ∼ 10 n_sat using functional renormalization group methods.

## Context

This work extends the quantum pion liquid (QπL) concept from Pisarski (2020) at nonzero temperature to zero temperature, showing the QπL persists but with exponential rather than power-law suppression of the effective mass. The analysis builds on Quantum HadroDynamics (Serot and Walecka, 1984, 1995, 1997) and parity-doubled QHD (Detar and Kunihiro, 1988; Jido et al., 2001; Zschiesche et al., 2006; Gallas et al., 2009; Steinheimer et al., 2011; Eser et al., 2015; Lakaschus et al., 2018; Suenaga et al., 2019; Marczenko et al., 2018, 2020; Cao and Liao, 2020; Minamikawa et al., 2020), and addresses the need for a stiff equation of state at intermediate densities followed by a soft quarkyonic equation of state (McLerran and Reddy, 2007; Kojo et al., 2009, 2010, 2011; McLerran and Reddy, 2018; Jeong et al., 2019; Duarte et al., 2020; Sen et al., 2020; Zhao et al., 2020). The Z(3) baryon model is a simplified analog of 't Hooft's Z(N) vortex confinement mechanism (1977, 1979), intended for numerical study on classical or quantum computers in 1+1 dimensions.
