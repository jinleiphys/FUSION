# Direct study of the alpha-nucleus optical potential at astrophysical energies using the 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu reaction

> In the model calculations of heavy element nucleosynthesis processes the nuclear reaction rates are taken from statistical model calculations which utilize various nuclear input parameters. It is found that in the case of reactions involving alpha particles the calculations bear a high uncertainty owing to the largely unknown low energy alpha-nucleus optical potential. Experiments are typically restricted to higher energies and therefore no direct astrophysical consequences can be drawn. In the present work a (p,alpha) reaction is used for the first time to study the alpha-nucleus optical potential. The measured 64Zn(p,alpha)61Cu cross section is uniquely sensitive to the alpha-nucleus potential and the measurement covers the whole astrophysically relevant energy range. By the comparison to model calculations, direct evidence is provided for the incorrectness of global optical potentials used in astrophysical models.

## Key claim

The measured cross section of the 64Zn(p,α)61Cu reaction provides the first direct evidence that global α-nucleus optical potentials used in astrophysical models are incorrect at astrophysically relevant energies, but this evidence is strictly limited to the 61Cu+α system; the authors note that further study is required to generalize the conclusion.

## Method

The cross section of the 64Zn(p,α)61Cu reaction was measured using the activation method with proton beams from the Atomki cyclotron in the energy range 3.5–8 MeV, covering the entire astrophysically relevant Gamow window for the inverse 61Cu+α system. Thin 64Zn targets (99.71% enrichment) were bombarded, and the induced γ-activity of the radioactive 61Cu product (half-life 3.33 h) was measured with a calibrated HPGe detector. The measured S-factors were compared to statistical model calculations using the TALYS and NON-SMOKER codes with various global α-nucleus optical potentials.

## Key numbers

* The 64Zn(p,α)61Cu cross section at the lowest measured energy (Ec.m. = 3.44 ± 0.03 MeV) is 0.333 ± 0.075 μbarn.
* At the highest measured energy (Ec.m. = 7.88 ± 0.08 MeV), the cross section is 23746 ± 4894 μbarn.
* The deviation between measured cross sections and statistical model predictions (using the standard McFadden and Satchler optical potential) reaches a factor of about five to ten at the lowest astrophysical energies.
* The astrophysical reaction rate for 61Cu(α,γ)65Ga calculated with the McFadden and Satchler potential and the Demetriou et al. potential differs by a factor of five at 3.5 GK γ-process temperature, and by up to one order of magnitude at 2 GK.
* The 64Zn(p,γ)65Ga cross section at Ec.m. = 3.44 ± 0.03 MeV is 331 ± 71 μbarn; at Ec.m. = 7.88 ± 0.08 MeV it is 658 ± 146 μbarn.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to test statistical model predictions for α-induced reactions relevant to the γ-process (p-process), where earlier measurements of (α,γ) cross sections on heavy isotopes showed deviations of up to an order of magnitude but were limited to energies above the astrophysically relevant range (Rauscher 2013). By using a (p,α) reaction for the first time in this context, the authors avoid complications from direct reaction channels like Coulomb excitation that could affect α-induced measurements (Rauscher 2013b). The result enables direct experimental constraint of the α-nucleus optical potential at astrophysical energies for the 61Cu+α system, and the method can be applied to other nuclei to improve γ-process network calculations.
