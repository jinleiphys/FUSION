# Effects of Coulomb and isospin symmetry breaking interactions on neutron-skin thickness

> Both the Coulomb interaction and isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) parts of the nuclear interaction break the isospin symmetry in atomic nuclei. Effects of these two kinds of interaction on properties of atomic nuclei, especially, the mass difference of mirror nuclei and the neutron-skin thickness of $ N = Z $ and $ N \ne Z $ nuclei, are discussed. It is found that corrections to the Hartree-Fock-Slater approximation for the Coulomb interaction negligibly affect the neutron-skin thickness, while the charge-symmetry breaking term originating from the strong interaction might affect it non-negligibly. According to our calculations, the ISB terms other than the Coulomb interaction affect the estimation of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, $ L $, by about $ 0 $--$ 12 \, \mathrm{MeV} $ using the correlation with the neutron-skin thickness.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction and isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) terms of the nuclear interaction both break isospin symmetry in nuclei, but corrections to the Hartree-Fock-Slater approximation for the Coulomb interaction have a negligible effect on neutron-skin thickness, while the charge-symmetry breaking term from the strong interaction may affect it non-negligibly; the ISB terms other than Coulomb affect the estimated density dependence of the symmetry energy, L, by about 0–12 MeV when using the correlation with neutron-skin thickness.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent nuclear density functional theory calculations using Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs), specifically the SAMi, SAMi-J, and SAMi-ISB families, including both Coulomb and ISB (charge-symmetry breaking and charge-independence breaking) terms in the EDF. They computed the neutron-skin thickness, charge radii differences, and mass differences of mirror nuclei for N=Z and N≠Z nuclei, and analyzed the correlation between neutron-skin thickness and the density dependence of the symmetry energy L.

## Key numbers

- The ISB terms other than the Coulomb interaction affect the estimation of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, L, by about 0–12 MeV using the correlation with neutron-skin thickness.
- The saturation density ρ_sat itself is changed due to the charge-independence breaking (CIB) term, as shown in the equation for P(ρ_sat, 0) = 0.
- The pressure of pure neutron matter at saturation density is given by P(ρ_sat, 1) = (L + L^CIB + L^CSB) ρ_sat / 3.

## Context

This work extends previous sensitivity studies of nuclear equation of state parameters to ISB interactions, building on the authors' earlier Letter (Naito 2022) that discussed ISB effects on charge-radii differences of mirror nuclei and the estimation of L. It also relates to prior work by Roca-Maza (2018) on Skyrme ISB interactions and by Baczyk (2018, 2019, 2021) on including ISB terms in Skyrme EDFs. The results enable more precise determinations of the symmetry energy density dependence from neutron-skin measurements by accounting for ISB corrections beyond the Coulomb interaction.
