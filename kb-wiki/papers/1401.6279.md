# Superdeformed structures and low $Ω$ parity doublet in Ne$-$S nuclei near neutron drip-line

> The structure of Ne, Na, Mg, Al, Si, P and S nuclei near the neutron drip-line region is investigated in the frame-work of relativistic mean field theory and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock formalism. The recently discovered nuclei $^{40}$Mg and $^{42}$Al, which are beyond the drip-line predicted by various mass formulae are located within these models. We find many largely deformed neutron-rich nuclei, whose structures are analyzed. From the structure anatomy, we find that at large deformation, low $Ω$ orbits of opposite parities (e.g. $\frac{1}{2}^+$ and $\frac{1}{2}^-$) occur close to each other in energy.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the recently discovered neutron-rich nuclei $^{40}$Mg and $^{42}$Al, which were predicted to be beyond the neutron drip-line by various mass formulae (Moller 1995, Samyn 2004), are actually bound and well within the drip-line when calculated using relativistic mean field (RMF) and Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) formalisms, provided pairing correlations are omitted.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 parameter set and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SkI4 parameter set for Ne, Na, Mg, Al, Si, P, and S isotopes near the neutron drip-line. They solved the field equations self-consistently by expanding wavefunctions in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis with up to 12 major oscillator quanta, and determined ground states by comparing prolate, oblate, and spherical solutions. Pairing correlations were omitted after tests showed that including them worsened agreement with experimental quadrupole deformations for light nuclei in this mass region.

## Key numbers

* $^{40}$Mg is predicted to be bound with binding energies of 269.7 MeV (RMF/NL3) and 262.8 MeV (SHF/SkI4), compared to the experimental value of 260.8 MeV for $^{36}$Mg.
* $^{42}$Al is predicted to be bound with binding energies of 291.2 MeV (RMF/NL3) and 286.2 MeV (SHF/SkI4), compared to the experimental value of 280.3 MeV for $^{38}$Al.
* The neutron drip-line for Mg is predicted at mass number 40 by both RMF and SHF, matching the infinite nuclear matter (INM) prediction and exceeding the finite range droplet model (FRDM) prediction of 40.
* For Ne, the drip-line is predicted at mass number 34 by both models, while the experimentally heaviest known isotope is $^{31}$Ne with binding energy 211.4 MeV (experiment), 216.0 MeV (RMF), and 213.2 MeV (SHF).
* For S, the drip-line is predicted at mass number 55 by both models, while the experimentally heaviest known isotope is $^{45}$S with binding energy 354.7 MeV (experiment), 353.4 MeV (RMF), and 350.4 MeV (SHF).
* Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for $^{20}$Ne is calculated as 0.535 (RMF) and 0.550 (SHF), compared to the experimental value of 0.727.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-rich light nuclei beyond the previously established drip-line predictions of mass models such as the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (1995) and the infinite nuclear matter (INM) model. The discovery of $^{40}$Mg and $^{42}$Al by Baumann (2007) motivated this re-examination, as those nuclei were considered neutron-unbound by earlier mass formulae. The results suggest that existing mass models may need modification for very neutron-rich light nuclei, and the predicted drip-lines provide targets for future experimental searches. The finding of low-$\Omega$ parity doublets at large deformation (e.g., $\frac{1}{2}^+$ and $\frac{1}{2}^-$ orbits close in energy) opens the possibility of studying parity doubling in superdeformed neutron-rich nuclei.
