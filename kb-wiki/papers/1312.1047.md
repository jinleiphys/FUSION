# Nonlocalized cluster dynamics and nuclear molecular structure

> A container picture is proposed for understanding cluster dynamics where the clusters make nonlocalized motion occupying the lowest orbit of the cluster mean-field potential characterized by the size parameter $``B"$ in the THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave function. The nonlocalized cluster aspects of the inversion-doublet bands in $^{20}$Ne which have been considered as a typical manifestation of localized clustering are discussed. So far unexplained puzzling features of the THSR wave function, namely that after angular-momentum projection for two cluster systems the prolate THSR wave function is almost 100$\%$ equivalent to an oblate THSR wave function is clarified. It is shown that the true intrinsic two-cluster THSR configuration is nonetheless prolate. The proposal of the container picture is based on the fact that typical cluster systems, 2$α$, 3$α$, and $α$+$^{16}$O, are all well described by a single THSR wave function. It will be shown for the case of linear-chain states with two and three $α$-clusters as well as for the $α$+$^{16}$O system that localization is entirely of kinematical origin, that is, due to the inter-cluster Pauli repulsion. It is concluded that this feature is general for nuclear cluster states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "container picture" for nuclear cluster dynamics, arguing that clusters make nonlocalized motion occupying the lowest orbit of a cluster mean-field potential, and that apparent localized clustering (such as the inversion-doublet bands in 20Ne) is actually of kinematical origin due to inter-cluster Pauli repulsion rather than genuine spatial localization.

## Method

The authors use the hybrid-Brink-THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave function, which combines the Brink model with the THSR wave function through parameters β (size parameter) and S (inter-cluster distance), to describe the α+16O system in 20Ne. They perform variational calculations with angular-momentum projection for states of the inversion-doublet bands (Jπ = 0+, 2+, 4+, 1-, 3-, 5-), minimizing energies in the two-parameter space of βx=βy and βz with Sz set to zero. They then compare single THSR-type wave functions to full Brink-GCM (generator coordinate method) solutions by computing squared overlaps.

## Key numbers

- The minimum energy for the intrinsic wave function of 20Ne is -159.66 MeV at Sz=0 and βx=βy=βz=1.8 fm.
- For the Jπ=0+ state, the minimum energy -159.66 MeV appears at Sz=0 and βx=βy=βz=1.8 fm.
- For the Jπ=1- state, the minimum energy -155.33 MeV appears at Sz=0 and βx=βy=βz=2.4 fm.
- The squared overlap between single THSR-type wave functions and Brink-GCM wave functions is 0.9929 for the 0+ state, 0.9879 for the 2+ state, 0.9775 for the 4+ state, 0.9998 for the 1- state, and 0.9987 for the 3- state.
- The minimum energies for projected states in the βx=βy, βz parameter space are: -159.85 MeV (0+, βx=βy=0.9 fm, βz=2.5 fm), -158.53 MeV (2+, βx=βy=0.0 fm, βz=2.2 fm), -155.50 MeV (4+, βx=βy=0.0 fm, βz=1.8 fm), -155.38 MeV (1-, βx=βy=3.7 fm, βz=1.4 fm), -153.07 MeV (3-, βx=βy=3.7 fm, βz=0.0 fm).

## Context

This work extends the THSR wave function approach, previously applied to gas-like cluster states such as the Hoyle state in 12C (Funaki et al., 2003) and the ground-state band of 8Be (Funaki et al., 2002), to the inversion-doublet bands in 20Ne which had been considered a classic example of localized clustering. The paper resolves a puzzle from earlier studies (Zhou et al., 2012, 2013) where prolate and oblate THSR wave functions were found nearly equivalent after angular-momentum projection, by showing that the true intrinsic two-cluster configuration is prolate and that oblate THSR wave functions correspond to rotation averages of prolate ones. The container picture enables a unified understanding of both dilute cluster-gas states and molecular-like cluster states as arising from nonlocalized cluster motion constrained by Pauli repulsion.
