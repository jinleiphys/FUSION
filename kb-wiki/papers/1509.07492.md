# Fluctuations of harmonic and radial flow in heavy ion collisions with principal components

> We analyze the spectrum of harmonic flow, $v_n(p_T)$ for $n=0\text{--}5$, in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,{\text{TeV}}$) with principal component analysis (PCA). The PCA procedure finds two dominant contributions to the two-particle correlation function. The leading component is identified with the event plane $v_n(p_T)$, while the subleading component is responsible for factorization breaking in hydrodynamics. For $v_0$, $v_1$, and $v_3$ the subleading flow is a response to the radial excitation of the corresponding eccentricity. By contrast, for $v_2$ the subleading flow in \emph{peripheral collisions} is dominated by the nonlinear mixing between the leading elliptic flow and radial flow fluctuations. In the $v_2$ case, the sub-sub-leading mode more closely reflects the response to the radial excitation of $\varepsilon_2$. A consequence of this picture is that the elliptic flow fluctuations and factorization breaking change rapidly with centrality, and in central collisions (where the leading $v_2$ is small and nonlinear effects can be neglected) the subsub-leading mode becomes important. Radial flow fluctuations and nonlinear mixing also play a significant role in the factorization breaking of $v_4$ and $v_5$. We construct good geometric predictors for the orientation and magnitudes of the leading and subleading flows based on a linear response to the geometry, and a quadratic mixing between the leading principal components. Finally, we suggest a set of measurements involving three point correlations which can experimentally corroborate the nonlinear mixing of radial and elliptic flow and its important contribution to factorization breaking as a function of centrality.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that factorization breaking in elliptic flow ($v_2$) arises from an interplay between linear response to the fluctuating elliptic geometry and nonlinear mixing of radial flow fluctuations with the average elliptic flow, with the relative importance of these two sources changing rapidly as a function of collision centrality.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic simulations of 5000 Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV across fourteen 5% centrality classes, using Phobos Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions with a two-component entropy model and a shear viscosity to entropy ratio of $\eta/s=0.08$ (with some comparisons at $\eta/s=0.16$). They applied principal component analysis (PCA) to the two-particle correlation matrix of harmonic flow $V_n(p_T)$ for harmonics $n=0$ through $5$, decomposing the covariance matrix into orthogonal eigenvectors to identify leading and subleading flow components. The authors constructed geometric predictors based on generalized eccentricities with optimized radial weight functions and nonlinear mixing terms to correlate with the principal components.

## Key numbers

- The analysis used 5000 simulated Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV
- Fourteen 5% centrality classes were analyzed, selected by impact parameter
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio was $\eta/s=0.08$ for the main results, with additional comparisons at $\eta/s=0.16$
- Pion freeze-out temperature was set at $T_{\rm fo} = 140$ MeV
- For most geometric predictors, the ratio $R_o/R_{\rm rms}\approx 3.0$ was used, except for directed flow components and the second elliptic flow component where $R_o/R_{\rm rms}\approx 2.0$ was optimal
- The factorization ratio $r_3(p_{T1},p_{T2})$ for triangular flow was well described by just two principal components at low momentum $p_T<2$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PCA analysis of triangular flow (Mazeliauskas and Teaney, 2015) to harmonics $n=0$ through $5$, and builds on the introduction of PCA to heavy ion flow analysis by Bhalerao et al. (2014). The study connects to experimental observations of factorization breaking in elliptic flow from ATLAS (2012) and CMS (2015), and to prior theoretical work on nonlinear mixing of harmonics by Borghini et al. (2005), Qiu et al. (2011), Gardim et al. (2011), and Teaney and Yan (2012). The results enable experimental measurements of three-point correlations between subleading elliptic flow, mean $p_T$ fluctuations, and the leading elliptic event plane, analogous to the three-plane correlations already measured for $v_5$, $v_2$, and $v_3$ by ATLAS (2015).
