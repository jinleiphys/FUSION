# Magnetic aspects of QCD at finite density and temperature

> Some magnetic aspects of QCD are discussed at finite density and temperature. Possibility of spontaneous magnetization is studied within Landau Fermi-liquid theory, and the important roles of the screening effects for gluon propagation are elucidated. Static screening for the longitudinal gluons improves the infrared singularities, while the transverse gluons receive only dynamic screening. The latter property gives rise to a novel non-Fermi-liquid behaviour for the magnetic susceptibility. Appearance of a density-wave state is also discussed in relation to chiral transition, where pseudoscalar condensate as well as scalar one takes a spatially non-uniform form in a chirally invariant way. Accordingly magnetization of quark matter oscillates like spin density wave. A hadron-quark continuity is suggested in this aspect, remembering pion condensation in hadronic phase.

## Key claim

Spontaneous magnetization in quark matter is possible via the Bloch mechanism using one-gluon-exchange interactions, but the screening effects for gluon propagation, static for longitudinal gluons and dynamic for transverse gluons, critically modify the magnetic susceptibility and can either favor or disfavor the ferromagnetic transition depending on the number of quark flavors.

## Method

The author studies ferromagnetic transitions in quark matter by evaluating the magnetic susceptibility within Landau Fermi-liquid theory, incorporating the one-gluon-exchange interaction and the hard-dense-loop resummation for screening effects. For the chiral-symmetry-related magnetic phase, a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used to examine the dual-chiral-density-wave (DCDW) configuration, where both scalar and pseudoscalar condensates take spatially non-uniform forms.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic susceptibility diverges around nuclear density for pure one-gluon-exchange interaction; with screening, the critical density shifts to lower densities for $N_f=1$ and to higher densities for $N_f=3$.
- The Debye screening mass is given by $m_D^2=\sum_{\rm flavors}g^2/2\pi^2k_{F,i}E_{F,i}$.
- At finite temperature, the magnetic susceptibility contains a $T^2\ln T$ term (non-Fermi-liquid effect) in addition to the usual $T^2$ term; the ferromagnetic region shrinks as temperature increases and shows no divergence at $T=60$ MeV.
- In the DCDW phase, the wave number $q$ is of order $O(2k_F)$, and the dynamical mass $M=-2G\Delta$ is plotted at $T=0$ with and without DCDW.
- For compact stars, the estimated magnetic field from the ferromagnetic phase is $10^{15-17}$ G.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ferromagnetic transitions in QCD by Tatsumi (2000, 2008a, 2008b) and connects to the Bloch mechanism for electron gas (Bloch, 1929). The non-Fermi-liquid behavior from dynamic screening parallels similar effects in the specific heat (Holstein, 1973) and the gap function in color superconductivity (Son, 1999). The DCDW phase is contrasted with the real kink crystal phase discussed by Nickel (2009) and with pion condensation in hadronic matter (Pion condensation literature), suggesting a hadron-quark continuity. The results enable further studies of magnetic field generation in magnetars and the phase diagram of QCD at moderate densities.
