# Effect of electronic environment on neutrino-nucleus reactions at r-process sites

> We discuss effects of the electron plasma on charged-current neutrino-nucleus reaction, $(ν_e,e^-)$ in a core-collapse supernova environment. We first discuss the electron screening effect on the final state interaction between the outgoing electron and the daughter nucleus. To this end, we solve the Dirac equation for the outgoing electron with the screened Coulomb potential obtained with the Thomas-Fermi approximation. In addition to the screening effect, we also discuss the Pauli blocking effect due to the environmental electrons on the spectrum of the outgoing electron. We find that both effects hinder the cross section of the charged-current reaction, especially at low incident energies.

## Key claim

Both electron screening (via the Thomas-Fermi screened Coulomb potential) and Pauli blocking from environmental electrons reduce the charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross section at r-process sites, especially at low incident neutrino energies; the magnitude of the reduction depends on the electron density and the nuclear excitation energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for the outgoing electron using a screened Coulomb potential obtained self-consistently from the Thomas-Fermi approximation, assuming a sharp-cut nuclear charge distribution and complete ionization. They then computed differential and total cross sections for the Fermi-type transitions in the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), multiplying by a Pauli blocking factor (1 - f_e) derived from a relativistic Fermi gas model for the environmental electrons. Calculations were performed for the reactions ⁵⁶Fe(ν_e, e⁻)⁵⁶Co and ²⁰⁸Pb(ν_e, e⁻)²⁰⁸Bi at electron densities of 10³², 10³³, and 10³⁴ cm⁻³ with an electron temperature of 0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Electron Fermi energies for ρ_e⁰ = 10³², 10³³, and 10³⁴ cm⁻³: 2.59, 5.99, and 13.1 MeV, respectively.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb(ν_e, e⁻)²⁰⁸Bi, the depth of the unscreened Coulomb potential between the outgoing electron and ²⁰⁸Bi is 25.2 MeV; at ρ_e⁰ = 10³¹, 10³², and 10³³ cm⁻³, it becomes 25.08, 24.97, and 24.73 MeV, respectively.
- The corresponding change in total cross section for ²⁰⁸Pb(ν_e, e⁻)²⁰⁸Bi is 2.25%, 4.48%, and 10.1% at those three densities.
- The neutrino temperature used for the incident neutrino energy distribution is 4 MeV, giving a peak in the distribution around 8.87 MeV.
- The Fermi transition in ⁵⁶Co is at E_x = 3.5 MeV; in ²⁰⁸Bi it is at E_x = 15.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of environmental effects on weak reactions in dense stars, such as the electron capture calculations of Itoh (2002), by applying both screening and Pauli blocking to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis. It improves upon the simple Fermi function and effective momentum approximations used in prior work (Engel, 1998) by using a self-consistent Thomas-Fermi screening potential within the DWBA framework. The results enable more accurate neutrino reaction rates for r-process network calculations, which previously neglected these environmental corrections.
