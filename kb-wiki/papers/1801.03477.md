# Small System Collectivity in Relativistic Hadron and Nuclear Collisions

> The bulk motion of nuclear matter at the ultra-high temperatures created in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider is well described in terms of nearly inviscid hydrodynamics, thereby establishing this system of quarks and gluons as the most perfect fluid in nature. A revolution in the field is underway, spearheaded by the discovery of similar collective, fluid-like phenomena in much smaller systems including p+p, p+A, d+Au, and $^3$He$+$Au collisions. We review these exciting new observations and their implications.

## Key claim

The discovery of collective, fluid-like phenomena in small collision systems (p+p, p+A, d+Au, and 3He+Au) challenges the expectation that hydrodynamics applies only to large nuclei, but the interpretation remains conditional: these observations may be explained either by a hydrodynamic description extended to small systems or by alternative mechanisms such as momentum correlations from initial-state effects, and the review does not definitively adjudicate between these possibilities.

## Method

The authors review experimental measurements of two-particle correlations, multiparticle cumulants, and Fourier flow coefficients (v2, v3, v4) in p+p, p+A, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions at RHIC and the LHC, comparing them to predictions from viscous hydrodynamic models and to the established signatures of collective flow in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions. They also examine systematic variations of the initial geometry by using different projectile species (p, d, 3He) on Au targets at RHIC, and discuss alternative non-hydrodynamic explanations such as momentum-domain correlations and color reconnection.

## Key numbers

- In Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is approximately η/s ~ 1/(4π), the conjectured lower bound for any fluid.
- The initial temperature in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (200 GeV per nucleon pair) is of order 350-400 MeV; in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (up to 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair) it is of order 400-600 MeV.
- The QGP crossover transition temperature is T ≈ 170 MeV, as determined by the inflection point of the Polyakov loop.
- In d+Au collisions at RHIC at √sNN = 200 GeV, two-, four-, and six-particle cumulant measurements indicate multiparticle-level correlations.
- In p+Pb collisions at the LHC at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the splitting v2{2} > v2{4} is observed, similar to Pb+Pb collisions, but this splitting disappears in p+p collisions.
- Hydrodynamic calculations for p, d, and 3He projectiles on Au at RHIC predict v2 values that agree with subsequent experimental measurements; the 3He+Au system was chosen to enhance triangular flow v3, which was also measured and agrees with predictions.

## Context

This review extends the hydrodynamic description of heavy-ion collisions established at RHIC and the LHC (the "Little Bang" standard model, as developed by Bjorken (1982), Heinz and Snellings (2013), and others) to much smaller systems, where collective behavior was not expected. The observations build on earlier searches for QGP signatures in p+p and pbar+p collisions at the Tevatron (E735, MiniMAX) and on the long history of collective models from Fermi (1950) and Landau (1953). The results enable systematic tests of whether hydrodynamics applies to systems with very small numbers of particles and open the question of whether alternative explanations (e.g., momentum-domain correlations, color reconnection) can be ruled out, with jet quenching remaining the one major heavy-ion signature not yet observed in small systems.
