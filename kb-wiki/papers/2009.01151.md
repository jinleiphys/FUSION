# Accessing the Single-Particle Structure of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in $^{208}$Pb

> New experimental data on the neutron single-particle character of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in $^{208}$Pb are presented. They were obtained from $(d,p)$ and resonant proton scattering experiments performed at the Q3D spectrograph of the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratory in Garching, Germany. The new data are compared to the large suite of complementary, experimental data available for $^{208}$Pb and establish $(d,p)$ as an additional, valuable, experimental probe to study the PDR and its collectivity. Besides the single-particle character of the states, different features of the strength distributions are discussed and compared to Large-Scale-Shell-Model (LSSM) and energy-density functional (EDF) plus Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) theoretical approaches to elucidate the microscopic structure of the PDR in $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the $(d,p)$ reaction as a valuable experimental probe for studying the single-particle structure of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in $^{208}$Pb, but the authors note that both theoretical models (Large-Scale-Shell-Model and energy-density functional plus Quasiparticle-Phonon Model) fail to generate enough spectroscopic strength above the neutron-separation energy, which could affect neutron-capture rate calculations.

## Method

High-resolution $(d,p)$ transfer reactions were performed using 22 MeV deuterons on a $^{207}$Pb target at the Q3D spectrograph of the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratory, achieving energy resolution better than 6 keV FWHM. Angular distributions were measured at three scattering angles (20°, 25°, and 30°) and analyzed with Distorted-Wave-Born-Approximation (DWBA) calculations using the coupled-channels program CHUCK3. These data were complemented by resonant proton scattering via isobaric analog resonances ($(p,p')_{\mathrm{IAR}}$) to probe neutron 1p-1h configurations, and compared to Large-Scale-Shell-Model (LSSM) and energy-density functional plus Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (EDF+QPM) calculations.

## Key numbers

* Angle-integrated $(d,p)$ cross section below the neutron-separation energy: $\sum \sigma_{(d,p)_{exp}} = 1524(17)$ $\mu$b, compared to LSSM prediction of 1470 $\mu$b and QPM prediction of 1676 $\mu$b
* For firm $1^-$ states above the neutron-separation energy: $\sum \sigma_{(d,p)_{exp}} = 254(9)$ $\mu$b, while LSSM predicts only 22 $\mu$b
* Spectroscopic factor for the major (3p$_{1/2})^{-1}$(4s$_{1/2})^{+1}$ fragment at 5292 keV: $S = 0.77(4)$ (experimental), compared to LSSM prediction of $S_{LSSM} = 0.56$ at 5226 keV
* Spectroscopic factor for the major (3p$_{1/2})^{-1}$(3d$_{3/2})^{+1}$ fragment at 5947 keV: $S = 0.66(4)$ (experimental), compared to LSSM centroid at 5912 keV with $S_{LSSM} = 0.78$
* Experimental $B(E1)$ value for the major (3p$_{1/2})^{-1}$(3d$_{3/2})^{+1}$ fragment: $13(1) \times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$fm$^2$, while LSSM predicts $45 \times 10^{-3}$ e$^2$fm$^2$
* QPM predicts a dominant (3p$_{1/2})^{-1}$(4s$_{1/2})^{+1}$ fragment with $S_{5.32\,\mathrm{MeV}} = 0.92$ and (3p$_{1/2})^{-1}$(3d$_{3/2})^{+1}$ strength concentrated in one state with $S_{6.12\,\mathrm{MeV}} = 0.68$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the PDR in $^{208}$Pb that used Nuclear Resonance Fluorescence (Ryezayeva et al., 2002), $(p,p')$ reactions (Poltoratska et al., 2012), and $(^{17}\mathrm{O},^{17}\mathrm{O}'\gamma)$ experiments (Crespi et al., 2014) to probe the isovector and isoscalar character of the low-lying $E1$ strength. The new $(d,p)$ data provide direct access to the neutron single-particle configurations contributing to the PDR, enabling comparison with LSSM calculations (Brown, 2000; Schwengner et al., 2010) and EDF+QPM calculations (Tsoneva et al., 2016). The results enable future studies of the PDR's microscopic structure across different mass regions using one-nucleon transfer reactions, potentially at next-generation exotic beam facilities with solenoidal spectrometers.
