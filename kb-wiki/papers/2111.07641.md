# Systematic study of Δ(1232) resonance excitations using single isobaric charge-exchange reactions induced by medium-mass projectiles of Sn

> The fragment separator FRS has been for the first time used to measure the (n,p) and (p,n)-type isobaric charge-exchange cross sections of stable 112,124Sn isotopes accelerated at 1A GeV with an uncertainty of 3% and to separate quasi-elastic and inelastic components in the missing-energy spectra of the ejectiles. The inelastic contribution can be associated to the excitation of isobar Δ(1232) resonances and to the pion emission in s-wave, both in the target and projectile nuclei, while the quasi-elastic contribution is associated to the nuclear spin-isospin response of nucleon-hole excitations. The data lead to interesting results where we observe a clear quenching of the quasi-elastic component and their comparisons to theoretical calculations demonstrate that the baryonic resonances can be excited in the target and projectile nuclei. To go further in this investigation, we propose to study the excitation of baryonic resonances taking advantage of the combination of high-resolving power magnetic spectrometers with the WASA calorimeter. These new measurements will allow us to determine the momenta of the ejectiles and pions emitted in coincidence after the single isobaric charge-exchange collisions, providing us unique opportunities to study the evolution of the baryonic resonance dynamics with the neutron-proton asymmetry through the use of exotic radioactive ion beams.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the inelastic component of single isobaric charge-exchange cross sections in medium-mass Sn isotopes can be associated with the excitation of Δ(1232) resonances in both target and projectile nuclei, with the caveat that the quasi-elastic component shows a clear quenching that depends on target neutron excess and projectile asymmetry.

## Method

The experiment used the FRS fragment separator at GSI in achromatic mode to measure (n,p)-type and (p,n)-type isobaric charge-exchange reactions of 112,124Sn beams at 1A GeV on polyethylene, carbon, copper, and lead targets. The magnetic rigidity of reaction products was determined using time-projection chambers at the intermediate (S2) and final (S4) focal planes, while atomic number was measured with multi-sampling ionization chambers and time-of-flight was obtained from plastic scintillators. Missing-energy spectra were reconstructed from the ejectile kinetic energies and unfolded using the Richardson-Lucy technique with regularization to achieve a resolution of 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for p(112Sn,112In)X: 291 ± 9 μb; for p(112Sn,112Sb)X: 604 ± 19 μb
- Total cross section for p(124Sn,124Sb)X: 585 ± 19 μb
- Missing-energy resolution achieved: 10 MeV, a factor of two better than the SATURNE experiments (Roy 1988)
- FRS resolving power: Bρ/ΔBρ = 1500, with angular acceptance of ±15 mrad
- Dispersion at intermediate focal plane S2: D_S2 = -7.48 cm/% with uncertainty 0.02 cm/%
- Dispersion at final focal plane S4: D_S4 = 7.56 cm/% with uncertainty 0.02 cm/%
- Magnification M = -1.011 ± 0.004
- Time-of-flight resolution: approximately 150 ps (FWHM)
- Atomic number resolution ΔZ/Z = 6 × 10^(-3) and mass resolution ΔA/A = 1 × 10^(-3) (FWHM)
- Energy shift of approximately 63 MeV observed for the inelastic peak between proton and other targets
- Cross sections for (124Sn,124Sb) decrease systematically by about 4% compared to (112Sn,112Sb)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering SATURNE experiments (Ellegaard 1985, Contardo 1986, Roy 1988) that first showed in-medium excitation of the Δ-resonance in peripheral single isobaric charge-exchange reactions, and follows earlier GSI investigations with 129Xe at 790A MeV (Summerer 1995) and later CHARMS collaboration measurements with 136Xe (Benlliure 2008) and 209Bi (Kelic 2004). The improved missing-energy resolution of 10 MeV, compared to approximately 50 MeV in earlier CHARMS work (Vargas 2013), enables better separation of quasi-elastic and inelastic components. The results enable future studies combining high-resolving power magnetic spectrometers with the WASA calorimeter to measure ejectile and pion momenta in coincidence, allowing investigation of baryonic resonance dynamics as a function of neutron-proton asymmetry using radioactive ion beams.
