# The Bonn nuclear quark model revisited

> We present the exact solutions to the equations of the lowest energy states of the colored and color-symmetric sectors of the Bonn quark model, which is SU(3) symmetric and is defined in terms of an effective pairing force with $su(4)$ algebraic structure. We show that the groundstate of the model is not color symmetrical except for a narrow interval in the range of possible quark numbers. We also study the performance of the Glauber coherent state, as well as of superconducting states of the BCS type, with respect to the description, not only of the absolute (colored) groundstate, but also of the minimum energy state of the color-symmetrical sector, finding that it is remarkably good. We use the model to discuss, in a schematic context, some controversial aspects of the conventional treatment of color superconductivity.

## Key claim

The groundstate of the Bonn quark model belongs to the unphysical colored sector for almost all quark numbers, except for a narrow interval around N = 3Ω where it becomes color-symmetrical; the Glauber coherent state and BCS-type superconducting states provide remarkably good descriptions of both the colored groundstate and the minimum energy state of the color-symmetrical sector, but a modified BCS approach treating all three colors on the same footing is required to properly describe the physically relevant color-symmetrical sector.

## Method

The authors present exact solutions to the lowest energy states of the colored and color-symmetrical sectors of the SU(3)-symmetric Bonn quark model, which is defined by an effective pairing force with su(4) algebraic structure. They use the Schwinger boson realization of su(4) to construct invariant subspaces and obtain the groundstate energies analytically by exploiting the property that the sum of matrix entries in each row of the Hamiltonian matrix is constant. They also study the performance of Glauber coherent states and BCS-type superconducting states by minimizing the Hamiltonian expectation value under constraints from the constants of motion and color neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- For the absolute groundstate energy in the Glauber coherent state approximation: for 0 ≤ N ≤ 2Ω−1, E₀ = (G/4)N(4Ω−N+2); for 2Ω−1 ≤ N ≤ 4Ω+1, E₀ = G(Ω²−Ω+N+1/4); for 4Ω+1 ≤ N, E₀ = (G/4)(N−2Ω)(6Ω−N+6).
- The exact groundstate energy of the color-symmetrical sector for 3Ω ≤ N ≤ 6Ω is E₀ = GΛ(Λ+Γ+3) = G(N/3)(2Ω+3−N/3), where 2Ω = 2Λ+Γ and 2N−6Ω = −3Γ.
- The color-symmetric BCS groundstate energy is E_BCS = (GN/9)(6Ω−N+1+4N/(3Ω)).
- For Ω=10, the groundstate is color symmetrical only at N=30 (i.e., N=3Ω).

## Context

This work revisits the Bonn quark model originally proposed by Petry et al. (some years prior to 2009) as a schematic model for the nucleus as a system of interacting quarks, inspired by the seniority model of nuclear pairing. The authors extend earlier work by Pittel et al. and by Bohr (2008) who introduced a generalized Bogoliubov transformation for color-symmetric BCS states. The exact solutions enable testing of approximation techniques, and the results are used to discuss controversial aspects of color superconductivity treatments in effective models of the NJL type, as studied by Alford et al. and Iida et al. The work enables future investigation of broken-pair states and the physical interpretation of Schwinger bosons in the Bonn model.
