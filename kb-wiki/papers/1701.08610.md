# Finite temperature gluon spectral functions from $N_f=2+1+1$ lattice QCD

> We investigate gluon spectral functions at finite temperature in Landau gauge, based on a subset of lattice QCD ensembles with $N_f=2+1+1$ dynamical twisted mass quarks flavors, generated by the tmfT collaboration. Our study uses a novel Bayesian approach for the extraction of non-positive definite spectral functions, which for each binned spatial momentum takes into account the gluon correlation functions at all available discrete imaginary frequencies. The spectral functions are extracted at three different lattice spacing, where for each of them, a scan of temperatures around the crossover transition is carried out at fixed scale. We find indications for the existence of a well defined quasi-particle peak. Due to a relatively small number of imaginary frequencies available, we focus on the momentum and temperature dependence of the position of this spectral feature. This dispersion relation reveals different in-medium masses for longitudinal and transversal gluons at high temperatures, qualitatively consistent with weak coupling expectations.

## Key claim

The paper finds indications for a well-defined quasi-particle peak in finite-temperature gluon spectral functions extracted from lattice QCD, but the authors caution that due to the relatively small number of available imaginary frequencies, they focus only on the momentum and temperature dependence of the peak position rather than the full spectral shape.

## Method

The authors computed longitudinal and transversal gluon correlation functions in Landau gauge on lattice QCD ensembles with \(N_f=2+1+1\) dynamical twisted mass quark flavors generated by the tmfT collaboration, using three different lattice spacings. They extracted gluon spectral functions from these correlators via a novel Bayesian approach (the generalized BR method) that handles non-positive definite spectra, using a zero default model based on the sum-rule for gluon spectra and performing the reconstruction on a frequency grid with 2000 bins and 512-bit arithmetic.

- Number of available imaginary frequency points per ensemble: \(N_{q_4}\in[4\ldots20]\)
- Number of bins for spectral reconstruction: \(N_\omega=2000\) over frequency range \(\omega\in[10^{-3},100]\) GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of gluon correlation functions at finite temperature (Mendes 2014, Aouane 2012, Cucchieri 2012, Maas 2011) and functional approaches (Reinosa 2016, Fischer 2014, Fister 2013) by extracting spectral functions rather than just propagators. It applies a recently developed Bayesian method (Rothkopf 2016) that generalizes the standard BR method (Burnier 2013) to non-positive definite spectra, overcoming limitations of the Maximum Entropy Method (Asakawa 2000) and quadratic priors (Dudal 2013). The results enable validation of quasi-particle models used in heavy-ion collision phenomenology, such as the parton-hadron string dynamics model (Bratkovskaya 2011, Berrehrah 2016), and can serve as input for self-consistent computations of transport coefficients in functional approaches to QCD.
