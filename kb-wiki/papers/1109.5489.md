# Comparative study of three-nucleon potentials in nuclear matter

> A new generation of local three-body potentials providing an excellent description of the properties of light nuclei, as well as of the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length, has been recently derived. We have performed a comparative analysis of the equations of state of both pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter obtained using these models of three-nucleon forces. None of the considered potentials simultaneously explains the empirical equilibrium density and binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter. However, two of them provide reasonable values of the saturation density. The ambiguity concerning the treatment of the contact term of the chiral inspired potentials is discussed.

# Comparative study of three-nucleon potentials in nuclear matter

## Key claim
None of the considered three-nucleon potentials simultaneously reproduces the empirical equilibrium density and binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter, although two of them yield reasonable values of the saturation density. The ambiguity in the treatment of the contact term of the chiral-inspired potentials introduces a theoretical uncertainty that is a cutoff effect of the same order as the next term in the chiral expansion.

## Method
The authors performed variational FHNC/SOC (Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain) calculations and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential combined with three different three-nucleon force models: the phenomenological Urbana IX (UIX), the chiral-inspired revision of the Tucson-Melbourne (TM') potential, and the local chiral NNLO three-nucleon potential (NNLOL). The parameters for the NNLOL and TM' potentials were taken from fits to the binding energies of 3H and 4He and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length performed by Kievsky et al. (2010). The expectation values of the contact terms were also evaluated analytically in a noninteracting Fermi gas to study the cutoff dependence.

## Key numbers
- For the NNLOL potential in noninteracting pure neutron matter at density 0.16 fm^-3, the expectation value of the contact term per nucleon is 3.60 MeV for cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV, decreasing to 0.81 MeV for Lambda = 800 MeV, and approaches 0 MeV in the infinite-cutoff limit.
- For the NNLOL potential in noninteracting symmetric nuclear matter at density 0.16 fm^-3 with Lambda = 500 MeV, the expectation value of the tau-contact term per nucleon is -4.37 MeV and of the scalar contact term is 6.93 MeV; these approach -5.55 MeV and +5.55 MeV respectively in the infinite-cutoff limit.
- The chiral symmetry-breaking scale Lambda_chi is set to 700 MeV, and the momentum cutoff in the NNLOL potential is set to 500 MeV.
- The UIX potential parameter A_{2pi} was obtained from the fit of the observed binding energy of 3H, while the strength U_0 was adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3.

## Context
This work extends the comparative study of three-nucleon forces by Kievsky et al. (2010), who used the hyperspherical harmonics formalism to compute light-nuclei properties and found that no single parametrization simultaneously reproduces the binding energies of 3H and 4He and the nd doublet scattering length. The present paper tests those parametrizations in nuclear matter, building on earlier FHNC/SOC calculations by Carlson et al. (1983) with early Urbana and Tucson-Melbourne potentials, and on variational calculations by Akmal et al. (1998) using Argonne v18 and UIX potentials that sizably underbind symmetric nuclear matter. The results enable future work to constrain three-nucleon forces using both nuclear matter saturation properties and the recent observation of a two-solar-mass neutron star by Demorest et al. (2010).
