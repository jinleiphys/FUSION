# Phase shift effective range expansion from supersymmetric quantum mechanics

> Supersymmetric or Darboux transformations are used to construct local phase equivalent deep and shallow potentials for $\ell \neq 0$ partial waves. We associate the value of the orbital angular momentum with the asymptotic form of the potential at infinity which allows us to introduce adequate long-distance transformations. The approach is shown to be effective in getting the correct phase shift effective range expansion. Applications are considered for the $^1P_1$ and $^1D_2$ partial waves of the neutron-proton scattering.

# Phase shift effective range expansion from supersymmetric quantum mechanics

## Key claim

The authors show that by associating the orbital angular momentum with the long-distance asymptotic behavior of the potential, Darboux (supersymmetric) transformations can be constructed that yield a correct effective range expansion for partial waves with ℓ > 0, a problem that previous SUSY approaches had not solved in principle.

## Method

Starting from a zero initial potential, the authors apply a chain of Darboux transformations: first, ℓ successive transformations using zero-energy eigenfunctions to generate an intermediate potential with the correct long-distance behavior and a background phase shift containing ℓ free parameters; then, an ℓ-fixed subchain of 2n−ν transformations that does not alter the asymptotic form. The free parameters in the background phase shift are fixed by requiring that undesired low-order powers of k vanish in the series expansion of tan δℓ(k), enforcing the standard effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹P₁ partial wave (ℓ=1), a 7th-order transformation with poles a₁=−0.7290 fm⁻¹, a₂=−0.7295 fm⁻¹, a₃=1.0368 fm⁻¹, b₁=0.4403 fm⁻¹, b₂=0.4408 fm⁻¹, b₃=3.3818 fm⁻¹ and x₀=3.0578 fm yields the effective range expansion k³ cot δ⁷₁(k) = −0.3182 − 3.1511 k² + …
- The resulting scattering length a₀₁ = 3.143 fm and effective range r₀₁ = −6.302 fm for ¹P₁.
- For the ¹D₂ partial wave (ℓ=2), a 6th-order transformation with poles a₁=−0.2047 fm⁻¹, a₂=−1.9800 fm⁻¹, b₁=1.2305 fm⁻¹, b₂=4.9631 fm⁻¹, x₀=−4.375 fm, and c=116.08 fm³ yields k⁵ cot δ⁶₂(k) = 0.4496 + 10.9878 k² + …
- The resulting scattering length a₀₂ = −2.224 fm and effective range r₀₂ = 21.976 fm for ¹D₂.
- The deep potential for ¹P₁ has a Pauli forbidden state at energy E = −a₃² = −44.46 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SUSY quantum mechanics approach to inverse scattering developed by Sukumar (1985), Baye and Sparenberg (1994), and Samsonov and Stancu (2002) by solving the long-standing problem of obtaining a correct effective range expansion for ℓ > 0 partial waves. The method is applied to neutron-proton scattering in the ¹P₁ and ¹D₂ channels, producing phase shifts that agree with the experimental data of Stoks et al. (1993) and potentials that asymptotically match the one-pion-exchange behavior of the Reid93 potential (Stoks et al., 1994). The approach enables the systematic construction of both shallow and deep phase-equivalent potentials with correct low-energy behavior for arbitrary ℓ.
