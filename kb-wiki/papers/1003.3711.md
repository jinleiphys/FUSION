# A formulation without partial wave decomposition for scattering of spin-1/2 and spin-0 particles

> A new technique has been developed to calculate scattering of spin-1/2 and spin-0 particles. The so called momentum-helicity basis states are constructed from the helicity and the momentum states, which are not expanded in the angular momentum states. Thus, all angular momentum states are taken into account. Compared with the partial-wave approach this technique will then give more benefit especially in calculations for higher energies. Taking as input a simple spin-orbit potential, the Lippman-Schwinger equations for the T-matrix elements are solved and some observables are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional (3D) technique for scattering of spin-1/2 and spin-0 particles that avoids partial wave decomposition by using momentum-helicity basis states, claiming this approach becomes more beneficial than partial-wave calculations especially at higher energies where adding more angular momentum states may become computationally infeasible.

## Method

The authors construct momentum-helicity basis states as eigenstates of the parity operator and the helicity operator, without expanding into angular momentum states. They derive symmetry relations for the potential and T-matrix elements that reduce the number of independent equations, yielding a single uncoupled Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for each parity state. As a test case, they solve this equation for a simple spin-orbit potential of the Malfliet-Tjon type, using a projectile with nucleon mass and a target with kaon mass.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit potential uses parameters: V_ca = 3.22, V_cb = 7.39, μ_c = 1.55, V_sa = 2.64, V_sb = 7.39, μ_s = 0.63 (units not specified in text)
- Calculations are performed for projectile laboratory energies from a few MeV up to 1 GeV
- The mass of particle 1 (projectile) is nucleon mass and particle 2 (target) is kaon mass

## Context

This work extends the 3D technique previously developed for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering and the deuteron, which had been successfully applied to the Bonn-B and AV18 realistic potential models (Refs. nn and deuteron, 2007). The formulation is simpler than for NN scattering because the system consists of nonidentical particles with total spin 1/2, requiring no antisymmetrization. The technique is intended to enable future investigations of kaon-nucleon (KN) scattering, provided a KN interaction in operator form becomes available.
