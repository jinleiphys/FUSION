# A generator of forward neutrons for ultra-peripheral collisions: $\textbf{n$\mathbf{_O^O}$n}$

> The study of photon-induced reactions in collisions of heavy nuclei at RHIC and the LHC has become an important direction of the research program of these facilities in recent years. In particular, the production of vector mesons in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) has been intensively studied. Owing to the intense photon fluxes, the two nuclei participating in such processes undergo electromagnetic dissociation producing neutrons at beam rapidities. Here, we introduce the $\textbf{n$\mathbf{_O^O}$n}$ (pronounced noon) Monte Carlo program, which generates events containing such neutrons. $\textbf{n$\mathbf{_O^O}$n}$ is a ROOT based program that can be interfaced with existing generators of vector meson production in UPC or with theoretical calculations of such photonuclear processes. $\textbf{n$\mathbf{_O^O}$n}$ can also be easily integrated with the simulation programs of the experiments at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the nOOn Monte Carlo generator, which produces events containing forward neutrons from electromagnetic dissociation in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC), but the generator is currently restricted to lead (Pb) nuclei only and relies on extrapolations of neutron multiplicity data from photon energies below 140 MeV up to 10^9 MeV using a logarithmic fit.

## Method

The generator computes nuclear break-up probabilities using experimental data on photonuclear cross sections for lead, combined with a Regge theory parametrization at higher energies. Neutron multiplicities are generated from a Gaussian approximation of the multiplicity distribution, with the average and dispersion extrapolated logarithmically from measurements up to 140 MeV. Neutron energies are sampled from evaluated nuclear data tables (ENDF) for photo-neutron reactions on 208Pb, and the neutrons are boosted to the laboratory frame after being generated isotropically in the nucleus rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for electromagnetic dissociation is constructed from experimental data covering photon energies up to 16.4 GeV, with data from Veyssiere (1970), Lepretre (1981), Carlos (1985), Armstrong (1971, 1972), Michalowski (1977), and Caldwell (1973, 1978)
- The generator computes break-up probabilities up to 50 neutrons and 5 excitations by default, which integrates more than 99% of the probability
- The normalization ratio (sum of P_Nn over P_Xn) is approximately 0.8 at small impact parameters and reaches a plateau around 0.98 at 30 fm
- Neutron emission spectra from the ENDF database are used for incident photon energies up to 140 MeV; for energies above 140 MeV the last bin of the spectrum is used
- The generator stores 1277 TGraphs for a maximum of 50 neutrons, accounting for all combinations of neutron multiplicities on both sides

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of existing UPC Monte Carlo programs like STARlight (Klein, 2016), which generates vector mesons but does not provide final state neutrons in the event output. The nOOn generator enables experimental studies proposed by Baltz (2002) and Guzey (2013) that use neutron-tagged UPC events to disentangle the two-fold ambiguity in photon energy for vector meson production at non-zero rapidity. The program can be interfaced with STARlight, PYTHIA (Sjostrand, 2007), SuperChic (Harland-Lang, 2018), or theoretical calculations such as the energy-dependent hot-spot model (Cepila, 2016, 2017, 2018).
