# Electromagnetic Transition Strengths in Heavy Nuclei

> We calculate reduced B(E2) and B(M1) electromagnetic transition strengths within and between K-bands in support of a recently proposed model for the structure of heavy nuclei. Previously, only spectra and a rough indication of the largest B(E2) strengths were reported. The present more detailed calculations should aid the experimental identification of the predicted $0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$ bands and, in particular, act to confirm or refute the suggestion that the model $0^+$ and $2^+$ bands correspond to the well known and widespread beta and gamma bands. Furthermore they pinpoint transitions which can indicate the presence of a so far elusive $1^+$ band by feeding relatively strongly into or out of it. Some of these transitions may already have been measured in $^{230}$Th, $^{232}$Th and $^{238}$U.

## Key claim

The paper calculates detailed B(E2) and B(M1) transition strengths within and between predicted K-bands in heavy nuclei, but the authors caution that the results are not definitive because the direction of transitions depends on the ordering of states in the spectrum and the wave functions are sensitive to how close in energy the states lie.

## Method

The authors model a heavy nucleus as a core and a cluster interacting via a deep local potential with nuclear and Coulomb terms, allowing the core to be in either its ground state or a 2+ excited state. They diagonalize low-dimensional matrices formed by coupling the core spin I with the relative orbital angular momentum L to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, using a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction as the non-central potential. They then calculate reduced B(E2) and B(M1) transition strengths from the resulting wave functions, presenting E2 strengths as ratios to the ground state band 2+ to 0+ transition and M1 strengths in Weisskopf units.

## Key numbers

- In-band stretched E2 transitions are typically a few hundred Weisskopf units in the Actinide region
- Cross-band E2 transitions are typically a few Weisskopf units (a few hundredths of in-band values)
- B(M1) strengths are generally rather small, scarcely exceeding 0.01 Wu
- The 0+ to 1+ transition between band heads has a B(M1) of 0.1028 Wu
- For 230Th: measured B(E2) for 2+(677.515 keV) to 4+(174.111 keV) is 10(4) Wu, compared to theoretical 9.2 Wu for 2+(0ex) to 4+(0gs)
- For 232Th: measured B(E2) for 2+(785.25 keV) to 2+(49.369 keV) is 7.2(7) Wu, compared to theoretical 3.0 Wu for 2+(2) to 2+(0gs)
- For 234U: measured B(E2) for 2+(926.720 keV) to 2+(43.4981 keV) is 4.9(8) Wu, compared to theoretical 3.6 Wu for 2+(2) to 2+(0gs)
- The 2+ to 0+ transition in the ground state band of 208Pb is measured as 8.4 plus or minus 0.5 Wu

## Context

This work extends a previously proposed cluster model (Buck, Merchant, and Perez, 2012) that generates excited 0+, 1+, and 2+ K-bands by coupling a 2+ excitation to a rotor, where previously only spectra and rough indications of the largest B(E2) strengths were reported. The calculations aim to help experimental identification of the predicted bands in Actinide nuclei like 230Th, 232Th, and 238U, and to test whether the model's 0+ and 2+ bands correspond to the well-known beta and gamma bands. The paper also notes that similar triplets of K-bands have been seen in lighter nuclei such as 16O, 24Mg, and 40Ca, and that other theoretical approaches including the cranked RPA (Neergard, Vogel, Ward, Kuliev), the collective model (Minkov, Raduta), the interacting boson approximation (Zamfir, Cottle), and the alpha particle cluster model (Schneidman) have studied positive parity bands in Actinide nuclei.
