# Microscopic description of rotation: From ground states to the extremes of ultra-high spin

> Recent progress in the microscopic description of rotational properties within covariant density functional theory (CDFT) is presented. It is shown that it provides an accurate description of rotational bands both in the paired regime at low spin and in the unpaired regime at ultra-high spins. The predictive power of CDFT is verified by comparing the CDFT predictions for band crossing features in the $A\geq 242$ actinides with new experimental data. In addition, possible role of the Coulomb antipairing effect for proton pairing is discussed.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) provides an accurate description of rotational bands in both the paired regime at low spin and the unpaired regime at ultra-high spin, but the details of alignment in the band crossing region (gradual versus sharp upbend) are not always reproduced, and this limitation applies to both the CRHB+LN and CSM-PNC approaches.

## Method

The author used the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) approach with approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method (CRHB+LN) to describe rotational bands in even-even and odd-mass actinides at normal deformation, and the cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory (the no-pairing limit of CRHB) to study triaxial superdeformed bands at ultra-high spin in the A ~ 154-160 mass region. Calculations employed the NL1 and NL3* parametrizations of CDFT, and results were compared with new experimental data on Pu, Cm, and Cf isotopes from Ref. [Hota.thesis] and with cranked shell model plus particle-number conserving (CSM+PNC) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The CRHB+LN calculations predict upbends in $^{242,244}$Pu at a frequency 0.01-0.02 MeV earlier than observed experimentally.
- The calculated transition quadrupole moment $Q_t$ of the TSD3(a) configuration in $^{158}$Er changes from 10.5 $e$b at spin $I=42$ to 9.0 $e$b at $I=72$, with $\gamma$ increasing from $12^\circ$ to $16^\circ$ over this spin range; the experimental value is $Q_t \sim 11$ $e$b with $\approx 15\%$ uncertainty.
- The CRHB+LN calculations are performed in a step of 0.01 MeV in the band crossing region.
- The CRHB and CRHB+LN calculations with a renormalization factor of $\gamma_p=0.9$ for the proton pairing channel lead to proton $\Delta_{\pi}^{(3)}$ indicators that are too low compared with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of normal-deformed rotational bands in actinides and light superheavy nuclei within CDFT, first performed in Ref. [AO.13], by comparing predictions with new experimental data from Ref. [Hota.thesis]. It contrasts the CRHB+LN approach with the cranked shell model plus particle-number conserving method (CSM+PNC) of Ref. [ZHZZZ.12], noting that CRHB+LN uses the same pairing strength for even-even and odd-mass nuclei while CSM+PNC does not. The analysis of the Coulomb antipairing effect for proton pairing builds on earlier work by A. E. R. (2001) and shows that the Gogny D1S force, as used in the CRHB(+LN) framework, should be treated as an effective pairing force without the Coulomb part. The study of triaxial superdeformed bands at ultra-high spin in $^{158}$Er extends earlier CRMF work on superdeformed bands in the A ~ 150 region.
