# The role of $qqqq\bar{q}$ components in the nucleon and the N(1440) resonance

> The role of $q\bar q$ components in the nucleon and the N(1440) resonance is studied by explicit coupling of the lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state to the $qqq$ components in the harmonic oscillator quark model. The lowest energy $qqqq\bar q$ component, where the 4-quark subsystem has the flavor-spin symmetry $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$, is close in energy to the lowest positive parity excitation of the nucleon in the $qqq$ quark model. The confining interaction leads to a strong mixing of the $qqqq\bar q$ system and the positive parity excited state of the $qqq$ system. This result is in line with the phenomenological indications for a two-component structure of the N(1440) resonance. The presence of substantial $q\bar q$ components in the N(1440) can bring about a reconciliation of the constituent quark model with the large empirical decay width of the N(1440).

## Key claim

The confining interaction leads to a strong mixing of the lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state with the lowest positive parity excited state of the $qqq$ system, producing a two-component structure for the N(1440) resonance; this mixing is conditional on the constituent quark mass being below approximately 275 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed harmonic oscillator quark model wave functions for the $qqq$ ground state, the $qqq$ first positive parity excited state, and the lowest positive parity $qqqq\bar q$ state with flavor-spin symmetry $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$. They computed the matrix elements of the confining interaction coupling the $qqq$ and $qqqq\bar q$ systems, and diagonalized the resulting $3\times 3$ Hamiltonian to obtain the mixed eigenstates. The hyperfine interaction was modeled as a flavor- and spin-dependent schematic interaction $-C_\chi\sum_{i<j} \vec\lambda_F^i\cdot\vec\lambda_F^j\,\vec\sigma_i\cdot\vec\sigma_j$ with state-dependent parameters $C_0$, $C_1$, and $C_2$.

## Key numbers

- The hyperfine interaction matrix element in the $qqq$ ground state is $-14C$; in the $qqqq\bar q$ configuration it is $-28C$.
- The coupling matrix element between the $qqq$ ground state and the $qqqq\bar q$ state is $V_c = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{5} m \left(\frac{\omega_3}{m}\right)^{3/2} \frac{\omega_5^3(5\omega_3+9\omega_5)}{(\omega_3+\omega_5)^4}$.
- The coupling matrix element between the excited $qqq$ state and the $qqqq\bar q$ state is $V_c^* = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{5} m \left(\frac{\omega_3}{m}\right)^{3/2} \frac{\omega_5^3(15\omega_3^2+20\omega_3\omega_5-27\omega_5^2)}{(\omega_3+\omega_5)^5}$.
- With hyperfine parameters $C_0=29$ MeV, $C_1=45$ MeV, $C_2=0$, the constituent quark mass $m$ in the range 250-350 MeV and oscillator parameter $\omega_3$ in the range 200-100 MeV yield a lowest eigenvalue of 939 MeV (the nucleon mass).
- The configuration mixing between the excited $qqq$ and $qqqq\bar q$ components ranges from 25% for a quark mass of 250 MeV to 3% for a quark mass of 300 MeV.
- The $qqqq\bar q$ admixture in the nucleon ground state is at most approximately 1% (at a quark mass of 250 MeV).
- The estimated energy of the uncoupled $qqq(q\bar q)^2$ 7-quark system falls roughly 200 MeV above the 5-quark system and approximately 700 MeV above the $qqq$ ground state.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model by explicitly including $q\bar q$ components, addressing the known underprediction of the N(1440) decay widths in the conventional 3-quark model noted by Bruno and Melde. The two-component structure of the N(1440) is consistent with phenomenological analyses by Morsch. The flavor-spin dependent hyperfine interaction follows the approach of Glozman (1998), and the 5-quark wave functions build on the work of Helminen and Chen. The results enable improved calculations of electromagnetic transition form factors (as noted by Li et al.) and pionic decay widths, potentially reconciling the constituent quark model with the large empirical decay width of the N(1440).
