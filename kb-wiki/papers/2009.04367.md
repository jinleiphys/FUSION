# The molecular nature of some exotic hadrons

> The exciting discovery by LHCb of the $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4450)^+$ pentaquarks, or the suggestion of a tetraquark nature for the $Z_c(3900)$ state seen at BESIII and Belle, have triggered a lot of activity in the field of hadron physics, with new experiments planned for searching other exotic mesons and baryons, and many theoretical developments trying to disentangle the true multiquark nature from their possible molecular origin. After a brief review of the present status of these searches, this paper focusses on recently seen or yet to be discovered exotic heavy baryons that may emerge from a conveniently unitarized meson-baryon interaction model in coupled channels. In particular, we will show how interferences between the different coupled-channel amplitudes of the model may reveal the existence of a $N^*$ resonance around 2 GeV having a meson-baryon quasi-bound state nature. We also discuss the possible interpretation of some of the $Ω_c$ states recently discovered at LHCb as being hadron molecules. The model also predicts the existence of doubly-charmed quasibound meson-baryon $Ξ_{cc}$ states, which would be excited states of the ground-state $Ξ_{cc}(3621)$ MeV, whose mass has only been recently established. Extensions of these results to the bottom sector will also be presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several recently observed exotic heavy baryons (such as the LHCb $\Omega_c^0$ states) can be interpreted as meson-baryon quasi-bound molecules generated dynamically from a unitarized coupled-channel interaction model, but the interpretation depends sensitively on model parameters and on whether Born terms are included, which can change the number and nature of predicted poles.

## Method

The authors employ a unitarized meson-baryon interaction model based on t-channel vector meson exchange from the hidden gauge formalism, with the scattering amplitude unitarized via the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels. The model includes pseudoscalar-baryon ($PB$) and vector-baryon ($VB$) interactions, with SU(4) symmetry broken by using physical hadron masses and by introducing a factor $\kappa_c \sim 1/4$ for transitions mediated by charmed vector meson exchange. The authors search for dynamically generated poles in the second Riemann sheet and compute couplings and compositeness for each channel.

## Key numbers

* The $N^*$ resonance from the $VB$ model appears around 1970 MeV with width around 65 MeV, coupling mostly to $K^*\Lambda$ and $K^*\Sigma$ states.
* Fitting the CBELSA/TAPS $\gamma p \to K^0 \Sigma^+$ data moves the resonance up to 2030 MeV, making it almost twice wider than in the original model.
* In the $(I,S,C)=(0,-2,1)$ sector, the $PB$ model (diagram a only) produces two poles at 3050.3 MeV (width 0.44 MeV) and 3090.8 MeV (width 12 MeV), with the higher state having a $D\Xi$ compositeness of 0.91.
* Including Born terms (diagrams a+b+c) produces three poles at 2997.4 MeV (width 14.4 MeV), 3045.5 MeV (width 1.7 MeV), and 3070.1 MeV (width 8.4 MeV).
* In the $VB$ sector, resonances appear at 3231 MeV (mainly $D^*\Xi$ molecule) and 3419 MeV ($\bar{K}^*\Xi'_c$ composite), with an additional pole coupling strongly to $\bar{K}^*\Xi_c$.
* In the bottom sector, two narrow resonances are predicted at 6418 MeV and 6519 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the unitarized meson-baryon approach previously applied to light-flavor systems (such as the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a $\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound state, studied by Dalitz (1960) and later by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), and Oller (2000)) to the charm and bottom sectors. The results for $\Omega_c^0$ states build on earlier molecular models by Hofmann (2005), Jimenez-Tejero (2009), and Romanets (2012), and are compared with the five narrow $\Omega_c^0$ resonances discovered by LHCb (2017). The predictions for doubly-charmed $\Xi_{cc}$ states extend the work of Oset and collaborators, using the ground-state $\Xi_{cc}(3621)$ MeV mass recently established by LHCb (2018). The paper enables future experimental searches for molecular-type resonances in heavy-flavor baryon spectroscopy, particularly in the $\Omega_b$ sector where current LHCb data show structures around 6300-6350 MeV that may not be explained by this model.
