# Spectral Function of Fermion Coupled with Massive Vector Boson at Finite Temperature in Gauge Invariant Formalism

> We investigate spectral properties of a fermion coupled with a massive gauge boson with a mass m at finite temperature (T) in the perturbation theory. The massive gauge boson is introduced as a U(1) gauge boson in the Stueckelberg formalism with a gauge parameter α. We find that the fermion spectral function has a three-peak structure for T \sim m irrespective of the choice of the gauge parameter, while it tends to have one faint peak at the origin and two peaks corresponding to the normal fermion and anti-plasmino excitations familiar in QED in the hard thermal loop approximation for T \gg m. We show that our formalism successfully describe the fermion spectral function in the whole T region with the correct high-T limit except for the faint peak at the origin, although some care is needed for choice of the gauge parameter for T \gg m. We clarify that for T \sim m, the fermion pole is almost independent of the gauge parameter in the one-loop order, while for T \gg m, the one-loop analysis is valid only for α\ll 1/g where g is the fermion-boson coupling constant, implying that the one-loop analysis can not be valid for large gauge parameters as in the unitary gauge.

## Key claim

The fermion spectral function for a fermion coupled to a massive vector boson exhibits a three-peak structure at intermediate temperatures (T ~ m) that is virtually independent of the gauge parameter α, while at high temperatures (T ≫ m) the one-loop analysis is valid only for α ≪ 1/g, meaning the one-loop calculation cannot be trusted for large gauge parameters such as in the unitary gauge.

## Method

The authors use the Stueckelberg formalism to introduce a massive U(1) gauge boson with gauge parameter α, then compute the fermion self-energy at one-loop order in the imaginary time formalism at finite temperature. The retarded self-energy is obtained by analytic continuation, and the spectral function is constructed from the imaginary part of the retarded propagator. The real part of the self-energy is obtained via dispersion relations with on-shell renormalization conditions.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant fixed to g = 0.5 for numerical calculations
- At T = 2.0 m, the fermion spectral function shows a clear three-peak structure at zero momentum with the real part of the pole almost proportional to g
- At T = 40.0 m, the pole for α = 0.1 is located in the upper energy plane, implying a loss of analyticity of the retarded propagator
- The residue of the peak at the origin at high temperature is of order m⁴/(g²T⁴)
- The high-temperature limit of the self-energy (Eqs. for Im Σ₊ and Re Σ₊) reduces to the HTL approximation in QED with terms proportional to T²

## Context

This work extends the study of fermion spectral functions from the Proca formalism used by Kitazawa et al. (2006) to the gauge-invariant Stueckelberg formalism, enabling a unified description across all temperature regimes. The three-peak structure at T ~ m was previously found for a massless fermion coupled to an elementary massive boson by Kitazawa et al. (2006), and the mechanism is understood in terms of Landau damping from collisions with thermally excited bosons. The high-temperature limit connects to the well-known HTL approximation in QED from Weldon (1982) and Braaten and Pisarski (1990), but the present work reveals that the one-loop analysis fails for large gauge parameters at high T, motivating future work on pinch singularities and infrared behavior.
