# Shear Viscosity of hadronic matter at finite temperature and magnetic field

> We calculate the transport coefficient of hadronic matter in the presence of temperature and magnetic field using the linear sigma model. In the relaxation time approximation, we estimate the shear viscosity over entropy density $η/s$. The point-like interaction rates of hadrons are evaluated through the $S$-matrix approach in the presence of a magnetic field to obtain the temperature and magnetic field-dependent relaxation time. We observe that the transport coefficients are anisotropic in the presence of the magnetic field. We calculate the temperature and magnetic field-dependent anisotropic shear viscosity coefficients by incorporating the estimated relaxation time. The value of viscosity over entropy density is lower in the presence of a magnetic field than the value of it in a thermal medium. The behavior of the perpendicular components of the shear-viscous coefficient is also discussed. We consider the temperature-dependent hadron masses from mean-field effects in this work.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of hadronic matter is lower in the presence of a magnetic field than in a pure thermal medium, but this result is obtained under the strong magnetic field approximation where only the lowest Landau level contribution is considered and the scattering amplitudes are simplified by excluding pole terms from three-point vertices.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model with four bosonic fields (three pions and one sigma) to describe hadronic matter, incorporating temperature-dependent effective masses from mean-field effects. They calculated anisotropic shear viscosity coefficients (parallel, perpendicular, and Hall components) within the relaxation time approximation, obtaining relaxation times through the S-matrix approach for point-like interactions in the presence of a constant magnetic field. For charged pions, the phase space was modified to account for Landau level quantization, and only the lowest Landau level contribution was retained under the strong magnetic field approximation.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Vacuum sigma masses considered: m_sigma = 500 MeV and 700 MeV
- Magnetic field strengths used: 5 m_pi^2, 10 m_pi^2, and 15 m_pi^2 (approximately 0.98, 1.96, and 2.94 GeV^2 respectively)
- Chiral symmetry restoration temperature: approximately 245 MeV
- The parallel component of shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta_parallel/s) is plotted as a function of temperature for both m_sigma = 500 MeV and 700 MeV cases

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients in hadronic matter using the linear sigma model by Chakraborty (2010) and Heffernan (2020), who computed shear and bulk viscosity at finite temperature without magnetic fields. It also builds on studies of magnetohydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions by Hongo (2013), Pu (2016), and Roy (2015). The results enable improved input parameters for hydrodynamic simulations of non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where strong magnetic fields of order 10^18 G at RHIC and 10^19 G at LHC are produced.
