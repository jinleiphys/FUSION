# Fission properties of superheavy nuclei for r-process calculations

> We computed a new set of static fission properties suited for r-process calculations. The potential energy surfaces and collective inertias of 3640 nuclei in the superheavy region are obtained from Self-Consistent Mean-Field calculations using the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid energy density functional. The fission path is computed as a function of the quadrupole moment by minimizing the potential energy and exploring octupole and hexadecapole deformations. The spontaneous fission lifetimes are evaluated employing different schemes for the collective inertias and vibrational energy corrections. This allows to explore the sensitivity of the lifetimes to those quantities together with the collective ground state energy along the superheavy landscape. We computed neutron induced stellar reaction rates relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis using the statistical approach and study the impact of collective inertias. The competition between different reaction channels including neutron induced rates, spontaneous fission and alpha decay is discussed for typical r-process conditions.

## Key claim

The authors computed static fission properties for 3640 superheavy nuclei using the BCPM energy density functional, but the spontaneous fission lifetimes are systematically overestimated compared to experimental data due to the static one-dimensional fission path and the imposition of axial symmetry, which neglects triaxiality and dynamic effects that would reduce the lifetimes.

## Method

The potential energy surfaces and collective inertias were obtained from Self-Consistent Mean-Field calculations using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with the BCPM energy density functional, constrained to the quadrupole moment while allowing octupole and hexadecapole deformations. Spontaneous fission lifetimes were evaluated using the WKB semiclassical approximation with three different collective inertia schemes: Adiabatic Time-Dependent HFB (ATDHFB), Gaussian Overlap Approximation to the Generator Coordinate Method (GOA-GCM), and a semiempirical formula. For odd nuclei, the Perturbative Nucleon Addition Method was used to compute bulk nuclear properties, and neutron-induced stellar reaction rates were computed using the Hauser-Feshbach statistical approach.

## Key numbers

- 3640 nuclei in the superheavy region (84 ≤ Z ≤ 120, 118 ≤ N ≤ 250) were computed
- BCPM reproduces experimental inner fission barrier heights with rms deviation of 1.29 MeV (Bjørnholm and Lynn data) and 1.51 MeV (Capote et al. data)
- BCPM reproduces experimental outer fission barrier heights with rms deviation of 0.81 MeV (Bjørnholm and Lynn) and 0.97 MeV (Capote et al.)
- BCPM reproduces experimental isomer excitation energies with rms deviation of 1.22 MeV
- Neutron separation energies S_n are reproduced with rms deviation of 0.30 MeV for even-even nuclei and 0.36 MeV including odd and odd-odd nuclei
- Binding energies of even-even nuclei are reproduced with rms deviation of 2.67 MeV, decreasing to 2.37 MeV when odd nuclei computed with PNAM are included
- Inner fission barrier heights are overestimated up to 2.5 MeV due to axial symmetry imposition
- The semiempirical collective inertia formula uses μ = 0.054 A^(5/3) MeV^(-1) with β_20 as collective variable

## Context

This work extends previous SCMF fission calculations using the BCPM functional (Giuliani 2013) from a reduced set of even-even nuclei to the entire superheavy landscape including odd nuclei, addressing the need for global fission datasets for r-process calculations noted by Goriely (2015). The results are compared with the microscopic-macroscopic FRDM model (Moller 1995) and the HFB21 model (Goriely 2010), and the computed fission properties enable r-process nucleosynthesis calculations in scenarios such as neutron star merger ejecta where fission recycling affects final abundance patterns.
