# Color Superconductivity and Chiral Symmetry Restoration at Nonzero Baryon Density and Temperature

> We explore the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter as a function of temperature and baryon number density, using a class of models for two-flavor QCD in which the interaction between quarks is modelled by that induced by instantons. Our treatment allows us to investigate the possible simultaneous formation of condensates in the conventional quark--anti-quark channel (breaking chiral symmetry) and in a quark--quark channel leading to color superconductivity: the spontaneous breaking of color symmetry via the formation of quark Cooper pairs. At low temperatures, chiral symmetry restoration occurs via a first order transition between a phase with low (or zero) baryon density and a high density color superconducting phase. We find color superconductivity in the high density phase for temperatures less than of order tens to 100 MeV, and find coexisting $<qq>$ and $<\bar q q>$ condensates in this phase in the presence of a current quark mass. At high temperatures, the chiral phase transition is second order in the chiral limit and is a smooth crossover for nonzero current quark mass. A tricritical point separates the first order transition at high densities from the second order transition at high temperatures. In the presence of a current quark mass this tricritical point becomes a second order phase transition with Ising model exponents, suggesting that a long correlation length may develop in heavy ion collisions in which the phase transition is traversed at the appropriate density.

## Key claim

The paper maps the phase diagram of two-flavor QCD using a model where the instanton-induced interaction is treated in mean field theory, finding that at low temperatures chiral symmetry restoration proceeds via a first order transition to a high-density color superconducting phase, and that a tricritical point separates this first order line from a second order transition at high temperatures, but the authors caution that much of the quantitative physics associated with the first order transition is model-dependent, while the physics near the tricritical point is universal.

## Method

The authors use a four-fermion interaction model with the color, flavor, and Lorentz structure of the instanton vertex in two-flavor QCD, implemented via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce bosonic collective fields for the chiral condensate and the scalar diquark condensate. They evaluate the thermodynamic potential in the saddle point (mean field) approximation, including a smooth form factor with cutoff Lambda = 0.8 GeV, and solve the coupled gap equations for the two order parameters as functions of temperature, chemical potential, and current quark mass.

## Key numbers

* At zero temperature and chemical potential, the chiral condensate parameter phi_0^vac = 0.4 GeV, used to fix the coupling G_1 Lambda^2 = 6.47.
* The first order chiral transition at T=0 occurs at chemical potential mu_0 = 0.292 GeV, where the vacuum phase and the color superconducting phase have equal pressure.
* At T=0 in the high-density phase, the diquark gap parameter Delta_0 = 0.072 GeV at the transition point mu = 0.292 GeV.
* Color superconductivity persists for temperatures T less than T_c^Delta of order tens to 100 MeV.
* The cutoff scale Lambda is taken as 0.8 GeV, with the anticipated physical range 0.5-1 GeV.
* The ratio of couplings used for most results is G_2 = G_1 * N_c / (2N_c - 2) = 3G_1/4 for N_c=3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Shuryak (1998) who used variational methods to estimate diquark condensates in the chirally restored phase, and by Bailin and Love (1984) who studied color superconductivity perturbatively at asymptotically high density. The paper generalizes these approaches to include nonzero temperature, current quark mass, and simultaneous chiral and diquark condensation, and identifies a tricritical point whose universal behavior (Ising model exponents) suggests that long correlation lengths may develop in heavy ion collisions traversing the phase transition at appropriate density.
