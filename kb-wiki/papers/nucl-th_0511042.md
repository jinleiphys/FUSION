# The near threshold N N -> d pi reaction in chiral perturbation theory

> The near-threshold n p -> d pi0 cross section is calculated in chiral perturbation theory to next-to-leading order in the expansion parameter sqrt{M m_pi}/Lambda_chi. At this order irreducible pion loops contribute to the relevant pion-production operator. While their contribution to this operator is finite, considering initial-and final-state distortions produces a linear divergence in its matrix elements. We renormalize this divergence by introducing a counterterm, whose value we choose in order to reproduce the threshold n p -> d pi0 cross section measured at TRIUMF. The energy-dependence of this cross section is then predicted in chiral perturbation theory, being determined by the production of p-wave pions, and also by energy dependence in the amplitude for the production of s-wave pions. With an appropriate choice of the counterterm, the chiral prediction for this energy dependence converges well.

## Key claim

The near-threshold $np \to d\pi^0$ cross section is calculated in chiral perturbation theory to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the expansion parameter $\sqrt{M m_\pi}/\Lambda_\chi \approx 0.4$, but the prediction for the energy dependence of the cross section requires introducing a counterterm whose value is fixed by reproducing the measured threshold cross section, meaning the calculation is not a pure prediction at threshold.

## Method

The authors use a "hybrid" chiral perturbation theory approach where chiral power counting is applied to the pion-production transition operator, while initial- and final-state nucleon wave functions are obtained from the Schrodinger equation using either phenomenological potential models (Nijmegen I/II, Argonne v18, Bonn A/B) or chiral wave functions matched to a square well at radius R. The leading-order (LO) operator includes the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and the one-body nucleon-recoil term, while the NLO operator includes irreducible pion-loop diagrams and a Delta-isobar excitation term, with the loop matrix element exhibiting a linear divergence that is renormalized by introducing a short-distance counterterm.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is $x = \sqrt{m_\pi/M} \approx 0.4$, with $P = \sqrt{M m_\pi + m_\pi^2/4} \approx 360$ MeV/c being the initial nucleon relative momentum at threshold
- The measured threshold cross section from TRIUMF gives $\alpha = 184 \pm 5$ $\mu$b and $\beta = 781 \pm 79$ $\mu$b for the reduced s-wave and p-wave cross sections respectively
- The LO prediction for the s-wave parameter $\bar\alpha_0$ using low-$\chi^2$ potential models yields $\bar\alpha_0 = 111 \pm 13$ $\mu$b, which is approximately 60% of the measured $\alpha$
- The LO energy-dependent part of the s-wave amplitude gives $\bar\alpha' = -57 \pm 4$ $\mu$b for potential models, with chiral wave functions giving $\bar\alpha' = 41 \pm 30$ $\mu$b
- The deuteron binding momentum is $\gamma = \sqrt{BM} = 45$ MeV, and the pion mass is taken as $m_\pi = 138.039$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of threshold pion production in few-nucleon systems, which had mixed success: charged-pion production reactions were moderately described by da Rocha et al. using the Weinberg-Tomozawa amplitude, while the $pp \to pp\pi^0$ reaction proved problematic due to its isospin structure forbidding the leading rescattering mechanism. The calculation is a preparatory step toward understanding charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) in $np \to d\pi^0$, particularly the forward-backward asymmetry measured by Allena et al. as $A_{\rm fb} = [17.2 \pm 8({\rm stat}) \pm 5.5({\rm sys})] \times 10^{-4}$, and enables future consistent chiral calculations of CSB parameters that also affect the $dd \to \alpha\pi^0$ reaction.
