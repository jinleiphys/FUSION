# New effective nuclear forces with a finite-range three-body term and their application to AMD+GCM calculations

> We propose new effective inter-nucleon forces with a finite-range three-body operator. The proposed forces are suitable for describing the nuclear structure properties over a wide mass number region, including the saturation point of nuclear matter. The forces are applied to microscopic calculations of $Z=N$ ($A\le 40$) nuclei and O isotopes with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. We present the characteristics of the forces and discuss the importance of the finite-range three-body term.

## Key claim

The paper proposes new effective nuclear forces (F3B forces) that include a finite-range three-body operator expressed by a one-range Gaussian, which can simultaneously reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter, the size and binding energy of light nuclei from alpha to calcium-40, and the alpha-alpha scattering phase shift, whereas forces with zero-range density-dependent terms (such as Gogny, Skyrme, and Modified Volkov forces) fail to describe the compact size of the alpha particle and the alpha-alpha scattering behavior.

## Method

The authors constructed effective central forces consisting of a two-range two-body Gaussian term (with ranges 1.2 fm and 0.7 fm, similar to the Gogny force) plus a one-range finite-range three-body Gaussian term (range parameter 1.25 fm²). They applied these forces to microscopic calculations of Z=N nuclei up to A=40 and oxygen isotopes using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM), including a two-body spin-orbit force with parameters from the G3RS force.

## Key numbers

- The F3B force gives an alpha particle binding energy of 28.2 MeV (experimental: 28.296 MeV) and a point-proton root-mean-square radius of 1.52 fm (experimental: 1.47 fm)
- For oxygen-16, the F3B force yields a binding energy of 129.7 MeV (experimental: 127.617 MeV) and a point-proton radius of 2.57 fm (experimental: 2.57 fm)
- For calcium-40, the F3B force gives a binding energy of 350.6 MeV (experimental: 342.046 MeV) and a point-proton radius of 3.38 fm (experimental: 3.39 fm)
- The two-body force parameters are v1(2) = -198.34 MeV (attractive) and v2(2) = 300.86 MeV (repulsive) with ranges a1(2)=1.2 fm and a2(2)=0.7 fm; the three-body strength is v(3)=600 MeV with range a(3)=1.25 fm²
- The F3B force reproduces the binding energy of the 0+1 state in neon-20 as 157.2 MeV (experimental: 160.647 MeV) and the excitation energy of the 1-1 state as 5.8 MeV (experimental: 5.788 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Tohsaki (2000), who introduced finite-range three-body operators in effective forces for alpha-cluster models, but simplifies the three-body operator from a three-range form to a one-range Gaussian suitable for practical AMD calculations of general nuclei. The new forces overcome limitations of the Minnesota force (Thompson, 1977) and Volkov force (Volkov, 1965), which cannot describe nuclear matter saturation, and also improve upon Gogny forces (Gogny, 1975), Skyrme-type forces (Chabanat, 1997; SIII), and Modified Volkov forces (MV1), which fail to reproduce the alpha particle size and alpha-alpha scattering. The results enable systematic microscopic studies of nuclear structure across a wide mass range, including unstable nuclei, using a single effective interaction without mass-number-dependent parameters.
