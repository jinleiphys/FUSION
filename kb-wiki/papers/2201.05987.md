# Light mesons with one dynamical gluon within basis light-front quantization

> We investigate the structure of the light meson with one dynamical gluon from the light-front QCD Hamiltonian, determined for their constituent quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors, together with a three-dimensional confinement. After fitting the light meson mass spectroscopy, the light-front wave functions provide a good quality description of the pion electromagnetic form factor, and the valence quark distribution functions following QCD scale evolution.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including one dynamical gluon in the light-front QCD Hamiltonian, together with a three-dimensional confinement, yields light-front wave functions that provide a good quality description of the pion electromagnetic form factor and valence quark distribution functions after QCD scale evolution, but this agreement is achieved only after fitting the model parameters to known light meson masses.

## Method

The authors solve the mass-squared eigenvalue problem for an effective light-front QCD Hamiltonian that includes kinetic energy, a three-dimensional confining potential, and quark-gluon interaction vertices, within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework. The meson state is expanded in Fock sectors containing quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon components, and the model parameters are fixed by fitting the known masses of six light mesons (pi, rho, a0, a1, pi', pi1). The resulting light-front wave functions are then used to compute the pion electromagnetic form factor via the Drell-Yan-West formula and the parton distribution functions, which are evolved to higher scales using next-to-next-to-leading order DGLAP equations.

## Key numbers

* The initial scale for DGLAP evolution is determined to be mu0^2 = 0.340 +/- 0.034 GeV^2, obtained by requiring the evolved valence quark PDF first moment to match the global QCD analysis value of <x>_valence = 0.48 +/- 0.01 at mu^2 = 5 GeV^2.
* The first moments of the valence quark, sea quark, and gluon distributions at mu^2 = 4 GeV^2 are <x> = {0.483, 0.096, 0.421}, respectively.
* Gluons carry {39.5, 42.1, 43.9, 44.6, 45.1} percent of pion momentum at the scales mu^2 = {1.69, 4, 10, 16, 27} GeV^2, respectively.
* The behavior of the pion valence PDF at large x is (1-x)^1.77, which approaches the reanalysis of E615 data.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to heavy mesons (Li 2017, Tang 2018, Chen 2018, Li 2019, Lan 2019) and light mesons without dynamical gluons (Jia 2018, Lan 2019, Lan 2019) by including one dynamical gluon in the Fock space, following the Hamiltonian formulation of Lan (2021). The results are compared with the BLFQ-NJL model (Lan 2019, Lan 2019) which had no dynamical gluon at the initial scale, and with global fits from JAM (Barry 2018) and xFitter (Novikov 2020). The obtained light-front wave functions can be used in future studies of generalized parton distributions, transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, and double parton correlations.
