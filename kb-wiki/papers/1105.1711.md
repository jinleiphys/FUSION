# Breakdown of PCAC in diffractive neutrino interactions

> We test the hypothesis of partially conserved axial current (PCAC) in high energy diffractive neutrino production of pions. Since the pion pole contribution to the Adler relation (AR) is forbidden by conservation of the lepton current, the heavier states, like the a_1 pole, ρ-π-cut, etc., control the lifetime of the hadronic fluctuations of the neutrino. We evaluate the deviation from the AR in diffractive neutrino-production of pions on proton and nuclear targets. At high energies, when all the relevant time scales considerably exceed the size of the target, the AR explicitly breaks down on an absorptive target, such as a heavy nucleus. In this regime, close to the black disc limit, the off-diagonal diffractive amplitudes vanish, while the diagonal one, π->π, which enters the AR, maximizes and saturates the unitarity bound. At lower energies, in the regime of short lifetime of heavy hadronic fluctuations the AR is restored, i.e. it is not altered by the nuclear effects.

## Key claim

The Adler relation (AR) between neutrino and pion cross sections, which follows from the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis, breaks down explicitly for diffractive neutrino production of pions on absorptive targets (such as heavy nuclei) at high energies where all relevant time scales exceed the target size, but is restored at lower energies when the lifetime of heavy hadronic fluctuations is short.

## Method

The authors employ a two-channel model replacing all heavy singularities contributing to the axial current (such as the a1 pole and ρ-π cut) by one effective pole "a", assuming the single-diffractive and elastic pion-proton cross sections are equal in the single-Pomeron approximation. They then introduce absorptive corrections from multiple Pomeron exchanges and calculate the unitarized elastic and diffractive cross sections using a Gaussian impact-parameter dependence for the input single-Pomeron elastic partial amplitude. For nuclear targets, they compute coherent and incoherent pion production amplitudes including Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections, integrating over nuclear density distributions with appropriate coherence length factors.

## Key numbers

- The absorptive correction factor K_AR = σ_sd^(πp)/σ_el^(πp) on a proton target shows a deviation from the Adler relation of about 30%
- For the proton target, the total pion-nucleon cross section is parameterized as σ_tot^(πp) = 13.6 mb × s^0.08 + 19.24 mb × s^(-0.458), with s in GeV^2
- The elastic slope parameter is B_el^(πp) = B_0 + 2α_Pom' ln s, where B_0 = 6 GeV^(-2) and α_Pom' = 0.25 GeV^(-2)
- For nuclear targets, the cross section in the regime l_c^a ≫ R_A scales as A^(1/3), while in the regime l_c^a ≪ R_A it scales as A^(2/3) for very heavy nuclei
- The coherence length for pion production is l_c^π = 2ν/(Q^2 + m_π^2), and for heavy states l_c^a = 2ν/(Q^2 + m_a^2)
- Numerical calculations for nuclear ratio R^(coh)_(A/N) at Q^2 = 0 use a constant σ_tot^(πN) = 25 mb

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Piketty and Stodolsky (1970) who identified a potential problem with PCAC in diffractive neutrino interactions, and the subsequent resolution by Belkov and collaborators and Kopeliovich and Marage (1992-1993) who showed that the ρ-π cut rather than the a1 pole dominates the axial current. The paper contradicts the earlier calculations of Belkov (1981) and Rein and Sehgal (1981) for nuclear targets, showing that their results are incorrect in the regime of long coherence length l_c^a ≫ R_A. The findings enable experimental tests of PCAC breakdown in future neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments, particularly through the predicted energy dependence of coherent pion production cross sections on nuclear targets.
