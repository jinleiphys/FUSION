# $η$ and $η'$ decays into lepton pairs

> In this work, we calculate the branching ratios for the $η(η')\rightarrow\bar{\ell}\ell$ decays, where $\ell = e,μ$. These processes have tiny rates in the standard model due to spin flip, loop, and electromagnetic suppression, for what they could be sensitive to New Physics effects. In order to provide a reliable input for the Standard Model, we exploit the general analytical properties of the amplitude. For that purpose, we invoke the machinery of Canterbury approximants, which provides a systematic description of the underlying hadronic physics in a data-driven fashion. Given the current experimental discrepancies, we discuss in detail the role of the resonant region and comment on the reliability of $χ$PT calculations. Finally, we discuss the kind of new physics which we think would be relevant to account for them.

## Key claim

The Standard Model branching ratios for η(η') → ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ are calculated using Canterbury approximants to provide a data-driven description of the transition form factors, with the caveat that the unknown double-virtual slope parameter a_{P;1,1} must be varied between the factorization limit (b_P²) and the OPE limit (2b_P²), and that intermediate hadronic states invalidate the unitary bound for η and η' decays.

## Method

The authors use Canterbury approximants (bivariate Padé approximants) to reconstruct the η and η' transition form factors from single-virtual data and theoretical constraints, then evaluate the two-photon loop integral exactly (including the Z-boson contribution) to obtain the decay amplitude. A toy model incorporating ρ, ω, and φ resonances with ππ threshold effects is employed to assess the systematic error from intermediate hadronic states, using a dispersive representation of the loop integral.

## Key numbers

- BR(η → e⁺e⁻) = 5.0(2) × 10⁻⁹ (combined result from the Canterbury approximant method)
- BR(η → μ⁺μ⁻) = 5.4(2) × 10⁻⁶ (combined result from the Canterbury approximant method)
- BR(η′ → e⁺e⁻) = 1.6(1) × 10⁻¹⁰ (combined result from the Canterbury approximant method)
- BR(η′ → μ⁺μ⁻) = 1.7(2) × 10⁻⁷ (combined result from the Canterbury approximant method)
- Slope parameter b_η = 0.576(12) from single-virtual TFF data (Escribano 2015)
- Slope parameter b_η′ = 1.31(4) from single-virtual TFF data (Escribano 2015)
- F_ηγγ(0,0) = 0.274(5) GeV⁻¹ from PDG Γ(η→γγ)
- F_η′γγ(0,0) = 0.344(6) GeV⁻¹ from PDG Γ(η′→γγ)
- Experimental BR(η→μ⁺μ⁻) = 5.8(8) × 10⁻⁶ (PDG combination of SATURNE II and Lepton-G measurements)
- Experimental upper limit BR(η′→e⁺e⁻) ≤ 5.6 × 10⁻⁹ (SND and CMD-3 at VEPP-2000)
- Experimental upper limit BR(η→e⁺e⁻) ≤ 2.3 × 10⁻⁶ (HADES combined p+p and p+Nb data)

## Context

This work extends the Canterbury approximant method previously applied to π⁰→e⁺e⁻ (Masjuan 2015) to the η and η′ cases, where intermediate hadronic states (ρ, ω, φ) produce additional imaginary parts absent in the pion case. The results improve upon earlier χPT calculations (Savage, Luke, Wise 1992; Gomez Dumm and Pich 1998) and VMD-based estimates (Quigg and Jackson 1968; Ametller et al. 1993; Silagadze 2006; Dorokhov 2007, 2009) by providing a data-driven systematic error assessment. The predictions enable comparison with ongoing and future experiments at COMPASS, LHCb, and VEPP-2000, and provide a Standard Model baseline for New Physics searches in lepton flavor universality tests.
