# On kaonic hydrogen. Phenomenological quantum field theoretic model revisited

> We argue that due to isospin and U-spin invariance of strong low-energy interactions the S-wave scattering lengths a^0_0 and a^1_0 of bar-KN scattering with isospin I=0 and I = 1 satisfy the low-energy theorem a^0_0 + 3 a^1_0 = 0 valid to leading order in chiral expansion. In the model of strong low-energy bar-KN interactions at threshold (EPJA 21,11 (2004)) we revisit the contribution of the Sigma(1750) resonance, which does not saturate the low-energy theorem a^0_0 + 3 a^1_0 = 0, and replace it by the baryon background with properties of an SU(3) octet. We calculate the S-wave scattering amplitudes of K^-N and K^-d scattering at threshold. We calculate the energy level displacements of the ground states of kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium. The result obtained for kaonic hydrogen agrees well with recent experimental data by the DEAR Collaboration. We analyse the cross sections for elastic and inelastic K^-p scattering for laboratory momenta of the incident K^- meson from the domain 70 MeV/c < p_K < 150 MeV/c. The theoretical results agree with the available experimental data within two standard deviations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the S-wave scattering lengths \(a^0_0\) and \(a^1_0\) of \(\bar{K}N\) scattering satisfy the low-energy theorem \(a^0_0 + 3 a^1_0 = 0\) to leading order in chiral expansion, and that the \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance does not saturate this sum rule, requiring its replacement by a baryon background with properties of an SU(3) octet.

## Method

The authors revisit their phenomenological quantum field theoretic model for strong low-energy \(\bar{K}N\) interactions at threshold (Ivanov et al., EPJA 21, 11 (2004)), replacing the \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance contribution with a baryon background having SU(3) octet properties. They calculate S-wave scattering amplitudes for \(K^-p\) and \(K^-n\) scattering at threshold using effective Lagrangians coupling strange baryon resonances to octets of baryons and pseudoscalar mesons, and compute the energy level displacements of kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium via the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring-Trueman (DGBTT) formula. They also analyze cross sections for elastic and inelastic \(K^-p\) scattering for laboratory momenta from 70 MeV/c to 150 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

*   S-wave amplitude of \(K^-p\) scattering at threshold: \(f^{K^-p}_0(0) = (-0.50 \pm 0.05) + i(0.35 \pm 0.02)\) fm.
*   S-wave amplitude of \(K^-n\) scattering at threshold: \(f^{K^-n}_0(0) = (+0.50 \pm 0.02) + i(0.04 \pm 0.00)\) fm.
*   Complex S-wave scattering lengths: \(\tilde{a}^0_0 = (-1.50 \pm 0.05) + i(0.66 \pm 0.04)\) fm and \(\tilde{a}^1_0 = (+0.50 \pm 0.02) + i(0.04 \pm 0.00)\) fm.
*   Energy level displacement of kaonic hydrogen ground state (including dispersive corrections): \(-\epsilon^{(\rm th)}_{1s} + i\Gamma^{(\rm th)}_{1s}/2 = (-223 \pm 21) + i(159 \pm 9)\) eV.
*   Energy level displacement of kaonic deuterium ground state: \(-\epsilon_{1s} + i\Gamma_{1s}/2 = (-343 \pm 42) + i(315 \pm 48)\) eV.
*   Contribution of the \(\sigma^{(I=1)}_{KN}(0)\)-term to the energy level shift of kaonic hydrogen: \((67 \pm 17)\) eV, using \(\sigma^{(I=1)}_{KN}(0) = (200 \pm 50)\) MeV.
*   S-wave scattering length of \(K^-d\) scattering: \(a^{K^-d}_0 = (-0.57 \pm 0.07)\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Ivanov et al., EPJA 21, 11 (2004)) by correcting the treatment of the \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance, which did not satisfy the low-energy theorem \(a^0_0 + 3 a^1_0 = 0\). The calculated energy level displacement for kaonic hydrogen agrees with experimental data from the DEAR Collaboration. The results for the complex S-wave scattering length \(\tilde{a}^0_0\) agree with the value obtained by Dalitz and Deloff (1991). The analysis enables future extraction of the \(\sigma^{(I=1)}_{KN}(0)\)-term from experimental data on kaonic hydrogen energy level shifts, and provides predictions for kaonic deuterium that can be tested by future experiments.
