# Nonstandard interactions of neutrinos with dense matter

> Nonstandard interaction is expected to be a crucial hint in explaining the experimental data on neutrino scattering off electrons. In this context, the nonstandard interaction vector and axial-vector couplings are needed to be extracted from recent experiments and a few of them are now available in the literature. With these coupling bounds, in this paper, I explore their impacts on the neutrinos interacting with the free electron gas in dense matter. To this end, I compute and predict the neutrino differential cross section and mean free path in dense matter for those existing experimental bounds. Interesting behavior in the neutrino cross sections and mean free path is found for the different nonstandard interaction couplings from different experiment constraints. I also found that the neutrino cross-section and mean free path in the dense matter are very sensitive to values and signs of the nonstandard interaction couplings, leading to different prediction results to the Standard Model cross-section and mean free path as well as their totals, which is given by a sum of the Standard Model and nonstandard interaction.

## Key claim

The paper explores how existing experimental bounds on nonstandard interaction (NSI) vector and axial-vector couplings, extracted from solar, atmospheric, reactor, and long-baseline neutrino experiments at 90% confidence level, affect the neutrino differential cross section and mean free path in dense matter; the author finds that the results are very sensitive to both the values and signs of these couplings, leading to predictions that can differ substantially from the Standard Model.

## Method

The author computes the neutral-current neutrino differential cross section per unit volume for elastic electron neutrino scattering off a free electron gas in dense matter, using an NSI effective four-fermion Lagrangian. The electron polarization tensor is evaluated in the finite-density medium using the electron propagator at a given electron Fermi momentum, and the response functions are derived from the imaginary parts of the transverse, longitudinal, axial, and mixed vector-axial polarizations. The neutrino mean free path is then obtained by integrating the differential cross section over the time and vector components of the momentum transfer, for initial neutrino energies of 5 MeV and 10 MeV and nuclear densities of 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 times the nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Fermi constant used: \(G_F = 1.023 \times 10^{-5} / M^2\) with nucleon free mass \(M = 939\) MeV
- Electron mass: \(m_e = 0.511\) MeV
- Weak mixing angle: \(\sin^2 \theta_W = 0.231\)
- Initial neutrino energies: \(E_\nu = 5\) MeV and \(E_\nu = 10\) MeV
- Fixed three-momentum transfer magnitude: \(|\mathbf{q}| = 2.5\) MeV
- Nuclear densities: \(\rho = 1.0\ \rho_0\), \(2.0\ \rho_0\), and \(3.0\ \rho_0\)
- For Borexino Phase I, the NSI coupling combinations yield vector and axial-vector pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-0.256, 0.164]\) and \([0.213, -0.107]\)
- For TEXONO, coupling combinations yield pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-1.60, -1.46]\) and \([0.46, 0.3]\)
- For LSND, coupling combinations yield pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-1.07, 0.93]\) and \([0.61, -0.39]\)
- For Super-K+KamLAND, coupling combinations yield pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-0.201, 0.159]\) and \([0.562, -0.458]\)
- For Borexino Phase II first region, coupling combinations yield pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-1.6, -1.14]\) and \([-1.22, -1.36]\)
- For Borexino Phase II second region, coupling combinations yield pairs such as \([\mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eV}, \mathcal{E}_{ee}^{eA}] = [-0.2, 0.26]\) and \([0.13, -0.01]\)
- The differential cross section increases by a factor of 5 at the peak when the initial neutrino energy increases from 5 MeV to 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino Standard Model interactions with dense matter by Kalempouw-Williams (2005), Horowitz (1990), Reddy (1998), and Hutauruk (2021, 2023), incorporating nonstandard interactions constrained by recent experiments including Borexino Phase I (Agarwalla 2012), Borexino Phase II (Coloma 2022), Super-Kamiokande and KamLAND (Bolanos 2008), LSND (Davidson 2003), and TEXONO (2010). The results provide predictions for the neutrino mean free path in dense matter that may be relevant for supernova simulations and neutron star cooling, where the mean free path is an important input.
