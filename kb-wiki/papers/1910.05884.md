# Study of $Ξ^-$ hypernuclei in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

> The properties of $Ξ^-$ hypernuclei are studied systematically using a two-dimensional Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach combined with three different $ΞN$ Skyrme forces fitted to reproduce the existing data. We explore the impurity effect of a single $Ξ^-$ hyperon on the radii, deformations, and density distributions of the nuclear core and point out qualitative differences between the different forces. We find that the $Ξ^-$ removal energy of $^{\hskip0.10em13}_{Ξp}$B [$^{12}$C(g.s.)+ $Ξ^-$(1p)] calculated by the SLX3 force is 0.7 MeV, which is in good agreement with a possible value of $0.82\pm0.17\;$MeV from the KEK E176 experiment. The theoretical prediction for this weakly bound state depends strongly on the deformation of the nuclear core, which is analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates properties of Ξ⁻ hypernuclei using a two-dimensional Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with three different ΞN Skyrme forces, finding that only the SLX3 force simultaneously reproduces the existing experimental data for both the weakly bound p-state hypernucleus ¹³_ΞpB and the ground-state hypernucleus ¹²_ΞsBe, while the other forces fail to match both datasets.

## Method

The authors employ a two-dimensional (axially symmetric) Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with the SLy4 nucleon-nucleon interaction and three different ΞN Skyrme forces (SLX0, SLX2, SLX3) that were previously fitted to reproduce the Ξ⁻ removal energy of ¹⁵_ΞpC. The deformed SHF Schrödinger equation is solved in cylindrical coordinates using an axially-deformed harmonic-oscillator basis, allowing self-consistent calculation of binding energies, deformations, radii, and density distributions for Ξ⁻ hypernuclei from ⁹_ΞLi to ²⁰⁹_ΞTl.

## Key numbers

* Ξ⁻ removal energy of ¹³_ΞpB [¹²C(g.s.) + Ξ⁻(1p)] with SLX3 force: 0.7 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.82 ± 0.17 MeV from KEK E176
* Ξ⁻ removal energy of ¹⁵_ΞpC [¹⁴N(g.s.) + Ξ⁻(1p)]: 1.1 MeV for all three forces (by construction), compared to experimental value 1.11 ± 0.25 MeV
* Ξ⁻ removal energy of ¹²_ΞsBe with SLX3: 5.2 MeV (2.6 MeV without ΞN Coulomb interaction)
* Ξ⁻ removal energy of ²⁰⁹_ΞsTl with SLX3: 31.8 MeV (8.8 MeV without ΞN Coulomb interaction)
* SLX3 force parameters: a₀ = -228 MeV·fm³, a₃ = 1000 MeV·fm⁶; transition density between repulsion and attraction occurs at ρ_N = 0.114 fm⁻³
* Core radius change δR_N for ⁹_ΞsLi: positive with SLX0/SLX2 (contraction), negative with SLX3 (dilatation)

## Context

This work extends previous one-dimensional Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Sun et al. (2018) to include deformation effects in two dimensions, and provides systematic predictions for Ξ⁻ hypernuclei across a wide mass range. The results are compared with cluster model calculations by Hiyama et al. (2008) and relativistic mean-field calculations by Hu et al. (2014), showing that the SLX3 force yields binding energies similar to those approaches. The paper enables future experimental identification of bound Ξ⁻ hypernuclei at J-PARC and other facilities, and provides constraints for determining more precise ΞN Skyrme force parameters.
