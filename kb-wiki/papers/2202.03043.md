# Dynamics of rotation in chiral nuclei

> The dynamics of chiral nuclei is investigated for the first time with the time-dependent and tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theories on a three-dimensional space lattice in a microscopic and self-consistent way. The experimental energies of the two pairs of the chiral doublet bands in $^{135}$Nd are well reproduced without any adjustable parameters beyond the well-defined density functional. A novel mechanism, i.e., chiral precession, is revealed from the microscopic dynamics of the total angular momentum in the body-fixed frame, whose harmonicity is associated with a transition from the planar into aplanar rotations with the increasing spin. This provides a fully microscopic and dynamical view to understand the chiral excitations in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral precession, a novel mechanism where the total angular momentum vector rotates about an axis in the body-fixed frame, is revealed from the microscopic dynamics of chiral nuclei, and that the harmonicity of this precession is associated with a transition from planar to aplanar rotations with increasing spin, but only for calculations performed at fixed angular momentum rather than fixed rotational frequency.

## Method

The authors employed time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) with initial states obtained from static three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) calculations, both solved on a three-dimensional cubic lattice with length 24 fm and mesh size 0.8 fm using the PC-PK1 density functional. The TAC-CDFT provided self-consistent solutions for given spins, from which time-dependent wave functions were evolved in the space-fixed frame, and the dynamical behavior of the total angular momentum was analyzed in the body-fixed frame by calculating the principal axes of inertia. For the odd-A nucleus 135Nd, two configurations were studied: πh11/2^2 ⊗ νh11/2^{-1} and π[h11/2^1(gd)^1] ⊗ νh11/2^{-1}.

## Key numbers

* At I = 19/2 ħ, the total energy surface minimum for the π[h11/2^1(gd)^1] ⊗ νh11/2^{-1} configuration in 135Nd is located at θ_J = 48.5° and φ_J = 0°, indicating planar rotation in the short-long plane.
* The TDCDFT calculations were performed in a cubic box with length 24 fm and mesh size 0.8 fm.
* For the total Routhian surface analysis, a rotational frequency of ħω = 0.46 MeV was used.
* The experimental energies of two pairs of chiral doublet bands in 135Nd are well reproduced by combining TAC-CDFT results with chiral excitation energies extracted from TDCDFT via Fourier analysis.

## Context

This work extends previous static approaches to nuclear chirality, such as the particle rotor model (Frauendorf and Meng, 1997), tilted axis cranking mean-field methods (Dimitrov, 2000), and random-phase approximation (Mukhopadhyay, 2007), which could not describe the dynamics of chiral excitations. The authors note that prior TAC-CDFT studies (Zhao, 2017) could only calculate the lower band of chiral doublet bands. This work enables a fully microscopic and self-consistent description of chiral doublet bands within density functional theory, and suggests that future work could derive a collective Hamiltonian with the Gaussian overlap approximation in the space of tilted angles, though many open questions remain about the quality of that approximation and the determination of mass inertia.
