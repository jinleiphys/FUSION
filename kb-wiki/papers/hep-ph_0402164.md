# Is There Only One Solution of the Dyson-Schwinger Equation for Quark Propagator in the Case of Non-zero Current Quark Mass

> In this letter it is shown on general ground that there exist two qualitatively distinct solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator in the case of non-zero current quark mass. One solution corresponds to the ``Nambu-Goldstone'' phase and the other one corresponds to the ``Wigner'' phase in the chiral limit.

# Is There Only One Solution of the Dyson-Schwinger Equation for Quark Propagator in the Case of Non-zero Current Quark Mass?

## Key claim
Contrary to the prevailing view in the literature that only the Nambu-Goldstone solution exists for non-zero current quark mass, the authors show that two qualitatively distinct solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator exist even when the current quark mass is non-zero. Both solutions have non-zero scalar self-energy function B(p^2), but one corresponds to the Nambu-Goldstone phase and the other to the Wigner phase from the chiral limit.

## Method
The authors separate the contributions of dynamical and explicit chiral symmetry breaking by differentiating the dressed quark propagator with respect to the current quark mass m, relating it to the scalar vertex. They then substitute the two known solutions from the chiral limit (Nambu-Goldstone with B(p^2) != 0 and Wigner with B(p^2) = 0) into the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations for the case m != 0, obtaining two distinct sets of equations for the scalar functions E(p^2) and F(p^2). A simple infrared-dominant model gluon propagator (Munczek-Nemirovsky model) is used to reduce the integral equations to algebraic equations, demonstrating the existence of both solutions.

## Key numbers
- The model gluon propagator scale parameter eta appears as the strength of the infrared slavery effect, with the condition p^2 < eta^2/4 defining the region where the Nambu-Goldstone solution has B(p^2) = (eta^2 - 4 p^2)^(1/2) and A(p^2) = 2.
- For p^2 >= eta^2/4, the Nambu-Goldstone solution gives B(p^2) = 0 and A(p^2) = (1/2)[1 + (1 + 2 eta^2/p^2)^(1/2)].
- The Wigner solution in the chiral limit gives A'(p^2) = (1/2)[1 + (1 + 2 eta^2/p^2)^(1/2)] with B'(p^2) = 0.

## Context
This work extends the rigorous proof of two distinct solutions (Nambu-Goldstone and Wigner) of the Dyson-Schwinger equation from the chiral limit to the case of non-zero current quark mass, contradicting the common assumption stated in Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Tandy (1997) that only one solution exists for m != 0. The approach enables a separate analysis of dynamical versus explicit chiral symmetry breaking effects, and the authors note it can be extended to finite chemical potential to study QCD phase structure. The method is general and does not depend on the rainbow approximation used for the explicit demonstration.
