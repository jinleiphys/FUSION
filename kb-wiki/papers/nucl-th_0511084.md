# Inhomogeneous freeze-out in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A QCD phase transition may reflect in a inhomogeneous decoupling surface of hadrons produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that due to the non-linear dependence of the particle densities on the temperature and baryon-chemical potential such inhomogeneities should be visible even in the integrated, inclusive abundances. We analyze experimental data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS and Au+Au collisions at BNL-RHIC to determine the amplitude of inhomogeneities.

## Key claim
Inhomogeneities in the temperature and baryon-chemical potential on the freeze-out surface in relativistic heavy-ion collisions do not average out in inclusive hadron abundances, due to the non-linear dependence of particle densities on these intensive variables; the effect is statistically significant only at intermediate CERN-SPS energies (30-160 GeV per nucleon), not at lower SPS or higher RHIC energies.

## Method
The authors extended the standard grand-canonical freeze-out model by assuming a Gaussian distribution of temperatures and baryon-chemical potentials across many "domains" on the decoupling surface, each of size ~1-2 fm. They computed average particle densities as convolutions of local ideal-gas densities (with excluded-volume corrections) over these Gaussian distributions, then performed least-square fits to experimental particle ratios from NA49 (Pb+Pb at 20-158 GeV per nucleon) and STAR (Au+Au at 130 and 200 GeV center-of-mass energy) to extract the mean and width parameters.

## Key numbers
- At intermediate SPS energies (30-160 GeV per nucleon), the inhomogeneous model gives a significantly lower chi-squared per degree of freedom than the homogeneous model, falling outside the 95.4% confidence interval for homogeneous freeze-out.
- For massless particles, the shift in average emission temperature is only quadratic in delta-T/mean-T: T*/mean-T = 1 + 3 (delta-T^2 / mean-T^2).
- For massive particles in the Boltzmann limit, the emission temperature shift is linear: T*/mean-T = 1 + (delta-T^2 / mean-T^2) * (m/mean-T + 3/2), which can be of order unity.
- The enhancement factor for heavy particle densities scales exponentially with (delta-mu_B / mean-T)^2.
- The excluded-volume parameter used was R_0 = 0.3 fm for all hadron species.
- The fits used a grid with 1 MeV steps in T and delta-T, and 10 MeV steps in mu and delta-mu.

## Context
This work extends the standard homogeneous grand-canonical freeze-out model used extensively by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2004) and Redlich et al. (2005) to include spatial inhomogeneities, motivated by predictions from Bower et al. (2001) and Paech et al. (2003) that a first-order QCD phase transition could produce density inhomogeneities on the freeze-out surface. The approach is related to "superstatistics" for non-equilibrium systems with fluctuating intensive quantities. The results suggest that at intermediate SPS energies the freeze-out surface is not well-stirred, potentially indicating a first-order phase transition, while at lower and higher energies a homogeneous description remains adequate. Future work could connect the observed inhomogeneity amplitudes to properties of the phase transition such as latent heat and interface tension.
