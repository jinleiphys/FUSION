> We have studied the $П„^-\to K^-О·^{(\prime)}ОЅ_П„$ decays within Chiral Perturbation Theory including resonances as explicit degrees of freedom. ## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$ decay data can be described by two approaches (exponential resummation and dispersive representation) that treat final-state interactions more soundly than a simple Breit-Wigner model, and that these fits yield competitive determinations of the $K^\star(1410)$ pole parameters; however, the Breit-Wigner model fails to account for the data on the $K\eta$ mode.

## Method

The authors computed the vector and scalar form factors for $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^{(\prime)}\nu_\tau$ decays within Resonance Chiral Perturbation Theory ($R\chi T$), including the $K^\star(892)$, $K^\star(1410)$, and $K^\star_0(1430)$ resonances. They implemented three different treatments of final-state interactions: a Breit-Wigner model with energy-dependent widths, an exponential resummation (JPP approach), and a dispersive representation (BEJ approach), and fitted the resulting form factors to the BaBar and Belle data on the $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$ decay.

## Key numbers

- $M_{K^{\star\prime}} = (1330^{+27}_{-41})$ MeV, the pole mass of the $K^\star(1410)$ resonance determined from fits to $\tau^-\to K^-\eta\nu_\tau$ data
- $\Gamma_{K^{\star\prime}} = (217^{+68}_{-122})$ MeV, the pole width of the $K^\star(1410)$ resonance from the same fits
- $\theta_P = (-13.3 \pm 1.0)^\circ$, the $\eta-\eta^\prime$ mixing angle used in the analysis
- $|V_{us}f_+^{K^-\pi^0}(0)| = 0.21664 \pm 0.00048$, the normalization factor taken from semileptonic kaon decay data
- $S_{EW} = 1.0201$, the electroweak correction factor used in the differential decay width formula

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\tau^-\to (K\pi)^-\nu_\tau$ decays by Jamin, Oller, and Pich (2006-2008) and by Boito, Escribano, and Jamin (2008-2010), which determined $K^\star(892)$ and $K^\star(1410)$ pole parameters from $K\pi$ data. The paper applies similar form factor techniques to the $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^{(\prime)}\nu_\tau$ channels, which are sensitive to the $K^\star(1410)$ resonance and can provide competitive parameter determinations. The results enable improved modeling of these decays in the TAUOLA Monte Carlo generator and may help discover the $\tau^-\to K^-\eta^\prime\nu_\tau$ decay mode, for which only an upper limit exists from BaBar (2012).
