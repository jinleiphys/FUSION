# Exclusive vector meson production with a leading neutron in photon - hadron interactions at hadronic colliders

> In this paper we study leading neutron production in photon - hadron interactions which take place in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions at large impact parameters. Using a model that describes the recent leading neutron data at HERA, we consider exclusive vector meson production in association with a leading neutron in $pp/pA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The total cross sections and rapidity distributions of $ρ$, $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ produced together with a leading neutron are computed. Our results indicate that the study of these processes is feasible and that it can be used to improve the understanding of leading neutron processes and of exclusive vector meson production.

# Exclusive vector meson production with a leading neutron in photon-hadron interactions at hadronic colliders

## Key claim
Exclusive vector meson production (rho, phi, J/Psi) in association with a leading neutron is feasible in pp and pA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, provided that the absorptive corrections (parameterized by a constant K-factor in the range 0.152 to 0.205) and the dipole-pion to dipole-proton amplitude ratio (R_q = 2/3) assumed in the model are correct.

## Method
The authors extend the color dipole formalism, previously applied to exclusive vector meson photoproduction in ep collisions, to gamma-pion interactions in hadronic colliders. They factorize the process into: (i) photon emission from one hadron (using equivalent photon fluxes for protons and nuclei), (ii) fluctuation of the photon into a quark-antiquark dipole, (iii) diffractive dipole-pion scattering described by the bCGC model for the dipole-proton amplitude scaled by R_q = 2/3, and (iv) formation of the vector meson and leading neutron via pion emission from the proton, using a pion flux model (f_3) with a form factor parameter b = 0.3 GeV^-2 and coupling g^2/(4 pi) = 14.4. The absorptive corrections are approximated by a constant K-factor constrained by HERA data on exclusive rho production with a leading neutron.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for rho production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: 110.51 nb (K_min), 130.14 nb (K_med), 149.04 nb (K_max)
- Total cross section for J/Psi production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: 0.35 nb (K_min), 0.42 nb (K_med), 0.48 nb (K_max)
- Total cross section for rho production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV: 139110 nb (K_min), 163821 nb (K_med), 187616 nb (K_max)
- Total cross section for J/Psi production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV: 234.50 nb (K_min), 276.16 nb (K_med), 317.05 nb (K_max)
- Total cross section for rho production in pAu collisions at sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV: 9176.88 nb (K_min), 10807.00 nb (K_med), 12376.70 nb (K_max)
- Predicted rho production rate at LHC: approximately 10^10 events per year in pp collisions and 10^7 events per year in pPb collisions
- Predicted J/Psi production rate at LHC: approximately 10^7 events per year in pp collisions and 10^4 events per year in pPb collisions
- The K-factor values used: K_min = 0.152, K_med = 0.179, K_max = 0.205, determined from HERA data on exclusive rho photoproduction with a leading neutron

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous model for inclusive and exclusive leading neutron processes in ep collisions (Goncalves et al., 2015, 2016) to photon-hadron interactions at hadronic colliders. The model uses the bCGC dipole amplitude (Kowalski, Motyka, Watt, 2006) which reproduces HERA data on exclusive rho and J/Psi production and LHC data on exclusive vector meson photoproduction in pp and pPb collisions (Goncalves et al., 2014, 2015). The predictions are smaller by approximately two orders of magnitude compared to vector meson photoproduction without a leading neutron (Vicmag et al., Schafer et al., Motyka and Watt, Glauber et al.), consistent with HERA results. The study enables experimental tests at RHIC and LHC using forward neutron tagging with Zero Degree Calorimeters (Contreras, 2015; STAR Collaboration), which could constrain the description of leading neutron processes and the underlying QCD dynamics at small x.
