# Thermal photons and dileptons

> We discuss the status of a subset of penetrating probes in relativistic nuclear collisions. Thermal photons and dileptons are considered, as well as the electromagnetic signature of jets.

# Thermal photons and dileptons

## Key claim
Thermal photons and dileptons are penetrating probes that can reveal properties of the hot and dense medium created in relativistic nuclear collisions, but the original goal of isolating a clear signal of chiral symmetry restoration from dilepton data has remained elusive due to the difficulty of directly extracting an axialvector correlator from heavy ion data.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical calculations of lepton pair emission rates using finite-temperature field theory, where the emission rate is expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the in-medium retarded photon self-energy related to the vector spectral density via Vector Meson Dominance. For photon production from jet-plasma interactions, Fokker-Planck equations are solved to obtain the time-evolution of hard parton distributions, with transition rates containing resummation effects typical of interactions with a thermal medium, and the resulting medium-modified fragmentation functions are used to compute pion and photon spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature of 370 MeV used in the jet-quenching calculation, consistent with the study in Turbide, Rapp, Gale (2004)
- Strong coupling constant α_s = 0.34 yields a good fit to the R_AA data for π^0 at p_T ≥ 4 GeV
- The R_AA data for π^0 covers an impressive range of almost 20 GeV/c
- When jet-photon conversions are omitted, total photon production is reduced by up to 45% around p_T = 3 GeV/c
- The intermediate-mass NA50 data does not demand a large contribution from plasma radiation (it is about 20% in the calculation shown)
- The K_jet factor of approximately 1.7 accounts for higher order effects in the pp cross section calculation
- The combination of uncertainties associated with determination of ⟨N_coll⟩ and systematic measurement uncertainties is roughly of the order of 10%

## Context
This review paper summarizes the status of electromagnetic probes in relativistic heavy ion collisions as of the Quark Matter 2005 conference, extending earlier work by the Helios/3 and CERES collaborations at SPS energies and by PHENIX at RHIC. The paper discusses how new NA60 data for In-In collisions at the CERN SPS have sufficient precision to distinguish between different theoretical approaches to in-medium vector meson modifications, favoring many-body calculations (Rapp and Wambach, 2000) over models with large mass shifts. The work enables a consistent treatment of electromagnetic signals and hard hadronic probes, showing that jet-photon conversion in the plasma is a significant contribution that must be included alongside thermal photons and fragmentation photons to describe PHENIX data.
