# Improved limits on the hadronic and semi-hadronic CP violating parameters and role of a dark force carrier in the electric dipole moment of $^{199}$Hg

> Combining the recently reported electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{199}$Hg atom due to breaking of parity and time-reversal symmetries with the improved relativistic atomic calculations, precise limits on the tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) electron-nucleus (e-N) coupling coefficient and the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interactions are determined. Using these limits with the nuclear calculations, we infer limits on the EDMs of neutron and proton as $d_n < 2.2 \times 10^{-26} |e| \rm{cm}$ and $d_p < 2.1 \times 10^{-25} |e| \rm{cm}$, respectively, and on the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) parameter and the combined up- and down- quark chromo-EDMs as $|\barθ| < 1.1 \times 10^{-10}$ and $|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 5.5 \times 10^{-27} |e|\rm{cm}$, respectively. These are the best limits till date to probe new sources of CP violation beyond the standard model (SM) from a diamagnetic atom. Role of considering a capable many-body method to account the electron correlation effects to all orders for inferring the above limits has been highlighted. From this analysis, constraints on the T-PT e-N coupling coefficient with a large range of mass of a possible dark matter carrier $χ$ between the atomic electrons and nucleus are given.

## Key claim

By combining the latest experimental EDM measurement of $^{199}$Hg with improved relativistic atomic calculations using the coupled-cluster method that includes electron correlation effects to all orders, the paper derives the most stringent limits to date on several CP-violating parameters from a diamagnetic atom, though these limits depend on the accuracy of both the atomic and nuclear calculations employed.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic atomic structure calculations for the $^{199}$Hg atom using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method, random phase approximation (RPA), and relativistic coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles (CCSD) to compute the atomic EDM enhancement factors for the tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) electron-nucleus interaction and the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interaction. They combined these atomic enhancement factors with the experimental upper limit on the $^{199}$Hg EDM from Graner et al. (2016) to extract limits on the T-PT coupling coefficient $C_T$, the NSM $S$, and then used nuclear model relations to infer limits on the neutron and proton EDMs, the QCD theta parameter, and quark chromo-EDMs. For the dark force carrier analysis, they computed the atomic EDM enhancement for a Yukawa-type interaction mediated by a massive gauge boson $\chi$ over a range of masses from 0.0008 to 4882.81 MeV/$c^2$.

## Key numbers

- Experimental upper limit on $^{199}$Hg EDM: $|d(^{199}{\rm{Hg}})| < 7.4 \times 10^{-30}$ $|e|$cm at 95% confidence level (Graner et al., 2016)
- Limit on T-PT e-N coupling coefficient: $|C_T| < 7.0 \times 10^{-10}$
- Limit on nuclear Schiff moment: $|S| < 4.2 \times 10^{-13}$ $|e|$fm$^3$
- Limit on neutron EDM: $d_n < 2.2 \times 10^{-26}$ $|e|$cm
- Limit on proton EDM: $d_p < 2.1 \times 10^{-25}$ $|e|$cm
- Limit on QCD theta parameter: $|\bar{\theta}| < 1.1 \times 10^{-10}$
- Limit on combined up- and down-quark chromo-EDMs: $|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 5.5 \times 10^{-27}$ $|e|$cm
- Limit on S-PS e-N coupling coefficient: $|C_P| < 1.9 \times 10^{-7}$
- CCSD-calculated dipole polarizability of Hg: 34.51 $|e|a_0^2$, compared to experimental value 33.91(34) $|e|a_0^2$ (Goebel et al.)
- For $m_{\chi} = \infty$, CCSD atomic EDM enhancement for T-PT interaction: $-3.17 \times 10^{-20}C_T \langle \sigma_N\rangle$ $|e|$cm (Fermi distribution) and $-318.46 \times 10^{-20}C_T^{\chi} \langle \sigma_N\rangle$ $|e|$cm (uniform distribution)
- For $m_{\chi} = 0.0008$ MeV/$c^2$, CCSD atomic EDM enhancement: $-5.4 \times 10^{-5} \times 10^{-18}C_T^{\chi} \langle \sigma_N\rangle$ $|e|$cm

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Sahoo (2015) by incorporating the improved experimental EDM measurement of $^{199}$Hg from Graner et al. (2016), which improved the limit by nearly an order of magnitude over the earlier measurement by Griffith et al. (2009). The atomic calculations improve upon the RPA-based analysis of Derevianko (2016) for the dark force carrier constraints by using the more accurate CCSD method that includes both core-polarization and pair-correlation effects to all orders. The derived limits on the neutron EDM ($d_n < 2.2 \times 10^{-26}$ $|e|$cm) are more stringent than the direct measurement limit of $d_n < 3.0 \times 10^{-26}$ $|e|$cm from Pendelbury et al. (2015). These results provide model-independent constraints on beyond-Standard-Model CP violation that can be used to test theories such as supersymmetry, multi-Higgs models, and left-right symmetric models.
