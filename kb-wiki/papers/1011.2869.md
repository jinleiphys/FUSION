# Microscopic calculation of the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be and 3H(alpha,gamma)7Li capture cross sections using realistic interactions

> The radiative capture cross sections for the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be and 3H(alpha,gamma)7Li reactions are calculated in the fully microscopic fermionic molecular dynamics approach using a realistic effective interaction that reproduces the nucleon-nucleon scattering data. At large distances bound and scattering states are described by antisymmetrized products of 4He and 3He/3H ground states. At short distances the many-body Hilbert space is extended with additional many-body wave functions needed to represent polarized clusters and shell-model-like configurations. Properties of the bound states are described well, as are the scattering phase shifts. The calculated S factor for the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be reaction agrees very well with recent experimental data both in absolute normalization and energy dependence. In case of the 3H(alpha,gamma)7Li reaction the calculated S factor is larger than available experimental data by about 15%.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully microscopic *ab initio* calculation using a realistic effective interaction (UCOM derived from Argonne V18) can reproduce the absolute normalization and energy dependence of the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be capture cross section, but for the mirror reaction 3H(alpha,gamma)7Li the calculated S-factor is about 15% larger than available experimental data.

## Method

The fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) approach was used to construct many-body wave functions for bound and scattering states of the 7Be and 7Li systems. At large distances, antisymmetrized products of 4He and 3He/3H ground states (frozen cluster configurations) were used, while at short distances the Hilbert space was extended with additional wave functions representing polarized clusters and shell-model-like configurations obtained by variation after projection (VAP) on spin-parity states. The microscopic R-matrix method was employed to impose proper boundary conditions for bound and scattering states.

## Key numbers

- Zero-energy S-factor for 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be: S34(0) = 0.593 keV b
- 7Be bound state energies relative to 4He-3He threshold: E(3/2-) = -1.49 MeV (experiment: -1.586 MeV), E(1/2-) = -1.31 MeV (experiment: -1.157 MeV)
- 7Li bound state energies relative to 4He-3H threshold: E(3/2-) = -2.39 MeV (experiment: -2.467 MeV), E(1/2-) = -2.17 MeV (experiment: -1.989 MeV)
- 7Be charge radius: 2.67 fm (experiment: 2.647(17) fm)
- 7Li charge radius: 2.46 fm (experiment: 2.390(30) fm)
- 7Li quadrupole moment: -3.91 e fm^2 (experiment: -4.00(3) e fm^2)
- UCOM interaction flow parameter: alpha = 0.20 fm^4, corresponding to a soft cutoff lambda = 1.5 fm^-1
- Channel radius: a = 12 fm
- Model space included about 50 intrinsic basis states for the inner part of the wave function
- When restricted to frozen configurations, the 3/2- and 1/2- states in 7Be were bound by only 240 keV and 10 keV respectively
- The ambiguity in binding energy from K-mixing elimination was about 150 keV

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster model calculations (Liu 1981, Langanke 1986, Mertelmeier 1986, Kajino 1986) that used phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions tuned to reproduce bound and scattering states within restricted model spaces. It also goes beyond hybrid approaches that combined asymptotic normalization coefficients from variational Monte Carlo (Nollett 2001) or no-core shell-model (Navratil 2007) with conventional potential models, which failed to describe both normalization and energy dependence simultaneously. The calculation enables consistent *ab initio* treatment of capture reactions involving composite projectiles, and the author notes that future calculations should investigate the role of three-body forces, which are expected to increase the splitting between the 3/2- and 1/2- states but have minor effect on the centroid energy.
