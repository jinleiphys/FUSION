# Shape-phase transitions in mixed parity systems and the onset of octupole deformation

> Shape-phase transitions in mixed parity boson systems are studied using mean field theory. It is shown that without parity projection, shape transitions in mixed parity systems are very similar to those in positive parity systems, both requiring a finite interaction strength for the onset of deformation. The shape-phase diagram in mixed parity systems, however, changes significantly after parity projection, with the critical point for the onset of octupole deformation moving to zero strength. Shape transitions in the Lipkin model are shown to exhibit the same features, indicating that this is a direct consequence of parity projection in finite, mixed parity systems independent of the nature of interacting particles.

## Key claim

Without parity projection, shape-phase transitions in mixed parity systems require a finite interaction strength for the onset of octupole deformation, just as in positive parity systems; however, after parity projection, the critical point for the onset of octupole deformation moves to zero strength, meaning any nonzero octupole interaction immediately induces deformation.

## Method

The author uses mean field theory with intrinsic condensate states to study shape-phase transitions in two analytically solvable models: the interacting boson model (IBM) in its \(sf\) and \(sdf\) versions, and the Lipkin model. Energy surfaces are derived from the expectation values of the Hamiltonians in the intrinsic states, both without and with parity projection, and the critical points for the onset of deformation are determined by expanding the energy surfaces around zero deformation.

## Key numbers

- In the \(sf\)-IBM without parity projection, the critical point for the onset of octupole deformation occurs at \(\eta_3^{\rm cr} = 4\), corresponding to a finite interaction strength \(\kappa_3 = \epsilon_f / 4N\).
- After parity projection in the \(sf\)-IBM, the critical point moves to \(\kappa_3 = 0\) for any \(\kappa_3 > 0\).
- In the Lipkin model without parity projection, the critical point for deformation occurs at \(\eta = 1\) or \(\kappa = \epsilon / N\).
- After parity projection in the Lipkin model, the critical point again moves to \(\kappa = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of shape-phase transitions from the well-studied spherical-to-quadrupole-deformed case in the \(sd\)-IBM (Dieperink 1980, Ginocchio 1980, Iachello 1998) to mixed parity systems relevant for octupole deformation in actinide nuclei. The finding that parity projection eliminates the spherical phase and moves the critical point to zero interaction strength parallels the known effect of number projection in BCS theory, where the critical pairing strength vanishes after projection. This result challenges the conventional description of octupole vibrations as a well-defined phase and has implications for interpreting new data on negative parity spectra in Rn, Ra, and Th nuclei from Eurogam and Gammasphere (Simpson 1995, Cocks 1997).
