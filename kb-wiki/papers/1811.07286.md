# Fate of the charm baryon $Λ_{c}$ in cold and hot nuclear matter

> I discuss the properties of the $Λ_{c}$ baryon in nuclear matter at zero or finite temperature. Starting from the Lagrangian based on the heavy-quark effective theory, I derive the effective Lagrangian for the $Λ_{c}$ baryon existing as an impurity particle. Adopting the one-loop calculation for nucleons, I derive the effective potential as the quantity for measuring the stability of the $Λ_{c}$ baryon in nuclear matter. The parameters in the Lagrangian are fitted to reproduce the scattering length of the nucleon and the $Λ_{c}$ baryon estimated in the lattice QCD simulations and the chiral extrapolations. I present that the $Λ_{c}$ baryon is bound in nuclei with the binding energy of about 20 MeV at normal nuclear-matter density. I discuss the case that the $Λ_{c}$ baryon moves with a constant velocity. I also discuss an increase of the nucleon number density near the $Λ_{c}$ baryon in nuclear matter, and show that the $Λ_{c}$ baryon is a useful probe to research the nuclear systems at high density.

## Key claim

The Λc baryon is bound in nuclear matter with a binding energy of about 20 MeV at normal nuclear-matter density, but this result depends on using scattering lengths from chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data rather than direct lattice results, because the lattice simulations used a heavier pion mass (above 410 MeV) that may differ from the realistic value.

## Method

Starting from a Lagrangian based on heavy-quark effective theory, the author derived a nonrelativistic effective Lagrangian for the Λc baryon as an impurity particle in nuclear matter, using a zero-range interaction between the Λc and nucleons. The coupling constant was constrained by fitting to the ΛcN scattering length obtained from chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD simulations by Haidenbauer and Krein (2017). The effective potential was computed at one-loop order for nucleons using the Matsubara formalism to include finite temperature and density effects.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the Λc baryon in nuclear matter: about 20 MeV at normal nuclear-matter density
- Mass of the Λc baryon used in the calculation: M = 2.286 GeV
- The scattering length from chiral extrapolations by Haidenbauer and Krein (2017) was used to constrain the coupling constant c1
- A sharp cutoff parameter Λ was introduced for regularization of momentum integrals, with the inverse of Λ interpreted as the size of the nucleon or Λc baryon

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ΛcN interactions based on heavy-quark spin symmetry by Liu (2011) and Meguro (2011), and uses lattice QCD results from Miyamoto (2017) as input after chiral extrapolation by Haidenbauer and Krein (2017). The calculated binding energy of about 20 MeV agrees with QCD sum rule results by Ohtani (2017), but contrasts with the quark-meson coupling model by Tsushima (2002) which predicted binding energies around 100 MeV. The work enables future studies of Λc baryons as probes of high-density nuclear systems and provides a framework for extending the analysis to moving Λc baryons and finite temperature effects.
