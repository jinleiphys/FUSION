# Dynamically groomed jet radius in heavy-ion collisions

> We explore the ability of a recently proposed jet substructure technique, Dynamical Grooming, to pin down the properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In particular, we compute, both analytically and via Monte-Carlo simulations, the opening angle $θ_g$ of the hardest splitting in the jet as defined by Dynamical Grooming. Our calculation, grounded in perturbative QCD, accounts for the factorization in time between vacuum-like and medium-induced processes in the double logarithmic approximation. We observe that the dominating scale in the $θ_g$-distribution is the decoherence angle $θ_c$ which characterises the resolution power of the medium to propagating color probes. This feature also persists in strong coupling models for jet quenching. We further propose for potential experimental measurements a suitable combination of the Dynamical Grooming condition and the jet radius that leads to a pQCD dominated observable with a very small sensitivity ($\leq 10\%$) to medium response.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the opening angle \(\theta_g\) of the hardest splitting identified by Dynamical Grooming in heavy-ion collisions is primarily sensitive to the decoherence angle \(\theta_c\) of the quark-gluon plasma, and that a suitable choice of grooming parameter and jet radius can produce an observable with less than or equal to 10% sensitivity to medium response, making it dominated by perturbative QCD dynamics.

## Method

The authors computed the \(\theta_g\) distribution analytically in the double logarithmic approximation of perturbative QCD, assuming a static brick medium with multiple soft scattering and factorization between vacuum-like and medium-induced emissions. They compared these analytic results to Monte-Carlo simulations using the JetMed generator (a pQCD-based parton shower) and the Hybrid model (a strong-coupling description), and evaluated medium response effects using state-of-the-art jet quenching Monte-Carlo generators.

## Key numbers

- The decoherence angle is defined as \(\theta_c = 2 / \sqrt{\hat q L^3}\), where \(\hat q\) is the quenching parameter and \(L\) the medium length.
- For an ALICE-like setup with \(\alpha_s = 0.2\), \(R = 0.4\), \(p_t = 100\) GeV/c, \(L = 4\) fm, and \(\hat q = 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, the veto region from vacuum-like emissions leads to a relative narrowing of the \(\theta_g\) distribution for \(a = 1, 2\).
- The proposed observable combination achieves a sensitivity to medium response of \(\leq 10\%\).
- The maximum of the vacuum \(\theta_g\) distribution scales as \(\ln(1/\theta_{\rm max}) = 1/(2a \bar\alpha) + \mathcal{O}(1)\), where \(\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha_s C_R / \pi\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of Dynamical Grooming, previously applied only to proton-proton collisions (Caucal 2021), to heavy-ion collisions where jet quenching occurs. It directly addresses the unresolved theoretical interpretation of SoftDrop measurements in heavy-ion collisions, where models with and without color decoherence mechanisms (e.g., JetMed, Hybrid, JetScape/MATTER+LBT) all reproduce the ALICE (2021) data. The paper enables future experimental measurements of the decoherence angle \(\theta_c\) by proposing a pQCD-dominated observable with minimal medium response contamination.
