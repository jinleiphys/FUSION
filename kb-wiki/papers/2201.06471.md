# The decay of $N^*$(1895) to light hyperon resonances

> In this talk I review the findings of our recent works where we have studied the decay of $N^*(1895)$ and the implications of such properties on the photoproduction of light hyperons. I discuss that meson-baryon interactions play an essential role in describing the nature of $N^*(1895)$ and report the details of our investigation of its decays to different meson-baryon systems and to final states involving $Λ(1405)$ and a proposed $Σ(1400)$. We find that the width of $N^*(1895)$ gets important contributions from the decay to light hyperon resonances. Such an information can be used to look for alternative processes to study $N^*(1895)$ in experimental data.

# The decay of $N^*(1895)$ to light hyperon resonances

## Key claim
The decay width of $N^*(1895)$ receives important contributions from decays to light hyperon resonances $\Lambda(1405)$ and a proposed $\Sigma(1400)$, with the branching ratios to these channels being comparable to those for conventional meson-baryon decays, but only when the two-pole structure of both $N^*(1895)$ and $\Lambda(1405)$ is taken into account, and the results depend on whether the poles are treated separately or their interference is considered.

## Method
The authors use effective Lagrangians for pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon vertices, with couplings previously determined from coupled-channel scattering calculations that reproduce experimental data on $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$ channels and kaonic hydrogen observables (Khemchandani 2013, Khemchandani 2018). They compute the decay amplitudes for $N^*(1895) \to K\Lambda(1405)$ and $N^*(1895) \to K\Sigma(1400)$ via triangle diagrams involving intermediate meson-baryon loops, projecting the final state onto p-wave. Radiative decay widths are calculated using vector meson dominance. The photoproduction cross sections for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ and $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma(1400)$ are then evaluated including s-channel $N^*$ exchange and t-channel $K$ and $K^*$ Reggeon exchange.

## Key numbers
- Decay width $\Gamma_{N^*(1895) \to K\Sigma(1400)} = (18.9 \pm 1.5)$ MeV (using average mass 1895 MeV and width 120 MeV for $N^*(1895)$ with pole interference)
- Decay width $\Gamma_{N^*(1895) \to K\Lambda(1405)} = (8.3 \pm 1.3)$ MeV (same averaging procedure)
- Branching ratio for $N^*_2$ (upper pole at $1912 - i54$ MeV) to $K\Sigma^*$: 11.4%; to $K\Lambda^*_2$: 1.1%
- Branching ratio for $N^*_1$ (lower pole at $1801 - i96$ MeV) to $K\Sigma^*$: 5.9%; to $K\Lambda^*_1$: 5.4%
- Radiative decay width $\Lambda(1405) \to \Lambda\gamma = (26.19 \pm 6.93)$ keV (with pole interference), in agreement with the PDG value of $27\pm8$ keV
- Radiative decay width $N^*(1895) \to p\gamma = (650.70 \pm 65.10)$ keV (with pole interference), corresponding to a branching ratio of 0.11–0.13% for the upper pole, near the PDG upper limit of 0.06%
- Pole positions: $N^*(1895)$ at $1801 - i96$ MeV and $1912 - i54$ MeV; $\Lambda(1405)$ at $1385 - i124$ MeV and $1426 - i15$ MeV; $\Sigma(1400)$ at $1399 - i36$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel dynamical approach of Khemchandani (2013) and Khemchandani (2018), which showed that $N^*(1895)$ and $\Sigma(1400)$ arise from meson-baryon interactions rather than from quark model predictions (which place the third $1/2^-$ nucleon above 2100 MeV, as in Hosaka 1997 and Takayama 1999). The results contradict earlier assignments of a $3/2^+$ $N^*(1895)$ proposed by Mart (1999) to describe $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ data, and instead support the partial wave analyses of Anisovich (2011) and Mart (2019) that attribute the 1900 MeV peak to $N^*(1900)$ $3/2^+$. The calculated photoproduction cross sections for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ agree with CLAS data (Moriya 2013), with $N^*(1895)$ exchange dominating the s-channel near threshold. The predictions for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma(1400)$ cross sections are intended to motivate experimental searches at facilities such as J-PARC (Noumi 2017).
