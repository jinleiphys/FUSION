# A New Expression for 3N Bound State Faddeev Equation in a 3D Approach

> A spin-isospin dependent three-dimensional approach has been applied for formulation of the three-nucleon bound state and a new expression for Faddeev equation based on three-nucleon free basis state has been obtained. Then the three-nucleon wave function has been obtained as a function of five independent variables.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new, simpler expression for the three-nucleon (3N) bound state Faddeev equation in a three-dimensional (3D) momentum-space formalism, representing the Faddeev component of the total 3N wave function as a function of only five independent variables after choosing a suitable coordinate system. The authors claim this new expression is more straightforward for numerical calculations than their previous 3D formulation (Bayegan, 2010), but they present no numerical results or convergence tests in this paper.

## Method

The authors start from the standard Faddeev equation for the 3N bound state with pairwise interactions, using a free 3N basis state defined by two Jacobi momenta (p and q) and the spin-isospin projections of all three nucleons. They insert completeness relations, evaluate the matrix elements of the permutation operator P and the two-body t-matrix, and apply the antisymmetry property of the Faddeev component (P23|psi> = -|psi>) to obtain a new integral equation. The final expression is then reduced to a form depending on five independent variables (p, x_p, cos(phi_pq), x_q, q) by choosing the spin polarization direction parallel to the z-axis and factoring out the azimuthal angle dependencies.

## Key numbers

* The total number of coupled Faddeev equations for either 3H or 3He is N = 48, derived from 2 (signs of sine functions) times (N_t x N_s) where N_t = 3 (isospin states) and N_s = sum_i N_{m_{s_i}} = 2+2+2 = 6 (spin states).
* The free 3N propagator is given by G0 = 1/(E - p^2/m - 3q^2/(4m)), where E is the binding energy of the 3N bound state.
* The energy carried by a two-body subsystem is epsilon = E - (3/(4m)) q^2.

## Context

This work extends the 3D approach previously developed for three-boson bound states (Schadow, 2001) and for Nd scattering (Harzchi, 2011) to the 3N bound state with spin and isospin degrees of freedom. It provides a reformulation that avoids the angular momentum algebra required in traditional partial-wave approaches, and the authors state that numerical calculations of 3N bound state observables using this formalism are "under preparation." The paper directly builds on and simplifies the earlier 3D formalism for the 3N bound state presented by Bayegan (2010).
