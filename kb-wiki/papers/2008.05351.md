# Transverse moments of TMD parton densities and ultraviolet divergences

> I review some open questions relating to the large transverse momentum divergences in transverse moments of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton correlation func- tions. I also explain, in an abbreviated and summarized form, recent work that shows that the resulting violations of a commonly used integral relation are not perturbatively suppressed. I argue that this implies a need for more precise definitions for the correlation functions used to describe transverse moments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that violations of the commonly used integral relation connecting weighted transverse moments of TMD parton densities to twist-3 collinear functions (such as the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman function) are not perturbatively suppressed, contrary to the behavior of the analogous zeroth-moment relation for unpolarized PDFs, implying a need for more precise definitions of the correlation functions used to describe transverse moments.

## Method

The author reviews the structure of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross sections and their transverse-momentum-weighted integrals, contrasting the TMD-based description at small transverse momentum with the collinear-factorization-based Y-term needed at large transverse momentum. He then examines the ultraviolet divergences that arise in the transverse momentum integrals defining both the zeroth moment of unpolarized TMD PDFs and the weighted moment of the Sivers TMD PDF, and compares the asymptotic behavior of the resulting violations of the integral relations.

## Key numbers

- The violation of the zeroth-moment relation for unpolarized TMD PDFs, Δf, is of order α_s(k_c), so it vanishes as the cutoff k_c and scale μ are taken to a hard scale Q with Q/Λ_QCD → ∞.
- The violation of the weighted-moment relation for the Sivers function, Δf_{1T}^⊥, behaves as α_s(k_c)^2 ln^2(k_c^2/m^2), where m is a small nonperturbative mass scale roughly of size Λ_QCD, so asymptotic freedom does not suppress it.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of integral relations between TMD and collinear correlation functions first derived by Boer, Mulders, and Pijlman (2003) and the treatment of ultraviolet divergences in TMD factorization. It directly builds on the recent calculation by Qiu, Rogers, and Sato (2020) showing the unsuppressed violation of the Sivers-function integral relation. The paper identifies open questions about how to define a consistent scheme for the twist-3 ETQS function T(x) that preserves the parton-model picture while handling the large-transverse-momentum region, and notes that future phenomenological refinements (such as those in Cammarota et al., 2020) will need to implement TMD evolution before applying the transverse-momentum cutoff.
