# Collins-Soper Kernel for TMD Evolution from Lattice QCD

> The Collins-Soper kernel relates transverse momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) at different energy scales. For small parton transverse momentum $q_T\sim Λ_\text{QCD}$, this kernel is non-perturbative and can only be determined with controlled uncertainties through experiment or first-principles calculations. This work presents the first exploratory determination of the Collins-Soper kernel using the lattice formulation of Quantum Chromodynamics. In a quenched calculation, the $N_f=0$ kernel is determined at scales in the range 250 MeV $< q_T < 2$ GeV, and an analysis of the remaining systematic uncertainties is undertaken.

## Key claim

This work presents the first exploratory determination of the Collins-Soper kernel from lattice QCD, but only in a quenched (N_f=0) approximation and with remaining systematic uncertainties that are analyzed but not fully controlled, meaning the results are a proof-of-principle rather than a precision determination.

## Method

The Collins-Soper kernel was extracted from ratios of nonsinglet quasi TMDPDFs at different hadron momenta, computed from equal-time correlation functions (quasi beam functions) with staple-shaped Wilson lines in boosted pion states on a single quenched lattice ensemble. The quasi beam functions were renormalized nonperturbatively using the RI' scheme with perturbative matching to the MS scheme, and the kernel was obtained by taking the ratio of Fourier-transformed renormalized quasi beam functions at two different momenta, with perturbative matching coefficients applied at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The Collins-Soper kernel was determined at scales in the range 250 MeV < q_T < 2 GeV, where q_T = 1/b_T.
- The lattice calculation used a single quenched ensemble with lattice spacing a = 0.06 fm, volume 32^3 x 64, and pion mass 1.207(3) GeV.
- Three external pion momenta were studied: P^z = 1.29, 1.94, and 2.58 GeV (corresponding to n_z = 2, 3, 4 with 2π/L spacing).
- Staple extents η ranged from 0.6 to 0.8 fm (η/a ∈ {10, 12, 14}).
- The extracted energies deviated from the continuum dispersion relation by 1.5(4)% for n_z = 2 to 3.5(4)% for n_z = 4.
- Combined contributions from operator mixing to the renormalized quasi beam function were at the 5%–25% level across the parameters studied.

## Context

This work implements for the first time the method proposed by Ebert et al. (2018, 2019) for extracting the Collins-Soper kernel from lattice QCD using LaMET, extending prior lattice studies of TMD physics by Musch et al. (2010, 2011), Engelhardt et al. (2015), and Yoon et al. (2016, 2017). The results enable future fully-controlled calculations with dynamical fermions, larger volumes, multiple lattice spacings, and controlled power corrections, which could provide independent first-principles input to global fits of TMDPDFs that currently show discrepancies in the kernel determination at q_T ≤ 500 MeV (Vladimirov, 2020).
