# Isospin violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering revisited

> We calculate isospin breaking in pion-nucleon scattering in the threshold region in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. All effects due to quark mass differences as well as real and virtual photons are consistently included. As an application, we discuss the energy dependence of the triangle relation that connects elastic scattering on the proton pi+- p --> pi+- p with the charge exchange reaction pi- p --> pi0 n.

## Key claim

Isospin violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, quantified via the triangle relation connecting elastic and charge-exchange channels, amounts to about 2.5–4.0% in the S-wave between √s = 1.08–1.14 GeV, with the bulk coming from virtual photons rather than quark mass differences; the authors do not fit low-energy constants to data but instead collect them from other determinations, focusing on isospin-breaking shifts rather than absolute amplitudes.

## Method

The authors calculate isospin-breaking corrections to pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory to third chiral order (O(p³)), including all effects from quark mass differences (md − mu), real and virtual photons, and soft bremsstrahlung in the soft-photon approximation. They work in infrared regularization to maintain chiral power counting, compute tree and one-loop diagrams for the channels π⁺p → π⁺p, π⁻p → π⁻p, and π⁻p → π⁰n, and project the amplitudes onto S-wave and P-wave partial waves. Low-energy constants are taken from other determinations rather than fitted, and the triangle relation, which vanishes in the isospin limit, is used as the measure of isospin violation.

## Key numbers

- Isospin violation in the triangle relation amounts to (2.5–4.0)% in the S-wave for center-of-mass energies √s = (1.08–1.14) GeV.
- Isospin breaking in the P-waves is at most of order 1%.
- The pion decay constant is taken as Fπ = (92.2 ± 0.2) MeV and the axial charge as |gA| = 1.2695 ± 0.0029.
- The renormalization scale is set to μ = 1 GeV for central values.

## Context

This work extends the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory analysis of pion-nucleon scattering lengths by Hoferichter, Kubis, and Meißner (2009) to energies above threshold, revisiting earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Fettes and Meißner (2001) that found different isospin-breaking effects. The authors note that the heavy-baryon approach fitted low-energy constants to data, potentially contaminating electromagnetic low-energy constants with strong higher-order contributions, whereas the present work restricts itself to isospin-breaking shifts. The results are intended to enable consistent incorporation of isospin breaking in future analyses of pion-nucleon scattering based on Roy–Steiner equations.
