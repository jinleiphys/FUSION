# A higher-harmonic observable for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a phenomenon in which electric charge is separated by a strong magnetic field from local domains of chirality imbalance in quantum chromodynamics. The CME-sensitive azimuthal correlator difference $Δγ$ between opposite- and same-sign charged hadron pairs is designed to detect charge separation along the magnetic field, on average perpendicular to the reaction plane. However, the search for the CME is hindered by large background contributions to $Δγ$ from particle correlations coupled with elliptic flow. In this work, we explore higher-harmonic components in differential $Δγ(ϕ_{\rm pair})$ as a function of the pair azimuthal angle. Such components could arise from event-by-event fluctuations of the magnetic fields throughout the collision zone, in both direction and magnitude. We show by using heavy-ion physics models that the hexadecapole component of $Δγ(ϕ_{\rm pair})$ is sensitive to the CME and insensitive to physics backgrounds. This could offer a unique observable for the CME that is robust against background contributions.

## Key claim

The hexadecapole (cos4φ) component of the differential azimuthal correlator Δγ(φ_pair) is sensitive to the chiral magnetic effect and insensitive to physics backgrounds, potentially offering a background-free observable, but only if the background contributions to the hexadecapole-to-quadrupole ratio c4/c2 remain smaller than the CME signal, which the authors estimate as on the order of ±1% from model studies.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV using the Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model with three values of the axial current over entropy density ratio n5/s (0, 0.1, 0.2) to generate CME signals, and used AMPT, HYDJET++, and UrQMD models to generate purely background events. They computed the differential correlator Δγ(φ_pair) as a function of pair azimuthal angle and fitted it to a Fourier series including sine and cosine terms up to cos6φ and sin5φ, extracting the harmonic coefficients c2, c4, c6, s1, s3, s5.

## Key numbers

- Three values of axial current strength were used: n5/s = 0, 0.1, and 0.2, representing 0%, 10%, and 20% CME signal strength respectively.
- A total of 73, 58, and 30 million AVFD events were produced for the three n5/s values; 400, 630, and 193 million minimum-bias Au+Au events at √s_NN = 200 GeV were simulated by AMPT, HYDJET++, and UrQMD respectively.
- The background c4/c2 ratio from all physics models (AMPT, HYDJET++, UrQMD, and AVFD with n5/s=0) is within ±1% for charged pions in 30-40% centrality Au+Au collisions.
- The c4 parameter from AVFD increases with increasing n5/s, while c4 from background models is close to zero; c6 and s5 are consistent with zero in all models.
- The hexadecapole in ΔC3(φ_b) from HIJING is consistent with zero within a statistical uncertainty of 10^-7.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, where the standard Δγ observable (Voloshin 2004) is contaminated by background from cluster correlations coupled with elliptic flow (Schlichting 2010). Previous efforts to isolate the CME include event-shape engineering (STAR 2019, 2021, 2023), the R-observable (STAR 2020), and higher-harmonic correlators such as γ_{224}, γ_{123}, and γ_{132} (Voloshin 2011, CMS 2017, ALICE 2020, Choudhury 2019). The proposed hexadecapole observable differs from these by examining the differential Δγ(φ_pair) rather than averaged correlators, and could enable a background-free CME search if the background c4/c2 ratio remains below the CME signal level.
