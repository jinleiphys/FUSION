# The Wroblewski Parameter from Lattice QCD

> Enhancement of strangeness production has since long been proposed as a promising signal of quark-gluon plasma production. A convenient indicator for it is the Wroblewski parameter which has been shown to be about a factor two higher in heavy ion collisions. Using a method proposed by us earlier, we obtained lattice QCD results for the Wroblewski parameter from our simulations of QCD with two light quarks both below and above the chiral transition. Our first principles based and parameter free result compare well with the A-A data.

## Key claim

The Wroblewski parameter computed from first-principles lattice QCD agrees with experimental results from heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC, but this agreement is based on quenched QCD simulations extrapolated to the transition temperature and requires cautious interpretation due to several approximations.

## Method

The authors computed quark number susceptibilities for light and strange quarks using lattice QCD simulations with two light dynamical quarks (mass 0.1 T_c) and in the quenched approximation, then extracted the Wroblewski parameter as λ_s = 2χ_s/(χ_u+χ_d). They performed continuum extrapolations for the quenched case by increasing the temporal lattice size from 4 to 14, and obtained the strange quark susceptibility by setting the valence quark mass to m_v/T_c = 1. The resulting λ_s(T) was extrapolated to T_c using simple ansatze.

## Key numbers

- λ_s is approximately 0.2 in most processes including proton-proton scattering, but about a factor of two higher in heavy ion collisions
- The transition temperature T_c differs by a factor of 1.6 between quenched and full QCD, yet the corresponding susceptibilities as a function of T/T_c differ by only a few per cent
- Quark number susceptibilities were computed at T = 2 T_c with temporal lattice sizes from 4 to 14 for continuum extrapolation
- The light dynamical quark mass used was 0.1 T_c

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Rafelski and Muller (1982) that strangeness enhancement signals quark-gluon plasma formation, and the Wroblewski parameter definition from Wroblewski (1985). The authors use a method they proposed earlier (Gavai and Gupta, 2003) to relate quark number susceptibilities to the Wroblewski parameter. The lattice results agree with fireball model analyses of RHIC and SPS data by Becattini and Heinz (1997), but the authors note that direct full QCD simulations near T_c with varying strange quark mass, temporal lattice size, and spatial volume are still needed, as are checks at nonzero chemical potential.
