# Tabulated equation of state for supernova matter including full nuclear ensemble

> This is an introduction to the tabulated data base of stellar matter properties calculated within the framework of the Statistical Model for Supernova Matter (SMSM). The tables present thermodynamical characteristics and nuclear abundances for 31 values of baryon density (10$^{-8}<ρ/ρ_0<$0.32, $ρ_0$=0.15 fm$^{-3}$ is the normal nuclear matter density), 35 values of temperature ($0.2<T<25$ MeV) and 28 values of electron-to-baryon ratio ($0.02<Y_e<0.56$). The properties of stellar matter in $β$-equilibrium are also considered. The main ingredients of the SMSM are briefly outlined, and the data structure and content of the tables are explained.

## Key claim

The paper presents tabulated equation-of-state data for supernova matter calculated within the Statistical Model for Supernova Matter (SMSM), which treats the full ensemble of nuclear species (A up to 1000) in grand-canonical equilibrium. The authors hedge that the model is not applicable at baryon densities above 0.32 times normal nuclear density or temperatures above 25 MeV, and that at very low temperatures (below about 1 MeV) the assumption of statistical equilibrium may be problematic.

## Method

The authors used a grand-canonical version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to compute thermodynamic properties and nuclear abundances for a grid of 31 baryon densities, 35 temperatures, and 28 electron fractions. The internal free energy of heavy nuclei (A>4) was parametrized with a liquid-drop model including bulk, surface, symmetry, and Coulomb terms, with electron screening treated in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The coupled conservation equations for baryon number and charge were solved self-consistently for all nuclear species with 1 ≤ A ≤ 1000 and 0 ≤ Z ≤ A, and contributions from electrons, positrons, and photons were added.

## Key numbers

- Density range: 31 values from ρ/ρ₀ = 10⁻⁸ to 0.32, where ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density.
- Temperature range: 35 values from T = 0.2 MeV to 25 MeV.
- Electron fraction range: 28 values from Yₑ = 0.02 to 0.56.
- Total grid points: 30380 (35 T × 28 Yₑ × 31 ρ/ρ₀).
- Liquid-drop parameters used: w₀ = 16 MeV (bulk), β₀ = 18 MeV (surface), T_c = 18 MeV (critical temperature), γ = 25 MeV (symmetry), ε₀ = 16 MeV (level-density parameter), r₀ = 1.17 fm (nuclear radius constant).
- Numerical accuracy: better than 1% for thermodynamic quantities; mass conservation checked with δ_X around 0.1%.
- Computation time: approximately 7.8 days on a 4×Dual core AMD 2.2 GHz processor.
- Main table file size: 6.8 MB (31497 lines); mass distribution file size: 319 MB (31360 lines, each with 1003 columns for A=1–1000).

## Context

This work extends the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM), previously used to describe nuclear multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions (Bondorf 1985, Botvina 1990), to astrophysical conditions relevant for core-collapse supernovae. It provides a tabulated equation of state that includes the full nuclear ensemble, unlike approaches that approximate the heavy-nucleus component by a single representative nucleus (Shen 1998, Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich 2010). The tables enable more accurate calculations of neutrino-induced reactions on individual nuclear species in supernova simulations, following earlier work by Botvina and Mishustin (2004, 2010) and complementing studies by Sumiyoshi and Ropke (2008) and Typel et al. (2010).
