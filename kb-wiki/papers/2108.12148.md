# Structural and decay properties of nuclei appearing in the $α$-decay chains of $^{296,298,300,302,304}$120 within the relativistic mean-field formalism

> An extensive study of $α$-decay half-lives for various decay chains of isotopes of $Z$ = 120 is performed within the axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism by employing the NL3, NL3$^*$, and DD-ME2 parameter set. The structural properties of the nuclei appearing in the decay chains are explored. The binding energy, quadrupole deformation parameter, root-mean-square charge radius, and pairing energy are calculated for the even-even isotopes of $Z$ = 100 $-$ 120, which are produced in five different $α$-decay chains, namely, $^{296}$120 $\rightarrow$ $^{260}$No, $^{298}$120 $\rightarrow$ $^{262}$No, $^{300}$120 $\rightarrow$ $^{264}$No, $^{302}$120 $\rightarrow$ $^{266}$No, and $^{304}$120 $\rightarrow$ $^{268}$No. A superdeformed prolate ground state is observed for the heavier nuclei, and gradually the deformation decreases towards the lighter nuclei in the considered decay chains. The RMF results are compared with various theoretical predictions and experimental data. The $α$-decay energies are calculated for each decay chain. To determine the relative numerical dependency of the half-life for a specific $α$-decay energy, the decay half-lives are calculated using four different formulas, namely, Viola-Seaborg, Alex-Brown, Parkhomenko-Sobiczewski, and Royer for the above said five $α$-decay chain. We notice a firm dependency of the half-life on the $α$-decay formula in terms of $Q_α$-values for all decay chains. Further, the present study also strengthens the prediction for the island of stability in terms of magic number at the superheavy valley in the laboratories.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with NL3, NL3*, and DD-ME2 parameter sets predict that the ground states of the heavier isotopes of Z=120 are superdeformed prolate, with deformation gradually decreasing toward lighter nuclei in the decay chains; the study finds a firm dependency of the calculated alpha-decay half-life on the specific analytical formula used for a given Q-value, and the results strengthen the prediction of an island of stability near the superheavy valley.

## Method

The axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism was solved self-consistently for even-even isotopes from Z=100 to Z=120 appearing in five alpha-decay chains starting from 296,298,300,302,304120 down to 260,262,264,266,268No. Binding energies, quadrupole deformation parameters, root-mean-square charge radii, and pairing energies were computed using the NL3, NL3*, and DD-ME2 parameter sets, with constant-gap BCS pairing for NL3 and NL3* and Bogoliubov transformation for DD-ME2. Alpha-decay energies (Q-values) were obtained from the binding energies of parent and daughter nuclei, and half-lives were then calculated using four different formulas: Viola-Seaborg, Alex-Brown, Parkhomenko-Sobiczewski, and Royer.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation of binding energy from RMF relative to experimental data: 1.5342 MeV (NL3), 1.9978 MeV (NL3*), 1.6282 MeV (DD-ME2); for comparison, FRDM gives 1.2064 MeV and WS3 gives 1.1982 MeV.
- Standard deviation of Q-values from RMF relative to experimental data: 1.0249 MeV (NL3), 0.8272 MeV (NL3*), 0.7645 MeV (DD-ME2); FRDM gives 0.5450 MeV and WS4 gives 0.5854 MeV.
- Ground-state quadrupole deformation for 296120 is approximately 0.55 and for 304120 is approximately 0.57 (NL3* parameter set).
- For isotopes of Z=100 to 110, the calculated beta2 values range from 0.1 to 0.3, consistent with FRDM predictions.
- Relative variation of solutions with basis size is less than or equal to 0.06% on binding energy, 0.01% on nuclear radius, and 0.008% on quadrupole deformation for 296120 and 304120.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of superheavy nuclei (Bhuyan 2012, Zhang 2005) and compares with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) predictions of Moller (1997, 2016), the WS3 and WS4 mass models, and the theoretical estimates of Heenen (2015). The study enables future experimental searches for the island of stability near Z=120 by providing systematic predictions of Q-values and half-lives across five decay chains, and it highlights the sensitivity of half-life predictions to the choice of analytical formula.
