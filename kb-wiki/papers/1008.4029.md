# Baryon formation and dissociation in dense hadronic and quark matter

> We study the formation of baryons as composed of quarks and diquarks in hot and dense hadronic matter in a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL)--type model. We first solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the diquark propagator and then use this to solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the baryon propagator. We find that stable baryon resonances exist only in the phase of broken chiral symmetry. In the chirally symmetric phase, we do not find a pole in the baryon propagator. In the color-superconducting phase, there is a pole, but is has a large decay width. The diquark does not need to be stable in order to form a stable baryon, a feature typical for so-called Borromean states. Varying the strength of the diquark coupling constant, we also find similarities to the properties of an Efimov states.

## Key claim

Stable baryon resonances (zero decay width) exist only in the phase of broken chiral symmetry; in the chirally symmetric phase no pole is found in the baryon propagator, and in the color-superconducting phase a pole exists but with a large decay width, meaning the baryon is unstable there.

## Method

The authors used a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type model with scalar diquark and baryon fields. They first solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the diquark propagator, then used that result to solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the baryon propagator, employing a static approximation for the quark-diquark coupling. The baryon spectral density was computed from the inverse propagator to identify poles and widths at various points in the temperature-chemical potential phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass in vacuum: 940 MeV (used to fix the baryon coupling constant \(G_B = 10.04\) GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- NJL parameters: \(G_S = 5.1\) GeV\(^{-2}\), momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV.
- Diquark coupling constant set to \(G_D = 3.11\) GeV\(^{-2}\) for most calculations (weak-coupling region).
- At point A (\(T = 0.03\) GeV, \(\mu_q = 0.25\) GeV) in the broken chiral symmetry phase, the baryon pole satisfies \(\omega_B + 3\mu_q \approx 0.94\) GeV, close to the nucleon rest mass.
- In the broken chiral symmetry phase, the imaginary part of the inverse baryon propagator is smaller than \(10^{-6}\) GeV in the homogeneous limit for \(M_B \in [-3m_q, 3m_q]\).
- At \(T = 0.05\) GeV, baryon resonances disappear for \(\mu_q = 0.31\) and 0.32 GeV (widths too large to be visible on the plotted scale).

## Context

This work extends previous NJL-type studies of diquark and baryon formation (Ishii 1995, Pepin 1999, Bentz 2001, Bentz 2002, Gastineau 2005) by including nonzero temperature and by not assuming the diquark to be a stable quasi-particle. Unlike those earlier Faddeev-equation approaches, the authors use a simpler Dyson-Schwinger method with a local quark-diquark coupling, which allows calculation of baryon spectral densities and dissociation conditions across the phase diagram. The results enable future studies of baryon properties in dense matter relevant to the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, and suggest connections to Borromean and Efimov states in atomic and nuclear physics.
