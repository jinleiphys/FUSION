# Temperature dependence of volume and surface symmetry energy coefficients of nucle

> The thermal evolution of the energies and free energies of a set of spherical and near-spherical nuclei spanning the whole periodic table are calculated in the subtracted finite-temperature Thomas- Fermi framework with the zero-range Skyrme-type KDE0 and the finite-range modified Seyler-Blanchard interaction. The calculated energies are subjected to a global fit in the spirit of the liquid-drop model. The extracted parameters in this model reflect the temperature dependence of the volume symmetry and surface symmetry coefficients of finite nuclei, in addition to that of the volume and surface energy coefficients. The temperature dependence of the surface symmetry energy is found to be very substantial whereas that of the volume symmetry energy turns out to be comparatively mild.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the volume symmetry energy coefficient is mild, while the surface symmetry energy coefficient shows a very substantial temperature dependence; however, the exact behavior of the volume symmetry energy coefficient depends on which of two definitions (parametrization I or II) is used for the mass-dependent symmetry coefficient.

## Method

The authors calculated the energies and free energies of 69 spherical and near-spherical nuclei (mass numbers 36 ≤ A ≤ 218, atomic numbers 14 ≤ Z ≤ 92) using the subtracted finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi framework with two effective interactions: the zero-range Skyrme-type KDE0 interaction and the finite-range modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) interaction. The calculated nuclear energies were then fitted globally to a Bethe-Weizsäcker liquid-drop mass formula to extract the temperature-dependent volume, surface, volume symmetry, and surface symmetry coefficients, using two alternative parametrizations for the mass-dependent symmetry coefficient.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, the volume symmetry energy coefficient a_sym^v is 33 MeV for KDE0 and 31 MeV for SBM interactions.
- At T=0, the surface symmetry energy coefficient a_sym^s is 44.8 MeV for KDE0 and 39.2 MeV for SBM interactions.
- At T=0, the coefficient β_E (from parametrization I) is 12.1 MeV for SBM and 13.9 MeV for KDE0 interactions.
- The volume energy a_v(T) and volume free energy f_v(T) change quadratically with temperature, approximated by a_v(T) = e(T=0) + T^2/K_1 with K_1 ~ 15.5 MeV and f_v(T) = f_v(T=0) - T^2/K_2 with K_2 ~ 24.0 MeV.
- The ratio a_sym^v/β_E and f_sym^v/β_F are nearly temperature independent, lying in the range of ~ 2.64 ± 0.01.
- The critical temperature T_c is 14.7 MeV for KDE0 and 14.9 MeV for SBM interactions.

## Context

This work extends earlier low-temperature calculations of symmetry coefficients by Donati et al. (T ≤ 2 MeV) using a schematic model and by Dean et al. using shell-model Monte Carlo, as well as density functional calculations by Lee and Mekjian (T ≤ 3 MeV). It also extends a more recent finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi calculation by De et al. that explored temperatures up to 8 MeV but suffered from ambiguous definitions of the symmetry coefficient for individual nuclei. The present results provide unambiguous temperature-dependent volume and surface symmetry coefficients for use in nuclear equation of state studies relevant to core-collapse supernova dynamics and explosive nucleosynthesis.
