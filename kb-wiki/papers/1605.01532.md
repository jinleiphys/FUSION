# Lectures on Bound states

> Even a first approximation of bound states requires contributions of all powers in the coupling. This means that the concept of "lowest order bound state" needs to be defined. In these lectures I discuss the "Born" (no loop, lowest order in $\hbar$) approximation. Born level states are bound by gauge fields which satisfy the classical field equations. As a check of the method, Positronium states of any momentum are determined as eigenstates of the QED Hamiltonian, quantized at equal time. Analogously, states bound by a strong external field $A^μ(\boldsymbol{x})$ are found as eigenstates of the Dirac Hamiltonian. Their Fock states have dynamically created $e^+e^-$ pairs, whose distribution is determined by the Dirac wave function. The linear potential of $D=1+1$ dimensions confines electrons but repels positrons. As a result, the mass spectrum is continuous and the wave functions have features of both bound states and plane waves. The classical solutions of Gauss' law are explored for hadrons in QCD. A non-vanishing boundary condition at spatial infinity generates a constant \order{α_s^0} color electric field between quarks of specific colors. Poincaré invariance limits the spectrum to color singlet $q\bar q$ and $qqq$ states, which do not generate an external color field. This restricts the \order{α_s^0} interactions between hadrons to string breaking dynamics as in dual diagrams. Light mesons lie on linear Regge and parallel daughter trajectories. There are massless states which may be significant for chiral symmetry breaking. Since the bound states are defined at equal time in all frames they have a non-trivial Lorentz covariance.

## Key claim

Even a first approximation of bound states requires contributions of all powers in the coupling, so the concept of "lowest order bound state" must be defined as lowest order in ℏ (the Born approximation) rather than in the coupling constant α, with Born-level states bound by classical gauge fields satisfying the classical field equations.

## Method

The author defines bound states as eigenstates of the Hamiltonian at equal time, with the gauge field operator taking the value of the classical field given by the equations of motion. For Positronium, the state is expressed in terms of electron and positron field operators with a c-numbered 4×4 wave function, and the requirement that this state be stationary in time determines the bound state equation. For QCD hadrons, the classical solutions of Gauss' law are explored with a non-vanishing boundary condition at spatial infinity, generating a constant order α_s^0 color electric field between quarks of specific colors.

## Key numbers

- The Bohr momentum scale for atomic constituents is p ∼ α m, where α is the fine structure constant and m the electron mass
- The Positronium potential energy from the classical field is V(r) = -α/r, with the wave function Φ₀(r) = N exp(-α m r)
- The linear potential in D=1+1 dimensions is eA⁰(x) = V'|x|, where V' is the constant field gradient
- Light mesons lie on linear Regge and parallel daughter trajectories, with massless states (M=0) that may be significant for chiral symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter equation approach to bound states (Lepage, 1978) by providing a physically motivated definition of the lowest-order approximation based on the ℏ expansion rather than the coupling expansion. The method contrasts with Non-Relativistic QED (NRQED) used in precision atomic calculations (Kinoshita; Baker, 2014), which is applicable only in the rest frame. For QCD, the approach enables a description of hadrons as color singlet qq̄ and qqq states with classical fields that restrict order α_s^0 interactions between hadrons to string breaking dynamics as in dual diagrams (Zweig, 2015; Harari, 1981), and the massless states may be relevant for understanding chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
