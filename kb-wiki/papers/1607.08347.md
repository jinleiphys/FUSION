# Kinematic sensitivity to the Fierz term of $β$-decay differential spectra

> The current most stringent constraints on exotic scalar or tensor couplings in neutron and nuclear $β$ decay, involving left-handed neutrinos, are obtained from the Fierz interference term. The sensitivity to this term in a correlation coefficient is usually driven by an energy-averaged kinematic factor that increases monotonically toward smaller values of the $β$ endpoint energies. We first point out here that this property does not hold for certain differential observables that are directly sensitive to the Fierz term, such as the $β$ or the recoil energy spectrum. This observation is relevant for the selection of sensitive transitions in searches for exotic couplings through spectrum shape measurements. We then point out previous errors in the exploitation of measurements of the $β-ν$ angular correlation coefficient and discuss their impact on the extraction of constraints on exotic couplings.

## Key claim

The monotonic increase of kinematic sensitivity to the Fierz term toward lower beta endpoint energies, which holds for the total decay rate, does **not** hold for differential observables such as the beta energy spectrum or the recoil momentum spectrum; for these, the sensitivity degrades at both very low and very high endpoint energies, with an optimal window around 0.6–3.8 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of beta energy spectra and recoil momentum spectra for allowed beta decays, generating \(10^8\) events per spectrum for various endpoint energies between 20 keV and 20 MeV. They fitted the simulated spectra with the appropriate theoretical functions (including the Fierz term \(b\) and, for recoil spectra, the angular correlation coefficient \(a\)) to extract the 1\(\sigma\) statistical uncertainty on \(b\) as a function of endpoint energy. They also derived an analytic approximation for the sensitivity from a two-bin ratio and compared direct fits of \(b\) with the incorrect re-interpretation of \(a\) via the \(\tilde{a}\)-prescription.

## Key numbers

* For a sample with \(10^8\) events, the smallest statistical uncertainty on the Fierz term extracted from the total decay rate would be \(\Delta b = 10^{-4}\).
* For the beta energy spectrum, transitions with endpoint energies in the range 0.6–3.8 MeV have sensitivities within 20% of the optimal kinematic sensitivity.
* For a Gamow-Teller transition with endpoint \(E_0 = 100\) keV, applying the \(\tilde{a}\)-prescription would overestimate the uncertainty on \(b\) by about an order of magnitude.
* For the \(^6\)He decay (endpoint \(E_0 = 3.5\) MeV), the relative difference between the direct fit of \(b\) and the \(\tilde{a}\)-prescription is 30%.
* In the \(^{32}\)Ar experiment, the coefficient multiplying \(b\) in the constrained linear combination \(\hat{a}\) was 0.1913, which is 11% smaller than the \(\langle m/W \rangle\) value of 0.214 expected from the \(\tilde{a}\)-prescription.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of kinematic sensitivity to the Fierz term beyond the well-known monotonic behavior for total decay rates (used, e.g., in super-allowed Fermi transitions by Hardy and Towner (2015)) to differential spectra. It corrects previous errors in the exploitation of \(\beta-\nu\) angular correlation measurements, specifically the improper application of the \(\tilde{X}\)-prescription (introduced by Jackson, Treiman, and Wyld (1957) and Paul (1970)) to recoil momentum distributions, as had been done in global fits by Severijns et al. (2006), Konrad et al. (2010), Wauters et al. (2014), and Vos et al. (2015). The results enable more informed selection of nuclear transitions for future spectrum-shape measurements and clarify the correct analysis procedure for extracting constraints on exotic couplings from recoil distributions.
