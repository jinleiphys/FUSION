# Fluctuation of Voids in Hadronization at Phase Transition

> Starting from the recognition that hadrons are not produced smoothly at phase transition, the fluctuation of spatial patterns is investigated by finding a measure of the voids that exhibits scaling behavior. The Ising model is used to simulate a cross-over in quark-hadron phase transition. A threshold in hadron density is used to define a void. The dependence of the scaling exponents on that threshold is found to provide useful information on some properties of the hadronization process. The complication in heavy-ion collision introduces the possibility of configuration mixing, which can also be studied in this approach. Numerical criteria on the scaling exponents have been found that can be used to discriminate phase-transition processes from other hadronization processes having nothing to do with critical phenomena.

## Key claim

The scaling exponents of void moments in hadronization can discriminate a cross-over phase transition from other hadronization processes, but the specific numerical values of these exponents depend on the density threshold used to define a void and on whether hadronization occurs at a unique temperature or over a range of temperatures.

## Method

The authors simulate a cross-over quark-hadron phase transition using the 2D Ising model on a 256x256 lattice with cell size epsilon=4. They define hadron density in each cell as proportional to the square of the aligned cell spin, then define bins of varying size and classify a bin as "empty" when its average hadron density falls below a threshold rho_0. For each configuration, they compute G_q moments from the void size distribution and study how the ensemble-averaged moments scale with the number of bins M, extracting scaling exponents gamma_q and sigma_q. They also study configuration mixing by assembling mixed configurations from quadrants of four independent pure configurations.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is identified as T_c = 2.315 (in units of J/k_B), where normalized factorial moments F_q exhibit scaling behavior.
- At T_c, the average hadron density obeys <rho> - <rho_c> proportional to (T_c - T)^eta with eta = 1.67.
- The scaling exponent gamma_q for the average G_q moments follows gamma_q = c_0 + c q with c = 0.8 at T_c and rho_0 = 20.
- The scaling exponent sigma_q for the fluctuation measure S_q follows sigma_q = s_0 + s q with s = 0.76 at T_c and rho_0 = 20.
- The lowest values of c and s are 0.7 and 0.67 respectively, found when rho_0 = 50.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of cluster scaling in the Ising model simulation of quark-hadron phase transition (Hwa and Zhang, 1999) and the analysis of rapidity gaps in hadronic processes (Hwa, 1999). It provides a new observable based on void patterns that is robust against configuration mixing, which is important because experimental data from heavy-ion collisions necessarily integrate over a range of evolution times. The approach enables experimental tests using p_T cuts to isolate narrow time intervals, building on earlier work showing that final-state hadron gas effects minimally disturb the scaling behavior.
