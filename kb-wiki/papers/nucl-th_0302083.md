# Quantum thermodynamic fluctuations of a chaotic Fermi-gas model

> We investigate the thermodynamics of a Fermi gas whose single-particle energy levels are given by the complex zeros of the Riemann zeta function. This is a model for a gas, and in particular for an atomic nucleus, with an underlying fully chaotic classical dynamics. The probability distributions of the quantum fluctuations of the grand potential and entropy of the gas are computed as a function of temperature and compared, with good agreement, with general predictions obtained from random matrix theory and periodic orbit theory (based on prime numbers). In each case the universal and non--universal regimes are identified.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quantum thermodynamic fluctuations of a chaotic Fermi gas are not universally described by random matrix theory at all temperatures; instead, universality holds only when fluctuations are controlled by local single-particle level statistics, while non-universal short periodic orbits (prime numbers) dominate the grand potential fluctuations at all temperatures and the entropy fluctuations at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors construct a Fermi gas model ("Riemannium") whose single-particle energy levels are the imaginary parts of the complex zeros of the Riemann zeta function, which exhibit GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble) statistics characteristic of chaotic systems without time-reversal symmetry. They compute the probability distributions of the fluctuating parts of the grand potential and entropy as functions of temperature by averaging over a chemical potential window, and compare these numerical results to analytical predictions derived from the diagonal approximation of the semiclassical trace formula (where prime numbers play the role of periodic orbits) and from random matrix theory.

## Key numbers

- The shortest periodic orbit energy scale: Ec = 2π / log 2 ≈ 9.06472 (arbitrary units)
- The corresponding temperature scale: Tc = Ec / 2π² ≈ 0.459224
- The asymptotic diagonal approximation variance of the grand potential at T=0: ⟨Ω̃²⟩_D ≈ 0.079290
- The universal saturation constant for the entropy variance in the intermediate temperature regime: I₁ ≈ 0.0709159
- The constant for the linear growth of entropy variance at low temperatures: I₂ ≈ 1.51836
- The mean level spacing at Fermi energy: δ = 2π / log(μ/2π), with T_δ = δ / 2π² ≈ 0.013 for the chemical potential window used

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed in Leboeuf and Monastra (2003) for the statistical properties of quantum thermodynamic fluctuations in integrable and chaotic ballistic Fermi gases, and applies it to the Riemannium model introduced in a prior paper (Leboeuf and Monastra, 2001). The Riemannium provides a unique testbed because all semiclassical ingredients (prime numbers as periodic orbits) are analytically known, unlike in realistic chaotic systems. The results confirm that ground-state energy fluctuations are controlled by short periodic orbits and are therefore non-universal, contradicting the naive expectation that fully chaotic dynamics implies universal random-matrix behavior for all thermodynamic quantities. The findings enable future tests of the interplay between universal and non-universal regimes in realistic systems such as atomic nuclei and metallic grains.
