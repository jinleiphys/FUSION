# Test of the $X(3872)$ Structure in Antiproton-Nucleus Collisions

> The present day experimental data on the $X(3872)$ decays do not allow to make clear conclusions on the dominating structure of this state. We discuss here an alternative way to study its structure by means of the two-step $\bar D^*$ (or $D$) production in $\bar p A$ reactions. If this process is mediated by $X(3872)$, the characteristic narrow peaks of the $\bar D^*$ (or $D$) distributions in the light cone momentum fraction at small transverse momenta will appear. This would unambiguously signal the $D \bar D^*$ + c.c. molecular composition of the $X(3872)$ state.

# Test of the X(3872) Structure in Antiproton-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

If the X(3872) state has a dominant D D* + c.c. molecular composition, then in antiproton-nucleus collisions the two-step production of D* (or D) mesons mediated by X(3872) will produce characteristic narrow peaks in the light cone momentum fraction distribution at small transverse momenta, which would unambiguously signal the molecular structure. The claim is conditional: the observable signal depends on the binding energy of the molecule, with smaller binding energies producing sharper peaks.

## Method

The authors model the process where an antiproton annihilates on a bound proton in a nuclear target to produce X(3872), which then propagates through the nuclear residue and undergoes a stripping reaction on a nucleon, releasing a D* or D meson. They use the generalized eikonal approximation with a Glauber-type expression for the differential cross section, incorporating the molecule wave function as an asymptotic solution of the Schroedinger equation, the total cross sections for pD and pD* interactions estimated from the color dipole model, and a nuclear density model with a high-momentum tail from short-range proton-neutron correlations.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the D0 D*0 molecule is estimated as Eb < 0.2 MeV (from Tomaradze (2015) based on CLEO data), with an alternative estimate of Eb ~ 0.5 MeV for neutral components and Eb ~ 8 MeV for charged components.
- The root-mean-square distance between components in the molecule is estimated as 1.1-4.4 fm, with the lower limit for charged components (Eb ~ 8 MeV) and the upper limit for neutral components (Eb ~ 0.5 MeV).
- The partial decay width X -> p pbar is estimated as Gamma_{X -> pbar p} ~ 30 eV (from Braaten (2007)).
- The total cross sections for pD and pD* interactions are estimated as sigma_{pD}^{tot} ~ sigma_{pD*}^{tot} ~ 14 mb at antiproton beam momentum p_lab = 7 GeV/c.
- The slope parameters for pD and pD* scattering are estimated as B_{pD} ~ B_{pD*} ~ 4 GeV^{-2}.
- The total Xp cross section is sigma_{X p}^{tot} = 26 mb for the D0 D*0 component and 23 mb for the D+ D*- component.
- The molecule composition is 86% D0 D*0 + c.c., 12% D+ D*- + c.c., and 2% Ds+ Ds*- + c.c. (from Aceti (2012)).
- The peaks in the light cone momentum fraction distribution appear at alpha = 2m_D*/m_X = 1.04 for D* and alpha = 2m_D/m_X = 0.96 for D at k_t = 0.
- For the Y(4360) state, the binding energy is 67 MeV and the total width is Gamma_{Y(4360)}^{tot} = 74 MeV, with an assumed branching ratio Gamma_{Y(4360) -> pbar p}/Gamma_{Y(4360)}^{tot} = 10^{-4}.
- The mass difference between D*0 and D1^0 mesons is ~ 414 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Larionov (2015) to use antiproton-nucleus reactions to explore the structure of X(3872). It addresses the ambiguity in existing decay data, which cannot distinguish between molecular, charmonium, tetraquark, or hybrid structures, as noted in studies by Guo (2014) showing that radiative decays are weakly sensitive to the structure at large distances. The proposed method could be applied to other exotic charmonium candidates such as Y(4360), which may be a D*0 D1^0 + c.c. molecule (Cleven 2015), and could also test molecular structures of light mesons like a0(980) and f0(980). The experimental studies are possible at PANDA, J-PARC, JLab, and COMPASS.
