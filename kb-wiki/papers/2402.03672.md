# The spin alignment of rho mesons in a pion gas

> We study the spin alignment of neutral rho mesons in a pion gas using spin kinetic or Boltzmann equations. The $ρππ$ coupling is given by the chiral effective theory. The collision terms at the leading and next-to-leading order in spin Boltzmann equations are derived. The evolution of the spin density matrix of the neutral rho meson is simulated with different initial conditions. The numerical results show that the interaction of pions and neutral rho mesons creates very small spin alignment in the central rapidity region if there is no rho meson in the system at the initial time. Such a small spin alignment in the central rapidity region will decay rapidly toward zero in later time. If there are rho mesons with a sizable spin alignment at the initial time the spin alignment will also decrease rapidly. We also considered the effect on $ρ_{00}$ from the elliptic flow of pions in the blast wave model. With vanishing spin alignment at the initial time, the deviation of $ρ_{00}$ from 1/3 is positive but very small.

## Key claim

The spin alignment of neutral rho mesons in a pion gas, as quantified by the deviation of the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ from 1/3, is very small and decays rapidly toward zero in the central rapidity region when no rho mesons are present at the initial time; even if rho mesons initially have sizable spin alignment, it also decreases rapidly.

## Method

The authors derived spin Boltzmann equations for neutral rho mesons from Kadanoff-Baym equations in the closed-time-path formalism, using the chiral effective theory with SU(2) flavor symmetry for the ρππ coupling. Collision terms were computed at leading order (LO) for the process ρ⁰ ↔ π⁺π⁻ and at next-to-leading order (NLO) for ρ⁰π± ↔ ρ⁰π± scattering and ρ⁰ρ⁰ ↔ π⁺π⁻ processes. The evolution of the spin density matrix was simulated numerically under the assumption of spatial homogeneity, with pion propagators regulated by including medium effects from self-energy corrections.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson mass is taken as m_ρ = 770 MeV and the pion mass as m_π = 139 MeV.
- The ρππ coupling constant is g_{ρππ} ≈ 5.9.
- With vanishing spin alignment at the initial time, the deviation of ρ₀₀ from 1/3 is positive but very small; the spin alignment in the central rapidity region decays rapidly toward zero at later times.

## Context

This work extends the spin Boltzmann equation approach previously developed for vector mesons by Sheng et al. (2019, 2022) and applies it specifically to the ρ⁰ meson in a pion gas, which differs from the φ meson case because the ρ⁰ has a very short lifetime and decays mainly inside the medium. The study is relevant to searches for the chiral magnetic effect (CME), since ρ⁰ decay to π± contributes background to the γ correlator used in CME measurements, as noted by STAR (2013) and Tang (2019). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on ρ⁰ spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions.
