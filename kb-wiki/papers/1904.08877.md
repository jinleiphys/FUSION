# Nonlinear flavor development of a two-dimensional neutrino gas

> We present a numerical survey of the nonlinear flavor development of dense neutrino gases. This study is based on the stationary, two-dimensional ($x$ and $z$), two-beam, monochromatic neutrino line model with a periodic boundary condition along the $x$ direction. Similar to a previous work, we find that small-scale flavor structures can develop in a neutrino gas even if the physical conditions are nearly homogeneous along the $x$ axis initially. The power diffusion from the large-scale to small-scale structures increases with the neutrino density and helps to establish a semi-exponential dependence of the magnitudes of the Fourier moments on the corresponding wave numbers. The overall flavor conversion probabilities in the neutrino gases with small initial sinusoidal perturbations reach certain equilibrium values at large distances which are mainly determined by the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry. Similar phenomena also exist in a neutrino gas with a localized initial perturbation, albeit only inside an expanding flavor conversion region. Our work suggests that a statistical treatment may be possible for the collective flavor oscillations of a dense neutrino gas in a multi-dimensional environment.

## Key claim
Small-scale flavor structures can develop in a dense neutrino gas even when physical conditions are nearly homogeneous along the transverse direction initially, and the overall flavor conversion probabilities at large distances reach equilibrium values determined mainly by the neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry, with little dependence on the neutrino density or mass hierarchy.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of the stationary, two-dimensional (x and z), two-beam, monochromatic neutrino line model with periodic boundary conditions along the x direction. They solved the equations of motion for flavor polarization vectors in direct space using a finite difference method derived from the Lax-Wendroff algorithm, discretizing the x axis into up to 240,000 equal intervals. The study surveyed a wide range of parameter space including normal and inverted neutrino mass hierarchies, various neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry ratios, and different neutrino self-coupling strengths, using both sinusoidal and localized initial perturbations.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino self-coupling strength μ values surveyed: 10, 25, and 50 (in units of the effective vacuum oscillation frequency ωeff = 1)
- Neutrino-antineutrino asymmetry α values surveyed: 0.6, 0.7, and 0.8
- Periodic box size L = 30 (in units of 1/ωeff)
- Neutrino velocity along z direction: vz = 1/√2 (emission angle π/4)
- Initial perturbation amplitudes: ε0± = 2×10⁻³ and ε1± = -10⁻⁴ for inverted hierarchy cases; ε1⁻ = 2ε1⁺ = -2×10⁻⁴ for normal hierarchy cases
- For the μ = 50 case with sleeping-top regime: ε0± = 10⁻³, ε1± = 5×10⁻⁵, and ε200± = 5×10⁻¹⁰
- Localized perturbation parameters: εg± = 2×10⁻³, σ² = 0.2 for inverted hierarchy; εg⁻ = 2εg⁺ = 4×10⁻³ for normal hierarchy

## Context
This work extends the pilot study by Mirizzi (2015) which featured a single parameter set, providing a comprehensive numerical survey of the two-beam neutrino line model. It builds on the linearized flavor stability analysis by Duan (2014) which predicted exponential growth of inhomogeneous oscillation modes, and on the flavor pendulum model by Hannestad (2006) which describes homogeneous bipolar oscillations. The results suggest that a statistical treatment may be possible for collective flavor oscillations of dense neutrino gases in multi-dimensional environments, and they demonstrate that power diffusion from large-scale to small-scale structures resembles the development of turbulence in fluid dynamics as noted by Mirizzi (2015) and kinematic decoherence in isotropic neutrino gases as studied by Raffelt (2007).
