# Electric dipole response of 208Pb from proton inelastic scattering: constraints on neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy

> The electric dipole (E1) response of 208Pb has been precisely determined by measuring Coulomb excitation induced by proton scattering at very forward angles. The electric dipole polarizability, defined as inverse energy-weighted sum rule of the E1 strength, has been extracted as 20.1+-0.6 fm^3. The data can be used to constrain the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb to 0.168(+-0.009)_expt(+-0.013)_theo(+-0.021)_est fm, where the subscript "expt" refers to the experimental uncertainty, "theor" to the theoretical confidence band and "est" to the uncertainty associated with the estimation of the symmetry energy at the saturation density. In addition, a constraint band has been extracted in the plane of the symmetry energy (J) and its slope parameter (L) at the saturation density.

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb, extracted from proton inelastic scattering at very forward angles, is 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³, which constrains the neutron skin thickness to 0.165 ± (0.009)_expt ± (0.013)_theor ± (0.021)_est fm, where the uncertainties are experimental, theoretical confidence band, and an estimate of the symmetry energy at saturation density, respectively.

## Method

A polarized proton beam at 295 MeV was scattered off an isotopically enriched 208Pb target, and the scattered protons were detected using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at angles between 0° and 10°. The E1 strength distribution was extracted by decomposing the cross sections into multipole components using two independent methods: polarization transfer analysis (using the total spin transfer Σ) and multipole-decomposition analysis (MDA) with distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations. The dipole polarizability was then computed as the inverse energy-weighted sum rule of the E1 strength.

## Key numbers

- Dipole polarizability of 208Pb: α_D = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³ (up to 130 MeV, combining present data up to 20 MeV with gamma absorption data above 20 MeV).
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Δr_np = 0.165 ± (0.009)_expt ± (0.013)_theor ± (0.021)_est fm.
- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: L = 43 ± (6)_expt ± (8)_theor ± (12)_est MeV (adopting J = 31 ± 2 MeV).
- Energy resolution of the experiment: 30 keV (FWHM).
- Beam energy: 295 MeV.
- Target thickness: 5.2 mg/cm².

## Context

This work extends the correlation between dipole polarizability and neutron skin thickness first noted by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010) using self-consistent mean-field calculations with the SV_min Skyrme interaction. The result provides a more precise constraint on the neutron skin thickness than the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (which gave Δr_np = 0.302 ± 0.175 ± 0.026 ± 0.005 fm), and agrees within error bars with antiprotonic atom measurements by Klos (2007) and elastic proton scattering results at 650 MeV and 295 MeV. The extracted constraint band in the J-L plane enables improved predictions for neutron star properties, supernova explosion dynamics, and other astrophysical phenomena, and the authors note ongoing work to measure dipole polarizabilities for 48Ca, 90Zr, 96Mo, 120Sn, and 144,154Sm to further constrain energy density functionals.
