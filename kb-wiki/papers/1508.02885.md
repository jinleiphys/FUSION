# Can we decide whether QCD is confining or not at high temperature?

> At high temperature measurements of the Polyakov loop suggest a deconfinement transition to the (strongly interacting) quark-gluon plasma. At the same time at the infinitely large temperature the four-dimensional QCD is reduced to the three-dimensional QCD that is confining. The Polyakov loop and related Z_3 symmetry are strict order parameters only for infinitely heavy quarks. In such a situation the SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries of confinement in the light quark sector could be helpful to distinguish between the confining and deconfining phase in a regime where SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R and U(1)_A symmetries are manifest. In order to reveal a presence or absence of these symmetries one needs to measure and compare correlation functions related by these symmetry transformations.

# Can we decide whether QCD is confining or not at high temperature?

## Key claim
The Polyakov loop and Z3 symmetry are strict order parameters only for infinitely heavy quarks, so they cannot definitively distinguish confinement from deconfinement in the light quark sector at high temperature. Instead, the SU(2)_CS (chiralspin) and SU(4) symmetries of confinement could serve as order parameters in the regime where chiral SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R and U(1)_A symmetries are already restored.

## Method
The author proposes measuring and comparing correlation functions of hadronic operators that are related by SU(2)_CS and SU(4) transformations but not by SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_A symmetries. Suitable operators include J=1 rho-meson operators (vector and axial-vector currents) and nucleon operators that belong to distinct chiral representations but the same chiralspin and SU(4) representations. Coincidence of these correlators would indicate confinement with restored chiral symmetry, while their inequality would indicate a deconfined plasma phase.

## Key numbers
- The JLQCD collaboration demonstrated that at T > T_c, both SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R and U(1)_A symmetries are restored (no explicit temperature value given in the text).
- At infinitely high temperature, four-dimensional QCD reduces to three-dimensional QCD, which is known to be confining (no explicit temperature scale provided).

## Context
This work extends the observation by Glozman and collaborators (2014-2015) that SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries emerge in hadron spectra upon elimination of near-zero modes from quark propagators, as seen in lattice simulations by the Graz group. It directly challenges the conventional interpretation of Polyakov loop measurements (Polyakov, 1978) as unambiguous deconfinement signals, and references the argument by Appelquist and Pisarski (1981) that high-temperature QCD reduces to a confining three-dimensional theory. The proposal enables lattice QCD practitioners to design specific correlation-function measurements that could settle whether the high-temperature phase of QCD is genuinely deconfined or remains confining with restored chiral symmetry.
