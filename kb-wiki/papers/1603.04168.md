# Hadronic molecular states from the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction

> In this work, the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction is studied in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach combined with the one-boson-exchange model. With the help of the hidden-gauge Lagrangian, the exchanges of pseudoscalar mesons ($π$ and $η$) and vector mesons ($ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$) are considered to describe the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction. Besides the direct vector-meson exchange which can be related to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, pseudoscalar-meson exchanges also play important roles in the mechanism of the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction. The poles of scattering amplitude are searched to find the molecular states produced from the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction. In the case of quantum number $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(1^{++})$, a pole is found with a reasonable cutoff, which can be related to the $f_1(1285)$ in experiment. Another bound state with $0^-(1^{+-})$ is also produced from the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction, which can be related to the $h_1(1380)$. In the isovector sector, the interaction is much weaker and a bound state with $1^+(1^{+})$ relevant to the $b_1(1235)$ is produced but at a larger cutoff. Our results suggest that in the hadronic molecular state picture the $f_1(1285)$ and $b_1(1235)$ are the strange partners of the $X(3872)$ and $Z_c(3900)$, respectively.

## Key claim

The $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction can produce hadronic molecular states that correspond to the experimentally observed $f_1(1285)$, $h_1(1380)$, and $b_1(1235)$, but the identification of the $b_1(1235)$ as a bound state requires a larger cutoff parameter than for the isoscalar states, indicating a weaker interaction in the isovector sector.

## Method

The authors studied the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction using a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach combined with the one-boson-exchange model. They derived potentials from hidden-gauge Lagrangians including exchanges of pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi$, $\eta$) and vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$), and searched for poles in the scattering amplitude to identify molecular states. An exponential regularization with cutoff $\Lambda$ was used, and the authors argued that an additional form factor for the exchanged meson is spurious and should be removed.

## Key numbers

- A bound state with $I^G(J^{PC}) = 0^+(1^{++})$ appears at mass $W \approx 1.285$ GeV with cutoff $\Lambda \approx 1.1$ GeV, related to the $f_1(1285)$ (experimental mass $1281.9 \pm 0.5$ MeV, width $24.2 \pm 1.1$ MeV)
- A bound state with $0^-(1^{+-})$ is produced from the $K\bar{K}^*$ interaction, related to the $h_1(1380)$
- In the isovector sector, a bound state with $1^+(1^{+})$ relevant to the $b_1(1235)$ requires a larger cutoff $\Lambda \approx 1.8$ GeV to produce a pole near $W \approx 1.389$ GeV
- The direct vector-meson exchange potential with $q^2 \to 0$ produces bound states in the isoscalar sector at $\Lambda \sim 0.8$ GeV, close to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term result at $\Lambda \sim 0.7$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular state picture from the charmed sector (where $X(3872)$ and $Z_c(3900)$ are interpreted as $D\bar{D}^*$ molecules, as studied by He (2015)) to the strange sector via $K\bar{K}^*$ interactions. It builds on prior chiral unitary approach studies by Roca (2005) and Geng (2015) that suggested $f_1(1285)$ as a dynamically generated state, but uses a full one-boson-exchange model rather than only the Weinberg-Tomozawa term. The results enable future searches for the predicted strange partners of known exotic states and suggest that the $h_1(1380)$ may have a charmed partner not yet observed experimentally.
