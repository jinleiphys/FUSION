# Charge-Dependence of the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> Based upon the Bonn meson-exchange-model for the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction, we calculate the charge-independence breaking (CIB) of the $NN$ interaction due to pion-mass splitting. Besides the one-pion-exchange (OPE), we take into account the $2π$-exchange model and contributions from three and four irreducible pion exchanges. We calculate the CIB differences in the $^1S_0$ effective range parameters as well as phase shift differences for partial waves up to total angular momentum J=4 and laboratory energies below 300 MeV. We find that the CIB effect from OPE dominates in all partial waves. However, the CIB effects from the $2π$ model are noticable up to D-waves and amount to about 40% of the OPE CIB-contribution in some partial waves, at 300 MeV. The effects from 3$π$ and 4$π$ contributions are negligible except in $^1S_0$ and $^3P_2$.

# Charge-Dependence of the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

## Key claim

Based on the Bonn meson-exchange model, the charge-independence breaking (CIB) of the nucleon-nucleon interaction due to pion-mass splitting is dominated by one-pion-exchange (OPE) in all partial waves, but two-pion-exchange contributions are noticeable up to D-waves and amount to about 40% of the OPE CIB contribution in some partial waves at 300 MeV. The total calculated CIB accounts for about 80% of the empirical value for the singlet scattering length difference, leaving 20-30% unexplained.

## Method

The authors used the Bonn meson-exchange model for the NN interaction and calculated CIB effects by replacing diagrams for the average of pp and nn scattering with corresponding np scattering diagrams, introducing the charged and neutral pion mass difference. They computed CIB phase shift differences for partial waves up to total angular momentum J=4 and laboratory energies below 300 MeV, as well as CIB differences in the 1S0 effective range parameters, considering contributions from OPE, 2π-exchange with NN, NΔ, and ΔΔ intermediate states, πρ-exchanges, and further 3π and 4π contributions (πσ+πω).

## Key numbers

- Empirical singlet scattering lengths: a^N_pp = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm, a^N_nn = -18.8 ± 0.3 fm, a_np = -23.75 ± 0.01 fm
- Empirical CIB scattering length difference: Δa_CIB = 5.7 ± 0.5 fm
- Pion masses used: m_π± = 139.568 MeV, m_π0 = 134.974 MeV (mass splitting of 3.4%)
- Total calculated Δa_CIB = 4.65 fm (about 80% of empirical value)
- Total calculated Δr_CIB = 0.115 fm (consistent with empirical value of 0.05 ± 0.13 fm)
- 1S0 phase shift difference at 1 MeV: 4.36 degrees; at 300 MeV: about 0.6 degrees
- 3P0 phase shift difference around 50 MeV: almost 1 degree; above 25 MeV: 0.95-0.65 degrees
- 3P1 phase shift difference above 25 MeV: about 0.35 degrees
- 3P2 phase shift difference above 25 MeV: around 0.2 degrees
- πNN coupling constant used: g^2_π/4π = 14.4

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Ericson and Miller (1983) and Coon and Machleidt (1986) that only addressed the singlet scattering length, by providing phase shift differences for partial waves up to J=4 and energies up to 300 MeV. It complements a companion paper on charge-asymmetry of the NN interaction by Li and Machleidt (1998). The results show that while OPE dominates CIB in all partial waves, the unexplained 20-30% of the singlet scattering length difference remains a puzzle, as recent chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion-photon exchange by Kolling (1998) actually decrease Δa_CIB further rather than resolving the discrepancy. The authors note that current chiral models for the NN interaction are not yet suitable for reliable CIB calculations in S and P waves.
