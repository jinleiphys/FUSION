# Sensitivity of elements of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter to the properties of neutron-rich systems

> The sensitivity of nuclear symmetry energy elements at the saturation density to the binding energies of ultra neutron-rich nuclei (neutron to proton ratio $\sim$ 2) and the maximum mass of neutron star is explored within a relativistic mean field model. Values of the interaction parameters governing the isovector strengths and the symmetry elements are determined in tighter bounds. Assessments based on the sensitivity matrix reveal that the properties of extreme neutron-rich systems play a predominant role in narrowing down the uncertainties in the various symmetry energy parameters. The calculations are extended over a wide range of nuclear matter density and the results are discussed.

## Key claim

The sensitivity analysis reveals that binding energies of nuclei with neutron-to-proton asymmetry δ > 0.3 (such as ²⁴O, ³⁰Ne, ³⁶Mg, and ⁵⁸Ca) play a crucial role in constraining the symmetry energy parameters, while nuclei with δ ∼ 0.26–0.28 do not significantly improve the uncertainties.

## Method

The authors constructed two relativistic mean field (RMF) models: SINPB, fitted to binding energies and charge radii of a standard set of nuclei including some with δ ∼ 0.26–0.28, and SINPA, which additionally included four highly asymmetric nuclei (δ > 0.3) and the observed maximum mass of a neutron star. They optimized the objective function χ² for each model and performed covariance analysis to determine uncertainties on parameters and observables, then used a sensitivity matrix to quantify how different groups of fit data influence the model parameters and nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy coefficient at saturation C⁰_sym: 33.95 ± 2.41 MeV for SINPB, 31.20 ± 1.11 MeV for SINPA
- Density slope parameter L₀: 71.55 ± 18.89 MeV for SINPB, 53.86 ± 4.66 MeV for SINPA
- Neutron skin Δr_np of ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.241 ± 0.040 fm for SINPB, 0.183 ± 0.022 fm for SINPA
- Incompressibility coefficient K₀: 206 ± 20 MeV for SINPB, 203 ± 6 MeV for SINPA
- Maximum neutron star mass from SINPA: 1.98 ± 0.03 M_⊙ (compared to observed value 2.01 M_⊙)
- Binding energy of ²⁴O changes by ∼2 MeV when Δr_np of ²⁰⁸Pb changes from 0.18 fm (SINPA) to 0.24 fm (SINPB)
- Saturation density ρ₀: 0.150 ± 0.002 fm⁻³ for SINPB, 0.151 ± 0.001 fm⁻³ for SINPA

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Mondal (2015) and Chen and Piekarewicz (2015), which suggested that very neutron-rich isotopes carry stronger isovector signatures. The results contradict the wide variations in C⁰_v and L₀ found across different energy density functionals surveyed by Dutra (2012, 2014), and show that including nuclei with δ > 0.3 can narrow uncertainties comparably to or better than analyses based on nuclear masses by Moller (2012) and Jiang (2012). The tighter constraints on symmetry energy parameters enable more precise predictions for neutron star structure and the neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb, and motivate experimental measurements of binding energies for exotic neutron-rich nuclei.
