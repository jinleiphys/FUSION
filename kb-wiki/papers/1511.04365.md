# Higher order effective interactions and effective bosonized model for 2-N particle states

> In this work, an effective fermion model with particular higher order interactions given by: $I_{II} = \sum_n^N g_{2^n} (\barψ_a ψ_a)^{2^n}$, for finite $N$, is investigated by means of the auxiliary field method by taking into account 2$n$-particle states as proposed in Ref. [1]. The bosonized model was found to exhibit an approximate symmetry when expanded for weak field fluctuations around the mean field solutions. In the present work, the role of the mean field solutions for the corresponding auxiliary fields is investigated. With the integration of fermions, the resulting determinant is expanded in a polynomial boson model in the weak field approximation and the approximate symmetry found for this series does not appear if the boson mean fields are set to zero.

# Higher order effective interactions and effective bosonized model for 2-N particle states

## Key claim
The approximate symmetry between N-fermion states found in a previous bosonized model (Braghin 2015) appears only when nontrivial mean field solutions (condensates) for the auxiliary fields are present; if the boson mean fields are set to zero, this approximate symmetry is completely absent.

## Method
The author considers a fermion model with a series of higher-order interactions of the form \(I_{II} = \sum_n^N g_{2^n} (\bar{\psi}_a \psi_a)^{2^n}\) for finite \(N\). Using the auxiliary field method, a set of \(N/2\) auxiliary fields is introduced via Gaussian integrals, with shifts chosen to cancel all polynomial interactions. The fermion fields are integrated out, and the resulting determinant is expanded in a polynomial boson model under the weak field approximation, considering only trivial (zero) solutions for the saddle point equations.

## Key numbers
- The coupling constants \(g_{2^n}\) have dimension \([g^{2^n}] = M^{d - (d-1)2^n}\), where \(M\) has dimension of mass.
- The auxiliary fields have dimension \([M^2]\).
- The series of interactions runs from \(n=1\) to \(N\), with \(N\) even.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Braghin 2015) of the same fermion model with higher-order interactions, which found an approximate symmetry in the bosonized model when mean field solutions were present. The present paper shows that this symmetry disappears when mean fields are taken to be zero, clarifying the role of condensates in generating the approximate degeneracy between 2-particle and 4-particle states observed in light scalar mesons and cold atoms. The approach is complementary to effective field theory treatments (EFT, 2014; EFT2, 2014) and uses the auxiliary field method as developed by Kashiwa (1983), Gross and Neveu (1974), Kleinert (2011), and Negele and Orland (1988).
