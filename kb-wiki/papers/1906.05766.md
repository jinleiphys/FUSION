# Chromopolarizabilities of bottomonia from the $Υ(2S,3S,4S) \to Υ(1S,2S)ππ$ transitions

> The dipion transitions $Υ(2S,3S,4S) \to Υ(1S,2S)ππ$ are systematically studied by considering the mechanisms of the hadronization of soft gluons, exchanging the bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states, and the bottom-meson loops. The strong pion-pion final-state interaction, especially including the channel coupling to $K\bar{K}$ in the $S$-wave, is taken into account in a model-independent way using the dispersion theory. Through fitting to the available experimental data, we extract values of the transition chromopolarizabilities $|α_{Υ(mS)Υ(nS)}|$, which measure the chromoelectric couplings of the bottomonia with soft gluons. It is found that the $Z_b$ exchange has a slight impact on the extracted chromopolarizablity values, and the obtained $|α_{Υ(2S)Υ(1S)}|$ considering the $Z_b$ exchange is $(0.29\pm 0.20)~\text{GeV}^{-3}$. Our results could be useful in studying the interactions of bottomonium with light hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper extracts values of the transition chromopolarizabilities for bottomonia from dipion transitions, finding that the value of $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(2S)\Upsilon(1S)}|$ is $(0.29\pm 0.20)~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ when including the $Z_b$ exchange mechanism, which has only a slight impact on the extracted value.

## Method

The authors systematically study the dipion transitions $\Upsilon(2S,3S,4S) \to \Upsilon(1S,2S)\pi\pi$ by considering three mechanisms: hadronization of soft gluons (parametrized via chiral contact terms), exchange of the bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states, and bottom-meson loop diagrams. The strong pion-pion final-state interaction, including channel coupling to $K\bar{K}$ in the $S$-wave, is treated model-independently using dispersion theory in the form of modified Omnes solutions. A simultaneous fit is performed to the experimental data on $\pi\pi$ invariant mass spectra and helicity angular distributions from the Belle and CLEO Collaborations.

## Key numbers

* $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(2S)\Upsilon(1S)}| = 0.29 \pm 0.20~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ (including $Z_b$ exchange and bottom-meson loops)
* $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(3S)\Upsilon(1S)}| = 0.06 \pm 0.03~\text{GeV}^{-3}$
* $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(4S)\Upsilon(1S)}| = (5.4 \pm 3.5) \times 10^{-4}~\text{GeV}^{-3}$
* $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(4S)\Upsilon(2S)}| = 0.43 \pm 0.01~\text{GeV}^{-3}$
* $\kappa_{\Upsilon(2S)\Upsilon(1S)} = 1.52 \pm 1.17$ (parameter in the chiral amplitude)
* $\chi^2/(\text{number of events})$ values: $794.7/98$ for $\Upsilon(2S) \to \Upsilon(1S) \pi\pi$, $288.4/151$ for $\Upsilon(3S) \to \Upsilon(1S) \pi\pi$, $75.3/43$ for $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(1S) \pi^+\pi^-$, and $14.7/23$ for $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(2S) \pi^+\pi^-$

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of dipion transitions between bottomonia by including the effects of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ states discovered by Belle in 2011, which were not considered in earlier extractions of chromopolarizabilities by Voloshin (2004) and Guo (2006). The value of $|\alpha_{\Upsilon(2S)\Upsilon(1S)}| = 0.29 \pm 0.20~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ agrees within errors with the result of $0.24 \pm 0.01~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ from Guo (2006) using a chiral unitary approach, and is smaller than the value of approximately $0.66~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ from Voloshin (2004) which did not include pion-pion final-state interactions. The extracted chromopolarizabilities will be useful for studying light-hadron-bottomonium interactions and for interpreting the hadro-bottomonium and baryo-bottomonium bound state predictions of Ferretti (2018) and Anwar (2018).
