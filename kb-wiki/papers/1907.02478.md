# Effective surface properties of light, heavy, and super-heavy nuclei

> Starting from light to superheavy nuclei, we have calculated the effective surface properties such as the symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and symmetry energy curvature using the coherent density fluctuation model. The isotopic chains of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Pb, and Z = 120 are considered in the present analysis, which cover nuclei over the whole nuclear chart. The matter density distributions of these nuclei along with the ground state bulk properties are calculated within the spherically symmetric effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field model by using the recently developed IOPB-I, FSUGarnet, and G3 parameter sets. The calculated results are compared with the predictions of the widely used NL3 parameter set and found in good agreement. We observe a few signatures of shell and/or sub-shell structure in the isotopic chains of nuclei. The present investigations are quite relevant for the synthesis of exotic nuclei with high isospin asymmetry including superheavy and also to constrain an equation of state of nuclear matter.

# Effective surface properties of light, heavy, and super-heavy nuclei

## Key claim
The effective surface symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and symmetry energy curvature calculated using the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) with densities from the E-RMF model show signatures of shell and/or sub-shell structure across isotopic chains from light to superheavy nuclei, with results in good agreement with the widely used NL3 parameter set, though the NL3 values for symmetry energy and its coefficients are overestimated relative to allowed empirical ranges due to its stiffer equation of state.

## Method
The authors calculated ground state bulk properties and matter density distributions for isotopic chains of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Pb, and Z=120 using the spherically symmetric effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model with the IOPB-I, FSUGarnet, G3, and NL3 parameter sets. These densities were then used as input to the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), which folds nuclear matter symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and symmetry energy curvature with a weight function derived from the density distribution to obtain the corresponding effective surface properties for each finite nucleus.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy at saturation J = 33.30 MeV (IOPB-I), 30.95 MeV (FSUGarnet), 31.84 MeV (G3), and 37.43 MeV (NL3)
- Slope parameter L = 63.58 MeV (IOPB-I), 51.04 MeV (FSUGarnet), 49.31 MeV (G3), and 118.65 MeV (NL3)
- Symmetry energy curvature K_sym = -37.09 MeV (IOPB-I), 59.36 MeV (FSUGarnet), -106.07 MeV (G3), and 101.34 MeV (NL3)
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.149 fm^{-3} (IOPB-I), 0.153 fm^{-3} (FSUGarnet), 0.148 fm^{-3} (G3), and 0.148 fm^{-3} (NL3)
- Binding energy per particle for ^{208}Pb: 7.870 MeV (IOPB-I), 7.902 MeV (FSUGarnet), 7.863 MeV (G3), and 7.885 MeV (NL3), compared to experimental value 7.867 MeV
- Charge radius for ^{208}Pb: 5.521 fm (IOPB-I), 5.496 fm (FSUGarnet), 5.541 fm (G3), and 5.509 fm (NL3), compared to experimental value 5.501 fm
- Neutron skin-thickness for ^{208}Pb: 0.221 fm (IOPB-I), 0.162 fm (FSUGarnet), 0.180 fm (G3), and 0.283 fm (NL3), compared to experimental value 0.17 fm

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Gai et al. (2011) and Bhuyan et al. (2018) that used the CDFM approach to study symmetry energy in finite nuclei, now covering the entire nuclear chart from light (O) to superheavy (Z=120) isotopes. The results are compared with the NL3 parameter set from Lalazissis et al. (1997) and the newer IOPB-I, FSUGarnet, and G3 parameter sets, which have softer equations of state. The findings are relevant for constraining the nuclear equation of state and guiding future synthesis of exotic and superheavy nuclei at facilities such as FRIB, GSI, RIKEN, and FAIR.
