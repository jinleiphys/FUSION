# Low-Energy Theorems for Gluodynamics at Finite Temperature

> We generalize the low-energy theorems of gluodynamics to finite temperature. Examples of the theorems in the low and high temperature limits are given.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes zero-temperature low-energy theorems of gluodynamics (pure SU(N) gauge theory) to finite temperature by showing that the operator \(T\partial/\partial T\) must be included in the relations, and that the standard form of the scalar glueball potential remains valid at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors start from the partition function of gluodynamics in the imaginary-time formalism and use dimensional analysis to express the grand potential per unit volume as \(\Omega/V = \Lambda^4 f(\Lambda/T)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the quantum scale parameter. They then derive finite-temperature low-energy theorems by differentiating the grand potential with respect to the coupling constant and applying the operator \((4 - T\partial/\partial T)\). The theorems are verified explicitly at low temperatures using an effective scalar glueball Lagrangian, and at high temperatures using two-loop perturbative results for the pressure.

## Key numbers

- The glueball mass is given by \(m_G^2 = 4B_0/\phi_0^2\), where \(B_0\) is a constant of mass dimension 4 and \(\phi_0\) is the \(T=0\) vacuum field.
- At high temperatures, the two-loop pressure is \(P = \frac{\pi^2}{45}(N^2-1)T^4 \left[1 - \frac{5N}{4\pi}\alpha_s\right]\) for SU(N) gauge theory.
- The high-temperature result for the correlation function is \(\left(T\frac{d}{dT}-4\right)^n \langle \theta^{\mu}_{\mu}\rangle = \frac{b}{72}\left(\frac{-b}{2\pi}\right)^n (n+1)! N(N^2-1) \alpha_s^{n+2} T^4\), where \(b = 11N/3\).
- The strong coupling constant as a function of temperature is \(\alpha_s(T) = \frac{2\pi}{b\ln(cT/\Lambda)}\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature low-energy theorems of Novikov et al. (1981) and the finite-momentum theorems of Migdal and Shifman (1982) to finite temperature. It enables the application of these theorems to lattice gauge theory simulations at non-zero temperature and to model calculations of the gluonic plasma phase. The authors note that extensions to QCD at finite baryon density are under investigation.
