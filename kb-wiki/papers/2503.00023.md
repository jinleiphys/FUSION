# Molecular 9Be+p Fusion Reaction

> We study (nuclear) fusion reactions in Beryllium. We argue that some are nuclear long-distance molecular processes of interest for low energy nuclear reactions. For these we develop a novel reaction model. We note a chain of reactions that can naturally arise in Beryllium target created by nonequilibrium proton reactions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the $^9\mathrm{Be}+p\to ^8\mathrm{Be}+d$ reaction (Q=0.5592 MeV) is not well described by the standard R-matrix approach because the weakly bound valence neutron in $^9\mathrm{Be}$ (separation energy 1.6654 MeV) enables a long-distance molecular neutron transfer process, invalidating the short-range nuclear surface assumption of R-matrix theory.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical Born-Oppenheimer molecular model treating the $^9\mathrm{Be}+p$ collision system as a nuclear quasi-molecule where the neutron is asymptotically bound either to a $^8\mathrm{Be}=\alpha+\alpha$ core or to the proton. They construct approximate eigenstates using a linear combination of nuclear orbitals (LCNO) method with square-well potentials matched to the experimental neutron binding energies and core charge radii, then treat the relative motion of the $^8\mathrm{Be}$ and proton semiclassically to compute the dynamic tunneling barrier and neutron transfer probability via Landau-Zener equations.

- Q-value for $^9\mathrm{Be}+p\to ^8\mathrm{Be}+d$: 0.5592 MeV
- Neutron separation energy from $^8\mathrm{Be}$ core in $^9\mathrm{Be}$: $E_s=1.6654$ MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: $E_d=2.22457$ MeV
- Q-value for $^9\mathrm{Be}+d\to ^{10}\mathrm{Be}+p$: 4.5877 MeV
- Q-value for $^{10}\mathrm{Be}+p\to ^8\mathrm{Be}+t$: 0.0050 MeV
- Imaginary momentum on $^8\mathrm{Be}-n$ side: $|\tilde{P}_\mathrm{Be}|=52.7$ MeV
- Imaginary momentum on deuteron ($p-n$) side: $|\tilde{P}_d|=45.7$ MeV

- Charge radius of $^9\mathrm{Be}$: $r_c=2.52$ fm

## Context

This work extends the molecular reaction approach previously applied to electron transfer in atomic collisions (Rakityansky 2024) to neutron transfer in nuclear fusion, specifically for the $^9\mathrm{Be}+p$ system. The authors argue that the R-matrix method (standard textbook material for reactions like $^3\mathrm{He}+d$ and $^{11}\mathrm{B}+p$) is inadequate for this reaction due to the weak neutron binding and long-range tunneling. The paper proposes that this molecular fusion mechanism could enable aneutronic multi-step reaction cycles in $^9\mathrm{Be}$ targets, analogous to the solar pp- and CNO-cycles, and suggests applications in laser-driven nonequilibrium fusion environments (Labaune 2013, Biro 2023, Csernai 2025).
