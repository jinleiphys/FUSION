# Anharmonicities of giant dipole excitations

> The role of anharmonic effects on the excitation of the double giant dipole resonance is investigated in a simple macroscopic model.Perturbation theory is used to find energies and wave functions of the anharmonic ascillator.The cross sections for the electromagnetic excitation of the one- and two-phonon giant dipole resonances in energetic heavy-ion collisions are then evaluated through a semiclassical coupled-channel calculation.It is argued that the variations of the strength of the anharmonic potential should be combined with appropriate changes in the oscillator frequency,in order to keep the giant dipole resonance energy consistent with the experimental value.When this is taken into account,the effects of anharmonicities on the double giant dipole resonance excitation probabilities are small and cannot account for the well-known discrepancy between theory and experiment.

## Key claim

When the oscillator frequency is renormalized to keep the giant dipole resonance (GDR) energy fixed at the experimental value, anharmonic effects on the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) excitation probabilities are small and cannot account for the well-known discrepancy between theory and experiment, contrary to the earlier claim by Bortignon and Dasso (1997).

## Method

The authors used a simple macroscopic anharmonic oscillator model with a quartic perturbation term (r⁴-type) to describe the intrinsic motion of the projectile nucleus. They solved the Schrödinger equation using first-order perturbation theory to find energies and wavefunctions, then evaluated electromagnetic excitation cross sections for the one- and two-phonon giant dipole resonances in relativistic heavy-ion collisions through a semiclassical coupled-channel calculation using the code RELEX, based on Winther and Alder theory.

## Key numbers

- For the ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb collision at 640 A·MeV, with anharmonicity parameter B = -100 MeV/fm⁴ (β ≈ -0.7 × 10⁻²), the DGDR cross section enhancement relative to the harmonic value is only 4% when the GDR energy is fixed at 13.4 MeV, compared to 22% when the oscillator frequency is kept constant.
- For the ¹³⁶Xe + ²⁰⁸Pb collision at 700 A·MeV, with B = -150 MeV/fm⁴, the DGDR cross section enhancement is less than 10% when the GDR energy is maintained at its physical value, compared to 62% when the oscillator frequency is kept constant.
- The experimental GDR energy for ²⁰⁸Pb is 13.4 MeV.
- The dimensionless anharmonicity parameter β varies in the range -0.014 < β < 0.014, yielding renormalized oscillator frequencies between 0.93 and 1.08 times the experimental GDR energy.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic anharmonic oscillator model of Bortignon and Dasso (1997) and Volpe et al. (1995), who claimed that a small anharmonic perturbation could reproduce both the DGDR excitation energy and cross section for ²⁰⁸Pb + ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹³⁶Xe + ²⁰⁸Pb collisions. The authors show that proper renormalization of the oscillator frequency eliminates the claimed enhancement, consistent with microscopic calculations by Peña and Bortignon (2000) and Hamamoto (1999) which found that anharmonic effects are small and scale as 1/A with mass number. The results indicate that anharmonicities alone cannot resolve the systematic discrepancy between harmonic model predictions and experimental DGDR cross sections, suggesting that other effects such as the GDR width must be considered.
