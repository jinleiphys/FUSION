# Epsilon expansion for a Fermi gas at infinite scattering length

> We show that there exists a systematic expansion around four spatial dimensions for Fermi gas in the unitarity regime. We perform the calculations to leading and next-to-leading orders in the expansion over epsilon=4-d, where d is the dimensionality of space. We find the ratio of chemical potential and Fermi energy to be mu/eF=1/2 epsilon^3/2 + 1/16 epsilon^5/2 ln epsilon -0.0246 epsilon^5/2 and the ratio of the gap in the fermion quasiparticle spectrum and the chemical potential to be Delta/mu=2/epsilon-0.691. The minimum of the fermion dispersion curve is located at |p|=(2m epsilon_0)^1/2 where epsilon_0/mu=2+O(epsilon). Extrapolation to d=3 gives results consistent with Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a systematic expansion in the spatial dimension parameter \(\epsilon = 4 - d\) for a Fermi gas at infinite scattering length (unitary regime), claiming that even when extrapolated to the physical case of three dimensions (\(\epsilon = 1\)), the series is reasonably well-behaved and yields results consistent with Monte Carlo simulations and experiments.

## Method

The authors construct a field-theoretic description of the unitary Fermi gas using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a bosonic auxiliary field, then expand around four spatial dimensions using dimensional regularization. They develop Feynman rules and an \(\epsilon\)-power counting scheme, showing that inverse powers of \(\epsilon\) from divergent integrals cancel systematically, and compute the effective potential and fermion self-energy to leading and next-to-leading order in \(\epsilon\).

## Key numbers

- Ratio of chemical potential to Fermi energy: \(\mu/\varepsilon_F = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon^{3/2} + \frac{1}{16}\epsilon^{5/2}\ln\epsilon - 0.0246\,\epsilon^{5/2} + \cdots\)
- Ratio of the gap in the fermion quasiparticle spectrum to the chemical potential: \(\Delta/\mu = 2\epsilon^{-1} - 0.691\) (to next-to-leading order)
- Minimum of the fermion dispersion curve is at momentum \(|\mathbf{p}| = (2m\varepsilon_0)^{1/2}\) where \(\varepsilon_0/\mu = 2 + O(\epsilon)\)
- Extrapolation to \(\epsilon = 1\) (three dimensions) gives \(\xi \approx 0.475\), \(\varepsilon_0/\mu \approx 2\), and \(\Delta/\mu \approx 1.31\)
- Numerical constant from two-loop integral: \(C \approx 0.14424\)

## Context

This work extends the observation by Nussinov and Nussinov (2004) that at four spatial dimensions the unitary Fermi gas becomes a noninteracting Bose gas, providing the first systematic analytical expansion for the system. The approach is analogous to the epsilon expansion used in the theory of second-order phase transitions (Wilson and Kogut, 1974). The results enable analytical treatment of problems inaccessible to Monte Carlo simulations, such as polarized Fermi gases (which suffer a fermion sign problem) and dynamical response functions, and the extrapolated values are consistent with Monte Carlo simulations by Carlson et al. (2005) and experimental measurements of \(\xi\) by Thomas (2005) and Hulet (polarized gas).
