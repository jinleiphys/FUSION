# Polarized Deep-Inelastic Scattering from Nuclei -- A Relativistic Approach

> A covariant formulation of spin-dependent deep-inelastic scattering from nuclei is presented. In the relativistic impulse approximation the inclusive nuclear cross section is described in terms of the amplitude for forward, virtual-photon scattering from an off-mass-shell nucleon. The general structure of the off-shell nucleon hadronic tensor is derived, and the leading behavior of the off-shell nucleon structure functions computed in the Bjorken limit. The formalism, which is valid for nucleons bound inside nuclei with spin 1/2 or 1, is applied to the case of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant, relativistic formulation of spin-dependent deep-inelastic scattering from nuclei that goes beyond the non-relativistic impulse approximation, showing that the usual convolution model for nuclear structure functions is not exact at the relativistic level and that off-shell effects in the nucleon structure functions become sizeable for Bjorken-x above approximately 0.8.

## Method

The authors derive the general structure of the antisymmetric part of the off-shell nucleon hadronic tensor for a spin-1/2 fermion, expanding it in terms of six independent, gauge-invariant basis tensors with scalar coefficient functions. They compute the leading-twist behavior of these off-shell structure functions in the Bjorken limit using a parton model with relativistic quark-nucleon vertex functions, then apply the formalism to the deuteron by expressing the nuclear structure function g1^D in terms of the off-shell nucleon tensor and the relativistic deuteron-nucleon scattering amplitude derived from a relativistic deuteron wave function containing S-, D-, and P-state components.

## Key numbers

- The D-state probability in the deuteron wave function used is ω_D = 4.7%, from the NN potential model of Buck and Gross (1990) with a pseudovector πNN interaction.
- The cut-off parameters for the scalar quark-nucleon vertices are Λ_0^(a) = 1.0 GeV and Λ_0^(b) = 1.1 GeV, with exponents n_0^(a) = 2.0 and n_0^(b) = 2.8, and mixing parameter β = 2.73.
- The cut-off for the pseudovector vertex is Λ_1 = 1.8 GeV with exponent n_1 = 3.2, and the spectator diquark masses are m_0 = 0.9 GeV (scalar) and m_1 = 1.6 GeV (pseudovector).
- The first moments of the polarized valence quark distributions in the proton are ∫Δu_V(x) dx = 0.99 and ∫Δd_V(x) dx = -0.27, saturating the Bjorken sum rule.
- The total momentum carried by valence quarks at the renormalization scale μ^2 ≈ (0.32 GeV)^2 is approximately 85%, leaving about 15% for the sea.
- The normalization constants for the quark-nucleon vertex functions in the deuteron are 0.8% and 1.7% smaller than for the free nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of off-shell nucleon structure in unpolarized deep-inelastic scattering from nuclei, previously developed by Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1994) and applied to the deuteron by Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1994), to the spin-dependent case. The formalism enables a model-independent proof of gauge invariance for the off-shell spin-dependent hadronic tensor and provides a framework for extracting neutron spin structure from deuteron data, which is essential for testing fundamental QCD sum rules such as the Bjorken sum rule. The results contradict the assumption that the convolution model is exact at the relativistic level, showing that convolution-breaking corrections arise from off-shell effects and relativistic P-state contributions.
