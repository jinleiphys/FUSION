# Nuclear astrophysics and neutrinos

> In this report, centered on the activities within the MURST-PRIN project "Fisica teorica del nucleo e dei sistemi a più corpi" we discuss recent advances on the following items: i)neutrinos as probes of the solar interior and of other astrophysical objects; ii)neutrinos as probes of physics beyond the Standard Model of electroweak interactions; iii)the role of nuclear physics in i) and ii); iv) who is doing what within the italian network; v)future projects.

## Key claim

This report reviews the status of neutrino physics as of late 1998, arguing that solar and atmospheric neutrino experiments provide strong evidence for physics beyond the Standard Model of electroweak interactions, but that the interpretation of these results depends critically on nuclear physics inputs that remain uncertain at the factor-of-two level for some key reactions.

## Method

The paper surveys recent theoretical and experimental work within the MURST-PRIN Italian research network on four interconnected topics: neutrinos as probes of stellar interiors (particularly the Sun), neutrinos as probes of non-standard electroweak physics, the nuclear physics required for neutrino production and detection, and specific ongoing calculations of rare neutrino fluxes (hep, Be-e-p, p-e-p). The author summarizes results from multiple collaborating groups, including the Milan, Pisa, Ferrara, and Turin nodes, and discusses future experimental projects (LENS, LUNA2, Borexino, Icarus).

## Key numbers

- Superkamiokande observed solar neutrino flux: Φ(B)_obs = (2.44 ± 0.10) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- Lower limit on solar central temperature from Superkamiokande data: T ≥ 1.49 × 10⁷ K, within 5% of the Standard Solar Model estimate T_SSM = 1.57 × 10⁷ K
- pp neutrino spectrum endpoint: E_ν = 0.420 MeV; ⁷Be neutrino line energy: E_ν = 0.861 MeV
- Supernova energy release: E_SN ≈ 5 × 10⁴⁶ J; neutrino number: N_SN ≈ 3 × 10⁵⁸
- For a Supernova-II at the Galactic center, Superkamiokande would collect ~5000 events, with ~1000 in the first second
- Upper bound on solar antineutrino flux (E > 8.3 MeV) from 101.9 days of Superkamiokande data: Φ < 9 × 10⁴ cm⁻² s⁻¹ at 95% C.L.
- Standard Solar Model hep neutrino flux prediction: Φ(hep)_SSM = 2.1 × 10³ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- Superkamiokande excess could be explained by Φ(hep) ≈ 30 × Φ(hep)_SSM
- Astrophysical S-factor for hep reaction: S_13_SSM = (2.3 ± 0.9) × 10⁻²⁰ keV·b
- Neutrinoless double beta decay upper bound on Majorana neutrino mass: m ≲ 0.5 eV
- pp reaction cross section uncertainty: 1% error on S_11 translates into 0.1% error on solar temperature
- ¹²³Te K-electron capture half-life measured: K_exp = 2.4 × 10¹⁹ yr (longest single-beta half-life measured)
- Neutrino capture cross section on ⁴⁰Ar calculated: 6.7 SNU
- Solar neutrino absorption rate on ²³Na calculated: 3.5 ± 1.3 SNU

## Context

This report extends the discussion of solar neutrino physics following the Superkamiokande results (1998) and the earlier Gallex and Sage detections of pp neutrinos in the early 1990s, which provided the first observational proof that the Sun is powered by nuclear fusion (Bethe 1939, Bethe and Critchfield 1938). The paper situates the Italian network's work within the broader effort to resolve the solar neutrino puzzle through improved nuclear cross sections (particularly for the pp reaction, ³He+³He, and ³He+⁴He reactions) and to interpret atmospheric neutrino anomalies as evidence for neutrino oscillations. The work enables future measurements of the solar central temperature via ⁷Be neutrino spectroscopy (Borexino, LENS) and underground nuclear astrophysics experiments (LUNA2 at Gran Sasso), and motivates refined calculations of the hep neutrino production cross section using few-body methods.
