# Nucleon structure from lattice QCD - recent achievements and perspectives

> We present recent developments in lattice QCD simulations as applied in the study of hadron structure. We discuss the challenges and perspectives in the evaluation of benchmark quantities such as the nucleon axial charge and the isovector parton momentum fraction, as well as, in the computation of the nucleon $σ$-terms, which involve the calculation of disconnected quark loop contributions.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations at or near the physical pion mass are now converging to experimental values for benchmark nucleon observables, but dedicated high-statistics studies with larger volumes and multiple lattice spacings are still required to resolve remaining discrepancies and finalize results for quantities such as the axial charge and the isovector momentum fraction.

## Method

The author surveys lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure observables using multiple fermion discretizations (twisted mass, clover, domain wall, staggered) with \(N_f=2\), \(N_f=2+1\), and \(N_f=2+1+1\) dynamical quark flavors. Connected three-point functions are computed via sequential inversions through the sink, and disconnected quark loop contributions are evaluated using stochastic noise techniques, truncated solver methods, and multi-GPU codes. Matrix elements are extracted from plateau fits of appropriate ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions, with renormalization performed non-perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge \(g_A\): results from multiple collaborations at or near physical pion mass are shown, with the expectation that dedicated studies with high statistics, larger volumes, and simulations at 3 lattice spacings will resolve the discrepancy.
- Isovector quark momentum fraction \(\langle x \rangle_{u-d}\) in the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: results at or near physical pion mass are converging to the experimental value.
- Isovector helicity fraction \(\langle x \rangle_{\Delta u - \Delta d}\) in the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV: results at or near physical pion mass are converging to the experimental value.
- Disconnected contributions to \(g_A^{u+d}\) and \(g_A^s\): at \(m_\pi = 373\) MeV with lattice spacing \(a = 0.082\) fm, using \(\sim 150,000\) measurements, the disconnected contributions are negative and non-zero, amounting to a \(\sim 10\%\) correction to \(\Delta\Sigma^{u+d}\).
- Quark spin sum rule: \(\sum_{q=u,d,s} J^q \sim 1/4\), leaving \(\sim 50\%\) of the nucleon spin unaccounted for.
- \(y_N\) parameter: \(y_N = 0.135(22)(33)(22)(9)\), where the first error is statistical, the second from chiral extrapolation using lowest order and next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory, the third from excited state contamination, and the fourth from cut-off effects.
- Strange sigma term bound: using the \(y_N\) value and phenomenological constraints on \(\sigma_{\pi N}\), \(\sigma_s \lesssim 250\) MeV.

## Context

This paper reviews lattice QCD progress in nucleon structure, extending the milestone hadron spectrum results of the BMW collaboration (Durr 2008) and the ETM collaboration to benchmark observables like \(g_A\) and the isovector momentum fraction. It addresses the challenge of disconnected quark loop contributions, which were previously a major unknown systematic, and demonstrates their computation using techniques developed by the author's group (Alexandrou 2012, 2013, 2014). The work enables future reliable predictions for hyperon and charmed baryon observables and provides input for dark matter searches by constraining the nucleon sigma terms.
