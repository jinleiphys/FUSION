# Shielding effects in fusion reactions of proton-halo nucleus

> To explain the experimental facts that the fusion cross sections of proton-halo nucleus on heavy target nucleus is not enhanced as expected, the shielding supposition has been proposed. Namely, the proton-halo nucleus is polarized with the valence proton being shielded by the core. In this paper, within the frame of the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, the fusion reactions by $^{17}$F on $^{208}$Pb around Coulomb barrier have been simulated. The existence of shielding effect is verified by microscopic dynamics analysis and its influence on the effective interaction potential is also investigated.

## Key claim
The paper verifies the existence of the "shielding effect" in proton-halo nucleus fusion, where the valence proton of the excited halo nucleus $^{17}$F is repelled from the target and shielded by the $^{16}$O core, thereby reducing the effective interaction radius and explaining why fusion cross sections are not enhanced as expected despite the larger halo radius.

## Method
The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model to simulate fusion reactions of $^{17}$F on $^{208}$Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier. They prepared the proton-halo nucleus $^{17}$F in its first excited state by treating it as a $^{16}$O core plus a valence proton, with special sampling for the halo structure and a "sphere-shell constrain" to maintain stability. The shielding effect was studied by tracing the relative position of the valence proton to the core during the reaction, and the dynamic effective interaction potential was calculated for different configurations of the valence proton.

## Key numbers
- The root-mean-square (rms) radius of $^{17}$F in the first excited state is $2.71 \pm 0.18$ fm, and the rms radius of the valence proton is 5.33 fm.
- The rms radius of the neutron in $^{17}$F (first excited state) is 2.478 fm, indicating the $^{16}$O core is smaller than ordinary $^{16}$O (rms radius of matter 2.710 fm).
- The initial distance between the valence proton and the center of mass of the core is set to $R = R_{\rm p} + 1.6$ fm, where $R_{\rm p}$ is the proton distribution radius of the $^{16}$O core.
- The wave-packet width for the valence proton is set to $\sqrt{2}$ fm, and its initial momentum is 25 MeV/c.
- The "sphere-shell constrain" is switched off at 150 fm/c during the reaction simulation.
- Fusion excitation functions were calculated for $^{17}$F+$^{208}$Pb and $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb; the $^{16}$O results were shifted in energy by a factor of 9/8 (the ratio of charges of F and O).
- The dynamic effective interaction potential was studied at $E_{\rm c.m.} = 85$ MeV for $^{17}$F+$^{208}$Pb and at $E_{\rm c.m.} = 76$ MeV for $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb, both at impact parameter $b = 0$ fm.

## Context
This work addresses the experimental puzzle from Rehm (1998) and Liang (2000), where fusion cross sections for the proton-halo nucleus $^{17}$F on $^{208}$Pb showed no enhancement compared to $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb, contrary to expectations from the larger halo radius. The shielding effect was previously proposed as a hypothesis, and this paper provides microscopic dynamical verification using the ImQMD model. The results enable future studies of fusion dynamics with weakly bound nuclei and suggest that similar shielding mechanisms may need to be considered in super-heavy element synthesis using halo-nucleus projectiles.
