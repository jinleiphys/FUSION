# Quark and pion condensates at finite isospin density in chiral perturbation theory

> In this paper, we consider two-flavor QCD at zero temperature and finite isospin chemical potential ($μ_I$) using a model-independent analysis within chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. We calculate the effective potential, the chiral condensate and the pion condensate in the pion-condensed phase at both zero and nonzero pionic source. We compare our finite pionic source results for the chiral condensate and the pion condensate with recent (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results and find that they are in excellent agreement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory calculations of the chiral condensate and pion condensate at finite isospin chemical potential, including a nonzero pionic source, show excellent agreement with recent (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results, with agreement generally improving from leading order to NLO.

## Method

The authors calculate the effective potential for two-flavor QCD at zero temperature and finite isospin chemical potential using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, including a pionic source term. They then compute the chiral condensate and pion condensate by taking derivatives of the effective potential with respect to the quark mass and pionic source, respectively, and determine the ground state by extremizing the effective potential with respect to the rotation angle α.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin chemical potential for the second-order transition from the vacuum phase to the pion-condensed phase is μ_I^c = m_π, where m_π is the physical pion mass.
- At tree level, the chiral condensate and pion condensate satisfy ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_{μ_I} = ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_0 cos α and ⟨π⁺⟩_{μ_I} = ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_0 sin α, where ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_0 = -f²B₀ is the tree-level vacuum quark condensate.
- The chiral condensate deviation Σ_{ψ̄ψ} is defined as -2m/(m_π²f_π²)[⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_{μ_I} - ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩₀^{j=0}] + 1, ensuring it equals 1 at μ_I=0 and j=0.
- The pion condensate deviation Σ_π is defined as -2m/(m_π²f_π²)⟨π⁺⟩_{μ_I}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO chiral perturbation theory calculations of thermodynamic properties in the pion-condensed phase (Adhikari and Andersen, 2020) and provides model-independent predictions that can be compared with lattice QCD simulations of finite isospin QCD (Lattice Collaboration, 2020). The results enable systematic comparison between chiral perturbation theory and lattice QCD for pion condensation, and the formalism can be extended to study the effects of external magnetic fields on the pion-condensed phase, where spatially inhomogeneous structures such as vortex lattices are expected.
