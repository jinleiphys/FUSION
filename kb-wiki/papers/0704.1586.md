# Core excitation in the elastic scattering and breakup of $^{11}$Be on protons

> The elastic scattering and breakup of $^{11}$Be from a proton target at intermediate energies is studied. We explore the role of core excitation in the reaction mechanism. Comparison with the data suggests that there is still missing physics in the description.

# Core excitation in the elastic scattering and breakup of $^{11}$Be on protons

## Key claim
Including core excitation and continuum coupling in the reaction model improves but does not fully resolve the discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental data for $^{11}$Be elastic scattering and breakup on protons at intermediate energies; the authors conclude that "there is still missing physics in the description."

## Method
The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) and eXtended Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (XCDCC) calculations for $^{11}$Be scattering on a proton target at approximately 40 and 60 MeV/nucleon. The $^{10}$Be-proton optical potential was fitted to elastic scattering data for $^{10}$Be, then used without renormalization for $^{11}$Be calculations that included breakup continuum and, in XCDCC, coupling to the $2^+$ excited state of the $^{10}$Be core.

## Key numbers
- Elastic scattering data for $^{11}$Be on protons at 49.3 MeV/nucleon from GANIL (Cortina et al.) and at 63.7 MeV/nucleon from NSCL (Shrivastava et al.) were analyzed.
- Breakup data at 63.7 MeV/nucleon were integrated into two energy bins: 0.5–3.0 MeV (spanning the 1.78 MeV resonance) and 3.0–5.5 MeV (spanning a resonance at 3.89 MeV).
- For CDCC calculations at 40 MeV/nucleon, the continuum was discretized up to 35 MeV, with 10 bins up to 10 MeV for s-, p-, and d-waves and 8 bins from 10–35 MeV.
- The $^{10}$Be-proton Woods-Saxon potential parameters at 40 MeV/nucleon were V=60.84 MeV, R_V=1.000 fm, a_V=0.7 fm, W=23.16 MeV, R_W=0.600 fm, a_W=0.6 fm; at 60 MeV/nucleon: V=31.64 MeV, R_V=1.145 fm, a_V=0.69 fm, W=8.78 MeV, R_W=1.134 fm, a_W=0.69 fm.
- For the lower energy breakup bin (0.5–3.0 MeV), converged CDCC calculations agreed well with the data, contrary to earlier findings in Shrivastava et al.
- For the higher energy breakup bin (3.0–5.5 MeV), core excitation within XCDCC lowered the cross section but did not reproduce the shape of the angular distribution.

## Context
This work extends the XCDCC method previously applied to breakup of $^{11}$Be on $^9$Be at approximately 60 MeV/nucleon (Summers and Nunes, 2005; Summers et al., 2006) to the case of a proton target, where nuclear-driven processes and recoil effects are important. It re-examines elastic scattering data from Cortina et al. (2004) and Shrivastava et al. (2004) that previously required large renormalizations of the optical potential, and breakup data from Shrivastava et al. (2004) that standard CDCC failed to describe. The results enable future work on including deuteron transfer channels and Faddeev-type treatments (Fonseca et al.) to address the remaining theory-experiment mismatch.
