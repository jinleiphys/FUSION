# Strong correlations of neutron star radii with the slopes of nuclear matter incompressibility and symmetry energy at saturation

> We examine the correlations of neutron star radii with the nuclear matter incompressibility, symmetry energy, and their slopes, which are the key parameters of the equation of state (EoS) of asymmetric nuclear matter. The neutron star radii and the EoS parameters are evaluated using a representative set of 24 Skyrme-type effective forces and 18 relativistic mean field models, and two microscopic calculations, all describing 2$M_\odot$ neutron stars. Unified EoSs for the inner-crust-core region have been built for all the phenomenological models, both relativistic and non-relativistic. Our investigation shows the existence of a strong correlation of the neutron star radii with the linear combination of the slopes of the nuclear matter incompressibility and the symmetry energy coefficients at the saturation density. Such correlations are found to be almost independent of the neutron star mass in the range $0.6\text{-}1.8M_{\odot}$. This correlation can be linked to the empirical relation existing between the star radius and the pressure at a nucleonic density between one and two times saturation density, and the dependence of the pressure on the nuclear matter incompressibility, its slope and the symmetry energy slope. The slopes of the nuclear matter incompressibility and the symmetry energy coefficients as estimated from the finite nuclei data yield the radius of a $1.4M_{\odot}$ neutron star in the range $11.09\text{-}12.86$ km.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutron star radii across a wide mass range (0.6–1.8 \(M_\odot\)) are strongly correlated with a linear combination of the slope of nuclear matter incompressibility (\(M_0\)) and the slope of the symmetry energy (\(L_0\)), rather than with either parameter individually; this correlation is almost independent of neutron star mass, but the specific coefficients \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) in the linear combinations \(K_0 + \alpha L_0\) and \(M_0 + \beta L_0\) vary with mass.

## Method

The authors built unified equations of state (EoSs) for the inner-crust-core region using 24 Skyrme-type effective forces, 18 relativistic mean field (RMF) models, and two microscopic calculations (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with Argonne \(V_{18}\) plus Urbana three-body force, and the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall variational EoS), all capable of describing 2 \(M_\odot\) neutron stars. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static neutron stars and computed Pearson correlation coefficients between the resulting radii (for masses 0.6–1.8 \(M_\odot\)) and the EoS parameters \(K_0\), \(Q_0\), \(M_0\), \(J_0\), \(L_0\), \(K_{sym,0}\), and \(K_{\tau,0}\) at saturation density, as well as with optimized linear combinations \(K_0 + \alpha L_0\) and \(M_0 + \beta L_0\).

## Key numbers

* The spread in radius of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star across all 44 EoSs is approximately 3.1 km.
* The correlation coefficient between \(R_{1.0}\) and \(L_0\) is 0.850; between \(R_{1.4}\) and \(L_0\) it is 0.745; between \(R_{1.8}\) and \(L_0\) it is 0.590.
* The correlation coefficient between \(R_{1.4}\) and \(K_0\) is 0.704; between \(R_{1.4}\) and \(M_0\) it is 0.743.
* For the linear combination \(M_0 + \beta L_0\), the correlation coefficient with \(R_{1.4}\) is 0.924 (with \(\beta = 17.089\)); with \(R_{0.6}\) it is 0.936 (with \(\beta = 43.115\)); with \(R_{1.8}\) it is 0.903 (with \(\beta = 8.970\)).
* For the linear combination \(K_0 + \alpha L_0\), the correlation coefficient with \(R_{1.4}\) is 0.850 (with \(\alpha = 0.883\)); with \(R_{0.6}\) it is 0.905 (with \(\alpha = 2.970\)); with \(R_{1.8}\) it is 0.782 (with \(\alpha = 0.432\)).
* Using constraints on \(M_0\) (1800–2400 MeV) and \(L_0\) (40–80 MeV) from finite nuclei data, the radius of a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star is predicted to lie in the range 11.09–12.86 km.
* If only RMF models are considered, the predicted \(R_{1.4}\) range becomes 11.82–13.25 km.

## Context

This work extends earlier correlation studies by Fortin (2016) and Fattoyev (2012, 2014), which examined neutron star radius correlations with individual EoS parameters like \(K_0\) and \(L_0\) but found weaker or mass-dependent correlations. The key advance is showing that the linear combination \(M_0 + \beta L_0\) yields a strong, nearly mass-independent correlation, analogous to the correlation between crust-core transition pressure and a linear combination of symmetry energy slope and curvature found by Ducoin (2011). The results enable tighter constraints on neutron star radii from nuclear physics experiments that determine \(M_0\) and \(L_0\), and can be linked to the empirical \(R \propto P^{1/4}\) relation identified by Lattimer (2001, 2007).
