# eta nucleus optical potential in a chiral unitary approach

> The self-energy of an eta in a nuclear medium is calculated in a chiral unitary model, and applied to eta states in nuclei. Our calculation predicts an attractive eta nucleus optical potential which can accommodate many eta bound states in different nuclei.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary model predicts an attractive η-nucleus optical potential that can accommodate many η bound states in nuclei, but the half-widths of these bound states are comparable to or larger than the level spacing, making experimental observation difficult.

## Method

The η self-energy in nuclear matter was calculated by summing in-medium ηn scattering amplitudes over nucleons in the Fermi sea, using a chiral unitary approach that solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation with coupled channels including {π⁻p, π⁰n, ηn, K⁰Λ, K⁺Σ⁻, K⁰Σ⁰, π⁰π⁻p, π⁺π⁻n}. The in-medium loop functions incorporate Pauli blocking, hyperon binding energy shifts, and hadron dressing effects (including pion self-energy from N-hole, Δ-hole, and Roper-hole excitations), with self-consistency achieved by iterating the η self-energy until convergence.

## Key numbers

- ηn scattering length: a_ηn = 0.264 + i 0.245 fm
- N*(1535) width from the model: 93 MeV, compared to experimental value 95 ± 15 MeV from Beijing Electron-Positron Collider (Bai)
- η-nucleus optical potential at normal nuclear matter density: U_η(ρ₀) = -54 - i 29 MeV
- Comparison value from Wass (reference): -20 - i 22 MeV
- Kaon self-energy used: Π_K(ω, q; ρ) = 0.13 m_K² ρ/ρ₀
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV; f_K = 1.22 f_π; f_η = 1.3 f_π
- Hyperon binding energy shift: δB(ρ) = +40 ρ/ρ₀ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to πN scattering (Inoue) to calculate the η self-energy in nuclear matter, finding a more attractive potential than earlier estimates by Wass. The results were applied by Garcia-Recio (2002) to solve the Klein-Gordon equation for η bound states in finite nuclei, showing that many bound states exist across different nuclei but with widths comparable to level spacings, with the optimal region for observation around ²⁴Mg.
