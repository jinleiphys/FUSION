# Properties of isospin asymmetric matter derived from chiral effective field theory

> We present and discuss properties of isospin asymmetric matter whose equation of state is derived from recent high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials and chiral effective three-nucleon forces. After a brief review of the chiral few-nucleon forces which we adopt, we concentrate on the symmetry energy and its density derivatives. We also explore the correlation between the symmetry energy at saturation density and its slope parameter, L. We estimate the truncation error across three orders of the chiral expansion for both the symmetry energy as a function of density and the slope parameter. Through an energy-density functional inspired by the liquid drop model, we establish a simple connection to finite nuclei. Specifically, we address the symmetry energy coefficient, the so-called reference (or equivalent) density, as well as the neutron skin thickness for 208Pb and 48Ca.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy and its slope parameter L derived from chiral effective field theory show a strong linear correlation (correlation coefficient 0.945), but the actual numerical predictions carry significant truncation uncertainties that depend on the chiral order and cutoff choice, with the range of L across all chiral orders stated as 58.1 ± 16.8 MeV and the symmetry energy at saturation within 33.4 ± 6.1 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order (N4LO) from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) combined with chiral effective three-nucleon forces, applying them in a nonperturbative particle-particle ladder approximation corresponding to the leading-order contributions in the hole-line expansion. The three-nucleon forces were incorporated as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions, with the 2PE 3NF contributions included effectively up to fifth order through parametrization of the LECs. The connection to finite nuclei was established through an energy-density functional inspired by the liquid drop model using a two-parameter Thomas-Fermi distribution for nucleon densities.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation at N4LO with 450 MeV cutoff: 37.8 ± 1.5 MeV
- Slope parameter L at N4LO with 450 MeV cutoff: 68.5 ± 3.0 MeV
- Range of L across all chiral orders: 58.1 ± 16.8 MeV
- Range of symmetry energy at saturation across all orders: 33.4 ± 6.1 MeV
- Correlation coefficient between symmetry energy at saturation and L: 0.945
- Final N3LO estimate for symmetry energy: 34.3 ± 3.6 (3.9) MeV
- Final N3LO estimate for L: 63.9 ± 6.4 (6.9) MeV
- Final N3LO estimate for isovector incompressibility K_sym: -53.7 ± 11.9 (17.9) MeV
- Neutron skin of 208Pb at N4LO with 450 MeV cutoff: 0.150 ± 0.004 fm
- Neutron skin of 208Pb at N4LO with 500 MeV cutoff: 0.156 ± 0.006 fm
- Neutron skin of 48Ca at N4LO with 450 MeV cutoff: 0.138 ± 0.002 fm
- Neutron skin of 48Ca at N4LO with 500 MeV cutoff: 0.142 ± 0.002 fm
- Final N3LO estimate for neutron skin of 208Pb: 0.144 ± 0.009 (0.011) fm
- Final N3LO estimate for neutron skin of 48Ca: 0.140 ± 0.006 fm
- Reference density range for 208Pb across all orders and cutoffs: 0.086 fm^-3 to 0.108 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy coefficient range for 208Pb: 19.1 MeV to 25.9 MeV
- Correlation coefficient between L and neutron skin of 208Pb: 0.988

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculations of symmetric and neutron matter from Sammarruca et al. (2018) based on the high-quality chiral potentials of Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) to isovector properties of asymmetric nuclear matter. The results connect to ongoing experimental efforts such as PREX II and CREX, which aim to measure neutron radii of 208Pb and 48Ca with unprecedented accuracy, and complement phenomenological studies using Skyrme density functionals such as that of Alam et al. (2014) which reported K_sym = -22.9 ± 73.2 MeV and L = 65.4 ± 13.5 MeV. The predictions enable constraints on the symmetry energy and its density dependence that are relevant for neutron star structure calculations, with the same chiral interactions having been applied to predict the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star in Sammarruca and Millerson (2019).
