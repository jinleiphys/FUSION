# Phase structure of quark matter and in-medium properties of mesons from Callan-Symanzik flows

> We compute meson spectral functions at finite temperature and density in the quark-meson model, supplemented with a computation of the phase diagram. In particular, we provide a detailed analysis of the non-analytic structure of the meson two-point functions which is of great relevance for phenomenological applications, such as moat regimes and inhomogeneous phases. Furthermore, it is also relevant from a field-theoretical standpoint as it provides an insight into the applicability of derivative expansions of the effective action to studies of general fermion-boson models, both at zero and finite chemical potential. Our computation is based on a functional renormalization group setup that preserves causality, all spacetime symmetries, and the Silver-Blaze property. The combination of these properties can only be achieved by a Callan-Symanzik regulator. Instead of momentum shell integrations, renormalization group flows generated by such a regulator describe the change of the theory induced by a change of the masses of the mesons and quarks. A particular focus of our work lies on the construction of controlled Callan-Symanzik flows in the presence of spontaneous and explicit chiral symmetry breaking by means of chiral Ward-Takahashi identities.

## Key claim

The authors compute meson spectral functions and the phase diagram of the quark-meson model using a Callan-Symanzik regulator within the functional renormalization group, but the results are obtained in the large-Nc limit where only fermionic loops are included and bosonic fluctuations are neglected, meaning the framework is presented as a benchmark for future studies that would include full bosonic fluctuations.

## Method

The authors employ a functional renormalization group setup with a Callan-Symanzik regulator that preserves causality, spacetime symmetries, and the Silver-Blaze property, where the RG flow describes changes induced by varying the masses of mesons and quarks rather than momentum-shell integration. They compute the effective potential and mesonic two-point functions in the large-Nc limit of the quark-meson model, using chiral Ward-Takahashi identities to control the flow of explicit chiral symmetry breaking induced by the fermionic CS regulator. The loop integrals are regularized with counterterms that respect the symmetries of the model, and the initial condition at the reference scale is tuned to reproduce physical vacuum observables.

## Key numbers

* The fermionic CS regulator is parametrized as R^psi_k(p) = Z^psi_k * i k, with the bosonic regulator R^phi_k(p) = Z^phi_k * k^2, where k denotes the RG scale.
* The effective potential in the large-Nc limit is given by U_k(phi) = U_{Lambda_0}(phi) - 8 N_c L_k(Lambda_0, phi), where N_c = 3 is the number of colors.
* The loop function L_k contains a sum over Matsubara frequencies with terms of the form sqrt(p^2 + h^2 pi^2 + (h sigma + k')^2) evaluated between k' = k and k' = Lambda_0.
* The fermionic cutoff can be identified with the light current quark mass m_l, with the flow stopped at k_{psi,min} = m_l.

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group framework developed in Braun (2022) for Callan-Symanzik regulators, which preserve the Silver-Blaze property and spacetime symmetries, to include chiral Ward-Takahashi identities for controlling explicit chiral symmetry breaking. The results enable future studies of moat regimes and inhomogeneous phases in QCD at finite density, as discussed in Rennecke (2021) and Rennecke (2023), and provide a benchmark for extending the derivative expansion of the effective action to general fermion-boson models. The large-Nc limit is chosen to permit analytic understanding and serves as a test case for the CS framework before including full bosonic fluctuations.
