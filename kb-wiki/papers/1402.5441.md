# ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and $pd$ Scattering to Next-to-Leading Order in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> We study the three-body systems of ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and $pd$ scattering and demonstrate, both analytically and numerically, that a new $pd$ three-body force is needed at next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory. We also show that at leading order these observables require no new three-body force beyond what is necessary to describe $nd$ scattering. We include electromagnetic effects by iterating only diagrams that involve a single photon exchange in the three-body sector.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at next-to-leading order (NLO) in pionless effective field theory, a new three-body force depending on the fine-structure constant α is required to obtain cutoff-independent results for pd scattering and the ³He binding energy, whereas at leading order no such new force is needed beyond what is necessary for nd scattering.

## Method

The authors solved coupled integral equations for doublet S-wave pd scattering in pionless effective field theory, including Coulomb effects by iterating only diagrams involving single photon exchange in the three-body sector. They performed both strictly perturbative and partial resummation calculations of the NLO scattering amplitude, fitting the new α-dependent three-body force to the ³He binding energy to demonstrate cutoff independence of the NLO phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding momentum γt = 45.7025 MeV, with effective range ρt = 1.765 fm
- The spin-singlet np scattering length as = -23.714 fm, with effective range ρs = 2.73 fm
- The pp scattering length aC = -7.8063 fm, with effective range rC = 2.794 fm
- The a priori uncertainty estimate for a NLO calculation is O((γtρt)²), or roughly 17%
- Without the new three-body force, significant cutoff variation appears for cutoffs much larger than 600 MeV in the NLO pd phase shifts and NLO corrections to the ³He binding energy

## Context

This work extends the previous calculations of Rupak and Kong (2001) and König and Hammer (2011) for pd scattering in pionless effective field theory. Rupak and Kong treated Coulomb corrections perturbatively and developed a power counting scheme supplementing the usual Q counting with an external momentum scale p, but encountered numerical problems at center-of-mass momenta below 20 MeV. König and Hammer extended this to NNLO using an optimized integration mesh, obtaining results down to about 3 MeV, but assumed that up to NLO the three-body forces from nd scattering were sufficient for pd scattering. The present paper shows this assumption fails at NLO, requiring a new α-dependent three-body force, and provides both analytical and numerical demonstration of this necessity. This enables systematic high-precision EFT calculations of three-nucleon systems including Coulomb effects, relevant for addressing long-standing puzzles such as the Ay puzzle.
