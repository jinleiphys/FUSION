# New Physics Searches via Beam Normal Spin Asymmetry in Bhabha Scattering

> We examine the sensitivity of the beam normal spin asymmetry in Bhabha scattering to beyond the Standard Model (BSM) mediators, in the context of the JLab polarized positron program. A key property of this observable is that the Standard Model contribution exhibits a zero crossing at a fixed scattering angle, providing a clean, effectively background-free point for these searches. We consider scalar, vector, and axial vector mediators and present projected bounds, finding that scalar and vector scenarios allow a significant extension of the search ranges beyond existing constraints.

# New Physics Searches via Beam Normal Spin Asymmetry in Bhabha Scattering

## Key claim

The beam normal spin asymmetry in Bhabha scattering at JLab can probe scalar, vector, and axial vector mediators with up to an order of magnitude extension in reach beyond existing constraints, but only if the Standard Model zero-crossing point at a fixed scattering angle provides a nearly background-free kinematic window and the experimental precision reaches approximately 1 ppm.

## Method

The authors calculated the beam normal spin asymmetry $B_n$ for Bhabha scattering $e^+ e^- \to e^+ e^-$ by projecting the scattering amplitude onto scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial vector, and tensor covariants. They computed the one-loop QED contribution to $B_n$ and the tree-level BSM contribution from $s$-channel mediator exchange, using the narrow-width approximation for the mediator propagator. The asymmetry numerator and denominator were integrated over a bin in the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass corresponding to a positron beam energy bin width $\delta E_+ = 0.5$ MeV, and projected bounds were derived by requiring the BSM contribution to remain within the anticipated experimental uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The JLab 11 GeV positron beam yields a maximum center-of-momentum energy $\sqrt{s} \simeq 106$ MeV for Bhabha scattering.
- The QED contribution to $B_n$ exhibits a zero crossing at $\theta \approx 120.4^\circ$, independent of $\sqrt{s}$ in the ultra-relativistic regime.
- The projected experimental precision for $B_n$ is estimated at $\sim 1$ ppm.
- The bin width adopted for the positron beam energy is $\delta E_+ = 0.5$ MeV.
- The BSM-induced asymmetry $\bar{B}_n^{\text{BSM}}$ grows proportionally to $\sqrt{s}$, while the QED contribution decreases as $1/\sqrt{s}$.
- For illustration, coupling strengths $g = 2 \times 10^{-4}$ (scalar) and $\epsilon = 2 \times 10^{-4}$ (vector/axial vector) produce BSM signals sizable compared to ppm-level precision near the zero-crossing.
- Up to an order of magnitude extension in reach is projected for scalar and vector mediators with masses around 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for light dark sector particles in the MeV to GeV mass range, a program pursued by experiments such as PADME, MAGIX@MESA, and the JLab polarized positron program. It builds on existing constraints from unpolarized Bhabha scattering (Bourilkov, 1999; L3, 2000) and from visible decay searches by A1@MAMI (Merkel, 2014), BaBar (2014), NA48/2 (2015), and NA64 (2019). The projected bounds are compared with limits from $(g-2)_e$ measurements (Fan, 2022; Pustyntsev, 2025), Belle II projections (Ferber, 2015), LHCb Run 3 projections (Ilten, 2015), Mu3e projections (Di Luzio, 2025), SINDRUM (1989), and PIONEER. The method can be extended to tensor-coupled interactions and double-polarization observables in future studies.
