# $β$-Delayed Neutron and Fission Calculations with Relativistic QRPA and Statistical Model

> A role of β-delayed neutron emission and fission in r-process nucleosynthesis attracts a high interest. Although the number of study on them covering r-process nuclei is increasing recently, uncertainties of β-delayed neutron and fission are still large for r-process simulations. Our purpose is to make a new database on β-delayed neutron emission and fission rates. To this end, the data that are not investigated experimentally have to be predicted. Microscopic theoretical approaches based on a nuclear energy density functional and statistical models are one of the competent tools for the prediction. To obtain βstrength function, p-n relativistic QRPA is adopted. Particle evaporations and fission from nuclear highly excited states are estimated by the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model. β-delayed neutron branching ratios (P_n) are calculated and compared with experimental data. β-delayed fission branching ratio (P_f) are also assessed by using four different fission barrier data. Calculated P_n values are in a good agreement with experimental data. It is found that energy withdrawal by β-delayed neutron emission sensitively varies P_n values for nuclei near the neutron drip line. P_f are sensitively dependent on fission barrier data. Newly calculated data on β-delayed neutron emission and fission are summarized as a table in supplement material. They are provided for studies of r-process as well as other fields such as nuclear engineering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) with the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model (HFM) yields beta-delayed neutron branching ratios (P_n) in good agreement with experimental data, but this agreement depends critically on the choice of a width parameter (Gamma=0.6 MeV for Lorentzian broadening) and on resetting negative-energy resonances to zero for nuclei near the neutron drip line.

## Method

The authors calculated beta strength functions for Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions using the pn-RQRPA based on covariant density functional theory with the D3C* interaction, then fed these strength functions into the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model implemented in the CCONE code to compute particle evaporation and fission probabilities. The beta strength functions were broadened using Lorentzian weight functions with a width parameter Gamma=0.6 MeV, determined by minimizing the root-mean-square deviation of calculated P_1n values against experimental data. For beta-delayed fission, four different fission barrier datasets (HFB-14, FRDM+QRPA, ETFSI, and SBM) were used to assess sensitivity.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation sigma_rms^1n = 0.601 for pn-RQRPA+HFM (all 267 nuclei), compared to 0.798 for pn-RQRPA alone, 0.595 for Gross Theory (GT2), and 0.512 for FRDM+QRPA+HFM.
* Optimal width parameter Gamma = 0.6 MeV for Lorentzian type weight function (sigma_rms^1n = 0.602) versus Gamma = 1.2 MeV for Gaussian type (sigma_rms^1n = 0.694).
* For P_1n < 1%, sigma_rms^1n = 0.952 for pn-RQRPA+HFM versus 1.084 for FRDM+QRPA+HFM (50 nuclei).
* For 1% <= P_1n < 10%, sigma_rms^1n = 0.446 for pn-RQRPA+HFM versus 0.442 for FRDM+QRPA+HFM (92 nuclei).
* For 10% <= P_1n < 50%, sigma_rms^1n = 0.570 for pn-RQRPA+HFM versus 0.482 for FRDM+QRPA+HFM (91 nuclei).
* For 50% <= P_1n <= 100%, sigma_rms^1n = 0.317 for pn-RQRPA+HFM versus 0.281 for FRDM+QRPA+HFM (34 nuclei).
* P_1n of 150Pd: approximately 40% for pn-RQRPA+HFM versus 1.6% for pn-RQRPA (cutoff method).
* One-neutron emission energy window of 220Ir: about 8.04 MeV; neighboring 219Os: about 7.40 MeV; 221Pt: about 5.65 MeV.
* S_1n of 240Ir: 269 keV for the global nuclear mass model versus 690 keV for FRDM1995.

## Context

This work extends the earlier pn-RQRPA calculations by Minato, Marketin, and Paar (2016) that used a simplified cutoff method assuming all states above the neutron threshold emit neutrons, by instead incorporating the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model to treat neutron-gamma competition and fission explicitly. The results are comparable to those from the FRDM+QRPA+HFM approach by Moller, Mumpower, and Kawano (2019) and the Gross Theory (GT2) by Yoshida and Tachibana (2000), but with the advantage of a fully self-consistent covariant density functional framework. The new database of beta-delayed neutron and fission branching ratios is provided as supplemental material for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations and nuclear engineering applications, while the authors note that further study of level structures and fission barriers of neutron-rich nuclei is needed for future improvements.
