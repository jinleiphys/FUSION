# Dibaryons as canonically quantized biskyrmions

> The characteristic feature of the ground state configuration of the Skyrme model description of nuclei is the absence of recognizable individual nucleons. The ground state of the skyrmion with baryon number 2 is axially symmetric, and is well approximated by a simple rational map, which represents a direct generalization of Skyrme's hedgehog ansatz for the nucleon. If the Lagrangian density is canonically quantized this configuration may support excitations that lie close and possible below the threshold for pion decay, and therefore describe dibaryons. The quantum corrections stabilize these solutions, the mass density of which have the correct exponential fall off at large distances.

# Dibaryons as canonically quantized biskyrmions

## Key claim
The canonically quantized rational map ansatz for the B=2 skyrmion can support dibaryon excitations with I=J that lie below the threshold for pion decay, but the calculation does not yield states that are stable against decay to two nucleons within the margin of error of the rational map approximation. The authors interpret the marginally bound I=J=0 state in the fundamental representation (5 MeV below two-nucleon threshold) as an accidental consequence of the approximate ansatz rather than a genuine bound state.

## Method
The authors generalize the rational map ansatz for the B=2 skyrmion to arbitrary irreducible representations of SU(2), then canonically quantize the system using collective rotational coordinates. They solve the resulting integrodifferential equation for the chiral angle numerically, obtaining the mass spectrum for different representations (j=1/2, 1, 3/2) and spin-isospin states (I=J=0,1). The parameters f_pi and e are fixed by fitting the empirical nucleon mass and isoscalar radius from prior work (Acus 1998).

## Key numbers
- Classical biskyrmion mass (j=1/2): 1918.5 MeV, with Delta M = -36.9 MeV relative to two nucleons
- Canonically quantized I=J=0 state (j=1/2): mass 1873.0 MeV, Delta M = -5.0 MeV, effective pion mass m = 62.9 MeV
- Classical biskyrmion mass (j=1): 2017.0 MeV, Delta M = -38.8 MeV
- Canonically quantized I=J=0 state (j=1): mass 1949.9 MeV, Delta M = 71.9 MeV, effective pion mass m = 88.1 MeV
- Canonically quantized I=J=1, m_t=0 state (j=1): mass 1998.5 MeV, Delta M = 120.5 MeV, effective pion mass m = 53.7 MeV
- Canonically quantized I=J=1, m_t=+/-1 states (j=1): mass 2012.0 MeV, Delta M = 134.0 MeV, effective pion mass m = 45.9 MeV
- Classical biskyrmion mass (j=3/2): 2138.9 MeV, Delta M = -41.1 MeV
- Canonically quantized I=J=0 state (j=3/2): mass 2049.7 MeV, Delta M = 171.7 MeV, effective pion mass m = 100.6 MeV
- Asymptotic fall-off exponent for the classical B=2 chiral angle: -2.56 (compared to -2 for B=1)

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model treatment of the B=2 system beyond the deuteron channel studied by Schroers, using the rational map approximation of Houghton for the axially symmetric ground state. The canonical quantization method follows the approach of Fujii, Acus, and collaborators, in contrast to conventional semiclassical quantization which gave deeply bound states (Basker). The results are compared to empirical indications for "supernarrow" dibaryons at 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV reported by Filkov (1999, 2000), which could be accommodated if their spins and isospins are 0, 1, 1 respectively. The calculation enables constraints on the SU(2) representation dimension if no dibaryon states are confirmed experimentally.
