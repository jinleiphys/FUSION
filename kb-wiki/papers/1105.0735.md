# η(1475) and f_1(1420) resonances in γγ* collisions and J/ψ\toγ(ρρ, γρ^0, γϕ) decays

> The available data on the $Q^2$ dependence of the $γγ*(Q^2)\to K\bar Kπ$ reaction cross section in the energy region 1.35-1.55 GeV is explained by the $η(1475)$ resonance production in contrast to their conventional interpretation with the use of the $f_1(1420)$ resonance. It resolves theoretically the contradiction between the suppression of the $η(1475)\toγγ$ decay width and the strong couplings of the $η(1475)$ to the $ρρ$, $ωω$, and $γρ^0$ channels. The experimental check of our explanation requires definition of the spin-parity of the resonance contributions, $R$, in $γγ*(Q^2)\to R\to K\bar Kπ$ and in $J/ψ\to\gammaR\toγγ(ρ^0, ϕ)$. This will help to solve difficulties accumulated in understanding properties of the $η(1475)$ state and its nearest partners.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed $Q^2$ dependence of the $\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)\to K\bar K\pi$ cross section in the 1.35–1.55 GeV region can be explained entirely by $\eta(1475)$ resonance production, contradicting the conventional interpretation that attributes this signal to the $f_1(1420)$ resonance. This resolution depends on the assumption that destructive interference between light and heavy vector mesons ($\rho^0$, $\omega$, $\phi$ and $\rho'^0$, $\omega'$, $\phi'$) suppresses the $\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma$ decay at $Q^2=0$ while allowing it to grow at nonzero $Q^2$.

## Method

The authors applied the vector dominance model (VDM) including contributions from both light vector mesons ($\rho^0$, $\omega$, $\phi$) and their heavier radial excitations ($\rho'^0$, $\omega'$, $\phi'$) to compute the $\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)$ transition amplitude. They fitted the resulting parametrization to existing data from TPC/2$\gamma$, CELLO, and L3 on the $Q^2$ dependence of $\widetilde{\Gamma}(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2))B(\eta(1475)\to K\bar K\pi)$, using the $\eta(1475)$ mass of 1475 MeV and total width of 85 MeV. They also estimated the $f_1(1420)\to\gamma(\rho^0,\phi)$ decay widths using the quark model and the Gell-Mann–Okubo–Sakurai mass formula for the axial-vector mixing angle.

## Key numbers

- $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\rho^0) \approx 1$ MeV (conservative estimate from BES and PDG branching ratios)
- $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma) > 1.45$ keV (lower bound from VDM using $J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma(\rho^0,\phi)$ data)
- L3 measured $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma)B(\eta(1475)\to K\bar K\pi) = 0.23 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.05$ keV (2007)
- CLEO II upper limit: $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma)B(\eta(1475)\to K\bar K\pi) < 0.089$ keV (90% C.L.)
- BES measured $B(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1440)\to\gamma\gamma\rho^0) = (1.07 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.11) \times 10^{-4}$
- BES measured $B(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1440)\to\gamma\gamma\phi) = (0.31 \pm 0.30) \times 10^{-4}$ (or $<0.82 \times 10^{-4}$ at 95% C.L.)
- Best fit with fixed $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma)B(\eta(1475)\to K\bar K\pi)=0.23$ keV gives $A=2.44$ keV$^{1/2}$, $h=-1.7$, $\chi^2=5$ for 9 degrees of freedom
- Best fit with free normalization gives $\Gamma(\eta(1475)\to\gamma\gamma)B(\eta(1475)\to K\bar K\pi)=0.3$ keV, $A=1.88$ keV$^{1/2}$, $A'=2.48$ keV$^{1/2}$, $h=-0.9$, $\chi^2=3.9$ for 8 degrees of freedom
- L3 measured $\widetilde{\Gamma}(f_1(1420)\to\gamma\gamma)B(f_1(1420)\to K\bar K\pi) = 3.2 \pm 0.6 \pm 0.7$ keV (using $F_{L3}$ form factor)
- $B(f_1(1420)\to\gamma\phi)/B(f_1(1420)\to K\bar K\pi) = 0.003 \pm 0.001 \pm 0.001$ (WA102)
- $B(f_1(1420)\to\gamma\rho^0)/B(f_1(1420)\to K\bar K\pi) < 0.02$ (WA102)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Achasov and Shestakov, 1980s) showing that pseudoscalar structures in $J/\psi\to\gamma\rho\rho$ and $J/\psi\to\gamma\omega\omega$ decays could be explained by $\eta(1440)$ decays at the resonance tail. It directly contradicts the conventional interpretation by the TPC/2$\gamma$, CELLO, MARK II, JADE, CLEO II, and L3 collaborations that attributed the $\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)\to K\bar K\pi$ signal to the $f_1(1420)$ resonance. The paper enables future experimental checks at B and C/tau factories (Belle, BaBar, CLEO II, BES III) requiring spin-parity determination of the resonance in $\gamma\gamma^*(Q^2)\to K\bar K\pi$ and in $J/\psi\to\gamma\gamma(\rho^0,\phi)$ decays to resolve the nature of the $\eta(1475)$ and its relation to the $\eta(1405)$ and $f_1(1420)$.
