# The Dirac oscillator: an alternative basis for nuclear structure calculations

> Background: The isotropic harmonic oscillator supplemented by a strong spin-orbit interaction has been the cornerstone of nuclear structure since its inception more than seven decades ago. In this paper we introduce---or rather re-introduced---the "Dirac Oscillator", a fully relativistic basis that has all the desired attributes of the ordinary harmonic oscillator while naturally incorporating a strong spin-orbit coupling. Purpose: To assess---to our knowledge for the first time---the power and flexibility of the Dirac Oscillator basis in the solution of nuclear structure problems within the framework of covariant density functional theory. Methods: Self-consistent calculations of binding energies and ground-state densities for a selected set of doubly-magic magic are performed using the Dirac-oscillator basis and are then compared against results obtained with the often-used Runge-Kutta method. Results: Results obtained using the Dirac oscillator basis reproduced with high accuracy those derived using the Runge-Kutta method and suggest a clear path for a generalization to systems with axial symmetry. Conclusions: Although the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit corrections has been the staple of the nuclear shell model since the beginning, the Dirac oscillator is practically unknown among the nuclear physics community. In this paper we illustrate the power and flexibility of the Dirac oscillator and suggest extensions to the study of systems without spherical symmetry as required in constrained calculations of nuclear excitations.

## Key claim

The Dirac oscillator basis, when used to solve covariant density functional theory (DFT) equations for doubly-magic nuclei, reproduces results from the standard Runge-Kutta method with high accuracy, while offering a natural path for generalization to axially-deformed systems where Runge-Kutta becomes less efficient.

## Method

Self-consistent calculations of binding energies and ground-state densities were performed for four doubly-magic nuclei (⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb) by expanding the Dirac Hamiltonian of covariant DFT in the Dirac oscillator basis and diagonalizing the resulting matrix. The Dirac oscillator Hamiltonian is obtained from the free Dirac Hamiltonian by the substitution p → p − imωβr, yielding a problem where both upper and lower components of the Dirac spinor satisfy a nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator equation with a strong spin-orbit term. Results were compared against those obtained using the conventional Runge-Kutta algorithm for the coupled radial Dirac equations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁰Ca: −8.539 MeV (Dirac oscillator) vs −8.538 MeV (Runge-Kutta)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁸Ca: −8.585 MeV (Dirac oscillator) vs −8.584 MeV (Runge-Kutta)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ¹³²Sn: −8.339 MeV (both methods)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ²⁰⁸Pb: −7.888 MeV (Dirac oscillator) vs −7.889 MeV (Runge-Kutta)
- Neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca: 0.1971 fm (Dirac oscillator) vs 0.1973 fm (Runge-Kutta)
- Neutron skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.2070 fm (Dirac oscillator) vs 0.2069 fm (Runge-Kutta)
- Oscillator length parameter fixed to b = 2.4 fm, with maximum node number n_max = 10 and maximum |κ| = 10 for convergence

## Context

This work extends the Dirac oscillator formalism originally introduced by Moshinsky and Szczepaniak (1989) and earlier by Ito, Mori, and Carriere (1967) into the context of covariant density functional theory, where the standard approach for spherical nuclei has been the Runge-Kutta method (Horowitz 1981, Serot 1984, Todd-Rutel 2005). The Dirac oscillator basis naturally incorporates the strong spin-orbit coupling that has been the cornerstone of the nuclear shell model since Haxel, Jensen, and Suess (1949) and Goeppert-Mayer (1949). The method enables future extension to axially-deformed nuclei and constrained calculations of nuclear excitations, where matrix diagonalization is more efficient than Runge-Kutta.
