# Direct transfer to $^{46,48}$K as a survey of the $π(s_{1/2})$$-ν(sdpf)$ interaction

> The collapse of the canonical $N=28$ magic number in nuclei with $Z<20$ has drawn significant interest as it relates to the emergence of an island of inversion centered on $^{42}$Si and $^{44}$S. In particular, interactions between the $πs_{1/2}$ orbital -- empty in $^{42}$Si and full in $^{44}$S -- and the neutron orbitals just above and below the $N=28$ gap are expected to be critical in this region, but remain relatively unexplored. In this paper, we expand upon the results of our previous study of the direct transfer reaction $^{47}$K(d,p$γ$)$^{48}$K [C.\,J.~Paxman \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. 134, 162504 (2025)] with the results of the complementary $^{47}$K(d,t$γ$)$^{46}$K reaction. Through this study, we present a comprehensive scan of the interaction between the critical $πs_{1/2}$ orbital and a broad range of neutron orbitals spanning nearly two full shells. We identify several discrepancies between the experimental results and state-of-the-art shell model calculations, which suggest a deficiency of the shell model to fully capture the complex proton configuration mixing in this region, highlighting a significant challenge for single-particle descriptions of the island of inversion.

## Key claim
The paper presents a comprehensive survey of the interaction between the proton πs₁/₂ orbital and neutron orbitals spanning two major shells (sd and pf) in the N=28 region, but identifies several discrepancies between experimental results and state-of-the-art shell model calculations that suggest the shell model fails to fully capture complex proton configuration mixing in this region.

## Method
The authors measured the direct transfer reactions ⁴⁷K(d,pγ)⁴⁸K and ⁴⁷K(d,tγ)⁴⁶K simultaneously at GANIL using a radioactive ⁴⁷K beam at 7.7 MeV/nucleon incident on a CD₂ target, with light ejectiles detected by the MUGAST silicon array, γ-rays by the AGATA array, and heavy recoils by the VAMOS++ magnetic spectrometer. Excitation energies were reconstructed from particle kinematics, and differential cross sections were extracted from angular distributions and compared to distorted-wave Born approximation calculations using TWOFNR to determine spectroscopic factors for each populated state.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy of 7.7 MeV/nucleon with average intensity of 5×10⁵ pps
- CD₂ target thickness of 0.31(2) mg/cm² (4% hydrogen to deuterium by number)
- FWHM of reconstructed excitation energy: 330 keV in ⁴⁸K and 420 keV in ⁴⁶K
- γ-ray FWHM of 7 keV at 1.8 MeV
- Systematic uncertainty of 20% applied to extracted spectroscopic factors
- Neutron separation threshold in ⁴⁶K at 6.87 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of ⁴⁷K(d,pγ)⁴⁸K (Paxman et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 134, 162504, 2025) by adding the complementary neutron pick-up reaction ⁴⁷K(d,tγ)⁴⁶K, providing the first measurement of ⁴⁶K by single-nucleon removal. The results build upon earlier studies of ⁴⁶K via ⁴⁸Ca(d,α) (Paul, 1971; Daehnick, 1974) and ⁴⁸Ca(p,³He) (Dupont, 1970, 1973; Daehnick, 1973), and enable benchmarking of shell model predictions for single-particle structure in the N=28 island of inversion centered on ⁴²Si and ⁴⁴S.
