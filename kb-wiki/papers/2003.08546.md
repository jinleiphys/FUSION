# Challenge for describing the cluster states starting with realistic interaction

> We aim to describe the cluster states of nuclear systems starting with a realistic interaction, which is a challenge of modern nuclear theories. Here, the short-range correlation of realistic interaction is treated by employing the damping factor, and the resultant interaction can be applied to the cluster structure of light nuclei. We start with a realistic interaction (G3RS) and transform it in this way, and the $α$-$α$ energy curve is compared with the results of phenomenological interactions. The attractive effect between two $α$'s is found to be not enough even with a damping factor for the short-range repulsion, and the necessity of a finite-range three-body term is discussed. With this three-body term, the resonance energy of the ground state and the scattering phase shift of two $α$'s can be reproduced. Also, the binding energy of $^{16}$O from the four $α$ threshold is reasonably reproduced. The linear-chain structure of three and four $α$ clusters in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are calculated with this interaction and compared with the results of the conventional approaches including the density functional theories.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (G3RS) with a damping factor for the short-range repulsion still produces insufficient attraction between two alpha clusters; a finite-range three-body term is necessary to reproduce the alpha-alpha energy curve, resonance energy, and scattering phase shift, even though the damping factor alone can regularize the short-range core.

## Method

The authors start with the realistic G3RS central interaction and multiply a damping factor F(r) = 1/(exp[1-r/r0])^m for r ≤ r0 to reduce the short-range repulsive core, comparing weaker (r0=0.5 fm, m=2) and stronger (r0=0.75 fm, m=3) damping. They then add a phenomenological finite-range three-body interaction with strength -9 MeV and range 0.1 fm^-2, and calculate alpha-alpha energy curves and scattering phase shifts using the Brink cluster model with (0s)^4 alpha clusters and the generator coordinate method (GCM). The interaction is applied to linear-chain configurations of three and four alpha clusters in 12C and 16O, with angular momentum projection.

## Key numbers

- The ground 0+ state of 8Be is experimentally located at 0.09184 MeV above the two-alpha threshold; with V_org, V_damp-W, and V_damp-S, the GCM ground state remains 1-2 MeV above threshold.
- The finite-range three-body interaction has strength V^(3) = -9 MeV and range μ = 0.1 fm^-2, with Majorana exchange parameter M = 0.645.
- For 16O with V_damp-W+V_f3b, the GCM ground 0+ state is obtained at -16.2 MeV from the four-alpha threshold (experimental value is -14.4 MeV), with a matter radius of 2.52 fm and charge radius of 2.65 fm (experimental 2.69 fm).
- The linear-chain band head energy for three alpha clusters in 12C is 8.9 MeV above the three-alpha threshold for V_damp-W+V_f3b, and 7.2 MeV for Tohsaki F1.
- The rotational band slope (ℏ^2/2I) for the three-alpha chain is 102 keV for V_damp-W+V_f3b and 119 keV for Tohsaki F1 (averaged between 0+ and 8+).
- For the four-alpha linear chain in 16O, the band head energy is 19.4 MeV above the four-alpha threshold (35.6 MeV from ground state) for V_damp-W+V_f3b, and 16.7 MeV for Tohsaki F1.
- The rotational band slope for the four-alpha chain between 0+ and 10+ is 0.051 MeV for V_damp-W+V_f3b and 0.063 MeV for Tohsaki F1.

## Context

This work extends previous cluster model studies that used phenomenological interactions like Volkov (1965) and Tohsaki F1 (1994) by starting from the realistic G3RS interaction (Tamagaki, 1968). It contrasts with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) used in Fermionic molecular dynamics (Neff, 2004) by treating short-range correlation through a simpler damping factor rather than unitary transformation. The results enable future application to light neutron-rich nuclei and provide a benchmark for deriving effective cluster-model interactions from chiral effective field theory, while the linear-chain results agree with density functional theory predictions (e.g., band head energies of 30-40 MeV from ground state in 16O).
