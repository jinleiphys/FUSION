# Neutrino emissivity of anisotropic neutron superfluids

> We examine the influence of the anisotropy of the superfluid energy gap and residual Fermi-liquid interactions in the triplet-correlated neutron liquid onto neutrino energy losses through neutral weak currents. The neutrino-pair emission caused by the pair breaking and formation processes and by the spin-wave decays is considered for the case of the $^{3}P_{2}$ pairing in the state with $m_{j}=0$. The simple analytical formulae are obtained. A comparison with the previous results of the average-angle approach shows that the gap anisotropy leads to quenching of the neutrino emissivity caused by the pair recombination processes on about 15% and to substantial suppression of the spin-wave decays. Residual particle-hole interactions increase the energy losses in both the channels on about 5%.

## Key claim

The anisotropy of the superfluid energy gap in triplet-correlated neutron liquid leads to quenching of neutrino emissivity from pair recombination processes by about 15% and to substantial suppression of spin-wave decays, while residual particle-hole interactions increase energy losses in both channels by about 5%, relative to the average-angle approximation.

## Method

The authors solved Dyson equations in the ladder approximation for effective three-point vertices in a superfluid neutron system with \(^{3}P_{2}\) pairing in the \(m_j=0\) state, incorporating both the anisotropic energy gap and residual Fermi-liquid interactions in the particle-hole channel. They evaluated the axial polarization tensor at zero momentum transfer and computed the neutrino emissivity from pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes and spin-wave decays (SWD) using the standard model of neutral weak currents, expanding the vertex function in spherical harmonics and truncating the Fermi-liquid parameters at \(l=0\).

## Key numbers

- Gap anisotropy quenches neutrino emissivity from PBF processes by about 15% compared to the average-angle approximation.
- Residual particle-hole interactions increase energy losses in both PBF and SWD channels by about 5%.
- The spin-wave energy \(\Omega_s\) lies below \(\bar{b}_{\min}=1/\sqrt{2}\), where \(\bar{b}_{\min}\) is the minimum of the anisotropic gap function \(\bar{b}(\mathbf{n}) = \sqrt{(1+3n_3^2)/2}\).
- The Fermi-liquid parameter \(\mathfrak{g}_0 = 0.842\) at Fermi momentum \(p_F = 1.7\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), while \(\mathfrak{g}_2/5 = 0.0438\) at the same density.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent treatment of neutrino emission from triplet-correlated neutron superfluids developed by Leinson (2010, 2011, 2012), correcting the earlier average-angle approximation used in those studies. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling within the minimal cooling paradigm of Page et al. (2004, 2009), where PBF processes from \(^{3}P_{2}\) pairing dominate at long cooling epochs. The findings also refine predictions for thermally-emitting neutron stars where spin-wave decays may contribute alongside modified Urca processes and nn-bremsstrahlung.
