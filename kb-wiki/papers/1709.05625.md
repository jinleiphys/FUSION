# Radiative Capture of Cold Neutrons by Protons and Deuteron Photodisintegration with Twisted Beams

> We consider two basic nuclear reactions: Radiative capture of neutrons by protons, $n+p\to γ+~d$ and its time-reversed counterpart, photodisintegration of the deuteron, $γ+d\to n+p$. In both of these cases we assume that the incoming beam of neutrons or photons is "twisted" by having an azimuthal phase dependence, {\it i.e.}, it carries an additional angular momentum along its direction of propagation. Taking a low-energy limit of these reactions, we derive relations between corresponding transition amplitudes and cross sections with plane-wave beams and twisted beams. Implications for experiments with twisted cold neutrons and photon beams are discussed.

# Radiative Capture of Cold Neutrons by Protons and Deuteron Photodisintegration with Twisted Beams

## Key claim

The paper derives relations between transition amplitudes and cross sections for the reactions n+p→γ+d and γ+d→n+p when the incoming neutron or photon beam carries orbital angular momentum (is "twisted"), but only in the low-energy limit where S-wave contributions dominate and the neutron spin degree of freedom is neglected; the derived selection rules for twisted photo-absorption depend on the target's position relative to the beam axis.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic quantum mechanics to describe twisted neutron states as Bessel-function wavefunctions that are eigenfunctions of energy, longitudinal momentum, and angular momentum projection Lz. For photons, they employ the vector potential formalism with Bessel-beam (BB) and Bessel-Gaussian (BG) modes, expressing twisted photon states as Fourier transforms of plane-wave states. Transition amplitudes for both reactions are computed by integrating the plane-wave matrix element over the Fourier amplitude of the twisted beam, including an impact parameter for the target's displacement from the beam axis. For the photodisintegration case, the nuclear electromagnetic current is decomposed into convection and magnetization contributions, and Wigner D-functions are used to rotate nuclear states to account for the photon's propagation direction.

## Key numbers

- Total reaction cross-section for radiative capture of cold neutrons: σ ∼ 300 mb, mainly from M1 isovector contribution dominating in S-wave capture
- Cold neutron energy range considered: E ∼ 10 meV
- Twisted cold neutrons experimentally generated at temperature 130 K with wavelength λ = 0.27 nm, carrying OAM projections m = 1, 2, 4
- Photon energy range for deuteron photodisintegration: ≥ 2.2 MeV
- Conical angle used in mesoscopic target calculations: θ_p = 1°
- Target size parameter used in cross-section ratio plots: w = 1/ϰ
- Pitch angles considered for photon beams: θ_k = 0.1 rad and 0.05 rad
- Gaussian waist for BG mode: w₀/λ = 6.0

## Context

This work extends the formalism of twisted particle beams, previously applied to atomic photo-absorption (Scholz 2014) and scattering (Serbo 2015), to the simplest nuclear reactions relevant to Big Bang nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution. The paper builds on prior treatments of radiative neutron capture (Rupak 2000) and deuteron photodisintegration (Fermi 1935, Akhiezer 1950, deShalit 1974, Walecka 2004), and is motivated by recent experimental generation of twisted cold neutrons (Clark 2015) and theoretical demonstrations of twisted MeV photon generation (Jentschura 2011, Katoh 2017, Petrillo 2016, Liu 2016, Taira 2016). The results enable diagnostic applications for twisted beam topology and open the question of experimental feasibility given that photon wavelengths at MeV energies are orders of magnitude smaller than typical mesoscopic targets.
