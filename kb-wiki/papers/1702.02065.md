# Antinucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory

> Results for the antinucleon-nucleon ($\bar NN$) interaction obtained at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory (EFT) are reported. A new local regularization scheme is used for the pion-exchange contributions that has been recently suggested and applied in a pertinent study of the $NN$ force within chiral EFT. Furthermore, an alternative strategy for estimating the uncertainty is utilized that no longer depends on a variation of the cutoffs. The low-energy constants associated with the arising contact terms are fixed by a fit to the phase shifts and inelasticities provided by a phase-shift analysis of $\bar pp$ scattering data. An excellent description of the $\bar NN$ amplitudes is achieved at the highest order considered. Moreover, because of the quantitative reproduction of partial waves up to $J=3$, there is also a nice agreement on the level of $\bar pp$ observables. Specifically, total and integrated elastic and charge-exchange cross sections agree well with the results from the partial-wave analysis up to laboratory energies of $300$ MeV, while differential cross sections and analyzing powers are described quantitatively up to $200$-$250$ MeV. The low-energy structure of the $\bar NN$ amplitudes is also considered and compared to data from antiprotonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The authors report that the antinucleon-nucleon interaction derived at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory achieves an excellent description of phase shifts and inelasticities up to partial waves with total angular momentum J=3, but the quantitative agreement with differential cross sections and analyzing powers is limited to laboratory energies up to 200-250 MeV, while total cross sections agree up to 300 MeV.

## Method

The chiral potential up to N³LO includes one-pion, two-pion, and three-pion exchange contributions along with contact terms up to four derivatives, with the long-range part fixed by G-parity transformation from the NN system. A local regularization scheme in coordinate space was used for pion-exchange contributions, while a nonlocal Gaussian regulator was applied to contact interactions. The low-energy constants were fitted to the phase shifts and inelasticity parameters from a partial-wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data, and annihilation was treated via an effective two-body channel that preserves unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The fit to phase shifts and inelasticities covers laboratory momenta up to 600 MeV/c (T_lab ≤ 175 MeV) at N³LO, with lower ranges at lower orders (300 MeV/c at LO, 500 MeV/c at NLO and N²LO)
- The effective χ² values for the N³LO fit with cutoff R=0.9 fm are: 0.004 for T_lab ≤ 25 MeV, 0.025 for T_lab ≤ 100 MeV, 0.115 for T_lab ≤ 200 MeV, and 2.171 for T_lab ≤ 300 MeV
- The cutoff range considered is R = 0.8 fm to R = 1.2 fm, corresponding to momentum-space cutoffs Λ ≈ 500 to 300 MeV via the relation Λ = 2R⁻¹
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is g_A = 1.29 with F_π = 92.4 MeV, giving g_NNπ = 13.1, consistent with empirical values
- The isospin-averaged pion mass is M_π = 138.039 MeV and the average nucleon mass is m = 938.918 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT study of the antinucleon-nucleon interaction at N²LO (Kang 2013) to the next order, N³LO, and adopts the local regularization scheme recently introduced for the NN interaction by Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (2015). The results are compared to the partial-wave analysis of antiproton-proton scattering data by Zhou and Timmermans (2012), which itself used chiral EFT for the long-range part. The improved description at higher energies and the inclusion of D-wave contact terms enable direct calculation of observables in the particle basis, including Coulomb effects and the physical threshold difference between pp and nn channels, which is about 2.5 MeV.
