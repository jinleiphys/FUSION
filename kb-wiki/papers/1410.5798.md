# Proton radius puzzle in Hamiltonian dynamics

> Relativistic lepton-proton bound-state eigenvalue equations for Hamiltonians derived from quantum field theory using second-order renormalization group procedure for effective particles, are reducible to two-body Schroedinger eigenvalue equations with the effective Coulomb potential that exhibits a tiny sensitivity to the characteristic momentum-scale of the bound system. The scale dependence is shown to be relevant to the theoretical interpretation of precisely measured lepton-proton bound-state energy levels in terms of a 4 percent difference between the proton radii in muon-proton and electron-proton bound states.

## Key claim

The 4% discrepancy between proton radii extracted from muon-proton and electron-proton bound states may arise because the effective two-body Schrödinger equation derived from quantum field theory via the renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) contains a scale-dependent form factor that modifies the energy correction due to the proton radius, with the effect being much larger for muonic systems than for electronic ones.

## Method

The author starts from a canonical front-form Hamiltonian for QED with electrons, muons, and protons, regulates it, and applies the RGPEP to derive a family of effective Hamiltonians labeled by a momentum scale parameter λ. Solving the RGPEP evolution equation to order α yields an effective two-body Schrödinger equation for lepton-proton bound states, where the Coulomb potential is multiplied by an RGPEP form factor f_λ that depends on the difference of squared invariant masses of the two-body system before and after the interaction. The proton electric form factor is inserted in the initial Hamiltonian and propagates unchanged into the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the ground-state energy due to the proton radius in the standard Schrödinger equation is ΔE = (4/3)(α μ r_p)^2 (μ α^2/2), where μ is the reduced mass and α ≈ 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- The muon is about 200 times heavier than the electron, making the muon-proton reduced mass about 200 times larger than the electron-proton reduced mass, so the energy correction from the proton radius is 200^3 times larger in muonic hydrogen.
- The RGPEP form factor is f_λ(p,k) = exp[-(p^2 - k^2)^2/(a μ)^4], where a is a dimensionless parameter of order 1; in momentum units of the Bohr momentum, the exponent contains (α/a)^4 ≈ 3×10^{-9}/a^4.
- The same λ in both systems implies that the parameter a is about 14 times smaller in the muon-proton system than in the electron-proton system, changing the coefficient c_λ from nearly 1 for electrons to about 0.92 for muons, corresponding to an 8% reduction in the extracted radius squared and a 4% reduction in the radius itself.

## Context

This work extends the RGPEP approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia (Glazek, 2004) to lepton-proton bound states, offering a potential resolution to the proton radius puzzle identified by Pohl et al. (2010) and Bernauer et al. (2010). The paper argues that standard atomic physics calculations using the Schrödinger equation with a Coulomb potential (e.g., those by Kinoshita, Pachucki, and Mohr) implicitly assume a point-like effective two-body description that may not capture the full QFT dynamics. The RGPEP framework provides a systematic path from QFT to the Schrödinger equation, but the author stresses that many other corrections must be calculated within this approach before the proton radius can be reliably extracted.
