# The nuclear matter equation of state with consistent two- and three-body perturbative chiral interactions

> We compute the energy per particle of infinite symmetric nuclear matter from chiral N3LO (next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order) two-body potentials plus N2LO three-body forces. The low-energy constants of the chiral three-nucleon force that cannot be constrained by two-body observables are fitted to reproduce the triton binding energy and the 3H-3He Gamow-Teller transition matrix element. In this way, the saturation properties of nuclear matter are reproduced in a parameter-free approach. The equation of state is computed up to third order in many-body perturbation theory, with special emphasis on the role of the third-order particle-hole diagram. The dependence of these results on the cutoff scale and regulator function is studied. We find that the inclusion of three-nucleon forces consistent with the applied two-nucleon interaction leads to a reduced dependence on the choice of the regulator only for lower values of the cutoff.

## Key claim

The saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter can be reproduced in a parameter-free approach using chiral N3LO two-body potentials plus N2LO three-body forces, but only when the low-energy constants of the three-nucleon force are fitted to triton binding energy and the 3H-3He Gamow-Teller transition matrix element; the regulator dependence is reduced only for lower cutoff values (414 and 450 MeV), not for the 500 MeV cutoff.

## Method

The authors computed the energy per particle of infinite symmetric nuclear matter up to third order in many-body perturbation theory using chiral N3LO two-nucleon potentials with three different cutoff scales (414, 450, and 500 MeV) and regulator functions. The N2LO three-nucleon force was included via a density-dependent two-body potential obtained by summing one nucleon over the Fermi sea, and the low-energy constants c_D and c_E were fitted for each cutoff to reproduce the experimental A=3 binding energies and the triton Gamow-Teller matrix element. The third-order particle-hole (ring) diagram was computed without simplifying approximations, which had not been done in previous perturbative chiral nuclear matter calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the 414 MeV cutoff potential at k_F = 1.3 fm^-1 with 3NF included: Hartree-Fock contribution = -28.792 MeV, second-order pp diagram = -7.388 MeV, third-order ph diagram = +0.581 MeV
- For the 450 MeV cutoff potential at k_F = 1.3 fm^-1 with 3NF included: Hartree-Fock contribution = -25.688 MeV, second-order pp diagram = -11.273 MeV, third-order ph diagram = +0.152 MeV
- For the 500 MeV cutoff potential at k_F = 1.3 fm^-1 with 3NF included: Hartree-Fock contribution = -19.503 MeV, second-order pp diagram = -13.511 MeV, third-order ph diagram = -1.516 MeV
- The 3NF contribution to the energy per nucleon in symmetric nuclear matter is about a factor of two larger than in pure neutron matter at k_F = 1.35 fm^-1 for the 500 MeV cutoff case
- The estimated uncertainty of the perturbative result is approximately 2 MeV per nucleon

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pure neutron matter (Coraggio, 2013) to symmetric nuclear matter, where the regulator dependence was found to be reduced by consistent 3NF inclusion. The calculation goes beyond earlier perturbative chiral nuclear matter studies (Sammarruca, 2012; Tews, 2013; Krueger, 2013; Kohno, 2013) by including the third-order particle-hole diagram, and complements coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen, 2014) that found significant contributions beyond the normal-ordered two-body approximation for 3NF. The results enable future investigations of the second-order 3p-3h diagram with two 3NF vertices, which may clarify whether the residual regulator dependence at 500 MeV cutoff arises from the perturbative expansion or from higher-order terms in chiral perturbation theory.
