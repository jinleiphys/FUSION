# Baryon chemical potential and in-medium properties of BPS skyrmions

> We continue the investigation of thermodynamical properties of the BPS Skyrme model. In particular, we analytically compute the baryon chemical potential both in the full field theory and in a mean-field approximation. In the full field theory case, we find that the baryon chemical potential is always exactly proportional to the baryon density, for arbitrary solutions. We further find that, in the mean-field approximation, the BPS Skyrme model approaches the Walecka model in the limit of high density - their thermodynamical functions as well as the equation of state agree in this limit. This fact allows to read off some properties of the $ω$-meson from the BPS Skyrme action, even though the latter model is entirely based on the (pionic) $SU(2)$ Skyrme field. On the other hand, at low densities, at the order of the usual nuclear matter density, the equations of state of the two models are no longer universal, such that a comparison depends on some model details. Still, also the BPS Skyrme model gives rise to nuclear saturation in this regime, leading, in fact, to an exact balance between repulsive and attractive forces. The perfect fluid aspects of the BPS Skyrme model, which, together with its BPS properties, form the base of our results, are shown to be in close formal analogy with the Eulerian formulation of relativistic fluid dynamics. Within this analogy, the BPS Skyrme model, in general, corresponds to a non-barotropic perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The baryon chemical potential in the BPS Skyrme model is exactly proportional to the baryon charge density for arbitrary solutions in the full field theory, while in the mean-field approximation this proportionality holds only asymptotically at high density, with the model approaching the Walecka model in that limit.

## Method

The authors analytically compute the baryon chemical potential both from the local thermodynamic relation (ε + P = μ ρ_B) using the exact field equations of the BPS Skyrme model, and from the mean-field definition μ̄ = (∂E/∂B)_V using the integrated bulk observables E(P) and V(P) expressed as target space integrals. They then derive explicit expressions for several specific potentials (step-function, cubic, non-compacton, and Skyrme potential) and compute the in-medium energy of skyrmions under external pressure.

## Key numbers

- For the step-function potential at equilibrium: E₀ = 2π² λ ν |B₀|, V₀ = π² (λ/ν) |B₀|
- For the step-function potential: μ = 2π⁴ λ² ρ̄_B, with ρ̄_B = B/V₀
- For the cubic potential at equilibrium: μ̄₀ = (π^(5/2))/(3√2) λ ν (3 + 1) = (4π^(5/2))/(3√2) λ ν
- For the cubic potential asymptotic high density: μ̄ = (π^(5/2)/√2) λ ν (B/B₀), P = (π/8)(B/B₀)² ν²
- For the non-compacton potential at high density: μ̄ = 2π⁴ λ² ρ̄_B as ρ̄_B → ∞
- For the non-compacton potential at vanishing density: μ̄ = (π³/2) λ ν + π² ν² (1/ρ̄_B) exp(-ν/(π λ ρ̄_B)) as ρ̄_B → 0
- For the step-function potential in-medium energy of a B₀ skyrmion: E_{B₀}(μ) = (B₀/2) μ (1 + 4π⁴ λ² ν²/μ²)
- Asymptotic in-medium energy for any potential: E_{B₀}(μ) = (B₀/2) μ as μ → ∞

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic analysis of the BPS Skyrme model previously developed by the same authors (Adam et al., 2014), which established the perfect fluid nature and exact equation of state of the model. It addresses the previously missing baryon chemical potential, which is crucial for applications to neutron star physics where β-equilibrium and phase transitions must be considered. The result that the mean-field BPS Skyrme model approaches the Walecka model at high density enables a connection between the Skyrme model parameters and ω-meson properties, while the exact treatment beyond mean-field provides a unique tool for studying nuclear matter with spatially non-constant energy densities, going beyond what is possible in typical effective field theories.
