# Crystalline boson phases in harmonic traps: Beyond the Gross-Pitaevskii mean field

> Strongly repelling bosons in two-dimensional harmonic traps are described through breaking of rotational symmetry at the Hartree-Fock level and subsequent symmetry restoration via projection techniques, thus incorporating correlations beyond the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) solution. The bosons localize and form polygonal-ring-like crystalline patterns, both for a repulsive contact potential and a Coulomb interaction, as revealed via conditional-probability-distribution analysis. For neutral bosons, the total energy of the crystalline phase saturates in contrast to the GP solution, and its spatial extent becomes smaller than that of the GP condensate. For charged bosons, the total energy and dimensions approach the values of classical point-like charges in their equilibrium configuration.

# Crystalline boson phases in harmonic traps: Beyond the Gross-Pitaevskii mean field

## Key claim

Strongly repelling bosons in two-dimensional harmonic traps form crystalline polygonal-ring patterns whose total energy saturates with increasing repulsion strength, in contrast to the Gross-Pitaevskii mean-field solution which diverges; this saturation holds for both contact and Coulomb interactions, but the spatial extent saturates only for contact interactions while for Coulomb interactions it grows toward the classical point-charge limit.

## Method

The authors apply a two-step method: first, unrestricted Bose-Hartree-Fock (UBHF) calculations using displaced Gaussian orbitals arranged in polygonal-ring configurations, with orbital widths and ring radii determined variationally by minimizing the total energy; second, restoration of rotational symmetry via angular-momentum projection techniques to obtain wave functions with definite total angular momentum (ground state L=0). The method is applied to N=2-7 bosons in a two-dimensional harmonic trap for both a repulsive contact potential (strength parameter R_delta) and a Coulomb interaction (strength parameter R_W).

## Key numbers

- For N=6 neutral bosons with contact interaction, the UBHF ring radius a saturates to approximately 2 l_0 as R_delta approaches infinity (Fig. 2a)
- For N=6 charged bosons with Coulomb interaction, the UBHF ring radius approaches the classical equilibrium radius a^cl_C = 1.334 l_0 R_W^(1/3) as R_W approaches infinity
- The interparticle distance on the pentagonal shell for N=6 is d = ((5-5^(1/2))/2)^(1/2) * a ≈ 1.176 a, showing the same saturation trend as the radius a
- Energy saturation for both contact and Coulomb cases is achieved already for R_delta (or R_W) approximately equal to 10
- For N=6 neutral bosons with contact interaction at R_delta=25, the projected (symmetry-restored) single-particle density shows a radially modulated circular pattern with a radius significantly smaller than the GP condensate radius

## Context

This work extends the Tonks-Girardeau (TG) fermionization regime from one-dimensional trapped bosons (Girardeau 1960, Dunjko et al. 2001) to two dimensions, showing analogous energy saturation and spatial extent saturation for contact interactions. The method of symmetry breaking followed by projection was previously applied to strongly correlated electrons in semiconductor quantum dots (Yannouleas and Landman 1999-2003). For charged bosons, the crystalline phases are finite-size analogs of the bulk Wigner crystal (Wigner 1934). The results enable experimental efforts to access strongly repelling boson regimes in 2D using techniques similar to those developed for 1D TG gases with trapped rubidium atoms and optical lattices (Paredes et al. 2004, Weiss et al. 2004).
