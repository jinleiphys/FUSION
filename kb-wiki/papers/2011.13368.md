# Covariant density functional theory input for r-process simulations in actinides and superheavy nuclei: the ground state and fission properties

> The systematic investigation of the ground state and fission properties of even-even actinides and superheavy nuclei with $Z=90-120$ from the two-proton up to two-neutron drip lines with proper assessment of systematic theoretical uncertainties has been performed for the first time in the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT). These results provide a necessary theoretical input for the r-process modeling in heavy nuclei and, in particular, for the study of fission recycling. Four state-of-the-art globally tested covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs), namely, DD-PC1, DD-ME2, NL3* and PC-PK1, representing the major classes of the CDFT models are employed in the present study. Ground state deformations, binding energies, two neutron separation energies, $α$-decay $Q_α$ values and half-lives and the heights of fission barriers have been calculated for all these nuclei. Theoretical uncertainties in these physical observables and their evolution as a function of proton and neutron numbers have been quantified and their major sources have been identified. Spherical shell closures at $Z=120$, $N=184$ and $N=258$ and the structure of the single-particle (especially, high-$j$) states in their vicinities as well as nuclear matter properties of employed CEDFs are two major factors contributing into theoretical uncertainties. However, different physical observables are affected in a different way by these two factors. For example, theoretical uncertainties in calculated ground state deformations are affected mostly by former factor, while theoretical uncertainties in fission barriers depend on both of these factors.

## Key claim

This study provides the first systematic CDFT-based input for r-process simulations in actinides and superheavy nuclei (Z=90-120) from two-proton to two-neutron drip lines, but the results carry systematic theoretical uncertainties that depend on the choice of covariant energy density functional and are quantified via the spread of predictions across four functionals (DD-PC1, DD-ME2, NL3*, PC-PK1), not as rigorous statistical errors.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with reflection-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric constraints for all even-even nuclei with Z=90-120 between the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines, using four globally tested covariant energy density functionals representing the major CDFT classes. Ground state deformations, binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, alpha-decay Q-values and half-lives, and fission barrier heights were computed; systematic theoretical uncertainties were quantified as the spread between the maximum and minimum predictions across the four functionals for each observable.

## Key numbers

- The study covers nuclei with proton numbers Z=90-120 from the two-proton to two-neutron drip lines, using four CEDFs: DD-PC1, DD-ME2, NL3*, and PC-PK1.
- The last bound Z=120 nucleus appears at N=324 in calculations with the PC-PK1 functional.
- For the DD-PC1 functional, several nuclei show extremely superdeformed minima lower than normal-deformed minima; for example, in Cf-240 (Z=98, N=142) the energy difference is 0.603 MeV, with the second fission barrier height at 7.207 MeV (RS-RHB) and 2.625 MeV (RA-RHB).
- The separable pairing interaction uses parameters G=728 MeV fm^3 and a=0.644 fm, derived from the Gogny D1S force.
- The basis truncation uses N_F=20 fermionic shells and N_B=20 bosonic shells for meson-exchange functionals.

## Context

This work extends previous CDFT studies of ground state properties in Z=90-104 nuclei (Agbemava et al., 2014) and superheavy nuclei (Agbemava et al., 2015, 2017) to the full Z=90-120 range with fission barrier predictions, filling a gap where only non-relativistic models such as FRDM, Thomas-Fermi, ETFSI-Q, HFB-14, and BCPM had been applied for r-process fission inputs. The results enable r-process simulations with fission recycling in neutron star merger scenarios, where neutron-to-seed ratios can exceed 100 and fission cycling determines the final abundance pattern of actinides and superheavy elements.
