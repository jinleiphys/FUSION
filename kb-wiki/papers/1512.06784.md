# The 1/N expansion method in quantum field theory

> The motivations of the 1/N expansion method in quantum field theory are explained. The method is first illustrated with the O(N) model of scalar fields. A second example is considered with the two-dimensional Gross-Neveu model of fermion fields with global U(N) and discrete chiral symmetries. The case of QCD is briefly sketched.

## Key claim

The 1/N expansion method provides a nonperturbative approach to quantum field theory by treating the limit N → ∞ as a solvable starting point, with 1/N corrections as a perturbation; however, the method's utility depends on the physical N being "sufficiently large" for the leading-order calculation to capture the dominant dynamics, and the O(N) scalar model itself suffers from instabilities and restrictions when examined beyond leading order.

## Method

The method is illustrated through two models: first, the O(N) model of N real scalar fields with a quartic interaction, where an auxiliary σ field is introduced to replace the composite φ^a φ^a interaction, allowing systematic identification of leading-order diagrams in 1/N. Second, the two-dimensional Gross-Neveu model of N fermion fields with a four-fermion interaction and discrete chiral symmetry is analyzed. In both cases, the effective potential is computed at leading order in 1/N by summing φ-loop diagrams with external σ lines, and renormalization is performed by absorbing divergences into the bare coupling constant and mass.

## Key numbers

- In the O(N) model, the β-function for the renormalized coupling λ(M) is β(λ) = λ²/(16π²) > 0, showing the coupling increases with energy scale M.
- The coupling λ(M) diverges at a critical scale M_cr = M₁ exp(16π²/λ₁), where λ₁ ≡ λ(M₁) is a reference value.
- In the O(N) model with μ² < 0 (spontaneous symmetry breaking), the vacuum expectation value is ⟨φ⟩ = √(−2Nμ²/λ), with (N−1) massless Goldstone bosons and one massive χ field with m_χ² = −2μ₀² at classical level.

## Context

This paper provides pedagogical lecture notes on the 1/N expansion method, extending the approach first introduced in quantum field theory by Wilson (1972) and applied to QCD by 't Hooft (1974). The O(N) scalar model analysis follows Coleman (1974), while the Gross-Neveu model treatment builds on Gross and Neveu (1974) and the earlier Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (1961) for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The method enables nonperturbative insights into theories that are otherwise intractable with ordinary coupling-constant perturbation theory, and it opens the way to studying dynamical mass generation and spontaneous symmetry breaking in large-N limits of gauge theories.
