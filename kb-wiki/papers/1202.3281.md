# Fragmentation and quark distribution functions for the pion and kaon with explicit flavor-SU(3)-symmetry breaking

> We investigate the unpolarized pion and kaon fragmentation functions, employing the nonlocal chiral-quark model, which manifests the nonlocal interactions between the quarks and pseudoscalar mesons, considering the explicit flavor-SU(3)-symmetry breaking in terms of the current-quark masses. Moreover, we study the quark-distribution functions, derived from the fragmentation ones with the Drell-Yan-Levi relation. Numerical results are evaluated to higher Q^2 by the DGLAP evolution and compared with the empirical data. The ratios between the relevant valance quark-distribution functions are also discussed. It turns out that the present results are in relatively good agreement with available data and other theoretical estimations.

## Key claim

The nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM), motivated by the instanton-liquid model, can reproduce the qualitative features of pion and kaon fragmentation and quark-distribution functions, but the results show significant differences from other models (e.g., NJL-Jet model) for kaon fragmentation, and the agreement with empirical data is only qualitative, with underestimates in the small-\(x\) and small-\(z\) regions attributed to the absence of quark-jet contributions.

## Method

The authors computed the elementary fragmentation functions \(d^{\phi}_q(z)\) for \(\pi^+\) and \(K^+\) using the nonlocal chiral-quark model (NLChQM) with a momentum-dependent effective quark mass of the form \(M(\partial^2)=M_0[2\Lambda^2/(2\Lambda^2-\partial^2)]^2\), expanding the nonlinear pseudoscalar-meson field to first order. The quark-distribution functions \(f^{\phi}_q(x)\) were derived from the fragmentation functions via the Drell-Levy-Yan (DLY) relation \(D^h_q(z) = (z/6) f^h_q(1/z)\). The weak-decay constants \(F_{\pi,K}\) were tuned to satisfy the valence-quark number normalization condition for the distribution functions, and the resulting functions were evolved to higher \(Q^2\) using the DGLAP evolution via the QCDNUM program.

## Key numbers

- The model scale is set at \(Q^2 = \Lambda^2 = 0.36\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- For Model I (current-quark masses \(m_u=m_d=5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s=150\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the tuned weak-decay constants are: \(F^{u\to\pi d}_\pi = 88.07\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(F^{u\to K s}_K = 118.30\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(F^{\bar{s}\to K\bar{u}}_K = 103.20\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For Model II (PDG center values \(m_u=2.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_d=5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_s=100\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the tuned weak-decay constants are: \(F^{u\to\pi d}_\pi = 88.49\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(F^{u\to K s}_K = 116.60\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(F^{\bar{s}\to K\bar{u}}_K = 95.94\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The renormalization constants for the PS-meson cloud effects are \(\mathcal{N}_u = 0.42\ (0.41)\) and \(\mathcal{N}_{\bar{s}} = 0.04\ (0.03)\) for Model I (Model II).
- Parameterizations of the valence-quark distribution functions at \(Q^2=0.36\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) for Model I: \(xf^{\pi}_u(x) = 3.22\, x^{1.18}\, (1-x)^{1.43}\), \(xf^{K}_u(x) = 7.08\, x^{1.48}\, (1-x)^{2.25}\), \(xf^{K}_{\bar{s}}(x) = 8.77\, x^{1.86}\, (1-x)^{1.74}\).
- Parameterizations for Model II: \(xf^{\pi}_u(x) = 3.21\, x^{1.18}\, (1-x)^{1.42}\), \(xf^{K}_u(x) = 8.51\, x^{1.62}\, (1-x)^{2.21}\), \(xf^{K}_{\bar{s}}(x) = 9.97\, x^{1.92}\, (1-x)^{1.84}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(2) flavor study (Nam, 2011) to the SU(3) flavor sector, including explicit flavor-symmetry breaking via the strange-quark mass. It contrasts with the NJL-Jet model (Matevosyan, 2010; Matevosyan, 2011) which includes quark-jet contributions and finds \(zD^K_u \sim zD^\pi_u/2\), whereas the present model finds \(zD^K_u \ll zD^\pi_u\) for all \(z\). The results enable comparison with empirical data from global analyses such as Sutton (1991) for pion distribution functions and Hirai (2007) for kaon fragmentation functions, and suggest that including quark-jet contributions could improve the small-\(x\) and small-\(z\) agreement.
