# Accessing GPDs through the exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with a large invariant mass

> We study the exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with a large invariant mass, working in the QCD factorisation framework. Explicitly, we consider a $ ρ$-meson or a charged $ π$ in the final state. This process gives access to chiral-even GPDs as well as chiral-odd GPDs. We focus here on the chiral-even sector. The computation is performed at leading order and leading twist. We discuss the prospects of measuring them in various experiments such as JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC and LHC (in ultraperipheral collisions). In particular, the high centre of mass energies available at collider experiments can be used to probe GPDs at small skewness $ ξ$. We also compute the polarisation asymmetries with respect to the incoming photon. The results for an alternative distribution amplitude (`holographic' form) are also compared with the predictions obtained with an asymptotic distribution amplitude.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with a large invariant mass can access chiral-even generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at leading twist and leading order, but the results depend on the choice of distribution amplitude (asymptotic versus holographic) and on the GPD parametrization (standard versus valence scenario), with the caveat that next-to-leading order corrections may be needed to distinguish between models.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for the process γ + N → γ + N' + m (where m = ρ⁰, ρ⁺, π⁺) in the QCD collinear factorization framework at leading twist and leading order. The hard scale is provided by the large invariant mass M²_γm of the photon-meson pair, with kinematic cuts -u', -t' > 1 GeV² and -t < 0.5 GeV². The amplitude is expressed as a convolution over the parton momentum fraction x and the meson distribution amplitude variable z, using the asymptotic distribution amplitude φ^as(z) = 6z(1-z) and a holographic form φ^hol(z) = (8/π)√[z(1-z)], with GPDs modeled through double distributions using two scenarios for the light sea quark flavor symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 131 MeV.
- The fully differential cross-section dσ/(dt du' dM²_γπ⁺) is computed for M²_γπ⁺ = 3, 4, 5 GeV² at S_γN = 20 GeV²; the holographic DA gives a result roughly twice that of the asymptotic DA.
- The single differential cross-section dσ/dM²_γπ⁺ peaks around M²_γπ⁺ ≈ 3 GeV² for S_γN = 8, 14, 20 GeV².
- The integrated cross-section σ_γπ⁺ as a function of S_γN peaks at around 20 GeV² and drops rapidly with increasing energy.
- Counting rates for LHC in ultraperipheral collisions (integrated luminosity 1200 nb⁻¹) for π⁺: 1.6–9.3 × 10³ events without S_γN cut, and 1.0–3.4 × 10² events with S_γN ≥ 300 GeV².
- Counting rates for future EIC (integrated luminosity 10⁷ nb⁻¹) for π⁺: 2.3–13 × 10³ events without S_γN cut, and 1.4–5.0 × 10² events with S_γN ≥ 300 GeV².
- At S_γN = 300 GeV², the region of maximum cross-section corresponds to ξ ≈ 5 × 10⁻³; at S_γN = 20000 GeV², ξ ≈ 7.5 × 10⁻⁵.

## Context

This work extends the study of 2→3 exclusive processes for accessing GPDs, building on prior calculations for ρ-meson final states by Boussarie et al. (2016) and for charged pions by Duplancic et al. (2018, 2022). It complements deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production by offering access to both chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs at leading twist, with the chiral-odd sector being particularly valuable since it is poorly constrained experimentally. The results enable feasibility studies for measuring GPDs at small skewness ξ at JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC, and LHC in ultraperipheral collisions, with the linear polarization asymmetry providing a potential discriminator between different GPD models.
