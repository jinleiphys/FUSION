# Formation of Deeply Bound Kaonic Atoms in (K^-,N) Reactions

> We study theoretically the (K^-,N) reactions for the formation of the deeply bound kaonic atoms, which were predicted to be quasi--stable with narrow widths, using the Green function method. We consider various cases with different target nuclei and energies systematically and find the clear signals in the theoretical spectra for all cases considered in this article. The signals show very interesting structures, such as the $RESONANCE DIP$ instead of the resonance peak. We discuss the origins of the interesting structures and possibilities to get new information on the existence of the kaonic nuclei from the spectra of the atomic state formations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that clear signals for deeply bound kaonic atoms can be observed in (K⁻,N) reaction spectra, but these signals often appear as "resonance dips" rather than peaks, and the spectral shapes depend critically on the angular momenta of the proton-hole and kaon states involved.

## Method

The authors used the Green function method to calculate the nuclear response function S(E) for (K⁻,p) reactions, solving the Klein-Gordon equation with kaon-nucleus optical potentials. They considered two main optical potentials: a phenomenological density-dependent potential from Batty (1997) and a microscopic chiral unitary potential from Ramos and Oset. The calculations included distortion effects from incoming kaon and outgoing proton waves, and considered only proton-hole states corresponding to ground states of daughter nuclei to avoid smearing from gamma-decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The elementary cross section for p(K⁻,p)K⁻ at T_K = 600 MeV is 8.8 mb/sr (with a correction to 3.6 mb/sr reported by Cieplý et al.)
- For ¹¹⁹In kaonic atoms with the phenomenological potential: 1s state binding energy = 4309.7 keV, width = 999.7 keV; 4f state binding energy = 1930.7 keV, width = 54.2 keV
- For ²⁰⁷Ti kaonic atoms with the phenomenological potential: 1s state binding energy = 7279.9 keV, width = 1544.3 keV; 4f state binding energy = 4629.0 keV, width = 434.4 keV
- For ¹¹⁹In kaonic atoms with the chiral unitary potential: 1s state binding energy = 4296.8 keV, width = 944.5 keV; 4f state binding energy = 1930.9 keV, width = 48.4 keV
- For ²⁰⁷Ti kaonic atoms with the chiral unitary potential: 1s state binding energy = 7241.8 keV, width = 1502.7 keV; 4f state binding energy = 4626.0 keV, width = 404.8 keV
- Proton separation energies used: ¹²C (1p₃/₂) = 16.0 MeV; ¹⁶O (1p₁/₂) = 12.1 MeV; ⁴⁰Ca (1d₃/₂) = 8.3 MeV; ¹²⁰Sn (1g₉/₂) = 10.7 MeV; ²⁰⁸Pb (3s₁/₂) = 8.0 MeV
- Momentum transfer for kaon binding energy of 0 MeV at T_K = 600 MeV is approximately 200 MeV/c
- Recoilless kinematics for ²⁰⁸Pb target occurs at T_K = 22 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Yamagata et al. 2005, 2006) on kaonic atom and kaonic nucleus formation using the Green function method, which was originally developed by Morimatsu and Yazaki (1985) for bound state formation reactions. The paper builds on the proposal by Kishimoto (1999) and Friedman (1999) for using (K⁻,N) reactions to study kaon-nucleus systems, and parallels earlier work on deeply bound pionic atom formation by Hirenzaki et al. (1991) and Toki et al. The results enable experimental planning for kaonic atom spectroscopy at facilities like J-PARC, and provide a framework for distinguishing between different kaon-nucleus optical potentials through precise spectral measurements.
