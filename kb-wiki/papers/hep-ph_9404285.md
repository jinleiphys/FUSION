# $Λ(1405)$ Contribution to Kaon-Nucleon Scattering Lengths In Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We examine the role of the $Λ(1405)$ in kaon-nucleon scattering lengths using chiral perturbation theory. The leading nonanalytic SU(3) corrections reduce the coupling of the $Λ(1405)$ to $KN$ compared to $Σπ$. S-wave $K^-p$ scattering is the only channel significantly affected by the $ Λ(1405)$ pole which substantially cancels against the leading term fixed by the vector current. This cancellation and the close proximity of the $Λ(1405)$ pole makes this SU(3) correction to $a(K^-p)$ large and at lowest order leaves the sign of $a(K^-p)$ undetermined. We extract two linear combinations of constants appearing at higher order in the chiral expansion from measurements of $KN$ scattering lengths. These constants are important for the offshell behaviour of $KN$ scattering amplitudes that play a central role in kaon condensation. The recent claims about the calculability of these constants are discussed.

## Key claim

The leading nonanalytic SU(3) corrections reduce the coupling of the Lambda(1405) to kaon-nucleon (KN) compared to its coupling to Sigma-pi; the resulting substantial cancellation between the tree-level and Lambda(1405) contributions to the K- p scattering amplitude, combined with the close proximity of the Lambda(1405) pole to the KN threshold, leaves the sign of the K- p scattering length undetermined at lowest order, contrary to earlier claims.

## Method

The author computed the leading nonanalytic SU(3)-violating corrections to the Lambda(1405) coupling to octet baryons and pseudo-Goldstone bosons using one-loop chiral perturbation theory in the heavy-baryon formalism, including meson and baryon wavefunction renormalization and vertex corrections from kaon and eta loops. The observed width of the Lambda(1405) (50 +/- 2 MeV) was used to fix the coupling to Sigma-pi, and the resulting coupling to KN was extracted. Two linear combinations of higher-order constants (C_p and C_n) were then fit to the experimentally measured K+ p and K+ n scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

*   Observed width of the Lambda(1405): Gamma = 50 +/- 2 MeV.
*   Extracted coupling of Lambda(1405) to Sigma-pi: |g(Lambda*)(Sigma pi)| = 0.58 +/- 0.01 (using f = f_pi = 132 MeV).
*   Extracted coupling of Lambda(1405) to KN: g(Lambda*)(NK) = (0.32 +/- 0.03) + (0.050 +/- 0.005)i.
*   Extracted bare coupling g(Lambda*) = 0.40 +/- 0.04.
*   Experimentally determined K+ p scattering length: a(K+ p) = -0.31 +/- 0.02 fm.
*   Fitted value for the higher-order constant C_p: (1/(4 pi)) (M_N/(M_N+M_K)) C_p = 0.25 +/- 0.02 fm.
*   Predicted K- p scattering length using the SU(3)-corrected coupling g(Lambda*)(NK): a(K- p) = 0.46 + 0.23i fm.
*   Predicted K- n scattering length range: +0.28 fm < a(K- n) < +0.43 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of kaon-nucleon scattering to include the leading SU(3) breaking in the Lambda(1405) couplings, building on the framework for baryon chiral perturbation theory developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991, 1992). The results contradict the findings of Lee, Jung, Min, and Rho (1993) and Lee, Brown, and Rho (1994), who claimed the sign of the K- p scattering length could be determined and that the higher-order constants could be computed from chiral symmetry arguments. The extracted constants C_p and C_n are essential for determining the offshell KN scattering amplitudes that play a central role in calculations of kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter, as pioneered by Kaplan and Nelson (1986, 1987).
