# Pseudorapidity distributions of produced charged hadrons in pp collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> The energy dependence of charged-hadron production in proton-proton collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is investigated in a nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources for particle production. Calculated charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions for pp at RHIC energies of sqrt(s) = 0.2 and 0.41 TeV, and at LHC energies of 0.9, 2.36 and 7 TeV are optimized with respect to the available data. Predictions for 14 TeV are made. The central source arising from gluon-gluon collisions becomes the major origin of particle production at LHC energies. The midrapidity dip is essentially determined by the interplay of the three sources.

## Key claim

The relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources can describe charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions in proton-proton collisions from RHIC to LHC energies, but the model's applicability depends on the number of produced particles being sufficiently large for nonequilibrium-statistical concepts to apply, and transport phenomena are not fully developed in pp due to the small transverse size of the system.

## Method

The author solves a linear Fokker-Planck equation (Uhlenbeck-Ornstein process) in rapidity space for three sources: two fragmentation sources from valence quark-gluon interactions centered at large rapidities, and one central source from gluon-gluon collisions centered at midrapidity. The partial distribution functions are Gaussian in rapidity space and converted to pseudorapidity space via an approximated Jacobian using the pion mass and average transverse momenta. The model parameters (time parameter, widths, and particle numbers) are determined by fits to PHOBOS and CMS data, with extrapolations to 14 TeV.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV (RHIC): midrapidity density dN/dη = 2.25^{+0.37}_{-0.30} (inelastic, |η| < 1, PHOBOS data)
- At sqrt(s) = 0.41 TeV (RHIC): midrapidity density dN/dη = 2.87^{+0.44}_{-0.43} (inelastic, |η| < 1, PHOBOS data)
- At sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV (LHC): midrapidity density dN/dη = 3.48 ± 0.02 ± 0.13 (NSD, |η| < 0.5, CMS data)
- At sqrt(s) = 2.36 TeV (LHC): midrapidity density dN/dη = 4.47 ± 0.04 ± 0.16 (NSD, |η| < 0.5, CMS data)
- At sqrt(s) = 7 TeV (LHC): midrapidity density dN/dη = 5.78 ± 0.01 ± 0.23 (NSD, |η| < 0.5, CMS data)
- At sqrt(s) = 14 TeV (LHC, prediction): midrapidity density dN/dη = 6.73 ± 0.30 (extrapolated)
- At 7 TeV, the central gluon-gluon source contributes 73% of the midrapidity density; fragmentation sources are centered at <η_{1,2}> ≃ ∓ 2.8
- At 0.2 TeV, the gluon-gluon source contributes about 20% at midrapidity; at 0.41 TeV it contributes 48%
- Average transverse momenta range from <p_T> = 0.39 GeV/c at 0.2 TeV to 0.61 GeV/c at 14 TeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic diffusion model (RDM), previously applied to heavy-ion collisions by Wolschin (2007) and Wolschin (2011), to proton-proton collisions at high energies. It is complementary to QCD-based approaches such as the dual parton model (Capella et al., 1982, 1994) and the quark-gluon string model (Kaidalov, 1982; Kharzeev and Levin, 2003), which do not include transport effects. The results enable predictions for 14 TeV LHC collisions and suggest that full development of transport phenomena requires a sufficiently large transverse system, as seen in heavy-ion collisions where peak positions shift with energy (Wolschin, 2007) unlike the nearly constant peak positions at η ≃ 2 observed here for pp.
