# Padé expansion and the renormalization of nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The importance of imposing physical boundary conditions on the $T$-matrix to remove the nonperturbative renormalization prescription dependence is stressed and demonstrated in two diagonal channels, $^1P_1$ and $^1D_2$, with the help of Padé expansion.

# Padé expansion and the renormalization of nucleon-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

The nonperturbative renormalization prescription dependence of the nucleon-nucleon T-matrix can be removed by imposing physical boundary conditions (phase shifts) on the renormalized parameters, and this is demonstrated using a Padé expansion of the Re(G) factor in the compact parametrization of the T-matrix. The authors stress that even with a rational power counting system, predictions cannot be relevant to physical situations unless the renormalization prescription is fixed by physical boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors employ a compact parametrization of the T-matrix, T^{-1} = V^{-1} - G, where G is an intrinsic nonperturbative factor containing the divergences. They parametrize Re(G_l(p)) in the low-energy region via a Padé expansion (or Taylor expansion at even lower energies), then fit the expansion coefficients to phase shift data from the Nijmegen PWA at low laboratory energies (0-10 MeV) for the ^1P_1 and ^1D_2 diagonal channels. The fitted coefficients represent the renormalization prescription parameters, and the phase shifts at higher energies (10-200 MeV) are then predictions. The potential is truncated at LO, NLO, and NNLO chiral orders from the EGM potential of Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meissner (2000-2004).

## Key numbers

- The low-energy fitting region for determining the Padé coefficients is E (T_lab) in (0, 10 MeV).
- The prediction region extends from E (T_lab) in (10 MeV, 200 MeV).
- For the ^1P_1 and ^1D_2 channels, the simplest expansion Re(G_l) ≈ g_{l;0} (a single constant) suffices.
- The potential is truncated at three chiral orders: leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration of nonperturbative renormalization prescription dependence in Yang and Huang (2005, Phys. Rev. C71, 034001) for contact potentials, now applied to realistic chiral potentials in the ^1P_1 and ^1D_2 channels. The approach contrasts with conventional methods such as those of Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meissner (2000-2004) and the KSW scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), where the strong interplay between power counting and renormalization makes the T-matrix develop prescription dependence. The Padé expansion of Re(G) provides a simple technical treatment that avoids solving the full Lippmann-Schwinger equation with various approximation methods, and enables testing whether chiral EFT systematically works for nucleon-nucleon scattering. The next steps are to apply this strategy to other channels, including coupled channels.
