# Single inclusive forward hadron production at next-to-leading order

> We discuss single inclusive hadron production from a high energy quark scattering off a strong target color field in the Color Glass Condensate formalism. Recent calculations of this process at the next-to-leading order accuracy have led to negative cross sections at large transverse momenta. We identify the origin of this problem as an oversubtraction of the rapidity divergence into the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation for the target. We propose a new way to implement the kinematical restriction on the emitted gluons to overcome this difficulty.

## Key claim

The authors identify that the negative cross sections at large transverse momenta in previous NLO calculations of single inclusive hadron production in the Color Glass Condensate formalism arise from an oversubtraction of the rapidity divergence into the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation, and propose a new implementation of a kinematical restriction on emitted gluons that can make the negative contribution arbitrarily small.

## Method

The authors start from the CXY (Chirilli-Xiao-Yuan) NLO cross section formulae for the quark channel \(q \to q\) and introduce an explicit rapidity factorization scale \(\xi_f\) that controls which part of the \(\xi\) integration is subtracted into the BK evolution of the target dipole. They then implement a "kinematical constraint" of light cone energy (\(k^-\)) ordering by making this factorization scale dependent on the transverse momentum of the produced particle, using the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (GBW) parametrization of the dipole cross section for numerical calculations.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale in the GBW model is parametrized as \(Q_s^2 = c A^{1/3} Q_{s0}^2 (x_0/x)^\lambda\) with \(c=0.56\), \(Q_{s0}=1\) GeV, \(x_0=3.04 \times 10^{-4}\), \(\lambda=0.288\).
- For \(\xi_f=0\) (the original CXY choice), the NLO multiplicity becomes negative when \(p_T\) exceeds a value of the order of the saturation scale \(Q_s \approx p_T\).
- With \(\xi_f \gtrsim 0.999\), the multiplicity remains positive up to \(p_T = 8\) GeV at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(\alpha_s=0.2\), \(\mu^2=10\) GeV\(^2\), and \(y_h=3.2\).
- The subtraction term \(\mathcal{J}(k_T,1)-\mathcal{J}_v(k_T,1)\) in the GBW model behaves as \(\sim -2 k_T^2 / Q_s^4\) at small \(k_T\) and is positive at large \(k_T\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of single inclusive hadron production by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2012) and addresses the negative cross section problem first observed numerically by Stasto, Xiao, and Yuan (2013). The authors build on suggestions by Kang, Vitev, and Xing (2014) regarding rapidity factorization scales and by Altinoluk, Armesto, Beuf, and Moscoso (2014) regarding kinematical constraints. The proposed method enables future resummation of large transverse momentum contributions similarly to recent developments for the NLO BK equation by Iancu et al. (2015) and Lappi and Mantysaari (2016).
