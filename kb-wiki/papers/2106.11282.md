# Analysis of the neutron matter equation of state and the symmetry energy up to fourth order of chiral effective field theory

> We present predictions for the neutron matter equation of state, from leading to fourth order of chiral effective field theory, using recently developed, accurate chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials. We find the impact of subleading three-neutron forces to be mild and attractive. We also show order-by-order predictions for the symmetry energy, and discuss its density dependence in relation to empirical constraints.

# Analysis of the neutron matter equation of state and the symmetry energy up to fourth order of chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The neutron matter equation of state and symmetry energy predictions at N3LO (fourth order) of chiral effective field theory are well converged and produce a relatively soft equation of state, with subleading three-neutron forces having a mild attractive impact of about 2 MeV at the highest density. However, these chiral EFT predictions are far from the values extracted from the PREX-II experiment, which yield a much stiffer symmetry energy with L = (109.56 ± 36.41) MeV compared to the authors' N3LO value of L = (49.58 ± 8.47) MeV at saturation density.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic calculations of the energy per particle in neutron matter using the nonperturbative particle-particle ladder approximation within the hole-line expansion framework. They employed chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from the Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk set spanning LO to N3LO with a regulator cutoff Λ = 450 MeV, and incorporated three-nucleon forces at N2LO and N3LO as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions. The symmetry energy was computed from the difference between neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter energies, with symmetric nuclear matter fixed to an empirical parametrization with energy -16.0 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers
- Energy per neutron in neutron matter at saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³): 15.56 ± 1.10 MeV at N3LO
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.57 ± 1.53 MeV at N3LO (compared to empirical constraint 33.3 ± 1.3 MeV from Tsang)
- Slope parameter L at saturation density: 49.58 ± 8.47 MeV at N3LO (compared to empirical constraint 59.6 ± 22.1 MeV from Tsang)
- Pressure in neutron matter at saturation density: 2.562 ± 0.438 MeV/fm³ at N3LO (compared to empirical constraint 3.2 ± 1.2 MeV/fm³ from Tsang)
- Subleading three-neutron force contribution: approximately 2 MeV attractive at the highest density
- Symmetry energy curvature K at ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³: -101 ± 23 MeV at N3LO (compared to constraint -180 ± 96 MeV from Tsang)
- Symmetry energy curvature K at ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³: -68.7 ± 15.7 MeV at N3LO (compared to constraint -79.2 ± 37.6 MeV from Tsang)
- Proton fraction in β-equilibrated matter at ρ ≈ 0.2 fm⁻³: close to 6%, below the direct Urca threshold of approximately 11%

## Context
This work extends previous chiral EFT calculations of neutron matter by Sammarruca and Millerson (2020) and Drischler et al. (2016) by including complete subleading three-nucleon forces up to N3LO with proper uncertainty quantification. The results are compared to empirical constraints from Tsang and Lynch (2021) and the PREX-II experiment (Reed et al., 2021). The soft equation of state predicted here is consistent with other chiral EFT calculations such as those using SRG-evolved interactions based on Entem-Machleidt (2003) potentials, but stands in tension with the much stiffer symmetry energy extracted from PREX-II. The work enables improved predictions for neutron star properties and provides microscopic benchmarks for constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy at and below saturation density.
