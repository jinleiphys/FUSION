# Towards a consistent approach to nuclear structure: EFT of two- and many-body forces

> We review the nuclear forces currently in use, i.e., the high-precision NN potentials of the 1990's and the nuclear two- and many-body forces based upon chiral effective field theory (EFT). We argue that the EFT approach is superior to any of the older schemes. Since accurate chiral forces are available now, the stage is set for microscopic nuclear structure to move into a new exciting era.

# Towards a consistent approach to nuclear structure: EFT of two- and many-body forces

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a superior framework for nuclear forces compared to the high-precision phenomenological potentials of the 1990s, because it connects to QCD, generates two- and many-body forces on the same footing, and can be systematically improved to any desired accuracy; however, the claim that "accurate chiral forces are available now" is qualified by the fact that only at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) does the chiral NN potential achieve a chi-squared per datum comparable to the phenomenological potentials, and the three-nucleon force (3NF) at N3LO is still under development.

## Method

The authors review the high-precision NN potentials (Nijmegen, Argonne V18, CD-Bonn) and contrast them with chiral EFT potentials constructed using Weinberg's power counting. For the chiral approach, the NN potential is computed order by order in chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) from contact terms and pion-exchange diagrams, then applied in the Lippmann-Schwinger or Schrodinger equation to obtain the NN amplitude. The N3LO potential uses 24 contact parameters and a regulator function with cutoff Lambda, and is fitted to reproduce the 1999 NN database below 290 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- The CD-Bonn potential predicts a deuteron D-state probability P_D = 4.85%, while local high-precision potentials yield P_D = 5.7(1)%; the CD-Bonn predicts 8.00 MeV for the triton binding energy, while local potentials predict 7.62 MeV.
- The N3LO chiral NN potential (Lambda = 500 MeV) reproduces the 1999 np database (2402 data below 290 MeV) with chi-squared/datum = 1.10, compared to 10.1 at NNLO and 36.2 at NLO; for the 1999 pp database (2057 data below 290 MeV), the N3LO chi-squared/datum = 1.50, compared to 35.4 at NNLO and 80.1 at NLO.
- The Argonne V18 potential reproduces the 1999 np database with chi-squared/datum = 1.04 and the 1999 pp database with chi-squared/datum = 1.38.
- The N3LO chiral potential uses 24 contact parameters for the np potential, compared to 35 for the Nijmegen PWA93 and 38 for the CD-Bonn high-precision potential; at NNLO only 9 parameters are used, and at NLO only 9 parameters are used.
- The first nonvanishing 3NF terms appear at NNLO (order Q^3) and involve two new parameters (D and E) that must be fixed from three-nucleon observables; four-nucleon forces first appear at N3LO.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral EFT nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1999), Kaiser, Brockmann, and Weise (1997), Epelbaum et al. (1998), and Entem and Machleidt (2003). It contrasts the chiral approach with the high-precision phenomenological potentials of the 1990s (Nijmegen, Argonne V18, CD-Bonn), which fit the NN data with chi-squared/datum near 1 but use 40-50 parameters and lack a direct connection to QCD. The work enables consistent microscopic nuclear structure calculations using chiral two- and many-body forces, with first applications to light nuclei already producing promising results (Nogga et al., 2004; Kowalski et al., 2004; Dean et al., 2004).
