# Improved Approximations for Fermion Pair Production in Inhomogeneous Electric Fields

> Reformulating the instantons in a complex plane for tunneling or transmitting states, we calculate the pair-production rate of charged fermions in a spatially localized electric field, illustrated by the Sauter electric field E_0 sech^2 (z/L), and in a temporally localized electric field such as E_0 sech^2 (t/T). The integration of the quadratic part of WKB instanton actions over the frequency and transverse momentum leads to the pair-production rate obtained by the worldline instanton method, including the prefactor, of Phys. Rev. D72, 105004 (2005) and D73, 065028 (2006). It is further shown that the WKB instanton action plus the next-to-leading order contribution in spinor QED equals the WKB instanton action in scalar QED, thus justifying why the WKB instanton in scalar QED can work for the pair production of fermions. Finally we obtain the pair-production rate in a spatially localized electric field together with a constant magnetic field in the same direction.

# Improved Approximations for Fermion Pair Production in Inhomogeneous Electric Fields

## Key claim

The WKB instanton action plus the next-to-leading order contribution in spinor QED equals the WKB instanton action in scalar QED, justifying why the scalar QED WKB instanton can accurately describe fermion pair production. This equivalence holds for both spatially localized (Sauter-type) and temporally localized electric fields, up to the order of $\mathcal{O}(\delta^3 \epsilon^6)$.

## Method

The authors reformulate the instanton method as a gauge-independent contour integral in the complex plane of space or time, using the phase-integral (WKB) method to avoid divergence problems at turning points. They calculate the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) WKB instanton actions for charged fermions in the Sauter electric field $E_0 \text{sech}^2(z/L)$ and the temporally localized field $E_0 \text{sech}^2(t/T)$, then integrate over frequency and transverse momentum to obtain the pair-production rate. The method is extended to include a constant magnetic field parallel to the electric field.

## Key numbers

- For the Sauter electric field with $\omega = 0$ and $\mathbf{k}_\perp = 0$, the leading-order WKB instanton action is $\mathcal{S}^{(0)}_{\{0\}} = Z(1 - \sqrt{1 - \epsilon^2})$, where $Z = 2\pi q E_0 L^2 = 2/(\delta \epsilon^2)$ and $\epsilon = m/(q E_0 L)$.
- For the temporally localized field with $k_z = 0$ and $\mathbf{k}_\perp = 0$, the action is $\mathcal{S}^{(0)}_{\{0\}} = Z_t(\sqrt{1 + \epsilon_t^2} - 1)$, where $Z_t = 2\pi q E_0 T^2 = 2/(\delta \epsilon_t^2)$ and $\epsilon_t = m/(q E_0 T)$.
- The pair-production rate per unit time and unit cross-sectional area for the Sauter electric field in scalar QED (with factor 2 for two charged scalar fields) is $\mathcal{N}^{(0)} \approx \frac{(qE_0)^{5/2}L}{4\pi^3 m} (1 - \epsilon^2)^{5/4} e^{-Z(1 - \sqrt{1 - \epsilon^2})} [1 - \frac{5}{16}(4+3\epsilon^2)b + \frac{105}{512}(4-\epsilon^2)^2 b^2 - \frac{315}{8192}(320-432\epsilon^2+124\epsilon^4-\epsilon^6)b^3]$, where $b = \delta/\sqrt{1-\epsilon^2}$ and $\delta = qE_0/(\pi m^2)$.
- The pair-production density for the temporally localized field is $\mathcal{N}^{(0)} \approx \frac{(qE_0)^{5/2}T}{4\pi^3 m} (1 + \epsilon_t^2)^{5/4} e^{-Z_t(\sqrt{1 + \epsilon_t^2} - 1)} [1 - \frac{5}{16}(4 - 3\epsilon_t^2)b_t]$, where $b_t = \delta/\sqrt{1 + \epsilon_t^2}$.
- For the Sauter electric field with a constant magnetic field $B$ in the same direction, the pair-production rate per unit area and unit time is $\mathcal{N}^{(0)} \approx \frac{qBL(qE_0)^{3/2}}{4\pi^2 m} (1 - \epsilon^2)^{3/4} e^{-Z(1 - \sqrt{1 - \epsilon^2})} \coth\left(\frac{\pi B}{E_0 (1 - \epsilon^2)^{1/2}}\right)$.
- The NLO WKB contribution in spinor QED for the Sauter field is $\mathcal{S}^{(2)}_{sp, \mathbf{k}_\perp} = -\frac{\pi^2 \delta \epsilon^2}{4\sqrt{1 + i\pi \delta \epsilon^2}}$, whose real part cancels the excess in the leading-order real spinor action up to $\mathcal{O}(\delta^3 \epsilon^6)$.

## Context

This work extends the instanton method of Kim and Page (2002, 2006) by reformulating instanton actions as gauge-independent contour integrals in the complex plane, and it agrees with the worldline instanton method of Dunne and Schubert (2005, 2006) including the prefactor. The results for the Sauter electric field are compared with the exact result of Nikishov (1970). The paper enables improved calculations of pair production in inhomogeneous electromagnetic fields relevant to upcoming laser experiments at facilities such as the Linac Coherent Light Source at SLAC and TESLA at DESY, and it provides a theoretical justification for using scalar QED WKB instantons to compute fermion pair production rates.
