# Nuclear Responses to Electro-Weak Probes and In-Medium Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We discuss two topics concerning the application of chiral perturbation theory to nuclear physics: (1) the latest developments in the study of possible kaon condensation in dense baryonic systems; (2) nuclear responses to electro-weak probes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral perturbation theory (CPT) provides a systematic framework for organizing nuclear dynamics, but the critical density for kaon condensation remains uncertain because the in-medium nucleon mass effect (the "mN* effect") has not been treated consistently beyond the leading 1/mN expansion, and because off-shell ambiguities in the pion-nucleon t-matrix persist in approximate calculations.

## Method

The authors apply heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to two problems: (1) kaon condensation in dense baryonic matter, where they analyze the chiral counting of the nucleon effective mass effect and off-shell ambiguities in the meson-nucleon scattering amplitude; (2) nuclear responses to electro-weak probes, where they use chiral counting to organize exchange-current contributions to nuclear currents, comparing with the conventional meson-exchange approach of Towner (1992) and with exact A=2 calculations.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for kaon condensation from the latest calculation is rho_c approximately 4 rho_0 (where rho_0 is normal nuclear matter density); with Brown-Rho scaling included, rho_c can be as low as 2 rho_0.
- For the Walecka model estimate of the mN* effect, the effective kaon mass mK*(rho -> infinity) is greater than or approximately 0.45 mK, and the effect becomes significant for mN* less than approximately 0.75 rho_0.
- For the nuclear axial-charge operator A_0, the ratio of exchange-current contribution to one-body contribution is delta_mec = 0.6 to 0.8 from systematic analyses of first-forbidden beta transitions by Warburton (1991) and Warburton and Towner (1994).
- For radiative capture n + p -> d + gamma, the experimental capture rate is sigma_exp = 334.2 +/- 0.5 mb, the impulse approximation prediction is sigma_IA = 302.5 +/- 4.0 mb, and the next-to-leading order CPT calculation by Park et al. (1995) gives sigma = 334 +/- 2 mb.

## Context

This paper extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to nuclear physics, building on the work of Weinberg (1990) for NN potentials and of Rho (1991) for exchange currents. It addresses the ongoing debate about kaon condensation initiated by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and the off-shell ambiguity problem raised by Yabu et al. (1993). The results enable more reliable calculations of neutrino-deuteron cross sections needed for the SNO heavy-water Cerenkov detector, and they clarify the relationship between the HBChPT approach and the Walecka model treatment of in-medium nucleon mass effects.
