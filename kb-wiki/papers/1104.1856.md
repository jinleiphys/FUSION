# Deformation effect on total reaction cross sections for neutron-rich Ne-isotopes

> Isotope-dependence of measured reaction cross sections in scattering of $^{28-32}$Ne isotopes from $^{12}$C target at 240 MeV/nucleon is analyzed by the double-folding model with the Melbourne $g$-matrix. The density of projectile is calculated by the mean-field model with the deformed Wood-Saxon potential. The deformation is evaluated by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The deformation of projectile enhances calculated reaction cross sections to the measured values.

## Key claim

The deformation of neutron-rich Ne isotopes, as evaluated by antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), enhances calculated total reaction cross sections to match measured values, but the model still underestimates the cross section for 31Ne, suggesting an extra weak-binding effect for neutrons may be important for that isotope.

## Method

The authors analyzed the isotope dependence of measured reaction cross sections for 28-32Ne scattering from a 12C target at 240 MeV/nucleon using the double-folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Projectile densities were calculated using a mean-field model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with deformation parameters determined from AMD calculations. Only the spherical (angle-averaged) part of the projectile density was used in the folding, as the non-spherical correction to the reaction cross section was shown to be negligible (0.01% for 30Ne).

## Key numbers

- For 12C+12C scattering at 250.8 MeV/nucleon, the Melbourne g-matrix gives a reaction cross section of 796 mb, compared to the experimental value of 782.0 ± 10 mb; the Love-Franey t-matrix (no medium effect) gives 918 mb
- The normalization factor F = 0.982 brings the Melbourne g-matrix result into agreement with the 12C+12C data
- For 27Al+12C at 250.7 MeV/nucleon, the calculated cross section with the normalization factor is 1164 mb, consistent with the experimental value of 1159 ± 14 mb
- Deformation parameters β2 used: 28Ne = -0.291 (oblate), 29Ne = 0.445, 30Ne = 0.400, 31Ne = 0.422, 32Ne = 0.335
- The increase in RMS radii from deformation is only 4-6%
- The difference in observed reaction cross sections between 31Ne and 30Ne is 86 mb, while the direct one-neutron removal cross section measurement gives 79 mb (Nakamura)
- The calculated difference in reaction cross sections between 31Ne and 30Ne is 41 mb, smaller than the experimental results
- For 31Ne, the last-odd-neutron single-particle energy is -1.947 MeV in the deformed WS potential with β2 = 0.422
- The measured separation energy of 31Ne is 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of reaction cross sections for neutron-rich Ne isotopes near the "Island of inversion" region, where the N=20 magic number is no longer valid and strong deformations are expected (Warburton, Mot95, Caurier, Utsuno, Iwas01, Yana03). The authors use AMD deformation parameters from Kimura to improve upon spherical mean-field calculations (Gogny D1S HF) that fail to reproduce the measured cross sections. The results enable future studies of weak-binding effects in 31Ne, which is the heaviest experimentally suggested halo nucleus (Nakamura), and demonstrate that reaction cross section measurements are sensitive to small (4-6%) changes in RMS radii from deformation.
