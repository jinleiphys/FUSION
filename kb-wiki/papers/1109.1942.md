# Kinematics of a cascade decay for the precision measurement of $^{29}{\rm Si}$ binding energy

> Comparison of a Penning trap and a flat-crystal spectrometer experiments gives a direct test of $E=mc^2$. The result is $1-Δmc^2/E =(-1.4 \pm 4.4) \times 10^{-7}$ for $^{29}{\rm Si}$ and $^{33}{\rm S}$. The dominant uncertainty is on the $γ$-ray measurement in neutron capture reactions, and the secondary $γ$-ray has the uncertainty 4.0 eV for $^{29}{\rm Si}$. We calculated the Doppler effect of the secondary $γ$-ray as $-646.9 \cos θ$ eV from the relativistic energy momentum relation of the $^{28}{\rm Si}(n,γ)^{29}{\rm Si}$ reaction. This corresponds to the full wave of half maximum of 431.3 eV. The error 4.0 eV comes mainly from the Bragg angle measurement between the centroids of the linewidths which means that only the most probable part of the whole data has been considered. It is necessary to confirm the assumption of the isotropy for the object to measure. We discussed a coincidence measurement as one of the methods to overcome the assumption.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reported 4.0 eV uncertainty on the secondary gamma-ray energy for 29Si from flat-crystal spectrometer measurements (Dewey 2006) is inconsistent with the calculated Doppler broadening of 431.3 eV, and that this discrepancy (1.2 sigma) arises from an unverified assumption of isotropy in the centroid measurement rather than from genuine precision.

## Method

The authors derived the relativistic energy-momentum conservation equations for the cascade decay 28Si(n,gamma)29Si* -> 29Si + gamma, calculating the Doppler shift of the secondary gamma-ray as a function of the angle between the primary and secondary photons. They computed the angular correlation function for the dipole-dipole transition through nuclear states with spins J_A=J_C=1/2 and J_B=3/2, and showed that averaging measurements at angles 0 and 180 degrees eliminates the Doppler shift contribution to the binding energy determination.

## Key numbers

- The test of E=mc^2 gives 1 - Delta mc^2/E = (-1.4 +/- 4.4) x 10^{-7} for 29Si and 33S
- The Doppler shift of the secondary gamma-ray is -646.9 cos(theta) eV, corresponding to a full width at half maximum of 431.3 eV
- The reported uncertainty on the secondary gamma-ray energy is 4.0 eV for 29Si (Dewey 2006)
- The velocity of the intermediate 29Si nucleus after primary gamma emission is 39 km/s
- The incident neutron velocity is 114 m/s (flux energy 0.056 eV), giving a negligible Doppler shift of -1.3 cos(theta) eV
- The primary gamma-ray energy is 3538966.3(1.6) eV and the secondary is 4933946.3(4.0) eV
- To achieve 4.0 eV uncertainty from linewidths alone would require more than 2(431.3/4.0)^2 ≈ 23252 measurements
- The lifetime of the 4943 keV state is 1.16 fs, corresponding to a natural linewidth of 0.57 eV
- The 6111 keV profile of 36Cl shows a linewidth of 93.5 eV for Delta theta = 0.01 arcsec

## Context

This work extends the comparison between Penning trap mass measurements (Rainville 2004, Shi 2005) and flat-crystal spectrometer gamma-ray measurements (Dewey 2006) that tests E=mc^2. The authors identify a specific problem with the 29Si binding energy determination: the assumption of isotropic recoil used to extract the 4.0 eV centroid uncertainty from a 431.3 eV broadened linewidth. They propose a coincidence measurement of the two cascade gamma-rays with angular correlation analysis as a method to eliminate the Doppler broadening assumption, which would also enable lifetime measurements via the Doppler shift attenuation method (Lieb 1988, Borner 1993).
