# Theoretical study of triaxial shapes of neutron-rich Mo and Ru nuclei

> Background: Recently, transition quadrupole moments in rotational bands of even-mass neutron-rich isotopes of molybdenum and ruthenium nuclei have been measured. The new data have provided a challenge for theoretical descriptions invoking stable triaxial deformations. Purpose: To understand experimental data on rotational bands in the neutron-rich Mo-Ru region, we carried out theoretical analysis of moments of inertia, shapes, and transition quadrupole moments of neutron-rich even-even nuclei around $^{110}$Ru using self-consistent mean-field and shell model techniques. Methods: To describe yrast structures in Mo and Ru isotopes, we use nuclear Density Functional Theory (DFT) with the optimized energy density functional UNEDF0. We also apply Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) to describe yrast and positive-parity, near-yrast band structures. Results: Our self-consistent DFT calculations predict triaxial ground-state deformations in $^{106,108}$Mo and $^{108.110,112}$Ru and reproduce the observed low-frequency behavior of moments of inertia. As the rotational frequency increases, a negative-$γ$ structure, associated with the aligned $ν(h_{11/2})^2$ pair, becomes energetically favored. The computed transition quadrupole moments vary with angular momentum, which reflects deformation changes with rotation; those variations are consistent with experiment. The TPSM calculations explain the observed band structures assuming stable triaxial shapes. Conclusions: The structure of neutron-rich even-even nuclei around $^{110}$Ru is consistent with triaxial shape deformations. Our DFT and TPSM frameworks provide a consistent and complementary description of experimental data.

# Theoretical study of triaxial shapes of neutron-rich Mo and Ru nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the high-spin behavior of neutron-rich even-even Mo and Ru isotopes around ¹¹⁰Ru is consistent with stable triaxial rotation, but acknowledges that the predicted triaxial ground-state minima are fairly shallow, which may explain why some other calculations (e.g., cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory) slightly favor axial configurations.

## Method

The authors used two complementary theoretical frameworks: (1) cranked self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations with the UNEDF0 Skyrme energy density functional, including Lipkin-Nogami pairing corrections, to compute potential energy surfaces, routhians, and equilibrium deformations; and (2) the Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM), which projects good angular-momentum states from triaxially deformed Nilsson+BCS quasiparticle configurations and diagonalizes a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian. At rotational frequencies above 0.3 MeV, pairing was neglected in the CHFB calculations and diabatic configurations were defined by occupation numbers in parity-signature blocks.

## Key numbers

- Triaxial ground-state deformations predicted for ¹⁰⁶Mo (β₂=0.19, γ=16°), ¹⁰⁸Mo (β₂=0.18, γ=18°), ¹⁰⁸Ru (β₂=0.16, γ=24°), ¹¹⁰Ru (β₂=0.16, γ=25°), and ¹¹²Ru (β₂=0.15, γ=24°) from CHFB+UNEDF0 calculations.
- The alignment of ν(h₁₁/₂)² and π(g₉/₂)² pairs occurs at similar rotational frequencies of ħω ≈ 0.3 MeV in both ¹⁰⁶Mo and ¹¹²Ru.
- In ¹⁰⁸Ru, β₂ increases from 0.15 at ħω=0 to 0.17 at ħω=0.3 MeV due to centrifugal stretching.
- TPSM calculations used γ values of 20° for ¹⁰⁶Mo, 25° for ¹⁰⁸Mo, 29° for ¹⁰⁸Ru, 28° for ¹¹⁰Ru, and 25° for ¹¹²Ru.
- The first ν(h₁₁/₂)² neutron alignment in TPSM is predicted around I=8, and the transition to a 4-quasiparticle ν(h₁₁/₂)²π(g₉/₂)² band around I=16.
- For ¹⁰⁶Mo, TPSM calculations at γ=5°, 15°, 20°, and 25° show that γ=20° best reproduces experimental band structures and transition quadrupole moments.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of triaxiality in the neutron-rich Mo-Ru region, including macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Skalski (1997) and Moller (2006, 2008), HFB+D1S calculations by Guzman (2010), and TPSM analyses by Bhat (2012) and Liu (2013). It directly addresses the challenge posed by recent experimental measurements of transition quadrupole moments by Snyder (2013), which cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations could not reproduce. The results enable future investigations of wobbling motion and chiral bands in these nuclei, and provide a benchmark for testing other theoretical approaches such as the interacting boson model.
