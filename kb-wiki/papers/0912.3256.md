# Hydrodynamics of fundamental matter

> First and second order transport coefficients are calculated for the strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma coupled to massless fundamental matter in the Veneziano limit. The results, including among others the value of the bulk viscosity and some relaxation times, are presented at next-to-leading order in the flavor contribution. The bulk viscosity is found to saturate Buchel's bound. This result is also captured by an effective single-scalar five-dimensional holographic dual in the Chamblin-Reall class and it is suggested to hold, in the limit of small deformations, for generic plasmas with gravity duals, whenever the leading conformality breaking effects are driven by marginally (ir)relevant operators. This proposal is then extended to other relations for hydrodynamic coefficients, which are conjectured to be universal for every non-conformal plasma with a dual Chamblin-Reall-like description. Our analysis extends to any strongly coupled gauge theory describing the low energy dynamics of Nc>>1 D3-branes at the tip of a generic Calabi-Yau cone. The fundamental fields are added by means of 1<<Nf<<Nc homogeneously smeared D7-branes.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of the strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma with massless fundamental matter saturates Buchel's bound at next-to-leading order in the flavor expansion parameter epsilon_h, and this saturation is suggested to hold generically for plasmas with gravity duals when conformality breaking is driven by marginally (ir)relevant operators in the limit of small deformations.

## Method

Transport coefficients were calculated using holographic duality, by studying fluctuations of the five-dimensional effective action obtained from dimensional reduction of the ten-dimensional D3-D7 brane system over the internal manifold X5. The perturbations were classified into tensorial, vectorial, and scalar channels according to their transformation under the SO(2) little group, and the retarded correlators were extracted from the on-shell boundary action after adding appropriate counterterms to cancel UV divergences. The calculations were performed perturbatively in the flavor expansion parameter epsilon_h up to order epsilon_h^2.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity to shear viscosity ratio: zeta/eta = (1/9) epsilon_h^2, where epsilon_h = (1/(8 pi^2)) lambda_h N_f/N_c for the N=4 case
- Effective relaxation time: tau^eff T = tau_{pi,0} T_0 + ((16 - pi^2)/(128 pi)) epsilon_h^2, with tau_{pi,0} T_0 = (2 - log 2)/(2 pi)
- Speed of sound: c_s = 1/sqrt(3) (1 - (1/12) epsilon_h^2)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 1/(4 pi) (unchanged from conformal value at this order)
- Temperature: T = T_0 (1 - (1/8) epsilon_h - (13/384) epsilon_h^2)
- Interaction measure: epsilon - 3p = (p_0/2) epsilon_h^2
- The combination T^2 kappa / p equals the conformal value T_0^2 kappa_0 / p_0 = 1/pi^2 at this order

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of transport coefficients in theories without fundamental flavors (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2005) to include massless fundamental matter in the Veneziano limit, building on the D3-D7 brane solutions of Bigazzi (2005) and Casero (2006). The results provide the first manageable toy models for the QCD plasma in its near-conformal regime, enabling comparisons with lattice QCD results for bulk viscosity (Meyer 2007). The authors conjecture universal relations for transport coefficients in all non-conformal plasmas with a dual Chamblin-Reall effective description, extending the framework of Gubser (2008) and Nellore (2009) to marginally (ir)relevant deformations.
