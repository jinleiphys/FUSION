# Lorentz-covariant nonlocal collision term for spin-1/2 particles

> We revisit the derivation of the nonlocal collision term in the Boltzmann equation for spin-1/2 particles, using both the Wigner-function approach by de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert, and the Kadanoff-Baym equation in $T$-matrix approximation. Contrary to previous calculations, our results maintain full Lorentz covariance of the nonlocal collision term.

## Key claim

The authors derive a nonlocal collision term for spin-1/2 particles in the Boltzmann equation that maintains full Lorentz covariance, correcting previous calculations where the Berry curvature term explicitly depended on a non-covariant frame vector \(\hat{t}^\mu\).

## Method

The authors revisit the derivation using both the Wigner-function approach of de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert (GLW) and the Kadanoff-Baym equation in \(T\)-matrix approximation, now keeping momentum derivatives of Dirac spinors in the scattering matrix elements that were previously neglected. For a current-current interaction of Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type, they obtain Lorentz-covariant expressions for the space-time shifts \(\Delta^\mu, \Delta'^\mu, \Delta_1^\mu, \Delta_2^\mu\) that replace the earlier frame-dependent Berry curvature term.

## Key numbers

- The nonlocal space-time shift in the previous work was \(\Delta_\mu = \frac{\hbar}{2m(p\cdot\hat{t}+m)} \epsilon_{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} \hat{t}^\nu p^\alpha \ms^\beta\), which depends on the non-covariant frame vector \(\hat{t}^\nu = (1,\boldsymbol{0})\).
- The Lorentz-covariant shifts for NJL-type interactions are given by Eqs. (4a–4d), each proportional to \(\frac{\hbar}{m} \frac{4 G_c G_d}{\hbar^2}\frac{m^4}{\mathcal{W}}\) times traces of Dirac matrices and projectors.
- The transition probability \(\mathcal{W}\) in Eq. (5) is \(\mathcal{W} = m^4 \frac{G_c G_d}{\hbar^2} 16 \, \mathrm{Re}\{\mathrm{Tr}[h \Gamma^{(d)} h_2 \Gamma^{(c)} \bar{h}] \mathrm{Tr}[\Gamma^{(d)} h_1\Gamma^{(c)}h'] - \mathrm{Tr}[h \Gamma^{(d)} h_1\Gamma^{(c)} h' \Gamma^{(d)} h_2 \Gamma^{(c)}\bar{h}]\}\).
- The spin-space integration measure satisfies \(\int \d S(p) = 2\), \(\int \d S(p)\, \ms^\mu = 0\), and \(\int \d S(p) \, \ms^\mu \ms^\nu = -2(g^{\mu\nu} - p^\mu p^\nu/p^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the spin kinetic theory developed by Weickgenannt et al. (2020, 2021) and de Groot, van Leeuwen, and van Weert (1980), where the nonlocal collision term previously contained a Berry curvature that violated Lorentz covariance. The corrected Lorentz-covariant collision term enables a consistent derivation of relativistic spin hydrodynamics via Chapman-Enskog expansion or the method of moments, and resolves a fundamental inconsistency in the description of spin-orbit angular momentum conversion in relativistic fluids.
