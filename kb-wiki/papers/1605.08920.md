# Odd-even staggering in neutron drip line nuclei

> We have done systematic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations in coordinate space on the one-quasi-particle energies and binding energy odd-even staggering (OES) in semi-magic nuclei with the zero-range volume, mixed and surface pairing forces in order to explore the influence of their density dependence. The odd-$N$ isotopes are calculated within the blocking scheme. The strengths for the pairing forces are determined in two schemes by fitting locally to reproduce pairing gap in $^{120}$Sn and globally to all available data on the OES of semi-magic nuclei with $Z\geq8$. In the former calculations, there is a noticeable difference between the neutron mean gaps in neutron-rich O, Ca, Ni and Sn isotopes calculated with the surface pairing and those with the mixed and volume pairing. The difference gets much smaller if the globally optimized pairing strengths are employed. The heavier Pb isotopes show the opposite trend. Moreover, large differences between the mean gap and the OES may be expected in both calculations when one goes towards the neutron drip line.

## Key claim
Systematic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate space show that the density dependence of the zero-range pairing force (volume, mixed, or surface) has a noticeable effect on the neutron mean gaps in neutron-rich O, Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes only when the pairing strengths are fitted locally to a single nucleus (¹²⁰Sn); this difference is largely suppressed when globally optimized pairing strengths are employed.

## Method
The authors performed spherical HFB calculations in coordinate space using the HFBRAD solver with the SLy4 Skyrme functional for the particle-hole channel. They employed three zero-range pairing forces (volume, mixed, and surface) and calculated one-quasi-particle energies and binding energy odd-even staggering (OES) for semi-magic isotopic chains (O, Ca, Ni, Sn, Pb). The odd-N isotopes were treated using the blocking scheme within the equal filling approximation, and the pairing strengths were determined in two schemes: by fitting to the pairing gap in ¹²⁰Sn and by a global fit to OES data for semi-magic nuclei with Z ≥ 8.

## Key numbers
- The pairing strengths fitted to the mean neutron gap of 1.31 MeV in ¹²⁰Sn are -189.2, -287.85, and -517.5 MeV fm³ for the volume, mixed, and surface pairing forces, respectively.
- For ¹²⁰Sn, the neutron pairing energy calculated with the surface pairing force (-28.161 MeV) differs by as much as 300-400 keV between the HFBRAD and HFBTHO (THO basis) codes.
- The maximum angular momentum for quasi-particle orbitals was taken as j_max = 25/2 for all nuclei heavier than oxygen, and j_max = 21/2 for O isotopes.
- Calculations were performed in a spherical box with a size of r = 30 fm and a step size of h = 0.2 fm (and 0.1 fm for the global fit section).

## Context
This work extends systematic HFB studies of pairing correlations in neutron-rich nuclei, such as those by Dobaczewski et al. (2001, 2002) and Bennaceur et al. (2002), which suggested that the density dependence of the pairing force may lead to drastic differences near the drip line. It contrasts with earlier HF+BCS calculations by PhysRevC.79.034306 (2009) which found no significant difference between different pairing forces. The results enable a more precise assessment of theoretical uncertainties in predicting the location of the neutron drip line and the properties of weakly bound orbitals, particularly by comparing coordinate-space solutions with those from harmonic oscillator bases.
