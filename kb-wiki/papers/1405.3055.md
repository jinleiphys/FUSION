# Hydrodynamic extension of the two component model for hadroproduction in heavy-ion collisions

> The dependence of the spectra shape of produced charged hadrons on the size of a colliding system is discussed using a two component model. As a result, the hierarchy by the system-size in the spectra shape is observed. Next, the hydrodynamic extension of the two component model for hadroproduction using recent theoretical calculations is suggested to describe the spectra of charged particles produced in heavy-ion collisions in the full range of transverse momenta, $p_T$. Data from heavy-ion collisions measured at RHIC and LHC are analyzed using the introduced approach and are combined in terms of energy density. The observed regularities might be explained by the formation of QGP during the collision.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the two-component model for hadroproduction, which uses an exponential plus a power-law term for proton-proton collisions, must be extended with a second power-law term and a hydrodynamic treatment of the exponential term to describe charged hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions across the full transverse momentum range; the observed regularities *might* be explained by the formation of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) during the collision.

## Method

The authors fit charged hadron spectra from lead-lead collisions at the LHC (ALICE, sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV) and gold-gold collisions at RHIC (PHENIX, sqrt(s) = 200 and 130 GeV) using a parameterization consisting of a hydrodynamic Bessel-function term for thermalized particles plus two power-law terms for mini-jet fragmentation. They extract the temperature parameter T_e from the hydrodynamic term and study its dependence on the estimated energy density of the collision, using a parameterization epsilon = epsilon_0 * (s/s_0)^(alpha/2) * N_coll^beta with alpha = 0.3 and beta = 0.5.

## Key numbers

- The surface velocity parameter beta_s in the hydrodynamic term is taken as 0.5 c, consistent with previous observations.
- The energy density parameterization uses alpha = 0.3 and beta = 0.5, with sqrt(s_0) = 200 GeV.
- The freeze-out temperature T_fo extracted from the data reaches approximately 145 MeV for high energy densities.
- The Bag model fit to T_e versus energy density yields B = 0.25 GeV/fm^3.
- The critical temperature for QGP from other calculations is cited as T_c ~ 155-160 MeV.
- The energy loss of jets passing through the nucleus is estimated as dE/dz * R_A ~ 7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-component model previously applied to gamma-gamma and proton-proton collisions (Bylinkin and Rostovtsev, 2014) to heavy-ion collisions, where collective motion requires a hydrodynamic treatment (using calculations from the hydrodynamic literature). The observed saturation of the freeze-out temperature near 145 MeV and the T_e proportional to (epsilon - B)^(1/4) behavior are interpreted as consistent with QGP formation and the Bag model equation of state. The approach enables systematic comparison of RHIC and LHC data on a common energy-density scale, and suggests that peripheral LHC collisions reach energy densities similar to central RHIC collisions.
