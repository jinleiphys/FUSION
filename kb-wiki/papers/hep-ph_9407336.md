# A nonlocal, covariant generalisation of the NJL model

> We solve a nonlocal generalisation of the NJL model in the Hartree approximation. This model has a separable interaction, as suggested by instanton models of the QCD vacuum. The choice of form factor in this interaction is motivated by the confining nature of the vacuum. A conserved axial current is constructed in the chiral limit of the model and the pion properties are shown to satisfy the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation. For reasonable values of the parameters the model exhibits quark confinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nonlocal, separable generalization of the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, with a Gaussian form factor motivated by confinement, can simultaneously satisfy the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation and produce quark confinement for reasonable parameter values, but only when the full conserved axial current (including nonlocal contributions) is used rather than the local piece alone.

## Method

The authors solve a nonlocal NJL model with a separable four-point interaction in the Hartree approximation, using a Gaussian form factor \(f(p)=\exp(-p^2/\Lambda^2)\) as a regulator. They construct a conserved axial current via a Noether-like method with straight-line paths, compute the pion mass and decay constant from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and fix the three parameters (\(G\), \(\Lambda\), \(m_c\)) by requiring \(m_\pi=140\) MeV and \(F_\pi=93\) MeV. The dynamical quark mass \(M(p)\) is obtained from the gap equation, and confinement is diagnosed by the absence of real poles in the Minkowski-space quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass fixed to 140 MeV and pion decay constant fixed to 93 MeV for all parameter sets.
* Dynamical quark mass \(M_0(0)\) ranges from 200 MeV to 500 MeV, with corresponding cut-off \(\Lambda\) ranging from 2222 MeV down to 623 MeV.
* Quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle^{1/3}\) ranges from \(-330\) MeV (for \(M_0(0)=200\) MeV) to \(-165\) MeV (for \(M_0(0)=500\) MeV).
* Current quark mass \(m_c\) ranges from 2.4 MeV to 18.9 MeV across the parameter sets.
* Confinement threshold in the chiral limit: \(M_0(0)/\Lambda > e^{-1/2}/2\), corresponding to \(M_0(0)\) above about 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based nonlocal NJL model of Diakonov and Petrov (1985) by using a Gaussian form factor that yields quark confinement, unlike the instanton form factor. It corrects the earlier approach by explicitly constructing the conserved axial current, showing that the Pagels–Stokar (1979) expression for \(F_\pi\) is incomplete without nonlocal contributions, a point also noted by Holdom and coworkers (1989, 1992). The model enables future studies of meson properties and chiral symmetry breaking in a covariant, confining framework without the ambiguities of cut-off regularization, and is contrasted with the non-covariant instantaneous model of the Rostock group (Schmidt et al., 1993) and the square-root form factor model of Buballa and Krewald (1992).
