# Pentaquarks from intrinsic charms in $Λ_b$ decays

> We study the three-body $Λ_b$ decays of $Λ_b\to J/ψpM$ with $M=K^-$ and $π^-$. The two new states ${\cal P}_{c1}\equiv {\cal P}_c(4380)^+$ and ${\cal P}_{c2}\equiv {\cal P}_c(4450)^+$ observed recently as the resonances in the $J/ψp$ invariant mass spectrum of $Λ_b\to J/ψp K^-$ can be identified to consist of five quarks, $uudc\bar c$, being consistent with the existence of the pentaquark states. We argue that, in the doubly charmful $Λ_b$ decays of $Λ_b\to J/ψpK^-$ through $b\to c\bar c s$, apart from those through the non-resonant $Λ_b\to pK^-$ and resonant $Λ_b\to Λ^*\to pK^-$ transitions, the third contribution with the non-factorizable effects is not the dominant part for the resonant $Λ_b\to K^-{\cal P}_{c1,c2}, {\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to J/ψp$ processes, such that we propose that the ${\cal P}_{c1,c2}$ productions are mainly from the charmless $Λ_b$ decays through $b\to \bar u u s$, in which the $c\bar c$ content in ${\cal P}_{c1,c2}$ arises from the intrinsic charms within the $Λ_b$ baryon. We hence predict the observables related to the branching ratios and the direct CP violating asymmetries to be ${\cal B}(Λ_b\to π^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to) J/ψp)/{\cal B}(Λ_b\to K^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to) J/ψp)=0.8\pm 0.1$, ${\cal A}_{CP}(Λ_b\to π^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to)J/ψp)=(-3.9\pm 0.2)\%$, and ${\cal A}_{CP}(Λ_b\to K^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to)J/ψp)=(5.8\pm 0.2)\%$, which can alleviate the inconsistency between the theoretical expectations from the three contributions in the doubly charmful $Λ_b$ decays and the observed data.

# Pentaquarks from intrinsic charms in $\Lambda_b$ decays

## Key claim
The two pentaquark states ${\cal P}_c(4380)^+$ and ${\cal P}_c(4450)^+$ observed in $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi p K^-$ decays may originate primarily from charmless $\Lambda_b$ decays through $b\to \bar u u s$, with the $c\bar c$ content arising from intrinsic charm within the $\Lambda_b$ baryon, rather than from the doubly charmful $b\to c\bar c s$ processes that are usually assumed.

## Method
The authors analyze the three-body $\Lambda_b$ decays $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi p M$ with $M=K^-$ and $\pi^-$, comparing contributions from doubly charmful decays (non-resonant, resonant via $\Lambda^*$ or $N^*$, and non-factorizable effects) with a new mechanism: charmless $\Lambda_b$ decays via $b\to \bar u u s$ where the $c\bar c$ pair in the pentaquark comes from intrinsic charm Fock states in the $\Lambda_b$ baryon. They perform a fit to LHCb data on branching ratios and CP asymmetries using effective Hamiltonian formalism with Wilson coefficients, extracting parameters $F_K$, $F_{{\cal P}_{c1}}$, $F_{{\cal P}_{c2}}$, and the strong phase $\delta_1$.

## Key numbers
- Predicted ratio ${\cal B}(\Lambda_b\to \pi^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to) J/\psi p)/{\cal B}(\Lambda_b\to K^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to) J/\psi p) = 0.58 \pm 0.05$
- Predicted direct CP asymmetry ${\cal A}_{CP}(\Lambda_b\to \pi^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to)J/\psi p) = (-7.4 \pm 0.9)\%$
- Predicted direct CP asymmetry ${\cal A}_{CP}(\Lambda_b\to K^-({\cal P}_{c1,c2}\to)J/\psi p) = (+6.3 \pm 0.2)\%$
- Fitted branching fraction ${\cal B}(\Lambda_b\to J/\psi p \pi^-) = (2.68 \pm 0.34) \times 10^{-5}$, compared to the LHCb measurement of $(2.51 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.13^{+0.45}_{-0.35}) \times 10^{-5}$
- Fitted ${\cal R}_{\pi K} = (8.38 \pm 0.77)\%$, compared to the LHCb data of $(8.24 \pm 0.49)\%$
- Fitted $\Delta{\cal A}_{CP} = (2.9 \pm 1.4)\%$, compared to the LHCb data of $(5.7 \pm 2.7)\%$
- Extracted parameters: $F_K = 2.8 \pm 0.2$, $F_{{\cal P}_{c1}} = 19.6 \pm 3.1$, $F_{{\cal P}_{c2}} = 5.5 \pm 1.0$, $\delta_1 = (54.8 \pm 31.9)^\circ$

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of the pentaquark states ${\cal P}_c(4380)^+$ and ${\cal P}_c(4450)^+$ discovered by LHCb (2015) in $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi p K^-$ decays. The authors propose an alternative to the molecular state interpretation (Karliner 2015, Chen 2015, Liu 2015) by invoking intrinsic charm in the $\Lambda_b$ baryon, a concept previously applied to proton structure (Brodsky 1997) and $B$ meson decays (Chang 2001, Brodsky 2001). The mechanism resolves the discrepancy between the naive expectation ${\cal R}_{\pi K} \sim (V_{cd}/V_{cs})^2 \simeq 0.05$ from doubly charmful decays and the observed value $\sim 0.08$, and explains the non-zero $\Delta{\cal A}_{CP}$ which cannot arise from $b\to c\bar c q$ processes alone. The predictions enable experimental tests through CP asymmetry measurements in $\Lambda_b\to J/\psi p \pi^-$ decays.
