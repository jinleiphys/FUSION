# Spin response and neutrino mean free path in neutron matter

> The longitudinal and transverse density- and spin-density response functions in Pure Neutron Matter (PNM) are derived over a wide range of densities within the Time Dependent Local Spin Density Approximation (TDLSDA). The underlying density functional was derived from an Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) computation of the equation of state of unpolarized and fully spin polarized pure neutron matter. In order to assess the dependence of the results on the specific underlying nucleon-nucleon Hamiltonian, we used both the phenomenological Argonne AV8'+UIX force, and local chiral forces up to N2LO. The resulting response function has then been applied to the study of the neutrino mean free path in PNM.

## Key claim

The paper derives longitudinal and transverse density- and spin-density response functions in pure neutron matter using the Time Dependent Local Spin Density Approximation (TDLSDA), and applies them to compute the neutrino mean free path; the results depend on the specific nucleon-nucleon Hamiltonian used, with the phenomenological Argonne AV8'+UIX force and local chiral forces up to N2LO giving different predictions, particularly at higher densities.

## Method

The authors first computed the equation of state for unpolarized and fully spin-polarized pure neutron matter using Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) with two different Hamiltonians: the phenomenological AV8'+UIX potential and local chiral EFT potentials up to N2LO. They then fitted these results to a density functional with quadratic dependence on spin polarization, and used this functional within the TDLSDA framework to derive the longitudinal and transverse response functions. The response functions were then applied to calculate the neutrino mean free path in pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Density range considered: 0.04 fm^-3 up to 2ρ0, where ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is the nuclear saturation density
- Spin polarization ξ = 0.2 used for longitudinal response calculations at densities ρ = 0.08, 0.16, and 0.32 fm^-3
- AFDMC calculations for fully spin-polarized pure neutron matter used A = 33 neutrons in a periodic box
- The spin symmetry energy (difference between energy per neutron of spin polarized and spin unpolarized systems) is substantially larger in EOSA (AV8'+UIX) than in EOSχ (chiral)

## Context

This work extends the TDLDA approach previously applied to isospin channels (Lip13, Lip16) to the study of density and spin-density response functions in pure neutron matter, including arbitrary spin polarization. The TDLSDA formalism is well known in condensed matter applications (Lip03). The results enable improved calculations of neutrino mean free path in neutron star matter, which is crucial for understanding supernova neutrino emissions and neutron star cooling. The paper also assesses the robustness of predictions against the underlying Hamiltonian by comparing phenomenological (AV8'+UIX) and chiral EFT potentials, following earlier work by Gandolfi (2014) and Tews (2016) on the equation of state.
