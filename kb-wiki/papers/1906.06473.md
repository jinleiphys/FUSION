# High-K isomer and the rotational properties in the odd-Z neutron-rich nucleus $^{163}$Eu

> The newly observed isomer and ground-state band in the odd-Z neutron-rich rare-earth nucleus $^{163}$Eu are investigated by using the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing treated by the particle-number conserving (PNC) method. This is the first time detailed theoretical investigations are performed of the observed $964(1)$ keV isomer and ground-state rotational band in $^{163}$Eu. The experimental data are reproduced very well by the theoretical results. The configuration of the $964(1)$ keV isomer is assigned as the three-particle state $\frac{13}{2}^{-}(ν\frac{7}{2}^{+}[633]\otimesν\frac{1}{2}^{-}[521]\otimesπ\frac{5}{2}^{+}[413]$). More low-lying multi-particle states are predicted in $^{163}$Eu. Due to its significant effect on the nuclear mean field, the high-order $\varepsilon_{6}$ deformation plays an important role in the energy and configuration assignment of the multi-particle states. Compared to its neighboring even-even nuclei $^{162}$Sm and $^{164}$Gd, there is a $10\%\sim15\%$ increase of $J^{(1)}$ of the one-particle ground-state band in $^{163}$Eu. This is explained by the pairing reduction due to the blocking of the nucleon on the proton $π\frac{5}{2}^{+}$[413] orbital in $^{163}$Eu.

## Key claim

The 964(1) keV isomer in 163Eu is assigned as a three-particle state with configuration ν7/2+[633] ⊗ ν1/2−[521] ⊗ π5/2+[413], but this assignment depends critically on including the high-order ε6 deformation in the nuclear mean field; without ε6, the second-lowest three-particle state would be a three-proton excitation that also matches the isomer energy.

## Method

The cranked shell model (CSM) Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing was diagonalized in a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space using the particle-number conserving (PNC) method, which avoids quasi-particle transformations and exactly accounts for Pauli blocking. Nilsson parameters were taken from Lund systematics, deformation parameters from Möller’s table (with ε4 adjusted to −0.02 for 163Eu), and effective pairing strengths G0 = 0.20 MeV and G2 = 0.02 MeV for both protons and neutrons. Occupation probabilities of cranked Nilsson orbitals were used to assign configurations of multi-particle states.

## Key numbers

- The isomer energy is 964(1) keV experimentally; the PNC-CSM calculation with ε6 ≠ 0 gives 1001.3 keV, and with ε6 = 0 gives 1057.0 keV.
- The ground-state band in 163Eu shows a 10%–15% increase in kinematic moment of inertia J(1) compared to neighboring even-even nuclei 162Sm and 164Gd.
- The proton contribution J(1)_p in 163Eu is larger than in 162Sm and 164Gd by about 18% in the low frequency range.
- The three-proton excitation state π5/2+[413] ⊗ π5/2−[532] ⊗ π3/2+[411] lies at 1509.9 keV with ε6 ≠ 0, versus 1134.9 keV with ε6 = 0, ruling it out as the isomer configuration.
- Deformation parameters used: ε2 = 0.275, ε4 = −0.02, ε6 = 0.042.

## Context

This work provides the first detailed theoretical investigation of the isomer and ground-state band in 163Eu, following experimental observations by Yokoyama (2017) and Patel (2017), who disagreed on the isomer’s configuration (Yokoyama proposed a two-neutron-plus-odd-proton state via deformed Hartree-Fock with angular momentum projection; Patel proposed a three-proton state via Nilsson-BCS). The PNC-CSM results support Yokoyama’s assignment and extend the method previously applied to even-even rare-earth nuclei by He (2018). The study also predicts additional low-lying multi-particle states in 163Eu and demonstrates that high-order ε6 deformation is essential for correct configuration assignment in this mass region.
