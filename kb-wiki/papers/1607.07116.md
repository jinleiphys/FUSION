# Investigation of the $η'N$ system using the linear sigma model

> In this paper, we investigate the $η'N$ system using the three-flavor linear sigma model including the effect of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking. The $η'N$ bound state is also found in the case including flavor symmetry breaking and coupling with the $ηN$ and $πN$ channels. The $η'N$ interaction becomes more attractive with the inclusion of flavor symmetry breaking, which causes mixing between the singlet and octet scalar mesons. The existence of such a bound state would have some impact on the $η'$-nucleus system, which is of interest from both theoretical and experimental viewpoints.

## Key claim

The authors find an $\eta'N$ bound state in the three-flavor linear sigma model when flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking is included, but the result depends on the mass of the sigma meson and requires scaling down the $\pi N$ transition vertex by a factor of 0.153 to reproduce experimental cross section data.

## Method

The authors use the three-flavor linear sigma model with nucleons to evaluate $\eta'N$, $\eta N$, and $\pi N$ interactions at tree level, including the effects of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking through non-degenerate quark masses and scalar meson mixing. They project the interaction onto s-wave, solve a coupled-channel scattering equation using dimensional regularization, and find the pole of the T-matrix in the complex energy plane to identify bound states.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave $\eta'N$ interaction at threshold is $V_{\eta'N\rightarrow\eta'N} = -7.14 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-1}$, more attractive than the previous calculation without flavor symmetry breaking which gave $-0.054$ MeV$^{-1}$
- The $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle is found to be $\theta_{ps} = -6.2^\circ$ for $m_\sigma = 700$ MeV
- The pole position of the $\eta'N$ bound state is $1839.7 - 7.2i$ MeV, corresponding to a binding energy of $57.0 - 7.2i$ MeV for $m_\sigma = 700$ MeV
- The scattering length is $a = -0.98 + 5.7 \times 10^{-2}i$ fm and the effective range is $r_e = 0.22 - 6.8 \times 10^{-3}i$ fm
- Varying the sigma meson mass from 610 to 743 MeV changes the binding energy from $74.6 - 3.6i$ MeV to $48.7 - 10.6i$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Sakai 2013, Sakai 2014) by including flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking and scalar meson mixing, which were not accounted for in those earlier calculations. The existence of an $\eta'N$ bound state would impact studies of the $\eta'$-nucleus system (Tsushima, Nagahiro 2004, Nagahiro 2006) and could modify the expected spectrum of $\eta'$ mesons in nuclear matter, which is relevant for understanding chiral symmetry restoration in the nuclear medium.
