# Dispersion-theoretical analysis of the electromagnetic form factors of the $Λ$ hyperon

> The electromagnetic form factors of the $Λ$ hyperon in the time-like region are determined precisely through a dispersion-theoretical analysis of the world data for the cross section of the annihilation process $e^+e^-\to \barΛΛ$. The spectral function is represented by a superposition of narrow and broad vector meson poles. We test different scenarios for the spectral function and obtain a good description of the world data in the time-like region. The uncertainties in the extracted form factors are estimated by means of the bootstrap sampling method. The analytical continuation of the form factors to the space-like region introduces large errors due to the lack of data. When the electric $Λ$ radius from chiral perturbation theory is taken as a constraint, the magnetic radius is predicted as $r_M = 0.681 \pm 0.002$ fm. We also extract various vector meson to baryon coupling constants.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors of the Λ hyperon in the time-like region are determined through a dispersion-theoretical analysis of the world data for e+e− → Λ̄Λ, but the analytical continuation to the space-like region introduces large errors due to the lack of data; when the electric Λ radius from chiral perturbation theory is taken as a constraint, the magnetic radius is predicted as rM = 0.681 ± 0.002 fm.

## Method

The spectral function for the isoscalar electromagnetic form factors of the Λ hyperon is parameterized as a superposition of narrow and broad vector meson poles, including the physical ω(782) and φ(1020) mesons in the narrow sector and the φ(2170) and ψ(3770) states with fixed masses plus one additional floating broad pole in the broad sector. The fit to the full data set of e+e− → Λ̄Λ cross sections from the DM2, BaBar, CLEO, and BESIII collaborations is performed with constraints from normalization conditions, superconvergence relations from perturbative QCD, and the electric charge radius from chiral perturbation theory. Uncertainties are estimated using the bootstrap sampling method.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic radius of the Λ hyperon: rM = 0.681 ± 0.002 fm, obtained when the electric radius from chiral perturbation theory (rE = 0.332 fm) is used as a constraint.
- Reduced χ2 of the best fit (Fit-2): 2.596; a slightly better fit (Fit-1) gives reduced χ2 = 1.531 but uses a double pole structure near threshold whose experimental status is unclear.
- ωΛΛ vector coupling: g1ωΛΛ = 18.04−0.11+0.13; tensor coupling: g2ωΛΛ = −34.45−0.24+0.20.
- φΛΛ vector coupling: g1φΛΛ = −16.81−0.24+0.19; tensor coupling: g2φΛΛ = 18.99−0.20+0.25.
- ψ(3770)ΛΛ vector coupling: g1ψ(3770)ΛΛ = −0.0040 ± 0.0008; tensor coupling: g2ψ(3770)ΛΛ = 0.0012 ± 0.0004.
- Tensor-to-vector coupling ratio for ψ(3770)ΛΛ: κψ(3770) = −0.3 ± 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical approach previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Lin et al. (2021) to the Λ hyperon case, using the same formalism of narrow and broad vector meson poles. It provides a unified analysis of the full world data set for e+e− → Λ̄Λ, including the recent BESIII measurements around 4 GeV, and improves upon earlier theoretical studies that only analyzed subsets of the data, such as those by Haidenbauer et al. (2016, 2020) using potential models, Cao et al. (2018) using Fano-type form factors, and Yang et al. (2019) and Li et al. (2021) using vector meson dominance parameterizations. The results enable future experimental investigations of the near-threshold oscillation structure and the pole around 4.163 GeV that may correspond to ψ(4160).
