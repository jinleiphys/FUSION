# Quasi-elastic Neutrino Scattering - an Overview

> A non-technical overview of charge current quasi-elastic neutrino interaction is presented. Many body computations of multinucleon ejection which is proposed to explain recent large axial mass measurements are discussed. A few comments on recent experimental results reported at NuInt11 workshop are included.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the discrepancy between the historical axial mass value of about 1.03 GeV from deuterium experiments and the larger values (1.2-1.35 GeV) reported by modern experiments like MiniBooNE can be explained by multinucleon ejection contributions that are absent in free-nucleon scattering, but the author notes that this explanation remains conditional on further experimental verification and implementation in Monte Carlo event generators.

## Method

The paper reviews charge current quasi-elastic (CCQE) neutrino scattering on nuclear targets, comparing experimental measurements of the axial mass parameter M_A from deuterium, oxygen, carbon, and iron targets. It discusses many-body nuclear model computations of multinucleon ejection (2p-2h and 3p-3h excitations) by the Martini-Ericson-Chanfray-Marteau (MEChM) model and the Nieves model, and examines how these contributions affect extracted M_A values when fitted to MiniBooNE double differential cross section data.

## Key numbers

- Historical deuterium measurements converged to M_A of order 1.03 GeV
- PCAC-based evaluation from weak pion production gives M_A = 1.077 ± 0.039 GeV
- K2K (oxygen, Q² > 0.2 GeV²): M_A = 1.2 ± 0.12 GeV
- K2K (carbon, Q² > 0.2 GeV²): M_A = 1.14 ± 0.11 GeV
- MINOS (iron, Q² > 0 GeV²): M_A = 1.19 ± 0.17 GeV; (Q² > 0.3 GeV²): M_A = 1.26 ± 0.17 GeV
- MiniBooNE (carbon, Q² > 0 GeV²): M_A = 1.35 ± 0.17 GeV; (Q² > 0.25 GeV²): M_A = 1.27 ± 0.14 GeV
- NOMAD (carbon, Q² > 0 GeV²): M_A = 1.07 ± 0.07 GeV
- Butkevich fit to MiniBooNE Q² data: M_A = 1.37 ± 0.05 GeV
- JSZ fit to MiniBooNE double differential data: M_A = 1.34 ± 0.06 GeV (with q_cut = 500 MeV/c)
- MEChM model: neutrino-carbon CCQE cross section increases from 7.46 to 9.13 (×10⁻³⁹ cm² per neutron) when including np-nh contributions; antineutrino-carbon changes from 2.09 to 2.07 (×10⁻³⁹ cm² per proton)
- New MINOS best fit: M_A = 1.16 GeV, error reduced by factor of 3
- MiniBooNE antineutrino DATA/MC cross section ratio: 1.21 ± 0.12
- Two-parameter fit from Nieves model: M_A = 1.077 ± 0.027 GeV, normalization λ = 0.917 ± 0.029; with q_cut = 400 MeV: M_A = 1.007 ± 0.034 GeV

## Context

This overview extends the long-standing discussion of the axial mass puzzle, where deuterium measurements (ANL, BNL) gave M_A ≈ 1.03 GeV but modern experiments on nuclear targets report larger values. The paper connects the multinucleon ejection mechanism proposed by Martini, Ericson, Chanfray, and Marteau (2010) and the microscopic computations by Nieves et al. (2011) to explain the MiniBooNE results. It enables future work by emphasizing the need to implement these many-body models in Monte Carlo event generators and to measure ejected nucleon kinetic energies as a cross-check of the multinucleon ejection mechanism.
