# Photon Production from Charge-Asymmetric Hot and Dense Matter

> A new channel of direct photon production from a quark gluon plasma (QGP) is explored. This process appears at Next-to-Leading-Order in the presence of a charge asymmetry in the heated matter and may be effectively described as the bremsstrahlung of a real photon from a thermal gluon. The photon production from this new mechanism is calculated in the effective theory of QCD at high temperature. The results show that the photon production rate may not as big as the annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density, but could become important in baryon-rich matter.

## Key claim

A new channel of direct photon production from a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) via a gluon-gluon-photon vertex appears at next-to-leading order only when the medium has a finite charge asymmetry (non-zero chemical potential), and while the rate is smaller than annihilation and Compton scattering at low baryon density, it may become important in baryon-rich matter.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop gluon-gluon-photon vertex in the Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) effective theory of QCD at high temperature, using the imaginary time formalism with Matsubara frequencies and a non-covariant propagator method. The resulting amplitude was incorporated into the photon self-energy, and the thermal photon emission rate was obtained from the imaginary part of the retarded photon self-energy, evaluated via the discontinuity across cuts including pole-pole and pole-cut contributions from the gluon spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation assumes two massless quark flavors with chemical potentials \(\mu_u = \mu_d = \mu_B/3\) and strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.4\).
- The thermal gluon mass is given by \(m_g^2 = C_A g^2 T^2/6 + N_F (T^2 + 3\mu^2/\pi^2)/12\).
- The photon differential rate from QCD annihilation and Compton processes at finite temperature and chemical potential is parameterized as \(E dR/d^3p = (5/9)(\alpha \alpha_s / 2\pi^2) (T^2 + \mu^2/\pi^2) e^{-E/T} \ln(2.912ET / [g^2(T^2 + \mu^2/\pi^2)] + G)\), where \(G = \ln(1 + \mu^2/\pi^2 T^2)\) for \(\mu/T \leq 1\) and \(G = \ln(1 + 0.139 \mu^2/T^2)\) for \(\mu/T \geq 1\).
- At temperature \(T = 200\) MeV, the new channel's contribution is much smaller than annihilation and Compton processes at low chemical potential, but grows with increasing chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dilepton production from gluon-gluon fusion via a quark loop at finite chemical potential (Majumder 2000, Majumder 2003) to the case of real photon production, which avoids constraints from Yang's theorem that limited the dilepton channel. It contrasts with prior photon production calculations that neglected baryon chemical potential (Owens 1986, Kapusta 1991, Fries 2002, Arnold 2001) and with earlier estimates at finite chemical potential (Dumitru 1993) that only included processes non-vanishing at \(\mu_B=0\). The results enable future phenomenological studies of photon signatures from the gluon sector of the QGP in baryon-rich matter, such as at the CERN SPS or RHIC, where the central region displays finite baryon density.
