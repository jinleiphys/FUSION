# Strange quark mass turns magnetic domain walls into multi-winding flux tubes

> Dense quark matter is expected to behave as a type-II superconductor at strong coupling. It was previously shown that if the strange quark mass $m_s$ is neglected, magnetic domain walls in the so-called 2SC phase are the energetically preferred magnetic defects in a certain parameter region. Computing the flux tube profiles and associated free energies within a Ginzburg-Landau approach, we find a cascade of multi-winding flux tubes as "remnants" of the domain wall when $m_s$ is increased. These flux tubes exhibit an unconventional ring-like structure of the magnetic field. We show that flux tubes with winding numbers larger than one survive for values of $m_s$ up to about 20% of the quark chemical potential. This makes them unlikely to play a significant role in compact stars, but they may appear in the QCD phase diagram in the presence of an external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when the strange quark mass \(m_s\) is increased from zero, the planar magnetic domain walls previously found in the 2SC color-superconducting phase (for \(m_s = 0\)) transform into a cascade of multi-winding flux tubes with ring-like magnetic field profiles, but these flux tubes with winding numbers larger than one only survive for \(m_s\) up to about 20% of the quark chemical potential, making them unlikely to play a significant role in compact stars.

## Method

The authors work within a Ginzburg-Landau framework extended from Haber (2017), incorporating the strange quark mass to lowest nontrivial order via mass insertions. They restrict to diagonal order parameters (three condensates \(\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3\) corresponding to \(ds\), \(us\), and \(ud\) pairing) and apply a double rotation of the gauge fields to decouple the field that penetrates the superconductor unperturbed. They compute flux tube profiles and free energies numerically for single flux tubes, using weak-coupling Ginzburg-Landau parameters extrapolated to strong coupling, and determine the lower critical field \(H_{c1}\) as a function of \(m_s/\mu_q\) and \(T_c/\mu_q\).

## Key numbers

* Multi-winding flux tubes with winding numbers larger than one survive for values of \(m_s\) up to about 20% of the quark chemical potential \(\mu_q\) (i.e., \(m_s/\mu_q \lesssim 0.2\)).
* The weak-coupling Ginzburg-Landau parameters used are: \(\mu^2 = \frac{48\pi^2}{7\zeta(3)}T_c^2(1-T/T_c)\), \(\lambda = \frac{72\pi^4}{7\zeta(3)}T_c^2/\mu_q^2\), \(h = -\frac{36\pi^4}{7\zeta(3)}T_c^2/\mu_q^2\), and \(\sigma = -\frac{24\pi^2}{7\zeta(3)}(T_c^2/\mu_q)\ln(T_c/\mu_q)\).
* The mixing angles for the gauge field rotation are given by \(\sin\vartheta_1 = e/\sqrt{3g^2+e^2}\) and \(\sin\vartheta_2 = \sqrt{3}e/\sqrt{3g^2+4e^2}\), where \(e \simeq 0.3\) is the elementary charge and \(g \simeq 3.5\) is the strong coupling constant for compact star conditions.
* The electric charge chemical potential is taken as \(\mu_e = m_s^2/(4\mu_q)\) from the unpaired phase.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic defects in the 2SC phase by Haber (2017), who showed that for zero strange quark mass, planar domain walls interpolating between 2SC\(_{\rm ud}\) and 2SC\(_{\rm us}\) are energetically preferred in a certain parameter region. The present paper introduces a nonzero strange quark mass, which breaks the degeneracy between these two phases and causes the domain wall to collapse into a sequence of multi-winding flux tubes. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram in the presence of external magnetic fields, though the authors note that the multi-winding flux tubes are unlikely to be relevant for compact stars due to the required small \(m_s/\mu_q\). The work also relates to studies of multi-winding flux tubes in CFL matter by Haber (2018) and in superconductor/superfluid mixtures by Alford (2007), but differs in that the 2SC multi-winding flux tubes exist in a much larger parameter region within the type-II regime.
