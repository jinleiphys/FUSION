# Extracting the Transverse Momentum Dependent Polarizing Fragmentation Functions

> We demonstrate that spontaneous transverse polarization of Lambda baryon ($Λ$) production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation can be described using the transverse momentum dependent polarizing fragmentation functions (TMD PFFs). Using a simple Gaussian model, we perform an extraction of the TMD PFFs by fitting the BELLE collaboration's recent measurement of the $Λ$ transverse polarization in back-to-back $Λ+h$ production in $e^+ e^-$ collisions, $e^{-} + e^{+} \rightarrow Λ^{\uparrow}+h+X$. We find that this simple model accurately describes the experimental data for $Λ$ production associated with pions and kaons, and we are able to determine TMD PFFs for different quark flavors. We use these newly extracted TMD PFFs to make predictions for the transverse polarization of $Λ$ produced in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at a future electron-ion collider, and find that such a polarization is around $10\%$ and should be measurable.

### Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a simple Gaussian model for transverse momentum dependent polarizing fragmentation functions (TMD PFFs) can describe the BELLE collaboration's measured transverse polarization of Lambda baryons in back-to-back Lambda-plus-hadron production in electron-positron annihilation, but the fit quality degrades when data with hadron momentum fraction above 0.5 are included, suggesting contributions from threshold logarithms or target mass corrections in that region.

### Method
The authors performed a fit of TMD PFFs to the BELLE data for Lambda and anti-Lambda transverse polarization in back-to-back production with pions and kaons at a hard scale Q = 10.58 GeV, using a Gaussian model for the transverse momentum dependence of both unpolarized and polarizing fragmentation functions. They integrated over the transverse momentum of the virtual photon to isolate the polarizing fragmentation function contribution, and used the MINUIT package with a bootstrap method generating 200 replicas to determine parameter uncertainties. The collinear fragmentation functions for Lambda were taken from the AKK08 parametrization, for pions from DSS14, and for kaons from DSS07.

### Key numbers
- The fit yields a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.694 for 96 data points with hadron momentum fraction below 0.5; this increases to 2.421 when data above 0.5 are included.
- The extracted Gaussian width for the polarizing fragmentation function is ⟨M_D^2⟩ = 0.118_{-0.012}^{+0.007} GeV^2.
- The predicted transverse polarization for Lambda production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at a future electron-ion collider is around 10% at Q = 10 GeV and x_B = 0.1.
- The unpolarized transverse momentum widths used are ⟨p_h⊥^2⟩ = 0.19 GeV^2 for light hadrons and ⟨k_⊥^2⟩ = 0.61 GeV^2 for parton distribution functions.

### Context
This work extends the TMD factorization approach to polarizing fragmentation functions, which are analogous to the Sivers function but for fragmentation rather than parton distribution. The extraction uses BELLE data (Guan 2018) and provides the first determination of TMD PFFs for different quark flavors, enabling predictions for Lambda polarization in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider. The results can test the predicted universality of TMD PFFs between electron-positron annihilation and SIDIS, as discussed by Boer (2010), and complement earlier collinear high-twist analyses of Lambda polarization in proton-proton collisions by Koike (2017) and Gamberg (2018).
