# Effects of event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations on bottomonium dynamics in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV

> We investigate the effects of event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations on bottomonium nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. The internal evolution of the heavy quarkonium is described by a time-dependent Schrödinger equation with a temperature-dependent complex heavy-quark potential, while the hot QCD medium evolution is simulated using the iEBE-VISHNU event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic framework. By incorporating both fluctuating and smooth hot media, we observe that both $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ of various bottomonium states are marginally affected by the medium fluctuations. By realistically simulating the dynamical evolution of bottomonium within a large set of event-by-event fluctuating hot QCD medium, this work provides key insights into the behavior of heavy-quarkonium observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations have only a marginal influence on bottomonium nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and this effect is small compared to the inherent uncertainties in the in-medium heavy-quark potentials.

## Method

The internal evolution of bottomonium states is described by a time-dependent Schrodinger equation with a temperature-dependent complex heavy-quark potential, using a screened Cornell-type parametrization for the real part and a parametrized thermal width for the imaginary part. The hot QCD medium evolution is simulated using the iEBE-VISHNU event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic framework with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, and bottomonium observables are computed by evolving b-bbar dipoles through either fluctuating event-by-event or smooth hydrodynamic backgrounds and projecting the final wave functions onto vacuum eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass set to m_b = 4.62 GeV in the Schrodinger equation
- Debye screening mass parametrized as mu_D = a_4 T sqrt(4pi N_c (1+N_f/6) alpha/3) with N_c=3, N_f=3, and a_4 in [0.100, 0.319]
- Critical temperature T_c = 0.17 GeV separating QGP and hadronic phases
- Specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08, hydrodynamic starting time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, decoupling energy density e_dec = 0.18 GeV/fm^3
- Mean charged-hadron multiplicity in 0-5% centrality: theoretical 1996.4 vs experimental 1943 +/- 56
- Mean charged-hadron multiplicity in 30-40% centrality: theoretical 505.6 vs experimental 512 +/- 15
- Mean charged-hadron multiplicity in 50-60% centrality: theoretical 172.1 vs experimental 183 +/- 8
- Mean charged-hadron multiplicity in 80-90% centrality: theoretical 16.5 vs experimental 17.5 +/- 1.8

## Context

This work extends previous real-time quantum-evolution studies of bottomonium in fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds (Islam 2020, Alalawi 2022) by performing a direct comparison between event-by-event and event-averaged hydrodynamic media within a Schrodinger-equation framework using a phenomenologically constrained complex heavy-quark potential. The results demonstrate that event-by-event fluctuations do not lead to quantitatively significant modifications of R_AA or v_2 for bottomonium states, which enables future studies to use smooth hydrodynamic backgrounds as a reliable approximation when computing these observables, reducing computational cost while maintaining accuracy.
