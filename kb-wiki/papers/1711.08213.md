# Tetraquark mixing framework for isoscalar resonances in light mesons

> Recently, a tetraquark mixing framework has been proposed for light mesons and applied more or less successfully to the isovector resonances, $a_0(980), a_0(1450)$, as well as to the isodoublet resonances, $K^*_0(800), K^*_0(1430)$. In this work, we present a more extensive view on the mixing framework and extend this to the isoscalar resonances, $f_0 (500)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_0 (1370)$, $f_0(1500)$. Tetraquarks in this framework can have two spin configurations containing either spin-0 diquark or spin-1 diquark and each configuration forms a nonet in flavor space. The two spin configurations are found to mix strongly through the color-spin interactions. Their mixtures, which diagonalize the hyperfine masses, can generate the physical resonances constituting the two nonets, which, in fact, coincide roughly with the experimental observation. We identify that $f_0 (500)$, $f_0(980)$ are the isoscalar members in the light nonet, and $f_0 (1370)$, $f_0(1500)$ are the similar members in the heavy nonet. This means that the spin configuration mixing, as it relates the corresponding members in the two nonets, can generate $f_0 (500), f_0 (1370)$ among the members in light mass, and $f_0(980), f_0(1500)$ in heavy mass. The complication arises because the isoscalar members of each nonet are subject to an additional flavor mixing known as OZI rule so that $f_0 (500), f_0 (980)$, and similarly $f_0 (1370), f_0 (1500)$, are the mixture of two isoscalar members belonging to an octet and a singlet in SU$_f$(3). The tetraquark mixing framework including the flavor mixing is tested for the isoscalar resonances in terms of the mass splitting and the fall-apart decay modes.

## Key claim

The tetraquark mixing framework, which previously explained isovector and isodoublet resonances, can also generate the isoscalar resonances $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_0(1370)$, and $f_0(1500)$ through strong mixing of two spin configurations (spin-0 diquark and spin-1 diquark), but the isoscalar case requires an additional flavor mixing between octet and singlet members of SU$_f$(3) that complicates the identification.

## Method

The authors constructed tetraquark wave functions in the diquark-antidiquark form using two diquark configurations: spin-0, color antitriplet, flavor antitriplet ($|000\rangle$) and spin-1, color sextet, flavor antitriplet ($|011\rangle$). They computed hyperfine masses from the color-spin interaction expectation values for these two configurations and diagonalized the resulting mass matrix to obtain physical eigenstates. For the isoscalar channel, they introduced flavor mixing between the octet and singlet members of each nonet, considering three cases: exact SU(3) symmetry, ideal mixing (maximal OZI rule separation), and general mixing with parameters fitted to the mass splitting of the physical resonances.

## Key numbers

* The light nonet members $f_0(500)$ has mass 400-550 MeV and $f_0(980)$ has mass 990 MeV; the heavy nonet members $f_0(1370)$ has mass 1200-1500 MeV and $f_0(1500)$ has mass 1505 MeV (from PDG data cited in the paper).
* The isovector member $a_0(1450)$ at 1474 MeV is heavier than the isodoublet member $K_0^*(1430)$ at 1425 MeV by approximately 50 MeV, consistent with the tetraquark mass hierarchy expectation.
* Constituent quark masses used as input: $m_u = m_d = 330$ MeV, $m_s = 500$ MeV, with the color-spin interaction strength $v_0$ fixed from the $D_0^*(2318)-D_2^*(2463)$ mass splitting.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing framework previously applied to isovector resonances $a_0(980), a_0(1450)$ and isodoublet resonances $K_0^*(800), K_0^*(1430)$ by Kim et al. (2016) to the isoscalar channel. It contrasts with approaches that treat these resonances as dynamically generated poles from meson-meson scattering (Boglione et al., 2002; van Beveren et al., 1986) or as mixtures of tetraquarks with glueballs (Maiani et al., 2006) or quarkonia (Giacosa et al., 2006). The framework enables testing of the tetraquark interpretation through mass splitting predictions and fall-apart decay modes for all members of both the light and heavy scalar nonets.
