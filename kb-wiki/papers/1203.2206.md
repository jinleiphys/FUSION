# Plasma effects on resonant fusion

> We investigate the effects of plasma interactions on resonance-enhanced fusion rates in stars. Starting from basic principles we derive an expression for the fusion rate that can serve as a basis for discussion of approximation schemes. The present state-of-the-art correction algorithms, based on the classical correlation function for the fusing particles and the classical energy shift for the resonant state, do not follow from this result, even as an approximation. The results of expanding in a perturbation solution for the case of a weakly coupled plasma are somewhat enlightening. But at this point we are at a loss as to how to do meaningful calculations in systems with even moderate plasma coupling strength. Examples where this can matter are: the effect of a possible low energy $^{12}$ C +$^{12}$ C resonance on X-ray bursts from accreting neutron stars or on supernova 1A simulations; and the calculation of the triple $α$ rate in some of the more strongly coupled regions in which the process enters, such as accretion onto a neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard "basically classical" approach to plasma corrections, which uses a classical correlation function and classical energy shift for the resonant state, cannot be trusted for resonance-enhanced fusion even at moderate plasma coupling strengths, and that the existing literature on this topic has grafted together pieces of lore without a coherent formal foundation.

## Method

The author derives an exact formal expression for the resonant fusion rate starting from a zero-range fusion interaction Hamiltonian and thermal field theory, then examines the relationship between this exact result and the ansatz used in prior work. The analysis includes a perturbative expansion to leading order in the plasma coupling for the weak-coupling case, and a critical examination of the assumptions underlying the "basically classical" approach that has been applied to resonant fusion in prior literature.

## Key numbers

* Plasma coupling parameter: Gamma = 0.62 / T_9 for a pure 12C plasma at density rho = 5 x 10^9 g cm^-3
* Temperature range considered: 4 < T_8 < 9 (where T_8 is temperature in units of 10^8 K)
* Screening potential parameter gamma = 2.293 Gamma^0.25 + 1.053 Gamma - 0.5551 log(Gamma) - 2.35
* Perturbative result for weak coupling: w = [1 + e^2 Z_1 Z_2 kappa_D beta] w_0, which is the standard Salpeter correction
* Possible resonant state energy in 12C+12C system: E_r = 1.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's prior formal analysis of plasma corrections to fusion reactions (Sawyer, 2005) and critically examines the screening corrections applied to resonant fusion by Cussons et al. and Itoh et al., who used a classical screening potential and resonance energy shift approach originally developed by Salpeter for non-resonant fusion. The paper identifies that the standard approach fails for resonant fusion because the energy release Q is effectively negative rather than much larger than the Gamow energy, and because the plasma state cannot be traced out independently of the fusion process. The work points to the need for quantum path integral methods, as used by Militzer and Pollack for static correlators, but notes that the time-dependent oscillatory factors in the exact expression make such calculations currently infeasible.
