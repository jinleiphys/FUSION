# Entanglement of scales as a possible mechanism for decoherence and thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Despite the fact that a system created in relativistic heavy ion collisions is an isolated quantum system, which cannot increase its entropy in the course of unitary quantum evolution, hydrodynamical analysis of experimental data seems to indicate that the matter formed in the collisions is thermalized very quickly. Based on common consideration of hydrodynamics as an effective theory in the domain of slow- and long-length modes, we discuss the physical mechanisms responsible for the decoherence and emergence of the hydrodynamic behavior in such collisions, and demonstrate how such physical mechanisms work in the case of the scalar field model. We obtain the evolution equation for the Wigner function of a long-wavelength subsystem that describes its decoherence, isotropization, and approach to thermal equilibrium induced by interaction with short-wavelength modes. Our analysis supports the idea that decoherence, quantum-to-classical transition and thermalization in isolated quantum systems are attributed to the experimental context, and are related to a particular procedure of decomposition of the whole quantum system into relevant and irrelevant from an observational viewpoint subsystems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that decoherence and thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions can arise from entanglement between long-wavelength (hydrodynamic) and short-wavelength (microscopic) modes, even though the total system remains isolated and undergoes unitary evolution; the authors explicitly hedge that this mechanism depends on the experimental context and the particular decomposition of the whole quantum system into "relevant" and "irrelevant" subsystems from an observational viewpoint.

## Method

The authors study a scalar \(\varphi^4\) quantum field theory model, splitting the field into long-wavelength modes (the relevant subsystem, smoothed over a volume \(V=1/k^{*3}\)) and short-wavelength modes (the environment). They derive a piecewise continuous evolution for the long-wavelength field expectation value by repeatedly redefining the long-wavelength projection at discrete time intervals, discarding information transferred to short-wavelength modes, which introduces a stochastic noise term \(\xi\). From this, they obtain a kinetic transport equation for the reduced Wigner function \(N_L(x,p)\) of the long-wavelength subsystem, which under a short-memory ("white noise") approximation takes the form of a Fokker-Planck-like equation with a Vlasov term and a diffusion term arising from fluctuations induced by interactions with short-wavelength modes.

## Key numbers

- The smoothing volume for defining long-wavelength modes is \(V=1/k^{*3}\), where \(k^\star\) is the characteristic momentum scale separating long and short modes.
- The assumed thermalization time from hydrodynamic fits to experimental data is \(\lesssim 1\) fm (referenced from prior work).
- The temporal correlation length for the long-wavelength density matrix in the rest frame is \(\lambda_t \propto \sqrt{\partial^0\rho/\tau^{00}}\), where \(\partial^0\rho\) and \(\tau^{00}\) are parameters from the derived kinetic equation.

## Context

This work extends the conceptual framework of environment-induced decoherence (Zeh, 1970s) and the projection operator technique (Rau, 1980s) to the specific context of relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the system is isolated but can be decomposed into long- and short-wavelength subsystems. The authors contrast their approach with classical approximations based on the color glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory and with the AdS/CFT correspondence (fluid/gravity duality), arguing that those approaches do not resolve the fundamental issue of entropy production in a closed quantum system. The paper enables further study of prethermalization (Berges, 2004) and the emergence of hydrodynamic behavior from first principles in quantum field theory, without invoking an external environment.
