# Microscopic Analysis of Nuclear Quantum Phase Transitions in the N=90 region

> The analysis of shape transitions in Nd isotopes, based on the framework of relativistic energy density functionals and restricted to axially symmetric shapes in Ref. \cite{PRL99}, is extended to the region $Z = 60$, 62, 64 with $N \approx 90$, and includes both $β$ and $γ$ deformations. Collective excitation spectra and transition probabilities are calculated starting from a five-dimensional Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom, with parameters determined by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes. The results reproduce available data, and show that there is an abrupt change of structure at N=90 that can be approximately characterized by the X(5) analytic solution at the critical point of the first-order quantum phase transition between spherical and axially deformed shapes.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the abrupt structural change at N=90 in Nd, Sm, and Gd isotopes can be approximately characterized by the X(5) analytic solution for a first-order quantum phase transition, but the microscopic potential is more realistic than the infinite square well assumed in X(5) and includes explicit treatment of both β and γ degrees of freedom, with β-γ coupling that was absent in the original X(5) model.

## Method
The authors performed constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations using the PC-F1 relativistic energy density functional to generate triaxial quadrupole binding energy maps in the β-γ plane for even-even Nd, Sm, and Gd isotopes with N ≈ 90. From these constrained solutions, they calculated deformation-dependent mass parameters, moments of inertia (using the Inglis-Belyaev formula), and zero-point energy corrections, then diagonalized a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole vibrational and rotational degrees of freedom to obtain excitation spectra and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Calculated R₄/₂ = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) = 3.0 for ¹⁵⁰Nd, compared to experimental value 2.93 and X(5) model prediction 2.91
- B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) increases from less than 30 Weisskopf units in ¹⁴⁴Nd to more than 160 Weisskopf units in ¹⁵²Nd
- The prolate minimum in ¹⁵⁰Nd extends flat over the interval 0.2 ≤ β ≤ 0.4
- The oblate minimum in ¹⁵²Nd and ¹⁵⁴Nd occurs at β ≈ -0.3, with barriers separating prolate and oblate minima of 6 MeV for ¹⁵²Nd and more than 7 MeV for ¹⁵⁴Nd
- The ratio E(6₁⁺)/E(0₂⁺) takes a value close to 1 in ¹⁵⁰Nd, a signature of first-order quantum phase transition
- The scaling factor α = 0.4 was applied to Inglis-Belyaev moments of inertia for ¹⁵⁰Nd to match the experimental 2₁⁺ energy

## Context
This work extends the analysis of shape transitions in Nd isotopes from Ref. (Li et al., 2009, PRL 99) that was restricted to axially symmetric shapes, now including both β and γ deformations and covering Z = 60, 62, 64 with N ≈ 90. The results improve upon axial GCM calculations with the Gogny force by Rodriguez and Egido (2008) and Rodriguez, Rodriguez-Guzman, and Robledo (2008), which found ¹⁴⁸Nd closer to X(5) predictions rather than ¹⁵⁰Nd. The approach enables parameter-free predictions of transition probabilities using bare proton charges, in contrast to algebraic models like the interacting boson model that require effective charges, and provides a fully microscopic description of quantum phase transitions starting from nucleonic degrees of freedom.
