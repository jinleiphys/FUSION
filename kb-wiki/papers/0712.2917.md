# Comment on "Viscous hydrodynamics relaxation time from AdS/CFT correspondence"

> This is a comment to hep-th/0703243. The paper determined the relaxation time tau_pi of the shear viscous stress for the N=4 SYM from AdS/CFT correspondence. The purpose of this comment is to point out that the value of tau_pi is 3 times larger than their result if one takes into account an additional term in the hydrodynamic equation.

## Key claim

The relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) for the shear viscous stress in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang–Mills (SYM) plasma is three times larger than the value reported by Heller et al. (2007), provided one includes an additional term in the constitutive equation that is mandatory for the second law of thermodynamics but was omitted in the original Israel–Stewart theory and in that prior work.

## Method

The authors re-analyze the boost-invariant expanding plasma of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM using the conservation equation and the constitutive equation of causal hydrodynamics, including the Muronga (2001) term that ensures the second law. They substitute the energy density obtained from the gravity (AdS/CFT) computation into the conservation equation to determine the dissipative part \(\Phi\), then use the full constitutive equation to extract the constants \(C_\tau\) and \(C_\eta\) that define \(\tau_\pi = C_\tau / T\) and \(\eta = C_\eta T^3\).

## Key numbers

- \(\tau_\pi = \frac{1-\ln 2}{2\pi T}\) when the Muronga term is included, which is three times larger than the value \(\tau_\pi = \frac{1-\ln 2}{6\pi T}\) reported by Heller et al. (2007).
- The energy density from gravity is \(\epsilon(\tau) = \frac{N_c^2}{2\pi^2} \frac{1}{\tau^{4/3}} \left\{ 1 - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3^{3/4} \tau^{2/3}} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{36} \frac{1+2\ln 2}{\tau^{4/3}} + \cdots \right\}\).
- The effective temperature is defined via \(\epsilon = \frac{3}{8} \pi^2 N_c^2 T^4\) for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM at strong coupling.

## Context

This comment corrects the relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) obtained by Heller et al. (2007) for the \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma using AdS/CFT. The correction arises from including a term first added by Muronga (2001) to ensure the second law of thermodynamics, which is normally higher order but becomes the same order as other terms in a rapidly evolving system. The corrected value is independently supported by a computation in a different setting by Natsuume and Okamura (2007), and the same point was made independently by Benincasa et al. (2007). This result clarifies the proper hydrodynamic interpretation of the gravity computation and provides a benchmark for causal hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions.
