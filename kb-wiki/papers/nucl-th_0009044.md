# Fishing for Narrow Dibaryons in pd->pX Reaction

> An analysis of experimental data, obtained at the Linear Accelerator of INR, is carried out with the aim of searching for supernarrow dibaryons in the reactions pd->p+X and pd->p+pX_1. Dibaryons with masses 1904\pm 2, 1926\pm 2, and 1942\pm 2 MeV have been observed in M_{X} missing mass spectra. In M_{X_1} missing mass spectra, the peaks have been found at M_{X_1}=966\pm 2, 986\pm 2, and 1003\pm 2 MeV. These values of M_{X_1} coincide with the ones obtained by a simulation of a decay of the dibaryons into γ+pn. The analysis of the data obtained leads to the conclusion that the observed dibaryons are supernarrow dibaryons, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. A possible interpretation of exited nucleon states with small masses is suggested.

# Fishing for Narrow Dibaryons in pd->pX Reaction

## Key claim
Three narrow peaks observed in missing mass spectra at 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV are interpreted as supernarrow dibaryons whose decay into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle, based on the additional observation that the decay products correspond to a photon plus neutron rather than a free neutron.

## Method
The experiment used the TAMS two-arm spectrometer at the INR Linear Accelerator with a 305 MeV proton beam on CD2 and C12 targets. The scattered proton was detected in the left arm at 70 degrees, while the second charged particle (proton or deuteron) from the decay of the X state was detected in three telescopes in the right arm at 34, 36, and 38 degrees. Missing mass spectra MX and MX1 were constructed from the measured coincidences and compared to simulations of supernarrow dibaryon decay into gamma + pn.

## Key numbers
- Three dibaryon masses observed in MX spectra: 1904 ± 2 MeV, 1926 ± 2 MeV, and 1942 ± 2 MeV
- Statistical significance of the peaks: 6.0 standard deviations (1904 MeV), 7.0 standard deviations (1926 MeV), and 6.3 standard deviations (1942 MeV)
- Three resonance-like states observed in MX1 spectra at 966 ± 2 MeV, 986 ± 2 MeV, and 1003 ± 2 MeV, consistent with simulation predictions of 965, 987, and 1003 MeV for gamma + n decay products
- Beam energy: 305 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Fil'kov et al., 1999; Fil'kov et al., 2000) that reported two narrow peaks at 1905 and 1924 MeV in the pd -> pX reaction, confirming those results and adding a new resonance at 1942 MeV. The interpretation as supernarrow dibaryons follows the theoretical framework of Mulders et al. (1980) and Fil'kov (1988) regarding dibaryons whose two-nucleon decay is Pauli-forbidden. The observed MX1 peaks at 966, 986, and 1003 MeV are compared to states reported by Tatischeff et al. (1997) at 1004, 1044, and 1094 MeV, which those authors attributed to excited nucleon states; the present authors suggest those peaks may instead arise from supernarrow dibaryon decay. The results enable further searches for supernarrow dibaryons with masses up to 2033 MeV predicted from the observed pattern.
