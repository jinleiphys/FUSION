# On the Migdal-Watson approach to FSI effects in meson production in NN collisions

> The influence of the nucleon-nucleon final state interaction (FSI) on properties of the meson production amplitude near threshold is discussed. For the nucleon-nucleon interaction a simple Yamaguchi potential as well as realistic potential models are considered. It is shown that FSI effects cannot be factorized from the production amplitude. The absolute magnitude of FSI effects depends on the momentum transfer (or on the mass of the produced meson) and hence is not universal. Only in the case of the production of rather heavy mesons like eta' or phi FSI effects become universal. The Jost function approach to FSI effects is critically examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Migdal-Watson factorization of final state interaction (FSI) effects from the meson production amplitude is not valid for quantitative predictions, and that FSI effects are not universal except for the production of very heavy mesons like eta' or phi, where universality emerges only for the mass dependence but not for the absolute magnitude.

## Method

The authors compute the total reaction amplitude for pp → ppx using a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with a one-boson-exchange (OBE) production mechanism, where a scalar meson μ (with mass equal to the pion mass, 135 MeV) is exchanged in the t-channel. They evaluate the loop diagram containing the off-shell NN T-matrix for the 1S0 partial wave, comparing results from a simple separable Yamaguchi potential with realistic NN potentials (Paris and Bonn models), and include Coulomb distortion between outgoing protons. The FSI enhancement factor F_NN = |Ψ(k)|^2 is calculated as a function of the final-state relative momentum k for meson masses corresponding to π, η, and η′.

## Key numbers

- For realistic NN potentials (Paris, Bonn), the off-shell T-matrix changes sign at an off-shell momentum q ≈ 350 MeV/c, while the Yamaguchi potential never changes sign.
- For the Paris and Bonn potentials, the FSI factor F_NN decreases with increasing meson mass; for the Yamaguchi potential, F_NN increases with meson mass.
- Differences between realistic NN models for the FSI factor are about 20% for pion production, but for η′ production the Paris result is almost a factor 2 larger than the Bonn result.
- The on-shell prescription Eq. (1) works relatively well near threshold, but for k ≥ 50 MeV/c (excess energy Q ≥ 3 MeV) deviations appear; at k = 100 MeV/c (Q ≈ 10 MeV) the on-shell and full DWBA results differ by a factor of around 2.
- The value of P(k) at k = 0 (the principal value loop integral term) is positive and about 3 to 5 units large.

## Context

This work extends and critically examines the Migdal-Watson approach (Migdal 1955, Watson 1952) and the factorization prescription used by Druzhinin et al. (1990), Wilkin et al. (1993), Shyam et al. (1994), Sibirtsev et al. (1995), and Titov et al. (1996). It confirms the arguments of Hanhart and Nakayama (1999) and Niskanen (1999) that simple multiplication by the on-shell NN T-matrix is insufficient for quantitative predictions. The paper enables future studies to properly account for off-shell effects in meson production calculations, emphasizing that FSI corrections must be calculated explicitly with the specific production amplitude and a realistic NN off-shell T-matrix rather than using universal enhancement factors.
