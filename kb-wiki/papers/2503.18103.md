# In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections from relativistic ab initio calculations

> The in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section is a pivotal quantity for studying the medium effects of strong interaction, and its precise knowledge is critical for understanding the equation of state for dense matter, intermediate-energy heavy-ion collision dynamics, and related phenomena. In this work, we perform a microscopic investigation of in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections, by utilizing the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with the Bonn potential. The fully incorporation of both positive- and negative-energy states in the RBHF solutions allows us to determine the single-particle potentials, the effective G matrix, and the scattering cross section uniquely. The momentum, density, and isospin dependence of the cross section for pp, nn, and np scattering are studied in detail. Our results provide a solid foundation for future parametrization studies of multiparameter dependency of total scattering cross sections.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections are calculated using relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with the Bonn A potential, fully incorporating both positive- and negative-energy states to determine single-particle potentials and the effective G matrix uniquely; the results are restricted to the Bonn A interaction and do not include three-nucleon forces, which the authors identify as a major source of uncertainty.

## Method

The authors solve the in-medium Thompson equation within RBHF theory to obtain the effective G matrix, which incorporates Pauli blocking through the Pauli operator, dispersion effects through effective single-particle energies, and relativistic medium effects through effective Dirac spinors. The G matrix is then used to construct S matrices for partial waves with total angular momentum J up to 10 hbar, from which scattering amplitudes and differential cross sections are computed. The calculations are performed in the full Dirac space including both positive- and negative-energy states, enabling exact extraction of the Lorentz structure of the single-particle potential.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 is taken as 0.16 fm^{-3}; calculations are performed at densities rho = 0.5 rho_0, rho_0, and 1.5 rho_0
- For symmetric nuclear matter at rho = 0.16 fm^{-3}, the nucleon Fermi momentum is k_F = 1.33 fm^{-1}, corresponding to a laboratory energy of approximately 147 MeV
- Phase shifts are computed up to the pion-production threshold at approximately 300 MeV laboratory energy
- Total np cross sections plateau around 25 mb for laboratory energies between 200 and 300 MeV
- Total momenta studied include P = 0, 0.1, 1.0, and 2.0 fm^{-1}
- Isospin asymmetries studied include alpha = 0, 0.4, and 0.8

## Context

This work extends previous RBHF calculations that were restricted to positive-energy solutions of the Dirac equation, as done by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Wang et al. (2021), by fully incorporating negative-energy states to achieve unambiguous determination of single-particle potentials. The results provide a rigorous ab initio framework for in-medium cross sections that can serve as input for transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions, complementing earlier nonrelativistic BHF calculations by Schulze et al. (1997) and Han et al. (2022). The authors note that a systematic comparison with other NN interactions and inclusion of three-nucleon forces will be pursued in future work.
