# Recent developments in heavy flavor probes in lattice QCD

> The analysis of heavy flavor lattice correlation functions to obtain insights into transport phenomena and bound state dissociation patterns is a difficult and interesting challenge from the point of view of lattice QCD spectroscopy. In this contribution to "Hard Probes 2015", the recent developments and advances from different lattice efforts to determine the relevant spectral properties will be reviewed and discussed. The difficulties underlying this line of research will be highlighted and the obtained results assessed. Among others, results on the dissociation of charm systems and bottomonia, as well as the diffusion of heavy quarks will be addressed.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of heavy flavor probes (quarkonium dissociation and heavy quark diffusion) are making progress, but results remain method-dependent and often lack continuum extrapolations; for charmonia, the dissociation temperature is still uncertain, with the finest quenched lattices suggesting lower dissociation temperatures around 1.5 T_c, while dynamical studies see no clear dissociation below 1.9 T_c.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of spectral functions reconstructed from Euclidean correlation functions using the maximum entropy method (MEM), direct fits with phenomenological ansaetze, and the Backus-Gilbert method. For the complex heavy quark potential, the real and imaginary parts are extracted from the thermal Wilson loop via an analytic ansatz parameterized by a single temperature-dependent Debye mass. For heavy quark diffusion, a purely gluonic colour-electric correlator in the heavy quark effective theory (HQET) framework is computed on quenched ensembles with multi-level algorithms and semi-analytic link integration, then renormalized perturbatively and extrapolated to the continuum.

## Key numbers

* Debye masses extracted from the complex heavy quark potential in quenched QCD range from m_D ~ 850 MeV at T ~ 840 MeV down to m_D ~ 330 MeV at T ~ 210 MeV (Burnier 2015).
* In quenched QCD, the charm diffusion coefficient is found to be 2 pi T D ~ 1 to 3 (Ding 2012).
* The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient in the continuum limit of quenched QCD at 1.5 T_c is estimated as kappa / T^3 ~ 1.8 to 3.4 (Francis 2015).
* The kinetic equilibration time for charm quarks near T_c is estimated as tau_kin = (1.8 to 3.4) * (T_c / T)^2 * (M / 1.5 GeV) fm/c (Francis 2015).
* In quenched QCD, the J/Psi and eta_c peaks visible at 0.73 T_c are suppressed in the spectral function around 1.5 T_c (Ding 2012).
* Dynamical N_f=2+1 studies with m_pi ~ 400 MeV observe no clear dissociation of the Upsilon below 1.9 T_c (Aarts 2014) and no dissociation of the Upsilon below 1.61 T_c in a separate study with m_pi ~ 160 MeV (Kim 2014).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on heavy quark free energies and potential model estimates of dissociation temperatures (Kaczmarek 2005) by presenting first-principles lattice calculations of the complex heavy quark potential (Rothkopf 2011, Burnier 2014). It contrasts quenched results for charmonium dissociation (Ding 2012, Ikeda 2014) with dynamical N_f=2 and N_f=2+1 studies (Aarts 2007, Skullerud 2014) and NRQCD-based bottomonium studies (Aarts 2011, Kim 2014). The heavy quark diffusion results (Francis 2015) provide the first continuum-extrapolated determination of the momentum diffusion coefficient in quenched QCD, enabling estimates of charm quark kinetic equilibration times that can be compared with light parton thermalization scales.
