# Multiparticle production processes from the Information Theory point of view

> We look at multiparticle production processes from the Information Theory point of view, both in its extensive and nonextensive versions. Examples of both symmetric (like pp or AA) and asymmetric (like pA) collisions are considered showing that some ways of description of experimental data used in the literature are of more general validity than usually anticipated.}

# Multiparticle production processes from the Information Theory point of view

## Key claim

The paper argues that many existing descriptions of multiparticle production data, both in symmetric (pp, AA) and asymmetric (pA) collisions, can be understood as natural consequences of information theory (MaxEnt) rather than requiring specific dynamical models, but this equivalence holds only to the extent that the data provide limited information, when data show features like asymmetry in rapidity distributions for pA collisions or minima at y=0 in e+e- collisions, the information-theoretic approach signals that additional unaccounted information remains.

## Method

The authors apply the maximum entropy (MaxEnt) method using both Shannon entropy (extensive) and Tsallis entropy (nonextensive) to derive probability distributions for transverse momentum and rapidity spectra, subject to constraints from energy and momentum conservation and known mean multiplicities. For symmetric collisions (pp, pbar-p, AA), they fit rapidity distributions using a single Lagrange multiplier beta derived from the invariant mass M, number of secondaries N, and mean transverse mass mu_T; for asymmetric pA collisions, they consider both a "tube" model (treating the nucleus as a collective object) and a sequential collision model, introducing separate inelasticity parameters K (projectile) and R (target nucleons) and an additional Lagrange multiplier for momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- For AA collisions, the nonextensivity parameter q ≈ 1.05; for pbar-p collisions, q ≈ 1.1 (from fits to pT distributions)
- The q = 1.015 value for AA collisions corresponds (via Eq. 6) to temperature fluctuations of order ΔT/T ≈ 0.12
- For NA49 PbPb data at SPS energies, rapidity fits yield q = 1.2, 1.16, and 1.04 (from top to bottom energy)
- For PHOBOS AuAu data at RHIC, rapidity fits yield q = 1.27, 1.26, and 1.29 (from top to bottom energy)
- The inelasticity parameter K is found to be approximately 0.5 for pp collisions
- The relation q = 1 + 1/k connects the nonextensivity parameter to the Negative Binomial distribution parameter k

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of information theory to multiparticle production (Utyuzh, Wilk, Wlodarczyk, 1999-2004) and connects to the broader use of Tsallis statistics in high-energy physics. The paper shows that the MaxEnt approach reproduces results from thermal models (such as the "partition temperature" model of Chou and Yang, 1985) but with fewer free parameters, and that the nonextensivity parameter q can be interpreted as a measure of dynamical fluctuations (temperature fluctuations via heat capacity, or multiplicity fluctuations via the Negative Binomial distribution). The approach enables systematic identification of missing information in data, for example, the asymmetry in pA rapidity distributions and the dip at y=0 in e+e- data indicate that additional constraints (such as rescattering or two-jet structure) must be incorporated.
