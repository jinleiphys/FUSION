# Gamow-Teller strength distributions in Xe isotopes

> The energy distributions of the Gamow-Teller strength are studied for even-even Xe isotopes with mass numbers from 124 to 142. A self-consistent microscopic formalism is used to generate the single particle basis, using a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations in BCS approximation. The Gamow-Teller transitions are obtained within a quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) approach using a residual spin-isospin interaction in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. We then discuss the pairing BCS treatment and the determination of the ph and pp residual interaction coupling constants. We study the GT+ and GT- strength distributions for the equilibrium nuclear shapes, which are an essential information for studies of charge-exchange reactions and double-beta processes involving these isotopes.

# Gamow-Teller strength distributions in Xe isotopes

## Key claim
The Gamow-Teller strength distributions for even-even Xe isotopes (A=124-142) are calculated using a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) approach, but the authors note that for isotopes with two equilibrium shapes (oblate and prolate), there is no strong dependence of the GT strength on the equilibrium shape when a wide excitation energy range is considered, and the influence of the sign of deformation is only apparent in small energy windows.

## Method
A deformed Hartree-Fock calculation with the Skyrme Sk3 interaction was performed assuming axial symmetry, with pairing correlations between like nucleons included in BCS approximation using either fixed pairing gap parameters or fixed pairing strength parameters. Gamow-Teller transitions were then obtained within a quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) using a separable spin-isospin residual interaction in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, with the particle-hole coupling constant derived consistently from the Skyrme energy density functional and the particle-particle coupling constant fitted to reproduce experimental beta-decay half-lives of three unstable Xe isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The particle-hole residual interaction coupling constant varies from 0.21 MeV in 124Xe to 0.19 MeV in 142Xe; an average value of 0.2 MeV was used for all isotopes.
- The particle-particle coupling constant was set to 0.07 MeV to reproduce experimental half-lives of 138Xe (844.8 s), 140Xe (13.60 s), and 142Xe (1.22 s).
- The GT resonance appears at around 13 MeV excitation energy in 124Xe and moves to 25 MeV in 142Xe.
- A dominant GT transition in 124Xe (prolate shape) reaches a strength of 0.34 g_A^2/(4 pi) at very low excitation energy.
- The Ikeda sum rule is fulfilled up to a few per thousand discrepancy using a cutoff energy of 30 MeV.
- The number of configurations involved in this mass region for the 30 MeV energy range is typically over one thousand.

## Context
This work extends previous deformed QRPA studies of Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the A~70 mass region (Sarriguren et al.) and in the neutron-deficient Hg-Pb-Po region (Moreno et al.) to the Xe isotopic chain, which is of special interest for double-beta decay processes and for charge-exchange reactions at the FAIR-GSI facility. The results provide essential nuclear structure input for calculating two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements and half-lives for 128Xe, 130Xe, and 136Xe, as previously carried out by Alvarez-Rodriguez et al., and for predicting cross sections of charge-exchange reactions such as (p,n) and (n,p) on Xe targets.
