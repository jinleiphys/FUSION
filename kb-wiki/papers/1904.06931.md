# Diversity of shapes and rotations in the gamma-soft 130Ba nucleus: first observation of a t-band in the A=130 mass region

> Several new bands have been identified in 130Ba, among which there is one with band-head spin 8+. Its properties are in agreement with the Fermi-aligned νh11/2^2 , 7/2+[523] \otimes 9/2-[514] Nilsson configuration. This is the first observation of a two-quasiparticle t-band in the A=130 mass region. The t-band is fed by a dipole band involving two additional h11/2 protons. The odd-spin partners of the proton and neutron S-bands and the ground-state band at high spins are also newly identified. The observed bands are discussed using several theoretical models, which strongly suggest the coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes polarized by rotation aligned two-proton and two-neutron configurations, as well as prolate collective rotations around axes with different orientations. With the new results, 130Ba presents one of the best and most complete sets of collective excitations that a gamma-soft nucleus can manifest at medium and high spins, revealing a diversity of shapes and rotations for the nuclei in the A = 130 mass region.

# Diversity of shapes and rotations in the gamma-soft 130Ba nucleus: first observation of a t-band in the A=130 mass region

## Key claim
The paper reports the first observation of a two-quasiparticle t-band (Fermi-aligned band) in the A=130 mass region, specifically in 130Ba, with a band-head spin of 8+. The interpretation relies on the assigned νh11/2^2 configuration with the 7/2-[523] ⊗ 9/2-[514] Nilsson orbitals, and the claim is conditional on the agreement with multiple theoretical models (TRS, TAC, PRM, PSM) that collectively suggest the coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes polarized by rotation-aligned two-proton and two-neutron configurations.

## Method
The 130Ba nucleus was populated via the 122Sn(13C,5n) reaction at a beam energy of 65 MeV using the XTU Tandem accelerator at Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro. Gamma-rays were detected by the GALILEO spectrometer (25 Compton-suppressed Ge detectors), with charged particles and neutrons detected by the EUCLIDES silicon apparatus and Neutron Wall array, respectively. Five new positive-parity bands were identified through γγγγ-coincidence analysis, and the previously known bands built on 10+ states were extended to higher spins; angular distributions were analyzed to extract mixing ratios and B(M1)/B(E2) and B(E2)out/B(E2)in ratios.

## Key numbers
- Band-head spin of the t-band: 8+
- Beam energy for the 122Sn(13C,5n) reaction: 65 MeV
- Target thickness: two self-supporting 122Sn foils, each 0.5 mg/cm^2
- Beam current: 5 pnA
- Accidental degeneracy between 18+ states of bands D1 and S2o: 14 keV apart
- Separation between 15+ states of bands D1 and S2o: 40 keV
- Rotational frequency for neutron h11/2 pair alignment from TRS calculations: 0.35 MeV/ħ
- TRS deformation parameters for near-oblate minimum: (β2, γ) ≈ (0.17, -45°)
- TRS deformation parameters for near-prolate minima: (β2, γ) ≈ (0.20, +15°) and (0.20, -15°)
- PRM deformation for νh11/2^-2 configuration: (β=0.21, γ=16.8°)
- PRM moment of inertia for νh11/2^-2 configuration: 22 ħ^2/MeV
- PRM deformation for νh11/2^-2 ⊗ πh11/2^2 configuration: (β=0.23, γ=24.2°)
- PRM moment of inertia for νh11/2^-2 ⊗ πh11/2^2 configuration: 18 ħ^2/MeV
- PSM quadrupole deformation for prolate calculations: ε2=0.22
- PSM quadrupole deformation for oblate calculations: ε2=-0.18
- Effective g-factor difference (gν-gR) for νh11/2 orbital: -0.64
- Effective g-factor difference (gν-gR) for νg7/2 orbital: -0.18

## Context
This work extends the study of high-K isomers and t-bands previously observed in the A≈180 region (e.g., 180W by Walker and 182Os) into the A=130 mass region, where the Fermi level for N≈74 lies mid-way among the h11/2 orbitals. The results build on prior studies of 130Ba including the Kπ=8- isomer work by Petrache (2018) and the S-band identifications by Ka. The newly identified t-band, odd-spin partners of the S-bands (S1' and S2o'), and the D1 band provide one of the most complete sets of collective excitations in a γ-soft nucleus, enabling future tests of tilted-axis cranking, particle-rotor, and projected shell models in the A=130 region. The work also enables comparison with neighboring nuclei such as 132Ce (Paul) and 129Ba (Byrne), where similar structures have not been observed.
