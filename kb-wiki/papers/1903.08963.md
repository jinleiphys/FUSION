# New Neutron Star Equation of State with Quark-Hadron Crossover

> We present a much improved equation of state for neutron star matter, QHC19, with a smooth crossover from the hadronic regime at lower densities to the quark regime at higher densities. We now use the Togashi et al.~equation of state (Togashi:2017), a generalization of the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall equation of state of uniform nuclear matter, in the entire hadronic regime; the Togashi equation of state consistently describes non-uniform as well as uniform matter, and matter at beta equilibrium without the need for an interpolation between pure neutron and symmetric nuclear matter. We describe the quark matter regime at higher densities with the Nambu--Jona--Lasinio model, now identifying tight constraints on the phenomenological universal vector repulsion between quarks and the pairing interaction between quarks arising from the requirements of thermodynamic stability and causal propagation of sound. The resultant neutron star properties agree very well with the inferences of the LIGO/Virgo collaboration, from GW170817, of the pressure vs. baryon density, neutron star radii, and tidal deformabilities. The maximum neutron star mass allowed by QHC19 is 2.35 $M_\odot$, consistent with all neutron star mass determinations.

## Key claim

The QHC19 equation of state, which implements a smooth crossover from hadronic to quark matter, agrees well with LIGO/Virgo inferences from GW170817 and supports neutron stars up to 2.35 solar masses, but this agreement depends on tightly constraining the quark vector repulsion strength \(g_V\) and diquark pairing strength \(H\) to satisfy thermodynamic stability and causality.

## Method

The authors construct the equation of state by using the Togashi et al. equation of state for hadronic matter from neutron drip to \(2n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is nuclear saturation density), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter above \(5n_0\), with a smooth polynomial interpolation in baryon chemical potential between the two regimes. They identify allowed ranges for the NJL parameters \(g_V\) (universal vector repulsion) and \(H\) (diquark pairing strength) by requiring that the sound velocity never exceeds the speed of light and that the pressure be thermodynamically stable (positive and convex).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass allowed by QHC19 is 2.35 \(M_\odot\) at \((g_V, H)/G \simeq (1.30, 1.65)\), at the causal boundary.
- For a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron star, QHC19 (set D) gives radius \(R = 11.6\) km and tidal deformability \(\Lambda = 350\).
- The Togashi equation of state parameters: saturation energy \(E_0 = 16.1\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_0 = 245\) MeV, symmetry energy \(J = 29.1\) MeV, slope parameter \(L = 38.7\) MeV.
- For comparison, APR values: \(E_0 = 16.0\) MeV, \(K_0 = 267\) MeV, \(J = 34.0\) MeV, \(L = 63.2\) MeV.
- The Togashi equation of state alone gives radius \(R = 11.6\) km and \(\Lambda = 360\) for a 1.4 \(M_\odot\) star; APR gives \(R = 11.5\) km and \(\Lambda = 268\).
- The constraint from the 2.17 \(\pm\) 0.1 \(M_\odot\) neutron star PSR J0740+6620 tightens the lower bound to \(g_V/G \gtrsim 0.9\).
- The central baryon density at maximum mass always exceeds \(5.5 n_0\); for set D the maximal core density is \(\simeq 6.1 n_0\).
- Radii at \(M = 1.4 M_\odot\) are \(\simeq 11.6\) km, consistent with the LIGO/Virgo inference of \(11.9 \pm 1.4\) km.

## Context

This work extends the earlier QHC18 equation of state by replacing the APR hadronic model with the Togashi equation of state (Togashi 2017), which consistently describes both uniform and non-uniform matter and all proton fractions without interpolation. The QHC19 results agree with the LIGO/Virgo pressure-density inference from GW170817 (Ligo-ns 2018a) and with the mass of PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019). The approach enables future finite-temperature extensions for simulations of neutron star mergers and core-collapse supernovae, and motivates microscopic QCD-based calculations of the parameters \(g_V\) and \(H\).
