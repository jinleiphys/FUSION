# Studies of low energy $l+p\to l+p+╬│$ process in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> This study presents a tree-level calculation of the scattering amplitude for the $lp\to lp╬│$ (with a hard photon) process within the framework of Chiral Perturbation Theory. Our calculations, based on the $O(p^2)$ and $O(p^3)$ nucleon-pion Lagrangians, aim to provide a theoretical prediction for the differential cross-section. The result shows that explicit inclusion of the nonzero lepton mass significantly influences the low energy differential cross section for $╬╝p\to ╬╝p ╬│$ process. The kinematic region of the present experimental data is beyond the validity domain of the $Žć$PT and is therefore not suitable for determining the low-energy constants (LECs). By comparing our results with future experimental data, we expect to determine the values of the LECs as a further test of $Žć$PT as an effective low-energy theory of QCD. The process is of significant interest as it can help to determine the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper provides a tree-level calculation of the scattering amplitude for the $lp \to lp\gamma$ process using covariant Chiral Perturbation Theory up to $O(p^3)$, but the kinematic region of existing experimental data (JLab E00-110) lies beyond the validity domain of $\chi$PT, making those data unsuitable for determining the low-energy constants (LECs). The explicit inclusion of nonzero lepton mass significantly influences the low-energy differential cross section for the $\mu p \to \mu p \gamma$ process.

## Method

The authors constructed the scattering amplitude for $ep \to ep\gamma$ using the $O(p^2)$ and $O(p^3)$ nucleon-pion Lagrangians in covariant Chiral Perturbation Theory, calculating tree-level diagrams for both the Bethe-Heitler process (photon radiation from the lepton leg) and Virtual Compton Scattering (photon radiation from the hadron leg). The hadronic part of the VCS amplitude was decomposed into 26 fundamental Lorentz structures, and the resulting amplitudes were truncated to $O(p^3)$ for consistency. The authors then performed a global fit of the LEC combinations $c_6 + c_7$ and $d_6 + 2d_7$ to experimental data from the JLab E00-110 experiment, using the four-fold differential cross section expressed in terms of $Q^2$, $x_B$, $t$, and $\phi$.

## Key numbers

- The global fit at $O(p^3)$ yields $c_6 + c_7 = 0.2114 \pm 0.0090$ and $d_6 + 2d_7 = -1.1951 \pm 0.0098$, with $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 3.65$; the $O(p^2)$ fit gives $c_6 + c_7 = -0.2786 \pm 0.0368$ with $\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 49.35$.
- The experimental data used for fitting cover $Q^2 = 1.8$ to $2.4$ GeV$^2$, $x_B = 0.33$ to $0.40$, and $-t = 0.17$ to $0.37$ GeV$^2$ (from Tables VII, VIII, XII, and XIII of Defurne et al. 2015).
- The MUSE experiment aims to measure $\mu p$ scattering cross-sections within $|Q^2| \sim 0.0016$ to $0.08$ GeV$^2$ to reach sub-percent precision on the charge radius.
- The muon mass is $m_\mu \approx 105.7$ MeV, making the ultra-relativistic approximation invalid for $\mu p$ scattering.

## Context

This work extends the application of covariant Chiral Perturbation Theory to the $lp \to lp\gamma$ process, building on prior developments in baryon $\chi$PT by Gasser (1983), Becher (1999), and the EOMS scheme (2003). The extracted LECs show significant discrepancy from those reported by Guerrero (2020) and Fuchs (2004) (approximately $c_6 + c_7 \approx 2.39$ and $d_6 + 2d_7 \approx -1.68$ after convention conversion), which the authors attribute to the high-$Q^2$ regime ($1.8$ to $2.4$ GeV$^2$) of the JLab data exceeding $\chi$PT validity. This work enables future determination of LECs from low-energy experiments like MUSE at PSI and PRad at JLab, and can help determine the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon.
