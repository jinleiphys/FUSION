# Bulk viscosity of spin-one color superconducting strange quark matter

> The bulk viscosity in spin-one color-superconducting strange quark matter is calculated by taking into account the interplay between the nonleptonic and semi-leptonic week processes. In agreement with previous studies, it is found that the inclusion of the semi-leptonic processes may result in non-negligible corrections to the bulk viscosity in a narrow window of temperatures. The effect is generally more pronounced for pulsars with longer periods. Compared to the normal phase, however, this effect due to the semi-leptonic processes is less pronounced in spin-one color superconductors. Assuming that the critical temperature of the phase transition is much larger than 40 keV, the main effect of spin-one color superconductivity in a wide range of temperatures is an overall increase of the bulk viscosity with respect to the normal phase. The corresponding enhancement factor reaches up to about 9 in the polar and A-phases, about 25 in the planar phase and about 29 in the CSL phase. This factor is determined by the suppression of the nonleptonic rate in color-superconducting matter and, therefore, may be even larger if all quark quasiparticles happen to be gapped.

# Bulk viscosity of spin-one color superconducting strange quark matter

## Key claim
Assuming the critical temperature of the spin-one color-superconducting phase transition is much larger than 40 keV, the main effect in a wide range of temperatures is an overall increase of the bulk viscosity relative to the normal phase, with enhancement factors reaching up to about 9 in the polar and A-phases, about 25 in the planar phase, and about 29 in the CSL phase, but this enhancement is primarily determined by the suppression of the nonleptonic rate and may be even larger if all quark quasiparticles happen to be gapped.

## Method
The bulk viscosity was calculated by solving the coupled rate equations for nonleptonic processes (u+d to s+u and reverse) and semi-leptonic Urca processes (u+e to d+nu_e and u+e to s+nu_e and their reverses) in spin-one color-superconducting strange quark matter, using the general formalism of Sa'd (2007). The weak interaction rates for the nonleptonic processes in the four spin-one phases (polar, A-phase, planar, CSL) were taken from Wang (2009), while the semi-leptonic rates were taken from Sa'd (2006), each expressed as the normal-phase rate multiplied by a phase-specific suppression factor involving the superconducting gap parameter phi(T).

## Key numbers
- Enhancement factor for bulk viscosity: up to about 9 in the polar and A-phases, about 25 in the planar phase, and about 29 in the CSL phase
- Critical temperature assumed: T_c = 2 MeV (approximately 2.3 x 10^10 K)
- Temperature of the semi-leptonic hump in the normal phase: T_hump^(Set A) approximately 2.1 MeV x (1 ms / tau)^(1/4) and T_hump^(Set B) approximately 1.4 MeV x (1 ms / tau)^(1/4)
- Temperature of the global maximum of bulk viscosity from nonleptonic processes alone: T_0,max^(Set A) approximately 47 keV x sqrt(1 ms / tau) and T_0,max^(Set B) approximately 41 keV x sqrt(1 ms / tau)
- Model parameters: Set A uses n = 5 rho_0, m_s = 300 MeV, alpha_s = 0.2; Set B uses n = 10 rho_0, m_s = 140 MeV, alpha_s = 0.1
- Ratio T_c / phi_0: approximately 0.8 (CSL), 0.66 (planar), 0.49 (polar), 0.81 (A-phase)
- Ungapped quasiparticle fraction N: N^A = N^polar = 1/9, N^planar approximately 0.0393, N^CSL = 928/27027 approximately 0.0343

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Sa'd (2007) on the interplay between nonleptonic and semi-leptonic weak processes in the bulk viscosity of normal strange quark matter to the spin-one color-superconducting phases studied by Schafer (2000), Schmitt (2002, 2004), and others. It utilizes the nonleptonic rates recently calculated by Wang (2009) and the semi-leptonic rates from Sa'd (2006) for the polar, A-phase, planar, and CSL pairing patterns. The results enable more realistic modeling of r-mode damping in neutron stars, particularly for pulsars with longer periods where the semi-leptonic corrections are more pronounced, and show that spin-one color superconductivity can produce much larger enhancement factors than the spin-zero case studied by Alford (2006).
