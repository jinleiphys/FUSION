# Equation of state of a meson gas from the PNJL model for interacting quarks

> We compute the equation of state of hadronic matter at finite temperature with zero net baryon density by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for interacting quarks in uniform background temporal color gauge fields. In the low temperature confining phase, unphysical thermal quark-antiquark excitations which would appear in the mean field approximation, are eliminated by enforcing vanishing expectation value of the "Polyakov-loop" of the background gauge field, while in the high temperature confining phase this expectation value is taken as close to unity allowing thermal excitations of free quarks and antiquarks. Mesonic excitations in the low temperature phase appear in the correlation energy as contributions of collective excitations. We describe them in terms of thermal fluctuations of auxiliary fields in one-loop (Gaussian) approximation, where pions appear as Nambu-Goldstone modes associated with dynamical symmetry breaking of the chiral symmetry in the limit of vanishing bare quark masses. We show that the equations of state reduces to that of free meson gas with numerically small corrections arising from the composite nature of mesons.

## Key claim

The equation of state of hadronic matter at low temperatures reduces to that of a free meson gas, with numerically small corrections from the composite nature of mesons, but only in the chiral limit (vanishing bare quark mass) where the separation of collective meson modes from non-collective individual excitations is analytically clean.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for two-flavor quarks in a uniform background temporal color gauge field, evaluated at zero net baryon density. They introduce auxiliary bosonic fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to describe mesonic excitations, then expand the effective action to quadratic order and perform a Gaussian (one-loop) integral over the mesonic fields. The Polyakov-loop expectation value is treated phenomenologically via an effective potential, and the quark distribution functions are replaced by Polyakov-loop-averaged distributions that suppress single-quark excitations in the confining phase.

## Key numbers

* In the chiral limit, the pressure from massless pions with three-fold isospin degeneracy is \(p_{\rm pion}^{\rm free} = 3 \times \frac{\pi^2}{90} T^4 = 0.33 \times T^4\).
* The sigma meson mass is \(m_\sigma = 2 M_0\), where \(M_0\) is the effective quark mass determined by the gap equation.
* The finite bare quark mass \(m_0\) is chosen to reproduce the pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV.
* The effective gluon potential at high temperatures is set to \(\mathcal{U}(T, \Phi=1) = -\frac{16\pi^2}{90} T^4\) to match the free quark-gluon plasma limit.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model originally proposed by Fukushima (2004) and developed by the TUM group (Ratti et al., 2006; Roessner et al., 2007; Ratti et al., 2008) by providing an explicit analytic derivation of the meson gas equation of state in the low-temperature confining phase, rather than relying solely on numerical computation of mesonic correlation energies. The results enable future studies of baryonic correlations at finite chemical potential, as the quark-triads appearing in the formalism resemble baryons but lack internal spatial correlations among the three quarks.
