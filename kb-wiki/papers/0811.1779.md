# Moments of Nucleon's Parton Distribution for the Sea and Valence Quarks from Lattice QCD

> We extend the study of lowest moments, $<x>$ and $<x^2>$, of the parton distribution function of the nucleon to include those of the sea quarks; this entails a disconnected insertion calculation in lattice QCD. This is carried out on a $16^3 \times 24$ quenched lattice with Wilson fermion. The quark loops are calculated with $Z_2$ noise vectors and unbiased subtractions, and multiple nucleon sources are employed to reduce the statistical errors. We obtain 5$σ$ signals for $<x>$ for the $u,d,$ and $s$ quarks, but $<x^2>$ is consistent with zero within errors. We provide results for both the connected and disconnected insertions. The perturbatively renormalized $<x>$ for the strange quark at $μ= 2$ GeV is $<x>_{s+\bar{s}} = 0.027 \pm 0.006$ which is consistent with the experimental result. The ratio of $<x>$ for $s$ vs. $u/d$ in the disconnected insertion with quark loops is calculated to be $0.88 \pm 0.07$. This is about twice as large as the phenomenologically fitted $\displaystyle\frac{< x>_{s+\bar{s}}}{< x>_{\bar{u}}+< x>_{\bar{d}}}$ from experiments where $\bar{u}$ and $\bar{d}$ include both the connected and disconnected insertion parts. We discuss the source and implication of this difference.

## Key claim

The paper extends lattice QCD calculations of the lowest moments ⟨x⟩ and ⟨x²⟩ of the nucleon parton distribution to include sea quarks via disconnected insertions, obtaining a 5σ signal for ⟨x⟩ for u, d, and s quarks but finding ⟨x²⟩ consistent with zero within errors; the ratio of ⟨x⟩ for s versus u/d in the disconnected insertion is about twice as large as the phenomenologically fitted ratio from experiments.

## Method

The calculation was performed on a 16³ × 24 quenched lattice with Wilson fermions using the sequential source technique for connected insertions and complex Z₂ noise vectors with unbiased subtractions for the disconnected insertion (quark loop) calculation. Multiple nucleon sources were employed to reduce statistical errors, and discrete symmetries (parity, γ₅ hermiticity, and charge-γ₅ hermiticity) were used to further reduce noise in the loop estimates. The matrix elements were extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions, with perturbative renormalization applied at μ = 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Perturbatively renormalized ⟨x⟩ for the strange quark at μ = 2 GeV: ⟨x⟩_{s+¯s} = 0.027 ± 0.006
- Ratio of ⟨x⟩ for s versus u/d in the disconnected insertion with quark loops: 0.88 ± 0.07
- The calculation used a 16³ × 24 quenched lattice with Wilson fermion
- 5σ signals were obtained for ⟨x⟩ for the u, d, and s quarks
- ⟨x²⟩ was found to be consistent with zero within errors

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of connected insertions for the first and second moments of nucleon parton distributions to include the disconnected insertion contributions, particularly for strange quarks, which had not been attempted before in lattice QCD. The result for ⟨x⟩_{s+¯s} = 0.027 ± 0.006 is consistent with experimental results, while the ratio of 0.88 ± 0.07 for s versus u/d in the disconnected insertion is about twice as large as the phenomenologically fitted ratio from experiments such as those by CTEQ and MRST. This work enables first-principles lattice QCD assessment of the strange-antistrange asymmetry via ⟨x²⟩_{s-¯s} to address the NUTEV anomaly.
