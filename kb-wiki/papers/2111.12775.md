# Trends of Neutron Skins and Radii of Mirror Nuclei from First Principles

> The neutron skin of atomic nuclei impacts the structure of neutron-rich nuclei, the equation of state of nucleonic matter, and the size of neutron stars. Here we predict the neutron skin of selected light- and medium-mass nuclei using coupled-cluster theory and the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method with two- and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. We find a linear correlation between the neutron skin and the isospin asymmetry in agreement with the liquid-drop model and compare with data. We also extract the linear relationship that describes the difference between neutron and proton radii of mirror nuclei and quantify the effect of charge symmetry breaking terms in the nuclear Hamiltonian. Our results for the mirror-difference charge radii and binding energies per nucleon agree with existing data.

## Key claim

The authors predict neutron skin thicknesses for light- and medium-mass nuclei using first-principles calculations and find a linear correlation with isospin asymmetry that agrees with the liquid-drop model, but the relationship has a larger slope than experimental fits and the uncertainties increase away from zero isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors used coupled-cluster theory in the singles-and-doubles (CCSD) approximation for nuclei with mass numbers 14 to 56 and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) for nuclei with mass numbers 6 to 18, employing chiral effective field theory interactions including N2LO_sat, DeltaN2LO_GO(394), DeltaN2LO_GO(450), and N2LO_E1 local chiral interactions. They performed Hartree-Fock diagonalization in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis, constructed prolate axially-deformed product states from natural orbitals, and normal-ordered the Hamiltonian retaining one- and two-body terms. The neutron skin thickness was computed as the difference between root-mean-squared point radii of neutron and proton density distributions, and the Coulomb contribution was subtracted using a liquid-drop model correction.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness prediction for Pb-208: 0.210 +/- 0.026 fm
- Coulomb correction formula: DeltaR_Coul_np approximately -Z A^{-1/3} x 0.0033 fm
- Slope ratio J/Q approximately 1.59 from the linear fit, larger than many Skyrme forces but consistent with Myers (1969)
- Compressibility coefficient extracted from mirror neutron skin relation: K approximately 138 MeV, smaller than the range 150-250 MeV from the interactions used
- Charge-symmetry-breaking terms account for approximately 1% of the mirror-difference binding energy per nucleon
- For Ca-48 with DeltaN2LO_GO(394): neutron skin with Coulomb = 0.150(36) fm, without Coulomb = 0.186(35) fm; mirror-difference BE/A with Coulomb = 1.382(28) MeV, without Coulomb = 0.015(23) MeV

## Context

This work extends the liquid-drop model relationships derived by Myers (1969, 1974, 1977, 1980) and demonstrated with mean-field methods by Pethick and Ravenhall (1996) to first-principles calculations using coupled-cluster and AFDMC methods. The results connect to experimental measurements from PREX-I (Abrahamyan 2012), PREX-II (Adhikari 2021), and CREX (Adhikari 2022) for parity-violating electron scattering, as well as antiprotonic x-ray data from Trzcinska (2001) and Jastrzebski (2004) and proton scattering data from Lapoux (2016) and Zenihiro (2018). The findings enable estimation of neutron radii for rare isotopes through measurement of proton radii in mirror nuclei, and can help constrain charge-symmetry-breaking terms in Skyrme density functional theory as discussed by Naito (2022).
