# Nonlinear Responses of Chiral Fluids from Kinetic Theory

> The second-order nonlinear responses of inviscid chiral fluids near local equilibrium are investigated by applying the chiral kinetic theory (CKT) incorporating side-jump effects. It is shown that the local equilibrium distribution function can be non-trivially introduced in a co-moving frame with respect to the fluid velocity when the quantum corrections in collisions are involved. For the study of anomalous transport, contributions from both quantum corrections in anomalous hydrodynamic equations of motion and those from the CKT and Wigner functions are considered under the relaxation-time (RT) approximation, which result in anomalous charge Hall currents propagating along the cross product of the background electric field and the temperature (or chemical-potential) gradient and of the temperature and chemical-potential gradients. On the other hand, the nonlinear quantum correction on the charge density vanishes in the classical RT approximation, which in fact satisfies the matching condition given by the anomalous equation obtained from the CKT.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that second-order nonlinear responses in inviscid chiral fluids can be derived from chiral kinetic theory (CKT) with side-jump effects, but only when the local equilibrium distribution function is defined in the co-moving frame with respect to the fluid velocity; the quantum corrections to charge density vanish under the relaxation-time approximation when anomalous hydrodynamic equations of motion are applied, satisfying the matching condition from the CKT.

## Method

The authors employed chiral kinetic theory (CKT) derived from quantum field theory, incorporating side-jump effects and the relaxation-time (RT) approximation for collisions. They first applied continuity equations to derive anomalous hydrodynamic equations of motion (EOM) from equilibrium Wigner functions, then solved for non-equilibrium distribution functions perturbatively in gradient expansions using the CKT with the anomalous hydrodynamic EOM. Finally, they computed the charge current and charge density directly from the non-equilibrium Wigner functions, considering both quantum corrections from the anomalous hydrodynamic equations and from the CKT and Wigner functions.

## Key numbers

* The second-order nonlinear response for charge currents under a "naive" RT approximation (without anomalous hydrodynamic EOM) gives terms proportional to **∇ × E** and **E × ∇μ**, where **E** denotes an electric field and μ denotes a chemical potential.
* When using anomalous hydrodynamic EOM (corresponding to an isolated system), the transport coefficients for the **∇ × E** and **E × ∇μ** terms in charge currents are modified, and additional terms **E × ∇T** and **(∇μ) × (∇T)** emerge, where T denotes temperature.
* For an inviscid chiral fluid, vorticity does not affect the nonlinear responses in charge currents up to the second order.
* The nonlinear quantum correction on charge density vanishes with hydrodynamic EOM in the RT approximation, consistent with the matching condition from the anomalous equation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of second-order nonlinear transport in Weyl-fermion systems using CKT by Gorbar et al. (2016) and Chen et al. (2016), which considered systems without collective motion (applicable to Weyl semimetals where interactions with impurities and phonons dominate). The present paper instead considers systems under the constraint of hydrodynamics with energy-momentum conservation, relevant for quark-gluon plasmas in heavy ion collisions and potentially for strongly interacting quasi-relativistic plasmas in Weyl semimetals. The results enable further investigation of anomalous transport in chiral fluids beyond linear response, particularly for understanding how interactions and collective motion modify the nonlinear transport coefficients that are protected by chiral anomaly at linear order.
