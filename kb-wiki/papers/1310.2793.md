# Fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges in an excluded volume hadron resonance gas model

> We present temperature ($T$) and baryonic chemical potential ($μ_B$) dependence of higher order fluctuations and correlation between conserved charges in Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas (EVHRG) model. Products of moments, such as ratio of variance to mean ($σ^2/M)$, product of skewness and standard deviation ($Sσ$), product of kurtosis and variance ($κσ^2$), for net-proton, net-kaon and net-charge have been evaluated on the phenomenologically determined freeze-out curve. Further, products of moments for net-proton and net-charge have been compared with the experimental data measured by STAR experiment. The dependence of the model result on the hadronic radius parameter has also been discussed.

## Key claim

The Excluded Volume Hadron Resonance Gas (EVHRG) model, which incorporates short-range repulsive interactions via a hard-core hadron radius, reproduces lattice QCD results for second-order susceptibilities up to T ≈ 0.17–0.18 GeV and shows better agreement than the ideal HRG model, but only when the baryon and meson radii are chosen appropriately (0.2–0.3 fm); the model's predictions for higher moments of net-proton and net-charge distributions are compared with STAR experimental data along the freeze-out curve, with the caveat that the hadronic radius parameter significantly affects the results.

## Method

The authors compute the grand canonical partition function for a gas of all hadrons up to 3 GeV mass, incorporating excluded volume corrections via a thermodynamically consistent scheme where the effective chemical potential is reduced by the product of the excluded volume and the total pressure. They evaluate second-, third-, and fourth-order susceptibilities for baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, as well as correlation functions (χ_BS, χ_QS, χ_BQ), as functions of temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B. Products of moments (σ²/M, Sσ, κσ²) for net-proton, net-kaon, and net-charge are calculated along the phenomenologically determined freeze-out curve and compared with STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Second-order baryon susceptibility χ_B² at μ=0: EVHRG with all hadron radii = 0.2 fm agrees with lattice QCD up to T ≈ 0.18 GeV; χ_Q² agrees with lattice for R_m = 0.2 fm and R_b = 0.3 fm.
- At large T (above 0.13 GeV), second-order fluctuations in EVHRG are reduced by approximately 30% compared to non-interacting HRG when all hadron radii are 0.2 fm.
- Fourth-order susceptibilities χ_B⁴ and χ_S⁴ from lattice QCD (N_τ = 6, 8) agree with HRG model up to T = 0.16 GeV; χ_Q⁴ from lattice is lower than both HRG and EVHRG.
- At μ_B = 0.4 GeV and above, the energy, entropy, and number densities in EVHRG are suppressed by a factor of about 2 compared to HRG when all hadron radii are 0.2 fm.
- The baryon-strangeness correlation coefficient C_BS at low temperature is much lower than 1; C_QS at very low temperature is approximately 1.5; C_BQ at low temperature is close to 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, which successfully describes hadron yields from AGS to RHIC energies (Braun-Munzinger, 2003; Cleymans, 1999), by incorporating repulsive interactions via excluded volume corrections as proposed by Rischke (1991) and Cleymans (1993). The study compares EVHRG predictions with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2012) and Borsanyi (2010) for susceptibilities, and with STAR experimental data for higher moments of net-proton and net-charge distributions. The results enable future comparisons with Beam Energy Scan data at RHIC and CBM experiments at FAIR, particularly for identifying signatures of the critical end point through divergent fluctuations.
