# Excitation Spectra of the ${}^{12}{\rm C}(p,d)$ Reaction near the $η'$-Meson Emission Threshold Measured in Coincidence with High-Momentum Protons

> The missing mass of the ${}^{12}{\rm C}(p,d)$ reaction has been measured near the $η'$-meson emission threshold in coincidence with a high-momentum proton to selectively collect $η'$-${}^{11}{\rm C}$ mesic nucleus formation events at GSI, Germany. A 2.5 GeV proton excites a carbon nucleus to form an $η'$-mesic nucleus emitting a deuteron forward with an energy of $\sim 1.6$ GeV. The deuteron is momentum-analyzed by the Fragment Separator used as a high-resolution spectrometer to deduce the excitation energy of the residual system. The large-acceptance detector WASA surrounding the target identifies high-momentum protons emitted in the decay of the $η'$-mesic nucleus. The measured semi-exclusive spectrum exhibits structures below the threshold though the statistical significance is limited. The spectrum is fitted by theoretically calculated spectra varying optical-potential parameters of the $η'$-nucleus interaction. The analysis results indicate $η'$-mesic nuclei formation for the real potential depth of $\sim -61$ MeV with a local statistical significance of $3.5 σ$ and, taking into account the look-elsewhere effect, a global significance of $2.1 σ$.

## Key claim

The measured semi-exclusive excitation spectrum of the 12C(p,d) reaction near the eta' emission threshold, obtained by tagging high-momentum backward protons, exhibits structures at approximately -30 and -6 MeV below threshold that are consistent with the formation of eta'-11C mesic nuclei, with a local statistical significance of 3.5 sigma and a global significance of 2.1 sigma after accounting for the look-elsewhere effect.

## Method

A 2.5 GeV proton beam from the SIS-18 synchrotron at GSI impinged on a carbon target, and forward-emitted deuterons from the 12C(p,d) reaction were momentum-analyzed by the Fragment Separator (FRS) used as a high-resolution spectrometer to deduce the excitation energy of the residual 11C system. The WASA large-acceptance detector surrounding the target identified high-momentum protons (0.7-1.2 GeV/c) emitted in the backward region (theta_p > 90 degrees) from the two-nucleon absorption decay channel of the eta'-mesic nucleus. The measured semi-exclusive spectrum was fitted by theoretically calculated spectra using the Green's function method, varying the real (V0) and imaginary (W0) optical-potential parameters of the eta'-nucleus interaction.

## Key numbers

- Real potential depth V0 = -61 +/- 1 (stat.) +/- 5 (syst.) MeV at nuclear saturation density, deduced from the best fit at (V0, W0) = (-62, -2) MeV
- Local statistical significance of 3.5 sigma (local p-value 2.6 x 10^-4) for the signal at point B (V0 = -62 MeV, W0 = -2 MeV)
- Global significance of 2.1 sigma (global p-value 1.7 x 10^-2) after accounting for the look-elsewhere effect
- Upper limit on the imaginary potential |W0| less than or approximately 10 MeV (68% C.L.)
- Spectral resolution sigma_ex = 1.6 +/- 0.1 MeV
- Chi-squared per degrees of freedom for the best fit: 65.76 / 62; for pedestal-only fit: 77.36 / 63, giving Delta chi-squared = 11.60
- Scale parameter mu = 1.43 +/- 0.42 for the best fit, consistent with unity within uncertainties
- Structures observed at excitation energies E_ex - E0 = -30 MeV and -6 MeV relative to the eta' emission threshold (E0 = 957.78 MeV)
- Number of (p,d) reaction events accumulated: 1.1 x 10^7 over 3 days of data taking
- Reduction factor of 446 in event count after applying the C_alpha cut and theta_p > 90 degrees selection

## Context

This work extends previous inclusive measurements of the 12C(p,d) reaction (Itahashi et al., 2016; Tanaka et al., 2017) that found structureless continua dominated by quasi-free meson production background, where signal cross sections were 2-3 orders of magnitude smaller than the inclusive cross section of 4.9-5.7 microbarn/(sr.MeV). By tagging high-momentum backward protons from the two-nucleon absorption decay channel, the signal-to-background ratio was improved by a factor of approximately 100 as predicted by transport simulations using JAM (Nara et al., 2000) and GiBUU. The derived potential depth V0 = -61 MeV agrees with photoproduction experiments (Nanova et al., 2013; Friedrich et al., 2016) within uncertainties and is consistent with quark-meson coupling model calculations (Cobos and Tsushima, 2024) and the chiral unitary approach (Nagahiro et al., 2012), but conflicts with recent calculations by Kumar (2025) and Gal (2025). This result enables future measurements with wider excitation energy ranges, increased detector acceptance for back-to-back two-nucleon detection, and studies using different decay channels such as K-Lambda at J-PARC or FAIR.
