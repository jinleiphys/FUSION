# Nuclear polarization corrections in the mu-4He+ Lamb shift

> Stimulated by the proton radius conundrum, measurements of the Lamb shift in various light muonic atoms are planned at PSI. The aim is to extract the rms charge radius with high precision, limited by the uncertainty in the nuclear polarization corrections. We present an ab-initio calculation of the nuclear polarization for mu-4He+ leading to an energy correction in the 2S-2P transitions of $δ_{pol}=-2.47$ meV $\pm$ 6%. We use two different state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonians and utilize the Lorentz integral transform with hyperspherical harmonics expansion as few-body methods. We take into account the leading multipole contributions, plus Coulomb, relativistic and finite-nucleon-size corrections. Our main source of uncertainty is the nuclear Hamiltonian, which currently limits the attainable accuracy. Our predictions considerably reduce the uncertainty with respect to previous estimates and should timely serve the mu-4He+ experiment planned for 2013.

## Key claim

The nuclear polarization correction for the mu-4He+ Lamb shift is calculated to be δ_{pol} = -2.47 meV ± 6%, which considerably reduces the uncertainty from previous estimates of -3.1 meV ± 20% but remains limited by the choice of nuclear Hamiltonian rather than by computational precision.

## Method

The authors performed an ab-initio calculation of the nuclear polarization correction using two state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonians: the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon force supplemented by the Urbana IX three-nucleon force (AV18/UIX) and a chiral effective field theory potential (χEFT) at N3LO/N2LO. They employed the Lorentz integral transform method combined with hyperspherical harmonics expansion to compute nuclear structure functions, and included leading multipole contributions plus Coulomb, relativistic, and finite-nucleon-size corrections up to order (Zα)^5.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear polarization correction for mu-4He+: δ^{A}_{pol} = -2.47 meV ± 6% (average of AV18/UIX and χEFT results)
- AV18/UIX result: δ^{A}_{pol} = -2.408 meV; χEFT result: δ^{A}_{pol} = -2.542 meV
- Leading dipole contribution δ^{(0)}_{D1}: -4.418 meV (AV18/UIX) and -4.701 meV (χEFT)
- Previous estimates gave δ^{A}_{pol} = -3.1 meV ± 20% (Bernabeu 1973, Rinker 1976, Friar 1977)
- 4He binding energy: 28.422 MeV (AV18/UIX) and 28.343 MeV (χEFT); experimental value within 0.3% and 0.1% respectively
- Point-proton radius: 1.432 fm (AV18/UIX) and 1.475 fm (χEFT); experimental value within 3% and 0.3% respectively
- Electric-dipole polarizability α_E: 0.0651 fm³ (AV18/UIX) and 0.0694 fm³ (χEFT)
- Threshold excitation energy of 4He: ω_{th} = 19.8 MeV
- Expansion parameter ξ√(2m_r ω) ≈ √(m_r/M_A) ≈ 0.17

## Context

This work extends ab-initio calculations of nuclear polarization from muonic deuterium (Pachucki 2011, Friar 2013) to muonic 4He+ for the first time. It significantly improves upon earlier estimates for mu-4He+ that used simple nuclear potentials or photo-absorption cross sections (Bernabeu 1973, Rinker 1976, Friar 1977), which had 20% uncertainty. The result enables extraction of the 4He charge radius from the planned PSI mu-4He+ Lamb shift measurement with a relative accuracy of 3×10⁻⁴, provided the intrinsic nucleon polarizability is evaluated separately.
