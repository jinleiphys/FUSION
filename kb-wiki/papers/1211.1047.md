# Jet-Tagged Back-Scattering Photons For Quark Gluon Plasma Tomography

> Direct photons are important probes for quark gluon plasma created in high energy nuclear collisions. Various sources of direct photons in nuclear collisions are known, each of them endowed with characteristic information about the production process. However, it has been challenging to separate direct photon sources through measurements of single inclusive photon spectra and photon azimuthal asymmetry. Here we explore a method to identify photons created from the back-scattering of high momentum quarks off quark gluon plasma. We show that the correlation of back-scattering photons with a trigger jet leads to a signal that should be measurable at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The authors propose that back-scattering photons from high-momentum quarks interacting with quark-gluon plasma can be isolated from other direct photon sources by using a jet trigger, with the signal appearing as a characteristic enhancement in the nuclear modification factor a few GeV below the trigger jet energy, rather than claiming a definitive detection.

## Method

The authors use the JETPHOX code to calculate jet-photon and jet-hadron yields at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy for trigger jets fixed around midrapidity in narrow transverse energy windows. They then use the PPM code to propagate leading jet partons of the away-side jet through a longitudinally boost-invariant fireball parameterization tuned to measured RHIC and LHC multiplicities, calculating energy loss and the back-scattering photon production rate proportional to \(T^2 \ln 1/T\). The photon spectrum is considered in a sector of \(\pm 15^\circ\) around the away-side direction relative to the trigger jet.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, a trigger jet window of \(E_T=60\)–\(65\) GeV/\(c\) is used; the back-scattering signal shows a strong correlation with the initial hard photon peak in the trigger window, with energy loss producing a tail extending to lower momenta.
- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, a trigger jet energy window of \(E_T=30\)–\(35\) GeV/\(c\) is used.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is approximated as the ratio of signal (back-scattering) over the sum of signal and background (initial hard and fragmentation photons); a characteristic peak appears just below the trigger jet window.
- The back-scattering photon rate formula includes a constant \(C=-1.916\) and a logarithmic factor \(\ln(3E_\gamma / \alpha_s \pi T)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Fries (2002) and Fries (2005) that estimated back-scattering photon yields from quarks scattering off thermal gluons and antiquarks in QGP, and by Turbide (2007) showing that such photons contribute to the single inclusive direct photon spectrum at intermediate \(P_T\). The authors address the difficulty of separating jet-medium photon sources from hard initial and fragmentation photons (dominant at larger \(P_T\)) and thermal photons (dominant at lower \(P_T\)), as well as the inconclusive experimental situation regarding azimuthal asymmetry \(v_2\) signatures proposed by Turbide (2005). The proposed jet-tagging method enables future measurements at RHIC and LHC to extract medium temperature and parton energy loss information from the back-scattering peak.
