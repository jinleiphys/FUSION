# Equation of state for $β$-stable hot nuclear matter

> We provide an equation of state for hot nuclear matter in $β$-equilibrium by applying a momentum-dependent effective interaction. We focus on the study of the equation of state of high-density and high-temperature nuclear matter, containing leptons (electrons and muons) under the chemical equilibrium condition in which neutrinos have left the system. The conditions of charge neutrality and equilibrium under $β$-decay process lead first to the evaluation of proton and lepton fractions and afterwards of internal energy, free energy, pressure and in total to the equation of state of hot nuclear matter. Thermal effects on the properties and equation of state of nuclear matter are assesed and analyzed in the framework of the proposed effective interaction model. Special attention is dedicated to the study of the contribution of the components of $β$-stable nuclear matter to the entropy per particle, a quantity of great interest for the study of structure and collapse of supernova.

# Equation of state for β-stable hot nuclear matter

## Key claim

The authors provide an equation of state for hot nuclear matter in β-equilibrium using a momentum-dependent effective interaction model (MDIM) that, unlike some prior approaches, allows temperature to affect not only the kinetic part of the energy density but also the interaction part, though thermal effects on the momentum-dependent interaction term become significant only above T = 10 MeV and remain modest (3-20% increase relative to the cold case depending on density).

## Method

The authors apply a momentum-dependent effective interaction model (MDIM) parameterized to reproduce microscopic calculations of nuclear and neutron-rich matter at zero temperature, then extend it to finite temperature. They solve self-consistently the conditions of β-equilibrium (μ_n = μ_p + μ_e, μ_e = μ_μ) and charge neutrality (n_p = n_e + n_μ) for nuclear matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons with neutrinos having left the system, calculating proton fractions, lepton fractions, internal energy, free energy, pressure, and entropy per particle over a density range of n_0 < n < 6n_0 and temperature range 0 < T < 30 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³; the study covers densities from n_0 to 6n_0 and temperatures from 0 to 30 MeV
- Symmetric nuclear matter binding energy at saturation: E(n = n_0) - mc² = -16 MeV, with incompressibility K = 240 MeV
- For the momentum-dependent interaction term V_C at n = 0.15 fm⁻³, thermal effects produce increases of 3% at T = 10 MeV and 20% at T = 30 MeV relative to the T = 0 case
- The symmetry energy at zero temperature is parameterized as E_sym^{tot}(u, T=0) = 13 u^{2/3} + 17 u, where u = n/n_0
- Muon threshold occurs at μ_μ = m_μ c² ≈ 105.7 MeV, expected to appear roughly at nuclear density n = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Finite range parameters for the momentum-dependent interaction: Λ₁ = 1.5 k_F⁰ and Λ₂ = 3 k_F⁰, where k_F⁰ is the Fermi momentum at saturation

## Context

This work extends the momentum-dependent effective interaction model of Prakash (1997) to study thermal effects on the equation of state of β-stable nuclear matter, with particular attention to the symmetry free energy and its impact on proton fraction and composition. The model reproduces results from microscopic calculations by Wiringa (1988) and Pandharipande (1998) for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, and the single-particle potential agrees with UV14+TNI, UV14+UVII, AV14+UVII, and DBHF interactions up to k = 3 fm⁻¹. The results enable improved modeling of supernova structure and collapse, hot neutron star properties at the birth stage, and neutrino process rates during cooling, building on prior work by Baym (1971), Bethe (1979), Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), Lattimer and Swesty (1991), and Takatsuka (1994, 1996).
