# Investigating nuclear beta decay using lattice quantum Monte Carlo approach

> We present an \textit{ab initio} calculation of nuclear $β$ decay within the framework of nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT), employing auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo methods to solve the nuclear many-body problem. Our approach combines next-to-next-to-leading order two- and three-body chiral interactions with one- and two-body axial current operators, all consistently derived in chiral effective field theory. Low-energy constants are determined exclusively from nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and few-body observables for systems with $A \leq 3$. Using these interactions and transition operators, we perform two-channel Monte Carlo simulations to compute the $β$-decay matrix element for $^6$He, obtaining results in reasonable agreement with experimental measurements. To address the Monte Carlo sign problem, we implement a perturbative expansion around a leading-order Hamiltonian with approximate Wigner-SU(4) symmetry. This systematic approach provides a foundation for extending NLEFT simulations to precision studies of weak processes in medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors present an *ab initio* calculation of nuclear beta decay using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo, but the results are obtained at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) in the chiral interaction rather than the higher-fidelity N³LO interaction, and the method relies on a perturbative expansion around an approximate Wigner-SU(4) symmetric Hamiltonian to control the sign problem.

## Method
The authors discretize N²LO chiral two- and three-body nuclear forces and one- and two-body axial currents on a cubic lattice with spacing 1.64 fm, then solve the many-body problem using auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo with a perturbative expansion around a leading-order Hamiltonian that respects approximate Wigner-SU(4) symmetry. For the triton decay, they also perform a direct Lanczos diagonalization of the full Hamiltonian on a 20 fm box to determine the three-body low-energy constants c_D and c_E by fitting to the experimental triton binding energy and half-life. For the ⁶He decay, they use a two-channel Monte Carlo simulation where the initial and final nuclei are evolved separately under the simplified Hamiltonian H₀, and the full transition matrix element is reconstructed via first-order perturbation theory.

## Context
This work extends the first NLEFT study of triton beta decay by Elhatisari et al. (2024), which used N³LO chiral interactions with only leading-order axial currents, by incorporating one- and two-body axial currents beyond leading order and by demonstrating the first lattice Monte Carlo simulation of a two-channel beta decay process (⁶He → ⁶Li). The method provides a foundation for extending NLEFT simulations to precision studies of weak processes in medium-mass nuclei, where traditional shell-model calculations (Brown 1985, Pinedo 1996, Langanke 1995) tend to overestimate Gamow-Teller matrix elements and require ad hoc quenching of g_A.
