# A NICER View of the Nearest and Brightest Millisecond Pulsar: PSR J0437$\unicode{x2013}$4715

> We report Bayesian inference of the mass, radius and hot X-ray emitting region properties - using data from the Neutron Star Interior Composition ExploreR (NICER) - for the brightest rotation-powered millisecond X-ray pulsar PSR J0437$\unicode{x2013}$4715. Our modeling is conditional on informative tight priors on mass, distance and binary inclination obtained from radio pulsar timing using the Parkes Pulsar Timing Array (PPTA) (Reardon et al. 2024), and we use NICER background models to constrain the non-source background, cross-checking with data from XMM-Newton. We assume two distinct hot emitting regions, and various parameterized hot region geometries that are defined in terms of overlapping circles; while simplified, these capture many of the possibilities suggested by detailed modeling of return current heating. For the preferred model identified by our analysis we infer a mass of $M = 1.418 \pm 0.037$ M$_\odot$ (largely informed by the PPTA mass prior) and an equatorial radius of $R = 11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}$ km, each reported as the posterior credible interval bounded by the 16% and 84% quantiles. This radius favors softer dense matter equations of state and is highly consistent with constraints derived from gravitational wave measurements of neutron star binary mergers. The hot regions are inferred to be non-antipodal, and hence inconsistent with a pure centered dipole magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper reports a mass of $M = 1.418 \pm 0.037$ M$_\odot$ and equatorial radius of $R = 11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}$ km for PSR J0437–4715, but the mass measurement is largely informed by the tight prior from radio pulsar timing (PPTA) rather than the X-ray data alone, and the radius favors softer dense matter equations of state.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian pulse profile modeling of NICER X-ray data using the X-PSI software, incorporating relativistic ray-tracing under the Oblate Schwarzschild plus Doppler approximation. They assumed two distinct hot emitting regions with various parameterized geometries (single-temperature, protruding dual-temperature, and concentric ring configurations) and used informative Gaussian priors on mass, distance, and orbital inclination from PPTA radio timing (Reardon 2024). Background was constrained using the 3C50 model with quantified systematic uncertainties, cross-checked against XMM-Newton EPIC MOS spectra and an AGN contribution estimate.

## Key numbers

- Mass: $M = 1.418 \pm 0.037$ M$_\odot$ (68% credible interval, largely from PPTA prior)
- Equatorial radius: $R = 11.36^{+0.95}_{-0.63}$ km (68% credible interval)
- Distance: $156.98 \pm 0.15$ pc (from PPTA radio timing, Reardon 2024)
- Binary inclination: $137.506 \pm 0.016$ degrees (from PPTA)
- Spin frequency: 174 Hz
- 3C50 dataset: 1.310 Ms filtered exposure, 2,095,698 total counts, background-subtracted average intensity 1.079 counts s$^{-1}$ at 0.3–2.0 keV
- AGN contribution: approximately 0.2 counts s$^{-1}$ in 0.3–3 keV band

## Context

This work extends NICER pulse profile modeling results previously reported for PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019, Riley 2019, Salmi 2023, Vinciguerra 2023) and PSR J0740+6620 (Miller 2021, Riley 2021, Salmi 2022, Salmi 2024, Dittmann 2024), which had radius uncertainties of approximately ±10%. PSR J0437–4715 is the brightest millisecond pulsar in the NICER target list and the closest observed MSP, enabling tighter constraints than previous sources. The inferred radius favors softer equations of state consistent with gravitational wave constraints from neutron star binary mergers, and the non-antipodal hot regions contradict a pure centered dipole magnetic field, enabling tests of magnetic field structure models such as those by Bilous (2019), Chen (2020), Kalapotharakos (2021), and Carrasco (2023).
