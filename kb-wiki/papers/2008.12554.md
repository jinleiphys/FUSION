# An analysis of transverse momentum spectra of various jets produced in high energy collisions

> With the framework of the multi-source thermal model, we analyze the experimental transverse momentum spectra of various jets produced in different collisions at high energies. Two energy sources, a projectile participant quark and a target participant quark, are considered. Each energy source (each participant quark) is assumed to contribute to the transverse momentum distribution to be the TP-like function, i.e. a revised Tsallis--Pareto-type function. The contribution of the two participant quarks to the transverse momentum distribution is then the convolution of two TP-like functions. The model distribution can be used to fit the experimental spectra measured by different collaborations. The related parameters such as the entropy index-related, effective temperature, and revised index are then obtained. The trends of these parameters are useful to understand the characteristic of high energy collisions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the transverse momentum spectra of various jets produced in high-energy collisions can be described by a single-component function, specifically the convolution of two TP-like (revised Tsallis-Pareto-type) functions within the multi-source thermal model, but the quality of fits is only approximate and in some cases shows large chi-squared per degree of freedom, indicating the need for further improvement.

## Method

The authors assume that a projectile participant quark and a target participant quark are the two main energy sources for jet production, each contributing a transverse momentum distribution described by a TP-like function (a revised Tsallis-Pareto-type function with an additional power index a0). The total transverse momentum distribution is obtained as the convolution of these two TP-like functions, and this model distribution is fitted to experimental data from STAR, ALICE, D0, CMS, and ATLAS collaborations across multiple collision systems (p-p, p-Pb, d-Au, Au-Au, Pb-Pb) and center-of-mass energies from 0.2 to 13 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV with t+tbar quarks, the effective temperature T = 0.032 +/- 0.008 GeV (mid-eta, |eta|<0.5) and T = 0.045 +/- 0.001 GeV (non-mid-eta, 0.8<eta<1.8)
- For Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with t+tbar quarks, T ranges from 0.130 +/- 0.050 GeV (0-10% centrality) to 0.150 +/- 0.051 GeV (50-80% centrality)
- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV with t+tbar quarks, the entropy index-related parameter n = 2.01 +/- 0.15 for leading jets and n = 2.10 +/- 0.22 for subleading jets
- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV with t+tbar quarks, small-R jets show T = 70.000 +/- 15.100 GeV (e+jet channel) and T = 90.000 +/- 15.520 GeV (mu+jet channel)
- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, the effective temperature T decreases with jet order from 3.600 +/- 0.195 GeV (leading jet) to 0.150 +/- 0.090 GeV (5th jet)

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model previously applied to identified particles (Liu, 2015) to the analysis of jet transverse momentum spectra, using a convolution of TP-like functions rather than the two-component or four-component functions commonly used in the field. The approach provides a unified description across different collision systems (p-p, p-A, A-A) and energy scales, though the authors note that perturbative QCD (pQCD) remains the standard theoretical framework for hard scattering processes. The parameter trends, particularly the effective temperature T and entropy index-related parameter n, enable future comparisons between jet production and identified particle production at different stages of system evolution.
