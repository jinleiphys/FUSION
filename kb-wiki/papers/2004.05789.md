# Yrast band in the heavy $N = Z$ nucleus $^{88}$Ru: $α$-cluster approach

> The yrast band in the heavy $N = Z$ nucleus $^{88}$Ru is studied in the framework of the $α$-cluster model in combination with double-folding potentials. It is found that the excitation energies of the yrast band in $^{88}$Ru can be nicely described within the $α$-cluster approach using a smooth and mildly $L$-dependent adjustment of the potential strength. This result is similar to well-established $α$-cluster states in nuclei with a (magic core $\otimes$ $α$) structure. Contrary, the yrast bands in neighboring $N \ne Z$ nuclei deviate from such a typical $α$-cluster behavior. Finally, the $α$-cluster model predicts reduced transition strengths of about 10 Weisskopf units for intraband transitions between low-lying states in the yrast band of $^{88}$Ru.

## Key claim

The yrast band in the heavy \(N = Z\) nucleus \(^{88}\)Ru can be described within the \(\alpha\)-cluster model using a smooth and mildly \(L\)-dependent adjustment of the potential strength, similar to well-established \(\alpha\)-cluster states in nuclei with a (magic core \(\otimes\) \(\alpha\)) structure; however, the author notes that both the \(\alpha\)-cluster model and shell model approaches with proton-neutron pairing can reproduce the observed excitation energies, so any conclusion on the experimental verification of proton-neutron pairing or significant \(\alpha\)-clustering must remain tentative without further experimental data.

## Method

The author applied the \(\alpha\)-cluster model using double-folding potentials with the energy- and density-dependent DDM3Y interaction. The nuclear potential was calculated by folding the \(\alpha\)-particle density with theoretical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov densities for the core nucleus \(^{84}\)Mo, obtained from Skyrme forces, in the frozen-density approximation. The total potential was the sum of the folded nuclear potential multiplied by a strength parameter \(\lambda\) (adjusted to reproduce experimental excitation energies) and a Coulomb potential from a homogeneously charged sphere. The number of nodes in the bound-state wave function was derived from the Wildermuth condition with \(Q = 16\) oscillator quanta, yielding an yrast band of nine positive-parity states from \(0^+\) to \(16^+\).

## Key numbers

- The yrast band in \(^{88}\)Ru is described up to \(J^\pi = (14^+)\) at excitation energy \(E^\ast = 6949\) keV (from Ceder20).
- The potential strength parameter \(\lambda\) decreases smoothly from about \(\lambda_0 \approx 1.2663\) for the \(0^+\) ground state to about 1.2185 for the \(14^+\) state.
- Extrapolation using a 4th-order fit predicts the unobserved \(J^\pi = 16^+\) state at \(E^\ast \approx 8370\) keV with \(\lambda = 1.2156\).
- The \(\alpha\)-cluster model predicts reduced \(E2\) transition strengths of about 10 Weisskopf units (W.u.) for intraband transitions between low-lying states: \(B(E2, 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+) = 8.8\) W.u., \(B(E2, 4^+ \rightarrow 2^+) = 12.1\) W.u., \(B(E2, 6^+ \rightarrow 4^+) = 12.3\) W.u., and \(B(E2, 8^+ \rightarrow 6^+) = 11.2\) W.u.
- The \(\alpha\)-binding energy of \(^{88}\)Ru is \(S_\alpha = -2.594\) MeV (from AME16).
- The volume integral \(J_R\) of the potential for the \(0^+\) ground state of \(^{88}\)Ru is 352.4 MeV fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the \(\alpha\)-cluster model, previously applied to well-established \(\alpha\)-cluster nuclei like \(^{96}\)Ru with a neutron-magic core (\(N_T = 50\)) (Mohr 2008) and to non-magic nuclei like \(^{46,54}\)Cr (Sou17, Mohr17), to the heavy \(N = Z\) nucleus \(^{88}\)Ru. It provides an alternative interpretation to studies that invoked proton-neutron pairing to explain the delayed band crossing in \(^{88}\)Ru (Ceder20, Hase04, Kan17). The results enable future experimental tests: measurement of \(E2\) transition strengths could distinguish between the \(\alpha\)-cluster model prediction of about 10 W.u. and shell model predictions of 20 or more W.u. (Hase04, Zuker15, Kan17), thereby clarifying the role of \(\alpha\)-clustering versus proton-neutron pairing in this nucleus.
