# Comparison of triton bound state properties using different separable representations of realistic potentials

> The quality of two different separable expansion methods ({\sl W} matrix and Ernst-Shakin-Thaler) is investigated. We compare the triton binding energies and components of the triton wave functions obtained in this way with the results of a direct two-dimensional treatment. The Paris, Bonn {\sl A} and Bonn {\sl B} potentials are employed as underlying two-body interactions, their total angular momenta being incorporated up to $j \leq 2$. It is found that the most accurate results based on the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler method agree within 1.5% or better with the two-dimensional calculations, whereas the results for the {\sl W}-matrix representation are less accurate.

## Key claim

The Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separable expansion method reproduces triton binding energies from direct two-dimensional calculations to within 1.5% or better for the Paris, Bonn A, and Bonn B potentials, while the W-matrix representation yields less accurate results under the same conditions.

## Method

The authors compared two separable expansion methods (W-matrix and EST) by computing triton bound-state properties using the Paris, Bonn A, and Bonn B realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, with total angular momenta included up to j ≤ 2. They solved the three-body bound-state equations using the separable representations and compared the resulting triton binding energies and wave function components against direct solutions of the two-dimensional homogeneous three-body equations. The EST method used form factors derived from the potential acting on scattering wave functions at a set of chosen energies, while the W-matrix method provided a rank-one separable representation of the two-body T matrix that preserves its analytic structure.

## Key numbers

- EST-based triton binding energies agreed with direct two-dimensional calculations within 1.5% or better for all three potentials (Paris, Bonn A, Bonn B)
- The W-matrix representation results were less accurate than the EST results, though no specific numerical deviation is given in the text
- The two-body interactions included total angular momenta up to j ≤ 2

## Context

This work extends earlier tests of separable expansion methods for realistic potentials, particularly the EST method tested for the Paris potential by Haid (1984) and the W-matrix method applied to the Malfliet-Tjon potential by Bart (1987) and Janu (1993). The study provides a systematic comparison of both methods against direct two-dimensional calculations for multiple realistic potentials, enabling reliable use of these separable representations in more complex calculations such as photodisintegration of three-nucleon systems (Schad 1997, Sandhas 1998) and pion absorption (Cant 1997).
