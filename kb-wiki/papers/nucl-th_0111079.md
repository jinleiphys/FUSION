# On contribution of three-body forces to $Nd$ interaction at intermediate energies

> Available data on large-angle nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering $Nd\to dN$ below the pion threshold give a signal for three-body forces. There is a problem of separation of possible subtle aspects of these forces from off-shell effects in two-nucleon potentials. By considering the main mechanisms of the process, we show qualitatively that in the quasi-binary reaction $N+d\to (NN)+N$ with the final spin singlet NN-pair in the S-state the relative contribution of the 3N forces differs substantially from the elastic channel. It gives a new testing ground for the problem in question.

## Key claim

The relative contribution of three-body forces (3NF) to the quasi-binary reaction \(N+d\to (NN)+N\) with a final spin-singlet NN-pair in the \(^1S_0\) state differs substantially from that in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering, providing a new testing ground to separate 3NF effects from off-shell effects in two-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The author uses a Born approximation model combining one-nucleon exchange (ONE), single scattering (SS), and \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation mechanisms, with the \(\Delta\) contribution identified as the 3NF component. Isospin analysis is performed to derive the ratio of 3NF amplitudes for singlet versus triplet final NN-pairs, and the formalism is adapted from the \(pd\to dp\) elastic scattering framework by substituting the deuteron final state with a scattering state of the NN-system at low relative energy. Calculations are performed using the Paris NN-potential with cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_\pi=0.6\) GeV and \(\Lambda_\rho=0.7\) GeV fitted to \(pp\to pn\pi^+\) and \(pd\to dp\) data.

## Key numbers

- The isospin ratio of the 3NF amplitude for the reaction with a final spin-singlet \(pn(^1S_0)\) pair relative to the triplet deuteron state is \(-\frac{1}{3}\), independent of the unknown relative phase or ratio of \(T=1/2\) and \(T=3/2\) amplitudes.
- The final NN-pair relative energy is restricted to \(E_{NN}<3\) MeV to ensure dominance of the \(^1S_0\) state.
- The node in the half-off-shell \(NN\)-scattering amplitude \(t_s(q,k)\) occurs at \(q\sim 0.4\) GeV/c, causing the ONE contribution to vanish at \(\theta_{cm}=100^\circ-130^\circ\) for initial energies 100-300 MeV.
- The node in the deuteron S-wave function \(u(q)\) corresponds to a null of the measured deuteron charge form factor \(G_C(Q)\) at transferred momentum \(Q\sim 4.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The model describes \(pd\to dp\) cross section well at \(T_p=150-250\) MeV and \(\theta_{cm}>120^\circ\).
- At the minimum of the cross section for reaction (r1), \(\theta^n_{cm}=120^\circ-140^\circ\), the role of 3NF increases due to vanishing ONE amplitude and decreasing SS contribution.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of three-body forces in elastic \(Nd\) scattering, where data below 200 MeV (Witala 1994, 1998, 1999; Cadman 2000; Bieber 2000) show that the Tucson-Melbourne 3NF reproduces cross sections but fails to explain tensor analyzing powers (Sakai). The author proposes the reaction \(p+d\to (pp)+n\) as a complementary process to separate 3NF effects from off-shell NN effects, which are difficult to disentangle in elastic scattering alone (Canton; Witala 1999). Future experimental study near the pion threshold combined with rigorous three-body calculations (Gloeckle 1996; Viviani 1998) could provide further insight into the structure of three-nucleon forces.
