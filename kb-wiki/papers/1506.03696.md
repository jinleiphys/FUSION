# Entanglement and Fast Quantum Thermalization in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Let $A$ be subsystem of a larger system $A \cup B$, and $ψ$ be a typical state from the subspace of the Hilbert space ${\cal H}_{AB}$ satisfying an energy constraint. Then $ρ_A(ψ)= {\rm Tr}_B | ψ\rangle \langle ψ|$ is nearly thermal. We discuss how this observation is related to fast thermalization of the central region ($\approx A$) in heavy ion collisions, where $B$ represents other degrees of freedom (soft modes, hard jets, collinear particles) outside of $A$. Entanglement between the modes in $A$ and $B$ plays a central role: the entanglement entropy $S_A$ increases rapidly in the collision. In gauge-gravity duality, $S_A$ is related to the area of extremal surfaces in the bulk, which can be studied using gravitational duals.

## Key claim

Fast thermalization (isotropization) of the central region in heavy ion collisions on timescales of τ ≈ 1 fm or less may be driven by quantum entanglement in the superposition of all possible scattering histories, rather than by the usual incoherent kinetic theory of multiple successive parton scatterings.

## Method

The authors apply a theorem from quantum statistical mechanics (von Neumann, 1929; Winter, 2010) stating that for a typical pure state ψ from a Hilbert subspace satisfying an energy constraint, the reduced density matrix ρ_A of a small subsystem A is nearly thermal provided the environment B has much larger Hilbert space dimension (d_B ≫ d_A). They argue that in heavy ion collisions, the central region A (soft modes) has far fewer degrees of freedom than the environment B (hard jets, collinear particles, soft modes outside A), so the post-collision superposition state ψ at τ ≈ 1 fm should produce a nearly thermal ρ_A through entanglement alone, without requiring multiple incoherent scatterings.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the rapidity of original nucleons drops from about 6 to 5, so at most about 40 percent of the total energy in the two-nucleus system ends up in the central region.
- The timescale for fast thermalization considered is τ ≈ 1 fm after the initial overlap of the two nuclei.
- Typical soft-mode energies in hydrodynamical simulations are 200 to 300 MeV; at τ ≈ 1 fm, at most a single scattering with momentum transfer less than about 200 MeV per particle is possible in the incoherent picture.

## Context

This work extends results on typical states and quantum thermalization from von Neumann (1929), Winter (2010), and others (Gemmer, Goldstein) to heavy ion collisions, contrasting with the standard kinetic theory picture of incoherent parton scatterings. It builds on earlier work on entanglement entropy and thermalization in strong interactions by Elze, Muller, and others. The paper enables further study of fast thermalization using gauge-gravity duality, where the Ryu-Takayanagi formula relates entanglement entropy to extremal surface areas in AdS models, and where colliding gravitational shocks have been studied by Yaffe and collaborators.
