# Radial and Non-Radial Oscillations of Protoneutron Stars with Hyperonic Composition

> This paper explores radial and non-radial oscillations of protoneutron stars (PNSs) as they evolve from hot, neutrino-rich configurations through deleptonization to cold, catalyzed states. The equation of state (EoS) is modeled using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field framework, with stellar evolution characterized by changes in entropy and lepton fraction. Both nucleonic and hyperonic compositions are considered. Non-radial $f$- and $p_1$-mode oscillations are computed using both the Cowling approximation and the full General Relativistic framework. Trapped neutrinos initially increase the error in the Cowling approximation for $f$-modes, which decreases during deleptonization and rises again in the cold phase. In contrast, $p_1$-mode errors peak during intermediate stages due to evolving pressure and density gradients. The emergence of hyperons modestly raises oscillation frequencies in both modes. Existing universal relations for $f$-mode frequency and damping time lack model independence for PNSs, motivating a more robust relation. In particular, our proposed universal relation involving the moment of inertia and $\tildeη$ shows strong agreement across all evolutionary phases, offering a temperature-sensitive, model-independent scaling for asteroseismology. Radial oscillations of a $1.4\,M_\odot$ PNS are also studied for different EoSs. Our results show that displacement ($ξ$) and pressure perturbation ($η$) profiles are highly sensitive to thermal state, composition, and compactness. Hyperonic stars show higher frequencies, altered node structures, and stronger pressure perturbations due to EoS softening. Differences in frequency separation $Δν_n$ and fundamental frequency $ν_0$ between nucleonic and hyperonic models provide clear observational diagnostics for probing the interiors of PNSs and constraining the EoS of dense matter.

# Radial and Non-Radial Oscillations of Protoneutron Stars with Hyperonic Composition

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that existing universal relations for f-mode frequency and damping time lack model independence for protoneutron stars (PNSs), and proposes a new universal relation involving the moment of inertia and effective compactness that remains valid across all evolutionary phases from hot, neutrino-rich configurations to cold, catalyzed states. The authors qualify that the Cowling approximation error for f-modes is smallest in hot, moderate-lepton PNSs with hyperons present, while for p1-modes it is lowest in cooler, high-lepton configurations.

## Method
The authors model the equation of state using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDME2 parameterization) framework with both nucleonic and hyperonic compositions, applying charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions. Non-radial f- and p1-mode oscillations are computed using both the Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) and the full General Relativistic framework, while radial oscillations are analyzed for a 1.4 solar mass PNS. The stellar evolution is characterized through four distinct snapshots: neutrino-trapped phase (entropy per baryon s_B=1, lepton fraction Y_l=0.4), deleptonization phase (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), neutrino-transparent cooling phase (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), and cold catalyzed state (T=0).

- Maximum mass for nucleonic stars: 2.44 solar masses (s_B=1, Y_l=0.4), 2.49 solar masses (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), 2.49 solar masses (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), 2.48 solar masses (T=0)
- Maximum mass for hyperonic stars: 2.33 solar masses (s_B=1, Y_l=0.4), 2.29 solar masses (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), 2.28 solar masses (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), 2.26 solar masses (T=0)
- f-mode frequency at 1.4 solar masses (full GR, nucleonic): 1.57 kHz (s_B=1, Y_l=0.4), 1.45 kHz (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), 1.46 kHz (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), 1.60 kHz (T=0)
- Percentage error of Cowling approximation for f-mode at 1.4 solar masses: 21.66% (s_B=1, Y_l=0.4), 20.69% (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), 19.18% (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), 28.13% (T=0) for nucleonic stars
- Damping time at 1.4 solar masses (full GR, nucleonic): 0.288 s (s_B=1, Y_l=0.4), 0.339 s (s_B=2, Y_l=0.2), 0.334 s (s_B=2, Y_nu_e=0), 0.285 s (T=0)
- Baryon saturation density n_0 = 0.152 fm^{-3} under DDME2 parameterization
- Nuclear matter properties: binding energy B/A = -16.4 MeV, compressibility modulus K_0 = 251.9 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.3 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 51.3 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Barman (2024), which studied non-radial oscillations in PNSs using full GR but did not include neutrino effects or hyperonic matter. It builds on the equation of state framework from Issifu (2023) and Issifu (2024), which was applied to quark core formation and mirror dark matter studies. The paper provides the first integrated analysis comparing Cowling approximation with full GR across the complete PNS evolutionary path with hyperonic degrees of freedom, enabling future asteroseismology studies to use the proposed temperature-sensitive universal relation for constraining the dense matter equation of state from gravitational wave observations.
