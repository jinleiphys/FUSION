# Theory of the electromagnetic production of hyperons

> Some basic properties of isobar models are discussed using the Saclay-Lyon, Kaon-MAID, and H2 models and a comparison of their predictions with experimental data is given for photo- and electroproduction of $K^+$ on the proton and for photoproduction of $K^0$ on the deuteron in specific kinematical regions. Results of the isobar models are also compared with the Regge-isobar hybrid model, Regge-plus-resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the different mechanisms used by isobar models to reduce the large Born-term contribution, either by including hyperon resonances (Saclay-Lyon A) or by applying hadronic form factors (Kaon-MAID, H2), lead to markedly different predictions for kaon photoproduction at very forward angles, and that existing data are insufficient to discriminate between them, especially for energies above 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors compare predictions of three isobar models (Saclay-Lyon A, Kaon-MAID, and H2) and several versions of the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model for photo- and electroproduction of \(K^+\) on the proton and photoproduction of \(K^0\) on the deuteron. They compute cross sections and momentum distributions using tree-level Feynman diagrams with different treatments of Born-term reduction (hyperon resonances vs. hadronic form factors) and compare them to experimental data from CLAS, SAPHIR, LEPS, SLAC, and other experiments. For the deuteron case, they use plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Saclay-Lyon A (SLA) model fits the deuteron data for \(d(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda p\) with \(\chi^2/n.d.f. = 0.64\), compared to 1.09 for H2 and 1.75 for KM2 (the authors' version of Kaon-MAID).
- The fitted values of the ratio \(r_{K_1K\gamma}\) (electromagnetic coupling ratio for neutral and charged modes of \(K_1\)) are -1.41, 0.47, and 7.75 for SLA, KM2, and H2, respectively.
- The RPR model describes data from threshold up to \(E_\gamma^{lab}\approx 16\) GeV (center-of-mass energy \(W = 5.56\) GeV).
- At \(E_\gamma^{lab}=2.15\) GeV and \(Q^2 = 0.07\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), the transverse cross section from the JLab Hall A data (E94-107) is \(\sigma_T\approx 0.38\ \mu\)b/sr.
- The KM model's original value for \(r_{K_1K\gamma}\) is -0.45; the fitted value in KM2 is 0.47 (opposite sign).

## Context

This work extends comparisons of isobar models (Saclay-Lyon, Kaon-MAID, H2) and the Regge-plus-resonance approach (RPR-2007, RPR-2011A, RPR-2011B) for hyperon photoproduction, building on earlier analyses by Bydzovsky and Sotona (2008) and the Kaon-MAID model by Mart and Bennhold (2002). The paper identifies the need for new experimental data at very small kaon angles (0–20 degrees) and energies \(2 < W < 3\) GeV to resolve model discrepancies, which would reduce uncertainties in calculations of hypernucleus electroproduction. The results also inform the role of longitudinal couplings in electroproduction, as tested against recent MAMI data.
