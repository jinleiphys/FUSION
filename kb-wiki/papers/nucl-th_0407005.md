# Light-Front Dynamics and Generalized Parton Distributions

> In this work we report on using light-front dynamics to describe generalized parton distributions, which are correlation functions encountered in virtual Compton scattering at large momentum transfer. This two photon process requires pair annihilation contributions which are subtle to handle in light-front dynamics. We are careful to derive such contributions in the light-front Bethe-Salpeter formalism. This derivation highlights the connection to the Fock space picture as well as provides a toy model for generalized parton distributions.Ultimately we use light-front dynamics to build a phenomenological parametrization for the proton's generalized parton distributions. This task is non-trivial due to the field theoretic constraints required of these distributions. To meet the constraints imposed by Lorentz covariance, we approach generalized parton distributions from the perspective of double distributions. Despite avid phenomenological use, surprisingly little work has been done to calculate double distributions. We first focus on a number of simple pedagogical examples and demonstrate how double distributions can be correctly determined. The double distributions we derive, including those for the proton, satisfy known constraints and are adequate for phenomenological estimates of cross sections. The models used allow one to study the interplay between the light-front formulation and Lorentz covariance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that light-front dynamics can be used to construct a phenomenological parametrization of the proton's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that satisfies Lorentz covariance constraints, but only by approaching the problem through double distributions rather than directly from the Fock space picture, and with the caveat that the derivation requires careful handling of pair annihilation contributions in the light-front Bethe-Salpeter formalism.

## Method

The author derives generalized parton distributions from the light-front Bethe-Salpeter formalism, using the ladder model in (3+1) dimensions as a toy system. The approach proceeds by first calculating double distributions from simple pedagogical examples, then extending the method to the proton, with the light-cone bound-state equation solved via a reduction scheme that separates kinematical and dynamical Poincaré generators. The electromagnetic form factors are computed to first order in the interaction using the Drell-Yan formula and its generalizations, with contributions from both valence (x > ζ) and non-valence (x < ζ) kinematic regions.

## Context

This work extends the light-front dynamics approach to generalized parton distributions, building on Dirac's (1949) classification of forms of relativistic dynamics and the Drell-Yan (1970) and West (1970) formulas for form factors. The paper provides a toy model for GPDs that connects to the Fock space picture via the overlap representation developed by Diehl (2000) and Brodsky (2000), while also addressing the challenge of timelike processes and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) that lack a direct Fock decomposition. The results enable phenomenological estimates of cross sections for virtual Compton scattering at large momentum transfer.
