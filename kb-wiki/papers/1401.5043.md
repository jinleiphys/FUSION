# Jet-quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ in the nonperturbative region

> We investigate the jet-quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) for $N_c=3$, defined nonperturbatively with the Wilson loop in the light-cone (LC) coordinate, at finite temperature $(T)$. Considering the effective static (heavy) quark-antiquark potential $V_{\bar{Q}Q}=σL+C-A/L$, where $L$ indicates the LC transverse separation between the quarks, we obtain $\hat{q}\approx8V_{\bar{Q}Q}/L^2$. The $T$ dependences for $L$ and other relevant parameters are extracted from the $T$-dependent instanton (trivial-holonomy caloron) length parameters and the lattice QCD data. By choosing $L\approx{a}\approx\barρ_T$, in which $a$ and $\barρ_T$ denote the lattice spacing and the $T$-dependent average (anti)instanton size, respectively, we acquire numerically that $\hat{q}=(5\sim25)\,\mathrm{GeV/fm}$ for $T=(0\sim0.6)$ GeV, and these values are well consistent with other estimations from AdS/CFT and experimental analyses. It turns out that $\hat{q}$ is produced almost by the Coulomb and constant potentials of $V_{\bar{Q}Q}$. We also observe that the ratio $T^3/\hat{q}$ turns into being saturated to $\sim4.45\times10^{-2}$ for $T\gtrsim0.4$ GeV, indicating the strongly-coupled QGP, and $\hat{q}$ behaves proportionally to $T^3$ for high $T$.

## Key claim

The jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) is computed nonperturbatively using a relation \(\hat{q} \approx 8V_{\bar{Q}Q}/L^2\) derived from an effective static quark-antiquark potential, with the transverse separation \(L\) chosen to be approximately equal to the temperature-dependent average instanton size \(\bar{\rho}_T\); the resulting values are consistent with AdS/CFT and experimental estimates, but the calculation relies on a specific choice of length scales from the instanton model and lattice QCD data.

## Method

The author defines \(\hat{q}\) nonperturbatively via the vacuum expectation value of a light-cone Wilson loop in the adjoint representation, equating it to an effective Wilson loop constructed from the static quark-antiquark potential \(V_{\bar{Q}Q} = \sigma L + C - A/L\). The temperature dependence of the relevant parameters (average instanton size \(\bar{\rho}_T\), instanton number density \(n_T\), and string tension \(\sigma_T\)) is extracted from the modified liquid-instanton model using the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution and from lattice QCD data for the string tension. The transverse separation \(L\) is set equal to the lattice spacing \(a\) and to \(\bar{\rho}_T\), while the light-cone distance \(L^-\) is related to the average inter-instanton distance \(\bar{R}_T\).

## Key numbers

*   \(\hat{q} = (5 \sim 25)\,\mathrm{GeV/fm}\) for \(T = (0 \sim 0.6)\) GeV.
*   The ratio \(T^3/\hat{q}\) saturates to \(\sim 4.45 \times 10^{-2}\) for \(T \gtrsim 0.4\) GeV.
*   At \(T=0\), the average instanton size \(\bar{\rho}_0 = 0.345\) fm, the instanton number density \(n_0 = 1.625 \times 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{GeV}^4\), and the gluon condensate \(\mathcal{G}_0 = 0.013\,\mathrm{GeV}^4\).
*   The asymptotic expression for high \(T\) is \(\hat{q}_{\infty} \approx 177.743 \times T^3\,[\mathrm{GeV/fm}]\) for \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends nonperturbative definitions of \(\hat{q}\) from the Wilson loop approach of Liu (2006) and the perturbative definition of Majumder (2007) by incorporating temperature-dependent instanton parameters and lattice QCD data. The results are consistent with AdS/CFT calculations for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric QCD by Liu (2006) and with time-averaged \(\hat{q}\) extracted from RHIC experiments by Eskola (2004) and Dainese (2004), while being larger than results from SU(2) lattice QCD by Majumder (2012). The study enables further investigation of the relation between \(\eta/s\) and \(T^3/\hat{q}\) to characterize the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma.
