# Hyperon electromagnetic timelike elastic form factors at large $q^2$

> We present estimates of the hyperon elastic form factors for the baryon octet and the $Ω^-$ baryon for large four-momentum transfer squared, $q^2$, in the timelike region ($q^2>0$). Experimentally, those form factors can be extracted from the $e^+ e^- \to B \bar B$ and $p \bar p \to B \bar B$ processes, where $B$ stands for a general baryon. Our results are based on calculations of the elastic electromagnetic form factors in the spacelike region ($Q^2 = - q^2 > 0$) within a covariant quark model. To connect the results in the spacelike region to those in the timelike region, we use asymptotic relations between the two regions which are constraints derived from analyticity and unitarity. We calculate the effective form factors $|G(q^2)|$ and compare them with the integrated cross section data $σ_{\rm Born} (q^2)$ from BaBar, BES III, and CLEO. The available data are at the moment restricted to $Λ$, $Σ^0$, $Σ^-$, $Ξ^-$, $Ξ^0$, and $Ω^-$ as well as to $e^+ e^- \to Λ\bar Σ^0 $ and $e^+ e^- \to Σ^0 \bar Λ$ reactions. Our results provide useful reference for future experiments and seem to indicate that the present data are still in the non-perturbative QCD region, while the onset for the asymptotic constraints from analyticity and unitarity happens much before the region of the perturbative QCD falloff of the form factors.

## Key claim

The authors estimate hyperon timelike elastic form factors at large four-momentum transfer squared by extrapolating spacelike-region calculations from a covariant quark model using asymptotic relations derived from analyticity and unitarity, but they note that these relations become valid at much lower \(q^2\) than the region where perturbative QCD falloff begins, and the present data still lie in the non-perturbative QCD region.

## Method

The authors first computed the elastic electromagnetic form factors of octet baryons (\(\Lambda, \Sigma^0, \Sigma^-, \Xi^-, \Xi^0\)) and the \(\Omega^-\) baryon in the spacelike region (\(Q^2 = -q^2 > 0\)) using a covariant spectator quark model with a quark-diquark structure, \(SU(3)\) flavor symmetry breaking, and vector meson dominance parametrization of the quark current. They then applied model-independent asymptotic relations (based on the Phragmén–Lindelöf theorem) to map the spacelike form factors to the timelike region (\(q^2 > 0\)), using a central estimate with a shift \(q^2 \to 2M_B^2 - q^2\) and upper/lower bounds from \(q^2 \to 4M_B^2 - q^2\) and \(q^2 \to -q^2\), respectively. The resulting effective form factor \(|G(q^2)|\) was compared with integrated cross section data from BaBar, BES III, and CLEO.

## Key numbers

* For the \(\Lambda\) baryon, data from BaBar, BES III, and CLEO cover \(q^2\) up to about 9 GeV\(^2\) (BaBar) and near 14.2 and 17.4 GeV\(^2\) (CLEO from \(\psi(3770)\) and \(\psi(4170)\) decays).
* For \(\Sigma^0\), \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Xi^-\), and \(\Xi^0\), data exist at \(q^2 \simeq 14.2\) GeV\(^2\) from BES III [\(\psi(3770)\) decay] and at \(q^2 \simeq 14.2\) and 17.4 GeV\(^2\) from CLEO.
* The model predicts that the asymptotic relations become valid much below the region where the perturbative QCD power-law falloff \(G_M \propto 1/q^4\) and \(G_E \propto 1/q^4\) dominates, with the authors providing estimates for \(q^2 > 20\) GeV\(^2\) for future experiments.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to nucleon and decuplet baryon form factors in the spacelike region (Ramalho et al., 2013; 2016) to the timelike region for hyperons, filling a gap where theoretical calculations are scarce. The results are compared with experimental data from BaBar (Aubert, 2007), BES III (Ablikim, 2013; 2018), and CLEO (Dobbs, 2014; 2017), and are intended to guide future experiments such as PANDA at FAIR-GSI (Singh, 2016). The work also tests the onset of asymptotic relations derived from analyticity and unitarity (Pacetti, 2015), showing they hold at lower \(q^2\) than the perturbative QCD regime.
