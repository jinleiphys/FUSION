# The nn quasi-free nd breakup cross section: discrepancies to theory and implications on the 1S0 nn force

> Large discrepancies between quasi-free neutron-neutron (nn) cross section data from neutron-deuteron (nd) breakup and theoretical predictions based on standard nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) forces are pointed out. The nn 1S0 interaction is shown to be dominant in that configuration and has to be increase to bring theory and data into agreement. Using the next-to-leading order (NLO) 1S0 interaction of chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) we demonstrate that the nn QFS cross section only slightly depends on changes of the nn scattering length but is very sensitive to variations of the effective range parameter. In order to account for the reported discrepancies one must decrease the nn effective range parameter by about 12 % from its value implied by 19charge symmetry and charge independence of nuclear forces.

# The nn quasi-free nd breakup cross section: discrepancies to theory and implications on the 1S0 nn force

## Key claim

The nn quasi-free scattering (QFS) cross section data from nd breakup at 25-26 MeV exceed theoretical predictions by about 18%, and this discrepancy can be removed by decreasing the nn 1S0 effective range parameter by approximately 12% from its charge-symmetry value of 2.75 fm to about 2.41 fm, while the nn scattering length has only a minor effect on the cross section.

## Method

The authors performed rigorous three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev calculations for nd breakup in the nn QFS configuration, using high-precision NN potentials (CD Bonn, Nijm I, Nijm II) alone and combined with the TM99 three-nucleon force. They then employed the next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory (chiPT) potential, which parametrizes the 1S0 interaction with two contact terms, to independently vary the nn scattering length and effective range parameters and study their separate effects on the QFS cross section.

## Key numbers

- The nn QFS cross section data at E_n^lab = 26 MeV (Siepe 2002) and E_n^lab = 25 MeV (Ruan 2001) overestimate the nd theory by approximately 18%.
- To remove the discrepancy, the nn 1S0 effective range parameter must be decreased from r_eff = 2.75 fm (charge-symmetry value) to r_eff = 2.41 fm, a change of about 12%.
- The required enhancement of the 1S0 nn force corresponds to a factor lambda = 1.08 multiplying the CD Bonn 1S0 matrix element.
- Varying the nn scattering length from a_nn = -10.9 fm to a_nn = -75.9 fm changed the nn QFS cross section maximum by less than about +/- 4%.
- Varying the effective range from r_eff = 2.03 fm to r_eff = 3.07 fm produced much stronger variations in the nn QFS cross section.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Witala (2010) showing that the nn QFS cross section is dominated by the 1S0 nn force component at energies below about 30 MeV, while the np QFS cross section is dominated by the 3S1-3D1 force. The discrepancy contrasts with the good agreement between theory and np QFS data from the same experiment (Siepe 2002). The authors propose that if the data are correct, the required large isospin breaking in the effective range (about 12%) is not supported by current chiPT estimates (Epelbaum, private communication), which predict isospin breaking effects under about 1%, and suggest that a di-neutron bound state may exist. They propose new experimental investigations using the 3H(gamma,p)nn reaction and evaluate proton spectra at various emission angles to search for such a state.
