# An Exactly Solvable Model of Interacting Bosons

> We introduce a class of exactly solvable boson models. We give explicit analytic expressions for energy eigenvalues and eigenvectors for an sd-boson Hamiltonian, which is related to the SO(6) chain of the Interacting Boson Model Hamiltonian.

# An Exactly Solvable Model of Interacting Bosons

## Key claim

The authors introduce a class of exactly solvable boson models for which explicit analytic energy level expressions can be given for all levels and analytic expressions can be given for at least the few lowest eigenstates, but only when the Hamiltonian parameter λ is real does the Hamiltonian become Hermitian with real eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors construct a one-parameter family of mutually commuting operators based on Gaudin algebra realizations of SU(1,1) using s- and d-boson creation and annihilation operators. They diagonalize these operators simultaneously by applying a Bethe ansatz, where eigenstates are built by acting with parametric raising operators on the boson vacuum, and the parameters satisfy a set of nonlinear constraint equations (Bethe ansatz equations). The energy eigenvalues are then expressed in closed form without explicitly solving these equations, while the eigenstates require solving for roots of hypergeometric polynomials.

## Key numbers

- The d-boson quasi-spin group SU(1,1) has eigenvalues of the operator S^0 given by (n/2) + (1+α)/4, where n is the total number of bosons and α = 0, 1, and 2 for s-, p-, and d-bosons respectively.
- For the s-d boson model, the function W(λ) takes the form W(λ) = (1/4)[5/(1-λ) - 1/λ].
- The energy eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian are given by E_n(λ) = [1/(λ(1-λ))] [n² + 2n], where n is the number of raising operators applied.
- The parameters in the general form are a₁=1, a₂=0, A=5/4, and B=1/4 for the s-d boson model.
- The first excited eigenstate is proportional to (A+B)/2 times [(d†·d†)/A - s†s†/B] acting on the vacuum.
- The second excited eigenstate involves the parameter γ = √[(2A+1)(2B+1)/(2A+2B+1)].

## Context

This work extends the Gaudin algebra approach (Gaudin, 1976) from interacting spins on a lattice to bosonic systems, providing an exactly solvable model for s-d boson Hamiltonians near but not necessarily at the SO(6) limit of the Interacting Boson Model (Arima and Iachello, 1975). It differs from prior efforts by Pan and collaborators (2000) on the U(5) to SU(3) transition and by Dukelsky and collaborators (2001) on bosons with repulsive pairing, which required numerical solution of nonlinear equations for eigenvalues. The model enables analytic calculation of energy levels and eigenstates for collective nuclear states described by the Interacting Boson Model, particularly for systems deviating from the exact SO(6) dynamical symmetry.
