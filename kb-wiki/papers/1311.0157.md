# Spectra and elliptic flow for identified hadrons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Using the VISHNU hybrid model that couples (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics to a microscopic hadronic transport model, we calculate the multiplicity, p_T spectra and elliptic flow for pions, kaons, and protons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions, using MC-KLN initializations with smoothed initial conditions, obtained by averaging over a large number of events. The results from our calculations are compared to data from the ALICE collaboration, showing nice agreement over several centrality bins. Using the same inputs, we predict the p_T spectra and elliptic flow for phi mesons and explore its flow development in the strong and weak coupling limits through hydrodynamic calculations with different decoupling temperatures. In addition we study the influence of baryon and anti-baryon annihilation processes on common observables and demonstrate that by including annihilation processes below a switching temperature of 165 MeV, VISHNU provides a good description of the multiplicity and p_T spectra for pions, kaons and protons measured by PHENIX and ALICE at both RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The VISHNU hybrid model, using MC-KLN initial conditions with smoothed initial conditions and a QGP specific shear viscosity of 0.16, provides a good description of identified hadron multiplicities, p_T spectra, and differential elliptic flow for pions, kaons, and protons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions, but the extracted viscosity value is slightly lower than earlier work because the scalar product method flow measurements used here over-estimate the mean flow signal compared to the four-particle cumulant method used previously.

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU hybrid model, which couples (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP fluid expansion to the microscopic hadronic transport model UrQMD for hadron rescattering, with a switching temperature of 165 MeV. Initial entropy density profiles were generated using the MC-KLN model by averaging over a large number of fluctuating entropy density distributions, and a modified Cooper-Frye formula with viscous corrections was used to convert hydrodynamic output into hadrons via the H2O Monte-Carlo event generator. The QGP specific shear viscosity was set to a constant value of 0.16 with relaxation time tau_pi = 3 eta/(s T), and the initial time was set to tau_0 = 0.9 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- QGP specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP = 0.16, reduced from 0.20 used in earlier work, to describe new v_2 data for pions, kaons, and protons measured by the scalar product method
- Switching temperature T_sw = 165 MeV between hydrodynamic and microscopic evolution
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.9 fm/c, obtained from fitting the slope of p_T spectra for all charged hadrons below 2 GeV
- Baryon-antibaryon annihilation processes reduce proton multiplicity by approximately 30%
- Kaon multiplicities are overpredicted by about 10% in central and about 25% in peripheral collisions
- Baryon-antibaryon annihilation increases pion multiplicity by about 4% and kaon multiplicity by about 2%
- Reducing (eta/s)_QGP from 0.20 to 0.16 increased calculated v_2(p_T) for identified hadrons by approximately 5%

## Context

This work extends previous VISHNU hybrid model studies that extracted QGP viscosity from integrated elliptic flow for all charged hadrons in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions (Song 2010, Song 2011) and extrapolated to 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions (Song 2011). It improves upon pure hydrodynamic calculations by Shen (2011) that used VISH2+1 with decoupling temperature 120 MeV and (eta/s)=0.20, which overpredicted proton yields and had issues with proton p_T spectra shapes in central collisions. The results enable predictions for phi meson spectra and elliptic flow at LHC energies, and demonstrate that baryon-antibaryon annihilation processes are essential for reproducing proton yields, showing that final chemical composition cannot be accurately described by a single chemical freeze-out temperature as assumed in Statistical Model approaches (Andronic 2012).
