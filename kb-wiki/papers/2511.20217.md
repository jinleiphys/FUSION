# First determination of fragmentation functions in an exotic-hadron candidate

> In recent years, there are experimental reports on exotic-hadron candidates, which have different quark configurations from ordinary $q\bar q$ and $qqq$ constituents. However, it is not easy to confirm their exotic nature from global observables such as masses, spins, parities, and decay widths. At high energies, internal quark and gluon configurations could become more apparent because hadrons should be described by fundamental degrees of freedom of quarks and gluons in quantum chromodynamics. One of such possibilities is to use the fragmentation functions (FFs). In this work, accurate FFs of an exotic hadron candidate $f_0$(980) are determined for the first time by an global analysis of experimental data on $e^+ + e^- \to f_0 (980)+X$ with recent precise measurements of the Belle collaboration. From the global analysis, we found that their second moments have a relation $M_u = M_d \ll M_s \sim M_g $ for up-quark, down-quark, strange-quark, and gluon FFs. Furthermore, the function $D_s^{f_0}(z)$ is distributed in the larger-$z$ region in comparison with the functions $D_{u}^{f_0}(z)$, $D_{d}^{f_0}(z)$, and $D_g^{f_0}(z)$. These facts support that the $f_0 (980)$ has the $s\bar s$ configuration at high energies. This is a new finding that $f_0 (980)$ should be considered mainly as the $s\bar s$ state, which is different from our usual understanding as a tetraquark (or $K\bar K$) hadron from low-energy studies. Our results could indicate the transition of the internal configuration picture that $f_0 (980)$ looks like a $q\bar q$ state at high energies although it is described by tetra-quark or $K\bar K$ molecule state at low energies. It sheds light on a new direction in exotic hadron physics.

## Key claim
The fragmentation functions of the exotic-hadron candidate f0(980) are determined for the first time, and their second moments and z-dependent forms indicate that f0(980) behaves as an s sbar state at high energies, even though low-energy studies suggest it is a tetraquark or K Kbar molecule. The author proposes that the internal configuration of f0(980) may transition from exotic at low energies to ordinary q qbar at high energies.

## Method
A global chi-squared analysis was performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in alpha_s on experimental data for e+ e- -> f0(980) + X from the HRS (SLAC), DELPHI (CERN), OPAL (CERN), and Belle (KEK) collaborations, totaling 63 data points. The fragmentation functions for u, d, s, c, b quarks and gluons were parameterized at an initial scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 as D_i(z) = N_i z^{alpha_i} (1-z)^{beta_i}, with Q^2 evolution calculated via the Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi equation. Uncertainties were estimated using the Hessian method.

## Key numbers
- Second moments at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: M_u = M_d = 0.001913 +/- 0.000044, M_s = 0.01205 +/- 0.00043, M_g = 0.01274 +/- 0.00048, establishing the relation M_u = M_d << M_s ~ M_g.
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the global fit: chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.37 with 13 free parameters.
- Parameters for the strange-quark fragmentation function: alpha_s = 3.546 +/- 0.032, beta_s = 1.935 +/- 0.031.
- Parameters for the gluon fragmentation function: alpha_g = -0.4477 +/- 0.0049, beta_g = 12.66 +/- 0.23.
- Parameters for the up-quark fragmentation function: alpha_u = 5.967 +/- 0.047, beta_u = 6.913 +/- 0.049.
- Second moments for charm and bottom: M_c = 0.003262 +/- 0.000041, M_b = 0.008502 +/- 0.000041.

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Kumano and collaborators (2008) to use fragmentation functions as probes of exotic hadron internal structure, which was previously impractical due to large experimental uncertainties. The analysis is enabled by precise 2025 Belle collaboration measurements from the KEK-B factory. The results contradict the low-energy tetraquark or K Kbar molecule picture of f0(980) established by the Particle Data Group (2024) and earlier studies, but the author suggests a parallel with the constituent counting rule analysis of Lambda(1405) photoproduction by Kumano and collaborators (2013), which indicated a similar transition from pentaquark at low energies to ordinary qqq at high energies. This work opens the possibility that exotic hadrons may appear as ordinary q qbar or qqq states at high energies.
