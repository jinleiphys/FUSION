# Longitudinal Viscous Hydrodynamic Evolution for the Shattered Colour Glass Condensate

> We investigate hydrodynamic evolution of the quark gluon plasma for the colour glass condensate type initial conditions. We solve full second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations in the longitudinal direction to find that non-boost invariant expansion leads to visible deformation on the initial rapidity distribution. The results indicate that hydrodynamic evolution with viscosity plays an important role in determining parameters for the initial distributions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that non-boost-invariant longitudinal hydrodynamic evolution, including shear and bulk viscosity, visibly deforms the initial rapidity distribution from the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) model, meaning that the observed hadron rapidity distribution reflects the initial gluon distribution only indirectly. The authors hedge that their results are qualitative because they use trial transport coefficients (including the conjectured minimum bound for shear viscosity) and neglect transverse expansion.

## Method

The authors solve full second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations (generalized from Israel-Stewart theory) in (1+1)-dimensional proper-time and space-time rapidity coordinates, without imposing boost invariance. Initial conditions are obtained from the Monte Carlo KLN (Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi) version of the kT-factorization formula for gluon production, using a (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state. The constitutive equations for bulk pressure and shear pressure are solved numerically using the piecewise parabolic method, with iteration on the expansion scalar to handle the mixing of derivatives.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature at mid-rapidity: 419 MeV for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC) and 490 MeV for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (LHC), at thermalisation time tau_0 = 1 fm/c.
* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 1/(4 pi), the conjectured minimum bound from AdS/CFT correspondence.
* Bulk viscosity coefficients from phi^4-theory: zeta_PiPi/eta = 5T/6 and zeta_Pi delta e/eta = -5T/2, giving conventional bulk viscosity zeta = (5/2)(1/3 - c_s^2) eta.
* Saturation scale parameters: Q^2_{s,0} = 2 GeV^2, lambda = 0.28, x_0 = 0.01, T_{A,0} = 1.53 fm^{-2}.
* Reduction of gluon multiplicity at mid-rapidity due to saturation scale cutoff: 21.5% at RHIC and 39.9% at LHC.
* Final evolution times: tau = 30 fm/c for Au+Au at RHIC and tau = 50 fm/c for Pb+Pb at LHC, when mid-rapidity temperature approaches T_c ~ 0.17 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic calculations (Hirano, 2002) and the CGC-based initial condition framework (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi, 2001-2004) by including full second-order viscous hydrodynamics in the longitudinal direction without assuming boost invariance (Bjorken, 1983). The results enable correction of the lambda parameter in CGC-inspired models, which controls the rapidity dependence of entropy production, and may help explain why CGC models based on RHIC data underpredicted multiplicity at LHC (Aamodt, 2010).
