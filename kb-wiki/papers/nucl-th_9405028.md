# Chaos and Order in the Shell Model Eigenvectors

> The energies and wave functions of stationary many-body states are analyzed to look for the signatures of quantum chaos. Shell model calculations with the Wildenthal interaction are performed in the $J-T$ scheme for 12 particles in the $sd$-shell. The local level statistics are in perfect agreement with the GOE predictions. The analysis of the amplitudes of the eigenvectors in the shell model basis with the aid of the informational entropy and moments of the distribution function shows evidence for local chaos with a localization length reaching 90\% of the total dimension in the middle of the spectrum. The degree of chaoticity is sensitive to the the strength of the residual interaction as compared to the single particle energy spacing.

## Key claim

The paper shows that standard level statistics (nearest-neighbor spacing and spectral rigidity) agree perfectly with the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) predictions for 12 particles in the sd-shell, but that wavefunction-based measures like informational entropy and number of principal components reveal incomplete mixing: the localization length reaches only 90% of the total dimension in the middle of the spectrum, not the full GOE limit.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in the J-T scheme for 12 valence particles in the sd-shell using the Wildenthal interaction. The Hamiltonian included one-body single-particle energies from the 16O core plus a two-body residual interaction. The matrix for JπT = 2+0 states had dimension 3273, and eigenvectors were analyzed using informational entropy and the number of principal components (NPC) to quantify wavefunction delocalization.

## Key numbers

- Matrix dimension for JπT = 2+0 states: 3273
- Levels used for level statistics analysis: 200 through 3000
- Maximum informational entropy in the middle of the spectrum shows a 12% deviation from the GOE prediction of 0.48N
- Localization length reaches 90% of the total dimension in the middle of the spectrum
- For a Gaussian distribution of amplitudes, the ratio exp(Sα)/(NPC)α equals the universal random matrix result of 1.44 (solid line in Fig. 3)
- Dimension of the JπT = 0+0 subspace: 839

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quantum chaos in many-body systems that focused primarily on level statistics (Haake 1991; Brody et al. 1981), and specifically goes beyond the largest previous spectral rigidity analysis which considered L up to 80 (Arve 1992). The paper introduces wavefunction-based measures (informational entropy and NPC) as more sensitive probes of chaos than level statistics alone, and shows that the mean field from single-particle energies suppresses chaotic mixing compared to the degenerate case where all single-particle energies are set to zero. This enables future studies of "local chaos" where wavefunctions exhibit Gaussian amplitude distributions but with a localization length smaller than the full Hilbert space dimension.
