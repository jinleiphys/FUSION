# QCD dynamics in mesons at soft and hard scales

> Using a ladder-rainbow kernel previously established for the soft scale of light quark hadrons, we explore, within a Dyson-Schwinger approach, phenomena that mix soft and hard scales of QCD. The difference between vector and axial vector current correlators is examined to estimate the four quark chiral condensate and the leading distance scale for the onset of non-perturbative phenomena in QCD. The valence quark distributions, in the pion and kaon, defined in deep inelastic scattering, and measured in the Drell Yan process, are investigated with the same ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Key claim
Using a ladder-rainbow kernel previously fitted to light-quark hadron properties, the authors show that the same truncation can simultaneously describe both soft-scale phenomena (the four-quark condensate and Weinberg sum rules) and hard-scale observables (valence quark distributions in the pion and kaon), but the infrared kernel component remains phenomenological because QCD is unsolved in that non-perturbative domain.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the dressed quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for meson bound states in Euclidean metric, using a ladder-rainbow truncation with an effective running coupling that combines an infrared enhancement term (fitted to the chiral condensate and pion/kaon masses) with a one-loop perturbative QCD ultraviolet term. For the four-quark condensate, they compute the transverse difference of vector and axial-vector current correlators and extract the condensate via the leading operator product expansion term. For valence quark distributions, they evaluate a Minkowski-space expression for the parton distribution function by analytically continuing Euclidean DSE/BSE solutions and evolving the results to experimental scales via leading-order DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers
- Four-quark condensate extracted at renormalization scale μ = 19 GeV is 65% greater than the vacuum saturation value ⟨q̄q⟩².
- Pion mass difference mπ± − mπ0 calculated from the Das-Guralnik-Mathur-Low-Young sum rule: 4.86 MeV (experiment: 4.43 ± 0.03 MeV).
- Pion valence quark momentum sum rule at model scale Q₀: ∫₀¹ dx x(uπ + d̄π) = 0.74; corresponding kaon sum = 0.76.
- Model scale Q₀ = 0.57 GeV determined by matching xⁿ moments for n = 1,2,3 to experimental values at (2 GeV)².
- Pion decay constant extracted from the low-P² limit of the vector-axial-vector difference correlator: fπ = 0.09 GeV (in the fπ = 0.0924 GeV convention).

## Context
This work extends the ladder-rainbow DSE/BSE approach previously applied to light meson spectra by Maris and Tandy (1999) and to the pion electromagnetic form factor by Maris and Tandy (2000). It goes beyond the exploratory DIS study of Hecht et al. (2000), which used phenomenological parameterizations, by employing fully numerical DSE solutions for propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The results enable future replacement of the phenomenological infrared kernel with specific non-perturbative mechanisms, and provide a covariant framework for computing parton distributions that avoids the regularization sensitivity of Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model approaches (Shigetani, 1993; Bentz, 1999) and the difficulties of constituent quark models and instanton-liquid models in joining perturbative and non-perturbative QCD elements.
