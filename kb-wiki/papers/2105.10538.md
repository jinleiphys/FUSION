# Delineating chiral separation effect in two-color dense QCD

> We study the chiral separation effect (CSE) in two-color and two-flavor QCD (QC$_2$D) to delineate quasiparticle pictures in dense matter from low to high temperatures. Both massless and massive quarks are discussed. We particularly focus on the high density domain where diquarks form a color singlet condensate with the electric charge $1/3$. The condensate breaks baryon number and $U(1)_A$ axial symmetry, and induces the electromagnetic Meissner effects. Within a quark quasiparticle picture, we compute the chiral separation conductivity at one-loop. We have checked that Nambu-Goldstone modes, which should appear in the improved vertices as required by the Ward-Takahashi identities, do not contribute to the chiral separation conductivity due to their longitudinal natures. In the static limit, the destructive interferences in the particle-hole channel, as in usual Meissner effects, suppress the conductivity (in chiral limit, to $1/3$ of the normal phase's). This locally breaks the universality of the CSE coefficients, provided quasiparticle pictures are valid in the bulk matter.

## Key claim

The chiral separation (CS) conductivity in two-color dense QCD with a diquark condensate is suppressed to 1/3 of the normal phase value in the static limit (for massless quarks), locally breaking the universality of the CSE coefficients if quasiparticle pictures are valid in the bulk matter; Nambu-Goldstone modes do not contribute to the conductivity due to their longitudinal nature.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral separation conductivity at one-loop order within a quark quasiparticle picture using linear response theory, employing the Nambu-Gorkov formalism to incorporate diquark condensates. They decompose the conductivity into particle-hole, particle-antiparticle, and antiparticle-antihole contributions, and analyze the static and dynamic limits for various temperatures, chemical potentials, and quark masses. The Matsubara summation is performed analytically, and the coherence factors and propagator factors are evaluated to determine the suppression mechanisms.

## Key numbers

* In the chiral limit at zero temperature, the static CS conductivity in the diquark condensed phase is 1/3 of the normal phase value (the normal phase static conductivity is normalized to 1).
* In the normal phase (massless limit, zero temperature), the static CS conductivity receives 2/3 from particle-hole contributions and 1/3 from particle-antiparticle contributions, summing to 1 (normalized to σ₀ = N_c μ / (2π²)).
* In the normal phase (massless limit, zero temperature), the dynamic CS conductivity (ω→0 at q=0) receives 0 from particle-hole and 1/3 from particle-antiparticle contributions, summing to 1/3.
* For |q| ≪ Δ, the particle-hole contributions vanish at zero temperature as e^{-Δ/T}/T → 0 due to the diquark gap.

## Context

This work extends studies of the chiral separation effect in two-color QCD, which had previously been examined for hadronic and quark-gluon-plasma phases by Buividovich (2020), to the cold dense diquark condensed phase. The calculations follow a similar methodology to that used for chiral magnetic conductivity by Kharzeev (2009), and build on the authors' prior model calculations for dense two-color QCD (Kojo 2021, Suenaga 2019, Kojo 2014). The results suggest that to recover the anomaly-constrained CSE coefficient, one must go beyond quasiparticle descriptions or linear response, for instance by treating vortices with fermion zero modes or zero modes on boundaries of the diquark condensed phase.
