# The N* Program at Jefferson Lab - Status and Prospects

> I discuss recent results on the electroproduction and photoproduction of mesons in the region of non-strange baryon resonances. Results on the quadrupole transition from the ground state nucleon to the Delta(1232) show the importance of pion cloud contributions to explain the observed quadrupole transition multipoles. The excitation of the "Roper" resonance is being studied in single pion production using unpolarized cross sections measurements and beam spin asymmetries. New results on the S_{11}(1535) transition formfactor give now a consistent picture over a large Q2 range. First results on electroproduction of p pi^+ pi^- show intruiging resonance structure. which is difficult to explain in an isobar model fit using known resonance properties, and may hint at excitation of a "missing" resonance. Searches for resonances in K^+Lambda also reveal resonance-like behavior. I briefly address a new avenue of N* physics using exclusive deeply virtual Compton scattering, recently measured for the first time at JLAB and at DESY.

## Key claim

The paper reports that new Jefferson Lab data on the electroexcitation of nucleon resonances provide a consistent picture of transition form factors for the Delta(1232) and the S11(1535), but the interpretation of the Roper resonance remains model-dependent and a strong resonance signal near 1.72 GeV in the pπ+π- channel may require the introduction of a "missing" baryon state not previously identified in pion scattering.

## Method

The author reviews results from the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measuring exclusive electroproduction and photoproduction of mesons (π0, π+, η, K+Λ, and π+π-) in the region of non-strange baryon resonances. Angular distributions of response functions were separated using the full azimuthal and polar coverage of CLAS, and multipole analyses were performed using unitary isobar model fits to extract transition form factors and helicity amplitudes for states including the Delta(1232), Roper N'(1440), and S11(1535). The paper also reports on first measurements of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) as a new tool for resonance studies.

## Key numbers

- REM = -0.0275 ± 0.005 at the photon point (Q2 = 0), from combined MAMI and LEGS data
- RSM follows approximately a straight line for Q2 > 0.35 GeV2: RSM = -(0.04 + 0.028 × Q2 (GeV2))
- CLAS data for REM and RSM cover the Q2 range 0.4 to 1.8 GeV2
- For the candidate N*3/2+(1720) state in pπ+π-: ΓNρ/Γtot(CLAS) ≈ 0.17, compared to ΓNρ/Γtot(PDG) ≈ 0.85 for the known state
- ΓΔπ/Γtot = 0.62 ± 0.12 for the candidate state
- Lattice QCD calculation gives REM = 0.03 ± 0.08 for the γNΔ(1232) transition (quenched QCD, approximately one decade before this paper)
- DVCS data were measured at Q2 = 1 GeV2 with the final state photon in the backward direction

## Context

This work extends the experimental program of resonance electroproduction previously conducted at MAMI, LEGS, GRAAL, and ELSA, and directly addresses discrepancies in older data for the Delta(1232) quadrupole transitions and the S11(1535) form factor. The results enable future tests of dynamical models that include pion cloud contributions (Sato and Yang, 1990s) versus relativized quark models (Warns and Aznauryan, 1990s) and hybrid baryon models (Li and Burkert, 1990s). The new CLAS data on the pπ+π- channel and K+Λ production open the possibility of discovering "missing" resonances predicted by Capstick and Roberts (1990s) that decouple from the Nπ channel, and the first DVCS measurements at JLab and DESY (Hermes collaboration) establish a new framework for studying resonance transitions at the parton level using generalized parton distributions.
