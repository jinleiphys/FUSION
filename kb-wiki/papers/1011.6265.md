# Anomalous Soft Photons associated with Hadron Production in String Fragmentation

> The bosonized QCD2+QED2 system for quarks with two flavors contains QCD2 and QED2 bound states, with an isoscalar photon at about 25 MeV and an isovector (I=1,I_3=0) photon at about 44 MeV. Consequently, when a quark and an antiquark at the two ends of a string pulls apart from each other at high energies, hadrons and soft photons will be produced simultaneously in the fragmentation of the string. The production of the QED2 soft photons in association with hadrons may explain the anomalous soft photon data in hadron-hadron collisions and e+e- annihilations at high energies.

## Key claim

The bosonized QCD2+QED2 system for quarks with two flavors contains stable bound states that can explain anomalous soft photon production, but only under the condition that the flux tube radius is approximately 0.35 fm and the quark transverse mass is 0.4 GeV, with the isoscalar photon mass at about 25 MeV and the isovector photon mass at about 44 MeV.

## Method

The author compactifies QCD4+QED4 into QCD2+QED2 by exploiting the dominance of longitudinal motion and transverse confinement in high-energy string fragmentation, using a flux tube radius derived from the root-mean-squared transverse momentum of produced pions. Non-Abelian bosonization for U(3) interactions and Abelian bosonization for flavor degrees of freedom are applied to find stable boson states, with the mass squared of each state given by a Klein-Gordon equation characterized by isospin-dependent masses. The coupling constants in 2D are related to 4D constants via the flux tube radius, and the mass scale for QED2 is taken as the quark current mass (1 MeV) while for QCD2 it is the transverse mass (400 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Flux tube radius: \(R_T \sim 0.35\) fm, derived from \(\sqrt{\langle p_T^2\rangle_\pi } = 0.56\) GeV in \(Z^0\) hadronic decay
- QCD2 coupling constant: \(g_{\rm QCD2} = 632.5\) MeV, giving string tension \(b = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\)
- QED2 coupling constant: \(e_{\rm QED2} = 96\) MeV
- Isoscalar photon mass: 12.8 MeV (massless quarks) or 25.3 MeV (with \(m_T = 400\) MeV, \(\mu = 1\) MeV)
- Isovector photon mass: 38.4 MeV (massless quarks) or 44.1 MeV (with \(m_T = 400\) MeV, \(\mu = 1\) MeV)
- QCD2 isoscalar hadron mass: 504.6 MeV (massless quarks) or 734.6 MeV (with \(\mu = m_T = 400\) MeV)
- QCD2 isovector hadron mass: 0 MeV (massless quarks) or 533.8 MeV (with \(\mu = m_T = 400\) MeV)
- Quark transverse mass: \(m_T = 0.4\) GeV, estimated from \(\sqrt{\langle p_T^2\rangle_\pi /2}\)

## Context

This work extends the Casher-Kogut-Susskind (1974) picture of hadron production in string fragmentation by adding simultaneous QED2 photon production, and it directly addresses the anomalous soft photon data from the DELPHI Collaboration (2006, 2008, 2010) and from hadron-hadron collision experiments such as Bel02a and Won10. The model explains why anomalous soft photons appear only with hadron production, why the photon yield is proportional to hadron yield, and why soft photons are more associated with neutral hadrons than charged hadrons. The origin of the soft photon group at \(p_T \sim 5\) MeV remains unknown and represents an open question for further investigation.
