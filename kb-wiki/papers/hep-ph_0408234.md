# Possible Interpretations of $D_{sJ}^+(2632)$ If It Really Exists

> We analyze various possible interpretations of the narrow state $D_{sJ}^+(2632)$ observed by SELEX Collaboration recently, which lies above threshold and has abnormal decay pattern. These interpretations include: (1) several versions of tetraquarks; (2) conventional $c\bar s$ meson such as the first radial excitation of $D_s(2112)$ with abnormally large SU(3) symmetry breaking; (3) conventional $c\bar s$ meson with abnormally large $η_1$ coupling; (4) heavy hybrid meson. We discuss the physical implications of each interpretation. For example, if the existence of $D_{sJ}^+(2632)$ is confirmed as the first radial excitation of $D_s(2112)$ by other experiments, it will be helpful to look for (1) its SU(3) flavor partners $D_{J}^{0,+}(2530)$; (2) its B-meson analogues $B_{J}^{0,+}(5840), B_{sJ}^+(5940)$; (3) S-wave two pion decay modes.

## Key claim

If the narrow state \(D_{sJ}^+(2632)\) observed by SELEX really exists, its abnormally small relative branching ratio \(\Gamma(D^0K^+)/\Gamma(D_s\eta)=0.16\pm0.06\) cannot be explained by a conventional \(c\bar s\) assignment under SU(3) flavor symmetry, which would predict a ratio of at least 2.3; therefore either the SU(3) symmetry assumption or the \(c\bar s\) assignment must be wrong, and the paper explores four alternative interpretations.

## Method

The authors first derive the expected decay ratio for a \(c\bar s\) triplet state using an SU(3)-symmetric effective Lagrangian and phase-space kinematics, showing a factor-of-15 discrepancy with experiment. They then analyze four interpretations: (1) tetraquark states in several configurations (diquark clusters, \(c\bar ss\bar s\) dominance, and isospin-mixed flavor eigenstates); (2) a conventional \(c\bar s\) first radial excitation with anomalously large SU(3) symmetry breaking, parameterized by an effective Lagrangian with quark-mass-dependent terms; (3) a conventional \(c\bar s\) state with an anomalously large coupling to the SU(3) singlet \(\eta_1\), using \(\eta\)-\(\eta'\) mixing; and (4) a heavy hybrid meson containing explicit glue, evaluated via large-\(N_c\) counting of decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The experimental relative branching ratio: \(\Gamma(D^0K^+)/\Gamma(D_s\eta)=0.16\pm0.06\) (SELEX, 90% C.L. upper limit on total width \(\Gamma<17\) MeV).
- Under SU(3) symmetry for a \(c\bar s\) assignment, the predicted ratio is \(\Gamma(D^0K^+)/\Gamma(D_s\eta)\ge 2.3\), using decay momenta \(k_{D^0K^+}=499\) MeV and \(k_{D_s\eta}=325\) MeV.
- For the large SU(3) breaking interpretation, the required parameter values are \(\gamma(m_s-\hat m)/(g_8+\alpha m_s)=4.8\) or \(1.1\).
- For the large \(\eta_1\) coupling interpretation, the required coupling ratio is \(g_1/g_8=16.2\) or \(-11.7\).
- For the tetraquark interpretation as the \(J^P=0^+\) isoscalar member of the **15** multiplet, the predicted ratio is \(\Gamma(D_{sJ}^+\to D^0K^+)/\Gamma(D_{sJ}^+\to D_s^+\eta)=0.25\).
- For the hybrid interpretation (\(c\bar s g_s^2 G\tilde G\) state), the large-\(N_c\) estimate gives \(\Gamma(D^0K^+)/\Gamma(D_s\eta)\sim 1/9\times 1.5\approx 0.16\) using \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This paper responds to the SELEX Collaboration's 2004 observation of \(D_{sJ}^+(2632)\), following the earlier discoveries of narrow charm-strange mesons \(D_{sJ}(2317)\) and \(D_{sJ}(2457)\) by BaBar, CLEO, and Belle. The work extends prior theoretical interpretations of those lower states (Bardeen, Dai, and others) and systematically evaluates four competing explanations, tetraquark models (Maiani, Chen, Chao, Liu), radial excitations (Chao, Barnes), and hybrid mesons, that had been proposed in rapid succession. The analysis enables future experimental searches for SU(3) flavor partners \(D_J^{0,+}(2530)\) and B-meson analogues \(B_J^{0,+}(5840), B_{sJ}^+(5940)\), and it highlights the need for dynamical mass calculations of heavy hybrid mesons.
