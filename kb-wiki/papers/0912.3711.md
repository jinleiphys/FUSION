# Bethe-Salpeter equations: mesons beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation

> We investigate masses of light mesons from a coupled system of Dyson--Schwinger (DSE) and Bethe--Salpeter equations (BSE), taking into account dominant non-Abelian, sub-leading Abelian, and dominant pion cloud contributions to the dressed quark-gluon vertex. The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel is constructed and the spectrum of light mesons calculated. Our model goes significantly beyond the rainbow-ladder. We find that sub-leading Abelian corrections are further dynamically suppressed, and that our results supersede early qualitative predictions from simple truncation schemes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that sub-leading Abelian corrections to the quark-gluon vertex are dynamically suppressed when combined with dominant non-Abelian corrections in a coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach, and that the combined effect of non-Abelian corrections and pion back-reaction nearly cancels for the vector meson channel.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark propagator and quark-gluon vertex, truncating the vertex to include the dominant non-Abelian one-loop diagram, the sub-leading Abelian one-loop diagram, and a pion back-reaction diagram. They then construct the corresponding axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel via functional differentiation of the quark self-energy, yielding a two-loop calculation for meson bound-state masses. The spectrum of light mesons is computed by solving the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the ground-state masses of the pion, sigma, rho, a1, and b1 mesons.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: 138 MeV for rainbow-ladder (RL), 142 MeV for non-Abelian (NA), 137 MeV for Abelian (AB), 142 MeV for AB+NA, 138 MeV for NA+PI (pion back-reaction); experimental value 138 MeV.
* Rho meson mass: 758 MeV for RL, 881 MeV for NA, 734 MeV for AB, 878 MeV for AB+NA, 805 MeV for NA+PI; experimental value 776 MeV.
* a1 meson mass: 926 MeV for RL, 1056 MeV for NA, 889 MeV for AB, 1052 MeV for AB+NA, 1040 MeV for NA+PI; experimental value 1230 MeV.
* b1 meson mass: 912 MeV for RL, 973 MeV for NA, 915 MeV for AB, 972 MeV for AB+NA, 941 MeV for NA+PI; experimental value 1230 MeV.
* Sigma meson mass: 645 MeV for RL, 884 MeV for NA, 602 MeV for AB, 883 MeV for AB+NA, 820 MeV for NA+PI; experimental range 400-1200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous beyond-rainbow-ladder investigations that separately considered pion unquenching corrections (Fischer 2008, Fischer 2009) and dominant non-Abelian corrections to the quark-gluon vertex (Fischer 2009) by including sub-leading Abelian corrections. The results supersede earlier qualitative predictions from simple truncation schemes such as those of Bender (1996) and Watson (2004), and demonstrate that naive addition of results from independent studies without combined dynamical calculation can be misleading. The study enables future investigations incorporating realistic input from the ghost and gluon sector of QCD.
