# Spin Boltzmann equation for non-relativistic spin-1/2 fermions

> We derive the spin Boltzmann equations for spin-1/2 fermions in a non-relativistic model with four-fermion contact interaction which conserves spin degrees of freedom. A great advantage of the model is that the spin matrix elements in collision terms can be completely worked out and be put into such a compact form that one can clearly see how spins are coupled in particle scatterings. A semi-classical expansion in the Planck constant has been made and the on-shell part of the spin Boltzmann equation up to the next-to-leading order is derived. At the leading order the equilibrium spin distribution can be obtained from the vanishing of the collision term for the spin density. The spin chemical potential emerges as a natural consequence of spin conservation. The off-shell part of the spin Boltzmann equation is also discussed. The work can be extended to more sophisticated interaction such as nuclear force in order to apply to spin polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions at low energies.

## Key claim

The authors derive spin Boltzmann equations for non-relativistic spin-1/2 fermions using a four-fermion contact interaction (NJL-type) that conserves spin, enabling a complete analytic evaluation of spin matrix elements in collision terms, a simplification not possible in the relativistic case. The derivation is performed via a semi-classical expansion in the Planck constant up to next-to-leading order, with the spin chemical potential emerging naturally from spin conservation at leading order.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equations derived from the Dyson-Schwinger equations on the closed-time-path (CTP) formalism for a non-relativistic Lagrangian with a four-fermion contact interaction of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type. They perform a Wigner transform and a semi-classical expansion in powers of \(\hbar\), then take traces of the evolution equation with and without Pauli matrices to obtain separate Boltzmann equations for the spin-averaged distribution \(\overline{f}(x,\mathbf{p})\) and the spin polarization vector \(\mathbf{S}(x,\mathbf{p})\). The collision terms are evaluated using the quasi-particle approximation for retarded and advanced self-energies and Green functions.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order Wigner functions are proportional to \((2\pi\hbar)\delta(p_0 - \mathbf{p}^2/(2m))\), with the matrix-valued spin-dependent distribution \(f_{s_1 s_2}^{(0)}(x,\mathbf{p})\) defined at \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^0)\).
- The first-order on-shell contributions at \(\mathcal{O}(\hbar^1)\) are assumed to have the same delta-function form: \(\hbar G_{\alpha\beta}^{\lessgtr(1)}(x,p) \propto -\hbar(2\pi\hbar)\delta(p_0 - \mathbf{p}^2/(2m))\).
- The spin polarization vector is extracted as \(\mathbf{S}(x,\mathbf{p}) = \frac{1}{2}\mathrm{Tr}[\boldsymbol{\sigma} g^{<}(x,p)]\), where \(g^{<}(x,p) = \overline{f}(x,\mathbf{p}) + \boldsymbol{\sigma}\cdot\mathbf{S}(x,\mathbf{p})\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic spin Boltzmann approach of Sheng (2021) to the non-relativistic regime, where spin and momentum decouple, making the spin structure of collision terms fully tractable. It provides a theoretical foundation for microscopic transport models of spin polarization in low-energy heavy-ion collisions (e.g., at HADES and STAR Beam Energy Scan energies around 2.4–3 GeV), where non-relativistic approximations are valid. The framework is designed to be extended to realistic nuclear forces (beyond the contact interaction) for application to global polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions at low energies.
