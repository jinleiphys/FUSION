# Equation of State for Nucleonic and Hyperonic Neutron Stars with Mass and Radius Constraints

> We obtain a new equation of state for the nucleonic and hyperonic inner core of neutron stars that fulfills the 2$M_{\odot}$ observations as well as the recent determinations of stellar radii below 13 km. The nucleonic equation of state is obtained from a new parametrization of the FSU2 relativistic mean-field functional that satisfies these latest astrophysical constraints and, at the same time, reproduces the properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei while fulfilling the restrictions on high-density matter deduced from heavy-ion collisions. On the one hand, the equation of state of neutron star matter is softened around saturation density, which increases the compactness of canonical neutron stars leading to stellar radii below 13 km. On the other hand, the equation of state is stiff enough at higher densities to fulfill the 2$M_{\odot}$ limit. By a slight modification of the parametrization, we also find that the constraints of 2$M_{\odot}$ neutron stars with radii around 13 km are satisfied when hyperons are considered. The inclusion of the high magnetic fields present in magnetars further stiffens the equation of state. Hyperonic magnetars with magnetic fields in the surface of $ \sim 10^{15}$ G and with values of $\sim 10^{18}$ G in the interior can reach maximum masses of 2$M_{\odot}$ with radii in the 12-13 km range.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new equation of state (EoS) for nucleonic and hyperonic neutron star cores that simultaneously satisfies the 2 solar mass maximum mass constraint and recent radius determinations below 13 km, but only by softening the EoS around saturation density while keeping it stiff at higher densities, and for hyperonic stars requires a slight modification of the parametrization.

## Method
The authors start from the FSU2 relativistic mean-field (RMF) functional and create a new parametrization (FSU2R) by adjusting the mixed quartic isovector-vector coupling (Λ_ω) and the quartic isoscalar-vector self-coupling (ζ) to soften the pressure near saturation density while maintaining stiffness at high densities. They then introduce hyperons with couplings fitted to hyperon-nucleon optical potentials from hypernuclei data, creating a slightly modified parametrization (FSU2H) that also satisfies the 2 solar mass constraint. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the effects of strong magnetic fields up to 10^18 G in the interior are included for magnetar calculations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses for PSR J0348+0432 and 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses for PSR J1614-2230 are used as constraints
- Saturation density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3
- For the FSU2R model: symmetry energy E_sym(n_0) = 30.2 MeV, slope L = 44.3 MeV, PNM pressure P(n_0) = 2.27 MeV fm^-3
- For the FSU2H model: symmetry energy E_sym(n_0) = 30.2 MeV, slope L = 41.0 MeV, PNM pressure P(n_0) = 2.06 MeV fm^-3
- Hyperonic magnetars with surface magnetic field ~10^15 G and interior fields ~10^18 G reach maximum masses of 2 solar masses with radii in the 12-13 km range
- The critical magnetic field B_c^e = 4.414 × 10^13 G
- NL3 model predicts maximum mass ≈ 2.8 solar masses and radii ≈ 13 km for maximum mass, ≈ 15 km for a 1.5 solar mass star

## Context
This work extends the FSU2 RMF parametrization of Chen (2014) by adjusting the Λ_ω and ζ couplings to simultaneously satisfy the 2 solar mass observations from Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013) while accommodating the small stellar radii (below 13 km) suggested by analyses from Guillot (2013), Lattimer (2015), and others. The paper addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon appearance typically softens the EoS too much to reach 2 solar masses, and shows that with the FSU2H parametrization hyperonic stars can still reach this mass. The inclusion of strong magnetic fields builds on work by Broderick (2000) and others on magnetar EoS, showing that fields up to 10^18 G can further stiffen the EoS to support 2 solar mass hyperonic magnetars. This enables future comparisons with precision measurements from NICER and gravitational-wave observations of neutron star mergers.
