# The isoscalar S-wave pi-N scattering length a+ from pi-deuteron scattering

> We consider constraints on the isoscalar S-wave pi-N scattering length a+ from pi-deuteron scattering, to third order in small momenta and pion masses in chiral perturbation theory. To this order, the pi-deuteron scattering length is determined by a+ together with three-body corrections that involve no undetermined parameters. We extract a novel value for a combination of dimension two low-energy constants which is in agreement with previous determinations.

## Key claim

The paper extracts a value for a combination of dimension-two low-energy constants from pi-deuteron scattering data using chiral perturbation theory to third order, but notes that the isoscalar S-wave pi-N scattering length a+ cannot be reliably extracted from the same data until a fourth-order calculation is performed, due to large cancellations between individual terms at third order.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory to third order (O(q^3)) to relate the pi-deuteron scattering length to the isoscalar S-wave pi-N scattering length a+ plus three-body corrections. They evaluated the three-body corrections (a^{(1b)} and a^{(1c,1d)}) using four different deuteron wavefunctions (Bonn, ANL-V18, Reid-SC, SSC) and combined these with the experimental pi-deuteron scattering length from the PSI-ETHZ pionic deuterium measurement to solve for a combination of the low-energy constants c1, c2, and c3.

## Key numbers

* Pi-deuteron scattering length from PSI-ETHZ measurement: a_pi_d = -0.0264 +/- 0.0011 M_pi^{-1}
* Dominant three-body correction a^{(1b)} averaged over wavefunctions: -0.02 M_pi^{-1}
* Extracted isoscalar scattering length a+ = -(3.0 +/- 0.5) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
* Karlsruhe-Helsinki value for comparison: a+ = -(8.3 +/- 3.8) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
* PSI-ETHZ value from pionic hydrogen and deuterium: a+ = (0...5) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
* Extracted combination of low-energy constants Delta = -(0.10 +/- 0.03) GeV^{-1}
* Conservative determination from fit values: Delta = -(0.18 +/- 0.75) GeV^{-1}
* Three-body corrections for Bonn wavefunction: a^{(1b)} = -0.02021 M_pi^{-1}, a^{(1c,1d)} = -0.0005754 M_pi^{-1}
* Three-body corrections for ANL-V18 wavefunction: a^{(1b)} = -0.01960 M_pi^{-1}, a^{(1c,1d)} = -0.0007919 M_pi^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach of Weinberg (1990) for relating pi-N scattering to pi-nucleus scattering, using modern deuteron wavefunctions and updated experimental data from the PSI-ETHZ group. The extracted combination of low-energy constants agrees with previous determinations from Bernard et al. (1995) and Mojzis (1996) that used independent pion-nucleon scattering data, suggesting a consistent picture of nucleon chiral perturbation theory is emerging. The authors note that a fourth-order calculation is needed to precisely extract the isoscalar S-wave pi-N scattering length from the accurately measured pi-deuteron scattering length.
