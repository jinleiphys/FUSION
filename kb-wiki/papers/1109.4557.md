# K*Lambda(1116) photoproduction and nucleon resonances

> We study the reaction mechanism of K*Lambda(1116) photoproduction off the proton target near threshold considering the contributions from nucleon-resonances. Employing the effective Lagrangian method at the tree-level Born approximation, we investigate the role of the D13(2080) and the D15(2200). We found that the D13 plays a crucial role to reproduce the experimental data well in the forward scattering region at low energy. We also present theoretical predictions on the single photon-beam asymmetry as well as the energy and angular dependence for the cross sections of this reaction.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $D_{13}(2080)$ nucleon resonance is crucial for reproducing experimental cross-section data for $K^*\Lambda(1116)$ photoproduction near threshold, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that their model underestimates data in the backward scattering region, which they attribute to neglecting $u$-channel hyperon resonances beyond the $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma^*$.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian method at tree-level Born approximation to compute differential and total cross sections for $\gamma p \to K^* \Lambda(1116)$. They included contributions from nucleon Born terms and two nucleon resonances, the $D_{13}(2080,3/2^-)$ and $D_{15}(2200,5/2^-)$, with electromagnetic coupling constants determined from experimental helicity amplitudes and strong coupling constants estimated from quark-model partial decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections were computed at photon energy $E_\gamma = 2.35$ GeV as a function of $\cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is the scattering angle between the incident photon and outgoing $K^*$ in the center-of-mass frame.
- Total cross sections were computed for photon energies in the range $E_\gamma = (2.05 \sim 2.65)$ GeV, with experimental data from the CLAS Collaboration (Hicks 2010).
- The electromagnetic coupling constants used were $h_{1D_{13}} = 0.829$, $h_{2D_{13}} = -0.845$, $h_{1D_{15}} = 0.346$, and $h_{2D_{15}} = 0.031$.
- The strong coupling constants used were $|g_{1D_{13}}| = 1.59$ and $|g_{1D_{15}}| = 1.03$.
- The photon-beam asymmetry $\Sigma$ was computed at $E_\gamma = 2.35$ GeV and showed non-zero values in the region $\cos\theta \approx (-0.15 \sim 0.5)$ only when resonance contributions were included.

## Context

This work extends earlier Born-term calculations by Oh (2006) and Kim (2011) that found insufficient production rates to match experimental data from the CLAS Collaboration (Hicks 2010). The study demonstrates that the $D_{13}(2080)$ resonance, with parameters from the Particle Data Group (Nakamura 2010) and quark-model predictions by Capstick (1992, 1998), is essential for describing the reaction near threshold. The results enable future measurements of the photon-beam asymmetry to further constrain the role of nucleon resonances in strange-meson photoproduction.
