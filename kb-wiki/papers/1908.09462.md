# Second order causal hydrodynamics in Eckart frame: using gradient expansion scheme

> In the present work, we develop a causal theory of relativistic non-ideal fluids up to the second order in the Eckart frame using gradient expansion scheme. Keeping the spirit of Mueller-Israel-Stewart formalism, the general forms of bulk viscosity, shear viscosity tensor and the heat flow vector are presented. Since each of the flux quantities explicitly carry curvature terms, we show that our formalism finds application in astrophysics in particular in the strong gravity regime. We elucidate two such applications namely in viscous thick accretion disks also known as Polish doughnuts and in addressing non-rotating equilibrium configuration, like neutron stars.

# Second order causal hydrodynamics in Eckart frame: using gradient expansion scheme

## Key claim

The paper develops a causal theory of relativistic non-ideal fluids up to second order in gradients in the Eckart frame, explicitly including curvature terms that were absent in earlier MIS (Mueller-Israel-Stewart) formulations. The formalism is claimed to be applicable to astrophysical systems in strong gravity regimes, specifically viscous thick accretion disks (Polish doughnuts) and non-rotating neutron star equilibrium configurations, though these applications are only outlined qualitatively rather than computed.

## Method

The author adopts the gradient expansion scheme previously used by Baier et al. (2008) and Romatschke (2010) in the Landau frame, and reformulates it in the Eckart frame for systems with a single conserved charge where heat flow is non-negligible. All possible second-order gradient terms of the fluid four-velocity, temperature, and chemical potential are systematically enumerated to construct general forms of bulk viscosity, shear viscosity tensor, and heat flow vector, including curvature terms arising from non-commutativity of covariant derivatives. The first-order solutions are substituted into second-order terms to produce relaxation-type dynamical equations with relaxation-time coefficients, following the MIS formalism.

## Key numbers

- Nine second order transport coefficients for shear viscosity: $\tau_{\pi}, \xi_{2}, \xi_{3}, \xi_{4}, \xi_{5}, \xi_{6}, \xi_{7}, \kappa_1, \kappa_2$
- Ten second order transport coefficients for bulk viscosity: $\zeta_1$ through $\zeta_{10}$ (though $\zeta_1$ is not explicitly listed in the final equation, the text states ten coefficients)
- Eleven second order transport coefficients for heat flow: $\tau_{q}, \xi_2$ through $\xi_{11}$ (the text uses $\chi$ notation in the final heat flow equation but states $\xi$ in the count)
- Relaxation-time coefficient for shear viscosity defined as $\tau_{\pi} = \lambda_1 / (2 c_1 \eta)$

## Context

This work extends the causal second-order hydrodynamics of Mueller-Israel-Stewart (Israel, 1976; Israel and Stewart, 1979) by incorporating curvature terms that were systematically included in the Landau frame by Baier et al. (2008) and Romatschke (2010) for heavy-ion collision applications. The paper adapts this approach to the Eckart frame, which is more suitable for astrophysical systems where heat flow and conserved charges are present. The formalism is intended to enable future studies of viscous accretion disks and neutron star structure in strong gravity, though no numerical calculations are performed for these systems in the present work.
