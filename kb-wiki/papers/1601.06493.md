# On Exotic Systems of Baryons in Chiral Soliton Models

> The role of zero mode quantum corrections to the energy of baryonic systems with exotic quantum numbers (strangeness) is discussed. A simple expression for the contribution depending on strange inertia is obtained in the $SU(3)-$collective coordinate quantization approach, and it is shown that this correction stabilizes the systems the stronger the greater their baryon number is. Furthemore, systems are considered which could be interpreted in the quark model language as containing additional $q\bar q-$pairs. It is argued that a strange skyrmion crystal should have additional binding in comparison with the $SU(2)-$quantized neutron crystal.

## Key claim
Zero-mode quantum corrections from rotations in the "strange" direction stabilize baryonic systems more strongly than isotopic rotations, with the stabilizing effect increasing as baryon number grows; however, the results are obtained within the rigid rotator model and should be considered a starting point for more elaborate investigations.

## Method
The author uses the SU(3) collective coordinate quantization approach (rigid rotator model) applied to classical soliton solutions of the Skyrme model. The energy expression includes the classical soliton mass plus rotational quantum corrections from zero modes, with the strange inertia \(\lambda_s\) and isospin inertia \(\lambda_r\) characterizing each system. The analysis covers both minimal SU(3) multiplets (containing only valence quarks) and nonminimal multiplets (interpreted as containing additional \(q\bar q\)-pairs), as well as skyrmion crystals.

## Key numbers
- For the Skyrme model with parameters \(F_\pi = 108\,\text{MeV}\), \(e = 4.84\): \(\lambda_s^{-1} \simeq 0.3\,\text{GeV}\) and \(\lambda_r^{-1} \simeq 0.1\,\text{GeV}\).
- Asymptotic relative binding energy per baryon from strange rotations: 0.33 in the Skyrme model, 0.46 in the model with explicit scalar meson.
- Relative binding energy of quantized dibaryons (divided by total energy of possible final states) is close to 0.2 in many cases.
- For an octet of baryons with one additional \(q\bar q\)-pair (\(m=1\)): \(\delta E_{rot} = 2/\lambda_s + 3/\lambda_r\).
- For the Skyrme model parameters, the energy surplus for adding one \(q\bar q\)-pair is \(\delta E_{rot} = [0.3(3B/2 + 2 - N) + 0.2N]\) in GeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral soliton approach to exotic baryon systems, following predictions of strange dibaryons (the H-particle) by Jaffe (1977) in the MIT bag model and earlier Skyrme model studies of SU(3) multiplets of dibaryons and tribaryons. The paper clarifies general properties not well understood in previous investigations, particularly the stabilizing role of strange-direction quantum corrections. It enables further study of strange skyrmion crystals and suggests that systems with large baryon number may be simpler objects than the B=1 sector, though vibrational corrections and meson mass terms remain to be fully incorporated.
