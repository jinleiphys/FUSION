# Nucleon resonances in the $π^- p \to K^0 Λ$ reaction near threshold

> We investigate the two-body reaction $π^- p \to K^{0} Λ$ within the effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model. In addition to the "background" contributions from $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange, $u$-channel $Σ(1192)$ and $Σ^*(1385)$ exchanges, and $s$-channel nucleon pole terms, the contributions from the nucleon resonances $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, and $N^*(1720)$ are investigated. It is shown that the inclusion of these nucleon resonances contributions leads to a good description of the experimental total and differential cross sections data at low energy region. The $s$-channel $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, and $N^*(1720)$ resonances and the $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange give the dominant contributions below $W = 1.76$ GeV, while the $u$-channel $Σ(1192)$ and $Σ^*(1385)$ exchanges give the minor contributions.

# Nucleon resonances in the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda$ reaction near threshold

## Key claim
The nucleon resonances $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, and $N^*(1720)$ provide dominant contributions to the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda$ reaction below $W = 1.76$ GeV, but the coupling constant $g_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)} = 1.31 \pm 0.08$ is determined under the assumption of a constant total decay width for the $N^*(1535)$, which yields a smaller ratio $R = 0.71 \pm 0.10$ compared to analyses using energy-dependent widths.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with the isobar model, computing tree-level Feynman diagrams for $s$-channel nucleon pole and nucleon resonances ($N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1720)$), $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange, and $u$-channel $\Sigma(1192)$ and $\Sigma^*(1385)$ exchanges. Coupling constants for $t$- and $u$-channel processes were obtained from $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry, while resonance couplings were derived from partial decay widths; a four-parameter $\chi^2$ fit (including the free $g_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)}$ and three relative phases) was performed against 385 differential cross section data points below $W = 1.76$ GeV.

## Key numbers
- $g_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)} = 1.31 \pm 0.08$ from the best fit (Fit I) to differential cross section data below $W = 1.76$ GeV.
- Ratio $R \equiv |g_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)} / g_{\eta N N^*(1535)}| = 0.71 \pm 0.10$, using $g_{\eta N N^*(1535)} = 1.85 \pm 0.24$ from the partial decay width of $N^*(1535) \to N\eta$.
- $\chi^2/dof = 2.3$ for Fit I (including $N^*(1535)$) versus $\chi^2/dof = 3.4$ for Fit II (without $N^*(1535)$).
- Cutoff parameters: $\Lambda_B = 0.86 \pm 0.02$ GeV (background) and $\Lambda_R = 0.85 \pm 0.04$ GeV (resonances) in Fit I.
- $N^*(1650)$ contribution is predominant over a wide energy region; $N^*(1535)$ contribution is significant from threshold to $W < 1.66$ GeV; $t$-channel $K^*$ exchange and $N^*(1720)$ dominate above $W > 1.7$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda$ reaction, which is a pure isospin $I=1/2$ channel free from $\Delta(1232)$ contributions, providing a clean probe of nucleon resonances coupling to $\pi N$ and $K\Lambda$ channels. It contrasts with partial wave analyses (Sibirtsev 2005; Ronchen 2012) that describe the data but make it difficult to extract individual resonance properties, and with the chiral unitary approach (Garcia-Recio 2003; Kaiser 1996) that dynamically generates the $N^*(1535)$. The extracted coupling ratio $R = 0.71 \pm 0.10$ is smaller than the value $1.3 \pm 0.3$ from Liu (2005) using $J/\psi$ decays with energy-dependent widths, and falls within the range $0.8$ to $2.6$ from coupled-channels calculations (Penner 2002). The results enable improved extraction of $N^*$ properties from hadronic production data and can inform coupled-channel analyses of $\pi N \to K\Lambda$ and related reactions.
