# Net baryon density in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We calculate the net baryon rapidity distribution in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) in the framework of the Parton Cascade Model (PCM). Parton rescattering and fragmentation leads to a substantial increase in the net baryon density at mid-rapidity over the density produced by initial primary parton-parton scatterings. The PCM is able to describe the measured net baryon density at RHIC.

## Key claim

The Parton Cascade Model (PCM), based on perturbative QCD parton distributions and multiple parton scattering, can explain the measured net baryon excess at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, without invoking nonperturbative baryon junction mechanisms. The model achieves qualitative agreement with data, but the authors note that the PCM is not applicable at lower SPS energies where it underpredicts the net baryon multiplicity by about 80%.

## Method

The authors used the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) implemented in the VNI/BMS code, which solves a relativistic Boltzmann equation for parton distribution functions including all lowest-order QCD scattering processes among massless quarks and gluons. Parton fragmentation processes (q→qg, q→qγ, g→gg, g→qq̅) were included, with branchings terminated when parton virtuality dropped below μ₀=1 GeV. The GRV-HO parton distribution parametrization was used with initialization scales Q₀² = (pTmin)² of 0.50 GeV² for √sNN=130 GeV and 0.59 GeV² for √sNN=200 GeV, and calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions both with and without parton rescattering and fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Net baryon multiplicity density at mid-rapidity from primary parton scatterings only: dN(B−B̅)/dy = 6.3 at √sNN=130 GeV and 5.0 at √sNN=200 GeV
- Full PCM calculation including rescattering and fragmentation yields net baryon density at mid-rapidity of approximately 18 at √sNN=130 GeV and near 14 at √sNN=200 GeV
- Experimental estimates quoted: dN(B−B̅)/dy ≈ 19±2 at √sNN=130 GeV and ≈ 14±4 at √sNN=200 GeV
- Average rapidity shift per subsequent parton collision after first hard scattering: roughly 0.65 units per collision
- At SPS energy √sNN=17.4 GeV, the PCM net-baryon multiplicity at central rapidity is only about 20% of the experimental value from NA49

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model approach to net baryon production at RHIC energies, contrasting with models based on baryon junctions (Kharzeev, 1996; Hijing/B) or quasiclassical glue fields (Color Glass Condensate) and fragmenting color flux tubes (Schwinger mechanism) that had underpredicted the data. The authors show that conventional pQCD-driven multiple parton scattering can account for the observed baryon excess, but note that the same approach fails at SPS energies (NA49 experiment), where nonperturbative mechanisms must dominate. The results enable further study of baryon number transport as a function of collision energy and system size.
