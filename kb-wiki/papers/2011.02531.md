# Weinberg operator contribution to the nucleon electric dipole moment in the quark model

> We evaluate the contribution of the CP violating gluon chromo-electric dipole moment (the so-called Weinberg operator, denoted as $w$) to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of nucleons in the nonrelativistic quark model. The CP-odd interquark potential is modeled by the perturbative one-loop level gluon exchange generated by the Weinberg operator with massive quarks and gluons. The nucleon EDM is obtained by solving the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation of the three-quark system using the Gaussian expansion method. It is found that the resulting nucleon EDM, which may reasonably be considered as the irreducible contribution, is smaller than the one obtained after $γ_5$-rotating the anomalous magnetic moment using the CP-odd mass calculated with QCD sum rules. We estimate the total contribution to be $d_n = w \times 20 \, e \, {\rm MeV}$ and $d_p = - w \times 18 \, e \, {\rm MeV}$ with 60% of theoretical uncertainty.

## Key claim

The irreducible contribution of the Weinberg operator to the nucleon electric dipole moment, calculated in the nonrelativistic quark model, is smaller than the reducible contribution obtained from gamma5-rotating the anomalous magnetic moment using QCD sum rules, and the total nucleon EDM is estimated with 60% theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors derived a CP-odd interquark potential from the Weinberg operator using perturbative one-loop level gluon exchange with massive quarks and gluons in the heavy quark effective theory. They solved the nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation for the three-quark system using the Gaussian expansion method with three different interquark potentials (Bhaduri, AL1, AP1) to obtain the nucleon wave function and EDM.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM: d_n = w x 20 e MeV (total contribution)
- Proton EDM: d_p = -w x 18 e MeV (total contribution)
- Irreducible contribution: d_n^(irr) = -w x (4-5) e MeV and d_p^(irr) = w x (4-5) e MeV, with the range indicating uncertainty from choice of quark model interaction
- Theoretical uncertainty of 60% for both neutron and proton total EDM
- Upper limit on Weinberg operator at scale mu = 1 TeV: |w(mu = 1 TeV)| < 2.3 x 10^(-10) GeV^(-2)
- Gluon mass m_g = 350 MeV taken from lattice QCD
- QCD coupling alpha_s = 0.3 chosen at energy scale close to nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of the Weinberg operator contribution to nucleon EDMs, which began with Weinberg's (1989) naive dimensional analysis giving d_N approx w x 90 e MeV, and continued with Bigi and Uraltsev (1991) who identified reducible and irreducible contributions. The reducible part was evaluated using QCD sum rules by Demir (2002) and Haisch (2019), yielding d_n = w x 25 e MeV and d_p = -w x 23 e MeV with 50% uncertainty. The present calculation provides the first evaluation of the irreducible contribution, which had been neglected in previous studies, and enables more accurate constraints on the Weinberg operator from neutron EDM experiments.
