# Forward dispersion relations for pion-kaon scattering and the K*0(800) resonance

> We review our recent analysis of $πK$ scattering data in terms of forward dispersion relations, and also present the parameters of the strange resonances. This work consists of fits to the data that are constrained to satisfy analyticity requirements. The method yields a set of simple and consistent parameterizations that are compatible with forward dispersion relations up to 1.6 GeV while still describing the data. We also obtain the pole parameters of the $K^*_0(800)$ and the $K^*(892)$ resonances.

## Key claim

The authors present a dispersive analysis of pion-kaon scattering that yields parameterizations compatible with forward dispersion relations up to 1.6 GeV, but the method requires larger deviations from data above that energy; the analysis produces a clear pole for the still controversial K*0(800) resonance, which according to the Particle Data Group still needs confirmation.

## Method

The authors first obtain unconstrained fits to data (UFD) for each partial wave independently, then check fulfillment of forward dispersion relations (FDR) to identify inconsistent data points, and finally impose the FDR and sum rules as constraints to produce constrained fits to data (CFD). The FDR are calculated at t=0 using one symmetric amplitude with one subtraction and one antisymmetric amplitude without subtractions, covering the isospin basis. The method also includes three sum rules for threshold parameters to improve results in the near-threshold region where no data exist.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length: m_pi a_0^{1/2} = 0.22 +/- 0.01 (from CFD)
- Scattering length: m_pi a_0^{3/2} = -0.054^{+0.010}_{-0.014} (from CFD)
- Combined scattering length from CFD: (1/3)(a_0^{1/2} - a_0^{3/2}) = 0.091^{+0.006}_{-0.005} m_pi^{-1}
- Combined scattering length from DIRAC collaboration: (1/3)(a_0^{1/2} - a_0^{3/2}) = 0.11^{+0.09}_{-0.04} m_pi^{-1}
- K*0(800) pole from CFD: Mass = 680 +/- 15 MeV, Width = 668 +/- 15 MeV, coupling |g| = 4.99 +/- 0.08 GeV
- K*(892) pole from CFD: Mass = 892 +/- 1 MeV, Width = 58 +/- 2 MeV, coupling |g| = 6.02 +/- 0.06
- FDR consistency: d_{T+}^2 = 0.39 and d_{T-}^2 = 0.13 for CFD in region sqrt(s_min) to m_K + m_eta; d_{T+}^2 = 0.17 and d_{T-}^2 = 0.70 for CFD in region m_K + m_eta to 1.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the group's previous dispersive analysis of pion-pion scattering (GarciaMartin 2011) to the pion-kaon system, and contrasts with earlier dispersive analyses using Roy-Steiner equations (Descotes-Genon 2006) that could only be applied below 0.935 GeV. The results provide model-independent constraints for Chiral Perturbation Theory (Gasser 1984) and other unitarized approaches (Dobado 1992), and enable comparison with Lattice QCD calculations of threshold parameters (Beane 2006) and resonances (Lang 2012).
