# Dominance of Sign Geometry and the Homogeneity of the Fundamental Topological Structure

> We propose and support the possibility that the shape of topological density 2-point function in pure-glue QCD is crucially, and possibly entirely, determined by the space-time folding (geometry) of the double-sheet sign-coherent structure of Ref.[1], while the distribution of topological density within individual sheets only determines the overall magnitude of the correlator at finite physical distances. A specific manifestation of this, discussed here, is that the shape of the correlation function (encoding e.g. the masses of pseudoscalar glueballs) is reproduced upon the replacement q(x) -> sgn(q(x)), i.e. by considering the double sheet of the same space-time geometry but with constant magnitude of topological density. Combined with previous results on the fundamental topological structure, this suggests that a collective degree of freedom describing topological fluctuations of QCD vacuum can be viewed as a global space-filling homogeneous double membrane. Selected possibilities for practical uses of this are discussed.

## Key claim

The shape of the topological density two-point function in pure-glue QCD is crucially, and possibly entirely, determined by the space-time folding (geometry) of the double-sheet sign-coherent structure, while the distribution of topological density within individual sheets only determines the overall magnitude of the correlator at finite physical distances. The authors propose that the shape of the correlation function is reproduced upon the replacement q(x) -> sgn(q(x)), i.e., by considering the double sheet of the same space-time geometry but with constant magnitude of topological density.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of pure-glue SU(3) gauge theory using the Iwasaki action on four ensembles with lattice spacings from 0.110 fm down to 0.055 fm, keeping the physical volume fixed at 3 fm^4. For each configuration, they computed the topological density using the overlap Dirac operator and constructed a "geometric" configuration by replacing each q(x) with its sign sgn(q(x)). They then compared the original two-point function G(r) with the geometric two-point function Gbar(r) after rescaling the latter to match at r=3 lattice units, using a relative overlap measure to quantify the agreement across physical distance intervals.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used: 0.110 fm (12^4 lattice), 0.082 fm (16^4), 0.066 fm (20^4), and 0.055 fm (24^4), all with physical volume V_p = 3.0 fm^4
- Number of configurations per ensemble: 142 (12^4), 50 (16^4), 20 (20^4), and 10 (24^4)
- The relative overlap between original and geometric correlators approaches unity for the physical distance interval [0.15 fm, 0.30 fm] as the lattice spacing decreases
- For the finest lattices (2.3-3.6 GeV), the ratio qbar^2(a) (the long-distance limit of G(r,a)/Gbar(r,a)) behaves as qbar^2(a) proportional to a

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings on sign-coherent topological structure in QCD configurations (Horvath 2003) and its dynamical origin (Alexandru 2005), building on the "Bottom-Up approach" framework (Horvath 2006). The paper proposes that the fundamental topological structure can be viewed as a global space-filling homogeneous double membrane, which if confirmed would provide a simple explicit link between short and long-distance physics. This could enable faster spectral calculations (glueball masses) by requiring only sign configurations rather than precise topological density values, and offers a novel way to test the exact nature of asymptotic freedom through measurement of qbar^2(a).
