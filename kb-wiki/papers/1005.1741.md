# Effect of pairing correlations on incompressibility and symmetry energy in nuclear matter and finite nuclei

> The role of superfluidity in the incompressibility and in the symmetry energy is studied in nuclear matter and finite nuclei. Several pairing interactions are used: surface, mixed and isovector dependent. Pairing has a small effect on the nuclear matter incompressibility at saturation density, but the effects are significant at lower densities. The pairing effect on the centroid energy of the isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR) is also evaluated for Pb and Sn isotopes by using a microscopic constrained-HFB approach, and found to change at most by 10% the nucleus incompressibility $K_A$. It is shown by using the Local Density Approximation (LDA) that most of the pairing effect on the GMR centroid come from the low-density nuclear surface.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations have a small effect (a few percent) on the nuclear matter incompressibility at saturation density, but the effects become significant (up to about a factor of 2) at lower densities; in finite nuclei, the pairing effect on the centroid energy of the isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR) changes the nucleus incompressibility K_A by at most 10%, with the magnitude depending on the type of pairing interaction used (surface, mixed, or isovector dependent).

## Method

The authors used a constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) approach to evaluate the centroid energy of the isoscalar GMR in Pb and Sn isotopes, employing several zero-range pairing interactions: isoscalar (IS) with surface-type (eta=1.0), mixed-type (eta=0.35 and 0.65), and isoscalar-plus-isovector (IS+IV) interactions (MSH and YS). In nuclear matter, the BCS gap equation was solved to compute the pairing gap, pairing energy density, and their contributions to the incompressibility K(rho) and symmetry energy S(rho) as functions of density. A Local Density Approximation (LDA) was also used to connect the nuclear matter results to finite nuclei by integrating the density-dependent incompressibility and symmetry energy over the nuclear density profile of 120Sn.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter incompressibility K_infinity without pairing is 230.2 MeV for the SLy5 Skyrme interaction; with the MSH pairing interaction, K_infinity changes to 223.9 MeV (a reduction of about 3%).
- For the IS mixed-type pairing (eta=0.35) with SLy5, K_infinity is 227.3 MeV; for the pure surface pairing (eta=1.00), K_infinity is 230.1 MeV (essentially unchanged from the no-pairing value).
- In 120Sn, the LDA gives K_Nucl = 119.8 MeV without pairing; the surface-type pairing (IS eta=1.00) contributes K_pair = -6.0 MeV, a reduction of about 5%.
- In 114Sn, the surface-type pairing decreases K_A by about 10% relative to the no-pairing CHFB result; in 120Sn, the reduction is about 5%.
- The symmetry energy at saturation J is 32.03 MeV without pairing; with the MSH pairing interaction, J becomes 31.33 MeV.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy is 48.25 MeV without pairing; with the MSH pairing interaction, L becomes 55.77 MeV (a change of about 15%).

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing effects on the GMR centroid energy initiated by Civitarese et al. (1991) and more recently by Li et al. (2008) and Khan et al. (2009), which had only used a pure surface pairing interaction. The present paper systematically compares surface, mixed, and isovector-dependent pairing interactions, using the SLy5, LNS, Sk255, and Sk272 Skyrme functionals. The results enable a more nuanced interpretation of the systematic softness of open-shell Sn and Cd isotopes observed in recent GMR measurements by Li et al. (2007, 2010) and Garg et al. (2009), suggesting that a surface-type pairing interaction may be responsible for the reduced incompressibility in open-shell nuclei.
