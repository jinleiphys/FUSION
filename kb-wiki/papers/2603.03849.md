# Nuclear matter properties and neutron star structures from an extended linear sigma model

> The properties of nuclear matter and the structures of neutron stars are analyzed with a baryonic extended linear sigma model in mean-field approximation, where the masses of baryons and mesons are generated via the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The couplings between the iso-scalar scalar meson and nucleons, $g_{σNN}$, the iso-vector scalar meson and nucleons, $g_{a_0 NN}$, and the four-vector meson couplings play an important role in the properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars. The introduction of the $δ$ meson leads to a plateau structure of the symmetry energy, $E_{\rm sym}(n)$, at intermediate densities, which is crucial to the consistency of neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb and the tidal deformability of a canonical neutron star. The explicit chiral symmetry breaking term is then introduced with a constant background field, $ξ$, which can be related to the current quark mass and thus the pion-nucleon sigma term, $σ_{πN}$. A negative $σ_{πN}$ leads to a stiffer EOS of neutron star matter and thus a larger maximum mass of neutron stars, but the value of $σ_{πN}$ needed to satisfy the astrophysical constraints is negative, not positive as the vacuum value. The study may provide insights into the running behaviors of the parameters in the low-energy effective model to give the density-dependent description for the EOS of neutron star matter.

## Key claim

The extended linear sigma model can reproduce neutron star observations only if the pion-nucleon sigma term takes a negative value (around -600 MeV), contradicting the positive vacuum value of 32-89 MeV, and if the couplings of the delta meson and four-vector mesons deviate from their empirical vacuum values.

## Method

The authors constructed a baryonic extended linear sigma model (bELSM) in mean-field approximation, where baryon and meson masses arise from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking via vacuum expectation values of scalar meson fields. They fitted model parameters to vacuum hadron spectra and nuclear matter properties around saturation density, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations. For three-flavor matter with beta equilibrium, an explicit chiral symmetry breaking term was introduced via a constant background field related to the pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility at saturation density for parameter sets with large four-vector meson couplings is around 500 MeV, larger than the empirical value of 200-300 MeV
- A pion-nucleon sigma term of approximately -600 MeV is needed to satisfy the MSP J0740+6620 constraint around 2 solar masses, while the empirical vacuum value is 32-89 MeV
- For the el-g30eg parameter set, the coupling values are: g_sigma_NN = -5.97, g_a0_NN = -0.671, g_omega_NN = 6.06, g_rho_NN = 3.45, and tilde_g_3 = 0.397
- The el-g3150eg parameter set has tilde_g_3 = 150, with g_sigma_NN = -6.38, g_a0_NN = -0.413, g_omega_NN = 7.09, g_rho_NN = 4.04

## Context

This work extends the baryonic extended linear sigma model previously developed by Ma (2023, 2025) to neutron star structure calculations. The plateau structure of symmetry energy at intermediate densities (around twice saturation density) aligns with prior studies by Paeng (2017), Ma (2018, 2021), Lee (2021), Zabari (2018), Miyatsu (2022), and Li (2022). The results are compared to the TM1 model of Sugahara (1993) and the FSU-delta6.7 model of Li (2022). The study enables future inclusion of density-dependent parameters and quantum corrections to the mean-field approximation for a more complete description of the equation of state.
