# Low--Energy Behavior of Two--Point Functions of Quark Currents

> We discuss vector, axial-vector, scalar and pseudoscalar two-point functions at low and intermediate energies. We first review what is known from chiral perturbation theory, as well as from a heat kernel expansion within the context of the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model of ref. \cite{12}. In this work we derive then these two-point functions to all orders in the momenta and to leading order in $1/N_c$. We find an improved high-energy behaviour and a general way of parametrizing them that shows relations between some of the two-point functions, which are also valid in the presence of gluonic interactions. The similarity between the shape of the experimentally known spectral functions and the ones we derive, is greatly improved with respect to those predicted by the usual constituent quark like models. We also obtain the scalar mass $M_S = 2 M_Q$ independent of the regularization scheme. In the end, we calculate fully an example of a nonleptonic matrix element in the ENJL--model, the $π^+-π^0$ electromagnetic mass difference and find good agreement with the measured value.

## Key claim

The paper derives vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar two-point functions to all orders in momenta and to leading order in 1/N_c within the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model, finding that the low-energy expansion method used in prior work (Bijnens et al., 1993) is inappropriate for drawing conclusions about intermediate Q^2 behavior, and that the correct resummation yields Q^2-dependent couplings and masses rather than the simple vector-meson-dominance forms previously assumed.

## Method

The authors sum the infinite series of bubble diagrams generated by the four-fermion couplings in the ENJL model to leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, without introducing collective fields. They compute the one-loop bare fermion-loop diagrams using proper-time regularization, then resum the geometric series to obtain the full two-point functions. The method is applied to vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar channels, and the results are used to calculate the pi+ - pi0 electromagnetic mass difference as a nonleptonic matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93.3 MeV (experimental value used for comparison)
- Constituent quark mass: M_Q = 265 MeV (fit #1 parameter)
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi = 1165 MeV (fit #1 parameter)
- Axial coupling: g_A = 0.61 (fit #1 parameter)
- Scalar mass: M_S = 2 M_Q, independent of regularization scheme
- Low-energy constant L_8 = (0.9 +/- 0.3) x 10^{-3} at the rho mass scale
- Low-energy constant L_10 = (-5.5 +/- 0.7) x 10^{-3} at the rho mass scale
- Vector meson mass at Q^2=0: M_V^2 = (3/2)(Lambda_chi^2/G_V)(1/Gamma(0,epsilon))
- Axial-vector meson mass at Q^2=0: M_A^2 = 6 M_Q^2 / [(1-g_A)(1 - Gamma(1,epsilon)/Gamma(0,epsilon))]

## Context

This work extends the ENJL model analysis of Bijnens et al. (1993) by going beyond the low-energy expansion to obtain the full Q^2 dependence of two-point functions, correcting the earlier approach that used a Seeley-De Witt heat kernel expansion. The results improve the agreement with experimentally known spectral functions compared to constituent quark models, and the calculation of the pi+ - pi0 electromagnetic mass difference provides the first nontrivial one-loop calculation in the ENJL model. The method enables future calculations at next-to-leading order in the 1/N_c expansion and allows inclusion of nonzero current quark masses.
