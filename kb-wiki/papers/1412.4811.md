# Charmed baryonic resonances in medium

> We discuss the behavior of dynamically-generated charmed baryonic resonances in matter within a unitarized coupled-channel model consistent with heavy-quark spin symmetry. We analyze the implications for the formation of $D$-meson bound states in nuclei and the propagation of $D$ mesons in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to FAIR energies.

## Key claim

Dynamically-generated charmed baryonic resonances in nuclear matter are studied within a unitarized coupled-channel model that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS), and the in-medium modifications of these resonances strongly depend on their coupling to channels with D, D*, and nucleon content, with resonances close to the DN or D*N thresholds changing their properties more evidently than those far off-shell.

## Method

The author employs a unitarized coupled-channel model based on an extended Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction that incorporates SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry in the light sector and HQSS in the heavy sector, solving the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels to obtain scattering amplitudes whose poles correspond to dynamically-generated charmed baryonic resonances. Nuclear medium modifications are incorporated by including Pauli blocking on nucleonic intermediate states and the D and D* meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner, with the self-energy and spectral functions for D and D* mesons obtained simultaneously as required by HQSS.

## Key numbers

- In the C=1, S=0, I=0 sector, a pole identified with the experimental Λc(2595) is obtained around 2618.8 MeV
- A spin-3/2 Λc* resonance is found at (2666.6 - i 26.7 MeV) and assigned to the experimental Λc(2625)
- For C=1, S=0, I=1 (Σc sector), three Σc resonances are obtained with masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV and widths 0.8, 188.0, and 87.0 MeV respectively
- A spin-3/2 Σc bound state at 2568.4 MeV is identified as the charmed counterpart of the Σ(1670)
- A second spin-3/2 Σc state is found at (2692.9 - i 33.5) MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channel studies of charmed baryonic resonances (Tolos 2004, Lutz 2003, Hofmann 2005, Mizutani 2006) by incorporating HQSS constraints that were missing in earlier models, following the approach of Garcia-Recio (2008) and Romanets (2012). The results enable predictions for D-meson bound states in nuclei, contrasting with earlier quark-meson coupling model predictions by Tsushima (1998), and provide transport coefficients for D-meson propagation in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to FAIR energies, with the spatial diffusion coefficient 2πT Dx showing a possible minimum at the hadron-to-QGP phase transition.
