# From Nuclear Structure to Nucleon Structure

> Similarities between nuclear structure study with many-body theory approach and nucleon structure calculations with lattice QCD are pointed out. We will give an example of how to obtain the connected sea partons from a combination of the experimental data, a global fit of parton distribution functions and a lattice calculation. We also present a complete calculation of the quark and glue decomposition of the proton momentum and angular momentum in the quenched approximation. It is found that the quark orbital angular momentum constitutes about 50% of the proton spin.

# From Nuclear Structure to Nucleon Structure

## Key claim

The paper argues that the connected-sea (CS) partons in nucleon structure are analogous to Z-graphs in nuclear many-body theory, and that the quark orbital angular momentum constitutes about 50% of the proton spin in the quenched approximation, with almost all of that orbital angular momentum coming from disconnected insertions (vacuum polarization) rather than from valence quarks.

## Method

The author draws parallels between nuclear many-body theory approaches (Hartree-Fock, RPA, core polarization) and lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure (quenched approximation, connected and disconnected insertions). For the spin decomposition, a complete lattice QCD calculation is performed on a 16^3 × 24 quenched lattice at β = 6.0 using Wilson fermions with three κ values corresponding to pion masses of 650, 538, and 478 MeV, with chirally extrapolated results. The connected-sea parton distributions are extracted by combining experimental data (HERMES semi-inclusive DIS for strangeness, E866 Drell-Yan for flavor asymmetry), the CT10 global PDF fit, and a lattice calculation of the ratio of strange to u/d momentum fractions in the disconnected insertion (R = 0.857(40)).

## Key numbers

- Gottfried sum rule violation: NMC measured I = 0.235 ± 0.026, implying ∫[bar d(x) - bar u(x)] dx = 0.148 ± 0.039
- Ratio of strange momentum fraction to u/d in disconnected insertion: R = 0.857(40) from lattice calculation with dynamical fermions
- Chirally extrapolated u and d quark momentum fraction: 0.64(5); angular momentum fraction: 0.70(5)
- Strange momentum fraction: 0.023(6); strange angular momentum fraction: 0.022(7)
- Glue momentum fraction: 0.33(6); glue angular momentum fraction: 0.28(8)
- Quark spin fraction of proton spin: 0.25(12)
- Quark orbital angular momentum fraction of proton spin: 0.47(13)
- Renormalized values at μ = 2 GeV in MSbar scheme: CI(u) ⟨x⟩ = 0.416(40), CI(d) ⟨x⟩ = 0.151(20), CI(u+d) ⟨x⟩ = 0.569(45), DI(u/d) ⟨x⟩ = 0.037(7), DI(s) ⟨x⟩ = 0.023(6), Glue ⟨x⟩ = 0.334(56)
- Preliminary anomaly contribution slope at q² = -0.186 GeV²: -0.045(16) on 24³ × 64 lattice with pion mass 330 MeV

## Context

This work extends the conceptual parallels between nuclear many-body theory (pioneered by Brown (1971) and Liu and Brown (1976) for giant resonances) and lattice QCD approaches to nucleon structure. The classification of parton degrees of freedom into valence, connected-sea (CS), and disconnected-sea (DS) was formulated by Liu (2000) to explain the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by NMC and confirmed by NA51 and E866 experiments. The complete decomposition of proton momentum and angular momentum into quark and glue components represents the first such calculation, enabling future definitive studies with dynamical fermions at the physical pion mass and continuum limit to compare with experiments.
