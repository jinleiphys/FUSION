# Negative Elliptic Flow from Anomaly Induced DCC Formation

> We discuss characteristic experimental signatures related to the mechanism of DCC formation triggered by the chiral U(1) anomaly in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We predict an enhancement of the fraction of neutral pions compared with all pions in the direction perpendicular to the scattering plane. To quantify the effect on the angular distribution of neutral pions, we compute the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ as a function of the transverse momentum. We find values of order -0.05 at small momenta for neutral pions. We also compute the $v_2$ parameter for inclusive photons, which is easier to measure, and confirmed that the negative a few percent effect prevails in this observable.

# Negative Elliptic Flow from Anomaly Induced DCC Formation

## Key claim

The chiral U(1) anomaly trigger mechanism for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions produces a negative elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ of order -0.05 for neutral pions at low transverse momentum, and a negative few percent effect for inclusive photons, but this prediction depends on the anomaly kick parameter being approximately $a_n = 0.1$ as estimated for RHIC conditions and may be partially affected by the neglect of quantum dissipation in the classical simulation.

## Method

The authors implemented the chiral anomaly effect as an initial "kick" to the $\pi^0$ fields in the linear sigma model simulation code developed by Asakawa et al. (1995), which includes boost-invariant longitudinal expansion of the hadronic debris. They computed the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ for neutral pions and inclusive photons from $\pi^0$ decay using a Monte-Carlo code coupled to the sigma model simulation, employing a coherent state approximation to extract particle numbers from field configurations. The approximate $v_2$ was defined using azimuthal angle bins relative to the reaction plane, with the anomaly kick parameter set to $a_n = 0.1$ based on a previous estimate for RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion $v_2^{app}$ is approximately -0.04 to -0.05 (4-5% negative) at low transverse momentum for kick parameter $a_n = 0.1$ at time $\tau = 11$ fm
- Photon $v_2^{app}$ is approximately -0.01 (1% negative) at low transverse momentum for kick parameter $a_n = 0.1$ at time $\tau = 11$ fm
- The number ratio $n(\pi^0)/n(\text{all }\pi)$ exceeds 1/3 by about 13% at low momenta $|\vec{k}| \leq 250$ MeV
- Photons exhibit an azimuthal angle variation of about 10% for transverse momenta $k_T \leq 500$ MeV
- Three values of the anomaly kick parameter were used in calculations: $a_n = 0.05$, $0.1$, and $0.2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on anomaly-induced DCC formation (Asakawa, Minakata, Muller 1996, 1998, 2002) by converting predictions into the elliptic flow language commonly used in heavy ion collision experiments. The negative $v_2$ predicted here is opposite to the positive elliptic flow commonly observed and attributed to hydrodynamical expansion of hadronic debris, making it a potential unique signature of the anomaly mechanism. The results suggest that measuring photon $v_2$ at low transverse momenta at RHIC experiments could test this DCC formation mechanism, as photon measurements are experimentally simpler than event-by-event $\pi^0$ reconstruction.
