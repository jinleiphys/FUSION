# Hard parton dispersion in the quark-gluon plasma, non-perturbatively

> The in-medium dispersion of hard partons, encoded in their so-called asymptotic mass, receives large non-perturbative contributions from classical gluons, i.e. soft gluons with large occupation numbers. Here, we discuss how the analytical properties of thermal amplitudes allow for a non-perturbative determination of the infrared classical contribution through lattice determinations in the dimensionally-reduced effective theory of hot QCD, EQCD. We show how these lattice determinations need to be complemented by perturbative two-loop matching calculations between EQCD and QCD, so that the unphysical (classical) ultraviolet behavior of EQCD is replaced by its proper quantum QCD counterpart. We show how lattice and perturbative EQCD are in good agreement in the UV and present an outlook on the two-loop quantum QCD contribution.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative determination of the classical contribution to the asymptotic mass of hard partons in the quark-gluon plasma by combining lattice EQCD data with perturbative two-loop matching calculations, but notes that only the full two-loop 4D QCD calculation will allow a definite conclusion on convergence.

## Method

The authors compute the classical contribution to the gluon condensate Zg by evaluating correlators of Lorentz-force insertions along a Wilson line in EQCD on the lattice at four temperatures, with continuum extrapolation. They then merge the lattice data at intermediate separations with perturbative EQCD at short distances and an IR tail ansatz at large separations, and perform a two-loop matching calculation in thermal 4D QCD to remove the artificial cutoff dependence introduced by the UV subtraction.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order condensates are Zg|^LO = T^2/6 and Zf|^LO = T^2/12
- The NLO correction to Zg is Zg|^NLO = -T mD/(2pi), where mD = g T sqrt(1+Nf/6) is the Debye mass
- The matching term from the two-loop calculation is Zg^match = (3 g^2 T^2)/(4 pi^2) [ln(2 L_min T) + gammaE - 3/2]
- The lattice data covers separations in the range 0.25 < g3d^2 L < 3
- The short-distance expansion coefficient c0 = 2/pi and c2 = 3/(2 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the program of non-perturbative determination of infrared contributions in hot QCD, previously applied to the transverse scattering kernel by Moore and Schlusser (2021). It builds on the mapping by Caron-Huot (2008) of classical contributions to EQCD and on the Klimov-Weldon (1982) one-loop asymptotic mass results. The method enables a fully non-perturbative determination of the classical contribution to the asymptotic mass, which is an essential ingredient for medium-induced radiation rates and transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma.
