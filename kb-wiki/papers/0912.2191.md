# Viscosity of hadron matter within relativistic mean-field based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings

> The shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscosities are calculated in a quasiparticle relaxation time approximation for a hadron matter described within the relativistic mean-field based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings. Comparison with results of other models is presented. We demonstrate that a small value of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio required for explaining a large elliptic flow observed at RHIC may be reached in the hadron phase. Large values of the bulk viscosity are noted in case of the baryon enriched matter.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (\(\eta/s\)) in the hadron phase can reach values close to the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound of \(1/4\pi\) near the critical temperature \(T_c \sim 180\) MeV, provided in-medium effects (scaling hadron masses and couplings) are included; this suggests that the small \(\eta/s\) needed to explain the large elliptic flow at RHIC may be achieved in the hadronic phase rather than requiring a deconfined quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors calculate shear (\(\eta\)) and bulk (\(\zeta\)) viscosities for hadron matter using the quasiparticle relaxation time approximation within the relativistic mean-field based model with scaled hadron masses and couplings (SHMC). The kinetic equations are solved for a multicomponent system including baryons (N, \(\Delta\), \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\), \(\Sigma^*\), \(\Xi^*\), \(\Omega\) and antiparticles) and meson excitations (\(\pi, K, \bar{K}, \eta, \sigma', \omega', \rho', K^*, \eta', \phi\)), with the relaxation time estimated from free and in-medium modified cross sections, matched to a low-temperature extrapolation for nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key numbers

- The sound velocity squared \(c_s^2\) in the SHMC model shows a pronounced minimum at \(T \simeq 180\) MeV, associated with the hadron-QGP cross-over.
- At \(T \sim 150\) MeV and zero baryon chemical potential, the ratio \(\eta/\zeta\) is only about 3 for both the SHMC and ideal gas (IG) models, indicating bulk viscosity is not negligible.
- Along the freeze-out curve for central Au+Au collisions, \(\eta/s\) decreases monotonically with increasing freeze-out temperature, approaching values close to the AdS/CFT bound \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) at \(T \sim T_c\).
- The \(\zeta/s\) ratio along the freeze-out curve exhibits a maximum at \(T_{\rm fr} \sim 85\) MeV.
- At freeze-out temperatures \(T_{\rm fr} \gtrsim 100\) MeV, the shear and bulk viscosities become comparable: \((\eta/s)_{\rm fr} \simeq 2(\zeta/s)_{\rm fr}\).
- For collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrsim 10\) GeV, the \(\eta/s\) ratio flattens at values below 0.2, as freeze-out occurs at nearly constant \(T_{\rm fr} \approx 165\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle viscosity calculations of Sasaki and Redlich (2008) by incorporating mean fields and scaling hadron masses and couplings (SHMC model), which was previously applied to heavy-ion collisions by Khvorostukhin, Toneev, and Voskresensky (2007, 2008). The results are compared with the ideal gas model, the Hagedorn-gas model, the excluded volume hadron gas model of Gorenstein, Hauer, and Moroz (2008), and the UrQMD-based estimate of Demir and Bass (2008). The findings enable future hydrodynamic calculations using temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential dependent transport coefficients, as advocated by Chaudhuri (2009), rather than constant values.
