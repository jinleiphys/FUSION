# QCD Sum Rules at High Temperature

> We generalize the sum rule approach to investigate the nonperturbative structure of QCD at high temperature. Salient features of the QCD phase above $T_c$ are discussed, and included in the form of power corrections or condensate insertions, in an operator product expansion of gauge invariant correlators. It is shown that for a plausible choice of condensates, QCD at high temperature exhibits color singlet excitations in the vector channels, as opposed to merely screened quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD sum rules can be extended to temperatures well above the deconfinement transition temperature \(T_c\), provided a suitable resummation is performed, and that for a plausible choice of condensates (including a nonzero \(\langle A_4^2\rangle\) arising from spontaneous gauge symmetry breaking), the high-temperature phase exhibits color singlet excitations in the vector channels rather than only screened quarks and gluons. The authors explicitly caution that the sum rules are not applicable around \(T_c\) itself, and that their conclusions depend on the assumption of gauge symmetry breaking above \(T_c\).

## Method

The authors generalize the operator product expansion (OPE) to finite temperature by calculating coefficient functions using Euclidean finite-temperature Feynman rules (Matsubara formalism), which effectively resums an infinite set of higher-dimensional operators. They incorporate the effects of spontaneous gauge symmetry breaking above \(T_c\) by including a background \(A_4\) potential (equivalent to an imaginary chemical potential) and by adding a \(\langle A_4^2\rangle\) condensate contribution. A Borel-transformed sum rule is then constructed for the retarded correlation function of the color-singlet vector-isovector current, matching the OPE calculation in the deep Euclidean region to a spectral ansatz consisting of a single resonance pole plus a continuum threshold.

## Key numbers

* The analysis assumes the hierarchy of scales \(T_c < g^2T < Q^2 < T\) for the applicability of the resummed OPE, where \(g\) is the QCD coupling and \(Q^2\) is the external momentum.
* The electric (plasmon) mass scale is \(gT\), and the nonperturbative magnetic mass scale is \(g^2T\).
* The continuum threshold is denoted \(\sqrt{s_0}\), and the resonance mass and residue are \(m_\rho\) and \(f_\rho\) respectively.
* The Borel sum rule is given by equation (19): \(f_\rho^2 m_\rho^2 e^{-m_\rho^2/M^2} = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} R(M^2,T)\), where \(M^2\) is the Borel parameter.
* The Wilson coefficient for the \(\langle A_4^2\rangle\) condensate is \(\mathcal{C}_{A_4^2} = +\frac{\alpha_s}{12\pi} \int_0^\infty ds\, 2n_\beta \frac{2-3Q^2/4}{(s+Q^2/4)^3}\).
* The Wilson coefficient for the electric condensate \(\langle E^2\rangle\) is \(\mathcal{C}_{E^2} = -\frac{\alpha_s}{6\pi} \frac{1}{Q^4} - \frac{\alpha_s}{72\pi} \frac{1}{Q^2} \int_0^\infty ds\, 2n_\beta \frac{2-3Q^2/4}{(s+Q^2/4)^3}\).
* The Wilson coefficient for the magnetic condensate \(\langle B^2\rangle\) is \(\mathcal{C}_{B^2} = +\frac{\alpha_s}{36\pi} \frac{1}{Q^2} \int_0^\infty ds\, 2n_\beta \frac{2-3Q^2/4}{(s+Q^2/4)^3}\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (SVZ) to finite temperature, building on earlier finite-temperature sum rule studies by Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (who found the \(\rho\) resonance disappears at \(T \sim 140\) MeV), Dosch and Narrison, and Furnstahl, Hatsuda, and Lee. The paper critically analyzes the breakdown of sum rules at low temperature (as also discussed by Adami, Hatsuda, and Zahed) and proposes that a resummed OPE can be applied at high temperatures to test DeTar's conjecture about dynamical confinement in the QCD plasma. The work enables future investigations of nonperturbative bound state formation in the quark-gluon plasma using sum rule techniques, provided the hierarchy of scales \(T_c < g^2T < Q^2 < T\) can be realized.
