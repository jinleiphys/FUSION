# Constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from heavy ion collisions

> Constraints on the Equation of State for symmetric matter (equal neutron and proton numbers) have been extracted from energetic collisions of heavy ions over a range of energies. Collisions of neutron-deficient and neutron-rich heavy ions now provide initial constraints on the EoS of neutron-rich matter at sub-saturation densities from isospin diffusions and neutron proton ratios. This article reviews the experimental constraints on the density dependence of Symmetry Energy at sub-saturation density.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that experimental constraints from heavy ion collisions, specifically isospin diffusion and neutron/proton double ratio measurements, can narrow the density dependence of the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, but the symmetry energy at saturation density \(S_0\) and the exponent \(\gamma_i\) cannot be determined independently from these data alone.

## Method

The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) transport model to simulate collisions of \({}^{124}\)Sn+\({}^{124}\)Sn and \({}^{112}\)Sn+\({}^{112}\)Sn at E/A=50 MeV, comparing calculated double ratios of neutron and proton energy spectra and isospin transport ratios to experimental data. A \(\chi^2\) analysis was performed to constrain the exponent \(\gamma_i\) parameterizing the density dependence of the symmetry potential energy density, and additional calculations at selected \(S_0\) values between 25 and 40 MeV were used to map approximate boundaries in the \(S_0\)–\(L\) plane.

## Key numbers

- The double ratio data rule out very soft (\(\gamma_i=0.35\)) and very stiff (\(\gamma_i=2\)) density-dependent symmetry terms; the \(2\sigma\) uncertainty range for \(\gamma_i\) is \(0.4 \le \gamma_i \le 1.04\), with a minimum \(\chi^2 \sim 2.1\) near \(\gamma_i=0.7\).
- Isospin diffusion data bracket \(\gamma_i\) values to \(0.4 \le \gamma_i \le 1.0\) using the same \(2\sigma\) criterion.
- The overlap of heavy-ion constraints with tin isotope skin thickness analysis yields a mean neutron skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb of about \(0.18 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- The lower box from Pygmy Dipole Resonance analysis is centered at \(S_0 = 32.5\) MeV; the upper box from Isobaric Analog State analysis is also centered at \(S_0 = 32.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior constraints on the symmetric matter equation of state from collective flow (Dan02) and kaon production (Fuc06) to the symmetry energy term at sub-saturation densities, using isospin diffusion (Tsa04, Liu07) and neutron/proton double ratios (Fam06). The heavy-ion results are compared to nuclear structure constraints from tin isotope skin thickness (Che10), Pygmy Dipole Resonance in \({}^{68}\)Ni and \({}^{132}\)Sn (Kli07, Car10), Isobaric Analog States (Dan09), and Giant Dipole Resonance data (Tri08). The consistent overlap among these constraints enables future experiments at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory, RIKEN, and GSI to explore the symmetry energy over a broader density range, including supra-normal densities.
