# Forces within hadrons on the light front

> In this work, we find the light front densities for momentum and forces, including pressure and shear forces, within hadrons. This is achieved by deriving relativistically correct expressions relating these densities to the gravitational form factors $A(t)$ and $D(t)$ associated with the energy momentum tensor. The derivation begins from the fundamental definition of density in a quantum field theory, namely the expectation value of a local operator within a spatially-localized state. We find that it is necessary to use the light front formalism to define a density that corresponds to internal hadron structure. When using the instant form formalism, it is impossible to remove the spatial extent of the hadron wave function from any density, and -- even within instant form dynamics -- one does not obtain a Breit frame Fourier transform for a properly defined density. Within the front formalism, we derive new expressions for various mechanical properties of hadrons, including the mechanical radius, as well as for stability conditions. The multipole ansatz for the form factors is used as an example to illustrate all of these findings.

## Key claim

The paper claims that only the light front formalism (fixed light front time $x^+=0$ with $x^-$ integrated out) can define spatial densities that encode internal hadron structure rather than irremovable wave function spread, and that the conventional Breit frame Fourier transform does not give a true spatial density. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on requiring densities to be finite in the limit of arbitrarily narrow spatial localization, and that densities for operators whose matrix elements depend on average transverse momentum $\mathbf{P}_\perp$ (such as spatial components of the energy-momentum tensor) cannot yield intrinsic internal densities.

## Method

The authors derive the connection between spatial densities and gravitational form factors $A(t)$ and $D(t)$ by starting from the fundamental quantum field theory definition of density as the expectation value of a local operator in a spatially-localized state, using wave packets with finite width $\sigma$ in transverse momentum. They compare the instant form (fixed $x^0=0$) and light front form (fixed $x^+=0$) formalisms, showing that only the light front approach allows factorization of the hadron wave function from the internal structure. They then decompose the classical continuum energy-momentum tensor on the light front into a flow tensor and a pure stress tensor, identifying the $D(t)$ form factor with the pure stress tensor and the $A(t)$ form factor with the light front momentum density.

## Key numbers

- The light front momentum radius is given by $\langle x_\perp^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{mom}} = 4 \frac{\mathrm{d}A(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}\big|_{t=0}$, providing a physical interpretation for the slope of $A(t)$.
- The integral of the light front momentum density $\varepsilon(\mathbf{x}_\perp)$ over all transverse space gives $P^+ A(0)$, imposing the momentum sum rule $A(0)=1$.
- For a spin-zero point particle, the Breit frame energy radius is $\langle r^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{Breit}} = \frac{3}{4M^2}$ (non-zero), while the light front momentum radius vanishes as expected.
- The Breit frame energy radius for spin-zero hadrons is $\langle r^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{Breit}} = 6 \frac{\mathrm{d}A(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}\big|_{t=0} - \frac{3}{4M^2} \{ A(0) + 2D(0) \}$, and for spin-half hadrons is $\langle r^2 \rangle_{\mathrm{Breit}} = 6 \frac{\mathrm{d}A(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}\big|_{t=0} - \frac{3}{4M^2} \{ A(0) - 2J(0) + 2D(0) \}$.
- The pion "mass radius" extracted from Kumano (2017) is $\sqrt{3/2}$ times the $P^+$ radius.

## Context

This work extends the light front formalism previously applied to electromagnetic form factors by Burkardt (2002), Miller (2007, 2009, 2018), and others to the gravitational form factors $A(t)$ and $D(t)$ associated with the energy-momentum tensor. It directly contradicts the conventional Breit frame Fourier transform approach used by Sachs (1962), Polyakov (2018), and Lorce (2018) for defining spatial densities of mechanical properties, building on the critique by Miller (2018) and Jaffe (2020) that three-dimensional Fourier transforms at fixed instant form time are invalid for internal structure. The results enable proper extraction of mechanical radii and stability conditions from experimental measurements of gravitational form factors accessible through deeply virtual Compton scattering at Jefferson Lab and the upcoming Electron Ion Collider, as well as from $\gamma\gamma^*\rightarrow\mathrm{hadrons}$ at Belle.
