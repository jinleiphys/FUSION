# Perturbative Effective Field Theory at Finite Density

> An accurate description of nuclear matter starting from free-space nuclear forces has been an elusive goal. The complexity of the system makes approximations inevitable, so the challenge is to find a consistent truncation scheme with controlled errors. Nonperturbative effective field theories could be well suited for the task. Perturbative matching in a model calculation is used to explore some of the issues encountered in extending effective field theory techniques to many-body calculations.

## Key claim

Perturbative matching in a model calculation with a separable potential shows that an effective field theory (EFT) approach can be extended to finite-density many-body systems, with the truncation error and breakdown scale in the medium determined by the same expansion parameter (Fermi momentum over the free-space breakdown scale) as in free space, provided the free-space constants are used without modification.

## Method

The authors use a separable potential with a characteristic mass scale m and a perturbative coupling α as the "true" underlying interaction. They perform perturbative matching in free space to determine the EFT coefficients order-by-order in both the momentum expansion and the coupling, using both cutoff regularization (CR) and dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (DR/PDS). They then apply the same EFT constants to calculate the ground state energy per particle of nuclear matter to second order in α, including Pauli-blocking effects, and compare the truncation errors between the true and EFT results.

## Key numbers

- The free-space breakdown scale from the model is Λ ≈ m, where m = 600 MeV is the characteristic mass scale of the underlying potential
- For equilibrium nuclear matter, k_F ≈ 260 MeV, giving ξ = k_F/m ≈ 0.43
- The leading truncation error with one constant (C_0 only) is ΔE_int/A = (g-1)(m²/πM)(-α/5 + α²/4)ξ⁵ + O(ξ⁶)
- The leading truncation error with two constants (C_0 and C_2) is ΔE_int/A = (g-1)(m²/πM)(3/10)(3α/7 - α²)ξ⁷ + O(ξ⁸)
- The first-order (Hartree-Fock) energy per particle for the true potential is E_int^(1)/A = (g-1)(α m²/3πM)[ξ³ - (3/5)ξ⁵ + (27/70)ξ⁷ + O(ξ⁹)]

## Context

This work extends the perturbative matching example used by Lepage (1997) for two-body scattering to the many-body domain, addressing issues in applying EFT techniques to nuclear matter calculations. It builds on earlier studies of effective theories for Fermi liquids (Shankar, 1994; Lutz, 1996) and mean-field descriptions of nuclei (Furnstahl, 1997), but focuses on connecting free-space EFT parameters to in-medium observables. The results enable systematic error estimation in nuclear matter calculations and clarify that off-shell ambiguities in the two-body sector are resolved by matching three-body on-shell amplitudes, as discussed by Paulo (1998) and Jim (1998).
