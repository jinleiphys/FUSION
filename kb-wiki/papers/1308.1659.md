# Information content of the weak-charge form factor

> Parity-violating electron scattering provides a model-independent determination of the nuclear weak-charge form factor that has widespread implications across such diverse areas as fundamental symmetries, nuclear structure, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron-star structure. We assess the impact of precise measurements of the weak-charge form factor of ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb on a variety of nuclear observables, such as the neutron skin and the electric-dipole polarizability. We use the nuclear Density Functional Theory with several accurately calibrated non-relativistic and relativistic energy density functionals. To assess the degree of correlation between nuclear observables and to explore systematic and statistical uncertainties on theoretical predictions, we employ the chi-square statistical covariance technique. We find a strong correlation between the weak-charge form factor and the neutron radius, that allows for an accurate determination of the neutron skin of neutron-rich nuclei. We determine the optimal range of the momentum transfer $q$ that maximizes the information content of the measured weak-charge form factor and quantify the uncertainties associated with the strange quark contribution. Moreover, we confirm the role of the electric-dipole polarizability as a strong isovector indicator. Accurate measurements of the weak-charge form factor of ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb will have a profound impact on many aspects of nuclear theory and hadronic measurements of neutron skins of exotic nuclei at radioactive-beam facilities.

## Key claim

The weak-charge form factor measured in parity-violating electron scattering is strongly correlated with the neutron radius, enabling an accurate determination of the neutron skin in neutron-rich nuclei, but the correlation between the weak-charge form factors of \({}^{208}\)Pb and \({}^{48}\)Ca shows significant model variance, meaning that measurements in both nuclei provide complementary rather than redundant information.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear Density Functional Theory with several non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) energy density functionals, including SV-min, DDME-min1, PC-min1, and FSUGold. They performed both a trend analysis, where model parameters were systematically varied to modify bulk nuclear matter properties, and a covariance analysis using the chi-square statistical covariance technique to compute Pearson correlation coefficients between the weak-charge form factor and other nuclear observables such as neutron skin, electric-dipole polarizability, and nuclear matter parameters.

## Key numbers

* PREX measured the weak-charge form factor of \({}^{208}\)Pb at momentum transfer \(q=0.475\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) as \(F_W(q_{\mathrm{PREX}}) = 0.204 \pm 0.028\)
* PREX determined the neutron skin of \({}^{208}\)Pb as \(r_{\mathrm{skin}}^{208} = r_n^{208} - r_p^{208} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}\;\mathrm{fm}\)
* The electric dipole polarizability of \({}^{208}\)Pb was measured as \(e^2\alpha_D^{208} = (20.1 \pm 0.6)\;\mathrm{fm}^3\)
* CREX aims to measure the neutron radius of \({}^{48}\)Ca with an accuracy of 0.02 fm; PREX-II aims for 0.06 fm uncertainty in the neutron skin of \({}^{208}\)Pb
* The momentum transfer for CREX is \(q_{\mathrm{CREX}} = 0.778\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\); for PREX-II it is \(q_{\mathrm{PREX}} = 0.475\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the statistical covariance analysis previously applied to correlations between the electric dipole polarizability and neutron skin by Reinhard (2010) to the weak-charge form factor, building on the PREX measurement by Abrahamyan (2012) and Horowitz (2012). The results show that combined measurements from PREX-II on \({}^{208}\)Pb and CREX on \({}^{48}\)Ca will provide complementary constraints on the isovector sector of energy density functionals, with \({}^{48}\)Ca offering a bridge between ab-initio calculations and density-functional theory, while also helping to discriminate among different Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean-field models.
