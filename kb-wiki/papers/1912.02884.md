# Electromagnetic properties of $^{21}$O for benchmarking nuclear Hamiltonians

> The structure of exotic nuclei provides valuable tests for state-of-the-art nuclear theory. In particular electromagnetic transition rates are more sensitive to aspects of nuclear forces and many-body physics than excitation energies alone. We report the first lifetime measurement of excited states in $^{21}$O, finding $τ_{1/2^+}=420^{+35}_{-32}\text{(stat)}^{+34}_{-12}\text{(sys)}$\,ps. This result together with the deduced level scheme and branching ratio of several $γ$-ray decays are compared to both phenomenological shell-model and ab initio calculations based on two- and three-nucleon forces derived from chiral effective field theory. We find that the electric quadrupole reduced transition probability of $\rm B(E2;1/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+_{g.s.}) = 0.71^{+0.07\ +0.02}_{-0.06\ -0.06}$~e$^2$fm$^4$, derived from the lifetime of the $1/2^+$ state, is smaller than the phenomenological result where standard effective charges are employed, suggesting the need for modifications of the latter in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. We compare this result to both large-space and valence-space ab initio calculations, and by using multiple input interactions we explore the sensitivity of this observable to underlying details of nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The first lifetime measurement of excited states in $^{21}$O yields a B(E2) value that is smaller than phenomenological shell-model predictions using standard effective charges, suggesting that the neutron effective charge in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes is lower than the average sd-shell value; however, the ab initio calculations do not include electromagnetic two-body currents, which are currently under development.

## Method

The experiment used a $^{24}$F secondary beam produced by fragmenting a 140 MeV/nucleon $^{48}$Ca beam on a $^{9}$Be production target at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. $^{21}$O was produced via multi-nucleon removal reactions on a secondary $^{9}$Be target, and emitted gamma rays were detected with GRETINA. The lifetime of the $1/2^+$ state was extracted using two complementary techniques: analysis of the low-energy tail in the gamma-ray spectrum from decays occurring downstream of the target, and the Recoil Distance Method using a $^{181}$Ta degrader at two different distances. The experimental results were compared to phenomenological shell-model calculations using the USDB Hamiltonian and to ab initio calculations using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) and the in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM) with multiple chiral EFT NN+3N interactions.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the $1/2^+$ state in $^{21}$O: $\tau_{1/2^+}=420^{+35}_{-32}\text{(stat)}^{+34}_{-12}\text{(sys)}$ ps
- Electric quadrupole reduced transition probability: $\rm B(E2;1/2^+ \rightarrow 5/2^+_{g.s.}) = 0.71^{+0.07\ +0.02}_{-0.06\ -0.06}$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- Lifetime of the $3/2^+$ state: $\tau_{3/2^+} = 8^{+21}_{-8}$ ps
- Energy of the $1/2^+$ state: $1221.5 \pm 2.2$ keV
- Degrader-reaction ratio (DRR): $22.6\% \pm 1.8\%$
- Phenomenological shell model (USDB with e_n=0.45) predicts B(E2)=1.69 e$^2$fm$^4$, while using orbital-dependent effective charges (e_n=0.374 for 1d-1d and e_n=0.248 for 1d-2s) gives B(E2)=0.82 e$^2$fm$^4$
- VS-IMSRG predicts B(E2)=0.42 e$^2$fm$^4$ and $\tau_{1/2^+}=704$ ps
- IM-NCSM with N$^2$LO$_\text{SAT}$ interaction gives B(E2)=0.67$\pm$0.15 e$^2$fm$^4$ and $\tau_{1/2^+}=444^{+128}_{-81}$ ps
- IM-NCSM with N$^3$LO$_\text{EM}$+N$^2$LO$_\text{L,400}$ gives B(E2)=0.37$\pm$0.01 e$^2$fm$^4$ and $\tau_{1/2^+}=804\pm22$ ps
- IM-NCSM with N$^3$LO$_\text{EM}$+N$^2$LO$_\text{NL,500}$ gives B(E2)=0.61$\pm$0.03 e$^2$fm$^4$ and $\tau_{1/2^+}=488\pm25$ ps
- IM-NCSM with N$^4$LO$_\text{EMN}$+N$^2$LO$_\text{NL,500}$ gives B(E2)=0.58$\pm$0.09 e$^2$fm$^4$ and $\tau_{1/2^+}=513^{+94}_{-69}$ ps

## Context

This work extends the benchmarking of ab initio nuclear theory beyond excitation energies to electromagnetic transition rates in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, where limited data previously existed. The results contradict the phenomenological shell model with standard effective charges (e_n=0.45 from Brown (2006)), but agree with orbital-dependent effective charges from Brown (1982) that account for core-polarization cancellation effects. The comparison with IM-NCSM calculations using four different chiral NN+3N interactions demonstrates that E2 observables provide a more sensitive test of nuclear forces than excitation energies alone, with interactions using nonlocal 3N regulators (such as N$^3$LO$_\text{EM}$+N$^2$LO$_\text{NL,500}$) better reproducing the experimental transition rates. This enables future systematic studies of effective charges across the oxygen isotopic chain and provides constraints for chiral EFT interactions, with new measurements on $^{22}$O (Petri, ANL) expected to further test the orbital-dependent effective charge picture.
