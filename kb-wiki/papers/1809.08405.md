# Resonances of unbound quantum many-body systems of nuclei

> Resonance is a general phenomenon which can happen in classic or quantum systems. An unbound many-body quantum system can undergo a self-resonant process. It has long been a challenge how to describe unbound many-body quantum systems in resonances. In this paper, we develop a novel first-principles method that is capable of describing resonant quantum systems. We exploit, for the first time, the advanced in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) in the complex-energy Gamow-Berggren representation with resonance and non-resonant continuum. The ab initio Gamow IMSRG has broad applications, such as, to the electromagnetic-interaction systems of atoms, molecules or quantum dots, and strong-interaction atomic nuclei. In the present work, we apply the method to loosely bound or unbound nuclear systems. Carbon and oxygen isotopes have been investigated with an optimized chiral effective field theory potential. The resonant states observed in neutron-rich 22O and 24O are well reproduced. The loose halo structure of the Borromean nucleus 22C is clearly seen by the density calculation, in which the continuum s-waves play a crucial role. Further, we predict low-lying resonant excited states in 22C. This method provides rigorous and tractable theoretical calculations for weakly-bound or unbound open quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper develops a first-principles method that, for the first time, combines the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) with the complex-energy Gamow-Berggren representation, enabling the description of bound, resonant, and non-resonant continuum states on equal footing for unbound nuclear many-body systems.

## Method

The authors construct a Gamow Hartree-Fock (GHF) basis in the complex-momentum plane using the chiral NNLO_opt interaction, then perform the IMSRG flow equation as an orthogonal transformation in the complex-symmetric rigged Hilbert space (Gamow IMSRG). For excited states, they apply the equation-of-motion IMSRG (Gamow EOM-IMSRG) truncated at the two-particle two-hole level, using the Magnus formulation and the White generator to decouple the Hamiltonian. Only the neutron d3/2 and s1/2 partial waves are treated in the Berggren basis for neutron-rich carbon and oxygen isotopes, while other channels use a real-energy discrete HF basis.

## Key numbers

- Experimental root-mean-squared matter radius of 22C: 3.44 ± 0.08 fm (from Togano 2016)
- Gamow IMSRG calculation with discrete HF s-waves gives 22C matter radius: 2.79 fm
- Gamow IMSRG calculation with continuum s-waves gives 22C matter radius: 3.06 fm
- Earlier experimental estimate of 22C matter radius: 5.4 ± 0.9 fm (from PhysRevLett.104.062701)
- Predicted 22C first 2+ state energy: 1.05 MeV (bound, width 0.000 MeV)
- Predicted 22C narrow resonances around 3.5 MeV with widths ~0.25 MeV (e.g., 4+ at 3.26 MeV with width 0.245 MeV, 3+ at 3.36 MeV with width 0.262 MeV)
- 12 major harmonic oscillator shells at ℏΩ = 20 MeV used to expand the HF basis

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG method, previously limited to harmonic oscillator or real-energy Hartree-Fock bases (Hergert 2016, PhysRevLett.106.222502), into the complex-energy Gamow-Berggren representation, following the approach of the no-core Gamow shell model and complex coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen 2012, PhysRevLett.108.242501). The method reproduces resonant states in neutron-rich 22O and 24O and provides the first ab initio continuum-coupled calculation of the Borromean halo nucleus 22C, predicting low-lying resonant excited states that have no experimental data yet. This enables rigorous theoretical studies of weakly-bound or unbound open quantum systems across nuclear physics and other fields with electromagnetic interactions.
