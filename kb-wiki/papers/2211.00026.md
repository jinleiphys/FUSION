# The Width of a Beta-decay-induced Antineutrino Wavepacket

> The time evolution of a neutrino is dependent on its initial properties at creation including flavor, energy, and wavepacket size. There exists no solid theoretical prediction for the latter property in the context of nuclear beta decay, despite the importance of this process for the past, present, and future of neutrino experimentation. In this paper, we provide a quantitative prediction for the size of a beta-decay-induced electron antineutrino wavepacket by treating the parent nucleus decaying to an entangled antineutrino-recoil system using the formalism of open quantum systems. Of central importance is the delocalization scale of the parent particle. We construct a systematic description of the hierarchy of localizing entanglements that provides an unambiguous statement of the relevant localization scale, found to be closely related to the diameter of the parent nucleus (e.g. $\sim$5-6~fm for beta-decaying fission daughters) and as low as the typical nucleon-nucleon correlation distance ($\sim$1~fm). Inside a nuclear reactor, for example, this translates to initial electron antineutrino wavepacket widths in the $σ_{ν,x}\sim10\mathrm{-}400$~pm range for $E_{\overlineν_e}>1.8$~MeV, with dependencies on decaying nucleus size, the emitted antineutrino energy, and the kinematics of the recoiling system. Wavepacket sizes in this envelope do not produce an observable effect on oscillation probability in foreseeable reactor experiments in the standard three-neutrino model, including JUNO which is expected to be sensitive to $σ_{ν,x}\lesssim3$~pm.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantitative prediction for the size of a beta-decay-induced electron antineutrino wavepacket, finding that the relevant parent localization scale is closely related to the diameter of the parent nucleus (approximately 5-6 fm for beta-decaying fission daughters) or as low as the typical nucleon-nucleon correlation distance (approximately 1 fm), which translates to initial antineutrino wavepacket widths in the range of 10-400 pm for reactor antineutrinos with energies above 1.8 MeV, a range that is too large to produce an observable effect on oscillation probability in foreseeable reactor experiments including JUNO.

## Method

The authors treat the parent nucleus decaying to an entangled antineutrino-recoil system using the formalism of open quantum systems and reduced density matrices. They construct a systematic description of the hierarchy of localizing entanglements that provides an unambiguous statement of the relevant localization scale, then derive the relationship between parent localization scale and neutrino coherence distance. The neutrino oscillation probability is calculated by projecting the time-evolved reduced density matrix onto flavor states at distance L, accounting for wavepacket separation effects through the off-diagonal width of the neutrino density matrix.

## Key numbers

* Parent nucleus delocalization scale is approximately 5-6 fm for beta-decaying fission daughters (nuclear diameter) and as low as approximately 1 fm (nucleon-nucleon correlation distance)
* Initial electron antineutrino wavepacket widths in the range sigma_nu,x ~ 10-400 pm for reactor antineutrinos with E_nu_e > 1.8 MeV
* JUNO is expected to be sensitive to sigma_nu,x less than or approximately 3 pm after 6 years of running
* Existing constraints from Daya Bay, KamLAND, and RENO give sigma_nu,x > 2.1 x 10^-13 m at 90% CL, and a dedicated Daya Bay measurement gives sigma_nu,x > 1 x 10^-13 m at 95% CL
* Relevant distance scales for reactor antineutrinos include inverse antineutrino energy approximately 0.1-0.7 pm, inter-atomic spacing in fuel rods approximately 0.1-1 nm, and fuel rod size approximately 10-100 cm

## Context

This work provides the first solid theoretical prediction for the wavepacket size of neutrinos from nuclear beta decay, a process that has lacked such a prediction despite its importance for past, present, and future neutrino experiments. The paper extends the approach used by Jones (2015) for pion decay-in-flight beams to the nuclear beta decay case, and addresses the hierarchy of entanglements that determine the parent localization scale. The result shows that the predicted wavepacket widths are too large to be observed by JUNO or other foreseeable reactor experiments, which contrasts with earlier expectations that JUNO might provide a measurement of the wavepacket effect. The formalism developed here could be applied to ultra-light nuclei where ab-initio wave functions are calculable using existing techniques.
