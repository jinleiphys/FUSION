# Heavy baryons in a pion mean-field approach: A brief review

> We review in this paper a series of recent works on properties of singly heavy baryons, based on a pion mean-field approach. In the limit of an infinitely heavy-quark mass, the heavy quark inside a heavy baryon can be regarded as a static color source. In this limit, a heavy baryon can be viewed as $N_c-1$ valence quarks bound by the pion mean fields which are created self-consistently by the presence of the $N_c$ valence quarks. We show that this mean-field approach can successfully describe the masses and the magnetic moments of the lowest-lying singly heavy baryons, using all the parameters fixed in the light-baryon sector except for the hyperfine spin-spin interactions. We also review a recent work on identifying the newly found excited $Ω_c$ baryons reported by the LHCb Collaboration. We discuss possible scenarios to identify them. Finally, we give a future perspective on this pion mean-field approach.

## Key claim

The pion mean-field approach, specifically the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM), can successfully describe the masses and magnetic moments of the lowest-lying singly heavy baryons using parameters fixed in the light-baryon sector, with the only additional free parameters being the hyperfine spin-spin interaction strengths determined from experimental data on heavy baryon masses.

## Method

The authors treat a singly heavy baryon as a bound state of \(N_c-1\) valence quarks in a self-consistent pion mean field, with the heavy quark treated as a static color source in the limit \(m_Q \to \infty\). The classical soliton mass is obtained by minimizing the combined valence and sea quark energies around the saddle point of the chiral field, and the collective Hamiltonian is derived by quantizing the rotational zero modes of the soliton embedded in flavor SU(3). Mass splittings are computed including first-order perturbative corrections from flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking and a hyperfine spin-spin interaction to lift the degeneracy between the spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 sextet states.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM), originally developed by Diakonov, Petrov, and collaborators (1987-1997) for light baryons based on Witten's large-\(N_c\) argument (1979, 1983), to the description of singly heavy baryons. The approach was recently extended by Yang, Kim, and collaborators (2016-2018) to compute masses, magnetic moments, and electromagnetic form factors of heavy baryons, and was used to interpret the five excited \(\Omega_c\) baryons reported by the LHCb Collaboration (Aaij et al., 2017). The framework enables predictions for yet-unobserved states such as \(\Omega_b^*\) and provides a unified description of both light and heavy baryon properties within the same mean-field picture.
