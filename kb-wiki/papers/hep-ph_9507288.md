# Hyperon Masses in Nuclear Matter

> We analyze hyperon and nucleon mass shifts in nuclear matter using chiral perturbation theory. Expressions for the mass shifts that include strong interaction effects at leading order in the density are derived. Corrections to our results are suppressed by powers of the Fermi momentum divided by either the chiral symmetry breaking scale or the nucleon mass. Our work is relevant for neutron stars and for large hypernuclei.

# Hyperon Masses in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The authors derive leading-order expressions for hyperon and nucleon mass shifts in nuclear matter using chiral perturbation theory, but caution that the leading correction from two-loop diagrams is suppressed by only ~M_B p_F/(4π f^2), which at nuclear density (p_F ~ 350 MeV/c) is not small, meaning the results may receive significant corrections from higher orders.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian consistent with SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R chiral symmetry of QCD, treating baryons as heavy static two-component fields. They compute the momentum-independent mass shift ΔM̃_B and the coefficient η_B of the |p|^2 term for the baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) from one-loop pseudo-Goldstone boson exchange and from six independent four-baryon contact operators at leading order in the density expansion, for a background of degenerate Fermi gases of neutrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- For proton-neutron symmetric nuclear matter with Fermi levels p_F^(n) = p_F^(p) = 270 MeV/c: ΔM_n^* = ΔM_p^* = -154 MeV, ΔM_Λ^* = -152 MeV, ΔM_Σ^* = -18 MeV (all Σ charge states), ΔM_Ξ^* = 0 MeV (both charge states)
- For neutron matter with p_F^(n) = 350 MeV/c: ΔM_n^* = -57 MeV, ΔM_p^* = -100 MeV, ΔM_Λ^* = -126 MeV, ΔM_Σ^+^* = 0 MeV, ΔM_Σ^0^* = -15 MeV, ΔM_Σ^-^* = -30 MeV, ΔM_Ξ^* = 0 MeV (both charge states)
- The function I(x) = 1 - 3x^2 + 3x^3 arctan(1/x) appears in the mass shift expressions
- The function D(x) = x^2/(1+x^2)^2 appears in the η_B coefficients
- Weinberg's extracted values: C_T ≈ (1/45 MeV)^2 and C_S ≈ -(1/88 MeV)^2 from S-wave nucleon scattering lengths

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory methods, previously applied to nucleon mass shifts by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and Montano, Politzer and Wise (1992), to the full baryon octet including hyperons. It provides model-independent mass relations analogous to the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula that are independent of the unknown four-baryon contact coefficients, such as 2ΔM̃_Σ0Σ0 - ΔM̃_Σ+ - ΔM̃_Σ- = 0. The results are relevant for neutron star models (Glendenning 1985; Prakash et al. 1995) and large hypernuclei (Dover 1992; Gal and Dover 1995), and the authors note that determination of the c_j coefficients from S-wave hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths could enable predictions of hyperon mass shifts in neutron stars and large hypernuclei.
