# Pairing correlations. Part 2: Microscopic analysis of odd-even mass staggering in nuclei

> The odd-even mass staggering in nuclei is analyzed in the context of self-consistent mean-field calculations, for spherical as well as for deformed nuclei. For these nuclei, the respective merits of the energy differences $Δ^{(3)}$ and $Δ^{(5)}$ to extract both the pairing gap and the time-reversal symmetry breaking effect at the same time are extensively discussed. The usual mass formula $Δ^{(3)}$, is shown to contain additional mean-field contributions when realistic pairing is used in the calculation. A simple tool is proposed in order to remove time-reversal symmetry breaking effects from $Δ^{(5)}$. Extended comparisons with the odd-even mass staggering obtained in the zero pairing limit (schematic model and self-consistent calculations) show the non-perturbative contribution of pairing correlations on this observable. PACS: 21.10Dr; 21.10.Hw; 21.30.-x. Keywords: Mean-field theories; Pairing correlations; Odd-even mass staggering;

## Key claim

The odd-even mass staggering (OES) in nuclei, often attributed solely to pairing correlations, contains significant additional contributions from the smooth part of the binding energy (including deformation effects) that must be removed to isolate the true pairing gap; the five-point formula Δ⁽⁵⁾ extracts the pairing-plus-polarization contribution, while the three-point formula Δ⁽³⁾ oscillates around this value due to the smooth energy component.

## Method

Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations with approximate particle number projection by the Lipkin-Nogami prescription were performed for spherical tin isotopes (¹⁰⁰Sn to ¹⁶⁹Sn) and deformed cerium isotopes (¹¹⁸Ce to ¹⁶⁶Ce). The Skyrme force SLy4 was used in the particle-hole channel, and a surface-peaked delta pairing force (Vτ = −1250 MeV·fm⁻³, cut-off 5 MeV above and below the Fermi level) was used in the T=1 particle-particle channel. The binding energy was separated into a smooth "HFBE" part (calculated as if all nuclei were even, with no blocking and no time-reversal symmetry breaking) and a rapidly varying part containing the pairing gap Δ(N) and the polarization energy E^pol(N) from time-reversal symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- For tin isotopes, |Δ⁽³⁾_HFBE| ≫ |Δ⁽⁵⁾_HFBE| = 0, confirming the smooth part of the energy contributes negligibly to Δ⁽⁵⁾
- Reducing the neutron pairing strength by 20% (from −1250 to −1000 MeV·fm⁻³) decreased Δ⁽³⁾_HFB and Δ⁽⁵⁾_HFB by approximately 20%, while the oscillation from the smooth energy part remained unchanged
- In the schematic BCS model with an equidistant doubly degenerate spectrum (level spacing δϵ = 400 keV), the asymptotic regime where Δ⁽⁵⁾ extracts the pairing gap was reached for Δ/δϵ ≈ 0.5, i.e., when Δ reaches 200 keV
- For realistic cerium spectra, Δ⁽⁵⁾_BCS extracts the quasiparticle energy precisely when Δ at the Fermi energy reaches 60% of the realistic HFB value

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Satula et al. (1998), who showed that deformation produces a direct contribution to the three-point odd-even mass formula in Hartree-Fock calculations with zero pairing, and the study of spherical tin isotopes by Bender et al., who found Δ⁽⁵⁾ to be a better approximation of the pairing gap than Δ⁽³⁾(odd). The paper reconciles these apparently contradictory results by demonstrating that the smooth energy contribution to Δ⁽⁵⁾ decreases from a non-zero value (from deformation effects) to zero as pairing increases, while the blocking contribution increases, so that for realistic pairing strengths Δ⁽⁵⁾ isolates the pairing-plus-polarization contribution. This enables the use of Δ⁽⁵⁾ as a clean observable for adjusting pairing force intensity, while the smooth part of the energy (probed by Δ⁽³⁾-Δ⁽⁵⁾) provides complementary information on the radial dependence of the pairing force.
