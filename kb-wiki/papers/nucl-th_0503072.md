# The generalised relativistic Lindhard functions

> We present here analytic expressions for the generalised Lindhard function, also referred to as Fermi Gas polarisation propagator, in a relativistic kinematic framework and in the presence of various resonances and vertices. Particular attention is payed to its real part, since it gives rise to substantial difficulties in the definition of the currents entering the dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper provides analytic expressions for the relativistic generalisation of the Lindhard function (Fermi gas polarisation propagator) for nucleons and for spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 resonances, with particular attention to the real part which requires subtracted dispersion relations and contact terms to handle divergences; the results are valid for scalar, vector, and tensor vertices and for arbitrary masses of the particles involved.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic polarisation propagator for a free Fermi gas, defined as a one-loop integral over the fermion propagator in the medium with vertices characterising the incoming and outgoing channels. They perform the frequency integration analytically by evaluating the residua in the lower half-plane, then reduce the remaining three-momentum integral to a set of basic functions \(U^{*[n]}_{rel}(M;\mathbf{q},q_0)\) defined in Eq. (C009). These functions are expressed in closed analytic form in terms of logarithms and scaling variables \(y^*_\pm\), with the inelasticity parameter \(\rho = 1 - (M^2 - m^2)/q^2\) and the quantity \(\Delta^* = -\sqrt{[(M-m)^2 - q^2][(M+m)^2 - q^2]}/q^2\) controlling the analytic structure. The generalised Lindhard functions for scalar (0-index), vector (1-index), and tensor (2-index) cases are then built from these basic functions plus contact terms derived from the \(k_F\)-dependent integrals \(\mathfrak{T}^{[n]}\).

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum \(k_F\) and Fermi energy \(E_F = \sqrt{k_F^2 + m^2}\) define the nuclear medium; the contact term \(\mathfrak{T}^{[0]} = \frac{1}{8\pi^2}\left[k_F E_F - m^2 \log\frac{k_F + E_F}{m}\right]\) (Eq. A422).
* The threshold and pseudo-threshold for resonance production are \(Q^T_\pm = \sqrt{\mathbf{q}^2 + (M \pm m)^2}\) (Eq. AC022).
* Critical momenta for boundary intersections: \(q_{\rm cr}^{(1)} = \frac{M+m}{m}k_F\), \(q_{\rm cr}^{(2)} = \frac{M-m}{m}k_F\), and \(k_F^{\rm cr} = \sqrt{M^2 - m^2}\) (Eqs. AC047, AC048).
* The basic function \(U^{*[0]}_{rel}\) is given in closed form by Eq. (AC209) with logarithmic functions \(l_1\), \(l_2\), \(l_3\) defined in Eqs. (A300) and (A122).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Lindhard function of Lindhard (1954) and its relativistic generalisations for the electroweak case by Fattoyev (1998) and Amaro et al. (2002) to include arbitrary vertices (scalar, vector, tensor) and resonances of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2, as well as the case of massless particles relevant for quark-gluon plasma. The analytic expressions enable calculations of the relativistic response to pions, rho-mesons, and other probes within RPA and related many-body frameworks, and provide a tool for evaluating the real part of the polarisation propagator via subtracted dispersion relations.
