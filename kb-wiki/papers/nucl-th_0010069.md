# Production of hot nuclei in Fermi energy domain: from peripheral to central collisions

> The production mechanism of highly excited nuclei in the Fermi energy domain is investigated. A phenomenological approach, based on the exciton model, is used for the description of pre-equilibrium emission. A model of deep inelastic transfer is employed for the peripheral collisions in the post-pre-equilibrium stage. An approach to describe more central collisions is proposed. A geometric overlap formula is employed in a way suitable for given energy domain. A simple geometric approach describing the interaction of participant and spectator zones is used to determine the incomplete fusion channel. Excitation energies of both fragments are determined. Results of the calculation are compared to available experimental data and an overall satisfactorily agreement is obtained. The model's ability to describe the production of the hot nuclei can be employed in the study of multifragmentation and/or in the production of rare beams.

# Production of hot nuclei in Fermi energy domain: from peripheral to central collisions

## Key claim
The paper presents a phenomenological framework combining pre-equilibrium emission (exciton model), deep inelastic transfer, and geometric overlap models that can describe the production of highly excited nuclei across peripheral to central collisions in the Fermi energy domain (20–50 MeV/nucleon), with the caveat that the model's free parameters (σ=0.25 and κ=0.3) were tuned to reproduce specific experimental data and may not be universally applicable without further validation.

## Method
The author developed a Monte Carlo approach that first treats pre-equilibrium emission using an exciton model with Weisskopf-Ewing emission widths and Maxwellian kinetic energy spectra. For peripheral collisions, the deep inelastic transfer (DIT) code of Tassan-Got (1994) is employed; for more central collisions where the di-nuclear configuration cannot form, a geometric overlap formula based on the abrasion-ablation model of Gosset (1977) is used, with participant zone capture determined by comparing neighboring nucleon volumes. The model also incorporates the Wilczynski (1982) angular momentum window framework for near-central collisions and uses the Serber approximation via the Matsuoka (1980) formula for spectator kinematics.

## Key numbers
- Pre-equilibrium emission parameters: σ=0.25 and κ=0.3, used to reproduce experimental neutron multiplicities in reactions of 35 MeV/nucleon ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁰Ca beams with ¹¹²Sn target
- Mean free path between nucleon-nucleon collisions: λ=6 fm, chosen for excitation energy calculation of spectator fragments
- Projectile energies studied: 20–50 MeV/nucleon range, with specific comparisons at 25 MeV/nucleon (⁹³Nb+¹¹⁶Sn), 30 MeV/nucleon (⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni), and 35 MeV/nucleon (⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁷Au)
- Target-like fragment kinetic energy spectrum in ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁷Au at 35 MeV/nucleon shows low energy component below 40 MeV and second component between 50 and 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological approach of Veselsky (1999) for pre-equilibrium emission and combines it with the deep inelastic transfer model of Tassan-Got (1994) and the abrasion-ablation model of Gosset (1977). It provides a computationally efficient alternative to transport and molecular dynamics models (reviewed in ref. TrRev) for describing hot nucleus production in the Fermi energy domain. The model enables systematic studies of multifragmentation and can be applied to rare beam production, with experimental studies underway at the Cyclotron Institute of Texas A&M University (FrgExp).
