# Thermal conductivity in dynamics of first-order phase transition

> Effects of thermal conductivity on the dynamics of first-order phase transitions are studied. Important consequences of a difference of the isothermal and adiabatic spinodal regions are discussed. We demonstrate that in hydrodynamical calculations at non-zero thermal conductivity, $κ\neq 0$, onset of the spinodal instability occurs, when the system trajectory crosses the isothermal spinodal line. Only for $κ= 0$ it occurs at a cross of the adiabatic spinodal line. Therefore ideal hydrodynamics is not suited for an appropriate description of first-order phase transitions.

## Key claim

In non-ideal hydrodynamics with non-zero thermal conductivity (κ ≠ 0), the onset of spinodal instability occurs when the system trajectory crosses the isothermal spinodal line (u_T² < 0), not the adiabatic spinodal line; only in the limit κ = 0 (ideal hydrodynamics) does instability appear at the adiabatic spinodal line (u_ṡ² < 0).

## Method

The authors linearized the non-relativistic Navier-Stokes, continuity, and heat transport equations around a reference point (n_r, T_r) or (n_r, ṡ_r), assuming small velocity and density/temperature fluctuations. They derived a dispersion relation for the growth increment γ(p) and analyzed its solutions in the limits of large thermal conductivity (κ p²/(c_V|γ|) ≫ 1), small thermal conductivity (κ ≪ c_V √c), and zero thermal conductivity (κ = 0), using a Van der Waals equation of state for illustration.

## Key numbers

- For the Van der Waals fluid near the critical point: n_cr = 1/(3b), T_cr = 8a/(27b); the maximal pressure point on the adiabatic spinodal line gives n_P,max = n_cr/3, T_P,max = 16 T_cr/45.
- For the NJL model chiral restoration transition: T_P,max ~ T_cr/2 ≃ 45 MeV.
- For the model in RandrupGQP for hQGP phase transition: T_P,max = 3 T_cr/5 ≃ 100 MeV.
- For the Van der Waals fluid near the critical point, the most rapidly growing mode in the large-κ limit has γ_m = (-u_T²) m n_cr / [(2√β + 1)(d̃η + ζ)] and p_m² = (-u_T²)√β / [(2√β + 1)c].
- For the Van der Waals fluid near the critical point, the most rapidly growing mode in the small-κ limit (slightly below ITS line) has p_m² ≃ -u_T²/(2c) and γ_3m ≃ κ u_T⁴/(4c c_V u_ṡ²).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Skokov and Voskresensky 2009a, 2009b) that focused on viscosity and surface tension effects in first-order phase transitions, but only roughly estimated thermal transport. It resolves a debate between works assuming instability at the adiabatic spinodal line (e.g., RandrupGQP) and those assuming instability at the isothermal spinodal line (e.g., FRS, PR, LL), showing that finite thermal conductivity selects the isothermal spinodal. The results enable proper treatment of spinodal instabilities in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star matter using non-ideal hydrodynamics, and clarify why ideal hydrodynamics is insufficient for describing first-order phase transitions.
