# Nonlocalized motion in two-dimensional container of $α$ particles in $3^-$ and $4^-$ states of $^{12}$C

> The first $3^-$ and $4^-$ states of $^{12}$C are studied in the present container model, in which the shift parameter is introduced to break the parity symmetry for projecting out the negative-parity states. Taking the limit as the shift parameter approaches zero and by variational calculations for one-deformed size parameter, the local energy minima are obtained for the $3^-$ and $4^-$ states. It is found that the obtained single THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke) wave functions for $3^-$ and $4^-$ states are 96% and 92% equivalent to the corresponding GCM wave functions, respectively. The calculated intrinsic densities further show that these negative-parity states of three clusters, different with the traditional understanding of rigid triangle structure, are found to have nonlocalized clustering structure in the two-dimensional container picture.

## Key claim

The first \(3^-\) and \(4^-\) states of \(^{12}\)C, traditionally understood as rigid equilateral triangular arrangements of three alpha particles, are instead found to exhibit nonlocalized clustering motion in a two-dimensional container picture, with the single THSR wave functions achieving 96% and 92% squared overlap with the corresponding Brink-GCM wave functions, respectively.

## Method

The authors extended the THSR (Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke) container model to negative-parity states by introducing shift parameters \(\mathbf{S}_1\) and \(\mathbf{S}_2\) that break parity symmetry in an intermediate step, then taking the limit of infinitesimal shift (side-length \(S = 0.5\) fm equilateral triangle) and performing variational calculations after angular-momentum and parity projection in the two-parameter space of deformed size parameters \(\beta_x = \beta_y\) and \(\beta_z\). The Volkov No.2 force (modified, Majorana parameter \(M = 0.59\)) and harmonic-oscillator size parameter \(\nu = 0.275\) fm\(^{-2}\) were used, identical to the earlier RGM calculations by Kamimura et al. (1981). The resulting single THSR wave functions were compared with Brink-GCM wave functions obtained by superposing hundreds of projected Brink wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The deeper local energy minimum for the \(3^-\) state occurs at \(\beta_x = \beta_y = 1.5\) fm and \(\beta_z = 3.0\) fm, with energy about \(-80.9\) MeV, very close to the RGM result of \(-81.2\) MeV by Kamimura et al. (1981).
- The deeper local energy minimum for the \(4^-\) state occurs at \(\beta_x = \beta_y = 1.9\) fm and \(\beta_z = 0.2\) fm, with energy \(-76.9\) MeV.
- The squared overlap between the single optimal THSR wave function and the Brink-GCM wave function is 96% for the \(3^-\) state and 92% for the \(4^-\) state.
- The squared overlap between the single optimal Brink wave function and the Brink-GCM wave function is only 78% for the \(3^-\) state and 72% for the \(4^-\) state.
- The energies obtained in the container model for the \(3^-\) and \(4^-\) states are 2.5 MeV deeper than those obtained from the Brink model.
- The squared overlap between the two local-minimum wave functions (prolate and oblate) is about 98% for both the \(3^-\) and \(4^-\) states.

## Context

This work extends the container model, previously applied to positive-parity states and the two-cluster \(\alpha+^{16}\)O system (Zhou et al., 2013, 2014), to negative-parity states of three-alpha clusters in \(^{12}\)C. It contradicts the traditional rigid triangular picture advocated by the algebraic cluster model of Bijker et al. (2002, 2017) and instead supports a nonlocalized, gas-like clustering similar to the Hoyle state description. The results enable future study of the more complicated \(1^-\) and \(5^-\) states, which could not be reproduced with the present single THSR wave function and may require superposition of multiple THSR configurations or inclusion of two-alpha correlations.
