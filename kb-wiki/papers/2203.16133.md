# Chiral asteroseismology: seismic oscillations caused by chiral transport in neutron stars and supernovae

> We study the novel asteroseismology of the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) of the quark number density in relativistic quark matter inside neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae and the chiral vortical wave (CVW) of the neutrino number density in relativistic neutrino matter at the core of supernovae. We call the oscillation modes for these chiral waves the chiral magnetic mode (CM-mode) and chiral vortical mode (CV-mode), respectively. We derive the dispersion relations of these new modes in the presence of the chirality flipping due to the finite quark mass and diffusion. We then estimate the possible frequencies of these modes and amplitudes of the resulting gravitational waves. In particular, since the CM-mode can exist only in quark matter with nearly gapless quarks (such as the two-flavor color superconductivity) for a sufficiently strong magnetic field, corresponding gravitational waves provide a new possible probe for such quark matter and the magnetic field in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that chiral magnetic waves (CMW) in quark matter and chiral vortical waves (CVW) in neutrino matter inside neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae can produce new seismic oscillation modes (CM-mode and CV-mode) that generate detectable gravitational waves, but only under specific conditions: the CM-mode requires quark matter with nearly gapless quarks (such as in the two-flavor color superconductivity phase) and a sufficiently strong magnetic field.

## Method

The authors derive dispersion relations for the CM-mode and CV-mode by solving linearized continuity equations for quark number and chiral charge densities in curved spacetime, incorporating chirality flipping due to finite quark mass and diffusion. They compute the chirality flipping rate using the Boltzmann equation with quark-quark scattering amplitudes in the high-density effective theory near the Fermi surface, and estimate the resulting gravitational wave frequencies and amplitudes using the Cowling approximation.

## Key numbers

- The metric functions for neutron stars or supernovae satisfy λ ∼ ρ ∼ GM/R ∼ 10⁻¹, so e^(λ-ρ) ∼ 1
- The quark number susceptibility in the ideal gas approximation is χ = 2N_c(μ̄²/π² + T²/3)
- The longitudinal chirality flipping rate from quark-quark scattering is Γ_flip,L ∼ F_c × 11(π+2)/(48π) × (α_s² m²)/(μ̄² q_s) × T², where q_s² = N_f g_s² μ̄²/π² is the Debye screening mass
- The frequency range for the CM-mode satisfies Γ_flip/(4π) ≪ f_CM ≪ 3V_CM²/(2πτ), where V_CM = e^(λ-ρ) × (N_c eB)/(6π² χ)
- The Cowling approximation estimates frequencies within at least 20% accuracy compared to full numerical results (citing Yoshida (1997) and Sotani (2020))

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral transport phenomena, previously mainly investigated in quark-gluon plasmas at RHIC and LHC (Kharzeev, 2015), to astrophysical settings of neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae. It introduces new oscillation modes (CM-mode and CV-mode) to the conventional asteroseismology classification of f-mode, p-mode, g-mode, r-mode, and w-mode (Kokkotas, 1999; Andersson, 2000). The detection of gravitational waves from these modes would provide a new probe for quark matter phases (such as the 2SC phase) and magnetic field strengths in compact stars, enabling observational tests of interior composition that complement existing asteroseismic methods.
