# APFEL: A PDF Evolution Library with QED corrections

> Quantum electrodynamics and electroweak corrections are important ingredients for many theoretical predictions at the LHC. This paper documents APFEL, a new PDF evolution package that allows for the first time to perform DGLAP evolution up to NNLO in QCD and to LO in QED, in the variable-flavor-number scheme and with either pole or MSbar heavy quark masses. APFEL consistently accounts for the QED corrections to the evolution of quark and gluon PDFs and for the contribution from the photon PDF in the proton. The coupled QCD+QED equations are solved in x-space by means of higher order interpolation, followed by Runge-Kutta solution of the resulting discretized evolution equations. APFEL is based on an innovative and flexible methodology for the sequential solution of the QCD and QED evolution equations and their combination. In addition to PDF evolution, APFEL provides a module that computes Deep-Inelastic Scattering structure functions in the FONLL general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme up to O($α_s^2$). All the functionalities of APFEL can be accessed via a Graphical User Interface, supplemented with a variety of plotting tools for PDFs, parton luminosities and structure functions. Written in Fortran 77, APFEL can also be used via the C/C++ and Python interfaces, and is publicly available from the HepForge repository.

## Key claim
APFEL is the first public PDF evolution library that enables combined QCD+QED DGLAP evolution up to NNLO in QCD and LO in QED, but the paper acknowledges that the three formally equivalent solution methods (QCED, QECD, QavD) for the coupled equations can produce differences in the photon PDF of up to 70% at small and large x, indicating that the QED evolution carries inherent theoretical uncertainties from subleading terms.

## Method
The coupled QCD+QED DGLAP evolution equations are solved in x-space by representing parton distributions on a grid in x, discretizing the evolution equations, and using higher-order interpolation followed by Runge-Kutta numerical integration. APFEL combines QCD and QED evolution operators sequentially rather than at the level of splitting functions, introducing separate factorization scales for QCD and QED that can vary independently. The code also provides a module computing Deep-Inelastic Scattering structure functions in the FONLL general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme up to O(α_s²).

## Key numbers
- PDF evolution tables generated in approximately 0.5 seconds for combined QCD+QED evolution on a 2.66 GHz Intel Core 2 Duo processor
- Memory required to execute: less than 2 MB
- QCD-only evolution benchmarking against HOPPET shows agreement at most approximately 0.02% at large x for NNLO evolution from Q₀² = 2 GeV² to Q² = 10⁴ GeV²
- Photon PDF differences between the QavD solution and the QCED/QECD solutions can reach up to 70% at small and large x when using a single evolution step
- When using 100 logarithmically spaced steps, differences between the three solution methods for the photon PDF reduce to at most 2%
- DIS structure function benchmarking against FONLLdis shows relative differences below 1% for F₂ᶜ and at most 1.3% for F₂ˡ at Q² = 10 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the capabilities of existing PDF evolution codes such as HOPPET (which handles QCD only) and partonevolution (which handles QCD+QED but in fixed-flavor-number scheme) by providing the first public code with combined QCD+QED evolution in the variable-flavor-number scheme with either pole or MSbar heavy quark masses. The code enables future PDF fitting efforts that consistently include QED effects, such as those needed for precision LHC predictions, and the authors identify that reducing the theoretical uncertainties in the photon PDF evolution will require including higher-order QED corrections and mixed O(α α_s) splitting functions.
