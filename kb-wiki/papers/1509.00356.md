# The Quarkyonic Star

> We discuss theoretical scenarios on crossover between nuclear matter (NM) and quark matter (QM). We classify various possibilities into three major scenarios according to the onset of diquark degrees of freedom that characterizes color-superconducting (CSC) states. In the conventional scenario NM occurs at the liquid-gas (or liquid-vacuum at zero temperature) phase transition and QM occurs next, after which CSC eventually appears. With the effect of strong correlation, the BEC-BCS scenario implies that CSC occurs next to NM and QM comes last in the BCS regime. We adopt the quarkyonic scenario in which NM, QM, and CSC are theoretically indistinguishable and thus these names refer to not distinct states but relevant descriptions of the same physical system. Based on this idea we propose a natural scheme to interpolate NM near normal nuclear density and CSC with vector coupling at high baryon density. We finally discuss the mass-radius relation of the neutron star and constraints on parameters in the proposed scheme.

## Key claim

The authors propose that nuclear matter, quark matter, and color-superconducting (CSC) states are theoretically indistinguishable in an intermediate density regime, meaning these labels refer not to distinct phases but to different descriptions of the same physical system; this "quarkyonic" scenario is presented as the most realistic alternative to conventional first-order transitions or BEC-BCS crossover scenarios.

## Method

The authors classify three scenarios for the crossover between nuclear matter and quark matter based on the onset of diquark degrees of freedom, then adopt the quarkyonic scenario. They use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with vector and diquark interactions, treating the vector coupling as a density-dependent parameter fitted to reproduce the Akmal-Pandaripande-Ravenhall (APR) nuclear equation of state at low densities and then interpolating to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at high densities. The resulting equation of state is used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}$
- Fitting range for the vector coupling: $\mu_B = (1.02 \sim 1.20)\ \text{GeV}$ or $n_B \simeq (1.5 \sim 3.5) n_0$ for the wide fit; $\mu_B = (1.02 \sim 1.035)\ \text{GeV}$ or $n_B \simeq (1.5 \sim 2) n_0$ for the narrow fit
- Diquark coupling values examined: $H = 1.5 G_s$ and $H = 1.6 G_s$, where $G_s \Lambda^2 = 3.67$ with $\Lambda = 631.4\ \text{MeV}$
- Vector coupling offset parameter $d = 0.4$ is required to achieve neutron star masses above $2 M_\odot$; values $d \lesssim 0.3$ make such masses impossible
- First-order phase transition between the 2SC and CFL phases occurs at $\mu_B = 1.31\ \text{GeV}$ for $H = 1.5 G_s$
- The vector coupling relaxes to $\gv \sim 0.5 G_s$ at large $\mu_B$
- The chiral phase transition is a smooth crossover due to the large vector coupling

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept first proposed by McLerran (2007) and further developed by Fukushima (2013), applying it to neutron star phenomenology. It contrasts with conventional approaches that assume first-order phase transitions between nuclear and quark matter, and with earlier interpolation studies by Masuda (2012, 2015), Alvarez-Castillo (2013), Hell (2014), and Kojo (2014, 2015) that did not manifestly treat microscopic dynamics in the interpolated domain. The paper also builds on the quark-hadron continuity conjecture connecting superfluid nuclear matter to the CFL phase, as discussed by Schafer (1998), Alford (1999), Fukushima (2004), and Hatsuda (2006). The results enable future work to address how strangeness changes in the crossover domain and to constrain non-perturbative gluon contributions at densities up to $n_B \sim 10 n_0$ using the $2 M_\odot$ neutron star constraint.
