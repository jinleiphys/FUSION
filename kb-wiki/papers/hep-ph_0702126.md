# Xi and Omega baryons in the Skyrme model

> The mass spectrum and magnetic moments of Ξand Ωbaryon resonances are investigated in the bound state approach of the Skyrme model. The empirical hyperon spectrum shows that several hyperon resonances share a pattern of (approximately) equal mass spacings between the states of same spin but of opposite parity. It is found that this pattern can be explained mostly by the energy difference between the P-wave and S-wave kaons bound to the soliton. Although one cannot exclude the possibility that these states can be described as pion-hyperon resonances, the present approach predicts that Ξ(1620) and Ξ(1690) have J^P = 12^-, while Ξ(1950) has J^P = 12^+. The differences with the quark model predictions for the Ξand Ωbaryon spectrum are pointed out. Several relations for the masses and magnetic moments of those resonances are also obtained.

## Key claim

The bound-state Skyrme model can explain the pattern of approximately equal mass spacings between hyperon resonances of same spin but opposite parity (e.g., 289 MeV between Λ(1116) and Λ(1405), 290 MeV between Ξ(1530) and Ξ(1820)) as arising from the energy difference between P-wave and S-wave kaons bound to the soliton, though the author notes that one cannot exclude the possibility that these states could instead be described as pion-hyperon resonances.

## Method

The author applies the bound-state approach of the Skyrme model, where strange hyperons are described as bound states of a soliton with K/K* mesons, without working with a specific Lagrangian. Instead, an improved mass formula is developed that introduces separate parameters for the hyperfine splitting constant c and the squared isospin operator expectation value \(\bar{c}\), and these mass parameters are fitted to known baryon masses (N, Δ, Λ(1116), Σ(1385), Ξ(1318), Λ(1405), Ξ(1820), and Ξ(2120)) to predict the spectrum of Ξ and Ω resonances.

## Key numbers

- Soliton mass \(M_{\rm sol} = 866\) MeV and moment of inertia \(\mathcal{I} = 1.01\) fm, fitted from nucleon (939 MeV) and Δ (1232 MeV) masses
- P-wave kaon parameters: \(\omega_1 = 211\) MeV, \(c_1 = 0.754\), \(\bar{c}_1 = 0.532\), fitted from Λ(1116), Σ(1385), and Ξ(1318)
- S-wave kaon parameters: \(\omega_2 = 479\) MeV, \(c_2 = 0.641\), \(\bar{c}_2 = 0.821\), fitted from Λ(1405), Ξ(1820), and Ξ(2120)
- Predicted masses: \(\Xi_{1/2^-,1}\) at 1658 MeV (assigned to Ξ(1690)?), \(\Xi_{1/2^-,2}\) at 1616 MeV (assigned to Ξ(1620)?), \(\Xi_{1/2^+,1}\) at 1932 MeV (assigned to Ξ(1950)?), \(\Xi_{3/2^+,1}\) at 2120 MeV (assigned to Ξ(2120)?)
- Predicted Ω masses: \(\Omega_{3/2^+,0}\) at 1694 MeV (assigned to Ω(1672)), \(\Omega_{3/2^-,1}\) at 1978 MeV, \(\Omega_{1/2^+,1}\) at 2140 MeV, \(\Omega_{3/2^+,1}\) at 2282 MeV (assigned to Ω(2250)?), \(\Omega_{3/2^-,2}\) at 2604 MeV

## Context

This work extends the bound-state Skyrme model approach of Callan and Klebanov (1985) and the subsequent successful description of ground-state octet and decuplet hyperons by Callan, Hornbostel, and Kleiman (1988) and Scoccola et al. (1989). It contrasts with the very different predictions of the non-relativistic quark model of Chao, Isgur, and Karl (1981), the relativized quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986), the one-boson exchange model of Glozman and Riska (1996), the large N_c QCD analysis, and the algebraic model of Bijker et al. (2000), all of which give conflicting spectra for excited Ξ and Ω states. The results enable future experimental searches for Ξ and Ω resonances with specific spin-parity assignments, particularly suggesting that Ξ(1620) and Ξ(1690) have \(J^P = \frac12^-\) while Ξ(1950) has \(J^P = \frac12^+\).
