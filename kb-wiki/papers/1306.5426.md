# D-meson propagation in hot dense matter

> The drag force and diffusion coefficients for a D meson are calculated in hot dense matter composed of light mesons and baryons, such as it is formed in heavy-ion collisions. We use a unitarized approach based on effective models for the interaction of a D meson with hadrons, which are compatible with chiral and heavy quark symmetries. We study the propagation of the D meson in the hadron matter in two distinct cases. On the one hand, we analyze the propagation of D mesons in matter at vanishing baryochemical potential, which is relevant for high-energetic collisions at LHC or RHIC. On the other hand, we show the propagation of D mesons in the hadronic medium following isentropic trajectories, appropiate at FAIR and NICA heavy-ion experiments. We find a negligible baryon contribution to the transport coefficients at vanishing chemical potential. However at finite baryochemical potential we obtain a large correction to the transport coefficients with the inclusion of nucleons and Delta baryons. The relaxation time for D mesons is reduced a factor 2-3 in the later case, producing a more thermalized D meson-spectrum for FAIR physics than for the typical LHC energies. We finally present results for the spatial diffusion coefficient of a D meson in hadronic matter and the possible existence of a minimum near the phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma at zero and finite baryochemical potential.

## Key claim

The drag force and diffusion coefficients for D mesons in hadronic matter are calculated using unitarized effective models consistent with chiral and heavy-quark symmetries; the baryon contribution to transport coefficients is negligible at zero baryochemical potential (relevant for LHC/RHIC) but becomes large at finite baryochemical potential (relevant for FAIR/NICA), reducing the relaxation time by a factor of 2-3.

## Method

The authors compute the drag force F(p) and momentum-space diffusion coefficients Γ₀(p) and Γ₁(p) for D mesons by solving the Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann equation in the limit where the D meson mass is much larger than the bath particles. The scattering amplitudes for D mesons with light mesons (π, K, K̄, η) are obtained from a chiral Lagrangian at leading and next-to-leading order, unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation; for D mesons with baryons (nucleons and Δ baryons), two unitarized coupled-channel schemes are used: an SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa model and an SU(6)×HQSS Weinberg-Tomozawa scheme. The transport coefficients are evaluated in the static limit (p = 100 MeV) for two cases: vanishing baryochemical potential (μ_B = 0) and isentropic trajectories at finite μ_B.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time for D mesons is reduced by a factor of 2-3 when baryons are included at finite baryochemical potential compared to the μ_B = 0 case.
- The D-meson momentum used for the static-limit calculations is p = 100 MeV.
- The low-energy constants for the D-meson–light-meson interaction are fixed to h₃ = 5.5 and h₅ = -0.45 GeV⁻².
- The Goldstone boson decay constant in the chiral limit is taken as f_π = 93 MeV.
- The SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa model uses a weak decay constant f = 1.15 f_π.
- The NLO chiral low-energy constant h₁ is fixed to -0.41 using the D–D_s mass difference.
- The temperature range considered extends up to T = 140 MeV, close to the transition temperature.

## Context

This work extends the results of Abreu (2011), who calculated D-meson transport coefficients with pions only, by including the complete set of pseudo-Goldstone bosons (kaons, antikaons, η mesons) and baryonic degrees of freedom (nucleons and Δ baryons). It contrasts with the approach of He (2011), which used parametrized interactions valid only near resonances, and with Ghosh (2011), whose leading-order effective Lagrangians produced unphysically large transport coefficients due to unitarity violation. The results enable predictions for D-meson thermalization and spatial diffusion in heavy-ion collisions across different beam energies, from LHC/RHIC (μ_B = 0) to FAIR/NICA (finite μ_B), and allow investigation of a possible minimum in the spatial diffusion coefficient near the deconfinement phase transition.
