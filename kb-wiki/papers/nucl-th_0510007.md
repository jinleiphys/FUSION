# Unintegrated parton distributions and pion production in pp collisions at RHIC's energies

> We compare results of $2 \to 1$ $k_t$-factorization approach with Kwieciński unintegrated parton distributions and the standard collinear factorization approach at RHIC and slightly smaller energies. Our approach contains only one free parameter responsible for internal parton motion in nucleons. In contrast to recent works in the literature our $k_t$-factorization approach includes also quark degrees of freedom in addition to purely gluonic terms. Both mid and forward rapidity regions are considered. We discuss uncertainties due to fragmentation functions. In general, the $k_t$-factorization approach gives a better description of the $p_t \sim$ 1 -- 4 GeV region both at mid and forward rapidity regions. Our approach leads to asymmetry in the production of $π^+$ and $π^-$, very similar to the one observed very recently by the BRAHMS collaboration.

# Unintegrated parton distributions and pion production in pp collisions at RHIC's energies

## Key claim

The $k_t$-factorization approach using Kwieciński unintegrated parton distributions (UPDF) provides a better description of pion production in pp collisions at RHIC energies (W = 200 GeV) than standard collinear factorization, particularly in the intermediate transverse momentum region $p_t \sim$ 1–4 GeV, but only when quark degrees of freedom are included alongside gluonic terms. The approach contains exactly one free parameter ($b_0$) responsible for internal parton motion in nucleons, and the description is sensitive to the choice of fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive pion cross sections using a $2 \to 1$ $k_t$-factorization formalism with Kwieciński UPDF, including three leading-order diagrams: $gg \to g$, $q_f g \to q_f$, and $g q_f \to q_f$. Parton-level cross sections are convolved with phenomenological fragmentation functions (BKK95, Kretzer00, AKK05) to obtain hadron-level distributions, using a Gaussian nonperturbative form factor $F^{np}(b) = \exp(-b^2/4b_0^2)$ in impact-parameter space. Results are compared to PHENIX, BRAHMS, STAR, and UA1 data at mid and forward rapidities.

## Key numbers

- The optimal value of the nonperturbative parameter is $b_0 = 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$, taken from prior gauge boson production studies.
- At W = 200 GeV and $\eta = 0$, the $k_t$-factorization approach describes PHENIX $\pi^0$ data well across a broad $p_t$ range; the $gg \to g$ contribution dominates up to $p_t \sim 3.5$ GeV at $\eta = 0$.
- At $\eta = 2.2$, the $q_f g \to q_f$ contribution exceeds the $gg \to g$ contribution already at $p_t \sim 1.5$ GeV; at $\eta = 3.2$, this occurs above $p_t \sim 1$ GeV.
- The standard collinear factorization approach underestimates STAR $\pi^0$ data at $\eta = 3.8$ by a factor of about 3; the $k_t$-factorization approach is almost consistent with the data, especially at smaller pion energies.
- The collinear approach gives results lower than experimental data by a factor of 3–7 depending on particle rapidity.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of Kwieciński UPDF to SPS pion production (Czech and Szczurek, 2005) up to RHIC energy W = 200 GeV, and contrasts with earlier $k_t$-factorization studies (Kharzeev and Levin, 2001; Szczurek, 2003) that included only gluon degrees of freedom. The inclusion of quark degrees of freedom produces a $\pi^+ - \pi^-$ asymmetry consistent with preliminary BRAHMS data, which the authors argue puts into question recent nuclear color glass condensate calculations based solely on gluonic effects. The approach enables future extraction of flavour-dependent fragmentation functions from forward-region asymmetry measurements.
