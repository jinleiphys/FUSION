# The contribution of chiral three-body forces to the monopole component of the effective shell-model Hamiltonian

> We present a study of the role played by realistic three-body forces in providing a reliable monopole component of the effective shell-model Hamiltonian. To this end, starting from a nuclear potential built up within the chiral perturbation theory, we derive effective shell-model Hamiltonians with and without the contribution of the three-body potential and compare the results of shell-model calculations with a set of observables that evidence shell-evolution properties. The testing ground of our investigation are nuclei belonging to fp shell, since the shell evolution towards shell closures in 48Ca and 56Ni provides a paradigm for shell-model Hamiltonians. Our analysis shows that only by including contributions of the three-body force the monopole component of the effective shell-model Hamiltonian is then able to reproduce the experimental shell evolution towards and beyond the closure at N=28.

## Key claim

The monopole component of the effective shell-model Hamiltonian derived from chiral two-nucleon forces alone cannot reproduce the observed shell evolution toward and beyond the N=28 closure in the fp shell; only by including contributions from a chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) does the monopole component yield the correct shell-closure properties, as demonstrated by comparing Hamiltonians with and without 3NF contributions while keeping the same single-particle energies.

## Method

The authors derived effective shell-model Hamiltonians for the fp-shell model space (0f1p orbitals outside 40Ca) starting from a chiral N3LO two-nucleon force (Entem and Machleidt) and a chiral N2LO three-nucleon force, without any renormalization of high-momentum components. The effective Hamiltonians were obtained via the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, including two-body diagrams up to third order in the 2NF and first-order one- and two-body diagrams from the 3NF, plus density-dependent two-body contributions from second-order three-body correlation diagrams summed over partially-filled model-space orbitals. Two classes of Hamiltonians were derived: H_eff^2NF (2NF vertices only) and H_eff^3NF (including 3NF contributions), and a third hybrid Hamiltonian H_eff^mon was constructed by combining the monopole component of H_eff^3NF with the multipole components of H_eff^2NF.

## Key numbers

- The 3NF contribution enlarges the neutron 0f7/2 - 1p3/2 gap in calcium isotopes at N=28 by 0.7 MeV compared to the 2NF-only calculation.
- The proton 0f5/2 - 0f7/2 ESPE separation in nickel isotopes at N=28 is about 5.8 MeV with H_eff^2NF and about 8.6 MeV with H_eff^3NF.
- The gap between proton ESPEs of 0f5/2 and 1p3/2 orbitals in nickel at N=28 is 0.8 MeV with 2NF-only and 1.6 MeV when 3NF contributions are included.
- The harmonic-oscillator parameter used was hbar-omega = 11 MeV, with intermediate states included up to N_max = 18 in the perturbative expansion.
- The single-particle energy of the 0f7/2 orbital was fixed at -1.1 MeV for protons and -8.4 MeV for neutrons, consistent with experimental values from 41Sc and 41Ca.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of p-shell nuclei (Coraggio, 2018), where they found that 3NF contributions preserve the spin-orbit splitting of 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 orbitals with mass number, while 2NF-only calculations caused the splitting to drop and become negative around A=8. The present study applies the same methodology to the fp shell, where the shell evolution toward doubly-magic 48Ca and 56Ni provides a paradigm for testing shell-model Hamiltonians. The results support earlier findings by Schwenk and coworkers (Otsuka, 2010; Holt, 2012, 2013, 2014; Hebeler, 2015; Simonis, 2016, 2017) that three-body forces are needed to reproduce experimental ground-state and excitation energies in oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. The work also connects to Zuker's earlier inference (Zuker, 2003) that the need to modify the monopole component of realistic Hamiltonians traces back to the lack of three-nucleon forces. The derived Hamiltonians enable future shell-model calculations for fp-shell nuclei with a consistent treatment of three-body forces from chiral effective field theory.
