# Three-body monopole corrections to the realistic interactions

## Key claim
A very simple three-body monopole term, linear in the total number of particles, can solve essentially all spectroscopic problems in the p, sd, and pf shells that had previously been assumed to require drastic revisions of the realistic two-body potentials. The claim is hedged by the fact that the full exploration of all contributing terms has already been done and produced excellent results, but at the time the authors did not know how to handle the large three-body contributions.

## Method
The author introduces a three-body monopole Hamiltonian term of the form \(n_{st}(n-2)\) and \(T_{st}(n-2)\), where two-body centroids are modulated by the total number of particles \(n\). This correction is applied to realistic G-matrix interactions (KLS, KB, BonnC) in the p, sd, and pf shells, and the resulting spectra are compared to experimental data and to phenomenological fits such as Cohen-Kurath (CK) and Wildenthal's USD. The key parameter \(\kappa\) is varied linearly with particle number to test the three-body mechanism.

## Key numbers
- In the p shell, a \(\kappa = 1.1\) correction to KLS G-matrices produces spectra in \(^{10}\)B that are practically as good as the Cohen-Kurath fit, except for the second \(J=3\) level.
- In the sd shell, KB and BC interactions with \(\kappa = 0.9\) and 0.85 corrections respectively produce good spectra for \(^{22}\)Na, while a constant \(\kappa\) is ruled out because the local value decreases to 0.60 for KB and 0.55 for BC in \(^{27}\)Si, \(^{28}\)Si, and \(^{29}\)Si.
- In the pf shell, KB1 produces a gap at \(^{56}\)Ni of 7.5 MeV against the observed 6.3 MeV; the first \(BE2(2 \rightarrow 0)\) transition in \(^{58}\)Ni falls short of the observed 140 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) by a factor of approximately 0.4.
- For \(^{48}\)Cr backbending, BC with \(\kappa = 0.43\) produces a pattern practically as good as KB3, while \(\kappa = 0.28\) (the correct value around \(A=56\)) destroys agreement with experiment.

## Context
This work extends the monopole correction approach first proposed by Poves (1976) for the Kuo-Brown interaction in the pf shell, and builds on the observation by Abzouzi et al. (1991) that three-body contributions were large and important but not understood at the time. The paper directly addresses the failure of two-body-only realistic interactions to reproduce the \(^{10}\)B spectrum in no-core shell model calculations by Navratil and Ormand (2002), and the long-standing problem of the \(^{22}\)Na spectrum in the sd shell that led to the phenomenological USD fit by Wildenthal (1984). The results enable future work to characterize three-body potentials from shell model studies, complementing exact Green's function Monte Carlo and no-core calculations that have recently become available.
