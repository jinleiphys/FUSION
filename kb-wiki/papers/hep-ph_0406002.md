# Gauge dependence of the fermion quasiparticle poles in hot gauge theories

> The gauge dependence of the complex fermion quasiparticle poles corresponding to soft collective excitations is studied in hot gauge theories at one-loop order and next-to-leading order in the high-temperature expansion, with a view towards going beyond the leading order hard thermal loops and resummations thereof. We find that for collective excitations of momenta k ~ eT the dispersion relations are gauge independent, but the corresponding damping rates are gauge dependent. For k<<eT and in k \to 0 limit, both the dispersion relations and the damping rates are found to be gauge dependent. The gauge dependence of the position of the complex quasiparticle poles signals the need for resummation. Possible cancellation of the leading gauge dependence at two-loop order in the case of QED is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at next-to-leading order in the high-temperature expansion, the dispersion relations for soft fermion collective excitations with momenta \(k\sim eT\) remain gauge independent, but the corresponding damping rates are gauge dependent; for momenta \(k\ll eT\) and in the \(k\to 0\) limit, both the dispersion relations and damping rates become gauge dependent, signaling the need for resummation beyond leading-order hard thermal loops.

## Method

The author calculates the one-loop fermion self-energy in hot gauge theories (Abelian and non-Abelian) using the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism, working in general covariant gauges with gauge parameter \(\xi\). The self-energy is decomposed into gauge-independent (Feynman gauge) and gauge-dependent parts, and both the real and imaginary parts are evaluated analytically up to next-to-leading order in the high-temperature expansion for soft momenta \(k,\omega\ll T\). The complex quasiparticle poles are then determined from the Dyson-Schwinger equation by solving for the dispersion relations and damping rates in different momentum regimes.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order HTL effective thermal mass is \(m = eT/\sqrt{8}\), which is gauge independent.
- For momenta \(k\sim eT\), the next-to-leading order damping rates are of order \(\gamma \sim e^2 T\) (up to a logarithm of the gauge coupling), but gauge dependent.
- In the \(k\to 0\) limit, the one-loop effective mass at next-to-leading order satisfies \(m^2 = \frac{e^2 T^2}{8} + \xi\frac{e^2 m^2}{8\pi^2}\ln\frac{T}{m}\), giving a gauge-dependent correction of order \(e^4 T^2 \ln(1/e)\).
- The damping rate at rest \(\gamma(0)\) is of order \(e^2 T\) with a gauge-dependent contribution of the same order.
- The gauge-dependent part of the imaginary self-energy at next-to-leading order is \(\mathrm{Im}\Sigma^{(0)}_\xi \simeq (\xi-1)\frac{e^2 T}{16\pi}\) for \(k\ll eT\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order hard thermal loop (HTL) results of Klimov, Weldon, and others (1980s) by computing next-to-leading order corrections to the fermion self-energy and quasiparticle poles. It fills a gap in the literature where previous studies either performed numerical analysis for \(k\sim T\) (Peshier, 1990s) or considered limiting cases of \(eT\ll k\ll T\) and \(k=0\) (Mitra, 1999). The gauge dependence found at one-loop order motivates the need for resummation schemes such as the Braaten-Pisarski HTL resummation program or the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action approach (Arrizabalaga, Mottola, Carrington, 2002-2003), and the paper discusses plausible cancellation of gauge dependence at two-loop order in QED.
