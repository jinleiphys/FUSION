# Escape and Spreading Properties of Charge-Exchange Resonances in Bi 208

> The properties of charge-exchange excitations of ${}^ {208}$Pb with $ΔL = 0$, i.e., the isobaric analog and Gamow-Teller resonances, are studied within a self-consistent model making use of an effective force of the Skyrme type. The well-known isobaric analog case is used to assess the reliability of the model. The calculated properties of the Gamow-Teller resonance are compared with recent experimental measurements with the aim of better understanding the microscopic structure of this mode.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent model of charge-exchange resonances in 208Bi using Skyrme-type effective forces, claiming that the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) properties can be reasonably reproduced only when both escape and spreading widths are included, but the model overestimates the GTR peak energy and underestimates the total width compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for 208Pb using SIII and SGII Skyrme interactions, then constructed Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) states for proton particle-neutron hole configurations. They built an energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian that couples the TDA space to unbound continuum states (for escape width) and to doorway states formed by coupling 1p-1h excitations with collective vibrations of the 208Pb core (for spreading width), solving for complex eigenvalues and computing strength functions and branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- IAR centroid energy: 18.49 MeV (SIII) and 18.64 MeV (SGII), compared to experimental value of 18.8 MeV
- IAR total width: 152 keV (SIII) and 99 keV (SGII), compared to experimental value of 232 keV
- IAR exhausts 97% of T- strength with both interactions
- GTR mean energy: 21.11 MeV (SIII) and 22.43 MeV (SGII), compared to experimental value of 19.2 MeV
- GTR variance-derived width (Gaussian approximation): about 3 MeV for both interactions, compared to experimental value of 3.8 MeV
- GTR strength fraction: about 61% in 18-24 MeV range (SIII) and about 68% in 19.5-24.5 MeV range (SGII)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent model previously applied to the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in 208Pb by Colo et al. (1992) to charge-exchange modes, improving upon earlier calculations by Gia et al. (1989) that included only continuum coupling. The results are compared with recent experimental measurements at Osaka (Aki et al., 1994) and preliminary data from MSU (Gal et al., private communication), and the model enables future calculations of branching ratios for proton decay of the GTR that can be directly confronted with coincidence experiments.
