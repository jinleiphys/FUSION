# Breaking of N=8 magicity in 13Be

> Structure of $^{13}$Be was investigated with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The variation after spin and parity projections was performed. An unnatural parity $1/2^-$ state was suggested to be lower than $5/2^+$ state indicating that vanishing of the N=8 magic number occurs in $^{13}$Be. A low-lying $3/2^+$ state with a $2\hbarω$ configuration was also suggested. Developed cluster structures were found in the intruder states. Lowering mechanism of the intruder states was discussed in terms of molecular orbitals around a $2α$ core.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the N=8 magic number is broken in 13Be, with a low-lying 1/2- state appearing below the 5/2+ state, but this conclusion depends on the specific effective interaction used (set 1 with strong spin-orbit force), while a weaker spin-orbit interaction (set 2) fails to reproduce the inversion and yields the normal shell-model ordering.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after spin and parity projection (VAP) to calculate energy levels and intrinsic structures of 13Be. They also constructed a 12Be+n model by adding a Gaussian wave packet neutron to AMD wave functions of 12Be core states, allowing improved description of the valence neutron's spatial extension. Single-particle energies and molecular orbital configurations were analyzed from the intrinsic AMD wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The set (1) interaction (Majorana parameter m=0.65, spin-orbit strengths u_I=-u_II=3700 MeV) gives 1/2- and 3/2+ states almost degenerate at the lowest energy, with a 5/2+ state above them in 13Be
- The set (2) interaction (weaker spin-orbit forces u_I=-u_II=2500 MeV) gives the 5/2+ state as the lowest, with 1/2- and 3/2+ states higher
- The 12Be+n model calculation with set (1) interaction gives energies about a few MeV lower than AMD+VAP results, with the 1/2- state being the lowest
- The theoretical 12Be-n threshold energy is -61.3 MeV
- The width parameter nu=0.17 fm^{-2} was used for Gaussian wave packets

## Context

This work extends previous AMD+VAP studies of 10Be, 11Be, and 12Be by Kanada-En'yo and collaborators, which successfully described parity inversion and cluster structures in those nuclei. The results support the experimental claim by Kondo (2010) of a low-lying 1/2- state in 13Be, contradicting the analysis by Fortune (2010). The paper provides a systematic molecular orbital description of neutron-rich Be isotopes, showing that the sigma_{1/2} orbital lowering in developed 2-alpha cluster structures explains the N=8 shell breaking across the isotopic chain.
