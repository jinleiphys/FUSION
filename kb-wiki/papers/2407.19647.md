# Correlation between alpha-decay half-lives and symmetry energy

> We study the alpha-decay half-lives of 84 <= Z <= 92 even-even nuclei in the semiclassical WKB approximation framework using the density-dependent cluster model and the density distribution described by various Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) models. Main goal of this work is to find a correlation between the alpha-decay half-lives and the stiffness of the symmtery energy. Parameters of KIDS model are determined to reproduce the nuclear data (energy and charge radii of 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb) and the neutron star observations including constraints by NICER, tidal deformability from GW170817, and the maximum mass limit of neutron star observations. We use the KIDS models (A-D) which have distinctive values for the parameters controlling the stiffness of the nuclear matter equation of state. We find that the alpha-decay half-lives increase systematically from KIDS-A model to KIDS-D model. This implies a correlation between the alpha-decay half-lives and the symmetry energy. We find that the correlation can be understood from the dependence of the particle distribution in the core and the surface region on the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a systematic correlation between alpha-decay half-lives and the stiffness of the symmetry energy, specifically that softer symmetry energy (smaller J and L parameters) leads to longer alpha-decay half-lives in neutron-rich heavy nuclei, with the effect most pronounced for nuclei with mass numbers up to A=224.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha-decay half-lives for 84 ≤ Z ≤ 92 even-even nuclei using the semiclassical WKB approximation within the density-dependent cluster model. They employed four KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) models (A-D) with distinct symmetry energy parameters, where the nuclear density distributions were expanded in Legendre polynomials to account for axial deformations. The preformation factor was determined using the cluster-formation model, and the global quantum number G was adjusted to G=24 for 82 < N_par ≤ 126 and G=26 for N_par > 126 to minimize deviation from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.235 fm for KIDS-A and 0.168 fm for KIDS-D, giving a ratio of 0.71
- Ratio of half-lives between KIDS-D and KIDS-A models: 1.2 to 1.5 for most nuclei, except 198Rn and 226U
- Root-mean-square deviation σ of half-lives: minimized with G values (24, 26), with average σ slightly smaller than for G=(26, 28)
- KIDS model parameters: KIDS-A (K0=230 MeV, J=33 MeV, L=66 MeV, Ksym=-139.5 MeV), KIDS-B (K0=240 MeV, J=32 MeV, L=58 MeV, Ksym=-162.1 MeV), KIDS-C (K0=250 MeV, J=31 MeV, L=58 MeV, Ksym=-91.5 MeV), KIDS-D (K0=260 MeV, J=30 MeV, L=47 MeV, Ksym=-134.5 MeV)
- Calculated half-lives fall within range [-0.5, 0.5] in log10 scale relative to experimental values (factors between 1/3 and 3), except 232Th which shows overestimation by factor larger than 10

## Context

This work extends previous studies using Skyrme energy density functionals by Seif (2018) that explored the relationship between symmetry energy and alpha-decay half-lives, and builds on the KIDS model framework developed by Gil and collaborators (2020, 2021, 2023). The results enable using alpha-decay half-lives as an additional laboratory observable, alongside neutron skin thickness measurements, to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy in neutron-rich nuclei. The finding that N=142 shows reduced sensitivity to symmetry energy warrants further investigation into the nuclear structure of heavy neutron-rich nuclei.
