# High energy electroproduction off complex nuclei

> We investigate hadron attenuation in deep-inelastic lepton scattering off complex nuclei in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment. Our transport theoretical simulations reveal strong prehadronic final state interactions of the reaction products with the surrounding nuclear medium early after the initial photon-nucleon interaction has taken place. In this work we compare our model results with the measured hadron multiplicity ratios for a Kr target at HERMES and provide an extended discussion of the double-hadron attenuation recently observed at HERMES.

## Key claim
The paper argues that hadron attenuation in deep-inelastic lepton scattering off complex nuclei is dominated by prehadronic final state interactions (FSI) of color-neutral prehadrons produced early after the initial photon-nucleon interaction, rather than by partonic energy loss alone. The authors hedge that their model, while achieving good agreement with most HERMES data, underestimates the Cronin-like increase of high-transverse-momentum hadrons, suggesting additional partonic effects may be present.

## Method
The authors split the lepton-nucleus reaction into two stages: first, the virtual photon interacts with a bound nucleon, producing a prehadronic final state via the Pythia and Fritiof event generators; second, these prehadrons are propagated through the nucleus using a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model that includes coupled-channel elastic and inelastic collisions. Prehadrons are assumed to interact with reduced cross sections proportional to the number of leading quarks they contain, and a universal formation proper time of 0.5 fm/c is used before they acquire full hadronic cross sections. The model accounts for nuclear shadowing via quantum mechanical coherence length effects and includes Fermi motion, binding energies, and Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers
- Formation proper time: \(\tau_f = 0.5\) fm/c in the rest frame of each hadron, used universally for all hadron species.
- Multiplicity ratio \(R_M^h\) for pions, kaons, and (anti-)protons on a \(^{84}\)Kr target at HERMES (27.6 GeV positron beam) shown as a function of \(z_h = E_h/\nu\) and photon energy \(\nu\); the ratio increases with \(\nu\) and decreases with \(z_h\).
- For charged hadrons on \(^{84}\)Kr, the multiplicity ratio shows a strong increase at transverse momenta above 1 GeV (Cronin effect), which the model does not reproduce.
- Double-hadron attenuation ratio \(R_2(z_2)\) predicted for \(^{14}\)N, \(^{84}\)Kr, and \(^{131}\)Xe targets; the ratio is similar for Kr and Xe, while single-hadron attenuation is larger for Xe than for Kr.
- The model's coupled-channel treatment yields quantitative agreement with experimental double-hadron data except for the last data point at \(z_2 = 0.5\) on Kr, where \(R_2 \approx 1\) cannot be explained.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BUU-based approach (Falter et al., 2004; Falter et al., 2003) that incorporated coherence length effects in photon-nucleus reactions, and contrasts with partonic energy-loss models (Arleo and Wang, 2004) that assume hadronization occurs far outside the nucleus. The paper also provides a more complete coupled-channel treatment compared to purely absorptive models (Accardi et al., 2003; Kopeliovich et al., 2004) that neglect particle production in final state interactions. The results enable improved interpretation of hadron formation lengths for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and provide a framework for future studies at JLab, EMC, and EIC energies.
