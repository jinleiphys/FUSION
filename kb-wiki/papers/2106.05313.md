# Impact of the PSR J0740+6620 radius constraint on the properties of high-density matter

> X-ray pulse profile modeling of PSR J0740+6620, the most massive known pulsar, with data from the NICER and XMM-Newton observatories recently led to a measurement of its radius. We investigate this measurement's implications for the neutron star equation of state (EoS), employing a nonparametric EoS model based on Gaussian processes and combining information from other x-ray, radio and gravitational-wave observations of neutron stars. Our analysis mildly disfavors EoSs that support a disconnected hybrid star branch in the mass-radius relation, a proxy for strong phase transitions, with a Bayes factor of $6.9$. For such EoSs, the transition mass from the hadronic to the hybrid branch is constrained to lie outside ($1,2$) $M_{\odot}$. We also find that the conformal sound-speed bound is violated inside neutron star cores, which implies that the core matter is strongly interacting. The squared sound speed reaches a maximum of $0.75^{+0.25}_{-0.24}\, c^2$ at $3.60^{+2.25}_{-1.89}$ times nuclear saturation density at 90% credibility. Since all but the gravitational-wave observations prefer a relatively stiff EoS, PSR J0740+6620's central density is only $3.57^{+1.3}_{-1.3}$ times nuclear saturation, limiting the density range probed by observations of cold, nonrotating neutron stars in $β$-equilibrium.

## Key claim

The radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 mildly disfavors equations of state that support a disconnected hybrid star branch (a proxy for strong first-order phase transitions) with a Bayes factor of 6.9, but does not rule them out; the transition mass from the hadronic to the hybrid branch is constrained to lie outside the range of 1 to 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors employed a nonparametric equation of state model based on Gaussian processes, conditioned on existing dense-matter EoS models from the literature, to infer neutron star properties from a combination of X-ray pulse profile data (NICER and XMM-Newton for PSR J0740+6620), radio mass measurements, and gravitational-wave observations (GW170817). They used hierarchical Bayesian inference to combine these datasets, marginalizing over nuisance parameters such as binary masses, and computed Bayes factors to compare EoS models with and without disconnected hybrid star branches, as well as models that violate the conformal sound-speed bound.

## Key numbers

* Bayes factor of 6.9 disfavoring EoSs with a disconnected hybrid star branch (a proxy for strong phase transitions)
* Squared sound speed reaches a maximum of 0.75^{+0.25}_{-0.24} c^2 at 3.60^{+2.25}_{-1.89} times nuclear saturation density (90% credibility)
* Central density of PSR J0740+6620 is 3.57^{+1.3}_{-1.3} times nuclear saturation density
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.56^{+1.00}_{-1.07} km (90% credibility)
* Maximum nonrotating neutron star mass: 2.21^{+0.31}_{-0.21} solar masses (90% credibility)
* Radius difference between 2.0 solar mass and 1.4 solar mass neutron stars: -0.12^{+0.83}_{-0.85} km (90% credibility)
* Bayes factor of approximately 1000 in favor of violation of the conformal sound-speed bound (c_s^2 > c^2/3)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Landry (2020) by incorporating the new NICER/XMM-Newton radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 from Miller (2021) and Riley (2021), the most massive known pulsar at 2.08 solar masses. The results are comparable to those of Raaijmakers (2021) and Pang (2021), who also analyzed the same radius measurement but used parametric EoS models; the present study's nonparametric approach allows more freedom to investigate phase transitions and the conformal sound-speed bound. The findings enable tighter constraints on the maximum neutron star mass and the density at which strongly interacting matter appears in neutron star cores, informing the classification of compact objects observed in gravitational waves such as GW190814.
