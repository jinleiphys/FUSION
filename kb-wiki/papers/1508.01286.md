# Cold Nuclear Matter Effects on Open and Hidden Heavy Flavor Production at the LHC

> We discuss a number of cold nuclear matter effects that can modify open heavy flavor and quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions and could thus also affect their production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, in addition to hot quark-gluon plasma production. We show some results for $p+$Pb collisions at sqrt s = 5 TeV at the LHC.

## Key claim

Cold nuclear matter effects (isospin, shadowing, energy loss, intrinsic charm, and absorption) can modify open heavy flavor and quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions and thus also affect their production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, in addition to hot quark-gluon plasma effects. The paper shows results for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5 TeV at the LHC, but notes that the strength of nuclear shadowing remains unclear and that fixed-target results suggest other effects like energy loss or intrinsic charm could be more important in the forward region.

## Method

The paper reviews several cold nuclear matter effects: isospin differences between neutrons and protons; modifications of parton distributions in nuclei (shadowing) parameterized by global fitting groups such as EPS09; initial-state energy loss and transverse momentum (k_T) broadening (the Cronin effect); final-state nuclear absorption for quarkonium states; and intrinsic charm. Calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_pPb(y,p_T) and the forward-backward ratio R_FB(y,p_T) are presented for J/psi and D meson production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, using the EPS09 LO and NLO shadowing sets and comparing to ALICE and LHCb data.

## Key numbers

- sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for p+Pb collisions at the LHC, with a center-of-rapidity shift of Delta y = 0.46 units relative to symmetric pp and Pb+Pb collisions
- EPS09 NLO central set for lead (A=208) used for shadowing calculations; EPS09 release includes 31 error sets
- For the core shadowing model fit to PHENIX d+Au J/psi data: core radius R ~ 2.4 fm with width d ~ 0.12 fm
- For open charm: k_T^2 values of 1.46 GeV^2 and 1.92 GeV^2 used with Peterson fragmentation parameter epsilon_c = 0.008 in calculations of R_pPb(p_T)
- J/psi mass = 3.097 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of cold nuclear matter effects from fixed-target experiments and RHIC (d+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) to LHC energies, specifically p+Pb collisions. It builds on the EPS09 global analysis of nuclear parton distributions (Eskola 2009) and prior work on J/psi suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Matsui and Satz). The paper enables future global analyses incorporating LHC data to provide new shadowing benchmarks, and calls for verification of cold matter energy loss models (Arleo 2012, 2013) with other processes. The centrality-dependent shadowing models (McGlinchey 2012) and absorption cross section studies (Lourenco 2008) are discussed as ongoing work.
