# Elliptic and Triangular Flow and their Correlation in Ultrarelativistic High Multiplicity Proton Proton Collisions at 14 TeV

> The spatial configuration of initial partons in high-multiplicity proton-proton scatterings at 14 TeV is assumed as three randomly positioned "hot spots". The parton momentum distribution in the hot spots is calculated by HIJING2.0 with some modifications. This initial condition causes not only large eccentricity $ε_2$ but also triangularity $ε_3$ and the correlation of $ε_2-ε_3$ event-plane angles. The final elliptic flow $v_2$, triangular flow $v_3$, and the correlation of $v_2-v_3$ event-plane angles are calculated by using the parton cascade model BAMPS to simulate the space-time parton evolution. Our results show that the $v_2-v_3$ correlation is different from that of $ε_2-ε_3$. This finding indicates that translations of different Fourier components of the initial spatial asymmetry to the final flow components are not independent. A dynamical correlation between the elliptic and triangular flow appears during the collective expansion.

# Elliptic and Triangular Flow and their Correlation in Ultrarelativistic High Multiplicity Proton Proton Collisions at 14 TeV

## Key claim
In high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 14 TeV, the final event-plane correlation between elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 peaks at zero degrees, contrary to the initial spatial eccentricity-triangularity correlation which peaks at 30 degrees. This indicates that the translations of different Fourier components of the initial spatial asymmetry to the final flow components are not independent, and a dynamical correlation between elliptic and triangular flow appears during the collective expansion.

## Method
The authors used HIJING2.0 with modifications to generate initial parton configurations from three independent fragmenting strings (one hard jet string and two remnant strings) in high-multiplicity p+p events at 14 TeV, selecting events with 50 < dN/dy(y=0) < 60. The spatial distribution of the three "hot spots" in the transverse plane followed a Gaussian profile with radius r0 = 0.2 fm, positioned according to the proton spatial density. The space-time parton evolution was simulated using the parton cascade model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings), solving the Boltzmann equation for on-shell massless Boltzmann particles with isotropic elastic binary scatterings and a constant mean-free-path to mean-particle-distance ratio as a global parameter.

## Key numbers
- Total hadron multiplicity dN/dy at y=0 has a mean value of 10.4 for p+p collisions at 14 TeV, with a high-multiplicity tail reaching 50-60 for selected events
- Probability to produce three strings in selected events (50 < dN/dy < 60) is more than 80%
- Hot spot size r0 = 0.2 fm; proton spatial density parameters: n0 = 0.17/fm^3, R0 = 0.56 fm, d = 0.112 fm
- For lambda_mfp/d = 2 (corresponding to eta/s approximately 0.376), v2 has a broad distribution between 0 and 0.1 with maximum at 0.06; v3 distribution is narrow and centered at 0.01
- For lambda_mfp/d = 0.5 (corresponding to eta/s approximately 0.094), v2 can reach 0.2 and v3 can reach 0.07
- Initial time for BAMPS simulation is 0.1 fm/c; cell length in transverse plane is Delta x = Delta y = 0.02 fm; longitudinal cell size is Delta eta approximately 0.1
- Parton scatterings stop when local energy density is lower than 1 GeV/fm^3
- Test particle method used with amplification factor of 3000; ten thousand runs computed for event-by-event analysis

## Context
This work extends the hot-spot scenario proposed by Casalderrey-Solana (2009) to high-multiplicity p+p collisions at LHC energies, motivated by the observation of a near-side ridge in p+p collisions at 7 TeV by the CMS collaboration (Khachatryan, 2010). It contrasts with earlier geometric overlap-eccentricity predictions for p+p collisions, which gave small elliptic flow values (v2 approximately 3% in minimum bias) in hydrodynamic calculations by Luzum (2009) and Prasad (2009) or in epsilon2-v2 scaling by d'Enterria (2010). Unlike nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC where calculations by Petersen (2010) and Qiu (2011) indicated no correlations between initial event-plane angles of epsilon2 and epsilon3, this work finds such correlations can exist in p+p collisions due to the smaller number of hot spots. The finding of dynamical correlation between v2 and v3 event-planes rotating toward each other suggests that initial correlations may be washed out, making it more difficult to extract initial conditions from flow observations, and calls for further verification in future studies.
