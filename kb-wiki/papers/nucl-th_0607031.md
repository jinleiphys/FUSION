# Heavy Ion Collisions at Relativistic Energies: Testing a Nuclear Matter at High Baryon and Isospin Density

> We show that the phenomenology of isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies (few AGeV range) is extremely rich and can allow a ``direct'' study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium. We work within a relativistic transport frame, beyond a cascade picture, consistently derived from effective Lagrangians, where isospin effects are accounted for in the mean field and collision terms. Rather sensitive observables are proposed from collective flows (``differential'' flows) and from pion/kaon production ($π^-/π^+$, $K^0/K^+$ yields). For the latter point relevant non-equilibrium effects are stressed. The possibility of the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at high baryon and isospin density, is finally suggested. Some signatures could come from an expected ``neutron trapping'' effect.

## Key claim

The phenomenology of isospin effects in heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies (few AGeV range) is extremely rich and can allow a "direct" study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium, but the abstract's suggestion of a transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase is conditional on the existence of quark stars and on specific parameter choices for the quark equation of state.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic transport framework (Relativistic Landau Vlasov method) derived from effective Lagrangians, extending the Non-Linear Walecka model to include isospin degrees of freedom via the ρ meson (vector) and δ meson (scalar) fields. They solve covariant Boltzmann-type transport equations using phase-space Gaussian test particles with a Monte-Carlo procedure for hard hadron collisions, including elastic and inelastic processes involving Δ(1232 MeV) and N*(1440 MeV) resonances and their decays into pion channels. For the deconfinement study, they impose Gibbs conditions for chemical potentials and pressure to construct the mixed hadron-quark phase using the MIT bag model for the quark phase and various hadronic EoS parameterizations (NLρ, NLρδ, GM3, DDF).

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin dynamics in heavy ion collisions from the equilibrium EoS properties (reviewed by Bao and Baran) to non-equilibrium transport, building on relativistic mean field approaches (Quantum Hadro-Dynamics) and earlier transport frameworks (Ko, Giessen). The authors show that chemical equilibrium models can lead to incorrect results for transient states of an open system, contradicting approaches that treat the baryon symmetry potential classically (Bao, QLi). The deconfinement study connects to the possibility of quark stars (Haensel, Drago) and the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis, suggesting that if quark stars exist, signals of mixed hadron-quark phase formation should be observable in intermediate-energy heavy-ion experiments. The work enables future experimental searches using radioactive beams at relativistic energies to probe the Lorentz structure of the isovector interaction at densities of astrophysical interest.
