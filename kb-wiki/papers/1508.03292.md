# Intrinsic Fluctuations of the Proton Saturation Momentum Scale in High Multiplicity p+p Collisions

> High multiplicity events in p+p collisions are studied using the theory of the Color Glass Condensate. We show that intrinsic fluctuations of the proton saturation momentum scale are needed in addition to the sub-nucleonic color charge fluctuations to explain the very high multiplicity tail of distributions in p+p collisions. The origin of such intrinsic fluctuations are presumably non-perturbative in nature. Classical Yang Mills simulations using the IP-Glasma model are performed to make quantitative estimations. We find that fluctuations as large as $\cal O$(1) of the average values of the saturation momentum scale can lead to rare high multiplicity events seen in p+p data at RHIC and LHC energies. Using the available data on multiplicity distributions we try to constrain the distribution of the proton saturation momentum scale and make predictions for the multiplicity distribution in 13 TeV p+p collisions.

# Intrinsic Fluctuations of the Proton Saturation Momentum Scale in High Multiplicity p+p Collisions

## Key claim
The authors show that intrinsic fluctuations of the proton saturation momentum scale, in addition to sub-nucleonic color charge fluctuations and geometric fluctuations, are necessary to explain the very high multiplicity tail of distributions in p+p collisions. These intrinsic fluctuations are of order O(1) relative to the average saturation momentum scale and are presumably non-perturbative in origin, with the width of the Gaussian fluctuation distribution showing negligible energy dependence between 200 GeV and 7 TeV.

## Method
The authors performed Classical Yang-Mills simulations using the IP-Glasma model, which combines the IP-Sat dipole model with non-linear Glasma gluon field dynamics. They introduced event-by-event Gaussian fluctuations of the saturation momentum scale Q_S^2 at every point in the transverse plane of the proton, with the distribution width σ treated as a free parameter. Gluon multiplicities per unit rapidity were computed after an evolution time of τ = 0.4 fm/c, and results were expressed in terms of scaled multiplicity N/⟨N⟩ to minimize absolute normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- The parameter B_G = 4 GeV^{-2} determines the width of the gluon distribution in the IP-Sat model, obtained from fits to HERA data.
- A value of Q_S(𝐬_⟂)/g^2μ(𝐬_⟂) = 0.65 was previously found to provide good fits to multiplicity distributions, with a variation to 0.45 giving slightly better agreement for scaled multiplicities N_ch/⟨N_ch⟩ > 6.
- A Gaussian fluctuation width σ ∼ 0.5 ± 0.1 with weak energy dependence provides a good description of multiplicity distributions in p+p collisions at RHIC and LHC energies from 200 GeV to 7 TeV.
- The variation of σ from 200 GeV to 7 TeV is constrained to be less than 0.1, leading to σ^2(Y) = 0.16 + 0.025(Y − 5.99), corresponding to σ ∼ 0.45 at √s = 900 GeV and σ ∼ 0.47 at √s = 2.36 TeV.
- For rare events (one out of ten thousand), the proton fluctuates into a configuration with Q_S^2 more than five times the average saturation scale.
- The inelastic cross section at 13 TeV is estimated as σ_NN^{inel}(√s = 13 TeV) ∼ 76 mb using the parameterization σ_NN^{inel}(√s/1 GeV) = 25.2 + 0.05 ln(√s) + 0.56 ln^2(√s) + 45.2/√s^{0.9} + 33.8/√s^{1.1} mb.
- The prediction for 13 TeV uses σ ∼ 0.51 and g^2μ/Q_S = 0.45.

## Context
This work extends previous IP-Glasma model studies by Schenke et al. (2013) which found that sub-nucleonic color charge fluctuations and impact parameter fluctuations alone produced multiplicity distributions too narrow compared to experimental data. The intrinsic saturation scale fluctuations build on theoretical work by Iancu et al. (2004) and Iancu et al. (2004) showing that stochastic dipole splitting in the color dipole picture leads to a Gaussian distribution of ln(Q_S^2) around its mean value, with variance growing linearly in rapidity. The results are consistent with earlier findings by Dusling et al. (2013) and Dusling et al. (2012) that fluctuations of the proton saturation scale by 5-6 times its minimum-bias value are required to model rare high multiplicity events where ridge correlations are observed. The framework enables predictions for multiplicity distributions at 13 TeV p+p collisions at the LHC and can be extended to light-heavy ion and heavy ion collisions.
