# Global Analysis of Data on the Proton Structure Function g1 and Extraction of its Moments

> Inspired by recent measurements with the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab, we perform a self-consistent analysis of world data on the proton structure function g1 in the range 0.17 < Q2 < 30 (GeV/c)**2. We compute for the first time low-order moments of g1 and study their evolution from small to large values of Q2. The analysis includes the latest data on both the unpolarized inclusive cross sections and the ratio R = sigmaL / sigmaT from Jefferson Lab, as well as a new model for the transverse asymmetry A2 in the resonance region. The contributions of both leading and higher twists are extracted, taking into account effects from radiative corrections beyond the next-to-leading order by means of soft-gluon resummation techniques. The leading twist is determined with remarkably good accuracy and is compared with the predictions obtained using various polarized parton distribution sets available in the literature. The contribution of higher twists to the g1 moments is found to be significantly larger than in the case of the unpolarized structure function F2.

## Key claim

The authors perform a self-consistent global analysis of world data on the proton spin structure function \(g_1\) and its moments, extracting both leading and higher twist contributions for the first time up to moment order \(n=7\), but the extraction relies on a model for the poorly measured transverse asymmetry \(A_2\) in the resonance region and on specific parameterizations for the unpolarized structure functions and the ratio \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\).

## Method

The analysis converts world data on the longitudinal polarization asymmetry \(A_\parallel\) into the structure function \(g_1\) using experimental inputs for \(F_1\), the ratio \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\), and a new model for the transverse asymmetry \(A_2\) in the resonance region. Nachtmann moments \(M_n(Q^2)\) for \(n = 1, 3, 5, 7\) are computed by integrating \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) over \(x\) at fixed \(Q^2\) bins from 0.17 to 30 (GeV/c)\(^2\), including elastic and inelastic contributions, with bin-centering, interpolation, and extrapolation procedures. Leading and higher twist contributions are extracted from the moments using the operator product expansion (OPE) and soft-gluon resummation techniques for the perturbative QCD evolution.

## Key numbers

* The first moment \(M_1(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 30\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) is \(125 \pm 4 \pm 10 \pm 2.5 \times 10^{-3}\) (statistical, systematic, low-\(x\) extrapolation errors).
* The third moment \(M_3(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 30\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) is \(78.7 \pm 5 \pm 11 \times 10^{-4}\).
* The fifth moment \(M_5(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 30\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) is \(158 \pm 20 \pm 23 \times 10^{-5}\).
* The seventh moment \(M_7(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 15.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) is \(597 \pm 51 \pm 80 \times 10^{-6}\).
* The singlet axial charge extracted from the first moment for \(Q^2 > 5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) is \(a_0^{\rm inv} = 0.145 \pm 0.018({\rm stat.}) \pm 0.103({\rm sys.}) \pm 0.041({\rm low}~x) \pm_{0.010}^{0.006}(\alpha_s)\) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme.
* The total systematic error on \(g_1\) averages 0.025 in absolute value, with contributions from \(A_\parallel\) (average 0.015), \(F_1\) (average 0.014), \(R\) (average 0.002), and \(A_2\) (average 0.004).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the proton spin structure by including high-precision CLAS data from Jefferson Lab covering the entire resonance region, enabling the first accurate evaluation of the \(Q^2\)-evolution of \(g_1\) moments up to \(n=7\). The extraction of higher twist contributions builds on the operator product expansion framework used in earlier studies of the unpolarized structure function \(F_2\) (Ricco et al., 2003; Hall C collaboration, 2001) and the phenomenon of local quark-hadron duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970; De Rujula et al., 1977). The results enable comparisons with lattice QCD predictions for leading twist matrix elements and with nonperturbative models of the nucleon, and provide input for future determinations of color electric and magnetic polarizabilities.
