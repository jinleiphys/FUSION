# Three-body losses of a polarized Fermi gas near a p-wave Feshbach resonance in effective field theory

> We study three-body recombination of fully spin-polarized ${}^6$Li atoms that are interacting resonantly in relative p-waves. Motivated by a recent experiment, we focus on negative scattering volumes where three atoms recombine into a deep dimer and another atom. We calculate the three-body recombination rate using a Faddeev equation derived from effective field theory. In particular, we study the magnetic field and temperature dependences of the loss rate and use the recombination data to determine the effective range of the p-wave atom-atom interaction. We also predict the existence of a shallow three-body bound state that manifests itself as a prominent feature in the energy-dependent three-body recombination rate.

# Three-body losses of a polarized Fermi gas near a p-wave Feshbach resonance in effective field theory

## Key claim

The authors use effective field theory (EFT) to calculate the three-body recombination rate of spin-polarized 6Li atoms near a p-wave Feshbach resonance, finding that the energy-dependent recombination rate coefficient K3(E) differs dramatically from the simplified Breit-Wigner model used by Waseem et al. (2018), even though the temperature-averaged loss coefficients L3(T) appear similar; the EFT approach predicts a shallow three-body bound state that manifests as a prominent feature in K3(E), and the authors determine the p-wave effective range r1 from the recombination data.

## Method

The authors derive a Faddeev integral equation from a short-range effective field theory for identical fermions interacting resonantly in relative p-waves, using a Breit-Wigner-like diatom propagator at leading order in an expansion in the ratio of resonance momentum to effective range. They solve the partial-wave projected integral equation for the atom-diatom amplitude in the J^P = 1+ channel, then compute the three-body recombination matrix element into deep dimers by treating the short-range three-body parameter as a complex optical potential in perturbation theory. The energy-dependent recombination rate K3(E) is thermally averaged using a Boltzmann distribution to obtain the temperature-dependent loss coefficient L3(T), which is compared to experimental data from Waseem et al. (2018) for magnetic field detunings B-B0 > 0 (negative scattering volumes).

## Key numbers

- Effective range r1 = -0.11(2) aB^{-1} (where aB is the Bohr radius), obtained from a chi-squared fit to the T = 2.1 microK data from Waseem et al. (2018), deviating by 60% from the estimate (r1)_est = -0.182(20) aB^{-1} used in that experiment
- Resonance position B0 = 159.17(5) G for the p-wave Feshbach resonance in the |F=1/2, m_F=+1/2> hyperfine state of 6Li
- Resonance strength product a_{1,bg} Delta B = -2.8(3) x 10^6 aB^3 G, obtained from a fit to thermalization rates by Nakasuji et al. (2013)
- Three-body force H = 4 (+5/-7) and short-distance three-body parameter log10(F/m^2) = 49.8 (+0.2/-0.1) at cutoff Lambda = 0.4 aB^{-1}
- Rescaled energy gap m(E3 - E2) = alpha + beta (B - B0) with alpha = -0.4(2) x 10^{-7} aB^{-2} and beta = -11(5) x 10^{-7} aB^{-2}/G, where E3 is the three-body resonance energy and E2 is the two-body resonance energy
- The threshold law for the recombination rate in the J^P = 1+ channel is K3 proportional to E^2 at total kinetic energy E = 0

## Context

This work extends the short-range EFT approach for p-wave interactions previously developed by Braaten et al. (2011), which focused on renormalization and the spectrum of three-body states, to the calculation of finite-temperature three-body loss rates. The authors directly compare with the experimental data of Waseem et al. (2018) from Osaka University, who used a simplified Breit-Wigner model for the energy-dependent recombination rate; the EFT approach reveals that the energy-dependent rate K3(E) falls off as E^2 at low energies while the Breit-Wigner model grows as E^{-1/2}, a difference that could be distinguished by new experiments at temperatures below 0.5 microK. The results also enable prediction of the magnetic field dependence of a fermionic three-body bound state with J^P = 1+, which could be studied on the positive scattering volume side of the Feshbach resonance where it becomes a resonance near the atom-dimer threshold.
