# Thermal nuclear pairing within the self-consistent quasiparticle RPA

> The self-consistent quasiparticle RPA (SCQRPA) is constructed to study the effects of fluctuations on pairing properties in nuclei at finite temperature and z-projection M of angular momentum. Particle-number projection (PNP) is taken into account within the Lipkin-Nogami method. Several issues such as the smoothing of superfluid-normal phase transition, thermally assisted pairing in hot rotating nuclei, extraction of the nuclear pairing gap using an improved odd-even mass difference are discussed. A novel approach of embedding the PNP SCQRPA eigenvalues in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles is proposed and applied to describe the recent empirical thermodynamic quantities for iron, molybdenum, dysprosium, and ytterbium isotopes.

# Thermal nuclear pairing within the self-consistent quasiparticle RPA

## Key claim
The self-consistent quasiparticle RPA (SCQRPA) with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection (LNSCQRPA) can describe pairing properties in hot rotating nuclei without discontinuity at any pairing interaction strength, temperature, or angular momentum, provided that quasiparticle-number fluctuations (QNF) are included to smooth out the sharp superfluid-normal phase transition that appears in conventional BCS and QRPA theories.

## Method
The authors construct the SCQRPA by solving a set of BCS-based equations that include corrections from QNF, where the quasiparticle occupation numbers are obtained from an integral equation involving the mass operator and quasiparticle damping rather than from a Fermi-Dirac distribution. Particle-number projection is applied via the Lipkin-Nogami method, and the resulting LNSCQRPA eigenvalues are embedded into the canonical ensemble (CE) and microcanonical ensemble (MCE) to compute thermodynamic quantities. The formalism is extended to finite z-projection M of angular momentum (noncollective rotation) by introducing two types of quasiparticle occupation numbers depending on single-particle spin projections.

## Key numbers
- For the half-filled schematic model with N = Omega = 10 equidistant levels (epsilon_k = k - (Omega+1)/2 MeV), the LNSCQRPA describes exact ground and first excited state energies without discontinuity around the critical pairing interaction G_c, where RPA, QRPA, and SCQRPA collapse.
- For N = Omega = 10 and 50, the pairing gap never collapses but decreases monotonously with increasing temperature T, while the heat capacity spike at T_c within FTBCS becomes a broad bump.
- For N = Omega = 10 with G = 0.5 MeV at T/T_c >= 1, the gap decreases monotonously with increasing angular momentum M but never vanishes; at M/M_c >= 3, the pairing gap reappears at T > T_1 and remains finite with further increasing T.
- The naive odd-even mass difference at T != 0 gives a negative gap for N = 9 at T > 2.4 MeV (Omega = 10, G = 0.9 MeV), while the improved formula from Eq. (gapN) agrees much better with the canonical gap.
- MCE entropies are calculated using delta_E = 1 MeV for the experimental comparison with Fe-56, Mo-94, Dy-162, and Yb-172.

## Context
This work extends the SCQRPA framework developed by the same authors (Dang, 2000s) to finite temperature and rotation, addressing the failure of conventional BCS and QRPA theories at the critical pairing strength G_c and critical temperature T_c. The exact solution of the pairing problem (Richardson, 1960s) shows no singularity at any G, motivating the inclusion of fluctuations. The approach smooths out the Mottelson-Valatin effect (Mottelson and Valatin, 1960) and the thermally assisted pairing reentrance (Moretto, 1970s; Balian, 1970s) that appear in FTBCS. The embedding into CE and MCE enables comparison with experimental thermodynamic data for Fe, Mo, Dy, and Yb isotopes (experimental data from 2000s), and the method is feasible for larger systems where exact diagonalization or Richardson equation solutions are impractical.
