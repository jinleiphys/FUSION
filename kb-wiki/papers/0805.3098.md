# Bethe--Salpeter wave functions in integrable models

> We investigate some properties of Bethe--Salpeter wave functions in integrable models. In particular we illustrate the application of the operator product expansion in determining the short distance behavior. The energy dependence of the potentials obtained from such wave functions is studied, and further we discuss the (limited) phenomenological significance of zero--energy potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the operator product expansion (OPE) can be used to determine the short-distance behavior of Bethe–Salpeter wave functions in integrable models, but cautions that zero-energy potentials have only limited phenomenological significance.

## Method

The authors study Bethe–Salpeter wave functions in two integrable models: the two-dimensional Ising model and the O(3) nonlinear sigma model. They apply the operator product expansion to analyze the short-distance behavior of these wave functions and examine the energy dependence of the potentials derived from them.

## Key numbers

- In the two-dimensional Ising model, the short-distance behavior of the Bethe–Salpeter wave function is determined using the OPE, with explicit calculations shown for the leading terms.
- In the O(3) nonlinear sigma model, the energy dependence of the potential obtained from the Bethe–Salpeter wave function is studied, with results presented for the zero-energy limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Bethe–Salpeter wave functions in quantum field theory, particularly building on the operator product expansion techniques developed by Wilson (1969) and others. The findings enable a better understanding of how integrable models can serve as testing grounds for non-perturbative methods in nuclear and particle physics, while also clarifying the limitations of using zero-energy potentials for phenomenological applications.
