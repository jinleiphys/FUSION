# Off-of-equilibrium effects on Kurtosis Along Strangeness-Neutral Trajectories

> The Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) is searching for the QCD critical point. The main signal for the critical point is the kurtosis of the distribution of proton yields obtained on an event-by-event basis where one expects a peak at the critical point. However, its exact behavior is still an open question due to out-of-equilibrium effects and uncertainty in the equation of state. Here we use a simplistic hydrodynamic model that enforces strangeness-neutrality, selecting trajectories that pass close to the critical point. We vary the initial conditions to estimate the effect of out-of-equilibrium hydrodynamics on the kurtosis signal.

## Key claim

Out-of-equilibrium hydrodynamic effects cause a significant spread in the kurtosis (fourth moment of the net-baryon distribution) probed on an event-by-event basis, even when trajectories pass through the same critical region; the specific value of kurtosis at freeze-out depends heavily on the initial conditions of the shear-stress tensor and bulk pressure, not just on the equation of state.

## Method

The authors used a simplified Bjorken-expanding hydrodynamic model with DNMR equations of motion, incorporating a QCD equation of state with a critical point matched to the 3D Ising universality class (following Karthein (2021) and Parotto (2018)). They enforced strangeness-neutrality and finite electric charge to select heavy-ion trajectories, varied initial conditions for the shear-stress tensor and bulk pressure, and implemented a bulk viscosity with critical scaling proportional to the cube of the correlation length. The kurtosis was evaluated at three fixed temperatures (1, 3, and 5 MeV below the critical temperature) by comparing the values along isentropes to the range produced by out-of-equilibrium effects.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity scaling: \(\zeta/s \sim (\xi/\xi_0)^3\), where \(\xi_0\) is a scaling constant for the correlation length in the critical region.
- Kurtosis evaluated at three temperatures: 1, 3, and 5 MeV below the critical temperature \(T_c\).
- The non-critical bulk viscosity form used: \(\frac{\zeta T}{w} = 36 \times \frac{1/3 - c_s^2}{8\pi}\), with the critical enhancement factor \([1 + (\xi/\xi_0)^3]\).
- In one case (bottom right panel of Fig. 4), the isentrope probes a \(\chi_4\) value outside the bounds produced by out-of-equilibrium effects.

## Context

This work extends the equation of state with a critical point developed by Karthein (2021) and Parotto (2018), which matched a 3D Ising critical region to lattice QCD at zero baryon density while enforcing strangeness-neutrality and finite electric charge. It builds on prior studies by Dore (2020) and Du (2021) showing that out-of-equilibrium effects alter the \((T,\mu_B)\) trajectories explored by the system. The results are qualitative and cannot make quantitative predictions, but they demonstrate that realistic model-to-data comparisons for the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC must account for event-by-event variations in initial hydrodynamic conditions when interpreting kurtosis signals for the QCD critical point.
