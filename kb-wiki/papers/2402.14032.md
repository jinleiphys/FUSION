# Recent advances in chiral EFT based nuclear forces and their applications

> During the past two decades, chiral effective field theory has evolved into a powerful tool to derive nuclear forces from first principles. Nearly all two-nucleon interactions have been worked out up to sixth order of chiral perturbation theory, while, with few exceptions, three-nucleon forces, which play a subtle, but crucial role in microscopic nuclear structure calculations, have been derived up to fifth order. We review the current status of these forces as well as their applications in nuclear many-body systems. While the ab initio description of light nuclei is generally very successful, we point out and analyze problems encountered with medium-mass nuclei. We also survey the construction of equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron-rich matter based on chiral forces. A focal point is the symmetry energy and its impact on neutron skins and systems of astrophysical relevance. The physics of neutron-rich systems, from nuclei to compact stars, is essentially determined by the density dependence of the symmetry energy. We review the status of predictions in comparison with latest empirical constraints, with particular attention to those extracted from parity violating electron scattering.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (ChET) now provides nuclear forces up to sixth order (N5LO) for two-nucleon interactions and fifth order for three-nucleon forces, but while light nuclei are described successfully, medium-mass nuclei still present unresolved problems that the authors analyze.

## Method

The authors review the systematic chiral perturbation theory expansion of nuclear forces order by order, from leading order (LO) through N5LO, using Weinberg power counting to organize Feynman diagrams by powers of momentum over the breakdown scale (approximately 0.7 GeV). They discuss both the "Delta-less" formulation, where Delta-isobar effects are absorbed into low-energy constants, and the "Delta-full" formulation with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, then survey applications to nuclear many-body systems including light and medium-mass nuclei, symmetric nuclear matter, and neutron-rich matter.

## Key numbers

- Two-nucleon forces have been derived up to sixth order (N5LO) of chiral perturbation theory; three-nucleon forces have been derived up to fifth order (N4LO)
- The breakdown scale Lambda_b is approximately 0.7 GeV (the rho meson mass)
- The Delta-isobar excitation energy Delta M = M_Delta - M_N_bar = 293.0817 MeV
- At N5LO, 26 new NN contact terms appear (proportional to Q^6), bringing the total number of NN contacts to 50
- In the Delta-less theory, the LECs c_3 and c_4 have magnitudes of about 3-5 GeV^{-1}; in the Delta-full theory they are around 1 GeV^{-1}
- The charged-pion mass is 139.5704 MeV, the neutral-pion mass is 134.9768 MeV, and the average pion mass is 138.0392 MeV

## Context

This review extends the chiral EFT program previously summarized by Machleidt and Entem (2011) and Epelbaum, Hammer, and Meissner (2009), now incorporating the highest-order calculations from Entem et al. (2015a, 2015b) for two-nucleon forces and Krebs, Gasparyan, and Epelbaum (2012, 2013) for three-nucleon forces. The work enables systematic uncertainty quantification in ab initio nuclear structure calculations and provides chiral equations of state for neutron-rich matter that can be compared with empirical constraints from parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as PREX and CREX.
