# Relativistic Dynamics and the Deuteron Axial Current

> The deuteron axial current is sensitive both to the form of the implementation of relativistic dynamics as well as to the details of the deuteron D state at moderate momentum transfer, making it a natural partner to the magnetic form factor for exploring details of nucleon-nucleon dynamics and associated electroweak properties.

# Relativistic Dynamics and the Deuteron Axial Current

## Key claim

The deuteron axial form factor $F_A(Q^2)$ is highly sensitive to the form of relativistic dynamics used (manifestly covariant Gross scheme versus light-front dynamics) and to the deuteron D-state contribution at moderate momentum transfer, but this sensitivity is not primarily due to two-body current contributions from pair terms or negative-energy P-state admixtures, unlike the case for the magnetic form factor $B(Q^2)$.

## Method

The author computed the deuteron axial form factor $F_A(Q^2)$ using the manifestly covariant Gross equation framework with a family of deuteron vertex functions from Buck and Gross (1979), varying the pseudoscalar/pseudovector coupling ratio $\lambda$ from 0.0 to 0.4. For comparison, light-front calculations were performed using the Paris potential wave functions and the Frankfurt et al. extraction scheme for $F_A(Q^2)$ from matrix elements of $A^+(0)$. The S-state and D-state contributions were isolated separately, and the effect of omitting negative-energy P-state contributions was examined within the Gross approach.

## Key numbers

- At $Q^2$ as low as 1 GeV$^2$, there is a noticeable difference among the Gross, light-front, and nonrelativistic calculations for the full deuteron axial form factor.
- The S-state contribution to $F_A(Q^2)$ has a node near $Q^2 = 18$ fm$^{-2}$.
- Switching from the Paris to the Nijmegen potential shifts the minimum in $F_A(Q^2)$ by only 1-2 fm$^{-2}$, which is much smaller than the differences between forms of relativistic dynamics.
- Varying the pseudoscalar/pseudovector coupling ratio $\lambda$ between 0.0, 0.2, and 0.4 produces little difference in the Gross calculation results.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic effects in the deuteron axial current previously studied by Frederico, Henley, and Miller (1991) within light-front dynamics, and contrasts those results with the manifestly covariant Gross equation approach developed by Gross (1982) and applied to electromagnetic currents by Arnold, Carlson, and Gross (1977, 1980). The paper shows that the deuteron axial form factor, together with the magnetic form factor $B(Q^2)$ studied by Van Orden, Devine, and Gross (1995), provides a sensitive testing ground for dynamical models even at moderate $Q^2$, and that different implementations of relativistic dynamics produce large quantitative differences primarily through the deuteron D-state contribution.
