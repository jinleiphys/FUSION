# $ππ$ scattering in a renormalized Hamiltonian matrix

> A Wilsonian approach to $ππ$ scattering based in the Glazek-Wilson Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) for Hamiltonians is analyzed in momentum space up to a maximal CM energy of $\sqrt{s}=1.4$ GeV. To this end, we identify the corresponding relativistic Hamiltonian by means of the 3D reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Kadyshevsky scheme, introduce a momentum grid and provide an isospectral definition of the phase-shift based on a spectral shift of a Chebyshev angle. We also propose a new method to integrate the SRG equations based on the Crank-Nicolson algorithm with a single step finite difference so that isospectrality is preserved at any step of the calculations. We discuss issues on the unnatural high momentum tails present in the fitted interactions and reaching far beyond the maximal CM energy of $\sqrt{s}=1.4$ GeV and how these tails can be integrated out explicitly by using Block-Diagonal generators of the SRG.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) with a block-diagonal generator can decouple high-momentum tails (reaching up to 30 GeV) from the physically relevant region below 1.4 GeV in ππ scattering, while preserving the phase shifts through a new spectral-shift method based on Chebyshev angles.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Hamiltonian derived via the Kadyshevsky 3D reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for ππ scattering. They discretize momentum space using a Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature grid and compute phase shifts from the eigenvalue spectrum using a "φ-shift" method that relates the shift in Chebyshev angles to scattering phase shifts. They then apply SRG evolution using a block-diagonal generator (with projectors P and Q separated by a cutoff Λ) to decouple the low-momentum and high-momentum subspaces, integrating the SRG equations with a Crank-Nicolson algorithm that preserves isospectrality.

## Key numbers

- The analysis is performed up to a maximal center-of-mass energy of √s = 1.4 GeV.
- The phenomenological ππ potentials exhibit long momentum tails reaching up to 30 GeV.
- Phase shifts for the S0 channel are calculated using a grid of N = 50 points, with the φ-shift method reproducing the model fit and experimental data from Garcia-Martin (2011).
- The block-diagonal SRG evolution uses a cutoff Λ ∼ 1400 MeV to separate the two subspaces.
- The φ-shift method works accurately even with a coarse grid of N = 25 points, showing improvement over the standard Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach, previously used mainly in nuclear physics to tame nuclear core interactions, to hadronic ππ scattering. It addresses the problem of unnaturally long momentum tails in phenomenological potentials from Mathelitsch (1986) by explicitly integrating them out. The method enables future treatment of three-body systems (such as ω and A₁ resonances decaying to three pions) by providing a reduced effective potential that contains far fewer momentum grid points while preserving the correct two-body phase shifts.
