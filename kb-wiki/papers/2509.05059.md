# Dispersion relations of deeply virtual Compton scattering: investigating twist-4 kinematic power corrections

> In this paper we include kinematic power corrections up to twist-four to the deeply virtual Compton scattering dispersion relation. We demonstrate that, both for (pseudo-)scalar and spin-$1/2$ targets, the formal expression of the $n$-subtracted leading-twist dispersion relations is preserved. However, the expression of the subtracted constants is modified by the kinematic powers. Importantly, the minimal-subtracted dispersion relation for the helicity-conserving amplitude, previously thought to depend only on the Polyakov-Weiss $D$-term, now also depends on the double distributions $F$ and $K$. These results are consistent with the ones obtained previously in the literature. Such a mixing may be critical for the Jefferson Lab kinematic range, as it is not suppressed for typical values of $t$ and $Q^{2}$ in the valence region. We therefore expect a strong impact on attempts to extract pressure forces from DVCS data.

## Key claim
The formal expression of n-subtracted leading-twist dispersion relations for deeply virtual Compton scattering is preserved when kinematic power corrections up to twist-four are included, but the subtracted constants are modified. The minimal-subtracted dispersion relation for the helicity-conserving amplitude, previously thought to depend only on the Polyakov-Weiss D-term, now also depends on the double distributions F and K, and this mixing is not suppressed for typical Jefferson Lab kinematics in the valence region.

## Method
The authors derived the dispersion relations for DVCS including kinematic twist-4 power corrections for both spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets. They demonstrated that the imaginary part of the Compton form factor is generated solely by the DGLAP region of the generalized parton distribution, preserving the form of the n-subtracted dispersion relations while modifying the coefficient functions. The modified coefficient functions were expressed in terms of double distributions F and K, and the impact on the deconvolution problem of the D-term was investigated using Gegenbauer polynomial expansions at leading order in alpha_s.

## Key numbers
- The kinematic twist-4 corrections scale as t/Q^2, where Q^2 is the photon virtuality and t is the momentum transfer squared
- For the D-term convolution with the twist-4 kernel, the integral yields: 4 * sum_n d_{2n+1}(t) * [1 - (t/(2*Q^2)) * (1 - 1/((2n+3)(n+1)))]
- The modification to the shadow D-term between the first two Gegenbauer modes is approximately 0.12 * |t/Q^2| at leading order in the twist expansion
- The ratio of coefficients for the first two Gegenbauer modes in the twist-4 contribution is 27/20 = 1.35, representing a 35% difference

## Context
This work extends previous studies of DVCS dispersion relations by Teryaev (2005), Anikin (2007), and Diehl (2007), and is consistent with results obtained by Braun (2014) for the one-time subtraction constant. The kinematic twist-4 corrections were computed building on the framework developed by Braun (2022, 2025) and Martinez-Fernandez (2025) for the DVCS coefficient function. The results call into question the common assumption that DVCS dispersion relations allow one to bypass the extraction of GPDs to access pressure and shear forces within the nucleon, particularly for Jefferson Lab kinematics. The authors note that studying the photon helicity flip amplitude H^{+-} may be more promising for disentangling Gegenbauer modes, as it has no pure leading-twist contribution.
