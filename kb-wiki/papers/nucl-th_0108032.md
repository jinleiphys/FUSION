# Pseudoscalar and vector meson production in NN collisions

> Heavy meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is discussed within a meson exchange model of hadronic interactions, paying special attention to the basic dynamics that determine the behavior of the cross sections near the threshold energy. The pp-->ppϕreaction is discussed as an example of the production of vector mesons in NN collisions. For the pseudoscalar meson production, results for the ηproduction in both the pp and pn collisions are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that heavy meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near threshold is dominated by the energy dependence of the on-shell NN final-state interaction, but that quantitative predictions require consistent treatment of half-off-shell NN interactions and the production current, which introduces model-dependent uncertainties that can change predicted cross sections by a factor of two or more.

## Method

The author develops a relativistic meson exchange model for the NN → NNM reaction using a Distorted Wave Born Approximation, where the total amplitude is constructed from NN T-matrices and a production current J derived from meson-nucleon T-matrices. The production current is modeled by splitting the MN T-matrix into pole and non-pole parts, including nucleonic, resonance, and mesonic currents, with the NN interaction taken from the Bonn group's Blankenbecler-Sugar reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation. The model is applied to pp → ppφ and η production in both pp and pn collisions.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer at threshold for φ meson production: q = 5.6 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a probed distance r = 0.18 fm
- Momentum transfer at threshold for η meson production: q = 3.9 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a probed distance r = 0.26 fm
- For η meson production, the threshold incident beam energy is approximately 1.25 GeV
- The OZI rule prediction for the ratio σ(pp→ppφ)/σ(pp→ppω) is R = 4.2 × 10⁻³, based on a deviation from ideal mixing angle α_V ≈ 3.7°
- The extracted φNN coupling constant from the DISTO data is in the range g_φNN ≈ −(0.2 − 0.9), compatible with the OZI value g_φNN ≈ −(0.60 ± 0.15)
- The predicted ratio R_φ/ω at low excess energies is 4 to 7 times the OZI estimate; at higher energies the enhancement decreases to a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends meson exchange model calculations of heavy meson production in NN collisions, building on earlier approaches by Sibirtsev, Batinic, Nakayama, Gedalin, Wilkin, and Baru. The paper specifically addresses the pp→ppφ reaction in connection with the question of hidden strangeness in the nucleon, following the experimental results from the DISTO collaboration (2001) that showed a φ/ω production ratio about eight times larger than the OZI rule estimate. The model enables systematic analysis of different meson production mechanisms and provides a framework for extracting the φNN coupling strength from angular distribution data, with current experimental efforts at COSY expected to reduce uncertainties.
