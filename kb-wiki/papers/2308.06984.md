# Exclusive quarkonium photoproduction in $A$+$A$ UPCs at the LHC in NLO pQCD

> We present the first study of coherent exclusive quarkonium ($J/ψ$, $Υ$) photoproduction in ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus collisions (UPCs) at the LHC in the framework of collinear factorization and next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD (pQCD). We make NLO predictions for the $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ rapidity distributions for lead (Pb) and oxygen (O) beams, and quantify their dependence on the factorization/renormalization scale, nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their uncertainties, and on differences between nuclear PDFs and generalized parton distribution functions (GPDs). We show that within the PDF-originating uncertainties our approach provides a good description of the available $J/ψ$ photoproduction data in Pb+Pb UPCs at the LHC but that the scale uncertainty is significant. We demonstrate that at NLO pQCD the quark contributions are important in the $J/ψ$ case but that gluons clearly dominate the $Υ$ cross sections. We also study how the scale dependence could be tamed by considering O+O/Pb+Pb ratios of the exclusive $J/ψ$ UPC cross sections, and how HERA and p+p/Pb LHC data can help in obtaining better-controlled NLO predictions in the $Υ$ case.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first NLO pQCD study of coherent exclusive quarkonium photoproduction in nucleus-nucleus UPCs at the LHC, showing that within nPDF uncertainties the approach describes available J/ψ data but that the scale uncertainty is significant (a factor of 55 at midrapidity for Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV), and that quark contributions dominate the J/ψ cross section at NLO while gluons dominate the Υ cross section.

## Method

The authors compute rapidity-differential cross sections for coherent J/ψ and Υ photoproduction in Pb+Pb and O+O UPCs using collinear factorization at NLO pQCD, folding photon fluxes from the Weizsäcker-Williams method with photon-nucleus cross sections obtained from the forward-limit nuclear GPDs (equated to nuclear PDFs from EPPS21, nNNPDF3.0, and nCTEQ15WZSIH). The NLO coefficient functions for gluons and quarks are taken from Ivanov et al. (2004), and the non-perturbative NRQCD matrix elements are fixed by matching to measured leptonic decay widths. For Υ, the authors also employ the Shuvaev transform for GPD modeling and a data-driven method that normalizes the pQCD nuclear ratio to HERA and LHC data.

## Key numbers

- Scale uncertainty for J/ψ in Pb+Pb UPCs at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: a factor of 55 at y=0 when varying μ from M_J/ψ/2 to M_J/ψ
- Scale uncertainty for J/ψ in Pb+Pb UPCs at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: a factor of 22 at y=0 for the same scale variation
- Optimal scale for J/ψ: μ ≈ 0.77 M_J/ψ
- For O+O/Pb+Pb same-energy ratios of J/ψ cross sections, the factor of 55 scale uncertainty at y=0 shrinks to a factor of 1.7
- For Υ, the scale is varied between M_Υ/4 and M_Υ, with weaker scale dependence than for J/ψ

## Context

This work extends the LO approach originally proposed by Ryskin (1992) for exclusive J/ψ photoproduction as a probe of small-x gluon distributions, and builds on the authors' previous NLO studies of J/ψ (Eskola et al., 2022) and Υ (Eskola et al., 2023) in A+A UPCs. The results show that J/ψ photoproduction at NLO may probe s-quark distributions rather than gluons directly, contrary to the LO expectation, while Υ remains a more direct gluon probe. The study enables future use of UPC data to constrain nuclear PDFs in global analyses, provided NNLO and NRQCD corrections are incorporated.
