# Wounded nucleon model with realistic nucleon-nucleon collision profile and observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the influence of the nucleon-nucleon collision profile (probability of interaction as a function of the nucleon-nucleon impact parameter) in the wounded nucleon model and its extensions on several observables measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that the participant eccentricity coefficient, $ε^\ast$, as well as the higher harmonic coefficients, $ε_n^\ast$, are reduced by 10-20% for mid-peripheral collisions when the realistic (Gaussian) profile is used, as compared to the case with the commonly-used hard-sphere profile. Similarly, the multiplicity fluctuations, treated as the function of the number of wounded nucleons in one of the colliding nuclei, are reduced by 10-20%. This demonstrates that the Glauber Monte Carlo codes should necessarily use the realistic nucleon-nucleon collision profile in precision studies of these observables. The Gaussian collision profile is built-in in {\tt GLISSANDO}.

## Key claim

The shape of the nucleon-nucleon collision profile (realistic Gaussian versus hard-sphere) significantly affects several heavy-ion observables, reducing participant eccentricity coefficients and multiplicity fluctuations by 10-20% for mid-peripheral collisions, but only when the Glauber Monte Carlo approach is used; the effect is absent for average numbers of wounded nucleons.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber Monte Carlo simulations using the GLISSANDO code with Woods-Saxon nuclear densities and nucleon-nucleon repulsion correlations (expulsion distance 0.9 fm). They compared two nucleon-nucleon collision profiles: a realistic Gaussian profile (parameterized from ISR pp scattering data, with A=0.92) and the commonly-used hard-sphere step function, both normalized to the same total inelastic NN cross section. The simulations were run for Pb+Pb, Au+Au, and Ar+Ca collisions at various energies, and the resulting eccentricity coefficients, higher harmonic coefficients, and multiplicity fluctuations were computed event-by-event.

## Key numbers

- Participant eccentricity ε* is reduced by 10% for Pb+Pb at σ_inel=32 mb (top SPS energy) and by 20% at σ_inel=63 mb (LHC) when using the Gaussian profile versus hard-sphere profile, for mid-peripheral collisions.
- Higher harmonic coefficients ε_n* (n=3,4,5) are reduced by approximately 15% for mid-peripheral collisions with the Gaussian profile.
- Multiplicity fluctuations ω_targ for Ar+Ca collisions at σ_inel=32 mb are reduced by approximately 15% with the Gaussian profile.
- The elastic NN cross section from the Gaussian profile is 6.8 mb, compared to 7.8 mb from the fit of Bialas (2006) and 7.4 mb from the hard-sphere profile.
- The Gaussian profile parameter A is set to 0.92 for all studied collisions.

## Context

This work extends the wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976) and the Glauber Monte Carlo approach used in codes such as GLISSANDO (Broniowski 2007). It contradicts the common practice of using a hard-sphere collision profile, showing that the realistic Gaussian profile (inferred from pp scattering data by Bialas (2006)) yields 10-20% lower eccentricity parameters and multiplicity fluctuations. This is relevant for precision hydrodynamic calculations probing the equation of state and viscosity parameters (e.g., Schenke 2010, Song 2007, Bozek 2009), and for the interpretation of elliptic flow and triangular flow measurements at RHIC and LHC. The results enable more accurate initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations and are directly implemented in the GLISSANDO code.
