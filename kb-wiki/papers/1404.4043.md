# The melting and abundance of open charm hadrons

> Ratios of cumulants of conserved net charge fluctuations are sensitive to the degrees of freedom that are carriers of the corresponding quantum numbers in different phases of strong interaction matter. Using lattice QCD with 2+1 dynamical flavors and quenched charm quarks we calculate second and fourth order cumulants of net charm fluctuations and their correlations with other conserved charges such as net baryon number, electric charge and strangeness. Analyzing appropriate ratios of these cumulants we probe the nature of charmed degrees of freedom in the vicinity of the QCD chiral crossover region. We show that for temperatures above the chiral crossover transition temperature, charmed degrees of freedom can no longer be described by an uncorrelated gas of hadrons. This suggests that the dissociation of open charm hadrons and the emergence of deconfined charm states sets in just near the chiral crossover transition. Till the crossover region we compare these lattice QCD results with two hadron resonance gas models --including only the experimentally established charmed resonances and also including additional states predicted by quark model and lattice QCD calculations. This comparison provides evidence for so far unobserved charmed hadrons that contribute to the thermodynamics in the crossover region.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with 2+1 dynamical flavors and quenched charm quarks, the authors show that for temperatures above the chiral crossover transition temperature, charmed degrees of freedom can no longer be described by an uncorrelated gas of hadrons, suggesting that dissociation of open charm hadrons and emergence of deconfined charm states sets in just near the chiral crossover transition. ## Method

The authors calculated second and fourth order cumulants of net charm fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness using (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action on lattices of size 32^3 x 8 and 24^3 x 6, with the charm quark sector treated in the quenched approximation. They analyzed ratios of these cumulants to probe the nature of charmed degrees of freedom in the vicinity of the QCD chiral crossover region, and compared the lattice results with two hadron resonance gas models: one using only experimentally established charmed resonances (PDG-HRG) and another including additional states predicted by quark model and lattice QCD calculations (QM-HRG).

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover transition temperature for the lattice setup used is 158(3) MeV, about 4 MeV larger than the continuum extrapolated result at physical quark masses of 154(9) MeV.
- At T = 160 MeV, the deviation of lattice QCD data from the PDG-HRG model for ratios of charmed baryon to meson contributions is 40% or larger.
- The ratio of charm and strange quark masses varies from m_c/m_s = 12.42 at beta = 6.39 to m_c/m_s = 11.28 at beta = 7.15.
- The quantity (chi_4^C - chi_2^C)/12, which is an upper bound for contributions from |C| > 1 channels, is less than 0.2% of chi_2^C for all temperatures analyzed.
- The temperature range covered on N_tau = 8 lattices is 156.8 MeV to 330.2 MeV.
- At T_c, differences between PDG-HRG and QM-HRG in the baryon sector are as large as 40%, while they are negligible in the meson sector.
- The mass difference between |C| = 2 and |C| = 1 charmed baryons is approximately 1.2 GeV, leading to a suppression factor of exp(-Delta/T) approximately 10^{-3} at T approximately 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to strangeness fluctuations by the same collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2014) to the charm sector, using similar ratios of cumulants to probe changes in degrees of freedom. The analysis shows that open charm hadrons start to dissolve close to the chiral crossover temperature, similar to what was previously observed for strange hadrons, and provides first-principles evidence for a substantial contribution of experimentally unobserved charmed baryons to the pressure of a hadron resonance gas, consistent with predictions from quark model calculations by Ebert et al. and lattice QCD calculations by Edwards et al. This enables improved modeling of charmed hadron production in heavy ion collision experiments and provides guidance for upcoming experiments at Jefferson Laboratory, FAIR, and the LHC.
