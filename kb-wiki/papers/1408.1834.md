# Linear confinement in momentum space: singularity-free bound-state equations

> Relativistic equations of Bethe-Salpeter type for hadron structure are most conveniently formulated in momentum space. The presence of confining interactions causes complications because the corresponding kernels are singular. This occurs not only in the relativistic case but also in the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation where this problem can be studied more easily. For the linear confining interaction the singularity reduces to one of Cauchy principal value form. Although this singularity is integrable, it still makes accurate numerical solutions difficult. We show that this principal value singularity can be eliminated by means of a subtraction method. The resulting equation is much easier to solve and yields accurate and stable solutions. To test the method's numerical efficiency, we performed a three-parameter least-squares fit of a simple linear-plus-Coulomb potential to the bottomonium spectrum.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the principal-value singularity in the momentum-space Schrödinger equation with a linear confining potential can be eliminated entirely through a subtraction method, yielding a singularity-free kernel that is much easier to solve numerically. The authors show this for the nonrelativistic case as a test bed, with the explicit hedge that the method is intended to be extended later to relativistic Bethe-Salpeter-type equations in the Covariant Spectator Theory framework.

## Method
The authors start from the momentum-space Schrödinger equation for a linear confining potential, which contains a Cauchy principal-value singularity in the kernel. They perform a partial-wave decomposition and then apply two subtraction techniques: one subtraction removes the principal-value singularity by adding and subtracting a term proportional to the derivative of the wave function, and another subtraction (based on Landé's method) removes the logarithmic singularity present in higher partial waves. The resulting integral equation has a completely nonsingular kernel and is solved numerically by expanding the wave function in a basis of cubic B-spline functions, converting the equation into a generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers
- The ten lowest S-wave energy eigenvalues for the unscreened linear potential converge to the exact Airy-function values; for example, the ground state energy is 2.338107 in units of \((\sigma^2/2m_R)^{1/3}\), matching the exact value to six decimal places with N=24 splines.
- For P-wave (ℓ=1), the ground state energy converges to 3.361258 in the same units with N=48 splines.
- The string tension σ and reduced mass m_R scale the energy eigenvalues as \((\sigma^2/2m_R)^{1/3}\); the numerical tests use σ = 2m_R = 1.
- A three-parameter least-squares fit of a linear-plus-Coulomb potential to the bottomonium spectrum was performed to test the method's numerical efficiency (no specific fit results or χ² values are given in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the momentum-space treatment of confining potentials by Gross and Milana (1991) and Savkli and Gross (1999) within the Covariant Spectator Theory, improving on earlier approaches that either kept a finite screening parameter (Eyre and Vary, 1986) or used screened potentials with barrier-penetration issues. The singularity-free equation enables accurate and stable numerical solutions for heavy quarkonia (e.g., bottomonium) and provides a foundation for solving fully relativistic bound-state equations in Minkowski space, avoiding the extrapolation uncertainties of Euclidean-space Dyson-Schwinger approaches (e.g., Maris and Tandy, 2003; Fischer, 2006).
