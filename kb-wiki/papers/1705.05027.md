# Ultrahigh-energy Cosmic-ray Nuclei from Black Hole Jets: Recycling Galactic Cosmic Rays through Shear Acceleration

> We perform Monte Carlo simulations of transrelativistic shear acceleration dedicated to a jet-cocoon system of active galactic nuclei. A certain fraction of galactic cosmic rays in a halo is entrained, and sufficiently high-energy particles can be injected to the reacceleration process and further accelerated up to 100 EeV. We show that the shear reacceleration mechanism leads to a hard spectrum of escaping cosmic rays, $dL_E/dE\propto E^{-1}-E^0$, distinct from a conventional $E^{-2}$ spectrum. The supersolar abundance of ultrahigh-energy nuclei is achieved due to injections at TeV-PeV energies. As a result, we find that the highest-energy spectrum and mass composition can be reasonably explained by our model without contradictions with the anisotropy data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that shear acceleration in the jet-cocoon system of radio-loud active galactic nuclei can recycle galactic cosmic rays into ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays (UHECRs), producing a hard spectrum ($dL_E/dE\propto E^{-1}-E^0$) and supersolar heavy-nuclei abundance that matches observations, but only for particles injected at TeV-PeV energies that can enter the discrete shear acceleration regime.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of transrelativistic discrete shear acceleration in a two-cylinder geometry representing an AGN jet and its surrounding cocoon, tracking 262,144 particles per nuclear species injected at the jet-cocoon boundary. They modeled particle transport using a random-walk scattering prescription with energy-dependent mean free paths (Kolmogorov turbulence in the cocoon, Bohm diffusion in the jet) and recorded particles as escaping when they diffused beyond the cocoon radius or jet length. The resulting source spectra were then propagated to Earth using CRPropa 3, including photomeson production, photodisintegration, pair production, and nuclear decay, and compared to Pierre Auger Observatory and Telescope Array data.

## Key numbers

- Maximum proton energy: $E_{p,\rm{max}} \sim 1.6$ EeV for the reference parameter set ($R_{\rm jet}=0.5$ kpc, $B_{\rm coc}=3\ \mu$G, $\beta_{\rm jet}=0.7$, $\xi_{c/j}=10$)
- Maximum iron energy: $E_{\rm Fe,\rm{max}} \sim 100$ EeV (from $E_{i,\rm{max}} = Z_i E_{p,\rm{max}}$ with $Z_{\rm Fe}=26$)
- Injection energy for discrete shear acceleration: $E_{i,\rm{inj}} \sim 15 Z_i$ TeV (from setting $\lambda_{i,\rm sl}=R_{\rm sl}$ with $R_{\rm sl}\sim 5$ pc)
- Adiabatic cooling timescale: $t_{\rm ad} \approx R_{\rm coc}/v_{\rm exp} \sim 1.6$ Myr (with $v_{\rm exp}=3000$ km/s)
- Required UHECR luminosity per source: $L_{\rm{UHECR}} \sim 2\times10^{40} - 2\times10^{41}$ erg/s (from FR I number density $\sim 10^{-5} - 10^{-4}$ Mpc$^{-3}$)
- Relative abundance ratio at same rigidity: $(f_{\rm{H}}, f_{\rm{He}}, f_{\rm{C-O}}, f_{\rm{Ne-Al}}, f_{\rm{Si-K}}, f_{\rm{Ca-Mn}}, f_{\rm{Fe}}) = (0.73, 0.21, 0.042, 0.011, 0.0053, 0.0014, 0.0037)$

## Context

This work extends the shear acceleration framework previously applied to AGN jets (Rieger 2004, Ostrowski 1998) by performing detailed Monte Carlo simulations for a jet-cocoon geometry and connecting it to UHECR observations. It differs from the scenario of Murase (2011) for UHECR acceleration in blazar jets and from Caprioli (2015), which relies on first-encounter boost in relativistic jets with $\Gamma\sim30$, by considering mildly relativistic kiloparsec-scale jets of Fanaroff-Riley I radio galaxies. The model enables a self-consistent explanation of both the UHECR spectrum and mass composition without tuning the abundance ratio, and predicts a cosmogenic neutrino flux of $\sim 10^{-10}$ GeV cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$ sr$^{-1}$.
