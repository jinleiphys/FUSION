# Precision Physics at LEP

> 1 - Introduction 2 - Small-Angle Bhabha Scattering and the Luminosity Measurement 3 - Z^0 Physics 4 - Fits to Precision Data 5 - Physics at LEP2 6 - Conclusions

## Key claim

This review paper synthesizes the precision electroweak measurements performed at the LEP collider, demonstrating that the Standard Model successfully describes the data at the quantum-loop level, but the abstract's claim of "precision physics" glosses over the fact that the analysis is a retrospective review of LEP1 and LEP2 results rather than a presentation of new experimental data.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical and experimental status of precision electroweak observables at LEP, covering small-angle Bhabha scattering for luminosity determination, Z-boson resonance physics (line-shape parameters, asymmetries, and heavy-flavor results), global fits to precision data, and the physics program at LEP2 energies above the W-pair threshold. The paper compiles results from the four LEP experiments (ALEPH, DELPHI, L3, OPAL) and compares them with Standard Model predictions, including radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- Z-boson mass: M_Z = 91.1863 ± 0.0020 GeV from combined LEP measurements
- Z-boson total width: Γ_Z = 2.4948 ± 0.0025 GeV from combined LEP data
- Hadronic peak cross section: σ_had^0 = 41.486 ± 0.053 nb from LEP combination
- Number of light neutrino species: N_ν = 2.984 ± 0.008 from the invisible Z width
- Effective weak mixing angle: sin^2θ_eff^lept = 0.23157 ± 0.00018 from leptonic asymmetries
- W-boson mass at LEP2: M_W = 80.35 ± 0.09 GeV (preliminary LEP2 combination)

## Context

This review extends the precision electroweak program initiated at LEP1 (1989-1995) and continued at LEP2 (1996-2000), consolidating results from the four LEP experiments and theoretical work by groups such as Altarelli (1997) and Hollik (1996). The paper enables future comparisons with Tevatron and LHC measurements, and provides the foundation for testing the Standard Model at the quantum level through global fits that constrain the Higgs boson mass indirectly.
