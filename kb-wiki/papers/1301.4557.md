# Hadronic molecules for charmed and bottom baryons near thresholds

> We study hadronic molecules formed by a heavy meson and a nucleon, DN and D*N (BbarN and Bbar*N) systems. Respecting the heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry, we consider the DN-D*N (BbarN-Bbar*N$) mixing induced by the one boson exchange potential including the tensor force. We find many bound and resonant states with J^P=1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm and 7/2^- in isospin singlet channels, while only a few resonant states with J^P=1/2^- in isospin triplet channels. The analysis of DN and D*N (BbarN and Bbar*N) molecules will be useful to study mass spectra of excited charmed (bottom) baryons with large angular momenta, when their masses are close to the DN and D*N (BbarN and Bbar*N) thresholds.

## Key claim

The paper claims that many bound and resonant states of heavy meson-nucleon molecules (DN, D*N, BbarN, Bbar*N) exist near thresholds, but this result is conditional on the inclusion of the tensor force from one-pion exchange and the mixing of PN and P*N channels; without these, no bound or resonant states are found.

## Method

The authors constructed meson exchange potentials (pi, rho, omega) between a heavy meson (D, D*, Bbar, Bbar*) and a nucleon, respecting heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry. They solved coupled-channel Schrodinger equations for PN and P*N channels with quantum numbers J^P = 1/2^pm, 3/2^pm, 5/2^pm, and 7/2^pm for isospin I=0 and I=1, using monopole-type form factors with cutoff parameters determined from deuteron properties and quark model ratios.

## Key numbers

- For (I,J^P) = (0,1/2^-) in D*N: binding energy -14.4 MeV (pi potential) and -82.5 MeV (pi rho omega potential); relative radii 1.51 fm and 0.86 fm respectively
- For (I,J^P) = (0,1/2^-) in Bbar*N: binding energy -57.8 MeV (pi potential) and -145.9 MeV (pi rho omega potential); relative radii 0.99 fm and 0.76 fm respectively
- For (I,J^P) = (0,1/2^+) in D*N: resonance at 1.4 MeV with half-width 0.2 MeV (pi potential); bound state at -81.5 MeV with radius 0.85 fm (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (0,3/2^-) in D*N: resonance at 63.5 MeV with half-width 7.9 MeV (pi potential); bound state at -13.7 MeV with radius 0.89 fm (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (0,3/2^+) in Bbar*N: bound state at -2.6 MeV with radius 1.81 fm (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (0,5/2^+) in D*N: resonance at 160.8 MeV with half-width 3.1 MeV (pi potential); resonance at 137.0 MeV with half-width 7.6 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (1,1/2^-) in D*N: resonance at 147.2 MeV with half-width 105.5 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- For (I,J^P) = (1,1/2^-) in Bbar*N: resonance at 50.7 MeV with half-width 75.5 MeV (pi rho omega potential)
- Mass splitting: 140 MeV between D and D* mesons; 46 MeV between Bbar and Bbar* mesons
- Coupling constants: g_pi = -0.59, beta = 0.9, lambda = 0.59 GeV^-1 for heavy meson vertices; g^2_piNN/4pi = 13.6 for pion-nucleon coupling

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic molecules by the same authors (Yasui 2009, Yamaguchi 2011) from the anti-DN and BN systems to the DN and BbarN systems, and differs from other studies (Mizutani 2006, GarciaRecio 2008, Hofmann 2005, Haidenbauer 2010, He 2010, Zhang 2012) by focusing on states near thresholds with large angular momentum. The results enable experimental searches for heavy baryon molecular states at facilities such as FAIR, RHIC, LHC, and J-PARC, and provide predictions for excited charmed and bottom baryons whose masses lie near DN and D*N (BbarN and Bbar*N) thresholds.
