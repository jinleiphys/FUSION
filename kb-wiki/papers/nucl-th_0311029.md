# Three-body mechanism of $η$ production

> The usefulness of the three-body mechanism of $η$-meson production is tested by extending to high energies a recently developed two step model of the $p d \to ^3$He $η$ reaction by us, which was successful in reproducing the energy dependence of the data near threshold. The $η$ production in this model, proceeds in two steps via the $p p \to πd$ and $πN \to ηp$ reactions with the intermediate particles being off-shell. The final state interaction(FSI) is incorporated through a $T$-matrix for $η^3$He elastic scattering constructed using few body equations. In a comparison of the calculated angular distributions with some recently available data, we discover the limitations of the model at high energies.

## Key claim

The two-step (three-body) model of the $p d \to {}^3$He $\eta$ reaction, which successfully reproduces the energy dependence of the total cross section near threshold, fails to describe the forward-peaked angular distributions observed at higher beam energies (930–1100 MeV), producing instead backward-peaked cross sections that disagree with the data.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier two-step model, where $\eta$ production proceeds via $pp \to \pi d$ followed by $\pi N \to \eta p$ with off-shell intermediate particles, to higher beam energies (930, 965, 980, 1037, and 1100 MeV). The calculation includes the Fermi motion of nucleons, the off-shell nature of the intermediate pion, and the $\eta^3$He final-state interaction (FSI) via a $T$-matrix constructed from few-body equations. The $pp \to \pi d$ amplitudes are taken from a parametrization of experimental data (Arndt), and the $\pi N \to \eta p$ amplitudes use a coupled-channel model with $S_{11}$, $P_{11}$, and $D_{13}$ resonances.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer in the $p d \to {}^3$He $\eta$ reaction is approximately 900 MeV/c near threshold and remains large (about 650 MeV/c) even at 1 GeV above threshold.
- At a beam energy of 930 MeV, restricting the intermediate pion to forward angles ($0^\circ$ to $10^\circ$) reproduces the shape of the experimental angular distribution but underestimates the magnitude by about a factor of 40.
- The model calculations are compared with data at beam energies of 930, 965, 980, 1037, and 1100 MeV.
- The spin-averaged amplitude squared, defined as $|f|^2 = (k_p/k_\eta)(\sigma_{\text{tot}}/4\pi)$, shows excellent agreement with data near threshold but underestimates the data at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier two-step model (Khemchandani, Kelkar, Jain 2003) from threshold to higher energies, where it was previously successful in reproducing the energy dependence of the total cross section. The results contradict a recent claim by Stenmark (2003) that the two-step model can reproduce the forward-peaked angular distributions at high energies; the authors argue that Stenmark's calculation uses a factorization approximation that neglects off-shell effects and artificially restricts the intermediate pion to forward angles. The failure of the model points toward missing physics, possibly from intermediate-state interactions, coupled-channel processes like $p d \to {}^3$He $\pi^0$, or short-distance dynamics in the deuteron and $^3$He wave functions.
