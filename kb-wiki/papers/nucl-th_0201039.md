# Dynamical particle-phonon couplings in proton emission from spherical nuclei

> We present results of coupled-channels calculations which are performed to understand the role of vibrational excitations of the daughter nucleus in proton emission decay of spherical nuclei. It is shown that the experimental decay rates from the 2$d_{3/2}$, 3$s_{1/2}$ and 1$h_{11/2}$ states in proton emitting nuclei $^{160,161}$Re are reproduced simultaneously when the quadrupole phonon excitation of the core nuclei is taken into account. We apply the formalism also to the $^{145}$Tm nucleus and show that good agreement is achieved with the recently measured branching ratio for the fine structure.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including quadrupole vibrational excitations of the daughter nucleus in coupled-channels calculations can simultaneously reproduce the experimental decay half-lives for proton emission from the 2d₃/₂, 3s₁/₂, and 1h₁₁/₂ states in ¹⁶⁰,¹⁶¹Re, resolving a previous discrepancy where the 2d₃/₂ state was systematically underestimated by about 30% in models without such couplings.

## Method

The author solved coupled-channels equations for spherical proton emitters using a Green's function approach to compute decay rates of resonance states. The Hamiltonian included a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential, spin-orbit interaction, Coulomb potential, and a coupling term for vibrational core excitations described by a dynamical deformation parameter βλ. The coupling was treated to all orders for the nuclear term and to linear order for Coulomb and spin-orbit terms, with only single-phonon excitations included.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶⁰,¹⁶¹Re, the three decay half-lives (2d₃/₂, 3s₁/₂, 1h₁₁/₂) are simultaneously reproduced with dynamical deformation parameter β₂ ∼ 0.16
- The first 2⁺ state in the core nucleus ¹⁶⁰W was observed at ħω₂ = 610 keV
- For the 2d₃/₂ state in ¹⁶⁰Re, the no-coupling calculation was off from experimental data by about 30%
- For ¹⁴⁵Tm, the measured ground state decay half-life was 3.1 ± 0.3 μs
- The measured branching ratio for fine structure in ¹⁴⁵Tm was 9.6 ± 1.5%
- For ¹⁴⁵Tm, the ground state half-life and branching ratio are simultaneously reproduced if β₂ > 0.22
- At β₂ = 0.18, the |f₇/₂ ⊗ 2⁺⟩ state admixes 1.08% in probability with the main |h₁₁/₂ ⊗ 0⁺⟩ state

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels formalism previously applied to deformed proton emitters (Esbensen and Davids 2000; Kruppa et al. 2000; Maglione et al. 1998) to spherical nuclei, addressing a known discrepancy where calculations without structure systematically underestimated decay half-lives for 2d₃/₂ states (Davids et al. 1997; Bingham et al. 1999). The approach is analogous to similar treatments of vibrational excitations in heavy-ion subbarrier fusion reactions (Dasgupta et al. 1998; Stelson 1995). The formalism enables extraction of dynamical deformation parameters from proton emission data and was applied to the fine structure in ¹⁴⁵Tm recently observed at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (Rykaczewski 2001), following earlier analysis by Davids and Esbensen (2001).
