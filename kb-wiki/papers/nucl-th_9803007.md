# Electromagnetic properties of off-shell particles and gauge invariance

> Electromagnetic properties of off-shell particles are discussed on the basis of a purely electromagnetic reaction: virtual Compton scattering off a proton. It is shown that the definition of off-shell electromagnetic form factors is not gauge invariant and that these cannot be investigated in practice. Using only fundamental requirements of gauge invariance it is demonstrated that off-shell effects are cancelled in the longitudinal components of the total conserved current by the minimal contact current, while the off-shellness appears only in the the transverse (gauge invariant) non-pole part. This provides the possibility to introduce an on-shell extrapolated form factor F_1^+(q^2) in a gauge invariant way for the unphysical time-like region, 4m_e^2<q^2, 4M_N^2.

## Key claim
The paper argues that off-shell electromagnetic form factors cannot be defined in a gauge-invariant way and therefore cannot be investigated in practice, even when the half-off-shell vertex satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. However, by using a minimal substitution scheme, off-shell effects can be completely cancelled in the longitudinal components of the total conserved current by the contact current, leaving only a gauge-invariant transverse piece that allows an on-shell extrapolated form factor to be introduced for the unphysical time-like region.

## Method
The authors use a field-theoretical approach based on minimal substitution of the gauge field into the three-point Green function for virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off a proton. They construct the total conserved nucleon current from reducible vertices and free Feynman propagators, then decompose it into on-shell Born, gauge-invariant regular, and non-gauge-invariant regular pieces. By analytically integrating the contact current derived from the minimal insertion, they show it exactly cancels the non-gauge-invariant part, leaving a conserved current composed only of gauge-invariant terms.

## Key numbers
- The off-shell form factor expansion is given as \( F_1^{+}(q^2, p^2) = F_1^{+}(q^2, M^2) + \frac{q^2}{M^2} \frac{p^2 - M^2}{M^2} A^{+}(q^2, p^2) \), where \( A^{+}(q^2, p^2) \) contains all off-shell effects.
- At the photon point (\( q^2 = 0 \)), gauge invariance demands \( F_1^{\pm}(q^2 = 0, p^2) \equiv 1 \).
- The non-gauge-invariant regular current \( J_{\mu \nu}^{\text{non-GI}} \) is exactly cancelled by the contact current \( J_{\mu \nu}^{C} = - J_{\mu \nu}^{\text{non-GI}} \), as shown in equation (jc2).
- The total conserved current reduces to \( J_{\mu \nu}^{N} = J_{\mu \nu}^{B\text{-on}} + J_{\mu \nu}^{GI} \), where both terms are gauge invariant individually.
- For real Compton scattering (\( q^2 = 0 \)), off-shell effects vanish: \( J_{\mu \nu}^{GI}(q^2=0) = J_{\mu \nu}^{\text{non-GI}}(q^2=0) = 0 \).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of off-shell form factors by Bincer (dispersion relations) and one-loop calculations by various authors, which suggested model dependence and potential influence on observables in electron-nucleus scattering. It contrasts with previous VCS studies (e.g., those by Scherer and collaborators in the 1990s) that transferred all off-shell effects to the regular part parametrized as generalized polarizabilities. The paper enables a gauge-invariant definition of on-shell extrapolated form factors in the unphysical time-like region (\( 4m_e^2 < q^2 < 4M_N^2 \)), which can be probed via the C-odd \( e^+ e^- \) asymmetry in di-lepton photoproduction off a proton.
