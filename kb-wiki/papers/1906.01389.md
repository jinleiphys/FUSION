# Analytical forms of wave function and form factors of deuteron

> On the received coefficients of the analytical forms for deuteron wave function in coordinate representation in form r^(l+1)*exp(-A*r) for the modern realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93, Reid93 and Argonne v18 are calculated charge GC(p), quadrupole GQ(p) and magnetic GM(p) deuteron form factors. An original dipole fit for proton and neutron isoscalar electric and magnetic form factors is used for calculations. Theoretical calculations of values of the deuteron form factor are compared with their experimental data of world collaborations (Bates, BLAST, Bonn, JLab, Mainz, Naval Research Lab, NIKHEF, Orsay, Saclay, SLAC, Stanford, VEPP3 and VEPP4) and reviews (Abbott, Boden, Garcon and Karpius). The change of the sign of form factors is in the pulse regions at 4.7-4.9 fm-1 for GC; at 12.8-14.7 fm-1 for GQ; at 6.3-8.1 and 11.4-12.2 fm-1 for GM. The theoretical values for the form factors GQ(0) and GM(0) were calculated at the boundary condition for the momentum at p2=0. Calculated positions of the zero for deuteron form factors are compared with values for other potential models. The peculiarities of parametrizations of deuteron form factors for theoretical approaches and fits of theoretical calculations to experimental values, which are described in the cited literature, are analyzed. Formulas for spherical and quadrupole form factors are writed, which are expressed in terms of the coefficients for the indicated deuteron wave function. At large values of momentum the asymptotics of the deuteron form factors are determined by the coefficients of the analytical forms for deuteron wave function, nucleon isoscalar form factors and the order of momentum in the denominator. The further use of deuteron form factors for obtaining polarization observables in processes involving deuteron as a projectile is discussed.

# Analytical forms of wave function and form factors of deuteron

## Key claim
Using previously obtained analytical forms of the deuteron wave function in coordinate representation for five modern realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93, Reid93, Argonne v18), the charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors are calculated and compared with experimental data from multiple world collaborations; the theoretical values agree with experiment only within certain momentum regions, and the sign changes of the form factors occur in specific pulse intervals that depend on the potential model used.

## Method
The author used analytical deuteron wave functions of the form u(r) = r * sum_i A_i exp(-a_i r) and w(r) = r^3 * sum_i B_i exp(-b_i r) with N=11 terms, previously fitted to numerical wave functions from the Nijmegen group potentials and Argonne v18. The spherical and quadrupole form factors S_0^(i) and S_2^(i) were computed analytically from these wave functions using spherical Bessel functions, then combined with an original dipole fit for proton and neutron isoscalar electric and magnetic form factors to obtain the deuteron charge G_C, quadrupole G_Q, and magnetic G_M form factors.

## Key numbers
- Sign change regions for form factors: G_C at 4.7-4.9 fm^-1; G_Q at 12.8-14.7 fm^-1; G_M at 6.3-8.1 fm^-1 and 11.4-12.2 fm^-1
- G_M(0) values: 1.6941 (NijmI), 1.6943 (NijmII), 1.6929 (Nijm93), 1.6936 (Reid93), 1.6929 (Argonne v18); experimental value 1.7135
- G_Q(0) values: 24.351 (NijmI), 24.214 (NijmII), 24.056 (Nijm93), 23.947 (Reid93), 24.210 (Argonne v18); experimental value 25.833
- Zero position of G_C: 4.80 fm^-1 (NijmI), 4.60 fm^-1 (NijmII), 4.81 fm^-1 (Nijm93), 4.60 fm^-1 (Reid93), 4.71 fm^-1 (Argonne v18); experimental value 4.30 +/- 0.16 fm^-1
- Zero position of G_Q: 12.4 fm^-1 (NijmI), 12.2 fm^-1 (NijmII), 13.8 fm^-1 (Nijm93), 12.8 fm^-1 (Reid93), 12.6 fm^-1 (Argonne v18)
- Zero positions of G_M: first zero at 8.0, 6.5, 7.4, 6.9, 6.8 fm^-1 and second zero at 14.7, 14.3, 15.3, 14.9, 13.5 fm^-1 for NijmI, NijmII, Nijm93, Reid93, Argonne v18 respectively
- Asymptotic momentum dependence: G_C ~ 1/p^8, G_Q ~ 1/p^12, G_M ~ 1/p^8 at large momentum

## Context
This work extends the analytical parametrization of deuteron wave functions previously developed by Zhaba (Zhaba1, Zhaba2) and applies them to compute form factors for the Nijmegen group potentials (Stoks 1994) and Argonne v18 (Wiringa 1995). The results are compared with experimental data from collaborations including Bates, BLAST, JLab, NIKHEF, Orsay, Stanford, VEPP3, and VEPP4, as well as reviews by Abbott, Boden, Garcon, and Karpius. The analytical forms enable further calculation of polarization observables such as tensor polarizations t_2j, vector polarizations t_1i, analyzing power A_yy, and spin correlation coefficients for elastic lepton-deuteron scattering, as well as theoretical values for the charge and magnetic radii of the deuteron.
