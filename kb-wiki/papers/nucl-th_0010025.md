# Dynamical Study of the Delta Excitation in N(e,e' pi) Reactions

> The dynamical model developed in [Phys. Rev. C 54, 2660 (1996)] has been applied to investigate the pion electroproduction reactions on the nucleon. It is found that the model can describe to a very large extent the recent data of p(e,e' pi^0) reaction from Jefferson Laboratory and MIT-Bates. The extracted magnetic dipole(M1), electric dipole(E2), and Coulomb(C2) strengths of the gamma N -> Delta transition are presented. It is found that the C2/M1 ratio drops significantly with Q^2 and reaches about -13% at Q^2=4 (GeV/c)^2, while the E2/M1 ratio remains close to the value \sim -3 % at the Q^2=0 photon point. The determined M1 transition form factor drops faster than the usual dipole form factor of the proton. We also find that the non-resonant interactions can dress the gamma N -> Delta vertex to enhance strongly its strength at low Q^2, but much less at high Q^2. Predictions are presented for future experimental tests. Possible developments of the model are discussed.

# Dynamical Study of the Delta Excitation in N(e,e' pi) Reactions

## Key claim

The dynamical model can describe the recent p(e,e' pi^0) data from Jefferson Laboratory and MIT-Bates to a very large extent, but the model's predictions at higher energies (W=1.292 GeV) disagree with data, indicating the need to include additional reaction mechanisms such as gamma N -> pi Delta -> pi N transitions and higher mass nucleon resonances.

## Method

The authors extended the SL dynamical model (Sato and Lee, 1996) from pion photoproduction to pion electroproduction by including form factors at each photon vertex, using a unitary transformation method to derive an effective Hamiltonian in the pi N, gamma N, and Delta subspace. The model solves coupled equations for pi N scattering and gamma N reactions, separating non-resonant mechanisms from the bare Delta excitation, and applies a gauge invariance prescription to define conserved currents. The bare gamma N -> Delta form factors G_M, G_E, and G_C were determined by fitting to photoproduction data at Q^2=0 and then to JLab electroproduction data at Q^2=2.8 and 4 (GeV/c)^2.

## Key numbers

- G_M(0)=1.85 and G_E(0)=0.025 for the bare gamma N -> Delta vertex, identical to values determined in the earlier SL model (1996)
- G_C(0)=-0.209 for the bare charge form factor, derived from the long wavelength limit using G_E(0)=+0.025
- The C2/M1 ratio (R_SM) for the dressed gamma N -> Delta transition reaches about -13% at Q^2=4 (GeV/c)^2
- The E2/M1 ratio (R_EM) for the dressed transition remains close to -2.7% at Q^2=0 and stays near -3% across the considered Q^2 region
- The bare M1 form factor G_M(Q^2) was parameterized with R_alpha(Q^2)=(1+aQ^2)exp(-bQ^2) where a=0.154 and b=0.166 (GeV/c)^2, using the dipole form factor G_D(Q^2)=1/(1+Q^2/0.71)^2
- At the resonance energy E_gamma=340 MeV, the bare Delta amplitude is about 60% of the full amplitude for the M1 transition and almost half for the E2 transition

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (1996) from pion photoproduction to pion electroproduction, building on previous approaches including the tree-diagram effective Lagrangian methods (MAID model) and dispersion-relation analyses. The key advance is the ability to separate non-resonant mechanisms from the bare Delta excitation, showing that the non-resonant interactions dress the gamma N -> Delta vertex to enhance its strength strongly at low Q^2 but much less at high Q^2. This enables future experimental tests at higher Q^2 to probe the bare quark core structure, which can be compared with constituent quark model predictions (Capstick, 1992; Bilger, 1993; Bourdeau, 1992) and with the empirical values listed by the Particle Data Group.
