# Heavy mesons in dense matter

> Charmed mesons in dense matter are studied within a unitary coupled-channel approach which takes into account Pauli-blocking effects and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. We obtain the open-charm meson spectral functions in this dense medium, and discuss their implications on hidden charm and charm scalar resonances and on the formation of D-mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of open-charm mesons (D and D*) is studied using a self-consistent coupled-channel approach that includes Pauli blocking and meson self-energies, but the actual numerical results depend strongly on whether one uses a broken SU(4) model or an SU(8) scheme incorporating heavy-quark symmetry, with the latter predicting only weakly bound D-mesic nuclei rather than the strongly bound states found in earlier work.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for D and D* mesons interacting with nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter, using two different bare interactions: a broken SU(4) s-wave Weinberg-Tomozawa potential supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term, and an SU(8) Weinberg-Tomozawa potential that treats heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons on equal footing in accordance with heavy-quark symmetry. The in-medium solution incorporates Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner, from which the meson spectral functions are computed. For the scalar resonances D_s0(2317) and X(3700), the D meson self-energy from the SU(4) model (without the phenomenological isoscalar-scalar term but with p-wave self-energy) is used as input to the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for two pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀), the D_s0(2317) resonance develops a width of order 100 MeV, up from zero width in vacuum.
- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀), the X(3700) hidden charm scalar resonance develops a width of order 200 MeV, up from a small width in vacuum.
- D^0-nucleus bound states are found to be weakly bound in the SU(8) scheme, in contrast to previous predictions of stronger binding by Tsushima (1999).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent coupled-channel approach previously applied to open-charm mesons by Tolos (2007) and Tolos (2009), and builds on the SU(8) heavy-quark symmetry framework developed by Magas (2009) and Gamermann (2010). The results contradict the earlier prediction of strongly bound D-mesic nuclei by Tsushima (1999), and suggest that experimental detection of such states will be challenging. The study enables future experimental searches at the PANDA and CBM experiments at FAIR, and proposes using transparency ratios to measure the in-medium widths of the D_s0(2317) and X(3700) resonances.
