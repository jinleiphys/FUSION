# Theoretical predictions of transverse kinematic imbalance in neutrino-nucleus interactions

> Distributions of transverse kinematic imbalance in neutrino-nucleus interactions in the few GeV regime are sensitive to nuclear effects. We present a study comparing the latest predictions of transverse kinematic imbalance from the interaction simulations, NuWro and GENIE. We dis- cuss the differences between the model predictions.

## Key claim

The paper compares predictions from two neutrino interaction simulations, NuWro and GENIE, for transverse kinematic imbalance observables in the few GeV regime, finding that the nominal GENIE simulation predicts a very sharp back-to-back peak in the transverse angle distribution that is not present when hadronic re-interactions are disabled, and that removing events with elastic proton final-state interactions makes the GENIE predictions more similar to NuWro.

## Method

The authors generated predictions using GENIE 2.10.0 with the nominal hA final-state interaction (FSI) model and NuWro 11q, using the NuMI on-axis neutrino and antineutrino flux shapes. They compared single-transverse kinematic imbalance variables (delta-pT, delta-alpha-T, delta-phi-T) and the double-transverse variable delta-pTT for quasi-elastic scattering and resonance production channels, including studies with FSI disabled and with elastic proton FSI events removed.

## Key numbers

- In the nominal GENIE simulation, events with a final state proton that underwent an elastic interaction amount to about 40% of quasi-elastic interactions at the NuMI beam energy.
- The GENIE hA elastic FSI model causes strong deceleration for lepton transverse momentum greater than approximately 200 MeV/c.
- A stronger FSI in GENIE is inferred by its higher proportion of events with delta-pT greater than approximately 250 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse kinematic imbalance observables introduced in prior work by the same authors (Pickering and Lu, 2015) and by Lu (2015), which showed that single-transverse imbalances have reduced dependence on neutrino energy and that double-transverse imbalance provides a method for reconstructing neutrino energy spectra independent of nuclear effects. The predictions are compared to recent measurements by the MINERvA collaboration, which found good agreement between data and an older GENIE simulation (version 2.6.2) that did not contain an elastic FSI component. Measurements of these observables are underway and should offer separation power among models of nuclear effects, leading to reduced systematic uncertainty for future neutrino cross-section and oscillation measurements.
