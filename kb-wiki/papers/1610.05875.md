# Surface tension of highly magnetized degenerate quark matter

> We study the surface tension of highly magnetized three flavor quark matter within the formalism of multiple reflection expansion (MRE). Quark matter is described as a mixture of free Fermi gases composed by quarks $u$, $d$, $s$ and electrons, in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions. Due to the presence of strong magnetic fields the particles' transverse motion is quantized into Landau levels, and the surface tension has a different value in the parallel and transverse directions with respect to the magnetic field. We calculate the transverse and longitudinal surface tension for different values of the magnetic field and for quark matter drops with different sizes, from a few fm to the bulk limit. For baryon number densities between $2-10$ times the nuclear saturation density, the surface tension falls in the range $2 - 20$ MeV /fm$^{2}$. The largest contribution comes from strange quarks which have a surface tension an order of magnitude larger than the one for $u$ or $d$ quarks and more than two orders of magnitude larger than for electrons. Our results show that the total surface tension is quite insensitive to the size of the drop. We also find that the surface tensions in the transverse and parallel directions are almost unaffected by the magnetic field if $eB$ is below $\sim 5 \times 10^{-3} $ GeV$^2$. Nevertheless, for higher values of $eB$, there is a significant increase in the parallel surface tension and a significant decrease in the transverse one with respect to the unmagnetized case.

## Key claim
The surface tension of magnetized three-flavor quark matter becomes anisotropic, with distinct parallel and transverse components relative to the magnetic field direction, but this anisotropy only becomes significant when the magnetic field strength exceeds approximately \(5 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2\); below this threshold the surface tension is essentially isotropic and unaffected by the field.

## Method
The authors model quark matter as a mixture of free Fermi gases of u, d, s quarks and electrons in chemical equilibrium under weak interactions, with charge neutrality enforced locally. Finite-size effects for quark matter droplets are included via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism, which modifies the density of states with a surface term. The magnetic field quantizes transverse particle motion into Landau levels, and the surface tension is computed separately for the direction parallel and transverse to the field by integrating the MRE-modified pressure expressions over the occupied Landau levels.

## Key numbers
- For baryon number densities between 2 and 10 times nuclear saturation density, the total surface tension falls in the range 2 – 20 MeV/fm\(^2\).
- The strange quark contribution to the surface tension can be as large as \(\sim 20\) MeV/fm\(^2\), which is an order of magnitude larger than for u or d quarks and more than two orders of magnitude larger than for electrons.
- The surface tensions in the transverse and parallel directions are almost unaffected by the magnetic field if \(eB\) is below \(\sim 5 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2\).
- For \(eB = 5 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^2\), the difference in surface tension with respect to the unmagnetized case is in the range \(\sim 20-200\%\) for typical compact star densities.
- The u and d quark surface tension never exceeds \(\sim 3\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
- The electron surface tension is below \(\sim 0.15\) MeV/fm\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of quark matter surface tension using the MRE formalism (Lugones 2011, Lugones 2013) by incorporating the effects of strong magnetic fields, which are relevant for magnetars and heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with the geometric approach of Garcia and Pinto (2013), who studied magnetized two-flavor quark matter within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and found a surface tension \(\sim 30\) MeV/fm\(^2\) for unmagnetized matter, significantly larger than the MIT bag model values reported here. The results enable improved modeling of the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars, where the critical surface tension for a sharp interface versus a mixed phase is around 65 MeV/fm\(^2\) (Voskresensky 2003), and of quark matter nucleation in magnetar cores.
