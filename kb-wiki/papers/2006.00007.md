# Effects of Longitudinal Short-Distance Constraints on the Hadronic Light-by-Light Contribution to the Muon $g-2$

> We present a model-independent method to estimate the effects of short-distance constraints (SDCs) on the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment $a_μ^\text{HLbL}$. The relevant loop integral is evaluated using multi-parameter families of interpolation functions, which satisfy by construction all constraints derived from general principles and smoothly connect the low-energy region with those where either two or all three independent photon virtualities become large. In agreement with other recent model-based analyses, we find that the SDCs and thus the infinite towers of heavy intermediate states that are responsible for saturating them have a rather small effect on $a_μ^\text{HLbL}$. Taking as input the known ground-state pseudoscalar pole contributions, we obtain that the longitudinal SDCs increase $a_μ^\text{HLbL}$ by $(9.1\pm 5.0) \times 10^{-11}$, where the isovector channel is responsible for $(2.6\pm 1.5) \times 10^{-11}$. More precise estimates can be obtained with our method as soon as further accurate, model-independent information about important low-energy contributions from hadronic states with masses up to 1-2 GeV become available.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent method to estimate the effects of longitudinal short-distance constraints (SDCs) on the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon \(g-2\), finding that these constraints increase \(a_\mu^\text{HLbL}\) by \((9.1\pm 5.0) \times 10^{-11}\) when ground-state pseudoscalar poles are used as low-energy input. The authors emphasize that this result is contingent on the assumption that low-energy contributions from states with masses up to 1–2 GeV are accurately known, and that the quoted uncertainty is dominated by the choice of the matching surface rather than by the asymptotic constraints themselves.

## Method

The authors construct multi-parameter families of interpolation functions for the longitudinal scalar function \(\bar{\Pi}_1(Q_1^2, Q_2^2, Q_3^2)\) that satisfy operator product expansion (OPE) and perturbative QCD (pQCD) constraints at high virtualities and are matched to a low-energy representation given by the \(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), and \(\eta'\) pole contributions at a defined matching surface \(\Sigma^\text{match}(r,\phi)\). The interpolation functions are built by multiplying an asymptotic form (derived from the quark-loop result and OPE constraints) by either a polynomial in \(\Sigma^{-1}\) or its inverse, or by a form including a logarithmic term, with coefficients fixed by requiring continuity of the function and its first \(N-1\) derivatives at the matching surface for each set of angular variables \((r,\phi)\).

## Key numbers

* The total increase in \(a_\mu^\text{HLbL}\) from longitudinal SDCs, using ground-state pseudoscalar poles as low-energy input, is \((9.1\pm 5.0) \times 10^{-11}\).
* The isovector channel contributes \((2.6\pm 1.5) \times 10^{-11}\) to this increase.
* The reference result for the isovector channel alone is \(\Delta a_\pi[\text{ref}] = 2.56 \times 10^{-11}\), obtained with the dispersive \(\pi^0\) transition form factor (TFF) from PionTFF (2019) and PionTFFshort (2018), using interpolant 1 with \(N=3\) and a matching scale \(m_\text{ref}^2 = 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The uncertainty from the pion TFF input is asymmetric: \(\delta^+_\text{TFF} = 0.06 \times 10^{-11}\) and \(\delta^-_\text{TFF} = 0.13 \times 10^{-11}\).
* The uncertainty from the fit to the quark-loop result (choice of polynomial degree and fitting radius) is \(\delta_\text{pQCD fit} = 0.09 \times 10^{-11}\).
* The shift from including NLO OPE corrections is at most \(0.05 \times 10^{-11}\) in magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for HLbL developed by Colangelo et al. (2014–2017) and Hoferichter et al. (2014–2018) by providing a method to incorporate longitudinal SDCs without relying on specific hadronic models. It agrees with the model-based estimates of BernSDCShort and BernSDCLong (2019–2020) and finds a significantly smaller effect than the earlier model of Melnikov and Vainshtein (2004), especially when up-to-date TFF input is used. The method enables future improvements by incorporating model-independent information on intermediate states with masses up to 1–2 GeV and can be generalized to transversal SDCs, paving the way for a fully data-driven estimate of the HLbL contribution to the muon \(g-2\).
