# Relating $D^* \bar{D}^*$ currents with $J^π= 0^+,1^+$ and $2^+$ to $Z_c$ states

> In this work we study the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ current with QCD sum rules. We write the correlation function using the general current corresponding to the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ system and then use spin projectors to obtain the correlation function in the $0^+, 1^+$ and $2^+$ spin-parity configurations. The purpose of the present work is to investigate the possibility of explaining the recently reported $Z_c(4025)$ as a $D^* \bar{D}^*$ moleculelike state. As a result we find a state for each spin case with a very similar mass: $M^{S=0} = \left(3943 \pm 104\right)$ MeV, $M^{S=1} = \left(3950 \pm 105\right)$ MeV, $M^{S=2} = \left(3946 \pm 104 \right)$ MeV. We discuss that our mass results, within error bars, for $1^+$ or $2^+$ are both compatible with $Z_c(4025)$. However, our results are also compatible with a possible $D^* \bar{D}^*$ bound state, in agreement with predictions of some previous works. We have also calculated the current-state coupling which turns out to be larger in the $2^+$ case.

## Key claim

The authors use QCD sum rules to study whether the recently observed $Z_c(4025)$ state can be interpreted as a $D^* \bar{D}^*$ moleculelike state, finding that both $1^+$ and $2^+$ spin-parity assignments are compatible with the experimental mass within error bars, but the results are also compatible with a possible $D^* \bar{D}^*$ bound state below threshold.

## Method

The authors construct a general interpolating current for the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ system and apply spin projectors to isolate the $0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$ components of the correlation function. They perform an operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension six, including perturbative, quark condensate, gluon condensate, mixed condensate, four-quark condensate, and triple gluon condensate contributions. The Borel transformed sum rules are then used to extract masses and current-state couplings for each spin-parity configuration.

## Key numbers

- Mass for $0^+$ state: $M^{S=0} = (3943 \pm 104)$ MeV
- Mass for $1^+$ state: $M^{S=1} = (3950 \pm 105)$ MeV
- Mass for $2^+$ state: $M^{S=2} = (3946 \pm 104)$ MeV
- Coupling for $0^+$ state: $\lambda^{S=0} = (17 \pm 4) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- Coupling for $1^+$ state: $\lambda^{S=1} = (30 \pm 6) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- Coupling for $2^+$ state: $\lambda^{S=2} = (39 \pm 8) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- The experimental $Z_c(4025)$ mass is $4026.3 \pm 2.6 \pm 3.7$ MeV, as reported by the BES collaboration
- Continuum threshold taken as $\sqrt{s_0} \sim 4.5$ GeV
- Charm quark mass used: $m_c = 1.23 \pm 0.05$ GeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ system, such as those by Chen (2013) and Cui (2013) who studied $1^+$ currents, and by Qiao (2013) who investigated tetraquark currents with $2^+$ spin-parity. The results are also compared with effective field theory approaches by Guo (2013) and by Martinez Torres and collaborators (2012), who predicted a $2^+$ bound state in the $D^* \bar{D}^*$ system with mass between 3900-3965 MeV. The findings enable further experimental clarification of whether $Z_c(4025)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ are distinct states or the same resonance seen in different decay channels.
