# Interpretations of the new LHCb $P_c(4337)^+$ pentaquark state

> Recently the LHCb collaboration has observed a new pentaquark state, the $P_c(4337)^+$. Owing to its proximity to the $χ_{c0}(1S) p$, $\bar{D}^* Λ_c$, $\bar{D} Σ_c$ and $\bar{D} Σ_c^*$ thresholds, this new pentaquark might very well be a meson-baryon bound state. However its spin and parity have not been determined yet and none of the previous possibilities can be ruled out. We briefly explore a few of these options and the consequences they entail in the present manuscript: (i) the $P_c(4337)^+$ might be a $χ_{c0}(1S) p$ bound state, (ii) the $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ might be $\bar{D}^* Λ_c$ and $\bar{D} Σ_c$ states close to threshold, respectively, where the Breit-Wigner mass might not correspond to the location of the poles, (iii) the locations of the $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4337)^+$ might be explained in terms of the $\bar{D}^* Λ_c$-$\bar{D} Σ_c$ and $\bar{D}^* Λ_c$-$\bar{D} Σ_c^*$ coupled channel dynamics. This last option, though not the most probable explanation, is still potentially compatible with the double peak solution of the $P_{cs}(4459)^0$ and with what we know of the $P_c(4312)^+$. As a byproduct of the previous explorations, we conjecture the existence of a series of anticharmed meson - antitriplet charmed baryon bound states and calculate their masses.

## Key claim

The paper explores three possible interpretations for the newly observed LHCb pentaquark \(P_c(4337)^+\), each with specific conditions: it might be a \(\chi_{c0}(1S) p\) hadrocharmonium bound state, a shallow \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\) molecular state where the Breit-Wigner mass may not correspond to the actual pole location, or a pole arising from \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\)-\(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\) or \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\)-\(\bar{D} \Sigma_c^*\) coupled channel dynamics, though the authors note the last option is not the most probable explanation.

## Method

The authors apply effective field theory (EFT) and one-boson-exchange (OBE) models to analyze the \(P_c(4337)^+\) as a meson-baryon bound state, considering its proximity to the \(\chi_{c0}(1S) p\), \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\), \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\), and \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c^*\) thresholds. They examine three scenarios: hadrocharmonium formation via two-gluon exchange, shallow bound states where Breit-Wigner masses misrepresent pole locations, and coupled channel dynamics within a contact-range EFT potential. The analysis includes power counting arguments to assess the relevance of pion exchanges and coupled channel effects at leading order.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(P_c(4337)^+\): \(4337^{+7}_{-4} {}^{+2}_{-2}\) MeV, width: \(29^{+26}_{-12} {}^{+14}_{-14}\) MeV, with statistical significance \(3.1-3.7\sigma\) depending on \(J^P\) assignment.
- Mass difference between \(P_c(4337)^+\) and \(P_c(4312)^+\): approximately 25 MeV, which coincides with the mass difference between the \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\) and \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\) thresholds: \(M(\bar{D} \Sigma_c) - M(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c) = 25.8\) MeV.
- For the \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\)-\(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\) coupled channel system, the ratio \(B_2 / \Delta_{CC} \sim 0.35\) for the \(P_c(4312)\) interpretation as a \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\) bound state about 9 MeV below threshold.
- For the \(\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c\)-\(\bar{D} \Sigma_c^*\) system, the EFT expansion parameter \(Q/M \sim 0.42-0.44\) and the coupled channel ratio \(|E_2|/\Delta_{CC} \sim 0.54-0.62\).
- Mass of \(P_c(4312)^+\): \(4311.9 \pm 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}\) MeV, width: \(9.8 \pm 2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5}\) MeV.
- For the \(P_{cs}(4459)^0\) double peak solution: \(M(P_{cs1}) = 4454.9 \pm 2.7\) MeV, \(\Gamma(P_{cs1}) = 7.5 \pm 9.7\) MeV; \(M(P_{cs2}) = 4467.8 \pm 3.7\) MeV, \(\Gamma(P_{cs2}) = 5.3 \pm 5.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of pentaquarks previously applied to the \(P_c(4312)^+\), \(P_c(4440)^+\), and \(P_c(4457)^+\) observed by LHCb (2019) and the \(P_{cs}(4459)^0\) (2020), proposing that the new \(P_c(4337)^+\) may fit into the same framework. The hadrocharmonium explanation builds on earlier work by Eides et al. (2015, 2017, 2019) on \(\chi_{c0} p\) binding via chromopolarizability, while the coupled channel analysis parallels the treatment of the \(P_{cs}(4459)^0\) double peak structure by Peng et al. (2020). The paper enables future experimental determination of the \(P_c(4337)^+\) spin and parity to discriminate between these interpretations, and predicts a series of anticharmed meson - antitriplet charmed baryon bound states as a byproduct.
