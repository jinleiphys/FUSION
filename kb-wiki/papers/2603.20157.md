# Detecting the 3D Ising model phase transition with a ground-state-trained autoencoder

> We develop a one-class, deep-learning framework to detect the phase transition and recover critical behavior of the 3D Ising model. A 3D convolutional neural network autoencoder (CAE) is trained on ground-state configurations only, without prior knowledge of the critical temperature, the Hamiltonian, or the order parameter. After training, the model is applied to Monte Carlo configurations across a wide temperature range and different lattice sizes. The mean-square reconstruction error is shown to be sensitive to the transition. Finite-size scaling of the peak location for the reconstruction error susceptibility yields the critical temperature $T_c=4.5128(58)$ and the correlation-length critical exponent $ν=0.63(27)$, consistent with results from the literature. Our results show that a one-class CAE, trained on zero-temperature configurations only, can recover nontrivial critical behavior of the 3D Ising model.

## Key claim
A one-class convolutional autoencoder trained exclusively on ground-state (T=0) configurations of the 3D Ising model, without any knowledge of the Hamiltonian, critical temperature, or order parameter, can detect the phase transition and recover the critical temperature and correlation-length exponent through finite-size scaling of the reconstruction error susceptibility, though the exponent uncertainty is large (43% relative).

## Method
The authors trained a 3D convolutional autoencoder (CAE) on 2000 ground-state configurations (1000 fully spin-up and 1000 fully spin-down) of the 3D Ising model. After training, the frozen CAE was applied to Monte Carlo configurations generated across 84 temperatures in the range T∈[3.0,6.0] for lattice sizes L=50 to L=130, with 3000-4000 configurations per temperature. The mean-square reconstruction error (MSE) between input and reconstructed configurations was computed, and its susceptibility χ_MSE = (V/T)(⟨MSE²⟩−⟨MSE⟩²) was used to define pseudocritical temperatures T_c(L) from peak locations, which were then fit to the finite-size scaling relation T_c(L)=T_c+AL^{-1/ν}.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature extracted: T_c = 4.5128(58), compared to literature weighted average of 4.511535(31)
- Correlation-length critical exponent: ν = 0.63(27), compared to literature weighted average of 0.62999(34)
- The fit yielded χ²/dof = 1.16
- Lattice sizes studied: L=50 to L=130, with 84 temperatures probed in the range T∈[3.0,6.0]
- Training used 2000 ground-state configurations; evaluation used 3000-4000 configurations per temperature per lattice size

## Context
This work extends the minimal-training approach from the 2D Ising model studied by Abuali (2025) to the 3D Ising model, moving from a supervised binary classifier to an unsupervised autoencoder. Prior machine-learning studies of the 3D Ising model remain comparatively sparse compared to the 2D case, with work by Kim (2018), Chng (2018), Zhang (2019), Li (2021), Guo (2023), Panda (2023), and Jang (2025) representing the main contributions. The results demonstrate that one-class training regimens can extract critical temperatures with per-mille precision and provide rough estimates of critical exponents, suggesting applicability to systems where the order parameter is unknown or difficult to access, such as quantum chromodynamics and other complex many-body systems.
