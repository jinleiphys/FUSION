# Persistence of charmed hadrons in QGP from lattice QCD

> We study the nature of charm degrees of freedom near the chiral crossover based on lattice QCD calculations of the second and fourth-order cumulants of charm fluctuations, and their correlations with net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness fluctuations. We show that below the chiral crossover temperature, $T_{pc}$, the thermodynamics of charm can be very well understood in terms of charmed hadrons. We present evidence that above $T_{pc}$, however, charm quark-like excitations emerge as new degrees of freedom contributing to the partial charm pressure. Nonetheless, up to temperatures as high as 175 MeV, charmed hadron-like excitations significantly contribute to the partial charm pressure.

## Key claim

Below the chiral crossover temperature \(T_{pc}\), charm thermodynamics is fully described by charmed hadrons; above \(T_{pc}\), charm quark-like excitations appear, but charmed hadron-like excitations still contribute significantly to the partial charm pressure up to at least 175 MeV (the highest temperature studied), with both degrees of freedom contributing equally at that point.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of second- and fourth-order cumulants of charm fluctuations and their correlations with net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness fluctuations on lattices with temporal extent \(N_{\tau}=8\) and aspect ratio 4, with some \(N_{\tau}=12\) results for cutoff checks. They constructed partial pressures for meson-like, baryon-like, and quark-like excitations from ratios of generalized susceptibilities, and compared these to predictions from a hadron resonance gas model augmented with quark-model states (QM-HRG).

## Key numbers

* Chiral crossover temperature: \(T_{pc}=156.5\pm1.5\) MeV.
* The ratio \(\chi^{BC}_{13}/\chi^{BC}_{22}\) becomes greater than 1 slightly above \(T_{pc}\) and approaches the non-interacting quark-gas limit of 3 for \(T>300\) MeV.
* Charmed hadrons remain the dominant contributors to total charm pressure until \(T=175.84\) MeV, the highest temperature explored.
* Charm quark becomes the dominant degree of freedom roughly above \(1.12\;T_{pc}\) (approximately 175 MeV).
* The contribution from the doubly-charmed \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) baryon (mass 3621 MeV) at \(T_{pc}\) is suppressed by a factor of \(10^{-4}\) relative to the singly-charmed \(\Lambda_c^{+}\) baryon (mass 2286 MeV).
* Results approach the ideal charm quark gas limit for \(240\;\text{MeV}<T\leq340\;\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends recent HotQCD collaboration studies (Bazavov 2023, Sharma 2024) that provided evidence for deconfinement via charm quark-like excitations above \(T_{pc}\), by now demonstrating the persistence of charmed hadron-like excitations inside the QGP. It builds on earlier lattice QCD comparisons with HRG models (Bazavov 2014, Sharma 2022) that confirmed charmed hadron melting at \(T_{pc}\), and on FASTSUM collaboration findings (Aarts 2022) of early modification of spectral functions in certain charm channels. The results enable a refined quasi-particle picture of the QGP where meson-like, baryon-like, and quark-like charm excitations coexist, with implications for understanding deconfinement and the nature of degrees of freedom in the crossover region.
