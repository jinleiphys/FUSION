# Thermodynamic and hydrodynamic characteristics of interacting system formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> To study the energy-dependent characteristics of thermodynamic and hydrodynamic parameters, based on the framework of a multi-source thermal model, we analyze the soft transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of the charged particles ($π^{-}$, $π^{+}$, $K^{-}$, $K^{+}$, $\bar{p}$, and $p$) produced in gold-gold (Au-Au) collisions at the center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV from the STAR Collaboration and in lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ and 5.02 TeV from the ALICE Collaboration. In the rest framework of emission source, the probability density function obeyed by meson momenta satisfies the Bose-Einstein distribution, and that obeyed by baryon momenta satisfies the Fermi-Dirac distribution. To simulate the $p_{T}$ of the charged particles, the kinetic freeze-out temperature $T$ and transverse expansion velocity $β_{T}$ of emission source are introduced into the relativistic ideal gas model. Our results, based on the Monte Carlo method for numerical calculation, show a good agreement with the experimental data. The excitation functions of thermodynamic parameter $T$ and hydrodynamic parameter $β_{T}$ are then obtained from the analyses, which shows an increase tendency from 7.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV in collisions with different centralities.

## Key claim
The kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse expansion velocity extracted from a multi-source thermal model with Bose-Einstein/Fermi-Dirac distributions increase with collision energy from 7.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV, contradicting the blast-wave model results from the STAR and ALICE Collaborations which show a decrease or flat trend; the authors find no evidence for a critical point in this energy range.

## Method
The authors fit soft transverse momentum spectra (0.2–2.5 GeV/c) of π⁻, π⁺, K⁻, K⁺, p̄, and p from Au–Au collisions at eight energies (7.7–200 GeV, STAR data) and Pb–Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV (ALICE data) using a multi-source thermal model. In each stationary emission source, meson momenta follow a Bose-Einstein distribution and baryon momenta follow a Fermi-Dirac distribution; a Lorentz transformation with a single transverse expansion velocity β_T is then applied to account for source expansion. The two free parameters (kinetic freeze-out temperature T and β_T) are extracted via Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers
- Collision energies analyzed: √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV (Au–Au, STAR) plus 2.76 and 5.02 TeV (Pb–Pb, ALICE).
- Fit range for p_T spectra: 0.2–2.5 GeV/c, excluding contributions from resonance decay and hard scattering.
- T and β_T both increase generally with collision energy from 7.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV, with T showing a slight increase from central to peripheral collisions (opposite to blast-wave model results).
- For central collisions, T increases with particle mass; β_T does not change significantly with particle mass in central collisions but decreases with particle mass in peripheral collisions.

## Context
This work extends the multi-source thermal model of Liu and collaborators (prior work) to extract thermodynamic and hydrodynamic parameters across a wide energy range, using the most basic Bose-Einstein/Fermi-Dirac distributions rather than the Tsallis distribution, Erlang distribution, or Hagedorn function commonly used in the field. The results contradict the blast-wave model fits by the STAR Collaboration (2014, 2017) and ALICE Collaboration (2011, 2018), which found that kinetic freeze-out temperature decreases or remains flat with energy and is higher in peripheral collisions. The authors argue their method has a more solid theoretical foundation and enables a simpler two-parameter description, while noting that the critical point of hadronic matter to QGP transition, if it exists, likely lies below 7.7 GeV.
