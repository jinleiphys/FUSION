# Towards Precise Simulations and Inference for the Neutron EDM

> Precision measurements of neutron properties, like its permanent electric dipole moment, rely on understanding complex experimental setups in detail. We show how the properties of stored and transported ultracold neutron ensembles can be simulated reliably. In a second step, we illustrate how they can be used for simulation-based inference of the parameters associated with underlying physics processes such as neutron capture or beta decay. Our proof of principle for simulation-based inference confronts a longstanding challenge with ultracold neutrons: low measurement statistics coupled with a complex apparatus.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that simulation-based inference (SBI) can reliably extract physics parameters from ultracold neutron (UCN) experiments despite low statistics and complex apparatus, but only as a proof of principle for a specific vertical time-of-flight measurement setup, not yet for full neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) analyses.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo simulation of UCN production, storage, and extraction from the SuperSUN superfluid-helium source at the Institut Laue-Langevin, incorporating energy-dependent loss mechanisms from wall interactions, helium impurities, and gravitational effects. They then applied simulation-based inference using neural density estimation to extract parameters such as the UCN production rate and loss rates from vertical time-of-flight measurements, which resolve one component of the UCN velocity spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Neutron beta-decay lifetime: approximately 880 s, used as the fundamental storage time limit for UCN in the converter
- SuperSUN converter dimensions: approximately 3 m length, 74.4 mm diameter, with a 50 mm diameter extraction guide
- Vertical extraction distance: 28.5 cm from the converter's central axis to the horizontal guide
- Converter wall optical potential: 115 neV, reduced to an effective trapping barrier of 96.5 neV when accounting for superfluid helium's 18.5 neV optical potential
- Cold neutron wavelength for production: 8.9 angstroms, corresponding to a velocity of approximately 440 m/s
- Gravitational potential for neutrons: 102.5 neV per meter
- UCN production rate scaling: proportional to E_max^(3/2), with a normalization constant C derived from a measured cold neutron flux of approximately 2.7 x 10^8 cm^(-2) s^(-1) angstrom^(-1)

## Context

This work extends analytical UCN transport models (Pendlebury, 1994) and phenomenological fits to SuperSUN data (Degenkolb, 2025) by introducing the first precision Monte Carlo simulations coupled with modern simulation-based inference techniques. The approach addresses the longstanding challenge of low UCN measurement statistics combined with complex apparatus, and enables future extraction of fundamental parameters such as neutron capture cross-sections and beta-decay correlations from UCN experiments, including full neutron EDM analyses.
