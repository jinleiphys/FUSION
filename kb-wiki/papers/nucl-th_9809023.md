# Gamma-Deuteron Compton Scattering in Effective Field Theory

> The differential cross section for gamma-deuteron Compton scattering is computed to next-to-leading order (NLO) in an effective field theory that describes nucleon-nucleon interactions below the pion production threshold. Contributions at NLO include the nucleon isoscalar electric polarizability from its 1/m_pi behavior in the chiral limit. The parameter free prediction of the gamma-deuteron differential cross section at NLO is in good agreement with data.

## Key claim

The differential cross section for gamma-deuteron Compton scattering is computed to next-to-leading order (NLO) in an effective field theory with no free parameters, and the result is in good agreement with experimental data at 49 MeV and 69 MeV; however, the calculation is restricted to photon energies below pion production threshold and the agreement at 69 MeV is described as "reasonable" rather than "excellent."

## Method

The authors compute gamma-deuteron Compton scattering using an effective field theory with consistent power counting that describes nucleon-nucleon interactions below pion production threshold, employing dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS). The calculation includes contributions from minimal electric coupling, the C_2 four-nucleon operator, single potential pion exchange, nucleon magnetic moment interactions, and pion loop graphs that give the dominant contribution to the nucleon isoscalar electric polarizability. The kinematic range is divided into two regimes: Regime I (photon energies below the deuteron binding energy B) and Regime II (photon energies between B and 100 MeV), with different power counting schemes applied in each.

## Key numbers

* Incident photon energies of 49 MeV and 69 MeV were used for the experimental data compared against the calculation.
* The isoscalar nucleon electric polarizability from pion loops is computed as alpha_{E,N} = 1.2 x 10^{-3} fm^3.
* The C_0 coefficients for nucleon-nucleon scattering are C_0^{(3S1)} = -5.51 fm^2 and C_0^{(1S0)} = -3.34 fm^2, with C_2^{(3S1)} = 9.91 fm^4 and D_2^{(3S1)} = 1.32 fm^4, all renormalized at scale mu = m_pi in the PDS scheme.
* The proton electric polarizability from combined analyses is given as alpha_{E,p} = (12.1 +/- 0.8 +/- 0.5) x 10^{-4} fm^3 and magnetic polarizability beta_{M,p} = (2.1 -/+ 0.8 -/+ 0.5) x 10^{-4} fm^3.
* A fit assuming the nucleon magnetic polarizability is the largest NNLO contribution gives beta_{M,N} = 6.5 x 10^{-4} fm^3, but with large uncertainty.
* The NNLO contributions to the cross section are estimated to be about 10%, and when the calculation is carried out to NNLO the prediction should be accurate to within 3%.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998) and deuteron electromagnetic properties to the process of gamma-deuteron Compton scattering. It provides a model-independent, analytic calculation that avoids the ambiguity of defining "nucleon structure" inside the deuteron bound state, in contrast to potential model calculations such as those by Levchuk and L'vov (1995) and Wilhelm, Wilbois, and Arenhoevel (1996) which treat nucleon polarizabilities as input parameters. The calculation enables future extraction of neutron polarizabilities from gamma-deuteron scattering data once precise proton polarizabilities are known, and the authors note that a complete NNLO calculation would improve the theoretical accuracy to 3%.
