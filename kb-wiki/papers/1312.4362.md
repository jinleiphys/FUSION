# Hot Spin Polarized Strange Quark Stars in the Presence of Magnetic Field using a density dependent bag constant

> The effect of magnetic field on the structure properties of hot spin polarized strange quark stars has been investigated. For this purpose, we use the MIT bag model with a density dependent bag constant to calculate the thermodynamic properties of spin polarized strange quark matter such as energy and equation of state. We see that the energy and equation of state of strange quark matter changes significantly in a strong magnetic field. Finally, using our equation of state, we compute the structure of spin polarized strange quark star at different temperatures and magnetic fields.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for hot spin polarized strange quark stars, increasing both temperature and magnetic field leads to a stiffer equation of state and a lower maximum gravitational mass, but this result depends on using a density-dependent bag constant rather than a fixed one; with the density-dependent bag constant, the maximum mass is lower than with a fixed bag constant.

## Method
The authors use the MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag constant of Gaussian form, calibrated to a hadron-quark transition at an energy density of 1100 MeV/fm³. They compute the thermodynamic properties of spin-polarized strange quark matter (u, d, s quarks) at finite temperature in a uniform magnetic field, imposing charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium with zero electron density. The equation of state is derived from the Helmholtz free energy, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are integrated to obtain the mass and radius of the strange quark star.

## Key numbers
- Transition density for hadron-quark phase change: \( n_0 = 0.98 \ \text{fm}^{-3} \), at an energy density of \( 1100 \ \text{MeV/fm}^3 \).
- Density-dependent bag constant parameters: \( {\cal B}_0 = 400 \ \text{MeV/fm}^3 \), \( {\cal B}_\infty = 8.99 \ \text{MeV/fm}^3 \), \( \gamma = 0.17 \).
- Quark masses: \( m_u = m_d = 0 \), \( m_s = 150 \ \text{MeV} \).
- Magnetic moments: \( \mu_u = 1.852 \mu_N \), \( \mu_d = -0.972 \mu_N \), \( \mu_s = -0.581 \mu_N \), with \( \mu_N = 5.05 \times 10^{-27} \ \text{J/T} \).
- Magnetic field values considered: \( B = 5 \times 10^{17} \ \text{G} \) and \( B = 5 \times 10^{18} \ \text{G} \); temperatures up to \( T = 30 \ \text{MeV} \).
- At \( T = 30 \ \text{MeV} \) and \( B = 5 \times 10^{18} \ \text{G} \), the maximum gravitational mass and corresponding radius for the density-dependent bag constant are lower than those for a fixed bag constant of \( 90 \ \text{MeV/fm}^3 \).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies on spin-polarized strange quark stars at zero temperature with a magnetic field (Bordhar and Alizade, 2013) and at finite temperature with a fixed bag constant (Bordhar and Alizade, 2013). It contrasts with those earlier results by introducing a density-dependent bag constant, which yields a stiffer equation of state and a lower maximum mass. The paper enables future comparisons of strange quark star structure predictions using different bag constant parametrizations and may inform observational searches for exotic compact stars.
