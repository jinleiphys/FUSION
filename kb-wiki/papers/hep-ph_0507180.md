# Photoproduction of the Theta^+ resonance on the nucleon in a Regge model

> We estimate the reaction mechanisms for the photoproduction of the Theta^+(1540) resonance on the nucleon, through K and K^* Regge exchanges. We compare the size of the cross sections for the gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ and gamma p -> K^0 bar Theta^+ reactions, and investigate their sensitivity to the spin-parity assignments J^P = 1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm for the Theta^+ resonance. The model allows to estimate the cross sections corresponding with a given upper bound on the width of the Theta^+. Within this model, the cross sections on the neutron are found to be around a factor 5 larger than the ones on the proton, due to the presence of charged K exchange for the reaction on a neutron target. Furthermore, the photon asymmetry is found to display a pronounced sensitivity to the parity of the Theta^+, making it a very promising observable to help determining the quantum numbers of the Theta^+ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper estimates photoproduction cross sections for the Theta^+(1540) resonance on the nucleon using K and K* Regge exchanges, but the numerical predictions are contingent on assuming a specific upper bound of 1 MeV for the Theta^+ width, which was not experimentally determined at the time.

## Method

The authors constructed a Regge model for the reactions gamma n -> K- Theta^+ and gamma p -> K0bar Theta^+, replacing standard Feynman propagators for K and K*(892) mesons with Regge propagators using linear trajectories. They computed gauge-invariant amplitudes for four possible spin-parity assignments of the Theta^+ (J^P = 1/2^+, 1/2^-, 3/2^+, 3/2^-), including t-channel, u-channel, and contact diagrams, with coupling constants normalized to a Theta^+ width of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Theta^+ mass taken as M_Theta = 1.54 GeV, consistent with experiments from Spring-8, DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR, and others.
- For J^P = 1/2^+ Theta^+, the K N Theta coupling constant g_{K N Theta} = 1.056, extracted assuming Gamma_{Theta -> K N} = 1 MeV.
- For J^P = 1/2^- Theta^+, the K N Theta coupling constant g_{K N Theta} = 0.1406, extracted assuming Gamma_{Theta -> K N} = 1 MeV.
- For J^P = 3/2^+ Theta^+, the K N Theta coupling constant g_{K N Theta} = 0.4741, extracted assuming Gamma_{Theta -> K N} = 1 MeV.
- For J^P = 3/2^- Theta^+, the K N Theta coupling constant g_{K N Theta} = 3.558, extracted assuming Gamma_{Theta -> K N} = 1 MeV.
- Maximum total cross sections for gamma n -> K- Theta^+ at E_gamma = 4 GeV: approximately 1 nb for J^P = 1/2^+, 0.2 nb for J^P = 1/2^-, 10 nb for J^P = 3/2^+, and 55 nb for J^P = 3/2^-.
- Cross sections on the neutron are approximately a factor 5 larger than those on the proton.
- K* N Theta coupling constant f_{K* N Theta} = 1.1, derived from chiral quark soliton model and rescaled to correspond to a 1 MeV width.

## Context

This work extends the Regge model previously applied to K Lambda and K Sigma photoproduction on the nucleon by Guidal, Laget, and Vanderhaeghen (1997, 2000) to the exotic Theta^+ pentaquark. It provides a framework to translate experimental upper limits on photoproduction cross sections into upper bounds on the Theta^+ width, which was needed for upcoming high-resolution experiments at Jefferson Lab. The model contrasts with earlier cross section predictions by Liu (2003), Nam (2003), Oh (2004), and others, which had been ruled out by JLAB g10 and g11 experiments.
