# Nuclear charm and bottom production: a comparison among high energy approaches

> We calculate the nucleon and nuclear photoproduction cross sections for heavy quarks within the $k_{\perp}$-factorization formalism, considering the current high energy approaches which include nuclear and saturation effects. Our results demonstrate that a future experimental analysis of this process would allow to constraint the QCD dynamics at high energies.

## Key claim

The authors calculate nucleon and nuclear photoproduction cross sections for heavy quarks within the \(k_{\perp}\)-factorization formalism and claim that a future experimental analysis of this process would allow to constrain QCD dynamics at high energies, but the hedge is that the results depend on which unintegrated gluon distribution is used, and the differences between approaches are large enough to discriminate only if saturation effects are present and the factorization is given by the \(k_{\perp}\)-factorization.

## Method

The authors compute heavy quark (charm and bottom) photoproduction cross sections using the \(k_{\perp}\)-factorization approach, where the cross section is expressed as a convolution of off-shell matrix elements with unintegrated gluon distributions. For the nucleon case, they use two parameterizations: the derivative of the collinear GRV94 LO gluon distribution (semihard approach) and the phenomenological saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (2003). For the nuclear case, they extend these approaches by using the derivative of the EKS nuclear gluon distribution and by applying the Glauber-Gribov formalism to the saturation model, obtaining an analytic expression for the nuclear unintegrated gluon distribution in the non-saturated limit. They compare these results with predictions from the collinear factorization approach using the EKS and AG nuclear gluon distributions.

## Key numbers

- Charm photoproduction cross section on protons: at \(W_{\gamma p} \approx 200\) GeV, the saturation model gives approximately 1 \(\mu\)b, while the semihard and collinear approaches give approximately 10 \(\mu\)b (from Fig. 1, approximate values).
- Bottom photoproduction cross section on protons: at \(W_{\gamma p} \approx 200\) GeV, the saturation model gives approximately 0.01 \(\mu\)b, while the semihard and collinear approaches give approximately 0.1 \(\mu\)b (from Fig. 1, approximate values).
- Charm nuclear photoproduction cross section on lead: at \(W \approx 100\) GeV, the saturation model gives approximately 0.1 mb, while the semihard and collinear approaches give approximately 1 mb (from Fig. 2, approximate values).
- Bottom nuclear photoproduction cross section on lead: at \(W \approx 100\) GeV, the saturation model gives approximately 0.001 mb, while the semihard and collinear approaches give approximately 0.01 mb (from Fig. 2, approximate values).
- The NLO \(K\)-factor for bottom photoproduction at \(\sqrt{s} = 1\) TeV is about 1.6.
- Charm quark mass used: \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV; bottom quark mass used: \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV.
- The saturation scale is given by \(Q_s^2(x) = (x_0/x)^{\lambda}\) GeV\(^2\) with parameters from Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (2003).
- The nuclear saturation scale is \(Q_{sA}^2(x) = \frac{1}{2} A T_A(b) \sigma_0 Q_s^2(x)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of heavy quark photoproduction in the \(k_{\perp}\)-factorization approach from the nucleon case (previously studied by Mariotto and Machado (2003) and Timneanu and Motyka (2003)) to the nuclear case, comparing predictions from the saturation model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 2003) and the derivative of collinear gluon distributions (GRV94 LO). The authors use the EKS nuclear gluon distribution (Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado, 1999) and the AG parameterization (Ayala and Gay Ducati, 2003) which includes high parton density effects. The results indicate that nuclear heavy quark photoproduction at future \(eA\) colliders (eRHIC/TESLA) could discriminate between different QCD dynamics, particularly if saturation effects are present.
