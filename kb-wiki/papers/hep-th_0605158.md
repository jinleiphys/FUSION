# Energy loss of a heavy quark moving through N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

> We use the AdS/CFT correspondence to determine the rate of energy loss of a heavy quark moving through N=4 SU(N_c) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma at large 't Hooft coupling. Using the dual description of the quark as a classical string ending on a D7-brane, we use a complementary combination of analytic and numerical techniques to determine the friction coefficient as a function of quark mass. Provided strongly coupled N=4 Yang-Mills plasma is a good model for hot, strongly coupled QCD, our results may be relevant for charm and bottom physics at RHIC.

## Key claim

The energy loss rate (viscous drag) for a heavy quark moving through strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma is independent of quark mass and given by dp/dt = -(pi/2) sqrt(lambda) T^2 v / sqrt(1-v^2), but this result is derived in the limit of large 't Hooft coupling and large N_c, and the authors note that applicability to QCD at RHIC depends on whether strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma is a good model for hot, strongly coupled QCD.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to map the problem of a moving quark in N=4 SYM plasma to a classical string ending on a D7-brane in an AdS5 black hole background. They employ a complementary combination of analytic and numerical techniques: first, they find a stationary analytic solution describing a quark moving under a constant external electric field that balances energy loss to the plasma, which yields the product of quark mass and damping rate; second, they analyze quasi-normal modes of the linearized string equation of motion around the static solution to extract the damping rate directly; third, they numerically solve nonlinear partial differential equations for back-to-back quark-antiquark string configurations to confirm and extend the results.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss rate: dp/dt = -(pi/2) sqrt(lambda) T^2 v / sqrt(1-v^2), independent of quark mass
- Thermal mass shift: Delta m(T) = (1/2) sqrt(lambda) T
- Friction coefficient for heavy quarks (m >> Delta m(T)): mu = pi T Delta m(T)/m = (pi/2) sqrt(lambda) T^2 / m
- Upper bound on friction coefficient: mu <= 2 pi T
- Flavor diffusion constant: D = 1/(pi Delta m(T)) = 2/(pi sqrt(lambda) T)
- Jet quenching parameter: d/dt <(p_perp)^2> = 4 pi Delta m(T) T^2 = 2 pi sqrt(lambda) T^3
- Critical mass ratio: m/Delta m(T) approximately 0.92, below which the D7-brane embedding jumps discontinuously to the horizon

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT approach to real-time dissipative dynamics in strongly coupled plasmas, providing the first quantitative nonperturbative calculation of heavy quark energy loss in any strongly coupled quantum field theory. It builds on the AdS/CFT correspondence established by Maldacena (1998), Witten (1998), and Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998), and on the finite-temperature black hole construction by Witten (1998). The results enable comparison with heavy ion collision observables at RHIC, such as elliptic flow v2 and the suppression factor R_AA for charm and bottom quarks, and suggest a universal upper bound mu/T <= 2 pi analogous to the viscosity bound of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005).
