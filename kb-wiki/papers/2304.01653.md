# Linear Stability of Shock Waves in Ultrarelativistic Anisotropic Hydrodynamics

> Linear stability of a plane shock waves in ultrarelativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics is investigated. The properties of the amplitudes of perturbations of physical quantities are studied depending on the components of the wave vector of a small harmonic perturbation. Analytical calculationsfor the longitudinal and transverse propagation of shock wave normal with respect to the anisotropy axis (beam-axis) and numerical calculations for an arbitrary polar angle are carried out.

## Key claim

Linear stability analysis of plane shock waves in ultrarelativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics shows that the unstable mode found in the original Gardner (1964) approach does not exist for the ultrarelativistic case, for both longitudinal and transverse propagation of the shock wave normal with respect to the anisotropy axis, under the assumption of constant anisotropy parameter.

## Method

The author linearizes the energy-momentum conservation equations around a constant state for a massless gas with equation of state ε = 3P, introducing small harmonic perturbations of the discontinuity surface of the form η exp[-i(ωt + kx + ly)]. The Laplace transform is applied to the perturbation amplitudes in the half-spaces ahead of and behind the shock wave, following the approach of Gardner (1964) and Russo (1987). The characteristic equation for the wave number m is derived from det A = 0, and the condition for the unstable mode (C₃ = 0) is obtained by requiring that the perturbation amplitudes vanish at infinity. Analytical calculations are performed for the longitudinal case (shock normal parallel to anisotropy axis) and transverse case (shock normal perpendicular to anisotropy axis), with numerical calculations for arbitrary polar angle.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound in the ultrarelativistic isotropic case: c_s² = 1/3
- Shock wave relation for isotropic ultrarelativistic case: v v' = 1/3
- The condition R = 0 reduces to the equation φ² - 2v'φ - (1 - v'²) = 0, with solutions φ₁,₂ = v' ± 1
- For the longitudinal anisotropic case, the shock wave relation is v v' = R_∥ / (3R)
- The unstable mode condition for the longitudinal case yields the identical equation φ² - 2v'φ - (1 - v'²) = 0

## Context

This work extends the linear stability analysis of shock waves from isotropic relativistic hydrodynamics (Gardner 1964, Russo 1987) to the anisotropic case relevant for early-stage heavy-ion collisions. The anisotropic hydrodynamics framework used is based on the Romatschke-Strickland form of the distribution function (Martinez and Strickland 2010) and builds on prior work by Kirakosyan et al. (2018) on sound propagation and Mach cone asymmetry in anisotropic media, and Kovalenko (2022) on shock wave solutions in anisotropic hydrodynamics. The result that no unstable mode exists for the ultrarelativistic case contrasts with the non-relativistic results of Gardner (1964) and Russo (1987), and enables further investigation of shock wave stability for non-constant anisotropy or with dissipative corrections.
