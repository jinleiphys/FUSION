# Structure of odd-A Ag isotopes studied via algebraic approaches

> The structure of the odd-$A$ silver isotopes $^{103-115}$Ag is discussed within the frame of the Interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM). An overview of their key properties is presented, with particular attention paid to the $"$J-1 anomaly$"$, represented by an abnormal ordering of the lowest 7/2$^{+}$ and 9/2$^{+}$ states. By examining previously published data and newly performed calculations, it is demonstrated that the experimentally known level schemes and electromagnetic properties of $^{103-115}$Ag can be reproduced well within IBFM-1 by using a consistent set of model parameters. The contribution of different single-particle orbitals to the structure of the lowest-lying excited nuclear states in $^{103-115}$Ag is discussed. Given that the $J$-1 anomaly brings down the 7/2$^{+}$ level from the $j^{-3}$ multiplet to energies which can be thermally populated in hot stellar environments, the importance of low-lying excited states in odd-$A$ silver isotopes for astrophysical processes is outlined.

## Key claim

The Interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-1) can reproduce the experimentally known level schemes and electromagnetic properties of odd-A silver isotopes $^{103-115}$Ag using a consistent set of model parameters, demonstrating that the J-1 anomaly (abnormal ordering of the lowest 7/2+ and 9/2+ states) arises from the exchange term in the particle-core coupling rather than from weak coupling.

## Method

The authors performed IBFM-1 calculations coupling a proton hole in the $\pi g_{9/2}$ orbital (along with $\pi p_{3/2}$, $\pi f_{5/2}$, $\pi p_{1/2}$, and $\pi d_{5/2}$ orbitals) to even-even Cd cores described by the IBM-1 extended consistent-Q formalism. The boson-fermion interaction parameters $A_0$, $\Gamma_0$, and $\Lambda_0$ were varied to reproduce experimental data, while single-particle energies and occupation probabilities were determined via BCS calculations with a pairing gap of 1.5 MeV. The ODDA program package was used to calculate level schemes and electromagnetic transition probabilities for all seven isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The $9/2^+ \rightarrow 7/2^+$ transition $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratio decreases from 0.00045(18) in $^{105}$Ag to 0.00009(8) in $^{115}$Ag, showing strong correlation with $\Delta E = E_{9/2^+} - E_{7/2^+}$
- The $7/2^+_1$ state in $^{107}$Ag has a half-life of 44.3(2) s; in $^{109}$Ag it is 39.79(21) s
- The $9/2^+_1$ state in $^{107}$Ag has a half-life of 2.85(10) ns with $B(M1)=0.018(1)$ W.u. and $B(E2)=81(29)$ W.u. for the $9/2^+ \rightarrow 7/2^+$ transition
- The $9/2^+_1$ state in $^{109}$Ag has a half-life of 2.60(12) ns with $B(M1)=0.0165(17)$ W.u. and $B(E2)=130(120)$ W.u. for the $9/2^+ \rightarrow 7/2^+$ transition
- Magnetic moments of $7/2^+$ states range from +4.398(5) $\mu_N$ in $^{107}$Ag to +4.447(2) $\mu_N$ in $^{113}$Ag, with corresponding $g$-factors of 1.2566(14) to 1.2706(6) $\mu_N$
- Electric quadrupole moments of $7/2^+$ states range from +0.84(9) b in $^{103}$Ag to +1.05(8) b in $^{117}$Ag
- The $g$-factor of the $9/2^+$ single-particle state in $^{105}$In is 1.2611(11) $\mu_N$, similar to the $g$-factors of Ag $7/2^+$ and $9/2^+$ states

## Context

This work extends previous IBFM-1 calculations on $^{111,113}$Ag by Lalkovski (2017) and $^{115}$Ag by Kisyov (2024) to the lighter $^{103-109}$Ag isotopes, providing a systematic study across the entire $^{103-115}$Ag chain. The results contradict the weak-coupling model predictions by Wood (1984), which could not reproduce the energy separation of the 7/2+ and 9/2+ states, and instead support the conclusion that particle-core mixing in these nuclei is not weak. The work enables improved reaction network calculations for astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis, as the low-lying 7/2+ states can be thermally populated in hot stellar environments and may affect half-lives and production rates.
