# Spontaneous Parity Violation in QCD At Finite Temperature: On the Inapplicability of the Vafa-Witten Theorem

> The generalization of the Vafa-Witten theorem ruling out parity violation to QCD at finite temperature is considered. It is shown that this generalization of the theorem rules out Lorentz-invariant parity violating operators from spontaneously acquiring vacuum expectation values. However, it does not rule out Lorentz-noninvariant parity-violating operators from acquiring expectation values. Other situations where the theorem is inapplicable are also discussed.

# Spontaneous Parity Violation in QCD At Finite Temperature: On the Inapplicability of the Vafa-Witten Theorem

## Key claim

The generalization of the Vafa-Witten theorem to finite temperature rules out Lorentz-invariant parity-violating operators from spontaneously acquiring vacuum expectation values, but it does **not** rule out Lorentz-noninvariant parity-violating operators from acquiring expectation values at finite temperature. The theorem also does not generally apply to operators containing quark bilinears.

## Method

The author recapitulates the Vafa-Witten functional integral argument, which relies on the fermion determinant and Yang-Mills action being real and non-negative, and on parity-violating Lorentz scalar gluonic operators becoming purely imaginary upon analytic continuation to Euclidean space, making the source term a pure phase that reduces the generating function. The generalization to finite temperature replaces the temporal extent with the inverse temperature and imposes appropriate boundary conditions (periodic for gluons, anti-periodic for quarks), preserving the non-negativity of the fermion determinant. The author then constructs explicit Lorentz-noninvariant parity-violating operators using the heat bath four-velocity, such as tr[(D × B) · B] and tr[(D × E) · E], which remain real in Euclidean space and thus evade the Vafa-Witten argument.

## Key numbers

- The inverse temperature τ is identified as the finite temporal extent in the Euclidean functional integral, with periodic boundary conditions for gluons and anti-periodic for quarks.
- For two-flavor QCD with a perturbation λ q̄ iγ₅ τₐ q, the functional determinant takes the form Det[⧸D - iλ γ₅ τₐ] = ∏ⱼ (εⱼ² + λ²), showing a minimum at λ=0 rather than a maximum.
- For QCD at finite isospin density, a pion condensate forms for |μ| > m_π, where μ is the isospin chemical potential, yielding nonzero ⟨q̄ iγ₅ τ₁,₂ q⟩.

## Context

This paper extends the Vafa-Witten theorem (Vafa and Witten, 1984) to finite temperature and identifies its limitations. The original theorem ruled out spontaneous parity violation in QCD via a functional integral argument; the author shows that the generalization to finite temperature is nontrivial because the heat bath introduces a preferred rest frame, allowing Lorentz-noninvariant parity-violating operators. The work also addresses challenges to the theorem raised by Azcoiti and Galante (1999) regarding the well-definedness of the free energy density, and by Aoki and Grotsch (1992) and Sharpe and Singleton (1998) concerning the inapplicability of the theorem to operators containing quark bilinears, as demonstrated by the Aoki phase in lattice QCD with Wilson fermions (Aoki, 1984, 1986) and by QCD at finite isospin density (Son and Stephanov, 2000). The results open the possibility that parity-violating phases at high temperature, relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions, have not been ruled out by the Vafa-Witten theorem.
