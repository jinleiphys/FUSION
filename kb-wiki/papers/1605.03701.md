# Effects of symmetry energy in $^{132}\rm {Sn}+^{124}\rm {Sn}$ reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon

> Based on the recently updated Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, we studied the effects of symmetry energy on the neutron to proton n/p ratio and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in central $^{132}\rm {Sn}+^{124}\rm {Sn}$ reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the n/p ratio and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in central $^{132}\rm {Sn}+^{124}\rm {Sn}$ reaction at 300 MeV/nucleon mainly probe the symmetry energy in the density region 1-1.5 times saturation density. However, the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio may be able to probe the density-dependent symmetry energy above 1.5 times saturation density by making some kinematic restrictions such as the azimuthal angle and kinetic energy cuts of emitting pions.

## Key claim

The neutron-to-proton (n/p) ratio and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 300 MeV/nucleon mainly probe the symmetry energy in the density region 1–1.5 times saturation density; however, the π⁻/π⁺ ratio may be able to probe the symmetry energy above 1.5 times saturation density if kinematic restrictions such as azimuthal angle and kinetic energy cuts are applied to the emitted pions.

## Method

The authors used the updated Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, which includes nucleon short-range correlations, in-medium inelastic baryon-baryon cross sections, and a momentum-dependent pion potential. They simulated central collisions of ¹³²Sn on ¹²⁴Sn at 300 MeV/nucleon and compared results using stiff (x=0) and soft (x=1) symmetry energy parameterizations. To isolate the effect of the symmetry energy above 1.5ρ₀, they also used a hybrid setting where x changed from 1 to 0 at that density threshold.

## Key numbers

* The maximal baryon density reached in central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 300 MeV/nucleon is about 1.7 times saturation density (ρ₀).
* The n/p ratio of the reaction system is 1.56.
* The average multiplicity of π⁻ is about 1.1 per event, and of π⁺ about 0.38 per event, in central collisions at 300 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter 1 fm.
* The π⁻/π⁺ ratio is approximately 3 in central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 300 MeV/nucleon.
* With azimuthal angle cuts (45° < φ < 135°, 225° < φ < 315°), the effects of the symmetry energy above 1.5ρ₀ become larger around 50 MeV pion kinetic energy, near the Coulomb peak.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li (2003) on ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon using an older IBUU model, and by Liu (2015) using a transport model with momentum-independent potentials and free cross sections. The simulations are directly motivated by ongoing experiments at RIBF-RIKEN using the SAMURAI Time Projection Chamber. The results indicate that the planned measurements may not probe symmetry energy around twice saturation density as originally hoped, but that kinematic cuts on pions could partially access higher densities. The paper calls for systematic studies of other observables (free n/p ratio, t/³He ratio, nucleon flows, eta production) under similar kinematic conditions.
