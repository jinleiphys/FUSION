# Isospin effects and sensitive observables in the Fermi energy domain

> We review recent results obtained for charge asymmetric systems at Fermi energies. Observables sensitive to the isospin dependent part of nuclear interaction are discussed, providing information on the symmetry energy behavior below normal density.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how observables in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy below normal density, but the experimental data for Ni+Au reactions at 52 MeV/u point to a symmetry energy behavior *in between* the asy-soft and asy-stiff parametrizations, rather than favoring one extreme.

## Method

The authors employ the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport approach to simulate semi-peripheral collisions of neutron-rich and neutron-poor nuclei (e.g., Sn+Sn at 35 and 50 MeV/u, Ni+Ni and Ni+Au at 52 and 74 MeV/u). They compare two parametrizations of the symmetry energy: an asy-soft case with an almost flat behavior below saturation density and an asy-stiff case with a faster decrease at lower densities. Isospin diffusion is quantified via the imbalance ratio for Sn+Sn systems, while for Ni+Au reactions the average isotopic content of light particles emitted from the projectile-like fragment is compared to experimental data from the INDRA collaboration after de-exciting primary fragments with the SIMON code.

## Key numbers

- Sn+Sn collisions at 35 and 50 MeV/u: imbalance ratio as a function of relative kinetic energy loss shows larger equilibration (smaller R) for the asy-soft case, corresponding to the larger value of symmetry energy at low density.
- Ni+Ni and Ni+Au reactions at 52 and 74 MeV/u: for Ni+Au at 52 MeV/u, where isospin transport effects are dominant, the experimental data points for the isotopic content of complex particles lie between the simulated results with the two iso-EOS.
- For Sn+Sn reactions at 50 MeV/u, impact parameter b = 6 fm: the ratio of neck IMF asymmetry to PLF asymmetry is larger in the asy-stiff case and depends only on the iso-EOS, not on the system considered.

## Context

This work extends the line of isospin transport studies in Fermi-energy collisions, following earlier work on isospin diffusion by Tsang (1992) and the INDRA collaboration (Emma). The results are compared to other recent estimates of the symmetry energy behavior, such as those from monopole resonances in Sn-isotopes (Garg, 2007) and from dissipative mechanisms in Ca+Ca,Ti reactions (Cardella). The analysis enables future constraints on the symmetry energy below normal density using both stable and rare isotope beams.
