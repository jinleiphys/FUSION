# Low Mass Dileptons from Pb+Au Collisions at CERN SPS

> We show that the dilepton spectra measured by the CERES collaboration in Pb + Au interactions for various charge multiplicities can be reproduced by a hadronic initial state with reduction in the masses of the vector mesons in the thermal bath. Though such an effect may also be achieved by a large broadening of the spectral function we show that the photon spectra is insensitive to this. It is found that for higher multiplicities a good description of the data can also be obtained with quark gluon plasma initial state if the reduction of the vector meson masses in the mixed and hadronic phases is taken into account. We observe that a thermal source with initial temperature ~ 200 MeV can reproduce the observed enhancement in the low mass region of the dilepton spectra. It is not possible to state which one of the two initial states (QGP or hadronic) is compatible with the data. These findings are in agreement with our earlier results obtained from the analysis of the WA98 photon spectra. We estimate the number of pion-pion collisions near the rho-peak of the dilepton spectra and argue that thermal equilibrium may have been achieved in the system, justifying the use of hydrodynamical model to describe the space time evolution.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement in low-mass dilepton spectra from Pb+Au collisions at CERN SPS can be reproduced by either a hadronic initial state with reduced vector meson masses or a quark-gluon plasma initial state with mass reduction in mixed and hadronic phases, but it is not possible to determine which initial state is compatible with the data.

## Method

The authors used a Bjorken-like hydrodynamical model for isentropic expansion, incorporating medium modifications of vector meson masses via universal scaling (Nambu scaling with exponent λ=1/2) and the Quantum Hadrodynamic (QHD) model. Static dilepton emission rates from quark-antiquark annihilation (QGP phase) and pion annihilation (hadronic phase) were convoluted with the space-time evolution, using the bag model equation of state for QGP and a hadronic gas of π, ρ, ω, η, a₁ mesons and nucleons. Initial temperatures were determined self-consistently from pion multiplicity using entropy conservation.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature ~200 MeV reproduces the observed enhancement in the low mass region of the dilepton spectra.
- For dNch/dη=150, initial temperatures were 168 MeV (universal scaling) and 199 MeV (QHD model).
- For dNch/dη=210, initial temperatures were 171 MeV (universal scaling) and 206 MeV (QHD model).
- For dNch/dη=270, initial temperatures were 179 MeV (QGP), 192 MeV (universal scaling), and 212 MeV (QHD model).
- For dNch/dη=350, initial temperatures were 187 MeV (QGP), 196 MeV (universal scaling), and 217 MeV (QHD model); a good QGP description required Ti=200 MeV with Tc=190 MeV.
- Freeze-out temperature was taken as Tf=120 MeV, initial formation time τi=1 fm/c.
- For higher multiplicities (dNch/dη=270 and 350), the number of π-π collisions per particle was ~2-3, indicating thermal equilibrium.
- For lower multiplicities (dNch/dη=150 and 210), the number of collisions per particle was <1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis of WA98 photon spectra (Sarkar, Alam, Hatsuda, 2000) to CERES dilepton data, showing that both electromagnetic probes can be explained by a thermal source with initial temperature ~200 MeV. It builds on prior approaches by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) and Cassing et al. (1996) who used reduced vector meson masses, and Rapp et al. (1997) who used large broadening of the ρ spectral function. The study finds that photon spectra are insensitive to broadening effects, while dilepton data admit both mass shift and broadening scenarios, and concludes that the number of π-π collisions supports thermal equilibrium for higher multiplicity events, justifying the hydrodynamical description.
