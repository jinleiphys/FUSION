# New Isobar Models for $K^+Λ$ Electroproduction

> The electroproduction of kaon on proton has been studied using two covariant isobar models. The models incorporate propagators and vertex factors developed in our previous works. In total, the current study includes 26 nucleon resonances and 20 hyperon resonances, with spins up to 13/2. In the electromagnetic vertices, we consider two alternative electromagnetic form factors alongside the commonly used dipole model. The unknown parameters in the models, such as longitudinal coupling constants and form factor cutoffs, are determined by fitting the calculated observables to nearly 2000 experimental data points. The resulting models demonstrate satisfactory consistency with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two new covariant isobar models for $K^+\Lambda$ electroproduction, which incorporate alternative electromagnetic form factors for baryon resonances alongside the standard dipole form, achieve satisfactory consistency with nearly 2000 experimental data points from the CLAS Collaboration, with the caveat that the improvement in $\chi^2/N$ is mathematically expected due to the additional free parameters introduced by the new form factors.

## Method

The authors constructed two covariant isobar models for the reaction $e+p\to e'+K^++\Lambda$, incorporating 26 nucleon resonances and 20 hyperon resonances with spins up to 13/2, using propagators and vertex factors from their previous photoproduction work. They employed two alternative electromagnetic form factors for baryon resonances, $F^{B^*}_1(Q^2)=F_{\rm D}(Q^2)\,\exp(-a_1Q^2)$ and $F^{B^*}_2(Q^2)=F_{\rm D}(Q^2)\,\text{sech}^2(a_2Q^2)$, alongside the standard dipole form $F_{\rm D}(Q^2)=(1+Q^2/\Lambda^2)^{-2}$, and determined unknown parameters including longitudinal coupling constants and form factor cutoffs by fitting the calculated observables to 1953 experimental data points from the CLAS Collaboration.

## Key numbers

* The models include 26 nucleon resonances and 20 hyperon resonances, with spins up to 13/2.
* The fit used 1953 experimental data points from the CLAS Collaboration.
* The dipole model achieved $\chi^2/N = 2.33$ (with 117 parameters), while Model 1 achieved $\chi^2/N = 2.16$ and Model 2 achieved $\chi^2/N = 2.17$ (each with 240 parameters).
* The total $\chi^2$ values were 4552 for the dipole model, 4222 for Model 1, and 4234 for Model 2.
* The Born coupling constant $g_{K\Lambda N}/\sqrt{4\pi}$ was constrained to $-3.000$ and $g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{4\pi}$ to $0.900$, following SU(3) predictions.
* The beam-recoil transferred polarization $P'_{x'}$ remains approximately constant at $-0.2$ across the full range of kaon scattering angles, while $P'_x$ remains near zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous covariant isobar model for kaon photoproduction (Luthfiyah, 2021) to the finite $Q^2$ region of electroproduction, building on earlier electroproduction studies (Sakinah, 2019) that included nucleon resonances only up to spin 9/2. The new models improve upon the KAON-MAID predictions by better reproducing the forward-peaking behavior of the unpolarized structure function and by including beam-recoil transferred polarization data that previous analyses (Mai, 2023) omitted due to computational challenges. The results enable further studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, and suggest that recent CLAS data indicate a more compact charge distribution for baryon resonances than captured by a dipole form factor.
