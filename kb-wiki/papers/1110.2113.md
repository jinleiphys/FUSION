# Exotic $1^{--}$ States in QCD Sum Rules

> Using the QCD sum rules we test if the charmonium-like structure Y(4260), observed in the $J/ψππ$ invariant mass spectrum, can be described with a $J/ψf_0(980)$ molecular current with $J^{PC}=1^{--}$. We consider the contributions of condensates up to dimension six and we work at leading order in $α_s$. We keep terms which are linear in the strange quark mass $m_s$. The mass obtained for such state is $m_{Y}=(4.67\pm 0.09)$ GeV, when the vector and scalar mesons are in color singlet configurations. We conclude that the proposed current can better describe the Y(4660) state that could be interpreted as a $Ψ(2S) f_0(980)$ molecular state. We also use different $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ currents to study the recently observed $Y_b(10890)$ state. Our findings indicate that the $Y_b(10890)$ can be well described by a scalar-vector tetraquark current.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis shows that a $J/\psi f_0(980)$ molecular current with $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ yields a mass of $(4.67\pm 0.09)$ GeV, which is incompatible with the $Y(4260)$ state but agrees with the $Y(4660)$ state; the authors therefore conclude that the current better describes a $\Psi(2S) f_0(980)$ molecular state rather than a $J/\psi f_0(980)$ bound state.

## Method

The authors computed the two-point correlation function for four-quark currents with $J^{PC}=1^{--}$ using the QCD sum rule approach at leading order in $\alpha_s$, including contributions from condensates up to dimension six and terms linear in the strange quark mass $m_s$. They evaluated the OPE side in the $g_{\mu\nu}$ Lorentz structure, applied a Borel transformation, and extracted the ground state mass by taking the derivative of the sum rule with respect to the Borel parameter $\tau$, using a continuum threshold $s_0$ determined by requiring the obtained mass to be 0.4 to 0.6 GeV below $\sqrt{s_0}$.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the $J/\psi f_0(980)$ molecular state in color singlet configuration: $m_Y = (4.67 \pm 0.09)$ GeV, with continuum threshold $\sqrt{s_0} = 5.10 \pm 0.10$ GeV and Borel window $(0.28 \leq \tau \leq 0.33)$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Mass of the $J/\psi f_0(980)$ molecular state in color octet configuration: $m_{Y_\lambda} = (5.00 \pm 0.10)$ GeV, with $\sqrt{s_0} = 5.50 \pm 0.10$ GeV and Borel window $(0.23 \leq \tau \leq 0.33)$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Mass of the $[bq][\bar{b}\bar{q}]$ tetraquark state (scalar-vector diquark configuration): $m_{Y_b} = (10.91 \pm 0.07)$ GeV, with $\sqrt{s_0} = 11.50 \pm 0.10$ GeV.
- Mass of the $[bs][\bar{b}\bar{s}]$ tetraquark state: $m_{Y_{bs}} = (10.97 \pm 0.10)$ GeV, with $\sqrt{s_0} = 11.60 \pm 0.10$ GeV.
- Mass of the $\Upsilon f_0(980)$ molecular state in color singlet configuration: $10.75 \pm 0.12$ GeV, with $\sqrt{s_0} = 11.3 \pm 0.1$ GeV.
- Mass of the $\Upsilon f_0(980)$ molecular state in color octet configuration: $11.08 \pm 0.11$ GeV, with $\sqrt{s_0} = 11.7 \pm 0.1$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule studies of exotic $1^{--}$ states, testing the molecular interpretation for $Y(4260)$ proposed by Oset (2008) and finding it incompatible, instead supporting the $\Psi(2S) f_0(980)$ molecular interpretation for $Y(4660)$ suggested by Guo (2008). For the bottom sector, the results for the $Y_b(10890)$ state are compared with the tetraquark interpretation of Ali (2009) and the QCD sum rule calculation of Huang (2009); the authors find that a scalar-vector tetraquark current describes the $Y_b(10890)$ well, though molecular currents cannot be ruled out within uncertainties. The analysis enables future discrimination between molecular and tetraquark assignments through more detailed decay channel measurements.
