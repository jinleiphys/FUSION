# Crystalline Color Superconductivity

> In any context in which color superconductivity arises in nature, it is likely to involve pairing between species of quarks with differing chemical potentials. For suitable values of the differences between chemical potentials, Cooper pairs with nonzero total momentum are favored, as was first realized by Larkin, Ovchinnikov, Fulde and Ferrell (LOFF). Condensates of this sort spontaneously break translational and rotational invariance, leading to gaps which vary periodically in a crystalline pattern. Unlike the original LOFF state, these crystalline quark matter condensates include both spin zero and spin one Cooper pairs. We explore the range of parameters for which crystalline color superconductivity arises in the QCD phase diagram. If in some shell within the quark matter core of a neutron star (or within a strange quark star) the quark number densities are such that crystalline color superconductivity arises, rotational vortices may be pinned in this shell, making it a locus for glitch phenomena.

## Key claim

Crystalline color superconductivity (the LOFF state) can arise in quark matter when the chemical potential difference between two quark species falls within a specific window, but only if the interaction is tuned so that the spin-one pairing channel is neither attractive nor repulsive; otherwise the range of chemical potential differences supporting the LOFF state is modified.

## Method

The authors constructed a variational ansatz for the LOFF wavefunction with Cooper pairs carrying nonzero total momentum 2q, pairing up and down quarks of the same helicity as color antitriplets. They derived the gap equation for a model Hamiltonian with a four-fermion interaction having the quantum numbers of single gluon exchange, and solved it to determine the range of the chemical potential difference δμ = (μ_d − μ_u)/2 where the LOFF state is favored over both the BCS state and the unpaired normal state. They then generalized the Hamiltonian to allow separate tuning of electric and magnetic gluon exchange couplings, which introduces both spin-zero and spin-one pairing channels.

## Key numbers

* The BCS state is favored for δμ < δμ₁ = Δ₀/√2, where Δ₀ is the BCS gap at δμ = 0, in the weak-coupling limit where Δ₀ ≪ μ̅.
* For the illustrative example with μ̅ = 400 MeV and strange quark mass M_s = 300 MeV, the electron chemical potential is μ_e = 53 MeV, giving μ_u = 365 MeV, μ_d = 418 MeV, and p_F^s = 290 MeV.
* The baryon number density in that illustrative example is 4 times nuclear matter density.
* The critical temperature for color superconductivity is estimated to be of order 10–50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original LOFF analysis by Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) from electron superconductivity with magnetic impurities to the QCD context of quark matter with differing chemical potentials. Unlike the original LOFF state, the quark matter condensates include both spin-zero and spin-one Cooper pairs. The paper enables future investigation of whether a crystalline color superconducting layer in a neutron star or strange quark star could pin rotational vortices and provide a locus for pulsar glitch phenomena, a mechanism previously associated only with the nuclear crust.
