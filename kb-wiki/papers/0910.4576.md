# Fragmentation of the photoabsorption strength in neutral and charged metal microclusters

> The line shape of the plasma resonance in both neutral and charged small sodium clusters is calculated. The overall properties of the multipeak structure observed in the photoabsorption cross section of spherical Na_8 and Na_20 neutral clusters can be understood in terms of Landau damping. Quantal configurations are shown to play an important role. In the case of charged Na_9+ and Na_21^+ clusters a single peak is predicted that carries most of the oscillator strength.

## Key claim

Landau damping (direct decay of the plasmon into particle-hole excitations) is the dominant mechanism explaining the multipeak structure observed in the photoabsorption cross section of neutral Na₈ and Na₂₀ clusters, but this damping is essentially absent in positively charged Na₉⁺ and Na₂₁⁺ clusters, where a single peak carries most of the oscillator strength.

## Method

The authors performed random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for spherical sodium clusters, using a discrete particle-hole basis constructed from self-consistent single-particle potentials obtained via the spherical jellium-background model with a semiclassical density variational formalism. The residual two-body interaction included both the Coulomb potential and an exchange-correlation term from the Gunnarsson-Lundqvist functional. The RPA equations were solved for dipole (λ=1) excitations, and the resulting oscillator strengths were folded with Lorentzian functions to account for coupling to thermal surface fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- For neutral Na₈, a single collective state at approximately 2.8 eV exhausts about 75% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR)
- For neutral Na₂₀, about the same strength is distributed between two lines at 2.6 eV and 2.9 eV
- The variances of the response functions for neutral clusters are σ ≈ 0.57 eV in both Na₈ and Na₂₀, giving a ratio σ/Ē ≈ 0.25
- For charged Na₉⁺, a single peak at approximately 3.1 eV exhausts about 93% of the EWSR
- For charged Na₂₁⁺, a single peak at approximately 3.0 eV exhausts about 83% of the EWSR
- The potential depth at the center increases by about 56% for Na₉⁺ and about 42% for Na₂₁⁺ compared to the neutral clusters (which have a central depth at approximately -5.4 eV)
- The folding widths used for comparison with experiment were Γ/ħω₀ ≈ 0.1 for Na₈ and 0.06 for Na₂₀
- The calculated static dipole polarizabilities within the LDA model are lower than experimental values by about 20%

## Context

This work extends earlier time-dependent local density approximation (TDLDA) calculations by Ekardt (1985) and Beck (1987) for small metal clusters, and parallels methods used in studies of giant resonances in nuclei (Van Giai, 1981). The RPA results for Na₂₀ show fragmentation similar to Ekardt's TDLDA calculation. The authors compare their microscopic RPA results with semiclassical calculations by Brack (1989), finding agreement within 2-4% for energy-weighted sum rules and centroids, and within 4-10% for static dipole polarizabilities. The work enables future experimental tests of the exchange-correlation part of the effective electron interaction in metal clusters, particularly through low-temperature photoabsorption measurements and detailed mapping of red-shifted dipole peaks.
