# $\bar K$ induced formation of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances on proton targets

> We perform a calculation of the cross section for nine reactions induced by $\bar K$ scattering on protons. The reactions studied are $K^-p \to Λπ^+ π^-$, $K^-p \to Σ^0 π^+ π^-$, $K^-p \to Λπ^0 η$, $K^-p \to Σ^0 π^0 η$, $K^-p \to Σ^+ π^- η$, $\bar K^0 p \to Λπ^+ η$, $\bar K^0 p \to Σ^0 π^+ η$, $\bar K^0 p \to Σ^+ π^+ π^-$, $\bar K^0 p \to Σ^+ π^0 η$. We find that in the reactions producing $π^+ π^-$ a clear peak for the $f_0(980)$ resonance is found, while no trace of $f_0(500)$ appears. Similarly, in the cases of $πη$ production a strong peak is found for the $a_0(980)$ resonance, with the characteristic strong cusp shape. Cross sections and invariant mass distributions are evaluated which should serve, comparing with future data, to test the dynamics of the chiral unitary approach used for the evaluations and the nature of these resonances.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in nine specific antikaon-proton reactions, the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances will be produced with clear signals, while the $f_0(500)$ resonance will be absent, based on a chiral unitary approach where these resonances are dynamically generated from meson-meson interactions. The claim is conditional on the validity of the production mechanism proceeding via a $t$-channel kaon exchange diagram, with the resonances formed from the strong coupling of the initial kaon to a virtual kaon.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary approach, solving coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations with transition potentials from lowest-order chiral Lagrangians, to obtain the $K\bar{K} \to \pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K} \to \pi\eta$ scattering amplitudes. These amplitudes were then combined with a $t$-channel kaon-exchange diagram and Yukawa meson-baryon-baryon vertices (with coupling constants $F=0.795$, $D=0.465$, and pion decay constant $f_\pi = 93$ MeV) to compute the differential cross sections $d^2\sigma/dM_{\rm inv} d\cos\theta$ for nine reactions induced by $K^-$ or $\bar{K}^0$ on proton targets, producing a $\Lambda$ or $\Sigma$ hyperon together with either $\pi^+\pi^-$ or $\pi\eta$ pairs.

## Key numbers

- The $f_0(980)$ resonance appears as a clear peak in $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distributions at $M_{\rm inv} \approx 980$ MeV for reactions such as $K^-p \to \Lambda\pi^+\pi^-$ and $K^-p \to \Sigma^0\pi^+\pi^-$.
- The $a_0(980)$ resonance appears as a strong cusp/peak in $\pi\eta$ invariant mass distributions at $M_{\rm inv} \approx 980$ MeV for reactions such as $K^-p \to \Lambda\pi^0\eta$, $K^-p \to \Sigma^0\pi^0\eta$, and $K^-p \to \Sigma^+\pi^-\eta$.
- The differential cross section for $\Lambda$ production in $K^-p \to \Lambda\pi^+\pi^-$ is approximately a factor of 10 larger than for $\Sigma^0$ production in $K^-p \to \Sigma^0\pi^+\pi^-$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.4$ GeV and $\cos\theta=1$.
- The cross section falls by about a factor of 10 from forward ($\cos\theta=1$) to backward ($\cos\theta=-1$) angles at $M_{\rm inv}=980$ MeV and $\sqrt{s}=2.4$ GeV.
- The cross section for $\Lambda$ production peaks at a laboratory kaon momentum $p_{\bar{K}} \approx 2.5$ GeV when evaluated at $M_{\rm inv}=980$ MeV and $\cos\theta=1$.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to $B$ and $D$ meson decays (Liang et al., 2014; Dai et al., 2014) and to $f_0(980)$ photoproduction (Marco et al., 1999; Battaglieri et al., 2008, 2009) to the new domain of kaon-induced reactions on proton targets. The predictions are intended to be tested against future data from facilities such as J-PARC, DAPHNE at Frascati, and a proposed secondary meson beam facility at Jefferson Lab, and would help distinguish between competing interpretations of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances as tetraquarks, meson molecules, or dynamically generated states.
