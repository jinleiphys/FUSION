# Instabilities in a Mean-field dynamics of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We discuss the features of instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter, in particular the relation between the nature of fluctuations, the types of instabilities and the properties of the interaction. We show a chemical instability appears as an instability against isoscalar-like fluctuations. Then starting from phenomenological hadronic field theory (QHD), including exchange terms, we discuss the symmetry energy and the relation to the dynamical response inside the spinodal region.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in asymmetric nuclear matter, chemical instabilities are triggered by isoscalar-like fluctuations (where protons and neutrons fluctuate in phase) rather than isovector-like fluctuations, due to the attractive interaction between protons and neutrons; this result is derived from a link between thermodynamics and Fermi liquid theory, and the authors note that the stability condition alone cannot distinguish between attractive and repulsive inter-component interactions.

## Method

The authors first establish a relation between the nature of fluctuations and the types of instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter by linking thermodynamics with Landau Fermi liquid theory for two-component systems, deriving stability conditions from coupled Landau-Vlasov equations. They then perform numerical simulations using a phase space test particle method with Gaussian wave packets to solve the Landau-Vlasov dynamics for nuclear matter in a box with periodic boundary conditions, including nucleon-nucleon collisions. Finally, they introduce Fock term contributions into a Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model using the Wigner transform of the one-body density matrix, comparing results with the Non-Linear Hartree (NLH) approximation and Skyrme interactions.

## Key numbers

- Initial density for numerical simulation: \(\rho^{(0)} = 0.09 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\) at temperature T = 5 MeV and asymmetry I = 0.5
- Box size for numerical simulation: L = 24 fm
- Time steps reported for density evolution: t = 0, 100, 200 fm/c
- Symmetry energy at saturation density \(\rho_0\): 31 MeV (used for parameter fitting in both NLH and NLHF models)
- Density for collective response study: \(\rho = 0.4 \rho_0\) (half saturation density)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter (Pethick 1988, 1993; Colonna 1994, 1998) by specifically addressing the binary character of asymmetric nuclear matter and the role of chemical instabilities. The authors compare their Non-Linear Hartree-Fock (NLHF) results with the Non-Linear Hartree (NLH) approximation including \(\rho\) and \(\delta\) mesons (Kubis and Kutschera 1997) and with Skyrme interactions, finding that the density dependence of the potential symmetry energy in NLHF is "softer" below saturation density and "stiffer" above compared to NLH. The work enables future comparisons with experimental data on isospin of intermediate mass fragments in multifragmentation and isospin flows, and suggests that the mixed behavior of the symmetry energy in NLHF (Asy-soft at subnuclear density, Asy-stiff above saturation) will have interesting effects on experimental observables depending on the baryon density region probed.
