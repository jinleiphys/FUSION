# Nucleon Generalized Parton Distributions from Full Lattice QCD

> We present a comprehensive study of the lowest moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions in N_f=2+1 lattice QCD using domain wall valence quarks and improved staggered sea quarks. Our investigation includes helicity dependent and independent generalized parton distributions for pion masses as low as 350 MeV and volumes as large as (3.5 fm)^3, for a lattice spacing of 0.124 fm. We use perturbative renormalization at one-loop level with an improvement based on the non-perturbative renormalization factor for the axial vector current, and only connected diagrams are included in the isosinglet channel.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first comprehensive lattice QCD calculation of the lowest moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) using \(N_f=2+1\) dynamical quark configurations with pion masses as low as 350 MeV, but the results are obtained using a hybrid action (domain wall valence quarks on improved staggered sea quarks) that breaks unitarity at finite lattice spacing, and only connected diagrams are included in the isosinglet channel.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions on MILC gauge configurations with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of Asqtad improved staggered sea quarks, using domain wall valence quarks with a fifth dimension \(L_5=16\) and HYP-smearing. They extract the generalized form factors \(A_{ni}(t)\), \(B_{ni}(t)\), \(C_{n0}(t)\), \(\widetilde A_{ni}(t)\), and \(\widetilde B_{ni}(t)\) for moments \(n=1,2,3\) by solving overdetermined systems of linear equations from ratios of three-point to two-point functions, with perturbative renormalization at one-loop level improved by the non-perturbative renormalization factor for the axial vector current.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses as low as 356.6(1.6) MeV (dataset 5, volume \(20^3\times 32\)) and 352.3(1.4) MeV (dataset 6, volume \(28^3\times 32\))
* Lattice spacing \(a = 0.1241\) fm with 2% uncertainty, determined from heavy quark spectroscopy
* Spatial volumes as large as \((3.5\ \text{fm})^3\) (corresponding to \(28^3\times 32\) lattice)
* Number of configurations used: 425 (heavy, \(m_\pi \approx 759\) MeV) to 655 (light, \(m_\pi \approx 357\) MeV) for the \(20^3\times 32\) volume, and 270 for the \(28^3\times 32\) volume
* Residual quark mass \((am)_{\text{res}}\) kept at least an order of magnitude smaller than the quark mass, with \(L_5=16\) chosen as the optimal fifth dimension extent
* DWF nucleon masses approximately 6% lower than corresponding Asqtad nucleon masses after matching Goldstone pion masses

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of nucleon GPDs by the QCDSF collaboration in quenched QCD (Gockeler, 2004) and by LHPC/SESAM in \(N_f=2\) lattice QCD (Hagler, 2003), which were limited to pion masses above 550 MeV. The use of \(N_f=2+1\) dynamical configurations with pion masses down to 350 MeV enables more reliable chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass using partially quenched mixed action chiral perturbation theory, and the results provide first-principles input for interpreting deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments and for extracting quark orbital angular momentum contributions to the nucleon spin.
