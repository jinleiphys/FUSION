# Pentaquark $Θ^*$ States in {27} Baryon Multiplet from Chiral Soliton Model

> We estimate the mass and the width of pentaquark $Θ^*$ states in the 27-plet from chiral soliton models. The calculations show that the mass of $Θ^*$ is about 1.60 GeV and the width for the process $Θ^*\to {KN}$ is less than 43 MeV. We also discuss the search for the existence of $Θ^*$ states in physical processes.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the pentaquark \(\Theta^*\) state in the 27-plet has a mass of about 1.60 GeV and a width for the decay \(\Theta^* \to KN\) of less than 43 MeV, but this width bound depends on the assumption that the experimentally reported \(\Theta^+\) width is less than 25 MeV.

## Method

The authors use the SU(3) chiral soliton model, quantizing the Skyrmion to obtain the collective Hamiltonian and mass eigenvalues for baryon multiplets. They fix model parameters (moments of inertia, symmetry-breaking coefficients \(\alpha, \beta, \gamma\)) by fitting to known baryon masses (e.g., \(\Delta\), \(\Sigma^*\), \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Theta^+\), and a candidate \(\Xi_{3/2}\) state) and then compute the \(\Theta^*\) mass from the 27-plet energy formula. The decay width for \(\Theta^* \to KN\) is derived from a pseudoscalar Yukawa coupling via the Goldberger-Treiman relation, using an axial-current coupling operator and including first-order flavor-symmetry-breaking mixing between multiplets.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(\Theta^*\): 1.60 GeV, from the chiral soliton model fit to the 27-plet.
- Width for \(\Theta^* \to KN\): less than 43 MeV, under the condition that \(\Gamma_{\Theta^+} < 25\) MeV.
- Mass of \(\Theta^+\) used as input: 1540 MeV (1.54 GeV).
- Mass of candidate \(\Xi_{3/2}\) used as input: 1860 MeV (1.86 GeV), from the NA49 experiment.
- Extracted model parameters: \(1/I_2 = 399\) MeV, \(\alpha = -663\) MeV, \(\beta = -12\) MeV, \(\gamma = 185\) MeV.
- Mixing coefficients: \(C_{\overline{10}} = 0.21\), \(C_{27} = 0.17\), \(C_{\bar{35}} = 0.16\), \(C_{35} = 0.08\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model predictions for pentaquark states beyond the anti-decuplet, following the successful prediction of the \(\Theta^+\) by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). It builds on the study of higher multiplets by Walliser and Kopeliovich (2003), who predicted \(\Theta^*\) masses of about 1.65/1.69 GeV, and by Borisyuk, Faber, and Kobushkin (2003), who predicted a mass around 1595 MeV and a width of 80 MeV. The present calculation provides a more stringent width bound and suggests experimental searches for \(\Theta^{*++}\), \(\Theta^{*+}\), and \(\Theta^{*0}\) in photon-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, pion-nucleon, and electron-nucleon collisions.
