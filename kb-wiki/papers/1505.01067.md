# Model of the N-Quark Potential in SU(N) Gauge Theory using Gauge-String Duality

## Key claim

The gauge-string duality model of the N-quark potential in pure Yang-Mills theories smoothly interpolates between the Δ-law at short distances and the Y-law at long distances, with the transition occurring at approximately L ≈ 0.6 fm for SU(3). The model reproduces lattice data remarkably well using only one free parameter κ, with the other two parameters (γ and σ) fixed from the quark-antiquark potential.

## Method

The author uses gauge-string duality with a deformed AdS₅ × S⁵ background geometry (soft wall model) to compute the static N-quark potential. Quarks are placed at vertices of a regular N-sided convex polygon, strings stretch between quarks on the boundary and a baryon vertex in the interior, and the total energy is obtained by extremizing the Nambu-Goto string action plus a vertex action with respect to the vertex location r₀. The potential is obtained parametrically via functions E(ν) and L(ν), where ν = σ r₀².

## Key numbers

- For SU(3), the model parameters are γ = 0.176 and σ = 0.44 GeV², taken from prior quark-antiquark potential work
- The single free parameter κ is fitted to α₃Q = 0.125 from lattice data, yielding κ = -0.083
- The ratio α₃Q/α_{QQ̅} ≈ 0.495, where α_{QQ̅} = (2π)³ Γ⁻⁴(1/4) γ
- The ratio σ₀/σ ≈ 1.007, favoring the Δ-law at short distances
- The transition between Δ-law and Y-law occurs at L ≈ 0.6 fm
- The string tension σ = e γ σ = e × 0.176 × 0.44 GeV²
- The normalization constant C = 1.87 GeV

## Context

This work extends the gauge-string duality approach previously applied to quark-antiquark potentials (Andreev and Zakharov, 2006; Andreev, 2008) and hybrid potentials (Andreev, 2014) to the N-quark case. It provides the first model that interpolates between the Δ-law (pairwise interactions) at short distances and the Y-law (three-body interactions with a Steiner point) at long distances, which lattice simulations by Suganuma (2006), Jahn (2004), and the PDF collaboration (2004) had suggested but no model had previously captured. The model enables analytic predictions for N > 3, where lattice data remain scarce, and provides a framework for understanding the transition between perturbative and confining regimes in multi-quark systems.
