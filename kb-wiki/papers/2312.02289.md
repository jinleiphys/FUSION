# Hints of Entanglement Suppression in Hyperon-Nucleon Scattering

> Hyperon ($Y=Σ,Λ$)-nucleon ($N=n,p$) interactions are crucial for understanding the existence of neutron stars heavier than two solar masses. Amid renewed experimental efforts, we study $YN$ scatterings from the perspective of quantum information, focusing on whether spin entanglement is suppressed in the s-wave channel, which is observed in $np$ scattering and leads to enhanced global symmetries. Using global fits of phase shifts from experimental data, we find hints of entanglement suppression among the eight flavor channels in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector, similar to the $np$ case. One exception is the $Σ^+p$ channel, where conflicting global fits lead to inconclusive outcome. We then propose ''quantum'' observables in $Σ^+p$ scattering to help resolve the differing global fits.

## Key claim
Using global fits of phase shifts from experimental data, the authors find hints that spin entanglement is suppressed in the s-wave channel for most of the eight flavor channels in the strangeness S=-1 sector of hyperon-nucleon scattering, similar to the np case, but the Sigma+p channel yields inconclusive results due to conflicting global fits.

## Method
The authors analyzed the entanglement power (EP) of the S-matrix for s-wave YN scattering, which depends on the difference between the spin-singlet (^1S_0) and spin-triplet (^3S_1) phase shifts. They computed the EP from phase shifts provided by three global fits: the Nijmegen soft-core model (NSC97), the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model (ESC16), and next-to-next-to-leading order chiral effective field theory (N^2LO chi-EFT). For inelastic channels, they decomposed the diagonal S-matrix entries into Identity and SWAP components to assess entanglement suppression.

## Key numbers
- Pion production threshold in p_CM for Lambda n -> Lambda p pi- is 382.8 MeV/c; for Sigma+ p -> Sigma+ n pi+ it is 390.3 MeV/c.
- The kinematic thresholds for inelastic flavor-changing scattering open at p_CM = 279 MeV/c for Lambda p and 283 MeV/c for Lambda n.
- In the Sigma+p channel, the NSC97 model predicts an attractive force in the ^3S_1 channel while ESC16 and N^2LO chi-EFT predict a repulsive force, with the latter two showing consistency up to p_CM ~ 250 MeV.
- For Sigma-p, the S-matrix is dominated by the SWAP gate at p_CM ~ 200 MeV; for Sigma0 p, it is dominated by the Identity gate at p_CM ~ 150 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the information-theoretic analysis of entanglement suppression previously applied to np scattering by Beane (2018) and Low (2021), who showed that suppression correlates with emergent Wigner SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry and Schrodinger symmetry. The study uses global fits from the Nijmegen potential models (NSC97, ESC16) and chi-EFT (Haidenbauer 2023) to analyze YN scattering, complementing lattice QCD simulations that assume SU(3)_f symmetry. The results enable future measurements of "quantum observables" such as recoil and target polarizations in Sigma+p scattering, which could resolve conflicting global fits and improve understanding of the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars.
