# Tetraquarks, hadronic molecules, meson-meson scattering and disconnected contributions in lattice QCD

> There are generally two types of Wick contractions in lattice QCD calculations of a correlation function --- connected and disconnected ones. The disconnected contribution is difficult to calculate and noisy, thus it is often neglected. In the context of studying tetraquarks, hadronic molecules and meson-meson scattering, we show that whenever there are both connected and singly disconnected contractions, the singly disconnected part gives the leading order contribution, and thus should never be neglected. As an explicit example, we show that information about the scalar mesons sigma, f0(980), a0(980) and kappa will be lost when neglecting the disconnected contributions.

## Key claim

Whenever a lattice QCD two-point correlation function of a four-quark interpolating field admits both connected and singly disconnected Wick contractions, the singly disconnected part is always of leading order in the 1/N expansion and in chiral perturbation theory, and therefore must never be neglected; neglecting it will produce unphysical results that cannot be related to the real world.

## Method

The authors use large-N counting (N = number of colors) to classify the four types of Wick contractions for four-quark interpolating fields, showing that direct connected and doubly disconnected diagrams are suppressed by 1/N relative to cross connected and singly disconnected diagrams. They then separate connected from disconnected contributions in leading-order SU(3) chiral perturbation theory by introducing an auxiliary quark flavor j to extend the theory to SU(4), and compute the connected-only scattering amplitudes for seven meson-meson channels. These connected-only amplitudes are inserted into the Bethe-Salpeter equation (chiral unitary approach) to locate the poles of the light scalar mesons, and the results are compared with the poles obtained using the full amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- With the full leading-order chiral amplitudes and a sharp cut-off q_max = 0.9 GeV, the sigma pole sits at (0.47 - i0.20) GeV in the second Riemann sheet; when disconnected contributions are neglected, the pole moves to (0.84 - i0.62) GeV.
- The f0(980) pole cannot be generated at all when only connected contractions are kept, because the K̅K channel decouples in the isoscalar case.
- With the full amplitudes, the a0(980) pole is at (1.01 - i0.06) GeV (q_max = 0.9 GeV); when disconnected contributions are dropped, the pole moves to (1.07 - i0.34) GeV in the third Riemann sheet.
- With the full amplitudes and q_max = 0.51 GeV, the kappa pole is at (0.73 - i0.25) GeV; when disconnected contributions are dropped, the pole moves to (0.93 - i0.54) GeV in the second Riemann sheet.
- The connected-only leading-order amplitude for π⁰K⁺ → π⁰K⁺ is (1/(4F²))(s - M_π² - M_K²), while the full leading-order amplitude is -t/(4F²).

## Context

This work extends the large-N counting arguments of 't Hooft (1973) and Witten (1979) to the specific case of four-quark interpolating fields in lattice QCD, and uses chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1978, Gasser and Leutwyler 1983) to make the argument quantitative for light scalar mesons. It directly addresses the tension between lattice calculations that neglected disconnected diagrams, such as those by Prelovsek et al. (2010) which reported energy levels above threshold interpreted as sigma and kappa, and later dynamical calculations by Lang et al. (2012) showing those levels were artifacts. The results enable lattice practitioners to correctly interpret tetraquark and meson-meson scattering simulations by demonstrating that disconnected diagrams must be included whenever they are kinematically allowed, and provide a concrete method (auxiliary quark flavor) for isolating connected contributions in chiral perturbation theory.
