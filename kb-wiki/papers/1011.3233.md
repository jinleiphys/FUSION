# Nucleon to Delta transition form factors with $N_F=2+1$ domain wall fermions

> We calculate the electromagnetic, axial and pseudo-scalar form factors of the Nucleon to $Δ(1232)$ transition using two dynamical light degenerate quarks and a dynamical strange quark simulated with the domain wall fermion action. Results are obtained at lattice spacings $a = 0.114$ fm and $a=0.084$ fm, with corresponding pion masses of $330$ MeV and $297$ MeV, respectively. High statistics measurements are achieved by utilizing the coherent sink technique. The dominant electromagnetic dipole form factor, the axial form factors and the pseudo-scalar coupling are extracted to a good accuracy. This allows the investigation of the non-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation. Particular emphasis is given on the extraction of the sub-dominant electromagnetic quadrupole form factors and their ratio to the dominant dipole form factor, $R_{EM}$ and $R_{SM}$, measured in experiment.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon-to-Delta(1232) transition form factors using \(N_F=2+1\) domain wall fermions yields results for the dominant magnetic dipole form factor that remain below experimental values at low momentum transfer, with a slope that is still smaller than experiment, despite using two lattice spacings (0.114 fm and 0.084 fm) and pion masses as low as 297 MeV.

## Method

The electromagnetic, axial-vector, and pseudo-scalar transition form factors were extracted from three-point correlation functions computed on two ensembles of dynamical domain wall fermion gauge configurations generated by the RBC-UKQCD collaborations, with volumes \(24^3\times 64\) (coarse, \(a=0.114\) fm) and \(32^3\times 64\) (fine, \(a=0.084\) fm). Sequential inversions through the sink were employed with the Delta state at rest, using three optimized linear combinations of sink indices to decouple the dominant dipole from the sub-dominant quadrupole form factors, and the coherent sink technique provided four measurements per sequential inversion. The form factors were extracted via a global \(\chi^2\) minimization of an overconstrained linear system using singular value decomposition.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic dipole form factor \(G_{M1}\) extracted at pion mass \(m_\pi = 330\) MeV on the coarse lattice (\(a^{-1}=1.73(3)\) GeV) and at \(m_\pi = 297\) MeV on the fine lattice (\(a^{-1}=2.34(3)\) GeV).
* Lattice spacings: \(a = 0.114\) fm (coarse) and \(a = 0.084\) fm (fine), with physical volume \((2.7\ \mathrm{fm})^3\) for both.
* Nucleon masses: \(m_N = 1.130(6)\) GeV (coarse) and \(m_N = 1.127(9)\) GeV (fine); Delta masses: \(m_\Delta = 1.457(11)\) GeV (coarse) and \(m_\Delta = 1.455(17)\) GeV (fine).
* Source-sink separation: 0.91 fm (coarse, 8 time slices) and 1.01 fm (fine, 12 time slices).
* Number of gauge configurations: 200 (800 measurements) for dominant form factors and 398 (1592 measurements) for sub-dominant form factors on the coarse lattice; 176 (704 measurements) and 309 (1236 measurements) respectively on the fine lattice.
* Renormalization constants: \(Z_V = 0.7161(1)\) (coarse) and \(Z_V = 0.7468(39)\) (fine); \(Z_A = 0.7161(1)\) (coarse) and \(Z_A = 0.74521(2)\) (fine).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the N-to-Delta transition by Alexandrou et al. (2007, 2008) that used a mixed action with domain wall valence quarks on a staggered sea, by employing fully dynamical \(N_F=2+1\) domain wall fermions from the RBC-UKQCD collaborations (Allton et al., 2008), which eliminates ambiguities from rooting and mixed-action matching. The use of two lattice spacings smaller than in the prior mixed-action calculation allows, for the first time, investigation of cutoff effects on these observables. The results enable simultaneous chiral extrapolations of the nucleon axial charge, the Delta axial coupling, and the strong coupling constants \(g_{\pi N\Delta}\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\) to the physical point, and provide lattice QCD input for testing the non-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.
