# Speed of sound peak in two-color dense QCD: confronting effective models with lattice data

> Lattice simulations of two-color, two-flavor Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at finite quark chemical potential have revealed a distinctive peak structure in the sound velocity. Although chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model have been employed to explain this phenomenon, neither approach has fully captured the observed behavior. To address this discrepancy, we have extended the NJL framework by incorporating the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS). This approach isolates medium contributions from divergent integrals, allowing for a more accurate treatment of finite-density effects. Our results indicate a clear increase in the diquark gap ($Δ$) with increasing chemical potential, consistent with what is also seen in perturbative QCD predictions at high densities. {}Furthermore, the MSS-modified NJL model successfully reproduces the observed peak in the sound velocity.

## Key claim

The Medium Separation Scheme (MSS) applied to the two-color Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model successfully reproduces the peak in the speed of sound observed in lattice simulations of two-color QCD, whereas the traditional regularization scheme (TRS) fails to capture this structure. The MSS approach isolates medium contributions from divergent integrals, avoiding spurious nonphysical medium effects that arise in conventional regularization.

## Method

The authors employed a two-color, two-flavor NJL model with scalar, pseudoscalar, and diquark interactions at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential. They compared two regularization procedures: the traditional three-dimensional sharp cutoff (TRS) and the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS), which separates vacuum divergences from medium-dependent contributions. The gap equations for the effective quark mass and diquark condensate were solved self-consistently, and thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, baryon density, and speed of sound were computed from the mean-field thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- Diquark condensate $\Delta$ in the TRS approach reaches zero at $\mu_B/m_\pi \sim 12.3$ ($\mu_B \approx 1.72$ GeV), while the MSS curve continues to increase slightly in the reliable range $\mu_B \lesssim 10 m_\pi$
- Model parameters: coupling $G = 7.20$ GeV$^{-2}$, current quark mass $m_q = 5.3$ MeV, cutoff $\Lambda = 657$ MeV, pion decay constant $f_\pi = 75.45$ MeV, pion mass $m_\pi = 140$ MeV, vacuum quark condensate $\langle \bar q q \rangle_0^{1/3} = -218$ MeV
- The MSS equation of state becomes softer than TRS for energy density $\varepsilon \approx 750$ MeV/fm$^3$
- The speed of sound squared $c_s^2$ from MSS reproduces the peak structure observed in lattice QCD data from Itou (2023), while TRS does not produce a peak

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-color QCD thermodynamics using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and the NJL model, which had not fully captured the sound velocity peak seen in lattice simulations by Itou (2023). The MSS regularization, introduced by Farias (2005) and applied in various contexts, provides a method to avoid cutoff artifacts that plague traditional regularization schemes. The results enable more reliable comparisons between effective models and first-principles lattice QCD calculations, and the analytical approximation for $c_s^2$ (similar to an expression derived by Gholami (2025) using renormalization group techniques) may be relevant for astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations.
