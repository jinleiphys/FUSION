# Estimating nucleon substructure properties in a unified model of p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions

> We apply a well tested hybrid transport model, which couples viscous hydrodynamics to a hadronic afterburner, to describe bulk observables in proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) initial conditions are modeled using the parametric TRENTO model with additional nucleon substructure parameters to vary the number and size of hot spots inside each nucleon, followed by a pre-equilibrium free streaming stage to match the full energy-momentum tensor of the initial state onto viscous hydrodynamics. Initial condition and QGP medium parameters, such as the temperature dependence of the QGP shear and bulk viscosities, are then calibrated using Bayesian parameter estimation to describe charged particle yields, mean $p_T$ and anisotropic flow harmonics of both collision systems in a single self-consistent framework. We find that the hybrid model provides a compelling, simultaneous description of both collision systems using appropriately chosen model parameters, and present new posterior estimates for the size and shape of the nucleon and temperature dependence of QGP shear and bulk viscosities.

# Estimating nucleon substructure properties in a unified model of p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions

## Key claim
The hybrid transport model can simultaneously describe p-Pb and Pb-Pb bulk observables at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV using a single set of model parameters, but the posterior estimates for nucleon substructure and QGP viscosities are less constraining than previous Pb-Pb-only analyses, particularly for shear viscosity.

## Method
The authors used the TRENTO model with parametric nucleon substructure (Gaussian constituents with variable number, width, and sampling radius) to generate initial conditions, followed by a free-streaming stage and matching to VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosity, then a hadronic afterburner using UrQMD. They performed Bayesian parameter estimation with a Latin hypercube design, training a Gaussian process emulator on computed observables (charged particle yield, mean pT, and flow harmonics v2, v3, v4) and sampling the posterior via Markov-chain Monte Carlo.

## Key numbers
- Constituent sampling radius r = 0.97 (+0.19, -0.16) fm from the combined p-Pb and Pb-Pb posterior
- Constituent Gaussian width w = 0.47 (+0.22, -0.16) fm from the combined p-Pb and Pb-Pb posterior
- The analysis was performed at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for both collision systems
- The posterior disfavors one constituent (no substructure) but has no strong preference for a specific number of constituents M > 1

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian calibration approach previously applied to Pb-Pb collisions by Bernhard (2018) to include p-Pb collisions simultaneously, testing whether the hydrodynamic paradigm can describe small systems with the same parameters as large systems. The shear viscosity posterior is fully consistent with the earlier Pb-Pb analysis at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 and 5.02 TeV but provides significantly less constraining power, while the bulk viscosity posterior shows similar constraining power from the small-system data, suggesting p-Pb collisions may help constrain radial expansion of the QGP medium.
