# Delta Decay in the Nuclear Medium

> The $Δ$ decay in the nuclear medium is calculated in the relativistic meson-nucleon model. The delta spreading width is calculated and compared with the Pauli-blocked $π$N decay width. The influence of relativistic mean fields is also studied. We stress the importance of understanding the delta spreading width in interpreting experiments involving delta resonances.

# Delta Decay in the Nuclear Medium

## Key claim

The delta spreading width (the decay channel Δ + N → N + N) is calculated in a relativistic meson-nucleon model and found to be large enough that a significant portion of deltas in nuclear matter undergo pionless decays, but the branching ratio between the spreading width and the πN decay width has a clear momentum dependence that must be properly accounted for when interpreting experiments involving delta resonances.

## Method

The authors calculate the delta decay width in nuclear matter using the Walecka model, a relativistic meson-nucleon model with scalar and vector mean fields. The πN decay width is computed with Pauli blocking by replacing the free nucleon propagator with one that includes a Fermi sea, and the spreading width is obtained from the imaginary part of the particle-hole polarization in the pion propagator, using a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7 and monopole form factors with cutoff parameters from the Bonn potential (Λ = 1.8 GeV for πNN and Λ = 0.85 GeV for πNΔ). The calculations are performed at half, normal, and twice nuclear saturation density, both with and without the inclusion of relativistic mean fields.

## Key numbers

- Free delta decay width: 115 MeV (experimental value used to fix the coupling constant fΔ = 2.15)
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation density (kF = 256 MeV): m* = 510 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at half saturation density (kF = 203 MeV): m* = 715 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at twice saturation density (kF = 323 MeV): m* = 261 MeV
- At saturation density, the πN decay is completely blocked for a delta at rest in the medium
- The πN decay width returns to its free value of 115 MeV for delta momentum |pΔ| > 650 MeV (without mean fields)
- Spreading width at saturation density with mean fields: varies from 75 MeV to 57 MeV depending on delta momentum
- Branching ratio ΓπN/Γs at saturation density: varies from 0 to 1.33
- At twice saturation density with mean fields, the spreading width is only 6-12% of the Fermi gas result

## Context

This work extends nonrelativistic calculations of delta decay in the medium by Oset and Salcedo (1987) and Arve and Helgesson (1994) into a relativistic framework using the Walecka model of Serot and Walecka (1986). The authors previously proposed (Kim, Schramm, and Horowitz, 1996) that the spreading width could introduce errors of about 0.1 GeV in extracting the nucleon axial form factor from neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments such as the BNL experiment of Ahrens et al. (1988). The results provide constraints for Monte Carlo simulations of atmospheric neutrino detectors like Kamiokande, where pions from delta decays cause uncertainties in identifying neutrino flavor.
