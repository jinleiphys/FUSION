# Neutral-current background induced by atmospheric neutrinos at large liquid-scintillator detectors: III. Comprehensive prediction for low energy neutrinos

> Atmospheric neutrinos play a vital role in generating irreducible backgrounds in liquid-scintillator (LS) detectors via their neutral-current (NC) interactions with $^{12}$C nuclei. These interactions may affect a wide range of research areas from the MeV to GeV energy range, such as the reactor and geo neutrinos, diffuse supernova neutrino background (DSNB), dark matter, and nucleon decay searches. In this work, we extend our preceding paper, by conducting a first-time systematic exploration of NC backgrounds as low as the MeV region of reactor and geo neutrinos. We utilize up-to-date neutrino generator models from GENIE and NuWro, a TALYS-based nuclear deexcitation package and a GEANT4-based detector simulation toolkit for our complete calculation. Our primary focus is to predict the NC background for experimental searches of inverse-beta-decay signals below the 100 MeV visible energy. In order to have deeper understanding of the characteristics of atmospheric neutrino NC interactions in LS, we investigate the model dependence of NC background predictions by using various data-driven models, including the initial neutrino-nucleon interactions, nuclear ground-state structure, final-state interactions, nuclear deexcitation processes, and secondary interactions of final-state particles.

## Key claim

This work provides the first systematic prediction of neutral-current (NC) backgrounds from atmospheric neutrino interactions with 12C in liquid-scintillator detectors down to the MeV energy region relevant for reactor and geo neutrino searches, but the predictions carry significant model dependence arising from choices in neutrino generator, axial mass, nuclear ground-state model, final-state interactions, and secondary particle interactions.

## Method

The authors used a three-step calculation pipeline: (1) initial neutrino-nucleon interactions on 12C were simulated using eleven different generator models from GENIE (versions 2.12.0 and 3.0.6) and NuWro (versions 17.10 and 19.02), varying the axial mass MA, nuclear model (relativistic Fermi gas, local Fermi gas, or spectral function), inclusion of two-particle two-hole (2p2h) effects, and final-state interaction model; (2) deexcitation of residual nuclei was handled by the TALYS package; (3) secondary interactions of final-state particles propagating in the liquid scintillator were simulated using GEANT4. The primary focus was predicting NC background for inverse-beta-decay signals below 100 MeV visible energy.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass MA values used across models: 0.96 GeV (GENIE 3.0.6 models), 0.99 GeV (Model-G1 and several NuWro models), 1.03 GeV (world average, used in Model-N2, N6, N7), and 1.35 GeV (MiniBooNE value, Model-N3)
- Atmospheric neutrino energy range considered: 0.1 to 20 GeV
- Visible energy threshold for the primary focus: below 100 MeV
- GENIE version updated from 2.12.0 to 3.0.6; NuWro version updated from 17.10 to 19.02

## Context

This work extends the preceding paper by Cheng et al. (2020), which calculated atmospheric neutrino NC interactions on 12C for DSNB and proton decay searches, by pushing predictions to lower energies relevant for reactor and geo neutrino studies at detectors like JUNO. The study uses updated neutrino generator versions (GENIE 3.0.6 and NuWro 19.02) that have been tuned against neutrino scattering data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA, and adds GEANT4-based detector simulation of secondary interactions that was absent in the prior work. The results enable future liquid-scintillator experiments to quantify irreducible NC backgrounds for reactor neutrino oscillation measurements, DSNB searches, and dark matter detection.
