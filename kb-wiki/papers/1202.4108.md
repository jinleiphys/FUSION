# The pK0Σ+ final state in proton-proton collisions

> This paper reports results from a study of the reaction pp->pK0Σ+ at beam momenta of p_{beam} = 2950, 3059, and 3200 MeV/c (excess energies of ε= 126, 161, and 206 MeV). Total cross sections were determined for all energies; a set of differential cross sections (Dalitz plots; invariant mass spectra of all two-body subsystems; angular distributions of all final state particles; distributions in helicity and Jackson frames) are presented for ε= 161 MeV. The total cross sections are proportional to the volume of available three-body phase-space indicating that the transition matrix element does not change significantly in this range of excess energies. It is concluded from the differential data that the reaction proceeds dominantly via the N(1710)P_{11} and/or N(1720)P_{13} resonance(s); N(1650)S_{11} and Δ(1600)P_{33} could also contribute.

## Key claim

The total cross sections for pp → pK⁰Σ⁺ at excess energies of 126, 161, and 206 MeV are proportional to the available three-body phase-space volume, indicating that the transition matrix element does not change significantly in this range; however, the differential data suggest the reaction proceeds dominantly via the N(1710)P₁₁ and/or N(1720)P₁₃ resonance(s), with possible contributions from N(1650)S₁₁ and Δ(1600)P₃₃.

## Method

The COSY-TOF collaboration measured the reaction pp → pK⁰Σ⁺ at three beam momenta (2950, 3059, and 3200 MeV/c) using the time-of-flight detector at the COSY synchrotron. Events were selected by requiring a prompt proton track from the target, a displaced K⁰_S → π⁺π⁻ decay vertex, and a fourth charged particle from the Σ⁺ decay (either Σ⁺ → pπ⁰ or Σ⁺ → nπ⁺). The Σ⁺ was reconstructed via the missing mass of the pK⁰ system, and acceptance corrections were determined from detailed Monte Carlo simulations modeling the detector response and event reconstruction.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section at ε = 126 MeV: σ_tot = 2.46 ± 0.13 (stat) ± 0.47 (syst) μb
* Total cross section at ε = 161 MeV: σ_tot = 4.13 ± 0.06 (stat) ± 0.79 (syst) μb
* Total cross section at ε = 206 MeV: σ_tot = 7.02 ± 0.36 (stat) ± 1.34 (syst) μb
* Acceptance for the reaction was approximately 1% (0.93% at 126 MeV, 1.05% at 161 MeV, 0.93% at 206 MeV)
* Integrated luminosities: 16.9 nb⁻¹ (ε = 126 MeV), 214 nb⁻¹ (ε = 161 MeV), and 6.4 nb⁻¹ (ε = 206 MeV)
* The ratio of acceptance-corrected Σ⁺ decay channels was N(Σ⁺→pπ⁰)/N(Σ⁺→nπ⁺) = 0.99 ± 0.16, compatible with the accepted branching ratio of 1.07

## Context

This work extends the COSY-TOF program on associated strangeness production, complementing earlier studies on pp → pK⁺Λ and pp → pK⁺Σ⁰ (COSY-TOF, 2010) as well as pp → ppω (Schulte-Wissermann, 2006; Wu, 2009). The data are the first to establish the excitation function for pp → pK⁰Σ⁺ at low excess energies (below 300 MeV), where only bubble chamber data existed from the 1960s (Louttit, 1961; Bierman, 1966; Alexander, 1967; Holmgren, 1968; Sondhi, 1968; Chinowsky, 1968; Firebaugh, 1968; Dunwoodie, 1968) showing a roughly constant cross section of about 20 μb at higher energies. The results enable simultaneous theoretical description of all three NKY channels (pK⁺Λ, pK⁺Σ⁰, pK⁰Σ⁺) and test models incorporating intermediate baryon resonances (Dover, 1993; Sibirtsev, 1999; Tsushima, 1999) as well as older meson-exchange approaches (Ferrari and Serio, 1968; Tsu Yao, 1962).
