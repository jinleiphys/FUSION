# Exotic Doubly Charmed Ds0*(2317)D and Ds1*(2460)D* Molecules

> The $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ heavy meson systems can exchange a kaon that is emitted in S-wave owing to the opposite intrinsic parity of the $D_{s0}^*$($D_{s1}^*$) and $D$($D^*$) mesons. As a consequence of the mass difference of the $D_{s0}^*$($D_{s1}^*$) and $D$($D^*$) mesons, the range of the kaon exchange potential will be significantly longer than expected, corresponding to an effective mass of about $200\,{\rm MeV}$. The potential will be very strong: the strength of the interaction is proportional to $(m_{D_{s0}} - m_D)^2 / f_π^2$ and $(m_{D_{s1}} - m_{D^*})^2 / f_π^2$. This combination of range and strength almost guarantees the existence of $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ bound states with $J^P = 0^{-}$ and $J^P = 0^{-}, 2^{-}$ respectively. Concrete calculations indicate a binding energy of $5-15\,{\rm MeV}$ independently of $J^P$. The $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ molecules have manifestly exotic flavour quantum numbers: $C=2$, $S=1$ and $I=1/2$. We expect the existence of bottom counterparts composed of the $B B_{s0}$ and $B^* B_{s1}^*$ mesons, which will be more bound and have a richer spectrum that might include a shallow P-wave state and an excited S-wave state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $D_{s0}^*(2317) D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460) D^*$ systems "almost guarantee" the existence of bound molecular states, with the hedge that predictions depend moderately on the unknown short-range physics (cut-off) and on whether the $D_{s0}^*$/$D_{s1}^*$ mesons are treated as compact or molecular states.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-kaon-exchange (OKE) potential between S-wave heavy mesons ($D$, $D^*$) and P-wave heavy mesons ($D_{s0}^*$, $D_{s1}^*$) using heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a non-local Gaussian regulator (cut-off $\Lambda = 0.5-1.0$ GeV) to find bound states, and also perform a Faddeev calculation for the three-body $DDK$ system to account for the molecular substructure of the $D_{s0}^*$ and $D_{s1}^*$.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass of the exchanged kaon: $\mu_K \approx 200$ MeV, giving a moderately long-range Yukawa potential.
- Binding energy for $D D_{s0}^*$ (compact scenario, $h=0.7$): $E_B = -(4-13)$ MeV, depending on cut-off.
- Binding energy for $D D_{s0}^*$ (molecular scenario, $\Lambda = 0.5$ GeV): $E_B = -4^{+3}_{-5}$ MeV.
- Binding energy for $D^* D_{s1}^*$ (molecular scenario, $\Lambda = 0.5$ GeV): $E_B = -5^{+3}_{-5}$ MeV.
- Binding energy for $D D_{s0}^*$ (molecular scenario, $\Lambda = 1.0$ GeV): $E_B = -13^{+8}_{-13}$ MeV.
- Binding energy for $D^* D_{s1}^*$ (molecular scenario, $\Lambda = 1.0$ GeV): $E_B = -15^{+9}_{-13}$ MeV.
- Binding condition parameter $\lambda_B$: $9.16 h^2$ for $D D_{s0}^*$ and $10.70 h^2$ for $D^* D_{s1}^*$; bound state exists for $|h| > 0.43$ and $0.40$ respectively.
- Three-body $DDK$ calculation (with $DD$ repulsion): $E_B = -(15-28)$ MeV below $D_{s0}K$ threshold.
- Bottom-sector $B B_{s0}$ binding energy ($\Lambda = 0.5$ GeV): $E_B = -14^{+6}_{-7}$ MeV.
- Bottom-sector $B B_{s0}$ binding energy ($\Lambda = 1.0$ GeV): $E_B = -40^{+20}_{-20}$ MeV.
- Bottom-sector P-wave $B B_{s0}$ binding energy ($\Lambda = 0.5$ GeV): $E_B = -4^{+4}_{-2}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism of enhanced-range kaon exchange previously identified in the $\Lambda_c(2590)\Sigma_c$ system by Geng et al. (2017) to the doubly charmed sector. It contrasts with standard one-pion-exchange (OPE) molecular predictions by Tornqvist (1993) and others, where the tensor force introduces strong cut-off dependence. The predicted $D_{s0}^*(2317)D$ and $D_{s1}^*(2460)D^*$ molecules have manifestly exotic flavor ($C=2$, $S=1$, $I=1/2$) and would be distinguishable from compact tetraquarks, which lattice QCD by Bicudo et al. (2015) and Francis et al. (2016) suggests do not exist in the charm sector. The work enables experimental searches at LHCb via heavy ion collisions and predicts richer spectra in the bottom sector (with $B_{s0}$ and $B_{s1}^*$ mesons not yet observed).
