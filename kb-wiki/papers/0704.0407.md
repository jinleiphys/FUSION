# Density dependent hadronic models and the relation between neutron stars and neutron skin thickness

> In the present work we investigate the main differences in the lead neutron skin thickness, binding energy, surface energy and density profiles obtained with two different density dependent relativistic hadronic models, within the Thomas-Fermi approximation. We show that the asymmetry parameter for low momentum transfer polarized electron scattering is not sensitive to the model parametrization differences.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within the Thomas-Fermi approximation, the asymmetry parameter for low momentum transfer polarized electron scattering is not sensitive to differences between the two density dependent hadronic models studied, even though those models produce different neutron skin thicknesses for lead-208.

## Method

The authors performed Thomas-Fermi calculations for lead-208 using two density dependent relativistic hadronic models: the TW model (with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings) and the NLωρ model (with constant couplings but additional nonlinear σ-ρ and ω-ρ terms). They implemented two different numerical prescriptions: one based on a nucleation method using binodal phase coexistence conditions, and another using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion. Results were compared with the NL3 parametrization of the nonlinear Walecka model.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness for lead-208 with the TW model: approximately 0.18 fm (harmonic oscillator method) and 0.22 fm (nucleation method)
- Neutron skin thickness for lead-208 with NL3: approximately 0.24 fm (harmonic oscillator method) and 0.30 fm (nucleation method)
- For the NLωρ model, as the coupling strength Λv increases, the neutron skin thickness moves from the NL3 value toward the TW value
- The asymmetry parameter at q ≈ 0.4 fm⁻¹ (the PREX experiment momentum transfer) shows less than 1% difference between models, despite up to 0.06 fm differences in neutron skin thickness
- The asymmetry changes by 12% (Dirac) and 11% (Thomas-Fermi) compared to the "no structure" case for the HS parametrization

## Context

This work extends the correlation between neutron skin thickness and neutron star properties established by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001), who showed that models yielding smaller neutron skins in heavy nuclei tend to yield smaller neutron star radii. The authors compare their results with the NL3 parametrization of the nonlinear Walecka model and with the SLy230a Skyrme interaction results from Chabanat et al. The study enables future experimental measurements from the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, which directly affects both neutron skin thickness and neutron star properties.
