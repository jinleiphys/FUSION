# On the absorption and production cross sections of $K$ and $K^*$

> We have computed the isospin and spin averaged cross sections of the processes $πK^*\to ρK$ and $ρK^*\to πK$, which are crucial in the determination of the abundances of $K^*$ and $K$ in heavy ion collisions. Improving previous calculations, we have considered several mechanisms which were missing, such as the exchange of axial and vector resonances ($K_1(1270)$, $K^*_2(1430)$, $h_1(1170)$, etc...) and also other processes such as $πK^*\to ωK, ϕK$ and $ωK^*,\,ϕK^*\to πK$. We find that some of these mechanisms give important contributions to the cross section. Our results also suggest that, in a hadron gas, $K^*$ production might be more important than its absorption.

## Key claim

The authors compute isospin- and spin-averaged cross sections for \(\pi K^* \to \rho K\) and \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\) (and related channels involving \(\omega\) and \(\phi\)), including previously omitted mechanisms such as axial- and vector-resonance exchange and anomalous-parity vertices. Their results suggest that, in a hadron gas, \(K^*\) production may be more important than its absorption, a conclusion that is conditional on the inclusion of these new mechanisms.

## Method

The cross sections are calculated in the center-of-mass frame using effective Lagrangians for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector (PPV), vector-vector-pseudoscalar (VVP), and vector-vector-vector (VVV) vertices, with couplings taken from hidden-local-symmetry and anomaly terms. For each reaction, the authors sum amplitudes from \(t\)-, \(u\)-, and \(s\)-channel diagrams, including exchanges of pseudoscalar mesons, vector mesons, axial resonances (e.g., \(K_1(1270)\), \(h_1(1170)\), \(a_1(1260)\), \(b_1(1235)\), \(f_1(1285)\)), and, for \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\), \(s\)-channel exchange of \(K^*_S\) states (\(0^+\), \(2^+\)) via triangular-loop vertices. The cross sections are then obtained by integrating the squared, spin- and isospin-averaged matrix elements over the Mandelstam \(t\) variable.

## Key numbers

* For \(\pi K^* \to \rho K\), the \(t\)-channel pseudoscalar exchange gives the largest contribution; the total cross section (including all mechanisms) is about one order of magnitude larger than those for \(\pi K^* \to \omega K\) and \(\pi K^* \to \phi K\).
* For \(\pi K^* \to \omega K\), inclusion of \(K_1(1270)\) exchange in the \(s\)-channel produces a significant change in the cross section (dashed vs. solid line in left panel of Fig. 4).
* For \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\), the \(t\)-channel pseudoscalar exchange gives a larger contribution than the \(t\)- or \(u\)-channel vector exchange or the \(u\)-channel pseudoscalar exchange; the \(s\)-channel vector exchange (considered in Cho et al.) gives a very small contribution.
* The final cross sections for \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\), \(\omega K^* \to \pi K\), and \(\phi K^* \to \pi K\) have comparable magnitude.
* The \(s\)-channel exchange of \(K^*_S\) resonances (e.g., \(K^*_2(1430)\) with mass 1431 MeV and width 56 MeV; a \(0^+\) state with mass 1643 MeV and width 48 MeV) gives a very relevant contribution to \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\) (compare Fig. 8 with Fig. 6).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Cho et al. (2003), which considered only a subset of diagrams for \(\pi K^* \to \rho K\) and \(\rho K^* \to \pi K\) and omitted axial-resonance exchange, anomalous-parity vertices, and channels involving \(\omega\) and \(\phi\). The present results are relevant for rate-equation modeling of \(K^*\) and \(K\) abundances in heavy-ion collisions, where the \(K^*/K\) ratio is measured (e.g., \(0.23 \pm 0.05\) in Au+Au at 200 GeV at RHIC and \(0.19 \pm 0.05\) in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV at LHC). The inclusion of the new mechanisms, especially the \(s\)-channel \(K^*_S\) exchange, is expected to strongly affect the predicted production and absorption rates, potentially altering the interpretation of the measured ratio.
