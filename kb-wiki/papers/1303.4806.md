# Charge symmetry breaking from a chiral extrapolation of moments of quark distribution functions

> We present a determination, from lattice QCD, of charge symmetry violation in the spin- independent and spin-dependent parton distribution functions of the nucleon. This is done by chirally extrapolating recent QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice simulations of the first several Mellin moments of the parton distribution functions of octet baryons to the physical point. We find small chiral corrections for the polarized moments, while the corrections are quantitatively significant in the unpolarized case.

# Charge symmetry breaking from a chiral extrapolation of moments of quark distribution functions

## Key claim
Charge symmetry violating (CSV) parton distribution functions in the nucleon are determined from lattice QCD by chirally extrapolating QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice simulations of the first several Mellin moments of octet baryons to the physical point; the chiral corrections are small for polarized moments but quantitatively significant for unpolarized moments, and the results are compatible with a simpler linear flavor expansion about the SU(3) symmetric point when evaluated at that point.

## Method
The authors performed a chiral perturbation theory fit to QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice data for the first spin-independent and zeroth and first spin-dependent Mellin moments of octet baryons, using finite-range regularization with a dipole regulator and a regulator mass of 1 GeV. The isospin-averaged fit parameters were then substituted into full SU(3) isospin-broken chiral perturbation theory expressions for the CSV terms, allowing evaluation at the physical point using the physical up-down quark mass ratio R = 0.553 ± 0.043 from Leutwyler (1996). The unknown overall scaling factors for each moment were fixed by matching the extrapolated isovector moments to experimental values at 4 GeV².

- δΔu¹⁻ = -0.0007(2) at the physical point (spin-dependent, C-odd, u-quark CSV)
- δΔd¹⁻ = -0.0002(1) at the physical point (spin-dependent, C-odd, d-quark CSV)
- δu¹⁺ = -0.0023(7) at the physical point (spin-independent, C-even, u-quark CSV)

- χ²/dof for each moment fit between 0.6 and 0.9

## Context
This work extends the original analysis of QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice data by Horsley et al. (2010) and Cloet et al. (2012), which used a linear flavor expansion about the SU(3) symmetric point to estimate CSV terms. The chiral extrapolation improves on that approach by including loop corrections and extrapolating to physical quark masses. The results agree with previous phenomenological estimates of CSV from the MIT bag model by Rodionov et al. (1994) and Londergan et al. (2003), and with the MRST analysis by Martin et al. (2003). The spin-independent CSV results support the conclusion that partonic CSV effects may reduce the NuTeV anomaly by up to 30%, while the spin-dependent CSV corrections to the Bjorken sum rule appear at the half-percent level, requiring significant improvement over the current 8% precision from the COMPASS experiment by Alekseev et al. (2010).
