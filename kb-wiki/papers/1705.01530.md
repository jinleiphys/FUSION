# Workshop on Chiral Forces in Low Energy Nuclear Physics - the LENPIC Meeting

> We present mini-proceedings of the international workshop on "Chiral Forces in Low Energy Nuclear Physics - the LENPIC Meeting" held at the Jagiellonian University, Krakow, Poland from February 10 to 11, 2017. The workshop focused on the new generation of chiral forces with the semi-local regularization and their applications to few- and many-nucleon systems. Included short contributions summarize talks given by E.Epelbaum, K.Hebeler, R.Roth, H.Witala, A.Calci, P.Reinert, J.P.Vary, H.Kamada, I.Ciepal, H.Krebs, H.Le, K.Topolnicki, K.Vobig and Yu.Volkotrub.

## Key claim

The workshop proceedings report on the status of the LENPIC collaboration's development of chiral nuclear forces and currents complete through at least fourth order (N3LO) in the chiral expansion, with the caveat that the numerical implementation of three-nucleon forces (3NFs) at N3LO remains a work in progress and represents a central challenge, particularly regarding regularization and partial-wave decomposition.

## Method

The LENPIC collaboration derived nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials up to fifth order (N4LO) in the chiral expansion using a local coordinate-space regularization of the long-range interaction, which preserves the analytic structure of the scattering amplitude. For three-nucleon forces, the collaboration implemented a semi-local regularization scheme via convolution integrals in momentum space, computing partial-wave matrix elements at N2LO with work ongoing for N3LO terms. The collaboration also derived consistent electromagnetic, axial, and pseudoscalar nuclear currents up to N3LO, requiring careful regularization to preserve continuity equations.

## Key numbers

- NN potentials developed up to fifth order (N4LO) in the chiral expansion, with all relevant pi N low-energy constants taken from pi N scattering without fine tuning
- 3NF partial-wave matrix elements at N2LO have been completed; N3LO terms are in progress
- NN scattering data fit extended to T_lab = 0-300 MeV using the 2013 Granada database
- For nucleon-deuteron scattering, large 3NF effects are predicted above approximately 60 MeV, with discrepancies between theory and data persisting up to approximately 140 MeV even when 3NFs are included
- For the symmetric-space-star configuration of Nd breakup at 13 MeV, nd and pd data clearly differ and disagree with theoretical predictions

## Context

This workshop proceedings summarizes the LENPIC collaboration's work extending the chiral effective field theory framework developed by Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner (2015) for NN potentials, and by Hebeler, Krebs, Epelbaum, Golak, and Skibinski (2015) for 3NF calculations. The work builds on earlier chiral NN potentials by Epelbaum (2006) and Machleidt and Entem (2011) that used non-local momentum-space regulators, which introduced finite-cutoff artifacts. The new semi-local regularization scheme reduces these artifacts and preserves the analytic structure of the scattering matrix near pion threshold. The results enable precision ab-initio calculations of light and medium-mass nuclei with quantified theoretical uncertainties, including applications to nucleon-deuteron scattering, electroweak reactions, and nuclear matter equations of state. The workshop also discussed using no-core shell model calculations of p-shell nuclei by Roth (2009) and Calci and Roth (2016) to constrain three-body low-energy constants, and NCSM with Continuum calculations by Baroni, Navratil, and Quaglioni (2013) for reactions such as n+4He.
