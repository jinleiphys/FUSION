# BEC-BCS Crossover and the Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Hot and Dense Nuclear Matter

> The effect of nucleon-nucleon correlations in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature is studied beyond BCS theory. Starting from a Hartree-Fock description of nuclear matter with the Gogny effective interaction, we add correlations corresponding to the formation of preformed pairs and scattering states above the superfluid critical temperature within the in-medium T-matrix approach, which is analogous to the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink theory. We calculate the critical temperature for a BEC superfluid of deuterons, of a BCS superfluid of nucleons, and in the crossover between these limits. The effect of the correlations on thermodynamic properties (equation of state, energy, entropy) and the liquid-gas phase transition is discussed. Our results show that nucleon-nucleon correlations beyond BCS play an important role for the properties of nuclear matter, especially in the low-density region.

## Key claim

The authors show that nucleon-nucleon correlations beyond BCS theory, treated via an in-medium T-matrix approach analogous to Nozières-Schmitt-Rink theory, significantly modify the thermodynamic properties of symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, particularly at low densities, but the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition remains unchanged at 15.9 MeV due to the perturbative treatment of correlations.

## Method

Starting from a Hartree-Fock description of symmetric nuclear matter with the Gogny D1 effective interaction, the authors add pair correlations above the superfluid critical temperature using the in-medium T-matrix (ladder) approximation with a separable Yamaguchi potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels. The correlation contribution to the density is separated into bound-state (deuteron) and scattering-state contributions, and the pressure is obtained by thermodynamic integration of the density over chemical potential, using the Hartree-Fock density as integration variable to handle the first-order liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Maximum superfluid critical temperature: \(T_c = 4.5\) MeV at density \(n = 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) (full calculation), compared to 7.2 MeV at \(n = 0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\) in the earlier work of Schmidt et al. (2000) without Gogny mean field
- Critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition: \(T_c^\text{liq-gas} = 15.9\) MeV, unchanged from the mean-field result of Ventura et al. (1998)
- Mott density at \(T = 5\) MeV: 0.07 fm\(^{-3}\); at \(T = 10\) MeV: 0.12 fm\(^{-3}\); at \(T = 15.9\) MeV: 0.18 fm\(^{-3}\); at \(T = 20\) MeV: 0.22 fm\(^{-3}\)
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Vacuum deuteron binding energy: \(E_b^0 = -2.225\) MeV, reproduced by the Yamaguchi potential with parameters \(\beta = 1.4488\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\lambda_{^1S_0} = 2994\) MeV fm\(^{-1}\), and \(\lambda_{^3S_1} = 4264\) MeV fm\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink approach previously applied to nuclear matter by the Rostock group (Schmidt et al., 2000; Stein et al., 2000) by incorporating the Gogny mean field, which provides a realistic description of nuclear saturation and the liquid-gas phase transition. The results enable future studies of the interplay between superfluidity and the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, though the authors note that screening effects (investigated by Cao et al., 2006) and more self-consistent treatments including deuteron clusters (Roepke et al., 1998) would be needed to reduce the liquid-gas critical temperature below the mean-field value.
