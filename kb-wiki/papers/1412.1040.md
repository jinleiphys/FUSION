# Polyakov-loop suppression of colored states in a quark-meson-diquark plasma

> A quark-meson-diquark plasma is considered within the PNJL model for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and restoration in quark matter. Based on a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to mesons and diquarks we present the thermodynamics of this system including the Mott dissociation of mesons and diquarks at finite temperature. A striking result is the suppression of the diquark abundance below the chiral restoration temperature by the coupling to the Polyakov loop, because of their color degree of freedom. This is understood in close analogy to the suppression of quark distributions by the same mechanism. Mesons as color singlets are unaffected by the Polyakov-loop suppression. At temperatures above the chiral restoration mesons and diquarks are both suppressed due to the Mott effect, whereby the positive resonance contribution to the pressure is largely compensated by the negative scattering contribution in accordance with the Levinson theorem.

# Polyakov-loop suppression of colored states in a quark-meson-diquark plasma

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop coupling suppresses the abundance of colored diquark states below the chiral restoration temperature, in close analogy to the suppression of quark distributions, while color-singlet mesons remain unaffected. This suppression is demonstrated most clearly in the superstrong coupling case where pions and diquarks have degenerate masses, so that the only difference between their thermodynamic contributions is their color charge.

## Method

The authors employ a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with scalar-pseudoscalar meson and scalar diquark interaction channels, using a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to describe two-particle correlations. The thermodynamic potential is evaluated in Gaussian approximation, with the diquark color trace explicitly computed to incorporate the Polyakov-loop background field, and the phase shifts for mesons and diquarks are obtained from the in-medium Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Key numbers

- Bare quark mass: m0 = 5.5 MeV; three-momentum cutoff: Lambda = 639 MeV; scalar coupling constant: GS * Lambda^2 = 2.134
- Vacuum constituent quark mass: 319 MeV; pion mass: 138 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Vacuum sigma-meson mass: 644 MeV (slightly unbound)
- Scalar diquark masses for three coupling cases: (A) Fierz value GD/GS = 3/4 (unbound); (B) strong coupling GD/GS = 1.0, m_D = 582 MeV; (C) superstrong coupling GD/GS = 1.5, m_D = m_pi (degenerate with pion)
- Total pressure is dominated by pion gas up to T ~ 120 MeV and by quark-gluon plasma above T ~ 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for two-quark correlations in quark matter, previously developed by Blaschke et al. (2013), to include Polyakov-loop coupling following the method of Wergieluk et al. (2012). It builds on the PNJL model framework established by Fukushima (2003), Ratti et al. (2005), and others, which introduced Polyakov-loop suppression of colored quark states. The results demonstrate that diquark states are suppressed in the confined phase analogously to quarks, which is a necessary step toward a Faddeev-type description of baryons as three-quark bound states in quark matter, as pursued by Cahill et al. (1988) and Reinhardt (1989). The approach enables future quantitative comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics from Borsanyi et al. (2010) and Bazavov et al. (2014), provided more hadronic states are included.
