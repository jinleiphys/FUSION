# QCD predictions for physical multimeson scattering amplitudes

> We use lattice QCD calculations of the finite-volume spectra of systems of two and three mesons to determine, for the first time, three-particle scattering amplitudes with physical quark masses. Our results are for combinations of $π^+$ and $K^+$, at a lattice spacing $a=0.063\;$fm, and in the isospin-symmetric limit. We also obtain accurate results for maximal-isospin two-meson amplitudes, with those for $π^+ K^+$ and $2K^+$ being the first determinations at the physical point. Dense lattice spectra are obtained using the stochastic Laplacian-Heaviside method, and the analysis leading to scattering amplitudes is done using the relativistic finite-volume formalism. Results are compared to chiral perturbation theory and to phenomenological fits to experimental data, finding good agreement.

# QCD predictions for physical multimeson scattering amplitudes

## Key claim

For the first time, three-particle scattering amplitudes at physical quark masses are determined from lattice QCD, specifically for combinations of pi+ and K+ in maximal-isospin channels where no resonances appear, and the results are compared to chiral perturbation theory and phenomenological fits, finding good agreement within the expected range of validity near threshold.

## Method

The authors use lattice QCD ensembles from the CLS collaboration with a fixed lattice spacing of 0.063 fm and physical pion and kaon masses (E250 ensemble), extracting finite-volume energy spectra for two- and three-meson systems using the stochastic Laplacian-Heaviside method. They apply the relativistic finite-volume formalism, solving quantization conditions for two- and three-particle K matrices, then solving integral equations to obtain physical three-meson scattering amplitudes in partial waves J^P = 0^-, 1^+, and 2^-.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.063 fm, with M_pi = 130 MeV and M_K = 500 MeV on the physical E250 ensemble
- M_pi L = 4.05 on E250, with 505 gauge configurations used
- Combined fits to 2pi+ and 3pi+ systems used 34 + 32 energy levels with 6 parameters, yielding chi^2/DOF = 1.14
- Combined fits to 2K+ and 3K+ systems used 40 + 53 energy levels with 6 parameters, yielding chi^2/DOF = 1.49
- Combined fits to 2K+, pi+K+, and 2K+pi+ systems used 25 + 40 + 50 energy levels with 10 parameters, yielding chi^2/DOF = 1.95
- All fits have chi^2 per degree of freedom lower than 2

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of three-pion systems at heavier quark masses, such as the HadSpec collaboration's calculation at M_pi = 391 MeV (Hansen 2020) and earlier studies at M_pi approximately 200 MeV (Horz 2019, Blanton 2019, Mai 2019). It provides the first physical-point determinations of pi+K+ and 2K+ two-meson amplitudes, and the first three-meson amplitudes with J^P = 1+ and 2-. The results enable future studies of three-particle resonances such as the omega(782) and a1(1260), and can be compared to chiral perturbation theory predictions (Bijnens 2021, 2022; Baeza-Ballesteros 2023, 2024) using the Inverse Amplitude Method.
