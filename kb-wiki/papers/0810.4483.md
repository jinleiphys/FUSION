# Faddeev-type calculations of few-body nuclear reactions including Coulomb interaction

> The method of screening and renormalization is used to include the Coulomb interaction between the charged particles in the description of few-body nuclear reactions. Calculations are done in the framework of Faddeev-type equations in momentum-space. The reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on observables is discussed.

## Key claim

The method of screening and renormalization can reliably include the Coulomb interaction in momentum-space Faddeev-type calculations of few-body nuclear reactions, provided the screening radius is chosen large enough for convergence; however, the procedure fails at zero relative energy between charged particles and converges slowly at very low energies, requiring extrapolation.

## Method

The author solves Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations in momentum-space partial-wave representation for three- and four-particle scattering, using a screened Coulomb potential of the form w_R(r) = w(r) exp[-(r/R)^n] with screening radius R and smoothness parameter n between 3 and 8. The diverging screened Coulomb contributions are isolated using the two-potential formalism and renormalized by phase factors, with the pure Coulomb amplitude known analytically and the short-range part calculated numerically at finite R and extrapolated to the R → ∞ limit.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius R = 10 to 30 fm is sufficient for convergence in most cases, with exceptions at very low energies and near pp final-state interaction regimes
- The method yields converged results for pp scattering at 0.01 MeV center-of-mass energy, contrary to claims that it fails below 0.1 MeV
- Smoothness parameter values 3 ≤ n ≤ 8 provide sufficiently rapid screening while avoiding oscillatory behavior in momentum-space representation
- The Coulomb repulsion converts the pp final-state interaction peak into a minimum with zero cross section at E_rel = 0 in p-d breakup and photodisintegration of ³He

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach of Taylor (1974) and Semon (1975) from two-particle scattering to few-body nuclear reactions, improving upon earlier limited applications to p-d scattering by Alt (1994, 2002) and Berthold (1990) that used quasiparticle equations with rank-1 separable potentials and approximated the screened Coulomb transition matrix. The method enables calculations of p-d elastic scattering and breakup, p-d radiative capture, photo- and electrodisintegration of ³He, α-d scattering, and reactions on stable and halo nuclei, and has been used to test approximate methods such as Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC), Glauber, and Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA).
