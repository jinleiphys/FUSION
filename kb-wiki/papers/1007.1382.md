# Chiral perturbation theory calculation for pn -> dpipi at threshold

> We investigate the reaction pn -> dpipi in the framework of Chiral Perturbation Theory. For the first time a complete calculation of the leading order contributions is presented. We identify various diagrams that are of equal importance as compared to those recognized in earlier works. The diagrams at leading order behave as expected by the power counting. Also for the first time the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the initial, intermediate and final state is included consistently and found to be very important. This study provides a theoretical basis for a controlled evaluation of the non-resonant contributions in two-pion production reactions in nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first complete leading-order calculation of the reaction pn → dππ within Chiral Perturbation Theory, identifying several diagrams of equal importance that were missing in earlier works; however, the expansion parameter is large (χ ≈ 0.54), so the authors caution that the chiral expansion may converge slowly and that higher-order contributions are needed to check convergence.

## Method

The authors constructed production operators from the effective chiral Lagrangian at leading order (O(χ)) and convoluted them with phenomenological NN wave functions from the CCF and CD-Bonn potentials in a hybrid approach, since ChPT NN interactions are not available at the relevant energies. The amplitudes were evaluated at threshold for the reaction pn → dπ⁰π⁰, where only the ³S₁ and ³D₁ partial waves of the initial NN state contribute, and the ππ system is in a relative S-wave with isospin zero. The NN interaction in the initial, intermediate, and final states was included consistently for the first time.

## Key numbers

- The initial center-of-mass momentum at two-pion production threshold is p_thr^(2) = √(2m_π M_N) ≈ 510 MeV.
- The expansion parameter is χ = p_thr^(2)/M_N ≈ 0.54.
- The individual amplitudes for the diagrams (in units of 10⁻² × MeV⁻¹) range from about -23.9 to +37.9 for the Born terms, with ISI and ImSI corrections of comparable magnitude (e.g., diagram 1b Born amplitude is -23.9, ISI contributions are 4 + i7.3 and 3 + i, ImSI is 14 + i7.5).
- The sum of all amplitudes for the ³S₁ partial wave is -6.5 + i9.6 (diagram 1b) and for the ³D₁ partial wave is 10.5 - i5 (diagram 1b).
- The predicted cross section at excess energy Q = 70 MeV is roughly a factor of two smaller than the central value of the existing data point, though the experimental uncertainty is very large (a factor of 25 larger than the data themselves).

## Context

This work extends the ChPT framework previously applied to single-pion production in NN collisions (Weinberg 1990, 1991; Cohen et al. 1996; Lensky et al. 2005) to the two-pion production channel pn → dππ, which had only been studied phenomenologically in earlier works (Russo et al. 1995; Cao et al. 2006). The calculation provides a theoretical basis for controlled evaluation of non-resonant contributions in two-pion production reactions and enables future inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance at NLO and the Roper resonance at NNLO, which will be necessary for comparison with experimental data at higher energies.
