# Recent developments in $q_T$ subtraction: EW corrections and power suppressed contributions

> The $q_T$ subtraction formalism represents a well established and successful technique to deal with the computation of QCD radiative corrections up to NNLO (and beyond) for a large class of processes relevant at the LHC. We have explored the possibility to apply $q_T$ subtraction to the computation of EW corrections with the (final) aim to develop a subtraction formalism suitable for the computation of mixed QCD-EW corrections. We present numerical results for the complete NLO EW corrections to the Drell-Yan production of a massive lepton pair. Furthermore, we have investigated the structure of the power suppressed contributions at small-$q_T$ in this process and we present new analytical results on the effects of the soft radiation emitted off a charged massive final state.

# Recent developments in $q_T$ subtraction: EW corrections and power suppressed contributions

## Key claim

The $q_T$ subtraction formalism, previously applied only to QCD corrections, can be extended to compute NLO electroweak corrections for Drell-Yan production, with the ultimate goal of developing a subtraction scheme for mixed QCD-EW corrections. The linear dependence on the slicing parameter $r_{\text{cut}}$ observed in the dominant $q\bar{q}$ channel arises specifically from soft radiation off the massive final-state leptons, not from initial-state radiation.

## Method

The authors applied the $q_T$ subtraction formalism to NLO EW corrections for Drell-Yan production of a massive lepton pair, using an abelianisation procedure to adapt the method from heavy-quark production. They implemented the calculation as an extension of an existing numerical program, computing tree-level matrix elements analytically and virtual EW corrections using Gosam, with validation against dipole subtraction (CS+Recola) and the Sanc generator. For the power corrections, they performed an analytical computation of the partonic cross section in the diagonal $q\bar{q}$ channel by expanding the real emission matrix element in the small-$r_{\text{cut}}$ limit, separating final-state radiation, initial-state radiation, and their interference.

## Key numbers

- NLO EW correction in the $q\bar{q}$ channel: $\Delta\sigma_{q\overline{q}} = -5.920 \pm 0.034$ pb (using $q_T$ subtraction with Gosam) compared to $-5.919 \pm 0.008$ pb (dipole subtraction with Recola), at 14 TeV with $m_l = 10$ GeV
- NLO EW correction in the $q\gamma$ channel: $\Delta\sigma_{q\gamma} = -0.6694 \pm 0.0008$ pb (using $q_T$ subtraction) compared to $-0.6690 \pm 0.0005$ pb (dipole subtraction)
- For physical muon mass $m_\mu = 105.658369$ MeV: combined correction $\Delta\sigma_{q\overline{q}}+\Delta\sigma_{q\gamma} = -29.95 \pm 0.04$ pb ($q_T$ subtraction) versus $-29.99 \pm 0.02$ pb (Sanc generator)
- The leading power correction for final-state radiation is linear in $r_{\text{cut}}$: $\sigmah^{\rm FS}_{\rm NLP}(s;\rcut) \propto r_{\text{cut}}$, with coefficient involving $\frac{3\pi}{8} \left[ \frac{6(5-\beta^2)}{3-\beta^2} + \frac{-47+8\beta^2+3\beta^4}{\beta(3-\beta^2)}\ln\frac{1+\beta}{1-\beta} \right]$
- The leading power correction for initial-state radiation is quadratic in $r_{\text{cut}}$: $\sigmah^{\rm IS}_{\rm NLP}(s;\rcut) \propto r_{\text{cut}}^2$ (modulo logarithmic enhancements)

## Context

This work extends the $q_T$ subtraction formalism, originally formulated by Catani and Grazzini (2007) for color-singlet production at NNLO, to the computation of electroweak corrections for the first time. It builds on prior work on transverse momentum resummation for heavy quark pair production (Zhu 2012, Li 2013, Catani 2014, Angeles-Martinez 2018) and the extension of $q_T$ subtraction to $t\bar{t}$ production (Catani 2019). The observed linear $r_{\text{cut}}$ dependence for final-state radiation mirrors the linear behavior found in heavy-quark production (Catani 2017, 2019), and the authors demonstrate that this linear dependence can be removed by a local soft subtraction. This work enables future computations of mixed QCD-EW corrections (Dittmaier 2014, 2015, de Florian 2018, Delto 2019, Bonciani 2019) and NNLO QED corrections to Drell-Yan production.
