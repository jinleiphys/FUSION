# Hyperonic crystallization in hadronic matter

> Published in Hadrons, Nuclei and Applications, World Scientific, Singapore, Proc.of the Conference Bologna2000. Structure of the Nucleus at the Dawn of the Century, G. Bonsignori, M. Bruno, A. Ventura, D. Vretenar Editors, pag. 319.

# Hyperonic crystallization in hadronic matter

## Key claim
The paper proposes that at densities around 1.5 times nuclear saturation and beyond, hyperons in neutron star matter may form a spatially ordered crystalline phase on a cubic lattice, which would be energetically favoured over the usual uniform gaseous configuration. This claim is preliminary and based on a highly simplified model that treats the nucleon background as uniform and neglects central and spin-spin interactions between hyperons in the same lattice node.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field model with scalar and vector meson exchange to describe interactions among nucleons and a single species of neutral hyperons. They apply condensed-matter techniques to treat hyperons as localized on the nodes of a cubic lattice, with the confining potential at each node approximated as harmonic and determined self-consistently from the superposition of Gaussian hyperon clouds and the nucleon mean field. The relative abundances of protons, neutrons, hyperons, and electrons are obtained from β-equilibrium conditions coupled to the mean-field equations, and the free energies of the liquid and solid phases are compared to locate a first-order phase transition.

## Key numbers
- Hyperons appear in hadronic matter at densities about **1.5 times nuclear saturation** and beyond.
- For a sample calculation, the crystal cell size is **a = 2.2142 fm**.
- The scalar meson cutoff is **Λ_σ = 1.4 GeV** and the vector meson cutoff is **Λ_ω = 2.5 GeV**.
- The hyperon–meson coupling constants relative to nucleon values are **g_σH/g_σN = 0.6** and **g_ωH/g_ωN = 0.65**, using Walecka values for the meson–nucleon interaction.

## Context
This work extends the standard relativistic mean-field treatment of hyperonic matter (Lattimer, prior work) by introducing the possibility of a spatially ordered solid phase, analogous to the "free electron model" in a Coulomb lattice (Ashcroft, prior work). The method enables calculation of the equation of state for the ordered phase and the parameters of a first-order fluid-to-crystal transition, which could affect neutron star structure and cooling. The authors note that screening effects of the nucleonic plasma and mixing of hadronic fields remain to be treated properly, and that central and spin-spin interactions between hyperons in the same node are neglected in this first approximation.
