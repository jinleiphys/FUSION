# Structure of even-even nuclei using a mapped collective Hamiltonian and the D1S Gogny interaction

> A systematic study of low energy nuclear structure at normal deformation is carried out using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory extended by the Generator Coordinate Method and mapped onto a 5-dimensional collective quadrupole Hamiltonian. Results obtained with the Gogny D1S interaction are presented from dripline to dripline for even-even nuclei with proton numbers Z=10 to Z=110 and neutron numbers N less than 200. The properties calculated for the ground states are their charge radii, 2-particle separation energies, correlation energies, and the intrinsic quadrupole shape parameters. For the excited spectroscopy, the observables calculated are the excitation energies and quadrupole as well as monopole transition matrix elements. We examine in this work the yrast levels up to J=6, the lowest excited 0^+ states, and the two next yrare 2^+ states. The theory is applicable to more than 90% of the nuclei which have tabulated measurements. The data set of the calculated properties of 1712 even-even nuclei, including spectroscopic properties for 1693 of them, are provided in CEA website and EPAPS repository with this article \cite{epaps}.

## Key claim

The CHFB+5DCH theory using the Gogny D1S interaction provides a systematic description of low-energy nuclear structure for even-even nuclei from dripline to dripline, but the authors explicitly note several caveats: the theory neglects two- and higher-order quasiparticle excitations, uses an adiabatic Hamiltonian that affects reliability at high angular momentum, and the mapping to the collective Hamiltonian is problematic for very rigid nuclei such as doubly magic ones.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations were performed on a large triaxial grid of quadrupole deformations for even-even nuclei with Z=10 to Z=110 and N less than 200, using the Gogny D1S interaction. The resulting potential energy surfaces and inertial masses were used to construct a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) via the Generator Coordinate Method with the Gaussian Overlap Approximation. The 5DCH was then solved numerically to obtain ground state properties and excited state spectra up to J=6, including yrast levels, the lowest excited 0+ states, and the two next yrare 2+ states.

## Key numbers

- Predicted charge radii have an accuracy of 0.6% compared to experiment
- On average, predicted excitation energy of the first 2+ state is 12% higher than experiment, with variances of 40-50%
- On average, predicted transition strength of the first 2+ excitation is 22% higher than experiment, with variances of 40-50%
- Predicted excitation energies of the first excited 0+ state are systematically 50% high compared to experiment
- The data set includes calculated properties of 1712 even-even nuclei, with spectroscopic properties for 1693 of them
- The distribution of the collective structure indicator R42 = E(4+1)/E(2+1) has a very sharp peak at the value 10/3, in agreement with existing data
- For 76Kr, the calculated 2+1 excitation energy is 21% higher than experiment; yrast 4+1 and 6+1 states are within 10% of experimental values
- For 152Sm, the 2+1 excitation energy is within 2% of the experimental value; the calculated R42 ratio is 3.0, matching experiment

## Context

This work is a major extension of the authors' earlier study of low-lying quadrupole excitations (Bertsch et al., 2007) and provides a systematic benchmark for the CHFB+5DCH methodology. It extends the global self-consistent mean-field calculations initiated by Tajima et al. (1996) and Lalazissis et al. (1996), and complements recent applications of the 5DCH mapping to Skyrme and relativistic mean-field Hamiltonians (Klimo et al., 2008; Niksic et al., 2009). The results serve as a baseline for comparisons with new Gogny force parameterizations and enable future studies of exotic nuclei near the driplines, where deviations from predicted accuracy would signal new phenomena in nuclear structure.
