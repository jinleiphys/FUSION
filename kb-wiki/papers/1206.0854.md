# Energy spectra of small bosonic clusters having a large two-body scattering length

> In this work we investigate small clusters of bosons using the hyperspherical harmonic basis. We consider systems with $A=2,3,4,5,6$ particles interacting through a soft inter-particle potential. In order to make contact with a real system, we use an attractive gaussian potential that reproduces the values of the dimer binding energy and the atom-atom scattering length obtained with one of the most widely used $^4$He-$^4$He interactions, the LM2M2 potential. The intensity of the potential is varied in order to explore the clusters' spectra in different regions with large positive and large negative values of the two-body scattering length. In addition, we include a repulsive three-body force to reproduce the trimer binding energy. With this model, consisting in the sum of a two- and three-body potential, we have calculated the spectrum of the four, five and six particle systems. In all the region explored, we have found that these systems present two bound states, one deep and one shallow close to the $A-1$ threshold. Some universal relations between the energy levels are extracted; in particular, we have estimated the universal ratios between thresholds of the three-, four-, and five-particle continuum using the two-body gaussian

# Energy spectra of small bosonic clusters having a large two-body scattering length

## Key claim
For bosonic clusters with A=4,5,6 particles interacting through a soft potential tuned to reproduce the LM2M2 helium-helium scattering length and dimer binding energy, each system presents exactly two bound states (one deep and one shallow close to the A-1 threshold) across the entire explored region of large positive and large negative scattering lengths, but the shallow states for A=5 and A=6 become unbound for negative scattering lengths when a repulsive three-body force is included, depending on the range of that force.

## Method
The authors constructed an attractive two-body gaussian potential with range R0=10 a.u. and varied its strength to reproduce the scattering lengths given by the modified LM2M2 potential V_lambda(r) across lambda values from 0.883 to 1.1. They added a repulsive hypercentral three-body gaussian force with range rho0=10 a.u., tuned to reproduce the trimer ground state energy E3^0 from the modified LM2M2 potential. The bound-state spectra for A=3-6 were computed using the non-symmetrized hyperspherical harmonic (NSHH) expansion method for the soft potentials, while the three-body calculations with the hard-core LM2M2 potential used the correlated hyperspherical harmonic (CHH) expansion.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Efimov physics in three- and four-body systems (Braaten 2006, Efimov 1970, 1971) to clusters with A=5 and A=6 particles. It builds on the authors' prior development of the NSHH method for up to six particles (Gattobigio 2009, 2011) and their demonstration that a soft gaussian potential can reproduce LM2M2 helium data (Kievsky 2011, Gattobigio 2011). The results confirm the universal two-level spectrum structure predicted for four-body systems (Platter 2004, von Stecher 2009, Deltuva 2011) and extend this observation to five- and six-body systems. The calculated threshold ratios agree with experiments on ultracold atoms (Pollack 2009, Zaccanti 2009, Ferlaino 2009, Zenesini 2012) and with theoretical predictions (von Stecher 2010). The work enables further exploration of universal few-body physics beyond four particles and provides benchmark calculations for testing effective field theory approaches.
