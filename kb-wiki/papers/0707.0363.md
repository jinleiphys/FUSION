# Detecting bubbles in exotic nuclei

> The occurrence of a bubble, due to an inversion of s$_{1/2}$ state with the state usually located above, is investigated. Proton bubbles in neutron-rich Argon isotopes are optimal candidates. Pairing effects which can play against the bubble formation are evaluated. They cannot prevent bubble formation in very neutron-rich argon isotopes such as $^{68}$Ar. This pleads for a measurement of the charge density of neutron-rich argon isotopes in the forthcoming years, with the advent of electron scattering experiments in next generation exotic beam facilities such as FAIR or RIBF.

# Detecting bubbles in exotic nuclei

## Key claim

Proton bubbles can occur in very neutron-rich argon isotopes such as ⁶⁸Ar due to an inversion of the 2s₁/₂ and 1d₃/₂ proton states, and pairing effects cannot prevent bubble formation in these extremely neutron-rich systems; however, in less neutron-rich candidates such as ⁴⁶Ar the bubble hypothesis remains uncertain because pairing effects can populate the 2s₁/₂ state up to approximately 50% occupation probability, significantly weakening the central density depletion.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed using the SkI5 and SLy4 Skyrme interactions, with a delta density-dependent pairing interaction for the HFB cases. The proton densities, charge radii, and form factors for elastic electron scattering at 300 MeV were computed, and giant resonance responses (isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole) as well as low-lying quadrupole states were calculated using HF+RPA and HFB+QRPA approaches.

## Key numbers

- Measured charge radius of ⁴⁶Ar: R_c = 3.44 ± 0.01 fm (laser spectroscopy at ISOLDE)
- Calculated charge radius difference between bubble and no-bubble hypotheses for ⁴⁶Ar: approximately 0.02 fm, too small for a clear signature
- Predicted B(E2) value for the first 2⁺ state in ⁴⁶Ar: 256 e²·fm⁴ (bubble case, SkI5) versus 24 e²·fm⁴ (no-bubble case, SLy4)
- Measured B(E2) for ⁴⁶Ar: 218 ± 31 e²·fm⁴ (Coulomb excitation, Gade 2003)
- Monopole resonance lowest state shift in ⁴⁶Ar: from 14.5 MeV (no bubble) to 16.0 MeV (bubble)
- Pairing occupation probability of the 2s₁/₂ state in ⁴⁶Ar: 54% (HFB calculation)
- Pairing interaction strength: V₀ = -330 MeV·fm³ (reproducing two-proton separation energy of ⁴⁶Ar)
- Electron scattering optimal angular range for bubble detection: 40 degrees to 100 degrees at 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier bubble predictions in ⁴⁶Ar from relativistic mean-field calculations by Todd-Rudel et al. (2004) and the analysis of s-d state inversion mechanisms by Grasso et al. (2007). It contradicts the earlier conclusion by Beiner and Lombard (1973) that bubble formation in the s-d region is prevented when using more elaborated interactions. The paper enables experimental searches for proton bubbles via electron scattering at next-generation facilities such as FAIR and RIBF, and motivates direct reaction studies on neutron-rich argon isotopes beyond ⁴⁶Ar, particularly ⁶⁰Ar and ⁶²Ar.
