# Neutrino Cross Sections: Low Energy

> Low-energy neutrino interactions with isolated nucleons are accurately described by the effective theory based on Fermi's groundbreaking description of neutron $β$-decay. On the other hand, the extension of this scheme to the case of neutrino interactions with nuclear matter -- the understanding of which is critical for the description of a variety of astrophysical processes -- involves non trivial difficulties, originating from the complexity of nuclear structure and dynamics. This chapter provides a concise introduction to the formalism of nuclear many-body theory, suitable to perform theoretical calculations of the nuclear matter response to neutrino interactions, as well as a detailed analysis of the relevant reaction mechanisms. The neutrino mean free path in nuclear matter and its implications for the description of astrophysical processes are also discussed.

# Neutrino Cross Sections: Low Energy

## Key claim

Low-energy neutrino interactions with isolated nucleons are accurately described by Fermi's effective theory, but extending this scheme to neutrino interactions with nuclear matter involves non-trivial difficulties originating from the complexity of nuclear structure and dynamics, and theoretical calculations necessarily rely on simplified models whose accuracy depends significantly on the kinematics of the process under consideration.

## Method

The author derives the neutrino-nucleon cross section in free space using Fermi's effective theory of weak interactions, then introduces a formalism based on non-relativistic nuclear many-body theory to describe neutrino interactions in nuclear matter. The nuclear matter response is computed using the Correlated Basis Functions (CBF) formalism and cluster expansion technique, which provides a consistent framework to derive effective nuclear Hamiltonians from state-of-the-art models of nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials. The effects of mean-field approximation, short-range correlations, and long-range correlations on the weak transition amplitudes and response functions are analyzed systematically using the non-interacting Fermi gas model as a baseline.

## Context

This chapter extends the formalism of Fermi's effective theory of neutron beta-decay, originally developed for isolated nucleons, to the case of neutrino interactions with nuclear matter, which is critical for describing astrophysical processes including supernova explosions, neutron star cooling, and binary neutron star mergers. The work builds upon prior studies of electron-nucleus scattering that confirmed the quenching of occupation probability of Fermi sea states due to short-range correlations (RevModPhys.65.817), and on earlier theoretical investigations of weak transition amplitudes in nuclear matter (burrows1998, burrows1999). The CBF formalism described enables future calculations of the neutrino mean free path in nuclear matter and its astrophysical implications for neutron star physics.
