# Thermodynamics of magnetized BPS baryonic layers and the effects of the Isospin chemical potential

> Through the Hamilton-Jacobi equation of classical mechanics, BPS magnetized Baryonic layers (possessing both baryonic charge and magnetic flux) have been constructed in the gauged non-linear sigma model (G-NLSM) minimally coupled to Maxwell theory, which is one of the most relevant effective theories for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) in the strongly interacting low-energy limit which also takes into account the electromagnetic interactions. Since the topological charge that naturally appears on the right hand side of the BPS bound is a non-linear function of the baryonic charge, the thermodynamics of these magnetized Baryonic layers is highly non-trivial. In this work, using tools from the theory of Casimir effect, we derive analytical relationship between baryonic charge, topological charge, magnetic flux and relevant thermodynamical quantities (such as pressure, specific heat and magnetic susceptibility) of these layers. The critical Baryonic chemical potential is identified. Quite interestingly, the grand canonical partition function can be related with the Riemann zeta function. On the technical side, it is quite a remarkable result to derive explicit expressions for all these thermodynamics quantities of a strongly interacting magnetized system at finite Baryon density. The effects of the Isospin chemical potential can be included as well: in particular, we will be able to construct explicitly the BPS bound and the corresponding BPS configurations also in the case in which the Isospin chemical potential is non-zero. The physical interpretations of our analytical results will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical thermodynamic quantities for magnetized baryonic layers in the gauged non-linear sigma model, but the baryonic charge does not take integer values due to the map U failing to define a closed cycle; integer baryon numbers must be imposed "by hands" and are only possible up to a maximum value determined by the system parameters.

## Method

The authors construct static BPS magnetized baryonic layers using the gauged non-linear sigma model minimally coupled to Maxwell theory, employing a parametrization of the SU(2) field in generalized Euler angles and a specific ansatz for the gauge field. They apply the Hamilton-Jacobi equation to derive a BPS bound and first-order equations, then use expansions in terms of Bessel functions (borrowed from Casimir effect theory) to obtain analytical approximations for the integration constant I0, which relates baryonic charge, topological charge, and magnetic flux.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is set to fπ = 2√2 fm⁻¹ ≈ 180 MeV, giving K = fπ²/4 = 2 fm⁻²
- The box volume is V = 4π³LrL², with Lr ≈ 25 fm and L = p/√(2K) from the condition L = p/√(2K)
- The maximum baryonic charge per p² is lim(I0→∞) B/p² = 8√K Lr π, which for K=2 fm⁻² and Lr=25 fm gives nmax = [8√K Lr π]
- The magnetic flux corresponding to √(2K)Φ/(p²πLr) = 1 is approximately Φ ≈ 10⁵ fm²
- The critical I0 value separating the two approximation regimes is of order I0 ≈ 10⁻¹²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous constructions of BPS magnetized baryonic layers (Canfora et al., 2023; Canfora et al., 2024; Canfora et al., 2025) by adding thermodynamic analysis and the effects of isospin chemical potential. It provides analytical tools for studying strongly interacting magnetized systems at finite baryon density, which are relevant for the nuclear lasagna phase in neutron stars, complementing numerical studies of nuclear pasta phases (Ravenhall et al., 1983; Hashimoto et al., 1984; Horowitz et al., 2015). The approach enables future work on the equation of state and speed of sound for these configurations, and allows inclusion of the isospin chemical potential through modified coupling constants.
