# Virial corrections to simulations of heavy ion reactions

> Within QMD simulations we demonstrate the effect of virial corrections on heavy ion reactions. Unlike in standard codes, the binary collisions are treated as non-local so that the contribution of the collision flux to the reaction dynamics is covered. A comparison with standard QMD simulations shows that the virial corrections lead to a broader proton distribution bringing theoretical spectra closer towards experimental values. Complementary BUU simulations reveal that the non-locality enhances the collision rate in the early stage of the reaction. It suggests that the broader distribution appears due to an enhanced pre-equilibrium emission of particles.

# Virial corrections to simulations of heavy ion reactions

## Key claim
Non-local treatment of binary collisions in QMD simulations, derived from a quasi-classical kinetic equation with T-matrix evaluated from the Paris potential, brings theoretical proton spectra closer to experimental values by enhancing the collision rate in the early pre-equilibrium stage of the reaction, though the improvement is limited to high-energy protons while light cluster production remains largely unaffected.

## Method
The authors incorporated non-local collision shifts into a QMD simulation code by evaluating the displacement parameters from derivatives of the two-particle T-matrix scattering phase shift, using the separable Paris potential in the Bethe-Goldstone approximation. The non-local corrections were implemented as an instant but non-local form, where particles at the point of closest approach are displaced to effective final coordinates according to the derived displacement formula. Complementary BUU simulations were performed for the same reaction to study the microscopic mechanism.

## Key numbers
- Central collision of $^{129}$Xe → $^{119}$Sn at 50 MeV/A was simulated.
- The non-local corrections brought the high-energy part of the proton spectrum closer to experimental values, while light cluster production (shown in Figs. b-f) remained nearly unchanged.
- The BUU simulation showed that non-local corrections enhance the collision rate in the early stage of the reaction, leading to increased pre-equilibrium particle emission.

## Context
This work extends the line initiated by Malfliet (1983) and continued by Kortemeyer, Daffin and Bauer (1996), who used classical hard-sphere-like non-local collisions that did not achieve full quantitative agreement with experiment. The authors improve upon these earlier approaches by using a quasi-classical kinetic equation derived from non-equilibrium Green's functions (Morawetz et al., 1996) and evaluating non-local collisions from the realistic Paris potential via the two-particle T-matrix. The results suggest that non-local corrections may play a vital role in searches for the nuclear matter equation of state, and the method can be incorporated into existing BUU and QMD codes without increasing computational time.
