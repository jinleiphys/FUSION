# Macroconstraints from Microsymmetries of Macrosystems

> The dynamics governing the evolution of a many body system is constrained by a nonabelian local symmetry. We obtain a general form of the global macroscopic conditions assuring that at the microscopic level the evolution respects the overall symmetry constraint. These conditions does not depend on detailed dynamics of the system. It is shown that a nonabelian internal symmetry leads to the new subsidiary condition imposed on the system. A structure of constraints is analysed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general set of macroscopic constraints that must be satisfied during the time evolution of a many-body system to ensure that the underlying nonabelian local symmetry is preserved at the microscopic level, independent of the detailed dynamics. The key hedge is that these constraints are necessary conditions for preserving the internal symmetry on the macroscopic level, but the authors note that for nonabelian symmetries the resulting constraint is highly nonlinear and an analytic solution is too optimistic to expect.

## Method

The authors consider a multiparticle quantum system with a local interaction invariant under an internal symmetry group G, treating the system in occupation number representation. They construct projection operators onto irreducible representations of the symmetry group and derive the probability that given particle numbers combine into a state transforming under a particular representation. By requiring that these probabilities remain constant in time, they obtain a general condition involving time derivatives of occupation numbers, expressed through group integrals over characters and matrix elements, with permutation normalization factors handled via analytic continuation to continuous variables using gamma and digamma functions.

## Key numbers

* The number of particle species W is given by W = Σ_j Σ_ζ_j d(α_j), where d(α) is the dimension of representation α.
* For a Lie group of rank k, there are exactly k simultaneously measurable charges related to the group G, leading to k linear relations between the occupation number variables.
* The constraints reduce the W-dimensional manifold of solutions to a (W−k−1)-dimensional submanifold of physical states consistent with the underlying internal symmetry.
* For abelian symmetries, the condition reduces to α_1 dN^(α_1)/dt + ... + α_n dN^(α_n)/dt = 0, which is equivalent to charge conservation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous treatment of internal symmetry in kinetic theory (Turko and Rafelski, 2000, hep-th/0003079) to a more general scheme independent of the specific form of kinetic equations. It builds on the established tradition of statistical mechanics approaches to high-energy strong interaction phenomena starting from Koppe (1948) and Fermi (1950), and on prior work incorporating internal symmetries into equilibrium thermodynamics (Redlich and Turko, 1980; Turko, 1981). The results enable the construction of equilibrium distributions via Lagrange multipliers, where the multiplier related to the new nonabelian constraint cannot be treated as a standard additive thermodynamical potential, and suggest that numerical estimations based on kinetic equations are the practical path forward.
