# QCD at finite isospin density

> QCD at finite isospin chemical potential mu_I has no fermion sign problem and can be studied on the lattice. We solve this theory analytically in two limits: at low mu_I where chiral perturbation theory is applicable, and at asymptotically high mu_I where perturbative QCD works. At low isospin density the ground state is a pion condensate, whereas at high density it is a Fermi liquid with Cooper pairing. The pairs carry the same quantum numbers as the pion. This leads us to a conjecture that the transition from hadron to quark matter is smooth, which passes several tests. Our results imply a nontrivial phase diagram in the space of temperature and chemical potentials of isospin and baryon number.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that in two-flavor QCD at finite isospin chemical potential, the transition from hadron matter (pion condensate) to quark matter (Cooper-paired Fermi liquid) is smooth, with no phase transition, based on the fact that the order parameter has the same quantum numbers in both regimes; however, they note that a first-order deconfinement phase transition at intermediate isospin chemical potential cannot be rigorously ruled out and requires lattice verification.

## Method

The authors solve QCD at finite isospin chemical potential analytically in two limits: at low chemical potential using chiral perturbation theory, and at asymptotically high chemical potential using perturbative QCD. They determine the ground state alignment of the pion field and the spectrum of excitations in the low-density regime, and compute the BCS gap from one-gluon exchange in the high-density regime. They then compare the order parameters and symmetry breaking patterns in both limits to argue for continuity between the hadronic and quark phases.

## Key numbers

- The pion condensation threshold occurs at \(|\mu_I| = m_\pi\); for \(|\mu_I| < m_\pi\) the ground state is the same as at \(\mu_I = 0\).
- The tilt angle \(\alpha\) of the chiral condensate satisfies \(\cos\alpha = m_\pi^2 / \mu_I^2\) for \(|\mu_I| > m_\pi\).
- The isospin density for \(|\mu_I| > m_\pi\) is \(n_I = f_\pi^2 \mu_I (1 - m_\pi^4 / \mu_I^4)\).
- At asymptotically high isospin density, the BCS gap is \(\Delta = b |\mu_I| g^{-5} e^{-c/g}\) with \(c = 3\pi^2/2\) and \(b \approx 10^4\).
- At large \(\mu_I\), the deconfinement temperature \(T_c'\) is of order \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}'\), which is much less than \(\Delta\).
- For finite baryon chemical potential at large \(\mu_I\), the superfluid state is destroyed through two phase transitions: one at \(\mu_B\) slightly below \(\Delta/\sqrt{2}\) and another at \(\mu_B = 0.754\Delta\), with an FFLO state in between.

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD at finite density to the isospin chemical potential, which avoids the fermion sign problem that plagues finite baryon chemical potential calculations, as emphasized by Alford, Kapustin, and Wilczek (1999). The low-density analysis uses chiral perturbation theory following methods developed for two-color QCD by Kogut et al. (1999), while the high-density analysis parallels the color superconductivity work of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and the BCS gap calculation of Son (1999). The quark-hadron continuity conjecture parallels the continuity between nuclear and quark matter in three-flavor QCD proposed by Schafer and Wilczek (1999). The results enable lattice studies of cold dense QCD in the \((T, \mu_I)\) plane and suggest a nontrivial phase diagram in the \((T, \mu_B, \mu_I)\) space, with implications for neutron star matter.
