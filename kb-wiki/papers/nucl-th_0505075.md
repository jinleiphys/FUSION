# Eta absorption by mesons

> Using the $[SU(3)_{\mathrm{L}} \times SU(3)_{\mathrm{R}}]_{\mathrm{global}% }\times [SU(3)_V]_{\mathrm{local}}$ chiral Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry, we evaluate the cross sections for the absorption of eta meson ($% η$) by pion ($π$), rho ($ρ$), omega ($ω$), kaon ($K$), and kaon star ($K^*$) in the tree-level approximation. With empirical masses and coupling constants as well as reasonable values for the cutoff parameter in the form factors at interaction vertices, we find that most cross sections are less than 1 mb, except the reactions $ρη\to K\bar K^*(\bar KK^*)$, $ωη\to K\bar K^*(\bar KK^*)$, $K^*η\toρK$, and $K^*η\toωK$, which are a few mb, and the reactions $πη\to K\bar K$ and $Kη\toπK$, which are more than 10 mb. Including these reactions in a kinetic model based on a schematic hydrodynamic description of relativistic heavy ion collisions, we find that the abundance of eta mesons likely reaches chemical equilibrium with other hadrons in nuclear collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

Using a chiral Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry, the authors compute tree-level cross sections for eta meson absorption by various mesons and find that most are below 1 mb, but reactions such as πη → K̄K and Kη → πK exceed 10 mb; including these in a kinetic model suggests that eta mesons likely reach chemical equilibrium with other hadrons in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, though the results depend on the cutoff parameter in the form factors.

## Method

The authors employed the [SU(3)L × SU(3)R]global × [SU(3)V]local chiral Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry to derive interaction Lagrangians for eta absorption by π, ρ, ω, K, and K* mesons. They evaluated tree-level Born diagrams for all relevant channels, introducing monopole form factors at interaction vertices with a cutoff parameter Λ = 1 GeV, and computed isospin- and spin-averaged cross sections as functions of center-of-mass energy. These cross sections were then used in a rate equation within a schematic hydrodynamic model of central heavy ion collisions at RHIC, assuming thermal equilibrium and longitudinal boost invariance, to study the time evolution of eta meson abundance.

## Key numbers

- Most eta absorption cross sections are less than 1 mb, except four reactions (ρη → K̄K*, ωη → K̄K*, K*η → ρK, K*η → ωK) which are a few mb, and two reactions (πη → K̄K and Kη → πK) which exceed 10 mb.
- The universal vector coupling constant is g = 5.94, derived from the KSFR relation using fπ = 92.4 MeV, mρ = 776 MeV, and mK* = 892 MeV.
- The parameter cV = 0.32 is obtained from the KSFR relation; cA = 0.49 is obtained from the empirical kaon decay constant fK = 1.22 fπ.
- The calculated rho meson decay width is Γρ ≈ 147 MeV (empirical value 150 MeV), and the K* decay width is ΓK* ≈ 49 MeV (empirical value 51 MeV).
- The anomalous coupling parameter cwz = -0.1 is determined from the ratio of K*0 → K0γ and K*± → K±γ decay widths.
- Increasing the cutoff parameter Λ to 2 GeV increases cross sections by about a factor of two; decreasing Λ to 0.5 GeV reduces cross sections by about a factor of four.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of eta meson dynamics in heavy ion collisions, which had focused on baryon-dominated matter at SIS energies via the N(1535) resonance (cited as N1535) and on meson-dominated matter at SPS energies (cited as Li). The authors use the same chiral Lagrangian framework previously applied to dilepton production (Song) and phi meson interactions in hot hadronic matter (Koch). The computed cross sections enable improved modeling of eta meson abundance in RHIC collisions, which is important for interpreting dilepton spectra from eta Dalitz decays and two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss interferometry affected by pions from eta decays.
