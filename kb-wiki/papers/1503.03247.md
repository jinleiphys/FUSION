# Study of vorticity in an exact rotating hydro model

> We study a semianalytic exact solution of the fluid dynamical model of heavy ion reactions, and evaluate some observable signs of the rotation.

# Study of vorticity in an exact rotating hydro model

## Key claim

The exact rotating hydrodynamical model can reproduce the monotonic expansion and steady decrease of rotation seen in more detailed 3+1D fluid dynamical calculations, but only if the initial angular velocity is set higher than the value from the numerical model because the exact model lacks the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability that enhances rotation at intermediate times. The model is applicable at lower collision energies (FAIR, NICA) while at top RHIC or LHC energies it provides only qualitative estimates with 15-20% accuracy.

## Method

The authors take a semianalytic exact solution of the Euler equation for a rotating, expanding fireball with cylindrical symmetry, assuming a constant-κ equation of state (ε = κp, p = nT) and Gaussian density profiles. They extract initial parameters (R₀, Y₀, ω₀, Ṙ₀, Ẏ₀) from the numerical PICR fluid dynamical model of Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 0.7 b_max, then solve the resulting ordinary differential equations for R(t), Y(t), and ω(t) using the Runge-Kutta method. The energy-density-weighted vorticity is computed as a function of space and time.

## Key numbers

- Initial angular momentum of the system: L_y = -1.05 × 10⁴ ħ, from the PICR model calculation of Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV/nucleon and b = 0.7 b_max
- For the first parameter set (T₀ = 250 MeV, ω₀ = 0.15 c/fm, R₀ = 2.5 fm, Y₀ = 4.0 fm, Ṙ₀ = 0.25 c, Ẏ₀ = 0.4 c): ω decreases from 0.150 c/fm at t=0 to 0.014 c/fm at t=8 fm/c; R grows from 2.5 fm to 8.19 fm; Y grows from 4.0 fm to 7.90 fm
- For the second parameter set (T₀ = 400 MeV, ω₀ = 0.1 c/fm, R₀ = 3.5 fm, Y₀ = 5.0 fm, Ṙ₀ = 0.25 c, Ẏ₀ = 0.30 c): total energy E_tot = 816 MeV/nucleon; rotational energy halves in 3.3 fm/c
- For the third parameter set (T₀ = 300 MeV, ω₀ = 0.1 c/fm, R₀ = 2.5 fm, Y₀ = 4.0 fm, Ṙ₀ = 0.20 c, Ẏ₀ = 0.25 c): total energy E_tot = 576 MeV/nucleon; rotational energy halves in 2.9 fm/c; at t=8 fm/c about 95% of energy content remains in the non-relativistic applicability domain
- Scaling parameters for finite system: α² = 0.4585, β² = 0.2911; for infinite Gaussian system: α² = 2.0, β² = 1.0

## Context

This work extends the family of exact rotating hydrodynamical solutions introduced by Csernai and collaborators (Csernai and Nagy, 2013) and tests them against the numerical PICR fluid dynamical model results of Csernai, Mishustin, and Wang (2013) and the analysis of Wang, Csernai, and collaborators (2014). The exact model provides insight into the competition between expansion and rotation in heavy-ion collisions, but lacks the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability that numerical simulations show enhances rotation at intermediate times. The results enable estimates of how rapidly rotational energy and angular velocity decrease due to explosive expansion, informing whether rotation-induced polarization signals can survive to freeze-out. The model is most reliable for lower-energy facilities (FAIR, NICA) and provides only qualitative guidance for RHIC and LHC energies.
