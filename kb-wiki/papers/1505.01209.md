# Higher order fermion effective polynomial interactions

> Three different fermion effective potentials given by series of bilinears, $\sum_j^N (\barψ_a ψ_a)^{2^j}$ , $\sum_j^N (\barψ_a ψ_a)^{j}$ and also $\sum_j^N (\barψ_a γ_μψ_a)^{2j}$ where $a=1,...N_r$ and integer $j$ are investigated by introducing sets of auxiliary fields. A mininal procedure is adopted to deal with the auxiliary fields and an effective bosonized model in each case is found by assuming weak field fluctuations, i.e. weak enough when compared to (normalized) coupling constants. Different fermion condensates are considered for the ground state in the first two series analysed and the factorization of all higher order condensates into the lowest order one is found in most cases, i.e. in general $<(\overlineψ_a ψ_a)^n> \propto <\barψ_a ψ_a>^n$. For the case of the third series built with vector-type bilinears no condensation is assumed to occur. The corresponding (weak) scalar fields effective models for the three cases are expanded in polynomial interactions. The resulting low energy effective boson model may a exhibit new approximate symmetry depending on the terms present in the original series-model and on the values of the coupling constants.

# Higher order fermion effective polynomial interactions

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether higher-order fermion condensates factorize into the lowest-order condensate (i.e., $<(\overline{\psi}_a \psi_a)^n> \propto <\overline{\psi}_a \psi_a>^n$) for three different series of fermion effective potentials, finding that factorization holds in most cases under the assumption of weak field fluctuations relative to the normalized coupling constants.

## Method

The author introduces sets of auxiliary fields via Gaussian integrals to linearize three types of fermion effective potentials: $\sum_j^N (\bar{\psi}_a \psi_a)^{2^j}$, $\sum_j^N (\bar{\psi}_a \psi_a)^{j}$, and $\sum_j^N (\bar{\psi}_a \gamma_\mu \psi_a)^{2j}$. A minimal procedure is adopted requiring the minimum number of auxiliary fields and shifts, assuming weak field fluctuations compared to the coupling constants. The resulting bosonized effective actions are derived by integrating out fermions, and the effective potentials are expanded in polynomial interactions using a large fermion mass derivative expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the series $\sum_n (\bar{\psi}_a \psi_a)^{2^n}$ with $N=3$, $N_r=2$, $\tilde{M}=0.9$, and $\tilde{\Lambda}=4$ in four dimensions: with $\tilde{g}_2=0.0001$, $\tilde{g}_4=0.001$, $\tilde{g}_8=0.001$, the resulting effective mass ratio is $M^*/M=1.02$ and $\tilde{\varphi}_1^{(0)}=0.65$
- With $\tilde{g}_2=0.01$, $\tilde{g}_4=0.001$, $\tilde{g}_8=0.001$: $M^*/M=1.02$ and $\tilde{\varphi}_1^{(0)}=0.65$
- With $\tilde{g}_2=0.01$, $\tilde{g}_4=0.01$, $\tilde{g}_8=0.01$: $M^*/M=1.03$ and $\tilde{\varphi}_1^{(0)}=0.66$
- With $\tilde{g}_2=1$, $\tilde{g}_4=0.01$, $\tilde{g}_8=0.01$: $M^*/M=3.41$ and $\tilde{\varphi}_1^{(0)}=1.02$
- The maximum power considered is $N_{max} = 4 \times N_r$ in four spacetime dimensions, beyond which no non-trivial fermion effective interaction exists due to the Grassmann algebra

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary field method previously applied to Gross-Neveu and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Gross and Neveu, 1974; Klevansky, 1992) to incorporate higher-order fermion composite states and condensates. It addresses the factorization hypothesis of higher-order condensates into the lowest-order one, which was proposed in QCD (Shuryak, 1982; Zong et al., 2002) but found to be a poor approximation in that context (nonfactorization, 2003; Braghin and Navarra, 2014). The results enable construction of effective boson models for systems where n-body states arise with approximate degeneracy among composite states, such as cold atoms and light scalar meson spectroscopy.
