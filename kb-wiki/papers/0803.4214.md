# Infrared Renormalization Group Flow for Heavy Quark Masses

> A short-distance heavy quark mass depends on two parameters, the renormalization scale mu controlling the absorption of ultraviolet fluctuations into the mass, and a scale R controlling the absorption of infrared fluctuations. 1/R can be thought of as the radius for perturbative corrections that build up the mass beyond its point-like definition in the pole scheme. Treating R as a variable gives a renormalization group equation. We argue that the sign of this anomalous dimension is universal: increasing R to add IR modes decreases m(R). The flow improves the stability of conversions between mass schemes, allowing us to avoid large logs and the renormalon. The flow in R can be used to study IR renormalons without using bubble chains, and we use it to determine the coefficient of the LambdaQCD renormalon ambiguity of the pole mass with a convergent sum-rule.

# Infrared Renormalization Group Flow for Heavy Quark Masses

## Key claim

The paper argues that treating the infrared cutoff scale \(R\) in short-distance heavy quark mass definitions as a continuous variable yields a renormalization group equation with a universal sign: increasing \(R\) to include more infrared modes always decreases the mass \(m(R)\). This flow allows scheme conversions that avoid both large logarithms and the \({\cal O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})\) renormalon ambiguity of the pole mass, and provides a convergent sum-rule for the normalization of that renormalon without relying on bubble-chain approximations.

## Method

The authors define the difference between the pole mass and any short-distance mass scheme as \(\delta m(R,\mu) = m_{\rm pole} - m(R,\mu)\), expand it in \(\alpha_s(\mu)\) with logarithms \(\ln^k(\mu/R)\), and derive an RGE in \(R\) for \(\mu\)-independent schemes (PS, static, kinetic) by taking \(R\,d/dR\, m(R) = -R\,d/dR\,\delta m(R)\) with \(\alpha_s(R)\) as the expansion parameter. They solve this RGE analytically using the QCD \(\beta\)-function and incomplete gamma functions, obtaining all-order expressions at LL, NLL, and NNLL order. The solution is then extended to the \(R_0 \to 0\) limit to extract the Borel transform of the pole-mass renormalon and a convergent sum-rule for its normalization \(P_{1/2}\).

## Key numbers

* LO anomalous dimensions for various schemes: \((\gamma_0^R)^{\rm PS} = 4C_F\), \((\gamma_0^R)^{\rm stat} = 2\pi C_F\), \((\gamma_0^R)^{\rm MSR} = 4C_F\), \((\gamma_0^R)^{\rm kinetic} = 16C_F/3\), \((\gamma_0^R)^{\rm jet} = 2 e^{\gamma_E} C_F\).
* For a top quark with 5 light flavors, the normalized anomalous dimensions are \(\{\tilde\gamma_0^R,\tilde\gamma_1^R,\tilde\gamma_2^R\} = \{0.348,0.108,0.231\}^{\rm PS}\), \(\{0.546,-0.061,0.143\}^{\rm stat}\), \(\{0.348,0.213,0.068\}^{\rm MSR}\).
* The convergent sum-rule yields \(P_{1/2} = 0.47 \pm 0.10\) for the normalization of the \(u=1/2\) renormalon ambiguity of the pole mass; the light-fermion bubble chain (large-\(n_f\) with naive-non-Abelianization) gives an overestimate of \(P_{1/2}=0.80\).
* Converting a Tevatron top mass of \(172.6 \pm 1.4\,{\rm GeV}\) (taken as MSR mass at \(R_0\)) to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme gives \(\overline m(\overline m)=163.0 \pm 1.3\,{}^{+0.6}_{-0.3} \pm 0.05\,{\rm GeV}\), where the errors are experimental, scheme uncertainty from \(R_0=3^{+6}_{-2}\,{\rm GeV}\), and NNLL conversion uncertainty respectively.

## Context

This work extends the concept of an infrared RGE for heavy quark masses, previously formulated for the kinetic scheme by Voloshin (1992) and Bigi et al. (1997), to a general framework applicable to any short-distance mass scheme (PS, static, MSR, jet). It provides an alternative to the traditional large-\(n_f\) bubble-chain method of Beneke (1998) for studying the \({\cal O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD})\) renormalon, and agrees with the \(P_{1/2}\) value obtained by Lee et al. (1999) and Pineda (2001) using a different expansion. The RGE solution enables stable, high-precision conversions between mass schemes with widely separated infrared scales, relevant for top-quark mass determinations from collider data and for connecting low-energy heavy-quark mass measurements to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme.
