# Exclusive photon-fusion production of even-spin resonances and exotic QED atoms in high-energy hadron collisions

> The cross sections for the single exclusive production of (pseudo)scalar and (pseudo)tensor hadrons, as well as of even-spin QED bound states formed by pairs of opposite-charge leptons or hadrons, are estimated for photon-fusion processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus at the RHIC, LHC and FCC colliders, as well as in proton-air interactions at the highest energies reached by cosmic-rays impinging on Earth. The UPC cross sections are computed in the equivalent photon approximation with realistic photon fluxes from the charged form factors of proton, lead, gold, and nitrogen ions. The production of four types of even-spin systems are considered: quarkonium (spin-0,2,4 meson bound states, from the lightest $π^0$ meson up to toponium), exotic hadrons (including candidate multiquark states), leptonium (positronium, dimuonium, and ditauonium), as well as mesonium (pionium, kaonium, D-onium, and B-onium) and baryonium (notably, protonium) QED atoms. The expected yields at the different colliders are presented for about 50 such even-spin composite resonances, for which the ALICE and LHCb experiments have potential reconstruction capabilities at the LHC. The impact of the diphoton decays of such even-spin states is also discussed as resonant backgrounds in the measurement of light-by-light scattering ($γγ\toγγ$) over $m_{γγ} = 0.1$--15 GeV in PbPb UPCs at the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper estimates cross sections for exclusive photon-fusion production of even-spin resonances and QED bound states in ultraperipheral collisions, but the results depend on the narrow-width approximation and on realistic photon fluxes derived from charged form factors, with theoretical uncertainties from the photon flux estimated at the few percent level and neglected.

## Method
The authors compute cross sections using Low's formula for resonance production in photon-photon collisions, combined with effective two-photon luminosities obtained from the gamma-UPC Monte Carlo code. The photon fluxes are derived from charged form factors of protons and ions (lead, gold, nitrogen), and the probability of no inelastic hadronic interaction is included via eikonal Glauber expressions for nucleus-nucleus, proton-nucleus, and proton-proton ultraperipheral collisions.

## Key numbers
- For AuAu at RHIC (200 GeV nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is 6.2 GeV.
- For PbPb at LHC (5.52 TeV), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is 160 GeV.
- For pp at LHC (14 TeV), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is 4.5 TeV.
- For PbPb at FCC (39.4 TeV), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is 1.2 TeV.
- For pp at FCC (100 TeV), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is 30.5 TeV.
- For proton-air collisions at GZK cutoff (400 TeV), the maximum photon-photon center-of-mass energy is approximately 35 TeV.
- The effective two-photon luminosities cover 12 to 14 orders of magnitude from masses of 0.1 MeV up to high-mass tails, following a power-law decrease dL/dm_X proportional to m_X^{-n} with exponent n = 1.25 to 1.8 depending on system and energy.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of photon-photon production of even-spin resonances and QED bound states in ultraperipheral collisions (Krauss 1997, Natale 1994, Baur 1998, Nystrand 1998, Baur 2001, Bertulani 2001, Ginzburg 1998, Kotkin 1998, and more recent works by Moreira 2016, Goncalves 2018, Azevedo 2019, Esposito 2021, Shao 2022, Niu 2022, Biloshytskyi 2022, d'Enterria 2022, Francener 2021, Fariello 2023, Dai 2024) by including multiple new hadronic resonances and exotic atoms not considered previously, using improved photon-photon luminosities with proper propagation of theoretical uncertainties, and adding predictions for the Future Circular Collider and cosmic-ray interactions at GZK cutoff energies. The results enable experimental searches at ALICE and LHCb for about 50 even-spin composite resonances, and quantify the impact of diphoton decays of such states as resonant backgrounds in light-by-light scattering measurements over diphoton masses of 0.1 to 15 GeV in PbPb ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC.
