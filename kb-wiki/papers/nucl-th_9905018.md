# Influence of damping on the excitation of the double giant resonance

> We study the effect of the spreading widths on the excitation probabilities of the double giant dipole resonance. We solve the coupled-channels equations for the excitation of the giant dipole resonance and the double giant dipole resonance. Taking Pb+Pb collisions as example, we study the resulting effect on the excitation amplitudes, and cross sections as a function of the width of the states and of the bombarding energy.

# Influence of damping on the excitation of the double giant resonance

## Key claim
The inclusion of spreading widths in coupled-channels calculations reduces the excitation probabilities and cross sections for the double giant dipole resonance, but this reduction is smaller than what would be inferred from the time-dependent occupation probabilities alone, and the effect disappears at high bombarding energies where the reaction is fast.

## Method
The authors solved time-dependent coupled-channels equations for the excitation of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) and double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) in Pb+Pb collisions, including a damping term proportional to the width of each state. They used a semiclassical straight-line trajectory approximation for relativistic energies and calculated the time-integrated transition probabilities (flux functions) that account for decay after excitation. For comparison, they also applied a harmonic model with Lorentzian photo-nuclear cross sections to study the effect of energy fragmentation of the resonances.

## Key numbers
- GDR energy in 208Pb: 13.5 MeV; DGDR energy: 27 MeV
- GDR width used: 4 MeV (experimental); DGDR width set to 5.7 MeV (following the ratio Γ_DGDR = √2 × Γ_GDR)
- Impact parameter fixed at b = 15 fm for flux function calculations
- Varying the GDR width from 0 to 4 MeV leads to a 10% decrease of the flux functions into GDR and DGDR states
- Bombarding energy studied: 640 A·MeV for the Pb+Pb system at GSI/SIS, Darmstadt
- Excitation probability decreases with approximately 1/b^4, so grazing collisions are weighted most strongly

## Context
This work extends the predictions of Baur and Bertulani (1986) on the feasibility of DGDR excitation in heavy-ion Coulomb collisions by providing a quantitative treatment of damping effects. The authors solve the coupled-channels equations with damping terms following the recipe of Baur and Bertulani (1986), and compare their results to the harmonic model of Aberg (1995). The study enables more accurate predictions for DGDR excitation cross sections at intermediate bombarding energies, and shows that the sudden approximation becomes valid at high energies where damping effects vanish.
