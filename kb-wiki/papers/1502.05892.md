# Perfect fluidity of a dissipative system: Analytical solution for the Boltzmann equation in $\mathrm{AdS}_{2}\otimes \mathrm{S}_{2}$

> In this paper we obtain an analytical solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation that describes the out-of-equilibrium dynamics of a radially expanding massless gas. This solution is found by mapping this expanding system in flat spacetime to a static flow in the curved spacetime $\mathrm{AdS}_{2}\otimes \mathrm{S}_{2}$. We further derive explicit analytic expressions for the momentum dependence of the single particle distribution function as well as for the spatial dependence of its moments. We find that this dissipative system has the ability to flow as a perfect fluid even though its entropy density does not match the equilibrium form. The non-equilibrium contribution to the entropy density is shown to be due to higher order scalar moments (which possess no hydrodynamical interpretation) of the Boltzmann equation that can remain out of equilibrium but do not couple to the energy-momentum tensor of the system. Thus, in this system the slowly moving hydrodynamic degrees of freedom can exhibit true perfect fluidity while being totally decoupled from the fast moving, non-hydrodynamical microscopic degrees of freedom that lead to entropy production.

## Key claim

The authors show that a dissipative system described by the relativistic Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation can exhibit perfect fluidity, its energy-momentum tensor matches that of an ideal fluid at every spacetime point, even while its entropy density deviates from the equilibrium form, provided the system possesses the specific symmetry of a conformal soliton flow in AdS₂⊗S₂ geometry.

## Method

The authors solved the Anderson-Witting-Boltzmann equation for a massless conformal gas undergoing radial expansion, mapping the flat-spacetime expanding system to a static flow in curved AdS₂⊗S₂ spacetime. They imposed a static velocity field u_μ = (−cosh ρ, 0, 0, 0) and a temperature profile T(ρ) = T₀/cosh ρ, which made the shear tensor and expansion rate vanish identically. The resulting partial differential equation for the single-particle distribution function was solved analytically by separation of variables, yielding an explicit expression for the non-equilibrium part of the distribution function in terms of an arbitrary function constrained by the energy-matching condition and positivity requirements.

## Key numbers

* The temperature profile in AdS₂⊗S₂ coordinates is T(ρ) = T₀/cosh ρ, where T₀ is a constant.
* The relaxation time is τ_rel = c/T(ρ) = (c/T₀) cosh ρ, with the constant c related to the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as η/s = 5c.
* The energy-matching condition reduces to ∫₀^∞ dγ γ³ 𝒥(γ) exp(−γ) = 0, which for the polynomial ansatz 𝒥(γ) ∼ aγ − 1 requires a = 1/4.
* The equilibrium entropy density is s_eq = 4T³(ρ)/π² for a conformal gas with degeneracy factor one.
* The scalar moments of the distribution function vanish for n = 2 (as required by the energy-matching condition) and are given by Θ_n = [(n−2)/(16π²)] T^(n+2)(ρ) Γ(n+2) ℋ(ρ) exp[−πT₀(1+ξ)/(2c)], where ℋ(ρ) is a positive-definite function and ξ > 0 is a parameter ensuring positivity.

## Context

This work extends the program of finding exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in expanding backgrounds, following the iterative numerical solutions for Bjorken flow with transverse expansion obtained by Denicol et al. (2014). The specific symmetry considered (conformal soliton flow) was previously applied to conformal fluid dynamics by Hatta et al. (2014) to obtain analytical solutions of second-order hydrodynamics. The result demonstrates a clean separation between hydrodynamic degrees of freedom (which behave as an ideal fluid) and non-hydrodynamic scalar moments that produce entropy, enabling deeper understanding of the domain of applicability of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for systems such as the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions.
