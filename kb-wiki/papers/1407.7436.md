# Azimuthal Jet Tomography at RHIC and LHC

> Results based on a generic jet-energy loss model that interpolates between running coupling pQCD-based and AdS/CFT-inspired holographic prescriptions are compared to recent data on the high-p_T pion nuclear modification factor and the high-p_T elliptic flow in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC. The jet-energy loss model is coupled to various (2+1)d (viscous hydrodynamic) fields. The impact of energy-loss fluctuations is discussed. While a previously proposed AdS/CFT jet-energy loss model with a temperature-independent jet-medium coupling is shown to be inconsistent with the LHC data, we find a rather broad class of jet-energy independent energy-loss models $dE/dx= κ(T) x^z T^{2+z}$ that can account for the current data with different temperature-dependent jet-medium couplings $κ(T)$ and path-length dependence exponents of $0\le z \le 2$.

## Key claim

A broad class of jet-energy independent energy-loss models of the form \(dE/dx = \kappa(T) x^z T^{2+z}\) can account for current RHIC and LHC data on the nuclear modification factor and high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow, but only if the jet-medium coupling \(\kappa(T)\) is allowed to depend on temperature and the path-length exponent \(z\) lies between 0 and 2; a previously proposed AdS/CFT model with temperature-independent coupling is inconsistent with LHC data.

## Method

The authors coupled a generic jet-energy loss model parametrized as \(dE/d\tau = -\kappa(T) E^a \tau^z T^{c=2+z-a} \zeta_q\) to three variants of (2+1)d viscous hydrodynamic background fields: VISH2+1, viscous RL hydro, and a blast wave flow with \(v_\perp = 0.6\). They compared the resulting nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow \(v_2\) to data from RHIC and LHC, scanning over the exponents \((a,z,c)\) and including skewed energy-loss fluctuations via a scaling factor \(\zeta_q\) with distribution parameter \(q\).

## Key numbers

- The pQCD-based model with exponents \((0,1,3,-1)\) (no fluctuations) reproduces RHIC and LHC data within uncertainties of the bulk space-time evolution, including the "surprising transparency" of the QGP at LHC.
- The conformal AdS/CFT-inspired model with exponents \((0,2,4,-1)\) (no fluctuations) describes RHIC data but overpredicts jet energy loss at LHC, failing to reproduce the observed transparency.
- An azimuthal variation of the jet-medium coupling of \(10-15\%\) (parameter \(X\) in \(\kappa(\phi) = \kappa\cdot(1+|\sin(\phi)|\cdot X)\)) is sufficient to resolve the high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) problem at LHC while maintaining a decent description of \(R_{AA}\) at RHIC.
- For an exponentially falling temperature-dependent coupling \(\kappa(T) = \kappa_1 e^{-b(T-T_1)}\), the values \(T_1 = 160\) MeV and \(T_e = 200\) MeV indicate the high-temperature medium is basically transparent.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of pQCD-based running-coupling models (e.g., AMY, HT, ASW, Molnar, CUJET2.0) and AdS/CFT-inspired holographic prescriptions for jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior studies by Betz and Gyulassy (2014) and Xu (2013) that identified a "high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) problem" in pQCD models, and it supports the suggestion by Xu (2013) of an additional temperature-dependent running of the coupling constant, as indicated by Lattice QCD (Kaczmarek, 2004). The results enable further discrimination between pQCD and holographic energy-loss mechanisms and motivate more precise measurements of azimuthal jet tomography at RHIC and LHC.
