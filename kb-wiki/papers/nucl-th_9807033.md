# Modeling Pionic Fusion

> Recently observed rare heavy ion fusion processes, where the entire available energy is carried away by a single pion, is an example of extreme collectivity in nuclear reactions. We calculate the cross section in the approximation of sudden overlap, modeling the initial and final nuclei by moving harmonic oscillator potentials. This allows for a fully quantum-mechanical treatment, exact conservation of linear and angular momenta and fulfillment of the Pauli principle. The results are in satisfactory agreement with data. Mass number dependence and general trends of the process are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate pionic fusion cross sections in the sudden overlap approximation using moving harmonic oscillator potentials, finding "satisfactory agreement" with data, but they note that the model systematically underpredicts measured cross sections (by approximately 40% for the He-3 + He-3 reaction and by a factor of several for C-12 + C-12) and that results are highly sensitive to the choice of oscillator parameters.

## Method

The authors treat pionic fusion as a stationary scattering problem using the Born approximation, where pion production occurs through coupling to a single nucleon via a p-wave interaction term. They model initial and final nuclei as Slater determinants of harmonic oscillator single-particle states with moving centers, project onto states of definite linear momentum, and compute the transition amplitude analytically using Gaussian overlap integrals. The cross section is obtained from Fermi's golden rule, summing over all possible single-nucleon pion vertices while preserving antisymmetry.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction p + p -> d + pi+, the oscillator parameter v = 216 MeV/c reproduces the experimental value 4*sigma_tilde approximately 0.42 fm^2.
* For the reaction He-3 + He-3 -> Li-6 + pi+ into the ground state, the model predicts a cross section approximately 40% below the experimental value of 111 +/- 11 nb.
* For the reaction C-12 + C-12 -> Mg-24 + pi0, the experimentally estimated cross section is 208 +/- 38 pb at a pion momentum of 41 MeV/c.
* The low-momentum approximation yields cross sections proportional to (k/m_pi)^3, where k is the pion momentum.
* The argument of the polynomial T in the low-momentum approximation is approximately p*sqrt(2/(A*eta*v*w)) approximately 6 at threshold energy, nearly independent of mass number A.

## Context

This work extends subthreshold pion production models (such as those by Vasak (1980, 1985), Li (1991), Bonasera (1990), and Providencia (1988)) to energies within approximately 10 MeV above absolute threshold, where statistical approaches must be replaced by low-energy many-body structure physics. The model enables calculations for pionic fusion reactions observed by Horn (1996), Bornec (1981), and Waters (1993), and provides a framework for predicting cross sections for heavier nuclei up to mass 20, beyond which the Coulomb barrier shuts off fusion.
