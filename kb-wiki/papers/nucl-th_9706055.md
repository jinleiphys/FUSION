# Decay of hot, rotating, compound nuclei

> The fusion process and the competition between fission and n, p and alpha-particle emission is studied. The calculations are performed for nuclei at excitation energies from 80 MeV up to about 300 MeV. The nuclear fission is described by a Langevin equation coupled to the Master equation for particle evaporation. A significant influence of the initial spin distribution on the prescission particles multiplicities is found.

## Key claim
The authors find that the initial spin (angular momentum) distribution of the compound nucleus has a significant influence on prescission particle multiplicities, and that a more precise knowledge of this distribution is necessary for meaningful comparison with experiment. The paper demonstrates that the fission barrier height decreases with increasing angular momentum, causing fission to compete more effectively with evaporation at high spin.

## Method
The authors model the decay of hot, rotating compound nuclei by coupling a Langevin equation for the fission degree of freedom to a Master equation for the evaporation of neutrons, protons, and alpha particles. The collective potential is evaluated using a deformed, hot, rotating liquid-drop model, with the one-dimensional fission path chosen in a three-dimensional deformation space. Collective inertia is obtained from the irrotational flow model, and the wall formula is used for the friction strength, with the Einstein relation linking friction and diffusion parameters.

## Key numbers
- For the compound nucleus ¹⁶⁰Yb at excitation energy E* = 251 MeV: calculated prefission neutron multiplicity = 5.98 (experimental: 6.10 ± 1.5), proton multiplicity = 0.94 (experimental: 0.51 ± 0.07), alpha multiplicity = 0.58 (experimental: 0.48 ± 0.07)
- For ¹⁶⁰Yb at E* = 293 MeV: calculated prefission neutron multiplicity = 7.80 (experimental: 8.50 ± 1.6), proton multiplicity = 1.19 (experimental: 0.70 ± 0.08), alpha multiplicity = 0.66 (experimental: 0.75 ± 0.08)
- For ¹²⁶Ba produced via ²⁸Si + ⁹⁸Mo at Elab = 204 MeV (E* = 131.7 MeV): prefission neutron multiplicity = 2.29, proton = 0.03, alpha = 0.79
- For ¹²⁶Ba at the lowest excitation energy E* = 84.1 MeV: prefission neutron multiplicity = 0.27, alpha multiplicity = 0.88
- The initial angular momentum L for ¹⁶⁰Yb calculations ranges from 0 to 45 ℏ, with the transient time for fission increasing as excitation energy decreases
- The maximal angular momentum of fused ¹²⁶Ba varies from about 60 ℏ to 100 ℏ depending on the production reaction

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Pomorski 1996, Str91) and builds on the non-statistical fission approach pioneered by Grange and Weidenmuller (1979). The calculations are compared with experimental data from Gonin (1990) for ¹⁶⁰Yb and are correlated with measurements performed by the DEMON group in Strasbourg (Han97) for ¹²⁶Ba. The results demonstrate that deformation and rotation effects on particle emission widths, particularly the stronger enhancement for charged particles, must be accounted for in comparisons with experiment, and that the preformation factor for alpha particles (taken as 0.2 for ¹⁶⁰Yb and 1.0 for ¹²⁶Ba) requires further theoretical development using temperature-dependent Thomas-Fermi approximations (Dietrich 1996).
