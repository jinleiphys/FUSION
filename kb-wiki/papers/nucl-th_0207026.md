# Dispersion relation analysis of neutral pion photo- and electroproduction at threshold using the MAID and SAID solutions

> Neutral pion photo- and electroproduction at threshold is analyzed in the framework of dispersion relations. For this purpose, we evaluate the real threshold amplitudes in terms of Born contributions and dispersion integrals determined by the imaginary parts of the MAID and SAID multipoles. The results show considerable cancellations between Born terms and resonance contributions. Good agreement with the data is found for photoproduction. While our dispersion analysis suggests considerable discrepancies for electroproduction, the present state of the experimental multipole analysis at finite Q^2 does not permit drawing conclusions at this time.

## Key claim

The dispersion relation analysis shows that the large cancellation between the nucleon pole term and contributions from higher multipoles, which was previously established for photoproduction, also occurs for electroproduction at finite Q^2; however, the present state of experimental multipole analysis at finite Q^2 does not permit drawing conclusions about the discrepancies suggested by the dispersion analysis for electroproduction.

## Method

The authors used fixed-t dispersion relations to compute real threshold amplitudes for neutral pion photo- and electroproduction, with the dispersion integrals determined by the imaginary parts of multipoles taken from two different models: the Unitary Isobar Model (MAID) and the phenomenological partial-wave analysis (SAID). The MAID model was extended by including all S-, P-, D-, and F-wave resonances with four-star PDG status and refitted to the SAID photoproduction database of 15,700 data points in the energy range from threshold to W=2000 MeV. The dispersion integrals were evaluated using these imaginary parts as input, with the assumption that multipoles have an asymptotic 1/W behavior for W greater than or equal to 2300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Threshold S-wave multipole for photoproduction: E0+ = -1.29 x 10^-3/m_pi (DR with MAID input) and E0+ = -1.35 x 10^-3/m_pi (DR with SAID input), compared to the experimental value of -1.33 +/- 0.11 x 10^-3/m_pi from Schmidt (2001)
- The nucleon pole term for photoproduction is E0+(pole) = -7.89 x 10^-3/m_pi, with the diagonal E0+ contribution of +2.84 (MAID) or +2.83 (SAID) and the M1+ kernel contribution of +4.09 (MAID) or +4.23 (SAID) providing the largest cancellations
- For electroproduction at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2: E0+ = 1.55 x 10^-3/m_pi and L0+ = -1.41 x 10^-3/m_pi (DR with MAID input)
- The P33(1232) resonance contributes Im M = 36.85 x 10^-3/m_pi (MAID) and 36.01 x 10^-3/m_pi (SAID) for the magnetic multipole at resonance
- The extracted E0+ values at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 from different experimental analyses range from 0.58 +/- 0.18 (Merkel, 2002) to 2.28 +/- 0.36 (DMT, 2001)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Hanstein, Drechsel, and Tiator (1998) for photoproduction to both photo- and electroproduction by using the MAID and SAID multipole solutions as input rather than solving coupled integral equations via the Omnes-Mushkashevili method. The results confirm the earlier finding that chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1996) correctly describe the threshold S-wave amplitude, and reveal that the discrepancy in the P3 amplitude and the quantity P2^2 - P3^2 persists across both dispersion approaches. The analysis enables future comparisons with upcoming electroproduction experiments that could resolve the current model-dependent extraction of multipoles at finite Q^2.
