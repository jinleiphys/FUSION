# Reggeon Field Theory and Self Duality: Making Ends Meet

> Motivated by the question of unitarity of Reggeon Field Theory, we use the effective field theory philosophy to find possible Reggeon Field Theory Hamiltonians $H_{RFT}$. We require that $H_{RFT}$ is self dual, reproduce all known limits (dilute-dense and dilute-dilute) and exhibits all the symmetries of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian. We find a family of Hamiltonians which satisfy all the above requirements. One of these is identical in form to the so called "diamond action" discussed in \cite{diamond,Balitsky05}. However we show by explicit calculation that the so called "diamond condition" is not satisfied beyond leading perturbative order.

## Key claim

The authors construct a family of self-dual Reggeon Field Theory Hamiltonians that reproduce both the JIMWLK and KLWMIJ limits and exhibit all symmetries of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian, but they explicitly show that the "diamond condition" on Wilson lines is violated beyond leading perturbative order, meaning their construction is not equivalent to the previously proposed diamond action.

## Method

The authors adopt an effective field theory approach, imposing self-duality under the dense-dilute interchange transformation and requiring the Hamiltonian to reduce to the known JIMWLK Hamiltonian (for dense target, dilute projectile) and KLWMIJ Hamiltonian (for dilute target, dense projectile) in the appropriate limits. They also enforce the discrete symmetries of JIMWLK (signature symmetry and charge conjugation) and construct a family of Hamiltonians using the left and right rotation operators \(\mathcal{J}_{L(R)}\) and \(\mathcal{I}_{L(R)}\) acting on the Wilson line operators \(U\) and \(\bar U\).

## Key numbers

- The JIMWLK Hamiltonian kernel contains the factor \(\frac{(\mathbf{x-z})\cdot(\mathbf{y-z})}{(\mathbf{x-z})^2(\mathbf{y-z})^2}\) multiplied by \(\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi^2}\).
- The "diamond condition" is shown to be violated at second order in the coupling \(g\).
- The Bernoulli numbers \(B_m^\pm\) appear in the expansion of the rotation operators, with \(B_1^- = -\frac{1}{2} = -B_1^+\) and all odd Bernoulli numbers beyond \(B_1\) vanishing.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of unitarity violation in JIMWLK evolution found by Kovner, Levin, Li, and Lublinsky (2020), where negative probabilities were shown to arise when JIMWLK acts on dense states. It builds on the zero-dimensional toy model of Kovner, Levin, and Lublinsky (2019) where self-duality was linked to unitarity, and contrasts with the earlier "diamond action" approach of Balitsky (2005) and the large-\(N_c\) Pomeron theory of Braun, which exhibited nonphysical bifurcating behavior. The construction provides an explicit algorithm for calculating scattering amplitudes once the Hamiltonian is specified, but the question of whether the proposed Hamiltonians actually restore unitarity remains open for future investigation.
