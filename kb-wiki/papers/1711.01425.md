# Semiclassical unified description of wobbling motion in even-even and even-odd nuclei

> A unitary description for wobbling motion in even-even and even-odd nuclei is presented. In both cases compact formulas for wobbling frequencies are derived. The accuracy of the harmonic approximation is studied for the yrast as well as for the excited bands in the even-even case. Important results for the structure of the wave function and its behavior inside the two wells of the potential energy function corresponding to the Bargmann representation are pointed out. Applications to $^{158}$Er and $^{163}$Lu reveal a very good agreement with available data. Indeed, the yrast energy levels in the even-even case and the first four triaxial super-deformed bands, TSD1,TSD2,TSD3 and TSD4, are realistically described. Also, the results agree with the data for the E2 and M1 intra- as well as inter-band transitions. Perspectives for the formalism development and an extensive application to several nuclei from various regions of the nuclides chart are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims a unified semiclassical description of wobbling motion applicable to both even-even and even-odd nuclei, but the harmonic approximation for the wobbling frequency is validated only for the yrast band and the first few excited bands in the even-even case, with the accuracy of the approximation decreasing for higher-lying states.

## Method

The authors dequantize the triaxial rigid rotor Hamiltonian using a time-dependent variational principle with an SU(2) coherent state as trial function, obtaining classical equations of motion in canonical form. They then quantize the classical phase space via Holstein-Primakoff and Dyson boson expansions, and further transform the Dyson boson representation into a Schrodinger equation in the Bargmann representation, which takes the form of a Lame equation. The harmonic approximation around the potential minima yields closed-form expressions for wobbling frequencies, which are applied to the even-even nucleus 158Er and the even-odd nucleus 163Lu.

## Key numbers

- For 158Er, the yrast energies are fitted using the formula E_J = a J(J+1) + b sqrt(J(J+1)), with parameters a = 1/(2*I1) and b = 0.5 * sqrt((1/I2 - 1/I1)(1/I3 - 1/I1)), where the moments of inertia are fitted to experimental data from Nica (reference in text).
- For the pedagogical comparison of methods, the moments of inertia were chosen as I1 = 125 hbar^2 MeV^-1, I2 = 31.4 hbar^2 MeV^-1, I3 = 42 hbar^2 MeV^-1.
- The maximal deviation between the three methods (diagonalization, Schrodinger equation, and wobbling energy formula) for yrast energies is less than 5 keV.
- The wobbling frequency for the even-even case is given by omega_I = [(1/I2 - 1/I1)(1/I3 - 1/I1) I(I+1)]^(1/2), and for the harmonic approximation around the minimum, omega_I = (I - 1/2) sqrt((1/I3 - 1/I1)(1/I2 - 1/I1)).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of wobbling motion previously developed for even-even nuclei in Raduta et al. (reference in text) to a unified framework covering both even-even and even-odd systems. It builds on the triaxial rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (reference in text) and the particle-rotor model of Ter-Vehn (reference in text), and connects to the boson expansion methods of Holstein and Primakoff (reference in text) and Dyson (reference in text). The results enable systematic application to triaxial super-deformed bands in lutetium isotopes (163,165,167Lu) and tantalum (167Ta), as well as future extension to other mass regions of the nuclide chart.
