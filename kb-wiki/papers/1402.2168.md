# Low-lying states in near-magic odd-odd nuclei and the effective interaction

> The iterative quasi-particle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) method we previously developed to accurately calculate properties of individual nuclear states is extended so that it can be applied for nuclei with odd numbers of neutrons and protons. The approach is based on the proton-neutron-QRPA (pnQRPA) and uses an iterative non-hermitian Arnoldi diagonalization method where the QRPA matrix does not have to be explicitly calculated and stored. The method is used to calculate excitation energies of proton-neutron multiplets for several nuclei. The influence of a pairing interaction in the $T=0$ channel is studied.

## Key claim

The iterative QRPA method previously developed for even-even nuclei is extended to odd-odd nuclei via the proton-neutron QRPA (pnQRPA), and the isoscalar pairing strength is tuned to reproduce experimental multiplet splittings; however, the method assumes isospin-symmetric T=1 pairing and omits Coulomb contributions to pairing, which the authors acknowledge as an approximation that should be refined in future work.

## Method

The authors implement an iterative non-hermitian Arnoldi diagonalization method for the pnQRPA matrix, avoiding explicit construction and storage of the full matrix by using transitional densities and fields built from the HFB U and V matrices. The Skyrme interaction is used for the particle-hole channel and a separable finite-range interaction for the pairing channel, with the isoscalar (T=0) pairing strength treated as a free parameter. Excitation energies of proton-neutron multiplets are calculated for several near-magic odd-odd nuclei, and the theoretical relative energies within each multiplet are compared to experimental data to determine the optimal G0/G1 ratio.

## Key numbers

- The optimal ratio of isoscalar to isovector pairing strength is G0/G1 = 1.2 for both SKX and SLy4 Skyrme interactions, based on RMS minimization over 104 experimental states.
- For the nucleus 51Sb83, calculations with Nmax=16 take about 6.5 minutes on a standard desktop computer (Intel Core i7-2600K, 3.4 GHz) for the I=7- state, requesting 15 lowest positive energy pnQRPA excitations converged with tolerance 10^-6.
- The pairing range parameter is fixed at a = 0.660 fm, taken from previous work (Carlsson 2012).
- Increasing G0/G1 beyond approximately 1.2 leads to imaginary eigenvalues appearing in the pnQRPA for N=Z nuclei, with a 10% increase from the last stable point causing instability.
- For the SKX interaction with (G1, G0) = (545, 763) MeV fm^3 (G0/G1 = 1.4), the relative energies of multiplet states in 51Sb83 converge rapidly with oscillator shell number, with Nmax=16 giving sufficient accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the iterative QRPA method developed by Toivanen (2010), Vesely (2012), and Carlsson (2012) from even-even nuclei to odd-odd nuclei via the pnQRPA formalism. The approach enables direct determination of the isoscalar pairing strength from multiplet splittings in odd-odd nuclei, rather than fitting it to beta-decay probabilities as done by Mustonen (2013) and Engel (1999). The results provide a first value of the T=0 pairing strength that can be used in future pnQRPA calculations for beta-decay, though the authors note that Coulomb contributions to pairing and isospin-breaking effects should be included in more complete treatments.
