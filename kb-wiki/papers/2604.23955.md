# Hard Probes in Ultraperipheral Collisions at LHCb

> Measurements of quarkonia production in peripheral and ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions are sensitive to photon-photon and photon-nucleus interactions, the partonic structure of nuclei, and the mechanisms of vector-meson production. In this contribution, quarkonium measurements with the highest precision currently accessible will be compared with the latest theoretical models. Additionally, new studies of $ρ(770)$ and $ϕ(1020)$ vector meson production at forward rapidity will be presented and contrasted with other experimental results. Future UPC measurements with the upgraded LHCb detector in Run 3 will also be discussed.

## Key claim

The LHCb collaboration presents quarkonium and light vector-meson measurements in peripheral and ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions that are sensitive to photon-photon and photon-nucleus interactions, but the abstract's claim of "highest precision currently accessible" applies specifically to the quarkonium measurements compared with theoretical models, while the new ρ(770) and φ(1020) results are presented as initial studies that require further quantification.

## Method

The analysis uses LHCb Run 2 data from PbPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (228 μb⁻¹ integrated luminosity) and pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV (5 fb⁻¹), recorded with the forward-rapidity LHCb detector covering pseudorapidity 2 < η < 5. Quarkonia and vector mesons are reconstructed via their dimuon and dihadron decay channels, with coherent photoproduction candidates selected by requiring low transverse momentum (pT < 100 MeV/c for the π⁺π⁻ and K⁺K⁻ analyses) and using the HeRSCheL detector to identify diffractive events.

## Key numbers

- PbPb data sample recorded at √sNN = 5.02 TeV with integrated luminosity 228 μb⁻¹; pp data sample at √s = 13 TeV with 5 fb⁻¹
- Mean pT of J/ψ photoproduction candidates: ⟨pT⟩ = 64.9 ± 2.4 MeV/c, consistent with coherent vector meson photoproduction
- χc₀(4500) and χc₁(4274) exotic states observed in J/ψφ invariant mass spectrum with greater than 4σ significance in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV
- φ(1020) meson observed in UPC at forward rapidity with significance greater than 5σ in K⁺K⁻ invariant mass distribution at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- π⁺π⁻ distribution contains approximately 20 million candidates in UPC PbPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV for pT < 100 MeV/c
- Excess of events observed near 1.7 GeV/c² in π⁺π⁻ spectrum, consistent with ρ(1450) and ρ(1700) mesons

## Context

This work extends LHCb's previous measurements of charmonium in hadronic collisions (LHCb 2016) and confirms earlier observations of excess J/ψ yield in peripheral PbPb collisions by ALICE (2015) and STAR (2019). The π⁺π⁻ results are compared to midrapidity measurements by ALICE (2020), while the K⁺K⁻ spectrum shows a richer structure in the forward region than seen by ALICE (2023), motivating further study. These measurements enable future Run 3 studies of photoproduction with the upgraded LHCb detector and SMOG2 fixed-target system, as well as searches for resonances through the γγ production channel.
