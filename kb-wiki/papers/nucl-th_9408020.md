# Collective Excitations and Ground State Correlations

> A generalized RPA formalism is presented which treats pp and ph correlations on an equal footing. The effect of these correlations on the single-particle Green function is discussed and it is demonstrated that a self-consistent treatment of the single-particle Green function is required to obtain stable solutions. A simple approximation scheme is presented which incorporates for this self-consistency requirement and conserves the number of particles. Results of numerical calculations are given for $^{16}$O using a G-matrix interaction derived from a realistic One-Boson-Exchange potential.

# Collective Excitations and Ground State Correlations

## Key claim

The generalized RPA (SRPA) formalism that treats particle-particle (pp) and particle-hole (ph) correlations on an equal footing yields stable, convergent solutions for finite nuclei only when the single-particle Green function is defined self-consistently beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation; without this self-consistency, the SRPA equations fail to converge for realistic model spaces, even though the reduced ph-RPA and pp-hh RPA equations do converge.

## Method

The authors formulate a super-RPA (SRPA) approach that includes ph, pp, and hole-hole (hh) correlations simultaneously, expressed via a wavefunction ansatz with 2p-2h correlation coefficients K. They solve the non-linear SRPA equations iteratively for 16O using G-matrix interactions derived from three One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) potentials (A, B, C) in oscillator bases. To achieve convergence, they introduce a self-consistent scheme where the single-particle Green function is approximated by a two-pole form with energies epsilon_< and epsilon_> determined from the occupation numbers and the Hartree-Fock single-particle energies via a sum rule, iterating until the occupation probabilities stabilize.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy for the small model space (p-shell holes, 1s0d particle states) using the self-consistent SRPA: approximately 9 MeV for all three OBE potentials.
- Correlation energy for the large model space (all hole states of 16O, 1s0d plus 1p0f particle states) using self-consistent SRPA: approximately 19 MeV for all three OBE potentials.
- Occupation probability for the p3/2 hole state in the small model space using SRPA with independent-particle-model propagators and OBE potential C: 0.814; for OBE potential A: 0.276.
- Occupation probability for the p1/2 hole state in the small model space using SRPA with independent-particle-model propagators and OBE potential C: 0.822; for OBE potential A: 0.375.
- Shifting all particle-state energies by a constant of around 3 MeV yields stable SRPA solutions even for the large model space.

## Context

This work extends the SRPA formalism of Ellis (1990) by addressing its failure to converge for realistic model spaces in 16O. The authors show that the instability arises from using Hartree-Fock single-particle propagators, and they cure it with a self-consistent Green function approach that conserves particle number, similar in spirit to the self-consistent Green function method of Dickhoff and Muther (1992) but simpler. The results move calculated ground-state properties (binding energy and radius) off the "Coester band" toward experimental values, complementing the Dirac-BHF approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1984) which improves saturation but leaves residual discrepancies of about 0.7 MeV per nucleon in energy and 0.2 fm in radius for light nuclei.
