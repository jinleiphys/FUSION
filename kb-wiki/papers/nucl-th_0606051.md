# Effect of equilibrium phase transition on multiphase transport in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The hadronization scheme for parton transport in relativistic heavy ion collisions is considered in detail. It is pointed out that the traditional scheme for particles being freezed out one by one leads to serious problem on unreasonable long lifetime for partons. A super-cooling of the parton system followed by a collective phase transition is implemented in a simple way. It turns out that the modified model with a global phase transition is able to reproduce the experimental longitudinal distributions of final state particles better than the original one does. The encouraging results indicate that a relevant parton transport model for relativistic heavy ion collision should take equilibrium phase transition into proper account.

## Key claim

The traditional particle-by-particle freeze-out scheme in parton transport models leads to an unphysically long lifetime for partons (up to ~100 fm/c), and implementing a global equilibrium phase transition with super-cooling followed by collective hadronization at t=5 fm/c reproduces experimental longitudinal distributions better than the original parton-wise hadronization approach.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting (v2.11) to simulate Au-Au central collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm and parton cross section 10 mb. They extracted the temperature of the parton system at different times by fitting the transverse mass distribution of d-quarks to a thermal equilibrium formula, then implemented a global phase transition by forcing all remaining partons to coalesce into hadrons at t = 5 fm/c (when the parton temperature had dropped to about 110 MeV), allowing the system to release latent heat and continue with hadronic cascade via the ART model.

## Key numbers

- Parton temperature at t = 0.5 fm/c: ~180 MeV, slightly above the predicted critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV
- Parton temperature at t = 5 fm/c: ~110 MeV, well below the critical temperature
- Parton lifetime in the original model: some partons survive up to t ~ 100 fm/c
- Impact parameter range: b ≤ 3 fm for central collisions
- Parton cross section used: 10 mb (and 3 mb for elliptic flow comparison)
- Hadron emergence: about 4% of hadrons already present at t < 5 fm/c
- Expected QGP lifetime: 1-5 fm/c; hadron freeze-out time: 20-40 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) by addressing a known deficiency in parton transport models where hadronization occurs particle-by-particle rather than collectively. The authors contrast their approach with the default AMPT v1.11 (which uses Lund string fragmentation) and AMPT v2.11 with string melting (which uses quark coalescence but still hadronizes partons individually). The modified model preserves the successful description of elliptic flow from the original AMPT while improving agreement with PHOBOS and BRAHMS experimental data on rapidity distributions, particularly for kaons and protons. This enables future development of parton transport models that incorporate equilibrium phase transitions more realistically.
