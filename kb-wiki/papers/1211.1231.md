# Maximum mass of neutron stars and strange neutron-star cores

> Recent measurement of mass of PSR J1614-2230 rules out most of existing models of equation of state (EOS) of dense matter with high-density softening due to hyperonization, based on the recent hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, leading to a "hyperon puzzle". We study a specific solution of "hyperon puzzle", consisting in replacing a too soft hyperon core by a sufficiently stiff quark core. We construct an analytic approximation fitting very well modern EOSs of 2SC and CFL color superconducting phases of quark matter. This allows us for simulating continua of sequences of first-order phase transitions from hadronic matter to the 2SC, and then to the CFL state of color superconducting quark matter. We obtain constraints in the parameter space of the EOS of superconducting quark cores, resulting from M_max> 2 M_sol. We also derive constraints that would result from significantly higher measured masses. For 2.4 M_sol required stiffness of the CFL quark core should have been close to the causality limit, the density jump at the phase transition being very small. Condition M_max > 2 M_sol puts strong constraints on the EOSs of the 2SC and CFL phases of quark matter. Density jumps at the phase transitions have to be sufficiently small and sound speeds in quark matter - sufficiently large. A strict condition of thermodynamic stability of quark phase results in the maximum mass of hybrid stars similar to that of purely baryon stars. Therefore, to get M_max>2 M_sol for stable hybrid stars, both sufficiently strong additional hyperon repulsion at high density baryon matter and a sufficiently stiff EOS of quark matter would be needed. However, it is likely that the high density instability of quark matter (reconfinement) indicates actually the inadequacy of the point-particle model of baryons in dense matter at very high densities.

## Key claim

Replacing a too-soft hyperon core with a sufficiently stiff quark core (in 2SC and CFL color-superconducting phases) can satisfy the M_max > 2 M_sun constraint from PSR J1614-2230, but only if density jumps at phase transitions are sufficiently small and sound speeds in quark matter are sufficiently large; for a 2.4 M_sun star, the CFL quark core stiffness would need to be close to the causality limit.

## Method

The authors constructed an analytic linear approximation (P = a(E - E_*)) that accurately fits modern numerical EOSs of 2SC and CFL color-superconducting quark matter from the NJL model, then used it to generate continua of EOSs parameterized by sound-speed parameter a and density-jump parameter lambda. They simulated sequences of first-order phase transitions from hadronic matter (using two representative baryon EOSs: a soft SR EOS and a stiff BM165 EOS) to 2SC quark matter and then to CFL quark matter at prescribed pressures, and computed the resulting maximum masses of hybrid neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun, requiring M_max > 2.0 M_sun for acceptable EOSs
- For non-interacting neutron Fermi gas: M_max = 0.7 M_sun
- Normal nuclear density: rho_0 = 2.7 × 10^14 g cm^-3 (baryon number density n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
- Soft SR baryon EOS gives M_max^(B) = 1.35 M_sun (Schulze and Rijken 2011)
- Stiff BM165 baryon EOS gives M_max^(B) = 2.04 M_sun (Bednarek et al. 2011)
- For the 2SC phase, parameter a ranges from 0.25 to 0.33 (nu = 4.1 to 4.6)
- For the CFL phase, parameter a ranges from 0.3 to 0.4 (nu = 3.5 to 4.3)
- For a 2.4 M_sun star with BM165 EOS, required CFL stiffness approaches the causality limit (a near 1) with very small density jump
- For soft SR EOS with 2SC+CFL core, even with lambda_2SC = 1.05, required a_CFL > 0.4
- For BM165 EOS with 2SC+CFL core and lambda_CFL > 1.2, required a_CFL significantly larger than values from Blaschke (2010) and Agrawal (2010)
- Reconfinement (Q→B transition) limits hybrid star M_max to approximately M_max^(B) = 1.35 M_sun for SR EOS and 2.04 M_sun for BM165 EOS

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" created by the 2 M_sun pulsar measurement (Demorest 2010), which ruled out most EOSs with high-density softening from hyperonization based on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions (Burgio 2011, Vidana 2011, Schulze and Rijken 2011). The authors extend prior work on hybrid stars with quark cores (Baldo 2003, Ozel 2010, Weissenborn 2011, Klaehn 2011, Bonanno 2012, Lastowiecki 2012) by systematically mapping the allowed parameter space of color-superconducting quark EOSs using an analytic approximation. The results enable future observational constraints: if a 2.2 or 2.4 M_sun pulsar is discovered, the required stiffness and density-jump conditions become much more restrictive, and the thermodynamic stability condition (reconfinement) suggests that achieving M_max > 2 M_sun for stable hybrid stars likely requires both strong additional hyperon repulsion at high density and a sufficiently stiff quark matter EOS.
