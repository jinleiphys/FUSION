# Light stops, blind spots, and isospin violation in the MSSM

> In the framework of the MSSM, we examine several simplified models where only a few superpartners are light. This allows us to study WIMP--nucleus scattering in terms of a handful of MSSM parameters and thereby scrutinize their impact on dark matter direct-detection experiments. Focusing on spin-independent WIMP--nucleon scattering, we derive simplified, analytic expressions for the Wilson coefficients associated with Higgs and squark exchange. We utilize these results to study the complementarity of constraints due to direct-detection, flavor, and collider experiments. We also identify parameter configurations that produce (almost) vanishing cross sections. In the proximity of these so-called blind spots, we find that the amount of isospin violation may be much larger than typically expected in the MSSM. This feature is a generic property of parameter regions where cross sections are suppressed, and highlights the importance of a careful analysis of the nucleon matrix elements and the associated hadronic uncertainties. This becomes especially relevant once the increased sensitivity of future direct-detection experiments corners the MSSM into these regions of parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in regions of MSSM parameter space where the spin-independent WIMP-nucleon cross section is suppressed (near "blind spots"), the amount of isospin violation in WIMP-nucleon scattering can be much larger than typically expected, and this feature is a generic property of parameter regions where cross sections are suppressed, not a fine-tuned special case.

## Method

The authors derive simplified analytic expressions for the Wilson coefficients of Higgs and squark exchange contributions to spin-independent neutralino-nucleon scattering, using a systematic expansion in v/M_SUSY for a bino-like LSP. They examine four simplified models where only a few superpartners are light, driven by naturalness considerations, and study the complementarity of constraints from direct-detection, flavor, and collider experiments. For the nucleon scalar matrix elements, they use a two-flavor chiral perturbation theory formalism (Method 1) that rigorously accounts for isospin violation and its uncertainties, comparing it with the traditional three-flavor approach (Method 2) and the micrOMEGAs implementation (Method 3).

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of blind spots in MSSM dark matter direct detection previously studied by Cheung (2012), Hisano (2012), Huang (2014), and Anandakrishnan (2014) for the Higgs sector, and by Ellis (2000) and Baer (2006) who first identified blind spots numerically in CMSSM scans. The paper uses the hadronic formalism developed by Crivellin (2013) for accurate determination of nucleon scalar matrix elements with rigorous uncertainty estimates, and enables future direct-detection experiments like XENON1T, LUX-ZEPLIN, and SuperCDMS SNOLAB to be interpreted with proper accounting for isospin-violating effects when the MSSM is pushed into blind-spot regions by null results.
