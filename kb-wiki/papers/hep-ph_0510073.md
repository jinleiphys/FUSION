# Study of Strangeness Condensation by Expanding About the Fixed Point of the Harada-Yamawaki Vector Manifestation

> Building on, and extending, the result of a higher-order in-medium chiral perturbation theory combined with renormalization group arguments and a variety of observations of the vector manifestation of Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry theory, we obtain a surprisingly simple description of kaon condensation by fluctuating around the "vector manifestation (VM)" fixed point identified to be the chiral restoration point. Our development establishes that strangeness condensation takes place at about 3 n_0 where n_0 is nuclear matter density. This result depends only on the renoramlization-group (RG) behavior of the vector interactions, other effects involved in fluctuating about the bare vacuum in so many previous calculations being "irrelevant" in the RG about the fixed point. Our results have major effects on the collapse of neutron stars into black holes.

## Key claim

Strangeness condensation occurs at approximately 3 times nuclear matter density (3 n₀), but this result depends only on the renormalization-group behavior of vector interactions when expanding about the vector manifestation fixed point (identified as the chiral restoration point), with other effects that dominate calculations from the bare vacuum being "irrelevant" in the RG sense.

## Method

The authors apply Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory with vector manifestation (HLS/VM) to dense matter, expanding about the VM fixed point rather than the matter-free vacuum. They compute the kaon-nucleon vector mean field from ω-meson exchange using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, incorporating medium-dependent scaling of the pion decay constant (Fπ → fπ* ≈ 0.8 Fπ at n₀) and the HLS parameter a = (Fσ/Fπ)² which flows to 1 at the fixed point. The critical density is estimated by tracking the density at which the in-medium kaon effective mass mK* drops to the electron chemical potential μe.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness condensation takes place at approximately 3 n₀, where n₀ is nuclear matter density
- The parametric pion decay constant Fπ ≈ 90 MeV is used, with in-medium reduction fπ* ≈ 0.8 Fπ at n = n₀
- The vector mean field felt by K⁻ at the fixed point (n ≈ 4 n₀) is enhanced by a factor of 3.1 relative to the matter-free expression, giving VK⁻ ≈ -516 MeV, sufficient to bring mK⁻* to zero
- At n ≈ 3 n₀, the in-medium kaon effective mass mK⁻* ≈ 124 MeV, increasing to approximately 165 MeV when the upper limit a ≈ 4/3 is included
- The chiral restoration density nχSR is estimated at n ≲ 4 n₀ in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the earlier chiral perturbation theory calculation of Thorsson, Prakash, and Lattimer (1994) which gave condensation density nc ≈ 3.1 n₀, but simplifies the description by exploiting the RG flow near the VM fixed point discovered by Harada and Yamawaki. The authors build on their own prior RG analysis of boson condensation (RGE-LRS) and the observation from next-to-next chiral perturbation theory (LBMR) that four-fermi interactions are essential for driving condensation but negligible in locating the critical density. The results have major implications for neutron star collapse into black holes, a scenario the authors plan to develop in a separate publication.
