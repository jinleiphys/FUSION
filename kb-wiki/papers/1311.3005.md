# Universal attractor in a highly occupied non-Abelian plasma

> We study the thermalization process in highly occupied non-Abelian plasmas at weak coupling. The non-equilibrium dynamics of such systems is classical in nature and can be simulated with real-time lattice gauge theory techniques. We provide a detailed discussion of this framework and elaborate on the results reported in~\cite{Berges:2013eia} along with novel findings. We demonstrate the emergence of universal attractor solutions, which govern the non-equilibrium evolution on large time scales both for non-expanding and expanding non-Abelian plasmas. The turbulent attractor for a non-expanding plasma drives the system close to thermal equilibrium on a time scale $t\sim Q^{-1} α_s^{-7/4}$. The attractor solution for an expanding non-Abelian plasma leads to a strongly interacting albeit highly anisotropic system at the transition to the low-occupancy or quantum regime. This evolution in the classical regime is, within the uncertainties of our simulations, consistent with the ``bottom up'' thermalization scenario~\cite{Baier:2000sb}. While the focus of this paper is to understand the non-equilibrium dynamics in weak coupling asymptotics, we also discuss the relevance of our results for larger couplings in the early time dynamics of heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that highly occupied non-Abelian plasmas at weak coupling exhibit universal attractor solutions governing the non-equilibrium evolution on large time scales, both for non-expanding and expanding plasmas. The attractor for a non-expanding plasma drives the system close to thermal equilibrium on a time scale \(t\sim Q^{-1} \alpha_s^{-7/4}\), while the expanding plasma attractor leads to a strongly interacting but highly anisotropic system at the transition to the low-occupancy quantum regime, consistent with the "bottom up" thermalization scenario within simulation uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed classical-statistical real-time lattice gauge theory simulations of non-Abelian plasmas in Fock-Schwinger gauge, using SU(2) gauge fields on a lattice with co-moving \(\tau,\eta\) coordinates. They fixed the residual gauge freedom via a generalized Coulomb gauge condition at a chosen time \(\tau_0\), implemented through iterative Fourier-accelerated gauge fixing, and extracted gauge fields and electric fields from link variables. The evolution was studied for both non-expanding and expanding geometries, with observables including energy density components and gauge-invariant hard scale quantities \(\Lambda_T^2\) and \(\Lambda_L^2\), computed perturbatively from mode expansions in terms of Hankel functions.

## Key numbers
- Thermalization time scale for non-expanding plasma: \(t \sim Q^{-1} \alpha_s^{-7/4}\), where \(Q\) is the characteristic momentum scale and \(\alpha_s\) the strong coupling constant.
- Normalization constants for the first set of physical mode solutions: \(c_2^{+} = \frac{\sqrt{\pi} e^{\pi\nu/2}}{2\pt}\) and \(c_1^{-} = \frac{\sqrt{\pi} e^{-\pi\nu/2}}{2\pt}\) for positive and negative frequency solutions respectively.
- Normalization constants for the second set of physical mode solutions: \(c_2^{+} = \tau_0 \frac{\sqrt{\pi} e^{\pi\nu/2}}{2} \pt\tau_0\) and \(c_2^{-} = \tau_0 \frac{\sqrt{\pi} e^{-\pi\nu/2}}{2} \pt\tau_0\).
- Zero transverse momentum mode normalization: \(\xi^{\nu+}(\tau) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\nu}} \left(\frac{\tau}{\tau_0}\right)^{-i\nu}\) and \(\xi^{\nu-}(\tau) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\nu}} \left(\frac{\tau}{\tau_0}\right)^{i\nu}\).
- Perturbative energy density expression: \(\epsilon(\tau) = 2N_g \int \frac{d^2\pvect}{(2\pi)^2}\frac{d\nu}{2\pi} \frac{\psqr}{2 \pt \tau} [f(\pvect,\nu,\tau) + 1/2]\), with \(N_g = 2(N_c^2-1)\).
- Hard scale observables: \(\Lambda_T^2(\tau) \simeq \frac{\int d^2\pvect d\pz 2\pt^2 \omega_p f(\pvect,\pz,\tau)}{\int d^2\pvect d\pz \omega_p f(\pvect,\pz,\tau)}\) and \(\Lambda_L^2(\tau) \simeq \frac{\int d^2\pvect d\pz 4\pz^2 \omega_p f(\pvect,\pz,\tau)}{\int d^2\pvect d\pz \omega_p f(\pvect,\pz,\tau)}\), with \(\omega_p \simeq p_T\) in the highly anisotropic limit.

## Context
This work extends the classical-statistical lattice approach to non-Abelian plasma thermalization, building on earlier mode analysis by Makhlin (1996) and Dusling (2011) for free field solutions in co-moving coordinates. The universal attractor solutions provide a framework for understanding the early-time dynamics of heavy ion collisions, connecting to the "bottom up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) and enabling quantitative predictions for the transition from classical to quantum regimes in both expanding and non-expanding plasmas. The results also inform the interpretation of larger coupling effects relevant to experimental heavy ion collision data.
