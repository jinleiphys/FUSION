# Low-energy eta-d resonance

> Elastic eta-d scattering is considered within the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) formalism for various eta-N input data. A three-body resonant state is found close to the eta-d threshold. This resonance is sustained for different choices of the two-body eta-N scattering length a_{ηN}. The position of the resonance moves towards the eta-d threshold when Re a_{ηN} is increased, and turns into a quasi-bound state at Re a_{ηN}=0.733 fm.

## Key claim

A three-body resonant state exists in the elastic eta-deuteron system close to the eta-d threshold, and this resonance is sustained for different choices of the two-body eta-N scattering length a_{eta N}. The resonance turns into a quasi-bound state when Re a_{eta N} reaches 0.733 fm, meaning the existence of the resonance depends on the strength of the eta-N attraction.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for elastic eta-deuteron scattering, using separable representations of the two-body T-operators. The nucleon-nucleon separable potential was taken from Garcilazo (1980) with parameters slightly modified to be consistent with more recent NN data, while the eta-N T-matrix was modeled via an S_{11} resonance propagator with vertex functions. Calculations were performed for collision energies from zero (eta-d threshold) up to 22 MeV, with the imaginary part of a_{eta N} fixed to 0.3 fm and the real part varied between 0.55 fm and 0.90 fm.

## Key numbers

- The transition from resonance to quasi-bound state occurs at Re a_{eta N} = 0.733 fm
- For Re a_{eta N} = 0.55 fm, the resonance energy E_{eta d}^{res} = 8.24 MeV with width Gamma_{eta d} = 9.15 MeV
- For Re a_{eta N} = 0.65 fm, E_{eta d}^{res} = 7.46 MeV, Gamma_{eta d} = 8.45 MeV
- For Re a_{eta N} = 0.75 fm, E_{eta d}^{res} = 6.01 MeV, Gamma_{eta d} = 5.87 MeV
- For Re a_{eta N} = 0.85 fm, E_{eta d}^{res} = 4.39 MeV, Gamma_{eta d} = 5.79 MeV
- For Re a_{eta N} = 0.90 fm, E_{eta d}^{res} = 3.73 MeV, Gamma_{eta d} = 6.81 MeV
- The range parameter for the eta-N vertex function alpha = 3.316 fm^{-1}
- The S_{11} resonance parameters: E_0 = 1535 MeV - (m_N + m_eta), Gamma = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier few-body calculations by Rakityansky et al. (1996) and Shevchenko et al. (1998) that suggested the possibility of eta-nuclei, and provides a more precise determination of the critical Re a_{eta N} value (0.733 fm) where the eta-d system transitions from resonant to quasi-bound behavior. The results are relevant for interpreting the near-threshold enhancement in the reaction np -> d eta observed by the Uppsala group (Calen et al., 1998), and suggest a rough lower bound of Re a_{eta N} >= 0.75 fm if that bump is explained by final-state interaction through an eta-d resonance. The work also enables future explicit calculations of cross-sections for reactions such as gamma d -> d eta and np -> d eta to test different eta-N scattering length values.
