# Strangeness production and flow in heavy-ion collisions

> We study strangeness (K^+, K^-, and Lambda) production and flow in Ni+Ni collisions at 1-2 AGeV, based on the relativistic transport model including the strangeness degrees of freedom. We find that strangeness spectra and flow are sensitive to the properties of strange hadrons in nuclear medium. The predictions of the chiral perturbation theory that the K^+ feels a weak repulsive potential and K^- feels a strong attractive potential are in good agreement with recent experimental data from FOPI and KaoS collaborations.

# Strangeness production and flow in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The chiral perturbation theory predictions that K⁺ experiences a weak repulsive potential and K⁻ experiences a strong attractive potential in nuclear matter are consistent with experimental data from FOPI and KaoS, but only when the kaon and antikaon in-medium energies are parameterized with specific choices for the scalar attraction parameters (a_K = 0.22 GeV²fm³, a_Ȳ = 0.35 GeV²fm³) that incorporate approximate cancellations between the range term and the density-dependent pion decay constant.

## Method
The authors extended a relativistic transport model (based on a nonlinear σ-ω Lagrangian) to include strangeness degrees of freedom, propagating nucleons, hyperons, kaons, and antikaons in their respective mean-field potentials. They included baryon-baryon (BB → BYK), pion-nucleon (πN → YK), and strangeness-exchange (πY → ȲN) production channels, with cross sections taken from Randrup-Ko, Cugnon, and Sibirtsev parameterizations, and simulated Ni+Ni collisions at 1–2 AGeV for various impact parameters.

## Key numbers
- Kaon effective mass increases by about 4% at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, corresponding to a weak repulsive potential.
- Antikaon effective mass drops by about 22% at ρ₀, corresponding to a strong attractive potential (somewhat smaller than the 200 ± 20 MeV attraction inferred from kaonic atom data).
- At 3ρ₀, the antikaon effective mass is approximately 170 MeV; with a 50 MeV correction for neutron-rich matter, m_Ȳ* ≈ 220 MeV, implying kaon condensation at ρ ~ 3ρ₀ given an electron chemical potential μ_e(3ρ₀) = 214 MeV.
- Without medium effects, the calculated K⁻ yield at 1.8 AGeV is a factor 3–4 below experimental data; including medium effects increases the yield by about a factor of 3, bringing it into agreement.
- The K⁻ yield at 1.8 AGeV roughly equals the K⁺ yield at 1.0 AGeV, despite the NN → NNKȲ cross section being about one order of magnitude smaller than NN → NKΛ near threshold (both have Q-values of -230 MeV).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of strangeness production and flow at SIS energies by Fang (1994), Maruyama (1994), Aichelin (1994), Li and Ko (1995, 1996), and Cassing (1995), using a relativistic transport model with explicit strangeness degrees of freedom. The results support the chiral perturbation theory framework for kaon-nucleon interactions developed by Lee (1994) and Weise (1994), and are relevant for determining kaon properties in dense matter needed for neutron star studies by Prakash (1995). The findings enable experimental constraints on the in-medium kaon and antikaon potentials from heavy-ion collision data, and suggest that kaon condensation in neutron stars may occur at densities around 3ρ₀.
