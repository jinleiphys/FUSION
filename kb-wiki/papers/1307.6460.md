# The 1-Jettiness event shape for DIS with NNLL resummation

> We propose the use of 1-jettiness, a global event shape, for exclusive single jet production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS). We derive a factorization formula, using the Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, differential in the transverse momentum and rapidity of the jet and the 1-jettiness event shape. It provides a quantitative measure of the shape of the final-state QCD radiation in the presence of the hard jet, providing a useful powerful probe of QCD and nuclear physics. For example, one expects differences in the observed pattern of QCD radiation between large and small nuclei and these can be quantified by the 1-jettiness event shape. Numerical results are given for this new DIS event shape at leading twist with resummation at the next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) level of accuracy, for a variety of nuclear targets. Such studies would be ideal at a future EIC or LHeC electron-ion collider, where a range of nuclear targets are planned.

## Key claim
The paper proposes 1-jettiness as a global event shape for exclusive single jet production in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering, claiming it can quantify differences in QCD radiation patterns between large and small nuclei. The hedge is that this is demonstrated at leading twist with NNLL resummation, and the observable is sensitive to nuclear effects only through the nuclear parton distribution functions, with power corrections depending on an unknown nuclear scale parameter alpha.

## Method
The authors derive a factorization formula using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the cross-section differential in jet transverse momentum, rapidity, and the 1-jettiness event shape tau_1. They perform resummation of Sudakov logarithms at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy and compute the cross-section at leading twist using the EPS09 nuclear PDF set. The factorization separates the cross-section into hard, beam, jet, and soft functions, where the beam function is further expanded via an operator product expansion into perturbative coefficients convoluted with nuclear PDFs.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy: s = A * Q_e^2, where Q_e = 90 GeV for the numerical results shown
- Jet transverse momentum: P_{J_T} = 20 GeV for the numerical results shown
- Jet rapidity: y = 0 for the tau_1 distribution results shown
- Fixed tau_1 = 1.5 GeV for the rapidity distribution results shown
- The nuclear scale parameterization: Q_s^2(A) ~ A^alpha * Lambda_QCD^2, where alpha depends on underlying nuclear physics
- The integration lower limit for nuclear PDFs: x_* = e^y * P_{J_T} / (Q_e - e^{-y} * P_{J_T})

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NLL-level results for a proton target (Kang 2012) to NNLL resummation for multiple nuclear targets, achieving the first NNLL resummation for a DIS event shape. It builds on the N-Jettiness formalism introduced by Stewart (2010) and the earlier DIS event shape work on thrust by Antonelli (1999) and broadening by Dasgupta (2001). The results are complementary to the independent NNLL study by Kang (2013) which examined three versions of 1-jettiness for DIS. The observable is designed for future experiments at the EIC or LHeC, where the ratio of distributions between different nuclear targets shows dramatic reduction in theoretical uncertainties and clear deviations from unity, enabling quantitative probes of nuclear PDF effects including shadowing, anti-shadowing, the EMC effect, and Fermi motion.
