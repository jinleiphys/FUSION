# Higher Order Processes in Electromagnetic Production of Electron Positron Pairs in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study higher-order effects in the electromagnetic production of electron-positron pairs in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Treating the field of the heavy ions as an external field and neglecting the interaction among electrons and positrons, we show that the $N$-pair creation amplitude is the antisymmetrised product of $N$ one-pair creation amplitudes and the vacuum amplitude. Neglecting contributions coming from exchange terms, we show that the total probability for $N$ pairs is approximately a Poisson distribution. We investigate further the structure of the reduced one-pair amplitude, concentrating especially on multiple-particle corrections. We calculate the first of these corrections in second order Magnus theory based on our previous result in second-order Born approximation for impact parameter $b$ zero. Explicit calculations show that the total probability is increased up to 10 \% by this correction for realistic collider parameters. The calculations can also be used to confirm the use of the Poisson distribution for the total probability.

## Key claim

The N-pair creation amplitude can be expressed as an antisymmetrised product of N reduced one-pair creation amplitudes times the vacuum amplitude, and after neglecting exchange terms the total N-pair probability approximately follows a Poisson distribution; however, the first multiple-particle correction to the reduced one-pair amplitude increases the total probability by up to 10% for realistic collider parameters, showing that higher-order processes beyond the "quasi boson" approximation are not negligible.

## Method

The authors treat the heavy-ion fields as external fields and neglect electron-positron interactions, using Feynman boundary conditions to derive the N-pair amplitude as a determinant of reduced one-pair amplitudes times the vacuum amplitude. They then apply second-order Magnus theory to extract the lowest-order multiple-particle correction to the one-pair creation amplitude, and evaluate this correction numerically using their previous analytic result for the differential pair creation amplitude in second-order Born approximation at impact parameter b = 0.

## Context

This work extends earlier models by Baur (1990), Rhoades-Brown (1991), and Best (1992), which all obtained a Poisson distribution for multiple-pair probabilities but were based on "quasi boson" approximations that treat each electron-positron pair as an unbreakable unit. The present paper shows that multiple-particle corrections exist in which electrons and positrons do not behave as quasi bosons, and that these corrections can be included consistently by modifying the reduced one-pair probability used in the Poisson distribution. The results enable more accurate calculations of multiple-pair production at colliders and confirm that the Poisson approximation remains useful despite these corrections.
