# Towards the finite-volume spectrum of the Roper resonance

> The finite-volume energy levels corresponding to the Roper resonance based on a two-flavor chiral effective Lagrangian at leading one-loop order are investigated. It is shown that the Roper mass can be extracted from these levels for not too large lattice volumes. Further, to include three-body $N ππ$ dynamics, a non-relativistic effective field theory for the Roper resonance within a covariant particle-dimer picture is introduced. This particle-dimer approach is a suitable framework to investigate three-particle scattering relevant for the Roper channel. The appearing dimer fields are analyzed, the energy levels of the Roper resonance in a finite volume are calculated and compared to the results from the chiral effective Lagrangian.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance mass can be extracted from finite-volume energy levels calculated using a two-flavor chiral effective Lagrangian at leading one-loop order, but only for not too large lattice volumes; the signature of avoided level crossing is clearly visible for the Nπ channel but is suppressed for the Δπ channel and for the three-particle Nππ channel in the particle-dimer approach.

## Method

The author first computes the Roper self-energy at third chiral order in baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) with pions, nucleons, delta baryons, and the Roper as explicit degrees of freedom, then replaces the spatial loop integral by a sum over quantized momenta to obtain finite-volume energy levels from the pole condition. Separately, a non-relativistic effective field theory with covariant dynamics is constructed using a particle-dimer formalism, where auxiliary dimer fields (Δ for nucleon-pion, σ for pion-pion, and R for the Roper itself) are introduced to reformulate the three-body Nππ problem as a two-body scattering problem; the finite-volume self-energy contributions from Nπ and Nσ intermediate states are evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- Roper pole mass: m_R = 1.365 GeV (from PDG)
- Critical value for avoided level crossing: m_R / m_N ≈ 1.45 (where m_N is the nucleon mass)
- For box sizes M_π L ≈ 6, the interacting energy levels asymptotically approach the non-interacting levels in both the BChPT and particle-dimer calculations
- The chiral expansion is limited to third chiral order, O(p^3), with the chiral-symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume treatment of the Roper resonance from Severt (2020), which considered only two-particle Nπ and Δπ final states, by introducing a particle-dimer framework that can incorporate three-particle Nππ dynamics. The particle-dimer approach reproduces the Nπ spectrum from BChPT accurately, and the Nσ contribution shows that three-particle energy levels lie close to non-interacting Nππ levels with suppressed avoided level crossing. The method is intended to enable comparison between lattice QCD calculations (such as those by Lang (2016) and Liu (2016)) and effective field theory, and could be applied to other hadronic systems like the a₁(1260) resonance.
