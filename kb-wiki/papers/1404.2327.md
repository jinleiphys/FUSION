# What hard probes tell us about the quark-gluon plasma: Theory

> In the study of the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, hard and electromagnetic (EM) processes play an essential role as probes of the properties of the dense medium. They can be used to study a wide range of properties of the dense medium in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, from space-time profiles of the dense matter, bulk transport coefficients to EM responses and the jet transport parameter. I review in this talk these medium properties, how they can be studied through hard and EM probes and the status of recent theoretical and phenomenological investigations.

## Key claim

Hard and electromagnetic probes can be used to extract a wide range of properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but the extraction relies on theoretical modeling and comparisons to experimental data, with significant uncertainties remaining in the determination of transport parameters such as the jet transport parameter and the EM emission rate.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical and phenomenological investigations of QGP properties using hard probes, including jet quenching, electromagnetic emission, and quarkonium suppression. It discusses how comparisons between experimental data and theoretical models, such as relativistic hydrodynamics for bulk medium evolution, perturbative QCD calculations for EM emission rates, and multiple approaches to parton energy loss within the JET Collaboration framework, are used to extract medium properties like the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and the jet transport parameter.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted from hydrodynamic simulations at RHIC and LHC: η/s ≈ 0.08–0.2
- Net NLO corrections to the EM emission rate increase the total rate by about 20% over the LO result
- Jet transport parameter at the center of most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) at initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c: q̂₀/T³ ≈ 4.6 ± 1.2 for a quark with initial energy 10 GeV, corresponding to q̂₀ ≈ 1.2 ± 0.3 GeV²/fm at T = 370 MeV
- Jet transport parameter at the center of most central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): q̂₀/T³ ≈ 3.7 ± 1.4 for a quark with initial energy 10 GeV, corresponding to q̂₀ ≈ 1.9 ± 0.7 GeV²/fm at T = 470 MeV
- Lattice QCD results suggest S-wave and P-wave charmonium states melt at T ≥ 1.5 T_c, while P-wave bottonium melts at T ≈ T_c and S-wave bottonium states remain up to 2 T_c

## Context

This review extends the line of work on hard probes of the QGP that began with early theoretical ideas at AGS and SPS energies and became practical at RHIC and LHC, where hard process cross sections are large enough for precision studies. It summarizes results from the JET Collaboration (Burke et al., 2013) which combined five different semi-analytic formulations (GLV-CUJET, HT-M, HT-BW, MARTINI, McGill-AMY) to extract the jet transport parameter, and discusses lattice QCD calculations of EM emission rates (Ding et al.) and quarkonium spectral functions (Asakawa et al., 2000). The work enables future precision studies of the temperature and energy dependence of the jet transport parameter and the role of non-perturbative contributions to EM emission rates.
