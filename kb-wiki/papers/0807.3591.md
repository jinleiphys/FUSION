# Thermal effects on the Fission Barrier of neutron-rich nuclei

> We discuss the fission barrier height of neutron-rich nuclei in a r-process site at highly excited state, which is resulted from the beta-decay or the neutron-capture processes. We particularly investigate the sensitivity of the fission barrier height to the temperature, including the effect of pairing phase transition from superfluid to normal fluid phases. To this end, we use the finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov method with a zero-range pairing interaction. We also discuss the temperature dependence of the fission decay rate.

## Key claim

The fission barrier height of neutron-rich nuclei in r-process sites is sensitive to temperature, but the effect is not uniform: for the near-stability nucleus \(^{236}\)U the barrier height continuously increases with temperature, while for the neutron-rich nucleus \(^{286}\)Fm it first increases then begins to decrease at T = 0.6 MeV, due to differences in the temperature dependence of the pairing energy at the minimum and maximum points of the potential energy surface.

## Method

The authors used the finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FTHFB) method with a zero-range volume-type pairing interaction, employing the SLy4 Skyrme parameter set. They constrained the nuclear shape with the quadrupole operator \(\hat{Q}_2\) assuming reflection and axially symmetric deformations, and computed the fission barrier height as the difference between the highest and lowest total energy in the potential energy surface for temperatures from 0.0 to 0.8 MeV. The fission decay rate was then estimated using the Bohr-Wheeler formula with the liquid-drop-model mass parameter.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{236}\)U, the fission barrier height increases from 20.517 MeV at T = 0.0 MeV to 26.845 MeV at T = 0.8 MeV.
- For \(^{286}\)Fm, the fission barrier height is 11.771 MeV at T = 0.0 MeV, rises to 14.782 MeV at T = 0.6 MeV, then decreases to 12.288 MeV at T = 0.8 MeV.
- The pairing energy at the ground-state minimum for \(^{236}\)U is -7.233 MeV at T = 0.0 MeV and becomes 0.000 MeV at T = 0.7 MeV, indicating the pairing phase transition.
- The pairing energy at the ground-state minimum for \(^{286}\)Fm is -21.793 MeV at T = 0.0 MeV and remains -11.052 MeV at T = 0.6 MeV, showing weaker temperature dependence.
- Using the temperature-dependent fission barrier, the decay widths are suppressed by a factor of about \(10^{-1}\) for \(^{286}\)Fm and \(10^{-3}\) for \(^{236}\)U at excitation energies larger than 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of fission barrier heights that were limited to zero-temperature calculations (Samyn 2005, Burvenich 2004, Stoitsov 2003) by incorporating thermal effects relevant to r-process environments where nuclei are highly excited by beta-decay or neutron-capture. The results enable more realistic modeling of fission recycling in r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly for the formation of elements around A \(\sim\) 130, and show that the commonly used zero-temperature approximation can significantly overestimate fission decay rates for nuclei near stability while having a smaller effect for neutron-rich nuclei.
