# Shrinking the Quark Gluon Plasma

> In recent years the understanding on the limits of the smallest possible droplet of the Quark Gluon Plasma has been called into question. Experimental results from both the Large Hadron Collider and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider have provided hints that the Quark Gluon Plasma may be produced in systems as small as that formed in pPb or dAu collisions. Yet alternative explanations still exist from correlations arising from quarks and gluons in a color glass condensate picture. In order to resolve these two scenarios, a system size scan has been proposed at the Large Hadron Collider for collisions of ArAr and OO. Here we make predictions for a possible future run of ArAr and OO collisions at the Large Hadron Collider and study the system size dependence of a variety of flow observables. We find that linear response (from the initial conditions to the final flow harmonics) becomes more dominant in smaller systems whereas linear+cubic response can accurately predict multi-particle cumulants for a wide range of centralities in large systems.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that in smaller collision systems (ArAr and OO), linear response from initial eccentricities to final flow harmonics becomes more dominant, whereas larger systems (PbPb, XeXe) require additional nonlinear (cubic) response to accurately describe multi-particle cumulants across a wide range of centralities. This scaling behavior may help distinguish between hydrodynamic and color glass condensate explanations for collective flow in small systems.

## Method

The authors used TRENTO initial conditions tuned to reproduce IP-GLASMA eccentricities, coupled with the event-by-event relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code v-USPhydro. They generated 3 million initial conditions per collision system for centrality binning and ran 31,000 hydrodynamic events per ion type (PbPb, XeXe, ArAr, OO) using a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s=0.047, a starting time τ0=0.6 fm, and a freeze-out temperature TFO=150 MeV. The equation of state was based on 2+1 lattice QCD results, and the particle list used was PDG16+.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.047
- Starting time for hydrodynamics: τ0 = 0.6 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: TFO = 150 MeV
- For OO collisions at fixed radius R=3 fm, the initial entropy is 75% larger than for PbPb collisions at the same radius
- The maximum temperature at the hottest spot in OO collisions is roughly ΔT ∼ 50 MeV larger than in PbPb collisions for events with the same multiplicity (M=556)
- Inelastic cross-section used: σNN = 71 mb at √sNN = 5.85 TeV and σNN = 72.5 mb at √sNN = 6.5 TeV
- For XeXe collisions, the charged particle multiplicity prediction overshot ALICE data and required rescaling by a factor of 0.9 (using a=108 instead of a=120) to reproduce experimental results

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of flow harmonics in PbPb collisions at √sNN=5.02 TeV (Alba 2017) and XeXe collisions at √sNN=5.44 TeV (Giacalone 2017, Noronha-Hostler 2018) to even smaller symmetric systems of ArAr and OO. It directly addresses the ongoing debate about whether collective flow signatures in small systems (pPb, dAu, 3HeAu) arise from a hydrodynamic quark-gluon plasma phase or from initial-state correlations in the color glass condensate picture (Kovchegov 2012, Iancu 2001). The predictions for ArAr and OO collisions at the LHC are intended to help resolve this question through a system size scan, and also provide baselines for future studies of jet quenching and strangeness enhancement in intermediate-size systems.
