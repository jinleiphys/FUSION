# The $A_y$-Problem in Refined Resonating Group Model calculations for ($p-^3{\rm He}$) Scattering

> We report on a microscopic Refined Resonating Group Model (RRGM) calculation of scattering of $p$ off ${}^3{\rm He}$ employing the Argonne-$v_{14}$ and the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials without three-nucleon forces at low energies up to 30 MeV. The calculated phase-shifts verify the well-known proton analyzing power $A_y$-problem. We demonstrate that with corrected ${}^3P_2$ phase-shifts experimental differential cross-section and analyzing power data can be explained.

## Key claim
Microscopic Refined Resonating Group Model (RRGM) calculations using the Argonne-v14 and Bonn nucleon-nucleon potentials without three-nucleon forces reproduce the well-known proton analyzing power Ay problem in (p-3He) scattering, and the authors demonstrate that by artificially correcting only the 3P2 phase-shift to its experimental value, the experimental differential cross-section and analyzing power data can be explained at low energies, though this single correction is insufficient at higher energies.

## Method
The authors performed RRGM calculations for (p-3He) scattering up to 30 MeV center-of-mass energy using two realistic NN potentials (Argonne-v14 and Bonn) without three-nucleon forces, employing two different model spaces for the 3He subsystem (a medium space with 5 basis vectors and a large space with 23 basis vectors). They included distortion channels to enlarge the model space in the interaction region and used the Kohn-Hulthén variational principle to solve the Schrödinger equation, obtaining phase-shifts and then calculating analyzing powers and differential cross-sections. The 3P2 phase-shift was then manually adjusted by adding 10.5 degrees at 5.20 MeV and 15.0 degrees at 22.5 MeV to match experimental values and test the effect on observables.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of 3He in the medium model: -5.588 MeV (Argonne-v14) and -6.287 MeV (Bonn), compared to experimental -7.718 MeV
- Binding energy of 3He in the large model: -6.895 MeV (Argonne-v14) and -7.416 MeV (Bonn), compared to experimental -7.718 MeV
- Charge r.m.s. radius of 3He in the medium model: 1.72 fm (Argonne-v14) and 1.62 fm (Bonn), compared to experimental 1.97 fm
- Charge r.m.s. radius of 3He in the large model: 1.86 fm (Argonne-v14) and 1.76 fm (Bonn), compared to experimental 1.97 fm
- To reach experimental 3P2 phase-shift, 10.5 degrees were added at Ec.m. = 5.20 MeV and 15.0 degrees at Ec.m. = 22.5 MeV
- The rearrangement channel (d-2He) opens at 5.7 MeV (Argonne-v14) and 6.5 MeV (Bonn) in the medium model, and at 7.1 MeV (Argonne-v14) and 7.6 MeV (Bonn) in the large model

## Context
This work extends earlier Resonating Group Model calculations by Reichstein (1971) and Heiss (1972) using much simpler NN interactions, and is contemporary with studies by Viviani (2001) using correlated hyperspherical harmonics with the av18 interaction and Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, though only at very low energies (Ec.m. ≤ 1.69 MeV). The results confirm that all realistic NN interactions studied (av8', av14, av18, and Bonn) show the same deficiency in P-wave phase-shifts, and the authors suggest the (p-3He) system is well suited for further studies of three-nucleon forces, particularly for contributions affecting the 3P2-3P0 splitting as noted by Pfitzinger (2001).
