# Novel rapidity dependence of directed flow in high energy heavy ion collisions

> For high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, we show that a combination of space-momentum correlations characteristic of radial expansion together with the correlation between the position of a nucleon in the nucleus and its stopping, results in a very specific rapidity dependence of directed flow: a reversal of sign in the mid-rapidity region. We support our argument by RQMD model calculations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s$ = 200 AGeV.

# Novel rapidity dependence of directed flow in high energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The combination of space-momentum correlations from radial expansion and the correlation between a nucleon's position in the nucleus and its stopping produces a sign reversal (wiggle) in directed flow at mid-rapidity for high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, without requiring a quark-gluon plasma equation of state or complete stopping.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD v2.4) model in cascade mode to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV with impact parameters b = 5-10 fm. They computed the first Fourier coefficient v1 = <cos phi> of the particle azimuthal distribution relative to the reaction plane, and the corresponding coordinate-space coefficient s1 = <cos phi_s>, for both nucleons and pions as a function of rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV, impact parameter range b = 5-10 fm, simulated with RQMD v2.4 in cascade mode
- For nucleons at mid-rapidity, both v1 and s1 show a negative slope, consistent with the predicted wiggle structure
- For pions at mid-rapidity, v1 shows a positive slope, opposite to the nucleon behavior
- At beam/target rapidity, s1 for pions has the opposite sign from v1, attributed to shadowing by nucleons

## Context

This work extends the study of directed flow from lower energy regimes (2-158 AGeV) where the experimental slope around mid-rapidity trends from positive at 2 AGeV (E895 Collaboration, Liu et al., 1998) to nearly zero at 158 AGeV (NA49 Collaboration, Appelshäuser et al., 1998). The predicted wiggle differs from earlier predictions for Ca+Ca at 350 AMeV (Soff et al., 1995), which relied on repulsive nucleon-nucleon collisions and an attractive mean field, and for Au+Au at 11 AGeV (Csernai and Röhricht, 1999; Brachmann et al., 1999), which required a QGP equation of state and complete stopping. The wiggle at mid-rapidity is accessible to current SPS experiment NA49 and future RHIC experiments, and its dependence on centrality, system size, and energy may reveal information on the relation between radial flow and baryon stopping.
