# Dynamically generated states from the $ηK^*\bar{K}^*$, $πK^*\bar{K}^*$, and $K K^*\bar{K}^*$ systems within the fixed-center approximation

> The three-body systems $ηK^* \bar{K}^*$, $πK^* \bar{K}^*$, and $K K^* \bar{K}^*$ are further investigated within the framework of fixed-center approximation, where $K^* \bar{K}^*$ is treated as the fixed-center, corresponding to the possible scalar meson $a_0(1780)$ or the tensor meson $f_2'(1525)$. The interactions between $η$, $π$, $K$, and $K^*$ are taken from the chiral unitary approach. The resonance structures appear in the modulus squared of the three-body scattering amplitude and suggest that $η/ π/ K$-$(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)/f'_2(1525)}$ hadron state can be formed. By scattering the $η$ meson on the fixed-center $(K^* \bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$, it is found that there is a distinct peak around 2100 MeV, as shown in the modulus squared of the three-body scattering amplitude, which can be associated with the meson $π(2070)$. For the scattering of the $η$ meson on the $(K^* \bar{K}^*)_{f_2'(1525)}$, a resonance structure around 1890 MeV is found and it can be associated with the $η_2(1870)$ meson. Other resonance structures are also found and can be associated with $π_2(1880)$ and $η(2010)$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that resonance structures appearing in the modulus squared of three-body scattering amplitudes for the $\eta K^*\bar{K}^*$, $\pi K^*\bar{K}^*$, and $K K^*\bar{K}^*$ systems can be associated with known mesons such as $\pi(2070)$, $\eta_2(1870)$, $\pi_2(1880)$, and $\eta(2010)$, but only under the assumption that the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ subsystem forms a bound state corresponding to either the scalar meson $a_0(1780)$ or the tensor meson $f_2'(1525)$, and using the fixed-center approximation which assumes the two-body bound state wave function is not significantly changed by the third particle.

## Method
The authors apply the fixed-center approximation (FCA) to three-body systems where $K^*\bar{K}^*$ is treated as a fixed-center bound state, taken as either the scalar meson $a_0(1780)$ or the tensor meson $f_2'(1525)$. The two-body interactions between the pseudoscalar mesons ($\eta$, $\pi$, $K$) and the vector mesons ($K^*$, $\bar{K}^*$) are obtained from the chiral unitary approach in coupled channels, and the three-body scattering amplitude $T$ is constructed by summing multiple-scattering diagrams of the third particle with the two-body bound state, including form factors and $s$-wave projections.

## Key numbers
- The scalar meson $a_0(1780)$ is obtained with mass 1769 MeV using cutoff $\Lambda = 1100$ MeV in the isospin=1, spin=0 sector.
- The tensor meson $f_2'(1525)$ is obtained with mass 1517 MeV using cutoff $\Lambda = 1009$ MeV in the isospin=0, spin=2 sector.
- For the $\eta (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system, a peak is found around 2122 MeV, associated with $\pi(2070)$.
- For the $\eta (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{f_2'(1525)}$ system, a peak is found around 1896 MeV, associated with $\eta_2(1870)$.
- For the $\pi (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system with total isospin $I=0$, a bump structure is located at 1988 MeV, associated with $\eta(2010)$.
- For the $\pi (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system with $I=1$, a bump structure is located around 2030 MeV, associated with $\pi(2070)$.
- For the $K (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system with $I=\frac{1}{2}$, a resonance is found around 2156 MeV.
- For the $K (K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system with $I=\frac{3}{2}$, a peak structure is found around 2130 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the previous study by Shen et al. (2022) which investigated the same three-body systems but only treated the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ fixed-center as the scalar meson $f_0(1710)$. The present paper considers additional allowed combinations of the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ system, specifically the scalar meson $a_0(1780)$ and the tensor meson $f_2'(1525)$, which were predicted as dynamically generated states from vector-vector interactions in the chiral unitary approach by Molina et al. (2008) and Geng et al. (2008). The results suggest that several known mesons ($\pi(2070)$, $\eta_2(1870)$, $\pi_2(1880)$, $\eta(2010)$) may be interpreted as three-body molecular states, and the work predicts unobserved states in the $K(K^*\bar{K}^*)_{a_0(1780)}$ system, including one with exotic quantum numbers $I(J^P) = \frac{3}{2}(0^-)$, which could guide future experimental searches.
