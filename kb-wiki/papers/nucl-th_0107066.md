# Many-body Theory at Extreme Isospin

> The structure of nuclei far off beta-stability is investigated by nuclear many-body theory. In-medium interactions for asymmetric nuclear matter are obtained by (Dirac-) Brueckner theory thus establishing the link of nuclear forces to free space interactions. HFB and RPA theory is used to describe ground and excited states of nuclei from light to heavy masses. In extreme dripline systems pairing and core polarization are found to be most important for the binding, especially of halo nuclei. The calculations show that far off stability mean-field dynamics is gradually replaced by dynamical correlations, giving rise to the dissolution of shell structures.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in nuclei far from beta-stability, mean-field dynamics are gradually replaced by dynamical correlations, leading to the dissolution of shell structures; this transition is most pronounced in extreme dripline systems where pairing and core polarization become the dominant binding mechanisms, rather than the static mean-field.

## Method

The authors use (Dirac-) Brueckner theory to derive in-medium interactions for asymmetric nuclear matter, linking nuclear forces to free-space interactions. They then apply Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory to describe ground and excited states of nuclei from light to heavy masses, solving the Gorkov equations for continuum pairing and using dynamical core polarization (DCP) self-energies to treat valence-core interactions.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Li is S$_{2n}$=320 keV, indicating the importance of continuum coupling.
- In $^{12}$C, the first 2$^+$ state is at E$_x$=4.44 MeV, moving down to E$_x$=1.66 MeV in $^{18}$C.
- The dipole polarizability changes only within 10% over the $^{10-22}$C isotopic chain.
- Ground state spectroscopic factors from core polarization calculations: $^{8}$B (3/2$^-$, 130 keV, S=0.73); $^{11}$Be (1/2$^+$, 510 keV, S=0.74); $^{17}$C (5/2$^+$, 760 keV, S=0.43); $^{19}$C (1/2$^+$, 263 keV, S=0.41).
- The single particle spectroscopic factor for $^{11}$Be is S$_n$(1/2$^*$,g.s.)=0.75, in good agreement with transfer and breakup data.

## Context

This work extends microscopic many-body theory from symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric systems, building on prior Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations by de Jong and Lenske (2000) and the density dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) field theory. It enables a consistent description of halo nuclei and dripline systems, showing that pairing and core polarization replace mean-field binding, and provides a framework for analyzing breakup reactions and response functions measured in experiments with secondary beams.
