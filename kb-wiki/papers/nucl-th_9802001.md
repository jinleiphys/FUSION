# Theoretical Interpretation of Low-Mass Dileptons

> An overview is given of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and baryochemical potential. Within hadronic models of the vector correlator its implications for low-mass dilepton spectra in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and baryochemical potential can be studied via modifications of the vector correlator in hadronic models, but the approach toward the phase boundary remains a theoretical challenge and the observation of degenerate vector- and axialvector correlators as a unique signal is difficult to detect.

## Method

The authors review hadronic model calculations of the vector-meson spectral functions (ρ, ω, φ) in a hot and dense medium, using a low-density expansion of the vector correlator that includes pion and nucleon Compton amplitudes, as well as resonant ρN scattering through baryon resonances such as N(1720) and Δ(1905). The in-medium ρ-meson spectral functions are then used to compute dilepton production rates, which are integrated over a fireball model or implemented in transport calculations (HSD model) for comparison with CERES and HELIOS-3 data from ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at SpS energies.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at zero baryochemical potential from lattice QCD: T_c = 150 ± 20 MeV.
- Pion sigma commutator: Σ_π = 69 MeV; nucleon sigma commutator: Σ_N = 45 ± 8 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16/fm³, the dilute gas expression predicts a ~35% drop in the quark condensate ratio, with naive extrapolation indicating chiral restoration at ~3ρ_0.
- In the chiral limit, the condensate ratio decreases as T² for small T, while vector meson masses change as T⁴.
- Baryon resonances with appreciable decay width into ρN include N(1520) with Γ_ρN = 24 MeV, Δ(1620) with 22.5 MeV, Δ(1700) with 45 MeV, N(1720) with 105 MeV, and Δ(1905) with 210 MeV.
- At ρ_N = ρ_0 and T = 0, the ρ-meson spectral function shows significant broadening with a low-mass shoulder; at ρ_N + ρ_Δ = 1.4ρ_0 and T = 166 MeV, temperature provides additional overall broadening.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral symmetry restoration in QCD, building on lattice results at μ_q = 0 (Laermann, 1996) and low-temperature expansions from chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1987; Gerber and Leutwyler, 1989). It discusses the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) for dropping vector meson masses versus the alternative scenario of mass degeneracy with axial partners (Eletsky, 1993). The hadronic model calculations are compared with dilepton data from the CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations at SpS energies, and the results enable further quantitative tests using transport models such as HSD.
