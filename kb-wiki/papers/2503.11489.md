# Identified Hadron Production at Hadron Colliders in Next-to-Next-to-Leading-Order QCD

> In this work we calculate for the first time the next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) QCD corrections to identified hadron production at hadron colliders. The inclusion of the NNLO correction has an important impact on all observables considered in this work. Higher order corrections reduce scale uncertainty and in almost all cases are moderate. Overall, good perturbative convergence is observed across kinematics and observables. The uncertainty due to missing higher orders is relatively small and, in many cases, smaller than the experimental uncertainty. The largest source of theoretical uncertainty at present is from the knowledge of the non-perturbative parton-to-hadron fragmentation functions (FF), which dwarfs the scale uncertainty in most kinematic ranges. The inclusion of NNLO corrections demonstrates the precision studies potential of this class of observables. To fully realize this potential, however, a new generation of improved fragmentation functions may be needed. The results of the present work will enable global fits of FF with NNLO precision.

## Key claim

The first NNLO QCD corrections to identified hadron production at hadron colliders are presented, showing good perturbative convergence and reduced scale uncertainty; however, the largest theoretical uncertainty now comes from the non-perturbative fragmentation functions (FF), which dominate over scale uncertainty in most kinematic ranges and may require a new generation of improved FF sets to fully realize the precision potential.

## Method

The NNLO massless coefficient function for single and double identified hadron production was computed numerically using the STRIPPER approach for infrared singularity handling, with two-loop four-parton amplitudes from Broggio et al. (2014) and one-loop amplitudes from OpenLoops 2. The collinear singularities associated with final-state partons were subtracted in the MSbar scheme numerically, and the partonic cross-section was convolved with non-perturbative fragmentation functions from the NNFF, MAPFF, and JAM sets, using NNPDF3.1 parton distribution functions and a 15-point scale variation approach.

## Key numbers

- For the single inclusive π0 pT distribution at √s = 8 TeV (ALICE data, |y| < 0.8): the NNLO scale uncertainty is about a factor of three smaller than the NLO uncertainty; for pT above about 20 GeV, the NNLO scale uncertainty becomes smaller than the overall data uncertainty.
- For pT(π0) > 6 GeV, the FF uncertainty exceeds the scale uncertainty by a factor of five or more, making FF variation the largest source of theoretical uncertainty.
- For the invariant mass m(π0,π0) distribution at √s = 13 TeV (|y| < 2.1, pT > 1 GeV): for m(π0π0) > 7 GeV, the NNLO scale uncertainty becomes negligible relative to the FF uncertainty.
- For the charged hadron z distribution inside jets (anti-kT, R = 0.4, |y(jet)| < 2.1) at √s = 5.02 TeV (ATLAS data): the two FF sets (JAM and NNFF) are incompatible within their respective FF uncertainties for intermediate z values 0.06 < z < 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the NLO QCD calculations for identified hadron production at hadron colliders (Aversa 1988, Jager 2002) and the recent NNLL soft-gluon resummation (Hinderer 2018) to full NNLO precision for the first time. It complements the very recent NNLO calculations for semi-inclusive DIS (Goyal 2023, Bonino 2024) and existing NNLO SIA calculations, enabling the possibility of global FF fits with NNLO precision from hadron collider data, which was previously a major capability gap.
