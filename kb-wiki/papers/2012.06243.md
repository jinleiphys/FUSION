# Fire streaks, electromagnetic effects, directed flow and lifetime of the plasma at SPS energies

> We present our calculation of electromagnetic effects, induced by the spectator charge on Feynman-$x_F$ distributions of charged pions in peripheral $Pb+Pb$ collisions at CERN SPS energies, including realistic initial space-time-momentum conditions for pion emission. The calculation is performed in the framework of a specific implementation of the fire-streak model, adopted to the production of both $π^-$ and $π^+$ mesons. Isospin effects are included to take into account the asymmetry in production of $π^+$ and $π^-$ at high rapidity. A comparison to a simplified model from the literature is made. We obtain a good description of the NA49 data on the $x_F$- and $p_T$-dependence of the ratio of cross sections $π^+/π^-$. The experimental data favors short times ($0.5<τ<2$~fm/$c$) for fast pion creation in the local fire-streak rest frame. The possibility of the expansion of the spectators is considered in our calculation, and its influence on the electromagnetic effect observed for the $π^+/π^-$ ratio is discussed. The influence of directed and elliptic flow, and vorticity on the observed effect is also estimated. We conclude that the fire-streak model, which properly describes the centrality dependence of $π^-$ rapidity spectra at CERN SPS energies, also provides realistic initial conditions for pion production. Consequently, it provides a quantitative description of the electromagnetic effect on the $π^+/π^-$ ratio as a function of $x_F$.

## Key claim

The fire-streak model, which already describes the centrality dependence of pion rapidity spectra at SPS energies, can also provide a quantitative description of the electromagnetic effect on the pion ratio, but only when the pion creation time is not fixed and instead depends linearly on the fire-streak excitation energy, with values between 0.5 and 2 fm/c.

## Method

The authors implement a specific version of the fire-streak model where colliding nuclei are divided into 1 fm x 1 fm transverse bricks that form fire streaks after collision, each fragmenting independently into pions via a parametrized fragmentation function fitted to NA49 rapidity distributions. The initial transverse-momentum distributions are taken from UrQMD simulations and parametrized as exponential functions. Electromagnetic effects from spectator charge are calculated by introducing a pion creation time in the fire-streak rest frame, with a linear dependence on fire-streak excitation energy, and by optionally including spectator expansion with radial surface velocity.

## Key numbers

- Pion creation time range favored by data: 0.5 < tau < 2 fm/c in the local fire-streak rest frame
- Inverse slope parameters from UrQMD fits at midrapidity: T_pi- = 165 MeV, T_pi+ = 163 MeV
- Best description of NA49 data obtained with spectator radial surface velocity beta_R = 0.2 in the spectator rest frame
- Fire-streak rotation angle alpha = 1.75 degrees gives good description of directed flow data
- Linear dependence parameter for pion creation time: a ≈ 0.08 (with tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c and tau_max = 2 fm/c)

## Context

This work extends earlier simplified models of electromagnetic effects in heavy-ion collisions, such as the single-point emission model with distance parameter d_E ≈ 0.5-1 fm used by Rybicki and collaborators (2014), by implementing realistic space-time-momentum initial conditions from the fire-streak model developed by the same authors (2018). The calculation enables quantitative extraction of the pion emission time and spectator expansion dynamics from NA49 data on the x_F- and p_T-dependence of the π+/π- ratio, and demonstrates that directed flow (v_1) produces a non-negligible effect while elliptic flow (v_2) and vorticity do not significantly affect the electromagnetic modification.
