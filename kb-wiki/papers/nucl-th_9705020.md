# A Hartree-Bose Mean-Field Approximation for IBM-3

> A Hartree-Bose mean-field approximation for the IBM-3 is presented. A Hartree- Bose transformation from spherical to deformed bosons with charge-dependent parameters is proposed which allows bosonic pair correlations and includes higher angular momentum bosons. The formalism contains previously proposed IBM-2 and IBM-3 intrinsic states as particular limits.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized Hartree-Bose mean-field approximation for IBM-3 that allows the deformation parameters in the transformation from spherical to deformed bosons to depend on the isospin component tau, and introduces a variable isospin bosonic pair correlation parameter alpha; the formalism contains the previously proposed IBM-2 and Ginocchio-Leviatan (1994) intrinsic states as particular limits, but the authors note that for isospin-conserving Hamiltonians the energy gain from breaking isospin invariance is relatively small.

## Method

The authors define a Hartree-Bose transformation from spherical boson operators (with angular momentum l, projection m, and isospin projection tau) to deformed boson operators, where the deformation parameters eta depend explicitly on the isospin component tau. A trial wavefunction for the ground state of an even-even system with proton excess is constructed using a correlated bosonic pair operator Lambda-dagger that mixes proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and proton-neutron bosons through a variational parameter alpha. The ground-state energy is minimized with respect to the deformation parameters and alpha by solving three coupled Hartree-Bose equations self-consistently, with the isospin matrix elements f1 and f2 calculated from the trial wavefunction.

## Key numbers

* For a system with 5 proton pairs and 3 neutron pairs interacting through the Ginocchio Hamiltonian (kappa=1 MeV), the minimum ground-state energy occurs at alpha approximately -0.32, not at alpha = -1/2 as in the Ginocchio-Leviatan formalism.
* The deformation parameters beta_tau become independent of tau when N_p = N_n (equal numbers of proton and neutron pairs), but diverge as N_p - N_n increases; the delta boson deformation beta_delta becomes very large compared to proton and neutron deformations.
* For the isospin-nonconserving Hamiltonian, single-particle energies used were: epsilon_s_pi = epsilon_s_nu = 0 MeV, epsilon_d_pi = epsilon_d_nu = 1.5 MeV, epsilon_s_delta = 2.3 |N_p - N_n| MeV, and epsilon_d_delta = 1.5 + 2.3 |N_p + N_n| MeV.

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic-state formalism for IBM-3 developed by Ginocchio and Leviatan (1994), which imposed charge-independent deformation parameters and a trial wavefunction with good isospin and additional isospin SU(3) symmetry. The new formalism allows treatment of isospin-breaking effects relevant for nuclei with Z approximately N in the mass region between Ca-40 and Sn-100, where proton-neutron pairing correlations become important. The approach enables future studies of higher angular momentum bosons (beyond l=0,2) and provides a framework for variation after isospin projection, which the authors suggest would yield a better approximation than the Ginocchio-Leviatan intrinsic state for isospin-conserving Hamiltonians.
