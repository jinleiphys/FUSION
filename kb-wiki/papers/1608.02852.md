# Spectra and rates of bremsstrahlung neutrino emission in stars

> We calculate the energy-differential rate for neutrino emission from electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung in stellar interiors taking into account the effects of electron screening and ionic correlations. We compare the energy-differential and the net rates, as well as the average $\barν_e$ and $\barν_x$ $(x = μ, τ)$ energies, for this process with those for $e^{\pm}$ pair annihilation, plasmon decay, and photo-neutrino emission over a wide range of temperature and density. We also compare our updated energy loss rates for the above thermal neutrino emission processes with the fitting formulas widely used in stellar evolution models and determine the temperature and density domain in which each process dominates. We discuss the implications of our results for detection of $\barν_e$ from massive stars during their pre-supernova evolution and find that pair annihilation makes the predominant contribution to the signal from the thermal emission processes.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung mostly produces sub-MeV neutrinos during pre-supernova evolution of massive stars, but that pair annihilation makes the predominant contribution to the detectable \(\bar\nu_e\) signal from thermal emission processes, especially for energies above the detection threshold of \(\sim 1.8\) MeV.

## Method

The authors derived the energy-differential rate for neutrino emission from electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung using leading-order Feynman diagrams with both charged-current and neutral-current interactions, incorporating electron screening via a static dielectric function and ionic correlations via a structure factor for a one-component plasma. They performed eight-dimensional Monte Carlo integrations using the Vegas algorithm to compute the rates, and compared them with rates for \(e^\pm\) pair annihilation, plasmon decay, and photo-neutrino emission over a wide range of temperature and density representative of massive stellar cores.

## Key numbers

- For an OCP composed of \(^{28}\)Si or \(^{56}\)Fe at \(T = 4 \times 10^9\) K and \(\rho/\mu_e = 10^8\) g cm\(^{-3}\), ionic correlations reduce the bremsstrahlung \(\bar\nu_e\) energy-differential rate by a factor of \(\sim 2\) at low energies and by a factor of \(\sim 1.3\) at high energies; at \(E_\nu \geq 1.8\) MeV the ratio \(F_{\bar\nu_e}/F_{\bar\nu_e}^0\) is close to 0.7.
- Four representative conditions are considered: \((T_9,\rho_7/\mu_e) = (0.87, 8.5\times 10^{-3})\) for C burning, \((2.3, 0.36)\) for O depletion, \((3.9, 1.9)\) for Si depletion, and \((7.1, 2.5\times 10^2)\) immediately prior to collapse.
- The average \(\bar\nu_e\) energy from pair annihilation in a non-degenerate and non-relativistic gas of \(e^\pm\) is estimated as \(\langle E_{\bar\nu_e}\rangle \sim m_e + 3k_BT\).
- The detection threshold for \(\bar\nu_e\) through capture on protons is \(E_{\rm th} \approx 1.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal neutrino emission spectra for \(e^\pm\) pair annihilation, plasmon decay, and photo-neutrino emission (Odrzywolek 2004, Dutta 2003, Ratkovic 2003, Misiaszek 2005, Odrzywolek 2007, Kato 2015, Patton 2015) by providing the first detailed calculation of the energy-differential rate for electron-nucleus bremsstrahlung including electron screening and ionic correlations. It also compares the updated energy loss rates with the fitting formulas of Itoh (1996) that are widely used in stellar evolution models. The results enable more accurate predictions of pre-supernova neutrino signals from massive stars, which could provide advance warning for supernova explosions and test stellar evolution models.
