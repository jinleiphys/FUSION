# Direct and indirect $α$ transfer in the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering

> The extensive elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering data measured at low energies show consistently an oscillating enhancement of the elastic cross section at backward angles that is difficult to describe within the conventional optical model. Given the significant $α$ spectroscopic factors predicted for the dissociation $^{16}$O$\toα+^{12}$C by the shell model (SM) and $α$-cluster model calculations, the contribution of the $α$ transfer channels to the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering should not be negligible, and is expected to account for the enhanced oscillation of the elastic cross section at backward angles. To reveal the impact of the $α$ transfer, a systematic coupled reaction channels (CRC) analysis of the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering has been performed where the multistep couplings between the elastic and inelastic scattering channels, the direct and indirect $α$ transfer channels were treated explicitly, using the real optical potentials and inelastic scattering form factors determined by the double-folding model. We show that a consistent CRC description of the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C data at different energies can be obtained over the whole angular region, using the $α$ spectroscopic factors determined recently in the large scale SM calculation. The present CRC results are, therefore, of interest not only for the nuclear scattering studies but also provide an important spectroscopic information on the cluster dissociation of $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

The oscillating enhancement of the elastic \(^{16}\text{O}+^{12}\text{C}\) cross section at backward angles, which cannot be described by the conventional optical model, is shown to arise from the coherent sum of elastic scattering and \(\alpha\) transfer amplitudes, but only when both direct and indirect \(\alpha\) transfer channels are included with \(\alpha\) spectroscopic factors from large-scale shell model calculations; the earlier 2-channel CRC analysis that treated the spectroscopic factor as a free parameter gave \(S_\alpha \approx 1.96\), more than twice the shell-model value of \(\approx 0.8\), indicating that indirect transfers are essential.

## Method

A systematic coupled reaction channels (CRC) analysis was performed using the code FRESCO, coupling the elastic scattering channel with direct and indirect \(\alpha\) transfer channels (via the \(0^+_2\), \(3^-_1\), \(2^+_1\) excited states of \(^{16}\text{O}\) and the \(2^+_1\) state of \(^{12}\text{C}\)). The real optical potentials and inelastic scattering form factors were obtained from the double-folding model with the CDM3Y3 density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction, while the imaginary optical potential was taken in a Woods-Saxon form with parameters fine-tuned to reproduce forward-angle elastic data. The \(\alpha\) spectroscopic factors were taken from the large-scale shell-model calculation of Volya (2015, 2017), and the CRC equations were solved with finite-range complex remnant terms and non-orthogonality corrections.

## Key numbers

- The \(\alpha\) separation energy of \(^{16}\text{O}\) in the ground state is \(E_\alpha(\text{g.s.}) = 7.162\) MeV.
- The best-fit \(\alpha\) spectroscopic factor from the 2-channel (direct transfer only) CRC analysis is \(S_\alpha \approx 1.96\) for the ground state of \(^{16}\text{O}\), obtained consistently at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 100\)–\(300\) MeV.
- The large-scale shell model predicts \(S_\alpha \approx 0.794\) for the ground state of \(^{16}\text{O}\), and \(S_\alpha \approx 0.535\), \(0.663\), and \(0.5\) for the \(0^+_2\), \(3^-_1\), and \(2^+_1\) excited states of \(^{16}\text{O}\), respectively.
- The Woods-Saxon geometry for the \(\alpha\) binding potential in the 2-channel CRC used \(R = 4.148\) fm and \(a = 0.55\) fm; for the multichannel CRC, \(R = 3.683\) fm and \(a = 0.55\) fm.
- The optical model fit at \(E_{\text{lab}} = 115.9\) MeV required a volume Woods-Saxon imaginary potential with diffuseness \(a_V \approx 0.08\) fm to reproduce the backward-angle oscillation, but this is unphysical; the CRC approach uses \(a_V = 0.60\) fm at this energy.

## Context

This work extends the earlier CRC analysis by Szilner et al. (2002) that considered only direct \(\alpha\) transfer with Woods-Saxon potentials, and the DWBA studies by Morlock et al. (2011) and Hamada et al. (2011), by including indirect \(\alpha\) transfers via excited states and using double-folded potentials. It contradicts the interpretation of Ohkubo and Hirabayashi (2014), who attributed the backward-angle oscillation to nuclear "ripples" from an external reflective wave in a coupled-channel calculation with artificially small diffuseness. The present results enable the use of elastic \(\alpha\) transfer data to extract spectroscopic information on cluster dissociation of \(^{16}\text{O}\), and demonstrate that the large-scale shell-model \(S_\alpha\) values can describe the data when indirect transfers are included.
