# Top-quark pair production cross sections at NNLO+NNLL in pPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 8.16 TeV

> Total and fiducial top pair ($t\bar{t}$) production cross sections in proton-lead (pPb) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 8.16 TeV are computed at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) accuracy including next-to-next-to-leading-log (NNLL) gluon resummation, using the CT14 and CT10 proton parton distribution functions (PDF), and the EPPS16 and EPS09 nuclear PDF parametrizations for the lead ion. The total cross sections amount to $σ(pPb\to t\bar{t}+X) = 59.0 \pm 5.3$(CT14+EPPS16)$\,^{+1.6}_{-2.1}$(scale) nb, and $57.5 \pm \,^{+4.3}_{-3.3}$(CT10+EPS09)$\,^{+1.5}_{-2.0}$(scale) nb, with small modifications with respect to the result computed using the free proton PDF alone. The normalized ratio of pPb to pp cross sections (nuclear modification factor) is $R_{pPb} = 1.04 \,^{\pm 0.07(EPPS16)}_{\pm0.03(EPS09)}$. In the lepton+jets decay mode, $t\bar{t} \to b\bar{b} W(\ellν) W(q\bar{q}')$, one expects $\sim$600 $t\bar{t}$ events in the 180 nb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity collected in pPb collisions at the LHC so far, after typical acceptance and efficiency losses. Differential cross sections at NLO accuracy are presented as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity of the top quarks, and of their decay b-jets and isolated leptons.

## Key claim

Total top-quark pair production cross sections in pPb collisions at 8.16 TeV are computed at NNLO+NNLL accuracy and show only a small (4%) net antishadowing enhancement relative to scaled pp results, with the nuclear modification factor R_pPb = 1.04, but differential distributions in rapidity and transverse momentum exhibit larger 5-10% modifications sensitive to the nuclear gluon density.

## Method

The total and differential cross sections were computed at NLO accuracy using the MCFM v8.0 code with CT10 and CT14 proton PDFs and EPS09 and EPPS16 nuclear PDF parametrizations for the lead ion. A K-factor of approximately 1.20 was computed with Top++ v2.0 at NNLO+NNLL accuracy using the proton PDFs alone, then applied to scale the NLO results to NNLO+NNLL accuracy; the nucleon-nucleon cross sections were then scaled by the lead mass number A=208 to obtain pPb cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Total pPb cross section with CT14+EPPS16: 59.0 ± 5.3 (PDF) +1.6/-2.1 (scale) nb
- Total pPb cross section with CT10+EPS09: 57.5 +4.3/-3.3 (PDF) +1.5/-2.0 (scale) nb
- Nuclear modification factor R_pPb = 1.04 +0.07/-0.03 (EPPS16/EPS09)
- Expected ~600 t-tbar events in the lepton+jets channel for 180 nb^-1 integrated luminosity at the LHC
- Fiducial cross section in lepton+jets channel with CT14+EPPS16: 6.82 ± 0.61 (PDF) +0.18/-0.24 (scale) nb
- K-factor from NLO to NNLO+NNLL: approximately 1.20
- Acceptance of fiducial cuts: 39.5% of total cross section

## Context

This work extends previous studies of top-quark pair production in nuclear collisions (d'Enterria, 2015) by providing NNLO+NNLL predictions for pPb at 8.16 TeV using the updated EPPS16 nuclear PDF set, complementing similar studies with electroweak bosons (Khachatryan, 2015; Aad, 2015; Alice, 2016) that are more sensitive to quark densities at smaller Bjorken-x values. The differential cross section predictions enable future measurements to constrain nuclear gluon densities in the high-x region (x > 0.05) where antishadowing and EMC effects are expected, and provide a baseline for heavy-quark energy loss studies in quark-gluon plasma formed in nucleus-nucleus collisions (Apolinario, 2017).
