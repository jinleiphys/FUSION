# A New Hadron Spectroscopy

> QCD-motivated models for hadrons predict an assortment of "exotic" hadrons that have structures that are more complex than the quark-antiquark mesons and three-quark baryons of the original quark-parton model. These include pentaquark baryons, the six-quark H-dibaryon, and tetraquark, hybrid and glueball mesons. Despite extensive experimental searches, no unambiguous candidates for any of these exotic configurations have been identified. On the other hand, a number of meson states, one that seems to be a proton-antiproton bound state, and others that contain either charmed-anticharmed quark pairs or bottom-antibottom quark pairs, have been recently discovered that neither fit into the quark-antiquark meson picture nor match the expected properties of the QCD-inspired exotics. Here I briefly review results from a recent search for the H-dibaryon, and discuss some properties of the newly discovered states --the proton-antiproton state and the so-called XYZ mesons-- and compare them with expectations for conventional quark-antiquark mesons and the predicted QCD-exotic states.

## Key claim

Despite strong theoretical motivation from QCD-inspired models, no unambiguous exotic hadrons (pentaquarks, H-dibaryons, tetraquarks, hybrids, or glueballs) have been identified; however, a number of recently discovered meson states and a proton-antiproton bound-state candidate do not fit the conventional quark-antiquark meson picture nor match the expected properties of the predicted QCD exotics.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the Belle, BaBar, BESII, and BESIII collaborations, including a search for the H-dibaryon in inclusive Υ(1S) and Υ(2S) decays using Λpπ⁻ and ΛΛ invariant mass distributions, and analyses of radiative J/ψ decays to p̅p and ππη′ final states. The review also covers measurements of XYZ mesons from e⁺e⁻ annihilation, initial-state-radiation processes, and B meson decays, with comparisons to charmonium and bottomonium potential-model expectations.

## Key numbers

- Upper limits on inclusive H-dibaryon branching ratios from Belle are at least a factor of twenty below the antideuteron rate over the entire |M_H − 2m_Λ| < 30 MeV mass interval
- BESII fit to the p̅p mass threshold enhancement in J/ψ → γp̅p gave a peak mass of 1859⁺⁶₋₂₇ MeV, about 18 MeV below 2m_p, with width Γ < 30 MeV; after final-state-interaction corrections, the peak mass shifted to 1831 ± 7 MeV with Γ < 153 MeV
- BESII study of J/ψ → γπη′ found a peak at 1834 ± 7 MeV with width 68 ± 21 MeV
- BESIII confirmed the p̅p threshold enhancement with significance >30σ and established J^PC = 0⁻⁺ via partial wave analysis; the ππη′ peak width was measured as 190 ± 38 MeV versus Γ_p̅p < 76 MeV
- X(3940) mass = 3943 ± 6 MeV, Γ ≤ 52 MeV (from inclusive e⁺e⁻ → J/ψ X); in e⁺e⁻ → J/ψ D D̅* decays, mass = 3942 ± 9 MeV, Γ = 37⁺²⁷₋₁₇ MeV
- X(4160) mass = 4156 ± 27 MeV, Γ = 139⁺¹¹³₋₆₅ MeV (from e⁺e⁻ → J/ψ D* D̅*)
- Z(4430) mass = 4433 ± 5 MeV, Γ = 45⁺³⁵₋₁₈ MeV, statistical significance 6.5σ (Belle, B → K π ψ′ decays)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing search for QCD-motivated exotic hadrons, following the non-confirmation of the Θ₅⁺ pentaquark reported by LEPS (2003) and the constraints on the H-dibaryon from double-Λ hypernuclei (the "Nagara" event). The paper contrasts the absence of these predicted exotics with the accumulating evidence for unexpected states such as the baryonium candidate near 1835 MeV and the XYZ mesons, which were first observed starting in 2003. These results challenge conventional quarkonium potential models and motivate further experimental searches and theoretical interpretations including molecular, tetraquark, hybrid, and hadrocharmonium models.
