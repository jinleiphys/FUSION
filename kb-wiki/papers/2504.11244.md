# Multireference covariant density functional theory for shape coexistence and isomerism in $^{43}$S

> We extend the multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) to describe the low-lying states of the odd-mass nucleus $^{43}$S near the neutron magic number $N=28$ with shape coexistence. The wave functions of the low-lying states are constructed as superpositions of configurations with different intrinsic shapes and $K$ quantum numbers, projected onto good particle numbers and angular momenta. The MR-CDFT successfully reproduces the main features of the low-energy structure in $^{43}$S. Our results indicate that the ground state, $3/2^-_1$, is predominantly composed of the intruder prolate one-quasiparticle (1qp) configuration $\nu1/2^-[321]$. In contrast, the $7/2^-_1$ state is identified as a high-$K$ isomer, primarily built on the prolate 1qp configuration $\nu7/2^-[303]$. Additionally, the $3/2^-_2$ state is found to be an admixture dominated by an oblate configuration with $K^π= 1/2^-$, along with a small contribution from a prolate configuration with $K^π= 3/2^-$. These results demonstrate the capability of MR-CDFT to capture the intricate interplay among shape coexistence, $K$-mixing, and isomerism in the low-energy structure of odd-mass nuclei around $N = 28$.

# Multireference covariant density functional theory for shape coexistence and isomerism in $^{43}$S

## Key claim
The multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT), extended to include both $K$-mixing and shape-mixing effects, reproduces the main features of the low-energy structure of $^{43}$S, but the excitation energy of the isomeric $7/2^-_1$ state remains somewhat overestimated, likely due to the omission of triaxial deformation effects.

## Method
The wave functions of low-lying states in $^{43}$S were constructed as superpositions of one-quasiparticle (1qp) configurations with different intrinsic shapes (quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$) and $K$ quantum numbers, projected onto good particle numbers and angular momenta. The generator coordinate method (GCM) was used to mix these configurations, with the PC-PK1 parameterization employed for the relativistic energy density functional and pairing correlations treated within the BCS approximation using a density-independent $\delta$ force.

## Key numbers
- The ground state $3/2^-_1$ is predominantly composed of the intruder prolate 1qp configuration $\nu1/2^-[321]$ with $\beta_2 \simeq 0.3$.
- The $7/2^-_1$ isomeric state is primarily built on the prolate 1qp configuration $\nu7/2^-[303]$ with $\beta_2 \simeq 0.22$ and $K^\pi = 7/2^-$, with a predicted $B(E2; 7/2^-_1 \to 3/2^-_1) = 0.24$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental value: 0.41 $e^2$fm$^4$).
- The MR-CDFT predicts $B(E2; 1/2^-_1 \to 3/2^-_1) = 144$ $e^2$fm$^4$ and $B(M1; 1/2^-_1 \to 3/2^-_1) = 0.45$ $\mu_N^2$ (experimental $B(M1)$: 1.2(3) $\mu_N^2$).
- The predicted $B(E2; 7/2^-_2 \to 3/2^-_1) = 59$ $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 46(9) $e^2$fm$^4$).
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the $7/2^-_1$ isomer is predicted as $Q_s = 20.9$ $e$fm$^2$ (experimental: 23(3) $e$fm$^2$), and the magnetic dipole moment as $\mu = -0.93$ $\mu_N$ (experimental: $-1.110(14)$ $\mu_N$).
- The $3/2^-_2$ state is dominated by an oblate configuration with $\beta_2 \simeq -0.3$ and $K^\pi = 1/2^-$, with a small admixture of a prolate configuration with $K^\pi = 3/2^-$.

## Context
This work extends the MR-CDFT framework, previously applied to even-even nuclei (Yao 2014, Yao 2015, Zhou 2023), to odd-mass nuclei by incorporating both $K$-mixing and shape-mixing effects. It addresses the low-energy structure of $^{43}$S near the $N=28$ shell gap, where previous studies using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics plus GCM (Kimura 2013), shell-model calculations (Chevrier 2014, Utsuno 2014), and experimental measurements (Gaudefroy 2009, Chevrier 2012, Longfellow 2020) have indicated shape coexistence and isomerism. The results enable further investigation of triaxial deformation effects in odd-mass nuclei around $N=28$, with an extension to incorporate triaxiality currently in progress.
