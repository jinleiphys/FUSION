# Simulation of heavy quarkonium equilibration in the quark-gluon plasma

> We simulate the heavy quarkonium equilibration through transport in a static and homogeneous quark-gluon plasma (QGP) box within the semi-classical Boltzmann approach incorporating both the leading-order and next-to-leading-order dissociation and regeneration reactions. The scattering amplitudes involved are taken from perturbative computations based on effective color-electric dipole coupling of the heavy quarkonium with thermal gluons. By coupling the Langevin simulation of single heavy quark diffusion and the Boltzmann transport of the heavy quarkonium in a real-time fashion, we demonstrate how the kinetic and chemical equilibrium of heavy quarkonium with single heavy quarks in the medium is achieved in terms of both the bound state's yields and momentum distributions. The pertinent equilibration time turns out to be comparable to the lifetime of the QGP created in the most central heavy-ion collisions at the LHC energies. The role of the intricate interplay between the open and hidden heavy sector in the process of equilibration is highlighted. This work provides a dynamical way of understanding the phenomenological success of statistical hadronization model for charmonium production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and also paves the way for realistic phenomenological applications to heavy quarkonium transport.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that heavy quarkonium (specifically J/ψ) in a static and homogeneous QGP box reaches both kinetic and chemical equilibrium with single heavy quarks through coupled Langevin and Boltzmann transport, with equilibration times comparable to the QGP lifetime in central LHC heavy-ion collisions, but only when the single heavy quarks themselves are first kinetically thermalized, and the bound state yield approaches a *relative* chemical equilibrium with open heavy quarks as dictated by the statistical balance equation, not absolute chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors simulate J/ψ transport in a static, homogeneous QGP box using a semi-classical Boltzmann equation that incorporates both leading-order (2↔2: Ψ+g ↔ Q+Q̅) and next-to-leading-order (2↔3: Ψ+p ↔ Q+Q̅+p) dissociation and regeneration reactions, with scattering amplitudes computed from perturbative color-electric dipole coupling. They couple this Boltzmann transport to a Langevin simulation of single charm quark diffusion in real time, tracking the evolution of yields and momentum distributions for 10 c-c̅ pairs (resembling central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC) at three fixed temperatures (1.1Tc, 1.3Tc, 1.5Tc).

## Key numbers

- 10 pairs of c and c̅ quarks are used in the simulation, resembling the realistic case in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies.
- The dissociation temperature for ground state charmonium J/ψ is determined to be about 1.6Tc (Tc being the pseudo-critical temperature).
- Simulations are performed at three constant temperatures: T = 1.1Tc, 1.3Tc, and 1.5Tc.
- The spatial smearing parameter σ for the recombination Gaussian is tested in the range σ ∈ [0.15 fm, 0.75 fm] and found not to affect numerical results.

## Context

This work extends prior semi-classical Boltzmann transport studies of heavy quarkonium equilibration (Yao 2017, Yao 2020) that were limited to 2↔2 reactions, and differs from open quantum system approaches (Brambilla 2020, Miura 2022) that handle only one correlated Q-Q̅ pair. By including both LO and NLO reactions with realistic amplitudes from the authors' previous work (Chen 2017, Zhao 2024), and coupling to Langevin single-quark diffusion, this study provides a dynamical foundation for the phenomenological success of the statistical hadronization model (Andronic 2017, 2021, 2025) for charmonium production, and enables future realistic phenomenological applications to heavy quarkonium transport in expanding QGP backgrounds.
