# Lattice QCD study of $πΣ-\bar{K}N$ scattering and the $Λ(1405)$ resonance

> A lattice QCD computation of the coupled channel $πΣ-\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes in the $Λ(1405)$ region is detailed. Results are obtained using a single ensemble of gauge field configurations with $N_{\rm f} = 2+1$ dynamical quark flavors and $m_π \approx 200$ MeV and $m_K\approx487$ MeV. Hermitian correlation matrices using both single baryon and meson-baryon interpolating operators for a variety of different total momenta and irreducible representations are used. Several parametrizations of the two-channel scattering $K$-matrix are utilized to obtain the scattering amplitudes from the finite-volume spectrum. The amplitudes, continued to the complex energy plane, exhibit a virtual bound state below the $πΣ$ threshold and a resonance pole just below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD study finds a virtual bound state below the πΣ threshold and a resonance pole just below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold in the coupled-channel scattering amplitudes, with pole positions that vary little across different fit parametrizations and are broadly consistent with chiral effective theory predictions, though the analysis is performed at a single lattice ensemble with unphysical quark masses (mπ ≈ 200 MeV, mK ≈ 487 MeV).

## Method

The authors computed finite-volume stationary-state energies from Hermitian correlation matrices using single-baryon and meson-baryon interpolating operators on a single CLS D200 ensemble with Nf = 2+1 dynamical quark flavors and a lattice spacing a = 0.0633 fm. They extracted energies via generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) diagonalization and multiple fit forms, then constrained coupled-channel πΣ–\(\bar{K}N\) scattering amplitudes using the Lüscher quantization condition with several parametrizations of the K-matrix (effective range expansions, inverse-K expansions, and Blatt-Biedenharn forms) truncated to ℓmax = 0 and ℓmax = 1 partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 200 MeV (specifically amπ = 0.06533(25), corresponding to mπ ≈ 200 MeV)
- Kaon mass: mK ≈ 487 MeV (amK = 0.15602(16))
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0633(4)(6) fm
- Spatial extent: mπL = 4.181(16)
- Nucleon mass: amN = 0.3143(37)
- Σ mass: amΣ = 0.3830(19)
- Λ mass: amΛ = 0.3634(14)
- Pion decay constant: afπ = 0.04226(13)
- Kaon decay constant: afK = 0.04910(11)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the Λ(1405) that used only single-baryon three-quark interpolating fields (Gubler 2016, Menadue 2011, Engel 2012, Hall 2014) and did not compute scattering amplitudes, as well as earlier quenched calculations of the \(\bar{K}N\) scattering length (Fukugita 1994) that neglected πΣ mixing. It is the first coupled-channel scattering study in lattice QCD to include baryons, following similar techniques applied to meson systems (Wilson 2015, Moir 2016, Briceno 2017, Woss 2019) and to nucleon-pion scattering in the Δ-resonance region (Bulava 2022). The results enable future lattice studies at physical quark masses and with multiple lattice volumes to resolve the two-pole versus single-pole debate for the Λ(1405) region.
