# Neutron interferometry and tests of short-range modifications of gravity

> We consider tests of short-distance modifications of gravity based on neutron interferometry in the scenario of large extra dimensions. Avoiding the non-computability problem in the calculation of the internal gravitational potential of extended sources, typical of models with zero-width brane, we determine the neutron optical potential associated with the higher-dimension gravitational interaction between the incident neutron and a material medium in the context of thick brane theories. Proceeding this way, we identify the physical quantity of the extra dimension model that the neutron interferometry is capable of constraining. We also consider interferometric experiments in which the phase shifter is an electric field, as in the test of the Aharanov-Casher effect. We argue that this experiment, with this non-baryonic source, can be viewed as a test of the short-range behavior of Post-Newtonian parameters that measure the capacity of the pressure and the internal energy for producing gravity.

## Key claim

Neutron interferometry can constrain short-range modifications of gravity from large extra dimensions, but only if a thick brane model is used to avoid the non-computability problem of the internal gravitational potential that arises in zero-width brane models with delta greater than or equal to 2. The authors argue that the Aharonov-Casher experiment, using an electric field as a non-baryonic phase shifter, can provide constraints on short-distance deviations of Post-Newtonian parameters that are independent of nuclear models.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutron optical potential associated with higher-dimensional gravitational interactions in a thick brane scenario, where the brane has a finite thickness described by a regular distribution function f(z). For baryonic sources, they determine the external contribution to the forward scattering length from the anomalous gravitational interaction between an incident neutron and atomic nuclei in a material medium. For non-baryonic sources, they compute the gravitational potential produced by the electric field inside a capacitor in the Aharonov-Casher experiment and derive the resulting phase shift on the neutron beam, expressing the constraints in terms of Yukawa parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Aharonov-Casher experiment used a field strength of E = 30 kV/mm, capacitor length L = 2.53 cm, and neutron wavelength lambda_n = 1.477 Angstrom, with a measured phase shift Phi_AC = 1.50 mrad and experimental error eta of order 10^-3 rad
- The upper limit on the Yukawa parameter from neutron interferometry is alpha*lambda^2 less than 0.26 x 10^20 m^2, valid for lambda less than 10^-4 m
- Neutron interferometry provides the strongest upper limits for amplifications of the Post-Newtonian gamma-parameter in the range between 1.4 x 10^-7 m and 10^-4 m

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of neutron interferometry tests of gravity (Frank, Pokotilovski, Greene, Snow) by addressing the divergence problems in zero-width brane models (ADD model) that prevented calculation of internal gravitational potentials for delta greater than or equal to 2. The authors build on thick brane approaches (Rubakov, Kaplan) previously applied to hydrogen-like atom spectroscopy (Dahia, Dahia2). The results enable comparison with constraints from the MTV-G experiment (based on electron spin precession) and hydrogen spectroscopy of 2P_1/2-2P_3/2 transitions (Dahia4), and can be interpreted as limits on short-distance deviations of the Post-Newtonian parameters beta_3 and beta_4 (following the notation of Misner, Thorne, and Wheeler).
