# A False Vacuum Skyrme Model for Nuclear Matter

> The low energy regime of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) presents enormous challenges due to its large coupling. Effective field theories, like the Skyrme model, are useful approaches to study properties of strong interaction at hadronic scales. We propose a Skyrme-type model with a self-dual sector and that treats the density of the baryonic charge as a self-interacting fluid. The dynamics reduces to Coleman's false vacuum problem for a scalar field that is a fractional power of that density. The main result is that such a Skyrme-type model is the first one to reproduce, with good accuracy, the experimental values of radii and binding energies for a very wide range of the mass number. The robust and simple properties of the model lead to many possible generalizations with implications not only in nuclear physics but also in other areas of Physics.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Skyrme-type model with a self-dual sector coupled to a self-interacting fluid theory, claiming it is the first Skyrme-type model to reproduce experimental values of radii and binding energies with good accuracy for a very wide range of mass numbers (from 1 to 240), though the model describes heavier nuclei better than light ones, with errors growing to two-digit percentages for baryonic charges below 10.

## Method

The model combines a modified Skyrme model with a self-dual sector (using a symmetric matrix h of scalar fields) with a fluid theory where the order parameter ψ is a fractional power of the baryonic charge density. The dynamics reduces to Coleman's false vacuum problem for a scalar field, yielding spherically symmetric bounce solutions. The Euler-Lagrange equations for the SU(2) Skyrme fields reduce to a single second-order partial differential equation for ψ, with an integration constant c that plays a role analogous to a running coupling constant, fixed by choosing a reference nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon reproduced with errors smaller than 1% for baryonic charges running from 20 to 240
- Errors smaller than 1% for baryonic charges above 60
- Errors between 10 and 20: not higher than 5%
- Errors for baryonic charges below 10: two-digit percentage
- Model tested against a list of N_c = 265 nuclei, containing all stable nuclei up to 208Pb and nuclei up to 240Pu with half-life greater than 10^3 years
- The nuclear matter density fall-off length scale a is approximately 0.524 Fermi
- The parameter s in the effective potential satisfies 2 < s < 6

## Context

This work extends the self-dual Skyrme model proposed by Ferreira (2017) and further explored by the same authors, which modified the original Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961, 1962) by contracting group indices with a symmetric matrix h of scalar fields. The model addresses the longstanding problem of excessive binding energies in Skyrmion solutions (Sutcliffe, 2007; Manton, 2004) and builds on attempts to introduce self-dual sectors (Adam, 2010, 2013) that improved binding energies for heavy nuclei and neutron stars. The reduction to Coleman's false vacuum problem (Coleman, 1977, 1985) provides a robust mechanism for the observed Q^(1/3) scaling of nuclear radii and the saturation of binding energy per nucleon. The approach enables future work on breaking self-duality through kinetic and potential terms for the h-fields, potentially connecting to results from the original Skyrme model.
