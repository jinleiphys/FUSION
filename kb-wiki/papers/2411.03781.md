# Speed of sound and trace anomaly in a unified treatment of the two-color diquark superfluid, the pion-condensed high-isospin matter, and the 2SC quark matter

> In a unified perturbative treatment from the high-density side, we compute the speed of sound and the trace anomaly as functions of the chemical potential $μ$ for the two-color diquark superfluid, the pion-condensed high-isospin matter, and the 2SC quark matter. We find that the corrections induced by the gap energy $Δ$ involve nontrivial interplay between the dimensionless magnitude $|Δ/μ|$ and the derivative $|\partialΔ/\partialμ|$. Even though the gap equation has a common structure for these phases of our interest, different numerical constants cause drastic changes in the speed of sound corrections. As long as $|Δ/μ|$ is dominant over the derivative, the gap effects increase the speed of sound, which is consistent with the expected behavior of exhibiting a peak at intermediate density. We then discuss the trace anomaly which is pushed down generally by the gap effects and turns out to be negative widely in the high density regime. For demonstration of non-perturbative enhancement, we take account of the instanton-induced interaction. We confirm a further increase in the speed of sound for the two-color diquark superfluid and the pion-condensed high-isospin matter, while the speed of sound for the 2SC quark matter deviates far below the conformal limit due to the derivative contribution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that gap corrections to the speed of sound and trace anomaly depend on a nontrivial interplay between the dimensionless gap magnitude \(|\Delta/\mu|\) and its derivative \(|\partial\Delta/\partial\mu|\); the speed of sound can exceed the conformal limit only when \(|\Delta/\mu|\) dominates over the derivative, while the trace anomaly is generally pushed downward and can become negative even when the speed of sound correction is minor.

## Method

The authors perform a unified perturbative calculation of the grand potential using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism, truncating the 2-particle-irreducible diagrams at the one-gluon-exchange (sun-set) level and later including instanton-induced interactions. They numerically solve the one-loop gap equation for the three systems (two-color diquark superfluid, pion-condensed high-isospin matter, and 2SC quark matter) without the approximations used in earlier analytical solutions, and then compute the pressure, speed of sound, and trace anomaly from the thermodynamic derivatives of the grand potential.

## Key numbers

* For the two-color diquark superfluid, the speed of sound exceeds the conformal limit (\(c_s^2 > 1/3\)) in the intermediate density region when gap effects are included; the numerical gap \(\Delta_{\mathrm{2c}}\) is larger than the analytical approximation by a factor at \(\mu \lesssim 10\;\mathrm{GeV}\).
* For the pion-condensed high-isospin matter, the gap \(\Delta_\pi\) is larger than \(\Delta_{\mathrm{2c}}\) by an order of magnitude at the same chemical potential, causing a drastic rise in \(c_s^2\) even at \(\mu_{\mathrm{I}} \gtrsim 2\;\mathrm{GeV}\).
* For the 2SC quark matter, the gap correction to \(c_s^2\) is minor and becomes negative at low density; the trace anomaly \(\mathfrak{T}\) can become negative around \(\mu_q \simeq 1\;\mathrm{GeV}\) due to \(\Delta_{\mathrm{2SC}}\) effects, with a large uncertainty band for renormalization scale choices \(X = 1, 2, 4\) where \(\bar{\Lambda} = X\mu\).
* The hierarchy of zero-energy gaps is \(\Delta_\pi > \Delta_{\mathrm{2c}} \gg \Delta_{\mathrm{2SC}}\) at asymptotically high density.

## Context

This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state (Freedman and McLerran, 1976; Kurkela et al., 2009) by including gap effects from diquark and pion condensates, updating earlier treatments (Alford et al., 2007; Kurkela et al., 2009) that used analytical gap approximations. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations of two-color QCD (Iida et al., 2022, 2024) and high-isospin matter (Brandt et al., 2022; Abbott et al., 2023), which show \(c_s^2\) exceeding the conformal limit and negative trace anomalies. The 2SC results provide a baseline for interpreting neutron star observations and suggest that non-perturbative mechanisms (such as instanton-induced interactions) may be needed to enhance the gap and produce a speed-of-sound peak at intermediate densities.
