# Effects of a background magnetic field on dilepton radiation

> We explore the feasibility of using dilepton radiations as a QGP magnetometer. We calculate the dilepton production rate in the presence of a time-dependent magnetic field typically found in heavy-ion collisions. We compute the thermal dilepton spectra from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s$ = 19.6 GeV BES energy -- using a realistic (3+1)-dimensional multistage hydrodynamic simulation. We find the thermal dilepton elliptic flow to be very sensitive to the strength of the magnetic field.

# Effects of a background magnetic field on dilepton radiation

## Key claim
The thermal dilepton elliptic flow is very sensitive to the strength of the magnetic field at the beginning of the hydrodynamic phase, but the invariant mass spectrum shows no distinguishable difference for magnetic field strengths in the range of 0.1 to 1 pion mass squared. The elliptic flow enhancement is also insensitive to the lifetime of the magnetic field, making it a potential probe of the initial magnetic field strength rather than its decay rate.

## Method
The authors calculated the leading-order electromagnetic-field-induced correction to the dilepton production rate by solving the Boltzmann equation with relaxation-time approximation, obtaining a linear-order correction proportional to the in-fluid electric field. They then combined this rate with a (3+1)-dimensional multistage hydrodynamic simulation of Au+Au collisions at 19.6 GeV, using a decaying magnetic field profile with a Gaussian smearing function and an induced electric field from Faraday's law, to compute thermal dilepton spectra and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s) = 19.6 GeV (BES energy at RHIC)
- Magnetic field strength range considered: eB ~ (0.1-1) m_pi^2 at the starting time of hydrodynamic evolution
- Magnetic field lifetime parameter tau_B = 0.5 fm (fixed in main comparison)
- Electric conductivity parametrization: sigma_el = T (fixed)
- Elliptic flow enhancement observed in invariant mass range: 0.5 GeV less than or equal to M less than or equal to 1.5 GeV
- Magnetic field strength at hydrodynamic start: eB_h = 0.5 m_pi^2 (used in lifetime comparison)

## Context
This work extends the study of electromagnetic probes as a tool for extracting QGP properties, following prior calculations of dilepton production rates by Churchill (2023) and electric conductivity effects by Rapp (2024). The authors note that previous Biot-Savart law estimations give eB ~ (1-10) m_pi^2 for off-central heavy-ion collisions, but measurements of Lambda hyperon polarization by STAR (2023) found the late-stage magnetic field to be at least one order of magnitude lower. This work enables the use of dilepton elliptic flow as a quantitative magnetometer for the QGP magnetic field during the hydrodynamic phase, complementing existing approaches that rely on chiral magnetic effect signatures.
