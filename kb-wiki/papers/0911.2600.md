# Strange mesons from SIS to FAIR

> The properties of $K$ and $\bar K$ mesons in nuclear matter at finite temperature are obtained from a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels which incorporates the $s$- and p-waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction. The in-medium solution accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on all the baryons involved, and $π$ and kaon self-energies. The $\bar K$ spectral function spreads over a wide range of energies, reflecting the melting of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the contribution of hyperon-hole components at finite temperature. In the $KN$ sector, the quasi-particle peak is considerably broadened with increasing density and temperature. We also study the energy weighted sum rules of the kaon propagator by matching the Dyson form of the propagator with its spectral Lehmann representation at low and high energies. The sum rules for the lower energy weights are fulfilled satisfactorily and reflect the contributions from the different quasi-particle and collective modes of the spectral function. We analyze the sensitivity of the sum rules to the distribution of spectral strength and their usefulness as quality tests of model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that energy-weighted sum rules (EWSRs) for the kaon propagator are fulfilled satisfactorily for lower energy weights, but only when the in-medium chiral unitary approach includes both s-wave and p-wave contributions to the kaon-nucleon interaction, with the fulfillment serving as a quality test of the model calculations.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energies of K and anti-K mesons in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature using a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels, incorporating both s-wave (from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and p-wave (from hyperon-hole excitations) contributions to the kaon-nucleon interaction. The in-medium solution accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on nucleons and hyperons via a sigma-omega model, and the dressing of pion and kaon propagators, with the self-energy obtained self-consistently by summing the transition amplitude over the nucleon Fermi distribution. The energy-weighted sum rules are derived by matching the Dyson form of the meson propagator with its spectral Lehmann representation at low and high energies.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- The anti-K potential from unitarized chiral dynamics and meson-exchange models gives attraction of order -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density
- Recent few-body calculations predict few-nucleon kaonic states bound by 50-80 MeV with widths of order 100 MeV
- The m_0^{(+)} sum rule saturates to 1 independently of meson momentum, nuclear density, or temperature
- Results are shown for meson momentum q = 150 MeV/c at density rho = rho_0 and temperature T = 0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral unitary approaches to kaon-nucleon interactions in nuclear matter (Ramos 1999, Tolos 2001, Tolos 2002) by incorporating p-wave contributions beyond s-wave and by applying energy-weighted sum rules as a consistency check. The study is relevant for understanding strange meson properties in heavy-ion collisions from SIS energies (Fuchs 2005) to FAIR energies at GSI, and it enables future tests of model calculations by using sum rules to constrain the accuracy of in-medium self-energy calculations.
