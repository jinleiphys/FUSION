# The fluctuations of quadrangular flow

> The ATLAS Collaboration has measured for the first time the fourth cumulant of quadrangular flow, $v_4\{4\}^4$. Unlike the fourth cumulants of elliptic and triangular flows, it presents a change of sign above 30\% centrality. We show that this change of sign is predicted by event-by-event hydrodynamics. We argue that it results from the combined effects of a nonlinear hydrodynamic response, which couples quadrangular flow to elliptic flow, and elliptic flow fluctuations.

# The fluctuations of quadrangular flow

## Key claim

The change of sign of the fourth cumulant of quadrangular flow, $v_4\{4\}^4$, observed by ATLAS above 30% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions, is predicted by event-by-event hydrodynamics and results specifically from the combined effects of a nonlinear hydrodynamic response coupling $v_4$ to $v_2$ and large fluctuations of elliptic flow, rather than from linear response to initial anisotropy in the fourth harmonic.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamic calculations using the v-USPhydro code with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions and a shear viscosity over entropy ratio of $\eta/s=0.08$, computing anisotropic flow coefficients $V_n$ at freeze-out for pions in each event. They decomposed $V_4$ into a linear part $V_{4L}$ and a nonlinear part proportional to $(V_2)^2$, defined the coefficient $\chi_4$ via $\chi_4 = \langle V_4(V_2^*)^2\rangle / \langle |V_2|^4\rangle$, and compared the resulting $v_4\{2\}$, $v_4\{\Psi_2\}$, and $v_4\{4\}^4$ to CMS and ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- $v_4\{4\}^4$ is positive up to 25% centrality and then negative in ATLAS data for charged particles with $0.5 < p_t < 5$ GeV; the hydrodynamic calculation shows the change of sign around 50% centrality.
- The nonlinear response coefficient $v_4\{\Psi_2\}$ satisfies $v_4\{\Psi_2\} < v_4\{2\}$ for all centralities in both CMS data (charged particles, $0.3 < p_t < 3$ GeV) and hydrodynamic calculations (pions, same $p_t$ range).
- The hydrodynamic calculation uses $\eta/s = 0.08$ and matches experimental data on $v_4\{\Psi_2\}$ but overestimates $v_4\{2\}$, indicating overestimation of the linear part $V_{4L}$.

## Context

This work extends the flow paradigm (Luzum, 2011) by addressing the puzzling sign change of $v_4\{4\}^4$ first reported by ATLAS (Aad, 2014), which contrasts with the positive fourth cumulants of elliptic flow $v_2\{4\}^4$ (Aamodt, 2010) and triangular flow $v_3\{4\}^4$ (ALICE, 2011) across all centralities. Prior work established that $v_2$ and $v_3$ are well described by linear response to initial anisotropies (Niemi, 2012; Gardim, 2014), but linear response in the fourth harmonic fails to explain $v_4$ fluctuations (Rybczynski, 2015; Ghosh, 2016). The decomposition of $V_4$ into linear and nonlinear parts (Yan, 2015; Giacalone, 2016) enables this explanation, and the results provide further evidence for fluidlike behavior of the matter created in ultrarelativistic Pb+Pb collisions.
