# Cold Atom Quantum Simulator for Dilute Neutron Matter

> The internal structure of neutron stars and the physical properties of nuclei depend on the equation of state (EOS) of neutron matter. Dilute neutron matter is a quantum system of spin-1/2 Fermi particles interacting via s-wave scattering. Although a nuclear system and an ultracold atomic system have length scales and energy scales that differ by several orders of magnitude, both systems follow a common universal EOS considering their non-dimensional universal interaction parameters. In this study, we determine the EOS of neutron matter in the dilute region, where the influence of the s-wave scattering length is dominant but that of the effective range is small, by utilizing a quantum simulator of ultracold $^6$Li atoms with Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The paper determines the equation of state (EOS) of dilute neutron matter by using ultracold $^6$Li atoms as a quantum simulator, under the condition that the influence of the s-wave scattering length is dominant while that of the effective range is small, which holds only in the dilute density region below approximately $n_c = 1.7 \times 10^{-3} n_0$.

## Method

The authors utilize a quantum simulator of ultracold $^6$Li atoms with Feshbach resonance to experimentally determine the universal EOS of spin-1/2 fermions interacting via s-wave scattering. They measure the dimensionless internal energy density as a function of the dimensionless interaction parameters $1/k_F a_s$ and $k_F r_e$, and then map these results onto neutron matter using the known neutron scattering length $a_s = -18.9(4)$ fm and effective range $r_e = 2.75(11)$ fm. The EOS for neutron matter is obtained by substituting the neutron parameters into the universal EOS function $f_\mathcal{E}(1/k_F a_s, k_F r_e)$.

## Key numbers

- Neutron s-wave scattering length: $a_s = -18.9(4)$ fm
- Neutron effective range: $r_e = 2.75(11)$ fm
- Characteristic wave number where the phase shift is maximal: $k_c = \sqrt{2/|a_s| r_e} = 0.20$ fm$^{-1}$
- Characteristic density where effective range effects become significant: $n_c = 1.7 \times 10^{-3} n_0$, where $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is the nuclear saturation density
- At $n = n_c$: $(-k_F a_s)_c = 3.7$, $(1/k_F a_s)_c = -0.27$, and $(k_F r_e)_c = 0.54$
- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Fermi wave number at saturation density: $k_{F0} = (3\pi^2 n_0)^{1/3} = 1.68$ fm$^{-1}$
- van der Waals length for $^6$Li atoms: $r_0 = 1.7$ nm

## Context

This work extends the universal physics of spin-1/2 fermions (the BCS-BEC crossover framework developed by Leggett (1980), Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink (1985), and others) to neutron matter, which is a key system for understanding neutron star structure and nuclear properties. The approach enables determination of the neutron matter EOS without relying on ab initio calculations that suffer from the fermion sign problem in quantum Monte Carlo methods. The results can be used to test theoretical models of neutron matter and, via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, to generate mass-radius curves for comparison with astronomical observations of neutron stars and gravitational wave data.
