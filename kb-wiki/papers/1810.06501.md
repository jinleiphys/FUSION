# Phenomenology using PARTONS software of Generalized Parton Distribution models built from Light-Front Wave-functions

> We present a procedure aiming at extending to the full kinematic domain a Generalized Parton Distribution obtained from a finite order truncation in Fock space. This method allows to fulfill both polynomiality and positivity at the same time and can be applied to any given models of Light-front wave-functions. In particular, we illustrate this on a three-body truncated wave-function of the chiral quark soliton model and show how a systematic phenomenology of GPD models based on LFWFs can be achieved with the help of the PARTONS framework, here using DVCS data. This paves the way for a unified phenomenology of GPDs and TMDs at the level of LFWFs, with the final goal of hadron tomography.

## Key claim

The paper presents a procedure to extend a Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) obtained from a finite-order truncation in Fock space to the full kinematic domain, fulfilling both polynomiality and positivity simultaneously; the method is illustrated on a three-body truncated wave-function of the chiral quark soliton model, but the authors state that the present goal is only to illustrate the procedure and do not claim accuracy of this specific model against data.

## Method

The method uses the Radon transform representation of GPDs via Double Distributions (DDs): the DGLAP region (|x| >= xi) computed from a finite-order Light-Front Wave-Function (LFWF) overlap is used to numerically invert the Radon transform and extract the DD, which then generates the GPD in the ERBL region (|x| <= xi). The resulting covariantly extended GPDs are fed into the PARTONS framework, which computes Compton Form Factors (CFFs) and DVCS observables through the full chain from GPD models to cross-sections and asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- The chiral quark soliton model is truncated at the first Fock sector (three valence quarks), with a low scale Q0^2 = 0.259 GeV^2.
- The beam-spin asymmetry A_LU is computed at JLab CLAS kinematics: x_B = 0.2415, t = 0.137 GeV^2, Q^2 = 1.946 GeV^2, using data from Pisano (2015).
- The GPD E^u is shown at t = -0.34 GeV^2 with the extension to the ERBL region.

## Context

This work extends the covariant extension method of Chouika (2017) to enable phenomenology of GPD models based on LFWFs, addressing the long-standing problem that finite Fock-space truncations violate polynomiality. It builds on the chiral quark soliton model of Lorce (2011) and uses the PARTONS framework of Berthou (2015) to produce DVCS observables comparable to CLAS data from Pisano (2015). The procedure enables systematic testing of valence-quark models against upcoming JLab 12 GeV data and paves the way for a unified phenomenology of GPDs and TMDs at the level of LFWFs, with the final goal of hadron tomography.
