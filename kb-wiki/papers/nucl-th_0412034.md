# Isoscaling behavior in the Fission Dynamics

> The fission processes of $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{116}$Sn + $^{116}$Sn are simulated with the combination of the Langevin equation and the statistical decay model. The masses of two fission fragments are given by assuming the process of symmetric fission or asymmetric fission by the Monte Carlo sampling with the Gaussian probability distribution. From the analysis to the isotopic/isotonic ratios of the fission fragments from both reactions, the isoscaling behavior has been observed and investigated in details. Isoscaling parameters $α$ and $β$ are extracted as a function of the charge number and neutron number, respectively, in different width of the sampling Gaussian probability distribution. It seems that $α$ is sensitive to the width of fission probability distribution of the mass asymmetrical parameter but $β$ is not. Both $α$ and $β$ drop with the increasing of beam energy and the reduced friction parameter.

## Key claim

Isoscaling behavior is observed in simulated fission fragments from Langevin dynamics, but the isoscaling parameter alpha is sensitive to the width of the Gaussian probability distribution used to sample the mass asymmetry parameter of the fission fragments, while beta is not; both parameters drop with increasing beam energy and reduced friction parameter.

## Method

The fission processes of 112Sn + 112Sn and 116Sn + 116Sn were simulated using a Combined Dynamical and Statistical Model (CDSM) coupling an overdamped Langevin equation with a Monte Carlo procedure for light particle emission. At the scission point, the mass asymmetry parameter of the two fission fragments was sampled from a Gaussian probability distribution with mean zero and variable width, assuming the fragments have the same N/Z ratio as the fissioning nucleus. The isotopic and isotonic yield ratios of fission fragments from the two reactions were then analyzed to extract isoscaling parameters alpha and beta as functions of charge number Z and neutron number N, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy of 8.4 MeV/nucleon used for the main simulations of 112Sn + 112Sn and 116Sn + 116Sn reactions.
- Gaussian width values for the mass asymmetry parameter: sigma_alpha0 = 0.04, 0.06, 0.08, and 0.20.
- Constant reduced friction parameter values tested: beta_0 = 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10 (dimensionless), replacing the one-body dissipation form factor.
- Isoscaling parameters alpha and beta extracted for fragments with Z = 44 to 54 and N = 58 to 68 as a function of beam energy.
- The square of the full width of isotopic and isotonic distributions shows a systematic increase with Z or N, with values approximately independent of sigma_alpha0.

## Context

This work extends the study of isoscaling behavior, previously observed experimentally in evaporation (Tsang et al., 2001), fission (Friedman, 2003; Veselsky, 2004), and multifragmentation, to the specific case of fission dynamics simulated with the Langevin equation. The results enable investigation of how the width of the fission fragment mass distribution and the competition between symmetric and asymmetric fission affect isoscaling parameters, and suggest that isoscaling in fission fragments could serve as a probe of the friction parameter in fission dynamics. The observed increase of alpha with Z for nearly symmetric fission is consistent with predictions from a simple liquid-drop model (Friedman, 2003), while the backbending behavior at intermediate Gaussian widths resembles features seen in neutron-induced fission data of 238,233U targets (Veselsky, 2004).
