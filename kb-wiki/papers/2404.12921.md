# Hydrodynamic Attractor in Ultracold Atoms

> The hydrodynamic attractor is a concept that describes universal equilibration behavior in which systems lose microscopic details before hydrodynamics becomes applicable. We propose a setup to observe hydrodynamic attractors in ultracold atomic gases, taking advantage of the fact that driving the two-body $s$-wave scattering length causes phenomena equivalent to isotropic fluid expansions. We specifically consider two-component fermions with contact interactions in three dimensions and discuss their dynamics under a power-law drive of the scattering length in a uniform system. By explicit computation, we derive a hydrodynamic relaxation model. We analytically solve their dynamics and find the hydrodynamic attractor solution. Our proposed method using the scattering length drive is applicable to a wide range of ultracold atomic systems, and our results establish these as a new platform for exploring hydrodynamic attractors.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that driving the two-body s-wave scattering length in a uniform ultracold Fermi gas creates dynamics equivalent to isotropic fluid expansion, and that this system exhibits a hydrodynamic attractor, universal equilibration behavior that emerges before standard hydrodynamics becomes accurate, provided the drive follows a power-law approach to the unitary limit.

## Method

The authors consider a two-component Fermi gas with contact interactions in three dimensions and derive a hydrodynamic relaxation model (Müller-Israel-Stewart type) for the dissipative pressure deviation π(t), using the Drude form for the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity ζ(ω). They analytically solve this equation for a power-law drive of the scattering length a(t) that asymptotically approaches the unitary limit, obtaining an exact solution that separates into an attractor part π_att(t) and an exponentially decaying non-hydrodynamic mode.

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscous relaxation time near unitarity is predicted as τ_ζ ∼ 0.7 ħ/k_B T ∼ 0.15 ms for a ⁴⁰K Fermi gas with Fermi energy E_F ∼ h × 20 kHz at T/T_F = 0.25.
- For the power-law protocol with α = 1/2 and 1/k_F ã = 0.5, the maximum rate of change of 1/k_F a is ∼ 4 kHz, within experimental capabilities near the narrow Feshbach resonance at 202 G.
- The attractor is expected to occur in the time window t ∼ (5–10) × τ_ζ ∼ 0.75–1.5 ms, where the contact has already been measured with 0.1 ms time resolution.

## Context

This work extends the concept of hydrodynamic attractors, previously studied in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and holography (Heller 2015, Romatschke 2018, Kurkela 2019), to ultracold atomic systems. It builds on measurements of relaxation times in strongly interacting Fermi gases (Patel 2020, Li 2022, Li 2024) and on the dynamic sweep theorem (Tan 2008) relating scattering length changes to energy production. The proposed protocol enables direct observation of attractor dynamics in a controlled, uniform system without fluid velocities, analogous to relativistic Hubble expansion (Du 2021), and opens a new platform for studying non-equilibrium universality in cold atoms.
