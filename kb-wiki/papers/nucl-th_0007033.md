# Critical Review Of Quark Gluon Plasma Signals

> Compelling evidence for a new form of matter has been claimed to be formed in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS. We critically review two suggested signatures for this new state of matter: First the suppression of the J/$Ψ$, which should be strongly suppressed in the QGP by two different mechanisms, the color-screening and the QCD-photoeffect. Secondly the measured particle, in particular strange hadronic, ratios might signal the freeze-out from a quark-gluon phase.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed J/ψ suppression and strange hadronic particle ratios in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS do not necessarily signal quark-gluon plasma formation, because conventional hadronic mechanisms (comoving meson absorption, charmonium formation length effects, and in-medium mass shifts from chiral symmetry restoration) can reproduce the data without requiring a deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors calculate J/ψ–nucleon cross sections using a nonperturbative QCD interpolation formula based on the transverse size of charmonium states, with wave functions from a non-relativistic charmonium model. They then compute J/ψ suppression in pA and Pb+Pb collisions using the UrQMD transport model, including formation time effects and the expansion of small wave packages. For particle ratios, they compare ideal gas thermal model fits to results from a chiral SU(3) model that includes dropping hadronic masses and effective chemical potentials due to strong scalar and vector fields, fitting to experimental ratios from Pb+Pb collisions at SPS.

## Key numbers

- σ(J/ψ–N) = 3.62 mb, σ(ψ′–N) = 20.0 mb, σ(χc10–N) = 6.82 mb, σ(χc11–N) = 15.9 mb (total quarkonium-nucleon cross sections from nonperturbative calculation)
- σ_abs(J/ψ–N) = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb from SLAC data at E_inc ~ 20 GeV
- Formation length l_f ≈ 3 fm for J/ψ and χ at SPS midrapidity, l_f ≈ 6 fm for ψ′
- Best-fit freeze-out parameters from chiral model: T = 144 MeV, μ_q ≈ 95 MeV (compared to ideal gas fit T = 168 MeV, μ_q = 88.67 MeV from Braun-Munzinger (1999))
- χ²_chiral = 26.5 for chiral model fit, χ²_ig = 13 for ideal gas fit (Braun-Munzinger 1999), χ²_ig-FFM = 21.6 for ideal gas with same feeding procedures
- Chiral model predicts chiral phase transition at T ≈ 150 MeV; no agreement with SPS data for T > T_c

## Context

This work critically examines the interpretation of J/ψ suppression and strange particle ratios as QGP signals, extending the analysis of Kharzeev (1996) and Hufner (1997) on charmonium absorption, and Braun-Munzinger (1999) on thermal fits. The authors show that a chiral SU(3) model with in-medium mass changes (constrained by chiral symmetry) can fit SPS particle ratios only for T < 150 MeV, contradicting claims that the data require freeze-out from a deconfined phase. The paper enables future work at RHIC and LHC by predicting color filtering effects in bottomium states as contrast signals to J/ψ.
