# Flow anisotropy due to momentum deposition in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> Minijets and jets are produced in large numbers in nuclear collisions at TeV energies, so that there are many of them in a single fireball. They deposit non-negligible amount of momentum and energy into the hydrodynamically expanding bulk and cause anisotropies of the expansion. Moreover, due to their multiple production in a single event the resulting anisotropies are correlated with the collision geometry and thus contribute positively also to event-averaged anisotropies in non-central collisions. Using simulations with three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model we demonstrate the importance of this effect. It must be taken into account if conclusions about the properties of the hot matter are to be drawn.

# Flow anisotropy due to momentum deposition in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim
Minijets and jets deposit enough momentum and energy into the hydrodynamically expanding bulk in TeV-energy nuclear collisions to cause significant flow anisotropies, and these anisotropies are correlated with collision geometry so that they contribute positively to event-averaged elliptic flow in non-central collisions. The effect must be taken into account if conclusions about the properties of the hot matter are to be drawn, but the authors use smooth initial conditions (optical Glauber model) to isolate the novel mechanism, acknowledging this is an "obsolete solution" chosen to make evaluation of the jet-driven effect easier.

## Method
The authors constructed a three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with a source term in the energy-momentum conservation equation, using the SHASTA algorithm to handle large gradients and shocks, and the Cooper-Frye freeze-out handled via the THERMINATOR2 package. They simulated Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV with smooth initial energy density profiles from the optical Glauber model, and implemented energy and momentum deposition from about 10 average dijet pairs per event (with p_t above 3 GeV/c) using a scaling where energy loss dE/dx scales with entropy density of the medium, with the scale parameter dE/dx|_0 varied to investigate its influence.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density in the central point of the fireball for vanishing impact parameter: 60 GeV/fm^3
- Hydrodynamic starting time: tau_0 = 0.55 fm/c
- Average number of dijet pairs per central Pb+Pb collision at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV: about 10 pairs with p_t above 3 GeV/c
- Parameters for the jet transverse momentum distribution: B = 14.7 mb/GeV, p_0 = 6 GeV, n = 9.5
- Energy loss scale dE/dx|_0 set to 4 GeV/fm for the main results
- Smearing width for energy and momentum deposition: 0.3 fm (varying to 0.15 fm and 0.6 fm did not influence results much)
- In non-central collisions (centrality class 20-30%), energy and momentum deposition from jets enhanced v_2 by about 50% compared to simulations with no jets
- In central collisions (vanishing impact parameter), hot spots with energy only could not account for even 50% of the anisotropies due to jets

## Context
This work extends the line of research showing that hard partons fully stopped in the quark-gluon plasma generate streams that contribute to flow anisotropy, as previously shown by Betz (year not given in text). The authors argue that this mechanism must be included in hydrodynamic simulations used to extract transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, because flow asymmetry is often used for measuring those properties. The paper contrasts its mechanism with the conventional scenario where anisotropy is due solely to anisotropies in the initial energy density distribution, and shows that momentum deposition during the fireball evolution cannot be mimicked by augmented initial conditions, as also noted by Martin (year not given in text). The results enable more reliable quantitative extraction of viscosities from comparisons of hydrodynamic simulations to experimental data.
