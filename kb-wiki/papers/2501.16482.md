# Hybrid Hadronization -- A Study of In-Medium Hadronization of Jets

> QCD jets are considered important probes for quark gluon plasma created in collisions of nuclei at high energies. Their parton showers are significantly altered if they develop inside of a deconfined medium. Hadronization of jets is also thought to be affected by the presence of quarks and gluons. We present a systematic study of the effects of a thermal bath of partons on the hadronization of parton showers. We use the JETSCAPE framework to create parton showers both in vacuum and in a brick of quark gluon plasma. The brick setup allows important parameters, like the size of the plasma as well as the collective flow of partons, to be varied systematically. We hadronize the parton showers using Hybrid Hadronization, which permits shower partons to form strings with thermal partons, or to recombine directly with thermal partons as well as with each other. We find a sizeable amount of interaction of shower partons with thermal partons during hadronization, indicating a natural continuation of the interaction of jet and medium during this stage. The observed effects grow with the size of the medium. Collective flow easily transfers from the thermal partons onto the emerging jet hadrons. We also see a significant change in hadron chemistry as expected in the presence of quark recombination processes.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Hybrid Hadronization, which combines quark recombination with string fragmentation, produces sizeable interactions between jet shower partons and thermal medium partons during hadronization, with effects that grow with medium size and smoothly transfer collective flow from the medium onto jet hadrons. The authors emphasize this is a systematic study of the model's behavior rather than a direct comparison to experimental data or tuning.

## Method
The authors used the JETSCAPE 3.0 framework to simulate light quark jets with initial energy 100 GeV traversing a static brick of quark-gluon plasma at temperature 300 MeV. MATTER generated the parton shower from initial virtuality down to a cutoff Q0 = 1 GeV, with LBT propagating low-virtuality partons through the brick until the hadronization time t = L/c. Hadronization was performed using Hybrid Hadronization, which allows shower partons to recombine directly with each other or with thermal partons, or to form strings that may include thermal partons, with the remnant string system handed to PYTHIA 8 for fragmentation.

## Key numbers
- Brick temperature: T = 300 MeV; hadronization temperature: Tc = 160 MeV
- Virtuality cutoff for MATTER: Q0 = 1.0 GeV
- Medium sizes L varied from 1 fm to 8 fm
- Longitudinal flow velocity: vx = 0.8c; transverse flow velocity: vy = 0.8c
- For L = 8 fm medium without flow, shower-thermal recombination becomes the most important hadron production channel below 2 GeV/c
- For L = 8 fm with longitudinal flow vx = 0.8c, shower-thermal recombination dominates up to approximately 4 GeV/c and contributes out to approximately 8 GeV/c
- Proton/pion ratio reaches approximately 1 for the largest medium (L = 8 fm) at soft momenta

## Context
This work extends the Hybrid Hadronization model (Han 2016) within the JETSCAPE framework to systematically study in-medium hadronization effects, building on earlier observations that quark recombination can explain large baryon-over-meson ratios and constituent quark scaling of elliptic flow in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC (Fries 2003, Greco 2003). The brick setup enables controlled variation of medium parameters, and the results enable future comprehensive comparisons to data in p+A and A+A collisions, with a separate effort ongoing for vacuum systems like e+e- and p+p.
