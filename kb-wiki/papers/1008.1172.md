# Muon capture on deuteron and 3He

> The muon capture reactions 2H(μ^-,ν_μ)nn and 3He(μ^-,ν_μ)3H are studied with conventional or chiral realistic potentials and consistent weak currents. The initial and final A=2 and 3 nuclear wave functions are obtained from the Argonne v18 or chiral N3LO two-nucleon potential, in combination with, respectively, the Urbana IX or chiral N2LO three-nucleon potential in the case of A=3. The weak current consists of polar- and axial-vector components. The former are related to the isovector piece of the electromagnetic current via the conserved-vector-current hypothesis. These and the axial currents are derived either in a meson-exchange or in a chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) framework. There is one parameter (either the N-to-Δaxial coupling constant in the meson-exchange model, or the strength of a contact term in the chiEFT model) which is fixed by reproducing the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium beta-decay. The model dependence relative to the adopted interactions and currents (and cutoff sensitivity in the chiEFT currents) is weak, resulting in total rates of 392.0 +/- 2.3 Hz for A=2, and 1484 +/- 13 Hz for A=3, where the spread accounts for this model dependence.

## Key claim

The total muon capture rates on deuteron and ³He are calculated with weak model dependence (spread of ±2.3 Hz for A=2 and ±13 Hz for A=3) when both the nuclear interactions and weak currents are treated consistently within either the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach or chiral effective field theory, and when the axial current is calibrated to reproduce the tritium beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for A=2 and A=3 nuclear wave functions using the hyperspherical-harmonics expansion method, with the Argonne v18 or chiral N3LO two-nucleon potentials (plus Urbana IX or chiral N2LO three-nucleon potentials for A=3). They evaluated matrix elements of weak polar-vector and axial-vector currents derived either from meson-exchange models or from chiral effective field theory, fixing one free parameter (the N-to-Δ axial coupling constant or a contact-term strength) to reproduce the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium beta-decay. For muon capture on deuteron, the final nn state was expanded in partial waves up to J≤2 and L≤3, including ¹S₀, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂–³F₂, and ¹D₂ channels.

## Key numbers

- Total muon capture rate on deuteron (doublet hyperfine state): 392.0 ± 2.3 s⁻¹, where the spread accounts for model dependence across interactions and currents.
- Total muon capture rate on ³He: 1484 ± 13 s⁻¹, where the spread accounts for model dependence across interactions and currents.
- For muon capture on ³He, the factor ℛ accounting for finite nuclear charge distribution was found to be within a percent of 0.98 for both Hamiltonian models.
- The deuteron binding energy calculated with AV18 was 2.22457 MeV and with N3LO was 2.22456 MeV (experimental: 2.224574(9) MeV).
- The triton binding energy calculated with AV18/UIX was 8.479 MeV and with N3LO/N2LO was 8.474 MeV (experimental: 8.482 MeV).
- The ³He binding energy calculated with AV18/UIX was 7.750 MeV and with N3LO/N2LO was 7.733 MeV (experimental: 7.718 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Schiavilla et al. (1998) and Marcucci et al. (2000) for weak proton capture reactions to muon capture on deuteron and ³He, treating both reactions simultaneously with consistent Hamiltonians and currents. It improves upon earlier calculations by Adam et al. (1990), Tatara et al. (1990), and Doi et al. (1990-1991) by constraining the axial current to reproduce the triton beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element, and upon the hybrid chiral calculation of Ando et al. (2002) by including all relevant partial waves in the nn final state. The results enable more reliable predictions for astrophysically important weak capture reactions on proton and ³He, whose rates cannot be measured experimentally.
