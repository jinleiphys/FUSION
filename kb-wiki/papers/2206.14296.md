# Investigating the coalescence-inspired sum rule for light nuclei and hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions

> A data-driven idea is presented to test if light nuclei and hypernuclei obey the coalescence-inspired sum rule, i.e., to test if the flow of a light nucleus or hypernucleus is the summed flow of each of its constituents. Here, the mass difference and charge difference among the constituents of light nuclei and hypernuclei are treated appropriately. The idea is applied to the available data for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3$ GeV fixed-target Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), published by the STAR collaboration. It is found that the sum rule for light nuclei is approximately valid near mid-rapidity ($-0.3 < y < 0$), but there is a clear violation of the sum rule at large rapidity ($y < -0.3$). The Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM), with baryonic mean-field plus nucleon coalescence, generates a similar pattern as obtained from the experimental data. In the present approach, the rapidity dependence of directed flow of the hypernuclei $\mathrm{_Λ^{3}H}$ and $\mathrm{_Λ^{4}H}$ is predicted in a model-independent way for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3$ GeV Au+Au collisions, which will be explored by ongoing and future measurements from STAR.

## Key claim

The coalescence-inspired sum rule for light nuclei (that the directed flow of a light nucleus equals the summed flow of its constituents) is approximately valid near mid-rapidity in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV Au+Au collisions, but shows a clear violation at larger rapidity (\(y < -0.3\)), with the violation pattern reproduced by the JAM transport model with baryonic mean field plus nucleon coalescence.

## Method

The author constructs combinations of light nuclei and hypernuclei that have identical constituent nucleon and hyperon content (same mass, charge, and strangeness at the constituent level), then tests whether the directed flow (\(v_1\)) of the composite object equals the sum of the \(v_1\) of its separated constituents. The method is applied to published STAR data for \(p\), \(d\), \(t\), \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV Au+Au collisions at 10-40% centrality, using a common kinematic region of \(0.4 < p_T/A < 1\) GeV/\(c\) and \(-0.5 < y < 0\). Linear algebra is used to identify five linearly independent combinations among the seven constructed indices.

## Key numbers

* For index 1 (\(p + d - ^3\)He), \(\Delta v_1\) is consistent with zero within uncertainties near mid-rapidity (\(-0.3 < y < 0\)), but deviates from zero at larger rapidity (\(y < -0.3\)) in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV Au+Au collisions at 10-40% centrality.
* For indices 2 and 3, \(\Delta v_1\) is again consistent with zero near mid-rapidity (\(-0.3 < y < 0\)) and shows significant deviation from zero at \(y < -0.3\) in the same collision system and centrality.
* A constant fit to the \(d\Delta v_1/dy\) slopes for the three independent indices 1, 2, and 3 yields \(C = 0.15 \pm 0.007\), providing an overall measure of sum rule violation.
* The predictions for hypernuclei \(^3_\Lambda\)H and \(^4_\Lambda\)H \(v_1\) are made for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV Au+Au collisions at 10-40% centrality, using the same kinematic region.

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration's observation of a breakdown of mass-number (\(A\)) scaling for light nuclear flow away from mid-rapidity in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV Au+Au collisions (STAR 2021). The data-driven method treats constituent mass and charge differences that are ignored in traditional \(A\) scaling, following a similar linear-algebra approach developed by Sheikh (2021) for hadron coalescence. The predictions for hypernuclei \(^3_\Lambda\)H and \(^4_\Lambda\)H directed flow provide a baseline for ongoing and future STAR measurements from the Beam Energy Scan Phase II program.
