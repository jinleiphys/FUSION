# Diagonalization in Reduced Hilbert Spaces using a Systematically Improved Basis: Application to Spin Dynamics in Lightly Doped Ladders

> A method is proposed to improve the accuracy of approximate techniques for strongly correlated electrons that use reduced Hilbert spaces. As a first step, the method involves a change of basis that incorporates exactly part of the short distance interactions. The Hamiltonian is rewritten in new variables that better represent the physics of the problem under study. A Hilbert space expansion performed in the new basis follows. The method is successfully tested using both the Heisenberg model and the $t-J$ model with holes on 2-leg ladders and chains, including estimations for ground state energies, static correlations, and spectra of excited states. An important feature of this technique is its ability to calculate dynamical responses on clusters larger than those that can be studied using Exact Diagonalization. The method is applied to the analysis of the dynamical spin structure factor $S(q,ω)$ on clusters with $2 \times 16$ sites and 0 and 2 holes. Our results confirm previous studies (M. Troyer, H. Tsunetsugu, and T. M. Rice, Phys. Rev. $ B 53$, 251 (1996)) which suggested that the state of the lowest energy in the spin-1 2-holes subspace corresponds to the bound state of a hole pair and a spin-triplet. Implications of this result for neutron scattering experiments both on ladders and planes are discussed.

# Diagonalization in Reduced Hilbert Spaces using a Systematically Improved Basis: Application to Spin Dynamics in Lightly Doped Ladders

## Key claim

The method improves convergence of reduced-basis Lanczos diagonalization by first changing to a basis that exactly incorporates short-distance correlations (e.g., rung singlets or plaquette eigenstates), then performing a Hilbert space expansion in that new basis. The authors claim this technique can produce dynamical responses on clusters larger than those accessible to exact diagonalization, but the accuracy is demonstrated only for quasi-one-dimensional systems (ladders and chains) and the method is presented as a complement to DMRG rather than a replacement.

## Method

The authors start by changing the basis from the standard site-local S^z basis to a basis that diagonalizes small clusters exactly, for 2-leg ladders this means using rung eigenstates (singlets and triplets) or plaquette eigenstates from 2x2 blocks. They then apply the Lanczos method in a reduced Hilbert space formed by truncating this new basis, keeping only the states with the largest coefficients in the ground state. The dynamical spin structure factor is computed by applying the spin operator to the truncated ground state and using a continued fraction expansion.

## Key numbers

- For a 2x16 undoped Heisenberg ladder, the exact ground state energy per site is -0.5781032 J; using the rung-basis with 300,000 states (1.6% of total space) yields 4 significant figures.
- The exact spin gap for the undoped 2x16 ladder is 0.505460384 J.
- For the anisotropic ladder with J_perp/J = 5.5, the spin gap was found to be 4.598105 J.
- For a 2x20 Heisenberg cluster, 1,600,000 states (0.04% of total space) were used to obtain spin correlations in agreement with world-line Monte Carlo at T=0.05 J.
- For the doped 2x16 ladder with 2 holes at J/t=0.4, the DMRG spin gap in the spin-1 subspace at momentum (pi,pi) is approximately 0.26 t.
- The nominal hole density studied is x = 2/32 = 0.0625.

## Context

This work extends the approach of reduced-basis diagonalization previously attempted by Wenzel and Jose (early 1990s) which suffered from slow convergence, and complements the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method of White (1992) which provides accurate equal-time correlations but not dynamical information. The specific application to doped ladders confirms the prediction by Troyer, Tsunetsugu, and Rice (1996) that the lowest-energy state in the spin-1 two-hole subspace is a bound state of a hole pair and a spin triplet. The method enables calculation of dynamical spin structure factors on clusters up to 2x16 sites, which are too large for exact diagonalization, and the results have implications for interpreting neutron scattering experiments on ladder compounds such as Sr14-xCaxCu24O41 and potentially for underdoped cuprate planes.
