# Solar models and solar neutrino oscillations

> We provide a summary of the current knowledge, theoretical and experimental, of solar neutrino fluxes and of the masses and mixing angles that characterize solar neutrino oscillations. We also summarize the principal reasons for doing new solar neutrino experiments and what we think may be learned from the future measurements.

# Solar models and solar neutrino oscillations

## Key claim
The paper presents a snapshot (as of March 1, 2004) of the theoretical understanding of solar neutrino fluxes and oscillation parameters, emphasizing that while the large mixing angle (LMA) oscillation solution is confirmed by the combination of solar and KamLAND reactor data, the most problematic and important source of uncertainty in predicting solar neutrino fluxes is the surface chemical composition of the Sun, whose systematic errors dominate over all other uncertainties.

## Method
The authors compare predictions from a series of standard solar models (BP00, BP04, BP04+) that incorporate different input data for nuclear fusion cross sections, equation of state, and surface chemical composition, and then perform a global analysis of all available solar neutrino and reactor anti-neutrino experimental data. The solar neutrino fluxes are treated as free parameters subject only to the luminosity constraint, and the evolution of neutrino wavefunctions in the Sun and Earth is solved numerically, with marginalization over all other variables including θ₁₃ and the atmospheric ΔM².

## Key numbers
- Best-fit solar neutrino oscillation parameters: Δm² = (7.3⁺⁰·⁴₋₀·₆) × 10⁻⁵ eV² and tan² θ₁₂ = 0.41 ± 0.05 (1σ uncertainties).
- The 1σ allowed range for active-sterile admixture: sin² η ≤ 0.10, implying less than 6% of the ⁸B flux is sterile neutrinos in the SNO energy range.
- Measured pp flux relative to BP04 theory: φ(pp)ₘₑₐₛᵤᵣₑd = (1.02 ± 0.02 ± 0.01) φ(pp)ₜₕₑₒᵣy.
- Measured ⁸B flux relative to BP04 theory: φ(⁸B)ₘₑₐₛᵤᵣₑd = (0.88 ± 0.04 ± 0.23) φ(⁸B)ₜₕₑₒᵣy.
- Measured ⁷Be flux relative to BP04 theory: φ(⁷Be)ₘₑₐₛᵤᵣₑd = (0.91⁺⁰·²⁴₋₀·₆₂ ± 0.11) φ(⁷Be)ₜₕₑₒᵣy.
- BP04 predicted CNO-generated solar luminosity: L_CNO = 1.6 ± 0.6% of total photon luminosity.
- Neutrino-inferred solar luminosity ratio: L⊙(neutrino-inferred)/L⊙ = 1.4⁺⁰·²₋₀·₃.
- Cosmological mass density in neutrinos (assuming m₁ << √(Δm²_solar)): Ω_ν = 0.0009 ± 0.0001.
- Upper bound on sin² 2θ₁₃ from atmospheric and solar data: sin² θ₁₃ < 0.052 at 3σ.
- Critical energies for vacuum-to-MSW transition: E(crit) ≃ 1.8 MeV (⁸B), ≃ 2.2 MeV (⁷Be), ≃ 3.3 MeV (pp), for tan² θ₁₂ = 0.41.
- The dominant uncertainty in ⁸B flux from chemical composition (Z/X) is 20%; for CNO fluxes it is ~35%.
- The 3σ range of Z/X implies Δ(Z/X)/(Z/X) = 0.15 (1σ), 2.5 times larger than the BP00 uncertainty.

## Context
This paper extends the series of standard solar models by Bahcall and collaborators (BP00, BP04) and synthesizes results from the KamLAND reactor experiment (first results published in 2002) with solar neutrino data from chlorine (Homestake), gallium (GALLEX/GNO, SAGE), Super-Kamiokande, and SNO experiments. The work confirms the LMA oscillation solution and quantifies how the surface composition controversy (new three-dimensional atmospheric models by Asplund and collaborators giving lower Z/X) has become the dominant systematic uncertainty, superseding earlier concerns about nuclear cross sections. The paper enables future low-energy solar neutrino experiments (such as Borexino and LENS) by specifying the precision needed to test the vacuum-to-MSW transition region around 2 MeV and to measure the pp and ⁷Be fluxes that constitute over 98% of the total solar neutrino flux.
