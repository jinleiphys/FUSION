# Single $π^0$ Production Off Neutrons Bound in Deuteron with Linearly Polarized Photons

> The quasifree $\overrightarrowγ d\toπ^0n(p)$ photon beam asymmetry, $Σ$, has been measured at photon energies, $E_γ$, from 390 to 610 MeV, corresponding to center of mass energy from 1.271 to 1.424 GeV, for the first time. The data were collected in the A2 hall of the MAMI electron beam facility with the Crystal Ball and TAPS calorimeters covering pion center-of-mass angles from 49 to 148$^\circ$. In this kinematic region, polarization observables are sensitive to contributions from the $Δ(1232)$ and $N(1440)$ resonances. The extracted values of $Σ$ have been compared to predictions based on partial-wave analyses (PWAs) of the existing pion photoproduction database. Our comparison includes the SAID, MAID, and Bonn-Gatchina analyses; while a revised SAID fit, including the new $Σ$ measurements, has also been performed. In addition, isospin symmetry is examined as a way to predict $π^0n$ photoproduction observables, based on fits to published data in the channels $π^0p$, $π^+n$, and $π^-p$.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first measurements of the photon beam asymmetry Σ for quasifree π⁰ photoproduction off neutrons bound in deuterium at photon energies from 390 to 610 MeV, but the results are presented uncorrected for potential final-state interaction (FSI) effects, which the authors argue are expected to be comparable to or smaller than the quoted systematic uncertainties from experimental sources.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the A2 hall of the MAMI electron beam facility using a linearly polarized photon beam produced via coherent bremsstrahlung from a diamond radiator, incident on a 10 cm liquid deuterium target. The π⁰ mesons and recoil neutrons were detected using the Crystal Ball and TAPS calorimeters, with event selection based on detecting three neutral particles (two photons from π⁰ decay and one neutron) and applying the sPlot technique to subtract background from random tagger coincidences, incorrect photon combinations, and non-π⁰n final states. The photon asymmetry Σ was extracted via unbinned extended maximum likelihood fits to the azimuthal angle distributions of the π⁰ production plane relative to the polarization plane.

## Key numbers

* Photon energy range: 390 to 610 MeV, corresponding to center-of-mass energy from 1.271 to 1.424 GeV.
* Pion center-of-mass angular coverage: 49° to 148°.
* Photon polarization ranged from 15% at Eγ = 390 MeV to a maximum of 55% at 610 MeV.
* The photon beam energy was measured with a resolution of approximately 4 MeV using the Glasgow-Mainz Tagged Photon Spectrometer.
* Mean systematic uncertainty from background subtraction: 3%.
* Overall systematic uncertainty from linear polarization calibration: 4.5% (combining 2% from SAID solution uncertainty and 4% from proton asymmetry extraction uncertainty).

## Context

This work extends the range of previous polarized measurements by the GRAAL Collaboration (Di Salvo et al., 2009), which covered Eγ from 703 to 1475 MeV, downward to lower energies where contributions from the Δ(1232) and N(1440) resonances are significant. The results are compared to predictions from three partial-wave analyses (SAID, MAID, and Bonn-Gatchina), and a revised SAID fit including the new Σ data has been performed. These measurements, together with recent A2 unpolarized cross sections (Briscoe et al., 2019) and E asymmetry data (Costanza et al., 2020), will enable better-constrained γn decay amplitudes for nucleon resonances.
