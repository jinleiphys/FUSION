# Testing the $χ_{c1}\, p$ composite nature of the $P_c(4450)$

> Making use of a recently proposed formalism, we analyze the composite nature of the $P_c(4450)$ resonance observed by LHCb. We show that the present data suggest that this state is almost entirely made of a $χ_{c1}$ and a proton, due to the close proximity to this threshold. This also suppresses the decay modes into other, lighter channels, in our study represented by $J/Ψp$. We further argue that this is very similar to the case of the scalar meson $f_0(980)$ which is located closely to the $K\bar K$ threshold and has a suppressed decay into the lighter $ππ$ channel.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the present data suggest the $P_c(4450)$ resonance is almost entirely a composite of a $\chi_{c1}$ and a proton, due to the close proximity of its mass to the $\chi_{c1}p$ threshold. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the assumption of a two-channel scenario where the compositeness relation is saturated by the $J/\Psi p$ and $\chi_{c1}p$ channels.

## Method

The authors employ a recently developed formalism (Ref. [ref.230715.3]) to calculate compositeness coefficients for a two-channel system consisting of $J/\Psi p$ (channel 1) and $\chi_{c1}p$ (channel 2). They solve two coupled equations: one imposing saturation of the compositeness relation and the other equating the total width of the $P_c(4450)$ to the sum of the partial decay widths to these two channels, using a Lorentzian mass distribution to handle the decay to $\chi_{c1}p$ when the resonance mass is near threshold.

## Key numbers

- The $P_c(4450)$ mass $m_P$ and width $\Gamma_P$ are taken from LHCb data (Ref. [ref.230715.1]) to determine the pole position $s_P = (m_P - i\Gamma_P/2)^2$.
- If the $J/\Psi p$ channel were the main component ($|X_1| \simeq 1$), the resulting width would be $\Gamma_P \simeq 1.5$ GeV, which is completely unrealistic.
- For the 2nd Riemann sheet, the compositeness coefficients are $X^f_1 = 0.004 \pm 0.002$ and $X^f_2 = 0.996 \pm 0.002$, with couplings $|\gamma_1| = 1.9 \pm 0.5$ GeV and $|\gamma_2| = 15.6 \pm 1.3$ GeV.
- For the 3rd Riemann sheet, the compositeness coefficients are $X^f_1 = 0.006 \pm 0.003$ and $X^f_2 = 0.994 \pm 0.003$, with couplings $|\gamma_1| = 2.4 \pm 0.6$ GeV and $|\gamma_2| = 14.6 \pm 1.1$ GeV.
- The partial decay widths for the 2nd Riemann sheet are $\Gamma_1 = 5.6 \pm 3.0$ MeV (to $J/\Psi p$) and $\Gamma_2 = 34.6 \pm 10.9$ MeV (to $\chi_{c1}p$).
- Even if the compositeness relation is only 80% saturated ($x=0.8$), the partial decay width to $\chi_{c1}p$ ($27.0 \pm 8.5$ MeV) remains about twice that to $J/\Psi p$ ($12.0 \pm 4.8$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the ideas first proposed by Guo (2015) that the $P_c(4450)$ peak might be an anomalous-threshold singularity on top of the $\chi_{c1}p$ branch-point threshold, rather than a genuine resonance. The authors draw a direct analogy to the scalar meson $f_0(980)$, which sits close to the $K\bar{K}$ threshold and has a suppressed decay into the lighter $\pi\pi$ channel (Ref. [ref.240715.1]). The analysis enables a clear experimental test: a precise measurement of the partial decay widths to $J/\Psi p$ and $\chi_{c1}p$ would confirm or rule out the proposed composite nature.
