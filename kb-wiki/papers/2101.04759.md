# Hydrodynamics of spin currents

> We study relativistic hydrodynamics in the presence of a non vanishing spin chemical potential. Using a variety of techniques we carry out an exhaustive analysis, and identify the constitutive relations for the stress tensor and spin current in such a setup, allowing us to write the hydrodynamic equations of motion to second order in derivatives. We then solve the equations of motion in a perturbative setup and find surprisingly good agreement with measurements of global $Λ$-hyperon polarization carried out at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors derive a complete set of constitutive relations for the stress tensor and spin current in relativistic hydrodynamics with a non-zero spin chemical potential, expanded to second order in derivatives, and show that a simple perturbative model based on these relations yields surprisingly good agreement with global Λ-hyperon polarization measurements at RHIC, though they caution that a full hydrodynamic simulation and proper treatment of hadronization is still needed.

## Method

The authors use a hydrostatic partition function approach to classify all possible terms in the constitutive relations for a parity-invariant, conformal fluid in 3+1 dimensions, imposing the second law of thermodynamics and conformal invariance to constrain transport coefficients. They then solve the resulting hydrodynamic equations of motion perturbatively around a Bjorken flow background, introducing linear perturbations that break transverse translations and axial rotation to mimic peripheral heavy-ion collisions, and relate the spin potential to the average hyperon polarization vector.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio satisfies η/s = 3η₀ / 4ε₀, where η₀ ≥ 0 is constrained by positivity of entropy production.
- The ideal fluid energy density is ε = ε₀ T⁴ and pressure is P = (1/3)ε₀ T⁴, with ε₀ a constant.
- The spin current at ideal order contains a term 8ρ₀ u^λ M_{νρ} where ∂P/∂M² = ρ₀ T².
- The temperature in Bjorken flow evolves as T = T₀ (τ₀/τ)^(1/3) − (η₀ / (2ε₀ τ)), where T₀ is the initial temperature at proper time τ₀.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin hydrodynamics initiated by Becattini (2013) and Florkowski (2017), and builds on the classification of spin current sources by Gallegos (2020). It provides the first complete set of constitutive relations to second order in derivatives for a conformal, parity-invariant fluid, enabling future full hydrodynamic simulations of hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
