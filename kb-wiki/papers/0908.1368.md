# Spin polarized states in neutron matter at a strong magnetic field

> Spin polarized states in neutron matter at a strong magnetic field are considered in the model with the Skyrme effective interaction (SLy4, SLy7 parametrizations). Analyzing the self-consistent equations at zero temperature, it is shown that a thermodynamically stable branch of solutions for the spin polarization parameter as a function of density corresponds to the negative spin polarization when the majority of neutron spins are oriented oppositely to the direction of the magnetic field. Besides, beginning from some threshold density being dependent on the magnetic field strength the self-consistent equations have also two other branches (upper and lower) of solutions for the spin polarization parameter with the positive spin polarization. The free energy corresponding to the upper branch turns out to be very close to the free energy corresponding to the thermodynamically preferable branch with the negative spin polarization. As a consequence, at a strong magnetic field, the state with the positive spin polarization can be realized as a metastable state at the high density region in neutron matter which under decreasing density at some threshold density changes into a thermodynamically stable state with the negative spin polarization. The calculations of the neutron spin polarization parameter and energy per neutron as functions of the magnetic field strength show that the influence of the magnetic field remains small at the field strengths up to $10^{17}$ G.

## Key claim

At strong magnetic fields (up to ~10¹⁸ G), neutron matter described by Skyrme effective interactions (SLy4, SLy7) can host a metastable state with positive spin polarization (majority of spins aligned *along* the field) at high densities, even though the thermodynamically stable branch always has negative spin polarization (spins opposite the field). This metastable state exists only above a threshold density that depends on the field strength.

## Method

The authors solved self-consistent equations at zero temperature for the neutron spin polarization parameter and chemical potential, derived from a Fermi-liquid approach with the Skyrme effective interaction (SLy4 and SLy7 parametrizations). The quasiparticle spectrum was split into two spin branches, and the distribution functions were obtained from the minimum condition of the thermodynamic potential. The magnetization contribution to the internal magnetic field was neglected due to the small neutron magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- Spontaneous spin polarization (at H=0) develops at density 3.70 ρ₀ for SLy4 and 3.59 ρ₀ for SLy7, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (SLy4) or 0.158 fm⁻³ (SLy7).
- For SLy7 at H = 5×10¹⁷ G, the threshold density for the positive-polarization branches is ρₜₕ ≈ 3.80 ρ₀; at H = 10¹⁸ G, ρₜₕ ≈ 3.92 ρ₀.
- At H = 10¹⁸ G and intermediate densities (0.5 ρ₀ ≲ ρ ≲ 3 ρ₀), the negative-polarization branch shows a plateau with Π₁ ≈ −0.08.
- Neutrons become totally spin polarized on the negative branch at ρ ≈ 6 ρ₀.
- The influence of the magnetic field on spin polarization and energy per neutron remains small for field strengths up to 10¹⁷ G.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-polarized neutron matter in strong magnetic fields by Perez-Garcia (2008), who used Skyrme and Gogny forces but found only one branch of solutions (with positive polarization, due to an incorrect sign in the quasiparticle spectrum). The present paper corrects that sign and reveals three distinct solution branches. The results contrast with Gogny D1S and D1P forces (Lopez-Val et al., 2005), which show no ferromagnetic transition below 7 ρ₀. The possible metastable positive-polarization state could affect neutrino opacities and cooling rates in magnetars, as discussed by Perez-Garcia (2008).
