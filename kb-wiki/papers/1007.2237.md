# Hadronic weak interaction in the two-nucleon system with effective field theories

> Weak interactions in two-nucleon system at low energies are explored in the framework of effective field theory. We review our recent calculations of parity-violating observables in radiative neutron capture on a proton at threshold where both pionful and pionless theories are employed.

# Hadronic weak interaction in the two-nucleon system with effective field theories

## Key claim
Parity-violating observables in radiative neutron capture on a proton can be calculated using effective field theories (both pionful and pionless), but the results show non-negligible dependence on cutoff values and renormalization scales, and the parity-violating asymmetry is sufficiently dominated by the leading-order one-pion-exchange contribution to determine the first significant digit of the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant if measured precisely.

## Method
The authors employed a pionful effective field theory with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory counting rules to derive the weak two-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), including one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and contact terms regularized with a monopole cutoff function. For the parity-violating polarization in unpolarized neutron capture, they used a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, where all interactions are represented by two-nucleon contact terms and the calculation is performed self-consistently at leading order.

## Key numbers
- Parity-violating asymmetry coefficient $a_\gamma$ from OPE alone ranges from $-0.0992$ to $-0.1119$ as cutoff $\Lambda$ varies from 500 MeV to 2000 MeV
- Total $a_\gamma$ (OPE + TPE) at renormalization scale $\mu = 2 m_\pi$ ranges from $-0.0987$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV to $-0.0966$ at $\Lambda = 2000$ MeV
- Total $a_\gamma$ at $\mu = m_\rho$ ranges from $-0.0885$ ($\Lambda = 500$ MeV) to $-0.0952$ ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV)
- Total $a_\gamma$ at $\mu = 1$ GeV ranges from $-0.0859$ ($\Lambda = 500$ MeV) to $-0.0949$ ($\Lambda = 2000$ MeV)
- The difference between $\Lambda = 500$ MeV and 1000 MeV is about 11% increase in magnitude for LO and about 54% for NNLO; above 1000 MeV the change reduces to about 1% for LO and 18% for NNLO
- The largest gap in $\mu$ dependence is about 15% of the smallest magnitude value $-0.0859$

## Context
This work extends the application of effective field theory to hadronic weak interactions in the two-nucleon system, following prior EFT approaches by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise; Savage (2001); Zhu et al. (2005); Liu (2007); Hyun et al. (2007); and Shin (2009). The authors contrast their EFT approach with the traditional one-meson-exchange (OME) picture, where benchmark calculations of weak coupling constants were done with a quark model by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980). The results enable future experimental measurements of $A_\gamma$ and $P_\gamma$ to constrain the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant $h^1_\pi$ and the weak dibaryon-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants $\hszero$ and $\htzero$, respectively.
