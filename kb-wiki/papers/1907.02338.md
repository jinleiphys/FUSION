# Statistical method in quark combination model

> We present a new method of solving the probability distribution for baryons, antibaryons and mesons at the hadronization of constituent quark and antiquark system. The hadronization is governed by the quark combination rule in the quark combination model developed by the Shandong Group. We use the method of the generating function to derive the outcome of the quark combination rule, which is much simpler and easier to be generalized than the original method. Furthermore, we use the formula of the quark combination rule and its generalization to study the property of multiplicity distribution of net-protons. Taking a naive case of quark number fluctuations and correlations at hadronization, we calculate ratios of multiplicity cumulants of final-state net-protons and discuss the potential applicability of quark combination model in studying hadronic multiplicity fluctuations and the underlying phase transition property in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new generating function method for solving the probability distribution of baryons, antibaryons, and mesons produced by the quark combination rule, which is simpler and more generalizable than the original combinatorial method; however, the applicability to three-dimensional phase space remains complicated because one cannot easily define a particular hadronization order to apply the rule.

## Method

The authors treat a system of \(N_q\) quarks and \(N_{\bar{q}}\) antiquarks stochastically populated in one-dimensional longitudinal rapidity space and apply the quark combination rule (QCR) or its generalized version (gQCR) to combine adjacent partons into hadrons sequentially. They derive recursive equations for the number of distinct queues \(F(N_M,N_B,N_{\bar{B}},0,0)\) that yield a given set of meson, baryon, and antibaryon numbers with no leftover quarks, and solve these recursions using generating functions. The resulting probability distribution is then used to compute moments and cumulant ratios for net-protons, incorporating flavor and spin selection factors and resonance decay parameters.

## Key numbers

- The number of distinct queues for the case \(N_M>0\), \(N_B=N_{\bar{B}}=0\) under the original QCR is \(F(N_M,0,0,0,0)=2\times 3^{N_M-1}\) for \(N_M\geq 1\).
- The generating function for the original QCR is \(A(x,y,z) = (1-x)/(1-3x-y-z)\).
- The final expression for the number of queues under the original QCR is \(F(N_M,N_B,N_{\bar{B}},0,0) = \frac{(2N_M+3N_B+3N_{\bar{B}})(N_M+N_B+N_{\bar{B}}-1)!}{N_M! N_B! N_{\bar{B}}!} 3^{N_M-1}\) for \(N_M>0\).
- For the generalized QCR with \(N_B=N_{\bar{B}}=0\), the solution is \(F(N_M,0,0,0,0) = \frac{1}{2}\left[(2+\sqrt{2})^{N_M} + (2-\sqrt{2})^{N_M}\right]\).
- The parameters for vector-to-pseudoscalar meson ratio and decuplet-to-octet baryon ratio are taken as \(R_{V/P}=0.45\) and \(R_{D/O}=0.5\) from studying hadronic yields in relativistic high energy collisions.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination model developed by the Shandong Group (SDQCM), which was previously applied to hadron production in \(e^+e^-\) and \(pp\) collisions (Xie 1985, Wang 1994, Shao 2006) and to rapidity distributions in heavy-ion collisions (Shao 2009, Sun 2011). The new generating function method simplifies the original combinatorial approach of Mo (1986) and enables calculation of multiplicity cumulants for net-protons, which are relevant for searching the QCD critical endpoint in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Stephanov 1999, Asakawa 2000). The results can be compared with experimental data on cumulant ratios to test the quark combination model's description of hadronic multiplicity fluctuations and the underlying phase transition.
