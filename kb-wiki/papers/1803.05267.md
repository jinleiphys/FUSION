# $P_{c}$-like pentaquarks in hidden strange sector

> Analogous to the work of hidden charm molecular pentaquarks, we study possible hidden strange molecular pentaquarks composed of $Σ$ (or $Σ^{*}$) and $K$ (or $K^{*}$) in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model. Our results suggest that the $ΣK$, $ΣK^{*}$ and $Σ^{*} K^{*}$ with $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{1}{2}^{-}$ and $ΣK^{*}$, $Σ^{*} K$ and $Σ^{*} K^{*}$ with $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ are all resonance states by coupling the open channels. The molecular pentaquark $Σ^{*} K$ with quantum numbers $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ can be seen as a strange partner of the LHCb $P_{c}(4380)$ state, and it can be identified as the nucleon resonance $N^{*}(1875)$ listed in PDG. The $ΣK^{*}$ with quantum numbers $IJ^{P}=\frac{1}{2}\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ can be identified as the $N^{*}(2100)$, which was experimentally observed in the $ϕ$ photo-production.

## Key claim

The authors claim that six molecular pentaquark states composed of Σ (or Σ*) and K (or K*) in the hidden strange sector are all resonance states after coupling to open channels, but they note that the total decay widths given are lower limits because only hidden strange channels (not channels with ubar quarks) and only two-body decays were considered.

## Method

The authors used the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), a constituent quark model with color confinement, one-gluon exchange, and chiral field interactions, to calculate effective potentials between Σ (or Σ*) and K (or K*). They then applied the resonating group method (RGM) to solve the scattering problem of the open channels, extracting resonance masses and partial decay widths from the cross sections computed via scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- ΣK bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 1/2^-: binding energy -18.8 MeV, mass 1669.2 MeV; resonance mass 1668.0 MeV, decay width 1.3 MeV (from ΛK S-wave channel)
- ΣK* bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 1/2^-: binding energy -7.2 MeV, mass 2077.8 MeV; resonance mass range 2056.6 to 2083.4 MeV, total decay width approximately 10 MeV
- Σ*K* bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 1/2^-: binding energy -21.9 MeV, mass 2255.1 MeV; resonance mass range 2219.0 to 2261.5 MeV, decay width at least 150 MeV
- ΣK* bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^-: binding energy -22.7 MeV, mass 2062.3 MeV; resonance mass range 2046.1 to 2061.4 MeV, decay width about 30 MeV
- Σ*K bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^-: binding energy -7.4 MeV, mass 1872.6 MeV; resonance mass range 1871.6 to 1875.7 MeV, very narrow (partial widths below 0.1 MeV for D-wave channels)
- Σ*K* bound state with IJ^P = 1/2 3/2^-: binding energy -6.8 MeV, mass 2270.2 MeV; resonance mass range 2256.5 to 2270.5 MeV, narrow (partial widths up to 3.7 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks (Huang and Ping, 2017) to the hidden strange sector by replacing cbar-c with sbar-s. It is analogous to hadron-level studies by He (2015) using a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach, which also identified Σ*K as N*(1875) and ΣK* as N*(2100). The results enable experimental searches for these predicted pentaquark states in phi photo-production and other reactions, and provide a quark-level interpretation for the nucleon resonances N*(1875) and N*(2100) listed in the Particle Data Group.
