# Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model at finite temperature

> The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model is used to examine the validity of some approximate methods in a many-body theory at finite temperatures. Namely, the thermal random phase approximation (TRPA) and the thermal renormalized random phase approximation (TRRPA) are studied. An average energy of the system, an average quasispin projection and a particle number variance are calculated within these approximation and exactly with the grand canonical ensemble partition function. On the whole the results of TRRPA are found to be in better agreement with the exact ones. The validity of the both approximation becomes better with increasing temperature as well as particle number.

# Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model at finite temperature

## Key claim

The thermal renormalized random phase approximation (TRRPA) provides better agreement with exact grand canonical ensemble results than the thermal random phase approximation (TRPA) or thermal mean-field approximation (TMFA) for the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, but only in the normal phase (effective coupling constant χ < 1) and at moderate temperatures T ≤ 0.5ε; at high temperatures (T ≫ ε) the differences between all approximations and exact results become negligible.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the LMG Hamiltonian exactly using the grand canonical partition function for a system of N = Ω = 10 fermions distributed over two degenerate levels with level spacing ε = 2 and coupling strength V. They then compute the average energy, average quasispin projection ⟨Ĵ_z⟩, and particle number variance within the thermal mean-field approximation (TMFA), thermal random phase approximation (TRPA), and thermal renormalized random phase approximation (TRRPA) using the thermo field dynamics (TFD) formalism, and compare all results to the exact grand canonical ensemble calculations as functions of temperature and effective coupling constant χ = V(N-1)/ε.

## Key numbers

- System size: N = Ω = 10 particles, level spacing ε = 2
- TRPA collective mode energy ω vanishes at χ = 1 (the collapse point); TRRPA avoids this collapse
- At χ = 0.95, noticeable differences between exact and approximate values occur only at moderate T ≤ 0.3ε
- The particle number variance ΔN differs between exact and approximate values by only 2-3%
- At T > 0.5ε, differences between exact and approximate results become negligible
- Typical value of ΔN/N for N = 10-30 is around 10%

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature studies of the LMG model by Kuriyama et al. (1996) and others, who examined thermal Hartree-Fock and TRPA within the mixed state representation, and by Avdeenkov, Kosov, and Vdovin (1996), who first proposed the TRRPA method. The paper demonstrates that TRRPA, which treats the temperature-dependent ground state more consistently by including a finite number of thermal quasiparticles, avoids the unphysical collapse of the collective mode that occurs in TRPA at χ = 1, a feature already known for the zero-temperature renormalized RPA from Ken-ji Hara (1964) and Rowe (1968). The results enable further applications of TRRPA to hot nuclear systems, though the authors note that investigations of the deformed phase (χ > 1) remain in progress.
