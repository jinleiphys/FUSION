# The XYZs of Charmonium at BES

> This contribution reviews some recent developments in charmonium spectroscopy, and discusses related theoretical predictions. The spectrum of states, strong decays of states above open charm threshold, electromagnetic transitions, and issues related to the recent discoveries of the "XYZ" states are discussed. Contributions that BES can make to our understanding of charmonium and related states are stressed in particular.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent developments in charmonium spectroscopy and argues that the "naive quark potential model" is much less accurate in some heavy quark systems than previously thought, with mass discrepancies of about 160 MeV in the charm-strange sector and the X(3872) state likely being a D\(^0\)D\(^{*0}\) molecule rather than a conventional \(c\bar c\) state.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results from Belle, BABAR, and CLEO on charmonium and charm-strange meson spectroscopy, and compares them to theoretical predictions from the nonrelativistic quark potential model and the \(^3\)P\(_0\) decay model for strong decays and E1 electromagnetic transitions. The paper also discusses how BES can use \(e^+e^-\) collisions to produce \(1^{--}\) resonances (\(\psi(3770)\), \(\psi(4040)\), \(\psi(4160)\), \(\psi(4415)\)) to study radiative transitions, strong decay amplitudes, and the new XYZ states.

## Key numbers

- D\(_{s0}(2317)\) mass is about 160 MeV below the potential model prediction of 2.48 GeV (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985).
- X(3872) mass is nearly degenerate with the neutral D\(^0\)D\(^{*0}\) pair threshold.
- Z(3930) mass: \(3929 \pm 5 \pm 2\) MeV; total width: \(29 \pm 10 \pm 2\) MeV; product \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} \cdot B_{DD} = 0.18 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.03\) keV (Belle, 2005).
- Theoretical \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} \cdot B_{DD}\) for a \(\chi_2'\) assignment: \(0.24-0.51\) keV.
- X(3943) mass: \(3943 \pm 6 \pm 6\) MeV; total width: \(15 \pm 10\) MeV or \(< 52\) MeV at 90% confidence level (Belle, 2005).
- Y(3940) mass: \(3943 \pm 11 \pm 13\) MeV; total width: \(87 \pm 22\) MeV (Belle, 2004).
- Predicted total width for \(\eta_c''\) (if X(3943) is \(\eta_c''\)): \(\Gamma = 70\) MeV in the \(^3\)P\(_0\) decay model.
- Predicted partial widths for \(\chi_2'(3929)\): DD\(^*\) = 11.3 MeV, DD = 34.3 MeV.
- Predicted partial width for \(\chi_1'(3940)\): DD\(^*\) = 140 MeV.
- Predicted E1 radiative partial width of \(\psi(4040) \to \chi_2'(3929)\): 56 keV; \(\psi(4160) \to \chi_1'(3940)\): 129 keV.
- \(\psi' \to J/\psi \pi\pi\) partial width: about 140 keV.
- B meson branching fraction \(B_{B\to K^+ \chi_1} = 5.3 \pm 0.7 \times 10^{-4}\).
- Product branching fraction for Y(3940): \(B_{B\to KY} \cdot B_{Y\to \omega J/\psi} = 7.1 \pm 1.3 \pm 3.1 \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This review extends the work of Swanson (2006) on charm meson spectroscopy and the theoretical studies of Barnes (2005) and Eichten (2005) on charmonium strong decays and E1 transitions. It addresses the discrepancies between potential model predictions and experimental discoveries such as the D\(_{s0}(2317)\) and X(3872), and it enables future BES measurements of radiative widths, strong decay amplitudes (especially D\(^*\)D\(^*\) angular distributions), and searches for the XYZ states and charmonium hybrids, which are relevant for the PANDA experiment at GSI.
