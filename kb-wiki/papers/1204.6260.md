# Fragmentation of spin-dipole strength in $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb

> An extension of time-dependent covariant density functional theory that includes particle-vibration coupling is applied to the charge-exchange channel. Spin-dipole excitation spectra are calculated an compared to available data for $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb. A significant fragmentation is found for all three angular-momentum components of the spin-dipole strength as a result of particle-vibration coupling, as well as a shift of a portion of the strength to higher energy. A high-energy tail is formed in the strength distribution that linearly decreases with energy. Using a model-independent sum rule,the corresponding neutron skin thickness is estimated and shown to be consistent with values obtained at the mean-field level.

## Key claim

Particle-vibration coupling, included via the relativistic time-blocking approximation (RTBA), produces significant fragmentation of all three angular-momentum components of the spin-dipole strength in $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb, and shifts a portion of the strength to higher energy, forming a high-energy tail that decreases linearly with energy. The resulting neutron skin thickness estimates are consistent with mean-field values but depend strongly on the choice of relativistic effective interaction, with the NL3 functional yielding larger skin thicknesses than empirical values.

## Method

The authors extended time-dependent covariant density functional theory by including particle-vibration coupling in the charge-exchange channel, using the relativistic time-blocking approximation (RTBA). They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function with an interaction that includes static $\rho$-meson and $\pi$-meson exchange terms, a zero-range Landau-Migdal term, and a frequency-dependent phonon coupling term $\Phi(\omega)$. Spin-dipole strength distributions were calculated for $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb using the NL3 relativistic mean-field effective interaction consistently for the ground state, RPA phonons, and charge-exchange excitations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{90}$Zr in the $t_{-}$ channel, the RTBA produces a broad resonance peaked at 23.5 MeV, in very good agreement with data from Yako (2006).
- The centroid energies of the spin-dipole components in $^{90}$Zr are: $m_{1}^{2^{-}}/m_{0}^{2^{-}} = 25.4$ MeV for $2^{-}$, 29.4 MeV for $0^{-}$, and 32.6 MeV for $1^{-}$.
- The shift of the centroid energy due to particle-vibration coupling is 2.5 MeV for $^{90}$Zr and 1 MeV for $^{208}$Pb.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the main resonance is centered at 25 MeV with an additional small peak at approximately 6 MeV; the $t_{+}$ channel strength is concentrated in a single peak at 7.5 MeV.
- The calculated sum rule $S_{-} - S_{+}$ is 160.963 fm$^2$ for $^{90}$Zr (experimental: $148 \pm 6 \pm 7 \pm 7$ fm$^2$ from Yako (2006)) and 1213.562 fm$^2$ for $^{208}$Pb.
- The calculated neutron skin thickness $\delta_{np}$ is 0.115 fm for $^{90}$Zr (experimental: $0.07 \pm 0.04$ fm from Yako (2006)) and 0.272 fm for $^{208}$Pb (empirical values range from 0.083 fm to 0.19 fm from various measurements).
- The calculated neutron rms radius for $^{90}$Zr is 4.308 fm (experimental: $4.26 \pm 0.04$ fm); for $^{208}$Pb it is 5.731 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous random phase approximation (RPA) studies of spin-dipole excitations by including particle-vibration coupling, which earlier non-self-consistent 2p2h RPA approaches (Drozdz, 1987) could not fully capture. It directly addresses the high-energy tail observed in $(p,n)$ and $(n,p)$ measurements by Yako (2006) and Wakasa (2010), which simple 1p1h RPA could not reproduce. The results enable more reliable extraction of neutron skin thickness from spin-dipole sum rules, though the authors note that the NL3 functional overestimates the skin thickness compared to empirical values from Clark (2003) and Tamii (2011), and suggest that modern functionals with lower asymmetry energy (e.g., DD-PC1) would yield smaller values.
