# Bridging the gap by shaking superfluid matter

> In cold compact stars, Cooper pairing between fermions in dense matter leads to the formation of a gap in their excitation spectrum and typically exponentially suppresses transport properties. However, we show here that weak Urca reactions become strongly enhanced and approach their ungapped level when the star undergoes density oscillations of sufficiently large amplitude. We study both the neutrino emissivity and the bulk viscosity due to direct Urca processes in hadronic, hyperonic and quark matter and discuss different superfluid and superconducting pairing patterns.

## Key claim

The exponential suppression of beta-equilibration rates (neutrino emissivity and bulk viscosity) in superfluid/superconducting dense matter is only effective at small oscillation amplitudes; when the amplitude of density oscillations becomes sufficiently large that the chemical potential imbalance approaches the pairing gap, the rates are strongly enhanced and can approach their ungapped levels.

## Method

The authors derive general expressions for the net rate and neutrino emissivity of direct Urca processes in gapped Fermi liquids, incorporating modification functions that capture the phase space available when the system is driven out of beta-equilibrium by density oscillations. They then compute the cycle-averaged emissivity and bulk viscosity for harmonic density oscillations, evaluating the modification functions for various pairing patterns (symmetric and asymmetric gaps) and applying them to hadronic, hyperonic, and quark matter using specific equations of state and gap models.

## Key numbers

- For hadronic matter with an APR equation of state, the susceptibility \(C\) ranges from \(C(n_0/4) \approx 20\,\mathrm{MeV}\) to \(C(5n_0) \approx 150\,\mathrm{MeV}\); for quark matter it ranges from \(C(n_0) \approx 30\,\mathrm{MeV}\) to \(C(5n_0) \approx 20\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For density oscillation amplitudes \(\Delta n/\bar{n} \sim 0.01\), the chemical potential amplitude \(\hat{\mu}_\Delta\) can become large enough to bridge gaps \(\Delta \sim 1\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- In the explicit hadronic matter example at \(T = 10^8\,\mathrm{K}\) with a proton gap \(\Delta_{p0} = 1\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(n \approx 1.3\, n_0\) and a neutron gap \(\Delta_{n0} \approx 0.1\,\mathrm{MeV}\) at \(n \approx 4\, n_0\), bridging the large proton gap at lower density boosts the results by up to \(10^{50}\).
- The bridging of the smaller neutron gap begins at \(\Delta n/\bar{n} \gtrsim 10^{-3}\), while the larger proton gap is bridged at \(\Delta n/\bar{n} \gtrsim 10^{-2}\).
- For hyperonic or quark matter with gaps \(\Delta \lesssim 0.01\,\mathrm{MeV}\), oscillations with amplitude as small as \(\Delta n/\bar{n} \lesssim 10^{-4}\) could bridge the gap.

## Context

This work extends the study of suprathermal enhancement of beta-equilibration rates in normal matter (Madsen 1992, Reisenegger 1994, Alford 2010) to superfluid/superconducting phases, where pairing gaps exponentially suppress transport at small amplitudes. The mechanism is relevant for unstable oscillations of rotating compact stars (f-modes, r-modes) and for large-amplitude oscillations from star quakes or tidal forces in neutron star mergers, where the suprathermal bulk viscosity could control oscillation amplitudes and emitted radiation.
