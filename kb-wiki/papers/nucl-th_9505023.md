# The longitudinal asymmetry of the $(e,e'p)$ missing momentum distribution as a signal of color transparency

> We use multiple scattering theory to evaluate the $Q^{2}$ dependence of the forward-backward asymmetry, $A_{z}$, of the missing momentum distribution in quasielstic $A(e,e'p)$ scattering, which is expected to be affected by color transparency. The novel features of our analysis are a consistent treatment of the structure of the ejectile state formed after absorption of the virtual photon by the struck proton and a careful evaluation of the background asymmetry induced by the nonvanishing real part of the proton-nucleon scattering amplitude. We find that the absolute magnitude of $A_{z}$ is dominated by this background at the $Q^{2}$'s attainable at the Continuous Electron Beam Accelerator Facility. However, the $Q^{2}$ dependence of the asymmetry is sensitive to the onset of color transparency, whose observation in high statistics experiments appears to be feasible, particularly using light targets such as $^{12}C$ or $^{16}O$.

## Key claim

The forward-backward asymmetry \(A_z\) of the missing momentum distribution in quasielastic \(A(e,e'p)\) scattering is dominated at accessible \(Q^2\) values by a background from the nonvanishing real part of the proton-nucleon scattering amplitude, but the \(Q^2\) dependence of the asymmetry remains sensitive to the onset of color transparency, particularly for light targets such as \(^{12}C\) or \(^{16}O\).

## Method

The authors used multiple scattering theory (MST) with a coupled-channel treatment of off-diagonal rescatterings to compute the forward-backward asymmetry \(A_z\) of the missing momentum distribution. They constructed the diffraction scattering matrix using an oscillator quark-diquark model of the proton, with the Pomeron contribution to the matrix elements obtained from a dipole cross section that vanishes at zero transverse separation, satisfying color transparency sum rules. The ejectile state formed after virtual photon absorption was treated as a compact state with transverse size \(\rho \sim 1/Q\), and the background asymmetry from the nonvanishing ratio of real to imaginary parts of the proton-nucleon scattering amplitude (\(\alpha_{pN}\)) was included for the first time in such calculations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}O\) at \(Q^2 \sim 2-5\) GeV\(^2\), inelastic intermediate states contribute only 25-30% of \(A_z\) for both kinematical windows in missing momentum \(p_z\) (0-200 MeV and 50-200 MeV).
- For \(^{40}Ca\) at the same \(Q^2\) range, inelastic intermediate states contribute only 10-15% of \(A_z\).
- The calculations cover the \(Q^2\) range \(2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 20\) GeV\(^2\), with the region \(Q^2 \lesssim 7\) GeV\(^2\) accessible at CEBAF.
- The oscillator frequency of the quark-diquark system was set to \(\omega_{qD} = 0.35\) GeV.
- The model yields \(\sigma_{diff}(pp)/\sigma_{el}(pp) \approx 0.25\), in agreement with experimental data.
- The number of included excited states was 4, and the number of off-diagonal rescatterings was 3; contributions from higher excitations and rescatterings with \(\nu > 3\) were negligible at \(Q^2 \lesssim 20\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color transparency in \((e,e'p)\) reactions by K1, JAz, and B1, which did not account for the large background asymmetry from the nonzero \(\alpha_{pN}\) and used a treatment of the ejectile state that the authors argue involves double counting. The paper also builds on the NE18 collaboration experiment at SLAC (1995), which found no substantial \(Q^2\) dependence of integrated nuclear transparency up to \(Q^2 \lesssim 7\) GeV\(^2\), and on the work of NSZ which first noted the sizeable asymmetry from \(\alpha_{pN}\). The results suggest that high-statistics measurements of the \(Q^2\) dependence of \(A_z\) at CEBAF, particularly on light targets like \(^{12}C\) or \(^{16}O\), could serve as a viable signature for the onset of color transparency.
