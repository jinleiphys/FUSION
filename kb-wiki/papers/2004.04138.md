# Deconfinement in the presence of a strong magnetic field

> We study the impact of a finite magnetic field on the deconfinement phase transition for heavy quarks by computing the fluctuations of the Polyakov loops. It is demonstrated that the explicit Z(3) breaking field increases with the magnetic field, leading to a decrease in the (pseudo) critical temperatures and a shrinking first-order region in the phase diagram. Phenomenological equations that capture the behaviors of the Z(3) breaking field at strong and weak magnetic fields for massive and massless quarks are given. Lastly, we explore the case of dynamical light quarks and demonstrate how an improved constituent quark mass function can enforce the correct magnetic field dependence of the deconfinement temperature in an effective model, as observed in Lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that an external magnetic field enhances the explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking induced by quarks, which leads to a decrease in the (pseudo)critical temperature for deconfinement and a shrinking of the first-order phase transition region in the phase diagram, provided the correct magnetic field dependence of the constituent quark mass is used in effective models.

## Method
The authors model the effective Polyakov loop potential as a sum of a pure glue potential (fitted to lattice QCD results) and a quark contribution that explicitly breaks Z(3) symmetry. The quark contribution is treated in a linear approximation for heavy quarks, and its strength is computed using the Landau-quantized dispersion relation for quarks in a constant magnetic field. The Polyakov loop expectation values and susceptibilities are then calculated from the gap equations and the curvature of the total potential.

## Key numbers
* The critical quark mass for a single flavor system at zero magnetic field is found to be $m_0 = 1.1$ GeV.
* The deconfinement critical endpoint temperature is constant at $T_{CEP} \approx 0.261$ GeV, independent of the magnetic field.
* The critical Z(3) breaking strength at the deconfinement critical endpoint is $h_c \approx 0.144$.
* For a single strange-quark flavor with mass $m_s = 1.4 m_0$, the deconfinement temperature extracted from the peak of the longitudinal susceptibility $\chi_L$ decreases from $0.26737$ GeV at $qB=0$ to $0.25579$ GeV at $qB=6 m_0^2$.

## Context
This work extends the model introduced by Lo et al. (2014) to include a finite magnetic field, providing a foundation for understanding the interplay between light quarks and gluons. It addresses the known problem in PNJL models, where a naive implementation predicts an increasing deconfinement temperature with magnetic field, contrary to lattice QCD results from Bali et al. (2012) and Endrodi et al. (2015). The study enables future lattice QCD investigations of Polyakov loop susceptibility ratios at finite magnetic fields and provides a framework for testing effective models against the correct magnetic field dependence of the deconfinement temperature.
