# A kinetic approach to eta' production from a CP-odd phase

> The production of (eta,eta')- mesons during the decay of a CP-odd phase is studied within an evolution operator approach. We derive a quantum kinetic equation starting from the Witten-DiVecchia-Veneziano Lagrangian for pseudoscalar mesons containing a U_A(1) symmetry breaking term. The non-linear vacuum mean field for the flavour singlet pseudoscalar meson is treated as a classical, self-interacting background field with fluctuations assumed to be small. The numerical solution provides the time evolution of momentum distribution function of produced eta'- mesons after a quench at the deconfinement phase transition. We show that the time evolution of the momentum distribution of the produced mesons depend strongly on the shape of the effective potential at the end of the quench, exhibiting either parametric or tachyonic resonances. Quantum statistical effects are essential and lead to a pronounced Bose enhancement of the low momentum states.

## Key claim

The production of $\eta'$ mesons during the decay of a CP-odd phase after a quench at the deconfinement phase transition depends strongly on the shape of the effective potential, exhibiting either parametric or tachyonic resonances; quantum statistical effects are essential and lead to a pronounced Bose enhancement of low momentum states, which the abstract notes but does not quantify as a factor of 4 enhancement for the considered case.

## Method

The authors start from the Witten-DiVecchia-Veneziano effective Lagrangian for pseudoscalar mesons, isolate the singlet component corresponding to the $\eta'$, and decompose the field into a classical vacuum mean field $\phi(t)$ and small quantum fluctuations $\chi$. They derive a quantum kinetic equation for the momentum distribution function ${\cal N}_+(\vec{k},t)$ using an evolution operator approach, solve the coupled system of the non-linear mean field equation and the kinetic equation numerically, and compare the full solution with a low-density approximation that neglects Bose statistics.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92$ MeV, with $f = \sqrt{3/2} f_\pi$.
- At zero temperature, the $U_A(1)$ anomaly parameter is $a \simeq 0.726$ GeV$^2$ and the mass parameter is $\mu^2 \simeq 0.171$ GeV$^2$, giving a vacuum ratio $(a/\mu^2)_{vac} \sim 4.24$.
- Four parameter sets are studied: Set I ($a/\mu^2$ at vacuum value), Set II ($a/\mu^2$ reduced to 1/2 of vacuum), Set III ($a/\mu^2$ reduced to 1/4 of vacuum), and Set IV ($a/\mu^2 = 0$, leading to tachyonic modes).
- For the momentum distribution at a given time, the full non-Markovian solution shows Bose enhancement by a factor of 4 for low momentum states compared to the low-density approximation.
- The time scale for the particle production process is $1 - 10$ fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic formulation of spontaneous pair creation in strong background fields, previously developed for Abelian fields in studies such as the Schwinger mechanism, to the non-Abelian context of $\eta'$ production from a CP-odd phase in QCD. It complements the work of Dahr (2001) which estimated decay rates of metastable states, by providing the full time evolution of the momentum distribution function. The approach enables future quantitative studies of $\eta'$ production signatures in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, and the technique linking effective Lagrangians to kinetic theory is generalizable to other quantum field theory applications.
