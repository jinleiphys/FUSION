# Colour-singlet clustering of partons and recombination model for hadronization of quark-gluon plasma

> $SU(N_c)$ colour-singlet restriction, along with flavour and spin symmetry, on thermal partonic ensemble is shown to recombine the partons with internal colour structure into colour-singlet multi-quark clusters which can be identified with various hadronic modes at a given temperature. This provides a possible basis for recombination model for hadronization of quark-gluon plasma. This also leads to a natural explanation for the ratio of (anti)protons to pions and the quark number scaling of the elliptic flow coefficient in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Colour-singlet clustering of partons and recombination model for hadronization of quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The colour-singlet projection operator applied to a thermal ensemble of free quarks and antiquarks, combined with flavour and spin symmetry, spontaneously reorganizes the partition function into Boltzmann factors of colour-singlet multi-quark clusters that can be identified with hadronic modes, providing a symmetry-based foundation for the recombination model of hadronization, but only under the assumption that mutual interactions among partons are neglected and that the system has first reached thermal equilibrium before interactions are slowly turned off.

## Method

The authors construct the colour-singlet partition function for a gas of quarks and antiquarks by applying the SU(N_c) colour-singlet projection operator to the grand canonical ensemble, using the Weyl-reduced Haar measure and diagonalizing the link variable U(g) in the maximal abelian subgroup (Cartan space) of SU(N_c). They evaluate the fermionic determinants in Fock space, perform the group integration exactly using orthonormal polynomial methods for N_c=3 and N_f=2 massless quarks in the infinite volume limit, and then extract the probability distribution of a single parton in a given multi-quark mode to derive hadron spectra and elliptic flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Proton-to-pion ratio at central rapidity for transverse momentum P_perp >> 3 zeta T is given by (5/4) exp(mu_B/T), independent of P_perp, where mu_B = 3 mu is the baryonic chemical potential.
- The energy cut-off for the expansion of the logarithm is epsilon > zeta T with zeta ~ 1.7 for N_f=2, giving a threshold of >= 2 zeta T for mesonic modes and >= 3 zeta T for baryonic modes.
- The weight factor for low-lying mesonic modes (m=1, b=0) is C_10 = 16; for low-lying baryonic/antibaryonic modes (m=0, b=1) it is C_01 = 20, exact for SU(2) flavour and SU(2) spin symmetry.
- The elliptic flow coefficient for the mb-th hadronic mode scales as (v_2)_mb = (alpha/T)(1-v)(2m+3b) p_perp, where alpha ~ 4% for non-central Au-Au collisions at RHIC.
- The proton-to-pion ratio from the total hadron number is ~0.6, and antiproton-to-pion is ~0.3, with mu_B/T ~ 0.33 at RHIC, consistent with PHENIX data.
- The recombination region of validity is 2 GeV/c <= P_perp <= 4 GeV/c; scaling deviates for P_perp < 1.5 GeV/c due to mass ordering.

## Context

This work extends the recombination model approach of Fries et al. (2003) and Greco et al. (2003), which treated hadronization via recombination of thermal and minijet partons under a sudden approximation, by showing that the recombination structure arises naturally from colour-singlet projection of the thermal parton ensemble based on symmetry considerations alone. The authors obtain a proton-to-pion ratio of (5/4)exp(mu_B/T), differing from the (5/3)exp(mu_B/T) found by Fries et al. (2003), and provide a symmetry-based explanation for the quark number scaling of elliptic flow observed at RHIC. The work enables a first-principles connection between the statistical mechanics of colour-singlet ensembles and phenomenological recombination models, though a full dynamical QCD treatment of hadronization remains an open question.
