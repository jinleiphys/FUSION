# Excitation modes of $^{11}$Li at E$_x \sim$ 1.3 MeV from proton collisions

> The cross section for p-$^{11}$Li inelastic scattering at 68 MeV/u is evaluated using the Multiple Scattering expansion of the total Transition amplitude (MST) formalism, and compared with the breakup in the shakeoff approximation. Three different potential models for $^{11}$Li are used to calculate the $^{11}$Li(p,p$'$) continuum excitations, and all show peaks below 3 MeV of excitation energy, both in resonant and some non-resonant channels. In the most realistic model of $^{11}$Li, there is a strong dipole contribution associated with attractive but not a fully-fledged resonant phase shifts, and some evidence for a J$_{nn}^π= 0_2^+$ resonant contribution. These together form a pronounced peak at around 1--2 MeV excitation, in agreement with experiment, and this supports the use of the MST as an adequate formalism to study excited modes of two-neutron nuclear halos.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the low-energy peak in p-\(^{11}\)Li inelastic scattering at around 1–2 MeV excitation can be reproduced by a three-body model of \(^{11}\)Li that includes a strong dipole (\(J_{nn}^\pi = 1^-\)) contribution associated with attractive but not fully-fledged resonant phase shifts, together with some evidence for a \(J_{nn}^\pi = 0_2^+\) resonant contribution; the peak is not a pure resonance but a combination of non-resonant and resonant mechanisms.

## Method

The authors used the Multiple Scattering expansion of the total Transition amplitude (MST) formalism to calculate the differential cross section for p-\(^{11}\)Li inelastic scattering at 68 MeV/u, treating \(^{11}\)Li as a three-body system (\(^9\)Li + n + n). They employed three different potential models for \(^{11}\)Li (labeled S, P0, and P2) to generate ground-state and continuum wave functions via Faddeev equations, and computed the inelastic cross section by summing contributions from dipole (\(J_{nn}^\pi = 1^-\)), second \(0^+\) (\(J_{nn}^\pi = 0_2^+\)), spin-dipole (\(0^-\)), and spinflip (\(1^+\)) excitations in a single-scattering approximation. Results were compared with experimental data and with the shakeoff approximation (SA), which includes only proton-core scattering and uses closure.

## Key numbers

- The P2 model predicts a low-lying resonance at \(E^*_f = 0.5\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 0.6\) MeV for \(J_{nn}^\pi = 0_2^+\).
- The experimental data from Korsheninnikov et al. (1997) for p-\(^{11}\)Li inelastic scattering at 68 MeV/u show a pronounced peak at around 1–2 MeV excitation energy.
- The MST calculations with the P2 model reproduce the shape, position, and magnitude of the low-energy peak up to about 5 MeV excitation; above \(\sim\)5 MeV, all models fail to reproduce the cross sections.
- The shakeoff approximation (SA) with \(\mathcal{R}=1\) gives cross sections more than twice the experimental magnitude in the angular region where the core scattering is well described.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of \(^{11}\)Li excitations by Thompson and Zhukov (1994) and Thompson et al. (1998), which predicted a possible \(0_2^+\) resonance, and by Karataglidis et al. (2000), who used the shakeoff approximation. The MST formalism improves on the shakeoff approach by including scattering from both the core and valence neutrons and by avoiding the closure approximation. The results support the use of MST to study excited modes of two-neutron halos and provide evidence for a strong dipole peak (not a full resonance) and a \(0_2^+\) resonance contribution, helping to clarify contradictory experimental findings on a low-lying state at \(E^* \sim 1.3\) MeV.
