# Non-equilibrium attractor in high-temperature QCD plasmas

> We establish the existence of a far-from-equilibrium attractor in weakly-coupled gauge theory undergoing 0+1d Bjorken expansion which goes beyond the energy-momentum tensor to the detailed form of the one-particle distribution function. We then demonstrate that the dynamics can be re-scaled at intermediate times and represented by universal exponents. Finally, we assess different procedures for reconstructing the full one-particle distribution function from the energy-momentum tensor along the attractor and discuss implications for the freeze-out procedure used in the phenomenological analysis of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

# Non-equilibrium attractor in high-temperature QCD plasmas

## Key claim
The existence of a far-from-equilibrium attractor in weakly-coupled gauge theory undergoing 0+1d Bjorken expansion is established not only for the energy-momentum tensor but also for the detailed form of the one-particle distribution function, with the attractor behavior emerging at roughly τ/τ_R ~ 0.5 and showing insensitivity to both initial anisotropy and initial occupancy.

## Method
The authors numerically solve the QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) Boltzmann equation, which includes elastic 2↔2 scattering and effective inelastic 1↔2 processes with dynamical screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. The one-particle distribution function is discretized on an optimized momentum-space grid, and Monte Carlo sampling is used to compute the collisional integrals. Simulations are initialized with either spheroidally-deformed thermal (RS) initial conditions or non-thermal color-glass-condensate (CGC) inspired initial conditions, varying both the initial anisotropy parameter ξ₀ and the initialization time τ₀.

## Key numbers
- Attractor convergence occurs at roughly τ/τ_R ~ 0.5, where different solutions collapse to a universal curve regardless of initial conditions.
- The scaling exponent for the turning point time ω_nm ~ ω₀^{B_nm} yields B_nm ≈ 0.51845 - 0.0285393 n + 0.0452043 m, with B_nm approximately 1/2 with small corrections.
- The scaling exponent for the moment magnitude \overline{M}^{nm} ~ ω₀^{A_nm} yields A_nm = -0.0604726 (2 - n) + 0.340507 m, showing strong dependence on m and weak dependence on n.
- At τ ~ τ_R, where P_L/P_L^{eq} ~ 65%, the moment M^{11} shows approximately 20% disagreement between EKT and both linearized ansatze for distribution function reconstruction.
- At late times τ > 5 τ_R, low-order moments are described within a few percent by all reconstruction prescriptions, while some discrepancy remains even at τ ~ 20 τ_R for the quadratic ansatz.

## Context
This work extends the study of non-equilibrium attractors in QCD kinetic theory beyond the energy-momentum tensor to higher moments of the distribution function, building on prior attractor studies in relativistic kinetic theory (Strickland 2018) and the bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier 2000). The results enable improved freeze-out procedures for phenomenological analysis of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, particularly for smaller systems such as peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions and proton-nucleus collisions, where the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) freeze-out ansatz is favored over linearized viscous corrections.
