# QCD phase diagram with isospin chemical potential

> In this contribution we investigate the phase diagram of QCD in the presence of an isospin chemical potential. To alleviate the infrared problems of the theory associated with pion condensation, we introduce the pionic source as an infrared regulator. We discuss various methods to extrapolate the results to vanishing pionic source, including a novel method based on the singular value spectrum of the massive Dirac operator, a leading-order reweighting and a spline Monte-Carlo fit. Our main results concern the phase transition boundary between the normal and the pion condensation phases and the chiral/deconfinement transition temperature as a function of the chemical potential. In addition, we perform a quantitative comparison between our direct results and a Taylor-expansion obtained at zero chemical potential to assess the applicability range of the latter.

### Key claim
The paper maps the QCD phase diagram in the presence of an isospin chemical potential using lattice simulations at physical quark masses, but the results for the phase boundary and crossover temperature are obtained after extrapolating an infrared regulator (pionic source) to zero, which introduces systematic uncertainties that the authors attempt to control via multiple extrapolation methods.

### Method
The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with three flavors of stout-smeared rooted staggered quarks and tree-level Symanzik-improved gluons on \(N_s^3 \times N_t\) lattices (primarily \(24^3 \times 6\), with additional \(N_t = 8, 10, 12\) for the phase boundary). They introduce a pionic source \(\lambda\) as an infrared regulator to handle the Goldstone mode associated with pion condensation, then extrapolate observables to \(\lambda \to 0\) using three techniques: a spline Monte-Carlo fit, a novel method based on the singular value spectrum of the massive Dirac operator (valence improvement), and leading-order reweighting to correct for sea-quark effects.

### Key numbers
- The threshold for pion condensation at zero temperature is \(\mu_I = m_\pi/2\) with \(m_\pi = 135\ \text{MeV}\).
- The chiral crossover temperature at \(\mu_I = 0\) on the \(24^3 \times 6\) lattice is \(T_c(0) = 150(3)(3)\ \text{MeV}\), slightly below the continuum result of \(155(3)(3)\ \text{MeV}\) from Borsanyi (2010).
- The phase boundary of the pion condensation phase follows \(\mu_I/m_\pi = 0.5\) up to \(T \approx 130\ \text{MeV}\) and flattens at \(T \approx 160\ \text{MeV}\), above which no pion condensate forms for the chemical potentials studied.
- The curvature of the crossover line gives \(\kappa \approx 0.015\) when translating to baryon chemical potential normalization.
- For \(T = 124\ \text{MeV}\), the Taylor expansion of the isospin density agrees with direct results up to the phase boundary at \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.5\); for \(T = 162\ \text{MeV}\), agreement with \(\mathcal{O}(\mu_I^3)\) expansion holds for all \(\mu_I\) considered, with \(\mathcal{O}(\mu_I^3)\) contributions becoming distinguishable at \(\mu_I/T \approx 0.5\).

### Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of QCD with isospin chemical potential by Kogut (2002, 2004), deForcrand (2007), Detmold (2012), and Endrodi (2014), using physical quark masses and an improved staggered action for the first time. The results enable a direct test of the Taylor expansion method (commonly used for baryon chemical potential, e.g., by Borsanyi (2011)) against non-perturbative data, showing its validity up to the pion condensation boundary. The novel singular-value-based extrapolation technique may inform future studies of the sign-problem-affected baryon density regime.
