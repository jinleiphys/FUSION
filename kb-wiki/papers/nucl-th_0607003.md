# Final State Interaction Effects in the $e^+e^-\to N\bar{N}$ Process near Threshold

> We use the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential for explanation of experimental data in the process $e^+e^- \to p\bar p$ near threshold. It is shown that the cross section and the electromagnetic form factors are very sensitive to the parameters of the potential. It turns out that final-state interaction due to slightly modified absorptive part of the potential allows us to reproduce available experimental data. We also demonstrated that the cross section in $n\bar{n}$ channel is larger than that in $p\bar{p}$ one, and their ratio is almost energy independent up to 2.2 GeV.

## Key claim

Using the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, the authors show that final-state interaction can reproduce the observed cross section and electromagnetic form factor ratio in \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\) near threshold, but only if the energy dependence of the absorptive part of the triplet potential is reduced by a factor of 8 to 10 relative to the original Paris potential.

## Method

The authors constructed the amplitude for \(e^+e^- \to N\bar{N}\) near threshold using a nonrelativistic approximation with Dirac form factors, then incorporated final-state interaction via the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential. They solved the Schrödinger equation for the \(N\bar{N}\) wave function in coordinate space, including coupled channels for \(L\) and \(L+2\) states due to tensor forces, and expressed the observable electric and magnetic form factors in terms of the radial wave functions at the origin. The free parameters \(\tilde{F}^I_i\) (smooth form factors without final-state interaction) were fitted to the cross section data up to 100 MeV of proton kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- The original Paris \(N\bar{N}\) potential yields a cross section that is more than one order of magnitude smaller than observed at 10 MeV of proton center-of-mass kinetic energy.
- To reproduce data, the energy dependence of the absorptive part of the triplet potential must be decreased by a factor of 8 to 10.
- The cross section in the \(n\bar{n}\) channel is larger than that in the \(p\bar{p}\) channel, and their ratio is almost energy independent up to 2.2 GeV.
- The ratio \(|G_E/G_M|\) for the proton shows strong energy dependence near threshold due to D-wave contribution, which the authors reproduce simultaneously with the cross section.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of final-state interaction in \(N\bar{N}\) systems, particularly the Paris potential models (Paris 1994, Paris 1999) and the Jülich model for \(N\bar{N}\) interaction. The authors note that Sibirtsev (2006) calculated the \(^3S_1\) partial wave contribution to the \(e^+e^- \to p\bar{p}\) cross section using the Jülich model but neglected the \(^3D_1\) partial wave, preventing them from reproducing the ratio \(|G_E/G_M|\). The present work enables improved determination of the energy-dependent absorptive part of the \(N\bar{N}\) potential in the \(J=S=1\) channel, and provides predictions for the \(n\bar{n}\) channel that can be tested by future experiments.
