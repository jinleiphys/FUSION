# Energy Dependent Growth of Nucleon and Inclusive Charged Hadron Distributions

> In the Color Glass Condensate formalism, charged hadron p_{T} distributions in p+p collisions are studied by considering an energy-dependent broadening of nucleon's density distribution. Then, in the Glasma flux tube picture, the n-particle multiplicity distributions at different pseudo-rapidity ranges are investigated. Both of the theoretical results show good agreement with the recent experimental data from ALICE and CMS at \sqrt{s}=0.9, 2.36, 7 TeV. The predictive results for p_{T} and multiplicity distributions in p+p and p+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider are also given in this paper.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate formalism, when augmented with an energy-dependent broadening of the nucleon's density distribution in position space, reproduces charged hadron transverse momentum spectra and multiplicity distributions in p+p collisions at LHC energies, but the KLR-AdS/CFT model only agrees with data at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV and at small \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) because it is valid only at small Bjorken-\(x\) (\(x<10^{-4}\)).

## Method

The authors computed charged hadron \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) distributions in p+p collisions using the KLN and KLR-AdS/CFT unintegrated gluon distribution functions within the Color Glass Condensate framework, incorporating an energy-dependent Gaussian width for the proton density distribution and a saturation scale that depends on the 3-dimensional rms proton radius. They then derived \(n\)-particle multiplicity distributions by convolving a negative binomial distribution (with parameters \(\bar{n}\) and \(k\) obtained from the Glasma flux tube picture) with the impact-parameter-dependent probability for an inelastic collision.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{\mathrm{in}}\) is taken as 52, 60, 70.45, 72, and 76.3 mb at \(\sqrt{s}=0.9, 2.36, 7, 8.8, 14\) TeV, respectively.
- The Gaussian width of the proton density distribution is given by \(B(\sqrt{s}) = \sigma_{\mathrm{in}}(\sqrt{s}) / 14.30\) fm\(^2\).
- The saturation scale parameters are \(Q_0^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\lambda = 0.288\) for the KLN model.
- The parameter \(\xi = 0.51\) in the KLN unintegrated gluon distribution is obtained by a \(\chi^2\) fit to experimental data.
- The parameter \(\zeta = 0.05\) in the Glasma flux tube expression for \(k\) is extracted from a fit to data at \(\sqrt{s}=2.36\) TeV and used for all other energies.
- The nuclear density for Pb uses \(\rho_0 = 0.1612\), \(R = 6.62\) fm, and \(a = 0.545\) fm in the Woods-Saxon form.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach of Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN model, 2001) and the KLR-AdS/CFT model of Kovchegov, Lu, and Rezaeian (2009) by incorporating an energy-dependent broadening of the nucleon's density distribution, as suggested by earlier work on gluon saturation. The results enable predictions for charged hadron \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) and multiplicity distributions in p+p and p+Pb collisions at LHC energies (5.02, 8.8, and 14 TeV) that can be tested by future LHC experiments.
