# Obtaining information on short-range correlations from inclusive electron scattering

> In view of recent data from the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab) on inclusive electron scattering off nuclei at high momentum transfer (Q^2 > 1 GeV^2) and their current analysis, it is shown that, if the scaling variable is properly chosen, the analysis in terms of scaling functions can provide useful information on Short Range Correlations (SRC). This is demonstrated by introducing a new relativistic scaling variable that incorporates the momentum dependence of the excitation energy of the (A-1)system, with the resulting scaling function being closely related to the longitudinal momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a properly chosen relativistic scaling variable, \(y_{CW}\), which incorporates the momentum dependence of the average excitation energy of the residual \((A-1)\) system, allows inclusive electron scattering data to be directly linked to longitudinal momentum distributions and thus to short-range correlations (SRC), minimizing the binding corrections that plague the standard \(y\)-scaling approach.

## Method

The authors introduce a new relativistic scaling variable \(y_{CW}\) by inserting into the energy conservation equation the average excitation energy of the \((A-1)\) system, \(\langle E_{A-1}^*(k) \rangle\), calculated from realistic spectral functions for nuclear matter and \(^3He\) and parameterized by the few-nucleon correlation (FNC) model of Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996). They then analyze experimental inclusive electron scattering data for \(^4He\), \(^{12}C\), and \(^{56}Fe\) from JLab, plotting the experimental scaling function \(F^A(q,Y)\) versus both the standard variable \(y\) and the new variable \(y_{CW}\), and comparing the results to theoretical longitudinal momentum distributions \(f^A(Y)\).

## Key numbers

*   The binding correction ratio \(B^A/F^A\) for \(^{12}C\) at \(y \approx -400\) MeV/c is about 0.6 for the standard variable \(y\), but is reduced to near zero for the new variable \(y_{CW}\) (Fig. 2).
*   At high values of \(|y_{CW}| \gtrsim 200-300\) MeV/c, the scaling function for complex nuclei scales to that of the deuteron with scaling constants \(C_4 = 2.7\), \(C_{12} = 4.0\), and \(C_{56} = 4.6\) (Fig. 4).
*   Final state interactions (FSI) have relevant effects on the scaling functions up to \(Q^2 \simeq 4-5\) GeV\(^2\) (Fig. 4).
*   The average excitation energy parameterization for nuclear matter gives \(b_{NM} = 37.3\) MeV and \(c_{NM} = 0.04\); for \(^3He\), \(b_3 = -2.94\) MeV and \(c_3 = -0.03\) (Eq. 13).

## Context

This work extends the traditional Y-scaling approach of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991) and Day et al. (1990) by addressing the long-standing problem that the standard scaling variable \(y\) does not account for the high excitation energies of correlated nucleons, making the scaling function differ from the longitudinal momentum distribution. The new variable \(y_{CW}\), following suggestions by Ciofi degli Atti and West (1995) and Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1996, 1997), minimizes these binding corrections and directly links inclusive data to SRC, enabling the extraction of high-momentum components of nucleon momentum distributions and providing scaling constants that agree with the theoretical predictions of Frankfurt et al. (1993) and the experimental ratio analysis of the JLab E02-019 collaboration.
