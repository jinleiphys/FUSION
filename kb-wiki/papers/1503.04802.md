# Tetraquark Cusp Effects from Diquark Pair Production

> We present the first study of the cusp effect (the movement of resonant poles due to the proximity of multiparticle thresholds) caused by the creation of diquark-antidiquark pairs. The cusp profile for such states is obtained from constituent counting rules. We compare the effectiveness of diquark cusps in moving resonant poles with that from a phenomenological form commonly used for meson-pair creation, and find that mesons tend to be more effective at lower energies $({\it e.g.}$, the $K\overline{K}$ threshold), while diquarks tend to be more effective at the charm [$X(3872)$] scales and above.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first dynamical study of the cusp effect (the movement of resonant poles due to the proximity of multiparticle thresholds) caused by the creation of diquark-antidiquark pairs, finding that meson-pair thresholds are more effective at dragging resonant poles at lower energies (e.g., the \(K\overline{K}\) threshold), while diquark-pair thresholds become more effective at the charm scale [\(X(3872)\)] and above, but only if the diquarks achieve sufficient physical separation (estimated at about 1 fm) to be treated as identifiable particles with well-defined masses.

## Method

The authors use dispersion relation techniques to compute the real part of the self-energy function \(\Pi(s)\) for a resonant propagator, incorporating the imaginary part from two-body phase space and form factors for pair production. For meson-pair thresholds, they adopt an exponential form factor \(F_i^2(s) = \exp(-\mu_i \rho_i^2/(1-\rho_i^2))\) with \(\mu_i = s_{\mathrm{th},i} R^2/12\) and \(R = 0.6\) fm; for diquark-pair thresholds, they use a power-law form factor \(F_i(s) = (s_{\mathrm{th},i}/s)^3\) derived from constituent counting rules for tetraquarks. They then compare the resulting cusp profiles (the behavior of \(\mathrm{Re}\,\Pi(s)\) near threshold) for the two cases across different heavy-quark systems.

## Key numbers

* For the \(X(3872)\) system, the mass difference \(m_{X(3872)} - m_{D^{*0}} - m_{D^0} = -0.11 \pm 0.21\) MeV, showing the state lies extremely close to the \(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\) threshold.
* The diquark form factor scales as \(F_i(s) = (s_{\mathrm{th},i}/s)^3\) at large \(s\), following from constituent counting rules for tetraquarks with \(n=4\) constituents.
* The mesonic form factor parameter \(\mu_i = s_{\mathrm{th},i} R^2/12\) equals 0.738 for the \(K\overline{K}\) case with \(R = 0.6\) fm, corresponding to \(\beta_i = 1.14\) GeV in the alternative exponential parametrization.
* The estimated diquark separation required for a well-defined threshold is about 1 fm, based on the calculated electromagnetic charge radius of the \(D^+\) meson (0.43 fm).
* For the \(X(3872)\) system with \(m_{1,i}=m_{D^0}\) and \(m_{2,i}=m_{\bar{D}^{*0}}\), the mass asymmetry parameter \(\epsilon = ((m_{1,i}-m_{2,i})/(m_{1,i}+m_{2,i}))^2 = 1.35 \times 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the cusp-effect formalism originally developed by Tornqvist (1995) and first applied to heavy exotic hadrons by Bugg (2008), and it builds on the diquark-antidiquark tetraquark model proposed by Maiani (2004). The authors contrast their approach with the molecular (meson-meson) interpretation of tetraquarks and with the hadro-charmonium model of Voloshin (2007). The results enable future phenomenological studies of how diquark-pair thresholds affect the masses of tetraquark resonances, particularly for the \(X(3872)\) and \(Z_c(4430)\) states, and complement a companion paper by Brodsky (2015) on the direct use of cross-section scalings.
