# Quark exchange and quark dynamics in diffractive electroproduction

> A Dyson-Schwinger-based model of pomeron exchange is employed to calculate diffractive rho-, phi- and J/psi-meson electroproduction cross sections. It is shown that the magnitude of the current-quark mass m_f of the quark and antiquark inside the produced vector meson determines the onset of the asymptotic-q^2 power-law behavior of the cross section, and how correlated quark-exchanges are included to provide a complete picture of the diffractive electroproduction of light vector mesons applicable over all energies and photon momenta q^2.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the current-quark mass \(m_f\) of the quark and antiquark inside the produced vector meson determines the onset of the asymptotic \(q^{-4}\) power-law behavior of the diffractive electroproduction cross section, with larger current-quark masses postponing this onset to larger values of \(q^2\); the model is claimed to be applicable over all energies and photon momenta \(q^2\) only when correlated quark exchanges are included at lower energies.

## Method

A Dyson-Schwinger-based model of pomeron exchange was employed, using dressed-quark propagators that incorporate confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, a parameter-free nonperturbative quark-photon vertex determined from the Ward-Takahashi identity, and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes fitted to low-energy observables. The quark-nucleon pomeron-exchange interaction was fixed from fits to \(\pi N\) and \(KN\) elastic scattering, and the \(q^2\) dependence of the electroproduction cross section arose entirely from the Schwinger functions in the quark-loop integration. For lower energies, correlated quark exchanges were included by reorganizing nonlocal interactions into sums of effective-meson exchanges, such as effective-\(\pi\) exchange.

## Key numbers

- The \(u\)-quark current-quark mass used was \(m_u = 5.5\) MeV; fictitious masses 10 and 25 times larger were tested to study the onset of asymptotic behavior.
- The \(\rho\)-meson electroproduction cross section at \(q^2 = 0\) is two orders of magnitude larger than that of the \(J/\psi\) meson, but they become nearly equal at larger \(q^2\).
- The energy at which pomeron exchange and effective-meson exchanges each contribute about half of the photoproduction cross section is \(W \approx 6\) GeV for \(\rho\) mesons and \(W \approx 3\) GeV for \(\phi\) mesons.
- The effective-\(\pi NN\) form factor was parameterized as \(F_{\pi NN}(t) = (1 - t/\Lambda^2)^{-1}\) with \(\Lambda \approx 1\) GeV.
- The \(\gamma\rho\pi\)-transition form factor is normalized so that \(f^{\gamma\rho\pi}(q^2=0, p^2=-m_\rho^2, -t=-m_\pi^2) = 1\).
- The dressed-quark propagator parameter \(\lambda = 0.566\) GeV was used.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger-based pomeron-exchange model developed by Pichowsky and Lee (1997) for diffractive vector meson electroproduction, and incorporates the dressed-quark propagator parametrization from Burden, Roberts, and Thomson (1996). The inclusion of correlated quark exchanges via effective-meson fields, following the approach of Tandy (1997), enables the model to describe diffractive electroproduction at lower energies where meson exchanges become important. This framework enables future calculations of \(\rho\)- and \(\phi\)-meson electroproduction at all energies and \(q^2\), and provides a means to probe nonperturbative gluon dynamics underlying pomeron exchange at facilities like TJNAF.
