# Double polarization asymmetry as a possible filter for $Θ^+$'s parity

> We present an analysis of the Beam-Target double polarization asymmetry for the photoproduction of $Θ^+$ in $γn\to Θ^+ K^-$. We show that this quantity can serve as a filter for the determination of the $Θ^+$'s spin-parity assignment near threshold. It is highly selective between $1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$ configurations due to dynamical reasons.

## Key claim

The beam-target double polarization asymmetry \( \mathcal{D}_{xz} \) in the reaction \( \gamma n \to \Theta^+ K^- \) can serve as a parity filter for the \( \Theta^+ \) near threshold, distinguishing between \( 1/2^+ \) and \( 1/2^- \) assignments because the asymmetry exhibits a similar \( \sin\theta_{\text{c.m.}} \) angular dependence but opposite sign for the two parity cases, a feature that holds even when \( K^* \) exchange contributions are included.

## Method

The authors calculated the beam-target double polarization asymmetry \( \mathcal{D}_{xz} \) for \( \Theta^+ \) photoproduction using a Born approximation model with effective Lagrangians for both \( 1/2^+ \) and \( 1/2^- \) parity assignments. They included contact, \( t \)-channel kaon exchange, \( s \)-channel nucleon exchange, and \( u \)-channel \( \Theta^+ \) exchange amplitudes, as well as \( K^* \) exchange contributions, and expressed the asymmetry in terms of CGLN-like amplitudes to isolate the dynamical differences between the two parity cases.

## Key numbers

- The \( \Theta^+ \) mass is taken as 1.535 GeV and its width as \( < 25 \) MeV, with a nominal width of \( \Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to K^+ n} = 10 \) MeV used in the calculations.
- For the \( 1/2^+ \) case, the coupling constant \( g_{\Theta NK} = 2.96 \) corresponds to \( \Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to K^+ n} = 10 \) MeV; the \( \Theta^+ \) magnetic moment is \( \mu_\theta = 0.13 (e_0 / 2 M_\Theta) \).
- For the \( 1/2^- \) case, the coupling constant \( g_{\Theta NK} = 0.61 \) corresponds to \( \Gamma_{\Theta^+ \to K^+ n} = 10 \) MeV.
- The \( K^* K \gamma \) coupling is \( g_{K^* K \gamma} = 0.744 \), determined from the \( K^{*\pm} \to K^\pm \gamma \) decay width of 50 keV.
- The neutron anomalous magnetic moment is \( \kappa_n = -1.91 \); the \( \rho NN \) tensor coupling used as a reference is \( |\kappa_\rho| = 3.71 \).
- Calculations are shown at center-of-mass energies \( W = 2.1 \) GeV and \( W = 2.5 \) GeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of \( \Theta^+ \) photoproduction cross sections (e.g., by Hyodo (2003), Nam (2003), and Liu and Ko (2003)) by focusing on a polarization observable that is less sensitive to model-dependent form factors. The proposed asymmetry measurement complements earlier single-polarization analyses by Zhao (2003) and the parity-determination method proposed by Thomas, Hicks, and Hosaka (2003) and further detailed by Hanhart (2003). The results enable experimental tests at facilities such as Spring-8, JLab, ELSA, and ESRF, and highlight the need to account for possible spin-3/2 pentaquark partners as studied by Dudek and Close (2003).
