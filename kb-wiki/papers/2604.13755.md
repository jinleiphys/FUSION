# Scattering lengths beyond the nuclear scale and the Efimov effect

> The interaction of neutrons and nuclei at low energies may potentially lead to scattering lengths several orders of magnitude larger than the effective range of the interaction, well beyond the nuclear scale. If such cases existed, they could lead to the observation of the Efimov effect in nuclei, a remarkable universal phenomenon that has been observed only in atoms. The interaction parameters of neutrons scattering off unstable nuclei can be explored in neutron-nucleus systems created after the fast removal of a few nucleons from a slightly heavier beam. The case of the $^{17}$B-$n$ system is considered, and the implications of its potentially huge scattering length on the structure of $^{19}$B as a $^{17}$B-$n$-$n$ Efimov trimer are discussed.

# Scattering lengths beyond the nuclear scale and the Efimov effect

## Key claim

If a neutron-nucleus system exists with a scattering length several orders of magnitude larger than the effective range (well beyond the nuclear scale), the three-body system of that nucleus plus two neutrons could exhibit the Efimov effect in nuclei. The paper considers the specific case of the ¹⁷B–n system and argues that preliminary data point toward an unprecedented |aₛ|/rₑ ∼ 100, which would lie within the range of existence of one or two Efimov trimers in ¹⁹B, but this remains to be confirmed.

## Method

The author derives an analytical expression for the relative-energy spectrum of a neutron–nucleus system produced via fast few-nucleon removal from a heavier projectile, using the sudden approximation and the asymptotic form of the initial bound-state wave function. The cross-section depends on three parameters: the neutron separation energy in the projectile (Sₙ), the scattering length (aₛ), and the effective range (rₑ) of the final-state neutron–nucleus interaction. An experimental campaign at RIKEN using the SAMURAI setup measured ¹⁷B–n coincidences from several beams (¹⁹C, ¹⁹B, ²⁰C, ²⁰N, ²¹N, ²²N) at 230 MeV/N, with flat acceptance in the first 2 MeV and resolution FWHM ∼ 0.4√E MeV, to extract the scattering parameters by fitting the observed energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- The neutron–neutron scattering parameters are (aₛ, rₑ) = (−18.5, 2.8) fm, the only known neutron–nucleus system with |aₛ|/rₑ ∼ 7.
- For the ¹⁷B–n system, a previous measurement at MSU (Spyrou 2010) gave an upper limit aₛ < −50 fm, with a preferred value of aₛ = −100 fm.
- The scaling factor for three identical bosons is λ ≈ 22.7; for the Li–Cs–Cs system the measured scaling factor was λ = 4.9(4), matching the predicted value of 4.88.
- The number of Efimov trimers is given by N ≈ (1/π) ln(|aₛ|/rₑ); for the ¹⁷B–n system preliminary results point to |aₛ|/rₑ ∼ 100.
- The neutron separation energies of the projectiles used range from Sₙ = 0.09 MeV (¹⁹B) to Sₙ = 4.61 MeV (²¹N), with ¹⁹C at Sₙ = 0.58 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for Efimov states from atomic physics, where the effect was first confirmed in Cs atoms (Kraemer et al. 2006) and later with scaling factor measurements in Li–Cs mixtures (Tung et al. 2014), into nuclear physics, where the effect has remained elusive since Efimov’s original 1970 prediction. The paper builds on a pioneering measurement of the ¹⁷B–n system by Spyrou et al. (2010) at MSU, which found a sharp increase in cross-section toward zero energy compatible with aₛ = −100 fm but could only set an upper limit. The RIKEN campaign described here aims to determine both aₛ and rₑ for the ¹⁷B–n system using multiple reaction channels, which would enable a test of whether ¹⁹B states correspond to Efimov trimers as suggested by Emiko (2019). If confirmed, this would be the first observation of the Efimov effect in nuclear systems.
