# Charge form factor of $π$ and $K$ mesons

> The charge form factor of $π$ and $K$ mesons is evaluated adopting a relativistic constituent quark model based on the light-front formalism. The relevance of the high-momentum components of the meson wave function, for values of the momentum transfer accessible to $CEBAF$ energies, is illustrated. The predictions for the elastic form factor of $π$ and $K$ mesons are compared with the results of different relativistic approaches, showing that the measurements of the pion and kaon form factors planned at $CEBAF$ could provide information for discriminating among various models of the meson structure.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the high-momentum components of the meson wave function (for momenta above 1 GeV/c) are essential for determining the pion and kaon charge form factors at momentum transfers accessible to CEBAF (Q² up to a few (GeV/c)²), contrary to the claim of Schlumpf (1994) that the form factor is insensitive to a large class of wave functions. The authors show that their wave functions, which are eigenfunctions of a mass operator reproducing meson mass spectra, do not belong to the limited class considered by Schlumpf (1994).

## Method

The authors evaluate the charge form factor of π and K mesons using a relativistic constituent quark model based on the light-front formalism, employing the general expression for the charge form factor of a pseudoscalar meson. They use eigenfunctions of the effective q-anti-q Hamiltonian of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), which reproduces meson mass spectra, considering two types of wave functions: one with only the linear confining term (w_conf) and one with the full Godfrey-Isgur interaction including one-gluon-exchange (w_GI). A simple monopole charge form factor for the constituent quarks is introduced, with the u and d quark radius fixed to reproduce the experimental pion charge radius.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum for the w_GI pion wave function is approximately 0.8 GeV/c, compared to approximately 0.3 GeV/c for the w_conf, harmonic oscillator, and power-law wave functions.
- For the w_conf pion wave function, approximately 90% of the form factor at Q² > 0.5 (GeV/c)² comes from components with k_perp > 0.3 GeV/c, and saturation is almost reached only when k_perp^U is about 1.5 GeV/c.
- For the w_GI pion wave function, the high-momentum tail corresponding to k_perp > 0.8 GeV/c is responsible for approximately 50% of the pion form factor at Q² > 0.5 (GeV/c)², and saturation is almost reached only when k_perp^U is about 2.5 GeV/c.
- The constituent u and d quark radius is set to (0.48 fm)² to reproduce the experimental pion charge radius of (0.660 ± 0.024 fm)².
- The pion mass corresponding to the confining potential alone is 1.024 GeV, while the full Godfrey-Isgur interaction yields 0.149 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model approach of Cardarelli et al. (1994) and directly contradicts the claim of Schlumpf (1994) that the pion charge form factor is insensitive to the high-momentum tail of the wave function at accessible energies. The predictions are compared with the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach and QCD sum rule technique, showing that existing pion data do not discriminate among models, but planned CEBAF measurements of pion and kaon form factors for Q² up to 3 (GeV/c)² could distinguish between different models of meson structure and provide information on the electromagnetic structure of light constituent quarks.
