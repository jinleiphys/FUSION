# Resonances in three-body systems with short and long-range interactions

> The complex scaling method permits calculations of few-body resonances with the correct asymptotic behaviour using a simple box boundary condition at a sufficiently large distance. This is also valid for systems involving more than one charged particle. We first apply the method on two-body systems. Three-body systems are then investigated by use of the (complex scaled) hyperspheric adiabatic expansion method. The case of the 2$^+$ resonance in $^6$Be and $^6$Li is considered. Radial wave functions are obtained showing the correct asymptotic behaviour at intermediate values of the hyperradii, where wave functions can be computed fully numerically.

## Key claim

The complex scaling method, combined with a simple box boundary condition at a sufficiently large distance, can produce accurate three-body resonance wave functions with correct asymptotic behavior even when long-range Coulomb interactions are present and the analytic asymptotic form is not known in advance.

## Method

The authors first test the complex scaling method on a two-body system (the 2- resonance in 16F) where the correct asymptotic form is known analytically. They then apply the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to three-body systems, computing the effective potentials (lambda-functions) numerically up to a hyperradius of 100 fm and using extrapolations for larger distances. The coupled radial equations are solved with a box boundary condition at large hyperradii (700 fm for 6Be, 1000 fm for 6Li) to obtain the 2+ resonance wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Two-body test: 2- resonance in 16F found at complex energy E = 0.5 - i 0.004 MeV using box boundary condition at r_max = 40 fm
- 6Be 2+ resonance: computed energy and width (E_R, Gamma_R) = (2.94, 1.45) MeV, compared to experimental (3.04, 1.16) MeV
- 6Li 2+ resonance: computed energy and width (E_R, Gamma_R) = (1.67, 0.50) MeV, compared to experimental (1.67, 0.54) MeV
- Basis size: partial waves with l_x and l_y up to 10, hypermomentum K up to 200 for s-waves, 90 for p-waves, and 60 for d-waves; slightly more than 2500 hyperspherical harmonics included
- Complex scaling angle: 0.15 radians for 6Be, 0.10 radians for 6Li
- Numerical effective potentials converged up to rho_max = 100 fm; radial wave functions match asymptotic behavior already at rho approximately 50 fm

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method for few-body resonances, previously applied to systems with only short-range interactions, to systems with multiple charged particles where the Coulomb asymptotics is not known analytically. The authors build on prior calculations of the 2+ resonance in 6He using the same adiabatic expansion method (Fedorov 2003, Garrido 2006) and test their approach on the analog states in 6Be and 6Li. The method enables computation of observables related to asymptotic wave functions, such as fragment energy distributions after resonance decay, without requiring prior knowledge of the correct asymptotic form.
