# Kaons and antikaons in hot and dense hadronic matter

> The medium modification of kaon and antikaon masses, compatible with low energy KN scattering data, are studied in a chiral SU(3) model. The mutual interactions with baryons in hot hadronic matter and the effects from the baryonic Dirac sea on the K(${\rm {\bar K}}$) masses are examined. The in-medium masses from the chiral SU(3) effective model are compared to those from chiral perturbation theory. Furthermore, the influence of these in-medium effects on kaon rapidity distributions and transverse energy spectra as well as the $K, \bar{K}$ flow pattern in heavy-ion collision experiments at 1.5 to 2 A$\cdot$GeV are investigated within the HSD transport approach. Detailed predictions on the transverse momentum and rapidity dependence of directed flow $v_1$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ are provided for Ni+Ni at 1.93 A$\cdot$GeV within the various models, that can be used to determine the in-medium $K^\pm$ properties from the experimental side in the near future.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) model predicts that kaon and antikaon masses drop substantially in dense hadronic matter, but the magnitude of this drop depends critically on whether the "range term" (repulsive scalar contributions proportional to the kinetic term of the pseudoscalar meson) is included; without this term the masses drop more steeply, while including it and fitting to KN scattering data produces a turnover in the kaon mass at around 0.8 times normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangian in the mean field approximation and in the relativistic Hartree approximation (including Dirac sea effects) to compute the in-medium masses of kaons and antikaons from their interactions with scalar fields (sigma and zeta), vector mesons (omega exchange), and the Weinberg-Tomozawa term. They then implemented these in-medium masses into the HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) transport approach to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 A·GeV and compute rapidity distributions, transverse energy spectra, and flow observables (directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2).

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged KN scattering length used for fitting is approximately -0.255 fm
- The pion-nucleon sigma term predicted by the model is 28 MeV; the kaon-nucleon sigma term is 463 MeV (close to the lattice value of 450 MeV)
- The coefficient d1 fitted to the KN scattering length is 5.63/mK in the mean field approximation and 4.33/mK in the relativistic Hartree approximation
- The kaon mass turnover occurs at around 0.8 times normal nuclear matter density (rho0)
- The calculations are performed for heavy-ion collisions at 1.5 to 2 A·GeV, with detailed predictions for Ni+Ni at 1.93 A·GeV
- The vacuum values used are f_pi = 93.3 MeV, f_K = 122 MeV, m_pi = 139 MeV, m_K = 498 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium hadron properties within the chiral SU(3) model, which had been applied to nucleons (Kristof 2001), vector mesons (Hartree approximation), and D-mesons (Mishra 2003). It compares the chiral SU(3) results to those from chiral perturbation theory (Kaplan and Nelson, Thorsson 1995), where the KN sigma term has large uncertainty (ranging from 2m_pi to 450 MeV). The results enable experimental determination of in-medium K± properties by comparing the predicted flow patterns (v1 and v2) with upcoming heavy-ion collision data from SIS energies.
