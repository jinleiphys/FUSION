# Ultracentral heavy ion collisions, transverse momentum and the equation of state

> Ultracentral heavy ion collisions due to their exceptionally large multiplicity probe an interesting regime of quark-gluon plasma where the size is (mostly) fixed and fluctuations in the initial condition dominate. Spurred by the recent measurement of the CMS collaboration we investigate the driving factors of the increase of transverse momentum, including a complete analysis of the influence of the QCD equation of state. Particularly interesting is the influence of the centrality selection as well as the initial energy deposition.

# Ultracentral heavy ion collisions, transverse momentum and the equation of state

## Key claim

The slope of the mean transverse momentum versus multiplicity in ultracentral collisions is not a direct measurement of the speed of sound; the equation of state is only a contributing factor, with important contributions from both the initial state parameters and the exact way centrality classes are determined.

## Method

The authors use the Trajectum framework, which combines a generalized TRENTo initial stage with viscous hydrodynamics and hadronic afterburner evolution using SMASH (with only resonance decays, no collision term). They vary the QCD equation of state by modifying the HotQCD parameterization parameters (p_id and a_n) while keeping the ultraviolet limit fixed, and also vary initial state parameters including nucleon width, initial energy fluctuations, and the TRENTo parameter q, as well as testing six different centrality definitions.

## Key numbers

- The average ultracentral initialization temperature is ⟨⟨T⟩⟩ = 0.42 GeV
- The speed of sound is evaluated at temperatures of 0.3 GeV and 0.42 GeV for comparison with fitted values
- The critical temperature used in the equation of state parameterization is T_c = 154 MeV
- The standard centrality selection uses charged particles with |η| < 0.8 and p_T > 0.4 GeV
- CMS results use forward calorimetric centrality; the paper recommends focusing on charged particles with p_T > 0.3 GeV for more accurate comparison
- The ultracentral analysis extends to the 0–0.001% centrality bin

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Gardim (2019) that the mean transverse momentum scales as p_T ∝ N_ch^(c_s^2), which would allow direct extraction of the speed of sound from ultracentral collisions. The authors show that while the equation of state contributes, the slope is also significantly affected by the TRENTo parameter q (which controls initial energy deposition) and by the experimental centrality definition, particularly whether calorimetric or charged multiplicity selection is used. The paper provides predictions for xenon and oxygen collisions at LHC energies, which could serve as tests of hydrodynamic behavior in smaller systems.
