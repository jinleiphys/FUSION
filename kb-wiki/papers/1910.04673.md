# Observing the deformation of nuclei with relativistic nuclear collisions

> I show that particle collider experiments on relativistic nuclear collisions can serve as direct probes of the deformation of the colliding nuclear species. I argue that collision events presenting very large multiplicities of particles and very small values of the average transverse momentum of the emitted hadrons probe collision geometries in which the nuclear ellipsoids fully overlap along their longer side. By looking at these events one selects interaction regions whose elliptic anisotropy is determined by the deformed nuclear shape, which becomes accessible experimentally through the measurement of the elliptic flow of outgoing hadrons.

# Observing the deformation of nuclei with relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The paper argues that selecting ultracentral nucleus-nucleus collision events with abnormally low average transverse momentum (p̅_t) isolates "body-body" configurations where the long axes of both deformed nuclei are aligned perpendicular to the beam axis and fully overlap. In these frozen geometries, the elliptic flow (v₂) of emitted hadrons directly reflects the quadrupole deformation parameter β of the colliding nuclei, provided the deformation is large enough to dominate over eccentricity fluctuations.

## Method
The author uses the TRENTO model of initial conditions to simulate 2×10⁷ ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions at LHC energies, selecting the 0–0.5% most central events (highest entropy/multiplicity). Within this class, events are sorted by their average transverse momentum p̅_t, which is anti-correlated with the initial system size R via the relation δp̅_t/⟨p̅_t⟩ = −3c²_s δR/⟨R⟩. The elliptic flow v₂ is estimated from the initial eccentricity ε₂ using the linear response v₂ = κ₂ε₂, with κ₂ = 0.16 for uranium. The same procedure is applied to ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹⁹⁷Au, and ¹²⁹Xe nuclei using their respective deformation parameters and response coefficients.

## Key numbers
- ²³⁸U deformation parameter β = 0.30, with nuclear radius R = 6.80 fm and diffuseness a = 0.60 fm
- ¹²⁹Xe deformation parameter β = 0.18, with R = 5.40 fm and a = 0.59 fm
- ¹⁹⁷Au deformation parameter β = −0.13, with R = 6.40 fm and a = 0.53 fm
- ²⁰⁸Pb deformation parameter β = 0 (spherical), with R = 6.62 fm and a = 0.55 fm
- Speed of sound used: c²_s ≈ 0.25 at LHC energies, c²_s ≈ 0.19 at RHIC energies
- Response coefficient κ₂ = 0.16 for ²³⁸U and ¹⁹⁷Au; κ₂ = 0.24 for ²⁰⁸Pb; κ₂ = 0.23 for ¹²⁹Xe
- In ultracentral U+U collisions, v₂ at low p̅_t reaches approximately βκ₂ = 0.30 × 0.16 = 0.048, implying ε₂ ≈ β
- The QGP lifetime is approximately 10⁻²² s

## Context
This work extends prior studies of deformed nuclei in relativistic collisions (²³⁸U+²³⁸U at RHIC and ¹²⁹Xe+¹²⁹Xe at LHC) by Adamczyk (2015), Sirunyan (2019), Acharya (2018), and Aad (2019), which averaged over all nuclear orientations and thus reduced sensitivity to deformation. The key innovation is using event-by-event p̅_t selection to freeze the nuclear orientation, building on the anti-correlation between system size and p̅_t established by Broniowski (2009), Mazeliauskas (2015), Bozek (2017), and Gardim (2019). If confirmed experimentally, this method enables direct tests of ab-initio nuclear structure calculations and could reveal quadrupole deformation for many stable nuclides, particularly using the versatility of RHIC.
