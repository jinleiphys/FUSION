# Two-pole nature of the $Λ(1405)$ from lattice QCD

> This letter presents the first lattice QCD computation of the coupled channel $πΣ-\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes at energies near $1405\,{\rm MeV}$. These amplitudes contain the resonance $Λ(1405)$ with strangeness $S=-1$ and isospin, spin, and parity quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(1/2^-)$. However, whether there is a single resonance or two nearby resonance poles in this region is controversial theoretically and experimentally. Using single-baryon and meson-baryon operators to extract the finite-volume stationary-state energies to obtain the scattering amplitudes at slightly unphysical quark masses corresponding to $m_π\approx200$ MeV and $m_K\approx487$ MeV, this study finds the amplitudes exhibit a virtual bound state below the $πΣ$ threshold in addition to the established resonance pole just below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. Several parametrizations of the two-channel $K$-matrix are employed to fit the lattice QCD results, all of which support the two-pole picture suggested by $SU(3)$ chiral symmetry and unitarity.

## Key claim
This lattice QCD study finds that the coupled-channel πΣ–K̅N scattering amplitudes near 1405 MeV exhibit two poles, a virtual bound state below the πΣ threshold and a resonance just below the K̅N threshold, but only at slightly unphysical quark masses (mπ≈200 MeV, mK≈487 MeV), with the authors noting that the real parts of the pole positions are somewhat larger than those determined at the physical point from experiment.

## Method
The authors computed coupled-channel πΣ–K̅N scattering amplitudes using a single ensemble of Nf=2+1 QCD gauge configurations (the CLS D200 ensemble) with lattice spacing a=0.0633 fm and spatial extent L=64a. They extracted finite-volume stationary-state energies from Hermitian correlation matrices containing single-baryon, πΣ, and K̅N interpolating operators, then constrained several parametrizations of the two-channel K-matrix (including effective range expansion and Weinberg-Tomozawa-inspired forms) by fitting to the finite-volume spectrum via the Lüscher method with ℓmax=0 partial waves.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: mπ≈200 MeV; kaon mass: mK≈487 MeV (lattice spacing a=0.0633(4)(6) fm)
- Box size in pion mass units: mπL=4.181(16)
- Lower pole position: E1=1392(9)(2)(16) MeV (on the (-,+) Riemann sheet)
- Upper pole position: E2=[1455(13)(2)(17)−i 11.5(4.4)(4)(0.1)] MeV (on the (-,+) Riemann sheet)
- Coupling ratio for lower pole: |c(1)πΣ/c(1)K̅N|=1.9(4)(6)
- Coupling ratio for upper pole: |c(2)πΣ/c(2)K̅N|=0.53(9)(10)
- Best-fit parameters (four-parameter fit with lowest AIC): A00=4.1(1.8), A11=−10.5(1.1), A01=10.3(1.5), B01=−29(18), with χ²=10.52 for 11 degrees of freedom

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies that only isolated the lowest finite-volume energy eigenstate using single-baryon three-quark operators (Gubler 2016, Menadue 2011, Engel 2012, Hall 2014) without computing scattering amplitudes, and improves upon the quenched K̅N scattering length calculation of Fukugita (1994) by including the kinematically-open πΣ channel. The results support the two-pole picture predicted by SU(3) chiral effective theory (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 2000) and are consistent with analyses of CLAS data (Mai 2014, Roca 2013) and ALICE femtoscopy data (ALICE 2022), while providing the first coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering amplitude from lattice QCD. Future work includes moving to physical quark masses, estimating residual finite-volume and lattice spacing effects, and studying the quark-mass trajectory toward the SU(3)-symmetric point to test chiral effective theory predictions.
