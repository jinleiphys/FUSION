# Chiral Perturbation Theory and the first moments of the Generalized Parton Distriputions in a Nucleon

> We discuss the first moments of the parity-even Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) in a nucleon, corresponding to six (generalized) vector form factors. We evaluate these fundamental properties of baryon structure at low energies, utilizing the methods of covariant Chiral Perturbation Theory in the baryon sector (BChPT). Our analysis is performed at leading-one-loop order in BChPT, predicting both the momentum and the quark-mass dependence for the three (generalized) isovector and (generalized) isoscalar form factors, which are currently under investigation in lattice QCD analyses of baryon structure. We also study the limit of vanishing four-momentum transfer where the GPD-moments reduce to the well known moments of Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs). For the isovector moment <x>_{u-d} our BChPT calculation predicts a new mechanism for chiral curvature, connecting the high values for this moment typically found in lattice QCD studies for large quark masses with the smaller value known from phenomenology. Likewise, we analyze the quark-mass dependence of the isoscalar moments in the forward limit and extract the contribution of quarks to the total spin of the nucleon. We close with a first glance at the momentum dependence of the isoscalar C-form factor of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first analysis of the first moments of nucleon Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) using covariant Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (BChPT) at leading-one-loop order, predicting both the momentum and quark-mass dependence for the six generalized isovector and isoscalar form factors. The authors note that their covariant BChPT formalism exactly reproduces the corresponding Heavy Baryon ChPT (HBChPT) result in the limit of small pion masses, but differs at larger pion masses through an infinite series of terms proportional to (m_pi/M_0)^n, which become relevant for present-day lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The authors evaluate the first Mellin moments of parity-even GPDs in a nucleon, corresponding to six generalized vector form factors (A_{2,0}, B_{2,0}, C_{2,0} in both isoscalar and isovector channels), using covariant BChPT with two light flavors at leading-one-loop order (O(p^2)). They construct effective Lagrangians coupling external symmetric, traceless tensor fields to nucleons and pions, perform a power-counting analysis to identify contributing diagrams, and compute loop corrections using a variant of Infrared Regularization. The analysis includes both the momentum dependence (t-dependence) and the quark-mass dependence (pion-mass dependence) of the form factors, with the nucleon mass itself treated as a function of pion mass using an O(p^4) BChPT result.

## Key numbers

- The chiral limit value of the nucleon mass M_0 is estimated to be around 890 MeV (from ref. [Mass]).
- The chiral extrapolation formula for the nucleon mass is stated to provide a stable extrapolation function up to effective pion masses of approximately 600 MeV.
- The input values used for numerical analysis include: g_A = 1.2, F_pi = 0.0924 GeV, M_0 = 0.889 GeV, c_1 = -0.817 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 3.2 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.4 GeV^{-1}, e_1^r(1 GeV) = 1.44 GeV^{-3}, delta_M = 0.
- The scale of chiral symmetry breaking Lambda_chi is approximately 1.2 GeV.
- The analysis is performed at a regularization scale mu = 2 GeV for comparisons with phenomenology.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering analyses of Chen and Ji as well as Belitsky and Ji (early 2000s), who studied the generalized isoscalar and isovector form factors at leading-one-loop order in the non-relativistic framework of HBChPT. The covariant BChPT approach provides a resummation of terms in (1/M_0)^n that become important at the larger pion masses used in present-day lattice QCD simulations, such as those from the LHPC and QCDSF collaborations. The results enable chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD data for the generalized form factors down to the physical quark masses, and the authors note that the tensor coupling to the pion field does not contribute at leading-one-loop order, unlike for the standard Dirac and Pauli form factors.
