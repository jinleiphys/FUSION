# MCNP6 fragmentation of light nuclei at intermediate energies

> Fragmentation reactions induced on light target nuclei by protons and light nuclei of energies around 1 GeV/nucleon and below are studied with the latest Los Alamos Monte Carlo transport code MCNP6 and with its cascade-exciton model (CEM) and Los Alamos version of the quark-gluon string model (LAQGSM) event generators, version 03.03, used as stand-alone codes. Such reactions are involved in different applications, like cosmic-ray-induced single event upsets (SEU's), radiation protection, and cancer therapy with proton and ion beams, among others; therefore, it is important that MCNP6 simulates them as well as possible. CEM and LAQGSM assume that intermediate-energy fragmentation reactions on light nuclei occur generally in two stages. The first stage is the intranuclear cascade (INC), followed by the second, Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei produced after INC. Both CEM and LAQGSM account also for coalescence of light fragments (complex particles) up to He4 from energetic nucleons emitted during INC. We investigate the validity and performance of MCNP6, CEM, and LAQGSM in simulating fragmentation reactions at intermediate energies and discuss possible ways of further improving these codes.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the validity and performance of MCNP6 and its event generators CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 for simulating fragmentation reactions on light nuclei at intermediate energies (around 1 GeV/nucleon and below), but the results are conditional on the choice of the Fermi breakup cut-off parameter \(A_{Fermi}\), which is a model-dependent parameter rather than a fixed physics characteristic.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo transport code MCNP6 with its default event generator CEM03.03, as well as CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 as stand-alone codes, to simulate fragmentation reactions induced by protons on light target nuclei (14N, 16O, 27Al, 28Si) at intermediate energies. The codes assume a two-stage reaction mechanism: an intranuclear cascade (INC) followed by Fermi breakup disintegration of light excited residual nuclei with atomic number \(A \leq A_{Fermi}\), plus a coalescence model for producing light fragments up to 4He from emitted cascade nucleons. The authors systematically varied the Fermi breakup cut-off parameter \(A_{Fermi}\) (using values of 12, 14, and 16) and compared calculated excitation functions and particle spectra against available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The coalescence radii used in the model are: \(p_c(d) = 90\) MeV/c, \(p_c(t) = p_c(^3He) = 108\) MeV/c, and \(p_c(^4He) = 115\) MeV/c, fitted originally for the reaction Ne+U at 1.04 GeV/nucleon.
- For incident energies in the interval 300 MeV \(\leq T_0 < 1\) GeV, better agreement with data is obtained using \(p_c\) values of 150, 175, and 175 MeV/c for d, t (3He), and 4He, respectively.
- The Fermi breakup model is applied to residual nuclei with atomic number \(A \leq A_{Fermi} = 12\) (default), compared to \(A_{Fermi} = 16\) used in GEANT4 and SHIELD-HIT.
- For p + 14N reactions, excitation functions for 14O, 13N, 12N, 13C, 12C, and 10C calculated with \(A_{Fermi} = 16\) agree better with data, while 9Be and 7Be are reproduced better with \(A_{Fermi} = 12\).
- For p + 16O reactions, almost all products are better predicted with \(A_{Fermi} = 14\); production of 11B is described a little better with \(A_{Fermi} = 16\), while 9Be and 7Be are reproduced better with \(A_{Fermi} = 12\).

## Context

This work extends previous validation efforts for MCNP6, which had been tested against a large variety of nuclear reactions on thin and thick targets but never specifically on fragmentation of light nuclei at intermediate energies. The study is part of a broader effort to improve fragmentation simulations in transport codes, following similar investigations for GEANT4 (Agostinelli et al., 2003), SHIELD-HIT (Gudowska et al., 2004), and PHITS (Niita et al., 2006). The results enable future refinement of the Fermi breakup model and the choice of \(A_{Fermi}\) in MCNP6, CEM, and LAQGSM to better reproduce experimental data for applications such as cosmic-ray-induced single event upsets, radiation protection, and cancer therapy.
