# SU(3) Symmetry and Scissors Mode Vibrations in Nuclei

> We show that a nearly perfect SU(3) symmetry emerges from an extended Projected Shell Model. Starting from a deformed potential we construct separate bases for neutron and proton collective rotational states by exact angular momentum projection. These rotational states are then coupled by diagonalizing a residual pairing plus quadrupole interaction. The states obtained exhibit a one-to-one correspondence with an SU(3) spectrum up to high angular momentum and excitation, and their wave functions have a near-maximal overlap with the SU(3) states. They can also be classified as rotational bands built on spin-1$\hbar$ phonon excitations, which may correspond to a geometrical scissors mode and its generalizations. This work is a direct demonstration that numerical angular momentum projection theory extends the Elliott's original idea to heavy nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a nearly perfect SU(3) symmetry emerges from a purely numerical extended Projected Shell Model calculation for heavy deformed nuclei, even though no explicit dynamical symmetry is built into the model and the strong spin-orbit force in heavy nuclei breaks the original Elliott SU(3) scheme at the single-particle level.

## Method

The authors extended the Projected Shell Model by constructing separate Nilsson-BCS quasiparticle vacuum states for neutrons and protons, performing exact angular momentum projection on each to obtain neutron and proton rotational states, then coupling these states by diagonalizing a residual pairing plus quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian. The calculation was performed for the nucleus 168Er using three major shells for neutrons (N=4,5,6) and protons (N=3,4,5), with deformation parameter ε2=0.273, and the resulting eigenstates were compared to SU(3) wave functions through angular momentum distribution analysis, direct overlap calculations, and B(E2) transition rates.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter ε2 = 0.273 for 168Er
- Neutron moment of inertia parameter Aν = 0.019 MeV and proton moment of inertia parameter Aπ = 0.048 MeV from projected states
- Best-fit SU(3) parameters: λm = 48, ℏω∞ = 2.9 MeV (χνπeff = 0.0403), and A = 0.013 MeV
- Effective SU(3) representations: [λν=32,0] for neutrons and [λπ=16,0] for protons, yielding total [λ=48,0] for the ground band
- Hamiltonian parameters (in MeV): χνν = 0.0206, χππ = 0.0160, χνπ = 0.0182, GνM = 0.1049, GπM = 0.1346
- Phonon energy decreases smoothly from 2.9 MeV to 2.5 MeV as phonon number increases from 1 to 7
- Overlaps of PSM wave functions with SU(3) wave functions for the ground band are very close to 1 (listed in Table 2)

## Context

This work extends the original Elliott SU(3) shell model (Elliott, 1958) to heavy deformed nuclei where the strong spin-orbit force breaks the single-particle SU(3) symmetry, building on earlier suggestions by Bhatt, Parikh and McGrory (1977) that SU(3)-like structures appear at the macroscopic level in yrast states. The paper also connects to the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM) and the two-rotor model, and enables microscopic study of scissors mode vibrations and other collective excitations in heavy nuclei through numerical angular momentum projection methods.
