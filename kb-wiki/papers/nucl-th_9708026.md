# Nucleon-Nucleon Phase Shifts and Pairing in Neutron Matter and Nuclear Matter

> We consider 1S0 pairing in infinite neutron matter and nuclear matter and show that in the lowest order approximation, where the pairing interaction is taken to be the bare nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in the 1S0 channel, the pairing interaction and the energy gap can be determined directly from the 1S0 phase shifts. This is due to the almost separable character of the NN interaction in this partial wave. Since the most recent NN interactions are charge-dependent, we have to solve coupled gap equations for proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton pairing in nuclear matter. The results, however, are found to be close to those obtained with charge-independent potentials.

## Key claim

In the lowest-order BCS approximation with the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction in the 1S0 channel, the pairing gap in neutron matter and nuclear matter can be determined directly from the 1S0 phase shifts, because the interaction is nearly rank-one separable near the pole of the T-matrix. This explains why all modern NN potentials that fit scattering data yield almost identical 1S0 pairing gaps, though this result is not expected to survive in more refined calculations that include density and spin-density fluctuations.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for the 1S0 channel in infinite neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter using the bare NN interaction, with free single-particle energies epsilon(k)=k^2/2m. For neutron matter, they computed the gap directly from the 1S0 phase shifts via the inverse scattering solution for a rank-one separable potential, using Eqs. (4) and (5) to obtain the diagonal interaction lambda v^2(k) from the phase shifts. For symmetric nuclear matter with charge-dependent potentials, they solved three coupled gap equations for neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton pairing, coupled through a common quasiparticle energy denominator.

## Key numbers

- Maximum 1S0 energy gap in neutron matter: 2.98 MeV for Nijmegen I potential, 3.05 MeV for Nijmegen II potential
- The 1S0 phase shift changes sign from positive to negative at a laboratory energy of approximately 248 MeV, corresponding to a single-particle momentum of k approximately 1.73 fm^-1
- Singlet neutron-neutron scattering length a0 = -18.8 +/- 0.3 fm
- Singlet neutron-neutron effective range r0 = 2.75 +/- 0.11 fm
- Below Fermi momentum k_F = 0.5 fm^-1, the pairing gap can be calculated with reasonable accuracy using the effective range approximation

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation between pairing gaps and NN phase shifts obtained by Emery and Sessler (1960) and Hoffberg et al. (1970), and explains the near interaction independence of 1S0 pairing gaps previously observed by Baldo et al. (1990), Chen et al. (1993), Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1993), Elgaroy et al. (1996), and Khodel et al. (1996). The results enable a direct connection between measured NN scattering data and superfluid properties of neutron stars and neutron-rich nuclei, though the authors note that more sophisticated calculations including density and spin-density fluctuations (Wambach et al., 1993; Schulze et al., 1996) will involve other partial waves where the simple separable argument does not apply.
