# Production of strange particles in hadronic interactions

> The NA61/SHINE collaboration has recently published high precision data on production of $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$ mesons, protons, antiprotons and $Λ$ hyperons in ${\rm pp}$ interactions at 20, 31, 40, 80 and 158 GeV/c, and in ${\rm pC}$ interactions at 31 GeV/c. The collaboration also presented experimental data on production of particles - $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p^\pm$, $ρ^0$, $ω$ and $K^{*0}$ in $π^-{\rm C}$ collisions at 158 and 350 GeV/c. The collaboration has compared these data with various Monte Carlo model calculations: UrQMD, EPOS, GiBUU, and others. All of the models have various problems. The latest version of the FTF (Fritiof) model of Geant4 solves most of these problems. In the FTF model, we have improved the fragmentation of quark-gluon strings with small masses and introduced dependencies of probabilities of strange mesons and baryon-antibaryon pair's creation on string masses. Due to these changes, we describe the data of the NA61/SHINE collaboration on particle production in ${\rm pp, pC}$, and $π^-{\rm C}$ interactions. The improved Geant4 FTF model also well reproduces experimental data on inclusive cross sections of $Λ, \barΛ$ and $K^{0}$ production in antiproton-proton interactions at various energies. The modified FTF model allows one to simulate realistic processes with two particle productions - $\bar{p}p \rightarrow Λ\barΛ$, $\bar{p}p \rightarrow K^{+} K^{-}$, $\bar{p}p \rightarrow Λ\barΣ$, and $\bar{p}p \rightarrowΣ\barΣ$, which will be studied in the future by the PANDA experiment at FAIR (GSI, Germany).

## Key claim

The improved Geant4 FTF (Fritiof) model, with new mass-dependent probabilities for strange quark pair and diquark-antidiquark pair creation during string fragmentation, describes the NA61/SHINE data on strange particle production in pp, pC, and π⁻C interactions, as well as antiproton-proton data, where previous models (UrQMD, EPOS, GiBUU) had various problems. The improvement is conditional on suppressing the probability of strange quark production at low string masses using a threshold formula, and the model still cannot describe K meson spectra at 40 GeV/c, which the authors note do not follow the common trend.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark pair creation probability: \(P_{\bar{s}s} = 0.108 \cdot (1-(m_{th}/M_{str})^4)\), where 0.108 is the asymptotic probability, \(m_{th}\) is the sum of two strange meson masses, and \(M_{str}\) is the string mass.
- Diquark-antidiquark pair creation probability: \(P_{qq-\bar{q}\bar{q}} = 0.15 \cdot (1-(1400 \cdot N_{b}/M_{str})^{2})\), where 0.15 is the asymptotic probability, \(M_{str}\) is the string mass, and \(N_{b}\) is the maximum number of baryons and antibaryons.
- Before improvement, FTF model calculations overestimated the maxima of K⁺ meson momentum distributions in pC interactions at 31 GeV/c by about 50%.
- FTF model calculations for antiprotons in π⁻C interactions at 158 GeV/c are slightly lower than data for antiprotons with momenta larger than 10 GeV/c.
- The model describes Λ production cross section in antiproton-proton interactions at low energies up to 3 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Geant4 FTF model to address problems identified in comparisons of NA61/SHINE data with Monte Carlo models including UrQMD 3.4, EPOS 1.99, and GiBUU 1.6, which all had various issues describing strange particle production. The improved model now performs comparably to EPOS 1.99 for Λ hyperon production in pp interactions at 158 GeV/c, and enables realistic simulations of binary reactions such as \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\), \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow K^{+}K^{-}\), \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \Lambda\bar{\Sigma}\), and \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \Sigma\bar{\Sigma}\) for the future PANDA experiment at FAIR.
