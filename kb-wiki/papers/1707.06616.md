# Efimov effect in $D$ spatial dimensions in $AAB$ systems

> The existence of the Efimov effect is drastically affected by the dimensionality of the space in which the system is embedded. The effective spatial dimension containing an atomic cloud can be continuously modified by compressing it in one or two directions. In the present article we determine for a general $AAB$ system formed by two identical bosons $A$ and a third particle $B$ in the two-body unitary limit, the dimensionsality $D$ for which the Efimov effect can exist for different values of the mass ratio $\mathpzc{A}=m_B/m_A$. In addition, we provide a prediction for the Efimov discrete scaling factor, ${\rm exp}\,(π/s)$, as a function of a wide range of values of $\mathpzc{A}$ and $D$, which can be tested in experiments that can be realized with currently available technology.

## Key claim

The Efimov effect can exist in AAB systems for a continuous range of spatial dimensions D between 2 and 4, with the specific dimensional band depending on the mass ratio A = m_B/m_A; the effect disappears at critical dimensions where the imaginary part s of the exponent goes to zero, not at the integer dimensions D=2 or D=1 as might be naively expected.

## Method

The authors generalized the Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosyan (STM) coupled integral equations for three particles with zero-range interactions to D spatial dimensions, then solved them in the unitary limit (infinite two-body scattering lengths) by applying Danilov's method. This method exploits the dominance of large-momentum regions and scale invariance to obtain homogeneous solutions of the form q^(r+is) for the spectator functions, where r = 1-D removes ultraviolet divergences and s determines the Efimov discrete scaling factor exp(pi/s). The characteristic equation for s was solved numerically for a wide range of mass ratios A and dimensions D.

## Key numbers

- For identical bosons (A=1), the Efimov effect exists only for dimensions 2.3 < D < 3.8, reproducing the result of Nielsen (1999).
- For D=3 and A=1, the imaginary part s = 1.00623, giving the well-known Efimov scaling factor exp(pi/s) approximately 22.7.
- The mass ratio A = m_B/m_A was studied over a wide range, with specific results shown for A = 6/133 (a 6Li-133Cs mixture) where the effective dimension D was mapped as a function of b_omega/a_3 (the ratio of the squeezed oscillator length to the D=3 AB scattering length).

## Context

This work extends the earlier result of Nielsen (1999) for identical bosons to mass-imbalanced AAB systems, and generalizes the D=3 analysis of Yamashita (2013) to continuous dimensions. The predictions for the discrete scaling factor as a function of D and A can be tested in current cold-atom experiments using heteronuclear mixtures (such as 6Li-133Cs) in squeezed traps, where compressing the atomic cloud in one or two directions creates an effective fractional dimension. The results also connect to the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for heavy-light-heavy systems, where the Efimov effect arises from a -1/R^2 effective potential with strength depending on D and A.
