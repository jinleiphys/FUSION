# The pion-nucleon $σ$ term from pionic atoms

> Earlier work suggested that the in-medium $πN$ threshold isovector amplitude $b_1(ρ)$ gets renormalized in pionic atoms by about 30% away from its $ρ=0$ free-space value, relating such renormalization to the leading low-density decrease of the in-medium quark condensate $<\bar q q>$ and the pion decay constant $f_π$ in terms of the pion-nucleon $σ$ term $σ_{πN}$. Accepting the validity of this approach, we extracted $σ_{πN}$ from a large-scale fit of pionic-atom level shift and width data across the periodic table. Our fitted value $σ_{πN}=57\pm 7$ MeV is robust with respect to variation of $πN$ interaction terms other than the isovector $s$-wave term with which $σ_{πN}$ was associated. Higher order corrections to the leading order in density involve some cancellations, suggesting thereby only a few percent overall systematic uncertainty. The value of $σ_{πN}$ derived here agrees with values obtained in several recent studies based on near-threshold $πN$ phenomenology, but sharply disagrees with values obtained in recent direct lattice QCD calculations.

# The pion-nucleon σ term from pionic atoms

## Key claim
The pion-nucleon σ term σπN = 57 ± 7 MeV is extracted from a large-scale fit of pionic-atom data across the periodic table, based on the model that the in-medium πN isovector s-wave amplitude b1(ρ) is renormalized by about 30% away from its free-space value. This value agrees with recent near-threshold πN phenomenology but sharply disagrees with recent direct lattice QCD calculations.

## Method
The authors performed global fits to strong interaction level shifts and widths of pionic atoms from Ne to U, including deeply bound states in Sn isotopes and 205Pb, using the Ericson-Ericson form of the pion-nucleus optical potential. The in-medium isovector s-wave amplitude b1 was parameterized via the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation to depend on σπN, and σπN was treated as a free parameter fitted simultaneously with other πN interaction parameters (b0, Im B0, c0, c1, Im C0) while varying the neutron-excess radius parameter γ. Fits were performed with both finite-range (FR) and zero-range (ZR) p-wave folding models.

## Key numbers
- σπN = 57 ± 7 MeV, the best-fit value from pionic atoms data across the periodic table
- Free-space isovector scattering length b1free = -0.0861 ± 0.0009 mπ-1, from chiral perturbation theory using pionic hydrogen and deuterium X-ray measurements
- Free-space isoscalar scattering length b0free = 0.0076 ± 0.0031 mπ-1
- Tomozawa-Weinberg leading-order chiral limit b1TW = -0.079 mπ-1, using fπ = 92.2 MeV
- Implied neutron skin of 208Pb from the χ2 minimum: 0.15 ± 0.03 fm
- χ2 per degree of freedom of 1.7 for 98 data points with six adjusted parameters
- When b1free was used in the double-scattering term instead of the in-medium b1, the lowest χ2 increased by about 5 units, corresponding to three standard deviations

## Context
This work extends the approach of Weise (2000, 2001) and Kolomeitsev, Kaiser, and Weise (2003) who introduced the in-medium renormalization of b1 via the quark condensate density dependence, but where σπN was held fixed around 50 MeV. The result σπN = 57 ± 7 MeV agrees with the recent value of 58 ± 5 MeV from πN phenomenology by Ruiz de Elvira et al. (2018) and the Bonn-Jülich group value of 59 ± 3 MeV (Hoferichter et al., 2015), but disagrees with the low value of 26 ± 7 MeV from lattice QCD by the JLQCD collaboration (2018). The analysis enables a determination of σπN that is largely independent of the highly model-dependent isoscalar scattering length b0free, instead relying on the larger and more precisely known isovector amplitude b1free.
