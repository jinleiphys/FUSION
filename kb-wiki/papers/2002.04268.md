# Asymptotic of the electric structure function and the deuteron wave function

> The main features of obtaining the asymptotic behaviour of the electric structure function A(p) at large values of the transmitted momentum are analysed. The asymptotic behaviour of the structure function A(p) was determined to take into account the asymptotic behaviour of the deuteron form factors and the original dipole approximation for the nucleon form factors. Asymptotic values of A(p) were obtained for the nucleon-nucleon potential Reid93 and compared with the calculations for different nucleon form factors models and their approximations. In the broad momentum range up to 12.5 fm^(-1), the basic forms of the asymptotic behaviour of the electric structure function are demonstrated and compared with the experimental data of the modern collaborations. As the analysis shows in most cases considered, the asymptotic for A(p) is represented in the form of the power function p^(-n).

## Key claim

The asymptotic behavior of the electric structure function \(A(p)\) for the deuteron at large transferred momenta is shown to follow a power-law form \(p^{-n}\), with the specific exponent depending on the nucleon-nucleon potential, the nucleon form factor model, and the theoretical approximation (non-relativistic or relativistic impulse approximation), rather than a single universal QCD prediction.

## Method

The author calculated the electric structure function \(A(p)\) using the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential with analytic deuteron wave functions in coordinate representation (29-term expansion). Seven different models and approximations for the nucleon form factors were employed: the original dipole fit (DFF), modified dipole fit 2 (MDFF2), relativistic harmonic oscillator model (RHOM), vector meson dominance model (VMDM), Kelly parameterization, JLab approximation, and Bradford parameterization. The asymptotic behavior was extracted by fitting the calculated \(A(p)\) values in the momentum range \(p = 10.6\)–\(12.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) to a power function \(p^{-N}\), and compared with experimental data from Stanford, Orsay, CEA, DESY, SLAC, Mainz, Bonn, Saclay, Bates, and JLab collaborations.

## Key numbers

* For the Reid93 potential with the dipole fit (DFF), the asymptotic exponent for \(A(p)\) in the range \(p = 10.6\)–\(12.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) is \(N = 8.44\).
* For the same potential and momentum range, the asymptotic exponents for other models are: MDFF2 \(N = 8.50\), RHOM \(N = 7.48\), VMDM \(N = 7.82\), JLab \(N = 8.68\), and Bradford \(N = 8.69\).
* The analytic asymptotic derived from the deuteron form factors and dipole nucleon form factors gives \(A \sim p^{-14}\).
* The approximation of JLab experimental data (Alexa1999, PR08019) in the momentum interval \(p \approx 10.6\)–\(12.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) yields \(\sqrt{A} \sim (p^2)^{-3.795}\), corresponding to \(A \sim p^{-15.18}\).
* The reduced chi-squared values (multiplied by \(10^{-16}\)) for the approximations compared to experiment are: approx1 = 1.21, approx2 = 1.63, RHOM = 3.46, VMDM = 0.27.
* The global fit of experimental data in the momentum interval \(p = 4.00874\)–\(12.36678\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) gives \(\sqrt{A} \sim p^{-5.787}\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of deuteron electromagnetic structure by Krutov (2005, 2006) for non-relativistic and relativistic impulse approximations, and by Garcon (2001) for dimensional scaling in pQCD. It also compares with calculations using Moscow, NijmI, NijmII, CD-Bonn, and Paris potentials by Khokhlov (2015), and with chiral effective field theory results by Piarulli (2013). The obtained asymptotic forms and their comparison with experimental data from JLab (Alexa1999, PR08019) enable improved predictions for polarization observables such as tensor \(t_{2j}\) and vector \(t_{1i}\) polarizations, spin correlation coefficients, and tensor asymmetries in processes involving the deuteron.
