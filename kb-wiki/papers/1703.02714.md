# Quantum tunneling with friction

> Using the phenomenological quantum friction models introduced by Caldirola-Kanai, Kostin, and Albrecht, we study quantum tunneling of a one-dimensional potential in the presence of energy dissipation. To this end, we calculate the tunneling probability using a time-dependent wave packet method. The friction reduces the tunneling probability. We show that the three models provide similar penetrabilities to each other, among which the Caldirola-Kanai model requires the least numerical effort. We also discuss the effect of energy dissipation on quantum tunneling in terms of barrier distributions.

## Key claim

The three phenomenological quantum friction models (Caldirola-Kanai, Kostin, and Albrecht) produce nearly identical tunneling probabilities for a one-dimensional potential, despite their different mathematical structures and despite earlier work showing they give different time-dependent widths for free wave packets. The Caldirola-Kanai model requires the least numerical effort and achieves the highest numerical accuracy.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent nonlinear Schrödinger equation for each friction model using a Crank-Nicholson method with a time-dependent friction coefficient that vanishes outside the interaction region. They used a Gaussian wave packet with an energy width of σ_E = 0.5 MeV and a Gaussian potential V(q) = V_0 exp(-q^2/2s^2) with V_0 = 100 MeV and s = 3 fm, simulating the ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni reaction. The tunneling probability was computed as the integrated probability density on the transmitted side after the wave packet had fully bifurcated.

## Key numbers

- Potential parameters: V_0 = 100 MeV, s = 3 fm; particle mass mc² = 29 × 938 MeV
- Energy width of wave packet: σ_E = 0.5 MeV (chosen to give agreement with time-independent method for tunneling probabilities above 10⁻⁴)
- Weak friction case (energy loss E_loss = 5 MeV at barrier top): dimensionless friction coefficients γ₀/c = 2.14 × 10⁻³ (Caldirola-Kanai), 2.16 × 10⁻³ (Kostin), 2.17 × 10⁻³ (Albrecht)
- Strong friction case (E_loss = 30 MeV at barrier top): γ₀/c = 13.8 × 10⁻³ (Caldirola-Kanai), 14.0 × 10⁻³ (Kostin), 14.0 × 10⁻³ (Albrecht)
- Grid sizes: Δτ = 0.00025, Δq = 0.01 fm for Caldirola-Kanai and Kostin models; Δτ = 0.00015, Δq = 0.01 fm for Albrecht model

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quantum friction models by Hasse (1975), who compared the three models for free wave packet propagation and damped harmonic oscillators but not for tunneling probabilities. It also relates to the coupled-channels approach to heavy-ion fusion reactions, where friction models have been used classically for energies above the Coulomb barrier (e.g., by Sierk and Nix, Zagrebaev). The authors note that their barrier distribution results resemble those from coupled-channels calculations for heavy systems such as ¹⁰⁰Mo+¹⁰⁰Mo reported by Rowley, Hagino, and Dasgupta (2006). The work enables future extension of quantum friction models to multi-channel cases and inclusion of random force (fluctuation) terms.
