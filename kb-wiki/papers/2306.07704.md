# Born-Infeld nonlinear electromagnetism in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the effect of the limiting field strength of Born-Infeld electromagnetism on the dynamics of charged particle scattering. We formulate the Born-Infeld limiting field in an invariant manner, showing that it is the electric field-dominated eigenvalue `$a$' of the field tensor $F^{μν}$ which is limited rather than the individual field vectors. Heavy ion collisions in particular provide uniquely large values of the field invariants that appear in the BI action, amplifying nonlinear effects. Thus ``$a$'' is the dominant input into the force between heavy ions that we use to compute the scattering angle as a function of impact parameter. We evaluate the BI effects, showing relevance at small impact parameters and exhibiting their dependence on the value of the limiting field strength.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in Born-Infeld (BI) nonlinear electromagnetism, the limiting field condition applies to the electric-field-dominated eigenvalue \(a\) of the field tensor \(F^{\mu\nu}\) rather than to the individual electric or magnetic field vectors, and that this invariant limit suppresses the scattering angle in relativistic heavy ion collisions at small impact parameters, with the effect depending on the unknown value of the limiting field strength \(E_0\).

## Method

The authors treat each heavy ion as a test particle and solve the BI field equations in the ion rest frame to obtain the displacement fields \(\mathbf{D}\) and \(\mathbf{H}\), then Lorentz boost these to the center-of-momentum frame. The resulting electric and magnetic fields are inserted into the Lorentz force equations of motion for two identical ions, which are integrated numerically using a fifth-order Runge-Kutta-Dormand-Prince method. The scattering angle is computed as a function of impact parameter for four pairs of colliding ions (C-C, Ca-Ca, Au-Au, U-U) at fixed Lorentz factors, and for several values of the limiting field constant \(E_0\).

## Key numbers

*   Born and Infeld's original calculated value of the limiting field constant: \(E_0 = 1.18 \times 10^{20} \, \text{V/m}\).
*   The quantum nonlinear scale from the Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective Lagrangian is \(E_\text{EHS} = 0.0112 E_0\).
*   The limiting acceleration from the Eliezer-Ford-O'Connell radiation reaction model is \(a_\text{EFO} = 4.80 \times 10^{31} \, \text{m/s}^2\) for electrons, corresponding to an electric field \(E_\text{EFO} = 1.53 E_0\).
*   For Au-Au collisions at \(\gamma=1.5\), at a fixed scattering angle of \(0.4^\circ\), the BI theory with \(E_0 = 10E_0\) predicts an impact parameter \(\rho_\text{BI} \approx 12\) fm, while Maxwell theory predicts \(\rho_\text{Max} \approx 15\) fm, a shift of approximately 25\%.

## Context

This work extends the study of Born-Infeld nonlinear electromagnetism from static or single-particle contexts to the dynamic environment of relativistic heavy ion collisions, which provide uniquely large field invariants. It builds on the foundational BI theory of Born and Infeld (1934) and Bialynicki-Birula (1983), and contrasts with the quantum nonlinearity scale of the Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective action (Heisenberg 1936, Schwinger 1951). The results enable future experimental determination of the BI limiting field constant \(E_0\) using peripheral heavy ion collision data, and motivate further work incorporating self-field effects and radiation reaction, such as the EFO model of Price (2021).
