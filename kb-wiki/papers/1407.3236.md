# Polarized light ions and spectator nucleon tagging at EIC

> An Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) with suitable forward detection capabilities would enable a unique experimental program of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from polarized light nuclei (deuterium 2H, helium 3He) with spectator nucleon tagging. Such measurements promise significant advances in several key areas of nuclear physics and QCD: (a) neutron spin structure, by using polarized deuterium and eliminating nuclear effects through on-shell extrapolation in the spectator proton momentum; (b) quark/gluon structure of the bound nucleon at x > 0.1 and the dynamical mechanisms acting on it, by measuring the spectator momentum dependence of nuclear structure functions; (c) coherent effects in QCD, by exploring shadowing in tagged DIS on deuterium at x << 0.1. The JLab MEIC design (CM energy sqrt{s} = 15-50 GeV/nucleon, luminosity ~ 10^{34} cm^{-2} s^{-1}) provides polarized deuterium beams and excellent coverage and resolution for forward spectator tagging. We summarize the physics topics, the detector and beam requirements for spectator tagging, and on-going R&D efforts.

# Polarized light ions and spectator nucleon tagging at EIC

## Key claim

Spectator nucleon tagging in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from polarized deuterium at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) would enable a model-independent extraction of free neutron structure functions via on-shell extrapolation in the spectator proton momentum, provided that forward detectors achieve complete coverage down to zero transverse recoil momentum and resolution of Δp_RT ≲ 20 MeV and Δα_R ≲ 10^{-3}.

## Method

The authors propose measuring the conditional DIS cross section e + D → e' + p + X as a function of the recoil proton's light-cone fraction α_R and transverse momentum p_RT, then removing the pole factor 1/(t - M_N^2)^2 and extrapolating to the unphysical pole at t = M_N^2, analogous to the Chew-Low extrapolation used for pion structure. For bound nucleon structure studies, the ratio of nuclear structure functions is measured as a function of spectator momentum up to t - M_N^2 ∼ -0.5 GeV^2 to probe the connection to short-range NN correlations. For coherent scattering at x ≪ 0.1, the interference between diffractive amplitudes on the proton and neutron is observed directly in the recoil momentum dependence of the tagged cross section.

## Key numbers

- JLab MEIC design center-of-mass energy: √s = 15-50 GeV/nucleon
- JLab MEIC design luminosity: ∼ 10^{34} cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Required recoil momentum resolution: Δp_RT ≲ 20 MeV and Δα_R ≲ 10^{-3}
- Required recoil coverage: p_RT < 200 MeV, p_R∥/p(beam) ∼ 0.8-1.2
- Simulated on-shell extrapolation uses s_eD = 1000 GeV^2 and integrated luminosity 10^6 nb^{-1}
- Uncertainty in t from beam smearing: ∼ 0.005 GeV^2 (the bin size in the simulated extrapolation)
- Shadowing interference effect at p_RT ∼ 100 MeV can be as large as 10-20%
- CLAS BoNuS fixed-target experiment covers recoil momenta p_R ≳ 70 MeV
- Bound nucleon structure measurements extend to t - M_N^2 ∼ -0.5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the pioneering fixed-target spectator tagging program at JLab with 6 GeV beam energy (CLAS BoNuS detector, Tkachenko 2014) to the collider environment with polarized beams. The on-shell extrapolation method for neutron structure builds on theoretical work by Sargsian and Strikman (2005) and is analogous to Chew-Low extrapolation for pion structure. The shadowing predictions for tagged DIS on deuterium follow calculations by Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (2011). The proposed measurements would enable flavor decomposition of quark spin densities, test the Bjorken sum rule, resolve the origin of the EMC effect at x > 0.25, and provide detailed tests of QCD coherence and shadowing at small x, complementing studies with heavier nuclei.
