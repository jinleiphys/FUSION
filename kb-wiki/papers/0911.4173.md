# A new way to perform partial wave decompositions of few-nucleon forces

> We formulate a general and exact method of partial wave decomposition (PWD) of any nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and any three-nucleon (3N) force. The approach allows one to efficiently use symbolic algebra software to generate the interaction dependent part of the program code calculating the interaction. We demonstrate the feasibility of this approach for the one-boson exchange BonnB potential, a recent nucleon-nucleon chiral force and the chiral two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force. In all cases very good agreement between the new and the traditional PWD is found. The automated PWD offered by the new approach is of the utmost importance in view of future applications of numerous chiral N3LO contributions to the 3N force in three nucleon calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general and exact method for partial wave decomposition (PWD) of any nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and any three-nucleon (3N) force, using symbolic algebra software to generate the interaction-dependent code; the method is demonstrated to yield "very good agreement" with traditional PWD for the BonnB potential, a chiral NNLO force, and the chiral two-pion-exchange 3N force, but the authors note that for the 3N case the feasibility study is limited to a small set of 16 basis states with J=1/2 and positive parity.

## Method

The authors express the NN potential in terms of six scalar spin-momentum operators and determine the expansion coefficients by taking traces over spin operators, solving a system of six coupled linear equations. For the 3N force, they use a similar operator-based approach in the Jacobi momentum basis with LS-coupling, reducing the eight-dimensional integral to a five-dimensional one by exploiting scalar invariance. The resulting integrands are generated analytically using symbolic algebra software (Mathematica) and then integrated numerically with Gaussian quadrature.

## Key numbers

- For the BonnB potential, matrix elements were compared at fixed p0 = 1 fm^-1 as a function of p'; agreement between new and traditional PWD is described as "excellent"
- For the chiral NNLO 3N force test, the momenta were chosen as p = 1 fm^-1, q = 2 fm^-1, p' = 3 fm^-1, q' = 4 fm^-1
- The 3N force parameters used: g_A = 1.29, F_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_pi = 138.0 MeV/c^2, c_1 = -0.81 GeV^-1, c_3 = -3.4 GeV^-1, c_4 = 3.4 GeV^-1
- For the 3N matrix element G(1,1), the five-fold integral converged to 443.618 fm^5 with N=36 Gaussian points, matching the standard PWD value of 443.618 fm^5
- For G(2,1), the converged value was 1200.219 fm^5, matching the standard PWD value of 1200.219 fm^5
- For G(6,12), the converged value was -5.49290 fm^5, compared to standard PWD value of -5.49274 fm^5
- For G(5,11), the converged value was -5.48630 fm^5, compared to standard PWD value of -5.48597 fm^5
- For NN calculations below pion production threshold, convergence is reached for j_max <= 8

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous operator-based formulation of two- and three-nucleon systems (Golak et al., 2009) to perform partial wave decompositions automatically using symbolic algebra, in contrast to traditional helicity-based PWD methods (Bonn group, 1980s) and direct three-dimensional approaches (Charlier et al., Hang et al., Imam et al., Teheran et al.). The method enables efficient handling of the numerous chiral N3LO contributions to the three-nucleon force (Bernard, 2007; Ishikawa, 2007) that would be prohibitively complex with traditional PWD, and is well-suited for implementation on parallel computing architectures for full-scale calculations.
