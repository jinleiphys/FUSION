# Emergent Hadrons and Diquarks

> We discuss the emergence of a low-energy effective theory with quarks, mesons, diquarks and baryons at vanishing and finite baryon density from first principle QCD. The present work also includes an overview on diquarks at vanishing and finite density, and elucidates the physics of transitional changes from baryonic matter to quark matter including diquarks. This set-up is discussed within the functional renormalisation group approach with dynamical hadronisation. In this framework it is detailed how mesons, diquarks, and baryons emerge dynamically from the renormalisation flow of the QCD effective action. Moreover, the fundamental degrees of freedom of QCD, quarks and gluons, decouple from the dynamics of QCD below the respective mass gaps. The resulting global picture unifies the different low energy effective theories used for low and high densities within QCD, and allows for a determination of the respective low energy constants directly from QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy effective theories (LEFTs) with quarks, mesons, diquarks, and baryons emerge dynamically from first-principle QCD via the functional renormalisation group (fRG) with dynamical hadronisation, but this emergence is systematically established only for baryon chemical potential to temperature ratios up to about 4, leaving larger densities to phenomenological studies that can be progressively embedded into QCD.

## Method

The authors use the functional renormalisation group approach with dynamical hadronisation (rebosonisation) to describe how composite fields such as mesons, diquarks, and baryons appear as dynamical degrees of freedom from the renormalisation flow of the QCD effective action. At lower momentum scales, gapped fundamental degrees of freedom (quarks and gluons) decouple from the off-shell dynamics, leaving only the lowest-lying hadronic states. The framework is applied to a simplified Quark-Meson-Diquark model Lagrangian, extending previous work on two-colour QCD to the three-colour case.

## Key numbers

- The first onset of baryon density occurs at baryon chemical potential mu_b = M_N - epsilon_B approximately 923 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter, where M_N approximately 939 MeV is the nucleon mass and epsilon_B approximately 16 MeV is the binding energy.
- Lattice-QCD estimates (with pion mass m_pi approximately 350 MeV) give the good diquark mass m_good = 694(22) MeV from diquark-diquark correlation decay.
- The maximum entropy method applied to lattice-QCD diquark spectral functions yields a diquark mass m_good = 0.60(2) GeV.
- The characteristic size of the diquark correlation inside hadrons is approximately 1 fm.
- Functional first-principles approaches to QCD currently provide quantitative predictions for baryon chemical potential restricted by mu_b/T less than or approximately 4.

## Context

This work extends the fRG-based dynamical hadronisation approach previously developed in Gies (2002) and Floerchinger (2009) to include diquarks and baryons alongside mesons, unifying the different low-energy effective theories used for low and high densities within QCD. It builds on prior Quark-Meson-Diquark model studies for two-colour QCD by Strodthoff (2011, 2013) and on the Quarkyonic Matter concept from McLerran (2007). The framework enables systematic determination of low-energy constants directly from QCD and provides a path to connect phenomenological studies at large densities with first-principle QCD calculations.
