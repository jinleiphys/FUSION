# Nucleon-Antinucleon Annihilation in Large Nc QCD with rho and omega Mesons

> We use classical/large Nc (number of colors) QCD to study nucleon--antinucleon annihilation at rest. We include pion, rho and omega fields in the classical dynamics, starting from the nonlinear sigma model. We begin with a spherical blob of pionic matter with zero baryon number and energy of twice the nucleon mass. This evolves according to the classical dynamics to pion and vector meson fields into the radiation zone. The radiation fields are quantized using the method of coherent states modified to include isospin and four momentum conservation. Empirical information is extracted from that coherent state. We find good agreement with data for single particle momentum and number spectra, and with branching ratios to many annihilation channels. All this emerges with nearly no free parameters.

## Key claim

The authors show that classical/large Nc QCD, including pion, rho, and omega fields, can reproduce the major features of nucleon-antinucleon annihilation at rest (single-particle momentum and number spectra, branching ratios) with nearly no free parameters, provided the initial pion blob size is fitted to the observed pion momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors model annihilation at rest by starting with a spherically symmetric blob of pionic matter of zero baryon number and energy equal to twice the nucleon mass, then evolve it using classical equations of motion derived from a gauged nonlinear sigma model that includes pion, rho, and omega fields. The asymptotic free fields in the radiation zone are quantized via coherent states with isospin and four-momentum conservation imposed, and all annihilation channels are obtained as projections of this single quantum state.

## Key numbers

* Initial blob size parameter a = 1.1 fm, fitted to reproduce the observed single-pion momentum distribution.
* Pion mass fixed at 140 MeV; vector meson (rho and omega) mass fixed at m = 770 MeV.
* Three values of the pion decay constant f_pi are used: 93 MeV (experimental), 75 MeV (gives correct nucleon mass in this formalism), and 62 MeV (another Skyrmion literature value); corresponding coupling constants g are 5.85, 7.26, and 8.78 respectively.
* Observed mean pion multiplicity of 5 with variance of 1 is reproduced; the calculated fraction of secondary pions from resonance decays is 30%, compared to the experimental 33%.
* For the combined I=0 and I=1 isospin channels (equal mixture), the branching ratio for the pi+ pi- channel and the pi+ pi- pi0 channel show the largest relative disagreement with data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Skyrme-model treatment of nucleon-antinucleon annihilation (Amado et al., 1994) that included only pions and omega mesons, by adding the rho meson field through a massive Yang-Mills formulation. The approach builds on the classical/large Nc QCD framework of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979), and on the Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961) for baryons as topological solitons. The inclusion of vector mesons enables the model to dynamically generate the observed fraction of pions from rho and omega decay without assuming these mesons were present in the baryons before annihilation, and the single coherent state with isospin and four-momentum constraints provides a unified description of all annihilation channels.
