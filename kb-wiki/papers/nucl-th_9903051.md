# Relation of hard and total cross sections to centrality

> We compare the fractions of the hard and geometric cross sections as a function of impact parameter. For a given definition of central collisions, we calculate the corresponding impact parameter and the fraction of the hard cross section contained within this cut. We use charm quark production as a definite example.

## Key claim

The fraction of the hard cross section contained within a centrality cut defined by the geometric cross section is significantly larger than the geometric fraction itself; for example, a 10% cut on the geometric cross section corresponds to 30-40% of the hard cross section for all systems considered, assuming no nuclear effects on hard probe production.

## Method

The author uses a three-parameter Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution, with parameters taken from electron scattering data where available, to calculate the nuclear overlap integral T_AB(b) for various symmetric and asymmetric nuclear collisions. The central fraction of the hard cross section f_AB is computed by integrating T_AB(b) over impact parameter up to a cut b_c, while the geometric cross section fraction f_geo is obtained by integrating the interaction probability [1 - exp(-T_AB sigma_NN)] over b up to b_c, using sigma_NN = 32 mb for SPS energies. Charm quark production at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is used as a specific example to illustrate the relationship between the two fractions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions, a central fraction of the hard cross section f_AB = 0.1 corresponds to b_c = 2.05 fm; for O+O collisions, the same f_AB = 0.1 gives b_c = 1.05 fm.
- For Au+Au collisions, f_AB = 0.01 corresponds to b_c = 0.52 fm; for O+O collisions, the same f_AB = 0.01 gives b_c = 0.33 fm.
- At b_c approximately 2R_A, the geometric fraction f_geo is about 0.75 for symmetric systems.
- For symmetric systems, the impact parameter corresponding to f_geo = 0.2 is b_c approximately 1.04 R_A.
- For Au+Au collisions, the central 10% of the geometric cross section (f_geo = 0.1) is obtained when b_c is about 4.7 fm, encompassing about 40% of the hard cross section (f_AB = 0.410).
- The central 5% of collisions (f_geo = 0.05) encompasses 17-23% of the hard cross section across all systems; the central 20% (f_geo = 0.2) garners 52-66% of the hard cross section.
- At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with MRS D-prime parton distributions, sigma_pp for charm quark pairs is 0.344 mb, and T_AB(0) for Au+Au is 29.3/mb, resulting in approximately 10 c-cbar pairs per Au+Au collision at b=0.
- With a 10% centrality cut (f_geo = 0.1), the average number of c-cbar pairs in Au+Au collisions is approximately 7.7.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Vogt (1999, revised from Ref. evw) by systematically comparing the hard and geometric cross section fractions as functions of impact parameter for a range of nuclear systems. The results enable experimentalists to translate centrality cuts defined by geometric cross section (e.g., from measured E_T distributions) into the corresponding fraction of hard probe production, which is essential for interpreting charm and other hard probe yields in heavy-ion collisions at SPS and LHC energies. The nuclear shape parameters are taken from electron scattering data of Vries (Ref. Vvv), and the formalism builds on standard nuclear overlap calculations described in Ref. EKL.
