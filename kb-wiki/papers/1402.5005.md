# Bound states -- from QED to QCD

> These lectures are divided into two parts. In Part 1 I discuss bound state topics at the level of a basic course in field theory: The derivation of the Schrödinger and Dirac equations from the QED Lagrangian, by summing Feynman diagrams and in a Hamiltonian framework. Less well known topics include the equal-time wave function of Positronium in motion and the properties of the Dirac wave function for a linear potential. The presentation emphasizes physical aspects and provides the framework for Part 2, which discusses the derivation of relativistic bound states at Born level in QED and QCD. A central aspect is the maintenance of Poincaré invariance. The transformation of the wave function under boosts is studied in detail in D=1+1 dimensions, and its generalization to D=3+1 is indicated. Solving Gauss' law for $A^0$ with a non-vanishing boundary condition leads to a linear potential for QCD mesons, and an analogous confining potential for baryons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the equal-time wave function of positronium in motion does not simply Lorentz contract as a classical rod would, but instead acquires a dynamic frame dependence due to the contribution of transverse photon exchange, which introduces a dependence on the exchanged energy and leads to Fock states with additional photons at lowest order when the bound state has nonzero momentum.

## Method

The author derives bound state equations by summing an infinite set of ladder Feynman diagrams in QED, obtaining a Dyson-Schwinger equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation at lowest order in the fine-structure constant. The analysis is then extended to moving frames by evaluating the kernel in Coulomb gauge, separating the instantaneous Coulomb part from the transverse photon propagator, and studying the time-ordered contributions that define equal-time Fock state wave functions.

## Key numbers

* Positronium binding energies at lowest order: \(E_b = -\frac{1}{4} m_e \alpha^2 / n^2 \simeq -6.8\) eV\(/n^2\) for principal quantum number \(n=1,2,\ldots\)
* Bohr momentum scale for non-relativistic bound states: \(|\mathbf{q}| \sim \alpha m_e\) and \(q^0 \sim \mathbf{q}^2/2m_e \sim \frac{1}{2}\alpha^2 m_e\)
* The hyperfine splitting in positronium is calculated to be \(\Delta\nu_{QED} = 203.39169(41)\) GHz, while a new measurement gives \(\Delta\nu_{EXP} = 203.3941 \pm 0.0016 \pm 0.0011\) GHz
* The Born diagram for elastic \(e^+e^-\) scattering scales as \(G \sim 1/\alpha\), and ladder diagrams with any number of photon exchanges are also of order \(1/\alpha\)

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of bound states in QED (deriving the Schrodinger and Dirac equations from the QED Lagrangian) to a fully relativistic, frame-independent framework that maintains Poincare invariance. It builds on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism of Salpeter and Bethe (1951) and the NRQED effective theory of Caswell (1985), and it provides a foundation for analogous treatments of QCD bound states, where solving Gauss' law with a non-vanishing boundary condition leads to a linear confining potential for mesons and baryons. The analysis of moving positronium wave functions follows the work of Jarvinen (2004) and clarifies how equal-time wave functions transform under boosts, enabling future studies of parton distributions and parton-hadron duality in QCD.
