# Jost function formalism with complex potential

> The Jost function formalism is extended with use of the complex potential in this paper. We derive the Jost function by taking into account the dual state which is defined by the complex conjugate the complex Hamiltonian. By using the unitarity of the S-matrix which is defined by the Jost function, the optical theorem with the complex potential is also derived. The role of the imaginary part of the complex potential for both the bound states and the scattering states is figured out. The numerical calculation is performed by using the complex Woods-Saxon potential, and some numerical results are demonstrated to confirmed the properties of extended Jost function formalism.

## Key claim

The Jost function formalism is extended to systems with a complex (non-Hermitian) potential by introducing a dual Hilbert space defined by the complex conjugate Hamiltonian, enabling the description of bound states, resonances, and scattering states within a unified framework. The authors derive the generalized optical theorem and show that the imaginary part of the potential produces negative imaginary energy shifts for bound states, but note that these shifts are not qualitatively consistent with previous microscopic particle-vibration coupling (PVC) calculations.

## Method

The authors derive the Jost function and its dual counterpart by solving the Schrodinger equation with a complex Woods-Saxon potential, using Green's function techniques and the Wronskian formalism to connect regular and irregular solutions. They define the S-matrix as the ratio of Jost functions and derive the generalized unitarity condition \(S^*_{lj}(k)\tilde{S}_{lj}(k)=1\) for real momentum \(k\), from which the generalized optical theorem follows. Numerical calculations are performed on the complex momentum and energy planes to locate the zeros of the Jost functions, which correspond to bound states and resonances.

## Context

This work extends the Jost function formalism previously developed for the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework by Mizuyama et al. (recent work) to handle complex optical potentials, which are central to the optical model of nucleon-nucleus scattering. The results are compared with microscopic optical potentials based on particle-vibration coupling (PVC) as developed by Mizuyama (2015, 2016), Hao (2017), and Branchon (2019), which successfully reproduce experimental scattering data without fitting parameters. The formalism enables future investigation of the relationship between the Riemann surface structure of the complex optical potential and the multiple Riemann sheets expected from coupled-channel methods, and may help construct a unified optical potential applicable to both nuclear structure (single-particle levels and fragmentation) and nuclear reactions.
