# Meson Mass Spectrum from Relativistic Equations in Configuration Space

> A method is described for solving relativistic quasi-potential equations in configuration space. The Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze and an equal-time equation, both relativistic covariant two-body equations containing the full Dirac structure of positive and negative energy states, are studied in detail. These equations are solved for a system of two constituent quarks interacting through a potential consisting of a one-gluon exchange part with running coupling constant plus a linear confining potential which is mostly scalar and partly vector, and the spectrum of all light and heavy mesons is calculated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two relativistic quasi-potential equations (Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze and equal-time) can be solved in configuration space for quark-antiquark systems, yielding meson mass spectra that reproduce experimental data, but only when the confining potential is mostly scalar with at most a small vector admixture (less than 20%) to avoid deconfinement in certain channels.

## Method

The authors Fourier transform the BSLT and equal-time quasi-potential equations from momentum space to configuration space, then project them onto basis states with definite parity and total angular momentum J to obtain coupled integro-differential equations. They solve these equations using cubic Hermite spline expansions with a cutoff at maximum distance, explicitly handling the singular behavior at x=0 induced by the Coulomb-like one-gluon-exchange potential through a factor x^gamma. The interaction consists of a one-gluon-exchange part with running coupling constant plus a linear confining potential that is mostly scalar with a possible vector fraction epsilon, examined in both Feynman and Coulomb gauges.

## Key numbers

- Regge slope beta approximately 1.2 GeV^2 from experiment; the authors use kappa = 0.33 GeV^2 for linear scalar confining potential
- For the BSLT equation with m=0.2 GeV, kappa=0.2 GeV^2, epsilon=alpha=c=0, the ground state 1S0 solution is found at M=1.23 GeV
- Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV and n_F=3 used for the running coupling constant
- Typical numerical parameters: x_max = 2 fm with k=30 splines for heavy quark systems, up to x_max = 5 fm with k=120 splines for the pion system
- Vector admixture epsilon > 0.2 makes the potential repulsive at large distances in the 1J_J^e channel, setting an upper bound on allowed vector confinement

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pi and rho mesons (Tiemeijer and Tjon, 1992) to the full light and heavy meson spectrum using relativistic covariant approximations to the Bethe-Salpeter equation. It builds on the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze approximation (Logunov and Tavkhelidze, 1963; Blankenbecler and Sugar, 1966) extended to unequal masses by Cooper and Jennings, and the equal-time approximation of Wallace and Mandelzweig (1988) which improves upon Salpeter's (1952) original equal-time approach. The method enables future calculations of electromagnetic form factors at high momentum transfer, as previously demonstrated for the pion and rho meson.
