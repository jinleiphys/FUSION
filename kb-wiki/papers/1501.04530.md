# Chaoticity and Coherence in Bose-Einstein Condensation and Correlations

> We review the properties of chaoticity and coherence in Bose-Einstein condensation and correlations, for a dense boson system in its mean-field represented approximately by a harmonic oscillator potential. The order parameter and the nature of the phase transition from the chaotic to the condensate states are studied for different fixed numbers of bosons. The two-particle correlation function in momentum space is calculated to investigate how the Bose-Einstein correlation depends on the degree of condensation and other momentum variables. We generalize the Bose-Einstein correlation analysis to three-particle correlations to show its dependence on the degree of condensation.

# Chaoticity and Coherence in Bose-Einstein Condensation and Correlations

## Key claim

The transition from a completely chaotic state (condensate fraction f0=0) to a completely coherent state (f0→1) is a second-order-type transition under a gradual decrease of the order parameter T/ħω, not a first-order phase transition; however, the "chaoticity" parameter λ extracted from two-particle correlations is a sensitive function of both the average pair momentum p and temperature T, and λ=1 is not a consistent measure of the absence of condensate fraction, as it attains unity in some kinematic regions for significantly coherent systems at temperatures much below Tc.

## Method

The authors place N identical bosons in a spherical harmonic oscillator potential (arising either externally or from the bosons' own mean fields) and solve the condensate number condition using a canonical ensemble for the n=0 ground state and a grand canonical ensemble for the n>0 excited states. They calculate the fugacity z as a function of the order parameter T/ħω, then evaluate the one-body and two-body density matrices in momentum space to obtain the two-particle momentum correlation function C(p,q) and the three-particle correlation function, using the known harmonic oscillator wave functions and the canonical fluctuation for the condensate state.

## Key numbers

- For N=2000 bosons, the critical order parameter Tc/ħω = 10.97 (from fitting) and 11.00 (from analytical formula).
- For N=1000 bosons, Tc/ħω = 8.56 (fitting) and 8.53 (analytical).
- For N=500 bosons, Tc/ħω = 6.63 (fitting) and 6.62 (analytical).
- For N=250 bosons, Tc/ħω = 5.12 (fitting) and 5.13 (analytical).
- The critical order parameter and boson number obey Tc/ħω = 0.6777 N^{0.36666}.
- For a pion system with r_rms=10 fm and N=2000, the condensate fraction f0 is about 0.67–0.8; for N=250, f0 is about 0.9.
- For N=2000 at T/ħω=12 (above Tc/ħω=10.97), the λ parameter is 1 for all p values.
- For N=2000 at T/ħω=9, λ rises from ~0.1 at p=ħ/a to 1 at p=2.4ħ/a.
- For N=2000 at T/ħω=6, λ starts close to zero at p=ħ/a and reaches unity at p=2.9ħ/a.
- For N=2000 at T/ħω=3, λ starts close to zero at p=ħ/a and reaches unity at p=3.1ħ/a.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Wong and Zhang, 2007) of how the chaoticity parameter λ depends on the degree of Bose-Einstein condensation for static non-relativistic and relativistic boson gases in a spherical mean-field harmonic oscillator potential. It builds on the foundational work of Politzer (1996) and Naraschewski and Glauber (1999) showing that BE condensation and BE correlations are intimately related, and on the investigation of Csörgő and Zimányi (1998) of the effect of BE condensation on two-pion correlations. The results enable quantitative comparison with three-particle correlation measurements such as those from the ALICE collaboration (2014) at the LHC, and provide a framework to interpret the experimentally observed increase of λ with average pair momentum seen by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC.
