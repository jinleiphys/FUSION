# Strange Stars with a Density-Dependent Bag Parameter

> We have studied strange quark stars in the framework of the MIT bag model, allowing the bag parameter B to depend on the density of the medium. We have also studied the effect of Cooper pairing among quarks, on the stellar structure. Comparison of these two effects shows that the former is generally more significant. We studied the resulting equation of state of the quark matter, stellar mass-radius relation, mass-central-density relation, radius-central-density relation, and the variation of the density as a function of the distance from the centre of the star. We found that the density-dependent B allows stars with larger masses and radii, due to stiffening of the equation of state. Interestingly, certain stellar configurations are found to be possible only if B depends on the density. We have also studied the effect of variation of the superconducting gap parameter on our results.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the MIT bag parameter $B$ has a more significant effect on the equation of state and stellar structure of strange quark stars than the Cooper pairing (colour superconductivity) of quarks, and certain stellar configurations are possible only when $B$ depends on density, even when they are ruled out under the constant-$B$ assumption.

## Method

The authors model strange quark stars in the colour-flavour-locked (CFL) phase using the MIT bag model, allowing the bag parameter $B$ to vary with baryon number density $\rho$ according to three different parametrizations from the literature (Liu et al. 2001, Burgio et al. 2001, and Aguirre 2003). They derive the equation of state (EOS) for CFL matter including terms up to order $m_s^4$ and to leading order in $\Delta/\mu$, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius, mass-central-density, and radius-central-density relations for sequences of strange stars.

## Key numbers

- The three models of $B(\rho)$ have zero-density values: $B(0)=114$ MeV/fm$^3$ = (172 MeV)$^4$ for model (a); $B_0=200$ MeV/fm$^3$ = (198 MeV)$^4$ for model (b); $B(0)=291.59096$ MeV/fm$^3$ = (217.6 MeV)$^4$ for model (c).
- The window of stability for strange quark matter requires $B \geq 57$ MeV/fm$^3$ = (145 MeV)$^4$ on the lower bound; for $m_s=150$ MeV and $\Delta=100$ MeV, $B$ must be less than about 117 MeV/fm$^3$, and for $\Delta=0$, $B$ must be less than about 77 MeV/fm$^3$.
- The mass-radius curves cross the constraint $M/M_\odot \simeq 0.15 R$ (in km) from Cottam et al. (2002) at $M/M_\odot \simeq 1.3$–$1.4$ across all three models.
- The strange quark mass is set to $m_s=150$ MeV and the gap parameter $\Delta$ is varied between 0 and 150 MeV in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the study of strange stars in the CFL phase by Lugones and Horvath (2002), who treated the bag constant as fixed, by incorporating a density-dependent bag parameter motivated by the analogy with the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model and by results from Liu et al. (2001), Burgio et al. (2001), and Aguirre (2003). It enables more realistic modelling of compact stars that can be compared with observational constraints such as the redshift measurements from EXO0748-676 by Cottam et al. (2002), and it opens the door to similar studies for hybrid stars and to including density-dependent gap parameters and perturbative corrections.
