# Plane correlations and hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions

> We use a nonlinear response formalism to describe the event plane correlations measured by the ATLAS collaboration. With one exception ($\left\langle \cos(2Ψ_2 - 6Ψ_3 + 4 Ψ_4) \right\rangle$), the event plane correlations are qualitatively reproduced by considering the linear and quadratic response to the lowest cumulants. For the lowest harmonics such as $\left\langle \cos(2Ψ_2+3Ψ_3 - 5Ψ_5) \right\rangle$, the correlations are quantitatively reproduced, even when the naive Glauber model prediction has the wrong sign relative to experiment. The quantitative agreement for the higher plane correlations (especially those involving $Ψ_6$) is not as good. The centrality dependence of the correlations is naturally explained as an average of the linear and quadratic response.

## Key claim

The nonlinear response formalism, using only linear and quadratic response to the lowest cumulants of the initial state, qualitatively reproduces the ATLAS event plane correlations for most observables, with the exception of the correlation \(\left\langle \cos(2\Psi_2 - 6\Psi_3 + 4 \Psi_4) \right\rangle\); quantitative agreement is achieved for the lowest harmonics such as \(\left\langle \cos(2\Psi_2+3\Psi_3 - 5\Psi_5) \right\rangle\), even when the naive Glauber model prediction has the wrong sign, but the agreement for higher plane correlations (especially those involving \(\Psi_6\)) is not as good.

## Method

The authors used a nonlinear response formalism based on 2+1D second-order BRSSS hydrodynamics with a constant shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\), an equation of state parametrizing lattice results (Laine 2006), and a constant freeze-out temperature \(T_{\rm fo}=150\) MeV. The initial state was characterized by cumulants of the transverse energy density (up to \(\mathcal{E}_6\)), and the linear and quadratic response coefficients (\(w_n\) and \(w_{n(pq)}\)) were calculated by perturbing a cylindrically symmetric Gaussian background geometry and running the perturbed Gaussian through the hydrodynamic tool chain. The event plane correlations were then computed by weighting these response functions with the spectrum of fluctuations from a Monte Carlo Glauber model (Phobos model, Alver 2008), and compared to ATLAS measurements using both high-resolution and low-resolution limits of the experimental event plane method.

## Key numbers

* The correlation \(\left\langle \cos(2\Psi_2+3\Psi_3 - 5\Psi_5) \right\rangle\) is quantitatively reproduced by the formalism, even when the naive Glauber model prediction has the wrong sign relative to experiment.
* The correlation \(\left\langle \cos(2\Psi_2 - 6\Psi_3 + 4 \Psi_4) \right\rangle\) is the one exception that is not qualitatively reproduced by the linear and quadratic response to the lowest cumulants.
* The shear viscosity to entropy ratio was set to the canonical value of \(1/4\pi\) throughout the hydrodynamic evolution.
* The freeze-out temperature was set to \(T_{\rm fo}=150\) MeV.
* The rms radius of the Gaussian initial condition was adjusted to match the rms radius of a smooth Glauber model for a given centrality, and the overall constant was adjusted to reproduce the measured \(dN_{\rm ch}/dy\) at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear response formalism developed by Teaney and Yan (2012) to describe the event plane correlations measured by the ATLAS collaboration (2013), following earlier event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations by Qiu and Heinz (2012) and Gardim et al. (2011) that showed mode mixing between harmonics. The formalism provides a transparent link between initial state fluctuations and final state correlations, suggesting that most microscopic details of the initial state beyond the lowest cumulants are irrelevant; this enables extraction of a limited number of initial state parameters from experiment for comparison with frameworks such as the Color Glass Condensate (Gale et al., 2012).
