# Thermal noise in a boost-invariant matter expansion in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We formulate a general theory of thermal fluctuations within causal second-order viscous hydrodynamic evolution of matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The fluctuation is treated perturbatively on top of a boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. Numerical simulation of thermal noise is performed for a lattice QCD equation of state and for various second-order dissipative evolution equations. Phenomenological effects of thermal fluctuations on the two-particle rapidity correlations are studied.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, treated as a perturbation on top of a boost-invariant (Bjorken) longitudinal expansion, produce measurable two-particle rapidity correlations whose magnitude and structure depend on the choice of second-order causal dissipative hydrodynamic formalism (Muller-Israel-Stewart versus Chapman-Enskog-like) and on the equation of state (conformal versus lattice QCD).

## Method

The authors formulate a linearized theory of thermal fluctuations around a boost-invariant background expansion, deriving evolution equations for the fluctuating energy density, flow velocity, and shear pressure tensor coupled to a stochastic noise term. They numerically solve these equations using the MacCormack predictor-corrector method for two second-order dissipative formalisms (Muller-Israel-Stewart and Chapman-Enskog-like) with both a conformal equation of state (p = epsilon/3) and a lattice QCD-based equation of state (s95p-PCE). The fluctuating fields at freeze-out are then used to compute two-particle rapidity correlations via the Cooper-Frye prescription.

## Context

This work extends the theory of hydrodynamic fluctuations from the relativistic Navier-Stokes (first-order) framework of Kapusta et al. (2011) and Young et al. (2013, 2014) into causal second-order dissipative hydrodynamics based on the Muller-Israel-Stewart formalism and the Chapman-Enskog-like approach of Jaiswal et al. (2013) and Bhalerao et al. (2013). The numerical treatment enables study of thermal noise effects with realistic lattice QCD equations of state, going beyond the analytic solutions previously available only for conformal fluids in Navier-Stokes theory. The results enable future comparisons with experimental two-particle correlation measurements at RHIC and LHC to assess whether thermal fluctuations contribute observably to long-range rapidity correlations.
