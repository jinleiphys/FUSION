# D=2 gluon condensate and QCD propagators at finite temperature

> We calculate the dimension two gluon condensate contribution to quark, gluon and ghost propagators at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the contribution of the dimension-two gluon condensate \(\langle A^2 \rangle\) to the quark, gluon, and ghost propagators at finite temperature, but the results are restricted to the lowest Matsubara mode in the plane-wave method and are gauge-dependent (evaluated in covariant gauges, with explicit dependence on the gauge parameter \(\xi\)).

## Method

The authors use the operator product expansion (OPE) and the plane-wave method to compute the non-perturbative contributions of the electric \(\langle A_0^2 \rangle_T\) and magnetic \(\langle A_i^2 \rangle_T\) gluon condensates to the self-energies of quarks, gluons, and ghosts at finite temperature. The non-perturbative gluon propagator is decomposed into longitudinal, transverse, and mixed tensor structures, and the condensates are related to the zero-Matsubara-mode moments of these propagator components. The resulting self-energy expressions are given in terms of the condensates and the gauge parameter, with the Slavnov-Taylor identity used to reduce the number of independent components.

## Key numbers

* The dimension-two gluon condensate contribution to the quark self-energy scalar functions is: \(a(p_0,p) = -\frac{2\pi\alpha_s}{N_c P^2} \left[\frac{1}{3}\langle A_i^2 \rangle_T - \langle A_0^2 \rangle_T\right]\) and \(b(p_0,p) = -\frac{2\pi\alpha_s p_0}{N_c P^2} \left[2\langle A_0^2 \rangle_T +\frac{2}{3} \langle A_i^2 \rangle_T\right]\).
* The ghost self-energy from the \(D=2\) condensate is: \(\Pi(p_0,p) = \frac{4 \pi \alpha_s N_c}{N_c^2 - 1} \left[ \frac{p_0^2}{P^2} \langle A_0^2 \rangle_T - \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{4}{3} - \frac{p_0^2}{P^2}\right) \langle A_i^2 \rangle_T\right]\).
* The gluon self-energy scalar functions \(\pi_l^{\langle A^2 \rangle}\), \(\pi_t^{\langle A^2 \rangle}\), \(\pi_m^{\langle A^2 \rangle}\), and \(\widetilde{\pi}^{\langle A^2 \rangle}\) are each expressed as linear combinations of \(\langle A_0^2 \rangle_T\) and \(\langle A_i^2 \rangle_T\) with coefficients that are functions of \(p_0\), \(p\), and the gauge parameter \(\chi = 1 - \xi\).

## Context

This work extends the OPE approach to finite-temperature QCD propagators by including the dimension-two gluon condensate, following earlier studies of the \(D=4\) gluon condensate contribution to the quark self-energy by Schaefer (1998) and to the gluon self-energy by Chakraborty (2011). The results enable quantitative predictions for the analytic structure of quark, gluon, and ghost propagators near the deconfinement temperature when combined with lattice QCD values for the condensates, and provide a starting point for estimating non-perturbative dilepton rates and transport properties in the quark-gluon plasma.
