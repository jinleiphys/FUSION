# Magnetic moment of hyperons in nuclear matter by using quark-meson coupling models

> We calculate the magnetic moments of hyperons in dense nuclear matter by using relativistic quark models. Hyperons are treated as MIT bags, and the interactions are considered to be mediated by the exchange of scalar and vector mesons which are approximated as mean fields. Model dependence is investigated by using the quark-meson coupling model and the modified quark-meson coupling model; in the former the bag constant is independent of density and in the latter it depends on density. Both models give us the magnitudes of the magnetic moments increasing with density for most octet baryons. But there is a considerable model dependence in the values of the magnetic moments in dense medium. The magnetic moments at the nuclear saturation density calculated by the quark meson coupling model are only a few percents larger than those in free space, but the magnetic moments from the modified quark meson coupling model increase more than 10% for most hyperons. The correlations between the bag radius of hyperons and the magnetic moments of hyperons in dense matter are discussed.

# Magnetic moment of hyperons in nuclear matter by using quark-meson coupling models

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of hyperons in dense nuclear matter show a strong model dependence: the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model predicts changes of only a few percent at saturation density, while the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model predicts increases of more than 10% for most hyperons, with the difference attributed primarily to whether the bag constant is density-independent (QMC) or density-dependent (MQMC).

## Method

The authors treat hyperons as MIT bags and describe the nuclear medium via mean-field scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) meson fields coupled to the light (u, d) quarks. They compute the magnetic moments of all octet baryons (p, n, Lambda, Sigma+, Sigma0, Sigma-, Xi0, Xi-) using SU(6) wave functions and the quark current operator, then evaluate the density dependence of the magnetic moments up to four times nuclear saturation density for both the QMC model (density-independent bag constant) and the MQMC model (density-dependent bag constant with an additional coupling parameter g'^B_sigma).

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3), the QMC model gives r_N (ratio of in-medium to free magnetic moment) = 1.053 for R_0 = 0.8 fm, while the MQMC model gives r_N = 1.246 for the same R_0.
- For the Lambda hyperon at saturation density, the QMC model yields r_Lambda = 0.997 (R_0 = 0.8 fm), essentially unchanged from free space; the MQMC model yields r_Lambda = 1.103 (R_0 = 0.8 fm), an increase of about 10%.
- The bag radius of a Lambda hyperon in the QMC model at saturation density is about 99.6% of its free-space value.
- In the MQMC model, the bag radius of nucleons increases by about 10-20% at saturation density.
- The QMC model predicts r_Sigma- = 1.077 (R_0 = 0.8 fm) and r_Xi- = 0.976 (R_0 = 0.8 fm) at saturation density; the MQMC model predicts r_Sigma- = 1.143 and r_Xi- = 1.032 for the same R_0.
- The R_0-dependence of r_B(rho_0) in the QMC model is less than 5%; in the MQMC model it is less than 2%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of in-medium nucleon magnetic moments by Saito and Thomas (1995) using the QMC model to the full octet of hyperons, and introduces the MQMC model for comparison. The MQMC model, which produces sigma and omega meson fields similar to those from Dirac phenomenology and quantum hadrodynamics, yields substantially larger medium modifications than the QMC model. The results are timely because the magnetic moment of a Lambda hyperon in the hypernucleus ^7_Lambda Li has been measured at BNL with preliminary results, and further experiments are planned at J-PARC. The strong correlation found between the bag radius and the magnetic moment in medium provides a diagnostic for future measurements to distinguish between the two models.
