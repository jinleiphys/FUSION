# A search of multiparticle correlations in 10.7 A GeV $^{197}Au$ and 200 A GeV $^{32}S$ interactions with emulsion nuclei by the Hurst method

> An analysis of pseudorapidity correlations in 10.7 A Gev $^{197}Au$ and 200 A GeV $^{32}S$ interactions with emulsion nuclei by the normalized range method has been carried out. The evidence for events with large multiparticle correlations is presented. The most significant "correlation force" effect is apparent in the interactions of light emulsion nuclei (CNO group) and gold nuclei at an energy of 10.7 A GeV, corresponding to an absolute disintegration of the target nucleus $n_b+n_g\simeq 0$.

## Key claim
The normalized range (Hurst) method reveals events with large multiparticle correlations in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, with the most significant "correlation force" effect appearing specifically in central interactions of gold nuclei with light emulsion nuclei (CNO group) at 10.7 A GeV, corresponding to events where the target nucleus undergoes complete disintegration (n_b + n_g approximately 0).

## Method
The authors applied the normalized range (Hurst) method to pseudorapidity distributions of secondary particles from 10.7 A GeV Au and 200 A GeV S interactions with nuclear emulsion. They divided a pseudorapidity interval of delta eta = 4 into 8192 bins, computed the normalized range H(k') = R(k')/S(k') as a function of bin size, and extracted the Hurst index h from power-law fits. Events were classified as correlated if the average event-level Hurst index h_ev exceeded 0.62, and the results were compared to FRITIOF 7.02 model simulations and random HRNDM-generated events.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Em at 10.7 A GeV: 315 experimental events analyzed; 187 events had h_ev greater than 0.62, and 16 events had h_ev greater than 0.8.
- For S+Em at 200 A GeV: 264 experimental events analyzed; 97 events had h_ev greater than 0.62, and zero events had h_ev greater than 0.8.
- The pseudorapidity interval used was delta eta = 4: for S at 200 A GeV, eta_min = 1.0 and eta_max = 5.0; for Au at 10.7 A GeV, eta_min = 0.3 and eta_max = 4.3.
- The threshold for event classification was h_ev greater than 0.62; events with h_ev greater than 0.7 or 0.8 were analyzed as more strongly correlated subsets.
- The compound system producing the largest correlations was estimated to produce approximately 40 percent of secondary particles for Au+Em and approximately 24 percent for S+Em.

## Context
This work extends the normalized range method previously proposed by Lebedev (no year given) for analyzing pseudorapidity correlations in multiparticle production, applying it to Au+Em and S+Em data from BNL/AGS and CERN/SPS. The results contradict the predictions of the FRITIOF 7.02 model and random HRNDM simulations, which do not reproduce the observed kink in the correlation curves. The authors suggest that if confirmed by other experiments (e.g., Pb+CNO interactions), these findings could indicate that asymmetric heavy-ion collisions may be more promising than symmetric ones (such as Pb+Pb or Au+Au studied by NA44, NA49, WA98) for observing quark-gluon plasma formation.
