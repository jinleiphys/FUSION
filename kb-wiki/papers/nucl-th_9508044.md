# K^+ production in p-C-collisions at a beam energy 1.2 GeV

> The isobar model and the resonance model are applied for the first analysis of the subthreshold $K^+$-meson production in proton-carbon collisions, which was perfomed at GSI at an emission angle of 40 degrees and a bombarding energy of 1.2 GeV. In this study, we focus on the role of the secondary processes $πN \to K^+ Y$ in normal nuclear matter density. It turns out that the present approach can reproduce very well both the $π^+$- and $K^+$- meson spectra. It is also demonstrated that the different kinds of descriptions for the $πN \to K^+ Λ$ reactions substantially differentiate the calculated results for the $p A \to K^+ X$ differential cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper claims that subthreshold $K^+$-meson production in proton-carbon collisions at 1.2 GeV can be reproduced by secondary $\pi N \to K^+ Y$ processes in normal nuclear matter density, but only if the angular distribution of the $\pi N \to K^+ \Lambda$ reaction is described by a resonance model rather than a simpler parametrization.

## Method

The authors applied the isobar model for $\pi^+$ production via $\Delta$ resonance decay and the resonance model for $K^+$ production via secondary $\pi N \to K^+ Y$ reactions, using a spectral function for the carbon target parametrized from $(e,e'C)$ and $(\gamma,\gamma'C)$ scattering data. The differential cross sections were calculated by convoluting the production cross sections with the target spectral function and an effective collision number $N_{eff}$ accounting for absorption and target mass dependence. The calculations were compared to GSI data for $\pi^+$ and $K^+$ spectra at 40 degrees emission angle and 1.2 GeV beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1.2 GeV; emission angle: 40 degrees
- Slope parameter for carbon spectral function: $\alpha = 82$ MeV/c
- Effective collision number parametrization: $N_{eff} = A^{0.74 \pm 0.01}$
- Total $K^+N$ cross section: $\sigma(KN) \simeq 12$ mb
- Angular distribution parametrization for $\pi N \to K^+ \Lambda$: $\eta(\sqrt{s}) = 10.9 \sqrt{s} - 17.6$ for threshold $< \sqrt{s} < 1.7$ GeV, and $\eta = 1$ for $\sqrt{s} > 1.7$ GeV
- Kaon momentum range for predicted angular distribution: $p_K = 0.5$ to $0.7$ GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kapusta, Cassing, Cleymans, and Sibirtsev (all cited in the paper) who suggested kaons in heavy ion collisions may be produced through secondary processes in the pure hadronic phase rather than from hot compressed matter. The analysis was motivated by GSI $KaoS$ collaboration measurements (Senger, cited in the paper) which found that first-step processes $pN \to K^+ \Lambda N$ alone underestimated the $K^+$ differential cross sections. The results support the scenario of Cassing et al. (cited in the paper) that $K^+$ mesons are produced mainly through secondary $\pi N \to K Y$ processes under normal nuclear matter conditions, and the authors urge experimental measurement of kaon angular distributions to differentiate between models.
