# Analyzing power in nucleon-deuteron scattering and three-nucleon forces

> Three-nucleon forces have been considered to be one possibility to resolve the well known discrepancy between experimental values and theoretical calculations of the nucleon analyzing power in low energy nucleon-deuteron scattering. In this paper, we investigate possible effects of two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces on the analyzing power and the differential cross section. We found that the reason for different effects on the analyzing power by different three-nucleon forces found in previous calculations is related to the existence of the contact term. Effects of some variations of two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces are investigated. Also, an expression for the measure of the nucleon analyzing power with quartet P-wave phase shifts is presented.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between experimental and calculated nucleon analyzing power in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering can be partially addressed by two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces, but only if the contact term is removed; the resulting increase in analyzing power is primarily a consequence of reproducing the triton binding energy rather than a direct spin-dependent effect, and no three-nucleon force model tested simultaneously resolves the analyzing power puzzle and preserves agreement with tensor analyzing power data.

## Method

The author solved the Faddeev equation in coordinate space for neutron-deuteron elastic scattering at a laboratory energy of 3 MeV, using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential as input. Various two-pion exchange three-nucleon force models (Brazil prime, Brazil, Brazil delta, and individual term-by-term variants) were added to the Hamiltonian, with cutoff masses chosen to reproduce the triton binding energy. Partial waves were truncated at total two-nucleon angular momentum j ≤ 2 and total three-nucleon angular momentum J = 19/2, with three-nucleon forces switched off for J ≥ 9/2.

## Key numbers

- At Elab^n = 3 MeV, experimental neutron analyzing power Ay(104.0°) = 5.96 ± 0.13 %, while AV18 alone gives Ay(105°) = 4.29 %
- AV18+BR'_{700} gives Ay(105°) = 4.50 % (increase of 5 % relative to AV18) and triton binding energy B3 = 8.44 MeV
- AV18+BR_{700} gives Ay(105°) = 3.62 % (decrease relative to AV18) and B3 = 8.36 MeV
- AV18+BR_{Δ,800} gives Ay(105°) = 4.43 % and B3 = 8.37 MeV
- AV18+W_a (with a = -14.4 μ^{-1}) gives Ay(105°) = 5.93 %, nearly matching the experimental value, and B3 = 8.49 MeV
- The doublet scattering length changes from 1.35 fm for AV18 alone to 0.68 fm for AV18+BR_{700}
- Quartet P-wave phase shifts at 3 MeV: δ(^4P_{1/2}) = 24.2° for AV18, 24.9° for AV18+W_a; Δ_{3/2-1/2} = 1.9° for AV18, 0.2° for AV18+W_a; Δ_{5/2-3/2} = 0.1° for AV18, 1.4° for AV18+W_a

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Witała (1994), Ishikawa (1994), and Kievsky (1995, 1996) that showed conflicting effects of different three-nucleon force models on the analyzing power. The author resolves this conflict by identifying the contact term (present in the Tucson-Melbourne and Brazil three-nucleon forces, absent in Brazil prime and Urbana models) as the source of the discrepancy. The paper enables future searches for three-nucleon forces with spin-orbit character, as suggested by modifications to the ^3P_J nucleon-nucleon forces explored by Tornow (1998) and others, which might resolve the analyzing power puzzle without distorting tensor observables.
