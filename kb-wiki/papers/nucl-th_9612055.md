# Pion Absorption Cross Section for ^2H and ^3He in the Delta-Isobar Region: A Phenomenological Connection

> The absorption of π^+ on ^3He in the $Δ$-region is evaluated with exact inclusion of the final state interaction among the three emerging protons. The absorption is described by a $πN \to Δ$ vertex and a $NΔ- NN$ transition t-matrix which are calculated from a phenomenological model for NN and πd reactions. In a calculation where the initial pion scattering effects are neglected, the predicted peaks of the pion absorption cross sections for ^2H and ^3He lie too high in energy in relation to the data. The effect of the final state three-nucleon interaction turns out to be too small for changing the magnitude and shifting the peak position of the total absorption cross section for ^3He. We demonstrate that the adjustment of the peak position for the deuteron cross section by small modifications of the $Δ$-parameters, automatically leads to the correct peak position in ^3He.

## Key claim

The predicted peaks of the total pion absorption cross sections for both ^2H and ^3He lie too high in energy relative to data when initial pion scattering effects are neglected; however, adjusting the bare Δ-mass and coupling strength by about 2% to fit the deuteron peak position automatically yields the correct peak position for ^3He as well, pointing to a common absorption mechanism.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for the three-proton final state from π⁺ absorption on ^3He, using the Betz-Lee phenomenological model for the NN-NΔ transition t-matrix and πN→Δ vertex. They excluded initial pion scattering and propagating πNN intermediate states, and treated the bare Δ-mass M⁰_Δ and coupling F⁰_Δ as adjustable parameters to shift the resonance peak. The final-state interaction among the three protons was included exactly for the first time in such a calculation.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical peak position for the deuteron total absorption cross section is about 30 MeV too high compared to data when using the original Betz-Lee parameters.
- The bare Δ-mass was adjusted from M⁰_Δ = 1280 MeV to 1260 MeV (a change of about 1.6%).
- The bare coupling constant was adjusted from F⁰_Δ = 0.98 to 1.00 (a change of about 2%).
- The effect of final-state interactions among the three protons on the total absorption cross section for ^3He is only about 2% and too small to be visible on the scale of the figure.
- The peak of the pion absorption cross section for both ^2H and ^3He occurs at T_π ≈ 130 MeV, whereas elastic and inelastic cross sections peak around 170 MeV.
- The range parameter Λ_Δ = 358 MeV/c is used in the vertex function.

## Context

This work extends the Betz-Lee model (Betz and Lee, 1980) for NN and πd reactions to the three-nucleon system, using exact Faddeev treatment of final-state interactions for the first time in pion absorption on ^3He. It is motivated by experimental studies of π⁺^3He→3p by the LADS collaboration at PSI. The results enable future inclusion of initial-state interactions and the diagonal NΔ potential (as in the AV28 potential) to improve agreement with experimental Dalitz plots and peak positions.
