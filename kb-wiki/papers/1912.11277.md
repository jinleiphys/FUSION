# Magneto-vortical effect in strongly coupled plasma

> Based on a holographic model incorporating both chiral anomaly and gravitational anomaly, we study the effect of magneto-vortical coupling on transport properties of a strongly coupled plasma. The focus of present work is on the generation of a vector charge density and an axial current, as response to vorticity in a magnetized plasma. The transport coefficients parameterising the vector charge density and axial current are calculated both analytically (in the weak magnetic field limit) and also numerically (for general values of the magnetic field). We find the generation of vector charge receives both non-anomalous and anomalous contributions, with the non-anomalous contribution dominating in the limit of strong magnetic field and the anomalous contribution sensitive to both chiral anomaly and gravitational anomaly. On the contrary, we find the axial current is induced entirely due to the gravitational anomaly, thus we interpret the axial current generation as chiral vortical effect. The corresponding chiral vortical conductivity is found to be suppressed by the magnetic field. By Onsager relation, these transport coefficients are responsible for the generation of a thermal current due to a transverse electric field or a transverse axial magnetic field, which we call thermal Hall effect and thermal axial magnetic effect, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a strongly coupled plasma, the generation of a vector charge density in response to vorticity receives both non-anomalous and anomalous contributions, with the non-anomalous contribution dominating in the strong magnetic field limit; the axial current induced by vorticity is entirely due to the gravitational anomaly, and the corresponding chiral vortical conductivity is suppressed by the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors use a holographic model in AdS5 with both vector and axial gauge fields, incorporating Chern-Simons terms to mimic the chiral anomaly (alpha terms) and a mixed gauge-gravitational Chern-Simons term (lambda term) to model the gravitational anomaly. They perturb the background magnetic brane solution with a metric perturbation that mimics a fluid vorticity along the magnetic field direction, then solve the linearized bulk equations of motion in the adiabatic limit (omega to 0) and small momentum limit (q to 0). The transport coefficients for vector charge density and axial current are calculated analytically in the weak magnetic field limit (B/T^2 much less than 1) and numerically for general values of the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The vector charge density is parameterized as J^t = xi(B,T) (B dot Omega), with xi computed analytically at small B and numerically for general B.
- The axial current is parameterized as J_5 = sigma(B,T) Omega, with the chiral vortical conductivity sigma found to be suppressed by the magnetic field.
- In the weak field perturbative study, the leading order metric fluctuation is delta g_ty^[0] = (1 - r_h^4/r^4) h_ty, where r_h is the horizon radius related to temperature by T = f'(r_h)/(4 pi).
- The axial field fluctuation at leading order is delta tilde A_z^[0] = 8 lambda (r_h^4/r^4 + 2 log((r^2+r_h^2)/r^2)) h_ty.
- The vector potential fluctuation at first order in B contains terms proportional to alpha lambda, with coefficient 160/3 alpha lambda - 128 log 2 alpha lambda in the asymptotic expansion.

## Context

This work extends the free-theory analysis of Hattori and Yin (2016), who found vector charge generation from spin-vorticity coupling under the lowest Landau level approximation, to the opposite limit of strong coupling using holography. The holographic model builds on prior work by Erdmenger et al. (2010) and Landsteiner et al. (2011) that incorporated chiral and gravitational anomalies via Chern-Simons terms. The results enable the prediction of thermal Hall effect and thermal axial magnetic effect via Onsager relations, and test the robustness of the spectral flow mechanism for vector charge generation beyond the free theory limit.
