# Strange and charm baryon masses with two flavors of dynamical twisted mass fermions

> The masses of the low-lying strange and charm baryons are evaluated using two degenerate flavors of twisted mass sea quarks for pion masses in the range of about 260 MeV to 450 MeV. The strange and charm valence quark masses are tuned to reproduce the mass of the kaon and D-meson at the physical point. The tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action is employed. We use three values of the lattice spacing, corresponding to $β=3.9$, $β=4.05$ and $β=4.2$ with $r_0/a=5.22(2)$, $r_0/a=6.61(3)$ and $r_0/a=8.31(5)$ respectively. %spacings $a=0.0855(5)$ and $a=0.0667(3)$ determined from the pion decay constant. We examine the dependence of the strange and charm baryons on the lattice spacing and strange and charm quark masses. The pion mass dependence is studied and physical results are obtained using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to extrapolate to the physical point.

## Key claim

The masses of low-lying strange and charm baryons computed with two degenerate flavors of twisted mass sea quarks show a very weak dependence on the lattice spacing, with results fully compatible with an O(a²) behaviour having an almost vanishing coefficient of the a² term, which justifies neglecting the O(a²) term in extrapolating results to the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors used three values of the lattice spacing (β=3.9, 4.05, and 4.2) with tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action and two degenerate flavors of twisted mass sea quarks at pion masses from about 260 MeV to 450 MeV. Strange and charm valence quarks were added as Osterwalder-Seiler fermions, with their masses tuned to reproduce the physical kaon and D-meson masses after chiral extrapolation. Baryon correlators were computed using standard interpolating fields for octet and decuplet baryons, and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory was used to extrapolate results to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass range: approximately 260 MeV to 450 MeV
- Lattice spacings: a=0.089(1) fm at β=3.9 (r₀/a=5.22(2)), a=0.070(1) fm at β=4.05 (r₀/a=6.61(3)), and a=0.056(1) fm at β=4.2 (r₀/a=8.31(5))
- Tuned strange quark masses: aμ_s=0.0216(7) at β=3.9, aμ_s=0.0178(5) at β=4.05, aμ_s=0.014(1) at β=4.2
- Tuned charm quark masses: aμ_c=0.27(3) at β=3.9, aμ_c=0.21(1) at β=4.05, aμ_c=0.17(2) at β=4.2
- Sommer parameter r₀ continuum value: 0.462(5) fm

## Context

This work extends previous ETMC studies of light and strange baryons (Alexandrou 2008, 2009) by including a finer lattice spacing and calculating charm baryon masses in addition to strange baryons, enabling a better assessment of continuum extrapolation with three lattice spacings. The results are compared with those from the PACS-CS collaboration (Aoki 2008), BMW collaboration (Durr 2008), and LHPC (Walker-Loud 2008) for strange baryons, and with hybrid action studies using MILC configurations for charm baryons. The study enables future lattice QCD investigations of hadron structure by first establishing that lattice discretization effects are under control for the baryon spectrum.
