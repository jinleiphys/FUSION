# What exactly is a Skyrmion?

> Skyrmions are well known to be baryons because their topological charge has been positively identified with the baryon number. Beyond that their identity has never been clear. In view of the possibility of skyrmion production through Disoriented Chiral Condensates in heavy ion collisions, the exact identity of the skyrmion must be resolved before they can be identified in experiments. It is shown that skyrmions are not individual baryons but coherent states of known baryons and higher resonances on a compact manifold associated with the spin and flavor symmetry group. An outline of how to calculate exactly the probability amplitudes of the superposition of physical baryon and excited baryon states that make up the skyrmion is given.

## Key claim

Skyrmions are not individual baryons but coherent states of known baryons and higher resonances on a compact manifold associated with the spin and flavor symmetry group; the paper provides an outline for calculating the probability amplitudes of the superposition of physical baryon and excited baryon states that make up the skyrmion, but notes that the detailed construction and application to heavy ion collisions will be presented elsewhere.

## Method

The paper considers the Skyrme model for flavor SU(2) and quantizes the N=1 skyrmion using collective coordinates on the SU(2) manifold S^3, obtaining wavefunctions that are polynomials on the three-sphere. To construct coherent states that involve only physical fermionic baryon states (odd-degree polynomials), the author maps the SU(2) coordinates to SO(3) coordinates via the relation A t_i A^dag = t_j R_{ji}(A), where R is a 3x3 rotation matrix, and then applies the generalized Segal-Bargmann transform method of Hall (2000) and Toller (1999) to construct annihilation operators and coherent states on the SO(3) manifold.

## Key numbers

* The topological charge N in the boundary condition F(r=0) = N pi is identified with the baryon number; only N=1 is considered, corresponding to one baryon.
* The Hamiltonian on S^3 is H = -1/(8 lambda) sum_i del_i^2 + M, where the Laplacian on S^3 satisfies -nabla^2 (a_i + i a_j)^l = l(l+2) (a_i + i a_j)^l.
* Odd integer l gives half-integral spin and isospin states (fermionic baryons), while even integer l gives integral spin and isospin states (bosonic, unphysical for baryons).
* The simplest proton spin-up wavefunction is (1/pi)(a_1 + i a_2) and the neutron spin-up wavefunction is (i/pi)(a_0 + i a_3), both polynomials of degree 1.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model interpretation beyond the identification of topological charge with baryon number by Witten (1983) and the collective coordinate quantization by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983). It addresses a problem raised by Kowalski and Wosiek (2002) regarding how to confront experimental data on skyrmion production through Disoriented Chiral Condensates in heavy ion collisions. The paper enables future calculations of the probability that a skyrmion formed in heavy ion collisions will be observed as a specific baryon or excited baryon state, with the detailed construction and applications to be presented in a subsequent paper by the same author.
