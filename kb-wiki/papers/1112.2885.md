# The color dipole picture of low-x DIS

> Deep inelastic electron scattering (DIS) from nucleons at low values of the Bjorken variable $x \cong Q^2/W^2 \lsim ~ 0.1$ proceeds via fluctuations of the photon into quark-antiquark dipole states that subsequently interact with the gluon field in the nucleon. Dependent on the interaction energy, $W$, the color-gauge-invariant dipole interaction with the gluon field in the nucleon, for any fixed dipole size, contains the limits of i) color transparency and ii) saturation, where "saturation" stands for the approach to a hadronlike dipole-proton interaction cross section. All essential features of the experimental results on low-x DIS, as a consequence of the color-gauge-invariant dipole interaction follow model independently i.e. without specific ansatz for the dipole cross section. The model-independent results in particular include the low-x scaling behavior of the photoabsorption cross section, $σ_{γ^*p} (W^2,Q^2) = σ_{γ^*p} (η(W^2,Q^2))$, with definite functional dependence on the low-x scaling variable $η(W^2,Q^2) \cong Q^2/Λ^2_{sat} (W^2)$ in the limits of $η(W^2,Q^2) \gg 1$ and $η(W^2,Q^2) \ll 1$, respectively. Consistency with the pQCD-improved parton model implies the definite value of $C_2 \cong 0.29$ for the exponent in the "saturation scale", $Λ^2_{sat} (W^2) \approx (W^2)^{C_2}$. The longitudinal-to-transverse ratio of the photoabsorption cross section at large $Q^2$ has the definite value of $R = 1/2 ρ$ with $ρ= 4/3$. For $W^2 \to \infty$ at any fixed $Q^2$, the photoabsorption cross section converges towards a $Q^2$-independent saturation limit that coincides with the cross section for $Q^2 = 0$ photoproduction.

## Key claim

The color dipole picture (CDP) of low-x deep inelastic scattering (DIS) yields model-independent predictions for the photoabsorption cross section, including scaling behavior in the variable η(W²,Q²) and definite values for the saturation-scale exponent C₂ = 0.29 and the longitudinal-to-transverse ratio R = 1/(2ρ) with ρ = 4/3, but these results depend on the condition that the dipole interaction is treated as a color-gauge-invariant two-gluon exchange and that consistency with the pQCD-improved parton model is imposed.

## Method

The author derives the photoabsorption cross section from the color dipole picture, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark dipole that interacts with the gluon field in the nucleon via gauge-invariant two-gluon exchange. The dipole cross section is expressed in terms of its Fourier transform in transverse momentum space, and the limits of color transparency (small dipole size) and saturation (large dipole size or high energy) are extracted analytically. Consistency between the CDP and the pQCD-improved parton model is enforced by combining the evolution equation for F₂ with the CDP prediction that F₂ becomes a function of W² alone at large Q².

## Key numbers

- Saturation-scale exponent: C₂ = 0.29, derived from ρ = 4/3 and the ratio ξ₂/ξ_L = 1.25, with an uncertainty range 0.27 ≤ C₂ ≤ 0.31 for 1 ≤ ξ₂/ξ_L ≤ 1.5.
- Longitudinal-to-transverse ratio: R = 1/(2ρ) with ρ = 4/3, giving F_L(x,Q²) = 0.27 F₂(x,Q²) at large Q².
- Power-law fit to F₂(W²): F₂(W²) = 0.063 × (W²/1 GeV²)^{0.29} from an eyeball fit to experimental data.
- Saturation scale range: Λ²_sat(W²) varies between approximately 2 GeV² and 7 GeV² in the HERA energy range.
- Q² range of data description: 0.036 GeV² ≤ Q² ≤ 316 GeV² for the specific ansatz interpolating between the η ≫ 1 and η ≪ 1 limits.

## Context

This work extends the generalized vector dominance (GVD) approach of Sakurai and Schildknecht (1972) and the color dipole formulation of Nikolaev and Zakharov, providing a model-independent derivation of low-x scaling and saturation. The CDP prediction of scaling in η(W²,Q²) = Q²/Λ²_sat(W²) is confirmed by experimental data, and the approach to a Q²-independent saturation limit for W² → ∞ at fixed Q² is consistent with the empirical Caldwell fit. The results enable a simple one-parameter extraction of the gluon distribution function, which is compared with global fits from GRV, CTEQ, and MSTW.
