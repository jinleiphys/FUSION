# Three-$α$ configurations of the second $J^π=2^+$ state in $^{12}$C

> We investigate geometric configurations of $α$ ($^4$He nucleus) clusters in the second $J^π=2^+$ state of $^{12}$C, which has been discussed as a rotational band member of the second $0^+$ state, the Hoyle state. The ground and excited $0^+$ and $2^+$ states are described by a three-$α$ cluster model. The three-body Schrödinger equation with orthogonality conditions is accurately solved by the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions. To analyse the structure of these resonant states in a convenient form, we introduce a confining potential. The two-body density distributions together with the spectroscopic information clarify the structure of these states. We find that main configurations of both the second $0^+$ and $2^+$ states are acute-angled triangle shapes originating from the $^8$Be($0^+$)$+α$ configuration. However, the $^8$Be$+α$ components in the second $2^+$ state become approximately 2/3 because the 8Be subsystem is hard to excite, indicating that the state is not an ideal rigid rotational band member of the Hoyle state.

# Three-α configurations of the second Jπ=2+ state in 12C

## Key claim

The second 2+ state in 12C is not an ideal rigid rotational band member of the Hoyle state (the second 0+ state), because the 8Be(0+)+α component in the 2+2 state is only approximately 2/3 of the wave function, whereas the Hoyle state is dominated by this configuration; the 8Be subsystem is hard to excite, so the state is better described as a partially rotational excited state.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for 12C using a three-α cluster model with an orthogonality condition model to enforce Pauli forbidden states. The stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussian basis functions is employed to obtain accurate wave functions for the 0+1, 0+2, 2+1, and 2+2 states. A confining potential is introduced to treat the resonant 0+2 and 2+2 states as bound states, enabling calculation of two-body density distributions, partial-wave components, and 8Be spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- The 2+2 state lies at 2.32 MeV above the three-α threshold (calculated), compared to the experimental value of 2.59(6) MeV
- The 0+2 (Hoyle) state lies at 0.84 MeV above threshold (calculated)
- Root-mean-square radii of α particles: 3.44 fm for the 0+2 state and 3.50 fm for the 2+2 state, both significantly larger than the ground state (1.71 fm)
- Charge radii: 3.83 fm (0+2) and 3.88 fm (2+2), compared to 2.39 fm for the ground state
- The 8Be(0+)+α spectroscopic factor S(0,0) = 0.668 for the 0+2 state, while for the 2+2 state the dominant channel (0,2) has S(0,2) = 0.419
- The 8Be(2+)+α component in the 2+2 state is only S(2,0) = 0.021, strongly suppressed because the 8Be(2+) excitation energy is 3.26 MeV (calculated), larger than the ~1.4 MeV spacing between the 0+2 and 2+2 states
- The spectroscopic amplitude peak positions are at 4.97 fm (0+2) and 6.20 fm (2+2), with corresponding rms radii of 5.84 fm and 7.38 fm

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on whether the 2+2 state in 12C is a rotational excitation of the Hoyle state, as proposed in the algebraic cluster model (ACM) by Bijker (2002, 2020) and Fortunato (2019). The authors use a non-microscopic three-α cluster model with orthogonality conditions, similar to the approach of Uegaki (1977, 1978, 1979) and Kanada-En'yo (2007), but with improved wave functions from the stochastic variational method. The results support the mean-field-like picture of Yamada (2005) and Funaki (2005, 2015), where one α particle is excited to a D orbit rather than the state being a rigid rotor. The analysis enables future study of the recently observed 4+2 state as another candidate Hoyle band member.
