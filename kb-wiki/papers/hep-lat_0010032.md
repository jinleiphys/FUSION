# Obstructions to dimensional reduction in hot QCD

> I describe results on screening masses in hot gauge theories. Wilsonian effective long distance theories called dimensionally reduced (DR) theories describe very well the longest screening length in pure gauge theories. In the presence of fermions, meson-like screening lengths dominate the long-distance physics for 3T_c/2 < T < 3 T_c, and thus obstruct perturbative DR. Extrapolation of our results indicates that a form of this obstruction may remain till temperatures of 10 T_c or higher, and therefore affect the entire range of temperature expected to be reached even at the Large Hadron Collider.

# Obstructions to dimensional reduction in hot QCD

## Key claim

Perturbative dimensional reduction (DR) fails for QCD with dynamical fermions at temperatures up to at least 10 T_c, because meson-like screening masses from quark-antiquark operators dominate the long-distance physics and cannot be captured by a DR theory that integrates out all fermion modes.

## Method

The author performed lattice simulations of 4-flavour QCD at temperatures of 3T_c/2, 2T_c, and 3T_c, collecting 1000–2000 uncorrelated configurations on various lattice sizes. Correlators were constructed from quark-antiquark operators corresponding to the zero-temperature π, σ, vector, and pseudo-vector mesons, and screening masses were extracted and compared to gluonic screening masses from the same configurations and to predictions from perturbatively matched DR theories.

## Key numbers

- In SU(2) pure gauge theory at 2T_c, the lowest screening mass ratio μ(0_+)/T = 2.9 ± 0.2 in the 4-d theory versus 2.86 ± 0.03 in the DR theory.
- In 4-flavour QCD at T = 2T_c = (2.14 ± 0.10)Λ_MSbar, the gluonic screening mass ratio μ_g(0_+)/T = 4.04 ± 0.05 in the 4-d theory versus 4.87 ± 0.07 in the DR theory.
- The ratio T_c/Λ_MSbar for 4-flavour QCD at the quark masses used is 1.07 ± 0.05.

## Context

This work extends earlier tests of dimensional reduction in scalar φ⁴ theories by Braaten (1995) and in the SU(2) pure gauge theory by Saumen (1999) and Hart (2000), where DR worked well for the longest screening length. It contradicts the expectation that perturbative DR applies to QCD with fermions, as previously assumed in work by Laine (2000) on DR with dynamical fermions. The results enable quantitative bounds on the temperature range where perturbative DR fails, and motivate non-perturbative approaches to hot QCD at LHC-relevant temperatures.
