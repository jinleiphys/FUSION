# Effect of potential-energy-model inaccuracies on predictions of fission-fragment mass distributions based on the Brownian shape-motion method

> Moller and Randrup presented a comprehensive calculation, based on the Brownian shape motion (BSM) method, of fission-fragment charge distributions [Phys. Rev. C 91 (2015) 044316 ] which obtained that ``a new region of asymmetry'' appeared for approximately $95 \le N \le 115$ and $ 75 \le Z \le 94 $. Available experimental results at the time, except for the observation of symmetric fission of $^{187}$Ir by Itkis et al. [Yad. Fiz. 52 (1990) 944], agreed with these predictions apart for minor differences in the transition regions between predicted symmetric and asymmetric fission. It was argued [Phys. Rev. C 91 (2015) 044316 ] that the inaccurate results for $^{187}$Ir were related to inaccuracies in the calculated potential-energy surface and that such inaccuracies are related to the (in)accuracies of the calculated ground-state masses for the corresponding mass splits. We present here more detailed discussions and investigate if differences between the fission-fragment mass yields presented in [Phys. Rev. C 91 (2015) 044316 ] and experiment can occur in other regions of fissioning nuclei.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the Brownian shape-motion (BSM) method prediction of asymmetric fission for \(^{187}\)Ir and the experimental observation of symmetric fission is caused by a 5 MeV overestimate of the calculated potential-energy surface for symmetric splits, which arises from unusually large errors (about 2.5 MeV per fragment) in the calculated ground-state masses of the symmetric fission fragments; this is the largest such error that can occur for any scission configuration in heavy-element fission, and it extends only to nuclides a few nucleons beyond \(^{187}\)Ir.

## Method

The authors compared experimental ground-state masses with masses calculated using the FRLDM2002 mass model (the potential-energy model used to construct the 5D potential-energy surfaces for the BSM random walks) for fission fragments along the unchanged charge distribution (UCD) lines of several fissioning systems. For each mass split, they summed the mass-model error of the two fragments to estimate the inaccuracy of the calculated potential-energy surface at scission. They then mapped this error for symmetric scission configurations across the heavy-element region (\(Z \ge 70\), \(N \ge 90\)) where experimental masses are available.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric fission of \(^{187}\)Ir, the calculated fragment ground-state masses are too high by about **2.5 MeV** each, leading to a potential-energy surface that is too high by **5 MeV** for the symmetric scission configuration.
- For symmetric fission of \(^{240}\)Pu, the mass discrepancy at symmetry is **almost zero**.
- The largest deviations in potential-energy accuracy at symmetric scission occur in the vicinity of the symmetric split of \(^{187}\)Ir; in the actinide to superheavy region, errors are consistently much smaller.
- For \(^{180}\)Hg, \(^{210}\)Po, \(^{226}\)Th, \(^{240}\)Pu, and \(^{258}\)Fm, deviations along the UCD lines are up to **\(\pm 2\) MeV**, but BSM calculations for these nuclides agree quite well with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Möller and Randrup (2015), who first predicted a new region of asymmetric fission for approximately \(95 \le N \le 115\) and \(75 \le Z \le 94\) using the BSM method, and who noted the discrepancy for \(^{187}\)Ir observed by Itkis et al. (1990). The present paper systematically investigates whether similar inaccuracies occur elsewhere in the nuclear chart, concluding that the large error is localized near \(^{187}\)Ir and that calculated fragment mass distributions should agree with future experiments elsewhere. This enables experimentalists to map out how far the region of symmetric fission extends within the predicted region of asymmetry.
