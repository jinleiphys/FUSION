# Vector resonances spin alignement as a probe of spin hydrodynamics and freeze-out

> We argue that a detailed analysis of the spin alignement of vector mesons can serve as a probe of two little-understood aspects of spin dynamics in the vortical fluid: The degree of relaxation between vorticity and parton spin polarization, and the degree of coherence of the hadron wavefunction at freeze-out. We illustrate these with a coalescence model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that measuring the full angular distribution of vector meson decay products (including both polar and azimuthal angles) can probe two poorly understood aspects of spin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions: the degree of relaxation between fluid vorticity and parton spin polarization, and the degree of coherence of the hadron wavefunction at freeze-out. The authors illustrate this with a coalescence model, but emphasize that the results are qualitative and depend sensitively on the details of hydrodynamic evolution and the coalescence Wigner function.

## Method

The authors construct a theoretical framework linking the spin density matrix of vector mesons (spin-1 particles, or "qutrits") to experimental observables via a Fourier decomposition of the decay angular distribution. They use group theory (Wigner D-matrices and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients) to relate the density matrix parameters to the measurable coefficients in the angular distribution, and then implement a coalescence model where quark spin (determined early) and orbital angular momentum from vorticity (determined at freeze-out) combine to produce the vector meson spin state, allowing for misalignment between these two sources of angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The density matrix of a spin-1 particle is parametrized as ρ = (1/3)(1 + √3 n₈·λ), where n₈ is an 8-dimensional vector and λ represents the Gell-Mann matrices.
- Pure states for the aligned density matrix occur at the three points (n₃, n₈) = (0, -1), (1/2)(±√3, 1).
- The maximum entropy (Cooper-Frye) state has the form ρ = [F/(F²+F+1)] Diag[F, 1, 1/F] with F ~ exp(Ω/T).
- The relation between the experimental coefficient ρ₀₀ and the density matrix parameters is: ρ₀₀ = (1/12)[3(n₈ - √3 n₃)cos(2θᵣ) - √3 n₃ + n₈ + 4].
- The azimuthal angle φᵣ is given by φᵣ = -(1/2)tan⁻¹(α₁,₋₁ / r₁,₋₁).
- The quark spin density matrix at early times is ρ^(1,2)_kl = (1/Z) exp[Ω̂·σ̂/(2T)], with Ω/T = 1/5 used in the illustrative calculation.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard measurement of Λ hyperon polarization (Lisa, 2016) and the simpler ρ₀₀ alignment parameter measured by ALICE and STAR collaborations. It builds on theoretical developments in spin hydrodynamics (uscausality, uscas2, relax, jeon, noronha, analspin1, analspin2) that suggest spin and vorticity are not always in equilibrium, and on coalescence models (coal1, coal2, coal3). The paper enables experimental tests of whether freeze-out follows a Cooper-Frye equilibrium picture (where φᵣ should have a uniform distribution) or involves more complex dynamics such as coalescence (where φᵣ should show peaked structure), and proposes a purity test of the density matrix to distinguish these scenarios.
