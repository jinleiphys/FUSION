# Signatures of the Tricritical Point in QCD

> Several approaches to QCD with two massless quarks at finite temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu suggest the existence of a tricritical point on the boundary of the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. In QCD with massive quarks there is then a critical point at the end of a first order transition line. We discuss possible experimental signatures of this point, which provide information about its location and properties. We propose a combination of event-by-event observables, including suppressed fluctuations in T and mu and, simultaneously, enhanced fluctuations in the multiplicity of soft pions.

## Key claim

In QCD with two massive quarks, the tricritical point present for massless quarks becomes a critical end-point (E) of a first-order phase transition line; the authors propose that this point may be accessible in heavy-ion collisions by varying control parameters such as collision energy and centrality, and that its existence can be detected through a combination of event-by-event observables.

## Method

The authors use a Landau-Ginzburg effective potential with a phi^6 term to describe the chiral symmetry breaking order parameter, where the tricritical point occurs when the coefficient b changes sign. They map the critical end-point E onto the universality class of the 3-dimensional Ising model, and propose that experimental trajectories in the T-mu plane can be controlled by varying collision energy, centrality, or rapidity. They then derive signatures from thermodynamic fluctuations and from the behavior of the nearly-massless sigma field near the critical point.

## Key numbers

- Estimated position of tricritical point P in two-flavor QCD: T_P ~ 100 MeV, mu_P ~ 600-700 MeV (from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and random matrix models)
- Chemical freeze-out at AGS (11 GeV·A): mu ~ 500-600 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out at SPS (160-200 GeV·A): mu less than or equal to 200 MeV
- For central S+S collisions at SPS: T_F ≈ 140-150 MeV
- For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS: T_F ≈ 120 MeV
- Critical exponent for h-like susceptibility divergence: gamma/beta*delta ≈ 0.8, so (Delta T)^2 ~ (Delta mu)^2 ~ ell^0.8
- 3D Ising model exponents: alpha ≈ 0.12, gamma ≈ 1.2

## Context

This paper extends earlier work on the QCD phase diagram by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) and Rajagopal (1995), and builds on model calculations by Berges and Rajagopal (1998) and Halasz, Jackson, Shrock, Stephanov, and Verbaarschot (1998) using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and random matrix models. The authors connect theoretical predictions for a critical end-point to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions, proposing that the critical point may be accessible between SPS and AGS energies. The work enables experimental searches for the QCD critical point using event-by-event fluctuation measurements, analogous to critical opalescence in liquid-gas phase transitions, and draws parallels to multifragmentation experiments in nuclear physics by Csernai and Kapusta (1986).
