# The chiral representation of the $πN$ scattering amplitude and the pion-nucleon sigma term

> We present a novel analysis of the $πN$ scattering amplitude in Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell scheme. This amplitude, valid up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ in the chiral expansion, systematically includes the effects of the $Δ(1232)$ in the $δ$-counting, has the right analytic properties and is renormalization-scale independent. This approach overcomes the limitations that previous chiral analyses of the $πN$ scattering amplitude had, providing an accurate description of the partial wave phase shifts of the Karlsruhe-Helsinki and George-Washington groups up to energies just below the resonance region. We also study the solution of the Matsinos group which focuses on the parameterization of the data at low energies. Once the values of the low-energy constants are determined by adjusting the center-of-mass energy dependence of the amplitude to the scattering data, we obtain predictions on different observables. In particular, we extract an accurate value for the pion-nucleon sigma term, $ σ_{πN}$. This allows us to avoid the usual method of extrapolation to the unphysical region of the amplitude. Our study indicates that the inclusion of modern meson-factory and pionic-atom data favors relatively large values of the sigma term. We report the value $σ_{πN}=59(7)$MeV and comment on implications that this result may have.

## Key claim
The paper claims that modern pion-nucleon scattering data, analyzed using Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell scheme and explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance, favor a relatively large value of the pion-nucleon sigma term, σπN = 59(7) MeV. This result is conditional on using the George-Washington or Matsinos partial-wave analyses rather than the older Karlsruhe-Helsinki analysis, which yields a lower value of 43(5) MeV.

## Method
The authors constructed a πN scattering amplitude up to O(p³) in Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme. They included the Δ(1232) resonance explicitly using the δ-counting and the consistent formulation of Pascalutsa for spin-3/2 fields. The low-energy constants were determined by fitting the center-of-mass energy dependence of the S- and P-wave phase shifts from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH), George-Washington (GW), and Matsinos (EM) partial-wave analyses up to Wmax = 1.2 GeV (1.16 GeV for EM). The sigma term was then extracted from the fitted value of the low-energy constant c₁ using the chiral expression at O(p³).

## Key numbers
- σπN = 59(7) MeV, the final value reported from averaging GW and EM results with higher-order uncertainty added in quadrature.
- σπN = 59(4) MeV from the GW analysis alone, with c₁ = −1.00(4) GeV⁻¹.
- σπN = 59(2) MeV from the EM analysis alone, with c₁ = −1.00(1) GeV⁻¹.
- σπN = 43(5) MeV from the KH analysis alone, with c₁ = −0.80(6) GeV⁻¹.
- The O(p⁷/²) Δ-loop correction to σπN is −6 MeV, taken as an irreducible theoretical uncertainty.
- The O(p⁴) loop corrections with O(p²) LECs contribute −2 to −4 MeV.
- The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy ΔGT = 2.1(8)% for GW, 1.9(4)% for EM, and 4.9(8)% for KH.
- The NΔ axial coupling hA = 2.87(4) from GW, compatible with the Δ-width value hA = 2.90(2).
- The isoscalar scattering length a₀₊⁺ = −0.4(7) × 10⁻² mπ⁻¹ (GW) and +0.2(3) × 10⁻² mπ⁻¹ (EM), compared to the pionic-atom value 0.0076(31) mπ⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends previous chiral analyses of πN scattering that suffered from convergence problems, such as heavy-baryon χPT (Fettes et al., 1998, 2000) and infrared baryon χPT (Becher et al., 2001), which failed to connect the subthreshold region to the physical one or produced large Goldberger-Treiman discrepancies. The EOMS scheme overcomes the analyticity issues of the infrared scheme. The results support the larger sigma term from the GW group (Pavan et al., 2001) over the canonical value from the KH analysis (Gasser et al., 1990). The method enables extraction of σπN directly from scattering data without extrapolation to the unphysical Cheng-Dashen point, and the results are relevant for dark-matter searches and studies of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter.
