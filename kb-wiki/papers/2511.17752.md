# Tensor network simulations of quasi-GPDs in the massive Schwinger model

> Generalized Parton Distribution functions (GPDs) are off-diagonal light-cone matrix elements that encode the internal structure of hadrons in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom. In this work, we present the first nonperturbative study of quasi-GPDs in the massive Schwinger model, quantum electrodynamics in 1+1 dimensions (QED2), within the Hamiltonian formulation of lattice field theory. Quasi-distributions are spatial correlation functions of boosted states, which approach the relevant light-cone distributions in the luminal limit. Using tensor networks, we prepare the first excited state in the strongly coupled regime and boost it to close to the light-cone on lattices of up to 400 lattice sites. We compute both quasi-parton distribution functions and, for the first time, quasi-GPDs, and study their convergence for increasingly boosted states. In addition, we perform analytic calculations of GPDs in the two-particle Fock-space approximation and in the Reggeized limit, providing qualitative benchmarks for the tensor network results. Our analysis establishes computational benchmarks for accessing partonic observables in low-dimensional gauge theories, offering a starting point for future extensions to higher dimensions, non-Abelian theories, and quantum simulations.

## Key claim

The authors present the first nonperturbative study of quasi-Generalized Parton Distributions (qGPDs) in the massive Schwinger model (QED2), using tensor networks to prepare the first excited state and boost it close to the light-cone on lattices of up to 400 sites, but the results are limited to 1+1 dimensions and the strongly coupled regime, with convergence to light-cone GPDs only approaching in the luminal limit.

## Method

The authors discretize the massive Schwinger model Hamiltonian on a spatial lattice with open boundary conditions, eliminate the U(1) gauge field via Gauss's law, and map fermion fields to spin degrees of freedom through a Jordan-Wigner transformation. They use Matrix Product States (MPS) with the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) algorithm to prepare the first excited state (the eta meson) in the strongly coupled regime, then apply a boost operator using the Time-Dependent Variational Principle (TDVP) to reach finite momentum states on lattices of up to 400 staggered sites. They compute quasi-PDFs and quasi-GPDs as spatial correlation functions of these boosted states and study their convergence as the boost increases toward the light-cone limit, complementing these with analytic calculations in the two-particle Fock-space approximation and the Reggeized limit.

## Key numbers

- Lattices of up to 400 staggered lattice sites (corresponding to 200 physical sites) are used for tensor network simulations.
- The eta meson mass in the strong coupling regime is given by \(m_\eta^2 = m_S^2 + m_\pi^2 = \frac{g^2}{\pi} - \frac{m\langle \overline{\psi}\psi\rangle_0}{f^2}\) with \(f = 1/\sqrt{4\pi}\).
- The vacuum chiral condensate in the chiral limit is \(\langle\overline\psi \psi\rangle_0 = -\frac{e^{\gamma_E}}{2\pi}m_S\) where \(\gamma_E = 0.577\) is the Euler constant.
- The eta mass ratio in strong coupling is \(m_\eta/m_S = (1 + 2e^{\gamma_E} m/m_S)^{1/2} \approx 1 + e^{\gamma_E} m/m_S\) with \(e^{\gamma_E} \approx 1.78\).
- The end-point exponent \(\beta\) in the light-front wavefunction ansatz is fixed by \(m^2_S - m^2 = \pi \beta \cot(\pi \beta)\).
- The GPD in the ERBL region uses the distribution amplitude \(\varphi_0(z) = z^\beta(1-z)^\beta / \mathrm{B}(\beta+1,\beta+1)\) with the lowest pole residue \(\mathcal R_0(\xi) = M_0^2 \sqrt{1-\xi^2}\).
- The Reggeized ERBL contribution uses a linear squared-mass spectrum \(M_n^2 \simeq M_*^2 + \lambda (n+\delta)\) for large \(n\), with slope \(\lambda > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of partonic structure in QED2 using Hamiltonian formulations and exact diagonalization, specifically their computation of quasi-PDFs for the eta meson (Grieninger 2024) and quasi-fragmentation functions (Grieninger 2024). It provides the first nonperturbative benchmark for quasi-GPDs in a gauge theory, complementing ongoing lattice QCD simulations (Lin 2020, Bhattacharya 2022) and phenomenological modeling (Ji 1997, Radyushkin 1998), and establishes a starting point for future extensions to higher dimensions, non-Abelian theories, and quantum simulations.
