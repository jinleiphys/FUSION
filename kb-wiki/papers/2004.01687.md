# Parton Distribution Functions from Ioffe Time Pseudodistributions from Lattice Calculations: Approaching the Physical Point

> We present results for the unpolarized parton distribution function of the nucleon computed in lattice QCD at the physical pion mass. This is the first study of its kind employing the method of Ioffe time pseudo-distributions. Beyond the reconstruction of the Bjorken-$x$ dependence we also extract the lowest moments of the distribution function using the small Ioffe time expansion of the Ioffe time pseudo-distribution. We compare our findings with the pertinent phenomenological determinations.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the unpolarized nucleon parton distribution function (PDF) using the Ioffe time pseudo-distribution method at the physical pion mass, but the results for the valence PDF at momentum fractions x greater than about 0.2 are larger than phenomenological determinations, indicating remaining systematic uncertainties from higher twist effects, discretization errors, and finite volume corrections.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements of a non-local quark bilinear with a straight Wilson line on three ensembles of 2+1 flavor clover Wilson fermions at pion masses of 172 MeV, 278 MeV, and 358 MeV. They form the reduced pseudo-ITD by taking the ratio of the matrix element at finite Ioffe time to that at zero Ioffe time, which removes Wilson line-related UV divergences, then match to the light-cone ITD in the MSbar scheme at next-to-leading order using a perturbative coefficient function. The valence PDF is extracted by fitting the matched ITD to a parameterized functional form q_v(x) = (1/N) x^a (1-x)^b (1 + c sqrt(x) + d x), and the results from the three ensembles are extrapolated to the physical pion mass using a polynomial in the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses of the three ensembles: 358(3) MeV, 278(3) MeV, and 172(6) MeV
- Lattice spacings: 0.094(1) fm for the two heavier ensembles, 0.091(1) fm for the lightest ensemble
- The matching procedure has a small effect of order O(alpha_s/pi) approximately 0.1 on the distribution
- The matched ITD and PDF are presented at a reference scale of mu = 2 GeV
- The extrapolated valence PDF is compared to phenomenological determinations from CJ15nlo (NLO), MSTW2008nnlo68cl_nf4 (NNLO), and NNPDF31_nnlo_pch_as_0118_mc_164 (NNLO) at 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Ioffe time pseudo-distribution method previously developed by the same group (Radyushkin 2017, Orginos 2017, Karpie 2017-2019, Joo 2019) to the physical pion mass, enabling a more meaningful comparison with phenomenological PDF determinations from global fits. The results are compared with the quasi-PDF approach of the Extended Twisted Mass collaboration (Alexandrou 2018) and their subsequent re-analysis using the pseudo-PDF method (Bhat 2020), finding better agreement with the latter. The study enables future systematic investigations of continuum extrapolation, finite volume effects, and higher twist contributions to lattice PDF calculations.
