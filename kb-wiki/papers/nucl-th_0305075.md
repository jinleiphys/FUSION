# Description of the particle ratios and transverse-momentum spectra for various centralities at RHIC in a single-freeze-out model

> The single-freeze-out model of hadron production is used to describe the particle ratios and the transverse-momentum spectra from RHIC. The emphasis is put on the new measurements done at the highest beam energy of 200 GeV. An overall very good agreement is found between the data and the model predictions. The data for different centrality windows are analyzed separately. A simple scaling of the two expansion parameters of the model with the centrality is found. Interestingly, this scaling turns out to be equivalent to the scaling of hadron production with the number of wounded nucleons.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out model, which assumes chemical and kinetic freeze-out occur at the same temperature, can simultaneously describe both particle ratios and transverse-momentum spectra at RHIC's top energy of 200 GeV across multiple centrality bins, with the caveat that the model's geometric parameters scale linearly with centrality in a way equivalent to wounded-nucleon scaling only for not-too-large values of centrality (c < 0.5).

## Method

The authors applied the single-freeze-out model of hadron production, which assumes a universal freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB, combined with a Hubble-like hydrodynamic expansion on a constant proper-time hypersurface (τ = constant) with a finite transverse size ρmax. They first fitted T and μB to particle ratios from the most central data using a χ² method, then independently fitted the geometric parameters τ and ρmax to transverse-momentum spectra for each centrality bin using the Cooper-Frye formula with full resonance decay cascades from the Particle Data Tables.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 165.6 ± 4.5 MeV and baryon chemical potential μB = 28.5 ± 3.7 MeV from fit to particle ratios at √sNN = 200 GeV, with χ²/n = 0.2
- For most central events (0-5% centrality), geometric parameters: τ = 7.58 fm and ρmax = 7.27 fm (combined fit to PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS, BRAHMS data)
- For most central events (0-5% centrality), average transverse flow velocity ⟨β⊥⟩ = 0.52 ± 0.02 and maximum transverse flow velocity β⊥max = 0.69 ± 0.02
- For the most peripheral PHENIX bin (80-91% centrality): τ = 2.76 ± 0.20 fm, ρmax = 1.43 ± 0.03 fm, ⟨β⊥⟩ = 0.32 ± 0.03, β⊥max = 0.46 ± 0.03
- Baryon chemical potential dropped from 41 MeV at √sNN = 130 GeV to 29 MeV at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Model ratios: π⁻/π⁺ = 1.009 ± 0.003, K⁻/K⁺ = 0.939 ± 0.008, p̄/p = 0.74 ± 0.04, p̄/π⁻ = 0.104 ± 0.010, K⁻/π⁻ = 0.174 ± 0.001

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out model previously applied to RHIC data at √sNN = 130 GeV (Broniowski, Florkowski 2001, 2002) to the full beam energy of 200 GeV and, crucially, to multiple centrality bins for the first time. The finding that ρmax scales approximately as (1-c) and τ/ρmax is approximately constant for c < 0.5 demonstrates equivalence to wounded-nucleon scaling (Bialas, Bleszynski, Czyz 1976), providing a geometric interpretation of centrality dependence. The results are compatible with the chemical freeze-out parameters of Braun-Munzinger, Redlich, and Stachel (2003) who obtained Tchem = 177 ± 7 MeV and μB = 29 ± 6 MeV. The approach enables systematic extraction of freeze-out geometry from transverse-momentum spectra across centrality, and the weak centrality dependence of thermodynamic parameters supports the interpretation of rapid thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.
