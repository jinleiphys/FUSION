# Comment on "Proton Spin Structure from Measurable Parton Distributions" by Ji, Xiong and Yuan (PRL109, 152005 (2012))

## Key claim

The claim by Ji, Xiong, and Yuan that the expression 1/2 ∫ x dx [H_q(x,0,0) + E_q(x,0,0)] measures the transverse angular momentum of quarks in a transversely polarized nucleon is incorrect. The operator J^{+i} that they interpret as transverse angular momentum is actually the light-front transverse boost operator, not the angular momentum operator.

## Method

The authors analyze the light-front quantization framework to identify the physical meaning of the operator J^{+i} used by Ji, Xiong, and Yuan. They compare the light-front transverse boost operators J^{+i} with the light-front transverse angular momentum operators J^{-i}, showing that J^{+i} = (1/√2)(K^i + J^i) (a kinematic boost operator) while J^{-i} = (1/√2)(K^i - J^i) (a dynamical rotation operator). They further demonstrate that quark and gluon spin operators in the A^+=0 gauge contribute to J^{-i} and not to J^{+i}.

## Key numbers

- The light-front transverse boost operators are given by J^{+1} = (1/√2)(K^1 + J^2) and J^{+2} = (1/√2)(K^2 - J^1), where K^i are boost operators and J^i are rotation operators.
- The light-front transverse angular momentum operators are given by J^{-1} = (1/√2)(K^1 - J^2) and J^{-2} = (1/√2)(K^2 + J^1).

## Context

This comment corrects the interpretation in Ji, Xiong, and Yuan (2012), who claimed a partonic interpretation of the Ji relation (Ji, 1997) as transverse angular momentum. The work extends the analysis of Leader (2011), who derived a relation for the instant-form transverse component of quark angular momentum in terms of GPDs H and E and verified that the Ji relation correctly describes longitudinal angular momentum. The result implies that no simple partonic interpretation exists for transverse angular momentum in light-front quantization, unlike the longitudinal case which can be expressed in terms of Wigner distributions (Lorce, 2011; Hatta, 2011; Ji, 2012).
