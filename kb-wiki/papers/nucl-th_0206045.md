# Isospin-rich nuclei in neutron star matter

> Stability of nuclei beyond the drip lines in the presence of an enveloping gas of nucleons and electrons, as prevailing in the inner crust of a neutron star, is studied in the temperature-dependent Thomas-Fermi framework. A limiting asymmetry in the isospin space beyond which nuclei cannot exist emerges from the calculations. The ambient conditions like temperature, baryon density and neutrino concentration under which these exotic nuclear systems can be formed are studied in some detail.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclei in the inner crust of a neutron star can exist far beyond the terrestrial neutron drip line due to the stabilizing pressure of the surrounding nucleonic gas, but there is a limiting neutron-proton asymmetry beyond which even these stabilized nuclei become thermodynamically unstable and cannot exist.

## Method

The authors employed a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi framework with the SKM* Skyrme energy density functional, using the Wigner-Seitz approximation to model the periodic lattice of nuclei in the inner crust. They applied the Bonche-Levit-Vautherin (BLV) subtraction procedure to isolate the nuclear cluster from the surrounding nucleonic gas, solving the density profiles self-consistently under conditions of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium with electrons and neutrinos. Calculations were performed for both zero and finite temperatures (up to 6 MeV) for various lead, calcium, and tin isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip line for lead isotopes at T=0 is located at A_cl=276 (mass number 276Pb)
- The proton drip line for isolated Pb nuclei is at A_cl=182; inclusion of lattice electrons extends it to A_cl=143
- The limiting asymmetry line and drip asymmetry line are found to be not very sensitive to the atomic number Z_cl of the nucleus
- For the representative system 80Ca, the Thomas-Fermi drip line is at 68Ca (80Ca is well beyond this line)
- The cell radius R_c used in calculations ranges from 12 fm to 30 fm at steps of 3 fm
- At a fixed proton fraction Y_p=0.3 and density 0.001 fm^-3, the global minimum in free energy density occurs at a specific lattice radius R_c
- The boiling temperature T_B, at which the nuclear cluster completely evaporates into nucleonic gas, decreases with decreasing average baryon density

## Context

This work extends earlier zero-temperature calculations by some of the same authors (Jde, 2002) on isolated nuclei beyond the drip line, placing them in the realistic astrophysical context of the inner neutron star crust with electrons, neutrinos, and lattice effects included. It builds upon the BLV subtraction procedure developed by Bonche, Levit, and Vautherin (1984) for treating hot nuclei in equilibrium with vapor, and applies it to the neutron star crust environment. The results enable future studies of the thermal evolution of nuclear clusters during neutron star formation and cooling, and provide a framework for understanding the nucleation process of different nuclear species from the nucleonic sea.
