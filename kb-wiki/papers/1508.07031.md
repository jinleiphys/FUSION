# Production of Charge in Heavy Ion Collisions

> By analyzing preliminary experimental measurements of charge-balance functions from the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic-Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC), it is found that pictures where balancing charges are produced in a single surge, and therefore separated by a single length scale, are inconsistent with data. In contrast, a model that assumes two surges, one associated with the formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma and a second associated with hadronization, provides a far superior reproduction of the data. A statistical analysis of the model comparison finds that the two-surge model best reproduces the data if the charge production from the first surge is similar to expectations for equilibrated matter taken from lattice gauge theory. The charges created in the first surge appear to separate by approximately one unit of spatial rapidity before emission, while charges from the second wave appear to have separated by approximately a half unit or less.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a model with two distinct surges of charge production, one from the formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma and a second from hadronization, provides a far superior reproduction of preliminary STAR charge-balance function data than any single-surge model, but the authors acknowledge that their blast-wave model is a simplified parametric description with several weaknesses, including the assumption of simultaneous disassociation and the neglect of a continuum of charge creation.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-surge blast-wave model where charge correlations in spatial rapidity are described by two Gaussian widths, σ_A for the first surge (associated with the QGP) and σ_B for the second surge (associated with hadronization). They translated these coordinate-space correlations into momentum-space balance functions for four species pairs (π⁺π⁻, K⁺K⁻, p p̅, K⁻p) using a Monte Carlo procedure that includes thermal generation of momenta, transverse collective flow, resonance decays, and detector acceptance/efficiency corrections. The model parameters were systematically varied in a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) exploration using model emulator techniques to compare against preliminary STAR data for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The first-surge width σ_A ≈ 1.0 unit of spatial rapidity; the second-surge width σ_B ≈ 0.4 units of spatial rapidity (default parameters that best reproduce data).
- The charge susceptibility to entropy ratio from the first surge was set to (χ_uu + χ_dd + χ_ss)/s = 0.18, consistent with lattice calculations at T ≈ 300 MeV.
- The strangeness to up-quark ratio from the first surge was χ_ss/χ_uu = 0.93, consistent with lattice calculations at T ≈ 300 MeV.
- The chemical freezeout temperature was T_chem = 165 MeV; the kinetic freezeout temperature was T_kin = 102 MeV; the transverse collective velocity was u_⟂ = 0.732.
- The baryon suppression factor was F_B = 2/3 to account for baryon annihilation below T_chem.
- For a one-surge model with σ_A = 1.0, the K⁺K⁻ balance function was well produced but the π⁺π⁻ balance function was wider than data and the K⁻p balance function could not be explained.

## Context

This work extends the charge-balance function analysis framework developed by Pratt (2012) and applies it to preliminary STAR Collaboration measurements from RHIC (Adamczyk 2015, Wang 2011, STAR 2011). The two-surge model directly contradicts single-surge pictures where balancing charges are separated by a single length scale. The analysis uses lattice QCD calculations of charge susceptibilities from Borsanyi (2011) and Ratti (2011) as input for the first surge. The results enable future three-dimensional analyses of charge correlations and motivate more realistic models of charge creation, transport, and emission that are currently being pursued by the authors.
