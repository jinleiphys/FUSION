# Thermal Photons in Strong Interactions

> A brief survey is given on the current status of evaluating thermal production of photons from a strongly interacting medium. Emphasis is put on recent progress in assessing equilibrium emission rates in both hadronic and quark-gluon matter. We also give an update on the status of comparing theoretical calculations with experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at the SPS, as well as prospects for RHIC. Finally, applications of photon rate calculations to colorsuperconducting quark matter are discussed.

## Key claim

Thermal photon emission rates from both hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases are now sufficiently well understood to enable quantitative comparisons with heavy-ion collision data, but the identification of thermal QGP radiation remains ambiguous due to competing nonthermal sources and uncertainties in the space-time evolution of the fireball.

## Method

The paper reviews thermal photon emission rates calculated using finite-temperature field theory (via the electromagnetic current-current correlation function) and kinetic theory (via scattering amplitudes for processes such as 2→2 scattering, Bremsstrahlung, and pair annihilation). For the quark-gluon plasma, complete leading-order perturbative QCD rates including Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference effects are presented; for hadronic matter, effective Lagrangian approaches within Hidden Local Symmetry and Massive Yang-Mills frameworks are used to compute rates from π, ρ, a1, K, K*, and baryonic channels. These rates are then convoluted over hydrodynamic or fireball space-time evolutions and compared to direct photon spectra from SPS and RHIC heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- For the QGP photon rate at large x = q0/T with two massless quark flavors: q0 dRγ/d3q = (5/9)(ααS/2π2) T2 e−x ln(1 + 2.912x/(4παs)), with αs = 0.2
- Bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation augment the 2→2 scattering rate by about a factor of 2 for x ≳ 4
- For finite net quark density, the factor T2 in the perturbative rate is approximately replaced by (T2 + μq2/π2); at (μq, T) ≃ (100, 200) MeV this is a correction of less than 5%
- Hadronic formfactors reduce the πρ→πγ rate by a factor of 3-4 in the 2-3 GeV range
- Strangeness-bearing meson (K and K*) reactions contribute at the 40-50% level of the πρa1 gas
- For SPS WA98 data, hydrodynamic calculations require T0max ≃ 330 MeV (average T0 ≃ 260 MeV) for thermal-only fits; fireball calculations with nuclear kt-broadening are compatible with average T0 ≃ 205 MeV (τ0 ≃ 1 fm/c)
- For RHIC, thermal QGP radiation dominates for qt ≳ 1 GeV; thermal and pQCD contributions compete up to qt ≃ 5 GeV
- For LHC, thermal photons may exceed pQCD production up to almost qt = 10 GeV
- In color-flavor-locked quark matter, Goldstone boson masses mπ ≈ 10 MeV; at T ∼ 70 MeV, CSC photon rates are quite similar to extrapolations of hadronic many-body calculations

## Context

This review extends earlier work on thermal photon production by Rapp and Wambach (2000), Alam et al. (2001), and Peitzmann and Thoma (2002), and provides an update on comparisons to WA98 data at the SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC. The complete leading-order QGP rates from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) are highlighted as a key recent achievement. The paper enables more quantitative searches for thermal QGP radiation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and opens the application of photon rate calculations to color-superconducting quark matter in compact star astrophysics.
