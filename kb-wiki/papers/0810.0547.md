# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in A<=7 nuclei including meson-exchange currents

> Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions including two-body meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions are reported for A<=7 nuclei. The realistic Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-2 three-nucleon potentials are used to generate the nuclear wave functions. The two-body meson-exchange operators are constructed to satisfy the continuity equation with the Argonne v18 potential. The MEC contributions increase the A=3,7 isovector magnetic moments by 16% and the A=6,7 M1 transition rates by 17--34%, bringing them into very good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in nuclei with A ≤ 7, including two-body meson-exchange current contributions, bring theoretical predictions into very good agreement with experimental data, with the caveat that the MEC operators are constructed to satisfy the continuity equation specifically with the Argonne v18 potential, and the three-body currents from the Illinois-2 potential are neglected.

## Method

The authors performed a two-step Quantum Monte Carlo calculation: first, a Variational Monte Carlo calculation to find a good trial function, followed by a Green's Function Monte Carlo calculation to refine the solution, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-2 three-nucleon potentials to generate nuclear wave functions. Two-body meson-exchange current operators were constructed using both a meson-exchange scheme for the static parts of the potential and a minimal-substitution scheme for the momentum-dependent parts, with additional model-dependent currents from ρπγ and ωπγ transition currents and Δ-isobar excitation treated via the transition-correlation-operator method.

## Key numbers

- MEC contributions increase the A=3,7 isovector magnetic moments by 16%
- MEC contributions increase the A=6,7 M1 transition rates by 17–34%
- Wave function renormalizations N^Δ from Δ-isobar admixtures range from 1.0023 for ²H to 1.073(1) for ⁷Be
- The πNN coupling constant f²πNN/4π = 0.075, ρNN coupling constant g²ρNN/4π = 0.55, and ωNN coupling constant g²ωNN/4π = 16.96
- The ρπγ coupling constant gρπγ = 0.56 and ωπγ coupling constant gωπγ = 0.63
- Monopole form factor cutoffs: Λπ = 0.75 GeV, Λρ = 1.25 GeV, Λω = 1.25 GeV
- The NΔ-transition magnetic moment μγNΔ = 3 μN, and the Δ magnetic moment μγΔΔ = 4.35 μN

## Context

This work extends the earlier Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electroweak transitions in A=6,7 nuclei reported by Pervin, Pieper, and Wiringa (2007), which used only one-body transition operators and noted that two-body MEC operators were known to increase isovector magnetic moments by 15–20% for A=3 nuclei from Carlson and Schiavilla (1998). The present calculations bring theoretical predictions into very good agreement with experimental data for A ≤ 7 nuclei, enabling future studies of heavier nuclei and more complex electroweak observables within the same theoretical framework.
