# Improved description of $^{34,36,46}$Ar(p,d) transfer reactions

> An improved description of single neutron stripping from $^{34,36,46}$Ar beams at 33 MeV/nucleon by a hydrogen target is presented and the dependence on the neutron-proton asymmetry of the spectroscopic factors is further investigated. A finite range adiabatic model is used in the analysis and compared to previous zero range and local energy approximations. Full three-body Faddeev calculations are performed to estimate the error in the reaction theory. In addition, errors from the optical potentials are also evaluated. From our new spectroscopic factors extracted from transfer, it is possible to corroborate the neutron-proton asymmetry dependence reported from knockout measurements.

# Improved description of $^{34,36,46}$Ar(p,d) transfer reactions

## Key claim

Using a finite-range adiabatic model with full three-body Faddeev calculations to estimate reaction-theory errors, the authors find that the slope of the reduction factor $R_s = S_{exp}/S_{th}$ as a function of neutron-proton asymmetry $\Delta S$ suggested by knockout data can be corroborated by transfer data, contrary to the earlier zero-range analysis which showed no such dependence. The claim is conditional on the inclusion of finite-range effects and the quantification of theoretical uncertainties, which for $^{36}$Ar reach approximately 20%.

## Method

The authors reanalyze single-neutron stripping reactions $^{34,36,46}$Ar(p,d)$^{33,35,45}$Ar at 33 MeV/nucleon using the finite-range adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) of Johnson and Tandy, where the deuteron distorting potential is computed from the matrix element of $V_{np}(U_{nA}+U_{pA})$ over the deuteron ground state. They compare these finite-range results to zero-range ADWA and the local energy approximation (LEA), and perform exact three-body Faddeev/AGS calculations in momentum space to estimate the error from the approximate treatment of the three-body dynamics. Optical potential uncertainties are evaluated by comparing the Chapel Hill and Koning-Delaroche global nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

* Spectroscopic factors extracted with finite-range ADWA: 0.92 for $^{34}$Ar, 2.21 for $^{36}$Ar, and 4.93 for $^{46}$Ar.
* Zero-range ADWA overestimates the cross section relative to finite-range by 5% for $^{34}$Ar, 9% for $^{36}$Ar, and 5% for $^{46}$Ar.
* LEA underestimates the cross section by 14% for $^{34}$Ar and 9% for $^{36}$Ar, but overestimates by 3% for $^{46}$Ar.
* Optical potential uncertainty: 8% for $^{34}$Ar, 7% for $^{36}$Ar, and 4% for $^{46}$Ar.
* Faddeev (three-body dynamics) uncertainty: 6% for $^{34}$Ar, 19% for $^{36}$Ar, and 11% for $^{46}$Ar.
* Total theoretical plus experimental uncertainty (summed in quadrature): 13% for $^{34}$Ar, 22% for $^{36}$Ar, and 14% for $^{46}$Ar.
* Large-scale shell model spectroscopic factors: 1.31 for $^{34}$Ar, 2.10 for $^{36}$Ar, and 5.16 for $^{46}$Ar.
* Separation energies of the single-hole states: 17.07 MeV for $^{33}$Ar ($2s_{1/2}$), 15.25 MeV for $^{35}$Ar ($1d_{3/2}$), and 8.07 MeV for $^{45}$Ar ($1f_{7/2}$).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ar(p,d) data originally published by Lee et al. (2007) and the experimental collaboration (ar-exp), which used zero-range ADWA with LEA corrections and found no dependence of the reduction factor on neutron-proton asymmetry, in disagreement with knockout measurements by Gade et al. (2008). By including finite-range effects and quantifying uncertainties via Faddeev calculations, the authors reconcile the transfer and knockout trends. The finite-range ADWA method follows the systematic study by Nguyen et al. (2010), and the Faddeev/AGS framework follows Deltuva et al. (2007, 2009). The results enable a more reliable comparison between transfer and knockout spectroscopic factors, and call for similar error quantification in knockout reaction theory.
