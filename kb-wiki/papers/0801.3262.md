# Kinematics and Quantum Field Theory of the Neutrino Oscillations Observed in the Time-modulated Orbital Electron Capture Decay in an Ion Storage Ring

> According to the recent experimental data of a GSI--experiment, the rate of the number of daughter ions $^{140}{\rm Ce}^{58+}$, produced by a nuclear K--shell electron capture (EC) of the H--like ion ${^{140}}{\rm Pr}^{58+}$, is modulated in time with a period $T_d=(7.06\pm 8) $seconds. We explain this phenomenon by neutrino--flavour mixing and show that this can be understood within standard quantum field theory and derive a value for the squared mass difference $Δm^2_{21}=m^2_2-m^2_1=(0.763\pm 8) \cdot 10^{-4} \mathrm{eV}^2$. This proves that such processes provide a precise method to investigate neutrino--flavour mixing.

## Key claim
The observed time modulation of the electron capture decay rate of H-like ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺ ions in the GSI storage ring, with a period of about 7 seconds, can be explained by neutrino-flavour mixing within standard quantum field theory, provided the mother ions are described by a wave-packet with finite momentum uncertainty; without this wave-packet description, the predicted oscillation period would be orders of magnitude too short (0.3 ms) and incompatible with the data.

## Method
The author models the initial state of the mother ion as a Gaussian wave-packet in momentum space with width σ_κ, centered at zero momentum. Using first-order time-dependent perturbation theory with the standard weak-interaction Hamiltonian and neutrino flavour mixing, the decay rate is computed as the time derivative of the squared transition amplitude, rather than the usual division by time, to preserve interference terms. The kinematics require that two neutrino mass eigenstates with different masses are emitted with the same momentum, which is possible only if the mother ion's momentum uncertainty allows the heavier neutrino to be emitted from a slightly moving component of the wave-packet, introducing a small energy violation ΔE that sets the oscillation frequency.

## Key numbers
- Oscillation period of the EC-decay rate: T_d = (7.06 ± 8) seconds (from the GSI experiment).
- Squared mass difference derived from the model: Δm²₂₁ = m₂² − m₁² = (0.763 ± 8) × 10⁻⁴ eV².
- Q-value of the decay: Q = 3348 ± 6 keV.
- Momentum difference between the two neutrino mass eigenstates: k₁ − k₂ = 1.12 × 10⁻¹¹ eV.
- Oscillation amplitude from the fit: a = 0.15(3); angular frequency: Ω = 0.89(1) s⁻¹.
- Best-fit Δm²₂₁ from global solar-neutrino and KamLAND data (for comparison): 8.0⁺⁰·⁶₋₀·₅ × 10⁻⁵ eV².
- Flavour mixing angle: sin(2ϑ₁₂) = 0.925(32).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the GSI experiment reported by Litvinov (2007, 2008), which observed periodic modulations in the electron capture decay of ¹⁴⁰Pr⁵⁸⁺ ions stored in the ESR ring. The author builds on the standard theory of weak interactions for heavy ions (used by Ivanov (2007) to describe EC/β⁺ ratios) and on the established framework of neutrino oscillations from Pontecorvo (1957) and Maki (1962). The result provides a new method for studying neutrino mass differences and mixing angles via orbital electron capture in storage rings, potentially enabling precision measurements of Δm²₂₁ in other EC-decay systems.
