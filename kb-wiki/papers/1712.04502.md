# Incoherent diffractive photoproduction of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ on heavy nuclei in the color dipole approach

> We calculate cross sections and transverse momentum distributions for the incoherent diffractive production of vector mesons $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ on heavy nuclei. In distinction to coherent diffraction, the nucleus is allowed to break up, but except for the vector meson no new particles are produced in the reaction. Within the color dipole approach, we derive the multiple scattering expansion of the incoherent diffractive cross section as an expansion over quasielastic scatterings of the color dipole. We also compare our results to the measurement of the ALICE collaboration for incoherent $J/ψ$ production at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76 \, \rm {TeV}$ and show predictions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02 \, \rm{TeV}$. We also briefly discuss a possible contribution to $J/ψ$ production in peripheral collisions in the $70 ÷90 \%$ centrality class.

# Incoherent diffractive photoproduction of J/ψ and Υ on heavy nuclei in the color dipole approach

## Key claim
The authors calculate that incoherent diffractive photoproduction of heavy vector mesons on nuclei can be described by a multiple scattering expansion of color dipoles, where the single scattering term dominates over a broad range of momentum transfer for heavy mesons, but nuclear absorption effects are non-negligible even for the small dipoles involved in J/ψ and Υ production. The forward dip in the incoherent cross section does not vanish at zero momentum transfer due to absorption factors, contrary to what would occur in a weak-scattering approximation.

## Method
The authors derive the incoherent diffractive cross section using the color dipole approach, starting from the Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering formalism for nuclear targets. They expand the cross section as a sum over quasielastic scatterings of the Q̅Q dipole, using a factorized ansatz for the off-forward dipole cross section with a diffractive slope B, and employ the BGK (Bartels-Golec-Biernat-Kowalski) dipole cross section fitted to HERA data with DGLAP evolution. The vector meson wave functions are taken as boosted Gaussian parametrizations for J/ψ and Υ.

## Key numbers
- For J/ψ production on ²⁰⁸Pb at W=30 GeV, double scattering takes over from single scattering only at |t| ≳ 2.5 GeV², and triple scattering at |t| ≳ 4.8 GeV².
- For J/ψ production on ²⁰⁸Pb at W=100 GeV, the crossing of single and double scattering moves to |t| ∼ 2.2 GeV².
- For Υ production on ²⁰⁸Pb at W=100 GeV, double scattering crosses single scattering only at |t| ∼ 4.5 GeV².
- The nuclear suppression ratio for J/ψ on lead shows substantial suppression at small x, with the ratio depending weakly on the dipole cross section fit used.
- The calculations are compared to ALICE data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and predictions are given at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.

## Context
This work extends the color dipole approach to incoherent diffractive photoproduction, building on earlier treatments of coherent diffraction by Goncalves et al. (2011, 2015, 2017) and the Glauber-Gribov formalism for hadron-nucleus scattering developed by Czyz and Glauber (1970) and Glauber and Matthiae (1970). The formalism generalizes the multi-channel treatment of inelastic shadowing corrections by Kolya and collaborators to the case of heavy quarkonium production. The results enable predictions for ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and for future electron-ion collider experiments, where the multiple scattering expansion provides a systematic way to compute transverse momentum distributions beyond the single-scattering approximation.
