# Effective-theory description of heavy-flavored hadrons and their properties in a hot medium

> This dissertation investigates exotic hadrons with heavy-quark content that may be understood as being generated dynamically from the hadron-hadron interaction. This interaction is derived from a suitable effective Lagrangian and properly unitarized in a full coupled-channel basis. In particular, we discuss the possible interpretation of some of the Ωc* excited states recently discovered at LHCb as being meson-baryon molecular states. We also discuss the dynamical generation of excited open-charm mesons from the scattering of pseudoscalar and vector charmed mesons off light mesons. We show that a double-pole structure is predicted for the D0*(2300) state, as well as for the D1(2430), while the Ds0*(2317) and the Ds1(2460) may be interpreted as molecular bound states. Extensions of these calculations to the bottom sector are also presented. Furthermore, we investigate the thermal modification of the open heavy-flavor mesons in a hot medium. By means of an extension to finite temperature of the unitarized effective interactions with the light mesons, we obtain the in-medium spectral properties of the D, D*, Ds, and Ds* ground-state mesons. We also analyze the temperature dependence of the masses and the decay widths of the dynamically generated states. Additionally, we provide results for the bottomed mesons by exploiting the heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetry of the Lagrangian. We employ the temperature-dependent spectral functions to compute charm Euclidean correlators. We also present calculations of off-shell transport coefficients in the hadronic phase implementing in-medium scattering amplitudes and the thermal dependence of the heavy-meson spectral properties.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several recently observed heavy-flavor exotic hadrons can be dynamically generated from hadron-hadron interactions derived from effective Lagrangians and unitarized in a coupled-channel basis, but this interpretation is conditional on the molecular picture and does not exclude other possible structures such as compact multiquark states.

## Method

The author derives hadron-hadron interactions from effective Lagrangians and unitarizes them in a full coupled-channel basis using a T-matrix approach with cutoff regularization. For finite-temperature calculations, the effective interactions are extended to nonzero temperature, and the in-medium spectral properties of open heavy-flavor mesons are obtained through self-energy corrections computed within the imaginary-time formalism. Transport coefficients such as drag force and diffusion coefficients are calculated using the in-medium scattering amplitudes and temperature-dependent spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- The D0*(2300) state is predicted to have a double-pole structure, as is the D1(2430) state
- The Ds0*(2317) and Ds1(2460) may be interpreted as molecular bound states
- At T=150 MeV, the pion mass is reduced to m_pi(T=150 MeV)=120 MeV (from vacuum value of 138 MeV)
- At T=150 MeV, the ground-state D(*) and D_s(*) meson masses decrease by Delta m_D(*)=4 MeV and Delta m_D_s(*)=2 MeV relative to their thermal masses
- At T=150 MeV, the dynamically generated D0*(2300) and D1(2430) states change mass by -2 MeV with widths 20 MeV larger
- At T=150 MeV, the bound states Ds0*(2317) and Ds1(2460) change mass by -2 MeV with width increase of 1 MeV
- The cutoff used in the center-of-mass frame for the two-meson loop function is Lambda_cm=800 MeV

## Context

This dissertation extends the effective-theory approach to heavy-flavor hadrons previously applied in works such as Oller (1997, 1998) for light mesons, and applies it to the charm and bottom sectors. The work enables comparison of finite-temperature spectral functions with lattice QCD calculations of Euclidean correlators, and provides transport coefficients that can be matched to results from the quark-gluon plasma phase extracted from Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion collision data. The calculations of off-shell transport coefficients improve upon previous approaches by implementing in-medium scattering amplitudes and thermal dependence of heavy-meson spectral properties.
