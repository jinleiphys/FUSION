# Saturation of nuclear matter and radii of unstable nuclei

> We examine relations among the parameters characterizing the phenomenological equation of state (EOS) of nearly symmetric, uniform nuclear matter near the saturation density by comparing macroscopic calculations of radii and masses of stable nuclei with the experimental data. The EOS parameters of interest here are the symmetry energy S_0, the symmetry energy density-derivative coefficient L and the incompressibility K_0 at the normal nuclear density. We find a constraint on the relation between K_0 and L from the empirically allowed values of the slope of the saturation line (the line joining the saturation points of nuclear matter at finite neutron excess), together with a strong correlation between S_0 and L. In the light of the uncertainties in the values of K_0 and L, we macroscopically calculate radii of unstable nuclei as expected to be produced in future facilities. We find that the matter radii depend strongly on L while being almost independent of K_0, a feature that will help to determine the L value via systematic measurements of nuclear size.

## Key claim

The authors find that the matter radii of unstable neutron-rich nuclei depend strongly on the density symmetry coefficient L while being almost independent of the incompressibility K0, which could allow L to be determined from systematic measurements of nuclear size, but only if the slope y of the saturation line is also constrained.

## Method

The authors constructed a macroscopic nuclear model using an extended Thomas-Fermi approximation with a phenomenological equation of state for nearly symmetric nuclear matter. They optimized the model parameters by fitting calculated masses and charge radii of stable nuclei (mass number A from 25 to 245) to experimental data for fixed values of the slope y and incompressibility K0, then used the resulting parameter sets to calculate root-mean-square matter and charge radii of neutron-rich Ni and Sn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The linear relation between symmetry energy S0 and density symmetry coefficient L is S0 ≈ 28 MeV + 0.075 L, nearly independent of K0.
- The saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter is −16.0 ± 0.5 MeV and the saturation density is 0.155 ± 0.015 fm⁻³.
- The root-mean-square deviations of calculated nuclear masses from experimental data for 1962 nuclides are ∼3–5 MeV for all combinations of y and K0.
- The root-mean-square deviations of calculated charge radii from experimental data for 92 stable nuclides (A ≥ 50) are about 0.06 fm.
- At neutron excess α ∼ 0.3, a difference of order 0.05–0.1 fm occurs in matter radii due to variation in the slope y.
- Under fixed L, K0-induced uncertainties in matter radii are limited to order ±0.015 fm on the neutron-rich side.
- The optimal values of the gradient energy coefficient F0 are confined to 66 ± 6 MeV fm⁵.
- The incompressibility was estimated to be K0 = 231 ± 5 MeV from giant monopole resonance observations.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Oyamatsu et al. (1998) that used two macroscopic nuclear models to examine constraints on L and S0 from nuclear observables. The authors compare their results with predictions from various Skyrme-Hartree-Fock schemes and the relativistic mean field model TM1 from Sugahara and Toki (1994). The findings enable future systematic measurements of matter radii of unstable neutron-rich nuclei at radioactive ion beam facilities to determine the density symmetry coefficient L, which would then allow the slope y to be deduced as a function of K0.
