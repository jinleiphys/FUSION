# Diquark Condensation in High Density Baryon Matter

> We argue that cold quark matter is a color superconductor. The Cooper pairs of QCD are spin-isospin zero, color anti-triplet diquarks. In this contribution we review some of the available information on the quark-quark interaction in QCD. We show that instantons provide a strongly attractive interaction in the scalar diquark channel. In the high density phase, this interaction leads to diquark Bose condensation with a gap on the order of 50 MeV.

## Key claim

Cold quark matter is a color superconductor formed by spin-isospin zero, color anti-triplet diquark Cooper pairs, with the gap estimated to be on the order of 50 MeV, but only under the condition that non-perturbative instanton effects provide the attractive interaction, as perturbative one-gluon exchange alone would yield gaps of only about 1 MeV.

## Method

The author reviews the quark-quark interaction in QCD, focusing on the instanton-induced effective four-fermion interaction and its Fierz-rearranged form in the diquark channel. A mean-field model is then constructed with both chiral (quark-anti-quark) and diquark condensates, solving coupled gap equations for the corresponding gaps Σ and Δ as a function of chemical potential. The instanton-induced coupling is taken to be exponentially suppressed at high density, G(μ)=G(0)exp[-N_f ρ² μ² θ(μ-μ_c)], with a monopole form factor of range Λ=300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum diquark gap Δ is on the order of 50 MeV, occurring at a chemical potential of μ=270 MeV where a first-order transition from the chiral condensed state to the diquark condensed state takes place.
- At μ=0, the chiral gap Σ is 400 MeV, used to fix the coupling G(0).
- The scalar diquark mass in the instanton liquid is m_S ≃ 400 MeV, with a scalar-vector diquark splitting of almost 500 MeV; lattice calculations give m_S ≃ 650 MeV and a smaller splitting m_V-m_S=100 MeV.
- The condensation energy is ε = -30 MeV/fm³, the coherence length ξ_Δ ≃ 0.8 fm, and the penetration depth ξ_A ≃ 1.5 fm.
- The critical color-magnetic field is B² ≃ (130 MeV)².
- The coupling constant from phenomenology is G ≃ 490 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of color superconductivity by Bailin and Love (1984), who found gaps of order 1 MeV at baryon densities ρ∼(5-10)ρ₀, by showing that non-perturbative instanton effects can produce gaps about two orders of magnitude larger. The results are consistent with earlier NJL model calculations of diquark masses by Vogl (1990) and with instanton liquid simulations by Schaefer, Shuryak, and Verbaarschot (1994). The paper enables further study of the rich phase structure of the condensed state, particularly when strange quarks are included, and suggests that the order parameter may be highly inhomogeneous due to the small condensation energy.
