# Relativistic SU(3) chiral baryon-baryon Lagrangian up to order q^2

> We construct the most general chiral effective Lagrangian for baryon-baryon contact interactions in flavor SU(3) up to order q^2 using a covariant power counting. A subset of these contact terms contributes to the baryon-baryon potential in chiral effective field theory. The Lorentz invariant effective Lagrangian is constructed to fulfill the invariance under charge conjugation, parity transformation, Hermitian conjugation and the local chiral symmetry group SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R. Goldstone bosons and external fields are included as well, thus providing additional four-baryon contact vertices involving e.g. pseudoscalar mesons and/or photons. In order to eliminate the linearly dependent terms, we use the Fierz identities, the equations of motion, and a Cayley-Hamilton relation for SU(3). As an application the baryon-baryon scattering contact potentials in low partial waves are considered.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the most general relativistic chiral effective Lagrangian for baryon-baryon contact interactions in flavor SU(3) up to order \(q^2\), but the abstract glosses over the fact that many of the relativistic terms actually contribute at higher order under non-relativistic reduction and can therefore be discarded in non-relativistic calculations up to order \(q^2\).

## Method

The authors construct the Lorentz-invariant Lagrangian by requiring invariance under charge conjugation, parity, Hermitian conjugation, and the local chiral symmetry group SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R, using the external field method to include Goldstone bosons and photons. Linearly dependent terms are eliminated using Fierz identities, equations of motion, cyclic properties of the trace, and a Cayley-Hamilton relation for SU(3). The resulting contact terms are then evaluated for baryon-baryon scattering in low partial waves, and a non-relativistic reduction is performed to identify which terms contribute at each chiral order.

## Key numbers

* The baryon-baryon contact Lagrangian is constructed up to chiral order \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\), with the chiral dimension of building blocks such as \(u_\mu\) being \(\mathcal{O}(q^1)\), and \(\chi_+\), \(f_{\mu\nu}^\pm\), \(\chi_-\) being \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\).
* At \(\mathcal{O}(q^0)\) terms with \(n=0,1,2\) covariant derivative pairs are needed; at \(\mathcal{O}(q)\) terms with \(n=0,1\) contribute; at \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\) only terms with \(n=0\) need to be considered.
* The nucleon mass is given as \(M_N \approx 1\) GeV, used to justify the non-relativistic expansion where suppressed terms contribute at order \(q/M_0\).
* In the two-flavor case (NN potential), there are two low-energy constants at leading order and seven additional low-energy constants at next-to-leading order.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor relativistic contact Lagrangian constructed by Girlanda (2010) to the three-flavor case, and builds upon the leading-order SU(3) baryon-baryon contact Lagrangian given by Savage (1995) and Polinder (2006). The results enable complete next-to-leading order calculations of baryon-baryon interactions in all strangeness and isospin channels, which are needed for hyperon-nucleon scattering calculations (Haidenbauer, 2013) and for studies of hypernuclei and strange baryonic matter.
