# Neutron width statistics in a realistic resonance-reaction model

> A recent experiment on s-wave neutron scattering from $^{192,194,196}$Pt found that the reduced neutron width distributions deviate significantly from the expected Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD), and several explanations have been proposed within the statistical model of compound nucleus reactions. Here, we study the statistics of reduced neutron widths in the reaction $n+ ^{194}$Pt within a model that combines the standard statistical model with a realistic treatment of the neutron channel. We find that, if the correct secular energy dependence of the average neutron widths is used, then the reduced neutron width distribution is in excellent agreement with the PTD for a reasonable range of the neutron-nucleus coupling strength and depth of the neutron channel potential. Within our parameter range, there can be a near-threshold bound or virtual state of the neutron channel potential that modifies the energy dependence of the average width from the $\sqrt{E}$ dependence, commonly assumed in experimental analysis, in agreement with the proposal of H. A. Weidenmüller [1]. In these cases, the reduced neutron width distributions extracted using the $\sqrt{E}$ dependence are significantly broader than the PTD. We identify a relatively narrow range of parameters where this effect is significant.

## Key claim
The reduced neutron width distribution in the n+194Pt reaction is in excellent agreement with the Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD) when the correct secular energy dependence of the average neutron widths is used, contradicting experimental claims of PTD violation. Apparent PTD violation can occur only if the energy dependence of the average width deviates from the commonly assumed sqrt(E) form, which can happen in the presence of a near-threshold bound or virtual state of the neutron channel potential.

## Method
The authors constructed a model Hamiltonian combining a mesh representation of the neutron channel (Woods-Saxon potential with parameters from the literature) with a Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) description of the internal compound-nucleus states. They solved the nonlinear eigenvalue problem for complex wavenumbers to find neutron resonances, using 100 GOE realizations with 160 resonances each in the energy range 1-14 keV. The baseline parameter set used a Woods-Saxon potential with V0 = -44.54 MeV, mean resonance spacing D = 82 eV, gamma decay width Gamma_gamma = 72 meV, and coupling strength v0 = 11 keV-fm^(1/2), with parameters varied to investigate near-threshold states.

## Key numbers
- Mean resonance spacing D = 82 eV and total gamma decay width Gamma_gamma = 72 meV taken from the RIPL-3 database for n+194Pt
- Baseline coupling strength v0 = 11 keV-fm^(1/2) chosen to reproduce the RIPL-3 neutron strength function S0*sqrt(En) = 2.0 x 10^(-4) eV^(-1/2) at neutron energy En = 8 keV
- For the baseline model, reduced chi-squared values chi^2_r = 0.9 (reduction A using model average widths) and chi^2_r = 1.0 (reduction B using sqrt(E) dependence) indicate excellent agreement with PTD
- For model M4 (V0 = -41.15 MeV, v0 = 1.6 keV-fm^(1/2) with a weakly bound state at E0 ≈ -2 keV), chi^2_r = 0.9 for reduction A but chi^2_r = 5.8 for reduction B (sqrt(E) assumption), showing significant deviation from PTD
- For models M4-M6 with the near-threshold state, the maximum-likelihood fit to a chi^2 distribution gave nu_fit = 0.92 for reduction B, compared to nu = 1 for PTD
- The average elastic scattering cross section at E = 8 keV ranges from 19-30 b for baseline models but jumps to 249-288 b for models M4-M6 with the near-threshold state

## Context
This work extends the statistical model of compound nucleus reactions to include a realistic treatment of the neutron channel, addressing the experimental finding by Koehler (2010) that s-wave neutron scattering from 192,194,196Pt showed reduced neutron width distributions deviating significantly from the Porter-Thomas distribution. The authors test the proposal by Weidenmuller (2010) that a near-threshold bound or virtual state could modify the energy dependence of average widths from the sqrt(E) form. Prior numerical and analytical work by Celardo (2011), Fyodorov (2015), Volya (2015), and Bogomolny (2017) had neglected the strong energy dependence of real and imaginary terms near threshold and had not used realistic parameters for Pt isotopes. The results enable future experimental measurements of elastic neutron cross sections to determine whether such a near-threshold state exists in Pt isotopes.
