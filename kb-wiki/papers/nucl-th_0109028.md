# Spin-isospin excitations and beta+/EC half-lives of medium-mass deformed nuclei

> A selfconsistent approach based on a deformed HF+BCS+QRPA method with density-dependent Skyrme forces is used to describe beta+ decay properties in even-even deformed proton rich nuclei. Residual spin-isospin forces are included in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. The quasiparticle basis contains neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing correlations in the BCS approach, while neutron-proton pairing interaction is treated as a residual force in QRPA. We discuss the sensitivity of Gamow-Teller strength distributions and beta+/EC half-lives to deformation, pairing and the strength of the particle-particle interaction. The dependence on deformation is also compared to that of spin M1 strength distributions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a selfconsistent deformed HF+BCS+QRPA method with Skyrme forces, including a residual particle-particle neutron-proton pairing interaction, can reproduce beta+/EC half-lives in medium-mass deformed proton-rich nuclei, but the agreement depends on the choice of the particle-particle coupling strength κ^{pp}_{GT} which is fitted phenomenologically rather than derived selfconsistently.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme force SG2, including BCS pairing correlations for neutrons and protons with gap parameters determined from experimental odd-even mass differences. They then solved proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) equations with a spin-isospin residual interaction consisting of a selfconsistent particle-hole part and a separable particle-particle neutron-proton pairing force in the J^π=1^+ channel. The calculations were carried out for the equilibrium deformed shapes (oblate and prolate) of even-even proton-rich isotopes in the A≈70 mass region, specifically the Ge, Se, Kr, and Sr isotopic chains from N=Z to N=Z+6.

## Key numbers

- The particle-hole coupling strength for A=74 with SG2 is χ^{ph}_{GT}=0.37 MeV, derived selfconsistently from the Skyrme force parameters.
- The adopted particle-particle coupling strength is κ^{pp}_{GT}=0.07 MeV, chosen as a single value for all nuclei studied, far from the QRPA collapse value around 0.1 MeV.
- The quenching factor used for half-life calculations is A^2 = [(g_A/g_V)_eff]^2 = [0.77(g_A/g_V)_free]^2 = 0.90.
- For ⁷⁶Sr, the optimum κ^{pp}_{GT} values are 0.03 MeV (oblate) and 0.07 MeV (prolate), showing shape dependence.
- The Q_EC window for ⁷⁴Kr is 3.1 MeV; for ⁷⁶Sr it is 6.1 MeV; for ⁷⁸Sr it is 3.8 MeV; for ⁸⁰Sr it is 1.9 MeV; for ⁷²Kr it is 5 MeV.
- The Ikeda sum rule ∑_ω [(M^ω_-)^2 - (M^ω_+)^2] = 3(N-Z) is always fulfilled in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous selfconsistent HF+BCS+QRPA calculations (Sarriguren et al., 2001) by including a residual particle-particle neutron-proton pairing force, which was missing in their earlier treatment. The approach builds on earlier spherical QRPA models and deformed calculations using Nilsson potentials (Krumlinde and Moller, 1984) and Woods-Saxon potentials (Moller and Randrup, 1990), as well as the work of Homma et al. (1996) who used Nilsson+BCS+QRPA with fitted coupling strengths. The results enable experimental identification of nuclear shapes through beta-decay measurements, particularly identifying ⁷⁴Kr and ⁷⁶,⁷⁸,⁸⁰Sr as the best candidates for distinguishing oblate versus prolate shapes from Gamow-Teller strength distributions below the Q_EC window.
