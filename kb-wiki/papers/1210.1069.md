# Pion-Nucleus Microscopic Optical Potential at Intermediate Energies and In-Medium Effect on the Elementary $πN$ Scattering Amplitude

> Analysis is performed of calculations of the elastic scattering differential cross sections of pions on the $^{28}$Si, $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb nuclei at energies from 130 to 290 MeV basing on the microscopic optical potential (OP) constructed as an optical limit of a Glauber theory. Such an OP is defined by the corresponding target nucleus density distribution function and by the elementary $πN$ amplitude of scattering. The three (say, "in-medium") parameters of the $πN$ scattering amplitude: total cross section, the ratio of real to imaginary part of the forward $πN$ amplitude, and the slope parameter, were obtained by fitting them to the data on the respective pion-nucleus cross sections calculated by means of the corresponding relativistic wave equation with the above OP. A difference is discussed between the best-fit "in-medium" parameters and the "free" parameters of the $πN$ scattering amplitudes known from the experimental data on scattering of pions on free nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the three parameters of the elementary pion-nucleon scattering amplitude (total cross section, ratio of real to imaginary forward amplitude, and slope parameter) are modified when the pion scatters inside a nucleus compared to scattering on free nucleons, with the in-medium interaction becoming weaker at the Delta-resonance energies around 130-290 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic optical potential using the optical limit of Glauber theory, defined by the target nucleus density distribution (symmetrized Fermi function) and the elementary pion-nucleon scattering amplitude. They solved the relativistic wave equation with this potential using the DWUCK4 code to calculate elastic scattering differential cross sections for pions on 28Si, 40Ca, 58Ni, and 208Pb at laboratory kinetic energies from 130 to 291 MeV. The three in-medium parameters (sigma, alpha, beta) were obtained by fitting the calculated cross sections to experimental data using an asynchronous differential evolution algorithm to minimize chi-squared.

## Context

This work extends the high-energy approximation (Glauber theory) approach to microscopic optical potentials developed by the same authors (Zemlyanaya et al., earlier work) and applies it to pion-nucleus scattering at intermediate energies where the Delta resonance dominates. The results are compared with the free pion-nucleon scattering parameters from Locher et al. (earlier work) and with calculations using the Kisslinger potential approach. The study enables future investigations of the ambiguity problem in parameter extraction and highlights the need for total reaction cross section data to resolve multiple minima in the chi-squared function, as well as the need for separate treatment of neutron-rich nuclei like 208Pb where proton and neutron numbers differ significantly.
