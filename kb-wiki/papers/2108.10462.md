# Mass spectra of doubly heavy tetraquarks in an improved chromomagnetic interaction model

> Doubly heavy tetraquark states are the prime candidates for tightly bound exotic states. We present a systematic study of the mass spectra of the $S$-wave doubly heavy tetraquark states $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ ($q=u, d, s$ and $Q=c, b$) with different quantum numbers $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$ in the framework of the improved chromomagnetic interaction (ICMI) model. The parameters in the ICMI model are obtained by fitting the conventional hadron spectra and are used directly to predict the masses of the tetraquark states. For heavy quarks, the uncertainties of the parameters are obtained by comparing the masses of doubly and triply heavy baryons with those given by lattice QCD, QCD sum rules, and potential models. Several compact and stable bound states are found in both the doubly charmed and doubly bottomed tetraquark systems. The predicted mass of the $cc\bar u\bar d$ state is consistent with the recent measurement from the LHCb collaboration.

# Mass spectra of doubly heavy tetraquarks in an improved chromomagnetic interaction model

## Key claim
The improved chromomagnetic interaction (ICMI) model predicts that the doubly charmed tetraquark state $cc\bar u\bar d$ with quantum numbers $IJ^P=01^+$ has a mass of 3761.6 MeV, which lies below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold and is consistent with the LHCb observation of $T_{cc}^+$, but the model's predictions for heavy-quark parameters carry uncertainties derived from comparing doubly and triply heavy baryon masses across multiple theoretical approaches.

## Method
The authors constructed an ICMI Hamiltonian for four-quark systems by combining chromomagnetic and chromoelectric interaction terms, integrating over spatial wave functions to obtain effective parameters $m_{ij}$ and $v_{ij}$. They fitted these parameters to known ground-state meson and baryon spectra, with heavy-quark parameters constrained by comparing doubly and triply heavy baryon masses from lattice QCD, QCD sum rules, and potential models. The mass spectra for $S$-wave $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ states ($Q=c,b$; $q=u,d,s$) with $J^P=0^+,1^+,2^+$ were obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a color-spin basis using the diquark-anti-diquark configuration, with Pauli exclusion enforced through symmetry factors $\delta_{ij}^S$ and $\delta_{ij}^A$.

## Key numbers
- The lowest $J^P=1^+$ state in the $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system (isospin singlet) has mass 3761.6 MeV, below the $D^{*+}D^0$ threshold
- The $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system has two $J^P=0^+$ states at 3836.7 MeV and 4126.7 MeV
- The $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system has additional $J^P=1^+$ states at 3950.9 MeV and 3979.7 MeV
- The $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ $J^P=2^+$ state has mass 4021.2 MeV
- The $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system has stable states: $10^+$ at 10479.1 MeV, $01^+$ at 10310.9 MeV, $11^+$ at 10496.1 MeV, $12^+$ at 10518.7 MeV
- The $bc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system has stable states: $00^+$ at 6994.1 MeV, $01^+$ at 7063.9 MeV
- The $bb\bar{n}\bar{s}$ system has stable states: $0^+$ at 10597 MeV, $1^+$ at 10489.2 MeV, $1^+$ at 10616.5 MeV
- The $bb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ system has a stable $0^+$ state at 10708.3 MeV
- The $bc\bar{n}\bar{s}$ system has stable states: $0^+$ at 7163.4 MeV, $1^+$ at 7218.1 MeV
- The $\Xi_{cc}$ baryon mass from the ICMI model ranges 3.627 to 3.657 GeV for $J^P=1/2$ and 3.692 to 3.722 GeV for $J^P=3/2$
- The $\Omega_{bbb}$ baryon mass from the ICMI model ranges 14.260 to 14.440 GeV

## Context
This work extends the ICMI model, previously applied to other multiquark systems by Hogaasen (2013), Weng (2018), and An (2021), to the doubly heavy tetraquark sector for the first time. The predicted $cc\bar u\bar d$ state at 3761.6 MeV with $IJ^P=01^+$ is consistent with the LHCb observation of $T_{cc}^+$ reported by Polyakov (2021), and the model finds several stable bound states in doubly bottomed and mixed charm-bottom systems that previous studies by Karliner (2017), Eichten (2017), and others had predicted with varying conclusions. The results enable experimental searches for additional doubly heavy tetraquark states at LHCb and future facilities, particularly the predicted stable $bb\bar{q}\bar{q}$ and $bc\bar{q}\bar{q}$ states that would decay only weakly.
