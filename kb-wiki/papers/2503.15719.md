# Phase transitions and finite-size effects in integrable virial statistical models

> We analyze thermodynamic models for fluid systems in equilibrium based on a virial expansion of the internal energy in terms of the volume density. We prove that the models, formulated for finite-size systems with $N$ particles, are exactly solvable to any expansion order, as expectation values of physical observables (e.g., volume density) are determined from solutions to nonlinear C-integrable partial differential equations (PDEs) of hydrodynamic type. In the limit $N\to \infty$, phase transitions emerge as classical shock waves in the space of thermodynamic variables. Near critical points, we argue that the volume density exhibits a scaling behavior consistent with the Universality Conjecture in viscous transport PDEs. As an application, we employ our framework to nuclear and quark matter, constructing a global quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram that reveals critical points for the nuclear liquid-gas transition and the hadron gas-quark-gluon plasma transition. We demonstrate how finite-size effects smear critical signatures, implying their potential impact on the search for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper proves that thermodynamic models based on a virial expansion of internal energy are exactly solvable for finite-size systems with N particles, and that phase transitions emerge as classical shock waves only in the thermodynamic limit N → ∞; finite-size effects smear critical signatures, implying that the search for the QCD critical point in experiments may be more challenging than previously thought.

## Method

The authors formulate a virial-type expansion of the internal energy in terms of volume density for fluid systems in the isothermal-isobaric ensemble, deriving a linear partial differential equation for the partition function and nonlinear C-integrable PDEs for the free energy and order parameter. They analyze the thermodynamic limit via Laplace's method and saddle-point approximation, identifying phase transitions with gradient catastrophe phenomena in the inviscid limit. Finite-size corrections are incorporated through a viscous Burgers-type equation with a 1/N term, and the framework is applied to QCD by constructing a phenomenological mean-field model with six virial coefficients fitted to nuclear and quark matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for nuclear liquid-gas transition: T_c,1 = 0.10 fm⁻¹ (≈ 20 MeV)
- Critical temperature for hadron gas–quark-gluon plasma transition: T_c,2 = 0.51 fm⁻¹ (≈ 100 MeV)
- Critical volume density for nuclear L-G transition: v_c,1 = 16.45 fm³ (≈ 1/(0.38 n₀) with n₀ ≈ 0.16/fm³)
- Critical volume density for HG-QGP transition: v_c,2 = 2.08 fm³ (≈ 1/(3 n₀))
- Spinodal points for HG-QGP transition at T = 0 MeV: v_a = 2.40 fm³ (≈ 1/(2.6 n₀)) and v_b = 1.89 fm³ (≈ 1/(3.3 n₀))
- Virial coefficients: a₂ ≈ 16 v₀ MeV, a₃ ≈ 108 v₀² MeV, a₄ ≈ -213 v₀³ MeV, a₅ ≈ 93 v₀⁴ MeV, a₆ ≈ -12 v₀⁵ MeV, b ≈ 0.075 v₀ (where v₀ = 1/n₀)
- Scaling of volume density near critical points: v_N = v_c + (γ/N^{1/4}) U( (Δx + ε'(v_c)Δt)/(α N^{-3/4}), Δt/(β N^{-1/4}) ) + O(N^{-1/2})

## Context

This work extends the integrable PDE approach to virial statistical models developed by Moro (2014) and Barra (2015) for van der Waals fluids, generalizing it to arbitrary virial expansion order and applying it to QCD matter. The framework enables construction of a global QCD phase diagram with both the nuclear liquid-gas transition and the hadron gas–quark-gluon plasma transition, complementing lattice QCD simulations (Nagata, 2021) and mean-field approaches (Sorensen, 2021; 2023). The finite-size results have direct implications for interpreting experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (STAR, 2025), NA61/SHINE (2025), and HADES (2020) experiments searching for the QCD critical point.
