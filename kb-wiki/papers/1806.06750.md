# Dilepton photoproduction measures the fluctuations of initial electromagnetic fields in nuclear collisions

> Dilepton production from two photon interactions $γγ\rightarrow l^+l^-$ are studied in semi-central and peripheral nuclear collisions. Based on Weizsäcker-Williams approach, it is shown that the dilepton photoproduction is proportional to the electromagnetic (EM) fields $\sim E^2B^2$ and therefore sensitive to the magnitude and lifetime of initial EM fields which last only for a short time and are hard to be measured in experiments directly. We propose dilepton photoproduction as a probe for the nuclear charge fluctuations, which are crucial for the electric/magnetic field induced chiral and charge particle evolutions. We calculate the relative standard deviation of dilepton mass spectrum with event-by-event fluctuating nuclear charge distributions (and EM fields).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that dilepton photoproduction from two-photon interactions in semi-central and peripheral nuclear collisions can serve as a probe for event-by-event fluctuations of initial electromagnetic fields, which are otherwise impossible to measure directly. The key hedge is that the observable proposed, the relative standard deviation of the dilepton mass spectrum, is only around 4–5% in the experimentally measurable region, making it a small but potentially measurable signal.

## Method

The authors use the Weizsäcker-Williams approach to approximate the electromagnetic fields of fast-moving nuclei as quasi-real photon densities, then compute the dilepton yield from γγ → l⁺l⁻ scattering via the Breit-Wheeler formula. They compare two scenarios: a smooth Woods-Saxon nuclear charge distribution and an event-by-event fluctuating charge distribution generated by Monte Carlo sampling of individual proton positions within the nucleus, where each proton has a finite size described by a Woods-Saxon profile. The relative standard deviation of the dilepton invariant mass spectrum is calculated over many events to isolate the effect of charge fluctuations from other uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The relative standard deviation of dilepton photoproduction yields is around (4–5)% in the invariant mass region 0.4 < M_{e⁺e⁻} < 2 GeV at √s_{NN} = 200 GeV Au-Au collisions.
- The impact parameter studied is b = 10 fm and b = 15 fm for semi-central and peripheral collisions.
- The maximum electromagnetic field strength in semi-central and peripheral collisions is of order eB ∼ 10 m_π² at RHIC and LHC energies.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for Au nuclei are: ρ₀ = 0.1694 fm⁻³, r₀ = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm.
- The proton charge distribution parameters are: r_{p0} = 1.2 fm, a_p = a = 0.535 fm, ρ_{p0} = 0.0458 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton photoproduction from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) into semi-central and peripheral nuclear collisions where hadronic interactions also occur, building on prior work by Baltz (2009) and Zha (2018) on two-photon processes in hadronic collisions. The proposed observable, event-by-event fluctuations in the dilepton mass spectrum, is intended to provide indirect access to the initial electromagnetic field configurations that drive chiral effects such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) by Huang (2013), whose experimental signals remain under debate due to contamination from strong collective expansion.
