# On the question of current conservation for the Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint theory

> The Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint theory are of special interest not only in view of applications for phenomenological calculations of mesonic spectra but also because they avoid no-go theorems about relativistic interactions. Furthermore, they provide a quantum mechanical description in a manifestly Lorentz invariant way using the concept of a multi-time wave function. In this paper, we place them into the context of the multi-time formalism of Dirac, Tomonaga and Schwinger for the first time. A general physical and mathematical framework is outlined and the mechanism which permits relativistic interaction is identified. The main requirement derived from the general framework is the existence of conserved tensor currents with a positive component which can play the role of a probability density. We analyze this question for a general class of Two-Body Dirac equations thoroughly and comprehensively. While the free Dirac current is not conserved, it is possible to find replacements. Improving on previous research, we achieve definite conclusions whether restrictions of the function space or of the interaction terms can guarantee the positive definiteness of the currents -- and whether such restrictions are physically adequate. The consequences of the results are drawn, with respect to both applied and foundational perspectives.

## Key claim

The Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint theory can be placed within the multi-time formalism of Dirac, Tomonaga and Schwinger, but the free Dirac current is not conserved for these equations; while replacement currents exist, no general argument guarantees their positive definiteness, and the paper provides definite conclusions about whether restrictions on the function space or on the interaction terms can ensure positive definiteness and whether such restrictions are physically adequate.

## Method

The author analyzes a general class of Two-Body Dirac equations for fermion-antifermion systems, originally formulated by Sazdjian (1990s), where the operators \(D_i\) take the form \(D_i = D_{i,0} + D_{3-i,0} \hat{V}\) with \(D_{i,0}\) being the free Dirac operators and \(\hat{V}\) a potential operator depending on \(x = x_1 - x_2\), \(\hat{p}\), \(\hat{P}\), and gamma matrices. The necessary compatibility condition \([D_1, D_2] = \lambda_1 D_1 + \lambda_2 D_2\) is imposed, leading to the requirement that the potential depends on \(x\) only via the covariantized relative coordinate \(x_\perp^\mu = \hat{\pi}^\mu_\nu x^\nu\) where \(\hat{\pi}^\mu_\nu\) projects orthogonal to the total momentum operator \(\hat{P}\). The existence and positive definiteness of conserved tensor currents are then examined, building on and critiquing prior work by Sazdjian (1990s).

## Key numbers

* The free Dirac current for the two-body system is \(j^{\mu\nu} = \overline{\psi} \gamma_1^\mu \gamma_2^\nu \psi\), with the positive component \(j^{00} = \psi^\dagger \psi\); this current is not conserved for the Two-Body Dirac equations.
* The consistency condition \([D_{1,0}^2 - D_{2,0}^2, \hat{V}] = 0\) reduces to \([\hat{p}_1^2 - \hat{p}_2^2, \hat{V}] = 0\), which further gives \([\hat{P} \cdot \hat{p}, \hat{V}] = 0\) and leads to \(P^\mu \frac{\partial \hat{V}}{\partial x^\mu} = 0\).
* The projection operator is \(\hat{\pi}^\nu_\mu = \left( 1 - \frac{\hat{P}_\mu \hat{P}^\nu}{\hat{P} \cdot \hat{P}} \right)\), and the potential depends on \(x\) only via \(\hat{x}_\perp^\mu = \hat{\pi}^\mu_\nu x^\nu\), which in the center of momentum frame reduces to \((0, \mathbf{x})\).

## Context

This work extends the multi-time formalism originally proposed by Dirac (1932) and developed by Bloch (1950s), Tomonaga (1946), and Schwinger (1948) to the Two-Body Dirac equations of constraint theory, which were previously studied for mesonic spectra calculations by Crater and Van Alstine (1990s) and Sazdjian (1990s). The paper addresses open questions from Sazdjian's analysis of scalar products and currents (1990s), finding that analysis incomplete, and provides a framework for understanding whether these equations can serve as a self-contained relativistic multi-time theory for two particles, potentially enabling a fundamental quantum mechanical description that avoids no-go theorems about relativistic interactions.
