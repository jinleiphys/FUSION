# Pseudorapidity dependence of anisotropic flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The pseudorapidity dependence of anisotropic flows $v_{1}$, $v_{2}$, $v_{3}$ , and $v_{4}$ of charged hadrons in heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider is studied in a multi-phase transport model. We find that while the string melting scenario, in which hadrons that are expected to be formed from initial strings are converted to their valence quarks and antiquarks, can explain the measured $p_{T}$-dependence of $v_{2}$ and $ v_{4} $ of charged hadrons at midrapidity with a parton scattering cross section of about 10 \textrm{mb}, the scenario without string melting reproduces better the recent data on $v_{1}$ and $v_{2}$ of charged hadrons at large pseudorapidity in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV . Our results thus suggest that a partonic matter is formed during early stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions only around midrapidity and that strings remain dominant at large rapidities. The $p_{T}$-dependence of $v_{1} $, $v_{2}$, $v_{3}$ and $v_{4}$ for charged hadrons at forward pseudorapidity is also predicted, and we find that while $v_{1}$ and $v_{2}$ are appreciable at large pseudorapidity the higher-order anisotropic flows $v_{3}$ and $v_{4} $ are essentially zero.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a partonic matter is formed during the early stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions only around midrapidity (|η| ≤ 3), while strings remain dominant at large pseudorapidity (|η| ≥ 3). This conclusion is hedged by the finding that the string melting scenario with a parton scattering cross section of about 10 mb, which explains midrapidity data, fails to reproduce the measured v1 and v2 at large pseudorapidity, whereas the default scenario without string melting does reproduce those data.

## Method
The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, a hybrid model combining initial conditions from the HIJING model with Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) for partonic interactions and a relativistic transport (ART) model for hadronic interactions. They compared two versions: the default AMPT model (without string melting) and the string melting scenario, where hadrons from initial strings are converted to valence quarks and antiquarks. Anisotropic flows v1, v2, v3, and v4 were computed as Fourier coefficients of the azimuthal distribution of charged hadrons as a function of pseudorapidity and transverse momentum in Au + Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- Parton scattering cross section of about 10 mb used in the string melting scenario to explain measured pT-dependence of v2 and v4 at midrapidity.
- Parton scattering cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb were tested in the string melting scenario for pseudorapidity dependence.
- Directed flow v1 at large pseudorapidity (|η| ≥ 3) was underestimated by the string melting scenario with both 3 mb and 10 mb cross sections.
- Elliptic flow v2 at large pseudorapidity (|η| ≥ 1.5) was overestimated by the string melting scenario with 10 mb cross section.
- The balance transverse momentum where v1 changes from negative to positive at η = 4 is about 1.0 GeV/c.
- v4 at η = 4 is less than 0.5%.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flows at midrapidity at RHIC, which had focused on v2 and v4 using the AMPT model with string melting (Lin 2001). It addresses the newly available experimental data on v1 and v2 at finite pseudorapidities from the STAR and PHOBOS collaborations, which had not been reproduced by theoretical models such as hydrodynamic models (Heinz 2004). The results enable future experimental tests of whether the constituent quark number scaling of meson and baryon elliptic flows seen at midrapidity is not valid at large pseudorapidity, and provide predictions for v1, v2, v3, and v4 at forward pseudorapidity for comparison with upcoming RHIC data.
