# Production of open-charm mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a theoretical framework to study open charm production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The coupling strength between the charm quarks and the QGP constituents, quantified by the spatial diffusion coefficient $2πTD_{s}$, is obtained by performing a phenomenological fit analysis to the lattice QCD calculations, resulting in $2πTD_{s}=const.$ (\textbf{Model-A}) and $2πTD_{s}=1.3 + (T/T_{c})^2$ (\textbf{Model-B}). We find that the relative azimuthal distribution of the initially back-to-back generated $c\bar{c}$ pairs presents a broadening behaviour, which is more pronounced for $c\bar{c}$ pairs with small initial $p_{\rm T}$, and when the Model-B approach is adopted. The competition between the initial drag and the subsequent collective effects tends to restrict the time dependence of charm quark $R_{\rm AA}$. Concerning the theoretical uncertainty on final D-meson nuclear modification, the nuclear shadowing and pp baseline components are dominat at high and low $p_{\rm T}$ ($p_{\rm T}\lesssim3~{\rm GeV/{\it c}}$), respectively. The measured D-meson $R_{\rm AA}(p_{\rm T})$ favors Model-A assumption for the diffusion coefficient both at RHIC and LHC, while their $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ prefer Model-B at moderate $p_{\rm T}$. These results confirm the necessity to consider the temperature- and/or momentum-dependence of $2πTD_{s}$ to describe well the D-meson $R_{\rm AA}$ and $v_{\rm 2}$ simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for open charm production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, finding that the measured D-meson nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}(p_{\rm T})\) favors a constant spatial diffusion coefficient (Model-A: \(2\pi TD_{s}=6\)) at both RHIC and LHC, while the elliptic flow \(v_{2}(p_{\rm T})\) prefers a temperature-dependent coefficient (Model-B: \(2\pi TD_{s}=1.3 + (T/T_{c})^2\)) at moderate \(p_{\rm T}\). The authors conclude that no single model simultaneously describes both observables, confirming the necessity of a temperature- and/or momentum-dependent \(2\pi TD_{s}\).

## Method

The authors simulate charm quark propagation through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using a Langevin transport equation that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss, with the spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi TD_{s}\) parameterized either as a constant (Model-A) or as a temperature-dependent function (Model-B) fitted to lattice QCD calculations. The QGP medium evolution is modeled with 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, initial conditions from a Glauber-based model with a longitudinal data-inspired parameterization, and charm quark initial momentum distributions from FONLL calculations with EPS09 nuclear PDF modifications. Hadronization of charm quarks into D mesons is implemented via a dual model including fragmentation and heavy-light coalescence, with the coalescence also incorporating higher hadronic states.

## Key numbers

* Spatial diffusion coefficient for Model-A: \(2\pi TD_{s}=6\) (constant), fitted to lattice QCD data.
* Spatial diffusion coefficient for Model-B: \(2\pi TD_{s}=1.3 + (T/T_{c})^2\), fitted to lattice QCD data.
* Momentum diffusion coefficient \(\kappa/T^{3}\) at \(T/T_{c}=1.5\): 2.09 for Model-A, 3.53 for Model-B (reference range 1.8-3.4 from Lattice QCD (Francis 2015)).
* Jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_{\rm Q}/T^{3}\) at \(T/T_{c}=1.88\): 4.19 for Model-A, 5.20 for Model-B (reference range 3.4-5.8 from JET Collaboration (2014)).
* Jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_{\rm Q}/T^{3}\) at \(T/T_{c}=2.61\): 4.19 for Model-A, 3.11 for Model-B (reference range 2.3-5.1 from JET Collaboration (2014)).
* Charm quark mass used in FONLL calculations: \(m_{c}=1.5\) GeV (varied between 1.3 and 1.7 GeV for uncertainty estimation).
* Initial time for hydrodynamical evolution: \(\tau_{0}=0.6\) fm/\(c\).
* Critical temperature for QGP to hadron gas transition: \(T_{c}=165\) MeV.
* Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(\eta/s=1/(4\pi)\).
* Mean charged particle multiplicity per participant pair in central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV: model gives 3.745, data range 3.64-3.82 (PHOBOS Collaboration).
* Low \(p_{\rm T}\) region where pp baseline uncertainty dominates: \(p_{\rm T}\lesssim 3\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for heavy quark transport in heavy-ion collisions by comparing two phenomenological models for the spatial diffusion coefficient \(2\pi TD_{s}\), fitted to lattice QCD calculations from Banerjee (2012), Kaczmarek (2014), and Ding (2012). The study builds on prior work by Akamatsu (2009), Das (2015), and the JET Collaboration (2014) regarding the temperature dependence of heavy quark diffusion, and addresses the open question raised by Aarts (2017) about propagating theoretical uncertainties from pp baseline calculations and nuclear shadowing parameterizations. The results enable future model-to-data comparisons that can constrain the temperature and momentum dependence of heavy quark transport coefficients, which is necessary to simultaneously describe D-meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_{2}\) measurements at RHIC and LHC.
