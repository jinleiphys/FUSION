# Effect of Longitudinal Fluctuations of $3$D Weizsäcker-Williams Field on Pressure Isotropization of Glasma

> We investigate the effects of boost invariance breaking on the isotropization of pressure in the glasma, using the $3+1$D glasma simulation. The breaking is attributed to spatial fluctuations of the classical color charge density along the collision axis. We present numerical results for pressure and energy density at mid-rapidity and across a wider rapidity region. It is found that, despite varying longitudinal correlation lengths, the behaviors of the pressure isotropizations are qualitatively similar. The numerical results suggest that, in the initial stage, longitudinal color electromagnetic fields develop, similar to those in the boost invariant glasma. Subsequently, these fields evolve into a dilute glasma, expanding longitudinally in a manner akin to a dilute gas. We also show that the energy density at mid-rapidity exhibits a $1/τ$ decay in the dilute glasma stage.

## Key claim

The paper shows that breaking boost invariance through longitudinal fluctuations of the classical color charge density does not qualitatively change the pressure isotropization of the glasma; despite varying longitudinal correlation lengths, the isotropization behavior remains qualitatively similar to the boost-invariant case.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D glasma simulations with spatial fluctuations of the classical color charge density along the collision axis to break boost invariance. They solved the classical Yang-Mills equations on a lattice to compute the time evolution of the energy-momentum tensor, extracting the longitudinal and transverse pressures and energy density at mid-rapidity and across a wider rapidity region for different longitudinal correlation lengths.

## Key numbers

- The energy density at mid-rapidity exhibits a 1/tau decay in the dilute glasma stage (where tau is proper time).
- The pressure isotropization behaviors are qualitatively similar across varying longitudinal correlation lengths, though no specific numerical values for the correlation lengths or isotropization times are provided in the available text.

## Context

This work extends previous boost-invariant glasma simulations (such as those by Lappi and McLerran) by incorporating longitudinal fluctuations of the color charge density, which are expected in realistic heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future studies of how boost-invariance breaking affects subsequent hydrodynamic evolution and observable signatures such as particle spectra and flow coefficients at RHIC and LHC energies.
