# A Modern View of Perturbative QCD and Application to Heavy Quarkonium Systems

> Perturbative QCD has made significant progress over the last few decades. In the first part, we present an introductory overview of perturbative QCD as seen from a modern viewpoint. We explain the relation between purely perturbative predictions and predictions based on Wilsonian effective field theories. We also review progress of modern computational technologies and discuss intersection with frontiers of mathematics. Analyses of singularities in Feynman diagrams play key roles towards developing a unified view. In the second part, we discuss application of perturbative QCD, based on the formulation given in the first part, to heavy quarkonium systems and the interquark force between static color charges. We elucidate impacts on order Lambda_QCD physics in the quark mass and interquark force, which used to be considered inaccessible by perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD predictions can be classified into three distinct types, purely perturbative expansions, Wilsonian effective field theory predictions, and model-assisted predictions, and failing to distinguish them leads to errors; in particular, purely perturbative series contain intrinsic uncertainties of order \((\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/E)^n\) from infrared renormalons, which are replaced by non-perturbative matrix elements in the Wilsonian EFT formulation.

## Method

The paper reviews the modern formulation of perturbative QCD, emphasizing the role of singularities in Feynman amplitudes for decomposing radiative corrections via factorization, operator product expansion (OPE), and Wilsonian effective field theories. It then applies this formulation to heavy quarkonium systems, analyzing the static QCD potential and the interquark force between static color charges, using asymptotic expansion and integration-by-regions techniques to separate short-distance and long-distance contributions.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic QCD scale is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 300\) MeV, which sets the scale where the running coupling becomes large.
- For the \(R\)-ratio, the minimal term of the asymptotic series occurs at order \(n_0 \approx 2\pi/(\beta_0 \alpha_s)\), with an uncertainty of order \((\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/E)^P\) where \(P=4\).
- The one-loop running coupling is given by \(\alpha_s(k) = 2\pi/[\beta_0 \log(k/\Lambda_{\rm QCD})]\), with \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) as the pole position.
- The static QCD potential is analyzed at distances \(r \lesssim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^{-1}\), where perturbative methods are applied.

## Context

This work extends the historical development of perturbative QCD as summarized by Kodaira (2005), covering three decades from qualitative confirmation to precision science with 10% accuracy. It builds on the bubble-chain renormalon analysis of Beneke (1998) and the asymptotic expansion method of Smirnov (2002), and it connects to heavy quarkonium studies reviewed by Brambilla (2004, 2010). The paper enables a unified view of perturbative QCD across subdivided fields and shows that order-\(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) effects in the quark mass and interquark force, previously considered inaccessible to perturbation theory, can be addressed.
