# Continuum Response and Reaction in Neutron-Rich Be Nuclei

> We study E1 resonances, breakup and fusion reactions for weakly bound Be nuclei. The absorbing-boundary condition (ABC) is used to describe both the outgoing and incoming boundary conditions. The neutron continuum plays important roles in response and reaction of neutron drip-line nuclei.

## Key claim

The absorbing-boundary condition (ABC) method provides a practical tool for treating the continuum in both time-dependent and time-independent approaches for neutron-rich Be nuclei, but the method's success depends on correctly describing the continuum wave functions only in the interacting region where potentials are non-zero.

## Method

The authors applied the ABC method to three different calculations: (1) time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a 3D coordinate grid to study giant dipole resonances in 8Be and 14Be, where the ABC was used to simulate outgoing boundary conditions; (2) a three-body quantum-mechanical calculation of elastic breakup cross sections for 11Be on 12C, solving an inhomogeneous equation for the scattered wave; (3) a time-dependent wave-packet calculation for fusion probability of 11Be on 208Pb, where the ABC was set non-zero inside the Coulomb barrier to simulate incoming boundary conditions. The Skyrme energy functional with the SGII parameter set was used for the TDHF calculations, and Woods-Saxon potentials were used for the breakup and fusion calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the TDHF calculation, the model space was a sphere of radius 22 fm, with the absorbing potential iε(r) zero for r < 10 fm and non-zero for r > 10 fm; time evolution was carried out up to T = 30 ħ/MeV.
- For the breakup reaction of 11Be on 12C, the radial region up to 30 fm for R and 50 fm for r was used, with iε(R,r) non-zero in the region 20 fm < R < 30 fm and 25 fm < r < 50 fm; n-10Be relative angular momenta were included up to l = 3.
- The elastic breakup cross section from the ABC calculation was substantially larger than the eikonal approximation at incident energies below 50 MeV/A.
- For the fusion calculation of 11Be on 208Pb, including n-C partial angular momenta only up to l = 2 showed strong fusion enhancement at sub-barrier energies, but enlarging the model space to l ≤ 70 caused this enhancement to disappear and even show suppression.

## Context

This work extends the ABC method, previously applied to electronic continuum in molecules and clusters by Nakatsukasa and Yabana (2001), to nuclear response calculations and reactions of weakly bound Be nuclei. The breakup calculation is compared with the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method from Ueda et al. (2002), and the fusion suppression result is consistent with a similar study for 11Be on 40Ca by Yabana (1997). The method enables treatment of continuum effects in deformed nuclei where constructing Green's functions in multi-dimensional space is difficult, and allows quantum-mechanical treatment of three-body continuum reactions where the eikonal approximation fails.
