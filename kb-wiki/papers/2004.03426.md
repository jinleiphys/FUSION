# Heavy quarkonia in a bulk viscous medium

> We study the properties of heavy quarkonia in a quark-gluon plasma in the presence of bulk viscous effects. Within the hard thermal loop approximation at one-loop, the dielectric permittivity of quark-gluon plasma is computed, where the bulk viscous effect enters through the deformation of the distribution functions of thermal quarks and gluons. Based on the modified dielectric permittivity, we compute the in-medium heavy quark potential, that includes non-pertubative string-like terms as well as the perturbative Coulombic term. We discuss how the bulk viscous effect modifies the real and imaginary parts of the in-medium potential. Several prescriptions are examined as to how to include the string-like non-perturbative potentials. Using the deformed potential, we compute the wave functions, binding energies, and decay widths of heavy quarkonia in a bulk viscous medium, and study their sensitivity to the strength of the bulk viscous effect. An estimate of the melting temperatures is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that bulk viscous corrections to the quark-gluon plasma modify heavy quarkonium properties through two distinct Debye masses (one for retarded/advanced propagators and one for symmetric propagators), and that the imaginary part of the in-medium potential is enhanced at small distances but suppressed at large distances for positive bulk viscous parameter Φ, with the overall effect depending on the sign and magnitude of the bulk viscous correction.

## Method

The authors compute the dielectric permittivity of a quark-gluon plasma within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation at one-loop, where bulk viscous effects enter through a deformation of the thermal distribution functions of quarks and gluons parameterized by a constant Φ and exponent a. Using the modified dielectric permittivity, they derive a complex in-medium heavy quark potential that includes both perturbative Coulombic and non-perturbative string-like terms, then solve the Schrödinger equation to obtain wave functions, binding energies, and decay widths of heavy quarkonia as functions of the bulk viscous parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonia in anisotropic media (Thakur 2013, Dumitru 2009, Strickland 2011) and in the presence of magnetic fields or fluid velocity, specifically addressing bulk viscosity which is expected to be enhanced near a critical point (Kharzeev 2007, Karsch 2007, Moore 2008). The approach builds on the computation of color dielectric permittivity with bulk viscous corrections by Du (2016) and examines multiple prescriptions for incorporating non-perturbative string contributions, including those from Thakur (2013), Guo (2019), and Lafferty (2019). The results enable estimates of melting temperatures for heavy quarkonia in non-equilibrium plasmas relevant to the beam energy scan program at RHIC.
