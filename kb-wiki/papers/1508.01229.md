# Detector resolution correction for width of intermediate states in three particle decays

> We propose a method that allows to take into account detector resolution in the partial wave analysis event-by-event fit as a special case. Implementation of the method is discussed and the applicability of the method is studied for the $J/ψ\to K^{*\pm}K^{\mp} \to K^+K^-π^0$ and $J/ψ\to K_2(1430)^\pm K^{\mp} \to K^+K^-π^0$ decays.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to incorporate detector resolution effects into event-by-event partial wave analysis (PWA) fits, but only under the specific condition that the mass resolution in the studied kinematic channel is much smaller than the width of the intermediate resonance, and that the cross section depends strongly on only a subset of kinematic variables (the invariant masses) while its dependence on the remaining angular variables can be neglected in the convolution.

## Method

The authors approximate the convolution of the differential cross section with the detector response function by a Taylor expansion in the invariant-mass-squared variables, keeping terms up to second order. They calculate the first and second derivatives of the cross section with respect to the invariant masses numerically using a finite-difference scheme, and pre-tabulate the average shifts and covariances of those invariant masses from Monte Carlo simulation of the detector response. The method is applied iteratively: a first fit ignoring resolution, then a second fit using the resolution correction computed from the first-fit solution.

## Key numbers

- For the $K^*(892)$ resonance, the method compensates approximately 80-90% of the bias in the fitted width and Blatt-Weisskopf radius when the ratio $\xi = \text{Res } M_{K\pi} / \Gamma$ is less than 0.2.
- For $K^*(892)$, the method introduces a bias in the fitted mass of about 0.2 MeV for $\xi = 0.1$, nearly independent of the detector response model.
- The numerical study uses generated phase space samples of approximately $0.9 \times 10^6$ events for each set of smearing parameters.
- For the first detector response model, the standard deviations for kaon track helix parameters are $\sigma_\kappa/\kappa = 5\times10^{-3}$, $\sigma_{\tan\lambda} = 5\times10^{-3}$, $\sigma_{\varphi_0} = 2.5\times10^{-3}$; for photons $\sigma_{e^{ph}}/e^{ph} = 2.5\times10^{-3}$, $\sigma_{\theta^{ph}} = 1\times10^{-2}$, $\sigma_{\varphi^{ph}} = 1\times10^{-2}$.
- For the second detector response model, the standard deviations for Cartesian momentum projections are $\sigma_{p^K_x} = \sigma_{p^K_y} = \sigma_{p^K_z} = 3\text{ MeV}$ for kaons and $\sigma_{p^{\pi^0}_x} = \sigma_{p^{\pi^0}_y} = \sigma_{p^{\pi^0}_z} = 5\text{ MeV}$ for pions.

## Context

This work extends the standard maximum-likelihood partial wave analysis framework used in experiments such as BESIII (Ablikim 2009) by providing a practical method to incorporate detector resolution effects that would otherwise be computationally prohibitive to compute via full convolution. The method is demonstrated for the decays $J/\psi \to K^*(892)^\pm K^\mp \to K^+K^-\pi^0$ and $J/\psi \to K_2(1430)^\pm K^\mp \to K^+K^-\pi^0$ using the covariant tensor formalism of Anisovich (2004) and PDG values for resonance parameters (Agashe 2014). The approach enables more accurate extraction of resonance widths and masses from PWA fits in $e^+e^-$ collider experiments, though its applicability degrades significantly when the mass resolution exceeds about 20% of the natural width.
