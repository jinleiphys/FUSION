# Kinetic freeze-out temperature from yields of short-lived resonances

> A method to determine the kinetic freeze-out temperature in heavy-ion collisions from measured yields of short-lived resonances is presented. The resonance production is treated in the framework of thermal model with an evolution between chemical and kinetic freeze-outs. The yields of many short-lived resonances are suppressed at $T = T_{\rm kin} < T_{\rm ch}$. We determine the values of $T_{\rm kin}$ and $T_{\rm ch}$ for various centralities in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV by fitting the abundances of both the stable hadrons and the short-lived resonances such as $ρ^0$ and $ \text{K}^{*0}$, that were measured by the ALICE collaboration. This allows to extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature from the measured hadron and resonance yields alone, independent of assumptions about the flow velocity profile and the freeze-out hypersurface. The extracted $T_{\rm ch}$ values exhibit a moderate multiplicity dependence whereas $T_{\rm kin}$ drops, from $T_{\rm kin} \simeq T_{\rm ch} \simeq 155$ MeV in peripheral collisions to $T_{\rm kin} \simeq 110$ MeV in 0-20% central collisions. Predictions for other short-lived resonances are presented. A potential (non-)observation of a suppressed $f_0(980)$ meson yield will allow to constrain the lifetime of that meson.

## Key claim
The paper presents a method to extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature from measured yields of short-lived resonances alone, independent of assumptions about flow velocity profiles or freeze-out hypersurfaces. The method relies on the assumption that short-lived resonances remain in partial chemical equilibrium with their decay products during the hadronic phase, and that their observed suppression relative to thermal model predictions directly reflects the temperature at which kinetic freeze-out occurs.

## Method
The authors use a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in partial chemical equilibrium (PCE) to describe the evolution of the system between chemical freeze-out at temperature T_ch and kinetic freeze-out at temperature T_kin. They fit the model simultaneously to ALICE data on stable hadron yields (pions, kaons, protons, Lambda, Xi, Omega) and short-lived resonance yields (rho^0, K*0, phi, K0_S) for four centrality bins in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with T_ch, V_ch, and T_kin as free parameters. The final yields of all species are evaluated at T = T_kin, and the fit quality is compared to a single freeze-out scenario where T_kin = T_ch.

## Key numbers
- For 0-20% central collisions: T_ch = 158.3 ± 2.8 MeV, T_kin = 107.1 ± 8.2 MeV, chi^2/dof = 10.5/7
- For 20-40% central collisions: T_ch = 161.7 ± 2.9 MeV, T_kin = 117.3 ± 10.8 MeV, chi^2/dof = 12.8/7
- For 40-60% central collisions: T_ch = 161.8 ± 2.9 MeV, T_kin = 131.2 ± 15.9 MeV, chi^2/dof = 10.6/7
- For 60-80% peripheral collisions: T_ch = 155.5 ± 2.5 MeV, T_kin = 155.5 (+2.5, -24.5) MeV, chi^2/dof = 19.1/7
- The single freeze-out scenario (T_kin = T_ch) gives chi^2/dof = 23.6/8 for 0-20% centrality, showing it cannot simultaneously describe stable hadrons and short-lived resonances in central collisions
- For T_kin = 100 MeV, the f0(980)/pi ratio is predicted to drop by about a factor of three relative to its value at T_ch = 155 MeV

## Context
This work extends the partial chemical equilibrium framework of Bebie (1992) and Hung (1997) to extract kinetic freeze-out temperatures from resonance yields, complementing traditional blast-wave model fits to p_T spectra as done by the ALICE collaboration (Abelev, 2013). The results are in qualitative agreement with blast-wave fits but avoid systematic uncertainties from assumed flow velocity profiles and freeze-out hypersurfaces. The method enables future studies of freeze-out dynamics at lower collision energies and analysis of other probes such as fluctuations and correlations of identified hadron numbers, as previously discussed by Jeon (1999) and Torrieri (2010).
