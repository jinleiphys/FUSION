# Towards Quantum Simulation of Rotating Nuclei using Quantum Variational Algorithms

> Quantum variational algorithms (QVAs) are increasingly potent tools for simulating quantum many-body systems on noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices. This work examines the application of the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) to four progressively complex models based on the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) framework. By incorporating single-particle spacings, pairing correlations, and rotational cranking terms, we evaluate VQE performance against exact diagonalization (ED) benchmarks. Our results demonstrate that while simpler models achieve high precision (errors $<0.005$), the transition to 8-spin-orbital Hamiltonians reveals significant scaling and optimization challenges. Notably, we show that Model IV, which employs a more expressive RealAmplitudes ansatz, successfully captures the qualitative physics of rotational alignment and reduces energy deviations compared to intermediate benchmarks. These results establish a systematic methodological baseline, identifying the breaking points of hardware-efficient ansatz while validating the potential of QVAs to model the complex competition between pairing and rotation in deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

While the abstract states that VQE achieves high precision (errors <0.005) for simpler models, the full results show that for the more complex 8-spin-orbital Hamiltonians (Models III and IV), energy deviations reach approximately 0.8 to 1.0 units, revealing significant scaling and optimization challenges that the abstract's summary glosses over.

## Method

The authors constructed four progressively complex CNS-inspired Hamiltonians incorporating single-particle level spacings, pairing correlations, and rotational cranking terms, then mapped these fermionic Hamiltonians to qubit operators using the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They applied the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) with hardware-efficient ansatze (EfficientSU2, RealAmplitudes, and custom layered ansatze) and the COBYLA optimizer, benchmarking results against exact diagonalization for ground-state energy, angular momentum expectation value ⟨J_x⟩, and bipartite entanglement entropy across cranking frequencies ω from 0.0 to 1.2.

## Key numbers

- Model I: VQE energy error of 6.595 × 10^{-8} relative to exact energy of -1.204987562112089, with entanglement entropy of 1.42 × 10^{-7}
- Model II: VQE ground-state energy of -3.162806 versus exact energy of -3.406542, giving energy difference of 2.437366 × 10^{-1}
- Model III: Energy deviations |ΔE| ranging from 0.645316 to 1.089416 across ω values 0.0-1.2, with VQE failing to reproduce exact ⟨J_x⟩ values
- Model IV: Energy deviations |ΔE| ranging from 0.000751 (at ω=0.6) to 0.159299 (at ω=1.1), with VQE capturing qualitative ⟨J_x⟩ increase from 0.000288 at ω=0.0 to 0.993430 at ω=1.2
- Model IV quantum resources: 8 qubits, 24 variational parameters, circuit depth of 24, 56 CNOT gates, and 13-21 Hamiltonian Pauli terms

## Context

This work extends the application of quantum variational algorithms, previously tested on models such as Fermi-Hubbard, Ising, and Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (Cade 2020, Cervia 2020), to the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky framework for rotating nuclei. It establishes a systematic methodological baseline for NISQ-era nuclear structure simulations, identifying breaking points of hardware-efficient ansatze that inform the development of next-generation symmetry-adapted or adaptive algorithms such as ADAPT-VQE (Grimsley 2018). The framework enables future scaling to larger valence spaces and additional physical effects including collective excitations and nuclear wobbling modes (Marshalek 1979, Prajapati 2024).
