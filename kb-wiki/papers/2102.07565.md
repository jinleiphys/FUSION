# Thermal evolution of relativistic hyperonic compact stars with calibrated equations of state

> A set of unified relativistic mean-field equations of state for hyperonic compact stars recently built in [M. Fortin, Ad. R. Raduta, S. Avancini, and C. Providencia, Phys. Rev. D {\bf 101}, 034017 (2020)] is used to study the thermal evolution of non-magnetized and non-rotating spherically-symmetric isolated and accreting neutron stars under different hypothesis concerning proton $S$-wave superfluidity. These equations of state have been obtained in the following way: the slope of the symmetry energy is in agreement with experimental data; the coupling constants of $Λ$ and $Ξ$-hyperons are determined from experimental hypernuclear data; uncertainties in the nucleon-$Σ$ interaction potential are accounted for; current constraints on the lower bound of the maximum neutron star mass are satisfied. Within the considered set of equations of state, the presence of hyperons is essential for the description of the cooling/heating curves. One of the conclusions we reach is that the criterion of best agreement with observational data leads to different equations of states and proton $S$-wave superfluidity gaps when applied separately for isolated neutron stars and accreting neutron stars in quiescence. This means that at least in one situation the traditional simulation framework that we employ is not complete and/or the equations of state are inappropriate. Another result is that, considering equations of state which do not allow for nucleonic dUrca or allow for it only in very massive NS, the low luminosity of SAX J1808 requires a repulsive $Σ$-hyperon potential in symmetric nuclear matter in the range $U_Σ^{(N)}\approx 10-30$ MeV. This range of values for $U_Σ^{(N)} $ is also supported by the criterion of best agreement with all available data from INS and XRT.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that the criterion of best agreement with observational data leads to different equations of state and proton S-wave superfluidity gaps when applied separately for isolated neutron stars and accreting neutron stars in quiescence, implying that at least in one situation the traditional simulation framework is not complete or the equations of state are inappropriate.

## Method

The authors used a set of unified relativistic mean-field equations of state for hyperonic compact stars, previously calibrated to experimental hypernuclear data and symmetry energy constraints, to simulate the thermal evolution of non-magnetized, non-rotating spherically-symmetric isolated and accreting neutron stars. They employed an upgraded version of the public domain code NSCool to compute cooling/heating curves, accounting for nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes, modified Urca, bremsstrahlung, and Cooper pair breaking and formation, with proton S-wave superfluidity treated under two extreme prescriptions for the pairing gap dependence on Fermi momentum. The simulated tracks were systematically compared with observational data from 19 isolated neutron stars and 24 X-ray transients.

* The slope of the symmetry energy for the selected equations of state is constrained to 40 less than or approximately equal to L less than or approximately equal to 62 MeV (from Lattimer 2013) or 30 less than or approximately equal to L less than or approximately equal to 86 MeV (from Oertel 2017).
* For a 1.8 solar mass neutron star with U_Sigma^(N) = 30 MeV, central baryonic number densities range from 0.34 fm^-3 (NL3omega rho) to 0.54 fm^-3 (DD2).
* The dispersion in radius for the canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star across models is Delta R_1.4 = 0.7 km.
* The low luminosity of SAX J1808 requires a repulsive Sigma-hyperon potential in symmetric nuclear matter in the range U_Sigma^(N) approximately 10-30 MeV.
* The observational data for isolated neutron stars span an age range 0.33 less than or equal to t less than or equal to 1300 kyr and surface effective temperature range 0.42 less than or equal to T_s^infinity less than or equal to 2 MK.
* The observational data for X-ray transients span photon luminosity L_gamma^infinity = 4.9 x 10^30 to 5.3 x 10^33 erg/s and average accretion rate Mdot = 2.5 x 10^-12 to 5.2 x 10^-9 solar masses per year.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperonic compact star cooling by Raduta (2017, 2019), Grigorian (2018), and Negreiros (2018), which focused on isolated neutron stars and did not systematically compare with both isolated and accreting neutron star data simultaneously. It contrasts with earlier claims by Haensel (1994), Schaab (1998), and Tsuruta (2009) that hyperon nucleation in neutron star cores is ruled out by thermal data. The results enable future work to refine the treatment of proton superfluidity and equation of state modeling, particularly to resolve the discrepancy between best-fit models for isolated versus accreting neutron stars.
