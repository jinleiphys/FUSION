# PCAC in Nuclear Medium and the Lovelace-Shapiro-Veneziano formula

> A simple way to enforce the Adler zero condition for pion amplitudes in the nuclear medium is to use the Lovelace quantization condition but with modified Regge Slope $α'$ This latter is related to change in the gluon condensate. Increasing nuclear density leads to a relative increase in the Regge Slope $α'$ Denoting this increase by a scale factor $λ$, the drop in the $ρ$-mass, $Δ-N$ mass difference, increase in $ππ$ scattering length, decrease of pion decay constant etc. are simply related to $λ$.

# PCAC in Nuclear Medium and the Lovelace-Shapiro-Veneziano formula

## Key claim
The Adler zero condition for pion amplitudes in nuclear medium can be satisfied by using the Lovelace quantization condition with a modified Regge slope α', where the modification is related to changes in the gluon condensate; the paper asserts that increasing nuclear density leads to a relative increase in α', but the quantitative estimates at ordinary nuclear densities yield only a modest scaling factor λ ≈ (1.06)^(1/2).

## Method
The author extends the Lovelace-Shapiro-Veneziano dual amplitude for ππ scattering to nuclear medium by replacing the vacuum Regge trajectory α(s) with a medium-modified trajectory α_med(s) = α_0,med + α'_med·s. The modified slope α'_med is related to the gluon condensate in medium via (α'_med/α')² = ⟨0|G²|0⟩/⟨0|G²|0⟩_med = λ², and the Adler zero condition in medium is enforced by requiring α_med(m²_π) = 1/2. Using the linear density approximation, the nucleon matrix element of the gluon condensate from the trace anomaly is combined with the QCD sum rule value of the vacuum gluon condensate to estimate λ.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum Regge slope: α' = 0.881 (GeV)^(-2), determined from ρ(770) mass 768.5 ± 0.6 MeV and ρ₃(1690) mass 1691 ± 5 MeV
- Vacuum intercept: α₀ = 0.479, giving α(m²_π) = 0.497, close to the Lovelace quantization condition value of 1/2
- The constant β determined from ρ width: β ≈ 1.45; from KSRF relation: β ≈ 1.39
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: F_π = 184 MeV; in medium: F*_π = F_π λ^(-1/2)
- S-wave scattering length in medium: a^(0*)_0 = a^0_0 λ
- ρ-mass in medium: (m*²_ρ - m²_π)/(m²_ρ - m²_π) = 1/λ
- Δ-N mass difference in medium: (m*²_Δ - m*²_N)/(m²_Δ - m²_N) = 1/λ
- Gluon condensate in vacuum: ⟨0|G_μνG^μν|0⟩ ≈ 0.5 (GeV)^4
- Nucleon matrix element: ⟨N|G_μνG^μν|N⟩ ≈ -32π² × 78 MeV
- Nuclear density: ρ ≈ 0.16 fm^(-3)
- Scaling factor at ordinary nuclear densities: λ ≈ (1.06)^(1/2)

## Context
This work extends the Lovelace-Shapiro-Veneziano dual resonance model (Lovelace 1968, Shapiro 1969, Veneziano 1968) to nuclear medium by incorporating the Nambu string picture (Nambu 1979) where the Regge slope is related to the gluon condensate. The approach builds on the Ademollo-Veneziano-Weinberg generalization (Ademollo, Veneziano, and Weinberg 1970) of the Lovelace quantization condition and the Johnson-Thorn bag model relation (Johnson and Thorn 1976) for α'. The paper enables predictions for in-medium modifications of hadron properties including ρ-mass dropping, Δ-N mass difference reduction, increased ππ scattering length, and decreased pion decay constant, which can be tested against heavy ion collision data and compared with QCD sum rule approaches such as those by Migdal (1978).
