# Ellipsoids of U(3) model

> The Cartan model of SO(3)/SO(2) matrices is applied to reduce of rotational degrees of freedom on coadjoint orbits of u^*(3) Poisson algebra. The seven--dimensional Poisson algebra u_SO(3) obtained by SO(3) reduction of u^*(3) algebra is found and canonical parametrization of u^*(3) orbits [p_1,p_2,p_3] is studied. The structure of bands formed by so--called families of S and P ellipsoids obtained by searching extremes of many--body SO(3) invariant Hamiltonians is investigated. The reduced four--dimensional system of equations of motion describing the simple schematic Hamiltonian based on the volume conservation is presented. A new set of canonical coordinates regarding the separation of motion for independent modes is found with the help of the Jacobi approach. Bohr Somerfield's quantization of new momentum space is studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Cartan model of SO(3)/SO(2) matrices can be applied to reduce rotational degrees of freedom on coadjoint orbits of the u*(3) Poisson algebra, but this reduction is valid only under the condition that the system is nearly spherically symmetric, specifically when the differences between the weight vector components satisfy p₁-p₂ ≪ p₃ and p₂-p₃ ≪ p₃.

## Method

The author applies the Cartan model of SO(3)/SO(2) matrices to perform an SO(3) reduction of the u*(3) Poisson algebra, obtaining a seven-dimensional Poisson algebra called u_SO(3). A canonical parametrization of u*(3) orbits labeled [p₁,p₂,p₃] is constructed using a new set of canonical coordinates (L, p, φ, γ) that separate the motion into independent modes via the Jacobi approach. The structure of equilibrium figures is investigated by searching for extremes of SO(3)-invariant Hamiltonians, leading to families of S and P ellipsoids, and Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization rules are applied to the new momentum space.

## Key numbers

* The u_SO(3) algebra is seven-dimensional, spanned by (L₃, Q₁, Q₂, Q₃, q₁, q₂, q₃) where Q_i ≡ Q_ii and q₁ ≡ Q_23 cyclically.
* The maximal angular momentum for S_i ellipsoids is L_i,max = p_i> - p_i<, where p_i> and p_i< are the larger and smaller of the two weight components not equal to p_i.
* For the schematic Hamiltonian H_ω,g(L,Q) = E₀ + (1/4) ω(L) Q, the frequency ω(L) ≈ ⟨p⟩⁻² ω_⟨r⟩ L², where ⟨p⟩ = (p₁ p₂ p₃)^(1/3).
* The derivative Ω_i,r(0) = (∂/∂p_i) H_ω,s,r(0) = [(2-r)(2+s)/(2s)] × (ω_⟨r⟩ ⟨p⟩ / p_i), with the physical range of parameters r ≤ 2 and s ≥ 0.
* The nuclear constant ħω ≈ 40 A^(-1/3) MeV, and the Pauli selection rule estimate p_V ≈ (1/6)(3A/2)^(4/3) ħ.

## Context

This work extends the study of coadjoint orbits from the Sp(6,R) group (Cerkasi, Journal of Mathematical Physics) to the simpler U(3) model, applying earlier studies by Rowe, and by Rosensteel and collaborators that employed the Sp(6,R) × SO(N-1) group for constructing curvilinear coordinates on N-body phase space. The S and P ellipsoids generalize the concept introduced by Riemann for classification of equilibrium figures in the Dirichlet-Dedekind-Riemann model (Chandrasekhar). The results provide a preliminary description of equilibrium figures that can be applied to nuclear collective spectra when the system is nearly spherically symmetric.
