# Baryon spin-flavor structure from an analysis of lattice QCD results of the baryon spectrum

> The excited baryon masses are analyzed in the framework of the $1/N_c$ expansion using the available physical masses and also the masses obtained in lattice QCD for different quark masses. The baryon states are organized into irreducible representations of $SU(6)\times O(3)$, where the $[{\bf{56}},\ell^P=0^+]$ ground state and excited baryons, and the $[{\bf{56}},2^+]$ and $[{\bf{70}},1^-]$ excited states are analyzed. The analyses are carried out to order 1/Nc and first order in the quark masses. The issue of state identifications is discussed. Numerous parameter independent mass relations result at those orders, among them the well known Gell-Mann-Okubo and Equal Spacing relations, as well as additional relations involving baryons with different spins. It is observed that such relations are satisfied at the expected level of precision. From the quark mass dependence of the coefficients in the baryon mass formulas an increasingly simpler picture of the spin-flavor composition of the baryons is observed with increasing quark masses, as measured by the number of significant mass operators.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $1/N_c$ expansion, applied to baryon masses from lattice QCD at unphysical quark masses (pion masses 391-702 MeV), provides a consistent description of the spin-flavor structure of baryons, but only under the explicit assumption that configuration mixing between different $SU(6)\times O(3)$ irreducible representations is negligible.

## Method

The authors performed fits of the $1/N_c$ expansion mass formulas, truncated to order $1/N_c$ and first order in quark masses, to baryon masses from lattice QCD calculations by the Jefferson Lab Lattice QCD Collaboration (Edwards 2011, Edwards 2012) for the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$, $[{\bf 56},2^+]$, and $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplets. They used lattice results at three pion masses (391, 524, and 702 MeV) for three flavors and at two pion masses (396 and 524 MeV) for two flavors, and also compared with fits to physical masses from the Particle Data Group (Beringer 2012). State identifications were determined by requiring natural fit coefficients and satisfaction of parameter-independent mass relations.

## Key numbers

* For the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$ multiplet at $M_\pi=391$ MeV, a four-parameter fit gave $\chi^2/N_{DF}=0.1546$ with coefficients $A=1131.32\pm5.144$ MeV, $B=71.628\pm2.950$ MeV, $C=6.155\pm1.914$ MeV, $D=96.766\pm1.814$ MeV.
* For the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$ multiplet at $M_\pi=702$ MeV (exact $SU(3)$ symmetry), the fit gave $\chi^2/N_{DF}=0$ with $A=1423.57\pm1.765$ MeV, $B=0\pm1.214$ MeV, $C=0\pm0.642$ MeV, $D=66.511\pm0.814$ MeV.
* The Gell-Mann-Okubo relation $2(N+\Xi)-(3\Lambda+\Sigma)=0$ for the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$ multiplet was satisfied to $38.34\pm74.53$ MeV at $M_\pi=391$ MeV and to $0\pm17.99$ MeV at $M_\pi=702$ MeV.
* The equal spacing relation $\Sigma-\Delta=\Xi-\Sigma=\Omega-\Xi$ for the $[{\bf 56},0^+]$ decuplet gave values $63.53\pm24.98$ MeV, $54.77\pm18.85$ MeV, and $54.44\pm17.13$ MeV at $M_\pi=391$ MeV, and $0\pm9.01$ MeV, $0\pm9.02$ MeV, and $0\pm9.02$ MeV at $M_\pi=702$ MeV.
* For the $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplet at $M_\pi=702$ MeV, the Gell-Mann-Okubo relations for $S=1/2$, $3/2$, and $5/2$ states were all satisfied to $0$ MeV within errors.

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological $1/N_c$ expansion analyses of excited baryon masses (Goity 1996, Pirjol 1997, Carlson 1998, Schat 2001, Goity 2002, Goity 2003, Pirjol 2003, deUrreta 2013) by applying the same framework to lattice QCD results from Edwards (2011) and Edwards (2012), which provide complete multiplets (unlike the experimentally incomplete $[{\bf 70},1^-]$ multiplet where only 17 of 30 states are known). The analysis enables tests of the $1/N_c$ expansion at unphysical quark masses and shows that the spin-flavor composition becomes simpler (fewer significant mass operators) as quark masses increase, which is relevant for interpreting lattice QCD results and for future studies of baryon structure at heavier quark masses.
