# Zero mode in a strongly coupled quark gluon plasma

> In connection with massless two-flavour QCD, we analyse the chiral symmetry restoring phase transition using three distinct gluon-quark vertices and two different assumptions about the long-range part of the quark-quark interaction. In each case, we solve the gap equation, locate the transition temperature T_c, and use the maximum entropy method to extract the dressed-quark spectral function at T>T_c. Our best estimate for the chiral transition temperature is T_c=(147 +/- 8)MeV; and the deconfinement transition is coincident. For temperatures markedly above T_c, we find a spectral density that is consistent with those produced using a hard thermal loop expansion, exhibiting both a normal and plasmino mode. On a domain T\in[T_c,T_s], with T_s approximately 1.5T_c, however, with each of the six kernels we considered, the spectral function contains a significant additional feature. Namely, it displays a third peak, associated with a zero mode, which is essentially nonperturbative in origin and dominates the spectral function at T=T_c. We suggest that the existence of this mode is a signal for the formation of a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma and that this strongly-interacting state of matter is likely a distinctive feature of the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The authors find that for temperatures between T_c and approximately 1.5 T_c, the dressed-quark spectral function contains a third peak (a zero mode) in addition to the normal and plasmino modes, across all six combinations of vertex Ansätze and interaction models considered; they suggest this zero mode is a signal for the formation of a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma and that this strongly-interacting state is likely a distinctive feature of the QCD phase transition.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger gap equation for massless two-flavor QCD at finite temperature using three different gluon-quark vertices (rainbow, Ball-Chiu, and an anomalous magnetic moment-improved vertex) and two different interaction models (Maris-Tandy and Qin-Chang). They locate the chiral symmetry restoration temperature T_c and use the maximum entropy method to extract the dressed-quark spectral function for temperatures above T_c, examining the spectral features across all six kernel permutations.

## Key numbers

- Best estimate for the chiral transition temperature: T_c = 147 +/- 8 MeV
- Individual T_c values (in GeV): rainbow+MT = 0.135, BC+MT = 0.150, DB+MT = 0.143, rainbow+QC = 0.142, BC+QC = 0.160, DB+QC = 0.149
- The zero mode exists on the temperature domain T in [T_c, T_s] with T_s approximately 1.5 T_c
- At T = 3 T_c, the dispersion relations satisfy omega_+/- (p ~ 0) = m_T +/- 0.33 |p|
- The plasmino minimum occurs at |p|/T approximately 2/3
- The Debye mass used in the longitudinal gluon propagator is m_g^2 = (16/5) T^2
- The chiral-limit in-pion condensate used for parameter fixing is <q qbar>^pi_{1 GeV} = (-0.24 GeV)^3

## Context

This work extends earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Qin 2010, Qin 2013) that found a zero mode using only the simplest rainbow-ladder truncation, by testing more sophisticated gluon-quark vertices (Ball-Chiu and anomalous magnetic moment-improved) and two different interaction models. The results are consistent with lattice-QCD analyses (Bazavov 2011) that give T_c = 154 +/- 9 MeV for two massless flavors, and with lattice studies (Karsch 2009) showing the plasmino branch persists near T_c. The finding that the zero mode appears robustly across all six kernels suggests it is a genuine feature of the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma, and the authors propose that the temperature domain where it exists defines the extent of the sQGP.
