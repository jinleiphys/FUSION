# Pauli principle forbids $Ω_{QQQ}Ω_{QQQ}Ω_{QQQ}$ bound states

> Lattice QCD studies have shown the attractive character of the $^1S_0$ $Ω_{QQQ}Ω_{QQQ}$, $Q=s,c,b$, interaction, predicting deeply bound states as the mass of the heavy quark increases. This has led to the question of the possible existence of bound states of more than two $Ω_{QQQ}$ baryons, in particular $Ω_{QQQ}Ω_{QQQ}Ω_{QQQ}$ bound states. We discuss how these states might not exist in nature in any flavor sector. The reason would be due to the simultaneous action of two consequences of the Pauli principle in the different partial waves: one at the baryon level, affecting the $^1S_0$ partial wave, and the other due to the quark substructure, affecting the $^5S_2$ partial wave.

## Key claim

The authors argue that three-Ω bound states (ΩQQQΩQQQΩQQQ) are unlikely to exist in any flavor sector (s, c, b), despite the attractive two-body interactions predicted by lattice QCD, because two separate Pauli-principle effects, one at the baryon level and one at the quark-substructure level, conspire to produce net repulsion in the three-body system.

## Method

The authors solved the ΩΩΩ three-body problem using the Faddeev method, employing as input the ¹S₀ ΩΩ potentials parametrized from lattice QCD calculations for s, c, and b flavors. For the ⁵S₂ partial wave, which has not been derived from lattice QCD, they used the Born-Oppenheimer approximation within a constituent quark cluster model to compute the interaction, incorporating antisymmetrization at the quark level. They then performed full three-body calculations including both the ¹S₀ and ⁵S₂ partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The ¹S₀ ΩΩ binding energy from lattice QCD (without Coulomb) is B = 1.6(6)(+0.7/-0.6) MeV for ΩΩ (strangeness -3), 5.68(0.77)(+0.46/-1.02) MeV for ΩcccΩccc, and 81(+16/-14) MeV for ΩbbbΩbbb.
- The ¹S₀ ΩcccΩccc scattering length including Coulomb repulsion is a⁰_C = -19(7)(+7/-6) fm.
- The recoupling coefficient between spin-0 Faddeev amplitudes in the Jᴾ = 3/2⁺ three-body state is -1/4, which changes the attractive two-body interaction into repulsive in the three-body system.
- The spin coefficient C(S) for the ⁵S₂ ΩΩ channel equals 1/3, which produces Pauli blocking: the two-baryon normalization at short range behaves as R⁴ instead of a constant, generating a dynamics-independent repulsive hard core.
- A naive spin-independent extrapolation of the ¹S₀ lattice interaction to the ⁵S₂ channel would yield bound states of 250–350 MeV for the b sector, but the full calculation including Pauli blocking finds no bound states.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of ΩΩ dibaryons by the HAL QCD Collaboration (Gongyo 2018), Lyu (2021), and Mathur (2023), which found deeply bound ¹S₀ states for heavy flavors, particularly in the bottom sector. The authors show that the three-body system does not follow the pattern seen in nuclear physics (deuteron → triton → α particle, where binding per nucleon increases) because Pauli effects at both the baryon and quark levels produce repulsion. The analysis uses methods previously applied to tetraquarks (Ader 1982) and other two-baryon systems (Garcilazo 1997, Valcarce 2005), and the results suggest that lattice QCD should directly compute the ⁵S₂ ΩΩ interaction to confirm the predicted Pauli blocking.
