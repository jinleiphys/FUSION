# Fractal structure of near-threshold quarkonium production off cold nuclear matter

> We investigate near-threshold production of quarkonium resonances in cold nuclear matter through a scaling theory with two exponents which are fixed by existing data on near-threshold J/psi production in proton-nucleus collisions. Interestingly, it seems possible to extend one of the multifractal dimensions to the production of other mesons in cold nuclear matter. The scaling theory can be tested and refined in experiments at the upcoming high-intensity FAIR accelerator complex in GSI.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that near-threshold quarkonium production in cold nuclear matter follows a scaling law with two exponents, where the multifractal exponent for nuclear mass number A depends linearly on the scaled energy variable Y, and this scaling may extend to other mesons beyond J/psi, but only for ground-state quarkonia and not for higher resonances.

## Method

The authors performed a global scaling analysis of existing J/psi production cross sections in pp and pA collisions from the lowest energies up to ISR energies, using data from multiple experiments. They constructed a dimensionless scaling function f(A,Y,h) = S_0 sigma, where Y = 0.5 log(S/S_0) is the scaled energy above threshold, and fitted a power-law parametrization f = Y^beta A^{alpha(Y)} f(tilde h) to the data corpus, with the multifractal exponent alpha taken as a linear function of Y. The fit was performed for data with nuclear mass number A > 50, and the pp data were fitted separately to determine the exponent beta.

## Key numbers

- Beta exponent for pp collisions: beta = 3.20 +/- 0.26, with Bf_1/S_0 = 2.0 +/- 0.4 nb (covariance -0.934)
- Multifractal exponent for pA collisions: alpha = (0.76 +/- 0.02) + (0.10 +/- 0.01) Y (covariance -0.984)
- Scaled cross section fit for pA data with A > 50: Bf/S_0 = 3.2 +/- 0.5 nb, beta = 3.0 +/- 0.3 (covariance -0.970)
- Glauber model absorption cross section: sigma_abs = (15 +/- 1) - (5.2 +/- 0.4) Y mb for Y <= 2.5
- Extended alpha for Upsilon production: alpha(Y,h) = (0.64 +/- 0.02) + (0.10 +/- 0.01) Y + (0.012 +/- 0.002) h, where h = M_H/M_p
- E772 measured alpha = 0.962 +/- 0.006 +/- 0.008 for Upsilon at sqrt S = 38.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of near-threshold quarkonium production beyond perturbative QCD approaches (Hard et al., Maltoni et al., Nelson et al.) which are not viable for Y near 1, and provides an alternative to the Glauber eikonal model parametrization (Carlos et al.) which cannot be experimentally distinguished from the scaling form. The scaling exponents define universality classes and can be tested at the upcoming FAIR accelerator complex at GSI, particularly with the SIS-100 accelerator probing Y <= 0.4, and may reveal the crossover between hadronic and quark descriptions of matter.
