# Theoretical predictions on polarization asymmetry for Drell-Yan process with spin-one deuteron and tensor-polarized structure function $b_1$

> We report recent theoretical progress on a polarization asymmetry in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with a polarized-deuteron target and the tensor-polarized structure function $b_1$. Experimental measurements are possible at JLab for $b_1$ and at Fermilab for the Drell-Yan process. First, we show a theoretical estimate for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan asymmetry in the Fermilab-E1039 experiment. We evolved tensor-polarized parton distribution functions, which explain existing HERMES $b_1$ data, at $Q^2=2.5$ GeV$^2$ to the $Q^2$ range of the Fermilab Drell-Yan measurements. Then, we predicted that the asymmetry is of the order of a few percent. The Drell-Yan experiment has an advantage to probe the tensor-polarized antiquark distributions, which were suggested by the HERMES experiment as a finite sum for $b_1$ ($\int dx b_1 (x) \ne 0$). Second, we predicted $b_1$ for the JLab experiment by the standard convolution model of the deuteron. Our theoretical $b_1$ structure function seems to be much different from the HERMES data. Furthermore, a significant distribution exists at very large $x$ ($>1$) beyond the kinematical limit $x_{max}=1$ for the proton. Because the standard deuteron-model estimate is much different from the HERMES data, there could be an interesting development as a new hadron-physics field if future JLab data will be much different from our conventional prediction.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the tensor-polarization asymmetry in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process at Fermilab is of the order of a few percent, but this prediction depends on which set of tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) is used; the set-2 analysis, which allows finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions and provides a better description of existing HERMES data, yields more reliable asymmetries than set-1, which assumes no tensor-polarized antiquarks at the initial scale.

## Method

The authors evolved tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) from the initial scale Q² = 2.5 GeV² (the average scale of the HERMES measurement) to the Q² range of the Fermilab Drell-Yan experiment using DGLAP evolution equations. They then computed the tensor-polarization asymmetry A_Q in the parton model using two parameterizations of the tensor-polarized PDFs (set 1 and set 2) from Kumano (2010). Separately, they calculated the tensor-polarized structure function b₁ for the deuteron using two convolution models: a basic convolution description and a virtual nucleon approximation (VNA), both employing the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function and MSTW2008 leading-order parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The HERMES collaboration measured the integral of b₁ over 0.002 < x < 0.85 as [0.35 ± 0.10(stat) ± 0.18(sys)] × 10⁻² in the range Q² > 1 GeV², and [1.05 ± 0.34(stat) ± 0.35(sys)] × 10⁻² in the whole measured range.
- The predicted Drell-Yan asymmetry A_Q is of the order of a few percent for the Fermilab-E1039 experiment.
- The theoretical b₁ distributions from the convolution models at Q² = 2.5 GeV² are much less than 10⁻³ at x < 0.6, whereas the HERMES data show xb₁ ∼ 1–3 × 10⁻³.
- The theoretical b₁ distributions show significant contributions at x > 1, beyond the kinematical limit x_max = 1 for the proton.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for tensor-polarized structure functions of spin-1 deuterons, following the HERMES measurement of b₁ (Airapetian, 2005) and earlier theoretical work by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1988) and Khan and Hoodbhoy (1991). The authors note that their convolution model results differ significantly from those earlier calculations, particularly in the sign of the S-D interference term and the presence of distributions at x > 1. The predictions are intended to guide upcoming experiments: the JLab E12-13-011 experiment to measure b₁ at medium x (0.3 < x < 0.5) and the Fermilab-E1039 (SpinQuest) Drell-Yan experiment, which can directly probe tensor-polarized antiquark distributions.
