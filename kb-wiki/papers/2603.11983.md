# The Matter Radius of 132Sn and the CREX-PREX Dilemma

> The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy remains a central open problem in nuclear physics. Parity violating electron scattering experiments have provided largely model-independent determinations of the neutron skin thickness of both 48Ca and 208Pb, whose consistent theoretical interpretation remains challenging. A new measurement of the matter radius of the unstable, doubly magic nucleus 132Sn provides an important additional constraint. Using a representative set of covariant energy density functionals spanning a wide range of isovector properties, we show that at least one of these models can simultaneously reproduce the charge and matter radii of132Sn. When interpreted together with the PREX and CREX results, the new measurement--much like CREX--favors a relatively soft symmetry energy. These findings underscore the need for an independent confirmation of the PREX result, such as the anticipated MREX campaign at the MESA facility.

# The Matter Radius of 132Sn and the CREX-PREX Dilemma

## Key claim

The new measurement of the matter radius of 132Sn, when interpreted together with the PREX and CREX results, favors a relatively soft symmetry energy, much like CREX, but the paper shows that at least one covariant energy density functional (FSUGarnet) can simultaneously reproduce the charge and matter radii of 132Sn, contrary to the original experimental claim that no theoretical calculations were consistent with both within errors.

## Method

The author employed three representative covariant energy density functionals (FSUGarnet, RMF022, and FSUGold2) spanning a wide range of isovector properties, with symmetry energy slope parameters L of 50.96, 63.52, and 112.84 MeV respectively. Predictions for charge, matter, neutron, and neutron-skin radii of 132Sn were computed and compared to experimental data from ISOLDE (charge radius) and RIKEN (matter radius). Probability distribution functions were generated from 10,000 configurations sampled according to the covariance matrix obtained from the optimization of each functional, and correlations between neutron skin thicknesses of 208Pb, 132Sn, and 48Ca were analyzed across 13 covariant functionals.

## Key numbers

- Matter radius of 132Sn: R_m = 4.758^{+0.023}_{-0.024} fm (RIKEN measurement)
- Charge radius of 132Sn: R_ch = 4.709(7) fm (ISOLDE measurement)
- FSUGarnet predictions for 132Sn: R_ch = 4.710(4) fm, R_m = 4.767(6) fm, neutron skin = 0.223(12) fm
- RMF022 predictions for 132Sn: R_ch = 4.706(4) fm, R_m = 4.799(7) fm, neutron skin = 0.280(13) fm
- FSUGold2 predictions for 132Sn: R_ch = 4.707(5) fm, R_m = 4.846(13) fm, neutron skin = 0.352(16) fm
- Experimental neutron skin of 132Sn: 0.178(39) fm (derived via error propagation)
- Symmetry energy slope parameters: L = 50.96 MeV (FSUGarnet, softest), 63.52 MeV (RMF022, intermediate), 112.84 MeV (FSUGold2, stiffest)

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to reconcile the PREX measurement on 208Pb (which favors a stiff symmetry energy and thick neutron skin) with the CREX measurement on 48Ca (which favors a softer symmetry energy and thinner skin), a tension that has challenged both density functional theory and ab initio approaches (Hu 2021, Reinhard 2022, Zhang 2022, and many others). The new RIKEN measurement of the 132Sn matter radius (Hijikata 2026) provides a third doubly magic nucleus with large isospin asymmetry that sharpens the constraint on isovector dynamics. The results enable a path forward by identifying that a CREX-like functional (FSUGarnet) can simultaneously satisfy the 132Sn and CREX constraints but fails to reproduce PREX, while a PREX-like functional (FSUGold2) does the opposite, underscoring the need for an independent confirmation of the PREX result such as the anticipated MREX campaign at the MESA facility (Schlimme 2024).
