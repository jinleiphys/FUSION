# Systematic study of bremsstrahlung emission in reactions with light nuclei in cluster models

> A new model of bremsstrahlung emission in the scattering of light nuclei is constructed with main focus on strict cluster formulation of nuclear processes. Analysis is performed in frameworks of the folding approximation of the formalism with participation of $s$-nuclei. Reactions $p$ +$^4$He, $^2$D + $^4$He, $^3$H + $^4$He, $^3$He + $^4$He are included to analysis. Systematic analysis of properties of emission of bremsstrahlung photons in the wide region of kinetic energy of relative motion of two nuclei from 7 to 1000~MeV is performed. Influence of the oscillator length on the calculated spectra of bremsstrahlung emission is analyzed. On the example of $^3$H + $^4$He, dependence of the bremsstrahlung spectra on parameters of nuclear component of interacting potential is established (at first time for the light nuclei). Experimental bremsstrahlung data for the proton-deuteron scattering and proton-$α$-particle scattering are analyzed on the basis of this model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new cluster model of bremsstrahlung emission in light-nucleus scattering using the folding approximation, but the model's validity is explicitly limited to s-nuclei and does not include full antisymmetrization between clusters, meaning it neglects Pauli principle effects that are known to be important at low energies.

## Method

The authors formulate a cluster model based on the folding approximation of the resonating group method, where the inter-cluster potential is derived from a Gaussian nucleon-nucleon interaction folded with shell-model density distributions of s-shell nuclei. They calculate bremsstrahlung cross sections using translation-invariant electromagnetic transition operators projected onto multipole components, and solve the Schrodinger equation for relative motion of two clusters with a local folding potential. The analysis covers four reactions (p + 4He, 2D + 4He, 3H + 4He, 3He + 4He) across a kinetic energy range of 7 to 1000 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic energy range of relative motion between two nuclei: 7 to 1000 MeV
- For the p + 197Au reaction at proton beam energy Ep = 190 MeV, experimental data exist in the photon energy region Egamma = 40 to 170 MeV (used for comparison, not original calculation)
- For alpha-alpha collisions, earlier theoretical data were obtained for initial energy Ei less than or equal to 10 MeV (Ref. 1985NuPhA.443..302B) and later extended to Ei less than or equal to 50 MeV (Ref. 2013JPhCS.436a2030D)
- For p + alpha scattering, bremsstrahlung cross section was calculated for incident proton energy 0 less than or equal to Ep less than or equal to 25 MeV (Ref. 1990PhRvC..42.1895L)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic two-cluster bremsstrahlung studies previously applied to systems such as alpha+alpha (Bayet al. 1985, Dohet-Eraly 2013), alpha+3He (Liu et al. 1990), and alpha+p (Liu et al. 1990, Dohet-Eraly 2014) by constructing a folding model formalism that can be applied to a wider set of reactions and energy ranges. The model enables systematic analysis of how oscillator length and nuclear potential parameters affect bremsstrahlung spectra, and provides a framework for describing experimental data from proton-deuteron and proton-alpha scattering that had not been addressed by earlier cluster approaches.
