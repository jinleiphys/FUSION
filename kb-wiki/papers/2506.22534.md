# Suppression of genuine tripartition in heavy nuclei: A self-consistent perspective

> We investigate the ternary fissions of $^{252}$Cf (spontaneous) and $^{236}$U (neutron-induced) into medium-mass fragments, as reported by the Dubna group, using the Hartree-Fock plus BCS method with the SLy6 Skyrme interaction. Compared to microscopic-macroscopic methods used so far, this self-consistent approach provides a greater flexibility of nuclear shapes. Our working hypothesis is that the shape evolution proceeds while the system is still mononuclear. The results show that a ternary fission valley emerges for intermediate elongations of the middle fragment, only when its mass is strongly constrained. This ternary mode is dynamically suppressed by the competition with the dominant binary decay channel. This suggests that a description based solely on quantum tunneling through the energy barrier is insufficient to evaluate its probability. To quantify this suppression, we apply a simple Langevin-type model of overdamped motion with constant damping and temperature, supplemented by a basic estimate of quantum tunneling where relevant. Under assumptions expected to yield an upper bound, we find the probability of ternary fission per binary decay to be on the order of $10^{-8}-10^{-9}$ for $^{252}$Cf and $10^{-10}-10^{-11}$ for $^{236}$U.

## Key claim

The self-consistent HFBCS calculations show that a ternary fission valley exists only when the mass of the middle fragment is strongly constrained, and that the ternary mode is dynamically suppressed by competition with the dominant binary decay channel, suggesting that a description based solely on quantum tunneling through the energy barrier is insufficient to evaluate its probability.

## Method

The authors used the Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HFBCS) method with the SLy6 Skyrme interaction on a 3D spatial mesh to compute potential energy surfaces for collinear ternary fission configurations of 252Cf and 236U. They imposed two neck constraints and a middle-fragment mass constraint to enforce tripartition, classified configurations tending toward bipartition as "forbidden" using density criteria, and estimated the dynamical suppression of ternary fission using a Langevin-type model of overdamped motion with constant damping and temperature, supplemented by a basic estimate of quantum tunneling where relevant.

## Key numbers

- For 252Cf at neck distance d+4 fm, the relative ternary fission probability is P = 3.4 × 10⁻⁸ at T = 2 MeV, with a traversal time Δt_tr ∼ 8.9 × 10⁻²² s
- For 252Cf at T = 1.5 MeV, the probability drops to P = 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁰
- For 252Cf at neck distance d+2 fm, the dynamical suppression is P_d = 2.1 × 10⁻⁶ with an additional quantum tunneling suppression of P_t ≈ 10⁻³ through a 3.5 MeV barrier
- For 236U at neck distance d+4 fm, the dynamical probability is P_d = 1.7 × 10⁻⁸ with additional quantum suppression of P_t ≈ 10⁻² through a 4.4 MeV barrier
- For 236U at neck distance d+2 fm, P_d = 2.0 × 10⁻⁵ with quantum suppression of P_t ≈ 10⁻⁷ through a 7.0 MeV barrier
- The overall probability of ternary fission per binary decay is on the order of 10⁻⁸–10⁻⁹ for 252Cf and 10⁻¹⁰–10⁻¹¹ for 236U

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic-macroscopic (MM) studies of ternary fission by Manimaran (2011), Tashkhodjaev (2011), Vijayaraghavan (2012, 2014, 2015), Holmvall (2017), and Karpov (2016), who estimated the barrier for true ternary fission of 252Cf to be approximately 7 MeV higher than for binary fission. It also relates to the MM model by Wada (2021) that yielded a probability of 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ for second scission in spontaneous fission of 252Cf but with a smaller fragment mass A=28. The self-consistent approach provides greater flexibility of nuclear shapes than the MM methods used previously, and the results enable future studies of tripartition in superheavy nuclei where static conditions are more favorable but dynamical suppression may remain critical.
