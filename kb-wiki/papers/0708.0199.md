# $ΛNN$ and $ΣNN$ systems at threshold: II. The effect of D waves

> Using the two-body interactions obtained from a chiral constituent quark model we study all $ΛNN$ and $ΣNN$ states with I=0,1,2 and J=1/2,3/2 at threshold, taking into account all three-body configurations with S and D wave components. We constrain further the limits for the $ΛN$ spin-triplet scattering length a_{1/2,1}. Using the hypertriton binding energy we find a narrow interval for the possible values of the $ΛN$ spin-singlet scattering length a_{1/2,0}. We found that the $ΣNN$ system has a quasibound state in the (I,J) = (1,1/2) channel very near threshold with a width of about 2.1 MeV.

# ΛNN and ΣNN systems at threshold: II. The effect of D waves

## Key claim
The inclusion of three-body D-wave components in the Faddeev equations increases attraction in the ΛNN and ΣNN systems, allowing a narrower constraint on the ΛN spin-triplet scattering length (1.41 ≤ a_{1/2,1} ≤ 1.58 fm) than previously found, and confirming a ΣNN quasibound state in the (I,J) = (1,1/2) channel with a width of about 2.1 MeV.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the coupled ΛNN–ΣNN system at threshold using two-body interactions derived from a chiral constituent quark model, including all three-body configurations with S and D wave components. The two-body t-matrices were expanded in Legendre polynomials, and the integral equations were solved using a contour rotation method to handle the three-body singularities of the ΛNN system. The hyperon-nucleon interactions were varied by adjusting the mass of the effective scalar exchange potentials to produce different families of potentials characterized by the ΛN scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- The hypertriton binding energy increases by about 50–60 keV when three-body D waves are included, compared to pure S-wave calculations from Garcilazo et al. (2007).
- The Λd scattering length A_{0,1/2} decreases by about 3–5 fm when D waves are included.
- The (I,J) = (0,3/2) ΛNN channel becomes bound if the ΛN spin-triplet scattering length a_{1/2,1} > 1.58 fm, compared to the previous threshold of 1.68 fm from Garcilazo et al. (2007) with only S waves.
- The allowed range for the ΛN spin-triplet scattering length is 1.41 ≤ a_{1/2,1} ≤ 1.58 fm.
- The allowed range for the ΛN spin-singlet scattering length is 2.33 ≤ a_{1/2,0} ≤ 2.48 fm, based on the hypertriton binding energy B_{0,1/2} = 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV.
- The ΣNN quasibound state in the (I,J) = (1,1/2) channel lies at approximately 2.8 − i 2.1 MeV.
- At p^Σ_LAB = 40 MeV/c, the on-shell transition potential V_{ΣΛ}(³S₁ → ³D₁) = 4.542 × 10⁻² fm², while V_{ΣΛ}(³S₁ → ³S₁) = −1.008 × 10⁻² fm², a factor four smaller.
- The corresponding on-shell transition amplitudes are t_{ΣΛ}(³S₁ → ³D₁) = 8.520 × 10⁻² + i 5.507 × 10⁻² fm² and t_{ΣΛ}(³S₁ → ³S₁) = −1.061 × 10⁻² − i 8.961 × 10⁻³ fm², roughly a factor eight smaller.

## Context
This work extends the previous study by Garcilazo et al. (2007) that considered only three-body S-wave configurations for the same ΛNN and ΣNN systems. The inclusion of D waves is motivated by the known importance of tensor forces in hyperon-nucleon interactions, particularly the ΣN(ℓ=0) → ΛN(ℓ=2) transition which dominates the Σ⁻p → Λn process as first observed by Strassner et al. (1995). The results enable experimental searches for narrow peaks near threshold in strangeness −1 three-baryon systems, as the predicted ΣNN quasibound state width of about 2.1 MeV is narrow enough to be experimentally observable.
