# Quantum Resources and Wigner Symmetry in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering from Effective Field Theory

> We study quantum resources in the spin degrees of freedom, such as entanglement, stabilizer magic, and non-local magic, in low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering through next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory. Treating each nucleon spin as a qubit, we calculate the corresponding resource-generating powers of the scattering operator at generic center-of-mass momentum and scattering angle $Θ$. The analysis retains $S$- and $P$-wave channels generated by two-derivative contact interactions. When the microscopic physics exhibits Wigner's $SU(4)$ spin-flavor symmetry, the neutron-proton amplitude becomes proportional to the spin-space identity operator and therefore generates no new resources after scattering, extending an observation previously made for leading-order $S$-wave scattering. The same-nucleon channel remains resource-generating because constraints from identical particles project out part of the Hilbert space. These results show how enhanced symmetries, partial-wave structure, and resource generation are intertwined in low-energy two-body scattering.

# Quantum Resources and Wigner Symmetry in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering from Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

When Wigner's SU(4) spin-flavor symmetry is exact, the neutron-proton scattering amplitude becomes proportional to the identity gate in spin space even beyond S-wave, meaning the scattering operator generates no new quantum resources (entanglement, magic, or non-local magic) from initially unentangled spin states; however, the same-nucleon channel remains resource-generating because Pauli antisymmetry projects out part of the Hilbert space, preventing the amplitude from collapsing to the identity.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-space transition amplitude for elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering through next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\pi\!\!/}\)), retaining S-wave channels (¹S₀ and ³S₁) with scattering lengths and effective ranges, and the four leading P-wave channels (¹P₁, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂) with scattering volumes. Treating each nucleon spin as a qubit, they calculate the entanglement power, stabilizer magic power, and non-local magic power of the scattering operator by averaging over all initially separable spin states (for entanglement) or all two-qubit stabilizer states (for magic), at generic center-of-mass momentum and scattering angle Θ. The Wigner-symmetric limit is imposed by setting the two-derivative contact couplings such that the effective ranges in the two S-wave channels are equal and all four P-wave scattering volumes are equal.

## Key numbers

- Physical S-wave scattering lengths: a(¹S₀) ≈ -23.7 fm, a(³S₁) ≈ 5.42 fm; effective ranges: r₀(¹S₀) ≈ 2.7 fm, r₀(³S₁) ≈ 1.753 fm (from NijmII analysis)
- Physical P-wave scattering volumes: a(¹P₁) = 2.80 fm³, a(³P₀) = -2.47 fm³, a(³P₁) = 1.53 fm³, a(³P₂) = -0.28 fm³
- Wigner-symmetric benchmark for np: both S-wave channels set to a = 5.42 fm and r₀ = 1.753 fm; all four P-wave scattering volumes set to a^(P) = 0.1975 fm³
- Maximum two-qubit stabilizer Rényi entropy: M₂^max = log(16/7); maximum non-local magic: log(4/3), reached at linear entropy E_lin = 1/2
- For forward scattering (Θ = 0°), the full unitary S operator is used; for nonforward directions, the normalized transition amplitude is used

## Context

This work extends the entanglement suppression analysis of Beane (2018), which connected Wigner SU(4) symmetry to vanishing entanglement power at leading order in S-wave scattering, to include NLO two-derivative operators and P-wave channels. It generalizes the observation of Low (2021) that the S-matrix can be written as a linear combination of identity and SWAP gates, showing that Wigner symmetry forces the full amplitude (including P-waves) to be proportional to the identity gate in spin space for np scattering. The results complement the phenomenological studies of Bai (2023) and Miller (2023), which computed spin entanglement using realistic potentials, by exposing which low-energy coefficients govern resource generation in an EFT framework. The work also extends the magic and non-local magic analyses of Robin (2024, 2025) beyond the S-wave approximation, and enables future studies of how enhanced symmetries, partial-wave structure, and quantum resource generation are intertwined in low-energy nuclear scattering.
