# Composite boson dominance in many-fermion systems

> I recently proposed a method of bosonization based on the use of coherent states of fermion composites, whose validity was restricted to smooth structure functions. In the present paper I remove this limitation and derive results which hold for arbitrary interactions and structure functions. The method respects all symmetries and in particular fermion number conservation. It reproduces exactly the results of the pairing model of atomic nuclei and of the BCS model of superconductivity in the number conserving form of the quasi-chemical equilibrium theory.

## Key claim

The paper removes a previous restriction that the structure functions of condensed composite bosons must be smooth, deriving results that hold for arbitrary fermion-fermion interactions and structure functions, while still respecting fermion number conservation. The method exactly reproduces the pairing model of atomic nuclei and the BCS model of superconductivity in the number-conserving form of quasi-chemical equilibrium theory.

## Method

The author evaluates the fermion partition function in a basis of coherent states of fermion composites (fermion number 2), then derives an effective bosonic action. A subtraction procedure is introduced to perform an expansion in inverse powers of the index of nilpotency Ω (half the dimension of the fermion space) when a condensate exists in a single quantum mode (the s-mode). The effective boson Hamiltonian is obtained by omitting time-derivative terms and replacing the coherent-state variables with canonical boson creation and annihilation operators.

## Key numbers

- The index of nilpotency Ω is defined as the largest integer such that (B̂_J)^Ω ≠ 0, assumed equal to half the dimension of the fermion space for all composites.
- The normalization condition for boson form factors is tr(B†_J B_K) = 2Ω δ_{J,K}.
- The coupling constants g_K scale as Ω^{-1} for infinite systems to yield finite energies.
- The ground state energy E_C scales as Ω, while the operators ℰ_K and 𝒱_K take values of order Ω^0.
- The phonon excitation energies E_K = √(ℰ_K² − (n/Ω 𝒱_K)²) are of order Ω^0.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous bosonization method (Palumbo, 2005) which was restricted to smooth structure functions, removing that limitation to handle arbitrary interactions. The approach provides a number-conserving alternative to the BCS method (which breaks fermion number conservation) and offers a potential justification for the Interacting Boson Model of Arima and Iachello (1975) by showing why only s- and d-bosons dominate. The method enables treatment of finite systems like atomic nuclei and small metallic grains, as well as systems with nonseparable interactions like BEC of fermion gases.
