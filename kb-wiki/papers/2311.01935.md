# Nuclear density dependence of polarization transfer in quasi-elastic ${\rm A}(\vec{e},e' \vec{p})$ reactions

> The ratio of the transverse and longitudinal component of polarization transfer to protons in the quasi-elastic $(\vec{e}, e^{\prime} \vec{p}\,)$ reaction, $P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z$, is sensitive to the proton's electromagnetic form factor ratio, $G_E/G_M$. To explore density-dependent in-medium modifications, a comparison of polarization transfer ratios involving protons from distinct nuclear shells, each with different local nuclear densities, has been proposed. In this study, we present such comparisons between four shells, $1s_{1/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$ in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and $1d_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$ in $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$. In an effort to account for other many-body effects that may differ between shells, we use a state-of-the-art relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) calculation and present the double ratios, $(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm Data}/(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm RDWIA}$ as well as the super ratios, $\left[(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm A}/(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm B}\right]_{\rm Data}/\left[(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm A}/(P^{\prime}_x/P^{\prime}_z)_{\rm B}\right]_{\rm RDWIA}$, for chosen shells A and B, as a function of effective local nuclear densities. We find that double ratios for individual shells show a dependence on the probed effective nuclear densities. Studying the ratios, we observed a systematic variation between pairs of higher- and lower-density shells.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first systematic search for nuclear-density-dependent modifications of the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio in the quasi-elastic A(\(\vec{e}, e' \vec{p}\)) reaction, finding that double ratios and super ratios of polarization transfer components show a systematic variation with effective local nuclear density that is consistent with a reduction of \(G_E/G_M\) for protons in higher-density shells, though the authors cannot fully exclude other density-dependent many-body effects.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed existing polarization transfer data from the A1 Collaboration at MAMI for protons knocked out of the \(1s_{1/2}\) and \(1p_{3/2}\) shells in \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) (at two missing-momentum settings) and the \(1d_{3/2}\) and \(2s_{1/2}\) shells in \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\). They compared the measured ratio \(P'_x/P'_z\) to state-of-the-art relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) calculations using free-proton electromagnetic form factors, computing double ratios \((P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm Data}/(P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm RDWIA}\) for each shell and super ratios \(\left[(P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm A}/(P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm B}\right]_{\rm Data}/\left[(P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm A}/(P'_x/P'_z)_{\rm B}\right]_{\rm RDWIA}\) for pairs of shells, as a function of effective local nuclear density obtained from the RDWIA model.

## Key numbers

*   The double ratio for individual shells shows a linear decrease as a function of effective nuclear density with a slope of \((-0.59 \pm 0.16)\,\mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   At zero effective density (\(\overline{\rho}=0\)), the linear fit to the double ratio has a value of \(1.066 \pm 0.015\), where unity is expected.
*   The beam energy for all measurements was \(600\,\mathrm{MeV}\); the squared four-momentum transfer \(Q^2\) was \(0.40\,(\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) low-\(p_{\rm miss}\) setting, \(0.18\,(\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) high-\(p_{\rm miss}\) setting, and \(0.25\,(\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) for the \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\) setting.
*   The average missing momentum \(\langle p_{\rm miss}\rangle\) for the \(1p_{3/2}\) shell in \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) was \(-82\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\) (low setting) and \(-171\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\) (high setting); for the \(1s_{1/2}\) shell it was \(-60\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\) (low) and \(-161\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\) (high); for the \(1d_{3/2}\) shell in \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}\) it was \(-123\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\); and for the \(2s_{1/2}\) shell it was \(-72\,\mathrm{MeV}/c\).

## Context

This work extends earlier polarization transfer measurements on \(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) at MAMI (Dieterich, 2000) and JLab (Strauch; Paolone) that favored medium modification models, and follows the suggestion by Ron (2012) to use double and super ratios to isolate density-dependent effects. The results are compared with predictions from the chiral quark soliton (CQS) model (Smith, 2004) and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Lu, 1997), finding consistency with a reduction of \(G_E/G_M\) in denser nuclear environments. The study enables future analyses of individual polarization transfer components to disentangle the separate behavior of \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) for bound protons.
