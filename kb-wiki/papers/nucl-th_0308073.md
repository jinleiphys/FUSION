# Electroproduction of hadrons on nuclei at GeV energies

> We investigate coherence length effects and hadron attenuation in lepton scattering off nuclei in the kinematic regime of the HERMES experiment. The elementary electron-nucleon interaction is described within the event generator PYTHIA while a full coupled-channel treatment of the final state interactions is included by means of a BUU transport model. The results of our calculations are in good agreement with the experimentally measured transparency ratio of incoherent rho0 electroproduction off Nitrogen and the multiplicity ratio R_M^h for charged hadrons, pions, kaons, protons and anti-protons in deep inelastic scattering off Nitrogen and Krypton targets.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hadron attenuation in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei can be explained by final-state interactions of hadrons in the nuclear medium, without invoking partonic energy loss or modified fragmentation functions, provided that leading hadrons interact with a reduced effective cross section (0.33 of the full hadronic cross section) during a formation time of at least 0.3 fm/c.

## Method

The authors combine the event generator PYTHIA v6.2 to describe the elementary electron-nucleon interaction with a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model for a full coupled-channel treatment of final-state interactions. Coherence length effects in the entrance channel are accounted for using a vector meson dominance (VMD) approach with a nuclear profile function that modifies the photon state inside the nucleus. The transport model propagates all produced particles (baryonic resonances, mesons) through the nucleus, including binary reactions, decays, and mean-field potentials, going beyond the purely absorptive treatment of Glauber theory.

## Key numbers

- Transparency ratio for incoherent rho0 electroproduction off Nitrogen: the model agrees with HERMES data using total rho0-nucleon cross section sigma_rhoN = 25 mb and elastic part sigma_rho^el = 3 mb.
- For charged hadron multiplicity ratios, the best fit uses leading hadron cross section sigma_lead = 0.33 sigma_h during the formation time.
- Formation times tau_f >= 0.3 fm/c are needed to describe the experimental data, with little sensitivity to higher values.
- For the Kr target, a constant formation time tau_f = 0.5 fm/c and sigma_lead = 0.33 sigma_h describe the multiplicity ratios for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons.
- The kinematic range of the HERMES experiment: photon energy nu = 7-23.4 GeV, Q^2 = 0.5-5 GeV^2.
- For the DIS calculation, kinematic cuts: x > 0.06, Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, y < 0.85, and W > 4 GeV (PYTHIA cut, compared to experimental W > 2 GeV).
- For particle identification: pion and kaon energies 2.5-15 GeV, proton and antiproton energies 4-15 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Falter et al., 2000, 2002, 2003a) that combined quantum mechanical coherence in the entrance channel with a coupled-channel transport model for final-state interactions. It directly addresses the HERMES experiment data on rho0 electroproduction and charged hadron multiplicity ratios. The results contradict the interpretation of Kopeliovich et al. (2001) that color transparency might be visible in the HERMES data, and provide an alternative to models based on partonic energy loss (Wang, 2003; Arleo, 2003) or rescaling of fragmentation functions (Accardi et al., 2002). The extracted formation times are compatible with antiproton attenuation studies in p+A reactions at AGS energies (Cassing et al., 2002). The work enables future experimental studies to distinguish between hadronic and partonic attenuation mechanisms by comparing the target mass scaling predicted here (weaker than A^(2/3)) with that from pQCD models.
