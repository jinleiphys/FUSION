# Neutrino production mechanisms in strongly magnetized quark matter: Current status and open questions

> We review the main neutrino emission mechanisms operating in dense quark matter under strong magnetic fields, with particular emphasis on conditions expected in the interiors of compact stars. We discuss the direct Urca and neutrino synchrotron processes in unpaired quark matter, incorporating the effects of Landau-level quantization. For the direct Urca process, the quantization of the electron energy spectrum plays a critical role, whereas quark quantization can often be neglected at sufficiently high baryon densities. The resulting field-dependent neutrino emissivity is anisotropic and exhibits an oscillatory behavior as a function of magnetic-field strength. We explore the implications of these effects for magnetar cooling and for possible anisotropic neutrino emission that could contribute to pulsar kicks. In addition, we review the $ν\barν$ synchrotron emission process, which, although subdominant, provides valuable insights into the interplay between magnetic fields and weak interactions in dense quark matter. Overall, our analysis highlights the nontrivial influence of strong magnetic fields on neutrino production in magnetized quark cores, with potential consequences for the thermal and dynamical evolution of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how strong magnetic fields (expected in magnetar interiors) modify neutrino emission from dense quark matter, finding that the direct Urca process emissivity becomes anisotropic and oscillatory as a function of field strength, with the electron Landau-level quantization being the critical effect while quark quantization can often be neglected at sufficiently high baryon densities.

## Method

The authors review the direct Urca process (d → u + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ and u + e⁻ → d + νₑ) and the neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron process in unpaired quark matter, incorporating Landau-level quantization of charged particles in a strong magnetic field. They analyze how the quantization of the electron energy spectrum affects the phase space and reaction rates, and compare the relative importance of electron versus quark quantization at different baryon densities. The resulting neutrino emissivity is computed as a function of magnetic field strength, showing oscillatory behavior and angular anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- The direct Urca process emissivity exhibits an oscillatory dependence on magnetic-field strength, with the oscillations arising from the sequential filling of electron Landau levels.
- The neutrino-antineutrino synchrotron process is described as "subdominant" relative to the direct Urca process in the parameter regimes considered.
- The field-dependent neutrino emissivity is anisotropic, with implications for possible pulsar kicks (no specific kick velocity number given).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on neutrino emission in neutron star matter (e.g., the standard direct Urca process in non-magnetized matter) by incorporating the strong magnetic fields characteristic of magnetars (surface fields ~10¹⁴–10¹⁵ G). The analysis builds on prior studies of Landau-level effects in dense matter by various authors, and the oscillatory emissivity pattern connects to earlier findings in electron-capture processes. The results enable more realistic cooling simulations for magnetars and provide a mechanism for anisotropic neutrino emission that could contribute to natal pulsar kicks, though the authors note that the synchrotron process remains subdominant and further work is needed on the interplay between magnetic fields and weak interactions in dense quark matter.
