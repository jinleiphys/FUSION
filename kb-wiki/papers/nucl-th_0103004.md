# Shape coexistence and tilted-axis rotation in neutron-rich hafnium isotopes

> We have performed tilted-axis-cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for a neutron-rich hafnium isotope ($^{182}$Hf) whose proton and neutron numbers are both in the upper shell region. We study whether the shell effects play a role in producing high-$K$ isomers or highly gamma-deformed states at high spin. In particular, the possibility of shape coexistence and the effect of wobbling motion are discussed.

## Key claim

Tilted-axis-cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for the neutron-rich hafnium isotope $^{182}$Hf suggest that shape coexistence among three types of gamma deformation (near-prolate with $\gamma\simeq \pm 10^{\circ}$ and oblate with $\gamma\simeq 60^{\circ}$) can occur at high spin, but the authors note that the mean-field description of high-$K$ states has certain limitations and that the question of whether high-$K$ states should be described as tilted rotation or $z$-axis cranked states requires future quantum mechanical calculations using angular momentum projection.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional tilted-axis-cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for $^{182}$Hf, solving the HFB equations self-consistently with a pairing-plus-Q·Q interaction and a spherical Nilsson model space based on the Kumar-Baranger choice (two major shells) plus extra i$_{13/2}$ proton and j$_{15/2}$ neutron orbits. They carried out both one-dimensional cranking (rotation axis fixed along the $x$-axis) and two-dimensional tilted-axis cranking, varying the tilt angle $\theta$ from $0^{\circ}$ to $90^{\circ}$ in increments of $0.5^{\circ}$ for each integer angular momentum $J$ up to $40\hbar$, using up-cranking and down-cranking procedures to explore multiple solutions.

## Key numbers

- The experimental energy of the tentatively assigned $K^{\pi}=13^+$ isomer is 2.572 MeV relative to the ground state; the calculated energies for $\Psi^{\rm t}(J,\theta=90^{\circ})$ are 2.101 MeV ($J=12\hbar$), 2.255 MeV ($J=13\hbar$), and 2.408 MeV ($J=14\hbar$).
- The calculated deformation for $\Psi^{\rm t}(J=13\hbar,\theta=90^{\circ})$ is $\beta=0.2661$ and $\gamma=0.015^{\circ}$.
- A tilted rotation minimum at $J=13\hbar$ with $\theta\simeq 15^{\circ}$ has an excitation energy of 2.593 MeV, with deformation $\beta=0.2704$ and $\gamma=4.62^{\circ}$.
- Input parameters for $^{182}$Hf: $\Delta^{\rm ini}_{\rm p}=0.725$ MeV, $\Delta^{\rm ini}_{\rm n}=0.625$ MeV, $\gamma^{\rm ini}=0^{\circ}$, $\beta^{\rm ini}=0.270$ or $0.268$.
- The energy difference between states of type (i) (oblate, $\gamma\simeq 60^{\circ}$) and type (ii) (negative gamma, $\gamma\simeq -10^{\circ}$) is roughly constant and small, approximately 500 keV.
- At $J=40\hbar$, the barrier height between minima at $\theta=0^{\circ}$ and $90^{\circ}$ is only about 50 keV.
- The $\theta=90^{\circ}$ minimum starts to appear at $J\simeq 8\hbar$ and becomes the lowest minimum at $J=12\hbar$ and higher spins.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of neutron-rich hafnium isotopes: Hilton and Mang (1979) predicted collective oblate deformation at high spin ($I=26\hbar$) in $^{180}$Hf using cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, while Xu, Walker, and Wyss (2000) used total routhian surface calculations to show non-collective prolate-deformed states becoming yrast for $I \agt 10\hbar$ in $^{182-186}$Hf. The present paper introduces a new HFB solution with near-prolate deformation ($\gamma\simeq -10^{\circ}$) involving j$_{15/2}$ neutron alignment that was not found in the TRS calculations of Xu et al. (2000), and discusses how wobbling motion could couple low-$K$ and high-$K$ states, enabling future studies using angular momentum projection or the generator coordinate method.
