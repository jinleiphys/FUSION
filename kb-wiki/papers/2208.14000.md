# Estimation of compositeness with correction terms

> The compositeness $X$ is defined as the probability to observe the composite structure such as the hadronic molecule component in a bound state. One of the model-independent approaches to calculate $X$ is the weak-binding relation. However, when the scattering length $a_{0}$ is larger than the radius of the bound state $R$, the central value of the compositeness $X$ becomes larger than unity, which cannot be interpreted as a probability. For the systems with $a_{0}>R$, we need to estimate the compositeness with the correction terms. For the reasonable determination of the compositeness, we first present the quantitative estimation of the correction terms. Because the exact value of the compositeness should be contained in its definition domain $0\leq X\leq 1$, we propose the reasonable estimation method with the uncertainty band by excluding the region outside of the definition domain of the compositeness. We finally estimate the compositeness of physical systems, and obtain the result which we can interpret as the fraction of the composite component.

## Key claim

The weak-binding relation for compositeness \(X\) can produce unphysical values \(X>1\) when the scattering length \(a_0\) exceeds the bound-state radius \(R\); the authors propose that including correction terms \(\mathcal{O}(R_{\rm typ}/R)\) and restricting the resulting uncertainty band to the domain \(0 \leq X \leq 1\) yields a physically interpretable probability.

## Method

The authors quantify the correction terms to the weak-binding relation via the dimensionless parameter \(\xi = R_{\rm typ}/R\), where \(R_{\rm typ}\) is the largest among the interaction range and effective-range expansion parameters. They define upper and lower boundaries \(X_u(\xi)\) and \(X_l(\xi)\) from the corrected relation, then truncate these boundaries to the interval \([0,1]\) to produce an uncertainty band \(\bar{X}_l \leq X \leq \bar{X}_u\). This band is applied to eight physical systems using experimental or lattice-QCD values for binding energy, scattering length, effective range, and interaction range.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron: \(B = 2.22\) MeV, \(a_0 = 5.42\) fm, compositeness \(0.74 \leq X \leq 1\)
- \(X(3872)\): \(B = 0.018\) MeV, \(a_0 = 28.5\) fm, compositeness \(0.53 \leq X \leq 1\)
- \(D^{*}_{s0}(2317)\): \(B = 44.8\) MeV, \(a_0 = 1.3\) fm, compositeness \(0.81 \leq X \leq 1\)
- \(D_{s1}(2460)\): \(B = 45.1\) MeV, \(a_0 = 1.1\) fm, compositeness \(0.55 \leq X \leq 1\)
- \(N\Omega\) dibaryon: \(B = 1.54\) MeV, \(a_0 = 5.30\) fm, compositeness \(0.80 \leq X \leq 1\)
- \(\Omega\Omega\) dibaryon: \(B = 1.6\) MeV, \(a_0 = 4.6\) fm, compositeness \(0.79 \leq X \leq 1\)

- \({}^{4}{\rm He}\) dimer: \(B = 1.30\) mK, \(a_0 = 189\) Bohr radii, compositeness \(0.93 \leq X \leq 1\)

## Context

This work extends the weak-binding relation originally formulated by Weinberg (1965) and refined by Kamiya et al. (2016), addressing the known problem that naive application yields \(X>1\) for systems like the deuteron. The method enables model-independent compositeness estimates for exotic hadron candidates such as \(X(3872)\) and \(D^{*}_{s0}(2317)\), as well as for nuclear and atomic systems, by providing uncertainty bands that respect the probabilistic interpretation of \(X\). The approach is connected to recent discussions by Li et al. (2021), Song et al. (2022), and Albaladejo et al. (2022) regarding the role of the effective range in compositeness determination.
