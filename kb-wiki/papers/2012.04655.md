# Dispersive analysis of the Primakoff reaction $γK \to K π$

> We provide a dispersion-theoretical representation of the reaction amplitudes $γK\to K π$ in all charge channels, based on modern pion-kaon $P$-wave phase shift input. Crossed-channel singularities are fixed from phenomenology as far as possible. We demonstrate how the subtraction constants can be matched to a low-energy theorem and radiative couplings of the $K^*(892)$ resonances, thereby providing a model-independent framework for future analyses of high-precision kaon Primakoff data.

## Key claim
The paper provides a dispersion-theoretical representation of the γK → Kπ reaction amplitudes that relates the chiral anomaly prediction at zero energy to the radiative couplings of the K*(892) resonances, but the representation is limited to good approximation up to √s = 1.2 GeV due to the onset of inelastic effects from the K*(1410) and D-wave contributions from the K₂*(1430).

## Method
The authors construct Khuri–Treiman-type equations for all charge channels of γK → Kπ, solving self-consistently for the s- and u-channel single-variable amplitudes while fixing t-channel singularities from phenomenology via vector meson dominance for ω and φ exchange, a dispersive treatment of the ρ(770) with ππ intermediate states, and tensor meson dominance for the a₂(1320). The s- and u-channel P-wave rescattering is implemented using elastic unitarity with Kπ intermediate states, employing pion–kaon P-wave phase shifts from Roy–Steiner analyses by Buettiker (2003) and Pelaez (2016, 2020). The subtraction constants are matched to the low-energy theorem from the chiral anomaly and to the radiative couplings of the K*(892) resonances.

## Key numbers
- The representation is valid up to √s = 1.2 GeV, limited by the K₂*(1430) D-wave (width Γ = 100(2) MeV) and the K*(1410) resonance (width Γ = 232(21) MeV) with large inelastic coupling to Kππ.
- The ω → π⁰γ partial width is 713.2(19.9) keV, giving |d_ω| = 2.32 ± 0.04 GeV⁻¹.
- The ρ⁰ → π⁰γ partial width is 70.1(9.0) keV, giving |d_ρⁿ| = 2.22 ± 0.15 GeV⁻¹.
- The φ → π⁰γ partial width is 5.5 ± 0.2 keV, giving |d̄_φ| = 0.135 ± 0.003 GeV⁻¹.
- The a₂ → K anti-K partial width is 5.2 ± 0.9 MeV, giving |g_T| = 22(2) MeV.
- The a₂ → γπ partial width is 311 ± 32 keV, giving |c_T| = 0.062 ± 0.003 GeV⁻¹.
- The φ → K⁺K⁻ partial width is 2.091 ± 0.022 MeV, giving |g_φᶜ| = 6.33 ± 0.03.
- The ω → K anti-K coupling is |g_ω| = 7.1 ± 0.8, determined from a VMD model fitted to time-like kaon form factor data.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to the anomalous γπ → ππ reaction by Hoferichter (2012, 2017) to the kaon sector, where the chiral anomaly predicts the same low-energy value for π⁰ production as in the pion case. The analysis provides a model-independent framework for analyzing high-precision kaon Primakoff data from the OKA experiment and the future COMPASS++/AMBER upgrade, and the resulting amplitudes can serve as building blocks for Compton scattering on kaons to extract kaon polarizabilities, the main motivation of the COMPASS kaon Primakoff program described by Moinester (2003). Previous theoretical work includes tree-level vector exchange calculations by Vysotsky (2015) and one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations for γK⁻ → K⁻π⁰ by Ebertshaeuser (2001) and Hacker (2008).
