# Modification of tensor force in \textit{p}-shell effective interaction

> In many shell model interactions, the tensor force monopole matrix elements often retain systematic trends originating in the bare tensor force. However, in the present work, we find that Isospin T = 0 tensor force monopole matrix elements of \textit{p}-shell effective interaction CK(8-16) do not share these systematic. We correct these discrepancies by modifying T = 0 tensor force two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) of CK(8-16) by the analytically calculated tensor force TBMEs. With some additional modification of single-particle energies and TBMEs, the revised effective interaction is named as CKN. The effective interaction CKN has been tested for the calculations of \textit{p}-shell nuclei of normal parity states from various physics viewpoints such as excitation spectra, electromagnetic moments, and electromagnetic and \textit{G}amow-\textit{T}eller (\textit{GT}) transitions. The obtained results are found to be satisfactory.

## Key claim

The widely used p-shell effective interaction CK(8-16) has incorrect Isospin T = 0 tensor force monopole matrix elements that do not follow the systematic trends of the bare tensor force (attractive for j_> j'_< configurations and repulsive for j_> j'_> configurations), contrary to what is typically assumed for shell model interactions.

## Method

The authors performed spin-tensor decomposition (STD) on the CK(8-16) interaction to isolate its central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. They identified that the T = 0 tensor force monopole matrix elements showed opposite signs to expectations, so they replaced these matrix elements with analytically calculated tensor force TBMEs using a Yukawa potential with strength parameter V0 fitted to reproduce the USDB interaction's tensor force monopole matrix elements. Additional adjustments were made to single-particle energies (setting ε_{3/2} = 0.736 MeV and ε_{1/2} = 2.23 MeV based on ⁵He data) and a few TBMEs to improve agreement with experimental excitation spectra, yielding the new interaction named CKN.

## Key numbers

- The unperturbed single-particle energy of the 1p_{3/2} orbital in CKN is 0.738 MeV; the 1p_{1/2} orbital is 2.23 MeV.
- The single-particle energy gap ε_{1/2} - ε_{3/2} was increased by 1.35 MeV from the original CK(8-16) interaction.
- The T = 0 tensor force matrix elements V̅_{p3p1}^{T=0} and V̅_{p1p1}^{T=0} were made more attractive by -0.125 MeV and -1.0 MeV, respectively.
- Two-body matrix elements V(3311; 10) and V(3333; 21) were modified by 1.0 MeV and -0.4 MeV, respectively.
- The rms deviation for magnetic dipole moments between theory and experiment is ±0.080 μ_N for CKN versus ±0.168 μ_N for CK(8-16).
- The 0₁⁺ and 2₂⁺ states of ¹²B were overpredicted by CK(8-16) by 1.502 MeV and 1.077 MeV, respectively; these were improved in CKN.
- The 2₁⁺ state of ¹⁰B measured at 3.587 MeV is predicted at 3.665 MeV by CKN.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tensor force systematics previously applied to the USDB interaction by Wang and collaborators and to pf-shell GX-family interactions by Jha et al. (2020). The authors identify that CK(8-16), CKPOT, and PTBME all share the same T = 0 tensor force discrepancies, which they correct using a semi-empirical method similar to that used for pf-shell interactions. The new CKN interaction enables improved shell model calculations for p-shell nuclei of normal parity, and the authors note that ab-initio approaches (Navratil, Barrett, Jurgenson) require three-nucleon forces to achieve comparable descriptions.
