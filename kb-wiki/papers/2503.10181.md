# Bayesian analysis of a (3+1)D hybrid approach with initial conditions from hadronic transport

> This study aims to apply statistical learning, specifically Bayesian inference, to the (3+1)D SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid model using initial conditions generated by the SMASH transport code itself, with the objective of constraining model parameters and gaining deeper insight on the temperature and baryochemical potential dependence of both the shear and the bulk viscosity. This study is performed in the hybrid approach SMASH-vHLLE, composed of the hadronic transport approach SMASH and the (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE. A Bayesian framework is employed, utilizing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling to explore the parameter space. The analysis compares model predictions against experimental observables, including particle yields, momentum and flow coefficients both at midrapidity as well as in forward and backward direction. We find that the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid framework, using hadronic initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at different beam energies, can reproduce a variety of experimental observables at midrapidity and forward/backward rapidities. Notably, the preferred posterior distribution suggests a near-vanishing specific shear viscosity in the high-temperature QGP phase, combined with moderate-to-large bulk viscosity around the phase transition region, although the constraints on baryochemical potential dependence are weak. Our findings reveal that a hadronic initial condition constrains the evolution more strictly at intermediate energies, making parameters such as the hydrodynamic onset time highly sensitive. Intriguingly, the extracted shear viscosity differs substantially from previous Bayesian analyses, motivating further systematic studies with higher-statistics data sets and refined modeling assumptions.

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis of the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid model with hadronic initial conditions finds that the preferred posterior distribution suggests a near-vanishing specific shear viscosity in the high-temperature QGP phase combined with moderate-to-large bulk viscosity around the phase transition region, although the constraints on baryochemical potential dependence are weak and the extracted shear viscosity differs substantially from previous Bayesian analyses.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference analysis using the (3+1)D SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid approach, where initial conditions are generated by the SMASH hadronic transport code itself rather than parametric models. They employed Latin Hypercube Sampling to explore a 14-dimensional parameter space (5 technical parameters, 6 shear viscosity parameters, and 4 bulk viscosity parameters), generated 750 parameter points with 250 events per centrality class at three collision energies (7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV), and used Gaussian Process emulators trained on simulation outputs to approximate the likelihood function. Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling via the Preconditioned Monte Carlo algorithm was then used to estimate posterior distributions by comparing model predictions against 168 experimental datapoints including particle yields, mean transverse momenta, and flow coefficients at midrapidity and forward/backward rapidities.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers three collision energies: 7.7 GeV, 19.6 GeV, and 200 GeV, spanning mean baryochemical potentials from approximately 0 to 400 MeV at freeze-out
* The total dataset comprises 168 experimental datapoints from STAR and PHOBOS collaborations
* The initialization time parameter range is [0.8, 2.5] times the passing time, yielding 0.4 fm - 1.25 fm at 200 GeV and 2.64 fm - 8.25 fm at 7.7 GeV
* The switching energy density parameter range is [0.25, 0.75] GeV/fm³
* The shear viscosity parameter (η/s)min has a prior range of [0.001, 0.35]
* The bulk viscosity peak amplitude ζ0 has a prior range of [0, 0.2]
* The bulk viscosity peak location εζ has a prior range of [0.5, 40] GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of QGP transport coefficients (Bernhard 2019, JETSCAPE 2020, Nijs 2020, Parkkila 2021) by using hadronic transport initial conditions from SMASH rather than parametric initial state models, which reduces the number of initial condition parameters and allows more focus on the functional form of viscosities. The study builds on the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid framework developed by Schafer (2021) and earlier work by Gotz (2022, 2023), and uses a larger parameter space than earlier work by Auvinen (2017) which explored only a five-dimensional space. The results motivate further systematic studies with higher-statistics data sets and refined modeling assumptions, particularly given the substantial difference in extracted shear viscosity compared to previous Bayesian analyses.
