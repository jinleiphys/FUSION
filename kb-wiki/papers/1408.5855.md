# Hoyle band and $α$ condensation in ${^{12}{\rm C}}$

> The excited states in ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ are investigated by using an extended version of the so-called Tohsaki-Horiuchi- Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function, where both the $3α$ condensate and ${^{8}{\rm Be}} + α$ cluster asymptotic configurations are included. A new method is also used to resolve spurious continuum coupling with physical states. We focus on the structures of the "Hoyle band" states, $2_2^+$, and $4_2^+$ states, which are recently observed above the Hoyle state and of the $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states, which are also quite recently identified in experiment. Their resonance parameters and decay properties are reasonably reproduced. All these states have gaslike configurations of the $3α$ clusters with larger root mean square radii than that of the Hoyle state. The Hoyle band is not simply considered to be the ${^{8}{\rm Be}}(0^+) +α$ rotation as suggested by previous cluster model calculations, nor to be a rotation of a rigid-body triangle-shaped object composed of the $3α$ particles. This is mainly due to the specificity of the Hoyle state, which has the $3α$ condensate structure and gives a rise to the $0_3^+$ state with a prominent ${^{8}{\rm Be}}(0^+)+α$ structure as a result of very strong monopole excitation from the Hoyle state.

## Key claim

The Hoyle band states ($0_2^+$, $2_2^+$, $4_2^+$) in ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ are not a simple $\Be(0^+)+\alpha$ rotational band nor a rigid-body triangle-shaped rotation of three $\alpha$ particles, but rather arise from the specific $3\alpha$ condensate nature of the Hoyle state, which produces a higher $0_3^+$ state with prominent $\Be(0^+)+\alpha$ structure via strong monopole excitation.

## Method

The author used an extended Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function that includes both $3\alpha$ condensate and $\Be+\alpha$ cluster asymptotic configurations, with axial symmetric deformation parameters $\vc{B}_1$ and $\vc{B}_2$ controlling the sizes of the $\Be$ core and the remaining $\alpha$ particle center-of-mass motion. A new method removed spurious continuum coupling by diagonalizing the mean square radius operator and imposing a cutoff $R_{\rm cut}=6.0$ fm, then diagonalizing the Hamiltonian with Volkov No.~2 force. Partial $\alpha$-decay widths were calculated using $R$-matrix theory with reduced width amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Hoyle state ($0_2^+$): binding energy $0.235$ MeV above $3\alpha$ threshold, r.m.s. radius $3.73$ fm, calculated total width $7.7\times10^{-7}$ MeV (experimental: $8.5(10)\times10^{-6}$ MeV)
- $2_2^+$ state: binding energy $1.62$ MeV above $3\alpha$ threshold, r.m.s. radius $3.90$ fm, calculated total width $0.93$ MeV (experimental: $1.01(15)$ MeV or $0.800(130)$ MeV)
- $4_2^+$ state: binding energy $3.71$ MeV above $3\alpha$ threshold, r.m.s. radius $4.51$ fm, calculated total width $2.4$ MeV (experimental: $1.7(2)$ MeV)
- $0_3^+$ state: binding energy $2.65$ MeV above $3\alpha$ threshold, r.m.s. radius $4.74$ fm, calculated total width $1.1$ MeV (experimental: $1.45(18)$ MeV)
- $0_4^+$ state: binding energy $3.91$ MeV above $3\alpha$ threshold, r.m.s. radius $4.22$ fm, calculated total width $0.6$ MeV (experimental: $1.42(8)$ MeV)
- $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+) = 295$ $e^2{\rm fm}^4$; $B(E2; 4_2^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+) = 591$ $e^2{\rm fm}^4$
- $M(E0; 0_2^+ \rightarrow 0_3^+) = 34.5$ ${\rm fm}^2$ (much larger than $M(E0; 0_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 6.4$ ${\rm fm}^2$)
- $S$-factor for $[0,0]$ channel: $1.85$ for $0_2^+$, $1.09$ for $0_3^+$, $0.25$ for $0_4^+$

## Context

This work extends the THSR wave function approach previously applied to the Hoyle state (Funaki 2003, 2005) to describe the newly observed $2_2^+$ state (Itoh 2011, Freer 2009, Gai 2012), $4_2^+$ state (Freer 2012), and the $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states (Itoh 2011). It contradicts the rigid $D_{3h}$ symmetry rotation proposed by Iachello and Gai, and the simple $\Be(0^+)+\alpha$ rotation picture from earlier cluster model calculations (Horiuchi, Kamimura, Uegaki, Baye). The results enable a unified understanding where the Hoyle state's $3\alpha$ condensate nature produces a fragmented band structure with the $0_3^+$ state as a higher nodal $\Be(0^+)+\alpha$ excitation, consistent with the $4\alpha$ condensate and $\C(0_2^+)+\alpha$ rotational band discussion in ${^{16}{\rm O}}$ (Funaki 2008, Ohkubo).
