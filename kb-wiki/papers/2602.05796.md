# Violation of the Conformal Limit at Finite Density: Insights from Effective Models and Lattice QCD

> In this work, we discuss recent results obtained with the application of the medium separation scheme (MSS) in different contexts where a clear violation of the conformal limit for the speed of sound at finite density has been observed in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We analyze several scenarios, including QCD at finite isospin density, two-color QCD, and two-flavor color superconductivity. Whenever possible, we compare our findings with lattice QCD (LQCD) results, showing that the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model combined with the MSS provides a consistent description across different regimes of the QCD phase diagram. Our analysis highlights how effective models, when properly regularized, can capture essential nonperturbative features of dense QCD matter, offering complementary insights to lattice simulations.

# Violation of the Conformal Limit at Finite Density: Insights from Effective Models and Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model combined with the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS) reproduces the violation of the conformal limit for the speed of sound at finite density across multiple QCD scenarios (isospin matter, two-color QCD, and two-flavor color superconductivity), but only when ultraviolet divergences are properly separated from medium-dependent contributions; the traditional regularization scheme (TRS) that applies a uniform cutoff to all momentum integrals introduces spurious medium effects that can obscure or alter this violation.

## Method

The authors apply the Medium Separation Scheme (MSS) to the NJL model in three distinct dense QCD systems: matter at finite isospin density, two-color QCD with diquark pairing, and three-color QCD with two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC). The MSS systematically separates divergent vacuum integrals from finite medium-dependent integrals using a vacuum mass scale M0, then regularizes only the vacuum contributions using either a 3D momentum cutoff or Pauli-Villars regularization. The gap equations and thermodynamic potentials are solved self-consistently to obtain the effective quark mass, condensates, pressure, energy density, and speed of sound as functions of chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For isospin matter: the speed of sound peak is located at mu_I/m_pi ~ 1.6 with maximum height c_s^2 ~ 0.56 in lattice QCD results from Brandt et al. (2022); later NPLQCD results from Abbott et al. (2023, 2024) found the peak shifted to mu_I/m_pi ~ 2.5
- The NJL model parameters for isospin matter: pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 139.57 MeV, light quark condensate <bar{u}u>^{1/3} = -250 MeV; vacuum effective quark mass M0 = 288.1 MeV (3D cutoff) and M0 = 236.9 MeV (Pauli-Villars)
- For the 3D cutoff regularization: Lambda_3D = 669.5 MeV, G_S*Lambda^2 = 2.029, m_c = 5.226 MeV; for Pauli-Villars: Lambda_PV = 863.3 MeV, G_S*Lambda^2 = 2.763, m_c = 5.205 MeV
- The 3D cutoff limits: mu_I/m_pi < 9.59; the PV cutoff limits: mu_I/m_pi < 12.37; values above these are extrapolations

## Context

This work extends recent lattice QCD findings by Brandt et al. (2022) and the NPLQCD Collaboration (Abbott et al., 2023, 2024) that observed a peak in the speed of sound exceeding the conformal limit c_s^2 = 1/3 in isospin matter, a result not initially reproduced by effective model approaches such as the linear sigma model with quarks and the NJL model with traditional regularization (Ayala et al., 2023). The study also connects to perturbative QCD analyses of two-color diquark superfluids and 2SC matter by Fukushima et al. (2024), and to density-functional approaches by Ivanytskyi et al. (2022) that recover conformal behavior at asymptotically large chemical potentials. The MSS approach, previously developed by Farias et al. (2005) and applied to various dense systems by Duarte et al. (2018) and Lopes et al. (2025), enables consistent comparison with lattice simulations across different QCD regimes and provides a framework for understanding how regularization choices affect predictions for neutron-star-relevant quantities.
