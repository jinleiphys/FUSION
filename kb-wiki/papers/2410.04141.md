# Kubo formula for spin hydrodynamics: spin chemical potential as leading order in gradient expansion

> We present a first-order dissipative spin hydrodynamic framework, where the spin chemical potential $ω^{μν}$ is treated as the leading term in the hydrodynamic gradient expansion, i.e., $ω^{μν}\sim \mathcal{O}(1)$. We argue that for the consistency of the theoretical framework, the energy-momentum tensor needs to be symmetric at least up to order $\mathcal{O}(\partial)$. We consider the phenomenological form of the spin tensor, where it is anti-symmetric in the last two indices only. A comprehensive analysis of spin hydrodynamics is conducted using both macroscopic entropy current analysis and microscopic Kubo formalism, establishing consistency between the two approaches. A key finding is the entropy production resulting from spin-orbit coupling, which alters the traditional equivalence between the Landau and Eckart fluid frames. Additionally, we identify cross-diffusion effects, where vector dissipative currents are influenced by gradients of both spin chemical potential and chemical potential corresponding to the conserved charge through off-diagonal transport coefficients. Two distinct methods for decomposing the spin tensor are proposed, and their equivalence is demonstrated through Kubo relations.

# Kubo formula for spin hydrodynamics: spin chemical potential as leading order in gradient expansion

## Key claim
The paper argues that when the energy-momentum tensor is symmetric, the spin chemical potential can be treated as leading order in the hydrodynamic gradient expansion (ω^{μν} ∼ O(1)), rather than the O(∂) ordering used in prior frameworks. This ordering is only consistent if the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor vanishes at first order in gradients, which the authors justify by showing that otherwise the entropy current analysis produces a logical contradiction between terms of different gradient order.

## Method
The authors perform a first-order dissipative spin hydrodynamic analysis using both macroscopic entropy current analysis and microscopic Kubo formalism via Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method. They decompose the dissipative parts of the energy-momentum tensor, conserved current, and spin tensor into irreducible tensor components, then derive constitutive relations by imposing the second law of thermodynamics (∂_μ S^μ ≥ 0). The Kubo formulae for the resulting transport coefficients are obtained from linear response theory, and two distinct decomposition schemes for the spin tensor are shown to be equivalent through these Kubo relations.

## Key numbers
- The spin chemical potential is treated as ω^{μν} ∼ O(1), in contrast to the alternative scheme where ω^{μν} ∼ O(∂)
- Four transport coefficients appear in the (h^μ, J^μ) sector: κ_{11}, κ_{12}, κ̃_{11}, and κ̃_{12}, with the Onsager relation giving κ_{12} = κ̃_{12}
- The entropy production inequality requires κ_{11} ≥ 0, κ̃_{11} ≥ 0, and κ_{12}^2 − κ_{11}κ̃_{11} ≤ 0
- Four spin transport coefficients are identified: χ_1 ≥ 0, χ_2 ≥ 0, χ_3 ≥ 0, and χ_4 ≥ 0
- Shear viscosity η ≥ 0 and bulk viscosity ζ ≥ 0 appear as in standard hydrodynamics

## Context
This work extends the spin hydrodynamic frameworks developed by Hattori et al. (2019), Fukushima et al. (2020), Daher et al. (2022), and Biswas et al. (2022, 2023), which treated ω^{μν} ∼ O(∂) and required an antisymmetric energy-momentum tensor. The authors build on the alternative approach of She et al. (2021) where ω^{μν} ∼ O(1) is possible with a symmetric energy-momentum tensor. The key new result is the identification of cross-diffusion effects between spin chemical potential gradients and chemical potential gradients, producing off-diagonal transport coefficients κ_{12} and κ̃_{12} that do not appear in the O(∂) ordering scheme. The work also demonstrates that spin-orbit coupling breaks the traditional equivalence between Landau and Eckart fluid frames, and provides Kubo formulae that can be used for microscopic calculations of these transport coefficients in QCD matter.
