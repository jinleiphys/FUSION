# Precision Dijet Acoplanarity Tomography of the Chromo Structure of Perfect QCD Fluids

> Dijet acoplanarity is dominated by vacuum (Sudakov) pQCD radiation even in Pb+Pb collisions, but future higher precision measurements of the tails of the acoplanarity distributions can help to resolve {\em separately} the medium opacity, $χ=L/λ$, and the color screening scale $μ^2$ from the path averaged BDMS saturation scale $Q_s^2[χ,μ]=\int dL\; \hat{q}(E,T(L))\propto μ^2 L/λ$, that is already well constrained by nuclear modification factor data on $R_{AA}(p_T)$. We compare Gaussian (BDMS) and non-Gaussian (GLV) models of medium broadening of vacuum (Sudakov) induced acoplanarity distributions in A+A. With few percent accuracy on the ratio of A+A to p+p distributions, experiments can easily identify non-Gaussian Landau and Rutherford tails due to multiple collisions. However, we find that sub-percent precision will be required to constrain $χ$ and $μ$ separately from $Q_s$.

## Key claim

Future higher precision measurements of the tails of dijet acoplanarity distributions in Pb+Pb collisions can help resolve separately the medium opacity χ = L/λ and the color screening scale μ² from the path-averaged BDMS saturation scale Qs², which is already well constrained by nuclear modification factor RAA(pT) data. However, sub-percent precision on the ratio of A+A to p+p distributions will be required to constrain χ and μ separately from Qs, while only few-percent precision could distinguish between Gaussian (BDMS) and non-Gaussian (GLV) multiple collision shapes.

## Method

The authors calculate dijet azimuthal acoplanarity distributions by convolving vacuum Sudakov pQCD radiation with medium-induced broadening, using an impact parameter (b) representation. They compare the one-parameter BDMS Gaussian approximation S_BDMS(b;Qs) = b²Qs²/4 with the two-parameter GLV multiple collision approximation S_GLV(b;χ,μ) = χ(μb K₁(μb) - 1), which includes non-Gaussian Landau and Rutherford tails. The vacuum Sudakov factor is evaluated using the formalism of Mueller (2016) and Chen (2016) with jet radius R = 0.4 and α ≈ 0.09, and the medium broadening is computed for a static one-component plasma with Yukawa screening scale μ.

## Key numbers

- For Q = 20 GeV jets, Qs² = ⟨χ μ² log(Q²/μ²)⟩ ∼ 10 GeV², which is much less than Q² = 400 GeV² for realistic nuclei.
- The vacuum+medium acoplanarity intercept at Δφ = π gives R(0) ≈ 0.6 for the ratio of A+A to p+p distributions.
- The GLV Rutherford tail scales as dN/dq² ∼ χ μ²/q⁴ for q ≫ Qs, while the vacuum Sudakov tail scales as ∼ α/q².
- For Qs² = 9.6 GeV² (BDMS) versus Qs² = 16 GeV² (GLV with μ = 0.5 GeV, χ = 10), the intercept at Δφ = π is identical, requiring very high precision to differentiate the models.
- The crossover point q_Ruth ≈ 10 GeV where BDMS and GLV ratio curves switch ordering.
- Variations of μ = 0.25, 0.50, 0.75 GeV with Qs² ≈ 10 or 16 GeV² show that sub-percent precision is needed to resolve χ and μ from Qs².

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Mueller (2016) and Chen (2016) for dijet acoplanarity by incorporating non-Gaussian GLV multiple collision series from Gyulassy (2002) instead of the BDMS Gaussian approximation. It builds on the ebe-vUSPH-BBMG and ebe-VISNU+CUJET3 frameworks (Jaki, 2015; Xu, 2014) that simultaneously account for soft flow and hard jet observables including RAA and v₂(pT) at the (χ²/dof) < 2 level, but have not converged on the fundamental chromo degrees of freedom. The study aims to determine whether dijet acoplanarity shape analysis can experimentally discriminate between weakly coupled wQGP and strongly coupled sQGMP (semi-Quark-Gluon-Monopole-Plasma) models of QCD fluids, particularly to test the sQGMP picture where color magnetic monopole components near Tc lead to enhanced jet transport and near-perfect fluidity.
