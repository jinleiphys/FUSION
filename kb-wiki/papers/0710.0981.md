# Illuminating the 1/x moment of parton distribution functions

> The Weisberger relation, an exact statement of the parton model, elegantly relates a high-energy physics observable, the 1/x moment of parton distribution functions, to a nonperturbative low-energy observable: the dependence of the nucleon mass on the value of the quark mass or its corresponding quark condensate. We show that contemporary fits to nucleon structure functions fail to determine this 1/x moment; however, deeply virtual Compton scattering can be described in terms of a novel F_{1/x}(t) form factor which illuminates this physics. An analysis of exclusive photon-induced processes in terms of the parton-nucleon scattering amplitude with Regge behavior reveals a failure of the high Q^2 factorization of exclusive processes at low t in terms of the Generalized Parton-Distribution Functions which has been widely believed to hold in the past. We emphasize the need for more data for the DVCS process at large t in future or upgraded facilities.

## Key claim

The Weisberger relation, which connects the \(1/x\) moment of parton distribution functions to the quark-mass dependence of the nucleon mass, is formally divergent in standard parton distribution fits and requires regularization; contemporary fits (e.g., GRV98, MRST98) fail to determine this moment reliably, and the widely assumed collinear factorization of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) at high \(Q^2\) breaks down at low momentum transfer \(t\) due to Regge contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the regularized \(1/x\) moment for several standard parton distribution function sets (MRST98 and GRV) using the Brodsky-Close-Gunion subtraction procedure, which removes divergent Regge terms. They analyze DVCS in terms of a parton-nucleon scattering amplitude with Regge behavior, employing a subtracted spectral representation to examine the \(t\)-dependence of the amplitude. They also evaluate the \(F_{1/x}(t)\) form factor in a Gaussian light-front constituent quark model and in a perturbative quark-diquark model.

## Key numbers

- For the MRST98 set (central gluon), the regularized \(1/x\) moment yields \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_u^2} = 3.4\), \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_d^2} = -1.1\), and \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_s^2} = -3.0\) at an input scale of \(1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- For the LO GRV set at \(0.26\ \text{GeV}^2\), the regularized moment gives \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_u^2} = 195\), \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_d^2} = -82\), and \(\frac{\delta M_N^2}{\delta m_s^2} = 0\).
- The gluon contribution to the regularized moment for MRST98 (central gluon) is approximately \(400\).
- The GRV98 valence pdf has a Regge exponent of \(-0.52\) and the light sea pdf an exponent of \(-0.85\), both causing divergence of the unregularized \(1/x\) integral.
- An upgraded Jefferson Laboratory with a \(12\ \text{GeV}\) beam could reach \(s \simeq 40\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(Q^2 \simeq 6\ \text{GeV}^2\), and \(t \simeq -3\ \text{GeV}^2\) for DVCS measurements.

## Context

This work extends the Weisberger relation (Weisberger, 1972) and the Brodsky-Close-Gunion regularization (Brodsky, Close, and Gunion, 1973) to modern parton distribution function fits, showing that current sets (GRV98 by Gluck et al., 1998; MRST98 by Martin et al., 1998) give widely varying results. It challenges the collinear factorization theorem for DVCS (Collins, 1998) by demonstrating that Regge poles at the GPD break-points cause a failure of factorization at low \(t\). The proposed \(F_{1/x}(t)\) form factor, accessible via DVCS in the Regge regime, enables a new determination of the \(1/x\) moment and the nucleon sigma term, independent of chiral perturbation theory, and motivates future measurements at upgraded facilities like Jefferson Laboratory.
