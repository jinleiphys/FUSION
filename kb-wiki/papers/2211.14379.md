# Opacity dependence of transverse flow, pre-equilibrium and applicability of hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We evaluate the full opacity dependence of collective flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions within a microscopic kinetic description based on the Boltzmann equation in the conformal relaxation time approximation. By comparing kinetic theory calculations to hydrodynamic and hybrid simulations for an average initial state, we point out shortcomings and inaccuracies of hydrodynamic models and present modified simulation setups to improve them.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that purely hydrodynamic simulations disagree with kinetic theory results for transverse flow observables even at very large opacities, due to differences in pre-equilibrium dynamics; however, a scaling scheme for the initial condition of hydrodynamics that accounts for pre-equilibrium effects can bring the two descriptions into agreement for opacities corresponding to \(4\pi\eta/s \lesssim 3\).

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the conformal relaxation time approximation (RTA) using a relativistic lattice Boltzmann method, and compare these kinetic theory results to ideal and second-order viscous hydrodynamic simulations (using the vHLLE code) for an average initial state corresponding to 30-40% most central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\ \text{TeV}\). They also employ an opacity expansion scheme linear in the collision rate, a hybrid scheme switching from kinetic theory to hydrodynamics, and the KøMPøST linear response framework modified with RTA Green's functions, to assess the opacity dependence of transverse flow and pre-equilibrium effects.

## Key numbers

- The initial condition has total transverse energy per rapidity \(\d E_\perp^0 / \d\eta = 1280\ \text{GeV}\) and effective radius \(R = 2.78\ \text{fm}\), with eccentricities \(\epsilon_2 = 0.416\), \(\epsilon_4 = 0.210\), \(\epsilon_6 = 0.0895\).
- The opacity parameter \(\hat{\gamma}\) is related to shear viscosity by \(\hat{\gamma} \times 4\pi\eta/s = 11.3\) for this initial condition.
- The scaling scheme brings agreement between hydrodynamics and kinetic theory for opacities where \(4\pi\eta/s \lesssim 3\).
- Final state observables are evaluated at \(\tau / R = 4\).
- The simulation domain for hydrodynamics is \([-8R,8R] \times [-8R, 8R]\) with \(401 \times 401\) grid points, and the time step uses \((\delta \tau / \tau)_{\rm M} = 0.01\) and \((\delta \tau / R)_{\rm M} = 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ambrus 2021) which found discrepancies between hydrodynamics and kinetic theory even at large opacities due to pre-equilibrium dynamics, and provides a quantitative framework to correct these discrepancies. It enables improved hybrid simulation setups that combine kinetic theory (or KøMPøST) with viscous hydrodynamics, and establishes a criterion for the applicability of hydrodynamics based on the system's equilibration time, relevant for small-system collisions where hydrodynamic descriptions are questionable.
