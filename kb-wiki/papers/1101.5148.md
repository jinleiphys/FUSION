# Subleading effects in QCD global fits

> I discuss several corrections to leading twist calculations of nucleon structure functions which are needed to include experimental data at large parton fractional momentum x and at low scales Q^2 in global fits of parton distribution functions. In particular I discuss the results of the CTEQ6X global fit, and some work in progress. Topics covered include the interplay of target mass and higher-twist corrections, the importance of nuclear corrections for deuterium target data, and applications to the study of quark-hadron duality. Implications for collider physics are highlighted.

## Key claim

The CTEQ6X global fit demonstrates that parton distribution functions (PDFs) at large parton fractional momentum x can be reliably extracted from deep inelastic scattering data at low Q^2 only if target mass corrections and higher-twist corrections are included simultaneously, but the resulting PDFs are nearly independent of the specific target mass correction prescription used, with modeling uncertainties absorbed into the phenomenological higher-twist term.

## Method

The CTEQ6X global fit was performed at next-to-leading order using a wide variety of data similar to that used in the CTEQ6M1 PDF determination, excluding neutrino data but adding E-866 dimuon data, CDF gamma+jet production data, CDF W lepton asymmetry data, and D0 W asymmetry data. Target mass corrections were implemented using three different prescriptions (Operator Product Expansion, xi-scaling, and Collinear Factorization), while higher-twist corrections were parameterized phenomenologically as a multiplicative factor F2_data = F2_TMC (1 + C(x)/Q^2) with C(x) = a x^b (1 + cx). Nuclear corrections for deuterium target data were implemented using the Paris deuteron wave function with on-shell nucleon structure functions or off-shell corrections from the MST model, and the DIS kinematic cuts were progressively relaxed from W > 3.5 GeV and Q > 2 GeV to W > 1.73 GeV and Q > 1.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- PDF uncertainty reduction of 10-20% for x less than approximately 0.6, and up to 40-60% at larger x, when using enlarged kinematic cuts (W > 1.73 GeV, Q > 1.3 GeV) compared to standard cuts
- Deuteron correction factor F2_D/(F2_p + F2_n) shows remarkable Q^2 dependence at Q^2 less than approximately 20 GeV^2
- Nuclear corrections to deuteron data become large starting at x greater than approximately 0.45, well inside the region included in standard kinematic cuts
- Quark-hadron duality holds within 5-10% when comparing Jefferson Lab resonance region data to CTEQ6X calculations with a rapidity separation cut of Delta y > 4
- Nuclear uncertainties on parton luminosities at the LHC (sqrt(s) = 7 TeV) grow quickly above 5-10% as sqrt(hat_s) exceeds 1 TeV

## Context

This work extends the CTEQ6X global fit published in Accardi (2009) by incorporating target mass corrections, higher-twist corrections, and nuclear corrections for deuterium target data into a consistent framework for PDF extraction at large x. The analysis builds on prior work by Owens (2010), Guffanti (2010), Radescu (2010), and Holt (2010) on high-x PDFs, and on the Georgi-Politzer formalism for target mass corrections, while also addressing the Berger criterion for applicability of perturbative QCD in the resonance region. The results enable more precise computations of QCD background processes for new physics searches at hadron colliders and systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments, while highlighting the need for better theoretical understanding of nuclear corrections and new data from parity-violating deep inelastic scattering or neutrino deep inelastic scattering on hydrogen targets.
