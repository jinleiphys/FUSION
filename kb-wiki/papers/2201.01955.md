# Proto-neutron star evolution with improved charged-current neutrino-nucleon interactions

> We perform simulations of the Kelvin-Helmholtz cooling phase of proto-neutron stars with a new numerical code in spherical symmetry and using the quasi-static approximation. We use for the first time the full set of charged-current neutrino-nucleon reactions, including neutron decay and modified Urca processes, together with the energy-dependent numerical representation for the inclusion of nuclear correlations with random-phase approximation. Moreover, convective motions are taken into account within the mixing-length theory. As we vary the assumptions for computing neutrino-nucleon reaction rates, we show that the dominant effect on the cooling timescale, neutrino signal and composition of the neutrino-driven wind comes from the inclusion of convective motion. Computation of nuclear correlations within the random phase approximation, as compared to mean field approach, has a relatively small impact.

## Key claim

The dominant effect on the cooling timescale, neutrino signal, and composition of the neutrino-driven wind during proto-neutron star evolution comes from the inclusion of convective motion via mixing-length theory, while the computation of nuclear correlations within the random phase approximation (compared to mean field approach) has a relatively small impact.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of the Kelvin-Helmholtz cooling phase of proto-neutron stars using a new numerical code in spherical symmetry with a quasi-static approximation, employing a Lagrangian grid and stationary neutrino transport via the Fast Multigroup Transport scheme. They implemented for the first time the full set of charged-current neutrino-nucleon reactions including neutron decay and modified Urca processes, with an energy-dependent numerical representation for nuclear correlations using random-phase approximation, and included convective motions via mixing-length theory.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass of the proto-neutron star: \(M_B = 1.6 M_\odot\), obtained from the progenitor model s15 of Woosley (2002) at about 500 ms after bounce
- Surface pressure boundary condition: \(P_s = \SI{e-6}{\MeV\per\fm\cubed}\)
- Mixing length coefficient \(\xi = 1\) used as standard value, with variation by \(\pm 20\%\) not changing results within overall model accuracy
- At baryon number density \(n_B = 0.1 \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and temperatures of 2 MeV and 0.3 MeV, the elastic approximation for charged-current rates yields \(\beta\)-equilibrium at lower electron fraction values compared to full phase space integration

## Context

This work extends previous quasi-static proto-neutron star evolution models by Burrows (1986), Keil (1995), Sumiyoshi (1995), Pons (1999), and Roberts (2012) by incorporating for the first time the full set of charged-current reactions with energy-dependent RPA correlations. The study builds on the opacity tables developed by Oertel (2020) and the convection treatment of Roberts (2012) and Mirizzi (2016), and enables more accurate predictions of the neutrino signal from the next galactic supernova for detectors with improved sensitivity.
