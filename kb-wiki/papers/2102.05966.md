# Effective field theory for dilute Fermi systems at fourth order

> We discuss high-order calculations in perturbative effective field theory for fermions at low energy scales. The Fermi-momentum or $k_{\rm F} a_s$ expansion for the ground-state energy of the dilute Fermi gas is calculated to fourth order, both in cutoff regularization and in dimensional regularization. For the case of spin one-half fermions we find from a Bayesian analysis that the expansion is well-converged at this order for ${| k_{\rm F} a_s | \lesssim 0.5}$. Further, we show that Pad{é}-Borel resummations can improve the convergence for ${| k_{\rm F} a_s | \lesssim 1}$. Our results provide important constraints for nonperturbative calculations of ultracold atoms and dilute neutron matter.

## Key claim

The perturbative effective field theory (EFT) expansion for the ground-state energy of the dilute Fermi gas is calculated to fourth order, and a Bayesian analysis shows the expansion is well-converged for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 0.5\), while Pade-Borel resummations can improve convergence for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 1\). The abstract’s claim of “well-converged” is qualified by the specific bound \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 0.5\) from the Bayesian analysis, and the improvement via resummation is limited to the larger range up to \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 1\).

## Method

The authors computed the Fermi-momentum (or \(k_{\rm F} a_s\)) expansion for the ground-state energy of the dilute Fermi gas to fourth order in perturbative EFT, using both cutoff regularization and dimensional regularization. For spin one-half fermions (\(g=2\)), they evaluated 33 fourth-order Hugenholtz diagrams (ladder, ring, two-particle irreducible, and two-particle reducible types), handling UV power divergences via two-body counterterm subtractions and logarithmic UV divergences that cancel for \(g=2\). For spin multiplicities \(g>2\), they included the leading three-body contact interaction (\(D_0\)) to renormalize logarithmic divergences, and they studied the convergence using Bayesian methods and Pade-Borel resummations.

## Key numbers

* The perturbative EFT expansion for the ground-state energy of spin one-half fermions is well-converged at fourth order for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 0.5\), based on a Bayesian analysis.
* Pade-Borel resummations improve the convergence of the expansion for \(|k_{\rm F} a_s| \lesssim 1\).
* The Bertsch parameter \(\xi\) for the unitary Fermi gas (infinite scattering length) from experiments with ultracold atoms is \(\xi \approx 0.376(4)\).
* The resummation of particle-particle ladders gives \(\xi_{\rm pp} \approx 0.237\), and resumming also hole-hole and mixed ladders gives \(\xi_{\rm ladders} \approx 0.5076\).
* Quantum Monte Carlo computations give \(\xi_{\rm QMC} = 0.372(5)\).
* The Luttinger-Ward type approach with resummed ladders gives \(\xi_{\rm LW} \approx 0.36\).
* The epsilon-expansion method gives \(\xi_{\epsilon} \approx 0.475\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative EFT calculations for dilute Fermi gases to fourth order, following up on the authors’ earlier Letter (Wellenhofer, Drischler, Schwenk, 2021) where first fourth-order results were presented. It provides analytic benchmarks that constrain nonperturbative quantum Monte Carlo calculations (e.g., Gandolfi, 2015) and experiments with ultracold atoms (e.g., Ku, 2012), and it enables improved constraints for dilute neutron matter relevant to neutron-star crusts. The results also connect to earlier work on the Fermi-momentum expansion by Lenz (1954), Efimov (1965), and Baker (1999), and to resummation studies for the unitary Fermi gas.
