# Study of the timelike electromagnetic form factors of the $Λ_c$

> In this paper, the reaction of electron-positron annihilation into $Λ_c^+\barΛ_c^-$ is investigated. The $Λ_c^+\barΛ_c^-$ scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The contact, annihilation, and two pseudoscalar-exchange potentials are taken into account in the spirit of the chiral effective field theory. The amplitudes of $e^+e^-\to Λ_c^+\barΛ_c^-$ are constructed by the distorted wave Born approximation method, with the final state interactions of the $Λ_c^+\barΛ_c^-$ re-scattering implemented. By fitting to the experimental data, the unknown couplings are fixed, and high-quality solutions are obtained. With these amplitudes, the individual electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region, $G_E^{Λ_c}$, $G_M^{Λ_c}$, and their ratio, $G_E^{Λ_c}/G_M^{Λ_c}$, are extracted. Both modulus and phases are predicted. These individual electromagnetic form factors reveal new insights into the properties of the $Λ_c$. The separated contributions of the Born term, contact, annihilation, as well as the two pseudoscalar exchange potentials to the electromagnetic form factors are isolated. It is found that the Born term dominates the whole energy region. The contact term plays a crucial role in the enhancement near the threshold, and the annihilation term is essential in generating the fluctuation of the electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the individual timelike electromagnetic form factors of the Lambda_c baryon (G_E, G_M, and their ratio) can be extracted from e+e- annihilation data by including final-state interactions via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and distorted wave Born approximation, with the caveat that the chiral effective field theory framework works well only up to about 4.63 GeV (p=362 MeV) and that the Born term dominates the whole energy region while the contact term is crucial for the threshold enhancement.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for Lambda_c+ anti-Lambda_c- scattering in the 3S1-3D1 coupled partial waves, using potentials from chiral effective field theory including contact, annihilation, and two pseudoscalar-exchange (kaon-loop football diagrams) terms. They then constructed the e+e- to Lambda_c+ anti-Lambda_c- production amplitudes via the distorted wave Born approximation method, fitting the unknown low-energy constants to BESIII cross-section and form factor data using MINUIT, with a regulator cutoff varied between 450 and 650 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The optimal solution uses a regulator cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV, yielding chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.364 at NLO (compared to 2.072 at LO)
- The mass difference between Sigma_c and Lambda_c is Delta M = 167 MeV, and between Xi_c and Lambda_c is Delta M' = 181 MeV
- The pion decay constant used is F_pi = 0.922 GeV
- The breakdown scale for uncertainty estimation is Lambda_b = 900 MeV
- The ratio |G_E/G_M| increases from 1 to approximately 1.3 in the energy region from threshold to 4.61 GeV
- The fine-structure constant is alpha = 1/137.036

## Context

This work extends the two-step procedure (Lippmann-Schwinger equation plus distorted wave Born approximation) previously applied to nucleon-antinucleon systems by Haidenbauer (2014) and to Lambda anti-Lambda, Sigma anti-Sigma, Xi anti-Xi systems by Haidenbauer (2020), now applied to the charmed baryon Lambda_c. The analysis follows the formalism developed by Dai (2017) for Lambda_c anti-Lambda_c scattering and uses chiral effective field theory with SU(3) flavor symmetry as formulated by Yan (1992). The results enable future experimental measurements of the individual electric and magnetic form factors and their phases, which could reveal polarized charge and magnetic moment distributions in the timelike region as proposed by Yang (2022).
