# Thermal photons as a sensitive probe of $α$-cluster in C+Au collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Different orientations of $α$-clustered carbon nuclei colliding with heavy ions can result in a large variation in the value of anisotropic flow. Thus, photon flow observables from clustered ${\rm^{12}C}$ and ${\rm^{197}Au}$ collisions could be a potential probe to study the `direct photon puzzle'. We calculate the transverse momentum spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_n$) of thermal photons from collisions of triangular $α$-clustered carbon and gold at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV at RHIC using a hydrodynamic model framework and compare the results with those obtained from unclustered carbon and gold collisions. The slope of the thermal photon spectra is found to vary moderately for different orientations of collisions. However, we find that the elliptic ($v_2$) and triangular flow ($v_3$) coefficients of direct photons for specific configurations are significantly larger and predominantly formed by the QGP radiation. A strong anti-correlation between initial spatial ellipticity and triangularity is observed in an event-by-event framework of $α$-clustered ${\rm C+Au}$ collisions. These special features provide us an opportunity to detect the exotic nature of cluster structure inside carbon nucleus using the photon probe in the future experiments.

## Key claim

The anisotropic flow coefficients ($v_2$ and $v_3$) of thermal photons from triangular $\alpha$-clustered carbon colliding with gold at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV are significantly larger than those from unclustered carbon collisions, and this enhancement is predominantly formed by QGP radiation; however, the slope of the thermal photon spectra varies only moderately for different orientations of the clustered carbon.

## Method

The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic model with event-averaged smooth initial entropy density distributions from a Monte Carlo Glauber framework. They placed three $\alpha$ clusters on the vertices of an equilateral triangle (with cluster radius $r_\alpha = 0.96$ fm and side length $l = 3.05$ fm) to model clustered carbon, and used a two-parameter Wood-Saxon distribution for unclustered carbon and gold. Thermal photon production was calculated by integrating next-to-leading order QGP photon rates and hadronic matter photon rates over the space-time evolution, with initial thermalization time $\tau_0 = 0.17$ fm/$c$ and freeze-out temperature $T_f = 160$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial average temperature for clustered carbon at orientation $\theta=\pi/2$: $\sim 440$ MeV; for unclustered carbon: $\sim 407$ MeV.
- Triangular eccentricity ($\varepsilon_3$) of initial entropy density: $0.43$ for $\theta=0$, $0.34$ for $\theta=\pi/4$.
- Elliptic eccentricity ($\varepsilon_2$) of initial entropy density: $0.20$ for $\theta=\pi/4$, $0.54$ for $\theta=\pi/2$; vanishingly small for $\theta=0$ and unclustered case.
- Root mean square radius of nuclear distribution for both clustered and unclustered carbon: $\sim 2.26$ fm.
- Binding energy of triangular $\alpha$-clustered carbon: $7.17$ MeV/nucleon.
- Normalized symmetric cumulants coefficient NSC(2,3) for clustered carbon is three times larger than for unclustered carbon.
- Direct photon $v_3$ and $v_2$ at $p_T = 2$ GeV are about $70\%$ of their respective maximum thermal photon values.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using anisotropic flow to probe nuclear geometry from nucleon-level studies (e.g., PHENIX (2018) measurements of p+Au, d+Au, and $^3$He+Au collisions) to the cluster-level structure of light nuclei, specifically the $\alpha$-clustering in carbon. It builds on earlier hydrodynamic studies of clustered carbon collisions by Broniowski (2013) and Bozek (2014) that focused on hadron flow, and applies the same logic to the direct photon puzzle, the longstanding discrepancy between measured and calculated photon anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions. The results suggest that photon flow observables from C+Au collisions at RHIC could provide a clean experimental signature to detect $\alpha$-cluster structure, and the strong anti-correlation between initial ellipticity and triangularity offers a new handle for future measurements.
