# Can $X(5568)$ be described as a $B_sπ$, $B\bar{K}$ resonant state?

> The D0 Collaboration has recently seen a resonant-like peak in the $B_sπ$ invariant mass spectrum, claimed to be a new state called $X(5568)$. Using a $B_sπ$--$B\bar{K}$ coupled channel analysis, implementing unitarity, and with the interaction derived from Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory, we are able to reproduce the reported spectrum, with a pole that can be associated to the claimed $X(5568)$ state, and with mass and width in agreement with the ones reported in the experimental analysis. However, if the $T$-matrix regularization is performed by means of a momentum cutoff, the value for the latter needed to reproduce the spectrum is $Λ= 2.80 \pm 0.04\ \text{GeV}$, much larger than a "natural" value $Λ\simeq 1\ \text{GeV}$. In view of this, it is difficult to interpret the nature of this new state. This state would not qualify as a resonance dynamically generated by the unitarity loops. Assuming the observed peak to correspond to a physical state, we make predictions for partners in the $D$, $D^\ast$, and $B^\ast$ sectors. Their observation (or lack thereof) would shed light into this issue.

## Key claim

The authors reproduce the D0 Collaboration's reported $B_s\pi$ invariant mass spectrum and find a pole that can be associated with the claimed $X(5568)$ state, with mass and width in agreement with experiment. However, the momentum cutoff required to regularize the $T$-matrix is $\Lambda = 2.80 \pm 0.04\ \text{GeV}$, much larger than a "natural" value of $\Lambda \simeq 1\ \text{GeV}$, making it difficult to interpret the state as a resonance dynamically generated by unitarity loops.

## Method

The authors perform a $B_s\pi$--$B\bar{K}$ coupled channel $T$-matrix analysis for $I(J^P) = 1(0^+)$, implementing unitarity with interactions derived from Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory. The only non-zero $S$-wave potential is the off-diagonal transition potential $V_{12}(s)$, while diagonal potentials vanish due to OZI suppression. The loop functions are regularized using a subtraction constant $a(\mu)$, which is the single free parameter fitted to the D0 Collaboration's $B_s\pi$ invariant mass spectrum data, with the background shape fixed from the D0 Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers

- Fitted subtraction constant: $a(\mu) = -0.97 \pm 0.02$ for the "cone cut" spectrum, and $a(\mu) = -0.98 \pm 0.02$ for the spectrum without the "cone cut"
- Extracted $X(5568)$ mass and width for "cone cut" spectrum: $M_X = 5564.2 \pm 2.6\ \text{MeV}$, $\Gamma_X = 26.7 \pm 1.2\ \text{MeV}$
- Extracted $X(5568)$ mass and width for no "cone cut" spectrum: $M_X = 5562.8 \pm 2.8\ \text{MeV}$, $\Gamma_X = 27.4 \pm 1.2\ \text{MeV}$
- Momentum cutoff equivalent to the fitted subtraction constant: $\Lambda = 2.80 \pm 0.04\ \text{GeV}$ ("cone cut") and $\Lambda = 2.83 \pm 0.04\ \text{GeV}$ (no "cone cut"), compared to a "natural" value $\Lambda \simeq 1\ \text{GeV}$
- Fit quality: $\chi^2_\text{d.o.f.} = 0.8$ for "cone cut" spectrum, $\chi^2_\text{d.o.f.} = 1.4$ for no "cone cut" spectrum
- Predicted charmed partner in $D_s\pi$, $DK$ sector: mass $2210.8 \pm 2.3\ \text{MeV}$, width $50.1 \pm 1.4\ \text{MeV}$
- Predicted $B^\ast_s\pi$, $B^\ast\bar{K}$ partner ($1^+$): mass $5610.8 \pm 2.6\ \text{MeV}$, width $26.8 \pm 1.3\ \text{MeV}$
- Predicted $D^\ast_s\pi$, $D^\ast K$ partner ($1^+$): mass $2346.7 \pm 2.3\ \text{MeV}$, width $46.5 \pm 1.3\ \text{MeV}$

## Context

This work provides the first theoretical attempt to reproduce the D0 Collaboration (2016) $B_s\pi$ spectrum showing the $X(5568)$ peak, using a coupled channel approach with Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory. The results extend discussions by Burns (2016) and Guo (2016) that a pure molecular state dynamically generated by unitarity loops is not favoured, and the large cutoff needed points to missing channels, other interaction sources, or "non-molecular" components such as tetraquarks. The predictions for partners in the charm and bottom sectors enable experimental tests: the predicted $D_s\pi$ state around $2.2\ \text{GeV}$ may correspond to a peak seen at $2.17\ \text{GeV}$ in the $D^+_s \pi^0$ spectrum from BaBar (Aubert 2006), and the LHCb Collaboration (2016) has presented preliminary negative results for $X(5568)$ in $pp$ collisions, creating a puzzle that further observations of the predicted partners could resolve.
