# Final state interactions in the electroweak nuclear response

> I review the description of the electroweak nuclear response at large momentum transfer within nonrelativistic many-body theory. Special consideration is given to the effects of final state interactions, which are known to be large in both inclusive and semi-inclusive processes. The results of theoretical calculations of electron-nucleus scattering observables are compared to the data, and the generalization to charged current neutrino-nucleus interactions is discussed.

# Final state interactions in the electroweak nuclear response

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic many-body theory (NMBT) approach, combined with the Correlated Glauber Approximation (CGA), provides quantitative parameter-free predictions of the electroweak nuclear response in the impulse approximation regime at beam energies above ~1 GeV, but the description of the quasi-free Delta production region shows significant disagreement with data, likely due to uncertainties in nucleon structure functions at low Q².

## Method

The author reviews the impulse approximation (IA) scheme, in which the nuclear cross section is written in terms of the nuclear spectral function P(p) describing the momentum and energy distribution of nucleons in the target nucleus. Final state interactions (FSI) are treated using the Correlated Glauber Approximation (CGA), which combines NMBT with a generalization of Glauber theory of high-energy proton scattering, assuming the struck nucleon moves along a straight trajectory (eikonal approximation) and spectators are fixed scattering centers (frozen approximation). The inclusive cross section in presence of FSI is expressed by folding the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) result with a function f_q(nu) that depends on the nuclear transparency T_A and the eikonal propagator.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparencies in Carbon are suppressed by 20-40% relative to the PWIA limit; in Gold the suppression is 50-70%
- Calculated transparencies neglecting NN correlations give T_A ≈ 0.5 for Carbon and ≈ 0.3 for Iron at Q² > 2 GeV², which are incompatible with experimental data
- The theoretical results account for measured ¹⁶O(e,e') cross sections at beam energies between 700 MeV and 1200 MeV and scattering angle 32° with accuracy better than 10%
- Pauli blocking effects become large in the Q² distributions at Q² < 0.2 GeV² for neutrino-nucleus scattering at E_nu = 1 GeV
- The correlation function g(r_1, r_j) is much less than 1 at interparticle distances |r_1j| < 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the NMBT approach developed by Benhar (1991) for describing nuclear responses to electromagnetic probes, and generalizes it to charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions. The CGA treatment of FSI builds on Glauber (1959) theory of high-energy proton scattering. The results are compared to experimental data from Garino (1992), O'Neill (1995), Abbott (1998), Garrow (2002), and Rohe (2005) for nuclear transparencies, and to LNF data for ¹⁶O(e,e') cross sections. The approach enables parameter-free predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments and suggests that Pauli blocking and FSI may explain the deficit of measured cross sections at low Q² relative to Monte Carlo simulations by Ishida.
