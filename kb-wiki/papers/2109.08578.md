# Conservation laws in a novel hybrid approach

> Heavy-ion collisions covering a wide range of collision energies provide a vast amount of observables characterizing the properties of strongly-interacting matter. In particular collisions towards the high baryon-density regime of the QCD phase-diagram have become of interest to study the postulated first order phase transition and to locate a possible critical end point. In this work, the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid is presented as a novel hybrid model to theoretically describe such heavy-ion collisions. In addition, the SMASH hadron resonance gas equation of state is introduced. The accuracy of the latter is shown to be of fundamental importance in order to conserve energy, baryon number and electric charge throughout the different stages of the hybrid model. Furthermore, the impact of an inaccurate equation of state on final state observables is discussed. This work constitutes a first validation of the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid in terms of conservation laws and excitation functions. It is expected to be applied to a broader range of observables in the future.

## Key claim

The SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid model conserves energy, baryon number, and electric charge to within 7% across a wide range of collision energies, but only when using an improved hadron resonance gas equation of state; the abstract's claim of "fundamental importance" of the equation of state is conditional on the fact that an unmodified equation of state leads to violations of up to 15%.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid model combining the SMASH hadronic transport code for initial and final stages with the vHLLE 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics code for the intermediate stage, using a chiral model equation of state for the hydrodynamic evolution. They extracted a hadron resonance gas equation of state from the SMASH particle list (all PDG hadrons up to 2.35 GeV) by solving coupled equations for temperature, pressure, and chemical potentials as functions of energy density, baryon density, and charge density, then compared an unmodified version with an improved version that included approximations for problematic low-energy-density regions. The model was validated by checking conservation laws across all stages (initial state, hydrodynamics, sampling, afterburner) for heavy-ion collisions from sqrt(s_NN) = 4.3 GeV to 200 GeV, and by computing excitation functions for pion, proton, and kaon yields and mean transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- Energy, baryon number, and electric charge conservation violations are no more than 7% in the SMASH-vHLLE-hybrid when using the improved equation of state in an ideal hydrodynamics setup.
- With the unmodified equation of state, violations of energy, baryon number, and charge conservation reach up to 15% during the sampling stage.
- The SMASH hadron resonance gas equation of state is defined down to an energy density of e = 0.01 GeV/fm^3.
- The hydrodynamic freezeout hypersurface is defined at a critical energy density of e_crit = 0.5 GeV/fm^3.
- Midrapidity yields increase by up to 8% at low collision energies when switching from the unmodified to the improved equation of state.
- The hybrid model is applicable for collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 4.3 GeV to 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the line of dynamically initialized hybrid approaches for intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, following earlier work by Akamatsu (2018) and Petersen (2017). It introduces a new combination of the SMASH transport model (Weil, 2016) with the vHLLE hydrodynamics code (Karpenko, 2013), and demonstrates that the accuracy of the hadron resonance gas equation of state at the particlization interface is critical for conserving quantum numbers, in contrast to earlier studies by Oliinychenko (2020) and Vovchenko (2021) which focused on different aspects of conservation. The validated model enables future application to a broader range of observables at low and intermediate collision energies, particularly for studying the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density.
