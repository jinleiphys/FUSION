# Energy losses in the black disc regime and correlation effects in the STAR forward pion production in dAu collisions

> We argue that in the small x processes, in the black disc QCD regime (BDR) a very forward parton propagating through the nuclear matter should loose a significant and increasing with energy and atomic number fraction of its initial energy as a result of dominance of inelastic interactions, causality and energy-momentum conservation. We evaluate these energy losses and find them to lead to the significant suppression of the forward jet production in the central NA collisions at collider energies with a moderate suppression of recoiling jet at central rapidities. We confront our expectations with the recent RHIC data of the STAR collaboration on the probability, $P$, for emission of at least one fast hadron at a central rapidity in association with production of a very forward high $p_t$ neutral pion in $pp$ and $dAu$ collisions. We calculate the A-dependence of $P$, and find that the data imply a strong suppression of leading pion production at central impact parameters. We also conclude that production of recoil jets in the hard subprocess is not suppressed providing further evidence for the dominance of peripheral collisions. Both features of the data are consistent with the onset of BDR. We suggest new phenomena and new observables to investigate BDR at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

In the black disc QCD regime (BDR) at small x, a forward parton propagating through nuclear matter loses a significant and increasing fraction of its initial energy as a result of dominance of inelastic interactions, causality, and energy-momentum conservation; the authors argue that this energy loss, rather than other mechanisms, explains the observed suppression of forward pion production in dAu collisions at RHIC, with the caveat that the effect is largest at central impact parameters while peripheral collisions dominate the observed yield.

## Method

The authors evaluate energy losses for a forward parton in the BDR by calculating the average fractional energy loss per inelastic ladder exchange using NLO DGLAP and BFKL approximations, then multiplying by the number of inelastic interactions N(b) at a given impact parameter. They model the probability for emission of at least one fast hadron at central rapidity in association with a forward high-pT neutral pion using geometric considerations and the BRAHMS data on the dependence of central multiplicity on the number of wounded nucleons, fitting the correlation parameters B and S measured by STAR.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of energy losses in the black disc regime, contrasting with the moderate-x regime where energy losses are proportional to the square of the distance propagated (Dokshitzer, 1991) and are much smaller (less than 4 GeV for a 200 GeV quark through a heavy nucleus, as found by Arleo, 2002). The paper confronts predictions with RHIC data from BRAHMS (2004), PHENIX (2004), and STAR (2005) on forward hadron suppression and correlations, finding that the data are consistent with the onset of BDR rather than with Color Glass Condensate models (Kharzeev, 2003) which predict dominance of central impact parameters and A^{5/6} scaling. The analysis enables future studies at RHIC and LHC to distinguish between BDR and CGC mechanisms through new observables such as the ratio of total hadron multiplicity in trigger events versus minimum bias events.
