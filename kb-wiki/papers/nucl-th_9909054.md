# Effective Vector Meson Masses in a Cutoff Field Theory

> Based on quantum hadrodynamics with a finite cutoff, the effective masses of vector mesons(ω, ρ) in nuclear medium are calculated. We use a low-energy effective Lagrangian which is obtained by integrating high-energy quantum fluctuations. Although we use an artificial cutoff, the cutoff-dependence can be removed order by order. It is shown that there is a strong correlation between the effective ω-meson mass and the effective nucleon mass at the normal density. It is also found that the effective ρ-meson mass m_ρ^* decreases as density increases. The rate of the decrease becomes smaller at high density. As a result, at the normal density, the m^*_ρ/m_ρis 0.85 \sim 0.95.

## Key claim

Using a low-energy effective Lagrangian derived from quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) with a finite cutoff, the authors calculate effective vector meson masses and find that the cutoff dependence can be removed order by order through phenomenological determination of couplings; the abstract's claim of a strong correlation between effective omega-meson mass and effective nucleon mass at normal density is conditional on the effective nucleon mass being not much larger than 0.80 times the free nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors start from a sigma-omega model Lagrangian defined at a physical cutoff Lambda_0 (of order a few GeV) and integrate high-energy quantum fluctuations down to an artificial cutoff Lambda = 1.5 GeV, producing a low-energy effective Lagrangian with higher-order sigma self-interactions. They determine the effective couplings by fitting to saturation conditions (binding energy -15.75 MeV at density 0.15 fm^-3) and to the effective nucleon mass M*_0 and incompressibility K as variable inputs, then compute the vector meson self-energies in the random phase approximation (RPA) using the nucleon propagator in nuclear medium. For the rho meson, they additionally fit two phenomenological coefficients to reproduce the linear density dependence of the rho mass from QCD sum rules at low density (rho/rho_0 < 0.2).

## Key numbers

* At normal nuclear density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3), the ratio m*_rho / m_rho is 0.85 to 0.95.
* The binding energy used for saturation conditions is E_b0 = -15.75 MeV at rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3.
* The incompressibility K is taken in the range 250 to 350 MeV, consistent with empirical analyses.
* The rho-nucleon coupling constants used are (g_rho, kappa_rho) = (2.63, 6.0), obtained from N-N forward dispersion relations.
* The QCD sum rule linear relation used as input is m*_rho / m_rho = 1 - (0.18 +/- 0.05) * (rho / rho_0).
* The artificial cutoff Lambda is set to 1.5 GeV; the Lambda-dependence of effective masses is less than 10 percent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear medium properties and vertex corrections in QHD with a finite cutoff (Kouno et al., 1999) and builds on the non-perturbative renormalization group (NPRG) approach of Wilson and Polchinski. It contrasts with ordinary renormalization procedures (Chin, 1977) that take the cutoff to infinity, and with QCD sum rule calculations (Hatsuda, 1993) and the quark meson coupling model (Saito, 1996). The results enable future studies of higher-loop corrections via NPRG equations and provide a framework for calculating other in-medium hadron properties with controlled cutoff dependence.
