# Influence of initial state fluctuations on the production of thermal photons

> Inhomogeneities in the initial QCD matter density distribution increase the production of thermal photons significantly compared to a smooth initial-state-averaged profile in the region $p_T > 1$ GeV/$c$ in an ideal hydrodynamic calculation. This relative enhancement is more pronounced for peripheral collisions, for smaller size systems as well as for lower beam energies. A suitably normalized ratio of central-to-peripheral yield of thermal photons reduce the uncertainties in the hydrodynamical initial conditions and can be a useful parameter to study the density fluctuations and their size. The fluctuations in the initial density distribution also lead to a larger elliptic flow of thermal photons for $p_T >$ 2.0 GeV/$c$ compared to the flow from a smooth profile.

## Key claim

Inhomogeneities in the initial QCD matter density distribution increase the production of thermal photons significantly compared to a smooth initial-state-averaged profile for \(p_T > 1\) GeV/\(c\) in an ideal hydrodynamic calculation, but the effect is conditional on collision centrality, system size, and beam energy. The relative enhancement is more pronounced for peripheral collisions, smaller systems, and lower beam energies.

## Method

The authors used an event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic framework with a Monte Carlo Glauber model where entropy density is distributed around wounded nucleons using a 2D Gaussian function with a free parameter \(\sigma\) controlling fluctuation size. Thermal photon \(p_T\) spectra and elliptic flow were calculated at RHIC (200A GeV Au+Au) and LHC energies for different centrality bins using state-of-the-art QGP and hadronic photon rates, with initial formation times \(\tau_0\) taken from the EKRT model (0.17 fm/\(c\) at RHIC, 0.14 fm/\(c\) at LHC). Results from fluctuating initial conditions were obtained by averaging over a sufficiently large number of random events, while smooth initial conditions were constructed by averaging 1000 fluctuating profiles.

## Key numbers

- For 0–20% central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the thermal photon \(p_T\) spectrum from fluctuating initial conditions is about 10% flatter than the spectrum from smooth initial conditions in the region \(2 \le p_T \le 4\) GeV/\(c\).
- The initial formation time \(\tau_0\) is taken as 0.17 fm/\(c\) at RHIC and 0.14 fm/\(c\) at LHC, and varies within a range of 0.17 to 0.60 fm/\(c\) in different hydrodynamic calculations at RHIC energy.
- The fluctuation size parameter \(\sigma\) is fixed at 0.4 fm for all centrality bins in the main calculations.
- At \(p_T = 4\) GeV/\(c\), more than 80% of thermal photons are emitted before the first 1 fm, but less than 10% of elliptic flow is developed by that time.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic framework developed by Holopainen et al. (2011) to thermal photon production, building on earlier calculations by Chatterjee et al. (2012) that showed fluctuating initial conditions explain PHENIX direct photon data better in the \(2 \le p_T \le 4\) GeV/\(c\) range. The results enable systematic studies of density fluctuations and their size through photon observables at different collision centralities and energies, with the ratio of central-to-peripheral yield proposed as a tool to reduce uncertainties in hydrodynamic initial conditions. The elliptic flow results from fluctuating initial conditions remain well below the PHENIX data, indicating further physics is needed.
