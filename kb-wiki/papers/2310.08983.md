# Heavy flavor production in the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD)

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions produce a hot and dense nuclear matter, through which one can study the phase diagram of QCD. Open and hidden heavy flavors are promising probes to search for the properties of the hot and dense nuclear matter under extreme conditions. We present how the production and interactions of open and hidden heavy flavors in heavy-ion collisions are realized in the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics, which is a non-equilibrium microscopic transport approach for the description of the dynamics of strongly interacting hadronic and partonic matter.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach can describe open and hidden heavy flavor production in heavy-ion collisions from SIS to LHC energies, but the quantitative agreement with experimental data for bottomonium suppression requires assuming that only about 10% of heavy (anti)quark scatterings in the QGP affect bottomonium production and dissociation.

## Method

The authors implement heavy quark pair production using the PYTHIA event generator with Glauber model initial conditions, rescaling momenta to match fixed-order next-to-leading logarithm (FONLL) calculations. Heavy quarks interact in the QGP through elastic scattering with off-shell massive partons using leading-order Feynman diagrams based on the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), where the strong coupling depends on temperature and baryon chemical potential. Hadronization proceeds via coalescence with light partons when the local energy density is between 0.4 and 0.75 GeV/fm^3, or by fragmentation using Peterson's function otherwise. For quarkonium production, the Wigner projection method is used with Remler's formalism, where the quarkonium radius depends on temperature via the heavy quark potential from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The spatial diffusion coefficient from DQPM is consistent with lattice QCD calculations (no explicit numerical value given in the text)
- Local energy density thresholds for heavy quark hadronization: coalescence occurs between 0.75 GeV/fm^3 and 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Dissociation temperature for Upsilon(1S) is about 3.3 T_c for the free energy heavy quark potential
- Upsilon(2S) has a dissociation temperature close to T_c
- The scattering cross section of two open heavy flavors reduces to about 10% for hidden heavy flavor in heavy-ion collisions
- R_AA and v_2 of D mesons are shown for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in 0-10% and 30-50% centrality classes
- R_AA of Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) is shown for 0-80% central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the PHSD transport approach, previously successful for bulk observables (Linnyk 2015), to heavy flavor production by incorporating PYTHIA for initial production, EPS09 for nuclear shadowing effects (Eskola 2009), and DQPM for QGP interactions (Moreau 2019). The approach enables quantitative comparison with experimental data from ALICE and CMS collaborations at LHC energies, and the finding that only 10% of heavy quark scatterings affect bottomonium provides a constraint for future theoretical developments on color-singlet versus color-octet interactions in the QGP.
