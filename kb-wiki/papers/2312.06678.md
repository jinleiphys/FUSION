# Constraining nucleon effective masses with flow and stopping observables from the S$π$RIT experiment

> Properties of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) can be probed by measuring the dynamical properties of nucleus-nucleus collisions. In this study, we present the directed flow ($v_1$), elliptic flow ($v_2$) and stopping (VarXZ) measured in fixed target Sn + Sn collisions at 270 AMeV with the S$π$RIT Time Projection Chamber. We perform Bayesian analyses in which EoS parameters are varied simultaneously within the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics-Skyrme (ImQMD-Sky) transport code to obtain a multivariate correlated constraint. The varied parameters include symmetry energy, $S_0$, and slope of the symmetry energy, $L$, at saturation density, isoscalar effective mass, $m_{s}^*/m_{N}$, isovector effective mass, $m_{v}^{*}/m_{N}$ and the in-medium cross-section enhancement factor $η$. We find that the flow and VarXZ observables are sensitive to the splitting of proton and neutron effective masses and the in-medium cross-section. Comparisons of ImQMD-Sky predictions to the S$π$RIT data suggest a narrow range of preferred values for $m_{s}^*/m_{N}$, $m_{v}^{*}/m_{N}$ and $η$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that directed flow, elliptic flow, and stopping observables from Sn+Sn collisions at 270 AMeV are sensitive to the splitting of proton and neutron effective masses and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section, and that Bayesian analyses of these data suggest a narrow range of preferred values for the isoscalar effective mass ratio, isovector effective mass ratio, and in-medium cross-section enhancement factor, but the constraints are multivariate and correlated rather than independent.

## Method

The SπRIT Time Projection Chamber measured directed flow (v1), elliptic flow (v2), and stopping (VarXZ) in fixed-target collisions of 108Sn, 112Sn, 124Sn, and 132Sn beams on 112Sn and 124Sn targets at 270 AMeV. The authors performed Bayesian analyses using the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics-Skyrme (ImQMD-Sky) transport code, simultaneously varying the symmetry energy S0, slope parameter L, isoscalar effective mass ms*/mN, isovector effective mass mv*/mN, and in-medium cross-section enhancement factor η to obtain multivariate correlated constraints. Coalescence-invariant flow was constructed by weighting contributions from p, d, t, 3He, and 4He by proton number to avoid model dependence from clusterization.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 270 AMeV for all Sn+Sn collisions
- Centrality selection: central events with average impact parameter ⟨b⟩ = 1 fm used for VarXZ analysis
- Rapidity range for flow data: 0 ≤ y0 ≤ 0.8 (limited by detector acceptance)
- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3 (reference value used in Taylor expansions)
- Saturation energy for symmetric nuclear matter: E0 = −15.8 ± 0.5 MeV (from prior work cited as Dutra (2012))
- Isoscalar incompressibility: K0 = 230 ± 30 MeV (from prior work cited as Dutra (2012))

## Context

This work extends previous SπRIT experiment results on pion production ratios (Estee (2021)) that constrained the neutron-proton effective mass splitting Δmnp*/δ and the slope parameter L, by adding flow and stopping observables that are sensitive to the isoscalar and isovector effective masses separately. The multivariate Bayesian approach captures correlations between EoS parameters that earlier single-observable analyses could not resolve, and the results can be combined with neutron star observations (e.g., from Danielewicz (2002) and Reed (2021)) to further constrain the nuclear equation of state at supra-saturation densities.
