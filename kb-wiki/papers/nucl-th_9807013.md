# Bremsstrahlung Dileptons in Ultra-relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We consider production of dilepton pairs through coherent electro-magnetic radiation during nuclear collisions. We show that the number of pairs produced through bremsstrahlung is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the yield measured by the CERES collaboration. Therefore, coherent bremsstrahlung can be ruled out as an explanation for the observed enhancement of low mass dileptons in CERES and HELIOS.

## Key claim

Coherent bremsstrahlung from decelerating nuclei produces dilepton yields about two orders of magnitude smaller than the CERES data, even when using a Gaussian charge distribution that overestimates the yield; therefore this mechanism cannot explain the observed low-mass dilepton enhancement.

## Method

The authors model ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions by assuming each nucleus decelerates during a finite collision time, with rapidity changing linearly in proper time. They compute the electromagnetic current for an extended Gaussian charge distribution, Fourier-transform it to obtain the dilepton production cross section, and integrate over the deceleration phase numerically. The calculation includes both iso-scalar and iso-vector contributions, which interfere destructively, and uses experimental acceptance cuts from the CERES collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The coherent bremsstrahlung yield is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the dilepton yield measured by the CERES collaboration at invariant masses around 400 MeV.
- Calculations are performed for collision times of 1 fm and 4 fm, with rapidity change Δy = 2.4.
- The transverse momentum spectrum is integrated over the invariant mass range 200–600 MeV for comparison with CERES data.
- The enhancement factor in nuclear versus proton collisions scales as Z₁Z₂, where Z₁ and Z₂ are the atomic numbers of the colliding nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mishustin et al. (1997), who considered only ω-meson production from nuclear deceleration, by including the full electromagnetic current including the iso-vector ρ-meson contribution and its destructive interference with the iso-scalar part. The results rule out coherent bremsstrahlung as an explanation for the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by CERES and HELIOS, supporting the need for alternative mechanisms such as in-medium modifications of vector meson masses (Brown and Rho, 1991) or modified pion dispersion relations in a baryon-rich environment.
