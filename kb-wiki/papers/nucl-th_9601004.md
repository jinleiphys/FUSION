# Six-quark configurations in the NN system correlated with experiment

> The nucleon-nucleon interaction at short range is analyzed in terms of six-quark configurations on a base of numerical solutions of modified RGM equations. It is shown that in low partial waves L=0,1 the system has a two-channel character: the $NN$ channel and the inner six-quark state ("bag") with specific color-spin structure. Starting from this analysis it is shown that polarization observables could be a good tool for investigation of a quark structure of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon interaction at short range in low partial waves L=0,1 can be described as a two-channel system consisting of an open NN channel with a nodal wave function and a closed six-quark "bag" channel (the s^6 configuration) that is orthogonal to the NN state; polarization observables in deuteron break-up reactions are sensitive to this inner quark structure, but the authors note that a quantitative description of NN scattering above 0.5-1 GeV is not expected from any quark microscopical approach.

## Method

The authors solved modified Resonating Group Method (RGM) equations for the six-quark system using a trial wave function that incorporates both two-centered shell-model configurations and six-quark shell-model states (s^6, s^4p^2, etc.) via delta-function terms. The quark Hamiltonian included one-gluon exchange, confinement, instanton-induced interaction, and chiral (pi and sigma meson) interactions, with parameters fitted to the non-strange baryon spectrum in two variants of the pi-qq form factor (hard cutoff at 1.5 GeV/c and soft cutoff at 0.6 GeV/c). The NN wave function at short range was obtained by projecting the full six-quark RGM solution into the NN channel using fractional parentage coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The node in the reduced NN wave function (after subtracting the s^6 configuration) is stable at distances r approximately 0.5-0.6 fm over the energy range 0 < E < 1 GeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for the Delta-Delta component in the deuteron is S_DeltaDelta = 0.02, and for the N*N component is S_N*N = 0.006.
- The probability of the ^3P*_1 state (S=1) in the deuteron is approximately 0.5%.
- The probability of the ^1P*_1 state (S=0) in the deuteron is less than 10^{-5}.
- The quark model parameters for variant (a): alpha_s = 0.42, g_c = 49.4 MeV/fm, V_0 = 26.0 MeV, y = 3, b = 0.54 fm; for variant (b): alpha_s = 0.44, g_c = 152.6 MeV/fm, V_0 = 144.6 MeV, y = 7, b = 0.56 fm.
- The calculated baryon masses for variant (a): N = 939 MeV, N** (1/2+) = 1437 MeV, N** (1/2+) tilde = 1703 MeV, Delta = 1230 MeV, Delta** = 1863 MeV; for variant (b): N = 939 MeV, N** = 1449 MeV, N** tilde = 1715 MeV, Delta = 1230 MeV, Delta** = 1839 MeV.
- The chiral coupling constant alpha_Ch = 0.0276.

## Context

This work extends earlier RGM calculations by Tamagaki (1979), Obukhovsky (1979), and Kusainov (1991) that identified the role of excited six-quark configurations (s^4p^2, s^3p^3) in NN interaction at short range, and connects to the Moscow potential approach of Kukulin (1985) and Dor (1991) which uses forbidden 0S and 1P states. The authors demonstrate that polarization observables in deuteron break-up reactions (T_20 analyzing power, polarization transfer kappa_0) are sensitive to the P-wave components of the six-quark deuteron wave function, which could enable experimental tests of quark degrees of freedom in nuclear phenomena at intermediate energies. The approach is presented as a development of models by Lomon (1980) and Kalashnikova (1990) for spin asymmetry in NN scattering.
