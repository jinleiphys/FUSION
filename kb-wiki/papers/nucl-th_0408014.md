# The Kinetic Energy Density in Kohn-Sham Density Functional Theory

> This work continues a program to systematically generalize the Skyrme Hartree-Fock method for medium and heavy nuclei by applying effective field theory (EFT) methods to Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT). When conventional Kohn-Sham DFT for Coulomb systems is extended beyond the local density approximation, the kinetic energy density tau is sometimes included in energy functionals in addition to the fermion density. However, a local (semi-classical) expansion of tau is used to write the energy as a functional of the density alone, in contrast to the Skyrme approach. The difference is manifested in different single-particle equations, which in the Skyrme case include a spatially varying effective mass. Here we show how to generalize the EFT framework for DFT derived previously to reconcile these approaches. A dilute gas of fermions with short-range interactions confined by an external potential serves as a model system for comparisons and for testing power-counting estimates of new contributions to the energy functional.

## Key claim

This work extends the effective field theory (EFT) framework for Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT) to include the kinetic energy density as an explicit functional variable, thereby reconciling the conventional DFT approach (which expands the kinetic energy density in terms of the density and its gradients) with the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach (which retains the kinetic energy density as an independent variable and yields a spatially varying effective mass). The reconciliation is demonstrated for a dilute gas of fermions with short-range interactions confined by an external potential, serving as a model system for testing power-counting estimates.

## Method

The authors introduce a generating functional with sources coupled to both the density operator and the kinetic energy density operator, then perform a double Legendre transformation via the inversion method of Fukuda et al. to obtain an energy functional of both density and kinetic energy density. The non-interacting Kohn-Sham system is defined with a position-dependent effective mass arising from the source coupled to the kinetic energy density, leading to single-particle equations of the Skyrme form. The interaction part of the effective action is constructed order-by-order in the EFT expansion using Hugenholtz diagrams, with the coefficients expressed in terms of scattering parameters (s-wave scattering length \(a_s\), effective range \(r_s\), and p-wave scattering volume \(a_p^3\)).

## Key numbers

- The non-interacting energy density for a uniform system is \(\mathcal{E}_0 = \frac{3}{5} \left(\frac{k_F^2}{2M}\right) \rho\), where \(\rho = \nu k_F^3 / (6\pi^2)\) and \(\nu\) is the spin degeneracy.
- The s-wave coupling constant is matched as \(C_0 = 4\pi a_s / M\), where \(a_s\) is the s-wave scattering length.
- The two-derivative s-wave coupling is \(C_2 = C_0 (a_s r_s / 2)\), where \(r_s\) is the effective range.
- The p-wave coupling constant is \(C_2' = 4\pi a_p^3 / M\), where \(a_p\) is the p-wave scattering volume.

## Context

This work continues a program initiated by Puglia et al. (2002) that applied EFT methods to DFT for dilute Fermi systems, and extends that framework to include the kinetic energy density as a functional variable. The approach directly addresses unresolved questions about the Skyrme Hartree-Fock method, which has been a major tool for nuclear structure since the work of Vautherin and Brink (1972) and Negele and Vautherin (1972), including why certain terms are included in the energy functional and what the expansion parameter is. The formalism enables systematic improvement of nuclear energy functionals with controlled error estimates, and provides a foundation for connecting modern treatments of few-nucleon problems to calculations in medium and heavy nuclei.
