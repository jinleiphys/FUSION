# Perturbative description of nuclear double beta decay transitions

> A consistent treatment of intrinsic and collective coordinates is applied to the calculation of matrix elements describing nuclear double beta decay transitions. The method, which was developed for the case of nuclear rotations, is adapted to include isospin and number of particles degrees of freedom. It is shown that the uncertainties found in most models, in dealing with these decay modes, are largely due to the mixing of physical and spurious effects in the treatment of isospin dependent interactions.

# Perturbative description of nuclear double beta decay transitions

## Key claim

The zero-energy state that appears in standard proton-neutron QRPA treatments of double beta decay, and which has been interpreted as a signature of a phase transition, is actually a consequence of the breakdown of isospin symmetry implicit in separate proton and neutron BCS solutions. A consistent separation of intrinsic and collective coordinates can restore isospin symmetry and remove this spurious mode from the physical spectrum, provided the treatment includes isospin-dependent interactions that are not necessarily isoscalar.

## Method

The authors adapt a collective-coordinate formalism originally developed for nuclear rotations to include isospin and particle-number degrees of freedom, applied to particles moving in a single j-shell coupled through a monopole pairing force. They introduce constraints that enforce the equivalence of rotating the intrinsic system versus rotating the body, expand the laboratory-frame Hamiltonian in powers of \(T^{-1}\) (where \(T\) is isospin), and treat the resulting interband interaction perturbatively. The formalism is tested against exact diagonalization results for Fermi-type double beta decay transitions.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy \(\omega_d\) is displayed for \(j = 9/2\), \(T_A = 5\), \(T = 3\), \(e_0 = 0.8\) MeV, \(g = 0.4\) MeV as a function of the ratio \(g_2/g\).
- Excitation energy \(\omega_d\) is displayed for \(j = 19/2\), \(T_A = 10\), \(T = 4\), \(e_0 = 0.63\) MeV, \(g = 0.2\) MeV as a function of the ratio \(g_2/g\).
- The energy released \(\Delta\) in the double beta decay matrix element expression is taken to be 0.5 MeV, following Hirsch (1997).
- The perturbative approach predicts exact results for \(g_2 = 0\) and "very satisfactory" results for other values of \(g_2/g\).

## Context

This work extends the collective-coordinate treatment of Bes and Kurchan (1990) from nuclear rotations to isospin and particle-number degrees of freedom, addressing instabilities found in standard proton-neutron QRPA calculations by Vogel and Fisher (1985), Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986), and Civitarese, Faessler, and Tomoda (1987). It directly confronts the zero-energy mode identified by Engel, Pittel, Stoitsov, Vogel, and Dukelsky (1997) and Hirsch, Hess, and Civitarese (1997), showing that this mode is spurious and can be eliminated through proper treatment of collective coordinates. The approach enables a perturbative description that exactly satisfies Ikeda's sum rule, unlike the renormalized QRPA of Toivanen and Suhonen (1995), and reproduces the suppression of matrix elements near the point where the proton-neutron symmetry-breaking interaction approaches the fully symmetric interaction.
