# Bulk viscosity in kaon-condensed color-flavor locked quark matter

> Color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter at high densities is a color superconductor, which spontaneously breaks baryon number and chiral symmetry. Its low-energy thermodynamic and transport properties are therefore dominated by the H (superfluid) boson, and the octet of pseudoscalar pseudo-Goldstone bosons of which the neutral kaon is the lightest. We study the CFL-K^0 phase, in which the stress induced by the strange quark mass causes the kaons to condense, and there is an additional ultra-light "K^0" Goldstone boson arising from the spontaneous breaking of isospin. We compute the bulk viscosity of matter in the CFL-K^0 phase, which arises from the beta-equilibration processes K^0<->H+H and K^0+H<->H. We find that the bulk viscosity varies as T^7, unlike the CFL phase where it is exponentially Boltzmann-suppressed by the kaon's energy gap. However, in the temperature range of relevance for r-mode damping in compact stars, the bulk viscosity in the CFL-K^0 phase turns out to be even smaller than in the uncondensed CFL phase, which already has a bulk viscosity much smaller than all other known color-superconducting quark phases.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity in the CFL-K^0 phase scales as T^7, avoiding the exponential Boltzmann suppression of the uncondensed CFL phase; however, in the temperature range relevant for r-mode damping in compact stars (around 1 MeV or lower), the bulk viscosity in the CFL-K^0 phase is even smaller than in the CFL phase, which already has a bulk viscosity much smaller than all other known color-superconducting quark phases.

## Method

The authors compute the bulk viscosity from the beta-equilibration processes K^0 <-> H+H and K^0 + H <-> H, where H is the superfluid boson from broken baryon number and K^0 is the Goldstone boson from broken isospin in the kaon-condensed phase. They use a two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective theory for the kaons to obtain self-consistent thermal masses and dispersion relations, and compute the one-loop self-energy for the H boson to obtain its imaginary part. The bulk viscosity is then obtained from the kaon density, kaon susceptibility, and the rates of the equilibration processes.

## Key numbers

* The bulk viscosity in the CFL-K^0 phase varies as T^7, in contrast to the exponential Boltzmann suppression by the kaon's energy gap in the uncondensed CFL phase.
* The zero-temperature kaon mass is estimated as m ~ 4 MeV and the kaon chemical potential as mu ~ 20 MeV for a quark chemical potential mu_q ~ 500 MeV and energy gap Delta ~ 30 MeV.
* The critical temperature T_c for kaon condensation is estimated to be at least of order tens of MeV.
* The low-temperature kaon specific heat in the CFL-K^0 phase scales as (2 pi^2 / (15 v^3)) (m / |delta m|)^(3/2) T^3, where delta m = m - mu and v = 1/sqrt(3).
* The low-temperature kaon susceptibility in the CFL-K^0 phase is approximately 4 f_pi^2, where f_pi ~ 100 MeV.
* The kaon density at low temperatures in the CFL-K^0 phase is approximately 4 mu f_pi^2 |delta m| / m.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of bulk viscosity in the CFL phase due to thermal kaons (Alford 2007) to the case where kaons are condensed (the CFL-K^0 phase). It contrasts with prior calculations of bulk viscosity for nuclear matter (Haensel 2000, Haensel 2001, Andersson 2004), hyperonic matter (Lindblom 2001, Haensel 2001), unpaired quark matter (Madsen 1999, Madsen 1992), and other color-superconducting phases (Manuel 2004, Alford 2006, Sa'd 2006, Manuel 2007). The result enables more accurate modeling of r-mode damping in compact stars that may contain CFL-K^0 quark matter, and provides a basis for computing other transport properties such as shear viscosity and thermal conductivity in this phase.
