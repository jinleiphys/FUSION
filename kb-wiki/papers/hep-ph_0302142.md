# Gapless two-flavor color superconductor

> A new, gapless two-flavor color superconducting phase that appears under conditions of local charge neutrality and $β$-equilibrium is revealed. In this phase, the symmetry of the ground state is the same as in the conventional two-flavor color superconductor. In the low-energy spectrum of this phase, however, there are only two gapped fermionic quasiparticles, and the other four quasiparticles are gapless. The origin and the basic properties of the gapless two-flavor color superconductor are discussed. This phase is a natural candidate for quark matter in cores of compact stars.

## Key claim

Under conditions of local charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium, a stable two-flavor color superconducting phase exists at lower baryon chemical potentials than previously thought, but it is a gapless rather than an ordinary 2SC phase, with the same ground-state symmetry but a different quasiparticle spectrum.

## Method

The authors use an SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar quark-antiquark and scalar diquark coupling channels, in the chiral limit with zero current quark mass. They solve simultaneously the gap equation, the electrical neutrality condition, and the color neutrality condition at zero temperature for a range of diquark coupling strengths, with the baryon chemical potential between about 350 and 450 MeV where strange quarks are assumed heavy and absent.

## Key numbers

- The gapless 2SC phase appears for intermediate diquark coupling strength in the range \(0.7 \lesssim \eta \lesssim 0.8\) (where \(G_D = \eta G_S\) with \(G_S = 5.0163\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and \(\Lambda = 0.6533\) GeV).
- At \(\mu = 400\) MeV and \(\eta = 0.75\), the solution gives a diquark gap \(\Delta \approx 68\) MeV and an electric chemical potential \(\mu_e \approx 148.4\) MeV.
- The conventional density relations between pairing quarks are violated in the gapless phase: \(n_{ur} = n_{ug} \neq n_{dr} = n_{dg}\).
- The gapless phase has four gapless and two gapped fermionic quasiparticles, compared to two gapless and four gapped in the ordinary 2SC phase.

## Context

This work extends the study of charge-neutral two-flavor color superconductivity, contradicting the earlier conclusion of Alford and Rajagopal (2002) that the 2SC phase is absent in compact stars, and refining the narrow window (10 to 15 MeV wide around \(\mu \approx 450\) MeV) found by Steiner, Reddy, and Prakash (2002). The gapless 2SC phase is distinct from the metastable gapless CFL phase of Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (2000). It enables further studies of magnetic properties, neutrino emissivities, and transport properties relevant to compact star cores.
