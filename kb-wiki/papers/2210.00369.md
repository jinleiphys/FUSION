# Electro-Weak Transitions in A=2,3 Nuclei in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> In this thesis, which is in the field of few-body systems, I am studying low-energy electro-weak interactions in light nuclear systems whose number of nucleons, $A$, is smaller than 4 ({\it i.e.,} $d$, $^3$H, $^3$He), where the main purpose of this work is to calculate the proton-proton ($pp$) fusion rate.

## Key claim

The thesis claims that the proton-proton (pp) fusion cross-section calculated using pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) is up to 5% larger than previous estimations, with a total uncertainty of 1.2% at 70% degree of belief, but this result depends on calibrating the two-body weak low-energy constant \(L_{1,A}\) from the triton beta-decay and on the assumption that the EFT is consistent for transitions between A=2 and A=3 systems.

## Method

The author develops a perturbative diagrammatic approach within pionless EFT to calculate electro-weak transitions between A=3 bound states (triton and helium-3) and between A=3 and A=2 systems. The two-body weak low-energy constant \(L_{1,A}\) is calibrated using the well-measured triton beta-decay (\(^3\mathrm{H} \rightarrow ^3\mathrm{He} + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e\)), and the framework is validated by comparing electromagnetic observables (magnetic moments of deuteron, triton, and helium-3, and the radiative capture \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\)) against experimental data up to next-to-leading order (NLO). A Bayesian approach is used to estimate the truncation uncertainty of the EFT expansion, treating the expansion parameter as a random variable with a log-normal distribution derived from information theory.

## Key numbers

* The pp fusion S-factor at zero energy is predicted to be up to 5% larger than previous estimations, with a total uncertainty of 1.2% at 70% degree of belief.
* Previous pionless EFT predictions for \(\Lambda_{pp}^2(0)\) range from 6.71 to 7.7 (unitless), including: 7.08 from effective range expansion (Bahcall and May, 1969), 7.04–7.7 at NLO (Kong and Ravndal), 6.71–7.03 at N\(^4\)LO (Butler and Chen), 7.09 ± 0.02 (Ando et al.), and 7.095 ± 0.07 (NPLQCD collaboration, 2017).
* The NLO corrections to electromagnetic observables amount to only a few percent, and the theoretical uncertainty from the order-by-order analysis is less than 1%.
* The triton beta-decay half-life is known to per-mill level accuracy (Chou).

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of two-nucleon electro-weak interactions (e.g., Kong and Ravndal; Butler and Chen; Ando et al.) to include three-nucleon systems, enabling the calibration of \(L_{1,A}\) from triton beta-decay rather than from muon capture on deuteron (as suggested by Chen et al.) or from lattice QCD at unphysical pion masses (NPLQCD collaboration). The results contradict the consistency of earlier \(\chi\)EFT predictions by Marcucci et al. (2013) and Acharya et al. (2016), which are inconsistent with each other, and the present calculation provides a simpler, renormalizable, and cutoff-independent framework with a comprehensive Bayesian uncertainty estimate. This enables a verified prediction of the pp fusion rate that may affect current models of solar evolution.
