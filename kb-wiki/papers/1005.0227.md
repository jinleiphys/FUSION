# Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in nuclear multifragmentation

> Nuclear multifragmentation in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions has long been associated with liquid-gas phase transition. We calculate the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s for an equilibrated system of nucleons and fragments produced in multifragmentation within an extended statistical multifragmentation model. The temperature dependence of eta/s exhibits surprisingly similar behavior as that for water. In the coexistence phase of fragments and light particles, the ratio eta/s reaches a minimum of comparable depth as that for water in the vicinity of the critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition. The effects of freeze-out volume and surface symmetry energy on eta/s in multifragmentation are studied.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s for an equilibrated system of nucleons and fragments produced in nuclear multifragregation exhibits a minimum at T ≈ 5 MeV in the liquid-gas coexistence phase, with a minimum value comparable to that of water near its critical point, but the abstract does not specify that this minimum (η/s)min ≈ 2.1 is significantly above the conjectured Kovtun-Son-Starinets lower bound of 1/4π.

## Method

The author used a modified microcanonical statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to generate an equilibrated ensemble of fragments from the breakup of a ¹⁵⁰Sm nucleus at fixed freeze-out volumes V = 3V₀ and V = 6V₀. The shear viscosity was computed via classical kinetic theory as η ≈ (1/3) Σᵢ nᵢ ⟨pᵢ⟩ λᵢ, where the mean free path λᵢ = 1/Σⱼ nⱼ σᵢⱼ uses Coulomb scattering cross sections, and the entropy was obtained from the thermodynamic relation S = -dF/dT. Two versions of the liquid drop mass formula (LDM1 without surface symmetry energy and LDM2 with it) were compared to study the effects of surface symmetry energy on η/s.

## Key numbers

- Minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s)min ≈ 2.1 for the LDM2 mass formula at freeze-out volume V = 6V₀, occurring at T ≈ 5 MeV in the liquid-gas coexistence phase of ¹⁵⁰Sm fragmentation.
- The shear viscosity from nucleon transport (Danielewicz) is given by the analytical fit η = (1700/T²)(n/n₀)² + [22/(1+10⁻³T²)](n/n₀)⁰·⁷ + 5.8T¹/²/(1+160T²), with units MeV/(fm²c).
- The conjectured KSS lower bound is η/s ≥ 1/4π ≈ 0.08, which is significantly below the multifragmentation minimum of ≈ 2.1.
- The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in water is T_c, with critical pressure P_c = 22.6 MPa, used for comparison in the inset of Fig. 4.

## Context

This work extends the study of transport properties in nuclear matter beyond nucleon-only transport calculations by Danielewicz (2000) and the shear viscosity estimates from transverse flow analysis by Schur (2000), which considered only nucleonic degrees of freedom in the early stages of heavy ion collisions. The finding that η/s in multifragmentation exhibits a minimum comparable to that of water near its critical point (Csernai, 2006) suggests that nuclear multifragmentation may serve as a laboratory for studying liquid-gas phase transition analogies, and the sensitivity of η/s to freeze-out volume and surface symmetry energy indicates that transport coefficients could provide additional constraints on the nuclear equation of state and symmetry energy parameters.
