# Scrutinizing the eta-eta' mixing, masses and pseudoscalar decay constants in the framework of U(3) chiral effective field theory

> We study the $η$-$η'$ mixing up to next-to-next-to-leading-order in $U(3)$ chiral perturbation theory in the light of recent lattice simulations and phenomenological inputs. A general treatment for the $η$-$η'$ mixing at higher orders, with the higher-derivative, kinematic and mass mixing terms, is addressed. The connections between the four mixing parameters in the two-mixing-angle scheme and the low energy constants in the $U(3)$ chiral effective theory are provided both for the singlet-octet and the quark-flavor bases. The axial-vector decay constants of pion and kaon are studied in the same order and confronted with the lattice simulation data as well. The quark-mass dependences of $m_η$, $m_{η'}$ and $m_K$ are found to be well described at next-to-leading order. Nonetheless, in order to simultaneously describe the lattice data and phenomenological determinations for the properties of light pseudoscalars $π, K, η$ and $η'$, the next-to-next-to-leading order study is essential. Furthermore, the lattice and phenomenological inputs are well reproduced for reasonable values of low the energy constants, compatible with previous bibliography.

## Key claim
The authors show that next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) calculations in U(3) chiral perturbation theory are essential to simultaneously describe lattice QCD data and phenomenological determinations for the properties of light pseudoscalars π, K, η, and η', even though the quark-mass dependences of m_η, m_η', and m_K are already well described at next-to-leading order.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic calculation of η-η' mixing up to NNLO in the δ-expansion of U(3) chiral perturbation theory, where δ counts powers of squared momenta, quark masses, and 1/N_C simultaneously. They constructed the relevant chiral Lagrangian at LO, NLO, and NNLO orders, computed the mixing parameters including higher-derivative, kinematic, and mass mixing terms, and derived the connections between the two-mixing-angle scheme parameters and the low energy constants (LECs) in both the singlet-octet and quark-flavor bases. The theoretical expressions were then confronted with lattice simulation data for meson masses and decay constants, as well as phenomenological inputs from radiative decays of vector resonances.

## Key numbers
- Lattice simulation points used have m_π < 500 MeV, with pion masses ranging from approximately 200 MeV to 700 MeV across different groups
- The LO mixing angle θ and masses m_η̄ and m_η̄' are given by analytic expressions involving M_0^2 (the singlet mass from the U(1)_A anomaly), m̄_K^2, and m̄_π^2, with Δ^2 = m̄_K^2 - m̄_π^2
- The FKS formalism assumes a single mixing angle φ for decay constants in the quark-flavor basis, with F_q and F_s as the diagonal decay constants
- At LO in the δ-expansion, F_8 = F_0 = F and θ_8 = θ_0 = θ, where F is the pNGB axial decay constant in the large N_C and chiral limits

## Context
This work extends previous studies of η-η' mixing in the chiral framework that were limited to NLO, such as those by Herrera-Siklody et al. (1998), Gerard et al. (2004, 2009), Mathieu et al. (2010), and Guo et al. (2011). It provides the first full NNLO analysis in the δ-expansion scheme, incorporating recent lattice simulation data from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (2011), RBC-UKQCD (2010, 2011, 2013), UKQCD (2012), and ETM (2013) that were not considered in earlier works. The results enable more precise determinations of U(3) chiral LECs and provide a top-down theoretical framework connecting the two-mixing-angle phenomenological scheme to fundamental chiral effective field theory parameters.
