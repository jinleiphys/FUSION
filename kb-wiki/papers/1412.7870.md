# Searching for isovector signatures in the neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes

> We search for potential isovector signatures in the neutron-rich oxygen and calcium isotopes within the framework of a relativistic mean-field theory with an exact treatment of pairing correlations. To probe the isovector sector we calibrate a few relativistic density functionals using the same isoscalar constraints but with one differing isovector assumption. It is found that under certain conditions, the isotopic chain in oxygen can be made to terminate at the experimentally observed ${}^{24}$O isotope and in the case of the calcium isotopes at ${}^{60}$Ca. To produce such behavior, the resulting symmetry energy must be soft, with predicted values for the symmetry energy and its slope at saturation density being $J\!=\!(30.92\pm0.47)$ MeV and $L\!=\!(51.0\pm1.5)$ MeV, respectively. As a consequence, the neutron-skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb is rather small: $R_{\rm skin}^{208}\!=\!(0.161\pm0.011)$ fm. This same model - labelled "FSUGarnet" - predicts $R_{1.4}\!=\!(13.1\pm0.1)$ km for the radius of a "canonical" 1.4$M_{\odot}$ neutron star, yet is also able to support a two-solar-mass neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the termination of the oxygen isotopic chain at the experimentally observed \({}^{24}\)O and the calcium chain at \({}^{60}\)Ca can be reproduced within a relativistic mean-field framework only if the symmetry energy is soft, with the neutron-skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb assumed to be small (around 0.16 fm) rather than the larger values that emerge from fits relying exclusively on well-measured isoscalar observables.

## Method

The authors calibrated several relativistic mean-field (RMF) density functionals using the same set of isoscalar constraints (binding energies, charge radii, giant monopole resonances, and a maximum neutron-star mass of 2.10 \(M_{\odot}\)) but added an assumed value for the neutron-skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb (0.12, 0.16, or 0.28 fm) as an isovector constraint. After calibration, they applied an RMF-plus-exact-pairing (RMF+EP) approach to compute ground-state energies along the oxygen and calcium isotopic chains, using valence spaces appropriate for each region (sd shell for oxygen, fp\(g_{9/2}\) shell for calcium).

## Key numbers

* The model that best reproduces the experimental drip lines (labeled FSUGarnet) predicts a symmetry energy at saturation density of \(J = (30.92 \pm 0.47)\) MeV and a slope of \(L = (51.0 \pm 1.5)\) MeV.
* The neutron-skin thickness of \({}^{208}\)Pb predicted by FSUGarnet is \(R_{\rm skin}^{208} = (0.161 \pm 0.011)\) fm.
* FSUGarnet predicts a radius of \(R_{1.4} = (13.1 \pm 0.1)\) km for a 1.4 \(M_{\odot}\) neutron star and a maximum neutron-star mass of \(M_{\rm max} = (2.07 \pm 0.02) M_{\odot}\).
* The stiff model (RMF028, with assumed \(R_{\rm skin}^{208} = 0.28\) fm) predicts a neutron-skin thickness of \(R_{\rm skin}^{208} = (0.287 \pm 0.020)\) fm and overbinds neutron-rich isotopes, erroneously predicting a drip line at \({}^{28}\)O.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calibration of the FSUGold2 functional (Chen and Piekarewicz, 2014), which used only isoscalar constraints and produced a stiff symmetry energy with a thick neutron skin. The present study shows that adding an isovector constraint (the neutron-skin thickness) can resolve the "oxygen anomaly", the failure of most mean-field models to reproduce the experimentally observed drip line at \({}^{24}\)O, without invoking three-nucleon forces as in the work of Otsuka (2010) or Hagen (2012). The resulting FSUGarnet model enables predictions for neutron-star radii and maximum masses that are consistent with the two-solar-mass neutron star observations of Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), while also providing a framework for future studies of drip-line physics with other classes of density functionals.
