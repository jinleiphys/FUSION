# Finite-volume quantization condition from the $N/D$ representation

> We propose a new model-independent method for determining hadronic resonances from lattice QCD. The formalism is derived from the general principles of unitarity and analyticity, as encoded in the $N/D$ representation of a partial-wave two-body amplitude. The associated quantization condition relates the finite-volume spectrum to the infinite-volume numerator, $\mathcal{N}$, used to reconstruct the scattering amplitude from dispersive relations. Unlike the original Lüscher condition, this new formalism is valid for energies coinciding with the left-hand cuts from arbitrary one- and multi-particle exchanges.

# Finite-volume quantization condition from the $N/D$ representation

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new quantization condition for extracting hadronic resonance parameters from lattice QCD that remains valid at energies below the nearest left-hand cut, unlike the original Lüscher condition which breaks down in that region. The formalism relies on the $N/D$ representation of the scattering amplitude and assumes that the finite-volume numerator function $\mathcal{N}_L$ can be replaced by its infinite-volume counterpart $\mathcal{N}$ up to exponentially suppressed corrections with the box size.

## Method

The authors derive a finite-volume quantization condition by applying the $N/D$ decomposition to the partial-wave two-body amplitude in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions. They separate the amplitude into a numerator $\mathcal{N}_L$ containing left-hand singularities and a denominator $\mathcal{D}_L$ containing right-hand unitarity cuts, then use the unitarity condition to write $\mathcal{D}_L$ as a sum over discrete momenta. The quantization condition is given by $\det \mathcal{D}_L = 0$, where the determinant is taken in angular momentum space, and the denominator is expressed in terms of the infinite-volume numerator function $\mathcal{N}_\ell(s)$.

## Key numbers

- The formalism applies to energies arbitrarily below the nearest left-hand cut branch point $s_{\rm lhc}$, extending the region of validity of the canonical Lüscher condition which only works for $s > s_{\rm lhc}$
- The finite-volume ground state masses are exponentially close to infinite-volume masses: $m_{i,L} = m_i + \mathcal{O}(e^{-m_\pi L})$
- The two-body phase space factor is $\rho_\ell(s) = \xi (p^\star)^{2\ell+1} / 8\pi \sqrt{s}$, where $p^\star = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{s}} \lambda^{1/2}(s, m_1^2, m_2^2)$ and $\lambda$ is the Källén triangle function
- The denominator function is normalized as $\mathcal{D}_\ell(\infty) = 1$

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume quantization condition framework originally developed by Lüscher (1986, 1991) and later generalized by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995), Kim, Sachrajda, and Sharpe (2005), and others. The new formalism addresses a known limitation: the Lüscher condition cannot be applied at energies below the nearest left-hand singularity, a region often probed in lattice QCD simulations (Green et al., 2021; Padmanath et al., 2022; Whyte et al., 2024). The approach simplifies and generalizes recent methods for incorporating one-particle exchange effects proposed by Klos et al. (2016), Meng et al. (2023), Raposo et al. (2023), Bubna et al. (2024), and Hansen et al. (2024). The formalism enables future applications to reactions such as $NN$ scattering and $DD^*$ scattering relevant for the $T_{cc}^+$ tetraquark.
