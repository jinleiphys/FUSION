# Lepton Polarization in Neutrino-Nucleon Interactions

> We derive generic formulas for the polarization density matrix of leptons produced in neutrino and antineutrino collisions and briefly consider some important particular cases. Next we employ the general formalism in order to include the final lepton mass and spin into the popular model by Rein and Sehgal for single pion neutrinoproduction.

## Key claim

The authors derive generic formulas for the lepton polarization density matrix in neutrino-nucleon and antineutrino-nucleon collisions, and then extend the Rein-Sehgal model for single pion neutrinoproduction to include final lepton mass and spin, enabling application to tau neutrino reactions where lepton mass effects are non-negligible.

## Method

The authors first derive covariant expressions for the lepton polarization density matrix by contracting the leptonic tensor (with definite lepton helicity) with the spin-averaged hadronic tensor, expressed in terms of six nucleon structure functions. They then apply this formalism to deep inelastic scattering, quasielastic scattering, and single resonance production, providing explicit structure function expressions for each case. Finally, they generalize the Rein-Sehgal model by modifying the leptonic current in the resonance rest frame to include lepton mass and helicity dependence, recalculating the coefficient functions S, B, and C that enter the resonance production amplitudes within the Feynman-Kislinger-Ravndal quark model framework.

## Key numbers

- The lepton polarization vector components are given by equations where the perpendicular and transverse projections contain an intrinsically indeterminate phase phi = phi_+ - phi_- (Eq. P_PT), making them unobservable in contrast to the longitudinal polarization and degree of polarization.
- For massless leptons, the polarization vector is fully polarized: P = (0,0, -+1) (the upper sign for neutrinos, lower for antineutrinos).
- The Breit-Wigner factor for the Delta resonance is given by |eta^Delta_BW(W)|^2 = (1/2pi)[Gamma_Delta(W)/((W^2-M_Delta^2)^2 + Gamma_Delta^2(W)/4)].
- The running width of the Delta resonance is Gamma_Delta(W) = Gamma_Delta^0 (M_Delta/W)^L [p_pi^*(W)/p_pi^*(M_Delta)]^(2L+1), where L=0 for S-wave or L=1 for P-wave decay.
- Only 70 of the total 144 coefficients V_n^{jk}, A_n^{jk}, and K_n^{jk} appearing in the resonance structure functions are nonzero.

## Context

This work extends the Rein-Sehgal model (Rein, 1981), which is widely used in neutrino event generators but neglects final lepton mass, making it inapplicable to tau neutrino reactions. The authors provide a covariant generalization that enables studies of lepton polarization in neutrino-nucleon interactions, which is important for interpreting tau lepton events in neutrino oscillation experiments (such as nu_mu to nu_tau oscillations) and for distinguishing oscillation channels in atmospheric and accelerator neutrino experiments. The formalism also addresses the muon polarization effects that can mimic electron neutrino events in detectors.
