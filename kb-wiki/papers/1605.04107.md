# On the Origin of the $XYZ$ Mesons

> In this talk we present a mechanism giving rise to exotic $XYZ$ four-quark states in the meson spectra within a constituent quark model approach. We discuss its generalization to five-quark states in the heavy baryon sector. Finally, we revise some other works in the literature and experimental data where this mechanism may be working.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the origin of exotic $XYZ$ four-quark states lies in the vicinity and coupling of two distinct two-meson thresholds, specifically the lower $(Q\bar Q)(n\bar n)$ threshold and the higher $(Q\bar n)(n\bar Q)$ threshold, but only when the higher threshold possesses an attractive interaction and the two thresholds are sufficiently close in energy; threshold vicinity alone is a necessary but not sufficient condition.

## Method

The authors analyze four-quark $(Q n \bar Q \bar n)$ and five-quark $(nnn Q \bar n)$ systems within a constituent quark model, solving the four-body problem using the hyperspherical harmonics formalism. They examine the convergence of the four-quark energy as a function of hyperangular momenta $K$ for different threshold energy differences $\Delta = E[(b\bar n)(n\bar b)] - E[(b\bar b)(n\bar n)]$, and study the Fredholm determinant from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to identify bound states. The analysis is extended to the bottom sector and to five-quark states in the heavy baryon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The $D^* + \bar D^*$ threshold lies at 4014 MeV, while the $J/\psi + \omega$ threshold lies at 3879 MeV, giving a difference of 135 MeV.
- For the bottom counterpart of the $X(3872)$, the $B\bar B^*$ threshold is at 10611 MeV and the $\Upsilon\omega$ threshold is at 10155 MeV, a separation of 456 MeV.
- When the threshold energy difference $\Delta$ is greater than approximately 50 MeV, no bound four-quark state forms; when $\Delta$ is less than approximately 50 MeV, a compact four-quark bound state may emerge.
- In the charmonium sector, 23 $XYZ$ states have been reported (20 in charmonium, 3 in bottomonium), of which only 8 have been observed independently by two collaborations with significance greater than $5\sigma$: $X(3872)$, $X(3915)$, $\chi_{c2}(2P)$, $G(3900)$, $Y(4140)$, $Y(4260)$, $Y(4360)$, and $Z_c^+(3900)$.
- The only bound state found in the $S$-wave $(Q\bar n)(n\bar Q)$ analysis appears in the $(I)J^{PC}=(0)1^{++}$ channel, corresponding to the $X(3872)$.
- For five-quark states, the channels with $J^P=5/2^-$ are the only ones that may host a compact five-quark state for all isospins, with a candidate $\Theta_c(3250)$ pentaquark at 3250 MeV/$c^2$.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Vijande et al. (2007) and Carames et al. (2009, 2011, 2012, 2014) to systematically explain the pattern of $XYZ$ mesons and pentaquarks. It contradicts the notion that any four-quark state near threshold is automatically a bound molecule, showing instead that threshold vicinity plus attractive interaction in the higher channel is required. The mechanism is contrasted with alternative explanations including conventional quarkonium (Eichten et al., 2004), meson-meson molecules (Tornqvist, 1994), quarkonium hybrids (Close, 2005), four-quark states (Vijande et al., 2007), and diquarkonium (Maiani et al., 2005). The results enable predictions for future experiments at BESIII, Belle II, LHC, and PANDA, and connect to lattice QCD calculations (Liu et al., 2012).
