# Thomas-Fermi studies of pairing in inhomogeneous systems: nuclear and cold atom systems at overflow

> A novel Thomas-Fermi (TF) approach to inhomogeneous superfluid Fermi-systems is presented and shown that it works well also in cases where the Local Density Approximation (LDA) breaks down. The novelty lies in the fact that the semiclassical approximation is applied to the pairing matrix elements not implying a local version of the chemical potential as with LDA. Applications will be given to the generic fact that if a fermionic superfluid in the BCS regime overflows from a narrow container into a much wider one, pairing is substantially reduced at the overflow point. Two examples pertinent to the physics of the outer crust of neutron stars and superfluid fermionic atoms in traps will be presented. The TF results will be compared to quantal and LDA ones.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel Thomas-Fermi (TF) approach to inhomogeneous superfluid Fermi-systems that works well even where the Local Density Approximation (LDA) breaks down, specifically when the coherence length exceeds the typical length scale of the mean-field potential variation. The key condition is that the semiclassical approximation is applied directly to the pairing matrix elements rather than implying a local version of the chemical potential as in LDA.

## Method

The authors apply the Thomas-Fermi approximation to the pairing matrix elements by replacing the quantal single-particle densities with their semiclassical on-shell TF densities in the evaluation of the pairing interaction matrix elements. They solve the BCS gap equation in this semiclassical framework for several geometries: a slab configuration with a potential pocket, a spherical double harmonic oscillator potential for cold atoms, a double-well potential in slab geometry, and Wigner-Seitz cells simulating the inner crust of neutron stars. The TF results are compared with full quantal BCS solutions and with LDA results to validate the approach.

## Key numbers

- For the slab geometry: pocket depth V₀ = -40 MeV, pocket half-width R = 10 fm, outer container half-width L = 100 fm, cutoff Λ = 50 MeV, coupling strength g = 150 MeV·fm³
- For the cold atom spherical trap: coupling strength g = -1.0, cutoff Λ = 164.34 in optical trap units with ω_opt = 2π × 1000 Hz, accommodating 10⁵ atoms in each spin state at full filling (μ = U)
- For the slab geometry at μ = 40, 0.5, and -5.0 MeV: LDA results can be locally wrong by as much as 50 percent compared to quantal and TF results
- For the Wigner-Seitz cell containing ¹⁵⁰⁰₃₈Sr: the central depression of the gap is stronger by about 30 percent in LDA than in TF
- For the inner crust: the gap practically vanishes at zero chemical potential when neutrons start to drip

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical treatment of pairing in inhomogeneous systems beyond the Local Density Approximation, which fails when the coherence length exceeds the typical length scale of potential variation (Kucharek 1989). The approach is applied to the generic phenomenon of pairing suppression at overflow points, relevant to the outer crust of neutron stars (Negele and Vautherin 1973, Baym-Bethe-Pethick 1971) and superfluid fermionic atoms in traps (Ketterle 1998). The results enable semiclassical studies of pairing in large systems where quantal solutions are numerically difficult, including Wigner-Seitz cell calculations for neutron star crusts and double-well potentials for Josephson current studies with cold fermionic atoms.
