# Large-basis shell-model calculations for p-shell nuclei

> Results of large-basis shell-model calculations for nuclei with A=7-11 are presented. The effective interactions used in the study were derived microscopically from the Reid93 potential and take into account the Coulomb potential as well as the charge dependence of T=1 partial waves. For A=7, a $6\hbarΩ$ model space was used, while for the rest of the studied nuclides, the calculations were performed in a $4\hbarΩ$ model space. It is demonstrated that the shell model combined with microscopic effective interactions derived from modern nucleon-nucleon potentials is capable of providing good agreement with the experimental properties of the ground state as well as with those of the low-lying excited states.

## Key claim

Large-basis no-core shell-model calculations using microscopically derived two-body effective interactions from the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential can reproduce experimental ground-state properties and low-lying excited states for A=7-11 p-shell nuclei, but the results depend on adjustable parameters such as the harmonic-oscillator frequency and the model-space size, and the approach systematically underbinds nuclei with higher ground-state isospin.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model calculations in complete NℏΩ model spaces (6ℏΩ for A=7, 4ℏΩ for A=8-11) using two-body effective interactions derived from the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method. The effective interactions included isospin-breaking contributions from the Coulomb potential and charge-dependent T=1 partial waves, with separate interactions for proton-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-neutron systems. A multi-valued effective interaction approach was employed to partially account for many-body effects, and the calculations were performed in the m-scheme using the Many-Fermion-Dynamics Code extended to handle different pn, pp, nn interactions.

## Key numbers

- For A=7 nuclei, calculations used a 6ℏΩ model space with ℏΩ=17 MeV and parameter k_Q=0.8; the m-scheme dimension for 7Li and 7Be reached 663,527
- For A=8-11 nuclei, calculations used a 4ℏΩ model space with ℏΩ values ranging from 14 to 17 MeV and two-nucleon model space parameter N_max=8
- To reproduce the experimental quadrupole moment of 7Li, effective charges of e^p_eff=1.18e and e^n_eff=0.18e were needed
- For the A=8 isobaric chain, average effective charges of e^p_eff=1.3e and e^n_eff=0.3e were required to reproduce 8Li and 8B quadrupole moments
- For the A=9 isobaric chain, average effective charges of e^p_eff=1.25e and e^n_eff=0.25e were needed
- For 10B, only at ℏΩ=17 MeV was the correct ground-state spin 3+0 obtained; at ℏΩ=14 and 15.5 MeV the calculated ground state was 1+0
- For 11Be, positive-parity states calculated in a 3ℏΩ model space were shifted relative to negative-parity states by 5.56 MeV
- Calculated rms proton radii were about 10% smaller than those from Variational Monte Carlo calculations

## Context

This work extends previous no-core shell-model calculations that had concentrated on 0s-shell nuclei and A=5,6 0p-shell nuclei (Zheng et al., 1995; Navratil and Barrett, 1996), and complements a recent study of A=10 nuclei for isospin-mixing corrections to the Fermi matrix element (Navratil and Barrett, 1997). The calculations are distinguished from other 0p-shell studies that use phenomenological interactions (Cohen and Kurath, 1965; Kumar, 1974; Warburton and Brown, 1992; Poves and Zuker, 1993) by employing microscopically derived effective interactions and large multi-configuration model spaces. The results enable calculations of electromagnetic and weak properties, scattering characteristics, and applications such as the 7Be+p ↔ 8B nuclear vertex constant, while pointing toward the need for three-body effective interactions to eliminate remaining free parameters.
