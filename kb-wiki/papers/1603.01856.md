# Open Questions On Nuclear Collective Motion

> The status of the macroscopic and microscopic description of the collective quadrupole modes is reviewed, where limits due to non-adiabaticity and decoherence are exposed. The microscopic description of the yrast states in vibrator-like nuclei in the framework of the rotating mean field is presented.

# Open Questions On Nuclear Collective Motion

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Unified Model's dichotomy between collective and intrinsic degrees of freedom is an idealization that breaks down due to non-adiabaticity and decoherence; the collective quadrupole modes progressively fragment as excitation energy increases, and the coherence length concept is introduced to quantify the resolution limit of collective degrees of freedom.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of the Bohr Hamiltonian (Geometric Collective Model) and the Interacting Boson Model for describing collective quadrupole excitations, comparing their phenomenological fits and microscopic derivations from mean-field theory. It then presents the rotating mean-field approach as a non-adiabatic, non-perturbative framework for studying yrast states, including the Tidal Wave concept for vibrator-like nuclei, and introduces the coherence angle and coherence length as measures of collectivity derived from overlaps of mean-field solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the microscopic Bohr Hamiltonian benchmark study (Delaroche 2010), the predicted excitation energy of the first 2+ state is on average 12% higher than experiment, and the transition strength is on average 22% higher, with variances of 40-50%.
- The predicted energies of the first excited 0+ state are systematically 50% high compared to experiment.
- For 102Pd, the yrast levels form a regular sequence with increasing B(E2, I→I-2) values up to I=14, while the IBM with boson number N_B=5 limits the regular yrast sequence at I=10.
- For the super deformed case in the coherence table, the moment of inertia J(2) is 97 hbar^2/MeV, the angular momentum span Delta J is 28, and the coherence angle Delta psi is 4 degrees.
- For the normal deformed case, J(2)=56 hbar^2/MeV, Delta J=14, and Delta psi=8 degrees.
- For the weak deformation (magnetic rotation) case, J(2)=14 hbar^2/MeV, Delta J=6, and Delta psi=20 degrees.

## Context

This review extends the work of Bohr and Mottelson's Unified Model (Nuclear Structure Vol. II) by exposing its limits due to non-adiabaticity and decoherence, building on the author's own recent review (Frauendorf 2015). It contrasts the phenomenological Geometric Collective Model (Caprio 2005) and Interacting Boson Model with microscopic mean-field approaches, and presents the Tidal Wave concept (Frauendorf, Gu, Sun 2011) as a new phenomenon in weakly deformed nuclei. The paper enables future work on quantifying the breakdown of collective motion through coherence lengths and on extending the Tidal Wave approach to describe non-yrast vibrational states.
