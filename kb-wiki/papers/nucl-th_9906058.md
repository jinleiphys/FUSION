# The short-range baryon-baryon interaction in a chiral constituent quark model

> The previous analysis of the short-range $NN$ repulsion originating from the Goldstone boson exchange hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks is revisited. We study in which respects the repulsion depends on the radial form of the spin-spin quark-quark force. We show that while the radial form affects the structure of the 6Q wave function, the short-range repulsion in the $NN$ system persists in any case. We extend our analysis to other $YN$ and $YY$ (flavor octet-octet) systems and demonstrate that the flavor-spin hyperfine interaction implies a short-range repulsion in these B=2 systems as well.

## Key claim

The short-range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) system, previously attributed to the Goldstone boson exchange hyperfine interaction between constituent quarks, persists regardless of the radial form of the spin-spin quark-quark force; however, the height of the repulsive core is sensitive to whether the flavor-singlet eta-prime-like exchange contribution is included, and the structure of the six-quark wave function at short range changes with the radial parametrization.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic chiral constituent quark model with a flavor-spin hyperfine interaction between quarks, derived from Goldstone boson exchange. They diagonalized a 4x4 Hamiltonian matrix in a basis of six-quark (6Q) shell-model configurations (s^6 and s^4p^2 orbital states) at zero separation distance between two three-quark clusters, using the Born-Oppenheimer adiabatic approximation. For the NN system, they recalculated with a modified radial form (setting the shift parameter r0 = 0) and readjusted coupling constants to reproduce the Delta-N mass splitting; for hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) systems, they evaluated the expectation value of the Hamiltonian for the lowest symmetry state |s^4p^2 [42]_O [321]_F [51]_{FS}> and compared it to the two-baryon threshold.

## Key numbers

* With the full interaction including eta-prime-like exchange, the lowest eigenvalue for the NN system in the ^3S_1 partial wave lies 1.403 GeV above the 2m_N threshold (2.648 GeV), using beta = 0.373 fm and g_8^2/4pi = 2.11.
* Without eta-prime-like exchange (g_0^2/4pi = 0), the lowest eigenvalue for the NN system in the ^3S_1 partial wave lies 0.565 GeV above the 2m_N threshold (2.9314 GeV), using beta = 0.522 fm and g_8^2/4pi = 1.29.
* For the YN system with YI = 1 1/2 and S = 0, the separation energy (interaction potential at zero separation) is 0.876 GeV above the lowest threshold (N-Lambda or N-Sigma).
* For the YY system with YI = 00 and S = 0, the separation energy is 1.102 GeV above the lowest threshold (Lambda-Lambda, Sigma-Sigma, or N-Xi).
* For the YY system with YI = -1 1/2 and S = 0, the separation energy is 1.371 GeV above the lowest threshold (Lambda-Xi).
* The harmonic oscillator parameter for the nucleon was determined as beta = 0.373 fm from the stability condition, giving a variational nucleon mass of 1.324 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis of Stancu, Pepin, and Glozman (1997) on NN short-range repulsion, revisiting the dependence on the radial form of the hyperfine interaction and extending the analysis to all flavor octet-octet baryon-baryon systems (YN and YY). The results contradict the possibility of a deeply bound H-particle, as previously suggested by Stancu, Pepin, and Glozman (1998). The study enables future dynamical treatments beyond the adiabatic approximation, such as the resonating group method, and calls for incorporation of multiple correlated Goldstone boson exchange interactions (scalar and vector meson exchanges) to provide a realistic description of middle- and long-range baryon-baryon physics.
