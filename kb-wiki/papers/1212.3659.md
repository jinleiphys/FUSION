# Dense nuclear matter and symmetry energy in strong magnetic fields

> The properties of nuclear matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field, including the density-dependent symmetry energy, the chemical composition and spin polarizations, are investigated in the framework of the relativistic mean field models FSU-Gold. The anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of the particles and the nonlinear isoscalar-isovector coupling are included. It is found that the parabolic isospin-dependence of the energy per nucleon of asymmetric nuclear matter remains valid for values of the magnetic field below $10^{5}B_{c}^{e}$, $B_{c}^{e}=4.414\times10^{13}$G being the electron critical field. Accordingly, the symmetry energy can be obtained by the difference of the energy per nucleon in pure neutron matter and that in symmetric matter. The symmetry energy, which is enhanced by the presence of the magnetic field, significantly affects the chemical composition and the proton polarization. The effects of the AMM of each component on the energy per nucleon, symmetry energy, chemical composition and spin polarization are discussed in detail.

## Key claim
The parabolic isospin-dependence of the energy per nucleon in asymmetric nuclear matter remains valid for magnetic fields below \(10^{5}B_{c}^{e}\) (where \(B_{c}^{e}=4.414\times10^{13}\) G is the electron critical field), allowing the symmetry energy to be extracted from the difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric matter even in the presence of a strong field. The symmetry energy is enhanced by the magnetic field, especially at low densities, and this enhancement significantly affects the chemical composition and proton polarization.

## Method
The authors employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) model FSU-Gold, which includes nonlinear isoscalar-isovector coupling (\(\Lambda_v\)) and \(\omega\) meson self-interactions (\(\zeta\)), to study nuclear matter in a uniform magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. They computed the energy densities of protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons using Landau level quantization for charged particles, including the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of all constituents (\(\kappa_p=1.7928\mu_N\), \(\kappa_n=-1.9130\mu_N\), \(\kappa_e=1.15965\times10^{-3}\mu_B\), \(\kappa_\mu=1.16592\times10^{-3}\mu_B\)). The system was constrained by chemical equilibrium (\(\mu_n-\mu_p=\mu_e=\mu_\mu\)) and charge neutrality (\(\rho_p=\rho_e+\rho_\mu\)).

## Key numbers
- Electron critical field: \(B_{c}^{e}=4.414\times10^{13}\) G, used as the unit of magnetic field strength.
- The parabolic law for energy per nucleon versus \(\beta^2\) holds for magnetic fields below \(10^{5}B_{c}^{e}\) at densities \(\rho_0\), \(2\rho_0\), \(3\rho_0\), and \(5\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the saturation density.
- The symmetry energy is enhanced in strong magnetic fields, with effects becoming noticeable above \(5\times10^{4}B_{c}^{e}\).
- Proton and muon fractions begin to deviate from field-free values at \(B>2\times10^{4}B_{c}^{e}\); full spin polarization of protons at \(\rho=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) occurs at \(B=5\times10^{4}B_{c}^{e}\).
- The threshold for direct URCA processes in field-free matter is a proton fraction of about 11% at low densities, rising to about 14% at high densities when muons are present.

## Context
This work extends prior RMF studies of dense matter in strong magnetic fields, such as those by S. D. et al. (who neglected nucleon AMM) and Mao et al. (who included nucleon AMM and found pure neutron matter instead of proton-rich matter at extreme fields). The use of the FSU-Gold interaction, which provides a good description of finite nuclei, allows a more realistic treatment of the density-dependent symmetry energy. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star interiors, particularly for understanding the composition, cooling via direct URCA processes, and spin polarization effects on superfluidity and rotational dynamics.
