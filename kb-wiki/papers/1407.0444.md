# Nature of isomerism in exotic sulfur isotopes

> We clarify the origin of the anomalously hindered $E2$ decay from the $4^+_1$ level in $^{44}$S by performing a novel many-body analysis in the shell model. Within a unified picture about the occurrence of isomerism in neutron-rich sulfur isotopes, the $4^+_1$ state is demonstrated to be a $K=4$ isomer dominated by the two-quasiparticle configuration $νΩ^π=1/2^-\otimesνΩ^π=7/2^-$. The $4^+_1$ state in $^{44}$S is a new type of high-$K$ isomer which has significant triaxiality.

## Key claim

The anomalously hindered E2 decay from the 4+1 level in 44S is demonstrated to originate from a K=4 isomer dominated by the two-quasiparticle configuration νΩπ=1/2- ⊗ νΩπ=7/2-, with the state exhibiting significant triaxiality rather than the axially symmetric shape typical of known high-K isomers.

## Method

The authors performed variation after angular-momentum projection (AM-VAP) as a beyond-mean-field approximation to the shell model, using the SDPF-MU interaction in the π(sd)Z-8 ν(pf)N-20 valence space. Wave functions were determined by minimizing the energy for each spin state using general single Slater determinants with angular-momentum projection and K-mixing, optimized via the conjugate gradient method. Overlap probabilities between the AM-VAP solutions and full shell-model wave functions were calculated to verify the quality of the approximation.

## Key numbers

- Overlap probabilities between full shell-model states and AM-VAP states in 44S: 0.92 for 0+1, 0.81 for 2+1, 0.86 for 4+1, 0.88 for 4+2, and 0.90 for 5+1
- Overlap probabilities in 43S: 0.96 for 1/2-1, 0.91 for 3/2-1, 0.85 for 5/2-1, 0.93 for 7/2-1, 0.93 for 7/2-2, 0.92 for 9/2-1, 0.93 for 9/2-2, 0.92 for 11/2-1, and 0.84 for 11/2-2
- The 4+1 state in 44S has 93% K=4 component, with deformation parameters β=0.23 and γ=28 degrees
- The 7/2-1 state in 43S has 98% K=7/2 component, with β=0.22 and γ=31 degrees
- Pairing gap 2Δ ≈ 2.5 MeV estimated from one-neutron separation energies of 43,44,45S
- Overlap probability of the 4+1 state with A[ν p3/2 ⊗ 7/2-1]J=4 is 0.66 and with A[ν f7/2 ⊗ 7/2-2]J=4 is 0.54
- Overlap probability between intrinsic state of K=4 members and its time-reversed state is only 0.02-0.08

## Context

This work extends the understanding of high-K isomerism, previously known only in heavier mass regions (A∼100, A∼130, A∼180, and A∼250) with axially symmetric shapes, to the light-mass region with 44S as the lightest case. The results contradict the beyond-mean-field calculation of Ref. [s44gogny] which missed the low-lying K=4 state due to time-reversal symmetry restrictions. The unified picture also explains the isomeric 7/2-1 state in 43S and the low-lying 0+2 state in 44S, and opens new experimental and theoretical opportunities for discovering high-K isomers across the nuclear chart.
