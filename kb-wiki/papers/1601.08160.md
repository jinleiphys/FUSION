# Predictions for $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb+Pb Collisions from a Multi-Phase Transport Model

> We present predictions from the string melting version of a multi-phase transport model on various observables in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We use the same version of the model as an earlier study that reasonably reproduced dN/dy, $p_{\rm T}$-spectra and elliptic flow of charged pions and kaons at low-$p_{\rm T}$ for central and semi-central heavy ion collisions at 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV. While we compare with the already-available centrality dependence data on charged particle $dN/dη$ at mid-pseudorapidity in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, we make predictions on identified particle dN/dy, $p_{\rm T}$-spectra, azimuthal anisotropies $v_n (n=2,3,4)$, and factorization ratios $r_{n}(η^{a},η^{b}) (n=2,3)$ for longitudinal correlations.

## Key claim

The string melting version of the AMPT model, using the same parameters that reasonably reproduced data at 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV, predicts that for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV the relative increase of azimuthal anisotropy coefficients \(v_n\) from 2.76 TeV is smallest for \(v_2\) and largest for \(v_4\), with the relative increase of \(v_2\) being larger in more peripheral collisions; however, the model fails to reproduce the centrality dependence of the mean transverse momentum and produces artificial mid-rapidity peaks in proton distributions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model (v2.26t5) with Lund string fragmentation parameters \(a=0.30\) and \(b=0.15\) GeV\(^{-2}\), strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s=0.33\), and a parton cross section of 3 mb. They simulated Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV and compared results with available ALICE and CMS data at 2.76 TeV and PHENIX data at 200 GeV, using the event-plane method for \(v_n\) at 200 GeV and the two-particle correlation method with a large \(\Delta\eta\) gap for \(v_n\) at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) at mid-pseudorapidity (\(|\eta|<1/2\)) for 0-5% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is reproduced by AMPT, with the ratio of \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) at 5.02 TeV to that at 2.76 TeV being close to the corresponding ALICE data.
- \(\pi^+\) dN/dy at mid-rapidity increases by \(\sim 33\%\) for 0-5% centrality and \(\sim 28\%\) for 20-30% centrality when going from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV.
- \(K^+\) dN/dy at mid-rapidity increases by \(\sim 34\%\) for 0-5% centrality and \(\sim 28\%\) for 20-30% centrality when going from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV.
- The ratio of mid-rapidity \(\pt\) spectra at 5.02 TeV to those at 2.76 TeV shows a weak increase from \(\sim 1.3\) at \(\pt=0\) to \(\sim 1.4\) at \(\pt=2\) GeV/\(c\) for both \(\pi^+\) and \(K^+\).
- Pion \(v_2\) at mid-rapidity for 20-30% centrality shows a relative increase of \(\sim 16\%\) from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV, insensitive to \(\pt\) within 0 and 2 GeV/\(c\).
- Charged particle mean transverse momentum \(\langle \pt \rangle\) at mid-pseudorapidity increases by only 1.7% to 3.0% when going from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model predictions from earlier studies at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (2.76 TeV) energies (Lin 2014) to the top LHC energy of 5.02 TeV, providing transport model predictions to contrast with recent hydrodynamic predictions for the same system (Niemi 2015, Noronha-Hostler 2015). The results enable comparisons between transport and hydrodynamic descriptions of azimuthal anisotropies and longitudinal correlations, and highlight the need to improve the quark coalescence model in AMPT to address the artificial mid-rapidity peaks in baryon distributions.
