# Probing the symmetry energy and the degree of isospin equilibrium

> The rapidity dependence of the single- and double- neutron to proton ratios of nucleon emission from isospin-asymmetric but mass-symmetric reactions Zr+Ru and Ru+Zr at energy range $100 \sim 800$ A MeV and impact parameter range $0\sim 8$ fm is investigated. The reaction system with isospin-asymmetry and mass-symmetry has the advantage of simultaneously showing up the dependence on the symmetry energy and the degree of the isospin equilibrium. We find that the beam energy- and the impact parameter dependence of the slope parameter of the double neutron to proton ratio ($F_D$) as function of rapidity are quite sensitive to the density dependence of symmetry energy, especially at energies $E_b\sim 400$ A MeV and reduced impact parameters around 0.5. Here the symmetry energy effect on the $F_D$ is enhanced, as compared to the single neutron to proton ratio. The degree of the equilibrium with respect to isospin (isospin mixing) in terms of the $F_D$ is addressed and its dependence on the symmetry energy is also discussed.

## Key claim

The slope parameter of the double neutron-to-proton ratio as a function of rapidity, taken from isospin-asymmetric but mass-symmetric mixing reactions (Zr+Ru and Ru+Zr), is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with the sensitivity enhanced at beam energies around 400 A MeV and reduced impact parameters around 0.5; however, the relative magnitude of the slope for stiff versus soft symmetry energy reverses between central and peripheral collisions, depending on the dominant emission mechanism.

## Method

The UrQMD transport model with a soft equation of state was used to simulate Zr+Ru and Ru+Zr reactions at beam energies from 100 to 800 A MeV and reduced impact parameters from 0 to 0.75. The rapidity dependence of unbound neutron-to-proton ratios and double neutron-to-proton ratios (defined as (n/p)_ZrRu / (n/p)_RuZr) was calculated for two parameterizations of the symmetry energy: E_sym(u) = 34 u^γ with γ = 0.5 (soft) and γ = 1.5 (hard). The slope parameter F_D was obtained by linearly fitting the double ratio over |Y_cm^(0)| ≤ 1.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 100, 200, 400, 600, and 800 A MeV.
- Reduced impact parameters studied: b/b_0 = 0, 0.25, 0.5, and 0.75, where b_0 = R_proj + R_targ.
- The double neutron-to-proton ratio shows enhanced sensitivity (~30%) to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, compared to the single neutron-to-proton ratio (~10%), at E_b = 400 A MeV and b/b_0 = 0.5.
- The F_D values show a broad maximum at E_b ~ 200–400 A MeV for intermediate impact parameters, while central collisions peak at 200 A MeV.
- The reversal of relative magnitudes of F_D for stiff versus soft symmetry energy occurs at b/b_0 ~ 0.2 for E_b = 400 A MeV.

## Context

This work extends the FOPI Collaboration mixing experiments (Rami 1999, Hong 2002) using the four mass-96 systems (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, Ru+Zr, Zr+Ru) to probe isospin equilibration, by additionally using the double neutron-to-proton ratio as a probe of the symmetry energy. The study builds on prior extractions of the symmetry energy at subnormal densities by Tsang (2004) and by Li (2005, 2006), which gave E_sym(u) ≈ 31.6 u^1.05 and E_sym(u) ≈ 31.6 u^0.69, respectively. The results enable future experimental selection of beam energies around 400 A MeV and semi-peripheral impact parameters to maximize sensitivity to the density dependence of the symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions.
