# Probing non-perturbative QCD aspects on particle production in pp collisions with forward-backward correlations using \texttt{PYTHIA8}

> We employ PYTHIA8 simulations to study forward-backward (FB) correlations in pp collisions at LHC energies, probing non-perturbative QCD dynamics via color reconnection (CR) and QCD radiation (ISR/FSR). Using \texttt{PYTHIA8} (v8.311) under ALICE/ATLAS kinematics, we analyze: extensive: FB multiplicity ($b_{\rm corr}^{\rm mult}$) and summed $p_{\rm T}$ ($b_{\rm corr}^{\sum p_{\rm T}}$) correlations; intensive: FB mean $p_{\rm T}$ ($b_{\rm corr}^{\overline p_{\rm T}}$) correlations; and \textit{strongly intensive:} $Σ_{\rm N_F N_B}$ quantity in symmetric pseudorapidity intervals, validated against available experimental data. Systematic studies of $b_{\rm corr}^{\rm mult}$ as functions of $η_{\rm gap}$, $η_{\rm sep}$, and $η$-$φ$ sectors show MPI-based CR (ranges 3.6, 5.4) and QCD Color Rope significantly improve agreement with data. ISR/FSR critically influence $b_{\rm corr}^{\rm mult}$ and $b_{\rm corr}^{\sum p_{\rm T}}$, with ISR dominant than FSR. Disabling ISR/FSR fails to replicate $η_{\rm gap}$-dependent trends. Further, $b_{\rm corr}^{\sum p_{\rm T}}$ shows minimal CR-range sensitivity but the correlation strength increases when CR is disabled. Intensive quantity, $b_{\rm corr}^{\overline p_{\rm T}}$, exhibit the opposite behavior to extensive observables i.e. the magnitude of $b_{\rm corr}^{\overline p_{\rm T}}$ increases with the increase of CR strength, highlighting the distinct influence of CR on intensive versus extensive observables. Its azimuthal dependence indicates that parton showers drive short-range correlations. The analysis of the strongly intensive quantity as a function of pseudorapidity gap indicates that FSR plays a dominant role at larger gaps, in contrast to the behavior observed in FB multiplicity correlations.

# Probing non-perturbative QCD aspects on particle production in pp collisions with forward-backward correlations using PYTHIA8

## Key claim

PYTHIA8 simulations with MPI-based color reconnection ranges of 3.6 and 5.4 and the QCD Color Rope model significantly improve agreement with ALICE data on forward-backward multiplicity correlations, but the QCD Color Rope mechanism achieves this agreement despite overpredicting charged-particle yields, indicating that the extensive nature of the observable masks underlying discrepancies and motivating the use of intensive and strongly intensive observables for robust understanding.

## Method

The authors generated 50 million inelastic non-diffractive pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV using PYTHIA8 version 8.311, varying the MPI-based color reconnection range (1.8, 3.6, 5.4), enabling/disabling ISR and FSR, and activating the QCD Color Rope hadronization mechanism. They computed the forward-backward multiplicity correlation strength b_corr(mult.), summed pT correlation b_corr(sum pT), mean pT correlation b_corr(mean pT), and the strongly intensive quantity Sigma(N_F N_B) in symmetric pseudorapidity intervals under ALICE and ATLAS kinematic cuts, comparing results against published experimental data.

## Key numbers

- b_corr(mult.) decreases monotonically with increasing pseudorapidity gap (eta_gap) for all CR ranges and delta_eta values at sqrt(s) = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV, consistent with ALICE data (Adam 2015)
- CR ranges 3.6 and 5.4 quantitatively reproduce experimental b_corr(mult.) within uncertainties, while default CR range 1.8 fails at all studied energies
- QCD Color Rope mechanism describes b_corr(mult.) data at sqrt(s) = 2.76 and 7 TeV within experimental uncertainties despite overpredicting charged-particle yields
- b_corr(sum pT) shows minimal sensitivity to CR range but its magnitude surges when CR is disabled
- b_corr(mean pT) magnitude increases with increasing CR strength, opposite to the behavior of extensive observables
- FSR plays a dominant role in the strongly intensive quantity Sigma(N_F N_B) at larger pseudorapidity gaps, contrasting with the behavior seen in FB multiplicity correlations

## Context

This work extends previous studies using PYTHIA8 (Kundu 2019, Cuautle 2019) that showed default CR configurations insufficiently describe FB correlations, and earlier investigations with PYTHIA6, HERWIG++, EPOS1.99, QGSJETII-04, and UrQMD (Bhattacharyya 2024) that found broad discrepancies with ATLAS and ALICE data (Aad 2012, Adam 2015). The study also addresses the strongly intensive observable Sigma, which previous PYTHIA8 Monash 2013 tune calculations (Erokhin 2021) failed to reproduce for preliminary ALICE results. The findings enable systematic tuning of non-perturbative QCD parameters in PYTHIA8 to achieve quantitative description of correlation observables across multiple LHC energies, and demonstrate that intensive and strongly intensive observables provide more robust tests of particle production dynamics than extensive ones.
