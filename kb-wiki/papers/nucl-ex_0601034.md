# Towards Searching for a Mixed Phase of Strongly Interacting QCD Matter at the JINR Nuclotron

> Available experimental data in respect to a possible formation of a strongly interacting mixed phase are outlined. A physical program is formulated for new facilities being opend in Dubna for acceleration of heavy ions with an energy up to 5 AGeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that accelerating heavy ions like gold at the JINR Nuclotron to the maximal planned energy of 5 AGeV may allow the study of hot and dense QCD matter properties, with the system potentially entering a mixed phase for a short time, though the expected observable signal for reaching this mixed phase should be rather weak.

## Method

The authors review existing experimental data on heavy-ion collisions from accelerators including the Dubna Synchrophasotron, Bevalac, AGS, SPS, and RHIC, and present dynamical trajectories from relativistic 3-fluid hydrodynamics calculations for central Au+Au collisions at various bombarding energies. They analyze the phase diagram in terms of temperature and baryon chemical potential, and examine observables such as elliptic flow, strangeness enhancement, transverse mass spectra, dilepton production, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlations as potential signals of a mixed phase or deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

- Maximal planned Nuclotron energy: \(E_{lab}=5\) AGeV
- The low-energy scanning program at SPS revealed interesting structure in energy dependence of observables at \(E_{lab} \sim 20-30\) AGeV
- At the top Nuclotron energy, the number of charged pions \((n_{\pi^+}+n_{\pi^-})\sim 200\) and kaons \(K^+\sim\) per central event
- The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) for protons changes sign near \(E_{lab}\sim 5\) AGeV, evolving from out-of-plane towards in-plane emission
- The "horn" structure in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio appears at \(E_{lab}\sim 30\) AGeV
- The inverse slope parameter of kaons shows a plateau at SPS energies, with a change in energy dependence around a beam energy of \(\sim 30\) AGeV
- The reduced pion multiplicity \(<n_\pi> / A_{part}\) for nucleus-nucleus collisions equals that for elementary NN interactions at the "horn" energy
- The measured values of \(\Delta\Phi_q\) for charge fluctuations vary between 0 and -0.05, significantly larger than expected for QGP fluctuations (\(-0.5 < \Delta\Phi_q < -0.15\))

## Context

This paper extends the search for a mixed phase of strongly interacting QCD matter to the energy range accessible at the JINR Nuclotron, complementing the FAIR GSI project (planned for after 2013) which will study compressed baryonic matter at \(E_{lab}=10-30\) AGeV. The work builds on experimental findings from the NA49 Collaboration at SPS, which revealed interesting structures in energy dependence of observables at \(E_{lab}\sim 20-30\) AGeV, and on theoretical calculations using relativistic 3-fluid hydrodynamics (IRT05, I05). The paper enables future experimental programs at the Nuclotron to investigate precursory phenomena related to confinement transition and chiral symmetry restoration, particularly through measurements of in-medium hadron modifications, elliptic flow, strangeness production, and charge fluctuations.
