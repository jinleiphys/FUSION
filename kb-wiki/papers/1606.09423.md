# Probing transfer to unbound states of the ejectile with weakly bound 7Li on 93Nb

> The two-step process of transfer followed by breakup is explored by measuring a rather complete set of exclusive data for reaction channels populating states in the ejectile continua of the $^7$Li+$^{93}$Nb system at energies close to the Coulomb barrier. The cross sections for $α+α$ events from one proton pickup were found to be smaller than those for $α+d$ events from one neutron stripping and $α+t$ events from direct breakup of $^7$Li. Coupled channels Born approximation and continuum discretized coupled channels calculations describe the data well and support the conclusion that the $α+d$ and $α+α$ events are produced by direct transfer to unbound states of the ejectile.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for the 7Li+93Nb system at near-barrier energies, the α+d and α+α coincidence events are produced by direct transfer to unbound states of the ejectile (1n-stripping to 6Li(3_1+) and 1p-pickup to 8Be(g.s.), respectively), but these processes together with direct breakup account for at most ~8% of the inclusive α yield, implying other mechanisms dominate.

## Method
Exclusive measurements of breakup fragments were performed using segmented large-area Si-telescope detectors at beam energies of 24, 28, and 30 MeV for the 7Li+93Nb system. Coincident α+α, α+d, and α+t events were identified via energy correlation spectra and relative energy calculations, with Monte Carlo simulations used to determine detection efficiencies. Coupled channels Born approximation (CCBA) calculations were applied to the transfer-breakup channels (1p-pickup to 8Be and 1n-stripping to 6Li), while continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations were used for direct breakup of 7Li.

## Key numbers
- Relative energy peaks observed: 92 keV for α+α (8Be ground state), 710 keV for α+d (6Li 3_1+ state at 2.18 MeV), and 2.16 MeV for α+t (7Li 7/2- state at 4.63 MeV)
- At 28 MeV: exclusive cross section for 1n-stripping to 6Li(3_1+) → α+d is 5.8 ± 0.4 mb (experimental) vs 6.2 mb (CCBA calculation)
- At 28 MeV: exclusive cross section for 1p-pickup to 8Be(g.s.) → α+α is 0.7 ± 0.1 mb (experimental, with 94Nb excitation ≤3 MeV) vs 0.56 mb (CCBA)
- At 28 MeV: exclusive cross section for 7Li(7/2-) → α+t is 3.3 ± 0.6 mb (experimental) vs 2.9 mb (CDCC)
- Inclusive α cross section at 28 MeV: 321 ± 48 mb
- Combined exclusive processes account for ~8% of inclusive α yield at 28 MeV
- Fusion-evaporation contribution to total α yield estimated at 10% (24 MeV) to 20% (30 MeV) from statistical model calculations

## Context
This work extends earlier measurements on the 7Li+65Cu system by Shrivastava (2006) which found 1n-stripping to unbound 6Li(3_1+) more probable than inelastic excitation of 7Li. It provides the first simultaneous measurement of absolute differential cross sections for both 1p-pickup and 1n-stripping followed by breakup, plus direct breakup, for a single system. The results contradict suggestions from studies by Luong (2011) and Simpson (2016) that 1p-pickup dominates over direct breakup; instead, direct breakup of 7Li is found to be the largest of the measured exclusive channels. The comprehensive data set and theoretical analysis serve as a benchmark for understanding reactions with unstable radioactive ion beams from ISOL facilities.
