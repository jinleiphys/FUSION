# Relativistic Description of Finite Nuclei Based on Realistic $NN$ Interactions

> A set of relativistic mean field models is constructed including the Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximation accounting for the exchange of isoscalar and isovector mesons as well as the pion. Density dependent coupling functions are determined to reproduce the components of the nucleon self-energy at the Fermi surface, obtained within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. It is investigated, to which extend the various mean field models can reproduce the DBHF results for the momentum dependence of the self-energies and the total energy of infinite matter. The mean field models are also used to evaluate the bulk properties of spherical closed-shell nuclei. We find that the Hartree-Fock model allowing for the exchange of $σ,\,ω,\,ρ,\,δ$ mesons and pions, yield the best reproduction of the DBHF results in infinite matter and also provides a good description of the properties of finite nuclei without any adjustment of parameters.

## Key claim

The paper constructs density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) models that, when including isoscalar and isovector mesons plus pions, can reproduce Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) results for nuclear matter and finite nuclei without parameter adjustment, but only if the coupling functions are fitted to DBHF self-energy components at the Fermi surface; the momentum dependence of the self-energies in the best DDRHF model is still only about half of that observed in the DBHF approach.

## Method

The authors constructed three DDRHF models (σ-ω, σ-ω-π, and σ-ω-π-ρ-δ) by fitting density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling functions to reproduce the scalar and time-like vector self-energy components from DBHF calculations (using the Bonn A potential) at the Fermi surface for nuclear matter densities of 0.100, 0.197, 0.313, and 0.467 fm⁻³. The σ-ω and σ-ω-π models were fitted to isospin-symmetric matter, while the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ model was fitted to asymmetric matter with proton fraction Yp=0.4. These models were then used to calculate the momentum dependence of self-energies and total energy in infinite nuclear matter, and subsequently applied to spherical closed-shell nuclei via a self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation in coordinate space using a discrete basis of spherical Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ for the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: 0.1583 fm⁻³ (compared to DBHF value of 0.181 fm⁻³)
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A at saturation for the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: -16.34 MeV (compared to DBHF value of -16.15 MeV)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ¹⁶O in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: -8.91 MeV (experimental: -7.98 MeV)
- Charge radius for ¹⁶O in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: 2.62 fm (experimental: 2.74 fm)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁰Ca in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: -9.18 MeV (experimental: -8.55 MeV)
- Charge radius for ⁴⁰Ca in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: 3.34 fm (experimental: 3.48 fm)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁸Ca in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: -8.81 MeV (experimental: -8.67 MeV)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁹⁰Zr in the σ-ω-π-ρ-δ DDRHF model: -8.83 MeV (experimental: -8.71 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock approach by including all mesons (σ, ω, ρ, δ, and π) with density-dependent couplings, addressing limitations of earlier DDRHF attempts by Fritz (1994) and Shi (1995) that only used density-dependent isoscalar couplings and omitted the δ meson. The study also improves upon density-dependent relativistic Hartree (DDRH) theories, which lack the spatial vector self-energy component and momentum-dependent self-energies present in DBHF. The approach enables a microscopic description of finite nuclei based on realistic NN interactions without parameter adjustment, providing a pathway toward full Dirac-Brueckner calculations for finite systems, which Van Giai (2010) identified as a major open problem in nuclear physics.
