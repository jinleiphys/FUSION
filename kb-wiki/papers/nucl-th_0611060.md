# Shell-model calculations with modern nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We report on a study of neutron-rich even-mass Te isotopes in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The strong short-range repulsion of the latter is renormalized by constructing a smooth low-momentum potential, V-low-k, that is used directly as input for the calculation of the effective interaction. In this paper, after a brief review of the theoretical framework, we present our results. Comparison shows that they are in very good agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized via the V-low-k approach, reproduce experimental spectra and B(E2) values for neutron-rich even-mass Te isotopes with very good agreement, though the agreement for the B(E2; 0+ → 2+1) in 136Te relies on a newer measurement that is 50% higher than the earlier value reported in the paper.

## Method

The authors derived a low-momentum potential V-low-k from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential by integrating out high-momentum modes down to a cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹, preserving the deuteron binding energy and phase shifts. They then used this smooth potential as input to compute the shell-model effective interaction via a folded-diagram (Q-box) method including diagrams up to second order, with the model space for Te isotopes treating ¹³²Sn as a closed core and valence protons and neutron holes occupying the 50-82 shell orbitals and valence neutrons the 82-126 shell orbitals. Calculations were performed with the OXBASH shell-model code using single-particle and single-hole energies taken from experimental spectra of ¹³³Sb, ¹³³Sn, and ¹³¹Sn.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Λ = 2.2 fm⁻¹ used for the V-low-k renormalization of the CD-Bonn potential.
- For ¹³⁴Te, differences between level energies calculated using CD-Bonn, Nijmegen II, and Argonne V₁₈ potentials (all renormalized via V-low-k) do not exceed 80 keV.
- Oscillator parameter ℏω = 7.88 MeV used in the harmonic-oscillator basis for Q-box diagram computation.
- B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) in ¹³²Te: calculated 38 Wu vs. experimental 43 ± 4 Wu.
- B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) in ¹³⁴Te: calculated 20 Wu vs. experimental 24 ± 3 Wu.
- B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) in ¹³⁶Te: calculated 44 Wu vs. experimental 25 ± 4 Wu (with a newer measurement giving a value 50% higher, in excellent agreement with the prediction).
- B(E2; 6⁺ → 4⁺) in ¹³²Te: calculated 1.9 Wu vs. experimental 3.3 ± 0.2 Wu.
- B(E2; 4⁺ → 2⁺) in ¹³⁴Te: calculated 4.3 Wu vs. experimental 4.3 ± 0.4 Wu.
- B(E2; 6⁺ → 4⁺) in ¹³⁴Te: calculated 1.9 Wu vs. experimental 2.05 ± 0.04 Wu.
- Effective proton charge used: 1.55e; effective neutron charge: 0.70e; effective neutron-hole charge: 0.78e.

## Context

This work extends the V-low-k approach previously introduced by Bogner et al. (2001, 2002) and applied by Covello et al. (2002, 2003) to shell-model calculations, demonstrating its viability as an alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method for deriving realistic effective interactions. The study focuses on neutron-rich Te isotopes around doubly magic ¹³²Sn, a region of current experimental interest due to measurements of B(E2) values via Coulomb excitation of radioactive ion beams (Radford, 2002). The results enable further shell-model studies of nuclei in this region and suggest that V-low-k potentials extracted from various modern NN potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen II, Argonne V₁₈) yield nearly identical results, pointing toward a nearly unique low-momentum NN potential.
