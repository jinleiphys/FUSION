# $^3$He Structure and Mechanisms of $p^3$He Backward Elastic Scattering

> The mechanism of $p^3$He backward elastic scattering is studied. It is found that the triangle diagrams with the subprocesses $pd\to ^3$He$ π^0$, $pd^*\to ^3$He$ π^0$ and $p(pp)\to^3$He$ π^+$, where $d^*$ and $pp$ denote the singlet deuteron and diproton pair in the $^1S_0$ state, respectively, dominate in the cross section at 0.3-0.8 GeV, and their contribution is comparable with that for a sequential transfer of a $np$ pair at 1-1.5 GeV. The contribution of the $d^*+pp$, estimated on the basis of the spectator mechanism of the $p(NN)\to ^3$He$ π$ reaction, increases the $p^3$He$\to ^3$He$ p$ cross section by one order of magnitude as compared to the contribution of the deuteron alone. Effects of the initial and final states interaction are taken into account.

## Key claim

The triangle diagrams involving the subprocesses $pd\to ^3$He$\,\pi^0$, $pd^*\to ^3$He$\,\pi^0$, and $p(pp)\to^3$He$\,\pi^+$ (where $d^*$ and $pp$ denote the singlet deuteron and diproton pair in the $^1S_0$ state) dominate the $p^3$He backward elastic scattering cross section at 0.3–0.8 GeV, and their contribution is comparable to that of sequential $np$ pair transfer at 1–1.5 GeV; however, the contribution of the $d^*+pp$ pairs, estimated via the spectator mechanism, increases the cross section by one order of magnitude relative to the deuteron alone, but this enhancement is almost completely counterbalanced by initial- and final-state interaction distortions.

## Method

The authors calculated the $p^3$He backward elastic scattering amplitude as a coherent sum of one-pion-exchange (OPE) triangle diagrams for the deuteron ($d$), singlet deuteron ($d^*$), and diproton ($pp$) configurations of $^3$He, using overlap integrals from Sciavilla (1990) and experimental cross sections for the subprocess $pd\to ^3$He$\,\pi^0$ from Berthet (1985). The singlet contributions were estimated via the spectator mechanism of pion absorption on $NN$ pairs, with isospin relations yielding a factor $C_I = 25/3$ relating the singlet and triplet amplitudes, and distortions in the initial and final states were included via an eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- The OPE model with only the deuteron underestimates the $p^3$He$\to\,^3$He$\,p$ cross section by a factor of 3–3.5 for $\Lambda_\pi = 0.65$ GeV/c and by 1.5–2.5 for $\Lambda_\pi = 1.3$ GeV/c, depending on beam energy.
- After including the singlet deuteron and diproton contributions, the cross section is overestimated by a factor of 2.5–4 (for $\Lambda_\pi = 0.65$ GeV/c) and 5–10 (for $\Lambda_\pi = 1.3$ GeV/c) before distortion corrections.
- The distortion factor $D(T_p)$ reduces the OPE cross section by one order of magnitude, bringing it into qualitative agreement with data.
- For the trinucleon wave function based on the CD Bonn $NN$ interaction, the sequential transfer cross section at $T_p > 0.5$ GeV is a factor of 30 smaller than for the RSC potential.
- The total cross section of $\pi^{+}\, ^{3}{\rm He}\to (pp)p$ at pion kinetic energy 37 MeV is $\sigma = 2.4 \pm 0.7$ mb, compared to $\sigma = 1.3$ mb for $\pi^0\, ^3{\rm He}\to dp$ at $T_p = 262$ MeV, giving a ratio consistent with $4/3$ expected from the spectator mechanism.

## Context

This work extends earlier DWBA studies of $p^3$He backward elastic scattering by Ladouz (1992), Blu (1996), and Uzikov (1998), which considered only sequential transfer of an $np$ pair, by adding OPE mechanisms involving intermediate pions. It also improves upon earlier OPE models by Barry (1973) and Berthet (1981) by including the singlet deuteron and diproton configurations of $^3$He and accounting for initial- and final-state distortions. The results enable future measurements of spin observables (e.g., the spin-spin correlation parameter $C_{y,y}$) at RCNP in Osaka to distinguish between the spin-independent singlet contributions and the spin-dependent triplet contributions, and they highlight the need to clarify the mechanism of the $pd\to ^3$He$\,\pi^0$ subprocess at energies above 1 GeV.
