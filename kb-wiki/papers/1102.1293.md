# Ab initio coupled-cluster theory for open-shell nuclei

> We develop a new method to describe properties of truly open-shell nuclei. This method is based on single-reference coupled-cluster theory and the equation-of-motion method with extensions to nuclei with $A\pm 2$ nucleons outside a closed shell. We perform proof-of-principle calculations for the ground states of the helium isotopes $^{3-6}$He and the first excited $2^+$ state in $^6$He. The comparison with exact results from matrix diagonalization in small model spaces demonstrates the accuracy of the coupled-cluster methods. Three-particle--one-hole excitations of $^4$He play an important role for the accurate description of $^6$He. For the open-shell nucleus $^6$He, the computational cost of the method is comparable with the coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles approximation while its accuracy is similar to coupled-cluster with singles, doubles and triples excitations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new equation-of-motion coupled-cluster method for nuclei with two nucleons outside a closed shell, claiming that for the open-shell nucleus 6He the computational cost is comparable to the coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (CCSD) approximation while its accuracy is similar to coupled-cluster with singles, doubles and triples excitations (CCSDT), but only within small model spaces where exact comparison is possible.

## Method

The authors extend single-reference coupled-cluster theory using equation-of-motion (EOM) techniques to describe nuclei with A±2 nucleons relative to a closed-shell reference. They derive the algebraic expressions and diagrammatic representations for the two-particle attached (2PA) and two-particle removed (2PR) EOM-CCSD methods, truncating the excitation operators at the 2p-0h/0p-2h level and the 3p-1h/1p-3h level. The method is tested by performing proof-of-principle calculations for helium isotopes 3-6He in small model spaces and comparing with full configuration interaction (FCI) results.

## Key numbers

- Ground states of 3-6He and the first excited 2+ state in 6He were computed as proof-of-principle calculations
- The computational cost of 2PA-EOM-CCSD(3p-1h) scales as n_u^5 n_o, where n_u is the number of unoccupied orbitals and n_o is the number of occupied orbitals
- The computational cost of 2PR-EOM-CCSD(1p-3h) scales as n_u^2 n_o^4
- For comparison, single-reference CCSD scales as n_u^4 n_o^2 and CCSDT scales as n_u^5 n_o^3

## Context

This work extends coupled-cluster methods, previously applied to doubly magic nuclei and nuclei differing by one nucleon (Hagen 2010, Hagen 2010a), to truly open-shell nuclei with A±2 nucleons. The EOM-CC approach for two-particle attachment/removal had seen very few applications in quantum chemistry (Nooijen and Bartlett, Wladyslawski) and is new in nuclear physics. The method enables first-principles studies of shell evolution across isotopic chains such as oxygen (14-28O) and calcium, and could provide effective single-particle energies and two-body interactions for constructing shell-model interactions as done by Hjorth-Jensen (1995).
