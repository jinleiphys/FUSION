# Anisotropic flow of strange particles in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies

> Anisotropic flow of K's, anti-K's, and lambdas is studied in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. At SPS energy the directed flow of kaons differs considerably at midrapidity from that of antikaons, while at RHIC energy kaon and antikaon flows coincide. The change is attributed to formation of dense meson-dominated matter at RHIC, where the differences in interaction cross-sections of hadrons become unimportant. The directed flows of strange particles, $v_1^{K,\bar{K}, Λ}(y)$, have universal negative slope at $|y| \leq 2$ at RHIC. The elliptic flow of strange hadrons is developed at midrapidity at times 3<t<10 fm/c. It increases almost linearly with rising p_t and stops to rise at p_t < 1.5 GeV/c reaching the same saturation value $v_2 ^{K,Λ}(p_t) \approx 10%$ in accord with experimental results.

# Anisotropic flow of strange particles in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies

## Key claim
At RHIC energies, the directed flow of kaons, antikaons, and lambdas all exhibit a universal negative slope at midrapidity (|y| ≤ 2), and their elliptic flow saturates at the same value of approximately 10% for transverse momenta above 1.5 GeV/c, attributed to the formation of dense meson-dominated matter where differences in hadronic interaction cross-sections become unimportant.

## Method
The authors employed the microscopic quark-gluon string model (QGSM), based on Gribov-Regge theory with string phenomenology for particle production in inelastic hadron-hadron collisions, to simulate minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy (Elab = 160 AGeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (√s = 130 AGeV). Anisotropic flow coefficients v1 (directed flow) and v2 (elliptic flow) were extracted via Fourier expansion of the azimuthal particle distribution as a function of rapidity and transverse momentum, with time evolution tracked at t = 3 fm/c, t = 10 fm/c, and at the final stage of the reaction.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC, directed flow v1 for K, anti-K, and Λ has a universal negative slope at |y| ≤ 2
- Elliptic flow v2 for strange hadrons at RHIC saturates at v2 ≈ 10% for pT ≥ 1.5 GeV/c
- Elliptic flow rises linearly with pT in the interval 0.25 ≤ pT ≤ 1.5 GeV/c
- Elliptic flow is close to zero for hadrons with pT ≤ 0.25 GeV/c
- Elliptic flow develops at midrapidity during the time interval 3 ≤ t ≤ 10 fm/c at both SPS and RHIC
- At SPS, directed flow of antikaons shows antiflow slope at midrapidity while kaon directed flow is close to zero

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions (Ollitrault 1992) and builds on macroscopic hydrodynamic and microscopic transport simulations showing early saturation of elliptic flow (Sorge 1997, Heiselberg and Levy 1999, Zhang et al. 1999, Kolb et al. 2001). The QGSM model had previously successfully described elliptic flow of charged particles at RHIC (Bravina et al. 2001) compared to experimental measurements (STAR Collaboration 2001). The results enable using strange particle flow as a probe of the early-stage dense matter formed at RHIC, with the universal behavior of kaon and antikaon flow suggesting that nuclear shadowing can mimic QGP formation signatures.
