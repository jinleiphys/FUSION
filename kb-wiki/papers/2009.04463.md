# Wave Functions of Pentadiagonal Matrices in the Weak Coupling Limit

> We consider a pentadiagonal matrix which will be described in the text. We demonstrate practical methods for obtaining weak coupling expressions for the lowest eigenvector in terms of the parameters in the matrix, v and w. It is found that the expressions simplify if the wave function coefficients are put in the denominator.

# Wave Functions of Pentadiagonal Matrices in the Weak Coupling Limit

## Key claim
The paper presents practical methods for obtaining weak coupling expressions for the lowest eigenvector of a pentadiagonal matrix with two parameters v and w, finding that the expressions simplify significantly when wave function coefficients are expressed with denominators rather than numerators. The results are shown to be valid only when both v and w are much smaller than the energy separations on the diagonal (the weak coupling limit).

## Method
The author constructs an 11x11 pentadiagonal matrix with diagonal energies E_n = nE and off-diagonal parameters v (nearest neighbor) and w (next-nearest neighbor). For the weak coupling limit, small numerical values of v and w are chosen (v = n x 10^{-4}, w = m x 10^{-8}), and Mathematica is used to obtain eigenvectors numerically. The eigenvector components are then fitted to analytic formulas, and the coefficients are verified by enumerating all possible perturbation theory paths and summing their energy denominators.

## Key numbers
- For the tridiagonal case (w=0) with v/E=0.01, the eigenvector components follow the pattern 1, -v/E, (v/E)^2/2, -(v/E)^3/6, ... up to (-1)^n (v/E)^n/n!
- For v=0.1, w=0: weak coupling a_0=1, a_1=-0.1, a_2=0.5000E-2, a_3=-0.1667E-3, a_4=0.4167E-5, a_5=-0.8333E-7, a_6=0.1389E-8, a_7=-0.1984E-10, a_8=0.2480E-12, a_9=-0.2756E-14, a_10=0.2756E-16
- For v=0.1, w=0: exact results give a_0=0.951, a_1=-0.9902E-1, a_2=0.4934E-2, a_3=-0.1641E-3, a_4=0.4094E-5, a_5=-0.8174E-7, a_6=0.1360E-8, a_7=-0.1941E-10, a_8=0.2435E-12, a_9=-0.2690E-14, a_10=0.2687E-16
- For v=0, w=0.1: weak coupling a_2=-0.5000E-1, a_4=0.1250E-2, a_6=-0.2084E-4, a_8=0.2604E-6, a_10=-0.2604E-8 (all odd components vanish)
- For v=0.1, w=0.1: weak coupling a_10=-0.1387E-8 versus exact a_10=-0.1427E-8
- For v=0.1, w=-0.1: weak coupling a_10=0.3395E-8 versus exact a_10=0.3987E-8

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Zamick and collaborators (Kingan and Zamick, 2019; Wolfe and Zamick, 2020) that examined tridiagonal, pentadiagonal, and heptadiagonal matrices, but previously only for the special case w=v. The analytic expressions for the pentadiagonal case with two independent parameters v and w are new. The author connects this schematic matrix work to realistic nuclear shell model calculations (Zamick, 2020) where magnetic dipole strength from the ground state was found to decrease exponentially with excitation energy. The results enable further investigation of whether the simple exponential behavior observed in realistic calculations is a general property of such matrices, and the author notes that the association between simplified models and realistic calculations needs more work.
