# Comparing event generator predictions and ab-initio calculations of $ν$-$^{12}$C neutral current quasi-elastic scattering at 1 GeV

> The measurement of neutrino oscillations and exotic physics searches are important parts of the physics program in the near future, with new state-of-the-art experiments planned within the next decade. Future and modern experiments in these fields will make use of nuclear targets. Event Generators (EGs) are software used in the analysis of neutrino oscillation experiments. EGs use to predict kinematic observables for a range of neutrino energies. These simulations may lack physics captured by more rigorous theoretical calculations. This work compares EG performance to nuclear theory calculations by comparing observables generated in the two frameworks. We provide a common set of definitions between theory and experiment and assess the physics contained in EG simulations. Neutral current quasi-elastic (NCQE) scattering events for neutrinos and anti-neutrinos on a $^{12}$C target are simulated with a specific EG, NEUT, used by the T2K experiment for its analysis. The simulated cross sections are compared to analytic calculations from nuclear theory within the factorization scheme. We compare the NEUT implementation of two different models on nuclear spectral functions: the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) and the correlated basis spectral function (CBF) to analytic calculations of the same models in the factorization scheme. For both nuclear physics models, we compare the appearance of features in the distributions relevant to experimental analyses. Qualitatively, the shape of the simulated distribution is similar to the one obtained through theory calculations; however, there are some discrepancies between the theory calculations and the NEUT simulation. While the EG simulations and analytic calculations with the same model of nuclear dynamics show similar overall features, there are still differences between the two.

## Key claim

While event generator (EG) simulations and analytic calculations using the same nuclear model (RFG or CBF) show similar overall features for neutral current quasi-elastic scattering on carbon-12 at 1 GeV, there remain 10-20% discrepancies in cross section magnitude and tail strength that could affect predicted event rates and interaction model uncertainties in oscillation and exotic physics searches.

## Method

The authors compared NEUT v5.3.6 (with modified form factors, axial mass, binding energy, and Pauli blocking disabled) to analytic factorization-scheme calculations for neutral current quasi-elastic (NCQE) scattering of neutrinos and anti-neutrinos on a 12C target at a fixed beam energy of 1 GeV. Both frameworks implemented the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) and correlated basis spectral function (CBF) models of nuclear dynamics. NEUT generated event-by-event Monte Carlo samples (5-15 million events per configuration), which were binned in energy transfer ω with 20 MeV bins and converted to double-differential cross sections using the NUISANCE framework, then compared to analytic calculations at fixed scattering angles of 15°, 30°, 60°, 70°, and 120°.

## Key numbers

- For ν-12C at θ=15° using RFG: NEUT peak dσ/dΩdω = 15.81 ± 0.08 fm²/MeV/sr; theory peak = 16.714 fm²/MeV/sr
- For ν-12C at θ=120° using RFG: NEUT peak = 0.193 ± 0.003 fm²/MeV/sr; theory peak = 0.159 fm²/MeV/sr
- For ν-12C at θ=15° using CBF: NEUT peak = 15.46 ± 0.08 fm²/MeV/sr; theory peak = 14.350 fm²/MeV/sr
- For ν-12C at θ=30° using CBF: NEUT peak = 5.85 ± 0.03 fm²/MeV/sr; theory peak = 5.015 fm²/MeV/sr
- For anti-ν-12C at θ=120° using RFG: NEUT peak = 0.0036 ± 0.0002 fm²/MeV/sr; theory peak = 0.0099 fm²/MeV/sr (NEUT is 30% of theory)
- Tail strength r_tail for ν-12C at θ=15° using CBF: NEUT = 10%; theory = 16%
- Tail strength r_tail for anti-ν-12C at θ=120° using CBF: NEUT = 3%; theory = 7%
- Axial mass set to MA = 1.03 GeV; Fermi coupling constant GF = 1.1803 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²; nucleon axial-vector coupling gA = 1.2694; Weinberg angle sin²θW = 0.2312

## Context

This work extends comparisons between event generators and ab-initio nuclear theory, following the factorization-scheme calculations of Rocco (2019) for neutral- and charged-current cross sections and the Green's function Monte Carlo work of Lovato (2018). It specifically benchmarks the NEUT generator used by T2K against analytic calculations with the same nuclear models (RFG and the CBF spectral function of Benhar (1994)). The results demonstrate the need for systematic benchmarking of EGs against rigorous theory to identify missing physics and improve simulations for future oscillation experiments like DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, as well as for exotic physics searches.
