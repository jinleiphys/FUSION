# Ab initio investigation of the $^7$Li($p,e^+e^-$)$^8$Be process and the X17 boson

> Observations of anomalies in the electron-positron angular correlations in high-energy decays in $^4$He, $^8$Be, and $^{12}$C have been reported recently by the ATOMKI collaboration. These could be explained by the creation and subsequent decay of a new boson with a mass of ${\sim}17$ MeV. Theoretical understanding of pair creation in the proton capture reactions used in these experiments is important for the interpretation of the anomalies. We apply the ab initio No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) to the proton capture on $^7$Li. The NCSMC describes both bound and unbound states in light nuclei in a unified way with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions as the only input. We investigate the structure of $^8$Be, the $p+^7$Li elastic scattering, the $^7$Li($p,γ$)$^8$Be cross section and the internal pair creation $^7$Li($p,e^+ e^-$)$^8$Be. We discuss the impact of a proper treatment of the initial scattering state on the electron-positron angular correlation spectrum and compare our results to available ATOMKI data sets. Finally, we calculate $^7$Li($p,X$)$^8$Be cross sections for several proposed models of the hypothetical X17 particle.

# Ab initio investigation of the \(^7\)Li(\(p,e^+e^-\))\(^8\)Be process and the X17 boson

## Key claim

The ATOMKI collaboration's observed anomaly in electron-positron angular correlations at approximately 140 degrees in the \(^7\)Li(\(p,e^+e^-\))\(^8\)Be reaction could be explained by a new boson (X17) of mass approximately 17 MeV/\(c^2\), but the present ab initio calculations show that proper treatment of the initial scattering state and nuclear structure effects significantly impacts the predicted angular correlation spectrum, and the anomaly is not reproduced by standard nuclear physics alone.

## Method

The authors applied the ab initio No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) to describe the \(^7\)Li+\(p\) system, using chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N\(^3\)LO with a 500 MeV cutoff plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions at N\(^2\)LO, softened via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to \(\lambda_{\mathrm{SRG}}=2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\). They computed \(^8\)Be bound and resonant states, \(p+^7\)Li elastic scattering eigenphase shifts, the radiative capture cross section \(^7\)Li(\(p,\gamma\))\(^8\)Be, and the internal pair creation process \(^7\)Li(\(p,e^+e^-\))\(^8\)Be, then compared their results to ATOMKI data sets from 2016/2019 and 2022. They also calculated cross sections for hypothetical X17 boson production assuming vector, pseudoscalar (axion-like), and axial vector couplings.

## Key numbers

- The NCSM extrapolated ground state energies are \(-38.4(2)\) MeV for \(^7\)Li, \(-36.7(2)\) MeV for \(^7\)Be, and \(-55.6(3)\) MeV for \(^8\)Be, compared to experimental values of \(-39.245\) MeV, \(-37.60\) MeV, and \(-56.50\) MeV respectively (about 2 percent underbinding).
- The NCSMC ground state of \(^8\)Be lies at \(-16.13\) MeV relative to the \(^7\)Li+\(p\) threshold in the \(N_{\mathrm{max}}=8\) space, compared to the experimental value of \(-17.25\) MeV.
- The first \(1^+\) resonance (experimental 17.64 MeV state) has NCSMC centroid at 0.734 MeV above threshold with width 0.0899 MeV, while the experimental values are 0.385(1) MeV and 0.0107(5) MeV.
- The second \(1^+\) resonance (experimental 18.15 MeV state) has NCSMC centroid at 1.098 MeV above threshold with width 0.1332 MeV, compared to experimental 0.895(4) MeV and 0.138(6) MeV.
- The NCSMC-pheno (phenomenologically shifted) second \(1^+\) resonance centroid is 0.905 MeV with width 0.1083 MeV, close to experiment.
- The hypothetical X17 boson mass is approximately 17 MeV/\(c^2\) as reported by ATOMKI.

## Context

This work extends the ab initio NCSMC approach to simultaneously describe bound states, scattering, radiative capture, and internal pair creation in the \(^8\)Be system, going beyond previous R-matrix analyses by Hayes (2022) and the hyperspherical harmonics calculations by Viviani (2022) for the \(p+^3\)H system. The calculations provide a framework to test the X17 boson hypothesis against standard nuclear physics explanations, and enable future comparisons with independent experimental searches such as those by NA64 at CERN and proposed experiments at the CERN SPS. The authors note that the \(^4\)He+\(^4\)He mass partition was not included, which may affect the description of states below the \(^7\)Li+\(p\) threshold.
