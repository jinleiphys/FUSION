# Meson-nucleus bound states in quark meson coupling model

> The formations of the $K(\bar{K})$, $D(\bar{D})$, and $B(\bar{B})$ meson-nucleus bound states in ${\rm{^{16}O}}$, ${\rm{^{40}Ca}}$, ${\rm{^{90}Zr}}$, ${\rm{^{197}Au}}$, and ${\rm{^{208}Pb}}$ nucleus are investigated using the quark meson coupling model. The model relies on a mean field description of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent interactions of scalar ($σ$, $δ$) and vector ($ω$, $ρ$) mesons with the (anti)quarks inside the bags, which is further extended to explore the properties of nuclei. We estimate the meson-nucleus bound state energies by solving the Klein-Gordon equations with the real potentials calculated self-consistently within the model, using a coordinate space approach. The calculations are carried out for different nuclear interactions. The effects of Coulomb interaction are considered in the present study for the charged mesons. Our study indicates the formation of rather deeply bound $B$-mesic states at the very central region of the nuclei, compared to the $D$ and $K$ mesons, offering a more promising probe to explore subtle nuclear medium effects. The investigations of such bound states are of particular interest for the upcoming $\rm{\bar{P}ANDA}$ at FAIR, J-PARC-E29, and JLab experiments.

## Key claim

The quark meson coupling model predicts the formation of meson-nucleus bound states for K, D, and B mesons with several target nuclei, with B-mesic states being more deeply bound than D or K states, but the actual binding depends on the self-consistent treatment of scalar and vector mean fields and the inclusion of the scalar-isovector delta meson, which introduces isospin symmetry breaking effects in asymmetric nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for nucleons in finite nuclei within the quark meson coupling model, treating nucleons as non-overlapping MIT bags whose light quarks interact directly with mean scalar (sigma, delta) and vector (omega, rho) meson fields. The meson-nucleus potentials are constructed from the self-consistently calculated mean fields, and the Klein-Gordon equation is solved in coordinate space to obtain bound state energies for K, D, and B mesons in 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 197Au, and 208Pb nuclei, including Coulomb effects for charged mesons.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant B = (170.2416 MeV)^4 used for all hadron calculations
- Proton bag radius fixed at 0.8 fm; neutron bag radius obtained as 0.8004 fm
- Current quark masses used: m_u = 2.16 MeV, m_d = 4.67 MeV, m_s = 93.4 MeV, m_c = 1270 MeV, m_b = 4180 MeV
- Nucleon-meson couplings: g_sigma = 6.26, g_omega = 8.17, g_delta = 18.63, g_rho = 9.33
- Sigma meson mass m_sigma = 418 MeV; delta meson mass m_delta = 1000 MeV
- Computational mesh: maximum radius 15 fm, mesh size 0.01 fm, convergence achieved within 5-6 iterations

## Context

This work extends the quark meson coupling model, originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and developed by Krein, Saito, and Thomas, to study meson-nucleus bound states beyond the pionic states first predicted by Friedman and Soff and observed by the GSI-S160 Collaboration (1996). The inclusion of the scalar-isovector delta meson, following Saito (1994) and Patra, addresses isospin symmetry breaking in asymmetric nuclei. The results are relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, J-PARC-E29, and JLab experiments aiming to produce and detect mesic-nucleus bound states.
