# Impact of hadronic and nuclear corrections on global analysis of spin-dependent parton distributions

> We present the first results of a new global next-to-leading order analysis of spin-dependent parton distribution functions from the most recent world data on inclusive polarized deep-inelastic scattering, focusing in particular on the large-x and low-Q^2 regions. By directly fitting polarization asymmetries we eliminate biases introduced by using polarized structure function data extracted under nonuniform assumptions for the unpolarized structure functions. For analysis of the large-x data we implement nuclear smearing corrections for deuterium and 3He nuclei, and systematically include target mass and higher twist corrections to the g_1 and g_2 structure functions at low Q^2. We also explore the effects of Q^2 and W^2 cuts in the data sets, and the potential impact of future data on the behavior of the spin-dependent parton distributions at intermediate and large x.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first results of a new global next-to-leading order (NLO) analysis of spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) that directly fits polarization asymmetries rather than pre-extracted structure functions, thereby eliminating biases from nonuniform assumptions about unpolarized structure functions. The analysis focuses on the large-\(x\) and low-\(Q^2\) regions, where the authors systematically include nuclear smearing corrections for deuterium and \(^3\)He nuclei as well as target mass and higher twist corrections, with the hedge that the results are primarily valid for intermediate and large \(x\) and that sea quark flavor decomposition and gluon polarization at small \(x\) require future work with semi-inclusive DIS and polarized hadron–hadron scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed a global NLO QCD analysis of inclusive polarized deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data from CERN (EMC, SMC, COMPASS), SLAC (E130, E142, E143, E154, E155, E155x), DESY (HERMES), and Jefferson Lab (Hall A and Hall B experiments), fitting the measured longitudinal and transverse polarization asymmetries directly rather than using extracted \(g_1\) structure functions. They implemented nuclear smearing corrections for deuterium and \(^3\)He targets within the weak binding approximation, and included target mass corrections (TMCs) as well as twist-3 and twist-4 higher twist corrections to the \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) structure functions at low \(Q^2\). The PDFs were parametrized at an input scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) using a standard functional form, with constraints from weak baryon decay matrix elements, and uncertainties were evaluated using the Hessian method with \(\Delta\chi^2 = 1\).

## Key numbers

* The nominal kinematic cuts used in the JAM analysis are \(Q^2 \geq 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 \geq 3.5\) GeV\(^2\).
* The total number of data points within these cuts is 1043, with an overall \(\chi^2/N_{\rm dat} = 0.98\) for the full JAM fit and 1.07 for the leading-twist-only fit.
* The first moments of the quark distributions are constrained by weak baryon decay relations: \(\Delta u^{+(1)} - \Delta d^{+(1)} = 1.269 \pm 0.003\) and \(\Delta u^{+(1)} + \Delta d^{+(1)} - 2 \Delta s^{+(1)} = 0.586 \pm 0.031\).
* The resonance region boundary is defined by \(W \geq W_{\rm res} \approx (1.8-2.0)\) GeV, which at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) limits the DIS region to \(x \lesssim 0.25\), and at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\) to \(x \lesssim 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends previous global spin-dependent PDF analyses by the DSSV (de Florian, Sassot, Stratmann and Vogelsang, 2009), LSS (Leader, Sidorov and Stamenov, 2010), BB (Blumlein and Bottcher, 2010), and AAC (Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration, 2009) groups, which typically fitted extracted structure functions rather than raw asymmetries and did not consistently apply nuclear smearing corrections at large \(x\). The JAM analysis enables more reliable extraction of spin-dependent PDFs at intermediate and large \(x\) by incorporating nuclear corrections shown to be essential in unpolarized PDF analyses (such as the CJ12 and JR-new fits), and by systematically including finite-\(Q^2\) corrections that are particularly important given the limited \(Q^2\) range of polarized DIS data. Future work will extend the analysis to sea quark flavor decomposition and gluon polarization using semi-inclusive DIS and polarized proton–proton scattering data.
