# Microscopic Description of Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in $^{48}$Ca

> The properties of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) for the double magic $^{48}$Ca are analyzed in the framework of a microscopic model based on Skyrme-type interactions. A method for simultaneously taking into account the coupling between one-, two-, and three-phonon terms in the wave functions of $0^{+}$ states has been developed. The inclusion of three-phonon configurations leads to a substantial redistribution of the ISGMR strength to lower energy $0^{+}$ states and also higher energy tail. Our results demonstrate that the developed approach enables to us to describe a gross structure of the ISGMR spreading width.

## Key claim

The inclusion of three-phonon configurations (beyond the usual one- and two-phonon coupling) substantially redistributes the ISGMR strength in $^{48}$Ca, shifting about 1% of the total monopole strength into the lower energy region ($E_x < 10.0$ MeV) and about 3% into the higher energy region ($E_x > 25.5$ MeV), while the main peak itself is shifted downwards by 700 keV compared to RPA.

## Method

The authors start from a Hartree-Fock (HF) calculation with the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional, then solve the random phase approximation (RPA) equations using the finite rank separable approximation (FRSA). They extend the wave functions of $0^+$ states to include linear combinations of one-, two-, and three-RPA phonon configurations (the PPC3 approach), with all two- and three-phonon configurations below 30 MeV built from phonons of multipoles $\lambda^\pi = 0^+, 1^-, 2^+, 3^-, 4^+, 5^-$ coupled to $J^\pi = 0^+$. The coupling matrix elements between one- and two-phonon configurations are computed, and the resulting linear equations are solved to obtain excitation energies and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The RPA calculation yields the lowest $0^+$ state above 14 MeV, with three dominant states at 18.3, 19.4, and 22.6 MeV exhausting 89.4% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) and about 79.3% of the total monopole strength below 30 MeV.
- The PPC2 (one- and two-phonon coupling) produces a single peak at 17.4 MeV exhausting 13.3% of the EWSR, and shifts 0.6% of the total monopole strength to low energies and 8.6% to high energies.
- The PPC3 (including three-phonon coupling) shifts the main peak by 1800 keV towards higher energies compared to PPC2, and shifts 0.6% of the total monopole strength from the ISGMR region to lower energies ($E_x < 10.0$ MeV) while pumping 5.8% from the high-energy tail back into the resonance region (10.0–25.5 MeV).
- The PPC3 centroid energy $m_1/m_0$ over 10.0–25.5 MeV is 19.1 MeV, compared to RPA (19.8 MeV), RCNP experiment (18.01 ± 0.10 MeV), and iThemba LABS experiment (18.40 ± 0.13 MeV).
- The PPC3 spreading width increases from 5.1 MeV (RPA) to 7.8 MeV; the experimental ISGMR width from TAMU is $6.68_{-0.36}^{+0.31}$ MeV (over 9.5–40.0 MeV) and from iThemba LABS is 7.78 MeV (over 12.0–24.0 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phonon–phonon coupling (PPC) approach (Severyukhin 2017, Arsenyev 2021) from heavy and superheavy nuclei to the medium-mass nucleus $^{48}$Ca, and goes beyond the standard RPA and two-phonon coupling by including three-phonon configurations. The results are compared with recent experimental ISGMR strength distributions from RCNP (Howard 2020) and iThemba LABS (Olorunfunmi 2022), as well as earlier TAMU data (Lui 2011). The approach enables a microscopic description of the gross structure of the ISGMR spreading width and provides a framework for studying the fragmentation of monopole strength in lighter nuclei where the resonance is not concentrated in a single peak.
