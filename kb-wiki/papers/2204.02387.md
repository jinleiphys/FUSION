# Probing the nuclear deformation with three-particle asymmetric cumulant in RHIC isobar runs

> $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV provide unique opportunities to study the geometry and fluctuations raised from the deformation of the colliding nuclei. Using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, we predict ${\rm ac}_{2}\{3\}$ ratios between these two collision systems and demonstrate that the ratios of ${\rm ac}_{2}\{3\}$, as well as the ratios of the involving flow harmonics and event-plane correlations, are sensitive to quadrupole and octupole deformations, which could provide strong constrains on the shape differences between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr. We also study the nonlinear response coefficients $χ_{4,22}$, which show insensitivity to the deformation effect.

# Probing the nuclear deformation with three-particle asymmetric cumulant in RHIC isobar runs

## Key claim

The ratio of the three-particle asymmetric cumulant ac2{3} between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV is predicted to be highly sensitive to the quadrupole deformation β2 of Ru and the octupole deformation β3 of Zr, provided that the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with tuned parameters is a valid description of the collision dynamics. The authors emphasize that their study is not aimed at precise quantitative prediction but at demonstrating the sensitivity of this observable to nuclear deformation.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU event-by-event hybrid model, which combines (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma phase with the UrQMD hadron cascade for the subsequent hadronic stage. Initial conditions were generated with the Trento model using Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions that include quadrupole (β2) and octupole (β3) deformation parameters for Ru and Zr, with parameter sets taken from density functional theory calculations. The three-particle asymmetric cumulant ac2{3} and related flow harmonics were calculated using the standard Q-cumulant method for charged particles with 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |η| < 2.

## Key numbers

- The iEBE-VISHNU parameters include: (η/s)min = 0.134, Tswitch = 0.151 GeV, and normalization constant 6.6 GeV.
- Two parameter sets for Ru: Ru-para-I with β2 = 0.12, β3 = 0.00, R0 = 5.093 fm, a = 0.478 fm; Ru-para-II with β2 = 0.16, β3 = 0.00, R0 = 5.093 fm, a = 0.471 fm.
- Two parameter sets for Zr: Zr-para-I with β2 = 0.00, β3 = 0.16, R0 = 5.021 fm, a = 0.524 fm; Zr-para-II with β2 = 0.00, β3 = 0.20, R0 = 5.021 fm, a = 0.517 fm.
- The experimental dataset contains about 2 billion events per collision system, while the model study used about 150 million events per system (3 million hydrodynamic events × 50 UrQMD oversamplings).
- The nonlinear response coefficient χ4,22 ratios are consistent with unity within errors, indicating insensitivity to nuclear deformation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the RHIC isobar run (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at 200 GeV), which was originally designed to search for the chiral magnetic effect but revealed unexpected differences in multiplicity and flow between the two systems (STAR Collaboration, 2021). Prior studies used elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 ratios to constrain deformation (Zhang, 2021; Xu, 2021), while this paper proposes the three-particle asymmetric cumulant ac2{3} as a more sensitive observable. The results enable simultaneous constraints on quadrupole and octupole deformations of Ru and Zr, complementing other proposed probes such as multiplicity distribution differences (Li, 2019), mean transverse momentum (Xu, 2021), and spectator neutrons (Liu, 2022).
