# Holographic Monopole Catalysis of Baryon Decay

> We study how monopole catalysis of baryon decay is realized in holographic QCD. Physics of monopole catalysis becomes much simpler in holographic description as it occurs due to the violation of the Bianchi identity for the 5D gauge symmetry when magnetic monopole is present. In holographic QCD we find a unified picture of the baryon number violation under magnetic monopole or electroweak sphaleron, giving a new mechanism of baryon number violation. We also embed our set-up in the string theory model by Sakai and Sugimoto.

## Key claim

The paper shows that monopole catalysis of baryon decay in holographic QCD occurs due to violation of the Bianchi identity for the 5D gauge symmetry when a magnetic monopole is present, providing a unified picture of baryon number violation under both magnetic monopoles and electroweak sphalerons. The authors hedge that this analysis applies in the large number of colors and large 't Hooft coupling limit, and that the decay rate is determined by the scale of instanton solitons rather than the monopole scale.

## Method

The authors work within the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, where baryons are realized as instanton solitons in a 5D flavor gauge theory on D8 branes. They introduce a magnetic monopole as a monopole-string extending along the radial (5th) direction, and compute the violation of the baryon number current by evaluating the Bianchi identity in the presence of this monopole background. The analysis is performed by expanding the 5D gauge field into normalizable modes (including pions and vector mesons) and non-normalizable background fields, then integrating the 5D baryon current along the radial direction to obtain the 4D baryon number violation rate.

## Key numbers

* The baryon number change rate from monopole catalysis is given by \( dB/dt = (1/\pi F_\pi)(\partial_t \pi^0) \), where \( F_\pi \) is the pion decay constant and \( \pi^0 \) is the neutral pion field at the monopole center.
* The 5D baryon number current is \( B^\mu = (1/8\pi^2) \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} dz \, \epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta} \, \text{Tr}(F_{\nu\alpha}F_{\beta z}) \), with the instanton number (baryon number) defined as \( B = (1/32\pi^2) \int dz dx^3 \epsilon^{MNPQR} \text{Tr}(F_{MN}F_{PQ}) \).
* The electromagnetic charge matrix for \( N_F=2 \) is \( Q = \text{diag}(2/3, -1/3) \), and the monopole-string background satisfies \( \int_{S^2} F^{EM} = -2\pi i \).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of monopole catalysis of baryon decay originally studied by Rubakov (1981) and Callan (1982) in the skyrmion picture, and specifically builds on the treatment by Callan and Witten (1983) of how Dirac monopoles cause skyrmion decay. The holographic approach provides a simpler description where baryon number violation arises from violation of the Bianchi identity, and unifies this with baryon number violation by electroweak sphalerons. The authors also embed their setup in the Sakai-Sugimoto string theory model (2004), where the monopole catalysis corresponds to dissolution of D4 branes (instanton baryons) into a D6 brane (monopole string), suggesting the process occurs without any barrier and holds beyond the large \( N_c \) limit.
