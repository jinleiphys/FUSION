# At the edge of shape coexistence in the Z=40 region

> In this contribution, the shape coexistence phenomenon near the proton sub-shell closure at Z=40 is analyzed. Particular emphasis is placed on extracting the nuclear deformation values by examining experimental B(E2) transition rates and observing how the kinematic moment of inertia evolves in these nuclei. Based on the analysis of these two observables, we arrive at the conclusion that the notable effects of shape coexistence at approximately Z=40 are largely diminished in Mo isotopes and are scarcely detectable in Ru isotopes.

# At the edge of shape coexistence in the Z=40 region

## Key claim
The notable effects of shape coexistence near the proton sub-shell closure at Z=40 are largely diminished in Mo isotopes and are scarcely detectable in Ru isotopes, based on analysis of experimental B(E2) transition rates and kinematic moment of inertia evolution. The authors qualify that while Sr and Zr show clear crossing of regular and intruder configurations, the strong interaction between these configurations in Mo smooths out the variation in deformation, and in Ru the impact of shape coexistence is minimal.

## Method
The authors extracted the quadrupole deformation parameter β from experimental B(E2: 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) values using the geometrical model formula β = 4π√(B(E2))/(3⟨I_i 0 2 0|I_f 0⟩ Z e R₀²) with R₀ = 1.2 A¹/³ fm. They also computed the kinematic moment of inertia J⁽¹⁾ from yrast band energies using the approximation J⁽¹⁾ ≈ ħ²(2J-1)/E_γ(J → (J-2)). These observables were analyzed for isotopic chains of Sr, Zr, Mo, and Ru as a function of neutron number, with regular and intruder configurations identified using the Interacting Boson Model with Configuration Mixing (IBM-CM).

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameter β for Sr and Zr shows a clear rapid onset at neutron number N=60, while for Mo the increase in β is more gradual, and for Ru the β value remains nearly constant across the isotopic chain.
- The crossing of regular and intruder configurations occurs around neutron number N=60 for Sr, Zr, and Mo isotopes, with Ru showing the closest approach between configurations at the same N=60 but no crossing.
- For Sr isotopes from N=60 onward, the kinematic moment of inertia demonstrates a relatively smooth trend indicative of rotational structure; for N=58, lowest angular momenta show vibrational-like structure while highest angular momenta exhibit rotational characteristics.
- For Zr and Mo isotopes from N=60 onward, there is clear presence of rotational structure; for N=54-60, observed backbending suggests coexistence of two structures within the yrast band.
- For Ru isotopes, the highest angular momenta exhibit an upward trend suggesting vibrational structure, while lowest angular momenta maintain a relatively horizontal trend typical of more deformed structure.

## Context
This work extends previous IBM-CM studies of Sr (Maya-Barbecho 2022), Zr (García-Ramos 2019), and Mo and Ru isotopes (Maya-Barbecho 2023) by focusing on deformation extraction from experimental data rather than model predictions. The analysis connects shape coexistence phenomena to Quantum Phase Transitions (QPTs), building on the framework established by Cejnar and Jolie (2009) and the Type II QPT classification developed by Gavrielov and collaborators (2019, 2022). The results enable future studies to distinguish between first-order Type II QPTs (Sr and Zr at N=60), second-order Type II QPTs (Mo), and second-order Type I QPTs (Ru at N=60), providing a systematic mapping of how shape coexistence evolves as one moves away from the Z=40 subshell closure.
