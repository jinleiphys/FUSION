# Width of a two-body coupled-channel resonance

> We study the width of a two-body resonance in a coupled-channel system. We demonstrate how the width does not come only determined by the available phase space for its decay to the detection channel, but it greatly depends on the relative position of the mass of the resonance with respect to the masses of the coupled-channels generating the state. Our results are consistent with the experimental observation of narrow hadrons lying well above their lowest decay threshold.

# Width of a two-body coupled-channel resonance

## Key claim

The width of a two-body resonance in a coupled-channel system is not determined solely by the available phase space for decay to the detection channel, but depends substantially on the relative position of the resonance mass with respect to the masses of the coupled channels that generate the state; narrow hadrons can therefore exist well above their lowest decay threshold, consistent with experimental observations of states such as the LHCb pentaquark Pc+(4380) and the X(3872).

## Method

The authors model the system as a coupled-channel problem obeying the non-relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation for S-wave interactions, with two channels: a lower-mass channel (particles with masses m1 and m2) and an upper-mass channel (particles with masses m3 and m4). The two-body potentials are parametrized as an attractive plus a repulsive Yukawa term, and the resonance width is calculated using the Breit-Wigner formula at the energy where the phase shift reaches 90 degrees. By varying the repulsive term in the lower channel or the coupling strength, the bound state of the coupled system is moved into the continuum and its width is studied as a function of its position relative to the two thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The upper channel is 25.6 MeV above the lower one in the primary calculation (Fig. 1).
- In a second scenario, the mass difference between upper and lower thresholds is reduced to 5.6 MeV (Fig. 2).
- The lower LHCb pentaquark Pc+(4380) has a mass of 4380 +/- 8 +/- 29 MeV and decays to J/Psi p with a width Gamma = 205 +/- 18 +/- 86 MeV; the phase space is of order 345 MeV.
- The X(3872) has a width Gamma < 1.2 MeV, with a mass difference of 7-8 MeV between the D0 Dbar*0 and D+ Dbar*- thresholds.
- For a fixed energy with respect to the lower threshold of Delta E_Th = 6.5 MeV, the width increases when the upper threshold moves away (Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the study of coupled-channel hadronic resonances, as suggested in compilations by Chen (2017), Chen (2016), Esposito (2016), Richard (2016), and Hosaka (2016), and builds on earlier three-body calculations by Garcilazo (2017). The mechanism is related to the "synchronization of resonances" proposed by Bugg (2008) and is analogous to a Feshbach resonance, where the open channel (Q Qbar - n nbar) gets trapped in a molecular state supported by the closed channel potential (Q nbar - n Qbar), as discussed by Braaten (2004) and Pilkuhn (2014). The results enable a better understanding of narrow exotic states such as the X(3872) and the LHCb pentaquarks, and suggest that the decay width in a particular channel can hint at the upper threshold contributing to the formation of the resonance.
