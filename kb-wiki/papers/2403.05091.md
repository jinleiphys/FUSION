# Phenomenological model for $γγ^* \to K\bar{K}^*(892)$ constraining the $f_1(1420)$ transition form factor

> We present a phenomenological study of the $γγ^*\to K\bar{K}^*(892)$ process by including the $s$-channel production of the $η(1475)$ and $f_1(1420)$ resonances. The non-resonant channel via $K$- and $K^*$-exchanges is investigated carefully by performing the Lorentz tensor decomposition and is constructed to yield a correct high-energy Regge behavior. The transition form factor of $f_1(1420)$ is adjusted to achieve a reasonable description of the existing L3 data in the $f_1(1420)$ resonance region. This model is intended to serve as a Monte Carlo generator for the analysis currently being performed by the BESIII Collaboration. We also estimate the polarized $γγ^*\to K\bar{K}^*(892)$ cross sections and demonstrate how to extract the transition form factors of $f_1(1420)$ from the polarized cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model for the $\gamma\gamma^* \to K\bar{K}^*(892)$ process that includes both resonant ($\eta(1475)$ and $f_1(1420)$) and non-resonant ($K$- and $K^*$-exchange) contributions, with the explicit caveat that the $f_1(1420)$ transition form factor is constrained using only the helicity-averaged L3 data, while the separation of the two independent form factors $F^{TT}$ and $F^{TL}$ relies on the quark model relation $F^{TT} = -F^{TL}$ rather than direct experimental determination.

## Method

The authors constructed the $\gamma\gamma^* \to K\bar{K}^*(892)$ amplitude by combining $s$-channel $\eta(1475)$ and $f_1(1420)$ resonance production with $t$- and $u$-channel $K$ and $K^*$ exchange mechanisms, including contact terms to ensure electromagnetic gauge invariance. The non-resonant amplitude was expressed using Lorentz tensor decomposition and Reggeized propagators for $K$ and $K^*$ mesons to yield correct high-energy behavior. The $f_1(1420)$ transition form factor was parameterized in dipole form, with the dipole mass $\Lambda_{f_1}$ adjusted to describe L3 data on $e^+e^- \to e^+e^- K_S^0 K^\pm \pi^\mp$ events across four $Q^2$ bins.

## Key numbers

- $f_1(1420)$ mass: 1426.3 MeV, width: 54.5 MeV
- $\eta(1475)$ mass: 1475 MeV, width: 90 $\pm$ 9 MeV
- $\eta(1475) \to \gamma\gamma$ decay width: $\Gamma_{\bar{\eta}\to\gamma\gamma} \simeq 0.23$ keV, giving $F_{\bar{\eta}\gamma^*\gamma^*}(0,0) \simeq 0.0414$ GeV$^{-1}$
- $\eta(1475)$ transition form factor monopole mass: $\Lambda_{\bar{\eta}} = 1470$ MeV
- $f_1(1420)$ transition form factor normalization: $F_{f_1\to\gamma^*\gamma^*}^{TL}(0,0) = 0.401$, fixed from L3 value $\tilde{\Gamma}_{f_1\to\gamma\gamma} \times \Gamma_{f_1\to K\bar{K}\pi}/\Gamma_{f_1} = 3.2$ keV
- $K^{*+} \to K^+\gamma$ decay width: $50 \pm 5$ keV, giving $g_{\gamma KK^*} = 0.203$
- $f_1(1420) \to K\bar{K}^*$ branching ratio: $96.0 \pm 1.0 \pm 1.0\%$ (WA102 Collaboration), giving $g_{f_1 KK^*} = 1.027$
- $\eta(1475) \to K\bar{K}^*$ coupling: $g_{\bar{\eta}KK^*} \simeq 2.04$ (from maximum $\Gamma_{\bar{\eta}\to K\bar{K}^*+\mathrm{c.c.}} \simeq 45$ MeV)
- Monopole masses for non-resonant form factors: $\Lambda_K = 872$ MeV, $\Lambda_{K^*} = M_{K^*} = 893.5$ MeV
- Regge trajectories: $\alpha_K(t) = 0.7(t-m_K^2)$, $\alpha_{K^*}(t) = 0.25 + 0.83t$

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of meson transition form factors, particularly the dipole parameterization used by L3 (2007) and the quark model approach of Schuler (1997), while incorporating the Reggeization technique previously applied by Ren (2022) to ensure correct high-energy behavior. The model is intended to replace the "GaGaRes" Monte Carlo program (Berends, 2001) used in preliminary BESIII analysis, which lacked interference between resonance amplitudes and did not include non-resonant mechanisms. The results enable the BESIII Collaboration to extract the $f_1(1420)$ transition form factors from polarized cross sections of $\gamma\gamma^* \to K\bar{K}^*(892)$, which is timely given the contribution of $f_1$ mesons to the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon's $g-2$.
