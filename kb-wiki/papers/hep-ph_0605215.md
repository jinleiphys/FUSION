# Particle physics catalysis of thermal Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We point out that the existence of metastable, tau > 10^3 s, negatively charged electroweak-scale particles (X^-) alters the predictions for lithium and other primordial elemental abundances for A>4 via the formation of bound states with nuclei during BBN. In particular, we show that the bound states of X^- with helium, formed at temperatures of about T=10^8K, lead to the catalytic enhancement of Li6 production, which is eight orders of magnitude more efficient than the standard channel. In particle physics models where subsequent decay of X^- does not lead to large non-thermal BBN effects, this directly translates to the level of sensitivity to the number density of long-lived X^-, particles (τ>10^5 s) relative to entropy of n_{X^-}/s < 3\times 10^{-17}, which is one of the most stringent probes of electroweak scale remnants known to date.

## Key claim

The existence of metastable, negatively charged electroweak-scale particles (X⁻) with lifetimes τ > 10³ s can catalyze thermal nuclear reactions during Big Bang Nucleosynthesis via bound-state formation with nuclei, leading to an eight-orders-of-magnitude enhancement of ⁶Li production compared to the standard channel, but this enhancement is conditional on the bound states forming at temperatures around T = 10⁸ K and on the subsequent decay of X⁻ not producing large non-thermal BBN effects.

## Method

The author calculates binding energies and properties of bound states between X⁻ particles and light nuclei (⁴He, ³He, ⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁸Be, T, D, p) using a variational approach with a uniform nuclear charge distribution. The cross section for the catalyzed reaction (⁴He X⁻) + D → ⁶Li + X⁻ is derived via a factorized quantum-mechanical approach relating the CBBN and SBBN S-factors, and the thermal evolution of ⁶Li abundance is computed by solving coupled Boltzmann equations for the bound-state fraction and ⁶Li production, using SBBN code outputs for D and ⁴He abundances as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of (⁴He X⁻) ground state: 397 keV (Bohr orbit), 352 keV (scaling radius), 346 keV (experimental charge radius)
- Photo-dissociation decoupling temperature T₀ for (⁴He X⁻): 8.2 keV
- CBBN S-factor at zero energy: S_CBBN(0) ≈ 0.3 MeV·bn, compared to S_SBBN = 1.8 × 10⁻² eV·bn
- CBBN Gamow energy: 1973 keV, compared to SBBN Gamow energy of 5249 keV
- CBBN thermally averaged cross section at T ∼ 10 keV: ⟨σ_C v⟩ ≈ 2 bn × T⁻²/³ exp(-23.7 T⁻¹/³)
- Constraint on X⁻ abundance relative to baryons: Y_X ≲ 3 × 10⁻⁷, corresponding to n_X⁻/s ≲ 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁷
- Lifetime constraint: τ ≲ 5 × 10³ s for heavy charged particles with standard annihilation cross sections
- Minimal mass splitting in stau-neutralino system: m_τ̃ - m_χ > 70 MeV

## Context

This work introduces a third mechanism (catalysis) by which particle physics can affect primordial abundances, in addition to the established effects of changing the n/p freeze-out timing (Sarkar) and non-thermal BBN from late decays of metastable particles (metastable). The CBBN mechanism extends prior work on BBN constraints on supersymmetric models with long-lived particles (cefo, kkm, jj) and on bound-state effects in dark matter scenarios (khlopov). The results enable new constraints on electroweak-scale remnants and provide a possible avenue for addressing the lithium problem, particularly the discrepancy between SBBN predictions for ⁷Li and observed values, by allowing ⁷Be destruction via (⁷Be X⁻) bound states at T ∼ 30 keV for X⁻ lifetimes between 2000 and 5000 seconds.
