# Suppression of elliptic flow in a minimally viscous quark-gluon plasma

> We compute the time evolution of elliptic flow in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using a (2+1)-dimensional code with longitudinal boost-invariance to simulate viscous fluid dynamics in the causal Israel-Stewart formulation. We show that even ``minimal'' shear viscosity eta/s=hbar/(4pi) leads to a large reduction of elliptic flow compared to ideal fluid dynamics, raising questions about the interpretation of recent experimental data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim
Even the conjectured absolute lower bound on shear viscosity, η/s = ħ/(4π), causes a large (~50%) reduction of elliptic flow compared to ideal fluid dynamics, raising the possibility that RHIC data may be inconsistent with this "minimal viscosity" bound. The authors note this conclusion is tentative because it is not yet based on a quantitative data comparison with a realistic equation of state and better initial conditions.

## Method
The authors developed a (2+1)-dimensional causal viscous hydrodynamic code (VISH2+1) using the Israel-Stewart formulation, with longitudinal boost-invariance and a fixed Eulerian space-time grid in curvilinear coordinates (τ, x, y, η). They simulated non-central Cu+Cu collisions at impact parameter b = 7 fm using standard Glauber model initial conditions with wounded-nucleon scaling, starting hydrodynamic evolution at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c with vanishing transverse flow, and used an equation of state with a phase transition at Tc = 164 MeV between a free quark-gluon gas and a hadron resonance gas.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity set to the conjectured lower bound: η/s = ħ/(4π)
- Initial peak energy density in central collisions: e₀ = 30 GeV/fm³
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Woods-Saxon radius for copper: R_Cu = 4.2 fm, surface thickness ξ = 0.596 fm, equilibrium density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Critical temperature for phase transition: Tc = 164 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_dec = 130 MeV
- Kinetic relaxation time τ_π varied between 1/2 and 1/4 of the Boltzmann gas estimate τ_π^Boltz = (6/T)(η/s)
- Elliptic flow reduced by almost 50% relative to ideal fluid dynamics for minimal viscosity
- Viscous corrections to the distribution function become larger than 50% for p_T > 2.5 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic studies by Teaney (2003), who attempted to constrain QGP shear viscosity using only viscous corrections to the freeze-out distribution function, and by Romatschke (2007), who reported smaller viscous effects on elliptic flow. The paper enables future quantitative comparisons with RHIC data using more realistic equations of state and initial conditions, and raises the question of whether QCD might violate the conjectured minimum viscosity bound from AdS/CFT (Policastro, 2001). The authors note that higher-order corrections to Israel-Stewart theory (Lublinsky and Shuryak, 2007) might reduce viscous effects on elliptic flow, which would make their tentative conclusion premature.
