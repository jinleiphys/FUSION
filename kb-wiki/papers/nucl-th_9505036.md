# Chaoticity in Vibrating Nuclear Billiards

> We study the motion of classical particles confined in a two-dimensional "nuclear" billiard whose walls undergo periodic shape oscillations according to a fixed multipolarity. The presence of a coupling term in the single particle Hamiltonian between the particle motion and the collective coordinate generates a fully selfconsistent dynamics. We consider in particular monopole oscillations and demonstrate that self-consistency is essential in order to induce chaotic single-particle motion. We also discuss the dissipative behaviour of the wall motion and its relation with the order-to-chaos transition in the dynamics of the microscopic degrees of freedom. Analogous considerations can be extended to higher multipolarities.

## Key claim

Self-consistency between the collective wall motion and the single-particle dynamics is essential to induce chaotic single-particle motion in a vibrating nuclear billiard, even under adiabatic conditions where the wall frequency is much smaller than the single-particle frequency; without this coupling, the motion remains regular despite the system being formally non-integrable.

## Method

The authors simulate classical non-interacting point particles confined in a two-dimensional deep potential well whose surface oscillates periodically according to a monopole (L=0) deformation, with the collective coordinate \(\alpha\) appearing explicitly in the Hamiltonian as an additional degree of freedom. They solve Hamilton's equations using a Runge-Kutta algorithm with time steps of \(1~fm/c\), conserving total energy with high accuracy, and study the dynamics for systems of one and ten particles. To characterize chaos, they compute Poincaré-style scatter plots of final versus initial radial coordinates and calculate the largest Lyapunov exponent from the divergence of nearby phase-space trajectories.

## Key numbers

- Wall oscillation frequency: \(\Omega = 0.05~c/fm\), corresponding to a wall period \(\tau_w \sim 125.66~fm/c\).
- Single-particle period: \(\tau_p \sim 43.3~fm/c\) for billiard radius \(R_o = 6~fm\) and temperature \(T = 36~MeV\).
- Equilibrium deformation parameter \(\bar \alpha\) satisfies \(\bar \alpha (1 + \bar \alpha) = 2T / (m R_o^2 \Omega^2)\); initial perturbation \(\delta \alpha = 0.15\).
- Largest Lyapunov exponent for one particle: \(<\lambda_1> = 8 \cdot 10^{-3}~c/fm\), giving chaotic onset time \(\tau_1 = 125.3~fm/c\).
- Largest Lyapunov exponent for ten particles: \(<\lambda_{10}> = 8.8 \cdot 10^{-2}~c/fm\), giving chaotic onset time \(\tau_{10} = 11.4~fm/c\).
- Uncertainty dimension for the fractal scatter plot in the one-particle coupled case: \(D = 0.899\) (time-independent).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Blocki et al. (1991), who studied non-interacting particles in a hard-wall container with prescribed wall oscillations but without self-consistency or total energy conservation, finding regular motion for low multipolarities. It contrasts with Bauer et al. (1990), who used the Vlasov equation with self-consistency and found regular undamped collective motion coexisting with weakly chaotic single-particle dynamics. The present results show that self-consistent coupling induces chaos even in the adiabatic monopole case and that ensemble averaging over chaotic events produces macroscopic dissipation, with damping times longer than those predicted by the wall formula.
