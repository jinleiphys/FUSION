# Mach cones in viscous heavy-ion collisions

> The formation of Mach cones is studied in a full $(3+1)$-dimensional setup of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, considering a transverse and longitudinal expanding medium at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider energies. For smooth initial conditions and central collisions the jet-medium interaction is investigated using high-energy jets and various values of the ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density, $η/s$. For small viscosities, the formation of Mach cones is proven, whereas for larger viscosities the characteristic structures smear out and vanish eventually. The formation of a double-peak structure both in a single- and in a multiple-jet event is discussed.

## Key claim

Mach cones can form in a full (3+1)-dimensional expanding heavy-ion collision at RHIC energies, but only if the ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density is sufficiently small (η/s < 0.5); for larger viscosities the characteristic structures smear out and vanish, and the double-peak structure observed in two-particle correlations arises primarily from deflected head shocks and diffusion wakes rather than from the Mach cones themselves.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann approach of multiparton scatterings) to simulate a massless gluon gas with isotropic binary collisions, treating the jet-medium interaction with the same cross section as bulk-medium interactions. They initialized smooth Glauber initial conditions with a Gaussian rapidity distribution for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, placed high-energy jets (20 GeV) on a semi-circle of radius 4 fm at mid-rapidity, and extracted azimuthal particle distributions for different fixed values of η/s and for three scenarios: a single jet at φ_jet = 180°, a single jet at φ_jet = 135°, and multiple jets with randomly chosen starting positions.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity over entropy density studied: η/s = 0.08, 0.2, and 0.5.
- Jet initial momentum: p_x = E_jet = 20 GeV.
- Jet starting positions initialized on a semi-circle with radius r = 4 fm.
- For η/s = 0.08 and p_T > 0.5 GeV, a double-peak structure appears at φ ≈ ±70° in the single-jet scenario with φ_jet = 180°.
- For η/s = 0.08 and higher transverse momenta (p_T > 0.5 GeV), the double-peak structure appears around φ ≈ ±120°.
- The critical energy density for the deconfined phase is e_crit ≈ 0.6 GeV/fm³.
- The multiple-jet scenario (scenario III) shows a double-peak structure for sufficiently high p_T and low η/s with peaks around φ ≈ 50°.
- The Mach cone forms for η/s < 0.5 in the multiple-jet scenario.

## Context

This work extends earlier static-box studies by Bouras et al. (2012) and hydrodynamic calculations by Betz et al. (2010) by including both transverse and longitudinal expansion in a full (3+1)-dimensional setup, and by systematically varying the shear viscosity. The results confirm the earlier finding by Satarov et al. (2005) that radial flow distorts Mach cone patterns, and show that the double-peak structure in two-particle correlations is primarily medium-driven rather than a direct Mach cone signal, which complicates disentangling jet contributions from initial-state fluctuation effects as discussed by Takahashi et al. (2009), Schenke et al. (2010), and others.
