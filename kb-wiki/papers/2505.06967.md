# Gamow-Teller strength distributions and stellar weak-interaction rates for $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se using the deformed pn-QRPA model

> We calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $ββ$-decay nuclei $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se using the deformed pn-QRPA model. We use a deformed Nilsson basis and consider pairing correlations within the deformed BCS theory. Ground state correlations and two-particle and two-hole mixing states were included in our pn-QRPA model. Our calculated strength distributions were compared with experimental data and previous calculation. The total Gamow-Teller strength and centroid placement calculated in our model compares well with the measured value. We calculate $β$-decay and positron capture rates on $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se in supernovae environments and compare them to those obtained from experimental data and previous calculation. Our study shows that positron capture rates command the total weak rates at high stellar temperatures. We also calculate energy rates of $β$-delayed neutrons and their emission probabilities.

## Key claim

The deformed pn-QRPA model with a schematic separable interaction and a deformed Nilsson basis can reproduce the measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se, but only after applying a quenching factor of $f_q^2 = (0.55)^2$; the model's calculated total strength and centroid placement agree well with experimental data, whereas the prior calculation by Ha and Cheoun (2015) using a Woods-Saxon basis and Brueckner G-matrix placed the centroid at a much higher energy.

## Method

The authors used a deformed pn-QRPA model with a deformed Nilsson single-particle basis and a schematic separable interaction (particle-hole strength $\chi = 0.01$ MeV, particle-particle strength $\kappa = 0.0955$ MeV), incorporating pairing correlations via deformed BCS theory. Ground-state and excited-state GT strength distributions were calculated for $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se, using experimentally adopted deformation parameters ($\beta = 0.26$ for $^{76}$Ge, $\beta = 0.19$ for $^{82}$Se) and Q-values from the mass compilation of Audi (2012). The calculated GT strength was multiplied by a quenching factor of $(0.55)^2$, and stellar $\beta$-decay and positron capture rates were computed for temperatures $T_9 = 1$ to $30$ and densities up to $10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$, including up to 70,000 parent states grouped into 200 bands of 50 keV width.

## Key numbers

- Total GT$_-$ strength for $^{76}$Ge: calculated value 16.30, compared to measured value 19.89 (from the $(p,n)$ experiment of Madey (1989) at 134.4 MeV).
- GT centroid energy for $^{76}$Ge: calculated at 8.66 MeV in the daughter nucleus, compared to measured centroid at 9.10 MeV.
- For $^{76}$Ge at stellar density $10^{10}$ g/cm$^3$, the phase space factor increases by 7 orders of magnitude as stellar temperature goes from $T_9 = 3$ to $30$; at $10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$, the increase is 26 orders of magnitude over the same temperature range.
- For $^{76}$Ge at low stellar temperatures ($T_9 \leq 10$), calculated $\beta$-decay rates are 2 to 4 orders of magnitude larger than positron capture rates; at $T_9 = 20$ positron capture rates are one order of magnitude larger, and at $T_9 = 30$ they are two orders of magnitude larger.
- For $^{82}$Se, positron capture rates are 1 to 2 orders of magnitude larger than $\beta$-decay rates at high $T_9$ values.

## Context

This work extends the deformed pn-QRPA calculations of Ha and Cheoun (2015), who used a Woods-Saxon potential and Brueckner G-matrix but did not include excited-state GT strength distributions or stellar weak rates. The present calculation avoids the Brink-Axel hypothesis by microscopically computing GT strength off parent excited states, following the approach of Nabi and collaborators (2005-2012) for other nuclei. The results provide GT strength distributions that can constrain double-beta decay matrix elements and offer stellar weak-interaction rates for $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se that may improve simulations of presupernova evolution and the r-process.
