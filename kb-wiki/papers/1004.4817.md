# Structure of Scalar Mesons f(0)(600), a(0)(980), f(0)(1370) and a(0)(1450)

> We calculate low-energy meson decay processes and pion-pion scattering lengths in a globally chirally invariant two-flavour linear sigma model exploring the quark content of the scalar mesons f(0)(600) and a(0)(980), as well as f(0)(1370) and a(0)(1450). To this end, we investigate which one of these sets of fields is more likely to contain quark-antiquark states.

# Structure of Scalar Mesons f0(600), a0(980), f0(1370) and a0(1450)

## Key claim

Using a globally chirally invariant two-flavor linear sigma model with vector and axial-vector mesons, the authors find that identifying the scalar fields σ and a0 with the resonances f0(1370) and a0(1450) (Scenario II) is favoured by experimental data, while identifying them with f0(600) and a0(980) (Scenario I) is disfavoured because the predicted two-pion decay width of the sigma meson is too small compared to the PDG range of 600–1000 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a U(2)R × U(2)L linear sigma model Lagrangian containing scalar (σ, a0), pseudoscalar (π, ηN), vector (ρ, ω), and axial-vector (a1, f1) degrees of freedom. They fixed six model parameters using known masses (mπ, mηN, mρ, ma1), the pion decay constant fπ, and the decay width Γρ→ππ, then determined the remaining parameters via χ² fits to the decay width Γa1→πγ and the ππ scattering lengths a0⁰ and a0². Two scenarios were tested: assigning σ and a0 to f0(600) and a0(980) (Scenario I) or to f0(1370) and a0(1450) (Scenario II).

## Key numbers

- In Scenario I, the sigma meson mass is constrained to mσ = 332(+145)(-44) MeV from the scattering length a0⁰; the resulting maximum Γσ→ππ is approximately 145 MeV, far below the PDG range of 600–1000 MeV.
- In Scenario I, the parameter Z = 1.67 ± 0.2 and m1 = 652(+123)(-652) MeV, with the latter constrained by requiring m1 ∈ [0, mρ].
- In Scenario II, using Γa0(1450) = (265 ± 13) MeV yields h2 ≈ 5(+5)(-30); combining Γa1→πγ = (0.640 ± 0.246) MeV with the m1 ≤ mρ condition gives Z = 1.67(+0.21)(-0.07) and m1 = 720(+55)(-140) MeV.
- In Scenario II, Γf0(1370)→ππ is approximately 300–500 MeV for mf0(1370) = 1200–1400 MeV, consistent with the PDG width range of 200–500 MeV when mf0(1370) ≤ 1380 MeV.
- The ππ scattering lengths used for constraints are a0⁰ = 0.218 ± 0.020 and a0² = -0.0457 ± 0.0125 from Peyaud (2003).

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model approach of Parganlija, Giacosa, and Rischke (earlier papers) to address the long-standing puzzle of whether scalar mesons below 1 GeV (f0(600), a0(980)) or above 1 GeV (f0(1370), a0(1450)) are predominantly quark-antiquark states. The results contradict the interpretation that f0(600) and a0(980) are conventional q̅q states, supporting instead the tetraquark or molecular interpretations proposed by other authors (e.g., Isgur, 1999). The model enables future extensions to include glueball mixing, three-flavor calculations, and studies of the chiral transition at nonzero temperature and chemical potential.
