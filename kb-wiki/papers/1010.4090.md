# Large N Expansion for Strongly-coupled Boson-Fermion Mixtures

> We study a many-body mixture of an equal number of bosons and two-component fermions with a strong contact attraction. In this system bosons and fermions can be paired into composite fermions. We construct a large N extension where both bosons and fermions have the extra large N degrees of freedom and the boson-fermion interaction is extended to a four-point contact interaction which is invariant under the O(N) group transformation, so that the composite fermions become singlet in terms of the O(N) group. It is shown that such O(N) singlet fields have controllable quantum fluctuations suppressed by 1/N factors and yield a systematic 1/N-expansion in terms of composite fermions. We derive an effective action described by composite fermions up to the next-to-leading-order terms in the large N expansion, and show that there can be the BCS superfluidity of composite fermions at sufficiently low temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a large \(N\) extension of a strongly-coupled boson-fermion mixture where the \(1/N\)-expansion provides a systematic, controllable approximation; the claim of BCS superfluidity of composite fermions at low temperatures is conditional on the leading-order and next-to-leading-order analysis of the effective action, and the physical case \(N=1\) is only accessible via extrapolation of the expansion.

## Method

The authors start from a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian of \(N\) copies of bosons and two-component fermions with a four-point contact interaction invariant under \(\mathrm{O}(N)\). They introduce auxiliary fermionic fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to rewrite the partition function in terms of composite fermion (CF) fields, then integrate out the original bosons and fermions. The resulting CF action has an overall factor \(N\), making the \(1/N\)-expansion equivalent to a loop expansion; the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) terms are computed by expanding the logarithm of the CF action and performing derivative expansions at low energy and momentum.

## Key numbers

* The s-wave scattering length \(a_{bf}\) is related to the bare coupling \(g_{bf}\) by \(\frac{m_R}{2\pi a_{bf}} = \frac{1}{g_{bf}} + \int_{|\mathbf{k}|\le\Lambda} \frac{d\mathbf{k}}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{1}{\varepsilon_b(\mathbf{k})+\varepsilon_f(\mathbf{k})}\), where \(m_R = m_b m_f/(m_b+m_f)\) is the reduced mass.
* The LO inverse propagator of CFs at zero temperature is expanded as \(G^{-1}(\mathbf{p},E) \simeq a - c\mathbf{p}^2 + dE\) with coefficients \(a = \frac{m_R}{2\pi a_{bf}} - \frac{m_R\sqrt{2m_R|\mu|}}{2\pi}\), \(c = \frac{1}{4\pi}\frac{m_R}{m_f+m_b}\sqrt{\frac{m_R}{2|\mu|}}\), and \(d = \frac{m_R}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{m_R}{2|\mu|}}\), where \(\mu = \mu_f + \mu_b\).
* The effective CF mass is \(m_F = m_f + m_b\) and the effective CF chemical potential is \(\mu_F = 2\omega_{bf}(\sqrt{X} - X)\) with \(X = |\mu|/\omega_{bf}\) and binding energy \(\omega_{bf} = 1/(2m_R a_{bf}^2)\).
* The number equation at zero temperature yields \(\frac{(3\pi^2 n_{\text{tot}})^{2/3}}{2m_F} = \mu_F \times (2 - 1/\sqrt{X})^{2/3}\), and for a strongly-coupled mixture with small \(n_{\text{tot}}^{1/3}a_{bf}\) the solution near \(X \sim 1\) is selected.

## Context

This work extends the large \(N\) method previously applied to dilute Bose gases (BBZ00, AMT01) and nonrelativistic Fermi gases (NS07, VSR07, AB08) to the case of strongly-coupled boson-fermion mixtures. It builds on a model originally proposed in Maeda et al. (2009) for dense QCD applications, where bosons represent diquarks, fermions represent unpaired quarks, and composite fermions represent nucleons. The derived effective theory of weakly-interacting composite fermions enables future studies of BCS superfluidity in such mixtures and may be applied to cold-atom experiments with heteronuclear Feshbach molecules, such as those observed in Rb-K mixtures (OSP06, ZIR08).
