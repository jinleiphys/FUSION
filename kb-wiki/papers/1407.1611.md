# Kaon Condensation in Baryonic Fermi Liquid at High Density

> We formulate kaon condensation in dense baryonic matter with anti-kaons fluctuating from the Fermi-liquid fixed point. This entails that in the Wilsonian RG approach, the decimation is effectuated in the baryonic sector to the Fermi surface while in the meson sector to the origin. In writing the kaon-baryon (KN) coupling, we will take a generalized hidden local symmetry Lagrangian for the meson sector endowed with a "mended symmetry" that has the unbroken symmetry limit at high density in which the Goldstone $π$, scalar $s$, and vectors $ρ$ (and $ω$) and $a_1$ become massless. The vector mesons $ρ$ (and $ω$) and $a_1$ can be identified as emergent (hidden) local gauge fields and the scalar $s$ as the dilaton field of the spontaneously broken scale invariance at chiral restoration. In matter-free space, when the vector mesons and the scalar meson -- whose masses are much greater than that of the pion -- are integrated out, then the resulting KN coupling Lagrangian consists of the leading chiral order ($O(p^1)$) Weinberg-Tomozawa term and the next chiral order ($O(p^2)$) $Σ_{KN}$ term. In addressing kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), one makes an expansion in the "small" Fermi momentum $k_F$. We argue that in the Wilsonian RG formalism with the Fermi-liquid fixed point, the expansion is on the contrary in $1/k_F$ with the "large" Fermi momentum $k_F$. The kaon-quasinucleon interaction resulting from integrating out the massive mesons consists of a "relevant" term from the scalar exchange (analog to the $Σ_{KN}$ term) and an "irrelevant" term from the vector-meson exchange (analog to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term). It is found that the critical density predicted by the latter approach, controlled by the relevant term, is three times less than that predicted by chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

Kaon condensation in dense baryonic matter, when formulated using a Wilsonian renormalization group approach starting from the Fermi-liquid fixed point (rather than from the vacuum as in chiral perturbation theory), predicts a critical density approximately three times lower than the chiral perturbation theory estimate, because the expansion parameter becomes \(1/k_F\) (large Fermi momentum) instead of \(k_F\) (small Fermi momentum).

## Method

The authors formulate kaon condensation by coupling anti-kaon fields to baryonic quasiparticles near the Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point, using a generalized hidden local symmetry Lagrangian with "mended symmetry" that includes vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(a_1\)) as emergent gauge fields and a scalar dilaton field. They perform a Wilsonian renormalization group decimation: in the baryonic sector, modes are integrated out toward the Fermi surface, while in the meson sector, modes are integrated out toward the origin. The kaon-quasinucleon interaction is derived from integrating out the massive mesons, yielding a "relevant" term from scalar exchange (analogous to the \(\Sigma_{KN}\) term) and an "irrelevant" term from vector-meson exchange (analogous to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term). The renormalization group flow of the kaon mass and coupling is analyzed at tree level and one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for kaon condensation predicted by the Wilsonian RG approach is three times less than that predicted by chiral perturbation theory.
- The ordinary nuclear matter density is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- In compact-star matter, mean-field applications of chiral Lagrangians typically predict the critical density \(n_K \sim 3n_0\).
- Within the regime of strong interactions such as in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the critical density is likely located at \(n_K \gtrsim 6n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Paeng and Rho (2014) and earlier work by Lee, Rho, and collaborators (LRS) that analyzed kaon condensation starting from the Fermi-liquid fixed point, contrasting with standard chiral perturbation theory treatments (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986) that expand in small Fermi momentum. The paper also builds on the notion of BR scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the "double decimation" renormalization group approach (Brown and Rho, 2002). The results suggest that kaon condensation could occur at densities low enough to soften the equation of state of compact-star matter, potentially challenging the interpretation that the observation of \(\sim 2\) solar-mass stars rules out kaon condensation.
