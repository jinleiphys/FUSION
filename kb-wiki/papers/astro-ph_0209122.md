# Observability of Neutron Stars with Quarks

> Recent Chandra observations have raised expectations that the objects RX J185635-3754 and 3C58 are either bare strange quark stars or stars with extended quark cores. However, these observations can also be interpreted in terms of normal neutron stars. Essential requirements for either explanation are that they simultaneously account for the observed (i) spectral features (i.e., thermal or nonthermal, possible lines), (ii) bounds on the inferred mass (M) and radius (R), and (iii) the cooling curves (effective temperatures T and luminosities L vs. age). Compact stars offer the promise of delineating QCD at finite baryon density (Lattimer and Prakash, 2001) in a fashion complementary to relativistic heavy ion collider experiments, which largely address the finite temperature, but baryon poor regime.

# Observability of Neutron Stars with Quarks

## Key claim

Recent Chandra observations of RX J185635-3754 and 3C58 can be interpreted as evidence for bare strange quark stars or stars with extended quark cores, but these observations can also be explained by normal neutron stars; the revised parallax for RX J185635-3754 yields a radius and mass consistent with many equations of state both with and without exotic matter, removing support for an "extremely soft" equation of state but leaving atmospheric composition and magnetic field effects unresolved.

## Method

The authors analyze multi-wavelength photon observations of the isolated neutron star RX J185635-3754 and the pulsar J0205+6449 in the supernova remnant 3C58, using revised parallax measurements that incorporate corrections for geometrical distortions of the HST camera. They relate measured flux and effective temperature to infer mass and radius through redshift factors, and compare the results to theoretical mass-radius trajectories for representative equations of state. For 3C58, they examine the thermal upper limit from Chandra observations and assess whether standard neutrino cooling processes can simultaneously fit the luminosities and ages of multiple neutron stars.

- 3C58 power law index: 1.73 ± 0.07
- 3C58 distance: 3.2 kpc
- Strange quark star photon spectrum: 30 < E/keV < 500 (compared to 0.1 < E/keV < 2.5 for normal cooling neutron stars)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lattimer and Prakash (2001) on using compact stars to probe QCD at finite baryon density, and builds on the parallax measurements of Walter (2001) as revised by Kaplan, van Kerkwijk, and Anderson (2002) and Walter and Lattimer (2002). The cooling analysis draws on prior work by Yakovlev (2002) and Page (2002a) showing that standard neutrino cooling cannot simultaneously fit the luminosities and ages of multiple neutron stars with the same equation of state. The results enable future observations with INTEGRAL (scheduled for launch in 2002) to potentially detect the distinctive hard spectrum and time evolution of bare strange quark stars, which would constitute an unmistakable signature of color superconductivity at stellar densities.
