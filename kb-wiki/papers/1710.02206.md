# Isolated photon production in proton-nucleus collisions at forward rapidity

> We calculate isolated photon production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions in the Color Glass Condensate framework. Our calculation uses dipole cross sections solved from the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with an initial condition fit to deep inelastic scattering data. For comparison, we also update the results for the nuclear modification factor for pion production in the same kinematics. We present predictions for future forward RHIC and LHC measurements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8$ TeV.

## Key claim

The authors calculate isolated photon production at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, finding that the nuclear suppression factor for photons is smaller at low transverse momentum and has a weaker transverse-momentum dependence than for pions, but the calculation is performed at leading order and the formalism is not fully applicable for transverse momenta above 5 GeV in RHIC kinematics.

## Method

The calculation uses dipole cross sections solved from the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, with initial conditions fit to HERA deep inelastic scattering data for the proton and extended to nuclei via an optical Glauber model using standard Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry. For photon production, the authors use the hybrid formalism where a large-x quark from the dilute projectile scatters off the dense gluonic target and radiates a photon, with an isolation cut applied to remove the fragmentation component. For comparison, they also update results for the nuclear modification factor for neutral pion production using the same dipole cross sections, with parton distribution functions from CTEQ6 and fragmentation functions from DSS at leading order.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear modification factor for isolated photons in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=8 TeV: suppression is smaller than for pions, with R_pA values shown for rapidities y=2, 3, 4 and isolation cuts R=0.4 and R=0.1
- Nuclear modification factor for isolated photons in p+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: a small Cronin peak appears around k_T ~ 5 GeV in both rapidity bins
- Number of binary collisions: N_bin=4.948 for p+Au, N_bin=2.568 for p+Al at RHIC, and N_bin=8.153 for p+Pb at LHC
- Inelastic cross sections used: sigma_inel=42 mb at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and sigma_inel=75 mb at sqrt(s)=8 TeV
- For p+Al collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, approximately 80% of the pion production cross section comes from regions where the nuclear saturation scale falls below that of the proton

## Context

This work extends the same CGC framework previously used by the authors for single inclusive particle production (Lappi 2013), forward J/Psi production (Ducloue 2015, 2016), and Drell-Yan cross sections (Ducloue 2017) to photon production, all calculated consistently with the same parametrization fit to HERA data. The predictions are intended for comparison with upcoming forward RHIC and LHC measurements, and the results show stronger suppression than NLO pQCD calculations using EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions (Helenius 2014), though nuclear PDFs are not well constrained at the small x values probed.
