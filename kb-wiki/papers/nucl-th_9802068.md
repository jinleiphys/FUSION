# Medium effect on photon production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> The effect of in-medium vector and axial-vector meson masses on photon production is studied. We assume that the effective mass of a vector meson in hot nuclear matter decreases according to a universal scaling law, while that of an axial-vector meson is given by Weinberg's mass formula. We find that the thermal production rate of photons increases with reduced masses, and is enhanced by an order of magnitude at T=160 MeV with $m_ρ=300$ MeV. Assuming a hydrodynamic evolution, we estimate the effect of the reduced masses on photon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The result is compared to experimental data from the WA80/WA98 collaboration.

# Medium effect on photon production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The thermal production rate of photons from hot hadronic matter increases by an order of magnitude at T=160 MeV when the in-medium rho meson mass is reduced to 300 MeV, but when the full space-time evolution of the expanding fireball is included, the net effect on the observable photon spectrum is much smaller because the reduced masses also lower the initial temperature and shorten the system lifetime, partially compensating the rate enhancement.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal photon production rates from hadronic matter using an effective Lagrangian with vector and axial-vector mesons, assuming the in-medium rho meson mass follows a universal scaling law and the a1 meson mass obeys Weinberg's mass formula (m*_a1 = sqrt(2) m*_rho). They then integrated these rates over a one-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion with Bjorken initial conditions for central S+Au collisions at 200A GeV, considering both a pure hadronic scenario and a scenario with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, using two different parameterizations for how the rho mass decreases with temperature.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photon production rate from pi+rho to pi+gamma increases by more than an order of magnitude at E_gamma ≈ 0.5 GeV with m_rho = 300 MeV at T = 160 MeV
- Initial temperature for pure hadronic scenario with free masses: T0 ≈ 300 MeV; with reduced masses: T0 ≈ 250 MeV
- Initial temperature for scenario with first-order phase transition: T0 = 203 MeV (determined by quark-gluon plasma properties)
- Critical temperature with free masses: Tc = 150 MeV; with reduced masses: Tc = 160 MeV
- Mixed phase lifetime with free masses: Δτ_mix = 20 fm/c; with reduced masses: Δτ_mix = 5 fm/c
- For pure hadronic scenario with no phase transition, inclusive photon production for p_t > 1 GeV/c is reduced by an order of magnitude with reduced in-medium masses compared to free masses
- Cooling rate with mass profile (A) is approximately 3 times slower than with mass profile (B) when temperature decreases from 160 MeV to 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends the photon production calculations of Kapusta et al. (1992) and Song (1993) by incorporating in-medium meson mass modifications suggested by Brown and Rho (1991) as a precursor to chiral symmetry restoration. It parallels the dilepton studies of Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) who showed that dropping vector meson masses can explain the CERES dilepton enhancement, and applies the same idea to photon production. The results are compared to experimental upper limits from the WA80 collaboration and preliminary WA98 data at CERN SPS energies. The paper enables future work to distinguish between medium effects on photon versus dilepton production and to test whether dropping-mass scenarios are consistent with both observables simultaneously.
