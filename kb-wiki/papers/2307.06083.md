# Searching for universality of dineutron correlation at the surface of Borromean nuclei

> The dineutron correlation is systematically studied in three different Borromean nuclei near the neutron dripline, 11Li, 14Be and 17B, via the (p, pn) knockout reaction measured at the RIBF facility in RIKEN. For the three nuclei, the correlation angle between the valence neutrons is found to be largest in the same range of intrinsic momenta, which can be associated to the nuclear surface. This result reinforces the prediction that the formation of the dineutron is universal in environments with low neutron density, such as the surface of neutron-rich Borromean nuclei.

## Key claim

The dineutron correlation, measured via the opening angle between valence neutrons in momentum space, is found to be largest in the same range of intrinsic momenta (below 0.4 fm\(^{-1}\)) for all three Borromean nuclei studied (\(^{11}\)Li, \(^{14}\)Be, and \(^{17}\)B), supporting the prediction that dineutron formation is a universal feature at the low-density surface of neutron-rich Borromean nuclei, although the magnitude of the correlation varies between nuclei.

## Method

The \((p,pn)\) quasi-free knockout reaction was measured at the RIBF facility in RIKEN using a cocktail beam of \(^{11}\)Li, \(^{14}\)Be, and \(^{17}\)B at average energies of 246, 265, and 277 MeV/nucleon, respectively, impinging on a 15-cm thick liquid hydrogen target from the MINOS device. The momenta of the outgoing proton and knocked-out neutron were used to reconstruct the intrinsic momentum of the removed neutron before knockout, and the correlation angle between the two valence neutrons was obtained from the Jacobi momenta in Y coordinates. Experimental results were compared to theoretical calculations combining three-body hyperspherical structure models with an eikonal sudden description of the \((p,pN)\) reaction.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{11}\)Li, the maximum average correlation angle occurs at \(k_y = 0.25\) fm\(^{-1}\) with a value of \(100.0(2)^{+29}_{-29}\) degrees.
- For \(^{14}\)Be, the maximum average correlation angle occurs at \(k_y = 0.25\) fm\(^{-1}\) with a value of \(95.9(10)^{+29}_{-29}\) degrees.
- For \(^{17}\)B, the maximum average correlation angle occurs at \(k_y = 0.25\) fm\(^{-1}\) with a value of \(96.4(19)^{+29}_{-29}\) degrees.
- The intrinsic momentum window yielding the maximum asymmetry in the correlation angle distribution for \(^{14}\)Be is \(0.2 < k_y < 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The experimental resolution on the intrinsic momentum distribution is 0.17 fm\(^{-1}\).
- The three-body model for \(^{14}\)Be gives a matter radius of 3.0 fm; for \(^{17}\)B, the calculated matter radius is 2.8 fm.
- For \(^{11}\)Li, the three-body model yields a core-\(nn\) rms distance of 4.9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Kubota (2020) for studying dineutron correlation as a function of peripherality in \(^{11}\)Li to two additional Borromean nuclei, \(^{14}\)Be and \(^{17}\)B, measured in the same experiment. The results reinforce theoretical predictions by Matsuo (2006) and Hagino (2007) that dineutron formation should be universal in low-density environments such as the surface of neutron-rich nuclei. The findings enable systematic comparisons of dineutron correlation strength across different Borromean systems and highlight the role of core excitations (e.g., the \(0^+_2\) state of \(^{12}\)Be) in modulating the observed correlation, as previously indicated by Kobayashi (2016).
