# Coulomb interaction between a spherical and a deformed nuclei

> We present analytic expressions of the Coulomb interaction between a spherical and a deformed nuclei which are valid for all separation distance. We demonstrate their significant deviations from commonly used formulae in the region inside the Coulomb radius, and show that they remove various shortcomings of the conventional formulae.

# Coulomb interaction between a spherical and a deformed nuclei

## Key claim

The paper presents analytic expressions for the Coulomb interaction between a spherical projectile and a deformed target that are valid for all separation distances, not only outside the Coulomb radius. The authors demonstrate that commonly used formulae deviate significantly inside the Coulomb radius, but note that for heavy-ion fusion reactions like 16O+238U at near-barrier energies, these deviations are localized well inside the barrier region and therefore do not affect fusion probabilities when nuclear and Coulomb radii are taken to be equal.

## Method

The authors express the Coulomb interaction between a spherical projectile and a deformed target using a Fourier transform representation, assuming uniformly charged objects with sharp surfaces. They perform computer-assisted analytic integration of the one-dimensional Fourier integrals to obtain closed-form expressions for the bare Coulomb potential and for linear and second-order Coulomb coupling form factors for multipoles up to lambda=6. The results are compared with three commonly used approximate models: a point projectile model (model I), a uniform charge model using the sum of radii (model II), and a uniform charge model using only the target radius (model III).

## Key numbers

- For the 16O+238U system, the Coulomb radius parameter was chosen to be 1.06 fm
- The quadrupole deformation parameter for 238U was taken as beta_2 = 0.289
- For the bare Coulomb potential outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), the formula reduces to V(0)(R) = Z_P Z_T e^2 / R
- For the quadrupole linear coupling form factor outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), F(1)_lambda=2(R) = 3 R_T^2 / (5 R^3)
- For the hexadecapole linear coupling form factor outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), F(1)_lambda=4(R) = R_T^4 / (3 R^5)
- For the hexacontatetrapole linear coupling form factor outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), F(1)_lambda=6(R) = 3 R_T^6 / (13 R^7)
- For the quadrupole second-order coupling form factor outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), F(2)_lambda=2(R) = 6 R_T^2 / (5 R^3)
- For the hexadecapole second-order coupling form factor outside the Coulomb radius (R > R_C), F(2)_lambda=4(R) = R_T^4 / (R^5)
- The active angles for the s-wave effective potential with J_max=2 are approximately 30.55 and 70.12 degrees

## Context

This work extends the Fourier transform method for Coulomb interactions developed by Krappe and collaborators, and provides analytic expressions that agree with the earlier results of Rowley for the bare Coulomb potential. The paper addresses shortcomings in three commonly used approximate models: the point projectile model used in most analyses of heavy-ion fusion reactions near and below the Coulomb barrier, and two uniform charge models found in textbooks. The new formulae remove the cusp and derivative discontinuities present in the conventional models at the matching distance between asymptotic and short-range regions. The authors suggest the improved formulae will be important for systems where the Coulomb radius exceeds the nuclear radius, such as heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-deficient isotopes, and for light heavy-ion collisions where internal absorption is weak.
