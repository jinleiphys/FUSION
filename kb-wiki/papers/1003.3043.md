# Remarks on the use of projected densities in the density dependent part of Skyrme or Gogny functionals

> I discuss the inadequacy of the "projected density" prescription to be used in density dependent forces/functionals when calculations beyond mean field are pursued. The case of calculations aimed at the symmetry restoration of mean fields obtained with effective realistic forces of the Skyrme or Gogny type is considered in detail. It is shown that at least for the restoration of spatial symmetries like rotations, translations or parity the above prescription yields catastrophic results for the energy that drive the intrinsic wave function to configurations with infinite deformation, preventing thereby its use both in projection after and before variation.

# Remarks on the use of projected densities in the density dependent part of Skyrme or Gogny functionals

## Key claim

The "projected density" prescription for density dependent forces/functionals, which was proposed to avoid the complex-density problems of the "mixed density" prescription, yields catastrophic results for spatial symmetry restoration (parity, rotations, translations) by driving the intrinsic wave function to configurations with infinite deformation and unbounded negative energies. This failure occurs because the projected density becomes decoupled from the intrinsic density, producing a mismatch that reduces the repulsive density dependent energy without limit as deformation increases.

## Method

The author performs parity projection calculations on octupole-deformed intrinsic states using the Gogny D1S force, comparing the "mixed density" and "projected density" prescriptions for the density dependent part of the Hamiltonian. For the nucleus 36Ar (reflection-symmetric ground state, surface-dominated density) and 224Ra (octupole-deformed ground state, volume-dominated density), the positive parity projected energy is computed as a function of the octupole moment Q3. The projected density prescription replaces the intrinsic density in the density dependent term with the parity-projected density, which in the strong deformation limit reduces to the average density rather than the intrinsic density.

## Key numbers

- The density dependent strength parameter t3 for Gogny D1S is 1350.0 MeV fm^4, for D1N is 1609.46 MeV fm^4, and for D1M is 1562.22 MeV fm^4.
- For 36Ar at a fixed beta2 of 0.23, the projected energy using the "projected density" prescription shows a decrease of tens of MeV as the octupole moment increases, with no minimum reached.
- For 224Ra, the intrinsic HFB energy shows a minimum at an octupole moment of approximately 4 b^(3/2) (corresponding to the ground state), while the projected energy with the "projected density" prescription decreases monotonically with increasing octupole moment.
- The contour plots of 224Ra at Q3 = 4 b^(3/2) show intrinsic density contours from 0.02 fm^(-3) (outer) to 0.14 fm^(-3) (inner), while the projected density has two symmetric "humps" each with roughly half the density of the single intrinsic hump.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of density dependent functional prescriptions in beyond-mean-field calculations, following the identification of complex-density problems with the "mixed density" prescription by Dobaczewski (2007) and Duguet (2009). The "projected density" prescription had been used successfully for particle number projection with the Gogny force by Valor (1997, 2000) and Schmid (2004), but the author shows it fails catastrophically for spatial symmetries. The paper suggests that the only viable path forward is to use functionals depending only on integer powers of the density, such as the Barcelona-Catania-Paris (BCP) functional (Robledo, 2008; Baldo, 2010), or to use approaches based on the Gaussian Overlap Approximation or topological GOA (Hagino, 2003) where the residual interaction is defined as second derivatives of the energy.
