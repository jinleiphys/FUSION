# Time of primordial Be-7 conversion into Li-7, energy release and doublet of narrow cosmological neutrino lines

> One of the important light elements created during the big bang nucleosynthesis is Be-7 which then decays to Li-7 by electron capture when recombination becomes effective but well before the Saha equilibrium recombination is reached. This means that Be-7 should wait until its recombination epoch even though the half-life of the hydrogenic beryllium atom is only 106.4 days. We calculate when the conversion from primordial Be-7 to Li-7 occurs taking into account the population of the hyperfine structure sublevels and solving the kinetic equations for recombination, photoionization and conversion rate. We also calculate the energies and the spectrum of narrow neutrino doublet lines resulting from Be-7 decay.

## Key claim
The conversion of primordial Be-7 into Li-7 occurs significantly earlier (at redshift z ≈ 30000) than the Saha equilibrium prediction (z ≈ 25000) because the decay time of recombined hydrogen-like Be-7 (half-life 106.4 days) is much shorter than the cosmological time, preventing equilibrium from being established.

## Method
The authors solved kinetic equations for recombination, photoionization, and conversion rate of Be-7, accounting for the population of hyperfine structure sublevels in hydrogen-like Be-7 (Be-7 with one electron). They calculated the net decay rate using laboratory values for neutral/helium-like Be-7, finding that with hyperfine states populated according to statistical weights (high spin temperature maintained by Lyman-alpha scattering, electron collisions, and CMB interactions), the hydrogen-like Be-7 half-life is 106.4 days. The recombination equations were integrated numerically using cosmological parameters (H₀ = 73 km/s/Mpc, Ωm = 0.24, Ωb = 0.043, ΩΛ = 0.76, T_CMB = 2.726 K) and a total recombination coefficient formula for hydrogenic ions.

## Key numbers
- Half-life of hydrogen-like Be-7 with hyperfine states distributed according to statistical weights: 106.4 days (derived from laboratory half-life of 53.2 days for neutral Be-7)
- Beryllium-to-lithium conversion occurs at redshift z ≈ 30000, compared to Saha equilibrium prediction of z ≈ 25000
- Q-value of Be-7 electron capture reaction: 861.8 keV
- Branching ratio for decay to excited Li-7 state (477.6 keV above ground): 10.4%
- Neutrino line 1: central energy 29.5 eV, full width at half maximum 2.3 eV (from 861.8 keV decay branch, 89.6% of decays)
- Neutrino line 2: central energy 13.1 eV, full width at half maximum 1 eV (from 384.2 keV decay branch, 10.4% of decays)
- Line width at half maximum δE/E = 7.8%
- Nuclear recoil energy: 56.5 eV for ground-state decay, 11.2 eV for excited-state decay
- Recoil velocity v/c = 5.85 × 10⁻⁵ for the 477.6 keV photon
- 2-photon decay rate from 2s state of hydrogen-like Be-7: 3.4 × 10⁴ s⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the understanding of the connection between big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) and recombination, specifically addressing the previously uninvestigated problem of Be-7 conversion timing. The authors build on the standard BBN abundance calculations of Serpico et al. (2004) and the recombination theory of Zel'dovich, Kurt, and Sunyaev (1968) and Peebles (1968). The paper demonstrates that atomic processes, including hyperfine structure populations, influence nuclear decay rates in a rare astrophysical example. The results enable calculation of the neutrino spectrum from Be-7 decay, producing the narrowest predicted lines in the cosmic neutrino background, though too weak for current detection. The energy release from the decay produces a y-type distortion of ∼10⁻¹⁶ in the CMB, comparable in entropy generation to tritium decay energy release.
