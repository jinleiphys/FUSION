# Measurement of the ratio of the sixth order to the second order cumulant of net-proton multiplicity distributions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the measurement of the sixth order cumulant and its ratio to the second order cumulant ($C_6/C_2$) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The influence of statistics and different methods of centrality bin width correction on $C_6/C_2$ of net-proton multiplicity distributions is demonstrated. There is no satisfactory method to extract $C_6/C_2$ with the current statistics recorded at lower energies by STAR at RHIC. With statistics comparable to the expected statistics at the planned future RHIC Beam Energy Scan II (BES II), no energy dependence of $C_6/C_2$ is observed in central collisions using the UrQMD model. We find if the transition signal is as strong as predicted by the PQM model, then it is hopefully observed at the upcoming RHIC BES II.

# Measurement of the ratio of the sixth order to the second order cumulant of net-proton multiplicity distributions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that with statistics comparable to the expected statistics at the planned RHIC Beam Energy Scan II (BES II), no energy dependence of $C_6/C_2$ is observed in central collisions using the UrQMD model; however, if the transition signal is as strong as predicted by the PQM model, it may be observable at the upcoming RHIC BES II, but only within a restricted transverse momentum range of $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$ and primarily at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model to generate minimum bias Au+Au collision events at multiple beam energies, with statistics comparable to those proposed for RHIC BES II. They applied the Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method with various centrality bin widths (each of $N_{ch}$, $\delta 0.2\%$, $\delta 1\%$, and $\delta 2.5\%$) to reduce initial size fluctuations, and studied the statistical dependence of $C_6/C_2$ of net-proton multiplicity distributions. The influence of different centrality determination methods (using charged $\pi$ and $K$ within $|\eta| < 0.5$, 1.0, 1.7, and 2.0) on $C_6/C_2$ was also examined, and statistical uncertainties were estimated using the delta theorem method with Skellam distributions.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV with 6M events in 0-30% central collisions, the uncertainty of $C_6/C_2$ is about 1.8 within $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$.
- The proposed number of events in 0-30% central collisions for RHIC BES II are 30M at 7.7 GeV, 80M at 11.5 GeV, and 130M at 19.6 GeV.
- With BES II statistics, the corresponding statistical uncertainties of $C_6/C_2$ at 7.7, 11.5, and 19.6 GeV are 1.68, 0.71, and 0.39, respectively, within $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV, the minimum value of $C_6/C_2$ in the critical region from the PQM model is around -1.5, giving a deviation of about 3.5$\sigma$ from the UrQMD baseline value of 0.5 within $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$.
- For $0.4 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/$c$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV, observing a $3\sigma$ deviation from 0.5 would require about 600M events.
- The UrQMD model generated 450M minimum bias events at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV, and 136M, 107M, 83M, 137M, 37M, and 57M at 7.7, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, respectively.
- For $C_6/C_2$ in 0-30% central collisions, the CBWC method in each $\delta 1\%$ centrality bin requires at least 10M minimum bias events to obtain reliable results.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of lower-order cumulant ratios such as $C_4/C_2$ (also denoted $\kappa\sigma^2$) in net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions measured by STAR and PHENIX at RHIC (STAR-proton, STAR-charge, phenix-charge). The authors build on the CBWC method previously applied to $C_4/C_2$ by Chen et al. (chenlz-JPG) and on studies of centrality resolution effects by Xiaofeng et al. (Xiaofeng-JPG). The paper enables future experimental searches for the QCD phase transition signal using $C_6/C_2$ at the upcoming RHIC BES II, and highlights the need for precise Lattice QCD calculations of $C_6/C_2$ with smaller uncertainties before the BES II program.
