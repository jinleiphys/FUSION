# Comment on "Nuclear Excitation by Free Muon Capture"

> In the paper [1] the process of free muon capture with simultaneous excitation of a nuclear isomer has been suggested, claiming that ``the effect can be detectable for selected isotopes". Here, we argue that this claim can not be confirmed. Briefly, the process is far from the dominant mechanism for nuclear excitation; it excites high energy nuclear levels that will not generally decay to the isomer; the proposal assumes all incident muons will fulfil energy criteria, ignoring dominant capture paths; and nuclei excited by muons will have a shortened lifetime due to muonic capture.

# Comment on "Nuclear Excitation by Free Muon Capture"

## Key claim
The authors argue that the claim by Gargiulo (2023) that nuclear excitation by free muon capture (NEμC) "can be detectable for selected isotopes" cannot be confirmed, because the process is far from the dominant mechanism for nuclear excitation, excites high-energy levels that will not generally decay to the isomer, assumes all incident muons fulfill energy criteria while ignoring dominant capture paths, and because nuclei excited by muons will have a shortened lifetime due to muonic capture.

## Method
The authors analyze the proposed NEμC mechanism by examining angular momentum and parity selection rules for s-wave versus p- or d-wave muon capture, comparing the proposed process with the dominant competing channels of radiative capture (RC) and Auger capture (AC) in muonic atoms. They also evaluate the muonic cascade process, where muons captured in highly excited states decay via Auger and radiative transitions with strong hyperfine mixing of muon-nucleus levels, and contrast this with the separable muon-nucleus picture assumed in the original proposal.

## Key numbers
- The suggested nuclear state of ²⁰⁷Pb at 4980.5 keV is highly excited and separated from the ground state by over 100 levels, making it unlikely to cascade to a metastable isomer.
- The paper provides no quantitative cross-section or rate numbers for NEμC; the argument is based on qualitative comparison of competing processes.

## Context
This comment extends the critique of the NEμC proposal by Gargiulo (2023) by invoking the established understanding of muonic atom dynamics, including the muonic cascade with dynamical splitting first discussed by Wu (1969) and Borie and Rinker (1982), improved by Michel (2019), and experimentally confirmed by Mux (2020). The authors argue that the original proposal incorrectly treats muonic and nuclear degrees of freedom as separable, and that the dominant mechanism for nuclear excitation in muonic atoms is the muonic cascade, not the proposed resonant capture. The comment enables future work to focus on the muonic dynamical-structure cascade as the primary route for nuclear excitation by muons.
