# Jet quenching and elliptic flow

> In jet quenching, a hard QCD parton, before fragmenting into a jet of hadrons, deposits a fraction of its energy in the medium, leading to suppressed production of high-$p_T$ hadrons. Assuming that the deposited energy quickly thermalizes, we simulate the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the QGP fluid. Explicit simulation of Au+Au collision with and without a quenching jet indicate that elliptic flow is greatly reduced in a jet event. The result can be used to identify the jet events in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Assuming that the energy deposited by a hard QCD parton in the QGP medium quickly thermalizes, the resulting hydrodynamic evolution of the fluid in Au+Au collisions shows that elliptic flow is greatly reduced in a jet event, potentially to near zero or small negative values in 0-5% centrality collisions, which could serve as a signature to identify jet events experimentally.

## Method

The author simulates the hydrodynamic evolution of the QGP fluid in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy using a modified version of the AZHYDRO code, which solves energy-momentum conservation equations with a source term representing the quenching jet. The jet energy loss is modeled as proportional to the local entropy density, with a reference energy loss of 14 GeV/fm at a reference entropy density of 140 fm^-3, consistent with observed high-pT suppression. The simulation uses an equation of state (EOS-Q) with a first-order phase transition and hadronic chemical freeze-out at Tc=164 MeV, with initial conditions of peak energy density 30 GeV/fm^3 at tau0=0.6 fm/c.

## Key numbers

* In central (b=0) Au+Au collisions at RHIC, a quenching jet with energy loss parameter dE/dx|0 = 14 GeV/fm deposits approximately 10 GeV of energy to the fireball as it passes through.
* For a 10 GeV parton in an expanding medium, the average energy loss in the pre-equilibrium stage is only 0.51 +/- 0.14 GeV, a small fraction of the total approximately 10 GeV loss.
* In a b=2.5 fm Au+Au collision (0-5% centrality) without a quenching jet, elliptic flow v2 is approximately 4-8% in the pT range 1-2.5 GeV; with a quenching jet, v2 is reduced to near zero or small negative values (less than -1%).
* The quenching jet induces a small negative directed flow v1 of approximately -1.5% to -2.5% in the pT range 1-2.5 GeV for b=2.5 fm collisions.
* The third flow harmonic v3 induced by the quenching jet is approximately 1% after correcting for trigger jet fragments with a cut angle of 55-60 degrees.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic simulations of jet quenching and conical flow by Chaudhuri (2005, 2006) and addresses the interplay between jet energy loss and elliptic flow, a key observable from RHIC. The paper builds on the phenomenological analysis of parton energy loss by Wang (2004) and the BDMPS energy loss formalism (Baier, 2000), and uses the standard AZHYDRO initialization from the QGP3v2 framework. The result that elliptic flow is strongly reduced in jet events offers a practical method for identifying jet events in heavy ion collisions, where background makes direct jet identification nearly impossible.
