# Quasifission dynamics and stability of superheavy systems

> Recent experiments revealed intriguing similarities in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{207}$Pb, $^{132}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U reactions at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The experimental data indicate that for all systems substantial energy dissipation takes place, in the first stage of the reaction, although the number of transferred nucleons is small. On the other hand, in the second stage, a large number of nucleons are transferred with small friction and small consumption of time. To understand the observed behavior, various reactions were analyzed based on the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. From a systematic analysis for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{40}$Ar+$^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb, $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U, $^{136}$Xe+ $^{198}$Pt, and $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U reactions, we find that TDHF reproduces well the measured trends. In addition, the Balian-Vénéroni variational principle is applied to head-on collisions of $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U, and the variance of the fragment masses is compared with the experimental data, showing significant improvement. The underlying reaction mechanisms and possible future studies are discussed.

# Quasifission dynamics and stability of superheavy systems

## Key claim
The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory reproduces the measured trends of energy dissipation, interaction times, and deformation across a wide range of heavy-ion reactions, including the striking similarity that the slope change in total kinetic energy loss and interaction times occurs after net transfer of about 5% of total nucleons for systems as different as Ni+Pb and U+U, though the authors caution that it remains unclear whether this has a physical meaning or is coincidental.

## Method
The authors performed 3D TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional for eight reaction systems: 40,48Ca+124Sn, 40Ca+208Pb, 40Ar+208Pb, 58Ni+208Pb, 64Ni+238U, 136Xe+198Pt, and 238U+238U, at energies around the Coulomb barrier. For 238U+238U head-on collisions, the Balian-Veneroni variational principle was additionally applied to compute the variance of fragment masses. Experimental data were obtained from the velocity filter SHIP at GSI for 64Ni+207Pb and 132Xe+208Pb, and from the VAMOS spectrometer at GANIL for 238U+238U.

## Key numbers
- For 64Ni+207Pb at Ec.m. = 289 MeV, TKEL saturates at about 45-50% of Ec.m.
- For 58Ni+208Pb at Ec.m. = 257 MeV, TKEL saturates at about 25% of Ec.m.
- For 238U+238U head-on collisions at Ec.m. = 875 MeV, contact times were 1.97 zs (tip-on-tip), 1.64 zs (tip-on-side), and 1.62 zs (side-on-side), with TKEL/Ec.m. values of 0.37, 0.30, and 0.26 respectively
- The Balian-Veneroni prescription gave mass variances sigma_A^2 of 236.4 (tip-on-side) and 148.2 (side-on-side), compared to TDHF values of 10.5 and 9.6; experimental values at corresponding TKEL were approximately 400 and 250
- In tip-on-tip 238U+238U collisions, a ternary quasifission process produced a third fragment with Z approximately 4.1 and N approximately 6.5 (beryllium-like)
- In tip-on-side 238U+238U collisions, about 11.2 neutrons and 6.6 protons were transferred on average from tip-aligned 238U to the other

## Context
This work extends experimental studies of nuclear molecule formation in superheavy systems by Comas (2012) for 64Ni+207Pb (Z=110), Heinz (2015) for 132Xe+208Pb (Z=136), and Golabek (2010) for 238U+238U (Z=184), showing striking similarities in energy dissipation and interaction times across these systems. The TDHF calculations provide a microscopic foundation for understanding quasifission dynamics that complements phenomenological approaches such as the Langevin-type model of Zagrebaev (2005, 2007), the dinuclear system model of Adamian (1997), and the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. The application of the Balian-Veneroni variational principle to 238U+238U represents an advance beyond standard mean-field descriptions, and the results enable future studies of multinucleon transfer for producing neutron-rich superheavy nuclei, as suggested by Langevin model predictions.
