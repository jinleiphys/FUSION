# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic moments and transitions in A <= 9 nuclei with meson-exchange currents derived from chiral effective field theory

> Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic moments and transitions are reported for A <= 9 nuclei. The realistic Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials are used to generate the nuclear wave functions. Contributions of two-body meson-exchange current (MEC) operators are included for magnetic moments and M1 transitions. The MEC operators have been derived in both a standard nuclear physics approach and a chiral effective field theory formulation with pions and nucleons including up to one-loop corrections. The two-body MEC contributions provide significant corrections and lead to very good agreement with experiment. Their effect is particularly pronounced in the A=9, T=3/2 systems, in which they provide up to 20% (40 %) of the total predicted value for the 9Li (9C) magnetic moment.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic moments and transitions for nuclei with A ≤ 9, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials, show that two-body meson-exchange current (MEC) operators derived from chiral effective field theory provide significant corrections that lead to very good agreement with experiment, with the effect particularly pronounced in A=9, T=3/2 systems where MEC contributions provide up to approximately 20% of the total predicted value for 9Li and approximately 40% for 9C magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations to construct trial wave functions, followed by Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations to refine the solutions for A ≤ 9 nuclei using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials. Electromagnetic current operators included one-body impulse approximation terms and two-body meson-exchange current operators derived from both a standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) and a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) formulation with pions and nucleons including up to one-loop corrections, with the χEFT operators regularized using a cutoff of Λ=600 MeV and low-energy constants fixed to reproduce experimental data in A=2-3 systems.

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electroweak transitions in A=6,7 nuclei reported in Pastore et al. (2008) and Pieper et al. (2007) to A=8,9 nuclei using the improved Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential instead of the earlier Illinois-2 potential. The χEFT MEC operators used here were developed in Pastore et al. (2008, 2009) and Piarulli et al. (2012), and the SNPA MEC operators follow the formalism described in Marcucci et al. (2005). The results enable future calculations of electroweak transitions in heavier nuclei and provide a systematic test of chiral effective field theory currents in nuclear structure calculations.
