# Effective two-body interactions in the s-d shell nuclei from sum rules equations in tranfer reactions

> Average effective two-body interaction matrix elements in the s-d shell have been extracted, from data on experimentally measured isospin centroids, by combining the recently derived new sum rules equations for pick-up reactions with similar known equations for stripping reactions performed on general multishell target states. Using this combination of stripping and pick-up equations, the average effective matrix elements for the shells, 1d^2_5/2, 2s^2_1/2 and 1d^2_3/2 respectively have been obtained. A new feature of the present work is that the restriction imposed in earlier works on target states, that it be populated only by active neutrons has now been abandoned.

## Key claim

Average effective two-body interaction matrix elements for the s-d shell orbits \(1d_{5/2}\), \(2s_{1/2}\), and \(1d_{3/2}\) can be extracted by combining sum rules for stripping and pick-up reactions on the same target, eliminating the isovector two-body correlation term \(<H^{01}>_{Tar}\) that had previously limited the approach; the restriction that target states be populated only by active neutrons is abandoned, but the present work still restricts target states to a single active orbit.

## Method

Isospin centroids of residual nucleus states populated via single-nucleon stripping and pick-up reactions were calculated from experimental energies and spectroscopic factors for targets in the s-d shell. By combining one stripping and one pick-up equation for the same target, the analytically intractable isovector two-body correlation term \(<H^{01}>_{Tar}\) was eliminated, yielding linear equations in the average effective interaction parameters \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{ik}}\) and \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{ik}}\). A least-squares fit was then performed over multiple target nuclei to extract the best values of these parameters for the \(1d_{5/2}^2\), \(2s_{1/2}^2\), and \(1d_{3/2}^2\) configurations, using \(^{16}\)O, \(^{28}\)Si, and \(^{32}\)S as inert cores respectively.

## Key numbers

*   \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -0.636\) MeV, \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -2.80\) MeV (present calculation, root-mean-square deviation 0.673 MeV).
*   \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{s_{1/2}s_{1/2}}} = -0.559\) MeV, \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{s_{1/2}s_{1/2}}} = -2.30\) MeV (present calculation, root-mean-square deviation 0.267 MeV).
*   \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{d_{3/2}d_{3/2}}} = -0.326\) MeV, \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{d_{3/2}d_{3/2}}} = -2.16\) MeV (present calculation, root-mean-square deviation 0.196 MeV).
*   For comparison, Kuo and Brown (Reference [41]) gave \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -0.398\) MeV and \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -2.31\) MeV; the MSDI Hamiltonian of Halbert et al. (Reference [42]) gave \(\overline{W^{T=1}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -0.248\) MeV and \(\overline{W^{T=0}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}} = -3.50\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule approach of French (1965) and others by applying the recently derived pick-up sum rules of Sharda et al. (1997) together with existing stripping sum rules to the s-d shell, removing the restriction that target states contain only active neutrons. The results are compared with the matrix elements of Kuo and Brown (1966) and the parameterized interactions (RIP and MSDI) of Halbert et al. (1971); the present values agree reasonably with the MSDI results but show large differences from the RIP and Kuo-Brown values, which the authors attribute to the larger configuration spaces used in those works. The analytical sum-rule method provides a simpler alternative to large-basis shell-model diagonalization for extracting average effective interactions, and the derived equations serve as constraints for future spectroscopic studies.
