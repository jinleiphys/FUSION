# Existence of a fourth Airy elephant in the nuclear rainbows for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering

> The number of gross structures in the 90$^\circ$ excitation function for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C elastic scattering, often called Airy elephants, has been of great interest. These structures are caused by refractive scattering and are separated by Airy minima. Their importance stems from their close relationship to the interaction potential between two $^{12}$C nuclei, which also describes the molecular resonances of the compound system at lower energies. Although a unique deep potential was usually determined from rainbow scattering at higher energies, a puzzling discrepancy persisted: the energy at which the Airy minimum $A1$ crosses 90$^\circ$ was $E_{c.m.}\approx$67 MeV for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C. This is remarkably low compared to approximately 100 MeV for both the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O systems. This question remained unanswered until the discovery of the secondary rainbow in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system. We report for the first time that the highest energy at which the dynamically generated Airy minimum of the secondary rainbow crosses 90$^\circ$ is about 100 MeV. This demonstrates that the fourth Airy elephant exists between the Airy minimum $A1$ of the primary nuclear rainbow and that, $A1^{(S)}$, of the secondary rainbow. The long-standing problem concerning the Airy minima and Airy elephants has finally been resolved after decades of concern by recognizing the existence of a dynamically generated secondary rainbow in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the fourth Airy elephant in the 90° excitation function for 12C+12C elastic scattering exists between the Airy minimum A1 of the primary nuclear rainbow and the Airy minimum A1(S) of the dynamically generated secondary rainbow, with the highest energy at which the Airy minimum crosses 90° being about 100 MeV in center-of-mass energy, resolving a long-standing puzzle where the A1 crossing energy for 12C+12C (E_c.m. ≈ 67 MeV) was remarkably lower than for 16O+12C and 16O+16O (approximately 100 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel calculations using an extended double folding model with the DDM3Y-FR effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, incorporating six-channel couplings that included the 0+, 2+ (4.44 MeV), 3- (9.64 MeV), and 4+ (14.08 MeV) states of 12C, including mutual excitations of the 2+ state. They systematically studied the energy evolution of Airy minima for both the primary nuclear rainbow and the secondary rainbow by calculating angular distributions at energies from 139.5 MeV to 240 MeV and tracking the crossing angles of Airy minima.

## Key numbers

- The Airy minimum A1 of the primary nuclear rainbow crosses 90° at E_c.m. ≈ 67 MeV for 12C+12C, compared to approximately 100 MeV for 16O+12C and 95 MeV for 16O+16O.
- The Airy minimum A1(S) of the secondary rainbow crosses 90° at E_L ≈ 210 MeV (E_c.m. = 105 MeV).
- The highest order Airy minimum that appears at 90° from the primary rainbow is A4 at E_L ≈ 58 MeV.
- At 240 MeV, the first-order Airy minimum A1(P) of the primary nuclear rainbow is determined at 43°, and the Airy minimum of the secondary bow A1(S) is located at θ_c.m. = 77°.
- The Airy minimum A3 crosses 90° at around E_L = 75 MeV.
- The Airy minimum A2 crosses 90° at E_L = 102 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Airy minima in 12C+12C scattering by McVoy and Brandan (1992), who identified three Airy elephants and predicted a final elephant above 130 MeV, and by Michel and Ohkubo (2004), who showed that the minimum at 74 MeV is actually Airy minimum A3. The study also builds on Demyanova et al. (2010), who concluded no Airy minimum crosses 90° above 130 MeV for the primary rainbow. The discovery of the dynamically generated secondary rainbow, first found in 16O+12C scattering by Ohkubo and Hirabayashi (2014) and confirmed in 13C+12C (2015) and 12C+12C (2025), enables resolution of the Airy elephant puzzle and suggests that experimental measurement of the 90° excitation function between E_L = 130-220 MeV is now highly desirable.
