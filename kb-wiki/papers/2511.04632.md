# Electromagnetic and Exotic Moments in Nuclear DFT

> Electromagnetic interactions serve as essential probes for studying and testing our understanding of the atomic nucleus, as they reveal emergent properties across the nuclear chart. We analyse their corresponding observables, which relate to charge and current distributions in nuclei expressed through their multipole components. We focus on theoretical results obtained within nuclear density functional theory (DFT) to derive self-consistent, symmetry-restored nuclear wave functions along with their spectroscopic multipole moments. We demonstrate how these compare with experimental data. We also discuss potential improvements in the formulation of magnetic dipole operators by including two-body meson-exchange contributions. Discussions of exotic symmetry-breaking moments emphasise their importance for understanding fine details of fundamental nuclear interactions. Detailed derivations are provided in the accompanying Supplemental Material.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear density functional theory (DFT) with symmetry restoration can reproduce experimental electromagnetic moments across the nuclear chart, but the agreement is qualitative rather than exact; the authors explicitly note that "nearly none of the data exactly matches" the single-particle estimates, and that determining exact values of multipole moments "remains a challenge across various theoretical approaches."

## Method

The authors derive electromagnetic multipole moments from self-consistent, symmetry-restored nuclear wave functions obtained within nuclear DFT, using both relativistic and non-relativistic formulations. They express electric and magnetic multipole moments as integrals over charge, current, and spin densities, and compare these theoretical results with experimental spectroscopic moments compiled from databases. The paper also discusses improvements to magnetic dipole operators by including two-body meson-exchange contributions and examines exotic symmetry-breaking moments.

## Context

This review extends earlier shell-model descriptions of electromagnetic moments, including the Nilsson deformed mean-field studies (Nilsson, 1955), and recent DFT analyses that have only recently been applied to odd nuclei where electromagnetic moments are directly measurable. The work connects to experimental compilations by Stone (2019, 2020, 2021) and a recent review by Georgette (2025). The paper enables future systematic comparisons between DFT predictions and experimental data across the nuclear chart, and opens the question of how to incorporate two-body meson-exchange contributions into magnetic dipole operators for improved accuracy.
