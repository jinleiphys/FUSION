# Hyper-order baryon number fluctuations at finite temperature and density

> Fluctuations of conserved charges are sensitive to the QCD phase transition and a possible critical endpoint in the phase diagram at finite density. In this work, we compute the baryon number fluctuations up to tenth order at finite temperature and density. This is done in a QCD-assisted effective theory that accurately captures the quantum- and in-medium effects of QCD at low energies. A direct computation at finite density allows us to assess the applicability of expansions around vanishing density. By using different freeze-out scenarios in heavy-ion collisions, we translate these results into baryon number fluctuations as a function of collision energy. We show that a non-monotonic energy dependence of baryon number fluctuations can arise in the non-critical crossover region of the phase diagram. Our results compare well with recent experimental measurements of the kurtosis and the sixth-order cumulant of the net-proton distribution from the STAR collaboration. They indicate that the experimentally observed non-monotonic energy dependence of fourth-order net-proton fluctuations is highly non-trivial. It could be an experimental signature of an increasingly sharp chiral crossover and may indicate a QCD critical point. The physics implications and necessary upgrades of our analysis are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The authors compute baryon number fluctuations up to tenth order at finite temperature and density using a QCD-assisted low-energy effective theory, and show that a non-monotonic energy dependence of these fluctuations can arise even in the non-critical crossover region of the QCD phase diagram, without requiring a critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors use a QCD-assisted Polyakov-loop--enhanced quark-meson (PQM) low-energy effective theory with two light quark flavors, where quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations are treated with the functional renormalisation group (fRG). The strange quark is not included dynamically but its effect on the momentum-scale running is accounted for by a linear scale-matching procedure that adjusts the temperature and chemical potential scales of the 2-flavor results to match 2+1-flavor QCD, using the kurtosis ratio R42^B and the curvature of the chiral phase boundary as matching observables. The baryon number susceptibilities are then obtained as derivatives of the thermodynamic potential with respect to baryon chemical potential, and are translated into collision-energy-dependent fluctuations using phenomenological freeze-out curves.

## Key numbers

* The temperature scale-matching factor between the 2-flavor LEFT and 2+1-flavor QCD is c_T = 1.247(12), determined by fitting the kurtosis ratio R42^B to lattice results from Borsanyi et al. (2018).
* The chemical potential scale-matching factor is c_muB = 1.110(66), derived from the curvature of the chiral phase boundary using kappa = 0.0153(18) from Borsanyi et al. (2020) and the LEFT curvature kappa_LEFT = 0.0193.
* The chiral crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential in the LEFT is adjusted to match the 2+1-flavor lattice result, with the curvature of the phase boundary given by kappa approximately 0.015 from both functional and lattice computations.
* The QCD-assisted LEFT produces baryon number fluctuations up to tenth order, with the kurtosis and sixth-order cumulant compared to experimental measurements from the STAR collaboration.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon number fluctuations up to fourth order by Fu et al. (2015, 2016) to hyper-order fluctuations (sixth through tenth order), using a significantly upgraded QCD-assisted effective theory that is directly embedded in functional QCD results. The approach builds on first-principles QCD computations of the phase boundary by Fischer et al. (2018), Fu et al. (2019), and Braun et al. (2019), which show a crossover that becomes sharper with increasing baryon chemical potential. The results enable direct comparison with experimental data from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program and provide predictions for fluctuation observables at collision energies where no experimental results are yet available, while also allowing assessment of the reliability of Taylor expansions around vanishing density commonly used in lattice QCD.
