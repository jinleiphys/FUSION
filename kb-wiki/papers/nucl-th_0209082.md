# Dependence of calculated binding energies and widths of $η$-mesic nuclei on treatment of subthreshold $η$-nucleon interaction

> We demonstrate that the binding energies and widths of eta-mesic nuclei depend strongly on subthreshold eta-N interaction. This strong dependence is made evident from comparing three different eta-nucleus optical potentials: (1) a microscopic optical potential taking into account the full effects of off-shell eta-nucleon interactions; (2) a factorization approximation to the microscopic optical potential where a downward energy shift parameter is introduced to approximate the subthreshold eta-nucleon interaction; and (3) an optical potential using on-shell eta-nucleon scattering length as the interaction input. Our analysis indicates that the in-medium $η$N interaction for bound-state formation is about 30 MeV below the free-space $η$N threshold, which causes a substantial reduction of the attractive force between the $η$ and nucleon with respect to that implied by the scattering length. Consequently, the scattering-length approach overpredicts the binding energies and caution must be exercised when these latter predictions are used as guide in searching for $η$-nucleus bound states. We also show that final-state-interaction analysis cannot provide an unequivocal determination of the existence of $η$-nucleus bound state. More direct measurements are, therefore, necessary.

## Key claim

The binding energies and widths of η-mesic nuclei depend strongly on the subthreshold η-nucleon interaction, with the in-medium ηN interaction for bound-state formation occurring about 30 MeV below the free-space ηN threshold, causing a substantial reduction of the attractive force compared to that implied by the scattering length.

## Method

The authors compared three different η-nucleus optical potentials: (1) a fully off-shell microscopic optical potential using a separable model for the ηN t-matrix based on the N*(1535) resonance; (2) a factorization approximation to the microscopic potential with an ad-hoc downward energy shift parameter Δ; and (3) an on-shell optical potential using only the free-space ηN scattering length as input. They solved the momentum-space relativistic three-dimensional integral equation using the inverse-iteration method to obtain binding energies and widths for nuclei with A ≥ 3.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium ηN interaction for bound-state formation is about 30 MeV below the free-space ηN threshold
- For the model of Bhalerao and Liu, the effective in-medium scattering length at Δ=30 MeV is a^eff_ηN = (0.23 + 0.09i) fm, compared to the threshold value a_ηN = (0.28 + 0.19i) fm
- The reduction factor R(Δ) is about 0.89 at Δ=20 MeV and 0.82 at Δ=30 MeV for the Bhalerao-Liu model
- For the model of Green and Wycech, at 30 MeV below threshold a^eff_ηN = (0.45 + 0.08i) fm, compared to a_ηN = (0.75 + 0.27i) fm at threshold (reduction of more than 1/3 of the real part)
- The real part of a_ηN from various models ranges from as low as 0.270 fm to as high as 1.000 fm, while the imaginary part varies between 0.190 fm and 0.399 fm
- For light nuclei (A ≤ 12), a decrease of Re a_ηN by a factor of about 2 causes ε_η to decrease by a factor greater than 10

## Context

This work extends the authors' own 1986 prediction of η-mesic nuclei (Haider 1986) and the coupled-channel analysis of Bhalerao and Liu which found attractive near-threshold ηN interaction. The study contradicts the common use of free-space scattering lengths from models such as those of Green and Wycech or Batinić et al. for predicting η-nucleus bound states, showing that such on-shell approaches overpredict binding energies. The analysis also revisits the final-state-interaction approach of Wilkin (1993) and Willis et al. for η-3He and η-4He systems, demonstrating that FSI analysis cannot provide an unequivocal determination of the existence of η-nucleus bound states, and that more direct measurements are necessary.
