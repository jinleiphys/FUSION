# Differential isospin-fractionation in dilute asymmetric nuclear matter

> The differential isospin-fractionation (IsoF) during the liquid-gas phase transition in dilute asymmetric nuclear matter is studied as a function of nucleon momentum. Within a self-consistent thermal model it is shown that the neutron/proton ratio of the gas phase becomes {\it smaller} than that of the liquid phase for energetic nucleons, although the gas phase is overall more neutron-rich. Clear indications of the differential IsoF consistent with the thermal model predictions are demonstrated within a transport model for heavy-ion reactions. Future comparisons with experimental data will allow us to extract critical information about the momentum dependence of the isovector strong interaction.

## Key claim
The paper shows that, during the liquid-gas phase transition in dilute asymmetric nuclear matter, the neutron/proton ratio of the gas phase becomes *smaller* than that of the liquid phase for energetic nucleons (above about 220 MeV kinetic energy for one parameter set), even though the gas phase is overall more neutron-rich when integrated over all momenta. This reversal, called "anti-IsoF," is predicted by a self-consistent thermal model and supported by transport model simulations of heavy-ion reactions.

## Method
The authors used a self-consistent thermal model with the isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction to compute the phase coexistence of liquid and gas phases in asymmetric nuclear matter via the Gibbs conditions. They then calculated the differential isospin-fractionation as the double neutron/proton ratio (gas over liquid) as a function of nucleon kinetic energy. Separately, they performed transport model simulations (IBUU04) for central collisions of 124Sn+124Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon and 132Sn+124Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon, using a density cut at 0.13ρ₀ to separate gas and liquid phases.

## Key numbers
- At T=10 MeV and P=0.1 MeV/fm³ with x=0: gas density ρ_G = 0.087ρ₀ (ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³), gas asymmetry δ_G = 0.791; liquid density ρ_L = 0.763ρ₀, liquid asymmetry δ_L = 0.296.
- At T=10 MeV and P=0.1 MeV/fm³ with x=-1: gas density ρ_G = 0.114ρ₀, gas asymmetry δ_G = 0.808; liquid density ρ_L = 0.714ρ₀, liquid asymmetry δ_L = 0.30.
- Integrated double neutron/proton ratios: 5.07 for x=-1 and 4.65 for x=0 at the same T and P.
- For x=0, a reversal of the normal IsoF occurs for nucleons with kinetic energies higher than about 220 MeV.
- The MDI interaction gives an incompressibility K₀ = 211 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin-fractionation (IsoF) during the liquid-gas phase transition, which was previously observed only as an integrated (momentum-averaged) phenomenon in thermodynamic models and experiments such as those by Xu et al. (2000) at NSCL/MSU. The differential (momentum-dependent) analysis reveals a reversal of the normal IsoF for energetic nucleons, providing a new observable that is sensitive to the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential. This enables future experimental comparisons to extract information about the isovector strong interaction, which is more fundamental than the density-dependent symmetry energy constrained by isospin diffusion data from the NSCL/MSU (2004).
