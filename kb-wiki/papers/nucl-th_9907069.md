# The Anapole Form Factor of the Deuteron

> We find the leading order result for the anapole form factor of the deuteron using effective field theory. The momentum dependence of the anapole form factor is different from that of the matrix element of the strange currents in the deuteron, which may provide a way for disentangling these two competing effects when analyzing parity violating electron-deuteron scattering experiments. We give closed form expressions for both the form factor and integrals often encountered in the NN effective field theory.

## Key claim

The leading-order anapole form factor of the deuteron, computed using effective field theory, has a momentum dependence that differs from the matrix element of strange axial currents in the deuteron; this difference may allow experimental separation of the two competing parity-violating effects in electron-deuteron scattering, provided data can be taken at sufficiently low momentum transfer where the effective field theory expansion is valid.

## Method

The authors computed the deuteron anapole form factor at leading order (LO) in the KSW power-counting scheme of effective field theory, retaining full dependence on the three-momentum transfer from the virtual photon. The calculation separated the form factor into three contributions: single nucleon anapole contributions, magnetic coupling contributions (from the isovector magnetic moment of the nucleon), and electric coupling contributions (from minimal coupling to pions and non-relativistic nucleons). Closed-form analytic expressions were obtained for each contribution using dimensional regularization and position-space methods, with integrals expressed in terms of elementary functions and dilogarithms.

## Key numbers

- The strong interaction four-nucleon contact coefficient in the triplet channel is \(C_0^{(\siii)} = -5.51\ \text{fm}^2\), renormalized at \(\mu = m_\pi\) in the PDS scheme.
- The pion decay constant is \(f = 132\ \text{MeV}\), the axial coupling constant is \(g_A \sim 1.25\), and the isoscalar and isovector magnetic moments are \(\kappa_0 = 0.44\) and \(\kappa_1 = 2.35\) in units of nuclear magnetons.
- The parity-violating amplitude at LO is given by \(\mathcal{A}^{(pv)}(|\mathbf{k}|) = 10^{-7} \left( 5.4\ g_{\Delta S} F_C(|\mathbf{k}|) - 0.21 \left( h^{(1)}_{\pi NN} / 10^{-7} \right) \tilde{A}_D(|\mathbf{k}|) \right) (1/M_N^2) \epsilon^{abi} \overline{e}\gamma_i e\ \varepsilon^*_a \varepsilon_b\).
- The binding momentum of the deuteron is \(\gamma = \sqrt{M_N B}\), where \(B\) is the deuteron binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the deuteron anapole moment (Savage and Springer, 1999) by generalizing the moment to the full momentum-dependent form factor, increasing the possibility of separating anapole effects from strange quark contributions in parity-violating electron-deuteron scattering. The calculation uses the KSW power-counting scheme (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) and methods from Binger (1999), and contrasts with the zero-range potential limit calculation by Khriplovich and Korkin (1999), whose result the authors argue does not have the correct behavior in the chiral limit. The results enable future extraction of both the strange axial matrix element \(g_{\Delta s}\) and the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) from electron scattering experiments, with the expectation that higher-order calculations (at NLO and beyond) will improve precision.
