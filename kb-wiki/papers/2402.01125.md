# Nakanishi integral representation of pseudoscalar fermion-antifermion bound states in the Bethe--Salpeter equation

> We propose a method to solve for the pseudoscalar bound state amplitude of a fermion and an antifermion from its Bethe--Salpeter equation (BSE) in the Minkowski space applying spectral representations. With the dressing of the fermion propagators specified by their spectral functions, we first derive the explicit expressions for the Nakanishi spectral functions of the Bethe--Salpeter wave function (BSWF) in terms of these propagators and the Bethe--Salpeter amplitude (BSA) directly from the definition in the momentum space. Applying the rainbow-ladder truncation in the covariant gauge, we then convert the BSE in the momentum space into identities that calculate the spectral functions of the BSA from those of the gauge-boson propagator and of the BSWF. These relations form a closed set of linear functionals for the Nakanishi spectral functions with analytical kernels, laying the foundation for the numerical solution of the BSE in the Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar fermion-antifermion bound states directly in Minkowski space using Nakanishi spectral representations, but only within the rainbow-ladder truncation in covariant gauge with dressed fermion propagators specified by their spectral functions, and the method is presented as laying the foundation for numerical solution rather than providing actual numerical results.

## Method

The author first derives explicit expressions for the Nakanishi spectral functions of the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF) in terms of the fermion propagator spectral functions and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) spectral functions, starting from the momentum-space definition of the BSWF. Applying the rainbow-ladder truncation in covariant gauge, the Bethe-Salpeter equation in momentum space is then converted into integral identities that calculate the spectral functions of the BSA from those of the gauge-boson propagator and of the BSWF, using linear functionals (D_-, D_+, P_-, K_-) that act on the Nakanishi spectral functions to represent operations such as changing denominator indices and multiplying by momentum variables.

- The BSA decomposition uses 4 scalar components with basis T_j(k,P) = (1, slashed{P}, slashed{k}, -i sigma_{kP}) multiplied by gamma_5, as given in Eq. (1)
- The Nakanishi integral representation denominator index n_j is required to satisfy n_j >= 1 for each scalar component j
- The angular integration limits z^-_th and z^+_th are usually chosen to be -1 and 1 respectively
- The spacetime metric is g^{mu nu} = {1, -1, -1, ..., -1} such that momentum p^mu is timelike when p^2 >= 0
- The number of space-time dimensions d is set to 4 after dimensional regularization

## Context

This work extends the analytical method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with Nakanishi spectral representations from scalar bound states of scalar particles (Kusaka 1995, Kusaka 1997, Jia 2023) to pseudoscalar bound states of fermions. The approach differs from indirect methods for solving Nakanishi spectral functions (Carbonell 2010, Frederico 2013, Karmanov 2021) by working directly from the native momentum-space BSE formulation. The method enables future numerical solution of the BSE in Minkowski space for pseudoscalar mesons, complementing the usual Euclidean-space solutions after Wick rotation (Maris 1999, Jia 2024).
