# Antikaon-nucleus dynamics: from quasibound states to kaon condensation

> Coupled-channel Kbar-N dynamics near threshold and its repercussions in few-body Kbar-nuclear systems are briefly reviewed, highlighting studies of a K^-pp quasibound state. In heavier nuclei, the extension of mean-field calculations to multi-Kbar nuclear and hypernuclear quasibound states is discussed. It is concluded that strangeness in finite self-bound systems is realized through hyperons, with no room for kaon condensation.

# Antikaon-nucleus dynamics: from quasibound states to kaon condensation

## Key claim

The paper concludes that strangeness in finite self-bound systems is realized through hyperons, with no room for kaon condensation under strong-interaction equilibrium conditions in terrestrial experiments, though this conclusion does not apply to kaon condensation in neutron stars where equilibrium is determined by weak interactions.

## Method

The paper reviews coupled-channel chiral SU(3) calculations of the Kbar-N interaction near threshold, few-body Faddeev and variational calculations for the K-pp system, and relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for single- and multi-Kbar nuclear quasibound states across nuclei from 12C to 208Pb. The RMF calculations scan over omega vector-field and sigma scalar-field coupling strengths, with Kbar coupling constants chosen to obey F-type SU(3) symmetry, to determine separation energies and widths as functions of the number of Kbar mesons.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg term gives V_Kbar = -55 MeV at nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, while the in-medium coupled-channel approach yields Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ -110 MeV
- K- atom data fits yield nuclear-matter depths -Re V_Kbar(rho_0) ~ 150-200 MeV at threshold
- The FINUDA collaboration reported a K-pp deeply bound state with (B, Gamma) = (115, 67) MeV; the DISTO collaboration reported (B, Gamma) = (103, 118) MeV
- Calculated K-pp binding energies from coupled-channel Faddeev calculations range from 50-95 MeV, with mesonic widths 45-110 MeV and nonmesonic widths ~20 MeV
- RMF calculations for single-Kbar quasibound states yield separation energies in the range 100-150 MeV for potentials compatible with K- atom data
- The omega KK coupling constant from SU(3) symmetry is g_omegaKK = 3.02, derived from g_rho_pipi = 6.04

## Context

This review extends earlier work on Kbar-nuclear quasibound states, particularly the K-pp system studied by the FINUDA collaboration (Agnello et al., 2005) and the DISTO collaboration (Yamazaki et al., 2008), and multi-Kbar calculations by Gazda et al. (2008, 2009). It contrasts with shallower potentials (40-60 MeV depth) obtained by Watson-like self-consistency requirements (Ramos and Oset, 2000; Cieply et al., 2001). The finding that multi-strange hypernuclei remain the lowest-energy configuration for multi-strange systems, consistent with earlier work by Schaffner et al. (1993), rules out kaon condensation in finite nuclei under strong-interaction equilibrium. Future experiments at GSI and J-PARC will explore the K-pp system in p(p,K+) and 3He(K-,n) reactions.
