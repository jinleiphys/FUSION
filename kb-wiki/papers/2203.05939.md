# Opportunities for new physics searches with heavy ions at colliders

> Opportunities for searches for phenomena beyond the Standard Model (BSM) using heavy-ions beams at high energies are outlined. Different BSM searches proposed in the last years in collisions of heavy ions, mostly at the Large Hadron Collider, are summarized. A few concrete selected cases are reviewed including searches for axion-like particles, anomalous $Žä$ electromagnetic moments, magnetic monopoles, and dark photons. Expectations for the achievable sensitivities of these searches in the coming years are given. Studies of CP violation in hot and dense QCD matter and connections to ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays physics are also mentioned.

## Key claim

Heavy-ion collisions at the LHC offer unique and complementary opportunities to search for beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) phenomena compared to proton-proton collisions, particularly for light and feebly-interacting particles, but the achievable sensitivities depend on the specific integrated luminosities delivered in future runs and on the development of dedicated trigger and reconstruction strategies for low-transverse-momentum signatures.

## Method

The paper reviews selected BSM search channels proposed for heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, focusing on photon-photon interactions in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) for axion-like particles, anomalous tau electromagnetic moments, magnetic monopoles, and dark photons. It summarizes the projected LHC heavy-ion luminosities for Runs 3 and 4 based on detailed simulations of beam parameters and filling patterns, and presents expected sensitivities for each search channel using existing or future datasets from the ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, and MoEDAL experiments.

## Context

This white paper extends the discussions submitted to the European Particle Physics Strategy Update (Bruce et al., 2018) and incorporates presentations from the 2021 ECT*-Trento workshop "Heavy Ions and New Physics" into the US Snowmass 2022 process. It builds on prior proposals for BSM searches in heavy-ion collisions, including axion-like particle searches via light-by-light scattering (Knapen, 2016; Bauer, 2017), tau anomalous moment measurements in UPCs (Beresford, 2019; Dyndal, 2020), and magnetic monopole production via the Schwinger mechanism (Gould, 2017; Gould, 2019). The paper enables future experimental searches by providing concrete luminosity projections and sensitivity estimates for the LHC Runs 3 and 4, and identifies synergies needed among accelerator, experiment, and theory communities to exploit these opportunities.
