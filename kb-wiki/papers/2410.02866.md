# Recent developments in relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations

> The study of thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics has led to numerous important developments in the last decade. We present a bird's eye view of the recent advances on the theory of fluctuations on three fronts; stochastic hydrodynamics, hydro-kinetics where fluctuations are included as additional modes that satisfy deterministic evolution equations, and effective field theory formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics. We compare and contrast these different but complimentary frameworks and highlight various recent progresses in each of them.

## Key claim

This review argues that three complementary frameworks, stochastic hydrodynamics, hydro-kinetics, and effective field theory, each provide distinct but overlapping perspectives on thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics, but all share the common feature that many open questions remain, particularly regarding the inclusion of velocity fluctuations and the full understanding of non-Gaussian fluctuations.

## Method

The paper surveys recent theoretical developments across three approaches: stochastic hydrodynamics (where hydrodynamic equations are promoted to stochastic evolution equations with noise), hydro-kinetics (where fluctuations are treated as additional deterministic modes with their own evolution equations), and effective field theory (where fluctuating hydrodynamics is formulated via the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism with symmetries including local KMS symmetry). The review uses nonlinear diffusion as a common example to illustrate how each framework handles fluctuations, dissipation, and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- 3+1 dimensional simulations represent the current state-of-the-art in stochastic hydrodynamic simulations (SINGH2019319)
- The diffusion constant D is given by D = λ/(χT), where λ is the transport coefficient, χ is the susceptibility, and T is temperature
- New transport coefficients (ϑ₁, ϑ₂, ϑ₃) appear in KMS blocks at next-to-leading order, with effects appearing at two-loops in the retarded two-point correlator proportional to ωk²
- Long-time tail corrections to the retarded two-point correlator scale as ω^(1/2)k²

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations that has seen major developments over the last decade, building on foundational formulations such as the Martin-Siggia-Rose formalism (Martin 1973) and earlier effective actions for ideal relativistic fluids (Brown 1992, and works by Bhattacharya, Banerjee, Jensen, and Haehl from 2012-2013). The paper compares stochastic approaches (SINGH2019319), hydro-kinetic frameworks (An 2019, An 2020, An 2022), and EFT formulations (Crossley 2015, Glorioso 2017, Jain 2020) to highlight their overlaps and remaining challenges. The review enables future work on observable signals of fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, particularly for the search for the QCD critical point, and identifies open questions including the need for full 3+1 dimensional Metropolis simulations of relativistic hydrodynamics and the incomplete understanding of how velocity fluctuations appear in the hydro-kinetic and EFT approaches.
