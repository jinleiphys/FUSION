# Delta Baryons in Neutron-Star Matter under Strong Magnetic Fields

> In this work, we study magnetic field effects on neutron star matter containing the baryon octet and additional heavier spin 3/2 baryons (the $Δ$'s). We make use of two different relativistic hadronic models that contain an additional vector-isovector self interaction for the mesons: one version of a relativistic mean field (RMF) model and the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model. We find that both the additional interaction and a strong magnetic field enhance the $Δ$ baryon population in dense matter, while decreasing the relative density of hyperons. At the same time that the vector-isovector meson interaction modifies neutron-star masses very little ($<0.1~M_\odot$), it decreases their radii considerably, allowing both models to be in better agreement with observations. Together, these features indicate that magnetic neutron stars are likely to contain $Δ$ baryons in their interior.

# Delta Baryons in Neutron-Star Matter under Strong Magnetic Fields

## Key claim
Strong magnetic fields (around 3×10¹⁸ G) enhance the population of Δ baryons in neutron-star matter while suppressing hyperons, but only when the vector-isovector ωρ meson self-interaction is included; without this interaction, the magnetic field effects on Δ populations are less pronounced and the opposite trend (hyperon enhancement) has been reported in prior work by Thapa (2020).

## Method
The authors employed two relativistic hadronic models, a modified Walecka-type relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the GM1 parametrization and the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, both augmented with an additional ωρ vector-isovector self-interaction. They solved the field equations at zero temperature under charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium, treating charged fermions in a constant external magnetic field via Landau level quantization, with leptons (electrons and muons) as a free Fermi gas. The Δ baryon coupling was varied through a free parameter β (for RMF) or r_V (for CMF), and particle populations were computed as functions of baryon density for magnetic field strengths up to 3×10¹⁸ G.

## Key numbers
- The Δ baryon is 292 MeV heavier than a neutron (the lightest spin-3/2 baryon).
- For the RMF model with GM1ωρ parametrization and β=1.0, Δ⁻ baryons reach 3% of total baryon density at n_B ≈ 0.8 fm⁻³ (without magnetic field).
- The ωρ interaction reduces the symmetry energy slope from 94 MeV (original GM1) to 69 MeV (GM1ωρ).
- The ωρ interaction modifies neutron-star masses by less than 0.1 M☉ but decreases radii considerably.
- The CMF model with ωρ interaction produces maximum stellar masses of approximately 2.1 M☉ for stars containing nucleons and hyperons.
- A constant magnetic field of B = 3×10¹⁸ G was used, corresponding to the largest field strength predicted in magnetar cores.
- The number of occupied Landau levels for protons at high density is ≤ 5.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of Δ baryons in neutron stars dating back to Glendenning (1982) and recent investigations by Thapa (2020) and others, but is the first to combine strong magnetic fields with the ωρ vector-isovector self-interaction. The ωρ interaction was previously shown to improve agreement with neutron-skin data (Horowitz, 2002), neutron-star radii (Schramm, 2002; Bizarro, 2015), and tidal deformability constraints (Dexheimer, 2018). The results indicate that magnetic neutron stars are likely to contain Δ baryons, and the EoS tables have been made available in the CompOSE repository for use in transport calculations and further astrophysical modeling. The authors note that a full general-relativistic treatment of magnetized stars (including anisotropic pressure and Lorentz forces) is left to future work.
