# Productions of $^3_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He in different coalescence channels in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV

> We study the productions of $Λ$-hypernuclei $^3_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He in the coalescence mechanism in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV. Considering the abundance and great importance of baryons and light (hyper-)nuclei on the collision dynamics, we include not only nucleon$+Λ$ coalescence but also nucleus+nucleon($Λ$) coalescence. We present contributions from different coalescence channels for $^3_Λ$H, $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He in their productions. We predict the production asymmetry between $^4_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$He, characterized by yield ratios $^4_Λ\text{He}/^4_Λ\text{H}$ and $(^4_Λ\text{H}-^4_Λ\text{He})/(^4_Λ\text{H}+^4_Λ\text{He})$, which can shed light on the existence constraints of the possible neutron-$Λ$ bound states $^2_Λn~(nΛ)$ and $^3_Λn~(nnΛ)$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the production asymmetry between $^4_{\Lambda}$H and $^4_{\Lambda}$He, quantified by yield ratios, can be significantly enlarged if the possible neutron-$\Lambda$ bound states $^2_{\Lambda}n$ and $^3_{\Lambda}n$ exist, because these states would enhance $^4_{\Lambda}$H production more strongly than $^4_{\Lambda}$He production; comparisons with future measurements can therefore constrain the existence of these bound states.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed analytical coalescence model (Wang, 2022) to the strange sector, applying it to Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV. The model uses a two-step method: first, primordial nucleons and $\Lambda$ hyperons coalesce into light (hyper-)nuclei via nucleon+nucleon/hyperon coalescence; second, previously formed nuclei (such as deuterons and tritons) capture remaining primordial nucleons and/or $\Lambda$ hyperons to form heavier hypernuclei. Analytical formulas for two-body, three-body, and four-body coalescence are derived using a spherical harmonic oscillator wave function and a factorized joint coordinate-momentum distribution, with the effective source radius taken from HBT interferometry.

- For three-body coalescence, the coordinate integral yields $\mathcal {A}_{H_j} = \frac{8^2 \sigma_{31}^3} { (C_1 R_f^2+\sigma_{31}^2) \sqrt{C_1 (R_f/\gamma)^2+\sigma_{31}^2} } \times \frac{\sigma_{32}^3}{ (C_2 R_f^2+\sigma_{32}^2) \sqrt{C_2 (R_f/\gamma)^2+\sigma_{32}^2}}$, with $C_1=2$ and $C_2=8/3$.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model previously developed for light nuclei (Wang, 2022) to the strange sector for hypernuclei production in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV. It builds on experimental measurements by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2021; STAR, 2022) of hypernuclei up to mass number $A=4$ at this energy, and on theoretical coalescence models (Sun, 2018; Sun, 2016; Liu, 2024) and thermal models (Andronic, 2010; Vovchenko, 2018). The predictions for the yield ratios $^4_{\Lambda}\text{He}/^4_{\Lambda}\text{H}$ and $(^4_{\Lambda}\text{H}-^4_{\Lambda}\text{He})/(^4_{\Lambda}\text{H}+^4_{\Lambda}\text{He})$ can be tested against future STAR data to constrain the existence of the neutron-$\Lambda$ bound states $^2_{\Lambda}n$ and $^3_{\Lambda}n$.
