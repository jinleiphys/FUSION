# Non-linear superposition law and Skyrme crystals

> Exact configurations of the four dimensional Skyrme model are presented. The static configurations have the profile which behaves as a kink and, consequently, the corresponding energy momentum tensor describes a domain wall. Furthermore, a class of exact time periodic Skyrmions is discovered. Within such class, it is possible to disclose a remarkable phenomenon which is a genuine effect of the Skyrme term. For a special value of the frequency the Skyrmions admit a non linear superposition principle. One can combine two or more exact elementary Skyrmions (which may depend in a non trivial way on all the space like coordinates) into a new exact composite Skyrmion. Due to such superposition law, despite the explicit presence of non linear effects in the energy momentum tensor, the interaction energy between the elementary Skyrmions can be computed exactly. The relations with the appearance of Skyrme crystals is discussed.

## Key claim

Exact static and time-periodic Skyrmion configurations of the four-dimensional Skyrme model in its original form (without mass term or modifications) are constructed using a generalized hedgehog ansatz; for the special frequency value \(\omega = 1/\sqrt{\lambda}\), the time-periodic Skyrmions satisfy a linear Helmholtz equation, enabling a non-linear superposition principle where multiple elementary Skyrmions can be combined into exact composite Skyrmions, provided the sum of their defining functions does not exceed unity in absolute value.

## Method

The author employs a generalized hedgehog ansatz where the Skyrme field is parametrized by a profile function \(\alpha\) and an orientational function \(\Theta\), with the orthogonality condition \((\nabla_\mu \alpha)(\nabla^\mu \Theta)=0\). For static configurations, \(\Theta\) is taken as a linear function of spatial coordinates and \(\alpha\) depends on a single coordinate, reducing the field equations to a first-order differential equation whose solutions are kink-like domain walls. For time-periodic Skyrmions, \(\Theta = \omega t\) and \(\alpha\) depends on all spatial coordinates; at the resonant frequency \(\omega = 1/\sqrt{\lambda}\), the field equation transforms via the substitution \(H = \sin\alpha\) into the linear Helmholtz equation \(\triangle H + (1/\lambda)H = 0\), allowing superposition of solutions.

## Key numbers

* The resonant frequency for the non-linear superposition law is \(\omega^\ast = 1/\sqrt{\lambda}\), where \(\lambda\) is the Skyrme coupling constant.
* The gradient of the orientational function for static domain walls is \((\nabla\Theta)^2 = L^{-2} = ((n_2)^2 + (n_3)^2)l^{-2}\), with \(n_2, n_3 \in \mathbb{Z}\) and \(l\) a constant with dimension of length.
* The energy per unit area of the static domain wall is given by \(E_A = K \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} [L^{-1}\sin\alpha (1+\lambda L^{-2}\sin^2\alpha)^{1/2}] \alpha' \, dx\), which is convergent due to boundary conditions.
* For periodic Skyrmions on a torus, the wave numbers satisfy \(1/\lambda = \sum_{i=1}^3 \mu_i^2\) with \(\mu_i = 1/L_i\), where \(2\pi L_i\) are the spatial periods.

## Context

This work extends the generalized hedgehog ansatz introduced by Canfora (2012, 2013) to construct exact solutions of the original Skyrme model without the modifications that spoil its original motivations (such as those in Simple1, Simple2, Simple3, Simple4, Simple5, Simple6). It provides the first exact domain wall in four-dimensional Skyrme theory and the first example in a non-linear, non-integrable four-dimensional theory where a superposition law coexists with explicit non-linear effects in the energy-momentum tensor. The results shed light on the formation of composite structures like the numerically constructed Skyrme crystals of Sutcliffe (2007) and Manton (2004), and enable exact computation of interaction energies between elementary Skyrmions for any number \(N\).
