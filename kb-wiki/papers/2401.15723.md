# Exploring the compactness of $α$ cluster in $^{16}$O nuclei with relativistic $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions

> Probing the $α$ cluster of $^{16}$O with the relativistic $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions has raised great interest in the heavy ion community. However, the effects of the $α$ cluster on the soft hadron observables vary largely among different studies. In this paper, we explain the differences by the compactness of the $α$ cluster in oxygen, using iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic simulations with different initial state $α$ cluster configurations. We also find several observables, such as the intensive skewness of the $[p_{\rm T}]$ correlator $Γ_{p_{\rm T}}$, the harmonic flows $v_2\{2\}$, $v_2\{4\}$, $v_3\{2\}$, and the $v_n^2-δ[p_{\rm T}]$ correlations $ρ(v_{2}^{2}, [p_{\rm T}])$, $ρ(v_{3}^{2}, [p_{\rm T}])$ in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions are sensitive to the compactness of the $α$ cluster in the colliding nuclei, which can be used to constrain the configurations of $^{16}$O in the future. Our study serves as an important step toward the quantitative exploration of the $α$ cluster configuration in the light nuclei with relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the conflicting predictions in previous studies of how alpha clustering affects soft hadron observables in relativistic oxygen-oxygen collisions can be resolved by considering the compactness of the alpha cluster (the ratio of the cluster root-mean-square radius to the tetrahedron side length, r_alpha/l), rather than assuming a single fixed cluster configuration.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hybrid hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU model, which combines TRENTo initial conditions, VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics for QGP expansion, and UrQMD for hadronic rescattering. They constructed four different nuclear density profiles for 16O: one Woods-Saxon distribution and three tetrahedral alpha-cluster configurations with varying compactness (r_alpha/l = 0.67, 0.44, 0.30), all constrained to the same root-mean-square radius of 2.73 fm. Approximately 50,000 hydrodynamic events were simulated for each density at sqrt(s_NN) = 6.5 TeV for the top 50% centrality.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square radius of free alpha nuclei is r_alpha = 1.71 fm, used as a reference for cluster compactness.
- The tetrahedral configurations used side lengths l = 3.0, 3.6, and 4.0 fm with corresponding cluster radii r_alpha = 2.0, 1.6, and 1.2 fm, giving r_alpha/l ratios of 0.67, 0.44, and 0.30.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters were R = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, and omega = -0.051 fm.
- The root-mean-square radius of 16O was fixed to 2.73 fm from nuclear structure experiments.
- The iEBE-VISHNU parameters included (eta/s)_min = 0.11, T_switch = 0.151 GeV, and normalization = 17 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha clustering in 16O using relativistic heavy ion collisions, which had produced conflicting predictions about the magnitude of cluster effects on flow observables. Prior studies using configurations from Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) calculations gave different results, which the authors attribute to different effective r_alpha/l values. The paper enables future experimental constraints on the compactness of alpha clusters in 16O using the proposed observables, particularly from the upcoming oxygen-oxygen collision programs at RHIC and the LHC.
