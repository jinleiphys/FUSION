# Nucleus-nucleus potentials in the scattering of tightly and weakly bound systems

> \begin{description} \item[Background] Fusion reactions play an important role in nucleosynthesis and in applications to society. Yet they remain challenging to model. \item[Purpose] In this work, we investigate the features of the nucleus-nucleus potentials that describe fusion cross sections and compare with those needed for realistic calculations of elastic scattering and other direct-reaction cross sections. \item[Method] We perform coupled-channel calculations for studying elastic and fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier with a tightly bound projectile ($^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm). We also perform Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel calculations to study elastic ($^{8}$B+$^{58}$Ni) and fusion ($^{6}$Li+$^{198}$Pt) of loosely bound projectiles in the same energy regime. \item[Results] We contrast the coupled-channel results with those obtained in a single-channel solution with different assumptions for polarization potentials to shed light on the relevant absorption terms required for the two different reaction channels. \item[Conclusions] Our results suggest that different approximations may be required for modeling direct processes and for modeling fusion reactions. \end{description}

## Key claim

The paper argues that the polarization potentials and absorption terms that successfully describe elastic scattering and direct reactions may not be appropriate for calculating fusion cross sections, suggesting that different approximations are required for modeling direct processes versus fusion reactions.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel (CC) calculations for the tightly bound system 16O+144Sm and Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations for the weakly bound systems 8B+58Ni (elastic) and 6Li+198Pt (fusion), all at energies around the Coulomb barrier. They contrasted these full coupled-channel results with single-channel solutions using different assumptions for polarization potentials, specifically the Thompson-Nagarajan-Lilley weighted mean local polarization potential implemented in the fresco code, to examine the absorption terms required for each reaction channel.

## Key numbers

- For the short-range Woods-Saxon imaginary potential simulating fusion absorption, typical parameters are: depth W0 = 50 MeV, reduced radius rw = 1.0 fm, and diffuseness aw = 0.2 fm.
- The classical approximation for fusion is considered valid for energies E greater than or approximately equal to VB + 2 MeV, where VB is the Coulomb barrier height.
- The classical fusion cross section is given by sigma_cl = pi * lg^2 / k^2, where lg is the grazing angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to model fusion reactions in heavy-ion collisions, building on the coupled-channel framework developed by Thompson, Nagarajan, and Lilley (1989) and the CDCC method of Sakuragi, Yahiro, and Kamimura (1983, 1986). The study contrasts with prior systematic optical potential parameterizations such as those by Koning and Delaroche (2003) for nucleon scattering and Alvarez et al. (2003) for heavier systems, which were designed primarily for elastic scattering and direct reactions. The results enable future work to develop separate effective potentials tailored specifically for fusion calculations rather than relying on potentials fitted to elastic scattering data.
