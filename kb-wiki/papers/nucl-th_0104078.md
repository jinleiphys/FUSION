# Transverse Momentum Dependence of Anomalous J/ψSuppression in Pb-Pb Collisions

> The recently published data for $<p^2_t>$ for $J/ψ$ production in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV are analyzed. For low values of transverse energy $E_t$, where normal suppression dominates, $<p^2_t> (E_t)$ scales with the path length of the gluons which fuse to make the $J/ψ$. In the $E_t$ domain of anomalous suppression $<p^2_t> (E_t)$ is found to rise linearly with the relative amount of anomalous suppression. This empirical law is reproduced within an analytically solvable transport model which allows high $p_t$ $J/ψ$'s to escape anomalous suppression. Interpreted in this way, the data for $<p^2_t> (E_t)$ lead to an estimate of $t_A \sim 4$ fm/$c$ for the duration of anomalous suppression.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed linear rise of $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ with the relative amount of anomalous suppression in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV can be reproduced by a transport model where high-$p_t$ $J/\psi$'s escape the region of anomalous suppression, with the caveat that this interpretation depends on simplifying assumptions (Gaussian phase-space distribution, instantaneous suppression at a single time $t_A$).

## Method

The authors analyze published NA50 data for $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ of $J/\psi$ in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV as a function of transverse energy $E_t$. They separate the data into domains of normal suppression ($E_t < 50$ GeV) and anomalous suppression ($E_t > 50$ GeV), then plot $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ versus the calculated gluon path length $\ell_g$ for normal suppression and versus the relative anomalous suppression $S_A/S_{ob}$ for the anomalous domain. They develop an analytically solvable transport model where $J/\psi$'s evolve freely from production until a time $t_A$, at which all $J/\psi$'s inside a radius $r_A$ are suppressed, and compare the model predictions to the empirical linear relation.

## Key numbers

* Slope constant for normal suppression: $a_{gN} = (0.102 \pm 0.006) \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2/\text{fm}$, extracted from the linear fit of $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ versus gluon path length $\ell_g$ in the normal suppression domain.
* Slope of the linear relation in the anomalous domain: $\delta p_t^2 = (0.132 \pm 0.007) \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2$, from the plot of $\langle p_t^2 \rangle_{ob}$ versus $S_A/S_{ob}$ for $E_t > 50$ GeV.
* Estimated duration of anomalous suppression: $t_A \sim 4$ fm/$c$, derived from the transport model using $\delta p_t^2 = 0.132 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2$, $\langle p_t^2 \rangle_N = 1.77 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2$, and $R^2 = \frac{2}{5} \langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{Pb}}$.
* The normal suppression domain is defined as $E_t < 50$ GeV; anomalous suppression sets in for $E_t > 50$ GeV, with a fluctuation subdomain at $E_t > 100$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $J/\psi$ suppression in Pb-Pb collisions by the NA50 collaboration, building on earlier studies of gluon rescattering in the initial state (Gavin 1991, Hufner 1997, Blaizot 1999) and the escape of high-$p_t$ $J/\psi$'s from a quark-gluon plasma (Matsui 1986, Blaizot 1996, Karsch 1998). The authors show that the previously predicted saturation or decrease of $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ at high $E_t$ from initial-state rescattering is contradicted by the new data. The empirical linear relation between $\langle p_t^2 \rangle$ and $S_A/S_{ob}$ provides a new constraint for models of anomalous suppression, and the extracted $t_A \sim 4$ fm/$c$ enables comparison with fireball lifetimes of 5-7 fm/$c$ discussed by Rapp (1999).
