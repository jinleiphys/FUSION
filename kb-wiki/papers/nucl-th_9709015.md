# Nucleon and nuclear spin structure functions

> Nuclear effects in polarized inelastic electron scattering off polarized $^3He$ and polarized $^2H$ are discussed; in the resonance region, Fermi motion effects are found to be much larger than in deep inelastic scattering. It is shown that improperly describing nuclear dynamics would lead to the extraction of unreliable neutron spin structure functions; on the other hand side, simple and workable equations, relating the Gerasimov -- Drell -- Hearn Integral for the neutron to the corresponding quantity for $^3He$ and $^2H$, are proposed. Nuclear effects in the recent E143 data are estimated by a proper procedure.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in polarized inelastic electron scattering off polarized $^3He$ and $^2H$ are much larger in the resonance region than in deep inelastic scattering, and improperly describing nuclear dynamics would lead to unreliable extraction of neutron spin structure functions; however, simple equations relating the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral for the neutron to the corresponding quantity for $^3He$ and $^2H$ remain workable for the integrated quantities.

## Method

The authors calculated the spin structure function ${\cal G}_1^A$ for polarized $^3He$ and $^2H$ targets using a convolution model that incorporates the spin-dependent spectral function of the nucleus and the nucleon spin structure functions. For $^3He$, they used the spectral function from Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1996) and for $^2H$ the Bonn potential wave function, with the nucleon spin structure functions in the resonance region modeled using the parametrization of Burkert and Li (1993) which includes contributions from the $P_{33}(1232)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ resonances. They also applied a linear algebra method to extract the isoscalar nucleon spin structure function from an analytical fit of the E143 deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon polarizations in $^3He$: $p_p = -0.030$ and $p_n = 0.88$, compared to world calculations giving $p_p = -0.028 \pm 0.004$ and $p_n = 0.86 \pm 0.02$
- The integral $I^{^3He}(Q^2)$ is approximated by the effective polarization formula with accuracy better than 5%
- In the deuteron at $Q^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$, nuclear effects in the resonance region are up to approximately 50% in the maxima of the resonances, compared to approximately 7-9% in deep inelastic scattering
- The integrals of the spin structure functions for the deuteron can be estimated with accuracy better than 3% by the simple formula $\Gamma_D(Q^2) = (1-3/2 P_D)(\Gamma_n(Q^2) + \Gamma_p(Q^2))/2$
- The deuteron binding energy is $\epsilon_D = -2.2246$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects in polarized structure functions from the deep inelastic scattering regime, previously studied by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1996) and Scopetta et al. (1997), into the resonance region where the planned experiments at TJNAF would operate. The authors show that while approximate formulas using effective nucleon polarizations (such as those used in DIS by the E143 collaboration) fail for the resonance region structure functions themselves, the integrals of these functions (including the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule) can still be reliably estimated using simple relations. The work enables proper extraction of neutron spin structure functions from upcoming polarized $^3He$ and $^2H$ data in the resonance region, using the linear algebra method proposed by Kaptari and Umnikov (1997).
