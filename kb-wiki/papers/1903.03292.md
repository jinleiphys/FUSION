# Update of a Multi-Phase Transport Model with Modern Parton Distribution Functions and Nuclear Shadowing

> A multi-phase transport (AMPT) model has been successful in explaining a wide range of observables in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this work, we implement a modern set of free proton parton distribution functions and an impact parameter-dependent nuclear shadowing in the AMPT model. After refitting the parameters of the two-component initial condition model to the experimental data on $pp$ and $p \bar p$ total and inelastic cross sections from $\sqrt s \sim $ 4 GeV to 13 TeV, we study particle productions in $pp$ and $AA$ collisions. We show that the updated AMPT model with string melting can reasonably describe the overall particle yields and transverse momentum spectra for both $pp$ and $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies after we introduce a nuclear scaling of the minijet transverse momentum cutoff for $AA$ collisions at LHC energies that is motivated by the color glass condensate. Since heavy flavor and high-$p_{\rm T}$ particles are produced by perturbative-QCD processes and thus directly depend on parton distribution functions of nuclei, the updated AMPT model is expected to provide a more reliable description of these observables.

# Update of a Multi-Phase Transport Model with Modern Parton Distribution Functions and Nuclear Shadowing

## Key claim

The updated AMPT model with CTEQ6.1M parton distribution functions and impact parameter-dependent EPS09sNLO nuclear shadowing can reasonably describe overall particle yields and transverse momentum spectra for both pp and AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, but only after introducing a nuclear scaling of the minijet transverse momentum cutoff for AA collisions at LHC energies that is motivated by color glass condensate physics. The model is expected to provide a more reliable description of heavy flavor and high-pT observables, though the paper shows no direct phenomenological improvements over the previous AMPT model for the observables studied.

## Method

The authors replaced the old Duke-Owens parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the AMPT model with the CTEQ6.1M set for free protons and implemented impact parameter-dependent EPS09sNLO nuclear shadowing functions. They refitted the energy-dependent parameters p0(s) and σsoft(s) of the HIJING two-component initial condition model to experimental pp and p-pbar total and inelastic cross section data from √s ≈ 4 GeV to 13 TeV. For AA collisions at LHC energies, they introduced an A-dependent scaling of the minijet transverse momentum cutoff p0, with the exponent q(s) parameterized as a function of collision energy, and adjusted the Lund string fragmentation parameter b from 0.4 to 0.15 GeV^-2 for AA collisions.

## Key numbers

- The fitted p0^pp(s) function ranges from approximately 2 GeV/c at low energies to over 6 GeV/c at the highest energies; the functional form is p0^pp(s) = -1.71 + 1.63 ln(√s) - 0.256 ln^2(√s) + 0.0167 ln^3(√s) with √s in GeV
- The fitted σsoft(s) function is given by σsoft(s) = 45.1 + 0.718 ln(√s) + 0.144 ln^2(√s) + 0.0185 ln^3(√s) in mb
- The nuclear scaling exponent q(s) for p0^AA = p0^pp * A^q(s) reaches 0.13 at √sNN = 10^5 GeV and approaches 0.16 at √sNN ≈ 10^7 GeV; q(s) = 0 for √sNN ≤ 200 GeV
- Lund fragmentation parameters used: a = 0.8 for both pp and AA collisions; b = 0.4 GeV^-2 for pp collisions and b = 0.15 GeV^-2 for AA collisions
- The model describes pp and p-pbar cross section data from √s ≈ 4 GeV to 13 TeV, and particle yields in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work updates the AMPT model (Lin 2004), which previously used the outdated Duke-Owens PDFs and a schematic nuclear shadowing parameterization from HIJING 1.0 (Wang 1991, Gyulassy 1994). The update follows a similar approach to the HIJING 2.0 model (Deng 2010), which replaced Duke-Owens PDFs with GRV PDFs. The EPS09sNLO nuclear shadowing (Eskola 2009) provides impact parameter dependence and is based on deep inelastic scattering, Drell-Yan, and RHIC pion production data. The nuclear scaling of p0 is motivated by color glass condensate saturation physics (McLerran 1993), where the saturation momentum Qs scales as A^(1/6). The updated model is expected to enable more reliable studies of heavy flavor and high-pT observables in future work, though the authors note that inelastic parton scatterings (Xu 2004) and impact parameter-dependent p0 scaling remain to be implemented.
