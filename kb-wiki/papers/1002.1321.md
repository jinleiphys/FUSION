# Instabilities in the Nuclear Energy Density Functional

> In the field of Energy Density Functionals (EDF) used in nuclear structure and dynamics, one of the unsolved issues is the stability of the functional. Numerical issues aside, some EDFs are unstable with respect to particular perturbations of the nuclear ground-state density. The aim of this contribution is to raise questions about the origin and nature of these instabilities, the techniques used to diagnose and prevent them, and the domain of density functions in which one should expect a nuclear EDF to be stable.

## Key claim

Semi-local nuclear energy density functionals of the Skyrme type can exhibit intrinsic instabilities with respect to specific perturbations of the nuclear density, independent of numerical details, which manifest as unphysical configurations at the energy minimum, broken symmetries, or divergent energy.

## Method

The authors analyze the stability of Skyrme-type EDFs by studying the static response of infinite nuclear matter (INM) to finite-size density perturbations at the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) level, using the analytic expression for the response function in symmetric INM. They examine the link between the coefficient \(C^{\Delta\rho}_1\) in the functional and the occurrence rate of instabilities observed in self-consistent calculations of finite nuclei, and categorize instabilities by the role of finite size: Landau parameters for long-range INM perturbations, RPA calculations for zero-energy modes, and surface-origin instabilities in finite nuclei not amenable to INM diagnosis.

## Key numbers

- The static susceptibility \(\Pi(\omega=0,\mathbf{q})\) must be positive for all values of the transferred momentum \(\mathbf{q}\) and the density \(\rho_0\); a change of sign signals a pole corresponding to a zero-energy collective mode.
- The short-wavelength (high \(\mathbf{q}\)) behavior of the response is driven by the coefficient \(C^{\Delta\rho}_1\), whose value correlates with the occurrence rate of the instability observed in calculations of finite nuclei (as reported in Ref. [mspli]).
- A similar convergence issue was identified concerning the susceptibility of the functional SkO to fluctuations of the spin density (Refs. [zdunczuk, schunck]).
- In Ref. [tensor], a parameterization with large negative values of \(C^J_t\) was found to yield an unphysical shell structure in seemingly random nuclei, with the energy landscape showing two minima, the unphysical one being energetically favored.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of instabilities in Skyrme EDFs beyond the well-known pathologies of the INM equation of state at high densities (Blaizot, Kutschera, Chabanat), which rarely affect nuclear structure calculations, to finite-size instabilities observed in self-consistent calculations of nuclei (Refs. [mspli, schunck]). The authors raise open questions about the quantitative link between INM and finite-nucleus instabilities, diagnosis of instabilities not accessible via small-amplitude expansion, manifestations in dynamical calculations, and the domain of density variation in which next-generation non-empirical functionals built via the Density Matrix Expansion (Negele, Bogner, Gebremariam) should be stable.
