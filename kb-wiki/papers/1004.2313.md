# Tensor coupling effects on spin symmetry in anti-Lambda spectrum of hypernuclei

> The effects of $\barО›\barО›П‰$-tensor coupling on the spin symmetry of $\barО›$ spectra in $\barО›$-nucleus systems have been studied with the relativistic mean-field theory. Taking $^{12}$C+$\barО›$ as an example, it is found that the tensor coupling enlarges the spin-orbit splittings of $\barО›$ by an order of magnitude although its effects on the wave functions of $\barО›$ are negligible. Similar conclusions has been observed in $\barО›$-nucleus of different mass regions, including $^{16}$O+$\barО›$, $^{40}$Ca+$\barО›$ and $^{208}$Pb+$\barО›$. It indicates that the spin symmetry in anti-lambda-nucleus systems is still good irrespective of the tensor coupling.

## Key claim

The inclusion of $\bar\Lambda\bar\Lambda\omega$-tensor coupling in relativistic mean-field calculations enlarges the spin-orbit splittings of $\bar\Lambda$ states by an order of magnitude compared to calculations without tensor coupling, yet the spin symmetry in anti-lambda-nucleus systems remains good because the tensor coupling has negligible effect on the wave functions of $\bar\Lambda$.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the PK1-Y1 effective interaction for $\Lambda$ hyperons, applying G-parity transformation with a universal reduction factor $\xi=0.3$ to obtain the $\bar\Lambda$-meson coupling strengths. They solved the Dirac equation for $\bar\Lambda$ in spherical nuclei, including a tensor coupling term $T_{\bar\Lambda} = -\frac{1}{2m_{\bar\Lambda}}\frac{f_{\omega\bar\Lambda}}{g_{\omega\bar\Lambda}}i\bm\gamma\cdot\bm\nabla V_{\bar\Lambda}$, with the ratio $\alpha = f_{\omega\bar\Lambda}/g_{\omega\bar\Lambda} = -1$ from the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka rule. Calculations were performed for $^{12}$C+$\bar\Lambda$, $^{16}$O+$\bar\Lambda$, $^{40}$Ca+$\bar\Lambda$, and $^{208}$Pb+$\bar\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splittings for $\bar\Lambda$ in $^{12}$C+$\bar\Lambda$ with tensor coupling: $1.37$ to $3.47$ MeV for $1p$, $2p$, $1d$, $1f$ states; without tensor coupling: $0.28$ to $0.64$ MeV
- For $^{16}$O+$\bar\Lambda$ with tensor coupling: $1.12$ to $2.48$ MeV; without: $0.16$ to $0.39$ MeV
- For $^{40}$Ca+$\bar\Lambda$ with tensor coupling: $0.75$ to $1.48$ MeV; without: $0.05$ to $0.26$ MeV
- For $^{208}$Pb+$\bar\Lambda$ with tensor coupling: $0.05$ to $0.76$ MeV; without: $0$ to $0.15$ MeV
- $\bar\Lambda$ mass used: $m_{\bar\Lambda} = 1115.7$ MeV
- Universal reduction factor: $\xi = 0.3$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin symmetry in antinucleon spectra by Zhou (2003) and in anti-Lambda spectra by Song (2009), which neglected both the impurity effects of $\bar\Lambda$ and tensor coupling. The tensor coupling effects on spin-orbit splitting in $\Lambda$ hypernuclei were previously discussed by Jennings (1990) and Yao (2008), and the PK1-Y1 interaction was developed by Lv (2009) for $\Lambda$ hypernuclei. The results enable further investigation of $\bar\Lambda$-nucleus systems and the role of tensor forces in antiparticle spectra.
