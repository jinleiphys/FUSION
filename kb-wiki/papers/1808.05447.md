# Renormalisation-group analysis of electromagnetic couplings in the pionless effective field theory

> The Wilsonian renormalisation group is applied to a system of two nonrelativistic particles interacting via short-range forces and coupled to an external EM field. By demanding that a fully off-shell one-particle-irreducible 5-point amplitude is independent of the cutoff, a renormalisation group equation is derived for the interaction current density. This is solved to obtain the fixed point corresponding to the unitary limit. The scaling behaviour of perturbations around this point is analysed. Some of these terms are related by gauge invariance to terms in the effective-range expansion; others describe short-range physics that is not included in the two-body potential. We construct observables including the bound-state form factor and show how the scaling of the terms in the interaction current is reflected in the power counting for their contributions to observables.

## Key claim

The Wilsonian renormalisation group (RG) is applied to the electromagnetic couplings of two nonrelativistic particles interacting via short-range forces, showing that the scaling of perturbations around the unitary fixed point determines the power counting for contributions to observables such as the bound-state form factor, with the first new low-energy constant (LEC) appearing at third order in the expansion.

## Method

The authors derive an RG equation for the two-body interaction current density by demanding cutoff independence of the fully off-shell one-particle-irreducible 5-point amplitude, using a symmetric gauging prescription for the cutoff that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. They solve this equation to find the fixed-point solution corresponding to the unitary limit, then analyse the scaling behaviour of perturbations around this fixed point by expanding them in eigenfunctions of the linearised RG equation. Observables including the bound-state form factor are constructed from the resulting 5-point amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The fixed-point potential for the unitary limit is given by \(\widehat V_F(\hat p) = -\left[1-\frac{\hat p}{2}\ln\frac{1+\hat p}{1-\hat p}\right]^{-1}\) (Eq. 3.1).
- The leading-order 5-point amplitude is \(T_S^0(q;p',p) = -T_S(p')\left[\mathrm{i}\,\frac{ZM^2}{4\pi q} \ln\frac{p+p'-q/2}{p+p'+q/2}\right] T_S(p)\), which is of order \(Q^{-3}\) in low-energy scales.
- The energy-dependent perturbations \(\phi_{2n-1}(\hat p)=\hat p^{2n}\widehat V_F^2(\hat p)\) have scaling eigenvalues \(\nu=2n-1=-1,\ 1,\ 3,\ \dots\).
- The potential-independent perturbations to the charge density have the form \(\phi^0_{lmn}(\hat q;\hat p',\hat p)=\widehat V_F(\hat p')\hat q^{2l+2}\,\hat p'^{2m}\,\hat p^{2n}\widehat V_F(\hat p)\) with eigenvalues \(\nu=2(l+m+n)+3\) for \(n,l,m=0,1,2,\dots\).
- The first potential-independent term (\(D_{000}\,q^2\)) appears at order \(Q^0\) (N\(^3\)LO in the notation of Kaplan (1998) and Chen (1999)).
- The scattering length is related to the coefficient \(C_{-1}\) by \(C_{-1}=-\pi/2 a\), and the bound-state energy is \(E_B=1/Ma^2\).

## Context

This work extends the RG analysis of Birse (1998) for the two-body potential to electromagnetic couplings, providing a general framework that reproduces the power counting previously obtained by Kaplan (1998), Chen (1999), and Valderrama (2014) using other methods. The approach uses a momentum cutoff rather than dimensional regularisation, making it closer to standard nuclear-physics methods, and it enables systematic determination of the power counting for short-range electromagnetic operators that are not encoded in the potential, such as those appearing in the deuteron charge form factor.
