# Reflections upon the Emergence of Hadronic Mass

> With discovery of the Higgs boson, science has located the source for $\lesssim 2$% of the mass of visible matter. The focus of attention can now shift to the search for the origin of the remaining $\gtrsim 98$%. The instruments at work here must be capable of simultaneously generating the 1 GeV mass-scale associated with the nucleon and ensuring that this mass-scale is completely hidden in the chiral-limit pion. This hunt for an understanding of the emergence of hadronic mass (EHM) has actually been underway for many years. What is changing are the impacts of QCD-related theory, through the elucidation of clear signals for EHM in hadron observables, and the ability of modern and planned experimental facilities to access these observables. These developments are exemplified in a discussion of the evolving understanding of pion and kaon parton distributions.

## Key claim

The Higgs boson accounts for less than 2% of the mass of visible matter, while the remaining more than 98% originates from the emergence of hadronic mass (EHM) through dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in quantum chromodynamics, a mechanism that simultaneously generates the 1 GeV nucleon mass scale and hides it completely in the chiral-limit pion.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of QCD, focusing on the running coupling, the dressed-quark propagator obtained from the gap equation, and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the pion. They analyze the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation and its extensions to the rho meson and proton to quantify how dynamical mass generation and Higgs-generated current masses combine in hadron masses, and they discuss parton distribution amplitudes and parton distribution functions as observables that expose EHM.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs boson is the source for less than 2% of the mass of visible matter; the remaining more than 98% is attributed to EHM.
- The nucleon mass is approximately 1 GeV, roughly 2000 times the electron mass.
- The renormalization-group-invariant process-independent running coupling saturates at infrared momenta: hat-alpha(0)/pi = 0.97(4).
- The calculated gluon mass-scale is m_0 = 0.43(1) GeV.
- In the chiral limit, the dressed-quark mass function at zero momentum M(0) is approximately m_N/3.
- The pion mass is m_pi approximately 0.15 m_N; the rho meson mass m_rho is greater than 5 m_pi; the kaon mass m_K is approximately 0.5 GeV.
- The EHM-induced enhancement factor for the pion is s_zeta2 = 20 at zeta_2 = 2 GeV.
- The chiral-limit rho meson mass m_rho^0 is approximately 0.75 GeV, approximately 2 M(0); the current-mass enhancement factor h_rho^zeta2 is approximately 3.6.
- The chiral-limit proton mass m_p^0 is approximately 3 M(0); the current-mass enhancement factor h_p^zeta2 is approximately 7.1.
- The pion parton distribution function at x approximately 1 and hadronic scale zeta_H is proportional to (1-x)^2.

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing effort to understand the origin of hadronic mass, building on the work of Nambu (1960), Goldstone (1961), and Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (1968) on chiral symmetry and its breaking. It contrasts with the Higgs mechanism as the sole source of mass and provides a framework for interpreting measurements at planned electron-ion colliders (EicC in China and EIC in the USA), which aim to achieve hadron tomography through generalized parton distributions and transverse momentum dependent parton distributions. The results enable quantitative connections between DCSB, confinement, and observable parton distributions, particularly for pions and kaons.
