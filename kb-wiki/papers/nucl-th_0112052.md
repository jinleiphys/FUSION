# Scattering of vector mesons off nucleons

> We construct a relativistic and unitary approach to 'high' energy pion- and photon-nucleon reactions taking the $ПЂN, ПЂО”$, $ПЃN$, $П‰N$, $О·N, K О›, K ОЈ$ final states into account. Our scheme dynamically generates the s- and d-wave nucleon resonances N(1535), N(1650) and N(1520) and isobar resonances $О”(1620)$ and $О”(1700)$ in terms of quasi-local interaction vertices. The description of photon-induced processes is based on a generalized vector-meson dominance assumption which directly relates the electromagnetic quasi-local 4-point interaction vertices to the corresponding vertices involving the $ПЃ$ and $П‰$ fields. We obtain a satisfactory description of the elastic and inelastic pion- and photon-nucleon scattering data in the channels considered. The resulting s-wave $ПЃ$- and $П‰$-nucleon scattering amplitudes are presented. Using these amplitudes we compute the leading density modification of the $ПЃ$ and $П‰$ energy distributions in nuclear matter. We find a repulsive energy shift for the $П‰$ meson at small nuclear density but predict considerable strength in resonance-hole like $П‰$-meson modes. Compared to previous calculations our result for the $ПЃ$-meson spectral function shows a significantly smaller in-medium effect. This reflects a fairly small coupling strength of the N(1520) resonance to the $ПЃN$ channel.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a relativistic and unitary coupled-channel model for pion- and photon-nucleon reactions that dynamically generates several nucleon and isobar resonances without including them explicitly in the interaction kernel, under the assumption that the interaction kernel is slowly varying in the energy window 1.4 GeV < в€љs < 1.8 GeV.

## Method

The authors develop a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach including ПЂN, ПЂО”, ПЃN, П‰N, О·N, KО›, and KОЈ final states, with the interaction kernel approximated by quasi-local 4-point vertices that are slowly varying in energy. Photon-induced processes are incorporated via a generalized vector-meson dominance assumption relating electromagnetic vertices to those involving ПЃ and П‰ fields. The s-wave ПЃN and П‰N scattering amplitudes are extracted from fits to elastic and inelastic ПЂN and ОіN scattering data in the S11, S31, D13, and D33 partial waves, and these amplitudes are then used to compute the leading density modification of ПЃ and П‰ meson spectral functions in nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends existing coupled-channel calculations by Feuster and Mosel (2000), Vrana, Dytman, and Lee (2000), and Manley (2000) by including ПЃN, П‰N, and photon-nucleon channels on equal footing for the first time, and by maintaining analyticity through the Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than using the K-matrix approximation. The resulting vector-meson nucleon scattering amplitudes enable computation of in-medium modifications of ПЃ and П‰ meson spectral functions in nuclear matter to leading order in density, with the finding that the ПЃ-meson in-medium effect is significantly smaller than in previous calculations due to weak coupling of the N(1520) resonance to the ПЃN channel.
