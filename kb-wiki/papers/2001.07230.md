# Probing few-body nuclear dynamics via 3H and 3He (e,e'p)pn cross-section measurements

> We report the first measurement of the \eep three-body breakup reaction cross sections in helium-3 ($^3$He) and tritium ($^3$H) at large momentum transfer ($\langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1.9$ (GeV/c)$^2$) and $x_B>1$ kinematics, where the cross section should be sensitive to quasielastic (QE) scattering from single nucleons. The data cover missing momenta $40 \le p_{miss} \le 500$ MeV/c that, in the QE limit with no rescattering, equals the initial momentum of the probed nucleon. The measured cross sections are compared with state-of-the-art ab-initio calculations. Overall good agreement, within $\pm20\%$, is observed between data and calculations for the full $p_{miss}$ range for $^3$H and for $100 \le p_{miss} \le 350$ MeV/c for $^3$He. Including the effects of rescattering of the outgoing nucleon improves agreement with the data at $p_{miss} > 250$ MeV/c and suggests contributions from charge-exchange (SCX) rescattering. The isoscalar sum of $^3$He plus $^3$H, which is largely insensitive to SCX, is described by calculations to within the accuracy of the data over the entire $p_{miss}$ range. This validates current models of the ground state of the three-nucleon system up to very high initial nucleon momenta of $500$ MeV/c.

## Key claim

The first measurement of (e,e'p) three-body breakup cross sections in both 3He and 3H at high momentum transfer (⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 1.9 (GeV/c)²) and xB>1 shows overall agreement with ab-initio calculations within ±20%, but the agreement is conditional: it holds for the full pmiss range for 3H and only for 100 ≤ pmiss ≤ 350 MeV/c for 3He, with rescattering effects needed to improve agreement above 250 MeV/c.

## Method

The experiment used the two high-resolution spectrometers (HRS) in Jefferson Lab Hall A with a 4.326 GeV electron beam incident on gas targets of hydrogen, deuterium, helium-3, and tritium. Scattered electrons were detected in the left-HRS at central momentum 3.543 GeV/c and angle 20.88°, while knocked-out protons were detected in the right-HRS at two settings covering missing momenta 40 ≤ pmiss ≤ 500 MeV/c. The cross sections were extracted from coincidence event yields with corrections for acceptance, radiation, bin migration, and target density effects, then compared to Faddeev-based calculations (Cracow) and spectral-function-based calculations (CK+CC1) using the CD-Bonn and AV18 nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- ⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 1.9 (GeV/c)², with central Q² = 2.0 (GeV/c)² at beam energy 4.326 GeV
- Missing momentum range covered: 40 ≤ pmiss ≤ 500 MeV/c
- Overall agreement between data and calculations within ±20% for the full pmiss range for 3H and for 100 ≤ pmiss ≤ 350 MeV/c for 3He
- Energy transfer ω = 0.78 GeV and xB = 1.4 at central kinematics
- Tritium target areal density: 85.1 ± 0.8 mg/cm²; helium-3 target: 53.4 ± 0.6 mg/cm²
- Tritium decay correction: 2.78 ± 0.18% of target nuclei decayed to 3He over six months
- θrq < 37.5° cut applied to minimize final-state interaction effects
- pmiss resolution better than 9 MeV/c from 1H(e,e'p) calibration

## Context

This work extends the sparse world data on tritium electron scattering, which dates back to the 1960s and 1980s (Collard 1963, Schiff 1963, Dow 1988, Beck 1987), and follows a recent extraction of the 3He/3H(e,e'p) cross-section ratio by Cruz-Torres (2019) that showed discrepancies at high missing momenta. The simultaneous measurement of both 3He and 3H at identical kinematics places stringent constraints on non-quasielastic reaction mechanisms, and the isoscalar sum of both cross sections validates current models of the three-nucleon ground state up to initial nucleon momenta of 500 MeV/c, enabling future tests of nuclear interaction models at high momentum.
