# Temperature-dependent cross sections for meson-meson nonresonant reactions in hadronic matter

> We present a potential of which the short-distance part is given by one gluon exchange plus perturbative one- and two-loop corrections and of which the large-distance part exhibits a temperature-dependent constant value. The Schrodinger equation with this temperature-dependent potential yields a temperature dependence of the mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave function and of meson masses. The temperature dependence of the potential, the wave function and the meson masses brings about temperature dependence of cross sections for the nonresonant reactions pi pi -> rho rho for I=2, KK -> K* K* for I=1, KK* -> K* K* for I=1, pi K -> rho K* for I=3/2, pi K* -> rho K* for I=3/2, rho K -> rho K* for I=3/2 and pi K* -> rho K for I=3/2. As the temperature increases, the rise or fall of peak cross sections is determined by the increased radii of initial mesons, the loosened bound states of final mesons, and the total-mass difference of the initial and final mesons. The temperature-dependent cross sections and meson masses are parametrized.

## Key claim

The paper claims that cross sections for seven nonresonant meson-meson reactions in hadronic matter depend on temperature, with the rise or fall of peak cross sections determined by increased radii of initial mesons, loosened bound states of final mesons, and the total-mass difference of initial and final mesons. This temperature dependence is derived from a potential whose large-distance part exhibits a temperature-dependent constant value, but the authors acknowledge that the heavy quark potential remains uncertain and that using the free energy as the potential (as done here) is an approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed a temperature-dependent quark-antiquark potential by interpolating between a perturbative QCD short-distance part (one-gluon exchange plus one- and two-loop corrections) and a large-distance constant plateau from lattice gauge results, parametrized as \(V_{ab}(\vec{r}) = -\frac{\vec{\lambda}_a}{2} \cdot \frac{\vec{\lambda}_b}{2} \frac{3}{4} D [1.3 - (T/T_c)^4] \tanh(Ar) + \frac{\vec{\lambda}_a}{2} \cdot \frac{\vec{\lambda}_b}{2} \frac{6\pi}{25} \frac{v(\lambda r)}{r} \exp(-Er)\). They solved the Schrodinger equation with this potential to obtain temperature-dependent quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions and meson masses, then used the Born approximation with the quark-interchange mechanism to compute unpolarized cross sections, averaging over prior-form and post-form transition amplitudes due to the post-prior discrepancy.

## Key numbers

- The large-distance plateau potential uses \(D = 0.7\) GeV and critical temperature \(T_c = 0.175\) GeV.
- At \(T/T_c = 0.97\), 0.94, 0.9, and 0.84, plateaus begin at approximately \(r = 1.15\) fm, 1.18 fm, 1.26 fm, and 1.38 fm, respectively.
- Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0.32\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.5\) GeV.
- At \(T=0\), the spin-spin interaction yields mass splittings \(m_\rho - m_\pi = 0.5989\) GeV and \(m_{K^*} - m_K = 0.3833\) GeV, compared to experimental values 0.6304 GeV and 0.3963 GeV.
- As \(T \to T_c\), pion and rho masses approach 0 GeV and 0.006 GeV respectively; kaon and vector kaon masses approach 0.179 GeV and 0.183 GeV respectively.
- Meson mass parametrizations are valid in the region \(0.6 \leq T/T_c \leq 0.99\).
- In-vacuum maximum cross sections for the seven endothermic reactions range from 0.47 mb to 1.41 mb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous vacuum calculations of meson-meson nonresonant reactions (Zhang and Xu, 2005) by incorporating temperature dependence through a potential based on lattice gauge results from Karsch, Laermann, and Peikert (2001). The study addresses a gap in the literature where cross sections for inelastic light meson scattering in hadronic matter had not been studied experimentally or theoretically at nonzero temperature. The temperature-dependent cross sections and meson masses are parametrized for use in modeling hadronic matter produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where pions, rhos, and kaons are the dominant constituents.
