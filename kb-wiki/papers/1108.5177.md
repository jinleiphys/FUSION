# Implications of a viscosity bound on black hole accretion

> Motivated by the viscosity bound in gauge/gravity duality, we consider the ratio of shear viscosity (eta) to entropy density (s) in black hole accretion flows. We use both an ideal gas equation of state and the QCD equation of state obtained from lattice for the fluid accreting onto a Kerr black hole. The QCD equation of state is considered since the temperature of accreting matter is expected to approach 10^{12}K in certain hot flows. We find that in both the cases eta/s is small only for primordial black holes and several orders of magnitude larger than any known fluid for stellar and supermassive black holes. We show that a lower bound on the mass of primordial black holes leads to a lower bound on eta/s and vice versa. Finally we speculate that the Shakura-Sunyaev viscosity parameter should decrease with increasing density and/or temperatures.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (\(\eta/s\)) in black hole accretion flows is small (comparable to the conjectured holographic bound) only for primordial black holes, and is several orders of magnitude larger than any known fluid for stellar-mass and supermassive black holes, regardless of whether an ideal gas or QCD equation of state is used.

## Method

The authors model one-temperature advection-dominated accretion flows (ADAF) around a Kerr black hole using full general relativistic equations of continuity and stress-energy conservation, with the Shakura-Sunyaev \(\alpha\)-prescription for turbulent viscosity (\(\alpha = 0.01\)). They solve the flow equations numerically using a shooting method for both an ideal gas equation of state and a lattice QCD equation of state, the latter fitted to trace anomaly data from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For a Kerr black hole with spin parameter \(a = 0.999\) and \(\alpha = 0.01\), the estimated \(\eta/s\) near the horizon is \(\eta/s \approx 4 \times 10^{-4} \, (GM m_p)/(k_B c) \approx 10^{15} \, (M/M_\odot) \, (\hbar/k_B)\).
- For stellar-mass black holes, \(\eta/s\) is of order \(10^{16} \, \hbar/k_B\); for supermassive black holes, \(\eta/s > 10^{21} \, \hbar/k_B\); for comparison, the QGP value at RHIC/LHC is \(\eta/s \sim 0.1 \, \hbar/k_B\).
- Consistency with the viscosity bound implies a lower bound on primordial black hole mass: \(M \gtrsim 10^{-16} M_\odot\).
- Using the fact that surviving primordial black holes must have \(M > 10^{15}\) gm, the authors find \(\eta/s > 0.1 \, \alpha \, \hbar/k_B\).
- The QCD crossover temperature is around \(1.5-2 \times 10^{12}\) K; the flow temperature near the horizon reaches \(\sim 2.5 \times 10^{12}\) K (normalized \(T = 0.25\)).

## Context

This work extends the application of the holographic viscosity bound (Kovtun-Son-Starinets, 2005) from heavy-ion physics to astrophysical black hole accretion, using both ideal gas and lattice QCD equations of state (Fodor, 2010; Petreczky, 2010). The results contradict the expectation that hot accretion flows near the horizon would exhibit a small \(\eta/s\) like the quark-gluon plasma; instead, the large values found for stellar and supermassive black holes suggest that the Shakura-Sunyaev \(\alpha\) parameter should decrease with increasing density and/or temperature. This enables future work on sub-Keplerian inner accretion flows and the possible role of cavitation in such systems.
