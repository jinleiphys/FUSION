# Pion generalized parton distribution from lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice calculation of the valence-quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the pion using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach. We focus on the zero-skewness limit, where the GPD has a probability-density interpretation in the longitudinal Bjorken $x$ and the transverse impact-parameter distributions. Our calculation is done using clover valence fermions on an ensemble of gauge configurations with $2+1+1$ flavors (degenerate up/down, strange and charm) of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) with lattice spacing $a \approx 0.12$ fm, box size $L \approx 3$ fm and pion mass $m_π\approx 310$ MeV. The parton distribution function and the form factor are reproduced as special limits of the GPD as expected. Due to the large errors, this exploratory study does not show a clear preference among different model assumptions about the kinematic dependence of the GPD. To discriminate between these assumptions, future studies using higher-statistics data will be crucial.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the valence-quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) of the pion using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach, but the exploratory study does not show a clear preference among different model assumptions about the kinematic dependence of the GPD due to large statistical errors.

## Method

The authors used clover valence fermions on an ensemble of gauge configurations with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by the MILC Collaboration. They computed time-independent, nonlocal correlators of a pion with finite momentum boost at three boosted momenta (0.86, 1.32, and 1.74 GeV) and four source-sink separations (0.72, 0.84, 0.96, and 1.08 fm) to extract the zero-skewness quasi-GPD matrix elements, then applied RI/MOM renormalization, Fourier transform, and one-loop matching to obtain the GPD in the MSbar scheme.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.12 fm, box size L ≈ 3 fm, pion mass m_π ≈ 310 MeV
* Three boosted pion momenta used: P^z = 0.86, 1.32, and 1.74 GeV (corresponding to n = 2, 3, 4 in units of 2π/L)
* Four source-sink separations: 0.72, 0.84, 0.96, and 1.08 fm
* Total measurements: 29440, 29440, 58880, and 58880 from smallest to largest source-sink separation
* RI/MOM renormalization scales: μ_R = 3.7 GeV, p_z^R = 6 × 2π/L
* Final GPD presented at renormalization scale μ = 4 GeV
* Momentum transfer values: t = {0, -2, -5} × (2π/L)^2

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach, previously applied to nucleon and meson PDFs by Lin (2014), Chen (2016), and Alexandrou (2015-2018), to the first lattice calculation of the full x-dependence of a pion GPD rather than just its moments as in earlier work by Gockeler (2003) and Hagler (2003). The results reproduce the pion PDF from Chen (2018) at t=0 and the pion form factor from Brommel (2006) as special limits, and enable future studies with higher statistics to discriminate between different GPD parametrizations such as the factorized form and the exponential form used in nucleon GPD analyses by Diehl (2004).
