# Bayesian inference and jet quenching

> These proceedings review the application of Bayesian inference to high momentum transfer probes of the quark--gluon plasma (QGP). Bayesian inference techniques are introduced, highlighting critical components to consider when comparing analyses. Recent calibrations using hadron observables are described, illustrating the importance of the choice of parametrization. Additional recent analyses that characterize the impact of the inclusion of jet observables, as well as soft-hard correlations, are reviewed. Finally, lessons learned from these analyses and important questions for the future are highlighted.

## Key claim

Bayesian inference provides a rigorous framework for extracting the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ from high-momentum transfer probes of the quark-gluon plasma, but the extracted posterior distributions depend critically on the choice of model parametrization, data selection, and treatment of uncertainties, with different parametrizations yielding consistent results over most of the temperature range while diverging at low temperatures due to flexibility differences.

## Method

The paper reviews Bayesian inference analyses that calibrate model parameters by comparing Monte Carlo simulations to experimental measurements of hadron and jet yield modification (RAA) at RHIC and LHC energies. The analyses use Bayes' theorem to compute posterior distributions of parameters such as $\hat{q}/T^3$, employing different model frameworks including the JETSCAPE multistage approach (MATTER+LBT) with hard-thermal-loop-inspired parametrizations and an information field parametrization based on random functions, with Gaussian processes and active learning used to manage computational costs.

## Key numbers

- $\hat{q}/T^3$ posterior distributions extracted for a quark with $E = 100$ GeV propagating through the medium, compared across two analyses (JETSCAPE hadron and information field) over a range of medium temperatures
- 90% credible interval of the information field posterior is consistently smaller than the JETSCAPE hadron posterior
- $\hat{q}/T^3$ posterior distributions calibrated using RHIC data at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200$ GeV and LHC data at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV, shown as a function of centrality selection
- $\hat{q}/T^3$ posterior distributions extracted for hadron-only and jet-only observables, evaluated at $E = 100$ GeV and $T = 200$ MeV, with hadron $\pT{}$-selected calibrations at thresholds of $>10$ GeV/$c$, $>20$ GeV/$c$, and $>30$ GeV/$c$
- $\hat{q}/T^3$ posterior distributions from LIDO model calibration shown for $E = 10$ GeV and $E = 100$ GeV as a function of $T$

## Context

This review extends the Bayesian inference methodology previously applied to the soft sector by Bernhard (2019), JETSCAPE (2020), and Nijs (2020) into the hard sector of heavy-ion collisions. It describes recent calibrations by the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2021, 2024) using hadron and jet observables, and by Xie (2022) using an information field parametrization, which together demonstrate that the choice of parametrization significantly impacts extracted $\hat{q}$ values at low temperatures. The work enables future multi-model comparisons and joint soft-hard calibrations that will discriminate between different physical formalisms for parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
