# Equilibration and baryon densities attainable in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Kinetic equilibration of the matter and baryon densities attained in central region of colliding Au+Au nuclei in the energy range of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3.3--39 GeV are examined within the model of the three-fluid dynamics. It is found that the kinetic equilibration is faster at higher collision energies: the equilibration time (in the c.m. frame of colliding nuclei) rises from $\sim$5 fm/c at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3.3 GeV to $\sim$1 fm/c at 39 GeV. The chemical equilibration, and thus thermalization, takes longer. We argue that the presented time evolution of the net-baryon and energy densities in the central region is a necessary prerequisite of proper reproduction of bulk observables in midrapidity. We suggest that for informative comparison of predictions of different models it is useful to calculate an invariant 4-volume ($V_4$), where the proper density the equilibrated matter exceeds certain value. The advantage of this 4-volume is that it does not depend on specific choice of the 3-volume in different studies and takes into account the lifetime of the high-density region, which also matters. The 4-volume $V_4=$ 100 fm$^4$/c is chosen to compare the baryon densities attainable at different different energies. It is found that the highest proper baryon density increases with the collision energy rise, from $n_B/n_0\approx$ 4 at 3.3 GeV to $n_B/n_0\approx$ 30 at 39 GeV. These highest densities are achieved in the central region of colliding system.

## Key claim

Kinetic equilibration (equality of longitudinal and transverse pressure components to within 10%) is faster at higher collision energies, but the authors explicitly note that chemical equilibration and full thermalization take longer; the reported equilibration times depend on the chosen accuracy criterion (here "no worse than 10%" rather than "better than 10%" used in prior work).

## Method

The authors used the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball fluids coupled by friction terms, to simulate central (b = 2 fm) Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 3.3 to 39 GeV per nucleon pair. They analyzed the time evolution of longitudinal and transverse pressures, net baryon density, energy density, temperature, and QGP fraction in a Lorentz-contracted central box (|x| ≤ 2 fm, |y| ≤ 2 fm, |z| ≤ γ_cm × 2 fm). They introduced an invariant 4-volume V_4 as a measure of the space-time region where the proper baryon density of the equilibrated matter exceeds a given threshold, and compared results for a first-order phase transition equation of state and a crossover equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time (in the c.m. frame) decreases from ~5 fm/c at √s_NN = 3.3 GeV to ~1 fm/c at 39 GeV.
- Highest proper baryon density (in the 4-volume V_4 = 100 fm^4/c) increases from n_B/n_0 ≈ 4 at √s_NN = 3.3 GeV to n_B/n_0 ≈ 30 at 39 GeV, where n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} is normal nuclear density.
- The 4-volume threshold chosen for comparison is V_4 = 100 fm^4/c, which can be viewed as 5×5×2 fm^3 × 2 fm/c.
- At √s_NN = 39 GeV, 70% of the baryon charge is stopped in the central Au+Au collision.

## Context

This work extends the earlier comparison of highest attainable densities by Randrup (2007) using the 3FD model, which was not included in that study. It complements analyses within the UrQMD model (Bravina 2008; De 2015) and QGSM model, which reported baryon densities in fixed-size central cells but could not be directly compared due to different cell choices. The invariant 4-volume approach is proposed to enable model-independent comparisons, and the results provide a necessary prerequisite for proper reproduction of bulk observables at midrapidity at NICA, FAIR, and BES-RHIC energies.
