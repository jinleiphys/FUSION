# Shell evolution of $N=40$ isotones towards $^{60}$Ca: First spectroscopy of $^{62}$Ti

> Excited states in the $N=40$ isotone $^{62}$Ti were populated via the $^{63}$V$(p,2p)$$^{62}$Ti reaction at $\sim$200~MeV/u at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory and studied using $γ$-ray spectroscopy. The energies of the $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^{+}_{\mathrm{gs}}$ and $4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1$ transitions, observed here for the first time, indicate a deformed $^{62}$Ti ground state. These energies are increased compared to the neighboring $^{64}$Cr and $^{66}$Fe isotones, suggesting a small decrease of quadrupole collectivity. The present measurement is well reproduced by large-scale shell-model calculations based on effective interactions, while ab initio and beyond mean-field calculations do not yet reproduce our findings. The shell-model calculations for $^{62}$Ti show a dominant configuration with four neutrons excited across the $N=40$ gap. Likewise, they indicate that the $N=40$ island of inversion extends down to $Z=20$, disfavoring a possible doubly magic character of the elusive $^{60}$Ca.

## Key claim

The first spectroscopy of \(^{62}\)Ti shows that its \(2^+_1\) and \(4^+_1\) excitation energies are increased compared to the neighboring \(^{64}\)Cr and \(^{66}\)Fe isotones, indicating a small decrease of quadrupole collectivity; however, large-scale shell-model calculations reproduce the data and show a dominant 4p-4h neutron configuration across \(N=40\), suggesting the \(N=40\) island of inversion extends down to \(Z=20\) and disfavoring a doubly magic character for \(^{60}\)Ca, even though the rising trend of the \(2^+_1\) energy might naively suggest a shell closure.

## Method

Excited states in \(^{62}\)Ti were populated via the \(^{63}\)V\((p,2p)^{62}\)Ti knockout reaction at approximately 200 MeV/u at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory, using a liquid hydrogen target inside the MINOS device surrounded by the DALI2\(^+\) \(\gamma\)-ray detector array. Doppler-corrected \(\gamma\)-ray spectra were obtained from 1880 reconstructed events, and transition energies were extracted via a two-dimensional \(\chi^2\) minimization of the simulated detector response against the data, with systematic uncertainties from calibration and possible state lifetimes.

## Key numbers

* \(E(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_{\mathrm{gs}})\) = 683(10) keV for \(^{62}\)Ti.
* \(E(4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) = 823(20) keV for \(^{62}\)Ti.
* Inclusive \((p,2p)\) cross section for \(^{63}\)V\((p,2p)^{62}\)Ti: 4.0(1) mb.
* Exclusive cross section for the \(2^+_1\) state: 1.3(4) mb; for the \(4^+_1\) state: 0.8(1) mb.
* The \(^{62}\)Ti ground-state deformation parameter from large-scale shell-model calculations: \(\beta = 0.28\).
* The \(N=40\) shell gap predicted by VS-IMSRG calculations for \(^{62}\)Ti: 3.7 MeV.

## Context

This work provides the first spectroscopic data for \(^{62}\)Ti, the closest \(N=40\) isotone to \(^{60}\)Ca accessible at current facilities, and tests competing theoretical pictures. The results agree with large-scale shell-model calculations using the LNPS interaction (Lenzi et al., 2010), which predict an island of inversion extending down to \(^{60}\)Ca, but disagree with symmetry-conserving configuration-mixing calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction (Rodriguez et al., 2016) and with ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations (Stroberg et al., 2017; Simonis et al., 2017), both of which overestimate the excitation energies and predict a larger \(N=40\) shell gap. The measurement enables benchmarking of theoretical approaches toward the neutron drip line and constrains predictions for the doubly magic character of \(^{60}\)Ca.
