# Critical velocity of superfluid flow through single barrier and periodic potentials

> We investigate the problem of an ultracold atomic gas in the superfluid phase flowing in the presence of a potential barrier or a periodic potential. We use a hydrodynamic scheme in the local density approximation (LDA) to obtain an analytic expression for the critical current as a function of the barrier height or the lattice intensity, which applies to both Bose and Fermi superfluids. In this scheme, the stationary flow becomes energetically unstable when the local superfluid velocity is equal to the local sound velocity at the point where the external potential is maximum. We compare this prediction with the results of the numerical solutions of the Gross-Pitaevskii and Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations. We discuss the role of long wavelength excitations in determining the critical velocity. Our results allow one to identify the different regimes of superfluid flow, namely, the LDA hydrodynamic regime, the regime of quantum effects beyond LDA for weak barriers and the regime of tunneling between weakly coupled superfluids for strong barriers. We finally discuss the relevance of these results in the context of current experiments with ultracold gases.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic expression for the critical current of superfluid flow through a potential barrier or periodic potential using a hydrodynamic scheme in the local density approximation (LDA), but this LDA prediction only applies when the barrier width is much larger than the healing length; for thin barriers or lattices where the healing length is comparable to the lattice spacing, quantum effects beyond LDA become important and the LDA prediction fails.

## Method

The authors used a hydrodynamic theory within the local density approximation at zero temperature, assuming a polytropic equation of state applicable to both dilute Bose gases (gamma=1) and unitary Fermi gases (gamma=2/3). They derived an implicit relation for the critical current that depends only on the maximum value of the external potential, not its shape. They compared this LDA prediction with numerical solutions of the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) equation for bosons and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for unitary fermions, for both single rectangular barriers and periodic potentials (optical lattices) across a range of barrier widths relative to the healing length.

## Key numbers

- For bosons with a single barrier, the LDA prediction in the V_max to 0 limit gives v_c/c_s^(0) approximately 1 - sqrt(3/2) * (V_max/gn_0)^(1/2) for thick barriers (L/xi >> 1), while GP results for weak thin barriers give v_c/c_s^(0) approximately 1 - (3/4) * (L*V_max/xi*gn_0)^(2/3)
- For unitary fermions with a single barrier, the LDA prediction in the V_max to 0 limit gives v_c/c_s^(0) approximately 1 - sqrt(2) * [V_max/(1+beta)E_F]^(1/2)
- For bosons in a periodic potential, GP results for L/xi = 10 and V_max/mu_(j=0) less than 0.9 fall into the swallow-tail regime with critical quasi-momentum P_c greater than hbar*q_B
- For unitary fermions in a periodic potential, BdG results for L*k_F = 0.497 (E_F/E_R = 0.1) and s = 0.1 gave v_c/c_s^(0) larger than unity due to numerical uncertainty estimated at 3% or less
- For the experiment by Miller et al., using the density at the e^(-2) waist of the lattice gives E_F/E_R approximately 0.56, meaning L*k_F approximately 1.1

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic LDA approach previously applied to bosons by Mamaladze and Hakim to both Bose and Fermi superfluids, and compares it with numerical GP and BdG calculations. It directly addresses the experiment by Miller et al. (2007) on unitary fermions in an optical lattice, finding that the experimental data disagree with both LDA and BdG predictions, suggesting that inhomogeneity of density and lattice intensity or nonstationarity may be important. The results enable identification of three regimes: LDA hydrodynamic flow for large L/xi, macroscopic flow beyond LDA for thin weak barriers, and weakly coupled superfluids for thin strong barriers, the latter connecting to Josephson junction physics studied by Danshita and others.
