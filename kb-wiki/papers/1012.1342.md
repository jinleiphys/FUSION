# Fluctuating initial conditions in hydrodynamics for two-particle correlations

> Event-by-event hydrodynamics, with fluctuating initial conditions, has shown to nicely reproduce several features of experimentally observed quantities in high-energy nuclear collisions. Here we discuss how it may help to understand, in a unified way, the various structures observed in the long-range two-particle correlations, both in nucleus-nucleus and p-p collisions. Suggestions of how experimentally this description could be tested are also discussed.

## Key claim

Event-by-event hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions can provide a unified explanation for both the near-side ridge and the away-side double-hump structures observed in long-range two-particle correlations, but this interpretation relies on the presence of high-energy-density tubes located close to the surface of the hot matter, and in p-p collisions requires a fraction of events with two such tubes.

## Method

The authors used the hydrodynamic code SPheRIO with fluctuating initial conditions generated by the NeXus event generator for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV. They introduced a simplified one-tube boost-invariant model to isolate the effect of a single high-energy-density tube on the fluid flow and resulting particle angular distributions. For p-p collisions, they considered a two-tube initial condition and computed the resulting two-particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV were simulated using NeXus fluctuating initial conditions
- The freeze-out temperature used in the one-tube model was 0.14 GeV
- Temporal evolution of energy density was computed at times 1.0, 3.5, and 8.5 fm in the one-tube model
- For p-p collisions, the two-tube initial condition was evolved at times τ = 1 and 2.75 fm
- The ridge in p-p collisions appears more enhanced when intermediate p_T of 1 to 3 GeV are considered
- The ridge in p-p collisions shows long-range Δη > 4 correlation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on fluctuating initial conditions in hydrodynamics (Hama et al., 2005; Andrade et al., 2006) and their earlier results on granular structures (Andrade et al., 2008), v2 fluctuations (Andrade et al., 2009), and HBT radii (Qian et al., 2009). It provides a hydrodynamic explanation for the ridge structures observed by the STAR, PHOBOS, and CMS collaborations, and proposes a unified mechanism for both nucleus-nucleus and p-p collisions. The work enables experimental tests via the proposed "2+1 correlation" method to separate ridge from jet contributions in p-p collisions.
