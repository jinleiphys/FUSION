# Angular momentum projected multi-cranked configuration mixing for reliable calculation of high-spin rotational bands

> By employing the angular momentum projection technique we propose a method to reliably calculate the quantum spectrum of nuclear collective rotation. The method utilizes several cranked mean-field states with different rotational frequencies and they are superposed in the sense of the configuration mixing or the generator coordinate method, after performing the projection; the idea was originally suggested by Peierls-Thouless in 1962. It is found that the spectrum as a result of the configuration mixing does not essentially depend on chosen sets of cranking frequencies if the number of mean-field states utilized in the mixing is larger than a certain small value. We apply this method to three examples employing the Gogny D1S effective interaction and show that it is useful to study high-spin rotational bands by means of the angular momentum projection method.

# Angular momentum projected multi-cranked configuration mixing for reliable calculation of high-spin rotational bands

## Key claim

The authors propose that superposing angular-momentum-projected cranked mean-field states with different rotational frequencies (configuration mixing or generator coordinate method) yields a rotational spectrum that does not essentially depend on the chosen set of cranking frequencies, provided the number of mean-field states used in the mixing is larger than a certain small value (approximately four to five). This claim is conditional on avoiding the band-crossing region (the g-band to s-band crossing) and on using a suitable norm cut-off to prevent discontinuities in the rotational spectrum.

## Method

The method uses selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) cranked mean-field states generated with the Gogny D1S effective interaction at several rotational frequencies. Angular momentum projection is performed on each cranked state, and the projected states are superposed via the Hill-Wheeler equation (generator coordinate method) with the cranking frequency as the generator coordinate. The authors apply an approximate number projection correction using chemical potentials from the first HFB state, and treat the Coulomb exchange contribution in the Slater approximation to preserve proton pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{164}$Er ground state band: the energy gain of the ground state by angular momentum projection from the non-cranked HFB state is 2.97 MeV; the total energy gain by the projected configuration mixing from the HFB energy is 3.81 MeV.
- For $^{164}$Er: the difference between the configuration mixing calculation and experimental excitation energy is less than 130 keV over the whole spin range $0 \le I \le 22$.
- For $^{164}$Er: the calculated moment of inertia from simple projection from the non-cranked HFB state is only 66% of the experimental value at the lowest spin.
- For $^{164}$Er: the selfconsistent cranked HFB calculation shows the neutron pairing gap vanishes after the crossing frequency $\hbar\omega_{\rm rot} \approx 0.24$ MeV.
- For $^{164}$Er: the calculated quadrupole deformation parameter of the ground state is $\beta_2 = 0.311$; the average pairing gaps are $\bar{\Delta} = 0.874$ MeV (neutrons) and 0.906 MeV (protons), about 10%–13% smaller than the even-odd mass differences.
- For $^{164}$Er: the oscillator basis uses $N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=12$; the Euler angle integration uses $N_\alpha = N_\gamma = 34$ and $N_\beta = 46$ mesh points.
- For $^{164}$Er: the excited $K^\pi=0^+$ rotational band from the Hill-Wheeler equation has about 6 MeV higher energy than the ground state.
- For $^{164}$Er: the configuration mixing with five randomly chosen cranking frequencies (with $N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=10$) gives standard deviations within the size of the plotted symbols, indicating convergence.

## Context

This work extends the Peierls-Thouless (1962) idea of integrating over cranking frequencies in angular momentum projection, addressing the long-standing problem of combining the cranking model with angular momentum projection for high-spin states. Prior approaches using projection from a single intrinsic state (either non-cranked or cranked) gave inaccurate spin-dependence of the moment of inertia, while treating the cranking frequency as a variational parameter after projection did not yield regular rotational patterns (as shown in ZSD07). The method enables reliable microscopic calculation of high-spin rotational bands using the Gogny D1S interaction, and the authors demonstrate applications to $^{164}$Er (rare-earth ground state band), $^{40}$Mg (neutron-rich nucleus), and $^{152}$Dy (superdeformed band). The work builds on the authors' earlier development of efficient quantum-number projection and GCM methods (Tagami and Shimizu, 2012) and their application to nuclear tetrahedral deformation (Tagami, Shimada, and Shimizu, 2013, 2015).
