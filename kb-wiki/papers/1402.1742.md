# Transition probabilities in the $U(3,3)$ limit of the symplectic IVBM

> The tensor properties of the algebra generators are determined in respect to the reduction chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(3,3) \supset U_{p}(3) \otimes \overline{U_{n}(3)}\supset U^{\ast}(3) \supset O(3)$, which defines one of the dynamical symmetry limits of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM). The symplectic basis according to the considered chain is thus constructed and the action of the $Sp(12,R)$ generators as transition operators between the basis states is illustrated. The matrix elements of the $U(3,3)$ ladder operators in the so obtained symmetry-adapted basis are given. The $U(3,3)$ limit of the model is further tested on the more complicated and complex problem of reproducing the $B(E2)$ transition probabilities between the collective states of the ground band in $^{104}Ru$, $^{192}Os$, $^{192}Pt$, and $^{194}Pt$ isotopes, considered by many authors to be axially asymmetric. Additionally, the excitation energies of the ground and $γ$ bands in $^{104}Ru$ are calculated. The theoretical predictions are compared with the experimental data and some other collective models which accommodate the $γ-$rigid or $γ-$soft structures. The obtained results reveal the applicability of the model for the description of the collective properties of nuclei, exhibiting axially asymmetric features.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $U(3,3)$ dynamical symmetry limit of the symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) can reproduce $B(E2)$ transition probabilities between ground band states in axially asymmetric nuclei, but only for a specific subset of "stretched" $SU^{\ast}(3)$ states with $(\lambda,\mu) = (L,L)$ and with the initial ground state corresponding to $(\lambda_0,\mu_0) = (0,0)$.

## Method

The tensor properties of the $Sp(12,R)$ algebra generators were determined with respect to the reduction chain $Sp(12,R) \supset U(3,3) \supset U_p(3) \otimes \overline{U_n(3)} \supset U^{\ast}(3) \supset O(3)$. The symplectic basis states were constructed by applying raising operators to the boson vacuum, and the matrix elements of the $U(3,3)$ ladder operators were evaluated analytically using the generalized Wigner-Eckart theorem with $U(3)$ isoscalar factors and reduced triple-barred matrix elements. The $B(E2)$ transition probabilities were then calculated for the ground state band by identifying the collective states with stretched $SU^{\ast}(3)$ multiplets $(L,L)$.

## Key numbers

- The reduced triple-barred matrix element for the $U(3,3)$ ladder operator $F$ between states differing by one boson in each subsystem is $\sqrt{(N_p+1)(N_n+1)}$ for the maximal coupling case $N'_p = N_p+1$ and $N'_n = N_n+1$.
- The reduced triple-barred matrix element for the conjugate ladder operator $G$ is $\sqrt{N_p N_n}$ for the transition from $N_p,N_n$ to $N_p-1,N_n-1$.
- The $B(E2)$ transition probabilities were calculated for the ground band in $^{104}\text{Ru}$, $^{192}\text{Os}$, $^{192}\text{Pt}$, and $^{194}\text{Pt}$ isotopes.
- Excitation energies of the ground and $\gamma$ bands in $^{104}\text{Ru}$ were calculated.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry limit of the IVBM introduced in Ganev (2014) for the $SU^{\ast}(3)$ algebra, which was previously applied only to energy spectra of $^{192}\text{Os}$. The paper develops the mathematical framework for calculating electromagnetic transition probabilities, enabling comparison with experimental data and with other collective models such as the $\gamma$-unstable rotor model of Wilets and Jean and the rigid triaxial rotor model of Davydov et al. The results test the model's applicability to axially asymmetric nuclei in the $A \approx 100$ and $A \approx 190$ mass regions.
