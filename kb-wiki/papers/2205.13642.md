# Heavy + heavy and heavy + light pseudoscalar to vector semileptonic transitions

> Using a symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction (SCI), we complete a systematic treatment of twelve semileptonic transitions with vector meson final states: $D\to ρ$, $D_{(s)}\to K^\ast$, $D_s\to ϕ$, $B\to ρ$, $B_s\to K^\ast$, $B_{(s)}\to D_{(s)}^\ast$, $B_c \to B_{(s)}^\ast, J/ψ, D^\ast$; and thereby finalise a unified analysis of semileptonic decays of heavy+heavy and heavy+light pseudoscalar mesons to both pseudoscalar and vector meson final states. The analysis is marked by algebraic simplicity, few parameters, and the ability to consistently describe systems from Nambu-Goldstone modes to heavy+heavy mesons. Regarding the behaviour of the transition form factors, the SCI results compare well wherever sound experimental or independent theory analyses are available; hence, the SCI branching fraction predictions should be a reasonable guide. Considering the ratios $R(D_{(s)}^{(\ast)})$, $R(J/ψ)$, $R(η_c)$, whose values are key tests of lepton universality in weak interactions, the SCI values agree with Standard Model predictions. The $B_{(s)}\to D_{(s)}^\ast$ transitions are used to predict the precursor functions that evolve into the universal Isgur-Wise function in the heavy-quark limit, with results that conform with those from other sources where such are available. The study also exposes effects on the transition form factors that flow from interference between emergent hadron mass from the strong interaction and Higgs boson couplings via current-quark masses, including flavour symmetry violation.

## Key claim

The SCI (symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector×vector contact interaction) provides a unified analysis of twelve semileptonic pseudoscalar-to-vector meson transitions that compares well with experimental and independent theoretical results where available, but the authors note that SCI branching fraction predictions should be considered "a reasonable guide" rather than definitive, and that for some transitions (e.g., B→ρ) the SCI results exceed experimental values by roughly 50%.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector×vector contact interaction (SCI) within the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs). They compute transition form factors (A0, A1, A2, V) for twelve semileptonic decays of heavy+heavy and heavy+light pseudoscalar mesons to vector meson final states, then integrate the differential decay width to obtain branching fractions. The analysis uses experimentally determined meson masses, CKM matrix elements, and meson lifetimes from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- D→ρ transition: SCI predicts A1(0)=0.52, A2(0)=0.36, V(0)=0.83, with ratios r2=0.69 and rV=1.58; experimental values from CLEO are A1=0.56(3), A2=0.47(7), V=0.84(11)
- D→K* transition: SCI predicts r2=0.68, rV=1.50; PDG average gives r2=0.802(21), rV=1.49(05)
- Ds→φ transition: SCI predicts r2=0.72, rV=1.64, r0=1.08; lattice QCD from Donald (2013) gives r2=0.74(12), rV=1.72(21), r0=1.14(6)
- B→ρ branching fraction: SCI predicts B(B0→ρ-eνe)=0.445×10-3; PDG reports 0.294(21)×10-3
- B→D* branching fraction: SCI predicts B(B0→D*-τντ)=15.3×10-3; PDG reports 15.7(9)×10-3
- Bc→J/ψ branching fraction: SCI predicts B(Bc→J/ψ τντ)=3.62×10-3; experimental ratio Rτ/μ=0.71(25) from Aaij (2017)
- Ds→φ branching fraction: SCI predicts B(Ds→φ eνe)=24.5×10-3; PDG reports 23.9(1.6)×10-3

## Context

This work extends the SCI analysis of pseudoscalar-to-pseudoscalar semileptonic transitions from Xu (2021) to vector meson final states, completing a unified treatment of twelve transitions. It provides a benchmark for future continuum Schwinger function method calculations extending Yao (2021) to vector final states, and the SCI results for Ds→φ agree well with lattice QCD results from Donald (2013). The study enables systematic comparison of form factor behaviour across transitions with different mass budgets, exposing effects from interference between emergent hadron mass and Higgs boson couplings.
