# Physics Beyond Standard Model in Neutron Beta Decay

> Limits from neutron beta decay on parameters describing physics beyond the Standard Model are presented. New Physics is described by the most general Lorentz invariant effective Hamiltonian involving vector, scalar and tensor operators and Standard Model fields only. Two-parameter fits to the decay parameters measured in free neutron beta decay have been done, in some cases indicating rather big dependence of the results on g_A/g_V ratio of nucleon form factors at zero four-momentum transfer.

## Key claim

Limits on beyond-Standard-Model parameters from neutron beta decay are extracted via two-parameter fits to measured decay parameters, but the results show a rather big dependence on the assumed value of the nucleon form factor ratio \(g_A/g_V\) at zero four-momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors use the most general Lorentz-invariant effective four-point Hamiltonian at the quark-lepton level, including vector, scalar, and tensor operators with Standard Model fields only. They perform two-parameter fits to experimental data on the neutron beta-decay correlation coefficients \(a\), \(A\), and \(B\) (from 11 data points selected from the PDG, excluding the neutron lifetime due to experimental ambiguity) by minimizing a \(\chi^2\) that accounts for the energy-dependent averaging \(\langle W^{-1}\rangle\) of each measurement. The fits treat the ratio \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\) together with one additional beyond-Standard-Model parameter at a time, using tree-level formulas with the Fermi function for QED corrections.

## Key numbers

*   One-parameter Standard Model fit yields \(\lambda = g_A/g_V = 1.2703 \pm 0.0014\) at 68.27% C.L., \(\pm 0.0023\) at 90% C.L., and \(\pm 0.0028\) at 95.45% C.L., in agreement with the PDG average of \(1.2701 \pm 0.0025\).
*   The \(\chi^2_{min}\) for the one-parameter Standard Model fit is 25.42.
*   Experimental data used include: \(a = -0.1054 \pm 0.0055\) (BYRNE 02) with \(\langle W^{-1}\rangle = 0.655\); \(A = -0.1189 \pm 0.0007\) (ABELE 02) with \(\langle W^{-1}\rangle = 0.534\); \(B = 0.9801 \pm 0.0046\) (SEREBROV 98) with \(\langle W^{-1}\rangle = 0.594\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Herczeg (prior work) and follows the fitting methodology of Gluck, Severijns, and Konrad (prior works) by using energy-averaged decay parameters. It uses the same data selection as the PDG but excludes the neutron lifetime due to its unresolved experimental ambiguity. The results enable improved constraints on scalar and tensor couplings in the effective Hamiltonian, and the authors note that fits including tensor terms (\(T_{LL}\) or \(T_{RR}\)) give minimally better \(\chi^2\) than the Standard Model, motivating further experimental and theoretical study of these operators.
