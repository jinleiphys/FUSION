# Chemical Equilibration of Antihyperons

> Rapid chemical equilibration of antihyperons by means of the interplay between strong annihilation on baryons and the corresponding backreactions of multi-mesonic (fusion-type) processes in the later, hadronic stage of an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision will be discussed. Explicit rate calculations for a dynamical setup are presented. At maximum SPS energies yields of each antihyperon specie are obtained which are consistent with chemical saturated populations of $T \approx 150-160 $ MeV. The proposed picture supports dynamically the popular chemical freeze-out parameters extracted within thermal models.

## Key claim

Rapid chemical equilibration of antihyperons in the later hadronic stage of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can be achieved through the interplay of strong annihilation on baryons and the corresponding backreactions of multi-mesonic fusion-type processes, provided that the initial baryon density is 1–2 times normal nuclear matter density and the annihilation cross section is approximately 50 mb.

## Method

The author solves a master equation for the antihyperon density derived from a Boltzmann-type equation, incorporating annihilation cross sections and multi-mesonic backreactions via detailed balance. The hadronic fireball expansion is modeled as an isentropic Bjorken expansion with accelerating radial flow, with initial temperatures set to 190 MeV for SPS and 150 MeV for AGS conditions. The initial antihyperon abundance is set to zero, and the rate equation is solved for each antihyperon species as a function of time and decreasing temperature.

## Key numbers

- Annihilation cross section for antihyperon on baryon: σ₀ ≈ 50 mb
- Equilibration timescale for antihyperons at 1–2 times normal nuclear matter density: 1–3 fm/c
- Initial temperature for SPS calculations: T₀ = 190 MeV
- Initial temperature for AGS calculations: T₀ = 150 MeV
- Initial energy density for SPS and AGS: about 1 GeV/fm³
- Radial flow parameters: R₀ = 6.5 fm, v₀ = 0.15 c, a₀ = 0.05 c²/fm
- Starting time for SPS: t₀ = 3 fm/c
- Starting time for AGS: t₀ = 5 fm/c
- Entropy per baryon ratio for global SPS results: S/A = 30
- Entropy per baryon ratio for WA97 midrapidity reproduction: S/A = 40
- Entropy per baryon ratio for AGS characteristic situation: S/A = 12
- Effective saturation temperature for antihyperons at SPS: T_eff ≈ 150–160 MeV
- Effective saturation temperature for anti-Λ at AGS: T_eff ≈ 120–130 MeV

## Context

This work extends the reasoning of Rapp and Shuryak (2000) regarding antiproton production via multi-mesonic backreactions to the case of antihyperons, and provides a dynamical foundation for the chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from thermal model analyses such as those by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, and collaborators (1996). The results support the interpretation of chemically saturated antihyperon populations observed by the NA49 and WA97 collaborations at CERN-SPS, and offer a natural explanation for the enhanced anti-Λ to antiproton ratio seen at AGS energies by the E802 collaboration, without invoking exotic mechanisms like color ropes or high-dense cluster formation. The paper enables future experimental tests at energy-upgraded GSI facilities through detailed measurement of all antihyperon species.
