# Photon radiation from quark-gluon plasma by means of chiral anomalies without magnetic field

> We discuss a new channel of photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma, which opens up as photon acquires a tachyonic mass in the presence of the CP-odd topological domains generated by the chiral anomaly. It allows photon radiation through the decay $q\to qγ$ and annihilation $q\bar q\to γ$ processes closely related to the chiral Cherenkov radiation. Unlike previous proposals this mechanism does not require an external magnetic field. The differential photon emission rate per unit volume is computed and shown to be comparable to the rate of photon emission in conventional processes.

# Photon radiation from quark-gluon plasma by means of chiral anomalies without magnetic field

## Key claim

Photon emission from quark-gluon plasma can proceed via a new channel, chiral Cherenkov radiation, in which the photon acquires a tachyonic mass from CP-odd topological domains generated by the chiral anomaly, without requiring an external magnetic field. The computed differential photon emission rate is comparable to conventional perturbative processes, but the mechanism only operates for one photon polarization at a time depending on the channel (decay or annihilation).

## Method

The author computes the photon production rate per unit volume from two processes: the decay channel \(q \to q\gamma\) and the annihilation channel \(q\bar{q} \to \gamma\), using the dispersion relation \(\omega^2 = \mathbf{k}^2 + \omega^2_\text{pl} + m_A^2\) where \(m_A^2 = -\lambda \sigma_\chi \omega\) with \(\sigma_\chi\) the chiral conductivity and \(\lambda = \pm 1\) the photon circular polarization. The scattering matrix elements are evaluated in the thermal medium with quark thermal mass \(m = gT/\sqrt{3}\), and the rates are summed over polarizations and integrated over phase space with equilibrium Fermi-Dirac distributions.

## Key numbers

- Plasma temperature used in the numerical example: \(T = 400\) MeV.
- Chiral conductivity assumed: \(\sigma_\chi = 1\) MeV.
- Sum of squared quark charges for the two lightest flavors: \(\sum_f Q_f^2 = 5/9\).
- At low photon energy \(\omega \ll T\), the decay channel spectrum scales as \(\omega \, d\Gamma_D/d^3k \propto 1/\omega^2\), producing a large logarithm \(\ln(T/m)\) in the total rate.
- At high photon energy \(\omega \gg T\), the decay channel rate is \(\omega \, d\Gamma_D/d^3k = 0.73 \cdot 2N_c \frac{e^2 Q^2}{8(2\pi)^4} |m_A^2| \frac{1}{\beta\omega} e^{-\beta\omega}\).
- At low photon energy \(\omega \ll T\), the annihilation channel rate is \(\omega \, d\Gamma_A/d^3k = N_c \frac{e^2 Q^2}{16(2\pi)^4} |m_A^2| \cdot \frac{1}{6}\).
- At high photon energy \(\omega \gg T\), the annihilation channel rate is \(\omega \, d\Gamma_A/d^3k = N_c \frac{e^2 Q^2}{16(2\pi)^4} |m_A^2| \cdot \frac{2}{3} e^{-\beta\omega}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals for photon radiation via the chiral anomaly in the presence of intense magnetic fields (Fukushima, 2012; Mamo, 2013; Mamo, 2015) by showing that no magnetic field is required, only the CP-odd topological domains generated by sphaleron-mediated transitions between QCD vacua (Kharzeev, 2009; Zhitnitsky, 2013). The mechanism is closely related to chiral Cherenkov radiation (Lehnert, 2004; Tuchin, 2018; Stewart, 2019). The computed rates are comparable to conventional perturbative contributions (Paquet, 2015), but the largest uncertainty is the chiral conductivity \(\sigma_\chi\), which can be extracted from charge separation measurements in heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev, 2007).
