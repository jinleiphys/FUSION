# QCD with Two Light Dynamical Chirally Improved Quarks: Baryons

> We present a study of baryon ground states and low lying excitations of non-strange and strange baryons. The results are based on seven gauge field ensembles with two dynamical light Chirally Improved (CI) quarks corresponding to pion masses between 255 and 596 MeV and a strange valence quark with mass fixed by the Omega baryon. The lattice spacing varies between 0.1324 and 0.1398 fm. Given in lattice units, the bulk of our results are for size 16^3\times 32, for two ensembles with light pion masses (255 and 330 MeV) we also use 24^3\times 48 lattices and perform an infinite volume extrapolation. We derive energy levels for the spin 1/2 and 3/2 channels for both parities. In general, our results in the infinite volume limit compare well with experiment. We analyze the flavor symmetry content by identifying the singlet/octet/decuplet contributions of the resulting eigenstates. The ground states compositions agree with quark model expectations. In some cases the excited states, however, disagree and we discuss possible reasons.

## Key claim

The study finds that baryon ground state compositions agree with quark model expectations, but some excited states disagree, possibly because the three-quark interpolators used do not couple strongly enough to certain resonances (such as the Roper resonance N(1440)) or because coupling to meson-baryon scattering states (e.g., πN) is neglected.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using two dynamical light Chirally Improved (CI) quarks on seven gauge field ensembles with pion masses from 255 to 596 MeV and a strange valence quark with mass fixed by the Omega baryon. They used the variational method with up to 24 interpolating fields per quantum channel to extract energy levels for spin 1/2 and 3/2 baryons of both parities, analyzing the flavor symmetry content by identifying singlet, octet, and decuplet contributions.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses range from 255(7) MeV to 596(5) MeV across seven ensembles
- Lattice spacing varies between 0.1324(11) fm and 0.1398(14) fm
- Bulk results use 16^3 × 32 lattices; two ensembles with pion masses 255 and 330 MeV also use 24^3 × 48 lattices for infinite volume extrapolation
- Nucleon ground state chiral extrapolation yields a mass roughly 7% larger than the experimental nucleon mass
- First excitation in the nucleon positive parity channel is approximately 500 MeV higher than the experimental Roper resonance N(1440)
- Delta(1232) chiral extrapolation comes out too high compared to experiment
- Sigma(1189) ground state extrapolates close to the experimental value
- Three low-lying states in the Sigma 1/2- channel extrapolate close to the experimental Sigma(1620) and Sigma(1750)
- Xi(1322) ground state chiral extrapolation agrees nicely with experiment

## Context

This work extends previous studies of single baryon correlators by Engel et al. (2010, 2012) with more ensembles and larger statistics, using the same Chirally Improved fermion action. It contrasts with results from Edwards et al. (2012) on anisotropic lattices and with studies including meson-baryon interpolators such as Lang et al. (2012) which demonstrated significant effects in the negative parity nucleon sector. The results enable future work incorporating explicit two-hadron interpolators to better resolve the observed discrepancies in excited states, particularly for the Roper resonance and other states where coupling to scattering channels may be important.
