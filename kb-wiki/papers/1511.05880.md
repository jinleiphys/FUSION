# Mass-radius constraints for the neutron star EoS - Bayesian analysis

> We suggest a new Bayesian analysis (BA) using disjunct M-R constraints for extracting probability measures for cold, dense matter equations of state (EoS). One of the key issues of such an analysis is the question of a deconfinement transition in compact stars and whether it proceeds as a crossover or rather as a first order transition. We show by postulating results of not yet existing radius measurements for the known pulsars with a mass of $2M_\odot$ that a radius gap of about 3 km would clearly select an EoS with a strong first order phase transition as the most probably one. This would support the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram under scrutiny in present and upcoming heavy-ion collision experiments.

# Mass-radius constraints for the neutron star EoS - Bayesian analysis

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that if future radius measurements for the two known pulsars with masses of 2 solar masses were to show a radius gap of about 3 km between them, this would select an equation of state with a strong first-order phase transition as the most probable one. This conclusion depends on the assumption of ficticious radius measurements with a 1-sigma accuracy of about 0.5 km and mean values of 15 km and 12 km respectively.

## Method
The authors construct a family of hybrid neutron star equations of state using a Maxwell construction between hadronic matter (with excluded volume corrections to the DD2 model) and quark matter (with multi-quark interactions including an 8-quark vector channel). They perform a Bayesian analysis over two free parameters, the excluded volume parameter p (0 to 80) and the 8-quark coupling constant η₄ (0 to 30), using both real observational constraints and ficticious radius measurements to compute posterior probabilities for each equation of state model.

## Key numbers
- Radius gap of about 3 km between the two 2-solar-mass pulsars would select a strong first-order phase transition as most probable
- Excluded volume parameter p varied in range from 0 to 80 (dimensionless, where p = 10 × v[fm³])
- 8-quark vector coupling η₄ varied in range from 0 to 30 (dimensionless)
- Saturation density n_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³ used as threshold for excluded volume corrections
- Ficticious radius measurements assumed: R = 12 ± 0.5 km for PSR J0348+0432 and R = 15 ± 0.5 km for PSR J1614-2230, with 1-sigma accuracy of about 0.5 km
- Nucleon hard-core radius r_N used to define van-der-Waals excluded volume v = 16π r_N³/3

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach of Steiner (2010) for quantifying equation of state likelihood from neutron star observations. It builds on the classification of hybrid star branches by Alford (2013) and the hybrid star twin solutions of Benic (2014). The method enables quantification of the discriminating power of future observational programmes such as NICER and SKA before actual measurements are made, and could support the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments.
