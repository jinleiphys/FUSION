# Calculating the angle between jet axes

> We study the angle between i) the standard jet axis, ii) the axis of a jet which has been groomed using soft drop, with reduced sensitivity to soft radiation, iii) the jet axis obtained with the winner-take-all recombination scheme, which is insensitive to soft radiation at leading power. We calculate the distributions for these angles at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, including non-global logarithms. The angle between the standard and groomed jet axis directly probes soft wide-angle radiation, leading to a novel factorization formula. This angle is also very sensitive to nonperturbative physics, which is directly connected to nonperturbative contribution to the rapidity anomalous dimension for transverse momentum distributions. Comparing our predictions to Pythia we find good agreement, and we foresee applications to jet substructure in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the distribution of the angular separation between three types of jet axes (standard, groomed via soft drop, and winner-take-all) at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, but the angle between the standard and groomed jet axis is limited to NLL accuracy for global logarithms due to the considerably more complicated factorization structure, while the other two angles achieve NLL' accuracy including non-global logarithms.

## Method

The authors derive factorization formulas within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the small-angle limit, where the angle is expressed as transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) relative to the jet axis. They perform resummation of logarithms of \(k_\perp/(p_T R)\) using renormalization group evolution, including non-global logarithms in the leading-color approximation, and for the standard-groomed angle they perform a joint resummation with the groomed jet radius \(R_g\) before integrating over it. The calculations are implemented in impact parameter space to avoid known issues in transverse momentum resummation, and the results are compared to the Pythia parton shower event generator.

## Key numbers

* The jet radius parameter used is \(R = 0.8\) for the standard anti-\(k_T\) algorithm.
* Only jets with rapidity \(|\eta| < 2\) are considered.
* The soft drop grooming parameter is \(z_{\text{cut}} = 0.1\) with several values of the angular exponent \(\beta\).
* The cross section is differential in jet transverse momentum \(p_T\) and the angle \(\theta = |\vec k_\perp|/p_T\).
* The natural scales for the hard function, collinear function, and soft function are \(\mu_H = p_T R\), \(\mu_C = k_\perp\), and \(\mu_S = k_\perp\) respectively for the standard-WTA angle.
* For the groomed-WTA angle, the collinear-soft function has a rapidity scale \(\nu_{\CS} = 2 p_T z_{\text{cut}}^{1/(1+\beta)} (k_\perp/(p_T R))^{\beta/(1+\beta)}\).
* The LL expression for the standard-WTA angle involves \(\exp[ -(\alpha_s C_i/\pi) \ln^2(p_T R/k_\perp) ]\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet substructure observables by introducing new angular observables between different jet axes, building on prior work on transverse momentum resummation (Collins 1984, Ji 2004, Becher 2010) and soft drop grooming (Larkoski 2014). The angle between the standard and groomed jet axis directly probes soft wide-angle radiation and is connected to the nonperturbative contribution to the rapidity anomalous dimension for transverse momentum distributions, which has been extracted by Landry (2002), Su (2014), and Bacchetta (2017). The results enable applications to jet substructure in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions, where the different sensitivity of the axes to soft radiation can be used to study parton energy loss and jet broadening.
