# A nonrelativistic quark model evaluation of exclusive $b\to c$ semileptonic decay of triply heavy baryons and $c\to s,d$ semileptonic decay of $cb$ baryons

> We present results for exclusive $b\to c$ semileptonic decays of ground state triply-heavy baryons and for semileptonic $c\to s,d$ decays of doubly heavy ground state $cb$ baryons. In both cases, we have derived for the first time heavy quark spin symmetry relations for the hadronic amplitudes near zero recoil. Though strictly valid in the limit of very large heavy quark masses and near zero recoil, they turn out to be reasonable accurate for the whole available phase space in these decays and for the actual heavy quark masses we use. With these relations we have made approximate, but model independent, predictions for ratios of decay widths. In the case of spin-1/2 $cb$ baryons, we find that hyperfine mixing in the wave function has a great impact on their $c\to s,d$ decay widths.

# A nonrelativistic quark model evaluation of exclusive \(b\to c\) semileptonic decay of triply heavy baryons and \(c\to s,d\) semileptonic decay of \(cb\) baryons

## Key claim
Heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) relations for hadronic amplitudes near zero recoil, derived for the first time for these systems, are strictly valid only in the limit of very large heavy quark masses and near zero recoil, but turn out to be reasonably accurate for the whole available phase space and for the actual heavy quark masses used. Using these relations, the authors make approximate but model-independent predictions for ratios of decay widths, finding that hyperfine mixing in the wave function has a great impact on the \(c\to s,d\) decay widths of spin-1/2 \(cb\) baryons.

## Method
The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with the AL1 potential (containing \(1/r\) and hyperfine terms from one-gluon exchange plus a linear confining term, with parameters fitted to light and heavy meson spectra) to compute wave functions variationally as sums of displaced Gaussians. For triply heavy baryons, they evaluate the Isgur-Wise functions \(\eta\), \(\chi\), and \(\xi\) from the HQSS relations and compute \(b\to c\) semileptonic decay widths; for doubly heavy \(cb\) baryons, they compute \(c\to s,d\) semileptonic decay widths including hyperfine mixing between spin-0 and spin-1 heavy-quark-pair configurations.

## Key numbers
- Triply heavy baryon masses (MeV): \(\Omega^*_{bbb}\) = 14398, \(\Xi^*_{bbc}\) = 11245, \(\Xi_{bbc}\) = 11214, \(\Xi^*_{ccb}\) = 8046, \(\Xi_{ccb}\) = 8018, \(\Omega^*_{ccc}\) = 4799 (variational calculation with AL1 potential).
- \(b\to c\) decay widths (ps\(^{-1}\)): \(\Xi_{ccb}\to\Omega^*_{ccc}\,e\bar\nu_e\) = \(8.01\times10^{-2}\), \(\Xi^*_{ccb}\to\Omega^*_{ccc}\,e\bar\nu_e\) = \(6.28\times10^{-2}\), \(\Xi_{bbc}\to\Xi_{ccb}\,e\bar\nu_e\) = \(7.98\times10^{-2}\), \(\Omega^*_{bbb}\to\Xi_{bbc}\,e\bar\nu_e\) = \(3.95\times10^{-2}\), \(\Omega^*_{bbb}\to\Xi^*_{bbc}\,e\bar\nu_e\) = \(6.34\times10^{-2}\) (using \(|V_{bc}|=0.0410\)).
- Model-independent ratio predictions satisfied at 3.4% and 0.25% level: \(2\Gamma(\Xi^*_{bbc}\to\Xi_{ccb})/\Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to\Xi^*_{ccb})\approx 1\) and \(\Gamma(\Xi^*_{bbc}\to\Xi^*_{ccb})/[4\Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to\Xi_{ccb})-10\Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to\Xi^*_{ccb})]\approx 1\).
- \(c\to s\) decay widths (10\(^{-14}\) GeV): \(\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Xi^0_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 3.74 (3.45 without mixing), \(\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Xi'^0_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 3.88 (1.66 without mixing), \(\Omega^{(1)0}_{cbs}\to\Omega^-_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 7.21 (3.12 without mixing), \(\Omega^{*0}_{cbs}\to\Omega^{*-}_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 10.2 (using \(|V_{cs}|=0.973\)).
- \(c\to d\) decay widths (10\(^{-14}\) GeV): \(\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Lambda^0_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 0.219 (0.196 without mixing), \(\Xi^{(1)+}_{cbu}\to\Sigma^0_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 0.198 (0.0814 without mixing), \(\Omega^{(1)0}_{cbs}\to\Xi^-_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 0.179 (0.164 without mixing), \(\Omega^{*0}_{cbs}\to\Xi^{*-}_b\, e^+\nu_e\) = 0.223 (using \(|V_{cd}|=0.225\)).
- Hyperfine mixing coefficients for physical spin-1/2 \(cb\) baryons: \(\Xi_{cb}^{(1)} = -0.902\,\Xi'_{cb} + 0.431\,\Xi_{cb}\) (mass 6967 MeV), \(\Xi_{cb}^{(2)} = 0.431\,\Xi'_{cb} + 0.902\,\Xi_{cb}\) (mass 6919 MeV); \(\Omega_{cb}^{(1)} = -0.899\,\Omega'_{cb} + 0.437\,\Omega_{cb}\) (mass 7046 MeV), \(\Omega_{cb}^{(2)} = 0.437\,\Omega'_{cb} + 0.899\,\Omega_{cb}\) (mass 7005 MeV).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of triply heavy baryon spectroscopy (Hasenfratz 1980, Silvestre-Brac 1996, Martynenko 2007, Roberts 2007, Zhang 2009, Meinel 2010) and of doubly heavy \(cb\) baryon decays (Sanchis-Lozano 1995, Faessler 2001, Kiselev 2001) by providing the first HQSS relations for semileptonic decays of these systems. The results enable model-independent tests of HQSS in future LHC measurements of triply heavy baryons (Chen 2011) and provide predictions for \(c\to s,d\) decays of \(cb\) baryons that are complementary to the more extensively studied \(b\to c\) decays (Ebert 2004, Hernandez 2007, Faessler 2009, Albertus 2009). The hyperfine mixing effects confirm earlier suggestions by Pervin (2007) and extend findings for \(b\to c\) decays and electromagnetic transitions (Albertus 2010, Branz 2010) to the \(c\to s,d\) channel.
