# The neutron star inner crust and symmetry energy

> The cell structure of clusters in the inner crust of a cold β-equilibrium neutron star is studied within a Thomas Fermi approach and compared with other approaches which include shell effects. Relativistic nuclear models are considered. We conclude that the symmetry energy slope L may have quite dramatic effects on the cell structure if it is very large or small. Rod-like and slab-like pasta clusters have been obtained in all models except one with a large slope L.

# The neutron star inner crust and symmetry energy

## Key claim

The symmetry energy slope L has dramatic effects on the cell structure of clusters in the inner crust of a cold beta-equilibrium neutron star, but only if L is very large or very small; models with intermediate L values (approximately 50-60 MeV) produce similar results, while rod-like and slab-like pasta clusters appear in all models except NL3, which has a large slope L of 118 MeV.

## Method

A self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approach was applied within relativistic mean-field models, including both non-linear Walecka models (NL3, NL3_ωρ, FSU, IU-FSU) and density-dependent coupling models (DD-ME2, DD-MEδ), to study the inner crust structure in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The cell structure (proton number Z, neutron number N, cell radius R_ws) was obtained from energy minimization at beta equilibrium for three cell shapes (droplet, rod, slab) across twelve density regions from the neutron drip density to the crust-core transition, treating Z and N as integers.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density: approximately 3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Crust-core transition density: approximately 8 × 10⁻² fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation: between 31.3 and 32.6 MeV for most models, except NL3 at 37.3 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L: between 45 and 60 MeV for most models, with NL3 at 118 MeV
- Standard inner crust region defined as ρ ≤ 4.71 × 10⁻² fm⁻³; pasta phase region defined as ρ ≥ 4.71 × 10⁻² fm⁻³
- Proton number in TF calculations near the outer crust transition: between 39 and 46
- NL3 cluster nucleon number at crust-core transition: less than 100
- IU-FSU and SLy4 cluster nucleon number at crust-core transition: more than 500
- All other models predict clusters not exceeding 200-250 nucleons
- Crust-core transition densities from dynamical spinodal calculations: SLy4 at 0.080 fm⁻³, NL3 at 0.054 fm⁻³, DD-ME2 at 0.072 fm⁻³, FSU at 0.074 fm⁻³, NL3_ωρ at 0.0855 fm⁻³, IU-FSU at 0.087 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach of Sidney et al. (2008, 2010) to study the inner crust structure with relativistic nuclear models, comparing with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of Grill et al. (2011) and the Hartree-Fock calculations of Negele and Vautherin (1973). The results confirm the findings of Oyamatsu (2007) that models with large L do not predict pasta shapes in beta-equilibrium matter, and agree with the dynamical spinodal calculations of Isaac et al. for crust-core transition densities. The work enables systematic comparison of how the symmetry energy slope affects cluster properties across different nuclear models, and confirms that low-energy collective modes in slab-like "lasagna" pasta configurations could contribute to the specific heat, as suggested by Luciano et al. (2011).
