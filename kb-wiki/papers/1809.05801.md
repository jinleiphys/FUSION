# Low-Energy Heavy-Ion Reactions and the Skyrme Effective Interaction

> The Skyrme effective interaction, with its multitude of parameterisations, along with its implemen- tation using the static and time-dependent density functional (TDHF) formalism have allowed for a range of microscopic calculations of low-energy heavy-ion collisions. These calculations allow variation of the effective interaction along with an interpretation of the results of this variation informed by a comparison to experimental data. Initial progress in implementing TDHF for heavy-ion collisions necessarily used many approximations in the geometry or the interaction. Over the last decade or so, the implementations have overcome all restrictions, and studies have begun to be made where details of the effective interaction are being probed. This review surveys these studies in low energy heavy-ion reactions, finding significant effects on observables from the form of the spin-orbit interaction, the use of the tensor force, and the inclusion of time-odd terms in the density functional.

## Key claim

This review surveys studies of low-energy heavy-ion reactions using the Skyrme effective interaction within the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) formalism, finding that observables are significantly affected by the form of the spin-orbit interaction, the use of the tensor force, and the inclusion of time-odd terms in the density functional, though the authors note that the border between included and excluded calculations is "a somewhat arbitrary choice" and that systematic variation of the pairing interaction on dynamics "has not been extensively studied."

## Method

The authors review TDHF calculations that solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for a Slater determinant of single-particle wave functions, where two nuclei in their ground states are placed in a computational box, given Galilean boosts corresponding to a specified center-of-mass energy and impact parameter, and time-evolved using the Skyrme-Kohn-Sham Hamiltonian. The review covers both full TDHF calculations and simpler approaches such as the frozen Hartree-Fock approximation and density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF), which extract nucleus-nucleus potentials from the microscopic interaction. The Skyrme interaction is presented in its full form including two-body, three-body, spin-orbit, and tensor terms, with the energy density functional derived from the interaction parameters.

## Key numbers

- The fusion cross-section formula is given as sigma_f approximately equal to pi(b_>^2 - b_<^2), where b_< and b_> are the minimum and maximum impact parameters for fusion at a given center-of-mass energy.
- Figure 1 shows the fusion region in the E_CM versus impact parameter plane for 16O + 16O collisions using the SkM* interaction (Bartel 1982), with error bars indicating the energy intervals where the transition between fusion and not-fusion is found.
- The BKN interaction (Bonche 1976) uses only the t_0 and t_3 terms of the Skyrme interaction, replacing momentum-dependent terms with a finite-range Yukawa potential, and specifically does not include the spin-orbit interaction.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on TDHF for heavy-ion collisions that used many approximations in geometry or the interaction, noting that implementations have now overcome all restrictions. It overlaps with a review by Simenel and Umar (2018) covering theoretical approaches to heavy-ion dynamics in TDHF and its extensions, and with a review by Xu et al. (2015) on spin-dependent aspects of the effective interaction. The review enables systematic variation of Skyrme force parameterizations within low-energy heavy-ion reaction calculations, building on the density-matrix expansion method of Negele and Vautherin (1972) that linked the Skyrme interaction to more realistic interactions via nuclear matter.
