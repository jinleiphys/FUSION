# Sensitivity of (d,p) reactions to high n-p momenta and the consequences for nuclear spectroscopy studies

> Theoretical models of low-energy (d,p) single-neutron transfer reactions are a crucial link between experimentation, nuclear structure and nuclear astrophysical studies. Whereas reaction models that use local optical potentials are insensitive to short-range physics in the deuteron, we show that including the inherent nonlocality of the nucleon-target interactions and realistic deuteron wave functions generates significant sensitivity to high n-p relative momenta and to the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction. We quantify this effect upon the deuteron channel distorting potentials within the framework of the adiabatic deuteron breakup model. The implications for calculated (d,p) cross sections and spectroscopic information deduced from experiments are discussed.

# Sensitivity of (d,p) reactions to high n-p momenta and the consequences for nuclear spectroscopy studies

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that low-energy (d,p) single-neutron transfer reactions exhibit significant sensitivity to high n-p relative momenta and to the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction model, but only when both the nonlocality of nucleon-target optical potentials and realistic S+D-state deuteron wave functions are included in the adiabatic deuteron breakup model. This sensitivity is absent in conventional analyses that use local optical potentials.

## Method
The authors performed exact calculations of the nonlocal adiabatic deuteron-target distorting potential U_dA using nonlocal nucleon-target optical potentials of Perey-Buck form and realistic deuteron wave functions from multiple nucleon-nucleon interaction models (Hulthen, Reid soft core, Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, and N4LO chiral EFT with five regulators). They solved the resulting integro-differential equation for the deuteron distorted waves iteratively after partial-wave expansion, then computed (d,p) cross sections for the 26Al(d,p)27Al reaction at E_d = 12 MeV using the transfer reactions code twofnr with zero-range approximation for the transition interaction.

## Key numbers
- For the d+26Al system at E_d = 12 MeV, the energy shift ΔE (to be added to E_d/2 when evaluating nucleon potentials) ranges from 31.2 MeV for the S-state Hulthen model to 74.6 MeV for the Reid soft core model and 66.2 MeV for Argonne v18.
- The D-state fraction of the matrix element ⟨φ0|V_np|φ0⟩ (denoted P_D) ranges from 32.6% for CD-Bonn to 39.7% for Reid soft core, despite the deuteron D-state probability P_D being only 4-7%.
- For the χEFT models, ⟨T_np⟩_V values range from 88.2 MeV (1.2 fm regulator) to 247.2 MeV (0.8 fm regulator), and ΔE ranges from 44.2 MeV to 71.6 MeV.
- For local nucleon potentials, the depths of real and imaginary parts of U_dA at the nuclear surface differ by less than 1.5% and 3.7% respectively between NN models, and cross sections change by less than 0.6%.
- For nonlocal potentials, the ℓ=2 transfer to the 27Al ground state shows cross section sensitivity of 20-200% depending on NN model; for the ℓ=2 transfer to the Jπ=9/2+ state at E_x = 7.806 MeV, effects are 20-50%.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Timofeyuk, Johnson, and Tostevin (2015) that first identified the energy shift ΔE in the adiabatic model when using nonlocal nucleon potentials, but which assumed only a central Hulthen S-state deuteron wave function. The present paper shows that including realistic S+D-state wave functions dramatically increases the sensitivity to high n-p momenta, with D-state components contributing approximately 40% of the relevant matrix element despite being only 4-7% of the wave function norm. This challenges the conventional local-potential analyses used in nuclear spectroscopy studies, particularly for reactions relevant to nuclear astrophysics such as the destruction of 26Al in stars. The results imply significant uncertainty in spectroscopic factors deduced from standard (d,p) reaction analyses and motivate revisiting the convergence of the Weinberg states expansion when nucleon nonlocality is included.
