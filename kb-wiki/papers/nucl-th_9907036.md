# Coulomb Distortion Effects for (e,e'p) Reactions at High Electron Energy

> We report a significant improvement of an approximate method of including electron Coulomb distortion in electron induced reactions at momentum transfers greater than the inverse of the size of the target nucleus. In particular, we have found a new parametrization for the elastic electron scattering phase shifts that works well at all electron energies greater than 300 $MeV$. As an illustration, we apply the improved approximation to the $(e,e'p)$ reaction from medium and heavy nuclei. We use a relativistic ``single particle'' model for $(e,e'p)$ as as applied to $^{208}Pb(e,e'p)$ and to recently measured data at CEBAF on $^{16}O(e,e'p)$ to investigate Coulomb distortion effects while examining the physics of the reaction.

## Key claim

The authors present an improved parametrization of elastic electron scattering phase shifts that works well at all electron energies above 300 MeV, but the approximation is adequate only for experiments at the few percent level and shows small deviations from exact phases for angular momentum values corresponding to the nuclear surface region.

## Method

The authors construct plane-wave-like electron wavefunctions using a local effective momentum approximation (LEMA) combined with a new two-term parametrization of elastic scattering phase shifts that requires calculating only two exact phase shifts (for κ=1 and κ=Int(pR)+5). They apply this approximate distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) to (e,e'p) reactions on 208Pb and 16O using a relativistic single-particle model with σ-ω meson exchange for the nucleon wavefunctions, comparing to exact DWBA results and to new CEBAF data.

## Key numbers

- Incident electron energy E_i = 412 MeV and outgoing proton kinetic energy T_p = 100 MeV for the 208Pb test case with electron scattering angle θ_e = 74°
- Incident electron energy E_i = 2441.6 MeV and outgoing proton kinetic energy T_p = 427 MeV for the 16O CEBAF data comparison
- The new phase parametrization reproduces exact DWBA results at about the 1-2% level near cross section peaks for heavy nuclei like 208Pb
- Spectroscopic factors extracted from 16O data: 61% for the p_1/2 orbital and 70% for the p_3/2 orbital
- Coulomb distortion effects for 16O at medium energy (500 MeV) were of order 3%
- The parametrization uses a nuclear radius formula R = 1.2A^(1/3) - 0.86/A^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the approximate Coulomb distortion treatment developed by Kim and Wright (1996, 1997) based on earlier work by Knoll (1973) and Lenz and Rosenfelder (1971), improving upon the previous κ²-dependence phase parametrization that failed at higher electron energies. The new parametrization enables analysis of (e,e'p) reactions at CEBAF energies (above 2 GeV) where exact DWBA calculations become computationally prohibitive, and importantly allows extraction of longitudinal and transverse structure functions even in the presence of strong Coulomb distortion, which is impossible in exact partial wave treatments.
