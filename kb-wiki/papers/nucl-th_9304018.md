# Electromagnetic Dipole Response as a Test of the $^{\bf 11}$Li g.s. Structure and the n-$^{\bf 9}$Li Interaction

> The electric dipole response of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Li is calculated in a hyperspherical three-body formulation, and is studied as a function of the interaction employed for $n-^9$Li to reflect the Pauli principle. Strength concentrations at lower energies are found but no narrow resonances. Only one possible scenario of $^{11}$Li structure is in close correspondence with MSU and RIKEN experimental data.

# Electromagnetic Dipole Response as a Test of the 11Li g.s. Structure and the n-9Li Interaction

## Key claim

The electric dipole response of 11Li, calculated in a hyperspherical three-body formulation, shows strength concentrations at lower energies but no narrow resonances; only one of three scenarios for the ground-state structure (the H scenario, which uses a shallow Gaussian potential with no explicit Pauli terms) is in close qualitative agreement with the MSU and RIKEN experimental data, while the WS-P and WS-F scenarios produce distributions that are too broad.

## Method

The authors performed three-body continuum calculations for 11Li using a hyperspherical harmonics expansion, treating the nucleus as a core (9Li) plus two halo neutrons. They considered three scenarios for the n-9Li interaction and Pauli principle treatment: the H scenario (shallow Gaussian potential with no explicit Pauli terms), the WS-P scenario (pairing-dominant, with only S=0 configurations in the 0p shell and repulsive s-wave Pauli core), and the WS-F scenario (spin-orbit-dominant, with repulsive potentials in occupied 0s and 0p3/2 states). The nn interaction was the Gogny-Pires-de Tourreil (GPT) potential in all cases, and continuum wave functions were calculated using the same three-body Hamiltonian as for the ground state.

## Key numbers

- The experimental MSU data for the electric dipole strength distribution peaks at around 0.6 MeV, with energy measured from the break-up threshold; the same peak position was reported for the RIKEN experiment at 42A MeV.
- The MSU fragmentation experiment for 11Li + Pb was carried out at 28A MeV.
- The 11Li ground state is weakly bound at about 300 keV (0.3 MeV), with calculated binding energies of 0.332 MeV (WS-F), 0.248 MeV (WS-P), and 0.295 MeV (H).
- The matter radii are 2.97 fm (WS-F), 3.00 fm (WS-P), and 3.31 fm (H).
- The mean energies for the E1 response are 4.7 MeV (WS-F), 2.8 MeV (WS-P), and 1.6 MeV (H).
- The hyper-radial moment <ρ^2> is 48.3 fm^2 (WS-F), 48.0 fm^2 (WS-P), and 76.3 fm^2 (H); <ρ^4> is 4129 fm^4 (WS-F), 4936 fm^4 (WS-P), and 16135 fm^4 (H).
- The effective range of the dipole operator is about 40 fm, and calculations were extended out to 150-200 fm.
- In the H scenario, the ground state wave function is 96.25% in the K=0, L=0, S=0, l_nn=0, l_(nn)c=0 partial wave; in WS-F the dominant components are 40.93% (K=2, L=0, S=0) and 54.21% (K=2, L=1, S=1); in WS-P the dominant component is 94.55% (K=2, L=0, S=0).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful hyperspherical harmonics calculations for the Borromean nucleus 6He (Danilin et al., 1991, 1992), which reproduced both the 0+ ground state and the known 2+ resonance at 1.8 MeV excitation energy, to the case of 11Li. The calculations are compared with the experimental data from MSU (Michigan fragmentation experiment) and RIKEN, and with earlier theoretical models: the point-dineutron cluster model of Bertulani and Bauer (1991, 1992) and the Green's function method of Esbensen and Bertsch (1992). The results show that the dipole response is sensitive to the ground-state structure and the treatment of the Pauli principle, and that only the H scenario agrees with experiment, though the question of how to handle the Pauli principle in halo nuclei remains open.
