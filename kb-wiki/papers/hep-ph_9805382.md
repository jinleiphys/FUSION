# Pion Breather States in QCD

> We describe a class of pionic breather solutions (PBS) which appear in the chiral lagrangian description of low-energy QCD. These configurations are long-lived, with lifetimes greater than $10^3$ fm/c, and could arise as remnants of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation at RHIC. We show that the chiral lagrangian equations of motion for a uniformly isospin-polarized domain reduce to those of the sine-gordon model. Consequently, our solutions are directly related to the breather solutions of sine-gordon theory in 3+1 dimensions. We investigate the possibility of PBS formation from multiple domains of DCC, and show that the probability of formation is non-negligible.

## Key claim

The paper claims that long-lived, classical pionic configurations called Pion Breather States (PBS) exist in the chiral lagrangian description of low-energy QCD, with lifetimes greater than 10^3 fm/c, but these configurations are at best metastable rather than exactly stable because the inequality E < Q m (where Q is isospin and m the pion mass) is never satisfied for the chiral lagrangian.

## Method

The authors numerically evolved the classical equations of motion derived from the chiral lagrangian (to O(p^2)) for soft pion fields, using a linear sigma model technique in the limit of large self-coupling to avoid constrained equations. They studied single-domain initial conditions with spherical and cylindrical symmetry, as well as multiple-domain configurations with two or three cylinders stacked along the z-axis, varying the isospin orientation between domains and the overall field strength. They also tested the effect of incoherent contamination by superposing sinusoidal field configurations with random phases onto the coherent initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- PBS lifetimes reach a maximum of 1200 fm for the lower-energy state, with central energy remaining almost constant at approximately 26 GeV
- The lower-energy PBS oscillates through of order 100 cycles of period approximately 10 fm, with amplitude 5.4 F_pi and radius approximately 4 fm
- A second, more energetic PBS was observed with energy approximately 112 GeV and lifetime approximately 4000 fm, oscillation frequency 0.0619 cycles/fm, amplitude 12.9 F_pi, and approximate radius 5 fm
- No third type of PBS was found with energy less than 4000 GeV
- In multiple-domain simulations with two domains, PBS formation occurred for roughly alpha < 90 degrees in about 50% of cases; for three domains, roughly 25% of sampled cases showed PBS formation
- The initial domain size was chosen at 10 fm with skin size approximately 1 fm; cylindrical domains had radius 5.5 fm and height 11 fm
- The PBS decay generates approximately 200 pions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domain evolution (Hormuzdiar and Hsu, 1998) and connects the chiral lagrangian dynamics of uniformly isospin-polarized domains to the sine-gordon model, relating the PBS to previously known 3+1 sine-gordon breathers (SGB). The results suggest that PBS could provide dramatic signals at RHIC for coherent, long-wavelength phenomena involving the QCD vacuum, and the authors note that the criteria for stability of such configurations is related to earlier work on time-dependent, non-dissipative configurations (Lee; Coleman).
