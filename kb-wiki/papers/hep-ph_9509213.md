# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Three-Dimensionally Expanding, Cylindrically Symmetric, Finite Systems

> The parameters of the Bose-Einstein correlation function may obey an {\it $M_t$-scaling}, as observed in $S + Pb$ and $Pb + Pb$ reactions at CERN SPS. This $M_t$-scaling implies that the Bose-Einstein correlation functions view only a small part of the big and expanding system. The full sizes of the expanding system at the last interaction are shown to be measurable with the help the invariant momentum distribution of the emitted particles. A vanishing duration parameter can also be generated in the considered model-class with a specific $M_t$ dependence.

## Key claim

The Bose-Einstein correlation function parameters may obey an $M_t$-scaling (where $M_t$ is the transverse mass) that implies the correlation functions view only a small part of a big and expanding system, rather than the full source; this behavior is shown to be a natural consequence of a cylindrically symmetric three-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion, but the authors note that the observed equality of all radius components "might be just a coincidence" and only valid in a certain $m_t$ interval.

## Method

The authors derive general relationships for Bose-Einstein correlation functions in the laboratory frame, the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS), and the longitudinal saddle-point system (LSPS), using a Wigner-function formalism with a classical emission function $S(x;p)$. They introduce a new class of analytically solvable models describing a three-dimensionally expanding, cylindrically symmetric finite boson source with a scaling longitudinal expansion, linear transverse flow profile, and Gaussian approximations for the proper-time distribution, space-time rapidity distribution, and transverse density distribution. The core/halo model is employed to separate resolvable core emission from unresolvable long-lived resonance decay contributions.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal radius parameter scales as $R_L \propto 1/\sqrt{m_t}$, introduced in ref. Sinyukov for an infinite, longitudinally expanding Bjorken tube
- Long-lived resonances contributing to the halo include $\omega$, $\eta$, $\eta'$, and $K^0$, all with lifetimes $1/\Gamma_{res}$ greater than 20 fm/c
- Short-lived resonances contributing to the core have lifetimes: $\rho$ at 1.3 fm/c, $N^*$ at 0.56 fm/c, $\Delta$ at 1.6 fm/c, and $K^*$ at 3.9 fm/c
- The effective intercept parameter $\lambda_*$ equals the square of the ratio of particles emitted from the core to all emitted particles at a given momentum $\mathbf{p}$
- The condition for linearization of $t$ and $r_z$ requires $\Delta\tau_*^2 \ll \tau_s^2$ and $\Delta\eta_*^2 \ll 1$

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional longitudinally expanding finite system model of Csorgo (1994) to three-dimensional expansion with cylindrical symmetry, and generalizes the thermal length-scale concepts from Sinyukov (1994) and the core/halo model. The paper provides a framework for interpreting the NA44 collaboration's observations of equal radius parameters in $S+Pb$ and $Pb+Pb$ reactions at CERN SPS, showing that such behavior can arise from three-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion rather than necessarily indicating a simple geometric picture. The results enable extraction of the full system sizes at last interaction from the invariant momentum distribution, and demonstrate that a vanishing duration parameter can be generated with a specific $M_t$ dependence.
