# Two Dimensional Turbulence in a Massless Fluid with a Relativistic Lattice Boltzmann Model

> We investigate a relativistic adaptation of the Lattice Boltzmann Method that reproduces the equations of motion for a turbulent, two-dimensional, massless hydrodynamic system. The classical Lattice Boltzmann Method and its extension to relativistic fluid dynamics is described. The numeric formulation is evaluated using a zero-averaged stirring force introduced into the numerics to induce turbulence, and the flow characteristics produced are compared to properties of a classical turbulent hydrodynamic flow. The model can reasonably be expected to offer quantitative simulations of electron fluid flows in graphene or Kagome lattices.

## Key claim

The relativistic Lattice Boltzmann model presented can reproduce the inverse energy cascade characteristic of two-dimensional turbulence in a massless fluid, but only when the forcing scheme prevents enstrophy transfer, making the Kolmogorov $-5/3$ power law the appropriate theoretical comparison rather than the $-2$ slope that would appear with non-zero enstrophy transfer.

## Method

The authors adapt the relativistic Lattice Boltzmann Method (RLBM) of Romatschke, Mendoza and Succi (2011) by adding a zero-averaged stirring force in Fourier space to induce turbulence in a two-dimensional massless hydrodynamic system. The model uses a spherical D3Q27 momentum lattice with periodic boundaries, the Jüttner distribution as the equilibrium probability distribution function, and a BGK-type collision operator with viscosity-to-entropy ratio set to 0.01. Simulations are run on lattices of sizes 128², 256², 512², and 1024² with constant volume, and the forcing is applied at wave numbers from a chosen driving scale \(k_f\) up to the maximum wave number allowed by the lattice.

## Key numbers

- Viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 0.01\) used in the collision term for all simulations.
- Lattice spacing \(\alpha\) set to 0.32, 0.16, 0.08, and 0.04 lattice units for lattice sizes 128, 256, 512, and 1024 respectively, maintaining constant volume.
- Driving wave number \(k_f\) set to 6 for most tests.
- Simulations run in 0.05 time unit increments for durations of 50.0, 100.0, 200.0, or 400.0 lattice time units.
- Fluid velocities in graphene are 0.3 percent the speed of light, with Fermi velocity \(v_F \sim 10^6\) m/s.
- Coupling constant in Kagome solids estimated to be 3.2 times that of graphene.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Lattice Boltzmann formulation of Romatschke, Mendoza and Succi (2011) to include a forcing term capable of generating turbulent flows, building on prior RLBM developments by Mendoza, Boghosian, Hermann and Succi (2010) and Gabbana, Simeoni, Succi and Tripiccione (2020). The model is intended to enable quantitative simulations of electron fluid turbulence in graphene and Kagome lattices, where recent work by Di Sante et al. (2019) has measured shear viscosity in Kagome solids using a holographic approach. The numerical validation against the Kolmogorov \(-5/3\) inverse cascade law provides a foundation for studying turbulent massless relativistic fluids in condensed matter systems.
