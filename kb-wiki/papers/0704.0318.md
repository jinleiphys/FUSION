# Effects of Dirac sea on pion propagation in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study pion propagation in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM). One of the interesting consequences of pion propagation in ANM is the mode splitting for the different charged states of pions. First we describe the pion-nucleon dynamics using the non-chiral model where one starts with pseudoscalar (PS) $π$N coupling and the pseudovector (PV) representation is obtained via suitable non-linear field transformations. For both of these cases the effect of the Dirac sea is estimated. Subsequently, we present results using the chiral effective Lagrangian where the short-distance behavior (Dirac vacuum) is included by re-defining the field parameters as done in the modern effective field theory approach developed recently. The results are compared with the previous calculations for the case of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM). Closed form analytical results are presented for the effective pion masses and dispersion relations by making hard nucleon loop (HNL) approximation and suitable density expansion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including Dirac-sea (vacuum) contributions to the pion self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) reduces the unrealistically large effective pion masses obtained from earlier calculations that only included tadpole and nucleon-loop diagrams, and that the mode splitting between charged pion states is driven by terms of order \(k_{p(n)}^3/M^{*2}\) which vanish in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium pion self-energy using a relativistic hadronic model with pseudoscalar (PS) \(\pi N\) coupling, then obtain the pseudovector (PV) representation via nonlinear field transformations following Matsui and Serot (1982). They compute the one-loop pion self-energy including both the vacuum (Feynman) part and the density-dependent (Fermi-sea) part, using the hard nucleon loop (HNL) approximation and density expansion in the long-wavelength limit. The Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved to obtain dispersion relations and effective masses for \(\pi^0\), \(\pi^+\), and \(\pi^-\) in asymmetric nuclear matter, with results compared to the chiral effective Lagrangian approach.

## Key numbers

* For Pb-like nuclei (asymmetry parameter \(\alpha = 0.2\)) at normal nuclear matter density with vacuum correction in the PS model: \(\Delta m_{\pi^0} = 16.8\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Delta m_{\pi^+} = 17.37\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Delta m_{\pi^-} = 17.41\ \text{MeV}\) (mass splittings relative to free pion mass).
* The PS coupling constant used is \(g_\pi^2/4\pi = 12.6\) (Engel, 1996).
* The PV coupling constant used is \(f_\pi^2/4\pi = 0.08\) (Ericson, 1988).
* The tachyonic mode for pions appears at densities as low as \(0.1\rho_0\) in mean-field theory (Serot, 1979; Kapusta, 1981), where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion propagation in symmetric nuclear matter by Kapusta (1981) and Matsui and Serot (1982) to the case of asymmetric nuclear matter, where proton and neutron densities differ. It also improves upon the authors' previous work (Biswas, 2006) by including the Dirac-sea (vacuum) contribution to the pion self-energy, which was neglected there, and by using a renormalizable model with PS coupling and nonlinear field transformations rather than a non-renormalizable PV coupling. The results enable more realistic calculations of pion properties in neutron-rich environments relevant to astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions, and provide closed-form analytical expressions for effective masses and dispersion relations that can be used in studies of dilepton production and pion condensation.
