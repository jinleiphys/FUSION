# Alpha clustering in warm and dense nuclear matter from heavy-ion collisions

> Although light nuclear clusters are known to affect the properties of warm and dilute nuclear matter, their role in warm and dense nuclear matter remains unclear due to the lack of experimental evidence for their modifications by the Mott effect in such an environment. To address this issue, we resort to intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, where light clusters are mainly produced in the transiently formed warm and dense matter. A kinetic approach, which includes dynamically the formation and dissociation of light clusters, is employed to deduce the strength of the Mott effects and the $α$-particle fraction in warm and dense nuclear matter from the light-nuclei yields measured by the FOPI Collaboration in central Au$+$Au collisions at energies of $0.25A$ to $0.6A~\rm GeV$. We find an unexpectedly abundant $α$ clustering in this environment, which will have profound implications for modeling the nuclear equation of state and describing supernovae and neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
The paper finds unexpectedly abundant alpha clustering in warm and dense nuclear matter produced in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, based on a Bayesian inference of the Mott-effect cutoff parameters from measured light-nuclei yields. The claim is conditional on the specific kinetic approach and the phase-space excluded-volume model used to describe the Mott effect, and the results are deduced for the transient nuclear matter where light clusters chemically freeze out in these collisions.

## Method
The authors employed a kinetic approach based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, which dynamically includes the formation and dissociation of light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles) alongside nucleons, pions, and Delta resonances. The in-medium modification of light clusters via the Mott effect is implemented using a phase-space excluded-volume approach, characterized by a set of cutoff parameters (F^cut) that determine the Mott momentum for each cluster species. A Gaussian process emulator was trained on 30 sets of kinetic approach calculations and used in a Bayesian inference to constrain these cutoff parameters by fitting the yields of deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles measured by the FOPI Collaboration in central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 0.25A, 0.4A, and 0.6A GeV.

## Key numbers
- The Bayesian analysis used experimental light-nuclei yields from central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 0.25A GeV, 0.4A GeV, and 0.6A GeV, as measured by the FOPI Collaboration.
- The priors for the cutoff parameters F^cut_2, F^cut_3, and F^cut_4 were uniform distributions over the ranges 0.13–0.25, 0.15–0.37, and 0.20–0.50, respectively.
- The box-calculation validation of the kinetic approach used a system of 80 neutrons and 80 protons in a 10x10x10 fm^3 box, corresponding to a baryon density of 0.16 fm^-3 (approximately nuclear saturation density rho_0) and a temperature of 20 MeV.
- The r.m.s. radii used for the internal wave functions of light clusters in free space were: sigma_d = 2.26 fm, sigma_t = 1.59 fm, sigma_h = 1.76 fm, and sigma_alpha = 1.54 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of light clusters in warm and dilute nuclear matter, such as those by Hagel (2012) and Qin (2012), to the warm and dense regime relevant for supernovae and neutron star mergers. It builds upon the kinetic approach developed by Wang (2023) that includes light clusters up to the alpha particle and the Mott effect. The results enable a quantitative determination of the alpha-particle fraction in dense matter, which has profound implications for modeling the nuclear equation of state, as discussed in works by Oertel (2017) and Sumiyoshi (2020), and for describing the physics of supernovae and binary neutron-star mergers.
