# Pion Photoproduction off Nucleon with Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory

> We analyze the $γN\toπN$ process in the negative parity channel with Hamiltonian effective filed theory which is successful in studying the nucleon resonances associated with the meson-nucleon scattering and lattice QCD simulations. We provide the scattering amplitude of pion photoproduction and extract the electric dipole amplitudes $E_{0+}$. The triquark component in $N^*(1535)$ is important for improving the consistence between our results and the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including a bare triquark component in the $N^*(1535)$ resonance, treated within Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT), improves the consistency between calculated electric dipole amplitudes $E_{0+}$ for pion photoproduction and experimental data, particularly for the imaginary parts on proton targets, though the description is not as precise as some chiral perturbation theory results near threshold.

## Method

The authors extend Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT), previously applied to $\pi N \to \pi N$ scattering and lattice QCD, to the $\gamma N \to \pi N$ process in the negative parity $S_{11}$ partial wave. They compute electromagnetic amplitudes from tree-level diagrams (s-channel, u-channel, t-channel with $\pi$ and $\rho$ exchange, and contact terms) and include contributions from the bare $N^*(1535)$ resonance, then incorporate final state interactions (FSI) via the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation using previously fixed hadronic parameters from $\pi N$ scattering. The electric dipole amplitude $E_{0+}$ is extracted from the resulting T-matrix.

## Key numbers

* The bare $N^*(1535)$ mass is set to $m_{N^*}^0 = 1601$ MeV, with couplings $g_{\pi N N^*} = 0.186$ and $g_{\eta N N^*} = 0.185$ taken from prior HEFT analysis of $\pi N$ scattering (Liu 2015).
* Fitted couplings from photoproduction data: $f_{\eta NN} = -1.42$, $g_{p\gamma N^{*+}} = 0.27$, and $g_{n\gamma N^{*0}} = -0.25$.
* The $\chi^2$ for the proton $E_{0+}$ amplitude is 2136 (in units of $10^{-18}$ m squared) below 1600 MeV, compared to 1574 from the J\"ulich model (Ronchen 2014).
* The contact term contributes more than 80% of ${\rm Re}(E_{0+})$ up to 1400 MeV center-of-mass energy.
* The $\rho$ meson exchange is found indispensable in both isospin channels around 1200 to 1400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the HEFT framework previously applied to $\pi N$ scattering and lattice QCD (Liu 2015, Liu 2016, Wu 2017) to include photoproduction, enabling a consistent description of both hadronic and electromagnetic reactions. It contrasts with K-matrix methods (Arndt 2002, Shklyar 2004) that omit real parts of meson-baryon propagators, and with chiral perturbation theory (Bernard 1994, Ma 2020) which works well near threshold but faces convergence issues at resonance energies. The results are compared with the EBAC dynamical coupled-channel model (Matsuyama 2006, Kamano 2013) and the J\"ulich model (Doring 2009, Ronchen 2014), and the authors note that including the bare triquark component of $N^*(1535)$ supports the pentaquark interpretation proposed by Zou (2010) and An (2008) to explain the mass ordering of $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1440)$.
