# Relativistic multirank interaction kernels of the neutron-proton system

> The multirank separable kernels of the neutron-proton interaction for uncoupled $S$ and $P$ partial waves (with the total angular momentum $J$=0,1) are proposed. Two different methods of a relativistic generalization of initially nonrelativistic form factors parametrizing the kernel are considered. Using the constructed kernels the experimental data for phase shifts in the elastic neutron-proton scattering for the laboratory energy up to 3 GeV and low-energy parameters are described. The comparison of our results with other model calculations are presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes multirank separable kernels for the neutron-proton interaction that describe elastic scattering phase shifts up to laboratory energy 3 GeV, but the authors note that the choice between two relativistic generalization methods (MY vs MYQ) is not dictated by phase-shift description quality but rather by convenience for specific future applications such as electrodisintegration.

## Method

The authors construct separable interaction kernels for uncoupled S and P partial waves (J=0,1) by applying two distinct covariant relativistic generalizations of nonrelativistic Yamaguchi-type form factors: one replaces three-momentum squared with four-momentum squared (method p), the other introduces a four-vector Q orthogonal to the total momentum (method Q). The kernels are inserted into the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and parameters are fitted to reproduce experimental phase shifts from the SAID program up to 3 GeV laboratory energy and low-energy scattering parameters from Dumbrajs (1983).

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts described for laboratory energy up to 3 GeV for uncoupled S and P partial waves
- For the ^1S_0^+ state, the MY3 model gives scattering length a_s = -23.750 fm and effective range r_0s = 2.70 fm; MYQ3 gives a_s = -23.754 fm and r_0s = 2.78 fm; experimental values are a_s = -23.748(10) fm and r_0s = 2.75(5) fm
- For the ^3P_0^+ state, the parameter s_0 = 3.8682 GeV^2 reproduces the sign change in phase shifts at the experimental kinetic energy where they cross zero
- For the ^1S_0^+ state, the parameter s_0 = 4.0279 GeV^2 is used
- The one-rank kernel works up to approximately 1 GeV laboratory energy except for the ^1P_1^+ partial wave
- CD-Bonn nonrelativistic model works up to approximately 0.66-0.75 GeV depending on the partial wave
- Graz II separable kernel works up to approximately 2 GeV for the ^1S_0^+ state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-rank separable kernel approach (Bondarenko 2008) by increasing the rank to two for P waves and three for the ^1S_0^+ state, enabling description up to 3 GeV rather than 1 GeV. It provides an alternative to nonrelativistic approaches like CD-Bonn (Machleidt 2000) and the SP07 partial-wave analysis (Arndt 2007), as well as other separable models like Graz II (Mathelitsch 1981). The constructed kernels are intended for future relativistic calculations of deuteron electrodisintegration far from threshold, where the choice of relativization method (p vs Q) becomes important for handling pole structures in off-shell calculations.
