# The Triton Charge Radius to Next-to-next-to-leading order in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> The triton point charge radius is calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory ($\mathrm{EFT}(\not{\!π})$), yielding a prediction of $1.14\pm0.19$ fm (leading order), $1.59\pm0.08$ fm (next-to leading order), and $1.62\pm0.03$ fm (NNLO) in agreement with the current experimental extraction of $1.5978\pm0.040$ fm [Angeli and Marinova, At. Data Nucl. Data Tables 99, 69 (2013)]. The error at NNLO is due to cutoff variation ($\sim$ 1%) within a reasonable range of calculated cutoffs and from a $\mathrm{EFT}(\not{\!π})$ error estimate ($\sim$ 1.5%). In addition new techniques are introduced to add perturbative corrections to bound and scattering state calculations for short range effective field theories, but with a focus on their use in $\mathrm{EFT}(\not{\!π})$

## Key claim

The triton point charge radius is calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory, yielding a prediction of 1.62 ± 0.03 fm that agrees with the experimental extraction of 1.5978 ± 0.040 fm, but the NNLO error estimate of approximately 2% includes both a 1.5% EFT error estimate and an additional ~1% from cutoff variation, which may indicate either slow divergence or convergence.

## Method

The calculation uses pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) with a power counting that expands interactions in contact terms ordered by (Q/Λ_π̸)^n where Λ_π̸ ~ m_π and Q ~ γ_t ≈ 45 MeV. The two-body sector is treated using dibaryon auxiliary fields in the Z-parametrization, with low-energy constants fit to the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ scattering lengths and effective ranges. For the three-body system, new techniques are introduced that allow perturbative corrections to bound-state calculations using a triton auxiliary field, enabling calculation of the triton vertex function and charge form factor to NNLO without the need for iterative numerical schemes or limiting procedures.

## Key numbers

- Triton point charge radius at LO: 1.14 ± 0.19 fm
- Triton point charge radius at NLO: 1.59 ± 0.08 fm
- Triton point charge radius at NNLO: 1.62 ± 0.03 fm
- Experimental triton point charge radius: 1.5978 ± 0.040 fm (Angeli and Marinova, 2013)
- Deuteron binding momentum γ_t = 45.7025 MeV
- ¹S₀ virtual bound-state momentum γ_s = -7.890 MeV
- Deuteron pole residue Z_t = 1.6908
- ¹S₀ pole residue Z_s = 0.9015
- Triton binding energy used for renormalization: -8.48 MeV (Wapstra, 1985)
- NNLO error estimate: ~1.5% from EFT power counting plus ~1% from cutoff variation, giving ~2% total

## Context

This work extends previous EFT(π̸) calculations of three-body observables such as nd scattering (Bedaque, 2002; Vanasse, 2013) and the triton-helium-3 energy difference (Ando, 2010; König, 2015) by introducing a new perturbative technique for bound-state calculations. The method builds on the trimer field approach of Hagen et al. (2013) for halo EFT but generalizes it to include perturbative corrections at NLO and NNLO. The results enable future calculations of other three-body electromagnetic observables in EFT(π̸) and provide a framework for treating bound-state corrections in short-range effective field theories more generally.
