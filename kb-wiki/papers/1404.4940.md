# The infrared limit of the Similarity Renormalization Group evolution and Levinson's theorem

> On a finite momentum grid with N integration points and weights the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) with a given generator G unitarily evolves an initial interaction with a cutoff on energy differences. This steadily drives the starting Hamiltonian in momentum space to a diagonal form in the infrared limit corresponding to a permutation of the eigenvalues and depends on G. Levinson's theorem establishes a relation between phase-shifts and the number of bound-states. We show that unitarily equivalent Hamiltonians on the grid generate reaction matrices which are compatible with Levinson's theorem but are phase-inequivalent along the SRG trajectory. An isospectral definition of the phase-shift in terms of an energy-shift is possible but requires in addition a proper ordering of states on a momentum grid in order to fulfill Levinson's theorem. We show how the SRG with different generators G induces different isospectral flows in the presence of bound-states, leading to distinct orderings in the infrared limit. While the Wilson generator induces an ascending ordering incompatible with Levinson's theorem, the Wegner generator provides a much better ordering, although not the optimal one. We illustrate the discussion with the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels.

# The infrared limit of the Similarity Renormalization Group evolution and Levinson's theorem

## Key claim

On a finite momentum grid, the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution with different generators drives the Hamiltonian to a diagonal form in the infrared limit (cutoff λ → 0) but induces different orderings of the eigenvalues; while the Wilson generator produces an ascending ordering incompatible with Levinson's theorem when bound states are present, the Wegner generator provides a much better ordering that allows isospectral phase-shifts to fulfill Levinson's theorem, although not the optimal one.

## Method

The authors solve the SRG flow equations numerically on a finite N-dimensional momentum grid with integration points p_n and weights w_n, using both the Wilson generator (G_s = T, the kinetic energy) and the Wegner generator (G_s = diag(H_s), the evolving diagonal part of the Hamiltonian). They apply these to a separable gaussian toy model potential that describes the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ partial-wave channels, and evaluate phase-shifts both through the standard Lippmann-Schwinger (LS) equation on the grid and through an energy-shift (eigenvalue) method that relates phase-shifts to differences between interacting and free eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum scale for bound-state emergence in the ³S₁ channel is estimated as Λ_c^LO ≈ 0.3 fm⁻¹ at leading order (using scattering length α₀ = 5.42 fm) and Λ_c^NLO = 0.29 fm⁻¹ at next-to-leading order (using effective range r₀ = 1.75 fm).
- The exact deuteron bound-state momentum scale is γ_d = 0.23 fm⁻¹.
- For a grid with N=20 points and UV cutoff Λ = 2 fm⁻¹, the Wegner generator places the deuteron bound-state eigenvalue at grid momentum p_n_BS → p₅ ≈ 0.254 fm⁻¹.
- The position of the deuteron bound-state that minimizes RMS phase-shift errors varies with grid size: for N=10 it is 0.135 fm⁻¹, for N=20 it is 0.254 fm⁻¹, for N=30 it is 0.232 fm⁻¹, for N=40 it is 0.221 fm⁻¹, for N=50 it is 0.215 fm⁻¹, and for N=100 it is 0.222 fm⁻¹.
- The SRG cutoff λ was varied in a range from 0.05 to 2.0 fm⁻¹ in the numerical calculations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the infrared limit of SRG evolution (Arriola 2013) and connects it to the eigenvalue method for scattering developed by Kukulin and collaborators (Kukulin 2009, Rubtsova 2010), which itself traces back to Lifshits' work on impurities in crystals. The paper also relates to the Lüscher formula (Lüscher 1985, 1990) used in lattice QCD for relating energy shifts to phase-shifts at large volumes. The results enable a framework for defining isospectral phase-shifts on finite momentum grids without solving scattering equations, and raise the open question of whether a specific SRG generator can be found that produces the optimal ordering of eigenvalues for fulfilling Levinson's theorem.
