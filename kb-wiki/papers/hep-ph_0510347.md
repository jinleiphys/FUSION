# Neutrino emission and cooling rates of spin-one color superconductors

> Neutrino emissivities due to direct Urca processes of several spin-one color-superconducting phases of dense quark matter are calculated. In particular, the role of anisotropies and nodes of the gap functions is analyzed. Results for the specific heat as well as for the cooling rates of the color-spin-locked, planar, polar, and {\it A} phases are presented and consequences for the physics of neutron stars are briefly discussed. Furthermore, it is shown that the {\em A} phase exhibits a helicity order, giving rise to a reflection asymmetry in the neutrino emissivity.

## Key claim

The neutrino emissivities and specific heats of spin-one color-superconducting quark matter phases are calculated, showing that the anisotropic gap structures and nodes in the polar, planar, and A phases produce qualitatively different cooling behavior compared to the isotropic CSL phase, though the results are strictly valid only at asymptotically high densities where perturbative QCD applies.

## Method

The neutrino emissivity from direct Urca processes (u + e- → d + ν and d → u + e- + ν̄) was computed using the Kadanoff-Baym formalism, with the W-boson polarization tensor evaluated from quark propagators in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for each spin-one color-superconducting phase. The imaginary part of the retarded polarization tensor was extracted after performing Matsubara sums, and the resulting expressions were simplified using the Fermi-liquid approximation with strong coupling corrections (κ = 2αs/(3π)) to open phase space for the Urca processes. The specific heat was calculated from the low-energy quasiparticle dispersion relations near the nodes of the gap functions.

## Key numbers

- CSL phase: isotropic gap with 8 gapped modes (λ = 2) and 4 ungapped modes (λ = 0)
- Polar phase: gap vanishes at south and north poles of Fermi sphere, with eigenvalue λ = sin²θk (8 modes) and 4 ungapped modes
- Planar phase: anisotropic but nonzero gap in all directions, with eigenvalue λ = 1 + cos²θk (8 modes) and 4 ungapped modes
- A phase: two gapped quasiparticle modes with eigenvalues λ = (1 + |cosθk|)² (4 modes) and λ = (1 - |cosθk|)² (4 modes), plus 4 ungapped modes; one gapped mode has two point nodes
- The angle θ0 for collinear Urca kinematics satisfies cosθ0 = 1 - κμe²/(μuμd), independent of neutrino energy pν

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino emission from color-superconducting quark matter beyond the previously studied spin-zero phases (gapless 2SC and CFL phases) to spin-one pairing channels, which are allowed for arbitrarily large Fermi momentum mismatches between quark flavors. The spin-one phases considered (CSL, planar, polar, and A) are analogues of phases in superfluid ³He, with the CSL and A phases corresponding to the B and A phases of ³He respectively. The results enable comparison of predicted cooling curves for neutron stars against observational data, potentially distinguishing which color-superconducting phase (if any) is realized in compact star interiors, and the A phase's helicity order predicts a reflection asymmetry in neutrino emission that could produce observable effects.
