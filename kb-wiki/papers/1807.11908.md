# Gradient resummation for nonlinear chiral transport: an insight from holography

> Nonlinear transport phenomena induced by chiral anomaly are explored within a 4D field theory defined holographically as $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-$AdS_5$. In presence of weak constant background electromagnetic fields, the constitutive relations for vector and axial currents, resummed to all orders in the gradients of charge densities, are encoded in nine momenta-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs). These TCFs are first calculated analytically up to third order in gradient expansion, and then evaluated numerically beyond the hydrodynamic limit. Fourier transformed, the TCFs become memory functions. The memory function of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) is found to differ dramatically from the instantaneous response form of the original CME. Beyond hydrodynamic limit and when external magnetic field is larger than some critical value, the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) is discovered to possess a discrete spectrum of non-dissipative modes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that all-order gradient resummation of nonlinear chiral transport, computed holographically, reveals that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) memory function differs dramatically from the instantaneous response assumed in the original CME, and that beyond the hydrodynamic limit with sufficiently large magnetic fields, the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) possesses a discrete spectrum of non-dissipative modes, a result that originates solely from the all-order resummation procedure.

## Method

The authors employ a holographic model of a 4D field theory defined as \(U(1)_V\times U(1)_A\) Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory in Schwarzschild-\(AdS_5\), with weak constant background electromagnetic fields. They linearize the charge densities and fields into constant backgrounds and space-time dependent fluctuations, then compute the constitutive relations for vector and axial currents at order \(\epsilon^1\alpha^1\) (first order in fluctuations, first order in background fields). The nine momentum-dependent transport coefficient functions (TCFs) are first calculated analytically up to third order in gradient expansion in the hydrodynamic limit (\(\omega,q\ll 1\)), then evaluated numerically beyond this limit, with the TCFs Fourier transformed into memory functions.

## Key numbers

* The CME TCF \(\sigma_{\bar{\chi}}\) in the hydrodynamic limit (with \(\pi T=1\)) expands as \(\sigma_{\bar{\chi}}=6+\frac{3}{2}i\omega (\pi + 2\log 2) -\frac{1}{8} \{\omega^2 [\pi^2+6(4\mathcal{C}+\log^22)]+q^2 (12\pi-24\log 2) \}+\cdots\), where \(\mathcal{C}\approx0.915966\) is Catalan's constant.
* The Hall diffusion TCF \(\mathcal{D}_H\) expands as \(\mathcal{D}_H=\kappa^2 \{72(3\log2-2)+i\omega6[\pi(2\pi +3\log2-6) +(9\log2-12)\log2]+\cdots\}\).
* The anomalous chiral Hall conductivity TCF \(\sigma_{a\chi H}\) expands as \(\sigma_{a\chi H}= \kappa \{6\log2 + i\omega \frac{1}{16} (48\mathcal{C}+5\pi^2)+\cdots\}\).
* The TCF \(\sigma_1\) is given by \(\sigma_1=162\kappa^2\bar{\mu}\bar{\mu}_5[6+\log2(5\log2-12)]+\cdots\).
* The TCF \(\sigma_2\) expands as \(\sigma_2=\frac{1}{8}(6\pi-\pi^2-12\log2)+108\kappa^2(\bar{\mu}^2+\bar{\mu}_5^2) [6 +\log2(5\log2-12)]+\cdots\).
* The TCF \(\sigma_3\) is \(\sigma_3= 9\kappa\bar{\mu} \log^22+\cdots\).
* None of the TCFs survive beyond asymptotically large \(\omega\gtrsim5\) (in units where \(\pi T=1\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Bu, Demircik, Lublinsky, 2016-2018) on all-order gradient resummation for chiral transport within the same holographic model, generalizing many fixed-order transport coefficients into full TCFs. The paper builds on the Müller-Israel-Stewart formulation of causal hydrodynamics (Müller, 1967; Israel and Stewart, 1976-1979) and prior work on nonlinear chiral transport from chiral kinetic theory (e.g., 2016-2018) and entropy current methods (e.g., 2011). The results enable the study of chiral magnetic waves beyond the hydrodynamic limit, including the discovery of non-dissipative discrete modes, and provide a framework for computing memory functions for anomaly-induced transport that respect causality.
