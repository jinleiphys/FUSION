# Reconciling hadronic and partonic analyticity in $b\to s\ell\ell$ transitions

> Rare $B$-meson decays mediated by $b\to s\ell\ell$ transitions constitute sensitive probes of physics beyond the Standard Model, and have triggered considerable interest due to hints for deviations from the Standard-Model prediction. To establish a discrepancy beyond a reasonable doubt, control over the nonlocal matrix elements involving charm loops is essential, which, for large spacelike virtualities, can be constrained by an operator product expansion with coefficients known at two-loop order. We observe that the analytic structure of this partonic calculation, whose understanding is important to put forward rigorous parameterizations, follows from simple triangle topologies and demonstrate explicitly how dispersion relations are fulfilled even in the case of anomalous thresholds. Crucially, these anomalous contributions match onto the ones expected when considering hadronic degrees of freedom, proving that the partonic calculation does not miss anomalous effects and justifying its use in regions of parameter space in which a perturbative description applies.

## Key claim

The partonic two-loop calculation of nonlocal form factors in $b\to s\ell\ell$ transitions does not miss anomalous threshold effects that arise in a hadronic description; the anomalous contributions in the partonic picture map directly onto those expected from hadronic degrees of freedom, justifying the use of the operator product expansion constraint in regions where perturbation theory applies.

## Method

The authors map each separately gauge-invariant class of two-loop partonic diagrams for $b\to s\gamma^*$ onto one-loop triangle topologies, identifying normal and anomalous thresholds from the triangle kinematics. They parameterize the discontinuities of the form factors using spectral functions expanded in conformal polynomials, fit the coefficients to the numerical two-loop results from Asatrian et al. (2019) as implemented in the EOS software, and verify that each diagram fulfills its own dispersion relation. The mapping between partonic and hadronic anomalous thresholds is demonstrated by comparing the trajectories of the anomalous branch point $s_+$ as a function of a parameter $\xi$ that encodes the kinematics of the left-hand cut.

## Key numbers

- For diagram (a), the anomalous branch point lies at $s_+ = m_b^2 - 4m_c^2 = 0.6$ (in units where $m_b=1$, $m_c^2/m_b^2=0.1$), with normal threshold $\sth = 4m_s^2 = 0$.
- For diagram (c), the anomalous threshold coincides with the pseudothreshold at $s_+ = m_b^2 = 1$, with normal threshold $\sth = 4m_c^2 = 0.4$.
- The partonic anomalous threshold for diagram (c) shifts to $s_+ = m_c(m_b^2 - m_s^2)/(m_c + m_s) < m_b^2$ when strange-quark mass is restored.
- For the hadronic $B\to K$ case, the physical point occurs at $\xi = 2.04$; for $B\to K^*$, at $\xi = 3.69$ and $\xi = 8.62$, using masses from the Particle Data Group (2024).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of anomalous thresholds in $b\to s\ell\ell$ transitions from Mutke et al. (2024), which studied the $u$-quark loop in a hadronic description via triangle topologies. It addresses concerns raised by Ciuchini et al. (2022) and Ladisa et al. (2022) about whether the partonic operator product expansion calculation from Asatrian et al. (2019) fully captures the analytic structure of nonlocal form factors. The results enable combined analyses that use both experimental data in the timelike region and perturbative constraints for large spacelike virtualities, supporting ongoing dispersive approaches by Gubernari et al. (2020, 2022, 2023) and Gopal et al. (2024).
