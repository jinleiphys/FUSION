# Effect of an electromagnetic field on the spectra and elliptic flow of particles

> In 2+1 dimensions, the evolution of flow under the influence of an external electromagnetic field is simulated. The external electromagnetic field is exponentially decaying with time. Under the same initial conditions, flow evolution with and without the external electromagnetic field is compared. It was found that the production of particles was enhanced when the external electromagnetic field was present. As the strength of the electromagnetic field increased, more particles were produced. Enhancement is greater at the high transverse momentum than at the low transverse momentum. In addition, the electromagnetic field also modifies the elliptic flow and the modification is related to the mass and charge of the particles.

# Effect of an electromagnetic field on the spectra and elliptic flow of particles

## Key claim
In (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with an exponentially decaying external electromagnetic field, particle production is enhanced and elliptic flow is modified, but the effects are modest because the electromagnetic field weakens rapidly over time; the non-equilibrium correction to the distribution function is the dominating factor influencing particle production, and the modification to elliptic flow depends on the mass and charge of the particles.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with an external electromagnetic field that decays exponentially in time and has a Gaussian spatial profile. The electromagnetic field was coupled to the fluid via the energy-momentum conservation equations including the Lorentz force term, and the distribution function was corrected using a relaxation-time approximation to the Boltzmann equation. Particle spectra were computed using the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription, and elliptic flow coefficients were extracted from Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal particle distributions.

## Key numbers
- Initial magnetic field strength: \( eB_0 = 0.08\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \simeq (2m_\pi)^2 \)
- Spatial width of magnetic field: \( \sigma_r = 2.0\ \mathrm{fm} \)
- Relaxation time: \( \tau_{rel} = 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}/c \)
- Initial energy density at RHIC: \( \varepsilon_0 = 55\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^3 \)
- Initial energy density at LHC: \( \varepsilon_0 = 166\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^3 \)
- Initial equilibration time: \( \tau_0 = 0.4\ \mathrm{fm} \)
- Freeze-out temperature: \( T_F = 130\ \mathrm{MeV} \)
- For pion spectra, particle production from ideal hydro without electromagnetic field is about 10% lower than with electromagnetic field when transverse momentum \( p_T > 2.5\ \mathrm{GeV} \)
- Deviations between numerical results and experimental data are within 15% over the full \( p_T \) range, regardless of whether the electromagnetic field is present

## Context
This work extends ideal (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions, such as those reviewed by Heinz (2005) and Shen (2014), by incorporating an external electromagnetic field that had been neglected in most previous hydrodynamic simulations. The electromagnetic field parameters are based on estimates from Deng (2012) and Bzdak (2011), and the non-equilibrium correction method follows Greif (2014). The results show that including the electromagnetic field improves agreement with ALICE data for transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and photons, and that photon elliptic flow is more sensitive to electromagnetic effects than hadron elliptic flow, suggesting that photons may serve as probes of early-stage electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions.
