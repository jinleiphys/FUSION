# Phase space of electron- and muon-neutrino and antineutrino scattering off nuclei

> We discuss the electron and muon neutrino and antineutrino double differential cross sections on carbon in the quasielastic as well as in the multinucleon and one pion production channels. By projecting them in the transferred momentum - transferred energy plane and in the neutrino energy - lepton scattering angle plane, as well as by performing simple considerations on the position of the quasielastic and Delta peaks and on their broadening, we explain the surprising dominance of the muon neutrino and antineutrino cross sections over the electron ones in particular kinematical conditions.

# Phase space of electron- and muon-neutrino and antineutrino scattering off nuclei

## Key claim
The muon neutrino and antineutrino quasielastic differential cross sections can dominate over the corresponding electron ones at forward scattering angles and low neutrino energies, contrary to the expectation from total cross sections. This inversion occurs specifically when the quasielastic peak for muon (anti)neutrinos lies at a larger transferred energy than for electron (anti)neutrinos, causing the muon cross section to "fall after" the electron one and sample a larger tail of the nuclear response.

## Method
The authors computed double differential cross sections for charged-current quasielastic, multinucleon (np-nh), and one-pion production (incoherent and coherent) channels on carbon using a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) approach. They projected these cross sections onto the (momentum transfer q, energy transfer ω) plane and the (neutrino energy Eν, lepton scattering angle θ) plane for fixed neutrino energies of 175 MeV and 575 MeV, comparing the phase space boundaries and response regions for electron and muon flavors.

## Key numbers
- At Eν = 175 MeV and cosθ = 0.98, the quasielastic peak position ωQE for electron (anti)neutrinos is 0.65 MeV, while for muon (anti)neutrinos it is 1.24 MeV, leading to larger muon cross sections.
- At Eν = 175 MeV and cosθ = 0, ωQE for electron (anti)neutrinos is 27.49 MeV, larger than the muon value of 22.48 MeV, giving the expected electron dominance.
- The Δ-resonance line parameter ΔM = (MΔ² − MN²)/(2MN) = 338 MeV defines the peak position for one-pion production.
- Cross sections are shown for neutrino energies of 175 MeV and 575 MeV, with strength in units of 10⁻³⁸ cm²/GeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier finding by Martini (2016) that muon neutrino quasielastic cross sections can exceed electron ones at forward angles, a result confirmed by Nikolakopoulos (2019) and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2019) using mean-field models. It contradicts the spectral function approach of Ankowski (2017), which found no such inversion when final-state interactions were omitted. The analysis provides a simple kinematic criterion based on the quasielastic peak position, enabling experimental cuts to avoid regions where cross-section modeling is uncertain, and suggests that (q, ω) representations could help constrain unmeasured electron (anti)neutrino cross sections from measured muon ones.
