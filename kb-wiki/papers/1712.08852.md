# On the moment of inertia of PSR J0348+0432

> The moment of inertia of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory by choosing suitable hyperon coupling constants. By this method, we find that the suggested radius of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range $R=12.957\sim12.246$ km by the observation $M$=1.97$\sim$2.05 M$_\odot$. We also find that the suggested moment of inertia $I$ of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range $I$=1.9073$\times$10$^{45}\sim$1.5940$\times10^{45}$ g.cm$^{2}$ by the observation $M$=1.97$\sim$2.05 M$_\odot$. Massive pulsars hint that the interaction inside them should be very "strong". Though hyperons considered will reduce the maximum mass, but in principle we may have models predicting maximum masses higher than 2 M${_\odot}$ by choosing suitable parameters, in a degree of freedom of hadron. Our calculations have proved the above and perfectly agree with the results both of Aaron W et al and Pétri J et al.

# On the moment of inertia of PSR J0348+0432

## Key claim
Using relativistic mean field theory with hyperon coupling constants chosen to break SU(6) symmetry and fit hyperon well depths, the moment of inertia of PSR J0348+0432 is predicted to lie in a specific range, but only two out of 25 tested parameter sets (NO.24 and NO.25) can reproduce the observed mass above 2 solar masses, and the quoted results rely specifically on parameter set NO.25.

## Method
The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the GL85 nucleon coupling constants was applied to compute the structure of neutron stars containing hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi). Hyperon-meson coupling constants were determined by fitting to experimental hyperon well depths in nuclear matter (U_Lambda = -30 MeV, U_Sigma = +40 MeV, U_Xi = -28 MeV), yielding 25 viable parameter combinations. Only parameter set NO.25 (x_sigma_Lambda=0.8, x_omega_Lambda=0.9319; x_sigma_Sigma=0.4, x_omega_Sigma=0.825; x_sigma_Xi=0.8, x_omega_Xi=0.945) produced a maximum mass (2.0572 solar masses) sufficient to describe PSR J0348+0432 and was used for the moment of inertia calculation.

## Key numbers
- Radius of PSR J0348+0432: R = 12.957 km at M = 1.97 solar masses, and R = 12.246 km at M = 2.05 solar masses (range R = 12.957 to 12.246 km)
- Moment of inertia: I = 1.9073 x 10^45 g.cm^2 at M = 1.97 solar masses, and I = 1.5940 x 10^45 g.cm^2 at M = 2.05 solar masses (range I = 1.9073 x 10^45 to 1.5940 x 10^45 g.cm^2)
- Central energy density: epsilon_c = 1.2601 x 10^15 g.cm^-3 at M = 1.97 solar masses, and epsilon_c = 1.7774 x 10^15 g.cm^-3 at M = 2.05 solar masses
- Maximum mass for parameter set NO.25: M_max = 2.0572 solar masses; for NO.24: M_max = 2.0132 solar masses
- Under SU(6) symmetry, the maximum mass was only 1.3374 solar masses, far below the observed mass

## Context
This work extends prior studies of neutron star moments of inertia by applying RMF theory specifically to the massive pulsar PSR J0348+0432, which was observed by Antoniadis (2013) with mass 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses. The results agree with the moment of inertia ranges from Aaron W (2008) for 1.338 solar mass neutron stars (I = 1.3 to 1.63 x 10^45 g.cm^2) and from Petri J (2011) for 2 to 2.2 solar mass neutron stars (I = 1 to 3 x 10^45 g.cm^2). The calculation demonstrates that hyperons reduce maximum mass (as shown by Glendenning 1985), but suitable parameter choices can still yield masses above 2 solar masses by breaking SU(6) symmetry. The results enable future constraints on the equation of state from potential moment of inertia measurements of PSR J0348+0432.
