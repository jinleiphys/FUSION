# Nuclear effects in photoproduction of heavy quarks and vector mesons in ultraperipheral PbPb and pPb collisions at the LHC

> The comparison of photoproduction cross sections for $c\bar{c}$ and b-b(bar) in PbPb and pPb collisions can give sensitivity to nuclear shadowing effects. The photoproduction of vector mesons is even more sensitive to the underlying gluon distributions. In this study we present the cross sections and rapidity dependence of the photoproduction of heavy quarks and exclusive production of vector mesons in ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV and sqrt(s_NN)=2.76$ TeV, respectively. The potentials of using these processes for constraining nuclear gluon shadowing are explored. It is found that photoproduction of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ in PbPb collisions in particular exhibit very good sensitivity to gluon shadowing.

## Key claim

The paper argues that comparing photoproduction cross sections of heavy quarks and vector mesons in PbPb and pPb collisions can provide sensitivity to nuclear gluon shadowing, but this sensitivity is conditional: it is weak for heavy-quark production in pPb collisions and for b-bbar production generally, and only becomes strong for J/psi and Upsilon production in PbPb collisions due to the quadratic dependence on the gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors calculate cross sections and rapidity distributions for inclusive photoproduction of c-cbar and b-bbar, and exclusive elastic photoproduction of J/psi and Upsilon(1s), in ultraperipheral pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They use the strong electromagnetic fields from relativistic heavy ions as photon sources, and compute results with multiple nuclear parton distribution function sets (MSTW08, EPS08, EPS09, HKN07) and photon parton distribution sets (GRV, SaS1D, CJK2) to assess sensitivity to gluon modifications.

## Key numbers

- For pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.0 TeV, the direct photoproduction cross section for c-cbar using MSTW08 is 3250 microbarns for the gamma-p component and 362 microbarns for the gamma-Pb component
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, the direct photoproduction cross section for c-cbar using MSTW08 is 570.1 mb, while using EPS08 it is 469.8 mb and using EPS09 it is 511.1 mb
- For exclusive J/psi photoproduction in PbPb collisions, the total cross section is 32.6 mb using MSTW08, 6.3 mb using EPS08, 13.9 mb using EPS09, and 22.1 mb using HKN07
- For exclusive Upsilon(1s) photoproduction in PbPb collisions, the total cross section is 51.3 microbarns using MSTW08, 32.2 microbarns using EPS08, 39.0 microbarns using EPS09, and 41.3 microbarns using HKN07
- For pPb collisions, the gamma-Pb component of J/psi production is 18.3 microbarns using MSTW08 but only 1.8 microbarns using EPS08, demonstrating strong shadowing sensitivity
- The rapidity shift between laboratory and center-of-mass frames for pPb collisions is delta y = 0.5 ln(208/82) = 0.47

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Adeluyi 2011, Adeluyi 2012) to the specific LHC energies of sqrt(s_NN)=5 TeV for pPb and sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV for PbPb, rather than the higher energies considered earlier. The idea of using photoproduction to constrain parton distributions was originally proposed by Goncalves (2001). The results show that J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction in PbPb collisions can discriminate between different nuclear gluon shadowing parameterizations (EPS08, EPS09, HKN07), while c-cbar production in pPb collisions could serve as a normalizer for PbPb measurements. The paper enables experimental comparisons by ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb, which all have capability to measure these vector mesons in the muon channel.
