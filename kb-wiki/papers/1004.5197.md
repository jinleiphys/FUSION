# Nuclear symmetry energy and core-crust transition in neutron stars: a critical study

> The slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density $L$ is pointed out as a crucial quantity to determine the mass and width of neutron-star crusts. This letter clarifies the relation between $L$ and the core-crust transition. We confirm that the transition density is soundly correlated with $L$ despite differences between models, and we propose a clear understanding of this correlation based on a generalised liquid drop model. Using a large number of nuclear models, we evaluate the dispersion affecting the correlation between the transition pressure $P_t$ and $L$. From a detailed analysis it is shown that this correlation is weak due to a cancellation between different terms. The correlation between the isovector coefficients $K_{\rm sym}$ and $L$ plays a crucial role in this discussion.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the transition density ρ_t is strongly correlated with the symmetry energy slope L, but demonstrates that the correlation between the transition pressure P_t and L is weak and model-dependent due to a cancellation between competing contributions, contrary to earlier claims that a precise determination of L would give a tight indication of P_t.

## Method

The authors used a large set of non-relativistic Skyrme-type interactions (21 models) and relativistic effective nuclear models (non-linear Walecka models and density-dependent coupling models), plus one microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation, to compute the core-crust transition point in neutron stars under β-equilibrium. The transition density, proton fraction, and pressure were determined at the crossing point between the β-equilibrium equation of state and the thermodynamic spinodal, and the results were analyzed within a generalized liquid-drop model (GLDM) framework to decompose the variation of P_t with L into contributions from the transition position and from the isovector coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The variation of transition density with L: δρ_t/δL = (−3.84 ± 0.24) × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹ fm⁻³
- The variation of transition asymmetry with L: δy_t/δL = (6.08 ± 0.82) × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹
- The variation of K_sym with L: δK_sym/δL = 3.07 ± 0.33
- The range of transition pressure P_t obtained across all models lies within 0.20 < P_t < 0.65 MeV/fm³ (citing Pethick 1995)
- The spinodal density in symmetric matter ρ_s is approximately (2/3)ρ₀ for all models considered

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Iida (2007), Xu (2009), and Vidana (2009) that had claimed a strong correlation between L and the core-crust transition properties, and it directly addresses the model-dependence issue raised in the pasta-phase literature. The paper shows that while the correlation between L and ρ_t is robust and can be understood through the curvature of neutron matter, the correlation between L and P_t is destroyed by a cancellation between the positive contribution from L itself and negative contributions from the decreasing ρ_t and the L-K_sym correlation. This result implies that experimental determination of L alone is insufficient to constrain the crust mass and moment of inertia, and that further constraints on the isoscalar equation of state and on the isovector coefficients at subsaturation densities are needed.
