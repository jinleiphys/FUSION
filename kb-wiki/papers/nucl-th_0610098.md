# Nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation function for light nuclei

> Nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation function have been presented for nuclear reactions with neutron-rich or proton-rich projectiles using a nuclear transport theory, namely Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. The relationship between the binding energy of projectiles and the strength of proton-neutron correlation function at small relative momentum has been explored, while proton-proton correlation function shows its sensitivity to the proton density distribution. Those results show that nucleon-nucleon correlation function is useful to reflect some features of the neutron- or proton-halo nuclei and therefore provide a potential tool for the studies of radioactive beam physics.

## Key claim

Nucleon-nucleon momentum correlation functions calculated with the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) model can reflect features of neutron- or proton-halo nuclei, but the relationship between correlation strength and nuclear structure is only demonstrated for specific light isotopes and depends on the assumed density distribution in the model initialization.

## Method

The authors used the IDQMD transport model to generate phase-space information for emitted nucleons from nuclear reactions, then constructed two-particle correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formalism, which convolves the emission function with the relative two-particle wave function. They simulated reactions of neutron-rich projectiles (e.g., 11Li fragmentation into 9Li + 2n at 28 MeV/u) and proton-rich projectiles (S isotopes) on a 12C target at 800 MeV/u in head-on collisions, extracting correlation strengths at fixed relative momenta.

## Key numbers

- Two-halo neutron correlation function from 11Li fragmentation into 9Li + 2n at 28 MeV/u reproduced experimental data from Ieki (1993), while core neutron-neutron correlation function did not match.
- Proton-neutron correlation strength (C_PN) was extracted at relative momentum q = 5 MeV/c and showed a rising trend with increasing binding energy per nucleon for Li, C, and N isotopes.
- Proton-proton correlation strength (C_PP) was extracted at q = 20 MeV/c for 27,28,29,30S isotopes; C_PP decreased with two-proton separation energy for normal density distributions, and decreased dramatically when a proton-halo density distribution was assumed for 27S.

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental observations of weakened neutron-neutron correlation functions in halo-nuclei induced systems (Ieki, 1993; Marques, 1996) and the Koonin-Pratt formalism (Pratt, 1984) for constructing correlation functions from space-time information. The IDQMD model builds on the QMD approach (Aichelin, 1991) by adding isospin-dependent mean field, two-body collisions, and Pauli blocking (Ma, 2001; Wei, 2004). The results suggest that nucleon-nucleon correlation functions could serve as a tool to diagnose halo structures in radioactive beam physics, but the approach requires further validation across a wider range of nuclei and reactions.
