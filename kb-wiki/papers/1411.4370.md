# Nuclear shell effect and collinear tripartition of nuclei

> A possibility of formation of the three reaction products having comparable masses at the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf is theoretically explored. This work is aimed to study the mechanism leading to observation of the reaction products with masses $M_1=$136---140 and $M_2=$68---72 in coincidence by the FOBOS group in JINR. The same type of ternary fission decay has been observed in the reaction $^{235}$U(n$_{\rm th}$,fff). The potential energy surface for the ternary system forming a collinear nuclear chain is calculated for the wide range of mass and charge numbers of constituent nuclei. The results of the PES for the tripartition of $^{252}$Cf(sf,fff) shows, that we have favorable dynamical conditions for the formation of fragments with mass combinations of clusters $^{68-70}$Ni with $^{130-132}$Sn and with missing cluster $^{48-52}$Ca.

# Nuclear shell effect and collinear tripartition of nuclei

## Key claim
The potential energy surface (PES) calculations for the spontaneous fission of 252Cf show favorable dynamical conditions for the formation of fragments with mass combinations of 68-70Ni with 130-132Sn and with a missing cluster of 48-52Ca, but only if the sequential fission mechanism proceeds with the heavy tin-like nucleus separating first due to its lower potential barrier, while the light super-deformed residual nucleus undergoes fission under the influence of the Coulomb field of the already-separated heavy fragment.

## Method
The authors calculated the potential energy surface (PES) for a collinear ternary system as a sum of the Q-value for ternary fission and the nucleus-nucleus interactions (Coulomb plus nuclear) between neighboring fragments, using the double folding procedure with effective nucleon-nucleon forces depending on nucleon distribution density. The yield of fission products was calculated using a statistical method based on driving potentials for the fissionable system, assuming a sequential mechanism where the first neck rupture separates the heavy fragment (tin-like nucleus) and the second rupture occurs while that fragment is still nearby, reducing the fission barrier of the light super-deformed residual nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The probability of the CCT channel 82Ge* + (154Nd* → 72Ni* + 82Ge*) is 3 × 10−4 for 236U*.
- The probability of the CCT channel 82Ge* + (154Nd* → 76Zn* + 78Zn*) is 1.5 × 10−4 for 236U*.
- The probability of the CCT channel 86Se* + (150Ce* → 66Fe* + 82Ge*) is 1.0 × 10−5 for 236U*.
- The probability of the CCT channel 86Se* + (150Ce* → 72Ni* + 76Zn*) is 1.4 × 10−5 for 236U*.
- The fission barrier Bf for the light strongly deformed fragment formed after separation of the tin-like nucleus is greater than 20 MeV.
- The minimum energy on the PES for the configuration 132Sn+72Ni+48Ca is higher by 15 MeV than that for the configuration 132Sn+50Ca+70Ni.
- The ternary fission probability by emission of an alpha particle relative to binary fission is about 2 × 10−3 for reactions from 229Th(nth,f) up to 251Cf(nth,f).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of collinear cluster tripartition (CCT) observed experimentally by the FOBOS group at JINR (Pyatkov 2010, Pyatkov 2012) for spontaneous fission of 252Cf and neutron-induced fission of 235U(nth,f). The authors build on earlier sequential fission calculations (Tashkhodjaev 2011) and the prediction that collinear configurations are preferred over oblate configurations for heavy ternary systems (Manim). The results enable interpretation of the observed coincidences of 68,70Ni isotopes with fragments of mass A ≈ 132-136, and explain why configurations with Ni as the middle cluster (rather than Ca) are much less probable, consistent with the small experimental yields of those channels.
