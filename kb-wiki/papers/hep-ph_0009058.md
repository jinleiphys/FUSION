# The Phases of QCD in Heavy Ion Collisions and Compact Stars

> I review arguments for the existence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential. I describe how heavy ion collision experiments at the SPS and RHIC can discover the tell-tale signatures of such a critical point, thus mapping this region of the QCD phase diagram. I then review the phenomena expected in cold dense quark matter: color superconductivity and color-flavor locking. I close with a snapshot of ongoing explorations of the implications of recent developments in our understanding of cold dense quark matter for the physics of compact stars.

# The Phases of QCD in Heavy Ion Collisions and Compact Stars

## Key claim

The QCD phase diagram as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential likely contains a critical point E where the first-order chiral phase transition line ends, and this critical point can be discovered through nonmonotonic signatures in heavy ion collisions as the collision energy is varied, though theorists cannot predict its location even to within a factor of two.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical arguments from universality classes, model calculations (including Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type models), and lattice QCD simulations to construct the QCD phase diagram for different quark mass scenarios. For the critical point search, the author analyzes event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse momentum and multiplicity in heavy ion collisions, and uses the theory of dynamical critical phenomena to estimate the maximum correlation length achievable given critical slowing down and the cooling rate of the fireball. For cold dense quark matter, the analysis employs the BCS-like pairing mechanism at the Fermi surface to describe color superconducting phases.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature at zero baryon density from lattice simulations is T_c ~ 140-190 MeV
- AGS collisions at sqrt(s) = 5 AGeV freeze out near mu ~ 500-600 MeV (baryon chemical potential)
- SPS collisions at sqrt(s) = 17 AGeV freeze out near mu ~ 200-300 MeV (baryon chemical potential)
- The estimated correlation length at freezeout near the critical point is xi_freezeout ~ 2/T_E ~ 3 fm, with T_E taken to be ~ 140 MeV
- The cooling rate of the fireball is estimated to be |dT/dt| = 4 MeV/fm
- The maximum correlation length scales with cooling rate as a^0.215, where a is proportional to |dT/dt|^{-1}
- NA49 measured sqrt(F) = 1.002 +/- 0.002 for the ratio of true to mixed-event width of mean p_T distributions
- Near the critical point, sqrt(F) is predicted to increase by ~ 3-5% for xi_freezeout ~ 3 fm
- Neutron star cores likely have quark chemical potential mu ~ 400-500 MeV (corresponding to baryon chemical potential ~ 1.2-1.5 GeV)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the QCD phase diagram by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) on the O(4) universality class of the chiral transition, and by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998, 1999) on the critical point and its experimental signatures. The analysis of critical slowing down builds on the theory of dynamical critical phenomena by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) and on Zurek's (1985) scaling law for defect formation at second-order phase transitions. The color-flavor locking phase was developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and by Schafer and Wilczek (1999). The paper enables experimental searches for the critical point at SPS and RHIC, and connects cold dense quark matter physics to neutron star phenomenology through the work of Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000) on the unlocking transition between 2SC and CFL phases.
