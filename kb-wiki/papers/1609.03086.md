# Three-body systems in pionless effective field theory

> Investigations of three-body nuclear systems using pionless effective field theory ($\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$) are reviewed. The history of $\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$ in $nd$ and $pd$ scattering is briefly discussed and emphasis put on the use of strict perturbative techniques. In addition renormalization issues appearing in $pd$ scattering are also presented. Bound state calculations are addressed and new perturbative techniques for describing them are highlighted. Three-body breakup observables in $nd$ scattering are also considered and the utility of $\mathrm{EFT}_{\notπ}$ for addressing them.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory (EFT_π̸) provides a systematic framework for three-body nuclear systems, but the power counting requires non-perturbative resummation at leading order for energies above the deuteron binding energy (2.22 MeV), and a three-body force is mandatory in the doublet S-wave channel for proper renormalization, with an energy-dependent three-body force first appearing at N²LO.

## Method

The paper reviews momentum-space calculations of neutron-deuteron (nd) and proton-deuteron (pd) scattering using the Faddeev integral equation formalism with dibaryon auxiliary fields. The two-body parameters are fitted using the Z-parametrization, which reproduces the deuteron bound state pole in the ³S₁ channel and the virtual bound state pole in the ¹S₀ channel. Higher-order corrections are treated either through a partial resummation technique or a strictly perturbative technique that avoids calculating the full off-shell scattering amplitude by moving effective range insertions into the inhomogeneous term of the integral equation.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: E_d^B = 2.22 MeV (the energy scale below which pions are not dynamical)
- Deuteron binding momentum: γ_t = 45.7025 MeV (used in Z-parametrization for ³S₁ channel)
- ¹S₀ virtual bound state pole binding momentum: γ_s = -7.890 MeV
- Residue about deuteron pole: Z_t = 1.6908
- Residue about ¹S₀ virtual bound state pole: Z_s = 0.9015
- LO deuteron wavefunction renormalization: Z_LO = 2γ_t/M_N
- LO three-body force Lagrangian coefficient: H_0(Λ) with regulator cutoff Λ

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998-2000) who established the need for a LO three-body force in the doublet S-wave channel, and of Bedaque and Grießhammer (1999) who calculated NLO corrections in the quartet channel. The paper contrasts the partial resummation technique of Bedaque et al. (2002) with the strictly perturbative approach of Vanasse (2013, 2015), which avoids the unphysical negative imaginary parts in NLO phase shifts above breakup threshold. The work enables systematic higher-order calculations of nd scattering, bound state properties like the triton charge radius, and three-body breakup observables, while also addressing renormalization issues in pd scattering that require a new isospin-dependent counterterm at NLO.
