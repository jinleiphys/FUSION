# Collective modes of asymmetric nuclear matter in Quantum HadroDynamics

> We discuss a fully relativistic Landau Fermi liquid theory based on the Quantum Hadro-Dynamics ($QHD$) effective field picture of Nuclear Matter ({\it NM}). From the linearized kinetic equations we get the dispersion relations of the propagating collective modes. We focus our attention on the dynamical effects of the interplay between scalar and vector channel contributions. A beautiful ``mirror'' structure in the form of the dynamical response in the isoscalar/isovector degree of freedom is revealed, with a complete parallelism in the role respectively played by the compressibility and the symmetry energy. All that strongly supports the introduction of an explicit coupling to the scalar-isovector channel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. In particular we study the influence of this coupling (to a $δ$-meson-like effective field) on the collective response of asymmetric nuclear matter ($ANM$). Interesting contributions are found on the propagation of isovector-like modes at normal density and on an expected smooth transition to isoscalar-like oscillations at high baryon density. Important ``chemical'' effects on the neutron-proton structure of the mode are shown. For dilute $ANM$ we have the isospin distillation mechanism of the unstable isoscalar-like oscillations, while at high baryon density we predict an almost pure neutron wave structure of the propagating sounds.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the inclusion of a scalar-isovector (δ-meson-like) coupling in Quantum HadroDynamics (QHD) significantly modifies the collective response of asymmetric nuclear matter, even when the symmetry energy at saturation density is held fixed at the same value. The authors show that the dynamical restoring force for isovector modes depends not only on the symmetry energy but also on the individual strengths of the ρ and δ coupling constants, leading to different sound velocities for interactions that yield the same a₄ parameter.

## Method

The authors develop a fully relativistic Landau Fermi liquid theory from the QHD effective field model, linearizing the kinetic equations for the Wigner function to obtain dispersion relations for collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter. They work within a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approximation (Hartree level) with non-linear σ self-interactions, including coupling to σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons, and solve the resulting homogeneous equations for longitudinal waves to determine sound velocities as functions of baryon density and asymmetry parameter α.

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, nucleon effective mass M* = 0.7 M_N (M_N = 939 MeV), incompressibility K_V = 240 MeV at ρ₀
- Symmetry energy parameter a₄ = 32 MeV used in calculations
- For Set I (no δ): f_ρ = 1.0 fm², f_δ = 0.00 fm²; for Set II (with δ): f_ρ = 2.8 fm², f_δ = 2.0 fm²
- The reduction in effective symmetry energy ΔE*_sym ∼ 4 MeV at ρ₀ when f_δ ∼ 2.0 fm², accounting for decreased sound velocity
- NL3 parametrization: ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³, M* = 0.6 M_N, K_V = 271.8 MeV, a₄ = 37.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends relativistic linear response theory for nuclear matter beyond previous RMF studies (Serot and Walecka, 1986; 1997) by explicitly including the scalar-isovector δ-meson channel, which was not considered in usual dynamical studies. The authors build on earlier relativistic Landau theory approaches (Matsui, 1981; Song, 2001; Cai, 2001) and non-relativistic studies of asymmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme-like interactions (Larionov, 1998). The results provide guidelines for predicting volume collective modes in finite β-unstable nuclei and support the introduction of both scalar and vector isovector meson fields on equal footing with their isoscalar counterparts, as suggested by static property studies (Liu, 2002).
