# Hidden Local Symmetry and the Vector Manifestation of Chiral Symmetry in Hot and/or Dense Matter

> The emergence and importance of hidden local symmetry in the structure of hadrons under extreme conditions is discussed. The topics covered are the potentially important role played by an infinite tower of vector mesons encoded in holographic dual QCD (or AdS/QCD) in chiral dynamics of mesons and baryons, in the vector dominance and its violation in EW response functions and the presence of the vector manifestation fixed point and its influence on the properties of hadrons in hot temperature (i.e., in relativistic heavy ion collisions) and in dense matter (i.e., in compact stars). Also discussed are the natural emergence of instantons/skyrmions from the infinite tower of vector mesons coupled to pions in AdS/QCD and their (speculated) role in chiral restoration at high density.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hidden local symmetry (HLS) is essential for a consistent effective field theory that allows vector meson masses to drop to the pion mass at the chiral restoration phase transition, but this "vector manifestation" fixed point may be difficult to observe experimentally because mundane hadronic processes mask the chiral symmetry signals in dilepton measurements.

## Method

The author reviews the Harada-Yamawaki HLS theory (HLS$_1$) which introduces vector mesons as hidden gauge fields alongside pions, with renormalization group equations that exhibit a fixed point at $g^*=0$, $a^*=1$ where the quark condensate vanishes. The paper also discusses the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD approach (HLS$_\infty$) where an infinite tower of vector mesons emerges from AdS/CFT duality, with baryons appearing as instantons in five dimensions that reduce to skyrmions in four dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The proton mass is approximately 1000 MeV, while the three light quarks have a total mass of a few MeV, indicating that approximately 98% of the proton mass comes from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking
- The scaling parameter $\Phi(n_0) \approx 0.78$ at nuclear matter density, extracted from the Landau parameter $\tilde{F}_1$ via the Migdal formula, implying $m_\omega(n_0)/m_\omega \approx 0.78$
- The ratio $f_\pi(n_0)/f_\pi(0) \approx 0.80$ found in deeply bound pionic atoms, comparable to $\sqrt{\langle\bar{q}q\rangle(n_0)/\langle\bar{q}q\rangle}$
- The instanton baryon calculation predicts $g_A \approx 0.69 \frac{N_c}{3} + \mathcal{O}(N_c^0)$ and $\mu_{an} \approx 2.16 \frac{m_N}{M_{KK}} \frac{N_c}{3} + \mathcal{O}(N_c^0)$, giving a ratio $R = g_A/\mu_{an} \approx 0.32$ compared to the experimental ratio 0.34
- With the $N_c \rightarrow N_c+2$ correction for $N_c=3$, the predictions become $g_A \approx 1.15$ and $\mu_{an} \approx 3.6$, close to experimental values $g_A=1.26$ and $\mu_{an}=3.7$
- The "flash temperature" above which chiral restoration effects become operative is approximately 120 MeV, and the "flash density" is approximately $2 n_0$

## Context

This review extends the Brown-Rho scaling framework (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry/vector manifestation theory (Harada and Yamawaki, 2001) to hot and dense matter relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions and compact stars. The paper contrasts the HLS approach with alternative models such as the parity-doublet linear sigma model that can admit non-zero fermion masses at chiral restoration. The work enables future theoretical development of a complete spectral function incorporating intrinsic background dependence, vector dominance violation, and many-body correlations, while cautioning that current dilepton data analyzed by van Hees and Rapp (2006) cannot falsify the HLS/VM scenario because chiral signals are masked by mundane hadronic processes.
