# Low-$x$ Physics in $pA$ Collisions and at the EIC

> In this proceeding, I review what we have learned, and what we will be able to learn, about low-$x$ physics from proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and how this complements future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) measurements. EIC not only will provide us essential and complementary information about the gluon saturation to the knowledge that we have learned from $pA$ collisions at RHIC and the LHC, it will also allow us to visualize the internal structure of protons and nuclei in a multi-dimensional fashion with unprecedented precision.

## Key claim

Gluon saturation physics, described by the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, can be tested through complementary measurements in proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC and at the future Electron-Ion Collider, but the evidence remains indirect and the two distinct unintegrated gluon distributions (dipole and Weizsacker-Williams) are not universal but process-dependent.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical status of low-x physics by examining the operator definitions of unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) in QCD, showing that gauge invariance leads to exactly two topologically distinct UGDs depending on the choice of future/past gauge links. It then compares leading-order and next-to-leading order calculations of forward single inclusive hadron production and dihadron production in pA collisions against RHIC and LHC data, and discusses how these complement planned measurements at the EIC, particularly dijet production in deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is parameterized as \(Q_s^2 = (x_0/x)^\lambda\) GeV\(^2\) with \(x_0 = 3.04 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(\lambda = 0.288\), describing HERA data for \(x < 10^{-2}\) and \(Q^2 < 450\) GeV\(^2\)
- The saturation momentum for a nucleus scales roughly as \(A^{1/3}\) times that for a proton
- The NLO calculation (SOLO package) breaks down for \(p_\perp > (2 \sim 3) Q_s(x)\), where the saturation formalism is no longer valid
- The adjoint representation saturation momentum is \(Q_{sg}^2 = \frac{2N_c^2}{N_c^2-1} Q_s^2\)

## Context

This review extends the saturation physics program from HERA measurements of geometrical scaling (Stasto, Golec-Biernat, Kwiecinski, 2000) and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation to recent NLO calculations of forward hadron production in pA collisions. It directly addresses the "tale of two gluon distributions" puzzle resolved by factorization studies (Dominguez, Marquet, Xiao, Yuan, 2011) and connects to the planned EIC program recommended by the US nuclear science advisory committee. The work enables future three-dimensional tomography of gluons in protons and nuclei through measurements of Wigner distributions and generalized TMDs at the EIC.
