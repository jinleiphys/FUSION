# Principal Component Analysis of collective flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In this paper, we implement Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to study the single particle distributions generated from thousands of {\tt VISH2+1} hydrodynamic simulations with an aim to explore if a machine could directly discover flow from the huge amount of data without explicit instructions from human-beings. We found that the obtained PCA eigenvectors are similar to but not identical with the traditional Fourier bases. Correspondingly, the PCA defined flow harmonics $v_n^\prime$ are also similar to the traditional $v_n$ for $n=2$ and 3, but largely deviated from the Fourier ones for $n\geq 4$. A further study on the symmetric cumulants and the Pearson coefficients indicates that mode-coupling effects are reduced for these flow harmonics defined by PCA.

# Principal Component Analysis of collective flow in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Principal Component Analysis applied directly to raw single-particle azimuthal distributions from hydrodynamic simulations can discover collective flow with its own data-driven bases, producing flow harmonics that are similar to traditional Fourier-defined harmonics for n=2 and 3 but deviate significantly for nв‰Ґ4, and these PCA-defined harmonics exhibit reduced mode-coupling effects compared to the Fourier expansion approach.

## Method

The authors performed 2000 event-by-event VISH2+1 hydrodynamic simulations with TRENTo initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at в€љs_NN = 2.76 A TeV across six centrality bins (0%-10% through 50%-60%). They constructed a final-state matrix M_f from the azimuthal angle distributions dN/dП† of charged hadrons (|y|<1.0) divided into 50 angular bins, then applied Singular Value Decomposition to obtain PCA eigenvectors and singular values. The PCA eigenvectors were compared to traditional Fourier bases, and the corresponding PCA flow harmonics v'_n were defined from paired singular values and compared to conventional Fourier flow harmonics v_n using symmetric cumulants and Pearson coefficients with initial eccentricities.

## Context

This work extends earlier PCA investigations of flow by Bhalerao (2014), Mazeliauskas (2015), Bozek (2017), and the CMS Collaboration (2017) that all applied PCA to preprocessed data already using Fourier expansion. The key advance is applying PCA directly to raw dN/dП† distributions without any prior Fourier transformation, testing whether unsupervised learning can discover flow bases autonomously. The finding that PCA-defined harmonics reduce mode-coupling effects challenges the long-held belief that nonlinear hydrodynamic evolution necessarily produces strong coupling between different flow harmonics, suggesting the nonlinearity of relativistic hydrodynamic systems in heavy-ion collisions should be re-evaluated. This enables future work using PCA to study flow with reduced mode-mixing and potentially cleaner extraction of initial-state eccentricity information.
