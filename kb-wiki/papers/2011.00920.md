# Light quark energy loss in a soft-wall AdS/QCD model

> We investigate the energy loss of light quarks in a holographic QCD model with conformal invariance broken by a background dilaton. We perform the analysis within falling string and shooting string, respectively. It turns out that the two methods give the same result: the presence of chemical potential and confining scale tends to enhance the energy loss, in accord with previous findings of drag force and jet quenching parameter.

# Light quark energy loss in a soft-wall AdS/QCD model

## Key claim
The presence of chemical potential and confining scale both enhance the energy loss of light quarks in the SW$_{T,\mu}$ model, as measured by both falling string and shooting string methods, but the authors note that the SW$_{T,\mu}$ model is not a bonafide solution of the classical equations of motion, so the results are qualitative rather than quantitative predictions for QCD.

## Method
The authors study light quark energy loss in the SW$_{T,\mu}$ holographic QCD model, which combines an AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black hole background with a soft-wall warp factor $h(z)=e^{c^2z^2}$ to introduce confinement. They employ two complementary approaches: the falling string method, where the stopping distance of a massless particle following a null geodesic in the WKB approximation is computed; and the shooting string method, where the instantaneous energy loss of a string endpoint starting near the horizon is calculated from the null geodesics that the endpoint follows.

## Key numbers
- The stopping distance ratio $x/x_{SYM}$ decreases with increasing $\mu/T$ at fixed $c/T$, and decreases with increasing $c/T$ at fixed $\mu/T$, for $|\vec{q}|=0.99\omega$.
- The energy loss ratio $(dE/dx)/(dE/dx)_{SYM}$ increases with increasing $\mu/T$ at fixed $c/T$, and increases with increasing $c/T$ at fixed $\mu/T$, evaluated at $x=0.01$.
- The confining scale range considered is $0 \leq c/T \leq 2.5$, motivated by lattice QCD comparisons.
- The chemical potential is given by $\mu = \sqrt{3}Q/z_h$, with black hole charge $0 \leq Q \leq \sqrt{2}$.
- The temperature is $T = (1/\pi z_h)(1 - Q^2/2)$.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet quenching in the SW$_{T,\mu}$ model, specifically the drag force calculations for heavy quarks by Zhang (2019) and the jet quenching parameter calculations by Zhang (2020), which found that chemical potential and confining scale enhance energy loss. The authors use the same holographic model introduced by Colangelo et al. (2011) and compare their results to the $\mathcal N=4$ SYM plasma results of Chesler et al. (2009) for falling string and of Gubser et al. (2008) for shooting string. The work enables qualitative comparison with RHIC and LHC jet quenching data, suggesting that including chemical potential and confining scale may lower the required temperature region needed to match experimental values.
