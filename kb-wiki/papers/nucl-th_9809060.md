# Binding energy of $Λ$ hypernuclei from realistic YN interactions

> s- and p-wave $Λ$ single-particle enrgies are obtained for a variety of $Λ$ hypernuclei from the relevant self-energy constructed within the framework of a perturbative many-body approach. Results are presented for present realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions such as Jülich B and Nijmegen SC models. Effects of the non-locality and energy-dependence of the self-energy on the bound states are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Juelich B realistic hyperon-nucleon interaction, when used in a perturbative many-body approach with a second-order expansion of the finite-nucleus G-matrix around the nuclear matter G-matrix, reproduces measured Lambda single-particle energies reasonably well, especially for s-wave states; however, the Nijmegen SC interaction produces results that are clearly underbound compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors start from a nuclear matter G-matrix obtained by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the bare YN interaction, then expand the finite-nucleus G-matrix around this nuclear matter G-matrix to second order in the difference between finite-nucleus and nuclear matter intermediate propagators. This finite-nucleus G-matrix is used to construct the hyperon self-energy at the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock level, and the real part of this self-energy serves as a non-local potential in a Schroedinger equation to obtain single-particle energies for s- and p-wave Lambda orbits in various hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- For $^{17}_{\Lambda}$O, the $1s_{1/2}$ binding energy from the full calculation (1st+2p1h) is -11.83 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -12.5 MeV for $^{16}_{\Lambda}$O
- For $^{17}_{\Lambda}$O, the $1p_{3/2}$ binding energy is -0.87 MeV (experimental $1p$ value: -2.5 MeV) and the $1p_{1/2}$ binding energy is -1.06 MeV
- For $^{41}_{\Lambda}$Ca, the $1s_{1/2}$ binding energy is -19.60 MeV (experimental: -20 MeV for $^{40}_{\Lambda}$Ca)
- For $^{91}_{\Lambda}$Zr, the $1s_{1/2}$ binding energy is -25.80 MeV (experimental: -23 MeV for $^{89}_{\Lambda}$Zr)
- For $^{209}_{\Lambda}$Pb, the $1s_{1/2}$ binding energy is -31.36 MeV (experimental: -27 MeV for $^{208}_{\Lambda}$Pb)
- The Nijmegen SC interaction gives -7.39 MeV for $^{17}_{\Lambda}$O $1s_{1/2}$, far from the experimental -12.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends microscopic hypernuclear structure calculations (Halderson, Kuo) by testing realistic YN interactions (Juelich B and Nijmegen SC models) against experimental Lambda single-particle energies, which were previously well reproduced by phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials (Motoba) or Skyrme YN interactions in Hartree-Fock calculations (Gal). The results enable future studies of scattering states and the implications of the more extended Lambda wave functions (compared to Woods-Saxon predictions) on the mesonic weak decay of hypernuclei.
