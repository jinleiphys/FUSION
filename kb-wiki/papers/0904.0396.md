# Collision system size dependence of dihadron azimuthal correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The system size dependence of dihadron azimuthal correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision is simulated by a multi-phase transport model. The structure of correlation functions and yields of associated particles show clear participant path-length dependences in collision systems with a partonic phase. The splitting parameter ($D$) and Root Mean Square Width ($Δϕ_{rms}$) of away side correlation functions increase with collision system size from $^{14}$N+$^{14}$N to $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions. The double-peak structure of away side correlation functions can only be formed in sufficient "large" collision systems. These properties provide some hints to study onset of deconfinement, which is related to the QCD phase boundary and QCD critical point, by an energy-size scan.

## Key claim

The double-peak structure on the away side of dihadron azimuthal correlation functions can only form in sufficiently "large" collision systems, with an onset occurring near the mass range of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, and this structure is sensitive to the effective degrees of freedom of the dense medium, which may provide hints about the onset of deconfinement.

## Method

The study used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT) in both its "Melt" version (which includes a partonic phase via string melting and parton cascade) and its "Default" version (which produces a pure hadron gas via Lund string fragmentation). Dihadron azimuthal correlations were computed for seven collision systems (¹⁴N+¹⁴N through ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) at √sNN = 200 GeV in the 0-10% most central collisions, using trigger particles with 2.5 < pTtrig < 6 GeV/c and associated particles with 1 < pTassoc < 3 GeV/c within |η| < 1, with background subtracted via the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) method.

## Key numbers

- Participant path-length ν ranges from 1.89 (¹⁴N+¹⁴N) to 5.33 (¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) for 0-10% centrality at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- Average parton collision number ncolparton ranges from 1.31 (¹⁴N+¹⁴N) to 7.24 (¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) in the Melt AMPT version.
- The double-peak structure on the away side becomes clearly visible starting from the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collision system (Npart = 65.97, ν = 2.76).
- Δφrms values from the Melt AMPT version are consistent with PHENIX data for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions.
- Splitting parameter D values from the Melt AMPT version are comparable to PHENIX data for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions.
- The parton interaction cross section used in the Melt AMPT version is 10 mb.
- Theoretical emission angles relative to jet are about 1.23 rad for QGP, 1.11 rad for hadronic gas, and zero for mixed phase.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of centrality-dependent dihadron correlations by PHENIX and theoretical simulations by the same group (Zhang et al., 2007) to a systematic scan of collision system size at fixed energy. It supports the energy-size (E-A) scan program proposed by the NA61 collaboration to investigate the QCD phase boundary and critical point. The results show that the double-peak structure and jet modification are sensitive to the effective degrees of freedom of the medium, distinguishing between a partonic phase (Melt AMPT) and a pure hadron gas (Default AMPT), and suggest that the onset of deconfinement may be observable through system-size scans near the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca mass range.
