# Field redefinition and its impact in relativistic hydrodynamics

> In this paper, we explore the impact of field redefinition on the spectrum of linearized perturbations in relativistic hydrodynamics. We observe that the spectrum of hydrodynamics modes is never affected by the local field redefinition, however, the spectrum of the non-hydrodynamic modes is affected. Through an appropriate all-order redefinition, non-hydrodynamic modes can be eliminated, leading to a new frame where the spectrum contains only hydrodynamic modes. We also observe that the resulting stress-energy tensor may have an infinite series in momentum space, with a convergence radius linked to the eliminated non-hydrodynamic mode. In certain special cases, higher-order terms in the stress-energy tensor under field redefinition may cancel, indicating that non-hydrodynamic modes are mere artefacts of the fluid variable choice and hold no physical significance, even if they appear to violate physical constraints. Using a special toy example, we find a criterion to distinguish between physical and unphysical non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Key claim

The spectrum of hydrodynamic modes is never affected by local field redefinition, but the spectrum of non-hydrodynamic modes is affected; through an appropriate all-order redefinition, non-hydrodynamic modes can be eliminated, leading to a new frame where the spectrum contains only hydrodynamic modes, though the resulting stress-energy tensor may have an infinite series in momentum space with a convergence radius linked to the eliminated non-hydrodynamic mode.

## Method

The authors analyze how field redefinition of fluid variables (velocity and temperature) transforms the dispersion polynomial of linearized perturbations in relativistic hydrodynamics. They derive the general form of the frame transformation matrix \(S_{ij}\) for uncharged fluids, showing that the dispersion polynomial in a transformed frame \(\hat P(\omega,k^2)\) relates to the original polynomial \(P(\omega,k^2)\) by \(\hat P = P \cdot \mathcal{F}\), where \(\mathcal{F}\) is a factor determined by the transformation functions \(F_u, F_T, G_u, G_T, R_u, R_T\). They then construct an algorithm to remove non-hydrodynamic modes by choosing these functions as infinite-order differential operators, and test the criterion using a toy example within the BDNK formalism.

## Key numbers

* The determinant of the frame transformation matrix is \(\mathcal{F}(-i\omega, k^2) = (1-i\omega F_u - k^2 R_u)[(1-i\omega G_T - k^2 R_T)(1-i\omega F_u - k^2 R_u) + k^2 F_T G_u]\), which factorizes into shear and sound channel contributions.
* In the shear channel, the extra factor from frame transformation is \((1-i\omega F_u - k^2 R_u)\); in the sound channel, it is \([(1-i\omega G_T - k^2 R_T)(1-i\omega F_u - k^2 R_u) + k^2 F_T G_u]\).
* The constants \(C_{\text{sh}} = \lim_{k\rightarrow 0} \left(\prod_{a=1}^{N_2^{\text{sh}}}[-{\mathfrak w}^a_{\text{sh}}]\right)\) and \(C_{\text{snd}} = \lim_{k\rightarrow 0} \left(\prod_{a=1}^{N_2^{\text{snd}}}[-{\mathfrak w}^a_{\text{snd}}]\right)\) appear in the conditions for removing non-hydrodynamic modes via all-order field redefinition.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of field redefinition in relativistic hydrodynamics from Bhattacharyya (2024) by providing detailed derivations and extended analysis of how non-hydrodynamic modes can be removed. It contrasts with the BDNK formalism (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, Kovtun, 2017-2019) which keeps the fluid frame unfixed to determine where first-order fluid dynamics maintains causality and stability, and with the Landau and Eckart frames (Israel, 1976, 1979) which impose external constraints on the stress tensor. The results enable distinguishing physical non-hydrodynamic modes from frame artefacts, which is essential for testing causality and stability constraints in relativistic fluid models.
