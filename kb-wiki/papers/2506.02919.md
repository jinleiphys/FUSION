# Testing Variational Perturbation Theory for Effective Actions Using the Gaudin-Yang Model

> The background field formalism based on effective actions is a compelling framework for developing an effective field theory for nuclear density functional theory. Among the challenges in carrying out this development is handling both the particle-hole and pairing channels beyond the mean-field level, which includes how to incorporate collective degrees of freedom. Here we use the exactly solvable one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model as a theoretical laboratory to explore candidate approaches. We compare Variational Perturbation Theory (VPT) to ordinary many-body perturbation theory and the inversion method, all to second order in their respective expansions, and verify issues with Hubbard-Stratonovich auxiliary fields. VPT outperforms the other approaches at this level over a wide range of densities. The next steps to extend this approach toward nuclei are outlined.

## Key claim

Variational Perturbation Theory (VPT) outperforms ordinary many-body perturbation theory and the inversion method when all are truncated to second order in their respective expansions, but this conclusion is limited to the exactly solvable one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model as a theoretical laboratory and the next steps toward nuclear applications remain to be carried out.

## Method

The authors used the exactly solvable one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model with attractive contact interactions and two fermion flavors, working in the zero-temperature thermodynamic limit. They compared three approaches, Variational Perturbation Theory (VPT), ordinary many-body perturbation theory, and the inversion method, all truncated to second order in their respective expansions, by computing the energy density and chemical potential as functions of the dimensionless coupling parameter λ = mC₀/(ħ²ρ). The exact Bethe ansatz solution served as the benchmark for evaluating the accuracy of each approximation across a wide range of densities.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coupling parameter is defined as λ = mC₀/(ħ²ρ), where low density corresponds to strong coupling (large λ) and high density to weak coupling (small λ).
- The exact dimensionless energy per particle ε̃(λ) is given by ε̃(λ) = -1 + (4/π)K³λ ∫_{-1}^{1} dy y² f(y), where f(y) and K are determined by coupled integral equations.
- The perturbative expansion for the energy has been worked out to very high order in prior work (Marino, 2019), and is shown to second order alongside the exact and BCS solutions.

## Context

This work extends the program of developing an effective field theory for nuclear density functional theory using the background field formalism, building on prior work by Furnstahl and collaborators (Hammer, 2000; Puglia, 2002; Bhattacharyya, 2004; Furnstahl, 2006). The Gaudin-Yang model serves as a testbed for comparing candidate approaches to handling particle-hole and pairing channels beyond mean-field level, with VPT offering a convergent alternative to the asymptotic standard perturbation theory. The next steps outlined include extending this approach toward finite nuclei in three dimensions.
