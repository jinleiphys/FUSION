# Energy dependence of jet transport parameter and parton saturation in quark-gluon plasma

> We study the evolution and saturation of the gluon distribution function in the quark-gluon plasma as probed by a propagating parton and its effect on the computation of jet quenching or transport parameter $\hat{q}$. For thermal partons, the saturation scale $Q^2_s$ is found to be proportional to the Debye screening mass $μ_D^2$. For hard probes, evolution at small $x=Q^2_s/6ET$ leads to jet energy dependence of $\hat{q}$. We study this dependence for both a conformal gauge theory in weak and strong coupling limit and for (pure gluon) QCD. The energy dependence can be used to extract the shear viscosity $η$ of the medium since $η$ can be related to the transport parameter for thermal partons in a transport description. We also derive upper bounds on the transport parameter for both energetic and thermal partons. The later leads to a lower bound on shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio which is consistent with the conjectured lower bound $η/s\geq 1/4π$. We also discuss the implications on the study of jet quenching at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the CERN Large Hadron Collider and the bulk properties of the dense matter.

## Key claim

The jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ acquires an energy dependence for hard probes through small-$x$ evolution of the gluon distribution in the quark-gluon plasma, but this energy dependence is absent for thermal partons because the saturation scale for thermal partons coincides approximately with the Debye screening mass.

## Method

The authors relate the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}_R$ to the unintegrated gluon distribution function of color sources in the medium, computed via hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed perturbation theory at finite temperature. For energetic jet partons, they apply the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) to resum large logarithms of both momentum scale and small momentum fraction, evolving the gluon distribution from an initial condition at scale $\mu^2 = T^2$ obtained from HTL calculations. The saturation scale $Q_s^2$ is determined self-consistently from the evolved gluon distribution, and the transport parameter is evaluated including saturation effects.

## Key numbers

- For a pure gluonic plasma, the integrated gluon distribution per gluonic color source at large $\mu^2/\mu_D^2 \gg 1$ in the small $N_c g^2 < x < \sqrt{N_c} g$ region is $xG(x,\mu^2) \approx C_A \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \frac{\pi^2}{6\zeta(3)} \frac{1}{2} \left[\frac{3}{2}\ln\frac{\mu^2}{\mu_D^2} + \frac{1}{3}\ln\frac{\mu_D}{xT}\right]$.
- The saturation scale for thermal partons is $Q_s^2(x_m) \approx \mu_D^2 \frac{3}{2} \sqrt{\frac{1}{\pi}\ln\frac{18T}{\mu_D}}$, where $x_m = Q_s^2/18T^2$.
- The transport parameter for thermal partons, including saturation effects, is $\hat{q}_R \approx \pi N_c^2 \alpha_s^2 T^3 \ln\frac{Q_m^2}{\mu_D^2}$ with $Q_m^2 = 18 T^2$.
- The hierarchy of saturation scales in a perturbative gluonic plasma is: $Q_s^2(x) \sim (N_c g^2)^2 T^2 \sim \mu_{\rm mag}^2$ for $x \sim 1$, $Q_s^2(x) \sim N_c g^2 T^2 \sim \mu_D^2$ for $x \sim N_c g^2$, and $Q_s^2(x) \sim T^2$ for $x \sim N_c^2 g^4$.

## Context

This work extends the relationship between jet transport parameter and gluon distribution established by Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (BDMPS) to include dynamical screening and small-$x$ evolution, going beyond the static potential model used in earlier parton energy loss calculations. The energy dependence of $\hat{q}$ connects to the shear viscosity $\eta$ of the medium via the low-energy limit of the transport parameter, as shown in a recent study by Majumder, Muller, and Bass (2007). The results enable improved phenomenological studies of jet quenching at RHIC and the LHC, and provide a lower bound on $\eta/s$ consistent with the conjectured bound $1/4\pi$ from AdS/CFT.
