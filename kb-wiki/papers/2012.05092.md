# New results about the canonical transformation for boson operators

> The Bogoliubov transformation for a monopole boson induces an unitary transformation connecting the Fock spaces of initial and correlated boson-s. Here we provide a very simple method for deriving the analytical expression for the overlap matrix of the basis states generating the two boson spaces.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical expression for the overlap matrix between the Fock space basis of initial bosons and the basis of correlated bosons obtained via the Bogoliubov transformation, using a method that is simpler than previous approaches. The result is valid only for the specific case of a monopole boson Hamiltonian with a quadratic form, and the transformation exists only when the coupling parameter satisfies \(|\kappa| \le 1/2\).

## Method

The authors start with a second-order boson Hamiltonian \(H = \epsilon b^\dagger b - X(b^{\dagger 2} + b^2)\) and apply the Bogoliubov transformation to define new boson operators \(\tilde{b}^\dagger = U b^\dagger - V b\). They then use the Bargmann representation, mapping boson operators to a real variable \(x\) and its derivative, converting the eigenvalue problem into a Schrödinger equation for a harmonic oscillator. The wavefunctions of this oscillator, expressed as products of Hermite polynomials and exponentials, are expanded in Taylor series to extract the overlap matrix elements \(\langle m | \tilde{n} \rangle\) between the original basis \(|m\rangle\) and the correlated basis \(|\tilde{n}\rangle\).

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the transformed Hamiltonian is \(\omega = \epsilon \sqrt{1 - 4\kappa^2}\), where \(\kappa = X/\epsilon\).
- The transformation exists only for \(|\kappa| \le 1/2\); the system becomes unstable for \(X > \frac{1}{2}\epsilon\) or \(X < -\frac{1}{2}\epsilon\).
- The Bogoliubov coefficients satisfy \(U^2 - V^2 = 1\), with explicit values \(U^2 = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4\kappa^2}}\right)\) and \(V^2 = \frac{1}{2}\left(-1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-4\kappa^2}}\right)\).
- The oscillator length is \(r^2 = 1/(m\omega) = 2UV\).
- The final overlap matrix is given by \(\langle m|\tilde{n}\rangle = \sqrt{m! n!} \, U^{-\frac{m+n+1}{2}} \sum_p \frac{(-1)^{\frac{m-p}{2}} (V/2)^{\frac{m+n}{2} - p}}{(\frac{m-p}{2})! (\frac{n-p}{2})! p!}\), with the restrictions that \(m-p\) and \(n-p\) are even, \(p \le \min(m,n)\), and \(|m-n|\) is even.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analytical results for the overlap matrix of the Bogoliubov transformation for bosons, previously obtained by Takahashi (1973), Rashid (1973), and Raduta (1977) using more tedious methods. The simpler derivation presented here enables easier application in formalisms that use monopole bosons, such as the Random Phase Approximation and the Interacting Boson Approximation. The result is particularly useful for treating second-order boson Hamiltonians where cross terms raise convergence difficulties, allowing one to work in the basis of correlated bosons where the Hamiltonian becomes harmonic.
