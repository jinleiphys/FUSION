# Charmonia decay widths in magnetized matter using a model for composite hadrons

> The decay widths of the charmonium states to $D¥bar D$ in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in the presence of a magnetic field are studied, using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with quark/antiquark constituents. The medium modifications of these partial decay widths arise due to the changes in the masses of the decaying charmonium state and the produced $D$ and $¥bar D$ mesons in the magnetized hadronic matter, calculated within a chiral effective model. The decay widths are computed using the light quark--antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian in terms of the constituent quark field operators. The results of the present investigation are compared with the in-medium decay widths obtained within the $^3P_0$ model. Within the $^3P_0$ model, the charmonium decay widths are calculated using the creation of a light quark--antiquark pair in the $^3P_0$ state. In the presence of a magnetic field, the Landau level contributions give rise to positive shifts in the masses of the charged $D$ and $¥bar D$ mesons. This leads to the decay of charmonium to the charged $D^+ D^-$ to be suppressed as compared to the neutral $D¥bar D$ pair in symmetric nuclear matter, whereas in asymmetric nuclear matter, the larger mass drop of the $D^+D^-$ pair, as compared to the $D^0 ¥bar {D^0}$ pair leads to the production of charged open charm meson pairs to be enhanced as compared to the charmonium decay channel to $D^0 {¥bar {D^0}}$.

## Key claim

The decay widths of charmonium states to charged versus neutral $D¥bar D$ pairs in magnetized nuclear matter depend sensitively on both the magnetic field strength and the isospin asymmetry of the medium, with the Landau level contributions to charged $D$ meson masses suppressing charged decays in symmetric matter but enhancing them in asymmetric matter, contrary to what the abstract's simplified contrast might suggest.

## Method

The authors compute partial decay widths of $¥psi(3686)$ and $¥psi(3770)$ to $D^+D^-$ and $D^0¥bar D^0$ using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons, where the charmonium and open charm mesons are explicitly constructed from constituent quark field operators. The decay width is calculated from the light quark-antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian, with medium modifications entering through the in-medium masses of the charmonium state and the $D$ and $¥bar D$ mesons, which are obtained from a chiral SU(3) effective model that includes the scalar dilaton field (mimicking gluon condensates) and Landau level contributions for charged mesons. Results are compared with those from the $^3P_0$ model.

## Context

This work extends the study of charmonium decay widths in nuclear matter from zero magnetic field (Mishra and Misra, earlier work using the same composite hadron model) to the magnetized case, and compares with results from the $^3P_0$ model (Mishra et al., 2018 on charmonium decay in magnetized matter). The chiral effective model used for in-medium masses builds on prior work by the same group on open charm mesons in magnetic fields (Mishra et al., 2017) and charmonium masses in magnetic fields (Mishra et al., 2018). The results are relevant for interpreting heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC where strong transient magnetic fields are present, and for compressed baryonic matter experiments at GSI, where isospin asymmetry effects on open charm production may be observable.
