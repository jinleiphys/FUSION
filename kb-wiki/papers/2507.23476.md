# In-medium effects of nucleon-nucleon cross sections in heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, we systematically investigate the in-medium effects of nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) cross sections on nucleonic and pionic observables in heavy-ion collisions, employing microscopic cross sections derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. Key observables include nuclear stopping, the neutron-to-proton ($n/p$) ratio, neutron-proton transverse flow differences, differential collective flow, pion multiplicities, and the resulting $(π^-/π^+)_{\rm like}$ ratio. The analysis disentangles the respective contributions from the scattering amplitude, the density of states, and the total momentum ($K$) of the colliding pairs. We find that larger in-medium $NN$ cross sections generally enhance free nucleon emission and nuclear stopping, with the nucleon effective mass playing a dominant suppressive role. However, it is insufficient to account only for the medium corrections from effective mass: both the medium effect from the scattering amplitude and the $K$-dependence exert noticeable influences on the observables. In particular, nuclear stopping is found to be highly sensitive to these in-medium modifications of cross sections. While the $n/p$ ratio and transverse flow difference remain largely insensitive, the differential collective flow and pion yields are strongly affected. These results indicate that the interplay between scattering amplitude, density-of-states and $K$-dependence is essential to accurately describe medium effects in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, when fully accounting for the scattering amplitude, density of states, and total momentum dependence, produces observable effects in heavy-ion collisions that cannot be captured by the commonly used effective-mass-only correction factor; nuclear stopping is highly sensitive to these in-medium modifications, while the neutron-to-proton ratio and transverse flow difference remain largely insensitive.

## Method

The authors employed the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate collisions of 132Sn on 124Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon, implementing five different prescriptions for the nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering cross section: free-space cross section, effective-mass-corrected cross section, full Brueckner-Hartree-Fock microscopic cross section, and two variants that isolate the contributions from the scattering amplitude and from the total momentum dependence. The analysis disentangled the respective contributions from the scattering amplitude (G-matrix), the density of states (effective mass), and the total momentum K of the colliding nucleon pair to observables including rapidity distributions, nuclear stopping, collective flows, and pion multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 270 MeV/nucleon for the 132Sn+124Sn reaction
- Impact parameter: b = 0.0 fm for central collisions and semi-central collisions
- Density used for cross section calculations: ρ = 1.5 ρ0 with isospin asymmetry β = 0.2
- Total momenta K considered for cross sections: 0.9, 2.25, 3.6, and 4.95 fm^{-1}
- Nuclear stopping observable vartl: values range from above 1.0 for σ*_BHF (over-stopping) to below 1.0 for σ_BHF and σ^K_BHF (under-stopping), with σ_free and σ_eff yielding intermediate values
- Free nucleons identified as those with local densities less than ρ0/8 in the final state

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic predictions of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework (Han 2022, Shang 2021) by implementing those cross sections into transport simulations and evaluating their impact on heavy-ion collision observables. It contrasts with the common approximation used in earlier studies (Li 2005, Guo 2013, Zhang 2021) where the in-medium cross section is factorized as the free-space cross section multiplied by the square of the ratio of in-medium effective reduced mass to bare reduced mass, which only accounts for changes in the density of states while neglecting medium modifications to the scattering amplitude. The results enable future work to constrain in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections using nuclear stopping as a sensitive observable, and confirm that the neutron-to-proton ratio and neutron-proton transverse flow difference remain clean probes of the symmetry energy largely unaffected by uncertainties in the in-medium cross section.
