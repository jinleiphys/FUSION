# Nuclear equation of state for arbitrary proton fraction and temperature based on chiral effective field theory and a Gaussian process emulator

> We calculate the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature based on chiral effective field theory interactions to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. Our results assess the theoretical uncertainties from the many-body calculation and the chiral expansion. Using a Gaussian process emulator for the free energy, we derive the thermodynamic properties of matter through consistent derivatives and use the Gaussian process to access arbitrary proton fraction and temperature. This enables a first nonparametric calculation of the equation of state in beta equilibrium, and of the speed of sound and the symmetry energy at finite temperature. Moreover, our results show that the thermal part of the pressure decreases with increasing densities.

# Nuclear equation of state for arbitrary proton fraction and temperature based on chiral effective field theory and a Gaussian process emulator

## Key claim
The authors present the first microscopic calculations of the nuclear equation of state for arbitrary proton fractions and finite temperatures using chiral effective field theory interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO), with the caveat that the results are limited to densities n ≤ 0.25 fm⁻³, proton fractions x ≤ 0.5, and temperatures T ≤ 20 MeV, and that the chiral expansion shows no indication of breakdown only up to n ≲ 0.25 fm⁻³.

## Method
The authors perform many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations around a self-consistent Hartree-Fock state using nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions from chiral EFT at N³LO with cutoff Λ = 450 MeV. They include all contributions from NN and 3N interactions up to second order, and at third order all interaction vertices that are NN or 3N with one line closing on itself. A Gaussian process emulator is constructed for the interaction free energy per particle to interpolate the equation of state across density, proton fraction, and temperature, enabling thermodynamically consistent derivatives and access to arbitrary nuclear conditions including beta equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Free energy per particle at T=0, n=0.16 fm⁻³, x=0.5 (symmetric matter) at N³LO MBPT(3): -15.1(4) MeV
- Free energy per particle at T=20 MeV, n=0.16 fm⁻³, x=0.5 at N³LO MBPT(3): -34.3(7) MeV
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric matter: T_c = 15.9–16.3 MeV
- Critical density for liquid-gas phase transition: n_c = 0.07–0.11 fm⁻³
- Critical pressure for liquid-gas phase transition: P_c = 0.30–0.40 MeV fm⁻³
- Symmetry free energy per particle at saturation density n₀ and T=0: F_sym(n₀)/A ≈ 30 MeV
- Proton fraction in beta equilibrium at N³LO: 4–8% for the temperatures considered
- Maximum density considered: n ≤ 0.25 fm⁻³
- Maximum temperature considered: T ≤ 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous chiral EFT calculations that focused on pure neutron matter or symmetric nuclear matter (Hebeler and Schwenk 2010, Tews 2013, Drischler 2019, Keller 2021) to arbitrary proton fractions and finite temperatures. The Gaussian process emulator approach enables a first nonparametric calculation of the equation of state in beta equilibrium, the speed of sound, and the symmetry energy at finite temperature, providing direct input for astrophysical simulations of supernovae and neutron star mergers. The results are compared to the virial equation of state (Horowitz and Schwenk 2006) at low densities and to the LS220 equation of state (Lattimer and Swesty 1991) for beta-equilibrium matter, and show overlap with the equation of state band from Hebeler et al. (2013) based on chiral EFT with piecewise polytrope extensions.
