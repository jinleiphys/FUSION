# Perturbative renormalizability of chiral two pion exchange in nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We study the perturbative renormalizability of chiral two pion exchange for the singlet and triplet channels within effective field theory, provided that the one pion exchange piece of the interaction has been fully iterated. We determine the number of counterterms/subtractions needed in order to obtain finite results when the cut-off is removed, resulting in three counterterms for the singlet channel and six for the triplet. The results show that chiral two pion exchange can be treated perturbatively up to a center-of-mass momentum of k ~ 200-300 MeV in the singlet channel and k ~ 300-400 in the triplet.

## Key claim

Chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) can be treated perturbatively in nucleon-nucleon scattering provided that one-pion exchange (OPE) is fully iterated, but only within a specific cut-off window (r_c = 0.6-0.9 fm) that represents a compromise between the different requirements of the singlet and triplet channels; outside this window the perturbative treatment fails at lower momenta.

## Method

The author uses a renormalized distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) in coordinate space, where the leading-order (LO) OPE potential is solved non-perturbatively with one counterterm, and the subleading chiral TPE contributions at NLO and N2LO are treated in first-order perturbation theory. The perturbative integrals are regularized with a coordinate-space cut-off r_c, and the divergences are removed by adding energy-dependent counterterms (delta-shell potentials) whose coefficients are fitted to the Nijmegen II phase shifts in the range k = 40-160 MeV. The number of required counterterms is determined by expanding the perturbative integrals in powers of k^2 and counting the number of divergent terms.

## Key numbers

- Three counterterms are needed to renormalize perturbative chiral TPE in the singlet 1S0 channel.
- Six counterterms are needed to renormalize perturbative chiral TPE in the triplet 3S1-3D1 coupled channel.
- Perturbative chiral TPE reproduces data up to k ~ 200-300 MeV in the singlet channel and k ~ 300-400 MeV in the triplet channel.
- The preferred cut-off window is r_c = 0.6-0.9 fm, approximately equivalent to a momentum-space sharp cut-off of Lambda ~ 350-500 MeV.
- The singlet scattering length is fixed to a_{0,s} = -23.74 fm and the triplet scattering length to a_{0,t} = 5.419 fm.
- The chiral couplings used are c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.40 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.40 GeV^{-1}, with f_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_pi = 138.03 MeV, g_A = 1.26, and d_{18} = -0.97 GeV^2.
- The chiral van der Waals length scale R_6 varies between 1.6-1.8 fm for typical chiral couplings.
- The first deeply bound state for the N2LO potential in the singlet appears at r_c = 0.70 fm; in the triplet the first appears at r_c = 0.45 fm.

## Context

This work extends the power-counting proposal of Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (2005) to subleading orders by investigating the perturbative renormalizability of chiral TPE, and is directly based on techniques sketched in Valderrama (2005). The results agree with the renormalization group analysis of Birse (2005) for the singlet channel but show a discrepancy for the triplet 3S1 phase, where Birse predicts an additional counterterm at order Q^{5/2} that is not required by perturbative renormalizability. The approach enables a systematic perturbative treatment of subleading chiral interactions that respects power counting and avoids the problems of non-perturbative renormalization, particularly the loss of predictive power from attractive singular channels and the sign ambiguity of subleading potentials.
