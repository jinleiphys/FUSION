# Benchmark calculation of inclusive electromagnetic responses in the four-body nuclear system

> Both the no-core shell model and the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic approaches are applied to the calculation of different response functions to external electromagnetic probes, using the Lorentz integral transform method. The test is performed on the four-body nuclear system, within a simple potential model. The quality of the agreement in the various cases is discussed, together with the perspectives for rigorous ab initio calculations of cross sections of heavier nuclei.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM) can be successfully applied to the solution of the bound-state-like equations required by the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method for inclusive response functions, achieving equivalent results to the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic (EIHH) approach for the four-body system, but only when a suitable range of harmonic-oscillator frequencies is chosen to ensure convergence for both ground and excited states.

## Method

The authors calculated the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) of the inclusive electromagnetic response functions for the four-body nuclear system (⁴He) using both the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic (EIHH) methods. The LIT was evaluated via the Lanczos algorithm, expressing the transform as a continued fraction in terms of Lanczos coefficients. The NCSM used a three-body cluster approximation for the effective interaction with a harmonic-oscillator basis, while the EIHH used a two-body cluster approximation with a hyperspherical harmonic basis; both methods employed the semirealistic Minnesota (MN) potential as the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He ground-state energy with the MN potential (no Coulomb): EIHH gives -30.779(1) MeV, NCSM gives -30.80(5) MeV; the five best results at Nmax=16 and Kmax=16 agree within 0.2%.
- Total dipole strength M0(hat D): EIHH = 0.7883(1) fm², NCSM = 0.786(6) fm²; best results agree within 0.1% for hbarOmega = 12, 19, and 28 MeV.
- Total quadrupole strength M0(hat Q): EIHH = 8.54(1) fm⁴, NCSM = 8.56(14) fm⁴; agreement within 1% for hbarOmega = 12, 19, and 28 MeV.
- For the dipole response, the discrepancy between NCSM (hbarOmega = 19 MeV) and EIHH results does not exceed 5% in the energy intervals immediately close to the disintegration threshold and in the dipole resonance peak region; for hbarOmega = 12 and 28 MeV, the discrepancy is at most 10% and 15%, respectively, within 3 MeV from threshold.
- For the quadrupole response, discrepancies between NCSM and EIHH do not exceed 10% in the range 25 MeV ≤ omega ≤ 60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the LIT method, previously combined with the EIHH approach for calculations up to A=7 (Bacca 2004) and with realistic forces for ⁴He (Gazit 2006), to the NCSM framework. The NCSM has been successfully applied to ab initio descriptions of nuclear properties up to A=16 and beyond (Navratil 2000), but had not been tested for continuum response calculations via integral transforms. The benchmark demonstrates that NCSM can achieve the precision required for LIT calculations, opening the possibility for ab initio studies of inclusive electromagnetic responses in heavier nuclei, though the large model spaces needed will require considerable computational effort and may benefit from softer effective field-theory interactions.
