# The $K^{\ast 0}Λ$ photoproduction off a neutron

> Inspired by the preliminary experimental data by the CLAS Collaboration, the $K^{\ast 0}Λ$ photoproduction off a neutron is studied within an effective Lagrangian approach. The contributions from the Born terms including $s$, $u$, and $t$ channels are considered to calculate the amplitude, with which the cross sections are calculated and compared with preliminary CLAS data. The theoretical results indicate that the contribution from the $K$-exchange $t$ channel plays a dominant role for the $K^{\ast 0}Λ$ photoproduction. The contribution from the $κ$ exchange is found one order of magnitude smaller than that from the $K$ exchange. Both the Regge model and the Feynman model are applied to treat the $t$-channel contribution. The discrepancy between two models is found small in the energy range of CLAS data and predicted to become obvious at energies higher than 3 GeV. More precise experimental data especially at backward angles will be helpful to further understand the interaction mechanism of the $K^{\ast 0}Λ$ photoproduction.

## Key claim

The $t$-channel $K$ exchange is overwhelmingly dominant for $K^{\ast 0}\Lambda$ photoproduction off a neutron, with the $\kappa$ exchange contribution being one order of magnitude smaller; however, this conclusion is based on a one-parameter fit to only 17 preliminary CLAS data points, and the nucleon resonance contribution (not included in the fit) may still be observable at medium and backward angles.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with Born terms from $s$-channel (nucleon pole), $t$-channel ($K$ and $\kappa$ exchanges), and $u$-channel ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Sigma^{\ast}$ exchanges) to compute the scattering amplitude and differential cross section for $\gamma n \rightarrow K^{\ast 0}\Lambda$. Both a Feynman model and a Regge model were applied to treat the $t$-channel propagators, with a single free parameter (the $t$-channel cutoff $\Lambda_t$) fitted to preliminary CLAS data at three beam-energy bins between 1.9 and 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Fitted $t$-channel cutoff $\Lambda_t = 1.05 \pm 0.01$ GeV (Feynman model) and $\Lambda_t = 2.28 \pm 0.12$ GeV (Regge model).
- Best-fit $\chi^2/dof = 1.99$ for the Feynman model and $\chi^2/dof = 1.68$ for the Regge model.
- The $\kappa$ exchange contribution is one order of magnitude smaller than the $K$ exchange contribution.
- Discrepancy between the Regge and Feynman models is small at beam energies $E_\gamma = 2.0$–$2.4$ GeV but becomes obvious at energies above 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of $K^{\ast}\Lambda$ photoproduction off the proton (Oh 2006, Kim 2011, Kim 2014) to the neutron target, using the same effective Lagrangian framework and coupling constants. It confirms the earlier suggestion by Oh (2006) that the $\kappa$ exchange contribution is suppressed relative to $K$ exchange. The results provide a baseline for interpreting future CLAS data at higher energies and backward angles, and enable tests of whether nucleon resonances such as $N(2120)$ contribute observably to this channel.
