# Question of Lorentz invariance in muon decay

> Possibilities to test the Lorentz invariance of the weak interaction in muon decay are considered. We derive the direction-dependent muon-decay rate with a general Lorentz-violating addition to the W-boson propagator. We discuss measurements of the directional and boost dependence of the Michel parameters and of the muon lifetime as a function of absolute velocity. The total muon-decay rate in the Lorentz-violating Standard Model Extension is addressed. Suggestions are made for dedicated (re)analyses of the pertinent data and for future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that muon decay can serve as a precision laboratory to test Lorentz invariance of the weak interaction, but the actual sensitivity to Lorentz violation depends critically on the directional and boost dependence of the decay rate, not just on the total rate; the total unpolarized muon-decay rate is actually insensitive to the Lorentz-violating tensor \(\chi^{\mu\nu}\) introduced in the W-boson propagator.

## Method

The authors derive the direction-dependent muon-decay rate to first order in Lorentz violation by adding a general complex tensor \(\chi^{\mu\nu}\) to the W-boson propagator in the tree-level matrix element for \(\mu^- \rightarrow e^- + \overline{\nu}_e + \nu_\mu\) and the corresponding \(\mu^+\) decay. They express the Lorentz-violating contributions in terms of parameters that modify the standard Michel parameters and introduce new frame-dependent terms. They then extract bounds on components of \(\chi^{\mu\nu}\) from existing measurements of the Michel parameter \(\varrho\) by the TWIST Collaboration and from comparisons of muon lifetimes at rest and in flight using data from the MuLan, MuCap, and CERN \(g-2\) experiments.

## Key numbers

* The TWIST Collaboration value \(\varrho_{\rm exp} = 0.74977(26)\) for \(\mu^+\) decay yields a 95% confidence limit on the Lorentz-violating parameter: \(-5.6 \times 10^{-4} < X_{rs}^{00} + \sin\zeta\cos\phi (X_{rs}^{0Z} - \frac{1}{2}X_{ra}^{0Z} + \frac{1}{4}\tilde{X}_{ia}^Z) < 1.5 \times 10^{-3}\).
* From muon lifetime comparisons at rest and in flight (\(\gamma \simeq 29.3\)), the relative difference \(\Delta = (\tau'/\gamma - \tau)/\tau\) for \(\mu^+\) is \(-1.7(9.1) \times 10^{-4}\), for \(\mu^-\) is \(-10.0(4.6) \times 10^{-4}\), and for the average \(\mu\) is \(-8.3(4.1) \times 10^{-4}\).
* The most likely values from a fit to lifetime data give \(c^{TT} - 0.1 c^{ZZ} = -1.2(1.1) \times 10^{-7}\) and \(b^T/m_\mu = -3.6(4.5) \times 10^{-6}\) for CPT-even and CPT-odd SME parameters, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective field theory approach to Lorentz violation in weak decays, which was applied to nuclear beta decay (Noordmans 2013), pion decay (Altschul 2013, Noordmans 2014), and kaon decay (Vos 2014). It proposes muon decay as a new testbed for Lorentz and CPT invariance, complementing existing tests from neutrino oscillations and neutral-meson systems. The results enable future dedicated analyses of existing TWIST and muon \(g-2\) data, as well as new experiments at planned muon-beam facilities, to improve bounds on Lorentz-violating coefficients in the Standard Model Extension.
