# Fermionic Light Dark Matter particles and the New Physics of Neutron Stars

> Dark Matter constitutes most of the matter in the presently accepted cosmological model for our Universe. The extreme conditions of ordinary baryonic matter, namely high density and compactness, in Neutron Stars make these objects suitable to gravitationally accrete such a massive component provided interaction strength between both, luminous and dark sectors, at current experimental level of sensitivity. We consider several different DM phenomenological models from the myriad of those presently allowed. In this contribution we review astrophysical aspects of interest in the interplay of ordinary matter and a fermionic light Dark Matter component. We focus in the interior nuclear medium in the core and external layers, i.e. the crust, discussing the impact of a novel dark sector in relevant stellar quantities for (heat) energy transport such as thermal conductivity or emissivities.

# Fermionic Light Dark Matter particles and the New Physics of Neutron Stars

## Key claim
Dark Matter (DM) particles with masses below about 1 GeV/c² can be gravitationally captured by neutron stars, and their scattering off nucleons in the dense stellar interior is significantly modified by in-medium effects such as Fermi blocking and finite temperature, which are often neglected in simplified estimates. The authors review how these medium corrections alter the DM mean free path and capture rate, and explore the possibility that DM scattering in the outer crust can excite phonons that affect thermal transport.

## Method
The authors calculate the differential cross-section per unit volume for fermionic Dirac DM particles scattering off nucleons in neutron star matter, using a spin-independent scalar-vector interaction Lagrangian. They incorporate in-medium effects including effective nucleon masses, Fermi blocking factors from the Pauli exclusion principle, and detailed balance factors for finite temperature, then integrate to obtain the DM mean free path as a function of kinetic energy, density, and temperature. For the low-density crust region, they compute the single-phonon excitation rate from DM scattering off nuclei in a periodic lattice using the Born approximation and a Maxwell-Juttner distribution for relativistic incoming DM particles.

## Key numbers
- DM capture rate for a neutron star: \( C_{\chi} \simeq 1.8 \times 10^{25} \left(\frac{1\, \rm GeV}{m_{\chi}}\right)\left(\frac{\rho_{\chi}}{\rho_{\chi,0}}\right) f_{\chi}\,\,\rm s^{-1} \), where \(\rho_{\chi,0}\simeq 0.3\) GeV/cm³ is the local DM density and \(f_{\chi}\) depends on the scattering cross-section relative to the geometrical cross-section \(\sigma_0 \sim 10^{-45}\) cm².
- The geometrical cross-section for DM capture in a neutron star is \(\sigma_0 = \frac{m_n R_{\rm NS}^2}{M_{\rm NS}} \sim 10^{-45}\) cm².
- For a typical progenitor of a compact star, the initial DM population is estimated as \( N_{\chi,0} \sim 10^{39} (\frac{m_\chi}{1\, \rm GeV}) \), assuming local DM densities at galactic distances of \(\sim 2\) kpc where \(\rho_{\chi}\sim 10^2 \rho_{\chi,0}\).
- The Chandrasekhar number for fermionic DM is \( N_{\rm Ch} \sim (M_{\rm Pl}/m_{\chi})^3 \sim 1.8 \times 10^{54}\,(\rm1 \,TeV/m_{\chi})^3 \).
- Nuclear saturation density is \( n_0 \simeq 0.17 \) fm⁻³, corresponding to a mass density \(\rho_0 \simeq n_0 m_N \simeq 2.4\times 10^{14}\) g/cm³.
- Outer crust densities range from \(\rho \simeq 2 \times 10^6\) to \(4 \times 10^{11}\) g/cm³.
- For DM particles with masses larger than \(\sim 2\) keV, evaporation effects can be ignored.
- The DM mean free path is calculated for kinetic energies up to \(E_\chi - m_\chi \approx 130\) MeV at \(T=0\) and \(n=n_0\) for \(m_\chi=0.5\) GeV.
- At \(T=0\) and \(n=n_0\), the simplified estimate neglecting medium corrections gives \(\lambda_\chi \simeq 1/\sigma_{\chi N} n \sim 5.9\) m assuming \(\sigma_{\chi N} \sim 10^{-41}\) cm².
- The Debye temperature for a bcc lattice is \(T_D \simeq 0.45 T_p\), with plasma temperature \(T_p = \omega_p / k_{\rm B} = \sqrt{\frac{4 \pi n_A Z^2 e^2}{k_{\rm B}^2 m_A}}\).
- The Maxwell-Juttner parameter for relativistic DM particles is \(\mu = m_\chi / (k_B T) \approx 6.7\) for \(\sqrt{\langle v^2 \rangle} \sim 0.6\).

## Context
This work reviews and extends prior studies on DM capture in neutron stars by Gould (1987), Kouvaris (2008), and Guver (2014), and incorporates in-medium scattering calculations from Bertoni (2013) and the authors' own previous work (Cermeno 2016a, 2016b). It also connects to studies of DM-induced quark deconfinement and neutron star to quark star conversion by Perez-Garcia and Silk (2014, 2015). The review enables future calculations of thermal conductivity and cooling behavior in neutron stars with a dark sector component, particularly by quantifying how DM scattering in the crust produces phonons that modify ion thermal transport, an effect previously considered only for neutrinos by Ferreras (2005).
