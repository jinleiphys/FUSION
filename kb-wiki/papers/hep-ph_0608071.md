# QCD glueball sum rules and vacuum topology

> Several key problems of QCD sum rules in the spin-0 glueball channels are resolved by implementing nonperturbative short-distance physics from direct instantons and topological charge screening. A lattice-based instanton size distribution and the IR renormalization of the nonperturbative Wilson coefficients are also introduced. Results of a comprehensive quantitative sum rule analysis are reviewed and their implications discussed.

# QCD Glueball Sum Rules and Vacuum Topology

## Key claim
Nonperturbative short-distance physics from direct instantons and topological charge screening resolves long-standing consistency problems of QCD sum rules in the spin-0 glueball channels, but the results depend critically on using a lattice-based instanton size distribution rather than the oversimplified "spike" approximation, and on implementing gauge-invariant IR renormalization of the nonperturbative Wilson coefficients.

## Method
The author implements an instanton-improved operator product expansion (IOPE) for the scalar (0++) and pseudoscalar (0-+) glueball correlators, incorporating direct instanton contributions and topological charge screening into the Wilson coefficients. A lattice-based Gaussian-tail parametrization of the instanton size distribution is used, and gauge-invariant IR renormalization excludes large instantons with size above the factorization scale. The Borel-transformed IOPE is matched to dispersive representations containing resonance poles and a local-duality continuum, and the resulting sum rules are solved for glueball masses, decay constants, and threshold parameters.

## Key numbers
- Scalar glueball mass: m_S = 1.25 ± 0.2 GeV
- Scalar glueball decay constant: f_S = 1.05 ± 0.1 GeV
- Pseudoscalar glueball mass: m_P = 2.2 ± 0.2 GeV
- Pseudoscalar glueball decay constant: f_P = 0.6 ± 0.25 GeV
- Scalar glueball width: Γ_S ≳ 0.3 GeV
- LET-induced subtraction constant Π_S(0) ≃ 0.6 GeV^4
- Direct instanton contribution Π_{S/P}^{(I,Ī)}(0) = ±2^7 π^2 n̄_dir ≃ 0.63 GeV^4
- Pseudoscalar LET subtraction constant Π_P(0) ≃ -0.02 GeV^4
- Topological screening contribution Π_P^{(scr)}(0) ≃ 0.59 GeV^4
- Screening length λ_D ∼ m_{η'}^{-1} ∼ 0.2 fm
- Quenched lattice result for scalar decay constant: f_S = 0.86 ± 0.18 GeV (Chen 2005)
- Instanton liquid model prediction: f_S^{(ILM)} = 0.8 GeV (Schaefer 1995)

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis by Forkel (2001) which first showed that direct instantons solve key problems in the scalar glueball channel, but replaces the oversimplified "spike" approximation for the instanton size distribution with a realistic lattice-based parametrization. It introduces for the first time topological charge screening contributions to the OPE in the 0-+ channel, resolving inconsistencies found by Zhang (2003). The results are consistent with quenched lattice results (Ishii 2002) and the instanton liquid model (Schaefer 1995), and the scalar decay constant prediction was subsequently confirmed by the first direct quenched lattice result (Chen 2005). The work enables improved predictions for radiative J/ψ and Υ decays into scalar glueballs relevant for experimental searches at CLEO and BES.
