# Low--Energy Kaon--Nucleon Interactions and Scattering at DA$Φ$NE

> We present in this contribution the basic formulæ for the analysis of low--momentum charged-- and neutral--kaon interactions in hydrogen, including as well a (brief) description of the problems left open by past experiments, and of the improvements DA$Φ$NE can be expected to offer over them. Interactions in deuterium and other light nuclei will be only briefly mentioned, and only in those respects touching directly upon the more ``elementary'' aspects of kaon--nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that DAΦNE, as the cleanest and lowest-energy kaon source ever built, can substantially improve knowledge of low-energy kaon-nucleon parameters, but this improvement is conditional on resolving ambiguities in separating isospin channels and on obtaining high-statistics measurements of P-wave contributions, which past experiments with extremely low statistics have failed to determine.

## Method

The author presents the amplitude formalism for two-body KN and anti-KN interactions using Pauli spinor amplitudes G and H, expanding them in partial waves with Coulomb corrections. The formalism is extended to a Klein-Gordon multichannel wave equation approach with complex potentials S(r) and U(r) to describe both scattering and kaonic hydrogen atomic levels, and a K-matrix (M-matrix) formalism is introduced to couple the dominant two-body inelastic channels to the elastic channel.

## Key numbers

- DAΦNE expected to produce about 1.25 × 10^10 K± and 8.5 × 10^9 K_L^0 per year (Snowmass year of 10^7 s)
- Momentum resolution: Δp/p of 1.1 × 10^(-2) for K± and 1.5 × 10^(-2) for K_L^0
- Kaon momenta: 127 MeV/c for K± (minus energy losses) and 110 MeV/c for K_L^0
- Accessible momentum range: 90 MeV/c ≤ p_lab ≤ 120 MeV/c
- c.m. energy available: 1442.4 MeV for incident K± and 1443.8 MeV for incident K_L^0
- K^-p charge-exchange threshold at w = 1437.2 MeV, corresponding to K^- lab momentum about 90 MeV/c
- K^- momentum range at DAΦNE: from about 59 to about 78 MeV/c
- Λ(1405) S-wave resonance below threshold at w = 1384 MeV (I=1, J^P=3/2^+)
- Σ(1385) P-wave resonance at w = 1384 MeV (I=1, J^P=3/2^+)
- Branching ratios from past data: 0.9 × 10^(-3) for K^-p → γΛ and 1.4 × 10^(-3) for K^-p → γΣ^0, with errors of order 15%
- Expected rates for radiative capture: about 10^4 events/year (two orders of magnitude above best previous experiment on Λ(1405) decay spectrum)

## Context

This work extends the low-energy kaon-nucleon phenomenology developed for pion-nucleon systems, addressing problems left open by past bubble-chamber experiments from the 1960s-1970s (TST Collaboration at NIMROD, experiments by Davies et al.) and kaonic hydrogen X-ray measurements that produced ambiguous results on hadronic shifts. The paper enables future experiments at DAΦNE to measure P-wave contributions, resolve the Λ(1405) spectroscopic classification, determine Σ(1385) couplings to the anti-KN channel (currently uncertain to 50% of the flavour-SU(3) value), and measure radiative capture cross sections to test cloudy-bag-model predictions.
