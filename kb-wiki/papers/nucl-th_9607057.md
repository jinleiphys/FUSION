# Behavior of the giant-dipole resonance in $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb at high excitation energ

> The properties of the giant-dipole resonance (GDR) are calculated as a function of excitation energy, angular momentum, and the compound nucleus particle decay width in the nuclei $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb, and are compared with recent experimental data. Differences observed in the behavior of the full-width-at-half-maximum of the GDR for $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb are attributed to the fact that shell corrections in $^{208}$Pb are stronger than in $^{120}$Sn, and favor the spherical shape at low temperatures. The effects shell corrections have on both the free energy and the moments of inertia are discussed in detail. At high temperature, the FWHM in $^{120}$Sn exhibits effects due to the evaporation width of the compound nucleus, while these effects are predicted for $^{208}$Pb.

# Behavior of the giant-dipole resonance in $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb at high excitation energy

## Key claim

The differences in the full-width-at-half-maximum (FWHM) of the giant-dipole resonance between $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb as a function of temperature are attributed to stronger shell corrections in $^{208}$Pb that favor the spherical shape at low temperatures, while at high temperature the FWHM in $^{120}$Sn exhibits effects due to the evaporation width of the compound nucleus, with similar effects predicted for $^{208}$Pb.

## Method

The authors calculated the GDR cross section using a thermal averaging over nuclear shapes and orientations, projecting angular momentum via a formalism that avoids the saddle-point instability of fixed-rotational-frequency methods. The GDR was modeled as a quantal, rotating harmonic oscillator with three principal modes, and the nuclear free energy was computed using the Nilsson-Strutinsky procedure extended to finite temperature, with shell corrections parameterized by spherical Bessel functions. The intrinsic dipole widths were taken as energy-dependent via $\Gamma_\nu = \Gamma_0 (E_\nu/E_0)^\delta$ with $\delta \approx 1.8$, and the intrinsic width $\Gamma_0$ was assumed independent of temperature.

## Key numbers

- For $^{120}$Sn: ground-state GDR centroid $E_0 = 14.99$ MeV and width $\Gamma_0 = 5.0$ MeV.
- For $^{208}$Pb: ground-state GDR centroid $E_0 = 13.65$ MeV and width $\Gamma_0 = 4.0$ MeV.
- Shell corrections in $^{208}$Pb at $T=0$ MeV are approximately $-14$ MeV, strongly favoring the spherical shape.
- Shell corrections in $^{120}$Sn are small (a few hundred keV at $T \sim 1.25$ MeV) and essentially negligible.
- For angular momentum $J \leq 25\hbar$, the FWHM is essentially unchanged from the $J=0\hbar$ value at $T=1.6$ MeV.
- The largest average angular momentum in the experimental systems studied is of order $20\hbar$.
- Temperatures inferred from experiment are uncertain at the level of approximately 0.2 MeV due to sensitivity to the level-density parameter.
- Shell corrections in $^{208}$Pb melt (approach zero) for temperatures of order $T = 2.5$ MeV.
- The parameterized shell corrections for $^{208}$Pb underestimate Nilsson-Strutinsky values by a few hundred keV for $T > 1.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic, large-amplitude thermal fluctuation framework for the GDR in hot nuclei (Alhassid 1993, Alhassid and Bush 1993, Alhassid and Bush 1994) by implementing a proper angular momentum projection method that avoids the saddle-point instability of the fixed-rotational-frequency approach. The calculations are compared with experimental data from inelastic alpha scattering (Thoennessen 1995) and provide a systematic analysis separating the roles of shell corrections, angular momentum, and compound nucleus lifetime. The shell correction effect and angular momentum dependence were also observed for $^{140}$Ce in Alhassid and Bush (1994). The results enable future investigation of motional narrowing effects, which would require both the response function and the angular distribution $a_2$ coefficient to differentiate between adiabatic and non-adiabatic regimes.
