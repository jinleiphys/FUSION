# Xi production at AGS energies

> A relativistic transport model is used to study Xi- production in 2-11A GeV Au+Au collisions. Introducing the strangeness-exchange reactions between antikaons and hyperons as the sources for Xi-, we find that the cascade yield in these collisions is in reasonable agreement with the data. Although the Xi- abundance does not reach chemical equilibrium unless the cross section for strangeness-exchange reactions is enhanced by six times, it exhibits the strongest enhancement with increasing centrality of collision and with increasing beam energy.

## Key claim

Strangeness-exchange reactions between antikaons and hyperons in a purely hadronic transport model can explain the measured $\Xi^-$ yield in 6A GeV Au+Au collisions, but the $\Xi^-$ abundance does not reach chemical equilibrium unless the cross section for these reactions is artificially enhanced by a factor of six.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic hadronic transport model ART (A Relativistic Transport model), which includes baryons (N, Δ, N*, Λ, Σ) and mesons (π, ρ, ω, η, K, K*), and extended it perturbatively to include the $\Xi$ particle. The $\Xi^-$ production was modeled via the strangeness-exchange reactions $\bar K \Lambda \to \pi \Xi$ and $\bar K \Sigma \to \pi \Xi$, using cross sections from a gauged flavor SU(3)-invariant Lagrangian in a coupled-channel approach, with the inverse reactions obtained from detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- The $\Xi^-$ yield from strangeness-exchange reactions in the ART model is in reasonable agreement with the E895 data for Au+Au collisions at 6A GeV.
- The $\Xi^-$ yield grows by a factor of 6 from peripheral to central collisions at 6A GeV, while the $\Lambda+\Sigma^0$ yield is enhanced by a factor of 4.
- The $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ ratio from the ART model is 0.026 for impact parameter b=0-3 fm, compared to an equilibrium model estimate of 0.042.
- At midrapidity (|y|<0.5), the $\Xi^-/(\Lambda+\Sigma^0)$ ratio is 0.03.
- The $\Xi^-$ yield approaches chemical equilibrium only when the strangeness-exchange cross section is enhanced by six times, giving $N_{\Xi^-} = 0.55$ per event.
- The $\Xi^-$ freezes out at a mean time of 8.4 fm/c and transverse radius of 4.6 fm, when the central energy density is about $\epsilon \approx 1$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- The $K^-/K^+$ ratio is 0.09 and independent of collision centrality for Au+Au collisions at 6A GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic transport approach previously applied to singly-strange kaons and lambdas at AGS energies (Abbot, Bear, and transport model references) to the doubly-strange $\Xi^-$ particle. It contrasts with findings at SPS energies, where the same strangeness-exchange reactions in transport models (Pal et al., 2001) underpredicted the WA97 data for $\Xi$ production in central Pb+Pb collisions, suggesting a possible QGP signal at those higher energies. The success of the purely hadronic scenario at AGS energies eliminates the need for exotic production mechanisms (such as QGP formation) to explain the measured $\Xi$ yield at these lower beam energies, and enables further study of chemical equilibration and strangeness enhancement as a function of collision energy.
