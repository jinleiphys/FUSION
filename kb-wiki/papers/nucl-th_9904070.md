# Correlator mixing and mass reduction as signals of chiral symmetry restoration

> Chiral symmetry restoration in a dense medium is to some extent a consequence of the nuclear pion cloud. These pions induce a mixing of the axial and vector current contributions in the axial and vector correlators. We discuss their influence on hadron masses and investigate the signal produced by the remaining contribution associated with chiral symmetry restoration. Using the quark-meson coupling model we find that the latter is associated with the reduction of hadron masses.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter has two distinct components: one arising from the pion cloud (which produces correlator mixing but negligible mass shifts) and one of non-pionic origin (which produces mass reduction proportional to the non-pionic part of the condensate evolution). The paper argues that within the quark-meson coupling model, the mass reduction follows only the non-pionic piece of the condensate evolution, contrary to scale-invariance arguments that do not distinguish between the two origins.

## Method

The authors use the cloudy bag model, made chirally symmetric by coupling the confining surface term to a pion field, and then introduce a chiral-singlet sigma field coupled to the transformed quark field Q (which is approximately independent of the soft pion content). They derive expressions for the nucleon sigma commutator, separating it into a pionic contribution from the average squared pion field and a non-pionic remainder, and relate the nucleon mass change in nuclear matter to the non-pionic part of the condensate evolution using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density, the quark condensate has decreased by about 35%.
- The nucleon mass change from the QMC model at normal nuclear matter density is approximately -200 MeV.
- The corresponding mass change for vector mesons at normal nuclear matter density is of order -140 MeV.
- The condensate evolution to first order in nucleon scalar density is given by: <ψ̅ψ(ρ_s)> / <ψ̅ψ(0)> = 1 - Σ_N ρ_s / (f_π² m_π²).

## Context

This work extends the concept of axial-vector correlator mixing from heat baths (Dey et al., 1990) to dense nuclear matter via the nuclear pion cloud (Chanfray et al., 1992). It contrasts with the scale-invariance approach of Brown and Rho (1991) which suggested hadron mass reduction linked to the full condensate evolution without distinguishing pionic and non-pionic contributions. The results enable a clearer interpretation of experimental signals for partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, separating correlator mixing effects from genuine mass shifts.
