# Using 1-Jettiness to Measure 2 Jets in DIS 3 Ways

> We predict cross sections in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) for the production of two jets---one along the proton beam direction created by initial state radiation (ISR) and another created by final state radiation after the hard collision. Our results include fixed order corrections and a summation of large logarithms up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy in resummed perturbation theory. We make predictions for three versions of a DIS event shape 1-jettiness, each of which constrains hadronic final states to be well collimated into two jets along the beam and final-state jet directions, but which differ in their sensitivity to the transverse momentum of the ISR from the proton beam. We use the tools of soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive factorization theorems for these three versions of 1-jettiness. The sensitivity to the ISR gives rise to significantly different structures in the corresponding factorization theorems---for example, dependence on either the ordinary or the generalized kperp-dependent beam function. Despite the differences among 1-jettiness definitions, we show that the leading nonperturbative correction that shifts the tail region of their distributions is given by a single universal nonperturbative parameter Omega1, even accounting for hadron mass effects. Finally, we give numerical results for Q^2 and x values explored at the HERA collider, emphasizing that the target of our factorization-based analyses is to open the door for higher-precision jet phenomenology in DIS.

## Key claim

The paper predicts cross sections for three versions of the DIS 1-jettiness event shape at NNLL accuracy, showing that despite different sensitivities to the transverse momentum of initial-state radiation, the leading nonperturbative correction to the tail region is given by a single universal parameter Omega1 for all three definitions.

## Method

The authors used soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive factorization theorems for three versions of the 1-jettiness event shape in deep inelastic scattering, each differing in how the jet axis and beam direction are defined. They performed renormalization group evolution to resum large logarithms to NNLL accuracy, and analyzed the nonperturbative corrections through a soft shape function, demonstrating universality of the leading shift parameter Omega1 across all three observables.

## Key numbers

- The resummation is performed up to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy in resummed perturbation theory
- The leading nonperturbative correction is given by a single universal parameter Omega1, a dimension-1 parameter defined by a matrix element of a soft Wilson line operator
- Numerical results are given for Q^2 and x values explored at the HERA collider, with Q^2 defined as the positive definite quantity from the virtual boson momentum transfer
- The invariant mass of the final state is given by p_X^2 = (1-x)/x * Q^2, where x is the Bjorken scaling variable ranging from 0 to 1

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based resummation techniques previously applied to e+e- event shapes (such as thrust at N^3LL by Becher (2008) and heavy jet mass by Chien (2010)) to DIS event shapes, going beyond the NLL accuracy achieved for DIS thrust by Antonelli (1999). The paper also generalizes the N-jettiness formalism introduced by Stewart (2010) to DIS with three distinct definitions, and enables higher-precision jet phenomenology in DIS that could be applied to data from HERA, Jefferson Lab, the future Electron-Ion Collider, and the Large Hadron Electron Collider.
