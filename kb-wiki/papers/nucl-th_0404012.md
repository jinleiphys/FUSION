# Cronin Effect at Different Rapidities at RHIC

> Calculations of the nuclear modification factor, R_dAu, for pi^0 production in dAu collisions at s_NN^1/2 = 200 GeV are presented. The applied pQCD-improved parton model incorporates intrinsic k_T. Nuclear multiscattering and nuclear shadowing are considered in the Au nucleus. Theoretical results are displayed for midrapidity and high pseudorapidity (eta), and compared to preliminary PHENIX and BRAHMS data.

# Cronin Effect at Different Rapidities at RHIC

## Key claim

The pQCD-improved parton model with intrinsic $k_T$, nuclear multiscattering (Cronin effect), and conventional (HIJING) nuclear shadowing can reproduce the measured nuclear modification factor $R_{dAu}$ for pion production in $dAu$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV across multiple rapidities, without requiring extra gluon saturation (CGC) effects at high $p_T$; however, the precise energy and rapidity dependence of the multiscattering parameters $\nu_m$ and $C$ is not yet verified at RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD calculations of $\pi^0$ production in $dAu$ collisions using a parton model extended with Glauber-type collision geometry, incorporating nuclear thickness functions (Woods-Saxon for Au, sharp sphere for deuteron). The parton distribution functions (MRST(cg)) were combined with a 2-dimensional Gaussian intrinsic $k_T$ distribution, where nuclear multiscattering was modeled as a broadening of the $k_T$ width via $\langle k_T^2 \rangle_{pA} = \langle k_T^2 \rangle_{pp} + \Delta(b)$, with $\Delta(b) = C \cdot h_{pA}(b)$, and conventional nuclear shadowing was included using the HIJING parametrization.

## Key numbers

- $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV: center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair for $dAu$ collisions at RHIC
- $\langle k_T^2 \rangle_{pp} = 2.5$ GeV$^2$: intrinsic $k_T$ width for $pp$ collisions at RHIC energy, determined from pion production data
- $C = 0.35$ GeV$^2$: average transverse momentum squared imparted per effective $NN$ collision, taken from SPS energy studies
- $\nu_m - 1 = 4$: limited number of semihard collisions assumed, implying total broadening $\Delta \sim 1$ GeV$^2$
- $p_T$ range: $2 < p_T < 10$ GeV for the calculations
- Centrality bins studied: $0-20\%$, $20-40\%$, $40-60\%$, and $60-88\%$ for midrapidity data
- Pseudorapidities studied: $\eta = 0$, $1$, $2.2$, and $3.2$ for forward rapidity comparisons

## Context

This work extends earlier pQCD calculations of the Cronin effect in $pA$ collisions at FERMILAB energies (Cronin 1975, Antreasyan 1979) to RHIC energies and forward rapidities, building on the authors' previous NLO pQCD framework (Barnafoldi 2002). The results are compared to preliminary PHENIX data at midrapidity and BRAHMS data at forward rapidities, and the approach contrasts with Color Glass Condensate (CGC) models that predict extra gluon saturation at forward rapidities; the pQCD description with conventional shadowing and multiscattering provides reasonable agreement without such extra suppression at high $p_T$, though the most peripheral centrality bin shows a discrepancy requiring further study.
