# Exotic tetraquarks at the HL-LHC with JETHAD: A high-energy viewpoint

> We review the semi-inclusive hadroproduction of a neutral hidden-flavor tetraquark with light and heavy quark flavor at the HL-LHC, accompanied by another heavy hadron or a light-flavored jet. We make use of the novel TQHL1.0 determinations of leading-twist fragmentation functions to describe the formation mechanism of a tetraquark state within the next-to-leading order perturbative QCD. This framework builds on the basis of a spin-physics inspired model, taken as a proxy for the lowest-scale input of the constituent heavy-quark fragmentation channel. Then, all parton-to-tetraquark fragmentation functions are consistently obtained via the above-threshold DGLAP evolution in a variable-flavor number scheme. We provide predictions for a series of differential distributions calculated by the hands of the JETHAD method well-adapted to NLL/NLO+ hybrid-factorization studies, where the resummation of next-to-leading energy logarithms and beyond is included in the collinear picture. We provide corroborating evidence that high-energy observables sensitive to semi-inclusive tetraquark emissions at the HL-LHC exhibit a fair stability under radiative corrections as well as MHOU studies. Our analysis constitutes a prime contact point between QCD resummations and the exotic matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semi-inclusive hadroproduction of neutral hidden-flavor tetraquarks at the HL-LHC, calculated within the NLL/NLO+ hybrid-factorization framework using the JETHAD method, exhibits fair stability under radiative corrections and missing higher-order uncertainty studies, but this stability is conditional on the use of variable-flavor number scheme fragmentation functions and is an intrinsic feature associated with final states sensitive to heavy flavor.

## Method

The author employs the NLL/NLO+ hybrid-factorization formalism, which combines collinear factorization with the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) resummation of next-to-leading energy logarithms, implemented via the JETHAD method. The formation mechanism of heavy-light tetraquarks (X_cu, X_cs, X_bu, X_bs) is described using the novel TQHL1.0 set of leading-twist fragmentation functions, obtained by performing next-to-leading order DGLAP evolution in a variable-flavor number scheme starting from an initial-scale input derived from the spin-physics inspired SNAJ model. Predictions are made for differential distributions of tetraquark production in association with a singly heavy-flavored hadron (H_c or H_b) or a light jet at the HL-LHC.

## Key numbers

- Tetraquark masses are set to m_X = 2(m_q + m_Q), where m_q is the light constituent quark mass and m_Q is the heavy constituent quark mass
- A two-loop running coupling with initial condition alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.11707 is used
- The BFKL kernel at the exponent includes the LO eigenvalues chi(n,nu) = -2*gamma_E - 2*Re{psi((1+n)/2 + i*nu)} and the NLO kernel correction with the beta_0 coefficient beta_0 = 11*N_c/3 - 2*n_f/3
- The transverse mass of the light-flavored jet coincides with its transverse momentum |kappa_J|

## Context

This work extends the high-energy QCD resummation program to exotic tetraquark states, building on previous studies of semi-hard reactions featuring singly heavy-flavored hadrons (Celiberto 2021) and vector quarkonia (Celiberto 2022, 2023). The natural stability of the high-energy resummation observed here was first identified in studies of Lambda_c hyperons (Celiberto 2021) and singly bottomed hadrons (Celiberto 2021), and the analysis constitutes a prime contact point between QCD resummations and exotic matter, enabling future precision studies of tetraquark production mechanisms at the HL-LHC.
