# Moments Method for Shell-Model Level Density

> The modern form of the Moments Method applied to the calculation of the nuclear shell-model level density is explained and examples of the method at work are given. The calculated level density practically exactly coincides with the result of full diagonalization when the latter is feasible. The method provides the pure level density for given spin and parity with spurious center-of-mass excitations subtracted. The presence and interplay of all correlations leads to the results different from those obtained by the mean-field combinatorics.

# Moments Method for Shell-Model Level Density

## Key claim
The Moments Method can reproduce the shell-model level density for fixed spin and parity with spurious center-of-mass excitations subtracted, yielding results that "practically exactly" coincide with full diagonalization when that is feasible. However, the method relies on the assumption that each partition's level density is close to Gaussian, defined only by its first two moments (centroid and width), with an empirical cutoff parameter eta between 2.5 and 3 to eliminate unphysical tails.

## Method
The method partitions the many-body basis by configuration (partition), each defined by a distribution of non-interacting particles over spherical orbitals. For each partition with given quantum numbers (spin, parity, isospin projection), the trace of the Hamiltonian and its square are computed to obtain the energy centroid and width (dispersion). The total level density is then reconstructed by summing finite-range Gaussians for each partition, weighted by the number of states in that partition. Spurious center-of-mass excitations are removed using recurrence relations that subtract states generated by the center-of-mass vector operator acting on lower-N-shell states.

## Key numbers
- The empirical cutoff parameter eta for the finite-range Gaussian is fixed between 2.5 and 3, based on theoretical arguments and experience.
- For $^{64}$Ge, level densities for $J^\pi = 0^+$ and $2^+$ are calculated in both $pf$-shell and $pf+g_{9/2}$ model spaces, with the larger space results only slightly exceeding those of the smaller space.
- For $^{26}$Al, the total level density is calculated in the $s$-$p$-$sd$-$fp$ model space restricted to $1\hbar\omega$ excitations, and compared to experimental data with "optimistic" and "pessimistic" uncertainty estimates.
- For $^{28}$Si in the $sd$ model space with USDB interaction, the scaling of pairing ($f_p$) and non-pairing ($f$) interaction components is studied, showing that pairing alone smooths partition irregularities when treated exactly rather than in BCS or HFB schemes.

## Context
This work extends the statistical spectroscopy approach of Wong (1986) and Kota (2010) by implementing the Moments Method with explicit spin and parity projection and spurious-state subtraction via recurrence relations from Jacquemin (1981) and Van Isacker (2002). It contrasts with mean-field combinatorial approaches such as those of Goriely (2008, 2009) and the Hilaire tables (nld2), which the authors find produce much higher and less smooth level densities. The method enables calculation of level densities in model spaces too large for full diagonalization, and the authors suggest future work to extract global empirical parameters (temperature, spin cut-off) and to extend calculations to larger many-shell spaces through parallelization.
