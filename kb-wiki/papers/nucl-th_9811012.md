# Solar neutrino processes in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

> The generalized version of the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) is applied to the description of processes of astrophysical interest and low-energy elastic NN scattering. The value of the astrophysical factor S_{pp}(0) = 5.52x10^{-25} MeV b is found to be enhanced by a factor of 1.42 with respect to the classical value S^*_{pp}(0) = 3.89x10{-25} MeV b obtained by Kamionkowski and Bahcall in the potential model approach (PMA). The astrophysical aspects of this enhancement are discussed. The cross sections for the disintegration of the deuteron by (anti-) neutrinos nu_e + D -> e^- + p + p, anti-nu_e + D -> e^+ + n + n and nu_e(anti-nu_e) + D -> nu_e(anti-nu_e) + n + p are calculated for the energies of (anti-) neutrinos ranging from thresholds up to 10 MeV. The results are discussed in comparison with the PMA data. The cross sections for anti-nu_e + D -> e^+ + n + n and anti-nu_e + D -> anti-nu_e + n + p averaged over the reactor anti-neutrino energy spectrum agree well with experimental data. The astrophysical factor S_{pep}(0) for the process p + e^- + p -> nu_e + D (or pep-process) is calculated relative to S_{pp}(0) in complete agreement with the result obtained by Bahcall and May. The reaction rate for the neutron-proton radiative capture is calculated in agreement with the PMA result obtained for pure M1 transition. It is shown that in the RFMD one can describe low--energy elastic NN scattering in complete agreement with low-energy nuclear phenomenology.

## Key claim

The generalized relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) predicts an enhancement of the astrophysical factor for solar proton burning by a factor of 1.42 relative to the classical potential model approach (PMA) result of Kamionkowski and Bahcall, but only after incorporating Coulomb repulsion between protons through an explicit Coulomb wave function and replacing the delta-function interaction with a smeared Yukawa potential.

## Method

The authors generalized the RFMD by replacing the local delta-function four-nucleon interaction with a smeared Yukawa potential defined by one-pion exchange, allowing the inclusion of explicit Coulomb wave functions for the relative motion of two protons. They then computed one-nucleon loop diagrams for the solar proton burning process p + p → D + e⁺ + νₑ, the pep-process p + e⁻ + p → νₑ + D, neutrino and antineutrino disintegration of the deuteron (νₑ + D → e⁻ + p + p, \(\bar{ν}_e\) + D → e⁺ + n + n, and νₑ(\(\bar{ν}_e\)) + D → νₑ(\(\bar{ν}_e\)) + n + p), neutron-proton radiative capture, and low-energy elastic NN scattering, using effective coupling constants derived from experimental S-wave scattering lengths for np, nn, and pp channels.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical factor for solar proton burning: \(S_{pp}(0) = 5.52 \times 10^{-25}\) MeV b, enhanced by a factor of 1.42 relative to the classical PMA value \(S^*_{pp}(0) = 3.89 \times 10^{-25}\) MeV b from Kamionkowski and Bahcall
- Effective coupling constants: \(G_{\pi np} = 3.27 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(G_{\pi nn} = 3.02 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(G_{\pi pp} = 3.04 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), calculated from scattering lengths \(a_{np} = (-23.748 \pm 0.010)\) fm, \(a_{nn} = (-16.40 \pm 0.09)\) fm, \(a_{pp} = (-17.10 \pm 0.20)\) fm
- Factor \(\mathcal{F}^e_{pp} = 1.78\) appearing in the amplitude of solar proton burning, computed from an integral involving the Yukawa potential and Coulomb wave function
- Theoretical uncertainty of the RFMD is not more than 9.5% for amplitudes and 19% for cross sections
- Cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino disintegration of the deuteron were calculated for energies from thresholds up to 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the RFMD previously developed by Ivanov, Oberhummer, Troitskaya, and Faber (1990s) by incorporating Coulomb repulsion between protons, which was absent in earlier versions that only used the Gamow penetration factor. The results contradict the critique by Bahcall and Kamionkowski that the RFMD cannot describe low-energy elastic NN scattering with non-zero effective range, and the authors show agreement with low-energy nuclear phenomenology. The calculated cross sections for deuteron disintegration by neutrinos and antineutrinos can be applied to the analysis of solar neutrino experiments at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), and the ratio \(S_{pep}(0)/S_{pp}(0)\) agrees completely with the result obtained by Bahcall and May.
