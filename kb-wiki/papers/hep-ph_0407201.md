# Observations on dA scattering at forward rapidities

> We point out that the suppression in the ratio R_dAu recently observed by the BRAHMS collaboration in forward scattering is stronger than usually appreciated. This is related to the fact that at forward rapidities BRAHMS measures negatively charged hadrons and that R_dAu is defined from the ratio of dA and pp scattering cross sections. We also investigate the influence of standard shadowing on R_dAu, and the typical values of partonic momentum fractions relevant in forward scattering. We find that x_Au > 0.02 dominate in the cross section.

## Key claim

The suppression in the ratio \(R_{\mathrm{dAu}}\) observed by BRAHMS at forward rapidities is actually stronger than usually appreciated, because BRAHMS measures negatively charged hadrons (\(h^-\)) at those rapidities, and the ratio is defined using \(pp\) scattering cross sections in the denominator. The authors show that the \(pp\to h^- X\) cross section is intrinsically smaller than the \(pp\to (h^++h^-)/2\) cross section, so the observed suppression in \(R_{\mathrm{dAu}}\) for \(h^-\) is more dramatic than it would be for charge-averaged hadrons.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations of inclusive hadron production in \(pp\) and \(d\mathrm{Au}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, using CTEQ6M parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions from Kretzer (2000) and KKP (2000). They computed the distribution of the cross section in bins of \(\log_{10}(x_2)\) (the parton momentum fraction in the gold nucleus) and the average values \(\langle x_1\rangle\), \(\langle x_2\rangle\), and \(\langle z\rangle\) as functions of transverse momentum and rapidity. They also incorporated leading-twist nuclear shadowing using the nPDF parameterization of Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (2003, 2004) and considered an alternative "shadowing 2" scenario with extended gluon shadowing up to \(x=0.1\).

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(p_T=1.5\) GeV, and \(\eta=3.2\), the distribution of the \(pp\to \pi^0 X\) cross section in \(\log_{10}(x_2)\) peaks at \(x_2 > 0.01\), with average \(\langle x_2\rangle\) in the range 0.03–0.05.
- The kinematic lower limit for \(x_2\) at \(\eta=3.2\) and \(p_T=1.5\) GeV is \(x_2^{\mathrm min} \approx 3.5\times 10^{-4}\), but contributions from such small \(x_2\) are highly suppressed.
- For \(p_T=5\) GeV at \(\eta=3.2\), the distribution in \(x_2\) is shifted to larger values, with \(\langle x_2\rangle\) approaching unity as the phase-space boundary is approached.
- Using the "shadowing 1" nPDF set, the ratio \(R_{\mathrm{dA}}\) for negatively charged hadrons at \(\eta=3.2\) and \(p_T\sim 3\) GeV is enhanced by about a factor of 1.5 compared to the ratio for summed charged hadrons at the same kinematics.
- Leading-twist nuclear shadowing alone gives at most a 15% suppression in \(R_{\mathrm{dA}}\) for summed charged hadrons at forward rapidities.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vogt (2004) by providing full NLO calculations and explicitly accounting for the isospin effect that enhances \(R_{\mathrm{dA}}\) for negatively charged hadrons, which was not considered in prior studies by the Color Glass Condensate models (2003), Qiu and Vitev (2003), or Vogt (2004). The finding that average \(x_2\) values in the gold nucleus are not very small (\(x_{\mathrm{Au}} \ge 0.02\) dominate) sets severe limitations on the ability of any initial-state small-\(x\) effects, such as saturation or shadowing, to explain the observed suppression. The authors note that preliminary BRAHMS data showing \(\sigma(d\mathrm{Au}\to h^+X)\) about three times larger than \(\sigma(d\mathrm{Au}\to h^-X)\) at \(p_T=3\) GeV indicate sizable nonperturbative contributions, possibly related to proton production via coalescence mechanisms discussed by Berera (1996) and Dumitru (2004).
