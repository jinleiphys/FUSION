# Parity-violating $πN N$ coupling constant in the chiral quark-soliton model

> We investigate the parity-violating $πNN$ Yukawa coupling constant $h^1_{πNN}$ within the framework of the SU(2) chiral quark-soliton model, based on the $ΔS=0$ effective weak Lagrangian derived within the same framework. We find that the parity-violating $πNN$ coupling constant is about $1\times10^{-8}$ at the scale of 1 GeV. The results of $h_{πNN}^1$ turn out to be sensitive to the Wilson coefficient. We discuss how the gluonic renormalization supresses the parity-violating $πNN$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The parity-violating $\pi NN$ coupling constant $h^1_{\pi NN}$ is found to be about $1\times10^{-8}$ at the scale of 1 GeV, but this result is sensitive to the Wilson coefficient $K$ and can even change sign around $K=6$, indicating that the gluonic renormalization strongly suppresses the coupling constant.

## Method

The authors compute $h^1_{\pi NN}$ within the SU(2) chiral quark-soliton model ($\chi$QSM) using the $\Delta S=0$ effective weak chiral Lagrangian derived in Lee (2004). Instead of solving the full four-point correlation function numerically, they employ a gradient expansion and collective quantization of the soliton, extracting the coupling constant from the next-to-leading order (NLO) Lagrangian in the large $N_c$ expansion. Three different solitonic profile functions (arctangent, physical with pion tail, and linear) are used to examine the dependence on the profile.

## Key numbers

* $h^1_{\pi NN} \approx 1\times10^{-8}$ at the renormalization scale of 1 GeV, with the physical profile function.
* The sign of $h^1_{\pi NN}$ changes around Wilson coefficient $K = 6$.
* The finite pion mass contributes approximately $10\%$ to $h^1_{\pi NN}$, as seen by comparing the physical profile function result with the arctangent one.
* The constituent quark mass $M_0 = M(k=0)$ is taken as 350 MeV, fixed by the saddle-point equation from the instanton vacuum of Diakonov (1985).
* The pion decay constant is $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, and the axial coupling $g_A = 1.26$ gives $r_0 = 0.582$ fm for the arctangent profile; the physical profile has $r_x = 0.749$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model calculations of Kaiser (1989) and Meissner (1998) by incorporating the effects of perturbative quark-gluon strong interaction through the Wilson coefficient, which the earlier Skyrme model studies did not include. The leading-order contribution to $h^1_{\pi NN}$ vanishes in the $\chi$QSM, consistent with the null result of the minimal Skyrme model by Shmatikov (1989), but the NLO term yields a finite value. The results enable future SU(3) extensions to assess the role of strange quark operators, which Kaplan (1992) argued could contribute significantly, though recent lattice work by Wasem (2011) found such contributions too small to extract reliably.
