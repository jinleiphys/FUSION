# Charged particle fluctuations and microscopic models of nuclear collisions

> We study the event-by-event fluctuations of the charged particles and compare the results of different Monte-Carlo Generators (MCG): VNIb, HIJING, HIJING/B${\bar B}$ and RQMD. We find that the $D$-measure can be used to distinguish between the different gluon populations that are present in the MCG models. On the other hand, the value of the $D$-measure shows high sensitivity to the rescattering effects in VNIb model, but lower sensitivity to the rescattering effects in RQMD model. We also find that the $D$-measures from AA are consistent with the $D$-measures from $pp$ for all generators except VNIb. Therefore, any deviation among the values of $D$-measure for different impact parameters and between $pp$ and $AA$ collisions may indicate that either the rescattering effects play a key role in the interactions or there is new physics in $AA$ collisions.

# Charged particle fluctuations and microscopic models of nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The D-measure of charged particle fluctuations can distinguish between different gluon populations in Monte Carlo generators, but its value is highly sensitive to rescattering effects in some models (VNIb) while showing lower sensitivity in others (RQMD). The authors hedge that any deviation between D-measure values for different impact parameters or between pp and AA collisions may indicate either strong rescattering effects or new physics in AA collisions, rather than definitively signaling QGP.

## Method

The authors calculated the D-measure (defined as 4⟨δQ²⟩/⟨Nch⟩) using four Monte Carlo generators: VNIb (parton cascade with gluon population), RQMD v2.4 (hadronic transport with string formation), HIJING v1.35 (pQCD minijets with string fragmentation), and HIJING/B\bar{B} v1.10 (HIJING with baryon junction mechanism). They compared results for Au+Au central collisions at √s = 200A GeV across different rapidity windows, applying correction factors for global charge conservation and asymmetric charge ratios. They also studied rescattering effects by running VNIb and RQMD with rescattering turned on and off, and compared D-measure values between pp and AA collisions.

## Key numbers

- For VNIb with rescattering turned off, the corrected D-measure value is around 1.0 for a rapidity window of (-2, 2) at √s = 200A GeV
- The ratio of gluons to quarks and antiquarks in VNIb for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is approximately 1.2; excluding valence quarks gives a ratio of 1.8
- For RQMD at √s = 130A GeV, rescattering effects on the D-measure are within 10%
- For VNIb with rescattering turned on, D-measure values are around 2.3; with rescattering turned off, values are approximately 1.0
- The ratio ⟨Q_i⟩²/⟨n_i⟩ for pp collisions at 200 GeV is near zero for smaller rapidity windows and around 0.2 for the full rapidity window
- The STAR collaboration found D-measure value around 3 with no centrality dependence at √s_NN = 130 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of charge fluctuations as a QGP signature initiated by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2000), who proposed the D-measure would be approximately 1 for QGP and around 4 for a pion gas. It responds to criticisms by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (2000) regarding proton contributions and by Fialkowski and Wit (2000) regarding rapidity dependence, as well as the correction method proposed by Bleicher, Koch, and Jeon (2000) using UrQMD. The study enables experimental comparison with RHIC data from STAR, and suggests that impact parameter dependence of the D-measure must be measured to determine whether rescattering effects or new physics dominate in AA collisions.
