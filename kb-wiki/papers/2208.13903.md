# Bethe-Salpeter kernel and properties of strange-quark mesons

> Focusing on the continuum meson bound-state problem, a novel method is used to calculate closed-form Bethe-Salpeter kernels that are symmetry consistent with any reasonable gluon-quark vertex, $Γ_ν$, and therewith deliver a Poincaré-invariant treatment of the spectrum and decay constants of the ground- and first-excited states of $u$, $d$, $s$ mesons. The predictions include masses of as-yet unseen states and many unmeasured decay constants. The analysis reveals that a realistic, unified description of meson properties (including level orderings and mass splittings) requires a sound expression of emergent hadron mass in bound-state kernels; alternatively, that such properties may reveal much about the emergence of mass in the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a novel, symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel construction incorporating emergent hadron mass (EHM) effects via an anomalous chromomagnetic moment term can simultaneously describe ground- and first-excited-state masses and decay constants of mesons containing u, d, and s quarks, achieving a factor of 4.6 improvement over rainbow-ladder truncation in reproducing the empirical spectrum, contingent on tuning the EHM strength parameter η = 1.2 while adjusting the interaction strength D to maintain m_ρ = 0.77 GeV.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled quark gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states using a momentum-dependent interaction that retains only the infrared-dominant Gaussian term. They construct the Bethe-Salpeter kernel by enforcing Ward-Green-Takahashi identities for vector and axialvector vertices, generalizing the method of Qin (2020) to handle nondegenerate quark masses (u, d, s). The gap equation kernel includes an anomalous chromomagnetic moment term τ_ν(l) = η κ(l²)σ_{lν} with Gaussian form factor, and the corresponding Bethe-Salpeter kernel is completed by solving integral equations for scalar coefficient functions that depend on total momentum P².

## Key numbers

- Rainbow-ladder truncation yields mean absolute relative difference of 13(8)% between calculated and experimental meson masses; EHM-improved kernel reduces this to 2.9(2.7)%
- EHM-improved kernel predictions: m_π = 0.140 GeV (PDG: 0.138 GeV), m_K = 0.494 GeV (PDG: 0.494 GeV), m_ρ = 0.77 GeV (PDG: 0.775 GeV)
- First excited states: m_π' = 1.302(7) GeV (PDG: 1.3(1) GeV), m_K' = 1.440(14) GeV (PDG: 1.460 GeV), m_ρ' = 1.423(48) GeV (PDG: 1.465(25) GeV)
- Current quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 2.7 MeV, m_s = 72 MeV; ratio 2m_s/(m_u+m_d) = 26.7 (cf. experimental 27.3_{-0.8}^{+0.7})
- Interaction parameters: ω = 0.8 GeV, ωD_RL = (1.01 GeV)³; D(η) = D_RL × (1+0.27η)/(1+1.47η) for η ∈ [0,1.6]

## Context

This work extends the EHM-improved kernel approach of Qin (2020), previously restricted to u, d mesons, to include strange quarks and first excited states. It addresses known failures of rainbow-ladder truncation (Munczek 1994, Bender 1996) such as incorrect level orderings for K'/π', ρ'/π', and ρ'/K*' states, and the underestimation of a₁-ρ and b₁-ρ mass splittings. The results enable systematic studies of meson properties as probes of emergent hadron mass in QCD, and the kernel construction method can be applied with any reasonable gluon-quark vertex.
