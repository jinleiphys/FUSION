# Exploring entanglement and spectral split correlations in three-flavor collective neutrino oscillations

> In environments with prodigious numbers of neutrinos, such as core-collapse supernovae, neutron star mergers, or the early universe, neutrino-neutrino interactions are dynamically significant. They can dominate neutrino flavor evolution and force it to be nonlinear, causing collective neutrino oscillations. Such collective oscillations have been studied numerically, for systems with up to millions of neutrinos, using mean-field or one-particle effective approximations. However, such a system of interacting neutrinos is a quantum many-body system, wherein quantum correlations could play a significant in the flavor evolution-thereby motivating the exploration of many-body treatments which follow the time evolution of these correlations. In many-body flavor evolution calculations with two neutrino flavors, the emergence of spectral splits in the neutrino energy distributions has been found to be correlated with the degree of quantum entanglement across the spectrum. In this work, for the first time, we investigate the emergence of spectral-splits in the three-flavor many-body collective neutrino oscillations. We find that the emergence of spectral splits resembles the number and location found in the mean-field approximation but not in the width. Moreover, unlike in the two-flavor many-body calculations, we find that additional degrees of freedom make it more difficult to establish a correlation between the location of the spectral splits and the degree of quantum entanglement across the neutrino energy spectrum.

## Key claim

For the first time, the emergence of spectral splits in three-flavor many-body collective neutrino oscillations is investigated, and the authors find that the number and location of spectral splits resemble those found in the mean-field approximation but not the width; however, unlike in two-flavor many-body calculations, the additional degrees of freedom make it more difficult to establish a correlation between the location of the spectral splits and the degree of quantum entanglement across the neutrino energy spectrum.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the many-body Hamiltonian comprising vacuum propagation and neutrino-neutrino interaction terms in SU(3) generator representation, using the single-angle approximation in a neutrino bulb geometry. They simulate ensembles of up to seven interacting neutrinos for the many-body calculations and up to twelve neutrinos for the mean-field calculations, computing the one-body von-Neumann entropies and polarization vector components P3 and P8 to identify spectral splits. The mean-field calculations replace the two-body interaction term with a one-body term where each neutrino interacts with the average field of all others, solving the resulting equation for the polarization vector trajectory.

## Key numbers

* Total entanglement entropy increases with system size from N=5 to N=7 neutrinos in three-flavor calculations, with the difference between two-flavor and three-flavor cases increasing with the number of neutrinos in the ensemble.
* For a system of 12 neutrinos in mean-field with initial state |νe^⊗12⟩, a single spectral split occurs between frequency modes ω8 and ω9 in both normal and inverted mass orderings.
* For a system of 12 neutrinos in mean-field with initial state |νe^⊗4 νμ^⊗4 ντ^⊗4⟩, three spectral splits emerge at ω1↔ω2, ω5↔ω6, and ω11↔ω12 in both normal and inverted mass orderings.
* For a system of 6 neutrinos in both mean-field and many-body calculations with initial state |νe^⊗3 νμ^⊗3⟩, two spectral splits emerge at frequency modes ω1↔ω2 and ω5↔ω6 in both normal and inverted mass orderings.
* For a system of 6 neutrinos with initial state |νe^⊗2 νμ^⊗2 ντ^⊗2⟩, three spectral splits emerge in both mean-field and many-body calculations, though the split at ω1↔ω2 is less clear than in the 12-neutrino case.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor many-body studies by Patwardhan (2021) and Cervia (2019) which found correlations between spectral split locations and entanglement entropy, and by Siwach (2023) which first studied three-flavor many-body collective oscillations with five neutrinos. The results contradict the two-flavor finding that neutrinos at spectral splits are the most entangled ones, suggesting that a more detailed analysis involving overlap probabilities of all three mass eigenstates (Pν1, Pν2, and Pν3) or multipartite entanglement measures may be necessary. This work enables future studies using quantum computers with qutrits for larger three-flavor ensembles, as classical simulations are limited by Hilbert space scaling as 3^N.
