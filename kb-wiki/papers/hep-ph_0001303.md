# Enhanced Global Symmetry Constraints on epsilon Terms

> Recently it has been proposed that the physical spectrum of a vector-like gauge field theory may exhibit an enhanced global symmetry near a chiral/conformal phase transition. The new symmetry is related to the possibility, supported by various investigations, that a parity-doubled spectrum develops as the number of fermions Nf is increased to a critical value above which it is expected that the symmetric phase is restored. We show that parity-doubling together with the associated enhanced global symmetry severely constrains the epsilon terms of the effective Lagrangian involving Goldstone bosons as well as massive spin-1 particles. We extend our analysis to underlying fermions in pseudo-real representations of the gauge group.

## Key claim

The authors show that if a vector-like gauge theory develops an enhanced global symmetry (either continuous or discrete) near a chiral/conformal phase transition, this symmetry severely restricts the allowed epsilon terms (involving the Lorentz antisymmetric tensor) in the effective Lagrangian for Goldstone bosons and massive spin-1 particles, with the continuous case forbidding all dimension-four epsilon terms except the ordinary Wess-Zumino term.

## Method

The authors construct a non-linearly realized effective chiral Lagrangian for the global symmetry \(SU_L(N_f)\times SU_R(N_f)\) spontaneously broken to \(SU_V(N_f)\), including Goldstone bosons and massive vector/axial-vector fields. They first gauge the Wess-Zumino action to generate epsilon terms, then generalize to a globally invariant Lagrangian with independent coefficients, and finally impose the enhanced symmetry conditions (either an extra continuous \(SU_L(N_f)\times SU_R(N_f)\) or a discrete \(Z_{2L}\times Z_{2R}\)) to determine which epsilon terms survive. The analysis is extended to underlying fermions in pseudoreal representations of the gauge group, where the global symmetry is \(SU(2N_f)\) broken to \(Sp(2N_f)\).

## Key numbers

* The critical number of flavors for the chiral/conformal transition in an \(SU(N)\) gauge theory is argued to be \(N_f \approx 4N\) (citing earlier work), with lattice simulations for \(N=3\) indicating substantial decrease in chiral symmetry breaking when \(N_f\) is about 4.
* The enhanced continuous symmetry condition for the \(SU_L(N_f)\times SU_R(N_f)\) case requires the parameters \(s=1\) and \(h=-1\) in the non-linear Lagrangian, yielding vector and axial-vector masses \(M^2 = m^2 - v^2/2\).
* For the pseudoreal representation case, the enhanced symmetry condition requires \(s=4\) and \(h=2\), giving \(M^2 = m^2 - 2v^2\).
* The Wess-Zumino coefficient is fixed as \(C = -i N / (240\pi^2)\) by matching the \(\pi^0 \rightarrow 2\gamma\) amplitude, where \(N\) is the number of colors.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Appelquist, Sannino, and collaborators (1999) that parity doubling near a chiral/conformal transition is associated with an enhanced global symmetry, which was previously studied using a linearly realized sigma model. The authors here use a non-linear realization and focus on the previously unexplored epsilon terms, showing that the continuous enhanced symmetry forbids all dimension-four parity-odd interactions between vectors and Goldstone bosons, while the discrete \(Z_{2L}\times Z_{2R}\) symmetry allows three specific terms. This enables distinctive phenomenological predictions such as the absence of vector meson decay into three Goldstone bosons, and extends the analysis to pseudoreal representations relevant for \(SU(2)\) gauge theories being studied on the lattice.
