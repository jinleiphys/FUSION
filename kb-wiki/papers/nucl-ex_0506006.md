# Dominant g(9/2)^2 neutron configuration in the 4+1 state of 68Zn based on new g factor measurements

> The $g$ factor of the $4_1^+$ state in $^{68}$Zn has been remeasured with improved energy resolution of the detectors used. The value obtained is consistent with the previous result of a negative $g$ factor thus confirming the dominant $0g_{9/2}$ neutron nature of the $4_1^+$ state. In addition, the accuracy of the $g$ factors of the $2_1^+$, $2_2^+$ and $3_1^-$ states has been improved an d their lifetimes were well reproduced. New large-scale shell model calculations based on a $^{56}$Ni core and an $0f_{5/2}1pg_{9/2}$ model space yield a theoretical value, $g(4_1^+) = +0.008$. Although the calculated value is small, it cannot fully explain the experimental value, $g(4_1^+) = -0.37(17)$. The magnitude of the deduced B(E2) of the $4_1^+$ and $2_1^+$ transition is, however, rather well described. These results demonstrate again the importance of $g$ factor measurements for nuclear structure determination s due to their specific sensitivity to detailed proton and neutron components in the nuclear wave functions.

## Key claim
The g factor of the 4_1^+ state in 68Zn is confirmed to be negative, g(4_1^+) = -0.37(17), indicating a dominant (0g_9/2)^2 neutron configuration; however, large-scale shell model calculations yield a small positive value g(4_1^+) = +0.008, which cannot fully explain the experimental negative sign.

## Method
The g factors and lifetimes of excited states in 68Zn were measured using projectile Coulomb excitation in inverse kinematics with a 180 MeV 68Zn beam on a multilayered carbon-gadolinium-tantalum-copper target. Spin precessions in the transient field of magnetized gadolinium were detected via particle-gamma coincidences using Ge detectors, and lifetimes were extracted from Doppler-broadened lineshapes using the Doppler-Shift-Attenuation Method. Large-scale shell model calculations were performed with a 56Ni core and an 0f_5/2 1p g_9/2 model space using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction renormalized via many-body perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- g(4_1^+) = -0.37(17) for 68Zn, confirming the negative sign from previous measurements
- g(2_1^+) = +0.58(6) for 68Zn, improved accuracy over previous value of +0.505(38)
- g(2_2^+) = +0.48(17) for 68Zn, consistent with previous value of +0.53(15)
- g(3_1^-) = +0.3(4) for 68Zn, consistent with previous value of +0.4(3)
- Lifetime of 4_1^+ state in 68Zn: tau = 1.18(8) ps
- Lifetime of 2_1^+ state in 68Zn: tau = 2.34(4) ps
- Theoretical g(4_1^+) from full shell-model calculation (SM-2): +0.008
- B(E2; 4_1^+ -> 2_1^+) for 68Zn: 166(9) e^2 fm^4 (experimental), 224.6 e^2 fm^4 (SM-2 calculation)
- B(E2; 2_1^+ -> 0_1^+) for 68Zn: 242(3) e^2 fm^4 (experimental), 190.9 e^2 fm^4 (SM-2 calculation)
- Average occupation probability of 0g_9/2 neutron orbit for 4_1^+ state in 68Zn: 2.4 (SM-2 calculation)

## Context
This work extends previous g factor measurements on even-A Zn isotopes (A=62-70) by Kenn et al. (2002) and Leske et al. (2005), where the 4_1^+ state g factor was first measured but with insufficient energy resolution to separate the 4_1^+ -> 2_1^+ gamma line from a neighboring 2_3^+ -> 2_1^+ line. The improved resolution confirms the negative g factor, which contradicts both collective model predictions and earlier shell model calculations. The results enable future measurements on the Ge isotones 66Ge and 70Ge to search for similar effects, and motivate inclusion of the 0f_7/2 orbit and meson-exchange current corrections in shell model calculations to resolve the sign discrepancy.
