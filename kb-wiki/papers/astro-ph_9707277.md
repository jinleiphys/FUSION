# Microscopic nuclear equation of state with three-body forces and neutron star structure

> We calculate static properties of non-rotating neutron stars (NS's) using a microscopic equation of state (EOS) for asymmetric nuclear matter, derived from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with explicit three-body forces. We use the Argonne AV14 and the Paris two-body nuclear force, implemented by the Urbana model for the three-body force. We obtain a maximum mass configuration with $ M_{max} = 1.8 M_{\sun}$ ($M_{max} = 1.94 M_{\sun}$) when the AV14 (Paris) interaction is used. They are both consistent with the observed range of NS masses. The onset of direct Urca processes occurs at densities $n \geq 0.65~fm^{-3}$ for the AV14 potential and $n \geq 0.54~fm^{-3}$ for the Paris potential. Therefore, NS's with masses above $M^{Urca} = 1.4 M_{\sun}$ for the AV14 and $M^{Urca} = 1.24 M_{\sun}$ for the Paris potential can undergo very rapid cooling, depending on the strength of superfluidity in the interior of the NS. The comparison with other microscopic models for the EOS shows noticeable differences.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a microscopic equation of state (EOS) for asymmetric nuclear matter, derived from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with explicit three-body forces, produces neutron star maximum masses of 1.8 and 1.94 solar masses for the AV14 and Paris potentials respectively, but these values depend on the specific two-body interaction used and the extrapolation procedure at high densities.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation with the continuous choice for the single-particle potential, using the Argonne AV14 and Paris two-body nuclear forces. They incorporated the Urbana model three-body force by reducing it to an effective density-dependent two-body force through averaging over the third particle position, adjusting the parameters A and U to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. The resulting EOS for beta-stable matter (neutrons, protons, electrons, muons) was then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute static, non-rotating neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass configuration: M_max = 1.8 M_sun for AV14 + TBF; M_max = 1.94 M_sun for Paris + TBF
- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter: n_o = 0.176 fm^-3 with E_o/A = -16.01 MeV (Paris); n_o = 0.178 fm^-3 with E_o/A = -16.46 MeV (AV14)
- Incompressibility: K_inf = 281 MeV (Paris); K_inf = 253 MeV (AV14)
- Onset density for direct Urca processes: n >= 0.65 fm^-3 (AV14); n >= 0.54 fm^-3 (Paris)
- Threshold mass for rapid cooling via Urca processes: M_Urca = 1.4 M_sun (AV14); M_Urca = 1.24 M_sun (Paris)
- Radius at maximum mass: R = 9.7 km (AV14 + TBF); R = 9.54 km (Paris + TBF)
- Central number density at maximum mass: n_c = 1.34 fm^-3 (AV14 + TBF); n_c = 1.33 fm^-3 (Paris + TBF)
- For a 1.4 M_sun neutron star: R = 11.0 km, n_c = 0.65 fm^-3 (AV14 + TBF); R = 11.09 km, n_c = 0.64 fm^-3 (Paris + TBF)

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations that used only two-body forces and failed to reproduce the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter, as noted by Coester (1970) and others. The authors compare their results with the variational many-body approach of Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988, WFF) and with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations by Li et al. (1992), finding that their non-relativistic BHF EOS with three-body forces is softer than both the WFF and DBHF results at high densities. The EOS enables future selfconsistent microscopic calculations of both neutron star structure and nuclear superfluid properties within the same many-body approach and with the same nuclear interaction.
