# QCD Matter: A Search for a Mixed Quark-Hadron Phase

> Physics aspects of a JINR project to reach the planned 5A GeV energy for the Au and U beams and to increase the bombarding energy up to 10A GeV are discussed. The project aims to search for a possible formation of a strongly interacting mixed quark-hadron phase. The relevant problems are exemplified. A need for scanning heavy-ion interactions in bombarding energy, collision centrality and isospin asymmetry is emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a mixed quark-hadron phase may be formed in heavy-ion collisions at the planned Nuclotron energy of 5A GeV, but the observable signal is expected to be weak because only a part of the system volume enters the mixed phase for a short time; the conditions become appreciably better at 10A GeV.

## Method

The authors use relativistic 3-fluid hydrodynamics to compute dynamical trajectories for central Au+Au collisions in the temperature-baryon density and temperature-baryon chemical potential planes, employing both a pure hadronic equation of state and one with a first-order phase transition described by a two-phase bag model. They also analyze the effects of isospin asymmetry by considering the conservation of both baryon and electric charges, using the Gibbs phase-equilibrium conditions to map the two-dimensional coexistence surface. The study further examines the influence of nuclear deformation by comparing tip-tip and body-body collisions of deformed Uranium nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Planned Nuclotron beam energy for Au and U ions: 5A GeV; upgrade target: 10A GeV
- For symmetric matter at T=0, the baryon density ranges along 3.5ρ₀ between the onset and completion of the transition
- At T=0, comparing x=0 and x=-0.2, the transition density decreases by δρ_B ≈ 0.5ρ₀
- For semi-central ²³⁸U-²³⁸U collisions at 1A GeV with b=7 fm, the system reaches baryon density around 3ρ₀, temperature 50-60 MeV, and energy density 500 MeV fm⁻³
- The compression in tip-tip U+U collisions is about 30% higher and the region with ρ > 5ρ₀ lasts approximately 40% longer than in body-body or spherical U+U collisions
- The measured η mass from d+C collisions at 2A GeV is M_η = 540.5 ± 2.1 MeV with resolution σ = 33.4 ± 6.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program proposed in Sissakian et al. (2006) for investigating dense QCD matter at the Nuclotron, and it builds on the low-energy scanning program at SPS (NA49 Collaboration) which revealed an interesting structure in the energy dependence of observables at E_lab ~ 20-30A GeV. The paper discusses the upcoming FAIR project at GSI (Darmstadt) for studying compressed baryonic matter in the energy range 10-30A GeV, and it enables future experimental searches for the mixed phase using neutron-rich beams and deformed Uranium nuclei at the Nuclotron.
