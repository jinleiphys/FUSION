# Flavor Dependence of Meson Melting Temperature in Relativistic Potential Model

> We study the melting temperature of heavy mesons in the hot medium of light quarks. By solving the covariant Schrödinger equations at finite temperature for mesons $D$, $ϕ$ and $J/ψ$, we obtained the temperature dependence of their masses, binding energies and averaged sizes and found the flavor dependence of the melting temperature: $T_D \simeq T_ϕ< T_{J/ψ}$. The sequential melting temperature can explain the difference in meson elliptic flow observed in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper finds a sequential melting temperature ordering \(T_D \simeq T_\phi < T_{J/\psi}\) for heavy mesons in a hot medium of light quarks, but this ordering is not sensitive to whether the quark potential is taken as the free energy \(F\) or the internal energy \(U\); the absolute melting temperatures, however, lie in a wide range depending on that choice.

## Method

The authors solve covariant Schrödinger equations at finite temperature for the radial wave functions of \(D\), \(\phi\), and \(J/\psi\) mesons, using a relativistic potential model with a central potential extracted from lattice QCD simulations. The potential is taken as the free energy \(F(r,T)\) (with the internal energy \(U\) considered as an alternative limit), and the screening mass \(\mu(T)\) is obtained from fitting lattice free-energy data. The meson mass, binding energy, and average size are computed as functions of temperature, and the melting temperature is defined as the point where the binding energy vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement temperature of light quarks is taken as \(T_c \simeq 170\) MeV.
- For the potential \(V=F\), the melting temperatures are \(T_D/T_c = 1.08\), \(T_\phi/T_c = 1.08\), and \(T_{J/\psi}/T_c = 1.28\).
- For the potential \(V=U\), the melting temperatures are \(T_D/T_c = 1.81\), \(T_\phi/T_c = 1.77\), and \(T_{J/\psi}/T_c = 2.51\).
- The overall range for \(D\) and \(\phi\) melting is \(1.1 \lesssim T/T_c \lesssim 1.8\), and for \(J/\psi\) it is \(1.3 \lesssim T/T_c \lesssim 2.5\).
- The maximum mass reduction near melting is about \(5\%\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(D\), and reaches \(20\%\) for \(\phi\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic potential model previously applied to \(c\bar c\) states at finite temperature by Guo et al. (2013) to mesons containing strange and charm quarks (\(D\), \(\phi\), \(J/\psi\)). It builds on lattice QCD simulations of the quark free energy by Kaczmarek et al. and Digal et al., and on the non-relativistic potential model for quarkonium by Satz. The sequential melting found here provides a mechanism to explain the observed ordering of elliptic flow \(v_2^\pi > v_2^\phi > v_2^{J/\psi}\) in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, enabling hydrodynamic calculations of meson formation at flavor-dependent freeze-out temperatures.
