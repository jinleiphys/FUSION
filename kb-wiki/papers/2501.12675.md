# Symmetry of screening masses of mesons in two-flavor lattice QCD at high temperatures

> We investigate spatial two-point correlation functions of mesonic operators in two-flavor lattice QCD at high temperatures. The simulated temperatures over the range $T \in [147, 330]$ MeV, where the critical temperature is estimated around 165 MeV. To ensure a good control of the chiral symmetry we employ the Möbius domain-wall fermion action for two degenerate flavors of quarks. With a lattice cut off $a^{-1}\sim 2.6$ GeV, the residual mass is reduced to 0.14 MeV. With the energy spectrum obtained from the screening mass at incremental values of the temperature range, we examine the $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry, the anomalous axial $U(1)$ as well as an enhanced symmetry which exchanges the spin degrees of freedom. We also study how the data approaches the perturbative prediction given by twice the Matsubara frequency of free quarks.

## Key claim
Using Möbius domain-wall fermions with a residual mass of only 0.14 MeV, the authors find that the SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral symmetry is effectively restored already at T = 190 MeV (about 1.15 T_c), while the axial U(1)_A symmetry breaking signal persists to higher temperatures, and an emergent chiral-spin SU(2)_CS symmetry appears at high T.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate flavors of Möbius domain-wall quarks at a fixed lattice spacing a ≈ 0.075 fm (a⁻¹ ≈ 2.6 GeV), covering temperatures from 147 to 330 MeV. They measured spatial two-point correlation functions of mesonic operators in six different iso-triplet channels (PS, S, V, A, T_t, X_t) and extracted screening masses using a cosh fit ansatz, then compared mass differences between symmetry-related channels to probe SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R, axial U(1)_A, and the emergent chiral-spin SU(2)_CS symmetries.

## Key numbers
- Simulated temperature range: T ∈ [147, 330] MeV, corresponding to [0.9, 2.0] T_c, with critical temperature T_c ≈ 165(3) MeV.
- Lattice cutoff: a⁻¹ ≈ 2.6 GeV; residual mass: 0.14(6) MeV.
- Lightest bare quark mass: m = 0.001, corresponding to 2.6 MeV (71% of the physical quark mass m_phys = 0.0014(2)).
- At T = 190 MeV and lightest mass: Δm_{V-A}/m_A ≈ 0.1% (SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R probe).
- At T = 165 MeV and lightest mass: Δm_{V-A}/m_A ≈ 2.8%.
- At T = 190 MeV and lightest mass: Δm_{X-T}/m_X ≈ 2.5% (axial U(1)_A probe).
- At T = 165 MeV and lightest mass: Δm_{X-T}/m_X ≈ 12.7%.
- Lattice size: L = 32 (2.37 fm) for most temperatures; L = 40 and 48 for the two lowest temperatures.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the JLQCD collaboration (Cossu 2015, Tomiya 2016, Aoki 2020) that found lattice artifacts in axial U(1)_A probes at high temperatures using other fermion formulations. It also builds on earlier observations of chiral-spin SU(2)_CS symmetry emergence by Rohrhofer (2017, 2019) and by Chiu (2023, 2024). The results are compared with HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2019) simulations using HISQ quarks, showing consistency except for the scalar channel where staggered fermion artifacts appear. The study enables future continuum extrapolation and extension to N_f = 2+1 flavors, as well as investigation of whether an additional transition exists above T_c triggered by the emergent chiral-spin symmetry.
