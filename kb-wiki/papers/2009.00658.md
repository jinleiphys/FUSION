# Microscopic investigation of the $^8$Li($n, γ$)$^9$Li reaction

> The $^8$Li($n,γ$)$^9$Li reaction plays an important role in several astrophysics scenarios. It cannot be measured directly and indirect experiments have so far provided only cross section limits. Theoretical predictions differ by an order of magnitude. In this work we study the properties of $^9$Li bound states and low-lying resonances and calculate the $^8$Li($n,γ$)$^9$Li cross section within the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) with chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions as the only input. The NCSMC is an ab initio method applicable to light nuclei that provides a unified description of bound and scattering states well suited to calculate low-energy nuclear scattering and reactions. Our calculations reproduce the experimentally known bound states as well as the lowest $5/2^-$ resonance of $^9$Li. We predict a $3/2^-$ spin-parity assignment for the resonance observed at 5.38 MeV. In addition to the a very narrow $7/2^-$ resonance corresponding presumably to the experimental 6.43 MeV state, we find several other broad low-lying resonances. Our calculated $^8$Li($n,γ$)$^9$Li cross section is within the limits derived from the 1998 National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory Coulomb-dissociation experiment [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 57}, 959 (1998)]. However, it is higher than cross sections obtained in recent phenomenological studies. It is dominated by a direct E1 capture to the ground state with a resonant contribution at $\sim0.2$ MeV due to E2/M1 radiation enhanced by the $5/2^-$ resonance.

## Key claim

The first *ab initio* calculation of the $^8$Li($n,\gamma$)$^9$Li cross section, performed within the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) using chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions as the only input, produces a cross section within the limits of the 1998 Coulomb-dissociation experiment but higher than recent phenomenological studies; however, the calculated thresholds were adjusted to experimental values, meaning the result is no longer a purely theoretical prediction.

## Method

The authors applied the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) method, which combines a discrete expansion over square-integrable energy eigenstates of $^9$Li with a continuous expansion over antisymmetrized $^8$Li+$n$ cluster channel states. The Hamiltonian used a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to fourth order (N$^3$LO) and a three-nucleon interaction up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO), softened by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) technique at a resolution scale of $\lambda_{\mathrm{SRG}}=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$. For the capture cross section calculation, the NCSMC results were phenomenologically adjusted (NCSMC-pheno) to reproduce experimental thresholds and the known $5/2^-$ resonance position, by shifting the $^8$Li $1^+$ excitation energy by $-45$ keV and adjusting the lowest $^9$Li NCSM eigenenergies in the $3/2^-$, $1/2^-$, and $5/2^-$ channels by about $-1$ MeV for the $3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ channels and $-0.3$ MeV for the $1/2^-$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the NCSMC method, previously applied to other light-nuclei reactions, to the first *ab initio* calculation of the $^8$Li($n,\gamma$)$^9$Li capture cross section, which cannot be measured directly and has theoretical predictions spanning an order of magnitude. The results are consistent with the upper limits from the Coulomb-dissociation experiment of Zecher (1998) but higher than the phenomenological potential model results of Li (2005) and the cluster model of Descouvemont (1993). The calculation enables more reliable reaction rates for inhomogeneous big bang nucleosynthesis and r-process scenarios, and the predicted $3/2^-$ spin-parity assignment for the 5.38 MeV resonance can be tested by future experiments.
