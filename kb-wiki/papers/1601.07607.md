# Effects of the tensor couplings on the nucleonic direct URCA processes in neutron star matter

> The relativistic neutrino emissivity of the nucleonic direct URCA processes in neutron star matter are investigated within the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation. We study particularly the influences of the tensor couplings of vector mesons $ω$ and $ρ$ on the nucleonic direct URCA processes. It is found that the inclusion of the tensor couplings of vector mesons $ω$ and $ρ$ can slightly increase the maximum mass of neutron stars. In addition, the results indicate that the tensor couplings of vector mesons $ω$ and $ρ$ lead to obvious enhancement of the total neutrino emissivity for the nucleonic direct URCA processes, which must accelerate the cooling rate of the non-superfluid neutron star matter. However, when considering only the tensor coupling of vector meson $ρ$, the neutrino emissivity for the nucleonic direct URCA processes slightly declines at low densities and significantly increases at high densities.That is to say that the tensor coupling of vector meson $ρ$ leads to the slow cooling rate of a low-mass neutron star and rapid cooling rate of a massive neutron star.

## Key claim

The inclusion of tensor couplings of both ω and ρ mesons in the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation enhances the total neutrino emissivity for nucleonic direct URCA processes, accelerating the cooling rate of non-superfluid neutron star matter; however, when only the ρ meson tensor coupling is considered, the neutrino emissivity slightly declines at low densities and significantly increases at high densities, implying a slow cooling rate for low-mass neutron stars and a rapid cooling rate for massive neutron stars.

## Method

The authors adopt the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation including tensor couplings of ω and ρ mesons to describe baryon interactions in neutron star matter, assuming a simple npeμ composition (neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They solve the coupled equations for nucleon self-energies, meson field equations, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the equation of state, particle fractions, effective masses, and the relativistic neutrino emissivity of the nucleonic direct URCA processes via the Fermi golden rule. Four cases are compared: A1 (no tensor couplings), A2 (with ω and ρ tensor couplings), B1 (no tensor couplings, different parameter set), and B2 (only ρ tensor coupling).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of neutron stars for case A1 (no tensor couplings): 1.99 M⊙, with radius 11.06 km and central density 1.07 fm⁻³.
- Maximum mass for case A2 (with ω and ρ tensor couplings): 2.00 M⊙, with radius 10.66 km and central density 1.10 fm⁻³.
- Maximum mass for case B1 (no tensor couplings): 2.31 M⊙, with radius 10.98 km and central density 0.98 fm⁻³.
- Maximum mass for case B2 (only ρ tensor coupling): 2.33 M⊙, with radius 10.91 km and central density 0.98 fm⁻³.
- The weak-coupling constant used in the neutrino emissivity calculation: G_F = 1.436 × 10⁻⁴⁹ erg cm³.

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron star cooling by examining the role of tensor couplings of vector mesons ω and ρ within the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework, building on prior work by Prakash (1992) and Yakovlev (2000) on direct URCA processes and by Furnstahl (1998) and Mao (2002) on tensor couplings in nuclear properties. The results enable more detailed predictions of cooling rates for neutron stars of different masses, particularly distinguishing between low-mass and massive neutron stars when only the ρ tensor coupling is included.
