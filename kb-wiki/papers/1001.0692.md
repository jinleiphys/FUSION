# On the critical exponent of η/s and a new exponent-less measure of fluidity

> We discuss on the critical exponent of $η/s$ for a fluid, and propose a new exponent-less measure of fluidity based on a mode-mode coupling theory. This exhibits a remarkable universality for fluids obeying a liquid-gas phase transition both in hydrodynamic as well as in nonhydrodynamic region. We show that this result is independent of the choice of the fluid dynamics, {\em viz.}, relativistic or nonrelativistic. Quark-Gluon Plasma, being a hot relativistic and a nearly perfect fluid produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, is expected to obey the same universality constrained by both the viscous and the thermal flow modes in it. We also show that if the elliptic flow data in RHIC puts a constraint on $η/s$ then the new fluidity measure for Quark-Gluon Plasma in turn also restricts the other transport coefficient, {\it viz.}, the thermal conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new dimensionless combination of transport coefficients, \({\cal F}_{\eta\kappa} = \eta / ({\cal D}_\kappa \xi)^{-1} T\), provides an exponent-free measure of fluidity that remains at a universal value of \(1/(6\pi)\) for all fluids obeying a liquid-gas phase transition, independent of whether the fluid dynamics is relativistic or nonrelativistic. This universality holds in both the hydrodynamic and nonhydrodynamic regions, and the authors argue that the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions should obey the same universality.

## Method

The authors analyze the dynamical structure function of density fluctuations for relativistic dissipative fluids, derived from the Navier-Stokes equation, which is a sum of three Lorentzian line shapes: a Rayleigh component centered at \(\omega=0\) with half-width \(\Gamma_R = {\cal D}_\kappa q^2\) and Brillouin doublets at \(\omega = \pm c_s q\) with half-width \(\Gamma_B = \frac{1}{2}{\cal D}_s q^2\). Using mode-mode coupling theory, they combine the Rayleigh width \(\Gamma_R\), shear viscosity \(\eta\), and correlation length \(\xi\) into the dimensionless quantity \(\Gamma^* = 6\pi\eta\Gamma_R / (T q^3)\), which they show from experimental data collapses onto a universal curve as a function of \(q\xi\), with limiting behaviors \(\Gamma^* = 1/(q\xi)\) in the hydrodynamic domain (\(q\xi < 1\)) and \(\Gamma^* = 1\) near the critical point (\(q\xi \geq 1\)).

## Key numbers

- The universal value of the new fluidity measure is \({\cal F}_{\eta\kappa} = 1/(6\pi)\) for all fluids obeying a liquid-gas phase transition.
- For the Quark-Gluon Plasma, the elliptic flow data from RHIC constrain the parametrization \(\eta/s \sim (a\epsilon^\delta + b)\) with \(\delta \sim 1\), \(a \sim 0.64 \sim 2/3\), and \(b \sim 0.1 \sim 1/(4\pi)\), where \(\epsilon = |T/T_C - 1|\).
- The Rayleigh width \(\Gamma_R = {\cal D}_\kappa q^2\) remains unchanged between nonrelativistic and relativistic fluids, as demonstrated for parameters \(q = 0.1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), chemical potential \(\mu = 200\ \text{MeV}\), \(T = 200\ \text{MeV}\), \(\eta/(n_0 S) = \zeta/(n_0 S) = 0.3\), and \(\kappa T/(n_0 S) = 0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the mode-mode coupling analysis of Swinney and Henry (1973) for nonrelativistic fluids to relativistic fluids, showing that the universal scaling of \(\Gamma^*\) with \(q\xi\) holds regardless of relativistic corrections. The paper builds on prior studies of density fluctuations in relativistic fluids by Kunihiro (2009) and on the lower bound conjecture \(\eta/s \geq 1/(4\pi)\) from gauge-gravity duality (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). The proposed fluidity measure enables a constraint on the thermal conductivity \(\kappa\) of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, which the authors suggest could be tested at RHIC with its upgrade and at the LHC.
