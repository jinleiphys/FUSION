# Microscopic study of energy and centrality dependence of transverse collective flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The centrality dependence of directed and elliptic flow in light and heavy systems of colliding nuclei is studied within two microscopic transport models at energies from 1 AGeV to 160 A GeV. The pion directed flow has negative slope in the midrapidity range irrespective of bombarding energy and mass number of the colliding ions. In contrast, the directed flow of nucleons vanishes and even develops antiflow in the midrapidity range in (semi)peripheral collisions at energies around 11.6 A GeV and higher. The origin of the disappearance of flow is linked to nuclear shadowing. Since the effect is stronger for a light system, it can be distinguished from the similar phenomenon caused by the quark-gluon plasma formation. In the latter case the disappearance of the flow due to the softening of the equation of state should be most pronounced in collisions of heavy ions. The centrality dependence of the elliptic flow shows that the maximum in the <v_2(b)> distribution is shifted to very peripheral events with rising incident energy, in accord with experimental data. This is an indication of the transition from baryonic to mesonic degrees of freedom in hot hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The disappearance of directed flow (antiflow) in the midrapidity range at AGS energies (11.6 A GeV) and above is attributed to nuclear shadowing rather than quark-gluon plasma formation, because the effect is stronger for light systems (S+S) than for heavy systems (Au+Au or Pb+Pb), which is opposite to what would be expected from a QGP-induced softening of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors employed two microscopic transport models: the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model for SIS energies (1 A GeV) and the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) for AGS (11.6 A GeV) and SPS (160 A GeV) energies. They simulated collisions of light (32S+32S) and heavy (197Au+197Au and 208Pb+208Pb) symmetric systems across a range of impact parameters from central (reduced impact parameter 0.15) to very peripheral (0.9), and calculated the directed flow (v1) and elliptic flow (v2) coefficients from the Fourier expansion of the azimuthal particle distribution as functions of reduced rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow of nucleons vanishes and develops antiflow in the midrapidity range at energies around 11.6 A GeV and higher for (semi)peripheral collisions
- At SIS energies (1 A GeV), nucleon directed flow reaches maximum at reduced impact parameter 0.3-0.45 for both S+S and Au+Au systems
- Pion directed flow has negative slope in the midrapidity range at all three energies (1, 11.6, and 160 A GeV) irrespective of system size
- At SPS energies (160 A GeV), nucleon antiflow slope at reduced impact parameter 0.9 is similar to that of pion flow
- Elliptic flow of nucleons in Au+Au collisions at 11.6 A GeV is at least twice as large as in S+S interactions
- Maximum in the mean elliptic flow of pions as a function of impact parameter occurs at b = 10 fm (reduced impact parameter 0.75) in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies
- Transition of elliptic flow from out-of-plane (negative v2) to in-plane (positive v2) occurs between 1 A GeV and 11.6 A GeV

## Context

This work extends microscopic transport studies of collective flow from SIS to SPS energies, building on the Fourier expansion method developed by Voloshin and Zhang (1996) and the elliptic flow analysis framework of Ollitrault (1992). The results contradict the interpretation that disappearance of directed flow necessarily indicates QGP formation, as proposed in hydrodynamic simulations by Amelin et al. (1991) and Bravina et al. (1994). The finding that nuclear shadowing produces stronger antiflow in light systems provides a method to distinguish shadowing from QGP effects by comparing light and heavy ion collisions, enabling future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC energies where the authors predict antiflow should appear in semicentral collisions with impact parameter below 3 fm.
