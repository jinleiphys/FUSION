# Virtual states and generalized completeness relation in the Friedrichs Model

> We study the well-known Friedrichs model, in which a discrete state is coupled to a continuum state. By examining the pole behaviors of the Friedrichs model in a specific form factor thoroughly, we find that, in general, when the bare discrete state is below the threshold of the continuum state, there should also be a virtual-state pole accompanying the bound-state pole originating from the bare discrete state as the coupling is turned on. There are also other second-sheet poles originating from the singularities of the form factor. We give a general argument for the existence of these two kinds of states. As the coupling is increased to a certain value, the second-sheet poles may merge and become higher-order poles. We then discuss the completeness relations incorporating bound states, virtual states, and resonant states corresponding to higher-order poles.

## Key claim

When the bare discrete state lies below the continuum threshold, turning on the coupling produces not only a bound-state pole on the physical sheet but also a virtual-state pole on the second sheet, a "shadow pole" in S-matrix theory; this virtual state is generally present unless the form-factor pole coincides with the discrete-state energy, in which case a pair of resonance poles appears instead.

## Method

The authors solve the Friedrichs model exactly for a specific form factor \( |f(\omega)|^2 = \sqrt{\omega}/(\omega + \rho^2) \), analytically continuing the inverse-propagator function \(\eta(\omega)\) to the second Riemann sheet. They locate the poles of the full Hamiltonian by solving \(\eta(\omega)=0\) on both sheets and track the pole trajectories as the coupling constant \(\lambda\) is varied. A general argument is given for the existence of virtual-state poles from both the discrete state and the form-factor singularities, and completeness relations are extended to include degenerate higher-order poles.

## Key numbers

* For \(\omega_0 > 0\) and small coupling (\(\omega_0 > \pi\lambda^2/\rho\)), the three poles are a pair of resonance poles at \(E_{1,2} = \omega_0 - \pi\lambda^2/(\rho \mp i\omega_0^{1/2}) + O(\lambda^4)\) and a virtual-state pole at \(E_3 = -\rho^2 + 2\rho\pi\lambda^2/(\omega_0+\rho^2) + O(\lambda^4)\).
* A triple pole occurs when \(d = \rho/3\), \(\omega_0 = \rho^2/3\), and \(\rho = (3/2)(\pi\lambda^2)^{1/3}\).
* A double pole (two degenerate virtual states) occurs when the two resonance poles merge on the negative real axis at \(d = \frac{1}{3}(\rho \pm \sqrt{\rho^2 - 3\omega_0})\) and \(d_0 = \frac{1}{3}(\rho \mp 2\sqrt{\rho^2 - 3\omega_0})\), with the condition \(\omega_0 < \rho^2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Likhoded (1997) on form-factor-induced poles in the Friedrichs model by providing a general argument for their existence and by demonstrating that a virtual-state pole always accompanies a bound-state pole when the bare discrete state lies below threshold, a shadow pole predicted by Eden (1964). The results enable the construction of generalized completeness relations that incorporate degenerate higher-order poles (double and triple poles) in the rigged-Hilbert-space formalism, building on prior work by Prigogine (1991), Gadella (1983, 1984), and Bohm (1997).
