# Finite-Temperature Corrections in the Dilated Chiral Quark Model

> We calculate the finite-temperature corrections in the dilated chiral quark model using the effective potential formalism. Assuming that the dilaton limit is applicable at some short length scale, we interpret the results to represent the behavior of hadrons in dense {\it and} hot matter. We obtain the scaling law, $\frac{f_π(T)}{f_π} = \frac{m_Q (T)}{m_Q} \simeq \frac{m_σ(T)}{m_σ}$ while we argue, using PCAC, that pion mass does not scale within the temperature range involved in our Lagrangian. It is found that the hadron masses and the pion decay constant drop faster with temperature in the dilated chiral quark model than in the conventional linear sigma model that does not take into account the QCD scale anomaly. We attribute the difference in scaling in heat bath to the effect of baryonic medium on thermal properties of the hadrons. Our finding would imply that the AGS experiments (dense {\it and} hot matter) and the RHIC experiments (hot and dilute matter) will ``see" different hadron properties in the hadronization phase.

## Key claim

The authors calculate finite-temperature corrections in the dilated chiral quark model and find that hadron masses and the pion decay constant drop faster with temperature than in the conventional linear sigma model, but this result depends on the assumption that the dilaton limit (where the sigma meson mass goes to zero and the axial coupling goes to one) is applicable at short length scales in dense matter.

## Method

The authors use the one-loop finite-temperature effective potential formalism applied to the Beane-van Kolck Lagrangian, which incorporates the QCD scale anomaly into a chiral quark model. They compute the temperature-dependent vacuum expectation value of the dilaton field by extremizing the effective potential, then derive the scaling of the pion decay constant, sigma mass, and constituent quark mass from this temperature-dependent vacuum. The results are compared with those from the conventional linear sigma model calculated at the same one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant scaling: \( f_\pi(T)/f_\pi = 1 - T^2/(4f_\pi^2) \) in the dilated chiral quark model, compared to \( f_\pi(T)/f_\pi = 1 - T^2/(12f_\pi^2) \) in the linear sigma model
- Sigma mass scaling: \( m_\sigma(T)/m_\sigma \simeq 1 - (1/3)(T^2/f_\pi^2) \) in the dilated chiral quark model
- Constituent quark mass scaling: \( m_Q(T)/m_Q = 1 - T^2/(4f_\pi^2) \)
- The coefficient multiplying \( T^2/f_\pi^2 \) in the pion decay constant scaling is three times bigger in the dilated chiral quark model than in chiral perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent scaling proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) to finite temperature, using the effective Lagrangian constructed by Beane and van Kolck that incorporates the QCD scale anomaly. The results differ from those of chiral perturbation theory at one loop (Gasser and Leutwyler) and the linear sigma model (Cheng), which do not include the QCD scale anomaly. The finding implies that heavy-ion experiments at AGS (dense and hot matter) and RHIC (hot and dilute matter) would see different hadron properties in the hadronization phase, and could be checked on lattice as well as in heavy-ion experiments.
