# Toy Model for Pion Production II: The role of three-particle singularities

> The influence of three-particle breakup singularities on s-wave meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is studied within the distorted wave Born approximation. This study is based on a simple scalar model for the two-nucleon interaction and the production mechanism. An algorithm for the exact numerical treatment of the inherent three-body cuts, together with its straightforward implementation is presented. It is also shown that two often-used approximations to avoid the calculation of the three-body breakup are not justified. The possible impact on pion production observables is discussed.

# Toy Model for Pion Production II: The role of three-particle singularities

## Key claim
Within a scalar toy model of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, the exact treatment of three-particle breakup singularities in the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) produces significant differences in both the magnitude and energy dependence of the total cross section compared to two commonly used approximations (the static approximation and the threshold approximation), suggesting that the inadequate handling of three-body dynamics may contribute to the poor description of polarization data for neutral pion production.

## Method
The authors constructed a scalar model where the pion is a scalar-isoscalar Klein-Gordon field coupled via Yukawa coupling to nucleons, the nucleon-nucleon interaction is modeled by exchange of a heavy scalar sigma meson (mass 550 MeV), and only ladder diagrams with either initial-state or final-state interactions are evaluated in time-ordered perturbation theory. They developed an algorithm based on Schwamb's subtraction method to handle the three-body cuts in the momentum integration, splitting the radial integration into regions containing three-body singularities (between boundaries p_a and p_b) and two-body singularities, and applying a transformation y^2 = p_b - p'' to remove logarithmic singularities at the integration boundaries.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass: 939 MeV; pion mass: 139 MeV; sigma meson mass: 550 MeV
- Total cross sections were calculated for excess energies Q/m_pi between 0 and 1 (i.e., between one-pion and two-pion thresholds)
- At Q = 1/2 m_pi, the phase of the initial-state interaction amplitude A^I shows sizable differences between the exact calculation and the static approximation
- The static approximation overestimates the full cross section over the entire energy regime between one-pion and two-pion thresholds, especially near threshold
- The threshold approximation, while exact at threshold, underestimates the full result considerably when approaching the two-pion threshold

## Context
This work extends the scalar toy model introduced in Motzke, Elster, and Hanhart (2002) to specifically study three-body singularities that had been avoided in all previous DWBA calculations of meson production in NN collisions (Hanhart 1997). The algorithm builds on the subtraction method developed by Schwamb (2001) for treating three-body breakup in NN potentials that incorporate pion production. The results suggest that the inadequate treatment of three-body dynamics may be a source of the discrepancies between meson exchange model predictions and polarization data for neutral pion production (pp -> pp pi^0), and the algorithm presented can be applied to realistic NN interactions in a straightforward way.
