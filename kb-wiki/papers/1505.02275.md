# Reaction rates of $^{64}$Ge($p,ﾎｳ$)$^{65}$As and $^{65}$As($p,ﾎｳ$)$^{66}$Se and the extent of nucleosynthesis in type I X-ray bursts

> The extent of nucleosynthesis in models of type I X-ray bursts and the associated impact on the energy released in these explosive events are sensitive to nuclear masses and reaction rates around the $^{64}$Ge waiting point. Using the well known mass of $^{64}$Ge, the recently measured $^{65}$As mass, and large-scale shell model calculations, we have determined new thermonuclear rates of the $^{64}$Ge($p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{65}$As and $^{65}$As($p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{66}$Se reactions with reliable uncertainties. The new reaction rates differ significantly from previously published rates. Using the new data we analyze the impact of the new rates and the remaining nuclear physics uncertainties on the $^{64}$Ge waiting point in a number of representative one-zone X-ray burst models. We find that in contrast to previous work, when all relevant uncertainties are considered, a strong $^{64}$Ge $rp$-process waiting point cannot be ruled out. The nuclear physics uncertainties strongly affect X-ray burst model predictions of the synthesis of $^{64}$Zn, the synthesis of nuclei beyond $A=64$, energy generation, and burst light curve. We also identify key nuclear uncertainties that need to be addressed to determine the role of the $^{64}$Ge waiting point in X-ray bursts. These include the remaining uncertainty in the $^{65}$As mass, the uncertainty of the $^{66}$Se mass, and the remaining uncertainty in the $^{65}$As($p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{66}$Se reaction rate, which mainly originates from uncertain resonance energies.

## Key claim
Using the newly measured mass of $^{65}$As and large-scale shell-model calculations, the authors find that, in contrast to previous work (Tu 2011), a strong $^{64}$Ge $rp$-process waiting point cannot be ruled out when all relevant nuclear physics uncertainties are considered.

## Method
The authors calculated new thermonuclear reaction rates for $^{64}$Ge($p$,$\gamma$)$^{65}$As and $^{65}$As($p$,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se using the experimental proton separation energy of $^{65}$As ($S_p = -90 \pm 85$ keV) and the AME2012 extrapolated value for $^{66}$Se ($S_p = 1720 \pm 310$ keV). They performed large-scale shell-model calculations with the GXPF1a interaction using the code NuShellX@MSU to obtain energy levels, proton-transfer spectroscopic factors, and gamma widths for states in $^{65}$As and $^{66}$Se, then summed resonant and direct-capture contributions over all relevant compound nucleus states.

## Key numbers
- Proton separation energy of $^{65}$As: $S_p = -90 \pm 85$ keV, derived from the measured $^{65}$As mass at HIRFL-CSR and the well-known $^{64}$Ge mass.
- Proton separation energy of $^{66}$Se: $S_p = 1720 \pm 310$ keV, from the AME2012 extrapolated mass.
- Only one experimental level is known in $^{65}$As, at $E_x = 187(3)$ keV.
- One confirmed level in $^{66}$Se at $E_x = 929(7)$ keV, with tentative assignments for levels at 2064(3) keV and 3520(4) keV.
- Capture on thermally excited states of $^{65}$As contributes at most 38% to the total $^{65}$As($p$,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se rate at 2 GK.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the $^{64}$Ge waiting point in the $rp$-process during type I X-ray bursts, which were limited by uncertain masses and reaction rates (Schatz 1998, Woosley 2004, Fisker 2008, Parikh 2009, Jose 2010). The new rates differ significantly from earlier estimates by Woehr (1994) and Rauscher (2000) and from the JINA REACLIB database rates. The results enable improved X-ray burst model predictions for the synthesis of $^{64}$Zn, nuclei beyond $A=64$, energy generation, and burst light curves, while identifying the remaining uncertainties in the $^{65}$As mass, the $^{66}$Se mass, and the $^{65}$As($p$,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Se resonance energies as key targets for future experimental work.
