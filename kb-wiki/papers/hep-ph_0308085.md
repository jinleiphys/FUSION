# Hadronic Production of Thermal Photons

> We study the thermal emission of photons from hot and dense strongly interacting hadronic matter at temperatures close to the expected phase transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Earlier calculations of photon radiation from ensembles of interacting mesons are re-examined with additional constraints, including new production channels as well as an assessment of hadronic form factor effects. Whereas strangeness-induced photon yields turn out to be moderate, the hitherto not considered t-channel exchange of omega mesons is found to contribute appreciably for photon energies above ~1.5 GeV. The role of baryonic effects is assessed using existing many-body calculations of lepton pair production. We argue that our combined results constitute a rather realistic emission rate, appropriate for applications in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Supplemented with recent evaluations of QGP emission, and an estimate for primordial (hard) production, we compute photon spectra at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies.

# Hadronic Production of Thermal Photons

## Key claim

The authors argue that their combined evaluation of hadronic photon emission rates, incorporating meson gas reactions with hadronic form factors, baryonic effects via in-medium rho spectral functions, and previously unconsidered t-channel omega exchange, constitutes the most realistic assessment to date for applications in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though they acknowledge that none of the individual approximations can be expected to be accurate in the phase transition region.

## Method

The authors compute thermal photon emission rates using two complementary frameworks: a massive Yang-Mills (MYM) effective Lagrangian approach for meson gas reactions (including strangeness channels and hadronic form factors), and a hadronic many-body approach based on in-medium rho meson spectral functions that incorporates baryonic effects. The rates are then convoluted over a fireball model space-time evolution with chemical off-equilibrium, supplemented with hard (primordial) photon production parametrized from proton-nucleon data and nuclear kT-broadening effects.

## Key numbers

- The D/S wave ratio for the a1 to rho pi decay using parameter set II is -0.099, compared to the experimental value of -0.107 ± 0.016
- Hadronic form factors suppress the pi rho to pi gamma rate by a factor of approximately 4 in the 2-3 GeV region
- The K* pi to K gamma reaction constitutes approximately 40% of the pi rho to pi gamma rate at q0 = 1 GeV, reducing to about 20% at q0 = 3 GeV
- The complete leading-order QGP calculation and the full hadronic result agree within a factor of approximately 2 over essentially all relevant energies below 3 GeV at T = 200 MeV
- The fireball evolution is started at a formation time tau0 less than or equal to 1 fm/c, with thermal freezeout at T_fo = 100-120 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of photon radiation from interacting mesons by Song (1993) and Steele et al. (1998), correcting Ward identity violations and using parameter sets consistent with experimental a1 decay data. The baryonic contributions build on many-body calculations of lepton pair production by Rapp and Wambach (1999, 2000) that successfully described dilepton data at SPS energies. The results enable more reliable extraction of QGP photon signals in heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies by providing improved hadronic baselines, and suggest a possible "quark-hadron duality" for electromagnetic emission near the phase boundary, similar to what was found for dilepton production by Rapp and Wambach (1999).
