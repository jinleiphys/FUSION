# Density functional approaches to nuclear dynamics

> We present background concepts of the nuclear density functional theory (DFT) and applications of the time-dependent DFT with the Skyrme energy functional for nuclear response functions. Practical methods for numerical applications of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (TDHFB) are proposed; finite amplitude method and canonical-basis TDHFB. These approaches are briefly reviewed and some numerical applications are shown to demonstrate their feasibility.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent mean-field description of nuclear saturation requires a state-dependent effective interaction, which is naturally incorporated through the density dependence of the energy density functional (EDF); the authors propose that the finite amplitude method (FAM) and canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) are feasible computational approaches for nuclear dynamics, though the full TDHFB treatment remains a challenging task.

## Method

The authors present the theoretical background of nuclear density functional theory (DFT) and derive the TDHFB equations for superfluid nuclei. They introduce the finite amplitude method (FAM) as a small-amplitude approximation that computes the residual field using finite differences of the HFB Hamiltonian, avoiding explicit construction of the residual interaction. They also develop the canonical-basis TDHFB method, which assumes a diagonal pair potential in the canonical basis (analogous to the BCS approximation) and solves coupled equations for canonical states, occupation probabilities, and pair densities in real time.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy per particle in infinite nuclear matter: \(B/A \approx 16\) MeV
- Fermi momentum at saturation: \(k_F \approx 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Fermi energy: \(T_F \approx 40\) MeV
- Effective mass from naive mean-field model: \(m^*/m \approx 0.4\), which disagrees with empirical values \(0.7 \lesssim m^*/m \lesssim 1\)
- Radius of repulsive core in nucleon-nucleon interaction: \(c \approx 0.5\) fm
- Wound integral in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory: \(\kappa \approx 15\%\)
- Giant dipole resonance peak energy in Xe isotopes: \(E_{\text{GDR}} \approx 15\) MeV
- Smoothing parameter used in Fourier transform of E1 response: \(\Gamma = 1\) MeV
- Deformation of \(^{140}\)Xe: \(\beta = 0.02\)

## Context

This work extends the nuclear density functional theory framework from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Day 1967, Negele 1970) and the density matrix expansion (Negele and Vautherin 1972) to time-dependent phenomena in heavy superfluid nuclei. The finite amplitude method was previously introduced by Nakatsukasa, Inakura, and Yabana (2007) for normal-phase nuclei and extended to QRPA by Avogadro and Nakatsukasa (2011); the canonical-basis TDHFB method was developed by Ebata (2010). These methods enable efficient computation of nuclear response functions for heavy open-shell nuclei, where full TDHFB calculations remain computationally prohibitive, and open the way for systematic studies of low-energy dipole strength in neutron-rich isotopes.
