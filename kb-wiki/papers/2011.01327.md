# Complete and incomplete fusion of $^{7}$Li projectiles on heavy targets

> We present a detailed discussion of a recently proposed method to evaluate complete and incomplete fusion cross sections for weakly bound systems. The method is applied to collisions of $^{7}$Li projectiles on different heavy targets, and the results are compared with the available data. The overall agreement between experiment and theory is fairly good.

## Key claim

The paper presents a detailed exposition of a recently proposed method (Cortes et al., 2020) that uses continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) wave functions to separately evaluate complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF) cross sections for weakly bound projectiles that break into two fragments, with the caveat that the method assumes matrix elements of imaginary potentials connecting bound channels to continuum bins are negligible and that the spectroscopic amplitude for the cluster configuration in the projectile bound states is treated as a free parameter in the range 0.7–1.0.

## Method

The authors perform CDCC calculations for collisions of ⁷Li projectiles (treated as a ³H + ⁴He cluster with separation energy 2.45 MeV) on ²⁰⁹Bi, ¹⁹⁷Au, ¹²⁴Sn, and ¹⁹⁸Pt targets, using short-range Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials (depth 50 MeV, radius 1.0 fm, diffusivity 0.2 fm) for each fragment-target interaction to represent fusion absorption. The total fusion cross section is split into contributions from bound channels (direct complete fusion, DCF) and from continuum-discretized bins; the continuum contribution is further decomposed into incomplete fusion of each fragment and sequential complete fusion using angular-momentum-dependent absorption probabilities. The continuum is discretized with bins up to a cut-off energy of 8 MeV and orbital angular momentum up to 3 ℏ, with narrower bins around resonances, and the spectroscopic amplitude for the ³H–⁴He cluster configuration is set to 0.8.

## Key numbers

*   The ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi system: calculated CF cross sections agree with data from Dasgupta et al. (2002, 2004) across all collision energies above and below the Coulomb barrier, using spectroscopic amplitude 0.8.
*   The ⁷Li + ¹⁹⁷Au system: calculated CF cross sections are very close to data from Palshetkar et al. (2014) at all collision energies.
*   The ⁷Li + ¹²⁴Sn system: calculated CF cross sections are very close to data from Palshetkar et al. (2018) at all collision energies.
*   The ⁷Li + ¹⁹⁸Pt system: theoretical CF cross section agrees with data from Singh et al. (2013) around and above the Coulomb barrier but overestimates experimental results at energies about 6 MeV below the barrier, where cross sections are as low as about 10⁻⁴ mb.
*   The ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi system: convergence of CF and TF cross sections is achieved with continuum cut-off energy ε_max = 8 MeV and maximum orbital angular momentum l_max = 3 ℏ; results with these parameters are nearly indistinguishable from those with ε_max = 10 MeV or l_max = 4 ℏ.
*   The ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi system: varying the spectroscopic amplitude between 0.7 and 1.0 changes the CF cross section by up to about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the method first reported in a letter by Cortes et al. (2020) for the ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi system to three additional heavy targets (¹⁹⁷Au, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹⁹⁸Pt). It improves upon prior semiclassical approaches (e.g., Diaz-Torres et al., 2007; Diaz-Torres, 2010, 2011) and quantum mechanical models that could only treat total fusion or asymmetric breakup (e.g., Hagino et al., 2000; Diaz-Torres, 2002), and it differs from the method of Lei and Moro (2019) which extracted CF but not ICF cross sections. The results enable future applications to other weakly bound projectiles and targets, and the authors note that the discrepancy for ¹⁹⁸Pt at very low energies may require further investigation of the spectroscopic amplitude or of the treatment of the bound-state cluster configuration.
