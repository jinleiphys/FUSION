# Prediction for the synthesis cross sections of new moscovium isotopes in fusion-evaporation reactions

> In the framework of the dinuclear system model, the synthesis mechanism of the superheavy nuclides with atomic number $Z=112, 114, 115$ in the reactions of projectiles $^{40,48}$Ca bombarding on targets $^{238}$U, $^{242}$Pu, and $^{243}$Am at a wide incident energies (excitation energy from 0-100 MeV) have been investigated systematically. Based on the available experimental excitation functions, the dependence of calculated synthesis cross sections on collision orientations has been studied thoroughly. The TKEs of these collisions with the fixed collision orientation show its orientation dependence which can be used to predict the tendency of kinetic energy diffusion. The TKEs are dependent on incident energies which have been discussed. The method of Coulomb barrier distribution function has been applied in our calculations which could treat all of the collision orientations from the tip-tip to side-side approximately. The calculations of excitation functions of $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U, $^{48}$Ca + $^{242}$Pu, and $^{48}$Ca + $^{243}$Am have a nice agreement with the available experimental data. The isospin effect of projectiles on production cross sections of moscovium isotopes and the influence of entrance channel effect on the synthesis cross sections of superheavy nuclei have been discussed. The synthesis cross section of new moscovium isotopes $^{278-286}$Mc have been predicted as large as hundreds pb, in the fusion-evaporation reactions of $^{35,37}$Cl + $^{248}$Cf, $^{38,40}$Ar + $^{247}$Bk, $^{39,41}$K + $^{247}$Cm, $^{40,42,44,46}$Ca + $^{238}$Am, $^{45}$Sc + $^{242}$Pu, and $^{46,48,50}$Ti + $^{243}$Np, $^{51}$V + $^{238}$U at the excitation energy interval of 0-100 MeV.

## Key claim

The dinuclear system model, incorporating a Gaussian-like Coulomb barrier distribution function to treat all collision orientations from tip-tip to side-side, predicts that new moscovium isotopes \(^{278-286}\)Mc can be synthesized with cross sections as large as hundreds of picobarns in specific fusion-evaporation reactions, provided the excitation energy is in the interval of 0–100 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed the dinuclear system (DNS) model, which describes fusion-evaporation as a three-stage process: capture of the projectile and target across the Coulomb barrier, nucleon transfer between the touching partners to form a compound nucleus, and de-excitation of the compound nucleus by evaporating light particles against fission. The penetration probability was calculated using the Hill-Wheeler formula with an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution function that averages over collision orientations from tip-tip to side-side. The fusion probability was obtained by solving a set of master equations for the mass probability diffusion of the dinuclear system, and the survival probability was computed using the Weisskopf evaporation theory and the Bohr-Wheeler fission formula.

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the DNS model by Feng and collaborators (Feng, 2009; Feng, 2010; Feng, 2015) to systematically predict cross sections for moscovium isotopes, which are currently only known for a few neutron-rich species (e.g., \(^{287,288,290}\)Mc synthesized at Dubna). The study enables experimental planning by identifying promising projectile-target combinations, such as \(^{35,37}\)Cl + \(^{248}\)Cf, \(^{40,42,44,46}\)Ca + \(^{238}\)Am, and \(^{51}\)V + \(^{238}\)U, for producing the unknown isotopes \(^{278-286}\)Mc. It also contradicts the notion that only cold or hot fusion with \(^{48}\)Ca beams is viable for superheavy element synthesis, suggesting that lighter projectiles on actinide targets may yield measurable cross sections.
