# Non-Equilibrium and Collective Flow Effects in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss the problems involved in extracting the nuclear equation-of-state from heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that the equation of state becomes effectively softer in non-equilibrium and this effect is observable in terms of collective flow effects. Thus, non-equilibrium effects must be included in transport descriptions on the level of the effective mean fields. A comparison with transverse momentum, rapidity, and centrality selected flow data show the reliability and limitations of the underlying interaction which was derived from microscopic Dirac-Brueckner (DB) results.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear equation of state (EOS) extracted from heavy-ion collisions is not directly the ground-state EOS of equilibrated nuclear matter; instead, non-equilibrium momentum-space anisotropies cause an effective softening of the EOS, and this effect must be included in transport descriptions to correctly interpret collective flow data.

## Method

The authors model non-equilibrium phase-space configurations using a Colliding Nuclear Matter (CNM) approximation, where the momentum distribution is represented by two interpenetrating Fermi ellipsoids. They compute the effective binding energy per nucleon for CNM configurations by subtracting the relative kinetic energy of the two currents from the total energy-momentum tensor, then compare this to the Local Density Approximation (LDA) which assumes isotropic equilibrated matter. These mean-field models are implemented in a relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation to simulate heavy-ion collisions and calculate collective flow observables (elliptic flow \(v_2\) and directed flow \(v_1\)).

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility of the nuclear EOS favored by comparisons with experiments is \(K \approx 230\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (soft EOS).
- For central \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(E_{lab} = 0.4\ A.\mathrm{GeV}\), the central pressure components (longitudinal \(P_L\) and transverse \(P_T\)) show anisotropy until \(t > 30\ \mathrm{fm}/c\), indicating local non-equilibrium during the compression phase.
- The effective binding energy per particle \(E_{CNM}^{bind}\) is shown as a function of total density \(\rho_{tot}\) (normalized to saturation density \(\rho_{sat}\)) for relative velocities \(v_{rel} = 0,\ 0.2,\ 0.4,\ 0.6\) for DBHF and DDH models, demonstrating softening with increasing \(v_{rel}\).
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) for peripheral \(Au+Au\) collisions is calculated from Fermi energies up to AGS energies; the CNM treatment restores agreement with data from the FOPI collaboration, whereas the LDA treatment would exclude the underlying EOS as too stiff.
- Transverse momentum dependence of collective flow \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) is shown for \(Ru+Ru\) and \(Au+Au\) collisions at \(0.4\ A.\mathrm{GeV}\) beam energy, with comparisons to FOPI data for protons (\(Z=1\)) and fragments (\(Z=2\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-equilibrium effects in heavy-ion collisions by Gaitanos and collaborators (2001, 2002) and by Sehn and collaborators, who developed the CNM approximation. It directly addresses the long-standing difficulty in extracting the nuclear EOS from flow data, which had been debated over the previous three decades (as reviewed in the paper). The results show that the softening from non-equilibrium is comparable in magnitude to the differences between ground-state EOS models (e.g., DBHF from the Tuebingen group versus the DBHF from the Munich group, or the DDH approach of Lenske and collaborators). This enables a more reliable determination of the high-density behavior of the nuclear EOS by requiring that non-equilibrium effects be included in transport descriptions, and points toward a unified description of nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and heavy-ion collisions using microscopic models.
