# Non-negligible influence of shape inheritance and staggering on α decay

> A series of findings in machine learning (ML) and decay theory are captured while exploring the role of deformation and preformation factors in α decay. We provide a novel and practical paradigm for developing physics-driven machine learning in nuclear physics research by introducing known decay theory and statistical correlation analysis. Furthermore, this analysis verifies the Geiger-Nuttall law, and the relationship between the decay energy and α formation amplitude, also releases a signal that nuclei with hexadecapole deformation are more likely to form α clusters. In particular, we identify two novel phenomena, shape inheritance, in which the deformation properties are partially transmitted from parent to daughter nuclei; and half-life inversion due to shape staggering of adjacent even-even nuclei. This phenomenon occurs frequently in neutron-deficient nuclei near lead isotopes, which is consistent with shape coexistence in experiments. Surprisingly, it reappeared within the predicted half-life of the 119 and 120 isotope chains in the eighth period of the periodic table. The half-life considering the inversion effect is preferable for the study of new nuclides and shape coexistence in experiments.

# Non-negligible influence of shape inheritance and staggering on α decay

## Key claim
The paper claims that shape inheritance (partial transmission of deformation properties from parent to daughter nuclei) and half-life inversion due to shape staggering of adjacent even-even nuclei are novel phenomena that significantly affect α-decay half-lives, but these effects are conditional: they appear primarily in neutron-deficient nuclei near lead isotopes and in predicted isotope chains of elements 119 and 120, and the inversion phenomenon is not reciprocal (not all shape differences produce a crossover in half-life predictions).

## Method
The authors performed Pearson and Spearman correlation analyses between half-life, decay energy, preformation factor, and deformation parameters (β₂ and β₄) for 369 known nuclei. They then trained a single-hidden-layer neural network with ten neurons using a physics-driven input selection that incorporated the Universal Decay Law framework, quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations of both parent and daughter nuclei, and preformation factors. The model was validated on 41 superheavy nuclei (Z ≥ 104) from the AME2020 mass table and used to predict half-lives for Z=119 and Z=120 isotope chains using both WS4 and FRDM databases as input.

## Key numbers
- Root mean square deviation of 0.313 for the comprehensive model on all 369 known nuclei, compared to 0.74 for the Universal Decay Law
- RMS accuracy improvement of 3.44 times on 41 test nuclei (Z ≥ 104): model achieved RMS of 0.26 versus 0.86 from the improved Universal Decay Law
- For the nucleus ²⁹²120, predicted half-lives from ML and UDL differ by a factor of ten (ML: -5.36, UDL: -6.40 in log₁₀ years using WS4 data)
- The neural network used only ten neurons in a single hidden layer

## Context
This work extends prior findings on the relationship between deformation and α-decay by Andreyev and collaborators (multiple works) and builds on the Universal Decay Law framework developed by Qi and collaborators. The correlation analysis confirms the Geiger-Nuttall law and the linear relationship between Q_α^{-1/2} and log₁₀P_α established in earlier work. The identification of shape inheritance provides a dynamical explanation for the shell effect strength at Z=82, N=126 previously reported. The inversion phenomenon is consistent with shape coexistence in neutron-deficient lead isotopes observed experimentally by Heyde and Andreyev. This work enables improved half-life predictions for superheavy element synthesis in the eighth period, particularly for Z=119 and 120 isotope chains.
