# Elliptic flow in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV

> The angular correlations measured in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV are decomposed into contributions from back to back emission and elliptic flow. Modeling the dominant term in the correlation functions as a momentum conservation effect or as an effect of the initial transverse velocity of the source, the remaining elliptic flow component can be estimated. The elliptic flow coefficient extracted from the CMS Collaboration data is 0.04-0.08. No additional small-angle, ridge-like correlations are needed to explain the experimental data.

# Elliptic flow in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV

## Key claim

The angular correlations measured by CMS in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV can be fully decomposed into a dominant back-to-back component (modeled either by momentum conservation or by a transverse boost of the source) plus an elliptic flow component, with no need for an additional ridge-like structure; however, the extracted elliptic flow coefficient depends on the assumed model for the background and likely contains significant non-flow contributions, especially at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The two-particle correlation function R(Δφ) from CMS data (for pairs with pseudorapidity separation |Δη|>2.0) was fitted with a combination of a first-harmonic term from momentum conservation and a second-harmonic term from elliptic flow, yielding coefficients c₁ and c₂. Alternatively, a boosted-source model was used where particles separated in pseudorapidity originate from sources moving with opposite transverse velocities, and the correlation function was fitted with the boost velocity β⊥ and elliptic flow v₂ as free parameters. The elliptic flow coefficient was extracted from the second-harmonic coefficient using v₂ = √[c₂/(2(⟨N⟩-1))], equivalent to the second-order cumulant method.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow coefficient extracted from CMS data using the momentum conservation model: v₂ = 0.07–0.1 in the bins where the ridge is observed
- Elliptic flow coefficient using the transverse boost model: v₂ = 0.06–0.08
- Most conservative estimate from fitting only the small-angle region (Δφ ≤ 3π/8): v₂ = 0.04–0.06
- Elliptic flow coefficient from the momentum conservation model increases from 0.03 in the lowest p⊥ bin to 0.1 in the highest p⊥ bin for high-multiplicity events
- Slope parameter for the Tsallis distribution of transverse momenta: T = (0.02 + 0.03√N_trk) GeV, with n = 6.6 and T = 0.145 GeV for minimum bias events
- Effective multiplicity N_eff for momentum conservation ranges from 10 (low p⊥ bins) to 80 (high momentum and high multiplicity bins)
- Coefficient c₁ (strength of momentum conservation correlations) is approximately 0.4–0.9

## Context

This work extends the search for collective phenomena in small systems, following the CMS observation of a ridge-like structure in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV (Khachatryan 2010). Previous hydrodynamic model estimates for elliptic flow in proton-proton collisions (Cunqueiro 2008, Avsar 2010) gave values of order 0.04–0.06 when viscosity effects are included. The analysis uses the momentum conservation formalism developed by Borghini (2000) and Bzdak (2010), and the extracted v₂ values are compared to PYTHIA simulations, which show similar second-harmonic components from non-flow jet-like correlations. The results enable future higher-statistics measurements in the soft momentum region p⊥ < 2.0 GeV to distinguish collective flow from non-flow effects, and challenge Monte Carlo event generators to reproduce the observed azimuthal correlations.
