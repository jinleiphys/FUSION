# Quark Dispersion Relation and Dilepton Production in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Under very general assumptions we show that the quark dispersion relation in the quark-gluon plasma is given by two collective branches, of which one has a minimum at a non-vanishing momentum. This general feature of the quark dispersion relation leads to structures (van Hove singularities, gaps) in the low mass dilepton production rate, which might provide a unique signature for the quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Under very general assumptions about the quark self-energy in the chiral limit, the quark dispersion relation in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) always contains two collective branches, one of which (the plasmino) has a minimum at non-zero momentum; this general feature, independent of the specific approximation used for the quark propagator, leads to sharp structures (van Hove singularities and gaps) in the low-mass dilepton production rate that could serve as a unique signature for QGP formation.

## Method

The authors derived the exact, implicit dispersion relations for quarks in the QGP from the poles of the fermion propagator, decomposed into helicity eigenstates, using the most general form of the quark self-energy in the chiral limit and the rest frame of the medium. They analyzed the regularity properties of the self-energy and vertex functions at vanishing momentum via the differential Ward identity to show that the initial slopes of the two dispersion branches are opposite and neither zero nor infinite, forcing the plasmino branch to have a minimum before approaching the light cone. They then computed the dilepton production rate from the imaginary part of the photon self-energy using effective quark propagators from both perturbative (hard-thermal-loop) and non-perturbative (gluon condensate) approximations.

## Key numbers

* In the perturbative hard-thermal-loop approximation, the effective quark mass is \(m_q^* = gT/\sqrt{6}\) and the plasmino branch has a minimum at \(p_{min} = 0.408\, m_q^*\).
* In the non-perturbative gluon condensate calculation, the effective quark mass is \(m_q^* = 1.15\, T\) for temperatures between the critical temperature \(T_c\) and \(4T_c\).
* The effective quark mass \(m_q^*\) is expected to be of order 0.5 GeV in the temperature regime accessible in experiments.
* The predicted structures in the dilepton spectrum appear for invariant masses around 1 GeV and below.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative hard-thermal-loop results of Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan (1990) and the non-perturbative gluon condensate calculations of Schafer and Thoma (1999) by proving that the existence of two collective quark branches with a plasmino minimum is a general feature of the QGP, not an artifact of any particular approximation. It enables the prediction that dilepton spectra at RHIC and LHC should show van Hove singularities and gaps at low invariant mass (below about 1 GeV), in contrast to the relatively flat spectra expected from a hadron gas as calculated by Rapp and Wambach (1999), potentially providing a clear experimental signature for deconfined quarks.
