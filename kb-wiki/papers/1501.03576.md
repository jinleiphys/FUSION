# Electrical Conductivity of an Anisotropic Quark Gluon Plasma : A Quasiparticle Approach

> The study of transport coefficients of strongly interacting matter got impetus after the discovery of perfect fluid ever created at ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision experiments. In this article, we have calculated one such coefficient viz. electrical conductivity of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) phase which exhibits a momentum anisotropy. Relativistic Boltzmann's kinetic equation has been solved in the relaxation-time approximation to obtain the electrical conductivity. We have used the quasiparticle description to define the basic properties of QGP. We have compared our model results with the corresponding results obtained in different lattice as well as other model calculations. Furthermore, we extend our model to calculate the electrical conductivity at finite chemical potential.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is calculated using a quasiparticle description and the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation; the result is that the conductivity decreases with increasing momentum-space anisotropy, and the model with anisotropy parameter ξ = 0.6 reproduces the spread of lattice data, though the lattice results themselves vary widely.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation to obtain the electrical conductivity for an isotropic QGP, then introduce a momentum-space anisotropy by stretching an isotropic Fermi-Dirac distribution along a direction n using the anisotropy parameter ξ. The QGP is described as a system of quasiparticles with temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent effective masses, and the relaxation time is taken from perturbative QCD. The calculation is performed for zero and finite quark chemical potential, summing over up, down, and strange flavors.

## Key numbers

- For ξ = 0.0 (isotropic case), σ_el/T increases monotonically with temperature starting from T = T_c (the crossover temperature).
- For ξ = 0.3 and ξ = 0.6, σ_el/T is lower than the isotropic case at all temperatures; the model with ξ = 0.6 gives reasonable agreement with the range of lattice data.
- At finite quark chemical potential μ_q = 200 MeV, σ_el/T becomes larger than at μ_q = 0 MeV for temperatures below T = 4 T_c, while at high temperatures (T ~ 8–10 T_c) the difference is very small.
- The relaxation time for quarks/antiquarks is given by τ_q = 1 / [15 α_s^2 T log(1/α_s) (1 + 0.06 N_F^eff)].

## Context

This work extends prior quasiparticle model calculations of transport coefficients by Srivastava et al. (earlier work) to include momentum anisotropy, motivated by the observation from viscous hydrodynamics and AdS/CFT that the QGP created in heavy-ion collisions has anisotropic pressures (P_L/P_T ≈ 0.5 for RHIC-like and 0.35 for LHC-like initial conditions, and as low as 0.31 in AdS/CFT). The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Aarts et al., Ding et al., Gupta et al., and Amato et al., as well as with the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) by Berrehrah et al. (2015). The work enables future studies incorporating a proper-time-dependent anisotropy parameter for more realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.
