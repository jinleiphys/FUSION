# Transport coefficients for multi-component gas of hadrons using Chapman Enskog method

> The transport coefficients of a multi-component hadronic gas at zero and non-zero baryon chemical potential are calculated using the Chapman-Enskog method. The calculations are done within the framework of an $S$-matrix based interacting hadron resonance gas model. In this model, the phase-shifts and cross-sections are calculated using $K$-matrix formalism and where required, by parameterizing the experimental phase-shifts. Using the energy dependence of cross-section, we find the temperature dependence of various transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, heat conductivity and diffusion coefficient. We finally compare our results regarding various transport coefficients with previous results in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors calculate transport coefficients for a multi-component hadronic gas using the Chapman-Enskog method within an S-matrix based interacting hadron resonance gas model, but the results depend on the energy dependence of cross-sections and are limited to temperatures between approximately 80 and 110 MeV where they agree with transport model calculations.

## Method

The authors used the Chapman-Enskog method to solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a gas of seven stable hadrons (pi, eta, K, N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) plus 112 resonances. Phase-shifts and cross-sections were calculated using K-matrix formalism for attractive interactions, while experimental phase-shift data were used for nucleon-nucleon interactions. The calculations were performed at zero baryon chemical potential and at mu_B = 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta_s/s for the multi-component hadronic gas at mu_B = 0 MeV shows a minimum near the liquid-gas phase transition temperature range
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio eta_v/s for the multi-component hadronic gas at mu_B = 0 MeV shows a maximum near the phase transition
- Results on transport coefficients are in reasonable agreement with transport models in the temperature range T = 80-110 MeV
- Calculations were performed at mu_B = 0 MeV and mu_B = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Wiranata (2013) that studied eta/s for a pi-K-N-eta system at vanishing mu_B without including NN interaction, and by Prakash (1993) that calculated transport coefficients for pi-K-N systems using experimental cross-sections. The authors improve upon these by incorporating a larger spectrum of interacting hadronic states (7 stable hadrons plus 112 resonances) and extending to finite chemical potential. The results enable comparison with transport models using the Kubo formalism by Demir (2008) and Rose (2017), and provide input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
