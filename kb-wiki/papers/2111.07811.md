# QCD Static Force in Gradient Flow

> We compute the QCD static force and potential using gradient flow at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling. The static force is the spatial derivative of the static potential: it encodes the QCD interaction at both short and long distances. While on the one side the static force has the advantage of being free of the $O(Λ_{\rm QCD})$ renormalon affecting the static potential when computed in perturbation theory, on the other side its direct lattice QCD computation suffers from poor convergence. The convergence can be improved by using gradient flow, where the gauge fields in the operator definition of a given quantity are replaced by flowed fields at flow time $t$, which effectively smear the gauge fields over a distance of order $\sqrt{t}$, while they reduce to the QCD fields in the limit $t \to 0$. Based on our next-to-leading order calculation, we explore the properties of the static force for arbitrary values of $t$, as well as in the $t \to 0$ limit, which may be useful for lattice QCD studies.

## Key claim

The paper computes the QCD static force using gradient flow at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling, showing that the gradient flow smearing (parameterized by flow time \(t\)) improves the convergence of the Fourier transform compared to direct lattice QCD calculations, but the finite correction term \(W_{\rm NLO}^F(\bar{t})\) is non-analytic at \(t=0\) due to \(\bar{t}\log\bar{t}\) terms.

## Method

The authors compute the static potential in gradient flow by replacing gluon fields with flowed fields \(B_\mu(x;t)\) in the Wilson loop definition, then differentiate with respect to \(r\) to obtain the static force \(F(r;t)\). They evaluate the momentum-space potential through next-to-leading order in \(\alpha_s\) using dimensional regularization in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) spacetime dimensions with Feynman gauge, renormalizing the strong coupling in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, and then Fourier transform to position space.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order static force in gradient flow is proportional to \({\cal F}_0(r;t) = \text{erf}(r/\sqrt{8t}) - (r/\sqrt{2\pi t})\exp(-r^2/(8t))\), which vanishes at \(r=0\) and approaches 1 in the limit \(t\to 0\).
* The finite one-loop correction \(W_{\rm NLO}^F(\bar{t})\) expands at \(\bar{t}=0\) as \(\bar{t}(-22\gamma_{\rm E}/3 + 277/18 - 31\log 2/3 - (22/3)\log\bar{t}) + O(\bar{t}^2)\).
* The small-\(\xi\) expansion of the finite correction \({\cal F}_{\rm NLO}^F(r;t)\) for \(\xi = r/\sqrt{t}\) is \(0.304930\,\xi^3 - 0.0332202\,\xi^5 + 0.00181358\,\xi^7 + O(\xi^9)\), accurate to better than \(10^{-4}\) for \(\xi < 0.5\).
* An approximating function for \({\cal F}_{\rm NLO}^F(r;t)\) is provided with coefficients \(c_1\) through \(c_{10}\) (e.g., \(c_1 = -0.0501648\), \(c_2 = 0.526758\)) and constants \(C_a = 109.358\), \(C_b = 43.8438\), \(C_c = 404.790\), accurate to better than 1% for \(1 < \xi < 10\) and better than 2% for \(\xi > 10\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculation of the static force by Brambilla et al. (2021) and the gradient flow formalism developed by Luscher (2010, 2011). It addresses the poor convergence of direct lattice QCD computations of the static force observed by Bazavov et al. (2014) and Brambilla et al. (2021), where discretization errors are sizable. The results enable lattice QCD studies to use gradient flow to improve convergence when extracting the static force, with the analytic expressions providing guidance for extrapolating flowed results to the \(t\to 0\) limit.
