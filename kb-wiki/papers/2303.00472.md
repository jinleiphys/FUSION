# Proton \textit{s}-resonance states of $^{12}$C and $^{14,15}$O within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field framework

> The excitation functions of proton elastic scattering on $^{12}$C and $^{14,15}$O nuclei at the energies near the proton-emission threshold are calculated using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) in continuum approach. For each excitation function, the first resonance is identified as the $s$-state resonance of the mean-field theory. For $^{15}$O, whose ground-state spin is nonzero, the $s$-state resonance splits into two resonances via the spin-spin component of the optical potential. With a slight adjustment of the strength of central potential, which is obtained from the SHF in continuum approach, the excitation functions of proton elastic scattering for the three nuclei can be explained with high accuracy. The proposed framework can provide a practical method to explain nuclear scattering at the energies near the proton-emission threshold with minimal experimental input.

## Key claim
The Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) mean-field optical potential, with only a single adjustable parameter for the central potential strength, can reproduce the excitation functions of proton elastic scattering on \(^{12}\)C and \(^{14,15}\)O near the proton-emission threshold, provided that a spin-spin term is included for the nonzero-spin target \(^{15}\)O. The abstract's claim of "high accuracy" is qualified by the need for a slight adjustment of the central potential strength (parameter \(\lambda\)) from the SHF-predicted value, and by the fact that the calculated resonance widths differ from experimental values by up to 10 keV.

## Method
The authors extended the Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculation to the continuum to generate a real optical potential for proton elastic scattering, including the central, Coulomb, and effective-mass terms. For the \(^{15}\)O target with nonzero ground-state spin, a spin-spin potential proportional to the central term was added, with its strength \(\alpha\) treated as a free parameter. The scattering equation was solved using the ECIS06 code, and the single-particle resonance width was extracted from the energy derivative of the phase shift at the resonance energy. The central potential strength \(\lambda\) was adjusted separately for each target to match the experimental resonance location.

## Key numbers
- \(^{12}\)C(\(p,p\)): first \(s\)-state resonance at 420 keV (center-of-mass) with calculated width \(\Gamma_{\text{cal}} = 26\) keV, compared to experimental \(\Gamma_{\text{exp}} = 31.7 \pm 0.8\) keV (Selove 1991); adjustment parameter \(\lambda = 1.03\).
- \(^{14}\)O(\(p,p\)): \(s\)-state resonance at 1270 keV with calculated width \(\Gamma_{\text{cal}} = 534\) keV, compared to experimental \(\sim 700\) keV (Goldberg 2004); adjustment parameter \(\lambda = 1.02\).
- \(^{15}\)O(\(p,p\)): two \(s\)-state resonances split by spin-spin interaction: \(0^-\) state at 536 keV with \(\Gamma_{\text{cal}} = 15\) keV (experimental \(25.6 \pm 4.6\) keV, Stefan 2014) and \(\lambda_{J=0} = 0.99\); \(1^-\) state at 729 keV with \(\Gamma_{\text{cal}} = 63\) keV (experimental \(76 \pm 5\) keV, Stefan 2014) and \(\lambda_{J=1} = 0.97\).
- Spin-spin potential strength relative to central potential: \(\alpha = 0.02\) (2% of the central term).

## Context
This work extends the SHF-in-continuum approach previously applied to low-energy radiative-capture reactions (Anh 2021, 2022) and to the \(^{10}\)Be(\(p,p\)) scattering (Anh 2022). It provides a microscopic alternative to phenomenological \(R\)-matrix analyses (Wigner 1947, Lane 1958) and Woods-Saxon potential fits (Peters 2003) for describing low-lying single-particle resonances near the proton-emission threshold. The analysis of the \(^{15}\)O(\(p,p\)) data from Stefan (2014) offers a new method to determine the spin-spin component of the optical potential, complementing earlier polarized-beam and transmission measurements (Nagamine 1970, Batty 1971, Fisher 1972) and folding-model calculations (Cunningham 2011, 2013). The results enable future studies of whether the small spin-spin strength (2% of central) is a general property for other nuclei and motivate development of Skyrme interactions suitable for both bound and unbound states.
