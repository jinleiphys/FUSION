# Chiral corrections to the Kroll-Ruderman theorem

> We calculate the one--loop corrections to the Kroll--Ruderman low--energy theorems for charged pion photoproduction in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We predict the threshold S--wave multipole $E_{0+}$ to be $E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(γp \to π^+ n) = ( 28.2 \pm 0.6 )\cdot 10^{-3}/M_π$ and $E_{0+}^{\rm thr} (γn \toπ^- p) = ( -32.7 \pm 0.6 )\cdot 10^{-3}/M_π$, respectively, for a fixed pion--nucleon coupling constant, $g_{πN} = 13.4$. A comparison to the existing data is also given.

## Key claim

The one-loop chiral corrections to the Kroll-Ruderman low-energy theorems for charged pion photoproduction are calculated up to and including all terms of order \(M_\pi^3\), yielding predictions for the threshold S-wave multipoles that agree with existing data but would conflict with a preliminary TRIUMF measurement of the inverse reaction \(\pi^- p \to \gamma n\).

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the threshold S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}^{(0)}\) and \(E_{0+}^{(-)}\) in the isospin basis to order \(M_\pi^3\). Pion loop diagrams, frozen kaon loops, and local contact terms (low-energy constants estimated via resonance exchange, including \(\rho\)-meson and \(\Delta(1232)\) contributions) are included, with the power counting made systematic by treating nucleons as static sources.

## Key numbers

- \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\gamma p \to \pi^+ n) = (28.2 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\) for fixed \(g_{\pi N}^2/4\pi = 14.28\)
- \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\gamma n \to \pi^- p) = (-32.7 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\) for fixed \(g_{\pi N}^2/4\pi = 14.28\)
- The Kroll-Ruderman tree-level values are \(27.6 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) for \(\pi^+ n\) and \(-31.7 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) for \(\pi^- p\) (using \(g_{\pi N}^2/4\pi = 14.28\), \(m = 928.27\) MeV, \(M_{\pi^+} = 139.57\) MeV)
- The isoscalar multipole \(E_{0+}^{(0)} = (-1.6 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\)
- The isovector multipole \(E_{0+}^{(-)} = (21.5 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-3}/M_\pi\)
- Published experimental values: \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^+ n) = (27.9 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Burg) and \((28.8 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Adam); \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}(\pi^- p) = (-31.4 \pm 1.3) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Burg) and \((-32.2 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Gold)
- Preliminary TRIUMF value for \(\pi^- p\): \((-34.6 \pm 1.0) \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi^+}\) (Kovash, statistical error only)

## Context

This work extends the earlier relativistic one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) by using heavy baryon CHPT to prove that all terms of order \(M_\pi^3\) are under control and not contaminated by higher loops. The results enable a stringent test of the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N}\) via the Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule combined with the Panofsky ratio, and together with precise neutral pion photoproduction amplitudes would allow investigation of isospin symmetry violation beyond leading order in the electromagnetic coupling.
