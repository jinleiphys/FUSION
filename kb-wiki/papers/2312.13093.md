# An effective field theory of damped ferromagnetic systems

> Using the in-in formalism, we generalize the recently constructed magnetoelastic EFT arXiv:2112.13873 [hep-th] to describe the damping dynamics of ferromagnetic systems at long wavelengths. We find that the standard Gilbert damping term naturally arises as the simplest leading-order symmetry-consistent non-conservative contribution within the in-in framework. The EFT is easily generalized to scenarios with anisotropy and inhomogeneity. In particular, we find the classic Landau-Lifshitz damping term emerges when isotropy is broken by a constant external background field. This provides a first principle explanation for distinguishing the two types of damping dynamics that were originally constructed phenomenologically. Furthermore, the EFT framework could also incorporate intrinsic anisotropy of the material in a straightforward way using the spurion method. For systems with inhomogeneity such as nontrivial spin textures, we find that the leading order derivative correction yields the generalized Gilbert damping equations that were found in condensed matter literature. This shows that the EFT approach enables us to derive the form of higher-derivative-order corrections in a systematic way. Lastly, using the phonon-magnon coupling deduced in the magnetoelastic EFT, we are able to make a prediction for the generic form of the phononic contribution to the damping equation.

# An effective field theory of damped ferromagnetic systems

## Key claim

The paper claims that the standard Gilbert damping term arises as the simplest leading-order symmetry-consistent non-conservative contribution within the in-in (Schwinger-Keldysh) effective field theory framework, but only for isotropic and homogeneous systems; when isotropy is broken by a constant external background field, the Landau-Lifshitz damping term emerges instead, providing a first-principles distinction between the two phenomenologically constructed damping dynamics.

## Method

The author applies the Schwinger-Keldysh (in-in) formalism to the recently constructed magnetoelastic EFT of Pavaskar, Penco, and Rothstein (2021), coupling the magnon Goldstone fields to an external sector via composite operators dressed with the broken SO(3)/SO(2) subgroup. The dissipative equations of motion are derived by promoting the global spin rotation symmetry to a local transformation and computing the in-in expectation values of the external sector operators through retarded Green's functions, whose spectral functions are constrained by symmetry and analyticity. The framework is then generalized to anisotropic materials by including background fields or spurion condensates, and to inhomogeneous materials by including higher-derivative couplings.

## Context

This work extends the conservative magnetoelastic EFT of Pavaskar, Penco, and Rothstein (2021) to include dissipative dynamics, using techniques previously applied to black hole and astrophysical systems by Endlich and Penco, and by Goldberger, Rothstein, and Li. It provides a first-principles derivation that distinguishes the Gilbert damping equation (Gilbert, 1955) from the Landau-Lifshitz damping equation (Landau and Lifshitz, 1935) based on symmetry principles, and reproduces the generalized Gilbert damping for spin textures found by Zhang and Zhang in the condensed matter literature. The framework enables systematic computation of higher-derivative corrections and predicts the generic form of phononic contributions to damping, which are expected to be significant in insulating materials where electron-magnon coupling is suppressed.
