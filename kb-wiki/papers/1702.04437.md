# Quark-gluon Vertex: A Perturbation Theory Primer and Beyond

> There has been growing evidence that the infrared enhancement of the form factors defining the full quark-gluon vertex plays an important role in realizing a dynamical breakdown of chiral symmetry in quantum chromodynamics, leading to the observed spectrum and properties of hadrons. Both the lattice and the Schwinger-Dyson communities have begun to calculate these form factors in various kinematical regimes of momenta involved. A natural consistency check for these studies is that they should match onto the perturbative predictions in the ultraviolet, where non-perturbative effects mellow down. In this article, we carry out a numerical analysis of the one-loop result for all the form factors of the quark-gluon vertex. Interestingly, even the one-loop results qualitatively encode most of the infrared enhancement features expected of their non-perturbative counter parts. We analyze various kinematical configurations of momenta: symmetric, on-shell and asymptotic. The on-shell limit enables us to compute anomalous chromomagnetic moment of quarks. The asymptotic results have implications for the multiplicative renormalizability of the quark propagator and its connection with the Landau-Khalatnikov-Fradkin transformations, allowing us to analyze and compare various Ans$\ddot{a}$tze proposed so far.

## Key claim

The one-loop perturbative calculation of the quark-gluon vertex form factors qualitatively encodes most of the infrared enhancement features expected of their non-perturbative counterparts, though the perturbative rise is only about 10% above the tree-level value compared to more than 100% seen in non-perturbative lattice and Schwinger-Dyson equation studies.

## Method

The authors performed a numerical analysis of the one-loop quark-gluon vertex in quantum chromodynamics using the tensor decomposition of Davydychev et al. (2000) into longitudinal and transverse form factors, computed for symmetric, on-shell, and asymptotic kinematical configurations of momenta. They employed momentum subtraction renormalization and evaluated both Abelian (QED-like) and non-Abelian (triple-gluon) contributions in arbitrary covariant gauge, using the singularity-free transverse basis of Kizilersu et al. (1995). The renormalized form factors were extrapolated into the infrared to examine their qualitative behavior.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop form factor \(1+\lambda_{1R}(p^2,-\mu^2)\) in the Landau gauge shows approximately 10% infrared increase from its tree-level value, while non-perturbative effects reveal more than 100% rise at the lowest momentum where lattice results exist (Skullerud 2003).
- In the Landau gauge at zero momentum, the longitudinal form factors saturate at values: \(\lambda_{1R}^b(p^2=0) \rightarrow 0.119\), \(\lambda_2^b(p^2=0) \rightarrow 0.177/\text{GeV}^2\), and \(\lambda_3^b(p^2=0) \rightarrow 0.183/\text{GeV}\), evaluated with \(\alpha=0.118\), \(m=0.115\) GeV, and \(\mu=2\) GeV.
- The infrared enhancement of \(1+\lambda_{1R}\) decreases with increasing quark mass at rates of approximately 7.2% for u/d quarks, 7% for s quarks, 2.2% for c quarks, and 0.3% for b quarks.
- The non-Abelian contribution to \(\lambda_{1R}\) in the Feynman gauge is about 95% more enhanced in the infrared compared to the Abelian contribution.

## Context

This work extends the one-loop perturbative calculation of the quark-gluon vertex by Davydychev et al. (2000) to provide explicit numerical and analytical benchmarks for non-perturbative studies from lattice QCD (Skullerud 2002, 2003, 2004) and Schwinger-Dyson equation approaches (Bhagwat 2004). The results serve as a consistency check that non-perturbative vertex constructions must reproduce the perturbative ultraviolet limit, and enable systematic improvement of vertex Ansatze used in studies of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and hadron properties.
