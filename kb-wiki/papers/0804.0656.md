# The role of $N^{*}(1535)$ in $η'$ production

> We study the near-threshold $η'$ production mechanism in nucleon-nucleon and $πN$ collisions under the assumption that sub-threshold resonance $N^{*}(1535)$ is predominant. In an effective Lagrangian approach which gives a reasonable description to the $pN \to pN η$ and $πp \to p η$ reactions, it is found that t-channel $π$ exchange make the dominate contribution to the $pN \to pN η'$ process, and a value of 6.5 for the ratio of $σ(pn \to pn η')$ to $σ(pp \to pp η')$ is predicted. A strong coupling strength of $N^{*}(1535)$ to $η' N$ ($g_{η' NN^*}^2 /4π= 1.1$) is extracted from a combined analysis to $pp \to pp η'$ and $πN \to N η'$, and the possible implication to the intrinsic component of $N^{*}(1535)$ is explored.

## Key claim

Under the assumption that the sub-threshold resonance \(N^{*}(1535)\) is predominant in near-threshold \(\eta'\) production, the authors find that t-channel \(\pi\) exchange makes the dominant contribution to the \(pN \to pN \eta'\) process, and they predict a ratio of 6.5 for \(\sigma(pn \to pn \eta')\) to \(\sigma(pp \to pp \eta')\), though they note that other \(N^{*}\) resonances cannot be definitely excluded due to scarce data.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach at the relativistic tree level, treating the reactions \(pp \to pp \eta(\eta')\), \(pn \to pn \eta(\eta')\), and \(\pi N \to N \eta(\eta')\) with Feynman diagrams. Meson exchanges are restricted to those observed in the decay channels of the adopted resonances, with most coupling constants fixed by experimental decay ratios and only cut-off parameters in form factors adjustable. The \(^{1}S_{0}\) proton-proton final state interaction is included via Watson-Migdal factorization using the Jost function, and the total cross section is computed by Monte Carlo integration over phase space.

## Key numbers

- The coupling strength of \(N^{*}(1535)\) to \(\eta' N\) is extracted as \(g_{\eta' NN^*}^2 /4\pi = 1.1\) from a combined analysis of \(pp \to pp \eta'\) and \(\pi N \to N \eta'\).
- The ratio of \(\sigma(pn \to pn \eta')\) to \(\sigma(pp \to pp \eta')\) is predicted to be 6.5.
- For the \(\eta\) production, the coupling constant \(g_{\eta NN}^2 /4\pi = 0.4\) is used, with cut-off parameters \(\Lambda_{\pi} = 1.05\) GeV for \(\pi NN\), \(\Lambda_{\rho} = 0.92\) GeV for \(\rho NN\), \(\Lambda_{\eta} = 2.00\) GeV for \(\eta NN\), and \(\Lambda_{M} = 0.80\) GeV for meson-nucleon-resonance vertexes.
- The \(^{1}S_{0}\) \(pp\) final state interaction uses scattering length \(a = -7.82\) fm and effective range \(r = 2.79\) fm (i.e., \(\alpha = -20.5\) MeV and \(\beta = 166.7\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(\eta\) production in nucleon-nucleon collisions (e.g., Wilkin, Riska, Nakappeta1, Shyam) to the \(\eta'\) channel, assuming the same dominant role of \(N^{*}(1535)\) resonance. It contrasts with an early analysis by Gedalin (no resonant term) and a relativistic meson exchange model by Nakaetapriearly (which invoked missing resonances \(S_{11}(1897)\) and \(P_{11}(1986)\)). The results enable future experimental tests at COSY (Moskal) to distinguish between \(\pi\) exchange dominance and direct gluon production mechanisms, and provide constraints on the intrinsic strangeness or gluonium content of the \(N^{*}(1535)\) resonance.
