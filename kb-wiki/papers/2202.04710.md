# Deconfinement and Error Thresholds in Holography

> We study the error threshold properties of holographic quantum error-correcting codes. We demonstrate that holographic CFTs admit an algebraic threshold, which is related to the confinement-deconfinement phase transition. We then apply geometric intuition from holography and the Hawking-Page phase transition to motivate the CFT result, and comment on potential extensions to other confining theories.

## Key claim

Holographic CFTs admit an algebraic error threshold against thermal noise, which coincides with the confinement-deconfinement phase transition temperature; however, unlike conventional quantum error-correcting codes where the logical error rate decays exponentially below threshold, the holographic code's logical error rate decays only polynomially in the system size.

## Method

The authors treat AdS/CFT as an approximate subsystem code, where logical information is stored in a central bulk region A and the remaining bulk degrees of freedom are treated as gauge qubits. They consider single-shot thermal noise applied locally to boundary sites, modeled by coupling each site to a heat bath at temperature T for a short time, and estimate the logical error rate by evaluating the Knill-Laflamme condition for subsystem codes, expressed in terms of commutators between logical operators and error operators. The logical error rate is then related to the vacuum-subtracted internal energy of the boundary CFT, whose scaling with the gauge group rank N determines the threshold behavior.

## Key numbers

- The logical error rate from gravitational dressing scales as $\mathcal{E}_O \sim O(1/N)$, where $N$ is the rank of the dual gauge group and $G_N \sim 1/N^2$ is Newton's constant.
- Below the deconfinement temperature $T < T_c$, the vacuum-subtracted internal energy $\langle \epsilon \rangle$ is at most $O(N^0)$, giving a logical error rate $\mathcal{E}(T<T_c) \sim O(N^0)/N^4$, which vanishes as $N \rightarrow \infty$.
- Above the critical temperature $T \gtrsim T_c$, the internal energy scales as $\langle \epsilon \rangle \sim N^2/L$ (where $L$ is the fixed length scale), yielding a logical error rate $\mathcal{E}(T\gtrsim T_c) \sim O(1)$ that remains finite as $N \rightarrow \infty$.

## Context

This work extends the connection between error thresholds and confinement-deconfinement phase transitions previously established for topological codes such as the toric code (Kitaev 2003, Dennis 2002), where the optimal threshold corresponds to the confinement-deconfinement transition in a random plaquette gauge model. The authors argue that the holographic code's threshold is set by the Hawking-Page phase transition (Witten 1998), which is dual to the confinement-deconfinement transition in strongly coupled boundary theories (Aharony 2003). This opens the possibility that the connection between thresholds and confinement may extend to other confining theories such as QCD, though the holographic code exhibits a qualitatively different algebraic (rather than exponential) suppression of logical errors below threshold.
