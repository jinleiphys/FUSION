# Quark Model Calculations of the N to Delta Reaction

> The electromagnetic excitation of the nucleon resonances is studied in the framework of Constituent Quark Models. Particular attention is devoted to the transition to the $Δ$ resonance and to the issue of a possible deformation of hadronic systems, mainly in connection with the problem of the quadrupole excitation. The analysis of the emerging discrepancies between data and theoretical predictions is discussed and shown to lead to important conclusions concerning the internal dynamical structure of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Constituent Quark Models (CQMs) systematically underestimate the electromagnetic transition strength for the N to Delta excitation, particularly the magnetic dipole (M1) amplitude by about 30% and the quadrupole (E2) strength, and that this discrepancy points to the need for explicit inclusion of meson cloud and/or quark-antiquark pair effects which are absent in pure valence quark models.

## Method

The author reviews several Constituent Quark Models (Isgur-Karl, Capstick-Isgur, hypercentral CQM, Goldstone Boson Exchange model, Bonn instanton model) and compares their predictions for the electromagnetic helicity amplitudes of the N to Delta transition. The transition amplitudes are calculated using a one-body quark current in impulse approximation, with the transverse photon-quark interaction given by Eq. (htm), and the resulting helicity amplitudes A1/2 and A3/2 are evaluated at the photon point (Q2=0) and as functions of Q2. The theoretical predictions are compared to experimental data and to pion cloud contributions from the Mainz dynamical model.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical M1 transition strength G_M1 from early quark model calculations is 3.7 (in units where the proton magnetic moment mu_P is used), compared to the measured value of about 5, representing an underestimate of roughly 30%.
- The PDG value for the quadrupole ratio R = -G_E2/G_M1 at the photon point is -0.02 ± 0.005; the hyperfine-interaction CQM gives R ≈ -0.02.
- The hypercentral CQM predicts a proton core radius of about 0.5 fm, which is required to reproduce the vanishing of the A1/2 helicity amplitude for the F15(1680) resonance.
- The hCQM potential parameters from the spectrum fit are tau = 4.59 and alpha = 1.61 (fm)^{-2}.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the long-standing problem of the N to Delta transition within Constituent Quark Models, following the early calculations of Becchi and Morpurgo (1965) and the Isgur-Karl model (1978). The paper contrasts the CQM predictions with the Mainz dynamical model (DMT) and coupled channel approaches, showing that pion cloud contributions can partially fill the gap between theory and data. The analysis supports a picture of the nucleon as a small quark core (radius ~0.5 fm) surrounded by a meson/sea-quark cloud, and points to the need for future work incorporating explicit three-quark-pion components and relativistic covariant formulations.
