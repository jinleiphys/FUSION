# Light Front Nuclear Theory

> High energy scattering experiments involving nuclei are typically analyzed in terms of light front variables. The desire to provide realistic,relativistic wave functions expressed in terms of these variables led me to try to use light front dynamics to compute nuclear wave functions. Here calculations of infinite nuclear matter in the mean field approximation and also in a light front version of Bruckner theory which includes NN correlations are reviewed. Applications of these wave functions to nuclear deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes are discussed. We find that relativistic mean field theory produces no EMC binding effect.

# Light Front Nuclear Theory

## Key claim

Relativistic mean field theory produces no EMC binding effect; the apparent depletion of the nuclear structure function at x ~ 0.5 vanishes when the correct covariant connection between the nucleon momentum distribution and the deep inelastic structure function is used, shifting the nucleon distribution peak to unity rather than 0.65.

## Method

The author reviews light front calculations of infinite nuclear matter using two approaches: first, the Walecka model in the mean field approximation, where classical meson fields are treated as constants; second, a light front version of Brueckner theory that includes nucleon-nucleon correlations via a chiral Lagrangian with nucleon, pion, sigma, omega, rho, eta, and delta fields, using the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation with retardation effects.

## Key numbers

- In the mean field approximation with Chin and Walecka (1974) parameters, the vector meson contribution to the total nuclear plus momentum is 0.35 of P+/Omega, while the nucleon contribution is only 0.65 of P+/Omega.
- The scalar potential reduces the effective nucleon mass to M* = 0.56 M_N, and the vector potential g_v V^- = 270 MeV.
- In the correlated Brueckner theory calculation, the computed nuclear compressibility is 180 MeV.
- The number of excess pions per nucleon is about 5%, accounting for about 2% of the nuclear plus momentum.
- The pionic enhancement of the longitudinal cross section sigma_L in electron-nucleus scattering is 15-30% at x values of 0.2-0.3.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier light front nuclear calculations (Miller 1997, 2000) and addresses the long-standing EMC effect puzzle, where the nuclear structure function is suppressed at x ~ 0.5. The mean field result contradicts the conventional interpretation that nucleons must carry 90% of the plus-momentum to explain the EMC effect (Sick 1992). The correlated calculation produces a smaller pionic content (5%) than the 20% found by Brown and Frankfurt, but agrees with the more recent Illinois calculation (Akmal 1997). The work enables future tests of pionic effects via longitudinal photon cross sections in electron-nucleus scattering, and provides a framework for computing boost-invariant nuclear wave functions needed for high-energy scattering analyses.
