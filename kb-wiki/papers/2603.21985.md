# Systematic Cranked Shell Model Calculations for $^{87, 89, 91}$Br

> A systematic investigation of the odd-mass neutron-rich bromine isotopes $^{87-91}$Br has been carried out within the configuration-constrained cranked shell model (CSM) framework. The calculated kinematic moments of inertia and angular-momentum alignments reproduce the experimental trends with excellent agreement for the proposed quasiparticle configurations, thereby supporting the assigned band structures. The total Routhian surface calculations reveal pronounced $γ$-softness in $^{87}$Br, shape coexistence in $^{89-93}$Br, and a gradual prolate-to-oblate transition approaching $N=56$. These results demonstrate that the CSM provides a reliable description of rotational behavior and shape evolution in this mass region, achieving a better level of accuracy, while offering a transparent interpretation of quasiparticle configurations and shape-driving effects.

# Systematic Cranked Shell Model Calculations for \(^{87, 89, 91}\)Br

## Key claim

The configuration-constrained cranked shell model (CSM) reproduces the experimental kinematic moments of inertia and angular-momentum alignments for the proposed one-quasiproton \(\pi g_{9/2}[440]1/2^+\) configurations in \(^{87,89,91}\)Br with excellent agreement at low to moderate rotational frequencies, but systematically underestimates \(\langle J_x \rangle\) at higher frequencies in \(^{87}\)Br, with the gap increasing toward \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.6\) MeV, indicating that neutron-driven collectivity and pairing reduction effects require improved treatment.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-constrained cranked shell model calculations using a triaxial Woods-Saxon potential with universal parametrization, incorporating the Strutinsky shell-correction method and BCS pairing correlations. Total Routhian surfaces were computed as functions of quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\), hexadecapole deformation \(\beta_4\), and triaxiality \(\gamma\) at rotational frequencies \(\omega = 0.0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6\) MeV/\(\hbar\) for each isotope, with specific quasiparticle orbitals blocked to generate rotational bands. The kinematic moments of inertia and aligned angular momenta were extracted from the calculated total Routhians and compared with experimental values derived from \(\gamma\)-ray transition energies.

## Key numbers

- \(^{87}\)Br: prolate deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.094\) at \(\omega = 0.0\) from CSM, compared to FRDM value \(\beta_2 = -0.105\); the \(9/2^+\) band is assigned to the \(\pi g_{9/2}[440]1/2^+\) Nilsson orbital with equilibrium \(\beta_2 \approx 0.1668\) for the one-quasiproton configuration.
- \(^{89}\)Br: prolate deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.115\) at \(\omega = 0.0\) from CSM, compared to FRDM value \(\beta_2 = 0.195\); experimental data available in the range \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.27\)–\(0.55\) MeV.
- \(^{91}\)Br: oblate deformation \(\beta_2 = -0.164\) at \(\omega = 0.0\) from CSM, compared to FRDM value \(\beta_2 = 0.241\); experimental data available in the range \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.25\)–\(0.5\) MeV.
- \(^{93}\)Br: oblate deformation \(\beta_2 = -0.204\) at \(\omega = 0.0\) from CSM, compared to FRDM value \(\beta_2 = 0.241\).
- \(^{85}\)Br: near-spherical shape with \(\beta_2 = -0.016\) at \(\omega = 0.0\) from CSM, compared to FRDM value \(\beta_2 = -0.042\).
- The pairing force strength parameters used: \(g_0^p = 17.90\), \(g_1^p = 0.176\), \(g_0^n = 18.95\), \(g_1^n = 0.078\), with pairing gap \(\widetilde{\Delta} = 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV.
- Contour line spacing in TRS plots is \(0.2\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends systematic CSM studies of neutron-rich bromine isotopes, building on prior experimental investigations of medium-spin states in \(^{87}\)Br and \(^{89}\)Br populated via cold-neutron-induced fission (Br87paper, Br89paper), and on large-scale shell model (LSSM) and discrete nonorthogonal (DNO) shell model calculations by Dudouet et al. (J.Dudouet) that identified a prolate-to-oblate transition at \(N=56\) with pseudo-SU3 symmetry. The results enable future exploration of higher-spin configurations and band crossings in this mass region, particularly the predicted sharp upbend in aligned angular momentum beyond the \(21/2^+\) state in \(^{89}\)Br and \(^{91}\)Br, and provide a benchmark for improved treatments of pairing reduction and core polarization in non-yrast bands.
