# On the treatment of the $Δ$-contribution in electromagnetic $pp$-knockout reactions

> The treatment of the $Δ$-current and its contribution in the exclusive $^{16}$O$(e,e'pp)^{14}$C and $^{16}$O$(γ,pp)^{14}$C knockout reactions are investigated in combination with the effects of correlations. Different parametrizations of the effective $Δ$-current and different treatments of correlations in the two-nucleon overlap function are considered. The results are presented and discussed for a suitable choice of kinematics. It is found that the investigation of different mutually supplementing kinematics is necessary to resolve the uncertainties in the theoretical ingredients and extract clear and unambiguous information on correlations.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates how different parametrizations of the effective Δ-current affect calculated cross sections for exclusive pp-knockout reactions, finding that the theoretical uncertainties from the Δ-treatment can be large and that no single parametrization is fundamental; instead, the choice of kinematics (symmetrical vs. super-parallel) determines whether the Δ-contribution is suppressed or maximized, and whether interference with the one-body current amplifies or minimizes the model dependence.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for the reactions ¹⁶O(e,e'pp)¹⁴C and ¹⁶O(γ,pp)¹⁴C using a direct knockout model with a nuclear charge-current operator, a two-nucleon overlap function (TOF) describing correlations, and a final-state wave function including optical potential distortions but neglecting mutual NN final-state interactions. They compared three parametrizations of the Δ-current: an unregularized pionic-only version (Δ(NoReg)), a version with π+ρ exchange regularized from NN-scattering data (Δ(NN)), and a version regularized from πN-scattering data (Δ(πN)), plus a variant with a medium-modified propagator (Δ(πN, mod)). The TOFs came from three approaches: a simple shell-model with Jastrow-type short-range correlations (SM-SRC), and two spectral-function-based methods including both short-range and long-range correlations (SF-A and SF-B).

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant for the M1 transition γN→Δ is G^{ΔN}_{M1} = 4.22, extracted from elementary photopionproduction cross sections.
* The Δ mass is M_Δ = 1232 MeV; the propagator uses an energy-dependent decay width Γ_Δ from the literature.
* For the Δ(NN) parametrization: f²_{πNN}/4π = 0.078, f²_{πNΔ}/4π = 0.224, f²_{ρNN}/4π = 7.10, f²_{ρNΔ}/4π = 20.45, with cutoffs Λ_{πNN}=1300 MeV, Λ_{πNΔ}=1200 MeV, Λ_{ρNN}=1400 MeV, Λ_{ρNΔ}=1000 MeV.
* For the Δ(πN) parametrization: f²_{πNΔ}/4π = 1.393, Λ_{πNΔ} = 287.9 MeV; f²_{πNN}/4π = 0.078, Λ_{πNN}=1300 MeV.
* In the symmetrical (e,e'pp) kinematics at E₀=855 MeV, ω=215 MeV, k=316 MeV/c, the Δ-contribution is very small and the cross section is dominated by the one-body longitudinal charge term; different TOFs change the size by factors of 2–3 (SM-SRC ~2–3 times larger than SF-B, SF-B 20–40% larger than SF-A).
* In the symmetrical (γ,pp) kinematics at E_γ=400 MeV, the Δ-current dominates completely; differences between Δ-parametrizations exceed one order of magnitude at the peak.
* In the (γ,pp) kinematics at E_γ=120 MeV, the Δ-current produces a significant enhancement of the ³P₁ component and a slight reduction of the ¹S₀ one; uncertainties from Δ-parametrizations are within a factor of ~2.
* In the super-parallel (e,e'pp) kinematics, the unregularized Δ(NoReg) treatment gives results differing both in size and shape from the three regularized versions, which are close to each other; destructive interference between one-body and Δ-currents occurs for regularized treatments.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic two-nucleon knockout reactions (Boffi and Giusti, 1996; Giusti and Pacati, 1997, 1998; Ryckebusch et al., 1994, 1997, 2004; Coraggio et al., 2003, 2004) by systematically comparing multiple Δ-current parametrizations in combination with different treatments of short-range and long-range correlations. The results show that symmetrical kinematics in (e,e'pp) are clean for extracting correlation information because the Δ-contribution is negligible, while symmetrical kinematics in (γ,pp) are dominated by the Δ-current and can help constrain its parametrization. The super-parallel kinematics in (e,e'pp) maximize interference effects and reveal dramatic differences between regularized and unregularized Δ-treatments, indicating that future experiments with complementary kinematics are necessary to resolve theoretical uncertainties and extract unambiguous information on short-range correlations.
