# Conformal hydrodynamics in Minkowski and de Sitter spacetimes

> We show how to generate non-trivial solutions to the conformally invariant, relativistic fluid dynamic equations by appealing to the Weyl covariance of the stress tensor. We use this technique to show that a recently studied solution of the relativistic conformally invariant Navier-Stokes equations in four-dimensional Minkowski space can be recast as a static flow in three-dimensional de Sitter space times a line. The simplicity of the de Sitter form of the flow enables us to consider several generalizations of it, including flows in other spacetime dimensions, second order viscous corrections, and linearized perturbations. We also construct the anti-de Sitter dual of the original four-dimensional flow. Finally, we discuss possible applications to nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a nontrivial solution of the relativistic conformally invariant Navier-Stokes equations in four-dimensional Minkowski space, previously derived using symmetry considerations, can be obtained more simply by Weyl-rescaling the metric to a static flow in three-dimensional de Sitter space times a line; the method works only for conformally invariant fluids where the pressure equals one-third of the energy density and bulk viscosity vanishes.

## Method

The authors start from the Minkowski metric in Bjorken coordinates and perform a Weyl rescaling by a factor of \(1/\tau^2\) to map the future wedge of \(\mathbf{R}^{3,1}\) onto \(dS_3 \times \mathbf{R}\). They then write the fluid velocity as a static configuration in the de Sitter frame (\(\hat{u}_\rho = -1\), all other components zero) and use the transformation laws for the velocity and energy density under Weyl rescalings to recover the original Minkowski-space flow. The same technique is extended to arbitrary spacetime dimensions, to include shear viscosity at first order in gradients, and to study linearized perturbations around the background flow.

## Key numbers

- The radial velocity in the Minkowski frame is given by \(v_\perp = \tanh \kappa(\tau,x_\perp) = \frac{2q^2 \tau x_\perp}{1 + q^2 \tau^2 + q^2 x_\perp^2}\), where \(q\) is an arbitrary dimensionful constant with units of inverse length.
- The energy density in the Minkowski frame is \(\epsilon = \frac{\hat\epsilon_0}{\tau^{4/3}} \frac{(2q)^{8/3}}{\left[1 + 2q^2(\tau^2 + x_\perp^2) + q^4(\tau^2 - x_\perp^2)^2\right]^{4/3}}\), with \(\hat\epsilon_0\) a dimensionless integration constant.
- In the de Sitter frame, the inviscid energy density scales as \(\hat\epsilon = \hat\epsilon_0 (\cosh\rho)^{-8/3}\) for \(d=4\).
- For a viscous fluid in \(d\) dimensions with shear viscosity \(\hat\eta = \mathrm{H}_0 \hat{T}^{d-1}\), the de Sitter temperature evolves as \(\hat{T}(\rho) = (\cosh\rho)^{-\frac{d-2}{d-1}} \left[ \hat{T}_0 + \frac{2(d-2)}{d(d-1)} \mathrm{H}_0 F_d(\rho) \right]\), where \(F_d(\rho)\) is a hypergeometric function defined in the text.

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken flow solution (Bjorken, 1982) by adding radial expansion through an \(SO(3)\) conformal symmetry, building on the earlier \(SO(3)\)-invariant flow of Gubser (2010). The method of Weyl-rescaling to a static de Sitter frame provides a systematic way to generate new analytic solutions of conformal hydrodynamics and to include viscous corrections, second-order terms, and linear perturbations. The results enable the study of anisotropic flow and instabilities in a toy model of quark-gluon plasma expansion at RHIC and the LHC, and they also allow construction of the dual anti-de Sitter black hole geometry via holography.
