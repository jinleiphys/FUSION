# Anomalous population of $^{10}$He states in reactions with $^{11}$Li

> Structure with the lowest energy observed in the $^{10}$He spectrum populated in the proton knockout reaction with $^{11}$Li beam has a peak at $1.2-1.5$ MeV. This peak is usually interpreted as a resonant $0^+$ ground state of $^{10}$He. Our theoretical calculations indicate that this peak is likely to be a pileup of $1^-$, $0^+$, and $2^+$ excitations with very similar shapes. %We predict a very specific nature of the $1^-$ excitation in $^{10}$He. Moreover, the ``soft'' $1^-$ excitation appears to be the lowest one in energy. Such an anomalous continuum response is traced to the halo structure of $^{11}$Li providing extreme low energy shift to all the expected continuum excitations. Competitions of the initial state structure (ISS) and the final state interaction (FSI) effects on the spectrum and three-body correlations in $^{10}$He are discussed. Analogous effect of the extreme low-energy shift could also be expected in other cases of $2n$ emitters populated in reactions with halo nuclei. Simplified example of the $^{10}$He spectrum in $α$ knockout from $^{14}$Be, is given. We also discuss limits on the properties of $^{9}$He stemming from the observed $^{10}$He spectrum.

## Key claim

The low-energy peak (1.2-1.5 MeV) in the $^{10}$He spectrum from proton knockout on $^{11}$Li, usually interpreted as a pure $0^+$ ground state, is likely a pileup of $1^-$, $0^+$, and $2^+$ excitations with very similar shapes; the "soft" $1^-$ excitation appears to be the lowest in energy, an anomalous continuum response traced to the halo structure of $^{11}$Li.

## Method

The authors model the sudden removal of a proton from $^{11}$Li by solving an inhomogeneous three-body Schrodinger equation for the $^{10}$He continuum in a $^{8}$He+$n$+$n$ cluster model. The source function for $^{10}$He production is obtained by acting on the $^{11}$Li wavefunction (combining a three-body cluster WF from Shulgina (2009) with a Woods-Saxon proton WF) with an annihilation operator in momentum space, then decomposing into angular momentum channels. The cross section is proportional to the outgoing flux, and calculations are performed both with and without final state interactions (FSI) to separate initial state structure (ISS) and FSI effects.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy peak in the $^{10}$He spectrum from $^{11}$Li knockout is observed at $E_T = 1.2(3)$ MeV (Korsheninnikov 1994) and $E_T = 1.4-1.5$ MeV with $\Gamma \sim 1.9$ MeV (Johansson 2010).
- In the $^{3}$H($^{8}$He,$p$) transfer reaction, a peak at $E_T = 2.1(2)$ MeV with $\Gamma \sim 2$ MeV was observed (Sidorchuk 2012).
- The realistic $^{11}$Li source yields peak positions for $0^+$, $1^-$, and $2^+$ states all in the range $E_T \sim 1.3-1.6$ MeV.
- The rms hyperradius of the $^{11}$Li wavefunction is $\langle \rho \rangle \sim 8.36$ fm.
- The $^{8}$He-$n$ $s$-wave scattering length used in the model is $a_s \approx 3$ fm, with the $p_{1/2}$ ground state of $^{9}$He at 2 MeV and the $d_{5/2}$ state at about 5 MeV.
- For the $0^+$ state, the source rms hyperradius is about 7.8 fm at 140 MeV/c; for $1^-$, about 10.5 fm at 175 MeV/c; for $2^+$, about 13.5 fm at 210 MeV/c.
- The limiting condition for consistency with data requires the $^{10}$He ground state peak position to be not lower than 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-body $^{8}$He+$n$+$n$ model of Grigorenko (2008) and the qualitative calculations of Sidorchuk (2012) to explain the apparent contradiction between knockout data (Korsheninnikov 1994, Johansson 2010) and transfer reaction data (Golovkov 2009, Sidorchuk 2012) on $^{10}$He. The authors demonstrate that the halo structure of $^{11}$Li produces an extreme low-energy shift in all continuum excitations, making the spectrum dominated by initial state structure rather than final state interactions. This work enables reinterpretation of existing $^{10}$He data and provides constraints on the $^{9}$He spectrum; it also suggests that analogous effects may occur in other $2n$ emitters populated in reactions with halo nuclei.
