# Resolving neutrino mass hierarchy from supernova (anti)neutrino-nucleus reactions

> We introduce a hybrid method to determine neutrino mass hierarchy by simultaneous measurements of detector responses induced by antineutrino and neutrino fluxes from accretion and cooling phase of type II supernova. The (anti)neutrino-nucleus cross sections for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{56}$Fe and $^{208}$Pb are calculated in the framework of relativistic nuclear energy density functional and weak Hamiltonian, while the cross sections for inelastic scattering on free protons, $p(\barν_{e},e^{+})n$, are obtained using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The simulations of (anti)neutrino fluxes emitted from a protoneutron star in a core-collapse supernova include collective and Mickheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein effects inside star. The emission rates of elementary decay modes of daughter nuclei are calculated for normal and inverted neutrino mass hierarchy. It is shown that simultaneous use of (anti)neutrino detectors with different target material and time dependence of the signal allow to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy from the ratios of $ν_e / $ $\barν_e$ induced particle emissions. The hybrid method favors detectors with heavier target nuclei ($^{208}$Pb) for the neutrino sector, while for antineutrinos the use of free protons and light nuclei ($\text{H}_2\text{O}$ or $\text{-CH}_2\text{-}$) represent appropriate choice.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a hybrid method to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy by simultaneously measuring detector responses from both neutrino and antineutrino fluxes during the accretion and cooling phases of a type II supernova, but the method relies on the relative ratios of events between different detector materials rather than absolute event counts, and the accretion phase signal is degenerate between normal and inverted hierarchies.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 12C, 16O, 56Fe, and 208Pb using the relativistic nuclear energy density functional (RNEDF) framework with the DD-ME2 effective interaction, and cross sections for inelastic scattering on free protons, p(ν̄e,e+)n, using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. They simulated supernova neutrino fluxes including collective neutrino-neutrino interactions and Mikheev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) matter effects for both normal and inverted mass hierarchies, then computed the number of detector events (gamma, 1-neutron, and 2-neutron emissions) for each target material over the accretion phase (0.2 s) and cooling phase (10 s) for a test supernova at 12000 light-years distance.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb in the cooling phase, the flux-averaged cross section for 1n-emission from 208Bi is 5.6×10−40 cm2 for normal mass hierarchy (NMH) and 3.8×10−40 cm2 for inverted mass hierarchy (IMH)
- For 208Pb in the cooling phase, the 2n-emission cross section is 1.2×10−40 cm2 for NMH and 0.6×10−40 cm2 for IMH
- Total emitted neutrons from 208Pb: approximately 880 for NMH and 575 for IMH, giving a ratio NnNMH/NnIMH ≈ 1.5
- Ratio of 2n-emissions from 208Pb: N2nNMH/N2nIMH ≈ 2.0
- Total neutrons emitted from 56Fe (both νe and ν̄e): approximately 165 for NMH and 110 for IMH, ratio ≈ 1.5
- For the hybrid method combining 1 kt water and 1 kt 208Pb in the cooling phase: NnNMH(H2O)/NnNMH(Pb) = 0.91 ± 0.05 and NnIMH(H2O)/NnIMH(Pb) = 1.36 ± 0.08
- Time-difference ratio for 208Pb: NC NMH(Pb)/NA NMH(Pb) = 7.3 ± 0.8 and NC IMH(Pb)/NA IMH(Pb) = 4.4 ± 0.5
- Initial accretion luminosities: 2.4 × 1052 ergs for νe, 2.0 × 1052 ergs for ν̄e, and 1052 ergs for other species
- Initial cooling luminosities: 1.2 × 1051 ergs for νe and ν̄e, 1.8 × 1051 ergs for non-electron species

## Context

This work extends previous approaches to resolving the neutrino mass hierarchy that used reactor neutrinos (Pet. 2002, Li. 2013, Cap. 2014), Earth matter effects on supernova neutrino signals (Lun. 2003, DDM. 2008), spectral swapping (Dua. 2007), and rise time of supernova ν̄e light curves (Ser. 2012). The hybrid method is designed to be used with existing and planned detectors such as HALO (208Pb) and Super-Kamiokande (water), and it enables a practical diagnostic tool for the next galactic supernova event to determine whether the neutrino mass hierarchy is normal or inverted.
