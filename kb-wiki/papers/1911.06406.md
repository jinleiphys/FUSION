# Global flow structure and exact formal transseries of the Gubser flow in kinetic theory

> In this work we introduce the generic conditions for the existence of a non-equilibrium attractor that is an invariant manifold determined by the long-wavelength modes of the physical system. We investigate the topological properties of the global flow structure of the Gubser flow for the Israel-Stewart theory and a kinetic model for the Boltzmann equation by employing Morse-Smale theory. We present a complete classification of the invariant submanifolds of the flow and determine all the possible flow lines connecting any pair of UV/IR fixed points. The formal transseries solutions to the Gubser dynamical system around the early-time (UV) and late-time (IR) fixed points are constructed and analyzed. It is proven that these solutions are purely perturbative (or power-law asymptotic) series with a finite radius of convergence. Based on these analyses, we find that Gubser-like expanding kinetic systems do not hydrodynamize owing to the failure of the hydrodynamization process which heavily relies on the classification of (non)hydrodynamic modes in the IR regime. This is in contrast to longitudinal boost-invariant plasmas where the asymptotic dynamics is described by a few terms of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion. We finally compare our results for both Bjorken and Gubser conformal kinetic models.

## Key claim

Gubser-like expanding kinetic systems do not hydrodynamize because the Knudsen number remains large and a slow manifold does not exist, in contrast to longitudinal boost-invariant plasmas where the asymptotic dynamics is described by a few terms of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion. The formal transseries solutions around the IR fixed point are proven to be convergent series with a finite radius of convergence, meaning the distribution does not converge to thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors analyze the Gubser flow for the Israel-Stewart (IS) theory and a kinetic model for the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA). They employ Morse-Smale theory to classify the invariant submanifolds and determine all possible flow lines connecting UV and IR fixed points, using the skew product flow formalism to convert the nonautonomous dynamical system into an autonomous one by introducing the variable τ = tanh ρ. Formal transseries solutions are constructed around the early-time (UV) and late-time (IR) fixed points and their convergence properties are analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The fixed points of the IS theory in the extended configuration space (T, π̄, τ) are: ΣU(A) = (0, +1/√5, -1), ΣI(A) = (0, +1/√5, +1), ΣU(B) = (0, -1/√5, -1), ΣI(B) = (0, -1/√5, +1), ΣU(C) = (+38c/15, 2, -1), ΣI(C) = (-38c/15, 2, +1), where c > 0 is a dimensionless scale relating shear relaxation time to η/s.
- Instability indices for the fixed points are: Ind[JU(A)] = 3 (source), Ind[JI(A)] = 0 (sink), Ind[JU(B)] = 2 (saddle), Ind[JI(B)] = 1 (saddle), Ind[JU(C)] = 2 (saddle), Ind[JI(C)] = 1 (saddle).
- The eigenvalues for ΣU,I(A) are (±(2 - 1/√5)/3, ±8/(3√5), ±2), and for ΣU,I(B) are (±(2 + 1/√5)/3, ∓8/(3√5), ±2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings on the Gubser flow as a dynamical system (Behtash 2017) and generalizes the analysis using Morse-Smale decomposition and transseries methods. It contrasts with the Bjorken flow case studied by Heller and Spalinski (2015) and others, where a hydrodynamic attractor exists and the asymptotic dynamics is described by a gradient expansion. The results enable a rigorous classification of when hydrodynamization occurs in expanding plasmas and show that the curvature of dS3 × R spacetime prevents the formation of a slow manifold, unlike the boost-invariant case where the attractor connects UV and IR fixed points.
