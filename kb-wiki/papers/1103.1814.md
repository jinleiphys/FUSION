# Homogeneous Relaxation at Strong Coupling from Gravity

> Homogeneous relaxation is a ubiquitous phenomenon in semiclassical kinetic theories where the quasiparticles are distributed uniformly in space, and the equilibration involves only their velocity distribution. For such solutions, the hydrodynamic variables remain constant. We construct asymptotically AdS solutions of Einstein's gravity dual to such processes at strong coupling, perturbatively in the amplitude expansion, where the expansion parameter is the ratio of the amplitude of the non-hydrodynamic shear-stress tensor to the pressure. At each order, we sum over all time derivatives through exact recursion relations. We argue that the metric has a regular future horizon, order by order in the amplitude expansion, provided the shear-stress tensor follows an equation of motion. At the linear order, this equation of motion implies that the metric perturbations are composed of zero wavelength quasinormal modes. Our method allows us to calculate the non-linear corrections to this equation perturbatively in the amplitude expansion. We thus derive a special case of our previous conjecture on the regularity condition on the boundary stress tensor that endows the bulk metric with a regular future horizon, and also refine it further. We also propose a new outlook for heavy-ion phenomenology at RHIC and ALICE.

## Key claim

The authors construct asymptotically AdS solutions of Einstein's gravity dual to homogeneous relaxation processes at strong coupling, perturbatively in the amplitude expansion, and argue that the metric has a regular future horizon order by order provided the shear-stress tensor follows an equation of motion; this derivation is a special case of their previous conjecture on regularity conditions for the boundary stress tensor.

## Method

The authors construct asymptotically AdS metrics in Fefferman-Graham coordinates for boundary energy-momentum tensors of the form where hydrodynamic variables are constant and only the shear-stress tensor depends on time, expanding perturbatively in the ratio of the shear-stress amplitude to the pressure. At each order in the amplitude expansion, all time derivatives are summed through exact recursion relations using Fourier transformation of the time dependence. The metric is then transformed to ingoing Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, and regularity of the future horizon is analyzed order by order to derive the equation of motion for the shear-stress tensor.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is the ratio of the amplitude of the non-hydrodynamic shear-stress tensor to the pressure, with no specific numerical value given for this ratio
- The equilibrium energy-momentum tensor takes the form $t_{\mu\nu} = (3u_{\mu}u_{\nu} + P_{\mu\nu})/(4b^4)$ where $b = 1/(\pi T)$
- The energy density is $\epsilon = 3/(4b^4)$ and the pressure is $p = 1/(4b^4)$
- The metric coefficients in the Taylor expansion near the boundary include $g_{(6)\mu\nu} = -(1/12)\Box t_{\mu\nu}$ and $g_{(8)\mu\nu} = (1/2)t_{\mu}^{\rho}t_{\rho\nu} - (1/24)\eta_{\mu\nu}(t^{\alpha\beta}t_{\alpha\beta}) + (1/384)\Box^2 t_{\mu\nu}$
- The scalar curvature of the five-dimensional spacetime is $R = -4/l^2$ (with $l=1$ in the chosen units)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous conjecture (Iyer and Mukhopadhyay, earlier work) that regularity of the future horizon in the gravity dual imposes a closed set of equations of motion on the boundary energy-momentum tensor, analogous to conservative solutions of the Boltzmann equation found by Carleman, Wild, and Morgenstern. The homogeneous relaxation case studied here connects to the scalar channel of quasinormal modes of AdS black branes at zero wavelengths (Horowitz, Starinets, Nunez, Kovtun, Berti) and enables a systematic derivation of non-linear corrections to the linear quasinormal mode equations, with potential applications to heavy-ion phenomenology at RHIC and ALICE.
