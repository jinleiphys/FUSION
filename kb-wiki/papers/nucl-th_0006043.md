# Coulomb and nuclear breakup of three-body halo nuclei

> We investigate dissociation reactions of loosely bound and spatially extended three-body systems. We formulate a practical method for simultaneous treatment of long-range Coulomb and short-range nuclear interactions. We use $^6$He (n+n+$α$) and $^{11}$Li (n+n+$^{9}$Li) as examples and study the two-neutron separation cross sections as functions of target and beam energy. Individual Coulomb and nuclear as well as interference contributions are also extracted.

## Key claim
The paper formulates a practical method to simultaneously treat long-range Coulomb and short-range nuclear interactions in breakup reactions of three-body halo nuclei, but the method relies on the sudden approximation (intrinsic halo motion slow compared to collision time) and a participant-spectator division that is only meaningful when the halo is larger than the combined sizes of the target and the participant.

## Method
The reaction is treated as independent collisions where one halo constituent (the participant) interacts with the target via an optical model that includes both Coulomb and nuclear potentials, while the other two constituents (spectators) are treated with a black-sphere model that counts them as absorbed if they lie within a cylinder of radius equal to the target plus spectator radius. The total two-neutron dissociation cross section is obtained by summing contributions from elastic scattering (diffraction) and absorption (stripping) for each possible participant, with momentum-transfer cutoffs applied for adiabatic excitation and for the three-body separation energy.

## Key numbers
- For a Pb target, the adiabatic cutoff is the largest and decisive: \(q_a > q_L\); for a C target, \(q_L\) is decisive (\(q_a < q_L\)); for a Cu target, the two cutoffs are comparable.
- The Coulomb contribution roughly scales with the square of the projectile charge; the nuclear part increases with projectile size.
- For a Cu target, the decreasing Coulomb contribution crosses the almost energy-independent nuclear contribution at about 300 MeV/nucleon for \(^6\)He and at 200 MeV/nucleon for \(^{11}\)Li.
- The absorption distances \(r_{ps}\) are determined by \(\frac{3}{5} r_{ps}^2 = \langle r^2 \rangle_t + \langle r^2 \rangle_s + 2\) fm\(^2\), with \(r_0\) = 1.26 fm, 1.30 fm, and 1.45 fm for Pb, Cu, and C targets, respectively.
- The binding energy ratio \(B_{ps}/B\) is approximately 3 for \(^6\)He and 1.4 for \(^{11}\)Li.

## Context
This work extends earlier fragmentary computations of halo breakup reactions (e.g., Bertsch et al. 1993, Oganessian et al. 1992, Suzuki et al. 1994, Banerjee et al. 1998, Cobis et al. 1998) by providing a consistent and systematic method that treats Coulomb and nuclear interactions simultaneously for three-body projectiles, rather than treating them as effective two-body systems or in independent models. The method enables systematic calculations of dissociation cross sections for three-body halos like \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li across beam energies and target masses, and the authors note that their results are consistent with estimates reported by Oganessian et al. (1992) but exceed low-energy experimental data by about a factor of two, as previously noted in Garrido et al. (1999).
