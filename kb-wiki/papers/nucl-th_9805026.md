# Backward Elastic p^3He-Scattering and High Momentum components of ^3He Wave Function

> It is shown that owing to a dominance of $np-$pair transfer mechanism of backward elastic $p^3He$- scattering for incident proton kinetic energies $T_p>1$ GeV the cross section of this process is defined mainly by the values of the Faddeev component of the wave function of $^3He$ nucleus, $ϕ^{23}({\bf q}_{23}, {\bf p}_1)$, at high relative momenta $q_{23}> 0.6 GeV/c$ of the NN-pair in the $^1S_0-$ state and at low spectator momenta $p_1\sim 0-0.2$ GeV/c.

## Key claim
The cross section of backward elastic p^3He-scattering at incident proton kinetic energies above 1 GeV is directly related to the high-momentum components of the Faddeev S-wave function of ^3He, specifically the relative momentum of the NN-pair in the ^1S_0 state above 0.6 GeV/c, while the spectator momentum remains low (0-0.2 GeV/c). This sensitivity contradicts earlier conclusions that the cross section had weak sensitivity to high-momentum components.

## Method
The author calculates the differential cross section of backward elastic p^3He-scattering at theta_c.m. = 180 degrees using the Born approximation for the sequential transfer (ST) mechanism of the noninteracting np-pair, which dominates over nonsequential transfer, interacting pair transfer, and one-pion exchange (OPE) mechanisms. The Faddeev wave function of ^3He from the solution of Faddeev equations for the RSC interaction potential is used, with a separable analytical parametrization. Rescatterings in the initial and final states are included via the Glauber-Sitenko approximation, and the OPE amplitude is modified from prior work using experimental pd -> ^3He pi^0 cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The cross section at theta_c.m. = 180 degrees is dominated by the ST mechanism for incident proton kinetic energies T_p > 1 GeV.
- The Faddeev component of the ^3He wave function is probed at high relative momenta q_23 > 0.6 GeV/c of the NN-pair in the ^1S_0 state.
- The spectator momentum p_1 involved in the amplitude is in the range 0-0.2 GeV/c.
- When the channel nu=1 (^1S_0 state of the NN-pair) is excluded from the full wave function, the cross section falls by 5-6 orders of magnitude for T_p > 1 GeV.
- The OPE mechanism contribution after including rescatterings is one order of magnitude lower than the experimental data.
- The spin-spin correlation parameter Sigma at theta_c.m. = 180 degrees and T_p = 1-2.5 GeV is approximately 0.1-0.15, independent of initial energy.
- Substituting the deuteron S-wave function for the ^3He wave function in the ST mechanism reduces the cross section by a factor of approximately 40.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of backward elastic p^3He-scattering from earlier studies by Uzikov and collaborators (1992, 1993) that identified the dominance of the sequential transfer mechanism but concluded the cross section had weak sensitivity to high-momentum components. The present paper demonstrates that the cross section is directly sensitive to high-momentum components of the ^3He Faddeev wave function, contrary to that earlier conclusion. The work also shows that the OPE mechanism, previously found to agree qualitatively with data by Berthold and collaborators, is suppressed by rescatterings and cannot explain the data at T_p > 1 GeV. This enables future measurements at higher energies to test phenomenological NN-potentials in describing the structure of lightest nuclei at high relative momenta.
