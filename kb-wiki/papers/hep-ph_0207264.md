# Parton Percolation and J/Psi Suppression

> The geometric clustering of partons in the transverse plane of nuclear collisions leads for increasing A or sqrt(s) to percolation. In the resulting condensate, the partons are deconfined but not yet in thermal equilibrium. We discuss quarkonium dissociation in this precursor of the quark-gluon plasma, with an onset of dissociation when the saturation scale of the parton condensate reaches that of the given quarkonium state.

# Parton Percolation and J/Psi Suppression

## Key claim

The onset of anomalous J/ψ suppression in nuclear collisions can be explained by parton percolation, the geometric clustering of partons in the transverse plane, which produces a deconfined but not yet thermalized parton condensate; dissociation of a given quarkonium state occurs when the saturation scale of this condensate reaches the inverse size of that state, providing a parameter-free description of the observed suppression pattern.

## Method

The authors calculate the parton density in the transverse plane of central AA collisions using next-to-leading order GRV94 parton distribution functions, then determine the percolation threshold via the condition n_q π r² = η_c = 1.128, where n_q is the parton density and r is the parton transverse size. They compare the resulting saturation scale Q_s to the inverse radii of charmonium states (J/ψ, χ_c, ψ′) obtained from the Schrödinger equation with the Cornell potential, assuming a charmonium state is dissociated if it finds itself in a condensate with Q_s ≥ Q_i, where Q_i is the scale of that state.

## Key numbers

- Percolation threshold: η_c = 1.128, determined by extensive numerical studies of percolation theory.
- At the percolation point, only the fraction 1 − exp(−η_c) ≈ 2/3 of the nuclear area is covered by parton discs.
- Critical exponent for the size of the largest cluster: γ = 43/18.
- For SPS energy (√s = 20 GeV), parton condensation begins for A ≈ 60, with Q_s ≈ 0.7 GeV; at RHIC energy (√s = 200 GeV), onset lowers to A ≈ 40.
- In Pb-Pb collisions at SPS, the percolation threshold for parton condensation is slightly below N_part ≈ 150 participants.
- Radii of charmonium states: r_J/ψ ≈ (0.9 GeV)^(−1), r_χ_c ≈ (0.6 GeV)^(−1), r_ψ′ ≈ (0.45 GeV)^(−1).
- Ratio of gluons to quarks+antiquarks in the parton condensate at Q = 1 GeV: about 1.2 for SPS energy, 4.0 for RHIC energy; in a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma it is about 0.5.
- Normal charmonium suppression break-up cross section: 5–6 mb, implying a mean free path of about 12 fm.

## Context

This work extends the parton percolation approach previously introduced by Armesto (2000) and Torino (2001) and connects it to the concept of parton saturation from the color glass condensate framework (reviewCGC, 2001). It provides a specific, parameter-free prediction for the onset of anomalous J/ψ suppression in terms of participant number, which can be compared to the two-step suppression pattern observed by the NA50 collaboration at CERN-SPS (NA38/50, 2000). The approach enables future studies of the centrality dependence of charmonium suppression at RHIC and LHC energies, and highlights the need for pA measurements at RHIC to disentangle normal nuclear absorption from anomalous suppression due to the parton condensate.
