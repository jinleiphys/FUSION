# Differential HBT Applied to Relativistic Fluid Dynamics

> The earlier introduced Differential HBT method aiming for detecting rotation is used to analyze the fluid dynamical model results of ultra-relativistic heavy ion reactions where the initial state has substantial angular momentum. The rotation effect and Kelvin Helmholtz Instability, lead to space-time momentum correlations, which can be detected by the method.

## Key claim

The Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) method can quantitatively estimate the angular momentum of the fireball in peripheral heavy ion collisions, but only if the emission probability from the far side of the system is reduced compared to the side facing the detector, breaking the mirror symmetry that would otherwise make the correlation function insensitive to the direction of flow velocity.

## Method

The authors performed a Particle-In-Cell fluid dynamical simulation of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, using a Juttner distribution for the pion emission function and a Cooper-Frye freeze-out description with a Gaussian emission time profile. They computed the standard two-pion correlation function and then constructed the Differential Correlation Function (DCF) as the difference between correlation functions measured at two symmetric angles forward and backward in the reaction plane. The rotation component of the flow was switched off in the simulation to isolate its effect on the DCF.

## Key numbers

- The angular momentum of the fireball at LHC energies is approximately L ≈ 10^6 ℏ (from reference VAC13).
- The DCF was evaluated at a collective transverse momentum of k = 5/fm (corresponding to p_t ≈ 1 GeV/c).
- The symmetry axis deflection angle was α = -11 degrees for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.36 TeV and α = -8 degrees for Au+Au at √s_NN = 0.2 TeV.
- The DCF was evaluated at a fluid dynamical evolution time of t = 3.56 fm/c (approximately 8 fm/c after initial touch).
- The impact parameter used was b = 0.7 b_max.
- The DCF was measured at pseudorapidity η = ±0.76, corresponding to polar angles of 90 ± 40 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the Differential HBT method introduced in Csernai and collaborators (2013) from simple few-source test systems to a full fluid dynamical model of heavy ion collisions. It builds on prior predictions of rotation in peripheral ultra-relativistic reactions (hydro1), Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability (hydro2), and flow vorticity (Csernai, Magas, and Wang 2012). The method enables quantitative experimental measurement of fireball rotation at LHC and RHIC, complementing polarization measurements (Becattini, Csernai, and Wang 2013) which are not proportional to total angular momentum.
