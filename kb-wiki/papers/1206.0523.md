# Possible $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ Molecular states in a chiral quark model

> We perform a systematic study of the bound state problem of $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ systems by using effective interaction in our chiral quark model. Our results show that both the interactions of $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ states are attractive, which consequently result in $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(0^{++})$ $D\bar{D}$ and $B\bar{B}$ bound states.

## Key claim
The chiral quark model predicts that both the \(D\bar{D}\) and \(B\bar{B}\) systems with quantum numbers \(I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(0^{++})\) form bound states, but the \(B\bar{B}\) state has a small root-mean-square radius that suggests it may be a tetraquark rather than a molecule. The existence and properties of these states remain model-dependent, as different prior calculations have reached conflicting conclusions.

## Method
The authors derived analytical effective interaction potentials between two S-wave heavy mesons by integrating over the internal coordinates of the mesons, using the chiral SU(3) quark model and its extended version that includes vector meson exchange. They then solved the Schrödinger equation with these potentials to obtain binding energies and root-mean-square radii for the \(D\bar{D}\) and \(B\bar{B}\) systems with isospin \(I=0\) and \(I=1\). A perturbative correction from coupling to the \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) or \(B^*\bar{B}^*\) channels was also included.

## Key numbers
- \(D\bar{D}\) binding energy (including coupled-channel correction): 1.0 + 2.3 = 3.3 MeV in the chiral SU(3) quark model; 33.3 + 1.4 = 34.7 MeV in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model without tensor coupling; 21.6 + 0.2 = 21.8 MeV in the extended model with tensor coupling.
- \(D\bar{D}\) root-mean-square radius: 3.7 fm in the chiral SU(3) quark model; 1.0 fm in the extended model without tensor coupling; 1.1 fm in the extended model with tensor coupling.
- \(D\bar{D}\) bound state mass range: 3695–3726 MeV.
- \(B\bar{B}\) binding energy (including coupled-channel correction): 19.2 + 1.2 = 20.4 MeV in the chiral SU(3) quark model; 81.8 + 11.3 = 93.1 MeV in the extended model without tensor coupling; 64 + 2.6 = 66.6 MeV in the extended model with tensor coupling.
- \(B\bar{B}\) root-mean-square radius: 0.9 fm in the chiral SU(3) quark model; 0.6 fm in both extended model cases.
- \(B\bar{B}\) bound state mass range: 10467–10540 MeV.
- No bound state found for \(I=1\) in either system.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model studies of \(B\bar{B}^*\), \(B^*\bar{B}^*\), \(D\bar{D}^*\), and \(D^*\bar{D}^*\) systems (Li et al., 2011) to the \(D\bar{D}\) and \(B\bar{B}\) channels. It agrees with the bound-state predictions of Valcarce et al. (2005), Ke et al. (2009), Liu et al. (2009), and Wong (2004), but contradicts Yang et al. (2009) who argued that no such molecules exist in the color-singlet channel. The results enable future experimental searches for these states and may help distinguish whether vector meson exchange or one-gluon exchange dominates the short-range quark interaction.
