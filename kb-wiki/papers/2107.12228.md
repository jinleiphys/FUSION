# Exact solution of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation with three-body forces in nuclear matter

> An exact treatment of the operators Q/e(ω) and the total momentum is adopted to solve the nuclear matter Bruecker-Bethe-Goldstone equation with two- and three-body forces. The single-particle potential, equation of state and nucleon effective mass are calculated from the exact G-matrix. The results are compared with those obtained under the angle-average approximation and the angle-average approximation with total momentum approximation. It is found that the angle-average procedure, whereas preventing huge calculations of coupled channels, nevertheless provides a fairly accurate approximation. On the contrary, the total momentum approximation turns out to be quite inaccurate compared to its exact counterpart.

## Key claim
The angle-average (AA) approximation for the Pauli operator and energy denominator provides a fairly accurate approximation to the exact solution of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation with two- and three-body forces, while the total momentum approximation (TMA) turns out to be quite inaccurate compared to its exact counterpart, especially at higher densities.

## Method
The authors solved the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) equation exactly by treating the angular dependence of the operators Q/e(ω) and the total momentum without approximation, including coupling between different partial waves. They used the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential together with a microscopic three-body force (3BF) reduced to an effective two-body force, and performed self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. The exact results were compared with those obtained under the angle-average approximation and the angle-average approximation combined with the total momentum approximation.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density from exact calculation: ρ_s = 0.188 fm^{-3} with binding energy E/A = -14.62 MeV
- Saturation density from AA approximation: ρ_s = 0.186 fm^{-3} with E/A = -14.47 MeV
- Saturation density from AA with TMA: ρ_s = 0.197 fm^{-3} with E/A = -15.09 MeV
- Incompressibility from exact calculation: K = 210.56 MeV
- Incompressibility from AA approximation: K = 212.73 MeV
- Incompressibility from AA with TMA: K = 239.98 MeV
- Symmetry energy from exact calculation: E_sym = 32.96 MeV at saturation density
- Symmetry energy slope from exact calculation: L = 74.60 MeV
- Matrix elements of effective G corresponding to mixing of total isospin T=0 and T=1 states is less than 0.1%

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Sartor (1990) and others who assessed the angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator and energy denominator, but without including three-body forces. The authors show that while the AA approximation is reliable, the total momentum approximation widely used in previous nonrelativistic BHF calculations (Brueckner et al., 1968; Zuo et al., 2002) introduces substantial errors. The exact treatment enables more accurate predictions for nuclear matter saturation properties, effective masses, and symmetry energy, which are important inputs for studies of heavy-ion collisions and neutron star properties.
