# Energy dependent chemical potentials of light hadrons and quarks based on transverse momentum spectra and yield ratios of negative to positive particles

> We describe the transverse momentum (or mass) spectra of $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p$, and $\bar{p}$ produced in central gold-gold (Au-Au), central lead-lead (Pb-Pb), and inelastic proton-proton ($pp$) collisions at different collision energies range from the AGS to LHC by using a two-component (in most cases) Erlang distribution in the framework of multi-source thermal model. The fitting results are consistent with the experimental data and the energy-dependent chemical potentials of light hadrons ($π$, $K$, and $p$) and quarks ($u$, $d$, and $s$) in central Au-Au, central Pb-Pb, and inelastic $pp$ collisions from the yield ratios of negative to positive particles obtained from the normalization constants are then extracted. The study shows that most types of energy-dependent chemical potentials decrease with increase of collision energy over a range from the AGS to LHC. The curves of all types of energy-dependent chemical potentials, obtained from the linear fits of yield ratios vs energy, have inflection points at the same energy of 3.526 GeV, which is regarded as the critical energy of phase transition from a hadron liquid-like state to a quark gas-like state in the collision system and indicates that the hadronic interactions play an important role in this period. At the RHIC and LHC, all types of chemical potentials become small and tend to zero at very high energy, which confirms that the collision system possibly changes completely from the hadron-dominant liquid-like state to the quark-dominant gas-like state and the partonic interactions possibly play a dominant role at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the energy-dependent chemical potentials of light hadrons and quarks, extracted from yield ratios of negative to positive particles, all exhibit inflection points at the same collision energy of 3.526 GeV, which they interpret as the critical energy for a phase transition from a hadron liquid-like state to a quark gas-like state. This claim is hedged by the statement that this energy is "regarded as" the critical energy and that the transition is "possibly" occurring, with the text noting that hadronic interactions "play an important role in this period."

## Method

The authors fitted transverse momentum (or transverse mass) spectra of π±, K±, p, and p̄ from central Au-Au, central Pb-Pb, and inelastic pp collisions across a center-of-mass energy range from the AGS to LHC using a two-component (or one-component) Erlang distribution within a multi-source thermal model. From the normalization constants of these fits, they extracted yield ratios of negative to positive particles (π⁻/π⁺, K⁻/K⁺, p̄/p), which were then modified by removing contributions from strong decays of high-mass resonances and weak decays of heavy flavor hadrons. Using these modified yield ratios and an empirical formula for chemical freeze-out temperature, they calculated the chemical potentials of π, K, p, u, d, and s quarks via relations derived from statistical thermal models.

## Key numbers

- The inflection point (maximum) of all types of energy-dependent chemical potentials occurs at the same collision energy of 3.526 GeV, interpreted as the critical energy for phase transition.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature is given by T_ch = T_lim / (1 + exp[2.60 - ln(√s_NN)/0.45]), where T_lim = 0.164 GeV and √s_NN is in GeV.
- For Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 2.67 GeV, the normalization constant N_0 for π⁺ is 12.051 ± 0.365 and for π⁻ is 20.811 ± 0.365.
- For Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the normalization constant N_0 for π⁺ is 314.469 ± 3.500 and for π⁻ is 293.451 ± 2.500.
- For Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV, the normalization constant N_0 for p is 52.211 ± 2.203 and for p̄ is 0.412 ± 0.020.
- For Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the normalization constant N_0 for p is 15.345 ± 0.266 and for p̄ is 11.195 ± 0.225.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on yield ratios and chemical potentials in heavy-ion collisions, particularly addressing the ongoing debate about the location of the critical end point (CEP) on the QCD phase diagram. The authors note that the STAR Collaboration found the critical energy may be at or below 19.6 GeV, while another study (Lao, 2019) suggested it may be about 4 GeV, and studies by Lacey et al. (2014, 2015) indicated it may be close to 62.4 GeV. The present work claims a specific critical energy of 3.526 GeV, which is lower than most prior estimates, and enables future studies to focus on this energy region for experimental searches of the QCD phase transition critical point.
