# Entanglement between deconfinement transition and chiral symmetry restoration

> We extend the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model by introducing an effective four-quark vertex depending on Polyakov loop. The effective vertex generates entanglement interactions between Polyakov loop and chiral condensate. The new model is consistent with lattice QCD data at imaginary quark-number chemical potential and real and imaginary isospin chemical potentials, particularly on strong correlation between the chiral and deconfinement transitions and also on the quark-mass dependence of the order of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint predicted by lattice QCD very lately. We investigate an influence of the entanglement interactions on a location of the tricritical point at real isospin chemical potential and a location of the critical endpoint at real quark-number chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an extended Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model with a Polyakov-loop-dependent four-quark vertex that generates entanglement interactions between the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop; this model can simultaneously reproduce lattice QCD data on the near-coincidence of chiral and deconfinement crossover transitions and on the quark-mass dependence of the order of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint, which the standard PNJL model cannot achieve without fine-tuning.

## Method

The authors modify the standard two-flavor PNJL model by replacing the constant scalar-type four-quark coupling \(G_{\rm s}\) with an effective vertex \(G_{\rm s}(\Phi)=G_{\rm s}[1-\alpha_1\Phi\Phi^*-\alpha_2(\Phi^3+\Phi^{*3})]\) that respects chiral symmetry, charge-conjugation symmetry, and extended \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) symmetry. They then solve the mean-field gap equations for the chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and the Polyakov loop \(\Phi\) at finite temperature \(T\) and imaginary quark-number chemical potential \(\mu_{\rm q}=i\theta_{\rm q}T\), as well as at real and imaginary isospin chemical potentials, and compare the resulting phase diagrams and susceptibilities with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers

* At zero chemical potential, the EPNJL model with \((\alpha_1,\alpha_2)=(0.2,0.2)\) and \(T_0=190\) MeV yields pseudocritical temperatures \(T_{\sigma}\approx T_{\Phi}\) that coincide within numerical errors, consistent with lattice QCD results of \(T_{\sigma}\approx T_{\Phi}\approx 173\pm 8\) MeV.
* The Roberge-Weiss endpoint temperature is \(T_{\rm E}\approx 185\) MeV in the EPNJL model, compared to \(T_{\rm E}\approx 189\) MeV in the standard PNJL model.
* The critical masses for the order of the Roberge-Weiss endpoint in the EPNJL model are \(m_0(1\to 2)=50\pm 5\) MeV (first-order to second-order) and \(m_0(2\to 1)=180\pm 5\) MeV (second-order to first-order), with the transition being second-order at intermediate masses around \(m_0=150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard PNJL model (Fukushima, 2004; Ratti, 2006) by incorporating entanglement interactions motivated by exact renormalization-group analyses (Braun, 2009; Kondo, 2010). It contrasts with the earlier PNJL-8V model (Sakai, 2010) that required scalar-type eight-quark and vector-type four-quark interactions to match lattice data, whereas the EPNJL model achieves consistency with lattice QCD results at imaginary chemical potential (D'Elia, 2009; de Forcrand, 2010) using only the entanglement vertex. The model enables predictions for the location of the tricritical point at real isospin chemical potential and the critical endpoint at real quark-number chemical potential, which are inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem.
