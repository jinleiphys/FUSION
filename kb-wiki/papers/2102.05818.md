# Comment on "$σ$-meson: Four-quark versus two-quark components and decay width in a Bethe-Salpeter approach"

> In a recent paper by N. Santowsky et al. [Phys. Rev. D 102, 056014 (2020)], covariant coupled equations were derived to describe a tetraquark in terms of a mix of four-quark states $2q 2\bar{q}$ and two-quark states $q\bar{q}$. These equations were expressed in terms of vertices describing the disintegration of a tetraquark into identical two-meson states, into a diquark-antidiquark pair, and into a quark-antiquark pair. We show that these equations are inconsistent as they imply a $q\bar{q}$ Bethe-Salpeter kernel that is $q\bar{q}$-reducible.

## Key claim

The authors show that the tetraquark equations of Santowsky et al. (2020), which include coupling to quark-antiquark channels, are inconsistent because they imply a quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter kernel that is quark-antiquark reducible, contradicting the definition of an irreducible kernel required for a bound state equation.

## Method

The authors write the three coupled equations of Santowsky et al. (2020) in matrix form, then solve for the tetraquark form factors and substitute the result into the equation for the quark-antiquark form factor. By comparing the resulting expression with the standard bound state equation for a quark-antiquark system, they identify the implied kernel and demonstrate that it contains the disconnected quark-antiquark propagator, making it reducible.

## Key numbers

- No original numerical results are presented; this is a theoretical comment identifying an inconsistency in the formalism of Santowsky et al. (2020).

## Context

This comment extends the earlier work of Heupel, Eichmann and Fischer (2012), who developed covariant tetraquark equations without coupling to quark-antiquark channels. The authors had previously attempted to include such coupling in Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (2014) by adding disconnected contributions to the connected part of the quark-antiquark interaction. The present work shows that the phenomenological approach of Santowsky et al. (2020) is mathematically inconsistent, indicating that a correct treatment of tetraquark coupling to two-quark states requires a different formalism.
