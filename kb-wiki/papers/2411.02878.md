# Rotating Neutron Stars with the Relativistic Ab Initio Calculations

> The equation of state (EOS) of extremely dense matter is crucial for understanding the properties of rotating neutron stars. Starting from the widely used realistic Bonn potentials rooted in a relativistic framework, we derive EOSs by performing the state-of-the-art relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations in the full Dirac space. The self-consistent and simultaneous consideration of both positive- and negative-energy states (NESs) of the Dirac equation allows us to avoid the uncertainties present in calculations where NESs are treated using approximations. To manifest the impact of rotational dynamics, several structural properties of neutron stars across a wide range of rotation frequencies and up to the Keplerian limit are obtained, including the gravitational and baryonic masses, the polar and equatorial radii, and the moments of inertia. Our theoretical predictions align well with the latest astrophysical constraints from the observations on massive neutron stars and joint mass-radius measurements. The maximum mass for rotating configurations can reach up to $2.93M_{\odot}$ for Bonn A potential, while the radius of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star for non-rotating case can be extended to around 17 km through the constant baryonic mass sequences. Relations with good universalities between the Keplerian frequency and static mass as well as radius are obtained, from which the radius of the black widow PSR J0952-0607 is predicted to be less than 19.58 km. Furthermore, to understand how rotation deforms the equilibrium shape of a neutron star, the eccentricity is also calculated. The approximate universality between the eccentricity at the Keplerian frequency and the gravitational mass is found.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotating neutron star configurations computed from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations in the full Dirac space, which self-consistently include both positive- and negative-energy states, produce maximum masses up to 2.93 solar masses for the Bonn A potential, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of nucleon-nucleon potential and the treatment of negative-energy states without approximations.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations in the full Dirac space using three parameterizations of the Bonn potentials (A, B, and C) to derive equations of state for beta-stable nuclear matter. They then used the RNS code to solve the Einstein field equations for axisymmetric, stationary space-times, computing equilibrium configurations of rotating neutron stars across a range of spin ratios and frequencies up to the Keplerian limit.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for rotating configurations reaches 2.93 solar masses for Bonn A potential, which is 21.7% higher than the static maximum mass of 2.43 solar masses
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star in the non-rotating case is 11.98 km (Bonn A), 12.17 km (Bonn B), and 12.32 km (Bonn C)
- At Keplerian frequency, the equatorial radius at maximum mass extends to 13.84 km for Bonn A, a 26.2% increase from the static case
- Keplerian frequencies for the full Dirac space calculations are 1.86 kHz (Bonn A), 1.84 kHz (Bonn B), and 1.83 kHz (Bonn C)
- The radius of the black widow PSR J0952-0607 is predicted to be less than 19.58 km
- Eccentricity at Keplerian frequency is 0.838 (Bonn A), 0.836 (Bonn B), and 0.836 (Bonn C)

## Context

This work extends previous RBHF studies of static neutron stars by Wang et al. (2022) and Qu et al. (2023) to include rotational dynamics, and it aligns with earlier findings by Weber (1992) and Cook (1994) that maximally rotating stars can have masses about 20% greater than static counterparts. The results enable predictions for rapidly rotating pulsars such as PSR J0952-0607 and provide universal relations between Keplerian frequency and static mass and radius that can be tested against future observations from NICER and gravitational wave detections.
