# Limiting temperature of pion gas with the van der Waals equation of state

> The grand canonical ensemble formulation of the van der Waals equation of state that includes the effects of Bose statistics is applied to an equilibrium system of interacting pions. If the attractive interaction between pions is large enough, a limiting temperature $T_0$ emerges, i.e., no thermodynamical equilibrium is possible at $T>T_0$. The system pressure $p$, particle number density $n$, and energy density $\varepsilon$ remain finite at $T=T_0$, whereas for $T$ near $T_0$ both the specific heat $C=d\varepsilon/dT$ and the scaled variance of particle number fluctuations $ω[N]$ are proportional to $(T_0-T)^{-1/2}$ and, thus, go to infinity at $T\rightarrow T_0$. The limiting temperature corresponds also to the softest point of the equation of state, i.e., the speed of sound squared $c_s^2=dp/d\varepsilon$ goes to zero as $(T_0-T)^{1/2}$. Very similar thermodynamical behavior takes place in the Hagedorn model for the special choice of a power, namely $m^{-4}$, in the pre-exponential factor of the mass spectrum $ρ(m)$.

## Key claim

If the attractive van der Waals parameter \(a\) is large enough (above a threshold \(a_b\) that depends on the repulsive parameter \(b\)), a limiting temperature \(T_0\) emerges for an equilibrium pion gas; at \(T_0\) the pressure, density, and energy density remain finite, but the specific heat and scaled variance of particle-number fluctuations diverge as \((T_0-T)^{-1/2}\) and the speed of sound squared goes to zero as \((T_0-T)^{1/2}\).

## Method

The authors apply a grand-canonical formulation of the van der Waals equation of state that incorporates Bose statistics (with \(\eta=-1\) in the ideal-gas integrals) to a system of pions with zero total electric charge (chemical potential \(\mu=0\)). They fix the excluded-volume parameter \(b=0.45\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) (corresponding to a hard-core radius \(r\approx0.3\ \mathrm{fm}\)) and treat the attraction parameter \(a\) as a free variable, solving the transcendental equation for the shifted chemical potential \(\mu^*\) numerically for temperatures up to 160 MeV. Thermodynamic functions (pressure \(p\), number density \(n\), energy density \(\varepsilon\)), the specific heat \(C=d\varepsilon/dT\), the speed of sound squared \(c_s^2=dp/d\varepsilon\), and the scaled variance \(\omega[N]\) of particle-number fluctuations are then computed from the solutions.

## Key numbers

* For \(b=0.45\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(a/b=200\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the van der Waals equation of state is close to that of the ideal pion gas (repulsive and attractive interactions approximately cancel).
* The maximum pion number density obtained in the study is \(n_{\mathrm{max}}\simeq 0.30\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), corresponding to a packing fraction \(\eta_{\mathrm{max}}=0.034\).
* At \(b=0.45\ \mathrm{fm}^3\), the limiting temperature is \(T_0\cong 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(a\cong 270\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\) and \(T_0\cong 155\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(a\cong 225\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\).
* For \(b=0\), the same limiting temperatures \(T_0\cong 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(155\ \mathrm{MeV}\) are reached at \(a\cong 219\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\) and \(168\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\), respectively.
* The most interesting physical behavior with \(T_0=150\pm10\ \mathrm{MeV}\) occurs for \(b\) in the range \(0\) to \(0.45\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(a\) in the range \(150\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\) to \(300\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^3\).
* Near \(T_0\), the specific heat \(C\) and scaled variance \(\omega[N]\) are proportional to \((T_0-T)^{-1/2}\), and the speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) is proportional to \((T_0-T)^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the grand-canonical van der Waals formalism previously developed for nuclear matter with Fermi statistics (Vovchenko et al., 2015) to a Bose system of pions, where particle number is not conserved. The emergence of a limiting temperature in the van der Waals pion gas is compared to the Hagedorn model (Hagedorn, 1965), where a limiting temperature arises from an exponentially increasing hadron mass spectrum; the authors show that for a specific power-law exponent \(\alpha=4\) in the Hagedorn mass spectrum \(\rho(m)\propto m^{-4}\exp(m/T_0)\), the thermodynamic behavior near \(T_0\) (finite \(p,n,\varepsilon\) but diverging \(C\) and \(\omega[N]\)) matches that of the van der Waals pion gas. The results suggest that the limiting temperature in the van der Waals model signals the breakdown of the hadron-gas description and should be interpreted as the deconfinement temperature when quark-gluon degrees of freedom are introduced.
