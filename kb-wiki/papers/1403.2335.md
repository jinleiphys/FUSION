# Double-parton scattering cross sections in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC

> Simple generic expressions to compute double-parton scattering (DPS) cross sections in high-energy proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, as a function of the corresponding single-parton cross sections, are presented. Estimates of DPS contributions are studied for two specific processes at LHC energies: (i) same-sign W-boson pair production in p-Pb, and (ii) double-J/psi production in Pb-Pb, using NLO predictions with nuclear parton densities for the corresponding single-parton cross sections. The expected DPS cross sections and event rates after typical acceptance and efficiency losses are also given for other processes involving J/psi and W,Z gauge bosons in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Double-parton scattering (DPS) cross sections in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions are enhanced by factors of up to about 600 and 9 million, respectively, compared to proton-proton collisions, but the actual observability of specific processes depends on accounting for branching ratios, acceptance, and efficiency losses that reduce visible yields by orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for DPS cross sections in p-A and A-A collisions by scaling the product of single-parton scattering (SPS) cross sections with nuclear geometry factors, using the Glauber model for nuclear thickness functions and an effective cross section sigma_eff_pp = 15 mb from LHC data. They compute numerical estimates for same-sign W-pair production in p-Pb at 8.8 TeV and double-J/psi production in Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV using NLO predictions (MCFM 6.2 for W/Z, color evaporation model for J/psi) with CT10 proton PDFs and EPS09 nuclear PDFs.

## Key numbers

- In p-Pb collisions, the effective DPS cross section sigma_eff_pA = 22.6 microbarns, giving a DPS enhancement factor of sigma_eff_pp / sigma_eff_pA approximately 3*A = 600 relative to pp.
- In Pb-Pb collisions, the effective DPS cross section sigma_eff_AA = 1.5 nb, giving a DPS enhancement factor of sigma_eff_pp / sigma_eff_AA proportional to A^3.3/5 approximately 9 x 10^6 relative to pp.
- Same-sign WW DPS cross section in p-Pb at 8.8 TeV: sigma_DPS = 150 pb, which is 1.5 times higher than the SPS background; about 10 DPS same-sign WW events expected in 2 pb^-1 integrated luminosity after acceptance cuts.
- Double-J/psi DPS cross section in Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV: approximately 200 mb; about 20% of single-J/psi events have a second J/psi from DPS; visible cross section d(sigma_DPS)/dy = 60 nb per dilepton decay mode, yielding about 240 double-J/psi events per unit rapidity in 1 nb^-1 integrated luminosity (four dilepton channels combined).
- At lowest impact parameters in Pb-Pb, approximately 35% of J/psi-producing collisions have a second J/psi from DPS.

## Context

This work extends the DPS formalism from proton-proton collisions (where sigma_eff_pp = 15 mb was measured by ATLAS and CMS in W+dijets data) to nuclear collisions, building on earlier theoretical work by Strikman (2001) on DPS in pA collisions and the authors' own preliminary studies (d'Enterria 2012, 2013). The results enable quantitative separation of DPS contributions from other sources of particle production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for J/psi pairs where DPS could be misinterpreted as evidence for c-cbar regeneration in the quark-gluon plasma. The large expected DPS signals in p-Pb also offer a way to determine the effective sigma_eff parameter independently of pp measurements, using the better-known transverse density profile of nuclei.
