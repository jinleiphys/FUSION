# Hybrid Color Glass Condensate and hydrodynamic description of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider small system scan

> Multi-particle correlation observables in the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider small system scan are computed in a framework that contains both initial state momentum anisotropies from the Color Glass Condensate effective theory and final state hydrodynamic evolution. The initial state is computed using the IP-Glasma model and coupled to viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations, which are followed by microscopic hadronic transport. All parameters of the calculation were previously constrained using experimental data on Au+Au collisions at the same center of mass energy. We find that the qualitative features of the experimental data, such as the system and centrality dependence of the charged hadron momentum anisotropy, can only be reproduced when final state interactions are present. On the other hand, we also demonstrate that the details of the initial state are crucially important for the quantitative description of observables in the studied small systems, as neglecting the initial transverse flow profile or the initial shear stress tensor, which contain information on the momentum anisotropy from the Color Glass Condensate, has dramatic effects on the produced final state anisotropy. We further show that the initial state momentum anisotropy is correlated with the observed elliptic flow in all small systems, with the effect increasing with decreasing multiplicity. We identify the precise measurement of $v_2$ in d+Au and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy at the same multiplicity as a means to reveal effects of the initial state momentum anisotropy.

## Key claim

The qualitative features of the experimental data on charged hadron momentum anisotropy in small systems at RHIC can only be reproduced when final state hydrodynamic interactions are present, but the details of the initial state from the Color Glass Condensate are crucially important for quantitative description, as neglecting the initial transverse flow profile or initial shear stress tensor has dramatic effects on the final state anisotropy.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid framework combining the IP-Glasma model for the initial state (which provides the full energy-momentum tensor including CGC momentum anisotropies), coupled to the viscous relativistic hydrodynamic code MUSIC, followed by the UrQMD hadronic transport model. All parameters were previously constrained using experimental data on Au+Au collisions at the same center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV. The initial energy-momentum tensor from the Yang-Mills evolution at time τ_init = 0.4 fm/c was used to initialize hydrodynamics, with the shear stress contribution and initial bulk viscous term treated according to the lattice QCD equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Switching time from Yang-Mills to hydrodynamics: τ_init = 0.4 fm/c, varied between 0.2 and 0.6 fm/c
- Switching temperature from fluid to particles: T_sw = 145 MeV
- Center-of-mass energy: √s = 200 GeV for all systems (p+p, p+Au, d+Au, ³He+Au, Au+Au)
- In 30-40% central Au+Au collisions, v₂ changes by ~35% when initial state details are modified; in 0-5% central p+Au collisions, v₂ changes by as much as 90%
- In p+Au collisions, dN_ch/dη changes by ~40% and ⟨p_T⟩ changes by up to 30% when initial flow and viscous stress are removed
- For Au+Au collisions with dN_ch/dη ≳ 100, the flow direction has no correlation with the initial momentum anisotropy
- For systems with fewer than 10 charged hadrons per unit rapidity, significant correlation exists between initial momentum anisotropy and final v₂

## Context

This work extends the hybrid IP-Glasma + hydrodynamics framework previously applied to p+Pb collisions at LHC by Mantysaari et al. (2017) and to Au+Au collisions by Schenke et al. (2019), now applied to the RHIC small system scan of p+p, p+Au, d+Au, and ³He+Au collisions. It goes beyond earlier studies by Greif et al. (2017) that used Boltzmann equation for gluonic final state interactions, by computing hadronic final states and using realistic hydrodynamic simulations constrained by heavy ion data. The results enable future 3D simulations and provide a benchmark for distinguishing initial-state CGC momentum anisotropy effects from final-state hydrodynamic response, particularly through precise measurements of v₂ in d+Au and Au+Au collisions at the same multiplicity.
