# Three-Cluster Equation Using Two-Cluster RGM Kernel

> We propose a new type of three-cluster equation which uses two-cluster resonating-group-method (RGM) kernels. In this equation, the orthogonality of the total wave-function to two-cluster Pauli-forbidden states is essential to eliminate redundant components admixed in the three-cluster systems. The explicit energy-dependence inherent in the exchange RGM kernel is self-consistently determined. For bound-state problems, this equation is straightforwardly transformed to the Faddeev equation which uses a modified singularity-free T-matrix constructed from the two-cluster RGM kernel. The approximation of the present three-cluster formalism can be examined with more complete calculation using the three-cluster RGM. As a simple example, we discuss three di-neutron (3d') and 3 alpha systems in the harmonic-oscillator variational calculation. The result of the Faddeev calculation is also presented for the 3' system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new three-cluster equation that uses two-cluster resonating-group-method (RGM) kernels, where the orthogonality of the total wavefunction to two-cluster Pauli-forbidden states is essential to eliminate redundant components; the explicit energy-dependence inherent in the exchange RGM kernel must be self-consistently determined, and the approximation can be examined against more complete three-cluster RGM calculations.

## Method

The authors formulate a three-cluster equation for three identical clusters using pairwise two-cluster RGM kernels, incorporating a projection operator onto the [3] symmetric Pauli-allowed space. They convert this equation into a Faddeev equation that uses a modified singularity-free T-matrix constructed from the two-cluster RGM kernel. The method is applied to the 0+ ground states of three di-neutron (3d') and 3-alpha systems using translationally-invariant harmonic oscillator basis states in a variational calculation, with self-consistent determination of the two-cluster relative energy.

## Key numbers

- For the 3d' system: variational calculation yields E = -0.4323 MeV using 2,927 basis states with N ≤ 88 total harmonic oscillator quanta; Faddeev calculation yields E = -0.438 MeV (with partial waves up to ℓ=6), compared to the full 3d' RGM result of E = -1.188 MeV
- For the 3-alpha system: final values are E = -5.97 MeV and ε = 9.50 MeV, compared to the 3-alpha RGM result of E = -7.5 MeV (experimental value E = -7.3 MeV)
- The 2-alpha system binding energy from the RGM calculation is E = -0.245 MeV (experimental value 92 keV)
- For the 3-alpha system with Coulomb turned off: E = -11.42 MeV, giving a Coulomb effect of 5.45 MeV
- The renormalized RGM approximation gives E = -4.99 MeV for 3-alpha; the orthogonality condition model (OCM) gives E = -4.68 MeV
- The lowest shell-model component amplitude for 3-alpha is c(04) = 0.790

## Context

This work extends the resonating-group method framework for composite-particle interactions, addressing the problem of how to use quark-model baryon-baryon interactions (Fujiwara 1996a, 1996b, 2001b) in few-baryon systems. The proposed three-cluster equation provides a more tractable alternative to full three-cluster RGM calculations, which remain beyond feasibility for quark-model baryon-baryon interactions. The authors note that a previous 3-alpha Faddeev calculation by Oryu (1994) using 2-alpha RGM kernels gave too large binding energy (E = -28.2 MeV with Coulomb off) because they did not treat the εK term properly or fully incorporate the projection operator P. The method enables future application to the hypertriton using quark-model nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions.
