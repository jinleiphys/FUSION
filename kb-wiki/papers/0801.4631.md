# Collective effects in central heavy-ion collisions

> In-medium effects on transverse-mass distributions of quarks and gluons are considered assuming a possible local equilibrium for colorless quark objects (mesons and baryons) created in central A-A collisions. It is shown that the average transverse momentum squared for these partons grows and then saturates when the initial energy increases. Within the quark-gluon string model it leads to the colliding energy dependence of hadron transverse mass spectra which is similar to that observed in heavy ion collisions. Comparison with other scenarios is given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed "anomalous" energy dependence of the effective transverse temperature (inverse slope) of kaon transverse-mass spectra in central heavy-ion collisions can be explained by in-medium effects on quark distributions inside hadrons, rather than requiring a deconfinement phase transition or mixed phase. The authors show that the average transverse momentum squared for quarks grows and then saturates with increasing collision energy, and this saturation property depends on the fireball temperature.

## Method

The authors assume local thermodynamic equilibrium for hadrons (mesons and baryons) in a fireball created in central A+A collisions, described by a relativistic invariant distribution function. They then calculate the distribution function of constituent quarks inside a hadron that is in local equilibrium with the surrounding nuclear matter, using a convolution of the hadron distribution with quark probability distributions. The average transverse momentum squared for quarks and for produced hadrons is computed within the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) / dual parton model (DPM), assuming Gaussian transverse momentum dependences for the quark distributions and fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum squared for K+ mesons produced in free p+p collisions is given as <p_t^2>_{K+}^{NN} = 0.14 GeV/c^2.
- Calculations are performed at a fireball temperature T = 150 MeV for the energy dependence of average transverse momentum squared of K+ mesons.
- The temperature dependence of <p_{K+,t}^2>_{hh}^{AA} is found to be rather weak in the interval T = 100 - 150 MeV.
- The results are sensitive to the mass value of the hadron which is locally equilibrated at sqrt(s_hh) ≤ 10 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the quark-gluon string model (Kaidalov, 1980s) and dual parton model (Capella, 1994) by incorporating in-medium effects on quark distributions. It offers an alternative to hydrodynamic models with phase transitions (Braz, 2004) that previously reproduced the anomalous effective temperature behavior but required the freeze-out temperature to closely follow the effective kaon temperature. The paper addresses the experimental observation of a fast increase of effective transverse temperature in the AGS domain (Ah, 2000), saturation at SPS energies (NA49), and a subsequent increase approaching RHIC energies (RHIC), which had been interpreted as evidence for the mixed phase (GGB, 2003; Mohanty, 2003). The approach is applicable only for heavy nuclei and not for the early interaction stage.
