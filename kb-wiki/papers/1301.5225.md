# Shell-model half-lives for r-process waiting point nuclei including first-forbidden contributions

> We have performed large-scale shell-model calculations of the half-lives and neutron-branching probabilities of the r-process waiting point nuclei at the magic neutron numbers N=50, 82, and 126. The calculations include contributions from allowed Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions. We find good agreement with the measured half-lives for the N=50 nuclei with charge numbers Z=28-32 and for the N=82 nuclei 129Ag and 130Cd. The contribution of forbidden transitions reduce the half-lives of the N=126 waiting point nuclei significantly, while they have only a small effect on the half-lives of the N=50 and 82 r-process nuclei.

# Shell-model half-lives for r-process waiting point nuclei including first-forbidden contributions

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations including both allowed Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions reproduce measured half-lives for N=50 and N=82 waiting point nuclei, but the contribution of first-forbidden transitions significantly reduces half-lives for N=126 waiting point nuclei while having only a small effect on N=50 and N=82 nuclei. The authors note that their model spaces are too restricted to recover the full Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden strength, but argue that missed strength resides mainly outside the Q_beta window and does not affect half-life calculations.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model diagonalization using the NATHAN code to calculate initial and final nuclear states and transition matrix elements for beta-decay of r-process waiting point nuclei at N=50, 82, and 126. For N=50 nuclei they used a model space with 0f and 1p orbits for protons and 0f, 1p, and 0g orbits for neutrons; for N=82 nuclei they used a model space built on a Sr-88 core; for N=126 nuclei they used a model space with orbits based on holes in a Pb-208 core with a generalized seniority truncation allowing maximum seniority 8. They determined quenching factors for first-forbidden transitions by fitting to experimental data for nuclei near N=82 and N=126, obtaining separate quenching factors for rank 0, 1, and 2 operators, and adjusted the Gamow-Teller quenching factor to reproduce the half-life of Cd-130.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor for Gamow-Teller transitions: q_GT = 0.66, slightly smaller than the customary value of 0.7
- Quenching factors for first-forbidden matrix elements: q(xi'v) = 1.266, q(w)=q(w')=0.66, q(x)=q(x')=0.51, q(u)=q(u')=0.38, q(z)=0.42
- For N=50 nuclei, first-forbidden contributions are less than 5% for Z >= 28, but contribute about 25% to the Co-77 decay
- For N=126 nucleus Ta-199, partial half-life due to rank 1 first-forbidden operators: 655 ms (using xi'y - xi(x+u) pivot) and 716 ms (using x+u pivot), compared to 651 ms in a converged calculation, agreeing within 10 percent
- Shell model half-life for Ni-78: 0.15 s, compared to experimental value 0.11 (+0.1/-0.06) s
- Shell model half-life for Zn-80: 0.53 s, compared to experimental value 0.545 +/- 0.016 s
- Shell model half-life for Ga-81: 1.03 s, compared to experimental value 1.217 +/- 0.005 s
- Shell model half-life for Ge-82: 6.90 s, compared to experimental value 4.55 +/- 0.05 s
- Shell model half-life for Cu-79: 0.27 s, compared to experimental value 0.188 +/- 0.025 s
- First-forbidden transitions contribute about 13% to the half-life of Cd-130

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of r-process waiting point half-lives by Martinez-Pinedo (1999) and Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2003) that considered only Gamow-Teller transitions, motivated by the finding from Borzov (2006) using QRPA with the Fayans energy functional that first-forbidden contributions significantly reduce N=126 half-lives. The calculations improve upon global models such as FRDM+QRPA by Moller (2003) and HFB+QRPA by Engel (1999) by including many-body configuration mixing essential for reproducing cross-gap correlations in double-magic nuclei like Ni-78. The results enable more reliable r-process simulations for the third abundance peak around A~200 and provide input for estimating beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities important for describing decay of r-process nuclei after freeze-out.
