# Hyperon-nucleon single-particle potentials with low-momentum interactions

> Single-particle potentials in Hartree-Fock approximation for different hyperon-nucleon (YN) channels are calculated in the framework of the effective low-momentum YN interaction V_lowk. In contrast to the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the available experimental data for the YN interaction are scarce. As a consequence no unique YN low-momentum potential V_lowk can be predicted from the various bare potentials. The resulting momentum- and density-dependent single-particle potentials for several different bare OBE models and for chiral effective field theory are compared to each other.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, unlike for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, no unique low-momentum hyperon-nucleon potential \(V_{\text{low }k}\) can be predicted from the various bare potentials because the scarce experimental data do not constrain the spin structure of the hyperon-nucleon interaction sufficiently.

## Method

The authors constructed effective low-momentum hyperon-nucleon interactions \(V_{\text{low }k}\) by solving a renormalization-group flow equation using several bare one-boson-exchange potentials (NSC89, NSC97a-f, J04) and a leading-order chiral effective field theory potential as initial conditions. They then calculated the momentum- and density-dependent single-particle potentials for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter within the Hartree-Fock approximation, using the diagonal partial-wave matrix elements of the resulting \(V_{\text{low }k}\).

## Key numbers

- The empirical \(\Lambda\) potential depth at rest in symmetric nuclear matter is \(U_{\Lambda}(p=0) \approx -30\ \text{MeV}\), confirmed by an analysis of \((\pi^-,K^+)\) inclusive spectra (Saha, year not given).
- At saturation density \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (Fermi momentum \(k_{F,0}=1.35\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)), the calculated \(U_{\Lambda}(p=0)\) ranges from \(-10.84\ \text{MeV}\) (NSC89) to \(-50.28\ \text{MeV}\) (J04), with the chiral EFT potentials giving \(-25.35\) to \(-27.37\ \text{MeV}\).
- The \(\Lambda\) potential at rest from the NSC97f model is \(-29.49\ \text{MeV}\) at saturation density, with the dominant partial-wave contributions being \(-15.37\ \text{MeV}\) from \(^1S_0\) and \(-20.85\ \text{MeV}\) from \(^3S_1\).
- Scattering lengths \(a_0\) for the \(^1S_0\) partial wave in the \(\Lambda p\) channel range from \(-0.71\ \text{fm}\) (NSC97a) to \(-2.70\ \text{fm}\) (NSC89), while chiral EFT values are \(-1.80\ \text{fm}\) for all cutoffs between 550 and 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Hartree-Fock and \(G\)-matrix calculations of hyperon single-particle potentials (Schulze, year not given; Vidana, year not given) by using a renormalization-group decimation to construct low-momentum interactions from multiple bare potentials. The results show that while most potentials reproduce the empirical \(\Lambda\) depth at saturation density, their predictions diverge at higher densities, which will affect calculations of hyperon concentrations in neutron stars and the maximum neutron-star mass. The paper enables systematic comparisons between different hyperon-nucleon interaction models in a many-body context, highlighting the need for forthcoming experiments at J-PARC and FAIR to constrain the interaction.
