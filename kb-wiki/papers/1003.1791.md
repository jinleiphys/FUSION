# A new inverse quasifission mechanism to produce neutron-rich transfermium nuclei

> Based on time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory, a new inverse quasifission mechanism is proposed to produce neutron-rich transfermium nuclei, in collision of prolate deformed actinides. Calculations show that collision of the tip of one nucleus with the side of the other results in a nucleon flux toward the latter. The role of nucleon evaporation and impact parameter, as well as the collision time are discussed.

## Key claim

Based on time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations, a new inverse quasifission mechanism is proposed for producing neutron-rich transfermium nuclei in collisions of prolate deformed actinides, specifically when the tip of the lighter nucleus collides with the side of the heavier one. The mechanism is demonstrated for the \(^{232}\)Th+\(^{250}\)Cf reaction, but the authors note that the TDHF evolution is stopped before fragments reach the box walls and that particle number fluctuations in the fragments are underestimated in TDHF.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations using the SLy4\(d\) Skyrme energy density functional with the tdhf3d code, simulating central and non-central collisions of \(^{232}\)Th and \(^{250}\)Cf at center-of-mass energies from 626.6 MeV to 1205 MeV. Five initial relative orientations of the prolate nuclei were studied (XX, XY, YX, YY, YZ), defined by how each nucleus's elongation axis is angled relative to the collision axis. The number of protons and neutrons in each fragment after separation was determined by integrating densities above 0.001 fm\(^{-3}\), with a correction of approximately 0.7 bound neutrons per fragment added to account for wave function tails.

## Key numbers

- The heaviest nucleus formed in the YX orientation at \(E_{c.m.}=915.8\) MeV is \(^{265}\)Lr, which has three more neutrons than the most neutron-rich lawrencium isotope observed experimentally to date.
- The maximum simulation time was \(6\times10^{-21}\) s, with a time step of \(1.5\times10^{-24}\) s.
- Contact time for the XX orientation exhibits a plateau at approximately \(2\times10^{-21}\) s, while other orientations reach \(3-4\times10^{-21}\) s at \(E_{c.m.}=1200\) MeV.
- The energy threshold for maximum density along the collision axis exceeding saturation density is \(E_{th.}^{XX}\simeq870\) MeV for XX, \(E_{th.}^{YX,XY}\simeq960\) MeV for YX and XY, and \(E_{th.}^{YY,YZ}\simeq1010\) MeV for YY and YZ.
- Transfermium production via inverse quasifission in the YX orientation at \(E_{c.m.}=915.8\) MeV is dominant up to impact parameter \(b\simeq3\) fm, corresponding to an angular momentum of approximately \(118\hbar\).
- At \(E_{c.m.}=915.8\) MeV in the YX orientation, approximately three neutrons are evaporated (about 1-2 per fragment).
- The final total kinetic energy of fragments for all inverse quasifission events in the YX configuration is approximately 650 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions by Golabek and Simenel (2009), which showed that nucleon flux in the neck depends on relative orientation of deformed actinides. It also builds on theoretical predictions from the dinuclear system model (Adamian et al., 2005), multidimensional Langevin equations (Zagrebaev et al., 2006), and constrained molecular dynamics (Maruyama et al., 2002) that shell effects in the \(^{208}\)Pb region should favor inverse quasifission. The proposed mechanism enables experimental searches for neutron-rich transfermium nuclei beyond the currently known isotopes, particularly in the region of the nuclear chart with 104-110 protons that is currently poorly characterized.
