# Nuclear giant quadruple resonance within transport approach and its constraint on nucleon effective mass

> We study the nuclear iso-scalar giant quadruple resonance~(ISGQR) based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck~(BUU) transport equation. The mean-field part of the BUU equation is described by the Skyrme nucleon-nucleon effective interaction, and its collision term, which embodies the two-particle-two-hole ($2$p-$2$h) correlation, is implemented through the stochastic approach. We find that the width of ISGQR for heavy nuclei is exhausted dominated by collisional damping, which is incorporated into the BUU equation through its collision term, and it can be well reproduced through employing a proper in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. Based on further Vlasov and BUU calculations with a number of representative Skyrme interactions, the iso-scalar nucleon effective mass at saturation density is extracted respectively as $m^{*}_{s,0}/m$ $=$ $0.83\pm0.04$ and $m^{*}_{s,0}/m$ $=$ $0.82\pm0.03$ from the measured excitation energy $E_x$ of the ISGQR of $\isotope[208]{Pb}$. The small discrepancy between the two constraints indicates the negligible role of $2$p-$2$h correlation in constraining $m_{s,0}^*$ with the ISGQR excitation energy.

## Key claim

The iso-scalar nucleon effective mass at saturation density is constrained to \(m^{*}_{s,0}/m = 0.82 \pm 0.03\) from full Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations and \(0.83 \pm 0.04\) from Vlasov calculations using the measured excitation energy of the ISGQR of \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\); the small discrepancy indicates that two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) correlations have a negligible role in constraining \(m_{s,0}^*\) with the ISGQR excitation energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equation using a lattice Hamiltonian method for the mean-field evolution, with the mean-field described by Skyrme nucleon-nucleon effective interactions. The collision term, which embodies two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) correlations, is implemented through a full-ensemble stochastic collision approach with a constant in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section \(\sigma_{\rm NN}^* = 0.60\,\sigma_{\rm NN}^{\rm free}\). The ISGQR of \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\) is excited by perturbing the initial coordinates and momenta of test nucleons according to the iso-scalar quadrupole operator, and the strength function \(S(E)\) is obtained via Fourier transform of the time evolution of the expectation value \(\langle\hat{Q}_{\rm ISQ}\rangle(t)\).

## Key numbers

- Iso-scalar nucleon effective mass from Vlasov calculation: \(m^{*}_{s,0}/m = 0.83 \pm 0.04\) for \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\).
- Iso-scalar nucleon effective mass from full BUU calculation: \(m^{*}_{s,0}/m = 0.82 \pm 0.03\) for \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\).
- Weighted average experimental excitation energy of ISGQR for \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\): \(E_x = 10.9 \pm 0.1\ {\rm MeV}\).
- Experimental excitation energies used for other nuclei: \(10.89 \pm 0.30\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(\isotope[208]{Pb}\), \(12.78 \pm 0.20\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(\isotope[144]{Sm}\), \(13.50 \pm 0.35\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(\isotope[116]{Sn}\), and \(14.56 \pm 0.20\ {\rm MeV}\) for \(\isotope[90]{Zr}\).
- In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section used: \(\sigma_{\rm NN}^* = 0.60\,\sigma_{\rm NN}^{\rm free}\).
- Lattice spacing: \(l_x = l_y = l_z = 0.5\ {\rm fm}\); time step: \(\Delta t = 0.5\ {\rm fm}/c\); number of parallel ensembles: \(N_E = 10000\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the ISGQR using the BUU transport equation (KHYPRC95, XJPRC102) by employing a lattice Hamiltonian method and a full-ensemble stochastic collision approach that improve ground-state stability and treatment of the collision term, particularly Pauli blocking. The extracted effective mass constraints are consistent with values from Skyrme-RPA calculations (ZZPRC93, ZZCPC45) and with results from the ISGQR of spherical and deformed samarium isotopes (YosPRC88). The finding that collisional damping dominates the ISGQR width for heavy nuclei, unlike the IVGDR case where Landau damping contributes significantly (WRPLB807), suggests that the ISGQR is an ideal probe for constraining the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section.
