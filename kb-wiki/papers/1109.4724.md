# Signature of strange dibaryon in kaon-induced reaction

> We examine how the signature of the strange-dibaryon resonances in the barKNN-piSigmaN system shows up in the scattering amplitude on the physical real energy axis within the framework of Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations. The so-called point method is applied to handle the three-body unitarity cut in the amplitudes. We also discuss the possibility that the strange-dibaryon production reactions can be used for discriminating between existing models of the two-body barKN-piSigma system with Lambda(1405).

## Key claim

The signature of strange-dibaryon resonances in the $\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N$ system can emerge as a clear peak or bump in the scattering amplitude on the physical real energy axis, but the prominence of this signal depends on which model of the two-body $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction is used, meaning the production reactions could discriminate between models of $\Lambda(1405)$.

## Method

The authors solved Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the $\bar{K}NN-\pi\Sigma N$ coupled-channel three-body system using separable potentials for the two-body interactions. They applied the point method, which evaluates amplitudes at finite positive imaginary energies and then extrapolates to the real axis via a continued fraction formula, to handle the three-body unitarity cut and moon-shape singularities in the driving term. Two models of the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction were compared: an energy-independent (E-indep.) separable potential and an energy-dependent (E-dep.) separable potential, both derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term with cutoff parameters fitted to $\bar{K}N$ cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The E-indep. model produces a strange-dibaryon resonance at $W_R = 2329.5 - i23.3$ MeV.
- The E-dep. model produces a strange-dibaryon resonance at $W_R = 2352.0 - i22.5$ MeV.
- A peak in $|X_{(\bar{K}N)N-(\bar{K}N)N}|^2$ appears around $W \sim 2310$ MeV for the E-indep. model.
- A bump in $|X_{(\bar{K}N)N-(\bar{K}N)N}|^2$ appears around $W \sim 2350$ MeV for the E-dep. model.
- Cutoff parameters for the E-indep. model: $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N} = 1000$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\pi\Sigma} = 700$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\bar{K}N} = 920$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Sigma} = 960$ MeV.
- Cutoff parameters for the E-dep. model: $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N} = 1000$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\pi\Sigma} = 700$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\bar{K}N} = 725$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Sigma} = 725$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strange-dibaryon resonances using AGS equations (Ref3, Ref4) and variational methods (Ref5, Ref6, Ref7) by calculating the scattering amplitude directly on the real energy axis, which is the necessary ingredient for computing cross sections measurable at facilities like J-PARC. The two models of the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction differ in their analytic structure: the E-indep. model has one pole for $\Lambda(1405)$, while the E-dep. model has two poles similar to the chiral unitary model (Ref12). The results suggest that kaon-induced strange-dibaryon production reactions could discriminate between these models, enabling a deeper understanding of $\Lambda(1405)$ and deeply bound kaonic nuclei.
