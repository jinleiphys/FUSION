# Electromagnetic reactions of few-body systems with the Lorentz integral transform method

> Various electromagnetic few-body break-up reactions into the many-body continuum are calculated microscopically with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method. For three- and four-body nuclei the nuclear Hamiltonian includes two- and three- nucleon forces, while semirealistic interactions are used in case of six- and seven-body systems. Comparisons with experimental data are discussed. In addition various interesting aspects of the $^4$He photodisintegration are studied: investigation of a tetrahedrical symmetry of $^4$He and a test of non-local nuclear force models via the induced two-body currents.

# Electromagnetic reactions of few-body systems with the Lorentz integral transform method

## Key claim

The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method enables fully microscopic calculations of electromagnetic break-up reactions into the many-body continuum for few-body nuclei, but the agreement with experimental data is not uniform across all systems: for $^3$He the three-nucleon force improves agreement with data, while for $^3$H it worsens it, and for $^4$He photodisintegration the theoretical prediction of a pronounced giant dipole peak is confirmed by some experiments but contradicted by others.

## Method

The LIT method avoids explicit calculation of continuum wave functions by solving a bound-state-like equation with a complex energy term, using expansions in hyperspherical harmonics (HH) with either correlation functions (CHH) or an effective interaction (EIHH). For three- and four-body nuclei the Hamiltonian includes realistic two- and three-nucleon forces (AV18 NN plus UIX 3N potentials), while for six- and seven-body systems semirealistic NN potentials (Malfliet-Tjon, Minnesota, Argonne V4') are used. Longitudinal response functions are computed with the non-relativistic charge operator including relativistic corrections, and total photoabsorption cross sections use the unretarded dipole approximation.

## Key numbers

- $^3$He/$^3$H longitudinal response $R_L(\omega,q)$ calculated at momentum transfers 250 MeV/c $\le q \le$ 500 MeV/c with AV18+UIX potentials
- $^4$He total photoabsorption cross section calculated with AV18+UIX yields a bremsstrahlungs sum rule $\Sigma_{\rm BSR}$ = 2.410 mb
- For $^4$He with AV18+UIX: $\langle r_{p/n}^2 \rangle$ = 2.04 fm$^2$, $\langle r_{pp/nn}^2 \rangle$ = 5.67 fm$^2$, $\langle r_{np}^2 \rangle$ = 5.34 fm$^2$
- Ratio $\langle r_{pp}^2 \rangle / \langle r_{p}^2 \rangle$ = 2.78 and $\langle r_{np}^2 \rangle / \langle r_{p}^2 \rangle$ = 2.62, compared to tetrahedral symmetry value of 2.67
- $^7$Li total photoabsorption cross section shows good agreement with experimental data from Ahrens
- $^6$He photoabsorption exhibits two separate peaks corresponding to a soft mode at low energies and a Gamov-Teller mode at higher energies

## Context

This work extends the LIT method introduced by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1994) from three-body systems to four-, six-, and seven-body nuclei, enabling ab initio calculations of reactions into the many-body continuum. The $^4$He photodisintegration results with realistic forces (AV18+UIX) confirm the earlier semirealistic calculations by Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1997) showing a pronounced giant dipole peak, reviving experimental activities by Nilsson and Shima. The method enables tests of modern nuclear force models (JISP by Shirokov et al., UCOM by Feldmeier et al.) via comparison of photoabsorption cross sections, and allows investigation of $^4$He tetrahedral symmetry through sum rules.
