# Testing gravity with the latent heat of neutron star matter

> The Seidov limit is a bound on the maximum latent heat that a presumed first-order phase transition of neutron-star matter can have before its excess energy density, not compensated by additional pressure, results in gravitational collapse. Because latent heat forces an apparent nonanalytic behaviour in plots correlating physical quantities (kinks in two-dimensional, ridges in three-dimensional ones), it can be constrained by data. As the onset of collapse depends on the intensity of gravity, testing for sudden derivative changes and, if they are large, breaching the Seidov limit would reward with two successive discoveries: such a phase transition (which could stem from hadron matter but also from a gravitational phase transition), and a modification of General Relativity (thus breaking the matter/gravity degeneracy). We illustrate the point with $f(R)=R+αR^2$ metric gravity.

## Key claim
The Seidov limit on the maximum latent heat of a first-order phase transition in neutron-star matter can be used to test modified gravity theories, because the onset of gravitational collapse depends on the intensity of gravity; breaching the Seidov limit would indicate both a phase transition and a modification of General Relativity, breaking the matter/gravity degeneracy. The authors illustrate this with \(f(R)=R+\alpha R^2\) metric gravity but note that the generalized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations they present are valid for a broader family of theories.

## Method
The authors derive the generalized TOV equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars in \(f(R)=R+\alpha R^2\) gravity, working in the Einstein frame via a conformal transformation and then inverting back to the Jordan frame. They numerically integrate the system of differential equations (including the scalar field equation of motion) for a family of equations of state (EoS) that are not constrained by astrophysical observations, spanning from extremely soft (ExS) to extremely rigid (ExR), and for positive values of the parameter \(\alpha\). They then extend Seidov's small-core analytic derivation of the maximum latent heat to this modified gravity theory, using a perturbation expansion near the center of the star.

## Key numbers
- The Seidov limit in General Relativity predicts a maximum energy-density jump \(\rho_2 - \rho_1 = \rho_1\left(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{3}{2}\frac{p_1}{\rho_1}\right)\) for a small core.
- For the stiffest EoS (ExR) with central pressure \(p_c = 4000\;\text{MeV/fm}^3\), the critical value of \(\alpha\) (where the conformal factor \(\mathcal{D}^2 = 1 + 2\alpha R\) becomes negative inside the star) is around \(\alpha = 20\;\text{km}^2\).
- For the extremely soft EoS (ExS) with central pressure \(p_c = 1000\;\text{MeV/fm}^3\) and \(\alpha = 25\;\text{km}^2\), the dilatation factor \((1 + 2\alpha R)\) touches zero inside the star.
- For the stiffest EoS ExR with \(\alpha = 50\;\text{km}^2\), the dilatation factor also crosses zero inside the star.

## Context
This work extends Seidov's original limit (Seidov, 1971) on the maximum latent heat in General Relativity to \(f(R)\) modified gravity, building on the authors' earlier study (Moreno, 2023) of the GR limit. It enables using latent heat as a diagnostic to distinguish between matter effects and gravitational modifications, complementing other tests such as the maximum-mass ceiling (Astashenok, 2021) and the Buchdahl-Bondi limit. The EoS band used is from the authors' group (Lope-Oter, 2021) and is constructed from chiral perturbation theory and perturbative QCD without astrophysical constraints, making it suitable for testing alternative gravity theories.
