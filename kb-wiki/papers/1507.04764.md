# Critical versus spurious fluctuations in the search for the QCD critical point

> The neighborhood of the QCD chiral critical point is characterized by intense fluctuations of the chiral field which could, in principle, generate pronounced experimental signatures. However, experimental uncertainties which are inherent to heavy ion collisions, as well as the modest size and duration of the formed plasma, will severely attenuate these signatures. Using Monte Carlo techniques, we study second-order event-by-event moments of pions as a prototype for signatures of the chiral critical point based on the enhancement of the correlation length and event-by-event analysis. We test their viability against some realistic ingredients, similar to the ones found in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key claim

Using Monte Carlo simulations of second-order event-by-event moments of pions, the authors find that signatures of the QCD chiral critical point based on enhanced correlation length are likely below 5% in realistic heavy-ion collision conditions, even under optimistic assumptions about critical slowing down and finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo framework that samples chiral field fluctuations using a Gaussian probability distribution for the sigma field, then samples pion occupation numbers from a Boltzmann distribution with a fluctuating pion mass. They incorporated spurious fluctuations from centrality bin width effects (volume fluctuations) and Gaussian temperature fluctuations with 5% standard deviation, and estimated the maximum attainable correlation length using a dynamical model of critical slowing down based on Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999). The simulation used parameters from STAR Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, with T = 130 MeV, plasma radius 6.8 fm, and only pions with transverse momentum below 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The maximum correlation length reached in the most optimistic scenario is 3.2 fm (2.0 times the baseline xi_0 = 1.6 fm), with a more realistic peak of 2.9 fm (1.8 times xi_0)
- The variance of charged pion multiplicity shows an increase of almost 10% for the maximum correlation length of 3.2 fm, but only 1.4% for a more moderate choice of the dynamical parameter A = A_max/10
- The quantity F(p_T) shows increases of 2.2% for xi = 2.9 fm and 3.0% for xi = 3.2 fm
- Spurious fluctuations increase the normalized variance of N from 1.0 to 2.0 at the baseline correlation length xi = 1.6 fm
- The mixed cumulant <Delta N Delta p_T> reaches up to 35% signal but is discarded as unrealistic due to the 30 MeV temperature difference between chemical and kinetic freeze-out

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Stephanov (1999, 2008) and Athanasiou (2010) for critical point signatures by testing them against realistic experimental conditions for the first time using computer simulations. The authors incorporate finite-size effects via Dirichlet boundary conditions and critical slowing down estimates from Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999), finding that spurious fluctuations from freeze-out condition variations and limited correlation length growth severely attenuate signals compared to equilibrium predictions. The results suggest that experimental searches at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program face significant challenges, and the authors propose testing strongly intensive fluctuation measures such as the Phi-measure for transverse momentum from Gazdzicki (1992) as potentially more robust alternatives.
