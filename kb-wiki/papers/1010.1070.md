# Skyrme-force dependence of fusion cross-sections using Wong formula in semiclassical formalism

> we use the nuclear proximity potential obtained recently for the Skyrme nucleus-nucleus interaction in the semiclassical extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approach using Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF) under frozen-density (sudden-without-exchange) approximation, and $Φ$=0$^0$. This method allows the barrier modifications by using different Skyrme forces, since a different Skyrme force would mean different barrier characteristics, and possibly a different force for different reaction cross-section.

## Key claim
The Skyrme force dependence in the semiclassical extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approach is evident for evaporation-residue cross-sections but not for capture cross-sections, meaning that different Skyrme forces (SIII, SV, GSkI) fit different reaction types, and barrier modification must be added empirically for cases where the force choice does not match.

## Method
The authors used the nuclear proximity potential derived from the Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF) under the frozen-density (sudden-without-exchange) approximation with azimuthal angle Φ=0°. They calculated the total interaction potential by adding Coulomb and centrifugal terms to the nuclear potential for deformed and oriented nuclei, then applied the ℓ-summed Wong formula with Hill-Wheeler penetrability to compute fusion cross-sections for Ca- and Ni-based reactions, comparing results for three Skyrme forces (SIII, SV, GSkI).

## Key numbers
- For capture cross-sections in ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U, ²⁴⁴Pu, and ²⁴⁸Cm reactions, all Skyrme forces (SIII, SV, GSkI) reproduce experimental data with only small changes in ℓ_max values (see insets in Fig. 1).
- For evaporation-residue cross-sections in ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni reactions, the SIII force gives the best fit, while for ⁶⁴Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo, the GSkI force gives the best fit.
- The deduced ℓ_max values for the ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni reaction using the fitted force compare nicely with experimental values from Beckerman (1981) at higher center-of-mass energies.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous use of the ℓ-summed Wong formula with multipole deformations up to hexadecapole (β₄) for capture cross-sections in superheavy nucleus formation (Kumar 2009, Bansal 2010), and also relates to the dynamical cluster-decay model (DCM) of Gupta and collaborators (2010) where barrier lowering arises naturally. The paper demonstrates that the choice of Skyrme force matters for evaporation-residue cross-sections but not for capture, enabling future studies to select appropriate Skyrme forces for different reaction channels rather than using a single universal force.
