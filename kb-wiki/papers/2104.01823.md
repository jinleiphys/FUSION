# Effective field theory for shallow P-wave states

> We discuss the formulation of a non-relativistic effective field theory for two-body P-wave scattering in the presence of shallow states and critically address various approaches to renormalization proposed in the literature. It is demonstrated that the consistent renormalization involving only a finite number of parameters in the well-established formalism with auxiliary dimer fields corresponds to the inclusion of an infinite number of counterterms in the formulation with contact interactions only. We also discuss the implications from the Wilsonian renormalization group analysis of P-wave scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the apparent inconsistency of effective field theory for shallow P-wave states, which leads to complex bare coupling constants in the large-cutoff limit, is an artifact of the "large-cutoff RG-invariant" renormalization approach rather than a fundamental limitation of the EFT itself; a proper subtractive renormalization scheme that includes an infinite number of counterterms yields a well-defined amplitude with no constraints on the effective range from renormalization alone.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for P-wave scattering with a sharp cutoff regulator, using a bare potential with two contact interaction terms proportional to \(C_2 p' p\) and \(C_4 p' p (p'^2 + p^2)\). They compare two renormalization approaches: the "large-cutoff RG-invariant" method, which matches the amplitude to the effective range expansion and then takes the cutoff to infinity, and a subtractive renormalization scheme that removes all power-law ultraviolet divergences by introducing subtraction scales \(\mu_n\) before taking the cutoff to infinity. The equivalence of the subtractive scheme to including an infinite number of counterterms in the contact-interaction formulation is explicitly demonstrated by constructing the full bare potential that reproduces the renormalized amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The effective range function for P-waves is parameterized as \(k^3 \cot \delta = -1/a + r k^2/2 + v_2 k^4 + \ldots\), where \(a\) is the scattering length, \(r\) the effective range, and \(v_2\) the shape parameter.
- For fine-tuned P-wave systems, the scaling scenarios considered are: \(1/a \sim M_{\rm lo}^3\), \(r \sim M_{\rm lo}\) (Eq. 1) and \(1/a \sim M_{\rm lo}^2 M_{\rm hi}\), \(r \sim M_{\rm hi}\) (Eq. 2), with \(v_n \sim M_{\rm hi}^{3-2n}\).
- In the large-cutoff RG-invariant approach, the bare coupling constants become complex for sufficiently large cutoff \(\Lambda\), with the condition for real \(C_2, C_4\) given by \(r \le -16\Lambda/(9\pi)\) for enhanced scattering length.
- The subtractively renormalized effective range function is \(k^3 \cot \delta = -1/a + r k^2/2 - (k^4/(2\pi)) \cdot 3(4\mu_1 + \pi r)^2 / (6\pi a^{-1} - 4\mu_3^3 + 3k^2(4\mu_1 + \pi r))\), where \(\mu_1\) and \(\mu_3\) are subtraction scales.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pedagogical papers on non-perturbative renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in EFT for S-waves (Epelbaum 2009, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020) to the case of P-wave scattering. It critically examines the "large-cutoff RG-invariant" approach used by Hammer (2019) and Habashi (2020), which concluded that renormalization forces the effective range to be negative for resonant P-waves, and contrasts it with the standard QFT renormalization procedure. The paper also revisits the halo EFT formulations of Bertulani (2002) and Bedaque (2003) for fine-tuned P-wave systems with auxiliary dimer fields, showing that the subtractive renormalization scheme resolves the apparent conflict with the Wigner bound without requiring auxiliary fields.
