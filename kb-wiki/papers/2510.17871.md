# Interactions of the deuteron with a hadronic medium

> We investigate the interactions of the deuteron with light mesons during the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions. We treat the deuteron as a weakly bound state and employ the quasi-free approximation to describe the $dπ$ interaction. The underlying elementary $Nπ$ amplitudes are described by a hybrid effective model, combining the non-resonant background from chiral perturbation theory with resonant contributions via Breit-Wigner parameterizations. These amplitudes are used to calculate the vacuum and thermally-averaged cross-sections for deuteron dissociation and production, namely, $d + π\rightarrow N + N' + π$ and the corresponding inverse reaction. We then use these cross sections in a rate equation to estimate the time evolution of the deuteron multiplicity. For the initial conditions we consider two models: the statistical hadronization model and the coalescence model, where the deuteron is treated as a hadronic molecule. Our findings suggest that the final deuteron yield does not retain a memory of its initial production mechanism.

## Key claim

The final deuteron yield in heavy-ion collisions does not retain a memory of its initial production mechanism, whether from the statistical hadronization model or the coalescence model, because interactions with pions in the hadronic gas drive the multiplicity toward a common value; however, this conclusion depends on the quasi-free approximation for the deuteron-pion interaction and the assumption that pions dominate the hadronic medium.

## Method

The authors computed deuteron-pion cross sections using the quasi-free (impulse) approximation, where the pion scatters off one constituent nucleon while the other acts as a spectator. The elementary nucleon-pion amplitudes combined a chiral perturbation theory non-resonant background with Breit-Wigner parameterizations for the Delta(1232) and Delta(1920) resonances, and included monopole form factors with a cutoff near 3 GeV to extend the description to higher energies. These vacuum cross sections were thermally averaged and used in a kinetic rate equation to evolve the deuteron multiplicity over time, starting from initial conditions given by either the statistical hadronization model or the coalescence model.

## Key numbers

* Deuterons have a binding energy of approximately 2.2 MeV, with quantum numbers I(J^P) = 0(1^+).
* The chiral perturbation theory coupling constants used are g_A = 1.267 and F_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* The cutoff scale for the monopole form factors is Lambda ~ 3 GeV, chosen to be greater than the chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV.
* The hadronic phase lifetime estimates from the literature are 4-8 fm/c at lower energies and 5-15 fm/c from HBT analyses.
* The Delta(1232) and Delta(1920) resonances in the P33 partial wave appear below sqrt(s) = 2 GeV in the p pi+ elastic scattering cross section.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of exotic hadrons (X, Y, Z states) in the hadron gas formed in heavy-ion collisions, where they used the hadronic medium as a filter to distinguish molecular from tetraquark states. It builds on the finding that both the statistical hadronization model (SHM) and the coalescence model (COM) can describe deuteron production data, as shown by ALICE (2022) and others. The results can be compared with the stochastic transport approach of Staudenmaier (2021), which found that multi-particle interactions reduce deuteron equilibration time, and with Sun (2022), who showed the deuteron yield remains nearly unchanged during hadronic expansion. The work enables future studies of how other molecular candidates, such as the X(3872), behave in the hadronic medium.
