# Improved short-range correlations and 0nbb nuclear matrix elements of 76Ge and 82Se

> We calculate the nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decays of $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se for the light-neutrino exchange mechanism. The nuclear wave functions are obtained by using realistic two-body forces within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA). We include the effects that come from the finite size of a nucleon, from the higher-order terms of nucleonic weak currents, and from the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations. Most importantly, we improve on the presently available calculations by replacing the rudimentary Jastrow short-range correlations by the more advanced unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). The UCOM corrected matrix elements turn out to be notably larger in magnitude than the Jastrow corrected ones. This has drastic consequences for the detectability of $0νββ$ decay in the present and future double beta experiments.

## Key claim
Replacing the traditional Jastrow short-range correlations with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) yields notably larger neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for 76Ge and 82Se, with the UCOM reducing bare matrix element magnitudes by only 7%–16% compared to the 30%–40% reduction from the Jastrow method.

## Method
The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with realistic Bonn-A G-matrix two-body forces and a 1p-0f-2s-1d-0g-0h11/2 single-particle model space. They applied corrections for nucleon finite size, higher-order terms of nucleonic weak currents, and short-range correlations, comparing the traditional Jastrow method with the UCOM approach. The particle-particle strength parameter gpp was fixed using experimental two-neutrino double-beta decay half-lives.

## Key numbers
- Bare matrix element M(0ν) for 76Ge: -8.529; after all corrections including UCOM: -6.080 (with gpp=1.0, gA=1.254)
- Bare matrix element M(0ν) for 82Se: -5.398; after all corrections including UCOM: -3.722 (with gpp=1.0, gA=1.254)
- Final UCOM-corrected matrix element range for 76Ge: |M(0ν)| = 5.355–6.555 (gpp=1.02–1.06, gA=1.0–1.254)
- Final UCOM-corrected matrix element range for 82Se: |M(0ν)| = 3.722–4.597 (gpp=0.96–1.00, gA=1.0–1.254)
- Predicted half-life for 76Ge: t1/2(0ν) = (0.96–1.44)×10^24 yr / (⟨mν⟩[eV])^2
- Predicted half-life for 82Se: t1/2(0ν) = (4.53–6.90)×10^23 yr / (⟨mν⟩[eV])^2
- Jastrow-corrected matrix element for 76Ge (with gpp=1.0, gA=1.254): -4.723; UCOM-corrected: -6.080

## Context
This work extends the pnQRPA calculations of Rodin et al. (2006) and Simkovic et al. (1999) by replacing the phenomenological Jastrow short-range correlation method with the more microscopic UCOM approach, previously demonstrated for 48Ca and 76Ge by Kortelainen and Suhonen (2007). The larger UCOM-corrected matrix elements significantly reduce predicted 0νββ half-lives compared to earlier Jastrow-based results, directly impacting the neutrino-mass sensitivity estimates for experiments such as NEMO 3, CUORICINO, and future large-scale detectors. The results also show that the Jastrow method overestimates short-range correlation effects, especially at higher multipoles.
