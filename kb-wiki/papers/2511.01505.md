# Systematic study of scalar, vector, and mixed density dependencies in relativistic mean-field descriptions of hyperonic matter in neutron stars

> We investigate the equation of state (EOS) of hyperonic neutron star (NS) matter within a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) framework. The effects of scalar, vector, and mixed density dependencies in meson-baryon couplings are systematically examined along with alternative forms of the $ρ$-meson coupling. Several meson-nucleon parameter sets are explored here for the first time for neutron stars and compared with the standard DD2 EOS. Most new parameterizations produce stiffer EOSs, leading to neutron stars with larger radii and higher tidal deformabilities. However, the inclusion of $Λ$ hyperons softens these EOSs, and the resulting maximum masses still satisfy the two solar mass limits and agree with NICER measurements. These results highlight the importance of exploring alternative density dependencies in constraining dense matter through multi-messenger observations.

# Systematic study of scalar, vector, and mixed density dependencies in relativistic mean-field descriptions of hyperonic matter in neutron stars

## Key claim

Most new density-dependent relativistic mean-field parameterizations produce stiffer equations of state than the standard DD2 model for both nucleonic and hyperonic matter, yet the inclusion of Λ hyperons softens these EOSs such that maximum masses remain above 2 solar masses and agree with NICER measurements at 95% credible interval, though some models are marginally inconsistent with the 90% tidal deformability constraint from GW170817.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for cold β-equilibrium neutron star matter within a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) framework, examining three types of density dependence for meson-baryon couplings: scalar density dependent (SDD), vector density dependent (VDD), and mixed density dependent (MDD), with either rational or exponential functional forms for the ρ-meson coupling. They solve the meson field equations simultaneously with constraints of charge neutrality, baryon number conservation, and β-equilibrium, then compute stellar structure by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for both purely nucleonic matter and matter including Λ hyperons coupled via SU(6) symmetry relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for hyperonic matter range from 2.09 M⊙ (SZR model) to 2.29 M⊙ (MZE and MZR models), all above the 2 M⊙ limit
- Radii at 1.4 M⊙ for nucleonic matter range from 12.95 km (SZR) to 13.82 km (VZR), consistent with NICER observations of PSR J0437-4715 and PSR J0030+0451 at 95% credibility (11.5–14.5 km)
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 M⊙ for nucleonic matter ranges from 650.12 (MPE) to 772.36 (VZR), broadly consistent with GW170817 constraints
- Λ hyperon onset densities vary from 0.326 fm⁻³ (MPE) to 0.350 fm⁻³ (MZE), with corresponding onset pressures from 36.65 MeV fm⁻³ (MPE) to 54.68 MeV fm⁻³ (MZR)
- The scalar coupling ratio RσΛ = gσΛ/gσN ranges from 0.52 (MZE and MZR) to 0.62 (VZR)

## Context

This work extends the DDRMF studies of Typel (2018), who investigated scalar-, vector-, and mixed-density dependencies in uniform nuclear matter, to the context of neutron stars including hyperons. It complements the mixed-density dependent study of Li (2023) that was limited to purely nucleonic matter, and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by observations of massive neutron stars such as PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013) and PSR J0740+6620 (Fonseca, 2021). The results enable future Bayesian analyses following the prescriptions of Char (2023, 2025) to further constrain the parameter space using multi-messenger data from NICER and gravitational wave events.
