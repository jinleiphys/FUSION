# Faddeev equation approach for three-cluster nuclear reactions

> In this lecture we aim to present a formalism based on Faddeev-like equations for describing nuclear three-cluster reactions that include elastic, transfer and breakup channels. Two different techniques based on momentum-space and configuration-space representations are explained in detail. An important new feature of these methods is the possibility to account for the repulsive Coulomb interaction between two of the three clusters in all channels. Comparison with previous calculations based on approximate methods used in nuclear reaction theory is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents two rigorous methods (momentum-space screening/renormalization and configuration-space complex scaling) for solving three-cluster nuclear reactions including elastic, transfer, and breakup channels, with the key advance being the ability to account for repulsive Coulomb interactions between two of the three clusters in all channels, though the breakup treatment is limited to systems with at most two charged particles.

## Method

The momentum-space method uses Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations with screened Coulomb potentials, where the screening radius is taken to infinity and the resulting diverging phase factors are renormalized analytically to recover full Coulomb amplitudes. The configuration-space method employs Faddeev-Merkuriev equations with a complex scaling transformation that avoids explicit treatment of complicated asymptotic boundary conditions, splitting the Coulomb interaction into short-range and long-range parts via a cut-off function. Both approaches are implemented in partial-wave bases and solved numerically, with the momentum-space equations solved via Neumann series summation using the iterative Pade method.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius convergence demonstrated for alpha-deuteron elastic scattering at 4.81 MeV deuteron lab energy: results with R=5 fm, 10 fm, and 15 fm are compared, showing convergence by R=15 fm
- Screening radius convergence demonstrated for alpha-deuteron breakup at 15 MeV alpha lab energy: results with R=10 fm, 15 fm, and 20 fm are compared, showing convergence by R=20 fm
- Optimal screening function exponent n is in the range 3 to 8, with n=4 used in the calculations shown
- Euler number C ≈ 0.5772156649 appears in the analytic expression for the renormalization phase
- Fine-structure constant α_e.m. ≈ 1/137 appears in the Coulomb parameter definition

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS formalism originally developed for three-nucleon systems with short-range interactions (Faddeev, 1960; Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas, 1967) to include Coulomb interactions via screening and renormalization (Taylor, 1974; Semon, 1975) and via configuration-space complex scaling (Nuttall, 1971; Moiseyev). The momentum-space results for proton-deuteron elastic scattering agree well with variational configuration-space solutions of Kievsky (2001) that used unscreened Coulomb with explicit boundary conditions. The methods enable rigorous treatment of three-cluster reactions involving charged particles, including elastic scattering, transfer, and breakup, for systems such as alpha-deuteron and proton-deuteron, with the configuration-space complex scaling approach being particularly promising for extending calculations beyond the p-d case.
