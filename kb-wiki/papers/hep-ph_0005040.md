# Dispersion Relations in Ultradegenerate Relativistic Plasmas

> The propagation of excitation modes in a relativistic ultradegenerate plasma is modified by their interactions with the medium. These modifications can be computed by evaluating their on-shell self-energy, which gives (gauge-independent) dispersion relations. For modes with momentum close to the Fermi momentum, the one-loop fermion self-energy is dominated by a diagram with a soft photon in the loop. We find the one-loop dispersion relations for quasiparticles and antiquasiparticles, which behave differently as a consequence of their very different phase-space restrictions when they scatter with the electrons of the Fermi sea. In a relativistic system, the unscreened magnetic interactions spoil the normal Fermi liquid behavior of the plasma. For small values of the Fermi velocity, we recover the non-relativistic dispersion relations of condensed matter systems.

# Dispersion Relations in Ultradegenerate Relativistic Plasmas

## Key claim

The one-loop dispersion relations for quasiparticles and antiquasiparticles in a relativistic ultradegenerate electromagnetic plasma are gauge-independent when evaluated on-shell, and they differ significantly between particles and antiparticles due to different phase-space restrictions from scattering with electrons in the Fermi sea. The unscreened magnetic interactions spoil normal Fermi liquid behavior in the relativistic regime, but for small Fermi velocities the non-relativistic dispersion relations of condensed matter systems are recovered.

## Method

The one-loop fermion self-energy was computed using the imaginary time formalism at zero temperature, with the photon propagator dressed by hard dense loop (HDL) resummation to account for Debye screening and Landau damping. For modes with momentum close to the Fermi momentum, the dominant contribution comes from a diagram with a soft photon in the loop; the on-shell imaginary part of the self-energy was evaluated first, then the real part was reconstructed via a Kramers-Kronig dispersion relation. The damping rates for electrons and positrons were computed separately using the spectral functions of the HDL photon propagators in Coulomb gauge.

## Key numbers

- Damping rate for quasiparticles near the Fermi surface: $\gamma_{+} \sim \frac{e^2}{24 \pi} |E - \mu| + \frac{e^2}{64 v^2_F M} (E - \mu)^2 + \mathcal{O}(|E-\mu|^3)$, where $M = \sqrt{e^2 \mu^2 v_F / \pi^2}$ is the Debye mass
- Damping rate for antiquasiparticles: $\gamma_{-} \sim e^2 M \left(\frac{v^2_F}{24 \pi} + \frac{1}{64}\right)$
- Real part of the self-energy for quasiparticles: $\text{Re} \Sigma_+(E,p) \sim \text{Re} \Sigma_+(\mu,p) + \frac{e^2}{12 \pi^2} (E-\mu) \ln \frac{M}{|E-\mu|} + \frac{e^2}{32 \pi v^2_F} |E-\mu| + \mathcal{O}((E-\mu)^2)$
- Real part of the self-energy for antiquasiparticles: $\text{Re} \Sigma_-(-E,p) \sim \text{Re} \Sigma_-(-\mu,p) + \left(\frac{e^2 v^2_F}{12 \pi^2} + \frac{e^2}{32 \pi}\right) (\mu - E) + \mathcal{O}((\mu - E)^2)$
- Non-relativistic lifetime of an electron: $\frac{1}{\tau} = \frac{\sqrt{3} \pi^2 \omega_p}{32} \left( \frac{E-\mu}{\mu}\right)^2 \frac{c^4}{v^4_F}$, which reduces to $\frac{1}{\tau} = \frac{\sqrt{3} \pi^2 \omega_p}{128} \left( \frac{\epsilon_{nr}-\mu_{nr}}{\mu_{nr}} \right)^2$ in the non-relativistic limit

## Context

This work generalizes the results of Le Bellac and Manuel (1997) for the damping rate of quasiparticles in degenerate ultrarelativistic plasmas to the case where the Fermi velocity $v_F \neq c$, allowing exploration of all energy domains from the ultrarelativistic to the non-relativistic limit. The non-relativistic limit reproduces the dispersion relations from the condensed matter literature by Pines and Nozieres (1988) and Holstein, Norton, and Pincus (1973). The results can be transported to QCD by replacing the electromagnetic coupling constant and adding color factors, and are relevant for understanding antiquark propagation in color superconducting phases of dense QCD, where the dispersion relations for antiquarks in the presence of a color gap have not yet been determined.
