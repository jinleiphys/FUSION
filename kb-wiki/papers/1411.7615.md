# New formulation of leading order anisotropic hydrodynamics

> Anisotropic hydrodynamics is a reorganization of the relativistic hydrodynamics expansion, with the leading order already containing substantial momentum-space anisotropies. The latter are a cause of concern in the traditional viscous hydrodynamics, since large momentum anisotropies generated in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are not consistent with the hypothesis of small deviations from an isotropic background, i.e., from the local equilibrium distribution. We discuss the leading order of the expansion, presenting a new formulation for the (1+1)--dimensional case, namely, for the longitudinally boost invariant and cylindrically symmetric flow. This new approach is consistent with the well established framework of Israel and Stewart in the close to equilibrium limit (where we expect viscous hydrodynamics to work well). If we consider the (0+1)--dimensional case, that is, transversally homogeneous and longitudinally boost invariant flow, {the new form of anisotropic hydrodynamics leads to better agreement with known solutions} of the Boltzmann equation than the previous formulations, especially when we consider finite mass particles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new formulation for the leading order of anisotropic hydrodynamics in the (1+1)-dimensional case (longitudinally boost invariant and cylindrically symmetric flow) that is consistent with Israel-Stewart theory in the close-to-equilibrium limit and, for the (0+1)-dimensional case, leads to better agreement with known solutions of the Boltzmann equation than previous formulations, especially for finite mass particles.

## Method

The author generalizes the Romatschke-Strickland form of the anisotropic distribution function to include separate anisotropy parameters for the longitudinal direction (Z) and the two transverse directions (X and Y, with X aligned with the transverse flow). The system of equations is closed by using the second moment of the Boltzmann equation in addition to energy-momentum conservation, and the collisional kernel is treated in the relaxation time approximation. The new formulation is tested against exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation for a longitudinally boost invariant and transversely homogeneous system.

## Key numbers

- For the (0+1)-dimensional case, the new formulation (eaHydro) shows large improvement in agreement with the exact solution compared to the original prescription (saHydro), especially for massive particles.
- The shear evolution τΠ_η is "very well reproduced" by the new formulation, where Π_η = (2/3)(P_T - (1/3)P_L) and τ is the longitudinal proper time.
- The bulk evolution τΠ_ζ still shows "some deviations" from the exact solution, where Π_ζ = (1/3)(2P_T + P_L - 3P_eq) and P_eq is the equilibrium pressure.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework originally developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), Ryblewski (2010), and others, which treats large momentum-space anisotropies non-perturbatively at leading order. The original prescription used the Romatschke-Strickland form (Romatschke, 2003) and did not account for transverse-plane anisotropies, requiring next-to-leading order treatment for systems with transverse expansion. The new formulation enables more accurate modeling of heavy-ion collisions with radial flow and is consistent with the well-established Israel-Stewart framework (Israel, 1979) in the near-equilibrium limit. The paper notes that bulk dynamics remains imperfectly reproduced, and suggests introducing an extra degree of freedom for isotropic pressure corrections as proposed by Nopoush (2014).
