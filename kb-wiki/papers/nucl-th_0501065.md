# Finite-size effect on two-particle production in continuous and discrete spectrum

> The formalism allowing one to account for the effect of a finite space-time extent of particle production region is given. Its applications to the lifetime measurement of hadronic atoms produced by a high-energy beam in a thin target, as well as to the femtoscopy techniques widely used to measure space-time characteristics of the production processes, are discussed. Particularly, it is found that the neglect of the finite-size effect on the pionium lifetime measurement in the experiment DIRAC at CERN could lead to the lifetime overestimation comparable with the 10% statistical error. The theoretical systematic errors arising in the calculation of the finite-size effect due to the neglect of non-equal emission times in the pair center-of-mass system, the space-time coherence and the residual charge are shown to be negligible.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neglecting the finite space-time extent of the pion production region in the DIRAC experiment's pionium lifetime measurement could lead to a lifetime overestimation comparable with the 10% statistical error, though the theoretical systematic errors from neglecting non-equal emission times, space-time coherence, and residual charge are shown to be negligible.

## Method

The author develops a formalism based on the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude to account for the finite space-time separation of particle emitters in two-particle production, treating both continuous and discrete spectrum cases. The formalism is applied to estimate the finite-size correction to the production of π⁺π⁻ pairs and atoms, using the two-particle space-time density matrix and the reduced Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the relative motion. The correction factors are derived by averaging bilinear products of these amplitudes over the distribution of relative space-time separations of short-lived emitters.

## Key numbers

- The Bohr radius of the π⁺π⁻ system is a = -387.5 fm.
- The s-wave ππ scattering length combination is f₀ = (1/3)(2a₀⁰ + a₀²) ≈ 0.2 fm.
- The characteristic momentum scale Qc = 4π/|a| = 6.4 MeV/c.
- The k-factor values for different Q₀ cuts: k = 0.615 for Q₀ = 2 MeV/c, 0.263 for Q₀ = 3 MeV/c, and 0.140 for Q₀ = 4 MeV/c.
- The finite-size correction to the number of non-atomic pairs at small Q and r* is positive (∼ 2f₀/r*) and changes sign at r* ∼ 10 fm due to the negative Coulomb FSI effect (∼ 2r*/a).
- For r* < 20 fm, the correction shows quasi-linear behavior in Q up to ∼ 50 MeV/c with a nearly universal negative slope.
- The typical ⟨r*⟩ for short-lived emitters is ∼ 10 fm, and r* ∼ 30 fm when one pion comes from ω-meson decay.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approximate treatment by Nemes (1985) that divided pion emitters into short-lived and long-lived categories with r* = 0 or r* = ∞, and the formalism by Lyuboshitz (1988) for weakly bound systems. The paper provides the theoretical basis for correcting the DIRAC experiment's pionium lifetime measurement, which aims to determine the ππ scattering length difference |a₀⁰ - a₀²| to 5% precision, improving on previous studies by Nagels (1979) and comparable to the precision of E865 at BNL and NA48/2 at CERN. The formalism also underlies correlation femtoscopy techniques for measuring space-time characteristics of particle production, as developed by Kopylov and Podgoretsky in the 1970s.
