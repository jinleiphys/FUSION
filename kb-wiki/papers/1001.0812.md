# Effects of the $Λ$(1405) on the Structure of Multi-Antikaonic Nuclei

> The effects of the $Λ$(1405) ($Λ^\ast$) on the structure of the multi-antikaonic nucleus (MKN), in which several $K^-$ mesons are embedded to form deeply bound states, are considered based on chiral symmetry combined with a relativistic mean-field theory. It is shown that additional attraction resulting from the $Λ^\ast$ pole has a sizable contribution to not only the density profiles for the nucleons and $K^-$ mesons but also the ground state energy of the $K^-$ mesons and binding energy of the MKN as the number of the embedded $K^-$ mesons increases.

## Key claim

The additional attraction from the Λ(1405) pole has a sizable contribution to the density profiles and binding energy of multi-antikaonic nuclei, but only when the lowest K⁻ energy lies below the Λ(1405) resonance region (ω_K⁻ ≈ 467 MeV); for |S| ≥ 12 the K⁻ mesons become unbound because the repulsive K⁻–K⁻ interaction overwhelms the attractive K⁻–N interactions.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field theory combined with a nonlinear effective chiral Lagrangian to describe multi-antikaonic nuclei with A=15, Z=8. They introduced the Λ(1405) pole contribution and range terms through second-order perturbation theory with respect to the axial current of hadrons, solving the coupled equations of motion for the K⁻ field, σ, ω, ρ mesons, and the Coulomb potential self-consistently. The parameters d_p, d_n, g_Λ*, and γ_Λ* were determined to reproduce the on-shell s-wave K⁻–N scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- The central baryon density reaches ρ_B^(0) ~ 3.5 ρ_0 (with ρ_0 = 0.153 fm⁻³) for |S| ~ 8.
- The lowest K⁻ energy ω_K⁻ is lowered by ~ 40 MeV from the case without the second-order effects due to the Λ(1405) pole attraction.
- For |S| ≥ 12 (with U_K = –80 MeV), K⁻ mesons become unbound, where ω_K⁻ ≳ m_Λ* – m_N = 467 MeV.
- The neutron skin thickness is 1–2 fm for |S| ~ 8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of multi-antikaonic nuclei (Muto, Maruyama, Tatsumi 2009) by incorporating the Λ(1405) pole contribution, which was motivated by the observation that ω_K⁻ enters the subthreshold resonance region. The results qualitatively agree with Gazda et al. (2008) regarding the instability of MKN as self-bound objects due to strong decay channels. The paper enables future studies of hyperon-mixing effects, which may lead to more strongly bound self-bound objects that decay only through weak processes, as suggested by a liquid-drop picture (Muto 2008), though this remains controversial depending on the adopted models (Gazda et al. 2009).
