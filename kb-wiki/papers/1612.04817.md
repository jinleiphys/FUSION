# Jet axes and universal transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation

> We study the transverse momentum spectrum of hadrons in jets. By measuring the transverse momentum with respect to a judiciously chosen axis, we find that this observable is insensitive to (the recoil of) soft radiation. Furthermore, for small transverse momenta we show that the effects of the jet boundary factorize, leading to a new transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation function. In contrast to the usual TMD fragmentation functions, it does not involve rapidity divergences and is universal in the sense that it is independent of the type of process and number of jets. These results directly apply to sub-jets instead of hadrons. We discuss potential applications, which include studying nuclear modification effects in heavy-ion collisions and identifying boosted heavy resonances.

## Key claim

By measuring the transverse momentum of hadrons in jets with respect to a recoil-insensitive (winner-take-all) jet axis, the observable becomes insensitive to soft radiation and the resulting transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation function is universal and free of rapidity divergences, unlike standard TMD fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors define a new class of transverse-momentum-dependent fragmentation functions (JTMDs) where the transverse momentum of a hadron is measured with respect to a recoil-free jet axis obtained via the winner-take-all recombination scheme. They derive factorization theorems for the cross section of hadron production inside jets in hadronic collisions, showing that for small transverse momenta (p_T R >> |k|) the jet boundary effects can be separated from the fragmentation, leading to a universal JTMD fragmentation function. The one-loop matching coefficients and renormalization group equations are calculated, and the all-orders anomalous dimensions are shown to be related to DGLAP splitting functions with a theta-function constraint.

## Key numbers

- The factorization theorem for the cross section holds with corrections of order R^2, where R is the jet radius (assumed R << 1)
- The further factorization of the fragmenting jet function into a boundary term B and a JTMD fragmentation function D holds with corrections of order |k|^2/(p_T^2 R^2), where p_T is the jet transverse momentum and |k| is the hadron transverse momentum relative to the jet axis
- The all-orders anomalous dimension for the JTMD fragmentation function is gamma'_ij(z,mu) = theta(z >= 1/2) * P_ji(z,mu), where P_ji are the DGLAP splitting functions

## Context

This work extends the study of fragmentation inside jets from longitudinal momentum distributions (studied by Procura (2009), Liu (2010), Jain (2011-2012), Bauer (2013), Ritzmann (2014), Chien (2015), and others) to transverse momentum distributions. It contrasts with standard TMD fragmentation functions used in Drell-Yan and SIDIS processes (Collins 2011, Echevarria 2016), which involve rapidity divergences and soft factors. The recoil-free axis approach builds on the winner-take-all recombination scheme introduced by Salam (unpublished) and Bertolini (2013). The results enable studies of nuclear modification effects in heavy-ion collisions and identification of boosted heavy resonances.
