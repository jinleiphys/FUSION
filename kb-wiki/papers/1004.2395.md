# Lambda and Anti-Lambda Hypernuclei in Relativistic Mean-field Theory

> Several aspects about $Λ$-hypernuclei in the relativistic mean field theory, including the effective $Λ$-nucleon coupling strengths based on the successful effective nucleon-nucleon interaction PK1, hypernuclear magnetic moment and $\barΛ$-hypernuclei, have been presented. The effect of tensor coupling in $Λ$-hypernuclei and the impurity effect of $\barΛ$ to nuclear structure have been discussed in detail.

# Lambda and Anti-Lambda Hypernuclei in Relativistic Mean-field Theory

## Key claim
The paper presents a new effective hyperon-nucleon interaction PK1-Y1, derived from the successful nucleon-nucleon interaction PK1, which reproduces single-Lambda binding energies across light to heavy hypernuclei with a root-mean-squared deviation of 0.851 MeV, but the tensor coupling and core polarization effects are found to be important only for magnetic moments, not for binding energies or spin-orbit splittings.

## Method
The authors extend the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian to include Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons with scalar sigma, vector omega, and tensor omega-Lambda couplings. The Dirac equation for hyperons and Klein-Gordon equations for mesons are solved self-consistently in a spherical box. The new PK1-Y1 parameter set is obtained by fitting single-Lambda binding energies of 10 hypernuclei from Carbon to Lead and spin-orbit splittings in Be-9 and C-13, with the tensor coupling ratio fixed to R_omegaLambdaLambda = 1.0.

## Key numbers
- PK1-Y1 coupling ratios: R_sigma = g_sigmaLambda/g_sigmaN = 0.580, R_omega = g_omegaLambda/g_omegaN = 0.620
- Root-mean-squared deviation for single-Lambda binding energies: Delta_b = 0.851 MeV (PK1-Y1), compared to 1.229 MeV (TM1-B), 1.200 MeV (NLSH-A), 0.906 MeV (NLSH-B)
- Root-mean-squared deviation for Lambda spin-orbit splitting of p state: Delta_p = 0.058 MeV (PK1-Y1), compared to 0.103 MeV (TM1-B), 0.100 MeV (NLSH-A), 0.257 MeV (NLSH-B)
- Lambda mass fixed to experimental value: M_Lambda = 1115.6 MeV
- Magnetic moment of O-17_Lambda (Y1): total mu_tot = -0.614 nucleon magneton, Schmidt value mu_Sch = -0.613 n.m.
- Spin-orbit splittings for anti-Lambda in O-16: range 0.1 to 0.8 MeV, smaller than anti-neutron splittings (0.2 to 1.9 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the RMF approach for ordinary nuclei, particularly the PK1 interaction developed by Meng et al. (2006), to hypernuclear systems. It builds on prior hypernuclear studies using non-relativistic models (Dalitz 1978) and other RMF parameter sets such as TM1-B and NLSH-A/B. The new PK1-Y1 interaction enables improved description of single-Lambda binding energies across the mass table, and the investigation of anti-Lambda spin symmetry extends earlier work on anti-nucleon spectra by the same group (PRL 91, 262501). The results open the way for self-consistent studies of anti-Lambda hypernuclei and further exploration of hyperon effects in neutron star matter.
