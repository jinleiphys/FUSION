# Proton and pion transverse spectra at RHIC from radial flow and finite size effects

> We show that the proton and pion transverse momentum distributions measured at RHIC for all collision centralities for pions and most of the collision centralities for protons, can be simultaneously described in terms of a thermal model with common values for the radial flow and temperature, when accounting for the finite size of the interaction region at the time of decoupling. We show that this description is obtained in terms of a simple scaling law of the size of the interaction region with the number of participants in the collision. The behavior of the proton to pion ratio at mid-rapidity can also be understood as a consequence of the strength of the radial flow and system size reached at RHIC energies.

# Proton and pion transverse spectra at RHIC from radial flow and finite size effects

## Key claim
The proton and pion transverse momentum distributions at RHIC can be simultaneously described by a thermal model with common temperature and radial flow values for all pion centralities and most proton centralities, *provided* that finite size effects of the interaction region at decoupling are included via a simple scaling law of the fireball radius with the cube root of the number of participants.

## Method
The authors model the fireball as a spherical confining volume of radius R at freeze-out, solving the Klein-Gordon equation for pions (bosons) and the Dirac equation for protons (fermions) with Dirichlet boundary conditions at the sphere surface. The invariant momentum distributions are computed by summing over discrete stationary states weighted by a Maxwell-Boltzmann thermal factor with a radial flow profile, approximating the Lorentz gamma factor by its average value over the fireball. The model is fit to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV across multiple centrality bins, with the fireball radius scaled as R = R_0 + C (N_part/2)^(1/3) using R_0=1 fm and C=1.28.

## Key numbers
- Temperature: T = 117 MeV (common for both pions and protons in the most central collisions)
- Pion surface radial flow velocity: beta_pi = 0.6
- Proton surface radial flow velocity: beta_p = 0.53 (approximately 10% smaller than pions)
- Fireball radius for most central collisions (0-10% centrality): R = 8 fm
- Fireball radius for most peripheral collisions (80-92% centrality): R = 2.9 fm
- Number of participants for 0-10% centrality: N_part = 325.2
- Number of participants for 80-92% centrality: N_part = 6.3
- Scaling law constants: R_0 = 1 fm, C = 1.28
- Pion strong interaction range: d_s ~ 1.4 fm
- Pion average separation at freeze-out: d ~ 0.6 fm (less than d_s, justifying liquid-like description)
- Two-pion correlation analysis performed at average momentum K = 260 MeV
- Displacement constant for correlation function comparison: tilde_R = 0.8 fm

## Context
This work extends the finite-size thermal model previously applied to charged and neutral pions at RHIC (Ayala et al., 2005) to also include protons, addressing the unexpected proton-to-pion ratio behavior observed by PHENIX (PHENIX Collaboration, 2003). It contrasts with standard thermal model fits (PHENIX Collaboration, 2003) that yield T_0 ~ 180 MeV (near hadronization temperature) and require varying temperature and flow with centrality, as seen in Hung and Shuryak (1998). The authors show that finite size effects can replace the need for centrality-dependent freeze-out parameters, and they compare their extracted effective radii to HBT side-radii measurements from PHENIX (PHENIX Collaboration, 2005). The work enables a unified description of hadron spectra across centralities using common thermodynamic parameters, with the key open question being the failure of the proton description for centralities below 40-50% and for p_t > 3 GeV, which the authors attribute to non-thermal fragmentation mechanisms.
