# Finite Energy Sum Rules in Potential Scattering

> We study scattering theory identities previously obtained as consistency conditions in the context of one-loop quantum field theory calculations. We prove the identities using Jost function techniques and study applications.

## Key claim

The paper proves that a set of finite energy sum rules, previously discovered as consistency conditions in one-loop quantum field theory calculations of Casimir energies, are general identities in potential scattering theory that hold for a wide class of nonsingular potentials, but fail for singular potentials such as the Dirac delta function even though Levinson's theorem continues to hold.

## Method

The authors derive the sum rules using Jost function techniques, constructing an auxiliary function whose analytic properties in the upper half k-plane allow contour integration to relate integrals over phase shifts to sums over bound state energies. The Born approximation is systematically subtracted from the phase shift to render the integrals convergent, and the construction is carried out explicitly for the antisymmetric channel in one dimension (which generalizes to s-waves in three dimensions), with extensions to higher partial waves, the symmetric channel, and fermion scattering discussed separately.

## Key numbers

- The sum rule for the antisymmetric channel in one dimension is: ∫₀^∞ (dk/π) k^(2n) d/dk [δ₋(k) - Σ_(ν=1)^m δ₋^(ν)(k)] = - Σ_j (-κ_(₋,j)²)^n, for m ≥ n
- For the symmetric channel with m = 2n, the anomalous term is I_(n,2n)^anom = (-1)^n/2 [∫₀^∞ dy V(y)]^(2n)
- For the delta function potential V(x) = -λ δ_D(x), the n=m=1 sum rule gives I_(1,1) = λ²/8 instead of the expected λ²/4
- The WKB approximation yields Σ_j κ_j^(2n) ≈ (2^(n+1)/π) (n!/(2n+1)!!) ∫₀^∞ dy [U(y)]^(n+1/2) where U(y) = -V(y)
- For n=1 in the WKB approximation: Σ_j κ_j² ≈ (4/(3π)) ∫₀^∞ dy [U(y)]^(3/2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous discovery of these sum rules as consistency conditions in quantum field theory calculations of Casimir energies (Graham, Jaffe, Quandt, Weigel 2001). The results are related to earlier work by Buslaev and Faddeev based on the Gel'fand-Dikii equation, though those authors subtracted local asymptotic expansions of the phase shift rather than Born approximations, making oversubtracted versions impossible. The sum rules provide a natural generalization of Levinson's theorem and enable new connections between scattering data and bound state properties, with potential applications to Casimir energy calculations in quantum field theory.
