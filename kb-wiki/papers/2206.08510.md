# Quantum computation of nuclear observables involving linear combination of unitary operators

> We present the quantum computation of nuclear observables where the operators of interest are first decomposed in terms of the linear combination of unitaries. Then we utilise the Hadamard test and the linear combination of unitaries (LCU) based methods to compute the expectation values. We apply these methods to calculate the electric quadrupole moment of deuteron. The results are compared for the Jordan-Wigner transformation and Gray code encoding. We discuss the versatility of our approach that can be utilized in general to calculate several observables on a quantum computer.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that expectation values of non-unitary nuclear observables can be computed on a quantum computer by decomposing them into linear combinations of unitary operators, but the accuracy is limited by the number of gates and qubits available, with the Hadamard test method proving more reliable than the LCU-based method due to smaller circuit depth.

## Method

The authors first decompose the non-unitary operator of interest (the electric quadrupole moment operator) into a linear combination of unitary operators expressed in second quantization form using Pauli spin matrices. They then implement two quantum algorithms: (1) a Hadamard test that computes the expectation value for each unitary component separately, and (2) an LCU-based method combined with a SWAP or destructive SWAP test to compute the overlap between the original wave function and the operator-applied state. The ground state wave function of the deuteron is obtained via the variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) using the REID68 potential, and the quadrupole moment is calculated for basis sizes 2 and 4 using both Gray code encoding and Jordan-Wigner transformation.

## Key numbers

- Experimental electric quadrupole moment of deuteron: 0.282(1) fm²
- Full classical calculation with REID68 potential yields binding energy -2.2247 MeV and quadrupole moment 0.2762 fm²
- For basis size 2 (n=0; l=0,2), classical calculation gives energy 67.9476 MeV and quadrupole moment -0.1846 fm²
- For basis size 2 with Gray code encoding, the quadrupole moment operator is Q₂ = -0.18144I + 0.18144Z₀ + 0.28394X₀
- For basis size 2 with Jordan-Wigner transformation, Q₂ = -0.18144I + 0.18144Z₁ + 0.14197(X₀X₁ + Y₀Y₁)
- For basis size 2 without VQE: H-test gives -0.1846(5) fm², LCU gives -0.1785(16) fm²
- For basis size 2 with VQE: H-test gives -0.1848(24) fm², LCU gives -0.1805(37) fm²
- For basis size 4 without VQE: H-test gives 0.1838(10) fm², LCU gives 0.1835(21) fm²
- For basis size 4 with VQE: H-test gives 0.1784(173) fm², LCU gives 0.1781(162) fm²

## Context

This work extends prior quantum simulations of the deuteron by Dumitrescu (2018) and Siwach (2021), which successfully deployed quantum simulations but suffered from accuracy limitations due to increasing circuit depth with larger basis sizes. The authors use the REID68 potential, which unlike the EFT-based potentials used in studies by Dumitrescu (2018), Lu (2019), and DiMatteo (2021), can account for the admixture of orbital angular momentum l=0 and l=2 states necessary for calculating the quadrupole moment. The work enables future quantum computation of other nuclear observables beyond energies, such as those needed for studying resonances involving non-unitary operators.
