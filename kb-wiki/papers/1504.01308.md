# Quark deconfinement and gluon condensate in a weak magnetic field

> We study QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) for the axial-vector current correlator in the presence of a magnetic field, in the weak field limit and at zero temperature. We find that the perturbative QCD as well as the hadronic contribution to the sum rules get explicit magnetic field-dependent corrections and that these in turn induce a magnetic field dependence on the deconfinement phenomenological parameter s_0 and on the gluon condensate. The leading corrections turn out to be quadratic in the field strength. We find from the dimension d=2 first FESR that the magnetic field dependence of s_0 is proportional to the absolute value of the light-quark condensate. Hence, it increases with increasing field strength. This implies that the parameters describing chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement behave similarly as functions of the magnetic filed. Thus, at zero temperature the magnetic field is a catalysing agent of both chiral symmetry breaking and confinement. From the dimension d=4 second FESR we obtain the behavior of the gluon condensate in the presence of the external magnetic field. This condensate also increases with increasing field strength.

## Key claim

Using QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) for the axial-vector current correlator in the weak magnetic field limit at zero temperature, the authors find that the deconfinement parameter \(s_0\) and the gluon condensate both increase with increasing field strength, implying that a weak magnetic field acts as a catalyst for both chiral symmetry breaking and confinement at zero temperature. This result is conditional on the weak field limit \(eB < s_0\) and the additional restriction \(s_0 < 3eB\) for the hadronic spectral function to remain valid.

## Method

The authors set up QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) for the axial-vector current correlator in the presence of a constant external magnetic field, working in the weak field limit (\(eB < s_0\)) and the chiral limit at zero temperature. They compute the perturbative QCD contribution to the correlator to second order in the magnetic field using the weak field expansion of the quark propagator, and the hadronic contribution using the charged pion propagator in a magnetic field expressed as a sum over Landau levels. The first two FESR (dimension \(d=2\) and \(d=4\)) are then solved to extract the magnetic field dependence of the deconfinement threshold \(s_0\) and the gluon condensate, using lattice QCD data for the light-quark condensate as a function of the field.

## Key numbers

* The leading magnetic corrections to the perturbative QCD contribution are quadratic in the field strength, with the coefficient \(C_0^{(2)} = -(17/18)(1/4\pi^2)\).
* The light-quark condensate is parametrized as \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle_{(eB)}/\langle \bar{q}q\rangle = 1 + a (eB) + b (eB)^2\) with \(a = 0.85\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) and \(b = 0.34\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\), where \(eB\) is in units of \(\text{GeV}^2\).
* The deconfinement parameter \(s_0\) is proportional to the absolute value of the light-quark condensate: \(s_0 = -8\pi \mathcal{B} \langle \bar{q}q\rangle_{(eB)}\).
* The gluon condensate \(C_4\langle O_4\rangle\) is given by \(C_4\langle O_4\rangle = -2(eB) \mathcal{B}\langle \bar{q}q\rangle_{(eB)} + 8\pi (\mathcal{B}\langle \bar{q}q\rangle_{(eB)})^2 + (17/18)(eB)^2/(4\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD finite energy sum rule approach, previously applied at zero temperature (QCDSR review) and at finite temperature (FESRT_1), to include the effects of a weak external magnetic field. It builds on the relation between the deconfinement parameter \(s_0\) and the quark condensate found at finite temperature by CAD1 (2001) and at finite temperature and density by abdglr (2005). The results provide a zero-temperature baseline for future finite-temperature studies of inverse magnetic catalysis, a phenomenon observed in lattice QCD where the critical temperature for deconfinement/chiral symmetry restoration decreases with increasing field strength (Fodor 2013; Bali 2012).
