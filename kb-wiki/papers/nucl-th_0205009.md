# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

> The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model is extended to include density dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The effective Lagrangian is characterized by a phenomenological density dependence for the $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ meson-nucleon vertex functions, adjusted to properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei. Pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction. The new density-dependent effective interaction DD-ME1 is tested in the analysis of the equations of state for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, and of ground-state properties of the Sn and Pb isotopic chains. Results of self-consistent RHB calculations are compared with experimental data, and with results previously obtained in the RHB model with non-linear self-interactions, as well as in the density dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) model. Parity-violating elastic electron scattering on Pb and Sn nuclei is calculated using a relativistic optical model with inclusion of Coulomb distortion effects, and the resulting asymmetry parameters are related to the neutron ground-state density distributions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model by introducing a phenomenological density dependence for the σ, ω, and ρ meson-nucleon couplings, with parameters adjusted simultaneously to nuclear matter properties and ground-state properties of finite spherical nuclei, rather than being derived directly from Dirac-Brueckner self-energies.

## Method

The authors adopt the functional forms for density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings from Typel and Wolter (1999), but adjust the parameters to nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy) and to binding energies, charge radii, and neutron-proton radius differences of spherical Sn and Pb isotopes. Pairing correlations are treated using the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction within the RHB framework, and the equations are solved by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a spherical oscillator potential.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³ (with 10% error bar used in fitting)
- Nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon: E/A = -16 MeV (with 5% error bar)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K₀ = 250 MeV (with 10% error bar)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 33 MeV (with 10% error bar)
- σ-meson mass: m_σ = 550 MeV (fixed in TW-99; free parameter in DD-ME1, resulting value very close to 550 MeV)
- ω-meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV
- ρ-meson mass: m_ρ = 763 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness in ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.20 ± 0.04 fm from proton scattering data, and 0.19 ± 0.09 fm from alpha scattering excitation of the isovector giant dipole resonance
- Empirical symmetry energy at saturation: a₄ = 30 ± 4 MeV
- Constraints on symmetry energy parameters from neutron skin: a₄ ≈ 30–34 MeV, 2 MeV/fm³ ≤ p₀ ≤ 4 MeV/fm³, and -200 MeV ≤ ΔK₀ ≤ -50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) approach of Typel and Wolter (1999) by incorporating the full RHB framework with finite-range Gogny pairing, rather than BCS approximation. It addresses limitations of standard non-linear RMF interactions such as NL3 and NL1, which overestimate neutron radii and produce a neutron matter equation of state inconsistent with the microscopic many-body results of Friedman and Pandharipande (1981). The new DD-ME1 interaction enables more reliable predictions for exotic nuclei far from stability, neutron skins, and parity-violating electron scattering observables that probe neutron density distributions.
