# Random Phase Approximation and neutrino-nucleus cross sections

> The Random Phase Approximation theory is used to calculate the total cross sections of electron neutrinos on $^{12}$C nucleus. The role of the excitation of the discrete spectrum is discussed. A comparison with electron scattering and muon capture data is presented. The cross section of electron neutrinos coming from muon decay at rest is calculated.

## Key claim

The Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections on \(^{12}\)C show that the contribution of discrete excited states is not negligible even at neutrino energies of a few hundred MeV, but the RPA has large theoretical uncertainties in describing low-lying unnatural parity states, requiring quenching factors to bring results into agreement with data.

## Method

The authors solved the one-body Schrödinger equation with a spherical Woods-Saxon potential to obtain a single-particle basis including the continuum, then used continuum RPA to calculate total cross sections for electron neutrinos on \(^{12}\)C. They tested three different particle-hole interactions (LM1, LM2, and PP) and a rescaled interaction (NI05), and compared results to electron scattering form factors, muon capture rates, and the measured \(^{12}\)C(\(\nu_e, e^-\))\(^{12}\)B cross section for neutrinos from muon decay at rest.

## Key numbers

- Energies of the isospin triplet 1\(^+\) excited states in \(^{12}\)C, \(^{12}\)N, and \(^{12}\)B: for \(^{12}\)C, RPA with LM1 gives 17.2 MeV, LM2 gives 18.8 MeV, PP gives 16.7 MeV, NI05 gives 15.1 MeV, experiment is 15.1 MeV.
- Muon capture rates on \(^{12}\)C: total experimental rate is 37.09 \(\times\) 10\(^3\) s\(^{-1}\); bare RPA total rates range from 68.25 to 72.22 \(\times\) 10\(^3\) s\(^{-1}\); quenched RPA total rates range from 49.79 to 58.67 \(\times\) 10\(^3\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Total cross section for \(^{12}\)C(\(\nu_e, e^-\))\(^{12}\)B from muon decay at rest: bare RPA gives 36.0, 42.0, and 46.0 \(\times\) 10\(^{-16}\) fm\(^2\); quenched RPA gives 7.0, 19.2, and 21.3 \(\times\) 10\(^{-16}\) fm\(^2\); experimental value is 14.0 \(\pm\) 1.2 \(\times\) 10\(^{-16}\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous continuum RPA studies of neutrino-induced excitations on \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O (Bot05a, Bot05b, Ble01, Co02, Co06) by focusing on the discrete low-lying excitation region. The results show that RPA fails to describe unnatural parity states like the 1\(^+\) state, consistent with earlier findings that the restriction to 1p-1h configurations is the source of the deficiency (Kre80, Dro90, Kam04). The paper enables future work using theories beyond RPA, such as including 2p-2h degrees of freedom, and suggests that deformation effects may be important for \(^{12}\)C (Krm05).
