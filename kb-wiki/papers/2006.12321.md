# $θ$-dependence of light nuclei and nucleosynthesis

> We investigate the impact of the QCD vacuum at nonzero $θ$ on the properties of light nuclei, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, and stellar nucleosynthesis. Our analysis starts with a calculation of the $θ$-dependence of the neutron-proton mass difference and neutron decay using chiral perturbation theory. We then discuss the $θ$-dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction using a one-boson-exchange model and compute the properties of the two-nucleon system. Using the universal properties of four-component fermions at large scattering length, we then deduce the binding energies of the three-nucleon and four-nucleon systems. Based on these results, we discuss the implications for primordial abundances of light nuclei, the production of nuclei in stellar environments, and implications for an anthropic view of the universe.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the QCD vacuum angle θ, which is experimentally constrained to be less than 10⁻¹⁰ by the neutron electric dipole moment, could be significantly larger without preventing the existence of life; the authors find that while θ values up to about 10⁻⁵ produce negligible changes to nuclear physics, larger values substantially alter light element abundances and stellar nucleosynthesis, but the anthropic constraints on θ are weak compared to those from the nEDM.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory to compute the θ-dependence of the pion mass, nucleon mass, and neutron-proton mass difference. They then construct a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction including π, σ, ρ, and ω exchange, solving the Schrödinger equation to find binding energies of the deuteron, dineutron, and diproton as functions of θ. For three- and four-nucleon systems, they exploit Wigner's SU(4) symmetry and universal properties of four-component fermions at large scattering length to deduce binding energies of ³H, ³He, and ⁴He.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton mass difference Δm_N(θ) = 1.51 MeV at θ = 1 and 2.47 MeV at θ = 2, compared to the real-world value of 1.29 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy B_d(θ) reaches a maximum of approximately 42.5 MeV at θ ≈ 3.0 (parameter set I, isospin symmetric case) and 22.8 MeV at θ ≈ 2.9 (parameter set II)
- The dineutron becomes bound for θ ≳ 0.18–0.24, with binding energies at θ = 1–2 similar to lattice QCD results of 7–13 MeV
- The diproton becomes bound for θ ≳ 0.6–0.8, with a constant Coulomb shift of −0.67 MeV (set I) and −0.72 MeV (set II) relative to the dineutron
- The neutron lifetime drops rapidly as θ deviates from zero, driven by the Δm_N(θ) dependence scaling roughly as the fifth power of the mass difference

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ubaldi (2008) who focused on CP-odd vertices and found weak constraints on θ, and by Banks, Dine, and collaborators who argued no anthropic bound on θ exists. The authors instead consider CP-even effects of θ on nuclear binding and nucleosynthesis, complementing prior work on quark mass variations by Flambaum (2002), Beane (2002), Epelbaum (2002), and others. The results enable future studies of simultaneous variation of θ with other fundamental parameters such as quark masses, and provide a framework for assessing anthropic constraints on the QCD vacuum angle independent of the neutron electric dipole moment.
