# Nucleon and pion electromagnetic form factors in a light-front constituent quark model

> Nucleon and pion electromagnetic form factors are evaluated in the spacelike region within a light-front constituent quark model, where eigenfunctions of a mass operator, reproducing a large set of hadron energy levels, are adopted and quark form factors are considered in the one-body current. The hadron form factors are sharply affected by the high momentum tail generated in the wave function by the one-gluon-exchange interaction. Useful information on the electromagnetic structure of light constituent quarks can be obtained from the comparison with nucleon and pion experimental data.

## Key claim

Nucleon and pion electromagnetic form factors can be described in a light-front constituent quark model using eigenfunctions of a mass operator that reproduces hadron energy levels, but only if the constituent quarks themselves are assigned a non-trivial electromagnetic structure via Dirac and Pauli form factors; without such quark form factors, the configuration mixing from the one-gluon-exchange interaction leads to a sharp disagreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved a light-front mass operator for the three-quark nucleon system using the Capstick-Isgur effective quark-quark interaction (including one-gluon-exchange and linear confinement), expanding the wave functions onto a harmonic oscillator basis up to 20 quanta via the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle. They then evaluated the nucleon and pion electromagnetic form factors in the spacelike region using the plus component of the one-body electromagnetic current in a frame where q⁺=0, incorporating Dirac and Pauli form factors for the constituent quarks. The parameters of the quark form factors were determined through a minimization procedure against experimental nucleon and pion form factor data up to Q² ≈ 30 (GeV/c)².

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0.220 GeV
- Calculated nucleon mass: M_N = 0.940 GeV
- Wave function composition: p_S = 98.1%, p_S' = 1.7%, p_D = 0.2%
- Nucleon magnetic moments with pointlike quarks and full CI interaction: μ_p^(CI) = 2.28, μ_n^(CI) = -1.19 (experimental: μ_p^(exp) = 2.793, μ_n^(exp) = -1.913)
- Nucleon magnetic moments with confining potential only: μ_p^(conf) = 2.74, μ_n^(conf) = -1.60
- Anomalous magnetic moments from fit: κ_u = 0.085, κ_d = -0.153
- Proton charge form factor slope at Q²=0: dG_E^p(th)/dQ² = -2.83 ± 0.20 (c/GeV)² [experimental: -3.0 ± 0.09 (c/GeV)²]
- Neutron charge form factor slope at Q²=0: dG_E^n(th)/dQ² = 0.33 ± 0.03 (c/GeV)² [experimental: 0.50 ± 0.015 (c/GeV)²]
- Pion charge radius: r_ch^π(th) = 0.71 fm [experimental: 0.660 ± 0.024 fm]
- Quark radii: ⟨r_1^u⟩ = 0.51 fm, ⟨r_1^d⟩ = 0.42 fm

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model approach of Chung, Coester, and Keister (1991) and Capstick and Keister (1995), who used Gaussian wave functions with pointlike quarks, by adopting eigenfunctions of the Capstick-Isgur mass operator that includes one-gluon-exchange configuration mixing. The results show that the high-momentum tail from the one-gluon-exchange interaction sharply affects form factors even at low Q², requiring constituent quarks with internal structure. This enables parameter-free predictions for other hadron form factors in the u-d sector, such as the N-Δ transition magnetic form factor, and provides a phenomenological constraint on the electromagnetic structure of light constituent quarks.
