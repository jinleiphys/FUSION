# A Study of Final State Effects in the electrodisintegration of a polarized Helium-3 target

> An approach for the description of the final state interaction in the evaluation of inclusive electromagnetic responses of a polarized Helium-3 target, is briefly illustrated. Preliminary results of calculations, where the final state interaction is fully taken into account for the two-body break-up channel, are compared with experimental data, showing a very encouraging improvement with respect to the plane wave impulse approximation results.

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary calculations of inclusive electromagnetic responses for a polarized Helium-3 target that include final state interaction (FSI) fully in the two-body break-up channel, showing improvement over plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) results, but note that FSI is not yet included in the three-body break-up channel and Coulomb effects and three-body forces are absent.

## Method

The authors evaluate matrix elements of the electromagnetic current for Helium-3 using three-nucleon scattering states obtained from a variational approach (complex Kohn variational principle) with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential. The final state interaction is included exactly in the two-body break-up channel (where the three-nucleon state behaves asymptotically like a proton-deuteron system) by decomposing the fully-interacting wave function into asymptotic and core components, while the three-body break-up channel is treated in PWIA. The calculations use relativistic kinematics and a relativistic electron-nucleon cross section, with the current operator approximated as a sum of one-body operators without two-body contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body break-up channel, FSI is included up to total angular momentum j' ≤ 5/2 for center-of-mass energy E_CM ≤ 10 MeV, up to j' ≤ 7/2 for 10 MeV < E_CM ≤ 30 MeV, and up to j' ≤ 11/2 for 30 MeV < E_CM < 100 MeV
- For all other partial waves, PWIA is used up to j' ≤ 17/2 for Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)² and up to j' ≤ 19/2 for Q² = 0.2 (GeV/c)²
- Unpolarized response functions R_L and R_T are calculated at momentum transfer |q| ~ 175 MeV
- The asymmetry A is presented for Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)² and Q² = 0.2 (GeV/c)² at low energy transfer and in the quasi-elastic peak region

## Context

This work extends previous PWIA calculations by the same group (CPS92, CPS95, KPSV, Sauer) that used realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and relativistic electron-nucleon cross sections but disregarded FSI between the knocked-out nucleon and the spectator pair. The results are compared with experimental data from TJLAB (Xu, Xu2) and with non-relativistic Faddeev calculations including FSI and meson exchange currents by the Bochum group (Glock1, Glock2). The approach enables future inclusion of FSI in three-body break-up channels, Coulomb effects, three-body forces, and extension to pion electroproduction from polarized Helium-3 targets.
