# Challenges of Lattice Calculation of Scalar Mesons

> I review a proposed pattern of the light scalar mesons with q\bar{q} mesons and glueball above 1 GeV and tetraquark mesoniums below 1 GeV. Several challenges and caveats of calculating these light scalar mesons with dynamical fermions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a pattern for light scalar mesons based on quenched lattice calculations and phenomenology, but stresses that this pattern is "not finalized" and must be scrutinized in future experiments and dynamical fermion calculations.

## Method

The author reviews quenched lattice QCD calculations using overlap fermions on \(12^3 \times 28\) and \(16^3 \times 28\) lattices with pion masses as low as 180 MeV, combined with a mixing scheme for isoscalar mesons fit to pseudoscalar pair decays and \(J/\Psi\) decays. The challenges of extending these calculations to full QCD with dynamical fermions are then enumerated, including the need to disentangle physical resonances from two-meson scattering states and to handle disconnected insertions for isoscalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- The quenched lattice calculation found \(a_0\) to be fairly independent of quark mass below the strange quark mass, approaching \(a_0(1450)\) in the chiral limit.
- The ratio \(\Gamma(f_0(1500)\rightarrow K\overline{K})/\Gamma(f_0(1500)\rightarrow \pi\pi) = 0.246\pm0.026\) is close to the octet prediction of 0.21, not the glueball prediction of 0.84 or the \(s\bar{s}\) prediction larger than unity.
- The prediction \(\Gamma(J/\psi\to \omega f_0(1710))/\Gamma(J/\psi\to \phi f_0(1710))=4.1\) is consistent with the observed value of \(6.6\pm 2.7\).
- A quenched lattice calculation with a tetraquark interpolation field found a low-lying scalar tetraquark mesonium at approximately 550 MeV, identified with \(\sigma(600)\).
- The quenched scalar glueball mass is 1710(50)(80) MeV.
- One \(N_f=2\) dynamical fermion calculation, after removing the partially quenched ghost \(\pi \eta_2\) state, found \(a_0\) at 1.51(19) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the quenched lattice calculation of \(a_0\) and \(K_0^*\) by Mathur et al. (2006) and the mixing study of \(f_0(1370), f_0(1500), f_0(1710)\) by Close et al. (2006). It contrasts with several \(N_f=2\) dynamical fermion calculations (e.g., Prelovsek 2008, Mathur et al. 2006, Frigori et al. 2007, Hidetoshi et al. 2008) that found the lowest \(a_0\) state near 1 GeV, which the author argues is likely a \(\pi\eta_2\) scattering state rather than the physical \(a_0(980)\). The paper enables future full QCD calculations that must include multi-hadron states in correlator fits and use variational methods or volume dependence to distinguish resonances from scattering states.
