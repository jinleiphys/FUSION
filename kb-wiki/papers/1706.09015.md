# Feynman-Hellmann theorem for resonances and the quest for QCD exotica

> The generalization of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem for resonance states in quantum field theory is derived. On the basis of this theorem, a criterion is proposed to study the possible exotic nature of certain hadronic states emerging in QCD. It is shown that this proposal is supported by explicit calculations in Chiral Perturbation Theory and by large-$N_c$ arguments. Analyzing recent lattice data on the quark mass dependence in the pseudoscalar, vector meson, baryon octet and baryon decuplet sectors, we conclude that, as expected, these are predominately quark-model states, albeit the corrections are non-negligible.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalization of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem for resonance states in quantum field theory and proposes a criterion based on the quark mass dependence of hadron masses to distinguish ordinary quark-model states from exotic states; the authors note that the criterion is validated by Chiral Perturbation Theory and large-\(N_c\) arguments, but the analysis of lattice data for the pseudoscalar, vector meson, baryon octet and baryon decuplet sectors shows that these states are predominately quark-model states, albeit with non-negligible corrections.

## Method

The authors extend the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to unstable resonance states in quantum field theory, then define scale-invariant ratios \(\gamma, \gamma', \beta, \beta'\) from the derivatives of hadron masses squared with respect to quark masses for each flavor multiplet. They compute the quark-model predictions for these ratios and compare them to values extracted from lattice QCD data from the QCDSF collaboration, using linear fits to the quark mass dependence along the SU(3)-symmetric line and away from it. The method is validated by explicit calculations in Chiral Perturbation Theory at one loop and by large-\(N_c\) arguments.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar octet: at the physical point, using LECs from Gasser (1984), \(\gamma_P = -\frac{1}{2}(1+9\times10^{-4})\) and \(\gamma'_P = 1+4\times10^{-2}\); at the SU(3)-symmetric point (\(M_\pi = M_K = M_\eta = 413\) MeV), \(\gamma_P = -\frac{1}{2}(1-5\times10^{-3})\) and \(\gamma'_P = 1-2\times10^{-2}\).
- Pseudoscalar octet: using lattice-fit LECs from Bijnens (2014) at the SU(3)-symmetric point, \(\gamma_P = -\frac{1}{2}(1-3\times10^{-2})\) and \(\gamma'_P = 1-0.12\).
- Baryon octet: at \(O(p^2)\) in ChPT, the LECs (in GeV\(^{-1}\)) are \(b_0 = -0.273(6)\), \(b_D = 0.0506(17)\), \(b_F = -0.179(1)\) at NLO, and \(b_0 = -0.886(5)\), \(b_D = 0.0482(17)\), \(b_F = -0.517(7)\) at NNLO from Ren (2015).
- Lattice data on the symmetric line (\(32^3\times64\) lattice): for \(\kappa_0 = 0.12090\), \(a M_\pi = 0.1747(5)\), \(a M_\rho = 0.3341(34)\), \(a M_N = 0.4673(27)\), \(a M_\Delta = 0.5675(64)\); for \(\kappa_0 = 0.12095\), \(a M_\pi = 0.1508(4)\), \(a M_\rho = 0.3209(27)\), \(a M_N = 0.4329(49)\), \(a M_\Delta = 0.5541(80)\).
- Linear fits on the symmetric line: \(dM_V/d\bar m = 7.66 \pm 2.47\), \(dM_B/d\bar m = 19.33 \pm 2.3\), \(dM_D/d\bar m = 12.28 \pm 4.13\) (lattice units).
- Critical hopping parameter: \(\kappa_{0,c} = 0.12098 \pm 0.00283\) from extrapolating \(M_\pi^2\) to zero.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Bernard (2010) for using sigma-terms to distinguish exotic hadrons, generalizing it to resonance states via a new Feynman-Hellmann theorem for unstable particles. The method provides an observable-based alternative to overlap calculations on the lattice, which suffer from short-distance ambiguities. The analysis of QCDSF lattice data (Bietenholz 2011) confirms that the low-lying meson and baryon multiplets are predominantly quark-model states, and the framework enables future studies of genuinely exotic candidates such as tetraquarks and pentaquarks, where the predicted ratios (e.g., \(\gamma_T = -1/2\) for tetraquarks) differ in sign from ordinary mesons.
