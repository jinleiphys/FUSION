# Off shell pi N amplitude and the p p->p p pi0 reaction

> We have used a conventional model for the $pp \rightarrow pp π^{0}$ reaction consisting of the Born term plus the $s$-wave rescattering term. As a novelty we have introduced the off shell dependence of the $πN$ $s$-wave isoscalar amplitude. This amplitude is appreciably enhanced when one moves to the off shell situations met in the problem and, as a consequence, the $pp \rightarrow pp π^{0}$ cross section becomes considerably larger than with the use of the $πN$ on shell amplitudes. Two different models for the off shell extrapolation found in the literature have been used and the cross sections obtained are large enough to account for the experimental data, although uncertainties remain due to the incomplete knowledge of the off shell extrapolation.

## Key claim

The off-shell dependence of the πN s-wave isoscalar amplitude, which is appreciably enhanced compared to the on-shell amplitude, can by itself explain the unexpectedly large experimental cross section for pp → pp π⁰ near threshold, although uncertainties remain due to incomplete knowledge of the off-shell extrapolation.

## Method

The authors used a conventional model for the pp → pp π⁰ reaction consisting of the Born term plus the s-wave rescattering term, incorporating the off-shell dependence of the πN s-wave isoscalar amplitude. They implemented two different models for the off-shell extrapolation found in the literature: the Hamilton model (based on σ-exchange plus a short-range piece) and a current algebra model satisfying PCAC constraints. The cross sections were computed in coordinate space using radial wave functions from the Paris and Bonn NN potentials, with Coulomb corrections applied.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell πN isoscalar amplitude parameter λ₁ = 0.0075, compared to λ₂ = 0.053 (from Höhler's results for on-shell πN scattering at threshold).
- At the half-off-shell kinematics relevant to the problem (q⁰ = 70 MeV, q = 370 MeV/c), the isoscalar amplitude is about five times bigger than the on-shell amplitude.
- In the Hamilton model: a_σ = 0.220 m_π⁻¹, a_sr = -0.233 m_π⁻¹, m_σ = 550 MeV.
- In the current algebra model: σ(0) = 25 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, f₃⁺ = 0.82 m_π⁻³, m₁ = 8.24 m_π, m₂ = 7.5 m_π, and F̄⁺(0, m_π²; m_π², m_π²) = -0.30 m_π⁻¹.
- Coulomb corrections decrease the cross section by 30% at η = 0.2 to 10% at η = 0.5 (η = p_πmax/m_π).
- The Born term is about 50% bigger with the Bonn NN potential compared to the Paris potential.
- Changing the off-shell amplitude at momenta above q = 500 MeV/c changes the cross section only at the level of 2%.

## Context

This work extends the conventional model of Lee and Riska (1993) and others for pp → pp π⁰, which using on-shell πN amplitudes gave cross sections far below experimental data. It provides an alternative to the relativistic NN potential approach of Horowitz and collaborators (1993), which explained the data via pair terms from N̄N components but suffered from large ambiguities. The results show that the off-shell πN isoscalar amplitude can account for the experimental data, and the authors suggest that future work on NN, N̄N, and N̄N̄ models (e.g., by Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990) could help reduce the remaining uncertainties.
