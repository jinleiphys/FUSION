# Hydrodynamic analysis of non-central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV

> We analyze non-central heavy-ion collisions at the relativistic energy within a full (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamic model. First, the initial parameters in the hydrodynamic model are chosen so that we reproduce the experimental data of both the rapidity distribution and the slope of transverse mass distribution in central Pb + Pb collisions at 158 $A$ GeV at the CERN SPS. We next study the validity of the ansatz of wounded nucleon scaling for the initial condition of the hydrodynamic model through analysis of the rapidity distribution of negative pions in non-central collisions. Moreover, a simple Monte Carlo method is introduced to exactly calculate the particle distribution from resonance decays.

## Key claim

The authors test whether the wounded nucleon scaling ansatz for the initial transverse density profile, widely used in (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic models, remains valid in a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic description of non-central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV. They find that while the ansatz works reasonably well for reproducing negative pion rapidity distributions, it fails to describe the centrality dependence of baryon stopping.

## Method

The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations using the Piecewise Parabolic Method (PPM) with an equation of state featuring a strong first-order phase transition between a QGP phase (massless u,d,s quarks and gluons) and a hadron resonance gas phase (mesons and non-strange baryons up to 2 GeV mass, with excluded volume correction). Initial parameters are tuned to reproduce central collision data, then the impact parameter is shifted without changing other parameters to simulate non-central collisions. Particle spectra are computed via the Cooper-Frye formula at freeze-out, with resonance decay contributions calculated using a simple Monte Carlo method.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at origin: \(E_0 = 3.9\) GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Initial baryon density at origin: \(n_{B0} = 0.46\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Initial longitudinal cutoff parameter: \(\tilde{z}_0 = 1.38\) fm
- Longitudinal baryon density exponent: \(\alpha = 1.7\)
- Freeze-out energy density: \(E_{\mathrm{f}} = 60\) MeV/fm\(^3\)
- Average thermal freeze-out temperature: \(\langle T_{\mathrm{f}} \rangle \sim 117\) MeV
- Average chemical potential at freeze-out: \(\langle \mu_{\mathrm{f}} \rangle \sim 323\) MeV
- Bag constant: \(B^{1/4} = 233\) MeV, giving critical temperature 160 MeV at zero baryon density
- Latent heat at zero baryon density: \(\Delta E(n_B=0) = 1.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\)
- Initial time: \(t_0 \sim 1.44\) fm
- Impact parameter for central collisions: \(b = 2.4\) fm (corresponding to 5% most central events with \(0 < b < 3.4\) fm)
- NN inelastic cross section: \(\sigma_{\mathrm{in}} = 30\) mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 20\) GeV

## Context

This work extends prior (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic studies of non-central collisions by Ollitrault (1992), Kolb et al., and Teaney and Shuryak (nutcrack phenomenon) to a full (3+1)-dimensional framework, which is necessary for describing rapidity-dependent observables. The authors validate the wounded nucleon scaling ansatz for energy density initial conditions used in those earlier works, but find it insufficient for baryon density. This enables future studies of elliptic flow and the nutcrack phenomenon at SPS energies, with the authors noting that resonance decays reduce elliptic flow (Hirano, forthcoming). The freeze-out temperature obtained is consistent with analyses by the NA49 Collaboration.
