# Heavy ion collisions and AdS/CFT

> We review some recent applications of the AdS/CFT correspondence to heavy ion collisions including a calculation of the jet quenching parameter in N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory and quarkonium suppression from velocity scaling of the screening length for a heavy quark-antiquark pair. We also briefly discuss differences and similarities between QCD and N=4 Super-Yang-Mills theory.

## Key claim

The AdS/CFT correspondence applied to N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory yields a jet quenching parameter and a velocity-dependent screening length for quarkonium that are numerically close to experimental estimates from RHIC, but the author cautions that this agreement may be accidental and that the motivation is to gain qualitative insights into QCD rather than to model the full energy-loss process.

## Method

The jet quenching parameter was computed from the thermal expectation value of a light-like Wilson loop in the adjoint representation, evaluated using the AdS/CFT correspondence in the limit of large N and large 't Hooft coupling. For quarkonium suppression, the screening length of a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving with velocity v was extracted from a boosted static Wilson loop, again using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Both calculations were performed for N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature in a five-dimensional anti-de Sitter black hole spacetime.

## Key numbers

- Jet quenching parameter for N=4 SYM: \(\hat{q}_{SYM} = \frac{\pi^{3/2} \Gamma(3/4)}{\Gamma(5/4)} \sqrt{\lambda} T^3 \approx 26.69 \sqrt{\alpha_{SYM} N} T^3\)
- For N=3 and \(\alpha_{SYM}=1/2\) at T=300 MeV: \(\hat{q}_{SYM} = 4.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm
- For N=3 and \(\alpha_{SYM}=1/2\) at T=400 MeV: \(\hat{q}_{SYM} = 10.6\) GeV\(^2\)/fm
- For N=3 and \(\alpha_{SYM}=1/2\) at T=500 MeV: \(\hat{q}_{SYM} = 20.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm
- Weak-coupling estimate at T=300 MeV: \(\hat{q}_{weak-coupling} \approx 0.94\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, smaller than the experimental estimate by at least a factor of 5
- Experimental estimate from RHIC data: time-averaged \(\hat{q}\) around 5-15 GeV\(^2\)/fm
- Screening length scaling: \(L_{max} \sim (1/T) (1-v^2)^{1/4}\)
- Dissociation temperature scaling: \(T_{diss}(v) \sim T_{diss}(v=0) (1-v^2)^{1/4}\)
- Conjectured ratio for QCD: \(\hat{q}_{QCD}/\hat{q}_{N=4} \sim \sqrt{47.5/120} \simeq 0.63\)
- Drag coefficient for heavy quark: \(\mu(p) = (\pi \sqrt{\lambda} / 2m) T^2\)
- Transverse momentum diffusion: \(\kappa_T = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} / (1-v^2)^{1/4} T^3\)
- Longitudinal momentum diffusion: \(\kappa_L = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} / (1-v^2)^{5/4} T^3\)

## Context

This review extends the AdS/CFT program for heavy ion collisions, building on the universal shear viscosity result of Policastro (2001) and its numerical coincidence with RHIC hydrodynamic analyses by Teaney (2003). The jet quenching calculation follows the BDMPS formalism of Baier (1996) and provides a strong-coupling alternative to weak-coupling estimates that fail by a factor of 5 at RHIC temperatures. The velocity-dependent screening length for quarkonium suppression extends the static potential approach from lattice QCD (Satz, 2005; Karsch, 2005) and predicts a marked increase in J/Psi suppression at high transverse momentum, testable at future high-luminosity RHIC runs and at the LHC.
