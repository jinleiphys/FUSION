# Analysis of the Fusion Hindrance in Mass-symmetric Heavy Ion Reactions

> The fusion hindrance, which is also denominated by the term extra-push, is studied on mass-symmetric systems by the use of the liquid drop model with the two-center parameterization. Following the idea that the fusion hindrance exists only if the liquid drop barrier (saddle point) is located at the inner side of the contact point after overcoming the outer Coulomb barrier, the reactions in which two barriers are overlapped with each other are determined. It is shown that there are many systems where the fusion hindrance does not exist for the atomic number of projectile or target nucleus $Z\leq43$, while for $Z>43$, all of the mass-symmetric reactions are fusion-hindered.

## Key claim

For mass-symmetric heavy-ion reactions, fusion hindrance (extra-push) is absent only for systems where the projectile or target atomic number Z is less than or equal to 43, and only within a limited range of mass numbers; for Z greater than 43, all mass-symmetric reactions are fusion-hindered. The condition for no hindrance is that the contact point of the two nuclei lies inside the liquid-drop saddle point, so the di-nucleus can evolve to a compound nucleus without overcoming an additional barrier.

## Method

The authors used the finite-range liquid drop model with a two-center parametrization of nuclear shapes, characterized by three parameters: dimensionless distance between centers (z), mass asymmetry (alpha), and neck parameter (epsilon). They fixed the neck parameter at its equilibrium value of epsilon = 0.1, determined by averaging over a thermal distribution of neck configurations at contact. For each mass-symmetric reaction (A Z + A Z), they calculated the position of the conditional saddle point and compared it to the contact point position (z0 = 1.5874 for symmetric systems) to determine whether an extra-push energy is required.

## Key numbers

- The contact point for mass-symmetric reactions is at z0 = cube root of 4 = 1.5874.
- The equilibrium neck parameter used in calculations is epsilon = 0.1.
- For Z less than 42, both Alow and Aup (the critical mass numbers where the contact point overlaps the saddle point) increase with Z, but the width (Aup - Alow) becomes narrower.
- For Z greater than 43, all mass-symmetric reactions are fusion-hindered.
- Using Z^2 as a criterion, the threshold for hindrance is Z^2 greater than 1849.
- Example reactions: 90Zr + 90Zr and 100Mo + 100Mo show no fusion hindrance, while 110Pd + 110Pd does show hindrance, consistent with experimental data from Keller, Schmidt, and Morawek.

## Context

This work extends the two-step fusion model previously developed by Abe and collaborators (2000), where fusion is divided into overcoming the Coulomb barrier and then passing over the ridge line of the liquid-drop potential. The authors apply the finite-range liquid drop model with two-center parametrization from Sue and Ueda to systematically map the border between normal and hindered fusion for symmetric systems. The results provide a clear Z-dependent criterion for the appearance of fusion hindrance, enabling future quantitative comparisons of fusion cross sections and probabilities, and the method can be extended to mass-asymmetric reactions in forthcoming work.
