# Recoil Polarization Measurements of the Proton Electromagnetic Form Factor Ratio to Q^2 = 8.5 GeV^2

> Among the most fundamental observables of nucleon structure, electromagnetic form factors are a crucial benchmark for modern calculations describing the strong interaction dynamics of the nucleon's quark constituents; indeed, recent proton data have attracted intense theoretical interest. In this letter, we report new measurements of the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio using the recoil polarization method, at momentum transfers Q2=5.2, 6.7, and 8.5 GeV2. By extending the range of Q2 for which GEp is accurately determined by more than 50%, these measurements will provide significant constraints on models of nucleon structure in the non-perturbative regime.

## Key claim
The recoil polarization method yields a proton form factor ratio that falls faster with Q^2 than the Rosenbluth method, and these new measurements extend the Q^2 range for which G_E^p is accurately determined by more than 50%, though the data do not yet satisfy the leading-twist, leading order pQCD dimensional scaling relation.

## Method
Longitudinally polarized electrons from a continuous beam at Jefferson Lab Hall C were scattered from a 20 cm liquid hydrogen target, and elastically scattered electrons and protons were detected in coincidence using the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) with a double Focal Plane Polarimeter (FPP) for protons and a large-acceptance lead-glass calorimeter (BigCal) for electrons. The ratio of transverse to longitudinal recoil proton polarization components was measured at the focal plane, and the spin transport through the HMS magnetic field was calculated using the COSY differential-algebra software to extract the target polarization ratio R = mu_p G_E^p / G_M^p. Elastic events were selected using cuts on the electron scattering angle predicted from proton kinematics and on the proton angle-momentum correlation, with background from pi0 photoproduction estimated via two-dimensional Gaussian extrapolation and Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers
- At Q^2 = 5.17 +/- 0.123 GeV^2: R = 0.443 +/- 0.066 (stat) +/- 0.018 (syst)
- At Q^2 = 6.70 +/- 0.190 GeV^2: R = 0.327 +/- 0.105 (stat) +/- 0.022 (syst)
- At Q^2 = 8.49 +/- 0.167 GeV^2: R = 0.138 +/- 0.179 (stat) +/- 0.043 (syst)
- Acceptance-averaged fractional inelastic backgrounds: 1.12% at Q^2 = 5.2 GeV^2, 0.77% at Q^2 = 6.7 GeV^2, and 5.9% at Q^2 = 8.5 GeV^2
- Standard radiative corrections to P_t/P_l were no greater than 0.13% (relative) for any of the three Q^2 values

## Context
This work extends the polarization transfer measurements of Jones (2000), Punjabi (2005), and Gayou (2002) to higher Q^2, confirming the continued fall of the ratio mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p with increasing Q^2 in disagreement with the Rosenbluth cross section results of Andivahis (1994), Christy (2004), and Qattan (2005). The data provide constraints on theoretical models including the Vector Meson Dominance fits of Lomon (2002, 2006), the covariant spectator model of Gross and Agbakpe (2006, 2008), the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Cloet (2009), and the Generalized Parton Distribution framework of Guidal (2005), and enable improved determination of the quark total angular momentum via Ji's sum rule.
