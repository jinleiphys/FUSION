# Disappearance of Squeezed Back-to-Back Correlations - a new signal of hadron freeze-out from a supercooled Quark Gluon Plasma

> We briefly discuss four different possible types of transitions from quark to hadronic matter and their characteristic signatures in terms of correlations. We also highlight the effects arising from mass modification of hadrons in hot and dense hadronic matter, as well as their quantum statistical consequences: the appearance of squeezed quantum states and the associated experimental signatures, i.e., the back-to-back correlations of particle - anti-particle pairs. We briefly review the theoretical results of these squeezed quanta, generated by in-medium modified masses, starting from the first indication of the existence of surprising particle - anti-particle correlations, and ending by considering the effects of chiral dynamics on these correlation patterns. A prerequisite for such a signature is the experimental verification that these theoretically predicted back-to-back correlation of particle anti-particle pairs are, in fact, observable in high energy heavy ion reactions. Therefore, the experimental observation of back-to-back correlations in high energy heavy ion reactions would be a unique signature, proving the existence of in-medium mass modification of hadronic states. On the other hand, their disappearance at some threshold centrality or collision energy would indicate that the hadron formation mechanism would have qualitatively changed: asymptotic hadrons above such a threshold are not formed from medium modified hadrons anymore, but rather by new degrees of freedom characterizing the medium. Furthermore, the disappearance of the squeezed BBC could also serve as a signature of a sudden, non-equilibrium hadronization scenario from a supercooled quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Key claim

The disappearance of squeezed back-to-back correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs at some threshold centrality or collision energy would indicate that hadron formation has qualitatively changed: asymptotic hadrons above such a threshold are not formed from medium-modified hadrons but rather by new degrees of freedom, and this disappearance could serve as a signature of a sudden, non-equilibrium hadronization scenario from a supercooled quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Method

The authors review four possible types of transitions from quark to hadronic matter (first-order equilibrium phase transition, second-order equilibrium phase transition, cross-over in equilibrium, and non-equilibrium hadronization) and their characteristic signatures in two-particle correlation functions. They then develop the theoretical framework for squeezed quantum states generated by in-medium mass modification of hadrons, using Bogoliubov-Valatin transformations to relate in-medium creation/annihilation operators to free ones, and derive the resulting back-to-back correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs. The formalism is applied to predict how the presence or absence of these correlations would distinguish between equilibrium hadronization scenarios and a sudden flash of hadrons from a supercooled QGP.

## Key numbers

- The extracted central temperature for the 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV is T_0 = 214 +/- 7 MeV, with baryochemical potential mu_B = 77 +/- 38 MeV.
- The critical end point of the first-order QCD phase transition line is located at T_CEP = 162 +/- 2 MeV and mu_CEP = 360 +/- 40 MeV according to lattice QCD estimates.
- At zero baryochemical potential, the peak of the renormalized chiral susceptibility gives T_c = 151(3) MeV, while critical temperatures based on strange quark number susceptibility and Polyakov loops are higher by 24(4) MeV and 25(4) MeV respectively.
- The Levy index of stability alpha is predicted to decrease to about 0.50 +/- 0.02 in the vicinity of the critical end point, based on universality class arguments from the 3-dimensional random field Ising model where eta = 0.50 +/- 0.05.
- The Bose-Einstein condensation threshold temperature for metastable He atoms is T_BEC = 0.5 micro-Kelvin.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of squeezed quantum states in high energy physics, building on the pioneering discovery by Andreev, Plumer and Weiner (1991) of surprising particle-antiparticle correlations, and on subsequent developments by Sinyukov (1994) and Asakawa and Csorgo (1996). The paper contrasts with the predominant literature on first-order phase transitions (Pratt, 1984; Bertsch, 1988; Rischke, 1996) which predicted R_out >> R_side but is excluded by experimental data showing R_out approximately equal to R_side at both CERN SPS and RHIC. The proposed disappearance of squeezed back-to-back correlations would enable experimental identification of a non-equilibrium hadronization scenario from a supercooled QGP, as suggested by Csorgo (1994), which cannot be represented in the (mu_B, T) plane.
