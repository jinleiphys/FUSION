# QCD thermodynamics and confinement from a dynamical quasiparticle point of view

> In this study it is demonstrated that a simple picture of the QCD gluon liquid emerges in the dynamical quasiparticle model that specifies the active degrees of freedom in the time-like sector and yields a potential energy density in the space-like sector. By using the time-like gluon density (or scalar gluon density) as an independent degree of freedom - instead of the temperature $T$ as a Lagrange parameter - variations of the potential energy density lead to effective mean-fields for time-like gluons and an effective gluon-gluon interaction strength at low density. The latter yields a simple dynamical picture for the gluon fusion to color neutral glueballs when approaching the phase boundary from a temperature higher than $T_c$ and paves the way for an off-shell transport theoretical description of the parton dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) can reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics for pure gluonic systems down to the phase boundary, and that the effective gluon-gluon interaction extracted from the model becomes strongly attractive at low gluon density, providing a dynamical picture for gluon fusion into color-neutral glueballs near the critical temperature.

## Method

The author uses the DQPM, which parametrizes gluonic quasiparticles with a Lorentzian spectral function characterized by a temperature-dependent mass M(T) and width γ(T), fitted to lattice QCD entropy density results. The entropy density functional is separated into time-like and space-like contributions using a projection operator Θ(±P²), allowing the decomposition of the total energy density into a kinetic part T⁺₀₀ (time-like quasiparticles) and a potential part T⁻₀₀ (space-like interactions). Derivatives of the potential energy density with respect to the time-like gluon density N⁺ are then computed to extract effective mean-field potentials and gluon-gluon interaction strengths.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle mass M(T) at T = 1.02 T_c is about 0.91 GeV, with width γ ≈ 0.15 GeV; at T = 2 T_c, M ≈ 0.86 GeV and γ ≈ 0.56 GeV.
- The ratio γ(T)/M(T) drops below 0.5 only for temperatures T > 1.25 × 10⁵ T_c.
- The effective gluon-gluon interaction v_gg(ρ_g) changes sign at ρ_g ≈ 1.4 fm⁻³, corresponding to T ≈ 1.045 T_c, becoming attractive at lower densities and repulsive at higher densities.
- The mean-field potential U(ρ_g) shows a distinct minimum at ρ_g ≈ 1.4 fm⁻³.
- The scalar density derivative dρ_s/d(T/T_c) follows approximately a quadratic scaling with an exponential correction term with parameters a₁ = 1.5 fm⁻³, a₂ = 104 fm⁻³, and b = 5.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical quasiparticle approach of Peshier (2004, 2005) by explicitly separating time-like and space-like contributions to thermodynamic quantities, providing a framework for off-shell transport descriptions. The extracted effective gluon-gluon interaction shows behavior analogous to nuclear matter clustering as described by the nonlinear σ-model, where a change in sign of the second derivative of the potential energy density indicates the onset of bound state formation. The results enable the development of the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) approach for modeling the transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
