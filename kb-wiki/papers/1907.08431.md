# Exclusive production of a large mass photon pair

> The scattering amplitude for photoproduction of a large invariant mass diphoton in the generalized Bjorken regime has a very peculiar and interesting analytical structure. The leading twist leading order amplitude is proportional to valence quark generalized parton distributions taken at the border value $x=\pm ξ$. Cross section estimates show that this process is measurable at JLab energies. The angular asymmetry triggered by a linearly polarized photon beam is large.

## Key claim

The leading-twist, leading-order amplitude for exclusive photoproduction of a large-invariant-mass diphoton is proportional to valence quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) evaluated exactly at the crossover line \(x = \pm \xi\), rather than requiring a convolution over \(x\); this unique analytic structure is not expected to survive at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors compute the scattering amplitude for the process \(\gamma N \to \gamma \gamma N'\) in the generalized Bjorken regime using QCD collinear factorization, expressing the amplitude in terms of hard coefficient functions derived from six Feynman diagrams and the four leading-twist quark GPDs (\(H^q, E^q, \tilde{H}^q, \tilde{E}^q\)). The coefficient functions are projected onto vector and axial-vector Fierz structures, and the resulting Compton form factors are evaluated analytically, showing that the integrals over \(x\) collapse to the GPDs at the border values \(x = \pm \xi\). Cross sections and beam-spin asymmetries are then estimated for proton and neutron targets at several center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- Unpolarized differential cross section \(\frac{d\sigma}{dM_{\gamma\gamma}^2 dt}\) is shown at \(t = t_{\text{min}}\) for \(S_{\gamma N} = 20\) GeV\(^2\), \(100\) GeV\(^2\), and \(10^6\) GeV\(^2\) (the latter multiplied by \(10^5\) in the figure).
- At \(S_{\gamma N} = 20\) GeV\(^2\) and \(t = t_{\text{min}}\), the azimuthal dependence of \(\frac{d\sigma}{dM_{\gamma\gamma}^2 dt du' d\phi}\) is plotted for \((M_{\gamma\gamma}^2, u') = (3, -2)\) GeV\(^2\), \((4, -1)\) GeV\(^2\), and \((4, -2)\) GeV\(^2\).
- The cross section exhibits a modulation of the form \(A + B \cos 2\phi\) with \(B\) negative, producing a minimum at \(\phi = 0\) and a maximum at \(\phi = \pi/2\); the linear polarization effects are described as "huge."

## Context

This work extends the study of hard exclusive photoproduction of a diphoton pair, previously introduced by Pedrak et al. (2017), and is complementary to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) because it is insensitive to gluon GPDs and singlet quark GPDs due to charge symmetry of the two-photon final state. The estimated cross sections are comparable in magnitude to those for timelike Compton scattering, whose feasibility at JLab has been demonstrated by Boer et al., but the process is not attractive for ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders, unlike TCS as studied by Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner. The large linear-polarization asymmetry opens the door to experimental extraction of valence-quark GPDs at the crossover line using polarized photon beams at JLab.
