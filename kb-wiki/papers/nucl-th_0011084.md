# The nucleon and mesons effective masses in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> Nucleon and meson effective masses in the nonlinear Relativistic Mean - Field Theory (RMF) introducing a nonlinear omega - rho and sigma coupling motivated by the Quark Meson Coupling model (QMC) is explored. It is shown that, in contrast to the usual Walecka model, not only the effective nucleon mass m_{eff,N} but also the effective sigma, rho meson masses (m_{eff, sigma}, m_{eff, rho}) and the effective omega meson mass m_{eff, omega} are nucleon density dependent.

# Nucleon and meson effective masses in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

## Key claim

The authors show that in a nonlinear Relativistic Mean-Field Theory extended with omega-rho and sigma couplings inspired by the Quark Meson Coupling model, not only the effective nucleon mass but also the effective sigma, rho, and omega meson masses become nucleon density dependent, in contrast to the standard Walecka model. However, this density dependence is conditional on the inclusion of specific nonlinear interaction terms (vector-scalar couplings g_omega_sigma and g_rho_sigma, and the omega-rho coupling Lambda_v), and the results depend on which parameter set (NL3, TM1, or QMC) is employed.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian density for nuclear matter including nucleons and sigma, omega, and rho mesons, with nonlinear scalar self-interactions (Boguta-Bodmer type), quartic vector self-interactions, and additional omega-rho and vector-scalar coupling terms. They apply the Feynman-Bogoliubov variational method to obtain effective masses for nucleons and mesons as functions of baryon density, using three parameter sets: NL3, TM1, and a QMC-inspired set. The effective masses are derived from extremum conditions on the thermodynamic potential of a trial system of free quasiparticles, and the static homogeneous solutions for meson fields are computed for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the standard Walecka model by incorporating nonlinear meson interactions motivated by the Quark Meson Coupling model of Li et al. and by the work of Horowitz and Piekarewicz on neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star crusts. The nonlinear couplings (Lambda_v, g_omega_sigma, g_rho_sigma) modify the density dependence of the omega and rho fields and the symmetry energy, enabling more realistic descriptions of neutron-rich matter and neutron star properties. The results enable future self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculations for finite nuclei such as ^{208}Pb, relevant to the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory.
