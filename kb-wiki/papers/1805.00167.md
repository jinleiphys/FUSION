# Quantum dissipation of a heavy quark from a nonlinear stochastic Schrödinger equation

> We study the open system dynamics of a heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma using a Lindblad master equation. Applying the quantum state diffusion approach by Gisin and Percival, we derive and numerically solve a nonlinear stochastic Schrödinger equation for wave functions, which is equivalent to the Lindblad master equation for the density matrix. From our numerical analysis in one spatial dimension, it is shown that the density matrix relaxes to the Boltzmann distribution in various setups (with and without external potentials), independently of the initial conditions. We also confirm that quantum dissipation plays an essential role not only in the long-time behavior of the heavy quark but also at early times if the heavy quark initial state is localized and quantum decoherence is ineffective.

## Key claim

The density matrix of a heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma relaxes to the Boltzmann distribution in various setups, but this is only guaranteed up to corrections of order \((\Delta E_k/T)^3\) from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem; the Lindblad equation itself does not explicitly guarantee the Boltzmann distribution as the static solution.

## Method

The authors apply the quantum state diffusion (QSD) approach by Gisin and Percival to a Lindblad master equation for a single heavy quark in the quark-gluon plasma, deriving a nonlinear stochastic Schrodinger equation for the wave function. They numerically solve this equation in one spatial dimension using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method for the Hamiltonian part and explicit forward steps for the stochastic terms, with periodic boundary conditions and a Gaussian approximation for the noise correlation function \(D(x) = \gamma \exp[-x^2/l_{\rm corr}^2]\).

## Key numbers

* Momentum distribution approaches the Boltzmann distribution with temperature \(T = 0.1M\) over a time scale \(\tau_{\rm relax} = 100\pi/M\) (derived from classical drag force \(\frac{dp}{dt} = -\frac{\gamma}{MTl_{\rm corr}^2} p\)).
* Slight deviations from the Boltzmann distribution are observed for momenta \(p \gtrsim 1.5M\) at late times (\(t = 620/M, 1240/M\)).
* The effect of dissipation on the decay rate is \(\simeq -0.048 - 0.125\langle\nabla^2\rangle_{\psi_i}/M^2\) for the given parameters, with \(\langle\nabla^2\rangle_{\psi_0}/M^2 \simeq 0, -0.02, -0.08\) for the free case, harmonic potential, and regularized Coulomb potential respectively.
* Wave function size is \(M\Delta x \simeq 3.5\) for the harmonic potential ground state and \(M\Delta x \simeq 1.9\) for the regularized Coulomb potential ground state, compared to the correlation length \(Ml_{\rm corr} = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system formulation for heavy quark pairs in the quark-gluon plasma developed by Akamatsu (2014) and others, which previously focused on stochastic potentials and thermal fluctuations. It provides the first numerical analysis of quantum dissipation for a single heavy quark using the quantum state diffusion approach, enabling future studies of heavy quarkonium dynamics where collisions of heavy quarks and antiquarks interfere nontrivially. The results contradict the naive rate equation approach by showing that a single decay rate cannot capture the relaxation of eigenstate occupation, as the relaxation time depends on initial conditions.
