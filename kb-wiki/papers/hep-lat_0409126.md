# Chiral dynamics in QCD at finite chemical potential

> We present the renormalized Taylor expansion of the chiral condensate with chemical potential. The relation with the Taylor coefficients of meson susceptibilities and the quark mass dependence of the quark number susceptibilities are obtained through chiral Ward identities and Maxwell relations. The continuum limit is obtained in quenched and two flavour QCD. In Nf=2 QCD, the quadratic response coefficients (QRCs) of the chiral condensate to chemical potential indicate that pion fluctuations decrease at finite chemical potential toward the critical end point. We discuss how these observables can be used to test other features of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The quadratic response coefficients (QRCs) of the chiral condensate to chemical potential indicate that pion fluctuations decrease at finite chemical potential toward the critical end point, but this conclusion is based on lattice simulations at a fixed renormalized quark mass corresponding to a heavy pion mass (m_pi/m_rho approximately 0.6), not at the physical quark mass.

## Method

The authors performed a renormalized Taylor expansion of the chiral condensate up to second order in the chemical potential, using chiral Ward identities and Maxwell relations to connect the Taylor coefficients to meson susceptibilities and quark number susceptibilities. They computed the continuum limit in both quenched QCD and two-flavour QCD using staggered quarks on lattices with varying lattice spacings, holding the renormalized quark mass fixed by tuning the bare mass to maintain a constant ratio m_pi^2/(m T).

## Key numbers

- In quenched QCD at T/T_c = 1.5 on the coarsest lattice (N_t = 4), the diagonal QRC in the T scheme gave T^2 C_S^{20}(T)/C_S(T) approximately -0.096
- The diagonal QRC in quenched QCD at T/T_c = 1.5 vanishes at the 99% confidence limit on extrapolation to the continuum
- At T = 1.5 T_c in quenched QCD, the conversion factor from T scheme to Z scheme (the temperature dependence of the condensate) is 0.26 plus or minus 0.01 for the renormalized mass used
- The ratio m_pi/m_rho was approximately 0.6 for the fixed renormalized quark mass used in the quenched simulations
- In two-flavour QCD with dynamical staggered quarks, choosing m/T_c = 0.1 corresponds to m_pi/m_rho = 0.36 plus or minus 0.01

## Context

This work extends the Taylor expansion method for finite chemical potential previously applied to the equation of state by the same group (pressure, biswa) and by others (gott, miya), and differs from the approach of Miya (miya) by performing direct Taylor expansions of operators rather than of parameters in fitting functions. The results enable future tests of the QCD phase diagram, particularly regarding the critical end point, and provide Maxwell relations that allow extrapolation of quark number susceptibilities to physical quark masses using the mass dependence computed here.
