# Shell-model study on properties of proton dripline nuclides with Z, N = 30-50 including uncertainty analysis

> The binding energies and proton separation energies of nuclides with $Z, N = 30-50$ are investigated, based on the shell model with an uncertainty analysis through statistical methods. Several formulas are used to obtain the binding energies and proton separation energies according to the shell-model calculations. The non-parametric Bootstrap method is applied to establish an uncertainty decomposition and recomposition framework. Moreover, it is used to estimate the stability of proton(s) emission for each nuclide. Two formulas for calculating the binding energies with a systematic uncertainty of $\sim0.3$ MeV are proposed, and a reliable extrapolation ability is examined. These binding energy formulas deduce similar forms of respective $S_{p}$ and $S_{2p}$ energies, which predict the extension of the nuclear boundary of this region. A nice description of the binding energies and proton separation energies is provided. The one- and two-proton separation energies and partial half-lives of proton emitting are predicted, thus showing a new dripline. Besides, there are 30 unstable nuclides predicted to be bound against proton(s)-emission. These nuclear properties will be useful in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two new formulas for calculating nuclear binding energies in the $f_{5}pg_{9}$ shell-model space that achieve a systematic uncertainty of approximately 0.3 MeV, and uses these to predict the proton dripline for nuclides with $Z, N = 30-50$, finding 30 unstable nuclides that are nevertheless predicted to be bound against proton(s)-emission.

## Method

Shell-model calculations using the JUN45 effective interaction in the $f_{5}pg_{9}$ model space (consisting of the $p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, and $g_{9/2}$ orbitals) were performed to obtain valence binding energies for nuclides with $Z, N = 30-50$. Several correction formulas (Eqs. 1-4) were fitted to experimental binding energies from AME2016 for 221 nuclides, with the non-parametric Bootstrap method (using $10^6$ samples) applied to decompose uncertainties into statistical and systematic components and to estimate prediction uncertainties for unknown nuclides.

## Key numbers

* Two proposed binding energy formulas (Eq. 2 and Eq. 3) achieve total uncertainties of 0.316 MeV and 0.317 MeV respectively for the 221 experimentally known nuclides in the $Z, N = 30-50$ region
* The recommended proton separation energy formula (Eq. 12) has a total uncertainty of 0.231 MeV for 198 experimentally known nuclides
* The recommended two-proton separation energy formula (Eq. 15) has a total uncertainty of 0.264 MeV for 178 experimentally known nuclides
* 30 unstable nuclides are predicted to be bound against proton(s)-emission
* The fitting parameter $a$ for the binding energy formula Eq. 2 is -9.61(1) MeV, with $b = -0.0944(8)$ MeV, $c = 0.0245(5)$ MeV, and $d = -0.0262(9)$ MeV

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of binding energies in the $f_{5}pg_{9}$ region beyond the approach of Herndl and Brown (1997), who used a simpler constant-plus-linear correction, and Caurier et al. (1999), who employed a correction including Coulomb and monopole terms. The uncertainty decomposition framework based on the Bootstrap method, previously applied by Cai et al. (2020) to alpha-decay studies, is here adapted for binding energy and separation energy predictions. The results provide input for nuclear astrophysics, particularly for the $\nu p$-process and Type I X-ray bursts, and enable predictions of the proton dripline location for medium-mass nuclei.
