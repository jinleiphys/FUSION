# Lattice studies of chimera baryons in Sp(4) gauge theory

> We study chimera baryons, fermion bound states composed of two (hyper)quarks transforming in the fundamental and one in the antisymmetric representation of a non-Abelian gauge group. While in QCD they coincide with ordinary baryons, in composite Higgs models (CHMs) with top partial compositeness, spin-1/2 chimera baryons serve as partners of the top quark and are responsible for its large mass. We perform non-perturbative lattice calculations of the low-lying spectrum of the chimera baryons, in a specific realization of CHMs based on a Sp(4) gauge theory. In the quenched approximation, we present the numerical results in the continuum and massless limits. Then, for dynamical fermions, we measure the spectrum and matrix elements by employing a newly developed spectral density analysis for several choices of the lattice parameters.

## Key claim

The paper presents non-perturbative lattice calculations of chimera baryon masses and matrix elements in an Sp(4) gauge theory, finding that in the quenched approximation the lightest chimera baryon (Sigma_CB) has a mass similar to the vector meson composed of antisymmetric-type hyperquarks, while for dynamical fermions the parity-even states are consistently lighter than parity-odd states, with the spin-1/2 parity-even Sigma_CB+ showing a larger overlap factor than Lambda_CB+.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of Sp(4) gauge theory with Wilson-Dirac hyperquarks in the fundamental and antisymmetric representations, using the standard plaquette gauge action. In the quenched approximation, they generated ensembles at five lattice couplings (beta=7.62, 7.7, 7.85, 8.0, 8.2) and extracted chimera baryon masses from two-point correlation functions with Wuppertal and APE smearing, extrapolating to continuum and massless limits. For dynamical fermions, they generated five ensembles at fixed beta=6.5 and antisymmetric hyperquark mass, employing a spectral density reconstruction method (Hansen-Lupo-Tantalo approach) with Gaussian and Cauchy kernels to extract energy spectra and renormalised overlap factors.

## Key numbers

- Quenched approximation: pNGB mass range for (f)-type hyperquarks was hat_m_PS in [0.28, 1.03] and for (as)-type hyperquarks hat_m_ps in [0.35, 1.84], with about 150 measurements
- Quenched ensembles used lattice sizes 60x48^3 for beta=7.7, 7.85, 8.0, 8.2 and 48x24^3 for the coarsest ensemble at beta=7.62
- Dynamical fermion ensembles: M1=(am_0^f=-0.71, 48x20^3), M2=(-0.71, 64x20^3), M3=(-0.71, 96x20^3), M4=(-0.7, 63x20^3), M5=(-0.72, 64x32^3), all with beta=6.5 and am_0^as=-1.01
- One-loop renormalisation factors: Delta_CB[gamma_5]=-26.67 and Delta_CB[gamma_i]=18.12 for vertex diagrams, with Delta_Sigma_1=-12.82

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of Sp(2N) gauge theories by the TELOS collaboration (Bennett 2023, Bennett 2022) and provides the first non-perturbative calculations of chimera baryon properties relevant for composite Higgs models with top partial compositeness (Ferretti 2013, Barnard 2013). The results enable quantitative input for phenomenological studies of top partner candidates in Sp(4) gauge theories, with the spectral density method (Hansen 2019) offering a new approach for extracting excited state information from lattice correlation functions.
