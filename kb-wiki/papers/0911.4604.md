# Higher-Twist Dynamics in Large Transverse Momentum Hadron Production

> A scaling law analysis of the world data on inclusive large-pT hadron production in hadronic collisions is carried out. A significant deviation from leading-twist perturbative QCD predictions at next-to-leading order is reported. The observed discrepancy is largest at high values of xT=2pT/sqrt(s). In contrast, the production of prompt photons and jets exhibits the scaling behavior which is close to the conformal limit, in agreement with the leading-twist expectation. These results bring evidence for a non-negligible contribution of higher-twist processes in large-pT hadron production in hadronic collisions, where the hadron is produced directly in the hard subprocess rather than by gluon or quark jet fragmentation. Predictions for scaling exponents at RHIC and LHC are given, and it is suggested to trigger the isolated large-pT hadron production to enhance higher-twist processes.

# Higher-Twist Dynamics in Large Transverse Momentum Hadron Production

## Key claim

The world data on inclusive large-pT hadron production in hadronic collisions show a significant deviation from leading-twist perturbative QCD predictions at next-to-leading order, with the discrepancy largest at high values of xT = 2pT/√s. This deviation is interpreted as evidence for non-negligible higher-twist contributions where the hadron is produced directly in the hard subprocess rather than by gluon or quark jet fragmentation, though the authors note that part of the discrepancy at large xT could also arise from threshold logarithms that should be resummed to all orders.

## Method

The authors performed a scaling law analysis of inclusive invariant cross sections for large-pT hadron production, extracting the experimental exponent nexp from measurements at different center-of-mass energies using the relation nexp(xT) = -ln(σinv(xT,√s1)/σinv(xT,√s2)) / ln(√s1/√s2). They compared these experimental exponents to next-to-leading order QCD calculations using CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions and de Florian-Sassot-Stratmann and Bourhis-Fontannaz-Guillet fragmentation functions, computing the difference Δ = nexp - nNLO for hadrons, prompt photons, and jets across multiple experiments.

## Key numbers

- E706 π0 data at √s = 31.6, 38.8 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 8.2 ± 0.11 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 6.1 ± 0.09, a discrepancy of about 2 units
- PHENIX π0 data at √s = 62.4, 200 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 6.7 ± 0.05 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 5.6 ± 0.08 at xT ≈ 10^{-1}
- PHENIX/ISR π0 comparison at √s = 62.4, 22.4 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 7.5 ± 0.19 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 6.2 ± 0.30 at xT = 0.2
- CDF prompt photon data at √s = 630, 1800 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 4.7 ± 0.09 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 4.3 ± 0.01
- D0 prompt photon data at √s = 630, 1800 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 4.5 ± 0.12 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 4.3 ± 0.01
- CDF jet data at √s = 546, 1800 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 4.3 ± 0.09 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 4.6 ± 0.01
- D0 jet data at √s = 630, 1800 GeV: ⟨nexp⟩ = 4.5 ± 0.04 compared to ⟨nNLO⟩ = 4.6 ± 0.01
- Two-component model fit yields B/A ≈ 50 GeV² (with all scales at pT) or B/A ≈ 15 GeV² (with scales at pT/2) for the relative strength of higher-twist to leading-twist contributions at xT = 0.2

## Context

This work extends the scaling law analysis of Brodsky (1973) and the higher-twist calculations of Berger (1980) for direct pion production subprocesses (gq → πq and qq̅ → πg) to a systematic comparison with world data from fixed-target to collider energies. The results support the interpretation of Brodsky (2008) that color-transparent higher-twist subprocesses such as uu → pd̅ can account for anomalous proton production features seen in heavy ion collisions at RHIC by Adler (2003). The analysis enables predictions for scaling exponents at RHIC and LHC energies and suggests triggering on isolated hadrons to enhance higher-twist contributions in future measurements.
