# Quasiparticle-vibration coupling in relativistic framework: shell structure of Z=120 isotopes

> For the first time, the shell structure of open-shell nuclei is described in a fully self-consistent extension of the covariant energy density functional theory. The approach implies quasiparticle-vibration coupling for superfluid systems. One-body Dyson equation formulated in the doubled quasiparticle space of Dirac spinors is solved for nucleonic propagators in tin isotopes which represent the reference case: the obtained energies of the single-quasiparticle levels and their spectroscopic amplitudes are in agreement with data. The model is applied to describe the shell evolution in a chain of superheavy isotopes $^{292,296,300,304}$120 and finds a rather stable proton spherical shell closure at Z = 120. An interplay of the pairing correlations and the quasiparticle-phonon coupling gives rise for a smooth evolution of the neutron shell gap between N = 172 and N = 184 neutron numbers. Vibrational corrections to the alpha decay energies reach several hundred keV and can be either positive and negative, thus also smearing the shell effects.

## Key claim

For the first time, the shell structure of open-shell nuclei is described in a fully self-consistent extension of the covariant energy density functional theory that includes both pairing correlations and quasiparticle-vibration coupling on equal footing; the proton number Z = 120 remains a rather stable spherical shell closure across the neutron range N = 172 to N = 184, but the neutron shell gap evolves smoothly due to an interplay of pairing and quasiparticle-phonon coupling.

## Method

The one-body Dyson equation formulated in the doubled quasiparticle space of Dirac spinors is solved for nucleonic propagators, using a Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) part plus an energy-dependent self-energy from quasiparticle-phonon coupling. The phonon frequencies are computed with the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) and the vertices determine the coupling of quasiparticles to vibrational modes. Benchmarking calculations are performed for tin isotopes (116Sn and 120Sn) before applying the model to the superheavy Z=120 isotope chain (292,296,300,304120).

## Key numbers

- In 116Sn and 120Sn, the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) model reproduces the experimentally observed single-quasiparticle energies and spectroscopic factors; for closed-shell proton subsystems the QVC shifts are considerably larger than for states very close to the Fermi energy.
- For the Z=120 isotopes, the RQRPA vibrational corrections to the total energies amount to 4-5 MeV, comparable to the shell correction energies.
- Alpha decay energies for even-even Z=120 isotopes (N=176-184) from the RMF model range from 11.23 MeV (N=182) to 11.81 MeV (N=176); with vibrational corrections (RMF+VC) they range from 11.41 MeV (N=182) to 11.91 MeV (N=184).
- The largest vibrational correction to the alpha decay energy occurs in 300120, where the decrease of the lifetime is up to an order of magnitude depending on the evaluation method.
- The expected spherical shell gaps in the superheavy mass region are considerably smaller (2-3 MeV) than in lighter nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the covariant energy density functional (CEDF) approach by incorporating quasiparticle-vibration coupling for superfluid systems, building on the Landau-Migdal theory framework developed in earlier work by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2008, 2010). It addresses the lack of consensus about spherical shell closures above Z=82 and N=126, where predictions such as Z=114, Z=120, or Z=126 for protons and N=172 or N=184 for neutrons exist in the literature (Bender et al., 1999; Smolanczuk and Piechaczek, 2007). The model enables more accurate predictions for superheavy element synthesis and can guide future experimental searches for Z=120 isotopes, which are at the limits of accessibility with available cold fusion reactions.
