# Quantifying multinucleon effect in Argon using high-pressure TPC

> Neutrino oscillation experiments use heavy nuclear targets to achieve sufficient interaction rates. Nuclear effects are introduced in the experimental environment by the use of these targets and need to be quantified as they add to the systematic errors. In the low energy region(around 1 GeV) multinucleon events are also present along with Quasi Elastic(QE) and Delta interactions. Therefore if these multinucleon events are not incorporated in the data set properly, we end up with an inaccurate reconstruction of neutrino energy. In our work, we have illustrated the importance of incorporation of multinucleon events for the reduction of systematic errors in physics predictions by DUNE-Near Detector(ND). To achieve this we have presented the event distribution ratio of Ar/C, Ar/Ar, and C/C as a function of squared four-momentum transfer by employing different nuclear models. This analysis recommends the addition of 2p2h or multinucleon events in the event sample and promotes model with Random Phase Approximations(RPA) effect for the analysis of the event sample to overcome or reduce the systematic uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating 2p2h (multinucleon) events combined with the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) effect in the Nieves model significantly reduces systematic uncertainties in neutrino energy reconstruction for the DUNE Near Detector, but this improvement is conditional on the model being tuned separately for neutrinos and antineutrinos, as the ratio of RPA-ON+2p2h to RPA-OFF event rates differs between them (1.2–1.4 for neutrinos versus 1.4–1.8 for antineutrinos).

## Method

The authors used the GENIE event generator (version 2.12.6) to simulate 1 million charged-current muon neutrino and antineutrino events on Argon and Carbon targets, using the DUNE-ND flux in the energy range 0.125–20.125 GeV. They compared two QE models: the default Llewellyn-Smith model with Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) and the Nieves model incorporating RPA correlations, with and without the addition of 2p2h (MEC) interactions. Event distributions were analyzed as a function of squared four-momentum transfer \(Q^2\) and three-momentum transfer \(q_3\), with detector cuts applied for muons (momentum > 200 MeV) and protons (kinetic energy > 20 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The DUNE-ND HPgTPC has a total volume of approximately 1.8 tons and a fiducial volume of approximately 1 ton, filled with P10 gas (90% Argon, 10% CH4) at 10 atm pressure.
- The neutrino flux peaks around 2.5 GeV and covers the range 0.125–20.125 GeV.
- The axial mass \(M_A\) used in the simulation is 0.99 GeV/\(c^2\).
- The hadronic energy distribution shifts by approximately 300 MeV toward higher energy when the RPA effect is included compared to the Default model.
- The ratio of RPA-ON+2p2h to RPA-OFF event rates as a function of \(Q^2\) becomes nearly constant between 1.2 and 1.4 for neutrinos and between 1.4 and 1.8 for antineutrinos.
- The Ar/C event ratio for the Default model shows a dip around 0.5 GeV\(^2\), while the RPA model shows minimal distortion from unity below 1 GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions relevant for the DUNE experiment, following studies by the MINERvA collaboration (2017, 2019) that showed differences in physics analysis when different QE models were applied to Carbon data. The paper builds on the approach of combining RPA correlations with 2p2h interactions, which had previously improved the description of MiniBooNE data as shown by Nieves et al. (2012) and Gran et al. (2013). The results enable DUNE to reduce systematic uncertainties in neutrino energy reconstruction by incorporating multinucleon events and RPA effects in the Near Detector analysis, though further tuning of models is required for both neutrinos and antineutrinos.
