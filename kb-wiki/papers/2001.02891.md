# Theoretical Analysis of Double Differential Cross Section of Proton, Deuteron and Triton for $p+^7$Li Reaction at 14 MeV

> Based on the statistical theory of light nucleus reactions (STLN), the description of the complicated emission processes of proton and light composite charged particles are further improved through considering the effects of Coulomb barriers both in incident and different outgoing reaction channels. And the analysis of the reaction channels including the sequential and simultaneous emission processes for $p + ^7$Li reaction is performed in detail. So the partial spectra of all of outgoing particles are also obtained for different reaction processes. The calculated double differential cross sections of total outgoing proton, deuteron and triton at $E_p = 14$ MeV agree well with the available experimental data for different outgoing angles. The ENDF-6 formatted data, which includes all of the reaction cross sections, elastic angular distributions, double differential cross sections of nucleon and light composite charged particles for $p + ^7$Li reaction, are also obtained by PUNF code.

## Key claim

The authors improve the statistical theory of light nucleus reactions (STLN) by incorporating Coulomb barrier effects in both incident and outgoing channels, enabling the calculation of double differential cross sections for charged particles (proton, deuteron, triton) from the p+⁷Li reaction at 14 MeV, with the caveat that agreement with experimental data is good but some peaks in the proton spectra are contaminated by scattering from ¹H, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O.

## Method

The authors extended the unified Hauser-Feshbach and exciton model within the STLN framework to include Coulomb barrier effects for charged particle emission channels, using the improved Iwamoto-Harada model for light composite particle emission and a linear momentum-dependent exciton state density model for angular distributions. They analyzed all open reaction channels for p+⁷Li at incident energies up to 20 MeV, including sequential and simultaneous emission processes, cluster separations (⁵He → n+α, ⁵Li → p+α, ⁸Be → α+α), and calculated partial spectra for each reaction channel using the PUNF code. The optical model potential parameters were determined by fitting reaction cross sections and elastic angular distributions, and the calculated double differential cross sections were compared with experimental data from Koori (1989, 1991).

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: Ep = 14 MeV for all calculations and comparisons with experimental data
- Reaction Q-values listed for 17 reaction channels, e.g., (p,γ)⁸Be: Q = +17.255 MeV, (p,n)⁷Be: Q = -1.643 MeV, (p,d)⁶Li: Q = -5.025 MeV, (p,t)⁵Li: Q = -4.434 MeV
- Threshold energies for reaction channels, e.g., (p,n)⁷Be: Eth = 1.879 MeV, (p,d)⁶Li: Eth = 5.7468 MeV, (p,t)⁵Li: Eth = 5.0709 MeV
- Compound nucleus ⁸Be reaches the twenty-seventh discrete energy level at 28.6 MeV for Ep = 14 MeV
- Coulomb barrier calculated with charge radii parameter rC = 1.2-1.5 fm, using measured charge radii from Angeli (2013)
- Only cross sections with values larger than 10⁻³ mb are shown in partial spectra figures

## Context

This work extends the STLN framework, previously applied successfully to neutron-induced reactions on 1p-shell nuclei such as ⁶Li (Zhang 2001), ⁷Li (Zhang 2002), ⁹Be (Sun 2009, Duan 2010), ¹⁰B (Zhang 2003), ¹¹B (Zhang 2003), ¹²C (Sun 2008, Zhang 1999), ¹⁴N (Yan 2005), ¹⁶O (Zhang 2001, Duan 2005), and ¹⁹F (Duan 2007), to proton-induced reactions on ⁷Li. It addresses limitations of prior R-matrix analyses (ENDF/B-VII.1, ENDF/B-VIII.0, JENDL-4.0/HE) and CDCC calculations (Guo 2013, 2019) which did not include sequential secondary particle emission processes or did not provide deuteron double differential cross sections. The calculated ENDF-6 formatted data enable Monte Carlo simulations for compact accelerator-driven neutron source design and fusion materials irradiation facility applications.
