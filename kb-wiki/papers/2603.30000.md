# From Sub-eikonal DIS to Quark Distributions and their High-Energy Evolution

> Relating the high-energy dipole description of deep-inelastic scattering to the standard light-ray operator formulation at finite Bjorken $x_B$ is essential for connecting the small-$x$ framework to the usual partonic description. I demonstrate that this connection already emerges at the first sub-eikonal order. At the differential level, the first sub-eikonal correction is governed by a quark TMD-like light-ray operator. In the inclusive limit, after complete phase-space integration, it reconstructs the standard nonlocal quark and helicity distributions at nonzero $x_B$. I then show independently that the same inclusive operator content follows from the high-energy limit of the leading-twist non-local operator product expansion, thereby establishing an explicit operator-level bridge between the shock-wave formalism and the non-local light-cone expansion. I further discuss the high-energy evolution of the corresponding operators at $x_B=0$. Rewriting the evolution equations in terms of dipole-type operator combinations, I identify an operator basis whose bilocal building blocks vanish in the zero-dipole-size limit, making the small-dipole behavior and the leading-logarithmic structure manifest. In the double-logarithmic approximation the evolution equations admit the usual mixed longitudinal-transverse Bessel-type solution when the transverse phase space is treated independently. When the transverse phase space is instead constrained by longitudinal ordering, the second logarithm is converted into a logarithm of energy, and in the symmetric double-logarithmic regime one recovers the fixed-coupling Kirschner-Lipatov exponent with the full finite-$N_c$ color factor $C_F$.

## Key claim

The first sub-eikonal correction to the high-energy dipole description of deep-inelastic scattering already reconstructs the standard nonlocal quark and helicity light-ray operators at finite Bjorken \(x_B\), establishing an explicit operator-level bridge between the shock-wave formalism and the non-local light-cone expansion, provided one does not take the strict \(x_B \to 0\) limit before completing the phase-space integration.

## Method

The author computes the transition amplitude for \(\gamma^*(q) \to q(k)\) in the shock-wave background field using the quark propagator with one endpoint inside the shock wave and the other outside, derived via the Schwinger representation and LSZ reduction. The amplitude is projected onto transverse and longitudinal photon polarizations, squared, and integrated over the final-state phase space to extract the quark TMD-like light-ray operator at the differential level and the inclusive quark and helicity distributions after complete phase-space integration. Separately, the same inclusive operator content is recovered from the high-energy limit of the leading-twist non-local operator product expansion. The high-energy evolution of the \(x_B=0\) operators \(Q^f_1\) and \(Q^f_5\) is rewritten in terms of dipole-type operator combinations that vanish in the zero-dipole-size limit, and the double-logarithmic approximation is solved under two distinct transverse phase-space treatments.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal photon polarization contribution to the amplitude is sub-sub-eikonal and is disregarded; the transverse contribution yields the leading sub-eikonal correction at order \(O(\lambda^{-2})\) relative to the eikonal approximation.
- The differential cross-section for \(\gamma^*_T \to q\) is given by \(d^2\sigma^{\gamma^*_T\to q}/d^2k = (\alpha_{\rm em}/s)\, e^2_f\, q_{1,f}(x_B,k_\perp)\), where \(q_{1,f}(x_B,k_\perp)\) is the quark TMD-like light-ray distribution.
- In the inclusive limit after complete phase-space integration, the result reconstructs the standard nonlocal quark distribution \(\langle \bar\psi(y^+,x_\perp) \gamma^- [y^+,x^+]_x \psi(x^+,x_\perp) \rangle\) at nonzero \(x_B\).
- In the double-logarithmic approximation, when transverse phase space is treated independently, the evolution equations admit a mixed longitudinal-transverse Bessel-type solution resumming powers of \((\alpha_s \ln(1/x_B)\ln Q_\perp^2)^n\).
- When transverse phase space is constrained by longitudinal ordering, the symmetric double-logarithmic regime recovers the fixed-coupling Kirschner-Lipatov exponent with the full finite-\(N_c\) color factor \(C_F\).

## Context

This work extends the program of connecting the high-energy dipole description of deep-inelastic scattering (Balitsky 1995, Nikolaev 1990, Mueller 1993) to the standard light-ray operator formulation at finite Bjorken \(x_B\) (Balitsky 1987, Collins 2011). It builds on prior studies of sub-eikonal corrections (Balitsky 2015, Balitsky 2016, Altinoluk 2020, Mukherjee 2023) and the small-\(x\) evolution of valence-quark quantum numbers in a dipole-Reggeon framework (Itakura 2003). The results enable a systematic operator-level connection between the shock-wave formalism and the non-local light-cone expansion, relevant for interpreting Electron-Ion Collider data across the transition from the small-\(x_B\) regime to the conventional finite-\(x_B\) partonic regime.
