# Energetic emissions from deconfinement in compact stars and their relation to the critical end point in the QCD phase diagram

> In this work we study the case of deconfinement in compact star interiors in the presence of a strong first order phase transition associated to a critical end point in the QCD phase diagram. Neutron stars fulfilling these conditions show a third branch in the mass-radius diagram with the first and second branches being the white dwarfs and neutron stars configurations. The transition to the third branch can be reached by a pure hadronic neutron star through an induced collapse releasing energy that corresponds to a mass-energy difference between the second and third branch configurations. Physical outcomes of this phenomenon that can potentially explain the already detected astrophysical signals are discussed. In particular we present energy estimations for the case of a fast radio burst, seen as a double-peak structure in the object's light curve.

## Key claim

A strong first-order deconfinement phase transition in neutron star interiors, linked to a critical end point in the QCD phase diagram, can produce a "third family" of compact stars; the collapse of a pure hadronic neutron star into its hybrid twin on this third branch releases an energy of about \(10^{51}\) erg, which may explain observed fast radio bursts (FRBs) with a double-peak light curve structure, provided the transition occurs via a corequake scenario in cold, non-rotating stars.

## Method

The author uses an equation of state (EoS) combining the DD2 hadronic model with excluded volume corrections and a quark matter EoS based on the NJL model with multiquark interactions (hNJL), parameterized by a vector coupling strength \(\eta_4\). For a set of \(\eta_4\) values (0.0, 1.0, 3.0, 5.0, 7.0, 10.0), the gravitational and baryonic masses of compact star configurations are computed, identifying the maximum hadronic mass and the corresponding hybrid twin star. The energy release \(\Delta E\) is calculated as the mass-energy difference \(\Delta M c^2\) between the initial hadronic neutron star and the final hybrid star, assuming the same baryonic mass.

## Key numbers

- The energy release \(\Delta E\) from the transition between the maximum hadronic neutron star and its hybrid twin is about \(10^{51}\) erg.
- The radius change \(\Delta R\) in the transition is between 1 and 1.5 km for some coupling constant values.
- The observed FRB energy from a direct collapse to a black hole is \(10^{40}\) erg (from Falcke & Rezzolla 2014).
- The maximum observed neutron star mass is 2 M\(_\odot\), from the pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230.
- The hadronic DD2 EoS with excluded volume yields neutron star radii of approximately 13–15 km.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deconfinement-induced corequakes (e.g., 1999JPhG...25..971D, 2002NuPhS.113..268B, 2004A&A...416..991A, Zdunik 2005) by using a modern EoS (Alvarez-Castillo et al. 2015) that produces a "third family" of high-mass twin stars near the maximum mass. It contrasts with the direct collapse scenario of Falcke & Rezzolla (2014) by introducing a metastable hybrid star state that can explain the double-peak structure observed in FRB 121002 (Champion 2015). The results enable future studies of rotating configurations (Bejger 2015) and applications to cosmic ray generation from supernovae or neutron star mergers.
