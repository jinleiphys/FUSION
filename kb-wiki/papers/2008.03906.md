# The non-resonance shake mechanism of the neutrinoless double electronic capture

> It is generally accepted that double neutrinoless electron capture is a resonance process. The calculations of the probability of shaking with the ionization of the electron shell occurring during the transformation of 152Gd and 164Er nuclei are performed below. These nuclides have the lowest resonance defect among all known nuclei, being considered as main candidates for discovering the neutrinoless mode of the transformation. The results show predominant contribution of the new mechanism for most of the candidate nuclei. The value of this amendment rapidly increases with an increasing resonance defect. Thus, in principle, double neutrinoless electron capture appears not to be a resonance process at all.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutrinoless double electron capture is not necessarily a resonance process; a non-resonance "shake" mechanism, in which the excess energy is carried away by an ejected electron, can dominate for nuclei with a resonance defect larger than about 3.4 keV. The authors hedge that for the specific case of 152Gd (with the smallest known resonance defect of 0.919 keV), the new mechanism contributes only a 23% correction, not a complete replacement of the resonance mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculated the probability of shake-off (electron ejection) accompanying neutrinoless double electron capture in 152Gd and 164Er using the single-electron approximation with the RAINE code package. Electron wave functions and energies were computed by the self-consistent Dirac-Fock method, and the shake amplitude was evaluated as the overlap integral between the initial-state electron wave function (in the parent atom) and the continuum-state wave function (in the daughter ion). The total width for the non-resonance mechanism was then compared to the standard Breit-Wigner resonance width to obtain the relative correction factor g.

## Key numbers

- For 152Gd, the resonance defect is Δ = 0.91(19) keV, derived from Q = 55.70(18) keV and E_A = 54.794(9) keV.
- At the real value of Δ = 0.919 keV for 152Gd, the non-resonance shake mechanism contributes a 23% increase in decay probability relative to the traditional resonance mechanism.
- At Δ = 3.43 keV, the contributions of the two mechanisms become equal (g = 1).
- At Δ = 10 keV, the non-resonance mechanism contribution would be 4.5 times greater than the resonance contribution.
- For 164Er, with Q = 6.82(12) keV, the non-resonance mechanism is a factor of 3 more effective than the traditional one.
- The refined half-life estimate for 152Gd is T_{1/2}^{0ν} ≈ 8.1 × 10^25 |1 eV / m_{ββ}|^2 yr.

## Context

This work extends the traditional resonance-fluorescent mechanism of neutrinoless double electron capture, which was previously considered the only viable mode (as in the analysis of 152Gd by the authors cited as [7]). The paper introduces a non-resonance shake mechanism based on the sudden approximation formalism of Migdal (1950s) and applies it to the candidate nuclei 152Gd and 164Er, which have the smallest known resonance defects. The results enable refined half-life estimates for these nuclei and suggest that 164Er, previously considered less favorable, becomes an attractive candidate for experimental searches for neutrinoless double electron capture as a test of the Majorana nature of the neutrino.
