# Study of H-dibaryon mass in Lattice QCD

> After a brief review of discovery of the H-dibaryon in lattice QCD, effect of the flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking on the H-dibaryon is studied by basing on the baryon-baryon (BB) interactions extracted from QCD on the lattice. The Schrodinger equation for Lambda Lambda - N Xi -Sigma Sigma coupled-channel is solved with the physical baryon masses and the potentials obtained from QCD at the flavor SU(3) limit. A resonant H-dibaryon is found between Lambda Lambda and N Xi thresholds in this treatment.

# Study of H-dibaryon mass in Lattice QCD

## Key claim
A resonant H-dibaryon exists between the ΛΛ and NΞ thresholds when the Schrödinger equation is solved using physical baryon masses combined with lattice QCD potentials extracted at the flavor SU(3) limit, under the assumptions that the major SU(3) breaking effect comes from baryon mass splittings and that hyperon interaction qualitative features remain intact.

## Method
The HAL QCD Collaboration extracted baryon-baryon (BB) potentials from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions measured in full QCD lattice simulations at the flavor SU(3) limit, using the renormalization group improved Iwasaki gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson quark action on a 32³ × 32 lattice with spacing a = 0.121(2) fm. They then solved the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the ΛΛ-NΞ-ΣΣ system, combining the lattice QCD potentials with physical baryon masses to incorporate flavor SU(3) breaking phenomenologically.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.121(2) fm, spatial lattice extent L = 3.87 fm
- Five quark masses simulated, producing pseudo-scalar meson masses ranging from 468.6(7) MeV to 1170.9(7) MeV
- Baryon octet masses measured from 1161(2) MeV to 2274(2) MeV across the five ensembles
- Binding energy of the H-dibaryon in the flavor SU(3) limit: 20–50 MeV for the simulated quark masses
- Root-mean-square distance of the H-dibaryon in the SU(3) limit is much smaller than the deuteron's point matter rms distance of 3.8 fm
- A resonance appears in the ΛΛ bar-phase-shift at an energy a little below the NΞ threshold

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous discovery of a bound H-dibaryon in the flavor SU(3) limit (Inoue 2010, Inoue 2011) by introducing phenomenological SU(3) breaking through physical baryon masses. The result that the bound state moves above the ΛΛ threshold and becomes a resonance under SU(3) breaking is consistent with similar findings reported by Beane (2011), Shanahan (2011), and Haidenbauer (2011). The study enables experimental searches for the resonant H-dibaryon via ΛΛ invariant-mass spectroscopy, with prospects for confirmation at J-PARC, RHIC, and LHC experiments.
