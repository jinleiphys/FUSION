# Building an Equation of State Density Ladder

> The confluence of major theoretical, experimental, and observational advances are providing a unique perspective on the equation of state of dense neutron-rich matter -- particularly its symmetry energy -- and its imprint on the mass-radius relation for neutron stars. In this contribution we organize these developments in an equation of state density ladder. Of particular relevance to this discussion is the impact of the various rungs on the equation of state and the identification of possible discrepancies among the various methods. A preliminary analysis identifies a possible tension between laboratory measurements and gravitational-wave detections that could indicate the emergence of a phase transition in the stellar core.

# Building an Equation of State Density Ladder

## Key claim

The paper organizes theoretical, experimental, and observational constraints on the neutron-star equation of state into a "density ladder" analogous to the cosmic distance ladder, and identifies a possible tension between laboratory measurements (particularly PREX) and gravitational-wave detections (GW170817) that could indicate the emergence of a phase transition in the stellar core, though the authors note that current error bars are too large to make a definite statement.

## Method

The authors construct a set of covariant density functionals (FSUGold2 and FSUGarnet) and refine them using Bayesian inference that incorporates chiral effective field theory predictions for pure neutron matter, parity-violating electron scattering measurements of neutron skin thickness, NICER mass-radius determinations, LIGO-Virgo tidal deformability constraints, and pulsar timing mass measurements. The resulting functionals are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to generate mass-radius relations and to compute neutron skin thicknesses for comparison with PREX and CREX experimental results.

## Key numbers

- Chiral EFT prediction for the slope of the symmetry energy: L = (59.8 ± 4.1) MeV at the 1σ level (Drischler 2020)
- PREX neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm, implying L = (106 ± 37) MeV
- FSUGold2 prediction for 208Pb neutron skin before refinement: 0.285 fm; after refinement with χEFT input: 0.203 fm
- CREX neutron skin thickness of 48Ca: 0.121 ± 0.035 fm (experimental)
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star from GW170817: Λ_1.4 = 190 (+390, -120) at 90% confidence
- Mass of PSR J0740+6620: M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙
- Mass of PSR J0952-0607: M = 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙
- NICER radius determinations for both PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620: approximately 12.4 km
- Radius range for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star from PREX: 13.25 km ≲ R_1.4 ≲ 14.26 km

## Context

This work extends the density ladder framework introduced by Piekarewicz (2022) and builds on the covariant density functional models FSUGold2 (Chen 2014) and FSUGarnet (Chen 2014). The paper confronts these models with the PREX measurement of the 208Pb neutron skin (Adhikari 2021), the CREX measurement of the 48Ca neutron skin (Adhikari 2022), the GW170817 tidal deformability (LIGO-Virgo 2017, 2018), NICER mass-radius determinations for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2019, 2021; Miller 2019, 2021), and pulsar timing masses (Cromartie 2019; Fonseca 2021; Romani 2022). The analysis enables future experimental tests at the Mainz Energy-recovery Superconducting Accelerator (MESA) and highlights the need for a more precise determination of the 208Pb neutron skin to resolve the tension between laboratory and astrophysical constraints.
