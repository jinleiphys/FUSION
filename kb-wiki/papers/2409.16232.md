# Probing the equation of state of neutron stars using neutrino oscillations

> We study the phenomena of neutrino oscillations and flavour mixing by incorporating the gravitational effects through the Dirac equation in curved spacetime inside a spherically symmetric star. We show that the flavour transition probabilities of the neutrinos depend on the interior spacetime metric as they propagate out of the star. As a consequence, we show that one could distinguish between different possible equation of states of nuclear matter even for an isolated neutron star if one could determine the flavour composition of emitted neutrinos near the stellar surface.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the flavour transition probabilities of neutrinos propagating out of a neutron star depend on the interior spacetime metric, which in turn is determined by the nuclear matter equation of state (EOS). This dependence could allow one to distinguish between different possible EOSs of nuclear matter even for an isolated neutron star, provided the flavour composition of emitted neutrinos near the stellar surface can be measured.

## Method

The authors model neutrino propagation through a spherically symmetric neutron star spacetime by solving the Dirac equation in curved spacetime with a diagonal tetrad ansatz. They compute the phase difference between neutrino mass eigenstates along radial geodesics, incorporating both gravitational corrections and the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) matter effect via charged-current interactions with background electrons. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are used to relate the interior metric to the nuclear matter EOS.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic electron density inside a neutron star is \(n_e \sim \text{fm}^{-3}\), corresponding to an interaction potential \(V \sim 10^2\) eV.
- The energy gap between neutrino mass eigenstates at the relevant scale for neutron star radius is \(\Delta\varepsilon \sim 10^{-11}\) eV, giving a characteristic electron density \(n_{\varepsilon} \sim 10^{-13}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The parameter \(\mathcal{A}\) inside a typical neutron star becomes \(\mathcal{A} \sim 10^{13}\).
- The adiabatic approximation ratio inside a typical neutron star is \((\dot{\theta}_m/\Delta\varepsilon_m) \sim 10^{-25} \ll 1\), confirming the adiabatic condition is satisfied.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutrino oscillations in curved spacetime (Ahluwalia 1996, Grossman 1997, Zhang 2016, Mandal 2021) by explicitly linking the flavour transition probability to the interior metric of a neutron star and thus to the nuclear matter EOS. It also incorporates the MSW matter effect (Mikheev and Smirnov 1987, Wolfenstein 1978) in a curved spacetime setting. The method enables a potential new probe of neutron star EOS that does not rely on tidal deformation measurements, which are unavailable for isolated neutron stars.
