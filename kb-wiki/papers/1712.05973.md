# Nuclear skin and the curvature of the symmetry energy

> The effect of correlations between the slope and the curvature of the symmetry energy on ground state nuclear observables is studied within the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. We consider different isovector probes of the symmetry energy, with a special focus on the skin of $^{208}{\rm Pb}$. We use a recently proposed meta-modelling technique to generate a large number of equation of state models, where the empirical parameters are independently varied. The results are compared to a set of calculations using 17 different Skyrme interactions. We show that the curvature parameter plays a non-negligible role on the neutron skin, while the effect is reduced in Skyrme functionals because of the correlation with the slope parameter.

## Key claim

The curvature of the symmetry energy ($K_{sym}$) has a non-negligible effect on the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}{\rm Pb}$, but this effect is reduced in Skyrme functionals because of the strong correlation between $K_{sym}$ and the slope parameter $L_{sym}$ that is built into those functionals.

## Method

The authors used the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation at second order in $\hbar$ with parametrized Wood-Saxon density profiles to calculate ground-state properties of spherical nuclei. They employed a meta-modelling technique that generates equation of state models by independently varying the empirical parameters $n_{sat}$, $E_{sat}$, $K_{sat}$, $E_{sym}$, $L_{sym}$, $K_{sym}$, $m_{sat}^*$, and $C_{fin}$, then retained only models that reproduce experimental binding energies (within 5 MeV) and charge radii (within 0.10 fm) of spherical magic nuclei. Results were compared to calculations using 17 different Skyrme interactions (SKa, SKb, Rs, SkMP, SLy2, SLy9, SLy4, SLy230a, SkI2-6, SKOp, SK255, SK272, KDE0v1).

## Key numbers

- For the 17 Skyrme interactions, the symmetry energy slope spans $44.3 \leq L_{sym} \leq 129.3$ MeV and curvature spans $-127.2 \leq K_{sym} \leq 159.5$ MeV
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $K_{sym}$ and $L_{sym}$ for Skyrme functionals is $C(K_{sym},L_{sym})=0.87$
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $K_{sym}$ and $L_{sym}$ for the meta-model after applying mass and radius constraints is much weaker (no explicit value given, but described as "poor")
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $m_{sat}^*$ and $C_{fin}$ is $C=0.88$ for Skyrme and $C=0.81$ for the meta-model
- For the SLy4 functional, the ETF approximation yields $\chi(B) = 4.9$ MeV for binding energies and $\chi(R_{\rm ch}) = 4.1 \cdot 10^{-2}$ fm for charge radii of spherical nuclei
- The minimum standard deviation achieved by the meta-model for masses is 2.7 MeV and for charge radii is $2.07 \cdot 10^{-2}$ fm

## Context

This work extends the meta-modelling approach of JM1 (2014) and Debi (2017) to study the role of $K_{sym}$ on isovector observables, which had received little attention because most EoS models impose correlations between $K_{sym}$ and lower-order parameters. The study shows that the strong $L_{sym}$-$K_{sym}$ correlation observed in Skyrme functionals (also noted by Mondal (2017) and Tews (2017)) is not required by mass reproduction constraints, suggesting it arises from the specific functional form rather than physical necessity. This enables future work to disentangle the effects of $L_{sym}$ and $K_{sym}$ when extracting symmetry energy constraints from neutron skin measurements and neutron star observations.
