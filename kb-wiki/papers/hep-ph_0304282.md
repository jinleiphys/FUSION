# Vector Manifestation and Violation of Vector Dominance in Hot Matter

> We show the details of the calculation of the hadronic thermal corrections to the two-point functions in the effective field theory of QCD for pions and vector mesons based on the hidden local symmetry (HLS) in hot matter using the background field gauge. We study the temperature dependence of the pion velocity in the low temperature region determined from the hadronic thermal corrections, and show that, due to the presence of the dynamical vector meson, the pion velocity is smaller than the speed of the light already at one-loop level, in contrast to the result obtained in the ordinary chiral perturbation theory including only the pion at one-loop. Including the intrinsic temperature dependences of the parameters of the HLS Lagrangian determined from the underlying QCD through the Wilsonian matching, we show how the vector manifestation (VM), in which the massless vector meson becomes the chiral partner of pion, is realized at the critical temperature. We present a new prediction of the VM on the direct photon-$π$-$π$ coupling which measures the validity of the vector dominance (VD) of the electromagnetic form factor of the pion: We find that the VD is largely violated at the critical temperature, which indicates that the assumption of the VD made in several analyses on the dilepton spectra in hot matter may need to be weakened for consistently including the effect of the dropping mass of the vector meson.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, in the vector manifestation (VM) scenario of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature, vector dominance (VD) of the pion electromagnetic form factor is largely violated at the critical temperature, contrary to the assumption of VD made in several analyses of dilepton spectra in hot matter.

## Method

The authors calculated hadronic thermal corrections to the two-point functions of pions and vector mesons at one-loop level using the hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective field theory in the background field gauge. They incorporated intrinsic temperature dependences of the HLS Lagrangian parameters through Wilsonian matching to QCD, and studied the temperature dependence of the pion velocity and the direct photon-pion-pion coupling near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The pion velocity at one-loop level is smaller than the speed of light due to the presence of the dynamical vector meson, in contrast to ordinary chiral perturbation theory including only pions at one-loop.
- The bare pion decay constant at the matching scale satisfies \(F^2_\pi (\Lambda ;T_c) \neq 0\) at the critical temperature, as derived from the Wilsonian matching condition.
- The VM conditions require \(g(\Lambda ;T) \to 0\) and \(a(\Lambda ;T) = F_\sigma^2(\Lambda;T)/F_\pi^2(\Lambda;T) \to 1\) as \(T \to T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation scenario previously formulated at large number of flavors (Harada and Yamawaki, 2001), at critical temperature (Harada and Sasaki, 2002), and at critical density (Harada, Kim, and Rho, 2002). It contradicts the assumption of vector dominance used in analyses of dilepton spectra in hot matter (Rapp and Wambach, 2000) and shows that the dropping mass of the vector meson may require weakening the VD assumption. The results enable future studies of dilepton spectra in hot matter that consistently include the violation of vector dominance near the critical temperature.
