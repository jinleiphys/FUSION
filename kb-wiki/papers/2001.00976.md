# First Exploration of Neutron Shell Structure Below Lead and Beyond $\boldsymbol{N=126}$

> The nuclei below lead but with more than 126 neutrons are crucial to an understanding of the astrophysical $r$-process in producing nuclei heavier than $A\sim190$. Despite their importance, the structure and properties of these nuclei remain experimentally untested as they are difficult to produce in nuclear reactions with stable beams. In a first exploration of the shell structure of this region, neutron excitations in $^{207}$Hg have been probed using the neutron-adding ($d$,$p$) reaction in inverse kinematics. The radioactive beam of $^{206}$Hg was delivered to the new ISOLDE Solenoidal Spectrometer at an energy above the Coulomb barrier. The spectroscopy of $^{207}$Hg marks a first step in improving our understanding of the relevant structural properties of nuclei involved in a key part of the path of the $r$-process.

# First Exploration of Neutron Shell Structure Below Lead and Beyond N=126

## Key claim

The paper reports the first direct measurement of single-neutron excitations in a nucleus below lead (Z=82) and beyond the N=126 neutron shell closure, specifically in 207Hg, but the spectroscopic factor assignments are tentative (denoted with parentheses in the table) and the angular distributions at the beam energy used (7.38 MeV/u) are described as "relatively indistinct," making the l-value assignments provisional.

## Method

The authors used the neutron-adding (d,p) transfer reaction in inverse kinematics, bombarding a radioactive beam of 206Hg at 7.38 MeV/u onto deuterated polyethylene targets at the ISOLDE Solenoidal Spectrometer (ISS) at CERN. Approximately 6000 protons from the 206Hg(d,p)207Hg reaction were identified from about 1.5x10^11 incident 206Hg ions over 82 hours of beam time, with the excitation-energy spectrum reconstructed using the solenoidal spectrometer technique in a 2.5-T magnetic field. Distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with the code Ptolemy were used to extract spectroscopic factors and assign l-values to the observed states.

## Key numbers

- Seven states were observed below 3 MeV excitation energy in 207Hg, with the ground state identified as l=4, Jpi=9/2+ at 0 keV
- The 3s1/2 state was observed at 1960(30) keV excitation energy, with its spectroscopic factor normalized to S=1.00
- The 2d5/2 strength is fragmented across three states at 1195(20), 1600(45), and 1810(20) keV, with a centroid at 1500(50) keV and summed spectroscopic factor of 1.02(17)
- The 2d3/2 state was observed at 2335(20) keV with S=1.00(17)
- The 1g7/2 state was observed at 2530(20) keV with S=0.62(12)
- The Q-value resolution was approximately 140 keV FWHM
- The beam energy was 1.52 GeV (7.38 MeV/u), with beam intensity of 3 to 8x10^5 206Hg ions per second
- The 206Hg beam was contaminated with 130Xe at a level of less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the study of single-neutron structure beyond N=126 for elements below lead, a region previously only probed by one transfer-reaction study using a long-lived 210Pb target (Ellegaard, 1976) that provided limited information on proton-hole states in 209Tl. The results enable comparison with the known single-neutron orbitals in 209Pb and 211Po, and provide experimental constraints for Woods-Saxon potential calculations that extrapolate the neutron binding energies along the N=127 isotonic chain. The data inform predictions of where the N=126 r-process bottleneck occurs, suggesting that below gadolinium (Z=64) the N=127 nuclei become unbound, which has implications for direct neutron-capture rates in the astrophysical r-process path that produces nuclei heavier than A~190.
