# Analysis of coupled-channel potentials with quark and hadron degrees of freedom

> The quark-antiquark potentials are known to be confining in the absence of the $\bar{q}q$ pair creation. On the other hand, the inter-hadron potentials vanish at large distance, because the interaction range is limited by the inverse pion mass. When the $\bar{q}q$ pair creation and annihilation are switched on, the channel coupling occurs between the quark and hadron degrees of freedom, modifying the behavior of potentials. In this work, we investigate the properties of the effective potentials obtained by eliminating one of the channels. We show that the coupling to the eliminated channel induces a non-local and energy dependent effective potential, irrespective of the properties of the transition potential. In addition, when the hadron channel having continuous scattering eigenstates is eliminated, the resulting inter-quark potential contains an imaginary part above the threshold to describe the decay effect into the meson pair.

# Analysis of coupled-channel potentials with quark and hadron degrees of freedom

## Key claim

The effective single-channel potentials obtained by eliminating either the quark or hadron channel are necessarily non-local and energy-dependent, regardless of the properties of the transition potential; moreover, when the hadron channel with continuous scattering eigenstates is eliminated, the resulting inter-quark potential acquires an imaginary part above threshold, but this imaginary part is lost if one naively applies a derivative expansion to obtain a local potential.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation for a quark-antiquark (ccbar) channel with a confining potential and a hadron-hadron (DDbar) channel with a potential that vanishes at large distance, coupled by a transition potential. Using the Feshbach method, they derive the effective single-channel Hamiltonians for each channel and examine the coordinate-space representation of the resulting effective potentials, including an analytic integration for the case of vanishing diagonal DDbar potential.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for the DDbar channel is given by Δ, with the lowest continuum state at E₀ = Δ.
- The analytic effective ccbar potential in the absence of the diagonal DDbar potential contains a term proportional to exp[-√(m_D(Δ - E) - i0⁺) |r' - r|] / (4π|r' - r|), where m_D is the D meson mass.
- The imaginary part of the effective ccbar potential appears for energies E ≥ Δ, described by √(m_D(Δ - E) - i0⁺) = -i√(m_D(E - Δ)).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of string breaking in static QQbar potentials studied in lattice QCD by Bali (2005) and the finite-quark-mass QQbar potentials studied by Ikeda (2011). The formulation clarifies how meson-meson thresholds modify the confining potential and how quark dynamics affect hadron potentials, with potential applications to systems such as the X(3872) charmonium state described as DDbar* scattering coupled to a ccbar core, and to the H dibaryon in strangeness S = -2 baryon-baryon scattering where six-quark states may appear.
