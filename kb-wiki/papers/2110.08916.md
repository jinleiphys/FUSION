# Low Density Neutron Star Matter with Quantum Molecular Dynamics: The Role of Vector Interactions

> The effect of isospin-dependent nuclear forces on the inner crust of neutron stars is modeled within the framework of Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD). To successfully control the density dependence of the symmetry energy of neutron-star matter below nuclear saturation density, a coupling potential between the $ω$ and $ρ$ meson fields is introduced. This approach is inspired by the baryon density and isospin density-dependent repulsive Skyrme force of asymmetric nuclear matter. In isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, the system shows nucleation, as nucleons are arranged into shapes resembling nuclear pasta. The dependence of clusterization in the system on the isospin properties is also explored by calculating two-point correlation functions. We show that, as compared to previous results that did not involve the $ω$-$ρ$ potential, the energy symmetry slope $L$ is successfully controlled by varying the $ω$-$ρ$ coupling strength. Nevertheless, the effect of changing the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy $L$ on the crust-core transition density does not seem significant. To the knowledge of the authors, This is the first implementation of a $ω$-$ρ$ coupling in a QMD model for the inner crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim
By introducing a coupling potential between the omega and rho meson fields in a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model, the authors show they can successfully control the symmetry energy slope L in low-density neutron star matter, but they find that varying L does not significantly affect the crust-core transition density.

## Method
The authors performed QMD simulations of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at densities below nuclear saturation, incorporating a new omega-rho meson coupling term inspired by density-dependent repulsive Skyrme forces. They analyzed cluster formation (nuclear pasta phases) in the inner crust by calculating two-point correlation functions, and systematically varied the omega-rho coupling strength to study its effect on the symmetry energy slope L and the crust-core transition density.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy slope L was successfully controlled by varying the omega-rho coupling strength, with the model allowing tuning of L values.
- The crust-core transition density showed no significant dependence on the value of L when the omega-rho coupling was varied.
- The system exhibited nucleation and nuclear pasta-like clustering in isospin-asymmetric matter, as characterized by two-point correlation functions.

## Context
This work extends previous QMD studies of neutron star crusts that did not include omega-rho coupling, and is inspired by the density-dependent repulsive Skyrme force approach used in asymmetric nuclear matter models. The authors claim this is the first implementation of omega-rho coupling in a QMD model for the inner crust. The results enable future studies of how vector meson interactions affect pasta phase formation and neutron star properties, though the weak dependence of the crust-core transition on L suggests other factors may dominate the transition density.
