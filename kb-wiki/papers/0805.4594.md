# Radiative and Collisional Jet Energy Loss in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We calculate radiative and collisional energy loss of hard partons traversing the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC and compare the respective size of these contributions. We employ the AMY formalism for radiative energy loss and include additionally energy loss by elastic collisions. Our treatment of both processes is complete at leading order in the coupling, and accounts for the probabilistic nature of jet energy loss. We find that a solution of the Fokker-Planck equation for the probability density distributions of partons is necessary for a complete calculation of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for pion production in heavy ion collisions. It is found that the magnitude of $R_{AA}$ is sensitive to the inclusion of both collisional and radiative energy loss, while the average energy is less affected by the addition of collisional contributions. We present a calculation of $R_{AA}$ for $Ď€^0$ at RHIC, combining our energy loss formalism with a relativistic (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic description of the thermalized medium.

# Radiative and Collisional Jet Energy Loss in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The magnitude of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for $\pi^0$ at RHIC is sensitive to the inclusion of both collisional and radiative energy loss, even though the average energy loss is dominated by radiative processes and the shape of $R_{AA}$ is not strongly altered by adding collisional contributions.

## Method
The authors employed the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) formalism to compute radiative energy loss of hard partons, and incorporated elastic collisional energy loss via a drag-plus-diffusion term that reproduces the leading-order mean energy loss rate and satisfies detailed balance. The evolution of the parton momentum distribution was obtained by solving a Fokker-Planck type rate equation, and the resulting jet distributions were combined with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model of the thermalized medium to compute $R_{AA}$ for $\pi^0$ production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV.

## Key numbers
- The strong coupling constant was fixed at $\alpha_s = 0.27$ to reproduce the most central data.
- $R_{AA}$ was calculated for $\pi^0$ at mid-rapidity for impact parameters $b = 2.4$ fm (most central, 0-5%) and $b = 7.5$ fm (mid-central, 20-30%) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV.
- A single quark jet with initial energy $E_i = 16$ GeV starting from the center of the medium ($b = 2.4$ fm) was used to illustrate the final probability distribution $P(E, t_f)$.
- The mean energy loss by elastic collisions at leading order is given by $dE/dt = C_{\rm coll} \pi \alpha_s^2 T^2 [\ln(ET/m_g^2) + D_{\rm coll}]$, where $m_g^2 = 2\pi \alpha_s T^2 (1 + N_f/6)$ is the thermal gluon mass and $C_{\rm coll}$, $D_{\rm coll}$ are ${\cal O}(1)$ constants.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculation of $R_{AA}$ using only radiative energy loss in the AMY formalism (Qin 2007) by consistently including elastic collisional energy loss in the same framework. The results confirm the finding of Zakharov (2007) that induced gluon radiation leads to much larger mean energy loss than elastic collisions. The study enables more complete comparisons with PHENIX data for $\pi^0$ suppression at RHIC and provides a foundation for future calculations of jet quenching observables that require accurate parton distribution functions rather than just average energy loss.
