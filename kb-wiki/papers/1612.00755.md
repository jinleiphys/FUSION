# Conditions for the existence of stable strange quark matter

> We discuss the possible existence of absolutely stable strange quark matter within three different types of chiral models. We will show that confinement plays a crucial role in determining the conditions for the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis to hold true. We discuss also which are the phenomenological signatures, related to measurements of masses and radii of compact stars, which would prove the existence of strange quark stars.

# Conditions for the existence of stable strange quark matter

## Key claim
The Bodmer-Witten hypothesis (that strange quark matter is absolutely stable) can be fulfilled only in quark models that implement both dynamical confinement and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking simultaneously; in pure chiral models without confinement, the hypothesis fails because the strange quark mass does not decrease sufficiently at densities near nuclear saturation.

## Method
The authors analyze three SU(3) chiral quark models within the Hartree approximation: the basic Chiral Model (CM), the Scaled Chiral Model (SCM) with a dilaton field, and the Chiral Chromodielectric Model (CCDM) which incorporates dynamical confinement via a color dielectric field. They compute the density dependence of the strange quark mass and the energy per baryon for beta-stable, charge neutral quark matter, fixing parameters by reproducing the tree-level pseudoscalar meson mass spectrum. For the CCDM, they additionally introduce vector meson interactions to compute the mass-radius relation of compact stars.

## Key numbers
- Within the CCDM, the minimum of the energy per baryon is E/A ~ 913 MeV, realized at a density slightly larger than nuclear saturation density.
- The maximum mass of a strange quark star obtained with the CCDM equation of state is ~1.94 solar masses, compatible with the measured mass of PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses).
- For the constant-speed-of-sound parametrization, the baryon density of strange quark matter droplets must satisfy n0 ≥ n_sat (nuclear saturation density) and the energy per baryon must lie in the range 830 MeV ≤ E/A < 930 MeV for absolute stability while preserving iron stability.
- A maximum mass of 2.4 solar masses (as inferred for the black widow pulsar) can be achieved with n0 = 1.12 n_sat and E/A = 900 MeV.
- A radius R1.4 ≤ 11 km for a 1.4 solar mass compact star would indicate the existence of strange quark stars coexisting with hadronic stars.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing Bodmer-Witten hypothesis (Bodmer, 1971; Witten, 1984) by showing that confinement is essential for its realization, contradicting earlier results from the NJL model (Buballa, 1998) and recent analyses (Klahn, 2015) which found no stable strange quark matter in chiral models without confinement. The paper builds on the two-families scenario proposed by Drago (2013, 2015) where compact hadronic stars and strange quark stars coexist, and addresses the tension noted by Kurkela (2014) and Alford (2015) between radius constraints and maximum mass requirements. Future measurements from NICER and other experiments could test the prediction that R1.4 ≤ 11 km would prove the existence of strange quark stars.
