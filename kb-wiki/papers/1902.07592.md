# A systematic study of the factors affecting central depletion in nuclei

> A systematic study of the central depletion of proton density has been performed in the isotonic chains of nuclei with neutron numbers $N = 20$ and $28$ using different variants of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. These models include either the non-linear contributions from the mesons with the coupling constants being density independent or the non-linearity of the mesonic fields realized through the density dependent coupling strengths. The central depletion in deformed nuclei tends to disappear irrespective of the occupancy of $2s_{1/2}$ state in contrast to the spherical nuclei in which the unoccupancy of $2s_{1/2}$ state leads to the central depletion. Due to the differences in the strength of spin-orbit potentials in these models, the central depletions are found to be model dependent. The influence of the central depletion on the neutron-skin thickness is also investigated. It appears that the effects of the central depletion do not percolate far enough to display its finger prints on the trends of the neutron-skin thickness.

## Key claim

The central depletion of proton density in nuclei is a complex phenomenon influenced by the combined effects of neutron-to-proton ratio, pairing correlations, deformation, and occupancy of the \(2s_{1/2}\) state; however, the occupancy of the \(2s_{1/2}\) state is a necessary but not sufficient condition for central depletion, and deformation plays a predominant role in quenching the bubble effect even when the \(2s_{1/2}\) state is unoccupied.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study using six variants of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model: NL3, NL3*, FSU-Gold, FSU-Garnet (non-linear meson interactions with density-independent couplings), and DD-ME2, DD-PC1 (density-dependent coupling strengths). They calculated the proton bubble parameter \(b_p = 1 - \rho_{p,c}/\rho_{p,max}\) for isotonic chains with neutron numbers \(N = 20\) and \(28\), including spherical nuclei (\(^{30}\)Ne, \(^{32}\)Mg, \(^{34}\)Si, \(^{46}\)Ar) and deformed nuclei (\(^{40}\)Mg, \(^{42}\)Si, \(^{44}\)S), and also performed a systematic study of the full isotopic chain of Si (\(^{24-48}\)Si) using the DD-ME2 model.

## Key numbers

* For spherical nuclei in the \(N = 20\) isotonic chain, the bubble parameter \(b_p\) values across models range from approximately 0.2 to 0.4, with \(^{34}\)Si showing \(b_p\) values from 0.27 (DD-PC1) to 0.41 (FSU-Gold).
* For the deformed nuclei \(^{40}\)Mg, \(^{42}\)Si, and \(^{44}\)S at their respective energy minima (DD-ME2 model), the bubble parameters are \(b_p = 0.15\), 0.08, and 0.16, respectively, with deformation parameters \(\beta = 0.4\) (prolate for \(^{40}\)Mg and \(^{44}\)S) and \(\beta = -0.4\) (oblate for \(^{42}\)Si).
* The bubble parameter \(b_p\) for \(^{48}\)Si (spherical, \(N = 34\)) is higher than that for the well-known bubble nucleus \(^{34}\)Si, with both having zero occupancy of the \(2s_{1/2}\) state and zero deformation.
* The nuclear matter properties of the RMF models include saturation densities \(\rho_{sat}\) ranging from 0.148 fm\(^{-3}\) (FSU-Gold) to 0.153 fm\(^{-3}\) (FSU-Garnet), and symmetry energy \(J\) values ranging from 30.9 MeV (FSU-Garnet) to 38.6 MeV (NL3*).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of bubble nuclei using RMF and other models (Mutschler 2016, Duguet 2016, Schuetrumpf 2017, Khan 2007, Grasso 2009) and addresses the mechanism of central depletion weakening in deformed nuclei, which was noted by Shukla (2014) but not investigated in detail. The finding that central depletion does not noticeably affect the systematics of neutron-skin thickness with asymmetry parameter \((N-Z)/A\) enables future studies to use neutron-skin systematics without needing to account for bubble effects, and the identification of \(^{48}\)Si as a strong bubble candidate motivates experimental searches for bubble structures in neutron-rich nuclei.
