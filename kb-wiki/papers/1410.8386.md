# QCD and nuclear physics. How to explain the coincidence between the action radius of nuclear forces and the characteristic scale of the neutron-neutron electrostatic interaction?

> An attempt is made to interpret, in the framework of QCD, the likeness between the shapes of the neutron-neutron strong and electrostatic interactions at the distances $\sim 1÷3$ fm.

# QCD and nuclear physics. How to explain the coincidence between the action radius of nuclear forces and the characteristic scale of the neutron-neutron electrostatic interaction?

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the striking similarity between the shapes of the neutron-neutron strong and electrostatic potentials at distances of 1-3 fm can be explained within QCD if the isolated nucleon state vector is not a color singlet but instead has a specific color structure that makes the distribution of the effective nuclear charge in the nucleon proportional to the electric charge distribution in the neutron. This explanation depends on two basic hypotheses: that a specific gauge and renormalization scheme exists where the nucleon state vector takes the proposed non-color-singlet form, and that weakly connected parts of effective quark-quark interactions dominate nucleon-nucleon interaction at distances above 0.5 fm.

## Method

The author parametrizes the electric charge distributions inside the proton and neutron using two exponential functions with radii a = 0.2 fm and b = 0.225 fm, then computes the resulting neutron-neutron electrostatic potential energy as a function of distance. The QCD analysis proceeds by proposing a specific form for the nucleon state vector (equation 1) where the color part is not a singlet but rather a particular antisymmetric combination, and showing that under this state vector, nucleons act as sources only of the 8th component of the gluon octet, with effective charges of -1 for u-quarks in proton and d-quarks in neutron, and +2 for d-quark in proton and u-quark in neutron. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is then represented as the sum of weakly connected parts of constituent quark pair interactions with this effective charge structure.

## Key numbers

* The electric charge distribution parameters: a = 0.2 fm and b = 0.225 fm for the exponential parametrization of nucleon charge densities.
* The minimum of the neutron-neutron electrostatic potential energy occurs at R_min ≈ 1.4 fm, which coincides with the action radius of nuclear forces.
* The test case for nucleon-nucleon interaction uses V^CS(r) = 3000/r (with V^CM = 0) to produce the qualitative shape shown in Figure 3.
* The effective charges in the proposed nucleon structure: Q = -1 for u-quarks in proton and d-quarks in neutron, Q = +2 for d-quark in proton and u-quark in neutron.

## Context

This work attempts to provide a QCD-based explanation for a phenomenological coincidence between strong and electrostatic interaction scales in the neutron-neutron system, contrasting with conventional approaches such as chiral perturbation theory, lattice QCD, the Skyrme model, relativistic mean field theory, QCD sum rules, nonrelativistic models with realistic potentials, contact interaction models, and bag models (as reviewed in a pedagogical review cited by the author). The proposed mechanism is analogous to the short-rangeness of interaction between helium atoms due to effective integral neutrality, rather than the meson-exchange picture where short-rangeness comes from meson field massiveness. The approach requires addressing the color degeneracy problem by suggesting possible spontaneous or dynamical breaking of color symmetry (SU(3) → SU(2) or SU(3) → SU(2) ⊗ U(1)), and predicts that if the 8th gluon component is massless, gluons radiated in nonrelativistic nuclear collisions would have energy spectra similar to photons from neutron-neutron scattering but with multiplicities differing by orders of magnitude due to the stronger coupling.
