# Mean first passage time for fission potentials having structure

> A schematic model of over-damped motion is presented which permits one to calculate the mean first passage time for nuclear fission. Its asymptotic value may exceed considerably the lifetime suggested by Kramers rate formula, which applies only to very special, favorable potentials and temperatures. The additional time obtained in the more general case is seen to allow for a considerable increment in the emission of light particles.

## Key claim

The mean first passage time (MFPT) for nuclear fission can considerably exceed the lifetime predicted by Kramers' rate formula when the fission potential has additional structure beyond a simple barrier, even for over-damped motion where Kramers' formula is normally considered applicable; this extra time allows for enhanced emission of light particles before scission.

## Method

The authors used a schematic model of over-damped motion with constant friction and temperature, applying an analytic formula for the mean first passage time from the potential minimum to an exit point beyond the barrier. They computed the MFPT for a cubic potential and for fifth-order potentials with additional shoulders or a second minimum and maximum, comparing the asymptotic MFPT to the inverse of Kramers' rate formula.

## Key numbers

- For a cubic potential with barrier height \(E_{\text{b}} = 8\) MeV, the asymptotic ratio \(\tau_{\text{mfpt}}/\tau_{\text{K}}\) becomes close to unity only when the temperature over barrier height is small enough, with deviations visible at larger temperatures.
- For a potential with a shoulder 20 MeV below the first minimum (or \(-2.5 E_{\text{b}}\) in terms of barrier height), the ratio \(\tau_{\text{mfpt}}/\tau_{\text{K}}\) shows a considerable overshoot over unity.
- The estimated Kramers time \(\tau_{\text{K}}\) at \(T/E_{\text{b}} \simeq 0.5\) is about \(200\,\hbar /\text{MeV}\), and with a typical \(\tau_{\text{mfpt}}/\tau_{\text{K}}\) of about 1.5, the additional time increment \(\Delta\tau_{\text{f}} = \tau_{\text{mfpt}} - \tau_{\text{K}}\) is roughly \(100\,\hbar /\text{MeV}\).
- The prefactor \(\gamma / \sqrt{C_{\text{a}}|C_{\text{b}}|}\) was estimated as \(\overline{\tau_{\text{coll}}} \simeq -3/4 + (5/4) T\) in units of \(\hbar/\text{MeV}\), valid between temperatures of 1 and 4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hofmann and Ivanyuk (2002) on the mean first passage time for fission, and addresses the question raised by H. Hofmann (1997) about whether Kramers' original rate formula applies to realistic fission situations. The results show that for potentials with structure beyond a simple barrier, the MFPT approach provides a more accurate estimate of the time the fissioning system stays together than Kramers' formula, and this extra time can account for additional light particle emission without relying on time-dependent concepts that depend on initial conditions. The authors note that further work is needed with more realistic potentials and coordinate-dependent friction, with the ultimate goal of incorporating such a formula into statistical codes that account for changing temperatures during the fission process.
