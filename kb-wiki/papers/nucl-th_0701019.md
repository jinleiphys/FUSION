# A Refined Numerical Result on the First Excitation Energy in the Two-Level Pairing Model

> The first excitation energy in the two-level pairing model is investigated in terms of the equilibrium and the small fluctuation around it. The basic idea is an extension of results presented in a previous paper by the present authors. In this investigation, the result obtained in the previous paper plays a central role: At the limit of the weak and the strong interaction strength, the results are in good agreement with the exact one. The former and the latter are calculated in the framework of the $su(2)\otimes su(1,1)$- and the $su(2)\otimes su(2)$-coherent state, respectively. On the basis of the above conclusion, the intermediate region for the interaction strength is described in terms of the idea of the interpolation and it is shown that the agreement of the result with the exact one is quite good.

## Key claim

The paper presents an interpolated function for the first excitation energy of the two-level pairing model that matches the exact result well across all interaction strengths, not just in the weak and strong limits where the earlier separate coherent-state approaches (su(2)⊗su(1,1) and su(2)⊗su(2)) were each individually accurate.

## Method

The authors start from the observation that the classical c-number replacement of the Hamiltonian depends on an arbitrary function g(K), leading to infinitely many possible classical solutions. They construct an interpolated function μ(K) that asymptotically matches the behavior of the su(2)⊗su(1,1) coherent state near K=0 and the su(2)⊗su(2) coherent state near K=Ω/2, then refine it with an additional factor ρ(K) to improve the quadratic term in the expansion around K=Ω/2. The first excitation energy is obtained as the frequency of small oscillations around the equilibrium state described by the coherent state.

## Key numbers

- For Ω=5, the interpolation parameters are n=2.8441, A=0.5020, B=5.9064 (from Eq. 15)
- For Ω=5, the refinement parameter C=0.4260 (from Eq. 23)
- The function μ_i(K) for the su(2)⊗su(1,1) case is μ_i(K)=√[K(K+1)](Ω-K) (Eq. 4)
- The function μ_iii(K) for the su(2)⊗su(2) case is μ_iii(K)=K(Ω-K) (Eq. 5)
- The leading term of μ_i(K) near K=0 is √K·Ω (Eq. 8)
- The leading term of μ_iii(K) near K=Ω/2 is (Ω/2)² (Eq. 9)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (I) (Tsue, Providencia, da Providencia, Yamamura, 2006) which calculated ground-state and first excitation energies using three different coherent states but found good agreement only in the weak (su(2)⊗su(1,1) coherent state) or strong (su(2)⊗su(2) coherent state) interaction limits. The interpolation method bridges these two regimes and shows that the symmetry changes gradually from su(2)⊗su(1,1) to su(2)⊗su(2) as interaction strength increases, enabling future investigation of which coherent state can describe this symmetry change.
