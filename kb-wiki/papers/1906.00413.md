# Flavor hierarchy of jet quenching in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Relativistic heavy-ion experiments have observed similar quenching effects for (prompt) $D$ mesons compared to charged hadrons for transverse momenta larger than 6-8~GeV, which remains a mystery since heavy quarks typically lose less energies in quark-gluon plasma than light quarks and gluons. Recent measurements of the nuclear modification factors of $B$ mesons and $B$-decayed $D$ mesons by the CMS Collaboration provide a unique opportunity to study the flavor hierarchy of jet quenching. Using a linear Boltzmann transport model combined with hydrodynamics simulation, we study the energy loss and nuclear modification for heavy and light flavor jets in high-energy nuclear collisions. By consistently taking into account both quark and gluon contributions to light and heavy flavor hadron productions within a next-to-leading order perturbative QCD framework, we obtain, for the first time, a satisfactory description of the experimental data on the nuclear modification factors for charged hadrons, $D$ mesons, $B$ mesons and $B$-decayed $D$ mesons simultaneously over a wide range of transverse momenta (8-300~GeV). This presents a solid solution to the flavor puzzle of jet quenching and constitutes a significant step towards the precision study of jet-medium interaction. Our study predicts that at transverse momenta larger than 30-40~GeV, $B$ mesons also exhibit similar suppression effects to charged hadrons and $D$ mesons, which may be tested by future measurements.

## Key claim

By consistently including both quark and gluon fragmentation contributions to light and heavy flavor hadron production within a next-to-leading order perturbative QCD framework, the authors obtain the first simultaneous description of nuclear modification factors for charged hadrons, D mesons, B mesons, and B-decayed D mesons over a wide transverse momentum range (8-300 GeV), resolving the flavor hierarchy puzzle of jet quenching.

## Method

The authors combine a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD framework for initial parton and hadron production with a linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model that simulates both elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (medium-induced gluon radiation via higher-twist formalism) interactions between jet partons and the quark-gluon plasma. The space-time evolution of the QGP fireball is described using the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics model CLVisc, with initial-state nuclear shadowing accounted for via EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The NLO pQCD calculation describes charged hadron and D meson spectra in p+p collisions at 5.02 TeV, with gluon fragmentation contributing approximately 40% to D meson yield at pT = 100 GeV.
- For central 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV, the model describes charged hadron RAA over pT = 8-300 GeV and D meson RAA over pT = 8-100 GeV, using strong coupling αs = 0.2 for vertices connecting to thermal partons and running coupling for jet parton vertices with Λ = 0.2 GeV.
- The model predicts that at transverse momenta larger than 30-40 GeV, B mesons exhibit similar suppression effects to charged hadrons and D mesons, with B mesons showing less suppression below this threshold due to the mass effect.
- The transport coefficient for inelastic radiation uses charm quark mass Mc = 1.27 GeV and bottom quark mass Mb = 4.19 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Cao et al. (2016, 2017) that built a systematic framework for heavy and light jet parton evolution but neglected gluon contributions to heavy flavor hadron production. It addresses the flavor hierarchy puzzle raised by experimental observations from the CMS Collaboration (2017, 2018) that prompt D mesons show similar quenching to charged hadrons at pT > 6-8 GeV, contrary to expectations from mass-dependent energy loss. The study enables precision jet quenching studies and quantitative extraction of QGP properties, with predictions testable by future high-luminosity LHC measurements.
