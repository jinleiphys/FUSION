# Mapping exchange in relativistic Hartree-Fock

> We show that formally for the standard ansatz relativistic point-coupling mean-field (RMF-PC) model a lagrangian density L is not equivalent in Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximations. The equivalency can be achieved only if we use a ``complete'' ansatz at the cost of introducing new parameters in the model. An approximate treatment of the exchange terms from standard RMF-PC indicates that these effects cannot be easily, if at all, absorbed by a Dirac-Hartree approximation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard ansatz for the relativistic point-coupling mean-field (RMF-PC) Lagrangian density is formally not equivalent in Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximations; equivalence can only be achieved by using a "complete" ansatz that introduces new parameters, and an approximate treatment of exchange terms from the standard RMF-PC model indicates these effects cannot be easily absorbed by a Dirac-Hartree approximation.

## Method

The authors start from the standard RMF-PC Lagrangian with linear and nonlinear point-coupling terms, then compute the expectation value in a Slater determinant and apply Fierz transformations to express exchange contributions as effective Hartree-like currents. They perform an order-of-magnitude analysis in the plane-wave (nuclear matter) limit and use a Gordon decomposition to derive an approximate Lagrangian density that maps the Hartree-Fock exchange terms onto a Dirac-Hartree structure, neglecting higher-order derivative and retardation effects.

## Key numbers

- The nonderivative exchange term \(\epsilon_{ex 1}\) is of order \(v^0\) (same order as the direct nonderivative term \(\epsilon_1\)), with \(\epsilon_{ex 1} \sim -\frac{1}{2}c_v \alpha_V \bar{\rho_0}^2\) where \(c_v \leq 1\)
- The derivative exchange terms \(\epsilon_{ex 2}^1\) and \(\epsilon_{ex 2}^2\) are of order \(v^2\), with \(\epsilon_{ex 2}^1 \sim -c_v \delta_V \frac{3.4}{6.02}\bar{\rho_0}^2 p_F^2\) and \(\epsilon_{ex 2}^2 \sim c_v \delta_V \frac{3.6}{6.02}\bar{\rho_0}^2 p_F^2\)
- In nuclear matter, \(|\vec{\mathcal B}_i|/(m_B \bar{\rho_i})\) is of order \(v\) and \(|\mathcal C_i|/(m_B^2 \bar{\rho_i})\) is of order \(v^2\), where \(v = p_F/m_v < 1\)

## Context

This work extends the point-coupling model studies of Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland (1992) and Rusnak and Furnstahl (1995) by explicitly examining whether exchange effects can be absorbed into a Hartree approximation. The authors show that, contrary to earlier assumptions, the exchange contributions from the standard RMF-PC ansatz produce new terms (tensor terms, isovector-scalar terms, and density-mixing nonlinearities) that cannot be fully absorbed by redefining coupling constants. This enables future quantitative comparisons of Hartree and Hartree-Fock point-coupling models after refitting parameters, and suggests that including these exchange effects may allow a larger effective mass closer to values predicted by Skyrme forces.
