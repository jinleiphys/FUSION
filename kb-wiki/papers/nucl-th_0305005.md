# On the uncertainty in the $0νββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements

> The nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ of the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) are evaluated for $^{76}$Ge,$^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe within the Renormalized Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) and the simple QRPA. Three sets of single particle level schemes are used, ranging in size from 9 to 23 orbits. When the strength of the particle-particle interaction is adjusted so that the $2νββ$ decay rate is correctly reproduced, the resulting $M^{0ν}$ values become essentially independent on the size of the basis, and on the form of different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. Thus, one of the main reasons for variability of the calculated $M^{0ν}$ within these methods is eliminated.

# On the uncertainty in the $0νββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements

## Key claim

When the particle-particle interaction strength $g_{pp}$ is adjusted to reproduce the known $2νββ$ decay half-life for each nucleus and each single-particle basis, the resulting $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ become essentially independent of the size of the single-particle basis (ranging from 9 to 23 orbits) and of the choice among three realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn-CD, Argonne, Nijmegen). This stabilization removes one of the main sources of variability in QRPA-based calculations, though the authors note the procedure is "rather crude" and cannot guarantee the method is trustworthy.

## Method

The authors evaluated $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ for $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe using both the standard QRPA and the Renormalized QRPA (RQRPA). For each nucleus, three single-particle bases were considered (ranging from 9 to 23 orbits, corresponding to $1\hbarω$ to $4\hbarω$ excitations), and three realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn-CD, Argonne, Nijmegen) were used to generate the G-matrix interaction. The particle-particle strength parameter $g_{pp}$ was adjusted separately for each combination of nucleus, basis size, and potential so that the calculated $2νββ$ half-life matched the experimentally measured value (or lower limit for $^{136}$Xe). The pairing strength $g_{pair}$ was adjusted to reproduce pairing gaps, while the particle-hole strength $g_{ph}$ was fixed at 1.0. Short-range correlations were included using a standard correlation function with parameters $a = 1.1$ fm$^{-2}$ and $b = 0.68$ fm$^{-2}$.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge: average RQRPA matrix element $\langle M^{0ν} \rangle = 2.40$ with variance $\sigma = 0.07$; predicted half-life $T_{1/2} = 2.3 \times 10^{27}$ y for $\langle m_ν \rangle = 50$ meV
- For $^{100}$Mo: average RQRPA matrix element $\langle M^{0ν} \rangle = 1.16$ with $\sigma = 0.11$; predicted half-life $T_{1/2} = 1.4 \times 10^{27}$ y for $\langle m_ν \rangle = 50$ meV
- For $^{130}$Te: average RQRPA matrix element $\langle M^{0ν} \rangle = 1.29$ with $\sigma = 0.11$; predicted half-life $T_{1/2} = 1.1 \times 10^{27}$ y for $\langle m_ν \rangle = 50$ meV
- For $^{136}$Xe: average RQRPA matrix element $\langle M^{0ν} \rangle = 0.98$ with $\sigma = 0.09$; predicted half-life $T_{1/2} = 1.9 \times 10^{27}$ y for $\langle m_ν \rangle = 50$ meV
- Phase-space factors $G^{0ν}$ used: $0.30 \times 10^{-25}$ y$^{-1}$eV$^{-2}$ for $^{76}$Ge, $2.19 \times 10^{-25}$ y$^{-1}$eV$^{-2}$ for $^{100}$Mo, $2.12 \times 10^{-25}$ y$^{-1}$eV$^{-2}$ for $^{130}$Te, $2.26 \times 10^{-25}$ y$^{-1}$eV$^{-2}$ for $^{136}$Xe
- The $2νββ$ half-lives used for $g_{pp}$ adjustment: $1.30 \times 10^{21}$ y for $^{76}$Ge, $0.08 \times 10^{21}$ y for $^{100}$Mo, $2.70 \times 10^{21}$ y for $^{130}$Te, and $0.81 \times 10^{21}$ y (lower limit) for $^{136}$Xe
- Short-range correlations reduce $M^{0ν}$ by approximately a factor of two
- Higher-order nucleon current terms suppress $M^{0ν}$ by about 30%

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing problem of large variability in QRPA calculations of $0νββ$ matrix elements, which had been noted in prior studies such as Simkovic et al. (1997), Stoica and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2001), and Civitarese and Suhonen (2003). By fixing $g_{pp}$ to reproduce the experimentally known $2νββ$ decay rates, the authors demonstrate that the $M^{0ν}$ values become stable across basis sizes and potentials, contrary to earlier conclusions. The resulting matrix elements are somewhat smaller than typical QRPA predictions but larger than the shell model results of Caurier et al. (1996). The work enables more reliable extraction of the effective Majorana neutrino mass $\langle m_ν \rangle$ from future $0νββ$ decay experiments, with the predicted half-lives for $\langle m_ν \rangle = 50$ meV providing benchmarks for next-generation searches.
