# Fate of the $ρ$-$a_1$ mixing in dilepton production

> We investigate the effect of chiral mixing on dilepton production by combining the in-medium spectral function in the chiral effective field theory with the state-of-the-art fluid dynamical simulations. We compare the spectral functions with different chiral symmetry restoration scenarios. We find that the scenario with proper chiral symmetry restoration that takes into account the degenerate $ρ$ and $a_1$ mesons leads to an increase of the yield in the window of $1.1<M<1.4$ GeV. Whereas, the low-temperature theorem of chiral mixing extrapolated toward a chiral crossover, often used in the literature, leads to a substantial overestimate at $M=1.2$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a proper chiral symmetry restoration scenario, which includes the degeneracy of the ρ and a₁ meson masses, produces a smooth enhancement in the dilepton yield in the invariant mass window of 1.1 < M < 1.4 GeV; in contrast, the commonly used low-temperature mixing theorem extrapolated to a chiral crossover substantially overestimates the yield at M = 1.2 GeV, producing a false signature.

## Method

The authors combined in-medium spectral functions from chiral effective field theory (using chiral perturbation theory with generalized hidden local symmetry) with a state-of-the-art relativistic viscous hydrodynamics model to simulate the space-time evolution of the QCD matter created in Pb+Pb collisions. They compared three scenarios for the vector spectral function: (A) without chiral symmetry restoration (constant ρ and a₁ masses), (B) with proper chiral symmetry restoration (mass degeneracy by dropping the a₁ mass as temperature approaches Tχ), and (C) with false chiral symmetry restoration (extrapolating the low-temperature mixing theorem to high temperatures). The dilepton production rate was integrated from an initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm to the kinetic freeze-out temperature Tfo = 116 MeV, with a smooth crossover between hadronic and QGP phases at Tχ = 154 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Chiral crossover temperature: Tχ = 154 MeV, used as the transition temperature between hadronic and QGP phases.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: Tfo = 116 MeV, the temperature at which integration of the dilepton production rate stops.
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 0.6 fm.
- Collision energy: √sNN = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at centrality 0–5%.
- Transverse momentum integration range: 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV; rapidity range: |η| < 0.8.
- The false CSR scenario (scenario C) leads to a substantial overestimate at M = 1.2 GeV.
- The proper CSR scenario (scenario B) produces a smooth enhancement in the window 1.1 < M < 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral mixing in heavy-ion collisions by combining spectral functions from Harada (2008) with realistic hydrodynamic simulations, going beyond the low-temperature mixing theorem of Dey (1990). The authors show that the naive extrapolation of the low-temperature theorem, often used in the literature, produces a fake signal, while the proper treatment of ρ–a₁ mass degeneracy yields a genuine signature of chiral symmetry restoration. This enables future experimental searches for chiral symmetry restoration in dilepton spectra, and the authors suggest extending the analysis to ω and φ mesons and to chiral mixing in a dense medium.
