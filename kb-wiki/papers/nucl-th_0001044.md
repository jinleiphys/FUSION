# Particle Multiplicities and Thermalization in High Energy Collisions

> We investigate the conditions under which particle multiplicities in high energy collisions are Boltzmann distributed, as is the case for hadron production in e^+e^-, pp, p-pbar and heavy ion collisions. We show that the apparent temperature governing this distribution does not necessarily imply equilibrium (thermal or chemical) in the usual sense, as we explain. We discuss an explicit example using tree level amplitudes for N photon production in which a Boltzmann-like distribution is obtained without any equilibration. We argue that the failure of statistical techniques based on free particle ensembles may provide a signal for collective phenomena (such as large shifts in masses and widths of resonances) related to the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The apparent thermal (Boltzmann) distribution of particle multiplicities observed in high-energy collisions does not necessarily imply true thermal or chemical equilibrium; it can arise from any process that uniformly populates free-particle phase space, as demonstrated in a toy model of photon production where no equilibration occurs.

## Method

The authors use the Darwin-Fowler (saddle-point) method to show that the microcanonical ensemble (MCE) for free particles converges to the canonical ensemble (CE) in the large-\(N\) limit, so that cross sections for multiparticle production can be rewritten with Boltzmann factors. They then construct an explicit tree-level QED example of \(f\bar{f} \rightarrow N\) photons, compute the differential cross section and number density, and compare the resulting multiplicities to a thermal fit.

## Key numbers

* The toy-model number density for a photon of mass \(m_i\) is proportional to \(\int d^3p \, (p^2+m_i^2)^{-3/2} e^{-\sqrt{p^2+m_i^2}/T}\), which differs from a pure Boltzmann integral \(m_i^2 T K_2(m_i/T)\) by a factor of order \(\ln(\beta E)\) relative to \(\beta E\).
* The chiral condensate at finite temperature in the chiral limit is \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_T = \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{T=0} \left[1 - \frac{3T^2}{24 f_\pi^2} - \frac{3}{8}\left(\frac{T^2}{12 f_\pi^2}\right)^2 + \mathcal{O}(T^6)\right]\), with \(f_\pi \approx 93\) MeV.
* The chiral phase transition temperature is estimated as 170 MeV in the chiral limit and 190 MeV with real-world quark masses.
* The thermal fits to data from LEP, CERN/SPS, BNL/AGS, and GSI/SIS yield temperatures in the range \(T \sim 50-170\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by C. N. Yang and collaborators (Yang, year not given) that an apparent "partition temperature" can arise without thermal equilibrium. It directly challenges the interpretation of thermal fits by Cleymans and Redlich (1998) and others (references BH1–BH8) that hadron multiplicities in \(e^+e^-\), \(pp\), \(p\bar{p}\), and heavy-ion collisions indicate true equilibrium. The paper argues that the failure of free-particle statistical models to fit data would be a signal for collective QCD phenomena (mass shifts, chiral symmetry restoration) at RHIC, and it enables future searches for genuine thermalization by distinguishing phase-space-dominated production from interacting thermal ensembles.
