# Pion and sigma meson dissociation in a modified NJL model at finite temperature

> We investigate pion and sigma meson correlations in hot quark matter within a modified NJL model. Special emphasis is on the transformation of mesonic bound states to resonances (Mott dissociation) when due to the partial chiral symmetry restoration with increasing temperature the threshold of quark-antiquark continuum states drops below the meson mass at the corresponding Mott temperature. The description is based on evaluating the polarization functions for quark-antiquark (meson) correlations as a function of the temperature and the results can be represented by introducing modulus and phase of the complex propagator functions for the mesonic states. We study the effect of modelling confinement by introducing a low-momentum cutoff in loop integrals. We make the ansatz that this cutoff is identified with the dynamically generated quark mass gap and find an increase of the continuum threshold which makes the otherwise unbound sigma meson a bound state in the vacuum. We discuss the in-medium behavior of the mesonic phase shifts including the Mott effect and find accordance with the Levinson theorem.

# Pion and sigma meson dissociation in a modified NJL model at finite temperature

## Key claim
The authors show that introducing a low-momentum cutoff identified with the dynamically generated quark mass gap in a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model can make the sigma meson a bound state in vacuum, whereas in the ordinary NJL model it lies in the scattering continuum at all temperatures. The Mott dissociation of both pion and sigma meson bound states into resonances occurs when the quark-antiquark continuum threshold drops below the meson mass with increasing temperature.

## Method
The authors solve the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass and evaluate the polarization functions for quark-antiquark correlations as a function of temperature within a two-flavor NJL model. They introduce an infrared cutoff in loop integrals to mimic confinement, making the ansatz that this cutoff equals the dynamically generated quark mass gap. Mesonic correlations are represented by complex propagator functions whose modulus and phase yield scattering phase shifts, decomposed into resonant and continuum parts, which are analyzed for the pion and sigma meson channels.

## Key numbers
- The sigma meson becomes a bound state at low temperatures when the infrared cutoff is set to \( p_{\rm min} = m(T) \), in contrast to the ordinary NJL model with \( p_{\rm min} = 0 \) where it remains unbound.
- The Mott temperature for each meson channel is defined implicitly by \( M_M(T_{\rm Mott,M}) = \sqrt{s_{\rm thr}(T_{\rm Mott,M})} \), where \( s_{\rm thr}(T) = 2\sqrt{p^2_{\rm min} + m^2(T)} \) for the modified model.
- Phase shifts are shown for temperatures of 200, 250, and 600 MeV, illustrating the transition from bound state to resonance behavior.
- The number of bound states below threshold is \( n_M = 1 \) for both pion and sigma meson channels in the modified model, consistent with the Levinson theorem.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of meson dissociation within the NJL model (Hufner 1994, Zhuang 1994) and its Polyakov-loop generalization (Rossner 2007, Wergieluk 2012, Yamazaki 2012, Blaschke 2013) by incorporating an infrared cutoff to model confinement. The approach is related to nonlocal chiral quark models (Schmidt 1994, Benic 2013) that also produce bound sigma mesons. The results enable a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck equation of state for quark matter that includes both bound state and scattering continuum contributions to the thermodynamics, with the phase shifts providing input for the virial expansion.
