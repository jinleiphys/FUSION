# Polarization observables in lepton-deuteron elastic scattering including the lepton mass

> Expressions for the unpolarized differential cross section and for various polarization observables in the lepton-deuteron elastic scattering, $\ell+D\to \ell+D$, $\ell=e$, $μ$, $τ$, have been obtained in one-photon-exchange approximation, taking into account the lepton mass. Polarization effects have been investigated for the case of a polarized lepton beam and polarized deuteron target which can have vector or tensor polarization. Numerical estimations of the lepton mass effects have been done for the unpolarized differential cross section and for some polarization observables and applied to the case of low energy muon deuteron elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for polarization observables in lepton-deuteron elastic scattering within the one-photon-exchange approximation, explicitly retaining the lepton mass terms that are usually neglected; the authors find that lepton mass effects on kinematics and observables are sizable at low beam energies, particularly for muon beams with momenta comparable to the muon mass.

## Method

The authors calculated the matrix element for elastic lepton-deuteron scattering using the general form of the deuteron electromagnetic current expressed in terms of three form factors (charge monopole \(G_C\), magnetic dipole \(G_M\), and charge quadrupole \(G_Q\)). They constructed the leptonic and hadronic tensors for unpolarized and polarized cases, including vector and tensor polarization of the deuteron target and longitudinal or transverse lepton beam polarization, then derived expressions for the differential cross section and spin correlation coefficients in two coordinate systems (along the lepton beam momentum and along the virtual photon momentum). Numerical estimates of mass effects were performed for muon-deuteron scattering at low energies relevant to the MUSE experiment.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of lepton-nucleon scattering with finite lepton mass, previously discussed for muon-proton scattering by P87, to the case of deuterium targets. It provides the theoretical framework needed for the MUSE experiment at PSI (Gilman 2013), which will measure elastic muon-proton and electron-proton scattering simultaneously to resolve the proton charge radius puzzle, and will also determine radii of light nuclei including deuterium. The results enable separation of the charge and quadrupole form factors of the deuteron through polarization measurements, which is not possible from unpolarized cross sections alone.
