# The effects of meson mixing on dilepton spectra

> The effect of scalar and vector meson mixing on the dilepton radiation from hot and dense hadronic matter is estimated in different isospin channels. In particular, we study the effect of $σ$-$ω$ and $ρ-a_0$ mixing and calculate the corresponding rates. Effects are found to be significant compared to standard $π$-$π$ and $K$-${\bar K}$ annihilations. While the mixing in the isoscalar channel mostly gives a contribution in the invariant mass range between the two-pion threshold and the $ω$ peak, the isovector channel mixing induces an additional peak just below that of the $ϕ$. Experimentally, the dilepton signals from $ρ$-$a_0$ mixing seem to be more tractable than those from $σ$-$ω$ mixing.

# The effects of meson mixing on dilepton spectra

## Key claim

Scalar-vector meson mixing (σ-ω and ρ-a₀) in hot and dense hadronic matter produces dilepton yields that are significant compared to standard π-π and K-$\bar{K}$ annihilation channels, but only in matter with finite nucleon chemical potential where G-parity violation is possible; the isovector (ρ-a₀) channel is experimentally more tractable than the isoscalar (σ-ω) channel.

## Method

The authors calculate thermal dilepton production rates from σ-ω and ρ-a₀ mixing using a Walecka-type hadronic model with nucleon-meson interactions, evaluating the mixed polarization function Π_μ(q₀,|q|) from nucleon-loop diagrams at finite temperature and density. The cross-sections for π+π→e⁺e⁻ (via σ-ω mixing) and π+η→e⁺e⁻ (via ρ-a₀ mixing) are computed and then integrated over thermal distributions of pions and eta mesons to obtain production rates, which are compared against standard π-π and K-$\bar{K}$ annihilation rates at temperatures of 50 and 75 MeV and baryon densities of 1.5 and 2.5 times normal nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- σ-ω mixing contributes in the invariant mass range between the two-pion threshold (~280 MeV) and the ω peak (~782 MeV), but the contribution is small compared to π-π annihilation
- ρ-a₀ mixing induces an additional peak just below the φ meson mass (~1020 MeV), specifically near the a₀ mass (~980 MeV)
- At T=50 MeV and ρ/ρ₀=1.5, the ρ-a₀ mixing contribution exceeds the π-π annihilation rate in the vicinity of M=1.0 GeV
- The a₀→πη decay width is taken as Γ_{a₀→πη}(m_{a₀})=59 MeV, using coupling f_{a₀ηπ}=0.44 (within the experimental vacuum width of 50-100 MeV)
- The ω meson width is taken as a constant 9.5 MeV
- Calculations are performed at temperatures T=50 MeV and T=75 MeV, and baryon densities ρ/ρ₀=1.5 and ρ/ρ₀=2.5, corresponding to conditions expected at GSI/HADES (density up to 3ρ₀, temperature 60-90 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of σ-ω mixing in nuclear matter by Chin (1977) and its application to heavy-ion collisions by Wolf et al. (1998) and Saito et al. (1998), and the zero-temperature estimate of ρ-a₀ mixing by Teodorescu et al. (2000). Unlike previous work limited to cross-section calculations, this paper provides the first thermal production rates for dileptons from both mixing channels. The results suggest that ρ-a₀ mixing offers a promising kinematic window for observing in-medium effects in dilepton spectra, particularly at facilities like HADES at GSI where high baryon density and moderate temperature are achieved. The calculations can be improved by including full finite-temperature many-body dressing of propagators, baryonic resonances, and transport simulations of the collision dynamics.
