# Effective Field Theory for Lattice Nuclei

> We show how nuclear effective field theory (EFT) and ab initio nuclear-structure methods can turn input from lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) into predictions for the properties of nuclei. We argue that pionless EFT is the appropriate theory to describe the light nuclei obtained in recent LQCD simulations carried out at pion masses much heavier than the physical pion mass. We solve the EFT using the effective-interaction hyperspherical harmonics and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo methods. Fitting the three leading-order EFT parameters to the deuteron, dineutron and triton LQCD energies at $m_π\approx 800$ MeV, we reproduce the corresponding alpha-particle binding and predict the binding energies of mass-5 and 6 ground states.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory (EFT) at leading order, with three parameters fitted to deuteron, dineutron, and triton binding energies from lattice QCD at a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, can reproduce the alpha-particle binding energy from the same lattice simulations and predict binding energies for mass-5 and mass-6 ground states, though the authors note that the EFT truncation error is estimated at about 40% and that results show cutoff dependence.

## Method

The authors constructed a leading-order pionless EFT Hamiltonian with local Gaussian-regulated two-body and three-body contact interactions, solved the two-body Schrodinger equation with the Numerov method to fit the two-body low-energy constants to the deuteron and dineutron lattice QCD binding energies, and used the effective-interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method to fit the three-body contact term to the triton binding energy. For systems with four or more nucleons, they employed both EIHH and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) methods to compute ground-state binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 19.5 ± 4.8 MeV (fitted from NPLQCD data)
- Dineutron binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 15.9 ± 3.8 MeV (fitted from NPLQCD data)
- Triton binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 53.9 ± 10.7 MeV (fitted from NPLQCD data)
- Alpha-particle binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 89 ± 36 MeV (EFT prediction; LQCD value is 107.0 ± 24.2 MeV)
- Helium-5 binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 98 ± 39 MeV (EFT prediction at Lambda = 2 fm^-1)
- Lithium-6 binding energy at m_pi = 805 MeV: 122 ± 50 MeV (EFT prediction at Lambda = 2 fm^-1)
- The S=1, T=0 scattering length for the lattice deuteron: 1.2 ± 0.5 fm (from cutoff variation 2-14 fm^-1)
- The EFT truncation error estimate: approximately 40%

## Context

This work extends the approach of using pionless EFT to connect lattice QCD results to nuclear structure, following earlier studies by Bedaque et al. (1999) on three-body forces and by Hammer et al. on the pion-mass dependence of few-body observables. The authors apply their method to recent NPLQCD data at m_pi = 805 MeV (NPLQCD, 2013) and compare with earlier lattice results at m_pi = 510 MeV from Yamazaki et al. (2012). The approach enables predictions for nuclei beyond the reach of current lattice QCD simulations (A = 5 and 6) and can be extended to hypernuclei, though the authors note unresolved discrepancies between different lattice QCD groups regarding the existence of two-nucleon bound states at these pion masses.
