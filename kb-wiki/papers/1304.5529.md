# Holographic Gravitational Anomaly in First and Second Order Hydrodynamics

> We compute, in the framework of the fluid/gravity correspondence, the transport coefficients of a relativistic fluid affected by chiral and gauge-gravitational anomalies, including external electromagnetic fields. The computation is performed at first and second order in the hydrodynamical expansion. We use a 5-dim holographic model with pure gauge and mixed gauge-gravitational Chern-Simons terms in the action. We reproduce at first order previous results on the anomaly induced current of a magnetic field and a vortex in a relativistic fluid, and compute at second order the anomalous and non anomalous transport coefficients by using a Weyl covariant formalism. We find a dissipative and anomalous correction to the chiral magnetic conductivity due to the time dependence of the magnetic field. We also find a new contribution from the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly to the shear waves dispersion relation. The role played by the chiral and gravitational anomalies in other transport coefficients is discussed.

## Key claim

Using the fluid/gravity correspondence, the authors compute transport coefficients of a relativistic fluid affected by chiral and gauge-gravitational anomalies at first and second order in the hydrodynamic expansion, finding that the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly (parameterized by the coefficient lambda) contributes not only to anomalous transport but also corrects non-anomalous second-order coefficients that had previously been computed without including this anomaly.

## Method

The authors employ a 5-dimensional holographic model with pure gauge and mixed gauge-gravitational Chern-Simons terms in the action, using the fluid/gravity correspondence to map black hole solutions in asymptotically anti-de Sitter space to relativistic hydrodynamics on the boundary. They compute the constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and U(1) current up to second order in derivatives using a Weyl covariant formalism, extracting transport coefficients by solving the bulk equations of motion with appropriate boundary conditions. The computation is performed in the Landau frame, and the results are expressed as expansions in the small parameter bar-mu = mu/T.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity: eta = r_+^3 / (16 pi G), where r_+ is the outer horizon radius of the black hole
* Electrical conductivity: sigma = (pi r_+^7 T^2) / (16 G m^2), where m is the black hole mass
* Chiral magnetic conductivity (first order): xi_B = - (sqrt(3) q (m + 3 r_+^4) kappa) / (8 pi G m r_+^2) + (sqrt(3) pi q T^2 lambda) / (G m), where kappa and lambda are anomaly coefficients
* Chiral vortical conductivity (first order): xi_V = - (3 q^2 kappa) / (4 pi G m) + (2 pi (2 q^2 - r_+^6) T^2 lambda) / (G m r_+^2)
* Second-order coefficient Lambda_8 = - (eta / r_+^2) * (1/(2 pi) - (1/(12 pi^3)) (14 log 2 - 1) bar-mu^2 + O(bar-mu^4))
* Second-order coefficient Lambda_9 = (eta / r_+^3) * (11/6 + (1/2) log 2 + (1/(12 pi^2)) (7 - 8 log 2) bar-mu^2 + O(bar-mu^4))
* Second-order anomalous coefficient tilde_Lambda_7 = - (32 eta / r_+^2) lambda
* Second-order coefficient xi_5 = - (1/(16 pi G)) * ( (1/2) log 2 + (1/(12 pi^2)) bar-mu^2 (11 - 24 log 2) + O(bar-mu^4) )

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of anomalous transport in relativistic fluids, reproducing at first order the anomaly-induced current in a magnetic field and vortex found in earlier studies (Kharzeev et al., 2011; Erdmenger et al., 2008; Banerjee et al., 2008). It goes beyond these by computing second-order transport coefficients including the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly, which had been shown to fix part of the first-order transport coefficients (Landsteiner et al., 2011). The results enable future studies of second-order anomalous hydrodynamic effects in systems such as the quark-gluon plasma, and the authors note that the derivative expansion breaks down in the presence of the gravitational anomaly (Jensen et al., 2012).
