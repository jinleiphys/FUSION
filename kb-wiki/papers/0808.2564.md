# Pionic transparency in semi-exclusive electroproduction off nuclei

> We investigate the early onset of pionic color transparency ($π$CT) observed at Jefferson Laboratory (JLAB) in semi--exclusive pion electroproduction reaction $A(e,e'π^+)$ off nuclei. In the present description the primary $γ^*p \to π^+ n$ interaction is described very well both for the longitudinal and the transverse polarizations. For the final state interactions a coupled--channel treatment of the interactions of transmitted hadrons allows to go beyond the Glauber approximation. We show that a proper distinction between the soft hadronic and hard partonic components of the electroproduction amplitude is essential for a quantitative description of the measured nuclear transparency. The data are well reproduced if one assumes that point--like configurations are produced in the regime of hard deep--inelastic scattering (DIS) off partons and dominate the transverse channel.

## Key claim

The observed rise in pionic nuclear transparency with photon virtuality $Q^2$ can be reproduced without invoking color transparency for all produced pions; instead, the effect arises primarily from the transverse (DIS) component of the reaction, where point-like configurations and time-dilated formation lengths dominate, while the longitudinal (hadronic) component remains unaffected by formation-time effects.

## Method

The authors model the primary $\gamma^* p \to \pi^+ n$ reaction by separating it into a soft hadronic part (described by Regge-trajectory exchange, dominated by the $\pi$-pole mechanism) and a hard partonic DIS part (described by the Lund fragmentation model via the JETSET implementation). Final-state interactions of produced hadrons are treated using a coupled-channel Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equation, which accounts for elastic and inelastic rescattering, Pauli blocking, and side-feeding, going beyond the Glauber approximation. The nuclear transparency is computed as the ratio of cross sections with and without FSI, using the actual acceptance conditions of the JLAB $\pi$CT experiment.

## Key numbers

- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ in the empirical factor $Q^4/(Q^2 + \Lambda^2)^2$ suppressing the DIS contribution at low $Q^2$ is fitted to $\Lambda \simeq 400$ MeV.
- The Lund model formation time in the (pre)pion rest frame is $\tau_F \simeq 0.17$ fm, compared to $\tau_F = 0.16$ fm for the alternative estimate with $\Delta M^2 = 1.4$ GeV$^2$.
- The coherence length $l_h$ varies from $\simeq 0.67$ fm at $Q^2 = 1.1$ GeV$^2$ down to $0.36$ fm at $Q^2 = 4.69$ GeV$^2$.
- The central kinematics cover $Q^2$ values of 1.10, 2.15, 3.00, 3.91, and 4.69 GeV$^2$ at $W \approx 2.2$ GeV.
- The pedestal value $X_0$ in the effective cross section is fixed using $\sqrt{\langle k_{\rm t}^2\rangle} = 350$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of the JLAB $\pi$CT experiment (Clasie 2006, Horn 2007) beyond the Glauber-model calculations of Larson (2006) and Cosyn (2007), which assumed color transparency affects all produced pions. The authors show that a proper separation of longitudinal (hadronic) and transverse (DIS) contributions is essential, and that the Lund-model formation times are about a factor of 3 larger than those used in prior work. The results suggest that longitudinal-transverse separated transparency measurements are needed for a definitive proof of color transparency, and that extending the analysis to $Q^2 \sim 10$ GeV$^2$ would be valuable.
