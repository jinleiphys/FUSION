# The Island of Inversion at $N=40$

> Our understanding of the structure of atomic nuclei largely derives from the nuclear shell model, which has proven widely successful. Further test to our interpretation of the nuclear properties is provided by the study of shell evolution. Increasing experimental information has shown that the nuclear energy shells change when going towards the most exotic nuclei, in turn making some shell closures disappear while others arise. In particular, the $N=40$ sub-shell closure has been the subject of extensive research due to the emergence of a so-called Island of Inversion, where deformed intruder configurations dominate the wave function of the ground state. An overview of recent experimental results in the $N=40$ Island of Inversion, particularly those performed with the combination of the MINOS hydrogen target and the DALI2 $γ$-ray array at the RIBF are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the N=40 Island of Inversion extends from Cr isotopes down to at least ⁶²Ti and likely to ⁶⁰Ca, based on the agreement between experimental data and shell model calculations using the LNPS interaction, but notes that spectroscopy of ⁶⁰Ca itself cannot yet be achieved at existing facilities.

## Method

The review summarizes experimental results from the SEASTAR project at RIKEN RIBF, where exotic neutron-rich isotopes were produced via fragmentation of ²³⁸U and ⁷⁰Zn primary beams, separated with the BigRIPS separator, and studied using quasi-free proton knockout reactions in the MINOS liquid hydrogen target. De-excitation gamma rays were detected with the DALI2 and DALI2+ arrays, and outgoing fragments were identified with the ZeroDegree spectrometer or SAMURAI magnet. The experimental results are compared systematically with shell model calculations using the LNPS interaction, which employs a ⁴⁸Ca core with the pf shell for protons and the 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0f₅/₂, 0g₉/₂, and 1d₅/₂ orbits for neutrons.

## Key numbers

- ⁶⁶Cr shows the lowest 2⁺₁ energy in the region at 386 keV, slightly below the ⁶⁴Cr value of 429 keV
- ⁶²Ti shows 2⁺₁ and 4⁺₁ transitions at 683(10) keV and 823(20) keV, respectively
- ⁵⁶Ca shows a 2⁺₁ transition at 1456(12) keV, while ⁵⁸Ca shows a possible transition at 1115(34) keV with 2.8σ significance
- LNPS calculations predict a 2⁺₁ energy of around 1.5 MeV for ⁶⁰Ca
- The 1/2⁻ isomeric state in ⁶⁷Co lies at 492 keV, and in ⁶⁹Co at 176(57) keV
- ⁷⁰Fe shows a candidate 6⁺ state at 2444 keV with effective lifetimes of τ=120(20) ps for the 2⁺₁ state and τ=2.3(1.5) ps for the 4⁺₁ state
- LNPS calculations predict a deformation parameter β∼0.33 at N=40 for Cr isotopes

## Context

This review extends the line of work on shell evolution and Islands of Inversion previously established at N=20 around ³²Mg (Detraz 1979, Motobayashi 1995), applying similar concepts to the N=40 region below ⁶⁸Ni. The experimental results from the SEASTAR project, particularly the spectroscopy of ⁶²Ti and ⁶⁶Cr, confirm predictions made by Lenzi, Nowacki, Poves, and Sieja (2010) using the LNPS interaction, and enable future studies of the proposed merging of the N=40 Island of Inversion with a new one around N=50, as predicted by Nowacki et al. (2016) using the PFSDG-U interaction.
