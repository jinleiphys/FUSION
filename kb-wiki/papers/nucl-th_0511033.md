# Room for an S = +1 pentaquark in K^+ - nucleus phenomenology

> Evidence for excitation of exotic S=+1 pentaquark degrees of freedom is presented by studying optical-potential fits to K^+ - nucleus total, reaction and elastic-differential cross section data at p(lab) = 500 - 700 MeV/c. Estimates of the underlying two-nucleon absorption K^+ n N --> Theta^+ N reaction cross section are made and are used for discussing the anticipated cross section of the strangeness exchange reaction K^+ N --> pi Theta^+.

# Room for an S = +1 pentaquark in K^+ - nucleus phenomenology

## Key claim

Evidence for excitation of exotic S=+1 pentaquark degrees of freedom is presented by showing that a density-dependent term added to the standard tρ optical potential can resolve the long-standing discrepancy between K^+ - nucleus cross section data and impulse-approximation calculations, without requiring that the Θ^+(1540) be a narrow KN resonance or have particular spin-parity values.

## Method

The authors performed optical-potential fits to K^+ - nucleus total, reaction, and elastic-differential cross section data on ^6Li, ^12C, ^28Si, and ^40Ca at p_lab = 488, 531, 656, and 714 MeV/c from BNL-AGS transmission experiments. They modified the standard tρ optical potential by adding a density-dependent term (either ρ^2 or \barρ ρ) to simulate two-nucleon absorption K^+ n N → Θ^+ N, and compared the quality of fit using χ^2/N as the metric.

## Key numbers

- At p_lab = 488 MeV/c, the best-fit tρ potential gave χ^2/N = 18.2 for 8 data points; adding the density-dependent term of Eq. (FGM97) reduced χ^2/N to 0.06.
- For all 32 data points at 4 energies, χ^2/N = 42.7 for the best-fit tρ potential was reduced to 0.65 using the empirical density-dependent modification.
- The threshold nuclear density for the new absorption mechanism was determined as ρ_th = 0.088(6) fm^{-3}, which lies between the average density of ^6Li (0.049 fm^{-3}) and the denser targets (0.104-0.112 fm^{-3}).
- The two-nucleon absorption cross section per nucleon for dense targets (C, Si, Ca) was estimated as approximately 3.5 mb at p_lab = 488 MeV/c.
- The estimated cross section for K^+ d → Θ^+ p is 0.1 - 0.5 mb.
- For the combined fit to integral and differential cross sections at 714 MeV/c (25 data points), the \barρ ρ form gave χ^2/N = 5.3, while the ρ^2 form gave χ^2/N = 24.6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Gal and Friedman, 2005) and the empirical density-dependent fits of Friedman et al. (1997a, 1997b) that first identified the excess reactivity in K^+ - nucleus cross sections. It contradicts the conventional tρ impulse-approximation model which cannot reproduce the density dependence of the data, as previously noted by Siegel et al. (1985) and confirmed by Ceolin and Renzi (1992) and Johnson et al. (1995). The results enable quantitative predictions for the K^+ d → Θ^+ p two-body production reaction and for (K^+,p) experiments on nuclear targets, which could test the existence of S=+1 pentaquark degrees of freedom. The microscopic mechanism was independently supported by Tolos et al. (2005) using meson-cloud KπNΘ^+ coupling.
