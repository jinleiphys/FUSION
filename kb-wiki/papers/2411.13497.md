# Global analysis of $μ\to e$ interactions in the SMEFT

> We study current experimental bounds on charged lepton flavor violating (CLFV) $μ$-$e$ interactions in the model-independent framework of the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT). Assuming a generic flavor structure in the quark sector, we consider the contributions of CLFV operators to low-energy observables, including $μ\to eγ$ and $μ\to e$ conversion for quark-flavor conserving operators and CLFV meson decays for quark-flavor violating operators. At high energy, we consider limits on CLFV decays of the Higgs and Z bosons and of the top quark, and obtain bounds on operators with light quarks by recasting searches for production of $eμ$ pairs in $pp$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We connect observables at low- and high-energy by taking into account renormalization group running and matching between CLFV operators. We also discuss the sensitivity of the future Electron-Ion Collider, where the prospective bounds are derived by imposing simple cuts on final state particles. We find that, in a single operator scenario, bounds on purely leptonic operators are dominated by $μ\rightarrow e γ$ and $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion. Semileptonic operators with down-type quarks are also dominantly constrained by low-energy observables, while LHC searches lead the bounds on up-type quark-flavor violating operators. Taking simplified multiple-coupling scenarios, we show that it is easy to evade the strongest low-energy bounds from spin-independent $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion, and that collider searches are competitive and complementary to constraints from spin-dependent $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion and other low-energy probes.

## Key claim
The paper presents a global analysis of charged lepton flavor violating (CLFV) μ→e interactions in the SMEFT framework, finding that while low-energy observables dominate constraints on purely leptonic and down-type quark operators, LHC searches lead bounds on up-type quark-flavor violating operators, and that simplified multiple-coupling scenarios can evade the strongest low-energy bounds from spin-independent μ→e conversion.

## Method
The authors study dimension-six SMEFT operators contributing to μ→e transitions, including lepton bilinear operators (dipole, Z-boson, and Higgs couplings) and semileptonic four-fermion operators (vector, scalar, and tensor types). They compute constraints from low-energy observables (μ→eγ, μ→e conversion in nuclei with Ti and Au targets, μ→3e, and CLFV meson decays) and high-energy probes (LHC searches for CLFV Higgs, Z, and top decays, plus pp→eμ production recast with CMS data), connecting the two regimes via one-loop renormalization group running and tree-level matching between SMEFT and LEFT, with two-loop Barr-Zee diagrams included for Yukawa contributions to the dipole operator.

## Key numbers
- BR(μ→eγ) < 3.1×10⁻¹³ at 90% C.L. from MEG II
- BR(μ+Au→e+Au) < 7×10⁻¹³ and BR(μ+Ti→e+Ti) < 6.1×10⁻¹³ at 90% C.L. from SINDRUM II
- BR(μ→3e) < 1.0×10⁻¹² at 90% C.L. from SINDRUM
- Photon dipole operator bound: |Γᵉ_γ| < 6.0×10⁻¹¹ at 90% C.L. from μ→eγ
- Z-boson dipole bound: |Γᵉ_Z| < 3.1×10⁻⁹ at 90% C.L. from μ→e conversion in Ti
- LFV Yukawa coupling bound: |Y′_e| < 1.2×10⁻⁶ at 90% C.L. from μ→eγ
- Z-coupling bounds: |c_{Lφ}^{(1)}|, |c_{Lφ}^{(3)}|, |c_{eφ}| < 4.3×10⁻⁷ (Ti) or 2.9×10⁻⁷ (Au) at 90% C.L.
- Semileptonic vector operator bounds (Ti target, 90% C.L.): C_{LQ,U} with uu < 6.9×10⁻⁸, C_{LQ,D} with dd < 7.4×10⁻⁸, C_{LQ,D} with ss < 3.7×10⁻⁵, C_{LQ,D} with bb < 2.8×10⁻⁵
- Semileptonic scalar operator bounds (Ti target, 90% C.L.): C_{LedQ} with dd < 8.4×10⁻⁹, C_{LeQu}^{(1)} with uu < 8.2×10⁻⁹
- Tensor operator bound: C_{LeQu}^{(3)} with uu < 3.1×10⁻⁸ (Ti target, 90% C.L.)
- RGE mixing coefficients: top-quark vector operators generate light-flavor vector operators at the 10⁻² level; strange-quark operators induce light-quark operators with coefficients ~O(10⁻³) at μ=2 GeV from Λ=1 TeV

## Context
This work extends previous EFT-based studies of μ→e transitions by Raidal (1997), Kuno (1999), Kitano (2002), Cirigliano (2009, 2017, 2022), Crivellin (2013, 2014, 2017), Calibbi (2017, 2021, 2022), Davidson (2022), Ardu (2021, 2022, 2024), and others, by providing a comprehensive global analysis that includes updated LHC data from CMS (2022), detailed EIC sensitivity projections, and up-to-date nucleon form factors and nuclear matrix elements from Hoferichter (2020, 2022), Rule (2021), and Haxton (2022, 2024). The results enable future experimental planning by identifying which directions in CLFV parameter space remain unconstrained and which experiments (EIC, LHC, Mu2e, COMET) are best positioned to probe them.
