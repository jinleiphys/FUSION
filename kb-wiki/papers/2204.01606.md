# Magnetic impurity in a one-dimensional few-fermion system

> We present a numerical analysis of spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ fermions in a one-dimensional harmonic potential in the presence of a magnetic point-like impurity at the center of the trap. The model represents a few-body analogue of a magnetic impurity in the vicinity of an $s$-wave superconductor. Already for a few particles we find a ground-state level crossing between sectors with different fermion parities. We interpret this crossing as a few-body precursor of a quantum phase transition, which occurs when the impurity `breaks' a Cooper pair. This picture is further corroborated by analyzing density-density correlations in momentum space. Finally, we discuss how the system may be realized with existing cold-atoms platforms.

## Key claim

For a few-body system of spin-1/2 fermions in a one-dimensional harmonic trap with a magnetic point-like impurity at the center, a ground-state level crossing between sectors of different fermion parity occurs already with a handful of particles, which the authors interpret as a few-body precursor of a quantum phase transition that in the many-body limit occurs when the impurity "breaks" a Cooper pair.

## Method

The authors perform full-configuration interaction (exact diagonalization) calculations in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis, using an effective two-body interaction inspired by the Lee-Suzuki method from nuclear physics to improve convergence. They validate their results for systems without the magnetic impurity using the transcorrelated method (TCM), which removes the wave function cusp via a similarity transformation of the Hamiltonian. The study is limited to systems with up to 9 fermions total, and the magnetic impurity is modeled as a spin-selective delta-potential at the trap center with equal magnitude but opposite sign for spin-up and spin-down particles.

## Key numbers

- For the 1-up + 1-down system with strong fermion-impurity interaction (G → ∞) and zero fermion-fermion interaction (g = 0), the ground-state energy equals ε_G + 3/2, where ε_G ≃ -G²/2 for large G.
- For the 1-up + 2-down system in the limit G → ∞, the variational analysis gives a critical fermion-fermion interaction strength g ≃ -1.95 (in harmonic oscillator units) below which the 1-up + 2-down system becomes energetically more favorable than the 1-up + 1-down system.
- For the limit g → -∞, the critical impurity-fermion coupling where the energies of the 1-up + 1-down and 1-up + 2-down systems become equal is G ≃ 0.5 (in harmonic oscillator units).
- For the 2-up + 2-down system without impurity, the pairing gap Δ is shown as a function of g, with data computed using n_b = 13 single-particle states and compared to earlier FCI calculations from D'Amico (2015).

## Context

This work extends the study of few-body precursors of many-body phenomena, specifically the quantum phase transition associated with a magnetic impurity in an s-wave superconductor (the Yu-Shiba-Rusinov problem). The authors build on prior work on pairing gaps in one-dimensional few-fermion systems by D'Amico (2015) and Grining (2015), and on effective interaction methods for cold atoms by Rotureau (2013). The results enable systematic exploration of how the many-body quantum phase transition emerges from few-body dynamics, and the authors discuss how the system may be realized with existing cold-atom platforms.
