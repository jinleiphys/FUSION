# Hydrodynamic Models for Heavy-Ion Collisions, and beyond

> A generic property of a first-order phase transition in equilibrium, and in the limit of large entropy per unit of conserved charge, is the smallness of the isentropic speed of sound in the ``mixed phase''. A specific prediction is that this should lead to a non-isotropic momentum distribution of nucleons in the reaction plane (for energies around 40 AGeV in our model calculation). On the other hand, we show that from present effective theories for low-energy QCD one does not expect the thermal transition rate between various states of the effective potential to be much larger than the expansion rate, questioning the applicability of the idealized Maxwell/Gibbs construction. Experimental data could soon provide essential information on the dynamics of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if a first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions proceeds close to equilibrium, the small isentropic speed of sound in the mixed phase should produce a non-isotropic momentum distribution of nucleons in the reaction plane at energies around 40 AGeV; however, the authors also show that thermal transition rates from effective low-energy QCD theories may not be much larger than the expansion rate, questioning whether the idealized equilibrium Maxwell/Gibbs construction is applicable.

## Method

The authors combine a linear sigma model with constituent quarks to compute the finite-temperature effective potential for the chiral phase transition, and use ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with an equation of state that includes a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma (hadronic phase described by nucleons interacting via scalar and vector fields, QGP phase described by the MIT Bag model with bag parameter B^{1/4}=235 MeV). They compute the nucleation rate for bubble formation via thermal activation and compare it to an assumed expansion rate of H=1 fm^{-1} to assess whether equilibrium conditions are likely.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral transition in the linear sigma model: T_c = 123.7 MeV at vanishing baryon-chemical potential (for g=5.5)
- Spinodal instability temperature: T_sp ≈ 108 MeV (for the chosen parameters at μ=0)
- Bag model critical temperature: T_c ≈ 170 MeV at ρ=0; critical baryon-chemical potential: μ_c = 1.8 GeV at T=0
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV; pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV; sigma mass: m_σ = 600 MeV (from λ^2 = 20)
- Specific entropy values used: s/ρ ≈ 10 at E_Lab^{kin} = 8 AGeV; s/ρ ≈ 20 at E_Lab^{kin} = 40 AGeV
- Correlation length in the metastable state (a) can exceed that in the equilibrium state (b) by a factor of 2-3 depending on supercooling

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic flow studies by Rischke (1996) and directed flow calculations by Danielewicz (1985) to examine how a first-order phase transition affects nucleon momentum distributions. It also builds on nucleation rate calculations by Kapusta and by Scavenius (2000) to question whether equilibrium thermodynamics applies. The paper predicts that upcoming Pb+Pb data at 40 AGeV can distinguish between equilibrium and non-equilibrium phase transition scenarios, and suggests that spinodal decomposition, analogous to hysteresis phenomena in condensed matter physics, could provide direct information about the QCD phase transition.
