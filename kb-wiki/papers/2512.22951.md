# The effect of inversion of $p$ and $f$ orbits on halo formation in heavy sodium isotopes

> The role of the inversion of the $p$ and $f$ shell-model orbits in the emergence of halo structures in the ground states of neutron-rich $^{34,37,39}$Na is investigated. Families of two- and three-body models are constructed with effective core-neutron interactions, with parameter choices based on a combination of the available experimental data and systematic trends, as well as the GPT $n$-$n$ interaction and a phenomenological three-body force. Our results indicate a possible one-neutron halo in $^{34}$Na, while $^{37,39}$Na exhibit features of Borromean halos. The halo formation is driven by the weakening of the shell gap and inversion of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ orbits expected to occur somewhere near these masses. We further show that the electric dipole response provides a clear and sensitive probe of halo structure in these isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the inversion of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ neutron shell-model orbits, expected to occur near neutron-rich sodium isotopes, can explain the emergence of one-neutron and Borromean halo structures in $^{34,37,39}$Na. The authors hedge that while their results indicate a possible one-neutron halo in $^{34}$Na and Borromean halos in $^{37,39}$Na, the available experimental data are insufficient to rigorously confirm the inversion scenario, and a slight preference for the inverted ordering is based on systematic trends rather than direct evidence.

## Method

The authors construct effective two-body (core+n) and three-body (core+2n) models using a discrete pseudostate representation with transformed harmonic oscillator bases. For the two-body systems, Woods-Saxon potentials with central and spin-orbit terms are fitted to reproduce experimental one-neutron separation energies, with separate parameter sets representing normal ($f_{7/2}$ lower) and inverted ($p_{3/2}$ lower) single-particle orderings. For the three-body Borromean systems $^{37,39}$Na, the hyperspherical harmonics framework is used with the GPT neutron-neutron interaction and a phenomenological three-body force adjusted to match two-neutron separation energies.

## Key numbers

- $^{34}$Na: one-neutron separation energy $s_{1n} = 0.17 \pm 0.50$ MeV (experimental, Gaudfroy 2012) or $0.17 \pm 0.75$ MeV (evaluated, Wang 2021)
- $^{36}$Na: evaluated $s_{1n} = -0.00 \pm 0.15$ MeV (unbound)
- $^{38}$Na: evaluated $s_{1n} = -0.70 \pm 0.20$ MeV (unbound)
- $^{37}$Na: two-neutron separation energy $s_{2n} = 0.84 \pm 0.15$ MeV (evaluated, Wang 2021)
- $^{39}$Na: adopted $s_{2n} = 0.50 \pm 0.25$ MeV (from Singh 2024, since evaluated data predicts unbound but Ahn 2022 observed it bound)
- Woods-Saxon parameters: $r_0 = 1.25$ fm, $a = 0.75$ fm, spin-orbit strength $V_{ls} = 28.00$ MeV fm$^2$
- Core radii from RMF-BCS: $R_\text{core} = 3.385$ fm ($^{33}$Na), $3.497$ fm ($^{35}$Na), $3.638$ fm ($^{37}$Na)
- $f_{7/2}$-$p_{3/2}$ energy gaps in inverted set: $0.405$ MeV ($^{36}$Na), $0.281$ MeV ($^{38}$Na)
- $f_{7/2}$-$p_{3/2}$ energy gaps in normal set: approximately $0.2$ MeV for $^{36,38}$Na
- Basis truncation: $K_{\text{max}} = 46$, $N = 20$ for ground states; $N = 24$, $K_{\text{max}} = 38$ for $B(E1)$ distributions

## Context

This work extends few-body studies of halo nuclei to the heavy sodium isotopic chain, following similar approaches applied to neutron-rich fluorine isotopes by Singh (2020) and Singh (2022). The inversion of $p$ and $f$ orbits near $N=20-28$ has been discussed for lighter elements by Hammamoto (2007, 2012), Kobayashi (2016), and Kahlbow (2024). The results are compared with relativistic mean-field (RMF-BCS) calculations by Geng (2004) and shell-model calculations by Otsuka (2022), and with preliminary interaction cross-section data from Suzuki (2014). The paper enables experimental tests via Coulomb dissociation measurements of the electric dipole response, which the authors show is a sensitive probe of halo structure and can distinguish between normal and inverted single-particle orderings.
