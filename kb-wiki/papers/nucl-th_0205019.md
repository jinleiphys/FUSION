# Statistical Hadronization of Supercooled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The fast simultaneous hadronization and chemical freeze out of supercooled quark-gluon plasma, created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, leads to the re-heating of the expanding matter and to the change in a collective flow profile. We use the assumption of statistical nature of the hadronization process, and study quantitatively the freeze out in the framework of hydrodynamical Bjorken model with different quark-gluon plasma equations of state.

## Key claim

The fast, simultaneous hadronization and chemical freeze-out of supercooled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be described by a statistical (apparent equilibrium) model, but the process is highly sensitive to the choice of QGP equation of state (EoS); the freeze-out is not possible from arbitrary initial states, and the entropy constraint is a sensitive condition that must be satisfied.

## Method

The authors model the sudden hadronization of supercooled QGP over a three-dimensional time-like hypersurface using conservation laws for energy-momentum, baryon number, strangeness, and strange quark pairs, assuming the post-freeze-out hadron gas is in apparent thermal and chemical equilibrium described by a grand-canonical ensemble. They embed this freeze-out treatment in the one-dimensional Bjorken hydrodynamical model and compare three QGP equations of state: an ideal gas EoS, the MIT bag model EoS with a bag constant, and a spinodal EoS with a temperature-dependent pressure suppression term. The post-freeze-out hadron gas is modeled as an ideal gas of all hadrons up to 2.5 GeV mass from the 2000 PDG listing, with strangeness saturation described by a fugacity parameter γ_S.

## Key numbers

* Initial conditions for the Bjorken model: energy density e₀ = 5 GeV fm⁻³ and baryon density (n_B)₀ = 1 fm⁻³ at proper time τ = 1 fm/c (for bag and spinodal EoS); for ideal gas EoS, e₀ = 8 GeV fm⁻³ was used.
* Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV used to fix parameters in the bag and spinodal EoS.
* For the spinodal EoS at the point where hadron gas temperature equals T_c = 160 MeV, the QGP supercooling degree T_Q/T_c is about 24%, and the ratio of hadron gas to QGP flow velocities v_H/v_Q = 0.80, corresponding to a final hadron gas flow velocity of 0.54c.
* Strangeness saturation parameter γ_S at T_H = T_c for the spinodal EoS: γ_S₁ = 0.74 for strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV; γ_S₂ corresponds to m_s = 250 MeV (value not explicitly given in the text for this point).
* The ratio R of entropy currents in hadron gas and QGP stays well above one for bag and spinodal models up to unrealistic freeze-out conditions.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach previously applied to hadron yield systematics in AA, pA, and elementary collisions (Cleymans, Becattini, 1990s-2000s) by incorporating it into a hydrodynamical freeze-out framework with supercooled QGP. The authors show that the first-order quasistatic phase transition is too slow to match the experimentally determined chemical decoupling time t_CFO ≲ 10 fm/c (Csörgő, 2000), motivating the need for supercooling and non-equilibrium hadronization. The results enable cell-by-cell freeze-out treatment in fluid dynamical models, with strangeness identified as the most sensitive observable for distinguishing different QGP equations of state.
