# Implicit vs Explicit Renormalization and Effective Interactions

> Effective interactions can be obtained from a renormalization group analysis in two complementary ways. One can either explicitly integrate out higher energy modes or impose given conditions at low energies for a cut-off theory. While the first method is numerically involved, the second one can be solved almost analytically. In both cases we compare the out coming effective interactions for the two nucleon system as functions of the cut-off scale and find a strikingly wide energy region where both approaches overlap, corresponding to relevant scales in light nuclei about 200MeV. This amounts to a great simplification in the determination of the effective interaction parameters.

## Key claim

Effective interactions for the two-nucleon system can be obtained either by explicit Block-Diagonal Similarity Renormalization Group (BD-SRG) evolution or by implicit renormalization using low-energy scattering data; the two approaches produce overlapping results for cut-off scales up to about 200 MeV, but only when the implicit method is used in the continuum limit rather than on a finite momentum grid.

## Method

The authors compare two methods for deriving effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions as functions of a momentum cut-off scale Λ. The explicit method uses the BD-SRG flow equations to unitarily evolve a separable gaussian potential (fitted to S-wave scattering length and effective range) on a finite momentum grid with N=50 Gauss points and Pmax=5 fm⁻¹. The implicit method solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for a contact-theory potential (polynomial in momenta) at leading order (LO, C₀ only) and next-to-leading order (NLO, C₀ and C₂), fitting the same effective range expansion parameters. The running coupling constants from both methods are compared as functions of Λ.

## Key numbers

- The overlap region where explicit BD-SRG and implicit contact-theory results agree extends over cut-off scales 0.5 fm⁻¹ ≤ Λ ≤ 1.5 fm⁻¹ for both the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels.
- The Wigner causality bound for the contact theory is Λ_WB ∼ 1.9 fm⁻¹ for ¹S₀ and Λ_WB ∼ 2.4 fm⁻¹ for ³S₁; above these values the NLO contact parameters become complex.
- The deuteron binding energy from the ³S₁ separable potential is E_d = -2.22 MeV, corresponding to a pole at p = iγ = i 0.2314 fm⁻¹.
- The effective range expansion reproduces NN data up to p ≤ Λ_ERE ∼ 100 MeV.
- For the ¹S₀ channel, the perturbative contribution of the long-distance one-pion-exchange piece (r ≥ 3 fm) after evolution is ≤ 10⁻² fm for 2.1 ≥ Λ ≥ 0.5 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent implicit renormalization approach of Arriola (2010) and the coarse-grained NN interaction studies of Navarro Perez (2011, 2012) by directly comparing with the explicit BD-SRG method of Anderson (2008). The authors show that the computationally expensive BD-SRG evolution on a finite grid can be replaced by the simpler implicit method for cut-offs up to about 1.5 fm⁻¹, enabling direct extraction of effective interaction parameters from low-energy NN scattering data without solving SRG flow equations. The approach connects to the V_low k method of Bogner (2001, 2003) and to the Skyrme-force phenomenology of Vautherin (1971) and Bender (2003).
