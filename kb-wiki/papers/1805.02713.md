# Calculating charged particle observables using modified Wood Saxon model in HIJING for U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 193 GeV

> We have implemented spherical harmonics in default Wood Saxon distribution of the HIJING model and calculated various physical observables such as transverse momentum, charged particle multiplicity, nuclear modification factor and particle ratios for charged particles at top RHIC energy with collisions of Uranium (U) nuclei. Results have been compared with available experimental data. We observe that, a particular type of collision configuration can produce significant magnitude change in observables. We have noticed that the tip-tip configuration shows higher magnitude of particle yield in central collisions, while the body-body configuration shows higher value in the cases of peripheral collisions, with the flip in the trend occurring for the mid-central U+U collisions. We observe that particle ratios are independent of configuration type.

## Key claim

The paper claims that implementing a modified Wood-Saxon (MWS) distribution with spherical harmonics in the HIJING model for U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 193 GeV produces configuration-dependent observables, but the abstract's claim that "a particular type of collision configuration can produce significant magnitude change in observables" is hedged by the observation that particle ratios are independent of configuration type and that the flip in trend between tip-tip and body-body configurations occurs for mid-central collisions.

## Method

The authors implemented spherical harmonics \(Y_{20}\) and \(Y_{40}\) with deformation parameters \(\beta_2 = 0.28\) and \(\beta_4 = 0.093\) into the standard Wood-Saxon nuclear density distribution within the HIJING event generator, creating a modified Wood-Saxon (MWS) distribution for prolate uranium nuclei. They then simulated U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 193 GeV for three orientation configurations (tip-tip, body-body, and random) and calculated charged particle multiplicity, pseudorapidity distribution, transverse momentum spectra, mean transverse momentum, nuclear modification factor \(R_{CP}\), and particle ratios. Results were compared with available experimental data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations and with Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity \(n_{ch}\) for body-body configuration is approximately 1250, while tip-tip configuration gives about 10% higher charged particles than body-body (maximum value).
- Most central collisions (0-5%) produce \(dN_{ch}/d\eta \sim 1100\), while most peripheral collisions produce \(\sim 300\) for U+U collisions.
- Mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_T \rangle\) is 0.42 GeV for most central collisions and 0.41 GeV for most peripheral collisions.
- The flip in trend between tip-tip and body-body configurations for \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) occurs around 10-20% centrality.
- STAR experimental data shows highest multiplicity of \(N_{ch} \sim 700\) for U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 193 GeV.
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{CP}\) shows that body-body configuration gives more suppression than tip-tip configuration.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the modified Wood-Saxon distribution in the AMPT formalism by Rihan et al. (2015) to the HIJING event generator, and builds on earlier deformation parameter values from atomic data tables. The study enables future event-by-event analysis of U+U experimental data at RHIC and provides a method to select specific collision configurations (tip-tip, body-body) to probe initial condition effects on QGP observables. The authors note that the absence of secondary multiple interactions of partons in HIJING may explain mismatches with experimental data, particularly for proton spectra.
