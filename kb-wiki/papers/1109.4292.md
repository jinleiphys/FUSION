# Exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonian for heavy nuclei

> We present a new exactly solvable Hamiltonian with a separable pairing interaction and non-degenerate single-particle energies. It is derived from the hyperbolic family of Richardson-Gaudin models and possesses two free parameters, one related to an interaction cutoff and the other to the pairing strength. These two parameters can be adjusted to give an excellent reproduction of Gogny self-consistent mean-field calculations in the canonical basis.

# Exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonian for heavy nuclei

## Key claim
The authors present a new exactly solvable Hamiltonian with separable pairing interaction and non-degenerate single-particle energies, derived from the hyperbolic family of Richardson-Gaudin models, which can reproduce Gogny self-consistent mean-field calculations in the canonical basis when its two free parameters (an interaction cutoff and pairing strength) are adjusted; however, the reproduction is qualitative for state-dependent gaps (which show large fluctuations in the Gogny force) and accurate only for the pairing tensor.

## Method
The hyperbolic Richardson-Gaudin integrals of motion were used to derive a linear combination Hamiltonian that yields a separable pairing interaction with a square-root energy dependence. The two free parameters (pairing strength G and cutoff alpha) were fitted by solving three coupled BCS equations that minimize differences between the model's state-dependent gaps and pairing tensor and those from Gogny HFB calculations in the canonical basis for protons in 154Sm and 238U. The exact solution was then obtained by solving the set of M non-linear coupled Richardson equations for the pair energies (pairons) in the complex plane.

## Key numbers
- For 154Sm protons: M = 31 pairs, L = 91 active levels, G = 2.24 x 10^-3, Delta = 0.1577, exact correlation energy = 2.9247 (units not specified but presumably MeV)
- For 238U protons: M = 46 pairs, L = 148 active levels, G = 1.99 x 10^-3, Delta = 0.1594, exact correlation energy = 2.6511 (units not specified but presumably MeV)
- The Hilbert space dimension for 154Sm is 1.98 x 10^24 and for 238U is 4.83 x 10^38, far beyond large-scale diagonalization limits
- An energy cutoff of 30 MeV above the Fermi energy was used, with occupation probabilities below 10^-3 above this cutoff
- For the schematic system of M=10 pairs in L=24 equally spaced levels (epsilon_i = i) with cutoff alpha = 24, pairons collapse to a critical value at G approximately 0.012

## Context
This work extends the hyperbolic family of Richardson-Gaudin models, previously applied to p-wave pairing in 2D lattices (Sierra, 2001; Rombouts et al., 2010), to heavy nuclei where the rational (constant pairing) Richardson model fails because it gives a constant gap that cannot reproduce the state-dependent gaps from Gogny force calculations. The exactly solvable Hamiltonian enables benchmarking of approximations beyond HFB (such as particle number projection) in realistic heavy nuclei, and could be combined with self-consistent HF approaches along the lines of Alhassid (2007) for well-bound nuclei, including T=1 proton-neutron pairing.
