# Symmetry Energy Expansion with Strange Dense Matter

> The quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram at large densities and low temperatures can be probed using both neutron stars and low-energy heavy-ion collisions. Heavy-ion collisions are nearly isospin-symmetric systems, whereas neutron stars are highly isospin asymmetric since they are neutron-rich. The symmetry-energy expansion is used to connect these regimes across isospin asymmetry. However, the current symmetry-energy expansion does not account for strange particles. In this work, we include finite strangeness by redefining the isospin-asymmetry parameter and the symmetry-energy expansion in a way that is consistent with QCD SU(3) flavor symmetry. Our new symmetry energy works well beyond typical neutron star central densities and admits a skewness term in the presence of strangeness for the case of weak equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper redefines the isospin-asymmetry parameter to include strangeness, showing that the conventional parameter δ_Q = 1-2Y_Q fails to locate the ground state of nuclear matter when strange particles are present, while the new parameter δ_I = 1+Y_S-2Y_Q restores it. The expansion works well beyond typical neutron star central densities but admits a skewness (δ_I^3) term in weak equilibrium (μ_S=0) where isospin reflection symmetry is broken by finite strangeness.

## Method

The authors generalized the symmetry-energy expansion by redefining the isospin-asymmetry parameter using the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula δ_I = 1+Y_S-2Y_Q, consistent with QCD SU(3) flavor symmetry. They tested this expansion using the chiral mean field (CMF) model at T=0 for two cases: isospin-reflection-symmetric matter (μ_S = -½μ_Q) and weak equilibrium (μ_S=0), fitting the energy per baryon as a function of δ_I to extract expansion coefficients E_sym,2 and E_sym,3.

## Key numbers

- The onset of strangeness in symmetric nuclear matter occurs at n_B ≈ 0.57 fm^{-3} for the CMF model with C2 coupling
- At n_B = 0.99 fm^{-3}, using δ_Q shifts the ground state of nuclear matter to δ_Q ≈ 0.2, while δ_I restores the minimum to δ_I = 0
- At n_B = 0.76 fm^{-3} in weak equilibrium, δ_Q shifts the ground state to δ_Q ≈ 0.03, while δ_I restores it to δ_I = 0
- The truncated Taylor expansion for weak equilibrium begins to fail at around ≈ 5.5 n_sat (where n_sat is saturation density)
- For isospin-reflection-symmetric matter, the expansion around n_sat remains accurate through densities exceeding ≈ 7 n_sat
- At n_B = 0.7 fm^{-3}, the conventional symmetry-energy expansion underestimates E/A by ≈ 80% relative to the underlying equation of state

## Context

This work extends the standard symmetry-energy expansion (Wiringa 1988, Steiner 2006, Drischler 2020) to include finite strangeness, which previous formulations could not handle. Prior work by Providencia (2012, 2013) investigated correlations between symmetry-energy coefficients and strangeness content, and Bednarek (2019) considered proton-neutron asymmetry in hyperonic matter, but neither systematically redefined the asymmetry parameter. The new expansion enables connecting neutron star observations and heavy-ion collision data to constrain the equation of state in the presence of strange particles, and provides a parametrization suitable for Bayesian inference frameworks and transport coefficient calculations in neutron star mergers.
