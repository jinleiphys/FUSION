# Strongly screening electron capture rates of chromium isotopes in presupernova

> Taking into account the effect of electron screening on the electron energy and electron capture threshold energy, by using the method of Shell-Model Monte Carlo and Random Phase Approximation theory, we investigate the strong electron screening capture rates of chromium isotopes according to the linear response theory screening model. The strong screening rates can decrease by about 40.43\% (e.g., for $^{60}$Cr at $T_9=3.44,Y_e=0.43$). Our conclusions may be helpful to the researches of supernova explosion and numerical simulation.

## Key claim

Electron screening reduces electron capture rates on chromium isotopes by up to about 40.43% (e.g., for $^{60}$Cr at $T_9=3.44$, $Y_e=0.43$), but this reduction is strongly dependent on density and temperature, with the screening factor approaching a common value at high density regardless of temperature.

## Method

The authors used Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for chromium isotopes $^{53-60}$Cr, then combined these with Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory and the linear response theory screening model (LRTM) of Itoh et al. (2002) to calculate electron capture rates both with and without strong electron screening. The screening potential was applied to modify both the incident electron energy (reducing it by D) and the capture threshold energy (increasing it by D), and the effective nuclear Q-value was also adjusted for plasma binding effects following Fuller et al. (1982).

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Cr at $T_9=3.44$, $Y_e=0.43$, the strong screening rates decrease by about 40.43% (screening factor C = 0.5957 at $\rho_7 = 115$).
- Minimum screening factors C_min for $^{53-60}$Cr at $T_9=3.44$, $Y_e=0.43$ range from 0.5957 ($^{60}$Cr) to 0.6923 ($^{54}$Cr), corresponding to rate reductions of 40.43% to 30.77%.
- For $^{61}$Cr without screening, the EC rate increases from $6.3096\times10^{-23}$ s$^{-1}$ at $10^7$ g/cm$^3$ to $3.71535\times10^2$ s$^{-1}$ at $10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$ at $T_9=3$ (Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus 1999).
- For $^{60}$Cr without screening, the FFN rates increase from $8.3946\times10^{-26}$ s$^{-1}$ at $10^7$ g/cm$^3$ to $1.2388\times10^3$ s$^{-1}$ at $10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$ at $T_9=3$ (Fuller et al. 1982).
- The screening potential D is given by $D = 7.525\times10^{-3} Z (10z\rho_7/A)^{1/3} J(r_s,R)$ MeV, valid for $10^{-5} \leq r_s \leq 10^{-1}$, $0 \leq R \leq 50$.
- The Q-value shift due to plasma binding is $\Delta Q \approx 2.940\times10^{-5} Z^{2/3} (\rho Y_e)^{1/3}$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of electron capture on chromium isotopes by Aufderheide et al. (1994) and Heger et al. (2001), who did not include strong electron screening effects, and by Nabi et al. (2016), who used pn-QRPA theory but also omitted screening. The results provide screening-corrected electron capture rates for chromium isotopes that can be incorporated into presupernova evolution models and core-collapse simulations, potentially affecting the calculated lepton fraction evolution and supernova dynamics.
