# Transition magnetic moment of $Λ$ $\to$ $Σ^0$ in QCD sum rules

> The $Λ$ $\to$ $Σ^0$ transition magnetic moment is computed in the QCD sum rules approach. Three independent tensor structures are derived in the external field method using generalized interpolating fields. They are analyzed together with the $Λ$ and $Σ^0$ mass sum rules using a Monte-Carlo-based analysis, with attention to OPE convergence, ground-state dominance, and the role of the transitions in the intermediate states. Relations between sum rules for magnetic moments of $Λ$ and $Σ^0$ and sum rules for transition magnetic moment of $Λ$ $\to$ $Σ^0$ are also examined. Our best prediction for the transition magnetic moment is $μ_{Σ^0Λ}= 1.60\pm 0.07\; μ_N$. A comparison is made with other calculations in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper computes the \(\Lambda \to \Sigma^0\) transition magnetic moment using QCD sum rules with generalized interpolating fields and a Monte-Carlo-based analysis, finding a best value of \(\mu_{\Sigma^0\Lambda} = 1.60 \pm 0.07\ \mu_N\). The abstract does not mention that this result depends on fixing the continuum threshold and the mixing parameter \(\beta\) to achieve a stable match, and that the chiral-odd sum rule at structure \(\mathrm{WO}_2\) is judged more reliable than the other two tensor structures.

## Method

The authors use the external-field method in QCD sum rules, starting from a time-ordered correlation function in a static background electromagnetic field. Three independent tensor structures (\(\mathrm{WE}_1\), \(\mathrm{WO}_1\), \(\mathrm{WO}_2\)) are derived from generalized interpolating fields for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^0\) that include a mixing parameter \(\beta\). The QCD side is evaluated via operator product expansion up to dimension 8, including vacuum susceptibilities and strange quark mass effects, and matched to the phenomenological side using a Monte-Carlo procedure that explores uncertainties in all QCD input parameters. The mass sum rules for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^0\) are used in combination with the transition sum rules to cancel the coupling strengths and exponential factors.

## Key numbers

* Transition magnetic moment: \(\mu_{\Sigma^0\Lambda} = 1.60 \pm 0.07\ \mu_N\) (nuclear magnetons), from the chiral-odd \(\mathrm{WO}_2\) sum rule at \(\beta = -0.2\) and continuum threshold \(w = 1.70\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Individual quark contributions from the \(\mathrm{WO}_2\) sum rule: \(\mu_{\Sigma^0\Lambda}^u = 1.05\ \mu_N\), \(\mu_{\Sigma^0\Lambda}^d = 0.53\ \mu_N\), \(\mu_{\Sigma^0\Lambda}^s \approx 0\ \mu_N\).
* Borel window for the \(\mathrm{WO}_2\) sum rule: \(1.20\ \mathrm{GeV}\) to \(1.70\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Continuum thresholds fixed from mass sum rules: \(w_1 = 1.60\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for \(\Lambda\), \(w_2 = 1.66\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for \(\Sigma^0\).
* Transition strength parameter \(A\) from the \(\mathrm{WO}_2\) sum rule: \(0.17(4)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule calculations of octet baryon magnetic moments (Ioffe, 1984; Chiu, 1986; Pasupathy, 1986; Wilson, 1987; Aliev, 2002; Sinha, 2005; Wang, 2008) and provides a comprehensive analysis of the \(\Lambda \to \Sigma^0\) transition, which had received limited attention in the traditional QCD sum rule approach (Zhu, 1998) and in light-cone sum rules (Aliev, 2001). The use of generalized interpolating fields, all three tensor structures, and a Monte-Carlo error analysis enables a more robust determination and allows examination of individual quark contributions. The results complete the electromagnetic structure of the octet baryons from the QCD sum rule perspective and can be compared with future experimental measurements or lattice QCD calculations.
