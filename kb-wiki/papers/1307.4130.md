# Symmetry Energy II: Isobaric Analog States

> Using excitation energies to isobaric analog states (IAS) and charge invariance, we extract nuclear symmetry coefficients, from a mass formula, on a nucleus-by-nucleus basis. Consistently with charge invariance, the coefficients vary weakly across an isobaric chain. However, they change strongly with nuclear mass and range from a_a~10 MeV at mass A~10 to a_a~22 MeV at A~240. Following the considerations of a Hohenberg-Kohn functional for nuclear systems, we determine how to find in practice the symmetry coefficient using neutron and proton densities, even when those densities are simultaneously affected by significant symmetry-energy and Coulomb effects. These results facilitate extracting the symmetry coefficients from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations, that we carry out using a variety of Skyrme parametrizations in the literature. For the parametrizations, we catalog novel short-wavelength instabilities. In comparing the SHF and IAS results for the symmetry coefficients, we arrive at narrow (+-2.4 MeV) constraints on the symmetry energy values S(rho) at 0.04<rho<0.13 fm^-3. Towards normal density the constraints significantly widen, but the normal value of energy a_a^V and the slope parameter L are found to be strongly correlated. To narrow the constraints, we reach for the measurements of asymmetry skins and arrive at a_a^V=(30.2-33.7) MeV and L=(35-70) MeV, with those values being again strongly positively correlated along the diagonal of their combined region. Inclusion of the skin constraints allows to narrow the constraints on S(rho), at 0.04<rho<0.13 fm^-3, down to +-1.1 MeV. Several microscopic calculations, including variational, Bruckner-Hartree-Fock and Dirac-Bruckner-Hartree-Fock, are consistent with our constraint region on S(rho).

## Key claim

Using excitation energies to isobaric analog states (IAS) and charge invariance, the authors extract nuclear symmetry coefficients on a nucleus-by-nucleus basis, finding that the coefficients vary weakly across an isobaric chain but change strongly with nuclear mass. The abstract glosses over the fact that the fitted surface symmetry coefficient likely significantly underestimates that for the limit of half-infinite matter, and that the analysis requires shell corrections and careful handling of correlations due to common ground-state subtraction.

## Method

The authors analyzed excitation energies to ground-state IAS from the compilation by Antony et al. (1997), supplemented by more recent measurements, applying microscopic shell corrections from Koura et al. (2000, 2005) to extract smooth bulk contributions. They extracted generalized mass-dependent asymmetry coefficients a_a(A) from the dependence of corrected excitation energies on the change in isospin squared T(T+1) scaled by mass number A, using a modified chi-squared minimization that accounts for correlations from common ground-state subtraction. For relating nucleus-by-nucleus results to bulk nuclear matter, they performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using various Skyrme parametrizations from the literature, cataloging short-wavelength instabilities and retaining only parametrizations stable in both long- and short-wavelength limits.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry coefficients range from a_a ~ 10 MeV at mass A ~ 10 to a_a ~ 22 MeV at A ~ 240
- For A >= 30, volume-surface competition formula yields a_a^V ~ 33.2 MeV and a_a^S ~ 10.7 MeV
- Comparing SHF and IAS results yields constraints on symmetry energy S(rho) of +/- 2.4 MeV at densities 0.04 < rho < 0.13 fm^-3
- Including asymmetry skin constraints narrows S(rho) constraints to +/- 1.1 MeV at 0.04 < rho < 0.13 fm^-3
- Combined constraints give a_a^V = (30.2 - 33.7) MeV and L = (35 - 70) MeV, with strong positive correlation along the diagonal
- The r.m.s. residual microscopic contribution delta_rms is approximately 0.5 MeV, close to the typical accuracy of mass formulas of about 0.65 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier efforts (Danielewicz et al. 2004, 2007, 2009) on extracting symmetry energy from IAS data, building upon prior work by Kohler (1969, 1976) and Cote et al. (1978) on surface and volume contributions within SHF descriptions. The results enable tighter constraints on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at subnormal densities, and when combined with asymmetry skin measurements, narrow the allowed range for the symmetry energy slope parameter L and volume symmetry coefficient a_a^V. Several microscopic calculations including variational, Bruckner-Hartree-Fock, and Dirac-Bruckner-Hartree-Fock approaches are found consistent with the constraint region on S(rho).
