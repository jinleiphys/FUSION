# Time Dependent Hadronization via HERMES and EMC Data Consistency

> Using QCD-inspired time dependent cross sections for pre-hadrons we provide a combined analysis of available experimental data on hadron attenuation in DIS off nuclei as measured by HERMES with 12 and 27 GeV and by EMC with 100 and 280 GeV lepton beam energies. We extract the complete four-dimensional evolution of the pre-hadrons using the JETSET-part of PYTHIA. We find a remarkable sensitivity of nuclear attenuation data to the details of the time-evolution of cross sections. Only cross sections evolving linearly in time describe the available data in a wide kinematical regime. Predictions for experimental conditions at JLAB (5 and 12 GeV beam energies) are included.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that only a linear time evolution of the pre-hadronic cross section between production and formation times can simultaneously describe hadron attenuation data from both the HERMES experiment (at 12 and 27 GeV beam energies) and the EMC experiment (at 100 and 280 GeV beam energies), while constant or quadratic time dependencies fail to match both datasets.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA event generator (specifically its JETSET string fragmentation component) to extract the full four-dimensional space-time production and formation points for each final hadron in deep inelastic scattering events on nuclei. They then propagated these pre-hadrons through the nuclear medium using a semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that allows elastic and inelastic rescattering with coupled-channel effects, implementing four different scenarios for the time evolution of the pre-hadronic cross section: constant, linear, quadratic, and a quantum diffusion model with a Q^2-dependent pedestal value.

## Key numbers

- HERMES data at 27 GeV beam energy and EMC data at 100 and 280 GeV beam energies were simultaneously described only by a linear time dependence of the pre-hadronic cross section, sigma*(t)/sigma = (t - t_P)/(t_F - t_P)
- For the quadratic scenario, the theoretical attenuation was too weak for both HERMES and EMC, with significant discrepancy for HERMES
- At 5 GeV beam energy, the averaged formation time is approximately 4 fm at z_h approximately 0.8
- For JLAB at 5 GeV, the average invariant mass populated in the first interaction is <W> = 2.2 GeV
- The constant cross section scenario used sigma*/sigma = 0.5 as a fixed ratio
- The quantum diffusion pedestal value was X_0 = r_lead * (1 GeV^2)/Q^2, with the constant chosen as 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Falter et al. (2004) that used constant pre-hadronic cross sections and were unable to simultaneously describe HERMES and EMC data. The analysis is similar in spirit to the quantum diffusion picture proposed by Farrar et al. (1988) and the theoretical framework of Dokshitzer et al. (1991) who argued that quantum mechanical considerations lead to a linear time dependence of the cross section. The results enable predictions for the JLAB/CLAS facility at 5 and 12 GeV beam energies, where the interplay between pre-hadronic and hadronic interactions can be studied, particularly through the different attenuation behavior of K+ and K- mesons.
