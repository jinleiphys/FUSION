# Hard and thermal probes of QGP from the perspective of Lattice QCD

> In this talk I review the current status of lattice QCD results on the hard and thermal probes of QGP, including jet quenching parameters, the melting of quarkonia and open heavy flavours, thermal photon/dilepton rates, electrical conductivity as well as heavy quark diffusion coefficients.

## Key claim

This review of lattice QCD results on hard and thermal probes of QGP summarizes the current status of calculations, noting that many results are still obtained in the quenched (pure gauge) limit and that systematic uncertainties from analytic continuation and finite lattice spacing remain to be fully controlled.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of fluctuations and correlations of conserved quantum numbers (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness, charm) to study the fate of open heavy hadrons; extraction of spectral functions from temporal Euclidean correlation functions via the Maximum Entropy Method to study quarkonium melting; and calculation of spatial correlation functions and screening masses to probe bound-state modifications. For transport properties, the review covers extraction of electrical conductivity and heavy quark diffusion coefficients from the low-frequency limit of vector spectral functions, and evaluation of the jet quenching parameter using electrostatic QCD (EQCD).

## Key numbers

* Transition temperature for the chiral crossover: \(T_c \approx 154\) MeV (HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations)
* In quenched lattice QCD, all charmonia are dissociated at 1.5 \(T_c\); S-wave bottomonia survive up to at least 2 \(T_c\) while P-wave bottomonia melt just above \(T_c\) (2-flavour and 2+1-flavour QCD studies)
* For \(c\bar{c}\) states, only \(\sim 2\%\) deviation of screening mass from pole mass arises at 220 MeV (\(\sim 1.4 T_c\)) in full QCD
* In quenched QCD, \(2\pi T D_c\) (charm quark diffusion coefficient) is temperature-independent and around 2 in the range \(\sim 1.5 T_c\) to \(\sim 3 T_c\); results from Heavy Quark Effective Theory give \(DT\) values 2–3 times larger
* Preliminary EQCD results for the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) are obtained at \(\sim 2 T_c\) and \(\sim 10 T_c\)

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD studies on QGP properties that began with determinations of the crossover temperature (HotQCD, Wuppertal-Budapest) and continues through calculations of quarkonium spectral functions (charm, bottomonia), electromagnetic probes (dilepton/photon rates, electrical conductivity), and transport coefficients (heavy quark diffusion, jet quenching). The results enable improved phenomenological modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, but the review notes that further studies with dynamical quarks, continuum extrapolations, and better control of systematic uncertainties from analytic continuation are still needed.
