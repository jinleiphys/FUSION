# Non-nucleon degrees of freedom in the deuteron from the d(\vec e,e'\vec p,)n break-up

> We analyzed contribution of quark degrees of freedom in the deuteron to the longitudinal, P_z', and transverse, P_x', polarizations of the proton in the d(\vec e,e'\vec p)n break-up. It is demonstrated that such effects work in correct direction to explain experimental data. We predict that the polarizations should change qualitatively behavior at p_m >200 MeV/c, (i) the polarizations become strongly dependent on the out-of-plain angle and (ii) there appears a structure in the both polarizations at p_m ~200 MeV/c.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including quark degrees of freedom (via NN*, N*N, and N*N* components in the deuteron wave function) works in the correct direction to explain experimental data on proton polarization in d(e,e'p)n breakup, but the authors note that they did not take into account meson-exchange currents, isobar currents, or final-state interactions, so the conclusion is conditional on those omissions.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal (P_z') and transverse (P_x') polarizations of the proton in the d(e,e'p)n reaction using the impulse approximation, including elastic electron scattering on the proton and inelastic scattering on the lightest nucleon resonances S11(1535) and D13(1520). The deuteron wave function at short distances was treated as a six-quark object using the Resonating Group Method (RGM), with the RGM distribution function derived from conventional NN deuteron wave functions (Paris and NIJM-2 potentials) modified by the RGM renormalization condition. The calculations were performed at fixed electron kinematics (E=1.669 GeV, E'=1.127 GeV, electron scattering angle 42.65 degrees) for various out-of-plane angles phi and missing momenta p_m.

## Key numbers

- The structure in polarization observables appears at p_m ≈ 230 MeV/c for the Paris potential and at p_m ≈ 200 MeV/c for the NIJM-2 potential.
- The experimental data were measured at incident electron energy E = 1.669 GeV, scattered electron energy E' = 1.127 GeV, and electron scattering angle θ_e = 42.65 degrees.
- The polarizations become strongly dependent on the out-of-plane angle φ for p_m ≳ 200 MeV/c.
- The out-of-plane angles shown in the calculations are φ = 0.57°, 23.5°, and 46.4°.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Kobushkin, Glozman, and others (Kobushkin, Glozman and Kuchina, Glozman and Kobushkin) that argued for the importance of non-nucleon degrees of freedom in the deuteron at internal momenta k > 200 MeV/c, based on both experimental evidence from JINR and theoretical RGM calculations. The paper directly compares its predictions to experimental data from Hu et al. (2009) and shows that including quark degrees of freedom improves agreement with those measurements. The results suggest that future experiments measuring polarization observables at p_m > 200 MeV/c could provide a strong test for the deuteron's quark structure at short distances.
