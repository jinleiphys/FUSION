# Lifetime measurements of 214Po and 212Po with the CTF liquid scintillator detector at LNGS

> We have studied the alpha decays of 214Po into 210Pb and of 212Po into 208Pb tagged by the coincidence with the preceding beta decays from 214Bi and 212Bi, respectively. The employed 222Rn, 232Th, and 220Rn sources were sealed inside quartz vials and inserted in the Counting Test Facility at the underground Gran Sasso National Laboratory in Italy. We find that the mean lifetime of 214Po is (236.00 +- 0.42(stat) +- 0.15(syst)) μs and that of 212Po is (425.1 +- 0.9(stat) +- 1.2(syst)) ns. Our results, obtained from data with signal-to-background ratio larger than 1000, reduce the overall uncertainties and are compatible with previous measurements.

## Key claim

The mean lifetime of 214Po is (236.00 ± 0.42(stat) ± 0.15(syst)) μs and that of 212Po is (425.1 ± 0.9(stat) ± 1.2(syst)) ns, obtained from data with a signal-to-background ratio larger than 1000; these results reduce the overall uncertainties compared to previous measurements and are compatible with them, but the abstract does not mention that the 212Po result is a weighted average of two separate source measurements (426.5 ± 1.4 ± 1.2 ns and 424.2 ± 1.1 ± 1.2 ns) that differ by about 2 ns.

## Method

The alpha decays of 214Po and 212Po were tagged via fast coincidence with the preceding beta decays of 214Bi and 212Bi, respectively, using the Counting Test Facility (CTF) liquid scintillator detector at the Gran Sasso National Laboratory. Sources were prepared by dissolving 222Rn (for 214Po) or using 232Th dissolved in TriButyl Phosphate and a 220Rn gas transfer system (for two separate 212Po sources) in quartz vials inserted at the detector center. The mean lifetimes were extracted using three independent analysis methods: a χ² minimization with a penalty factor for background, an analytical fit including exponential background, and an unbinned mean value approach with corrections for finite time window and constant background.

## Key numbers

- 214Po mean lifetime: (236.00 ± 0.42(stat) ± 0.15(syst)) μs, corresponding to a half-life of (163.6 ± 0.3) μs, from approximately 3.9 × 10⁵ candidate events over 10.2 days live-time
- 212Po mean lifetime (combined result): (425.1 ± 0.9(stat) ± 1.2(syst)) ns, corresponding to a half-life of (294.7 ± 1.0) ns, from approximately 2.8 × 10⁵ candidate events across two sources
- 212Po mean lifetime from first source: (426.5 ± 1.4(stat) ± 1.2(syst)) ns, from approximately 1.1 × 10⁵ candidates over 6.3 days
- 212Po mean lifetime from second source: (424.2 ± 1.1(stat) ± 1.2(syst)) ns, from approximately 1.7 × 10⁵ candidates over 15.5 days
- Signal-to-background ratio for 214Po with energy cuts: approximately 1.82 × 10⁴ (about 3.1 × 10⁵ signal events versus 17 background events in the selected time window)
- Signal-to-background ratios for 212Po sources with energy cuts: approximately 2.2 × 10³ (first source) and 3.3 × 10³ (second source)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of 214Po and 212Po lifetimes, which had larger uncertainties and were performed with lower signal-to-background ratios, as compiled in the Nuclear Data Sheets (2009) and Table de Radionucléides (2007, 2010). The results are compatible with earlier measurements by von Dardel (1950), Dobrowolski (1961), and Alexeyev (2013) for 214Po, and by Bunyan (1949), Astner (1963), and Sanyal (1975) for 212Po, but with significantly reduced uncertainties. The improved precision enables more accurate modeling of natural radioactivity backgrounds in low-background experiments such as Borexino and supports studies of antineutrino spectra relevant to geoneutrino research.
