# Erratum: Investigating Neutron Polarizabilities through Compton Scattering on ${}^3$He

> We provide updated predictions for elastic γ${}^3$He cross sections and asymmetries that correct erroneous results we published in Phys. Rev. Lett. 98, 232303 (2007) and Nucl. Phys. A 819, 98 (2009).

## Key claim

The corrected chiral perturbation theory calculation yields elastic gamma-3He cross sections and asymmetries that are larger than previously reported, but the qualitative conclusions about neutron polarizability sensitivity remain unchanged.

## Method

The authors corrected three errors in their earlier computer code for the two-nucleon Compton operator at O(Q^3) in chiral perturbation theory: the isospin dependence of the operator was missing, a factor of two was omitted in the S=0 to S=1 transition piece, and the implementation of diagram E's S=0 to S=1 part was wrong. The corrected calculations used the chiral EFT Idaho NN potential at N3LO combined with an N2LO three-nucleon force to generate cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The corrected O(e^2 Q) cross section at omega_cm = 60 MeV is approximately 10% larger than the old incorrect result.
- At omega_cm = 120 MeV, the corrected cross section is 50% larger at forward angles and 60% larger at backward angles.
- Cross section differences for polarized targets are now approximately 20% larger in Fig. 3 (Delta_z) and approximately 40% larger in Fig. 4 (Delta_x) compared to the erroneous results.
- The corrected results are shown at photon energies of 60, 80, and 120 MeV in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context

This erratum corrects erroneous results published in Shukla et al. (2007) in Physical Review Letters and Shukla et al. (2009) in Nuclear Physics A. The corrected calculations strengthen the conclusion that the Compton cross section on 3He is significantly larger than on deuterium, and that 3He has larger sensitivity to neutron electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities than the deuteron. A concurrent publication by Margaryan (2017) presents corrected versions of all O(Q^3) calculations and extends them to an EFT with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom.
