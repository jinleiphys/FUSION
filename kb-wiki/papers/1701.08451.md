# $D$ - meson production at very forward rapidities: estimating the intrinsic charm contribution

> We study $D$ - meson production at forward rapidities taking into account the non - linear effects in the QCD dynamics and the intrinsic charm component of the proton wave function. The total cross section, the rapidity distributions and the Feynman - $x$ distributions are calculated for $p p$ collisions at different center of mass energies. Our results show that, at the LHC, the intrinsic charm component changes the $D$ rapidity distributions in a region which is beyond the coverage of the LHCb detectors. At higher energies the IC component dominates the $y$ and $x_F$ distributions exactly in the range where the produced $D$ mesons decay and contribute the most to the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux measured by the ICECUBE Collaboration. We compute the $x_F$ - distributions and demonstrate that they are enhanced at LHC energies by approximately one order of magnitude in the $0.2 \le x_F \le 0.8$ range.

## Key claim

The intrinsic charm (IC) component of the proton wave function enhances D-meson production at very forward rapidities by approximately one order of magnitude in the Feynman-x range \(0.2 \le x_F \le 0.8\) at LHC energies, but this enhancement occurs in a rapidity region beyond the coverage of the LHCb detectors; at higher energies (UHECR range), the IC component dominates the rapidity and \(x_F\) distributions exactly where D-meson decays contribute most to the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux measured by ICECUBE.

## Method

The authors computed D-meson production in pp collisions using two contributions: (1) gluon-gluon interactions described by the color dipole formalism, where a gluon fluctuates into a \(c\bar{c}\) pair and interacts with the target via the dipole-nucleon scattering amplitude; and (2) charm-in-the-initial-state contributions, where intrinsic charm quarks from the projectile interact with the dense target within the DGLAP \(\otimes\) CGC factorization scheme. The dipole-nucleon scattering amplitude was modeled using the BUW parametrization updated to describe LHC forward particle production data, and the parton distributions were taken from the CTEQ 6.5 leading-order parametrization with the maximum intrinsic charm normalization (approximately 3.5%) for the BHPS, meson cloud, and sea-like models.

## Key numbers

* Total charm production cross section at high energies is reasonably well described assuming charm mass \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV, with predictions using CTEQ 6.5 without intrinsic component matching experimental data from LHCb and ALICE.
* The rapidity distribution ratio between BHPS2 intrinsic charm predictions and the standard CTEQ 6.5 (no IC) reaches a factor of approximately 8 at very forward rapidities, with the position of the maximum shifting to larger rapidities as center-of-mass energy increases.
* The Feynman-x distributions are enhanced by approximately one order of magnitude in the range \(0.2 \le x_F \le 0.8\) at LHC energies when intrinsic charm is included.
* The total charm cross section predictions using different intrinsic charm models (BHPS2, MC2, SL2) are almost identical, differing only at the level of a few percent, because the total cross section is dominated by central rapidities where both parton x values are small.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed in Carvalho et al. (2016) for D-meson production at forward rapidities by consistently including the gluon-gluon contribution to charm production, which was previously treated only as a background using PYTHIA. The study is motivated by the need to understand the prompt neutrino background for astrophysical neutrino measurements by ICECUBE, as discussed in Enberg et al. (2008) and Laha et al. (2014). The results enable improved estimates of the prompt atmospheric neutrino flux at ultra-high energies and provide predictions for future forward-physics measurements at the LHC and for the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment.
