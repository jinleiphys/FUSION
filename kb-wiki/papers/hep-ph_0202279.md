# Predictions for deeply virtual Compton scattering on a spin-one target

> We consider hard leptoproduction of a photon on a spin-one target and give the general azimuthal angular dependence of the differential cross section. Furthermore, we estimate the beam spin asymmetry for an unpolarized deuteron target at HERMES.

## Key claim

The paper provides the general azimuthal angular dependence of the differential cross section for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) on a spin-one target, and estimates the beam spin asymmetry for an unpolarized deuteron target at HERMES kinematics, noting that the approximations used are "rather crude" and the result "can serve to obtain a qualitative understanding" of the generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion (OPE) approach at leading twist-two and leading order in perturbation theory to derive the Fourier decomposition of the squared Bethe-Heitler (BH), squared DVCS, and interference terms for a spin-1 target. They then estimate the beam spin asymmetry for deuteron by approximating the Compton form factor (CFF) as a product of the elastic form factor and valence quark distributions, using the MRS A' parametrization, and comparing with the analogous proton case.

## Key numbers

- For deuteron target at HERMES kinematics with Q² = 4 GeV², x_B = 0.1, and Δ² = -0.3 GeV²: A_LU(φ) ~ -0.13 sin(φ)
- For proton target under the same kinematics: A_LU(φ) ~ -0.16 sin(φ)
- For positron-proton scattering at HERMES with ⟨x_B⟩ = 0.11, ⟨Δ²⟩ = -0.27 GeV², and ⟨Q²⟩ = 2.6 GeV²: A_LU(φ) ~ 0.3 sin(φ)
- The deuteron mass is M = 1875.6 MeV, requiring Q² ≥ 4 GeV² to control M²/Q² corrections

## Context

This work extends the DVCS formalism from spin-0 and spin-1/2 targets (previously developed by Belitsky, Müller, Kirchner, and Schäfer) to spin-1 targets, specifically the deuteron. It enables experimental access to the nine twist-two generalized parton distributions of the deuteron through Fourier analysis of azimuthal angular dependencies, complementing information from deep-inelastic scattering (as studied by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar, 1989) and elastic scattering (as studied by Brodsky, Ji, and Lepage, 1983; Gilman and Gross, 2001). The predictions are consistent with the oversimplified model that describes existing DVCS data from H1 (Adloff et al., 2001), HERMES (Airapetian et al., 2001), and CLAS (Stepanyan et al., 2001).
