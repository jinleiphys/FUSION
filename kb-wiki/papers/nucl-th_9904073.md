# Tri-axial Octupole Deformations and Shell Structure

> Manifestations of pronounced shell effects are discovered when adding nonaxial octupole deformations to a harmonic oscillator model. The degeneracies of the quantum spectra are in a good agreement with the corresponding main periodic orbits and winding number ratios which are found by classical analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that phase transitions in finite systems can be defined and classified by the topology of the microcanonical entropy surface \(S(E,N)\) and its curvature determinant \(D(E,N)\), without taking the thermodynamic limit; the authors claim this approach reveals about half of the phase space that is lost in conventional canonical treatments.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical Monte Carlo simulations of a \(q=3\)-state Potts lattice gas on a \(50\times50\) square lattice, covering the entire \(\{E,N\}\) space with a mesh of about 1000 knots at spacings \(\Delta\epsilon=0.04\) and \(\Delta n=0.02\). At each knot they collected approximately \(2\times10^8\) events to construct histograms for the probability of the system being in a narrow region of phase space, from which local derivatives \(\beta = (\partial S/\partial E)_N\) and \(\beta\mu = -(\partial S/\partial N)_E\) were extracted and interpolated to obtain the full entropy surface and its curvature determinant.

## Key numbers

* The multicritical point \(P_m\) was located at \(\beta_m = 1.48 \pm 0.03\), \(\beta\mu_m = -2.67 \pm 0.02\), corresponding to \(\epsilon_m \sim -1\) and \(n_m \sim 0.7\).
* The continuous phase transition of the ordinary \(q=3\)-Potts model occurs at \(n=1\) and \(\epsilon = -1.57\) (near point \(C\)).
* The simulation used a \(50\times50\) lattice (\(L^2 = 2500\) sites) with a mesh spacing of \(\Delta\epsilon = 0.04\) and \(\Delta n = 0.02\).

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical thermostatics approach previously developed by Gross and collaborators for atomic clusters and nuclei (Gross 1997, Gross 1998), and applies it to the \(q=3\) Potts lattice gas model. The authors contrast their method with conventional canonical thermodynamics (Gibbs 1902, Lee and Yang 1952), arguing that the canonical partition sum loses information about the convex intruder in the entropy surface that characterizes first-order phase transitions. The results enable the study of phase transitions in finite and non-extensive systems, including the identification of multicritical points, without requiring the thermodynamic limit.
