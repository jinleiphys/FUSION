# Deformation and weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei

> We use the self-consistent mean-field theory to discuss the ground state and decay properties of $Λ$ hypernuclei. We first discuss the deformation of $Λ$ hypernuclei using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach. We show that, although most of hypernuclei have a similar deformation parameter to the core nucleus, the shape of $^{28}$Si is drastically altered, from oblately deformed to spherical, if a $Λ$ particle is added to this nucleus. We then discuss the pionic weak decay of neutron-rich $Λ$ hypernuclei using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS method. We show that, for a given isotope chain, the decay rate increases as a function of mass number, due to the strong neutron-proton interaction.

# Deformation and weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that while most Lambda hypernuclei have a deformation parameter similar to their core nucleus, the addition of a Lambda particle to 28Si drastically alters its shape from oblate to spherical; this shape change occurs because the energy difference between the oblate and spherical configurations in 28Si is only 0.754 MeV and can be easily inverted by the Lambda particle.

## Method
The authors use the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the NL3 parameter set to compute quadrupole deformation parameters for Si isotopes and their corresponding hypernuclei, solving the Dirac equations for nucleons and the Lambda particle self-consistently with axial symmetry assumed. For the pionic weak decay study, they employ the non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock method with the SIII parameter set and the BCS approximation for pairing, using the formalism of Bando and Takaki (1984) to evaluate the decay width for C isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The energy difference between the oblate and spherical configurations in 28Si is 0.754 MeV.
- The total decay width of a free Lambda particle is 2.50 x 10^-12 MeV, used as the unit for plotting pionic decay widths.
- The pionic decay width for the proton mode (Lambda to p + pi-) increases in neutron-rich C isotopes, while the neutron mode (Lambda to n + pi0) is suppressed due to the Pauli principle.

## Context
This work extends prior self-consistent mean-field studies of hypernuclei, which had successfully reproduced the mass number dependence of Lambda binding energy from 12-Lambda-C to 208-Lambda-Pb (Rayet 1981, Rufa et al. 1990, Lanskoy and Yamamoto 1997, Mares and Jerusalimov 1994). The deformation study complements a similar non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock investigation by Zhou et al. (2007) by using the relativistic mean-field method as an alternative interaction choice. The pionic decay analysis for neutron-rich hypernuclei addresses an open question about how the pionic decay mode is suppressed as neutron number increases, and the authors note that a similar conclusion about the proton mode enhancement was reached with shell model calculations by Motoba and Itonaga (1994). This work enables further studies of how the neutron-drip line may be extended by the addition of a Lambda particle, as previously discussed by Vretenar et al. (1998) and Zhou et al. (2008).
