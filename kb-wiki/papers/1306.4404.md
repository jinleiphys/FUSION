# Friedel crystals and the outer crust of magnetars

> The strong magnetic fields found on the surface of magnetars are known to have profound effects in the physics of atoms in magnetar envelopes. We argue that the Friedel oscillations in the Coulomb force between the ions due to electron shielding can, for certain values of the parameters, be the dominant effect determining the crystal structure in the outer crust of magnetars. We estimate the densities and magnetic fields for which this occurs, compute some of the elastic moduli and lattice phonon dispersion relations in this "Friedel crystal" phase.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the outer crust of magnetars, under specific conditions of density and magnetic field, the long-range oscillatory Friedel oscillations in the ion-ion potential (rather than the short-range screened Coulomb repulsion) can become the dominant effect determining the crystal structure, leading to a "Friedel crystal" phase where ions are spaced by exactly half the electron Fermi wavelength along the magnetic field direction.

## Method

The authors computed the electron-screened ion-ion potential in a strong magnetic field using the one-loop electron-hole polarization function from Sharma and Reddy (2010), then minimized the lattice energy per ion for a tetragonal lattice to determine the equilibrium longitudinal spacing. They derived analytic expressions for the elastic moduli tensor components λ^{zz}_{zz} and λ^{zz}_{xx} by summing the Friedel potential contributions over ions along the magnetic field direction, and verified these approximations with numerical lattice sums.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal lattice spacing is fixed at a_parallel = π/k_e, where k_e is the electron Fermi momentum; numerically 1/k_e ≈ 3.0 × 10^{-11} cm × (A_66/Z_28) × (B_15/ρ_8)
- The Friedel crystal exists for mass densities ρ ≲ 5.2 × 10^8 g/cm^3 × (A_66/Z_28) × B_15^{3/2} (where all electrons are in the lowest Landau level)
- The elastic modulus λ^{zz}_{zz} ≈ 5.9 × 10^{29} erg/cm^3 × (Z_28^4 ρ_8^3)/(A_66^3 B_15 T_1^2) in most of the parameter space
- The melting temperature for the longitudinal structure is T_melt ≈ 1 MeV × (A_66^2 B_15^3)/(ρ_8^2), far above magnetar crust temperatures
- Quantum fluctuations give root-mean-square displacement relative to lattice spacing ≈ 0.08 × (Z_28^2 ρ_8^4 / A_66^5 B_15^5)^{1/4}

## Context

This work extends the study of electron screening in strong magnetic fields by Sharma and Reddy (2010), who first computed the anisotropic Friedel oscillations in the ion-ion potential and conjectured they could affect crystal structure. It contrasts with prior calculations of neutron star crust elastic properties by Fuchs (1936), Ogata and Ichimaru (1990), Strohmayer et al. (1991), Horowitz and Hughto (2008), and Baiko (2011), none of which considered the effect of high magnetic fields on lattice structure. The results enable computation of phonon dispersion relations and elastic moduli for magnetar crust seismology, particularly for interpreting quasi-periodic oscillations observed in giant flares.
