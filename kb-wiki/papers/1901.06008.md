# Pion structure in a nuclear medium

> The structure and electroweak properties of the pion in symmetric nuclear matter are presented in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The pion is described as a bound state of a dressed quark-antiquark pair governed by the Bethe-Salpeter equation. For the in-medium current-light-quark properties we use the quark-meson coupling model, which describes successfully the properties of hadron in a nuclear medium. We found that the light-quark condensates, the pion decay constant and pion-quark coupling constant decrease with increasing nuclear matter density. We then predict the modifications of the charge radius of the charged pion in nuclear matter.

# Pion Structure in a Nuclear Medium

## Key claim
The electroweak properties of the pion in symmetric nuclear matter are modified by the surrounding medium, with the light-quark condensates, pion decay constant, and pion-quark coupling constant all decreasing as nuclear matter density increases, while the pion mass remains nearly constant up to about normal nuclear density. The authors predict that the charge radius of the charged pion increases with nuclear matter density, but this prediction is based on a model that assumes in-medium light-quark properties derived from nucleon properties may be applied to quarks inside other hadrons.

## Method
The authors combine two theoretical frameworks: the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, in which the pion is described as a bound state of a dressed quark-antiquark pair governed by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which provides the in-medium current-light-quark properties. The in-medium quark propagator is modified by a vector field, and the electromagnetic form factor of the positively charged pion is calculated using a dressed quark-photon vertex, with the vector field eliminated by a shift of integration variable.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density is taken as $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- The current quark mass used in the QMC model is $m_q = 16.4$ MeV
- The binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is 15.7 MeV
- The in-medium pion form factor is suppressed by about 10% at normal nuclear density
- The pion mass is found to be almost constant up to $1.25 \rho_0$ (1.25 times normal nuclear density)

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Hutauruk et al. (2016) and Hutauruk (2018) to study pion electroweak properties in nuclear matter, using the QMC model originally developed by Guichon (1988) which has been successfully applied to many nuclear and hadron systems. The study is connected to the broader investigation of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear environments, as discussed by Brown and Rho (1991, 1996), and relates to the EMC effect observed by the European Muon Collaboration (1983). The results enable predictions for how pion structure changes in dense nuclear matter, which could be tested in future experiments involving pions in nuclear targets.
