# Dependence of total kinetic energy of fission fragments on the excitation energy of fissioning systems

> We elucidated the reason why the average total kinetic energy (TKE) of fission fragments decreases when the excitation energy of the fissioning systems increases as indicated by experimental data for the neutron-induced fission events. To explore this problem, we used a method based on the four-dimensional Langevin equations we have developed. We have calculated the TKE of fission fragments for fissioning systems $^{236}$U$^{*}$ and $^{240}$Pu$^{*}$ excited above respective fission barriers, and compared the results with experimental data for n + $^{235}$U and n + $^{239}$Pu reactions, respectively. From the Langevin-model analysis, we have found that the shape of the abundant heavy fragments changes from almost spherical for low excitation domain to highly prolate shape for high excitation energy, while that of the light fragments does not change noticeably. The change of the "shape" of the heavy fragments causes an increase of a distance between the charge centers of the nascent fragments just after scission as excitation energy increases. Accordingly, the Coulomb repulsion between the two fragments decreases with an increase of the excitation energy, which causes the decrease of the average TKE. In this manner, we found that the change of the shape of the heavy fragment as a function of the excitation energy is the key issue for the TKE of fission fragments to decrease as the excitation energy of the fissioning nuclei increases. In other words, washing out of the shell effects which affect the shape of the heavy fragments is the key reason for the decreasing energy dependence of the average TKE of the fission fragments.

## Key claim

The decrease of average total kinetic energy (TKE) of fission fragments with increasing excitation energy of the fissioning system is caused by the washing out of shell effects that keep the heavy fragment nearly spherical at low excitation; at higher excitation the heavy fragment becomes more prolate, increasing the distance between charge centers at scission and thereby reducing Coulomb repulsion.

## Method

The authors used a four-dimensional Langevin model with shape parameters (elongation, two fragment deformations, and mass asymmetry) from the two-center shell model, solving the Langevin equations with collective inertia from the Werner-Wheeler approximation and friction from the wall-and-window formula. They calculated TKE for fissioning systems \(^{236}\)U\(^{*}\) and \(^{240}\)Pu\(^{*}\) at excitation energies above their fission barriers, and compared results with experimental data for neutron-induced fission of \(^{235}\)U and \(^{239}\)Pu. The dumping factor \(k_s\) for wall-and-window friction was adjusted to 0.55 to reproduce absolute TKE values, and zero-point energies of (1,1,1,2) MeV were assigned to the four dynamical variables.

## Key numbers

- Average TKE for \(^{236}\)U\(^{*}\) and \(^{240}\)Pu\(^{*}\) decreases with increasing neutron energy; calculated values agree with experimental data within 1 MeV for neutron energies below 6 MeV, corresponding to about 0.6% accuracy.
- The dumping factor \(k_s\) was set to 0.55; changing \(k_s\) from 0.27 to 0.55 gives a constant shift to average TKE of about 3 to 4 MeV but does not affect the dependence on excitation energy.
- Zero-point energies \(\{\hbar\omega_\mu\} = (1,1,1,2)\) MeV were used; \(\hbar\omega_4=2\) MeV was fixed because \(\hbar\omega_4=1\) MeV gave too sharp mass distributions and \(\hbar\omega_4=3\) MeV gave too large symmetric components.
- The average quadrupole moment \(\langle Q_{20}(A)\rangle\) of heavy fragments (A=130-144) is about 2-3 barns at low excitation, while light fragments (A=90-110) show \(\langle Q_{20}(A)\rangle \simeq 7-8\) barns; the dip at A=130 disappears gradually as excitation energy increases above 17 MeV.
- At excitation energies above 47 MeV, the shapes of both light and heavy fragments become independent of excitation energy, entering the liquid-drop regime.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-dimensional Langevin studies (Ishizuka 2017, Usang 2019) that explained fission fragment mass distributions and TKE through a correlated twin transition, and predicted a new super asymmetric fission mode in superheavy nuclei (Ishizuka 2020). The present study resolves a long-standing puzzle noted in experimental data (Dyachenko, Surin, Akimov, Duke 2015) that TKE decreases with increasing neutron energy, contrary to naive expectation. The results enable improved modeling of prompt neutron and gamma emission in nuclear reactors, and the authors note that inclusion of multichance fission effects is ongoing for future work.
