# An alternative scheme for pionless EFT: neutron-deuteron scattering in the doublet S-wave

> Using the effective-range expansion for the two-body amplitudes may generate spurious sub-threshold poles outside of the convergence range of the expansion. In the infinite volume, the emergence of such poles leads to the breakdown of unitarity in the three-body amplitude. We discuss the extension of our alternative subtraction scheme for including effective range corrections in pionless effective field theory for spinless bosons to nucleons. In particular, we consider the neutron-deuteron system in the doublet S-wave channel explicitly.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed subtraction scheme for removing spurious poles from resummed effective-range corrections in pionless EFT from spinless bosons to nucleons, specifically for neutron-deuteron scattering in the doublet S-wave (triton channel). The justification for the scheme must be modified for nucleons because the SU(4) symmetry of the three-nucleon Lagrangian prevents absorbing the subtraction-induced polynomial into a single three-body force, so the authors *postulate* that the subtracted and unsubtracted resummations are equivalent at low energies.

## Method

The authors use the dimer formalism of pionless EFT to write coupled Faddeev equations for the spin-triplet and spin-singlet neutron-dimer amplitudes in the doublet S-wave channel. They replace the standard dimer propagator, which contains a spurious high-momentum pole from non-perturbative effective-range resummation, with a modified propagator where the spurious pole is subtracted and expanded as a low-energy polynomial in the boosted relative momentum. The three-body forces H0 and H2 are fitted to the experimental nd scattering length and to a phenomenological phase shift calculation, respectively.

## Key numbers

* Spin-triplet effective range parameters: a_t = 5.42 fm, r_t = 1.76 fm, giving deuteron binding energy 2.2 MeV.
* Spin-singlet effective range parameters: a_s = -23.71 fm, r_s = 2.37 fm.
* Physical pole positions: triplet bound state at q*_{1,t} = 45.72 MeV; singlet virtual state at q*_{2,s} = -7.89 MeV.
* Spurious pole positions: triplet at q*_{2,t} = 178.50 MeV; singlet at q*_{2,s} = 152.45 MeV.
* The LO three-body force H0 is fitted to the experimental nd scattering length a_{nd}^{1/2} = (0.65 ± 0.04) fm (Dilg 1971).
* The N2LO three-body force H2 is fitted to the real part of the doublet phase shift from Kievsky et al. (1996) at momentum p ≈ 85 MeV.
* The breakdown scale is taken as the pion mass, Λ_b = 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the subtraction scheme developed by Ebert et al. (2021) for spinless bosons to the three-nucleon system, and was tested in finite volume by Pang et al. (2022). It addresses a known problem in pionless EFT where non-perturbative resummation of effective-range corrections introduces spurious poles that violate unitarity in the three-body amplitude, as noted by Bedaque et al. (1997). The scheme enables consistent finite-volume calculations of the three-nucleon system for lattice QCD applications, following the NREFT quantization condition approach of Hammer et al. (2017).
