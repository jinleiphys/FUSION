# The Role of the Dilaton in Dense Skyrmion Matter

> In this note, we report on a remarkable and surprising interplay between the omega meson and the dilaton chi in the structure of a single skyrmion as well as in the phase structure of dense skyrmion matter which may have a potentially important consequence on the properties of compact stars. In our continuing effort to understand hadronic matter at high density, we have developed a unified field theoretic formalism for dense skyrmion matter using a single Lagrangian to describe simultaneously both matter and meson fluctuations and studied in-medium properties of hadrons. The effective theory used is the Skyrme model Lagrangian gauged with the vector mesons rho and omega, implemented with the dilaton field that describes the spontaneously broken scale symmetry of QCD, in a form consistent with the symmetries of QCD and our expectations regarding the high density limit. We analyze the restoration of scale invariance and chiral symmetry as the density of the system increases. In order to preserve the restoration of scale symmetry and chiral symmetry, signalled in our case by the vanishing of the expectation value of the dilaton, and to be consistent with the vector manifestation of hidden local symmetry, a density dependent omega coupling is introduced. We uncover the crucial role played by both the dilaton and the omega meson in the phase structure of dense medium and discover how two different phase transition regimes arise as we dial the dilaton mass.

# The Role of the Dilaton in Dense Skyrmion Matter

## Key claim

The paper claims that a density-dependent coupling between the omega meson and the baryon current, introduced by multiplying the Wess-Zumino term by a factor of (χ/f_χ)^3, resolves a conflict between the Skyrme model with vector mesons and the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point of hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory. The resolution is conditional on the dilaton mass: for "low" dilaton masses (≤ 1200 MeV) the model predicts a single chiral phase transition with simultaneous restoration of scale and chiral symmetry, while for "high" dilaton masses (≥ 1300 MeV) a two-step transition occurs through an intermediate half-skyrmion phase.

## Method

The authors construct a Skyrme model Lagrangian gauged with ρ and ω vector mesons and including a dilaton field χ that implements spontaneously broken scale symmetry of QCD. They modify the Wess-Zumino term from its standard scale-invariant form ω_μ B^μ to (χ/f_χ)^3 ω_μ B^μ, which acts as an effective density-dependent coupling. For the B=1 sector, they solve the hedgehog ansatz numerically for the profile functions F(r), G(r), W(r), and C(r) at various dilaton masses. For dense matter, they place skyrmions on an FCC crystal lattice and compute the expectation values ⟨χ⟩ and ⟨σ⟩ as functions of the lattice size L_F (related to baryon density by ρ_B = 1/2L_F^3), studying the phase structure for different dilaton masses.

## Key numbers

- For the B=1 skyrmion in the long-range dilaton (LRD) regime (m_χ = 720 MeV): baryon mean square radius √⟨r²⟩_B = 0.12 fm, energy mean square radius √⟨r²⟩_E = 0.39 fm, soliton mass M_sol = 916 MeV.
- For the B=1 skyrmion in the short-range dilaton (SRD) regime (m_χ = 2000 MeV): baryon mean square radius √⟨r²⟩_B = 0.50 fm, energy mean square radius √⟨r²⟩_E = 0.60 fm, soliton mass M_sol = 1459 MeV.
- The transition between LRD and SRD regimes for the single skyrmion occurs abruptly between m_χ = 1200 MeV and m_χ = 1300 MeV.
- In the LRD regime for dense matter (m_χ = 720 MeV), the phase transition where ⟨χ⟩ vanishes occurs at about three times nuclear matter density n_0 (nuclear matter density ρ_0 = 0.17/fm³ corresponds to L_F ≈ 1.43 fm).
- In the SRD regime for dense matter (m_χ = 3000 MeV), the half-skyrmion phase with ⟨σ⟩ = 0 but ⟨χ⟩ ≠ 0 appears at a density n_p lower than the critical density n_c where ⟨χ⟩ vanishes.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers (Park et al., 2005-2007) on dense skyrmion matter simulated on crystal lattices, which had incorporated vector mesons and the dilaton into the Skyrme Lagrangian. It directly addresses a problem identified in that earlier work: the omega meson mass could not vanish at high density as required by the vector manifestation fixed point of HLS theory (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003), leading to unphysically strong repulsion. The paper also connects to the half-skyrmion phase transition predicted by symmetry arguments on crystal lattices (Goldhaber and Manton, 1987; Kugler and Shtrikman, 1989) and to Georgi's (1990) vector symmetry enhancement SU(2)² → SU(2)⁴. The results have potential consequences for compact star phenomenology, including kaon condensation (Pandharipande et al., 1995), maximum neutron star masses (Akmal et al., 1998), and low-mass black hole formation (Brown et al., 1999).
