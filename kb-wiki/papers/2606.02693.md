# Stochastic Dynamics of Heavy Quarks in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> We study the stochastic dynamics of heavy quarks propagating through the strongly coupled plasma of $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory at nonzero temperature in terms of the corresponding Kolmogorov equation, which correctly describes their kinetic equilibration and the non-Gaussian fluctuations in their momenta without having to restrict their velocity to the non-relativistic regime. Leveraging the heavy quark limit, we show that the evolution of the momentum space distribution function can be reformulated as a Hamilton-Jacobi problem, and therefore can be solved in terms of first-order ordinary differential equations. We solve these evolution equations in an infinite thermal plasma with a constant temperature for initial conditions specified by spherically symmetric heavy quark momentum distributions with a phenomenologically motivated shape that is steeply falling at large momentum. To highlight the distinctive features of the kinetic equilibration process, we compare their solutions with Fokker-Planck dynamics with the same drag coefficient and fluctuations chosen by hand to guarantee equilibration. We find qualitatively similar dynamics at small momentum, and very different dynamics at large momentum, where, much like in jet quenching phenomena, the steepness of the momentum distribution gives a larger relevance to unlikely events in which a heavy quark loses little momentum in a given time step. Such events are much less unlikely in the Kolmogorov evolution than in Fokker-Planck evolution with the same mean energy loss, meaning that equilibration at large momentum is significantly delayed. Our results provide a systematic description of heavy quarks propagating through strongly coupled plasma from the ultra-relativistic to the non-relativistic regime and point the way towards implementation in phenomenological studies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Kolmogorov transport equation for heavy quarks in strongly coupled plasma can be reformulated as a Hamilton-Jacobi problem solvable via first-order ordinary differential equations, and that at large momentum the full Kolmogorov dynamics yields significantly delayed equilibration compared to Fokker-Planck dynamics with the same drag coefficient, because non-Gaussian fluctuations make large momentum-loss events much less unlikely than in the Fokker-Planck approximation.

## Method

The authors derive the Kolmogorov equation for the heavy quark momentum distribution in the heavy quark limit (leading order in T/M) from the probability distribution P(k;v,t) of momentum transfers, computed explicitly in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory at large N_c and large 't Hooft coupling lambda via gauge-gravity duality. They reformulate this first-order nonlinear partial differential equation as a Hamilton-Jacobi problem, solving it by the method of characteristics using Hamiltonian evolution equations for the dimensionless momentum u = p/M and its conjugate variable x = partial f/partial u. They solve these evolution equations numerically for spherically symmetric initial momentum distributions that are steeply falling at large momentum, comparing the results to Fokker-Planck dynamics with the same drag coefficient and fluctuations chosen by hand to guarantee equilibration.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless time variable is defined as tau = t * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 / M, where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling, T is the plasma temperature, and M is the heavy quark mass.
* The dimensionless momentum transfer variable is C = 2k / (pi * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 * Delta t) = 2k / (pi * M * Delta tau).
* The saddle point approximation used in deriving the Kolmogorov equation requires Delta tau * M/T = sqrt(lambda) * T * Delta t >> 1.
* In strongly coupled N=4 SYM, the Einstein relation is violated by a factor scaling like gamma^(3/2) at large gamma (where gamma is the Lorentz boost factor).
* The heavy quark velocity is v = p / sqrt(p^2 + M^2), and u = |u| = gamma * v.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of the Kolmogorov transport equation for heavy quarks (Rajagopal 2025) by providing explicit solutions for phenomenologically relevant momentum distributions and analyzing the range of validity. It contrasts with standard Langevin/Fokker-Planck approaches (Akamatsu 2008, Beraudo 2009, He 2013) which require the Einstein relation for equilibration but fail for relativistic quarks, and with Boltzmann transport formulations (Gossiaux 2008, Cao 2016) which require model-dependent parametrizations of the collision kernel. The results enable future phenomenological implementations including finite-size expanding plasma and hadronization effects.
