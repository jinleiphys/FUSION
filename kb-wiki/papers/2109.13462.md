# Beta Decay in Medium-Mass Nuclei with the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group

> We review the status of ab initio calculations of allowed beta decays (both Fermi and Gamow-Teller), within the framework of the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when the Gamow-Teller transition operator is consistently evolved using the in-medium similarity renormalization group (including two-body currents derived from chiral effective field theory), the long-standing empirical "quenching" of Gamow-Teller strength essentially vanishes for medium-mass nuclei, though this conclusion is qualified by significant state-dependent scatter in lighter p-shell nuclei.

## Method

The author uses the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) to evolve both the nuclear Hamiltonian and the beta decay transition operators consistently, starting from chiral NN+3N interactions. The Gamow-Teller operator includes one-body and two-body currents up to N³LO in chiral EFT, with all operators normal-ordered and truncated at the two-body level (IMSRG(2) approximation). For superallowed Fermi decays, the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction δ_C is computed from the ab initio Fermi matrix element using a Hartree-Fock basis that includes Coulomb and isospin-breaking strong forces.

## Key numbers

- Gamow-Teller quenching factor for sd and pf shell nuclei: q = 1.00 (standard deviation 0.15) when using the full consistently evolved operator including two-body currents; when including p-shell nuclei, q = 0.99 (standard deviation 0.21)
- Bare στ operator (without evolution or two-body currents) yields a best-fit quenching factor of approximately 0.77 for sd and pf shell nuclei
- For the ¹⁴O → ¹⁴N superallowed Fermi decay, the VS-IMSRG calculation shows strong dependence on the e_max truncation and on whether the initial or final nucleus is used as the normal-ordering reference, with δ_C values varying by several tenths of a percent
- For ⁴⁶Cr → ⁴⁶V, the VS-IMSRG shows approximate reference independence and small two-body corrections, but only a hint of convergence with respect to e_max

## Context

This work extends the ab initio treatment of beta decay beyond previous studies such as Ekström (2014), which used coupled cluster for a few light nuclei, and Gysbers (2019), which applied no-core shell model, coupled cluster, and VS-IMSRG across multiple shells. The paper provides additional VS-IMSRG calculations not presented in Gysbers (2019) and discusses the state-dependence of the quenching factor. For superallowed Fermi decays, the VS-IMSRG results for δ_C are compared to the phenomenological values adopted by Towner and Hardy (2015), revealing that converged ab initio calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections remain challenging, particularly for lighter nuclei like ¹⁴O.
