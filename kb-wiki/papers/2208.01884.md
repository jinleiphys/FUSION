# Longitudinal spin polarization of $Λ-$hyperons in a thermal model

> We briefly review the thermal model predictions related to longitudinal spin polarization of $Λ-$hyperons emitted from a hot and rotating hadronic medium produced in the non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The thermal vorticity and thermal shear contributions to the longitudinal spin polarization of Lambda hyperons are of opposite sign and nearly cancel each other in a thermal model with single freeze-out, leading to disagreement with experimental data unless the Lambda hyperon mass is replaced by the constituent strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors used an extended thermal model with single freeze-out that includes elliptic deformations of the emission region and transverse flow, parameterized by anisotropy parameters epsilon and delta. They computed the mean Pauli-Lubanski vector for Lambda hyperons emitted from the freeze-out hypersurface, using the spin polarization tensor defined as a combination of thermal vorticity and thermal shear tensors, with temperature gradient contributions derived from perfect-fluid hydrodynamic equations using the speed of sound c_s = 1/sqrt(3).

## Key numbers

- For centrality class 30-60% at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, the n=2 azimuthal harmonic of longitudinal spin polarization from thermal vorticity is -0.000292 and from thermal shear is +0.000275, giving a net value of -0.0000172 when temperature gradients are included
- When temperature gradients are excluded, the net n=2 harmonic becomes +9.8 x 10^{-6}
- The Lambda hyperon mass used is m_Lambda = 1.116 GeV; the strange quark mass used for comparison is m_s = 0.486 GeV
- The freeze-out temperature used is T_f = 165 MeV
- The p_T integration range for azimuthal angle dependence is 0 to 3 GeV
- The experimental data compared are from STAR at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for centrality 20-60%

## Context

This work extends the thermal model with single freeze-out (Broniowski 2001, Florkowski 2019) to include thermal shear contributions to spin polarization, following suggestions by Becattini (2021) and Liu (2021) that symmetric hydrodynamic gradients contribute to local spin polarization. The results contradict hydrodynamic model predictions based solely on thermal vorticity (Becattini 2017) that fail to reproduce STAR measurements of longitudinal spin polarization (STAR 2019). The finding that replacing the Lambda mass with the strange quark mass yields agreement with data follows an approach used by Fu (2021). The work enables further investigation of whether the thermal shear mechanism or the effective strange quark mass prescription is the correct physical explanation for the observed polarization patterns.
