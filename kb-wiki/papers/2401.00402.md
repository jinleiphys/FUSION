# 3D Multi-system Bayesian Calibration with Energy Conservation to Study Rapidity-dependent Dynamics of Nuclear Collisions

> Considerable information about the early-stage dynamics of heavy-ion collisions is encoded in the rapidity dependence of measurements. To leverage the large amount of experimental data, we perform a systematic analysis using three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of multiple collision systems -- large and small, symmetric and asymmetric. Specifically, we perform fully 3D multi-stage hydrodynamic simulations initialized by a parameterized model for rapidity-dependent energy deposition, which we calibrate on the hadron multiplicity and anisotropic flow coefficients. We utilize Bayesian inference to constrain properties of the early- and late- time dynamics of the system, and highlight the impact of enforcing global energy conservation in our 3D model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that enforcing global energy conservation in a 3D hydrodynamic model of heavy-ion collisions, rather than allowing a free normalization parameter for energy density, forces the Bayesian calibration to prefer a large specific bulk viscosity at low temperatures when fitting rapidity-dependent data; this large bulk viscosity is not required when only mid-rapidity data are used.

## Method

The authors performed fully 3D multi-stage hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code initialized by a 3D Glauber model with parameterized rapidity-dependent energy deposition, followed by the UrQMD hadronic afterburner. They calibrated 20 model parameters against charged hadron multiplicity and elliptic flow measurements as functions of pseudorapidity for Au-Au and d-Au collisions at 200 GeV from the PHOBOS, BRAHMS, STAR, and PHENIX experiments, using Gaussian Process emulators and Bayesian inference to obtain posterior probability distributions.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity data: 120 data points for Au-Au and 162 data points for d-Au
- Elliptic flow data: 21 data points for Au-Au and 24 data points for d-Au
- Beam energy: 200 GeV for both Au-Au and d-Au collisions
- Rapidity loss parameters most strongly constrained at incoming rapidities of 4 and 6 units, corresponding to the beam rapidity at 200 GeV
- The preferred bulk viscosity extends beyond the 90% prior range for ζ/s
- Mid-rapidity analysis (|η| < 1) yields bulk viscosity constraints consistent with previous JETSCAPE calibration (PhysRevLett.126.242301)

## Context

This work extends previous 2D boost-invariant Bayesian calibrations (Bernhard 2019, PhysRevLett.126.242301, PhysRevLett.126.202301) to full 3D dynamics, building on 3D initial state models developed by Shen (2017) and others (PhysRevC.105.064905, PhysRevC.96.044912). The key advance is enforcing global energy conservation in the 3D model, which removes the free normalization parameter used in prior analyses and reveals a strong preference for large bulk viscosity when rapidity-dependent data are included. This enables future Bayesian system scans of all high-energy data at RHIC and LHC, and provides a crucial baseline for coupling hard physics (jet simulations) to hydrodynamic evolution.
