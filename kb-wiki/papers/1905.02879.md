# Restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry in $N=32$ and $34$ isotones described by relativistic Hartree-Fock theory

> Restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) along the $N=32$ and $34$ isotonic chains and the physics behind are studied by applying the relativistic Hartree-Fock theory with effective Lagrangian PKA1. Taking the proton pseudo-spin partners $(\pi2s_{1/2},\pi1d_{3/2})$ as candidates, systematic restoration of PSS along both isotonic chains is found from sulphur (S) to nickel (Ni), while distinct violation from silicon (Si) to sulphur is discovered near the drip lines. The effects of the tensor-force components introduced naturally by the Fock terms are investigated, which can only partly interpret the systematics from calcium to nickel, but fail for the overall trends. Further analysis following the Schrödinger-like equation of the lower component of Dirac spinor shows that the contributions from the Hartree terms dominate the overall systematics of the PSS restoration, and such effects can be self-consistently interpreted by the evolution of the proton central density profiles along both isotonic chains. Specifically the distinct PSS violation is found to tightly relate with the dramatic changes from the bubble-like density profiles in silicon to the central-bumped ones in sulphur.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the systematic restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) along the N=32 and N=34 isotonic chains from sulphur to nickel is dominated by the Hartree terms rather than the tensor-force components introduced by the Fock terms, with the distinct PSS violation from silicon to sulphur being tightly related to the dramatic change from bubble-like proton density profiles in silicon to central-bumped ones in sulphur.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations using the effective Lagrangian PKA1, which includes one-pion exchange and rho-tensor couplings via Fock terms, for even-even N=32 and N=34 isotones from silicon (Z=14) to nickel (Z=28) under spherical symmetry. Pairing correlations were handled with the BCS approximation using the finite-range Gogny D1S pairing force. The pseudo-spin orbital splittings of the proton pseudo-spin partners (π2s₁/₂, π1d₃/₂) were analyzed by reducing the radial Dirac equation to a Schrödinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor, allowing quantitative evaluation of contributions from kinetic, Hartree, and Fock terms.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-spin orbital splitting ΔEπ1p' = Eπ1d₃/₂ - Eπ2s₁/₂ shows distinct violation from silicon to sulphur, with the sub-shell Z=16 opening in ⁴⁸S (N=32) and a less soundable one in ⁵⁰S (N=34)
- From sulphur to nickel, the PSO splitting monotonously decreases with proton number, eventually presenting appropriate restoration of PSS at ⁶⁰,⁶²Ni
- The tensor-force contributions to ΔEπ1p' are weakened when approaching the spin-saturated proton Z=20 (⁵²,⁵⁴Ca) and enhanced afterwards as protons become spin-unsaturated
- The Hartree term dominates the overall evolution of ΔEπ1p', including the distinct violation at sulphur, with partial cancellation by the kinetic and Fock terms

## Context

This work extends studies of pseudo-spin symmetry in spherical nuclei, originally identified by Arima (1969) and Hecht (1969), and later recognized as a relativistic symmetry by Ginocchio (1997) with conservation condition V(r)+S(r)=0 or d(V+S)/dr=0 by Meng (1998). The study builds on recent experimental evidence for new magicities at N=32 and N=34 in calcium isotopes (Dinca 2005, Gade 2006, Wienholtz 2013, Steppenbeck 2013) and the prediction that the opening of N=34 magicity may relate to bubble-like structure in ⁴⁸Si (Li 2019). The RHF-PKA1 approach used here is the only covariant density functional that self-consistently reproduces both new magicities N=32 and N=34 in calcium. The results enable future studies of how bubble-like density profiles and central density evolution govern PSS restoration across isotonic chains.
