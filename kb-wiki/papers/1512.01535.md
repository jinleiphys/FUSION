# On Quasibound N* Nuclei

> The possibility for the existence of unstable bound states of the S11 nucleon resonance N$^*$(1535) and nuclei is investigated. These quasibound states are speculated to be closely related to the existence of the quasibound states of the eta mesons and nuclei. Within a simple model for the N N$^*$ interaction involving a pion and eta meson exchange, N$^*$-nucleus potentials for N*-$^3$He and N*-$^{24}$Mg are evaluated and found to be of a Woods-Saxon like form which supports two to three bound states. In case of N*-$^3$He, one state bound by only a few keV and another by 4 MeV is found. The results are however quite sensitive to the N N$^*$ $π$ and N N$^*$ $η$ vertex parameters. A rough estimate of the width of these states, based on the mean free path of the exchanged mesons in the nuclei leads to very broad states with $Γ\sim$ 80 and 110 MeV for N*-$^3$He and N*-$^{24}$Mg respectively.

## Key claim

Within a simple one-meson-exchange model, quasibound states of the N*(1535) resonance with ³He and ²⁴Mg nuclei may exist, but the results are highly sensitive to the unknown πNN* and ηNN* vertex parameters, and the states are predicted to be very broad (Γ ~ 80–110 MeV), making them difficult to observe as narrow resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed an elementary NN* → NN* potential using one-pion and one-eta exchange diagrams in a non-relativistic approximation, neglecting spin-dependent terms. They then folded this elementary potential with nuclear densities (a sum of Gaussians for ³He and a Woods-Saxon form for ²⁴Mg) to obtain N*-nucleus potentials. Bound-state energies were found by solving the Schrödinger equation for a Woods-Saxon fit to these potentials using a hypergeometric-function condition, and widths were estimated from the mean free path of exchanged mesons in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- N*-³He: one bound state at -0.03 MeV (few keV) and another at -3.9 MeV, depending on coupling parameter set; Woods-Saxon parameters V₀ = 18–37 MeV, a = 0.8–0.84 fm, R = 1.3–1.4 fm
- N*-²⁴Mg: two bound states at -17.1 and -1.8 MeV for one parameter set, and three at -47.6, -20.8, and -2.6 MeV for another; Woods-Saxon parameters V₀ = 34–76 MeV, a = 0.9–0.98 fm, R = 2.9 fm
- Estimated widths: Γ ≈ 80 MeV for N*-³He and Γ ≈ 110 MeV for N*-²⁴Mg, at a cross-section parameter σ₀ = 3 fm²
- The ω₀ (N* minus N mass difference) used in the width calculation is approximately 597 MeV

## Context

This work extends the historical idea of bound Δ-nucleus states, which was investigated experimentally at MAMI (Bartsch et al.) and theoretically by Walcher, though criticized by Ramos et al. due to large non-mesonic decay widths (~100 MeV). The present calculation suggests that N*-nucleus quasibound states, if they exist, would be closely related to η-mesic nuclei (such as η-⁴He and η-²⁵Mg) and would be very broad, implying that η-mesic nuclear states might also be broad. The results call for more sophisticated calculations with better-constrained vertex parameters before firm conclusions can be drawn.
