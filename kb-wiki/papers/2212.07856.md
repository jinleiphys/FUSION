# Can the two-pole structure of the $D_0^*(2300)$ be understood from recent lattice data?

> It was demonstrated in a series of papers employing unitarized chiral perturbation theory that the phenomenology of the scalar open-charm state, the $D_0^*(2300)$, can be understood as the interplay of two poles, corresponding to two scalar-isospin doublet states with different SU(3) flavor content. Within this formalism the lightest open charm positive parity states emerge as being dynamically generated from the scattering of the Goldstone-boson octet off $D$ mesons, a picture that at the same time solves various problems that the experimental observations posed. However, in recent lattice studies of $Dπ$ scattering at different pion masses only one pole was reported in the $D_0^*$ channel, while it was not possible to extract reliable parameters of a second pole from the lattice data. In this paper we demonstrate how this seeming contradiction can be understood and that imposing SU(3) constraints on the fitting amplitudes allows one to extract information on the second pole from the lattice data with minimal bias. The results may also be regarded as a showcase how approximate symmetries can be imposed in the $K$-matrix formalism to reduce the number of parameters.

## Key claim

The seeming contradiction between the two-pole structure of the \(D_0^*(2300)\) predicted by unitarized chiral perturbation theory and the single-pole results from lattice QCD studies can be resolved: the lattice data are consistent with a second pole, but its location is poorly constrained because poles on hidden Riemann sheets produce effects on the physical axis that are nearly indistinguishable for different combinations of pole distance and residue strength.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the nine \(K\)-matrix parametrizations previously used by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (HadSpec) to fit lattice QCD energy levels for coupled-channel \(D\pi\)-\(D\eta\)-\(D_s\bar{K}\) scattering at a pion mass of about 391 MeV. They searched for poles on all Riemann sheets and studied the correlation between each pole's distance from the \(D_s\bar{K}\) threshold and its residues to the three channels. They then proposed a new \(K\)-matrix parametrization that imposes SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints, reducing the number of free parameters, and fitted it to the same lattice energy levels.

## Key numbers

* The bound-state pole on the physical sheet (RS111) was found at approximately 2276 MeV across all nine HadSpec parametrizations, consistent with the UChPT result of \(2263^{+8}_{-14}\) MeV.
* The higher pole on sheet RS221 showed a large spread across parametrizations, with real parts ranging from about 2866 MeV (Amplitude 9) to 3710 MeV (Amplitude 3) and widths from about 308 MeV to 1412 MeV.
* A linear fit of the RS221 pole distance from the \(D_s\bar{K}\) threshold versus the square root of the residue to the \(D\pi\) channel gave a y-intercept of \(5.8 \pm 0.9\) GeV, indicating a well-constrained effect at threshold despite the scattered pole locations.
* The UChPT pole on RS221 was located at \(2467^{+32}_{-25} - i\,113^{+18}_{-16}\) MeV, with a distance from the \(D_s\bar{K}\) threshold of \(-34^{+32}_{-25}\) MeV (i.e., slightly below threshold).

## Context

This work extends the series of unitarized chiral perturbation theory studies by Kolomeitsev (2003), Guo (2006, 2009), Albaladejo (2016), and Du (2017) that predicted two scalar-isospin doublet states in the \(D_0^*\) channel. It directly addresses the lattice QCD analysis by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Moir 2016), which reported only one pole. The authors demonstrate that the lattice data are compatible with a second pole and that imposing SU(3) flavor symmetry on the \(K\)-matrix, as previously applied in studies of the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) by Baru (2004), can better constrain future analyses. This enables more reliable extraction of the second pole from lattice data and provides a general method for incorporating approximate symmetries into \(K\)-matrix fits.
