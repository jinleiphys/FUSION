# Relation between (e,e') sum rules in 6,7Li and 4He nuclei.Experiment and cluster model

> The sums over (e,e') spectra of 6Li and 7Li nuclei which correspond to the longitudinal sum rule are studied. It is suggested that due to the cluster structure of the lithium isotopes these sums may approximately be expressed in terms of such a sum pertaining to the alpha-particle. Calculation of these sums is performed in the framework of cluster models with antisymmetrization done with respect to all the nucleons. At momentum transfers higher than 0.8 fm^{-1} the relations expressing the A=6 or 7 sum in terms of the A=4 sum prove to be valid with rather high accuracy. In the region of momentum transfers around 1 fm^{-1} the longitudinal correlation functions of 6Li and 7Li nuclei are found to be close to that of the alpha-particle. The experimental longitudinal sums in the range between 0.450 and 1.625 fm^{-1} are employed to perform comparison with those calculated in the framework of cluster models. Out of the mentioned experimental sums, those in the range between 0.750 and 1.000 fm^{-1} in the 6Li case and between 0.750 and 1.125 fm^{-1} in the 7Li case are obtained in the present work. In the 6Li case a complete agreement is found while in the 7Li case an agreement only at a qualitative level is observed.

# Relation between (e,e') sum rules in 6,7Li and 4He nuclei. Experiment and cluster model

## Key claim

The longitudinal (e,e') sum rules for 6Li and 7Li can be approximately expressed in terms of the 4He sum rule at momentum transfers above 0.8 fm^{-1}, due to the cluster structure of these lithium isotopes; however, the relation is accurate only at a qualitative level for 7Li, while for 6Li a complete agreement with experiment is found.

## Method

The authors calculate the longitudinal sum rules S_L(q) for 6Li and 7Li using cluster models with full antisymmetrization with respect to all nucleons. For 7Li, a two-cluster alpha+triton model is employed with the relative motion function from Dubovichenko (2010); for 6Li, a three-cluster alpha+p+n model is used with the relative motion function from Kukulin et al. (1984). The theoretical sums are compared with experimental longitudinal sums extracted from (e,e') measurements at the Kharkov Institute of Physics and Technology, using a power-law extrapolation (omega^{-2.5}) for the unmeasured high-energy tail.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfers q >= 0.8 fm^{-1}, the relations S_L^{6Li}(q) ≈ S_L^{4He}(q) + 1 and S_L^{7Li}(q) ≈ S_L^{4He}(q) + S_L^{3H}(q) are accurate to within a few percent.
- The rms value of the 7Li cluster relative motion function rf(r) is 3.4 fm, derived from charge radii.
- Experimental longitudinal sums were obtained in the range 0.750 to 1.000 fm^{-1} for 6Li and 0.750 to 1.125 fm^{-1} for 7Li in the present work.
- The extrapolation of the experimental response functions increased the (S_L)_inel sums by 8 to 19 percent.
- At q = 0.9 fm^{-1}, the relative deviation of the 7Li correlation term calculated without full antisymmetrization from that with full antisymmetrization is 6%; at q = 1.1 fm^{-1} it is 18%; at q = 1.3 fm^{-1} it reaches 40%.

## Context

This work extends the study of electronuclear sum rules, which determine two-nucleon correlation functions, to clustered light nuclei. The authors build on earlier experimental work at Kharkov (Buki et al., 1977, 2004, 2005) that found the 6Li inelastic sum approaches its high-q limit faster than for other nuclei. The cluster-model relations are derived using model-independent sum-rule expressions for 4He and 3H from Efros (1999, 2000). The results enable a simplified understanding of longitudinal sum rules in clustered nuclei and suggest that such relations could be used to test whether a nucleus is clustered, as noted by Diaz-Torres.
