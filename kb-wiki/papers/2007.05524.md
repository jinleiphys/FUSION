# Hydrodynamic gradient expansion in linear response theory

> A foundational question in relativistic fluid mechanics concerns the properties of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion at large orders. We establish the precise conditions under which this gradient expansion diverges for a broad class of microscopic theories admitting a relativistic hydrodynamic limit, in the linear regime. Our result does not rely on highly symmetric fluid flows utilized by previous studies of heavy-ion collisions and cosmology. The hydrodynamic gradient expansion diverges whenever energy density or velocity fields have support in momentum space exceeding a critical momentum, and converges otherwise. This critical momentum is an intrinsic property of the microscopic theory and is set by branch point singularities of hydrodynamic dispersion relations.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic gradient expansion in linear response theory converges if and only if the momentum-space support of the energy density and velocity fields is smaller than a critical momentum scale \(k_*\); if the support exceeds \(k_*\) or extends to infinity, the series diverges geometrically or factorially, respectively. This critical momentum is an intrinsic property of the microscopic theory, set by branch point singularities of the hydrodynamic dispersion relations, and the result does not rely on the highly symmetric fluid flows used in prior studies of heavy-ion collisions and cosmology.

## Method

The authors consider linearized perturbations around thermal equilibrium in conformal, parity-invariant relativistic fluids in flat spacetime, using the Landau frame. They parametrize the gradient expansion of the viscous stress tensor \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}\) in terms of infinite series in spatial Laplacians acting on three elementary tensorial structures built from the velocity and energy density perturbations. By matching the resulting dispersion relations for shear and sound modes to the exact microscopic dispersion relations order by order in spatial momentum \(k\), they derive explicit formulas for the transport coefficients \(a_n, b_n, c_n\) in terms of the series coefficients of \(\omega_\perp(k)\) and \(\omega_\parallel^\pm(k)\). The large-order growth of these transport coefficients is then controlled by the location of the nearest branch-point singularity in the complex \(k\)-plane, and the convergence of the position-space gradient series is analyzed using the Paley-Wiener theorem for compactly supported Fourier transforms.

## Key numbers

* The critical momentum scale for the shear channel in Müller-Israel-Stewart theory is \(|k_*^{(A)}| = 1/\sqrt{4 D \tau_\pi}\), where \(D = \eta/(s T)\) is the diffusion constant and \(\tau_\pi\) is the relaxation time; for the example with \(s = T = \eta = \tau_\pi = 1\), this gives \(k_* = 0.5\).
* The transport coefficients \(a_n\) in Müller-Israel-Stewart theory are \(a_n = s T \, \mathcal{C}_n D^{n+1} \tau_\pi^n\), where \(\mathcal{C}_n\) are Catalan numbers, leading to \(\limsup_{n\to\infty} |a_n|^{1/n} = 4 D \tau_\pi\).
* The gradient expansion converges when \(k_{\text{max}}^2 < 1/(4 D \tau_\pi)\), diverges geometrically when \(1/(4 D \tau_\pi) \leq k_{\text{max}}^2 < \infty\), and diverges factorially when \(k_{\text{max}} \to \infty\) (as demonstrated numerically for the shear channel with \(\gamma=0.1\), \(t=1\), \(x=0.5\)).
* The matching conditions relate transport coefficients to dispersion relation coefficients: \(a_n = [k^{2n+2}](i s T \omega_\perp)\), and analogous formulas for \(b_n\) and \(c_n\) involve \(\omega_\parallel^+\) and \(\omega_\parallel^-\).

## Context

This work unifies two previously separate lines of research: studies of the divergence of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion in expanding plasmas (Heller 2013, Buchel 2016, Aniceto 2018, Denicol 2019) and studies of the finite radius of convergence of hydrodynamic dispersion relations in holography (Withers 2018, Grozdanov 2019). It extends these results by showing that the convergence of the position-space constitutive relations is governed by the same branch-point singularities that set the radius of convergence of the dispersion relations, and that the condition depends on the momentum-space support of the initial data rather than on highly symmetric flows. The work enables future studies of optimal truncation of the gradient series as a function of initial conditions and opens the door to extracting nonhydrodynamic data from the asymptotic series via resurgence, as explored in subsequent work by the same authors (Heller 2020).
