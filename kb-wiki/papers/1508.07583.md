# Two Nucleon Systems at $m_π\sim 450~{\rm MeV}$ from Lattice QCD

> Nucleon-nucleon systems are studied with lattice quantum chromodynamics at a pion mass of $m_π\sim 450~{\rm MeV}$ in three spatial volumes using $n_f=2+1$ flavors of light quarks. At the quark masses employed in this work, the deuteron binding energy is calculated to be $B_d = 14.4^{+3.2}_{-2.6} ~{\rm MeV}$, while the dineutron is bound by $B_{nn} = 12.5^{+3.0}_{-5.0}~{\rm MeV}$. Over the range of energies that are studied, the S-wave scattering phase shifts calculated in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels are found to be similar to those in nature, and indicate repulsive short-range components of the interactions, consistent with phenomenological nucleon-nucleon interactions. In both channels, the phase shifts are determined at three energies that lie within the radius of convergence of the effective range expansion, allowing for constraints to be placed on the inverse scattering lengths and effective ranges. The extracted phase shifts allow for matching to nuclear effective field theories, from which low energy counterterms are extracted and issues of convergence are investigated. As part of the analysis, a detailed investigation of the single hadron sector is performed, enabling a precise determination of the violation of the Gell-Mann--Okubo mass relation. [An Erratum to the published version is included as an appendix. It details the impact of an error discovered in 2020 and corrects typographical errors.]

## Key claim

At a pion mass of approximately 450 MeV, lattice QCD calculations show that both the deuteron and the dineutron are bound, with binding energies of \(B_d = 14.4^{+3.2}_{-2.6}\) MeV and \(B_{nn} = 12.5^{+3.0}_{-5.0}\) MeV respectively, though these results are obtained at a single lattice spacing and have not been extrapolated to the continuum limit, with the quoted uncertainties intended to encompass the expected effects of such extrapolations.

## Method

The NPLQCD Collaboration performed lattice QCD calculations on three ensembles of \(n_f=2+1\) flavor gauge-field configurations with spatial extents of \(L=2.8\) fm, \(3.7\) fm, and \(5.6\) fm, using a clover-improved quark action at a lattice spacing of \(b=0.1167(16)\) fm. Single-nucleon and two-nucleon correlation functions were computed from quark propagators generated using the multigrid algorithm or GPUs, with energies extracted from plateaus in effective mass plots using both the mean under Jackknife and the Hodges-Lehmann robust estimator under Bootstrap resampling. The Luescher quantization condition was then applied to extract S-wave scattering phase shifts in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels from the finite-volume energy levels.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d = 14.4^{+3.2}_{-2.6}\) MeV at \(m_\pi \sim 450\) MeV
- Dineutron binding energy: \(B_{nn} = 12.5^{+3.0}_{-5.0}\) MeV at \(m_\pi \sim 450\) MeV
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 449.9(0.3)(0.3)(4.6)\) MeV (statistical, systematic, scale-setting uncertainties)
- Nucleon mass: \(M_N = 1226(01)(01)(12)\) MeV
- Lattice spacing: \(b = 0.1167(16)\) fm
- Spatial volumes: \(L = 2.801(29)\) fm (ensemble A), \(3.734(38)\) fm (ensemble B), \(5.602(58)\) fm (ensemble C)
- Kaon mass: \(m_K = 595.9(0.2)(0.2)(6.1)\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's previous calculations at the SU(3) symmetric point (Beane et al., 2012, 2013) to a lighter pion mass of approximately 450 MeV, and provides the first lattice QCD determination of both deuteron and dineutron binding at this quark mass. The extracted phase shifts enable matching to nuclear effective field theories (EFTs), following the approach of Beane et al. (2012) for hyperon-nucleon interactions and Barnea et al. (2013) and Baru et al. (2015) for nucleon-nucleon interactions, allowing for extraction of low-energy counterterms and investigation of EFT convergence.
