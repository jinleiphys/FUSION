# Subtraction of the spurious translational mode from the RPA response function

> It is well known that within self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) on top of Hartree-Fock (HF), the translational symmetry should be restored. Due to approximations at the level of the practical implementation, this restoration may be only partial. As a result, one has spurious contributions in the physical quantities that are extracted from RPA. While there are several recipes in the literature to overcome this drawback in order to produce transition densites or strength functions that are free from spurious contamination, there is no formalism associated with the full RPA response function. We present such formalism in this paper. Our goal is to avoid spurious contamination when the response function is used in many-body frameworks like the particle-vibration coupling theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism to remove spurious center-of-mass contamination from the full RPA response function in coordinate-space continuum RPA calculations, rather than only from transition densities or strength functions as done previously; the method is validated for cases where the spurious translational mode does not lie exactly at zero energy due to practical approximations.

## Method

The authors start from the assumption that the RPA transition density operator can be decomposed into a physical part plus spurious contributions proportional to the momentum operator \(\hat P\) and the position operator \(\hat Q\), with orthogonality conditions enforced. They then extend this decomposition to the RPA response function \(R^{11}_{qq'}(\mathbf{r}\mathbf{r}';\omega)\) in coordinate space, deriving an expression for the physical response function \(\tilde{R}^{11}\) by subtracting terms involving the gradient of the ground-state density and integrals of the original response function. The corrected response function is then used as input to particle-vibration coupling (PVC) calculations via the Dyson equation to obtain the perturbed Green's function and level densities.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{208}\)Pb with the SLy5 Skyrme interaction, the energy-weighted sum rule \(\tilde{m}_1\) for the E1 operator (Eq. e1op1) after spurious subtraction is 872.3 (units not specified but consistent with \(e^2\) fm\(^2\) MeV), compared to the uncorrected \(m_1 = 1350\) and a spurious contribution \(\delta m_1 = 477.7\).
* For the isoscalar compression dipole in \(^{208}\)Pb with SLy5, the corrected \(\tilde{m}_1\) is \(5.588 \times 10^6\) (units consistent with fm\(^6\) MeV), while the uncorrected \(m_1 = 1.368 \times 10^7\) and \(\delta m_1 = 8.092 \times 10^6\).
* The continuum RPA calculations for \(^{208}\)Pb used a radial mesh extending to 20 fm with a radial step of 0.2 fm, and included states up to angular momentum \(\ell = 12\).
* For the PVC calculation in \(^{24}\)O, RPA electric dipole phonons were included up to 60 MeV, and the level density smoothing parameter was 0.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior methods for removing spurious center-of-mass contamination from RPA transition densities and strength functions (Giai and Sagawa, 1981; Suzuki, 1984; Sagawa and Colo, 1990; Hamamoto and Sagawa, 1990; Shlomo and Bertsch, 1975; Terasaki and Engel, 2006) to the full RPA response function, which had not been addressed previously. The formalism is motivated by the use of the RPA response function as input to particle-vibration coupling calculations in coordinate space (Mizuyama and Colo, forthcoming), and enables the systematic removal of spurious contributions from self-energy functions and level densities in such many-body frameworks.
