# Microscopic calculation of the spin-dependent neutron scattering lengths on 3He

> We report on the spin.dependent neutron scattering length on 3He from a microscopic calculation of p-3H, n-3He, and d-2H scattering employing the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential with and without additional three-nucleon force. The results and that of a comprehensive R-matrix analysis are compared to a recent measurement. The overall agreement for the scattering lengths is quite good. The imaginary parts of the scattering lengths are very sensitive to the inclusion of three-nucleon forces, whereas the real parts are almost insensitive.

# Microscopic calculation of the spin-dependent neutron scattering lengths on 3He

## Key claim
The real parts of the spin-dependent neutron scattering lengths on 3He agree within 5 percent with experimental data and are almost insensitive to the inclusion of three-nucleon forces, whereas the imaginary parts are very sensitive to such forces and can therefore serve as a means to learn about three-nucleon interactions.

## Method
The authors performed Resonating Group Model (RGM) calculations of p-3H, n-3He, and d-2H scattering in the 4He system, using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential with and without additional three-nucleon forces (Urbana IX and V3*). They employed a model space with six two-fragment channels and included all S, P, and D wave contributions to the Jπ = 0+, 1+, 2+, 0-, 1-, and 2- channels, using the Kohn-Hulthén variational principle with a Gaussian basis. The results were compared to a comprehensive R-matrix analysis of the 4He system and to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Real part of singlet scattering length a0: AV18 gives 7.81(2) fm, AV18+UIX gives 7.62(1) fm, AV18+UIX+V3* gives 7.57(5) fm, R-matrix gives 7.400(3) fm, experiment gives 7.370(58) fm (Zimmer)
- Imaginary part of singlet scattering length a0: AV18 gives -4.96(2) fm, AV18+UIX gives -4.07(3) fm, AV18+UIX+V3* gives -3.42(1) fm, R-matrix gives -4.449(1) fm, experiment gives -4.448(5) fm (Kohl)
- Real part of triplet scattering length a1: AV18 gives 3.468(1) fm, AV18+UIX gives 3.333(1) fm, AV18+UIX+V3* gives 3.310(1) fm, R-matrix gives 3.281(2) fm, experiment gives 3.278(53) fm (Zimmer)
- Imaginary part of triplet scattering length a1: AV18 gives -0.0067(1) fm, AV18+UIX gives -0.0052(1) fm, AV18+UIX+V3* gives -0.0049(1) fm, R-matrix gives -0.0013(2) fm, experiment gives -0.001(2) fm (Kohl)
- Triton binding energy for AV18+UIX at dimension 70: -8.460 MeV, compared to the numerically exact value of -8.478 MeV (Nogga)
- Experimental thresholds: 3He-p at 0.763 MeV, d-d at 4.033 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of neutron scattering on tritons using correlated hyperspherical harmonics (ANT) and a previous RGM calculation with a modest model space (BP), both of which showed weak dependence on the choice of potentials. The new results are compared to recent high-precision measurements of the real parts of the spin-dependent neutron scattering lengths on 3He (Zimmer) and to older experimental determinations of the imaginary parts (Kohl). The calculations show that the short-range repulsion in the Urbana IX three-nucleon force may be too strong and that the radial dependence of attraction and repulsion should be changed, enabling future refinements of three-nucleon force models.
