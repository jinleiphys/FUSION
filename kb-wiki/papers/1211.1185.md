# Magnetic Moment of Constituent Fermions in Strongly Interacting Matter

> We investigate the magnetic moment operator for constituent fermion masses for chirally symmetric theories. Constituent fermion masses are generated through a yukawa interaction of the fermion with a scalar (or /and psuedoscalar) field via the vacuum expectation value (VEV) of the scalar (or and psuedoscalar) field. We especially consider the high baryon density $π_0$ condensed phase, in which chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken, with space varying expectation values of the $ σ$ and $π_0$ fields. This phase has a spin polarized fermi sea as the ground state. We show that there is indeed generated a macroscopic magnetization in this phase, contrary to what one would have found, if one just used a primitive phenomenological magnetic moment formula for explicit/ current fermion masses. Furthermore, this analysis reveals that the magnetization of this state goes up as the VEV, that determines the 'mass', comes down with increasing baryon density. The consequent high magnetic field that is generated will destabilize this state at a threshold density. This is important in the context of neutron stars, as such a high density state may be responsible for very high magnetic fields in the dense core of neutron stars. This could potentially be the origin of magnetars - the stars with the largest magnetic fields in the universe.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the high baryon density pi-zero condensed phase of strongly interacting matter, a macroscopic magnetization is generated from the magnetic moment operator for constituent fermion masses, contrary to what a naive phenomenological formula using explicit/current fermion masses would predict. This magnetization increases as the chiral mass parameter decreases with rising baryon density, and the resulting magnetic field will destabilize the condensed state at a threshold density, potentially explaining the origin of magnetars.

## Method

The authors compute the magnetic moment operator from first principles using the chiral Gellmann-Levy sigma model Lagrangian, performing a Gordon decomposition of the electromagnetic current in the presence of space-varying vacuum expectation values of the sigma and pi-zero fields. They compare this full chiral calculation to the naive magnetic moment formula that uses the explicit mass in the denominator, showing that the phase factors from the condensate wavefunction and the operator cancel in the full treatment. The upper bound on the magnetic field is obtained by equating the condensation energy density to the magnetic energy density.

## Key numbers

- The sigma particle mass of 1200 MeV corresponds to lambda-squared approximately 75; a more realistic sigma mass of 600 MeV gives lambda-squared approximately 20.
- At a baryon density of approximately 1/F^3 (where F is the pion decay constant), the chiral mass parameter is about half its value at zero density (m = g f_pi).
- The upper bound for the magnetic field the condensate can sustain is approximately 10^17 gauss, close to the approximately 10^16 gauss core field found in the authors' magnetar model.

## Context

This work extends the study of pi-zero condensation in nuclear matter, originally considered by Dautry and Baym (1977) and later by Kutschera, Broniowski, and Kotlorz (1990) for two flavors, to include the correct treatment of the magnetic moment operator. The authors resolve a discrepancy pointed out by R. F. Sawyer, where the naive magnetic moment formula gave a vanishing space-averaged magnetic moment for the spin-polarized neutron ground state, while the full chiral calculation restores proportionality between total spin and total magnetic moment. This enables a physical mechanism for generating the extremely high magnetic fields observed in magnetars, the stars with the largest magnetic fields in the universe.
