# Low energy kaon photoproduction from nuclei

> We study $K^+$-meson production in $γ{A}$ interaction at energies below the reaction threshold in free space. The Thomas-Fermi and spectral function approaches are used for the calculations of the production process. It is found that the measurement of the differential spectra may allow to reconstruct the production mechanism and to investigate the dispersion relations entering the production vertex. It is shown that the contribution from secondary pion induced reactions to the total kaon photoproduction is negligible for $E_γ{<}$1.2 GeV so that strangeness production at low energies is sensitive to the nuclear spectral function.

# Low energy kaon photoproduction from nuclei

## Key claim

The measurement of differential spectra for $K^+$ photoproduction from nuclei at photon energies below the free-space $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ threshold (approximately 911 MeV) may allow reconstruction of the production mechanism and investigation of nuclear dispersion relations, provided that light targets such as $^{12}C$ are used and the contribution from secondary pion-induced reactions is negligible for $E_\gamma < 1.2$ GeV.

## Method

The authors calculate $K^+$ production cross sections in $\gamma A$ reactions using two different nuclear models: the Thomas-Fermi approximation with a momentum-dependent nuclear potential and the spectral function approach for $^{12}C$. The elementary $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ amplitude is parameterized by fitting Born terms and low-energy resonances to experimental differential cross section data from ELSA at photon energies from 0.926 to 2.1 GeV. The $K^+$ final-state distortion is treated via a Glauber model with an effective nucleon number $A_{eff}$, and the contribution from two-step processes involving intermediate pions is evaluated separately.

## Key numbers

- The free-space $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ reaction threshold is at photon energy $\simeq 911$ MeV.
- For $^{12}C$, the Fermi momentum is $q_F = 221$ MeV/c; for nuclear matter, $q_F \simeq 263$ MeV/c.
- At $E_\gamma \simeq 800$ MeV, the total cross section calculated with the spectral function differs from the Thomas-Fermi result by a factor of about 6.
- The $K^+N$ cross sections range from 9 to 18 mb at kaon momenta below 1 GeV/c, giving a kaon mean-free-path of $\simeq 3.5$ to 7 fm at normal nuclear density.
- For carbon targets, the $K^+$ distortion factor $\kappa$ indicates roughly 20% probability for the kaon to interact in the nuclear interior.
- The ratio $\xi$ of the total $\gamma C \to K^+\Lambda X$ cross section calculated with off-shell versus on-shell elementary amplitudes is $1.0 \pm 0.1$ over the full photon energy range.
- The reduced $\chi^2/N$ for the fit to elementary $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda$ data is 3.88.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of subthreshold particle production in hadron-nucleus collisions (Newton, Kerman, Jackson) and proton-induced reactions (Sibirtsev 1997) to the photoproduction channel, where the photon illuminates the entire nucleus rather than just the periphery. The authors use nuclear dispersion relations from Sick (1987) and spectral functions from Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996) to model high-momentum components of the nuclear wave function. The results enable future experimental studies at Jefferson Laboratory (programs 91-016 and 91-014) to extract in-medium kaon self-energies and to distinguish between different nuclear dispersion relations through measurements of $K^+$ transverse momentum and momentum spectra at forward angles.
