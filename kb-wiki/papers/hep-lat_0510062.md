# The nucleon axial charge in full lattice QCD

> The nucleon axial charge is calculated as a function of the pion mass in full QCD. Using domain wall valence quarks and improved staggered sea quarks, we present the first calculation with pion masses as light as 354 MeV and volumes as large as (3.5 fm)^3. We show that finite volume effects are small for our volumes and that a constrained fit based on finite volume chiral perturbation theory agrees with experiment within 7% statistical errors.

# The nucleon axial charge in full lattice QCD

## Key claim
The nucleon axial charge g_A can be calculated from lattice QCD with pion masses as light as 354 MeV, and a constrained fit using finite-volume chiral perturbation theory yields a value at the physical pion mass that agrees with experiment within 7% statistical errors, provided the finite-volume effects are small for the volumes used.

## Method
The calculation uses a hybrid lattice QCD approach: domain wall valence quarks (which preserve chiral symmetry on the lattice) on dynamical improved staggered sea quark configurations generated by the MILC collaboration (Asqtad action). Nucleon three-point and two-point correlation functions are computed on two lattice volumes, (2.5 fm)^3 and (3.5 fm)^3, at five pion masses down to 354 MeV, and the axial current is renormalized nonperturbatively using the five-dimensional conserved current for domain wall fermions.

## Key numbers
- Lightest pion mass achieved: 354 MeV (on both (2.5 fm)^3 and (3.5 fm)^3 volumes)
- Largest spatial volume: (3.5 fm)^3 (28^3 × 32 lattice)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.12406 fm (determined from heavy quark spectroscopy, with 2% uncertainty)
- Renormalized axial charge at m_π = 354 MeV on (2.5 fm)^3: g_A = 1.244 ± 0.058 (655 configurations)
- Renormalized axial charge at m_π = 353 MeV on (3.5 fm)^3: g_A = 1.212 ± 0.059 (270 configurations)
- Constrained χPT fit result at physical pion mass (140 MeV): g_A = 1.212 ± 0.084
- Pion decay constant measured on these lattices: f_π = 92.4 MeV ± 3%

## Context
This work extends earlier quenched and unquenched lattice calculations of g_A to lighter pion masses and larger volumes than previously attained. It addresses concerns raised by Sasaki (2001) and Khan (2004) about finite-volume sensitivity of the axial charge, demonstrating that for volumes of 2.5 fm and 3.5 fm the volume effects are under control and consistent with chiral perturbation theory predictions from Beane (2004) and Detmold (2005). The results are consistent with earlier hybrid calculations by the LHPC collaboration using SESAM configurations (Dolgov, 2002) and with RBCK collaboration results (Ohta, 2004), while QCDSF/UKQCD results (Khan, 2004) appear systematically lower. This work enables future calculations at even lighter pion masses (300 and 250 MeV) for unrestricted χPT parameter determination, extension to partially quenched calculations, and evaluation of disconnected diagrams for nucleon spin structure.
