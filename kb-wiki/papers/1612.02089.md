# Internal Charmonium Evolution in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We employ a time-dependent Schrödinger equation to study the evolution of a $c\bar c$ dipole in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Medium effects on the heavy-quark potential in the QGP are found to significantly affect the timescales of the internal evolution of the dipole. Color-screening can enhance the overlap of the expanding wavepackage with excited states at high temperature, while it is reduced at lower temperatures where the dipole favors the formation of the charmonium ground state. We investigate the consequences of this mechanism on the double ratio of charmonium nuclear modification factors, $R_{AA}^{ψ(2S)}/R_{AA}^{J/ψ}$, in heavy-ion collisions. The impact of the transition mechanisms on this ratio turns out to be rather sensitive to the attractive strength of the potential, and to its temperature dependence.

## Key claim

The internal evolution of a $c\bar c$ dipole in a quark-gluon plasma, governed by a time-dependent Schrödinger equation with an in-medium heavy-quark potential, can cause transitions between charmonium eigenstates that significantly affect the double ratio $R_{AA}^{\psi(2S)}/R_{AA}^{J/\psi}$, but the sign and magnitude of this effect depend sensitively on the attractive strength of the potential and its temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors solved the radial time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a $c\bar c$ dipole using the Crank-Nicholson method, with the in-medium heavy-quark potential taken from lattice QCD results for either the free energy $F(r,T)$ or the internal energy $U(r,T)$. The initial $c\bar c$ wavefunction was a Gaussian whose width was adjusted to reproduce the observed $J/\psi$ to $\psi(2S)$ yield ratio in $pp$ collisions, and the dipole was evolved in a static QGP medium at fixed temperature. The overlap of the time-evolved wavefunction with vacuum charmonium eigenstates gave the time-dependent fractions of each state, from which nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}^{mS}$ and their double ratio were computed.

## Key numbers

- The initial Gaussian width of the $c\bar c$ dipole wavefunction was set to $\sigma_0 = 0.23$ fm or $\sigma_0 = 0.62$ fm, both chosen to reproduce the experimental $N_{pp}^{1S}/N_{pp}^{2S}$ ratio in $pp$ collisions.
- For a stationary $c\bar c$ dipole initialized as a pure $J/\psi$ state in a static medium at $T = 1.5\,T_c$ with the free energy potential, the overlap with the $\psi(2S)$ eigenfunction increased for times $t < 2$ fm/$c$.
- At $T = 1.5\,T_c$ with the free energy potential and $\sigma_0 = 0.23$ fm, the double ratio $R_{AA}^{2S}/R_{AA}^{1S}$ markedly increased with time; this increase was significantly less pronounced when using the internal energy potential.
- At $T = T_c$ with the free energy potential and $\sigma_0 = 0.23$ fm, the double ratio dropped below one for times $t > 2$ fm/$c$.
- For the larger initial width $\sigma_0 = 0.62$ fm at $T = 1.5\,T_c$, the double ratio still reached well above one at later times for both potential types, but at $T = T_c$ it dropped dramatically within the first 1 fm/$c$ and stayed small thereafter.

## Context

This work extends the sequential suppression picture of charmonia in the quark-gluon plasma (Satz, 2005) by incorporating the dynamical evolution of the $c\bar c$ dipole wavefunction, which allows transitions between ground and excited states that are not accounted for in most current transport models (Zhao, 2007; Zhu, 2004; Strickland, 2011; Ferreiro, 2014; Song, 2015; Chen, 2015). The results suggest that such "dynamical feeddown" effects could help resolve the discrepancy between transport model predictions (Chen, 2013) and experimental data (Khachatryan, 2014) for the double ratio $R_{AA}^{\psi(2S)}/R_{AA}^{J/\psi}$ in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. The authors indicate that similar effects are expected for bottomonia, particularly for $\Upsilon(2S)$ and $\Upsilon(3S)$, to be discussed in a future manuscript.
