# The Delta(1232) Resonance in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> I discuss the problem of formulating the baryon chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) in the presence of a light resonance, such as the $Δ(1232)$, the lightest nucleon resonance. It is shown how to extend the power counting of $χ$PT to correctly account for the resonant contributions. Recent applications of the resulting chiral effective-field theory to the description of pion production reactions in $Δ$-resonance region are briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard power counting of baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) fails in the presence of the Δ(1232) resonance because the resonance's excitation energy (Δ ≈ 300 MeV) sets a lower effective scale than the chiral symmetry breaking scale (Λ ≈ 1 GeV), and proposes a "δ-expansion" that treats the scale hierarchy as m_π ≪ Δ ≪ Λ to correctly account for resonant contributions in different energy domains.

## Method

The author develops a power-counting scheme (the "δ-expansion") where the small parameter δ = Δ/Λ is introduced, with m_π/Λ counted as δ². The counting of one-Delta-reducible (ODR) propagators is shown to depend on the energy domain: in the low-energy region (p ∼ m_π) ODR propagators count as O(1/δ), while in the resonance region (p ∼ Δ) they count as O(1/δ³) after resummation of self-energy corrections. The scheme is then applied to compute pion electroproduction and radiative pion photoproduction amplitudes to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the δ-expansion.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) excitation energy Δ ≡ M_Δ − M_N ≈ 300 MeV
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ is believed to be of order 1 GeV
- For pion electroproduction at Q² = 0.127 GeV² and W = 1.232 GeV, the low-energy constant g_C is fixed to −2.6 from fitting σ_TL data
- For the Δ⁺ magnetic dipole moment in the heavy-baryon limit, the imaginary part is given by Im μ_{Δ⁺} = (h_A² M_Δ)/(48π f_π²) √(Δ² − m_π²) (e/2M_Δ)
- The resonance region is formally defined as the region of momentum p where |p − Δ| ≤ δ³ Λ

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1978) by addressing the problem of light resonances, specifically the Δ(1232). It contrasts with the Small Scale Expansion (SSE) of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1997), which counts Δ ∼ m_π and overestimates Δ contributions at low energies while underestimating them at resonance energies. The δ-expansion enables systematic calculations of processes such as Compton scattering, pion electroproduction, and radiative pion photoproduction in the Δ-resonance region, and provides predictions for the pion-mass dependence of the γNΔ transition that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations from Alexandrou et al. (2004).
