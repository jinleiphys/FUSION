# On the large-Q^2 behavior of the pion transition form factor

> We study the transition of non-perturbative to perturbative QCD in situations with possible violations of scaling limits. To this end we consider the singly- and doubly-virtual pion transition form factor $π^0\toγγ$ at all momentum scales of symmetric and asymmetric photon momenta within the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach. For the doubly virtual form factor we find good agreement with perturbative asymptotic scaling laws. For the singly-virtual form factor our results agree with the Belle data. At very large off-shell photon momenta we identify a mechanism that introduces quantitative modifications to Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage scaling.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a mechanism that introduces quantitative modifications to Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) scaling for the singly-virtual pion transition form factor at very large off-shell photon momenta, but the authors cannot reliably determine whether this truly modifies the ERBL scaling limit with their numerical methods.

## Method

The authors compute the pion transition form factor using the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach with a rainbow-ladder truncation (Maris-Tandy model). They extract the on-shell form factor from off-shell kinematics by calculating the form factor inside the spacelike cone where no singularities appear, then using a fit constrained by the vector-meson pole location to extrapolate to the on-shell point. The dressed quark-photon vertex is solved from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, which dynamically generates timelike vector-meson poles.

## Key numbers

* The chiral-limit anomaly value F(0,0)=1 is reproduced; at the physical pion mass the numerical result is F(0,0)=0.996.
* Replacing both dressed vertices by bare ones gives F(0,0) ≈ 0.29; a Ball-Chiu vertex gives F(0,0) ≈ 0.86.
* The singly-virtual form factor shows a continued rise beyond the region of experimental data, settling into asymptotic behavior from momenta around η₊ ∼ 10²–10³ GeV² onwards.
* Non-perturbative effects induce important changes in observables up to momenta of order ∼30 GeV.
* The asymptotic value of the singly-virtual form factor is quantitatively influenced by the ρ-meson pole and substantially deviates from the perturbative limit of 1.
* A non-zero, realistic vector-meson width changes the asymptotic value at the level of 1‰ compared to the zero-width result.

## Context

This work extends previous DSE/BSE studies of the pion transition form factor by Raya (2015, 2016) and introduces a new technique to compute the TFF on the entire domain of spacelike momenta. The results favor the Belle data (Uehara 2012) over the BaBar data (Aubert 2009) for the singly-virtual form factor, and are mostly compatible with both. The mechanism identified is general to all multi-photon processes with at least one soft photon, and the quantitative importance of vector-meson poles up to very large momenta has been realized previously in light-cone sum rules by Khodjamirian (1997), Agaev (2010, 2012), and Mikhailov (2016).
