# Longitudinal response functions of 3H and 3He

> Trinucleon longitudinal response functions R_L(q,omega) are calculated for q values up to 500 MeV/c. These are the first calculations beyond the threshold region in which both three-nucleon (3N) and Coulomb forces are fully included. We employ two realistic NN potentials (configuration space BonnA, AV18) and two 3N potentials (UrbanaIX, Tucson-Melbourne). Complete final state interactions are taken into account via the Lorentz integral transform technique. We study relativistic corrections arising from first order corrections to the nuclear charge operator. In addition the reference frame dependence due to our non-relativistic framework is investigated. For q less equal 350 MeV/c we find a 3N force effect between 5 and 15 %, while the dependence on other theoretical ingredients is small. At q greater equal 400 MeV/c relativistic corrections to the charge operator and effects of frame dependence, especially for large omega, become more important. In comparison with experimental data there is generally a rather good agreement. Exceptions are the responses at excitation energies close to threshold, where there exists a large discrepancy with experiment at higher q. Concerning the effect of 3N forces there are a few cases, in particular for the R_L of 3He, where one finds a much improved agreement with experiment if 3N forces are included.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculations of trinucleon longitudinal response functions beyond the threshold region that fully include both three-nucleon (3N) forces and Coulomb forces, finding that for momentum transfers q ≤ 350 MeV/c the 3N force effect is between 5 and 15%, while at q ≥ 400 MeV/c relativistic corrections and frame dependence become more important; the agreement with experiment is generally good except for responses near threshold at higher q, where a large discrepancy remains.

## Method

The authors calculate the longitudinal response functions R_L(q,ω) for 3H and 3He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique with a Lanczos method for computational efficiency. They employ two realistic NN potentials (configuration space BonnA and AV18) and two 3N potentials (UrbanaIX and Tucson-Melbourne), with the Coulomb force included for 3He. The charge operator includes the non-relativistic one-body term plus Darwin-Foldy and spin-orbit relativistic corrections, and the response is computed in lab, anti-lab, and Breit frames to study frame dependence.

## Key numbers

- 3N force effect between 5 and 15% for q ≤ 350 MeV/c
- Calculations performed for q values up to 500 MeV/c and ω up to 300 MeV
- At q=500 MeV/c, the Darwin-Foldy term leads to non-negligible effects while the spin-orbit term gives negligible contributions
- At q=500 MeV/c, different nucleon form factor parametrizations lead to a 3% reduction in R_L with the Höhler parametrization
- At q=500 MeV/c, the Breit frame result is about 4% higher than the lab frame result at the quasi-elastic peak, with a peak shift of about 5 MeV
- At q=500 MeV/c, the anti-lab frame result is about 7% higher than the lab frame result at the quasi-elastic peak, with a peak shift of about 6 MeV
- J_max = 15/2 used for q = 250 and 300 MeV/c; J_max = 21/2 used for q = 350-500 MeV/c
- At q=500 MeV/c, about 2% of the Coulomb sum rule strength remains unconverged

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations by Meijgaard and Tjon (1992) that used the s-wave Malfliet-Tjon potential, and complements the work of Viviani et al. who used expansion techniques restricted to the 3He near-threshold region with only two-body breakup. The calculations enable systematic comparison with experimental data from Retzlaff et al., Dow et al., Marchand et al., and Morgenstern, and provide a baseline for future studies of transverse response functions where disentangling 3N force effects from exchange current effects would be more difficult.
