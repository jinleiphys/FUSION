# Mixing mechanism for the $J^{P}=0^{+}$ mesons

> There are three scalar nonets in the Particle Data Group (PDG), one of which includes [$a_0(980), K_0^*(700)$], another includes [$a_0(1450), K_0^*(1430)$], and the third includes [$a_0(1710), K_0^*(1950)$]. Motivated by Ref.[1], we examine an alternative mixing mechanism that could potentially explain the small mass difference between the $a_0 (1450)$ and $K_0^* (1430)$. According to the tetraquark mixing model, two types, distinguished by their color-spin structures, are necessary to describe the tetraquark structure of the two nonets containing [$a_0(980), K_0^*(700)$] and [$a_0(1450), K_0^*(1430)$]. Considering the color-spin structures, we argue that the mixing mechanism generating $a_0(1450)$ and $K_0^* (1430)$ on the one hand, and $a_0(1710)$ and $K_0^* (1950)$ on the other hand might be relevant for resolving the small mass difference. We also discuss the limitations of other mixing mechanisms that generate the two nonets involving [$a_0(980),K_0^*(700)$] and [$a_0(1450)$, $K_0^* (1430)$] or [$a_0(980),K_0^*(700)$] and [$a_0(1710)$, $K_0^* (1950)$]

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the marginal mass ordering in the $0^{+}_B$ nonet (where $M[a_0(1450)] \gtrsim M[K_0^*(1430)]$) can be resolved by a mixing mechanism between the tetraquark state $|0^{+}_B\rangle_4$ and the $q\bar{q}(\ell=1)$ state $|0^{+}\rangle_2$, which generates the $0^{+}_B$ and $0^{+}_C$ nonets, while preserving the tetraquark nature of the $0^{+}_A$ nonet, a condition the authors argue is not satisfied by the earlier mixing mechanism of Black et al. (1999).

## Method

The authors formulate a $2\times 2$ mass matrix in the basis of tetraquark and $q\bar{q}(\ell=1)$ states, using a mixing Lagrangian ${\cal L}=-\gamma\text{Tr}(NN^\prime)$ that depends only on flavor structure. They diagonalize this matrix to obtain physical masses and wave functions as functions of the mixing parameter $\gamma$, and examine three possible mixing scenarios: generating the $0^{+}_A$ and $0^{+}_B$ nonets, the $0^{+}_A$ and $0^{+}_C$ nonets, or the $0^{+}_B$ and $0^{+}_C$ nonets. The analysis focuses on the isovector ($I=1$) and isodoublet ($I=1/2$) channels, where SU$_f$(3) symmetry is preserved.

## Key numbers

* The mass difference between $a_0(1450)$ and $K_0^*(1430)$ in the $0^{+}_B$ nonet is marginal, approximately $\Delta M \approx 14$ MeV.
* The mixing parameters from the tetraquark mixing model are $\alpha\approx \sqrt{2/3}$ and $\beta\approx 1/\sqrt{3}$.
* In the Black et al. (1999) mixing mechanism at maximum mixing, the $a_0(980)$ state has equal tetraquark and $q\bar{q}(\ell=1)$ components: $|a_0(980)\rangle = 0.707 |us\bar{s}\bar{d}\rangle - 0.707 |u\bar{d}\rangle$, and the $K_0^*(700)$ state has $|K_0^*(700)\rangle = 0.857 |ud\bar{d}\bar{s}\rangle - 0.515 |u\bar{s}\rangle$, obtained at $\gamma^2_{\text{max}}=0.33$ GeV$^4$.
* The maximum mixing parameter is given by $\gamma^2_{\text{max}} = (M^2_{A^\prime} - M^2_A)^2/4$, at which the pre-mixing masses become equal: $m^2_a = m^2_{a^\prime} = \frac{1}{2}[M^2_A + M^2_{A^\prime}]$, and the mixing coefficients satisfy $C = D = 1/\sqrt{2}$.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing model of Kim et al. (2016, 2017, 2018), which describes the $0^{+}_A$ and $0^{+}_B$ nonets as linear combinations of two tetraquark types with different color-spin structures. It contrasts with the mixing mechanism of Black et al. (1999), which mixes a single tetraquark type with $q\bar{q}(\ell=1)$ states to generate the $0^{+}_A$ and $0^{+}_B$ nonets but dilutes the tetraquark nature of $a_0(980)$ and $K_0^*(700)$. The proposed mixing between $|0^{+}_B\rangle_4$ and $|0^{+}\rangle_2$ to generate the $0^{+}_B$ and $0^{+}_C$ nonets enables preserving the tetraquark interpretation of the $0^{+}_A$ nonet from Jaffe (1977) while explaining the appearance of the newly included $a_0(1710)$ in the PDG.
