# Beyond-mean-field calculations of allowed and first-forbidden $β^-$ decays of $r$-process waiting-point nuclei

> $β$-decay rates of neutron-rich nuclei, in particular those located at neutron shell closures, play a central role in simulations of the heavy-element nucleosynthesis and resulting abundance distributions. We present $β$-decay half-lives of even-even $N=82$ and $N=126$ $r$-process waiting-point nuclei calculated in the approach based on relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation with quasiparticle-vibration coupling. The calculations include both allowed and first-forbidden transitions. In the $N=82$ chain, the quasiparticle-vibration coupling has an important impact close to stability, as it increases the contribution of Gamow-Teller modes and improves the agreement with the available data. In the $N=126$ chain, we find the decay to proceed dominantly via first-forbidden transitions, even when the coupling to vibrations is included.

## Key claim

The paper claims that quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) significantly improves agreement with experimental beta-decay half-lives for N=82 waiting-point nuclei near stability by increasing Gamow-Teller contributions, but for N=126 nuclei first-forbidden transitions remain dominant even when QVC is included, with the caveat that no quenching of transition matrix elements was applied.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) calculations extended to include quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC), using the NL3 parametrization of the meson-nucleon Lagrangian for the particle-hole channel and a monopole-monopole force for isovector pairing. They computed beta-decay half-lives for even-even N=82 and N=126 r-process waiting-point nuclei including both allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden (FF) transitions, using the bare value of the weak axial coupling constant g_A = -1.27641.

## Key numbers

- For N=82 nuclei near stability, QVC decreases half-lives by several orders of magnitude compared to RQRPA, with the effect being strongest close to stability where the decay Q value is small
- In the N=126 chain, the decrease of half-lives due to QVC is at most one order of magnitude when FF transitions are included
- For N=82 nuclei far from stability (Z=34), FF transitions contribute about 50-55% of the total decay rate within both RQRPA and RQRPA+QVC
- For N=82 nuclei near stability (Z=50-52), RQRPA predicts FF contributions reaching approximately 100%, while with QVC the FF contribution remains below about 35%
- In the N=126 chain, RQRPA finds FF contributions of about 75% for Z=54-58, increasing to 100% close to stability
- Within RQRPA+QVC for N=126, 0- operators are responsible for more than 75% of the FF decay along the chain

## Context

This work extends previous global QRPA beta-decay rate calculations by Moeller (2003), Marketin (2016), and Ney (2020), which show significant disagreements particularly for nuclei above N=126. The approach builds on the charge-exchange RQRPA framework with QVC first developed by Robin (2016), and aims to improve reliability by incorporating a more precise description of nucleonic correlations. The results enable better constraints on r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, particularly for the third abundance peak, and the authors plan to examine FF transitions in other magic isotonic and isotopic chains in future work.
