# Pairing correlations in nuclear systems, from infinite matter to finite nuclei

> Finite nuclei such as those found in the chain of even tin isotopes from ^{102}Sn to ^{130}Sn, exhibit a near constancy of the 2^+_1-0^+_1 excitation energy, a constancy which can be related to strong pairing correlations and the near degeneracy in energy of the relevant single particle orbits. Large shell-model calculations for these isotopes reveal that the major contribution to pairing correlations in the tin isotopes stems from the ^1S_0 partial wave in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Omitting this partial wave and the ^3P_2 wave in the construction of an effective interaction, results in a spectrum which has essentially no correspondence with experiment. These partial wave are also of importance for infinite neutron matter and nuclear matter and give the largest contribution to the pairing interaction and energy gap in neutron star matter.

## Key claim

The near constancy of the \(2^+_1-0^+_1\) excitation energy across the even tin isotopes from \(^{102}\)Sn to \(^{130}\)Sn can be related to strong pairing correlations, and the major contribution to these pairing correlations stems from the \(^1S_0\) partial wave of the nucleon-nucleon interaction; omitting both the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) partial waves from the effective interaction produces a spectrum with essentially no correspondence with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for even tin isotopes using a model space of valence neutron holes outside a \(Z=50, N=82\) core, with single-hole energies taken from experiment. They started from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, computed a \(G\)-matrix via the Bethe-Goldstone equation in a harmonic-oscillator basis, and then constructed effective interactions using a folded-diagram expansion (the \(\hat{Q}\)-box method) including one-body and two-body diagrams through third order. To test the role of specific partial waves, they compared four different approximations to the effective interaction: the full optimal interaction (\(V_{\mathrm{eff}}\)), the bare \(G\)-matrix with all partial waves up to \(l=10\), a \(G\)-matrix containing only the \(^1S_0\) partial wave, and a \(G\)-matrix omitting both the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The experimental \(2^+_1-0^+_1\) excitation energy for even tin isotopes ranges from 1.29 MeV (\(^{116}\)Sn) to 1.13 MeV (\(^{124}\)Sn) and back to 1.23 MeV (\(^{130}\)Sn).
- The optimal effective interaction \(V_{\mathrm{eff}}\) reproduces these energies within about 0.2 MeV, giving values from 1.17 MeV (\(^{116}\)Sn) to 1.46 MeV (\(^{130}\)Sn).
- The bare \(G\)-matrix interaction (all partial waves) yields a more compressed spacing, from 1.14 MeV (\(^{116}\)Sn) down to 0.97 MeV (\(^{130}\)Sn).
- The \(G\)-matrix containing only the \(^1S_0\) partial wave gives a larger spacing, from 1.38 MeV (\(^{116}\)Sn) to 1.18 MeV (\(^{130}\)Sn).
- Omitting both the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) partial waves produces a spectroscopic catastrophe: for \(^{126}\)Sn the ground state becomes a \(4^+\) state, and the \(2^+_1-0^+_1\) excitation energy becomes negative (\(-0.32\) MeV for \(^{126}\)Sn, \(-0.21\) MeV for \(^{130}\)Sn).
- For infinite neutron matter, the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap peaks at about 3 MeV at a Fermi momentum \(k_F \approx 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the \(^3P_2\)-\(^3F_2\) channel in pure neutron matter, the pairing gap reaches up to 0.29 MeV (CD-Bonn) or 0.36 MeV (Nijm II) at \(k_F = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), with values diverging among potentials above \(k_F \sim 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- In \(\beta\)-stable matter, the \(^1S_0\) proton gap is \(\le 1\) MeV, and the \(^3P_2\) neutron gap is of order \(\sim 0.1\) MeV; relativistic effects reduce the \(^3P_2\) gap to less than half the non-relativistic value.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations from infinite neutron star matter (where the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) partial waves are known to drive superfluidity) to finite nuclei, specifically the chain of even tin isotopes. The authors build on prior shell-model calculations for Sn isotopes by Schneider (1994), Grawe (1995), Fogelberg (1994), and Hoffman (1996), and on earlier studies of pairing gaps in neutron matter by Baldo (1990, 1992), Kucharek (1996), and Elgaroy (1996). The key finding, that omitting the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) partial waves destroys agreement with experiment, establishes a direct link between the partial waves responsible for neutron star superfluidity and the pairing correlations that govern the low-lying spectra of finite nuclei. This enables future work to use the tin isotope chain as a laboratory for testing effective interactions derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.
