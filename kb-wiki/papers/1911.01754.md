# Search for the $N$(1685) in $ηπ$-Photoproduction

> The nucleon-like member $N(1685)$ of the speculative baryon antidecuplet denotes one possible explanation for the narrow peak-structure around $W = 1.68$ GeV observed in the total cross section of $η$-photoproduction off the neutron. If this baryon existed, it would likely to be seen in other reactions as well. While the aforementioned peak, whatever its nature is, was confirmed by several experiments, claims for signatures of the $N(1685)$ in other reactions and observables are mainly made by V. Kuznetsov et al. using GRAAL data. Their latest work suggests signals of both $N(1685)$ charge states in all isospin channels of $ηπ$-photoproduction off the proton and neutron. This contribution reports on challenging these claims with data from the A2 at MAMI experiment employing photon beam energies from $E_γ$=1.43-1.58 GeV. The $ηπ^0p$ and $ηπ^+n$ final states produced from a hydrogen target were studied and new analysis cuts were tested in order to enhance a possible signal.

## Key claim

The paper challenges claims by Kuznetsov et al. (2017) of a narrow N(1685) resonance signature in all isospin channels of ηπ-photoproduction, finding no statistically significant excess around 1685 MeV in the ηp or ηn invariant mass distributions from A2 at MAMI data, though small deviations in one energy bin warrant further investigation.

## Method

Exclusive measurements of γp → ηπ⁰p and γp → ηπ⁺n were performed using a tagged photon beam (Eγ = 1.43–1.58 GeV) incident on a 10-cm liquid hydrogen target at the A2 at MAMI experiment. Events were selected via kinematic fitting, particle identification using dE/E, time-of-flight, and pulse-shape analyses, and background was removed via unbinned likelihood fits to missing-mass or invariant-mass distributions using Monte Carlo simulations. Additional cuts on m(π⁰p) < 1180 MeV and Van Hove plot sector selection were applied to suppress the dominant ηΔ(1232) isobar and enhance a potential πN(1685) signal.

## Key numbers

- Photon beam energy range: Eγ = 1.43–1.58 GeV
- One data set of 310 hours was used out of three available data sets of similar size
- A cut requiring m(π⁰p) < 1180 MeV was applied to suppress ηΔ(1232) background
- The confidence level cut for kinematic fit was CL > 2.7 × 10⁻³
- Cluster thresholds in CB and TAPS calorimeters were set to 20 MeV
- The experimental trigger required energy deposition higher than ~550 MeV in the Crystal Ball detector

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of γN → ηπN reactions by LNS (Nakabayashi 2006), GRAAL (Ajaka 2008), CBELSA/TAPS (Horn 2008, Gutz 2011, Gutz 2014), and A2 at MAMI (Kashevarov 2009, Kashevarov 2010, Annand 2015, Kaeser 2016, Sokhoyan 2018), none of which found evidence for N(1685). It directly challenges the claims of Kuznetsov et al. (2017) using GRAAL data. The results enable future analyses with all available A2 data, larger Monte Carlo samples using the Bonn-Gatchina model, and comparisons with the Mainz model (Sokhoyan 2018) to reach a definitive conclusion on the existence of N(1685) in ηπ-photoproduction.
