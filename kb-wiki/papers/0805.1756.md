# Multiplicity scaling in ideal and viscous hydrodynamics

> Using numerical results from ideal and viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with three different equations of state, for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at different centralities and initial energy densities, we explore the dependence of the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow, v_2/epsilon, and the produced entropy fraction, Delta S/S_0, on the final charged hadron multiplicity density dN_ch/dy per unit transverse overlap area S, (1/S)(dN_ch/dy). The viscous hydrodynamic simulations are performed with two different versions of the Israel-Stewart kinetic evolution equations, and in each case we investigate the dependence of the physical observables on the kinetic relaxation time. We find approximate scaling of v_2/epsilon and Delta S/S_0 with (1/S)(dN_ch/dy), with scaling functions that depend on the EOS and, in particular, on the value of the specific shear viscosity eta/s. Small scaling violations are seen even in ideal hydrodynamics, caused by a breaking of the scale invariance of ideal fluid dynamics by the freeze-out condition. Viscous hydrodynamics shows somewhat larger scale-breaking effects that increase with increasing eta/s and decreasing system size and initial energy density. We propose to use precision studies of these scaling violations to help constrain the shear viscosity eta/s of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Multiplicity scaling in ideal and viscous hydrodynamics

## Key claim

The approximate scaling of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow (v₂/ε) with final charged hadron multiplicity density per unit transverse overlap area holds for both ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, but viscous hydrodynamics introduces scaling violations that increase with larger specific shear viscosity η/s, smaller system size, and lower initial energy density. These scaling violations, rather than the scaling itself, may provide a path to constrain the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the VISH2+1 code with both ideal and viscous (Israel-Stewart second-order) fluid dynamics, using three different equations of state (EOS I for massless gas, SM-EOS Q for a first-order phase transition, and EOS L for a lattice-QCD-matched crossover). They simulated Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at impact parameter b=7 fm with varying initial energy densities and explored two versions of the Israel-Stewart evolution equations (full and simplified), varying the kinetic relaxation time τ_π between 1.5η/sT and 6η/sT. The freeze-out surface was taken at constant decoupling temperature T_dec=130 MeV, with final hadron spectra computed via the Cooper-Frye integral.

## Key numbers

- For Cu+Cu at b=7 fm with SM-EOS Q and the simplified Israel-Stewart equation, viscous suppression of elliptic flow reaches almost 70% below the ideal fluid value at p_T=2 GeV/c
- For Au+Au at b=7 fm with SM-EOS Q and the simplified Israel-Stewart equation, viscous suppression is about 40% below ideal at p_T=2 GeV/c
- For Au+Au at b=7 fm with EOS L and the full Israel-Stewart equation, viscous suppression is about 25% below ideal at p_T=2 GeV/c
- The difference between full and simplified Israel-Stewart equations for EOS L with τ_π=3η/sT is about 10% on a relative scale, or about 3% on the absolute scale set by ideal fluid dynamics
- For SM-EOS Q and EOS L, the difference between full and simplified Israel-Stewart dynamics ranges from about 5% for Au+Au to about 15% for Cu+Cu at the largest τ_π value of 6η/sT
- The freeze-out temperature used was T_dec=130 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of elliptic flow scaling originally studied by Voloshin et al. (2003, 2006), who showed that v₂/ε scales with (1/S)(dN_ch/dy) across different collision systems and energies. It directly addresses the discrepancy between earlier viscous hydrodynamic results from Song and Heinz (2007), which showed strong viscous suppression for Cu+Cu, and those from Romatschke and Romatschke (2007) for Au+Au, demonstrating that the difference arises primarily from system size effects and equation of state choices rather than from the different Israel-Stewart formulations. The results enable future precision studies of scaling violations to constrain η/s, though the authors caution that improved calculations matching viscous hydrodynamics to a realistic hadron cascade below T_c are needed before quantitative extraction of η/s from data can be attempted.
