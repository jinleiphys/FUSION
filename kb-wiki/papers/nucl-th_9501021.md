# Faddeev approach to the octet and decuplet baryons

## Key claim
After determining model parameters solely from mesonic data, the calculated masses of both the nucleon and the delta deviate by less than 5% from experimental values and show only a very weak dependence on the constituent quark mass, but only for constituent quark masses between 411 MeV and 800 MeV; the strange baryons deviate by approximately 15% at the lowest allowed quark mass.

## Method
The authors extend a relativistic Faddeev model, previously applied only to the baryon octet, to also treat the baryon decuplet. Starting from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type Lagrangian, they compute two-body T-matrices in the ladder approximation for both the quark-antiquark (meson) and quark-quark (diquark) channels, then solve the three-body Faddeev equations using a static approximation that replaces the exchanged quark propagator by a constant. The model contains five parameters (two current quark masses, a cutoff, and two coupling constants) which are fixed using only mesonic observables, after which baryon masses are predicted.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass: 901 MeV (4% deviation from experimental value) at constituent quark mass m_u* = 411 MeV
- Delta mass: 1212 MeV (2% deviation from experimental value) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Constituent quark mass range for which nucleon and delta are reproduced within 5%: 411 MeV to 800 MeV
- Lower bound on constituent up quark mass: 411 MeV (required to bind the Delta(1232))
- Scalar diquark mass (ud): 710 MeV with binding energy 112 MeV at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Scalar diquark mass (us): 899 MeV with binding energy 129 MeV at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Sigma mass: 1081 MeV (9% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Lambda mass: 941 MeV (16% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Xi mass: 1114 MeV (15% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Sigma* mass: 1401 MeV (1% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Xi* mass: 1597 MeV (4% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Omega- mass: 1801 MeV (8% deviation) at m_u* = 411 MeV
- Pion decay constant: 93 MeV (used as input to fix parameters)
- Pion mass: 135 MeV (used as input)
- Kaon mass: 495 MeV (used as input)
- Rho meson mass: 770 MeV (used as input)

## Context
This work extends the relativistic Faddeev approach from the baryon octet (previously treated by Buck et al. and Ishii et al.) to include the baryon decuplet within the NJL model framework. The authors confirm the finding of Ishii et al. (1993) that including both scalar and axial vector diquark channels simultaneously increases the three-body binding energy, requiring a less bound scalar diquark. The results contrast with the solitonic approach of Weigel et al., which obtained best mass splittings at a constituent quark mass of 390 MeV; the present model cannot reach that low a mass due to the lack of a confinement mechanism. The larger deviations for strange baryons suggest that the t'Hooft interaction, which affects only scalar diquarks and therefore only octet baryons, should not be neglected in future work.
