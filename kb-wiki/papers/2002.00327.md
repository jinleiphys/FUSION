# Coupling of shape and pairing vibrations in a collective Hamiltonian based on nuclear energy density functionals

> The quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, based on relativistic energy density functionals, is extended to include a pairing collective coordinate. In addition to quadrupole shape vibrations and rotations, the model describes pairing vibrations and the coupling between shape and pairing degrees of freedom. The parameters of the collective Hamiltonian are determined by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (RMF+BCS) calculations in the space of intrinsic shape and pairing deformations. The effect of coupling between shape and pairing degrees of freedom is analyzed in a study of low-energy spectra and transition rates of four axially symmetric $N=92$ rare-earth isotones. When compared to results obtained with the standard quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, the inclusion of dynamical pairing increases the moment of inertia, lowers the energies of excited $0^+$ states and reduces the E0-transition strengths, in better agreement with data.

## Key claim

The paper extends the standard quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (QCH) to include a pairing collective coordinate, explicitly coupling shape and pairing degrees of freedom; the authors hedge that the present implementation is simplified by assuming axial shape symmetry and neglecting pairing rotations, so only two intrinsic collective coordinates (axial quadrupole deformation β and intrinsic pairing deformation α) are considered.

## Method

Constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (RMF+BCS) calculations were performed in the (β, α) plane using the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force. The resulting single-nucleon wave functions, energies, and occupation probabilities provided microscopic input for the collective Hamiltonian parameters: three mass parameters (Bββ, Bαα, Bβα), the moment of inertia (calculated via the Inglis-Belyaev formula), and the collective potential. The eigenvalue problem for the quadrupole-and-pairing collective Hamiltonian (QPCH) was solved by expanding eigenfunctions in a basis of one-dimensional harmonic oscillator states for β and α.

## Key numbers

- For 156Gd, the global minimum of the potential energy surface is at (β, α) = (0.325, 12).
- In 156Gd, the 0+2 and 0+3 states are lowered by approximately 0.534 MeV and 1.271 MeV, respectively, when dynamical pairing is included compared to the QCH.
- The lowering of the 0+2 level across the four N=92 isotones ranges from approximately 0.1 to 0.9 MeV; for the 0+3 state the interval is approximately 1.2 to 1.7 MeV.
- The expectation values of α for the ground state and first-excited state of 156Gd are 6.09 and 11.01, respectively, considerably smaller than the α value at the energy minimum.
- The pairing strength used is G = -771.68 MeV fm3, enhanced by 6% compared to the original value.

## Context

This work extends the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) model for quadrupole vibrations and rotations, previously developed by Niksic, Li, and collaborators (2009-2018) based on relativistic density functionals, to include pairing vibrations. It builds on earlier generator coordinate method (GCM) studies by Pomorski (2007) and Vaquero et al. (2011, 2013) that considered coupling of shape and pairing degrees of freedom, but uses a computationally more tractable collective Hamiltonian approach suitable for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei. The results enable future applications to nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence and to triaxial degrees of freedom, and may impact calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay.
