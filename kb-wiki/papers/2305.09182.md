# Evidence of bicluster structure in the ground state of $^{20}$Ne

> We explore the structure of the ground state of $^{20}$Ne by investigating various density profiles. Four candidates for the ground state configurations, (a) $j$-$j$ coupling and (b) SU(3) shell model and (c) $5α$ and (d) $^{16}{\rm O}+α$ cluster model configurations are generated by utilizing the antisymmetrized quasicluster model. A high-energy reaction theory, the Glauber model, relates these one-body density distributions and reaction observables. The angular distributions of the elastic scattering cross sections clearly distinguish these configurations and tell which is the most plausible one: The ground state of $^{20}$Ne favors a 16+4 nucleon bi-cluster structure. A comprehensive investigation of other electric observables also supports this conclusion.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{20}$Ne favors a 16+4 nucleon bicluster structure, but the 16-nucleon component corresponds to a $p$-shell closed configuration rather than the $4\alpha$ tetrahedron structure of free $^{16}$O, and the internal region may involve $\alpha$ cluster breaking not detectable by proton scattering.

## Method

The authors generated four candidate configurations for the $^{20}$Ne ground state using the antisymmetrized quasicluster model (AQCM): (a) $j$-$j$ coupling shell model, (b) SU(3) shell model, (c) $5\alpha$ cluster, and (d) $^{16}$O+$\alpha$ bicluster. They computed one-body density distributions for each configuration and used the Glauber model to calculate proton-nucleus elastic scattering cross sections, total reaction cross sections, and elastic charge form factors, comparing these with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The O$\alpha$ (bicluster) configuration has intercluster distance $d_5 = 3.012$ fm, nearly equal to the sum of matter radii of $^{16}$O (2.57 fm) and a four-nucleon cluster (1.85 fm)
- Diffuseness parameters: 0.501 fm ($j$-$j$), 0.544 fm (SU(3)), 0.507 fm ($5\alpha$), and 0.585 fm (O$\alpha$)
- Calculated $B(E2\downarrow)$ values: 6.42 $e^2$fm$^4$ ($j$-$j$), 18.8 $e^2$fm$^4$ (SU(3)), 51.0 $e^2$fm$^4$ ($5\alpha$), and 41.9 $e^2$fm$^4$ (O$\alpha$), compared to experimental 56.0$\pm$8.0 $e^2$fm$^4$
- The squared overlap between the tetrahedron $4\alpha$ and $p$-shell configurations of $^{16}$O is 0.024; the squared overlap between O$\alpha$ and SU(3) configurations is 0.39
- All configurations set the root-mean-square point-proton radius to 2.89 fm, reproducing the measured charge radius of $^{20}$Ne

## Context

This work extends the AQCM analysis previously applied to $^{16}$O by Horiuchi et al. (2022, 2023), which found a $4\alpha$ tetrahedron ground state, and addresses the controversy between cluster and shell-model descriptions of $^{20}$Ne. Prior studies include the $^{16}$O+$\alpha$ inversion doublet model by Horiuchi (1968) and Fujiwara (1980), the $5\alpha$ cluster model by Zhou (2014), and the non-clustered rotational model by Horikawa (1971, 1972) and Hatakeyama (2019). The results enable future investigations of cluster structures in heavier $N=Z$ nuclei such as $^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si, $^{32}$S, and $^{36}$Ar.
