# Precision mass measurements around ${}^{84}$Mo rule out ZrNb cycle formation in the rapid proton-capture process at type I X-ray bursts

> The rapid proton-capture ($rp$-) process is one of the primary, explosive thermonuclear burning processes that drive type I X-ray bursts. A possible termination of the $rp$-process at around ${}^{84}$Mo was previously suggested by the formation of a ZrNb cycle. We report here precision mass measurements at around ${}^{84}$Mo, which have concluded the possibility of the cycle. The experiment was conducted using the multi-reflection time-of-flight spectrograph at RIKEN RI Beam Factory, and the masses of ${}^{79}$Y, ${}^{83}$Nb, ${}^{84}$Mo, ${}^{88}$Ru, and an isomer in ${}^{78}$Y were measured. For ${}^{84}$Mo, and ${}^{88}$Ru, and the isomeric state of ${}^{78}$Y, their masses are experimentally determined for the first time with uncertainties of $δm \approx 20~{\rm keV/c^2}$. The mass precision of ${}^{79}$Y and ${}^{83}$Nb is improved to $13~{\rm keV/c^2}$ and $9.6~{\rm keV/c^2}$, respectively. The new $α$-separation energy of ${}^{84}$Mo, 1.434(83) MeV, unambiguously rules out the possibility of forming the ZrNb cycle. The X-ray burst simulation with the new masses shows that our measurements effectively remove the large final abundance uncertainties in the $A=80-90$ mass region. The new mass values improve the prediction power for the composition of the nuclear ashes in X-ray bursts.

## Key claim

Precision mass measurements of proton-rich nuclei around \({}^{84}\)Mo, including first-time determinations for \({}^{84}\)Mo, \({}^{88}\)Ru, and an isomer in \({}^{78}\)Y, rule out the formation of the ZrNb cycle in the rapid proton-capture process at type I X-ray bursts; the new \(\alpha\)-separation energy of \({}^{84}\)Mo, 1.434(83) MeV, is too high for the cycle to form under realistic burst conditions, contradicting earlier predictions based on the FRDM92 mass model.

## Method

The experiment used the multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrograph (MRTOF-MS) at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory, coupled to the ZeroDegree Spectrometer at BigRIPS. Proton-rich unstable nuclei were produced via fragmentation of a 345 MeV/nucleon \({}^{124}\)Xe beam, stopped in a radio-frequency carpet-type helium gas catcher, and their time-of-flight spectra were measured for doubly charged ions in the \(A = 88\), 84, 83, 79, and 78 isobar series. Masses were extracted using the single reference method with isobaric mass references of the same \(A/q\), and the \(\beta\)-decay half-life of the observed \({}^{78}\)Y state was measured to confirm it as the isomeric state.

## Key numbers

* \({}^{84}\)Mo mass excess: \(-54137(22)\) keV, determined for the first time with uncertainty \(\delta m \approx 22\) keV/c\(^2\).
* \({}^{88}\)Ru mass excess: \(-54250(19)\) keV, determined for the first time with uncertainty \(\delta m \approx 19\) keV/c\(^2\).
* \({}^{78}\)Y\(^{\rm m}\) mass excess: \(-51959(15)\) keV, determined for the first time with uncertainty \(\delta m \approx 15\) keV/c\(^2\).
* \({}^{83}\)Nb mass excess: \(-57629.2(96)\) keV, precision improved to 9.6 keV/c\(^2\) (a factor of 17 improvement over the literature value of 162 keV).
* \({}^{79}\)Y mass excess: \(-57984(13)\) keV, precision improved to 13 keV/c\(^2\).
* \(\alpha\)-separation energy of \({}^{84}\)Mo: \(S_{\alpha} = 1.434(83)\) MeV.
* Mass resolving power: \(R_{\rm m} \approx 6.5 \times 10^5\).
* X-ray burst simulation: final abundance uncertainties at \(A=82\) and \(A=83\) reduced from factors of 10 and 5.5 (at \(3\sigma\) with AME20 masses) to 8% each with the new masses.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing question about the ZrNb cycle, first proposed by Schatz (1998) based on the FRDM92 prediction of a very low \(S_{\alpha}\) for \({}^{84}\)Mo (\(-0.58\) MeV). Previous mass measurements of \({}^{83}\)Nb by Xing (2018) using isochronous mass spectrometry at CSRe and of \({}^{80}\)Zr by Hamaker (2021) using Penning trap mass spectrometry at LEBIT had left the \({}^{84}\)Mo mass as the final missing piece. The new results confirm the existence of a pronounced island of low \(S_{\alpha}\) in neutron-deficient Mo isotopes, first suggested by Haettner (2011) for \({}^{85}\)Mo, but show it is not strong enough to form the ZrNb cycle. The improved masses remove large final abundance uncertainties in the \(A=80-90\) region, enabling more reliable predictions of nuclear ash composition in X-ray bursts and improving the interpretation of crustal cooling observations in quasi-persistent soft X-ray transients.
