# Fission half-lives of super-heavy nuclei in a microscopic approach

> A systematic study of 160 heavy and super-heavy nuclei is performed in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach with the finite range and density dependent Gogny force with the D1S parameter set. We show calculations in several approximations: with axially symmetric and reflexion symmetric wave functions, with axially symmetric and non-reflexion symmetric wave functions and finally some representative examples with triaxial wave functions are also discussed. Relevant properties of the ground state and along the fission path are thoroughly analyzed. Fission barriers, Q$_α$-factors and lifetimes with respect to fission and $α$-decay as well as other observables are discussed. Larger configuration spaces and more general HFB wave functions as compared to previous studies provide a very good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the Gogny D1S force, using larger configuration spaces and more general wave functions than previous studies, provides a very good agreement with experimental data for fission half-lives and other observables of super-heavy nuclei, though the agreement is qualified by the use of several approximations (axial/reflection symmetry, triaxial examples) and the WKB method for half-life calculations.

## Method

The authors performed constrained HFB calculations for 160 heavy and super-heavy nuclei (Z=100-126, N=150-190) using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force with the D1S parameter set, expanding quasiparticle operators in axially symmetric deformed harmonic oscillator bases with N0=15 and q=1.5. They computed fission barriers along the quadrupole moment Q2 path with reflection symmetry breaking included, and evaluated spontaneous fission half-lives using the WKB approximation with the adiabatic time-dependent HFB collective inertia in the cranking approximation. Alpha-decay half-lives were calculated using the Viola-Seaborg phenomenological formula with Qα values derived from calculated ground-state binding energies.

## Key numbers

- 160 heavy and super-heavy nuclei were studied in the region 150 ≤ N ≤ 190, 100 ≤ Z ≤ 126
- For ²⁷⁰Hs, large spherical gaps were found at neutron numbers 164, 184, and 228; prolate gaps at 162; oblate gaps at 172 and 178
- Proton spherical gaps were found at Z = 92, 114, and 126; spherical-oblate gap at Z = 120; prolate gaps at Z = 104 and 108
- For ¹⁰⁰Fm isotopes, log₁₀(T_sf) ranges from 13.17 s (N=150) to -6.38 s (N=166); for ¹⁰⁸Hs isotopes, log₁₀(T_sf) ranges from -5.43 s (N=150) to -4.13 s (N=168)
- For ¹⁰⁶Sg isotopes, log₁₀(T_α) ranges from -2.43 (N=150) to 7.23 (N=172); log₁₀(T_sf) ranges from -3.01 (N=150) to -5.79 (N=170)
- The zero-point energy correction used in the action integral was taken as 0.5 MeV for all isotopes considered
- The rotational energy correction was computed using the cranking approximation to the Yoccoz moment of inertia, with an empirical factor of 0.7 applied to account for the overestimation relative to exact angular momentum projection

## Context

This work extends earlier HFB calculations with the Gogny force by Berger et al. (2000, 2001) using a larger configuration space and more general wave functions with simultaneously broken symmetries. It also provides a systematic comparison with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Möller et al. (1995) and Sobiczewski et al. (2003), and with HF+BCS calculations using Skyrme forces by several groups. The results enable predictions of the dominant decay mode (alpha decay vs. spontaneous fission) for super-heavy nuclei up to Z=126, guiding future experimental searches for new isotopes.
