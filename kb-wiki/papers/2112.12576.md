# Angular Power Spectrum and Elliptic Flow from Event Maps in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Azimuthal and polar angle distributions of particles produced in heavy ion collisions carry important information about the early state of the system and the evolution of the Quark Gluon Plasma. The pixelization code HEALPix was created by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory to analyze the cosmic microwave background. As has been shown using public data from the ALICE experiment, its two-dimensional representation of a sphere containing pixels of equal area has a broader application to heavy ion collisions. The angular power spectrum, an application of HEALPix, is directly related to the particle flow and is calculated through the angles theta and phi on the sphere. The elliptic flow is calculated from simulated AMPT events and publicly available CMS data. We show that HEALPix can be used to detect the presence of elliptic flow for lead-lead collisions and thus has broader applications in the study of QGP in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

HEALPix, originally developed for cosmic microwave background analysis, can detect elliptic flow in lead-lead collisions at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV, but the method's reliability depends on subtracting the m=0 modes to correct for detector acceptance edge effects near the polar caps, and the agreement with traditional Q-cumulants is stronger for AMPT simulated data than for CMS open data.

## Method

The authors used the HEALPix pixelization code to map the polar angle theta and azimuthal angle phi distributions of charged hadrons onto equal-area pixels on a sphere, then computed the angular power spectrum coefficients C_l from spherical harmonic decomposition of the corrected event density maps. They applied this method to both simulated events from the AMPT model (Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV, ~200,000 events, impact parameter b_max < 10 fm, with string melting activated) and to public CMS open data (Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV). The elliptic flow coefficient v_2 was extracted from the angular power spectrum using the relation |v_n|^2 = (2n+1)/2 * (C_n^{m!=0} / |b_nn|^2) * (|b_00|^2 / 4pi) and compared with v_2 from the Q-cumulants two-particle correlation method.

## Key numbers

- AMPT simulations used Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV with ~200,000 events and impact parameter b_max < 10 fm
- CMS open data used Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV with particles selected at p_T > 0.9 GeV and |eta| < 2.4
- AMPT data used particles with p_T > 0.2 GeV and |eta| < 2.4 for one analysis, and p_T > 0.9 GeV for comparison with CMS
- The sphere was divided into 768 pixels, corresponding to an l_max of 23 for the spherical harmonic decomposition
- The C_0 value was fixed at 4pi for all angular power spectra due to normalization
- For isotropic data, ~10,000 events per 5% centrality bin were mapped and averaged

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Naselsky (2012) and the methods of Machado (2018) to use the CMB angular power spectrum technique for heavy ion collisions, but applies it to CMS data rather than ALICE data, largely eliminating the edge effects from detector acceptance cuts that were problematic in prior work. The study demonstrates that the angular power spectrum method yields v_2 values consistent with the standard Q-cumulants method (Bilandzic, 2010), and shows that turning off string melting in AMPT (removing QGP indicators) diminishes the l=2 mode. This enables future work using machine learning on HEALPix Mollweide maps to differentiate events containing elliptic flow.
