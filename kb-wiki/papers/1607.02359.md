# Momentum broadening in unstable quark-gluon plasma

> Quark-gluon plasma produced at the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is unstable, if weakly coupled, due to the anisotropy of its momentum distribution. Chromomagnetic fields are spontaneously generated and can reach magnitudes much exceeding typical values of the fields in equilibrated plasma. We consider a high energy test parton traversing an unstable plasma that is populated with strong fields. We study the momentum broadening parameter $\hat q$ which determines the radiative energy loss of the test parton. We develop a formalism which gives $\hat q$ as the solution of an initial value problem, and we focus on extremely oblate plasmas which are physically relevant for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The parameter $\hat q$ is found to be strongly dependent on time. For short times it is of the order of the equilibrium value, but at later times $\hat q$ grows exponentially due to the interaction of the test parton with unstable modes and becomes much bigger than the value in equilibrium. The momentum broadening is also strongly directionally dependent and is largest when the test parton velocity is transverse to the beam axis. Consequences of our findings for the phenomenology of jet quenching in relativistic heavy ion collisions are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The momentum broadening parameter $\hat q$ in an unstable, weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma with an oblate momentum distribution grows exponentially in time due to the interaction of the test parton with unstable chromomagnetic modes, rather than being time-independent as in equilibrium; this exponential growth is the main result, and the effect is largest when the test parton velocity is transverse to the beam axis.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism that computes $\hat q$ as the solution of an initial value problem, using a one-sided Fourier transform to handle the time-dependent unstable plasma. They solve the Vlasov equation coupled with Maxwell's equations in the hard-loop approximation, expressing the electric and magnetic fields in terms of initial fluctuations of the distribution function and initial fields, then compute the field correlators along the test parton's trajectory to obtain the momentum broadening parameter.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic mass scale $m$ is defined as $m^2 \equiv 2 \sum_\sigma q_\sigma^2 \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{n_\sigma(\vec p)}{p}$, and in numerical calculations $m=1$ is used.
- For short times, $\hat q$ is of the order of the equilibrium value, but at later times it grows exponentially and becomes much bigger than the equilibrium value.
- The total transverse momentum broadening in equilibrium is proportional to the path length $L$ ($\langle p^2_T \rangle_{\rm tot} = \hat q \, L$), whereas in the unstable case $\langle p^2_T(t) \rangle$ can grow exponentially with $L$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of collisional energy loss in unstable QGP (Carrington 2015) by computing the momentum broadening parameter $\hat q$, which determines the radiative component of energy loss. It goes beyond earlier calculations of $\hat q$ in anisotropic but static plasmas (Romatschke 2006, Baier 2008) that did not account for the exponential growth of unstable modes, and it confirms the suggestion from numerical simulations (Dumitru 2007) and the Langevin formulation (Majumder 2009) that unstable modes contribute significantly to $\hat q$. The results enable a more complete description of jet quenching during the brief pre-equilibrium phase of heavy ion collisions.
