# Universal quark to gluon ratio in medium-induced parton cascade

> We investigate the radiative break-up of a highly energetic quark or gluon in a high-temperature QCD plasma. Within an inertial range of momenta $T \ll ω\ll E$, where $E$ denotes the energy of the original hard parton (jet) and $T$ the temperature of of the medium, we find that, as a result of the turbulent nature of the underlying parton cascade, the quark to gluon ratio of the soft fragments tends to a universal constant value that is independent of the initial conditions. We discuss implications of this result to jet quenching physics and the problem of thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The quark-to-gluon ratio of soft fragments in a medium-induced parton cascade approaches a universal constant value, independent of whether the initial hard parton is a quark or a gluon, but only within an inertial range of momenta \(T \ll \omega \ll E\) where the turbulent nature of the cascade dominates and inverse parton merging or elastic processes are negligible.

## Method

The authors formulate coupled evolution equations for the inclusive in-medium parton distributions (fragmentation functions) of gluons, flavor-singlet quarks, and non-singlet quarks, using a Markovian branching rate that scales as \(1/\sqrt{\omega}\) in the LPM regime. They search for stationary non-equilibrium (Kolmogorov-Zakharov) solutions of the form \(D(x) \propto 1/\sqrt{x}\) for gluons and singlet quarks, and \(D_{\rm NS}(x) \propto \sqrt{x}\) for valence quarks, and derive the algebraic constraint that fixes the quark-to-gluon ratio from the balance of \(g \to q\bar{q}\) and \(q \to qg\) splitting kernels. They also compute the scale-invariant energy and particle fluxes associated with these spectra.

## Key numbers

* The universal quark-to-gluon ratio in the stationary turbulent regime is \(Q/G \approx 0.07 \times 2 N_f\), which for \(N_f = 3\) gives \(Q/G \approx 0.42\) (Eq. 23).
* The energy flux constant for gluon-initiated processes is \(\gamma_\glu = \int_0^1 dz\, z\, (\Kc_\glug(z) + \Kc_\qg(z)) \log(1/z) \approx 25.78 + 0.177 N_f\) (Eq. 30).
* The energy flux constant for quark-initiated processes is \(\gamma_\q = \int_0^1 dz\, z\, (\Kc_\qq(z) + \Kc_\gq(z)) \log(1/z) \approx 11.595\) (Eq. 33).
* The particle flux constant for the non-singlet valence distribution is \(\gamma_\NS = \int_0^1 dz\, \Kc_\qq(z) \log(1/z) \approx 6.82\) (Eq. 38).
* For \(N_f=3\), the contributions to the total energy flux from different processes are: \(g\to gg\) contributes 25.8, \(g\to q\bar{q}\) contributes 0.5, and \(q\to gq\) contributes 4.9 (all in units of \(G\)) (Eq. 35).
* The probability for a gluon to undergo a \(g\to q\bar{q}\) splitting is \(\Pc^{\rm split}_\qg \simeq 3.54 \, \tau / \sqrt{x}\) for \(N_f=3\) (Eq. 18).

## Context

This work extends the analytic treatment of purely gluonic turbulent cascades by Blaizot (2013) and Blaizot (2015) to include quarks and gluons simultaneously, showing that the chemistry of the cascade is uniquely determined by the balance of splitting kernels. It enables quantitative predictions for the soft fragment composition in jet quenching at heavy-ion colliders and for the thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma, where the universal ratio provides a clear signature of the turbulent regime. The analysis relies on the locality of interactions in the inertial range, which ensures that the dynamics near \(x \sim 1\) do not affect the small-\(x\) universal behavior.
