# Hot origin of the Little Bang

> Ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions produce a quark-gluon matter which lies in the future light cone originating from given points on the $t=z=0$ plane of the Minkowski spacetime manifold. We show that in a weak coupling regime the Minkowski vacuum of massless fields presents itself in the "Little Bang" region as a thermal state of low $p_{T}$ particles, in close analogy to the Unruh effect for uniformly accelerated observers which are causally restricted to a Rindler wedge. It can shed some light on the mechanisms of early time thermalization in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Hot origin of the Little Bang

## Key claim

In a weak coupling regime and for low transverse momentum particles, the Minkowski vacuum of massless fields presents itself in the future light cone region of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions as a thermal state, in close analogy to the Unruh effect for uniformly accelerated observers. The author shows this for an effectively two-dimensional massless scalar field model, with the caveat that the result is approximate and holds only when interactions are weak and transverse momenta satisfy p_T * tau << 1.

## Method

The author considers a massless scalar quantum field model with a polynomial potential in Minkowski spacetime and uses Euclidean functional integral techniques to relate the Minkowski vacuum partition function to a thermal partition function. By transforming to Milne coordinates (the natural coordinate system for the future light cone) and assuming weak coupling and effectively two-dimensional dynamics (low transverse momenta), the author shows that the Euclidean functional integral for the Minkowski vacuum restricted to the future light cone becomes identical in form to a thermal partition function with periodic boundary conditions. The analysis follows the method previously applied by Unruh (1976) to derive the Unruh effect for scalar fields with interactions.

## Key numbers

- The temperature of the thermal state seen by the Milne observers is T = 1/(2 pi tau), where tau = sqrt(t^2 - z^2) is the longitudinal proper time.
- The condition for the approximation to hold is p_T * tau << 1, where p_T is transverse momentum.
- At the LHC, the saturation scale is Q_s^2 ~ 5 GeV^2, giving an estimated initial proper time tau_in ~ Q_s^{-1} ~ 0.1 fm and an initial temperature T_in ~ Q_s/(2 pi) ~ 0.35 GeV.
- At RHIC, the saturation scale is Q_s^2 ~ 2 GeV^2.
- The periodicity parameter in the Euclidean functional integral is beta_M = 2 pi / b, where b is an arbitrary scale parameter that cancels out in physical variables.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between the "Little Bang" of heavy ion collisions and cosmology, building on the Milne coordinate description of the fireball region (Bjorken, 1983; Milne, 1935). The author draws a direct parallel to the Unruh effect (Unruh, 1976) and to earlier work by Olson (2015) who showed entanglement between fields in the future and past light cones. The results suggest a modification of the bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier et al., 2001) by proposing that hard gluons with typical transverse momentum Q_s are immersed in a Minkowski vacuum thermal bath with temperature 1/(2 pi tau) immediately after they are freed from classical fields at tau ~ Q_s^{-1}, potentially speeding up hydrodynamization and possibly addressing the direct photon flow puzzle observed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.
