# Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for stable and causal relativistic hydrodynamics

> We construct stable and causal effective field theories (EFTs) for describing statistical fluctuations in relativistic diffusion and relativistic hydrodynamics. These EFTs are fully non-linear, including couplings to background sources, and enable us to compute n-point time-ordered correlation functions including the effects of statistical fluctuations. The EFTs we construct are inspired by the Maxwell-Cattaneo model of relativistic diffusion and Müller-Israel-Stewart model of relativistic hydrodynamics respectively, and have been derived using both the Martin-Siggia-Rose and Schwinger-Keldysh formalisms. The EFTs non-linearly realise the dynamical Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) symmetry, which ensures that n-point correlation functions and interactions in the theory satisfy the appropriate fluctuation-dissipation theorems. Since these EFTs typically admit ultraviolet sectors that are not fixed by the low-energy infrared symmetries, we find that they simultaneously admit multiple realisations of the dynamical KMS symmetry. We also comment on certain obstructions to including statistical fluctuations in the recently-proposed stable and causal Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun model of relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper constructs stable and causal effective field theories (EFTs) for statistical fluctuations in relativistic diffusion and hydrodynamics, but notes that these EFTs admit multiple simultaneous realizations of the dynamical Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) symmetry because the ultraviolet sectors are not fixed by low-energy infrared symmetries; the authors also find that the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) formalism does not generalize to a consistent Schwinger-Keldysh EFT.

## Method

The authors first develop a Schwinger-Keldysh EFT for the Maxwell-Cattaneo model of relativistic diffusion using both the Martin-Siggia-Rose and Schwinger-Keldysh formalisms, then generalize this to a Müller-Israel-Stewart-based EFT for full relativistic hydrodynamics. The construction non-linearly realizes the dynamical KMS symmetry to ensure fluctuation-dissipation theorems are satisfied, and the authors analyze the linearized mode spectrum in boosted frames to verify stability and causality conditions.

## Key numbers

- The retarded 2-point function for charge density in the Maxwell-Cattaneo diffusion model is \( G^R_{J^tJ^t}(\omega,k) = \frac{- \sigma k^2}{i\omega (1 - i\omega\tau) - D k^2} \), where \(\sigma\) is conductivity, \(\tau\) is relaxation time, and \(D = \sigma/\chi\) is the diffusion constant.
- The retarded 2-point function for transverse flux is \( G^R_{J^i_\perp J^j_\perp}(\omega,k) = \frac{i\omega\sigma}{1-i\omega\tau} k^{ij} \).
- Stability in all boosted frames requires the relaxation time to satisfy \(\tau > D > 0\).
- The gapped longitudinal mode in a boosted frame has dispersion \(\omega = -i\frac{\sqrt{1-v_0^2}}{\tau - v_0^2 D} + \ldots\), and the transverse gapped mode has \(\omega = -i\frac{\sqrt{1-v_0^2}}{\tau} + \ldots\).
- The asymptotic propagation speed at large wavevector is \(c_\infty = (\tau - D)/(\tau - v^2_0 D)\), with \(0 < c_\infty < 1\) under the stability bound.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh EFT framework for relativistic hydrodynamics originally developed by Glorioso and Liu (2018), addressing the instability and acausality problems identified by Hiscock and Lindblom (1985, 1987) in standard relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics. The construction is inspired by the Müller-Israel-Stewart formalism (Muller 1967, Israel 1976, Israel and Stewart 1979) and the Maxwell-Cattaneo model of relativistic diffusion (Jou et al. 1988), and it enables systematic computation of n-point time-ordered correlation functions including statistical fluctuations while preserving causality and stability perturbatively at all loop orders. The paper also comments on obstructions to including fluctuations in the BDNK model (Bemfica et al. 2017, Kovtun 2019), contrasting with the recent work of Abbasi and Tahery (2022) which used a BDNK-like EFT for diffusion.
