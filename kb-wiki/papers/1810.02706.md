# Polarization and Chirality: the quantum features of the Quark Gluon Plasma

> Polarization and chirality are direct manifestations of quantum mechanics in the Quark Gluon Plasma as a relativistic fluid. This is one of the reasons why they are intriguing phenomena, that have attracted so much attention lately. In this talk I will review what is, in my view, the current theoretical understanding and highlight the most interesting recent result.

## Key claim

Polarization and chirality are direct manifestations of quantum mechanics in the Quark Gluon Plasma as a relativistic fluid, but the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is yet to be discovered in heavy ion collisions, while polarization has already seen first positive evidence reported by the STAR experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical understanding of polarization and chirality in the Quark Gluon Plasma, focusing on the Chiral Magnetic Effect and global polarization phenomena. For polarization, the author describes a method using the density operator at Local Thermodynamic Equilibrium (LTE) to calculate the mean spin vector of spin-1/2 particles, which depends on thermal vorticity and includes contributions from temperature gradients, fluid vorticity, and acceleration. The author also discusses the transition from standard viscous hydrodynamics to Chiral-Relativistic-Magneto-Hydrodynamics (chi-RMHD) for studying the CME.

## Key numbers

- The CME current formula is given as j_el = (e^2 / 2 pi^2) mu_A B, where mu_A is the axial chemical potential
- The mean axial current in presence of vorticity is j^mu_A = (T^2/6 + mu^2 / 2 pi^2) omega^mu
- The mean spin vector formula for spin-1/2 particles involves a factor of 1/(8m) and includes the Fermi-Dirac distribution n_F = (1 + exp[beta(x) dot p - mu(x) Q/T(x)] + 1)^{-1}
- The acceleration of the plasma at the outset of the hydrodynamical stage is estimated to be A ~ 10^30 g

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Vilenkin (1980) for chiral effects and builds on the first experimental evidence of global polarization reported by the STAR collaboration. The work connects to ongoing efforts to develop chi-RMHD codes (Boyarsky, 2012; Landsteiner, 2013; Hattori, 2017) and to hybrid codes like AFVD (Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics). The paper identifies discrepancies between hydrodynamic model predictions and new STAR results for the azimuthal dependence of spin components, and suggests that developing relativistic hydrodynamics with spin (Florkowski, 2018) is a major theoretical step needed to resolve these issues.
