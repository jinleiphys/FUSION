# Stability of the neutron-proton-electron matter under strong magnetic fields: the covariant Vlasov approach

> The neutron-proton-electron(npe) matter under a strong magnetic field is studied in the context of the covariant Vlasov approach. We use a Walecka-type hadronic model and the dispersion relations for the longitudinal and transverse modes are obtained. The instability regions for longitudinal and transverse modes are also studied. The crust-core transition of a magnetized neutron star is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives dispersion relations for both longitudinal and transverse density modes in neutron-proton-electron matter under strong magnetic fields using a covariant Vlasov approach, but the analysis is restricted to zero temperature and the mean-field approximation, with the magnetic field treated as uniform and external.

## Method

The authors start from a Walecka-type hadronic model (NL3 and FSU parametrizations) and use the covariant Wigner function formalism to derive a semiclassical Vlasov equation for neutrons, protons, and electrons in a uniform external magnetic field. They linearize the Vlasov equation around equilibrium, apply the Oberman-Ron transform to handle the angular dependence introduced by the magnetic field, and obtain the dispersion relations for longitudinal (parallel to the field) and transverse (perpendicular to the field) collective modes by solving a 5x5 matrix equation involving density and scalar density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The electron density at zero temperature is given by \(\rho^{(0)}_e = \frac{eB}{2\pi^2} \sum_{n=0}^{n_{max}} g_n p_F^{(e)}(n)\), where \(n_{max} = \left[\frac{\mu_e^2 - m_e^2}{2eB}\right]\) (floor function), \(g_n = 1\) for \(n=0\) and \(g_n = 2\) for \(n \geq 0\), and \(p_F^{(e)}(n) = \sqrt{\mu_e^2 - m_e^2 - 2eBn}\).
- The electromagnetic coupling constant is \(e = \sqrt{4\pi/137}\).
- The magnetic field strength considered is up to \(10^{15}\) G, characteristic of magnetars.
- The NL3 and FSU parametrizations are used for nuclear matter parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Fang et al. (2016, 2017) that calculated the crust-core transition in magnetized neutron stars using dynamical spinodal sections from the linearized Vlasov equation, but only for longitudinal modes propagating along the magnetic field. The present formalism generalizes the approach to allow calculation of density modes propagating in arbitrary directions with respect to the magnetic field, and it opens the possibility to compute electrical and thermal conductivity in magnetized matter, which is important for understanding magnetar cooling. The method builds on techniques from magnetized plasma physics developed by Kelly and Oberman-Ron, and on earlier covariant Wigner function treatments of relativistic electron gases by Hakim et al.
