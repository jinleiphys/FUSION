# On high-order perturbative calculations at finite density

> We discuss the prospects of performing high-order perturbative calculations in systems characterized by a vanishing temperature but finite density. In particular, we show that the determination of generic Feynman integrals containing fermionic chemical potentials can be reduced to the evaluation of three-dimensional phase space integrals over vacuum on-shell amplitudes - a result reminiscent of a previously proposed "naive real-time formalism" for vacuum diagrams. Applications of these rules are discussed in the context of the thermodynamics of cold and dense QCD, where it is argued that they facilitate an extension of the Equation of State of cold quark matter to higher perturbative orders.

# On high-order perturbative calculations at finite density

## Key claim

The authors prove that generic Feynman integrals containing fermionic chemical potentials at zero temperature can be reduced to three-dimensional phase space integrals over vacuum on-shell amplitudes, validating the "naive real-time formalism" for all Euclidean n-point functions up to arbitrary order in perturbation theory, whereas previously this formalism had only been checked on a case-by-case basis up to partial three-loop order.

## Method

The authors derive a set of "cutting rules" within the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory, showing that any 1PI N-loop n-point Feynman graph at finite chemical potential can be decomposed into a sum of 0-cut through N-cut contributions. Each j-cut term involves removing j fermionic propagators from the original graph, evaluating the resulting (N-j)-loop (n+2j)-point amplitude at T=μ=0 with all external momenta real-valued, placing the cut momenta on shell (q₀ = iE), and integrating over the cut three-dimensional momenta with weights -θ(μ-E)/(2E). The proof proceeds in three steps: first establishing the form of the integral at vanishing chemical potential, then generalizing to finite density by modifying residues with θ(E-μ) factors, and finally rewriting θ(E-μ) = 1-θ(μ-E) to recover the cutting rules.

## Key numbers

- The current state-of-the-art perturbative equation of state for zero-temperature quark matter is from a three-loop, or O(α_s²), calculation.
- The cutting rules reduce the evaluation of N-loop graphs to phase space integrals over (N-j)-loop vacuum amplitudes, with the chemical potential dependence isolated in θ(μ-E) factors.
- The authors demonstrate the rules explicitly on a two-loop "sunset" integral, showing it decomposes into a 0-cut term (the μ=0 graph), two 1-cut terms, and one 2-cut term, each involving on-shell vacuum amplitudes.
- The regularization of unphysical divergences in cut graphs is handled via principal value prescription, with the integral defined as lim_{ε→0⁺} {∫_{-∞}^{-ε} + ∫_{ε}^{∞}} dp₀/(2π) ∫_p.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach to cold and dense QCD thermodynamics, where the sign problem prevents lattice methods (deForcrand, 2010) and the available first-principles methods are limited to Chiral Effective Theory at low densities (Machleidt, 2011) and perturbative QCD at ultrahigh density (Kraemmer, 2003). The cutting rules were proposed but not explicitly derived in Kurkela (2009) for the three-loop equation of state; this paper provides a full proof to all orders. The results enable extension of the cold quark matter equation of state to four-loop order (O(α_s³)), with the logarithmic contributions α_s³ ln² α_s and α_s³ ln α_s near completion in a forthcoming publication. The formalism connects to earlier work by Dashen (1969) on statistical-physics quantities and scattering amplitudes, and to the "naive real-time formalism" discussed in Andersen (2000).
