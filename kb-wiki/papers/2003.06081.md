# Signature dependent triaxiality for shape evolution from superdeformation in rapidly rotating $^{40}$Ca and $^{41}$Ca

> We investigate the possible occurrence of the highly-elongated shapes near the yrast line in $^{40}$Ca and $^{41}$Ca at high spins on the basis of the nuclear energy-density functional method. Not only the superdeformed (SD) yrast configuration but the yrare configurations on top of the SD band are described by solving the cranked Skyme-Kohn-Sham equation in the three-dimensional coordinate-space representation. It is suggested that some of the excited SD bands undergo band crossings and develop to the hyperdeformation (HD) beyond $J \simeq 25 \hbar$ in $^{40}$Ca. We find that the change of triaxiality in response to rotation plays a decisive role for the shape evolution towards HD, and that this is governed by the signature quantum number of the last occupied orbital at low spins. This mechanism can be verified in an experimental observation of the positive-parity SD yrast signature-partner bands in $^{41}$Ca, one of which ($Œ±=+1/2$) undergoes crossings with the HD band while the other ($Œ±=-1/2$) shows the smooth evolution from the collective rotation at low spins to the non-collective rotation with oblate shape at the termination.

# Signature dependent triaxiality for shape evolution from superdeformation in rapidly rotating $^{40}$Ca and $^{41}$Ca

## Key claim

The change of triaxiality in response to rotation plays a decisive role for shape evolution from superdeformation (SD) towards hyperdeformation (HD) in $^{40}$Ca and $^{41}$Ca, and this is governed by the signature quantum number of the last occupied orbital at low spins; specifically, occupation of the $\nu[200]1/2 (r=+\ii)$ orbital favors positive-$\gamma$ deformation and enables evolution to HD, while occupation of the signature-partner $\nu[200]1/2 (r=-\ii)$ orbital favors negative-$\gamma$ deformation leading to band termination around $I=20$.

## Method

The cranked Skyrme-Kohn-Sham equation was solved in the three-dimensional coordinate-space representation with a mesh size of 0.8 fm and 12 points per direction, using the SkM* functional (and several other functionals for comparison). Configurations were specified by occupation numbers of single-particle orbitals labeled by parity and signature quantum numbers, and the selfconsistent solutions were traced by gradually increasing the rotational frequency in steps of 0.1 MeV/$\hbar$ with no constraint on shape except reflection symmetry.

- The MD configuration in $^{40}$Ca reaches $\beta = 0.97$, $\gamma = -0.9^\circ$ at $\omega_{\rm rot}=2.5$ MeV/$\hbar$ ($I \simeq 39$).
- The negative-parity SD bands $[6554]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ and $[6545]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ in $^{40}$Ca undergo band crossings at $I = 19$--$23$ and $I = 24$--$28$, respectively, with aligned angular momentum of $\sim 8\hbar$, developing deformation from $\beta=0.45$ to $0.65$.
- The configurations $[5645]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ and $[5654]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ in $^{40}$Ca terminate around $I=20$ with $\gamma \simeq -60^\circ$.
- In $^{41}$Ca, the positive-parity SD yrast signature-partner bands $[6555]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ ($\alpha=+1/2$) and $[5655]_\nu[5555]_\pi$ ($\alpha=-1/2$) are predicted, where the $\alpha=+1/2$ band undergoes crossings with the HD band while the $\alpha=-1/2$ band shows smooth evolution to non-collective oblate rotation at termination.

## Context

This work extends the cranked Skyrme-Kohn-Sham calculations of Inakura et al. (2002) and the cranked relativistic-mean-field calculations of Ray et al. (2016) for $^{40}$Ca, which predicted SD and HD bands but did not clarify how triaxial deformation and termination emerge. The present study provides a microscopic mechanism based on the signature quantum number of the $[200]1/2$ orbital, and proposes that this mechanism can be verified experimentally by observing the positive-parity SD yrast signature-partner bands in $^{41}$Ca. The results enable targeted experimental searches for HD bands in the $A\sim40$ mass region.
