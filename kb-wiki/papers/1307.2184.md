# Functional renormalization group study of the two-flavor linear sigma model in the presence of the axial anomaly

> The $SU(2)_A \times U(2)_V$-symmetric chiral linear sigma model in the presence of the axial anomaly is studied in the local-potential approximation of the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG). The renormalization group (RG) flow is investigated in a truncation which reproduces recent results for the $U(2)_A \times U(2)_V$-symmetric model in the limit of vanishing axial anomaly strength. We search for the conjectured O(4) fixed point in the presence of the $U(1)_A$ anomaly and analyze its stability properties.

## Key claim
The authors search for the conjectured O(4) fixed point of the chiral phase transition in two-flavor QCD using the functional renormalization group, but find that in the presence of the axial anomaly (c ≠ 0) the O(4) fixed points FP6 and FP7 are unstable, contradicting the naive expectation that the O(4) universality class describes the second-order transition.

## Method
The authors study the SU(2)_A × U(2)_V-symmetric linear sigma model with eight scalar fields (σ, π, η, a) using the functional renormalization group in the local-potential approximation. They expand the effective potential to fourth order in the fields, derive flow equations for the couplings using two equivalent parameterizations (one in terms of invariants φ, ξ, α and one in terms of original fields), and search for fixed points by numerically solving the system of coupled beta functions in d=3 spatial dimensions.

## Context
This work extends the FRG study of Fukushima (2010) for the U(2)_A × U(2)_V-symmetric model to include the U(1)_A anomaly terms (c, y, z couplings) that break the symmetry to SU(2)_A × U(2)_V. The authors test the O(4) conjecture of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) that the chiral phase transition for N_f=2 falls into the O(4) universality class when the anomaly is present. The results show that at finite anomaly strength the O(4) fixed points are unstable, but the authors argue that in the limit c → -∞ (physically relevant for reproducing the eta meson mass) the heavy η and a fields decouple via the Appelquist-Carazzone theorem, potentially restoring O(4) critical behavior.
