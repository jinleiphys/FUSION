# Phase-Space Exploration in Nuclear Giant Resonance Decay

> The rate of phase-space exploration in the decay of isovector and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances in $^{40}$Ca is analyzed. The study is based on the time dependence of the survival probability and of the spectrum of generalized entropies evaluated in the space of 1p-1h and 2p-2h states. If the 2p-2h background shows the characteristics typical for chaotic systems, the isovector excitation evolves almost statistically while the isoscalar excitation remains largely localized, even though it penetrates the whole available phase space.

# Phase-Space Exploration in Nuclear Giant Resonance Decay

## Key claim
The rate at which an initially excited giant resonance explores its available phase space depends critically on both the isospin character of the excitation and the chaoticity of the 2p-2h background states. The isovector giant quadrupole resonance in ⁴⁰Ca evolves almost statistically when the background exhibits Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) fluctuations, while the isoscalar resonance remains largely localized even though it penetrates the entire available phase space.

## Method
The authors diagonalize a nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of a mean-field part and a residual two-body interaction in a basis of 1p-1h and 2p-2h states up to 50 MeV excitation energy in ⁴⁰Ca, using 26 1p-1h and 3014 2p-2h states. They compute the time-dependent survival probability P(t) and the spectrum of generalized entropies K_q(t) for both isovector and isoscalar quadrupole excitations, comparing three classes of 2p-2h background fluctuations: degenerate, Poissonian (integrable), and GOE (chaotic). The initial state is defined by the action of a one-body external field operator on the ground state.

## Key numbers
- The model space contains 26 1p-1h states and 3014 2p-2h states, for a total of 3040 states.
- For the isovector excitation with GOE background, the asymptotic value of P(t) multiplied by the total number of states N=3040 reaches an average value close to 3 (specifically 3.08).
- The characteristic "excitation time" is approximately 10⁻²² seconds, and the correlation hole extends over a time interval four orders of magnitude longer.
- The asymptotic information entropy K reaches 7.10 for the isovector excitation and 6.53 for the isoscalar excitation, compared to K^GOE = 7.29 and a uniform-distribution maximum of approximately 8.02.
- Initial information entropies are 2.56 (isovector) and 2.40 (isoscalar).
- For q approaching 0, the generalized entropy K_q approaches a value of 8, indicating that the whole space of 3040 states is visited on the level of small probabilities.

## Context
This work extends the model developed by Drozdz et al. (1994) and Drozdz et al. (1993) for studying the interplay between collectivity and chaos in nuclear giant resonances. It builds on earlier studies of quantum chaos in nuclei that were mostly based on level statistics (Bohigas et al., 1984), which the authors note is restrictive because it requires very precise energy resolution and does not address the role of collectivity. The finding that the isovector excitation approaches the factor-of-3 elastic enhancement predicted by random-matrix theory (Verbaarschot et al., 1985) and considered a quantum signature of chaos (Lewenkopf et al., 1991) provides a new dynamical test of chaoticity that could be verified experimentally through the spectral autocorrelation function. The work enables future experimental searches for the predicted correlation hole in the average survival probability, which is accessible via the convolution formula relating transition strength distributions to time-dependent quantities.
