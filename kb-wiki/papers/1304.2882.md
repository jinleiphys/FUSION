# Electromagnetic Structure of the Z_c(3900)

> The observation of the exotic quarkonium state Z_c(3900) by the BESIII and Belle collaborations supports the concept of hadronic molecules. Charmonium states interpreted as such molecules would be bound states of heavy particles with small binding energies. This motivates their description using an effective theory with contact interactions. In particular, we focus on the electromagnetic structure of the charged state Z_c(3900). Using first experimental results concerning spin and parity, we interpret it as an S-wave molecule and calculate the form factors as well as charge and magnetic radii up to next-to-leading order. We also present first numerical estimations of some of these observables at leading order.

## Key claim

The paper interprets the Z_c(3900) as an S-wave hadronic molecule composed of charm mesons and calculates its electromagnetic properties using a pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)), but the authors explicitly state that the binding momentum of the Z_c is too large for the EFT expansion to be quantitatively reliable, so the results should be considered rough model estimates rather than precise predictions.

## Method

The authors treat the Z_c(3900) as a virtual state (negative scattering length) of D and D* mesons in a relative S-wave with total spin J=1, using a non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interactions (EFT(π̸)) up to next-to-leading order (NLO). They employ the "dimeron" formalism with an auxiliary Z field to describe the molecular state, calculate the charge and magnetic form factors from Feynman diagrams involving photon couplings to the constituent mesons, and extract the charge radius and magnetic moment from the low-energy expansion of these form factors.

## Key numbers

- Z_c(3900) mass: M_Z = (3899.0 ± 8.5) MeV, width: Γ_Z = (46 ± 30) MeV
- Binding energy: B = (-23.13 ± 6.01) MeV (negative, indicating a resonance/virtual state)
- Binding momentum: γ = (-211.6 ± 27.5) MeV
- Scattering length: a = (-0.93 ± 0.12) fm
- Squared charge radius at leading order: ⟨r_c²⟩ = (0.11 ± 0.03) fm²
- The binding momentum |γ| ≈ 212 MeV exceeds the pion mass m_π ≈ 140 MeV, violating the EFT expansion parameter condition Q/m_π < 1

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory, previously used for the deuteron (Chen 1999, Phillips 2002) and the X(3872) charm molecule (Braaten 2003, AlFiky 2005, Canham 2009), to the newly discovered Z_c(3900) state reported by BESIII and Belle (2013). The results provide first model estimates of the electromagnetic structure of this exotic state, but the authors note that quantitative predictions require going beyond the pionless EFT to include explicit pions, as the binding momentum is too large for the current framework to be fully applicable.
