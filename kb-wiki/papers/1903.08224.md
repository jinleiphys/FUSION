# Thermodynamics of Neutrons in a Magnetic Field and its Implications for Neutron Stars

> We investigate the effects of a magnetic field on the thermodynamics of a neutron system at finite density and temperature. Our main motivation is to deepen the understanding of the physics of a class of neutron stars known as magnetars, which exhibit extremely strong magnetic fields. Taking into account two facts, (i) the existence of a pressure anisotropy in the presence of a magnetic field and (ii) that the quantum field theory contribution to the pressure is non-negligible, we show that the maximum value that the inner magnetic field of a star can reach while being in agreement with the magnetohydrostatic equilibrium between the gravitational and matter pressures becomes $10^{17}$ G, an order of magnitude smaller than the previous value obtained through the scalar virial theorem; that the magnetic field has a negligible effect on the neutron system's equation of state; that the system's magnetic susceptibility increases with the temperature; and that the specific heat $C_V$ does not significantly change with the magnetic field in the range of temperatures characteristic of protoneutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the maximum inner magnetic field of a neutron star consistent with magnetohydrostatic equilibrium is \(10^{17}\) G, an order of magnitude smaller than the previous estimate from the scalar virial theorem, but only when both the pressure anisotropy induced by the magnetic field and the quantum field theory contribution to the pressure are taken into account.

## Method

The authors model a neutron system at finite density and temperature using an effective Lagrangian where neutrons interact with a uniform magnetic field via their anomalous magnetic moment (AMM). They calculate the grand-canonical thermodynamic potential in the one-loop approximation, separating it into a quantum-field-theory (QFT) vacuum contribution, a statistical many-particle contribution, and a zero-point subtraction term. From the thermodynamic potential, they derive the energy density, parallel and perpendicular pressures (including the Maxwell contribution from the magnetic field itself), and the magnetization, then analyze the equation of state and the conditions for magnetohydrostatic equilibrium.

## Key numbers

*   The maximum inner magnetic field of a neutron star consistent with magnetohydrostatic equilibrium is \(10^{17}\) G, an order of magnitude smaller than the previous value obtained through the scalar virial theorem.
*   For a chemical potential \(\mu = 1.2\) GeV, the parallel pressure (including the Maxwell contribution) has a root at \(B^{(0)}_\parallel \approx 4.5 \times 10^{18}\) G.
*   For a chemical potential \(\mu = 1.2\) GeV, the perpendicular pressure (without the Maxwell contribution) has a root at \(B^{(0)}_\perp \approx 2.9 \times 10^{19}\) G.
*   The upper bound on the magnetic field from the condition that the chemical potential must be greater than or equal to the combination \(M_N \pm k_N B\) is approximately \(B_{max}(\mu = 2.1 \text{ GeV}) \approx 1.93 \times 10^{20}\) G.

## Context

This work extends studies of the equation of state of magnetized neutron star matter (e.g., Lattimer (year not given), and the works cited as EoS-AMM through Insignificance) by explicitly incorporating the pressure anisotropy known from earlier work (Canuto (year not given), and the work cited as Quark-Matter-field) and the previously ignored quantum field theory contribution to the pressure. The result that the maximum inner field is an order of magnitude smaller than the scalar virial theorem estimate (Nuclear-matter-field, year not given) implies that the effect of the AMM interaction on the neutron equation of state is negligible for the allowed field range, similar to the conclusion for charged particles in Insignificance (year not given). The finding that magnetic susceptibility increases with temperature mirrors a result from lattice QCD calculations for the deconfined quark-gluon plasma (Bonati, year not given).
