# Partial-transpose-guided entanglement classes and minimum noise filtering in many-body Gaussian quantum systems

> The reduction and distortion of quantum correlations in the presence of classical noise leads to varied levels of inefficiency in the availability of entanglement as a resource for quantum information processing protocols. While generically minimizing required entanglement for mixed quantum states remains challenging, a class of many-body Gaussian quantum states ($\mathcal{N}$IC) is here identified that exhibits two-mode bipartite entanglement structure, resembling that of pure states, for which the logarithmic negativity entanglement measure remains invariant upon inclusion of the classical correlations and optimal entanglement resources can be clearly quantified. This subclass is found to be embedded within a broader class of many-body Gaussian states ($\mathcal{N}$-SOL) that retain two-mode entanglement structure for detection processes. These two entanglement classes are relevant in theoretical and experimental applications from the scalar field vacuum to the local axial motional modes of trapped ion chains. Utilizing the subspace that heralds inseparability in response to partial transposition, a minimum noise filtering process is designed to be necessary, sufficient, and computable for determining membership in these classes of entanglement structure. Application of this process to spacelike regions of the free scalar field vacuum is found to improve resource upper bounds, providing new understanding of the entanglement required for the quantum simulation of quantum fields as observed by arrays of local detectors.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two entanglement classes of many-body Gaussian quantum states, $\mathcal{N}$-SOL and $\mathcal{N}$IC, for which the logarithmic negativity entanglement measure can be consolidated into two-mode form and, in the $\mathcal{N}$IC subclass, remains invariant upon inclusion of classical correlations, enabling optimal entanglement resource quantification. The authors hedge that while generic minimization of required entanglement for mixed states remains challenging, these classes are computable and physically relevant.

## Method

The authors define the $\mathcal{N}$-SOL property via symplectic orthogonality of local partial-transpose (PT) eigenvectors in the negativity-contributing subspace ($\mathcal{V}_{\mathcal{N}}$), and show it is necessary and sufficient for consolidating multimode bipartite Gaussian entanglement into a tensor-product series of two-mode squeezed vacuum states (TMSVS) via local symplectic transformations. They then design a minimum noise filtering (MNF) procedure that iteratively subtracts classical noise from the consolidated covariance matrix, isolating pure TMSVS cores and establishing separability structure; states for which the remaining halo is separable after all cores are extracted are identified as $\mathcal{N}$IC-decomposable.

## Key numbers

* The logarithmic negativity is given by $\mathcal{N} = -\sum_{j=1}^{n_-} \log_2 \tilde{\nu}_j$, where $\tilde{\nu}_j$ are the PT-symplectic eigenvalues less than one (Eq. 4).
* For symmetric two-mode Gaussian states, the $\mathcal{V}_{\mathcal{N}}$ subspace is characterized by a single PT-symplectic eigenvalue $\tilde{\nu}_- = \sqrt{(n+k_p)(n-k_q)}$, with two-mode squeezing parameter $r = -\frac{1}{2} \ln \tilde{\nu}_-$.
* The MNF procedure requires $n_-$ iterations for a state with $n_-$ negativity-contributing PT-symplectic eigenvalues, producing $n_-$ core pairs plus a halo.
* For $\mathcal{N}$IC-decomposable states, the minimum negativity of the pure-state decomposition satisfies $\mathcal{N}_p^{\text{min}} = \mathcal{N} > 0$.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement consolidation technique originally applied to disjoint regions of the free lattice scalar field vacuum by Klco (2022) to all many-body bipartite Gaussian states satisfying the $\mathcal{N}$-SOL property. It generalizes the symmetric two-mode Gaussian state analysis of Giedke (2003) and Wolf (2004) to multimode systems, and provides a computable classifier for states where optimal entanglement resources can be quantified, enabling improved resource upper bounds for quantum simulation of quantum fields as observed by arrays of local detectors.
