# Investigating the soft and hard limits in transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions

> The transverse momentum spectra and their multiplicity dependence serve as key tools for extracting parameters to be compared with theoretical models. Over the past decade, the scientific community has extensively studied the possibility of a system analogous to quark-gluon plasma, predicted in heavy nuclei collisions, also existing in collisions involving light nuclei and protons. We have reanalysed the data published by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC. We present the dependence of the mean transverse momenta obtained in the soft and soft+hard (mixed) parts. Finally, we also discuss possible refinements of the analyses concerning the use of statistical parameters of higher order, aimed at a more detailed way of comparing the models with data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions exhibit a universal crossing point at approximately 0.6 GeV/c when comparing multiplicity-class spectra to the inclusive spectrum, and that below this value the mean transverse momentum and variance show no dependence on charged-particle multiplicity or collision energy across the LHC energy range from 0.9 to 13 TeV.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed published ALICE Collaboration data on charged-particle transverse momentum spectra in inelastic pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 0.9, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV, using the published multiplicity classes based on tracklets in the Silicon Pixel detector and V0 detector amplitudes. They separated each spectrum into a soft region (0.15 to 0.6 GeV/c) and a mixed soft+hard region (0.6 to 4.0 GeV/c) based on the observed crossing point in ratios of normalized spectra to the inclusive spectrum. They computed the first two statistical moments (mean and variance) of the pT distributions in each region and compared the experimental results with Pythia 8 (version 8.309, Monash 2013 tune) and Epos 4 (version 4.0.0) Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- The universal crossing point of the ratios of normalized pT spectra to the inclusive spectrum occurs at approximately 0.6 GeV/c, observed at all four collision energies (0.9, 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV) and across all multiplicity classes.
- The inclusive charged-particle pseudorapidity densities used are: 4.20 +/- 0.03 at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV (|eta| < 0.5), 5.91 +/- 0.45 at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV (|eta| < 0.8), 5.96 +/- 0.23 at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV (|eta| < 0.5), and 7.60 +/- 0.50 at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV (|eta| < 0.8).
- The soft region is defined as 0.15 GeV/c <= pT <= 0.6 GeV/c; the mixed region as 0.6 GeV/c < pT <= 4.0 GeV/c.
- The maximum pT range studied is capped at 4.0 GeV/c, set by the highest pT bin available at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV.
- Pythia 8 simulations used approximately 50 million inelastic events at each sqrt(s), with diffractive components representing 31% of the total cross-section at 0.9 TeV and 27% at 13 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of whether collective effects analogous to quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions exist in small systems such as pp collisions, following studies by ALICE (2010-2020), CMS (2016), and ATLAS (2018). The observation of a universal crossing point at 0.6 GeV/c and the invariance of mean pT and variance in the soft region is consistent with earlier findings by the CDF collaboration (2001) at sqrt(s) = 630 GeV and 1.8 TeV. The results challenge conventional interpretations that extend collective flow analyses to pT ranges above 0.6 GeV/c, and the authors advocate for using higher-order statistical moments (variance, skewness) to discriminate between models such as Pythia 8 and Epos 4, which produce the same mean values despite different underlying assumptions (perturbative QCD with string fragmentation versus collective effects with hydrodynamics).
