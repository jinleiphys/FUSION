# Study of the neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb in mean field models

> We study whether the neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ of ${}^{208}$Pb originates from the bulk or from the surface of the neutron and proton density distributions in mean field models. We find that the size of the bulk contribution to $Δr_{np}$ of ${}^{208}$Pb strongly depends on the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy, while the surface contribution does not. We note that most mean field models predict a neutron density for ${}^{208}$Pb between the halo and skin type limits. We investigate the dependence of parity- violating electron scattering at the kinematics of the PREX experiment on the shape of the nucleon densities predicted by the mean field models for ${}^{208}$Pb. We find an approximate formula for the parity-violating asymmetry in terms of the central radius and the surface diffuseness of the nucleon densities of ${}^{208}$Pb in these models.

## Key claim

The known linear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and the density slope of the nuclear symmetry energy arises specifically from the *bulk* contribution to the skin, while the surface contribution remains nearly constant across mean-field models; this means that constraining the neutron radius (not just the rms radius) could be as useful for determining the symmetry energy slope.

## Method

The authors decomposed the neutron skin thickness Δrₙₚ of ²⁰⁸Pb into a bulk part (Δrₙₚᵇᵘˡᵏ, depending only on the equivalent sharp radii Rₙ and Rₚ) and a surface part (Δrₙₚˢᵘʳᶠ, depending on the surface diffuseness parameters bₙ and bₚ) using standard definitions from nuclear density distributions. They computed these quantities for a large set of relativistic (RMF) and nonrelativistic (NRMF) mean-field models, then correlated the bulk and surface contributions with the symmetry energy slope L. For parity-violating electron scattering, they performed exact Dirac phase-shift analysis (DWBA) at the PREX kinematics (1 GeV electrons, 5° scattering angle) to compute the asymmetry A_LR and parametrized it in terms of the central radii and surface diffuseness of the nucleon densities.

## Key numbers

- The central radii of neutrons in ²⁰⁸Pb vary within Cₙ ≈ 6.7–6.85 fm in NRMF models and 6.8–7.0 fm in RMF models; for protons, Cₚ ≈ 6.65–6.7 fm (NRMF) and 6.7–6.77 fm (RMF).
- The surface diffuseness parameters for neutrons are aₙ ≈ 0.53–0.55 fm (NRMF) and 0.55–0.59 fm (RMF); for protons, aₚ ≈ 0.43–0.47 fm.
- The parity-violating asymmetry A_LR at PREX kinematics is parametrized as 10⁷ A_LR ≈ α + β(Cₙ−Cₚ) + γ(aₙ−aₚ) with α = 7.33, β = −2.45 fm⁻¹, and γ = −3.62 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Warda (2010) and Centelles (2010) by explicitly separating bulk and surface contributions to the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and showing that the correlation with the symmetry energy slope L is driven by the bulk part. It also provides a simple parametrization of the parity-violating asymmetry measured by the PREX experiment at JLab, enabling future extraction of both the central radius and surface diffuseness of the neutron distribution from a single asymmetry measurement. The results contradict the possibility of a pure skin-type neutron distribution (where aₙ−aₚ = 0) in mean-field models, as aₙ−aₚ is always non-vanishing.
