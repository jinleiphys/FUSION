# "Onishi" formulas

> The term "Onishi" formula refers to a family of related formulas for the overlap amplitude of two Bogolyubov quasi-fermion vacua. As a common feature, these formulas display a square root, which gives rise to an apparent sign ambiguity. For some members of the family, this sign ambiguity is real and unavoidable while in some other cases it is not. The relation between the different Onishi formulas is discussed.

# Onishi formulas

## Key claim

The sign ambiguity in the "Onishi" formula for the overlap amplitude of two Bogolyubov quasi-fermion vacua is real and unavoidable for the general formula of Ring and Schuck (1980), because it stems from the double-valued spin representation of the orthogonal group of Bogolyubov transformations; however, the original formula of Onishi and Yoshida (1966) has no sign ambiguity because it applies only when both vacua have non-zero overlap with the physical vacuum, which renders their phases well defined.

## Method

The paper analyzes the isomorphism between the group of Bogolyubov transformations (linear transformations preserving the fermion anticommutator) and the orthogonal group O(2d), then identifies the Fock space representation of this group as the spin representation of Brauer and Weyl (1935). Using the Block-Messiah decomposition (Bloch 1962) for unitary, proper Bogolyubov transformations with even-dimensional single-fermion state spaces, the author derives the overlap amplitude formula called the "Onishi" formula in Ring and Schuck (1980) and shows its sign ambiguity arises from the double-valuedness of the spin representation. The original Onishi and Yoshida formula is derived via the Thouless expansion (Thouless 1960), and its relation to Robledo's 2009 Pfaffian formula is established through properties of skew symmetric matrices and Cayley's Pfaffian identity (Cayley 1849).

## Key numbers

- The single-fermion state space has finite dimension \(d\); the Clifford algebra generated by field operators is Cl(2d).
- The group of Bogolyubov transformations is isomorphic to O(2d), with unitary Bogolyubov transformations forming the subgroup O(2d, ℝ) of orthogonal transformations in \(2d\) real dimensions.
- For proper Bogolyubov transformations (det \(g = 1\)), the subgroup is SO(2d) or SO(2d, ℝ); improper transformations (det \(g = -1\)) change number parity in the Fock space representation.
- In the Block-Messiah decomposition, the overlap amplitude \(\langle | \bar g | \rangle = \sqrt{|A^*|}\) where \(A\) is the upper-left \(d \times d\) block of the coordinate representation matrix \(G\); the square root has an indefinite sign.
- For the Onishi and Yoshida formula, \(\langle \tilde g_1 | \tilde g_2 \rangle = \exp \tfrac12 \tr \log (1 + F_1^\dagger F_2) = \sqrt{|1 + F_1^\dagger F_2|}\), where the square root is defined by continuity with \(\sqrt{1} = 1\).
- The Robledo formula gives \(\langle \tilde g_1 | \tilde g_2 \rangle = (-1)^{d(d-1)/2} \, \pf \begin{pmatrix} F_1^\dagger & 1 \\ -1 & F_2 \end{pmatrix}\), where \(\pf\) denotes the Pfaffian.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the "sign problem of the Onishi formula" first discussed by Neergård and Wüst (1983) and referenced in subsequent literature (Schmidt 2004, Oi 2005, Robledo 2009, and others). The paper clarifies the relationship between the general formula from Ring and Schuck (1980) and the original formula of Onishi and Yoshida (1966), showing that the former has an unavoidable sign ambiguity while the latter does not. It also demonstrates how Robledo's 2009 Pfaffian-based formula follows directly from the Onishi and Yoshida formula via Cayley's Pfaffian identity, providing a unified understanding of these related expressions. The results enable unambiguous calculation of overlap amplitudes in generator coordinate method applications and projection of quasi-fermion vacua onto conserved quantum number eigenspaces in nuclear structure calculations.
