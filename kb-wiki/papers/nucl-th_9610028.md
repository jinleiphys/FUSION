# Dirac-Foldy term and the electromagnetic polarizability of the neutron

> We reconsider the Dirac-Foldy contribution $μ^2/m$ to the neutron electric polarizability. Using a Dirac equation approach to neutron-nucleus scattering, we review the definitions of Compton continuum ($\barα$), classical static ($α^n_E$), and Schrödinger ($α_{Sch}$) polarizabilities and discuss in some detail their relationship. The latter $α_{Sch}$ is the value of the neutron electric polarizability as obtained from an analysis using the Schrödinger equation. We find in particular $α_{Sch} = \barα - μ^2/m$ , where $μ$ is the magnitude of the magnetic moment of a neutron of mass $m$. However, we argue that the static polarizability $α^n_E$ is correctly defined in the rest frame of the particle, leading to the conclusion that twice the Dirac-Foldy contribution should be added to $α_{Sch}$ to obtain the static polarizability $α^n_E$.

# Dirac-Foldy term and the electromagnetic polarizability of the neutron

## Key claim

The static electric polarizability of the neutron, defined in the neutron's rest frame, differs from the Schrödinger polarizability extracted from neutron-atom scattering experiments by twice the Dirac-Foldy contribution (2μ²/m). The authors argue that the correct static polarizability αⁿ_E is obtained by adding 2μ²/m to the Schrödinger polarizability α_Sch, contrary to previous assumptions that equated these quantities.

## Method

The authors use a Dirac equation approach to neutron-nucleus scattering, starting from a relativistic Hamiltonian that includes a point neutral Dirac particle with an anomalous magnetic moment and a separate continuum polarizability term. They perform a nonrelativistic reduction of this Hamiltonian and examine the coefficient of the E² term in two different frames: the p=0 frame and the v=0 frame (where v is the particle velocity operator). The velocity operator is defined as ∂H/∂p, and the authors argue that the rest frame of the neutron is defined by v=0, not p=0, citing the Aharonov-Casher effect as experimental confirmation. They then compare their result with the Compton scattering analysis of Bernabéu and Tarrach (1986) and with the Schrödinger equation analyses used in neutron-atom scattering experiments by Schmiedmayer et al. (1991) and Koester et al. (1988).

## Key numbers

- The difference between the Schrödinger polarizability and the static polarizability is |α_Sch - αⁿ_E| = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- α_Sch from Schmiedmayer et al. (1991) is 12 ± 1.5 ± 2.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- α_Sch from Koester et al. (1988) is 0.0 ± 5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- The correction term 2μ²/m has magnitude 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, which is about 10% of the Schmiedmayer et al. result

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bernabéu and Tarrach (1986), who connected Compton scattering polarizabilities to the classical static polarizability via dispersion relations, and the Dirac equation treatment of a neutral particle with anomalous magnetic moment by Foldy (1958). The paper clarifies a discrepancy between the Compton-defined polarizability (used in proton measurements and chiral perturbation theory calculations) and the Schrödinger polarizability extracted from neutron-atom scattering experiments by Schmiedmayer et al. (1991) and Koester et al. (1988). The result enables a consistent comparison between neutron and proton electric polarizabilities, which is important for understanding nucleon substructure. The authors note that ongoing experiments by Kopecky and Leeb with expected errors smaller than the correction term may resolve the existing experimental discrepancy between the two neutron-atom scattering results.
