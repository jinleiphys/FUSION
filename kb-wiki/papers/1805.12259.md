# First-forbidden transitions in the reactor anomaly

> We study the dominant forbidden transitions in the antineutrino spectra of the fission actinides from 4 MeV onward using the nuclear shell model. Through explicit calculation of the shape factor, taking into account Coulomb corrections, we show the expected changes on cumulative electron and antineutrino spectra. Compared to the usual allowed approximation this results in a minor decrease of electron spectra from 4 MeV and onward, whereas an increase of several percent is observed in antineutrino spectra. We show that, despite their limited number, forbidden transitions dominate the spectral flux for most of the experimentally accessible range. Based on the shell model calculations we attempt a parametrization of forbidden transitions and propose a spectral correction for all forbidden transitions. We enforce correspondence with the ILL dataset using a summation+conversion approach. When compared against modern reactor neutrino experiments, the resultant spectral change is observed to be of comparable magnitude and shape as the reported spectral shoulder, drastically decreasing the statistical significance of the latter.

## Key claim

First-forbidden beta decays, previously assumed negligible in reactor antineutrino spectra, produce spectral corrections of comparable magnitude and shape to the observed 4-6 MeV "shoulder" in modern reactor neutrino experiments, drastically reducing its statistical significance when calculated using the nuclear shell model with full Coulomb corrections.

## Method

The authors used the nuclear shell model (code NUSHELLX@MSU) to calculate the shape factors of 29 dominant forbidden transitions with endpoints above 4 MeV, selected from the compilation of Sonzogni (2015). They employed the Behrens-Bühring formalism for spectral shape, including finite-size and Coulomb corrections, and enforced correspondence with the ILL experimental dataset using a summation-plus-conversion approach. A Monte Carlo parametrization of non-unique first-forbidden transitions was developed from the calculated shape factors, with uncertainties propagated from the spread in fit parameters and from axial-vector coupling constant quenching.

## Key numbers

* Forbidden transitions constitute more than 60% of the total electron flux in the 4-6 MeV shoulder region for 235U, and exceed 65% at 6 MeV for all fission actinides.
* The cumulative antineutrino spectrum shows an increase of up to 5% in the 5-6 MeV region relative to the allowed approximation.
* Electron spectra see a modest 2% decrease in the 4 to 8 MeV region compared to the allowed approximation with weak magnetism correction.
* The selected 29 transitions correspond to at least 50% of the cumulative electron flux in the 2-8 MeV region of interest.
* The agreement between calculated and reference electron spectra is better than 1% up to 7 MeV after the conversion procedure.

## Context

This work extends earlier parametrized treatments of forbidden transitions by Hayes (2014) and microscopic treatments by Fang (2015), and directly addresses the spectral shoulder reported by Daya Bay (An 2016), RENO (Seo 2018), and Double Chooz (Abe 2014). Previous arguments by Hayes (2015) that forbidden influence is negligible, based on pseudoscalar transitions like 92Rb and 96Y, are shown to be insufficient because many important pseudoscalar transitions are not pure 0- to 0+ decays and receive large higher-order contributions. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for reactor antineutrino spectra and reduce the motivation for sterile neutrino explanations of the reactor anomaly.
