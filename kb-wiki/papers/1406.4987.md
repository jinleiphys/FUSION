# Chiral symmetry breaking in QCD Lite

> A distinctive feature of the presence of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD is the condensation of low modes of the Dirac operator near the origin. The rate of condensation must be equal to the slope of (Mpi^2 Fpi^2)/2 with respect to the quark mass m in the chiral limit, where Mpi and Fpi are the mass and the decay constant of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons. We compute the spectral density of the (Hermitian) Dirac operator, the quark mass, the pseudoscalar meson mass and decay constant by numerical simulations of lattice QCD with two light degenerate Wilson quarks. We use CLS lattices at three values of the lattice spacing in the range 0.05-0.08 fm, and for several quark masses corresponding to pseudoscalar mesons masses down to 190 MeV. Thanks to this coverage of parameters space, we can extrapolate all quantities to the chiral and continuum limits with confidence. The results show that the low quark modes do condense in the continuum as expected by the Banks-Casher mechanism, and the rate of condensation agrees with the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation. For the renormalisation-group-invariant ratios we obtain [Σ^RGI]^(1/3)/F =2.77(2)(4) and Lambda^MSbar/F = 3.6(2), which correspond to [Σ^\MSbar(2 GeV)]^(1/3) =263(3)(4) MeV and F=85.8(7)(20) MeV if FK is used to set the scale by supplementing the theory with a quenched strange quark.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the low modes of the Dirac operator do condense near the origin in the continuum limit of two-flavor QCD, and that the rate of this condensation agrees with the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation, but only after extrapolating lattice data to both the chiral and continuum limits using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as the functional form for the extrapolation.

## Method

The authors performed numerical lattice QCD simulations with two light degenerate Wilson quarks using the O(a)-improved Wilson action on CLS ensembles at three lattice spacings (0.048, 0.065, and 0.075 fm) and several quark masses corresponding to pion masses down to 190 MeV. They computed the mode number of the Dirac operator, from which they derived the effective spectral density, and also computed two-point functions to extract the pseudoscalar meson mass and decay constant. All quantities were extrapolated to the chiral and continuum limits using functional forms dictated by next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory and Symanzik effective theory.

## Key numbers

*   Lattice spacings used: 0.075 fm, 0.065 fm, and 0.048 fm, as measured from the kaon decay constant F_K.
*   Quark masses ranged from 6 to 40 MeV, with pseudoscalar meson masses down to 190 MeV.
*   Renormalisation-group-invariant ratio: [Sigma^RGI]^(1/3)/F = 2.77(2)(4).
*   Ratio of the MSbar Lambda parameter to F: Lambda^MSbar/F = 3.6(2).
*   Chiral condensate in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV: [Sigma^MSbar(2 GeV)]^(1/3) = 263(3)(4) MeV.
*   Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: F = 85.8(7)(20) MeV.
*   Chiral condensate from the spectral density extrapolation: Sigma^(1/3) = 261(6)(8) MeV.

## Context

This work provides a first-principles verification of the Banks-Casher mechanism in two-flavor QCD, extending earlier exploratory studies of the spectral density (Giusti 2008, Fukaya 2010, Cichy 2013) by achieving a precise continuum and chiral extrapolation. The results confirm the GMOR relation and yield precise values for the leading-order low-energy constants of two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, which can serve as inputs for phenomenological analyses of chiral dynamics (Leutwyler 2008) and for studies of theories at non-zero temperature or models of electroweak symmetry breaking (Patella 2012).
