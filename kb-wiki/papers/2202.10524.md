# Standard Model prediction for paramagnetic EDMs

> Standard Model $CP$ violation associated with the phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark mixing matrix is known to give small answers for the EDM observables. Moreover, predictions for the EDMs of neutrons and diamagnetic atoms suffer from considerable uncertainties. We point out that the $CP$-violating observables associated with the electron spin (paramagnetic EDMs) are dominated by the combination of the electroweak penguin diagrams and $ΔI =1/2$ weak transitions in the baryon sector, and are calculable within chiral perturbation theory. The predicted size of the semileptonic operator $C_S$ is $7\times 10^{-16}$ which corresponds to the equivalent electron EDM $d_e^{\rm eq} = 1.0 \times 10^{-35}e\,{\rm cm}$. While still far from the current observational limits, this result is three orders of magnitude larger than previously believed.

## Key claim

The Standard Model CP-violating phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) quark mixing matrix induces paramagnetic electric dipole moments (EDMs) via a combination of electroweak penguin diagrams and ΔI=1/2 weak transitions in the baryon sector, yielding a result three orders of magnitude larger than previously believed, though still far from current observational limits.

## Method

The authors calculate the semileptonic operator C_S (which parametrizes CP-odd electron-nucleon interactions) using chiral perturbation theory at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO). The dominant contribution arises from electroweak penguin diagrams at EW^3 order, where the CP-odd, P-even K_S meson generated by the electroweak penguin couples to nucleons via the ΔS=±1 weak transition, and the resulting K_S exchange is integrated out. The NLO corrections from baryon pole diagrams are computed using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- C_S(LO+NLO) = 6.9 × 10^{-16}
- Equivalent electron EDM d_e^{eq} = 1.0 × 10^{-35} e cm
- Rephasing invariant CKM combination J = Im(V_ts^* V_td V_ud^* V_us) = 3.1 × 10^{-5} (with ~6% uncertainty)
- Current experimental limit: |d_e^{equiv}| < 1.1 × 10^{-29} e cm (from ThO molecule, Andreev 2018)
- NLO corrections give 30% correction for the proton and 40% for the neutron
- Maximum new physics scale probed by paramagnetic EDMs: Λ_NP^{max} ~ 5 × 10^7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimate by Pospelov (2013) which gave d_e^{eq} ~ 10^{-38} e cm via a two-photon exchange (EW^2 EM^2) mechanism, and supersedes the direct electron EDM estimate by Yamaguchi (2020) of ~6 × 10^{-40} e cm. The result is in clear distinction from neutron EDM predictions from δ_KM (Seng 2014, few × 10^{-32} e cm with wide uncertainty), as the C_S operator can be predicted rather precisely using chiral perturbation theory and can be further improved with lattice QCD methods. The calculation establishes a robust Standard Model benchmark for future paramagnetic EDM experiments, with some proposals (Vutha 2017) envisioning statistical sensitivity reaching d_e ~ O(10^{-35}–10^{-37}) e cm.
