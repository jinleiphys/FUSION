# Existence of the critical endpoint in the vector meson extended linear sigma model

> The chiral phase transition of the strongly interacting matter is investigated at nonzero temperature and baryon chemical potential mu_B within an extended (2+1) flavor Polyakov constituent quark-meson model which incorporates the effect of the vector and axial vector mesons. The effect of the fermionic vacuum and thermal fluctuations computed from the grand potential of the model is taken into account in the curvature masses of the scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. The parameters of the model are determined by comparing masses and tree-level decay widths with experimental values in a chi^2-minimization procedure which selects between various possible assignments of scalar nonet states to physical particles. We examine the restoration of the chiral symmetry by monitoring the temperature evolution of condensates and the chiral partners' masses and of the mixing angles for the pseudoscalar eta-eta' and the corresponding scalar complex. We calculate the pressure and various thermodynamical observables derived from it and compare them to the continuum extrapolated lattice results of the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration. We study the T-mu_B phase diagram of the model and find that a critical end point exists for parameters of the model, which give acceptable values of chi^2.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a critical endpoint (CEP) exists in the T-mu_B phase diagram of the (2+1) flavor Polyakov constituent quark-meson model extended with vector and axial vector mesons, but only for parameter sets that yield acceptable chi-squared values in the model parametrization. The existence of the CEP is conditional on the specific choice of scalar meson mass assignments and the inclusion of fermionic vacuum fluctuations.

## Method
The authors constructed a (2+1) flavor Polyakov quark-meson model incorporating vector and axial vector mesons, with constituent quarks propagating on a constant gluon background in the temporal direction. They computed the grand potential in a hybrid approximation that includes fermionic vacuum and thermal fluctuations while treating mesons at mean-field level, with renormalization of the fermionic vacuum contribution. Model parameters were determined through a chi-squared minimization procedure comparing tree-level masses and decay widths to experimental values, selecting between different assignments of scalar nonet states to physical particles.

## Key numbers
- The Polyakov loop potential uses T_0 = 182 MeV for m_s = 95 MeV, or T_0 = 187 MeV for m_s = 150 MeV
- The improved gluonic Polyakov loop potential uses a mapping with t_YM(t_glue) approximately 0.57 times t_glue, with T_c^YM = 270 MeV and T_c^glue in the range [180, 270] MeV
- The renormalized fermionic vacuum contribution is given by Omega_v = -(3/(8 pi^2)) sum_f m_f^4 ln(m_f/M_0), where M_0 is the renormalization scale
- The constituent quark masses are m_u,d = (g_F/2) phi_N and m_s = (g_F/sqrt(2)) phi_S

## Context
This work extends the extended linear sigma model (ELsigmaM) developed in 2013 with U(3)_L x U(3)_R global symmetry to finite temperature and baryon chemical potential by incorporating the Polyakov loop and fermionic vacuum fluctuations. It builds upon prior studies of the Polyakov quark-meson (PQM) model for N_f=2 (Skokov 2010, Gupta 2011) and N_f=2+1 (Chatterjee 2011, Schaefer 2011), where inclusion of fermionic vacuum fluctuations was shown to affect the order of the phase transition. The results enable comparison with continuum extrapolated lattice results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration and provide predictions for the location of the critical endpoint that could guide future experimental searches at facilities exploring high baryon density.
