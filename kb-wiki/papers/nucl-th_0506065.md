# Low-momentum Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions

> We present a first exploratory study for hyperon-nucleon interactions using renormalization group techniques. The effective two-body low-momentum potential V_low-k is obtained by integrating out the high-momentum components from realistic Nijmegen YN potentials. A T-matrix equivalence approach is employed, so that the low-energy phase shifts are reproduced by V_low-k up to a momentum scale Lambda ~ 500 MeV. Although the various bare Nijmegen models differ somewhat from each other, the corresponding V_low-k interactions show convergence in some channels, suggesting a possible unique YN interaction at low momenta.

## Key claim

This exploratory study applies renormalization-group decimation to hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions for the first time, but unlike the NN case where a unique low-momentum potential emerges, the six Nijmegen YN models (NSC97a–f) do **not** collapse to a single unique V_low-k potential, although convergence is seen in some channels.

## Method

The authors start from six realistic Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange YN potentials (NSC97a–f) and integrate out high-momentum components using a T-matrix equivalence approach with a momentum cutoff Λ ≈ 500 MeV. They employ the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki (ALS) iteration method to solve the renormalization-group flow equation, obtaining a phase-shift-equivalent low-momentum potential V_low-k for each bare model. For the I=1/2 channel, which couples ΛN and ΣN states with different masses, a modified ALS iteration is required to handle the coupled-channel equations and threshold effects.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Λ ≈ 500 MeV (≈ 2.5 fm⁻¹) is used for the model-space momentum scale.
- The ALS iteration uses 64 grid points in the P-space (Λ_P = 500 MeV) and 64 grid points in the Q-space (Λ_Q = 10 GeV); grid points were varied between 32 and 70, and Λ_Q varied by ±2 GeV to verify insensitivity.
- The bare Nijmegen fits describe the known YN cross-section data with χ²/N ≈ 0.55, based on only 35 data points.
- For the I=3/2 channel, the V_low-k potentials become more attractive than the bare potentials, as shown for the ¹S₀, ³S₁, and ¹P₁ partial waves.
- For the I=1/2 ΛN → ΛN channel, the V_low-k potentials are again more attractive, with the largest shift seen for NSC97f and the smallest for NSC97a in the ¹S₀ partial wave.
- The Σ threshold in the ΛN → ΛN ³S₁ channel appears for lab momenta above 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the V_low-k renormalization-group approach, previously applied successfully to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Bogner et al., 2003; Schwenk et al., 2002), into the hyperon-nucleon sector for the first time. The NN case yields a unique low-momentum potential independent of the high-precision input model, driven by the wealth of phase-shift data; here the sparse YN data (only 35 points) prevent such uniqueness. The resulting softer V_low-k potentials are intended as input for future ab-initio calculations of hypernuclei and hyperonic matter, and the authors note that calculations with the revised Jülich YN potential (Jülich group, 2001) are in preparation.
