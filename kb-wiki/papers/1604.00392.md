# An Exponential Regulator for Rapidity Divergences

> Finding an efficient and compelling regularization of soft and collinear degrees of freedom at the same invariant mass scale, but separated in rapidity is a persistent problem in high-energy factorization. In the course of a calculation, one encounters divergences unregulated by dimensional regularization, often called rapidity divergences. Once regulated, a general framework exists for their renormalization, the rapidity renormalization group (RRG), leading to fully resummed calculations of transverse momentum (to the jet axis) sensitive quantities. We examine how this regularization can be implemented via a multi-differential factorization of the soft-collinear phase-space, leading to an (in principle) alternative non-perturbative regularization of rapidity divergences. As an example, we examine the fully-differential factorization of a color singlet's momentum spectrum in a hadron-hadron collision at threshold. We show how this factorization acts as a mother theory to both traditional threshold and transverse momentum resummation, recovering the classical results for both resummations. Examining the refactorization of the transverse momentum beam functions in the threshold region, we show that one can directly calculate the rapidity renormalized function, while shedding light on the structure of joint resummation. Finally, we show how using modern bootstrap techniques, the transverse momentum spectrum is determined by an expansion about the threshold factorization, leading to a viable higher loop scheme for calculating the relevant anomalous dimensions for the transverse momentum spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an "exponential regulator" for rapidity divergences in transverse-momentum-sensitive QCD observables, which is implemented via a multi-differential factorization of the soft-collinear phase-space and acts as a gauge-invariant cutoff on rapidity integrals; the authors claim this regulator provides an alternative non-perturbative regularization that can be related to threshold factorization, but they hedge that the approach is demonstrated primarily for the color-singlet transverse momentum spectrum in hadron-hadron collisions at threshold, with broader applicability suggested rather than proven.

## Method

The authors exploit the consistency between different factorization formulas for the same cross-section in distinct kinematic limits, specifically connecting the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function to the threshold soft function through an exponential deformation of the light-cone coordinates: they define a regulated soft function \(S(\vec{b}_\perp, \tau) = S(i b_0 \tau/2, i b_0 \tau/2, \vec{b}_\perp)\) where \(\tau = 1/\nu\) acts as a rapidity regulator, and show that the \(\vec{b}_\perp = 0\) limit of this function reduces to the threshold soft function \(S_{\rm thr}(\tau)\). Using this connection, they calculate the rapidity anomalous dimension by extracting it from the consistency relation between the fully-differential factorization and the standard TMD factorization, and demonstrate a bootstrap technique using harmonic polylogarithms to determine the soft function at higher loops.

## Key numbers

* The rapidity anomalous dimension \(\gamma_R\) satisfies \(\mu^2 \frac{d}{d\mu^2} \gamma_R(\mu |\vec{b}_\perp|/b_0; \alpha_s(\mu)) = -\Gamma_{\rm cusp}(\alpha_s(\mu))\), where \(b_0 = 2e^{-\gamma_E}\) and \(\Gamma_{\rm cusp}\) is the cusp anomalous dimension.
* The all-orders form of the rapidity anomalous dimension is given by \(\gamma_R(\mu |\vec{b}_\perp|/b_0; \alpha_s(\mu)) = \int_{\mu^2}^{b_0^2/\vec{b}_\perp^2} \frac{d\mu'^2}{\mu'^2} \Gamma_{\rm cusp}(\alpha_s(\mu')) + \gamma_r(\alpha_s(b_0/|\vec{b}_\perp|))\).
* The soft function expansion uses \(\ln S(b^+, b^-, \vec{b}_\perp; \mu) = \sum_{i=0}^\infty (\alpha_s(\mu)/(4\pi))^i S_i(b^+, b^-, \vec{b}_\perp; \mu)\), assuming non-Abelian exponentiation.
* The cusp anomalous dimension expansion is \(\Gamma_{\rm cusp}(\alpha_s(\mu)) = \sum_{i=0}^\infty (\alpha_s(\mu)/(4\pi))^{i+1} \gcusp_i\), and the non-cusp anomalous dimensions \(\gamma_{r,h,s}\) have analogous expansions.

## Context

This work extends the rapidity renormalization group (RRG) framework of Chiu (2011, 2012) and the Collins-Soper formalism of Collins (1981, 2011) by providing a new regulator that connects transverse-momentum resummation to threshold resummation, building on the threshold factorization techniques of Anastasiou (2013), Li (2013), and Duhr (2013). The exponential regulator enables the use of modern bootstrap methods and existing three-loop threshold calculations to determine the rapidity anomalous dimension, which the authors state will be presented in full for three loops in a companion paper; this enables higher-loop calculations of the transverse momentum spectrum for Drell-Yan and Higgs production at colliders.
