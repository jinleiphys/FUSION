# Exact Self-Dual Skyrmions

> We introduce a Skyrme type model with the target space being the 3-sphere S^3 and with an action possessing, as usual, quadratic and quartic terms in field derivatives. The novel character of the model is that the strength of the couplings of those two terms are allowed to depend upon the space-time coordinates. The model should therefore be interpreted as an effective theory, such that those couplings correspond in fact to low energy expectation values of fields belonging to a more fundamental theory at high energies. The theory possesses a self-dual sector that saturates the Bogomolny bound leading to an energy depending linearly on the topological charge. The self-duality equations are conformally invariant in three space dimensions leading to a toroidal ansatz and exact self-dual Skyrmion solutions. Those solutions are labelled by two integers and, despite their toroidal character, the energy density is spherically symmetric when those integers are equal and oblate or prolate otherwise.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a Skyrme-type model with space-time-dependent coupling constants, which allows the self-duality equations to become conformally invariant in three dimensions and yields exact, regular Skyrmion solutions on \(\mathbb{R}^3\), circumventing the Chandrasekhar argument that previously prevented finite-energy solutions of the force-free equation on \(\mathbb{R}^3\).

## Method
The authors construct a model with target space \(S^3\) and an action containing quadratic and quartic terms in field derivatives, whose coupling strengths \(m(x^\rho)\) and \(e(x^\rho)\) are allowed to depend on space-time coordinates. They impose the specific form \(m(\vec{r}) = m_0 f(\vec{r})\) and \(e(\vec{r}) = e_0 f(\vec{r})\) to make the self-duality equations imply the static Euler-Lagrange equations. Using a toroidal ansatz invariant under a diagonal subgroup of two commuting \(U(1)\) symmetries (internal and external), they solve the self-duality equations exactly to obtain explicit field configurations labeled by two integers \(n\) and \(m\).

## Key numbers
- The topological charge of the solutions is \(Q = -m n\).
- The total energy saturates the Bogomolny bound: \(E = 4\pi^2 \frac{m_0}{e_0} |m n|\).
- The energy density for the spherically symmetric case (\(m^2 = n^2\)) is \(\mathcal{E} = \frac{16 m_0}{e_0 a^3} \frac{n^2}{((r/a)^2+1)^3}\).
- The energy density decays as \(1/r^6\) as \(r \to \infty\).
- The function \(f\) at the origin is \(f(r\to 0) = \frac{2}{\sqrt{m_0 e_0 a}} \sqrt{\left| \frac{m}{n} \right|}\).

## Context
This work extends the self-dual Skyrme-type model of Ferreira (2013), which only supported regular solutions on the three-sphere \(S^3\) due to the Chandrasekhar obstruction on \(\mathbb{R}^3\). By introducing space-time-dependent couplings, the authors obtain exact, regular Skyrmion solutions on \(\mathbb{R}^3\) for the first time. The solutions are analogous to the exact Hopfions of the \(S^2\) model (hopfion99) but now with target space \(S^3\) and a self-dual sector. The results enable further study of conformal symmetry breaking, rotational modes, and semi-classical quantization of these self-dual Skyrmions.
