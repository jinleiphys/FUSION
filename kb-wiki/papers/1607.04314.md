# Synchrotron contribution to photon emission from quark-gluon plasma

> We study the influence of the magnetic field on the photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma created in $AA$ collisions. We find that even for very optimistic assumption on the magnitude of the magnetic field for noncentral $AA$ collisions the effect of magnetic field is very small.

## Key claim
Even under the most optimistic assumption for the magnetic field strength in noncentral heavy-ion collisions (eB = m_π²), the synchrotron contribution to photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma is very small; for more realistic field values (eB ~ 0.1 m_π²) the effect is practically negligible, contradicting earlier claims by Tuchin (2014) that the synchrotron mechanism could explain the direct photon puzzle.

## Method
The author develops a light-cone path integral (LCPI) formalism that treats on an equal footing the effects of multiple scattering of quarks in the thermal bath and the curvature of quark trajectories due to the collective magnetic field. The formalism is applied to both the bremsstrahlung process (q → γ q) and the annihilation process (q\bar{q} → γ), using an oscillator approximation for the dipole cross section and standard thermal quasiparticle masses for quarks and photons. Numerical calculations are performed for temperatures T = 250 MeV and T = 500 MeV, with the magnetic field set to eB = m_π².

## Key numbers
- At T = 250 MeV and eB = m_π², the synchrotron contribution to the photon emission rate from both bremsstrahlung and annihilation processes lies considerably below the contribution from leading-order 2→2 processes (Compton and annihilation).
- At T = 500 MeV and eB = m_π², the synchrotron contribution remains practically negligible compared to the leading-order contribution.
- For the more realistic field value eB ~ 0.1 m_π² (typical for Au+Au collisions at √s = 0.2 TeV with impact parameter b ~ 6 fm and proper time τ ~ 0.2 fm), the synchrotron contribution is smaller by a factor of ~10² than at eB = m_π².
- The transport coefficient used is \hat{q} ≈ 0.2 GeV³ at T = 250 MeV, consistent with estimates from jet quenching data.

## Context
This work extends the LCPI formalism previously used by Zakharov (2007) to derive the photon emission rate from higher-order collinear processes (q → γ q and q\bar{q} → γ) obtained by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) using hard thermal loop resummation. The results contradict the qualitative analysis of Tuchin (2014), who found a large synchrotron contribution that could explain the direct photon puzzle. The present calculations show that multiple scattering strongly suppresses synchrotron radiation, and that the magnetic field effect cannot resolve the discrepancy between observed photon elliptic flow and theoretical expectations from thermal emission.
