# Thermodynamic properties and shear viscosity over entropy density ratio of nuclear fireball in a quantum-molecular dynamics model

> Thermodynamic and transport properties of nuclear fireball created in the central region of heavy-ion collisions below 400 MeV/nucleon are investigated within the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamic (IQMD) model. These properties including the density, temperature, chemical potential, entropy density ($s$) and shear viscosity ($η$), are calculated by a generalized hot Thomas Fermi formulism and a parameterized function, which was developed by Danielewicz. As the collision goes on, a transient minimal $η/s=5/4π-10/4π$ occurs in the largest compression stage. Besides, the relationship of $η/s$ to temperature ($T$) in the freeze-out stage displays a local minimum which is about 9-20 times $1/4π$ around $T$ = 8-12 MeV, which can be argued as indicative of a liquid gas phase transition. In addition, the influences of nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross section ($σ_{NN}$) and symmetry energy coefficient ($C_{s}$) are also discussed, and it is found that the results are sensitive to $σ_{NN}$ but not to $C_{s}$.

# Thermodynamic properties and shear viscosity over entropy density ratio of nuclear fireball in a quantum-molecular dynamics model

## Key claim
The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) of nuclear fireball formed in heavy-ion collisions below 400 MeV/nucleon exhibits a local minimum at freeze-out temperatures of 8-12 MeV, which the authors argue is indicative of a liquid-gas phase transition; however, this interpretation is hedged by noting that the extracted η/s values (9-20 times the KSS bound of 1/4π) are based on a parameterized formula for shear viscosity that assumes the system is largely equilibrated, a condition not fully achieved during the collision.

## Method
The authors simulated head-on Au+Au collisions using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state, then extracted thermodynamic properties (density, temperature, chemical potential, entropy density) from the simulated matter density and kinetic energy density in a central spherical volume (radius 5 fm) using the generalized hot Thomas-Fermi formalism. Shear viscosity was calculated from the Danielewicz parameterization as a function of density and temperature, scaled inversely by the nucleon-nucleon cross section factor Cσ, and the η/s ratio was studied as a function of time, temperature, and density for beam energies from 50 to 400 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Maximum nuclear matter density reached in central region: 1.5ρ₀ to 2.0ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Maximum kinetic energy density: 10 MeV fm⁻³ to 25 MeV fm⁻³ for the beam energies studied
- Transient minimal η/s at largest compression stage: 5/4π to 10/4π (approximately 0.4, about 5 times the KSS bound of 1/4π)
- Local minimum of η/s at freeze-out stage: 0.76 to 0.84 (about 9-10 times KSS bound) at temperatures of 8-12 MeV for freeze-out densities ρ/ρ₀ in [0.19,0.31]
- Range of η/s local minimum at freeze-out: 9-20 times 1/4π
- Phase transition temperature from intermediate mass fragment multiplicity: 7-10 MeV, slightly lower than the η/s minimum range of 8-12 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficients tested: 15, 25, and 35 MeV, with no significant effect on results
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section scaling factors tested: Cσ = 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5

## Context
This work extends the study of η/s as a probe of phase transitions from ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions (where η/s near the KSS bound indicates a perfect liquid in quark-gluon plasma, as studied by Demir (2009), Lacey (2007), and others) to intermediate energies below 400 MeV/nucleon, where nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is expected. The authors build on prior work by Danielewicz (1984, 2009) for the shear viscosity parameterization and by Faessler and collaborators (Khoa 1992, Puri 1992) for the hot Thomas-Fermi formalism. The results are consistent with the authors' previous calculations (Zhou 2012) and with the expectation from the pressure-density phase diagram that transition temperature increases with freeze-out density (Gupta, Ma). The findings enable future experimental proposals for direct probes of shear viscosity and entropy density in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions to constrain transport properties around the liquid-gas phase transition.
