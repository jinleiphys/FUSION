# Microscopic description of the ground state properties of recently reported new isotopes

> Microscopic investigations for the observed properties of the recently reported five unstable new isotopes are carried out. The ground state properties are calculated in the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework and the results reproduce the experiment well as expected. The α - decay lifetimes are calculated in the double folding model using WKB approximation which requires the relevant Q values of α - decay and the α - daughter potential. The latter is obtained by folding the effective M3Y nucleon nucleon potential with the RMF nucleon density distributions for the daughter nucleus and that of the α particle which is assumed to be of Gaussian shape. the corresponding decay half - lives obtained by using available phenomenological expression are also presented, discussed and compared. It is observed that the Q values calculated in the RMF framework , though reasonably agree with the experiment, are not accurate enough for the reliable WKB calculation of decay half- lives. We therefore, advocate that the use of accurate (e.g. experimental) Q values is crucial for the reliable description of the experimental α - decay half-lives.

# Microscopic description of the ground state properties of recently reported new isotopes

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) framework reproduces ground state properties of five newly observed neutron-deficient isotopes well, but the calculated Q-values for alpha decay, while reasonably agreeing with experiment, are not accurate enough for reliable WKB calculation of decay half-lives; the authors advocate that using experimental Q-values is crucial for obtaining a reliable description of experimental alpha-decay half-lives.

## Method

Ground state properties (binding energies, radii, deformation parameters, and Q-values) were calculated using the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework with the NL3* parameter set and, for comparison, the PC-PK1 relativistic energy density functional. Alpha-decay half-lives were computed using the WKB approximation with the alpha-daughter potential obtained from the double folding model, folding the effective M3Y nucleon-nucleon potential with RMF nucleon density distributions for the daughter nucleus and a Gaussian-shaped alpha particle density. For odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, a "tagging" prescription was used to assign fixed occupancy to the last odd nucleon level, preserving time reversal invariance for the remaining even-even core.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for the five new isotopes: $^{233}_{97}$Bk (1757.75 MeV with NL3*), $^{229}_{95}$Am (1737.42 MeV with NL3*), $^{225}_{93}$Np (1718.32 MeV with NL3*), $^{216}_{92}$U (1658.88 MeV with NL3*), $^{223}_{95}$Am (1693.68 MeV with NL3*)
- Average deviation of binding energies from experiment: approximately 0.6% for NL3* and 0.1% for PC-PK1
- Quadrupole deformation parameter beta for $^{233}_{97}$Bk: 0.218 (NL3*), indicating moderate deformation; most other nuclei have beta values close to zero (near spherical)
- Experimental Q-value for $^{233}_{97}$Bk: 7.90 MeV; NL3* calculated Q-value: 8.56 MeV; PC-PK1: 11.07 MeV; AB mass formula: 8.44 MeV
- Experimental Q-value for $^{216}_{92}$U: 8.49 MeV; NL3*: 6.31 MeV; PC-PK1: 6.10 MeV; AB: 8.79 MeV
- Experimental half-life of $^{233}_{97}$Bk: 21$^{+48}_{-17}$ seconds; WKB with experimental Q-values: 19.0 seconds; phenomenological expression: 22.58 seconds
- Experimental half-life of $^{216}_{92}$U: 3.8$^{+8.8}_{-3.2}\times10^{-3}$ seconds; WKB with experimental Q-values: 4.85$\times10^{-3}$ seconds; phenomenological expression: 2.68$\times10^{-3}$ seconds
- Calculated charge radii for $^{213}_{87}$Fr: 5.595 fm (NL3*), 5.601 fm (PC-PK1), experimental: 5.598 fm

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework, previously applied across the nuclear chart including superheavy elements (see references PGR.89, YKG.90, PRI.96, VRE.05, BHA.11), to five new neutron-deficient isotopes with Z>=92 recently observed in multi-nucleon transfer reactions of $^{48}$Ca + $^{248}$Cm at the velocity filter SHIP at GSI (Devaraja et al., 2015). The calculations use the NL3* parameter set (Lalazissis et al., 2009), an improved version of the widely used NL3 set (Lalazissis et al., 1997), and compare with PC-PK1 results from continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations (Xia et al., 2017) and the mass formula of Bhagwat (2014). The alpha-decay half-lives are compared with the phenomenological expression of Dasgupta-Schubert and Reyes (2007). The work enables future microscopic calculations of alpha-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient heavy nuclei, provided accurate experimental Q-values are used, and highlights the need for improved Q-value predictions from microscopic theories.
