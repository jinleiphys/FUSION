# EoS constraints from nuclear masses and radii in a meta-modeling approach

> The question of correlations among empirical equation of state (EoS) parameters constrained by nuclear observables is addressed in a fully empirical meta-modeling approach. A recently proposed meta-modeling for the nuclear EoS in nuclear matter, is augmented with a single finite size term to produce a minimal unified EoS functional able to describe the smooth part of the nuclear ground state properties. This meta-model can reproduce the predictions of a large variety of models, and interpolate continuously between them. The parameter space is sampled and filtered through the constraint of nuclear mass reproduction. We show that this simple analytical meta-modeling has a predictive power on masses, radii, and skins comparable to full HF or ETF calculations with realistic energy functionals. The covariance analysis on the posterior distribution shows that no physical correlation is present between the different EoS parameters. Concerning nuclear observables, a strong correlation between the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron skin is observed, in agreement with previous studies.

# EoS constraints from nuclear masses and radii in a meta-modeling approach

## Key claim

The authors show that a minimal meta-modeling of the nuclear equation of state, using only a Taylor expansion around saturation density plus a single isoscalar surface term, can reproduce nuclear masses, radii, and skins with predictive power comparable to full Hartree-Fock or Extended Thomas-Fermi calculations using realistic energy functionals. However, the covariance analysis reveals that no physical correlation exists between the different EoS parameters themselves, despite the strong correlation observed between the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron skin.

## Method

The authors augment a previously proposed meta-modeling for the nuclear EoS in homogeneous matter (Casali 2014) with a single isoscalar finite-size gradient term parameterized by $C_{fin}$, creating a minimal unified EoS functional for finite nuclei. They implement this functional in both an analytical Extended Thomas-Fermi mass model (using Fermi function density profiles with variationally determined diffuseness) and a full variational ETF approach, then sample the multi-dimensional parameter space with a flat prior distribution and filter through the constraint of reproducing experimental nuclear masses from the AME2012 mass table.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density reference value: $n_{sat} = 0.1540$ fm$^{-3}$ (analytical ETF reference set)
- Saturation energy reference value: $E_{sat} = -16.04$ MeV (analytical ETF reference set)
- Symmetry energy reference value: $E_{sym} = 33.43$ MeV (analytical ETF reference set)
- Slope of symmetry energy reference value: $L_{sym} = 77.92$ MeV (analytical ETF reference set)
- Isoscalar incompressibility reference value: $K_{sat} = 255.91$ MeV (analytical ETF reference set)
- Finite size parameter optimized value: $C_{fin} = 59$ MeV fm$^5$ (from minimization of average relative dispersion on symmetric nuclei)
- Systematic error on binding energies: approximately $\Delta E \approx 100 \times A$ keV, independent of neutron richness
- Variation interval for $C_{fin}$: $\pm 13$ MeV fm$^5$ (estimated from residuals within $\pm 0.5$ MeV per nucleon)
- Variation interval for $L_{sym}$: $\pm 30.84$ MeV (relative $\pm 39.58\%$)
- Variation interval for $K_{sym}$: $\pm 142.71$ MeV (relative $\pm 6516.44\%$)

## Context

This work extends the meta-modeling concept introduced by Casali (2014) for nuclear matter to finite nuclei by adding a single finite-size term, and addresses the methodological issue of whether correlations among EoS parameters extracted from data fits are physical or spuriously induced by the functional form assumed in phenomenological models such as Skyrme, Gogny, or RMF (Nazarewicz 2014, Ducoin 2011). The finding that the slope of symmetry energy strongly correlates with the neutron skin, virtually unaffected by uncertainty in other empirical quantities, confirms earlier studies (Brown, Typel, Piekarewicz) and highlights the importance of neutron skin measurements for constraining the nuclear EoS. The work enables future systematic investigations of radii and neutron skins in nuclei using a framework free from a-priori functional correlations.
