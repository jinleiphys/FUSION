# Hadron yields, the chemical freeze-out and the QCD phase diagram

> We present the status of the chemical freeze-out, determined from fits of hadron yields with the statistical hadronization (thermal) model, with focus on the data at the LHC. A description of the yields of hadrons containing light quarks as well as the application of the model for the production of the J/$ψ$ meson is presented. The implications for the QCD phase diagram are discussed.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization (thermal) model, with its minimal set of parameters (temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, and volume V), successfully describes hadron yields in heavy-ion collisions across a wide energy range, and at LHC energies the chemical freeze-out temperature coincides with the lattice QCD pseudo-critical temperature, indicating hadron formation from deconfined matter; however, the model's success relies on the assumption of rapid chemical freeze-out and does not require extensions such as strangeness suppression factors or repulsive interactions to describe current data.

## Method

The authors fit measured hadron multiplicities in central (0-10%) Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV using a grand-canonical statistical hadronization model based on the hadron resonance gas equation of state, with temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, and volume V as free parameters. All known hadrons (stable and decaying) are included, with contributions from charmed hadron decays to pions, kaons, and neutral kaons accounted for, and a hard-sphere excluded-volume approach with identical radii (R_meson = R_baryon = 0.3 fm) is employed. The model is also applied to J/ψ production using the charm quark production cross section as an additional input, assuming charm quarks thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma but are not in chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Best fit values for central Pb–Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: T = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV, μ_B = 0.7 ± 3.8 MeV, V_(Δy=1) = 5280 ± 410 fm³, with χ²_min = 29.1 per 18 degrees of freedom.
- The fit describes hadron yields spanning 9 orders of magnitude.
- Relative contributions from charmed hadron decays to the yields: 0.7% for pions, 2.9% for K±, and 3.1% for K⁰.
- The lattice QCD (pseudo-)critical temperature at vanishing μ_B is 155 ± 9 MeV.
- The shadowing factor for the c̅c production cross section at forward rapidity (2.5 < y < 4.0) is 0.71 ± 0.10.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of chemical freeze-out studies in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier statistical model analyses by Andronic (2008) and Cleymans (1998), and compares the extracted freeze-out parameters to lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2014). The results show that at low μ_B the chemical freeze-out temperature coincides with the QCD crossover temperature, supporting the picture of hadron formation from deconfined matter, and the successful description of J/ψ production via statistical hadronization challenges the traditional Debye screening interpretation, opening the possibility of using charmonium as a probe of the QCD phase boundary.
