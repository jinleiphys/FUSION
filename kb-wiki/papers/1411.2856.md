# A new quark-hadron hybrid equation of state for astrophysics - I. High-mass twin compact stars

> Aims: We present a new microscopic hadron-quark hybrid equation of state model for astrophysical applications, from which compact hybrid star configurations are constructed. These are composed of a quark core and a hadronic shell with a first-order phase transition at their interface. The resulting mass-radius relations are in accordance with the latest astrophysical constraints. Methods: The quark matter description is based on a quantum chromodynamics (QCD) motivated chiral approach with higher-order quark interactions in the Dirac scalar and vector coupling channels. For hadronic matter we select a relativistic mean-field equation of state with density-dependent couplings. Since the nucleons are treated in the quasi-particle framework, an excluded volume correction has been included for the nuclear equation of state at suprasaturation density which takes into account the finite size of the nucleons. Results: These novel aspects, excluded volume in the hadronic phase and the higher-order repulsive interactions in the quark phase, lead to a strong first-order phase transition with large latent heat, i.e. the energy-density jump at the phase transition, which fulfils a criterion for a disconnected third-family branch of compact stars in the mass-radius relationship. These twin stars appear at high masses ($\sim$ 2 M$_\odot$) that are relevant for current observations of high-mass pulsars. Conclusions: This analysis offers a unique possibility by radius observations of compact stars to probe the QCD phase diagram at zero temperature and large chemical potential and even to support the existence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic hadron-quark hybrid equation of state that can produce high-mass twin compact stars (around 2 solar masses) consistent with current pulsar observations, but only when the hadronic phase includes an excluded volume correction and the quark phase includes higher-order repulsive interactions, together generating a sufficiently large latent heat at the phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by combining a relativistic mean-field model for hadronic matter (DD2 parametrization with an excluded volume correction for nucleon finite size) with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including 8-quark interactions (NJL8) for quark matter. A Maxwell construction is applied for the first-order phase transition between the two phases, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations for compact star configurations.

## Key numbers

- The excluded volume parameter is set to v = (1/0.35) fm^3, corresponding to a nucleon radius of r_nuc ≈ 0.55 fm.
- The maximum baryon density in the hadronic model is n_max = 0.35 fm^{-3}.
- For the NJL8 model, the 4-quark vector coupling ratio is fixed at η_2 = 0.08, while η_4 is varied from 0.0 to 30.0.
- The latent heat at the phase transition ranges from Δε ≈ 0.81 ε_crit (for η_4 = 0.0) to Δε ≈ 0.70 ε_crit (for η_4 = 30.0).
- The critical density for the onset of quark matter is n_B^crit ≈ 1.5 n_sat, where n_sat = 0.149 fm^{-3}.
- Maximum masses on the hybrid star branch range from M_max = 1.92 to 2.30 M_⊙.
- The radii difference between twin stars (δR) ranges from 1.59 km (η_4 = 0.0) down to 0.13 km (η_4 = 30.0).
- For η_4 = 0.0, the hadronic maximum mass is 1.89 M_⊙ with radius 15.21 km, and the twin radius is 13.61 km.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological ZHAHP approach of Zdunik (2012) and Alford (2013) by providing a microscopic realization of a hybrid equation of state that satisfies the latent heat criterion (Δε > 0.6 ε_crit) for a disconnected third family of compact stars. The model enables predictions for radius differences of high-mass twin stars that could be tested by future X-ray missions such as NICER, and supports the possibility that the high-mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) could be such twin stars with radii differing by at least about 1 km.
