# EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force on "Thermalization in Non-abelian Plasmas"

> Recently, different proposals have been put forward on how thermalization proceeds in heavy-ion collisions in the idealized limit of very large nuclei at sufficiently high energy. Important aspects of the parametric estimates at weak coupling may be tested using well-established classical-statistical lattice simulations of the far-from-equilibrium gluon dynamics. This has to be confronted with strong coupling scenarios in related theories based on gauge-string dualities. Furthermore, closely related questions about far-from-equilibrium dynamics arise in early-universe cosmology and in non-relativistic systems of ultracold atoms. These were central topics of the EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force meeting held on December 12-14, 2011, at the University of Heidelberg, which we report on.

## Key claim

The paper reports on an EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force meeting that assessed competing weak-coupling and strong-coupling scenarios for thermalization in non-abelian plasmas, concluding that classical-statistical lattice simulations can test parametric estimates but that the role of longitudinal expansion and the possibility of Bose-Einstein condensation in gauge theories remain unresolved.

## Method

The meeting brought together theorists working on weak-coupling parametric estimates, classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations, gauge-string duality (AdS/CFT) approaches, and related far-from-equilibrium dynamics in early-universe cosmology and ultracold atomic gases. Discussions focused on comparing analytical proposals for thermalization scenarios with numerical results from classical Yang-Mills simulations, particularly regarding plasma instabilities, turbulent cascades, and overpopulated initial conditions characterized by the saturation momentum scale Q_s.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum Q_s grows with energy and nuclear size; when Q_s is large, the coupling constant is small, but gluons at saturation have occupancies ~ 1/alpha_s
- The strong coupling constant at RHIC or LHC energy is typically alpha_s ~ 0.3 - 0.4
- Initial gluon energy density after collision is epsilon_0 ~ Q_s^4 / alpha_s, with number density n_0 ~ Q_s^3 / alpha_s
- The dimensionless combination n_0 epsilon_0^{-3/4} ~ 1/alpha_s^{1/4} in the initial state, compared to ~ 1 in equilibrium, indicating overpopulation by a factor alpha_s^{-1/4} >> 1
- Weak coupling techniques provide quantitative understanding of lattice QCD thermodynamics for temperatures above 2 to 3 T_c
- Viscous hydrodynamics from AdS/CFT starts to be valid already when P_L/P_perp ~ 0.5, well before full pressure isotropization

## Context

This report summarizes the first EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force meeting (December 2011, Heidelberg) and extends discussions from earlier work on plasma instabilities by Mrowczynski (2008, 2009) and on classical-statistical simulations by Romatschke (2005), Berges (2007), and Fukushima (2011). It confronts weak-coupling thermalization scenarios proposed by Kurkela and collaborators (2011) with strong-coupling results from AdS/CFT reviewed by Janik (2011), and connects to Bose condensation studies in scalar theories by Berges and Sexty (2012) and in cold atom systems by Gasenzer (2011). The report identifies open questions about whether longitudinal expansion prevents isotropization, whether Bose condensation occurs in gauge theories, and whether first-principle initial conditions from the glasma picture can be reliably computed.
