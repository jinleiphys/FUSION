# The horn in the kaon to pion ratio

> A microscopic approach has been employed to study the kaon productions in heavy ion collisions. The momentum integrated Boltzmann equation has been used to study the evolution of strangeness in the system formed in heavy ion collision at relativistic energies. The kaon productions have been calculated for different centre of mass energies ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$) ranging from AGS to RHIC. The results have been compared with available experimental data. We obtain a non-monotonic horn like structure for $K^+/π^+$ when plotted with $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ with the assumption of an initial partonic phase beyond a certain threshold in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. However, a monotonic rise of $K^+/π^+$ is observed when a hadronic initial state is assumed for all $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$. Experimental values of $K^-/π^-$ are also reproduced within the ambit of the same formalism. Results from scenarios where the strange quarks and hadrons are formed in equilibrium and evolves with and without secondary productions have also been presented.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces the experimentally observed non-monotonic "horn" structure in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio as a function of $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ only when an initial partonic phase is assumed for collision energies at and above $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=8.76$ GeV; a purely hadronic initial state for all energies yields a monotonic rise that disagrees with data at higher energies.

## Method

The authors used a momentum-integrated Boltzmann equation to evolve the densities of strange quarks and anti-quarks in the partonic phase and of $K^+$ and $K^-$ in the hadronic phase, assuming non-strange quarks and hadrons are in complete thermal and chemical equilibrium. The production rates for strangeness in the partonic phase were computed from gluon fusion ($gg\rightarrow s\bar{s}$) and quark-antiquark annihilation ($q\bar{q}\rightarrow s\bar{s}$) using lowest-order QCD cross sections, while kaon production in the hadronic phase included meson-meson, meson-baryon, and baryon-baryon interactions with cross sections taken from resonance models and the literature. The space-time evolution of temperature and baryon chemical potential was obtained by solving relativistic hydrodynamic equations with boost invariance, using initial temperatures derived from measured hadronic multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures range from $T_i=0.115$ GeV at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=3.32$ GeV to $T_i=0.40$ GeV at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV (Table I).
- The transition temperature between partonic and hadronic phases is taken as $T_c=190$ MeV for $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\ge 8.76$ GeV.
- Baryonic chemical potentials range from $\mu_B=595$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=3.32$ GeV to $\mu_B=28$ MeV at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV (Table II).
- The initial density of strange quarks or kaons is assumed to be $20\%$ away from the corresponding equilibrium density.
- The threshold for assuming an initial partonic phase is $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=8.76$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kapusta and collaborators (1990s) who used similar Boltzmann equation methods for strangeness evolution in heavy ion collisions, and contrasts with the statistical model of Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999) which assumed thermal equilibrium for all degrees of freedom. The results support the interpretation that the horn in $K^+/\pi^+$ arises from the release of color degrees of freedom in a deconfined phase, consistent with the strangeness enhancement signal for Quark Gluon Plasma formation proposed by Rafelski (1982). The paper enables future studies to use the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio as a diagnostic for the onset of deconfinement in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies.
