# Proton Recoil Energy and Angular Distribution of Neutron Radiative Beta Decay

> We analyse the proton recoil energy and angular distribution of the radiative beta-decay of the neutron to leading order in the large baryon mass expansion by taking into account the contributions of the proton-photon correlations. We show that the account for the proton-photon correlations does not contradict the description of the radiative corrections to the lifetime of the neutron and the proton recoil energy spectrum of the neutron beta-decay in terms of the functions (α/π) g_n(E_e) and (α/π) f_n(E_e), where E_e is the electron energy. In addition we find that the contributions of the proton-photon correlations in the radiative beta-decay of the neutron to the proton recoil asymmetry C are of order 10^(-4). They make the contributions of the radiative corrections to the proton recoil asymmetry C symmetric with respect to a change A_0 <--> B_0, where A_0 and B_0 are the correlation coefficients of the neutron beta-decay.

## Key claim

The authors show that including proton-photon correlations in the radiative beta-decay of the neutron does not contradict the standard description of radiative corrections to the neutron lifetime and proton recoil energy spectrum using the functions \((\alpha/\pi) g_n(E_e)\) and \((\alpha/\pi) f_n(E_e)\), but these correlations contribute to the proton recoil asymmetry \(C\) at order \(10^{-4}\), which must be accounted for at the \(10^{-5}\) accuracy level.

## Method

The authors calculated the proton recoil energy and angular distribution of neutron radiative beta-decay to leading order in the large baryon mass expansion, following the approach of Gluck (1997). They performed the integration over the antineutrino 3-momentum first, then divided the photon energy spectrum into soft-photon and hard-photon regions, using a finite-photon mass regularization for infrared divergences. The soft-photon contribution was treated analytically, while the hard-photon contribution was computed numerically.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of proton-photon correlations to the proton recoil asymmetry \(C\) is of order \(10^{-4}\).
- The contributions of proton-photon correlations to the neutron lifetime \(\tau_n\) and the proton recoil energy spectrum \(a(T_p)\), integrated over proton recoil energy, are of order \(10^{-5}\).
- The soft-photon contribution to the integrated parameter \(\Delta X^{(s)}_2\) equals \(0.016051\ \mathrm{MeV}^5\).
- The soft-photon contribution to the integrated parameter \(\Delta X^{(s)}_0\) equals \(-0.010201\ \mathrm{MeV}^5\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ivanov (2012) by revising the contribution of radiative beta-decay to include proton-photon correlations, following the method of Gluck (1997). It confirms that the standard radiative correction functions of Sirlin (1967) and Shann (1971) remain valid for the lifetime and proton recoil energy spectrum, but the proton recoil asymmetry \(C\) receives additional corrections that make the radiative contributions symmetric under the exchange \(A_0 \leftrightarrow B_0\), consistent with the leading-order result of Treiman (1958). This enables more precise searches for beyond-Standard-Model contributions at the \(10^{-4}\) level in neutron beta-decay experiments.
