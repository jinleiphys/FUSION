# Masses of hadrons in nuclei

> We emphasize the central role played by the spectral function in the description of hadrons in matter and discuss the applicability of the quasiparticle concept to the propagation of hadrons in dense nuclear matter. Theoretical and experimental results relevant for the in medium properties of vector mesons and kaons are briefly reviewed. We also present novel results for the $ρ$ and $ω$ spectral functions in nuclear matter, deduced from a coupled channel analysis of pion-nucleon scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the in-medium mass of a hadron is not a well-defined concept unless the spectral function shows a clear quasiparticle peak; for rho mesons and negative kaons in nuclear matter, the spectral function may lack such a structure, making the full spectral function necessary for describing the response of the many-body system.

## Method

The authors perform a relativistic and unitary coupled-channel analysis of meson-nucleon scattering, including the channels πN, ρN, ωN, πΔ, and ηN. They fit elastic πN scattering data and pion-induced meson production data in the energy range 1.45 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 1.8 GeV using an effective Lagrangian with local 4-point interactions, then extract the vector-meson–nucleon scattering amplitudes. These amplitudes are used to compute the ρ and ω self energies in nuclear matter to leading order in density via the low-density theorem.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ (corresponding to k_F = 268 MeV).
- ρ-N scattering length: a_ρN = (-0.3 + 0.7 i) fm.
- ω-N scattering length: a_ωN = (-0.5 + 0.1 i) fm.
- To lowest order in density at nuclear matter density: Δm_ρ ≈ 30 MeV, Δm_ω ≈ 50 MeV, ΔΓ_ρ ≈ 140 MeV, ΔΓ_ω ≈ 20 MeV.
- The center-of-gravity of both the ρ and ω spectral functions is shifted down in energy by about 10 %.
- The low-lying mode in the ω spectral function carries about 20 % of the energy-weighted sum rule.

## Context

This work extends earlier effective hadronic model calculations by Rapp, Chanfray, and Wambach (1997) and by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) by incorporating constraints from meson-nucleon scattering data in the energy regime relevant for vector mesons in matter (√s ≈ 1.7 GeV), rather than extrapolating from low-energy data. The results support the dynamical scenario discussed by Brown and Rho (1991) and suggest that complementary experiments with photon and pion induced vector meson production off nuclei would be useful for further exploring in-medium properties.
