# The Spin Structure of the Proton

> It is shown that the proton "spin crisis'' or "spin puzzle" can be understood by the relativistic effect of quark transversal motions due to the Melosh-Wigner rotation. The quark helicity $Δq$ measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering is actually the quark spin in the infinite momentum frame or in the light-cone formalism, and it is different from the quark spin in the nucleon rest frame or in the quark model. The flavor asymmetry of the Melosh-Wigner effect for the valence $u$ and $d$ quarks and the intrinsic sea $q \bar{q}$ pairs are also the important ingredients in a SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model framework to understand the "spin puzzle". Such a picture of the spin structure can be tested by use of several simple relations to measure the quark spin distributions in the quark model.

## Key claim

The proton "spin crisis" can be resolved without requiring gluon polarization or orbital angular momentum to account for the missing spin, provided one recognizes that the quark helicity Δq measured in polarized deep inelastic scattering is the quark spin in the infinite momentum frame, which differs from the quark spin in the nucleon rest frame due to the relativistic Melosh-Wigner rotation from quark transversal motions.

## Method

The author constructs a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model in which the proton wave function contains scalar and vector diquark spectators with different masses (600 MeV and 900 MeV respectively). The Melosh-Wigner rotation factor W_D(x,k⊥) is applied to convert between quark spin in the proton rest frame and quark helicity in the infinite momentum frame, and the mass difference between scalar and vector spectators generates the flavor asymmetry between valence u and d quarks needed to reproduce the observed ratio F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ.

## Key numbers

- The Melosh-Wigner rotation factor ⟨M_q⟩ for light flavor quarks is around 0.75, and approaches 1 for heavy flavor quarks.
- To reproduce the observed Ellis-Jaffe sums Γ₁ᵖ = 0.136 and Γ₁ⁿ = -0.03, the model requires average Wigner rotation factors ⟨W_S⟩ = 0.56 and ⟨W_V⟩ = 0.92 for scalar and vector spectators respectively.
- The scalar spectator mass is taken as m_S = 600 MeV and the vector spectator mass as m_V = 900 MeV, with quark mass m_q = 330 MeV and harmonic oscillator scale β_D = 330 MeV.
- A global fit of polarized deep inelastic scattering data yields Δd = -0.43 ± 0.03, which exceeds the SU(6) quark model constraint |Δd_v| ≤ 1/3, implying an additional negative sea contribution Δd_s.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ma (1991, 1996, 1998) applying the Melosh-Wigner rotation to the proton spin problem, and builds on the quark-spectator-diquark model framework. It contradicts the initial interpretation of the EMC result that the proton spin must receive large contributions from gluon polarization or orbital angular momentum. The framework enables future tests through relations connecting quark helicity distributions Δq(x), quark transversity distributions δq(x), and quark orbital angular momentum L_q(x), as proposed by Ma, Schmidt, and Soffer (1998).
