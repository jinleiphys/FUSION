# Stellar Weak Interaction Rates and Energy Losses for fp-Shell Nuclei in the Proton-Neutron Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation

> Nuclear weak interaction rates for fp-shell nuclei in stellar matter and the associated energy losses are calculated using a modified form of proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces. The stellar weak rates are calculated over a wide range of densities (10 < ρY_{e} (gcm^{-3}) < 10^{11}) and temperatures (10^{7} < T(K) < 30 x 10^{9}). This is the first ever extensive compilation of weak interaction rates in stellar matter calculated over a wide temperature-density grid and over a larger mass range. The calculated capture and decay rates take into consideration the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations. We have calculated stellar weak interaction rates for a total of 619 nuclei in the mass range A = 40 to 100. These also include many important neutron-rich nuclei which play an important role in the evolution process of stellar collapse. This is our second paper in a series where we will be presenting our results on an abbreviated scale of temperature and density for the mass range A = 18 to 100. This paper contains the stellar weak rates in the mass range 40 to 60.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first extensive compilation of stellar weak interaction rates and associated energy losses for fp-shell nuclei in the mass range A=40-60, calculated using a modified proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with separable Gamow-Teller forces, but the results are limited to an abbreviated temperature-density grid and only cover the lower half of the intended mass range (A=40-60 out of A=40-100).

## Method

The authors employed a modified pn-QRPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces to calculate stellar electron capture and beta decay rates, as well as associated neutrino and gamma energy losses, for 619 nuclei in the mass range A=40-100. The calculations incorporated the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations, and were performed over a wide grid of stellar densities (10 < ρY_e (g cm^{-3}) < 10^{11}) and temperatures (10^7 < T(K) < 30 × 10^9). The paper presents results on an abbreviated scale of temperature and density specifically for the mass range A=40-60.

## Key numbers

- Stellar weak rates calculated for a total of 619 nuclei in the mass range A=40-100, with results presented for A=40-60 in this paper
- Density range covered: 10 < ρY_e (g cm^{-3}) < 10^{11}
- Temperature range covered: 10^7 < T(K) < 30 × 10^9
- For ^40Ca → ^40Sc at T9=30.0 and density 3.0: electron capture rate = 0.278 (units not specified in text)
- For ^40Ca → ^40K at T9=30.0 and density 3.0: electron capture rate = 1.905, positron decay rate = -2.098
- For ^56Ca → ^56Sc at T9=3.0 and density 3.0: electron capture rate = -2.661, positron decay rate = 0.285
- For ^56Ca → ^56K at T9=3.0 and density 3.0: electron capture rate = -38.825, positron decay rate = -100 (indicating negligible rate)

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of stellar weak interaction rates by the same group (Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus, previous work) by applying the pn-QRPA method to a larger set of fp-shell nuclei important for stellar collapse simulations. The inclusion of many neutron-rich nuclei in the mass range A=40-100 addresses a gap in previous compilations, as these species play crucial roles in the evolution of core-collapse supernovae. The results enable more accurate modeling of presupernova evolution and the electron capture process during stellar collapse, though the full compilation for A=60-100 is deferred to a subsequent paper in the series.
