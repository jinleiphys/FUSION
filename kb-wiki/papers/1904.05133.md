# Pomeron, nucleon-resonance, and $(0^+,0^-,1^+)$-meson contributions in $ϕ$-meson photoproduction

> We investigate the reaction mechanism of the $ϕ$-meson photoproduction off the proton target, i.e., $γp\toϕp$, up to $\sqrt{s}=2.8$ GeV. For this purpose, we employ an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation, and we employ various experimental and theoretical inputs. As a theoretical setup, the vectorlike Pomeron ($C=+1$) is taken into account as a parameterized two-gluon exchange contribution. We also consider $f_1(1285)$ axial-vector-meson, ($π,η$) pseudoscalar-meson, and ($a_0,f_0$) scalar-meson exchanges in the $t$ channel, in addition to the experimentally confirmed nucleon resonances, such as $N^*(2000,5/2^+)$ and $N^*(2300,1/2^+)$, for the direct $ϕ$-meson radiations in the $s$ and $u$ channels. We provide numerical results for the total and differential cross sections as well as the spin-density matrices in the Gottfried-Jackson, Adair, and helicity frames. We observe that, together with the universally accepted pomeron contribution, the considered meson and nucleon-resonance contributions play significant roles in reproducing the experimental data for the forward and backward $ϕ$-meson scattering-angle regions, respectively, indicating the nontrivial interferences between mesonic and baryonic contributions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the reaction mechanism of φ-meson photoproduction off protons requires contributions from the vectorlike Pomeron, axial-vector f₁(1285) meson, pseudoscalar (π, η) and scalar (a₀, f₀) meson exchanges in the t-channel, plus the nucleon resonances N*(2000,5/2⁺) and N*(2300,1/2⁺) in the s- and u-channels, to reproduce the CLAS experimental data up to √s = 2.8 GeV; however, the bump structure at the most forward angle cosθ = 0.925 around √s ≈ 2.2 GeV may arise from another mechanism beyond the scope of this work.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation with phenomenological form factors. The vectorlike Pomeron is treated as a parameterized two-gluon exchange using the Donnachie-Landshoff model, while the f₁(1285) axial-vector meson, (π, η) pseudoscalar mesons, and (a₀, f₀) scalar mesons are exchanged in the t-channel with Reggeized propagators. Direct φ-meson radiations via the proton and the two selected nucleon resonances N*(2000,5/2⁺) and N*(2300,1/2⁺) are included in the s- and u-channels, with coupling constants extracted from PDG data and fitted to reproduce the CLAS differential cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of φ-meson photoproduction beyond the earlier Pomeron-only models by Titov (1999) and the Donnachie-Landshoff model, incorporating the CLAS Collaboration data from Seraydaryan (2013) and Dey (2014) that cover the full angular range up to √s = 2.8 GeV. The study contradicts the interpretation of the near-threshold bump structure as arising solely from coupled-channel effects between φp and K⁺Λ(1520) channels as proposed by Ozaki (2009) and Ryu (2012), or from a single hypothetical spin-3/2 resonance as suggested by Kiswandhi (2010, 2011). The results enable future investigations of hidden-strangeness pentaquark states analogous to the charmed P_c⁺ states observed by LHCb, and provide extracted branching ratios for N*→φN decays that can be compared with open-strangeness N*→K*Λ decays from the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis by Anisovich (2017).
