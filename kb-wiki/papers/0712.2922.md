# Absorption-Fluctuation Theorem for Nuclear Reactions: Brink-Axel, Incomplete Fusion and All That

> We discuss the connection between absorption, averages and fluctuations in nuclear reactions. The fluctuations in the entrance channel result in the compound nucleus, Hauser-Feshbach, cross section, the fluctuations in the intermediate channels, result in modifications of multistep reaction cross sections, while the fluctuations in the final channel result in hybrid cross sections that can be used to describe incomplete fusion reactions. We discuss the latter in details and comment on the validity of the assumptions used in the develpoment of the Surrogate method. We also discuss the theory of multistep reactions with regards to intermediate state fluctuations and the energy dependence and non-locality of the intermediate channels optical potentials.

# Absorption-Fluctuation Theorem for Nuclear Reactions: Brink-Axel, Incomplete Fusion and All That

## Key claim

The paper argues that fluctuations in the final channel of a nuclear reaction produce hybrid cross sections that describe incomplete fusion, and that the Surrogate method's success relies on approximations (Weisskopf-Ewing, on-shell description) whose validity requires theoretical scrutiny; the intermediate-state fluctuations in multistep reactions introduce a Brink-Axel contribution that becomes significant at low energies where the collision time is comparable to the decay time of the intermediate collective state.

## Method

The author derives the incomplete fusion cross section from a decomposition of the final-channel wave function into optical and fluctuation pieces, expressing the inelastic-breakup cross section as the sum of a direct term and an incomplete-fusion term that involves a fusion imaginary potential. For multistep direct reactions, the intermediate-channel Green's function is separated into an average part (containing the damping width of the one-phonon collective state) and a fluctuation part, and the two-step fluctuation cross section is evaluated using energy averaging of products of rapidly fluctuating Green's functions. The treatment of the optical potential's energy dependence and non-locality is examined through the Feshbach form, and the construction of dual scattering states for energy-dependent optical potentials is shown to require the half-on-energy-shell T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 208Pb + 208Pb at 640 MeV per nucleon bombarding energy, the fluctuation contribution to the double giant dipole resonance excitation cross section is 30 percent.
- At the lower energy of 100 MeV per nucleon, the fluctuation contribution to the same process is 100 percent.
- The two-step fluctuation cross section is given by sigma_fl^(2) = (Gamma_d1_downward * tau_c(E) / (2 hbar)) * sigma_MS^(2), where Gamma_d1_downward is the damping width of the one-phonon doorway and tau_c(E) is the collision time.
- The energy-averaged n-step cross section is expressed as a series in powers of (Gamma_d1_downward * tau_c(E) / hbar) with binomial coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multistep direct reaction theory of Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (1980) by incorporating intermediate-state fluctuations that give rise to the Brink-Axel mechanism in multiphonon excitations, building on prior treatments of multiphonon giant resonances by Carlson, Canto, and collaborators (1999) and by Gu and Weidenmuller (2001). The analysis of the Surrogate method connects to the incomplete fusion formalism developed by Hussein and McVoy (1985) and by Austern et al. (1987), and raises theoretical issues about the Weisskopf-Ewing approximation and off-shell effects that should guide future assessments of why the Surrogate method works as well as it does. The discussion of energy-dependent optical potentials and dual scattering states points to the need for an equivalent energy-independent optical potential, as previously considered by Hussein and Moniz (1984), to improve the convergence of the FKK multistep direct reaction theory.
