# Mean-field approach to superdeformed high-spin states in 40Ca and neutron-rich 50S regions

> With the use of the symmetry-unrestricted cranked SHF method in the 3D coordinate-mesh representation, a systematic search for the SD and HD rotational bands in the N=Z nuclei from 32S to 48Cr has been done, and SD and HD solutions have been found in 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, and in 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, 48Cr, respectively. The SD band in 40Ca is found to be extremely soft against both the axially symmetric (Y30) and asymmetric (Y31) octupole deformations. Possible presense of SD states in neutron-rich sulfur isotopes from 46S to 52S has also been investigated, and deformation properties of neutron skins both in the ground and SD states are discussed.

## Key claim

The superdeformed (SD) band in 40Ca is found to be extremely soft against both axially symmetric (Y30) and asymmetric (Y31) octupole deformations, suggesting that negative-parity rotational bands built on non-axial Kπ=1− octupole vibrations may exist near the SD yrast band.

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-unrestricted cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations in a fully three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate-mesh representation, without imposing any symmetry restrictions. They compared these results with standard symmetry-restricted calculations imposing reflection symmetries about the (x,y), (y,z), and (z,x) planes to identify effects of reflection symmetry breaking. For the neutron-rich sulfur isotopes, they extracted half-density radii and surface diffuseness parameters by fitting density distributions along each principal axis with a Woods-Saxon function.

## Key numbers

- SD local minima found in 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca, and 44Ti in the region 0.4 ≤ β2 ≤ 0.8
- HD local minima found in 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, and 48Cr in the region β2 ≥ 0.8
- The SD band in 40Ca is slightly triaxial with γ = 6°-9° (8°-9°) for SIII (SkM*) interaction, terminating at I ≃ 24 for both interactions
- The SD band in 44Ti is more triaxial with γ = 18°-25° (13°-19°) for SIII (SkM*), terminating at I ≃ 12 and 16 respectively
- For 40Ca with SIII (SkM*) interaction, a strongly collective octupole vibrational mode with Kπ=1− was found at about 1.1 (0.6) MeV excitation from the SD band head
- For the SD state in 50S: neutron rms radius = 4.15 fm, proton rms radius = 3.75 fm; neutron β2 = 0.59, proton β2 = 0.70; neutron half-density radius along major axis = 5.65 fm, along minor axis = 3.09 fm

## Context

This work extends the systematic theoretical search for SD and hyperdeformed (HD) rotational bands in N=Z nuclei from 32S to 48Cr, following experimental discoveries of SD bands in 36Ar, 40Ca, and 44Ti by Svensson (2000), Ideguchi (2001), and Leoni (2000). The authors also investigate neutron-rich sulfur isotopes from 46S to 52S near the neutron drip line, where they predict a new "SD doubly closed" region associated with SD magic numbers Z=16 and N≃30-32. The results enable future searches for negative-parity rotational bands and soft Kπ=0− and 1− vibrational modes in nuclei with deformed neutron skins, and motivate development of SHF-Bogoliubov plus quasiparticle RPA approaches that can simultaneously treat deformation, pairing, and continuum effects.
