# Double Resonance in Dalitz Plot of M(pLambda)-M(KLambda) in DISTO Data on p+p rightarrow p+Lambda+K+ at 2.85 GeV

> The X(2265) resonance was previously observed in DISTO data of p+p rightarrow p+Lambda+K+ at 2.85 GeV on an attempt of searching for the kaonic nuclear state K-pp rightarrow p + Lambda. In the present paper we report an additional finding, namely, a double resonance type phenomena, not only with a peak at M(pLambda) = 2265 MeV/c2 but also a broad bump at M(K+ Lambda) ~ 1700 MeV/c2. This "double-resonance" zone is expressed as XY(2265, 1700). The latter bump may result from nearby nucleon resonances, typically N*(1710), as well as by attractive K - Lambda final-state interaction. We point out that this double resonance XY(2265, 1700) as seen in DISTO at 2.85 GeV cannot be populated kinematically in a HADES experiment at 3.5 GeV.

# Double Resonance in Dalitz Plot of M(pΛ)-M(KΛ) in DISTO Data on p+p → p+Λ+K+ at 2.85 GeV

## Key claim
The previously reported X(2265) resonance in the p+p → p+Λ+K+ reaction at 2.85 GeV is accompanied by a second, broad bump at M(K+Λ) ~ 1700 MeV/c², forming a double-resonance zone XY(2265, 1700); this double resonance cannot be populated kinematically in a HADES experiment at 3.5 GeV, explaining the non-observation of X(2265) at that higher energy.

## Method
The DISTO collaboration analyzed exclusive data from the p+p → p+Λ+K+ reaction at a proton kinetic energy of 2.85 GeV, applying a proton angular cut (|cosθ_cm(p)| < 0.6) to suppress ordinary decay processes and enhance the X(2265) component. Acceptance-uncorrected data were normalized bin-by-bin by Monte Carlo pure phase-space events analyzed identically, producing deviation spectra from flat phase-space distributions in the M(pΛ) versus M(K+Λ) Dalitz plot.

## Key numbers
- X(2265) resonance peak at M(pΛ) = 2265 MeV/c² (previously reported as centered around 2267 MeV/c², ~100 MeV below threshold).
- Broad bump at M(K+Λ) ~ 1700 MeV/c², attributed to N*(1710) which has a width of 50 to 250 (~100) MeV and decays to K+ and Λ with 5-25% branching ratio.
- The sticking probability of Λ(1405) and proton to form X(2265) is found to be very high (>0.5).
- The double resonance point XY(2265, 1700) lies outside the kinematically allowed Dalitz domain for T_p = 3.5 GeV and higher energies.

## Context
This work extends the DISTO collaboration's previous observation of X(2265) (Yamazaki 2010, Maggiora 2010) as a candidate for the kaonic nuclear state K⁻pp, following the theoretical prediction by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and Yamazaki (2002). The double-resonance interpretation explains why the HADES collaboration (Agakishiev 2015) did not observe X(2265) in the same reaction at 3.5 GeV, and why the 2.5 GeV data (Kienle 2012) showed diminished yield due to insufficient energy for Λ(1405) production. The E27 experiment at J-PARC (Ichikawa 2015) reported a K⁻pp-like structure in the d(π+, K+) reaction with a signal-to-background ratio of ~0.01. The double-resonance phenomenon is compared to a similar effect observed in low-energy heavy-ion reactions (Shimoura 1986).
