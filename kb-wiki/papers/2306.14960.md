# Parton Distributions from Boosted Fields in the Coulomb Gauge

> We propose a new method to calculate parton distribution functions (PDFs) from lattice correlations of boosted quarks and gluons in the Coulomb gauge. Compared to the widely used gauge-invariant Wilson-line operators, these correlations greatly simplify the renormalization thanks to the absence of linear power divergence. Besides, they enable access to larger off-axis momenta under preserved 3D rotational symmetry, as well as enhanced long-range precision that facilitates the Fourier transform. We verify the factorization formula that relates this new observable to the quark PDF at one-loop order in perturbation theory. Moreover, through a lattice calculation of the pion valence quark PDF, we demonstrate the aforementioned advantage and features of the Coulomb gauge correlation and show that it yields consistent result with the gauge-invariant method. This opens the door to a more efficient way to calculate parton physics on the lattice.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be calculated from lattice correlations of boosted quarks and gluons in the Coulomb gauge, which avoids the linear power divergence present in the standard gauge-invariant Wilson-line approach, but the method is verified only at one-loop order in perturbation theory and the factorization formula's proof is deferred to future study.

## Method

The authors define a quasi-PDF from equal-time quark bilinear correlations in the Coulomb gauge, without Wilson lines, and verify its factorization onto the standard PDF at next-to-leading order in perturbation theory. They then perform a lattice QCD calculation of the pion valence quark PDF using both the Coulomb gauge and gauge-invariant methods on a 2+1 flavor ensemble with lattice spacing 0.06 fm and volume 48^3 x 64, using momentum-smeared sources at momenta up to 2.24 GeV. The Coulomb gauge is fixed per time slice by minimizing a functional of the link variables, and the bare matrix elements are renormalized using a hybrid scheme where the Coulomb gauge correlation requires no linear divergence subtraction.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.06 fm and volume L_s^3 x L_t = 48^3 x 64 for the primary ensemble used in the pion PDF calculation.
* Valence pion mass m_pi = 300 MeV used in the lattice calculation.
* On-axis momenta |p| = 1.72 GeV (n_z=4) and 2.15 GeV (n_z=5), and off-axis momentum |p| = 2.24 GeV (n=(3,3,3)) used for the pion PDF extraction.
* Approximately 20% increase in signal-to-noise ratios of both two-point and three-point correlations at t_s/a <= 10 for the off-axis momentum (3,3,3) compared to the on-axis (0,0,5) case.
* The Coulomb gauge correlation shows no linear divergence, verified by comparing ratios at two lattice spacings (0.04 fm and 0.06 fm) which agree except at |z| ~ a.

## Context

This work extends the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) framework proposed by Ji (2013) for calculating PDFs from lattice QCD, which traditionally uses gauge-invariant Wilson-line operators that suffer from linear power divergence and require complicated renormalization as studied by Dorn (1986), Maiani (1991), and Ji (2017). The Coulomb gauge approach was previously suggested within LaMET for gluon helicity by Ji (2013) and Hatta (2013), but this paper provides the first full implementation for quark PDFs including a lattice calculation. The method enables access to larger off-axis momenta under preserved 3D rotational symmetry and simplifies renormalization, potentially making lattice calculations of more computationally demanding quantities like transverse-momentum distributions and generalized parton distributions more efficient.
