# M-Theory Exotic Scalar Glueball Decays to Mesons at Finite Coupling

> Using the pull-back of the perturbed type IIA metric corresponding to the perturbation of arXiv:hep-th/1306.4339's M-theory uplift of arXiv:hep-th/0902.1540's UV-complete top-down type IIB holographic dual of large-$N$ thermal QCD, at finite coupling, we obtain the interaction Lagrangian corresponding to exotic scalar glueball($G_E$)-$ρ/π$-meson interaction, linear in the exotic scalar glueball and up to quartic order in the $π$ mesons. In the Lagrangian, the coupling constants are determined as (radial integrals of) arXiv:hep-th/1306.4339's M-theory uplift's metric components and six radial functions appearing in the M-theory metric perturbations. Assuming $M_G>2M_ρ$, we then compute $ρ\rightarrow2π, G_E\rightarrow2π, 2ρ, ρ+2π$ decay widths as well as the direct and indirect (mediated via $ρ$ mesons) $G_E\rightarrow4π$ decays. For numerics, we choose $f0[1710]$ and compare with previous calculations. We emphasize that our results can be made to match PDG data (and improvements thereof) exactly by appropriate tuning of some constants of integration appearing in the solution of the M-theory metric perturbations and the $ρ$ and $π$ meson radial profile functions - a flexibility that our calculations permits.

## Key claim
The authors compute decay widths for an exotic scalar glueball ($G_E$) into $\rho$ and $\pi$ mesons using a top-down holographic approach, and claim that by tuning integration constants in the M-theory metric perturbations and meson radial profile functions, their results can be made to exactly match Particle Data Group (PDG) data, a flexibility not present in prior holographic calculations.

## Method
The authors start from the M-theory uplift of a delocalized SYZ type IIA mirror of a UV-complete type IIB holographic dual of large-$N$ thermal QCD at finite gauge coupling, as constructed in earlier work (Misra et al., 2013). They perturb the M-theory metric with six radial functions $q_{1-6}(r)$ corresponding to an exotic scalar glueball with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$, solve the linearized Einstein equations near the horizon $r=r_h$ and in the UV, and obtain the interaction Lagrangian for $G_E$-$\rho$/$\pi$ meson couplings up to quartic order in pions. Using this Lagrangian and assuming $M_G > 2M_\rho$, they compute the decay widths for $\rho \rightarrow 2\pi$, $G_E \rightarrow 2\pi, 2\rho, \rho+2\pi$, and direct and indirect $G_E \rightarrow 4\pi$ decays.

## Key numbers
- The lightest scalar glueball mass from lattice QCD is quoted as 1600–1800 MeV.
- For numerical comparison, the authors choose the $f_0[1710]$ resonance (mass 1710 MeV) as the exotic scalar glueball candidate.
- The MQGP limit is defined by $\frac{g_s M^2}{N} \ll 1$, $g_s N \gg 1$, with finite $g_s$ and $M$, where $M$ can be set to 3 (matching $N_c=3$).
- The near-horizon solution for $q_1(r)$ is obtained with the Neumann boundary condition $q_1'(r_h)=0$ enforced by setting $a_1 = a_2/2$ (with $n=1$), leading to $q_1(r \sim r_h) = 0$ after further constraints.
- The UV solution for $q_1(r)$ requires $c_1=0$ for the solution to vanish, yielding an asymptotic form proportional to $1/r^4$ and $1/r^6$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous holographic calculations of glueball masses (Sil, Yadav, Misra, 2017) and meson spectra (Yadav, Misra, Sil, 2017) in the same M-theory uplift framework. It contrasts with bottom-up holographic models (soft-wall, hard-wall) and the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005), which have been used for glueball-meson interactions. The specific calculation of glueball decays into mesons was previously considered in the type IIA Sakai-Sugimoto model by Hashimoto et al. (2008) and more recently by Brunner and Hashimoto (2017); this paper provides the first top-down M-theory treatment at finite coupling. The results enable future tuning to match PDG data exactly, and the framework can incorporate improvements in experimental measurements.
