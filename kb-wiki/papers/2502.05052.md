# Short Note on Spin Magnetization in QGP

> We outline the theory of spin magnetization applicable to the QGP (quark-gluon plasma) epoch of the Universe. We show that a fully spin-polarized single flavor up-quark gas could generate a cosmic magnetic fields in excess of $10^{15}$ Tesla, far in excess of a possible upper limit to the primordial field. The complete multi component ferro-magnetized primordial fermion gas we consider consists of (five) nearly free electrically charged quarks, and leptons (electrons, muons, tau). We present details of how the magnetization is obtained using a grand partition function approach and point to the role of the nonrelativistic particle component. In the range of temperature 150 MeV to 500 MeV our results are also of interest to laboratory QGP experiments. We show that the required polarization capable to explain large scale structure magnetic fields observed has $1/T$ scaling in the limit of high $T$, and could be very small, at pico-scale. In the other limit, as temperature decreases in the expanding Universe, we show that any magnetic fields present before hadronization can be carried forward to below quark confinement condition temperature by polarization of electrons and muons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fully spin-polarized single flavor up-quark gas could generate cosmic magnetic fields in excess of \(10^{15}\) Tesla during the QGP epoch, but that the actual polarization required to explain observed large-scale magnetic fields could be as small as pico-scale (\(10^{-12}\) alignment fraction) due to \(1/T\) scaling at high temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the maximum possible spin magnetization for each fermion species (five quarks and three charged leptons) present in the primordial QGP using a grand partition function approach with Landau energy levels, then compare these upper bounds to the primordial magnetic field range obtained by scaling present-day intergalactic magnetic field observations backward via flux conservation. They derive the magnetization from the free energy derivative with respect to magnetic field, evaluating the massless limit with a spin polarization potential \(\Pi\) that couples to intrinsic spin and induces a bias in particle number between spin-up and spin-down states.

## Key numbers

- Maximum up-quark magnetization at \(k_B T = 300\) MeV: \(\mathcal{B}_{q\bar{q}} < 9.1 \times 10^{15}\) T
- Maximum lepton magnetization at \(k_B T = 300\) MeV: \(\mathcal{B}_{\ell\bar{\ell}} < 1.6 \times 10^{16}\) T
- Ratio of electron to up-quark magnetization: \(\mathcal{B}_{e\bar{e}} / \mathcal{B}_{u\bar{u}} \sim 2.2\)
- Required alignment fraction at \(T = 300\) MeV: \(10^{-12} < f(300\ \mathrm{MeV}) < 10^{-4}\)
- Hagedorn temperature marking hadronization: \(T_\mathrm{H} \approx 150\) MeV
- Temperature range considered for QGP epoch: \(500\ \mathrm{MeV} > T > 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Present-day intergalactic magnetic field bounds: \(10^{-12}\ \mathrm{T} > \mathcal{B}_0 > 10^{-20}\ \mathrm{T}\)
- Contemporary CMB temperature: \(k_B T_0 = 2.35 \times 10^{-4}\) eV
- Net baryon-to-photon ratio today: \(\eta_\gamma = 6.14 \pm 0.02 \times 10^{-10}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior spin magnetization studies (Steinmetz and Rafelski, 2023) from electron-positron pairs near Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis to light quark-antiquark pairs in the QGP phase before hadronization. The results are relevant to understanding the origin of large-scale (\(\sim 1\) Mpc) cosmic magnetic fields known from Faraday rotation measurements (Pomakov, 2022) and blazar observations (Neronov, 2010), and may connect to the Hubble tension problem (Jedamzik, 2025). The theoretical framework enables future evaluation of how spin polarization from color magnetism (speculated by Iwazaki, 2005 and Miransky, 2015) could contribute to primordial magnetization, and provides a basis for numerical comparison between the massless Fermi-Dirac treatment and the Boltzmann limit.
