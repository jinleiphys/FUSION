# Nuclear electric dipole moment in the cluster model with a triton: $^7$Li and $^{11}$B

> We calculate the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the nuclei $^7$Li and $^{11}$B in the cluster model with $α$ ($^4$He) and triton ($^3$H) clusters as degrees of freedom. The $^7$Li and $^{11}$B nuclei are treated in the two- and three-body problem, respectively, using the Gaussian expansion method, assuming the one-meson exchange P, CP-odd nuclear forces. We find that $^7$Li and $^{11}$B have larger sensitivity to the CP violation than the deuteron. It is also suggested that the EDMs of $^7$Li and $^{11}$B, together with those of $^6$Li, $^9$Be and the $1/2^+_1$ excited state of $^{13}$C, obey an approximate counting rule accounting for the EDM of the cluster and the $α-N $ polarization. We show their sensitivity on the hadronic level CP violation in terms of the chiral effective field theory, and discuss their role in probing new physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the electric dipole moments (EDMs) of \(^{7}\)Li and \(^{11}\)B have larger sensitivity to CP violation than the deuteron, but this result is conditional on the cluster model assumption that the nuclei are composed of \(\alpha\) (\({}^{4}\)He) and triton (\({}^{3}\)H) clusters, and on the use of one-meson exchange P, CP-odd nuclear forces as the dominant CP-violating mechanism.

## Method

The authors treat \(^{7}\)Li as a two-body \(\alpha\)-triton system and \(^{11}\)B as a three-body \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\)-triton system, solving the Schrödinger equation with the Gaussian expansion method under the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM). The CP-odd nuclear force is modeled as a one-pion exchange interaction, and the nuclear EDM is decomposed into contributions from the intrinsic nucleon EDM and from the polarization of the nucleus by the CP-odd force, with the triton spin matrix elements computed from the cluster wave functions.

## Key numbers

* The triton spin matrix element in \(^{11}\)B is 0.76 (dimensionless), used to convolute the EDM of the \({}^{3}\)H cluster.
* The polarization contribution to the EDM of \(^{7}\)Li is \(0.005 \, \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\, {\rm fm}\) from the \(\alpha\)-t system, plus the triton cluster EDM.
* The polarization contribution to the EDM of \(^{11}\)B is \(0.008 \, \bar G_\pi^{(1)} e\, {\rm fm}\) from the \(\alpha\)-t system, plus 0.76 times the triton cluster EDM.
* The linear coefficient \(a_\pi^{(1)}\) for the isovector CP-odd coupling is \(1.6 \times 10^{-2} e\, {\rm fm}\) for both \(^{7}\)Li and \(^{11}\)B.
* The standard model CKM contribution to the EDM is predicted as approximately \(2 \times 10^{-31} e\, {\rm cm}\) for both \(^{7}\)Li and \(^{11}\)B.

## Context

This work extends previous cluster-model calculations of nuclear EDMs for \(^{6}\)Li, \(^{9}\)Be (Yamanaka et al., 2017), and the \(1/2^+_1\) excited state of \(^{13}\)C (Yamanaka et al., 2018), and proposes an approximate counting rule that relates the EDMs of these light nuclei to the sum of the triton or deuteron cluster EDM and the \(\alpha\)-nucleon polarization. The results enable future storage-ring experiments to probe new physics beyond the standard model, including supersymmetric models, left-right symmetric models, and the QCD \(\theta\)-term, with prospective sensitivity to CP-violating scales up to 1-10 TeV and to \(\bar\theta\) values of order \(10^{-13}\).
