# Viscous coefficients of a hot pion gas

> The steps essentially involved in the evaluation of transport coefficients in linear response theory using Kubo formulas are to relate the defining {\em retarded} correlation function to the corresponding {\em time-ordered} one and to evaluate the latter in the conventional perturbation expansion. Here we evaluate the viscosities of a pion gas carrying out both the steps in the {\em real time} formulation. We also obtain the viscous coefficients by solving the relativistic transport equation in the Chapman-Enskog approximation to leading order. An in-medium $ππ$ cross-section in used in which spectral modifications are introduced in the propagator of the exchanged $ρ$.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates the shear and bulk viscosities of a hot pion gas using both linear response theory (Kubo formulas) in the real-time formulation of thermal field theory and the relativistic transport equation in the Chapman-Enskog approximation, finding that the two approaches yield equivalent expressions when the pion width is interpreted as the inverse relaxation time.

## Method

The author derived spectral representations for retarded and time-ordered two-point correlation functions in the real-time formulation of thermal field theory, then used these to relate the retarded correlation function of the energy-momentum tensor to the time-ordered one for perturbative evaluation. Separately, the relativistic Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport equation was solved in the first Chapman-Enskog approximation using an in-medium ππ cross-section that incorporates spectral modifications in the ρ-meson propagator through one-loop self-energy diagrams.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of viscous coefficients for a pion gas that used vacuum cross-sections from chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1966; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984) or experimental phase shifts (Prakash et al., 1993; Chen et al., 2007; Davesne, 1996; Itakura et al., 2008) by incorporating an in-medium ππ cross-section with ρ-meson spectral modifications. The results enable more realistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where the small η/s ratio near 1/4π (Kovtun et al., 2005) is crucial for explaining the large elliptic flow observed experimentally.
