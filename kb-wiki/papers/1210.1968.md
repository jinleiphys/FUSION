# SQM studied in the Field Correlator Method

> By using the recent nonperturbative equation of state of the quark gluon plasma derived in the formalism of the Field Correlator Method, we investigate the bulk properties of the strange quark matter in beta-equilibrium and with charge neutrality at T=p=0. The results show that the stability of strange quark matter with respect to $^{56}Fe$ is strongly dependent on the model parameters, namely, the gluon condensate $G_2$ and the q$\bar{\rm q}$ interaction potential $V_1$. A remarkable result is that the width of the stability window decreases as $V_1$ increases, being maximum at $V_1=0$ and nearly zero at $V_1=0.5$ GeV. For $V_1$ in the range $0\leq V_1\leq0.5$ GeV, all values of $G_2$ are lower than $0.006-0.007\;{\rm GeV}^4$ obtained from comparison with lattice results at $T_c\;(μ=0)\sim170$ MeV. These results do not favor the possibilities for the existence of (either nonnegative or negative) absolutely stable strange quark matter.

## Key claim

The stability of strange quark matter (SQM) relative to iron-56 depends strongly on the model parameters (gluon condensate G2 and quark-antiquark interaction potential V1), and the values of G2 required for stability are lower than the range 0.006-0.007 GeV^4 obtained from lattice results at the critical temperature, which does not favor the existence of absolutely stable SQM.

## Method

The authors used the nonperturbative equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma derived in the Field Correlator Method (FCM) to investigate the bulk properties of strange quark matter in beta-equilibrium and with charge neutrality at zero temperature and pressure. They solved the coupled equations for chemical potentials under the constraints of weak equilibrium and charge neutrality, determining the gluon condensate G2 and strange quark mass m_s for fixed values of the energy per baryon and the interaction potential V1 in the range 0 to 0.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The energy per baryon of iron-56 is 930.4 MeV, used as the stability threshold throughout the paper
- For V1=0 and m_s=0, the maximum G2 value within the stability window is G2 < 0.005 GeV^4
- For V1=0.1 GeV and m_s=0, the stability condition gives G2 < 0.002 GeV^4
- For V1=0.5 GeV and m_s=0, the stability condition gives G2 approximately 10^{-5} GeV^4
- The lattice comparison value for G2 is in the range 0.006-0.007 GeV^4 at critical temperature T_c ~ 170 MeV (mu=0)
- At V1=0 and m_s=0, the ratio of energy per baryon for SQM to two-flavor matter is 0.87
- The correlation length lambda is 0.34 fm (full QCD)
- For V1=0, the hadronic electric charge per baryon saturates at approximately 0.0056 at m_s ~ 0.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the application of the Field Correlator Method (developed by Simonov (1987) and Dosch) to strange quark matter, following earlier work by Pereira (2012) on strange star surfaces. It contrasts with the MIT Bag Model approach of Farhi and Jaffe (1984) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model applications, by using a nonperturbative framework that naturally includes confinement dynamics. The results enable comparison with lattice simulations and experiments at RHIC and LHC, while suggesting that the parameter values required for stable SQM are inconsistent with lattice determinations of the gluon condensate.
