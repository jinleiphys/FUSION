# Tidal Deformability of Quark Stars with Repulsive Interactions

> In an early work, we applied a QCD-based equation of state to the study of the stellar structure of self-bound strange stars, obtaining sequences with maximum masses larger than two solar masses and radii ranging from 8 to 12 Km. In this work, we update the previous calculations and compare them with the most recent data, including the very recent determination of the mass and radius of the massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620 performed by the NICER and XMM-Newton Collaborations. Our equation of state is similar to the MIT bag model one, but it includes repulsive interactions, which turn out to be essential to reproduce the accumulated experimental information. We find that our EOS is still compatible with all astrophysical observations but the parameter window is now narrower.

# Tidal Deformability of Quark Stars with Repulsive Interactions

## Key claim

The MFTQCD equation of state, which includes repulsive interactions among quarks derived from QCD, remains compatible with all current astrophysical observations including the NICER/XMM-Newton mass-radius determination of PSR J0740+6620 and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, but the allowed parameter window in the bag constant and coupling parameter space is now narrower than in the earlier analysis of Franzon et al. (2012).

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using an equation of state derived from a mean-field treatment of QCD (MFTQCD), where the gluon field is decomposed into soft and hard components; the soft fields generate a dynamical gluon mass and a bag-like condensate term, while the hard gluons are treated as classical color fields producing a repulsive term proportional to the square of the baryon density. For a fixed baryon density, they impose chemical equilibrium via weak processes, charge neutrality, and baryon number conservation to determine the Fermi momenta of u, d, s quarks and electrons, then compute the energy density and pressure. The tidal deformability parameter Lambda is computed from the tidal Love number k2 and the stellar compactness, requiring solution of an additional set of differential equations for the perturbation function H(r).

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses larger than 2 solar masses are obtained, with radii ranging from 8 to 12 km (from the earlier work, consistent with the current parameter sets).
- The stability condition requires the energy per baryon at zero pressure to be less than or equal to 934 MeV for three-flavor quark matter and greater than or equal to 934 MeV for two-flavor quark matter.
- Three parameter sets are used: Set I with B_QCD = 70 MeV/fm^3 and xi = 0.0011 MeV^-1; Set II with B_QCD = 60 MeV/fm^3 and xi = 0.0016 MeV^-1; Set III with B_QCD = 50 MeV/fm^3 and xi = 0.0022 MeV^-1.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 gives 70 < Lambda_1.4 < 580 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star at the 90% confidence level.
- Quark masses used: m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV, m_e = 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work updates the earlier study by Franzon et al. (2012) which applied the MFTQCD equation of state from Davesne (1995) to strange quark stars. It extends the analysis to include the most recent constraints: the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses from Cromartie et al. 2020), the NICER/XMM-Newton radius determination for that pulsar, and the tidal deformability bound from the GW170817 merger event (LIGO/Virgo Collaboration 2018). The results are compared with the updated MIT bag model used by Parisi et al. (2020) and with other quark matter calculations by Tanja et al. (2008, 2010) and Lattimer et al. (2010). The authors note that purely hadronic equations of state (Lattimer et al. 2018, 2021) appear to reproduce the experimental data more easily than quark matter models, and that further reduction of observational error bars will be crucial to rule out strange quark star models.
