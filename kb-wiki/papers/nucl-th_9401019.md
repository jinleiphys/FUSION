# Novel soft-photon analysis of $ppγ$ below pion-production threshold

> A novel soft-photon amplitude is proposed to replace the conventional Low soft-photon amplitude for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung. Its derivation is guided by the standard meson-exchange model of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. This new amplitude provides a superior description of $ppγ$ data. The predictions of this new amplitude are in close agreement with potential-model calculations, which implies that, contrary to conclusions drawn by others, off-shell effects are essentially insignificant below pion-production threshold.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel soft-photon amplitude, \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\), to replace the conventional Low soft-photon amplitude for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and demonstrate that this new amplitude eliminates the discrepancy between soft-photon approximations and potential-model calculations for \(pp\gamma\) data below pion-production threshold, implying that off-shell effects are essentially insignificant in this kinematic range.

## Method

The authors derived two general classes of soft-photon amplitudes, \(M^{TsTts}_\mu\) and \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\), guided by the structure of the standard meson-exchange model of the two-nucleon interaction. They applied these amplitudes to calculate \(pp\gamma\) cross sections at various energies (42, 157, and 280 MeV) using state-of-the-art phase shifts from the latest Nijmegen \(pp\) partial-wave analysis, and compared the results with experimental data and potential-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- At 42 MeV for \(\theta_q=\theta_p=26^\circ\), the \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\) and \(M^{{\rm Low}(s,t)}_\mu\) amplitudes gave similar results that agreed with Manitoba data and the Hamada-Johnston-potential calculation.
- At 157 MeV for \(\theta_q=\theta_p=35^\circ\), the \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\) and \(M^{{\rm Low}(s,t)}_\mu\) amplitudes gave very similar results that agreed reasonably with Harvard data and a Paris-potential calculation.
- At 280 MeV for \(\theta_q=12.4^\circ\) and \(\theta_p=12^\circ\), the \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\) amplitude agreed well with TRIUMF data and curves from the Paris and Bonn potentials, while the conventional Low amplitude predicted cross sections that were too small for forward (\(\theta_\gamma \leq 30^\circ\)) and backward (\(\theta_\gamma \geq 150^\circ\)) photon angles.
- At 100 MeV for \(\theta_q=\theta_p=\theta_\gamma=30^\circ\), the four energies at which \(F^e_\alpha\) is evaluated in \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\) were \(s_{1p}=3.648\) GeV\(^2\), \(s_{2q}=3.640\) GeV\(^2\), \(s_{2p}=3.632\) GeV\(^2\), and \(s_{1q}=3.655\) GeV\(^2\), while \(\overline{s}=3.644\) GeV\(^2\), with differences in c.m. energy less than about 3 MeV.
- The difference between \(s_i=3.709\) GeV\(^2\) and \(s_f=3.578\) GeV\(^2\) at 100 MeV corresponds to a c.m. energy difference of some 34 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the soft-photon approach of Low (1958) and addresses the discrepancy found by Workman and Fearing (1986) between the conventional Low amplitude and potential-model calculations for TRIUMF data at 280 MeV. The new amplitude \(M^{TuTts}_\mu\) provides a description of \(pp\gamma\) data that matches contemporary potential-model calculations, enabling the conclusion that off-shell effects are insignificant below pion-production threshold, contrary to earlier interpretations.
