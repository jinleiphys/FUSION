# Q^2-evolution of nucleon-to-resonance transition form factors in a QCD-inspired vector-meson-dominance model

> We adopt the vector-meson-dominance approach to investigate Q^2-evolution of N-R transition form factors (N denotes nucleon and R an excited resonance) in the first and second resonance regions. The developed model is based upon conventional NRγ-interaction Lagrangians, introducing three form factors for spin-3/2 resonances and two form factors for spin-1/2 nucleon excitations. Lagrangian form factors are expressed as dispersionlike expansions with four or five poles corresponding to the lowest excitations of the mesons ρ(770) and ω(782). Correct high-Q^2 form factor behavior predicted by perturbative QCD is due to phenomenological logarithmic renormalization of electromagnetic coupling constants and linear superconvergence relations between the parameters of the meson spectrum. The model is found to be in good agreement with all the experimental data on Q^2-dependence of the transitions N-Δ(1232), N-N(1440), N-N(1520), N-N(1535). We present fit results and model predictions for high-energy experiments proposed by JLab. Besides, we make special emphasis on the transition to perturbative domain of N-Δ(1232) form factors.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model incorporating at least four vector mesons, linear superconvergence relations, and phenomenological logarithmic renormalization of coupling constants can reproduce the Q²-evolution of nucleon-to-resonance transition form factors across both perturbative and non-perturbative domains, with the hedge that the model uses a conventional but mathematically inconsistent Rarita-Schwinger interaction Lagrangian for spin-3/2 resonances.

## Method
The authors construct a VMD model where Lagrangian form factors for spin-3/2 resonances (three form factors F₁, F₂, F₃) and spin-1/2 resonances (two form factors G₁, G₂) are expressed as dispersionlike expansions with four or five poles corresponding to the lowest excitations of ρ(770) and ω(782) mesons. Correct high-Q² behavior from perturbative QCD is enforced through linear superconvergence relations between meson-spectrum parameters and phenomenological logarithmic renormalization of electromagnetic coupling constants, without invoking unphysical power suppression. The model is fitted to experimental helicity amplitude data for the transitions N→Δ(1232), N→N(1440), N→N(1520), and N→N(1535).

## Key numbers
- Experimental data on transitions NΔ(1232), NN(1440), NN(1520), NN(1535) are available up to Q² = 6 GeV² from exclusive experiments at JLab, MIT-Bates, MAMI and others.
- Inclusive SLAC measurements are available for Δ(1232) up to almost 10 GeV² and for N(1535) up to 21 GeV².
- Proposed JLab experiments aim to measure Δ(1232) and N(1535) multipole moments up to Q² = 14 GeV².
- The model requires at least four vector mesons to impose correct asymptotic behavior via superconvergence relations; the simplest four-meson model has only one independent parameter.
- The QCD scale parameter used is Λ ≈ Λ_QCD = 0.215 ± 0.025 GeV.
- For the non-spin-flip form factor F₁, the asymptotic behavior is F₁(Q²) ~ 1/(Q⁶ ln³(Q²/Λ²)) with n₁ ≈ 3.
- The asymptotic scaling predictions are: A₁/₂(Q²) ~ C_A(1/2)/Q³, A₃/₂(Q²) ~ C_A(3/2)/Q⁵, S̃₁/₂(Q²) ~ C_S/Q⁴.

## Context
This work extends the VMD approach previously applied to elastic eN scattering (Gari and Krümpelmann, 1985; Gari and Krümpelmann, 1992; Lomon, 2001; Lomon, 2002) and the lowest-resonance electroproduction (Wan and Iachello; Devenish, 1976) to the first four low-lying baryon resonances. It differs from the model of Dubnička (2003) by incorporating phenomenological logarithmic renormalization of vector-meson spectrum parameters and by neglecting continuum contributions. The model enables predictions for high-energy experiments proposed at JLab and provides a framework for describing the transition to the perturbative QCD regime, particularly for the N→Δ(1232) form factors, with further applications to nucleon axial and elastic form factors planned in subsequent publications (Vereshkov and Volchanskiy, 2007).
