# Analytic continuation of single-particle resonance energy and wave function in relativistic mean field theory

> Single-particle resonant states in spherical nuclei are studied by an analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method within the framework of the self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. Taking the neutron resonant state $ν1g_{9/2}$ in $^{60}$Ca as an example, we examine the analyticity of the eigenvalue and eigenfunction for the Dirac equation with respect to the coupling constant by means of a $\pade$ approximant of the second kind. The RMF-ACCC approach is then applied to $^{122}$Zr and, for the first time, this approach is employed to investigate both the energies, widths and wave functions for $l\ne 0$ resonant states close to the continuum threshold. Predictions are also compared with corresponding results obtained from the scattering phase shift method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method can be successfully combined with relativistic mean field (RMF) theory to extract both the energies, widths, and wave functions for single-particle resonant states in exotic nuclei, including for the first time states with nonzero orbital angular momentum (l ≠ 0) close to the continuum threshold. The authors hedge that the method's validity depends on the analyticity of the Dirac equation eigenvalue and eigenfunction with respect to the coupling constant, which they verify for the specific case of the ν1g9/2 state in ⁶⁰Ca.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for spherical nuclei within the RMF framework using a spherical box of radius R₀ = 20 fm and mesh size δr = 0.05 fm. They increase the attractive potential by a multiplicative coupling constant λ, causing resonant states to become bound for λ > λ₀ (the branch point), then use Padé approximants of the second kind (order L=N=4) to analytically continue the complex wave number k(λ) and wave functions φ(k,r) from the bound-state region (λ > λ₀) back to the physical resonance region (λ = 1). The resonance energy E and width Γ are extracted from the complex wave number via relativistic relations, and the inner wave function (r < R₀) is matched to Riccati-Hankel functions in the outer region where nuclear potentials vanish.

## Context

This work extends the ACCC method, previously applied to non-relativistic Schrödinger equations with Woods-Saxon potentials (Cattapan, 1990s) and to stable nuclei ¹⁶O and ⁴⁸Ca in RMF theory (Yang, 2001), to exotic nuclei near the neutron drip line. It enables the systematic study of single-particle resonant states in nuclei like ¹²²Zr, which is the core of the giant neutron halo predicted by relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory (Meng and Ring, 1998). The approach can be combined with BCS theory to describe exotic phenomena in unstable nuclei, providing an alternative to the scattering phase shift method (Sandulescu, 2000) that also yields complex wave functions for resonant states.
