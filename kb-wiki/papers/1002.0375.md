# Unstable Hadrons in Hot Hadron Gas in Laboratory and in the Early Universe

> We study kinetic master equations for chemical reactions involving the formation and the natural decay of unstable particles in a thermal bath. We consider the decay channel of one into two particles, and the inverse process, fusion of two thermal particles into one. We present the master equations the evolution of the density of the unstable particles in the early Universe. We obtain the thermal invariant reaction rate using as an input the free space (vacuum) decay time and show the medium quantum effects on $π+π\leftrightarrow ρ$ reaction relaxation time. As another laboratory example we describe the $K+K \leftrightarrow ϕ$ process in thermal hadronic gas in heavy-ion collisions. A particularly interesting application of our formalism is the $π^{0}\leftrightarrow γ+γ$ process in the early Universe. We also explore the physics of $π^{\pm}$ and $μ^{\pm}$ freeze-out in the Universe.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unstable hadrons in a hot thermal bath can remain in chemical equilibrium with their decay products for much longer than their vacuum lifetimes would suggest, because the inverse production reactions (two particles fusing into one) occur at rates comparable to the decay rates in the dense thermal medium. The key condition is that the thermal bath must maintain sufficiently high densities of the decay products to drive the reverse reaction.

## Method

The authors derive kinetic master equations for the time evolution of unstable particle densities in a thermal bath, considering the one-to-two particle decay channel and its inverse fusion process. They express the thermal reaction rates in terms of the vacuum decay lifetime, incorporating quantum statistical effects (Bose enhancement and Pauli blocking) through Uehling-Uhlenbeck factors in the phase space integrals. The formalism is applied to several specific reactions: π+π ↔ ρ in hot hadronic gas, K+K ↔ φ in heavy-ion collisions, π⁰ ↔ γ+γ in the early Universe, and π± ↔ μ±+ν processes.

## Key numbers

- π⁰ equilibration time is shorter than the Universe expansion time by approximately 14 orders of magnitude at T=10 MeV
- The vacuum decay lifetime of π⁰ is 8.4×10⁻¹⁷ s
- The Planck mass used in the expansion rate calculation is m_pl = 1.2211×10¹⁹ GeV
- For the ρ meson in the Boltzmann low-density limit, the chemical equilibration time is τ_ρ ≈ 1.7 fm; in the high-density quantum medium near hadronization temperature, this decreases to τ_ρ ≈ 1.25 fm
- The hadronization temperature boundary between quark-gluon plasma and hadron phase is near T_h = 170 MeV
- The effective degeneracy analysis covers the temperature range 5 < T < 200 MeV for the early Universe
- The lower temperature limit of validity for the study is approximately 6 MeV, below which particle-antiparticle symmetry breaks down

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of reaction relaxation times in laboratory e⁺e⁻γ plasma (Kuznetsova, 2008) to hadronic systems in both heavy-ion collisions and the early Universe. The paper provides the first detailed derivation of relaxation times for one-to-two particle reactions in thermal media, which had previously been less studied than the more common two-to-two reactions (Kolb and Turner, 1988). The results enable more accurate modeling of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions and improve understanding of particle abundance evolution in the early Universe, particularly for π⁰, π±, μ±, ρ, and φ mesons. The finding that π⁰ remains in chemical equilibrium with photons down to very low temperatures (3-4 MeV) contradicts the naive expectation that its short lifetime would cause early freeze-out.
