# A new semiempirical formula for exotic cluster decays of nuclei

> A new semiempirical formula, with only three parameters, is proposed for cluster decay half-lives. The parameters of the formula are obtained by making a least square fit to the available experimental data. The calculated half-lives are compared with an other model-independent scaling law proposed earlier by Horoi {\it et al}. Also, the calculated results of this formula are compared with the recent results of the preformed cluster model for $^{12}$C and $^{14}$C emissions from different deformed and superdeformed Nd and Gd parents. The results are in good agreement with experiments as well as other models.

## Key claim

A three-parameter semiempirical formula (the AZ-formula) can reproduce experimental cluster-decay half-lives with an rms deviation of 0.89, but the authors caution that the constants were fitted to data from the trans-actinide region and may not be equally reliable for predictions in the trans-tin region.

## Method

The authors propose a formula of the form log T₁/₂ = (a A₂ η + b Z₂ η_Z)/√Q + c, where A₂ and Z₂ are the cluster mass and charge, η and η_Z are mass and charge asymmetries, and Q is the decay energy. The three parameters a=10.603, b=78.027, and c=-80.669 were obtained by a least-squares fit to the available experimental cluster-decay half-lives alone. The results are compared with the six-parameter scaling law of Horoi et al. (1994) and with the preformed cluster model (PCM) of Gupta and collaborators for ¹²C and ¹⁴C emissions from deformed and superdeformed Nd and Gd parents.

## Key numbers

- The AZ-formula has three parameters: a=10.603, b=78.027, c=-80.669, obtained by least-squares fit to experimental cluster-decay data.
- The rms deviation of the AZ-formula fit is d_rms=0.89 (s).
- The truncated AF version (b=0) gives a=30.568, c=-51.348 with d_rms=1.652 (s); the truncated ZF version (a=0) gives b=112.197, c=-89.025 with d_rms=1.112 (s).
- The Horoi et al. (1994) scaling law uses six parameters: a₁=9.1, b₁=-10.2, a₂=7.39, b₂=-23.2, x=0.416, y=0.613, fitted to 119 α-decays and 11 cluster-decays from even-even parents.

## Context

This work extends the tradition of semiempirical decay laws that began with Geiger and Nuttal (1911) for α-decay and includes the recent scaling law of Horoi et al. (1994) which covers both α and cluster decays with six parameters. The new AZ-formula is simpler (three parameters) and fitted exclusively to cluster-decay data, showing better agreement with experiments than the Horoi et al. formula for the cases tested. The formula enables rapid predictions for new cluster-decay experiments, similar to the approach of Poenaru et al. (1991), and its predictions for ¹²C and ¹⁴C emissions from Nd and Gd parents show the same structural trends as the more elaborate preformed cluster model.
