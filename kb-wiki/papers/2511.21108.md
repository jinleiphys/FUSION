# The phases of QCD reached in terrestrial and cosmic colliders

> We review the current state of knowledge of the phase diagram of QCD through lattice, effective field theories, and chiral models. Several sections through the three dimensional phase diagram are known for $N_f=2+1$ with good precision. Due to technical advances in lattice techniques over the last decade or so, new aspects of the phase diagram can now be explored. We review current lattice results. The newly acquired knowledge can be used to reconstruct the full phase diagram for physical QCD, \ie, $N_f=1+1+1$. We remark on the computations which would help understand this better, and what the current constraints are on matter in neutron star cores. We also remark on the physics of the chiral transition and neutron stars in the 't Hooft large $N_c$ limit.

## Key claim
The paper argues that enough information is now available from lattice simulations to patch together the full phase diagram of physical QCD (with three distinct quark masses, $N_f=1+1+1$), but this reconstruction relies on several caveats, including the assumption that the baryon density at the zero-temperature transition is not high enough for color superconducting phases to manifest.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD results, effective field theories (specifically a thermal extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model organized by operator scaling dimension), and chiral models to constrain the phase diagram. Thermodynamic arguments and Gibbs' phase rules are used to connect results from different regions of the phase space, including the $T$-$\mu_B$ plane, the $\mu_Q$-$T$ plane, and the chiral limit. The review synthesizes data from multiple lattice collaborations and extrapolates results from $N_f=2+1$ to the physical $N_f=1+1+1$ case.

## Key numbers
- The crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is $T_{co}=156.5\pm1.5$ MeV (HotQCD Collaboration) or $158.0\pm0.6$ MeV (Borsanyi et al.).
- The curvature of the crossover line in the baryon chemical potential direction is $\kappa_2^B=0.015(4)$ (HotQCD) or $\kappa_2=0.0153(18)$ (Borsanyi et al.).
- The chiral critical temperature extrapolated to the chiral limit is $T_c=132^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV.
- The estimated zero-temperature transition baryon chemical potential for physical meson masses is $\mu_B^1=1280\pm170$ MeV (using HotQCD curvatures) or $1280\pm75$ MeV (using Borsanyi et al. curvatures).
- A recent lattice computation found a color superconducting gap $\Delta$ of roughly 300 MeV or lower for $\mu_I \ge 1500$ MeV.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing effort to map the QCD phase diagram, building on foundational work by Pisarski (1983) and the Columbia plot studies by Brown (1990) and Cuteri (2021). It synthesizes recent lattice results from the HotQCD Collaboration (2018) and Borsanyi et al. (2020) to provide a unified picture connecting heavy-ion collision experiments to neutron star interiors. The work enables future quantitative tests of effective field theories against lattice data and outlines specific computations needed to resolve the phase diagram's topology, such as the location of the critical endpoint and the behavior of the color superconducting gap.
