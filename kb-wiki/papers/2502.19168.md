# Chiral Representation of the Nucleon Mass at Leading Two-loop Order

> We calculate the nucleon mass in a manifestly relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory up to the leading two-loop order. Through dimensional counting analysis, we perform the chiral expansion and verify the validity of the extended-on-mass-shell scheme at the two-loop level. As a result, we obtain the complete chiral representation of the nucleon mass up to $\mathcal{O}(p^5)$, which preserves the original analytic properties and satisfies the correct power counting. The obtained chiral result is well-suited for chiral extrapolation and provides an excellent description of lattice QCD data across a broad range of pion masses. We find that the $\mathcal{O}(p^5)$ contribution is small, approximately $10$ MeV, and varies only mildly with increasing pion mass, demonstrating good convergence of the nucleon mass up to pion masses of about 350 MeV at two-loop order.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme is valid at the two-loop level in baryon chiral perturbation theory, yielding a complete chiral representation of the nucleon mass up to $\mathcal{O}(p^5)$ that preserves correct analytic properties and power counting, with the $\mathcal{O}(p^5)$ contribution being small (approximately 10 MeV) and varying mildly with increasing pion mass, showing good convergence up to pion masses of about 350 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the nucleon mass in manifestly relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory up to leading two-loop order ($\mathcal{O}(p^5)$) using the EOMS renormalization scheme. They performed dimensional counting analysis (strategy of regions) to identify and subtract power-counting-breaking terms, and used integration-by-parts reduction to express two-loop contributions in terms of 10 independent master integrals. Ultraviolet divergences were handled by splitting bare low-energy constants into renormalized parts and beta-function terms, with non-local subdivergences canceled by one-loop subdiagrams.

## Key numbers

* The $\mathcal{O}(p^5)$ contribution to the nucleon mass is approximately 10 MeV, varying only mildly with increasing pion mass
* Good convergence of the chiral expansion is demonstrated up to pion masses of about 350 MeV at two-loop order
* The number of independent two-loop master integrals after reduction is 10 (from an initial set of 26)
* For the special case $g=0$, the two-loop beta function for the nucleon mass in the chiral limit is $\beta_m^{(22)} = 9 m^5 / (32 F^4)$
* For the general case $g \neq 0$, the two-loop beta function for the nucleon mass in the chiral limit is $\beta_m^{(22)} = 9 (5 g^4 - 2 g^2 + 1) m^5 / (32 F^4)$

## Context

This work extends previous one-loop EOMS calculations in baryon chiral perturbation theory (Fuchs 2003, Yao 2016) to the two-loop level, and contrasts with earlier two-loop nucleon mass calculations using the heavy baryon formalism (McGovern 1998) and infrared regularization (Schindler 2006, 2007), which suffer from unphysical analytic properties or convergence breakdown above pion masses around 360 MeV. The full EOMS result preserves correct analytic structure and is suitable for chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD data across a broad range of pion masses, enabling more reliable extraction of the nucleon sigma term via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.
