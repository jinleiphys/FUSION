# Causal hydrodynamic fluctuation in Bjorken expansion

> We investigate effects of causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in the longitudinally expanding quark gluon plasma on final entropy distributions in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in a boost-invariant Bjorken-expanding quark-gluon plasma cause the final entropy per unit rapidity and transverse area to fluctuate event-by-event, with a small fraction of events showing final entropy lower than the initial entropy, which is not a violation of the second law but a manifestation of the fluctuation theorem.

## Method

The authors solve the causal (Israel-Stewart-type) stochastic relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a one-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, including Gaussian white noise terms for both shear stress and bulk pressure whose variances are set by fluctuation-dissipation relations. They evolve the system from a fixed initial temperature of 0.22 GeV at proper time 1 fm, using a parametrized cross-over equation of state and fixed transport coefficients (eta/s = 1/4 pi, zeta/s = 15(1/3 - c_s^2)), and simulate 2000 events to build the entropy distribution at late proper time.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: T0 = 0.22 GeV at initial proper time tau0 = 1 fm
- Transition temperature: Tc = 0.17 GeV with width Gamma = 0.02 Tc
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 1/4 pi
- Fluid element volume: Delta x = Delta y = 1 fm, Delta eta_s = 1, giving volume ~ 1 fm^3
- Time step: Delta tau = 0.1 fm
- Mean final entropy per unit rapidity and transverse area at tau = 50 fm: 28.06(15)/fm^2 for fluctuating hydrodynamics, compared to 28.05/fm^2 for viscous hydrodynamics without noise
- Initial entropy per unit rapidity and transverse area: s0 tau0 = 22.49/fm^2
- Normalized variance of final entropy distribution is inversely proportional to the volume, with width a few percent of initial entropy for volume ~ 1 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, previously formulated by Calzetta (1997), Kapusta (2011), Kumar (2013), and Murase (2013), by applying them specifically to the boost-invariant Bjorken expansion scenario (Bjorken, 1982). It contrasts with conventional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics that only include initial-state fluctuations of the transverse profile, showing that thermal fluctuations during the evolution also produce event-by-event variations in final entropy. The results enable future inclusion of hydrodynamic fluctuations in small colliding systems (proton-proton, proton-nucleus, peripheral nucleus-nucleus) where the inverse-volume scaling makes the effect significant.
