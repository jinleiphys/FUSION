# Is a color superconductor topological?

> A fully gapped state of matter, whether insulator or superconductor, can be asked if it is topologically trivial or nontrivial. Here we investigate topological properties of superconducting Dirac fermions in 3D having a color superconductor as an application. In the chiral limit, when the pairing gap is parity even, the right-handed and left-handed sectors of the free space Hamiltonian have nontrivial topological charges with opposite signs. Accordingly, a vortex line in the superconductor supports localized gapless right-handed and left-handed fermions with the dispersion relations E=+/-vp_z (v is a parameter dependent velocity) and thus propagating in opposite directions along the vortex line. However, the presence of the fermion mass immediately opens up a mass gap for such localized fermions and the dispersion relations become E=+/-v(m^2+p_z^2)^(1/2). When the pairing gap is parity odd, the situation is qualitatively different. The right-handed and left-handed sectors of the free space Hamiltonian in the chiral limit have nontrivial topological charges with the same sign and therefore the presence of the small fermion mass does not open up a mass gap for the fermions localized around the vortex line. When the fermion mass is increased further, there is a topological phase transition at m=(μ^2+Δ^2)^(1/2) and the localized gapless fermions disappear. We also elucidate the existence of gapless surface fermions localized at a boundary when two phases with different topological charges are connected. A part of our results is relevant to the color superconductivity of quarks.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a color superconductor can be topologically nontrivial, finding that the answer depends on the parity of the pairing channel and the fermion mass. In the chiral limit with parity-odd pairing, the system is topologically nontrivial and supports gapless vortex-line fermions that are robust against a small fermion mass, but a topological phase transition occurs at a critical mass \( m = \sqrt{\mu^2 + \Delta^2} \).

## Method

The author computes the \(\mathbb{Z}\)-valued topological charge (winding number) of the free-space mean-field Hamiltonian for superconducting Dirac fermions in 3D, using the symmetry class DIII classification. The Bogoliubov–de Gennes equation is solved for a singly quantized vortex line to find localized fermion modes, and their dispersion relations are obtained via perturbation theory in the fermion mass and momentum along the vortex. Boundary problems between phases with different topological charges are also analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Topological charge for even-parity pairing in the chiral limit: \( N_R = \Delta_0 / (2|\Delta_0|) \) and \( N_L = -\Delta_0 / (2|\Delta_0|) \), giving total \( N = 0 \).
- Topological charge for odd-parity pairing in the chiral limit: \( N_R = N_L = \Delta_0 / (2|\Delta_0|) \), giving total \( N = \Delta_0 / |\Delta_0| \).
- Topological phase transition for odd-parity pairing occurs at \( m = \sqrt{\mu^2 + \Delta_0^2} \); for \( m^2 < \mu^2 + \Delta_0^2 \), \( N = \Delta_0 / |\Delta_0| \); for \( m^2 > \mu^2 + \Delta_0^2 \), \( N = 0 \).
- Dispersion of vortex-line fermions for even-parity pairing with nonzero mass: \( E = \pm v \sqrt{m^2 + p_z^2} \), where \( v \) is a parameter-dependent velocity (Eq. 27).
- Dispersion of vortex-line fermions for odd-parity pairing: \( E = v p_z \) and \( E = -v p_z \), with \( v \) given by Eq. (45); velocity vanishes at \( m = \mu \).
- For a boundary where the pairing gap changes sign, gapless surface fermions have dispersion \( E = \pm |v| \sqrt{p_x^2 + p_y^2} \) in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the classification of topological superconductors (Schnyder 2008, Roy 2008, Qi 2009a) to the relativistic Dirac fermion systems relevant to color superconductivity in quark matter (Alford 2007). It parallels the known behavior of the 2D \( p_x + ip_y \) superconductor (Read 1999, Gurarie 2007), where a topological phase transition occurs as a function of chemical potential. The results enable future studies of topologically protected Majorana fermion modes in dense QCD matter and suggest that the color-flavor-locked phase may host such excitations under certain conditions.
