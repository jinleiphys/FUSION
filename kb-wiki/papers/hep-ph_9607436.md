# A New Approach to Chiral Perturbation Theory for Matter Fields

## Key claim

The paper proposes a prescription for separating hard- and soft-momentum contributions in effective field theories that allows a relativistic treatment of matter fields in chiral perturbation theory while preserving Weinberg's power counting, but only for the case when the nucleon is nearly on shell (requiring $k^2 \approx M^2$ rather than $k^2 = 0$).

## Method

The author defines an operator $\hat{S}$ that extracts the unrenormalized soft part of a Feynman diagram by taking loop momenta to be of order $Q$, making a covariant $Q/M$ expansion of the integrand, and exchanging the order of integration and summation of the resulting power series. The hard part $(G - \hat{R}\hat{S}G)$ is then absorbed into the parameters of the Lagrangian as local counterterm contributions, where $\hat{R}$ performs standard renormalization to remove ultraviolet divergences. The prescription is applied explicitly to the one-loop nucleon self-energy with a $\pi N$ intermediate state, and the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass and the $\pi N$ scattering amplitude are discussed.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant $f_\pi \approx 93$ MeV is used as a reference scale.
- The axial coupling $g_{\rm A} \approx 1.26$ appears in the one-loop self-energy calculation.
- The leading nonanalytic contribution to the nucleon mass from the one-loop self-energy is $-\frac{3 g_{\rm A}^2 m_\pi^3}{32\pi f_\pi^2}$, with corrections of order $m_\pi/(2\pi M)$.
- The soft part of the one-loop self-energy is of order $Q^3/M^2$, while the hard part contains terms of order $M$ and $Q^2/M$.
- A Feynman diagram with $L$ loops and $E_{\rm N}$ external baryon lines carries order $Q^\nu$ with $\nu = 2 + 2L - \frac{1}{2}E_{\rm N} + \sum_i V_i (d_i + \frac{1}{2}n_i - 2)$.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988), which failed to maintain power counting because anti-nucleon contributions were not absorbed into Lagrangian parameters. It contrasts with the heavy-baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991), which recovers power counting by integrating out the anti-nucleon field but requires a more complicated Lagrangian with $1/M$ corrections. The approach aims to combine the simplicity of the relativistic Lagrangian used by Gasser et al. with the consistent low-energy expansion of heavy-baryon ChPT, and is motivated by applications to finite-density nuclear matter where relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (Serot, 1986) has been efficient.
