# Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation for $A \le 71$ Revisited

> Accurate mass determination of short-lived nuclides by Penning-trap spectrometers and progress in the spectroscopy of proton-rich nuclei have triggered renewed interest in the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME). The energy levels of the members of $T=1/2, 1, 3/2,$ and 2 multiplets and the coefficients of the IMME are tabulated for $A\le 71$. The new compilation is based on the most recent mass evaluation (AME2011) and it includes the experimental results on energies of the states evaluated up to end of 2011. Taking into account the error bars, a significant deviation from the quadratic form of the IMME for the $A=9, 35$ quartets and the $A=32$ quintet is observed.

## Key claim
The paper compiles isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) coefficients for multiplets with mass number A ≤ 71, based on the AME2011 mass evaluation and experimental level schemes up to the end of 2011, and finds that while the quadratic IMME holds for most multiplets, significant deviations from the quadratic form are observed for the A=9 and A=35 quartets and the A=32 quintet when error bars are taken into account.

## Method
The authors collected experimental mass excesses from the AME2011 evaluation and energy levels from the National Nuclear Data Center online database, then performed least-square fits to the quadratic IMME (M = a + b T_z + c T_z^2) for all known T=1/2, 1, 3/2, and 2 multiplets with complete experimental data. For quartets and quintets, they also extracted cubic (d) and quartic (e) coefficients from the extended IMME to test for higher-order isospin-breaking effects. The compilation includes 382 doublets, 132 triplets, 25 quartets, and 7 quintets.

## Key numbers
- The weighted fit to all b coefficients gives b = -691.39(90) A^(2/3) + 1473.44(95) keV; the unweighted fit gives b = -723.4 A^(2/3) + 1927.9 keV.
- The neutron-hydrogen mass difference used is Δ_nH = 782.34664 keV.
- The d coefficient for the A=9 ground state quartet (J^π = 3/2^-) is confirmed as clearly non-zero, with values ranging from about 2 keV to about 100 keV across quartets.
- The d coefficient for the lowest-lying A=32 quintet is d = 0.83 ± 0.22 keV.
- The classical uniformly charged sphere estimate for the c coefficient is c = (3e^2)/(5 r_0) A^(-1/3), with e^2 = 1.44 MeV·fm.

## Context
This work extends the earlier IMME compilation by Britz et al. (1998) by incorporating recent high-precision Penning-trap mass measurements and updated level schemes, particularly for pf-shell nuclei up to A=71. The observed deviations from the quadratic IMME for the A=9 and A=35 quartets and the A=32 quintet point to isospin mixing effects, as previously discussed by Brodeur (2012) for A=9 and by Signoracci and Brown (2011) for A=32. The compilation enables systematic tests of isospin symmetry breaking in nuclear structure and provides input for shell-model studies of charge-dependent forces.
