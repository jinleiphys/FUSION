# Tetrahedral shape and surface density wave of $^{16}$O caused by $α$-cluster correlations

> $α$-cluster correlations in the $0^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are studied using the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, with which nuclear structures are described from nucleon degrees of freedom without assuming existence of clusters. The intrinsic states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O have triangle and tetrahedral shapes, respectively, because of the $α$-cluster correlations. These shapes can be understood as spontaneous symmetry breaking of rotational invariance, and the resultant surface density oscillation is associated with density wave (DW) caused by the instability of Fermi surface with respect to particle-hole correlations with the wave number $λ=3$. $^{16}$O($0^+_1$) and $^{16}$O($3^-_1$) are regarded as a set of parity partners constructed from the rigid tetrahedral intrinsic state, whereas $^{12}$C($0^+_1$) and $^{12}$C($3^-_1$) are not good parity partners as they have triangle intrinsic states of different sizes with significant shape fluctuation because of softness of the $3α$ structure. $E3$ transition strengths from the $3^-_1$ to $0^+_1$ states in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are also discussed.

## Key claim

The intrinsic states of $^{16}$O($0^+_1$) and $^{16}$O($3^-_1$) exhibit tetrahedral shapes due to $\alpha$-cluster correlations, and these two states can be regarded as a set of parity partners constructed from a single rigid tetrahedral intrinsic state, whereas the analogous triangle-shaped states in $^{12}$C are not good parity partners because of significant shape fluctuation and softness of the $3\alpha$ structure.

## Method

The authors applied the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with variation after spin-parity projection (AMD+VAP) to describe nuclear structures from nucleon degrees of freedom without assuming existence of clusters. They performed calculations for the $0^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, and analyzed the intrinsic density distributions, multipole decomposition of surface densities, occupation probabilities in harmonic oscillator shell-model expansion, and $E3$ transition strengths. They also provided a complementary analysis using the Brink-Bloch $\alpha$-cluster wave function to demonstrate the correspondence between cluster configurations and surface density waves.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters for $^{12}$C($0^+_1$): $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.31,0.13)$
- Quadrupole deformation parameters for $^{12}$C($3^-_1$): $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.33,0.11)$
- Quadrupole deformation parameters for $^{16}$O($0^+_1$) and $^{16}$O($3^-_1$): $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.24,0.09)$
- Experimental binding energy of $^{16}$O: 127.62 MeV; AMD+VAP 1-base calculation gives 122.9 MeV
- Experimental excitation energy of $^{16}$O($3^-_1$): 6.13 MeV; AMD+VAP 1-base calculation gives 9.4 MeV
- Experimental $B(E3; 3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ for $^{16}$O: 205(106) W.u.; AMD+VAP 1-base calculation gives 151 W.u. and 2-base calculation gives 163 W.u.
- Experimental $B(E3; 3^-_1\to 0^+_1)$ for $^{12}$C: 103(17) W.u.; AMD+VAP 1-base calculation gives 5.3 W.u., 2-base gives 41 W.u., and 23-base gives 61 W.u.
- Root-mean-square radius for $^{16}$O($0^+_1$) intrinsic state: $R_0=2.75$ fm; for $^{16}$O($3^-_1$): $R_0=2.77$ fm
- Root-mean-square radius for $^{12}$C($0^+_1$) intrinsic state: $R_0=2.45$ fm; for $^{12}$C($3^-_1$): $R_0=3.03$ fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD studies of $\alpha$-cluster correlations in $^{12}$C (Kanada-En'yo 2006) and their earlier finding of a tetrahedral shape in $^{16}$O (Kanada-En'yo 2012). It connects to the long history of cluster model studies by Wheeler (1937), Brink (1970), and Bauhoff (1984) who suggested tetrahedral $4\alpha$ configurations, and to recent first-principle calculations by Epelbaum (2013) that found tetrahedral $4\alpha$ configurations in $^{16}$O. The paper enables a unified understanding of triangle and tetrahedral shapes as surface density waves caused by particle-hole correlations with wave number $\lambda=3$, analogous to density wave phenomena in infinite nuclear matter and condensed matter systems.
