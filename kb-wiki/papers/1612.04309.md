# Equation of state of nuclear and neutron matter at third-order in perturbation theory from chiral EFT

> We compute from chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at third-order in perturbation theory including self-consistent second-order single-particle energies. Particular attention is paid to the third-order particle-hole ring-diagram, which is often neglected in microscopic calculations of the equation of state. We provide semi-analytic expressions for the direct terms from central and tensor model-type interactions that are useful as theoretical benchmarks. We investigate uncertainties arising from the order-by-order convergence in both many-body perturbation theory and the chiral expansion. Including also variations in the resolution scale at which nuclear forces are resolved, we provide new error bands on the equation of state, the isospin-asymmetry energy, and its slope parameter. We find in particular that the inclusion of third-order diagrams reduces the theoretical uncertainty at low densities, while in general the largest error arises from omitted higher-order terms in the chiral expansion of the nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The inclusion of third-order perturbative diagrams (particularly the particle-hole ring diagram, often neglected) reduces theoretical uncertainty at low densities, but the largest error in the equation of state arises from omitted higher-order terms in the chiral expansion of nuclear forces rather than from many-body perturbation theory truncation.

## Method

The authors computed the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at third-order in many-body perturbation theory using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO* (where N3LO* indicates incomplete treatment of three-body forces at that order). They employed self-consistent second-order single-particle energies and included the third-order particle-hole ring diagram alongside particle-particle and hole-hole diagrams, using both partial-wave decomposition and semi-analytic expressions for benchmark test interactions.

## Key numbers

- The third-order particle-hole diagram contributes |E^(3)_ph(rho_0)| ~ (1-2) MeV in symmetric nuclear matter from coarse-resolution chiral NN-potentials with cutoffs Lambda <= 500 MeV
- At nuclear matter saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3), the noninteracting Fermi gas kinetic energy is E_kin = 22.1 MeV
- For symmetric nuclear matter at rho_0, first-order contributions range from -30.1 MeV (Lambda=414 MeV) to -19.5 MeV (Lambda=500 MeV); second-order self-consistent contributions range from -9.7 MeV to -15.7 MeV
- The third-order particle-particle contribution at rho_0 ranges from +0.8 MeV (Lambda=414 MeV) to +2.2 MeV (Lambda=500 MeV); particle-hole contribution ranges from -0.3 MeV to -2.1 MeV
- For neutron matter at rho_n = 0.16 fm^-3, variations across cutoffs are about Delta E_n ~ 2 MeV; at rho_n = 0.25 fm^-3, variations are about Delta E_n ~ 3 MeV
- The ratio of differences between chiral orders for neutron matter is R_4^450 ~ 0.4 and R_4^500 ~ 0.8 for most densities
- For symmetric nuclear matter, there remains a Delta E ~ 6 MeV uncertainty in energy per particle at rho_0

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of nuclear matter equations of state from chiral effective field theory, particularly those by Hebeler (2010), Gandolfi (2012), and Sammarruca (2015), by including the often-neglected third-order particle-hole ring diagram and self-consistent second-order single-particle energies. The study addresses discrepancies between theoretical predictions of the isospin-asymmetry energy and its slope parameter, which tend to lie just outside experimental bands from Lattimer (2013, 2014). The results enable improved uncertainty quantification for neutron star and supernova modeling, and provide semi-analytic benchmarks for testing partial-wave decomposition codes.
