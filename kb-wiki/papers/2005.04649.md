# Dense baryonic matter with chiral imbalance and charged pion condensation

> After the first predictions in seventies that in dense nuclear matter, for example, in neutron stars there could be pion condensation phenomenon, it was shown that at least s-wave pion condensation is unlikely to occur in such a medium in different approaches including NJL model consideration with neutrality and $β$-equilibrium condition. Then it has been found a condition that can promote this phenomenon in dense baryonic matter, it is chiral imbalance, so now it is interesting if this generation can survive rather strict requirement of electric neutrality and $β$-equilibrium. Here in this paper the generation of pion condensation in dense baryonic matter by chiral imbalance is investigated. It is shown that electric neutrality and $β$-equilibrium conditions do not spoil this phenomenon and this leads to interesting applications for physics of neutron stars. There are several possible mechanisms of generation of chiral imbalance in dense matter. In a view of latest and forthcoming NICER results and the first observed and possibly new events of neutron star mergers it is rather interesting to explore how possible chiral imbalance in neutron star can influence and change EOS and, hence, mass-radius relation. It is a first step in that direction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral imbalance (via chiral isospin and/or chiral chemical potentials) can generate charged pion condensation in dense baryonic matter even under the strict constraints of electric charge neutrality and β-equilibrium, conditions previously thought to suppress this phenomenon for physical quark masses.

## Method

The authors use a (3+1)-dimensional Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with u and d quarks and massless electrons, incorporating baryon, electric charge, chiral isospin, and chiral chemical potentials. They impose β-equilibrium (μ_d = μ_u + μ_e) and local electric charge neutrality (⟨n_Q⟩ = 0) as constraints, then compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation to map out phase diagrams in the (ν₅, ν) plane for various values of baryon chemical potential μ and chiral chemical potential μ₅.

## Key numbers

- At μ = 500 MeV and μ₅ = 0 MeV, the charged pion condensation (PC_d) phase is realized for chiral isospin chemical potential ν₅ from 400 MeV up to 450 MeV (a range of about 50 MeV).
- At μ = 500 MeV and μ₅ = 150 MeV, the PC_d phase appears for ν₅ from 200 MeV to 370 MeV.
- At μ = 400 MeV and μ₅ = 300 MeV, the PC_d phase is realized for a range of ν₅ values spanning about 270 MeV.
- The charge neutrality line first crosses the PC_d phase at μ = 380 MeV for chiral isospin chemical potential values of several hundred MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies that predicted charged pion condensation in dense quark matter with isospin imbalance (eklim, 2001) but later showed it is forbidden under charge neutrality and β-equilibrium for physical quark masses (abuki, 2005). It builds on findings that chiral imbalance can promote pion condensation (ekk, 2015; ekkz, 2016) by now incorporating the astrophysically relevant neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions. The results enable future studies of how chiral imbalance in neutron stars could modify the equation of state and mass-radius relation, relevant to NICER observations and neutron star merger events.
