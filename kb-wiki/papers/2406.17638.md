# Towards Hypernuclei from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory

> Understanding the strong interactions within baryonic systems beyond the up and down quark sector is pivotal for a comprehensive description of nuclear forces. This study explores the interactions involving hyperons, particularly the $Λ$ particle, within the framework of nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT). By incorporating $Λ$ hyperons into the NLEFT framework, we extend our investigation into the $S = -1$ sector, allowing us to probe the third dimension of the nuclear chart. We calculate the $Λ$ separation energies ($B_Λ$) of hypernuclei up to the medium-mass region, providing valuable insights into hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon ($YNN$) interactions. Our calculations employ high-fidelity chiral interactions at N${}^3$LO for nucleons and extend it to $Λ$ hyperons with leading-order S-wave $YN$ interactions as well as $YNN$ forces constrained only by the $A=4,5$ systems. Our results contribute to a deeper understanding of the SU(3) symmetry breaking and establish a foundation for future improvements in hypernuclear calculations.

## Key claim

By incorporating Λ hyperons into nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with leading-order S-wave hyperon-nucleon interactions and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon forces constrained only by the A=4,5 systems, the authors calculate Λ separation energies for hypernuclei up to the medium-mass region, though the three-body forces require fitting to a limited set of hypernuclei and the hypertriton's shallow binding prevents precise determination of the corresponding low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors extended the NLEFT framework to the S=-1 sector by adding Λ hyperons to a high-fidelity chiral interaction at N³LO for nucleons, using leading-order S-wave ΛN contact interactions and ΛNN three-body forces. They employed the auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo method with impurity lattice Monte Carlo techniques, fitting the hyperon-nucleon low-energy constants to the unpolarized Λp→Λp cross section while ensuring the hypertriton remains a shallow bound state, and systematically analyzed 343 combinations of smeared ΛNN interactions to determine the optimal configuration.

## Key numbers

- Λ hyperon mass set to mΛ = 1115.68 MeV; nucleon mass set to mN = 938.92 MeV
- Singlet scattering length as = -2.89 fm and effective range rs = 3.28 fm for ΛN interactions
- Triplet scattering length at = -1.60 fm and effective range rt = 3.94 fm for ΛN interactions
- Local smearing parameter sL = 0.07 and non-local smearing parameter sNL = 0.5 for nucleon interactions
- Local smearing parameter sL = 0.5 used for three-body force smearing with ranges up to d = 3 (corresponding to 2.28 fm)
- Non-local smearing parameters sNL = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3 considered for three-body force variants
- Hypertriton Λ-d separation expected to be approximately 11 fm
- Box sizes up to L = 24 fm used in exact calculations for finite volume extrapolation

## Context

This work extends the NLEFT method previously applied to nucleonic systems (Elhatisari 2022) into the hypernuclear sector, building on earlier impurity lattice Monte Carlo studies (Frame 2020) that used simplified Wigner SU(4)-symmetric interactions for light hypernuclei. The calculations enable future structure analysis of hypernuclei using the pinhole algorithm similar to work done for carbon (Shen 2022), and provide a foundation for incorporating Σ⁰ mixing effects and explicit two-pion exchange interactions in future improvements.
