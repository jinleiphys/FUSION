# QCD at high baryon density in a random matrix model

> A high density diquark phase seems to be a generic feature of QCD. If so it should also be reproduced by random matrix models. We discuss a specific one in which the random matrix elements of the Dirac operator are supplemented by a finite chemical potential and by non-random elements which model the formation of instanton-anti-instanton molecules. Comparing our results to those found in a previous investigation by Vanderheyden and Jackson we find additional support for our starting assumption, namely that the existence of a high density diquark phase is common to all QCD-like models.

## Key claim

The existence of a high-density diquark (colour-superconducting) phase is a generic feature of QCD-like models, but the low-density part of the phase diagram is highly sensitive to the fraction of instanton-anti-instanton molecules and thus to detailed instanton dynamics, while the high-density region is robust.

## Method

The authors extend a random matrix model previously used by Vanderheyden and Jackson (2000) by replacing the uniform temperature term (first Matsubara frequency) with a diagonal matrix where a fraction α of entries take a fixed value d (modelling instanton-anti-instanton molecule formation) and the rest are zero. They derive the thermodynamic potential for chiral and diquark condensates via a saddle-point approximation, then solve the gap equations numerically to map phase diagrams in the μ–d plane for various values of α and the coupling ratio B/A.

## Key numbers

- For B/A = 0.75 (one-gluon-exchange and instanton-induced interaction), chiral symmetry restoration is first-order for small d (d < 1.57) when α = 1.
- For α = 1, the tricritical point disappears when α ≠ 1; the chiral transition becomes first-order for all values of d.
- For B/A = 1.4, the transition from the mixed phase (χΔ) to the diquark phase (Δ) is first-order for d < 1.1 when α = 1.
- For α = 1, the chiral condensate vanishes at zero density only if all instantons and anti-instantons form molecules (α = 1).
- For Nc = 2, the ratio B/A is necessarily equal to 1; at non-zero chemical potential the system always prefers diquark condensation over chiral symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends the random matrix model of Vanderheyden and Jackson (2000) by incorporating a more realistic treatment of temperature effects based on instanton-anti-instanton molecule formation, following the zero-density approach of Wettig, Schäfer, and Weidenmüller (1996). The results support the generic nature of the diquark phase found in NJL-like models (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1998) and instanton-based approaches (Rapp, Schäfer, Shuryak, Velkovsky 1998), while showing that the low-density chiral transition depends on the detailed instanton dynamics. The work enables future quantitative studies by identifying the need for realistic models of the molecule fraction α(μ,T) and strength d(μ,T) as functions of chemical potential and temperature.
