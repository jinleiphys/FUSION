# Unified treatment of sub-saturation stellar matter at zero and finite temperature

> The standard variational derivation of stellar matter structure in the Wigner-Seitz approximation is generalized to the finite temperature situation where a wide distribution of different nuclear species can coexist in the same density and proton fraction condition, possibly out of $β$-equilibrium. The same theoretical formalism is shown to describe on one side the single-nucleus approximation (SNA), currently used in most core collapse supernova simulations, and on the other side the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) approach, routinely employed in r- and p-process explosive nucleosynthesis problems. In particular we show that in-medium effects have to be accounted for in NSE to have a theoretical consistency between the zero and finite temperature modeling. The bulk part of these in-medium effects is analytically calculated and shown to be different from a van der Waals excluded volume term. This unified formalism allows controlling quantitatively the deviations from the SNA in the different thermodynamic conditions, as well as having a NSE model which is reliable at any arbitrarily low value of the temperature, with potential applications for neutron star cooling and accretion problems. We present different illustrative results with several mass models and effective interactions, showing the importance of accounting for the nuclear species distribution even at temperatures lower than 1 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the standard variational Wigner-Seitz treatment of stellar matter to finite temperature, showing that the single-nucleus approximation (SNA) and nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) approaches can be derived from the same theoretical formalism, provided in-medium effects are included in NSE to ensure consistency with the zero-temperature limit.

## Method

The authors map the energetics and composition of a microscopic Wigner-Seitz cell into a cluster model with density- and isospin-dependent modifications of the cluster surface energy, using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation. They derive variational equations for zero-temperature stellar matter without imposing beta-equilibrium, then extend to finite temperature by calculating the partition sum of independent cells, leading to generalized NSE equations with energy-space clusters as the correct degrees of freedom. A cut-off in the cluster density of states is applied to avoid double counting of scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium mass number for symmetric nuclei (I=0) in vacuum is given by A_eq = 2a_s/a_c, yielding A ≈ 55; at saturation density (ρ_el = ρ_0p = ρ_0/2), A_eq → ∞, recovering the homogeneous matter limit.
- The residual transition region for pasta phases exists at densities of order ρ_0/5 to ρ_0/3, depending on the interaction.
- The electron energy density expression is valid above 10^4 g·cm^{-3} where electrons may be considered free.
- A phenomenological pairing term Δ(A) = ±12/√A is used for even mass nuclei, where + corresponds to even-even and - to odd-odd nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the seminal zero-temperature Wigner-Seitz formalism of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) and Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (1971) to finite temperature, and addresses inconsistencies between the SNA models (e.g., Lattimer-Swesty 1991, Shen 1998) and NSE approaches (e.g., Hillebrandt and Wolff 1985, recent implementations by Hempel and others). The unified formalism enables quantitative control of deviations from SNA under different thermodynamic conditions and provides NSE models reliable at arbitrarily low temperatures, with potential applications to neutron star cooling and accretion problems.
