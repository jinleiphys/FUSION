# NNLO hard-thermal-loop thermodynamics for QCD

> We calculate the thermodynamic functions of a quark-gluon plasma for general N_c and N_f to three-loop order using hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory. At this order, all the ultraviolet divergences can be absorbed into renormalizations of the vacuum, the HTL mass parameters, and the strong coupling constant.We show that at three loops, the results for the pressure and trace anomaly are in very good agreement with recent lattice data down to temperatures T~2T_c.

## Key claim

The authors show that hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) yields thermodynamic functions for QCD that agree with lattice data down to temperatures T ~ 2 T_c, but only when using a perturbative mass prescription from dimensional reduction rather than a variational mass prescription, which would give complex results.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential for a quark-gluon plasma with general N_c and N_f to three-loop order using HTLpt, expanding in powers of m_D/T and m_q/T and keeping terms through order g_s^5. They renormalize ultraviolet divergences using local counterterms for the vacuum, the Debye and fermion masses, and the strong coupling constant, and determine the Debye mass from the NLO expression of three-dimensional electric QCD (EQCD) while setting the fermion thermal mass m_q = 0.

## Key numbers

- Pressure and trace anomaly agree with lattice data down to T ~ 2 T_c ~ 340 MeV for both N_f = 3 and N_f = 4
- The correction from NLO to NNLO reaches 100% near T_c
- For N_f = 3, three-loop running with Λ_MSbar = 344 MeV gives α_s(5 GeV) = 0.2034
- The central renormalization scale is μ = 2π T, varied by a factor of two for the uncertainty band
- The g_s^3 contribution to the QCD free energy is smaller than the g_s^2 contribution only if α_s ≤ 0.07, corresponding to T ~ 10^5 GeV or T ~ 5 × 10^5 T_c

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt approach previously applied to pure Yang-Mills theory (Strickland 2007) to include dynamical quarks, and compares with lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2010) and the hotQCD collaboration (Cheng 2008). The results show that including fermions gives much better agreement with lattice data than the pure-glue case, and the NNLO HTLpt results are consistent with a quasiparticle picture down to T ~ 2 T_c. The calculation is limited to order g_s^5 by the magnetic mass problem (Linde 1980), preventing extension to N^3LO without input from three-dimensional lattice calculations.
