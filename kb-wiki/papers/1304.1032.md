# CCQE, 2p2h excitations and ν-energy reconstruction

> We analyze the MiniBooNE muon neutrino CCQE-like dσ/dT_μ/dcosθ_μdata using a theoretical model that, among other nuclear effects, includes RPA correlations and 2p2h (multinucleon) mechanisms. These corrections turn out to be essential for the description of the data. We find that MiniBooNE CCQE-like data are fully compatible with former determinations of the nucleon axial mass M_A ~ 1.05 GeV. This is in sharp contrast with several previous analysis where anomalously large values of M_A ~ 1.4 GeV have been suggested. We also show that because of the the multinucleon mechanism effects, the algorithm used to reconstruct the neutrino energy is not adequate when dealing with quasielastic-like events. Finally, we analyze the MiniBooNE unfolded cross section, and show that it exhibits an excess (deficit) of low (high) energy neutrinos, which is an artifact of the unfolding process that ignores 2p2h mechanisms.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE CCQE-like data are fully compatible with the standard nucleon axial mass \(M_A \sim 1.05\) GeV, provided that 2p2h (multinucleon) mechanisms and RPA correlations are included in the theoretical model; this contradicts earlier analyses that extracted anomalously large values of \(M_A \sim 1.4\) GeV by fitting the same data with models that omitted those nuclear effects.

## Method

The authors computed the charged-current quasielastic-like double differential cross section \(d\sigma/dT_\mu d\cos\theta_\mu\) for muon neutrinos on \({}^{12}\)C using a microscopic model that sums the genuine one-nucleon QE contribution (including RPA polarization effects) and the 2p2h contribution from gauge-boson absorption by correlated nucleon pairs exchanging virtual mesons (\(\pi\), \(\rho\)). The nuclear model had no free parameters for the nuclear effects, as they were fixed in prior studies of photon, electron, and pion interactions with nuclei. The theoretical cross sections were then compared to the MiniBooNE flux-averaged data, and the neutrino energy reconstruction algorithm (Eq. 1) was tested by examining the distribution of true neutrino energies that produce a given reconstructed energy \(\ereco\).

## Key numbers

* The nucleon axial mass extracted from the fit to MiniBooNE CCQE-like data using the full model (including RPA and 2p2h) is \(M_A \sim 1.03 \pm 0.02\) GeV, consistent with the world average of about 1.05 GeV.
* In contrast, a fit using only the genuine QE contribution (without 2p2h) to the same data yields \(M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17\) GeV (from the MiniBooNE analysis).
* The MiniBooNE published unfolded cross section data were re-scaled by a factor of 0.89 to match the theoretical normalization in the right panel of Fig. 3.
* The 2p2h contribution to the total CCQE-like cross section on carbon is shown to be comparable in size to the genuine QE contribution at neutrino energies around 1 GeV (see Fig. 3, right panel).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic nuclear model of Nieves (2004) and Nieves (2011) for photon, electron, and pion reactions to neutrino scattering, and directly addresses the puzzle raised by MiniBooNE data that suggested an anomalously large axial mass. It agrees with the earlier work of Martini (2009) and Martini (2010) that first pointed out the importance of 2p2h contributions, but finds a factor-of-two difference in the size of the multinucleon effects compared to the Lyon group. The results also corroborate the phenomenological approach of Lalakulich (2012) and the SuSA+2p2h calculations of Amaro (2010, 2011). The paper enables improved neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation experiments by demonstrating that the standard QE-based algorithm (Eq. 1) is unreliable when 2p2h events are present, and it shows that the MiniBooNE unfolded cross section is an artifact of ignoring those mechanisms.
