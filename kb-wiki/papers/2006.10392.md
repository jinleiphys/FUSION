# Microscopic Calculation of $Λ-α$ Folding Potential

> We construct a folding potential between the $α$ and $Λ$ particles based on underlying nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions. Starting from a phenomenological $Λ$-N potential and a Gaussian form of the $α$-particle wave function we obtain for the built $α$-$Λ$ interaction a bound $^5_Λ$He state with the binding energy (3.10 MeV), which is consistent with recent experimental data $3.12 \pm 0.02$ MeV. When in turn an exact solution of the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation for the $α$-particle calculated with the CDBonn, Nijmegen or Argonne V18 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential is used and the phenomenological Gaussian $Λ$-N potential is replaced by the realistic (Nijmegen NSC97f) potential approximated by a rank-1 separable form, then $^5_Λ$He is overbound. In particular, its binding energy given by the folding potential generated with the $α$ particle wave function based on the CDBonn potential is 7.47 MeV. Although the rank-1 separable $Λ$-N potential reproduces the exact scattering length and the effective range of the original $Λ$-N potential, the overbinding results from the lack of the required repulsive properties in the assumed separable form.

# Microscopic Calculation of Λ-α Folding Potential

## Key claim
The folding potential constructed from realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions overbinds the ⁵_ΛHe hypernucleus, yielding binding energies far from the experimental value of 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV, even though the rank-1 separable Λ-N potentials reproduce the exact scattering length and effective range of the original potentials. The overbinding arises specifically from the lack of required repulsive properties in the assumed separable form, not from a failure to match low-energy scattering parameters.

## Method
The authors construct a Λ-α folding potential by evaluating matrix elements of the inner YN potential between products of the α-particle wave function and a plane wave for the Λ particle. The α-particle wave function is obtained either from a simple Gaussian form or from exact solutions of the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equation using the CD-Bonn, Nijmegen, or Argonne V18 realistic NN potentials. The Λ-N interaction is taken as either a phenomenological Gaussian potential (Hiyama et al.) or realistic meson-theoretical potentials (Chiral, Jülich, Nijmegen NSC97e/f, NSC89, ESC16, HC-D, Ehime) approximated by rank-1 separable forms of Yamaguchi or Gaussian type, with parameters fixed to reproduce the scattering length and effective range in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ partial waves.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of ⁵_ΛHe using the phenomenological Hiyama Λ-N potential with Gaussian α wave function: 3.10 MeV, consistent with the experimental value of 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV.
- Binding energy using the CD-Bonn α wave function with the Nijmegen NSC97f Λ-N potential (Gaussian separable form): 7.47 MeV, showing overbinding by more than 4 MeV.
- Binding energies using realistic Λ-N potentials with realistic α wave functions range from approximately 5.15 MeV (Nijmegen NSC89, Yamaguchi form, AV18 α) to 9.26 MeV (Chiral Λ=600, Gaussian form, RGM α), all exceeding the experimental value.
- The Hiyama Λ-N potential has scattering lengths of -1.28 fm (¹S₀) and -0.67 fm (³S₁) with effective ranges of 2.33 fm and 3.08 fm respectively (Λ-neutron, charge-independent).
- The Nijmegen NSC97f Λ-N potential has scattering lengths of -2.68 fm (¹S₀) and -1.67 fm (³S₁) with effective ranges of 3.07 fm and 3.34 fm respectively (Λ-neutron).

## Context
This work extends the study of light hypernuclei using cluster models, where phenomenological Λ-α potentials of simple Gaussian type have been used previously to study double-Λ hypernuclei (Hiyama et al., 1997) and the Σ-Σ-α system (Oo and Kamada, earlier work). The paper directly addresses the known problem that phenomenological central Λ-N potentials overbind ⁵_ΛHe, which is well known for its anomalously small binding energy. The results contradict the expectation that using more realistic inputs (exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky α wave functions and realistic Λ-N potentials) would improve agreement with experiment; instead, these inputs produce stronger overbinding. The work enables future studies using higher-rank separable approximations or direct use of unabbreviated Λ-N forces to incorporate the necessary repulsive components.
