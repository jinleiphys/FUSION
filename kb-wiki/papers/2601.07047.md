# Unfolding of exotic near-threshold structure and decay dynamics in $^{17}$B

> Neutron-rich boron isotopes provide a valuable testing ground for threshold-driven structure and reaction phenomena, including halo formation and exotic decay modes. In particular, the structure of $^{17}\mathrm{B}$ and its relation to unbound $^{16}\mathrm{B}$ are of special interest. The $^{16}\mathrm{B}$ nucleus is slightly unbound by approximately $50~\mathrm{keV}$, while $^{17}\mathrm{B}$ is bound with a neutron separation energy of about $1.4-1.6~\mathrm{MeV}$. The observation of a $1640~\mathrm{keV}$ $γ$ ray in $^{17}\mathrm{B}$, which we argue originates from a $1/2^-$ excited state, points to a remarkable situation in which $γ$ decay and two-neutron decay can compete. We analyze and identify the main reasons for this competition: $L=2$ emission of the neutron pair, and structural realignment driven by the proximity of the one-body threshold, in particular the nearby $s$-wave neutron decay channel. The decay is a unique near-threshold $L=2$ process in which multiple structural components contribute, each with coexisting direct and virtual sequential amplitudes whose interference governs the observables. Because threshold dynamics, continuum coupling, and interference of multiple quantum pathways are universal, closely related scenarios arise in ultracold atoms near Feshbach resonances, few-body atomic and molecular breakups, mesoscopic and photonic open systems, and other areas where open-quantum-system effects impact observables. We employ advanced theoretical models to study this first-of-its-kind case and provide a coherent theoretical perspective based on configuration interaction and complex-energy formalisms that incorporate both reaction continuum and structural effects near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed 1640 keV γ ray in $^{17}$B originates from a $1/2^-$ excited state that lies above the two-neutron separation threshold, creating a competition between γ decay and two-neutron decay that is enabled by the $L=2$ character of the neutron pair emission and structural realignment from the nearby $s$-wave one-neutron decay channel.

## Method

The authors employ configuration interaction shell model (SM) calculations with the YSOX Hamiltonian and Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations with a chiral effective interaction to describe the structure of $^{17}$B states. They then analyze the two-neutron decay dynamics using a Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) three-body formalism that treats $^{17}$B as an inert $^{15}$B core plus two valence neutrons, expanding the wave function in hyperspherical harmonics with Berggren ensemble for the hyperradial coordinate. The γ-decay widths are computed from reduced transition probabilities using experimental γ-ray energies, and spectroscopic factors for sequential decay paths through $^{16}$B intermediate states are evaluated in both SM and GSM frameworks.

## Key numbers

* $^{16}$B is unbound by approximately 50 keV (abstract) or 40–100 keV (text), with a $3^-$ ground-state resonance at 46(3) keV
* $^{17}$B has a neutron separation energy of about 1.4–1.6 MeV, with two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}=1.38(21)$ MeV
* Observed γ rays in $^{17}$B: 1089 keV (from $5/2^-$ first excited state) and 1640 keV (from $1/2^-$ state)
* SM-predicted $B(M1)$ for $1/2^-_1 \rightarrow 3/2^-_1$ transition: 0.305 $\mu_N^2$ (SM) and 0.227 $\mu_N^2$ (GSM), giving γ width $\Gamma = 1.56 \times 10^{-8}$ MeV
* SM pair-removal spectroscopic factor from $^{17}$B($1/2^-$) to $^{15}$B($3/2^-$) ground state: approximately 0.6, with 55% $(0d_{5/2})^2$ and 38% $(0d_{5/2})^1(1s_{1/2})^1$ configurations
* $s$-wave spectroscopic factor for $^{17}$B($1/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{16}$B($0^-$): 0.34 (SM) and 0.69 (GSM)
* $d$-wave spectroscopic factor for $^{17}$B($1/2^-$) $\rightarrow$ $^{16}$B($3^-$): 0.11 (SM) and 0.33 (GSM)

## Context

This work extends the study of threshold-driven nuclear structure in neutron-rich boron isotopes, building on experimental observations of $^{15}$B as a two-neutron halo nucleus (Kalpakchieva 2000, Kanungo 2005) and recent measurements of $^{16}$B and $^{17}$B states (Mozumdar 2025, Yang 2021). The analysis uses the YSOX shell-model Hamiltonian (Yuan 2012) and the Gamow shell model with chiral interactions (Michel 2002, 2009, 2021) to address the competition between γ and neutron decay near threshold. The results enable future experimental studies of two-neutron angular and energy correlations as probes of the $s$-wave component tied to halo structure, and connect to universal threshold phenomena seen in ultracold atoms near Feshbach resonances and few-body breakups.
