# Exclusive diffractive photon bremsstrahlung at high energies

> We discuss the $p p \to p p γ$ reaction at the LHC. We consider diffractive classical bremsstrahlung mechanisms including effects of non point-like nature of protons. In addition, we take into account (vector meson)-pomeron, photon-pion as well as photon-pomeron exchange processes for the first time in the literature. The integrated diffractive bremsstrahlung cross section ($E_γ>100$ GeV) is only of the order of $μ$b. We try to identify regions of the phase space where one of the mechanisms dominates. The classical bremsstrahlung dominates at large forward/backward photon pseudorapidities, close to the pseudorapidities of scattered protons. In contrast, the photon-pomeron (pomeron-photon) mechanism dominates at midrapidities but the related cross section is rather small. In comparison the virtual-omega--rescattering mechanism contributes at smaller angles of photons (larger photon rapidities).

## Key claim

The paper argues that exclusive diffractive photon bremsstrahlung in \(pp \to pp\gamma\) at LHC energies is dominated by classical bremsstrahlung at large forward/backward photon pseudorapidities, but the integrated cross section for photons above 100 GeV is only of order microbarns; the photon-pomeron exchange mechanism dominates at midrapidities but with a much smaller cross section, and the virtual-omega rescattering contributes at smaller photon angles.

## Method

The authors calculate amplitudes for several diffractive mechanisms: classical bremsstrahlung from initial- and final-state protons (driven by pomeron exchange), virtual-omega rescattering, photon-pion exchange, and photon-pomeron exchange. They include off-shell form factors for non-point-like protons with a cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_N\) and use Donnachie-Landshoff pomeron parameters fixed to elastic \(\pi N\) and \(NN\) scattering. Differential cross sections are computed at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV as functions of photon energy, transverse momentum, pseudorapidity, and the relative azimuthal angle between outgoing protons.

## Key numbers

- Integrated diffractive bremsstrahlung cross section for \(E_\gamma > 100\) GeV is of order \(\mu\)b.
- Classical bremsstrahlung dominates at photon pseudorapidities \(|\eta_\gamma| > 8.5\) (the CMS ZDC acceptance region).
- The photon-pomeron exchange mechanism dominates for \(\eta_\gamma < 6\), but its cross section is "rather small."
- Two values of the off-shell form factor cut-off parameter are used: \(\Lambda_N = 0.8\) GeV and \(\Lambda_N = 1\) GeV, producing the lower and upper bounds on the classical bremsstrahlung cross section.
- The classical bremsstrahlung distribution in relative azimuthal angle \(\phi_{12}\) between outgoing protons shows a large enhancement at back-to-back configurations (\(\phi_{12} = \pi\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of exclusive \(pp \to pp\gamma\) by Khoze (who gave only approximate formulas treating protons as point-like) and is similar in approach to the authors' previous work on \(pp \to pp\omega\) (CLSS) and \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) (LS13_pi0). The diffractive bremsstrahlung mechanisms are analogous to the Drell-Hiida-Deck mechanism for diffractive \(\pi N\) production. The results enable experimental tests using the ZDC detectors (for forward photons) and ALFA or TOTEM detectors (for scattered protons) at the LHC, and could help determine hadronic and electromagnetic off-shell form factors.
