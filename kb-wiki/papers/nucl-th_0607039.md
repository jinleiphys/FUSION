# Neutron Rich Nuclei in Heaven and Earth: Proceedings of the International Conference on Current Problems in Nuclear Physics and Atomic Energy

> The nucleus of 208Pb -- a system that is 18 order of magnitudes smaller and 55 orders of magnitude lighter than a neutron star -- may be used as a miniature surrogate to establish important correlations between its neutron skin and several neutron-star properties. Indeed, a nearly model-independent correlation develops between the neutron skin of 208Pb and the liquid-to-solid transition density in a neutron star. Further, we illustrate how a measurement of the neutron skin in 208Pb may be used to place important constraints on the cooling mechanism operating in neutron stars and may help elucidate the existence of quarks stars.

## Key claim

A nearly model-independent correlation exists between the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and the liquid-to-solid transition density in a neutron star, such that an accurate measurement of the neutron skin (to within 0.05 fm via the PREX experiment) can constrain neutron-star properties; however, the specific predicted value of the neutron skin (0.21 fm for the FSUGold model) is presented as an upper bound that could be revised downward with further softening of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The author constructed a relativistic mean-field model (FSUGold) with nonlinear meson interactions, fitted to binding energies and charge radii of magic nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, 116Sn, 132Sn, 208Pb) and constrained by collective modes (giant monopole resonance in 208Pb and 90Zr, isovector giant dipole resonance in 208Pb) using relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) calculations. The equation of state from this model was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron-star structure, assuming a uniform liquid core of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons in beta equilibrium with a polytropic interpolation to the outer crust.

## Key numbers

- Neutron radius of 208Pb: Rn ≈ 5.7 fm (stated as approximate value)
- Charge radius of 208Pb known to better than 0.001 fm; neutron radius uncertainty estimated at about 0.2 fm
- PREX aims to measure neutron radius of 208Pb to within 0.05 fm
- FSUGold predicts neutron skin of 208Pb: Rn - Rp = 0.21 fm (compared to NL3 prediction of 0.28 fm)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 230 MeV for FSUGold, K = 271 MeV for NL3
- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.148 fm^-3 for both models
- Symmetry energy at saturation: S0 = 32.7 MeV for FSUGold, 36.9 MeV for NL3
- Symmetry energy density dependence parameter: γ = 0.64 for FSUGold, γ = 0.98 for NL3
- FSUGold neutron-star radius for 1.4 solar-mass star: R = 12.66 km (NL3: 15.05 km)
- FSUGold maximum neutron-star mass: Mmax = 1.72 solar masses (NL3: 2.78 solar masses)
- FSUGold threshold density for direct URCA process: ρ_URCA = 0.47 fm^-3 (NL3: 0.21 fm^-3)
- FSUGold minimum mass for direct URCA cooling: M_URCA = 1.30 solar masses (NL3: 0.84 solar masses)
- FSUGold transition density from uniform to nonuniform matter: ρc = 0.076 fm^-3 (NL3: 0.052 fm^-3)
- GMR centroid energy in 208Pb: FSUGold predicts 14.04 MeV (experiment: 14.17 ± 0.28 MeV)
- GMR centroid energy in 90Zr: FSUGold predicts 17.98 MeV (experiment: 17.89 ± 0.20 MeV)
- IVGDR peak energy in 208Pb: FSUGold predicts 13.07 MeV (experiment: 13.30 ± 0.10 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the correlation between neutron skin thickness and neutron-star properties first established by Brown (2000) and Horowitz (2001), using a new relativistic parameter set (FSUGold from Todd-Rutel, 2005) that improves upon the NL3 parameter set of Lalazissis (1996, 1999) by incorporating constraints from collective modes. The results bring covariant meson-baryon predictions for the neutron skin closer to nonrelativistic Skyrme parameterizations (Furnstahl, 2001), and the paper anticipates that the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory will provide a model-independent measurement of the neutron skin of 208Pb, enabling constraints on neutron-star cooling mechanisms and potentially revealing the existence of quark stars.
