# Light-Front Quantum Chromodynamics: A framework for the analysis of hadron physics

> An outstanding goal of physics is to find solutions that describe hadrons in the theory of strong interactions, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). For this goal, the light-front Hamiltonian formulation of QCD (LFQCD) is a complementary approach to the well-established lattice gauge method. LFQCD offers access to the hadrons' nonperturbative quark and gluon amplitudes, which are directly testable in experiments at existing and future facilities. We present an overview of the promises and challenges of LFQCD in the context of unsolved issues in QCD that require broadened and accelerated investigation. We identify specific goals of this approach and address its quantifiable uncertainties.

## Key claim

Light-front QCD (LFQCD) is a complementary *ab initio* approach to lattice QCD for solving hadron structure, but it faces unresolved challenges including vacuum complexities, zero-mode contributions, Fock-space truncation, and the need for systematic regularization and renormalization schemes before it can deliver quantitative predictions.

## Method

The paper reviews the light-front Hamiltonian formulation of QCD, where quantization occurs at fixed light-cone time τ = t + z/c rather than ordinary time t, yielding boost-invariant light-front wave functions (LFWFs) as eigensolutions. It surveys applications to hadron structure, QCD at finite temperature and density, nuclear reactions, and intense-field QED, and discusses relationships with lattice QCD, Dyson-Schwinger approaches, AdS/QCD holography, and methods such as Discretized Light Cone Quantization (DLCQ) and basis light-front quantization (BLFQ).

## Key numbers

- Seven kinematic (interaction-independent) generators of Poincaré transformations are available in LFQCD, compared to only six in other relativistic Hamiltonian frameworks.
- The AdS/QCD correspondence yields a light-front Schrödinger equation with an effective confining potential U(ζ²) that takes the form of a harmonic oscillator when chiral QCD action remains conformally invariant.
- The impact variable ζ² = b²⊥ x(1-x) measures the physical separation of quark constituents within a hadron at fixed light-cone time τ and is conjugate to the invariant mass squared M²q̄q.

## Context

This white paper, produced by the ILCAC Working Group in 2013, surveys the LFQCD approach as an alternative to lattice QCD (which provides masses but not wave functions) and Dyson-Schwinger methods (formulated in Euclidean space). It extends earlier work on Dirac's front form of Hamiltonian dynamics (Dirac, 1949) and connects to recent developments in AdS/CFT duality (Maldacena, 1997) and AdS/QCD models (Brodsky and de Téramond, 2008). The paper identifies specific goals for the field, including developing symmetry-preserving regularization schemes, constructing computer codes for eigenvalue problems, and establishing theoretical bounds on truncation errors.
