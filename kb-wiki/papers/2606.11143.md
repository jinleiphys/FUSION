# Hindered $ΔK=1$ Dipole Strength in octupole bands in $N=90$ $^{154}$Gd from Lifetime Measurements with $γ-γ$ fast timing technique

> The lifetimes of the low-lying negative-parity $1^-$ state at 1414~keV and $2^-$ state at 1398~keV in $^{154}$Gd have been measured using the $γ$--$γ$ fast-timing technique with the VENTURE array at VECC, Kolkata. The states were populated through the $β$ decay of $^{154}$Tb, produced in proton-induced reactions at the K130 cyclotron. From the measured lifetimes, absolute $B(E1)$ transition strengths were deduced. The extracted $B(E1)$ values are compared with those of neighboring Gd isotopes and with Gogny-HFB-based $sdf$-IBM calculations. The results show that the measured $E1$ strengths from these states are strongly hindered compared with the corresponding $ΔK=0$ transitions, providing evidence for weak $ΔK=1$ dipole strength in $^{154}$Gd.

## Key claim

The measured absolute $B(E1)$ transition strengths from the $1^-$ state at 1414 keV and the $2^-$ state at 1398 keV in $^{154}$Gd are strongly hindered compared to the corresponding $\Delta K=0$ transitions, providing evidence for weak $\Delta K=1$ dipole strength; the authors interpret these states as members of the $K^\pi=1^-$ band based on the similarity of their $E1$ strengths, which are about four orders of magnitude smaller than the $B(E1)$ value for the $1^-$ state at 1241 keV assigned to the $K^\pi=0^-$ band.

## Method

Lifetimes of the $1^-$ state at 1414 keV and the $2^-$ state at 1398 keV in $^{154}$Gd were measured using the $\gamma$-$\gamma$ fast-timing technique with the VENTURE array (eight CeBr$_3$ scintillators and two Compton-suppressed Clover HPGe detectors) at VECC, Kolkata. The states were populated via $\beta$ decay of $^{154}$Tb, produced through the $^{154}$Gd(p,n)$^{154}$Tb reaction at the K130 cyclotron. Lifetimes were extracted using the Generalized Centroid Difference (GCD) method, and absolute $B(E1)$ transition strengths were deduced from the measured lifetimes using known branching and mixing ratios.

## Key numbers

* Half-life of the $2^-$ state at 1398 keV in $^{154}$Gd: $t_{1/2} = 46(5)$ ps, yielding $B(E1; 2^- \rightarrow 2^+) = 2.4 \times 10^{-6}$ W.u. and $B(M2) = 8.4 \times 10^{-3}$ W.u.
* Half-life of the $1^-$ state at 1414 keV in $^{154}$Gd: $t_{1/2} = 35(5)$ ps, yielding $B(E1; 1^- \rightarrow 0^+) = 5.1 \times 10^{-7}$ W.u. and $B(E1; 1^- \rightarrow 2^+) = 2.4 \times 10^{-6}$ W.u.
* For comparison, the $B(E1; 1^- \rightarrow 0^+)$ value for the $1^-$ state at 1241 keV (assigned to $K^\pi=0^-$) is $4.1 \times 10^{-2}$ W.u., about four orders of magnitude larger than the $E1$ strengths from the $K^\pi=1^-$ states.
* Half-life of the $4^+$ state in the ground-state band measured as a validation: $t_{1/2} = 65(5)$ ps, consistent with the literature value of 61(4) ps from earlier $\gamma$-$\gamma$ delayed coincidence work.

## Context

This work provides the first lifetime measurements for the $2^-$ state at 1398 keV and the $1^-$ state at 1414 keV in $^{154}$Gd, filling a gap in the systematics of $\Delta K=1$ dipole strengths in the Gd isotopic chain at $N=90$. The results extend the earlier Coulomb-excitation study by Sugawara et al., which reported sizable dipole strength for the $K^\pi=0^-$ band but no comparable enhancement for the $K^\pi=1^-$ structure, and are compared with Gogny-HFB-based $sdf$-IBM calculations by Nomura et al. (2015). The measured hindrance of $\Delta K=1$ transitions supports the $K$ assignments proposed by Meyer et al. and adopted in the ENSDF decay datasets, and enables quantitative band-mixing analyses to understand the role of Coriolis coupling and configuration mixing in suppressing $\Delta K=1$ $E1$ strength in this mass region.
