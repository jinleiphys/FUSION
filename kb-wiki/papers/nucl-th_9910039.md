# Charge exchange in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Elastic charge-exchange in relativistic heavy ion collisions is responsible for the non-disruptive change of the charge state of the nuclei. We show that it can be reliably calculated within the eikonal approximation for the reaction part. The formalism is applied to the charge-pickup cross sections of 158 GeV/nucleon Pb projectiles on several targets. The relative contributions of pion- and rho-exchange are determined, using a single-particle model for the internal structure of the nuclei. The calculated cross sections are non-negligible for heavy targets. It is shown that these cross sections can be useful to obtain information on the Gamow-Teller transition strengths of the nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that elastic charge-pickup cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be reliably calculated using an eikonal approximation with pion and rho exchange, and that these cross sections are proportional to the Gamow-Teller transition strengths of the nuclei involved, provided the reaction is peripheral and dominated by surface nucleons.

## Method

The authors used the eikonal approximation to describe the reaction part of charge-exchange in relativistic heavy ion collisions, with the interaction mediated by pion and rho exchange potentials. They applied a single-particle model for the internal nuclear structure, using Woods-Saxon or Gaussian density distributions, and employed the "t-rho-rho" approximation for the transparency function. The formalism was applied to calculate charge-pickup probabilities and cross sections for 158 GeV/nucleon Pb projectiles on C, Al, Cu, Sn, Au, and Pb targets, with the matrix elements evaluated using a surface approximation that averages over participating surface nucleons.

## Key numbers

* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + C: 0.0106 mb
* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + Al: 0.0394 mb
* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + Cu: 0.105 mb
* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + Sn: 0.253 mb
* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + Au: 0.572 mb
* Charge-pickup cross section per participant nucleon squared for Pb (158 GeV/nucleon) + Pb: 0.587 mb
* Pion coupling constant J_pi: approximately 400 MeV fm^3
* Rho coupling constant J_rho: approximately 790 MeV fm^3
* Nucleon-nucleon cross section at 158 GeV/nucleon: 52 mb
* Peak charge-pickup probability for Pb + Pb: about 10^-5

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approach developed by Bertulani (1993) for charge-exchange in nucleus-nucleus scattering to relativistic energies, specifically for charge-pickup reactions. It builds on earlier studies of charge-exchange at lower energies, such as the (p,n) reactions at 100 MeV by Taddeucci et al. (1987) and heavy-ion reactions at 100 MeV/nucleon by Osterfeld et al. (1992), which also showed proportionality to Gamow-Teller strengths. The results enable future experiments at CERN and elsewhere to extract beta-decay transition strengths from charge-pickup cross sections, provided backgrounds such as charge-exchange with pion production can be distinguished.
