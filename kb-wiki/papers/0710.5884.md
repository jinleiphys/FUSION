# Neutrino Interaction Calculations from MeV to GeV Region

> The Quasi-Elastic (QE) contribution of the nuclear inclusive electron model developed in Nucl. Phys. A627 543 (1997) is extended to the study of neutrino/antineutrino Charged Current (CC) and Neutral Current (NC) induced nuclear reactions at intermediate energies. Long range nuclear (RPA) correlations, Final State Interaction (FSI) and Coulomb corrections are included within the model. RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role in the whole range (100--500 MeV) of studied neutrino energies. Results for inclusive muon capture for different nuclei through the Periodic Table are also discussed. In addition, and by means of a Monte Carlo cascade method to account for the rescattering of the outgoing nucleon, we also study the CC and NC inclusive one nucleon knockout reactions off nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously developed nuclear inclusive electron model to neutrino and antineutrino charged-current and neutral-current reactions, but the model's validity is explicitly qualified to the intermediate energy range of 100-500 MeV where long-range RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role.

## Method

The model incorporates long-range nuclear RPA correlations, final state interactions (FSI) via a Monte Carlo cascade method for outgoing nucleon rescattering, and Coulomb corrections. The quasi-elastic contribution from the electron scattering model of Amaro et al. (1997) is adapted to charged-current and neutral-current neutrino/antineutrino reactions. Inclusive muon capture calculations are performed for various nuclei across the Periodic Table.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear inclusive electron model developed by Amaro et al. (1997) in Nucl. Phys. A627 543 to neutrino and antineutrino reactions. The model enables calculations of both inclusive and one-nucleon knockout reactions at intermediate energies, providing a framework that can be compared with future experimental data from neutrino oscillation experiments in the MeV to GeV region.
