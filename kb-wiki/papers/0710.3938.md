# Multiplicity Fluctuations in Limited Segments of Momentum Space in Statistical Models

> Multiplicity fluctuations in limited segments of momentum space are calculated for a classical pion gas within the statistical model. Results for the grand canonical, canonical, and micro-canonical ensemble are obtained, compared and discussed. We demonstrate that even in the large volume limit correlations between macroscopic subsystems due to energy and momentum conservation persist. Based on the micro-canonical formulation we make qualitative predictions for the rapidity and transverse momentum dependence of multiplicity fluctuations. The resulting effects are of similar magnitude as the predicted enhancement due to a phase transition from a quark-gluon plasma to a hadron gas phase, or due to the critical point of strongly interacting matter, and qualitatively agree with recently published preliminary multiplicity fluctuation data of the NA49 SPS experiment.

## Key claim
Multiplicity fluctuations in limited momentum-space segments, quantified by the scaled variance, depend nontrivially on the location and size of the observed segment when energy and momentum conservation are enforced in the micro-canonical ensemble, even in the large-volume limit. This effect is of similar magnitude to the predicted enhancement from a quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas phase transition or from the critical point of strongly interacting matter, and qualitatively agrees with preliminary NA49 SPS data.

## Method
The author calculates multiplicity distributions of negatively charged pions in a classical ideal Boltzmann pion gas within the grand canonical, canonical, and micro-canonical ensembles. For the micro-canonical ensemble, an asymptotic large-volume solution is used, expressing the scaled variance as a ratio of determinants of second-rank cumulant matrices that encode correlations from conserved charge, energy, and momentum. The analysis considers static, homogeneous fireballs with purely thermal spectra at T=160 MeV, and examines cuts in rapidity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- For a neutral (Q=0) system in the large-volume limit, the scaled variance for full 4π acceptance is ω^{gce}_{4π}=1 (grand canonical), ω^{ce}_{4π}=0.5 (canonical), and ω^{mce}_{4π}≈0.235 (micro-canonical) at T=160 MeV.
- For μ=+m/2, the 4π scaled variances are ω^{gce}_{4π}=1, ω^{ce}_{4π}=0.706, and ω^{mce}_{4π}=0.353 at T=160 MeV.
- For μ=-m/2, the 4π scaled variances are ω^{gce}_{4π}=1, ω^{ce}_{4π}=0.294, and ω^{mce}_{4π}=0.147 at T=160 MeV.
- Under acceptance scaling with q=1/9 (each bin containing 1/9 of total yield), the scaled variances for μ=0 are ω^{gce}_{q=1/9}=1, ω^{ce}_{q=1/9}=0.944, and ω^{mce}_{q=1/9}=0.915.

## Context
This work extends studies of ensemble dependence in statistical models, where earlier work (e.g., CEfluc1, 2005) showed that scaled variance differs between ensembles even in the thermodynamic limit, unlike mean multiplicities. The paper goes beyond prior acceptance-scaling approximations (Eq. 4) by explicitly incorporating correlations from energy and momentum conservation in limited momentum-space segments. The results provide a baseline for thermal/statistical fluctuations against which signals of deconfinement onset (ood, 2004), phase transition effects (phasetrans, 2004), or critical point signatures (critpoint, 1998) can be compared in heavy-ion collision experiments at CERN SPS and BNL RHIC.
