# Stability of hexaquarks in the string limit of confinement

> The stability of systems containing six quarks or antiquarks is studied within a simple string model inspired by the strong-coupling regime of quantum chromodynamics and used previously for tetraquarks and pentaquarks. We discuss both six-quark $(q^6)$ and three-quark--three-antiquark $ (q^3\bar q{}^3)$ states. The quarks are assumed to be distinguishable and thus not submitted to antisymmetrization. It is found that the ground state of $(q^6)$ is stable against dissociation into two isolated baryons. For the case of $ (q^3\bar q{}^3)$, our results indicate the existence of a bound state very close to the threshold. The investigations are extended to $(q^3Q^3)$ and $(Q^3\bar q^3)$ systems with two different constituent masses, and their stability is discussed as a function of the mass ratio.

# Stability of hexaquarks in the string limit of confinement

## Key claim

The string model of confinement, which combines flip-flop and connected flux-tube interactions, predicts that the ground state of a six-quark system (q^6) is stable against dissociation into two baryons, and that a three-quark–three-antiquark system (q^3 q̅^3) exhibits a bound state very close to threshold, but only under the assumption that quarks are distinguishable and antisymmetrization is neglected entirely.

## Method

The authors adopt a simplified string potential where the confinement energy is the minimum of flip-flop diagrams (disconnected clusters) and connected Steiner-tree diagrams, with the string tension set to unity. For baryons they use the Δ interaction (half the sum of all pairwise distances), and for tetraquarks and hexaquarks they take the minimum over all possible partitions into color-singlet clusters. The six-body Schrödinger equation is solved variationally using both a hyperspherical expansion (for equal masses) and a correlated Gaussian expansion (for both equal and unequal masses), with the nonlinear parameters optimized to minimize the ground-state energy.

## Key numbers

- For equal-mass (q^6) with M=m=1, the hyperspherical L=0 energy is E₀ = 7.230 (in units where the string tension is 1 and ℏ=m=1); the variational Gaussian energy is 7.237, giving a binding energy Δ = −0.491 relative to the two-baryon threshold at 7.728.
- For equal-mass (q^3 q̅^3) with M=m=1, the hyperspherical L=2 energy is E₂ = 6.999; the variational Gaussian energy is 6.981, exactly equal to the lowest threshold (three-meson or meson-plus-tetraquark) at 6.981, indicating a zero-energy bound state at this level of approximation.
- For (Q^3 q̅^3) with mass ratio M/m=2, the variational energy is 6.314 versus threshold 6.335, giving Δ = −0.021; for M/m=5, the energy is 5.762 versus threshold 5.852, giving Δ = −0.090.
- For (Q^3 q^3) with M/m=1, Δ = −0.491; with M/m=5, Δ = −0.233; the critical mass ratio for loss of binding is estimated at M/m ≈ 8–10.

## Context

This work extends the same string model previously applied to tetraquarks (Vijande, 2007) and pentaquarks (Richard, 2009), where the flip-flop interaction gave more attraction than the additive color-factor model used in earlier multiquark calculations. The results contradict the earlier conclusion of Carlson (1991) that no six-quark bound states exist under a similar string potential, a difference attributed to the use of diagonal (uncorrelated) Gaussians in that earlier work. The findings suggest that stable dibaryons with exotic flavor configurations may exist, and that lattice QCD calculations (Inoue, 2010; Beane, 2010) of the H-dibaryon (ssuudd) finding a loosely bound state or near-threshold resonance are consistent with the extra attraction from the string picture. The model enables future studies incorporating antisymmetrization and a reformulation of the interaction as an operator in color space.
