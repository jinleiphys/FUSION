# Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of fully-heavy compact hexaquarks

> We used a diffusion Monte Carlo technique to describe the properties of fully-heavy compact arrangements (no dibaryon molecules) including six quarks and no antiquarks within the framewok of a constituent quark model. Only arrangements whose wavefunctions were eigenvectors of $L^2$ with eigenvalue $\ell$ = 0 were taken into account, what means that we only considered the subset of all the possible color-spin combinations that make the total wavefunctions antisymmetric with respect to the interchange of any two quarks of the same type. This means arrangements with spin $S=0$ for $cccccc$, $bbbbbb$, $cccccb$, $bbbbbc$ and $cccbbb$ and spin $S=0,1,2$ for the $ccccbb$ and $bbbbcc$ hexaquarks. In all cases, the masses of the six-quark arrangements are larger that the ones corresponding to the sum of any of the two baryons we can split them into, but smaller that the ones for a set of six isolated quarks, i.e., all them are bound systems. The analysis of their structure indicates that all the hexaquarks considered in this work are compact objects, except the $cccbbb$, that appears to be a loose association of two baryons.

## Key claim

All fully-heavy hexaquarks composed of six quarks (no antiquarks) are bound systems with masses lower than their six isolated quarks, but all are unstable against decay into two baryons; the $cccbbb$ hexaquark is not a compact object but a loose association of two baryons, unlike the other configurations studied.

## Method

The authors used a diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) algorithm to solve the Schrödinger equation for six-quark systems within the constituent quark model, employing the AL1 potential of Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1996). The trial wavefunction was a product of radial functions (eigenfunctions of $L^2$ with $\ell=0$) and color-spin functions made antisymmetric by direct diagonalization of the antisymmetry operator, avoiding the standard Clebsch-Gordan construction. Only color-singlet states with total spin $S=0$ (for $cccccc$, $bbbbbb$, $cccccb$, $bbbbbc$, $cccbbb$) or $S=0,1,2$ (for $ccccbb$, $bbbbcc$) were considered.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Silvestre-Brac and Semay (1996) and the DMC methodology of the authors' prior work on heavy baryons (2021) to fully-heavy hexaquarks, going beyond the dibaryon or diquark approximations used in most prior theoretical studies (e.g., Wang (2021), Vijande (2022)). The finding that all compact hexaquarks are metastable by 200-300 MeV against two-baryon decay, and that $cccbbb$ is not compact, agrees with the conclusion of Vijande (2022). The results provide mass predictions and structural information that can guide experimental searches for fully-heavy hexaquarks at facilities such as LHCb.
