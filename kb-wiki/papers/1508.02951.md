# Light Pseudoscalar Mesons in Bethe-Salpeter Equation with Instantaneous Interaction

> The light pseudoscalar mesons play a twofold role: they may or have to be regarded both as low-lying bound states of the fundamental degrees of freedom of quantum chromodynamics as well as the (pseudo-) Goldstone bosons of the spontaneously broken chiral symmetries of quantum chromodynamics. We interrelate these aspects in a single quantum-field-theoretic approach relying on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism in instantaneous approximation by very simple means: the shape of the pseudoscalar-meson Bethe-Salpeter wave function dictated by chiral symmetry is used in Bethe-Salpeter equations for bound states of vanishing mass, in order to deduce analytically the interactions which govern the bound states under study. In this way, we obtain exact Bethe-Salpeter solutions for pseudoscalar mesons, in the sense of establishing the rigorous relationship between, on the one hand, the relevant interactions and, on the other hand, the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes that characterize the bound states.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that, within the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter formalism, the interaction potential required to produce massless pseudoscalar mesons (Goldstone bosons) from a specific ansatz for the Salpeter amplitude can be derived analytically, but only under the condition that the bound-state mass is exactly zero; the resulting potential is confining only when the constituent quark mass is less than a model parameter μ, and nonconfining otherwise.

## Method
The authors apply an inversion technique to the Salpeter equation (the instantaneous reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) for pseudoscalar mesons. They adopt a Fierz-invariant Lorentz structure for the interaction kernel, Γ⊗Γ = ½(γ_μ⊗γ^μ + γ_5⊗γ_5 − 1⊗1), and insert an ansatz for the Salpeter amplitude φ₂(p) ∝ (p²+μ²)^{-3/2}, motivated by Dyson-Schwinger studies of the quark mass function in the chiral limit. The configuration-space potential V(r) is then extracted from the bound-state equation via Fourier-Bessel transforms, both analytically for special cases (m=0 and m=μ) and numerically for arbitrary constituent masses m.

## Key numbers
- The Salpeter amplitude ansatz in momentum space: φ₂(p) = 4 √(μ³/π) (p²+μ²)^{-3/2}, normalized such that ‖φ₂‖² = 1.
- At the origin in momentum space: φ₂(0) = 4/√(π μ³); for μ=1, φ₂(0) ≈ 2.256785.
- For massless constituents (m=0), the potential at small r behaves as V(r) → π/[2 r ln(μ r)] → −∞ as r→0.
- For m=μ, the potential is V(r) = −(π/2) exp(−m r)/[r K₀(m r)], which at large r decays as V(r) → −√(π m/(2 r)) → 0.
- For m=0, the potential at large r rises as V(r) → √(8/(π μ⁵ r⁷)) exp(μ r) → ∞ (confining).
- For m→∞, the potential approaches the trivial limit V(r) → −m.

## Context
This work extends the inversion technique previously developed by Lucha and Schöberl (2013) for the reduced Salpeter equation to the full Salpeter equation, specifically targeting light pseudoscalar mesons. It connects the Bethe-Salpeter formalism to results from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Maris and Roberts, 1997; Maris and Tandy, 1997) that relate the pseudoscalar vertex function to the quark mass function in the chiral limit. The paper enables future work to determine whether the model parameter μ can be derived from fundamental QCD parameters, and clarifies the conditions under which instantaneous interactions can produce confining potentials for Goldstone bosons.
