# Reaction e^+e^-\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^- at energies \sqrt{s}\leq 1 GeV

> The cross section of reaction e^+e^-\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^- is calculated for energies 0.65\leq \sqrt{s}\leq1 GeV in the framework of the generalized hidden local symmetry model. The calculations are compared with the data of CMD-2 and BaBaR. It is shown that the inclusion of heavy isovector resonances Žü(1450) and Žü(1700) is necessary for reconciling calculations with the data. It is found that at \sqrt{s}\approx1 GeV the contributions of above resonances are much larger, by the factor of 30, than the Žü(770) one, and are amount to a considerable fraction \sim0.3-0.6 of the latter at \sqrt{s}\sim m_Žü.

## Key claim

The generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) model, in its simplest form with minimal derivatives, fails to reproduce the measured cross section of \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) at energies above 0.8 GeV; inclusion of the heavy isovector resonances \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) is necessary to reconcile calculations with data, but the quality of fit remains "not quite good" even with these additions.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section using the GHLS model, incorporating resonant \(\rho(770)\) production, \(a_1(1260)\pi\) intermediate states, and contact (non-resonant) \(\gamma^*\to 4\pi\) terms derived from the GHLS Lagrangian. They then added Breit-Wigner parametrizations for \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) with constant widths and masses from the Particle Data Group, assuming \(a_1\pi\) dominance in the heavy resonance decays, and fitted the free parameters \(x_{\rho'}\) and \(x_{\rho''}\) to the CMD-2 and BaBaR data sets separately.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s}\approx 1\) GeV, the combined contribution of \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) is larger than the \(\rho(770)\) contribution by a factor of 30 (BaBaR fit) to 32 (CMD-2 fit), for \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV.
- At \(\sqrt{s}\sim m_\rho\) (approximately 0.77 GeV), the heavy resonance contribution amounts to a fraction 0.3 (CMD-2 fit) to 0.6 (BaBaR fit) of the \(\rho(770)\) contribution, for \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV.
- Fitting CMD-2 data with both \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) (variant 3, \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV) yields \(x_{\rho'} = 96.8 \pm 1.5\) and \(x_{\rho''} = -208.7 \pm 2.5\), with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f} = 14.5/9\).
- Fitting BaBaR data with both resonances (variant 3, \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV) yields \(x_{\rho'} = 273.2 \pm 1.4\) and \(x_{\rho''} = -514.5 \pm 2.3\), with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f} = 11.2/15\).
- The masses used are: \(m_{\rho'} = 1.459\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{\rho'} = 0.147\) GeV; \(m_{\rho''} = 1.72\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{\rho''} = 0.25\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier GHLS model calculations by Achasov and Kozhevnikov (2000, 2005) for \(\rho\to 4\pi\) decays to the full \(e^+e^-\to 4\pi\) reaction, including for the first time the contact non-resonant contributions and the \(a_1\pi\) intermediate state. The conclusions contradict prior analyses by CMD-2 (2002), Ecker (1995), and Lichard (1999), which claimed small or absent heavy resonance contributions; the authors attribute this to those works using non-chiral-invariant Lagrangians or different derivative expansions. The results enable future tests of the robustness of heavy resonance contributions using linear sigma-model approaches and motivate study of intermediate states involving the scalar \(\sigma\) meson.
