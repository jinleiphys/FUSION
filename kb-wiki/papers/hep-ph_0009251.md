# Constraints for the nuclear sea quark distributions from the Drell-Yan process at the SPS

> Nuclear modifications to the Drell-Yan dilepton production cross sections in p+A and A+A collisions in the leading twist approximation are caused by nuclear effects in the parton distributions of bound nucleons. For non-isoscalar nuclei, isospin corrections must also be considered. We calculate these effects for p+A and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies. Our goal is to place constraints on nuclear effects in sea quark distributions in the region x > 0.2. We show that the net nuclear corrections remain small for p+A collisions at E_lab=450 GeV. However, in Pb+Pb collisions at E_lab=158 AGeV, effects of > 20% are predicted at large M. The data collected by the NA50 collaboration could thus be used to constrain the nuclear effects in the sea quark distributions in the region of the EMC effect, x > 0.3.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Drell-Yan dilepton production in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (158 AGeV) can constrain nuclear modifications of sea quark distributions in the EMC-effect region (x > 0.3), but only if experimental precision of 5-10% is achieved; for p+A collisions at 450 GeV the net nuclear effects remain small due to cancellation between valence and sea quark modifications.

## Method

The authors compute leading-twist Drell-Yan cross sections in p+A and Pb+Pb collisions using nuclear parton distributions from the EKS98 parametrization, which is based on DGLAP evolution from an initial scale Q0=1.5 GeV. They compare ratios of cross sections with and without nuclear modifications, separating genuine nuclear effects from isospin corrections for non-isoscalar nuclei. The calculations are performed at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) to verify that K-factors cancel in the ratios.

## Key numbers

- For p+W collisions at E_lab=450 GeV and M~10 GeV, nuclear parton distributions modify cross sections by only 6%
- For Pb+Pb collisions at E_lab=158 AGeV, nuclear effects of >20% are predicted at large M (M~7-8 GeV)
- Below the J/Psi peak (M~4 GeV), nuclear corrections in Pb+Pb are only about 5%
- The NLO K-factor for Drell-Yan is K_DY ~ 1.6-1.7, but ratios remain close to LO values
- At sqrt(s)=30 GeV and y_cm=0.04, the ratio R_DY^{W/D} computed with MRST98 distributions shows isospin corrections that depend on the parton distribution set used

## Context

This work extends the DGLAP analysis of nuclear parton distributions by Eskola, Kolhinen, and Salgado (1998, 1999) (the EKS98 parametrization), which was constrained primarily by deep inelastic scattering data from NMC, SLAC, and E665 collaborations and by Drell-Yan data from E772 and E866 experiments at Fermilab. The paper identifies that the NA50 collaboration's Pb+Pb data at the CERN SPS can provide new constraints on the EMC effect for sea quarks, which were previously assumed to follow the same modification as valence quarks in the EKS98 analysis. This enables improved determination of the nonperturbative input distributions for nuclear parton distributions at x > 0.2, where direct experimental constraints were lacking.
