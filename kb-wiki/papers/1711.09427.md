# Hyperons in hot dense matter: what do the constraints tell us for equation of state?

> For core-collapse and neutron star merger simulations it is important to have at hand adequate equations of state, describing the underlying dense and hot matter as realistically as possible. Here, we present two newly constructed equation of state (EoS) including the entire baryon octet. Both EoS are compatible with the main constraints from nuclear physics, both experimental and theoretical. One of the EoS is equally describing maximum mass for cold $β$-equilibrated neutron stars of $2 M_\odot$ in agreement with recent observations. The predictions obtained with the new EoS are compared with the results obtained with DD2Y, the only presently existing EoS containing the baryon octet, that satisfies the same constraints within uncertainties. The main difference between our new EoS models and DD2Y is the harder symmetry energy of the latter. We show that the density dependence of the symmetry energy has a direct influence on the amount of strangeness inside hot and dense matter and consequently on thermodynamic quantities, e.g. the temperature for given entropy per baryon. We expect these differences to affect the evolution of a protoneutron star or binary neutron star mergers. We also propose several parametrizations calibrated to $Λ$ hypernuclei based on the DD2 and SFHo models that satisfy the two solar mass constraint.

## Key claim

The paper presents two new equation of state (EoS) models including the entire baryon octet that are compatible with nuclear physics constraints, but only one of them (SFHoY) simultaneously satisfies the 2 solar mass neutron star maximum mass constraint, while the other (SFHoY*) fails this constraint; the key difference from the existing DD2Y model is the harder symmetry energy of DD2Y, which directly influences the amount of strangeness in hot dense matter.

## Method

The authors constructed two new relativistic mean field (RMF) EoS models (SFHoY and SFHoY*) based on the SFHo nuclear interaction, including all baryons of the octet with hyperon couplings adjusted to reproduce hypernuclear data and, for SFHoY, rescaled to maintain a 2 solar mass maximum mass. They compared these models with the existing DD2Y EoS by analyzing cold neutron star properties, particle fractions, and thermodynamic quantities across a range of temperatures, baryon number densities, and charge fractions, using the statistical model by Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich for inhomogeneous matter at sub-saturation densities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for cold neutron stars: DD2Y = 2.04 solar masses, SFHoY = 1.99 solar masses, SFHoY* = 1.75 solar masses
- Radius at fiducial mass of 1.4 solar masses: SFHo and SFHoY = 11.9 km, DD2 and DD2Y = 13.3 km
- Central baryon number density at maximum mass configuration: DD2Y = 1.00 fm^-3, SFHoY = 1.18 fm^-3, SFHoY* = 1.25 fm^-3
- Total strangeness fraction at maximum mass: DD2Y = 0.04, SFHoY = 0.02, SFHoY* = 0.05
- Saturation density: DD2 = 0.149 fm^-3, SFHo = 0.158 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation: DD2 = 31.7 MeV, SFHo = 31.6 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L: DD2 = 55.0 MeV, SFHo = 47.1 MeV
- Hyperon single-particle potentials at saturation: U_Lambda(N) = -30 MeV, U_Xi(N) = -18 MeV (DD2Y) and -14 MeV (SFHoY), U_Sigma(N) = +30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the DD2Y model by Marques (2017), which was the first EoS containing the entire baryon octet satisfying the 2 solar mass constraint, by introducing two new models based on the SFHo interaction from Steiner (2013) that has a softer symmetry energy. The results enable more realistic core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations by providing EoS tables that cover the necessary ranges of temperature, density, and electron fraction, and they show that the density dependence of the symmetry energy directly affects strangeness content and thermodynamic properties in hot dense matter, which may influence protoneutron star evolution and merger dynamics.
