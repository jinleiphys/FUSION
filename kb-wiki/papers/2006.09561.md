# Is perturbative study of ground-state correlations valid?

> Perturbative approaches have often been used to include the effects of ground-state correlations in extended theories of the random-phase approximation. Validity of such approaches is investigated for a solvable model where comparison with exact solutions can be made. It is pointed out that there is a case where perturbative approaches give good results in spite of the fact that interaction strength is far beyond a perturbative region.

# Is perturbative study of ground-state correlations valid?

## Key claim

The perturbative approach (PA) for ground-state correlations can yield occupation probabilities within a few tens of percent of exact values even when the interaction strength is far beyond the perturbative region, but this agreement arises from cancellation between competing non-linear effects rather than from genuine perturbative validity.

## Method

The validity of perturbative expressions for the occupation probability \(n_\alpha\) and the correlated two-body density matrix \(C_2\) was tested against exact solutions of the Lipkin model for particle numbers \(N=4, 6, 10\). The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) equations were used to interpret the exact solutions, and the perturbative limit of TDDM was derived to obtain the standard PA expressions. For \(^{16}\)O, PA results were compared with exact diagonalization approach (EDA) using the same Skyrme III residual interaction and single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- For the Lipkin model with \(N=4\): PA gives \(\mathcal{N} < 1.077\) for \(\chi < 0.5\) (genuine perturbative region); at \(\chi=1\), \(n_p = 0.083\) with 26% error and \(\mathcal{N}=1.333\).
- For the Lipkin model with \(N=6\): at \(\chi=2\), PA gives \(n_p = 0.2\) and \(\mathcal{N}=2.2\) (far beyond perturbative region), yet the error in \(n_p\) is only 16%.
- For the Lipkin model with \(N=10\): at \(\chi=1\), \(n_p = 0.028\) with 28% error and \(\mathcal{N}=1.278\); PA validity is restricted to \(\chi < 0.5\) where \(\mathcal{N}<1.07\).
- For \(^{16}\)O: PA gives \(\mathcal{N}=2.128\); errors in occupation probabilities for the \(1p_{3/2}\), \(1p_{1/2}\), and \(1d_{5/2}\) states are 21%, 26%, and 24%, respectively, compared to EDA.
- For \(^{16}\)O: the EDA energy of the 1st \(0^+\) excited state is 4.42 MeV lower than the unperturbed HF energy; the ground-state energy in EDA is 16.27 MeV lower than the HF configuration.

## Context

This work addresses a controversy raised by Neck et al. (no year given in text) who claimed that PA calculations by Taka et al. (no year given), Nishii et al. (no year given), and Adachi et al. (no year given) overestimate ground-state correlations in closed-shell nuclei by using perturbative expressions far beyond their justified region. The study extends previous work on the renormalized RPA (Rowe, no year given), self-consistent RPA (no year given), and extended second RPA (Nishii et al., no year given) by systematically testing PA against exact solutions of the Lipkin model. The results enable a nuanced assessment: PA can be accidentally accurate in some cases (e.g., \(N=6\) Lipkin model) due to cancellation effects, but in general its validity is limited to \(\chi < 0.5\) for the Lipkin model, and for \(^{16}\)O the agreement is fortuitous despite complete breakdown of perturbation assumptions.
