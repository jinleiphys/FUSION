# Squeezed back-to-back correlations of $ϕϕ$ in Au+Au and d+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energies

> We investigate the squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of $ϕϕ$, caused by the mass modification of the particles in the source medium, in the heavy-ion collisions of Au+Au and d+Au at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies. The BBC functions are calculated using the modified masses extracted from experimental data and the source space-time distributions provided by the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. Our investigations indicate that the BBC of $ϕϕ$ may perhaps be observed in the collisions of d+Au and the peripheral collisions of Au+Au at the RHIC. We suggest to measure the BBC experimentally for understanding the mass modifications of the $ϕ$ meson in the collisions.

## Key claim

The squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of φφ may perhaps be observed in d+Au collisions and peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, but the observation is conditional on the mass modification of φ mesons in the source medium being sufficiently large, as extracted from experimental data on K⁺K⁻ invariant mass distributions.

## Method

The authors used the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 with event-by-event MC-Glb initial conditions at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c and a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.08 to simulate particle-emitting sources for Au+Au and d+Au collisions at RHIC energies. They extracted modified φ meson masses and widths from experimental data measured by the STAR Collaboration (2009) by fitting a two-component model that separates φ mesons decaying inside versus outside the source medium. The BBC functions were then calculated using the Bogoliubov transformation formalism with these modified masses and the hydrodynamic source space-time distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the lowest transverse momentum region (p_T = 0.5 GeV/c) in Fit 1, the in-medium mass M* = 1.0157 ± 0.0003 GeV/c² and width Γ* = 9.785 ± 0.373 MeV/c², with fraction f = 0.000 ± 0.008 of φ mesons decaying outside the source.
- For p_T = 0.7 GeV/c in Fit 1, the fraction f = 0.481 ± 0.031; for p_T = 1.0 GeV/c, f = 0.753 ± 0.014.
- In Fit 2, with fixed f = 0.4 at p_T = 0.5 GeV/c, M* = 1.0148 ± 0.0005 GeV/c² and Γ* = 12.069 ± 0.921 MeV/c².
- In Fit 3, with fixed f = 0.8 at p_T = 0.5 GeV/c, M* = 1.0107 ± 0.0016 GeV/c² and Γ* = 20.197 ± 5.773 MeV/c².
- The vacuum φ meson mass M₀ = 1.02 GeV/c² and the Monte Carlo width Γ₀ = 6.09 MeV/c².
- The BBC functions were calculated for transverse momentum regions 0.4 < |k_T| < 0.5 GeV/c and 0.8 < |k_T| < 0.9 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of φφ and K⁺K⁻ BBC using ideal hydrodynamic sources (Zhang 2015) by incorporating viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) and extracting modified masses from experimental data (STAR Collaboration 2009). The study also builds on the theoretical framework of squeezed correlations from Asakawa, Csorgo, and Gyulassy (1996, 1999) and Padula (2006). The results enable experimental searches for BBC of φφ pairs at RHIC, particularly in d+Au and peripheral Au+Au collisions, which would provide a new probe of φ meson mass modifications and interactions with the hadronic medium.
