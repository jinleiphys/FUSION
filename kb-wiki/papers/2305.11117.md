# Moments of proton GPDs from the OPE of nonlocal quark bilinears up to NNLO

> For the first time, we present a lattice QCD determination of Mellin moments of unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton from an analysis of the quasi-GPD matrix elements within the short-distance factorization framework. We perform our calculation on an $N_f$=2+1+1 twisted mass fermions ensemble with a clover improvement at lattice spacing $a=0.093$ fm and a pion mass of $m_π=260$ MeV. Focusing on the zero-skewness case, the iso-vector and iso-scalar quasi-GPDs are calculated from the $γ_0$ definition, as well as a recently proposed Lorentz-invariant definition. We utilize data on both symmetric and asymmetric kinematic frames, which allows us to obtain the Mellin moments for several values of the momentum transfer, $-t$, in the range 0.17 to $2.77~\rm{GeV}^2$. We use the ratio scheme for GPDs, i.e. renormalization group invariant ratios with leading-twist factorization formula and perturbatively calculated matching coefficients up to the next-next-to-leading order (NNLO) to extract Mellin moments of GPDs, which are consistent with renormalization-group improved results. We compare our determination from quasi-GPDs with the results extracted using standard calculations of Mellin moments of local operators, specifically those related to the electromagnetic and gravitational form factors. We estimated the moments of GPDs up to the fifth ones for the first time. By extrapolating the Mellin moments to $-t=0$, we obtained the quark charges, momentum fraction, as well as the angular momentum contributions to the proton spin. The impact parameter space interpretation of the GPD moments is discussed, which provides insights into the spatial distribution of unpolarized quarks and their correlations in the transverse plane of an unpolarized or transversely polarized proton.

## Key claim

For the first time, the authors present a lattice QCD determination of Mellin moments of unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the proton up to the fifth moment, using short-distance factorization of quasi-GPD matrix elements with perturbative matching coefficients up to next-next-to-leading order (NNLO), but the results are obtained on a single ensemble with a pion mass of 260 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.093 fm, and the iso-scalar matching is only available up to NLO.

## Method

The authors compute quasi-GPD matrix elements for the proton on an Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass fermions ensemble with clover improvement at a=0.093 fm and mπ=260 MeV, using both the γ0 definition and a Lorentz-invariant definition of quasi-GPDs. They utilize data from symmetric and asymmetric kinematic frames at zero skewness, with hadron momentum P3=1.25 GeV and momentum transfer -t ranging from 0.17 to 2.77 GeV2. The bare matrix elements are renormalized using a ratio scheme (renormalization group invariant ratios with zero-momentum quasi-PDF matrix elements as denominator), and Mellin moments are extracted via leading-twist short-distance factorization with perturbative matching coefficients up to NNLO for the iso-vector case and NLO for the iso-scalar case.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.093 fm; pion mass: mπ = 260 MeV
- Hadron momentum: P3 = 1.25 GeV (with additional ensembles at 0.83 and 1.67 GeV for one -t value)
- Momentum transfer range: -t from 0.17 to 2.77 GeV2
- Source-sink separation: ts = 10a = 0.93 fm
- Lattice size: Ns × Nt = 32³ × 64
- Number of configurations: 67 to 329 depending on the kinematic point; total statistics up to 84,224 measurements for the most precise point
- Mellin moments estimated up to the fifth moment for the first time

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF method (Ji, 2013) and the Lorentz-invariant amplitude framework (Bhattacharya et al., 2022; Constantinou et al., 2022) to extract GPD Mellin moments via short-distance factorization, complementing traditional lattice calculations of local operator matrix elements (Hagler, 2003; Alexandrou, 2011; Constantinou, 2014) which have been limited to the first three moments due to signal decay and power-divergent mixing. The results enable comparison with experimental extractions from deeply-virtual Compton scattering (Kumericki, 2016; Mueller, 2014) and provide first access to higher moments (fourth and fifth) that are inaccessible via traditional local operator methods, as well as allowing determination of quark charges, momentum fraction, and angular momentum contributions to the proton spin through extrapolation to -t=0.
