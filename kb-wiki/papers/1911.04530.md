# NLO impact factor for inclusive photon$+$dijet production in $e+A$ DIS at small $x$

> We compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor for inclusive photon $+$dijet production in electron-nucleus (e+A) deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) at small $x$. An important ingredient in our computation is the simple structure of ``shock wave" fermion and gluon propagators. This allows one to employ standard momentum space Feynman diagram techniques for higher order computations in the Regge limit of fixed $Q^2\gg Λ_{\rm QCD}^2$ and $x\rightarrow 0$. Our computations in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory include the resummation of all-twist power corrections $Q_s^2/Q^2$, where $Q_s$ is the saturation scale in the nucleus. We discuss the structure of ultraviolet, collinear and soft divergences in the CGC, and extract the leading logs in $x$; the structure of the corresponding rapidity divergences gives a nontrivial first principles derivation of the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution equation for multiparton lightlike Wilson line correlators. Explicit expressions are given for the $x$-independent $O(α_s)$ contributions that constitute the NLO impact factor. These results, combined with extant results on NLO JIMWLK evolution, provide the ingredients to compute the inclusive photon $+$ dijet cross-section at small $x$ to $O(α_s^3 \ln(x))$. First results for the NLO impact factor in inclusive dijet production are recovered in the soft photon limit. A byproduct of our computation is the LO photon+ 3 jet (quark-antiquark-gluon) cross-section.

## Key claim

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor for inclusive photon+dijet production in electron-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. The computation includes resummation of all-twist power corrections Q_s^2/Q^2, and the structure of rapidity divergences provides a first-principles derivation of the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution equation for multiparton lightlike Wilson line correlators.

## Method

The computation employs standard momentum space Feynman diagram techniques using "shock wave" fermion and gluon propagators in the Regge limit of fixed Q^2 >> Λ_QCD^2 and x → 0. The authors separate quantum fluctuations into target modes (with l^- < Λ_0^-) and projectile modes (with l^- > Λ_0^-), where the former contribute to JIMWLK evolution of the weight functional and the latter constitute the NLO impact factor. Ultraviolet divergences are regulated using dimensional regularization in d = 2 − ε dimensions, while collinear divergences are handled by promoting partons to jets using a cone algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The NLO impact factor provides O(α_s) contributions that are x-independent
- Combined with NLO JIMWLK evolution, these results enable computation of the inclusive photon+dijet cross-section at small x to O(α_s^3 ln(x))
- The computation is performed in the regime Q^2 ≫ Λ_QCD^2 with fixed Q^2 and x → 0
- The saturation scale Q_s enters through power corrections Q_s^2/Q^2 that are resummed to all twists
- The NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian H_NLO is of order α_s^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' leading-order computation of photon+dijet production in e+A DIS (Roy and Venugopalan, 2019) to NLO, and recovers existing NLO impact factor results for inclusive dijet production in the soft photon limit. The computation provides the first NLO results for inclusive dijet, inclusive photon, and photon+jet measurements at an Electron-Ion Collider, and yields as a byproduct the LO photon+3-jet (quark-antiquark-gluon) cross-section. These results, combined with NLO JIMWLK evolution from Balitsky (2013), Kovner (2013), and Caron-Huot (2013), enable precision tests of gluon saturation at future EIC experiments.
