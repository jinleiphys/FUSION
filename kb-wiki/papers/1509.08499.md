# Pion-nucleon correlations in finite nuclei in a relativistic framework: effects on the shell structure

> The relativistic particle-vibration coupling (RPVC) model is extended by the inclusion of spin- and isospin-flip excitation modes into the phonon space, introducing a new mechanism of dynamical interaction between nucleons with different isospin in the nuclear medium. Protons and neutrons exchange by collective modes which are formed by isovector $π$ and $ρ$-mesons, in turn, softened considerably because of coupling to nucleons of the medium. These modes are investigated within the proton-neutron relativistic random phase approximation (pn-RRPA) and relativistic proton-neutron time blocking approximation (pn-RTBA). The appearance of isospin-flip states with sizable transition probabilities at low energies points out that they are likely to couple to the single-particle degrees of freedom and, in addition to isoscalar low-lying phonons, to modify their spectroscopic characteristics. Such a coupling is quantified for the shell structure of $^{100,132}$Sn and found significant for the location of the dominant single-particle states.

## Key claim

The relativistic particle-vibration coupling (RPVC) model is extended by including isospin-flip excitation modes (formed by isovector π and ρ-mesons) into the phonon space, introducing a new mechanism of dynamical interaction between protons and neutrons; this coupling is found to produce additional shifts of the dominant single-particle states ranging from a few hundred keV to 1 MeV in 100,132Sn, with the effect being particularly dramatic for states far from the Fermi surface due to strength redistribution.

## Method

The authors performed proton-neutron relativistic random phase approximation (pn-RRPA) and proton-neutron relativistic time blocking approximation (pn-RTBA) calculations to obtain isovector phonon frequencies and vertices, using the NL3* parameter set with a pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling and a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.6. These isovector phonons (with multipolarities Jπ = 0± through 6±) were then included alongside isoscalar phonons in the nucleonic self-energy within the Dyson equation framework, solving in the diagonal approximation to obtain fragmented single-particle energies and spectroscopic factors for 100,132Sn.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of pion dynamics provides additional shifts of the dominant single-particle states ranging from a few hundred keV to 1 MeV in 100,132Sn
- In 132Sn, a strong 2- state in the S- branch of the spin-dipole resonance is located at -1.7 MeV relative to the ground state of 132Sn
- In 100Sn, a strong 2- state appears at about 25 MeV in the S- branch and around 0 MeV in the S+ branch
- Phonon modes below 15 MeV for 132Sn and below 20 MeV for 100Sn (with respect to daughter nuclei for isospin-flip cases) were included in the model space
- Typical values of isoscalar phonon vertices γμ are between 0.1 MeV and 1 MeV, occasionally reaching a few MeV; isovector phonon vertices ζλ are smaller by a factor of 2-3 on average

## Context

This work extends the relativistic particle-vibration coupling model of Litvinova and collaborators (2006-2015) by adding isospin-flip phonons to the previously considered isoscalar phonon space, building on earlier pn-RRPA and pn-RTBA calculations for spin-isospin transitions by Paar et al. (2004) and Marketin et al. (2012). The inclusion of pion-exchange dynamics beyond the mean-field level addresses the long-standing challenge of treating pion degrees of freedom in finite nuclei, as pursued by the "Moscow school" (Migdal, 1974) and "Stony Brook school" (Brown and Weise, 1976), and enables quantitative investigation of how soft pion modes affect single-particle spectroscopic properties in exotic nuclei.
