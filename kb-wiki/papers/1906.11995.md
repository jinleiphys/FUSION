# $DK$, $DDK$, and $DDDK$ molecules--understanding the nature of the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$

> The $DK$ interaction is strong enough to form a bound state, the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$. This in turn begs the question of whether there are bound states composed of several charmed mesons and a kaon. Previous calculations indicate that the three-body $DDK$ system is probably bound, where the quantum numbers are $J^P = 0^{-}$, $I=\tfrac{1}{2}$, $S = 1$ and $C = 2$. The minimum quark content of this state is $cc\bar{q}\bar{s}$ with $q=u,d$, which means that, if discovered, it will be an explicitly exotic tetraquark. In the present work. we apply the Gaussian Expansion Method to study the $DDDK$ system and show that it binds as well. The existence of these three and four body states is rather robust with respect to the $DD$ interaction and subleading (chiral) corrections to the $DK$ interaction. If these states exist, it is quite likely that their heavy quark symmetry counterparts exist as well. These three-body $DDK$ and four-body $DDDK$ molecular states could be viewed as counterparts of atomic nuclei, which are clusters of nucleons bound by the residual strong force, or chemical molecules, which are clusters of atoms bound by the residual electromagnetic interaction.

## Key claim
The $DDK$ and $DDDK$ systems form bound molecular states with binding energies that are robust against uncertainties in the $DD$ interaction and subleading chiral corrections to the $DK$ interaction, provided the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ is dominantly a $DK$ bound state.

## Method
The authors used the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) to solve the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation for the three-body $DDK$ and four-body $DDDK$ systems. The $DK$ interaction was modeled as a contact-range potential with a Gaussian regulator, with the coupling strength fixed to reproduce the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ as a $DK$ bound state with 45 MeV binding energy, while the $DD$ interaction was described by a one-boson-exchange potential involving sigma, rho, and omega meson exchange. The authors also treated the $DDDK$ system as an effective three-body $DDD_{s0}^*$ system using a one-kaon-exchange potential for the $DD_{s0}^*$ interaction.

## Key numbers
- The $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ mass is 2317.7 MeV, with a $DK$ binding energy of 45 MeV used as input.
- The $DDK$ three-body system binds with approximately 65-70 MeV below the $DDK$ threshold, depending on the cutoff and repulsive core parameters.
- The $DDDK$ four-body system binds with approximately 90-100 MeV below the $DDDK$ threshold.
- The root mean square radius of the $DK$ subsystem in the $D_{s0}^*$ ranges from 1.28 fm to 2.53 fm depending on the cutoff $R_c$ (1-3 fm) and repulsive core strength.
- For the $DDD_{s0}^*$ effective system, binding energies range from 11.5 MeV to 37.2 MeV (without $DD$ interaction) and 13.9 MeV to 43.3 MeV (with $DD$ interaction) for cutoff values $\Lambda'$ from 0.8 to 1.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by Sanchez Sanchez et al. (2017) and Martinez Torres et al. (2018), who predicted a $DDK$ bound state using Faddeev equations and a $D D_{s0}^*$ two-body approximation. The present study confirms those results with the GEM method and extends the analysis to the four-body $DDDK$ system for the first time. The existence of these molecular states, with minimum quark content $cc\bar{q}\bar{s}$, would constitute explicitly exotic tetraquarks, and the authors note that heavy quark symmetry counterparts likely exist as well. The $DDDK$ bound state is suggested to be more amenable to lattice QCD observation than experimental production.
