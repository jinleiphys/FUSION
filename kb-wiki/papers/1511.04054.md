# Electromagnetic form factors of one neutron halos with spin 1/2+ ground state

> The electromagnetic form factors for single neutron halo nuclei Be-11, C-15 and C-19 are calculated. The calculations are performed in halo effective field theory (EFT) where the halo nuclei are approximated as made of a single neutron and a core. The form factors depend on the single neutron separation energy, the s-wave neutron-core scattering effective range and a two-body current. The EFT expressions are presented to leading order for C-15 and next-to-leading order for Be-11 and C-19.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors for the single-neutron halo nuclei Be-11, C-15, and C-19 are calculated in halo effective field theory (EFT), where the results depend on the neutron separation energy, the s-wave neutron-core scattering effective range, and a two-body current; the calculations are performed to leading order for C-15 and next-to-leading order for Be-11 and C-19, with the caveat that the effective range is treated non-perturbatively for C-15 due to its large value.

## Method

The halo nuclei are approximated as a shallow s-wave bound state of a single neutron and a spin-zero core, described by an auxiliary dimer field in halo EFT. The electromagnetic form factors are computed from Feynman diagrams with photon insertion between initial and final ground states, using the zed-parameterization for Be-11 and C-19 where the effective range is perturbative, and a non-perturbative treatment for C-15 where the effective range is large. The strong interaction couplings are related to the neutron-core scattering effective range expansion parameters, and the magnetic form factor additionally requires a two-body magnetic coupling fitted to known magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Be-11: binding energy B = 500 keV gives binding momentum gamma = 29.22 MeV; wave function normalization Z_d - 1 = 0.69 from Coulomb dissociation data; EFT charge radius sqrt(<r_E^2>) = (2.40 +/- 0.02) fm using core radius sqrt(<r_c^2>) = (2.357 +/- 0.018) fm, compared to experimental value sqrt(<r_E^2>)_exp = (2.463 +/- 0.016) fm.
- Be-11: magnetic moment kappa_phi^(exp) = -1.6814, yielding two-body coupling L_M = -2.25 from the EFT relation.
- C-19: binding energy 0.575 +/- 0.055 MeV and Z_d - 1 = 0.73 from radiative capture analysis; predicted charge radius contribution <r_E^2> - <r_c^2> = 0.09 fm^2.
- C-15: binding energy B = 1.2181 MeV; effective range rho = 2.67 fm or Z_d = 2.66 from fit to capture data; charge radius contribution <r_E^2> - <r_c^2> = 0.11 fm^2; magnetic moment magnitude |kappa_phi^(exp)| = (1.720 +/- 0.009) with tentative value kappa_phi^(exp) = -(1.77 +/- 0.05) yielding L_M = -2.0.
- C-15: p-wave resonance at energy E_r = 1.885 MeV with width Gamma_r = 40 keV; scattering volume a_1 = -5.6 x 10^(-8) MeV^(-3) and effective range r_1 = -11 x 10^3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT treatment of electromagnetic properties of halo nuclei, following earlier calculations for Be-11 by Hammer (2011) and for C-15 and C-19 by Phillips (2010), Hammer (2011), and Acharya (2013). The form factor calculations enable future electron scattering experiments on these exotic nuclei to probe charge and magnetic distributions, and the EFT framework provides systematic error estimates from the Q/Lambda expansion. The analysis for C-15 presents an alternative to the non-perturbative p-wave treatment of PhysRevC.86.044608, using instead a non-perturbative s-wave effective range that gives a slightly better fit to capture data.
