# Cold Atom Quantum Simulator for String and Hadron Dynamics in Non-Abelian Lattice Gauge Theory

> We propose an analog quantum simulator for simulating real time dynamics of $(1+1)$-d non-Abelian gauge theory well within the existing capacity of ultracold atom experiments. The scheme calls for the realization of a two-state ultracold fermionic system in a 1-dimensional bipartite lattice, and the observation of subsequent tunneling dynamics. Being based on novel loop string hadron formalism of SU(2) lattice gauge theory, this simulation technique is completely SU(2) invariant and simulates accurate dynamics of physical phenomena such as string breaking and/or pair production. The scheme is scalable, and particularly effective in simulating the theory in weak coupling regime, and also bulk limit of the theory in strong coupling regime up to certain approximations. This paper also presents a numerical benchmark comparison of exact spectrum and real time dynamics of lattice gauge theory to that of the atomic Hamiltonian with experimentally realizable range of parameters.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an analog quantum simulator for real-time dynamics of (1+1)-d SU(2) lattice gauge theory using ultracold fermions in a bipartite optical lattice, but the scheme is exact only in the weak coupling regime (g to 0, a to 0) where the boundary flux is large (l_i much greater than 0); in strong and intermediate coupling regimes, the mapping between the gauge theory Hamiltonian and the atomic Hamiltonian is approximate.

## Method

The authors map the loop-string-hadron (LSH) formulation of SU(2) lattice gauge theory in (1+1)-d to a fermionic Hubbard model on a bipartite lattice. The LSH formalism replaces the original Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian with gauge-invariant operators characterized by three local quantum numbers (n_l for loop flux, n_i for incoming string, n_o for outgoing string) at each site. In the weak coupling limit where n_l is large and approximately constant across sites, the gauge theory Hamiltonian reduces to a form decoupled from the bosonic loop mode, which is then mapped to an ionic Hubbard model with tunable on-site interaction and tunneling parameters. The authors perform exact diagonalization to compare the spectrum and real-time dynamics of the full gauge theory Hamiltonian, the approximated LSH Hamiltonian, and the atomic Hamiltonian for experimentally realizable parameter ranges.

## Key numbers

- The electric energy term in the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian is H_E = (g^2 a / 2) sum over links of E^a(j)E^a(j), where g is the gauge coupling and a is the lattice spacing.
- The interaction term is H_I = (1/(2a)) sum over links of [psi-dagger(j) U(j) psi(j+1) + h.c.].
- The strong coupling vacuum has n_l(j) = 0 for all j, with n_i = 1, n_o = 1 for odd sites and n_i = 0, n_o = 0 for even sites.
- The weak coupling approximation requires boundary flux l_i much greater than 0, yielding n_l(j) approximately equal to l_i for all j.
- The approximated interaction Hamiltonian in the weak coupling limit is H_I^(approx) = (1/(2a)) sum over j of [chi_o^+(j)chi_o^-(j+1) + chi_o^-(j)chi_o^+(j+1) + chi_i^+(j)chi_i^-(j+1) + chi_i^-(j)chi_i^+(j+1)].

## Context

This work extends the loop-string-hadron formalism developed by Raychowdhury (2019) for SU(2) gauge theory and builds on prior proposals for analog quantum simulation of lattice gauge theories using ultracold atoms, including the work of Zohar (2011, 2012, 2013, 2015) on Kogut-Susskind and quantum link model formulations, and the experimental demonstration of U(1) gauge theory simulation by Mil (2020) and Yang (2020). Unlike previous proposals that used quantum link models with finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces that do not reproduce the Kogut-Susskind spectrum in low dimensions, this scheme preserves the exact SU(2) gauge invariance and can be generalized to periodic boundary conditions and higher dimensions. The paper enables future experimental realization of non-Abelian gauge theory dynamics and provides a path toward quantum simulating full QCD by generalizing to SU(3) and higher dimensions.
