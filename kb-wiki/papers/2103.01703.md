# A Relativistic Quantum Approach to Neutrino and Antineutrino Emissions via the Direct Urca Process in Strongly Magnetized Neutron-Star Matter

> We study neutrino and antineutrino emission from the direct Urca process in neutron-star matter in the presence of strong magnetic fields. We calculate the neutrino emissivity of the direct Urca process, whereby a neutron converts to a proton, an electron and an antineutrino, or a proton-electron pair converts to a neutron-neutrino pair. We solve exact wave functions for protons and electrons in the states described with Landau levels. We find that the direct Urca process can satisfy the kinematic constraints even in density regions where this process could not normally occur in the absence of a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The direct Urca process can satisfy kinematic constraints and produce neutrino emissivity even in density regions where the proton fraction is below the usual threshold of 1/9, but only when the magnetic field is strong enough (B ≥ 10¹⁶ G); for weaker fields (B ≲ 10¹⁵ G), the process does not occur in those forbidden regions.

## Method

The authors solved exact relativistic Dirac wave functions for protons and electrons in Landau levels under a uniform magnetic field along the z-direction, using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach for nuclear matter with three different parameter sets (SF1, SF2, SF3) that give different density dependences of the symmetry energy. They calculated the neutrino emissivity from the direct Urca process (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ and p + e⁻ → n + νₑ) at low temperature (T = 0.50 keV) for magnetic field strengths B = 10¹⁵, 10¹⁶, and 10¹⁷ G, both with and without muons included in the matter composition.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino emissivity was calculated at T = 0.50 keV, chosen because magnetar surface temperatures are T ≈ 0.28–0.72 keV (Kaminker 2009).
- For B = 10¹⁷ G, the direct Urca emissivity appears at densities below the zero-field threshold ρ_DU and shows comb-like oscillations; smoothed over Δρ = 0.01 fm⁻³, the emissivity remains larger than the B = 0 case but not drastically so.
- For B = 10¹⁵ G, the emissivities in the forbidden region (x_p < 1/9) are too small to appear in the figure for the SF3 parameter set.
- The three parameter sets (SF1, SF2, SF3) all give a symmetry energy of 32 MeV at normal nuclear density, with slope parameters L = 92.7, 84.1, and 81.0 MeV respectively.
- The critical density ρ_DU is defined as the density where the proton fraction x_p = 1/9; for SF3 with muons, the proton fraction exceeds 1/9 but the direct Urca still does not appear at B = 0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino-pair emission from Landau-level transitions in magnetized neutron-star matter (Maruyama et al. 2018) and axion emission from magnetars (Fortunato et al. 2021). It goes beyond the perturbative treatment of Leinson and Perez (2001), who assumed all charged particles populate the lowest Landau level, by exactly solving wave functions for transitions between multiple Landau levels. The results enable future work on exact calculations of direct Urca emissivity at very low proton fractions (x_p ≪ 1/9) and exploration of different equations of state (e.g., Chamel 2013, Rikovska-Stone et al. 2011) to understand the role of symmetry energy density dependence.
