# Asymptotic Derivation of the Simplified $P_N$ Equations for Nonclassical Transport with Anisotropic Scattering

> This paper introduces new asymptotic analysis used to derive the set of simplified nonclassical $P_N$ equations with anisotropic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents an asymptotic method to derive the simplified \(P_N\) (\(SP_N\)) equations for nonclassical transport that can handle anisotropic scattering, generalizing prior work that was limited to isotropic scattering; the derivation is performed under the assumption of a globally diffusive regime with optically thick systems where absorption and sources are small compared to scattering.

## Method

The authors start from the nonclassical Boltzmann transport equation with an \(s\)-dependent total cross section \(\Sigma_t(s)\) and anisotropic scattering represented by a Legendre polynomial expansion. They apply a scaling where \(\Sigma_t(s) = \sigma(s/\varepsilon)/\varepsilon\), \(Q = \varepsilon q\), and \(1-c = \varepsilon^2 \kappa\) with \(\varepsilon \ll 1\), then perform an asymptotic expansion in \(\varepsilon\) using spherical harmonic expansions and an operator inversion technique to derive the nonclassical \(SP_N\) equations. The method is explicitly applied to derive the nonclassical \(SP_1\) equation with anisotropic scattering.

## Key numbers

* The nonclassical \(SP_1\) equation with anisotropic scattering is given by \(-\frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{\langle s^2 \rangle}{2 \langle s \rangle} + \frac{c \bar{\mu}_0}{1-c \bar{\mu}_0} \langle s \rangle \right] \nabla^2 \Phi(\bm x) + \frac{1-c}{\langle s \rangle} \Phi(\bm x) = Q(\bm x)\), where \(\bar{\mu}_0 = a_1\) is the mean scattering cosine and \(\langle s^m \rangle\) are moments of the free-path distribution.
* In the classical limit (exponential free-path distribution), this reduces to \(-\frac{1}{3 \Sigma_t (1 - c \bar{\mu}_0)} \nabla^2 \Phi(\bm x) + \Sigma_a \Phi(\bm x) = Q(\bm x)\) with \(\Sigma_a = \Sigma_t (1 - c)\).
* The nonclassical \(SP_1\) equation with isotropic scattering from prior work is \(-\frac{1}{6} \frac{\langle s^2 \rangle}{\langle s \rangle} \nabla^2 \Phi(\bm x) + \frac{1-c}{\langle s \rangle} \Phi(\bm x) = Q(\bm x)\).

## Context

This work extends the nonclassical transport theory of Larsen and Vasques (2015), who derived the nonclassical \(SP_1\) equation with anisotropic scattering but whose asymptotic method could not produce higher-order equations. It also generalizes the approach of Vasques and Slaybaugh (2019), who derived nonclassical \(SP_N\) equations for isotropic scattering only. The new method enables the derivation of higher-order nonclassical \(SP_N\) equations with anisotropic scattering, which are needed to accurately model nonclassical transport in diffusive systems such as pebble-bed reactors, boiling water reactors, photon transport in clouds, and other heterogeneous media.
