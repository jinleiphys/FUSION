# Quark matter subject to strong magnetic fields: phase diagram and applications

> In the present work we are interested in understanding various properties of quark matter subject to strong magnetic fields described by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop. We start by analysing the differences arising from two different vector interactions in the Lagrangian densities, at zero temperature, and apply the results to stellar matter. We then investigate the position of the critical end point for different chemical potential and density scenarios.

# Quark matter subject to strong magnetic fields: phase diagram and applications

## Key claim
The flavor-dependent and flavor-independent vector interactions in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop produce different equations of state for magnetized quark matter, and the presence of a strong magnetic field can drive a system that has no critical end point at zero field into a first-order phase transition, but only if the magnetic field is sufficiently strong (above approximately eB = 0.1 GeV²) to wash out multiple critical end points caused by Landau level effects.

## Method
The authors used the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with Polyakov loop (PNJL) and two different vector interaction terms (a flavor-dependent model P1 and a flavor-independent model P2) to compute the thermodynamic potential, pressure, and equation of state for quark matter in a static uniform magnetic field. At zero temperature they applied the resulting equations of state to quark stars and hybrid stars (using the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model for the hadronic phase with a Maxwell construction), and at finite temperature they located the critical end point on the QCD phase diagram for various isospin and strangeness scenarios.

## Key numbers
- Maximum quark star masses reach up to 2.12 M₀ (HK parametrization, P2, x=0.6, B=3.1×10¹⁸ G) and 1.98 M₀ (RKH parametrization, P2, x=0.6, B=3.1×10¹⁸ G)
- Maximum hybrid star masses reach up to 2.35 M₀ (HK and RKH parametrizations, P2, x=0.10 and x=0.22, B=3.1×10¹⁸ G)
- For non-magnetized quark stars with HK parametrization, P2 gives maximum masses from 1.56 M₀ (x=0.1) to 1.91 M₀ (x=0.6)
- For non-magnetized quark stars with RKH parametrization, P2 gives maximum masses from 1.35 M₀ (x=0.1) to 1.74 M₀ (x=0.6)
- The critical end point disappears when the isospin asymmetry reaches μ_d = 1.45 μ_u at zero magnetic field
- For eB > 0.1 GeV² only one critical end point exists; for eB < 0.1 GeV² multiple critical end points can appear
- Inverse magnetic catalysis is observed at finite chemical potential and zero temperature for moderate magnetic fields, with the effect disappearing at large eB

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram under strong magnetic fields (Skokov 2009, magnetar observations) using the PNJL model with two parameter sets known as HK (Hatsuda 1994) and RKH (Rehberg 1996). The results confirm earlier calculations by Cavagnoli (2010) indicating that deconfinement phase transitions are more easily attained with asymmetric nuclear matter. The finding that magnetic fields can induce a first-order phase transition in systems that otherwise have no critical end point opens new possibilities for understanding magnetar interiors and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, while the maximum masses near 2.35 M₀ for hybrid stars are relevant for interpreting neutron star observations.
