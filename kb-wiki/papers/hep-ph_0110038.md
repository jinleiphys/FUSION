# Nuclear enhanced power corrections to DIS structure functions

> We calculate nuclear enhanced power corrections to structure functions measured in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We find that the nuclear medium enhanced power corrections at order of $O(α_s/Q^2)$ enhance the longitudinal structure function $F_L$, and suppress the transverse structure function $F_1$. We demonstrate that strong nuclear effects in $σ_A/σ_D$ and $R_A/R_D$, recently observed by HERMES Collaboration, can be explained in terms of the nuclear enhanced power corrections.

# Nuclear enhanced power corrections to DIS structure functions

## Key claim

Nuclear medium enhanced power corrections at order O(α_s/Q^2) can explain the strong nuclear effects in σ_A/σ_D and R_A/R_D observed by the HERMES Collaboration, but only when the Q^2 of the HERMES data is much smaller than that of other experiments, the counterintuitive Q^2 dependence of the ratio is driven by the strong Q^2 dependence of the kinematic parameter ε, not by the power corrections themselves.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading twist-4 power corrections to the DIS structure functions F_L and F_1 in lepton-nucleus scattering, arising from Feynman diagrams where a quark interacts with a gluon from a different nucleon after the hard collision (Fig. 1c). They factorize the hadronic tensor into a partonic hard part and a twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function T_qF^A, then model this correlation function as T_qF^A(x,Q^2) ≈ λ^2 A^{1/3} φ_q/A(x,Q^2) with λ^2 = 0.01 GeV^2 extracted from Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening. Using generic parton distributions at low Q^2, they compute the double ratio R_R = [(1+ε R_A)/(1+R_A)] / [(1+ε R_D)/(1+R_D)] for a ¹⁴N target and compare its x_B, Q^2, and ε dependence to HERMES data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio σ_L^A/σ_L^D for scattering off a ¹⁴N target was observed by HERMES to be as large as 2.
- The model parameter λ^2 ≈ 0.01 GeV^2 is used for the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function.
- At Q^2 = 0.6 GeV^2 and x_B = 0.0175, the calculated R_A ≈ 1.6 and R_D ≈ 0.32, giving R_A/R_D ∼ 5.
- When x_B decreases from 0.03 to 0.0125 at Q^2 = 0.6 GeV^2, ε falls steeply from 0.9 to 0.25.
- When Q^2 changes from 0.5 GeV^2 to 0.9 GeV^2 at x_B = 0.0175, ε decreases from 0.78 to 0.13 (a factor of 6), while the size of power corrections changes by less than a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization approach for power corrections developed by Qiu (1990) and Qiu and Sterman (1991) to nuclear targets, where the nuclear size A^{1/3} enhances the twist-4 contributions. It directly addresses the anomalous nuclear effects observed by the HERMES Collaboration (Ackerstaff et al., 2000), which had generated theoretical interest from Kopeliovich, Raufeisen, and Tarasov (2000), Barshay and Kreyerhoff (2000), and Miller, Brodsky, and Karliner (2000). The calculation enables a QCD-based understanding of low-Q^2 nuclear DIS data and motivates the study of even higher-power (1/Q^4) nuclear enhanced corrections for improved predictions.
