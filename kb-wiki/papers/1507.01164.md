# Chiral geometry in multiple chiral doublet bands

> The chiral geometry of the multiple chiral doublet bands with identical configuration is discussed for different triaxial deformation parameters $γ$ in the particle rotor model with $πh_{11/2}\otimes νh_{11/2}^{-1}$. The energy spectra, electromagnetic transition probabilities $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$, angular momenta, and $K$-distributions are studied. It is demonstrated that the chirality still remains not only in the yrast and yrare bands, but also in the two higher excited bands when $γ$ deviates from $30^{\circ}$. The chiral geometry relies significantly on $γ$, and the chiral geometry of the two higher excited partner bands is not as good as that of the yrast and yrare doublet bands.

## Key claim

The chirality (static and vibrational) persists not only in the yrast and yrare bands but also in the two higher excited partner bands (bands 3 and 4) when the triaxial deformation parameter γ deviates from 30°, although the chiral geometry of the higher excited partner bands is not as good as that of the yrast and yrare doublet bands.

## Method

The particle rotor model (PRM) was used with a symmetric particle-hole configuration πh₁₁/₂ ⊗ νh₁₁/₂⁻¹, a quadrupole deformation β = 0.25, and moment of inertia 𝒥 = 30 MeV⁻¹ℏ². The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a strong coupling basis for triaxial deformation parameters γ = 30°, 27°, 24°, 21°, 18°, and 15°, and the energy spectra, B(M1) and B(E2) transition probabilities, angular momentum components, and K-distributions were calculated for the four lowest bands.

## Key numbers

- For γ = 30°, the energies of bands 3 and 4 are identical when spin I = 16 to 19 ℏ, indicating static chirality.
- For γ = 15°, the energy difference between bands 3 and 4 is larger than 0.4 MeV, suggesting the absence of static chirality.
- The moment of inertia used in all calculations is 𝒥 = 30 MeV⁻¹ℏ².
- The quadrupole deformation parameter is β = 0.25.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment used is Q₀ = 3.5 e·b.
- The gyromagnetic ratios used are gR = Z/A = 0.44, gp = 1.21, and gn = -0.21, corresponding to the A ∼ 130 mass region.

## Context

This work extends previous PRM studies of multiple chiral doublet (MχD) bands with identical configuration, which were initially examined for the ideal chiral system at γ = 30° by Chen et al. (2010). It also builds on the study of B(M1) staggering as a fingerprint for chiral doublet bands at various γ values by Qi et al. (2009). The results demonstrate that MχD with identical configuration can survive when γ deviates from 30°, supporting the experimental observation of such bands in ¹⁰³Rh reported by Kuti et al. (2014), and they enable further investigation of higher excited bands where the level density becomes too high to distinguish chiral partners.
