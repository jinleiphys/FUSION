# Multipole analysis of kaon photoproduction data

> Methods used in the multipole analysis of low-energy pion-photoproduction data have been tested against the database of kaon-photoproduction measurements. Results for some multipoles are in qualitative agreement with existing phenomenological models, while others are unstable, given the present database. These findings are compared to those of previous studies.

# Multipole analysis of kaon photoproduction data

## Key claim

The method used for low-energy pion photoproduction multipole analysis can be applied to kaon photoproduction, yielding qualitative agreement with existing phenomenological models for some multipoles, but the extracted multipoles are not unique and some remain unstable given the present database.

## Method

Single-energy fits were performed to low-energy kaon photoproduction data (threshold to 1.8 GeV center-of-mass energy) using a two-step procedure where multipoles up to E2- and M2- were varied, while higher partial waves were taken from Born plus K* exchange diagrams as used in the kaon MAID fit. Data included differential cross sections from CLAS and polarization observables (P, Sigma, T, O_x, O_z) from GRAAL, with fits yielding chi-squared per degree of freedom between 0.5 and unity.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: threshold to 1.8 GeV in center-of-mass energy (W_CM), covering the N(1650)S11, N(1710)P11, and N(1720)P13 resonances
- Fit quality: chi-squared per degree of freedom between 0.5 and unity for all searches
- Specific energy point shown: lab photon energy of 1027 MeV (W_cm = 1675 MeV)
- Multipole units: 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+} for all extracted multipole values
- Extended fits up to 1.9 GeV show M1+ real part dropping to zero with a rise in imaginary part near 1.9 GeV, supporting a N(1900) contribution

## Context

This work extends the multipole analysis method of GRAAL (Grushin, 2005) from low-energy pion photoproduction to kaon photoproduction, testing the same two-step procedure that uses known t-channel exchange to remove the overall phase ambiguity. The findings confirm the problem of multiple indistinguishable solutions previously reported by Sandorfi (2010), but are slightly more optimistic in showing qualitative agreement with the kaon MAID phenomenological fit for some multipoles. The results enable future work on model-independent amplitude reconstruction for kaon photoproduction, though the author notes the method's validity depends on the model-independence of the t-channel process and recommends repeating fits with different models for this contribution.
