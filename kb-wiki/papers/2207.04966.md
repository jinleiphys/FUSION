# Rebuttal to: "Comment on Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{\rm NN}=200$ GeV"

> Recently, F. Wang commented on our work "Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{\rm NN} = 200$ GeV" and made several claims to support his conclusion that our results are fallacious. His conclusion and claims are not only incorrect; they show a fundamental disconnect with the rudiments of the $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ correlator. This rebuttal addresses the root misconception responsible for Wang's false claims.

# Rebuttal to: "Comment on Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV"

## Key claim

The authors argue that Wang's critique of their earlier work is based on the misconception that the inverse variance of the R_Psi2(Delta S) distribution scales as sigma_R^{-2} approximately N_ch * Delta_gamma proportional to v_2, whereas they demonstrate that sigma_R^{-2} is proportional to 1/N_ch and is only sensitive to charge-dependent non-flow background, not to elliptic flow v_2.

## Method

The authors use the anomalous viscous fluid dynamics (AVFD) model to simulate background-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions. They extract the inverse variance sigma^{-2}_{R_Psi2} from the R_Psi2(Delta S) correlator distributions, which measures charge separation relative to the Psi_2 event plane via ratios of correlation functions for real and charge-shuffled events, both parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field direction. They compare the N_ch dependence of v_2 and the inverse variances for real and shuffled events, and also perform event-shape selection via fractional cuts on the q_2 flow vector distribution to test sensitivity to v_2 variations.

## Key numbers

- sigma^{-2}_{R_Psi2} is shown to be proportional to 1/N_ch (Fig. 1b), with the same scaling for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr isobars but different N_ch values.
- v_2 is proportional to the shuffled term in Eq. 4, while the difference between real and shuffled terms (Eq. 4) reflects only charge-dependent non-flow correlations.
- The STAR isobar data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV show ratios R_Ru/Zr less than unity for both Delta_gamma/v_2 and 1/sigma_R, with more significant deviations from unity for Delta_gamma/v_2.

## Context

This rebuttal addresses the comment by Wang (2022) on the authors' earlier work (Lacey and Magdy, 2022) which analyzed post-blind results from the STAR Collaboration's isobar data (STAR, 2021). The STAR isobar data had shown ratios between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions of Delta_gamma/v_2 and 1/sigma_R that were less than unity, contrary to the expectation R_Ru/Zr > 1 for the chiral magnetic effect (CME). The authors' earlier work had reported that a non-negligible background difference influences these ratios. This rebuttal refutes Wang's claim that sigma_R^{-2} approximately N_ch * Delta_gamma proportional to v_2, which the authors argue is a fundamental misconception about the R_Psi2(Delta S) correlator. The work enables proper interpretation of charge separation measurements in heavy ion collisions and clarifies the relationship between the R_Psi2 correlator and flow-driven backgrounds.
