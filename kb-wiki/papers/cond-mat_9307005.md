# Bosonization of Fermi liquids

> We bosonize a Fermi liquid in any number of dimensions in the limit of long wavelengths. From the bosons we construct a set of coherent states which are related with the displacement of the Fermi surface due to particle-hole excitations. We show that an interacting hamiltonian in terms of the original fermions is quadratic in the bosons. We obtain a path integral representation for the generating functional which in real time, in the semiclassical limit, gives the Landau equation for sound waves and in the imaginary time gives us the correct form of the specific heat for a Fermi liquid even with the corrections due to the interactions between the fermions. We also discuss the similarities between our results and the physics of quantum crystals.

## Key claim

The paper presents a bosonization scheme for Fermi liquids in any number of dimensions, valid only in the long-wavelength limit and within a restricted Hilbert space of states generated by small deformations of the filled Fermi sea. The authors show that the interacting fermionic Hamiltonian becomes quadratic in the bosonic operators, but this result holds specifically for fixed-point Hamiltonians that contain only marginal operators, deliberately excluding relevant operators that lead to instabilities such as superconductivity or magnetism.

## Method

The authors construct bosonic creation and annihilation operators from particle-hole excitations near the Fermi surface, smeared over patches of size \(\Lambda\) and thickness \(D\) with \(q \ll D \ll \Lambda \ll p_F\). They build coherent states that represent deformed Fermi surfaces parameterized by a bosonic displacement field \(u_{\vec{q}}(\vec{k})\), and derive a coherent-state path integral representation for the generating functional. The bosonic Hamiltonian is obtained by rewriting the fermionic kinetic and interaction terms in terms of these bosonic operators, yielding a quadratic form for the fixed-point Hamiltonians that correspond to the Landau theory of Fermi liquids.

## Key numbers

* The stability condition for a Fermi liquid in two dimensions is \(N(0) f_n \geq -1\) for all \(n\), where \(N(0) = p_F / (\pi v_F)\) is the density of states at the Fermi surface.
* The change in free energy per unit volume for a deformed Fermi surface in two dimensions is \(\Delta F / V = (p_F^2 / 8\pi^2) \sum_{m=0}^{+\infty} (1/N(0) + f_m) u_m^2\).
* The discontinuity in the energy \(E(\vec{k})\) at the Fermi surface is \(\Delta E(\vec{p}_F) = |Z_{\vec{p}_F}| (\epsilon^0_{\vec{p}_F} + \mu)/2\), where \(Z_{\vec{p}_F}\) is the quasiparticle residue.
* The local density of states at the Fermi surface scales as \(\Lambda^{d-1} v_F^{-1}\), making the dimensionless coupling constant \(F_m = \Lambda^{d-1} v_F^{-1} f_m\) vanish for \(d > 1\) in the scaling limit \(\Lambda \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends Haldane's (1992) bosonic algebra for density fluctuations of a Fermi liquid, which generalized the one-dimensional bosonization of Tomonaga (1950) and the earlier work of Luther. The paper provides a microscopic foundation for the phenomenological stability analysis of Pomeranchuk (1958) and its renormalization-group reinterpretation by Shankar (1991). The coherent-state path integral enables calculation of the low-temperature specific heat and the Landau equation for sound waves, and the authors discuss analogies with quantum fluctuations of crystal surfaces, noting that quantum fluctuations of the fixed-point Hamiltonian wash out the possibility of a roughening transition in two dimensions.
