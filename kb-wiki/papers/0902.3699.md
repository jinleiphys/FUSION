# Properties of hyperonic matter in strong magnetic fields

> We study the effects of strong magnetic fields on the properties of hyperonic matter. We employ the relativistic mean field theory, which is known to provide excellent descriptions of nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The two additional hidden-strangeness mesons, $σ^{\ast}$ and $ϕ$, are taken into account, and some reasonable hyperon potentials are used to constrain the meson-hyperon couplings, which reflect the recent developments in hypernuclear physics. It is found that the effects of strong magnetic fields become significant only for magnetic field strength $B>5 \times 10^{18}$ G. The threshold densities of hyperons can be significantly altered by strong magnetic fields. The presence of hyperons makes the equation of state (EOS) softer than that in the case without hyperons, and the softening of the EOS becomes less pronounced with increasing magnetic field strength.

## Key claim

The effects of strong magnetic fields on hyperonic matter become significant only for magnetic field strengths above \(B > 5 \times 10^{18}\) G, and the threshold densities for hyperon appearance can be significantly altered by such fields; the softening of the equation of state caused by hyperons becomes less pronounced as the magnetic field strength increases.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set, including nucleons and hyperons (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\)) interacting via \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma^{\ast}\), and \(\phi\) mesons. They solved the coupled field equations, \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions, and charge neutrality self-consistently at fixed baryon density in a constant external magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis, incorporating the anomalous magnetic moments of all baryons. Two cases of hyperon-hyperon (YY) interaction were considered: a weak YY interaction suggested by the Nagara event and a strong YY interaction from earlier measurements.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength threshold for significant effects is \(B > 5 \times 10^{18}\) G, corresponding to \(B^{*} = B/B_{c}^{e} > 10^{5}\) where \(B_{c}^{e} = 4.414 \times 10^{13}\) G is the electron critical field.
- Nuclear matter saturation density in the TM1 model is \(\rho_{0} = 0.145\) fm\(^{-3}\), with energy per nucleon \(-16.3\) MeV, symmetry energy \(36.9\) MeV, and incompressibility \(281\) MeV.
- The \(\Lambda\) potential depth in saturated nuclear matter is set to \(U_{\Lambda}^{(N)} = -30\) MeV, the \(\Sigma\) potential to \(U_{\Sigma}^{(N)} = +30\) MeV, and the \(\Xi\) potential to \(U_{\Xi}^{(N)} = -15\) MeV.
- For the weak YY interaction, \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq -5\) MeV; for the strong YY interaction, \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq -20\) MeV.
- The \(\sigma^{\ast}\) meson mass is \(m_{\sigma^{\ast}} = 980\) MeV and the \(\phi\) meson mass is \(m_{\phi} = 1020\) MeV.
- For \(B^{*}=10^{6}\), the proton fraction exceeds \(0.5\) before hyperons appear at approximately \(4\rho_{0}\), while for \(B^{*}=0\) the proton fraction reaches \(\sim 0.2\) around \(2\rho_{0}\) before hyperon onset.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strong magnetic field effects on neutron star matter by Lattimer and Prakash (2001, 2002) and Mao et al. (2003, 2006), which used GM1-3 and ZM models with equal meson-hyperon couplings for all hyperons. The present study incorporates recent hypernuclear data, particularly the Nagara event which provided a precise \(\Lambda\Lambda\) binding energy of \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 7.25 \pm 0.19^{+0.18}_{-0.11}\) MeV, indicating a weaker YY interaction than previously thought. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors with strong magnetic fields, especially for evaluating how the YY interaction and hyperon composition affect the equation of state and maximum mass of neutron stars.
