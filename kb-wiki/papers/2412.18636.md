# Analysis of NOvA and MicroBooNE charged-current inclusive neutrino measurements within the SuSAv2 framework

> In this work we compare the SuSAv2 model, based on the superscaling phenomenon and the relativistic mean field theory, with charged-current inclusive neutrino cross sections from the NOvA and MicroBooNE experiments, whose targets are composed primarily by 12 C and 40 Ar, respectively. The neutrino energy in these experiments covers a kinematic range from tens of MeV to roughly 20 GeV. Thus, we consider the different reaction mechanisms that contribute significantly to these kinematics, namely quasielastic, two-particle two-hole meson exchange currents, resonances and deep inelastic scattering contributions.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2 model, which combines superscaling and relativistic mean field theory, can describe charged-current inclusive neutrino cross sections on both carbon (NOvA) and argon (MicroBooNE) targets across a wide energy range from tens of MeV to roughly 20 GeV, provided that quasielastic, two-particle two-hole meson exchange currents, resonance, and deep inelastic scattering contributions are all included.

## Method

The authors compared the SuSAv2 model predictions with CC-inclusive neutrino cross section data from the NOvA experiment (target primarily 67% carbon, 16% chlorine, 11% hydrogen, 3% titanium, 3% oxygen) and the MicroBooNE experiment (liquid argon target). The quasielastic channel was described using SuSAv2 scaling functions derived from relativistic mean field theory, the 2p2h-MEC channel used relativistic Fermi gas calculations, the resonance region employed the Dynamical Coupled-Channels model from the Osaka group, and deep inelastic scattering used the Bodek-Ritchie parameterization for single-nucleon structure functions.

## Key numbers

- NOvA neutrino flux covers energies up to roughly 20 GeV, with the near detector target composed of 67% carbon, 16% chlorine, 11% hydrogen, 3% titanium, and 3% oxygen
- MicroBooNE operates on the Booster Neutrino Beamline with a liquid argon target
- The DCC model is valid in the region W_X less than or equal to 2.1 GeV and Q^2 less than or equal to 3 GeV
- For the NOvA case with flux up to 20 GeV, differences up to 20% at very forward angles can be observed between Bosted-Christy and Bodek-Ritchie parameterizations for the SoftDIS channel
- The superscaling property is satisfied when the transferred momentum q exceeds approximately 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SuSAv2 analyses that were tested against electron scattering data and CC-inclusive measurements from T2K and MINERvA on carbon targets (Gonzalez-Rosa 2022, PhysRevD.108.113008). The SuSAv2 model has been partially implemented in the GENIE event generator. The comparison with NOvA and MicroBooNE data tests the model on different nuclear targets (carbon-rich and argon) than previous studies, and enables future applications to DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande experiments which will explore similar or higher neutrino energies.
