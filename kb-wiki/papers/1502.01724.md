# Direct photon production at RHIC and LHC energies

> Direct photon spectra and elliptic flow v2 in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies are investigated within a relativistic transport approach incorporating both hadronic and partonic phases - the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD). The results suggest that a large v2 of the direct photons - as observed by the PHENIX Collaboration - signals a significant contribution of photons produced in interactions of secondary mesons and baryons in the late stages of the collision. In order to further differentiate the origin of the direct photon azimuthal asymmetry, we compare our predictions for the centrality dependence of the direct photon yield to the recent measurements by the PHENIX Collaboration and provide predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies with respect to the direct photon spectra and v2(pT) for 0-40% centrality.

## Key claim

The large elliptic flow \(v_2\) of direct photons observed by the PHENIX Collaboration is not primarily a signal of early quark-gluon plasma radiation but instead signals a significant contribution from photons produced in secondary meson and baryon interactions during the late hadronic stage of the collision, with the QGP contributing at most about 50% of the direct photon yield.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which solves generalized off-shell Kadanoff-Baym equations to describe the full evolution of heavy-ion collisions from initial hard scatterings through a dynamical deconfinement phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), hadronization, and subsequent hadronic interactions. Photon production was calculated from multiple sources: partonic interactions (\(q+\bar{q}\to g+\gamma\) and \(q+g\to q+\gamma\)), hadronic decays (\(\pi,\eta,\omega,\eta',\phi,a_1\)), binary hadronic reactions (\(\pi+\pi\to\rho+\gamma\), \(\pi+\rho\to\pi+\gamma\), \(\rho+N\to N+\gamma\)), and bremsstrahlung from elastic meson-meson and meson-baryon scatterings using the soft photon approximation.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the QGP contributes "slightly less than half" of the observed direct photon yield at low transverse momentum.
- The centrality scaling exponent for the integrated thermal photon yield in PHSD is \(\alpha=1.5\), in agreement with the PHENIX measurement of \(\alpha=1.48\pm0.08\pm0.04\).
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of photons from the QGP saturates after about 5 fm/c at a relatively low value of about 0.02.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV (0-40% centrality), the PHSD calculations agree with preliminary ALICE data within about a factor of 2 for transverse momenta \(p_T\) from 1 to 4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHSD studies of photon production at RHIC energies (Linnyk 2013) and provides new predictions for LHC energies. It directly addresses the puzzle raised by the PHENIX Collaboration's observation that direct photon \(v_2\) is comparable to pion \(v_2\), which contradicted earlier theoretical expectations that QGP photons should carry little flow. The results support the interpretation of Dusling (2009) that direct photon \(v_2\) can serve as a viscometer, but attribute the large flow to hadronic rather than partonic sources. The calculations enable future comparisons with final ALICE data to further differentiate the origin of direct photon azimuthal asymmetry.
