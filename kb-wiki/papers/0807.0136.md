# A microscopic three-cluster model with nuclear polarization applied to the resonances of 7Be and the reaction 6Li(p,3He)4He

> A microscopic model for three-cluster configurations in light nuclei is presented. It uses an expansion in terms of Faddeev components for which the dynamic eqations are derived. The model is designed to investigate binary channel processes in a compound system. Gaussian and oscillator bases are used to expand the wave function and to represent appropriate boundary conditions. We study the effect of cluster polarization on ground and resonance states of 7Be, and on the astrophysical S-factor of the reaction 6Li(p,3He)4He.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic three-cluster model that incorporates cluster polarization (the ability of weakly bound subsystems to change size and shape when interacting) and shows that including polarization of the \(^{6}Li\) and \(^{3}He\) clusters significantly improves agreement with experimental data for the \(^{7}Be\) spectrum and the \(^{6}Li(p,^{3}He)^{4}He\) reaction, though the ground state remains slightly overbound by 0.115 MeV relative to experiment.

## Method

The authors expand the three-cluster wave function for \(^{7}Be\) (modeled as \(^{4}He+d+p\)) into Faddeev components, using a Gaussian basis to describe bound two-cluster subsystems (\(^{6}Li\) and \(^{3}He\)) and an oscillator basis for the relative motion of the third cluster. They solve the Schrodinger equation via a coupled channels formalism, first diagonalizing the two-cluster Hamiltonians to obtain eigenstates, then transforming the three-cluster Hamiltonian to that representation, and finally imposing binary-channel boundary conditions to extract scattering and bound-state solutions.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{6}Li+p\) threshold is computed at 4.015 MeV relative to the \(^{4}He+^{3}He\) threshold, compared to the experimental value of 4.020 MeV.
- The \(^{4}He+d+p\) threshold is computed at 5.852 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 5.493 MeV.
- The ground state energy of \(^{7}Be\) (\(J^{\pi}=3/2^{-}\)) is \(-1.702\) MeV relative to the \(^{4}He+^{3}He\) threshold, versus the experimental \(-1.587\) MeV.
- The \(7/2^{-}\) resonance is at \(2.820 + i0.130\) MeV, compared to experiment \((2.983 \pm 0.05) + i(0.175 \pm 0.007)\) MeV.
- The \(5/2^{-}\) resonance is at \(5.040 + i1.343\) MeV, compared to experiment \((5.143 \pm 0.10) + i1.20\) MeV.
- The root-mean-square radius of \(^{3}He\) increases from 1.311 fm (single cluster function) to 1.696 fm (four Gaussian functions); for \(^{6}Li\) it increases from 1.650 fm to 2.288 fm.
- Convergence of the \(^{6}Li\) ground state energy requires only 4 Gaussian functions versus more than 20 oscillator functions.
- About 100 oscillator states are needed in the three-cluster calculation to achieve unitarity of the S-matrix with precision better than 0.1%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic three-cluster approach previously developed by the Kiev-Antwerp collaboration (Vasilevsky et al., 2004) by introducing a hybrid Gaussian-oscillator basis (GOB) to better describe cluster polarization, whereas earlier work used a bi-oscillator basis. The model is applied to \(^{7}Be\), which has been studied by many microscopic methods (e.g., Arai et al., 2002; Furutani et al., 1985; Kajino et al., 1986) and is relevant to the astrophysical reactions \(^{3}He(\alpha,\gamma)^{7}Be\) (solar neutrino problem) and \(^{6}Li(p,^{3}He)^{4}He\) (big-bang nucleosynthesis). The results enable more accurate calculations of the astrophysical S-factor for the latter reaction and provide a framework for studying polarization effects in other light-nucleus systems.
