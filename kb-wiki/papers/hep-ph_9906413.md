# Quantum Boltzmann equations for mixing scalar fields

> We report on a work in progress, whose goal is a systematic field theoretical derivation of the quantum transport equations for baryon production in the electroweak plasma at a first order phase transition in the limit of slowly varying background fields (thick wall limit). We start with the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the two point Green function written in the closed time contour (CTC) formalism. The quantum Boltzmann equations for the density matrix arise when the SD-equations are expanded to the first order in the gradients in the on-shell limit. In this paper we consider only scalar fields, but the formalism can easily be extended to fermions.

## Key claim
The authors derive quantum Boltzmann equations for mixing scalar fields from first principles using the Schwinger-Dyson equations in the closed time contour formalism, but only in the limit of slowly varying background fields (thick wall limit) and to first order in gradients. The work is presented as a progress report, with the formalism restricted to scalar fields while noting extension to fermions is straightforward.

## Method
Starting from the exact Schwinger-Dyson equations for the two-point Green function in the closed time contour formalism, the authors recast these as Kadanoff-Baym equations. They then perform a gradient expansion to first order in derivatives, valid when the Compton wavelength of excitations is much smaller than the characteristic length scale of background variation. The on-shell limit is taken by factoring out the singular structure of the Wigner function, and the resulting quantum Boltzmann equation is obtained by integrating over positive frequencies.

## Key numbers
- The baryon asymmetry parameter is given as \(\eta_B \equiv n_B/s \simeq 2-7 \times 10^{-11}\), from nucleosynthesis constraints.
- The electroweak phase boundary thickness is estimated as \(L \sim (10-20)/T\), where \(T\) is the temperature.
- The gradient expansion parameter is \(\partial_X \partial_k \sim 1/LT \ll 1\), since typical plasma excitations have \(\partial_k \sim k^{-1} \sim T^{-1}\).
- In the multi-field case, each homogeneous particle pole \(\omega_\pm\) splits into three poles with weight 1/3 each, with shifts \(\{\delta\omega, \delta\omega e^{2i\pi/3}, \delta\omega e^{-2i\pi/3}\}\) where \(\delta\omega \propto (H_z)_{12}^{2/3}\).

## Context
This work extends the systematic field-theoretic treatment of baryogenesis transport equations beyond earlier semiclassical approaches, specifically those studied in references jpt2 and cjk1 which considered only diagonal Boltzmann equations with classical forces. The formalism enables computation of CP-violating sources and quantum coherence effects in electroweak baryogenesis scenarios, particularly for the minimal supersymmetric extension of the Standard Model. The authors note that in the adiabatic limit where the wall width \(L\) satisfies \(L \gg 1/\Gamma\) (with \(\Gamma\) the interaction rate), collisions damp off-diagonal elements and one recovers the semiclassical Boltzmann equations, but non-adiabatic effects may be important for CP-violating phenomena that vanish in the adiabatic limit.
