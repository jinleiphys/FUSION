# Exactly separable version of X(5) and related models

> One-parameter exactly separable versions of the X(5) and X(5)-beta^2 models, labelled as ES-X(5) and ES-X(5)-beta^2 respectively, are derived by using in the Bohr Hamiltonian potentials of the form u(beta)+u(gamma)/beta^2. Unlike X(5), in these models the beta_1 and gamma_1 bands are treated on equal footing. Spacings within the gamma_1 band are well reproduced by both models, while spacings within the beta_1 band are well reproduced only by ES-X(5)-beta^2, for which several nuclei with R_{4/2}=E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) ratios and [normalized to E(2_1^+)] beta_1 and gamma_1 bandheads corresponding to the model predictions have been found.

## Key claim

The paper derives exactly separable versions of the X(5) and X(5)-β² models (ES-X(5) and ES-X(5)-β²) by using a potential of the form u(β)+u(γ)/β² in the Bohr Hamiltonian, which avoids the approximation of replacing β² by its average value that was required in the original X(5) models; however, the small-angle approximation for γ is still used and is justified only for large γ stiffness, which the authors find to be the case when comparing to experimental data.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr collective Hamiltonian and assume a separable potential of the form u(β)+u(γ)/β², which leads to exact separation of the β and γ equations. For the β-equation, they solve exactly for two cases: an infinite square well potential (giving ES-X(5)) and a harmonic oscillator β²/2 potential (giving ES-X(5)-β²). The γ-equation is solved using a harmonic oscillator approximation (3c)²γ²/2 for small γ angles, with the stiffness parameter c as the single free parameter controlling all bandhead positions and internal spacings.

## Key numbers

- The ground state band ratio R₄/₂ = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) is reported for both models, with good agreement found for nuclei with R₄/₂ > 3.15 (well-deformed nuclei)
- For the β₁ band spacing ratios, ES-X(5)-β² gives values of exactly 1 for both R₂,₀,β,g and R₄,₂,β,g, while ES-X(5) gives values around 1.5, and X(5) gives values of 1.8 and 1.7 respectively
- The γ stiffness parameter c is found to be of order 10 when fitting experimental data, corresponding to (3c)² ≈ 900
- 18 nuclei were identified as good examples for ES-X(5)-β² and 4 examples for ES-X(5) based on matching R₄/₂ ratios and normalized β₁ and γ₁ bandheads

## Context

This work extends the X(5) critical point symmetry introduced by Iachello (2001) and the X(5)-β² model by Bonatsos et al. (2004), addressing two key limitations: the approximate separation of variables (which required replacing β² by its average value) and the unequal treatment of β and γ bands. The exactly separable approach follows earlier work by Wilets and Jean (1956), Fortier (1970), and Heyde (1977) on potentials of the form u(β)+u(γ)/β². The results enable systematic identification of deformed nuclei near axial symmetry where the β₁ and γ₁ bands are treated on equal footing, with detailed B(E2) transition rate calculations deferred to a future publication.
