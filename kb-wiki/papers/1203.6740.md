# A renewed look at eta' in medium

> We revisit the question of whether the U_A(1) symmetry is effectively restored in hot and dense medium. In particular, by generalizing the Witten-Veneziano formula to finite temperature, we investigate whether the mass of eta'-meson will change in medium due to the restoration of chiral symmetry.

# A renewed look at $\eta'$ in medium

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the large $N_c$ limit of QCD, the $\eta'$ mass will become degenerate with the other Goldstone bosons when chiral symmetry is restored, but this conclusion is conditional on the chiral limit and the large $N_c$ approximation; the actual quenching of the $\eta'$ mass in nuclear medium is estimated to be proportional to the reduction of the chiral condensate, with a 20% reduction in the order parameter implying a 20% reduction in the $\mathrm{U}_A(1)$-breaking part of the $\eta'$ mass.

## Method

The authors generalize the Witten-Veneziano formula for the $\eta'$ mass to finite temperature by relating the gluonic correlation function $U_0(0)$ to the temperature derivative of the gluon condensate via a low-energy theorem derived from the heavy quark expansion. They then combine this with the anomaly relation to express the $\eta'$ mass in terms of the gluon condensate and the coupling $\langle 0|G\tilde{G}|\eta'\rangle$, which they estimate using the quark axial current correlation function and the behavior of disconnected diagrams in the chiral limit when chiral symmetry is restored.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum $\mathrm{U}_A(1)$-breaking part of the $\eta'$ mass is estimated as $m_{\eta'} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{33}} \frac{1}{f_\pi} \langle \frac{\alpha}{\pi}G^2 \rangle^{1/2} \approx 464$ MeV, using $f_\pi = 130$ MeV and $\langle \frac{\alpha}{\pi}G^2 \rangle = (0.35\ \mathrm{GeV})^4$.
- In the chiral limit, when the chiral condensate $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle \rightarrow 0$, the $\eta'$ mass squared $m_{\eta'}^2$ goes to zero.
- For nuclear medium, if the chiral order parameter reduces by 20%, the $\mathrm{U}_A(1)$-breaking part of the $\eta'$ mass reduces by the same fraction.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Lee (1996) and Cohen (1996) on $\mathrm{U}_A(1)$ symmetry restoration in correlation functions, providing a direct relation between the $\eta'$ mass and chiral order parameters via a generalized Witten-Veneziano formula. The study was motivated by RHIC data on two-pion Bose-Einstein correlations at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV Au+Au collisions (Vertesi 2009, Csorgo 2009) suggesting $\eta'$ mass quenching, and by nuclear target experiments (Jido 2011, Nagahiro 2011, Nanova 2011) probing partial quenching in nuclear medium. The result enables quantitative predictions for $\eta'$ mass modification in heavy-ion collisions and nuclear matter, though the authors note that quantitative estimates in nuclear medium are difficult because density effects are subleading in the large $N_c$ limit.
