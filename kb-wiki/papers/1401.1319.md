# Self-consistent description of single-particle levels of magic nuclei

> Single-particle levels of seven magic nuclei are calculated within the Energy Density Functional (EDF) method by Fayans et al. Three versions of the EDF are used, the initial Fayans functional DF3 and its two variations, DF3-a and DF3-b, with different values of spin-orbit parameters. Comparison is made with predictions of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method with the HFB-17 functional. For the DF3-a functional, phonon coupling (PC) corrections to single-particle energies are found self-consistently with an approximate account for the tadpole diagram. Account for the PC corrections improves agreement with the data for heavy nuclei, e.g. for 208 Pb. On the other hand, for lighter nuclei, e.g. 40,48 Ca, PC corrections make the agreement a little worse. As estimations show, the main reason is that the approximation we use for the tadpole term is less accurate for the light nuclei.

## Key claim

Single-particle levels of seven magic nuclei are calculated using the Fayans Energy Density Functional (EDF) method, and phonon coupling (PC) corrections are included self-consistently for the DF3-a functional; the PC corrections improve agreement with data for heavy nuclei like 208Pb but worsen agreement for lighter nuclei such as 40,48Ca, because the approximate treatment of the tadpole diagram is less accurate for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors used three versions of the Fayans EDF (DF3, DF3-a, DF3-b) with different spin-orbit parameters to compute single-particle energies for seven magic nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 56Ni, 78Ni, 100Sn, 132Sn, 208Pb). For the DF3-a functional, phonon coupling corrections to single-particle energies were calculated self-consistently, including both pole and tadpole diagrams, using the method of Khodel. The results were compared with experimental data and with predictions from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method using the HFB-17 functional.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of spin-orbit differences from experiment across all nuclei: 0.60 MeV for DF3-b, 0.68 MeV for DF3-a, 1.04 MeV for DF3, and 2.16 MeV for HFB-17
- Total average deviation of single-particle energies from experiment across all 105 levels: 0.89 MeV for DF3-b, 0.98 MeV for DF3-a, 0.89 MeV for DF3, and 1.40 MeV for HFB-17
- For 208Pb, the total average deviation of single-particle energies is 0.44 MeV (DF3-b), 0.51 MeV (DF3-a), 0.43 MeV (DF3), and 1.15 MeV (HFB-17)
- For 40Ca, the total average deviation is 1.08 MeV (DF3-b), 1.25 MeV (DF3-a), 1.35 MeV (DF3), and 1.64 MeV (HFB-17)
- Experimental spin-orbit splitting for the 1f neutron level in 40Ca is 5.71 MeV; DF3-a predicts 1.80 MeV above this value, while HFB-17 predicts 4.24 MeV above
- The 3- phonon in 208Pb has a theoretical excitation energy of 2.684 MeV (experimental: 2.615 MeV) and B(E3) of 7.093 x 10^5 e^2 fm^6 (experimental: 6.12 x 10^5 e^2 fm^6)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach of Khodel and Saperstein (KhS) and the Fayans EDF method (Fayans et al., 2000) to a systematic comparison of single-particle spectra across seven magic nuclei, using new experimental data compiled for 35 spin-orbit doublets. The phonon coupling corrections are computed more self-consistently than in earlier work by Khodel and Platonov, using a self-consistent basis and self-consistent phonon vertices. The DF3-a functional, previously shown to work well for uranium and transuranium nuclei (Tolokonnikov and Saperstein), is used for the PC calculations; the new DF3-b set is optimized specifically for spin-orbit energy differences. The results enable improved description of single-particle spectra in heavy nuclei and provide a benchmark for future self-consistent treatments of phonon coupling, particularly for light nuclei where the tadpole approximation needs refinement.
