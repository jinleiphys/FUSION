# First Test of Lorentz Invariance in the Weak Decay of Polarized Nuclei

> A new test of Lorentz invariance in the weak interactions has been made by searching for variations in the decay rate of spin-polarized 20Na nuclei. This test is unique to Gamow-Teller transitions, as was shown in the framework of a recently developed theory that assumes a Lorentz symmetry breaking background field of tensor nature. The nuclear spins were polarized in the up and down direction, putting a limit on the amplitude of sidereal variations of the form |(Γ_{up} - Γ_{down})| / (Γ_{up} + Γ_{down}) < 3 * 10^{-3}. This measurement shows a possible route toward a more detailed testing of Lorentz symmetry in weak interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental test of Lorentz invariance in the weak decay of polarized nuclei, specifically for Gamow-Teller transitions, by searching for sidereal variations in the decay rate of spin-polarized 20Na. The result places a limit on the amplitude of such variations, but the measurement is only sensitive to the time-dependent (sidereal) components of the Lorentz-violating background field, not the constant term.

## Method

The experiment used a beam of 20Na nuclei produced via the 20Ne(p,n)20Na reaction, stopped in a buffer gas cell, and polarized by optical pumping using circularly polarized laser light at 589 nm. The nuclear polarization was reversed by switching the laser helicity, and the beta asymmetry was measured with a plastic scintillator to determine the polarization magnitude, while the gamma decay rate from the daughter nucleus was measured with two NaI detectors to extract lifetimes for each polarization direction. The gamma-ray asymmetry, constructed from lifetime differences between up and down polarization, was searched for a sidereal time dependence to constrain Lorentz violation.

## Key numbers

- Limit on sidereal variation amplitude: |(Gamma_up - Gamma_down)| / (Gamma_up + Gamma_down) < 3 x 10^{-3} at 95% confidence level.
- Gamma-ray asymmetry bound: |A_gamma| < 4 x 10^{-4} at 95% confidence level, combining data from both detectors.
- Average nuclear polarization: 23%, with an uncertainty of about 5% due to averaging over the polarization lifetime.
- 20Na half-life: 0.45 s (447.9 +/- 2.3 ms from literature), with the nonpolarized data deviating 12 ms from this value.
- Beta asymmetry parameter A = 1/3 for the 2+ -> 2+ Gamow-Teller transition, with endpoint energy 11.2 MeV.
- Limits on equatorial components: xi_2 N^1 = (-12 +/- 9) x 10^{-4} with 95% C.L. interval [-29, +6] x 10^{-4}; xi_2 N^2 = (-3 +/- 8) x 10^{-4} with 95% C.L. interval [-19, +14] x 10^{-4}.
- Corresponding SME parameter limits: tilde_X^1_i in [-9, +2] x 10^{-3} and tilde_X^2_i in [-6, +4] x 10^{-3} at 95% C.L.

## Context

This work extends tests of Lorentz invariance into the weak interaction sector using beta decay, complementing earlier searches for beta-emission anisotropy in unpolarized forbidden decays of 90Y, 137Cs, and 99Tc by Newman (1976) and Ullman (1978). The theoretical framework developed by Noordmans (2013) relates the measured parameter xi_2 to the standard model extension (SME) parameters of Kostelecky and coworkers (1998), enabling comparison with other weak-sector tests such as neutral-meson oscillations, neutrino oscillations, and beta-decay endpoint spectrum measurements. The result demonstrates a route toward more detailed testing of Lorentz symmetry in weak interactions, with an optimized setup currently being analyzed that uses polarization perpendicular to the Earth's rotation axis and improved gamma detection less sensitive to beta particles.
