# Superscaling predictions for NC and CC quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Quasielastic double differential neutrino cross sections can be obtained in a phenomenological model based on the superscaling behavior of electron scattering data. In this talk the superscaling approach (SuSA) is reviewed and its validity is tested in a relativistic shell model. Results including meson exchange currents for the kinematics of the MiniBoone experiment are presented.

## Key claim
The superscaling approach (SuSA), which assumes universality of the scaling function extracted from electron scattering data for weak interactions, provides reasonable predictions for charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but only when meson exchange currents (MEC) are included does the model approach the MiniBooNE data, and even then only for lepton scattering angles below about 50 degrees.

## Method
The authors computed the scaling function from inclusive (e,e') data using a relativistic shell model with a Dirac-Equation based potential plus Darwin term (DEB+D) to describe final-state interactions. They then applied this phenomenological scaling function to predict double differential cross sections for CC and NC neutrino-nucleus scattering, and extended the model to include two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) meson exchange current contributions for the CC weak interaction sector.

## Key numbers
- For the CC reaction 12C(νμ,μ−) at neutrino energy ε = 1 GeV, SuSA predictions are applicable and reasonable for lepton scattering angles above 15 degrees, with small error.
- Inclusion of 2p-2h MEC contributions yields results comparable with MiniBooNE data for scattering angles θ ≤ 50 degrees, but lies below the data at larger angles.
- Scaling of the first kind was tested for momentum transfers q = 0.5, 0.7, 1.0, 1.3, and 1.5 GeV/c, with curves collapsing into one.
- Scaling of the second kind was tested for nuclei 12C, 16O, and 40Ca, with curves collapsing into one.

## Context
This work extends the superscaling analysis of electron scattering data (Day 1990, Donnelly 1999) to weak interactions, building on the SuSA framework established by Amaro (2005). The inclusion of MEC follows recent investigations by Amaro (2010a, 2010b) for (e,e') reactions and extends those to the CC weak sector (Amaro 2010c). The results are compared directly with MiniBooNE collaboration data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010), and the authors note that further inclusion of correlation diagrams required by gauge invariance and other relativistic effects may improve agreement with data at larger angles.
