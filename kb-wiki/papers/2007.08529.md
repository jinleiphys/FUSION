# Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering: EFT analysis and nuclear responses

> The cross section for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) depends on the response of the target nucleus to the external current, in the Standard Model (SM) mediated by the exchange of a $Z$ boson. This is typically subsumed into an object called the weak form factor of the nucleus. Here, we provide results for this form factor calculated using the large-scale nuclear shell model for a wide range of nuclei of relevance for current CE$ν$NS experiments, including cesium, iodine, argon, fluorine, sodium, germanium, and xenon. In addition, we provide the responses needed to capture the axial-vector part of the cross section, which does not scale coherently with the number of neutrons, but may become relevant for the SM prediction of CE$ν$NS on target nuclei with nonzero spin. We then generalize the formalism allowing for contributions beyond the SM. In particular, we stress that in this case, even for vector and axial-vector operators, the standard weak form factor does not apply anymore, but needs to be replaced by the appropriate combination of the underlying nuclear structure factors. We provide the corresponding expressions for vector, axial-vector, but also (pseudo-)scalar, tensor, and dipole effective operators, including two-body-current effects as predicted from chiral effective field theory. Finally, we update the spin-dependent structure factors for dark matter scattering off nuclei according to our improved treatment of the axial-vector responses.

## Key claim

The paper provides nuclear form factors and structure factors for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) calculated using large-scale nuclear shell model, covering both Standard Model predictions and beyond-Standard-Model operators; crucially, the authors stress that for vector and axial-vector operators beyond the Standard Model, the standard weak form factor does not apply and must be replaced by the appropriate combination of underlying nuclear structure factors.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale nuclear shell model calculations to obtain weak form factors and nuclear structure factors for nuclei relevant to current CEνNS experiments (cesium, iodine, argon, fluorine, sodium, germanium, and xenon). They derived the decomposition of the CEνNS cross section into Wilson coefficients of effective operators, hadronic matrix elements, and nuclear structure factors, then generalized the formalism to include vector, axial-vector, (pseudo-)scalar, tensor, and dipole effective operators beyond the Standard Model, including two-body-current effects from chiral effective field theory. The nuclear responses were classified into coherent charge responses (F^M_±), semi-coherent spin-orbit responses (F^Φ''_±), and non-coherent axial responses (S_ij).

## Context

This work extends large-scale nuclear shell model calculations previously developed for nuclear structure factors in direct-detection dark matter searches (Menendez 2012, Klos 2013, Hoferichter 2015-2018) to the CEνNS process. The results provide an alternative to the relativistic mean-field methods (Horowitz 2003, Yang 2019) and nonrelativistic energy-density functionals (Patton 2012, Co 2020) currently used for CEνNS interpretation, and complement the first-principles coupled-cluster calculation for argon (Payne 2019). The level of agreement between shell-model, relativistic mean-field, and coupled-cluster results suggests that form factor uncertainties are not as severe as claimed in some previous work (Aristizabal Sierra 2019). The formalism enables improved constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model from current and future CEνNS experiments, and also provides updated spin-dependent structure factors for dark matter scattering.
