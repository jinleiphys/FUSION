# Understanding the symmetry energy using data from the ALADIN-2000 Collaboration taken at the GSI Large Neutron Detector

> The present study deals with the extraction of the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. Using the isospin quantum molecular dynamical (IQMD) model, the dependence of the sum of the charge number for fragments with $Z\ge2$ ($Z_\textrm{bound}$) on the multiplicity of neutrons ($M_n$) from the projectile spectator fragmentation of $^{124}$Sn and $^{124}$La at 600 MeV/nucleon is compared with the experimental results of the ALADIN-2000 Collaboration. The comparison suggest a soft symmetry energy. In addition, the sensitivities of the symmetry energy towards the $Z_\textrm{bound}$ dependence on proton multiplicity ($M_p$), neutron-to-proton single [$R(n/p)$] and double ratio [$R_D(n/p)$] are also examined. The $Z_\textrm{bound}$ dependence of $R(n/p)$ is found to be the most sensitive observable towards the symmetry energy. The ALADIN Collaboration should extend the results for $R(n/p)$ in the near future.

## Key claim

Comparison of IQMD model calculations with ALADIN-2000 Collaboration data for neutron multiplicity from projectile spectator fragmentation of 124Sn and 124La at 600 MeV/nucleon suggests a soft symmetry energy, but the agreement is better for the neutron-rich projectile (124Sn) than for the neutron-poor projectile (124La) due to Coulomb effects.

## Method

The authors used the isospin quantum molecular dynamical (IQMD) model to simulate projectile spectator fragmentation for 124Sn+natSn and 124La+natSn reactions at 600 MeV/nucleon. They compared the calculated dependence of Zbound (sum of charge numbers for fragments with Z≥2) on neutron multiplicity (Mn) against experimental data from the ALADIN-2000 Collaboration, using soft (γi=0.5) and hard (γi=1.5) parametrizations of the symmetry energy. The sensitivity of Zbound dependence on proton multiplicity (Mp), single neutron-to-proton ratio R(n/p), and double neutron-to-proton ratio RD(n/p) was also examined using a sensitivity factor St.

## Key numbers

- Neutron multiplicity maximum: Mn ≈ 11 for 124Sn and Mn ≈ 9 for 124La at Zbound/ZProjectile ≈ 0.7 with soft symmetry energy
- Soft symmetry energy parameter: γi = 0.5; hard symmetry energy parameter: γi = 1.5
- Symmetry kinetic energy coefficient: Cs,k ≈ 25 MeV; symmetry potential energy coefficient: Cs,p = 35.19 MeV
- Coalescence parameters: R0 = 3.5 fm, PFermi = 268 MeV/c
- IQMD model parameters: α = -390 MeV, β = 320 MeV, γ = 1.14 (soft momentum dependent equation of state)
- R(n/p) peak positions: at Zbound/ZProjectile ≈ 0.7 with soft symmetry energy and at Zbound/ZProjectile ≈ 0.5 with hard symmetry energy

## Context

This work extends comparisons of ALADIN-2000 data previously analyzed with the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) by Trautmann (2011), which reproduced data satisfactorily only for Zbound/ZProjectile ≥ 0.5. The IQMD dynamical model reproduces the data across the whole Zbound range, addressing the failure of the statistical model at low Zbound. The study also builds on prior work using neutron-to-proton elliptic flow by Russotto (2011) and Cozma (2011) that favored a soft symmetry energy, and on the use of double neutron-to-proton ratios proposed by Zhang (2008). The authors recommend that the ALADIN Collaboration extend measurements to include R(n/p) data to validate the finding that this observable is most sensitive to the symmetry energy.
