# A view of Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> We review the physics of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and the results and perspectives for the measurements of the radius of the neutron distribution of the nucleus, of the weak mixing angle, and of new neutrino interactions due to physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the current status and future prospects of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) research, emphasizing that while all experimental results to date are compatible with the Standard Model prediction, CEνNS provides a powerful probe of nuclear neutron distributions, the weak mixing angle at low energy, and beyond-Standard-Model physics, but the interpretation of reactor-based results like Dresden-II remains subject to criticism regarding the quenching factor, reactor-related background, and incompatibility with CONUS results.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of CEνNS, including the Standard Model cross-section formula that depends on the weak charge of the nucleus and the neutron and proton form factors. They then summarize experimental results from the COHERENT experiment (using CsI and liquid argon detectors at the Spallation Neutron Source) and the Dresden-II reactor experiment, and discuss how these data are analyzed to extract the neutron distribution radius, the weak mixing angle, and constraints on beyond-Standard-Model interactions mediated by photons, new vector bosons, or scalar bosons.

## Key numbers

- COHERENT CsI detector mass: 14.6 kg; LAr detector mass: 29 kg; Dresden-II germanium detector mass: 3 kg
- Neutrino flux at COHERENT: approximately 4.3 × 10^7 ν cm⁻² s⁻¹; at Dresden-II: approximately 4.8 × 10^13 ν̅_e cm⁻² s⁻¹
- CEνNS coherency condition for a heavy nucleus with A ≈ 100: momentum transfer |q| ≲ 40 MeV, implying nuclear recoil energy T ≲ 10 keV
- Neutrino energy threshold for CEνNS: E_ν ≲ 30 MeV; SNS neutrino energies below about 53 MeV; reactor antineutrino energies below about 10 MeV
- Average neutron distribution radius for CsI from COHERENT data: R_n^(CsI) = 5.47 ± 0.38 fm (AtzoriCorona 2023)
- Upper bound on neutron distribution radius for Ar from COHERENT LAr data: R_n^(Ar) < 4.2 fm at 1σ (Cadeddu 2020)
- Disentangled radii from COHERENT + APV combination: R_n^(Cs) = 5.32^(+0.30)_(-0.23) fm, R_n^(I) = 5.30^(+0.3)_(-0.6) fm (AtzoriCorona 2023)
- Weak mixing angle from COHERENT CsI data alone: sin²ϑ_W = 0.231^(+0.027)_(-0.024) (AtzoriCorona 2023)
- Weak mixing angle from APV on Cs atoms: sin²ϑ_W = 0.2398^(+0.0016)_(-0.0015) (AtzoriCorona 2023)
- Combined COHERENT + APV result with simultaneous neutron distribution determination: sin²ϑ_W = 0.2423^(+0.0032)_(-0.0029) (AtzoriCorona 2023)
- COHERENT CsI data show 6σ evidence of nuclear structure suppression of full coherence (AtzoriCorona 2023)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical prediction of CEνNS by Freedman (1973) and the first experimental observation by the COHERENT collaboration (2017), summarizing results through 2023. It contextualizes CEνNS measurements alongside parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX on ²⁰⁸Pb, CREX on ⁴⁸Ca) as complementary probes of neutron distributions, and compares weak mixing angle determinations with those from high-energy colliders (Tevatron, LEP, SLC, LHC) and other low-energy experiments (APV on Cs atoms). The paper enables future work by projecting improvements from upcoming COHERENT detectors, reactor experiments (CONUS, TEXONO, CONNIE, MINER), and new facilities (European Spallation Source, MOLLER, P2@MESA), while noting that CEνNS constitutes the "neutrino floor/fog" background for dark matter searches.
