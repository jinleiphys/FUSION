# eta, eta-prime photoproduction and electroproduction off protons

> Photo- and electroproduction of eta, eta-prime mesons on protons are investigated within a relativistic chiral unitary approach based on coupled channels. The s wave potentials for electroproduction and meson-baryon scattering are derived from a chiral effective Lagrangian which includes the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporates important features of the underlying QCD Lagrangian such as the axial U(1) anomaly. The effective potentials are iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation and cross sections for eta, eta-prime photo- and electroproduction from protons are obtained. The results for the eta-prime photoproduction cross section reproduce the appearance of an S_(11) resonance around 1.9 GeV observed at ELSA. The inclusion of electromagnetic form factors increases the predicted eta electroproduction cross sections, providing a qualitative explanation for the hard form factor of the photocoupling amplitude observed at CLAS.

## Key claim

The authors' relativistic chiral unitary approach, which includes the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporates the axial U(1) anomaly, can reproduce the appearance of an S_11 resonance around 1.9 GeV in eta-prime photoproduction, but the comparison with data is only valid in the near threshold region because the calculation is restricted to s waves.

## Method

The authors derived s-wave potentials for electroproduction and meson-baryon scattering from a U(3)_L × U(3)_R chiral effective Lagrangian that includes the eta-prime meson and the axial U(1) anomaly. These potentials were iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain the full T-matrix for meson-baryon scattering and the full electric dipole and longitudinal s-wave amplitudes for photo- and electroproduction. A global fit was performed to available data for meson-proton and photon-proton reactions for center-of-mass energies between 1.5 and 2.0 GeV, and the resulting model was used to predict cross sections for eta and eta-prime production.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used in the Lagrangian is f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- The characteristic scale of chiral perturbation theory is Lambda_chi = 4 pi F_pi = 1.2 GeV.
- The global fit to data was performed for center-of-mass energies sqrt(s) between 1.5 and 2.0 GeV.
- The eta electroproduction data from CLAS span invariant momentum transfers Q^2 from 0.375 to 0.875 GeV^2.
- The S_11 resonance in eta-prime photoproduction appears around 1.9 GeV, consistent with the ELSA observation of S_11(1897).
- The eta-prime meson mass is 958 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channel analyses that treated the eta as a pure octet state and neglected eta-eta-prime mixing, such as the approach by Kaiser, Weise, and collaborators. The authors demonstrate that including the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom in a chiral unitary framework can dynamically generate the S_11(1897) resonance observed at ELSA, and that the inclusion of channel-dependent electromagnetic form factors can qualitatively explain the hard form factor of the S_11(1535) photocoupling observed at CLAS. This enables future studies of baryon resonances at energies up to 2 GeV within a chiral symmetry framework, and provides predictions for processes such as pion-induced eta-prime production.
