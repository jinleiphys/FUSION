# Thermo-magnetic effects in quark matter: Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model constrained by lattice QCD

> The phenomenon of inverse magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry in QCD predicted by lattice simulations can be reproduced within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model if the coupling~$G$ of the model decreases with the strength $B$ of the magnetic field and temperature~$T$. The thermo-magnetic dependence of $G(B,T)$ is obtained by fitting recent lattice QCD predictions for the chiral transition order parameter. Different thermodynamic quantities of magnetized quark matter evaluated with $G(B, T)$ are compared with the ones obtained at constant coupling, $G$. The model with $G(B,T)$ predicts a more dramatic chiral transition as the field intensity increases. In addition, the pressure and magnetization always increase with $B$ for a given temperature. Being parametrized by four magnetic field dependent coefficients and having a rather simple exponential thermal dependence our accurate ansatz for the coupling constant can be easily implemented to improve typical model applications to magnetized quark matter.

# Thermo-magnetic effects in quark matter: Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model constrained by lattice QCD

## Key claim

The inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) of chiral symmetry observed in lattice QCD can be reproduced within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model if the four-fermion coupling constant G is made to decrease with both magnetic field strength B and temperature T, based on a precise fit to lattice data rather than on qualitative arguments about asymptotic freedom. The authors show that this same G(B,T) fitted to reproduce the pseudocritical temperature also yields qualitatively correct results for pressure, entropy, energy density, and magnetization, whereas a constant G gives qualitatively different behavior.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor NJL model in the mean-field approximation with a noncovariant cutoff regularization within the magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR) scheme. They fit the thermo-magnetic coupling G(B,T) using a Fermi-type distribution formula with four B-dependent coefficients to reproduce lattice QCD results from Fodor et al. (2016) for the average light-quark condensate (Sigma_u + Sigma_d)/2 at finite temperature and magnetic field. They then compute the normalized pressure, entropy density, energy density, thermal susceptibility, specific heat, sound velocity, interaction measure, and magnetization, comparing results obtained with G(B,T) versus a constant coupling G.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters used: cutoff Lambda = 0.650 GeV, current quark mass m = 5.5 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 86 MeV
- The constant coupling that fits lattice results in vacuum: G = 4.50373 GeV^{-2}
- The extrapolated G(0,0) from the fit: 4.6311 GeV^{-2}, yielding a vacuum condensate <psi_bar_f psi_f>^{1/3}_{00} = -236.374 MeV
- At eB = 0.8 GeV^2, the pressure predicted with G(B,T) is about twice the value at B = 0 around the chiral crossover
- The fitting parameters for G(B,T) at eB = 0.0 GeV^2: c = 0.900, T_a = 0.168 GeV, s = 3.731, beta = 40.000
- The fitting parameters at eB = 0.8 GeV^2: c = 1.289, T_a = 0.158 GeV, s = 1.804, beta = 11.506

## Context

This work extends the approach of Farias et al. (2014) who first showed that IMC could be explained in the NJL model by making G decrease with B and T, but here the parametrization is based on a precise fit to lattice QCD data from Fodor et al. (2016) rather than on qualitative arguments. The results contradict the behavior of the conventional NJL model with constant coupling, which fails to predict IMC and gives qualitatively different thermodynamic quantities. The work enables improved model applications to magnetized quark matter in heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars, and the authors note that a forthcoming study will extend the approach to the SU(3) version with a thermo-magnetic dependence for the six-fermion coupling K as well.
