# Probing the density dependence of symmetry energy via multifragmentation at sub-saturation densities

> Symmetry energy for asymmetric nuclear matter at subsaturation densitieswas investigated in the framework of an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model.Asingle ratio of neutrons and protons is comparedwith the experimental data of Famiano et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 97, 052701 (2006)]. We have also performed a comparison for the double ratio with experimental as well as different theoretical results of Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck in 1997, Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck in 2004, Boltzmann-Nordeim-Vlasov, and Improved QuantumMolecular Dynamics models. It is found that the double ratio predicts the softness of symmetry energy, which is a little underestimated in the single ratio. Furthermore, the study of the single ratio is extended for different kinds of fragments, while the double ratio is extended for different neutron-rich isotopes of Sn.

## Key claim

The double ratio of neutrons to protons predicts a soft symmetry energy (γ_i = 0.5) at sub-saturation densities, while the single ratio slightly underestimates the softness because it still mixes Coulomb effects with the symmetry energy signal.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state (compressibility κ = 200 MeV) and isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. They simulated collisions of 112Sn + 112Sn, 124Sn + 124Sn, and 132Sn + 132Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon for semi-central (b = 2 fm) and semi-peripheral (b = 6 fm) geometries, using both soft (γ_i = 0.5) and stiff (γ_i = 1.5) symmetry energy parametrizations. Clusters were identified via a coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff 268 MeV/c and distance cutoff 3.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Soft symmetry energy corresponds to γ_i = 0.5; stiff symmetry energy corresponds to γ_i = 1.5.
- The kinetic symmetry energy coefficient C_{s,k} = 25 MeV; the symmetry potential energy coefficient C_{s,p} = 35.19 MeV.
- The soft equation of state uses parameters α = -356 MeV, β = 303 MeV, and γ = 7/6, giving isoscalar compressibility κ = 200 MeV.
- The coalescence model uses R_0 = 3.5 fm and P_Fermi = 268 MeV/c.
- The double ratio increases with the neutron difference between isotope pairs: differences of 8, 12, and 20 neutrons were studied for the pairs (132Sn,124Sn), (124Sn,112Sn), and (132Sn,112Sn), respectively.
- The ImQMD model with γ_i = 0.75 at impact parameter b ≤ 2 fm gave the best fit to experimental double ratio data among prior models.

## Context

This work extends prior comparisons of single and double neutron-to-proton ratios from the MSU/NSCL experiment by Famiano et al. (2006) at 50 MeV/nucleon. The authors compare their IQMD results with earlier calculations from the BUU97 model by Li (1997), the IBUU04 model by Li (2006), the BNV model by Wolter (2007), and the ImQMD model by Zhang (2009). The study enables a more systematic test of symmetry energy stiffness by examining three Sn isotopes and by extending the single ratio analysis to light charged particles and intermediate mass fragments up to higher kinetic energies, revealing a cross-over between soft and stiff symmetry energy predictions for fragments.
