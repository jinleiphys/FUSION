# kt-factorization for Hard Processes in Nuclei

> Two widely proposed kt-dependent gluon distributions in the small-x saturation regime are investigated using two particle back-to-back correlations in high energy scattering processes. The Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution, interpreted as the number density of gluon inside the nucleus, is studied in the quark-antiquark jet correlation in deep inelastic scattering. On the other hand, the unintegrated gluon distribution, defined as the Fourier transform of the color-dipole cross section, is probed in the direct photon-jet correlation in pA collisions. Dijet-correlation in pA collisions depends on both gluon distributions through combination and convolution in the large Nc limit.

## Key claim

The authors establish that an effective kt-factorization can be achieved for hard processes in nuclei scattered by a dilute probe, but only by modifying the parton distributions at small-x; these modified distributions are no longer universal across processes, though they can be constructed from a few universal building blocks. The claim is conditional on taking the large nuclear number limit and the back-to-back correlation limit where the transverse momentum imbalance is much smaller than the individual jet momenta.

## Method

The authors analyze two-particle back-to-back correlations in three scattering processes: quark-antiquark jet production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS), direct photon-jet production in pA collisions, and dijet production in pA collisions. They apply power counting in the correlation limit (q_perp much less than P_perp) to extract leading-order contributions, using the Eikonal approximation to simplify scattering amplitudes with multiple gluon exchanges. The results are cross-checked against direct calculations in the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism using color-dipole wave functions and Wilson line correlators.

## Key numbers

- The Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution at small-x for a large nucleus is given by: xG^(1)(x,k_perp) = (S_perp / (pi^2 alpha_s)) * ((N_c^2 - 1)/N_c) * integral (d^2 r_perp / (2pi)^2) * (e^{-i k_perp dot r_perp} / r_perp^2) * (1 - e^{-r_perp^2 Q_s^2 / 4}), where N_c = 3 and Q_s is the gluon saturation scale.
- The differential cross section for the DIS quark-antiquark jet correlation factorizes as: d sigma / d(phase space) = delta(x_gamma* - 1) * x_g G^(1)(x_g, q_perp) * H_{gamma_T* g to q qbar}, with the hard partonic cross section H = alpha_s alpha_em e_q^2 * (hat{s}^2 + Q^4) / (hat{s} + Q^2)^4 * (hat{u}/hat{t} + hat{t}/hat{u}).
- The second gluon distribution (Fourier transform of dipole cross section) is given by: xG^(2)(x, q_perp) approximately equals (q_perp^2 N_c) / (2 pi^2 alpha_s) * S_perp * F_{x_g}(q_perp), where F_{x_g}(q_perp) = integral (d^2 r_perp / (2pi)^2) e^{-i q_perp dot r_perp} S_{x_g}^{(2)}(0, r_perp).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Xiao and Yuan (2010) on non-universality of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions at small-x, and builds on the color glass condensate framework developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Iancu, Leonidov, and McLerran (2002). The results contradict naive kt-factorization for hadron-hadron collisions as shown by Collins and Qiu (2007) and Rogers and Mulders (2010), but demonstrate that an effective factorization can be restored for dilute-on-dense scattering in the large N_c limit. The paper enables direct experimental tests of the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution at the planned Electron-Ion Collider, and provides a complete theoretical framework for interpreting dihadron correlation data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, where previous calculations by Marquet (2007) included only part of the necessary contributions.
