# Extraction of trans-helicity worm-gear distributions and opportunities at the Electron-Ion Collider in China

> We present a global analysis of the trans-helicity worm-gear distribution function, $g_{1T}^\perp$, by fitting the longitudinal-transverse double spin asymmetry data of the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. The analysis is performed within the framework of transverse momentum dependent factorization and evolution. It is found that the $u$-quark favors a positive distribution and the $d$-quark favors a negative distribution, which is consistent with previous model calculations and phenomenological extractions. Based on the fit to existing world data, we also study the impact of the proposed electron-ion collider in China and conclude that it can significantly improve the precision of the worm-gear distribution function and hence enhance our understanding of nucleon spin structures.

## Key claim
The authors perform a global fit of the trans-helicity worm-gear distribution \(g_{1T}^\perp\) to existing SIDIS double-spin asymmetry data and find that the \(u\)-quark distribution is positive while the \(d\)-quark distribution is negative, though the \(d\)-quark result remains consistent with zero within uncertainties. The analysis requires imposing parameter reductions (e.g., equal shape parameters for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks, vanishing sea-quark contributions) because the existing world data are not precise enough to constrain all parameters.

## Method
The authors fit the longitudinal-transverse double spin asymmetry \(A_{LT}^{\cos(\phi_h-\phi_S)}\) from SIDIS data using TMD factorization at small \(\delta = |P_{hT}|/(zQ)\) with TMD evolution at next-to-leading order. The optimal TMD PDFs and FFs are evolved from a saddle point using the SV19 parametrization for unpolarized functions, while the worm-gear distributions are parametrized at the initial scale with a functional form for valence quarks and a separate form for sea quarks (set to zero in the baseline fit). The fit uses 1000 replica data sets to estimate uncertainties, with a total \(\chi^2/N = 0.84\) achieved.

## Key numbers
- Total \(\chi^2/N = 0.84\) for the global fit to world data from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab.
- Data included: 192 data points with \(\delta < 0.5\) and \(Q > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (80 points with \(\delta < 0.3\) and \(Q > 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
- The EicC is designed with a \(3.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) electron beam (80% polarization), a \(20\ \mathrm{GeV}\) proton beam (70% polarization), and instantaneous luminosity \(\sim 2\times10^{33}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-2}\mathrm{s}^{-1}\).
- The truncation for transverse moments is \(k_T^{\rm max} = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological extractions of the worm-gear distribution by Bhattacharya (2021) and Horstmann (2022), which also found positive \(u\)-quark and negative \(d\)-quark distributions, and is consistent with model calculations from the light-cone constituent quark model (Pasquini, 2008; Bacchetta, 2010) and the spectator diquark model (Bacchetta, 2008). The baseline fit enables a quantitative projection of how the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC) will improve precision, filling the kinematic gap between the JLab 12 GeV program (large-\(x\), valence region) and the US EIC (small-\(x\) down to \(10^{-4}\)).
