# Probing the equilibration of the QCD matter created in heavy-ion collisions with dileptons

> A systematic study of intermediate invariant mass dilepton production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV is performed, using next-to-leading-order (NLO) thermal QCD dilepton emission rates with a multistage dynamical approach which includes event-by-event IP-Glasma initial conditions, relativistic viscous fluid dynamics, and a hadronic afterburner. Considering dilepton yield and anisotropic flow, special attention is paid to the out-of-equilibrium aspects, both thermal and chemical, and to the contribution of the Drell-Yan process. The relative contribution of each of those different channels to dilepton observables is calculated and discussed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that dilepton production in the intermediate invariant mass region (approximately 2-4 GeV) is sensitive to the early-time chemical equilibration of the QCD matter, with the preequilibrium stage becoming the dominant source of dileptons for invariant masses above 2.2 GeV, but only under the assumption that the equilibrium emission rate can be applied to the preequilibrium stage via Landau matching.

## Method
The authors used the iEBE-MUSIC multistage model with event-by-event IP-Glasma initial conditions, the KøMPøST preequilibrium evolution (from 0.1 to 0.8 fm/c), relativistic viscous fluid dynamics (MUSIC), and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD). Dilepton production was calculated using next-to-leading-order (NLO) thermal QCD emission rates, with a phenomenological suppression factor SF(T,τ) = 1 - exp(-A τ/τ_R) applied to account for the under-population of quarks and antiquarks during chemical equilibration, where the chemical relaxation time τ_R = 4π η/(Ts) and the constant A was fixed by requiring SF(τ_chem) = 0.9 with τ_chem = 1.2 τ_R.

## Key numbers
- Collision system: Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Initial proper time for preequilibrium (τ_0^EKT): 0.1 fm/c
- Initial hydrodynamics time (τ_0^hydro): 0.8 fm/c
- Specific shear viscosity: 0.12
- Freeze-out energy density (ε_frz): 0.18 GeV/fm³
- Chemical freeze-out temperature (T_frz): 0.166 GeV
- Chemical equilibrium times at three temperatures: 1.19 fm/c (T=0.3 GeV), 1.79 fm/c (T=0.2 GeV), and 3.57 fm/c (T=0.1 GeV)
- Preequilibrium stage becomes dominant over thermal dileptons for invariant mass M > 2.2 GeV
- At invariant mass 4-5 GeV, only 40% of dilepton production remains with quadratic suppression factor compared to instant chemical equilibrium
- Drell-Yan contribution reduces elliptic flow by 2-8% (0-20% centrality), 2-11% (20-40% centrality), and 3-16% (40-60% centrality) at M = 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends the multistage modeling approach previously applied to photon production by Gale et al. (2021) to dilepton observables, using the same KøMPøST preequilibrium framework and IP-Glasma initial conditions. It builds upon studies of chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions by Kurkela and collaborators (2018) and complements earlier dilepton studies by Kasmaei et al. (2018), Churchill et al. (2020), Coquet et al. (2021), and Garcia-Montero et al. (2024) by incorporating realistic initial states, complete NLO emission rates, and full dynamical modeling. The results enable future Bayesian inference studies to determine the preequilibrium lifetime and chemical equilibration timescale from experimental dilepton data.
