# On effective Chern-Simons Term induced by a Local CPT-Violating Coupling using $γ_5$ in Dimensional Regularization

> We resume a long-standing, yet not forgotten, debate on whether a Chern-Simons birefringence can be generated by a local term $b_μ\barψγ^μγ_5ψ$ in the Lagrangian (where $b_μ$ are constants). In the present paper we implement a new way of managing $γ_5$ in dimensional regularization. Gauge invariance in the underlying theory (QED) is enforced by this choice of defining divergent amplitudes. We investigate the singular behavior of the vector meson two-point-function around the $m^2=0$ and $p^2=0$ point. We find that the coefficient of the effective Chern-Simons can be finite or zero. It depends on how one takes the limits: they cannot be interchanged due to the associate change of symmetry. For $m^2=0$ we evaluate also the self-mass of the photon at the second orderin $b_μ$. We find zero.

## Key claim

The coefficient of the effective Chern-Simons term induced by a local CPT-violating axial vector coupling can be either finite or zero, depending on the order in which the limits \(m^2 \to 0\) and \(p^2 \to 0\) are taken; these limits cannot be interchanged due to an associated change in symmetry.

## Method

The authors use dimensional regularization with a recently proposed extension of \(\gamma_5\) (denoted \(\gamma_\chi\)) to generic dimension \(D\), which preserves gauge invariance and Lorentz covariance. They compute the one-loop photon two-point function in QED extended by a constant axial vector term \(b_\mu \bar\psi \gamma^\mu \gamma_5 \psi\), evaluating the divergent and convergent parts of the triangle diagram for the cases \(m^2=0, p^2 \neq 0\); \(p^2=0, m^2 \neq 0\); and \(|p^2| \ll m^2\). The trace of gamma matrices involving \(\gamma_\chi\) is handled via an expansion in powers of \((D-4)\) around \(D=4\).

## Key numbers

* For \(m^2=0\) and \(p^2 \neq 0\), the induced Chern-Simons coefficient is \(c_\mu = b_\mu / (4\pi^2)\) (from eq. total.2).
* For \(p^2=0\) and \(m^2 \neq 0\), the total Chern-Simons coefficient vanishes (from eq. ano.13).
* For \(|p^2| \ll m^2\), the coefficient \(c_\mu\) is proportional to \(p^2 / m^2\) (from the analysis in Section virtual).
* At second order in \(b_\mu\) for \(m^2=0\), the photon self-mass (self-energy) evaluates to zero.

## Context

This work resumes a long-standing debate on whether a Chern-Simons birefringence term can be generated by a local CPT-violating coupling in QED, previously studied by Colladay and Kostelecky (1996) and Altschul (2004). The results disagree with the dimensional regularization calculation of Chung (1998) but agree with calculations using Pauli-Villars regularization. The paper clarifies that the ambiguity at the point \(m^2=0, p^2=0\) arises from a change in symmetry: for \(m^2=0\) the \(b_\mu\) term can be removed by a chiral transformation at the classical level, leaving only an anomalous contribution. This enables a consistent treatment of the \(\gamma_5\) problem in dimensional regularization for CPT-violating extensions of QED.
