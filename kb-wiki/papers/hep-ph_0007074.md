# Deconfinement in QCD at Finite Temperature

> We present a simple and intuitive picture for the deconfinement of quarks and gluons at finite temperature: as the temperature increases, QCD behaves like QED at T=0. We show this by calculating the QCD coupling constant as a function of the temperature and of the external momenta used to probe quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at high temperature and small external momenta squared, QCD's running coupling behaves like that of an Abelian theory (QED at T=0), implying deconfinement. This conclusion depends on the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation, which isolates the dominant gauge-invariant terms and shows that the effective beta function changes sign at small Q².

## Method

The author calculates the QCD beta function at finite temperature using the HTL resummation program of Braaten and Pisarski (1990), which ensures gauge invariance by keeping only the leading T² contributions. The gluon self energy and triple gluon vertex are computed in the high-temperature limit, and the renormalization group equation for the strong coupling is solved to one-loop order. An effective number of colors and flavors is defined to cast the running coupling in the same form as the T=0 theory.

## Key numbers

- The deconfined phase is expected to exist at temperatures above and around 0.2 GeV, based on lattice simulations.
- For T = 0.5 GeV, the effective beta function changes sign around Q² ≈ 0.05 GeV².
- For T = 1 GeV, the effective beta function changes sign around Q² ≈ 0.2 GeV².
- The running coupling is calculated using α_s measured at m_Z ≈ 91 GeV as the reference point, with B(T² = 1 GeV², Q₀² ≈ 83 × 10³ GeV²) ≈ 0.

## Context

This work extends the HTL resummation program of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to provide a physical picture of deconfinement as a transition from a non-Abelian to an Abelian theory. It addresses previous difficulties with gauge invariance in finite-temperature beta function calculations (Kobes 1995, Chaichian 1996, Sasaki 1997) and the vertex dependence of the renormalized coupling (Fujimoto 1988, Nakkagawa 1987, Baier 1990). The results enable perturbative calculations of quark-gluon plasma properties at high temperature and small momentum transfer, and can be extended to finite chemical potential by replacing T² with T² + 3μ_cp²/π² in quark loops.
