# Neutrinos, Weak Interactions, and r-process Nucleosynthesis

> Two of the key issues in understanding the neutron-to-proton ratio in a core-collapse supernova are discussed. One of these is the behavior of the neutrino-nucleon cross sections as supernova energies. The other issue is the many-body properties of the neutrino gas near the core when both one- and two-body interaction terms are included.

# Neutrinos, Weak Interactions, and r-process Nucleosynthesis

## Key claim
The neutron-to-proton ratio in core-collapse supernovae, which determines r-process nucleosynthesis yields, is influenced by two poorly understood factors: the detailed behavior of neutrino-nucleon cross sections at supernova energies (including weak magnetism terms) and the many-body properties of the dense neutrino gas near the core when both one-body MSW and two-body neutrino-neutrino forward scattering terms are included. The paper does not claim to have solved these issues but rather presents recent work toward addressing them, noting that exact solutions to the full neutrino many-body problem have not yet been found.

## Method
The paper examines antineutrino scattering off protons via the reaction \(\overline{\nu}_e + p \rightarrow e^+ + n\) using a covariant matrix element that includes vector, axial-vector, tensor, induced tensor, induced scalar, and induced pseudo-scalar form factors. It analyzes the sensitivity of a proposed low-energy beta-beam facility (with Lorentz boost factor \(\gamma\) between 7 and 14) combined with a water Cerenkov detector to measure these cross sections and determine the weak magnetism form factor. For the neutrino gas many-body problem, the paper develops an SU(2) algebraic formalism with coherent states and a path integral representation of the evolution operator, then applies a stationary path approximation to relate the many-body problem to a Schrodinger-type nonlinear equation.

## Key numbers
- The Cabibbo angle cosine is \(\cos \theta_C = 0.974\).
- The difference in anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons is \(\mu_p - \mu_n = 3.706\).
- At the highest Lorentz boost factor \(\gamma\), the weak magnetism term suppresses the total number of \(\overline{\nu}_e + p \rightarrow e^+ + n\) events in a water Cerenkov detector by as much as 17%.
- The weak magnetism form factor may be determined with better than several percent accuracy using angular distribution information of emitted positrons.

## Context
This work extends the original suggestion by Burbidge et al. (1957) that neutrino-rich ejecta outside the collapsed core in a supernova could be an r-process site, and builds on later proposals that r-process nuclei may be produced at multiple sites (Qian, 1997). The paper addresses two specific issues raised in the context of neutrino-driven wind models (Woosley, 1994; Witti) and binary neutron-star systems (Rosswog, 1998). The beta-beam approach for measuring neutrino-nucleon cross sections was previously proposed by Zucchelli (2002) and developed for low-energy applications by Volpe (2003, 2006), while the algebraic treatment of the neutrino many-body problem extends work by Pantaleone (1992), Qian (1994, 1995), and Balantekin (2006). The paper enables future work on including sterile neutrinos in the algebraic formalism and on identifying signatures of neutrino-flux truncation in the fossil record of r-process isotopes.
