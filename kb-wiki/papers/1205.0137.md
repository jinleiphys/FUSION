# Effective matrix model for deconfinement in pure gauge theories

> We construct matrix models for the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theories, without dynamical quarks, at a nonzero temperature T. We generalize models with zero and one free parameter to study a model with two free parameters: besides perturbative terms ~T^4, we introduce terms ~T^2 and ~T^0. The two N-dependent parameters are determined by fitting to data from numerical simulations on the lattice for the pressure, including the latent heat. Good agreement is found for the pressure in the semi-quark gluon plasma (QGP), which is the region from Tc, the critical temperature, to about ~4 Tc. Above ~1.2 Tc, the pressure is a sum of a perturbative term, ~ +T^4, and a simple non-perturbative term, essentially just a constant times ~ -Tc^2 T^2. For the pressure, the details of the matrix model only enter within a very narrow window, from Tc to ~1.2 Tc, whose width does not change significantly with N. Without further adjustment, the model also agrees well with lattice data for the 't Hooft loop. This is notable, because in contrast to the pressure, the 't Hooft loop is sensitive to the details of the matrix model over the entire semi-QGP. For the (renormalized) Polyakov loop, though, our results disagree sharply with those from the lattice. Matrix models provide a natural and generic explanation for why the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theories is of first order not just for three, but also for four or more colors. Lastly, we consider gauge theories where there is no strict order parameter for deconfinement, such as for a G(2) gauge group. To agree with lattice measurements, in the G(2) matrix model it is essential to add terms which generate complete eigenvalue repulsion in the confining phase.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a two-parameter matrix model for the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theories without dynamical quarks, finding good agreement with lattice data for the pressure and 't Hooft loop but a sharp disagreement for the renormalized Polyakov loop, which the authors identify as "the outstanding problem in the model."

## Method

The authors generalize previous matrix models with zero and one free parameter to a model with two free parameters, introducing terms proportional to T^2 and T^0 in addition to the perturbative T^4 term. The two N-dependent parameters are determined by fitting to lattice data for the pressure and latent heat. The model is then extended to G(2) gauge groups, where additional terms generating complete eigenvalue repulsion in the confining phase are required to match lattice results.

## Key numbers

- The semi-QGP region where the model agrees with lattice pressure data extends from T_c to about 4 T_c
- Above approximately 1.2 T_c, the pressure is a sum of a perturbative term proportional to +T^4 and a non-perturbative term proportional to -T_c^2 T^2
- The width of the window where matrix model details matter for the pressure, from T_c to about 1.2 T_c, does not change significantly with N
- For the order-disorder interface tension at N=3, the two-parameter model gives 0.022 T_c^2 compared to lattice values of 0.0155 T_c^2 (Beinlich 1996) and 0.0194 T_c^2 (Lucini 2005)
- For N=4, the two-parameter model gives 0.093 T_c^2 compared to the lattice value of 0.121 T_c^2 (Lucini 2005)
- For N=6, the two-parameter model gives 0.35 T_c^2 compared to the lattice value of 0.394 T_c^2 (Lucini 2005)
- At infinite N, the two-parameter model gives 0.0139 N^2 T_c^2 compared to the lattice value of 0.0138 N^2 T_c^2 (Lucini 2005)

## Context

This work extends earlier matrix models by Meisinger (2001) and Dumitru (2010) from zero and one free parameter to two free parameters. The model provides a natural explanation for why the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theories is first order for N greater than or equal to 3, in contrast to loop models where the transition could be second order for N greater than or equal to 4. The authors identify a major discrepancy with lattice results for the renormalized Polyakov loop from Gupta (2007) and Mykkanen (2012), where the lattice shows a broad semi-QGP from T_c to at least 3 T_c while the model gives a narrow width of only 1.2 T_c. The work enables future tests through measurements of the 't Hooft loop and mass splitting in the adjoint Higgs phase near T_c.
