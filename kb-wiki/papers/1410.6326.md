# Systematic study of nuclear matrix elements in neutrinoless double-beta decay with a beyond mean-field covariant density functional theory

> We report a systematic study of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) in neutrinoless double-beta decays with a state-of-the-art beyond mean-field covariant density functional theory. The dynamic effects of particle-number and angular-momentum conservations as well as quadrupole shape fluctuations are taken into account with projections and generator coordinate method for both initial and final nuclei. The full relativistic transition operator is adopted to calculate the NMEs. The present systematic studies show that in most of the cases there is a much better agreement with the previous non-relativistic calculation based on the Gogny force than in the case of the nucleus $^{150}$Nd found in Song et al. [Phys. Rev. C 90, 054309 (2014)]. In particular, we find that the total NMEs can be well approximated by the pure axial-vector coupling term with a considerable reduction of the computational effort.

## Key claim

The total nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta decay, calculated with a beyond mean-field covariant density functional theory, show much better agreement with previous non-relativistic Gogny-force calculations than was found for the specific case of $^{150}$Nd in Song et al. (2014), and the total NMEs can be well approximated using only the pure axial-vector coupling term, which considerably reduces computational effort.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of NMEs for ten candidate nuclei using a beyond mean-field covariant density functional theory based on the point-coupling energy density functional PC-PK1. They incorporated dynamic effects of particle-number and angular-momentum conservation, as well as quadrupole shape fluctuations, through projections and the generator coordinate method for both initial and final nuclei. The full relativistic transition operator derived from one-body charge-changing nuclear currents was used to compute the NMEs, and the results were compared with previous non-relativistic calculations using the Gogny D1S force.

## Key numbers

- The dynamic correlation energy from beyond mean-field effects ranges from 1.6 MeV to 6.0 MeV across the candidate nuclei.
- For spherical configurations with particle-number projection, the normalized NME $\tilde M^{0\nu}$ ranges from 3.66 ($^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti) to 13.25 ($^{150}$Nd to $^{150}$Sm).
- The axial-vector coupling term contributes 81% to 95% of the total NME across all nuclei, with most cases showing approximately 94-95%.
- The error from first-order non-relativistic reduction of the transition operator is within 2% for most nuclei, and the tensor term contribution is within 5% of the total NME.
- The final NME values (with full configuration mixing) range from 2.94 ($^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti) to 6.58 ($^{100}$Mo to $^{100}$Ru) for the PC-PK1 calculation.
- Particle-number projection increases the normalized NME by 127% for $^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti, 49% for $^{116}$Cd to $^{116}$Sn, 55% for $^{124}$Sn to $^{124}$Te, and 58% for $^{136}$Xe to $^{136}$Ba.

## Context

This work extends the beyond mean-field density functional theory approach to NME calculations by using a relativistic energy density functional (PC-PK1), whereas previous systematic studies such as Rodriguez et al. (2010) used the non-relativistic Gogny D1S force. The results show that the relativistic and non-relativistic calculations give consistent NMEs for most candidate nuclei, with the notable exception of $^{150}$Nd, where the discrepancy arises from different predictions of single-particle level densities and pairing properties near the Fermi surface. The finding that the total NME can be approximated by the axial-vector coupling term alone enables future studies to reduce computational effort significantly, and the calculated NMEs provide input for extracting upper limits on the effective Majorana neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits.
