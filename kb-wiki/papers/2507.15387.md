# Spin correlations and Bell nonlocality in $Λ\barΛ$ pair production from $e^+e^-$ collisions with a thrust cut

> We present a comprehensive theoretical study of spin correlations in $Λ\barΛ$ production from $e^+e^-$ annihilation, providing the theoretical predictions for the Belle II experiment. Using soft-collinear effective theory, we perform the first resummation of large logarithms for the longitudinal ($C_{LL}$) and transverse ($C_{TT}$) spin correlations for events with a cut on the thrust variable. Our calculation achieves next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy and incorporates the determination of polarized fragmenting jet functions. This framework provides robust predictions with significantly reduced theoretical uncertainties compared to fixed-order parton model approaches. Furthermore, we establish a direct mapping between the experimentally accessible spin correlation, $C_{TT}$, and a testable CHSH-Bell inequality. This result reframes $C_{TT}$ as a quantitative probe of quantum decoherence, providing a novel tool to measure the degree of parton-level entanglement that survives the fragmentation and hadronization process.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first resummation of large logarithms for longitudinal ($C_{LL}$) and transverse ($C_{TT}$) spin correlations in $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ production from $e^+e^-$ collisions with a thrust cut, achieving next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy; however, the predictions are specifically for the dijet limit ($T \to 1$) where fixed-order calculations fail due to large Sudakov logarithms, and the framework assumes factorization within soft-collinear effective theory.

## Method

The authors employ soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for the thrust-differential cross section of polarized $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ pair production, decomposing it into hard, thrust soft, and polarized fragmenting jet functions (FJFs). They perform a Laplace transform to convert the convolution structure into a simple product, enabling all-order resummation of Sudakov logarithms to NNLL accuracy. The polarized FJFs are defined at the operator level as light-cone correlators of gauge-invariant collinear quark fields, extending the unpolarized FJF formalism to include longitudinal and transverse spin dependence for hyperon production.

## Key numbers

- The Born-level partonic cross sections are given by $\mathrm{d} \sigma_0^U/\mathrm{d}\Omega = \mathrm{d} \sigma_0^L/\mathrm{d}\Omega = N_c \alpha_{em}^2 (1+\cos^2\theta)/(4Q^2)$ and $\mathrm{d} \sigma_0^T/\mathrm{d}\Omega = N_c \alpha_{em}^2 \sin^2\theta/(4Q^2)$, where $N_c=3$ is the number of colors.
- The thrust variable $\tau \equiv 1-T$ characterizes event topology, with $\tau \to 0$ corresponding to the dijet limit and $\tau \to 1/2$ describing spherical events.
- The spin correlations are defined as $C_{LL} = \mathrm{d}\sigma^L/\mathrm{d}\sigma^U$ and $C_{TT} = \mathrm{d}\sigma^T/\mathrm{d}\sigma^U$, where in the parton model $C_{LL} = \sum_q e_q^2 \mathcal{D}^L_{\Lambda/q}(z_1) \mathcal{D}^L_{\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{q}}(z_2) / \sum_q e_q^2 \mathcal{D}^U_{\Lambda/q}(z_1) \mathcal{D}^U_{\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{q}}(z_2)$ and $C_{TT} = \sum_q e_q^2 \sin^2\theta \mathcal{D}^T_{\Lambda/q}(z_1) \mathcal{D}^T_{\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{q}}(z_2) / \sum_q e_q^2 (1+\cos^2\theta) \mathcal{D}^U_{\Lambda/q}(z_1) \mathcal{D}^U_{\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{q}}(z_2)$.

## Context

This work extends the parton model framework for spin correlations in $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ production (Chen 1994, Yang 2024) by incorporating systematic QCD resummation effects under kinematic constraints imposed by a thrust cut, which was previously lacking. The formalism builds on the fragmenting jet function approach developed by Procura (2009) and Jain (2011) for unpolarized hadrons, generalizing it to polarized hyperon production. The results enable precise predictions for upcoming Belle II measurements (Belle-II 2018) and establish a direct mapping between $C_{TT}$ and a testable CHSH-Bell inequality, providing a novel tool to quantify quantum decoherence induced by the fragmentation and hadronization process.
