# Deuteron Production in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions: A Comparison of the Coalescence and the Minimum Spanning Tree Procedure

> The formation of deuterons in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies is investigated by employing two recently advanced models -- the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method and the coalescence model by embedding them in the PHQMD and the UrQMD transport approaches. While the coalescence mechanism combines nucleons into deuterons at the kinetic freeze-out hypersurface, the MST identifies the clusters during the different stages of time evolution. We find that both clustering procedures give very similar results for the deuteron observables in the UrQMD as well as in the PHQMD environment. Moreover, the results agree well with the experimental data on deuteron production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.8$ GeV (selected for the comparison of the methods and models in this study). A detailed investigation shows that the coordinate space distribution of the produced deuterons differs from that of the free nucleons and other hadrons. Thus, deuterons are not destroyed by additional rescattering.

## Key claim

The coalescence and Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) procedures yield very similar results for deuteron observables when applied to the same underlying nucleon distributions from either the UrQMD or PHQMD transport models, and both agree well with experimental data for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 GeV, provided the MST analysis is performed at an appropriate freeze-out time.

## Method

The authors embedded two cluster recognition algorithms, the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) method and a phase-space coalescence model, into both the PHQMD and UrQMD transport approaches. The MST algorithm identifies clusters based on spatial proximity (cutoff r0 = 4 fm in the local rest frame) at a given time during the evolution, while the coalescence model combines proton-neutron pairs at kinetic freeze-out if their relative momentum is below 0.285 GeV and relative distance below 3.575 fm, with a spin-isospin probability factor of 3/8. Both transport models were run in cascade mode (no potential) and potential mode (with density-dependent Skyrme-like nucleon-nucleon potentials).

## Key numbers

* Deuteron yield in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 GeV: both coalescence and MST procedures reproduce the NA49 experimental data across rapidity and transverse momentum distributions.
* Coalescence parameters: relative momentum cutoff Delta P < 0.285 GeV and relative distance cutoff Delta R < 3.575 fm for proton-neutron pairs to form a deuteron.
* MST spatial cutoff: r0 = 4 fm in the local rest frame of the cluster.
* Spin-isospin combinatorial factor for deuteron formation: P_d = 3/8.
* MST analysis times: t0 = 100 fm/c for UrQMD potential mode, t0 = 60 fm/c for UrQMD cascade mode, and t0 = 50 fm/c for PHQMD (both modes).
* Proton yield difference: UrQMD has about 15% more protons than PHQMD, compensated by more strange baryons in PHQMD.
* Systematic uncertainty in nucleon phase-space distributions between models: 15-30% at central rapidities and transverse momenta below 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light cluster production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, comparing the MST method (previously used in PHQMD by Aichelin (2019) and Glassel (2021)) with the coalescence approach (previously applied to UrQMD by Sombun (2018) and Botvina (2014, 2016)). The study uses the psMST library developed by Kireyeu (2021) to apply both algorithms on equal footing to both transport models. The results confirm the earlier finding by Glassel (2021) that deuterons survive the expansion phase because they occupy different coordinate-space regions than free nucleons and hadrons, and they enable future applications of either clustering method to other collision energies and systems with confidence in their equivalence.
