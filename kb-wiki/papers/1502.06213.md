# Hadrons from Coalescence plus Fragmentation in AA collisions from RHIC to LHC energy

> In a coalescence plus independent fragmentation approach we calculate the $p_T$ spectra of the main hadrons: $π, K, p, \bar p, Λ, ϕ$ in a wide range of transverse momentum from low $p_T$ up to about 10 GeV. The approach in its main features was developed several years ago at RHIC energy. Augmenting the model with the inclusion of some more main resonance decays, we show that the approach correctly predicts the evolution of the $p_T$ spectra from RHIC to LHC energy and in particular the baryon-to-meson ratios $p/π,\bar p/π,Λ/K$ that reach a value of the order of unit at $p_T \sim 3\, \rm GeV$. This is achieved without any change of the coalescence parameters. The more recent availability of experimental data up to $p_T \sim 10\rm\, GeV$ for $Λ$ spectrum as well as for $p/π$ and $Λ/K$ shows some lack of yield in a limited $p_T$ range around 6 GeV. This indicates that the baryons $p_T$ spectra from AKK fragmentation functions are too flat at $p_T\lesssim 8 \,\rm GeV$. We also show that in a coalescence plus fragmentation approach one predicts a nearly $p_T$ independent $p/ϕ$ ratio up to $p_T \sim 4 \rm\, GeV$ followed by a significant decrease at higher $p_T$. Such a behavior is driven by a similar radial flow effect at $p_T< 2 \,\rm GeV$ and the dominance of fragmentation for $ϕ$ at larger $p_T$.

## Key claim

The coalescence plus fragmentation model, without any change of coalescence parameters, correctly predicts the evolution of hadron transverse momentum spectra from RHIC to LHC energy and baryon-to-meson ratios reaching unity at pT ~ 3 GeV, but shows a systematic lack of yield around pT ~ 6 GeV for baryons, indicating that the AKK fragmentation functions for baryons are too flat at pT less than about 8 GeV.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant coalescence model based on the Wigner formalism, solved via Monte Carlo, to hadronize thermalized quark-gluon plasma partons at T = 165 MeV with radial flow, combined with independent fragmentation using AKK fragmentation functions for mini-jet partons above pT ~ 3 GeV. The fireball parameters (radial flow, volume, lifetime) are constrained by matching experimental charged particle multiplicity and transverse energy at both RHIC Au+Au sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV and LHC Pb+Pb sqrt(s) = 2.76 ATeV for central collisions (0-10%). Resonance decays from states including rho, omega, K*, Delta, Sigma, and Lambda(1405) are included to improve the low-pT description.

## Key numbers

- Baryon-to-meson ratios p/pi, anti-p/pi, Lambda/K reach a value of order unity at pT ~ 3 GeV
- At RHIC: beta_max = 0.37, R_perp = 8.7 fm, tau = 4.5 fm/c, volume ~ 1100 fm^3, dN_ch/dy ~ 670, dE_T/dy = 760 GeV
- At LHC: beta_max = 0.60, R_perp = 10.2 fm, tau = 7.8 fm/c, volume ~ 2500 fm^3, dN_ch/dy ~ 1600, dE_T/dy = 2100 GeV
- Quark masses: m_u,d = 330 MeV, m_s = 450 MeV; temperature T = 165 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential mu_b = 10 MeV at RHIC, approximated to zero at LHC
- Wigner function parameters: Delta_p^meson = 0.19 GeV, Delta_p^proton = 0.33 GeV, Delta_p^Lambda = 0.38 GeV
- Energy density of fireball epsilon ~ 0.7 GeV/fm^3 at both RHIC and LHC
- For anti-protons, coalescence and fragmentation contributions become comparable at pT ~ 5 GeV; for pions, at pT ~ 3.5 GeV; for Lambda, fragmentation becomes dominant at pT > 6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the coalescence plus fragmentation approach of Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) from RHIC to LHC energies, adding more resonance decays and updating to AKK fragmentation functions. It enables testing the model against new experimental data up to pT ~ 10 GeV for Lambda spectra and baryon-to-meson ratios, revealing a systematic deficit around pT ~ 6 GeV that points to needed improvements in baryon fragmentation functions or inclusion of in-medium parton shower effects. The approach contrasts with the ALCOR model of Biro, Levai, and Zimanyi (1994) and the unitarity-enforcing approach of Shao, Wang, and collaborators (2009), which require fitted parameters to describe hadron spectra.
