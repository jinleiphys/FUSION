# Density Matrix Functional Theory for the Lipkin model

> A Density Matrix Functional theory is constructed semi-empirically for the two-level Lipkin model. This theory, based on natural orbitals and occupation numbers, is shown to provide a good description for the ground state energy of the system as the two-body interaction and particle number vary. The application of Density Matrix Functional theory to the Lipkin model illustrates that it could be a valuable tool for systems presenting a shape phase-transition such as nuclei. The improvement of one-body observables description as well as the interest for Energy Density Functional theory are discussed.

# Density Matrix Functional Theory for the Lipkin Model

## Key claim

A semi-empirical Density Matrix Functional Theory (DMFT) constructed for the two-level Lipkin model provides a good description of the ground state energy across varying particle number and two-body interaction strength, but only after introducing an empirically determined N-dependent reduction factor that scales as N^{-2/3} to correct for the overestimation of correlation energy that occurs when the N=2 functional form is naively extended to larger particle numbers.

## Method

The author constructs a DMFT functional for the Lipkin model by separating the total energy into a mean-field part (expressed as a functional of the one-body density matrix) and a correlation part. The correlation functional is first derived exactly for the N=2 case, then extended to N ≥ 3 by assuming the total correlation energy is a sum over all N(N-1)/2 pairs, each contributing as in the N=2 case. A reduction factor η(N) is introduced and optimized by minimizing the integrated squared deviation between the DMFT minimum energy and the exact ground state energy over χ ∈ [0,3] for each N. The optimal η(N) is found to follow η(N) = 1.5 N^{-2/3}. The functional is minimized with respect to the mixing angle α and occupation number n₀ (with φ=0) to obtain the ground state energy.

## Key numbers

- The optimal reduction factor follows η(N) = 1.5 N^{-2/3}, determined by fitting optimal η values for N from 5 to 20
- The correlation energy scales as N^{4/3} when using the semi-empirical functional with η(N) = 1.5 N^{-2/3}
- For N=2, the exact ground state energy is E = -√(1+χ²) where χ = V(N-1)/ε
- The Hartree-Fock energy is E⁰_HF = -εN/2 for χ ≤ 1 and E⁰_HF = -(εN/4χ)(1+χ²) for χ > 1
- The critical χ for symmetry breaking (where α becomes non-zero) is infinite for N=2, approximately 1.6 for N=5, and approaches 1 as N → ∞

## Context

This work applies DMFT, originally proposed by Gilbert (1975), to the two-level Lipkin model (Lipkin 1965) as a test case for systems exhibiting shape phase transitions. The Hartree-Fock approximation fails for this model, particularly near χ ∼ 1 (Aga 1966), while configuration mixing methods like the Generator Coordinate Method (Rin 1980) provide accurate results but are computationally demanding. The DMFT approach offers a less computationally intensive alternative that could be extended to nuclear Energy Density Functional theory, where current single-reference EDF approaches (Skyrme or Gogny type) face difficulties when extended to multi-reference calculations (Dob 2007). The scaling behavior η(N) ∝ N^{-2/3} is consistent with the analytical large-N scaling of ⟨J²_x⟩/N² obtained by Dus (2004).
