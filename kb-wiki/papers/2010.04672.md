# BCS-BEC Crossover Effects and Pseudogap in Neutron Matter

> Due to the large neutron-neutron scattering length, dilute neutron matter resembles the unitary Fermi gas, which lies half-way in the crossover from the BCS phase of weakly coupled Cooper pairs to the Bose-Einstein condensate of dimers. We discuss crossover effects in analogy with the T-matrix theory used in the physics of ultracold atoms, which we generalize to the case of a non-separable finite-range interaction. A problem of the standard Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach and different ways to solve it are discussed. It is shown that in the strong-coupling regime, the spectral function exhibits a pseudo-gap at temperatures above the critical temperature T_c. The effect of the correlated density on the density dependence of T_c is found to be rather weak, but a possibly important effect due to the reduced quasiparticle weight is identified.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in dilute neutron matter near the unitary limit, the spectral function exhibits a pseudogap at temperatures slightly above the critical temperature T_c, but this effect is weak and likely too small to be observable in neutron stars. The authors identify that the standard Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) approach gives unsatisfactory occupation numbers, and they demonstrate that subtracting the full self-energy rather than only the Hartree-Fock part yields more consistent results.

## Method
The authors compute the T-matrix (vertex function) within the ladder approximation using both a non-separable V_lowk interaction and a separable Gaussian interaction fitted to reproduce the BCS critical temperature. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using Weinberg eigenvalues and eigenvectors, then compute the self-energy, spectral function, and occupation numbers from the Dyson equation truncated at first order or solved fully. The critical temperature is determined via the Thouless criterion using the condition that the maximum Weinberg eigenvalue equals one at k=0, ω=0.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a ≈ -18.7 ± 0.6 fm (Gonzales 1999) or -16.59 ± 1.17 fm (Babenko 2013)
- Effective range of nn interaction: r₀ = 2.83 ± 0.11 fm (Babenko 2013)
- Separable interaction parameters fitted to V_lowk with cutoff Λ = 2 fm⁻¹: g_l=0 = -1.644 fm, q₀ = 1.367 fm⁻¹
- Separable interaction scattering length: a = -15.9 fm
- Conversion factor: 1 fm⁻² = 41.44 MeV
- Critical temperatures shown for μ = 1, 5, 10, 20 MeV at T = 1.01 T_c(μ): 0.27, 1.09, 1.57, and 1.53 MeV respectively
- For V_lowk with cutoff Λ, the chemical potential must satisfy μ [MeV] ≲ 4 (Λ [fm⁻¹])² for the results to be valid

## Context
This work extends previous NSR-like studies of BCS-BEC crossover effects in neutron matter by Ramanan (2013, 2018, 2020), Tajima (2019), and Ohashi (2020), but now computes the self-energy explicitly to access the spectral function and occupation numbers. The pseudogap has been predicted for symmetric nuclear matter by Schnell (1999) and observed experimentally in ultracold Fermi gases by Gaebler (2010), but the authors note that self-consistent T-matrix approaches and quantum Monte-Carlo calculations (Jensen 2019) show less pronounced pseudogap effects. The results enable future studies of the spin susceptibility and specific heat in neutron matter, though these effects are probably too weak for neutron star observations.
