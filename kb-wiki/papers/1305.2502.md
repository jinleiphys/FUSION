# Three-nucleon forces and nuclei at the extremes

> Neutron-rich nuclei become increasingly sensitive to three-nucleon forces. These components of nuclear forces are at the forefront of theoretical developments based on effective field theories of quantum chromodynamics. We discuss our understanding of three-nucleon forces and their impact on exotic nuclei, and show how new measurements test and constrain them. Three-nucleon forces therefore provide an exciting link between theoretical and experimental nuclear physics frontiers.

# Three-nucleon forces and nuclei at the extremes

## Key claim
Three-nucleon forces, as derived from chiral effective field theory, are essential for explaining the location of the neutron dripline in oxygen isotopes and the magic number N=28 in calcium isotopes, but the paper's central hedge is that these forces are most impactful in neutron-rich systems where they provide repulsive interactions between valence neutrons, and that the predicted radius range for neutron stars depends in about equal amounts on the uncertainty in three-nucleon forces and on the extrapolation to high densities.

## Method
The author uses chiral effective field theory (EFT) to derive nuclear forces organized in a systematic expansion in powers of Q/Λ_b, where Λ_b ~ 500 MeV, including one- and multi-pion exchanges and contact interactions. Three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO) are included with two low-energy couplings c_D and c_E fit to ^3H and ^4He, and the calculations are applied in shell-model and many-body frameworks to predict ground-state energies, excitation energies, and two-neutron separation energies for oxygen, calcium, and N=8 isotones. For neutron matter, the first complete N^3LO calculation including NN, 3N, and 4N forces is performed to constrain the equation of state and neutron star properties.

## Key numbers
- The breakdown scale of chiral EFT is Λ_b ~ 500 MeV.
- The neutron dripline in oxygen isotopes shifts from ^28O (with NN forces only) to the experimentally observed ^24O when chiral 3N forces are included.
- The predicted 2^+ excitation energy in ^54Ca is 1.7 – 2.2 MeV (with 3N forces to first order only, the 2^+ energy is higher).
- The neutron skin thickness of ^208Pb is predicted to be 0.17 ± 0.03 fm, in agreement with a determination from the complete electric dipole response.
- The radius of a typical 1.4 M_sun neutron star is constrained to R = 9.7 – 13.9 km, based on N^3LO neutron-matter results combined with the heaviest 2 M_sun neutron star.
- The two-neutron separation energy from ^50Ca to ^52Ca shows a flat behavior, in excellent agreement with new precision TITAN Penning-trap mass measurements.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of three-nucleon forces from chiral EFT, building on prior developments by the author and collaborators (e.g., the oxygen anomaly explanation in Oxygen (2012) and calcium studies in Calcium (2012) and CCCa (2013)). It connects theoretical predictions for neutron-rich nuclei with experimental measurements at rare isotope beam facilities such as MoNA and R3B-LAND, and with neutron star observations including the 2 M_sun neutron star from Demorest (2010). The results enable tighter constraints on the symmetry energy and its density derivative, and provide a framework for future studies of proton-rich nuclei along the N=8 and N=20 isotones to the proton dripline.
