# Quenching of N = 28 shell gap and a low-lying quadrupole mode in the vicinity of neutron-rich N = 28 isotones

> We investigate the low-lying 2$^+$ states of $N=28$ isotones ($^{48}$Ca, $^{46}$Ar, $^{44}$S and $^{42}$Si) with using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory in which the pairing is taken into account self-consistently. The quadrupole mode with very small excitation energies emerges in $^{46}$Ar, $^{44}$S and $^{42}$Si due to the strong quadrupole correlations triggered by the quenching of the $N=28$ shell gap. The importance of the quadrupole correlations between the protons and neutrons, and the role of the pairing correlation to reinforce the lowest 2$^+$ mode are discussed. It is also shown that the $B$(E2$\uparrow$) values of $^{48}$Ca and $^{46}$Ar are plausibly explained by our calculations.

## Key claim

The quenching of the N=28 shell gap, combined with proton holes in the sd-shell, triggers strong quadrupole correlations between protons and neutrons that generate very low-lying 2+ states in neutron-rich N=28 isotones, with the pairing correlation enhancing the B(E2↑) values by about 20%.

## Method

The authors applied the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory with the Skyrme SkM* interaction for the particle-hole channel and a simple pairing form for the particle-particle channel. They performed linear response calculations by adding a weak instantaneous quadrupole external field to the ground state and extracted strength functions through Fourier transformation of the time-dependent expectation values, using a smoothing parameter of 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

- N=28 shell gap in ⁴⁸Ca: 3.86 MeV; in ⁴⁶Ar: 3.5 MeV (reduced compared to ⁴⁸Ca)
- ⁴⁶Ar ground state pairing gaps: proton Δᵖ = 1.16 MeV, neutron Δⁿ = 1.77 MeV
- ⁴⁸Ca: calculated 2⁺ peaks at 3.40 and 9.12 MeV below the IS GQR at 17 MeV; experimental 2₁⁺ at 3.83 MeV with B(E2↑)=95±32 e²fm⁴
- ⁴⁶Ar: very strong peaks emerge around 1 MeV in all channels; experimental B(E2↑)=196±39 e²fm⁴ at E(2₁⁺)=1.58 MeV from Coulomb excitation
- ⁴⁴S: slightly deformed shape with β=0.08; ⁴²Si: oblate shape with β=-0.19
- Pairing correlation enhances B(E2↑) by about 20% in this work

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of N=28 shell quenching, which has been experimentally investigated by Sorlin et al. (1996) and theoretically by Retamosa et al. (1997). The calculations use the Cb-TDHFB method previously developed by Ebata and Nakatsukasa (2010) and applied to dipole and quadrupole modes. The results show that the strong quadrupole correlations between protons and neutrons, coupled with pairing correlations, generate low-lying 2⁺ states in ⁴⁶Ar, ⁴⁴S, and ⁴²Si, and the calculated B(E2↑) for ⁴⁶Ar agrees with the Coulomb-excitation measurement but not with the lifetime measurement by Mengoni et al. (2010).
