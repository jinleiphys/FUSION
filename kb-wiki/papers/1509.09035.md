# Nuclear Reactions at Intermediate Energies

> In the domain of Nuclear reactions at intermediate energies, the QCD coupling constant $α_s$ is large enough ($\sim$ 0.3 - 0.5) to render the perturbative calculational techniques inapplicable. In this regime the quarks are confined into colorless hadrons and it is expected that effective field theories of hadron interactions via exchange of hadrons, provide useful tools to describe such reactions. In this contribution we discuss applications of one such theory, the effective Lagrangian model, in describing the hadronic reactions at intermediate energies whose measurements are the focus of a vast international experimental program.

# Nuclear Reactions at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
Effective Lagrangian models, particularly the coupled-channels K-matrix method and single-channel effective Lagrangian models, can describe intermediate-energy hadronic reactions reasonably well despite the inapplicability of perturbative QCD, but these models invariably require *a priori* knowledge of unknown parameters related to coupling constants and hadron masses.

## Method
The author applies a coupled-channels K-matrix method to meson production in photon-nucleon reactions, where the S-matrix is unitary provided the K-matrix is taken to be real and Hermitian, and all non-resonance contributions from Born, u-channel, and t-channel diagrams are generated without new parameters. For double strangeness production in K- induced reactions and charmed hadron production in antiproton-proton annihilation, a single-channel effective Lagrangian model (SCELM) is used, where baryons are treated as Dirac particles and the full field-theoretic structure of interaction vertices is retained.

## Key numbers
- QCD coupling constant α_s at intermediate energies is approximately 0.3 to 0.5, rendering perturbative methods inapplicable
- For the γ p → η p reaction, the S_11(1535) resonance dominates the total cross section from near threshold to invariant mass W values of 1.7 GeV (corresponding to E_γ ∼ 1.1 GeV)
- The S_11(1650) resonance is important for E_γ between 0.950 GeV and 1.28 GeV, while the P_13(1720) resonance contributes for E_γ between 1.1 GeV and 2.2 GeV
- For the p(K-, K+)Ξ- reaction, the zero degree differential cross sections for Ξ- hypernuclear production on 12C and 28Si peak around beam momentum of 1.0 GeV/c with peak cross section of more than 1 μb
- The total cross section for the p̅ p → Λ̅_c- Λ_c+ reaction at p̅ beam momentum of 15 GeV/c is around 25 μb
- The total cross section for the p̅ + p → D̅0 D0 reaction at p̅ beam momentum of 15 GeV/c is around 550 nb

## Context
This work extends the application of effective Lagrangian methods to intermediate-energy nuclear reactions where perturbative QCD fails, building on prior coupled-channels K-matrix studies by Shyam (2008, 2010) and the Giessen group (Shklyar, 2013; Cao, 2013). The predictions for charmed hadron production in antiproton-proton annihilation are directly relevant to the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, and the Ξ- hypernuclear production calculations will be tested at future J-PARC experiments, complementing lattice QCD approaches that remain computationally challenging for reaction problems (Briceno, 2014).
