# Parity violation in low energy neutron deuteron scattering

> Parity violating effects for low energy elastic neutron deuteron scattering are calculated for DDH and EFT-type of weak potentials in a Distorted Wave Born Approximation, using realistic hadronic strong interaction wave functions, obtained by solving three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space. The results of relation between physical observables and low energy constants can be used to fix low energy constants from experiments. Potential model dependencies of parity violating effects are discussed.

## Key claim

Parity violating observables in low-energy neutron-deuteron scattering can be calculated using a "hybrid" Distorted Wave Born Approximation approach that combines exact three-body Faddeev wave functions from realistic strong interactions with DDH and EFT-type weak potentials, but the results depend on the choice of cutoff parameters and strong interaction models, limiting the precision with which low energy constants can be fixed from experiments.

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space using realistic hadronic strong interaction potentials (AV18+UIX) to obtain parity-conserving wave functions for neutron-deuteron scattering. They then applied Distorted Wave Born Approximation to calculate parity violating matrix elements using three types of weak potentials: the standard DDH meson-exchange potential, a pionless EFT potential, and a pionful EFT potential, each with two different cutoff parameter sets. The parity violating observables (neutron spin rotation angle per unit length and the relative difference of total cross sections for opposite helicities) were expressed as linear combinations of matrix elements of the weak potential operators between the strong-interaction wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron spin rotation angle per unit length is finite and well-defined in the zero energy limit of n-d scattering, calculated by evaluating Im(R)/p^(l'+l+1) at zero energy
- The parameter P (relative difference of total cross sections) is calculated at 15 KeV neutron kinetic energy in the laboratory system, where real and imaginary parts of R-matrix elements become comparable
- For DDH-II potential with AV18+UIX strong interaction, the dominant contribution to PV effects comes from the J=3/2 channel, with the n=1 operator giving -0.522e+00 fm^2 for the S'=3/2, J=3/2 matrix element
- Cutoff parameters used: DDH-I (Lambda_pi=1.72 GeV, Lambda_rho=1.31 GeV, Lambda_omega=1.50 GeV), DDH-II (infinite cutoffs); pionless EFT-I (mu=0.138 GeV), pionless EFT-II (mu=1.0 GeV); pionful EFT-I (Lambda=0.8 GeV), pionful EFT-II (Lambda=1.0 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach previously applied to weak and electromagnetic processes in three-body and four-body systems by Song (2007), Lazauskas (2009), and others, and addresses the growing recognition that the traditional DDH method (Desplanques 1979) cannot reliably describe the full set of experimental parity violating data within the same parameter set. The results provide a pathway to constrain low energy constants from experiments on few-body systems, which is necessary because the five independent observable parameters in two-body systems cannot all be measured with existing experimental techniques, as noted by Girlanda (2008), Phillips (2008), and Schindler (2009).
