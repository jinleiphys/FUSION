# Alpha particle production by molecular single-particle effect in reactions of $^{9}$Be just above the Coulomb barrier

> The $α$-particle production in the dissociation of $^{9}$Be on $^{209}$Bi and $^{64}$Zn at energies just above the Coulomb barrier is studied within the two-center shell model approach. The dissociation of $^{9}$Be on $^{209}$Bi is caused by a molecular single-particle effect (Landau-Zener mechanism) before the nuclei reach the Coulomb barrier. Molecular single-particle effects do not occur at that stage of the collision for $^{9}$Be+$^{64}$Zn, and this explains the absence of fusion suppression observed for this system. The polarisation of the energy level of the last neutron of $^{9}$Be and, therefore the existence of avoided crossings with that level, depends on the structure of the target.

## Key claim

The dissociation of ⁹Be on ²⁰⁹Bi just above the Coulomb barrier is caused by a molecular single-particle effect (Landau-Zener mechanism) at two very close avoided crossings before the nuclei reach the barrier, whereas this effect does not occur for ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn at that stage of the collision, explaining the absence of fusion suppression for the latter system; the polarisation of the last neutron energy level of ⁹Be, and therefore the existence of avoided crossings, depends on the target structure.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic two-center shell model (TCSM) to calculate single-particle neutron levels for the systems ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi and ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn as a function of internuclear separation. The dissociation probability of ⁹Be at isolated avoided crossings was computed using the Landau-Zener formula, with radial velocities obtained from classical equations of motion for the nucleus-nucleus potential derived from the TCSM and Strutinsky method. Cross sections for α-particle production were calculated by summing over partial waves, distinguishing between complete fusion (l ≤ l_cr) and scattering (l_cr ≤ l ≤ l_max) trajectories.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi, the coupling matrix elements |H'₁₂| at avoided crossings 1 and 2 are 0.1073 MeV and 0.1250 MeV, respectively; the corresponding derivatives d(ε₁-ε₂)/dr are 0.0184 MeV fm⁻¹ and 0.0352 MeV fm⁻¹.
- Calculated cross sections for α-particle production in ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi underestimate experimental data by a factor of 3.7-4.7 in the low range of incident energies studied.
- For ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi, calculations were performed at incident energies E_cm = 44.1 MeV (l_cr = 8, l_max = 14) and E_cm = 57.5 MeV (l_cr = 16, l_max = 26).
- For ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn, the Coulomb barrier is V_B ≈ 15.5 MeV at r_B ≈ 9.43 fm from the TCSM calculation, compared to experimental values V_B_exp = 16.2 MeV and r_B_exp = 10 fm.
- For ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn, the touching configuration occurs at r_t ≈ 7.45 fm and a neck starts to form at r ≈ 9.21 fm.
- The Coulomb barrier for ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi is V_B ≈ 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TCSM study of ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi (Diaz-Torres, Thompson, and Scheid, 2004, Nucl. Phys. A, in press) by comparing with the ⁹Be+⁶⁴Zn system, where experimental data from Moraes et al. (2000) show no fusion suppression, in contrast to the ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi system studied by Signorini et al. (2001). The results enable future experimental clarification of α-particle production cross sections in ⁹Be+²⁰⁹Bi and suggest that molecular single-particle effects in the approach phase may serve as a general indicator of fusion suppression mechanisms for weakly bound projectiles on heavy targets.
