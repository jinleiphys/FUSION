# Subthreshold $K^+$ Production on Nuclei by $π^+$ Mesons

> The inclusive $K^+$ mesons production in $π^+$--nucleus reactions in the subthreshold energy regime is analyzed with respect to the one--step ($π^+n\to K^+Λ$) and the two--step ($π^+n\to ηp_1,~ ηp_2\to K^+Λ$) incoherent production processes on the basis of an appropriate folding model, which allows one to take into account the various forms of an internal nucleon momentum distribution as well as on-- and off--shell propagation of the struck target nucleon. Contrary to proton--nucleus reactions primary reaction channel is found to be significant practically at all considered energies. Detailed predictions for the $K^+$ total and invariant differential cross sections from \piC-- and \piPb--collisions at subthreshold energies are provided.

## Key claim
Contrary to proton-nucleus reactions, the one-step primary channel ($\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$) dominates subthreshold $K^+$ production in pion-nucleus collisions at all considered energies when the struck target nucleon is assumed on-shell; however, under the off-shell assumption, the one-step and two-step ($\pi^+n\to \eta p$, $\eta p\to K^+\Lambda$) channels become comparable only at very low energies ($\epsilon_0 < 600$ MeV) when using nucleon momentum distributions without large high-momentum tails.

## Method
The authors developed a folding model that convolves the elementary $\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$ cross section (parameterized from Cugnon et al. (1990)) with the nuclear density and various nucleon momentum distributions, including shell-model, exponential-tail, double-Gaussian, Fermi gas, Hartree-Fock, and correlated-nuclear-matter forms. For the two-step process, they folded the $\pi^+n\to \eta p$ cross section (parametrized from experimental data assuming isospin invariance) with the $\eta p\to K^+\Lambda$ cross section (estimated by scaling to the $\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$ cross section at the same invariant energy) and an effective number of np pairs. The model accounts for both on-shell and off-shell propagation of the struck target nucleon, and neglects kaon rescattering (justified for forward-angle and total yield calculations).

## Key numbers
- Free threshold for $\pi^+n\to K^+\Lambda$: 0.76 GeV; for $\pi^+n\to \eta p$: 0.56 GeV; for $\eta p\to K^+\Lambda$: 0.20 GeV.
- Total $K^+$ production cross section on $^{12}$C at 600-650 MeV incident pion energy: 0.5-30 $\mu$b, depending on the nucleon momentum distribution used.
- The cross section calculated with the double-Gaussian momentum distribution (21) is larger by a factor of 2-10 than that with the shell-model distribution (16) in the 600-650 MeV region.
- For $\pi^+$Pb$^{208}$ at 600-650 MeV, the total $K^+$ cross section is larger by a factor of 10 than for $\pi^+$C$^{12}$.
- Fractions of nucleons with momenta above the Fermi momentum (250 MeV/c): 10% for shell-model distribution (16), 13% for the $(e,e'p)$-derived distribution (17), and 25% for the double-Gaussian distribution (21).
- The lower limit of neutron momenta needed for $K^+$ production at 600 MeV incident pion energy is about 200-250 MeV/c (off-shell assumption).

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of subthreshold kaon production from proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions (e.g., works by Cassing et al. (1990) and Müller et al. (1992)) to pion-induced reactions, for which no experimental data existed at the time. It provides the first detailed predictions for $\pi^+$C and $\pi^+$Pb collisions, enabling future experiments to distinguish between one-step and two-step production mechanisms and to probe high-momentum nucleon components in nuclei. The authors note that further theoretical work on the $\eta p\to K^+\Lambda$ elementary cross section is needed to clarify the relative role of the two channels.
