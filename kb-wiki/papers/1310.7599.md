# Emergent Collectivity in Nuclei and Enhanced Proton-Neutron Interactions

> Enhanced proton-neutron interactions occur in heavy nuclei along a trajectory of approximately equal numbers of valence protons and neutrons. This is also closely aligned with the trajectory of the saturation of quadrupole deformation. The origin of these enhanced p-n interactions is discussed in terms of spatial overlaps of proton and neutron wave functions that are orbit-dependent. It is suggested for the first time that nuclear collectivity is driven by synchronized filling of protons and neutrons with orbitals having parallel spins, identical orbital and total angular momenta projections, belonging to adjacent major shells and differing by one quantum of excitation along the z-axis. These results may lead to a new approach to symmetry-based theoretical calculations for heavy nuclei.

# Emergent Collectivity in Nuclei and Enhanced Proton-Neutron Interactions

## Key claim
Enhanced proton-neutron interactions occur in heavy nuclei along a trajectory of approximately equal numbers of valence protons and neutrons, and this is closely aligned with the saturation of quadrupole deformation. The authors suggest for the first time that nuclear collectivity is driven by synchronized filling of protons and neutrons with orbitals having parallel spins, identical orbital and total angular momenta projections, belonging to adjacent major shells and differing by one quantum of excitation along the z-axis, though this is presented as a suggestion rather than a proven mechanism.

## Method
The authors extracted empirical δVpn values (a measure of average proton-neutron interaction) from double differences of binding energies for even-Z and odd-Z nuclei in the Z=50-82, N=82-126 region. They calculated spatial overlaps of proton and neutron Nilsson wave functions using standard Nilsson parameters (κ=0.0637, μ=0.42 for neutrons and 0.0637, 0.6 for protons) at three deformation values (ε=0.05, 0.22, and 0.3), allocating nuclei to these categories according to their R4/2 values. The overlaps were computed as integrals of the product of squared wave functions in spherical coordinates.

## Key numbers
- δVpn peaks in odd-Z nuclei are approximately 4 times larger than in even-even nuclei along the Zval ≈ Nval line
- R4/2 ≡ E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) varies from less than 2 near closed shells to approximately 3.33 for well-deformed axial rotors
- Deformation onset occurs for R4/2 greater than 3.3
- For ¹⁵⁴Sm, the Nilsson deformation parameter is ε ≈ 0.95β₂ ≈ 0.32
- In ¹⁵⁴Sm, 6 of 12 valence protons occupy normal parity orbitals while 6 occupy 1h₁₁/₂ orbitals; 6 of 10 valence neutrons occupy normal parity orbitals while 4 occupy 1i₁₃/₂ orbitals
- In the proposed coupling scheme for ¹⁵⁴Sm, all 12 valence protons sit in the (24,0) irrep of U(15) and all 10 neutrons sit in the (30,4) irrep of U(21), yielding a (54,4) irrep for all valence nucleons

## Context
This work extends the study of δVpn singularities from light Z=N nuclei (Isacker, 1990s) to heavy nuclei, where such effects were previously unexpected due to spin-orbit and Coulomb forces. The authors build on earlier work by Cakirli (2005) showing muted δVpn peaks in even-even heavy nuclei, and now present new results for odd-Z nuclei with sharper, enhanced peaks. The calculated overlaps show agreement comparable to Density Functional Theory calculations (Mario, 2010s) but provide a more physically intuitive explanation. The proposed 0[110] coupling scheme offers a potential alternative to pseudo-SU(3) approaches (Raju, Draayer, 1990s-2000s) that could simplify symmetry-based shell model calculations for heavy nuclei, and suggests that isoscalar S=1 proton-neutron pairs, previously studied in medium-mass nuclei with Z≈N (Pittel, Simkovic, 2000s), may also be relevant in heavy nuclei with Zval ≈ Nval.
