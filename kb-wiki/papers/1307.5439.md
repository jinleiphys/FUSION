# Thermal and transport properties of a non-relativistic quantum gas interacting through a delta-shell potential

> This work extends the seminal work of Gottfried on the two-body quantum physics of particles interacting through a delta-shell potential to many-body physics by studying a system of non-relativistic particles when the thermal De-Broglie wavelength of a particle is smaller than the range of the potential and the density is such that average distance between particles is smaller than the range. The ability of the delta-shell potential to reproduce some basic properties of the deuteron are examined. Relations for moments of bound states are derived. The virial expansion is used to calculate the first quantum correction to the ideal gas pressure in the form of the second virial coefficient. Additionally, all thermodynamic functions are calculated up to the first order quantum corrections. For small departures from equilibrium, the net flows of mass, energy and momentum, characterized by the coefficients of diffusion, thermal conductivity and shear viscosity, respectively, are calculated. Properties of the gas are examined for various values of physical parameters including the case of infinite scattering length when the unitary limit is achieved.

## Key claim

The delta-shell potential can reproduce the deuteron's binding energy, scattering length, effective range, and root-mean-square radius to within a few percent of experimental values, but the shape parameter P = -0.047 differs substantially from the experimental value of -0.007, indicating the potential's limitations at higher energies.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrödinger equation for a delta-shell potential V(r) = -v δ(r-R), derived phase shifts and bound-state energies analytically using spherical Bessel functions, and then employed the virial expansion to compute the second virial coefficient and first-order quantum corrections to thermodynamic functions. Transport coefficients (diffusion, thermal conductivity, shear viscosity) were calculated for small departures from equilibrium using the phase-shift formalism. The deuteron properties were fitted by adjusting the range R and strength g to match the measured binding energy of 2.2246 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2246 MeV (experimental value used for fitting)
- Fitted model parameters: R = 1.564 fm, g = 1.406, Λ = 0.899 fm⁻¹
- Effective range from delta-shell model: r₀ = 1.79 fm (experimental value: 1.76 fm)
- Shape parameter from delta-shell model: P = -0.047 (experimental value: -0.007)
- Root-mean-square radius from delta-shell model: r_rms = 1.95 fm (experimental value: 1.97 fm)
- Scattering length for S-wave: a_sl/R = g/(g-1), giving a_sl = 5.42 fm when fitted to deuteron data
- For g < 1: no bound states exist; for g ≥ 1: bound states exist from l=0 up to l_max = [(g-1)/2]

## Context

This work extends Gottfried's (1966) two-body quantum treatment of the delta-shell potential to many-body physics, calculating thermodynamic and transport properties via virial expansion. The deuteron modeling follows the approach of Epelbaum (2004) for experimental values, and the scattering length formalism builds on Newton (1966) and Sitenko (1975). The results enable systematic study of non-relativistic quantum gases with short-range interactions, particularly in the unitary limit of infinite scattering length, and provide a benchmark for more realistic nuclear potentials.
