# Effects of $Λ$ hyperons on the nuclear equation of state in a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock model

> We predict the energy per baryon in nuclear matter with non-zero fraction of $Λ$ hyperons. We include Dirac effects on the nucleons as well as the $Λ$ and describe how the latter is implemented. We use the nucleon-hyperon meson-exchange potentials from the Juelich group, the latest as well as an earlier version. The dependence of the results on the many-body framework and on the nucleon-hyperon interaction model is discussed.

## Key claim

The effect of replacing nucleons with Λ hyperons on the energy per baryon in symmetric nuclear matter depends sensitively on both the nucleon-hyperon (NY) interaction model and the many-body framework used, with the NY05 potential producing increased binding while the NY94 potential produces decreased binding as hyperon concentration rises.

## Method

The author performed Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter containing a non-zero fraction of Λ hyperons, using relativistic momentum-space nucleon-nucleon (Bonn B) and nucleon-hyperon (Juelich NY94 and NY05) potentials. Dirac effects were included on both nucleons and Λ hyperons by self-consistently computing effective masses from the single-particle energies, with the ΛΛ interaction neglected and Λ concentrations kept low. The NΛ G-matrix was obtained from a coupled-channel Bethe-Goldstone equation including both Λ and Σ intermediate states, with the pseudoscalar coupling of pions and kaons converted to pseudovector coupling to avoid unphysically large antiparticle contributions.

## Key numbers

- The NY05 potential yields about 50 MeV for the Λ binding energy in nuclear matter, whereas the NY94 potential gives a value close to 30 MeV.
- The depth of the single-Λ potential is constrained by hypernuclei data to be 27-30 MeV (Dover).
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter remains essentially unchanged with increasing Λ concentration when using the NY94 potential in DBHF calculations.
- At about twice saturation density, the DBHF curves with the NY94 potential show a "cross over" where larger Λ concentrations start to yield increased attraction.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation of a single Λ impurity in nuclear matter (Sammarruca 2008) to finite Λ concentrations, and contrasts with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by the Catania group and the Barcelona group (Vidana et al. 2000, 2004; Rios et al. 2005) that used local Nijmegen NY potentials. The results enable future determination of equilibrium hyperon fractions in neutron star matter under β-stability conditions, but the strong model dependence of the NY interaction means conclusions about strange neutron star properties must be interpreted with caution.
