# Predictions of baryon directed flow in heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density

> Predictions of the proton directed flow ($v_1$) in semicentral Au+Au collisions in the energy range between 4.5 and 7.7 GeV are done. The calculations are performed within the model of three-fluid dynamics with crossover equation of state, which well reproduces the proton $v_1$ both below 4.5 GeV and above 7.7 GeV, as well as bulk observables in the energy range of interest. It is predicted that the proton flow evolves non-monotonously. At the energy of 7.2 GeV it exhibits antiflow (i.e. negative slope of $v_1(y)$) in the midrapidity. At 7.7 GeV, the flow returns to the normal pattern in accordance with the STAR data. The midrapidity $v_1$-slope excitation functions within the first-order phase and crossover transitions to quark-gluon phase (QGP) turn out to be qualitatively similar, but the amplitude of the wiggle in the crossover scenario is much smaller than that in the strong first-order phase transition. Therefore, the change of sign followed by minimum at 7.2 GeV in the $v_1$-slope excitation function indicates onset of (weak phase or crossover) transition to QGP. The second change of the sign around 10 GeV results from interplay between incomplete baryon stopping and transverse expansion of the system.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the proton directed flow in semicentral Au+Au collisions evolves non-monotonously between 4.5 and 7.7 GeV, with antiflow (negative slope of v1(y)) appearing at 7.2 GeV before returning to normal flow at 7.7 GeV, but this wiggle amplitude is much smaller for a crossover transition than for a strong first-order phase transition, meaning the sign change indicates onset of a weak phase or crossover transition rather than a first-order transition.

## Method
The calculations were performed within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which simulates the early-stage nonequilibrium using two counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids and a third fireball fluid for newly produced particles, coupled by friction terms. Three different equations of state were implemented: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition (1PT), and one with a smooth crossover transition, with the crossover EoS validated against proton v1 data below 4.5 GeV and above 7.7 GeV as well as bulk observables.

## Key numbers
- Proton directed flow exhibits antiflow (negative slope) at midrapidity at collision energy of 7.2 GeV in semicentral Au+Au collisions
- At 7.7 GeV, the flow returns to the normal pattern in accordance with STAR data
- The freeze-out energy density used in the 3FD model is ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm³
- The hadronic EoS used has incompressibility K = 190 MeV
- The crossover pressure starts to deviate from hadronic pressure at baryon densities n_B > 4-5 n_0 at temperatures 100-150 MeV
- The second sign change in the v1-slope excitation function occurs around 10 GeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of proton directed flow from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (STAR, 2014) and STAR fixed-target data at 3 and 4.5 GeV (STAR, 2020; STAR, 2021) into the unexplored energy range between 4.5 and 7.7 GeV. It builds on prior 3FD model studies by Ivanov and Soldatov (2014, 2015, 2016) that indicated crossover or weak-first-order transition to QGP, and addresses the suggestion by Du, Shen, Jeon, and Gale (2023) that the sign change around 10-20 GeV results from baryon stopping rather than a first-order phase transition. The predictions enable experimental verification at NICA, FAIR, HIAF, and J-PARC-HI facilities.
