# Lessons from Non-Abelian Plasma Instabilities in Two Spatial Dimensions

> Plasma instabilities can play a fundamental role in quark-gluon plasma equilibration in the high energy (weak coupling) limit. Early simulations of the evolution of plasma instabilities in non-abelian gauge theory, performed in one spatial dimension, found behavior qualitatively similar to traditional QED plasmas. Later simulations of the fully three-dimensional theory found different behavior, unlike traditional QED plasmas. To shed light on the origin of this difference, we study the intermediate case of two spatial dimensions. Depending on how the "two-dimensional'' theory is formulated, we can obtain either behavior.

## Key claim

The qualitative behavior of non-abelian plasma instabilities in dimensionally reduced simulations depends on whether gauge-field polarizations outside the simulated spatial dimensions are included; in two spatial dimensions, retaining all polarizations yields continued exponential growth like one-dimensional simulations, while discarding the out-of-plane polarization yields linear growth like three-dimensional simulations.

## Method

The authors performed classical Vlasov simulations of SU(2) gauge theory in 2D+3V (two spatial dimensions, three velocity dimensions) for moderately anisotropic, oblate particle distributions axi-symmetric about the z axis. They compared two formulations: one retaining all gauge field polarizations (A_x, A_y, A_z) and one setting the out-of-plane polarization A_x to zero, using the same numerical methods as prior three-dimensional simulations (Arnold, Moore 2006). The simulations were seeded with either tiny initial magnetic fields or large non-perturbative configurations, and the magnetic energy growth was tracked over time.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.125/m_infinity on a 256x256 lattice of physical size L^2 = (32/m_infinity)^2 for canonical two-dimensional simulations.
- For the Nielsen-Olesen instability analysis, the condition for suppression is (g A_x)^2 > 2 g B_0, where B_0 is the background magnetic field.
- The condition for Nielsen-Olesen instabilities to become important is B >> k^2/g, where k is the typical momentum scale of the magnetic field.
- For two-dimensional simulations with A_x included and large initial conditions, exponential growth continues until fields become limited by lattice artifacts at a scale that increases steadily as lattice spacing is reduced from a = 0.25/m_infinity to smaller values.
- For two-dimensional simulations with A_x=0 and large initial conditions, late-time linear energy growth is robust for lattice spacings down to a = 0.25/m_infinity.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-dimensional simulations (Rebhan, Romatschke, Strickland 2005) that found exponential growth similar to abelian plasmas, and three-dimensional simulations (Arnold, Moore 2006) that found linear growth instead. The paper resolves the discrepancy by showing that the difference arises from whether out-of-plane gauge field polarizations become scalar fields in the dimensionally reduced theory, which can suppress Nielsen-Olesen instabilities (Nielsen, Olesen 1978) that would otherwise destroy abelianization. The results rule out using 1D+3V simulations to study the fate of non-abelian Weibel instabilities, but suggest that 2D+3V simulations without the out-of-plane polarization can reproduce three-dimensional behavior.
