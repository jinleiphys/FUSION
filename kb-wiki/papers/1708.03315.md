# Scale dependence of deuteron electrodisintegration

> A model calculation of deuteron electrodisintegration is used to explore renormalization group (RG) scale dependence for knock-out reactions. When evolved to different resolutions with the similarity RG (SRG), the initial deuteron wave function, the current operator, and the final state interactions (FSI) are all modified, but how they combine depends strongly on the kinematic region. In some regions, for example, the FSI are largely unaffected by evolution, while elsewhere FSI are greatly reduced. For certain kinematics, the impulse approximation at a high RG resolution gives an intuitive picture in terms of a one-body current breaking up a short-range correlated neutron-proton pair, although FSI distort this simple picture. With evolution to low resolution, however, the cross section is unchanged but a very different and arguably simpler intuitive picture emerges, with the evolved current efficiently represented at low momentum through derivative expansions or low-rank singular value decompositions. A consequence is that intuition about physics such as the role of short-range correlations or $D$-state mixing in particular kinematic regimes can be strongly scale dependent.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the physical interpretation of deuteron electrodisintegration, including the roles of short-range correlations and D-state mixing, is strongly dependent on the renormalization group (RG) scale, not just on kinematics, and that working at low resolution can yield a simpler intuitive picture with reduced final state interactions (FSI) despite the conventional wisdom that low-resolution potentials are ill-suited for high-momentum transfer reactions.

## Method
The authors used the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to evolve the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential, the deuteron wave function, the electromagnetic current operator, and the final scattering state to different resolution scales λ. They then computed the longitudinal structure function fL for deuteron electrodisintegration in various kinematic regions, comparing the contributions of the initial state, current operator, and final state interactions at each scale to understand how the components recombine to leave the cross section invariant.

## Key numbers
- SRG evolution scale λ = 2 fm⁻¹ was used for evolved wave functions and potentials, compared to unevolved (λ = ∞) AV18.
- Momentum transfer q² = 36 fm⁻² was used for heatmap plots of the current operator evolution.
- The deuteron D-state probability is steadily reduced as λ is lowered, with the interior D-state component greatly reduced when evolving from λ = ∞ to λ = 2 fm⁻¹.
- Final state interaction contributions Δψ^λ(p'; k) in the ³S₁ channel become suppressed for momenta k ≳ λ, with suppression also at small momenta for large outgoing momentum p'.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SRG application to deuteron electrodisintegration (More, Bogner, Furnstahl, 2015) by analyzing in greater detail how the physics interpretation changes with scale. It contrasts with conventional approaches using high-resolution potentials (e.g., AV18) where short-range correlations dominate the intuitive picture, and shows that low-resolution evolution enables efficient representation of the evolved current through derivative expansions or singular value decompositions. The results enable future application of RG methods to more complex nuclear knock-out reactions and provide guidance for choosing an optimal scale to simplify calculations or improve extraction of structure information from experiment.
