# Unitarity Constraints on Neutrino Mass and Mixings

> We explore the implications of imposing the constraint that two neutrino flavors (which for definiteness we take to be muon and tau neutrinos) are similarly coupled to the mass basis in addition to the unitarity constraints. Implications of this scheme for specific experimental situations are discussed.

## Key claim
Imposing the constraint that muon and tau neutrinos are similarly coupled to the mass basis (i.e., their mixing matrix elements are proportional for all but one mass eigenstate) forces the third mass eigenstate to decouple from all other flavors, reducing the effective dimension of the flavor space by one.

## Method
The author starts with an N×N neutrino mixing matrix for three active flavors plus an arbitrary number of sterile neutrinos. The constraint that Uμi and Uτi are proportional for all i ≠ 3 is written as sinφ Uμi = eiη cosφ Uτi, with an arbitrary angle φ and phase η. Using unitarity, the author derives that this forces Uα3 = 0 for α ≠ μ,τ, and expresses the mixing matrix in a simplified form that reduces the number of free parameters from N(N−1)/2 to (N²−3N+4)/2.

## Key numbers
- For the CHOOZ experiment, the bound on the mixing element for a sterile fourth neutrino is |Ue4|² < 0.047.
- The best limit on |Ue4|² is < 0.005, from the BUGEY experiment.
- The current upper bound on the neutrinoless double beta decay effective mass is Mee < ∼ 0.5 eV.

## Context
This work is motivated by SuperKamiokande observations of atmospheric neutrinos suggesting maximal νμ–ντ mixing, and by theoretical neutrino mass models (Ramond, year not given). It extends the author's prior work (Balantekin, year not given) on unitarity constraints. The result enables simplified treatment of matter-enhanced neutrino transformations with multiple flavors and level crossings, and is related to recent work (author not named, year not given) on an Sp(4) symmetry in the neutrino mass sector. The scheme also provides a framework for fitting atmospheric neutrino data and for interpreting reactor and double beta decay experiments.
