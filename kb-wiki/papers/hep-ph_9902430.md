# Mean Field Dynamics in Non-Abelian Plasmas from Classical Transport Theory

> Based on classical transport theory, we present a general set of covariant equations describing the dynamics of mean fields and their statistical fluctuations in a non-Abelian plasma in or out-of-equilibrium. A procedure to obtain the collision integrals for the Boltzmann equation from the microscopic theory is described. As an application, we study a hot non-Abelian plasma close to equilibrium, where the fluctuations are integrated out explicitly. For soft fields, and at logarithmic accuracy, we obtain Bödeker's effective theory.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general set of covariant equations for mean fields and their statistical fluctuations in a non-Abelian plasma from classical transport theory, and show that for soft fields near equilibrium and at logarithmic accuracy, these equations reproduce Bödeker's effective theory, but only after neglecting cubic fluctuations, linearizing the fluctuation dynamics, and omitting the mean field strength term under the condition that \(g |\bar F^{\mu\nu}_a| / m_D \ll T\).

## Method

The authors start from the microscopic phase space density \(f(x,p,Q)\) for classical colored point particles obeying Wong equations and the Yang-Mills equation, then split all quantities into mean values and fluctuations. They take statistical averages to obtain exact kinetic equations for the mean fields and fluctuations, where the collision integrals arise as correlators of fluctuations. For a hot non-Abelian plasma close to equilibrium with small gauge coupling \(g \ll 1\), they linearize the fluctuation dynamics, neglect cubic terms, and solve the resulting equations for the fluctuations using a one-sided Fourier transform and an expansion in powers of the mean fields \(\bar A\) and \(\bar {\cal J}\), then evaluate the statistical averages over initial conditions to obtain the collision integrals at leading logarithmic order.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass: \(m_D^2 = - 2 g^2 N \int d \tilde P p_0 \, d \bar f^{\rm eq}(p_0)/d p_0\) (classical); for gluons in the adjoint, \(m_D^2 = g^2 N T^2 / 3\) (quantum).
- Color conductivity: \(\sigma = \frac{4 \pi m_D^2}{3 N g^2 T \ln(1/g)}\).
- Noise correlator: \(\langle \nu_a^i(x) \nu_b^j(y) \rangle = 2 T \sigma \delta^{ij} \delta_{ab} \delta^{(4)}(x-y)\).
- Leading-logarithmic collision integral: \(\langle \xi^{(1)}_{\rho,a}(x,v) \rangle = - \frac{g^2}{4\pi} N T \ln(1/g) \, v_\rho \int \frac{d\Omega_{{\bf v}'}}{4\pi} {\cal I}(v,v') \bar{\cal J}^0_a(x,v')\), with kernel \({\cal I}(v,v') = \delta^{(2)}({\bf v}-{\bf v}') - \frac{4}{\pi} \frac{({\bf v}\cdot{\bf v}')^2}{\sqrt{1-({\bf v}\cdot{\bf v}')^2}}\).

## Context

This work extends the classical transport theory approach previously applied to Abelian plasmas (referenced as "K") to the non-Abelian case, and generalizes the Vlasov equations (obtained when fluctuations are neglected) to include collision integrals derived from microscopic fluctuations. The result reproduces the effective theory of Bödeker (1998) for soft modes at logarithmic accuracy, and the collision integral matches that of Arnold (1998). The formalism enables systematic treatment of out-of-equilibrium non-Abelian plasmas and provides a link between classical transport theory and quantum field theoretical treatments beyond one-loop level, with open questions including generalization to out-of-equilibrium situations where the Coulomb logarithm may change the mean field equations.
