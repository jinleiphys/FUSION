# Relativistic Microscopic Description of Proton-Nucleus Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> We study elastic proton-nucleus scattering at intermediate energies. The nucleon-nucleus optical potential is derived from the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and the Dirac-Brueckner approach for nuclear matter. Our calculations, which do not contain any adjustable parameters, yield good agreement with experiment.

# Relativistic Microscopic Description of Proton-Nucleus Scattering at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim

The authors derive a parameter-free optical potential for elastic proton-nucleus scattering from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential via the Dirac-Brueckner approach, and obtain good agreement with experimental data, but only when the target density is taken from a relativistic Hartree (RH) or relativistic density-dependent Hartree (RDH) calculation rather than from the fully self-consistent relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) density, which underestimates the root-mean-square radius.

## Method

The DBHF results for nuclear matter from the Bonn A potential are parameterized as an effective sigma-omega Lagrangian with density-dependent coupling constants. The nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter is calculated up to fourth-order Feynman diagrams (Hartree-Fock plus two-particle-one-hole contributions for the imaginary part), and the nucleon-nucleus optical potential for finite nuclei is obtained via the local density approximation using target densities from RDHF, RDH, or RH calculations. The Dirac equation for the projectile is converted to a Schrödinger-equivalent equation to compute differential cross sections and analyzing powers.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation from DBHF with Bonn A: density = 0.185 fm⁻³, energy per nucleon = −15.6 MeV
- Real scalar potential in interior of ⁴⁰Ca: approximately −420 MeV
- Real vector potential in interior of ⁴⁰Ca: approximately +320 MeV
- Effective sigma meson mass fixed at 550 MeV; omega meson mass fixed at 782.6 MeV
- Incident laboratory energies studied: 150, 300, 400, and 450 MeV for p+⁴⁰Ca
- At T_lab = 300 MeV, the real central potential becomes repulsive in the nuclear interior; at T_lab = 450 MeV it is repulsive throughout all radial space

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Brockmann and Toki (1992) from nuclear matter to finite-nucleus scattering, combining the microscopic origin of DBHF with the simplicity of the Walecka model (QHD-I). It contrasts with the relativistic impulse approximation (which ignores medium effects) and with purely phenomenological Dirac approaches by Clark and collaborators (1983–1985). The main limitation identified is the need for improved nuclear densities from self-consistent calculations; the RDHF density used here underestimates the root-mean-square radius of ⁴⁰Ca, and using the RH density from the Walecka model gives better agreement with experiment.
