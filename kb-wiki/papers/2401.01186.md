# The reaction $πN \to ωN$ in a dynamical coupled-channel approach

> This talk is on a refined investigation on light flavor meson-baryon scatterings, using a dynamical coupled-channel approach, i.e. the Jülich-Bonn model. The previous channel space of $πN$, $πΔ$, $σN$, $ρN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$ and $K Σ$ is extended by adding the $ωN$ final state. The spectra of $N^*$ and $Δ$ resonances are extracted, based on the result of a global fit to a worldwide collection of data, in the energy region from the $πN$ threshold to center-of-mass energy $z=2.3$ GeV (approximately $300$ parameters against $9000$ data points). A negative value of the $ωN$ elastic spin-averaged scattering length has been extracted.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the real part of the ωN elastic spin-averaged scattering length is negative in both fit scenarios, which disfavors the formation of in-medium bound states of the ω meson, but the authors note that statistical errors are hard to address because special weights had to be added to the ωN data during the fit.

## Method

The Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel model, which solves Lippmann-Schwinger-like scattering equations, was extended by adding the ωN final state to the existing channel space of πN, πΔ, σN, ρN, ηN, KΛ, and KΣ. A global fit was performed with 304 parameters against approximately 9000 data points from πN-induced reactions in the energy region from the πN threshold to center-of-mass energy z = 2.3 GeV. Two fit scenarios (fit A and fit B) with different starting parameter sets were implemented to estimate uncertainties, and the spin-averaged ωN scattering length was extracted from the resulting amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Spin-averaged ωN scattering length: (−0.24 + 0.05i) fm from fit A and (−0.21 + 0.05i) fm from fit B
- 304 fit parameters adjusted against approximately 9000 data points, of which 174 belong to the ωN reaction
- 17 N* poles and 15 Δ poles were found in the extracted resonance spectrum
- The residues of N(1535) 1/2−, N(1710) 1/2+, and N(1680) 5/2+ in the ωN channel are relatively larger than other states, indicating stronger coupling strengths
- For comparison, the absolute value of the scattering length from Strakovsky is given as |ā_ωN| = 0.82 ± 0.03 fm

## Context

This work extends the Jülich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel model, which has been developed over decades by Schuetz (1994, 1995, 1998), Krehl (2000), Gasparyan (2003), Doering (2009, 2011), Roenchen (2013, 2014, 2015, 2018, 2022), and Wang (2022), by adding the ωN final state to the hadronic part. The negative real part of the ωN scattering length contradicts the possibility of in-medium ω bound states and is consistent with most prior extractions summarized by Koike (1996), Klingl (1998), Lutz (2001), Shklyar (2004), Muehlich (2006), Paris (2008), and Ishikawa (2019). This model can be extended in the future to ω photoproduction and to study the internal structure of the resonances.
