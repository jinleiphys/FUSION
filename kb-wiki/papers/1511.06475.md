# Secondary electrons emitted during nuclear $β^{-}$ decay in few-electron atoms

> `Additional' ionization of light atoms and ions during nuclear $β^{-}$ decay is investigated. The procedure which can be used to determine the corresponding transition probabilities and the velocity/energy spectrum of secondary electrons is developed. Emission of very fast secondary electrons ($δ-$electrons) from $β^{-}$-decaying atoms is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a closed analytical formula for the velocity/energy spectrum of secondary electrons emitted during nuclear β⁻ decay in few-electron atoms, but the derivation relies on the sudden approximation (valid when β⁻-electron velocities are 50–200 times larger than atomic electron velocities) and assumes non-relativistic wave functions for the secondary electrons, limiting applicability to energies ≤ 50 keV for arbitrary atoms and ≤ 25 keV for light atoms.

## Method

The authors compute final-state probability amplitudes as overlap integrals between the parent atom's wave function and the product of the final ion's wave function with a Coulomb-distorted free-electron wave function (Eq. Cwave). For one-electron tritium, they derive closed-form expressions for the velocity spectrum S_e(v; Q) using the sudden approximation, then extend the formalism to few-electron atoms by introducing an effective nuclear charge parameter q to account for electron-electron correlations. Numerical integration over velocity space (0 to c = α⁻¹ in atomic units) yields normalized spectral functions and absolute bound-free transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Total bound-free transition probability for tritium (³H → ³He²⁺ + e⁻ + e⁻(β) + ν̄): P_bf ≈ 0.02627265(10), or approximately 2.627% of all β⁻ decays.
- Normalization constant for the tritium velocity spectrum: S(Q) ≈ 196.611833628395.
- For tritium, the overall probability to observe a secondary electron with velocity between v₁ = 0.6 a.u. and v₂ = 3.0 a.u. is approximately 0.0219602769, or 2.196028% of all β⁻ decays.
- More than 90% of secondary electrons from tritium β⁻ decay have velocities between v = 0.4 a.u. and v = 3.2 a.u.
- For the β⁻ decay of the Li atom from its ground 2²S state, the corresponding bound-free probability is approximately 15%.

## Context

This work extends the sudden-approximation treatment of β⁻ decay ionization pioneered by Migdal (1940) and later developed by Frolov (2005) and others, providing the first closed analytical formula for the secondary-electron velocity spectrum rather than relying solely on numerical overlap integrals. The method enables experimental extraction of electron-electron correlation information in parent atoms by analyzing secondary-electron spectra, and the authors note that for the two-electron H⁻ ion, bound-free transition probabilities can be comparable to or exceed bound-bound transition probabilities, in contrast to the tritium case where bound-free probability is only ~2.6%.
