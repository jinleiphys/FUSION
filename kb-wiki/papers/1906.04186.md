# An Effective Theory of Quarkonia in QCD Matter

> For heavy quarkonia of moderate energy, we generalize the relevant successful theory, non-relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics (NRQCD), to include interactions in nuclear matter. The new resulting theory, NRQCD with Glauber gluons, provides for the first time a universal microscopic description of the interaction of heavy quarkonia with a strongly interacting medium, consistently applicable to a range of phases, such as cold nuclear matter, dense hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma. The effective field theory we present in this work is derived from first principles and is an important step forward in understanding the common trends in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus data on quarkonium suppression.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the established energy-loss/fragmentation approach to quarkonium suppression in nuclear matter is incompatible with LHC data, and that a new effective field theory (NRQCD with Glauber gluons, NRQCD\(_{\rm G}\)) is needed to describe the propagation of the quarkonium state itself through the medium, rather than just the parton that fragments into it.

## Method

The authors first test the energy-loss approach by combining leading-power NRQCD factorization for quarkonium production with a Poisson-distribution model of medium-induced radiative energy loss, using a viscous 2+1D hydrodynamic background. They then construct the new effective theory by identifying the relevant momentum scaling regimes (Coulomb and Glauber gluon exchanges) for the interaction of non-relativistic heavy quark-antiquark pairs with collinear, soft, and static sources in the medium, and derive the corresponding Lagrangian and Feynman rules.

## Key numbers

* The relative velocity squared \(\lambda^2 \sim 0.1\) for bottomonium and \(\lambda^2 \sim 0.3\) for charmonium in the quarkonium rest frame.
* The soft scale \(m\ve\) is about 1.5 GeV for both bottomonium and charmonium.
* The energy-loss model predicts a flat \(R_{AA}\) suppression factor of 3 to 5 for both \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) in minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
* The double ratio \(\psi(2S)/J/\psi\) from the energy-loss model is 10-20% above unity, while CMS data show the \(\psi(2S)\) is 2 to 3 times more suppressed than the \(J/\psi\).
* The coupling between the hard parton and the medium is varied in the range \(g = 1.7 - 1.9\) in the energy-loss calculations.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of SCET\(_{\rm G}\) (Idilbi 2008, Ovanesyan 2011) for light hadrons and its extension to heavy mesons (Kang 2016) to the case of quarkonia. It contradicts the energy-loss/fragmentation picture (Arleo 2017, Spousta 2016) for explaining quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, showing that such models fail to describe the magnitude and hierarchy of suppression seen in CMS and ATLAS data. The new NRQCD\(_{\rm G}\) framework aims to provide a universal microscopic description applicable to cold nuclear matter, hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma, enabling future quantitative extraction of medium transport properties from quarkonium measurements at RHIC and the LHC.
