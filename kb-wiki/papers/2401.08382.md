# Shear oscillations in neutron stars and the nuclear symmetry energy

> The shear and interface modes excited inside the neutron star due to the presence of elasticity depend on the properties of both the crust and core region. To examine how such eigenfrequencies depend on the neutron star properties, we solve the eigenvalue problem by adopting the relativistic Cowling approximation. Then, we confirm that the number of the interface modes excited in the star is generally equivalent to the number of the interface where the shear modulus discontinuously becomes zero, but we also find that the number of interface modes becomes smaller than that of the interface for the stellar model with lower or higher value of the slope parameter $L$. Furthermore, we derive the empirical relations for expressing the shear modes and one of the interface modes ($i_1$-mode in the text), which is the mode whose amplitude becomes dominant at the interface between the crust and envelopes and at the interface between the phases of slablike and cylindrical nuclei. At the end, we also show the possibility of identifying the higher QPO frequencies observed in GRB 200415A with the shear oscillations, as an alternative possibility instead of the torsional oscillations.

## Key claim

The number of interface modes excited in a neutron star is generally equal to the number of interfaces where the shear modulus discontinuously becomes zero, but this number becomes smaller than the number of interfaces for stellar models with either lower or higher values of the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy, specifically when \(L\) is too small (the \(i_1\)-mode disappears) or too large (the \(i_4\)-mode disappears).

## Method

The author constructs non-rotating, spherically symmetric neutron star equilibrium models using the Oyamatsu-Iida phenomenological equation of state (OI-EOS) family, which is parameterized by the nuclear saturation parameters \(K_0\) (incompressibility) and \(L\) (density dependence of symmetry energy). The eigenvalue problem for shear (\(s\)-) and interface (\(i\)-) modes is solved under the relativistic Cowling approximation (metric perturbations neglected) for \(\ell=2\) oscillations, using the standard shear modulus for spherical nuclei and the Pethick-Potekhin (1998) formulas for cylindrical and slablike nuclear pasta phases.

## Key numbers

* The current experimental constraints on the nuclear saturation parameters are \(K_0 = 240 \pm 20\) MeV and \(L = 60 \pm 20\) MeV.
* The transition density from spherical-hole nuclei to uniform matter (core-crust boundary) in the OI-EOSs is at most \(\sim 2n_0/3\), where \(n_0 \approx 0.15-0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The empirical relation for \(i\)-mode frequencies is \(f_{i_i} M\ (\text{kHz}/M_\odot) = a_{0i} + a_{1i}x + a_{2i}x^2\), where \(x = M_{1.4}/R_{12}\) with \(M_{1.4} \equiv M/1.4M_\odot\) and \(R_{12} \equiv R/12\) km.
* The empirical relation for \(s\)-mode frequencies is \(f_{s_i} R\ (\text{kHz km}) = b_{0i} + b_{1i}x\).
* For the stellar model with \(K_0 = 230\) MeV and \(L = 42.6\) MeV, a \(1.44 M_\odot\) neutron star has a radius of 10.2 km.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Sotani 2023) which derived empirical relations for \(i\)- and \(s\)-mode frequencies using only two equations of state, by now systematically examining the dependence on nuclear saturation parameters across a wider range of OI-EOSs. The results enable future asteroseismology of neutron stars: once \(i\)- or \(s\)-mode frequencies are observed, the empirical relations may allow extraction of crustal properties and the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy. The author also suggests that higher quasi-periodic oscillation frequencies observed in GRB 200415A (Castro-Tirado 2021) could be identified with shear oscillations as an alternative to torsional oscillations.
