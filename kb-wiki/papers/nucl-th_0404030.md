# Collective excitations induced by pairing anti-halo effect

> Important features of low-frequency collective vibrational excitations in neutron drip line nuclei are studied. We emphasize that pairing anti-halo effect in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory play crucial roles to realize collective motions in loosely bound nuclei. We study the spatial properties of one particle - one hole (1p-1h) states with/without selfconsistent pairing correlations by solving simplified HF(B) equations in coordinate space. Next, by performing Skyrme-HFB plus selfconsistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) we investigate the first 2+ states in neutron rich Ni isotopes. Three types of calculations, HFB plus QRPA, resonant BCS plus QRPA, and RPA are compared.

# Collective excitations induced by pairing anti-halo effect

## Key claim

Pairing anti-halo effect in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory plays crucial roles to realize collective vibrational excitations in neutron drip line nuclei, but only when pairing correlations are treated selfconsistently; non-selfconsistent treatments such as resonant BCS plus QRPA fail to produce collective modes because the spatial localization of hole wave functions is lost.

## Method

The author first studied the spatial properties of one particle-one hole (1p-1h) states with and without selfconsistent pairing correlations by solving simplified HF(B) equations in coordinate space using a Woods-Saxon potential. Then, Skyrme-HFB plus selfconsistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations were performed for the first 2+ states in neutron rich Ni isotopes, comparing three types of calculations: HFB plus QRPA, resonant BCS plus QRPA, and RPA (no pairing). The QRPA problem was solved by the response function method in coordinate space with box boundary conditions, using the Skyrme SLy4 parameter for the mean field and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- The average pairing gap in the simplified HFB calculations was approximately 1.5 MeV in the region -51 MeV < V_WS < -39 MeV, with pairing parameters V_pair = -350 MeV fm^-3 and rho_c = 0.16/2 fm^-3.
- In the Skyrme-HFB plus QRPA calculations, the pairing interaction parameters were V_pair = -555 MeV fm^-3 and rho_c = 0.16 fm^-3, chosen to give an average neutron pairing gap of approximately 12/sqrt(A) in ^86Ni.
- The box boundary conditions were R_box = 30 fm for the simplified HFB calculations and R_box = 20 fm for the Skyrme-HFB plus QRPA calculations.
- The quasiparticle cut-off energy was 50 MeV and the angular momentum cut-off was l_max = 7 hbar in the HFB and QRPA calculations.
- In resonant BCS calculations, a constant pairing gap of 0.8 MeV was chosen to reproduce low-lying quasiparticle energies from Skyrme-HFB, with an additional cut-off radius R_BCS = 10 fm to identify resonant states.

## Context

This work extends the concept of pairing anti-halo effect introduced by Bennaceur and Dobaczewski (2000) for ground state density distributions to the domain of collective vibrational excitations. The study directly compares HFB plus QRPA with resonant BCS plus QRPA and RPA approaches, showing that only selfconsistent pairing correlations can localize hole wave functions and enable collective modes in loosely bound nuclei. The author notes that this mechanism also explains experimentally observed anomalously large B(E2) values in neutron rich nuclei such as ^32Mg and ^30Ne (Yamagami, 2004). The work enables future studies of low-frequency collective modes in drip line nuclei using fully selfconsistent HFB plus QRPA frameworks.
