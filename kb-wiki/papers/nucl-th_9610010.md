# Nuclear Density-Dependent Effective Coupling Constants in the Mean-Field Theory

> It is shown that the equation of state of nuclear matter can be determined within the mean-field theory of $σω$ model provided only that the nucleon effective mass curve is given. We use a family of the possible nucleon effective mass curves that reproduce the empirical saturation point in the calculation of the nuclear binding energy curves in order to obtain density-dependent effective coupling constants. The resulting density-dependent coupling constants may be used to study a possible equation of state of nuclear system at high density or neutron matter. Within the constraints used in this paper to $M^*$ of nuclear matter at saturation point and zero density, neutron matter of large incompressibility is strongly bound at high density while soft neutron matter is weakly bound at low density. The study also exhibits the importance of surface vibration modes in the study of nuclear equation of state.

# Nuclear Density-Dependent Effective Coupling Constants in the Mean-Field Theory

## Key claim

The equation of state of nuclear matter can be determined within the mean-field theory of the sigma-omega model using only the nucleon effective mass curve, provided that the effective mass curves are chosen to reproduce the empirical saturation point and to keep the scalar and vector coupling constants real. The resulting density-dependent coupling constants allow neutron matter to be either bound or unbound depending on the choice of effective mass parameter alpha, with the constraint that alpha must lie between 1.0 and 3.5 to maintain real coupling constants.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) including one-baryon-loop vacuum fluctuation effects and use the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem combined with the thermodynamic relation between pressure, energy per nucleon, and single-particle energy to derive a differential equation linking the energy per nucleon to the nucleon effective mass. They then choose a family of parametrized effective mass curves M*(k_F) that pass through the empirical saturation point (k_0 = 1.42 fm^-1, E_A = -15.75 MeV) and satisfy the boundary condition M*(0) = M, with a free parameter alpha controlling the shape. For each alpha, they integrate the differential equation to obtain the binding energy curve and then extract the density-dependent scalar and vector coupling constants g_s^2/m_s^2 and g_v^2/m_v^2 from the RHA expressions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: k_0 = 1.42 fm^-1 with binding energy E_A = -15.75 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility ranges from K^-1 = 61 MeV (alpha = 1.0) to 313 MeV (alpha = 2.25) to 565 MeV (alpha = 3.5)
- For neutron matter at alpha = 3.5: binding energy = 11.1 MeV, incompressibility = 1258 MeV, saturation Fermi momentum = 1.94 fm^-1
- The parameter alpha is constrained to the range 1.0 < alpha < 3.5 to keep both coupling constants real
- Neutron matter is bound for alpha in the regions 1.0 < alpha < 1.65 and 2.79 < alpha < 3.5, and unbound for 1.65 < alpha < 2.79

## Context

This work extends the relativistic sigma-omega model of Walecka (1974) and Serot and Walecka (1986) by constructing density-dependent coupling constants from effective mass curves, rather than using fixed coupling constants. It builds on the approach of Brockmann and Toki (1992), who used density-dependent coupling constants adjusted to reproduce Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results, but here the authors include vacuum fluctuation effects within RHA. The resulting density-dependent coupling constants enable studies of neutron matter equations of state and can be compared with the constant-coupling results of Chin (1977). The sensitivity of the equation of state to coupling constants at intermediate density (around half saturation density) suggests that detailed study of surface vibration modes in finite nuclei is important for extrapolating to high density.
