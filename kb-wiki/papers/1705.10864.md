# Pinning Down the Axial-Vector Coupling Constant in Nuclei and Dense Matter

> The in-medium property of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ in nuclei and dense baryonic matter, a long standing problem in nuclear physics, is reformulated in terms of the recently constructed scale-invariant hidden local symmetric ($bs$HLS) Lagrangian that encompasses the physics of baryon density ranging from nuclear matter $n_0$ to that of massive compact stars, $\sim 6n_0$. It is shown that unlike the pion decay constant $f_π$ that slides with the vacuum change induced by, and dropping with, increasing density, the axial-current constant $g_A$ should remain more or less unaffected by the decrease of chiral condensate in nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions (involving the space component of the axial current) whereas it gets strongly enhanced in axial-charge transitions (involving the time component of the axial current) as density increases. This phenomenon confirms the "chiral filter hypothesis" inferred from the current algebras in 1970's. These phenomena can be taken as a "nuclear physics proof" of Weinberg's folk theorem on quantum effective field theories. The implications of these predictions on giant Gamow-Teller resonances in nuclei and on first-forbidden beta transitions (relevant to nuclear astrophysical processes) are discussed.

## Key claim

The axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) for Gamow-Teller transitions (space component of the axial current) should remain unaffected by the decrease of the chiral condensate with increasing density, whereas for axial-charge transitions (time component) it should become strongly enhanced as density increases; this prediction is conditional on the validity of the scale-invariant hidden local symmetric Lagrangian and the associated "intrinsic density dependence" derived from matching to QCD.

## Method

The author reformulates the in-medium behavior of \(g_A\) using the recently constructed scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (\(bs\)HLS) Lagrangian, which incorporates scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry into a baryonic chiral Lagrangian with pions, nucleons, vector mesons (\(\rho,\omega\)), and a scalar dilaton field \(\chi\). The "bare" parameters of the Lagrangian are determined by matching to QCD current correlators at a matching scale, making them dependent on condensates (quark condensate, dilaton condensate) that slide with density; this "intrinsic density dependence" is then used to compute the axial current operators in nuclear matter, separating space and time components in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- Free-space axial coupling constant: \(g_A \approx 1.27\) in vacuum.
- Predicted scaling factor for the dilaton condensate/pion decay constant: \(\Phi \approx 1/(1 + 0.25\, n/n_0)\) up to nuclear matter density \(n_0\).
- At nuclear matter density \(n_0\), \(\Phi(n_0) \approx 0.8\).
- Predicted enhancement factor for axial-charge transitions at \(n_0\): \(\epsilon(n_0) \approx 2.0 \pm 0.2\).
- Experimental enhancement factor in Pb region: \(\epsilon^{\text{exp}} = 2.01 \pm 0.05\).
- Two-body exchange-current ratio: \(R = 0.5 \pm 0.1\) over density range \(n \sim (0.5-1.0)n_0\).
- For \(N=126\) isotones with \(Z=72\), the first-forbidden contribution quenches the half-life by a factor of \(\sim 5\) (without the \(\epsilon\) factor); a back-of-envelope estimate suggests the soft-pion-exchange contribution could make the half-life an order of magnitude shorter.

## Context

This work extends the "chiral filter hypothesis" from the 1970s (based on current algebras and soft-pion theorems) by reformulating it within a modern effective field theory framework that incorporates scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry. The predictions confirm earlier conjectures by Rho (1991) about dilaton condensate scaling and provide a "nuclear physics proof" of Weinberg's folk theorem on quantum effective field theories. The results have implications for giant Gamow-Teller resonances in nuclei (where the author argues that quenching observed in simple shell-model calculations may disappear when the model space covers \(\Delta\)-hole excitations) and for first-forbidden beta transitions relevant to nuclear astrophysics, potentially affecting half-life estimates in \(N=126\) isotones as studied by Honma et al.
