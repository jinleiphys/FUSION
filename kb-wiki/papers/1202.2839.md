# Continuum effects and three-nucleon forces in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes

> We employ interactions from chiral effective field theory and compute binding energies, excited states, and radii for isotopes of oxygen with the coupled-cluster method. Our calculation includes the effects of three-nucleon forces and of the particle continuum, both of which are important for the description of neutron-rich isotopes in the vicinity of the nucleus O-24. Our main results are the placement of the neutron drip-line at O-24, the assignment of spins, parities and resonance widths for several low-lying states of the drip-line nucleus, and an efficient approximation that incorporates the effects of three-body interactions.

## Key claim

The authors show that including both three-nucleon forces (approximated as an in-medium two-body potential) and the particle continuum in coupled-cluster calculations yields the neutron drip line at O-24 and reproduces excitation energies and resonance widths for several states, but the approximation of replacing the finite-nucleus mean field by symmetric nuclear matter introduces a systematic error that makes the energy difference between light and heavy oxygen isotopes not correctly reproduced.

## Method

The authors employed chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N3LO from Entem and Machleidt (2003) and approximated the leading chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) as an in-medium NN interaction derived by Holt et al. (2010) by integrating one nucleon over the Fermi sea of symmetric nuclear matter with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.05 fm^-1. Ground states of closed-shell oxygen isotopes were computed with coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) plus triples corrections via Lambda-CCSD(T), while open-shell isotopes and excited states were treated with equation-of-motion (EOM) coupled-cluster methods; the particle continuum near the drip line was included using the Gamow shell model with a complex-momentum basis.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of O-24: the neutron drip line is placed at O-24, with O-25 and O-26 unbound by about 0.4 MeV and about 0.1 MeV, respectively, relative to O-24.
* O-28 is predicted to be unbound with respect to O-24 by about 4 MeV with a resonant width of about 1 MeV.
* Excited states in O-24: the 2_1^+ state has excitation energy 4.56 MeV (experiment: 4.7(1) MeV) and width 0.03 MeV (experiment: 0.05^{+0.21}_{-0.05} MeV); the 1_1^+ state has excitation energy 5.2 MeV (experiment: 5.33(10) MeV) and width 0.04 MeV (experiment: 0.03^{+0.12}_{-0.03} MeV).
* Additional computed states in O-24: 4_1^+ at 6.2 MeV (width 0.005 MeV), 3_1^+ at 6.9 MeV (width 0.01 MeV), 2_2^+ at 7.0 MeV (width 0.04 MeV), and 1_2^+ at 8.4 MeV (width 0.56 MeV).
* For O-16, the computed point matter radius is 2.23 fm and point charge radius is 2.24 fm, about 0.3 fm smaller than experiment.

## Context

This work extends earlier ab initio computations by Hagen et al. (2009) that used only NN interactions and could not reproduce the drip line, and complements shell-model studies by Volya and Zelevinsky (2005) that included the continuum but used empirical two-body interactions, and by Otsuka et al. (2010) that included 3NFs but kept a core of O-16 with empirical single-particle energies. The results enable future studies of neutron-rich nuclei beyond oxygen by providing a computationally feasible approximation for 3NFs in large model spaces, though the approximation of symmetric nuclear matter for the in-medium force needs refinement for lighter isotopes.
