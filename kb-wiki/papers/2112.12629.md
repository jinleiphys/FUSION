# Influence of the nuclear symmetry energy slope on observables of compact stars with $Δ$-admixed hypernuclear matter

> In this work, we study the effects of nuclear symmetry energy slope on neutron star dense matter equation of state and its impact on neutron star observables (mass-radius, tidal response). We construct the equation of state within the framework of covariant density functional theory implementing coupling schemes of non-linear and density-dependent models with viability of heavier non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. The slope of symmetry energy parameter ($L_{\text{sym}}$) is adjusted following density-dependence of isovector meson coupling to baryons. We find that smaller values of $L_{\text{sym}}$ at saturation favour early appearance of $Δ$-resonances in comparison to hyperons leading to latter's threshold at higher matter densities. We also investigate the dependence of $L_{\text{sym}}$ on tidal deformability and compactness parameter of a $1.4~M_\odot$ neutron star for different equation of states and observe similar converging behaviour for larger $L_{\text{sym}}$ values.

## Key claim
The paper shows that smaller values of the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L_{\text{sym}}\) at saturation favor the early appearance of \(\Delta\)-resonances over hyperons in neutron star matter, while larger \(L_{\text{sym}}\) values lead to converging behavior in tidal deformability and compactness for a \(1.4~M_\odot\) neutron star. This result is conditional on the specific covariant density functional models (GM1 and DD-MEX) and the chosen meson-baryon coupling schemes.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state within covariant density functional theory using two coupling parametrizations: the non-linear GM1 model and the density-dependent DD-MEX model. They adjusted the slope of the symmetry energy (\(L_{\text{sym}}\)) by varying the density-dependence of the isovector \(\rho\)-meson coupling to baryons via the coefficient \(a_\rho\) in the exponential function \(g_{\rho N}(n)= g_{\rho N}(n_{0}) e^{-a_{\rho}(x-1)}\). The matter composition included nucleons, the full baryon octet (hyperons), \(\Delta\)-resonances, and leptons, with chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality enforced. Neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities were computed from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the tidal Love number formalism.

## Key numbers
- Saturation properties of the GM1 model: \(n_0 = 0.153~\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0 = -16.30~\text{MeV}\), \(K_0 = 300~\text{MeV}\), \(E_{\text{sym}} = 32.50~\text{MeV}\), \(L_{\text{sym}} = 93.86~\text{MeV}\), \(m^*_N/m_N = 0.700\).
- Saturation properties of the DD-MEX model: \(n_0 = 0.152~\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(E_0 = -16.14~\text{MeV}\), \(K_0 = 267~\text{MeV}\), \(E_{\text{sym}} = 32.27~\text{MeV}\), \(L_{\text{sym}} = 49.58~\text{MeV}\), \(m^*_N/m_N = 0.556\).
- For GM1 with \(L_{\text{sym}} = 35~\text{MeV}\), the threshold density for hyperons in NY\(\Delta\) matter (\(R_{\sigma\Delta}=1.20\)) is \(2.95~n_0\) and for \(\Delta\)-resonances is \(1.87~n_0\).
- For DD-MEX with \(L_{\text{sym}} = 35~\text{MeV}\), the threshold density for hyperons in NY\(\Delta\) matter (\(R_{\sigma\Delta}=1.20\)) is \(2.51~n_0\) and for \(\Delta\)-resonances is \(1.44~n_0\).
- The joint radius constraints for a \(1.4~M_\odot\) neutron star from PSR J0030+0451 and GW170817 are satisfied by EOS models with \(L_{\text{sym}} \leq 65~\text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy effects on dense matter, which were often limited to purely nucleonic compositions (e.g., Jiang (2019), Huang (2020), Wang (2021)), by including hyperons and \(\Delta\)-resonances. It builds on the covariant density functional framework of Glendenning (1991) for GM1 and Taninah (2020) for DD-MEX, and incorporates the density-dependent isovector coupling approach from Sinha (2017). The results enable tighter constraints on the symmetry energy slope from neutron star observations, particularly from NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and from the gravitational wave event GW170817.
