# Relativistic description of electron scattering on the deuteron

> Within a quasipotential framework a relativistic analysis is presented of the deuteron current. Assuming that the singularities from the nucleon propagators are important, a so-called equal time approximation of the current is constructed. This is applied to both elastic and inelastic electron scattering. As dynamical model the relativistic one boson exchange model is used. Reasonable agreement is found with a previous relativistic calculation of the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron. For the unpolarized inelastic electron scattering effects of final state interactions and relativistic corrections to the structure functions are considered in the impulse approximation. Two specific kinematic situations are studied as examples.

## Key claim

Within a quasipotential framework, the authors construct an "equal time" (ET) approximation to the deuteron electromagnetic current by assuming that the singularities from the nucleon propagators dominate and that the relative-energy dependence of the vertex functions can be neglected. This approximation yields reasonable agreement with previous relativistic calculations of elastic form factors, but the paper's main hedge is that the ET choice is a specific approximation whose validity is tested by comparing to earlier BSLT-based results, and that for inelastic scattering the current conservation is only restored when meson-exchange currents are added, which are deferred to a future paper.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic one-boson-exchange (OBE) model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, solved within the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze (BSLT) quasipotential approximation. For the electromagnetic current, they construct an equal-time (ET) approximation where the relative-energy dependence of the nucleon propagators is integrated out analytically, assuming the vertex functions are smooth in that variable. They then compute elastic electromagnetic form factors and unpolarized inelastic electron-deuteron scattering structure functions (R_L, R_T, R_TT, R_TL) in the impulse approximation, including final-state interactions via the half-off-shell NN t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Elastic charge form factor F_C: the dip position is sensitive to the model; at low momentum transfer the ET and BSLT approximations give results in good agreement with each other, with differences only at high q^2.
- Elastic magnetic form factor F_M: negative energy components in the wave function yield large contributions in the dip region; when included, the dip shifts to higher momentum transfer and the two quasipotential reductions (ET and BSLT) agree well.
- Tensor polarization t_20: results from both ET and BSLT calculations agree with experimental data up to q > 4 fm^{-1}.
- The inclusive cross section structure functions R_TT and R_TL vanish upon integration over the azimuthal angle phi'^cm due to their cos(2 phi'^cm) and cos(phi'^cm) dependence, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of the deuteron electromagnetic current by Hummel and Tjon (1990) and Zuilhof and Tjon (1980), who used the BSLT quasipotential approximation for both the interaction and the current. The ET approximation introduced here differs from that earlier work by keeping the full propagator structure in the current vertex rather than using the BSLT two-body propagators for both initial and final states. The paper enables future inclusion of meson-exchange currents (MEC) for the breakup reaction to restore current conservation, which the authors state will be addressed in a subsequent paper. The results are compared to nonrelativistic models using different forms of electromagnetic operators (F_1 vs. G_E), showing that large relativistic effects appear in the interference structure function R_TL even at moderate momentum transfer.
