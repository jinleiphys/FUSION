# 9Be scattering with microscopic wave functions and the CDCC method

> We use microscopic 9Be wave functions defined in a alpha+alpha+n multicluster model to compute 9Be+target scattering cross sections. The parameter sets describing 9Be are generated in the spirit of the Stochastic Variational Method (SVM), and the optimal solution is obtained by superposing Slater determinants and by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian. The 9Be three-body continuum is approximated by square-integral wave functions. The 9Be microscopic wave functions are then used in a Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculation of 9Be+208Pb and of 9Be+27Al elastic scattering. Without any parameter fitting, we obtain a fair agreement with experiment. For a heavy target, the influence of 9Be breakup is important, while it is weaker for light targets. This result confirms previous non-microscopic CDCC calculations. One of the main advantages of the microscopic CDCC is that it is based on nucleon-target interactions only; there is no adjustable parameter. The present work represents a first step towards more ambitious calculations involving heavier Be isotopes.

# 9Be scattering with microscopic wave functions and the CDCC method

## Key claim
Microscopic 9Be wave functions from an α+α+n multicluster model, when used in CDCC calculations with only nucleon-target optical potentials and no adjustable parameters, produce fair agreement with experimental elastic scattering data for 9Be+208Pb and 9Be+27Al; the agreement is conditional on the breakup channels being included for the heavy target, while for the light target breakup effects are minor.

## Method
The authors generate 9Be wave functions using a stochastic variational method (SVM) approach, superposing 175 Slater determinants of Gaussian single-particle wave functions in an α+α+n cluster model, then diagonalizing the Hamiltonian with the Minnesota central interaction and a spin-orbit term. The resulting bound and pseudostate wave functions (with positive energies approximating the three-body continuum) provide transition densities used in a microscopic CDCC calculation, where the projectile-target interaction is obtained by folding nucleon-target optical potentials (Koning-Delaroche for neutrons, Coulomb-only for protons) over the projectile densities. The coupled-channel equations are solved with the R-matrix method.

## Key numbers
- 9Be ground state (3/2⁻) root mean square matter radius: 2.45 fm, in agreement with experimental value 2.45 ± 0.01 fm
- 9Be first excited state (1/2⁺) root mean square matter radius: 3.83 fm
- Ground state quadrupole moment: 5.82 e²fm², compared to experimental 5.288 ± 0.038 e²fm²
- B(E1) from ground 3/2⁻ to first 1/2⁺ state: 0.0460 e²fm², within the experimental range 0.027(2) to 0.0685 e²fm²
- B(E2) from ground 3/2⁻ to 5/2⁻ state: 25.8 e²fm⁴
- Number of Slater determinants superposed: N = 175
- Oscillator parameter b = 1.36 fm
- Minnesota exchange parameter: u = 0.993 for positive parity, u = 0.967 for negative parity
- Spin-orbit strength V_ls = -20 MeV·fm⁵, range r_ls = 0.1 fm
- Truncation energy E_max = 15 MeV, maximum projectile angular momentum j_max = 7/2
- Coulomb barrier energies: approximately 38.9 MeV for 9Be+208Pb, approximately 8.0 MeV for 9Be+27Al
- Laboratory energies studied for 9Be+208Pb: 38, 44, 50, and 75 MeV

## Context
This work extends the microscopic CDCC formalism previously applied to 7Li (Descouvemont and Hilaire, 2013) and 6He (Descouvemont, 2016) to the three-cluster projectile 9Be, using the SVM for basis generation. It confirms the findings of non-microscopic CDCC calculations for 9Be+208Pb and 9Be+27Al (Descouvemont et al., 2015; Cravo et al., 2015) that breakup effects are important for heavy targets but weaker for light targets. The method enables future calculations for heavier Be isotopes (10Be, 11Be) where the number of degrees of freedom is larger, and where a microscopic description with only nucleon-target interactions would be particularly valuable given the poor knowledge of fragment-target optical potentials for exotic nuclei.
